to send back

1. VERB

/ sɛnd bæk / (AmE), / sɛnd bæk / (BrE)
to refer a matter or legal case to a different committee, authority, or court for a new decision or further consideration
refer a matter to another authority
legal

Examples

The appeals court decided to send back the case to the lower court for review.
If new evidence emerges, the committee may send back the proposal for further investigation.
The judge chose to send back the file to another panel for reconsideration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remit - to send a case or matter to another authority for a decision
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00952006-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛnd bæk / (AmE), / sɛnd bak / (BrE)
to return something to the place or person it came from
return something to its sender or place

Examples

Please send back the signed contract as soon as possible.
If the item is damaged, you can send back your purchase for a refund.
The teacher asked us to send back the completed forms by email.

Synset Examples

I forgot to send back the library book on time.
Please send back the report by Friday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to send or bring something back to where it came from
