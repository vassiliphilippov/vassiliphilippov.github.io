to take a gamble

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈɡæm.bəl / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈɡæm.bl̩ / (BrE)
to do something that involves risk in the hope of gaining an advantage or a desired result
take a risk, especially for possible gain

Examples

It might be risky, but sometimes you have to take a gamble to get ahead in life.
They decided to take a gamble on the new restaurant despite mixed reviews.
Investors often take a gamble when supporting emerging tech companies.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you have to take a gamble to succeed.
They decided to take a gamble on the weather for the picnic.
He took a gamble on the new business venture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a chance - to take a risk in hopes of success
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid taking risks
