to tear down

1. VERB

/ tɛr daʊn / (AmE), / tɛə daʊn / (BrE)
to demolish or destroy a building or other structure completely, making it level with the ground
demolish or destroy a structure

Examples

The city decided to tear down the old stadium to make room for a new park.
Protesters tried to tear down the controversial statue during the demonstration.
They finally tore down the derelict house that had stood empty for years.

Synset Examples

The building was levelled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - to pull or knock down a building
  • Synonym: level - to make something level, especially by demolishing it
  • Antonym: build - to construct by putting parts or materials together

2. VERB

/ tɛr daʊn / (AmE), / teə daʊn / (BrE)
to demolish (a building or structure) or to remove something completely
demolish or remove completely

Examples

The old warehouse was torn down to make space for a new park.
Protesters demanded that the government tear down the unfair laws.
They decided to tear down the fence between the two gardens.

Synset Examples

They plan to tear down the abandoned house soon.
We need to tear down these barriers to communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - destroy or pull down (a building)
  • Antonym: to build up - to construct or develop (especially a building or structure)
