to take advantage of

1. VERB

/ teɪk ədˈvæntɪdʒ əv / (AmE), / teɪk ədˈvɑːntɪdʒ əv / (BrE)
to use someone's weakness or a situation for one's own benefit, typically in an unfair or unethical way
exploit someone or something unfairly

Examples

It's wrong to take advantage of people who trust you.
She felt that her colleagues were trying to take advantage of her generosity.
Scammers often take advantage of elderly people online.

Synset Examples

That scanger always tries to take advantage of others.
That sleazoid tried to take advantage of her kindness.
That scummer took advantage of my kindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to use someone or something unfairly for your own benefit
