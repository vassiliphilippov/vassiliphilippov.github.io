to take form

1. VERB

/ teɪk fɔrm / (AmE), / teɪk fɔːm / (BrE)
to develop or begin to have a clear or definite shape, character, or existence
develop into a clear or distinct entity

Examples

The vague idea for a novel started to take form in her mind.
Over time, the sculpture began to take form before our eyes.
Their business proposal gradually took form as they discussed their goals.

Synset Examples

our plans began to take shape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take shape - to develop a distinct or definite form or result

2. VERB

/ teɪk fɔrm / (AmE), / teɪk fɔːm / (BrE)
to begin to develop or become recognizable as a definite shape or idea
begin to become recognizable

Examples

After months of planning, the new community center finally began to take form.
Her novel ideas started to take form as she outlined her chapters.
The painter watched the image take form on the canvas with each new brushstroke.

Synset Examples

The sculpture began to take form after several weeks of work.
Her ideas started to take form during the brainstorming session.
The project will take form once we finalize the budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to come into view; become apparent or prominent
  • Synonym: to materialize - to become actual or real; to take physical form
  • Antonym: dissolve - to gradually disappear or fade away
