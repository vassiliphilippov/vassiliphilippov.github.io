to luck out

1. VERB

/ lʌk aʊt / (AmE), / lʌk aʊt / (BrE)
to have good fortune or succeed by chance, often unexpectedly
to succeed or have good fortune by chance
informal

Examples

We really lucked out with the weather on our wedding day; it was sunny all afternoon.
You might luck out and find tickets still available for the concert.
I lucked out and got the job even though I was late to the interview.

Synset Examples

I lucked out and found the last parking spot in the lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lucky - to experience good luck or fortune
  • Antonym: to miss out - to fail to benefit from something, to not participate

2. VERB

/ lʌk aʊt / (AmE), / lʌk aʊt / (BrE)
to have good luck or a fortunate outcome, especially unexpectedly or by chance
have unexpected good fortune
informal

Examples

I couldn't believe I lucked out and got front-row seats to the concert.
They really lucked out when the weather stayed sunny all weekend.
She lucked out and found her lost keys at the park.

Synset Examples

I really lucked out with my new job.
She lucked out and found a great deal on shoes.
We lucked out when the rain stopped for the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike it lucky - to experience sudden good luck
