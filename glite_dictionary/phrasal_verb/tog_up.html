to tog up

1. VERB

/ tɑg ʌp / (AmE), / tɒg ʌp / (BrE)
to put on special or elaborate clothes in order to look attractive or impressive
dress in special or fancy clothes
informal

Examples

We decided to tog up for the gala dinner and wear our best outfits.
She loves to tog up in vintage dresses when she goes dancing.
Everyone togged up for the annual awards ceremony.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or special clothes to look nice

Etymology

From 'tog' (slang for clothes) probably from Latin 'toccare', through obsolete thieves' cant 'toggery'.
