to pull down

1. VERB

/ pʊl daʊn / (AmE), / pʊl daʊn / (BrE)
to demolish or destroy a building or structure so that it is level with the ground
demolish or destroy a building

Examples

The city decided to pull down the old theater to make way for a new park.
They pulled down the condemned house last week after it became unsafe.
Many historic buildings were pulled down during the urban renewal project.

Synset Examples

The building was levelled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - to completely destroy a building
  • Synonym: to knock down - to demolish a building by force
  • Antonym: erect - to build or construct a building
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01665698-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊl daʊn / (AmE), / pʊl daʊn / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to come down, often by physical force; to bring down to a lower position
cause to come down by force

Examples

The workers had to pull down the damaged wall after the storm.
She managed to pull down the old curtains despite their weight.
Protesters attempted to pull down the statue during the demonstration.

Synset Examples

The policeman downed the heavily armed suspect
The mugger knocked down the old lady after she refused to hand over her wallet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring down - cause something to come down or fall
