to hear out

1. VERB

/ hɪr aʊt / (AmE), / hɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to listen to someone until they have finished saying everything they want to say, especially when it is important or necessary to be fair
listen to someone fully

Examples

Please hear out my explanation before you make a decision.
She insisted that everyone hear out her side of the story.
He patiently heard out all the complaints before responding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listen to - pay attention to what someone is saying; give ear to

2. VERB

/ hɪr aʊt / (AmE), / hɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to listen to someone until they have finished speaking, especially to allow them to explain fully
listen until someone finishes speaking

Examples

Please hear out my proposal before making a decision.
She always makes time to hear out her employees' concerns.
We should hear out both sides of the argument before we judge.

Synset Examples

The teacher was willing to hear out any concerns.
It's important to hear out everyone's opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listen to - to give attention to someone or something in order to hear them
