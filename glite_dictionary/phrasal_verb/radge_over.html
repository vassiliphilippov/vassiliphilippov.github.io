to radge over

1. VERB

/ rædʒ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / rædʒ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Scottish) To become extremely angry or upset, often over something minor.
get excessively angry or upset (chiefly Scottish)
informal british

Examples

He tends to radge over the slightest inconvenience, which makes working with him difficult.
There’s no need to radge over a late train; just relax and wait.
She often radges over things that most people would ignore.

Synset Examples

Don't radge over small mistakes; it's not worth it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry

Etymology

Scottish dialect, from 'radge' meaning crazy or enraged.
