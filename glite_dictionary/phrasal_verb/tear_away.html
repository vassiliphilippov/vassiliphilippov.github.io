to tear away

1. VERB

/ tɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tɛə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or detach something by pulling or ripping it violently and forcefully
remove forcibly by pulling or ripping

Examples

The wind was strong enough to tear away several roof tiles from the building.
I had to tear away the old wallpaper before painting the walls.
He tore away the plastic covering to reveal the new phone inside.

Synset Examples

The passing bus tore off her side mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rip off - to remove something swiftly or violently, especially by tearing
  • Synonym: to detach - to separate or remove something from something else
