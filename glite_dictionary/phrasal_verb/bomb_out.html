to bomb out

1. VERB

/ bɑm aʊt / (AmE), / bɒm aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone homeless by destroying their home with bombs, especially in wartime
make someone homeless with bombs

Examples

Many families were bombed out during the air raids in World War II.
The attack bombed out hundreds of residents, leaving them with nowhere to go.
After their apartment building was bombed out, they had to seek refuge elsewhere.
