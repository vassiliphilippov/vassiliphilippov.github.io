to brush off

1. VERB

/ brʌʃ ɔf / (AmE), / brʌʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to refuse to consider or pay attention to someone or something; to dismiss or ignore them in a casual or abrupt way
dismiss or ignore someone or something

Examples

She tried to explain, but he simply brushed off her concerns.
The company brushed off the reporter's questions about safety.
Despite criticism from others, he brushed off their remarks and continued his work.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - refuse to consider; to put out of mind
  • Synonym: to ignore - take no notice of; pay no attention to
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brʌʃ ɔf / (AmE), / brʌʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to dismiss or ignore someone or something in a casual or indifferent way
dismiss or ignore casually

Examples

He tried to brush off the criticism, but it clearly bothered him.
You can't just brush off your team's suggestions if you want to improve.
Politicians often brush off tough questions during interviews.

Synset Examples

Don't brush off your responsibilities; take them seriously.
She would brush off compliments with a smile.
He tried to brush off her concerns as unfounded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to treat something as unworthy of serious consideration
  • Synonym: to ignore - to refuse to take notice of or acknowledge

3. VERB

/ brʌʃ ɔf / (AmE), / brʌʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to dismiss someone or something in a casual or unimportant way; to refuse to consider or pay attention to someone or something seriously
dismiss or ignore casually

Examples

She tried to explain her idea, but he simply brushed off her concerns.
The company brushed off complaints from customers, refusing to make any changes.
He brushed off their suggestions, saying he already had a plan.

Synset Examples

After he brushed me off, I shot back, 'Hello yourself!'
He brushed off the scurf from his jacket after dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to; disregard
  • Antonym: to consider - to think about carefully
