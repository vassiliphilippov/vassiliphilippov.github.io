to bob under

1. VERB

/ bɑb ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / bɒb ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to disappear suddenly and briefly beneath the surface, especially of water
disappear briefly beneath the surface

Examples

The duck would bob under the water every time it saw a fish.
He bobbed under the surface to avoid being spotted by the others.
The cork kept bobbing under with each passing wave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - to plunge headfirst into water
  • Antonym: surface - to rise to the surface of water
