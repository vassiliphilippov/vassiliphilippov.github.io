to push up

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ʌp / (AmE), / pʊʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to move or force something to a higher position by pushing it, especially with sudden or strong force
force something upwards by pushing

Examples

The strong wind pushed up the branches against the window.
Rescue workers pushed up the debris to reach the trapped survivors.
The blast pushed up the metal roof, causing it to warp badly.

Synset Examples

The front of the trains that had collided head-on thrust up into the air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust up - to push upward forcefully
  • Antonym: to press down - to push downwards with force
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02719727-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ ʌp / (AmE), / pʊʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to move or lift something by pressing or pushing it from underneath or below
move or lift by pushing from beneath

Examples

He managed to push up the window after it got stuck.
The seedlings will push up through the soil in spring.
She had to push up the box to reach the shelf above.

Synset Examples

She pushed up her children when the policeman came by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - to raise something to a higher position
  • Antonym: to pull down - to move or bring something to a lower position by pulling
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01877777-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pʊʃ ʌp / (AmE), / pʊʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to move or raise something to a higher position by applying force from below or behind
raise by pushing from below

Examples

He used his arms to push up the window and let in some fresh air.
The construction workers will push up the beams to assemble the frame of the building.
You must push up the lid carefully to avoid spilling the contents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - to raise something to a higher position
  • Antonym: to pull down - to move something to a lower position, typically by pulling
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01877523-v
Synset POS: v
