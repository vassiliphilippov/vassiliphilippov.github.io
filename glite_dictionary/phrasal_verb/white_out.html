to white out

1. VERB

/ waɪt aʊt / (AmE), / waɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to cover writing or marks with a white correction fluid in order to hide them
to cover with white correction fluid

Examples

I made a mistake, so I had to white out the incorrect word.
She carefully whited out the numbers before rewriting them on the form.
If you white out the error, your essay will look much cleaner.

Synset Examples

white-out the typo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove written or printed marks
