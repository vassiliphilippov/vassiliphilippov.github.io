to go to sleep

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə sliːp / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə sliːp / (BrE)
to begin sleeping or to prepare for sleep, typically by lying down and closing one's eyes
begin or prepare to sleep

Examples

She always reads a book before she goes to sleep.
After a long day, he couldn't wait to go to sleep.
The children were told to go to sleep early because of the trip tomorrow.

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall asleep - to enter a state of sleep
  • Antonym: to wake up - to cease sleeping; to become awake

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə sliːp / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə sliːp / (BrE)
to fall asleep or retire for the night; to enter a state of sleep
to begin sleeping

Examples

After a long day of hiking, I couldn't wait to go to sleep early.
He tends to go to sleep on the couch while watching TV.
If you go to sleep too late, you'll feel tired in the morning.

Synset Examples

He had to go to sleep early for the big day ahead.
I usually go to sleep by 10 PM.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall asleep - to pass from wakefulness to sleep
  • Antonym: to wake up - to stop sleeping and become awake
