to go off

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
to happen in a particular way, especially referring to events or performances
happen in a particular manner

Examples

The concert went off without a hitch despite the bad weather.
Her presentation went off better than she had expected.
The party went off smoothly, and everyone had a great time.

Synset Examples

how did your talk go over?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn out - to happen in a particular way or have a particular result

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
(of a gun, bomb, or similar device) to explode or fire.
(of a gun) to fire or discharge

Examples

The alarm will go off if someone opens the door after midnight.
Everyone ducked when the gun suddenly went off in the crowded room.
During the fireworks show, several rockets went off at the same time.

Synset Examples

The gun fired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - (of a gun or weapon) to discharge a bullet or shot
  • Synonym: to explode - to burst or shatter violently and noisily as a result of rapid combustion or excessive internal pressure

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
(of a device such as an alarm, explosive, or gun) to be discharged, activated, or to start making a loud noise suddenly.
be discharged or activated (device)

Examples

The fire alarm suddenly went off during our meeting, causing everyone to evacuate.
The bomb went off minutes after the building was cleared.
Her phone alarm goes off every morning at 6 a.m. to wake her up.

Synset Examples

the explosive devices went off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - to burst or shatter violently and noisily as a result of rapid combustion or excessive internal pressure
  • Synonym: to detonate - to cause to explode
  • Antonym: to go on - (of a device or power) to start working or become activated

4. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
(of a machine, light, or device) to stop running, functioning, or operating; to become disconnected or cease activity.
stop running or functioning (machine or device)

Examples

If the alarm goes off, it means there's a power failure in the system.
Her computer went off in the middle of her work, causing her to lose important files.

/ ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
(of a device, alarm, or explosive) to start ringing, sounding, or exploding suddenly; to activate without warning.
to ring, sound, or explode suddenly

Examples

When the timer went off, she knew the cookies were ready.
Everyone jumped when the bomb went off unexpectedly.
