to weather the challenge

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛðər ðə tʃəˈlændʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə ðə tʃəˈlændʒ / (BrE)
to successfully endure or deal with a difficult situation or period
to endure a difficult situation

Examples

Despite many setbacks, the team managed to weather the challenge and complete the project.
She drew on her resilience to weather the challenge of starting a business during a recession.
Learning to weather the challenge of criticism made him a stronger leader.

Synset Examples

How will you weather the challenges ahead?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty
  • Antonym: to succumb - to fail to resist pressure or a difficult situation

Etymology

From the verb 'weather', originally referring to surviving adverse weather; now used figuratively.
