to hit up against

1. VERB

/ hɪt ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / hɪt ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to encounter or face difficulties, opposition, or obstacles, often unexpectedly or in the course of an activity
encounter difficulties or obstacles

Examples

During the negotiations, we hit up against several unexpected legal issues.
Whenever he tries something new, he seems to hit up against skepticism from his peers.
Our team hit up against technical problems that delayed the project.

Synset Examples

I hit up against a major obstacle during the project.
She hit up against criticism for her controversial stance.
They hit up against resistance when proposing the changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run up against - to unexpectedly face difficulties or resistance
