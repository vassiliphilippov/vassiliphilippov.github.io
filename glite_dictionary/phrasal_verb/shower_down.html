to shower down

1. VERB

/ ˈʃaʊɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃaʊə daʊn / (BrE)
to fall or pour down abundantly like rain; to come down in large amounts from above
fall or pour abundantly from above

Examples

Ash began to shower down from the erupting volcano, covering the city in gray dust.
Confetti showered down on the newlyweds as they left the church.
Stars seemed to shower down from the sky during the meteor shower.

Synset Examples

Meteors showered down over half of Australia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour down - to rain heavily; to fall in a stream from above
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - to rain lightly with very fine drops

2. VERB

/ ˈʃaʊ.ər daʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃaʊ.ə daʊn / (BrE)
(of rain, particles, or objects) to fall in droplets or small amounts from above, typically in large numbers and swiftly.
fall in drops or small amounts from above

Examples

Hundreds of confetti pieces began to shower down from the ceiling during the celebration.
As sparks showered down from the welding torch, we kept a safe distance.
Leaves shower down onto the ground every autumn, covering the pathways in gold.

Synset Examples

The rain started to shower down unexpectedly.
They watched the fireworks shower down on New Year's Eve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rain - water that falls from the clouds in drops
  • Synonym: to pour down - to rain very heavily
