to gimp along

1. VERB

/ ɡɪmp əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡɪmp əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to walk with difficulty, especially with a limp, typically due to injury or physical impairment
walk with difficulty, limp

Examples

After his injury, he had to gimp along behind the group.
She watched her friend gimp along the path after twisting his ankle.
Despite his sprained foot, he managed to gimp along and finish the hike.

Synset Examples

He had to gimp along after twisting his ankle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - to walk with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot

Etymology

From American English 'gimp', meaning a limp or a person with a limp, likely from older dialectal terms.
