to cool down

1. VERB

/ kul daʊn / (AmE), / kuːl daʊn / (BrE)
to become less intense, excited, or angry; to diminish in emotion or activity
to become less intense or emotional

Examples

After the argument, she needed time to cool down before talking again.
His excitement about the new project began to cool down after a few weeks.
When tempers cool down, we can discuss the issue rationally.

Synset Examples

His enthusiasm cooled considerably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become less angry or excited

2. VERB

/ kul daʊn / (AmE), / kuːl daʊn / (BrE)
to make something cooler or become less hot
make or become cooler

Examples

Let the soup cool down before serving it to the guests.
After the engine overheated, we had to wait for it to cool down.
Opening the window helped the room cool down quickly.

Synset Examples

Chill the food

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to warm up - to make something warmer or become more warm
  • Synonym: cool - to make cool or become cool

3. VERB

/ kul daʊn / (AmE), / kuːl daʊn / (BrE)
(of a substance or environment) to become less hot; to lose heat and approach a lower temperature.
lose heat; become less hot

Examples

You should let the soup cool down before eating it.
After exercise, walk around to help your body cool down gradually.
The engine takes a while to cool down after a long drive.

Synset Examples

The air cooled considerably after the thunderstorm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to warm up - to increase in temperature; become warmer
  • Synonym: chill - to make or become cold; cool to a lower temperature

4. VERB

/ kuːl daʊn / (AmE), / kuːl daʊn / (BrE)
to become or make something less hot, or to become calmer after being angry or excited
become less hot or less angry

Examples

He went for a walk to cool down after the argument.
The weather should cool down once the sun sets.
