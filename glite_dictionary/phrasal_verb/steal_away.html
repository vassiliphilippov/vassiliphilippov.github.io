to steal away

1. VERB

/ stiːl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / stiːl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly and secretly, so that no one notices
to leave secretly and quietly

Examples

She tried to steal away from the party without anyone noticing.
He stole away during the meeting, hoping no one would see him leave.
As dawn approached, the thieves stole away into the shadows.

Synset Examples

The lecture was boring and many students slipped out when the instructor turned towards the blackboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip away - to leave quietly or without being noticed
  • Synonym: to sneak out - to leave a place secretly or unexpectedly
