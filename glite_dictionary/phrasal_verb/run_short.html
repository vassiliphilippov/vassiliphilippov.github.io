to run short

1. VERB

/ rʌn ʃɔrt / (AmE), / rʌn ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to have nearly used up or exhausted a supply of something; to have little of something left
almost out of something

Examples

We'd better buy more milk before we run short again.
They ran short of supplies during the long winter.
If you don't budget carefully, you might run short of money before payday.

Synset Examples

The money had gone after a few days
Gas is running low at the gas stations in the Midwest

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to run out - to have no more of something left; to be completely spent
