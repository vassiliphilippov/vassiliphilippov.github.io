to make headway

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈhɛdˌweɪ / (AmE), / meɪk ˈhɛdˌweɪ / (BrE)
to make progress toward achieving something or gaining an advantage, especially after overcoming difficulties
make progress or gain an advantage

Examples

Despite the heavy rain, the construction crew managed to make headway on the bridge repairs.
The company is finally making headway in reducing its carbon emissions.
It took hours, but we made headway through the dense forest.

Synset Examples

The home team was gaining ground
After defeating the Knicks, the Blazers pulled ahead of the Lakers in the battle for the number-one playoff berth in the Western Conference
win points

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - to move forward or make progress
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up or lose ground

2. VERB

/ meɪk ˈhɛdˌweɪ / (AmE), / meɪk ˈhɛd.weɪ / (BrE)
to make progress, especially when this is slow or difficult
to make progress

Examples

Despite strong resistance, the team managed to make headway on the project.
After several hours, the hikers began to make headway through the dense forest.
It took months, but the researchers finally started to make headway in their experiments.

Synset Examples

She made headway despite the challenges faced.
Despite the setbacks, we made significant headway today.
I hope to make headway by next week.
She felt they were finally making headway in negotiations.
We're finally making headway on the project.
We're making headway on the project despite the challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a destination
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with progress

Etymology

From 'headway' (forward movement or progress), originally nautical, combined with 'make'.
