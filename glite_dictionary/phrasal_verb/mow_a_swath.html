to mow a swath

1. VERB

/ moʊ ə swɑːθ / (AmE), / məʊ ə swɒθ / (BrE)
to cut a path through an area, especially by using a scythe or mower to clear away grass or plants
cut a path through vegetation

Examples

The hikers mowed a swath through the dense undergrowth to reach the clearing.
With his scythe, the farmer quickly mowed a swath across the overgrown field.
Emergency workers mowed a swath through the debris to rescue the trapped family.

Synset Examples

He mowed a swath through the tall grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut a path - to create a passage by clearing a way through
