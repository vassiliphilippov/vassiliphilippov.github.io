to sit through

1. VERB

/ sɪt θru / (AmE), / sɪt θruː / (BrE)
to stay until the end of something, especially something boring or unpleasant, even though you would prefer to leave
endure until the end of something unpleasant

Examples

We had to sit through a three-hour meeting that could have been an email.
She managed to sit through the entire play, even though she wasn't interested.
He didn't want to sit through another one of his uncle's long, dull stories.

Synset Examples

He could barely sit through the boring movie.
They decided to sit through the performance despite the rain.
I had to sit through the entire lecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endure - to suffer patiently through something painful or unpleasant
  • Synonym: to tolerate - to allow or accept something unpleasant or difficult
