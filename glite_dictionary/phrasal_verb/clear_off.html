to clear off

1. VERB

/ klɪr ɑf / (AmE), / klɪə ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something from a surface or from sight, usually so it is no longer visible or in the way
remove something from sight or surface

Examples

Please clear off the table before dinner so we have space to eat.
He cleared off the desk, putting all the papers into drawers.
Could you clear off your things from the couch so I can sit down?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away
  • Synonym: to clear away - to remove and take away completely
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00181704-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klɪr ɑf / (AmE), / klɪə ɒf / (BrE)
to remove objects, dirt, or substances from a surface so that it becomes empty or clean
remove items from a surface

Examples

Before setting the table, please clear off any dishes or crumbs.
She cleared off her workbench to make space for the new project.
It took him an hour to clear off all the snow from the car.

Synset Examples

You need to clear off your desk before the meeting.
They told us to clear off the snow from the driveway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clear away - to remove objects from a place, especially to make it tidy

Synset POS: None
