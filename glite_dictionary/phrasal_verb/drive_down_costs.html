to drive down costs

1. VERB

/ draɪv daʊn kɑsts / (AmE), / draɪv daʊn kɒsts / (BrE)
to reduce expenses, especially in a business or organizational context
to reduce expenses

Examples

The company implemented new measures to drive down costs across all departments.
Management is always looking for innovative ways to drive down costs in production.
By outsourcing some services, they were able to drive down costs significantly.

Synset Examples

The company aims to drive down costs next quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut costs - to reduce expenses
  • Antonym: increase costs - to make expenses higher
