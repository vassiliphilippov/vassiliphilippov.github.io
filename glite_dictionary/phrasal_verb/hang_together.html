to hang together

1. VERB

/ hæŋ təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / hæŋ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to be logically or meaningfully connected as a single unit; to form a coherent or unified whole
to be connected or form a coherent whole

Examples

Her explanation did not hang together with the facts we know.
The novel's various storylines hang together remarkably well.
Without evidence, his argument simply doesn't hang together.

Synset Examples

In my heart I can make the world hang together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cohere - to stick together; be united
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to fail to remain connected or coherent
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02628114-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hæŋ təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / hæŋ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to remain united or cooperative, especially in the face of challenges or adversity
remain united; cooperate in difficulty

Examples

During difficult negotiations, the employees chose to hang together despite management's demands.
If our community can hang together, we'll overcome this crisis.
The friends knew they had to hang together to finish the challenging project.

Synset Examples

The team must hang together for a successful outcome.
Let's make sure we all hang together during tough times.
If we hang together, we'll get through this.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stick together - to remain united and support each other
