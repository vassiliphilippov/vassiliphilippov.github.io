to skim off

1. VERB

/ skɪm ɑf / (AmE), / skɪm ɒf / (BrE)
to remove a substance from the surface of a liquid, especially by using a utensil
remove from the surface of a liquid

Examples

She used a spoon to skim off the fat that had risen to the top of the soup.
The workers skimmed off the cream from the surface of the milk at the dairy.
After the oil spill, special equipment was used to skim off the oil floating on the water.

Synset Examples

skim cream from the surface of milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01263582-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skɪm ɑf / (AmE), / skɪm ɒf / (BrE)
to select and take the best people or things from a group, leaving others behind
take the best from a group

Examples

Elite universities often skim off the top students from high schools across the country.
The new company managed to skim off the best engineers from its competitors.
Critics say the policy helps banks skim off the wealthiest clients, leaving poorer customers with fewer options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cherry-pick - to selectively choose the best or most desirable items, people, or ideas
