to take away

1. VERB

/ teɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / teɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or get rid of something, especially something abstract such as a feeling, right, or obstacle
remove or get rid of something abstract

Examples

Therapy helped take away his fear of public speaking.
New regulations take away some of the rights workers previously enjoyed.
The apology did not take away the pain she felt.

Synset Examples

The death of her mother removed the last obstacle to their marriage
God takes away your sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or position
