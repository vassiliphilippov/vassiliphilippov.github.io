to bleach out

1. VERB

/ bliːtʃ aʊt / (AmE), / bliːtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to make something become white or lighter in color, especially by exposure to sunlight or chemicals
make something become lighter or white

Examples

After a few weeks in the sun, the curtains bleached out almost completely.
Chlorine can bleach out stubborn stains from clothing.
The artist used a solution to bleach out parts of the photograph for effect.

Synset Examples

The sun bleached the red shirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to whiten - make or become white or whiter
  • Antonym: to darken - make or become darker in color
