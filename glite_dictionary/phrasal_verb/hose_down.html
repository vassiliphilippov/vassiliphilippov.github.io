to hose down

1. VERB

/ hoʊz daʊn / (AmE), / həʊz daʊn / (BrE)
to spray water onto someone or something using a hose, typically to clean or water it
spray with water from a hose

Examples

After the muddy game, the coach decided to hose down all the soccer balls.
The fire crew had to hose down the burning building for several hours.
On hot days, my father would hose down the driveway to cool it off.

Synset Examples

hose the lawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to apply liquid in tiny drops over an area
  • Synonym: wash - to clean something with water or another liquid

2. VERB

/ hoʊz daʊn / (AmE), / həʊz daʊn / (BrE)
to spray water onto something, typically using a hose, in order to clean it
spray with water to clean

Examples

After the construction work, the crew had to hose down the dusty pavement before leaving.
Please hose down the car to remove all of the mud before you bring it into the garage.
They hosed down the equipment after each use to maintain cleanliness.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean something by using water or another liquid
