to double up

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌb.əl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ʌp / (BrE)
to share a room or bed intended for one person, often due to lack of space or resources
share a room/bed meant for one

Examples

If there aren’t enough beds, we may have to double up for the night.
During the conference, many participants had to double up in the same hotel rooms.
When we traveled as children, my brother and I would often double up to save money.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02300678-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdʌb.əl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ʌp / (BrE)
to bend over or curl up, usually because of laughter or pain
bend over or curl up (laughter/pain)

Examples

The comedian's joke was so funny that the whole audience doubled up with laughter.
She doubled up in pain after accidentally bumping her knee on the table.
He doubled up and grabbed his stomach as the cramps intensified.

Synset Examples

He doubled and vomited violently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to double over - to bend over, typically from pain or laughter
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02067237-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈdʌbəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ʌp / (BrE)
to stake the winnings from one bet on a subsequent wager, risking increasing the amount at stake to double initial gains or losses.
stake winnings from one bet on another

Examples

He decided to double up after his first win, hoping to double his money.
Many gamblers are tempted to double up when they're on a winning streak.
She lost everything by constantly doubling up her bets at the casino.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parlay - to use winnings from a previous bet as the stake for a new bet
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
