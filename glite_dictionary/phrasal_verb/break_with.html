to break with

1. VERB

/ breɪk wɪð / (AmE), / breɪk wɪð / (BrE)
to end a relationship or association with someone or something, especially in a formal or decisive way
end a relationship or association

Examples

After years of disagreement, the organization decided to break with its long-standing partner.
The artist chose to break with tradition and pursue a new style entirely.
Several members of the committee broke with the party over the controversial decision.

Synset Examples

China broke with Russia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split with - to separate from or end association with someone or something
