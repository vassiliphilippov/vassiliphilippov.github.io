to take care of

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈkɛr əv / (AmE), / teɪk ˈkeə əv / (BrE)
to look after someone's well-being or to deal with or handle a task or problem
look after or deal with

Examples

It's important to take care of your health by eating well and exercising regularly.
Could you take care of my plants while I'm away on vacation?
She always makes sure to take care of any problems as soon as they arise.

Synset Examples

'We need to take care of ourselves,' she said, and I replied, 'So true, bestie!'
I will take care of the arrangements for the meeting.
He promised to take care of the problem immediately.
Please take care of the pets while I'm away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look after - to be responsible for or care for someone or something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or pay proper attention to someone or something
