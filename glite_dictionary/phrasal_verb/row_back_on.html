to row back on

1. VERB

/ roʊ bæk ɑn / (AmE), / rəʊ bæk ɒn / (BrE)
to withdraw from or reverse a previous decision or statement, often due to pressure or reconsideration
withdraw or reverse a previous statement

Examples

After much criticism, the minister was forced to row back on the new tax policy.
The publisher had to row back on his earlier statements as more facts emerged.
Facing public outrage, the board quickly rowed back on its controversial decision.

Synset Examples

The company had to row back on its expansion plans.
He had to row back on his earlier commitment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retract - to take back a statement or decision
  • Synonym: to backtrack - to go back on a previous statement or action
  • Antonym: to confirm - to affirm or uphold a previous decision
