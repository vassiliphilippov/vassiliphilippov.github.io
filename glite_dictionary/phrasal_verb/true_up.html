to true up

1. VERB

/ truː ʌp / (AmE), / truː ʌp / (BrE)
to make something exactly level, balanced, straight, or concentric, especially in a technical or mechanical context
make level, straight, or balanced (technical)

Examples

The machinist had to true up the metal rod before assembly.
After mounting the wheel, he carefully trued up the rim to prevent wobbling.
They spent hours truing up the surface of the table to ensure it was perfectly flat.

Synset Examples

true up the cylinder of an engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to place or arrange things in a straight line or correct relative positions
