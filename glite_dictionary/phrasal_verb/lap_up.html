to lap up

1. VERB

/ læp ʌp / (AmE), / læp ʌp / (BrE)
(of an animal) to drink a liquid by taking it up with the tongue, usually in small repeated movements.
drink with the tongue (animal)

Examples

The kitten eagerly lapped up the cream from its bowl.
After a long walk, the dog lapped up water from the dish.
In the wild, deer cautiously lap up water from the edge of the stream.

Synset Examples

The cat lapped up the milk
the cub licked the milk from its mother's breast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lick - to pass the tongue over something, usually to taste or moisten it
