to welsh out

1. VERB

/ wɛlʃ aʊt / (AmE), / wɛlʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to fail to do something you promised or agreed to do, especially by avoiding payment or a commitment
fail to fulfill a promise or commitment

Examples

He tried to welsh out of his part of the bargain at the last minute.
Don't welsh out now that everyone is counting on you.
She promised to help, but then she welshed out and left us on our own.

Synset Examples

It's frustrating when he always tries to welsh out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renege - to go back on a promise or agreement
  • Synonym: default - to fail to fulfill an obligation, especially a financial one

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from a negative stereotype related to Welsh people, considered offensive by some.
