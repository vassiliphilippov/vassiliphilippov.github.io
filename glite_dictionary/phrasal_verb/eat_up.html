to eat up

1. VERB

/ it ʌp / (AmE), / iːt ʌp / (BrE)
to finish eating all the food on one's plate or serving
finish all the food

Examples

Please eat up your vegetables before you leave the table.
The children ate up every last crumb of cake at the party.
He quickly ate up his breakfast so he wouldn't be late for work.

Synset Examples

She polished off the remaining potatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to polish off - to finish or consume something completely, especially food
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ it ʌp / (AmE), / iːt ʌp / (BrE)
to use up or consume a resource, supply, or material, often rapidly or completely
use up resources or materials

Examples

Heating bills really eat up our budget during the winter months.
This software update will eat up a lot of your phone's storage space.
The long commute has started to eat up all my free time.

Synset Examples

this car consumes a lot of gas
We exhausted our savings
They run through 20 bottles of wine a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consume - to use up or destroy; to eat or drink
  • Synonym: exhaust - to use (resources) completely
  • Antonym: conserve - to use (resources) carefully and not waste them
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ it ʌp / (AmE), / iːt ʌp / (BrE)
To enclose or envelop completely, as if swallowing; to consume entirely in a figurative or literal sense.
enclose or consume completely

Examples

The fast-growing city eventually ate up all the surrounding farmland.
The darkness seemed to eat up the fading light as night fell.
The thick fog ate up the coastline until nothing was visible.

Synset Examples

The huge waves swallowed the small boat and it sank shortly thereafter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to swallow up - envelop or consume completely
  • Synonym: to devour - to consume destructively or greedily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01585330-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ it ʌp / (AmE), / iːt ʌp / (BrE)
to consume food eagerly or completely; to finish eating all that is served
consume all of one's food

Examples

Please eat up your dinner so we can have dessert.
The children quickly ate up all the cookies on the plate.
Before we leave, make sure you eat up your breakfast.

Synset Examples

She always tells me to eat up and enjoy life.
Make sure to eat up before we head out.
He encouraged his kids to eat up their vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something quickly and eagerly
  • Antonym: to pick at - to eat food slowly or without enthusiasm
