to put off

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)
to make someone feel intense dislike or distaste towards something or someone
discourage or disgust; cause dislike

Examples

The strong smell of garlic can really put off some people from trying the dish.
His arrogant attitude tends to put off potential clients.
I was put off by the dirty tables at the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - to cause someone to feel revulsion or strong disapproval
  • Antonym: to attract - to cause someone to feel interested or drawn to something
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)
to delay or postpone something to a later time
postpone; delay to a later time

Examples

We had to put off our meeting because several people were sick.
Don’t put off doing your homework until the last minute.
They decided to put off their holiday until next year.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to arrange for something to happen at a later time
  • Antonym: to bring forward - to reschedule for an earlier time

/ pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)

Examples

Don't let their laughter put off your performance.

4. VERB

/ pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)
To discourage someone from doing something or to cause someone to lose enthusiasm or interest.
discourage; cause loss of enthusiasm

Examples

Her negative comments really put off a lot of new team members.
The smell in the kitchen put me off eating anything at the restaurant.
Don't let one bad experience put you off trying again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - Cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: to encourage - Give support, confidence, or hope to someone

5. VERB

/ pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)
to avoid or delay dealing with a duty, question, or problem; to postpone doing something unpleasant or difficult
to postpone or avoid dealing with something

Examples

He always tries to put off answering tough questions during meetings.
She decided not to put off her responsibilities any longer and finished her work.
It’s easy to put off unpleasant tasks, but eventually they must be done.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something to a later time
