to dwell on

1. VERB

/ ˈdwɛl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈdwɛl ɒn / (BrE)
to think, speak, or write at length about something, especially something unpleasant or troubling
speak or think at length about

Examples

He tends to dwell on his mistakes long after they've happened.
Try not to dwell on negative thoughts before bed.
They dwelled on the failures instead of celebrating their successes.

Synset Examples

Don't dwell on the past
She is always harping on the same old things

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harp on - to repeatedly talk or complain about a subject
  • Antonym: to move on - to stop thinking or talking about something

Etymology

From Old English 'dwellan' (to go astray, hinder), evolved in Middle English to mean 'remain'; sense of 'dwell on' as 'linger over a subject' is from the 19th century.
