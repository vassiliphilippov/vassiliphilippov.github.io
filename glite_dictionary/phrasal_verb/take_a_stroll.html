to take a stroll

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə stroʊl / (AmE), / teɪk ə strəʊl / (BrE)
to walk in a slow, relaxed way, especially for pleasure
walk casually or leisurely

Examples

After dinner, we decided to take a stroll around the neighborhood to enjoy the fresh air.
Whenever she feels stressed, she likes to take a stroll through the park.
They often take a stroll together on Sunday mornings.

Synset Examples

We took a stroll through the picturesque village by the river.
They took a stroll along Zil lane during sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - to walk in a slow, relaxed way
