to take control

1. VERB

/ teɪk kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / teɪk kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to begin to have power or authority over something or someone; to assume control
assume power or authority

Examples

After the manager resigned, Sarah quickly took control of the situation.
The new government is expected to take control next month.
If you want to succeed, you need to take control of your own destiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize control - to suddenly and forcefully take control
  • Antonym: lose control - to no longer have control
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ teɪk kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / teɪk kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to gain authority over a person, group, place, or situation and start making decisions
gain authority and make decisions

Examples

The new manager immediately took control of the team and implemented major changes.
During the emergency, she took control and organized the evacuation.
When the project fell behind schedule, he took control to get it back on track.

Synset Examples

He took control of the cockpit during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize control - suddenly or forcefully take authority
  • Antonym: lose control - to no longer have power or authority
