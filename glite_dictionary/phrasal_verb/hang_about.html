to hang about

1. VERB

/ hæŋ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / hæŋ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to linger, wait, or stay in a place without any clear purpose
linger or wait idly

Examples

We had to hang about for an hour before the bus finally arrived.
She doesn't like to hang about after meetings and prefers to leave quickly.
There's often a group of teenagers who hang about the park on weekends.

Synset Examples

There's no need to hang about; let's get started.
Don't hang about; we have places to be.
We decided to hang about until the rain stopped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang around - to stay or spend time in a place for no particular reason
