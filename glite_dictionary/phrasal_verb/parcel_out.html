to parcel out

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑr.səl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.səl aʊt / (BrE)
to divide and distribute something in portions or shares
divide and distribute in portions

Examples

The land was parceled out among the four brothers after their father died.
The teacher parceled out the art supplies to each group in the class.
After collecting the donations, the organizers parceled out food and blankets to the needy families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or share something among people or places
  • Synonym: to allocate - to assign or set apart for a particular purpose

Etymology

From 'parcel' (Middle English, from Old French 'parcelle'), meaning a portion, and 'out', denoting distribution.

2. VERB

/ ˌpɑːr.səl ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.səl ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to divide something into parts and distribute it among several people or groups, especially in small portions
distribute in parts

Examples

The landowner decided to parcel out the farmland among his three children.
The funds were parceled out to different departments based on their needs.
It's unfair to parcel out praise when everyone contributed equally to the project.

3. VERB

/ ˈpɑrsəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəl aʊt / (BrE)
to divide something into parts and distribute or allocate it to different people or groups
distribute in parts

Examples

The organization will parcel out the donations to several local charities.
It took hours to parcel out the supplies among the refugees.
After the harvest, the farmer parceled out vegetables to his neighbors.

Synset Examples

She decided to parcel out the inheritance among her children.
They plan to parcel out the land for development.
The manager will parcel out tasks to each team member.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out shares or portions
  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute resources or duties

Etymology

From the noun 'parcel' (Middle English, from Old French parcelle) plus the adverb 'out'.
