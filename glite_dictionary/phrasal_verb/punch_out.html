to punch out

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ ˈaʊt / (AmE), / pʌntʃ ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to record the time of leaving work, especially by using a time clock
register departure from work

Examples

Remember to punch out before you leave the building at the end of your shift.
She forgot to punch out yesterday, so her hours weren't recorded correctly.
At 5 p.m., everyone lines up to punch out and head home.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to punch in - to record the time of arriving at work, especially by using a time clock

Etymology

Originally from mechanical time clocks, which workers would 'punch' to record arrival and departure times.
