to rake over the past

1. VERB

/ reɪk oʊvər ðə pæst / (AmE), / reɪk əʊvə ðə pɑːst / (BrE)
to keep bringing up or discussing past events, especially mistakes or unpleasant incidents, often unnecessarily
repeatedly discuss past events or mistakes

Examples

She tends to rake over the past whenever an argument starts.
There's no need to rake over the past; let's focus on the future.
It's unhealthy to constantly rake over the past instead of moving forward.

Synset Examples

He decided to rake over the past mistakes to learn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwell on the past - to think or talk too much about past events
  • Antonym: to move on - to stop thinking or worrying about something from the past and progress
