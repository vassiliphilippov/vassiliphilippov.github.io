to pass in

1. VERB

/ pæs ɪn / (AmE), / pɑːs ɪn / (BrE)
to submit something for consideration, inspection, or processing, especially by handing it over to someone in authority
submit or hand over for consideration

Examples

Please pass in your homework when you finish.
All employees must pass in their ID badges at the end of the contract.
The teacher reminded us to pass in the permission slips by Friday.

Synset Examples

He forgot to pass in his checks for reimbursement.
Make sure to pass in your checks after the meeting.
You need to pass in your checks before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hand in - to give something to a person in authority for submission
  • Synonym: to turn in - to submit or deliver something, especially an assignment or document
