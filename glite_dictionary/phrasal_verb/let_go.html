to let go

1. VERB

/ lɛt ˈgoʊ / (AmE), / lɛt ˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
to release something or someone from one's hold or grasp
release from one's grasp

Examples

Please let go of my arm, you're hurting me.
When the train stopped, she let go of the handrail and stepped off.
You need to let go if you want to move forward.

Synset Examples

Let go of the door handle, please!
relinquish your grip on the rope — you won't fall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to let go of something that is held
  • Antonym: to hold on - to keep a grip on something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01477180-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɛt ɡoʊ / (AmE), / lɛt ɡəʊ / (BrE)
to stop worrying or being tense; to relax and allow oneself to feel free or at ease
relax, stop worrying

Examples

It's important to let go when you're on vacation and enjoy yourself.
Sometimes you just need to let go of your fears and trust the process.
After a long week, she tried to let go and have fun at the party.

Synset Examples

Don't be so worried all the time — just let go!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
  • Antonym: to tense up - to become nervous or anxious
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02742938-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lɛt ɡoʊ / (AmE), / lɛt ɡəʊ / (BrE)
to release something held, stop being emotionally attached, or dismiss someone from a job.
release, stop attachment, or dismiss from a job

Examples

After months of worrying, she finally decided to let go of her fears and move forward.
It was a difficult decision for the manager to let go several employees during the restructuring.
You'll feel better once you learn to let go of past mistakes.

Synset Examples

She knew it was time to let go and close her eyes for the last time.
The company decided to let one of the interns go.
The company had to let several employees go due to budget cuts.
He decided to let go and not hold a grudge anymore.
It's hard to let go of past mistakes.
It's time for him to let go of those apron strings.
He had to let go of his attachment to the job.
You need to unlax and let go of your worries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to let go of something physically or emotionally
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from employment
  • Antonym: to hold on - to keep one's grip (literal or figurative)
