to shimmy on down

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɪmi ɑn daʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi ɒn daʊn / (BrE)
to move or dance energetically, especially toward a particular place or destination
move or dance energetically toward a place
informal

Examples

As soon as the music started, she couldn't resist and shimmy on down to the dance floor.
We're all going to shimmy on down to the beach for the fireworks show.
Everyone grabbed a partner and began to shimmy on down the hallway in celebration.

Synset Examples

It's time to shimmy on down and enjoy the concert.
Let's shimmy on down to the dance floor!
They told me to shimmy on down and join the fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shimmy - to shake or move one's body quickly and energetically

Etymology

From 'shimmy' (to dance in a lively way; from 1910s American English dance term) + 'on down' (informal phrase meaning 'to move to a lower place or toward a destination').
