to blubber out

1. VERB

/ ˈblʌbɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈblʌbə aʊt / (BrE)
to say something while crying or sobbing, often in a way that is difficult to understand
utter words while crying

Examples

She blubbered out an apology through her tears, barely able to speak.
He blubbered out the terrible news to his friends, unable to control his sobbing.
Through her sobs, she finally blubbered out everything that had happened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sob - to speak or cry with short, convulsive gasps
  • Degree Lower: mumble - to speak in a low, unclear way
