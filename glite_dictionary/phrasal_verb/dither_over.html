to dither over

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪðɚ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪðə ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to hesitate or be indecisive when making a decision, especially about something minor
hesitate or be indecisive

Examples

She always seems to dither over what to order at restaurants.
You can't dither over this decision any longer; we need an answer now.
Instead of acting quickly, he was dithering over every detail.

Synset Examples

She tends to dither over small choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hesitate - to be slow to act because of uncertainty
  • Antonym: to decide - to make a choice or come to a conclusion

Etymology

From the verb 'dither', possibly from dialectal English, meaning 'to tremble or behave irresolutely', and 'over' as a preposition indicating the subject of hesitation.
