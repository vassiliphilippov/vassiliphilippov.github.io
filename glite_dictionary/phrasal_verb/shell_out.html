to shell out

1. VERB

/ ʃɛl aʊt / (AmE), / ʃɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to pay or give money, especially when it is a large amount or given unwillingly
pay or give out money (often reluctantly)
informal

Examples

Despite complaining about the price, he finally shelled out for the concert tickets.
I don't want to shell out a fortune on a new phone just yet.
Every year, her parents shell out hundreds of dollars for her dance lessons.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present
dole out some money
shell out pocket money for the children
deal a blow to someone
the machine dispenses soft drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay - to give money in exchange for something
  • Synonym: to fork out - to pay money, often unwillingly or reluctantly
  • Synonym: to cough up - to reluctantly give money or something of value

Etymology

Originally American slang from the 1800s, likening the act of removing money from a shell (pocket or wallet).
