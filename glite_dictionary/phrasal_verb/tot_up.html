to tot up

1. VERB

/ tɑt ʌp / (AmE), / tɒt ʌp / (BrE)
to calculate the total amount or sum of something, especially by adding up a series of numbers or items
add up to find the total
british

Examples

After shopping, she totted up the receipts to see how much she'd spent.
The accountant quickly totted up the day's earnings in his notebook.
Could you tot up the number of chairs we need for the event?

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add up - to find the total by addition

Etymology

From British English "tot" (meaning 'a small amount'), plus 'up'.
