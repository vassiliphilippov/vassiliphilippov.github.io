to sack someone

1. VERB

/ sæk ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / sæk ˈsʌmwən / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from their job, especially abruptly or as a penalty
to dismiss from a job
informal british

Examples

The company decided to sack several workers after the merger.
If you keep arriving late, they might sack you.
He was sacked for serious misconduct.

Synset Examples

She was shocked to hear they would sack her.
He decided to sack the underperforming employee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire someone - to dismiss someone from their job
  • Synonym: dismiss someone - to end someone’s employment
  • Antonym: hire someone - to employ someone for a job

Etymology

From the noun 'sack' (a dismissal from employment), originally slang, possibly from French 'sac', alluding to giving a servant a sack for their belongings when dismissed.
