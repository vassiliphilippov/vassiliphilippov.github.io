to throw overboard

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ˈoʊvərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / θrəʊ ˈəʊvəbɔːd / (BrE)
to throw someone or something from a boat into the water
to throw from a boat into water

Examples

They decided to throw overboard the damaged cargo during the storm.
Fearing for their safety, the sailors threw overboard the extra supplies.
He watched in horror as they threw overboard his suitcase by mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jettison - to throw or drop something from a ship or aircraft

2. VERB

/ θroʊ ˈoʊvərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / θrəʊ ˈəʊvəbɔːd / (BrE)
to reject, eliminate, or discard something as unwanted or unnecessary
reject, eliminate, or discard

Examples

We had to throw overboard several old traditions to modernize the company.
The director decided to throw overboard the original script and start anew.
It can be hard to throw overboard habits that no longer serve you.

Synset Examples

The team decided to throw overboard unnecessary features from the project.
He had to throw overboard his old plans for a better strategy.
To save time, we should throw overboard all the extra tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something unwanted
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep or continue to have something
