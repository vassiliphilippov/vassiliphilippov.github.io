to trice up

1. VERB

/ traɪs ʌp / (AmE), / traɪs ʌp / (BrE)
to hoist or lift something up and secure it with a rope, especially by lashing it in place
hoist up and secure with a rope

Examples

The sailors had to trice up the hammocks before cleaning the deck.
Please trice up the loose ropes to prevent anyone from tripping over them.
They quickly triced up the cargo before the storm hit.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'trisen' (to hoist), related to nautical usage.

2. VERB

/ traɪs ʌp / (AmE), / traɪs ʌp / (BrE)
to raise, lift, or pull up and secure with a line, rope, or cord, especially by tying.
raise or secure with a rope or cord

Examples

The sailors had to trice up the loose ropes before the storm arrived.
She quickly triced up the window shade to let more sunlight into the room.
They triced up the hammock to keep it out of the way during the day.

Synset Examples

trice a window shade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoist - to raise or lift, especially by mechanical means

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'trīsen' (to hoist).
