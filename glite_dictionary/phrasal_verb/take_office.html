to take office

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈɑːfɪs / (AmE), / teɪk ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
to begin serving in an official position, especially after being appointed or elected
begin serving in an official position

Examples

The newly elected governor will take office next week.
After months of preparation, she was finally ready to take office as company president.
When did the mayor officially take office after the election results were announced?

Synset Examples

When will the new President take office?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to leave office - to stop serving in an official position
