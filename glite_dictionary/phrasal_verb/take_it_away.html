to take it away

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɪt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / teɪk ɪt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or clear something, especially something regarded as unwanted or unsatisfactory.
remove or clear something unwanted

Examples

If you don't like the dish, just ask the server to take it away.
After the meeting, the staff will take it away and clean up the room.
The faulty equipment was immediately taken away for repair.

Synset Examples

He looked at the waiter and said, 'Take it away.'
The chef said to take it away if it wasn't right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or position
