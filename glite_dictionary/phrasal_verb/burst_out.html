to burst out

1. VERB

/ bɝst aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːst aʊt / (BrE)
to appear or happen suddenly and unexpectedly
appear or happen suddenly

Examples

The sun burst out from behind the clouds after a rainy morning.
Fans burst out of the stadium when the game ended in an unexpected victory.
Smoke suddenly burst out from the engine, causing everyone to panic.

Synset Examples

The farm popped into view as we turned the corner
He suddenly popped up out of nowhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pop up - to appear suddenly or unexpectedly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02161949-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɝst aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːst aʊt / (BrE)
to suddenly begin to say something or express an emotion, especially by speaking or making a noise
suddenly begin speaking or expressing emotion

Examples

She couldn't help it and burst out laughing in the middle of the meeting.
"That's not fair!" the boy suddenly burst out.
Everyone burst out cheering when the final goal was scored.

Synset Examples

We burst out laughing
‘I hate you,’ she burst out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclaim - to say something suddenly and loudly
  • Antonym: to hold back - to restrain from expressing an emotion or words
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00931971-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɝst aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːst aʊt / (BrE)
to erupt or intensify suddenly, especially with emotion, sound, or activity
suddenly erupt or intensify

Examples

When the announcement was made, cheers burst out across the stadium.
She couldn't hold back any longer and burst out laughing.
Violence burst out unexpectedly after the tense negotiations broke down.

Synset Examples

Unrest erupted in the country
Tempers flared at the meeting
The crowd irrupted into a burst of patriotism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to break out suddenly and violently
  • Synonym: to flare up - to become suddenly intense or violent
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
