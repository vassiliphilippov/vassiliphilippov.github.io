to care for

1. VERB

/ kɛr fɔr / (AmE), / keə fə / (BrE)
to look after someone who is sick or injured and provide necessary treatment or attention
look after or treat (someone ill or injured)

Examples

After the accident, nurses worked tirelessly to care for the injured passengers.
He stayed home from work to care for his sick daughter.
Medical volunteers helped care for people affected by the hurricane.

Synset Examples

The doctor treated my broken leg
The nurses cared for the bomb victims
The patient must be treated right away or she will die
Treat the infection with antibiotics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat - to give medical care to someone for an illness or injury
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00078513-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɛr fɔr / (AmE), / keə fə / (BrE)
to have a liking, fondness, or preference for something or someone
to like or be fond of

Examples

I do not care for spicy food, so I'll choose something milder.
Do you care for some tea after your meal?
She doesn't care for action movies, preferring comedies instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like - to enjoy or find agreeable
  • Antonym: dislike - to not like or be opposed to
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01832565-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkɛr fər / (AmE), / ˈkeə fə / (BrE)
to feel affection for someone or something; to be fond of or attached to
to feel affection or fondness for

Examples

She clearly cares for her friends a great deal.
Do you care for classical music?
He still cares for his childhood pet, even after all these years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like - to find something agreeable or enjoyable
  • Degree Higher: to adore - to love and admire deeply
  • Antonym: dislike - to not like someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01780135-v
Synset POS: v
