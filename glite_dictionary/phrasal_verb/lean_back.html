to lean back

1. VERB

/ lin bæk / (AmE), / liːn bæk / (BrE)
to move your upper body backwards, often into a more relaxed or reclined position
move upper body backwards

Examples

She decided to lean back in her chair and close her eyes for a moment.
He leaned back to get a better view of the painting on the ceiling.
If you lean back too far, you might tip over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recline - to lean or lie back in a relaxed position
