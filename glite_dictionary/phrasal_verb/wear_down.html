to wear down

1. VERB

/ wɛr daʊn / (AmE), / wɛə daʊn / (BrE)
to make something gradually thinner, weaker, or less effective by continuous use or pressure
make thinner or weaker by use

Examples

Years of walking in the same shoes eventually wore down the soles.
Wind and rain can wear down even the hardest rocks over time.
Their constant criticism began to wear down her confidence.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - gradually wear away by natural agents
  • Synonym: to wear out - use something until it is no longer usable
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00470295-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɛr daʊn / (AmE), / wɛə daʊn / (BrE)
to make someone or something gradually weaker, less confident, or more tired through continuous pressure, effort, or stress
gradually exhaust or weaken through stress or use

Examples

After hours of negotiations, the constant arguments began to wear down even the toughest participants.
The repetitive tasks at his new job quickly wore down his enthusiasm.
She was determined not to let criticism wear her down, but it became more difficult over time.

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired
  • Antonym: to revive - to restore to life or consciousness

Synset POS: v
