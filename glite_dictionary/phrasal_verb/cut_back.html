to cut back

1. VERB

/ kʌt bæk / (AmE), / kʌt bæk / (BrE)
to reduce the amount, size, or number of something, especially in order to save money
reduce the amount of something

Examples

After reviewing my finances, I decided to cut back on dining out.
The company had to cut back its workforce due to declining sales.
Doctors often advise patients to cut back on sugar to improve their health.

Synset Examples

reduce your daily fat intake
The employer wants to cut back health benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount, size, or degree
