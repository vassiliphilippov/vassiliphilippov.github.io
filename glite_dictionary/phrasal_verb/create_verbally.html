to create verbally

1. VERB

/ kriˈeɪt ˈvɜrbəli / (AmE), / kriˈeɪt ˈvɜːbəli / (BrE)
to produce or invent something using spoken words or language rather than physical means
produce using spoken words

Examples

She can create verbally stunning stories on the spot, captivating everyone in the room.
The teacher asked the students to create verbally a dialogue based on the lesson.
Some comedians create verbally their routines through improvisation rather than by writing them out first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation
  • Synonym: to compose - to write or create a work, especially music or literature
