to send for

1. VERB

/ sɛnd fɔr / (AmE), / sɛnd fə / (BrE)
to order or request someone to come, especially by sending a message or making a formal request
to request someone to come

Examples

If you feel unwell, I will send for the doctor immediately.
She decided to send for her lawyer before answering any questions.
They had to send for help when their car broke down in the countryside.

Synset Examples

She was called into the director's office
Call the police!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summon - to order someone to be present
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to send someone away or allow them to leave

2. VERB

/ sɛnd fɔr / (AmE), / sɛnd fə / (BrE)
to request or order the presence of someone or something to come to a place, usually because they are needed or required.
request someone or something to come

Examples

The doctor told me to send for him if her condition got worse.
When the power failed, they had to send for an electrician immediately.
She decided to send for her old diaries from storage.

Synset Examples

They will send for a toss to choose the team leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summon - to call someone to be present
