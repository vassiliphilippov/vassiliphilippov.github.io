to keel over

1. VERB

/ kil ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / kiːl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to fall over suddenly, often as a result of illness, dizziness, or losing balance
fall over suddenly; collapse

Examples

She felt dizzy and keeled over in the middle of the room.
During the parade, one of the soldiers suddenly keeled over from heat exhaustion.
If you work in this heat for too long, you might keel over.

Synset Examples

the man had a heart attack and keeled over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time and fall over
  • Antonym: to stand up - to rise to a standing position

Etymology

From nautical usage 'keel' (the main structural element of a ship); to turn upside down as a ship does.

2. VERB

/ kil ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / kiːl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to fall over or collapse suddenly, especially as a result of illness, exhaustion, or shock
collapse or fall over suddenly

Examples

Halfway through the speech, the mayor keeled over and had to be carried off stage.
If you stay out in this heat too long, you might keel over.
Everyone burst out laughing when the comedian pretended to keel over from embarrassment.

Synset Examples

The old tree finally keeled over in the storm.
He suddenly keeled over during the marathon.
She nearly keeled over from laughter at the joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down suddenly, especially because of loss of strength or support
  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time and fall to the ground
