to make good on

1. VERB

/ meɪk gʊd ɑn / (AmE), / meɪk gʊd ɒn / (BrE)
to fulfill or carry out a promise, commitment, or obligation
fulfill a promise or obligation

Examples

She always tries to make good on her promises, no matter how difficult.
The company failed to make good on the guarantees it made to its customers.
After months of delay, he finally made good on his debt to his friend.

Synset Examples

They need to make good on their promise.
She will make good on her commitment to help.
He promised to make good on his bet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fulfill - to carry out or complete a promise or duty
  • Antonym: to break a promise - not do what one has committed to
