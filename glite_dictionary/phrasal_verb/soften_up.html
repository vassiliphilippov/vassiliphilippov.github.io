to soften up

1. VERB

/ ˈsɔfən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsɒfən ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone more receptive, agreeable, or lenient; or to make something physically softer and more pliable
make more lenient or softer

Examples

She tried to soften up her parents before asking for a favor.
The rain will soften up the ground, making it easier to dig.
They sent gifts to soften up the potential client before negotiations began.

Synset Examples

He tried to soften up the manager with compliments.
She needed to soften up the dough before baking.
The sun will soften up the ice by afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften - to make or become less hard, harsh, or severe
  • Antonym: to harden - to make or become harder, less sympathetic, or more strict
