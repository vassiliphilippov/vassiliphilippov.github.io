to dress up in a shroudie

1. VERB

/ drɛs ʌp ɪn ə ˈʃraʊdi / (AmE), / drɛs ʌp ɪn ə ˈʃraʊdi / (BrE)
to wear a ghost costume, especially for Halloween; to dress as a ghost typically using a simple sheet or shroud
wear a ghost costume for Halloween

Examples

Every Halloween, the children dress up in a shroudie and try to scare their neighbors.
She loves to dress up in a shroudie because it's the easiest costume to make.
When I was young, I would dress up in a shroudie and pretend to haunt the house.

Synset Examples

Kids love to dress up in a shroudie for trick-or-treating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on special clothes, often for a particular occasion

Etymology

From 'shroud' (a cloth used to wrap a body, especially for burial), here meaning a simple ghost costume; 'shroudie' is a diminutive or playful form.
