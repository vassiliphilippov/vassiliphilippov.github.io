to putter around

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌt̬ɚ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpʌtə əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move or occupy oneself in an aimless or unhurried way, without a clear purpose
move or do things aimlessly

Examples

He likes to putter around the garden on Sunday mornings.
I spent the afternoon puttering around the house, not really getting anything done.
She puttered around in the kitchen before finally starting dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to potter around - to move or do things aimlessly

Etymology

Origin of 'putter' uncertain; possibly from 'potter', meaning to move aimlessly.
