to trifle with

1. VERB

/ ˈtraɪfəl wɪð / (AmE), / ˈtraɪf(ə)l wɪð / (BrE)
to treat someone or something without seriousness, respect, or proper consideration; to handle something in a careless or frivolous manner.
treat lightly or carelessly

Examples

He warned her not to trifle with tasks that could affect the entire team.
You shouldn't trifle with other people's emotions, as it can cause real harm.
During negotiations, they realized the opponent did not trifle with important details.

Synset Examples

They decided to trifle with the rules during the game.
Don't trifle with my feelings; be honest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to toy with - to consider something or someone in a casual or non-serious way
  • Antonym: to take seriously - to treat something with appropriate importance or respect

Etymology

From the verb 'trifle' (from Old French 'trufler', to mock), plus 'with.'
