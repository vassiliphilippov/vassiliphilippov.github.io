to ease up

1. VERB

/ iːz ʌp / (AmE), / iːz ʌp / (BrE)
to reduce pressure, force, or intensity; to become less strict, severe, or active
reduce pressure or intensity

Examples

You should ease up on the accelerator as you approach the intersection.
The coach decided to ease up on the team's training schedule after their big win.
Tensions finally began to ease up as the negotiations progressed.

Synset Examples

he eased off the gas pedal and the car slowed down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or strict
  • Antonym: to tighten - to make or become more tight or intense
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ iz ʌp / (AmE), / iːz ʌp / (BrE)
to become less intense, severe, or forceful; to decrease in strength or strictness
become less intense or severe

Examples

The rain finally began to ease up after hours of heavy downpour.
If you ease up on the throttle, the engine will run more smoothly.
Tensions will ease up once the negotiations are underway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to intensify - to become or make more intense
