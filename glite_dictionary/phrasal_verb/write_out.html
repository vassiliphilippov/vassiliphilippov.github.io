to write out

1. VERB

/ raɪt aʊt / (AmE), / raɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to put something into writing, especially in complete or detailed form
put into writing in full form

Examples

Please write out your full name on the application form.
The doctor wrote out a prescription for the patient.
She is writing out the instructions so you don't forget anything.

Synset Examples

write out a contract

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write up - to write a report or description about something in detail
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01758384-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ raɪt aʊt / (AmE), / raɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to fill in and issue (a document, cheque, prescription, etc.) by writing the necessary details
fill in and issue a document, cheque, etc.

Examples

She had to write out a check to pay the bill.
The doctor will write out a prescription for your medication.
Could you please write out the receipt for this purchase?

Synset Examples

write out a check
cut a ticket
Please make the check out to me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make out - to complete and issue a document, cheque, etc.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01066821-v
Synset POS: v
