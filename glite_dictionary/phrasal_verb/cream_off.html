to cream off

1. VERB

/ kriːm ɑf / (AmE), / kriːm ɒf / (BrE)
to remove a top layer or portion, especially the best or richest part, from a liquid or mixture, typically by skimming
remove the top layer, esp. by skimming

Examples

She used a spoon to cream off the layer of fat from the top of the stew.
The dairy worker had to cream off the cream from the milk before bottling it.
It is important to cream off any impurities that float to the surface during cooking.

Synset Examples

skim cream from the surface of milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skim - to remove a substance from the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From 'cream' (the fatty layer on milk) + 'off'; refers to the dairy practice of removing cream from milk.

2. VERB

/ krim ɔf / (AmE), / kriːm ɒf / (BrE)
to remove the best people or the most desirable things from a group, especially for one's own advantage
select the best for oneself

Examples

The company tends to cream off the top students from leading universities.
They accused the agency of trying to cream off the most lucrative contracts for itself.
Every year, talent scouts cream off the best young players for their clubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cherry-pick - select the best or most desirable
  • Synonym: skim - remove the top layer, often the best part

Etymology

From the practice of removing the cream, the richest part of milk, for use; figurative use since the 19th century.

3. VERB

/ kriːm ɔf / (AmE), / kriːm ɒf / (BrE)
to take or remove the best part of something or the top layer, often leaving the rest behind
remove the best part or top layer

Examples

The manager tried to cream off the most talented employees for his new project.
Before serving, you should cream off the fat floating on the stew.
They were accused of attempting to cream off the profits instead of sharing them fairly.

Synset Examples

He tried to cream off the best profits from the deal.
You need to cream off the top layer of the soup.
Make sure to cream off that excess fat before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skim off - to remove a substance from the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From 'cream' (the thick, fatty layer on top of milk) + 'off'; refers to removing this top layer and by extension, the best part.
