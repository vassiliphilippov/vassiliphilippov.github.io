to patch up

1. VERB

/ pætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / pætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to reconcile or restore good relations after a disagreement or argument
reconcile after a disagreement

Examples

They finally decided to patch up their differences and move forward.
After the heated argument, it took weeks for them to patch up.
We should try to patch up things before the family meeting.

Synset Examples

After some discussion we finally made up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make up - to become friends again after a quarrel
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00766947-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / pætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to mend or repair something, especially by applying a patch to cover a hole or damaged area
mend by applying a patch

Examples

She used a piece of fabric to patch up the torn jeans.
They had to patch up the roof after the storm damaged several tiles.
He quickly patched up the hole in the wall before guests arrived.

Synset Examples

patch a hole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mend - to repair something that is broken or damaged
  • Synonym: repair - to restore something to a good condition after damage or wear

3. VERB

/ pætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / pætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to repair something, especially quickly or temporarily; to resolve or improve a problem or relationship
repair; fix or improve a situation

Examples

The mechanic managed to patch up the leak just enough to get us home.
After their heated disagreement, the two colleagues tried to patch up their working relationship.
She quickly patched up her torn jacket before heading out to the meeting.

Synset Examples

I need to patch up my jeans before wearing them.
It's time to patch up the roof before the rainy season.
They decided to patch up their friendship after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mend - to repair something that is broken or damaged
  • Synonym: fix - to repair or restore something to working order
  • Antonym: damage - to harm or spoil something
