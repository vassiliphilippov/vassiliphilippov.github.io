to fall back

1. VERB

/ fɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move or lag behind others in movement, progress, or development; to lose ground relative to others.
move or lag behind in progress or movement

Examples

You must not fall back in your studies if you want to graduate on time.
As the runners approached the final lap, two competitors began to fall back.
Technological advances mean that companies can fall back quickly if they do not innovate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to catch up - to reach or come level with someone who is ahead
  • Synonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep pace with others or a schedule

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɔl bæk / (AmE), / fɔːl bæk / (BrE)
to rely on or use something as a last resort when other options have failed
rely on as a last resort

Examples

When the primary plan failed, we had to fall back on our savings.
If he loses his job, he can fall back on his teaching credentials.
The company decided to fall back on traditional advertising when the online campaign proved ineffective.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to depend on - to rely on someone or something for support or help

3. VERB

/ fɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to return to previous bad habits or behavior, especially after an attempt to change or improve
return to previous bad behavior

Examples

After months of sobriety, he began to fall back into old drinking habits.
She promised to change but eventually fell back into lying to her friends.
It's easy to fall back into unhealthy routines during stressful times.

/ fɔl bæk / (AmE), / fɔːl bæk / (BrE)

retreat or move backward

Examples

If things get too risky, we’ll fall back and reconsider our plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to withdraw from a position, often to avoid danger
