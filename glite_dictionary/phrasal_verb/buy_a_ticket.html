to buy a ticket

1. VERB

/ baɪ ə ˈtɪkət / (AmE), / baɪ ə ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
to purchase admission for an event, journey, or activity, typically by paying money
purchase admission or entry

Examples

I arrived at the station early to buy a ticket before the morning rush.
She forgot to buy a ticket for the theater performance and it sold out.
You can buy a ticket online or at the venue's box office.

Synset Examples

I need to buy a ticket to the concert.
She decided to buy a ticket to the movie.
He bought a ticket to the concert last week.
He wants to buy a ticket to the championship game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purchase a ticket - to acquire a ticket by paying for it
  • Antonym: sell a ticket - to give a ticket to someone in exchange for payment
