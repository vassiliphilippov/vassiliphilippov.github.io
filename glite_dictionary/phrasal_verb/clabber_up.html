to clabber up

1. VERB

/ ˈklæbər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈklæbə ʌp / (BrE)
(of milk or cream) to sour, curdle, or thicken into clabber.
to curdle or thicken milk

Examples

If you leave fresh milk out in the heat, it will clabber up overnight.
The recipe called for the cream to clabber up before being churned into butter.
She forgot about the jug, and it started to clabber up on the windowsill.

Synset Examples

She accidentally let the milk clabber up on the counter.
The cream will clabber up if left out too long.
Don't clabber up the milk; it's meant to be fresh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to curdle - to form curds or lumps, especially in milk
  • Synonym: sour - (of milk) to become acidic or spoiled

Etymology

From 'clabber', meaning sour or curdled milk, of Irish origin (Old Irish 'clabar', meaning thick mud or mire).
