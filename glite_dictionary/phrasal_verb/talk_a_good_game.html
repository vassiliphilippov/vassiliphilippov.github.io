to talk a good game

1. VERB

/ tɔk ə ɡʊd ɡeɪm / (AmE), / tɔːk ə ɡʊd ɡeɪm / (BrE)
to speak convincingly or confidently about one's abilities or intentions, especially without necessarily having the skill or follow-through to match one's words
claim to be capable or skilled, often without proof

Examples

He always talks a good game, but rarely follows through on his promises.
She talked a good game about her leadership skills during the meeting.
You can't just talk a good game; you have to prove you can do it.

Synset Examples

She knows how to talk a good game during interviews.
He can talk a good game but lacks follow-through.
Don't just talk a good game; prove your skills!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction about one's achievements, possessions, or abilities
  • Antonym: to deliver - to actually achieve or perform what has been promised or claimed
