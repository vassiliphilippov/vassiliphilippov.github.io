to speed up

1. VERB

/ spiːd ʌp / (AmE), / spiːd ʌp / (BrE)
to move, happen, or make something move or happen at a faster rate
to move or occur faster

Examples

If you don't speed up, we'll miss the train.
The production line was sped up to meet the deadline.
Traffic always speeds up after the construction zone ends.

Synset Examples

The car accelerated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accelerate - to begin to move more quickly
  • Antonym: to slow down - to reduce speed or make something go slower
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00439133-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spiːd ʌp / (AmE), / spiːd ʌp / (BrE)
to make something or someone move, happen, or progress more quickly; to increase the speed of.
make move or happen faster

Examples

The driver tried to speed up to catch the green light.
Can you speed up the process so we finish earlier?
Production will speed up once the new machines arrive.

Synset Examples

He accelerated the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accelerate - to begin to move more quickly or to cause something to happen faster
  • Antonym: to slow down - to move or happen more slowly; to reduce speed
