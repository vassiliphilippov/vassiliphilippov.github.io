to blow wide open

1. VERB

/ bloʊ waɪd ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / bləʊ waɪd ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to cause something, especially a secret or mystery, to be suddenly and completely revealed or exposed
reveal or expose completely

Examples

After the whistleblower came forward, the scandal blew wide open and shocked the public.
The case blew wide open when a key witness agreed to testify.
As soon as the evidence was released, the conspiracy theory blew wide open.

Synset Examples

The investigation blew wide open after new evidence was found.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expose - to make something visible or known that was hidden
  • Antonym: to cover up - to hide the truth about something
