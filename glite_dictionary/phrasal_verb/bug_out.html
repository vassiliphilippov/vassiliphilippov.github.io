to bug out

1. VERB

/ bʌɡ aʊt / (AmE), / bʌɡ aʊt / (BrE)
(of a person's eyes) to bulge or protrude outward, often in surprise or shock.
(of eyes) bulge outward

Examples

I was so startled that my eyes seemed to bug out of my head.
His eyes bugged out when he saw the size of the cake.
If you keep staring at the screen, your eyes will bug out eventually.

Synset Examples

His eyes popped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pop - (of eyes) to protrude or stick out suddenly
  • Antonym: to recede - (of eyes, features) move back or become less prominent; the opposite physical direction

Etymology

From the noun 'bug' (insect, bug-eyed) + out (preposition/particle); possibly by analogy to bulging eyes resembling those of certain insects.
