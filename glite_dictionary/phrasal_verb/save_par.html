to save par

1. VERB

/ seɪv pɑr / (AmE), / seɪv pɑː / (BrE)
to complete a golf hole using the exact number of strokes allowed by par, typically after a situation where more strokes seemed likely
achieve par on a golf hole

Examples

Despite hitting his tee shot into the bunker, he managed to save par on the fifth hole.
She made a difficult ten-foot putt to save par and stay in contention.
If you miss the green, it's important to have a good short game to save par.

Synset Examples

He managed a remarkable putt from the rough to save par.
With a perfect chip shot, he saved par on the hole.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bogey - a score of one stroke over par on a hole in golf
  • Degree Higher: birdie - a score of one stroke under par on a hole in golf
