to muddy up

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌdi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi ʌp / (BrE)
to make something dirty by covering it or mixing it with mud
make dirty with mud

Examples

Don't muddy up your shoes by walking through that puddle.
The children muddied up the hallway after playing outside in the rain.
Try not to muddy up the floor after I just cleaned it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddy - make dirty with mud
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01255439-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌdi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi ʌp / (BrE)
to make something less clear, more confused, or more complicated than it needs to be
make something less clear or more complicated

Examples

Please don't muddy up the instructions with unnecessary details.
Legal jargon can really muddy up what should be a simple agreement.
His constant interruptions only muddy up the conversation.

Synset Examples

Don't muddy up the paint; keep it simple.
He tends to muddy up his arguments with confusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - make something harder to understand or less clear
  • Antonym: to clarify - make something easier to understand, make clear
