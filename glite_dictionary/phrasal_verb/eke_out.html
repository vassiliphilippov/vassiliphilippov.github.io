to eke out

1. VERB

/ ik aʊt / (AmE), / iːk aʊt / (BrE)
to obtain or achieve something with difficulty or great effort, often in small quantities
obtain with difficulty

Examples

Despite low wages, he managed to eke out a living in the city.
She eked out every last drop of ink from the dried-up pen.
The investigators eked out crucial details from reluctant witnesses.

Synset Examples

He eked out some information from the archives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape by - to manage to live with very little money or resources

Etymology

From Old English *ēcan* (to increase, add to), influenced by Middle English usage of 'eke' meaning to supplement.

2. VERB

/ ik aʊt / (AmE), / iːk aʊt / (BrE)
to make a supply or resource last longer by using or supplementing it carefully, especially when it is scarce or insufficient
supplement when something is not enough

Examples

Many families had to eke out their food supplies during the winter months.
She tried to eke out her small income by working extra shifts.
The soldiers eked out their rations with wild berries they found in the forest.

Synset Examples

He eked out his meager pay by giving private lessons
Braque eked out his collages with charcoal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplement - to add something in order to improve or complete it

Etymology

From Old English *ecan* (to increase, add to).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ik aʊt / (AmE), / iːk aʊt / (BrE)
to manage to make a supply of something last longer by careful use, or to get just enough to survive, especially with difficulty
survive or manage with difficulty

Examples

After losing his job, he had to eke out a living by doing odd jobs.
They managed to eke out their savings until he found another job.
The refugees tried to eke out an existence in the harsh desert conditions.

Synset Examples

He eked out his years in great poverty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape by - to manage to live with barely enough money or resources

Etymology

From Old English *ēacan* (to increase), related to 'eke' meaning to add or increase.

4. VERB

/ ik aʊt / (AmE), / iːk aʊt / (BrE)
to manage to make a supply or resource last longer by using it carefully, or to obtain or achieve something with difficulty and effort
manage with difficulty or supplement resources

Examples

For years he eked out a living by doing odd jobs around the village.
They had to eke out their food supplies until help arrived.
She eked out her small salary with occasional freelance work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape by - manage to survive with very little
  • Synonym: to get by - manage to survive despite difficulties
