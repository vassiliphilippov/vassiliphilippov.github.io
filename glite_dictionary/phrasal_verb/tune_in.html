to tune in

1. VERB

/ tun ɪn / (AmE), / tjuːn ɪn / (BrE)
to adjust a radio or television set in order to receive a particular station or program
adjust device to receive a station

Examples

Millions of Americans tune in to their favorite shows each week.
She quickly tuned in the radio to catch the morning news.
If you tune in at 8 p.m., you'll hear the live concert broadcast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to tune out - to stop listening to or ignore; opposite action of paying attention or adjusting to receive
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02175958-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tuːn ɪn / (AmE), / tjuːn ɪn / (BrE)
to watch or listen to a broadcast such as a television or radio program
listen to or watch a broadcast

Examples

I always tune in to the local news at six every evening.
Millions of viewers tuned in to watch the championship game.
You can tune in online if you can't reach a TV.

Synset Examples

Make sure to tune in for the live concert tomorrow.
Don't forget to tune in to the podcast tonight!
You can tune in to the radio station on your phone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to listen - to give attention with the ear; to hear attentively
  • Synonym: watch - to look at or observe attentively

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'tune' (adjust a radio/TV receiver) plus 'in.'
