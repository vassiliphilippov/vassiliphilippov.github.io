to harsh on

1. VERB

/ hɑrʃ ɑn / (AmE), / hɑːʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to criticize or treat someone in a very severe or unkind way
criticize or treat severely
informal american

Examples

The coach always harshes on the new players for their mistakes.
She didn't want her parents to harsh on her about her grades.
I wish my boss would stop harshing on me over every little error.

Synset Examples

the teacher keeps harshing on the same kid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be hard on - to treat someone strictly or severely
