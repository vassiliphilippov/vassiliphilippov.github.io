to bear up

1. VERB

/ bɛr ʌp / (AmE), / beə ʌp / (BrE)
to remain cheerful or show courage while facing difficulty or adversity
remain cheerful in adversity

Examples

She managed to bear up despite the bad news she received.
Even after losing his job, he tried to bear up for the sake of his family.
It's hard to bear up under so much pressure, but he remained optimistic.

Synset Examples

She bore up under the enormous strain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endure - to suffer patiently; to bear something difficult
  • Antonym: to give in - to yield or stop resisting

2. VERB

/ bɛr ʌp / (AmE), / bɛə ʌp / (BrE)
to remain strong or maintain one's courage and composure in difficult circumstances
endure or stay strong in difficulties

Examples

Despite all the setbacks, she continued to bear up and kept a positive attitude.
It was difficult for him to bear up after the loss of his friend.
The community tried to bear up during the long period of uncertainty.

Synset Examples

He managed to bear up despite the sad news.
They will bear up through tough times together.
She tried to bear up under the pressure of exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endure - to continue despite difficulty or suffering
  • Antonym: collapse - to give way completely under pressure or stress
