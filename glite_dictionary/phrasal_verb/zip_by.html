to zip by

1. VERB

/ zɪp baɪ / (AmE), / zɪp baɪ / (BrE)
to move past very quickly; to go by with great speed
move past very quickly

Examples

The sports car seemed to zip by in a blur as we stood on the sidewalk.
I watched the scenery zip by outside the train window during my morning commute.
Time will zip by when you're busy enjoying yourself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiz by - to move past swiftly with a whizzing sound or sensation
  • Synonym: flash by - to move past extremely quickly

Etymology

From 'zip' (imitative of a sudden movement or sound) + 'by'.
