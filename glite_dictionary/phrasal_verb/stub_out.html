to stub out

1. VERB

/ stʌb aʊt / (AmE), / stʌb aʊt / (BrE)
to extinguish (a cigarette, cigar, etc.) by pressing the burning end against a surface
extinguish by crushing (cigarette, etc.)

Examples

He reached down to stub out his cigarette before entering the building.
Please stub out your cigar; smoking isn’t allowed here.
She carefully stubbed out the cigarette in the ashtray.

Synset Examples

stub out your cigarette now
stub out your cigar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put out - to extinguish something, especially a fire or light
