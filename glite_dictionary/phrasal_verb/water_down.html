to water down

1. VERB

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə daʊn / (BrE)
to make something less strong, intense, or forceful, often by adding water or by diluting its content, effect, or meaning
make less strong or intense

Examples

The company decided to water down its safety regulations after pressure from investors.
She always waters down her orange juice to make it less sweet.
The movie's message was watered down to appeal to a wider audience.

Synset Examples

water down the mixture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilute - make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent
  • Synonym: to weaken - make less strong or powerful
  • Antonym: to strengthen - make or become stronger
