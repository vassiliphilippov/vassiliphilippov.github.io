to ooze through

1. VERB

/ uːz θruː / (AmE), / uːz θruː / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow or leak slowly and gradually through something.
flow or leak slowly through

Examples

Water began to ooze through the cracks in the dam.
A sticky resin oozed through the bark of the tree after it was cut.
The smell of fresh paint seemed to ooze through the closed door.

Synset Examples

Blood oozed through the bandage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seep through - to flow or leak slowly through something
  • Synonym: to permeate - to spread or pass through something, especially gradually
