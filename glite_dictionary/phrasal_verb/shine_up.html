to shine up

1. VERB

/ ʃaɪn ʌp / (AmE), / ʃaɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to try to make oneself liked by someone, often with insincere praise or behavior; to ingratiate oneself
ingratiate oneself (often insincerely)

Examples

He's just trying to shine up to the manager to get a promotion.
You won't get anywhere by constantly shining up to your teachers.
She spent the whole evening shining up to the new boss, hoping to impress him.

Synset Examples

She is playing up to the chairman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely, to gain favor
  • Synonym: to suck up - to try to win favor by insincere praise or flattery
  • Antonym: to offend - to cause displeasure or hurt feelings; the opposite of ingratiating oneself
