to pour out

1. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to express (feelings, thoughts, or emotions) freely and without restraint, especially in speech or writing
express emotions or thoughts freely

Examples

Whenever she feels overwhelmed, she pours out her feelings in her journal.
He finally poured out his heart to his best friend after years of silence.
During the meeting, members poured out their concerns about the company's future.

Synset Examples

The woman poured out her frustrations as the judge listened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vent - to express feelings, especially anger, freely
  • Antonym: to suppress - to prevent the expression of feelings or thoughts
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
(of people or things) to emerge or flow out in large numbers or amounts, usually in a continuous stream.
emerge or flow in large numbers

Examples

Hundreds of fans began to pour out of the stadium after the match ended.
As the bell rang, students poured out of their classrooms and filled the hallways.
When the doors finally opened, a crowd poured out into the bright sunlight.

Synset Examples

The crowds spilled out into the streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spill out - to come out in a rush or stream, often used for crowds or things overflowing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02074078-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to gradually pour a liquid, especially wine, in order to leave sediment behind
gradually pour to leave sediment behind

Examples

The waiter carefully poured out the aged red wine, making sure no sediment entered the glass.
She poured out the solution into a new container to separate it from the precipitate at the bottom.
When serving old port, it's important to pour out slowly to keep the sediment undisturbed.

Synset Examples

the sommelier decanted the wines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decant - to pour liquid from one container into another, especially to separate sediment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02074224-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to flow or cause to flow out in a large quantity or continuously, especially with a steady stream
flow or emit in large quantity

Examples

As the bottle tipped, water began to pour out onto the table.
After the storm, muddy water would pour out from the hillside.
He watched the crowd pour out of the stadium after the game ended.

Synset Examples

effused brine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flow - move along steadily and continuously
  • Degree Lower: dribble - flow or let flow in small drops
