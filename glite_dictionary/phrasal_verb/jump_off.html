to jump off

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɔf / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒf / (BrE)
to start quickly, especially with strong initial success
to start quickly or successfully

Examples

The new business jumped off with strong sales in its first month.
She jumped off to an early lead in the marathon.
The project jumped off smoothly thanks to careful planning.

Synset Examples

The freshman jumped off to a good start in his math class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get off to a good start - to begin well or successfully
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00347758-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɔf / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒf / (BrE)
to move quickly downward by leaping from a raised position or structure
leap down from a height

Examples

The firefighter had to jump off the burning balcony to escape the flames.
She watched as the children dared each other to jump off the high diving board.
He hesitated before finally deciding to jump off the old stone wall.

Synset Examples

the parachutist didn't want to jump
every year, hundreds of people jump off the Golden Gate bridge
the widow leapt into the funeral pyre

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap off - to jump down from a height

Synset POS: v
