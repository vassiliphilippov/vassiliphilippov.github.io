to soil oneself

1. VERB

/ sɔɪl wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / sɔɪl wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to involuntarily urinate or defecate in one's clothing, usually due to fear, shock, or extreme surprise
to accidentally urinate or defecate in clothing (from fear, etc.)
informal

Examples

The haunted house was so terrifying, I almost soiled myself.
He laughed so hard at the joke that he thought he might soil himself.
As the thunder crashed loudly, she feared she would soil herself from shock.

Synset Examples

The horror movie was so intense, I nearly soiled myself.
She was so surprised, she nearly soiled herself.
He was so scared that he almost soiled himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wet oneself - to involuntarily urinate in one's clothing
  • Synonym: mess oneself - to involuntarily defecate in one's clothing
