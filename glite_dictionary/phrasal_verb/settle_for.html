to settle for

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬l̩ fɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl fə / (BrE)
to accept or agree to something that is less than what was wanted or hoped for
accept less than desired

Examples

After weeks of searching, they finally settled for a less expensive hotel.
He didn't want to settle for an ordinary life and dreamed of doing something extraordinary.
She felt she shouldn't settle for less than she deserved in her career.

Synset Examples

She refused to settle for anything less than excellence.
He had to settle for a smaller apartment this year.
He refused to settle for second-rate products.
After a long day, I settled for peas and rice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accept - to take something that is offered
  • Synonym: compromise - to make concessions; to accept less than desired
