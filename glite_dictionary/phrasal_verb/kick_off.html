to kick off

1. VERB

/ kɪk ɑf / (AmE), / kɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to begin or start something officially or formally
begin officially; start

Examples

The ceremony will kick off at 9 a.m. sharp with a welcome speech.
The annual festival kicked off with a colorful parade through the city center.
Before we kick off the meeting, does anyone have any announcements?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start doing something
  • Antonym: to terminate - to bring to an end

Etymology

Originally from the action of starting a football game by kicking the ball from the center.

2. VERB

/ kɪk ɔf / (AmE), / kɪk ɒf / (BrE)
To start or begin an event, activity, or process.
begin; start an activity or event

Examples

We always kick off our meetings with a quick round of updates.
The campaign officially kicked off last weekend with a big celebration.

Synset Examples
