to laugh at

1. VERB

/ læf æt / (AmE), / lɑːf æt / (BrE)
to make fun of or ridicule someone or something in a mocking or scornful way
mock or ridicule someone or something

Examples

The children began to laugh at his strange hat during recess.
Nobody likes to be laughed at for making a simple mistake.
She tried not to laugh at his funny accent, but it was difficult.

Synset Examples

The satirists ridiculed the plans for a new opera house
The students poked fun at the inexperienced teacher
His former students roasted the professor at his 60th birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone or something, often by imitating them
  • Synonym: ridicule - to make someone or something look foolish by laughing at them
