to work away

1. VERB

/ wɜrk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɜːk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to continue working persistently or steadily, often for a long period of time
to continue working persistently

Examples

She continued to work away at her novel, adding a few pages every night.
The volunteers worked away for hours to clean the beach after the storm.
Despite the distractions, he worked away in silence until the report was finished.

Synset Examples

He’ll work away until he finishes the task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to keep trying in spite of difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop making an effort
