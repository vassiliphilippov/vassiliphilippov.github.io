to dial out

1. VERB

/ daɪl aʊt / (AmE), / daɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to disconnect oneself from a telephone, video, or online conversation or meeting, especially by ending participation remotely
leave a call or meeting

Examples

I may need to dial out of the call if something urgent comes up.
During the video conference, she quietly dialed out to take another meeting.
If you're done with the session, just dial out and we'll follow up later.

Synset Examples

She preferred to dial out when discussions got heated.
I need to dial out of this meeting early.
He decided to dial out of the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dial in - to join a telephone, video, or online conversation or meeting
  • Synonym: to log out - to sign out of a digital system or service
