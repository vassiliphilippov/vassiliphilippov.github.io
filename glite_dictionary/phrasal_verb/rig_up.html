to rig up

1. VERB

/ rɪɡ ʌp / (AmE), / rɪɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to assemble or construct something quickly, often as a temporary or makeshift solution
assemble or construct temporarily

Examples

We managed to rig up a tent using some rope and old sheets.
The engineers had to rig up a temporary bridge after the flood.
Can you rig up some lights for the stage before the show starts?

Synset Examples

Can he rig up a P.A. system?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set up - to arrange or assemble something for use
  • Antonym: to take down - to dismantle or disassemble something

Etymology

From 'rig' (to equip or set up), of uncertain origin, plus 'up.' Historically used in nautical contexts.
