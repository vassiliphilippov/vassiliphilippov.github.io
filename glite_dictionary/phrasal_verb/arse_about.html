to arse about

1. VERB

/ ɑrs əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ɑːs əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to waste time or behave in a lazy or idle way, often instead of doing something productive
behave lazily or waste time (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

Stop arsing about and help me tidy the house, will you?
He's been arsing about all afternoon instead of finishing his homework.
We can't afford to arse about if we want to catch the train.

Synset Examples

Her son is just bumming around all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess around - to behave in a silly or unproductive way
  • Antonym: work - to engage in productive activity or labor

Etymology

From British English "arse" (buttocks) + "about"; informal slang formation dating to the mid-20th century.
