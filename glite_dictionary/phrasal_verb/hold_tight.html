to hold tight

1. VERB

/ hoʊld taɪt / (AmE), / həʊld taɪt / (BrE)
to grip something firmly, usually with one's hands, especially for safety or support
grip something firmly

Examples

Before the roller coaster started, the operator told us to hold tight to the safety bar.
He had to hold tight to the railing during the storm to avoid falling.
When the child was scared, she held tight to her mother's hand.

Synset Examples

She clutched my arm when she got scared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to take and keep a firm hold of something
  • Antonym: to let go - to release a hold or stop holding something

2. VERB

/ hoʊld taɪt / (AmE), / həʊld taɪt / (BrE)
to grasp something firmly or securely, especially with the hands
grasp firmly or securely

Examples

Hold tight to the handrail as you step onto the moving walkway.
She told her son to hold tight to his backpack in the crowded station.
If the wind picks up, hold tight to your hat or it might blow away.

Synset Examples

As the roller coaster climbed, I told my friend to hold tight.
You should hold tight to the railing while climbing the stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to take and keep a firm hold of; to grasp tightly
