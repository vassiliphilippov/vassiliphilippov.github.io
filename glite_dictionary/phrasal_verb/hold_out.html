to hold out

1. VERB

/ hoʊld aʊt / (AmE), / həʊld aʊt / (BrE)
to extend or stretch something, especially a part of the body, forward or outward
extend or stretch out

Examples

She held out her hand to greet him.
The security guard held out his arm to stop the crowd.
Please hold out your ticket for scanning.

Synset Examples

He held out his hand
extend a hand
the bee exserted its sting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extend - to stretch out or make something longer or wider
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00027698-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hoʊld aʊt / (AmE), / həʊld aʊt / (BrE)
to refuse to give in or agree until one's demands are met; to persist in waiting for something better or more desirable
wait for something better; not compromise

Examples

Despite several offers, she decided to hold out for a higher salary.
He will hold out until he gets the apology he wants.
They held out for better terms, refusing to settle early.

Synset Examples

He held out for the dessert and did not touch the cheeses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold on - to wait or continue despite difficulties
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02647825-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hoʊld aʊt / (AmE), / həʊld aʊt / (BrE)
to last or remain in usable condition for a period of time, especially when supplies or resources are limited
to last or remain usable

Examples

The batteries won't hold out much longer in this flashlight.
Her old shoes held out through the entire hiking trip.
We hope our food supplies will hold out until help arrives.

Synset Examples

This dress wore well for almost ten years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last - to continue to function or be serviceable for a period of time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02710992-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ hoʊld aʊt / (AmE), / həʊld aʊt / (BrE)
to resist or refuse to yield to someone or something, especially for a period of time
resist; refuse to yield

Examples

The defenders managed to hold out against the siege for weeks.
She refused to hold out any longer and signed the agreement.
Although the team was tired, they continued to hold out until the final whistle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to give in - to finally agree to something you had opposed or resisted
  • Synonym: to withstand - to remain undamaged or unaffected by; to resist
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to authority
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01119003-v
Synset POS: v
