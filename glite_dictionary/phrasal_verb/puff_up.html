to puff up

1. VERB

/ pʌf ʌp / (AmE), / pʌf ʌp / (BrE)
to praise someone or something with exaggerated or excessive enthusiasm
praise extravagantly

Examples

The media tends to puff up celebrities for even small achievements.
Her boss would often puff up her contributions in meetings.
Critics puffed up the new play, calling it a masterpiece.

Synset Examples

The critics puffed up this Broadway production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overpraise - to praise excessively
  • Antonym: to belittle - to make something or someone seem unimportant (opposite)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01067294-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʌf ʌp / (AmE), / pʌf ʌp / (BrE)
to become filled with pride, arrogance, or anger, often showing this in one's appearance or behavior
become proud, arrogant, or angry

Examples

He tends to puff up when people compliment his work.
She puffed up with anger at the unfair accusation.
Whenever he talks about his promotion, he can't help but puff up.

Synset Examples

The mother was swelling with importance when she spoke of her son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell with pride - to become very proud, often visibly
  • Antonym: to deflate - to lose confidence or pride, often after being proud
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02602905-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pʌf ˈʌp / (AmE), / pʌf ˈʌp / (BrE)
to make something larger, inflated, or swollen, either physically or figuratively
make larger or swollen; inflate

Examples

She used a pump to puff up the air mattress for the guests.
The frog will puff up its body to appear larger when threatened.
Some companies puff up their sales figures to attract investors.

Synset Examples

The estimates were puffed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to fill something with air or gas so that it expands
  • Antonym: to deflate - to let air or gas out of something, making it smaller
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00265464-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ pʌf ʌp / (AmE), / pʌf ʌp / (BrE)
to become swollen or to cause something to become swollen; to expand in size or volume, often due to air, liquid, or inflammation
swell or cause to enlarge

Examples

Her face began to puff up after she got stung by a bee.
The bread will puff up in the oven as it bakes.
He tends to puff up his chest when he's feeling proud.

Synset Examples

Her faced puffed up from the drugs
puffed out chests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to swell up - to become larger and rounder, usually due to fluid or air
  • Antonym: to deflate - to reduce or release air or gas; to become smaller in size
Source: WordNet
