to come to a halt

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə ə hɔlt / (AmE), / kʌm tə ə hɒlt / (BrE)
to stop moving or progressing; to stop completely
stop moving or progressing

Examples

The parade came to a halt when it started to rain heavily.
Traffic came to a halt because of the accident on the highway.
Production comes to a halt every time there is a power outage.

Synset Examples

The tires screeched as the car came to a halt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to cease moving or operating
  • Degree Lower: pause - to interrupt movement or activity for a short period
