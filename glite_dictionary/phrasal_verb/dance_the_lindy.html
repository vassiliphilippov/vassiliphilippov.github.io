to dance the Lindy

1. VERB

/ dæns ðə ˈlɪndi / (AmE), / dɑːns ðə ˈlɪndi / (BrE)
to perform the Lindy Hop, a lively style of swing dance originating in the United States in the 1920s and 1930s
perform the Lindy Hop dance

Examples

They were excited to dance the Lindy for the first time at the festival.
She learned how to dance the Lindy from her grandparents.
At the retro-themed party, many couples took turns to dance the Lindy on the main floor.

Synset Examples

They danced the Lindy at the swing party last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lindy Hop - a lively swing dance originating in Harlem

Etymology

From 'Lindy Hop', named after aviator Charles Lindbergh's 1927 flight ('hop') and later abbreviated to 'the Lindy'.
