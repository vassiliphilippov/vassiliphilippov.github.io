to angle for

1. VERB

/ ˈæŋɡəl fɔr / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡəl fə / (BrE)
to try to obtain something indirectly, often by hinting or using subtle methods
try to obtain indirectly

Examples

During the meeting, he kept angling for a promotion by mentioning his achievements.
She was clearly angling for an invitation to the exclusive party.
Many politicians are adept at angling for support without openly asking for it.

Synset Examples

They angle for farthings during negotiations to get better terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish for - to try to get something, usually praise or information, indirectly

Etymology

From the verb 'angle' meaning 'to fish with a hook', metaphorically extended to 'fishing for' something indirectly.
