to cage in

1. VERB

/ keɪdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / keɪdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to confine something or someone in a cage or a space with barriers, restricting movement
confine in a cage or enclosed space

Examples

The zookeepers had to cage in the wild animal for everyone’s safety.
He felt as if life had caged in his dreams, leaving him powerless to escape.
The chickens were caged in to protect them from predators at night.

Synset Examples

The animal was caged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
