to jolly along

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑli əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒli əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to encourage someone or help them feel happier, often by being cheerful or supportive
encourage or cheer someone up

Examples

She did her best to jolly along the team after their defeat.
Sometimes it's necessary to jolly along reluctant students during a difficult lesson.
He managed to jolly along his colleagues with his infectious humor.

Synset Examples

She tried to cheer up the disappointed child when he failed to win the spelling bee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cheer up - to make someone happier or more cheerful

Etymology

From the adjective 'jolly' (cheerful, lively), of Middle English origin, plus 'along' meaning to accompany or go with.
