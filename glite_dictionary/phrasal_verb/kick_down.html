to kick down

1. VERB

/ kɪk daʊn / (AmE), / kɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to force something, especially a door, open by kicking it violently
open (a door) by kicking

Examples

The police had to kick down the door to rescue the family.
He threatened to kick down the gate if they wouldn't let him in.
It took three tries to finally kick down the old wooden door.

Synset Examples

kick in the doors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick in - to force open (a door) by kicking
