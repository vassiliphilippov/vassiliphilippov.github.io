to get behind

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to fail to keep up with others or with a schedule; to lag or fall behind
fall behind or lag behind

Examples

If you get behind on your assignments, it's hard to catch up.
She tends to get behind in her work when she's distracted.
Don't get behind on your bills, or you may face late fees.

Synset Examples

But in so many other areas we still are dragging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall behind - to fail to maintain the same progress as others
  • Antonym: to catch up - to reach the same level as others after falling behind
