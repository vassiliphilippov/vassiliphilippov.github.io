to block off

1. VERB

/ blɑk ɑf / (AmE), / blɒk ɒf / (BrE)
to close a road, path, or area so that people cannot enter or pass through it
close off a passage or area

Examples

The police had to block off the street after the accident.
Construction crews will block off Main Street for the festival this weekend.
They used barriers to block off the entrance to the building.

Synset Examples

block the way
barricade the streets
stop the busy road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barricade - block with a barrier to prevent passage
  • Antonym: to open up - make something accessible or allow passage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01480632-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ blɑk ɔf / (AmE), / blɒk ɒf / (BrE)
to close or obstruct (an entrance, path, or area) so that people cannot enter or use it
to close or obstruct access to something

Examples

The police decided to block off the street after the accident.
Workers will block off the main entrance while repairs are underway.
Protesters tried to block off the road to prevent traffic from passing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to close off - to seal or barricade an area so entry is not possible
  • Antonym: to open up - to make something accessible that was previously closed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01129387-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ blɑk ɔf / (AmE), / blɒk ɒf / (BrE)
to obstruct or close off an entrance, passage, or area, preventing people or things from passing through
obstruct or close a passage or area

Examples

The police blocked off the main street during the parade.
We had to block off one end of the hallway for repairs.
They blocked off the entrance to the building after the accident.

Synset Examples

We shut off the valve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut off - to prevent something from continuing or passing, especially the flow of something
  • Antonym: to open up - to make something accessible or remove an obstruction
