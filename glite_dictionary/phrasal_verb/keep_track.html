to keep track

1. VERB

/ kiːp træk / (AmE), / kiːp træk / (BrE)
to remain informed or aware of something; to monitor or record progress or details regularly
remain informed or aware of something

Examples

It's difficult to keep track of all my appointments without a calendar.
She uses an app to keep track of her expenses every month.
They kept track of their progress throughout the year.

Synset Examples

I keep track of the stock market developments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lose track - to fail to remain informed or aware of something
