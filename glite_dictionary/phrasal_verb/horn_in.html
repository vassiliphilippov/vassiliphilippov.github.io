to horn in

1. VERB

/ hɔrn ɪn / (AmE), / hɔːn ɪn / (BrE)
to intrude or interfere in a situation where one is not wanted or invited, especially in a meddlesome way
intrude or meddle without invitation

Examples

I wish you wouldn't always horn in on my conversations with my friends.
Several reporters tried to horn in on the meeting, but they were quickly asked to leave.
He resented it when his parents would horn in on decisions that should be his alone.

Synset Examples

This guy is always nosing around the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • Synonym: to butt in - to interrupt or interject oneself into a conversation or situation

Etymology

Possibly from the idea of an animal forcibly inserting its horns into something, meaning to push oneself in uninvited.
