to hop back in

1. VERB

/ hɑp bæk ɪn / (AmE), / hɒp bæk ɪn / (BrE)
to get back into a vehicle, room, or place quickly or energetically
get back into quickly (vehicle/place)

Examples

She realized she forgot her bag and hopped back in the car.
He waved goodbye and hopped back in the truck before the rain started.
After getting directions, they hopped back in and drove off quickly.

Synset Examples

After the party, we hopped back in the gadi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump back in - to re-enter quickly or energetically, often into a vehicle or activity
