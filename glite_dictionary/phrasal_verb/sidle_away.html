to sidle away

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdəl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdəl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move away from a place or person quietly and unobtrusively, often in a furtive or cautious manner
leave quietly or sneakily

Examples

When the teacher turned her back, Tom tried to sidle away from the group discussion.
She felt embarrassed and wanted to sidle away from the crowded room unnoticed.
As soon as the argument started, I saw him quietly sidling away towards the exit.

Synset Examples

They began to sidle away from the awkward conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip away - to leave quietly and unnoticed
  • Synonym: to sneak away - to leave stealthily or furtively

Etymology

From "sidle" (to move sideways), first attested in English in the 17th century, from 'side' + '-le' (frequentative suffix).
