to undergo a lobotomy

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈɡoʊ ə ləˈbɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɡəʊ ə ləˈbɒtəmi / (BrE)
to have a surgical operation in which nerve pathways in the brain are cut, typically to treat mental illness (now obsolete and rarely performed)
have a brain surgery to cut nerve pathways
medical

Examples

The patient had to undergo a lobotomy as a last resort for treatment.
In the mid-20th century, many people underwent a lobotomy for psychiatric conditions.
He feared having to undergo a lobotomy after his diagnosis.

Synset Examples

The film depicted a character undergoing a lobotomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobotomy - a surgical operation involving incision into the prefrontal lobe of the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'lobos' (lobe) and 'tome' (cutting); 'lobotomy' coined in 1930s.
