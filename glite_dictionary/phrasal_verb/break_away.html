to break away

1. VERB

/ breɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / breɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to suddenly escape from control, capture, or confinement; to flee quickly from someone or something
suddenly escape or flee

Examples

The horse managed to break away from its handler during the parade.
Several prisoners tried to break away from the guards but were quickly recaptured.
When the crowd became overwhelming, she decided to break away and head back home.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get free from a place where you have been kept as a prisoner or not allowed to leave
  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from danger or trouble

/ breɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / breɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave or withdraw from an organization, group, or association, especially to form a separate group

Examples

The club threatened to break away from the association over disagreements with leadership.
