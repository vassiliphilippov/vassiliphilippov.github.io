to draw away

1. VERB

/ drɔ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / drɔː əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move ahead of one's competitors in a race or competition
move ahead of competitors

Examples

As the final lap began, the runner managed to draw away from the rest of the field.
The team drew away in the second half, leaving their rivals far behind.
She started to draw away after the first mile, opening up a clear lead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull ahead - to move into the lead in a race or competition
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to lose position and be overtaken in a race or competition
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02003720-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ drɔː əˈweɪ / (AmE), / drɔː əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling it away from its place or position
remove by pulling away

Examples

She quickly drew away the curtain to let in some light.
He gently drew away the bandage to examine the wound.
The magician drew away the cloth to reveal a hidden object.

Synset Examples

She placed the tray down and drew off the cloth
draw away the cloth that is covering the cheese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - take something away from its place
  • Synonym: to draw off - remove by pulling away
