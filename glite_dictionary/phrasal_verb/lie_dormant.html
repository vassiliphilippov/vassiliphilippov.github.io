to lie dormant

1. VERB

/ laɪ ˈdɔrmənt / (AmE), / laɪ ˈdɔːmənt / (BrE)
to be inactive or not developing for a period of time, although capable of becoming active later
remain inactive or undeveloped

Examples

The volcano has lain dormant for centuries, but experts warn it could erupt again.
Her passion for painting lay dormant until she retired from her job.
After the merger, the company's plans for expansion remained dormant for several years.

Synset Examples

His work lay dormant for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inactive - not working or not doing anything
  • Antonym: to awaken - to become active or to wake up
