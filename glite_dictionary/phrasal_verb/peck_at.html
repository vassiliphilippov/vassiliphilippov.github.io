to peck at

1. VERB

/ pɛk ət / (AmE), / pɛk ət / (BrE)
to eat small amounts of food, often without enthusiasm or appetite
eat small amounts without enthusiasm

Examples

She only pecked at her dinner, barely touching her food.
During lunch, John would peck at his sandwich while reading emails.
The child pecked at the vegetables and asked for dessert instead.

Synset Examples

The anorexic girl just picks at her food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nibble - to eat in small bites
  • Antonym: to devour - to eat eagerly or in large amounts

Etymology

From the verb 'peck,' originally referring to a bird striking at something with its beak, + 'at.'
