to tie one's future to

1. VERB

/ taɪ wʌnz ˈfjuːtʃɚ tu / (AmE), / taɪ wʌnz ˈfjuːtʃə tuː / (BrE)
to commit one's future to a particular course of action, person, or decision
commit future to something

Examples

After much thought, she chose to tie her future to the family business.
When you tie your future to a single goal, you must prepare for the risks involved.
Many young entrepreneurs are willing to tie their future to innovative start-up companies.

Synset Examples

He decided to tie his future to this new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commit oneself to - to dedicate oneself to a course or decision
