to shack up

1. VERB

/ ʃæk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃæk ʌp / (BrE)
(of two people) to live together as a couple without being married, often implying an informal or casual arrangement.
live together as a couple, not married
informal

Examples

After college, they decided to shack up in a tiny apartment downtown.
Some people disapprove when couples shack up before marriage.
She had been shacking up with her boyfriend for a year before they got engaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cohabit - to live together and have a sexual relationship without being married

Etymology

From the American English slang 'shack' (small house) plus 'up', originally meaning to take shelter; evolved to mean cohabitation.

2. VERB

/ ʃæk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃæk ʌp / (BrE)
(of two people) to live together and have a sexual relationship, usually without being married.
to live together romantically without marriage
informal

Examples

After dating for a year, they decided to shack up in a small apartment downtown.
Some parents worry about their children choosing to shack up before marriage.
He wondered if it would be wise to shack up with his longtime partner.

Synset Examples

He was nervous about shack up with his girlfriend.
It's common for young couples to shack up together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cohabit - to live together as a couple, often without being married

Etymology

From 'shack' (a small, roughly built house) + 'up'; originally U.S. slang from early 20th century.
