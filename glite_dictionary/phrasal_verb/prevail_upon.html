to prevail upon

1. VERB

/ prɪˈveɪl əˈpɑn / (AmE), / prɪˈveɪl əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something that they are reluctant to do
persuade someone to do something

Examples

We finally managed to prevail upon our neighbor to lend us his lawnmower.
She tried to prevail upon her parents to let her travel alone.
The board members prevailed upon the director to reconsider his resignation.

Synset Examples

She managed to prevail upon him for a favor.
I had to prevail upon her to join us.
They tried to prevail upon him to change his mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persuade - to cause someone to do something through reasoning or argument

Etymology

From 'prevail', meaning 'to succeed', combined with 'upon' to form a phrase meaning 'to succeed in persuading'.
