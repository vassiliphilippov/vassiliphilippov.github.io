to distract from

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstrækt frʌm / (AmE), / dɪˈstrækt frɒm / (BrE)
to cause someone to stop giving their attention to something by drawing it away to something else
divert attention away from

Examples

Try not to let her constant questions distract from your concentration during the exam.
The loud music in the restaurant can distract from conversation at the table.
He worried that his flashy tie would distract from the importance of his message.

Synset Examples

Don't let that joker distract you from your work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to divert - to cause someone or something to change course or turn from one direction to another
  • Antonym: to focus on - to direct attention or effort toward something
