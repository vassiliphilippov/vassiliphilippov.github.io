to stand up for

1. VERB

/ stænd ʌp fɔr / (AmE), / stænd ʌp fə / (BrE)
to defend or support someone or something, especially when they are being attacked or criticized
defend or support someone or something

Examples

It's important to stand up for what you believe is right, even when it's difficult.
She was the only one brave enough to stand up for the new student.
If you don't stand up for yourself, people might take advantage of you.

Synset Examples

He stood up for his friend
She stuck up for the teacher who was accused of harassing the student

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defend - protect from harm or attack
  • Synonym: stick up for - informal: to defend or support someone
  • Antonym: to oppose - take action against; to resist or fight
