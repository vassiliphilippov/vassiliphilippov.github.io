to fall in

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to become part of a group or organization; to join or be incorporated into a collective or movement
join a group or organization

Examples

After some hesitation, he decided to fall in with the local hiking club.
Several young activists chose to fall in with the protest movement.
She was eager to fall in with the new team at her workplace.

Synset Examples

He joined the Communist Party as a young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to become a member of a group or organization

2. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of soldiers) to form a line or take one's position in a military formation as ordered.
join a line or formation (military)

Examples

The sergeant ordered the recruits to fall in for inspection.
As the bugle sounded, the soldiers quickly fell in along the parade ground.
Following the command, the entire platoon began falling in by the barracks.

Synset Examples

Troops fall in!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: form up - to arrange in a military formation
  • Antonym: to fall out - to leave a military formation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02439706-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of a structure or organization) to collapse inward or break down, either physically or metaphorically.
collapse inward or break down

Examples

The ceiling started to fall in after the heavy rainstorm.
Several companies in the industry may fall in if the economic crisis continues.
We had to evacuate because the roof threatened to fall in at any moment.

Synset Examples

The wall collapsed
The business collapsed
The dam broke
The roof collapsed
The wall gave in
The roof finally gave under the weight of the ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: to give way - to yield or break under pressure
  • Antonym: to hold up - to remain standing or intact
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01993067-v
Synset POS: v
