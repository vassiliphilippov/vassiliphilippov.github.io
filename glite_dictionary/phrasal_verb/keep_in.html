to keep in

1. VERB

/ kip ɪn / (AmE), / kiːp ɪn / (BrE)
to require someone to remain indoors or inside a place, often as a form of discipline or due to circumstances such as illness or bad weather.
make someone stay indoors or inside

Examples

The teacher decided to keep in the students who misbehaved during recess.
Heavy rain will keep in the children this afternoon.
Her mother had to keep in Sophie because she was feeling unwell.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to let out - to allow someone to go outdoors or leave a place
