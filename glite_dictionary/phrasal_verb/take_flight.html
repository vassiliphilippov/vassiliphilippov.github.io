to take flight

1. VERB

/ teɪk flaɪt / (AmE), / teɪk flaɪt / (BrE)
to begin flying or to escape quickly, especially in order to avoid danger or pursuit
begin to fly or escape quickly

Examples

The birds take flight at the slightest sound in the forest.
As soon as the alarm rang, the thieves took flight into the darkness.
Hundreds of butterflies take flight when the weather grows warm.

Synset Examples

He watched the planes take flight from the airport.
When the sun sets, the bats take flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Antonym: land - to come down and rest on the ground
