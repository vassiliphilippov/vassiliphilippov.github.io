to move it

1. VERB

/ ˈmuv ɪt / (AmE), / ˈmuːv ɪt / (BrE)
(informal) To hurry up; to move or act more quickly.
hurry up; act quickly
informal

Examples

You'll be late for school if you don't move it!
The coach yelled at the team to move it during practice.
Come on, move it or we'll miss the train!

Synset Examples

He shouted to move it before the rain starts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - to do something more quickly
  • Antonym: to slow down - to move or act more slowly
