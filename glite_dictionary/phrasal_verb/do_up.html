to do up

1. VERB

/ du ˈʌp / (AmE), / də ˈʌp / (BrE)
to wrap or decorate something, especially as a gift, to make it look attractive
wrap or decorate something attractively

Examples

She did up the birthday present with silver ribbon and a bow.
They did up the gift so beautifully that no one wanted to open it.
The homemade cookies were done up in festive wrapping for the holidays.

Synset Examples

The gift was done up in pretty red paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrap - to cover something completely with paper, cloth, etc.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01286214-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ du ˈʌp / (AmE), / duː ˈʌp / (BrE)
to make oneself look attractive or neat by careful attention to appearance, especially when dressing or applying makeup
make oneself look attractive by dressing or grooming

Examples

She decided to do up her hair and put on her best dress for the party.
It took him an hour to do up for the romantic dinner.
Before the graduation ceremony, the students all did up for group photos.

Synset Examples

She dolled herself up for the night out with her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or special clothes
