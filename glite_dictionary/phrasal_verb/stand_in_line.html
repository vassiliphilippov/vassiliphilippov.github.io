to stand in line

1. VERB

/ stænd ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / stænd ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
to wait in a queue for something; to form or join a line of people waiting for their turn
wait in a queue
american

Examples

We had to stand in line for over an hour before the movie started.
Every morning, students stand in line to enter the school building.
He dislikes having to stand in line at the bank during lunchtime.

Synset Examples

She stood in line for hours to get concert tickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - a line of people waiting for something
  • Synonym: wait in line - to wait in a queue (chiefly US)
