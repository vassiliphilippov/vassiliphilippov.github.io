to cry out

1. VERB

/ kraɪ aʊt / (AmE), / kraɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to utter a loud sound, typically in response to surprise, fear, pain, or strong emotion
shout loudly, usually in emotion

Examples

The crowd began to cry out in excitement as the winner was announced.
She cried out in fear when she heard a sudden noise in the dark.
If you ever need help, just cry out and someone will come.

Synset Examples

‘I won!’ he exclaimed
‘Help!’ she cried
‘I'm here,’ the mother shouted when she saw her child looking lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something loudly
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or quietly
