to pay off

1. VERB

/ peɪ ɔf / (AmE), / peɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to result in a benefit, advantage, or success, especially after effort or investment
to yield a positive result or benefit

Examples

Her dedication to her studies finally paid off when she graduated with honors.
All the hard work on the project paid off with a successful product launch.
If you practice regularly, your efforts will eventually pay off.

Synset Examples

His efforts finally paid off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
  • Antonym: to fail - to not achieve a desired result or benefit

2. VERB

/ peɪ ɔf / (AmE), / peɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to give someone money or another reward in return for work, help, or as compensation for something
give money or reward to someone in return

Examples

After a month of hard work, the company finally paid off its contractors.
They decided to pay off the witnesses to keep them from testifying.
The manager agreed to pay off the staff for their overtime hours.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compensate - to give someone something, typically money, in recognition of loss or work
