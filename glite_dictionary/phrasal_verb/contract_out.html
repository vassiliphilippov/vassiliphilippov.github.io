to contract out

1. VERB

/ kənˈtrækt aʊt / (AmE), / kənˈtrækt aʊt / (BrE)
to formally refuse to pay a union levy, especially one used for political purposes
refuse to pay union levy (political)
british

Examples

Union members have the right to contract out of paying the political levy.
She chose to contract out when she disagreed with the union's political activities.
Many employees are unaware that they can contract out of certain union payments.

2. VERB

/ kənˈtrækt aʊt / (AmE), / kənˈtrɑːkt aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange for work to be done by a person or company outside your own organization, rather than employing staff to do it
arrange work to be done externally

Examples

The company decided to contract out its cleaning services to a local firm.
Many organizations contract out IT support instead of hiring full-time employees.
They have contracted out the catering for the annual conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier
