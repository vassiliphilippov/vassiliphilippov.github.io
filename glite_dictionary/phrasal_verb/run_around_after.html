to run around after

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌn əˌraʊnd ˈæf.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌn əˌraʊnd ˈɑːf.tə / (BrE)
to follow, chase, or attend to someone or something energetically and often repeatedly, especially to take care of their needs or demands
chase or attend to energetically
informal

Examples

He was exhausted after running around after his children all morning.
I wish you’d stop running around after him; he should handle things himself.
She’s tired of running around after everyone at work and wants a break.

Synset Examples

Stop running around after me; I can handle it.
She’s always running around after her dog at the park.
He spent the whole afternoon running around after his kids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to pursue someone or something in order to catch them
  • Synonym: to look after - to take care of someone or something
