to log

1. VERB

/ lɑɡ / (AmE), / lɒɡ / (BrE)
to record observations, results, or information systematically, especially for future reference.
record observations or results systematically

Examples

Researchers must log each experiment's results in the official laboratory notebook.
Don't forget to log your findings before leaving the site for the day.
Inspectors are required to log every safety issue they discover during their rounds.

Synset Examples

Please log your findings in the shared document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to set down in writing for reference
  • Synonym: document - to create an official record of something

Etymology

From Middle English logge (record of a ship's progress), originally referring to a wooden float used to measure a ship's speed.
