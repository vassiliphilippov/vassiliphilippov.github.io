to fill the bill

1. VERB

/ fɪl ðə bɪl / (AmE), / fɪl ðə bɪl / (BrE)
to be suitable for a particular purpose or need; to be what is required or wanted
to be suitable or sufficient

Examples

That candidate seems to fill the bill for the job opening.
If you need a portable computer, this tablet fills the bill perfectly.
A simple black dress often fills the bill for many formal events.

Synset Examples

Does this restaurant fit the bill for the celebration?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fit the bill - to be suitable or exactly what is needed

Etymology

Originally American English, likely from theatrical terminology meaning to provide the acts needed to complete a playbill.
