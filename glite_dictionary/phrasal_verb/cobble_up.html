to cobble up

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːbəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkɒbəl ʌp / (BrE)
to make or put something together in a quick or rough way, often using whatever materials are available
put together quickly or roughly

Examples

We managed to cobble up a meal from the leftovers in the fridge.
The team had to cobble up a solution before the deadline.
She quickly cobbled up a costume for the play using spare fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation
  • Synonym: to assemble - to bring together or construct from parts

Etymology

From 'cobble' meaning to mend roughly, origin uncertain; phrasal form with up emphasizes completion.
