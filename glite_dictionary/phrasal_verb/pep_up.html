to pep up

1. VERB

/ pɛp ʌp / (AmE), / pɛp ʌp / (BrE)
to make something or someone more lively, energetic, or interesting
make livelier or more interesting

Examples

Let’s pep up the meeting with some music and snacks.
She tried to pep up the presentation by adding colorful slides.
They pepped up their outfits with some bright accessories.

Synset Examples

juice up a party
pep up your paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liven up - make more lively or energetic
  • Synonym: to invigorate - give strength or energy to
  • Antonym: dull - make less lively or interesting

Etymology

From 'pep' (from 'pepper', meaning energy or liveliness) plus 'up'.

2. VERB

/ pɛp ʌp / (AmE), / pɛp ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone or something more lively, energetic, or enthusiastic, especially by encouragement or cheers
make more lively or energetic

Examples

The coach tried to pep up the team with a motivational speech.
Music can really pep up a dull party.
She always knows how to pep up a boring conversation.

Synset Examples

The crowd cheered the demonstrating strikers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - to give support or confidence to someone
  • Synonym: to cheer up - to make someone feel happier
  • Antonym: to depress - to make someone feel sad or without energy

Etymology

From 'pep' (short for 'pepper' or 'peppery', meaning energy or liveliness) + 'up'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00860463-v
Synset POS: v
