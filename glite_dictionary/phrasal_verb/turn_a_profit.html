to turn a profit

1. VERB

/ tɝn ə ˈprɑːfɪt / (AmE), / tɜːn ə ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
to make a profit; to earn more money than is spent in operating a business or activity
make a profit

Examples

It took the new restaurant over a year to turn a profit.
Despite several setbacks, the startup managed to turn a profit last quarter.
Most small businesses struggle to turn a profit in their first year of operation.

Synset Examples

The company has not profited from the merger

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to break even - to have no profit or loss
