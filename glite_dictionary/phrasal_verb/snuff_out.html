to snuff out

1. VERB

/ snʌf aʊt / (AmE), / snʌf aʊt / (BrE)
to extinguish a flame, fire, or light; to cause something burning to stop burning.
extinguish a flame or light

Examples

She carefully snuffed out the candle before leaving the room.
Firefighters managed to snuff out the blaze after several hours of hard work.
He used his fingers to snuff out the match before tossing it away.

Synset Examples

Too big to be extinguished at once, the forest fires at best could be contained
quench the flames
snuff out the candles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extinguish - to cause (a fire or light) to stop burning or shining; synonym for snuff out

Etymology

From 'snuff' (to extinguish a candle flame) + 'out'.

2. VERB

/ snʌf aʊt / (AmE), / snʌf aʊt / (BrE)
to put an end to something; to kill a person or destroy something so that it no longer exists
put an end to; kill or extinguish

Examples

The dictator tried to snuff out any sign of opposition.
She devoted her life to organizations that seek to snuff out hunger and poverty.
A sudden heart attack snuffed out his promising career.

Synset Examples

The Nazis snuffed out the life of many Jewish children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extinguish - to put out a fire, light, or flame
  • Synonym: to wipe out - to destroy something completely

Etymology

From the action of extinguishing a candle's flame, with 'snuff' referring to removing the burnt wick.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00479148-v
Synset POS: v
