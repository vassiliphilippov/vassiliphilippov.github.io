to go in with

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɪn wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɪn wɪð / (BrE)
to join or collaborate with someone, especially for a shared project, plan, or investment
join or collaborate with someone

Examples

Several companies decided to go in with us to develop the new product.
We should go in with Mark if we want to win the contract.
Are you willing to go in with me on this investment?

Synset Examples

She chose to go in with the best team.
They plan to go in with a strong proposal.
I decided to go in with my friends on the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work jointly with others
  • Synonym: join - to become a member or part of something
