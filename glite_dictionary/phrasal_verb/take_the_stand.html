to take the stand

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə stænd / (AmE), / teɪk ðə stænd / (BrE)
to give testimony as a witness in a court of law
testify in court

Examples

The prosecutor called her to take the stand and recount what she had witnessed.
He was nervous as he prepared to take the stand in the high-profile trial.
Many witnesses refused to take the stand, fearing public scrutiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to testify - to make a formal statement of what one swears is true, especially in a court of law
