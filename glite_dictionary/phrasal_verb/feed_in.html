to feed in

1. VERB

/ fid ɪn / (AmE), / fiːd ɪn / (BrE)
to supply or introduce something continuously into a process, machine, or system
supply/introduce continuously

Examples

The operator must feed in the raw material slowly to avoid jams.
Once you feed in your details, the software will process your request automatically.
They fed in the data during the experiment for real-time analysis.

Synset Examples

feed carrots into a food processor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - to put something into something else
  • Antonym: remove - to take something out of something else
