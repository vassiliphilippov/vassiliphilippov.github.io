to hire a shill

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪɚ ə ʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə ə ʃɪl / (BrE)
to employ someone to give a false or biased promotion or endorsement, often in a deceptive or misleading manner
employ someone for deceptive promotion

Examples

The company decided to hire a shill to post fake reviews about their product.
Unscrupulous businesses sometimes hire a shill to sway public opinion online.
She refused to hire a shill, believing honest advertising was more effective.

Synset Examples

They hired a shill to promote their new game online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shill - a person who promotes products or ideas for personal gain, often deceitfully

Etymology

From 'hire' meaning to employ, and 'shill,' North American slang for a person paid to give credibility to an enterprise, possibly from 'shillaber', of uncertain origin.
