to sneap at

1. VERB

/ sniːp æt / (AmE), / sniːp æt / (BrE)
to sneer at or mock someone in a scornful manner
to sneer or mock someone
archaic

Examples

It is considered rude to sneap at others in public.
She would often sneap at her rivals with a sly remark.
The children were taught not to sneap at those less fortunate than themselves.

Synset Examples

It's not nice to sneap at people's misfortunes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful way
  • Synonym: taunt - to provoke or challenge in a mocking or insulting manner
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'snappen' (to snap, rebuke).
