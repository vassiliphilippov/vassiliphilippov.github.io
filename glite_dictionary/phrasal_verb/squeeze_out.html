to squeeze out

1. VERB

/ skwiz aʊt / (AmE), / skwiːz aʊt / (BrE)
to obtain or extract something with difficulty or effort
obtain with difficulty

Examples

It took hours to squeeze out the truth from the reluctant witness.
She managed to squeeze out a few more dollars from her tight budget.
After much questioning, he finally squeezed out the information needed for his report.

Synset Examples

He eked out some information from the archives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eke out - to get with great effort or in small amounts
  • Synonym: extract - remove or obtain something with effort
