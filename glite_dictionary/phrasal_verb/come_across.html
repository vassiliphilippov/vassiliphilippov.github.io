to come across

1. VERB

/ kʌm əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / kʌm əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to find or discover someone or something unexpectedly or by chance
find or discover by chance

Examples

While cleaning the attic, I came across a box of old family photos.
You never know what you might come across when you explore the city.
She came across an interesting article while browsing the internet.

Synset Examples

the archeologists chanced upon an old tomb
she struck a goldmine
The hikers finally struck the main path to the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stumble upon - to find something by accident
  • Synonym: to discover - to find something that was hidden or unknown
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to find or notice something

2. VERB

/ kʌm əˈkrɑs / (AmE), / kʌm əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to be perceived or make an impression in a particular way; to be understood or interpreted in a certain manner
be perceived or understood

Examples

His apology didn't come across as sincere to the team.
In her speech, the message came across very clearly.
Sometimes I worry that my emails come across as too abrupt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come off - to appear or be perceived in a particular manner

/ kʌm əˈkrɑs / (AmE), / kʌm əˈkrɒs / (BrE)

Examples
