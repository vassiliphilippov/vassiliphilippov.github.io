to rent out

1. VERB

/ rɛnt aʊt / (AmE), / rɛnt aʊt / (BrE)
to allow someone to use something, especially property, in exchange for payment for a period of time
to let property or services for a fee

Examples

We decided to rent out our spare room during the summer holidays.
The company plans to rent out office space to small businesses.
They rented out their beach house last year to help cover expenses.

Synset Examples

We rent out our apartment to tourists every year
He hired himself out as a cook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - to grant use or occupation of property under a lease
  • Synonym: let - to allow someone to use your property in return for payment (British usage)

2. VERB

/ rɛnt aʊt / (AmE), / rɛnt aʊt / (BrE)
to allow someone to use property or land in exchange for regular payment; to lease or let property to another person
lease or let property to another

Examples

They decided to rent out their spare room for extra income.
The company plans to rent out several apartments to tourists during the summer.
She rents out her parking space while she is on vacation.

Synset Examples

They decided to rent out their spare room on Airbnb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - to grant the use of property by contract for a specified time in exchange for payment
  • Synonym: let - to allow someone to use property in exchange for rent

WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
