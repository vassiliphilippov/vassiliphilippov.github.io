to check off

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk ɔf / (AmE), / tʃɛk ɒf / (BrE)
to mark an item on a list to show it has been dealt with or completed, often by placing a check mark beside it
mark as completed on a list

Examples

As I packed my bag, I checked off each item on the list.
Please remember to check off all completed tasks by the end of the day.
She carefully checked off the students' names as they entered the classroom.

Synset Examples

Please check each name on the list
tick off the items
mark off the units

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tick off - to mark an item on a list as done, often used in British English
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00664100-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʃɛk ɔf / (AmE), / tʃɛk ɒf / (BrE)
to mark an item on a list to show that it has been completed, dealt with, or verified
mark as completed on a list

Examples

She checked off each task as she finished it.
Please check off the boxes next to the items you have packed.
He quickly checked off all the names on the attendance sheet.

Synset Examples

She checked off items on her shopping list as she went.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tick off - mark as completed or noted on a list (British English)
