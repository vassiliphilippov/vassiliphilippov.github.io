to hang in

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ˈɪn / (AmE), / hæŋ ˈɪn / (BrE)
to keep going or remain persistent, especially during difficult times; refuse to give up
persist, refuse to give up
informal

Examples

Even when the situation seemed hopeless, Maria decided to hang in and keep trying.
You just need to hang in until things get better at work.
It's important to hang in, especially when life gets tough.

Synset Examples

he persisted to call me every night
The child persisted and kept asking questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying; to quit

2. VERB

/ hæŋ ɪn / (AmE), / hæŋ ɪn / (BrE)
to persist or remain steady and committed, especially when facing difficulty or adversity
persist or remain steady
informal

Examples

Even when things get tough, it's important to hang in and not give up.
He encouraged his friend to hang in until the end of the marathon.
Just hang in—the situation will improve soon.

Synset Examples

You need to hang in for just a few more hours.
She decided to hang in despite the challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to continue in a course of action in spite of difficulty
