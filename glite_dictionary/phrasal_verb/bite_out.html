to bite out

1. VERB

/ baɪt aʊt / (AmE), / baɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to say something abruptly or harshly, often with anger or irritation
say harshly or abruptly

Examples

"Don't touch that!" she bit out angrily, startling everyone in the room.
He bit out a harsh order, making his frustration clear to the team.
When asked about the delay, the manager bit out a terse explanation and walked away.

Synset Examples

She bit out a curse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - to say something quickly and angrily
