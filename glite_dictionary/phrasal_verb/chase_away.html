to chase away

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tʃeɪs əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave, often suddenly or by pursuing them, used both literally and figuratively
force to leave; drive away

Examples

The loud noise quickly chased away the birds from the garden.
She tried to chase away her worries by focusing on her work.
Security guards chased away the vandals before any damage was done.

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drive away - to compel someone or something to go away
  • Antonym: to attract - to draw someone or something toward oneself
