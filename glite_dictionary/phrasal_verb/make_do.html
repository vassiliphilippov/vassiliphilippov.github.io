to make do

1. VERB

/ meɪk duː / (AmE), / meɪk dʊ / (BrE)
to manage with what is available, despite being limited or not ideal
manage with limited resources

Examples

We didn't have any candles, so we had to make do with a flashlight.
They were out of milk, but she managed to make do with water in her recipe.
He couldn't afford a new coat, so he decided to make do with his old one for another winter.

Synset Examples

We got by on just a gallon of gas
They made do on half a loaf of bread every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get by - to manage to survive or accomplish despite difficulties

2. VERB

/ meɪk duː / (AmE), / meɪk duː / (BrE)
to manage or cope with what is available, especially when resources are limited
cope with limited resources

Examples

During the power outage, we had to make do with candles for light.
Even though she didn’t have all the ingredients, she managed to make do and cook a meal.
After losing his job, he learned to make do with much less money than before.

Synset Examples

We'll have to make do with what we have.
In tough times, we learn to make do.
They managed to make do during the shortage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get by - to manage with difficulty, especially financially
  • Synonym: to manage - to cope or succeed despite difficulties
