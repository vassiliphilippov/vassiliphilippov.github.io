to ginger up

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɪndʒər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒə ʌp / (BrE)
to make something more lively, interesting, or exciting
make more lively or interesting

Examples

We tried to ginger up the meeting with some music and snacks.
They hired a new designer to ginger up the company's website.
The teacher used creative stories to ginger up the lesson and engage the students.

Synset Examples

juice up a party
pep up your paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pep up - to make more lively or energetic
  • Synonym: to spice up - to make something more interesting or exciting

Etymology

From 'ginger' (the spice), used figuratively to mean stimulate or enliven; phrase dates back to 19th century Britain.
