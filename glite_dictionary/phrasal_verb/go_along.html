to go along

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to continue in a particular state, activity, or condition
continue; carry on doing something

Examples

As the meeting went along, people became more comfortable sharing their ideas.
She decided to go along with her studies, even when it got challenging.
The day went along quietly as everyone worked on their own projects.

Synset Examples

Keep on working!
We continued to work into the night
Keep smiling
We went on working until well past midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to keep happening, existing, or doing something without stopping
  • Antonym: stop - to cease from doing something; to come to an end

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to cooperate with or pretend to cooperate with someone or something, especially temporarily or to avoid conflict
cooperate with or pretend to cooperate

Examples

Sometimes you have to go along with the plan, even if you disagree with it.
She decided to go along with his story until she could find out the truth.
I wasn't sure about the idea, but I went along to keep the peace.

Synset Examples

He decided to play along with the burglars for the moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play along - pretend to cooperate or cooperate for a time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02422308-v
Synset POS: v
