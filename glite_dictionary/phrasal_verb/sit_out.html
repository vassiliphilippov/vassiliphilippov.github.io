to sit out

1. VERB

/ sɪt aʊt / (AmE), / sɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to remain in a place or situation until it is finished, especially something unpleasant or boring; to endure until the end.
endure until something is over

Examples

She decided to sit out the storm in her small cottage.
Even though the meeting was tedious, he managed to sit out the entire discussion.
Many fans chose to sit out the match due to the heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick it out - to endure a difficult situation until it ends
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00672097-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɪt aʊt / (AmE), / sɪt aʊt / (BrE)
To not take part in an activity, such as a dance, game, or event, often by choice or necessity.
not participate in an activity

Examples

She decided to sit out the last few dances because she was tired.
After his injury, Mark had to sit out the entire basketball season.
Several team members chose to sit out the protest for personal reasons.

Synset Examples

He sat out the game

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to participate - to take part in an activity or event
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02732418-v
Synset POS: v
