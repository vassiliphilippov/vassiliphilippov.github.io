to tower over

1. VERB

/ ˈtaʊɚ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaʊə ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to be much taller, larger, or more impressive than someone or something, often in a way that makes them seem less important or significant
be much taller or more dominant than

Examples

The new skyscraper towers over all the other buildings in the city center.
At six foot five, he towers over most of his colleagues.
The historical castle towers over the small village nestled below its walls.

Synset Examples

The mountains tower over the valley like giants.
She felt he towered over her in every way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overshadow - to appear more important or noticeable than someone or something else
