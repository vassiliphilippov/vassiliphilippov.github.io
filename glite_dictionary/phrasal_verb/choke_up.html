to choke up

1. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to block or obstruct a passage, pipe, or opening, especially causing something to stop moving through it
block or obstruct a passage

Examples

Leaves and debris can easily choke up the gutters if not cleaned regularly.
Heavy traffic may choke up the city streets during rush hour.
The narrow pipes tend to choke up with mineral deposits over time.

Synset Examples

My nose is all stuffed
Her arteries are blocked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clog - to block or become blocked with a thick or sticky substance
  • Antonym: to unblock - to remove an obstruction from something

2. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ʌp / (BrE)
To become so emotional that it is difficult to speak.
become unable to speak from emotion

Examples

She began to choke up as she spoke about her late husband.
Many people choke up during weddings or graduation ceremonies.
He tried not to choke up while delivering the farewell speech.

Synset Examples

It's normal to choke up when speaking about loss.
She began to choke up when remembering her childhood.
He choked up during the emotional movie scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break up - to become emotionally overwhelmed and start crying
  • Antonym: compose oneself - to regain self-control, especially after being emotional
