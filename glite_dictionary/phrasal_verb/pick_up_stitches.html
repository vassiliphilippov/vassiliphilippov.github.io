to pick up stitches

1. VERB

/ pɪk ʌp ˈstɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp ˈstɪtʃɪz / (BrE)
to insert the knitting needle into previously dropped or unfinished stitches to reconnect or repair knitting work
repair knitting by reattaching stitches

Examples

After dropping a few loops, she skillfully picked up stitches to restore her knitting.
If you pick up stitches too tightly, the repaired area may pucker.
He practiced how to pick up stitches along the edge of his scarf.

Synset Examples

She had to pick up stitches after dropping her knitting.
If you lose a stitch, you must pick up stitches carefully.
He learned to pick up stitches to fix his sweater.
