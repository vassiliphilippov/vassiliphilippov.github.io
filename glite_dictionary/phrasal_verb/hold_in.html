to hold in

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ɪn / (BrE)
to restrain or suppress (an emotion, expression, or reaction) so that it is not shown or perceived by others
restrain or suppress an emotion or reaction

Examples

He tried to hold in his laughter during the serious ceremony.
Sometimes it's unhealthy to hold in your feelings for too long.
She couldn't hold in her excitement when she saw the surprise.

Synset Examples

She conceals her anger well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to the expression of a feeling or reaction
  • Antonym: to let out - to allow (an emotion or reaction) to be expressed

2. VERB

/ hoʊld ɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ɪn / (BrE)
to restrain or control an emotion, reaction, or urge so that it is not expressed or released
restrain or control emotions or urges

Examples

She tried to hold in her laughter during the serious meeting.
Sometimes it's better to express your feelings than to hold in your anger.
He couldn't hold in his excitement when he heard the news.

Synset Examples

moderate your alcohol intake
hold your tongue
hold your temper
control your anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to hold back from action; to keep under control
  • Antonym: to let out - to release or express something that is held inside

3. VERB

/ hoʊld ɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ɪn / (BrE)
to prevent (something, especially an emotion or bodily function) from being expressed or released; to restrain or suppress
restrain or suppress; prevent expression or release

Examples

She struggled to hold in her laughter during the serious conversation.
It's unhealthy to constantly hold in your feelings instead of talking about them.
He managed to hold in his anger until the meeting was over.

Synset Examples

He tried to hold in the fart during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to prevent something from being expressed or known
  • Antonym: release - to allow something to be expressed or come out
