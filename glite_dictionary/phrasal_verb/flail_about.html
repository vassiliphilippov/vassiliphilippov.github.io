to flail about

1. VERB

/ fleɪl əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / fleɪl əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to move or act wildly or in a disorganized or ineffective way
move or act wildly and ineffectively

Examples

The panicked swimmer began to flail about in the water, desperate for rescue.
During the argument, he would just flail about without making any clear point.
Instead of making progress, the team continued to flail about with different unsuccessful strategies.

Synset Examples

When he lost the game, he started to flail about in frustration.
Instead of focusing, he started to flail about with his ideas.
She began to flail about in the water, struggling to stay afloat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thrash about - to move the body violently in an uncontrolled way
