to toss in with

1. VERB

/ tɑs ɪn wɪð / (AmE), / tɒs ɪn wɪð / (BrE)
to join or associate oneself with a group or cause, often by deciding to take part or support it
join or associate with a group

Examples

After much debate, she decided to toss in with the environmental campaign.
Several local businesses chose to toss in with the charity event this year.
If you toss in with us, you'll be part of an exciting adventure.

Synset Examples

He decided to toss in with the new project team.
They chose to toss in with us for the trip.
I might toss in with the group if they invite me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to become a member or part of something
  • Antonym: leave - to depart from or stop participating in a group
