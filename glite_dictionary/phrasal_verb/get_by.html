to get by

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
to manage to survive or accomplish something with the limited resources or means available
manage or survive with limited means

Examples

We barely get by on my salary, but we manage to pay the bills each month.
During the lean years, she had to get by with simple meals and no luxuries.
Even with little training, he managed to get by in his new job.

Synset Examples

We got by on just a gallon of gas
They made do on half a loaf of bread every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make do - to manage with what is available, often when resources are lacking
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593624-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
to manage to avoid being caught or suffer the consequences of something, especially something improper or forbidden
manage to avoid consequences

Examples

Some students try to get by without doing much work, but it usually catches up with them.
He was surprised he could get by with such a flimsy excuse.
She wondered if she would get by after breaking the rules.

Synset Examples

She gets away with murder!
I couldn't get out from under these responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get away with - to escape blame or punishment for an act
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00812391-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
to pass or move in front of someone or something, especially in a competitive or physical context.
pass or move in front of

Examples

He quickly got by the defenders to score the winning goal.
Excuse me, could I get by you to reach the exit?
During the marathon, she got by several runners in the final mile.

Synset Examples

Bride's Biscuit got by the other dogs to win the race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overtake - pass someone or something ahead, especially in a race or competition
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02053912-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
to manage to survive or continue to exist, usually with limited resources or in difficult circumstances
manage to survive with difficulty

Examples

During the recession, many families had to get by on reduced incomes.
She doesn't earn much, but she can get by with what she has.
When studying abroad, I managed to get by with only basic knowledge of the language.

Synset Examples

I can get by on a small budget.
They manage to get by without much help.
Sometimes you just need to get by day to day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manage - to succeed in doing something, especially something difficult
  • Synonym: cope - to deal effectively with something difficult
  • Antonym: to thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful (often in contrast to merely surviving)
