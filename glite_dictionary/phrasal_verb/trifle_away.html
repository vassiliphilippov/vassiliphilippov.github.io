to trifle away

1. VERB

/ ˈtraɪ.fəl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.fəl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to waste (time, money, or opportunities) in a careless or unimportant way
waste time, money, or chances carelessly

Examples

He managed to trifle away his entire inheritance on useless gadgets and parties.
Don't trifle away your free time when you could be learning something new.
She regretted having trifled away so many opportunities in her youth.

Synset Examples

wanton one's money away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - to use or expend carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose
  • Synonym: to squander - to waste (something, especially money or time) in a reckless manner
  • Antonym: conserve - to protect from harm or destruction; to use sparingly

Etymology

From the verb 'trifle' (to treat without seriousness) + away; 'trifle' from Old French 'trufler' (to mock).
