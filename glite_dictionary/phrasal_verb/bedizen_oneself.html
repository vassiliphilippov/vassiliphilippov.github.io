to bedizen oneself

1. VERB

/ bɪˈdaɪzən wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / bɪˈdʌɪz(ə)n wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to dress oneself in bright, showy, or tasteless clothing or ornaments
dress in showy or tasteless clothing

Examples

She loves to bedizen herself in glittering jewelry for every celebration.
The performers bedizened themselves in feathers and sequins for the parade.
He would bedizen himself with colorful scarves and elaborate hats to attract attention.

Synset Examples

She decided to bedizen herself for the party.
They bedizen themselves in extravagant costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deck oneself out - to dress up or decorate oneself in a striking way
  • Synonym: adorn oneself - to decorate or add beauty to oneself

Etymology

From 'bedizen', probably an altered form of 'decent', meaning to dress, with 'be-' as a prefix.
