to toss off

1. VERB

/ tɑs ɑf / (AmE), / tɒs ɒf / (BrE)
to drink something quickly and in one go, especially an alcoholic beverage
drink something quickly, especially alcohol

Examples

He grabbed his glass and tossed off the whisky in one swallow.
She could toss off a pint faster than anyone else at the pub.
Before heading out, they tossed off a round of shots together.

Synset Examples

He downed three martinis before dinner
She killed a bottle of brandy that night
They popped a few beers after work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down - to drink something quickly or in one movement
  • Synonym: knock back - to drink something, especially alcohol, quickly
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɑs ɔf / (AmE), / tɒs ɒf / (BrE)
to write or produce something quickly and with little effort
write or produce quickly

Examples

She managed to toss off a reply to the email before the meeting started.
He can toss off an article in under an hour if needed.
The composer would often toss off a melody in just a few minutes.

Synset Examples

She dashed off a note to her husband saying she would not be home for supper
He scratched off a thank-you note to the hostess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dash off - to write or do something quickly
  • Synonym: to bang out - to produce something quickly and often carelessly
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
