to allow for

1. VERB

/ əˈlaʊ fər / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ fə / (BrE)
to consider or include something when making plans or calculations; to make provision for something as a possibility
to make provision for; take into account

Examples

When planning the event, be sure to allow for possible delays.
The architect allowed for extra space in case the building needed expanding.
We always allow for some unexpected expenses in our budget.

Synset Examples

This leaves no room for improvement
The evidence allows only one conclusion
allow for mistakes
leave lots of time for the trip
This procedure provides for lots of leeway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take into account - to consider something as part of a total situation
  • Antonym: discount - to disregard or leave out of consideration
