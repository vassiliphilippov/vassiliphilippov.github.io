to keep down

1. VERB

/ kip daʊn / (AmE), / kiːp daʊn / (BrE)
to prevent something or someone from rising, growing, or becoming successful, especially by using force or authority
suppress or control by force

Examples

The regime used violence to keep down any opposition.
Strict rules were imposed to keep down the workers' demands.
The military tried to keep down protests by deploying troops in the city.

Synset Examples

The government quashes any attempt of an uprising
China keeps down her dissidents very efficiently
The rich landowners subjugated the peasants working the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - forcibly put an end to something
  • Synonym: to subdue - bring under control, especially by force
  • Antonym: to encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to (opposite sense)

2. VERB

/ kip daʊn / (AmE), / kiːp daʊn / (BrE)
to prevent the number or amount of something from increasing; to limit or control
limit the number or amount

Examples

Strict laws were introduced to keep down the number of accidents on the road.
They are trying to keep down costs as much as possible this year.
Extra police were hired to keep down violence at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - to restrict the size, amount, or number of something
  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount or degree
  • Antonym: increase - to become larger in number or amount
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kip daʊn / (AmE), / kiːp daʊn / (BrE)
to manage not to vomit food or drink after eating or drinking; to not throw up
manage not to vomit

Examples

After the operation, she was finally able to keep down solid food.
He felt nauseous all day and couldn't keep down anything he ate.
It's important to try to keep down fluids if you have a stomach virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw up - to vomit
