to work off

1. VERB

/ wɝk ɑf / (AmE), / wɜːk ɒf / (BrE)
to get rid of something, especially an unwanted feeling, debt, or excess weight, by physical activity or effort
get rid of by working or effort

Examples

After the holidays, I need to work off the weight I gained from all the food.
She finally worked off her student loan after years of payments.
They encouraged him to work off his stress by going for a run.

Synset Examples

work off the extra pounds you have gained over the holidays
we must work off the debt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pay off - to repay in full (especially a debt)
  • Synonym: to burn off - to get rid of calories or fat through exercise
