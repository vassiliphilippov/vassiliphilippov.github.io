to smooth over

1. VERB

/ smuð ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / smuːð ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to try to make a problem or disagreement seem less serious or to deal with it superficially, often without resolving the underlying issue
make a problem seem less serious

Examples

She tried to smooth over the argument by changing the subject.
The manager attempted to smooth over the team's mistakes after the loss.
He always smooths over any tension at family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gloss over - to treat something, especially a problem, as if it were not important
