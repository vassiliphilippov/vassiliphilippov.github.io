to erupt into riot

1. VERB

/ ɪˈrʌpt ˌɪntu ˈraɪət / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt ˌɪntuː ˈraɪət / (BrE)
to suddenly become violent or chaotic, especially referring to a crowd or group starting a riot
to suddenly become a riot

Examples

After the verdict, the protest erupted into riot as anger grew among the crowd.
The peaceful demonstration unexpectedly erupted into riot when tensions escalated.
Authorities acted quickly when the soccer fans began to erupt into riot at the stadium.

Synset Examples

The crowd erupted into riot after the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break out - to start suddenly, especially violence or conflict
  • Antonym: to subside - to become less intense or stop, opposite of erupt
