to outgrow one's fear

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈɡroʊ wʌnz fɪr / (AmE), / aʊtˈɡrəʊ wʌnz fɪə / (BrE)
to become mature enough no longer to be affected by a particular fear or anxiety
stop being affected by a fear as one matures

Examples

As she got older, she managed to outgrow her fear of the dark.
Many children eventually outgrow their fear of speaking in front of a class.
With therapy, Jack was able to outgrow his fear of dogs.

Synset Examples

Many students outgrow their fear of public speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to successfully deal with or control a problem or feeling

Etymology

From the verb 'outgrow' (to leave behind as one grows), combining 'out-' (beyond) and 'grow'.
