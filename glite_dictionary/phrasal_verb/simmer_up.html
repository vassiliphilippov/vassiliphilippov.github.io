to simmer up

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪm.ɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.ə ʌp / (BrE)
to reach a gentle boiling point or cook a liquid gently at a low temperature, just below boiling
to gently bring to a simmer

Examples

Let the sauce simmer up on low heat until it thickens.
She waited for the soup to simmer up before adding the spices.
The chef told us to simmer up the broth slowly for the best flavor.

Synset Examples

Let the water simmer up before adding the pasta.
He watched the pot until it began to simmer up.
Make sure to simmer up the broth for rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boil - to cook or be cooked by being immersed in boiling water or other liquid
  • Synonym: simmer - to cook gently just below boiling point
