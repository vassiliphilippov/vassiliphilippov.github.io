to log up

1. VERB

/ lɑɡ ʌp / (AmE), / lɒɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to record or accumulate a particular amount of something, especially distance or time travelled, in a log or record
record or accumulate (distance, time, etc.) in a log

Examples

The pilot has logged up over 5,000 flying hours.
We logged up 300 miles during our road trip.
He hopes to log up more time on the new simulator this month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect a quantity over time
  • Synonym: record - to keep a written account of something
