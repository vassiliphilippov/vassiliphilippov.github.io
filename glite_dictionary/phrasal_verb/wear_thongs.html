to wear thongs

1. VERB

/ wɛɹ θɔŋz / (AmE), / wɛə θɒŋz / (BrE)
(Australian) To wear flip-flop sandals on one's feet.
wear flip-flop sandals (Australian)
british

Examples

Every summer, Australians love to wear thongs to the beach.
At the barbecue, nearly everyone was wearing thongs despite the rocky ground.
She forgot she was wearing thongs and stepped right into a puddle.

Synset Examples

That guy is such a bogan; he always wears thongs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wear flip-flops - to wear sandals made of rubber with a thong between the toes
  • Degree Higher: wear shoes - to have shoes on one's feet

Etymology

Thong derives from Old English 'þwang' meaning a narrow strip of leather; Australian plural 'thongs' refers to flip-flop sandals.
