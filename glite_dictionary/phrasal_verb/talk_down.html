to talk down

1. VERB

/ tɑk daʊn / (AmE), / tɔːk daʊn / (BrE)
to speak to someone in a way that shows you think they are less intelligent or important than you are
speak condescendingly to someone

Examples

Please don't talk down to me—I understand perfectly well.
Her manager tends to talk down to junior staff during meetings.
He realized he was starting to talk down to his students and quickly changed his approach.

Synset Examples

He talks down to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat someone with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Synonym: to belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00965047-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɑk daʊn / (AmE), / tɔːk daʊn / (BrE)
to direct and control the landing of an aircraft by giving instructions via radio communication
guide an aircraft to land by radio

Examples

The air traffic controller expertly talked down the small private jet during low visibility conditions.
When the pilot lost consciousness, the tower staff had to talk down the plane safely.
It requires calm communication skills to talk down a troubled aircraft in an emergency.

Synset Examples

the control tower talked down the plane whose pilot fell ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to bring an aircraft down safely on the ground
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00751590-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tɔk daʊn / (AmE), / tɔːk daʊn / (BrE)
to speak to someone as if they are less intelligent or important; to belittle someone through conversation or remarks
belittle someone through speech

Examples

You shouldn’t talk down to your colleagues just because you have more experience.
She felt frustrated when he tried to talk down her ideas during the meeting.
It’s unkind to constantly talk down to children as if they can’t understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - make someone or something seem unimportant
  • Synonym: to patronize - treat with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
