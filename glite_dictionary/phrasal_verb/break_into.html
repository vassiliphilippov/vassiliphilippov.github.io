to break into

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / breɪk ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to suddenly begin to express or perform something, such as talking, singing, or crying
suddenly start expressing or doing something

Examples

She suddenly broke into laughter during the meeting, startling everyone in the room.
The little boy broke into tears when he saw his lost puppy.
During the ceremony, the choir broke into song, filling the hall with music.

Synset Examples

break into a yodel
break into a song
break into tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst into - to begin suddenly, especially expressing an emotion or activity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00988449-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ breɪk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / breɪk ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to suddenly begin doing something, especially to start moving or behaving in a different way (such as starting a new pace or activity)
suddenly begin an activity or new pace

Examples

As the music changed, the dancers broke into a fast waltz.
Without warning, the dog broke into a run across the park.
When the director arrived, the actors broke into rehearsal immediately.

Synset Examples

The dancers broke into a cha-cha
The horse broke into a gallop
