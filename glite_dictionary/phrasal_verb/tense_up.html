to tense up

1. VERB

/ tɛns ʌp / (AmE), / tɛns ʌp / (BrE)
to become tense, nervous, or uneasy, often as a result of stress or anticipation
become tense or nervous

Examples

Whenever she speaks in public, she tends to tense up and forget her lines.
I could feel my shoulders tense up as the deadline approached.
He tensed up the moment he heard the unexpected noise.

Synset Examples

He tensed up when he saw his opponent enter the room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00026146-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɛns ʌp / (AmE), / tɛns ʌp / (BrE)
to become or make someone become nervous, anxious, or unable to relax
become nervous or anxious

Examples

She always tenses up before public speaking.
I could feel my shoulders tense up as the exam started.
He tensed up when he heard the door creak open at night.

Synset Examples

he got a phone call from his lawyer that tensed him up

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to relax - to become less tense or anxious
