to deviate from the plan

1. VERB

/ ˈdiːvieɪt frəm ðə plæn / (AmE), / ˈdiːvieɪt frəm ðə plan / (BrE)
to depart from or fail to follow the established plan or intended course of action
to depart from a planned course

Examples

We can't afford to deviate from the plan at this crucial stage of the project.
If problems arise, consult your manager before you deviate from the plan.
The company failed because its leaders constantly deviated from the plan without considering the consequences.

Synset Examples

He tends to deviate from the main topic.
Don't deviate from the plan we've set.
It's important not to deviate in this project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to stick to the plan - to continue following the established plan

Etymology

From Latin 'deviatus', past participle of 'deviare' (to turn aside), plus English phrase 'from the plan'.
