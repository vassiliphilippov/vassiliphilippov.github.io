to die out

1. VERB

/ daɪ aʊt / (AmE), / daɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to become extinct; to cease to exist completely, especially gradually over time
become extinct or disappear

Examples

Polar bears could die out if climate change continues unchecked.
Some cultural traditions have died out over the centuries.
Scientists warn that the rare butterfly may die out without urgent conservation efforts.

Synset Examples

Dinosaurs died out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to become extinct - to cease to exist as a species or group
  • Antonym: to survive - to continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship

2. VERB

/ daɪ aʊt / (AmE), / daɪ aʊt / (BrE)
To become extinct or cease to exist gradually.
cease to exist; become extinct

Examples

If we do not protect endangered species, they may die out completely.
Many languages have died out due to lack of use over generations.
The custom began to die out as modern technology became more widespread.

Synset Examples

Many traditions die out if not passed down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or to exist
  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Antonym: to survive - to continue to live or exist
