to tip over

1. VERB

/ tɪp ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / tɪp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to cause something to overturn from an upright or normal position
to cause to overturn

Examples

Be careful not to tip over the glass of water on the table.
The strong wind may tip over the trash bins outside.
He accidentally tipped over the lamp while reaching for his book.

Synset Examples

The cat knocked over the flower vase
the clumsy customer turned over the vase
he tumped over his beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock over - to cause something to fall by hitting it
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position; not tipped over
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01913971-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɪp ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / tɪp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to (cause to) turn from an upright or normal position so that it falls or is no longer upright
fall or make fall from upright position

Examples

Be careful not to tip over the glass of water on the table.
High winds caused several trees to tip over during the storm.
If you lean back too far in your chair, you might tip over.

Synset Examples

The big vase overturned
The canoe tumped over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overturn - to turn something upside down or cause it to tip over
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position, not tipped over
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
