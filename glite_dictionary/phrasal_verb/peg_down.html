to peg down

1. VERB

/ pɛg daʊn / (AmE), / pɛg daʊn / (BrE)
to define or specify something clearly and exactly; to set or narrow down the limits or details of something
define or specify clearly

Examples

It's hard to peg down the exact cause of the problem.
The committee tried to peg down the rules for participation.
Can you peg down a specific time for the meeting?

Synset Examples

I cannot narrow down the rules for this game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to narrow down - to reduce the number of possibilities or to make more specific
  • Synonym: to define - to state or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00717446-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɛɡ daʊn / (AmE), / pɛɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to fasten or secure something, especially a tent or covering, to the ground using pegs or pins
fasten or secure with pegs

Examples

We need to peg down the tent before the wind picks up.
The workers quickly pegged down the plastic sheets to protect the tools from rain.
She made sure to peg down each corner of the tarpaulin to keep it from blowing away.

Synset Examples

peg a tent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stake down - to fasten or secure something to the ground with stakes
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01342965-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pɛɡ daʊn / (AmE), / pɛɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to clarify, fix, or finalize something with certainty
clarify or finalize firmly

Examples

We need to peg down the timeline before making any announcements.
It's important to peg down the requirements so everyone is clear on their tasks.
They finally pegged down the date for the annual conference.

Synset Examples

We need to peg down the details before the meeting.
Can we peg down the budget for this project?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nail down - to determine or finalize something exactly
  • Synonym: to pin down - to define or establish something clearly
