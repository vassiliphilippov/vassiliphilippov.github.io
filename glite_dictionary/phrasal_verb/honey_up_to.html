to honey up to

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌni ʌp tə / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ʌp tuː / (BrE)
to try to ingratiate oneself with someone, often by being excessively flattering or friendly
ingratiate oneself with someone

Examples

She decided to honey up to the manager in hopes of getting promoted.
It's obvious he's trying to honey up to the new teacher with all those compliments.
Don't honey up to me—I know when you're being insincere.

Synset Examples

She tried to honey up to her boss for a raise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to butter up - to flatter someone excessively
  • Synonym: to ingratiate - to try to win favor with someone

Etymology

From 'honey,' used metaphorically to mean sweetness or flattery, plus 'up to.'
