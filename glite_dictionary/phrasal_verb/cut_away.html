to cut away

1. VERB

/ kʌt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of a film or television director or editor) to switch rapidly to another scene or angle during filming or broadcasting.
switch to another scene (film/TV)

Examples

When the dialogue grew tense, the director signaled to cut away to a wide shot.
The program will often cut away to the audience during a performance.
At the crucial moment, the camera cut away from the speaker to show reaction shots.

Synset Examples

‘cut away now!’ the director shouted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut to - to transition directly to a different scene or camera angle (film/TV)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01864306-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove something by cutting it off or away from the main part
remove by cutting off or away

Examples

He used a saw to cut away the dead wood from the tree.
The tailor will cut away the excess fabric before sewing the hem.
Rescue workers had to cut away metal to free the trapped driver.

Synset Examples

cut away the branch that sticks out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - remove by cutting; sever completely
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01556769-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kʌt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to lessen or remove something intangible, such as feelings or spirit, often causing emotional pain or emptiness
remove or diminish emotionally

Examples

His constant criticism began to cut away her confidence over time.
The shocking news seemed to cut away his sense of security.
Over the years, loss and disappointment would slowly cut away at her joy.

Synset Examples

His harsh words seemed to cut away her soul.
The betrayal cut away his soul, leaving him hollow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut out - to remove completely, also used figuratively for excluding something or someone
