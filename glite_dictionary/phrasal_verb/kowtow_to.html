to kowtow to

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊˌtaʊ tu / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌtaʊ tuː / (BrE)
to act in an excessively obedient or submissive way towards someone in authority; to show servile deference
act in an overly submissive way

Examples

He refused to kowtow to the unreasonable rules set by his employer.
Some politicians are willing to kowtow to powerful lobbyists to gain favor.
Despite pressure from her superiors, she never kowtowed to their demands.

Synset Examples

She won't kowtow to anyone, no matter the pressure.
It's frustrating to see him always kowtow to his critics.
He refused to kowtow to the demands of his boss.
It's unnecessary to kowtow to authority all the time.
They expected her to kowtow to their outdated traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grovel - act in an obsequiously submissive manner
  • Antonym: stand up to - resist or refuse to submit to authority

Etymology

From Chinese 叩头 (kòutóu), meaning 'to knock one's head (in bowing)'.
