to use up

1. VERB

/ juːz ʌp / (AmE), / juːz ʌp / (BrE)
to consume all of something so that nothing is left; to exhaust a supply or resource
consume or exhaust completely

Examples

The kids used up all the paint before the project was finished.
We've used up nearly all of our vacation days this year.
Be careful not to use up the last of the milk.

Synset Examples

this car consumes a lot of gas
We exhausted our savings
They run through 20 bottles of wine a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to use up the whole of something
  • Synonym: run out (of) - to have no more of something left
  • Antonym: conserve - to avoid using up completely; to preserve a resource
