to break bad

1. VERB

/ breɪk bæd / (AmE), / breɪk bæd / (BrE)
to become involved in criminal activity or morally questionable behavior, especially after previously leading a law-abiding life
to turn to a life of crime
informal american

Examples

After losing his job, he started to break bad and became involved with illegal activities.
She never imagined her brother would break bad and get arrested for theft.
Many crime dramas depict ordinary people who break bad under pressure.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, people break bad when they feel cornered.
The character in the show chose to break bad for his family's sake.
He decided to break bad after years of working a mundane job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go rogue - to behave independently and possibly dishonestly
  • Antonym: reform - to stop engaging in criminal or immoral behavior

Etymology

American English slang; popularized by the title of the television series 'Breaking Bad' (2008); regional US usage predates the show.
