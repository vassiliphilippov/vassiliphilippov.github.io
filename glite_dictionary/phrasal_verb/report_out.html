to report out

1. VERB

/ rɪˈpɔrt aʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːt aʊt / (BrE)
(of a committee) to return a bill to the full legislative body after consideration and possible revision.
return a bill to a legislature after review
legal

Examples

The committee voted to report out the new healthcare bill to the Senate for further debate.
After months of discussion, they finally reported out the amended legislation.
If the panel refuses to report out the bill, it will not reach the floor for a vote.
