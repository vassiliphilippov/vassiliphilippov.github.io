to shove along

1. VERB

/ ʃʌv əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ʃʌv əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
(informal) To leave or move away, especially when said abruptly or rudely.
leave or move away, informal
informal

Examples

I think it's time we shoved along before it gets too crowded.
The guard told us to shove along and not block the entrance.
If you aren't buying anything, please shove along so others can get in.

Synset Examples

shove off!
The children shoved along
Blow now!
let's blow this place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shove off - To leave, often abruptly or rudely
  • Synonym: to go away - To leave a place
  • Antonym: stay - To remain in a place
