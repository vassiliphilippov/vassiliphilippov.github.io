to put one across

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌn əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / pʊt wʌn əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone; to fool someone, especially by making them believe something untrue
deceive or trick someone

Examples

I can't believe you managed to put one across on the whole committee.
He thought he could put one across on us, but we saw through his story.
They tried to put one across with a fake letter, but it didn't work.

Synset Examples

The immigrant was duped because he trusted everyone
You can't fool me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - to trick or deceive someone
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick someone
  • Synonym: hoax - to deceive or trick someone as a prank
