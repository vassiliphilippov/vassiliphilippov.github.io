to take ill

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɪl / (AmE), / teɪk ɪl / (BrE)
to become sick or unwell suddenly
become sick or unwell

Examples

She began to take ill after eating some bad seafood on holiday.
If you take ill during the trip, please let us know immediately.
He was suddenly taken ill at the start of the concert and had to leave.

Synset Examples

She began to take ill after eating the spoiled food.
He might take ill if he doesn't wear a coat.
The child will take ill if he doesn't get enough rest.
She was taken ill and had to leave work early.
If you feel dizzy, you might be taken ill.
He was unexpectedly taken ill during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall ill - to become sick or unwell
  • Synonym: take sick - to become sick or unwell (chiefly US)
  • Antonym: to recover - to return to health after illness
