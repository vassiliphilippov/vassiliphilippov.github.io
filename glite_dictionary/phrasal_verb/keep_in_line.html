to keep in line

1. VERB

/ kip ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / kiːp ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
to control someone or make sure they behave properly, often through authority or discipline
control or enforce proper behavior

Examples

The manager had to keep in line a team of very strong personalities.
Strict rules are needed to keep in line the people in this facility.
She was praised for her ability to keep in line even the most disruptive students.

Synset Examples

She manipulates her boss
She is a very controlling mother and doesn't let her children grow up
The teacher knew how to keep the class in line
she keeps in line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discipline - the practice of training people to obey rules or a code of behavior
  • Synonym: control - the power to influence or direct people's behavior
