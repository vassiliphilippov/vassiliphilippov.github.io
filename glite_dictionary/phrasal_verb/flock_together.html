to flock together

1. VERB

/ flɑk təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / flɒk təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or things) to come together or gather in a group, often attracted by a common interest or purpose.
come together in groups

Examples

Students from the same hometown often flock together at university, forming tight-knit groups far from home.
Birds instinctively flock together to protect themselves from predators.
During the festival, tourists flock together in the city center to enjoy the celebrations.

Synset Examples

Friends tend to flock together at parties.
People often flock together based on shared interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come or bring together in a group
  • Antonym: to disperse - to go or spread in different directions, to scatter
