to turn in

1. VERB

/ tɝn ɪn / (AmE), / tɜːn ɪn / (BrE)
to go to bed or prepare for sleep, especially at night
go to bed; prepare for sleep

Examples

I'm feeling tired, so I think I'll turn in early tonight.
She usually turns in after reading a few chapters of her book.
After the long journey, they turned in as soon as they reached the hotel.

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to bed - to get into bed in order to sleep

/ tɝːn ɪn / (AmE), / tɜːn ɪn / (BrE)

/ tɝn ɪn / (AmE), / tɜːn ɪn / (BrE)
to surrender or deliver someone or something to an authority or other person, especially the police.

Examples

The authorities urged anyone with information to turn in the suspect immediately.
She felt guilty and chose to turn in her friend who was involved in the theft.
