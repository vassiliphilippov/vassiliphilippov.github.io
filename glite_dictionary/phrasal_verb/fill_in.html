to fill in

1. VERB

/ fɪl ɪn / (AmE), / fɪl ɪn / (BrE)
to do someone else's job or take their place temporarily as a substitute
act as a substitute; temporarily replace someone

Examples

Can you fill in for Sarah while she's on vacation?
He was asked to fill in when the regular host called in sick.
They needed someone to fill in at the front desk for a few hours.

Synset Examples

The young teacher had to substitute for the sick colleague
The skim milk substitutes for cream — we are on a strict diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to take the place of someone or something
  • Synonym: to stand in - to temporarily do someone else's job or fulfill their role

2. VERB

/ fɪl ɪn / (AmE), / fɪl ɪn / (BrE)
to provide someone with necessary or recent information about something
give someone necessary or recent information

Examples

I missed last week's class, so Tom filled me in on the important points.
Please fill me in if there are any changes to the schedule.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information to someone
  • Synonym: update - to bring someone up to date with new information

3. VERB

/ fɪl ɪn / (AmE), / fɪl ɪn / (BrE)
to represent the effect of shade or shadow on (a drawing or painting) by adding lines, colors, or shading
to depict shade or shadow in art

Examples

The artist carefully filled in the shadows to make the portrait look realistic.
You should fill in the background areas to create a sense of depth.
She filled in the sketch with soft shading to represent the evening light.

/ fɪl ɪn / (AmE), / fɪl ɪn / (BrE)
to complete something by adding missing information; to do someone's job or take their place temporarily

Examples

I can fill in for Sarah while she is on vacation.
The editor asked me to fill in the details that were missing in the article.

Synset Examples
