to bring around

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / brɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to restore someone to health; to cure or revive someone from illness or unconsciousness
to make healthy or revive someone

Examples

The quick intervention helped bring around the patient after his fainting spell.
Doctors hope that the new medication will bring around those suffering from the illness.
With proper care, they managed to bring around the injured hiker.

Synset Examples

The treatment cured the boy's acne
The quack pretended to heal patients but never managed to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to heal - to make or become healthy again
  • Synonym: cure - to restore to health

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / brɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to cause someone to change their opinion or adopt a different course of action
cause to change someone's opinion

Examples

It took weeks of negotiation to finally bring around the rest of the team.
Her calm explanation eventually brought around her skeptical parents to her point of view.
The new evidence was enough to bring around even the most stubborn critics.

Synset Examples

His urgent letter finally brought me around to give money to the school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persuade - to convince someone to do or believe something
  • Synonym: to convince - to make someone believe something or agree to do something
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00769167-v
Synset POS: v
