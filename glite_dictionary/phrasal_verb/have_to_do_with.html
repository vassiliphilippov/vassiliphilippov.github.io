to have to do with

1. VERB

/ hæv tə du wɪð / (AmE), / hæv tə dʊ wɪð / (BrE)
to be connected with or relevant to something; to concern or relate to
be relevant to; concern

Examples

Does this information have to do with our new project?
Her research primarily has to do with environmental issues.
The argument had to do with a misunderstanding about the schedule.

Synset Examples

There were lots of questions referring to her talk
My remark pertained to your earlier comments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relate to - to be connected with something
  • Synonym: concern - to be about something or someone
