to take place

1. VERB

/ teɪk pleɪs / (AmE), / teɪk pleɪs / (BrE)
to happen or occur, especially as the result of being planned or scheduled
to happen or occur

Examples

The annual festival will take place next weekend in the city square.
No changes can take place without your approval.
The accident took place late at night during a heavy storm.

Synset Examples

What is happening?
The meeting took place without an incident
Nothing occurred that seemed important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occur - to happen or take place
  • Synonym: to happen - to take place, especially without being planned
  • Antonym: not happen - to fail to take place or occur
