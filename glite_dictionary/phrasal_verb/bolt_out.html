to bolt out

1. VERB

/ boʊlt aʊt / (AmE), / bəʊlt aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and quickly, often in a rush or out of surprise or fear
leave suddenly and quickly

Examples

As soon as the alarm rang, everyone bolted out of the building.
He bolted out of the meeting room without saying a word to anyone.
The cat bolted out the open door when it heard the loud noise.

Synset Examples

The listeners bolted when he discussed his strange ideas
When she started to tell silly stories, I ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash out - to leave quickly or suddenly
  • Degree Lower: to slip away - leave quietly or unobtrusively
  • Degree Higher: to flee - run away quickly from a place or situation of danger

Etymology

From the verb 'bolt' meaning 'to move suddenly,' influenced by the image of an animal dashing from a hiding place.
