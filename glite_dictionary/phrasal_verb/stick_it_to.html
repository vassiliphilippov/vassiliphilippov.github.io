to stick it to

1. VERB

/ stɪk ɪt tu / (AmE), / stɪk ɪt tə / (BrE)
to punish, get back at, or challenge someone, often in a defiant or vengeful way
to retaliate against or challenge someone
informal

Examples

With her fiery speech, she really stuck it to her detractors.
Many workers wanted to stick it to the company by joining the strike.
He couldn't resist sticking it to his rival when the opportunity arose.

Synset Examples

He wanted to stick it to the critics with his new album.
She decided to stick it to her boss by quitting.
He loves to stick it to anyone who doubts him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get back at - to take revenge on someone
  • Synonym: to show up - to embarrass or outperform someone in public
