to queer your identity

1. VERB

/ kwɪr jɚ aɪˈdɛn.t̬ə.t̬i / (AmE), / kwɪə jɔː aɪˈdɛn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
to explore, challenge, or redefine one's identity, often by rejecting conventional or socially expected categories, especially regarding gender and sexuality.
explore or redefine one's identity

Examples

Many young people feel empowered when they decide to queer their identity and reject strict labels.
Workshops on campus encourage students to queer their identity through open discussion and creative expression.
It's common for individuals to queer their identity at different stages in their lives as they seek authenticity.

Synset Examples

It's okay to queer your identity as you grow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explore your identity - to consider or examine one's sense of self

Etymology

From the verb 'queer,' meaning to challenge norms, from the earlier noun and adjective 'queer' (strange, non-normative), ultimately from German 'quer' (oblique, transverse).
