to even up

1. VERB

/ ˈivən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈiːvən ʌp / (BrE)
to make the edges of a pile or stack, especially paper, level or aligned; to make things equal or even
make edges or things level or equal

Examples

She used a ruler to even up the edges of the documents before stapling them.
The printer paused for a moment to even up the stack of freshly printed pages.
Make sure to even up the sheets so they fit neatly into the envelope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to straighten - to make something straight or level
  • Synonym: to align - to arrange in a straight line or in proper position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01991662-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈivən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈiːvən ʌp / (BrE)
to make something equal, fair, or balanced by adjusting differences
make equal or fair by adjustment

Examples

The manager tried to even up the teams by giving the weaker side an extra player.
To even up the accounts, she repaid what she owed as soon as possible.
After a controversial call, the referee awarded a free kick to even up the game.

Synset Examples

engineers will work to correct the effects of air resistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to even out - to make level, smooth, or uniform by eliminating differences
