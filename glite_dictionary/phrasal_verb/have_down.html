to have down

1. VERB

/ hæv daʊn / (AmE), / hav daʊn / (BrE)
to have mastered or learned something so thoroughly that it can be recalled or performed easily and accurately
have something mastered
informal

Examples

After weeks of practice, I finally have down the steps to the new dance.
She had down every answer before the test even started.
Once you have down the basics, the rest becomes much easier.

Synset Examples

She has the names of the fifty states down pat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master - to become highly skilled or proficient in something
