to pick out

1. VERB

/ pɪk aʊt / (AmE), / pɪk aʊt / (BrE)
To detect or identify something or someone using the senses, especially by noticing or distinguishing them among others.
detect or identify using the senses

Examples

Even in a crowded room, she could easily pick out her friend's voice.
It was hard to pick out the constellations with so many city lights.
The judge managed to pick out the best performance from dozens of strong contestants.

Synset Examples

The fleeing convicts were picked out of the darkness by the watchful prison guards
I can't make out the faces in this photograph

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make out - to discern or recognize with difficulty
