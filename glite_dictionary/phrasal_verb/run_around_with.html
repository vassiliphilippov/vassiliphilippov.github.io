to run around with

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌn əˌraʊnd wɪð / (AmE), / ˈrʌn əˌraʊnd wɪð / (BrE)
to spend time or associate with someone, especially casually or with people whose reputation may be questionable
associate socially, often with questionable people

Examples

After switching schools, he started to run around with a new group of friends.
Her parents worried she might run around with troublemakers in the neighborhood.
I used to run around with him when we were teenagers.

Synset Examples

He doesn't want to run around with that crowd anymore.
She’s been known to run around with the wrong people.
They used to run around with each other in high school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hang out with - to spend time relaxing, usually with friends
  • Synonym: associate with - to spend time with a particular group of people
