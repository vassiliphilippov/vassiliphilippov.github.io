to steamroll the discussion

1. VERB

/ ˈstiːmˌroʊl ðə dɪˈskʌʃən / (AmE), / ˈstiːmˌrəʊl ðə dɪˈskʌʃən / (BrE)
to dominate a conversation in an overbearing or forceful manner, often ignoring the contributions of others
forcefully dominate a conversation

Examples

During meetings, he often tries to steamroll the discussion instead of listening to different viewpoints.
It's important not to steamroll the discussion if you want everyone to feel heard.
She accused her colleague of steamrolling the discussion on every key issue.

Synset Examples

He tried to steamroll the discussion, but we stood our ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominate the conversation - to take control of a conversation and not allow others to contribute

Etymology

From 'steamroll', derived from the noun 'steamroller', a machine that flattens surfaces, used figuratively to suggest overwhelming force.
