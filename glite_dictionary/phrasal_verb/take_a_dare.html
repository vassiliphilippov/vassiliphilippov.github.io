to take a dare

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə dɛr / (AmE), / teɪk ə deə / (BrE)
to accept a challenge to do something difficult or risky that someone has dared you to do
accept a challenge or dare

Examples

She was the only one brave enough to take a dare and jump into the lake.
He never backs down when challenged; he always takes a dare.
They dared me to sing in public, and I couldn't resist taking a dare.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refuse a dare - choose not to accept a dare or challenge
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00952369-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk ə dɛr / (AmE), / teɪk ə deə / (BrE)
to accept and attempt to do something one has been dared or challenged to do, often something difficult or bold
accept and attempt a challenge (dare)

Examples

She decided to take a dare and jump into the icy lake.
No one in the group would take a dare except for Mark, who was always up for a challenge.
Would you ever take a dare if it involved something scary like skydiving?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dare - to challenge someone to do something bold or difficult
