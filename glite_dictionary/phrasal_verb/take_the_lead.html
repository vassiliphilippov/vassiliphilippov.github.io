to take the lead

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə lid / (AmE), / teɪk ðə liːd / (BrE)
to assume control, responsibility, or initiative in a situation or activity
assume control or initiative

Examples

During the emergency, Sarah decided to take the lead and organize the evacuation.
If this team wants to win, someone needs to take the lead and make tough decisions.
When the discussion stalled, John stepped in to take the lead and move things forward.

Synset Examples

She always knows how to take the lead in discussions.
He was chosen to take the lead on the new project.
It's time for us to take the lead and innovate.
Ze took the lead in the project effortlessly.
The horse raced out of the traps and took the lead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - to be in command or to guide others
  • Antonym: to follow - to go after or come behind, not take control
