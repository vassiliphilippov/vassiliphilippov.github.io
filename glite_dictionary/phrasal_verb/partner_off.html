to partner off

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑrtnər ˈɔf / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə ˈɒf / (BrE)
to form a pair or pairs, especially for a particular purpose or activity
form a pair or pairs

Examples

At the dance, everyone was asked to partner off before the music started.
The teacher told the students to partner off for the science experiment.
It took a few minutes for the group to partner off and begin the activity.

Synset Examples

The two old friends paired off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pair off - to form couples or pairs
