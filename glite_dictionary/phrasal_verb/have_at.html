to have at

1. VERB

/ hæv æt / (AmE), / hæv æt / (BrE)
to attack, deal with, or begin something with energy or enthusiasm, especially in an informal way
approach or tackle energetically
informal

Examples

Once the meeting ended, everyone was eager to have at the pizza.
If you're ready, please have at the next problem on the list.
The kids couldn't wait to have at the pile of gifts under the tree.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
