to beat up

1. VERB

/ bit ʌp / (AmE), / biːt ʌp / (BrE)
to attack someone and injure them by hitting them repeatedly, often as an act of aggression or punishment
attack and injure by repeated hitting

Examples

The gang threatened to beat up anyone who entered their territory.
He was beaten up after leaving the bar late at night.
Stop beating up your little brother—it’s not fair to him.

Synset Examples

Thugs beat him up when he walked down the street late at night
The teacher used to beat the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - to physically attack someone
  • Synonym: attack - to act violently towards someone
