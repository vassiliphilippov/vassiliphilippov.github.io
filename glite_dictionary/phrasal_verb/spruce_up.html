to spruce up

1. VERB

/ spruːs ʌp / (AmE), / spruːs ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone or something look neater, tidier, or more attractive
make neater or more attractive

Examples

We decided to spruce up the office before the guests arrived.
She likes to spruce up her appearance with a new outfit every season.
After the long winter, many people spruce up their gardens.

Synset Examples

Spruce up your house for Spring
titivate the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make something neat and orderly
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make something untidy or disordered
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00294739-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sprus ʌp / (AmE), / spruːs ʌp / (BrE)
to make oneself or something look cleaner, neater, or more attractive, especially for a special occasion
make cleaner or more attractive

Examples

He decided to spruce up his apartment before inviting guests over.
You should spruce up a bit for the interview today.
The town center was spruced up for the summer festival.

Synset Examples

He spruced up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make something tidy or orderly
  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or special clothes
