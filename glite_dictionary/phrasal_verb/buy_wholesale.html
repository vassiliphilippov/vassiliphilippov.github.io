to buy wholesale

1. VERB

/ baɪ ˈhoʊlˌseɪl / (AmE), / baɪ ˈhəʊlseɪl / (BrE)
to purchase goods in large quantities at reduced prices, typically for resale or business use
purchase goods in bulk at reduced prices

Examples

Many small shop owners buy wholesale in order to stock their stores efficiently.
If you buy wholesale, you can often get much better deals than in regular stores.
She decided to buy wholesale to save money when starting her business.

Synset Examples

They bought the furniture wholesale to save money.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: buy retail - to purchase goods in small quantities at regular prices
