to write up

1. VERB

/ raɪt ˈʌp / (AmE), / raɪt ˈʌp / (BrE)
to put information into a complete or formal written form
put into complete written form

Examples

After conducting the experiment, she had to write up the results for her report.
The manager asked him to write up the minutes of the meeting by the end of the day.
It took longer than expected to write up the official proposal for the new project.

Synset Examples

write out a contract

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write out - to write something in full, especially by hand
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ raɪt ʌp / (AmE), / raɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to bring someone or something to public notice by writing about them, either with praise or criticism.
report or publicize by writing (with praise or criticism)

Examples

The magazine decided to write up the local artist's achievements in its latest issue.
She was thrilled to be written up in the business section of the newspaper.
The manager threatened to write up any employee who failed to follow the safety rules.

Synset Examples

The New York Times wrote him up last week
Did your boss write you up?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: report - to give a spoken or written account of something; to tell people about something
  • Synonym: cover - to report or write about a subject or event for a newspaper, magazine, etc.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01070156-v
Synset POS: v
