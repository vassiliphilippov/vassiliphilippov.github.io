to swing from branch to branch

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ frəm bræntʃ tuː bræntʃ / (AmE), / swɪŋ frəm brɑːntʃ tuː brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
to move quickly through trees by holding and releasing branches, typically by swinging or moving arms alternately from one branch to another
move through trees by holding branches

Examples

The playful monkeys swing from branch to branch in the forest canopy all day long.
I watched in amazement as she managed to swing from branch to branch across the entire tree.
Some animals, like gibbons, swing from branch to branch to travel quickly through the jungle.

Synset Examples

The kids laughed as the monkey swung from branch to branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - to move back and forth or from side to side while suspended or on an axis
