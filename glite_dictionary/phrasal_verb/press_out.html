to press out

1. VERB

/ prɛs aʊt / (AmE), / prɛs aʊt / (BrE)
to shape or form something, especially a plastic object, by pressing material into a mold or die
form (plastic) by molding under pressure

Examples

The factory can press out thousands of plastic bottles every day.
They pressed out vinyl records from heated plastic pellets.
High-pressure machines are used to press out intricate toy parts.

Synset Examples

press a record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - to shape a substance using a hollow form
  • Synonym: cast - to form an object by pouring liquid material into a mold
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prɛs aʊt / (AmE), / prɛs aʊt / (BrE)
to obtain (a liquid or other substance) from something by applying mechanical force
extract by pressing

Examples

She used a juicer to press out the fresh orange juice for breakfast.
They pressed out the oil from the seeds using heavy machinery.
He pressed out the excess water from the spinach before cooking it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ prɛs aʊt / (AmE), / prɛs aʊt / (BrE)
to extinguish something, especially a burning object like a cigarette, by crushing it
extinguish by crushing, esp. a cigarette

Examples

She pressed out her cigarette and left the room quietly.
Please press out any burning embers before you throw the match away.
He pressed out his cigar in the ashtray, ending the conversation.
