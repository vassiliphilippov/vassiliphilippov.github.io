to slice through

1. VERB

/ slaɪs θru / (AmE), / slaɪs θruː / (BrE)
to move quickly and smoothly through something, as if cutting it with a sharp edge
move smoothly through something

Examples

The bird sliced through the morning mist as it flew over the lake.
The skater sliced through the ice with remarkable speed and precision.
A bullet sliced through the silence, startling everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

His hand sliced through the air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut through - move quickly and directly through something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01920026-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ slaɪs θruː / (AmE), / slaɪs θruː / (BrE)
to cut through something smoothly and easily, typically with a sharp tool
cut through smoothly and easily

Examples

The chef used a sharp knife to slice through the ripe tomato effortlessly.
With little resistance, the laser was able to slice through the metal plate.
The boat's hull seemed to slice through the water as it gained speed.

Synset Examples

The knife is sharp enough to slice through anything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut through - to pass or make a way through something by cutting
