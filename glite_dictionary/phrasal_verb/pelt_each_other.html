to pelt each other

1. VERB

/ pɛlt iːtʃ ˈʌðɚ / (AmE), / pɛlt iːtʃ ˈʌðə / (BrE)
(of two or more people) to throw objects at one another, often in a playful or aggressive way.
throw objects at one another

Examples

The kids pelted each other with snowballs until they were exhausted.
During the food fight, students pelted each other with bits of leftover pasta.
As soon as the rain started, we pelted each other with handfuls of wet leaves.

Synset Examples

Children began to pelt each other with water balloons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelt - to throw things with force at someone or something
