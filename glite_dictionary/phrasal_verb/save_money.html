to save money

1. VERB

/ seɪv ˈmʌni / (AmE), / seɪv ˈmʌni / (BrE)
to reduce one's expenses or put aside money for future use instead of spending it immediately
reduce expenses or set aside money

Examples

I'm trying to save money by bringing my lunch to work instead of eating out.
She managed to save money for a trip abroad next year.
If you want to buy a new car, you'll need to start saving money every month.

Synset Examples

His scheme to save money worked better than expected.
He has his mind about one goal this year: saving money.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spend money - to pay out money on goods or services
  • Synonym: to economize - to reduce expenditure; to use money, resources, etc. carefully
