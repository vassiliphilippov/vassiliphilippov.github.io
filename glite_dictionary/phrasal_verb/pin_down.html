to pin down

1. VERB

/ pɪn daʊn / (AmE), / pɪn daʊn / (BrE)
to place someone in a confining or embarrassing position from which it is difficult to escape
place in a confining or embarrassing position

Examples

The lawyer tried to pin down the witness with tough questions.
Journalists attempted to pin down the candidate on her stance, but she avoided answering directly.
Despite their efforts, no one could pin down the CEO about the company's future plans.

Synset Examples

He was trapped in a difficult situation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02501834-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɪn daʊn / (AmE), / pɪn daʊn / (BrE)
to clearly define, identify, or specify something; to make something precise or certain
to define or specify something clearly

Examples

It's hard to pin down the exact reasons for his decision.
The cause of the problem was difficult to pin down.
She finally managed to pin down a date for the meeting.

Synset Examples

I cannot narrow down the rules for this game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to narrow down - to reduce to a more limited number, make more specific
  • Synonym: to specify - to state something in an exact and detailed way
