to stop dead

1. VERB

/ stɑp dɛd / (AmE), / stɒp dɛd / (BrE)
to stop moving suddenly and completely, often due to surprise or shock
to stop suddenly and completely

Examples

I stopped dead in the middle of the road when I heard the siren.
She stopped dead as soon as she saw the snake in front of her.
He was running down the hall but stopped dead when the principal called his name.

Synset Examples

When he saw the police car he froze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to suddenly stop moving, often from fear or surprise

2. VERB

/ stɑp dɛd / (AmE), / stɒp dɛd / (BrE)
to come to a complete and sudden stop, especially in movement or speech
suddenly stop completely

Examples

He stopped dead in the middle of the road when the car appeared.
The children stopped dead when they heard the loud crash.
She stopped dead and stared at the unexpected visitor.

Synset Examples

He had to stop dead when he realized he forgot something.
She talked so passionately that I had to stop dead and listen.
The dog stopped dead at the sight of the squirrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halt - to stop moving or operating
  • Synonym: freeze - to stop moving suddenly, often from surprise or fear
