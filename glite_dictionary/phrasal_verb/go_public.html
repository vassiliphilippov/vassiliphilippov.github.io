to go public

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
(of a company) to start selling shares to the public for the first time, usually through a stock exchange.
become a publicly traded company

Examples

The tech startup plans to go public next year to raise additional capital.
When the company went public, its stocks soared on the first day of trading.
Many private firms dream of going public to expand their business and reputation.

Synset Examples

They decided to go public with their new product next week.
The company will go public after several years of private funding.
We plan to go public to attract more investors.
