to put hand up

1. VERB

/ pʊt hænd ʌp / (AmE), / pʊt hænd ʌp / (BrE)
to raise one's hand as a signal of willingness, desire to speak, or to answer a question, especially in a group or classroom setting
raise a hand to signal willingness or speak

Examples

If you know the answer, just put your hand up and wait to be called.
The children were asked to put their hand up if they needed help.
She was shy, but eventually put her hand up to join the discussion.

Synset Examples

She put her hand up to volunteer for the project.
He put his hand up to answer the question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise hand - to lift your hand as a signal, especially in a group
