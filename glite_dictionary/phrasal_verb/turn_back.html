to turn back

1. VERB

/ tɝn ˈbæk / (AmE), / tɜːn ˈbæk / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave or to prevent them from entering or continuing; to drive away or repel, literally or metaphorically
to force away or drive off

Examples

The security guards turned back anyone without a ticket.
Heavy rain turned back the hikers before they reached the summit.
She tried to stay positive and turned back her doubts.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
