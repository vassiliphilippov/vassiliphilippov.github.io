to clamp down

1. VERB

/ klæmp daʊn / (AmE), / klæmp daʊn / (BrE)
to take strict action to stop or control undesirable or illegal activities
take strict action to suppress

Examples

The government decided to clamp down on corruption in the banking sector.
After a rise in vandalism, the school began to clamp down on rule-breakers.
Online platforms are starting to clamp down on the spread of false information.

Synset Examples

The police clamped down on illegal drugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crack down - to take severe measures against, especially to enforce laws or rules
  • Antonym: permit - to allow or approve, the opposite of suppressing
