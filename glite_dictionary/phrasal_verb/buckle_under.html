to buckle under

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌkəl ˈʌndər / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to agree or yield reluctantly, especially as a result of pressure or force
yield or consent reluctantly

Examples

She finally buckled under and accepted the offer, though she had many doubts.
After weeks of criticism, the government buckled under public pressure and changed its policy.
He didn't really want to go on the trip, but he buckled under his friends' persuasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give in - to cease resistance and yield
  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand force or pressure, refuse to give in
