to book in for

1. VERB

/ bʊk ɪn fɔr / (AmE), / bʊk ɪn fə / (BrE)
to make a reservation or arrange an appointment for something, such as an event or service
make a reservation for something

Examples

I called the clinic to book in for my annual check-up.
She needs to book in for a yoga class next week.
They advised me to book in for the conference early to secure a place.

Synset Examples

I need to book in for my dentist appointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve - to arrange for something to be kept for your use at a later time
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide that a planned event or arrangement will not happen
