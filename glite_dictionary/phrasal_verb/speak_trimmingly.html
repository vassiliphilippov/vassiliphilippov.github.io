to speak trimmingly

1. VERB

/ spiːk ˈtrɪmɪŋli / (AmE), / spiːk ˈtrɪmɪŋli / (BrE)
to speak in a concise, sometimes critical manner
speak concisely or critically

Examples

She often speak trimmingly when discussing policies she disagrees with.
The manager spoke trimmingly about the team's recent performance.
Critics speak trimmingly of the film's lack of originality.

Synset Examples

She spoke trimmingly about the recent changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
