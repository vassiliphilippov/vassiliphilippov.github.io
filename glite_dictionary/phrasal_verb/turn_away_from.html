to turn away from

1. VERB

/ tɝn əˈweɪ frəm / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈweɪ frəm / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid, reject, or refuse to consider someone or something
to avoid or reject

Examples

After years in finance, she decided to turn away from corporate life and start her own business.
Many young people turn away from traditional news sources in favor of social media.
He made a conscious choice to turn away from negative thinking and focus on gratitude.

Synset Examples

He chose to turn away from his fears and embrace change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • Antonym: embrace - to accept or support willingly
