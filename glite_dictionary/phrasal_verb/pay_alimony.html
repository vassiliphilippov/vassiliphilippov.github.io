to pay alimony

1. VERB

/ peɪ ˈælɪˌmoʊni / (AmE), / peɪ ˈælɪməni / (BrE)
to make regular payments to a former spouse for their financial support after a divorce or separation, as required by law or agreement.
make spousal support payments after divorce
legal

Examples

He is required by the court to pay alimony every month.
After their divorce, she refused to pay alimony until the settlement was finalized.
Many people struggle financially when they have to pay alimony for several years.

Synset Examples

He struggled to pay his alimony every month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alimony - regular payments for a former spouse's support after separation or divorce
  • Synonym: pay child support - make regular payments for the financial support of a child following separation or divorce

Etymology

From Latin 'alimonia' (nourishment), via Old French and Medieval Latin.
