to grace someone with

1. VERB

/ ɡreɪs ˈsʌmwʌn wɪð / (AmE), / ɡreɪs ˈsʌmwən wɪð / (BrE)
to bestow something upon someone, typically as an honor or courtesy; to present or provide something in a gracious manner
to bestow something as an honor

Examples

She kindly graced us with her presence at the celebration.
The famous author graced the event with an inspiring speech.
Would you grace me with the honor of a dance?

Synset Examples

Miladdo, would you grace us with your wisdom?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor someone with - to give someone special recognition or a reward
  • Synonym: to bless someone with - to provide someone with something desirable

Etymology

From the verb 'grace', ultimately from Latin 'gratia' (favor, thanks) via Old French 'grace'.
