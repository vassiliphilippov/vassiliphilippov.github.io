to check in

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk ɪn / (AmE), / tʃɛk ɪn / (BrE)
to officially announce your arrival at a hotel, airport, or other place by registering your presence, often at a reception desk or counter
register arrival at a place

Examples

You must check in at the front desk when you arrive at the hotel.
Passengers are required to check in two hours before their flight.
She checked in online the night before her departure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to check out - to announce departure and settle bills at a hotel or similar place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00968184-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʃɛk ɪn / (AmE), / tʃɛk ɪn / (BrE)
to contact someone briefly to ask about their well-being or situation
contact to ask how someone is

Examples

I just wanted to check in and see how you were doing after your surgery.
It's important to check in with friends when they're going through a tough time.
She called her parents every Sunday to check in and catch up.

Synset Examples

I called to check in: how are you?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to touch base - to make contact or communicate briefly with someone
