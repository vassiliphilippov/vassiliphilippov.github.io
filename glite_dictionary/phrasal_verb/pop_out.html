to pop out

1. VERB

/ pɑp aʊt / (AmE), / pɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to come out suddenly or with a quick, forceful movement
come out suddenly or forcefully

Examples

When I opened the box, a toy snake popped out and startled me.
The toast popped out of the toaster as soon as it was done.
Her eyes nearly popped out when she saw the surprise party.

Synset Examples

you stick a bill in the vending machine and the change pops out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burst out - to emerge or escape suddenly and with force
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00530491-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɑp aʊt / (AmE), / pɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to bulge outward suddenly or noticeably, especially from a surface or container
bulge outward suddenly or noticeably

Examples

The balloon was so full that parts of it began to pop out.
Her eyes seemed to pop out in surprise when she saw the bill.
The buttons on his shirt threatened to pop out after a big meal.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulge - to swell or curve outward

3. VERB

/ pɑp aʊt / (AmE), / pɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to appear suddenly or unexpectedly; to happen without warning

Examples

The cat seemed to pop out of nowhere, startling everyone in the room.
Rainbow colors would pop out as the artist applied water to the canvas.
Just as we were leaving, an old friend popped out from behind a tree to surprise us.

/ pɑp aʊt / (AmE), / pɒp aʊt / (BrE)

leave a place briefly

Examples

She popped out for some fresh air during the meeting break.
Could you pop out and get some milk on your way back?
