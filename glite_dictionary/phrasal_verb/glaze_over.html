to glaze over

1. VERB

/ ɡleɪz ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡleɪz ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of the eyes) to become shiny and unfocused, usually due to tiredness, boredom, or unconsciousness.
eyes become shiny and unfocused

Examples

I could see her eyes glaze over as the meeting dragged on.
After staring at the computer for hours, his eyes began to glaze over.
If you don't get enough sleep, your eyes might glaze over during class.

Synset Examples

Her eyes glazed over from lack of sleep

2. VERB

/ ɡleɪz ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡleɪz ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of the eyes) to become glassy or have a glass-like, unfocused appearance, often indicating boredom or lack of attention.
eyes becoming glassy or unfocused

Examples

Whenever the meeting drags on, her eyes glaze over and she stops paying attention.
After hours of studying, I could feel my eyes start to glaze over.
His eyes would glaze over every time someone mentioned tax law.

Synset Examples

Her eyes glaze over when she is bored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - to look fixedly with eyes wide open
