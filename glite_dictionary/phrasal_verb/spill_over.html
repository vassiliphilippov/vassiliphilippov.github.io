to spill over

1. VERB

/ spɪl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / spɪl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of liquid, people, events, etc.) to overflow the boundaries of something; to extend beyond the usual limits.
overflow or spread beyond boundaries

Examples

The crowd began to spill over into the neighboring streets as the concert ended.
Emotions can sometimes spill over from work into personal life.
Water started to spill over the rim of the bathtub when nobody was watching.

Synset Examples

The crowds spilled out into the streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to flow or spread over the edge or limit
  • Antonym: to contain - to keep within limits or boundaries
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02074078-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spɪl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / spɪl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an emotion) to become so strong that it cannot be contained and is openly displayed.
(of emotion) become uncontainable and expressed

Examples

Her excitement began to spill over during the award ceremony, and she could not stop smiling.
Sometimes his frustration would spill over into angry words during meetings.
The joy in the stadium spilled over into the streets as fans celebrated the victory.

Synset Examples

The children bubbled over with joy
My boss was bubbling over with anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bubble over - overflow with emotion or excitement

Synset POS: v
