to gouge the customer

1. VERB

/ ɡaʊdʒ ðə ˈkʌstəmɚ / (AmE), / ɡaʊdʒ ðə ˈkʌstəmə / (BrE)
to charge a customer excessively or unfairly, especially by raising prices unfairly
charge customers unfairly or excessively

Examples

It's unethical for companies to gouge the customer during emergencies.
Some airlines have been accused of gouging the customer with hidden fees.
We refuse to gouge the customer just to increase our profits.

Synset Examples

Don't gouge the customers just to make a profit.
He was accused of gouging prices during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcharge - to charge someone too much money for something
  • Antonym: undercharge - to charge too little for something

Etymology

From the verb 'gouge', meaning to overcharge, which originates from Old French 'gouge' (chisel, gouge), referring metaphorically to cutting into prices.
