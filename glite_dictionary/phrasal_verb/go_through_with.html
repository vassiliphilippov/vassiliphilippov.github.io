to go through with

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈθruː wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈθruː wɪð / (BrE)
to do or finish something that you have promised or planned to do, especially when it is difficult or unpleasant
finish something despite difficulty

Examples

Despite her doubts, she decided to go through with the surgery.
He almost canceled the presentation but managed to go through with it at the last minute.
They wondered if the city council would actually go through with the proposed changes.

Synset Examples

Did he go through with the treatment?
He implemented a new economic plan
She followed up his recommendations with a written proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry out - to perform or complete a task or plan

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈθruː wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈθruː wɪð / (BrE)
to continue with and complete something that has been planned or decided, especially when it is difficult or you are uncertain
to complete something planned despite difficulty

Examples

Despite her doubts, she finally decided to go through with the wedding.
I'm not sure I can go through with the surgery, but I know it's necessary.
They didn't want to move, but they had to go through with their plans.

Synset Examples

She decided to go through with the surgery despite her fears.
They have to go through with their commitments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry out - to perform or complete a task or promise
