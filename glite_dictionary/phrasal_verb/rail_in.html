to rail in

1. VERB

/ reɪl ɪn / (AmE), / reɪl ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose or surround something with rails or fencing
enclose with rails or fencing

Examples

The cemetery is railed in to prevent animals from entering.
They decided to rail in the playground for the children's safety.
He railed in his vegetable garden to keep out rabbits.

Synset Examples
