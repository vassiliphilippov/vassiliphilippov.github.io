to shade off

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪd ɑf / (AmE), / ʃeɪd ɒf / (BrE)
to cast a shadow over something, partially blocking light; to make an area less bright or less distinct by casting shade
cast a shadow over

Examples

Tall trees shade off the patio during the hottest hours of the day.
The mountains shade off part of the valley every evening as the sun sets.
She chose a spot where a large umbrella shaded off her table from the afternoon sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade - to block light from reaching an area
  • Antonym: to illuminate - to light up; to make brighter
