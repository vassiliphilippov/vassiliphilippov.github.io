to feel bad for

1. VERB

/ fil bæd fər / (AmE), / fiːl bæd fə / (BrE)
to experience sympathy, guilt, or remorse about someone or something
experience sympathy or remorse

Examples

I feel bad for leaving you alone last night.
She feels bad for what happened during the meeting.
They felt bad for not inviting him to the party.

Synset Examples

I always feel bad for forgetting her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel sorry for - to experience pity or sympathy for someone
