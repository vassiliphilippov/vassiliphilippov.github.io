to make for

1. VERB

/ meɪk fər / (AmE), / meɪk fə / (BrE)
to result in or cause a particular outcome or situation
to cause or result in

Examples

A good education can make for a successful career.
Heavy traffic does not make for a relaxing commute.
Fresh ingredients make for a tastier meal.

Synset Examples

2. VERB

/ meɪk fɔr / (AmE), / meɪk fə / (BrE)
to move toward or head in the direction of something
move toward a place or direction

Examples

After the meeting, she decided to make for the nearest coffee shop.
The hikers quickly made for the shelter as the storm approached.
When the alarm sounded, everyone made for the exits.
