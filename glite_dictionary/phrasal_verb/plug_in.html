to plug in

1. VERB

/ plʌɡ ɪn / (AmE), / plʌɡ ɪn / (BrE)
to connect a piece of electrical equipment to a power supply by inserting its plug into a socket
connect electrical device to power supply

Examples

Before you use the blender, make sure to plug in the cord.
She forgot to plug in her phone last night, so the battery was dead this morning.
Can you plug in the lamp? It's too dark in here.

Synset Examples

Please plug in the toaster!
Connect the TV so we can watch the football game tonight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unplug - to disconnect electrical equipment from a power supply

2. VERB

/ plʌɡ ɪn / (AmE), / plʌɡ ɪn / (BrE)
To connect an electrical device to a power source by inserting its plug; also, to become involved with or connected to an activity, organization, or system.
connect to power or become involved

Examples

Before you use the blender, make sure to plug in the cord.
At the conference, she encouraged participants to plug in to the latest developments in the field.
If your speakers aren't working, did you remember to plug in both wires?

Synset Examples

Make sure to plug in the device for power.
I need to plug in my phone before it dies.
Don't forget to plug in your laptop before class.
It's important to plug into new trends in technology.
You can plug into the network easily.
She decided to plug into the community volunteer program.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unplug - to disconnect from a power source, or to remove oneself from involvement
