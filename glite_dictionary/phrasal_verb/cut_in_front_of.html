to cut in front of

1. VERB

/ kʌt ɪn frʌnt ʌv / (AmE), / kʌt ɪn frʌnt ɒv / (BrE)
to suddenly move into a position ahead of someone, usually rudely or without warning, especially while driving or waiting in line.
move ahead of someone abruptly

Examples

The cyclist nearly fell when a car cut in front of him at the intersection.
Please don't cut in front of people when you're waiting in line.
I was upset when someone cut in front of me at the checkout.

Synset Examples

I was driving when another car suddenly cut in front of me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - to suddenly move in front of someone and force them to slow down or stop, especially in traffic
