to dash off

1. VERB

/ dæʃ ɔf / (AmE), / dæʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to write or create something quickly, often without careful attention or detail
write quickly without much care

Examples

She dashed off a quick note before leaving for work.
The reporter dashed off the article in less than an hour.
He often dashes off emails without checking for mistakes.

Synset Examples

She dashed off a letter to her lawyer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - to write hastily or carelessly
  • Antonym: to compose - to create or put together carefully
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01022603-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dæʃ ɑf / (AmE), / dæʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to write or compose something quickly and without much thought
write quickly and hastily

Examples

She managed to dash off a quick email before the meeting started.
He can dash off a poem in just a few minutes.
I need to dash off a letter to my grandmother this afternoon.

Synset Examples

She dashed off a note to her husband saying she would not be home for supper
He scratched off a thank-you note to the hostess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - to write hastily or carelessly
  • Antonym: to compose - to create or write, especially with care or thought
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01704514-v
Synset POS: v
