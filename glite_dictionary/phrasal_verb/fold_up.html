to fold up

1. VERB

/ foʊld ʌp / (AmE), / fəʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to bend or lay something so that one part covers another; to make compact by folding
to make (something) compact by folding

Examples

Please fold up the map before you put it back in the drawer.
She folded up the blanket and placed it on the shelf.
The chairs are easy to fold up and store when not in use.

Synset Examples

fold up the newspaper
turn up your collar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - to bend something over on itself
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01280538-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ foʊld ʌp / (AmE), / fəʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to bend or collapse into a compact form, especially by means of hinges or joints; to become folded up
to become folded or collapsed

Examples

The lawn chair is easy to fold up and store in the garage.
Many strollers fold up for convenient transport.
When the table is not in use, it can fold up against the wall.

Synset Examples

The bed folds in a jiffy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unfold - to open or spread out something that was folded
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00565772-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ foʊld ˈʌp / (AmE), / fəʊld ˈʌp / (BrE)
to collapse or neatly put away something, especially by bending or arranging its parts together
to collapse or put away neatly

Examples

Could you fold up the blanket and put it on the shelf?
After the picnic, we need to fold up the chairs and tables.
The stroller is designed to fold up for easy storage in the car.

Synset Examples

She decided to fold up the table after dinner.
We should fold up the chairs before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in, often suddenly
