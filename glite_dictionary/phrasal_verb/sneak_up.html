to sneak up

1. VERB

/ snik ʌp / (AmE), / sniːk ʌp / (BrE)
to approach someone or something stealthily and without being noticed
approach stealthily and unnoticed

Examples

The cat tried to sneak up on the birds in the garden.
Watch out—someone might sneak up behind you as a prank.
Mistakes can sneak up on you if you're not careful.

Synset Examples

Age creeps up on you

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to creep up - to move slowly and quietly towards someone or something, often unnoticed
