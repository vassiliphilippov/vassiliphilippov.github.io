to knock out

1. VERB

/ nɑk aʊt / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone become unconscious, especially by hitting them hard
make unconscious by hitting

Examples

The boxer managed to knock out his rival in the third round.
He was accidentally knocked out when the ball hit his head.
The medicine was so strong that it almost knocked out the patient.

Synset Examples

the boxing champion knocked out his opponent in a few seconds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stun - to make someone unconscious or unable to think clearly
  • Antonym: to revive - to bring someone back to consciousness
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ nɑk aʊt / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to eliminate or remove someone or something from a competition, situation, or activity, especially by defeating them
eliminate someone or something

Examples

If they win this game, they will knock out the defending champions from the tournament.
The virus was engineered to knock out a specific gene in the mice.
A single mistake could knock out even the strongest teams in the early rounds.

Synset Examples

knock out a target

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something or someone
  • Antonym: advance - to move on to the next stage, often by not being eliminated

/ nɑk aʊt / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt / (BrE)

informal

Examples
