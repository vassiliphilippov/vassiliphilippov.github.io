to gear up

1. VERB

/ ɡɪr ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare yourself, someone else, or something for an activity or event, especially by making the necessary arrangements or gathering equipment
prepare or make ready for something

Examples

The team needs to gear up for the championship match tomorrow.
We're gearing up for the big product launch next week.
As winter approaches, many homeowners gear up by insulating their houses.

Synset Examples

Get the children ready for school!
prepare for war
I was fixing to leave town after I paid the hotel bill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - make ready or get ready
  • Synonym: to equip - provide with necessary items

Etymology

Formed from "gear" (meaning equipment) + the adverb "up," used from the mid-20th century in the sense of making ready.

2. VERB

/ ɡɪr ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare oneself, equipment, or organization for a particular activity or event
prepare for an activity or event

Examples

The team needs to gear up before the project deadline approaches.
We should gear up for the busy holiday season ahead.
Many retailers gear up months in advance for major sales events.

Synset Examples

It's time to gear up for winter preparations.
We need to gear up for the upcoming competition.
They always gear up early for the annual festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
