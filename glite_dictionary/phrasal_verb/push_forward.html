to push forward

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / pʊʃ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to move ahead forcefully by making way through a crowd or obstacles, often by physically pushing others aside
move ahead by pushing through

Examples

The crowd made it difficult for her to push forward to the front of the stage.
He had to push forward through the throng to catch his train.
Despite the heavy resistance, the soldiers continued to push forward towards their objective.

Synset Examples

she barged into the meeting room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force one's way - to move forward by pushing others aside

WordNet ID: oewn-02000606-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / pʊʃ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to continue to make progress or move ahead, especially despite obstacles or difficulties
make progress despite difficulties

Examples

Despite the setbacks, our team kept pushing forward until we achieved our goals.
He faced many challenges in his career, but he never stopped pushing forward.
Even when things get tough, it's important to keep pushing forward.

Synset Examples

Life’s a struggle, but we keep pushing forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to press on - to continue moving forward or making progress, especially in a determined way
