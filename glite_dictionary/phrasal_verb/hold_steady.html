to hold steady

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / həʊld ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
to keep something or someone in a stable and unmoving position
keep stable and unmoving

Examples

Please hold steady while I take this photo.
She tried to hold steady as the boat rocked in the waves.
The technician used clamps to hold steady the equipment during the test.

Synset Examples

He used a vise to hold the wood steady while cutting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steady - firm and not moving or shaking
  • Synonym: to stabilize - to make or become stable
