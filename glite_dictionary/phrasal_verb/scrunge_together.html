to scrunge together

1. VERB

/ skrʌndʒ təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / skrʌndʒ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to gather or assemble something quickly and with minimal resources, often in a rushed or makeshift manner
hastily gather or assemble
informal

Examples

Despite the late notice, he managed to scrunge together enough money for the rent.
We had to scrunge together supplies for the impromptu picnic.
She tried to scrunge together a last-minute meal when her friends arrived unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

She tried to scrunge together a last-minute meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape together - gather together with effort, especially money or resources
  • Synonym: to scrounge - seek to obtain something, typically money or food, at the expense or through the generosity of others
