to run on

1. VERB

/ rʌn ɑn / (AmE), / rʌn ɒn / (BrE)
to continue for longer than expected or without interruption
continue uninterrupted

Examples

The meeting ran on for hours without any resolution.
She let the engine run on for a few minutes before turning it off.
His lectures often run on longer than scheduled.

Synset Examples

The disease will run on unchecked
The party kept going until 4 A.M.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02690077-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn ɑn / (AmE), / rʌn ɒn / (BrE)
to talk or narrate at excessive or unnecessary length, often without interruption or relevance
talk at unnecessary length

Examples

She tends to run on whenever she gets nervous during a presentation.
He ran on about his travels for almost an hour without taking a breath.
If you run on like this in your essay, you'll lose your reader's interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramble - to talk or write in a confused or overly lengthy way
  • Synonym: to digress - to stray from the main subject in speech or writing
  • Antonym: be concise - to express oneself clearly and with brevity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00965904-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rʌn ɑn / (AmE), / rʌn ɒn / (BrE)
to be powered by a particular fuel or to rely on something as a source of energy or motivation
be powered by or depend on

Examples

Most modern cars run on unleaded petrol instead of diesel.
Some people seem to run on very little sleep and still function well.
This generator can run on both gasoline and propane.

Synset Examples

I can only run on coffee and adrenaline today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run off - to be powered by a particular type of energy, fuel, etc.
