to distil complex ideas

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪl kəmˈplɛks aɪˈdiəz / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl kəmˈplɛks aɪˈdɪəz / (BrE)
to simplify complicated concepts or extract the main idea or meaning from them
simplify or extract main points from complex concepts

Examples

Good teachers know how to distil complex ideas into language their students can grasp.
Her presentation managed to distil complex ideas without losing any important details.
In academic writing, it's crucial to distil complex ideas into concise arguments.

Synset Examples

The team aims to distil complex ideas into simple terms.
It's important to distil the essence of the story.
She can distil her thoughts into concise phrases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraphrase - express the meaning using different words, especially simplification
  • Antonym: elaborate - explain in more detail (opposite process, making more complex)

Etymology

From Latin 'destillare' (to trickle down), figuratively used with 'distil' meaning to extract the essence.
