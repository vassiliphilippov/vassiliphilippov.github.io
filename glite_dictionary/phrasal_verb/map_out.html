to map out

1. VERB

/ mæp aʊt / (AmE), / mæp aʊt / (BrE)
to plan or arrange something carefully and in detail, often before taking action
plan or arrange in detail

Examples

She mapped out her career goals before graduating from university.
The architect carefully mapped out every stage of the building project.
Before starting the novel, the writer mapped out the entire plot on paper.

Synset Examples

map one's future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
  • Synonym: devise - to invent or plan something carefully

2. VERB

/ mæp aʊt / (AmE), / mæp aʊt / (BrE)
to carefully plan or arrange the details of something in advance
to plan or arrange in detail

Examples

We need to map out our strategy before approaching the investors.
She carefully mapped out her schedule for the week to stay organized.
The team is mapping out the steps needed to complete the project.

Synset Examples

The plan was perfect, all mapped out in his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
  • Antonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously

Etymology

From the noun 'map' (a representation of an area) and the verbal particle 'out', meaning to lay out as on a map.
