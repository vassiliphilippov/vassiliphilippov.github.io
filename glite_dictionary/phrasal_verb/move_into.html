to move into

1. VERB

/ muːv ˈɪntu / (AmE), / muːv ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to enter or go inside a place or area; to begin to occupy or inhabit a space.
to enter a place or begin to occupy it

Examples

They plan to move into the new office next week.
As the storm approached, heavy rain began to move into the city.
After graduation, she decided to move into a small apartment downtown.

Synset Examples

the boat entered an area of shallow marshes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enter - to come or go into a place
