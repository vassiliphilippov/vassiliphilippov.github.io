to catch it from

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ɪt frəm / (AmE), / kætʃ ɪt frɒm / (BrE)
(informal) To be reprimanded, punished, or scolded by someone, especially in response to some wrongdoing or mistake.
be reprimanded or punished by someone
informal

Examples

If you forget your homework again, you're going to catch it from Miss Harris.
He knew he would catch it from his parents when they found out about the broken window.
We all caught it from the boss after missing the deadline.

Synset Examples

If you skip class, you’ll really catch it from the teacher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get in trouble with - to cause someone to become annoyed or angry at you; to be reprimanded
