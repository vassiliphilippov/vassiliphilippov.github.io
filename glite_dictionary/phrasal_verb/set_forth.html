to set forth

1. VERB

/ sɛt fɔrθ / (AmE), / sɛt fɔːθ / (BrE)
to begin a journey; to depart for a particular place
to begin a journey

Examples

The explorers will set forth at dawn on their expedition.
They set forth for Paris early in the morning to avoid the traffic.
After saying goodbye to her family, she set forth on her new adventure.

Synset Examples

The family took off for Florida

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially to start a journey
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a destination after a journey
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02018017-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛt ˈfɔrθ / (AmE), / sɛt ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
to state or present something clearly and formally, especially in writing or speech
state or present formally

Examples

The terms of the contract are clearly set forth in this document.
She set forth her ideas during the meeting with great clarity.
The rules were set forth at the beginning of the course.

Synset Examples

set forth one's reasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: state - to express something formally in speech or writing
  • Synonym: to announce - to make something known publicly or officially
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01003339-v
Synset POS: v
