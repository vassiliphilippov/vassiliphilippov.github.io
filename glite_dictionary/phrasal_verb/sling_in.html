to sling in

1. VERB

/ slɪŋ ɪn / (AmE), / slɪŋ ɪn / (BrE)
to add or include something quickly and without much care
add or include quickly
informal

Examples

Could you sling in a few more snacks for the road trip?
She slung in a last-minute reference to impress the interviewer.
Let me sling in an extra towel just in case you need it.

Synset Examples

Can you sling in a few more details to the report?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw in - to add something as an extra
  • Synonym: to toss in - to put something in quickly or carelessly

Etymology

From "sling" meaning to throw, of Scandinavian origin; phrasal verb construction with "in".
