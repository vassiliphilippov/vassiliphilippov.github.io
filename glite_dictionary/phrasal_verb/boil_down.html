to boil down

1. VERB

/ bɔɪl daʊn / (AmE), / bɔɪl daʊn / (BrE)
(of an idea, situation, or statement) to have as its main or most important aspect.
have as the main or essential aspect

Examples

The issue boils down to whether we can afford it or not.
Her explanation boiled down to a simple request for help.
It all boils down to making the right decision at the right time.

Synset Examples

The proposal boils down to a compromise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sum up - express the essential aspects in a succinct way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00238590-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɔɪl daʊn / (AmE), / bɔɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to cook food or liquid until most of the liquid has evaporated, leaving a thick or concentrated mixture
reduce by boiling to concentrate

Examples

Simmer the sauce and let it boil down until it thickens.
The fruit mixture needs to boil down before you make the jam.
The broth will boil down to a rich, flavorful base after several hours.

Synset Examples

The cook reduced the sauce by boiling it for a long time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to decrease in volume by boiling
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00237885-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɔɪl daʊn / (AmE), / bɔɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to cook a liquid until much of it has evaporated, leaving a thicker or more concentrated substance
reduce by boiling (cooking)

Examples

Simmer the mixture and let it boil down until slightly thickened.
The fruit was boiled down to make a rich, sweet syrup.
Make sure the stock boils down to enhance its flavor before serving.

Synset Examples

The sauce should reduce to one cup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make a liquid more concentrated by boiling away water
