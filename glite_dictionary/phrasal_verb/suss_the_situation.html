to suss the situation

1. VERB

/ sʌs ðə sɪˈtʃueɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌs ðə sɪtʃuˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
to figure out or understand what is happening in a particular situation, especially by observing or thinking carefully
figure out or understand a situation
informal british

Examples

It took him a few minutes to suss the situation and respond appropriately.
Detectives tried to suss the situation before making any arrests.
Can you suss the situation and tell me what's really going on?

Synset Examples

She can suss the situation quickly and effectively.
Let’s suss this problem before it escalates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to figure out - to understand or solve something
  • Synonym: to suss out - to realize or discover the truth about someone or something

Etymology

Informal; 'suss' is from British slang, possibly from 'suspect' or abbreviation of 'suspicion'.
