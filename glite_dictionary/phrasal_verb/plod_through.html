to plod through

1. VERB

/ plɑd θruː / (AmE), / plɒd θruː / (BrE)
to work or move slowly and steadily through a task or series of tasks, often with effort or perseverance
work through something slowly and steadily

Examples

She had to plod through hundreds of pages before finishing the report.
Even when tired, he continued to plod through his daily chores.
They plodded through the paperwork late into the night.

Synset Examples

He would plod through the assignments one by one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - continue in a course of action despite difficulty
