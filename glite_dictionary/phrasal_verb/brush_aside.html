to brush aside

1. VERB

/ brʌʃ əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / brʌʃ əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
To refuse to consider or acknowledge something or someone; to dismiss as unimportant.
dismiss or disregard; not consider

Examples

She tried to explain her side, but he simply brushed aside her concerns.
The company brushed aside criticism from the media and continued with its plans.
He brushed aside my suggestion without even listening to it.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - refuse to consider; disregard
  • Synonym: to ignore - intentionally pay no attention to something

2. VERB

/ brʌʃ əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / brʌʃ əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to dismiss or ignore something, treating it as unimportant
dismiss or ignore as unimportant

Examples

The manager chose to brush aside all criticisms and focus on the project.
He tried to brush aside her questions, but she insisted on an answer.
It's not wise to brush aside concerns about your health.

Synset Examples

Don’t brush aside your health; it’s very important.
She decided to brush aside her worries for the day.
He tried to brush aside the negative comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
  • Synonym: to ignore - to refuse to pay attention to something or someone
