to sort out

1. VERB

/ sɔrt aʊt / (AmE), / sɔːt aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or organize things into groups or categories; to classify or put in order
arrange or classify by category

Examples

It took me hours to sort out all the paperwork into the right folders.
The teacher asked the students to sort out the books by subject.
We need to sort out the donated clothes before sending them to charity.

Synset Examples

How would you classify these pottery shards — are they prehistoric?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange systematically; put in order
  • Synonym: to categorize - to place in a particular class or group

2. VERB

/ sɔrt aʊt / (AmE), / sɔːt aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve a problem or difficulty, or make something clear by dealing with confusion or lack of order
make clear; resolve confusion or difficulty

Examples

It took several hours to sort out the misunderstanding between the two teams.
Let me sort out these files so we can find what we need.
She finally sorted out her travel plans after a lot of confusion.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - make something clear or easier to understand
  • Synonym: resolve - find a solution to a problem or difficulty
  • Antonym: to confuse - cause someone to become bewildered or unclear

3. VERB

/ sɔrt aʊt / (AmE), / sɔːt aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve or clarify problems, difficulties, or confusion
resolve or clarify problems

Examples

We need to sort out the misunderstanding before the meeting starts.
After reviewing the documents, she managed to sort out the billing errors.
The committee met to sort out the details of the new policy.

Synset Examples

They need to sort out their differences quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - find a solution to a problem or difficulty
  • Synonym: to clear up - make something less confused or uncertain
