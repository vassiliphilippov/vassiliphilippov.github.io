to parcel up

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːr.səl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.səl ʌp / (BrE)
to wrap or package items together, typically for delivery or storage
to package or wrap items together

Examples

Could you parcel up the gifts before the guests arrive?
After sorting through his things, he parceled up his old clothes for donation.
She spent the afternoon parcelling up the books to mail to her friend.

Synset Examples

We need to parcel up these items carefully for shipping.
Please parcel up the orders for delivery tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack up - to put things into a container or package, often for moving or travel
  • Antonym: to unwrap - to remove the wrapping or packaging from something
