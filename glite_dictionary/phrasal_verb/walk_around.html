to walk around

1. VERB

/ wɔk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to walk in a place without a specific purpose or direction, often for leisure or exploration
move about on foot without a fixed aim

Examples

We decided to walk around the city and explore its hidden streets.
She likes to walk around the park in the mornings to clear her mind.
Tourists often walk around the area to discover interesting shops and cafes.

Synset Examples

We were walking around in the neighborhood to see whether we could find an open drugstore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɑk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move on foot so as to go around the edge or perimeter of something, rather than through or over it.
move on foot around an object

Examples

We had to walk around the construction site to get to the park.
She decided to walk around the puddle instead of stepping through it.
Tourists walk around the old city walls every evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circle - to move in a circle or round something
  • Antonym: to walk through - to move on foot through the inside or middle of something
