to roll out

1. VERB

/ roʊl aʊt / (AmE), / rəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to make something flat or straight by unrolling it
unroll to make flat or straight

Examples

She asked him to roll out the rug before putting the furniture in place.
We rolled out the blueprint on the table to examine the details.
Could you roll out the poster so everyone can see it?

/ roʊl aʊt / (AmE), / rəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to introduce or distribute something systematically or strategically, especially to the public or a large area

The company will roll out its latest smartphone in several countries this month.

3. VERB

/ roʊl aʊt / (AmE), / rəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to officially introduce and distribute a new product or service to the public for the first time
officially launch or distribute a new product/service

Examples

The company will roll out its new smartphone model next month.
After a successful pilot phase, the government began to roll out the vaccination program nationwide.
They plan to roll out the streaming service in Europe by the end of the year.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to introduce a new product or service to the public
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove a product or service from availability

Etymology

From 'roll' (to turn over) and 'out', in the sense of unrolling something to reveal or begin using it; used figuratively for product launches from early 20th century.
