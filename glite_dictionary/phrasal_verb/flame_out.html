to flame out

1. VERB

/ fleɪm aʊt / (AmE), / fleɪm aʊt / (BrE)
to fail or come to an end suddenly and dramatically, especially after a promising start; to lose energy or interest suddenly
to fail or lose momentum suddenly

Examples

If you push yourself too hard, you might flame out before reaching your goals.
The promising new tech company flamed out after just one year in business.
Many athletes flame out early in their careers due to injuries or burnout.

Synset Examples

If you don't manage stress, you might flame out before the deadline.
The project started strong but began to flame out quickly.
His enthusiasm for the job flamed out after a few months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burn out - to lose effectiveness, energy, or enthusiasm due to stress or overwork

Etymology

Originally an aviation term describing a jet engine's sudden loss of power due to flame extinction, now used figuratively.
