to cotton up

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑtən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən ʌp / (BrE)
to try to ingratiate oneself with someone, often in a way that is insincere
ingratiate oneself with someone
informal

Examples

He always tries to cotton up to the boss when he wants a day off.
I could tell she was cottoning up to the teacher for a better grade.
Stop cottoning up to me—I know you want something.

Synset Examples

She is playing up to the chairman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play up to - to try to win someone's favor by behaving in a flattering way
  • Synonym: suck up to - to ingratiate oneself with someone in a way that is obvious or insincere
  • Synonym: to butter up - to flatter someone excessively to gain favor

Etymology

From 'cotton' (v.), 16th-century English slang for to be in agreement or get on well with someone.
