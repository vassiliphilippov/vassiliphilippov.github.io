to write off

1. VERB

/ raɪt ɔf / (AmE), / raɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to compose text fluently and without hesitation, often quickly or with little effort
compose text fluently and quickly

Examples

She can write off a three-page report in under an hour if she's focused.
The novelist managed to write off another chapter before lunch.
Without overthinking, he would often write off witty responses to readers' letters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dash off - to write something quickly and carelessly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01704399-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ raɪt ɔf / (AmE), / raɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to officially cancel a debt or an amount of money owed, recognizing it as uncollectible
cancel a debt or financial loss

Examples

The bank decided to write off the loan after repeated attempts to collect payments failed.
After the company went bankrupt, several large debts were written off by creditors.
It took years, but eventually, they had to write off the remaining balance as a loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forgive - to stop requiring repayment of a debt; to pardon financially
  • Synonym: cancel - to annul or invalidate, including financial obligations
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02483078-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ raɪt ɔf / (AmE), / raɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to reduce the official or estimated value of an asset, especially in financial records, often because it is no longer considered useful or recoverable
reduce asset's recorded value (accounting)

Examples

The company decided to write off several pieces of outdated equipment at the end of the year.
After the car accident, the insurance had to write off the vehicle as a total loss.
Many businesses write off bad debts in their annual financial statements.

Synset Examples

For tax purposes you can write off the laser printer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depreciate - to lower the value of something over time, often for accounting purposes

Etymology

From 'write' + 'off', originally accounting terminology referring to making an entry in financial records.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00316757-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ raɪt ɔf / (AmE), / raɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to accept that a loss, debt, or failure cannot be recovered, or to regard something or someone as not worth further consideration
accept as a loss; dismiss as worthless

Examples

After months of trying to fix the old car, he finally decided to write it off as a loss.
The accountant suggested that the company should write off the bad debts at the end of the year.
It would be a mistake to write off her chances after just one poor performance.

Synset Examples

write it off as a loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
  • Synonym: discount - to regard something as unworthy of consideration or as less important

Etymology

From the phrase 'to write off' in accounting, meaning to cancel out an item as a loss.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00594758-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ ˈraɪt ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to officially record an asset as having no value or an amount as unlikely to be collected, usually for accounting or tax purposes
declare an asset or debt as worthless

Examples

The company decided to write off the unsold merchandise after the holiday season.
Due to non-payment, the bank was forced to write off millions in loans.
After repeated attempts to collect, they chose to write off the outstanding debts.

Synset Examples

The accountant suggested we write off the old equipment.
I had to write off that debt as a loss.
She decided to write off the bad investment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to write down - to reduce the recorded value of an asset
