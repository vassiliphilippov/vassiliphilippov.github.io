to get stuck on

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt stʌk ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt stʌk ɒn / (BrE)
to focus excessively on something, especially a minor or trivial issue, often becoming unable to move past it
to fixate on a trivial issue

Examples

Sometimes teams get stuck on details that don't really matter.
Try not to get stuck on minor mistakes and focus on the bigger picture.
She tends to get stuck on a single point and can't move forward until it's resolved.

Synset Examples

Let's not get stuck on this bikeshed; we have bigger problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwell on - to think or talk about something for a long time, often more than is necessary
  • Synonym: obsess over - to think about something unceasingly or persistently
