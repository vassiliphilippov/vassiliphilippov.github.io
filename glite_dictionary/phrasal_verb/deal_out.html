to deal out

1. VERB

/ diːl aʊt / (AmE), / diːl aʊt / (BrE)
to distribute or give something, especially in portions or to several people
distribute or give out

Examples

She began to deal out the cards to each player at the table.
The manager tried to deal out praise to everyone who contributed to the project.
Volunteers will deal out food and blankets to the people affected by the storm.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give something out to several people or places
  • Antonym: collect - to gather things together, especially from different places or people

2. VERB

/ diːl aʊt / (AmE), / diːl aʊt / (BrE)
to give or distribute something in portions to several people, especially cards or roles
distribute or give out portions

Examples

The teacher dealt out the examination papers to the class.
He dealt out the cards before the game began.
Justice was dealt out swiftly to those who broke the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give something to each member of a group
  • Antonym: collect - to gather together; the opposite action of giving out

Etymology

From Middle English *delen* (to divide) and out, referring to the action of giving out cards.
