to dry up

1. VERB

/ ˌdraɪ ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌdraɪ ˈʌp / (BrE)
to lose water or moisture; to become completely dry
lose water or moisture

Examples

The river began to dry up during the long summer drought.
All the paint will dry up if you leave the can open overnight.
His creativity seemed to dry up after years of working nonstop.

Synset Examples

In the desert, you get dehydrated very quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehydrate - to lose or remove water from; to become dry
  • Antonym: to moisten - to make or become slightly wet
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00211534-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ draɪ ˈʌp / (AmE), / draɪ ˈʌp / (BrE)
to lose all moisture and become shriveled or withered
lose all moisture and shrink

Examples

The lake began to dry up during the drought, leaving cracked mud behind.
If you don’t water the plant, it will dry up and die.
After months without rain, the fields have completely dried up.

Synset Examples

a mummified body was found

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wither - to become dry and shriveled
  • Synonym: to shrivel - to contract and wrinkle from loss of moisture
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00243091-v
Synset POS: v
