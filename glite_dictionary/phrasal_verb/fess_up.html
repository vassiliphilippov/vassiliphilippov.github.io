to fess up

1. VERB

/ fɛs ʌp / (AmE), / fɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to admit or acknowledge something, especially a wrongdoing or mistake
admit a wrongdoing or error

Examples

After hours of questioning, he finally fessed up to breaking the window.
It's better to fess up now than to be caught in a lie later.
She fessed up about her mistake during the meeting.

Synset Examples

the writer of the anonymous letter owned up after they identified his handwriting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to own up - to admit or confess to something one has done
  • Synonym: to confess - to admit that you have done something wrong or illegal
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true, or refuse to admit

Etymology

Shortened from 'confess up', dating from early 20th-century American English.

2. VERB

/ fɛs ʌp / (AmE), / fɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to admit or confess to something, especially reluctantly or after being prompted
admit or confess to something
informal

Examples

If you broke the vase, you should just fess up and take responsibility.
She finally decided to fess up about what happened at the meeting.
After hours of questioning, he fessed up to taking the money.

Synset Examples

You should fess up before it gets worse.
It's time to fess up about your mistake.
He finally decided to fess up to his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit to something, especially a fault or crime

Etymology

Shortened from 'confess,' originally from Latin 'confessus.'
