to stand pat

1. VERB

/ stænd pæt / (AmE), / stænd pæt / (BrE)
to refuse to change one's opinion, decision, or position; to remain firm or stubborn in one's stance
refuse to change one's opinion or position

Examples

Despite criticism from her colleagues, she decided to stand pat on her decision.
The negotiators will stand pat until the other side makes a concession.
Even in the face of new evidence, he stood pat on his original statement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to give in - to yield to pressure or persuasion; to abandon one's position
  • Synonym: to hold firm - to maintain one's opinion or position firmly

Etymology

Originally a poker term from American English, with 'pat' meaning a hand that is satisfactory as dealt.
