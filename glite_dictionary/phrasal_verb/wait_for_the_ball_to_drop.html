to wait for the ball to drop

1. VERB

/ weɪt fər ðə bɔl tə drɑp / (AmE), / weɪt fə ðə bɔːl tə drɒp / (BrE)
to wait for an important or decisive event to happen, often with anticipation or uncertainty
await a decisive or important event

Examples

We're all just waiting for the ball to drop before making a final decision.
Investors are waiting for the ball to drop regarding the new regulations.
She didn't want to celebrate too soon and decided to wait for the ball to drop first.

Synset Examples

I’ll wait for the ball to drop before celebrating.
Don't jump to conclusions, wait for the ball to drop.
She prefers to wait for the ball to drop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wait for the other shoe to drop - to wait for an expected event, especially something bad or inevitable
