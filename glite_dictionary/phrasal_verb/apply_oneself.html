to apply oneself

1. VERB

/ əˈplaɪ ˌwʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / əˈplaɪ ˌwʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to work hard and give your full attention to a particular task or activity
to dedicate effort; work hard on something

Examples

If you really apply yourself, you can achieve great results in this course.
She decided to apply herself to learning French before her trip to Paris.
John didn't apply himself last year, but this year he is much more focused.

Synset Examples

He realized he needed to apply himself to his studies.
If you apply yourself, success will follow.
To master a skill, you must apply yourself diligently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work hard - to put a lot of effort into your work
  • Antonym: to slack off - to avoid work or not put in effort
