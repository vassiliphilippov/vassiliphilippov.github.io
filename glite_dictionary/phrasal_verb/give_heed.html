to give heed

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv hid / (AmE), / ɡɪv hiːd / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to something or someone; to take notice
pay attention or take notice

Examples

It is wise to give heed to expert advice when making big decisions.
He refused to give heed to the repeated warnings and acted recklessly.
We must give heed to the needs of the community if we want to succeed.

Synset Examples

He didn't give heed to her advice and regretted it.
You should give heed to the warnings about the storm.
Always give heed to your gut feelings.
It's wise to pay heed to your mentor's advice.
He didn't pay heed to the warning signs and got lost.
You should pay heed to the weather forecast before traveling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pay attention - to focus on or heed something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention
  • Synonym: heed - to pay careful attention to
