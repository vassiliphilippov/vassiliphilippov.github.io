to buy it

1. VERB

/ baɪ ɪt / (AmE), / baɪ ɪt / (BrE)
To believe or accept something as true, especially a statement or explanation that may be doubtful.
accept as true (informal)
informal

Examples

I'm not going to buy it—his story sounds too suspicious.
She told him she was sick, but he didn't buy it for a second.
They claimed the product would double your energy, but I didn't buy it.

Synset Examples

She didn't buy it when he said he was late.
Don't buy it; he's just trying to manipulate you.
I almost bought it when he told that story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall for - be deceived by; believe something false
  • Synonym: swallow - to accept an idea or explanation without question
  • Antonym: to see through - recognize the true nature of a trick or lie

Etymology

Figurative extension of 'buy' meaning to accept or believe (an idea, story, etc.), attested from mid-20th century.
