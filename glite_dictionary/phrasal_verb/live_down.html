to live down

1. VERB

/ lɪv daʊn / (AmE), / lɪv daʊn / (BrE)
to behave or continue to live in such a way that people eventually forget or forgive a past mistake, failure, or embarrassment
make people forget a previous mistake or shame

Examples

After making that embarrassing speech, he knew he might never live down the incident.
She worked hard to live down her early mistakes at the company.
It took years before the scandal finally started to live down in the public's memory.

Synset Examples

You can never live this down!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outlive - to live longer than something or someone

2. VERB

/ lɪv daʊn / (AmE), / lɪv daʊn / (BrE)
to succeed in making others forget or overlook a previous embarrassing or shameful action or reputation
outlive shame or embarrassment from past actions

Examples

No matter how hard he tried, Mark could never live down his embarrassing fall at the wedding.
She hopes that volunteering in the community will help her live down her past mistakes.
Their company is still living down the scandal from several years ago.

Synset Examples

He couldn't live down his college days of partying.
She tried to live down her embarrassing mistake.
It's hard to live down that reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get over - to recover from an unpleasant experience or emotion
  • Synonym: to blot out - to try to forget something unpleasant
