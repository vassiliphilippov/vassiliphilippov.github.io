to get along

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to have a harmonious or friendly relationship with someone
have a good relationship

Examples

Despite their differences, the two colleagues get along remarkably well.
Do you get along with your new neighbors?
I've always gotten along easily with people from different backgrounds.

Synset Examples

My boss and I get along very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get on - have a harmonious or friendly relationship with someone
  • Antonym: to fall out - to have a disagreement or break in a relationship

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to manage or progress in a particular situation; to make progress
manage or progress

Examples

How are you getting along in your new job?
