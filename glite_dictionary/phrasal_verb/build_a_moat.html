to build a moat

1. VERB

/ bɪld ə moʊt / (AmE), / bɪld ə məʊt / (BrE)
to construct a deep, wide ditch around a building, often filled with water, especially for defense
construct a defensive ditch around a building

Examples

The king ordered his men to build a moat around the new palace for protection.
In medieval times, it was common to build a moat to deter invaders.
The villagers gathered together to build a moat as part of the fortress defenses.

Synset Examples

They built a moat around the castle for defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moat - a deep, wide ditch, usually filled with water, surrounding a castle or fort for defense
