to give birth

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv bɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɪv bɜːθ / (BrE)
to cause something, such as an idea, movement, or event, to come into existence; to originate or produce something new
to originate or create (figurative)

Examples

The new theory gave birth to a whole branch of mathematics.
Her innovative approach gave birth to a successful company.
The artist's experiences gave birth to a powerful body of work.

Synset Examples

Marx and Engels gave birth to communism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to originate - to begin, arise, or be caused; to start
  • Synonym: to conceive - to form or develop an idea in the mind
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01635501-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv bɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɪv bɜːθ / (BrE)
to produce a baby or young animal from one's body
to produce offspring

Examples

After a long labor, she finally gave birth to a healthy baby boy.
Most mammals give birth to live young rather than laying eggs.
The vet said the dog would give birth within the next twenty-four hours.

Synset Examples

My wife had twins yesterday!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - to give birth to (used more formally or in past tense)
  • Synonym: to deliver - to give birth (medical/formal usage)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00056644-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈbɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈbɜːθ / (BrE)
to produce a baby or young animal from the body; to deliver a child
to deliver a baby

Examples

She hopes to give birth to her child at home with her family present.
Many animals migrate to safe places before they give birth to their young.
The doctor was on call the night Maria gave birth to twins.

Synset Examples

She plans to give birth in a few weeks.
She hopes to give birth at home this time.
The hospital is prepared for her to give birth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deliver - to assist in the birth of a baby or to bring forth a baby
