to center on

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛnɾɚ ɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə ɒn / (BrE)
to have as the main subject or focus; to concentrate attention or interest on something or someone
focus on; have as main subject

Examples

The meeting will center on the company's plans for expansion next year.
Her research centers on climate change and its global impact.
Much of the film's plot centers on the mystery surrounding the old mansion.

Synset Examples

Her entire attention centered on her children
Our day revolved around our work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to focus on - to direct attention or interest towards something

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛntɚ ɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə ɒn / (BrE)
To have something or someone as the main focus or central point.
have something as the main focus

Examples

The discussion centered on the upcoming election and its potential outcomes.
Her research centers on the effects of climate change on agriculture.
The documentary centered on the lives of artists in New York City.

Synset Examples

The region centers on Charleston

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to focus on - to concentrate attention or effort on something
  • Synonym: to revolve around - to be mainly concerned with or depend on something
