to cling to

1. VERB

/ klɪŋ tu / (AmE), / klɪŋ tə / (BrE)
to hold tightly to someone or something, usually with one's hands or arms, often out of fear, need, or affection
hold tightly to someone or something

Examples

The frightened child began to cling to her mother's leg during the thunderstorm.
Even as the train pulled away, he continued to cling to the door handle.
Some people cling to old traditions, refusing to accept change.

Synset Examples

She clutched my arm when she got scared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold on to - to keep a firm grip on something
  • Antonym: to let go of - to release one's grip on something

Etymology

From Old English 'clingan' (to adhere or stick); 'to' added as a preposition in phrasal verb form.

2. VERB

/ klɪŋ tu / (AmE), / klɪŋ tə / (BrE)
to hold tightly onto something or continue to believe or feel something strongly, especially in difficult situations
hold onto something firmly; remain attached to beliefs

Examples

Even after all these years, she still clings to her childhood memories.
The wet shirt clung to his skin in the rain.
Many people cling to their routines when facing uncertainty.

Synset Examples

The fabric will cling to your body if it's wet.
They cling to their beliefs even when challenged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold on to - to keep holding something firmly
  • Antonym: to let go of - to stop holding something; to release
