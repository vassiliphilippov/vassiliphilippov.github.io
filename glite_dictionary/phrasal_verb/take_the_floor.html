to take the floor

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə flɔr / (AmE), / teɪk ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to stand up and walk onto the dance floor in order to dance
stand up to dance

Examples

As the band began to play, several couples took the floor with graceful enthusiasm.
It's tradition for the bride and groom to take the floor for the first dance.
He nervously waited for his cue before taking the floor at the ballroom competition.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01987522-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk ðə flɔr / (AmE), / teɪk ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to stand up and begin speaking in a formal meeting or debate
stand up to speak in a meeting

Examples

After the chairman finished, Dr. Lee took the floor to present his findings.
Anyone wishing to take the floor must first signal to the moderator.
When she takes the floor, everyone listens attentively to her arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: address - to speak to a group; to give a speech
  • Antonym: yield the floor - to give up one's turn to speak in a formal setting
