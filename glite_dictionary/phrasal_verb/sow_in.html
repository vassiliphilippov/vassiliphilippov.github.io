to sow in

1. VERB

/ soʊ ɪn / (AmE), / səʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to plant seeds by placing them in or on the ground
plant seeds in or on soil

Examples

In early spring, gardeners often sow in lettuce seeds directly into the soil.
The farmer decided to sow in a variety of wildflowers along the edge of the field.
Before the rain arrives, we should sow in the pumpkin seeds to ensure good growth.

Synset Examples

sow the ground with sunflower seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - to put seeds, bulbs, or plants into the ground to grow
  • Antonym: harvest - to gather crops from the fields
