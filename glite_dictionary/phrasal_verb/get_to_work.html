to get to work

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tə ˈwɝk / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə ˈwɜːk / (BrE)
to begin working or start carrying out tasks, especially after a delay or interruption
begin working or start tasks

Examples

After chatting for a while, we decided it was time to get to work on the project.
She likes to get to work early so she can leave by five o'clock.
Once everyone arrived, they quickly got to work setting up the event.

Synset Examples

Stop gruzzing and just get to work already!
After the meeting, he said, 'Yookay, let's get to work!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin doing something
  • Synonym: get down to business - to begin to focus on the serious matter at hand
  • Antonym: stop working - to cease working or end work
