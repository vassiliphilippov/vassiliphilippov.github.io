to get (someone) down

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈju daʊn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈjuː daʊn / (BrE)
to make someone feel unhappy, sad, or discouraged
to make someone feel sad or depressed

Examples

Rainy days always get me down if I can't go outside.
Don't let your mistakes get you down; everyone makes them from time to time.
She tries not to let criticism get her down, but sometimes it's hard.

Synset Examples

Don't let the weather get you down today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depress - to make someone feel very unhappy
  • Antonym: to cheer up - to make (someone) feel happier
