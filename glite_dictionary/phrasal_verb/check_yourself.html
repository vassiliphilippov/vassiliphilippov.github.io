to check yourself

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk jərˈsɛlf / (AmE), / tʃɛk jəˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to assess or reconsider your behavior or attitude, especially to avoid making a mistake or causing problems
reassess one's behavior or attitude
informal

Examples

Before you say something hurtful, you should check yourself.
He had to check himself when he realized he was about to insult his colleague.
Sometimes, it's important to check yourself and reflect on your actions.

Synset Examples

He reminded her to check herself before she wrecked herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to think twice - to reconsider before taking action
  • Antonym: lose control - to fail to restrain your actions or emotions
