to border on

1. VERB

/ ˈbɔrdər ɑn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə ɒn / (BrE)
to be very close to a particular quality, state, or condition; to come near to being something (often something undesirable)
be very close to (a quality or condition)

Examples

The chef's disregard for hygiene borders on negligence.
Her enthusiasm for details borders on obsession at times.
Some of the jokes in the show border on being offensive.

Synset Examples

This borders on discrimination!
His playing approaches that of Horowitz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - come close to being or resembling

2. VERB

/ ˈbɔrdər ɑn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə ɒn / (BrE)
to be very close to being or becoming (something, often something undesirable or extreme)
almost reach a certain state or quality

Examples

His jokes often border on being offensive, but he never quite crosses the line.
The enthusiasm at the concert bordered on hysteria as the band took the stage.
Her patience with the noisy children borders on saintly.

Synset Examples

His behavior borders on disrespectful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to come near to something in quality or degree
