to spoil for

1. VERB

/ spɔɪl fɔr / (AmE), / spɔɪl fə / (BrE)
to have a strong desire or urge to do something, especially to start a fight or argument
have a strong urge for (esp. a fight)

Examples

Lately, Jake seems to be spoiling for an argument with anyone he meets.
The boxer appeared to be spoiling for a rematch after his narrow defeat.
It's clear she's spoiling for a chance to prove herself in the competition.

Synset Examples

She is itching to start the project
He is spoiling for a fight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: itch for - to have a strong wish or urge for something
