to sleep rough

1. VERB

/ sliːp rʌf / (AmE), / sliːp rʌf / (BrE)
to sleep outdoors without shelter or proper protection, often because of homelessness or lack of accommodation
sleep without shelter outdoors

Examples

Many homeless people have no choice but to sleep rough on cold nights.
When their hostel closed down, they started sleeping rough in a city park.
After losing his job, he slept rough for several months before getting help.

Synset Examples

He decided to sleep rough for a night to experience the homeless lifestyle.
During the festival, many chose to sleep rough on the streets.
Surviving the winter, they had to sleep rough in the abandoned building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep outdoors - to sleep outside in the open air
  • Antonym: sleep indoors - to sleep inside with shelter
