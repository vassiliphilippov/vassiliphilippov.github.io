to bring to justice

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ tə ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / brɪŋ tʊ ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
to make sure that someone is held legally responsible and punished for a crime or wrongdoing
hold accountable or punish for wrongdoing

Examples

After years in hiding, the notorious thief was finally brought to justice by the police.
The international task force worked together to bring to justice those responsible for the cyberattack.
Many believe that more should be done to bring to justice people who commit environmental crimes.

Synset Examples

The authorities promised to bring the criminals to justice.
We must work together to bring these offenders to justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to punish - to subject someone to a penalty for an offense
  • Antonym: to acquit - to declare someone not guilty of a charge
