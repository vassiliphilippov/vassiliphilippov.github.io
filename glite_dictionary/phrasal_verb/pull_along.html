to pull along

1. VERB

/ pʊl əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / pʊl əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to move something by dragging or hauling it, especially with effort or against resistance
drag or haul something with effort

Examples

She had to pull along her suitcase for several blocks.
The old cart was difficult to pull along the muddy path.
He helped me pull along the fallen tree out of the driveway.

Synset Examples

Can you shlep this bag of potatoes upstairs?
She pulled along a large trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - to pull something along the ground with effort
  • Synonym: haul - to pull or drag something with effort
