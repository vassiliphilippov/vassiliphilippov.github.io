to dam up

1. VERB

/ dæm ʌp / (AmE), / dæm ʌp / (BrE)
to block or obstruct the flow of water (or another substance) by means of a dam or similar barrier
block or obstruct with a dam

Examples

The engineers decided to dam up the river before the rainy season began.
Heavy debris had dammed up the stream, causing flooding in the area.
She tried to dam up her emotions, but eventually they spilled out.

Synset Examples

dam the gorges of the Yangtse River

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to stop movement or progress; to obstruct
  • Antonym: to unblock - to remove an obstruction; to clear a blockage
