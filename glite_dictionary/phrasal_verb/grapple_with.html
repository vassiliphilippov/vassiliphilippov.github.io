to grapple with

1. VERB

/ ˈɡræpəl wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈɡræpəl wɪð / (BrE)
to try hard to deal with or understand a difficult problem, subject, or situation
struggle to deal with a difficult issue

Examples

Many students grapple with balancing their coursework and social lives.
The government continues to grapple with the issue of climate change.
He grappled with the decision for weeks before finally choosing a career path.

Synset Examples

He had to grapple with his feelings after the breakup.
She will grapple with the complex math problem all night.
The team must grapple with tight deadlines this week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wrestle with - to struggle or try hard to deal with a problem
