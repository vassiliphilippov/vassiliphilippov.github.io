to flow from

1. VERB

/ floʊ frəm / (AmE), / fləʊ frəm / (BrE)
to result from; to be caused by something
be the result of

Examples

Many challenges flow from the rapid expansion of the company.
The new regulations flow from the committee's latest recommendations.
His confidence flows from years of experience in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result from - to be caused by or as a consequence of something
