to rattle through

1. VERB

/ ˈrætl θruː / (AmE), / ˈrætl θruː / (BrE)
to do, read, or deal with something quickly and without pausing
do or deal with quickly

Examples

Let's rattle through these questions so we can finish early.
She rattled through her explanation without stopping for questions.
We need to rattle through the paperwork before the deadline.

Synset Examples

Let’s rattle through the agenda quickly.
I’ll rattle through the documents before our meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush through - to do something very quickly, often without care
