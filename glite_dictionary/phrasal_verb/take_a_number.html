to take a number

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
to receive a numbered ticket or token indicating your place in a queue for service
wait your turn by receiving a number

Examples

When you arrive at the clinic, make sure to take a number at the reception desk.
If there are people ahead of you, please take a number and wait to be called.
I had to take a number before the butcher could serve me at the counter.

Synset Examples

Please take a number and wait for your turn.
At the bakery, I had to take a number to be served.
You can take a number and join the queue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - to wait in line for service or entry
