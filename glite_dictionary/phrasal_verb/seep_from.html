to seep from

1. VERB

/ siːp frəm / (AmE), / siːp frɒm / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow or leak slowly out of a place or substance through small openings.
flow or leak out slowly

Examples

Water began to seep from the crack in the wall after heavy rain.
Oil can seep from old machinery and stain the floor.
A faint smell seeped from the basement, suggesting mold was present.

Synset Examples

Blood started to seep from the wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through small openings
