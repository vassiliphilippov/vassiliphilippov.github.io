to get a line on

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə laɪn ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə laɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to obtain information or knowledge about someone or something, especially to help understand them or make a decision
obtain information about something or someone
informal

Examples

Before investing, it's wise to get a line on the company's financial history.
Can you get a line on who will be at the meeting tomorrow?
She tried to get a line on his whereabouts from mutual friends.

Synset Examples

I need to get a line on that new project.
Can you get a line on the latest updates?
He managed to get a line on the missing report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find out - discover information about something
  • Synonym: get wind of - learn of or hear a piece of information, typically informally
