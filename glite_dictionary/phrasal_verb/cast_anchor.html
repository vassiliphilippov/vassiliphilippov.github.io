to cast anchor

1. VERB

/ kæst ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / kɑːst ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
to lower an anchor from a vessel to secure it in place
to lower an anchor to secure a vessel

Examples

The ship cast anchor just off the coast before dawn.
We decided to cast anchor when we reached the sheltered bay.
After sailing for hours, the captain ordered them to cast anchor and rest.

Synset Examples

We anchored at Baltimore

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to weigh anchor - to lift an anchor and prepare to sail

2. VERB

/ kæst ˈæŋkəɹ / (AmE), / kɑːst ˈaŋkə / (BrE)
to drop an anchor in order to secure, moor, or stop a ship or boat
drop an anchor to secure a vessel

Examples

After sailing through rough seas, the crew decided to cast anchor in a sheltered bay.
As soon as they reached the harbor, the captain instructed them to cast anchor.
We will cast anchor once we find a safe spot near the shore.

Synset Examples

Before the storm, we must cast anchor securely.
The captain decided to cast anchor near the island.
They will cast anchor at sunset to rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drop anchor - to lower an anchor from a vessel to secure it
  • Antonym: to weigh anchor - to lift the anchor in preparation to set sail

Etymology

From 'cast' meaning 'to throw' and 'anchor', a heavy object used to moor a vessel.
