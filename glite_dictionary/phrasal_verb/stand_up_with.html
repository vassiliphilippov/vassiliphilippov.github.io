to stand up with

1. VERB

/ stænd ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / stænd ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to support or join someone in a cause or to defend a group or person
support or join someone in a cause

Examples

Many students chose to stand up with the teachers during the strike.
She decided to stand up with her friend after hearing her story.
If you believe in something, don't be afraid to stand up with others who share your cause.

Synset Examples

He decided to stand up with the protestors for justice.
They chose to stand up with the community during the crisis.
I will always stand up with my friends in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up for - to defend or support a person or cause, especially publicly
