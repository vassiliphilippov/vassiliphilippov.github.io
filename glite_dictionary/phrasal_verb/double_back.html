to double back

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˈbæk / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to turn around and go back in the direction from which one has come
to retrace one's steps

Examples

We missed the trail marker and had to double back to find it.
She realized she had lost her keys, so she quickly doubled back to the office.
If you see a dead end, just double back and try another route.

Synset Examples

The hikers got into a storm and had to turn back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn back - to reverse one's direction or course; to return

2. VERB

/ ˈdʌbəl bæk / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl bæk / (BrE)
to turn around and return along the same path or to a previous position
turn around and return

Examples

We realized we'd gone too far and had to double back to find the right trail.
He forgot his wallet at home and needed to double back to get it.
The hikers doubled back when they saw the fork in the path.

Synset Examples

After realizing the mistake, she had to double back.
He decided to double back and check the store again.
If we missed the exit, we'll need to double back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn back - to reverse direction and go back
