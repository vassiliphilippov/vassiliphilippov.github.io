to keep pace

1. VERB

/ kip peɪs / (AmE), / kiːp peɪs / (BrE)
to move, progress, or develop at the same rate as someone or something else
go at the same speed or rate

Examples

As technology advances rapidly, it can be hard to keep pace with the latest developments.
The runners struggled to keep pace with the leader in the final lap.
She works hard to keep pace with her more experienced colleagues.

Synset Examples

The child cannot keep step with his big brother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to maintain the same rate of progress as others

2. VERB

/ kip peɪs / (AmE), / kiːp peɪs / (BrE)
to move or develop at the same rate or speed as someone or something else
move or develop at same rate as another

Examples

It's difficult for small companies to keep pace with rapid technological changes.
She struggled to keep pace during the intense workout session.
Governments must keep pace with public expectations for transparency.

Synset Examples

You need to keep pace if you want to succeed.
The team must keep pace with the tight deadlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep up - to move or progress at the same rate as someone or something
