to nail down

1. VERB

/ neɪl daʊn / (AmE), / neɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to succeed in obtaining or securing a position, deal, or opportunity firmly
secure a position or opportunity

Examples

After several interviews, she finally nailed down the job she wanted.
It took weeks of negotiations before the company nailed down the lucrative contract.
He worked hard to nail down a spot on the national soccer team.

Synset Examples

He nailed down a spot at Harvard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to obtain or achieve something, especially after effort
  • Antonym: to lose - to fail to obtain or secure something

2. VERB

/ neɪl daʊn / (AmE), / neɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to make something definite or clearly defined; to establish or specify exactly
define or specify clearly

Examples

It's difficult to nail down the exact cause of the problem.
We need to nail down the details of the contract before signing.
She finally nailed down what she wanted to study at university.

Synset Examples

I cannot narrow down the rules for this game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pin down - to determine or define something exactly
  • Synonym: to narrow down - to reduce the number of possibilities; make more specific

3. VERB

/ neɪl daʊn / (AmE), / neɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to make something definite or final; to settle or finalize the details of something
make definite or finalize

Examples

We need to nail down the meeting time before sending out invitations.
After weeks of negotiation, the two companies finally nailed down the terms of the agreement.
Can you nail down exactly what the client wants before we start working on the project?

Synset Examples

let's finalize the proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finalize - to make final; to complete all details
  • Synonym: settle - to resolve or decide finally
  • Antonym: to postpone - to delay or defer final decision or action

Etymology

Figurative use of 'nail' (to fasten) with 'down', emerging in the 20th century.
