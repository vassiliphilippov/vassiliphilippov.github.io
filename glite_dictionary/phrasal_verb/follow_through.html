to follow through

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑːloʊ θruː / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ θruː / (BrE)
to continue an action or plan to its completion; to carry out fully.
complete an action to the end

Examples

If you want to succeed, you must follow through on your commitments.
She started a new workout routine and managed to follow through for six months.
It's important to follow through with your plans, even when it gets difficult.

Synset Examples

Did he go through with the treatment?
He implemented a new economic plan
She followed up his recommendations with a written proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry out - to perform or complete a task
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or abandon an effort

2. VERB

/ ˈfɑloʊ θru / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ θruː / (BrE)
to carry a swinging or striking motion to its natural completion after hitting or releasing a ball, especially in sports
complete a swing or motion after hitting a ball

Examples

A good golfer always remembers to follow through on their swing.
If you don't follow through, the shot won't have enough power or accuracy.
The coach stressed that every player must follow through after releasing the basketball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - the action of moving back and forth or in a curve, especially in sports

3. VERB

/ ˈfɑː.loʊ ˌθruː / (AmE), / ˈfɒl.əʊ ˌθruː / (BrE)
to do what needs to be done to complete a plan, promise, or commitment, especially after starting it
carry out to completion (promise or plan)

Examples

If you want to succeed, you must follow through on your goals and not give up.
Many politicians make promises but rarely follow through with real action.
I appreciate that you always follow through and finish what you start.

Synset Examples

He promised to follow through with his training plan.
You must follow through on your commitments.
It's important to follow through after making a promise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry out - to perform or complete a task or plan
  • Antonym: abandon - to stop supporting or completing something; to give up on it
