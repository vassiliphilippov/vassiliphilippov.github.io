to crop up

1. VERB

/ krɑp ʌp / (AmE), / krɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to appear or happen suddenly or unexpectedly, often requiring immediate attention
appear or happen unexpectedly

Examples

Problems can crop up when you least expect them.
New issues tend to crop up just before the deadline.
Questions might crop up during the presentation, so be prepared.

Synset Examples

The farm popped into view as we turned the corner
He suddenly popped up out of nowhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arise - to emerge or become apparent, often suddenly
  • Synonym: to occur - to happen or take place

2. VERB

/ krɑp ʌp / (AmE), / krɒp ʌp / (BrE)
(of something) to appear or happen unexpectedly, especially a problem or opportunity.
appear or occur unexpectedly

Examples

Unexpected expenses can sometimes crop up when renovating a house.
New topics often crop up during group discussions.
If any problems crop up, please let me know immediately.

Synset Examples

New challenges always seem to crop up unexpectedly.
Surprises tend to crop up during big projects.
Issues can crop up if we’re not careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arise - to begin to occur or to exist
  • Synonym: to emerge - to become visible or apparent
  • Antonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or to exist
