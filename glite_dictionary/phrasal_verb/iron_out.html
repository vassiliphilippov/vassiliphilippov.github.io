to iron out

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪərn aʊt / (AmE), / ˈaɪən aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve or settle problems, difficulties, or disagreements; to put things right by removing obstacles
resolve problems or settle disagreements

Examples

The manager helped iron out differences between the team members.
They need to iron out the final details before signing the contract.
It took hours to iron out all the technical issues with the software.

Synset Examples

we need to iron out our disagreements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - to find a solution to a problem or conflict
  • Synonym: to smooth over - to make a disagreement or difficulty seem less serious by dealing with it

Etymology

From the literal idea of smoothing wrinkles out of fabric with an iron, transferred to figurative use in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪərn aʊt / (AmE), / ˈaɪən aʊt / (BrE)
to press and smooth cloth or clothing with a heated iron in order to remove wrinkles
press and smooth with a heated iron

Examples

She carefully ironed out the wrinkles from her dress before the meeting.
You should iron out your shirt if you want it to look neat.
He ironed out the creases in the tablecloth for the dinner party.

Synset Examples

she stood there ironing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - to press (clothes or fabric) with a heated iron to remove wrinkles

3. VERB

/ ˌaɪərn ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌaɪən ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to resolve or clarify problems, disagreements, or details, especially through discussion or negotiation
resolve or clarify problems or details

Examples

Before we can sign the contract, we need to iron out a few more details.
The team spent several hours trying to iron out the disagreements between departments.
Hopefully, the new process will iron out most of the issues we've experienced so far.

Synset Examples

We need to iron out the details before the meeting.
Let's iron out the schedule for next week.
They will iron out any issues after the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - to find a solution to a problem or difficulty
  • Synonym: to smooth over - to make a problem seem less serious or to try to make it less of an issue

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from smoothing wrinkles in fabric with an iron to smoothing out problems.
