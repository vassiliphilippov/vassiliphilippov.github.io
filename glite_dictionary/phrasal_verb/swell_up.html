to swell up

1. VERB

/ swɛl ʌp / (AmE), / swɛl ʌp / (BrE)
to become larger or rounder than normal, especially as a result of an accumulation of fluid, injury, or illness
become abnormally enlarged

Examples

Her ankle began to swell up after she twisted it on the stairs.
Allergy sometimes causes the eyelids to swell up and become itchy.
The bread dough will swell up as it rises, so be sure to give it room.

Synset Examples

The bellies of the starving children are swelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to fill with air or gas and make larger
  • Antonym: to deflate - to make smaller by releasing air or gas

2. VERB

/ swɛl ʌp / (AmE), / swɛl ʌp / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or other object) to become larger or rounder in size, usually as a result of internal pressure, injury, or emotion.
become larger in size or intensity

Examples

When she twisted her ankle, it began to swell up almost immediately.
If you add too much yeast, the dough will swell up in the oven.
A sense of excitement started to swell up within the crowd as the concert began.

Synset Examples

Her pride began to swell up after the compliment.
The balloon will swell up when you fill it with air.
Tears started to swell up in his eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to fill something with air or gas, causing it to become larger
  • Antonym: to deflate - to become smaller by losing air or pressure; to reduce in size
