to shut off

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt ɔf / (AmE), / ʃʌt ɒf / (BrE)
to isolate or separate someone or something from others; to prevent connection or communication
isolate or separate

Examples

After the accident, he completely shut off from his family and friends.
The village was shut off from the rest of the region by heavy snowfall for days.
When she felt overwhelmed, she tended to shut off from those around her.

Synset Examples

She was shut off from the friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to cause something or someone to be alone or kept apart from others
  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart

2. VERB

/ ʃʌt ɔf / (AmE), / ʃʌt ɒf / (BrE)
to stop the flow or passage of something by closing a valve, switch, etc.
to stop the flow or passage of something

Examples

Please shut off the water before repairing the sink.
The mechanic shut off the engine to prevent further damage.
If you smell gas, shut off the supply immediately.

Synset Examples

We shut off the valve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn off - to stop the operation or flow of something, especially with a switch
  • Synonym: to switch off - to stop a device from operating by turning a switch to the off position

/ ʃʌt ˈɔf / (AmE), / ʃʌt ˈɒf / (BrE)
to stop the flow or operation of something, especially by means of a control or switch
stop the flow or operation of something

Examples

The technician shut off the water supply to repair the leak.
They shut off the engine immediately after arriving at the dock.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn off - to deactivate or stop a device or flow
  • Synonym: to switch off - to stop a device or flow by operating a switch
  • Antonym: to turn on - to activate or start a device or flow
  • Antonym: to switch on - to activate or start a device or flow by operating a switch
