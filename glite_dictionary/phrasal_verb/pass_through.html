to pass through

1. VERB

/ pæs ˈθru / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈθruː / (BrE)
to travel through a place without staying for long or making it your main destination
to travel through a place

Examples

Many migratory birds pass through this region each spring.
We didn't stay in the village; we just passed through on our way to the coast.
As the parade passed through the city, the streets filled with laughter and music.

Synset Examples

The tourists moved through the town and bought up all the souvenirs
Some travelers pass through the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel through - to travel through a place
  • Antonym: to stop over - to stay briefly at a place during a longer journey

2. VERB

/ pæs ˈθru / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈθruː / (BrE)
to cause something to move or be filtered through something else
cause to move or filter through

Examples

To separate the impurities, they pass through the solution using a fine mesh.
The laboratory technician passed through the liquid to obtain a filtered sample.
In this process, gases are passed through the membrane to remove toxins.

Synset Examples

Pass a chemical through a solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - to pass a substance through a device to remove unwanted parts
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02057114-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pæs ˈθru / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈθruː / (BrE)
(of troops or individuals) to move across or penetrate (an enemy line or area), typically during a military engagement.
move across enemy lines (military)

Examples

The soldiers managed to pass through enemy territory under the cover of darkness.
During the night, our battalion attempted to pass through the enemy line undetected.
Several units tried to pass through, but only a few were successful in reaching the other side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break through - forcefully make way through a barrier or defences
  • Antonym: retreat - withdraw from enemy forces or lines
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
