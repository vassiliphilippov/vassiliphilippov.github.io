to outshine each other

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈʃaɪn iːtʃ ˈʌðɚ / (AmE), / aʊtˈʃaɪn iːtʃ ˈʌðə / (BrE)
(of two or more people) to try to perform or seem better than the others, especially in a way that draws attention.
compete to perform or seem better than others

Examples

During the awards ceremony, the nominees tried to outshine each other with their impressive speeches.
In the group project, the students were more focused on outshining each other than on working together.
At the fashion show, the models seemed to outshine each other with every outfit they presented.

Synset Examples

At the egofest, everyone was trying to outshine each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - to strive against others to achieve something
  • Antonym: to cooperate - to work together toward a common goal
