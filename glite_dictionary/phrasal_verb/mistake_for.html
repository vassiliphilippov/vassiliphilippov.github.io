to mistake for

1. VERB

/ mɪˈsteɪk fər / (AmE), / mɪsˈteɪk fə / (BrE)
to wrongly identify someone or something as another person or thing
to wrongly identify as another

Examples

She mistook the sugar for salt when baking the cake.
At night, I mistook the statue for a real person.
He mistook her kindness for romantic interest.

Synset Examples

They mistook the ditch weed for real cannabis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confuse with - to fail to distinguish between two things or people
