to skate around

1. VERB

/ skeɪt əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / skeɪt əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to avoid talking about or dealing with a difficult subject or issue directly
avoid addressing directly

Examples

When questioned about the budget cuts, the manager chose to skate around the issue.
Politicians often skate around direct questions in interviews.
It's frustrating when people skate around problems instead of facing them.

Synset Examples

He always skates around the topic of his mistakes.
Don't skate around the main point; just say it.
She decided to skate around the issue during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodge - avoid dealing with something directly
  • Synonym: to evade - avoid giving a clear answer or facing a subject
  • Antonym: to confront - address a subject or problem directly and openly
