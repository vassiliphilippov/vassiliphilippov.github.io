to quake at

1. VERB

/ kweɪk æt / (AmE), / kweɪk ət / (BrE)
to tremble or shake because of fear or apprehension
tremble or shake from fear

Examples

The soldiers could not help but quake at the sound of approaching artillery.
Even the bravest warriors might quake at the roar of a lion.
She tried not to quake at the thought of giving a speech to so many people.

Synset Examples

He couldn't help but quake at the scary movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble at - to shake involuntarily due to fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: fearless - showing no fear
