to let down

1. VERB

/ lɛt daʊn / (AmE), / lɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to disappoint someone by failing to do what is expected or promised
to disappoint; fail to meet expectations

Examples

I promised not to let down my team during the big match.
She felt hurt when her friends let down her by not showing up to her birthday.
He tried hard, but ultimately he let down everyone who was counting on him.

Synset Examples

Her boyfriend let her down when he did not propose marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappoint - to fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of someone
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support or confidence; the opposite of letting someone down
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01802947-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɛt daʊn / (AmE), / lɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to move something or someone from a higher to a lower position carefully
move to a lower position

Examples

He slowly let down the rope until it touched the ground.
Please let down the blinds so the sunlight doesn't glare on the screen.
The firefighter let down the rescued cat from the tree using a ladder.

Synset Examples

take down the vase from the shelf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lower - move something to a lower position

/ lɛt daʊn / (AmE), / lɛt daʊn / (BrE)
