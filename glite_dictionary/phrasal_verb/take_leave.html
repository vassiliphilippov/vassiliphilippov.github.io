to take leave

1. VERB

/ teɪk liv / (AmE), / teɪk liːv / (BrE)
to depart from a place or person, often formally; to go away or leave
depart or go away

Examples

After a brief discussion, he took leave of his colleagues and left the office.
She politely took leave from the gathering before heading home.
Before sunrise, the soldiers took leave of their families and boarded the bus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially to start a journey
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place after a journey
