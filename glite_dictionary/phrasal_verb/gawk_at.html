to gawk at

1. VERB

/ ɡɑk æt / (AmE), / ɡɔːk ət / (BrE)
to stare openly and often rudely or in surprise or wonder
stare openly or rudely

Examples

Tourists couldn't help but gawk at the celebrity as he walked by.
Please don't gawk at the accident scene; it's disrespectful to those involved.
He stopped to gawk at the breathtaking mountain view for several minutes.

Synset Examples

Don't gawk at the performers; give them space!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare at - to look fixedly or vacantly at someone or something
  • Degree Lower: to glance at - to look briefly or quickly

Etymology

Probably from Scots 'gawk' (to gape or stare), possibly related to Old Norse 'ga' (to heed, look).
