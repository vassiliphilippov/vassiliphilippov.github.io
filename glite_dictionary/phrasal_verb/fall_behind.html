to fall behind

1. VERB

/ fɔl bəˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / fɔːl bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to move back or retreat, especially in a situation where others are advancing or progressing
move back; retreat

Examples

The troops were forced to fall behind as the enemy advanced.
After hours of hiking, several members had to fall behind and rest.
When the competition intensified, he decided to fall behind rather than take unnecessary risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to move away from the enemy or from a position, especially to avoid danger
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward, especially towards the enemy or a goal
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01116231-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɔl bəˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / fɔːl bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to move or progress more slowly than others, or fail to keep up with a schedule, standard, or competitors
fail to keep pace; lag behind

Examples

If you fall behind in your studies, it's hard to catch up later.
Many small businesses fell behind during the economic downturn.
She was running fast at first, but soon began to fall behind the others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lag behind - to remain at a lower level than others in development or progress
  • Antonym: to catch up - to reach the same standard or level as others
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02001894-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ fɔl bəˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / fɔːl bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to fail to keep up with others or to not make as much progress as expected
to not keep up or make progress

Examples

If you don't study regularly, you might fall behind in your coursework.
She was ill for two weeks and started to fall behind with her assignments.
The company can't afford to fall behind its competitors in technology.

Synset Examples

If you don't study, you'll fall behind in class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to catch up - to reach the same level or make up for lost progress
