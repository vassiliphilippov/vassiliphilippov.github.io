to take fire

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to begin to burn or burst into flames
start to burn; burst into flames

Examples

The pile of dry leaves took fire after a spark landed on them.
The house quickly took fire as the flames spread from the kitchen.
If the chemicals are not stored properly, they can take fire with little warning.

Synset Examples

Marsh gases ignited suddenly
The oily rags combusted spontaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to put out a fire or flame

2. VERB

/ teɪk ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to come under attack, especially by gunfire or shooting
to come under gunfire or attack

Examples

As soon as the convoy entered the city, it began to take fire from both sides of the street.
The soldiers had to stay low when they started to take fire during the mission.
Despite the helicopter taking fire, the pilot managed to land safely.

Synset Examples

During training, soldiers learn how to take fire responsibly.
The team began to take fire as they approached the target.
They had to take fire from the enemy for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come under fire - to be attacked with guns or criticized severely
