to crow about

1. VERB

/ kroʊ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / krəʊ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to boast or talk proudly about something, especially in a way that annoys others
boast or brag (often annoyingly)

Examples

There's no need to crow about your victory in front of everyone.
After finishing the project first, she wouldn't stop crowing about her success.
He often crows about how much money he makes compared to his coworkers.

Synset Examples

He had to crow about his promotion at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about an achievement or possession
  • Synonym: brag - to talk about oneself or achievements in a proud or self-satisfied way

Etymology

From the verb 'crow' (to make a triumphant sound), by analogy with the cock's crowing.
