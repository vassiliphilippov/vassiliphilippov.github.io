to square away

1. VERB

/ skwɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / skweə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to arrange or put (something) in order; to organize tidily or prepare for use
put in order; organize tidily
american

Examples

Before the guests arrived, she squared away the living room to make it look presentable.
I need to square away all these files before we leave the office.
Make sure everything is squared away before the end of your shift.

Synset Examples

Tidy up your room!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make neat and organized
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make untidy or disordered

Etymology

Originally an American term, using 'square' in the sense of 'to make straight or tidy'.
