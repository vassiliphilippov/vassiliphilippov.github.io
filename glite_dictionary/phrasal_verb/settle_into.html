to settle into

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬l ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to become comfortable with or adjust to a new place, situation, or routine
become comfortable in a new situation

Examples

After a few weeks, she began to settle into her new apartment and enjoy the neighborhood.
It always takes time for children to settle into a new school.
He struggled at first but eventually settled into his new responsibilities at work.

Synset Examples

He began to settle into his new lifestyle effortlessly.
They will settle into their new routine gradually.
I hope to settle into my new role quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - to change your behavior to suit a new situation
  • Synonym: to adjust - to become or make something suited to new conditions
  • Antonym: struggle - to experience difficulty becoming comfortable
