to grass over

1. VERB

/ ɡræs ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to cover (an area of land) with grass, especially by planting or allowing grass to grow
cover land with grass

Examples

The city decided to grass over the old parking lot to create a green space.
After the construction was finished, workers grassed over the disturbed soil to prevent erosion.
Many homeowners choose to grass over bare patches in their yards for a neater appearance.

Synset Examples

The owners decided to grass their property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sod - to cover land with turf or sod
