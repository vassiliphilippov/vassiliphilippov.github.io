to ride herd on

1. VERB

/ raɪd hɜrd ɑn / (AmE), / raɪd hɜːd ɒn / (BrE)
to supervise or manage people or activities very closely and carefully
supervise or manage closely

Examples

It's difficult to ride herd on multiple projects when resources are limited.
As a teacher, she often has to ride herd on her students during field trips.
He was brought in to ride herd on the company's struggling marketing team.

Synset Examples

She had to ride herd on the volunteers during the event.
It's challenging to ride herd on a group of energetic kids.
The manager needs to ride herd on the team to meet deadlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to supervise - to watch over and direct the work of others
  • Synonym: to oversee - to watch over and ensure the proper execution of a task or group

Etymology

Originally from American Western usage of cowboys managing cattle herds.
