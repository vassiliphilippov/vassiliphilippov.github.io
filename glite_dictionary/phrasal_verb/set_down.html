to set down

1. VERB

/ sɛt daʊn / (AmE), / sɛt daʊn / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, aircraft, etc.) to land or come to rest on the ground or another surface.
land; come to rest (of vehicle, aircraft)

Examples

The helicopter will set down in the clearing ahead.
Do not set down until you receive clearance from air traffic control.
The rescue boat set down safely after navigating the rough waters.

Synset Examples

The bird landed on the highest branch
The plane landed in Istanbul

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to come down through the air onto the ground or another surface
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛt daʊn / (AmE), / sɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to put something into writing or record it officially, such as a statement, rule, or composition.
to write down or record officially

Examples

The committee set down its recommendations in the final report.
He carefully set down every word she spoke so he wouldn't forget.
The laws were set down centuries ago but are still in effect today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to set down in writing or another permanent form
  • Synonym: note - to write down or record something briefly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01022365-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sɛt daʊn / (AmE), / sɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to place or position something or someone carefully onto a surface or in a particular spot.
place or position carefully

Examples

He gently set down the box on the kitchen table.
The pilot set down the helicopter in a small clearing.
After walking for hours, she set down her backpack and rested.

Synset Examples

The hotel was set down at the bottom of the valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put down - to place something onto a surface
  • Antonym: to pick up - to lift something from a surface
