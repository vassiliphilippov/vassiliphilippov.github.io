to fling off

1. VERB

/ flɪŋ ɑf / (AmE), / flɪŋ ɒf / (BrE)
to write (something) quickly and without much effort
write quickly and easily

Examples

She managed to fling off a quick email before leaving the office.
The author would often fling off short stories in a single afternoon.
He flung off a hasty apology note before rushing out the door.

Synset Examples

She dashed off a note to her husband saying she would not be home for supper
He scratched off a thank-you note to the hostess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dash off - to write or complete something quickly
  • Degree Lower: scribble - to write something carelessly or hurriedly
