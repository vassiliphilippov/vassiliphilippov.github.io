to fall over

1. VERB

/ fɔl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to lose balance and drop to the ground, usually by tipping forward or to the side
lose balance and drop to the ground

Examples

He slipped on the ice and fell over in front of everyone.
Please pick up those chairs before they fall over.
The baby laughed when the blocks fell over on the carpet.

Synset Examples

The old woman went over without a sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to topple - to fall forward, especially because of being unbalanced
  • Synonym: trip - to catch one's foot and stumble or fall
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00031270-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɔl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to lose one's balance and collapse to the ground by tripping or stumbling.
stumble and collapse

Examples

Watch your step on the icy path or you might fall over.
The toddler giggled every time she would fall over while learning to walk.
He tripped on the carpet and fell over in front of everyone.

Synset Examples

Be careful not to fall over the curb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trip - to stumble or lose one's footing
  • Synonym: to fall down - to drop to the ground, often after losing balance
  • Antonym: to stand up - to rise to a standing position
