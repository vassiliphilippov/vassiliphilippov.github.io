to crank up

1. VERB

/ kræŋk ʌp / (AmE), / kræŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to start an engine or machine by turning a handle or crank
start an engine by cranking

Examples

He cranked up the old lawnmower and hoped it would run.
The mechanic cranked up the engine to check if the repairs worked.
We crank up the generator every morning during the power outage.

Synset Examples

crank up the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to start up - to begin operating, especially for a machine or engine

2. VERB

/ kræŋk ʌp / (AmE), / kræŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to rotate or start something by turning a crank
rotate or start by using a crank

Examples

He had to crank up the old tractor before it would start.
The mechanic cranked up the engine manually when the battery died.
She watched him crank up the generator during the power outage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crank - to rotate or operate by turning a crank
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01598070-v

3. VERB

/ kræŋk ʌp / (AmE), / kræŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or raise the level or intensity of something, especially a machine or activity
increase or raise the level of something

Examples

Let's crank up the volume so everyone can hear the music.
The company plans to crank up production next month to meet demand.
When it started getting dark, they cranked up the lights in the stadium.

Synset Examples

She decided to crank up the heater since it was so cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn up - to increase the level or intensity of something, especially a device or machine
  • Antonym: lower - to reduce the level, amount, or intensity of something

Etymology

From 'crank' (to turn a handle to operate something) + 'up', originally referring to increasing a machine's power by turning a crank.
