to lie about

1. VERB

/ laɪ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / laɪ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to spend time doing nothing in particular; to wait or be idle without any specific purpose
be idle; do nothing in particular

Examples

After lunch, we decided to lie about on the grass and enjoy the sunshine.
He tends to lie about all weekend instead of doing something productive.
Books and magazines were scattered as the children lay about in the living room.

Synset Examples

She did all the work while he lay around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang around - to spend time doing nothing in particular
  • Synonym: loaf - to spend time aimlessly or lazily
