to second-guess

1. VERB

/ ˌsɛk.əndˈɡɛs / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.əndˈɡɛs / (BrE)
to criticize or evaluate an action or decision with the benefit of hindsight, often implying doubt or a belief that a different decision should have been made
evaluate or criticize with hindsight

Examples

It is easy to second-guess the decision once you know the outcome.
The media loves to second-guess the coach after a loss.
She tried not to second-guess herself during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something; synonym in evaluative sense
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something; antonym in evaluative sense

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'second guess' (a reconsideration), based on 'first guess.'
