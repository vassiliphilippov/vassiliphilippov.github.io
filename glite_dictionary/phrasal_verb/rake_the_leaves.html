to rake the leaves

1. VERB

/ reɪk ðə livz / (AmE), / reɪk ðə liːvz / (BrE)
to gather fallen leaves from the ground using a rake
gather fallen leaves with a rake

Examples

Every Saturday, the kids help their parents rake the leaves in the backyard.
It's best to rake the leaves before the first snowfall of the year.
After a windy storm, we had to rake the leaves scattered all over our lawn.

Synset Examples

It's time to rake the leaves before it rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to bring together; collect
