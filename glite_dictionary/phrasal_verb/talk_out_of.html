to talk out of

1. VERB

/ tɔk ˈaʊt əv / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to persuade someone not to do something
persuade not to do something

Examples

She managed to talk him out of quitting his job.
I tried to talk my friend out of buying that expensive car.
No one could talk her out of moving to another country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
  • Antonym: to talk into - to persuade someone to do something
