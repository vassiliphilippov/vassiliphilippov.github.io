to feel off

1. VERB

/ fil ɔf / (AmE), / fiːl ɒf / (BrE)
to experience a sense of discomfort, unease, or not being quite well, either physically or emotionally
to feel unwell or uneasy
informal

Examples

I didn't eat anything bad, but I'm just feeling off today.
He canceled his plans because he was feeling off and needed to rest.
Whenever I'm feeling off, I like to take a walk outside to clear my mind.

Synset Examples

She left the meeting early, feeling off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feel unwell - to experience being ill or sick
  • Antonym: feel fine - to feel well, healthy, or comfortable
