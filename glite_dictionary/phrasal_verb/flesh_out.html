to flesh out

1. VERB

/ flɛʃ aʊt / (AmE), / flɛʃ aʊt / (BrE)
To add more details or substance to something, such as an idea, plan, or account; to make something more complete or clear.
add details or substance; make more complete

Examples

We need to flesh out the proposal before presenting it to the committee.
Can you flesh out this outline with more specific examples?
Her report was brief, but she later fleshed out her argument during the discussion.

Synset Examples

She elaborated on the main ideas in her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborate - add more detail or information
  • Antonym: sketch - present the main points without detail

3. VERB

/ flɛʃ aʊt / (AmE), / flɛʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to add more details or information to something to make it more complete or substantial
add details to make more complete

Examples

The director asked the writer to flesh out the main character's background.
We should flesh out our business plan before presenting it to investors.
Her advisor suggested she flesh out the arguments in the final report.

Synset Examples

He took time to flesh out his character's backstory.
We need to flesh out the details of the proposal.
Can you flesh out your ideas for the presentation?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborate - to give more details about something
  • Synonym: to expand - to add further detail or explanation

Etymology

From the literal sense of adding flesh to a skeleton, figuratively applied to ideas or plans.
