to roll around

1. VERB

/ roʊl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rəʊl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a particular time or event) to arrive or happen again, especially as part of a regular cycle.
occur again at a regular time

Examples

When her birthday rolls around, she always throws a big party.
Tax season rolls around faster than you expect every year.
By the time summer rolled around, the whole family was ready for a vacation.

Synset Examples

Christmas rolled around again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come around - to happen again, especially regularly

/ roʊl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rəʊl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
