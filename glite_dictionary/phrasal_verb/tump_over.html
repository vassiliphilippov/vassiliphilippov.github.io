to tump over

1. VERB

/ tʌmp ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / tʌmp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(chiefly Southern US) to tip over or turn from an upright position to its side or upside down.
to tip or overturn (Southern US)
informal american

Examples

Be careful or the canoe will tump over when you stand up.
The wind was so strong it made several trash bins tump over last night.
I accidentally tumped over my coffee cup reaching for the phone.

Synset Examples

The big vase overturned
The canoe tumped over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overturn - to turn upside down or cause to tip over
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position, not tipped over

Etymology

Probably from a blend of 'tip' and 'dump', chiefly Southern US regional.

2. VERB

/ tʌmp ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / tʌmp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to cause something to tip over from an upright or normal position; to overturn or knock over.
to tip or overturn something
informal american

Examples

Be careful not to tump over the canoe when you get in.
The wind was so strong it tumped over several chairs on the porch.
He accidentally tumped over his soda while reaching for his phone.

Synset Examples

The cat knocked over the flower vase
the clumsy customer turned over the vase
he tumped over his beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overturn - cause to turn over or capsize
  • Synonym: to knock over - to cause something to fall over
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical or erect position

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from regional Southern U.S. dialect, perhaps a blend of 'tip' and 'dump.'
