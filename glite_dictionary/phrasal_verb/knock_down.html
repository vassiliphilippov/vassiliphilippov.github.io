to knock down

1. VERB

/ nɑk daʊn / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn / (BrE)
to hit or strike someone or something so that they fall to the ground
strike so as to make fall

Examples

The boxer managed to knock down his opponent in the third round.
A sudden gust of wind could easily knock down the fence.
She was accidentally knocked down by a player during the game.

Synset Examples

He decked his opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floor - to knock someone to the ground, especially in boxing
  • Synonym: deck - to knock someone down, especially with a punch
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01415000-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ nɑk daʊn / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to fall to the ground by hitting or pushing
cause to fall to the ground

Examples

The cyclist was knocked down by a car on the busy street.
She accidentally knocked down the vase while cleaning the shelf.
Security quickly knocked down the intruder when he tried to run.

Synset Examples

The policeman downed the heavily armed suspect
The mugger knocked down the old lady after she refused to hand over her wallet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down - to bring to the ground
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01242426-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ nɑk daʊn / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn / (BrE)
to destroy or demolish (something) as if by an explosion; to cause to collapse suddenly and forcefully
destroy or demolish as if by an explosion

Examples

The old warehouse was knocked down last week after the fire.
They plan to knock down the building to make way for new apartments.
The blast knocked down several walls in the factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - pull or knock down (a building); destroy completely
  • Synonym: to destroy - put an end to the existence of (something) by damaging or attacking it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00336968-v
Synset POS: v
