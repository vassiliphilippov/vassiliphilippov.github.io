to pit against each other

1. VERB

/ pɪt əˈɡɛnst iːtʃ ˈʌðər / (AmE), / pɪt əˈɡɛnst iːtʃ ˈʌðə / (BrE)
to cause two or more people or groups to compete or conflict with each other
set in competition with one another

Examples

The new reality show will pit against each other contestants from all walks of life.
The game is designed to pit against each other two players in a series of complex challenges.
It's unfair to pit against each other colleagues who have always worked well as a team.

Synset Examples

The coach decided to pit the two best players against each other.
They often pit the candidates against each other in debates.
The media loves to pit celebrities against one another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - to strive to gain or win something by defeating or establishing superiority over others
  • Antonym: to cooperate - to work jointly towards the same end
