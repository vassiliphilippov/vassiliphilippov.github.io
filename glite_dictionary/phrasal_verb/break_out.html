to break out

1. VERB

/ breɪk aʊt / (AmE), / breɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from storage or stowage in order to prepare it for use
remove from storage for use

Examples

The crew was ordered to break out emergency supplies during the storm.
We had to break out the life jackets when the alarm sounded.
They will break out the tents as soon as we reach the campsite.

2. VERB

/ breɪk aʊt / (AmE), / breɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to start suddenly, especially something unpleasant such as war, fighting, or disease
start suddenly (war, fighting, disease)

Examples

Riots could break out at any moment if the situation doesn't improve.
A fire broke out in the factory, causing widespread panic among workers.
If the disease breaks out again, the authorities are prepared to respond quickly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
