to bear away

1. VERB

/ bɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / beə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to carry or remove someone or something from a particular place, environment, or emotional state; to transport into a new location or condition
take or carry away from a place or state

Examples

The strong current threatened to bear away the small boat.
Memories of her childhood would often bear away her thoughts during quiet afternoons.
He tried not to let his emotions bear away his judgment in the tense negotiation.

Synset Examples

Their dreams carried the Romantics away into distant lands
The car carried us off to the meeting
I'll take you away on a holiday
I got carried away when I saw the dead man and I started to cry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry away - to take someone or something from one place to another; to transport

Etymology

From Middle English *beren* (to carry) and *away* (away, off).
