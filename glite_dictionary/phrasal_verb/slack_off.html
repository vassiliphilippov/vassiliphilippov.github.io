to slack off

1. VERB

/ slæk ɑf / (AmE), / slæk ɒf / (BrE)
(of something) to decrease in amount, intensity, or activity; to become less severe or forceful.
become less intense or severe

Examples

The heavy rain finally began to slack off by mid-afternoon.
Traffic tends to slack off late at night in the city.
If the winds slack off soon, the planes will be able to land safely.

Synset Examples

The storm abated
The rain let up after a few hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abate - to become less strong or intense
  • Synonym: to let up - to become less strong or intense, especially about something unpleasant

Etymology

From 'slack' (meaning loose, less tense) + 'off'.
