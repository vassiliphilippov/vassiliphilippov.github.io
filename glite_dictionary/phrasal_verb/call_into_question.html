to call into question

1. VERB

/ kɔl ˈɪntu ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈɪntuː ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
to express doubt about the truth, validity, or appropriateness of something
express doubt or challenge validity

Examples

The scientist's new findings call into question previous theories about climate change.
His honesty was called into question after the incident at work.
Recent events have called into question the effectiveness of the current policy.

Synset Examples

We must question your judgment in this matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to question the validity or truth of something
  • Antonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or validity of something
