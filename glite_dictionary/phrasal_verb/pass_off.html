to pass off

1. VERB

/ pæs ɔf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
(of an unpleasant feeling or situation) to gradually disappear or become less intense.
disappear slowly (of pain, symptoms, etc.)

Examples

The headache will pass off if you get some rest.
After a few minutes, her anger began to pass off.
The storm seemed severe, but it started to pass off by evening.

Synset Examples

The pain eventually passed off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade - to gradually become less strong or clear and then disappear
  • Antonym: to linger - to continue to exist for longer than expected (opposite sense for fading away)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00422644-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pæs ɑf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
to emit or release (gases or odors), often in a discreet or unnoticed manner
to emit gases or smells

Examples

The sewage treatment plant is designed not to pass off any unpleasant odors into the neighborhood.
After eating beans, he tried to quietly pass off the resulting gas without anyone noticing.
The chemical factory was fined when it continued to pass off harmful fumes into the atmosphere.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00105087-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pæs ˈɔf / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈɒf / (BrE)
to be accepted as or cause someone or something to be accepted as a different person or thing, often by deception
pretend to be someone or something else

Examples

He tried to pass off the fake painting as an original.
She managed to pass off as a university student during the conference.
The company attempted to pass off the defective goods as high-quality products.

Synset Examples

She passed off as a Russian agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pose as - pretend to be someone else, usually to deceive others
  • Synonym: masquerade - pretend to be someone or something else
  • Antonym: reveal oneself - show one's true identity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02138957-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ pæs ɔf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to be accepted as something they are not; to present falsely
pretend something or someone is something else

Examples

He tried to pass off a fake painting as an original masterpiece.
She managed to pass off her imitation purse as a designer brand at the party.
They attempted to pass off the incident as a harmless joke, but no one believed them.

Synset Examples

She passed the glass off as diamonds
He passed himself off as a secret agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguise - to give a false appearance or impression
  • Synonym: to impersonate - to pretend to be another person
  • Antonym: to expose - to reveal the true nature or identity of something or someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02056889-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ pæs ɑf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
to treat something, especially criticism or an insult, as unimportant or not worth serious attention
disregard or dismiss as unimportant

Examples

She tried to pass off his rude remark as a joke.
It's not wise to pass off criticism without considering it.
He attempted to pass off their concerns, but they insisted on answers.

Synset Examples

She passed off the insult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
  • Synonym: to brush off - to reject or ignore someone or something
  • Antonym: pay attention to - to focus one's attention on something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00803184-v
Synset POS: v
