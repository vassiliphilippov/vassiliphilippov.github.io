to plow ahead

1. VERB

/ plaʊ əˈhɛd / (AmE), / plaʊ əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to continue to do something determinedly, especially despite difficulties or opposition
continue resolutely despite difficulties

Examples

Despite the bad weather, the team decided to plow ahead with the construction.
She chose to plow ahead with her plans, even though others advised caution.
We will plow ahead with the project, no matter what challenges arise.

Synset Examples

He went ahead with the project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to press on - to continue moving forward or making progress, especially despite difficulties
  • Synonym: to persist - to continue steadily in a course of action
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause before saying or doing something, often due to uncertainty
