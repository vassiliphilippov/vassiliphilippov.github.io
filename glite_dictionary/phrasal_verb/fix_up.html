to fix up

1. VERB

/ fɪks ʌp / (AmE), / fɪks ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange or organize something, such as a meeting or appointment
arrange or organize something

Examples

I can fix up a meeting with the manager for next week if you’d like.
She promised to fix up an appointment with a specialist for me.
They managed to fix up the details of the trip in just a few hours.

Synset Examples

Can you arrange a meeting with the President?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - to organize or make plans for something to happen
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01023638-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɪks ʌp / (AmE), / fɪks ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange for someone to meet or obtain something or someone, often as a favor or to help them
arrange to provide something or someone

Examples

I can fix up an interview for you with the manager if you'd like.
She tried to fix up her roommate with her cousin last weekend.
After I moved to the city, my aunt offered to fix up an apartment for me.

Synset Examples

I'll fix you up with a nice girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set up - to arrange a meeting, date, or introduction, often for romantic purposes
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01205989-v
Synset POS: v
