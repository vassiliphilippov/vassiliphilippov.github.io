to ratchet down

1. VERB

/ ˈrætʃət daʊn / (AmE), / ˈrætʃɪt daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce or decrease something gradually in a series of steps, typically in a way that cannot be easily reversed
decrease gradually in steps

Examples

The company decided to ratchet down production due to lower sales.
After the negotiations stalled, both sides began to ratchet down their expectations.
In response to inflation, the central bank chose to ratchet down interest rates gradually.

Synset Examples

a ratcheting lopping tool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - make smaller in amount, degree, or size
  • Antonym: increase - make greater in amount, degree, or size

Etymology

From 'ratchet', a device allowing movement in one direction only, plus 'down' indicating reduction.
