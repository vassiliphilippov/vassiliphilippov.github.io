to latch on

1. VERB

/ lætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / lætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to take hold of or become firmly attached to something
attach or take hold of firmly

Examples

The infant instinctively latched on to its mother's finger.
Certain viruses latch on to host cells to start an infection.
After several attempts, the hook finally latched on to the metal loop.

Synset Examples

The biochip latches onto the genes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to seize suddenly and roughly
  • Antonym: to let go - to release hold or attachment

Etymology

From 'latch' (to fasten or lock), combined with 'on'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01218758-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / lætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to understand or realize something, especially after not immediately grasping it
come to understand something

Examples

It took me a while to latch on to the new concept, but now it makes sense.
After a few hints, she finally latched on to what he was talking about.
He was slow to latch on, but eventually he understood the instructions.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to understand or comprehend something, especially after some time
  • Synonym: to get it - to understand or grasp something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00591761-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / lætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand, accept, or adopt an idea or method, especially after a period of resistance or unfamiliarity
adopt or understand an idea or method

Examples

After some hesitation, the team finally latched on to the new marketing strategy.
It took a while for the public to latch on to the importance of recycling.
Children can quickly latch on to new trends at school.

Synset Examples

take up new ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take up - to adopt or accept an idea, suggestion, or course of action

Etymology

Originally from the sense of a physical latch catching on to something; metaphorically extended to catching or holding on to ideas.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00603507-v
Synset POS: v
