to kick up

1. VERB

/ kɪk ʌp / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to cause (dust, dirt, etc.) to rise or scatter into the air by kicking or moving forcefully
cause dust or dirt to rise by kicking

Examples

The horses galloped down the road and kicked up a cloud of dust behind them.
Please wipe your feet so you don't kick up dirt on the clean floor.
The children ran across the dry field, kicking up little puffs of dust as they played.

Synset Examples

kick up dust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stir up - to cause something to move or rise, especially dust or trouble
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01374316-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɪk ʌp / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to cause something, especially trouble, dust, a fuss, etc., to appear or occur suddenly or forcefully
provoke or stir up (trouble, dust, etc.)

Examples

The protesters began to kick up a lot of trouble in the city square.
Strong winds can kick up dust on the old country roads.
He tends to kick up a fuss whenever things don't go his way.

Synset Examples

Her behavior provoked a quarrel between the couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stir up - to cause or provoke something, especially trouble or emotions, to rise
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less agitated or to make a situation more peaceful
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01650408-v
Synset POS: v
