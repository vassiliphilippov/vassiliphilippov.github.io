to winnow out

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪnoʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate someone or something from consideration, often as part of a selection process
eliminate from consideration

Examples

The first round of interviews will winnow out the least qualified candidates.
The judges have to winnow out the weaker entries before the final competition.
After reviewing the applications, she quickly winnowed out those that didn’t meet the requirements.

Synset Examples

John was ruled out as a possible suspect because he had a strong alibi
This possibility can be eliminated from our consideration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something
  • Synonym: to exclude - to prevent someone or something from entering or being considered
  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or category

Etymology

From Old English 'windwian' (to separate grain by blowing), extended metaphorically to selection processes.
