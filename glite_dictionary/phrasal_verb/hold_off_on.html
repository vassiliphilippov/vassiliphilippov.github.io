to hold off on

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɔf ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒf ɒn / (BrE)
to delay or postpone doing something; to wait before proceeding with an action
delay or postpone an action

Examples

Let's hold off on discussing the budget until others arrive.
They decided to hold off on the announcement until they had more information.
We should hold off on making changes until we review all the data.

Synset Examples

He chose to caut his plans until he had more information.
Let's hold off on making a decision until next week.
Can you hold off on that until I get back?
They decided to hold off on the launch due to issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something to a later time
  • Antonym: to proceed - to begin or continue with an action
