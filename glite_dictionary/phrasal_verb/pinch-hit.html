to pinch-hit

1. VERB

/ pɪntʃ hɪt / (AmE), / pɪntʃ hɪt / (BrE)
to substitute for someone temporarily, especially in a role or task; originally used in baseball when a player bats in place of another
temporarily substitute for someone

Examples

When the team needed a quick replacement, she agreed to pinch-hit for the absent manager.
I'm happy to pinch-hit if you need someone to cover your shift this afternoon.
He has pinch-hit for several speakers at conferences when people dropped out unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

He had to pinch-hit for the missing speaker.
She pinch-hit for her colleague during the presentation.
Can you pinch-hit while I take a break?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to take the place of another person or thing

Etymology

Originally from baseball; in use since early 20th century, referring to a batter who replaces another.
