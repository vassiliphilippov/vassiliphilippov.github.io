to push on

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ɑn / (AmE), / pʊʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to continue moving forward, especially with effort or determination
continue moving forward

Examples

Despite the heavy rain, the hikers decided to push on to the next campsite.
Even after several setbacks, the team continued to push on toward their goal.
As the night grew colder, we had no choice but to push on until we found shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to press on - continue moving forward determinedly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01997581-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ ɑn / (AmE), / pʊʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to continue doing something, especially with determination or in spite of difficulties
continue despite difficulties

Examples

Despite the rain and mud, the hikers decided to push on toward the summit.
Even when the workload increased, she continued to push on with her studies.
We faced many setbacks, but our team managed to push on and finish the project.

Synset Examples

We must push on despite the challenges.
Let’s push on until we reach our goal.
Even when tired, they decided to push on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to persist in an action or belief in spite of difficulties
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or abandon an effort
