to cut through

1. VERB

/ kʌt θruː / (AmE), / kʌt θruː / (BrE)
to travel across or pass over something, especially by taking a direct route through it
travel across or pass over

Examples

We can cut through the park to make our walk shorter.
The river cuts through the valley, creating a dramatic landscape.
They cut through the crowd to reach the exit quickly.

Synset Examples

The caravan covered almost 100 miles each day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to move, pass, or extend across something
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌt θru / (AmE), / kʌt θruː / (BrE)
to move or pass through something that blocks or obstructs; to break or penetrate through.
penetrate or break through an obstruction

Examples

The headlights cut through the dense fog, making the road visible again.
Her sharp voice cut through the chatter in the busy restaurant.
A strong wind cut through the silence of the night.

Synset Examples

The sunlight cut through the clouds after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pierce - to go into or through something, especially with force or effort
  • Synonym: to break through - to force a way through a barrier
