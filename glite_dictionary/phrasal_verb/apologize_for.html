to apologize for

1. VERB

/ əˈpɑː.ləˌdʒaɪz fɔr / (AmE), / əˈpɒl.əˌdʒaɪz fə / (BrE)
to express regret or say sorry for something one has done or said, especially for a mistake or offense
express regret for a mistake or offense

Examples

I want to apologize for what happened yesterday at the meeting.
She apologized for being late to the dinner party.
They took a moment to apologize for any inconvenience they may have caused.

Synset Examples

He found it necessary to apologize for his mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apologize - to say sorry or express regret
  • Synonym: excuse - to seek to remove blame or justify actions; sometimes to offer reasons rather than apologies

Etymology

From Greek 'apologizesthai' (to give an account), via Late Latin 'apologizare', + for (preposition).
