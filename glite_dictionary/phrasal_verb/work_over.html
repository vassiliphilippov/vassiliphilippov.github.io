to work over

1. VERB

/ ˈwɝːk ˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to attack someone physically, especially by beating them, usually as a punishment or act of aggression
to assault or beat someone

Examples

The gang threatened to work over anyone who reported them to the police.
After losing the game, he was afraid the older boys would work over him in the locker room.
They worked over the suspect before calling the authorities.

Synset Examples

Thugs beat him up when he walked down the street late at night
The teacher used to beat the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beat up - to hit or physically assault someone repeatedly

2. VERB

/ wɝːk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / wɜːk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to review, examine, or revise something thoroughly and carefully
review or examine thoroughly

Examples

Before submitting your assignment, make sure to work over it for any mistakes.
She had to work over the entire manuscript to catch errors and inconsistencies.
They will work over the proposal together before sending it to the client.

Synset Examples

He decided to work over the contract details carefully.
I need to work over my presentation before the meeting.
She worked over the report to improve its clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go over - to examine or review something carefully
