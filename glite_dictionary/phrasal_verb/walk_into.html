to walk into

1. VERB

/ wɔk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / wɔːk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to unexpectedly encounter or become involved in a situation, often without intending to
to unexpectedly encounter a situation

Examples

I didn't mean to walk into an argument, but suddenly everyone was upset with me.
She managed to walk into an amazing job right after college.
He walked into a complicated situation he hadn't anticipated.

Synset Examples

He didn't expect to walk into a surprise meeting.
I hope to walk into a new opportunity soon.
She accidentally walked into the wrong classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stumble into - to find oneself involved in something by accident
  • Antonym: to walk out of - to leave a situation abruptly, especially to avoid it
