to hedge in

1. VERB

/ hɛdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / hɛdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose or restrict, especially by or as if by a hedge; to surround closely
enclose or restrict closely

Examples

Tall trees hedge in the garden on every side, creating a sense of privacy.
He felt totally hedged in by the strict rules at boarding school.
Developers often hedge in their properties to mark boundaries and keep out animals.

Synset Examples

hedge the property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround on all sides
  • Synonym: to fence in - to enclose with a fence

2. VERB

/ hɛdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / hɛdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
(finance) To protect oneself against financial loss or uncertainty by taking offsetting positions or other limiting actions.
limit financial risk; protect investments

Examples

Many companies hedge in their investments to avoid unexpected losses.
Smart investors hedge in during volatile market conditions.
The bank hedged in its foreign currency exposure to minimize risk.

Synset Examples

Investors tend to hedge in during uncertain times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insure - to protect against financial loss
  • Antonym: to expose - to leave unprotected or open to risk

Etymology

From 'hedge' (to protect, limit) plus preposition 'in'; financial sense developed by analogy to physical hedging.
