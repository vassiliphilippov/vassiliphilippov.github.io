to move back and forth

1. VERB

/ muːv bæk ənd fɔrθ / (AmE), / muːv bæk ənd fɔːθ / (BrE)
to move first in one direction and then in the opposite direction repeatedly
to move in alternating directions

Examples

The pendulum continued to move back and forth with steady rhythm.
When nervous, he tends to move back and forth along the hallway.
Waves move back and forth against the shore with constant motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oscillate - to move or swing back and forth at a regular speed
  • Synonym: rock - to move gently backwards and forwards or from side to side
