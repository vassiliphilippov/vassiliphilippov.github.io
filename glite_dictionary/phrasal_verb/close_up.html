to close up

1. VERB

/ kloʊz ʌp / (AmE), / kləʊz ʌp / (BrE)
to unite or bring together the edges of something, or to bring into contact so that there is no gap or opening
bring together edges or parts

Examples

Please close up the wound with these stitches.
After reading, don't forget to close up the book and put it back on the shelf.
The umbrella was damaged and wouldn't close up properly.

Synset Examples

close the circuit
close a wound
close a book
close up an umbrella

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to open up - to make or become open
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01294544-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kloʊz ʌp / (AmE), / kləʊz ʌp / (BrE)
to stop operating a business, store, or other establishment, either temporarily or permanently; to shut a place down
shut or cease to operate a business/building

Examples

The small bookstore will close up for good at the end of the month.
She reminded the staff to close up the office before leaving on Friday night.
Due to the holiday, we’ll close up early this afternoon.

Synset Examples

The owners decided to move and to close the factory
My business closes every night at 8 P.M.
close up the shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut down - to cease operations, especially of a business or machine
  • Antonym: to open up - to begin or resume operations, especially of a business or premises
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02431242-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kloʊz ʌp / (AmE), / kləʊz ʌp / (BrE)
to stop talking or refuse to speak; to become silent, especially suddenly or unwillingly
fall silent; refuse to speak

Examples

As soon as the detective started asking questions, the witness closed up and wouldn't say a word.
Whenever someone mentions her childhood, she tends to close up and change the subject.
The children closed up instantly when their father entered the room.

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut up - to stop talking or be silent
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01042933-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kloʊz ʌp / (AmE), / kləʊz ʌp / (BrE)
to block or seal a passage, entrance, or opening so that it cannot be used
block or seal an opening

Examples

After the storm, workers had to close up the gaps in the dam.
The old tunnels were closed up to prevent unauthorized entry.
They decided to close up the entrance with bricks for safety reasons.

Synset Examples

obstruct the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seal off - to block entry to something, often for safety or security
  • Antonym: to open up - to open or make accessible a previously closed space
