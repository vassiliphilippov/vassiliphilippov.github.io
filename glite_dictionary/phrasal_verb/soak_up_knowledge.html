to soak up knowledge

1. VERB

/ soʊk ʌp ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / səʊk ʌp ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to absorb information or ideas eagerly and easily
eagerly absorb information

Examples

She loves to soak up knowledge whenever she reads new books.
During his internship, he tried to soak up knowledge from experienced colleagues.
Children often soak up knowledge quickly in their early years.

Synset Examples

Children soak up knowledge quickly when they are young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - take in or soak up information or liquid
  • Synonym: to learn - gain knowledge or skill by studying or experience
