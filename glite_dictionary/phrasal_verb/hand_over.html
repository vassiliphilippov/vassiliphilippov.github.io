to hand over

1. VERB

/ hænd ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / hænd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to give control or possession of someone or something to another person, usually unwillingly or as required by rule or law
surrender or give someone/something to another

Examples

The manager refused to hand over the documents until the lawyer arrived.
She had to hand over her keys when she left the building.
The company will hand over control to the new owners next month.

Synset Examples

the guard delivered the criminal to the police
render up the prisoners
render the town to the enemy
fork over the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to give up to the power or control of another
  • Synonym: to give up - to stop holding or keeping something; to yield control
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep possession or control of something

2. VERB

/ hænd ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / hænd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to give or transfer possession or control of something to someone else, often reluctantly or under obligation
give or transfer possession to someone

Examples

I expect you to hand over all the receipts at the end of the month.
Please hand over your passport to the authorities upon arrival.
The company had to hand over control to new management after the merger.

Synset Examples

She will hand over the report at the meeting.
You must hand over the keys to the new owner.
He refused to hand over the documents to the police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to give up possession, often under pressure
  • Antonym: keep - retain possession of something
