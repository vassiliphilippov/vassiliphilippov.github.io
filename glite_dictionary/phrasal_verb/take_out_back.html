to take out back

1. VERB

/ teɪk aʊt bæk / (AmE), / teɪk aʊt bak / (BrE)
(informal) To resolve a dispute or conflict privately, often by engaging in a physical fight away from others.
resolve a dispute privately, often with a fight
informal

Examples

After the heated argument, they agreed to take it out back to settle things.
The bouncer told them to take it out back if they wanted to fight.
Rather than arguing in the restaurant, Mark suggested they take it out back.

Synset Examples

They decided to take the argument out back to settle it.
