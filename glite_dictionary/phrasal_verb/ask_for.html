to ask for

1. VERB

/ æsk fɔr / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə / (BrE)
to request something or express a desire to have or do something
request or express desire for something

Examples

If you need help, don't be afraid to ask for it.
He went to the counter to ask for directions to the library.
Whenever you visit, remember to ask for Mr. Thompson at the reception desk.

Synset Examples

She requested an extra bed in her room
She called for room service
when you call, always ask for Mary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - to ask for something politely or formally
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00754770-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ æsk fɔr / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə / (BrE)
to behave in a way that makes something, especially trouble or criticism, likely to happen
act in a way that makes something likely to happen

Examples

If you drive that fast on icy roads, you're asking for trouble.
Challenging the boss in front of everyone is really asking for criticism.
By ignoring safety warnings, the company was clearly asking for a disaster.

Synset Examples

ask for trouble
invite criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invite - to cause or make likely (trouble, criticism, etc.) by one's actions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01763987-v
Synset POS: v
