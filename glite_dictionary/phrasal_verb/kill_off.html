to kill off

1. VERB

/ kɪl ɔf / (AmE), / kɪl ɒf / (BrE)
to destroy or kill all or most members of a group, especially deliberately or systematically
destroy or kill all or most of a group

Examples

The invasive species quickly killed off many native plants in the area.
Overhunting has killed off several animal populations in the region.
The company plans to kill off some outdated product lines this year.

Synset Examples

Hitler wanted to exterminate the Jews, Gypsies, Communists, and homosexuals of Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exterminate - to destroy completely, especially living things

2. VERB

/ kɪl ɔf / (AmE), / kɪl ɒf / (BrE)
to eliminate or remove someone or something completely, especially by deliberate action or as a result of harsh conditions
eliminate completely; wipe out

Examples

The studio decided to kill off several minor characters in the series finale.
Scientists warn that pollution could kill off many coral reefs.
The company plans to kill off its unpopular product line next year.

Synset Examples

The producers decided to kill off the main character.
The cold weather can kill off fragile plants.
We need to kill off outdated policies for better efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - remove or get rid of completely
  • Synonym: to destroy - put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • Antonym: preserve - maintain something in its original or existing state
