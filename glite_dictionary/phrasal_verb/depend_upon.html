to depend upon

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd əˈpɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to trust or rely on someone or something with confidence
trust or rely on confidently

Examples

You can always depend upon your friends during tough times.
She knew she could depend upon his discretion in sensitive matters.
The team has learned to depend upon each other for support and encouragement.

Synset Examples

she is someone you can really rely on when times get rough
you can rely on his discretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rely on - to trust or have confidence in someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02670095-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd əˈpɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to be determined or influenced by something; to be contingent on
be determined by; be contingent on

Examples

The success of the project will depend upon careful planning and teamwork.
Whether we go hiking tomorrow will depend upon the weather forecast.
Their decision seemed to depend upon who arrived at the meeting first.

Synset Examples

The outcomes rides on the results of the election
Your grade will depend on your homework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depend on - to be determined by or contingent on something
