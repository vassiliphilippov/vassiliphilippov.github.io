to come up with

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / kʌm ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to produce, think of, or devise an idea, plan, answer, or solution
to produce or invent (an idea or solution)

Examples

We need to come up with a new marketing strategy before the end of the quarter.
She always manages to come up with creative ideas during meetings.
After hours of brainstorming, the team finally came up with a solution to the problem.

Synset Examples

Can you come up with a solution by tomorrow?
He came up with a clever idea for the project.
They struggled to come up with the rations during the crisis.
She managed to come up with a brilliant idea.
He failed to come up with the rations needed for winter.
I need to come up with a new strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to invent or plan something, usually with creativity
  • Synonym: to think of - to have an idea or solution come to mind

2. VERB

/ kʌm ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / kʌm ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to produce, supply, or think of something that is needed or requested
produce or supply something needed

Examples

We need to come up with the rations for our camping trip.
She managed to come up with a solution just before the deadline.
Can you come up with enough money to pay the bill?

Synset Examples

We need to come up with the rations for our camping trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provide - to supply something that is needed or wanted
  • Synonym: supply - to make something available or provide it
  • Antonym: to run out of - to have no more of something left
