to whittle down

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪt̬l daʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪtəl daʊn / (BrE)
to gradually reduce the size, number, or amount of something by removing small parts or pieces
gradually reduce by removing parts

Examples

We need to whittle down our list of candidates to just three.
Over time, the committee was able to whittle down the budget without sacrificing quality.
She whittled down her shoe collection to only her favorite pairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount
  • Antonym: increase - to make something larger in amount or size

Etymology

From 'whittle' (to carve or pare by cutting small slices), of uncertain origin, plus 'down' as reducing direction.

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪt̬l daʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.əl daʊn / (BrE)
to gradually reduce the size, amount, or importance of something
reduce gradually; make smaller or less significant

Examples

We need to whittle down these applicants to just three finalists for the interview.
Her tasks were whittled down so she could focus on the most important issues.
The list of expenses is being whittled down to fit the budget.

Synset Examples

She tried to whittle the project down to its essential parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
  • Synonym: to cut down - to make something smaller or less
  • Antonym: increase - to make greater in size, amount, or importance

Etymology

Based on 'whittle' (to carve or pare something gradually), applied figuratively to abstract reductions.
