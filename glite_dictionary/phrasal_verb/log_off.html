to log off

1. VERB

/ lɔg ɔf / (AmE), / lɒg ɒf / (BrE)
to end a session on a computer system or website by disconnecting or signing out
disconnect from a computer system

Examples

Please log off when you leave the computer lab.
She forgot to log off her email account before leaving the office.
After you finish your work, be sure to log off to protect your information.

Synset Examples

Please log off before you go home

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to log in - to enter a computer system by providing credentials
  • Synonym: sign out - to exit a computer system or website

Etymology

From 'log' (record of activity) + 'off', in reference to ending a logged session.

2. VERB

/ lɔɡ ɑf / (AmE), / lɒɡ ɒf / (BrE)
to disconnect from a computer system or network, ending a session
disconnect from a computer system

Examples

Before you leave the office, remember to log off your computer.
She usually logs off social media at night to improve her sleep.
He forgot to log off and someone else accessed his account.

Synset Examples

You should log off to protect your account.
She decided to log off and take a break.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to log in - to access a computer system by entering a username and password

Etymology

From 'log' (record of activities) + 'off,' reflecting the act of ending a session in early computing.
