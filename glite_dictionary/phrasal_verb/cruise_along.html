to cruise along

1. VERB

/ kruːz əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / kruːz əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to travel at a leisurely pace along a route, typically for pleasure or relaxation
travel leisurely along a route

Examples

We love to cruise along country roads on summer evenings.
The boat began to cruise along the river, passing small villages and forests.
They decided to cruise along the coast, stopping at beaches whenever they wished.

Synset Examples

We decided to cruise along the coast this summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive along - to drive at a steady pace along a route
