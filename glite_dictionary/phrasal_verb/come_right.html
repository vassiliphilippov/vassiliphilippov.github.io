to come right

1. VERB

/ kʌm raɪt / (AmE), / kʌm raɪt / (BrE)
(of a situation) to resolve itself or improve, often after a period of difficulty or concern.
to resolve or improve (of a situation)

Examples

After a few setbacks, everything began to come right at last.
Sarah assured me that the situation would come right if we stayed patient.
He was worried about the project, but it all seemed to come right in the end.

Synset Examples

Just trust the process; it will all come right.
With some effort, things will come right soon.
Don't worry, everything will come right in the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work out - to develop in a satisfactory way or be resolved
  • Antonym: to go wrong - to develop in an unsatisfactory or problematic way
