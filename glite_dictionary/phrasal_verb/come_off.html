to come off

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɑf / (AmE), / kʌm ɒf / (BrE)
(of something fixed or attached) to become detached or separated from where it was attached.
become detached or separated

Examples

The label began to come off after a few days in the rain.
Be careful when you wash the shirt so the buttons don’t come off.
The bicycle's chain might come off if you pedal too hard.

Synset Examples

His retina detached and he had to be rushed into surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall off - to become detached and drop down from something
  • Antonym: stick - to become attached to a surface or object
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01302344-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm ɑf / (AmE), / kʌm ɒf / (BrE)
to happen or take place in a particular way, especially as planned or expected
happen in a particular way

Examples

The event came off without any problems and everyone had a great time.
Her speech came off much better than she expected.
How did your presentation come off in front of the board?

Synset Examples

how did your talk go over?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go over - to happen or be received in a particular way
  • Antonym: to fall through - to fail to happen as planned
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00344552-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kʌm ɑf / (AmE), / kʌm ɒf / (BrE)
(of a part) to become detached or broken from the whole; to separate from something it was attached to.
to become detached or broken off

Examples

Be careful with that chair—the leg might come off if you put too much weight on it.
One of the buttons on my coat has come off and I need to sew it back.
A piece of the wallpaper started to come off when I cleaned the wall.

Synset Examples

Her tooth chipped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break off - to detach or become detached from the main part, especially suddenly

Synset POS: v
