ticked off

1. ADJ

/ tɪkt ɔf / (AmE), / tɪkt ɒf / (BrE)
Annoyed or angry.
annoyed or angry (informal)
informal

Examples

She was really ticked off when she heard about the delays.
I get ticked off every time someone interrupts me at work.
He seems ticked off about something, but he won't say what it is.

Synset Examples

He was ticked off by the last-minute changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong displeasure
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling angry or upset
