to pass by

1. VERB

/ pæs baɪ / (AmE), / pɑːs baɪ / (BrE)
to move past someone or something without stopping
move past without stopping

Examples

I watched the parade pass by my house from the window.
He didn't even notice me as he passed by on his way to work.
Several cars passed by while we waited at the bus stop.

Synset Examples

A black limousine passed by when she looked out the window
He passed his professor in the hall
One line of soldiers surpassed the other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go by - to move past or be seen while moving past
  • Synonym: to overtake - to catch up with and move in front of while traveling in the same direction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02055618-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pæs baɪ / (AmE), / pɑːs baɪ / (BrE)
to be missed or ignored, often while being nearby or accessible; to go past without being noticed or taken up
be missed or ignored

Examples

She let the chance to travel abroad pass by without taking it.
He felt that life was passing by while he stayed in his small town.
Don't let another opportunity pass by—take action while you can.

Synset Examples

It seemed like opportunities always passed him by.
She watched her friends celebrate while the parade passed her by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlook - to fail to notice or consider something important
  • Synonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone
  • Antonym: notice - to become aware of something or someone
Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
