to whistle for help

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪsəl fər hɛlp / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl fə hɛlp / (BrE)
to signal for assistance by whistling, especially in an emergency or when needing rescue
signal for assistance by whistling

Examples

If you get separated from the group, you can whistle for help to let others know your location.
During the hike, he had to whistle for help after injuring his ankle.
As soon as she realized she was lost, she started whistling for help.

Synset Examples

You can whistle for help if you get lost in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call for help - to shout or otherwise make noise to attract help
