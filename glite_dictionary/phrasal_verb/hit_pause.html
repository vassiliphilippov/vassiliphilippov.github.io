to hit pause

1. VERB

/ hɪt pɔz / (AmE), / hɪt pɔːz / (BrE)
to temporarily stop or take a break from an activity, often in order to consider, rest, or reassess before continuing
temporarily stop or take a break
informal

Examples

Before we continue, let's hit pause and review the agenda one more time.
Sometimes you need to hit pause on your work to prevent burnout.
She decided to hit pause on her studies after a stressful semester.

Synset Examples

She needed to hit pause to rethink her career path.
Before making a decision, let's hit pause and discuss.
I decided to hit pause on my plans to travel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop in action or speech

Etymology

Originally referring to pressing the 'pause' button on media devices; now extended metaphorically to life situations.
