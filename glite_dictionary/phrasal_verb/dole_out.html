to dole out

1. VERB

/ doʊl aʊt / (AmE), / dəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to distribute or give something to several people, especially in small portions or amounts
distribute something in small amounts

Examples

The government will dole out food supplies to families in need during the crisis.
She patiently doles out advice to anyone who asks for help at work.
The coach doled out praise to the team after their impressive victory.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present
dole out some money
shell out pocket money for the children
deal a blow to someone
the machine dispenses soft drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or share something among a group
  • Synonym: to hand out - to give something to each person in a group

Etymology

From 'dole', meaning a portion or share, with origins in Middle English and Old English 'dal' (part, share).

2. VERB

/ doʊl aʊt / (AmE), / dəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to give something, especially money, food, or information, to several people, usually in small amounts over a period of time
to give out in portions

Examples

The teacher doled out the test papers to the students one by one.
During the festival, volunteers doled out food to the crowd.
After the meeting, she doled out assignments to each team member.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give things out to a number of people
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or required

Etymology

From the noun 'dole' meaning portion or distributions (Middle English, from Old English 'dal' meaning 'part, share').

3. VERB

/ doʊl aʊt / (AmE), / dəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to distribute or give something, especially in small quantities or over a period of time
distribute or give out

Examples

The manager agreed to dole out bonuses to the top-performing employees.
During the crisis, the government began to dole out emergency funds to communities in need.
She was known to dole out compliments generously, making everyone feel appreciated.

Synset Examples

They decided to dole out food to the homeless.
The charity will dole out supplies to those in need.
It’s kind of her to dole out advice whenever I ask.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or share something to a number of people
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired

Etymology

From "dole" (from Middle English, meaning a portion distributed as charity) + "out".
