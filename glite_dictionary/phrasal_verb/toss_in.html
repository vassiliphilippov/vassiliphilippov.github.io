to toss in

1. VERB

/ tɑs ɪn / (AmE), / tɒs ɪn / (BrE)
to add (a remark or comment) casually or incidentally to a conversation
add a comment casually

Examples

She likes to toss in her opinions even when no one asks.
He tossed in a sarcastic remark during the meeting.
Whenever there's silence, Mark will toss in a random fact.

Synset Examples

‘I don't agree with this,’ she tossed in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw in - to insert or add (something) in a conversation
