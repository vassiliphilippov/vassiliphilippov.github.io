to beat down

1. VERB

/ bit daʊn / (AmE), / biːt daʊn / (BrE)
to persuade someone to reduce the price of something through bargaining or negotiation
persuade to lower a price

Examples

She managed to beat down the price by pointing out several flaws in the item.
After a long discussion, they beat down the landlord to a more reasonable rent.
He tried to beat down the car dealer on the final price but was only partly successful.

Synset Examples

She beat the dealer down to a much better price

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haggle - to argue about the price of something
  • Antonym: raise - to increase the price or amount
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01887543-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ biːt daʊn / (AmE), / biːt daʊn / (BrE)
to persuade someone to reduce the price of something, especially during a negotiation or sale
persuade seller to lower price

Examples

After some tough negotiating, she managed to beat down the price to half its original value.
I tried to beat down the cost of the used car, but the dealer wouldn't budge.
He always attempts to beat down the seller whenever he goes to the market.

Synset Examples

She beat the merchant down $100

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haggle - to dispute or bargain persistently, especially over the cost of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02264194-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bit daʊn / (AmE), / biːt daʊn / (BrE)
(of the sun, rain, etc.) to shine or fall very strongly and directly.
shine or fall strongly (of weather)

Examples

The sun beat down on the hikers as they crossed the desert.
At noon, the heat beat down relentlessly on the empty beach.
Despite the clouds, the rain beat down on the roof throughout the night.

Synset Examples

The sun beat down on the hikers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blaze - shine brightly and intensely (of the sun)
  • Synonym: scorch - to affect painfully with intense heat
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
