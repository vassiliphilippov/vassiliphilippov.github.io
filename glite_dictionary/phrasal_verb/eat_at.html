to eat at

1. VERB

/ it ət / (AmE), / iːt ət / (BrE)
to gradually damage, weaken, or erode something, especially someone's feelings, resolve, or condition
gradually damage or wear away

Examples

The constant criticism began to eat at her self-esteem over time.
Doubt can eat at your confidence if you let it grow.
Financial worries were starting to eat at their relationship.

Synset Examples

Her confidence eroded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away or be worn away
  • Synonym: to wear down - to weaken or damage gradually through persistent action
