to round out

1. VERB

/ raʊnd aʊt / (AmE), / raʊnd aʊt / (BrE)
to make something bigger, better, or more complete by adding to it
make more complete or whole

Examples

She added another chapter to round out the story.
The salad helps to round out the meal.
His experiences abroad rounded out his education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - make whole or finished
  • Synonym: finish - bring to an end or completion
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ raʊnd aʊt / (AmE), / raʊnd aʊt / (BrE)
to express a number as the nearest whole, ten, hundred, etc.
express as a rounded number

Examples

The total was $97.74, but he decided to round out the figure to $100.
For reporting purposes, please round out all sales numbers to the nearest hundred.
We can round out the data to simplify the calculations.
