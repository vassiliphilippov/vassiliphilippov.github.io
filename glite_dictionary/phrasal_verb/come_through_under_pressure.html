to come through under pressure

1. VERB

/ kʌm θruː ˌʌndɚ ˈprɛʃɚ / (AmE), / kʌm θruː ˌʌndə ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
to perform successfully or effectively despite stressful or difficult circumstances
perform well despite stress

Examples

He can always come through under pressure when the team needs him most.
Some athletes never come through under pressure, no matter how skilled they are.
She came through under pressure and delivered a flawless presentation.

Synset Examples

He always manages to come through under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rise to the occasion - to perform better than usual in a challenging situation
  • Antonym: crack under pressure - to fail in a stressful situation
