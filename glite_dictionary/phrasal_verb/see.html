to see

1. VERB

/ siː / (AmE), / siː / (BrE)
to accompany or escort someone to a particular place, especially as a courtesy
escort someone to a place

Examples

Let me see you to the gate before you leave.
He insisted on seeing his guests to their car.
She always sees her children to the school bus in the morning.

Synset Examples

I'll see you to the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - to go with someone to provide company or protection
  • Synonym: to accompany - to go somewhere with someone
