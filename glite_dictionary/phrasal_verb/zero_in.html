to zero in

1. VERB

/ ˈziːroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to adjust a firearm so that it is aimed to hit a target accurately at a specific distance
adjust a firearm's aim for accuracy

Examples

The marksman carefully zeroed in his rifle before the competition began.
Before hunting season, she zeroes in her shotgun to ensure accurate shots.
They spent the afternoon zeroing in their new scopes at the shooting range.

Synset Examples

He zeroed in his rifle at 200 yards

Etymology

From the noun 'zero' (the setting where a gun's sights align with point of impact) + 'in', phrasal sense developed in the 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈziːroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to direct or focus on a particular point or target with precision, especially using navigational aids or as part of a search.
focus or aim precisely at a target

Examples

The missile automatically zeroes in on the source of the signal.
She quickly zeroed in on the key evidence that solved the case.
The searchlights zeroed in on the thief as he tried to escape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - to aim at or direct toward a particular object or goal
  • Synonym: to home in - to move or be aimed toward a target or objective

Etymology

Originally from the term 'zero' used in firearms and navigation, meaning to adjust aim so the sights are aligned with the bullseye (zero point).
