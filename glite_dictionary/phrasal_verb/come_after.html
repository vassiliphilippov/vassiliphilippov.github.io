to come after

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈæftər / (AmE), / kʌm ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
(of an event or thing) to happen after something else in time, especially as a consequence or result.
happen after in time

Examples

A lengthy debate came after the proposal was announced.
The celebrations came after months of hard work.
A sharp decline in sales came after the product recall.

Synset Examples

A terrible tsunami followed the earthquake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to follow - to happen or come after something or someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02726229-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to follow someone in a position, role, or order; to be the successor of someone or something
succeed or follow as successor

Examples

After the CEO resigned, John was chosen to come after him.
Spring comes after winter every year.
Who will come after the current champion if she retires?

Synset Examples

Carter followed Ford
Will Charles succeed to the throne?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to take over a position or title after someone
  • Antonym: to precede - to come before in time or order
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02411715-v
Synset POS: v
