to take down

1. VERB

/ teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to move something or someone from a higher to a lower position
lower from a higher position

Examples

Could you take down the box from the top shelf for me?
She carefully took down the old painting to clean it.
They asked him to take down the ladder after everyone had climbed down.

Synset Examples

take down the vase from the shelf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place

2. VERB

/ teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to write something down, especially so that you can remember it or record it officially
write down; record in writing

Examples

Could you take down my phone number, please?
The secretary took down everything the manager dictated during the meeting.
I always take down important details in my notebook so I don't forget them.

Synset Examples

she noted everything the teacher said that morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note down - to write something down so that you remember it
  • Synonym: to write down - to put information in writing

3. VERB

/ teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to criticize or humiliate someone, usually by undermining their status or reputation, often verbally
criticize or humiliate verbally

Examples

The columnist tried to take down the politician with harsh words in his editorial.
She was known to take down anyone who acted arrogantly in meetings.
His classmates would often take down his confidence with spiteful comments.

4. VERB

/ teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to demolish or dismantle a structure so that it is level with the ground

Examples
