to cool off

1. VERB

/ kuːl ɑf / (AmE), / kuːl ɒf / (BrE)
(of emotions, enthusiasm, or activity) to become less intense or active.
become less intense or active

Examples

After their argument, it took Tom a while to cool off before he was ready to talk.
Excitement over the new product began to cool off after the initial launch.
Her anger will eventually cool off if you just give her some space.

Synset Examples

His enthusiasm cooled considerably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subside - to become less intense, violent, or severe
  • Synonym: to diminish - to become or make less; decrease in size, extent, or range
  • Antonym: to flare up - to suddenly become more intense or worse

2. VERB

/ kuːl ɑf / (AmE), / kuːl ɒf / (BrE)
to become calm or less excited, especially after being angry or agitated
become calmer after agitation

Examples

He needed some time alone to cool off after the heated argument.
Let’s all take a break and cool off before we discuss this further.
She went for a walk to cool off and clear her mind.

Synset Examples

After the fight both men need to cool off.
It took a while after the baby was born for things to settle down again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - become less angry, excited, or upset
  • Antonym: to flare up - suddenly become angry or agitated
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01767681-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kul ɔf / (AmE), / kuːl ɒf / (BrE)
to become less enthusiastic or interested in someone or something; to lose strong feelings of affection or excitement
lose enthusiasm or affection

Examples

After working at the company for a few years, she began to cool off on the idea of staying there long-term.
He was very enthusiastic about the project at first, but now he seems to have cooled off.
Many fans cool off once the excitement of the new season fades away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to warm to - become more enthusiastic about someone or something
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ kul ɔf / (AmE), / kuːl ɒf / (BrE)
to become cooler in temperature or to become calmer or less angry; to reduce heat or emotional intensity
become cooler or calmer

Examples

After exercising, he sat in the shade to cool off.
She needed a few minutes alone to cool off after the heated argument.
The weather finally started to cool off in the evening.

Synset Examples

You should get in the sea to cool off.
Feeling angry, she took a long walk to cool off.
She made fresh lemonade to cool off on a hot day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become less angry or excited
  • Antonym: to heat up - to become warmer; to become more intense (emotionally or otherwise)
