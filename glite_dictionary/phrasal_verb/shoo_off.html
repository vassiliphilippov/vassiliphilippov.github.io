to shoo off

1. VERB

/ ʃu ˈɔf / (AmE), / ʃu ˈɒf / (BrE)
to drive away a person or animal by saying 'shoo!' or by making gestures
drive away by saying 'shoo!' or gesturing

Examples

She tried to shoo off the birds that were pecking at her plants.
The security guard shooed off the teenagers loitering outside the store.
He waved his arms to shoo off the stray dogs near the gate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chase away - to make someone or something leave by chasing them
  • Antonym: invite - to ask someone to come in or stay; opposite of driving away

Etymology

From the exclamation 'shoo,' used since the late 17th century to urge away animals or people.
