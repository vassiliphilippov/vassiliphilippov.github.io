to hobnob with

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑbˌnɑb wɪð / (AmE), / ˈhɒbˌnɒb wɪð / (BrE)
to mix or associate socially, especially with people of higher status or influence
socialize with influential people

Examples

As a journalist, she often gets the chance to hobnob with politicians and business leaders.
He doesn’t like to hobnob with the upper class, preferring the company of old friends.
At the awards ceremony, rising stars eagerly hobnobbed with their idols.

Synset Examples

The new intern was eager to hobnob with the executives.
They love to hobnob with celebrities at the gala.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialize with - to spend time with others in a friendly way

Etymology

From earlier 'hob and nob', meaning 'to drink together', from the phrase 'hob or nob' (give or take), probably from Old English 'habban' (to have) and 'nabban' (not to have).
