to feel like a cuck

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə kʌk / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə kʌk / (BrE)
to feel humiliated, betrayed, or emasculated, especially because of a partner's perceived or actual infidelity
feel humiliated or betrayed (esp. by infidelity)
informal offensive

Examples

After learning about the rumors, he couldn't help but feel like a cuck in front of his friends.
She said the revelation made her feel like a cuck, even though they weren't married.
Whenever he saw them together, he would feel like a cuck, overwhelmed by jealousy and shame.

Synset Examples

He felt like a cuck when he discovered her infidelity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliated - feeling ashamed or embarrassed; having lost pride
  • Synonym: betrayed - feeling let down or deceived, especially by someone trusted

Etymology

From 'cuck', a shortening of 'cuckold', referring historically to a man whose wife is unfaithful.
