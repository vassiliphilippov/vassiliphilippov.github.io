to get fresh with

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt frɛʃ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡɛt freʃ wɪð / (BrE)
to behave in a disrespectful or overly familiar way toward someone, often in a flirtatious or insolent manner
behave disrespectfully or familiarly
informal

Examples

If you get fresh with the teacher, you'll end up in detention.
She warned him not to get fresh with her at the party.
The customer started to get fresh with the waitress, making her uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

Don't get fresh with me, I know what you're up to.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be cheeky - to behave in a bold, rude, or disrespectful manner
  • Antonym: show respect - to act with proper respect or deference
