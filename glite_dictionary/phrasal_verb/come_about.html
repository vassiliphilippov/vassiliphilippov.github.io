to come about

1. VERB

/ kʌm əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / kʌm əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to happen or occur, especially by chance or unexpectedly
to happen; to occur

Examples

The change in policy came about after months of discussion.
I’m not sure how the mistake came about, but we need to fix it.
Major advances in technology often come about through collaboration.

Synset Examples

What is happening?
The meeting took place without an incident
Nothing occurred that seemed important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to happen - to take place; to occur
  • Synonym: to occur - to happen; to take place
