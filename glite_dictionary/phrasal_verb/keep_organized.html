to keep organized

1. VERB

/ kip ɔrɡəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / kiːp ɔːɡəˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
to maintain something in a tidy or orderly condition
maintain tidiness or order

Examples

It is important to keep organized when working on multiple projects at once.
She uses a planner to keep organized throughout the week.
I struggle to keep organized unless everything has its proper place.

Synset Examples

She kept her memories neatly organized in a box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep tidy - to keep something neat and in order
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make untidy or disordered
