to load up on

1. VERB

/ loʊd ʌp ɑn / (AmE), / ləʊd ʌp ɒn / (BrE)
to gather or obtain a large amount of something, or to consume something in large quantities
gather or consume a lot of something

Examples

Before the trip, we should load up on snacks and water.
Many people load up on sunscreen before going to the beach.
During flu season, I always load up on vitamin C.

Synset Examples

They decided to load up on supplies before the storm.
It's wise to load up on water before hiking.
I like to load up on vitamins during winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stock up on - to buy or collect a large quantity of something for future use
