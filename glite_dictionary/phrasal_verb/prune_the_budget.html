to prune the budget

1. VERB

/ prun ðə ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / pruːn ðə ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
to reduce expenses or cut unnecessary items from a budget
reduce budget expenses

Examples

Due to lower revenues, the finance manager had to prune the budget significantly this year.
If we want to stay profitable, we'll need to prune the budget and remove any unnecessary spending.
They regularly prune the budget to adapt to changing market conditions.

Synset Examples

He decided to prune his budget to save more money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut the budget - to reduce the amount of money allocated in a budget

Etymology

From the verb 'prune' (to cut away unwanted parts), figuratively applied to budgets.
