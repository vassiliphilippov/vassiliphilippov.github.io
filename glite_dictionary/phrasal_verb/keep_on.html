to keep on

1. VERB

/ kip ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp ɒn / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to remain in a position, role, or state; to continue to maintain or employ
continue to employ or maintain

Examples

Despite the company's financial troubles, they decided to keep on all their employees.
The manager agreed to keep on the old assistant for another month.
If you work hard, they may keep on offering you contracts in the future.

Synset Examples

this garment retains its shape even after many washings

Related Terms

2. VERB

/ kip ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp ɒn / (BrE)
to continue doing something without stopping
continue doing something

Examples

She told him to keep on working until he finished the project.
If you keep on talking during the movie, I'll have to ask you to leave.
Even after several failures, he decided to keep on trying.

Synset Examples

Keep on smiling, and everything will be alright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process
