to botch up

1. VERB

/ bɑtʃ ʌp / (AmE), / bɒtʃ ʌp / (BrE)
To do something badly or make a mess of a task; to ruin through clumsy or careless work.
do badly; make a mess of
informal

Examples

He tried to fix the leaky sink but ended up botching up the entire plumbing system.
Be careful not to botch up your application, or you might not get the job.
She botched up the recipe and the cake didn't rise properly.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to do something badly or make mistakes
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or correct something

Etymology

From 'botch' (to spoil by poor work), originally Middle English, possibly related to Old French 'boche' (a swelling, bump).
