to rise to the challenge

1. VERB

/ raɪz tu ðə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / raɪz tə ðə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
to respond successfully and with determination when faced with a difficult task or situation
respond successfully to a difficult situation

Examples

Despite limited resources, the community managed to rise to the challenge and rebuild.
Whenever there's a crisis, she knows how to rise to the challenge.
The players were nervous but determined to rise to the challenge of the finals.

Synset Examples

During tough times, he managed to rise to the challenge.
Their team will rise to the challenge, no doubt.
She always knows how to rise to the challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet the challenge - to deal with or fulfill the requirements of a difficult situation
