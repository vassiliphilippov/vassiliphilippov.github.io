to jack up the volume

1. VERB

/ dʒæk ʌp ðə ˈvɑːl.jum / (AmE), / dʒæk ʌp ðə ˈvɒl.juːm / (BrE)
to increase the loudness of music or sound, especially by turning up a control
increase the volume
informal

Examples

Could you jack up the volume? I can barely hear the music.
The DJ decided to jack up the volume as the crowd got more energetic.
If you jack up the volume on your headphones, be careful not to damage your hearing.

Synset Examples

They plan to jack the volume for the next performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn up the volume - to increase the volume
  • Antonym: lower the volume - to decrease the volume

Etymology

From the informal phrasal verb 'jack up' meaning 'to raise or increase', combined with 'volume'.
