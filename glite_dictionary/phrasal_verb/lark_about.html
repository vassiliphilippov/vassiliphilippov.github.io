to lark about

1. VERB

/ lɑrk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / lɑːk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to behave in a playful, silly, or boisterous way, often making jokes or getting into mischief
play or behave in a silly way
informal british

Examples

After school, the children love to lark about in the playground until it's time to go home.
Stop larking about and help me tidy up the living room, please.
They were larking about so much in the store that the manager had to ask them to quiet down.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly or playful way
  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: to mess about - to behave in a silly or aimless way

Etymology

From 'lark' (slang sense: a playful or mischievous act), British English slang, early 19th century.
