to spit out

1. VERB

/ spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to eject something from the mouth suddenly and forcefully
eject suddenly from the mouth

Examples

He tasted the soup and immediately spit out a mouthful because it was too salty.
After chewing the gum for a while, she decided to spit out the flavorless wad.
The young child spit out his vegetables when he didn't like the taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spit up - to bring up something from the stomach or mouth, often for babies
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00102057-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to say something quickly or forcefully, especially with anger or contempt
say something angrily or forcefully

Examples

He spat out the accusation before anyone could stop him.
She spit out her response without even looking up from her desk.
He spits out insults at anyone who disagrees with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - to say something suddenly and angrily
  • Degree Lower: mutter - to speak in a low, angry or complaining voice
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01047190-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to force (phlegm, sputum, or another substance) from the lungs or mouth by ejecting it forcefully
eject phlegm or matter from the mouth

Examples

The patient was instructed to spit out any phlegm after coughing.
Please do not spit out gum on the sidewalk.
He coughed violently and spit out a thick glob of sputum into the tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expectorate - to cough or spit out phlegm from the throat or lungs
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00006238-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to forcefully expel something from the mouth
expel from the mouth

Examples

She quickly spit out the sour candy when she realized how bad it tasted.
Please spit out your gum before entering the classroom.
The child spat out the medicine as soon as she tasted it.

Synset Examples

He had to spit out the gum before entering the store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spew - to eject forcefully, often refers to liquid or vomit
