to drop the writ

1. VERB

/ drɑp ðə rɪt / (AmE), / drɒp ðə rɪt / (BrE)
to withdraw, dismiss, or cancel a legal document known as a writ
withdraw or cancel a legal writ
legal

Examples

After new evidence emerged, the prosecution agreed to drop the writ and let the case go.
If the company chooses to drop the writ, the lawsuit will come to an immediate end.
The lawyer advised her client to drop the writ to avoid further legal costs.

Synset Examples

They chose to drop the writ rather than pursue the case.
The judge decided to drop the writ after reviewing the evidence.
He was relieved when the court decided to drop the writ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to remove or take away something, often officially
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to formally remove a case, charge, or document from consideration
