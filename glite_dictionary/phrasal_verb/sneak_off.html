to sneak off

1. VERB

/ snik ɔf / (AmE), / sniːk ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation quietly and secretly, so as not to be noticed
leave secretly or quietly

Examples

She tried to sneak off before anyone noticed she was gone.
During the meeting, Tom managed to sneak off for a quick phone call.
The children sneaked off to play outside after finishing their homework.

Synset Examples

The lecture was boring and many students slipped out when the instructor turned towards the blackboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip away - to leave quietly and unnoticed
  • Synonym: to slip out - to leave furtively, especially from a place or group
