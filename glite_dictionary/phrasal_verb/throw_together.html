to throw together

1. VERB

/ θɹoʊ təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / θrəʊ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to produce or assemble something hastily and without much care or attention to detail
make hastily or carelessly

Examples

I had to throw together a quick dinner for unexpected guests last night.
She threw together the report just before the meeting, so it lacked detail.
The decorations looked like they had been thrown together at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cobble together - to put together roughly or hastily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01743556-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈθroʊ təˌɡɛðər / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ təˌɡɛðə / (BrE)
to arrange or assemble things quickly or without a clear plan, often in a careless or random order
assemble or arrange quickly and randomly

Examples

We had to throw together a presentation at the last minute.
She threw together dinner with whatever she found in the fridge.
The report looked as if it had been thrown together without much thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to bring or gather things together
  • Antonym: throw apart - to separate or scatter what was brought together
