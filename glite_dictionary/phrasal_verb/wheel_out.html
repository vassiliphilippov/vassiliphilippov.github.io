to wheel out

1. VERB

/ wil aʊt / (AmE), / wiːl aʊt / (BrE)
to present, introduce, or show something, especially something that has been prepared in advance or is used routinely
present or introduce (often routinely)

Examples

Every year, the company wheels out its classic holiday ad campaign.
The politician was wheeled out to answer questions on the scandal.
During the festival, they wheel out traditional recipes from the region.

Synset Examples

The team will wheel out the new software next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring out - to make something available or introduce it to the public
  • Synonym: to roll out - to officially launch or introduce a new product or service
