to clap up

1. VERB

/ klæp ʌp / (AmE), / klæp ʌp / (BrE)
To make or put together hastily and carelessly.
make/assemble hastily and carelessly

Examples

They managed to clap up a shelter with whatever materials were lying around.
He clapped up an excuse for being late, but no one believed him.
The committee clapped up a set of rules without much thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cobble together - to make something quickly and not very carefully
