to go straight down

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ streɪt daʊn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ streɪt daʊn / (BrE)
to proceed directly without turning or deviating along a particular path or route, usually downward or further along a road, street, or corridor
proceed directly along a route or downward

Examples

If you go straight down the hallway, you'll find the conference room on your left.
To reach the main square, just go straight down this street for two blocks.
They told me to go straight down the stairs to get to the lobby.

Synset Examples

Just go straight down this road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go straight - proceed directly without turning
