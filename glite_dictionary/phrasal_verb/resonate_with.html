to resonate with

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛzəˌneɪt wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪt wɪð / (BrE)
to evoke a feeling of shared emotion, understanding, or strong response in someone
evoke a strong emotional response

Examples

The movie's powerful message really resonated with the audience.
Her personal story resonated with people from all walks of life.
The candidate's promises failed to resonate with younger voters.

Synset Examples

Khia's music resonates with many youth today.
His story seemed to resonate with many listeners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike a chord - to cause someone to feel sympathy or emotion; to resonate with
