to wash oneself of

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ wʌnˈsɛlf əv / (AmE), / wɒʃ wʌnˈsɛlf ɒv / (BrE)
to rid oneself of guilt, blame, responsibility, or negative feelings
rid oneself of guilt, blame, or negative feelings

Examples

She hoped to wash herself of regret by talking to her friend.
Many people seek ways to wash themselves of past mistakes and move forward.
He tried to wash himself of guilt after the argument.

Synset Examples
