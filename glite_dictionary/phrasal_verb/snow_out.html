to snow out

1. VERB

/ snoʊ aʊt / (AmE), / snəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to be forced to miss or cancel an event or plans because of snow or bad weather
miss/cancel plans due to snow
informal

Examples

The concert was snowed out due to the blizzard last night.
We had to snow out of our holiday party because the roads were too dangerous.
Many students snow out of classes during major storms in the winter.

Synset Examples

We decided to snow out of the party due to the weather.
They had to snow out early because of the heavy snowfall.
She chose to snow out and enjoy a quiet evening instead.
