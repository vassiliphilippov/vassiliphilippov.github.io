to scare up

1. VERB

/ skɛr ʌp / (AmE), / skeə ʌp / (BrE)
to find, obtain, or gather something, often with some difficulty or effort
obtain or gather with effort

Examples

Can you scare up some volunteers for tomorrow's cleanup?
Despite the short notice, she managed to scare up a few last-minute gifts.
They tried to scare up enough chairs for everyone at the meeting.

Synset Examples

Can you scare up some volunteers for the event?
He managed to scare up enough donations for the cause.
Let’s scare up some fun activities for the kids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rustle up - to prepare or obtain something quickly, especially food
  • Synonym: find - to discover or obtain something
