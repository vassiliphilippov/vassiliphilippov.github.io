to arouse curiosity

1. VERB

/ əˈraʊz ˌkjʊriˈɑsəti / (AmE), / əˈraʊz ˌkjʊəriˈɒsəti / (BrE)
to stimulate someone's interest or desire to know more about something
stimulate someone's interest or wonder

Examples

The documentary managed to arouse curiosity about ancient civilizations among the students.
New scientific discoveries often arouse curiosity in the media and the public.
A colorful display in the storefront window was enough to arouse curiosity among passersby.

Synset Examples

The book can arouse curiosity about history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provoke curiosity - to cause someone to feel curious
  • Antonym: satisfy curiosity - to fulfill or answer someone's curiosity
