to rinse off

1. VERB

/ rɪns ɔf / (AmE), / rɪns ɒf / (BrE)
to remove soap, dirt, or residue from something by washing it quickly with water
wash soap or dirt away with water

Examples

Please rinse off all the soap before leaving the shower.
She quickly rinsed off the vegetables under cold water.
After swimming, he had to rinse off the chlorine from his skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wash off - to remove something from a surface by washing

2. VERB

/ rɪns ɔf / (AmE), / rɪns ɒf / (BrE)
to wash away soap, dirt, or residue using clean water, typically as the final step of cleaning
wash away dirt or soap with water

Examples

After scrubbing the dishes, make sure to rinse off all the soap before drying them.
He quickly rinsed off in the outdoor shower after swimming in the pool.
You should always rinse off mud from your shoes before coming inside.

Synset Examples

The outdoor aluminum shower was perfect for rinsing off.
