to play to win

1. VERB

/ pleɪ tə wɪn / (AmE), / pleɪ tə wɪn / (BrE)
to compete or take part in an activity with the intention of achieving victory or success, rather than just participating
compete with aim to win

Examples

If you want to improve quickly, you need to play to win every match.
Some people just enjoy the game, but I always play to win.
The coach encouraged the team to play to win instead of settling for a draw.

Synset Examples

She always enters competitions ready to play to win.
If you want to succeed, you have to play to win.
In this game, we must play to win and not just participate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: play for fun - to participate in a game or activity mainly for enjoyment rather than to win
