to stay fresh

1. VERB

/ steɪ frɛʃ / (AmE), / steɪ frɛʃ / (BrE)
to remain in a good, unspoiled condition; not to rot or spoil
remain unspoiled or good

Examples

These berries should stay fresh for up to a week if refrigerated properly.
I always buy bread from this bakery because it tends to stay fresh longer than others.
Once opened, the juice won't stay fresh for more than a couple of days.

Synset Examples

These potatoes keep for a long time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spoil - become unfit for consumption; deteriorate
  • Synonym: keep - remain in a good or usable condition
