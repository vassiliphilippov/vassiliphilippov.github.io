to hold together

1. VERB

/ hoʊld təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / həʊld təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to keep parts joined or united; to remain whole or functional
keep parts joined; stay united

Examples

The old book was so fragile it could barely hold together without extra care.
Strong leadership helped the group hold together during tough times.
If the parts don't hold together, the structure will collapse.

Synset Examples

Despite the challenges, the team managed to hold together.
The glue should help hold together the broken vase.
They used tape to hold together the torn pages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces or stop being effective
  • Synonym: to stick together - to remain united or attached

2. VERB

/ hoʊld təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / həʊld təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to remain unified or keep something unified, maintaining its structure or function
remain unified or keep unified

Examples

The old book barely held together after so many years of use.
Her leadership held together the team during the crisis.
The cake held together surprisingly well, even without eggs.

Synset Examples

The glue held the project together perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces or fail to remain unified
