to top and tail

1. VERB

/ tɑp ənd teɪl / (AmE), / tɒp ənd teɪl / (BrE)
to remove both ends from a vegetable or similar item, especially before cooking
remove both ends of a vegetable

Examples

Before boiling the beans, you should always top and tail them for best results.
She quickly topped and tailed the carrots before adding them to the salad.
He spent the afternoon topping and tailing a large pile of fresh rhubarb.

Synset Examples

He showed me how to top and tail the radishes.
