to reel off

1. VERB

/ ril ɔf / (AmE), / riːl ɒf / (BrE)
to unwind or unroll something from a reel, or as if from a reel
unwind or unroll from a reel

Examples

He began to reel off the fishing line as the fish pulled away.
She carefully reeled off several meters of thread for her project.
The technician reels off cable from the spool to connect the devices.

Synset Examples

unreel the tape
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01526308-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ril ɔf / (AmE), / riːl ɒf / (BrE)
to recite or list a series of things quickly and easily, often without pause or much thought
recite or list quickly and easily

Examples

At the meeting, she could reel off statistics about the company's performance without hesitation.
He likes to reel off football scores to impress his friends.
When asked about the capitals of Europe, she reeled off the answers effortlessly.

Synset Examples

he could rattle off the names of all the chemical elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rattle off - to say or list things rapidly and easily

Etymology

Figurative use of 'reel' (as in winding or unwinding something like thread or film) plus 'off'.
