to let out a curse

1. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt ə kɝs / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt ə kɜːs / (BrE)
to utter a curse or swear word, especially as an expression of anger, frustration, or pain
utter a curse or swear word

Examples

Frustrated by his mistake, he let out a curse under his breath.
She accidentally dropped the plate and let out a curse in the kitchen.
Whenever he stubs his toe, he can't help but let out a curse.

Synset Examples

He let out a curse after stubbing his toe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to swear - to use offensive language, especially as an expression of anger
  • Antonym: to bless - to speak words of approval or wish good upon someone, opposite of cursing
