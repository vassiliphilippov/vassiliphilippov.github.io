to run high

1. VERB

/ rʌn haɪ / (AmE), / rʌn haɪ / (BrE)
(of emotions, expectations, or tensions) to be very intense or strong.
become very intense or strong (about feelings, etc.)

Examples

Emotions often run high during heated political debates.
At the end of the season, tensions run high among the players and coaches.
Hopes ran high before the launch of the new product.

Synset Examples

Emotions run high when discussing politics.
Expectations run high for the upcoming concert.
Tensions run high during the playoffs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to run low - to be at a low level or become less intense (about resources, feelings, etc.)
