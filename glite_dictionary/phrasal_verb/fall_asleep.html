to fall asleep

1. VERB

/ fɔl əˈslip / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈsliːp / (BrE)
to begin to sleep after being awake
begin to sleep

Examples

I usually fall asleep within minutes of going to bed.
She was so tired that she fell asleep during the movie.
You might fall asleep if you keep reading in that comfortable chair.

Synset Examples

he always falls asleep during lectures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to wake up - to stop sleeping and become conscious
  • Synonym: to doze off - to start sleeping, especially unintentionally or briefly

2. VERB

/ fɔl əˈslip / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈsliːp / (BrE)
to begin sleeping; to enter a state of sleep
begin to sleep

Examples

He tends to fall asleep as soon as his head hits the pillow.
I sometimes fall asleep on the train ride home after work.
Try not to fall asleep during the professor's long lecture.

Synset Examples

I always fall asleep while reading in bed.
It's easy to fall asleep during boring lectures.
She tried not to fall asleep during the movie.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to wake up - to stop sleeping and become awake
  • Synonym: to nod off - to fall asleep, especially briefly or unintentionally
