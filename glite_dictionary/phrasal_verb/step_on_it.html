to step on it

1. VERB

/ stɛp ɑn ɪt / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn ɪt / (BrE)
to move or act more quickly; to hurry, especially when driving or traveling
to go faster; to hurry up
informal

Examples

If we want to catch the last train, we'd better step on it.
The taxi driver stepped on it when he heard we were late for our flight.
Come on, step on it or we'll miss the beginning of the movie.

Synset Examples

He rushed down the hall to receive his guests
The cars raced down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - to do something more quickly
  • Antonym: to slow down - reduce speed; go more slowly
