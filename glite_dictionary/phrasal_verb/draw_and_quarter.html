to draw and quarter

1. VERB

/ drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːrtɚ / (AmE), / drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːtə / (BrE)
to execute someone by tying them to four horses and pulling them apart; also, to punish someone extremely severely (often used figuratively).
execute by dismemberment; punish extremely severely

Examples

In medieval times, people would draw and quarter traitors as a public warning.
Some critics threatened to draw and quarter the director for ruining the classic film.
He exaggerated, saying his parents would draw and quarter him for breaking curfew.

Synset Examples

In medieval times, they would draw and quarter criminals publicly.
He joked about the punishment being as harsh as draw and quarter.
The old legend said they would draw and quarter traitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismember - to cut off the limbs of a person or animal
  • Synonym: to execute - to put someone to death as a legal penalty
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from inflicting punishment or harm

Etymology

From the historical method of execution in England, where criminals were "drawn" (dragged) and then "quartered" (dismembered).
