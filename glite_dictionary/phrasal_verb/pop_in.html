to pop in

1. VERB

/ pɑp ɪn / (AmE), / pɒp ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place quickly or briefly, usually for a short visit
enter briefly; make a short visit

Examples

Could you pop in to my office this afternoon for a quick chat?
She decided to pop in at her friend's house on her way home from work.
I just wanted to pop in and see how you were feeling after your operation.

Synset Examples

He popped in for two minutes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drop in - to visit someone informally and without arrangement
  • Antonym: to pop out - to leave a place quickly or briefly

2. VERB

/ pɑp ɪn / (AmE), / pɒp ɪn / (BrE)
to visit someone briefly and informally, often without prior notice
pay a brief, informal visit
informal

Examples

I’ll pop in later this afternoon to drop off the documents.
My neighbors sometimes pop in for a quick chat after dinner.
You can pop in whenever you’re in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

We should pop by and say hello to her.
I’ll pop in later to check on you.
She decided to pop in for a quick visit.
Feel free to pop in anytime for coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drop in - to visit someone without making arrangements in advance; sometimes used interchangeably with 'pop in'
  • Synonym: to call in - to make a short visit, especially in British English
