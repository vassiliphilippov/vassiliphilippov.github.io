to heave up

1. VERB

/ hiv ʌp / (AmE), / hiːv ʌp / (BrE)
to lift or raise something, often with effort or force
lift or raise with effort

Examples

They managed to heave up the heavy box onto the shelf.
With a grunt, she heaved up the anchor from the riverbed.
Workers heaved up the steel beam into position using a crane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoist - to raise or lift, especially with mechanical means
  • Antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position

Etymology

From Old English 'hebban' (to lift) and 'up' (direction).
