to take notice

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / teɪk ˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
to observe or become aware of something with particular attention or interest
pay special attention; become aware

Examples

She failed to take notice of the warning signs until it was too late.
Tourists often take notice of the city's historic landmarks and monuments.
He spoke so softly that no one seemed to take notice.

Synset Examples

Take notice of the great architecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notice - to become aware of someone or something; to observe
  • Antonym: to ignore - to intentionally pay no attention to someone or something
