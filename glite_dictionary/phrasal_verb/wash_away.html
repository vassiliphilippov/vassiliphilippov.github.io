to wash away

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɒʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to disregard or eliminate something as unimportant or irrelevant
set aside as irrelevant

Examples

They tried to wash away all the criticism by shifting the topic to their achievements.
The joy of the festival helped residents wash away their worries and disagreements.
Time can wash away old grievances, allowing people to start anew.

Synset Examples

wash away all the differences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
  • Synonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

2. VERB

/ wɑʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɒʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or carry something off by the use of water or another liquid, often with soap or a cleaning agent
remove by washing with water or liquid

Examples

The heavy rain managed to wash away all the mud from the driveway.
She tried to wash away the ink stain, but it was stubborn.
A strong current can wash away small bridges during a flood.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to make something free from dirt or impurities

3. VERB

/ wɑʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɒʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate (something) completely, typically by the action of water or a similar force
remove or eliminate completely

Examples

The summer rains will wash away the dust from the streets.
She hoped that a good cry would wash away her sadness.
Over time, nature will wash away all traces of the old path.

Synset Examples

Tears can wash away the pain, even if just for a moment.
Time can wash away old grievances if we let it.
The rain will wash away the dirt on the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove all traces of something
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep or continue to have something
