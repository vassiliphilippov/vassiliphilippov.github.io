to help oneself

1. VERB

/ hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to take or use something for oneself, often without asking for permission
take or use for oneself, often without permission

Examples

At the party, guests were allowed to help themselves to the buffet.
When no one was looking, he helped himself to a slice of cake.
Please help yourself to some coffee while you wait.

Synset Examples

She helped herself to some of the office supplies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take - to get or acquire something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01196102-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to be unable to stop oneself from doing something, especially something undesirable; usually used in negative or interrogative contexts.
be unable to stop oneself

Examples

I know I shouldn't eat more cake, but I just can't help myself.
He tried not to laugh during the meeting, but he couldn't help himself.
Whenever she hears good news, she can't help herself from sharing it with everyone.

Synset Examples

I can't help myself — I have to smoke
She could not help watching the sad spectacle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restrain oneself - to stop oneself from acting on an impulse or desire
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02732039-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to take food or drink for oneself without waiting to be served; to serve oneself, especially at a meal.
serve oneself (with food or drink)

Examples

Feel free to help yourself to some coffee while you wait.
At the buffet, guests can help themselves to as much food as they like.
If you're hungry, just help yourself from the fridge.

Synset Examples

I served him three times, and after that he helped himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve oneself - to take food or drink for oneself
