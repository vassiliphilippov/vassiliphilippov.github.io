to burgeon forth

1. VERB

/ ˈbɜrdʒən fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdʒən fɔːθ / (BrE)
(of a plant or shoot) to begin to grow or develop rapidly; to sprout buds or branches.
begin to grow or sprout vigorously

Examples

In spring, tiny green shoots burgeon forth from the ground after months of winter dormancy.
New leaves burgeoned forth on the old oak tree with the arrival of warmer weather.
The seeds planted last week have finally begun to burgeon forth in the garden.

Synset Examples

the potatoes sprouted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprout - to begin to grow; shoot forth as a plant from a seed

Etymology

From Middle English 'burgeonen' (grow, bud), influenced by Old French 'bourgeonner'. 'Forth' added as an adverb to emphasize outward growth.
