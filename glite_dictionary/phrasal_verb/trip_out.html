to trip out

1. VERB

/ trɪp aʊt / (AmE), / trɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to experience altered mental or emotional states, especially as a result of taking drugs; to become high or stoned
become intoxicated or high (on drugs)
informal slang

Examples

He used to trip out on weekends when he was in college.
Some people trip out after taking psychedelic mushrooms.
She was tripping out and couldn't stop laughing at everything.

Synset Examples

He trips every weekend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get high - to become intoxicated, especially with drugs
  • Antonym: to sober up - to become sober after being intoxicated

WordNet ID: oewn-01203572-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ trɪp aʊt / (AmE), / trɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to become extremely emotional, excited, irrational, or distracted, often unexpectedly
become overly excited or irrational
informal

Examples

He tends to trip out when things get overwhelming at work.
The kids tripped out after seeing the surprise party decorations.
I started to trip out when the music got really loud and intense.

Synset Examples

She tends to trip out when she's really tired.
They always trip out over the latest tech gadgets.
He tripped out on the vibrant colors during the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak out - to become very anxious, upset, or excited

Etymology

From the verb 'trip' (to stumble) + 'out'; influenced by 1960s slang relating to psychedelic experiences.
