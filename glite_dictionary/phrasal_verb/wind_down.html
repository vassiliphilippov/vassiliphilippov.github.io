to wind down

1. VERB

/ waɪnd daʊn / (AmE), / waɪnd daʊn / (BrE)
to relax after a period of activity or stress, or to come gradually to an end
relax or finish gradually

Examples

After a stressful day at work, I like to wind down with a cup of tea.
The party began to wind down as guests started leaving.
We need to wind down the project over the next few weeks.

Synset Examples

We usually wind down our meetings with a recap.
After a long week, I need to wind down at the beach.
She likes to wind down with a good book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to become less tense or anxious
  • Synonym: to wrap up - to finish or conclude something
