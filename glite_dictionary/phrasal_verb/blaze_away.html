to blaze away

1. VERB

/ bleɪz əˈweɪ / (AmE), / bleɪz əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to speak forcefully and passionately, often criticizing or attacking someone energetically
speak passionately and forcefully

Examples

At the town hall meeting, she blazed away at the mayor’s new policies.
The journalist blazed away at the government’s handling of the crisis during the broadcast.
During the debate, he stood up and blazed away at his critics for several minutes.

Synset Examples

He blazed away at his opponents in the Senate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to criticize or oppose someone strongly
  • Antonym: to defend - to protect or support someone against criticism
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00769963-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bleɪz əˈweɪ / (AmE), / bleɪz əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to fire a gun or other weapon rapidly and repeatedly
shoot rapidly and repeatedly

Examples

The soldiers blazed away at the advancing enemy without pause.
He blazed away at the targets as soon as the signal was given.
The outlaws blazed away from behind cover, making it hard for the posse to approach.

Synset Examples

He blazed away at the men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fire away - to begin shooting or to shoot repeatedly

3. VERB

/ bleɪz əˈweɪ / (AmE), / bleɪz əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to perform an artistic passage, such as in music or acting, with exceptional brilliance and speed
perform brilliantly and rapidly (arts)

Examples

The pianist blazed away during the encore, leaving the audience breathless.
She blazed away through her monologue, drawing praise for her energy and skill.
In the competition, he blazed away from one solo to the next with dazzling confidence.

Synset Examples

Mr. Jones blazed away in one passage after another to loud applause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dash off - to do something quickly or hurriedly, especially with skill
