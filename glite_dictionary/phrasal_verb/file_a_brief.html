to file a brief

1. VERB

/ faɪl ə briːf / (AmE), / faɪl ə briːf / (BrE)
to formally submit a written legal argument or statement to a court
submit written argument to court
legal

Examples

The attorney had to file a brief before the hearing deadline.
Both parties agreed to file a brief supporting their respective arguments.
Her legal team filed a brief challenging the court's earlier decision.

Synset Examples
