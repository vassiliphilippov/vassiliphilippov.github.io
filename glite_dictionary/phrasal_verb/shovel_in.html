to shovel in

1. VERB

/ ˈʃʌvəl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl ɪn / (BrE)
to earn or receive large amounts of money, especially quickly or with little effort
earn large sums of money
informal

Examples

Despite the economic downturn, the company continues to shovel in profits each quarter.
He managed to shovel in a fortune after launching his online business.
Some celebrities shovel in millions just for appearing in commercials.

Synset Examples

Since she accepted the new position, she has been raking it in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rake in - to earn or receive a lot of money

2. VERB

/ ˈʃʌvəl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl ɪn / (BrE)
to eat a large amount of food quickly and greedily
eat food quickly and greedily
informal

Examples

At the buffet, he started to shovel in the pasta as if he hadn't eaten in days.
You can't just shovel in your dinner and run; we should enjoy the meal.
The kids shoveled in their cereal before rushing off to school.

Synset Examples

The children gobbled down most of the birthday cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gobble down - to eat food quickly and greedily
  • Synonym: to wolf down - to eat food very quickly and eagerly
