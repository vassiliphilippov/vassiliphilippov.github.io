to gum up

1. VERB

/ ɡʌm ʌp / (AmE), / ɡʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to cause something to become clogged or stuck, especially by a sticky substance; to make something stop working properly by causing it to become stuck
to clog or stick together

Examples

Years of neglect had gummed up the machinery beyond repair.
If you use too much glue, it'll gum up the moving parts.
Grease and dirt can quickly gum up a bicycle chain.

Synset Examples

the inside of the pipe has gummed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clog - to block or become blocked so movement is difficult
  • Synonym: jam - to become or make stuck so that movement or operation is prevented

Etymology

From 'gum' (a sticky substance), used figuratively since early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ɡʌm ʌp / (AmE), / ɡʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to cause something to become blocked, jammed, or to stop functioning smoothly
cause to become blocked or jammed
informal american

Examples

If too much dust gets inside, it will gum up the machine's gears.
A little miscommunication can gum up the entire process.
The copy machine gummed up right before our big meeting.

Synset Examples

The printer will gum up if you use cheap paper.
Don't let that project gum up your schedule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - make unable to move or work by blocking
  • Synonym: clog - block or become obstructed

Etymology

From 'gum' (sticky substance), used figuratively to mean hinder or cause to stick; first used in this sense in early 20th century American English.
