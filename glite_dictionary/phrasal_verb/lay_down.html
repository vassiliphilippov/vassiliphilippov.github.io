to lay down

1. VERB

/ leɪ daʊn / (AmE), / leɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to officially establish or state a rule, law, or principle that must be followed
establish a rule or principle

Examples

The committee will lay down new guidelines for the safety procedures.
The law lays down strict penalties for tax evasion.
Before you start, let me lay down a few ground rules.

Synset Examples

make laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enact - to make (a law) officially valid
  • Antonym: repeal - to officially cancel a law or regulation
