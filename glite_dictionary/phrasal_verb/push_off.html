to push off

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ɔf / (AmE), / pʊʃ ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) to leave or to start a journey; to go away.
to leave; to begin a journey (informal)
informal

Examples

It's getting late—we should push off if we want to arrive before dark.
After finishing their coffee, the hikers decided to push off for the mountain trail.
He told the annoying salesman to push off and leave him alone.

Synset Examples

She told him to push off and stop bothering her.
We need to push off before the rain starts.
It’s time to push off and start our journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set off - to begin a journey
  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially to start a journey
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey
