to go steady

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
to have a regular romantic relationship with someone, often exclusively and over a period of time
be in a steady romantic relationship

Examples

My sister and her boyfriend decided to go steady after dating for a few months.
Are you and Jake going steady, or is it just casual?
They went steady all through college before getting married.

Synset Examples

Did you know that she is seeing an older man?
He is dating his former wife again!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: date - to go out with someone socially as a romantic partner
  • Antonym: to break up - to end a romantic relationship
