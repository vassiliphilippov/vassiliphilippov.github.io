to jockey for position

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑːki fər pəˈzɪʃ(ə)n / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒki fə pəˈzɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
to compete or maneuver to gain an advantage or a better place relative to others, especially before a decision or event
compete to gain advantage or better place

Examples

Several tech companies are jockeying for position in the competitive AI market.
The runners tried to jockey for position at the start of the race.
Politicians often jockey for position ahead of important elections.

Synset Examples

In the meeting, everyone tried to jockey for position.
Employees often jockey for position when promotions are announced.
They had to jockey for position during the intense bidding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - to strive against others for an objective
  • Synonym: maneuver - to move skillfully or carefully to achieve a goal

Etymology

From 'jockey', originally meaning a professional horse rider, plus 'for position'; figurative use developed in the 19th century.
