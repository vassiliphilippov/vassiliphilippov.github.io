to head home

1. VERB

/ hɛd hoʊm / (AmE), / hɛd həʊm / (BrE)
to go or return to one's home, especially after being elsewhere
to go or return home

Examples

It's getting late, so I think I'll head home now.
After visiting the museum, we decided to head home before sunset.
She grabbed her umbrella and started heading home as it began to rain.

Synset Examples

After the movie, we went home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go home - to move toward one's home; depart for home
  • Degree Lower: leave - to depart from a place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02009510-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hɛd hoʊm / (AmE), / hɛd həʊm / (BrE)
to go back to one’s home, especially at the end of the day or after an activity
go back to one’s home

Examples

After the meeting ended, we decided to head home and relax.
It's getting dark, so let's head home before it gets any later.
She usually heads home around six o'clock every evening.

Synset Examples

It's starting to get late, we should head home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return home - to go back to your home
  • Antonym: to leave home - to depart from one's home
