to move around

1. VERB

/ muːv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / muːv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to travel from place to place, especially for work, preaching, or performing official duties
travel from place to place for work or duties

Examples

As a young doctor, she had to move around the country taking temporary assignments.
Missionaries often move around from village to village to spread their message.
The judge would move around the region, holding court in different towns each month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel - to go from one place to another, typically over a distance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02106280-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ muːv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / muːv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to pass to the other side of an object or obstacle by changing direction
go to the other side of something

Examples

She had to move around the fallen tree to reach the gate.
We watched the cat carefully move around the sleeping dog.
The hikers decided to move around the large boulder blocking their path.

Synset Examples

turn the corner
move around the obstacle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go around - to avoid or pass by something by changing direction
  • Synonym: to circumnavigate - to go completely around something, especially in a purposeful way
