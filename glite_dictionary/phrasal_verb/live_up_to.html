to live up to

1. VERB

/ lɪv ʌp tu / (AmE), / lɪv ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
to do as well as expected or required; to fulfill (standards, expectations, ideals, etc.)
fulfill expectations or standards

Examples

The film failed to live up to the critics' high expectations.
She worked hard to live up to her reputation as an excellent teacher.
Can the new restaurant live up to all the hype?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall short of - to fail to reach the required standard or expectation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02677669-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɪv ʌp tu / (AmE), / lɪv ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
to fulfill or satisfy the expectations or standards set by oneself or others
fulfill expectations or standards

Examples

He strives to live up to his teacher's high standards.
The movie didn't quite live up to the hype surrounding its release.
She felt pressure to live up to her parents' expectations.

Synset Examples

He promised to deliver up to his parents' expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to measure up to - to be as good as expected or required
