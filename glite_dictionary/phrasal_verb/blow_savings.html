to blow savings

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ˈseɪvɪŋz / (AmE), / bləʊ ˈseɪvɪŋz / (BrE)
to spend one's savings quickly or extravagantly, often in a careless or wasteful manner
spend savings extravagantly
informal

Examples

After years of hard work, he decided to blow his savings on a sports car.
It's not wise to blow your savings on things you don't really need.
She regretted blowing her savings during her vacation abroad.

Synset Examples

He decided to blow his savings on a luxury vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to squander - to waste (something, especially money or time) in a reckless or foolish manner
