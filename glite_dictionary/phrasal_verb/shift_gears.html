to shift gears

1. VERB

/ ʃɪft ɡɪrz / (AmE), / ʃɪft ɡɪəz / (BrE)
to change one's approach, activity, or strategy, especially to adapt to new circumstances.
change approach or strategy

Examples

When the discussion stalled, the moderator decided to shift gears to a lighter topic.
After months of slow progress, the project team needed to shift gears and try a new approach.
In his career, he often had to shift gears to stay ahead of industry changes.

Synset Examples

She can easily shift gears when plans change unexpectedly.
Let’s shift gears and discuss our marketing strategy.
Make sure to shift gears using the PRNDL properly.
We need to shift gears if we want to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to change tack - to adopt a different course of action or approach
