to look on

1. VERB

/ lʊk ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to watch something happen without becoming involved or taking action
observe without taking part

Examples

Crowds gathered to look on as the firefighters battled the blaze.
I couldn't just look on while my friend was being treated unfairly.
He often looked on during family arguments but rarely got involved.

Synset Examples

They watched as the murderer was executed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - to observe attentively

2. VERB

/ lʊk ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to regard or consider (someone or something) in a particular way
to consider or regard

Examples

Many people look on failure as a valuable learning experience.
She tends to look on every challenge as an opportunity.
They look on him as the leader of the group.

Synset Examples

she looked on this affair as a joke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regard - to think of someone or something in a particular way
  • Synonym: to consider - to think carefully about something, especially in making decisions

3. VERB

/ lʊk ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to watch an event or activity without taking part in it
watch without participating

Examples

The children gathered to look on as the magician performed his tricks.
Many people just look on when they see an accident instead of helping.
She prefers to look on during meetings rather than join the discussions.

Synset Examples

He decided to look on while others debated.
She chose to look on rather than getting involved.
It's best to look on and not get too involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to observe - to watch carefully, especially in order to learn something
  • Antonym: to participate - to take part in an activity or event
