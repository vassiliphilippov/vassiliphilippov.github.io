to plow the sands

1. VERB

/ plaʊ ðə sændz / (AmE), / plaʊ ðə san(d)z / (BrE)
to search or dig through sand, especially in order to find something hidden such as artifacts or treasures
search or dig through sand for something hidden

Examples

The archaeologists spent days plowing the sands in hopes of discovering ancient relics.
Many fortune hunters travel to the coast to plow the sands for valuable objects after a storm.
In the story, he would often plow the sands with his metal detector searching for buried coins.

Synset Examples

They had to plow the sands to find buried treasures.
In the desert, explorers often plow the sands for ancient artifacts.
He learned to plow the sands during his archaeological expedition.
