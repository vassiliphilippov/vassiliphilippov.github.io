to leach into

1. VERB

/ litʃ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / liːtʃ ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
(of a substance or quality) to gradually seep or flow into something, often by the action of liquid, or to slowly become part of an area, situation, or person's thoughts.
gradually seep or flow into something

Examples

Over time, the chemicals can leach into the groundwater and contaminate it.
Negative thoughts may leach into your mind if you don't stay positive.
Salt can leach into the soil after heavy rainfall, affecting plant growth.

Synset Examples

Don't let negativity leach into your thoughts.
Her worries began to leach into her daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seep into - to flow or leak slowly into something
  • Synonym: to permeate - to spread through or be present throughout something

Etymology

From the verb 'leach', meaning to drain away, originally from Middle English 'lechen' (to filter liquids).
