to zero in on

1. VERB

/ ˈzɪəroʊ ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to direct all your attention or effort towards a particular person or thing; to focus closely on something specific
focus attention on something specific

Examples

The company needs to zero in on areas where it can reduce costs.
Once the scientist zeroed in on a possible cause, the investigation moved quickly.
During the debate, she zeroed in on her opponent's weaknesses.

Synset Examples

The detective managed to zero in on the suspect quickly.
I need to zero in on the main issue first.
Let's zero in on our target audience for the campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to focus on - to direct attention or effort to something

Etymology

Originally from military usage referring to adjusting weapons to aim precisely at a target (‘zero’ meaning the exact point of aim).
