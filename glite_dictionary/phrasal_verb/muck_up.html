to muck up

1. VERB

/ mʌk ʌp / (AmE), / mʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to make something dirty, especially with mud or filth
make dirty with mud or filth

Examples

Be careful not to muck up your shoes when you walk through the field.
The children mucked up the kitchen after making cookies.
He mucked up his trousers while helping in the garden.

Synset Examples

The child mucked up his shirt while playing ball in the garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - to make unclean or soiled
  • Antonym: clean - to make something free from dirt
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mʌk ʌp / (AmE), / mʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to spoil, ruin, or make a mess of something, especially by making mistakes
spoil or ruin something
informal

Examples

If you muck up this report, we’ll have to start all over again.
She accidentally mucked up the cake recipe and it didn’t rise.
Try not to muck up your room before your friends arrive.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to do something badly or spoil something
  • Synonym: to screw up - to make a serious mistake or mess something up
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or correct something

Etymology

From the noun "muck" (dirt, filth), used figuratively to mean "make dirty" or "spoil".
