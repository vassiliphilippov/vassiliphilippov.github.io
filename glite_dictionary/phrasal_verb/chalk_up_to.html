to chalk up to

1. VERB

/ tʃɑk ʌp tu / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ʌp tuː / (BrE)
to consider or regard something as being caused by a particular reason
attribute something to a cause

Examples

Many people chalk their success up to a mixture of talent and luck.
The coach chalked the team's loss up to a lack of preparation.
She wasn't angry; she simply chalked his mistake up to inexperience.

Synset Examples

She chalked the misunderstanding up to poor communication.
They can chalk their losses up to bad luck.
He chalked his success up to hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attribute to - to regard as being caused by
  • Synonym: blame on - to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong

Etymology

From the use of chalk to record scores or tallies, extended metaphorically to mean 'to attribute to'.
