to smell out

1. VERB

/ smɛl aʊt / (AmE), / smɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to recognize, discover, or detect something, especially something hidden or suspicious, as if by using one's sense of smell
detect or recognize (often something hidden)

Examples

The reporter managed to smell out the truth behind the scandal.
It's amazing how quickly she can smell out a lie.
Our dog seems able to smell out food wherever we hide it.

Synset Examples

He can smell out trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detect - to discover or identify the presence of something
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or detect something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02129925-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ smɛl aʊt / (AmE), / smɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to discover or detect something, especially a problem or secret, by using intuition or instinct rather than the senses
discover by instinct or intuition

Examples

The journalist managed to smell out corruption in the local government.
She has an uncanny ability to smell out trouble before it starts.
It didn't take him long to smell out the real reason for their meeting.

Synset Examples

I sense his hostility
I smell trouble
smell out corruption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detect - discover something that is hidden or not easily noticed
  • Synonym: sense - become aware of something, especially through intuition
