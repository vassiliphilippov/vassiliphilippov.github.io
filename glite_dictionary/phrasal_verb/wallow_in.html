to wallow in

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑloʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɒləʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to indulge oneself in an emotion, situation, or memory, especially when it is unpleasant or self-pitying, without making an effort to change it
indulge yourself in emotions or memories

Examples

Sometimes it's tempting to wallow in your problems instead of facing them head-on.
After the breakup, he spent weeks wallowing in self-pity at home.
You can't wallow in regrets forever if you want to move forward.

Synset Examples

She decided to wallow in her memories of happier times.
Don’t wallow in sadness; get up and try again.
After failing the test, he chose to wallow in self-pity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwell on - to think or talk about something for a long time, often unpleasant
  • Antonym: to move on - to stop being upset or preoccupied with something and continue with life

Etymology

From 'wallow' (to roll about), originally of animals in mud; figurative usage since the 17th century.
