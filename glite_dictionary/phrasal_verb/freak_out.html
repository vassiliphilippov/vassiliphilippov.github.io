to freak out

1. VERB

/ ˈfrik aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfriːk aʊt / (BrE)
to suddenly become very anxious, upset, or afraid; to lose control due to shock or fear
lose control due to shock or fear
informal

Examples

She started to freak out when she realized her passport was missing.
Don't freak out; everything is going to be fine.
He freaked out after hearing the sudden loud noise in the middle of the night.

Synset Examples

When he saw the accident, he freaked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - to become suddenly very anxious or afraid and lose self-control
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become or help someone become less upset or excited
