to join in

1. VERB

/ dʒɔɪn ɪn / (AmE), / dʒɔɪn ɪn / (BrE)
to take part in an activity or event with others, especially one that is already in progress
participate in an activity with others

Examples

Everyone was laughing so much that I had to join in.
Neighbours are welcome to join in the festivities.
She hesitated for a moment, then joined in the discussion.

Synset Examples

He couldn't help but join in the baby dance.
He couldn't help but join in on their soy banter.
They all joined in for the zombie dance at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to participate - to take part in an activity or event
  • Antonym: to sit out - to choose not to participate in an activity or event
