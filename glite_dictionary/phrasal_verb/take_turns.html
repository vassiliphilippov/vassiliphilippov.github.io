to take turns

1. VERB

/ teɪk tɝːnz / (AmE), / teɪk tɜːnz / (BrE)
(of two or more people) to do something one after another in a planned or regular way; to alternate doing something.
alternate doing something

Examples

The children take turns playing with the dog in the backyard.
We took turns driving so neither of us got too tired during the long trip.
Please take turns answering the questions so everyone has a chance to speak.

Synset Examples

We take turns on the night shift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternate - to happen or do something in turn repeatedly

2. VERB

/ teɪk tɝːnz / (AmE), / teɪk tɜːnz / (BrE)
(of two or more people) to do something one after another in regular succession, especially by alternating or sharing responsibilities or opportunities.
to alternate or share responsibility or opportunity

Examples

After dinner, the children take turns washing the dishes and sweeping the floor.
We should take turns driving on the long road trip so no one gets too tired.
During the meeting, everyone took turns sharing their ideas for the new project.

Synset Examples

They take turns to keep house on weekends.
The kids took turns riding the toboggan down the hill.
They took turns to ride the cotton pony at the petting zoo.
They took turns to keep watch during the night.
The kids took turns hitting the piñata at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternate - to occur or do something by turns; to take turns
