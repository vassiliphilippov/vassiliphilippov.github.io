to line up for

1. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp fɔr / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp fə / (BrE)
to wait in a queue to receive or do something
wait in a queue for something

Examples

We had to line up for an hour just to get tickets to the show.
The children eagerly lined up for ice cream after lunch.
People are already lining up for the store's big Black Friday sale.

Synset Examples

The whelk stall was busy, with customers lining up for a taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - to line up or form a line while waiting for something (British usage)
