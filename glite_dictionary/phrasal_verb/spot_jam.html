to spot jam

1. VERB

/ spɑt dʒæm / (AmE), / spɒt dʒam / (BrE)
to interfere with a single frequency of radio or radar transmissions by intentional electronic jamming
to jam a single radio frequency

Examples

The military used advanced equipment to spot jam enemy communications during the conflict.
Operators were trained to spot jam specific frequencies targeted by hostile forces.
It is essential to spot jam only the necessary channels to avoid disrupting friendly signals.

Synset Examples

This operator is spot-jammed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - to deliberately interfere with radio or radar signals
  • Antonym: to barrage jam - to jam a wide range of frequencies simultaneously
