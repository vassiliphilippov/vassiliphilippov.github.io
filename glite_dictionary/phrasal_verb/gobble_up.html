to gobble up

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɑb.əl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡɒb.əl ʌp / (BrE)
to eat a large amount of food quickly and eagerly
eat quickly and eagerly

Examples

The children gobbled up their dinner so quickly that there were no leftovers.
She gobbled up the pizza before anyone else could have a slice.
The stray dog gobbled up every scrap of food they gave it.

Synset Examples

The children gobbled down most of the birthday cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something eagerly and in large amounts

Etymology

From the verb 'gobble' (imitative of the sound made when eating quickly) plus 'up'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɑbəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbəl ʌp / (BrE)
To eat something quickly and eagerly.
eat quickly and eagerly

Examples

The children gobbled up their snacks after school.
She gobbled up the sandwich so fast that no crumbs were left.
We gobbled up the birthday cake before anyone could ask for a second piece.

Synset Examples

He gobbled up the dessert before anyone else.
They gobbled up the popcorn during the movie.
The kids gobbled up their dinner in minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something eagerly and in large amounts
  • Antonym: nibble - to eat something by taking small bites
