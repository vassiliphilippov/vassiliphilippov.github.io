to break cover

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / breɪk ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to come out of hiding or to reveal oneself, especially after having remained concealed for a period
emerge from hiding; reveal oneself

Examples

The deer had to break cover and run across the field to escape the hunters.
After hours of waiting, the journalist finally decided to break cover and approach the source.
When the alarm sounded, the thieves were forced to break cover and flee the building.

Synset Examples

The spy had to break cover to deliver crucial information.
When the storm passed, they decided to break cover and head outside.
He waited for the right moment to break cover during the mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to move out of or away from something and become visible
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden or secret
