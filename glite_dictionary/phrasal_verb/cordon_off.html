to cordon off

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔrdən ɔf / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdən ɒf / (BrE)
to prevent people from entering or leaving an area by placing a line of police, barriers, or tape around it
close off an area with barriers or tape

Examples

The police quickly cordoned off the street after the accident.
Firefighters cordoned off the building to keep bystanders at a safe distance.
Officials cordoned off the park during the investigation.

Synset Examples

The police roped off the area where the crime occurred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to block off - to close something so that people or things cannot pass through
  • Synonym: to seal off - to prevent people from entering or leaving an area, especially to stop the spread of something dangerous

Etymology

From French 'cordon' (ribbon, cord), related to 'cord'.
