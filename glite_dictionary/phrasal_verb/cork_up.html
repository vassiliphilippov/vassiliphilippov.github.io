to cork up

1. VERB

/ kɔrk ʌp / (AmE), / kɔːk ʌp / (BrE)
to seal a bottle or container by inserting a cork into its opening
seal a container with a cork

Examples

After filling the wine bottles, the winemaker carefully corked up each one by hand.
Make sure you cork up the bottle tightly to preserve the freshness of the juice.
She quickly corked up the container and put it away for later use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to uncork - to remove the cork from a bottle
  • Synonym: stopper - to close or seal a bottle with a stopper

2. VERB

/ kɔrk ʌp / (AmE), / kɔːk ʌp / (BrE)
to seal a bottle or container by inserting a cork into its opening
seal with a cork

Examples

After pouring a glass, she made sure to cork up the bottle tightly.
The vintner instructed us to cork up any unfinished bottles to preserve the wine.
Be sure to cork up the jar after adding the ingredients so nothing spills out.

Synset Examples

I decided to cork up the bottle to save the wine.
Make sure to cork up the jar after using the spices.
She found the bottle corked up and undrinkable.
The wine had corked up, ruining the flavor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to uncork - to remove a cork from a bottle or container

Etymology

From 'cork' (a stopper made from the bark of the cork oak) + 'up' (to close or complete an action).
