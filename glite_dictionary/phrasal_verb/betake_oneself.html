to betake oneself

1. VERB

/ bɪˈteɪk wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / bɪˈteɪk wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to go or move to a particular place, often used in formal or literary contexts
formally, to go to a place
archaic

Examples

After the meeting, she betook herself to her study to reflect on the day.
He betook himself to the countryside for some quiet and solitude.
As the storm approached, the villagers betook themselves to safer ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go - to move or travel from one place to another

Etymology

From Middle English 'bētaken', formed from 'be-' (intensive prefix) + 'take', and reflexive usage with 'oneself'.
