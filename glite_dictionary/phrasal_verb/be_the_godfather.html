to be the godfather

1. VERB

/ bi ði ˈɡɑdˌfɑðɚ / (AmE), / biː ðə ˈɡɒdˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
to take on the role of godfather, especially in a religious context by accepting responsibility for a child's spiritual upbringing
assume the role of godfather

Examples

He was honored when asked to be the godfather of his nephew.
She didn't expect her brother to be the godfather at the baptism.
Many people feel a deep sense of duty when they be the godfather for a child.

Synset Examples

He asked me to be the godfather of his child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godfather - a male sponsor at a child's baptism
