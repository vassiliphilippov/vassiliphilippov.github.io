to get across

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to cross from one side of something to the other, such as a street, river, or area.
to cross from one side to another

Examples

We managed to get across the river before it got dark.
It's not easy to get across busy highways on foot.
He couldn't get across the border without the right documents.

Synset Examples

The caravan covered almost 100 miles each day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to go across from one side to the other
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01916152-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈkrɑs / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
(of an idea or message) to become understood or make an impression on someone; to be communicated clearly.
become understood or clear

Examples

It took several explanations for the importance of safety to get across to the new employees.
The seriousness of the situation didn't get across until she saw the look on his face.
Sometimes her enthusiasm doesn't get across in written messages as it does in person.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dawn on - to become apparent or clear to someone
  • Synonym: to sink in - to become fully understood or absorbed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00593150-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to communicate an idea or message successfully, so that it is understood by others
communicate something successfully

Examples

She found it difficult to get across her point during the heated discussion.
Good teachers know how to get across complex ideas in simple terms.
Despite the language barrier, he managed to get across what he meant.

Synset Examples

I couldn't get across the message
He put over the idea very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to convey - to communicate or make known
  • Synonym: to put across - to communicate something effectively
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00746890-v
Synset POS: v
