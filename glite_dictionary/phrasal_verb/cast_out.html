to cast out

1. VERB

/ kæst aʊt / (AmE), / kɑːst aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place or group, often in a way that is permanent or shows rejection
to expel or banish

Examples

The tribe decided to cast out those who broke their laws.
After years of tension, he was finally cast out of the community.
The corrupt official was cast out from public office following the scandal.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place
  • Synonym: to banish - to send someone away as punishment

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kasta' (to throw) and Old English 'ūt' (out).

2. VERB

/ kæst aʊt / (AmE), / kɑːst aʊt / (BrE)
to expel someone or something from a community or group; to force out or exclude.
expel from a group or community

Examples

Anyone who broke the rules would be cast out of the village.
He felt like an outsider, cast out by his former friends after the argument.
The group cast out members who do not adhere to their strict code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: to exclude - to prevent someone from taking part in something
  • Antonym: to accept - to receive or welcome someone into a group

Etymology

From Middle English 'casten' (to throw) and 'out', forming a phrasal verb meaning to throw out.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02509666-v
Synset POS: v
