to tide over

1. VERB

/ taɪd ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / taɪd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to supply with what is needed to get through a short period of difficulty or until something better is available
help through a difficult period

Examples

The advance payment will tide over the company until sales pick up next quarter.
We borrowed some money from friends to tide over the summer months.
A loan from her parents helped tide over the difficult time after she lost her job.

Synset Examples

This money will keep us going for another year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get by - manage with difficulty to survive or make progress
  • Synonym: to make do - manage with the limited resources one has

Etymology

From the verb 'tide' in the sense of 'to float or drift with the tide,' combined with 'over.'

2. VERB

/ taɪd ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / taɪd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to supply someone with what they need for a short time until something more substantial becomes available
provide temporary support

Examples

A small loan helped tide over the family until he could find another job.
I keep snacks in my desk to tide over my hunger in the afternoon.
She asked her parents to tide over her until her next paycheck arrived.

Synset Examples

She saved money to tide her over during the holidays.
I bought some snacks to tide me over until dinner.
He borrowed a book to tide him over the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sustain - to support physically or mentally for a period of time
  • Synonym: to help out - to assist someone in a time of need

Etymology

From the verb 'tide', meaning to carry along; alludes to surviving a difficult period, like riding out a high tide.
