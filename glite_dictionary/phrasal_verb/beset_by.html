to beset by

1. VERB

/ bɪˈsɛt baɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈsɛt baɪ / (BrE)
troubled or surrounded by difficulties or problems
troubled or surrounded by difficulties

Examples

The city was beset by severe flooding after the heavy rains.
She felt beset by doubts as the deadline approached.
The company has been beset by financial difficulties for years.

Synset Examples

The team was beset by challenges throughout the season.
They were beset by problems during the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagued by - persistently troubled by difficulties or worries
  • Antonym: free from - not troubled or affected by difficulties

Etymology

From Middle English *beseten*, from Old English *besettan*, 'to surround' + *by* for the prepositional phrase.
