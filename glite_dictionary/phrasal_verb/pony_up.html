to pony up

1. VERB

/ ˈpoʊni ʌp / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni ʌp / (BrE)
to pay or contribute money, especially when it is reluctant or expected
pay or contribute money (reluctantly)
informal american

Examples

We all had to pony up for the group gift at the last minute.
After much hesitation, she finally ponied up the money for the repairs.
You can't keep avoiding the bill—it's time to pony up your share.

Synset Examples

He coughed up some money for his children's tuition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cough up - to give money reluctantly
  • Synonym: to shell out - to pay money, often a large or unwelcome amount

Etymology

Originating in 19th-century American English; the exact origin is uncertain, but 'pony' was slang for money in some contexts.
