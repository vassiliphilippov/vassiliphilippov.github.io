to throw out

1. VERB

/ θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to dismiss or reject something, especially in a legal or formal context; to cease to consider a case, proposal, or request.
dismiss, especially legally or formally

Examples

The judge decided to throw out the lawsuit for lack of evidence.
Several complaints were thrown out before the hearing even began.
If the motion is irrelevant, the court may throw out the entire case.

Synset Examples

This case is dismissed!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to formally end or reject something, such as a case or meeting
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ θɹoʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to dispose of something by discarding it; to cast away or get rid of.
discard or get rid of

Examples

She decided to throw out all the old magazines piling up in the living room.
If the food smells bad, you should throw out it immediately.
Every spring, I throw out clothes I no longer wear.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something no longer useful
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something; not discard
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove someone from a position or office, often because of wrongdoing or for disciplinary reasons
remove from a position or office

Examples

After the scandal, the board decided to throw out the CEO from his job.
The committee voted to throw out the treasurer due to financial mismanagement.
She was thrown out of the organization after repeatedly violating their rules.

Synset Examples

The chairman was ousted after he misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oust - to remove from a position or office, especially by force or compulsion
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove from employment or official position

4. VERB

/ θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place, institution, or position, especially because they are not wanted or allowed to stay
force someone to leave or expel

Examples

The security guards threw out the unruly customer after the argument escalated.
She was thrown out of the meeting for continuously interrupting the speaker.
If you don't pay your rent, the landlord might throw out your belongings.

Synset Examples

He was expelled from his native country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to officially make someone leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: to eject - to force someone to leave a place or position
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone to enter or remain
