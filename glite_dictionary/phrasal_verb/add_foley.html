to add foley

1. VERB

/ æd ˈfoʊli / (AmE), / ad ˈfəʊli / (BrE)
to include sound effects in a film, television, or audio production to enhance realism
add sound effects for realism in media

Examples

The director decided to add foley to bring the rainstorm scene to life.
They usually add foley in post-production to make the footsteps sound realistic.
Sound engineers meticulously add foley to sync with the actors' movements.

Synset Examples

They added foley to enhance the film's realism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound design - the art or practice of creating sound for films and media
  • Synonym: add soundtrack - to add a soundtrack or auditory accompaniment

Etymology

Foley is named after Jack Foley, an early sound effects artist in film.
