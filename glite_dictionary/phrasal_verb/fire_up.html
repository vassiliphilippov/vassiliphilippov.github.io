to fire up

1. VERB

/ faɪɚ ʌp / (AmE), / faɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to arouse or excite strong feelings, emotions, or enthusiasm in someone
arouse or excite strong feelings

Examples

The coach's speech really fired up the team before the final match.
Her passionate words fired up the crowd, inspiring them to take action.
News of the changes fired up both excitement and anxiety among the employees.

Synset Examples

The ostentatious way of living of the rich ignites the hatred of the poor
The refugees' fate stirred up compassion around the world
Wake old feelings of hatred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excite - to stir up strong feelings or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: to ignite - to arouse or inflame feelings or passions

2. VERB

/ ˈfaɪɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to begin smoking a cigarette, cigar, or pipe by lighting it
begin smoking by lighting (cigarette, etc.)

Examples

After dinner, several people stepped outside to fire up their cigars.
He likes to fire up a pipe while reading the newspaper in the morning.
She watched him fire up a cigarette on the balcony.

Synset Examples

After the meal, some of the diners lit up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to light up - to light a cigarette, cigar, or pipe; to begin smoking
