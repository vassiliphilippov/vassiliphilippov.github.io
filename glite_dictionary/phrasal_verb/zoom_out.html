to zoom out

1. VERB

/ zum aʊt / (AmE), / zuːm aʊt / (BrE)
to adjust one's view to see a wider area; to consider a situation from a broader or more general perspective
view more broadly; see the bigger picture

Examples

If you zoom out, you'll notice how all the departments contribute to the company's success.
Sometimes it's helpful to zoom out and think about your long-term goals.
The map allows you to zoom out to see the entire region.

Synset Examples

Let’s zoom out to see the bigger picture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to zoom in - to focus on a smaller or more specific area or detail

Etymology

From 'zoom' (move with a loud sound or increase magnification) plus 'out' (away from a focus). 'Zoom' derives from an imitative origin.
