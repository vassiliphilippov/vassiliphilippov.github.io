to draw a line

1. VERB

/ drɔ ə laɪn / (AmE), / drɔː ə laɪn / (BrE)
to establish a clear boundary or limit, especially concerning acceptable behavior or standards
set a boundary or limit

Examples

We have to draw a line between friendship and professional relationships.
She decided to draw a line regarding how late her children could stay out.
Sometimes it's necessary to draw a line and stick to your principles.

Synset Examples

It's important to draw a line between work and play.

Let's draw a line and agree to disagree.
I have to draw the line at unacceptable behavior.
She decided to draw the line when it came to honesty.
It's important to draw the line at work-life balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to draw the line - to set a definite limit, especially of what one will accept
