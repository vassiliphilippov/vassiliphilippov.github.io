to walk straight into

1. VERB

/ wɔk streɪt ˈɪntu / (AmE), / wɔːk streɪt ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to enter a place directly and confidently, without hesitation or delay
enter a place directly and confidently

Examples

She didn’t hesitate—she walked straight into the manager’s office and asked for a meeting.
He walked straight into the restaurant and greeted his friends.
The dog walked straight into the house after being let off the leash.

Synset Examples
