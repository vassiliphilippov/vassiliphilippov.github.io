to last out

1. VERB

/ læst aʊt / (AmE), / lɑːst aʊt / (BrE)
to continue to exist or endure through a difficult period until the end; to survive or withstand something challenging
endure through a difficult period

Examples

Despite the exhaustion, she managed to last out until the end of her shift.
The old generator won't last out through another storm without repairs.
He was determined to last out the harsh winter without leaving his home.

Synset Examples

ride out the storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold out - to resist or survive, to endure through difficulty
  • Antonym: to give in - to stop resisting; to surrender
