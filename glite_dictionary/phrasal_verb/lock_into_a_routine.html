to lock into a routine

1. VERB

/ lɑk ˈɪntu ə ruˈtin / (AmE), / lɒk ˈɪntu ə ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
to establish or become firmly fixed in a regular pattern or way of doing things
establish a regular pattern

Examples

After her new job started, she managed to lock into a routine that made life easier.
It's challenging to lock into a routine after moving to a different city.
Once you lock into a routine, daily tasks often feel more manageable.

Synset Examples

It's hard to lock into a routine when traveling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle into a routine - to gradually adapt or accustom oneself to a regular way of doing things
