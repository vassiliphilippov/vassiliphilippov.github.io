to make sense

1. VERB

/ meɪk sɛns / (AmE), / meɪk sɛns / (BrE)
to be reasonable, logical, or easy to understand
to be reasonable or understandable

Examples

His explanation didn't make sense to anyone in the room.
It finally makes sense now that I have all the details.
Can you explain it in a way that will make sense to everyone?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add up - to appear reasonable or logical
  • Antonym: make no sense - to be illogical or not understandable
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ meɪk sɛns / (AmE), / meɪk sɛns / (BrE)
to be clear, reasonable, or easy to understand; to be logical or coherent
to be logical or understandable

Examples

After reading the instructions again, everything finally made sense to John.
Her theory doesn’t make sense when you consider the latest evidence.
Can you explain it differently? It doesn’t make sense to me this way.

Synset Examples

His explanation didn’t make sense to me.
Her story finally began to make sense after the details were clarified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be logical - to follow principles of logic
  • Antonym: be confusing - to be unclear or hard to understand
