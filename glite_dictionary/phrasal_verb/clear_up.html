to clear up

1. VERB

/ klɪr ʌp / (AmE), / klɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to make something less confusing or ambiguous; to explain or resolve something so that it becomes clear
make something less confusing or ambiguous

Examples

Can you clear up what happened at the meeting yesterday?
The manager tried to clear up the misunderstanding between the two departments.
Once the facts were known, everything was finally cleared up.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand
  • Antonym: to confuse - to make unclear or difficult to understand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00622730-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klɪr ʌp / (AmE), / klɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to finish a task or deal with something completely, leaving nothing unfinished
finish a task completely

Examples

I plan to clear up all my emails before I leave the office.
It took her the whole weekend to clear up the paperwork.
He managed to clear up his assignments just in time for the deadline.

Synset Examples

I finally got through this homework assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - bring a task to an end or completion
  • Antonym: leave unfinished - not complete or not finished
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00485823-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ klɪr ʌp / (AmE), / klɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to make (something) easier to understand; to clarify or explain something so that it is no longer confusing or ambiguous
make something clearer or easier to understand

Examples

Let me clear up any confusion you have about the instructions.
The police worked hard to clear up the details of the case.
Can you clear up what you meant in yesterday's meeting?

Synset Examples

clarify the mystery surrounding her death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand by explaining it clearly
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something less clear or difficult to understand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941888-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ klɪr ʌp / (AmE), / klɪə ʌp / (BrE)
(of weather) to become clear after being cloudy or rainy.
weather: become clear

Examples

The sky will clear up by the afternoon, so we can have our picnic outside.
It was raining all morning, but it finally cleared up around noon.
They waited at the café until the weather started clearing up.

Synset Examples

The sky cleared after the storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brighten up - (of weather) to become brighter or sunnier
