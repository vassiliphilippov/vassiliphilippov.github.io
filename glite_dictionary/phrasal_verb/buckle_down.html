to buckle down

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌkəl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl daʊn / (BrE)
to start working hard and with serious intent; to apply oneself earnestly to a task
begin to work hard in earnest

Examples

It's time to buckle down and finish your homework before dinner.
After months of procrastination, she finally buckled down to study for the exam.
The team knows they need to buckle down if they want to win the championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knuckle down - to begin to work seriously; apply oneself

Etymology

Originally from the literal sense of fastening one's buckle, suggesting readiness to begin work; figurative use dates to the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌk.əl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.əl daʊn / (BrE)
to start working hard and putting serious effort into something, especially after a period of delay or distraction
begin to work seriously; focus and work hard

Examples

It's almost finals week, so I really need to buckle down and study every night.
After months of procrastination, she finally decided to buckle down and complete her thesis.
If the team doesn't buckle down, they might not reach their quarterly goals.

Synset Examples

It's time to buckle down and finish this project.
We should buckle down before the deadline approaches.
He needs to buckle down if he wants to pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knuckle down - to start working hard and seriously
