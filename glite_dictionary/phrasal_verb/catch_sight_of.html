to catch sight of

1. VERB

/ kætʃ saɪt əv / (AmE), / kætʃ saɪt ɒv / (BrE)
to see or notice something or someone, especially suddenly or briefly
see or notice briefly

Examples

I was walking through the market when I suddenly caught sight of an old friend.
From the window, she caught sight of a shooting star crossing the night sky.
Just as the bus was leaving, he caught sight of his missing backpack on a bench.

Synset Examples

Did you catch sight of the new cafe opening nearby?
As I walked, I caught sight of a beautiful sunset.
I finally caught sight of my friend at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - see or notice quickly or suddenly
  • Antonym: miss - fail to see or notice something
