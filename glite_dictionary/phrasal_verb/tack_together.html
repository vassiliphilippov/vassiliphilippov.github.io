to tack together

1. VERB

/ tæk təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / tæk təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to join or assemble parts or elements loosely or hastily, often in a temporary or makeshift way
join parts together quickly or loosely

Examples

She managed to tack together a last-minute costume for the school play.
The report was tacked together from several unrelated sources and lacked coherence.
They quickly tacked together a shelter using some old boards and a tarp.

Synset Examples

She pieced a quilt
He tacked together some verses
They set up a committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put together - assemble or combine parts to create a whole
  • Synonym: piece together - combine separate parts to form a whole, especially information

Etymology

From 'tack' (to fasten briefly) + 'together'. 'Tack' from Middle English *takken* (to fasten), from Old French *taque* (a nail or pin).
