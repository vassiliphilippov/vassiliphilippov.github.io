to come calling

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈkɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈkɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
to arrive, appear, or present itself, often unexpectedly, especially in reference to opportunities, events, or people seeking attention.
arrive or present itself, often unexpectedly

Examples

When opportunity came calling, she did not hesitate to take a risk.
Just when he thought he was forgotten, his past came calling again.
Success comes calling to those who persist through adversity.

Synset Examples

When success came calling, she was ready to seize it.
The opportunity to collaborate came calling at the perfect time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show up - to arrive or make an appearance somewhere
  • Synonym: to knock on the door - to approach, especially with a request or opportunity
