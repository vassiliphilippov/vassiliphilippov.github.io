to bash out

1. VERB

/ bæʃ aʊt / (AmE), / bæʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to produce or create something quickly and without much care, especially a piece of writing or music
produce hastily, usually writing or music
informal

Examples

The songwriter managed to bash out the lyrics for three new songs over the weekend.
We had to bash out a response to their complaints before the end of the day.
She can bash out a decent essay in just thirty minutes.

Synset Examples

He can bash out a new song in an hour.
They bashed out a quick report before the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock out - to produce or complete something quickly, especially a piece of work
