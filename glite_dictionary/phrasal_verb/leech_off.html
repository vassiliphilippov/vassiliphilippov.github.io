to leech off

1. VERB

/ litʃ ɑf / (AmE), / liːtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to habitually exploit or take advantage of someone in order to obtain money, food, or support without giving anything in return
exploit someone for support
informal

Examples

He would always leech off his family instead of finding a job.
I don’t want anyone trying to leech off my hard work and success.
She felt guilty for leeching off her friends during difficult times.

Synset Examples

The politician was accused of trying to leech off public funds.
Don't leech off your friends; contribute to the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponger - a person who habitually lives off others
  • Synonym: to mooch off - to take advantage of someone's generosity without giving anything in return

Etymology

From the noun 'leech' (a bloodsucking worm), used figuratively as a verb; 'off' as a preposition indicating from whom the advantage is taken.
