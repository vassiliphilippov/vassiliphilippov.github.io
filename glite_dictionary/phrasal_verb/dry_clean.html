to dry clean

1. VERB

/ ˈdraɪ ˌklin / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌkliːn / (BrE)
to clean clothes or fabrics using chemical solvents instead of water
clean with chemical solvents (not water)

Examples

I need to dry clean my suit before the wedding.
You should dry clean this dress to avoid damaging the fabric.
They dry cleaned the coat and removed all the stains.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to launder - to wash clothing or linens, usually with water and detergent
