to take root

1. VERB

/ teɪk ruːt / (AmE), / teɪk ruːt / (BrE)
to become settled or firmly established in a place or way of life
to become established or settled

Examples

After years of moving from city to city, she finally began to take root in her new town.
It took time for his business to take root in the community, but now it's thriving.
Many young people move frequently before they take root and start families.

Synset Examples

He finally settled down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to settle down - to begin to live a quieter and more stable life
