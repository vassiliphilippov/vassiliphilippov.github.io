to eat out

1. VERB

/ it aʊt / (AmE), / iːt aʊt / (BrE)
to have a meal at a restaurant or at someone else’s home instead of eating at home
have a meal at a restaurant or elsewhere

Examples

We usually eat out on Fridays to try new restaurants in the city.
After a long week, they decided to eat out instead of cooking dinner at home.
Do you want to eat out tonight or stay in and make something?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine out - to eat dinner at a restaurant or elsewhere
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ it aʊt / (AmE), / iːt aʊt / (BrE)
to have a meal at a restaurant instead of at home
dine at a restaurant

Examples

We usually eat out on Fridays to celebrate the end of the week.
Since the kitchen is being renovated, we've been eating out every night.
They decided to eat out because neither of them wanted to cook after work.

Synset Examples

After the promotion, he started eating out and living high on the hog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine out - to have dinner or a meal at a restaurant
