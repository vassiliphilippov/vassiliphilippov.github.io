to change over

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒ ˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒ ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to switch from using one system, method, or product to another; to adopt a new plan or policy
switch from one system or method to another

Examples

The factory will change over to solar power next year.
They decided to change over from the old software to a new system.
It takes time for employees to change over to a different way of working.

Synset Examples

We converted from 220 to 110 Volt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - to change from one thing to another (especially suddenly)
  • Synonym: convert - to change something into a different form or use

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒ ˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒ ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to shift from one system, activity, or state to another; to make an exchange or transition
to shift or transition to something else

Examples

Remember to change over from summer to winter tires before the snow comes.
It took the team a few minutes to change over the equipment between games.
