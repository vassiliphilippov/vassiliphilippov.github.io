to level with

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛvəl wɪð / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl wɪð / (BrE)
to speak honestly and openly to someone, especially about something difficult or unpleasant
speak honestly and openly

Examples

If you want my advice, you should level with your friends about what happened.
She felt relieved after she finally leveled with her parents about the accident.
I appreciate it when people level with me instead of hiding the truth.

Synset Examples

It's time to level with the team about the budget cuts.
He decided to level with his boss about the mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be honest with - to be truthful with someone
  • Antonym: to deceive - to deliberately mislead or trick someone

Etymology

From the verb 'level', meaning to make even or straight; used figuratively for communicating on equal terms.
