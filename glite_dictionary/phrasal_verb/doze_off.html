to doze off

1. VERB

/ doʊz ɔf / (AmE), / dəʊz ɒf / (BrE)
to gradually fall asleep, often unintentionally or for a short time
fall asleep unintentionally

Examples

Despite all her efforts, she began to doze off during the long meeting.
I often doze off while reading in bed at night.
He was embarrassed when he dozed off on the train and missed his stop.

Synset Examples

he always falls asleep during lectures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nod off - fall asleep, often briefly and unintentionally
  • Antonym: to wake up - stop sleeping and become awake

2. VERB

/ doʊz ɔf / (AmE), / dəʊz ɒf / (BrE)
to fall asleep, especially briefly or unintentionally
fall asleep briefly or unintentionally

Examples

He kept dozing off during the long meeting despite his best efforts to stay awake.
I nearly dozed off while reading on the train.
She often finds herself dozing off in front of the television in the evening.

Synset Examples

The children giggled, calling him a dormouse for dozing off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nod off - to fall asleep, especially briefly or unintentionally
