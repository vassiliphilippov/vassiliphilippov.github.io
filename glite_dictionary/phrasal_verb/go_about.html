to go about

1. VERB

/ ˈɡoʊ əˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ əˌbaʊt / (BrE)
to begin to do or deal with something, especially in a particular way
begin to deal with something

Examples

He had no idea how to go about fixing the car.
If you want to start a business, you must go about it carefully.
She wasn't sure of the best way to go about solving the problem.

Synset Examples

approach a task
go about a difficult problem
approach a new project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tackle - to make a determined effort to deal with a problem or difficult task
  • Synonym: address - to think about and begin to deal with an issue or problem
