to go against

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to oppose or resist something, such as a rule, decision, or prevailing trend
oppose or resist

Examples

It takes courage to go against popular opinion in your community.
She decided to go against her parents' wishes and study art instead of law.
Sometimes employees go against company policy to stand up for what they believe is right.

Synset Examples

buck the trend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppose - to act against or provide resistance to
  • Antonym: to conform - to comply with rules, standards, or laws
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02383942-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to act in disregard of rules, laws, contracts, or promises; to violate or oppose something expected or agreed upon
act in disregard of rules or promises

Examples

He knew that to go against the company's policy could have serious consequences.
Breaking your promise would go against everything you said you stood for.
She refused to go against her conscience, even under pressure from others.

Synset Examples

offend all laws of humanity
violate the basic laws or human civilization
break a law
break a promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to fail to obey a law or rule
  • Synonym: to violate - to act in breach of a law or promise
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to act in opposition to or fail to comply with (a rule, law, or expectation)
violate or oppose a rule or expectation

Examples

His actions went against company policy and resulted in disciplinary measures.
Wearing such bright colors goes against the school's dress code.
The new proposal goes against everything we have agreed on in previous meetings.

Synset Examples

This sentence violates the rules of syntax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to violate - to break or fail to comply with (a rule or formal agreement)
