to brood over

1. VERB

/ brud ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / bruːd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to think deeply and anxiously about something, especially a problem or worry, often for a long time
to dwell anxiously on a worry

Examples

He tends to brood over past mistakes instead of moving forward.
She couldn't help but brood over the unresolved argument with her friend.
Don't brood over what you can't change; focus on what you can do now.

Synset Examples

She would brood above her failures for days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ruminate - to think carefully and deeply about something
  • Synonym: to dwell on - to think or talk about something for a long time
  • Antonym: to let go - to stop thinking or worrying about something

Etymology

From Middle English 'broden' (to hatch eggs), figuratively extended to deep, persistent thought.
