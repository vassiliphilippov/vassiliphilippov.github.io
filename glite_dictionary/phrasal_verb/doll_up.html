to doll up

1. VERB

/ dɑl ʌp / (AmE), / dɒl ʌp / (BrE)
to dress or make oneself look smart or attractive, usually with special care
make oneself look attractive
informal

Examples

She always likes to doll up before a big party.
He dolled up for the company gala, wearing his sharpest suit.
Before the awards show, the actors spent hours dolling up backstage.

Synset Examples

She dolled herself up for the night out with her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes
  • Synonym: to make up - to apply cosmetics to one's face

Etymology

From the noun 'doll' (a child's toy) used figuratively, plus the particle 'up'; first recorded in the early 20th century.
