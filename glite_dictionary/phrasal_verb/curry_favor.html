to curry favor

1. VERB

/ ˈkɝː.i ˈfeɪ.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌr.i ˈfeɪ.və / (BrE)
to try to gain someone's approval or advantage by flattery or servile behavior
seek approval by flattery

Examples

Politicians often curry favor with donors in order to fund their campaigns.
He tried to curry favor by bringing coffee for everyone each morning.
Some employees constantly curry favor with management to secure promotions.

Synset Examples

He often curries favor by volunteering for extra tasks.
It's not wise to curry favor through flattery.
She tries to curry favor with the boss every week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely, to gain favor
  • Synonym: ingratiate oneself - to try to become popular with someone by praising or pleasing them
  • Antonym: to offend - to cause displeasure or resentment

Etymology

From the Old French 'estriller favel' (to rub down a chestnut horse), by folk etymology linking 'favel' to 'favor'.
