to tie down

1. VERB

/ taɪ daʊn / (AmE), / taɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to secure something by fastening it with ropes or similar restraints to prevent movement; to restrict someone's freedom of action.
secure with ropes or restrain movement

Examples

They used heavy straps to tie down the furniture in the moving truck.
She felt tied down by her responsibilities at home and work.
Please tie down the tent before the storm arrives.

Synset Examples

tie down the prisoners
tie up the old newspapers and bring them to the recycling shed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tie up - to fasten or secure with string, rope, or cord
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01289516-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ taɪ daʊn / (AmE), / taɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to limit someone's freedom or independence by making them depend on or responsible for something or someone
restrict someone's freedom through obligation

Examples

He felt tied down by his family responsibilities and longed for more freedom.
She didn't want to be tied down by a permanent job just yet.
They worried that buying a house would tie them down to one place.

Synset Examples

He was tied down by his work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
  • Antonym: free - to release from restriction or obligation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00887289-v
Synset POS: v
