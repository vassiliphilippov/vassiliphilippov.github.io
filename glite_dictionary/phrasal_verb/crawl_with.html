to crawl with

1. VERB

/ krɔl wɪð / (AmE), / krɔːl wɪð / (BrE)
To be filled or infested with a large number of people, animals, or things, often unpleasant or undesirable.
be filled with (often unpleasant) things

Examples

The abandoned building was crawling with rats after years of neglect.
Her kitchen became crawling with ants over the summer.
The festival grounds were crawling with people by noon.

Synset Examples

My backpack crawled with ants after sitting on the ground.
The rotten fruit was crawling with maggots.
The garden was crawling with insects this summer.
The old house was crawling with spiders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swarm with - to be full of large numbers of moving things or people
  • Synonym: overrun - to be filled or invaded by large numbers
