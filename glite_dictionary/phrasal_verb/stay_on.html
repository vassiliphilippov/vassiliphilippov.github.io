to stay on

1. VERB

/ steɪ ɑn / (AmE), / steɪ ɒn / (BrE)
to continue to remain in a place, position, or situation after others have left or after the expected time has passed
continue to remain in a place or role

Examples

She decided to stay on at the university to complete her postgraduate degree.
Many employees choose to stay on after reaching retirement age.
He was supposed to leave last month, but he is staying on for another project.

Synset Examples

After graduation, she stayed on in Cambridge as a student adviser
Stay with me, please
despite student protests, he remained Dean for another year
She continued as deputy mayor for another year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remain - to continue to exist, especially after other similar people or things have ceased to exist
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place or a person
