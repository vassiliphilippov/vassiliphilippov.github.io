to pull back

1. VERB

/ pʊl bæk / (AmE), / pʊl bæk / (BrE)
to move or draw something to a position further back or away from a forward position
move or draw something backward

Examples

He told her to pull back the curtain so the sunlight could enter the room.
As the ball came toward his face, he instinctively pulled back his head.
The troops received orders to pull back from the front line.

Synset Examples

Pull back your arms!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to push forward - to move or press something to a forward position

hold open edges (surgery)
medical

Examples

The surgeon used forceps to pull back the skin and expose the underlying tissue.
We need to pull back the wound edges to gain better access for the procedure.
