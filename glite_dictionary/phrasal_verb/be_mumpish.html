to be mumpish

1. VERB

/ bi ˈmʌmpɪʃ / (AmE), / biː ˈmʌmpɪʃ / (BrE)
to be sulky or gloomy in mood or manner
to be sulky or gloomy
archaic

Examples

He tends to be mumpish whenever things don't go his way.
Please don't be mumpish about the change in plans.
After losing the game, she couldn't help but be mumpish for the rest of the evening.

Synset Examples

Don't be so mumpish; let's enjoy the day!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulk - to be silent or bad-tempered out of annoyance or disappointment
  • Antonym: cheerful - being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'mumpish', an English adjective meaning sulky or gloomy, likely from the dialect verb 'mump' (to sulk or grimace).
