to chew out

1. VERB

/ tʃu aʊt / (AmE), / tʃuː aʊt / (BrE)
to reprimand or scold someone harshly, especially in an angry manner
scold or reprimand harshly

Examples

The coach chewed out the team after their poor performance in the game.
She was chewed out by her manager for arriving late to the meeting.
If you keep talking back in class, the teacher will chew out you in front of everyone.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to criticize someone angrily for their behavior
  • Synonym: to dress down - to reprimand someone severely
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone

Etymology

From the metaphorical use of 'chew', suggesting vigorous talking or scolding.
