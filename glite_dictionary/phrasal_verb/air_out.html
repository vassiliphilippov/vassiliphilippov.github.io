to air out

1. VERB

/ ɛr aʊt / (AmE), / eə aʊt / (BrE)
to expose something to fresh air in order to remove stale odors, moisture, or to refresh it
expose to fresh air

Examples

After winter, we always air out the house to get rid of the musty smell.
Make sure to air out your gym bag so it doesn't start to stink.
She left the windows open to air out the bedroom.

/ ɛr aʊt / (AmE), / eə aʊt / (BrE)

Examples

3. VERB

/ ɛr aʊt / (AmE), / eə aʊt / (BrE)
to allow fresh air to circulate in a space or to dry or ventilate something by exposing it to air
let fresh air circulate; ventilate

Examples

We opened all the windows to air out the apartment after painting.
It's a good idea to air out your clothes before storing them for the season.
He left his sneakers outside to air out and get rid of the smell.
