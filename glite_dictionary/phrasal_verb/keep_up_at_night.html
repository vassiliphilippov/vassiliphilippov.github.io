to keep up at night

1. VERB

/ kip ʌp ət naɪt / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp ət naɪt / (BrE)
to prevent someone from sleeping because of worry, anxiety, or other preoccupations
prevent sleep due to worry or anxiety

Examples

Financial stress can keep up at night if you don't have a plan to manage it.
The pain in his leg kept up at night for weeks after the accident.
Thinking about climate change sometimes keeps up at night.

Synset Examples

Worries about her job keep her up at night.
The thought of an upcoming exam keeps him up at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep awake - to prevent someone from falling asleep
