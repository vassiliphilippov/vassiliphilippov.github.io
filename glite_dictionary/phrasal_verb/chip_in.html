to chip in

1. VERB

/ tʃɪp ɪn / (AmE), / tʃɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to contribute money or resources to a common cause, especially with others
contribute to a common cause

Examples

Everyone decided to chip in a few dollars to buy a farewell gift for Sam.
If you chip in with your ideas, we can finish the project faster.
We all chipped in and cleaned up the park after the event.

Synset Examples

I gave at the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contribute - to give or supply for a common purpose

Etymology

Originally American English, possibly from the idea of adding chips (tokens) to a pool in poker.

2. VERB

/ tʃɪp ɪn / (AmE), / tʃɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to contribute money, resources, or effort towards a common purpose, especially with others
contribute to a shared expense or effort
informal

Examples

We all decided to chip in and buy a surprise cake for her birthday.
If everyone chips in, we can cover the cost of the new equipment.
She always chips in when someone needs help paying for lunch.

Synset Examples

Everyone should chip in for the party supplies.
Can you chip in a few dollars for lunch?
Let's all chip in for the group gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contribute - to give in order to help achieve or provide something
  • Synonym: to donate - to give money or goods for a cause or to help

Etymology

Possibly from the 19th-century gambling term 'chip in', referring to placing chips (tokens representing money) in a pot.
