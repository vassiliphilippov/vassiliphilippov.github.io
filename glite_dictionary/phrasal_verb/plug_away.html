to plug away

1. VERB

/ plʌɡ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / plʌɡ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to work steadily and persistently at something, especially something difficult or tedious
work persistently at a task

Examples

She continued to plug away at her studies despite the many challenges.
Even though progress was slow, he plugged away until the entire project was completed.
Writers often have to plug away at their craft for years before achieving recognition.

Synset Examples

She keeps plugging away at her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - continue in a course of action even in the face of difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or discontinue an effort

Etymology

Originally from the sense of 'plug' meaning to press on or persist, plus 'away' to indicate continuous action.

2. VERB

/ plʌɡ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / plʌɡ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to continue working steadily and with determination, especially on something difficult or tedious
work steadily and persistently
informal

Examples

If you plug away at your studies, you will improve over time.
She plugged away at her manuscript until she finally finished the novel.
Despite many setbacks, the scientists kept plugging away at the challenging research problem.

Synset Examples

Students must plug away at this problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to continue in a course of action despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or cease making an effort
