to bring down

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to cause the defeat or removal of someone in a position of power, especially a government or ruler
cause the defeat or removal (especially of a ruler)

Examples

Several scandals threatened to bring down the entire administration.
It was the people’s movement that eventually brought down the dictator.
Opposition parties are determined to bring down the corrupt government.

Synset Examples

The Czar was overthrown
subvert the ruling class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overthrow - to remove forcibly from power
  • Synonym: to depose - to remove from office suddenly and forcefully
  • Synonym: to topple - to cause to fall, especially a regime or government
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02407551-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to cause something or someone, especially an aircraft, to move from a higher to a lower position or to land on the ground
cause to fall or land (esp. aircraft)

Examples

The firefighters managed to bring down the damaged helicopter safely despite the storm.
The hunter tried to bring down the bird with a single shot.
After hours of negotiation, the pilot finally brought down the plane without incident.

Synset Examples

the pilot managed to land the airplane safely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to bring an aircraft or a person safely to the ground
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01985050-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to elicit enthusiastic applause or approval from an audience
elicit strong applause or approval

Examples

The young comedian managed to bring down the crowd with his hilarious routine.
Her stunning performance truly brought down the audience last night.
If you give it your all, you might bring down the house at the talent show.

Synset Examples

Her playing brought down the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring down the house - to elicit overwhelming applause from the audience

Examples

She brings down the curtains every spring to wash them.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

6. VERB

/ brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to be in a worse or lower state, especially to reduce someone's spirits or cause negative results; (occasionally) to enliven a space or situation.
to reduce spirits; cause trouble; sometimes enliven

Examples

The negative comments from her coworkers really brought down her confidence.
The new policy has brought down profits for many small businesses.
The cheerful decorations brought down the formality of the office, making it feel more inviting.

Synset Examples

Her actions brought down on the team unexpected consequences.
Dismal Jimmy brought down the team's spirits again.
The carnival brought joy to the entire town.
Her latest sofa painting brought life to the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depress - make someone feel less cheerful or hopeful
  • Antonym: to cheer up - make someone feel happier or more cheerful
  • Antonym: lift - raise spirits or mood
