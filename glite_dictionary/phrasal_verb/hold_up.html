to hold up

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ʌp / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to present someone or something as an example, especially to praise or admire them
present as an example for praise

Examples

The teacher decided to hold up Tom’s project as a model for the rest of the class.
She was held up as an example of dedication and hard work.
The organization likes to hold up success stories to inspire new members.
