to remain stagnant

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn ˈstæɡnənt / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn ˈstæɡnənt / (BrE)
to show no growth, change, or progress over a period of time
not grow, change, or progress

Examples

If productivity remains stagnant, the company may face financial difficulties.
Her career had remained stagnant for several years before she decided to retrain.
The population numbers have remained stagnant since the last census.

Synset Examples

The economy has remained stagnant for too long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stagnate - to stop developing or making progress
