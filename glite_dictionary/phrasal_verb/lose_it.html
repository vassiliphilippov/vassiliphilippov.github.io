to lose it

1. VERB

/ luːz ɪt / (AmE), / luːz ɪt / (BrE)
to lose control of one's emotions, typically by becoming very upset or angry
lose emotional control
informal

Examples

He was trying to stay calm, but eventually he lost it and started yelling at everyone.
If I don't take a break soon, I'm going to lose it at work.
She completely lost it when she saw the state of the kitchen.

Synset Examples

When she heard that she had not passed the exam, she lost it completely
When her baby died, she snapped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - suddenly lose control or become angry
  • Antonym: to keep it together - to remain calm and in control

2. VERB

/ luːz ɪt / (AmE), / luːz ɪt / (BrE)
to suddenly become extremely angry, upset, or unable to control your emotions
become very angry or lose self-control
informal

Examples

I almost lost it when the car in front of me stopped suddenly.
She tried to stay calm but eventually lost it during the meeting.
Sometimes, when the stress gets too much, he just loses it.

Synset Examples

She’s been so stressed, I’m afraid she might lose it soon.
He almost lost it when he heard the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blow up - to become very angry suddenly
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm
