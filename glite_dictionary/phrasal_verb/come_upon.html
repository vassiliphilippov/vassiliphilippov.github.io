to come upon

1. VERB

/ kʌm əˈpɑn / (AmE), / kʌm əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to find or discover something unexpectedly or by chance
find unexpectedly or by chance

Examples

While cleaning the attic, I came upon a box of old photographs.
You might come upon some interesting wildlife if you take this trail.
She was surprised when she came upon her childhood diary in the drawer.

Synset Examples

the archeologists chanced upon an old tomb
she struck a goldmine
The hikers finally struck the main path to the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chance upon - to find or encounter by chance
  • Synonym: stumble across - to discover by accident
  • Synonym: to run into - to meet or find unexpectedly

2. VERB

/ kʌm əˈpɑn / (AmE), / kʌm əˈpɒn / (BrE)
(formal) to take possession of (property, assets, or rights), especially as an inheritance or through legal means.
take possession, especially by inheritance
legal

Examples

After his uncle passed away, he came upon the family estate and its responsibilities.
She was surprised to come upon a small fortune when the will was read.
The lawyer explained how the heir would officially come upon all the property.

Synset Examples

She entered upon the estate of her rich relatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enter upon - to begin to have or use something officially, especially property or rights
