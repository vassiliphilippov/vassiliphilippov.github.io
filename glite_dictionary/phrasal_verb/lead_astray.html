to lead astray

1. VERB

/ lid əˈstreɪ / (AmE), / liːd əˈstreɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone to go in the wrong direction or to guide someone wrongly, especially by giving incorrect information
to guide wrongly or deceive

Examples

The tour guide accidentally led astray half the group, and they got lost in the city.
False information can easily lead astray a person unfamiliar with the area.
She was led astray by the wrong directions and missed her appointment.

Synset Examples

The pedestrian misdirected the out-of-town driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or belief
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02004320-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lid əˈstreɪ / (AmE), / liːd əˈstreɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone to behave badly or do something wrong, often by giving bad advice or setting a poor example
cause someone to behave badly

Examples

The peer group can sometimes lead astray even the most well-behaved children.
He was led astray by false friends and made poor decisions.
Parents worry that certain movies might lead astray impressionable teenagers.

Synset Examples

It was common practice to lead off the young ones, and teach them bad habits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - to give someone the wrong idea or impression
  • Synonym: corrupt - to make someone dishonest or immoral
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02586477-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lid əˈstreɪ / (AmE), / liːd əˈstreɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone to believe something that is not true; to mislead or deceive someone
mislead or deceive someone

Examples

The con artist tried to lead astray the investors with false promises of high returns.
Her friends warned her not to let bad influences lead her astray at college.
The misleading advertisement led astray many customers who thought the product was genuine.

Synset Examples

The insurance company deceived me when they told me they were covering my house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00856102-v
Synset POS: v
