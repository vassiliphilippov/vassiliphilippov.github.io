to turn out to be true

1. VERB

/ tɝn aʊt tə bi tru / (AmE), / tɜːn aʊt tə bi truː / (BrE)
to be discovered or shown to be correct, factual, or accurate
be revealed as correct or factual

Examples

The scientist's predictions turned out to be true after further experiments confirmed her theory.
It turned out to be true that the old house was once a famous writer's residence.
Although many doubted his claim, it turned out to be true in the end.

Synset Examples

The rumor about the Albany beef turned out to be true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prove - to be shown or found to be true or correct
  • Antonym: turn out to be false - to be revealed as incorrect or untrue
