to play on fears

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɑn fɪrz / (AmE), / pleɪ ɒn fɪəz / (BrE)
to exploit or manipulate people's fears in order to gain an advantage or influence behavior
exploit or manipulate fear

Examples

Politicians often play on fears of crime to gain support during elections.
The advertisement sought to play on fears about personal safety to sell security systems.
Rumors were spread to play on fears of economic instability in the community.

Synset Examples

The media plays on the fears of Boobus Americanus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - use a situation or person unfairly for one's own advantage
  • Synonym: to manipulate - control or influence (a person or situation) cleverly or unscrupulously
