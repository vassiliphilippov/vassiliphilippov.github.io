to ride roughshod

1. VERB

/ raɪd ˈrʌfˌʃɑd / (AmE), / raɪd ˈrʌfʃɒd / (BrE)
to treat someone or something inconsiderately or harshly, without regard for their feelings or rights
treat someone harshly or inconsiderately

Examples

The new manager tended to ride roughshod over employees' opinions, making decisions unilaterally.
We must not allow one group to ride roughshod over the rights of others.
In his rush to finish the project, he rode roughshod over important safety procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bulldoze - to coerce or treat someone in an overbearing way
  • Synonym: trample - to ignore or crush someone's rights or feelings with force
  • Antonym: respect - to treat with consideration and regard

Etymology

From the literal expression referring to riding horses shod with rough, spiked shoes, implying ruthlessness; dates from the 18th century.
