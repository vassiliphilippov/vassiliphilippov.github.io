to curl up

1. VERB

/ kɝl ʌp / (AmE), / kɜːl ʌp / (BrE)
to move so that your body is bent and your arms and legs are close to each other, often to make yourself comfortable or because you are cold, frightened, or in pain.
bend body into a curved position

Examples

After a long day, she liked to curl up on the sofa with a good book.
The cat curled up next to the fireplace and fell asleep.
When the thunderstorm started, the child instinctively curled up under his blanket.

Synset Examples

She curled farther down under the covers
She fell and drew in

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to straighten out - to make or become straight after being curled or bent
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02102736-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɝl ʌp / (AmE), / kɜːl ʌp / (BrE)
to lie or sit in a position with your arms and legs close to your body, usually for comfort, warmth, or rest
sit or lie with body bent close together

Examples

On cold nights, I like to curl up under a big blanket and read a book.
The dog curled up by the fireplace and soon fell asleep.
She always curls up on the couch to watch her favorite movies.

Synset Examples

The moggy curled up on the windowsill, enjoying the sun.
Her blacky cat always curls up on my lap.
