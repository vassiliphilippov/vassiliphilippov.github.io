to move on to

1. VERB

/ muːv ɑn tuː / (AmE), / muːv ɒn tʊ / (BrE)
to proceed or transition to discussing or dealing with a different subject, activity, or topic
proceed to new subject or activity

Examples

After finishing the introduction, the presenter decided to move on to the main points.
Once we resolve this issue, we can move on to discussing our long-term goals.
She suggested that the team move on to the next item on the agenda.

Synset Examples

Let's move on to the next topic in discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proceed to - to go forward or continue with something
