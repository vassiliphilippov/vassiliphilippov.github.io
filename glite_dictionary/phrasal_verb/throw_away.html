to throw away

1. VERB

/ θroʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / θrəʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or get rid of something because it is no longer needed or wanted
get rid of; remove

Examples

Don't forget to throw away any expired food from the refrigerator.
She decided to throw away his old letters after the breakup.
You shouldn't throw away opportunities that might lead to your success.

Synset Examples

he shed his image as a pushy boss
shed your clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something that is no longer useful
  • Antonym: keep - to retain or continue to have
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01516062-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ θroʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / θrəʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to dispose of something by discarding it; to get rid of by throwing in the trash or removing from use
dispose of by discarding

Examples

Please throw away any trash before you leave the room.
He accidentally threw away an important document with the junk mail.
We are throwing away old clothes to make more space in the closet.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something as useless or unwanted
  • Antonym: keep - to retain or hold onto something rather than dispose of it
