to clam up

1. VERB

/ klæm ʌp / (AmE), / klæm ʌp / (BrE)
to suddenly stop talking or refuse to speak, especially when feeling nervous or unwilling to share information
suddenly stop talking; refuse to speak

Examples

When I asked about his childhood, he immediately clammed up and wouldn't say anything more.
She tends to clam up whenever she's around strangers.
The witness clammed up in court and refused to answer any questions.

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut up - to stop talking or be quiet

Etymology

From the noun 'clam' (a shellfish that closes its shell tightly) plus 'up'; likening a person who stops speaking to a clam shutting its shell.
