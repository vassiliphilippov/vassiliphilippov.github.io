to hold on

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒn / (BrE)
to wait and remain connected on a telephone line; to not hang up
wait on the telephone line

Examples

Could you hold on while I transfer your call to the manager?
Please hold on; your call is important to us and will be answered shortly.
If you hold on a moment, I'll check if she's available.

Synset Examples

Please hang on while I get your folder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang on - to wait or remain connected, especially on the telephone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00792171-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒn / (BrE)
to wait or stop for a short time, often while expecting further instructions or developments
wait or stop briefly
informal

Examples

Hold on a minute while I check if he's in his office.
She asked me to hold on during the phone call while she found the document.
If you hold on, I'll be right with you.

Synset Examples

Hold on a moment!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wait - to stay in one place or delay action until a particular time or event

3. VERB

/ hoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒn / (BrE)
to grasp or grip something firmly to avoid letting go
grasp or grip something firmly

Examples

She had to hold on to the railing while climbing the slippery steps.
Please hold on to the rope so you don't fall.
He held on tightly as the boat rocked in the waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to take and keep a firm hold of something
  • Antonym: to let go - to release one's hold on something

/ hoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒn / (BrE)

Examples

He reminded everyone to hold on and not lose hope during the tough times.
