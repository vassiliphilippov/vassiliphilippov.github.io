to take over

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / teɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to assume responsibility for the expenses, debts, or obligations of another person or entity.
assume another's expenses or debts

Examples

The new company will take over the debts of the smaller firm.
When her father retired, she agreed to take over his financial obligations.
The government had to take over the hospital's outstanding loans to keep it running.

Synset Examples

I'll accept the charges
She agreed to bear the responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assume - to take on a responsibility or duty
  • Synonym: to inherit - to receive money, property, or debts from someone after they die

2. VERB

/ teɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to begin to have control, responsibility, or leadership of something from someone else
assume control or responsibility

Examples

When the new manager took over, several changes were implemented immediately.
She is expected to take over the project by the end of the month.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ teɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to seize control of something, especially without proper authority and possibly by force; to usurp or appropriate as one's own
seize control without authority

Examples

The general attempted to take over the government after the president resigned.
A rival company tried to take over the family business through aggressive tactics.
After the king died suddenly, his brother sought to take over the throne.
