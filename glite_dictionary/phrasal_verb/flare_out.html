to flare out

1. VERB

/ flɛr aʊt / (AmE), / fleə aʊt / (BrE)
to become wider at one end or towards the bottom; to spread or splay outward
become wider at one end

Examples

The sleeves of her dress flare out at the wrists, giving it a dramatic look.
The road flares out as it approaches the intersection.
At the bottom, the vase flares out to form a wide, stable base.

Synset Examples

The bellbottom pants flare out

Related Terms

  • Antonym: taper - to become gradually narrower toward one end
