to hang up

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ʌp / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to put a telephone receiver back in its place and end the call
end a phone call by replacing the receiver

Examples

She finished the conversation and hung up the phone quickly.
Please hang up after you leave your message.
He forgot to hang up, so the line was busy all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hang up on - to end a phone call suddenly and deliberately while someone is speaking
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01311687-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hæŋ ʌp / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to end or interrupt a telephone conversation by breaking the connection, especially by replacing the receiver or pressing a button
end a phone conversation

Examples

She got frustrated and hung up before I could explain.
Please don't hang up, your call is important to us.
He was so angry that he hung up in the middle of my sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hang up (phone) - end a phone call
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00363656-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hæŋ ʌp / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to suspend something from above so that it is held in place off the ground or attached to a surface, often using a hook or similar device
suspend or attach something from above

Examples

Please hang up your coat in the closet when you arrive.
She hung up the new calendar on the kitchen wall.
He was busy hanging up the decorations for the party.

Synset Examples

Hang that picture on the wall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to take down - remove something that is hanging or suspended

/ hæŋ ʌp / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to end a telephone call by breaking the connection
end a phone call

Examples

She didn't say goodbye before she hung up the phone.
I accidentally hung up while trying to put the call on hold.

Synset Examples

They decided to hang up after the heated discussion.

Related Terms
