to work it

1. VERB

/ wɝk ɪt / (AmE), / wɜːk ɪt / (BrE)
to perform in a confident, skillful, or attractive way, often in order to impress others
perform confidently or stylishly
informal

Examples

She really knows how to work it when she's on stage.
If you want that promotion, you’ll need to work it in your next meeting.
He decided to work it at the party, charming everyone he met.

Synset Examples

They encouraged him to work it during the audition.
She always knows how to work it on the dance floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - to walk with a proud, confident gait
  • Antonym: to underperform - to perform less well than expected
