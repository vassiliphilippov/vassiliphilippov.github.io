to file away

1. VERB

/ faɪl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / faɪl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put documents or objects in an organized place, especially in a container or cabinet, for future reference or safekeeping.
organize and store for future reference

Examples

It's important to file away your receipts in case you need them for taxes.
She carefully filed away all the patient records in alphabetical order.
I usually file away important emails in a separate folder.

Synset Examples

File these bills, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: file - to place documents in systematic order for storage

2. VERB

/ faɪl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / faɪl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put documents, papers, or information in a place where they can be kept safely and found later, especially in an archive or filing system.
put into an archive or filing system

Examples

After reading the report, she filed away the papers for future reference.
Please file away these documents in the cabinet marked 'archive'.
Old receipts are filed away every year to keep the office organized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archive - to put or store documents in an archive

/ faɪl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / faɪl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to organize and store documents or items in a systematic way for future reference
store and organize for future use

Examples

He carefully filed away every letter he ever received for his records.
