to stay on top of

1. VERB

/ steɪ ɑn tɑp əv / (AmE), / steɪ ɒn tɒp əv / (BrE)
to remain informed about or in control of something so that problems are avoided
remain in control of or manage effectively

Examples

It's important to stay on top of your email so you don't miss anything important.
She works hard to stay on top of her demanding job responsibilities.
Regular updates help managers stay on top of changing market trends.

Synset Examples

I need to stay on top of my assignments.
Being a pertineer helps him stay on top of tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep up with - to continue to be aware of or manage something effectively
  • Antonym: lose track of - to stop being aware of or lose control of something
