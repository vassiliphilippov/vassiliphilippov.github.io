to hire out

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to provide the use of something or one's services to others temporarily in exchange for payment
provide use or services for pay temporarily

Examples

Every summer, we hire out our cottage to vacationers.
The company decided to hire out equipment to local businesses.
After losing his job, he began to hire out as a handyman.

Synset Examples

We rent out our apartment to tourists every year
He hired himself out as a cook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rent out - to allow someone to use something temporarily for payment
