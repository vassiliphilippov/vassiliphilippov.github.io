to march into battle

1. VERB

/ mɑrtʃ ˈɪntu ˈbætəl / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ ˈɪntuː ˈbætl̩ / (BrE)
to move in an organized, deliberate way toward a combat situation, often suggesting determination or readiness for conflict.
proceed toward combat or conflict

Examples

The soldiers marched into battle with unwavering determination.
As the sun rose, the army marched into battle ready to defend their homeland.
In the movie, the hero courageously marched into battle alongside his comrades.

Synset Examples

The infantry marched bravely into battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - to move forward, especially toward a goal or into battle
  • Antonym: retreat - to withdraw from a combat situation
