to cast down

1. VERB

/ kæst daʊn / (AmE), / kɑːst daʊn / (BrE)
to make someone feel sad, discouraged, or disheartened
make someone feel sad or discouraged

Examples

The news of his failure cast down his entire family.
Her harsh criticism cast down his spirits for days.
I don't mean to cast down your enthusiasm, but the project faces many challenges.

Synset Examples

These news depressed her
The bad state of her child's health demoralizes her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depress - to make someone feel sad or hopeless
  • Antonym: to hearten - to make someone feel more cheerful or confident

Etymology

From the verb 'cast' (to throw) + 'down', literally meaning to throw something downward; used figuratively since the 14th century for lowering spirits.
