to take the mask off

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə mæsk ɑf / (AmE), / teɪk ðə mɑːsk ɒf / (BrE)
to reveal one's true self or identity, especially after hiding one's feelings or intentions
reveal true self or identity

Examples

During the interview, he decided to take the mask off and speak honestly about his experiences.
It's refreshing when someone chooses to take the mask off instead of pretending to be perfect.
After months of acting distant, she finally took the mask off and shared her true feelings.

Synset Examples

In the meeting, she decided to take the mask off and be honest.
It's time to take the mask off and show your true self.
He finally took the mask off and revealed his feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop the mask - to stop pretending and show one's real feelings or character
  • Antonym: put on a mask - to hide one's true feelings or identity
