to strip off

1. VERB

/ strɪp ɔf / (AmE), / strɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something completely, especially clothing or a covering, often quickly or in one piece
remove something completely or quickly

Examples

Before starting the renovation, you should strip off all the old paint from the walls.
He quickly stripped off his jacket and joined the game in his t-shirt.
The technician needs to strip off the insulation before repairing the wires.

Synset Examples

Please strip off the old wallpaper before painting.
He decided to strip off his shirt and jump into the pool.
It's time to strip off the protective layer of the new phone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away or off
