to zip up

1. VERB

/ zɪp ʌp / (AmE), / zɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to close something, especially an item of clothing or a bag, by using a zipper
close with a zipper

Examples

He quickly zipped up his suitcase before leaving for the airport.
Don’t forget to zip up your coat before going outside in the snow.
She zipped up her backpack to keep everything from falling out.

Synset Examples

Zip up your jacket — it's cold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unzip - to open something with a zipper
