to drop down

1. VERB

/ drɑp daʊn / (AmE), / drɒp daʊn / (BrE)
to fall or move from a higher position to a lower one, either suddenly or gradually
move or fall to a lower level

Examples

She watched the leaf drop down from the tree and land softly on the ground.
When the temperature dropped down below zero, the pipes froze.
He quickly dropped down to avoid being seen through the window.

Synset Examples

He sank to his knees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to descend - to move downward; to go down
  • Antonym: rise - to move upward; to go up
