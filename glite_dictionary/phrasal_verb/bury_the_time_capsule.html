to bury the time capsule

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛri ðə taɪm ˌkæpsəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛri ðə taɪm ˌkæpsjuːl / (BrE)
to place a container filled with objects or messages intended to be opened in the future into the ground for later discovery
to put a time capsule underground for future discovery

Examples

The school will bury the time capsule during the anniversary celebration next month.
Families often bury the time capsule in their backyard as a tradition for future generations.
They gathered their memories, sealed them in a box, and buried the time capsule beneath the old oak tree.

Synset Examples

They plan to bury the time capsule next week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open the time capsule - to retrieve and uncover the contents of a previously buried time capsule
