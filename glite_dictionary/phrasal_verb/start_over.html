to start over

1. VERB

/ stɑrt ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / stɑːt ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to begin to do something again from the very beginning, often after a setback or failure
begin again from the start

Examples

After losing my files, I had to start over and rewrite my entire report.
Sometimes relationships don’t work out, and people choose to start over in a new city.
If you make a big mistake on the exam, it's often best to start over and try again.

Synset Examples

She was ready to start over after the hard year.
After the mistake, I had to start over completely.
Sometimes, it’s best to start over from scratch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restart - to start something again
  • Synonym: begin again - to begin once more from the beginning
