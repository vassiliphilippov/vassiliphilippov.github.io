to sponge down

1. VERB

/ spʌndʒ daʊn / (AmE), / spʌndʒ daʊn / (BrE)
to wash or clean something using a wet sponge, often to remove dirt, sweat, or to freshen up
wash with a wet sponge

Examples

After a long run, he decided to sponge down with cool water.
The nurse will sponge down the patient to reduce the fever.
She quickly sponged down the kitchen counters before the guests arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wipe down - to clean or dry a surface by rubbing it with a cloth
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00036678-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spʌndʒ daʊn / (AmE), / spʌndʒ daʊn / (BrE)
to clean (someone or something) by rubbing with a wet sponge
clean by rubbing with a wet sponge

Examples

After the gym, he used a damp cloth to sponge down his sweaty face and arms.
The nurse will sponge down the patient to help reduce the fever.
Please sponge down the car before you start waxing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wipe down - to clean the surface of something by wiping it
