to well up

1. VERB

/ wɛl ʌp / (AmE), / wɛl ʌp / (BrE)
(of feelings, emotions, or other things) to rise or become strongly felt, often becoming visible or apparent.
to rise or become strongly felt (esp. emotions)

Examples

Tears began to well up in her eyes as she listened to the sad story.
A sense of pride would well up inside him whenever he saw his children succeed.
Anger can sometimes well up without warning, leaving a person feeling overwhelmed.

Synset Examples

Strong emotions welled up
Smoke swelled from it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - to rise and move forward in a powerful way (emotions, feelings)

2. VERB

/ wɛl ʌp / (AmE), / wɛl ʌp / (BrE)
(of a liquid or emotion) to rise to the surface or become apparent, often suddenly and with intensity.
rise to the surface (emotion or liquid)

Examples

As she listened to the sad story, tears began to well up in her eyes.
A sense of pride would often well up in him at his daughter's achievements.
Gratitude welled up inside her as she accepted the award.

Synset Examples

Tears began to well up in her eyes.
Emotions can well up unexpectedly during a movie.
He felt joy well up inside him at the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - (of emotion or liquid) to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward
  • Antonym: to subside - (of emotion or liquid) to become less intense or to go down to a lower level
