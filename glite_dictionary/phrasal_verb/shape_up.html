to shape up

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪp ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to develop or progress in a positive or satisfactory way
develop positively; show promising improvement

Examples

The project is finally starting to shape up after weeks of hard work.
Her plans for the business are beginning to shape up nicely.
The new park is really shaping up to be an attractive place for families.

Synset Examples

He progressed well in school
My plants are coming along
Plans are shaping up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come along - to make progress; to advance or improve
  • Synonym: progress - to move forward or develop towards an improved state

2. VERB

/ ʃeɪp ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to improve one's behavior, performance, or physical fitness, especially after a period of neglect or poor conduct
improve behavior or performance

Examples

If you don't shape up, you'll be in danger of failing the class.
After a warning from her boss, she began to shape up at work.
Many people resolve to shape up at the start of the new year.

Synset Examples

It's time for him to shape up and take responsibility.
You need to shape up if you want to keep your job.
She decided to shape up before the fitness competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to become better or make something better
  • Antonym: to deteriorate - to become worse
