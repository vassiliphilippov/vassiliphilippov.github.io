to sit in

1. VERB

/ sɪt ɪn / (AmE), / sɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to take part in a form of protest in which people occupy a place as an act of civil disobedience, usually to demand change
participate in a protest by occupying a place

Examples

Several students decided to sit in at the university administration office to demand tuition reform.
Workers will sit in at the factory until management addresses their concerns.
Activists sat in overnight to protest racial segregation at local restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an organized public demonstration expressing objection

Etymology

Formed from the verb "sit" + preposition "in"; widely used since the US civil rights movement of the 1960s.

2. VERB

/ sɪt ɪn / (AmE), / sɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to attend a meeting, class, or event as a visitor or observer, but not as a regular participant
attend as a visitor or observer

Examples

Anyone interested in the project is welcome to sit in on the planning meetings.
I asked my supervisor if I could sit in on the interview to gain some experience.
Several students came to sit in on the lecture even though they weren't enrolled in the class.

Synset Examples

Can I sit in on your Intermediate Hittite class?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to observe - to watch something carefully, especially as part of a process of learning or assessment
  • Antonym: to participate - to take part in an activity or event
