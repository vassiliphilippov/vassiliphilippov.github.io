to gouge out

1. VERB

/ ɡaʊdʒ aʊt / (AmE), / ɡaʊdʒ aʊt / (BrE)
to make a deep cut or groove in a surface, often by scraping or digging forcefully
make a deep cut or groove in a surface

Examples

The construction worker accidentally gouged out a large groove in the metal panel.
Repeatedly moving the heavy table gouged out marks across the hardwood floor.
Careless handling of the tool gouged out a chunk from the side of the sculpture.

Synset Examples

The woman's spiked heels gouged out the wooden floor

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: scratch - to make a shallow cut or mark on a surface
  • Degree Higher: to carve - to cut into a surface to form a shape or pattern

Etymology

From 'gouge', from Old French 'gouge' (chisel), from Late Latin 'gubia'.
