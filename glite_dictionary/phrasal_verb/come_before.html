to come before

1. VERB

/ kʌm bɪˈfɔr / (AmE), / kʌm bɪˈfɔː / (BrE)
to precede someone or something in time, order, or rank; to be the predecessor of
to precede; to be predecessor of

Examples

These events come before the official ceremony begins.
The needs of the many often come before the desires of the few.
Several other applicants came before you for this position.

Synset Examples

Bill preceded John in the long line of Susan's husbands
