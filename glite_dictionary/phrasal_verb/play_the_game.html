to play the game

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ðə ɡeɪm / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
to take part in a competitive activity or situation, typically according to the established rules or conventions.
engage in a competitive activity

Examples

She loves to play the game even when the odds are against her.
If you want to succeed in this business, you have to play the game.
He didn't want to play the game by their rules and chose to walk away.

Synset Examples

He decided to play the game in hard mode for a challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - to try to win or gain something in competition with others
