to lumber with

1. VERB

/ ˈlʌmbɚ wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbə wɪð / (BrE)
to move clumsily or heavily, often because of carrying something awkward or heavy
move clumsily or heavily

Examples

The movers had to lumber with the old piano down the narrow hallway.
After a long day, she lumbered with her groceries up the steep stairs.
He watched the bear lumber with a fallen tree branch through the dense forest.

Synset Examples

She watched him lumber with the awkwardly shaped furniture.
He tried to lumber with the heavy boxes up the stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly with heavy steps, typically because of exhaustion or harsh conditions
  • Synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging one's feet along or without lifting them fully from the ground

Etymology

From 'lumber' (to move in a slow, awkward way) + 'with'.
