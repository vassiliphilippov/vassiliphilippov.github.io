to draw a large crowd

1. VERB

/ drɔ ə lɑrdʒ kraʊd / (AmE), / drɔː ə lɑːdʒ kraʊd / (BrE)
to attract a large number of people as spectators or an audience
attract many spectators

Examples

The concert is expected to draw a large crowd this Saturday evening.
Street performers often draw a large crowd in busy tourist areas.
Despite the rain, the festival managed to draw a large crowd of enthusiastic fans.

Synset Examples

The football match drew a large crowd at the stadium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attract an audience - to draw people to watch or listen, especially at an event
  • Degree Higher: pack the house - to fill a venue with many people
