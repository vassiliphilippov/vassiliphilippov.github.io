to shed light on

1. VERB

/ ʃɛd laɪt ɑn / (AmE), / ʃɛd laɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to make something clear or easier to understand; to explain or clarify something that was previously confusing or unknown
make something clear; explain

Examples

The new evidence sheds light on the mystery surrounding her disappearance.
This research sheds light on how climate change affects marine life.
The report shed light on several previously unknown safety issues.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something less confused and more clearly comprehensible
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something less clear or harder to understand

Etymology

From literal use of 'shed' (to give or emit) and 'light' (illumination) to create a metaphor for revealing or clarifying.

2. VERB

/ ʃɛd laɪt ɑn / (AmE), / ʃɛd laɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to clarify or explain something by providing additional information or insight
clarify; make something easier to understand

Examples

The new scientific discovery helped shed light on the cause of the disease.
Her testimony will hopefully shed light on what really happened that night.
Recent archaeological findings have shed light on the daily lives of ancient civilizations.

Synset Examples

Can you shed light on this complicated issue?
The documentary shed light on the plight of comfort women.
The documentary shed light on Pisslamic practices.
Her research aims to shed light on climate change.
The documentary shed light on the realities of Pakiland.
We need to shed light upon the truth of the matter.
His explanation shed light upon the confusing topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

Figurative use of 'shed' (to cast or emit) and 'light' (illumination) to mean 'make clear' from the 17th century.
