to straighten up

1. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən ʌp / (BrE)
to stand or sit up straight, adjusting one’s posture to be more erect
stand or sit more upright

Examples

After sitting hunched over his desk, he needed to straighten up and stretch.
She told her students to straighten up before the principal entered the room.
Whenever he felt nervous, he would straighten up and try to look confident.

Synset Examples

He drew himself up when he talked to his superior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slouch - to sit, stand, or move in a lazy, drooping way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01986700-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən ʌp / (BrE)
to make a place neat and organized by putting things in their proper places
to tidy or organize

Examples

Before guests arrive, let's straighten up the living room.
The teacher asked the students to straighten up their desks after class.
She spent Saturday morning straightening up her apartment.

Synset Examples

I told my kids to straighten up their toys.
He needs to straighten up and focus on work.
Please straighten up your desk before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make a place clean and orderly
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make something disorganized or untidy
