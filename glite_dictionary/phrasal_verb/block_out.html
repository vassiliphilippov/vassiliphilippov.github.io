to block out

1. VERB

/ blɑk aʊt / (AmE), / blɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to indicate or outline something in a rough or general way, without giving detailed information
sketch or outline roughly

Examples

Let me block out the main points of the proposal before we discuss the details.
They quickly blocked out a basic schedule for the new project.
We can block out the story structure before starting to write the full script.

Synset Examples

We sketched out our plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sketch out - to describe or plan something in a general way, without details
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01008744-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ blɑk aʊt / (AmE), / blɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent something such as light, sound, or air from entering or passing through
prevent from entering (light, sound, etc.)

Examples

Heavy curtains can block out unwanted streetlights at night.
The dense foliage blocks out most of the sunlight in the forest.
She wore sunglasses to block out the glare from the snow.

Synset Examples

block out the strong sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut out - to prevent from entering or coming in
  • Antonym: to let in - to allow something such as light or air to enter
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01480168-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ blɑk aʊt / (AmE), / blɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to plan where songs, scenes, or actions should be placed within a theatrical production
plan arrangement of scenes or songs in theatre

Examples

They spent the afternoon blocking out the first act to ensure smooth transitions between the scenes.
Before choosing the cast, she blocked out the key moments of the play.
