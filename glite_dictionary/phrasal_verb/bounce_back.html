to bounce back

1. VERB

/ baʊns bæk / (AmE), / baʊns bæk / (BrE)
to recover quickly from illness, difficulty, or disappointment; to regain health or strength after a setback
recover quickly from illness or difficulty

Examples

After months of sickness, she managed to bounce back faster than anyone expected.
The company's stock fell sharply but began to bounce back within a few weeks.
Doctors were surprised to see him bounce back so quickly from the operation.

Synset Examples

He got well fast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health or strength
  • Antonym: relapse - to fall back into a previous condition, especially illness

2. VERB

/ baʊns bæk / (AmE), / baʊns bæk / (BrE)
to recover quickly after experiencing difficulties, setbacks, or illness
recover quickly from setbacks

Examples

After losing her job, she managed to bounce back and found a new position in a few months.
The team was able to bounce back from last week's defeat with a convincing win.
It can take some time to bounce back after major surgery, but a positive attitude helps.

Synset Examples

He always manages to bounce back from tough times.
After the setback, she was determined to bounce back stronger.
The economy is expected to bounce back after the recession.
After the gerbilectomy, the little guy bounced back quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after difficulty
  • Antonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state after improvement
