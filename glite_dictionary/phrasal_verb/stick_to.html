to stick to

1. VERB

/ stɪk tu / (AmE), / stɪk tʊ / (BrE)
to continue doing or using something, or to obey a rule, plan, or agreement without changing it
continue or adhere to something

Examples

She decided to stick to her original plan despite the criticism.
If you want to see results, you must stick to your training schedule.
They stuck to their promise and finished the work on time.

Synset Examples

Stick to your principles
stick to the diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere to - to continue to obey or support a rule or belief
  • Antonym: deviate from - to do something differently from what is expected or planned
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00352060-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɪk tu / (AmE), / stɪk tʊ / (BrE)
to adhere or attach firmly to a surface or object
adhere firmly to a surface

Examples

Make sure the tape will stick to the cardboard before you use it for your project.
The mud stuck to my shoes after walking through the wet field.
These labels don't stick to plastic containers very well in the fridge.

Synset Examples

Will this wallpaper adhere to the wall?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick firmly to a surface
  • Synonym: cling - to hold on tightly or stick closely
  • Antonym: to detach - to remove or separate from something to which it is attached
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01359453-v
Synset POS: v
