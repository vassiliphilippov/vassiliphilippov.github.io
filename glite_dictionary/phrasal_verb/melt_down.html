to melt down

1. VERB

/ mɛlt daʊn / (AmE), / mɛlt daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce or cause something to be reduced from a solid to a liquid state, usually by heating
turn to liquid by heating

Examples

The jeweler decided to melt down some old rings to create a new piece.
To make the sauce, slowly melt down the butter in a small saucepan.
Scrap metal is often melted down before being recycled into other products.

Synset Examples

melt butter
melt down gold
The wax melted in the sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melt - to change from a solid to a liquid by heating
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00445584-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɛlt daʊn / (AmE), / mɛlt daʊn / (BrE)
to experience a catastrophic failure, especially by overheating (as in a nuclear reactor), or to lose control emotionally; to break down emotionally or mentally.
collapse or emotional breakdown

Examples

If the cooling system fails, the reactor might melt down and cause a disaster.
After hearing the worst possible news, she began to melt down in front of everyone.
Parents sometimes melt down from the stress of balancing work and family life.

Synset Examples

The reactor could melt down if not properly cooled.
He started to melt down after hearing the bad news.
She had a melt down when her plans fell apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break down - to lose control of one's emotions; to collapse emotionally
  • Synonym: to implode - to collapse inward violently (often used figuratively for failure)
  • Antonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after a breakdown
