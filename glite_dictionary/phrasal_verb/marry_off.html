to marry off

1. VERB

/ ˈmæri ɔf / (AmE), / ˈmæri ɒf / (BrE)
to arrange or cause someone, usually a family member, to get married, often by organizing or facilitating the match
to arrange someone's marriage

Examples

Her parents tried to marry off her to a wealthy suitor.
Some cultures still expect families to marry off their children at an early age.
Despite their efforts, they couldn't marry off their eldest son.

Synset Examples

They plan to marry off their daughter by next summer.
She felt pressured to marry off her children quickly.
It's traditional to marry off the eldest daughter first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange a marriage - to organize someone else's marriage
