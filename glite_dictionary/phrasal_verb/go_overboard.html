to go overboard

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈoʊvərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvəbɔːd / (BrE)
to do something to an excessive or extreme degree, beyond what is reasonable or necessary
do something excessively or to extremes
informal

Examples

I think you went overboard with the spices in this dish—it's a bit too hot for me.
She tends to go overboard when buying gifts for her friends.
Don't go overboard with the decorations; a few balloons are enough.

Synset Examples

He tends to go overboard when it comes to planning parties.
They really went overboard with the holiday decorations.
Don't go overboard with your spending this month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as better or worse than it really is; act excessively
  • Synonym: to go too far - to go beyond the limits of what is acceptable

Etymology

Originally a nautical term meaning to fall over the side of a ship into the water; figurative sense developed in the 20th century.
