to draw off

1. VERB

/ drɔ ɑf / (AmE), / drɔː ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling or drawing it away carefully
remove by pulling away

Examples

She carefully drew off the bandage from his arm.
At the end of the meal, the waiter drew off the tablecloth with a flourish.
He drew off his gloves before shaking hands.

Synset Examples

She placed the tray down and drew off the cloth
draw away the cloth that is covering the cheese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place
  • Synonym: to detach - to separate something that is attached
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01595695-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ drɔ ɔf / (AmE), / drɔː ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or take a part of a supply, especially a liquid, from a container or source
remove a portion from a supply source

Examples

The technician drew off a sample of the liquid for testing.
You should draw off any excess oil before refilling the engine.
They drew off funds from the reserve account to cover the expenses.

Synset Examples

She drew $2,000 from the account
The doctors drew medical supplies from the hospital's emergency bank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - remove money or supplies from a source
  • Antonym: to add - to put something with something else; opposite of remove
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02316367-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ drɔ ɑf / (AmE), / drɔː ɒf / (BrE)
to remove a liquid from a larger source or container, often by pouring or draining
remove liquid from a source

Examples

After boiling the pasta, remember to draw off the remaining water before adding the sauce.
The brewery drew off a sample from the large fermentation tank for testing.
They carefully draw off excess oil to prevent spilling during repairs.

Synset Examples

We can draw off the excess liquid before serving.
He will draw off the paint into a smaller container.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain - to cause liquid to flow away from something

Synset POS: None
