to make off

1. VERB

/ meɪk ɔf / (AmE), / meɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to leave quickly, especially after committing a crime or taking something unlawfully
leave quickly after a crime or theft

Examples

The burglars managed to make off with jewelry worth thousands of pounds.
As soon as he saw the police, he tried to make off down a side street.
Someone broke into the warehouse and made off with several computers.

Synset Examples

The thief made off with our silver
the accountant absconded with the cash from the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abscond - to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection or arrest
  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
