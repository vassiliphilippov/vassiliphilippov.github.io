to own up

1. VERB

/ oʊn ʌp / (AmE), / əʊn ʌp / (BrE)
to admit or acknowledge that you have done something wrong or made a mistake
admit wrongdoing or mistake
informal

Examples

It's better to own up to your mistakes than to cover them up.
She finally owned up to breaking the vase after hours of questioning.
If you own up now, the consequences will be less severe.

Synset Examples

the writer of the anonymous letter owned up after they identified his handwriting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit that you have done something wrong or illegal
  • Antonym: to deny - to say that something is not true or that you did not do something
