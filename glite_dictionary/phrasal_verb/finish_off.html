to finish off

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ ɔf / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to complete something, especially the last part of a task or activity
complete the last part of a task

Examples

I need to finish off these reports before I leave the office.
She stayed late to finish off the cake for the party.
Once you finish off the last chapter, the book is complete.

Synset Examples

I finally got through this homework assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - to bring something to an end or make it whole
  • Antonym: start - to begin doing something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00485823-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ ˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ ˌɒf / (BrE)
to consume, use up, or complete something, especially by using the last of it or bringing it to an end
consume or complete something

Examples

They managed to finish off the last slice of cake before anyone else could get it.
She stayed up late to finish off her assignment before the deadline.
He opened a new bottle of juice after we finished off the old one.

Synset Examples

We finished off the six-pack of rolls in no time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to use up - to consume all of a supply
  • Synonym: complete - to make something whole or finished
