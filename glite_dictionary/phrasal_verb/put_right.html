to put right

1. VERB

/ pʊt raɪt / (AmE), / pʊt raɪt / (BrE)
to correct a mistake or problem; to restore something to its proper state or order
to correct or fix something

Examples

We called a technician to put right the problem with our heating system.
She apologized and tried her best to put right the misunderstanding.
It took some time, but eventually, they managed to put right the financial errors.

Synset Examples

we need to iron out our disagreements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fix - to repair or correct something
  • Synonym: correct - to remove errors or faults
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make a mistake or cause disorder
