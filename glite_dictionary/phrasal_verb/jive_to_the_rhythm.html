to jive to the rhythm

1. VERB

/ dʒaɪv tə ðə ˈɹɪðəm / (AmE), / dʒaɪv tʊ ðə ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
to dance energetically and rhythmically to music, especially in a lively or enthusiastic manner
dance energetically to music

Examples

Whenever her favorite song comes on, she can't help but jive to the rhythm in the living room.
The crowd encouraged everyone to get up and jive to the rhythm during the concert.
At the party, we all started jiving to the rhythm, laughing and having a great time.

Synset Examples

She loves to jive to the rhythm of the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jive - to dance a lively style of swing dance
  • Synonym: boogie - to dance energetically to music

Etymology

From 'jive', a lively dance style from African-American jazz culture, 1930s U.S.
