to pad out

1. VERB

/ pæd aʊt / (AmE), / pæd aʊt / (BrE)
to add unnecessary or extra material to something in order to make it longer or fuller
add extra or unnecessary material

Examples

The novelist had to pad out the story to meet the publisher's minimum word count.
Some speakers pad out their presentations with anecdotes to fill the allotted time.
He realized he shouldn't pad out his essays with irrelevant information.

Synset Examples

They pad out the report with unnecessary details.
He tried to pad out his resume with extra skills.
We need to pad out the presentation with more visuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to increase something, often artificially, by adding extra material
  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more interesting by adding extra details, sometimes unnecessary
