to couple on

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌpəl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkʌpəl ɒn / (BrE)
to join or link together, especially by means of a mechanical or formal connection
to link together or connect

Examples

The two train cars were coupled on to form a longer passenger train.
Can we couple on these additional proposals to the main contract?
To increase the system's capacity, the engineer suggested coupling on another generator.

Synset Examples

can we couple these proposals?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join together so as to provide communication or access
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to disconnect
