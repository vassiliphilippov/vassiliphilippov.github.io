to fit in

1. VERB

/ fɪt ɪn / (AmE), / fɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to be harmonious or compatible; to go well together with something else
be compatible; go well together

Examples

The new curtains fit in perfectly with the modern decor.
These two melodies just don't fit in together in the same song.
His proposal didn't fit in with the overall theme of the project.

Synset Examples

The colors don't harmonize
Their ideas concorded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - to be in agreement; to go well together
  • Antonym: clash - to be incompatible or to not go well together
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02705946-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɪt ɪn / (AmE), / fɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to be accepted as a member of a group or to blend harmoniously with others
be accepted as part of a group

Examples

She was nervous on her first day, hoping she would fit in with her new classmates.
Some people change their appearance in order to fit in with a group.
Despite his efforts, Mark never really managed to fit in at his new job.

Synset Examples

If you want to fit in, you must be able to take a joke.
It's important to fit in during the team meeting.
She tried hard to fit in at her new school.
He struggled to fit in with the new group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blend in - to look or seem the same as those around you and not be easily noticeable

Synset POS: None
