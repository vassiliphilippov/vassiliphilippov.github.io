to burn in

1. VERB

/ bɝn ɪn / (AmE), / bɜːn ɪn / (BrE)
to test, calibrate, or run new equipment or settings for a period to ensure they function properly or to improve safety, especially with electronics and tires
test or calibrate new equipment or settings

Examples

Many audiophiles believe it's necessary to burn in new speakers before they reach their optimal sound quality.
The technician advised us to burn in the computer components to catch any early failures.
You should burn in your new tires for the first 100 miles to ensure proper traction.

Synset Examples

Make sure to burn in the new settings before finalizing.
It’s important to burn in these new tires before a long trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break in - to use something new until it is comfortable or safe for use

2. VERB

/ bɝn ɪn / (AmE), / bɜːn ɪn / (BrE)
to operate (a piece of equipment, especially electronic hardware) continuously for a period to test its reliability or stabilize performance before regular use
test hardware by continuous use

Examples

It is standard practice to burn in the graphics card before shipping it to customers.
Technicians usually burn in new server hardware overnight to check for problems.
After installing the upgrade, he decided to burn in the system for 24 hours.

Synset Examples

They need to burn in the graphics card for optimal performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress test - a test to determine the stability of hardware under heavy load

Etymology

From 'burn' (to use intensely) + 'in'; used in electronics since mid-20th century.
