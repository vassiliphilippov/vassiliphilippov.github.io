to come to (someone's) aid

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə eɪd / (AmE), / kʌm tə eɪd / (BrE)
to help or assist someone, especially in a time of need or difficulty
to help or assist someone

Examples

When the child was lost, several strangers came to her aid without hesitation.
As soon as I slipped on the ice, my friends came to my aid.
The local community quickly came to the aid of those affected by the flood.

Synset Examples

The firefighters came to her aid during the blaze.
He rushed to come to her aid when she fell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - to give assistance or support
  • Synonym: assist - to give support or aid
  • Antonym: abandon - to leave someone without help or support
