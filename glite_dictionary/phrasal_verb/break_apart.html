to break apart

1. VERB

/ breɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / breɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to separate something into its component parts; to cause something to come apart into pieces
separate into pieces; disassemble

Examples

The old machine finally broke apart after years of heavy use.
She tried to break apart the puzzle to see how it worked.
Rising tension threatened to break apart the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disassemble - to take apart into pieces
  • Antonym: to assemble - to put together into a single unit
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01661660-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ breɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / breɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to separate into pieces with force, often suddenly or noisily; to smash into parts
split or smash into parts

Examples

The glass vase broke apart when it hit the floor.
The old bridge broke apart during the storm.
If you twist it too hard, the toy will break apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shatter - to break suddenly into many pieces
  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces, usually gradually
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ breɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / breɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to separate into pieces or to be divided, often suddenly or forcefully
separate into pieces

Examples

If you bend the stick too far, it will break apart.
After years of tension, the community began to break apart.
The glass vase hit the floor and broke apart into tiny shards.

Synset Examples

The country is disunifying
Yugoslavia broke apart after 1989

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break up - to separate into smaller pieces or end a relationship
  • Synonym: to disintegrate - to break into small parts or lose cohesion
