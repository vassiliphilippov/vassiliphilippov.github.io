to buck for

1. VERB

/ bʌk fɔr / (AmE), / bʌk fə / (BrE)
to try hard to gain favor, promotion, or some advantage, especially by seeking approval from someone in authority
seek favor or advancement

Examples

Several employees are bucking for a promotion in the next review cycle.
He was clearly bucking for attention during the entire meeting.
Don’t waste time bucking for praise—focus on doing your job well.

Synset Examples

They are bucking for the approval of their manager.
She seems to be bucking for additional responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to angle for - to try subtly to obtain something, typically a favor or advantage

Etymology

Possibly from North American English 'buck', meaning to strive or compete for something.
