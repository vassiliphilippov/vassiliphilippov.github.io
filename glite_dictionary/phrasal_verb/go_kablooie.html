to go kablooie

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ kəˈblu.i / (AmE), / ɡəʊ kəˈbluː.i / (BrE)
to explode, break, or stop working suddenly and completely, often with dramatic effect
suddenly explode or break down
informal american

Examples

My computer went kablooie right before I finished the report.
The old television finally went kablooie after years of use.
When the power supply went kablooie, the whole house lost electricity.

Synset Examples

The old car engine went kablooie during the race.
Her plans went kablooie when it started raining.
The fireworks went kablooie in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go bust - to fail financially or break down
  • Synonym: to explode - to burst or shatter violently
  • Synonym: malfunction - to fail to function properly

Etymology

Imitative origin; "kablooie" mimics the sound of an explosion and gained use in US English in the mid-20th century.
