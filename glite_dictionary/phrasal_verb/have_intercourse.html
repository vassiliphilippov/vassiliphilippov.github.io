to have intercourse

1. VERB

/ hæv ˈɪntərˌkɔrs / (AmE), / hæv ˌɪntəˈkɔːs / (BrE)
to engage in sexual activity with someone
engage in sexual activity

Examples

Many people believe couples should not have intercourse before marriage.
The doctor asked if she had recently had intercourse.
They decided to have intercourse only after discussing it openly.

Synset Examples

Adam knew Eve
Were you ever intimate with this man?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have sex - to have sexual intercourse
  • Antonym: to abstain - to refrain from having sexual activity

Etymology

From Latin 'intercursus' (a running between, commerce, intercourse), from 'inter-' (between) + 'currere' (to run).
