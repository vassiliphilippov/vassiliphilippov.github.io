to bear on

1. VERB

/ bɛr ɑn / (AmE), / beə ɒn / (BrE)
to be relevant to or have an effect upon something
be relevant to; affect

Examples

The new evidence may bear on the outcome of the investigation.
His financial situation does not bear on our decision.
These regulations bear on every aspect of the industry.

Synset Examples

Will the new rules affect me?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affect - to have an influence on something
  • Synonym: relate to - to be connected to; be relevant to
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00137133-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɛr ɑn / (AmE), / bɛə ɒn / (BrE)
to press, drive, or impel (someone) to action or completion of an action
to press or impel someone to act

Examples

The strict deadline bore on the team, motivating them to work late into the night.
Parental expectations often bear on students to pursue certain careers.
Financial pressures bore on him to accept the job offer quickly.

Synset Examples

He pushed her to finish her doctorate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push - to encourage or force someone to do something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00768022-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɛr ɑn / (AmE), / beə ɒn / (BrE)
to be relevant or applicable to; to have a connection with something
be relevant or connected

Examples

Her research findings bear on the environmental policies being considered.
The evidence does not bear on the central issue of the case.
Several new studies bear on our understanding of climate change.

Synset Examples

There were lots of questions referring to her talk
My remark pertained to your earlier comments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pertain to - to relate to or be about something
  • Synonym: refer to - to mention or have relation to something

Synset POS: v
