to feed on

1. VERB

/ fid ɑn / (AmE), / fiːd ɒn / (BrE)
to be sustained, nourished, or maintained by something, especially in a figurative sense
be sustained or nourished by

Examples

As a writer, she feeds on the experiences she gathers from her travels.
Some people feed on the excitement of competition and always strive for more.
He fed on the support of his family during difficult times.

Synset Examples

He fed on the great ideas of her mentor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thrive on - to flourish because of something
  • Synonym: to depend on - to rely on for support or sustenance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01205854-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fid ɑn / (AmE), / fiːd ɒn / (BrE)
(of an animal) to eat something as its main food source; (figurative) to rely heavily on something for support or sustenance.
eat as main food source; rely heavily on

Examples

Many caterpillars feed on the leaves of specific plants.
News organizations sometimes feed on public fear to attract more viewers.
The wolves in this region feed on deer and other small animals.

Synset Examples

Lions feed on gazelles in the savannah.
Some companies feed on customer data to improve services.
That bug is a parasite, feeding off the host plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsist on - to maintain or support oneself, especially at a minimal level, by using a particular food or resource
  • Synonym: prey on - to hunt and eat as food; to take advantage of
