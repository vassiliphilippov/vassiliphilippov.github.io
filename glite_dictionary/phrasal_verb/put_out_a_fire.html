to put out a fire

1. VERB

/ pʊt aʊt ə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / pʊt aʊt ə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to extinguish a fire, stopping it from burning or spreading
extinguish a fire

Examples

The firefighters worked quickly to put out a fire in the apartment building.
Always keep an extinguisher nearby in case you ever need to put out a fire.
It took three hours to put out a fire that started in the warehouse.

Synset Examples

They had to put out a fire in the kitchen last night.
She managed to put out the fire before it spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extinguish - to cause a fire or light to stop burning or shining
