to put one past

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌn pæst / (AmE), / pʊt wʌn pɑːst / (BrE)
to successfully deceive or trick someone, especially by making them believe something that is not true
deceive or trick someone successfully
informal

Examples

She tried to put one past her older brother, but he caught her in the act.
Don't think you can put one past me; I'm watching you closely!
He managed to put one past the security guard and sneak inside.

Synset Examples

He thought he could put one past the manager.
She tried to put one past her parents, but failed.
You won't put one past me again!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull a fast one - to successfully trick someone; to deceive cleverly
