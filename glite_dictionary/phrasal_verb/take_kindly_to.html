to take kindly to

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈkaɪndli tu / (AmE), / teɪk ˈkaɪndli tʊ / (BrE)
to be willing or inclined to accept or approve of something, often used in the negative to show dislike or resistance
be inclined to accept or approve

Examples

She did not take kindly to criticism from her colleagues.
The committee will not take kindly to any last-minute changes to the proposal.
Our dog doesn't take kindly to strangers entering the garden.

Synset Examples

He did not take kindly to my critical remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welcome - to accept or receive gladly
  • Antonym: to resist - to oppose or show unwillingness towards something

2. VERB

/ teɪk ˈkaɪndli tu / (AmE), / teɪk ˈkaɪndli tə / (BrE)
to react willingly, supportively, or favorably (often with a negative, meaning to respond unfavorably) toward someone or something
react favorably or unfavorably

Examples

He doesn't take kindly to being interrupted during meetings.
Most cats do not take kindly to being bathed.
The manager did not take kindly to suggestions that his methods were outdated.

Synset Examples

He doesn't take kindly to rude comments.
They take kindly to anyone who helps them.
She did not take kindly to the criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welcome - receive or accept gladly
  • Antonym: to resent - feel bitterness or indignation at a person, act, or remark
