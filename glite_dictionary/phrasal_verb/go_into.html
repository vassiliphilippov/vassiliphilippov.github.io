to go into

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈɪntuː / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to enter a place, area, or state
to enter (a place or area)

Examples

The children went into the classroom quietly when the bell rang.
We go into the tunnel just before the train crosses the river.
She hesitated for a moment before going into the dark room.

Synset Examples

the boat entered an area of shallow marshes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enter - to come or go into a place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02020375-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to be used or required for a particular purpose or to contribute to the making of something
to be used or required for something

Examples

A lot of planning went into the wedding to make it perfect.
Considerable time goes into preparing each lesson for the students.
Fresh ingredients go into every dish served at the restaurant.

Synset Examples

A lot of energy went into the organization of this banquet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be used for - to serve a particular function or purpose
