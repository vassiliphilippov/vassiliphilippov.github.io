to get off the ground

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to begin or start to be successful; to be put into action after initial preparation
to begin or start to progress

Examples

We are still trying to get off the ground with our new business venture.
The campaign didn't get off the ground due to a lack of funding.
Once the first sponsors signed up, the project finally got off the ground.

Synset Examples

the project took a long time to get off the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin doing something; to commence
  • Synonym: to take off - (informal) to begin to be successful or active
