to bleed dry

1. VERB

/ bliːd draɪ / (AmE), / bliːd draɪ / (BrE)
to take all of someone's or something's resources, money, or strength so that nothing is left
to exhaust resources completely

Examples

The high cost of medical treatment can bleed dry even the most diligent savers.
If you let your competitors copy your ideas, they might bleed dry your business.
Corrupt officials managed to bleed dry the country's entire welfare system.

Synset Examples

Don't let them bleed you dry with hidden fees.
They will bleed dry any resources left untapped.
The company is at risk of bleeding dry without new investments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain - to remove all of something from; to deplete
  • Synonym: to impoverish - to make someone poor or deprived of resources
