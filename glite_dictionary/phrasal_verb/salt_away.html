to salt away

1. VERB

/ sɔlt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sɒlt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to save or store something, especially money, for future use, often secretly or gradually
store or save for future use

Examples

She managed to salt away a considerable sum from her modest salary over the years.
Investors often salt away assets in offshore accounts for tax purposes.
He quietly salted away enough money to take a long vacation after retirement.

Synset Examples

store grain for the winter
The bear stores fat for the period of hibernation when he doesn't eat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to save up - to keep money aside for future needs
  • Antonym: to spend - to use money for purchases or expenses

Etymology

From the practice of preserving meat or fish with salt, figuratively extended to saving money or valuables.
