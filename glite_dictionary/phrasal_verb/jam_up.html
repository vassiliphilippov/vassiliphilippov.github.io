to jam up

1. VERB

/ dʒæm ʌp / (AmE), / dʒam ʌp / (BrE)
(of a machine, road, etc.) to become congested or blocked so that normal movement or operation is hindered.
become blocked or congested

Examples

If you pour grease down the sink, the pipes can jam up quickly.
Rush hour caused all the highways to jam up for miles.
The copy machine will jam up if too much paper is loaded at once.

Synset Examples

The printer will jam up if you use that paper.
Traffic started to jam up before the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clog up - (of things like pipes or drains) to become blocked
  • Antonym: to clear up - to (cause to) become free of blockage or obstruction
