to sit in with

1. VERB

/ sɪt ɪn wɪð / (AmE), / sɪt ɪn wɪð / (BrE)
to join a group informally or attend as a participant, especially in a meeting, class, or musical performance, without being a regular member.
join a group informally or participate as a guest

Examples

The famous saxophonist was invited to sit in with the jazz band during their evening set.
She asked if she could sit in with the class to observe their discussion.
Several students were allowed to sit in with the research group for the experiment.

Synset Examples

I plan to sit in with the band this weekend.
She was excited to sit in with the guest speaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to become a member or take part in something
  • Synonym: to participate - to be involved in an activity with others
