to push away

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move someone or something away from oneself by pushing, usually with the hands or arms
move away by pushing

Examples

She tried to push away the thought, but it kept returning.
He pushed away the plate because he wasn't hungry anymore.
When the dog jumped up, she gently pushed away its paws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to push aside - to move something out of the way by pushing
  • Antonym: pull towards - to move something nearer by pulling

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To reject or distance oneself from someone, especially emotionally or socially.
reject or distance someone

Examples

Whenever someone gets too close to him emotionally, he tends to push away.
She regretted having pushed away her friends during a difficult period in her life.
He realized he was pushing away people who genuinely cared about him.

Synset Examples

He often pushes away anyone trying to help him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - To refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something or someone
  • Antonym: welcome - To accept someone warmly and with enthusiasm
