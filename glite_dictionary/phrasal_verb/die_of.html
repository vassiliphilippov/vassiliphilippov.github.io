to die of

1. VERB

/ daɪ ʌv / (AmE), / daɪ ɒv / (BrE)
to pass away as a result of a specified cause, such as a disease or condition
pass away from a specified cause

Examples

Many people in the 19th century would die of diseases that are now preventable.
The plant will die of thirst if you don't water it regularly.
She was devastated to learn that her pet had died of old age.

Synset Examples

That's harsh; what did your last slave die of?
I can't believe you said that; what did your last slave die of?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die from - to pass away as a result of a specified cause, subtle difference in usage with 'die of'
