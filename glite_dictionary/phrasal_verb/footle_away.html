to footle away

1. VERB

/ ˈfuːtəl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈfuːtəl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to waste time or resources on trivial or unimportant matters; to spend time aimlessly
waste time or resources aimlessly

Examples

He tends to footle away most mornings instead of doing something productive.
She realized she had footled away hours scrolling through her phone.
You shouldn't footle away this opportunity to learn something new.

Synset Examples

Don't just footle away your time; focus on your goals.
I love to footle away my weekends with friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fritter away - to waste time, money, or energy on unimportant things
  • Synonym: to squander - to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless or foolish manner

Etymology

From 'footle' (to behave aimlessly), possibly derived from 'footle' meaning nonsense, of uncertain origin; patterned after phrasal verbs like 'fritter away'.
