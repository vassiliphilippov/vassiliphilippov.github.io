to play tricks

1. VERB

/ pleɪ trɪks / (AmE), / pleɪ trɪks / (BrE)
to deceive or try to deceive someone, often in a playful or mischievous way
deceive someone playfully or slyly

Examples

Don't play tricks on your little brother; it's not nice to deceive him.
She suspected her friends were trying to play tricks on her by hiding her keys.
Children sometimes play tricks on their teachers during April Fools' Day.

Synset Examples

We tricked the teacher into thinking that class would be cancelled next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone or cheat them
  • Synonym: play a joke - to do something to make someone look foolish or to amuse oneself at their expense
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02581808-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pleɪ trɪks / (AmE), / pleɪ trɪks / (BrE)
to perform mischievous or deceptive acts, often to amuse oneself at the expense of others
perform mischievous or deceptive acts

Examples

The children loved to play tricks on their babysitter when she wasn't looking.
He tends to play tricks on his coworkers every April Fools' Day.
Someone has been playing tricks with the office supplies again.

Synset Examples

That little imp played tricks on everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play pranks - to carry out practical jokes or mischievous acts
  • Synonym: to deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
