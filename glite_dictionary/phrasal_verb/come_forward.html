to come forward

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈfɔr.wɚd / (AmE), / kʌm ˈfɔː.wəd / (BrE)
to offer oneself or volunteer, especially to provide information, help, or take action; to make oneself known or visible in a situation.
offer oneself or volunteer, make oneself known

Examples

No one was willing to come forward with information about the incident.
After the appeal, several witnesses came forward to help the investigation.
She finally decided to come forward and tell her side of the story.

Synset Examples

Young people should step to the fore and help their peers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step forward - to offer to help or give information
  • Antonym: to hold back - to not volunteer or make oneself known

2. VERB

/ kʌm ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / kʌm ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to offer oneself or volunteer, especially to provide information or help
to volunteer or present oneself

Examples

After the announcement, no one was willing to come forward and claim responsibility.
Several witnesses finally came forward with new evidence.
She decided to come forward and tell the police what she had seen.

Synset Examples

He decided to come forward with the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volunteer - to offer to do something without being forced or paid to do it
