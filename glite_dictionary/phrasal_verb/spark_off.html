to spark off

1. VERB

/ spɑrk ɔf / (AmE), / spɑːk ɒf / (BrE)
to cause something, especially a process, event, or reaction, to begin suddenly; to trigger.
provoke or trigger (an event or reaction) suddenly

Examples

The announcement sparked off a heated debate among the committee members.
His remarks sparked off widespread protests across the city.
A simple misunderstanding can sometimes spark off a major conflict.

Synset Examples

trigger a reaction
actuate the circuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigger - to cause something to start; set off
  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a reaction, especially an intense one
  • Antonym: calm - to cause to become calm or quiet, opposite of incite or spark off
