to do well by

1. VERB

/ du ˈwɛl baɪ / (AmE), / dʊ ˈwɛl baɪ / (BrE)
to treat (someone) with respect, kindness, or consideration
treat someone with respect or kindness

Examples

He always tries to do well by his employees, giving them fair wages and a positive environment.
The organization has done well by the local community, offering support and resources over the years.
She feels it is important to do well by her friends, especially when they need help.

Synset Examples

children should do well by their parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat well - to behave toward someone in a kind or considerate way
  • Antonym: do badly by - to treat someone poorly or unfairly
