to be sick

1. VERB

/ bi sɪk / (AmE), / bi sɪk / (BrE)
to vomit; to bring up the contents of the stomach through the mouth
to vomit
british

Examples

He suddenly had to be sick after eating the spoiled food.
She felt dizzy and knew she was going to be sick on the bus.
Several children had to be sick during the long car journey.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: to throw up - to vomit (informal)
  • Synonym: to regurgitate - bring swallowed food up again to the mouth
