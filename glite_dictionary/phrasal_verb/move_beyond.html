to move beyond

1. VERB

/ muːv bɪˈjɑnd / (AmE), / muːv bɪˈjɒnd / (BrE)
to progress past a particular issue, stage, or obstacle, especially towards something new or better
progress past something, especially figuratively

Examples

It's important for the team to move beyond past mistakes and focus on the future.
She finally managed to move beyond her disappointment and start a new project.
As a society, we must move beyond superficial solutions and address the root causes.

Synset Examples

We need to move beyond inspiration porn to real change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty
  • Synonym: to get over - to recover from or stop being affected by something
