to bow out

1. VERB

/ baʊ aʊt / (AmE), / baʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a job, competition, or activity in a polite or dignified way, especially when retiring or withdrawing
withdraw gracefully; retire politely

Examples

After thirty years in politics, she decided to bow out and spend more time with her family.
The defending champion chose to bow out of the tournament citing an injury.
When new management arrived, he thought it best to bow out and let others take over.

Synset Examples

He bowed out when he realized he could no longer handle the demands of the chairmanship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to leave one's job and stop working, usually because of age or choice
  • Synonym: to resign - to formally leave a job or position

Etymology

From the act of making a bow when leaving as a gesture of respect or farewell.

2. VERB

/ baʊ aʊt / (AmE), / baʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to withdraw from an activity, role, or obligation, especially in a polite or formal way
withdraw from a role or obligation

Examples

After many years in public service, she decided to bow out and enjoy her retirement.
He was expected to speak at the event but chose to bow out at the last moment.
It takes courage to bow out gracefully when the time is right.

Synset Examples

He bowed out when he heard how much work was involved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to remove oneself from participation
  • Synonym: to step down - to resign from a position or role
  • Antonym: join - to become involved in an activity or group

3. VERB

/ baʊ aʊt / (AmE), / baʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to withdraw or resign gracefully from a situation, role, or commitment
withdraw gracefully from a situation

Examples

After twenty years at the company, he decided to bow out and enjoy his retirement.
She plans to bow out of the project before the final phase begins.
It's admirable when a leader knows when to bow out rather than overstay their welcome.

Synset Examples

He chose to bow out rather than risk embarrassment.
She decided to bow out of the competition gracefully.
It's better to bow out than to make a scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step down - to resign or leave a position or office
  • Synonym: to drop out - to withdraw from participation
  • Antonym: to persist - to continue firmly or obstinately
