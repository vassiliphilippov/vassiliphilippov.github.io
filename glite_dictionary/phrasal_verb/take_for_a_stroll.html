to take for a stroll

1. VERB

/ teɪk fər ə stroʊl / (AmE), / teɪk fə ə strəʊl / (BrE)
to go for a relaxed or leisurely walk with someone or something
go for a leisurely walk with someone or something

Examples

Every evening, she likes to take her dog for a stroll around the neighborhood before dinner.
He decided to take his grandparents for a stroll in the park on Sunday afternoon.
After lunch, they would often take the baby for a stroll to help her fall asleep.

Synset Examples

She took her baby for a stroll in the pram.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - walk in a leisurely way
