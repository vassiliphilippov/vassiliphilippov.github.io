to black out

1. VERB

/ blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to make a place or area completely dark, especially by turning off or covering all lights
make completely dark; deprive of light

Examples

During the storm, the city decided to black out all buildings to avoid detection.
The crew needed to black out the theater for the special effects to work properly.
If you black out your room, you'll sleep much better with no light distractions.

Synset Examples

The dining room blackened out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to darken - to make or become dark or darker
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00313430-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to lose consciousness, especially suddenly and temporarily, often as a result of trauma or shock.
lose consciousness suddenly

Examples

He was so dehydrated after the race that he suddenly blacked out on the pavement.
She blacked out for a few seconds after hitting her head on the door frame.
If you stand up too quickly, you might black out for a moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time
  • Synonym: to pass out - to become unconscious unexpectedly
  • Antonym: to come to - to regain consciousness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00023861-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent information from being published or visible, especially by censoring text or images
to censor or suppress information

Examples

The government ordered the media to black out certain controversial details of the report.
Sensitive information in the document was blacked out before release to the public.
During wartime, authorities would often black out names to protect identities.

Synset Examples

parts of the newspaper article were blacked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - to examine and remove content considered unacceptable
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make information known or visible
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00202270-v
Synset POS: v
