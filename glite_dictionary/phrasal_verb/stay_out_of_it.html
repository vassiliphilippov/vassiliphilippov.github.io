to stay out of it

1. VERB

/ steɪ aʊt əv ɪt / (AmE), / steɪ aʊt əv ɪt / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid becoming involved in a situation, argument, or conflict
avoid involvement in a situation

Examples

When the argument started, Anna decided to stay out of it entirely.
I think it’s best for us to stay out of it until things calm down.
He wisely chose to stay out of it rather than take sides.

Synset Examples

When the girls are fighting, it's best to stay out of it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: get involved - to become part of a situation or activity
