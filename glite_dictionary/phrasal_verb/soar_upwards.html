to soar upwards

1. VERB

/ sɔr ˈʌpwɚdz / (AmE), / sɔː ˈʌpwədz / (BrE)
to rise rapidly or suddenly to a higher level
rise rapidly (to higher level)

Examples

Stock prices began to soar upwards after the company announced record profits.
The eagle spread its wings and soared upwards on the morning breeze.
Temperatures are expected to soar upwards this weekend, reaching record highs.

Synset Examples

the dollar soared against the yen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plummet - fall rapidly or suddenly to a lower level
  • Synonym: rise - move to a higher position or level
