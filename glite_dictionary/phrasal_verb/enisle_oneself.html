to enisle oneself

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈaɪl wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ɪnˈaɪl wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to deliberately isolate oneself from others, as if placing oneself on an island
to isolate oneself

Examples

He sometimes enisles himself in his office when he needs to concentrate deeply.
She warned him not to enisle himself from friends after the move.
It's tempting to enisle oneself during stressful times, but support is important.

Synset Examples

He tends to enisle himself during busy meetings.
Don't enisle yourself; join the conversation!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolate oneself - to separate oneself from others

Etymology

From 'enisle' (to make into an island), based on Middle English and Old French 'isle' (island).
