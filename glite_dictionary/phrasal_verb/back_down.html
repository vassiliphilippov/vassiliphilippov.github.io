to back down

1. VERB

/ bæk daʊn / (AmE), / bæk daʊn / (BrE)
to withdraw from a position, claim, or argument, especially due to opposition or pressure
withdraw or give up a stance

Examples

She refused to back down even when the criticism grew stronger.
After a long debate, he finally backed down and apologized for his mistake.
The company will not back down from enforcing its safety standards.

Synset Examples

The bully had to back down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give in - to stop resisting; to yield
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to refuse to change your decision or position

2. VERB

/ bæk daʊn / (AmE), / bæk daʊn / (BrE)
to withdraw from a position or commitment, especially because of opposition or difficulty
withdraw from a commitment or position

Examples

After much debate, the company finally decided to back down from its initial demands.
He refused to back down even when faced with strong opposition from his colleagues.
We won't back down just because things are getting tough.

Synset Examples

He bowed out when he heard how much work was involved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - give in or surrender, especially in a dispute
  • Antonym: to stand firm - refuse to change one's decision or position

3. VERB

/ bæk daʊn / (AmE), / bæk daʊn / (BrE)
to withdraw from a position or commitment, especially in a confrontation or argument, often admitting defeat or yielding to pressure
withdraw from a position, concede

Examples

After a heated debate, he finally decided to back down and accept the compromise.
No matter how much pressure she faced, she refused to back down from her decision.
The company wouldn’t back down even when threatened with legal action.

Synset Examples

She had to back down after realizing her mistake.
Like an old soldier, he never backs down from a challenge.
He refused to back down from the argument.
They expected him to back down, but he stood firm.
If you think I’ll back down, you’re damn straight mistaken.
As a circuit slugger, she never backs down from a fight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give in - to yield or cease opposition
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to maintain one's position and not yield
