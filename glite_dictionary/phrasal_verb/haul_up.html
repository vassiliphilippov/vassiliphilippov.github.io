to haul up

1. VERB

/ hɔl ʌp / (AmE), / hɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or driver) to come to a stop or bring a vehicle to a stop after moving, especially by the side of a road or at a specific location.
to stop a vehicle after driving

Examples

The taxi hauled up outside the hotel, and we quickly got out.
He hauled up at the curb to let his friend jump in.
They hauled up in front of the store, looking for a parking spot.

Synset Examples

The Rolls pulled up on our front lawn
The chauffeur hauled up in front of us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull up - to stop a vehicle after driving
