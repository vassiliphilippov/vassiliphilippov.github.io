to sally out

1. VERB

/ ˈsæli aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsæli aʊt / (BrE)
to rush out suddenly from a place, especially in order to attack or surprise someone
rush out suddenly, often to attack

Examples

The soldiers sallied out from behind the gates to launch a surprise attack on the enemy.
Just as the thief passed by, the police sallied out from their hiding spot and arrested him.
She sallied out of her room, determined to confront whoever was making the noise.

Synset Examples

The attackers leapt out from the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring out - to jump out suddenly
  • Synonym: to burst out - to move suddenly from a place with force or energy

Etymology

From the noun 'sally', from French 'saillie' (a sudden rushing forth), from Latin 'salīre' (to leap).

2. VERB

/ ˈsæli aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsæli aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly, energetically, or forcefully, often on a short excursion or attack
leave suddenly or energetically

Examples

The soldiers decided to sally out at dawn to surprise the enemy.
She sallied out into the rain, determined not to let the weather spoil her day.
When the alarm rang, they sallied out of the office, eager for their lunch break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rush out - to leave a place quickly and energetically
  • Antonym: retreat - to withdraw from a place, especially suddenly or rapidly

Etymology

From 'sally', from Old French saillie (a sudden rushing forth), from Latin sallīre (to leap) + out.

Synset POS: v
