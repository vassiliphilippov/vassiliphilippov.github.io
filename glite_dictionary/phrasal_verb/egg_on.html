to egg on

1. VERB

/ ɛɡ ɑn / (AmE), / ɛɡ ɒn / (BrE)
to encourage or urge someone to do something, especially something unwise or risky
encourage someone to do something risky

Examples

His friends tried to egg on him to jump off the bridge, but he refused.
She was egged on by the crowd to finish her speech despite the heckling.
Don't egg on your brother to misbehave; you'll both get in trouble.

Synset Examples

The other children egged the boy on, but he did not want to throw the stone through the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage someone to act, especially illegally or violently
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *eggja* (to incite).

2. VERB

/ ɛɡ ɑn / (AmE), / ɛɡ ɒn / (BrE)
to encourage or urge someone to do something, especially something unwise or wrong
encourage someone (often unwisely)

Examples

His friends tried to egg on the prank, even though he wasn't sure about it.
Please don't egg on your brother when he's already upset.
The crowd began to egg on the players as the match grew more intense.

Synset Examples

She didn’t want to egg him on during the argument.
He decided to egg her on during the debate.
Don't egg him on; let him decide.
His friends urged him to egg on his opponent.
Don't egg on the kids; they’ll start fighting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to urge on - to encourage or drive someone forward
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular action

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *eggja* (to incite).
