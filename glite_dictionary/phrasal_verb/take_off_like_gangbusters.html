to take off like gangbusters

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɔf laɪk ˈɡæŋˌbʌstərz / (AmE), / teɪk ɒf laɪk ˈɡæŋˌbʌstəz / (BrE)
To become very successful or popular in a sudden, dramatic way.
become suddenly and greatly successful
informal american

Examples

Her online business took off like gangbusters after she landed her first big client.
The new smartphone took off like gangbusters, selling a million units in its first week.
Attendance at the art exhibition is taking off like gangbusters thanks to rave reviews.

Synset Examples

Their campaign took off like gangbusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
  • Antonym: flop - to fail completely

Etymology

From American slang "gangbusters" (meaning with great force or success), popularized by the radio show "Gang Busters" in the 1930s-40s.
