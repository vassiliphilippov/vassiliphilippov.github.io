to brush up

1. VERB

/ brʌʃ ʌp / (AmE), / brʌʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to improve or refresh one's knowledge of something already learned, especially by review or practice
refresh or improve knowledge

Examples

She decided to brush up on her Spanish before traveling to Mexico.
I need to brush up my math skills for the upcoming exam.
He spent the weekend brushing up on his presentation techniques.

Synset Examples

I reviewed the material before the test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revise - to go over previously studied material
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember; lose memory of something learned
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00698618-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brʌʃ ʌp / (AmE), / brʌʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to improve or refine something, especially a skill or knowledge that has become rusty
improve or refresh a skill/knowledge

Examples

She decided to brush up on her Spanish before her trip to Madrid.
The manager encouraged staff to brush up their customer service skills.
He needs to brush up his knowledge of company procedures for the upcoming audit.

Synset Examples

polish your social manners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - improve or refine something to a high standard
  • Synonym: practice - perform an activity repeatedly to improve skill
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00474730-v
Synset POS: v
