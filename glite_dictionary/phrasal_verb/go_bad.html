to go bad

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ bæd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bæd / (BrE)
to become unfit for consumption or use, especially as a result of spoilage or decay
become unfit for use or consumption

Examples

If you leave milk out of the fridge, it will go bad quickly.
The fruit had gone bad before we had a chance to eat it.
Despite the cold weather, some of the meat went bad during the long journey.

Synset Examples

the meat must be eaten before it spoils

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil - to become or make unfit for use or consumption, especially through decay
  • Synonym: rot - to decay or decompose, becoming unfit for consumption
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00210685-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ bæd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bæd / (BrE)
(of a machine or system) to stop operating or functioning; to break down or fail.
stop functioning or fail (machines, systems)

Examples

The dishwasher started to go bad and eventually stopped working altogether.
If your computer begins to go bad, you might lose important files.
After ten years of use, the motor finally went bad and had to be replaced.

Synset Examples

The engine finally went
The car died on the road
The bus we travelled in broke down on the way to town
The coffee maker broke
The engine failed on the way to town
her eyesight went after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break down - (of a machine) to stop working
  • Synonym: to give out - to stop functioning, especially gradually or after a period of use (machines, systems)
  • Synonym: malfunction - to fail to function properly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00435327-v
Synset POS: v
