to ramble on

1. VERB

/ ˈræm.bəl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈræm.bəl ɒn / (BrE)
to talk or write at length in a confused or aimless way, often about trivial matters
talk or write at length aimlessly

Examples

She tends to ramble on when she's nervous and loses track of the conversation.
The professor rambled on for hours, making it hard to follow the lecture.
Please try not to ramble on during your presentation; keep your points clear and brief.

Synset Examples

This novel rambles on and jogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go on - to continue speaking without stopping
  • Synonym: to digress - to leave the main subject temporarily in speech or writing

Etymology

From 'ramble' (to wander) + on (adverb indicating continuation of action).
