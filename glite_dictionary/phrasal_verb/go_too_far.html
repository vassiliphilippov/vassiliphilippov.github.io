to go too far

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tu ˈfɑr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tuː ˈfɑː / (BrE)
to exceed reasonable or acceptable limits in speech, action, or behavior
exceed acceptable limits

Examples

She tends to go too far with her jokes, sometimes offending people.
In his attempt to discipline the team, the coach went too far and was criticized by parents.
It's important to speak up, but don't go too far and become disrespectful.

Synset Examples

Don't go too far in your criticism of her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overstep the mark - to go beyond what is considered acceptable or appropriate
  • Synonym: to go overboard - to do something to an excessive degree
  • Antonym: to hold back - to restrain oneself from excessive action or words
