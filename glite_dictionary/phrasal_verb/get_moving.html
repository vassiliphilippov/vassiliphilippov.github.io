to get moving

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈmuːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈmuːvɪŋ / (BrE)
to begin to be active or start doing something; to hurry up or get going
begin to be active; hurry up
informal

Examples

We need to get moving if we want to catch the train on time.
Come on, let’s get moving or we’ll be late for the meeting.
She told everyone to get moving and finish their chores.

Synset Examples

Get cracking, please!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get going - to start moving or begin taking action
  • Synonym: to get cracking - to begin doing something quickly; to hurry up

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈmuːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈmuːvɪŋ / (BrE)
to begin to move or start taking action, especially with some urgency
begin to move or take action
informal

Examples

If we don't get moving now, we'll be late for the meeting.
She told everyone to get moving so they wouldn't miss the train.
Let's get moving before the weather gets worse.

Synset Examples

We should get moving if we want to catch the bus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get going - to start moving or begin an activity
