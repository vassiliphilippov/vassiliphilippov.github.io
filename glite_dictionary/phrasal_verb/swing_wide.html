to swing wide

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ waɪd / (AmE), / swɪŋ waɪd / (BrE)
to open (a door, gate, etc.) completely, especially by moving it on its hinges outward or inward
open fully, especially on hinges

Examples

She swung wide the heavy doors to welcome the guests into the hall.
With a loud creak, the old gate was swung wide to allow the cars to enter.
He swung wide the window to let in the cool evening breeze.

Synset Examples

He swung the barn door wide to let the sunlight in.
