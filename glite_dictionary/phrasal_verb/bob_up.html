to bob up

1. VERB

/ bɑb ʌp / (AmE), / bɒb ʌp / (BrE)
to appear or arise unexpectedly or suddenly; to originate or come into being, often unexpectedly.
arise or appear unexpectedly

Examples

Several doubts began to bob up as the meeting continued.
New issues tend to bob up whenever we think the problem is solved.
The memory would bob up unexpectedly during quiet moments.

Synset Examples

a question arose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arise - to emerge or become apparent
  • Synonym: to emerge - to appear or come into view

2. VERB

/ bɑb ʌp / (AmE), / bɒb ʌp / (BrE)
to appear or occur suddenly and unexpectedly, often briefly
appear suddenly or unexpectedly

Examples

New ideas often bob up when you are in the shower.
Just as we thought the problem was solved, another issue bobbed up.
Opportunities tend to bob up when you least expect them.

Synset Examples

New opportunities always seem to bob up when you least expect them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crop up - to appear or happen unexpectedly
  • Synonym: to emerge - to come into view or become apparent
  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
