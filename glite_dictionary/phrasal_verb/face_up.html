to face up

1. VERB

/ feɪs ʌp / (AmE), / feɪs ʌp / (BrE)
to deal with or accept an unpleasant or difficult situation directly and courageously
deal with something directly

Examples

It's time to face up to the reality of the situation.
She finally faced up to her mistakes and apologized.
We need to face up to the challenges ahead if we want to succeed.

Synset Examples

You must confront your problems
He faced the terrible consequences of his mistakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confront - to face something, especially unpleasant, directly
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or not deal with (something)
