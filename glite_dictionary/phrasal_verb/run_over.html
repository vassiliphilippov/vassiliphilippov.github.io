to run over

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / rʌn ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to overflow or spill beyond the limits or brim of a container.
overflow or spill over the edge

Examples

Be careful not to let the milk run over when you boil it.
The river ran over its banks after the heavy rain.
The soda was poured too quickly and began running over the glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to spill over the top or edge of a container
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02076087-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / rʌn ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to injure or kill someone or something by driving a vehicle over them
to drive over and injure or kill

Examples

The careless driver ran over the cat as he turned the corner.
Be careful crossing the road, or you could be run over by a car.
She was almost run over while chasing her dog into the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock down - to hit someone or something, causing them to fall
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00106714-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rʌn ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / rʌn ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to drive a vehicle over someone or something, usually causing injury or damage
drive a vehicle over someone or something

Examples

The driver didn't see the cat and accidentally ran over it.
Be careful not to run over the curb when parking the car.
He was almost run over as he crossed the busy street.

Synset Examples

The car almost ran over the dog in the street.
Please don't run over the flowers with the lawnmower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike someone or something with a vehicle
  • Antonym: miss - to avoid hitting someone or something with a vehicle
