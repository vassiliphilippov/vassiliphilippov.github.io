to scour out

1. VERB

/ ˈskaʊɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈskaʊə aʊt / (BrE)
to clean something thoroughly, especially by scrubbing or washing with effort
clean thoroughly by scrubbing

Examples

Before reusing the jar, make sure to scour out any residue inside.
She scoured out the old bathtub until it shone like new.
They always scour out the pans after each use to prevent buildup.

Synset Examples

It's important to scour out the dirt before painting.
You should scour out the oven for better results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub - to rub something hard to clean it
