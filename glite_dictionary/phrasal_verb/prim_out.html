to prim out

1. VERB

/ prɪm aʊt / (AmE), / prɪm aʊt / (BrE)
to dress in a prim, formal, or excessively proper way
to dress primly or formally

Examples

She likes to prim out in conservative suits for every business meeting.
Before the awards ceremony, he decided to prim out and wore a starched shirt and tie.
They always prime out for Sunday service, wearing their finest and most proper attire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or special clothes
