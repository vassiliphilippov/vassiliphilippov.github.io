to get hold of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt hoʊld ʌv / (AmE), / ɡɛt həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
to succeed in contacting or communicating with (someone)
contact or communicate with someone

Examples

I've been trying to get hold of you all afternoon, but your phone was off.
She finally managed to get hold of her doctor about the test results.
If you can't get hold of me, please leave a message.

Synset Examples

Our advertisements reach millions
He never contacted his children after he emigrated to Australia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact - to communicate with someone
  • Synonym: reach - to succeed in communicating with someone

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt hoʊld əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
(of a feeling, emotion, or condition) to take control of or strongly affect someone suddenly.
to suddenly affect or take control of someone

Examples

A fit of laughter got hold of her during the meeting.
Panic got hold of him as he realized the door was locked.
A sense of dread got hold of everyone as the storm approached.

Synset Examples

Fear seized the prisoners
The patient was seized with unbearable pains
He was seized with a dreadful disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take control of suddenly or forcibly (emotion or condition)
  • Synonym: to overcome - to be strongly affected by a feeling or emotion
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00739661-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt hoʊld ʌv / (AmE), / ɡɛt həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
to take or grasp something with one's hands; to physically obtain or take possession of something
physically take or grasp something

Examples

Let me get hold of that book so I can show it to the class.
He tried to get hold of the rope before it slipped away.
Can you get hold of the handle and pull the door open?

Synset Examples

Take a cookie!
Can you take this bag, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take - to get into one's possession; physically seize
  • Synonym: grab - to seize suddenly and roughly; to grasp
