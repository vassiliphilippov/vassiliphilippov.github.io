to foot up

1. VERB

/ fʊt ʌp / (AmE), / fʊt ʌp / (BrE)
to add up a column of numbers, especially in a financial or accounting context
add a column of numbers

Examples

Before submitting the report, please foot up all the expenses once more.
She spent the afternoon footing up the sales figures from last month.
After he footed up the column, he realized there was a calculation error.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add up - to calculate the total of several numbers
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00642800-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fʊt ʌp / (AmE), / fʊt ʌp / (BrE)
to add up numbers in a list or column to find the total amount
add up to find total

Examples

Please foot up the figures in this column before you file the report.
She spent hours footing up the receipts from last month.
After checking, he realized he had footed up the numbers incorrectly.

Synset Examples

Make sure to foot up all the receipts for reimbursement.
He needed to foot up the expenses before submitting the report.
They had to foot up the total costs for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sum up - to calculate the total of
  • Synonym: to calculate - to determine numerically, especially by addition

Etymology

From the noun 'foot' (bottom of a column) plus 'up', referring to calculating a sum at the foot of a list.
