to beat back

1. VERB

/ biːt bæk / (AmE), / biːt bæk / (BrE)
to force someone or something to move away or retreat, especially by using force or determined effort
force back or repel, often by force

Examples

The firefighters managed to beat back the flames before they reached the village.
The army beat back several attacks during the night.
She struggled to beat back her fears and continue with the presentation.

Synset Examples

repel the enemy
push back the urge to smoke
beat back the invaders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repel - to drive back, especially by force
  • Synonym: to push back - to force backward or resist the advance of something
