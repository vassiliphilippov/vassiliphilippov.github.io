to tote up

1. VERB

/ toʊt ʌp / (AmE), / təʊt ʌp / (BrE)
to calculate the total of several numbers or amounts by adding them together
add up a total; calculate the sum

Examples

Every month, I tote up my expenses to see where my money went.
The teacher asked the students to tote up their test scores for the semester.
We need to tote up the votes before announcing the winner.

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add up - to calculate the total of several numbers
  • Synonym: sum - to find the sum of numbers

Etymology

From 'tote', perhaps from West African *tota* (to pick up, carry), plus 'up' as an intensifier.
