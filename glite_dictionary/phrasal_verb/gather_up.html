to gather up

1. VERB

/ ˈɡæðər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə ʌp / (BrE)
to collect and lift (things) together, especially by picking them up with one's hands or arms
collect and lift together

Examples

She bent down to gather up the scattered papers from the floor.
Before leaving, he stopped to gather up his belongings.
The children rushed to gather up the autumn leaves into a big pile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to bring or gather things together
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01980103-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɡæðər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə ʌp / (BrE)
to collect or bring together into one group or place, especially items that are scattered or spread out
collect things into one group

Examples

After the picnic, we gathered up all the trash and packed it away.
The teacher asked the students to gather up their belongings before leaving the classroom.
She quickly gathered up her notes and rushed to the meeting.

Synset Examples

You can get the results on Monday
She picked up the children at the day care center
They pick up our trash twice a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to bring things together into one place or group
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or spread things apart
