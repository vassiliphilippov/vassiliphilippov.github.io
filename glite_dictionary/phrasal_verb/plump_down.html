to plump down

1. VERB

/ plʌmp daʊn / (AmE), / plʌmp daʊn / (BrE)
to set something or oneself down with a sudden, heavy, or noisy motion
set down heavily or noisily

Examples

She plumped down on the couch after a long day at work.
He plumped down his suitcase by the door with a sigh of relief.
The cat plumped down in the armchair and immediately fell asleep.

Synset Examples

He planked the money on the table
He planked himself into the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plonk down - set something or oneself down heavily or noisily
  • Synonym: drop - let or make (something) fall
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01503211-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ plʌmp daʊn / (AmE), / plʌmp daʊn / (BrE)
to sit or drop heavily or suddenly, often in a relaxed or careless way
sit or drop down heavily

Examples

After a long day, she plumped down on the sofa and sighed with relief.
He plumped down his bag in the hallway and went to get a drink.
Instead of standing in line, the children plumped down on the grass to wait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flop down - to sit or lie down heavily and suddenly

Synset POS: v
