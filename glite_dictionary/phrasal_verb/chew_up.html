to chew up

1. VERB

/ tʃu ʌp / (AmE), / tʃuː ʌp / (BrE)
to criticize or scold someone harshly; to censure severely or angrily
scold or criticize harshly

Examples

The coach chewed up the team after their poor performance.
If you arrive late again, the boss will definitely chew up you.
She was chewed up by her supervisor for missing the deadline.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chew out - criticize or scold someone harshly
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration

2. VERB

/ tʃu ʌp / (AmE), / tʃuː ʌp / (BrE)
to destroy or damage thoroughly, especially by biting, tearing, or harsh treatment
damage or destroy thoroughly

Examples

The dog completely chewed up my new shoes while I was out.
The old machine chewed up the paper, making it unreadable.
The movie company chewed up the novel's story and spit out something unrecognizable.

Synset Examples

He chewed up the documents thinking they were trash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • Synonym: ruin - to spoil or severely damage something
