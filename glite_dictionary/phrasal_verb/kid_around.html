to kid around

1. VERB

/ kɪd əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kɪd əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a playful or joking manner, not taking things seriously
joke or play in a light-hearted way
informal

Examples

The kids were kidding around in the backyard all afternoon.
He always likes to kid around with his colleagues to break the ice.
Sarah was kidding around when she made that silly face during dinner.

Synset Examples

They were just kidding around during the meeting.
Stop kidding around and get serious about your homework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - to say things to make people laugh, not serious

Etymology

From the informal verb 'kid' (to joke), which originated in the 19th century, likely from the noun 'kid' (child), suggesting playfulness.

2. VERB

/ kɪd əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kɪd əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to joke or behave in a playful or teasing way, especially without serious intent
joke or tease playfully
informal

Examples

My brother and I used to kid around for hours when we were children.
Please stop kidding around and focus on your homework.
They were just kidding around, so don't take their comments too seriously.

Synset Examples

I love to kid around with my friends on weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - say or do things intended to make people laugh
  • Synonym: tease - make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful way
