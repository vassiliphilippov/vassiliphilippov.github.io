to put aside

1. VERB

/ pʊt əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / pʊt əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to stop thinking about or dealing with something, often temporarily; to dismiss from one's mind or attention
stop considering or dealing with for a time

Examples

She decided to put aside her anger and listen calmly.
Let's put aside our differences and work together for a solution.
He tried to put aside his worries before the big meeting.

Synset Examples

it's time for you to put away childish things

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set aside - to reserve or leave undisturbed for a purpose
  • Synonym: to put away - to put out of sight; to discard or dismiss from the mind
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00780930-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊt əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / pʊt əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to move something to one side or stop using it, often temporarily
move something to one side or stop using

Examples

After she finished reading, she put aside the book and turned off the lamp.
Please put aside your phone while we eat dinner together.
He put aside his notes, ready to focus on the presentation.

Synset Examples

the children were told to put away their toys
the students put away their notebooks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put away - to place something in its proper position or stop using it
  • Synonym: to set aside - to reserve something for a particular purpose or stop using temporarily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01618895-v
Synset POS: v
