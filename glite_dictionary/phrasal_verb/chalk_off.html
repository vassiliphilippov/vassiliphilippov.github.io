to chalk off

1. VERB

/ tʃɑk ɔf / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ɒf / (BrE)
to mark something as completed or to remove it from a list, typically by drawing a line or making a note
mark as completed or remove from a list

Examples

After finishing the report, she chalked off another task from her list.
At the end of the day, I like to chalk off everything I accomplished.
They chalked off the completed assignments on the classroom board.

Synset Examples

Let's chalk off the completed projects before the meeting.
You need to chalk off those tasks from the list.
I managed to chalk off three items from my to-do list.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to check off - to mark items as completed on a list

Etymology

From 'chalk' (the writing material) + 'off', referring to the act of marking with chalk.
