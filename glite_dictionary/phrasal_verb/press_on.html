to press on

1. VERB

/ prɛs ɑn / (AmE), / prɛs ɒn / (BrE)
to continue moving forward or persist in an activity, especially despite difficulties or obstacles
continue moving forward; persist

Examples

Despite the rain and mud, they decided to press on with the hike.
She wanted to rest, but there was little time, so she pressed on.
Even though the path was unclear, he continued to press on toward his goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to continue in a course of action despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop making an effort; to quit

2. VERB

/ prɛs ɑn / (AmE), / prɛs ɒn / (BrE)
to continue doing something in a determined way, especially despite difficulties or obstacles
continue determinedly despite difficulties

Examples

Despite the setbacks, we decided to press on with the project until it was finished.
Even though the weather worsened, the hikers chose to press on up the mountain.
She told herself to press on when she felt like quitting the race.

Synset Examples
