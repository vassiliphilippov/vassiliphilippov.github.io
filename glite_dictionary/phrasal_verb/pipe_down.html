to pipe down

1. VERB

/ paɪp daʊn / (AmE), / paɪp daʊn / (BrE)
to become quiet or to tell someone to be quiet
become quiet; tell someone to be quiet

Examples

Could you please pipe down so I can concentrate on my work?
The teacher asked the class to pipe down before the test.
It took a while for the excited children to pipe down after the announcement.

Synset Examples

The audience fell silent when the speaker entered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be quiet - to stop making noise or talking
  • Synonym: shut up - to stop talking; to be quiet (informal)

Etymology

Originally nautical, referring to the use of a boatswain's pipe to signal silence on shipboard.

2. VERB

/ paɪp daʊn / (AmE), / paɪp daʊn / (BrE)
to become quiet or stop talking, especially after being told to do so
become quiet; stop talking
informal

Examples

Could you pipe down for a moment while I make this phone call?
When the movie started, the audience was asked to pipe down and enjoy the film.
He wouldn't pipe down even after being told to several times.

Synset Examples

The teacher asked the students to pipe down.
Can you pipe down so I can concentrate?
She finally told him to pipe down during the movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut up - to stop talking or be quiet, often abruptly
  • Antonym: to speak up - to talk more loudly or express an opinion openly

Etymology

Thought to originate from nautical slang, where 'pipe down' was an order given with a boatswain's pipe to signal sailors to be quiet and go below decks.
