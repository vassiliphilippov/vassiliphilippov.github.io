to put up to

1. VERB

/ pʊt ʌp tu / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp tə / (BrE)
to encourage or persuade someone to do something, especially something they might not do otherwise
persuade or encourage to do something

Examples

His brother put up to playing the prank even though he was hesitant at first.
She wouldn't have complained if someone hadn't put up to it.
I was put up to asking that question by my classmates.

Synset Examples

He was put up to the challenge by his friends.
He was put up to the task by his boss.
She felt put up to perform after their encouragement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persuade - to cause someone to do something through reasoning or argument
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
