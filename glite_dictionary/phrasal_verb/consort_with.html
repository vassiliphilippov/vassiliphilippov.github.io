to consort with

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɔrt wɪð / (AmE), / kənˈsɔːt wɪð / (BrE)
to associate or keep company with someone, especially with a particular group, often implying disapproval or distinction
associate or keep company with

Examples

Rumors spread that the politician would consort with questionable characters to gain influence.
During her travels, she preferred to consort with scholars and intellectuals.
Some parents disapprove if their children consort with bad company.

Synset Examples

She decided to consort with the local artists for inspiration.
The queen will consort with her advisors before making decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate with - to spend time with someone socially or professionally
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'consors' (partner, associate) via Old French and Middle English.
