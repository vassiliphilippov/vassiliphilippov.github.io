to show off

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ ˈɔf / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ˈɒf / (BrE)
to display something or behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration, often boastfully or ostentatiously
display or behave to attract attention

Examples

She likes to show off her painting skills at family gatherings.
Stop trying to show off; it's getting embarrassing for everyone.
He couldn't resist showing off his new watch to his coworkers.

Synset Examples

he showed off his new sports car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flaunt - to display (something) ostentatiously, especially in order to provoke envy or admiration
  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: hide - to keep out of sight; conceal from view or attention
