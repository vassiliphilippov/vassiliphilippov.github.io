to keep up with the times

1. VERB

/ kip ʌp wɪð ðə taɪmz / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp wɪð ðə taɪmz / (BrE)
to stay current or modern by adapting to new trends, ideas, or developments
stay current with new trends

Examples

Many businesses have to keep up with the times to remain competitive in today's market.
If you want your music to stay relevant, you need to keep up with the times.
My grandfather refuses to use a smartphone because he doesn't want to keep up with the times.

Synset Examples

The old store failed to keep up with the times.
It's essential to keep up with the times in business.
She redesigned her website to keep up with the times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modernize - to make something more modern or up to date
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to become less current or up to date than others
