to fall down

1. VERB

/ fɔl daʊn / (AmE), / fɔːl daʊn / (BrE)
to suddenly lose an upright position and move downwards to the ground or a lower level
lose an upright position and drop

Examples

Be careful not to fall down the stairs when it’s wet.
The old barn is starting to fall down after years of neglect.
She tripped on the curb and fell down in the street.

Synset Examples

The vase fell over and the water spilled onto the table
Her hair fell across her forehead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - suddenly fall or give way
  • Synonym: to topple - overbalance and become unsteady and fall down
  • Antonym: to stand up - to rise to a standing position
