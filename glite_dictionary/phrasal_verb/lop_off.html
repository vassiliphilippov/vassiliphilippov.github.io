to lop off

1. VERB

/ lɑp ɔf / (AmE), / lɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something from a larger whole by cutting, especially with a single quick motion
remove by cutting

Examples

The gardener decided to lop off the low-hanging branches from the tree.
He accidentally lopped off the tip of his finger while slicing vegetables.
The company plans to lop off several departments to reduce costs.

Synset Examples

cut off the ear
lop off the dead branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - to separate or remove by cutting
