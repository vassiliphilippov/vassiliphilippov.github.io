to storm off

1. VERB

/ stɔrm ˈɔf / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and angrily, often as a reaction to something upsetting or offensive
leave angrily and abruptly

Examples

After the heated discussion, Jake decided to storm off without saying another word.
She didn't want to storm off in anger, but the comment hurt her deeply.
Whenever things don't go his way, he tends to storm off instead of talking it out.

Synset Examples

He decided to storm off after the argument.
She couldn't believe he would just storm off.
Don't storm off; let's talk about this.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to walk out - to leave a place or situation suddenly, especially because you are angry or dissatisfied
  • Synonym: march off - to leave somewhere purposefully, often in an angry or determined way
  • Antonym: calmly leave - to leave in a quiet, composed manner
