to slough off

1. VERB

/ slʌf ɑf / (AmE), / slʌf ɒf / (BrE)
to get rid of or discard something that is no longer wanted or useful, especially something considered undesirable
discard or get rid of

Examples

After years of bad habits, he decided to slough off his unhealthy lifestyle.
The company plans to slough off its underperforming branches next year.
She tried to slough off all feelings of guilt as she started a new chapter in her life.

Synset Examples

the candidate sloughed off his former campaign workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to cast off or let fall (something)
  • Synonym: discard - to throw away or get rid of (something)

Etymology

From Middle English 'slough' (shed skin), related to Old English 'slōh'; the phrasal verb developed by reference to snakes shedding their skin.

2. VERB

/ slʌf ɑf / (AmE), / slʌf ɒf / (BrE)
(of tissue) to separate and fall away from the surrounding living tissue, especially as part of a natural or pathological process.
tissue separates and falls away
medical

Examples

The damaged skin will eventually slough off as part of the healing process.
During recovery, the infected tissue may slough off and expose healthy layers beneath.
After several days, the wound began to slough off the necrotic tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to let something fall off naturally or discard it
