to queer (one's) perspective

1. VERB

/ kwɪr maɪ pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / kwɪə maɪ pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
to challenge, disrupt, or reconsider one's usual way of viewing or understanding something, often from a non-normative or critical standpoint
challenge or disrupt one's viewpoint

Examples

Reading queer theory helped me queer my perspective on gender and identity.
Traveling to new countries always queers my perspective on culture and tradition.
Workshops on social justice can really queer your perspective and encourage critical thinking.

Synset Examples

I love to queer my perspective on life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge (one's) assumptions - to question preconceived ideas or beliefs
  • Antonym: confirm (one's) perspective - to reinforce or strengthen one's existing viewpoint

Etymology

From 'queer' (verb), early 20th century, meaning to challenge norms; 'perspective' meaning viewpoint.
