to whisk off

1. VERB

/ wɪsk ɑf / (AmE), / wɪsk ɒf / (BrE)
to brush or wipe something off quickly and lightly
brush or wipe off lightly

Examples

She used her hand to whisk off the crumbs from the table.
He quickly whisked off the dust from his jacket before the meeting.
The waitress whisked off the spilled sugar with a napkin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brush off - remove something by brushing

Etymology

From 'whisk' (to move quickly with a sweeping motion) + 'off'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01437887-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɪsk ɔf / (AmE), / wɪsk ɒf / (BrE)
to take someone or something away quickly and suddenly
take away quickly and suddenly

Examples

The waiter whisked off our plates before we had even finished eating.
She was whisked off to hospital after the accident.
Security whisked off the celebrity as soon as the crowd grew too large.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snatch away - to take something away quickly and unexpectedly
  • Antonym: to drag out - to take someone or something away forcefully and usually slowly
