to fathom why

1. VERB

/ ˈfæðəm waɪ / (AmE), / ˈfaðəm waɪ / (BrE)
to understand the reason for something, especially when it seems puzzling or difficult
understand the reason for something

Examples

I just can't fathom why anyone would want to leave such a beautiful place.
She tried to fathom why the project had failed despite their best efforts.
It's hard to fathom why he reacted so angrily to a simple question.

Synset Examples

I can't fathom why he would quit his job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to figure out - to understand or solve something
  • Synonym: to comprehend - to grasp mentally; understand

Etymology

From 'fathom', originally meaning to measure depth (Old English 'fæðm' - outstretched arms) + 'why'.
