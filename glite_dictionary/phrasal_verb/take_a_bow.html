to take a bow

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə baʊ / (AmE), / teɪk ə baʊ / (BrE)
to acknowledge applause, praise, or recognition by bowing or otherwise showing appreciation
acknowledge praise or recognition

Examples

After the performance ended, the dancers took a bow to thank the applauding audience.
She deserves to take a bow for her dedication to the project.
The chef took a bow when the crowd cheered for his incredible dessert.

Synset Examples

They finally took a bow for what they did

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accept credit - to receive recognition or praise for something you have done
  • Synonym: receive applause - to be applauded or given praise by an audience

2. VERB

/ teɪk ə baʊ / (AmE), / teɪk ə baʊ / (BrE)
To acknowledge applause or appreciation, typically by bending forward at the waist after a performance.
acknowledge applause after a performance

Examples

After the final note, the singer took a bow to thank the enthusiastic audience.
The actors took a bow together at the end of the play.
She smiled and took a bow after her winning performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - the act of bending forward to show respect or acknowledgment
