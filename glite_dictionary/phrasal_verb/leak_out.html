to leak out

1. VERB

/ lik aʊt / (AmE), / liːk aʊt / (BrE)
(of secret or confidential information) to become known to the public, especially unintentionally or without official approval.
become known (of secret information)

Examples

The details of the agreement eventually leaked out to the press.
Rumors about the new project leaked out before the official announcement.
Despite tight security, the confidential information somehow leaked out.

Synset Examples

The news leaked out despite his secrecy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leak - to allow (a substance or information) to escape

2. VERB

/ lik aʊt / (AmE), / liːk aʊt / (BrE)
(of secret or confidential information) to become known to the public or revealed unintentionally.
(of information) become known

Examples

Information tends to leak out before official announcements.
If we aren't careful, the results might leak out ahead of time.
Rumors about the merger started to leak out late last week.

Synset Examples

Information tends to leak out before official announcements.
The news will leak out if we're not careful.
I hope my secret doesn't leak out before the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get out - (of information) to become known to the public
  • Synonym: leak - allow (information) to become known unlawfully or accidentally
