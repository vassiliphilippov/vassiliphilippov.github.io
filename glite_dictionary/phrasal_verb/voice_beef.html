to voice beef

1. VERB

/ vɔɪs bif / (AmE), / vɔɪs biːf / (BrE)
to express a complaint or dissatisfaction, especially in an informal way
express a complaint
informal slang

Examples

It's okay to voice beef if you believe the rules are unfair.
Several employees voiced beef about the lack of parking spaces at work.
If you have a problem, don't hesitate to voice beef at the meeting.

Synset Examples

She voiced her beef about the new policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • Synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a bad-tempered way
