to advocate for

1. VERB

/ ˈæd.vəˌkeɪt fɔr / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.keɪt fə / (BrE)
to publicly support or argue in favor of a particular cause, policy, or idea
publicly support a cause or policy

Examples

She has always advocated for equal rights in the workplace.
Many organizations advocate for cleaner energy solutions to combat climate change.
He tirelessly advocated for the needs of underprivileged children in his community.

Synset Examples

Her deafness motivated her to advocate for accessibility.
He advocated for legalizing euthanasia in his state.
She identified as dilawan, advocating for democratic reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or approval to something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or resist something

Etymology

From Latin 'advocare' (to call to one’s aid), with 'for' indicating the supported cause.
