to dob in

1. VERB

/ dɑb ɪn / (AmE), / dɒb ɪn / (BrE)
to inform on someone or report their wrongdoing, especially to an authority
inform on someone, report wrongdoing
informal british

Examples

He threatened to dob in anyone who broke the rules.
She didn't want to dob in her classmates, even though she knew what they had done.
Some people think it's wrong to dob in a friend to the police.

Synset Examples

If you see something wrong, you should dob the person in.
You can't dob on your friends; it’s not right.
He decided to dob on his colleagues after the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitch - to secretly inform on someone; slang for betraying a confidence
  • Synonym: grass up - British slang, to inform the authorities on someone
  • Antonym: to cover for - to protect someone by withholding the truth from authorities

Etymology

Originated in Australian slang, possibly from British dialect 'dob' (to give or share) in the early 20th century.
