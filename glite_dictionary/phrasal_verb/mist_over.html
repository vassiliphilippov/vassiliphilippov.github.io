to mist over

1. VERB

/ mɪst ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / mɪst ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially glass) to become covered with a thin layer of moisture or mist, making it difficult to see through.
become covered with mist or condensation

Examples

The car windows quickly misted over on the cold morning.
Her eyes misted over with tears as she listened to the speech.
The mirror misted over after a long, hot shower.

Synset Examples

The windshield misted over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fog up - to become covered with fog or condensation, reducing visibility

2. VERB

/ mɪst ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / mɪst ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of eyes) to fill with tears, making it difficult to see clearly, typically due to emotion.
(of eyes) to fill with tears from emotion

Examples

Her eyes misted over when she thought of her childhood home.
The old man’s gaze misted over as he recalled his late wife.
I watched as my friend’s eyes misted over during the heartfelt reunion.

Synset Examples

Her eyes misted over with tears during the emotional speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tear up - to fill with tears, especially from emotion

3. VERB

/ mɪst ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / mɪst ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a surface) to become covered with a fine layer of moisture, making it difficult to see through.
become covered with moisture or mist

Examples

The car windows began to mist over in the early morning chill.
As she breathed on the mirror, it started to mist over.
The glass misted over quickly when we brought it out of the cold.

Synset Examples

The windows began to mist over as the temperature dropped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fog up - to become or cause to become covered with fog or steam
