to wait on

1. VERB

/ weɪt ɑn / (AmE), / weɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to serve or act as an attendant for someone, especially by bringing food or drinks to their table
act as an attendant or server

Examples

The friendly waiter will wait on your table shortly.
She used to wait on wealthy families during summer holidays.
He has waited on celebrities at that exclusive restaurant.

Synset Examples

May I serve you?
She attends the old lady in the wheelchair
Can you wait on our table, please?
Is a salesperson assisting you?
The minister served the King for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - to bring food or drink to someone or act as a helper
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02546367-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ weɪt ɑn / (AmE), / weɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to delay taking action until something happens or until someone gives a response or decision
defer action until response or result

Examples

We're going to wait on the official report before making a statement.
She decided to wait on his approval before submitting the application.
You shouldn't wait on others to chase your dreams.

Synset Examples

He'll wait on the results from the interview.
Can you wait on me to finish my work?
I will wait on your decision before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wait for - to remain until something expected happens
Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
