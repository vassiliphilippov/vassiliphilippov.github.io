to sop up

1. VERB

/ sɑp ʌp / (AmE), / sɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to absorb or take in a liquid or, figuratively, information, feelings, etc.
absorb or take in fully

Examples

She used a towel to sop up the spilled juice on the floor.
The sponge quickly sopped up all the water from the counter.
He tried to sop up as much information as possible during his internship.

Synset Examples

The sponge absorbs water well
She drew strength from the minister's words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (liquid, energy, or information)
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ sɑp ʌp / (AmE), / sɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to absorb or remove (a liquid) using a soft or absorbent material, such as a sponge, cloth, or bread
absorb or soak up a liquid

Examples

She used a towel to sop up the spilled water from the floor.
He broke off some bread to sop up the delicious sauce on his plate.
The paper towels quickly sopped up the juice before it could stain the carpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in a liquid or substance
