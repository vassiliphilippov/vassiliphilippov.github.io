to drop anchor

1. VERB

/ drɑp ˈæŋkər / (AmE), / drɒp ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
to lower an anchor from a ship or boat in order to keep it in a particular place
secure a vessel by lowering its anchor

Examples

We decided to drop anchor in the sheltered bay for the night.
The ship dropped anchor just outside the busy harbor as the sun was setting.
Before the storm hit, the captain quickly dropped anchor to secure the vessel.

Synset Examples

We anchored at Baltimore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchor - to secure a vessel in place using an anchor

2. VERB

/ drɑp ˈæŋkər / (AmE), / drɒp ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
to bring a boat or ship to a stop by lowering its anchor; to settle or come to rest in a particular place
lower an anchor to stop a boat or settle somewhere

Examples

Just before sunset, the captain decided to drop anchor in the sheltered bay.
Let's drop anchor here for the night and get some rest.
When the weather worsened, they quickly dropped anchor to avoid drifting.

Synset Examples

It's time to drop anchor and enjoy the view.
They decided to drop anchor as the storm approached.
The crew will drop anchor near the island.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to weigh anchor - to lift an anchor so that a ship can start moving again
