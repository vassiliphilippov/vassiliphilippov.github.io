to come away

1. VERB

/ kʌm əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌm əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to become detached or separated from something
become detached

Examples

The paint started to come away from the walls after the flooding.
Be careful when you pull that tape, or it might come away in your hands.
Over time, the sticker will come away if the surface isn't cleaned first.

Synset Examples

His retina detached and he had to be rushed into surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall off - to become detached by falling from a surface
  • Antonym: stick - to remain attached or adhere
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01302344-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌm əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation in a particular state or condition, often as a result of an experience
leave in a certain condition

Examples

After the meeting, she came away feeling hopeful about the company's future.
Many tourists come away with a new appreciation for the local culture.
He came away from the experience with valuable lessons learned.

Synset Examples

She came away angry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or situation
