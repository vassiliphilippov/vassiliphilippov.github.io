to flame up

1. VERB

/ fleɪm ʌp / (AmE), / fleɪm ʌp / (BrE)
(of something) to become ignited or burst into flames suddenly.
suddenly become ignited

Examples

The dry wood in the fireplace flamed up quickly when the match touched it.
If the gas is left on, it can suddenly flame up when exposed to a spark.
The old campfire unexpectedly flamed up again during the windy night.

Synset Examples

The furnace wouldn't fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - catch fire or cause to catch fire
  • Antonym: to go out - (of a fire) to stop burning; to cease burning

2. VERB

/ fleɪm ʌp / (AmE), / fleɪm ʌp / (BrE)
to suddenly burn or shine with a bright, intense flame or light
to suddenly burn or shine brightly

Examples

The campfire began to flame up as we added more wood.
As the oil was poured onto the grill, the flames flamed up dangerously high.
During the ceremony, each torch would flame up one after another.

Synset Examples

Every star seemed to flare with new intensity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flare up - to suddenly burn or become bright or intense

3. VERB

/ fleɪm ʌp / (AmE), / fleɪm ʌp / (BrE)
to suddenly catch fire or become more intense, either literally or figuratively
suddenly ignite or intensify

Examples

The campfire began to flame up as more wood was added.
Be careful when you pour in the oil; it might flame up unexpectedly.
Tensions in the meeting began to flame up after the controversial statement.

Synset Examples

The campfire began to flame up as more wood was added.
