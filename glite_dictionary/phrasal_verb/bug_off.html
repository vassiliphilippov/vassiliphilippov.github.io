to bug off

1. VERB

/ bʌɡ ɑf / (AmE), / bʌɡ ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) to go away or leave someone alone, often used as an imperative to tell someone to stop bothering you.
(informal) go away or leave someone alone
informal

Examples

If you’re not going to help, just bug off and let me work.
She told her little brother to bug off when he kept pestering her.
I wish those telemarketers would bug off and stop calling every evening.

Synset Examples

When I’m busy, I just want everyone to bug off.
He told the annoying salesman to bug off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to buzz off - go away or leave someone alone (informal)
