to string out

1. VERB

/ strɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / strɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or stretch something, or a series of things, in a line or over a distance; to space things out along a line or path
arrange in a line or series

Examples

A line of hikers was strung out along the narrow mountain path.
The lights were strung out across the fence, creating a festive look for the party.
The runner's belongings were strung out from the starting point all the way to the finish line.

Synset Examples

the houses were strung out in a long row

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spread out - to distribute things so that they cover a larger area or distance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01363418-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ strɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / strɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to make something continue or last longer than necessary, often unnecessarily or deliberately.
prolong or extend unnecessarily

Examples

The lawyer tried to string out the proceedings to benefit his client.
She managed to string out her vacation by working remotely.
Don't string out the negotiations or we might lose the deal.

Synset Examples

He managed to string out his answers during the interview.
Don't string out this project; we need it done quickly.
They tried to string out the meeting as long as possible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drag out - to prolong something unnecessarily
  • Synonym: to prolong - to make something last longer
  • Antonym: to shorten - to make something shorter in duration
Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
