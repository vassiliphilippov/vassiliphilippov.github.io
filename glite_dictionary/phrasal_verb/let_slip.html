to let slip

1. VERB

/ lɛt slɪp / (AmE), / lɛt slɪp / (BrE)
to reveal something, especially information or a secret, unintentionally or without meaning to
unintentionally reveal something

Examples

He accidentally let slip the details of her surprise party.
During the interview, she let slip that a new product was coming soon.
I didn't mean to let slip the secret; it just happened.

Synset Examples

She let it slip that they were moving next month.
She let slip that they were planning a vacation.
I accidentally let slip my surprise for her birthday.
I didn’t mean to let that slip during dinner.
He let slip the news about the promotion.
He let the secret slip while chatting with friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blurt out - to say something suddenly and without thinking, especially information
  • Synonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret information unintentionally or prematurely
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to not say something or not reveal information
