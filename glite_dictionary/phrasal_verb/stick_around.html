to stick around

1. VERB

/ stɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / stɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to stay in a place and not leave; to remain nearby or present for a period of time
remain in a place; not leave

Examples

If you stick around after the show, you can meet the performers.
I'm not sure what's happening next, but I'll stick around to find out.
Many students stuck around after class to ask the professor questions.

Synset Examples

We are staying in Detroit; we are not moving to Cincinnati
Stay put in the corner here!
Stick around and you will learn something!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to continue to be in a particular place
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place

2. VERB

/ stɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / stɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to stay in a place, waiting or remaining nearby, especially for a period of time or until something happens
stay or remain nearby
informal

Examples

If you stick around for a few more minutes, the manager will see you.
She decided to stick around after the show to meet the actors.
I can't stick around much longer because I have another appointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang around - to remain idly in or near a place
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ stɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / stɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to remain in a place for a period of time, usually waiting for something to happen
remain in a place, wait for something
informal

Examples

Why don't you stick around for a while and have some coffee with us?
She decided to stick around after the concert in case there was an encore.
They stuck around even though the event was running late.

Synset Examples

Make sure to stick around for the surprise announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to remain in a place; not leave
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place
