to get past

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt pæst / (AmE), / ɡɛt pɑːst / (BrE)
To succeed in overcoming or moving beyond an obstacle, problem, or negative feeling.
overcome or move beyond something

Examples

It's never easy to get past a major disappointment, but support from friends helps a lot.
He struggled for months to get past the loss of his job, but eventually found a new opportunity.
Once you get past the initial fear, public speaking becomes much more manageable.

Synset Examples

We need to get past these difficulties together.
She managed to get past her anxiety during the interview.
It’s hard to get past the mistakes we made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in controlling or defeating a difficulty or feeling
  • Synonym: to move on - to stop focusing on something upsetting or difficult and continue with life
