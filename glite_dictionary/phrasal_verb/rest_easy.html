to rest easy

1. VERB

/ rɛst ˈiːzi / (AmE), / rɛst ˈiːzi / (BrE)
to feel calm, relieved, or free from worry, especially after being reassured about something
to feel relieved or reassured

Examples

You can rest easy knowing your valuables are safe in the hotel room.
After receiving the test results, she could finally rest easy.
The manager told his staff to rest easy because their jobs were secure.

Synset Examples

You can rest easy knowing the project is on track.
He can finally rest easy about his exam results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worry - to feel anxious or troubled about actual or potential problems
  • Synonym: to relax - to become less tense, anxious, or stressed
