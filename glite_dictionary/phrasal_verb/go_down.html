to go down

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
to be defeated, especially in a competition or conflict
be defeated (phrasal verb)

Examples

Despite their efforts, the team went down in the final match.
It seemed the company would go down without a fight.
If the defending champion goes down tomorrow, it will be a big upset.

Synset Examples

If America goes down, the free world will go down, too

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose - to fail to win; to be defeated
  • Antonym: win - to be victorious; to defeat others in a contest or battle
