to bear down on

1. VERB

/ bɛr daʊn ɑn / (AmE), / beə daʊn ɒn / (BrE)
(of a ship) to sail or move towards another vessel, often with intent or purpose.
approach another vessel (nautical)

Examples

The pirate ship began to bear down on the merchant vessel at sunrise.
As the storm intensified, we noticed a large freighter bearing down on us from the north.
The captain gave the command to bear down on the enemy craft in the distance.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɛr daʊn ɔn / (AmE), / beə daʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to press heavily or exert strong force upon something, often causing strain or pressure
exert strong pressure or force

Examples

Heavy winds began to bear down on the small fishing village, causing widespread damage.
The weight of the bookshelves started to bear down on the old wooden floor.
As the storm intensified, snow continued to bear down on the mountain lodge.

Synset Examples

The snow bore down on the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to press down on - to apply pressure or weight onto something
