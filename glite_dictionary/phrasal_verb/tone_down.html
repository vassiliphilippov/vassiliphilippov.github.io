to tone down

1. VERB

/ toʊn daʊn / (AmE), / təʊn daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the intensity or force of a sound, especially by muffling or wrapping
reduce the intensity of a sound

Examples

She used thick curtains to tone down the noise from outside.
You should tone down your voice when speaking in the library.
They toned down the music by placing a blanket over the speakers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften - make something less loud or harsh
  • Antonym: to amplify - increase the strength or intensity of sound
