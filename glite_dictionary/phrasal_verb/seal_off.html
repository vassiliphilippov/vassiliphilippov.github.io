to seal off

1. VERB

/ siːl ɔf / (AmE), / siːl ɒf / (BrE)
to prevent people or things from entering or leaving an area, especially by using barriers or official controls
block access to an area

Examples

The police decided to seal off the area while they investigated the suspicious package.
After the chemical spill, workers quickly sealed off the factory to protect the public.
Authorities sealed off several streets, making it impossible for traffic to pass through.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockade - to prevent people or goods from entering or leaving a place, especially during a conflict

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sil ɔf / (AmE), / siːl ɒf / (BrE)
to make something tightly closed or secure against leakage, entry, or escape
make something airtight or leakproof

Examples

The workers sealed off the leaking pipe to prevent further damage.
Authorities decided to seal off the contaminated area for safety.
After the renovation, they sealed off the basement windows to keep out the moisture.

Synset Examples

seal the windows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to close off - to block or shut off to passage
  • Antonym: open - to make something not closed or sealed
