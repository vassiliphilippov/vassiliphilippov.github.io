to frame up

1. VERB

/ freɪm ʌp / (AmE), / freɪm ʌp / (BrE)
to construct something by fitting or joining parts together
construct by joining parts

Examples

The workers will frame up the new house before the roof is added.
It took them all morning to frame up the wooden structure for the shed.
Once you've framed up the project, you can start working on the details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to fit things together to make something
  • Antonym: to disassemble - to take apart something that was constructed

Etymology

From the verb 'frame' (to construct) + 'up'.
