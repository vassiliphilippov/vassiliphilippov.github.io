to hollow out

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove the inside of something, leaving an empty space within
remove the inside of something

Examples

The workers will hollow out the old log to make a canoe.
Pumpkin carvers hollow out the insides before adding a face.
Decades of erosion began to hollow out the mountain cave.

Synset Examples

hollow out a tree trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to dig out or remove material from the inside
