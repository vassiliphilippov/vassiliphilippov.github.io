to brace oneself for

1. VERB

/ breɪs ˈwʌnˌsɛlf fɔr / (AmE), / breɪs ˈwʌnˌsɛlf fə / (BrE)
to prepare mentally or emotionally for something difficult, unpleasant, or challenging that is expected to happen
prepare mentally for something unpleasant

Examples

She tried to brace herself for the news she was about to hear.
Everyone in the company had to brace themselves for impending layoffs.
He closed his eyes and braced himself for the impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to steel oneself for - prepare mentally for something difficult
  • Synonym: get ready for - prepare for something to happen

Etymology

From 'brace' (to prepare, make firm) + reflexive 'oneself', used from the 19th century.
