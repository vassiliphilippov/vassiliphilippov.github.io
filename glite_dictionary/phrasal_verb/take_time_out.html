to take time out

1. VERB

/ teɪk taɪm aʊt / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm aʊt / (BrE)
to stop what you are doing in order to rest or do something different for a short period
pause activity to rest or do something else

Examples

She decided to take time out from her studies to travel abroad for a year.
It's important to take time out from work to recharge your energy.
Many athletes take time out after a busy season to rest and recover.

Synset Examples

Let's take time out for a quick break.
You should take time out to relax this weekend.
They will take time out to discuss the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a break - to stop working or doing an activity for a short period to rest
