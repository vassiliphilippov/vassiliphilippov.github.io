to spark a debate

1. VERB

/ spɑrk ə dɪˈbeɪt / (AmE), / spɑːk ə dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to cause a discussion or argument to begin, especially one that is lively or intense
cause a discussion to start

Examples

The announcement of new regulations sparked a debate among industry leaders and consumers alike.
Her controversial opinion in the meeting sparked a debate that lasted for hours.
The documentary sparked a debate on social media about environmental responsibility.

Synset Examples

That libtard comment really sparked a debate.
The Tory government's new policy has sparked debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignite a debate - to start a debate
  • Antonym: quell a debate - to suppress or stop a debate
