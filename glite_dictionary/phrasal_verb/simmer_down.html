to simmer down

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɪmə daʊn / (BrE)
to become calm or less agitated after being excited, angry, or upset
to become calm after agitation

Examples

Once the children simmered down, we were able to continue the lesson.
She needed a few minutes alone to simmer down after the argument.
It took the crowd a while to simmer down after the announcement.

Synset Examples

After the fight both men need to cool off.
It took a while after the baby was born for things to settle down again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become less angry, upset, or excited

Etymology

Figurative use of 'simmer,' meaning to cook gently below boiling, extended to emotions since the mid-19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɪmə daʊn / (BrE)
to become calm or less agitated after being excited, angry, or upset, or to tell someone to do so
become calmer; calm down
informal

Examples

After the argument, everyone needed a few minutes to simmer down.
Can you please simmer down and listen to what I'm saying?
It took her a while to simmer down after hearing the news.

Synset Examples

She told him to simmer down and take a deep breath.
It's time to simmer down and think rationally.
You need to simmer down before you say something you'll regret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become or make someone calmer
  • Antonym: to flare up - to become suddenly angry or more agitated

Etymology

Figurative use of 'simmer' (to cook gently just below boiling) to suggest reducing emotional heat.
