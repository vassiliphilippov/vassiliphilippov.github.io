to shut down

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt daʊn / (AmE), / ʃʌt daʊn / (BrE)
to stop operating or cause something to stop operating, especially permanently or temporarily; to close a business, machine, or system
stop operating (business, machine, etc.)

Examples

The company decided to shut down its overseas factories due to rising costs.
Before you leave the office, please shut down your computer properly.
They had to shut down production temporarily after the accident.

Synset Examples

The owners decided to move and to close the factory
My business closes every night at 8 P.M.
close up the shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to close down - to stop operating (a business, shop, etc.) permanently

2. VERB

/ ʃʌt daʊn / (AmE), / ʃʌt daʊn / (BrE)
to stop the operation or functioning of something, typically a machine, business, or system, often by turning it off or ceasing activity
stop or terminate operation

Examples

The factory will shut down next week for maintenance.
During the storm, authorities decided to shut down the airport.
She had to shut down her computer because it was overheating.

Synset Examples

We need to shut down the project for now.
The company decided to shut down operations temporarily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close - to bring an activity or operation to an end
  • Antonym: to start up - to begin operation, especially of a machine, company, or system
