to pop off

1. VERB

/ pɑp ɑf / (AmE), / pɒp ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) To leave a place quickly or suddenly, often without notice.
leave quickly; go away suddenly
informal

Examples

She had to pop off before the meeting ended to catch her train.
I'm just going to pop off home for lunch and come back later.
Without saying goodbye, he decided to pop off as soon as the concert finished.

2. VERB

/ pɑp ɑf / (AmE), / pɒp ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) To die, usually used humorously or euphemistically.
(informal) die; pass away
informal

Examples

Everyone was shocked when the main character suddenly popped off halfway through the movie.
My old computer finally popped off after years of faithful service.
He joked that he wanted to travel the world before he popped off.

Synset Examples

informal
