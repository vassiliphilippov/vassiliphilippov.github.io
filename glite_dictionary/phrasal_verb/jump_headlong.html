to jump headlong

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ˈhɛd.lɒŋ / (BrE)
To begin or undertake something quickly and without careful thought, especially in a reckless or impulsive manner.
begin something impulsively or recklessly

Examples

He jumped headlong into the new job without reading the contract carefully.
Instead of planning, she jumped headlong into organizing the event on a whim.
Many young entrepreneurs jump headlong into business ventures with little experience.

Synset Examples

He jumped headlong into the new project without thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunge (into) - to enter suddenly or enthusiastically into an activity or situation
  • Synonym: wade in - to start doing something in a forceful and enthusiastic way
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause before doing something, usually because you are uncertain
