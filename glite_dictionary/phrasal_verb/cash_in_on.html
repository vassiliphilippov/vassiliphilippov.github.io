to cash in on

1. VERB

/ kæʃ ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to take advantage of a situation for personal gain, often financial
take advantage for gain

Examples

Several companies tried to cash in on the growing popularity of electric cars.
He wrote a book to cash in on his sudden fame.
Scammers often cash in on people's fears during a crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalize on - to use a situation to gain advantage

Etymology

From "cash in" meaning to convert to money, plus "on" indicating the source or opportunity exploited.

2. VERB

/ kæʃ ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to profit from or take advantage of a situation or opportunity, often in a way that is considered opportunistic
profit from or exploit an opportunity

Examples

The company tried to cash in on the trend by launching a new product line.
Some people seek to cash in on others' misfortune by selling overpriced goods after disasters.
The author managed to cash in on the popularity of the series with a best-selling sequel.

Synset Examples

He decided to cash in on his investment.
Investors were eager to cash-in on the successful launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalize on - to take advantage of or make the most of an opportunity

Etymology

From 'cash in' (to exchange something for money) + 'on', implying gaining profit from an opportunity.
