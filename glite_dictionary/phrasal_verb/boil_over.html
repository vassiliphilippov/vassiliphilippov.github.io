to boil over

1. VERB

/ bɔɪl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / bɔɪl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to overflow or cause to overflow the container while boiling.
overflow while boiling

Examples

Be careful or the soup will boil over and make a mess on the stove.
If you leave the kettle unattended, it might boil over.
The pot boiled over and the water spilled onto the counter.

Synset Examples

The milk is boiling over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to flow over the brim or edge
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00375939-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɔɪl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / bɔɪl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to suddenly lose self-control and express strong emotion, especially anger
suddenly lose emotional control

Examples

She tried to stay calm, but her frustration eventually boiled over during the discussion.
If you keep ignoring the problem, emotions are bound to boil over at some point.
His anger boiled over when he discovered the mistake.

Synset Examples

Emotions can boil over if not addressed properly.
Don't let your anger boil over during the meeting.
The pot began to boil over as I was distracted.
His rage boiled over when he heard the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - to suddenly lose control and express anger violently
  • Antonym: to simmer down - to become less angry or emotional
