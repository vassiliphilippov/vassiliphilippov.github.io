to drop dead

1. VERB

/ drɑp dɛd / (AmE), / drɒp dɛd / (BrE)
to die suddenly or unexpectedly, often used informally
to die suddenly or unexpectedly
informal

Examples

He seemed perfectly healthy, so everyone was shocked when he dropped dead at work.
People can drop dead from a heart attack with little or no warning.
When the performer dropped dead on stage, the audience thought it was part of the act.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist as a living being
  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal)
  • Synonym: to perish - to die, especially in an accident or disaster

2. VERB

/ drɑp dɛd / (AmE), / drɒp dɛd / (BrE)
to suddenly die, collapse, or feel extremely weak or faint, often due to shock or exhaustion
suddenly die or collapse

Examples

I felt so exhausted I thought I would drop dead before reaching the finish line.
He was so shocked by the news he nearly dropped dead on the spot.
If you don't slow down, you're going to drop dead from overwork.

Synset Examples

The news left me feeling like I could drop dead.
If I see him again, I might just drop dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often as a result of exhaustion or illness
  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time because of a temporarily insufficient supply of oxygen to the brain
