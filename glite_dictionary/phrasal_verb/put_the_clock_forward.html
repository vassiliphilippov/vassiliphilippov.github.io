to put the clock forward

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə klɑk ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / pʊt ðə klɒk ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to adjust a clock to a later time, especially as part of beginning daylight saving time
adjust clock for daylight saving

Examples

Don't forget to put the clock forward when daylight saving starts this Sunday.
Every spring we put the clock forward by one hour and lose some sleep.
My phone automatically puts the clock forward on the correct date.

Synset Examples

We need to put the clock forward an hour for daylight saving.
She forgot to put the clock forward last weekend.
It's time to put the clock forward for spring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to put the clock back - to adjust a clock to an earlier time, especially at the end of daylight saving time
