to cave in

1. VERB

/ keɪv ɪn / (AmE), / keɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to collapse suddenly, either physically (as a structure) or metaphorically (as in giving up under pressure)
collapse or yield under pressure

Examples

The roof began to cave in after the heavy snowfall.
Under relentless questioning, the suspect finally caved in and confessed.
If we don't repair the tunnel soon, it might cave in without warning.

Synset Examples

The wall collapsed
The business collapsed
The dam broke
The roof collapsed
The wall gave in
The roof finally gave under the weight of the ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: to give in - to yield under pressure or surrender

2. VERB

/ keɪv ɪn / (AmE), / keɪv ɪn / (BrE)
To yield or submit to pressure, persuasion, or force, often reluctantly.
yield to pressure or demands

Examples

When negotiations got tough, the manager refused to cave in to the demands of the union.
Although she was tempted, she didn't cave in to the peer pressure at the party.
After hours of debate, the city council finally caved in and approved the proposal.

Synset Examples

Don't cave in to peer pressure; stick to your values.
She refused to cave in during the heated discussion.
They threatened to leave unless he caved in to their requests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give in - to stop resisting; to yield
  • Antonym: to resist - to refuse to yield to pressure or force
