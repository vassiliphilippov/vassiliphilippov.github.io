to cool it

1. VERB

/ ˈkul ɪt / (AmE), / ˈkuːl ɪt / (BrE)
to become calm or quiet, especially after being excited or angry; to calm down
become calm or quiet
informal

Examples

After the argument, I needed a moment to cool it before going back into the meeting.
She told her brother to cool it when he started yelling at the referee.
Sometimes it's best to step outside and cool it if you're feeling frustrated.

Synset Examples

After the fight both men need to cool off.
It took a while after the baby was born for things to settle down again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become less angry or excited
