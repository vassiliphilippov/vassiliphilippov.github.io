to face an issue

1. VERB

/ feɪs ən ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / feɪs ən ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
to confront or deal with a problem or difficulty directly and openly
confront a problem directly

Examples

Management must face an issue when it arises, rather than ignore it.
Whenever we face an issue together, we find a solution faster.
She prefers to face an issue head-on rather than avoid confrontation.

Synset Examples

Let's face these issues as they come.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: address an issue - to deal with or discuss a problem
  • Antonym: avoid an issue - to steer clear of or not face a problem
