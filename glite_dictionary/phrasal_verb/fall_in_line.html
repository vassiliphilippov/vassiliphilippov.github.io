to fall in line

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
to conform to or agree with a particular standard, position, or set of rules, especially after initial reluctance
conform or agree with a position or standard

Examples

Most employees eventually fall in line with the company’s policies.
After much debate, the committee members finally fell in line with the proposal.
It took some convincing, but he agreed to fall in line with the new regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conform - to behave according to socially acceptable conventions or standards
  • Antonym: rebel - to resist authority, control, or tradition

2. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
to conform to rules, expectations, or the behavior of others, especially after initial reluctance
conform or comply with rules or norms

Examples

After some initial hesitation, the new employees fell in line with the company's dress code.
The government expects citizens to fall in line with its new regulations.
If you don't fall in line soon, you may face disciplinary action.

Synset Examples

You need to fall in line with the new policies.
If you want to succeed, you must fall in line.
Everyone must fall in line for the upcoming changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conform - to act in accordance with rules or standards
  • Antonym: rebel - to resist authority, control, or tradition
