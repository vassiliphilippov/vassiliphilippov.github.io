to act like a ponce

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə pɑns / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə pɒns / (BrE)
to behave in a pretentious, showy, or affected manner, especially in an effeminate or ostentatious way
behave in a pretentious or affected way
informal british

Examples

Please don't act like a ponce at the party; just be yourself.
He was acting like a ponce, flaunting his expensive watch in front of everyone.
People get annoyed when she acts like a ponce to impress her friends.

Synset Examples

He acts like a ponce, always showing off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration
  • Antonym: be modest - to behave in a humble or unpretentious manner

Etymology

ponce: British slang, possibly from French 'pancier' (to beg), later extended to mean a pretentious man.
