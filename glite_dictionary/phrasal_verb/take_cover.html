to take cover

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈkʌv.ɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ˈkʌv.ə / (BrE)
to move to a place where you are protected from danger or bad weather
find shelter from danger or weather

Examples

When lightning struck, the group quickly took cover under a picnic shelter.
The zookeeper advised visitors to take cover during the sudden hailstorm.
As the shooting started, everyone in the street took cover behind parked cars.

Synset Examples

The soldiers were ordered to take cover during the attack.
You should take cover from the rain under that awning.
When the storm hits, we should take cover inside.
It’s best to find a friendly bush to take cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shelter - protected place from danger or bad weather
  • Antonym: expose oneself - to be unprotected in dangerous situations
