to whisk away

1. VERB

/ wɪsk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɪsk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to take someone or something away quickly and suddenly
remove quickly and suddenly

Examples

The magician whisked away the tablecloth without disturbing a single dish.
She was whisked away in a waiting car before anyone could say goodbye.
Security quietly whisked away the celebrity through a side exit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snatch - to take something quickly and often roughly
  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something quickly, firmly, or forcibly
