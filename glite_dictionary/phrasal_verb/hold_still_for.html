to hold still for

1. VERB

/ hoʊld stɪl fɔr / (AmE), / həʊld stɪl fə / (BrE)
to tolerate or endure something, especially something unpleasant or undesired
tolerate or endure (unpleasant things)

Examples

I won't hold still for any more of your excuses.
She refused to hold still for unfair treatment at work.
They are not going to hold still for further price increases.

Synset Examples

I won't stand for this kind of behavior!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand for - to tolerate or accept something
  • Synonym: to put up with - to tolerate or accept something undesirable
  • Synonym: to tolerate - to allow the existence or occurrence of something unpleasant
