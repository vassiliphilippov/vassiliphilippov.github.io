to shed blood

1. VERB

/ ʃɛd blʌd / (AmE), / ʃɛd blʌd / (BrE)
to kill or injure people, especially in the context of violent conflict; to cause blood to be spilled
to kill or injure in violence

Examples

The rebels were determined not to shed blood unless absolutely necessary.
She pleaded for peace, urging both sides not to shed blood over the dispute.
Throughout history, many wars have shed blood needlessly.

Synset Examples

They will never stop shedding the blood of their enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kill - to cause the death of someone or something
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or injuring
