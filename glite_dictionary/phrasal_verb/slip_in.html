to slip in

1. VERB

/ slɪp ɪn / (AmE), / slɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to insert or mention something in a subtle, casual, or unobtrusive way
insert or mention casually

Examples

She tried to slip in a compliment during the conversation.
The author slipped in a few jokes into the otherwise serious article.
He slipped in his resignation letter without drawing attention.

Synset Examples

She slipped in a reference to her own work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mention - to refer to something briefly
  • Antonym: to leave out - to not include or omit something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01027611-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ slɪp ɪn / (AmE), / slɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to lose one's balance and fall, especially due to muddy or slippery conditions
lose balance and fall (in mud or slippery area)

Examples

Be careful near the stream, or you might slip in and hurt yourself.
She slipped in the mud during her morning walk and got her clothes dirty.
He laughed, remembering how he had slipped in the wet grass at last year's picnic.

Synset Examples

She slipped in the mud while hiking yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - to move downward, typically rapidly and freely without control
