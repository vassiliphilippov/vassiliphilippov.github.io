to work nights

1. VERB

/ wɝːk naɪts / (AmE), / wɜːk naɪts / (BrE)
to be employed or do one's job during the nighttime hours, often on a regular or recurring basis
to work during the night

Examples

After her promotion, she had to work nights at the hospital.
Many employees prefer to work nights because the pay is higher.
He used to work nights, but now he has a regular daytime schedule.

Synset Examples

He decided to work nights to fit school into his schedule.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: work days - to work during the daytime hours
