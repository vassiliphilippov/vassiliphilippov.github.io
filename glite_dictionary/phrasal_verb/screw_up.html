to screw up

1. VERB

/ skruː ʌp / (AmE), / skruː ʌp / (BrE)
to tighten or contort (a part of the body, especially the face) into a tense or strained expression
tighten or contort (esp. face) into a strained expression

Examples

She screwed up her face in confusion when she heard the strange noise.
Whenever he tastes something sour, he screws up his mouth unconsciously.
The child screwed up his eyes tightly before jumping into the pool.

Synset Examples

screw up one's face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimace - make a twisted expression showing discomfort or pain

2. VERB

/ skruː ʌp / (AmE), / skruː ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mess of something; to destroy or ruin something by making a mistake
make a mess of; ruin by mistake
informal

Examples

I didn't mean to screw up the project, but I made a big mistake.
He always screws up when he gets nervous during tests.
They completely screwed up the order, so we had to wait another hour.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make a mistake or do something badly
  • Synonym: to muck up - to botch or do something incompetently (British informal)
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ skru ʌp / (AmE), / skruː ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mistake or do something badly; to ruin or spoil something through error or carelessness
make a mistake; ruin something
informal

Examples

If you screw up this report, we could lose the client.
I really hope I don’t screw up my job interview tomorrow.
She was afraid she would screw up her lines during the performance.

Synset Examples

Don't screw this up; it's our last chance.
I always screw up when I’m nervous.
Don’t worry; everyone screws up sometimes.
She didn’t mean to screw up the recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make a mistake or do something badly
  • Synonym: bungle - to carry out a task clumsily or incompetently
  • Antonym: get right - to do something correctly
