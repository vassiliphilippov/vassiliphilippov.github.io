to settle in

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬.əl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl ɪn / (BrE)
to begin to feel comfortable and adjust to a new place, job, or situation
become comfortable in a new place or role

Examples

It will take some time for the new students to settle in at the school.
After moving to the city, it took her a month to fully settle in.
Once you settle in, everything will start to feel more familiar.

Synset Examples

It took him a few days to settle in after moving.
The organization helps refugee families settle in the city.
She found it easy to settle in at her new job.
Make sure to settle in before the meeting starts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - become adjusted to new conditions
  • Synonym: to acclimatize - become accustomed to a new environment
