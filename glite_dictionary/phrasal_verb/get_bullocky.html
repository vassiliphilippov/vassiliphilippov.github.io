to get bullocky

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈbʊləki / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈbʊləki / (BrE)
to become aggressive or stubborn, especially when confronted or challenged
become aggressive or obstinate
informal

Examples

Whenever the topic of overtime comes up, he tends to get bullocky and refuses to compromise.
She warned her team not to get bullocky during the negotiation process.
If you get bullocky about every detail, progress will be slow.

Synset Examples

She got bullocky when discussing her promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: become stubborn - to begin to refuse to change one's mind or behavior
  • Antonym: yield - to give way or submit

Etymology

Derived from 'bullocky', meaning like or characteristic of a bullock driver, noted for stubbornness or toughness.
