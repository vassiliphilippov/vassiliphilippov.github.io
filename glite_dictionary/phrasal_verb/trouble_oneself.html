to trouble oneself

1. VERB

/ ˈtrʌbəl wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəl wʌnˈself / (BrE)
to make an effort to do something, especially when it is not strictly necessary; to take the trouble to do something
make an effort to do something

Examples

She didn't trouble herself to respond to the invitation.
If you don't want to trouble yourself, I can handle it alone.
He never troubles himself with details he finds unimportant.

Synset Examples

He did not trouble to call his mother on her birthday
Don't bother, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bother - to make the effort to do something
