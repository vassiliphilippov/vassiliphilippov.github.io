to drool over

1. VERB

/ ˈdruːl ˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdruːl ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with great desire or envy, often in an obvious or exaggerated way
look at with intense desire or envy

Examples

Every time I see that car, I can't help but drool over it.
The children drooled over the colorful cakes displayed in the bakery window.
He spent hours drooling over the latest gadgets in the electronics shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to covet - to want something very much, especially something belonging to someone else
  • Synonym: envy - to desire what someone else has
  • Antonym: to despise - to strongly dislike or look down on something or someone

2. VERB

/ ˈdruːl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈdruːl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to show intense desire, longing, or admiration for something, often in an exaggerated or obvious way
show strong desire or admiration
informal

Examples

Every time she saw the sports car in the showroom, she couldn't help but drool over it.
He would drool over the latest gadgets in the tech magazine for hours on end.
The students drooled over the display of delicious pastries at the bakery window.

Synset Examples

He began to drool over the pictures of gourmet meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desire - strong feeling of wanting to have something
  • Synonym: to admire - regard something or someone with respect and approval
