to take on

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ɒn / (BrE)
to accept a task, responsibility, or challenge, especially one that is difficult
accept a challenge or responsibility

Examples

She decided to take on the new project despite its difficulties.
Will you take on the responsibility of leading the team?
He loves to take on challenging assignments at work.

Synset Examples

I'll tackle this difficult task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undertake - to agree to do a task or job
  • Antonym: refuse - to decline to accept something
