to gussy up

1. VERB

/ ˈɡʌsi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡʌsi ʌp / (BrE)
to dress or decorate someone or something in a showy or elaborate way in order to look more attractive
dress or decorate elaborately
informal

Examples

They decided to gussy up the old barn with fresh paint and new curtains.
She likes to gussy up before going out to fancy restaurants.
The committee gussied up the office for the annual holiday party.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or special clothes
  • Synonym: to fancy up - to make more decorative or elaborate

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the nickname Gussie, used to mean 'showily dressed person' in early 20th century US slang.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡʌsi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡʌsi ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone or something more attractive or elaborate by dressing up or adding decorations
make more attractive by dressing up or decorating
informal

Examples

She loves to gussy up her living room with fresh flowers every week.
Before the gala, he tried to gussy up with a new suit and shiny shoes.
They spent hours gussying up the storefront for the holiday season.

Synset Examples

He gussied up the old dress with some new accessories.
Don't gussy your room too much; keep it cozy.
She likes to gussy up for her friend's weddings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes
  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make neater, smarter, or more attractive

Etymology

Origin uncertain; likely an American slang term first recorded in the early 20th century.
