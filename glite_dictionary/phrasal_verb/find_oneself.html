to find oneself

1. VERB

/ faɪnd wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / faɪnd wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to discover or come to understand one's true personality, abilities, or purpose in life
discover one's true self

Examples

After years of traveling, she finally began to find herself and understand what made her happy.
Some people take a gap year to find themselves before starting university or work.
He moved abroad, hoping to find himself in a new environment.

Synset Examples

My son went to Berkeley to find himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discover oneself - to become aware of one's own identity or capabilities

2. VERB

/ faɪnd wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / faɪnd wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to discover or come to understand one's true character, abilities, or purpose in life, often through personal experience or self-reflection.
discover or understand one's true self

Examples

After quitting his job, he traveled through Asia to find himself.
Many people take a gap year to find themselves before starting university.
She spent months in the countryside, hoping to find herself away from the noise of the city.

Synset Examples

My son went to Berkeley to find himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discover oneself - to become aware of one's true character or potential
  • Antonym: to lose oneself - to become so involved in something that awareness of oneself is diminished
