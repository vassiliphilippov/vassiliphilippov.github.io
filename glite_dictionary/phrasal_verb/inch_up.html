to inch up

1. VERB

/ ɪntʃ ʌp / (AmE), / ɪntʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or rise slowly and by small amounts
to increase slowly; rise gradually

Examples

The company's profits continued to inch up throughout the year despite fierce competition.
As the deadline approaches, my anxiety tends to inch up little by little.
Housing prices have begun to inch up after several months of stagnation.

Synset Examples

Her confidence began to inch up after the compliment.
The price of gas continues to inch up each week.
The temperature is expected to inch up tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to creep up - to increase slowly, often unnoticed
  • Synonym: rise - to move upward or increase
