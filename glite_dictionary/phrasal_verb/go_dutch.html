to go Dutch

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈdʌtʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈdʌtʃ / (BrE)
to share the cost of something, especially a meal, equally between two or more people
share expenses equally

Examples

Whenever we eat out, we always go Dutch to keep things fair.
They decided to go Dutch on the taxi fare after the party.
It's common among friends to go Dutch when dining at restaurants.

Synset Examples

My boyfriend and I always go Dutch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split the bill - to divide the cost of something equally

Etymology

Likely from the stereotype of Dutch frugality; first recorded in the 19th century.
