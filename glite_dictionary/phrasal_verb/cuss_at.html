to cuss at

1. VERB

/ kʌs æt / (AmE), / kʌs æt / (BrE)
to swear or use offensive language toward someone or something
swear at someone or something
informal

Examples

He started to cuss at the computer after it crashed again.
Drivers often cuss at each other during heavy traffic in the city.
My grandmother doesn’t like it when anyone cusses at people in her house.

Synset Examples

She always cusses at the traffic jams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse at - to use offensive language toward someone or something

Etymology

Variant of 'curse,' from Middle English 'curssen.'
