to find redemption

1. VERB

/ faɪnd rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (AmE), / faɪnd rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (BrE)
to achieve forgiveness or salvation, especially after wrongdoing or a period of guilt.
achieve forgiveness or salvation

Examples

After years of mistakes, he hoped he could find redemption through good deeds.
The novel's main character struggles to find redemption for his past.
Many religions teach that anyone can find redemption if they truly repent.

Synset Examples

Even the sinner can find redemption if they try.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redeem oneself - to do something that compensates for past faults or wrongdoing

Etymology

Redemption from Latin 'redemptio' (a buying back, release).
