to originate in

1. VERB

/ əˈrɪdʒəˌneɪt ɪn / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪneɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to have as its source; to come from a particular place, period, or situation
to come from a source

Examples

Many of the traditions celebrated today originate in ancient festivals.
This rumor originated in the local community before spreading nationwide.
The disease is believed to originate in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come from - to have as a source or origin

Etymology

From Latin *originari* (to arise, begin), from *origo* (origin).
