to stand fire

1. VERB

/ stænd ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / stænd ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to endure danger, criticism, or pressure without flinching; to face adversity with courage
endure danger or criticism bravely
archaic

Examples

The soldiers were trained to stand fire even in the direst circumstances.
Despite harsh criticism from the media, the politician managed to stand fire and remain composed.
She had to stand fire when her decision was questioned by the entire committee.

Synset Examples

To prove his bravery, he had to stand fire in the heat of battle.
The veteran firefighter had to stand fire during the rescue.
She decided to stand fire and defend her friend's honor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withstand - to resist or stand up to something successfully
  • Antonym: to crumble - to break down or collapse under pressure
