to lay off

1. VERB

/ leɪ ɔf / (AmE), / leɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to stop doing something, especially an activity that is annoying or bothering someone
stop doing something, especially annoying someone
informal

Examples

You need to lay off criticizing her work every day.
The doctor told him to lay off junk food for a while.
Hey, lay off and give me some space!

Synset Examples

Quit teasing your little brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quit - to stop or discontinue an action or activity
  • Synonym: to cease - to bring or come to an end
  • Antonym: to continue - to keep doing something without stopping
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02686624-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ leɪ ˈɔf / (AmE), / leɪ ˈɒf / (BrE)
to terminate the employment of a worker, usually for economic reasons and not due to the worker's performance
dismiss from a job (economically motivated)

Examples

The company had to lay off 200 workers due to declining sales.
After being laid off, he started looking for a new job right away.
Many employees fear they might be laid off during the next round of budget cuts.

Synset Examples

She was laid off together with hundreds of other workers when the company downsized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to formally remove someone from their job
  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job, usually because of poor performance or misconduct
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02408679-v
Synset POS: v
