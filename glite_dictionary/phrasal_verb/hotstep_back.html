to hotstep back

1. VERB

/ ˈhɒt.stɛp ˈbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɒt.stɛp ˈbæk / (BrE)
to quickly move or jump backward, especially in alarm or surprise
move backward quickly in fear or surprise

Examples

She couldn't help but hotstep back when the cat hissed at her.
The crowd hotstepped back as the fireworks suddenly exploded nearby.
He hotstepped back instinctively as the branch snapped close to his face.

Synset Examples

He took a hotstep back when he saw the snake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump back - to move or leap backward suddenly, usually from surprise or alarm
  • Synonym: recoil - to suddenly spring or jump back
