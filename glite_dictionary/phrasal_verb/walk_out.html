to walk out

1. VERB

/ wɔk aʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place or event suddenly and unexpectedly, often as a protest or because of anger.
leave suddenly, often in protest or anger

Examples

After the heated argument, she walked out and never came back.
The workers threatened to walk out if their demands were not met.
Several attendees walked out during the controversial speaker's presentation.

Synset Examples

The customer that was not served walked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm out - to leave a place angrily and suddenly
  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02014252-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɑk aʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
To leave suddenly and unexpectedly, often as a show of protest or disapproval.
leave suddenly as protest or disapproval

Examples

After the heated argument, she walked out and didn't come back for hours.
Many employees threatened to walk out unless their demands were met.
He walked out on the meeting when he heard the news.

Synset Examples

She walked out on her husband and children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • Synonym: storm out - to leave suddenly and angrily, especially after an argument
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00616355-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ wɑk aʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
(of employees) to stop working and leave as a form of protest or to press for demands, especially during a strike.
stop working in protest, as in a strike

Examples

The factory workers decided to walk out after negotiations with management broke down.
When their safety concerns were ignored, the engineers threatened to walk out the next day.
Teachers across the city plan to walk out in support of better classroom funding.

Synset Examples

The auto workers are striking for higher wages
The employees walked out when their demand for better benefits was not met

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - refusal to work as a form of protest by employees
