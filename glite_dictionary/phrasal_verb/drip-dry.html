to drip-dry

1. VERB

/ drɪp ˈdraɪ / (AmE), / drɪp ˈdraɪ / (BrE)
to dry (clothes or fabric) by hanging them up while wet, without using heat or wringing
to dry clothes by hanging them up wet

Examples

You can just drip-dry these shirts after washing; there's no need for the dryer.
She prefers to drip-dry her delicate fabrics to avoid shrinking them.
After swimming, my towel drip-dried on the line in the backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air-dry - to dry by exposure to air; not using heat
