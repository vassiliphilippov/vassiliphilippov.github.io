to sack out

1. VERB

/ sæk aʊt / (AmE), / sæk aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) To go to bed or fall asleep, usually for the purpose of resting for the night.
go to bed or fall asleep (informal)
informal american

Examples

After a long day at work, I just want to sack out and get some sleep.
We didn't sack out until nearly 2 a.m. after the party.
Whenever he's tired, he can sack out almost anywhere, even on the floor.

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn in - (informal) go to bed, especially at night
  • Synonym: to hit the hay - (slang) go to bed, fall asleep

Etymology

Possibly from American slang 'hit the sack' (go to bed), with 'sack' meaning bed.
