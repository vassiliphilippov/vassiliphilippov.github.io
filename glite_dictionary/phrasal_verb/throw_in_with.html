to throw in with

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn wɪð / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn wɪð / (BrE)
to join or align oneself with a group, cause, or side
join or support a group or side

Examples

After much deliberation, she decided to throw in with the opposition party.
Many startups throw in with larger companies to survive the competition.
He wasn't sure at first, but eventually he threw in with the committee's plan.

Synset Examples

He decided to throw in with the new team.
She chose to throw in with the winners.
They always throw in with the loudest voices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to join forces - to act together with others to achieve something
  • Synonym: to side with - to support or align with someone in an argument or conflict
