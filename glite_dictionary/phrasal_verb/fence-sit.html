to fence-sit

1. VERB

/ fɛns sɪt / (AmE), / fɛns sɪt / (BrE)
to remain neutral or indecisive, especially by not taking sides in a dispute or making a decision
remain neutral or undecided
informal

Examples

He tends to fence-sit whenever a controversial issue is discussed at work.
The committee can't afford to fence-sit on such an important matter.
If you always fence-sit, people may see you as indecisive.

Synset Examples

She prefers to fence-sit rather than take sides in arguments.
If you fence-sit, you might miss great opportunities.
It's hard to make progress when you choose to fence-sit.
You can't sit on the fence about this decision.
She tends to sit on the fence during political debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit on the fence - to avoid making a decision or choosing a side
  • Antonym: to take sides - to support one party or position over another
  • Antonym: to decide - to make a choice or come to a conclusion

Etymology

From the idiom 'sit on the fence', metaphorically describing someone not choosing a side.
