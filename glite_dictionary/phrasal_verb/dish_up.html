to dish up

1. VERB

/ dɪʃ ʌp / (AmE), / dɪʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to serve food onto plates, or to provide something, especially food, to people
serve food or provide something

Examples

Could you please dish up some more rice for the guests?
After hours in the kitchen, she was finally ready to dish up dinner.
The chef loves to dish up creative meals that surprise his customers.

Synset Examples

We serve meals for the homeless
She dished out the soup at 8 P.M.
The entertainers served up a lively show

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to serve up - to present or provide food or something for people's enjoyment
  • Synonym: to dish out - to distribute food or something else, sometimes in a casual or generous manner
  • Antonym: to clear away - to remove dishes and food after a meal

Etymology

From 'dish' (noun, meaning plate or container for food) + 'up' (particle indicating completion or upwards motion).

2. VERB

/ dɪʃ ʌp / (AmE), / dɪʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to serve food onto plates or at the table, especially from a larger container
to serve food at the table

Examples

She offered to dish up the soup while I set the table.
Every Sunday, Grandma would dish up a hearty roast dinner for the family.
The chef dished up the main course with a flourish.

Synset Examples

She always knows how to dish up the best meals.
He will dish up dessert after dinner is served.
They dish up generous portions at that diner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - to present food to someone at a meal
