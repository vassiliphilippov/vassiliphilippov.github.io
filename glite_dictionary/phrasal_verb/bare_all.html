to bare all

1. VERB

/ bɛr ɔl / (AmE), / beə ɔːl / (BrE)
to reveal everything about oneself, especially personal or private information; to be completely open or honest
reveal everything; be completely open

Examples

After years of silence, he decided to bare all in his new memoir.
The actress chose to bare all during the emotional interview.
Sometimes it takes courage to bare all and tell the truth.

Synset Examples

During the interview, she decided to bare all about her struggles.
The documentary aimed to bare all about the celebrity's life.
He chose to bare all, sharing his deepest secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - admit or disclose something private, often wrongdoing
  • Antonym: to conceal - keep something secret; not reveal information
