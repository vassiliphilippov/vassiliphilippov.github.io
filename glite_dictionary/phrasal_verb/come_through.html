to come through

1. VERB

/ kʌm θru / (AmE), / kʌm θruː / (BrE)
to survive or continue to exist after experiencing adversity or difficulty
survive an adversity

Examples

Despite the serious accident, he managed to come through with only minor injuries.
The company had to lay off workers, but it eventually came through the crisis.
She came through her illness stronger than ever.

Synset Examples

He survived the cancer against all odds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to survive - to continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship

/ kʌm θru / (AmE), / kʌm θruː / (BrE)
to succeed in achieving something, especially after a difficult or challenging period
to succeed after difficulty

Even though the team faced many obstacles, they always come through in the end.
We were counting on you to organize the event, and you really came through for us.

5. VERB

/ kʌm θru / (AmE), / kʌm θruː / (BrE)
(of a message, signal, or communication) to be successfully received or transmitted.
be successfully transmitted (of messages)

Examples

The radio signal finally came through after the storm had passed.
Your email hasn't come through yet; could you try sending it again?
Despite the static, her voice came through clearly over the phone.

Synset Examples
