to run dry

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈdraɪ / (AmE), / rʌn ˈdraɪ / (BrE)
(of a river, well, etc.) to become empty of water or liquid; to stop having any more water or liquid.
become empty of water or liquid

Examples

In the hottest months, the small creek would often run dry by August.
We must conserve water or our reservoirs will run dry in a drought.
If your inspiration has run dry, take a break and return later.

Synset Examples

The river runs dry in the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dry up - to become completely dry or to make something dry by removing its water
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02777714-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn draɪ / (AmE), / rʌn draɪ / (BrE)
to become empty of a supply or resource; to be exhausted or depleted.
become empty or depleted

Examples

After hours of searching for inspiration, my ideas finally ran dry.
The well will run dry if there is no rain this season.
Sometimes, even the most talented writers run dry creatively.

Synset Examples

After hours of brainstorming, our ideas ran dry.
The river has run dry due to the drought.
My creativity seems to run dry at the moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dry up - to become dry or to be depleted completely, especially of a resource
  • Synonym: to run out - to be used up so that nothing is left

Synset POS: None
