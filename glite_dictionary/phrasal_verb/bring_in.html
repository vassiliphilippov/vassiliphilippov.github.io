to bring in

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ɪn / (AmE), / brɪŋ ɪn / (BrE)
to introduce or cause a new person or object to be present or involved in a particular situation or place
introduce a new person or object into a situation

Examples

The company decided to bring in a consultant to improve efficiency.
We need to bring in more volunteers for the event next week.
The school hopes to bring in new teaching methods this year.

Synset Examples

He brought in a new judge
The new secretary introduced a nasty rumor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to introduce - to bring someone or something into use or operation for the first time

/ brɪŋ ɪn / (AmE), / brɪŋ ɪn / (BrE)

The security cameras bring in live video feeds to the control room.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Examples
