to hunt down

1. VERB

/ hʌnt daʊn / (AmE), / hʌnt daʊn / (BrE)
to chase and capture or kill wild animals for food or sport
pursue and catch wild animals

Examples

The poachers would often hunt down antelope in the reserve for their meat.
Wolves instinctively hunt down weaker animals in the herd.
It took the trackers days to hunt down the elusive wild boar through the dense forest.

Synset Examples

Goering often hunted wild boars in Poland
The dogs are running deer
The Duke hunted in these woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunt - to pursue and kill wild animals
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01146304-v

2. VERB

/ hʌnt daʊn / (AmE), / hʌnt daʊn / (BrE)
to search for and successfully find someone or something, especially after a thorough or determined effort
search for and find after effort

Examples

Detectives worked tirelessly to hunt down the suspect responsible for the theft.
After hours of searching, she finally hunted down the recipe her grandmother used to make.
Journalists tried to hunt down more information about the anonymous whistleblower.

Synset Examples

She had to hunt down the missing files in the office.
They planned to hunt down the best deals at the market.
He was determined to hunt down the rare book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to track down - to search for and find someone or something after difficulty
