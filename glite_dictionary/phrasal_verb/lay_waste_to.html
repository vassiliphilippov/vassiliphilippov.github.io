to lay waste to

1. VERB

/ leɪ ˈweɪst tu / (AmE), / leɪ ˈweɪst tə / (BrE)
to completely destroy or devastate a place, leaving it ruined
to destroy or devastate utterly

Examples

The invading army laid waste to entire villages, leaving nothing but ashes behind.
A series of wildfires laid waste to the forests across the region last summer.
If we do not act soon, pollution will lay waste to our planet's natural beauty.

Synset Examples

The enemy lay waste to the countryside after the invasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devastate - to cause extensive destruction or ruin
  • Synonym: to destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired

Etymology

From the verb 'lay' + 'waste' (Middle English, from Old North French 'waste', from Latin 'vastare' meaning 'to devastate')
