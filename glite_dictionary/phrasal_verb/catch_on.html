to catch on

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / katʃ ɒn / (BrE)
To become popular or fashionable; to gain widespread acceptance or use.
become popular or fashionable

Examples

The song was slow to catch on, but now everyone loves it.
Her new hairstyle quickly caught on among teenagers.
It took a few years for smartphones to catch on with older adults.

Synset Examples

This fashion caught on in Paris

Related Terms

  • Synonym: become fashionable - come into fashion, become trendy or popular
  • Antonym: fade - lose popularity gradually, become less popular

2. VERB

/ kætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / kæʧ ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand or realize something, especially after some initial difficulty or delay
to begin to understand

Examples

After several explanations, he finally caught on to the new concept.
It took her a while to catch on, but she eventually understood the joke.
You’ll catch on quickly once you start practicing the technique.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to understand - to grasp the meaning or significance of something
  • Synonym: grasp - to comprehend or understand clearly
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to understand or notice something

3. VERB

/ kætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / katʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand or realize something; to grasp the meaning or idea
to understand; to realize

Examples

It took her a moment to catch on to what they were discussing.
You’ll catch on quickly once you try a few problems yourself.
Many students catch on faster when lessons are interactive.

Synset Examples

I didn’t catch on to his joke until later.
She will catch on to the new rules quickly.
It took me a while to catch on to the trend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to understand - to perceive the meaning of something
  • Synonym: grasp - to physically or mentally seize; to understand
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to notice or understand
