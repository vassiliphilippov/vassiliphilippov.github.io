to barge in

1. VERB

/ bɑrdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / bɑːdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to rudely or abruptly interrupt a conversation or enter a place without invitation or warning
rudely interrupt (conversation or place)

Examples

Please don't barge in when others are speaking; wait for your turn.
He tends to barge in during meetings, which annoys his colleagues.
They were having a private conversation when someone suddenly barged in and changed the topic.

Synset Examples

her husband always chimes in, even when he is not involved in the conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking by suddenly speaking oneself
  • Synonym: to cut in - to interrupt someone who is speaking

2. VERB

/ bɑrdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / bɑːdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place, room, or conversation suddenly and rudely, without being invited
enter uninvited or interrupt rudely
informal

Examples

I'm sorry to barge in like this, but I have urgent news.
She always tends to barge in when we're in the middle of a meeting.
The kids barge in without knocking, startling everyone.

Synset Examples

let's crash the party!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking or working by suddenly saying or doing something
  • Synonym: to intrude - to go into a place or situation where you are not wanted or invited

Etymology

From 'barge', meaning to move clumsily or forcefully, combined with 'in'.
