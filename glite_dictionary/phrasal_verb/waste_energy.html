to waste energy

1. VERB

/ weɪst ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / weɪst ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
to use physical or mental effort in a way that is unproductive or ineffective
use energy ineffectively

Examples

Don't waste energy arguing over things that don't matter.
She realized she was wasting energy worrying about the future instead of taking action.
It's important not to waste energy during a long-distance race.

Synset Examples

Don't waste your energy; time is short for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conserve energy - to use energy carefully so as not to waste it
