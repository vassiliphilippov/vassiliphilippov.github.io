to drop the idea

1. VERB

/ drɑp ði aɪˈdiə / (AmE), / drɒp ði aɪˈdɪə / (BrE)
to decide not to continue with a plan or suggestion
abandon a plan or suggestion

Examples

After much consideration, she finally decided to drop the idea of quitting her job.
The team had to drop the idea due to lack of funding.
He suggested a camping trip, but they quickly dropped the idea when they saw the weather forecast.

Synset Examples

He decided to drop the idea of moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandon - to give up completely; stop supporting or pursuing
  • Antonym: to pursue - to continue with or follow a plan or idea
