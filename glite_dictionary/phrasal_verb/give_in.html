to give in

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪn / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to agree to do something or accept something after first refusing or resisting, usually reluctantly
consent or surrender reluctantly

Examples

She finally gave in to her children's demands for ice cream.
After hours of negotiation, he refused to give in to their pressure.
No matter how much they argued, she wouldn't give in easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to give way or surrender under pressure
  • Antonym: to resist - to refuse to accept or give in to something

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪn / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to stop resisting; to agree to something that you were opposing or not wanting to do
stop resisting; yield or surrender

Examples

After hours of debate, she finally gave in and accepted our proposal.
The children begged for ice cream until their father gave in.
He rarely gives in to pressure from others.

Synset Examples

The government bowed to the military pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to surrender or give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of something

3. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪn / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to stop resisting; to surrender or yield, often after opposition or persuasion
to surrender or yield

Examples

After several attempts to resolve the dispute, he finally decided to give in.
The children begged for hours until their mother had to give in to their requests.
She refused to give in to the pressure from her colleagues.

Synset Examples

After hours of arguing, she decided to give in.
Sometimes it's best to give in for peace.
He never wanted to give in to peer pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to stop fighting or resisting and agree to do what someone else wants
  • Synonym: yield - to give in to pressure or force
  • Antonym: to resist - to refuse to surrender or yield
