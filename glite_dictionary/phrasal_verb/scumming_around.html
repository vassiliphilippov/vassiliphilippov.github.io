to scumming around

1. VERB

/ ˈskʌmɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈskʌmɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a lazy or unscrupulous way, especially while avoiding work or responsibility
behave lazily or unscrupulously
informal

Examples

He spent most of last summer just scumming around instead of getting a job.
The manager warned the team against scumming around during work hours.
After failing his exams, he was accused of scumming around and not taking life seriously.

Synset Examples

He's been scumming around, looking for easy money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loaf around - to spend time in an idle or lazy way
  • Synonym: to slack off - to avoid work or responsibility

Etymology

From 'scum' (informal term for a contemptible person) + 'around'.
