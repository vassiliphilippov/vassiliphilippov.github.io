to let off

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɔf / (AmE), / lɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to allow someone to avoid punishment, duty, or an unpleasant situation; to excuse or release someone from an obligation
excuse or release from duty or punishment

Examples

The teacher decided to let off the student with just a warning.
Because it was his first offense, the judge chose to let off the accused.
She was surprised when her boss agreed to let off her from working overtime.

Synset Examples

Please excuse me from this class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - to pardon or exempt from a duty or punishment
  • Antonym: to punish - to inflict a penalty on someone for wrongdoing

2. VERB

/ lɛt ɑf / (AmE), / lɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to release someone from a duty, punishment, or responsibility, or to allow someone to go without penalty
release or excuse from responsibility or penalty

Examples

The principal decided to let off the students with a warning for being late.
He was let off the project because of his prior commitments.
Though she forgot her homework, her teacher let her off this time.

Synset Examples

The teacher decided to let off the students early today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - to forgive someone or to allow them not to do something
  • Antonym: to punish - to subject someone to a penalty for wrongdoing
