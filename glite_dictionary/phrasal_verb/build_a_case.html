to build a case

1. VERB

/ bɪld ə keɪs / (AmE), / bɪld ə keɪs / (BrE)
to collect and organize evidence or arguments in order to support a particular position or accusation
gather evidence or arguments to support a claim

Examples

The prosecution hopes to build a case with the new evidence collected from the crime scene.
She spent weeks trying to build a case for additional funding for the project.
Journalists often build a case by gathering multiple sources and verifying the facts.

Synset Examples

He needs to build a case for why he deserves a raise.
You’ll have to build a case if you want to change the policy.
The lawyer worked hard to build a case against the defendant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a case - to present arguments in favor of something
