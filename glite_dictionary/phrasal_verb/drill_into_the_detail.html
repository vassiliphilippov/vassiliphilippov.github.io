to drill into the detail

1. VERB

/ drɪl ˈɪntu ðə dɪˈteɪl / (AmE), / drɪl ˈɪntuː ðə dɪˈteɪl / (BrE)
to examine something thoroughly and carefully, especially by investigating specific aspects or components
examine thoroughly or carefully

Examples

Before presenting to the board, we should drill into the detail of our proposal.
The auditor plans to drill into the detail of their financial records.
As we drilled into the detail, more questions about the project arose.

Synset Examples

We need to drill into the details before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine in detail to discover meaning or essential features
  • Synonym: to scrutinize - to examine closely and critically
  • Antonym: skim - to read or examine superficially or quickly
