to louse up

1. VERB

/ laʊs ʌp / (AmE), / laʊs ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mess of or ruin something; to do something badly or spoil it
make a mess of; ruin
informal american

Examples

Don't louse up the project by skipping important steps.
He really loused up his interview by forgetting the manager's name.
She was worried that one wrong move would louse up the entire plan.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make mistakes or cause confusion; to ruin something
  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: to screw up - to do something badly or make a serious mistake

Etymology

From the noun 'louse' (a small parasitic insect), used metaphorically for something undesirable; 'up' as an intensifier. American English, early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ laʊs ʌp / (AmE), / laʊs ʌp / (BrE)
to spoil, damage, or make mistakes in something
spoil or make a mess of something
informal american

Examples

I'm afraid I loused up the report by missing several important details.
He loused up his chances of promotion by arriving late to the interview.
They realized too late that they'd loused up the scheduling for the conference.

Synset Examples

I really loused up the presentation with my mistakes.
She loused up the recipe by forgetting the main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to do something badly or incorrectly
  • Synonym: to screw up - to make a serious mistake; to spoil something

Etymology

From 'louse' meaning a contemptible person, used figuratively; American English, early 20th century.

3. VERB

/ laʊs ʌp / (AmE), / laʊs ʌp / (BrE)
to spoil, damage, or ruin something through mistake or incompetence
make a mess of; ruin
informal american

Examples

Try not to louse up the report this time.
She really loused up her audition by forgetting her lines.
If you louse up the interview, you won't get another chance.

Synset Examples

He was worried he would louse up the project deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make a mistake or do something badly
  • Synonym: to screw up - to make a serious mistake or do something badly

Etymology

From 'louse', an insect, used slangily for an objectionable person; 'louse up' arose in 20th-century American English.
