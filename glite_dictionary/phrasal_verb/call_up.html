to call up

1. VERB

/ kɔl ʌp / (AmE), / kɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
to telephone someone; to attempt to contact someone by phone
telephone someone

Examples

I'll call up Sarah later to see how she's doing.
She decided to call up her grandmother to wish her a happy birthday.
I'm going to call up the doctor and make an appointment.

Synset Examples

I tried to call you all night
Take two aspirin and call me in the morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone - to communicate with someone by telephone
  • Synonym: to ring up - to call someone on the telephone (especially British English)

2. VERB

/ kɔl ʌp / (AmE), / kɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
to bring something forward for discussion or consideration, especially in a formal setting such as a meeting or court
bring forward for consideration

Examples

The judge decided to call up the case for immediate review.
At the meeting, she called up an old proposal for discussion.
The committee may call up any issue that requires urgent attention.

Synset Examples

The case was called up in court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring up - to mention or introduce for discussion

3. VERB

/ kɔl ʌp / (AmE), / kɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
to cause to recall or bring to mind a memory or piece of information; to remember something.
recall or remember; bring to mind

Examples

Hearing that song always calls up memories of my childhood.
Try to call up the details of the meeting for your report.
She called up the image of her grandmother's smile in her mind.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remember - to bring to mind or recall something from memory

4. VERB

/ kɔl ʌp / (AmE), / kɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
to order someone, especially military personnel, to begin active service; to summon to duty or arms
summon to active military service

Examples

The government decided to call up reservists in response to the crisis.
Many young men were called up during the conflict to serve in the army.
He received a letter telling him he would be called up for military duty.

Related Terms
