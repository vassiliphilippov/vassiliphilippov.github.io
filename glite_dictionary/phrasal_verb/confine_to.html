to confine to

1. VERB

/ kənˈfaɪn tu / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪn tʊ / (BrE)
to restrict something or someone within certain limits; to not allow to go beyond a particular area, activity, or subject
restrict within limits

Examples

Please confine to the topic during your presentation so everyone can follow the discussion.
Because of the infection risk, the patient was confined to her room.
Many students feel confined to a narrow curriculum and wish for more electives.

Synset Examples

I limit you to two visits to the pub a day
please confine your questions to the topic
our actions are circumscribed by our biology, personality, and by the social and cultural context into which we are born

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit to - to set or keep within boundaries; restrict to a particular extent
  • Antonym: expand beyond - to go outside or exceed set limits or boundaries
