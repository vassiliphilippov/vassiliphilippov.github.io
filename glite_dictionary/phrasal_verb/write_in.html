to write in

1. VERB

/ ˈraɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to send a letter or communication, especially to an organization, radio, or television program, usually expressing an opinion or a request
send a letter to an organization, etc.

Examples

Hundreds of fans wrote in to request their favorite songs.
You can write in with your questions and comments about the show.
After hearing the news story, several people wrote in to share their experiences.

Synset Examples

Many listeners wrote in after the controversial radio program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write - to compose and send a letter or message
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00995498-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ raɪt ɪn / (AmE), / raɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to cast a vote by adding the name of a candidate who is not listed on the ballot
to add an unlisted candidate's name on a ballot

Examples

Several voters decided to write in an independent candidate during the mayoral election.
If you don't like any of the listed options, you can always write in your preferred choice.
Last year, many people wrote in unexpected names for city council.

Synset Examples

Many voters wrote in the names of strangers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: write-in - a vote for an unlisted candidate by writing their name on the ballot
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02467230-v
Synset POS: v
