to feel for

1. VERB

/ fiːl fɔr / (AmE), / fiːl fə / (BrE)
to experience sympathy or compassion for someone who is suffering; to empathize with
to sympathize or empathize with someone's suffering

Examples

I really feel for you after hearing about your loss.
She could see how upset he was and genuinely felt for him.
We all feel for the victims of the disaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sympathize - to share or understand another's feelings
  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another

2. VERB

/ fiːl fər / (AmE), / fiːl fə / (BrE)
to experience empathy or sympathy for someone; to understand and share another person's feelings, especially of distress or hardship
to empathize or sympathize with someone

Examples

I really feel for you and hope things get better soon.
Many people feel for those affected by the natural disaster.
She felt for her colleague, who was going through a difficult divorce.

Synset Examples

I really feel for you during this tough time.
I can feel for anyone facing such challenges.
She can feel for her friend struggling with loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sympathize - to share or understand another person's feelings
  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share another person's emotions
