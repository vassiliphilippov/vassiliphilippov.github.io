to barter away

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑɹ.tɚ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.tə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to give something up by trading it for something else, especially when this is seen as a loss or sacrifice
give up by trading; relinquish in exchange

Examples

He regretted having bartered away his family heirloom for such a small sum.
They bartered away their only means of transportation for food during the crisis.
Sometimes we barter away our free time for promises of career advancement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade away - to give something up by trading it for something else

Etymology

From 'barter' (to exchange goods without money) + 'away,' indicating removal or loss.
