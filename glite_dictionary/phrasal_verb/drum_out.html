to drum out

1. VERB

/ drʌm aʊt / (AmE), / drʌm aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a position or organization, often publicly or with disgrace
force someone to leave a position or group

Examples

After the scandal, he was drummed out of the company with no ceremony.
The officer was drummed out of the army for misconduct.
She feared being drummed out of the organization if her secret was discovered.

Synset Examples

The chairman was ousted after he misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job
  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Antonym: to appoint - to assign to a position or office

Etymology

Originally from the military practice of expelling someone to the beat of a drum.

2. VERB

/ drʌm aʊt / (AmE), / drʌm aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a group, organization, or position, especially in a formal or public way
expel someone from a group or organization

Examples

After the incident, the team decided to drum out the player responsible for the breach.
He was drummed out of the army for his misconduct.
The organization may drum out members who fail to follow its code of ethics.

Synset Examples

They decided to drum out anyone involved in the fraud.
The scandal forced the company to drum out several employees.
The committee will drum out any member who violates rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave, especially from an organization
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from a position or job

Etymology

From the literal use of a drum in military ceremonies when dismissing someone from service.
