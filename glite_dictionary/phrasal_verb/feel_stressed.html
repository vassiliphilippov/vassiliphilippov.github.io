to feel stressed

1. VERB

/ fil strɛst / (AmE), / fiːl strɛst / (BrE)
to experience anxiety or pressure in response to challenging situations
experience anxiety or pressure

Examples

Whenever I have too much work, I feel stressed and find it hard to concentrate.
She tends to feel stressed before important exams or interviews.
He admitted that he felt stressed during the move to a new city.

Synset Examples

What with all the deadlines, I'm feeling stressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel anxious - to feel nervous or uneasy about something
  • Antonym: feel relaxed - to be free from tension or anxiety
  • Degree Higher: feel overwhelmed - to feel unable to cope with the demands placed on you
  • Degree Lower: feel uneasy - to feel slightly uncomfortable or worried
