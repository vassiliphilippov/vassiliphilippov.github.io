to bucket along

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌkɪt əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪt əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to move quickly or at high speed, especially in a vehicle or on foot
move quickly; rush
informal british

Examples

The old van bucketed along the country road, rattling over every bump.
Pedestrians had to bucket along to catch the early train.
As the storm approached, we bucketed along the path to reach shelter in time.

Synset Examples

He rushed down the hall to receive his guests
The cars raced down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Synonym: race - to move or go very fast
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move slowly or waste time
