to lie low

1. VERB

/ laɪ ˈloʊ / (AmE), / laɪ ˈləʊ / (BrE)
to avoid attracting attention to oneself; to keep out of sight or be inconspicuous, especially to avoid trouble.
avoid attracting attention; remain inconspicuous

Examples

After the scandal broke, the politician decided to lie low for a while.
We need to lie low until things calm down before contacting anyone.
She was advised to lie low and not draw any more attention to herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep a low profile - to avoid attracting attention
  • Antonym: to stand out - to attract attention, be conspicuous

2. VERB

/ laɪ loʊ / (AmE), / laɪ ləʊ / (BrE)
to keep out of sight or avoid attracting attention in order to escape detection, especially by the authorities
avoid attracting attention to escape detection

Examples

After the robbery, they decided to lie low until things calmed down.
She had to lie low to avoid being recognized by the press.
The suspect is believed to be lying low in another city to evade capture.

Synset Examples

After we knock off that liquor store we'll have to lay low for a while

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - to put oneself in a place where one cannot be seen or found

3. VERB

/ laɪ loʊ / (AmE), / laɪ ləʊ / (BrE)
to keep out of sight or avoid attracting attention, especially to escape trouble or remain unnoticed
avoid attention; stay inconspicuous

Examples

After the incident, he decided to lie low until things settled down.
It's wise to lie low if you want to avoid any more trouble from them.
She chose to lie low rather than speak publicly about the controversy.

Synset Examples
