to sling

1. VERB

/ slɪŋ / (AmE), / slɪŋ / (BrE)
to throw or toss (something) casually or with a swift motion, especially with reference to small objects like keys.
throw or toss casually

Examples

She asked him to sling the keys over to her from across the patio.
He would often sling the keys onto the kitchen counter after coming home from work.
Don't sling the keys like that—you might lose them under the sofa.

Synset Examples

He forgot to sling the keys to his neighbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toss - to throw something lightly or casually
  • Synonym: throw - to send something through the air by force of the arm

Etymology

Old Norse *slyngva* (to hurl).
