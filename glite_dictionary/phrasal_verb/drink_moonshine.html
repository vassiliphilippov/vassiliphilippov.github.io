to drink moonshine

1. VERB

/ drɪŋk ˈmuːnʃaɪn / (AmE), / drɪŋk ˈmuːnʃaɪn / (BrE)
to consume homemade or illegally made distilled alcohol, typically of uncertain quality or legality
consume illicit homemade alcohol

Examples

Many rural residents used to drink moonshine during times of prohibition.
He got sick after trying to drink moonshine at the party last night.
Some people still secretly drink moonshine in remote areas.

Synset Examples

He reminisced about drinking moonshine with friends last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshine - illegally produced distilled alcohol

Etymology

'Moonshine' from early 19th-century American English, meaning 'illicitly distilled liquor', referring to its secret production by moonlight.
