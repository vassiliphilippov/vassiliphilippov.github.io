to open fire

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊpən ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to begin shooting a weapon, especially guns, at people or things
to begin shooting a weapon

Examples

The soldiers were ordered to open fire as soon as the enemy approached.
The police did not open fire until they were threatened.
Without warning, the rebels suddenly opened fire on the convoy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cease fire - to stop firing weapons
