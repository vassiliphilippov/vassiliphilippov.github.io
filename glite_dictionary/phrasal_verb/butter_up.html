to butter up

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʌtə ʌp / (BrE)
to flatter someone excessively with the intention of gaining their favor or something in return
flatter insincerely for personal gain

Examples

If you want that promotion, don’t just butter up the boss—work hard too.
She tried to butter up her parents so they would let her go to the party.
They spent the whole meeting buttering up the new manager with compliments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, especially to gain favor

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌtɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʌtə ʌp / (BrE)
to flatter someone excessively in order to gain their favor or approval
flatter to gain favor
informal

Examples

She tried to butter up her teacher to get a better grade.
There's no need to butter up your boss; your work speaks for itself.
He always butters up his parents before asking for money.

Synset Examples

She tried to butter the cony to win him over.
She tried to butter up her teacher for a better grade.
You shouldn't have to butter up your boss for recognition.
He always knows how to butter up his parents for money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, especially for personal gain
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval towards someone or something

Etymology

From the literal act of buttering bread, suggesting smoothing or making something more appealing.
