to shift someone's schedule

1. VERB

/ ʃɪft ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈskɛdʒuːl / (AmE), / ʃɪft ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈʃɛdjuːl / (BrE)
to change the time or arrangement of someone's planned activities or appointments
change planned times or arrangements

Examples

I had to shift my schedule so I could attend my daughter's recital.
The manager asked if I could shift my schedule to cover the evening shift.
He often needs to shift his schedule to accommodate last-minute changes.

Synset Examples

She had to shift her schedule to accommodate the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reschedule - to change the time of an event to a different time or date
