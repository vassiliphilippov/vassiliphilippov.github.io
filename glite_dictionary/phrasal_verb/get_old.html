to get old

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt oʊld / (AmE), / ɡɛt əʊld / (BrE)
(informal) To become tiresome, uninteresting, or annoying through repetition or prolonged exposure.
become tiresome or uninteresting
informal

Examples

Her jokes about the weather get old after a while.
The same excuses from the manager are really starting to get old.
This show was funny at first, but it does get old quickly.

Synset Examples

His constant dickery is getting old fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear thin - To become less effective or acceptable through overuse
