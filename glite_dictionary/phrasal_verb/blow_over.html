to blow over

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / bləʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an argument, incident, or difficulty) to go away or be forgotten with the passage of time; to gradually diminish in importance or intensity.
to disappear or be forgotten gradually

Examples

Everyone hopes the controversy will blow over soon.
Tensions can be high at first, but they usually blow over with time.
Don't worry about the argument; it will blow over eventually.

Synset Examples

The pain eventually passed off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - to come to an end or stop happening
  • Synonym: to subside - to become less intense or severe

2. VERB

/ bloʊ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / bləʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an argument, crisis, or difficult situation) to gradually go away, become less serious, or be forgotten.
become less serious or disappear (crisis, argument, etc.)

Examples

I'm hoping this controversy will blow over before the end of the month.
She waited for the argument to blow over before speaking to her friend again.
Once the excitement had blown over, things returned to normal.

Synset Examples

The scandal will eventually blow over.
Just wait; this issue will blow over soon.
I hope the storm blows over quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subside - to become less intense or severe
  • Antonym: to escalate - to become more intense or serious
