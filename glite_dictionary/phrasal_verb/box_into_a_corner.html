to box into a corner

1. VERB

/ bɑks ˈɪntu ə ˈkɔrnər / (AmE), / bɒks ˈɪntu ə ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
to put someone in a difficult position with few or no options left; to limit someone's choices or ability to escape a problem
trap or restrict someone's options

Examples

He realized he had boxed himself into a corner with his promises.
Negotiators feared that strict demands would box them into a corner.
By refusing to compromise, she eventually boxed herself into a corner.

Synset Examples

Don't box yourself into a corner with that decision.
Avoid boxing yourself into a corner during negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - to catch someone in a difficult or inescapable situation
  • Synonym: paint into a corner - to put oneself or someone else in a difficult situation with limited options
