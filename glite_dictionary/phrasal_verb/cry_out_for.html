to cry out for

1. VERB

/ kraɪ aʊt fɔr / (AmE), / kraɪ aʊt fə / (BrE)
to be in urgent or desperate need of something
need badly or urgently

Examples

The city cries out for improved public transportation.
This situation cries out for immediate action from the authorities.
Many communities are crying out for clean drinking water.

Synset Examples

This question cries out for an answer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to require - to need something for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: lack - to be without something that is needed
