to look after

1. VERB

/ lʊk ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / lʊk ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to keep under observation or assume responsibility for something or someone, especially to ensure proper functioning or safety
supervise or take responsibility for

Examples

The committee will look after all aspects of the project.
Could you look after my suitcase while I go to the restroom?
She was hired to look after the company's financial records.

Synset Examples

The lawyer has to take the initiative and look after all the proceedings of the court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to supervise - to oversee an activity or people
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or oversee
