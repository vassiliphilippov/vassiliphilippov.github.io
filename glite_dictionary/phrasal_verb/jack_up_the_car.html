to jack up the car

1. VERB

/ dʒæk ʌp ðə kɑr / (AmE), / dʒæk ʌp ðə kɑː / (BrE)
to lift a car off the ground using a jack, typically to carry out repairs or maintenance
lift a car using a jack

Examples

The mechanic had to jack up the car to replace the flat tire.
Always engage the parking brake before you jack up the car for safety.
She jacked up the car to check the brakes in the driveway.

Synset Examples

The mechanic had to jack up the car for repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - raise to a higher position

Etymology

From 'jack', meaning a mechanical lifting device, and 'up', expressing direction.
