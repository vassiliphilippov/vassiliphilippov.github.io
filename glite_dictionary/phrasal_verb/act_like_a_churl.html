to act like a churl

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə tʃɝl / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə tʃɜːl / (BrE)
to behave in a rude, boorish, or selfish manner
behave in a rude or selfish way

Examples

Whenever he is tired, he tends to act like a churl and snap at everyone.
You shouldn't act like a churl just because things aren't going your way.
Despite being the host, she acted like a churl and ignored her guests.

Synset Examples

He was acting like a churl, refusing to share his toys.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: act like a gentleman - behave in a courteous way
  • Synonym: boorish - rude and insensitive

Etymology

From 'churl' meaning a rude or boorish person; 'churl' from Old English 'ceorl' (freeman, common man).
