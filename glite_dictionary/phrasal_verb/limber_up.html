to limber up

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪmbɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbə ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare one's body for physical activity by stretching or warming up the muscles
prepare the body for exercise by stretching

Examples

It's important to limber up before running to avoid injury.
The dancers spent ten minutes limbering up before rehearsal started.
Every morning, he limbers up with a series of stretches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warm up - to prepare for physical activity by gentle exercise

Etymology

From 'limber' (supple, flexible), of uncertain origin, + 'up' (particle indicating preparation or increase).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪmbər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbə ʌp / (BrE)
to stretch and prepare your muscles with gentle exercises before taking part in physical activity
prepare muscles before exercise

Examples

It's important to limber up before running to reduce the risk of injury.
The team spent ten minutes limbering up on the field before the match.
She always limbers up with some yoga stretches before her workout.

Synset Examples

I need to limber up before my yoga class.
You should limber up to avoid injuries while exercising.
The coach had us limber up with some stretches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warm up - to prepare the body for physical activity by gentle exercise or movement

Etymology

From 'limber' (flexible, supple), origin uncertain, plus the adverb 'up'.
