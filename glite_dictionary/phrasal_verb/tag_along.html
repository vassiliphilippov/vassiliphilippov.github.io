to tag along

1. VERB

/ tæɡ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / tæɡ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to go somewhere with someone, especially when you have not been invited or are not needed
go with someone, often uninvited

Examples

Do you mind if I tag along to the market with you?
Little kids often want to tag along with their older siblings wherever they go.
He didn't invite me, but I decided to tag along anyway.

Synset Examples

my younger brother often tagged along when I went out with my friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to follow - to go or come after a person or thing
  • Synonym: join - to go somewhere in order to be with someone

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tæɡ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / tæɡ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to accompany someone, often informally or without a specific invitation
accompany someone informally
informal

Examples

Do you mind if I tag along with you to the store?
Some friends decided to tag along for the road trip at the last minute.
My sister loves to tag along whenever I go to the park.

Synset Examples

I always let my little brother tag along.
He decided to tag along on their hiking trip.
Can I tag along with you to the concert?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come along - to accompany or go somewhere with someone
