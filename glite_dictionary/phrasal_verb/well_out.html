to well out

1. VERB

/ wɛl aʊt / (AmE), / wɛl aʊt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or emotion) to flow out freely and in large amounts
flow out freely and in large amounts

Examples

Tears began to well out of her eyes as she listened to the sad news.
Blood welled out from the small cut on his finger.
Memories welled out of him as he looked through the old photographs.

Synset Examples

Tears streamed down her face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour out - to flow quickly and in large quantities
