to fit out

1. VERB

/ fɪt aʊt / (AmE), / fɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to provide someone with clothes or to dress someone in suitable attire
to provide with clothes; dress

Examples

The charity helped fit out the refugees with warm winter clothing.
We had to fit out the children for their new school uniforms before the term started.
Every cadet was fitted out with a full set of ceremonial attire for the parade.

Synset Examples

Parents must feed and dress their child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put clothes on someone
  • Antonym: undress - to remove clothes from someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00047662-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɪt aʊt / (AmE), / fɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to supply someone or something with all necessary equipment or furnishings, especially for a particular purpose
equip or furnish for a purpose

Examples

The company will fit out the new office with modern furniture and technology.
They spent weeks fitting out the yacht before the long voyage.
All the soldiers were fitted out with uniforms and equipment before deployment.

Synset Examples

The expedition was equipped with proper clothing, food, and other necessities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equip - to supply with necessary items for a particular purpose

Synset POS: v
