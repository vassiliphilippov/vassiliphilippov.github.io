to rock the stage

1. VERB

/ rɑk ðə steɪdʒ / (AmE), / rɒk ðə steɪdʒ / (BrE)
to give an energetic and successful performance, especially in front of an audience
perform energetically and successfully on stage
informal

Examples

The singer managed to rock the stage despite the technical difficulties.
Last night, the dance group really rocked the stage with their performance.
She's known to rock the stage every time she performs live.

Synset Examples

That band really knows how to rock the stage!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring down the house - to receive enthusiastic applause or a great reaction from the audience
