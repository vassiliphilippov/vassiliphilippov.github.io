to cooccur with

1. VERB

/ koʊ.əˈkɝː wɪθ / (AmE), / kəʊ.əˈkɜː wɪð / (BrE)
(of two or more things) to happen or appear together or at the same time, especially in speech or writing.
occur together or at the same time

Examples

Certain words often cooccur with each other in natural language texts.
Symptoms of the disorder frequently cooccur with signs of anxiety.
Economic downturns may cooccur with political instability in some regions.

Synset Examples

The word ‘hot’ tends to cooccur with ‘cold’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coincide with - to happen at the same time as something else
  • Antonym: differ from - to be unlike or not the same as

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'co-' (meaning 'together') and 'occur', from Latin 'occurrere' (to occur).
