to start to sag

1. VERB

/ stɑrt tə sæɡ / (AmE), / stɑːt tə sæɡ / (BrE)
to begin to droop or hang down loosely, typically due to weight or wear
begin to droop or hang loosely

Examples

After several years, the old bookshelf began to start to sag in the middle.
You’ll notice the mattress will start to sag if it isn’t properly supported.
The heavy fruit caused the branches to start to sag under their weight.

Synset Examples

The curtains started to sag after years of hanging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sag - to sink, droop, or bend downward by weight or pressure
