to fudge one's way out

1. VERB

/ fʌdʒ wʌnz weɪ aʊt / (AmE), / fʌdʒ wʌnz weɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to avoid responsibility or escape a difficult situation by using deceitful or evasive tactics
evade responsibility by being deceitful

Examples

He managed to fudge his way out of the meeting by pretending he was ill.
Whenever there's an issue at work, she tries to fudge her way out instead of taking responsibility.
They couldn't fudge their way out of the situation when the truth came out.

Synset Examples

She always tries to fudge her way out of trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - avoid or escape, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • Antonym: face up to - to confront or accept responsibility

Etymology

From 'fudge' (to falsify or evade), origin uncertain, possibly from dialectal English.
