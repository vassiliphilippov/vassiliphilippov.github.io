to tie in

1. VERB

/ taɪ ɪn / (AmE), / taɪ ɪn / (BrE)
to be connected or related in a relevant or meaningful way
be connected or related

Examples

Her comments tie in nicely with the main theme of the book.
The new evidence ties in with what we already know about the case.
These two ideas tie in together to form a coherent argument.

Synset Examples

This ties in closely with his earlier remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relate - to have a connection with something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02741981-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ taɪ ɪn / (AmE), / taɪ ɪn / (BrE)
to show that something is connected logically or causally to something else
show a logical or causal connection

Examples

The professor tried to tie in current events with the lesson.
Her research findings tie in closely with the existing theories.
Can you tie in this information with the case?

Synset Examples

I cannot connect these two pieces of evidence in my mind
colligate these facts
I cannot relate these events at all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relate - to show or make a connection between two things
  • Synonym: to connect - to show or establish a relationship or link
