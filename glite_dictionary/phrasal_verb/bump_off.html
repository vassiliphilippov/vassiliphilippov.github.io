to bump off

1. VERB

/ bʌmp ɔf / (AmE), / bʌmp ɒf / (BrE)
to kill someone deliberately, especially in a criminal or premeditated way
kill someone deliberately (informal)
informal

Examples

The gangster threatened to bump off anyone who talked to the police.
They plotted to bump off the witness before the trial could begin.
Rumor has it that a rival family bumped off the mob boss last night.
