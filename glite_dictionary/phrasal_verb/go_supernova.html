to go supernova

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ suːpərˈnoʊvə / (AmE), / ɡəʊ suːpəˈnəʊvə / (BrE)
to explode and become a supernova (of a star); to suddenly become extremely intense, explosive, or dramatic (figurative)
to explode as a supernova; become extremely intense

Examples

Scientists predict that Betelgeuse may go supernova within the next hundred thousand years.
After months of simmering resentment, their argument finally went supernova during the meeting.
The band’s popularity seemed to go supernova after the release of their latest album.

Synset Examples

The star is expected to go supernova in a few million years.
Their relationship seemed to go supernova after the vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - to burst or shatter violently and noisily as a result of rapid combustion, excessive internal pressure, or other process
