to call forth

1. VERB

/ kɔl fɔrθ / (AmE), / kɔːl fɔːθ / (BrE)
to cause something to appear, occur, or be produced, especially as a reaction or response
provoke or evoke a response

Examples

The solemn music seemed to call forth memories of loved ones lost.
His bold actions called forth widespread admiration from his colleagues.
The leader's speech called forth a passionate response from the audience.

Synset Examples

Her behavior provoked a quarrel between the couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring to mind or elicit a feeling, memory, or response
  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a reaction, especially a strong or unwelcome one
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01650408-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɔl fɔrθ / (AmE), / kɔːl fɔːθ / (BrE)
to bring something into existence or action, often as if by magic or strong influence
summon or bring into action

Examples

The leader's speech was intended to call forth courage in his followers.
The legend says the ritual can call forth ancient spirits.
Her poem called forth strong emotions from everyone who heard it.

Synset Examples

raise the specter of unemployment
he conjured wild birds in the air
call down the spirits from the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summon - to order someone to come or to call up for action
  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
