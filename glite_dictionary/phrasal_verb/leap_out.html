to leap out

1. VERB

/ lip aʊt / (AmE), / liːp aʊt / (BrE)
to be very noticeable or obvious, especially in a way that attracts attention
be highly noticeable or obvious

Examples

His bright red shirt really leaps out in the crowd.
One detail leaped out at me as I read the report.
The headline leaped out from the front page of the newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be easily seen or noticed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02680375-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lip aʊt / (AmE), / liːp aʊt / (BrE)
to jump out suddenly from a hiding place, especially to surprise someone
jump suddenly from hiding to surprise

Examples

The cat leapt out from behind the door and startled me.
As we passed by the hallway, someone leaped out and yelled 'surprise!'
Bandits leapt out of the shadows as the travelers approached.

Synset Examples

The attackers leapt out from the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jump out - to spring or move out suddenly from a place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01970508-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ liːp aʊt / (AmE), / liːp aʊt / (BrE)
to be very noticeable or prominent, or to become obvious quickly
to stand out, be very noticeable

Examples

Her brilliant smile made her leap out in any photograph.
One mistake really leaps out from the report.
The headline leapt out at me as I glanced at the newspaper.

Synset Examples

Her bright dress made her leap out in the crowd.
The answer seemed to leap out at me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be very noticeable or conspicuous
