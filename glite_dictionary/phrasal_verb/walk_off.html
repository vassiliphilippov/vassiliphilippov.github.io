to walk off

1. VERB

/ wɑk ɔf / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒf / (BrE)
to take something without permission; to steal.
take or steal without permission

Examples

Someone tried to walk off with my bag while I was distracted.
The criminal attempted to walk off with the store's cash register.
She noticed someone about to walk off with her umbrella at the café.

Synset Examples

he walked off with my wife!
The thief walked off with my gold watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take another person's property without permission
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02327429-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɑk ɔf / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation, often suddenly or in a way that shows annoyance or disapproval
leave suddenly or abruptly

Examples

After the argument, Tom walked off without saying a word.
The referee walked over to the player, who simply walked off the field.
She got upset and walked off in the middle of the meeting.

Synset Examples

The actor walked off before he got his cue
I got annoyed and just walked off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm out - to leave a place angrily and suddenly
  • Synonym: to walk away - to leave or abandon a situation or place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02014079-v
Synset POS: v
