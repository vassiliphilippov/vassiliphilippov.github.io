to whittle away

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪt̬l əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.əl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually reduce something by cutting away small pieces; to diminish bit by bit.
gradually reduce or cut away

Examples

Over the years, constant rain had whittled away the stone steps.
They whittled away the block of wood until only a thin sliver remained.
Government spending is slowly whittling away the country’s savings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less

Etymology

From 'whittle' (to carve or cut small bits) + 'away' (to remove).

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪtl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to spend time doing something in a relaxed and leisurely way, especially to pass time pleasantly or idly
spend time in a relaxed, leisurely way

Examples

During the long summer afternoons, she would whittle away the hours reading novels on the porch.
Many retirees whittle away their days gardening or chatting with friends at the café.
He liked to whittle away his free time playing chess in the park.

Synset Examples

He likes to whittle away his free time by carving wood.

Etymology

From 'whittle' (to carve wood) plus 'away'; originally referring to passing time by carving or doing something leisurely.
