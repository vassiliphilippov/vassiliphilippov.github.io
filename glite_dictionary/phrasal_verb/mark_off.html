to mark off

1. VERB

/ mɑrk ɔf / (AmE), / mɑːk ɒf / (BrE)
to indicate that something has been checked, noted, or completed, especially by placing a mark next to it
indicate checked/completed with a mark

Examples

I will mark off the completed tasks on this checklist.
She marked off each item as she packed it in her suitcase.
Once you finish each chapter, remember to mark off your progress in the workbook.

Synset Examples

Please check each name on the list
tick off the items
mark off the units

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to check off - to indicate by checking or ticking
  • Synonym: to tick off - to indicate by ticking or checking
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00664100-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɑrk ɔf / (AmE), / mɑːk ɒf / (BrE)
to draw lines or set boundaries to separate or define an area or section
set boundaries or delimit

Examples

The construction workers used tape to mark off the area for safety.
Please mark off each completed item on the checklist as you go.
They marked off plots of land for each family at the community garden.

Synset Examples

mark out the territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demarcate - to set the limits or boundaries of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00235426-v
Synset POS: v
