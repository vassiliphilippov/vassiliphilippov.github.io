to eat away at

1. VERB

/ it əˈweɪ æt / (AmE), / iːt əˈweɪ æt / (BrE)
to gradually damage, erode, or destroy something over time, often used figuratively
gradually damage or erode

Examples

Decades of neglect began to eat away at the old wooden bridge.
Doubt continued to eat away at his sense of certainty.
Constant criticism can eat away at a person's self-esteem over time.

Synset Examples

Stress will eat away at your health if ignored.
Negative thoughts can eat away at your confidence.
Worries can eat away at your happiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away or be worn away
  • Degree Higher: to destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or works
  • Synonym: to wear away - to gradually make something disappear or become thinner by continuously using or rubbing it
