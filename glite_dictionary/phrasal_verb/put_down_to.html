to put down to

1. VERB

/ pʊt daʊn tə / (AmE), / pʊt daʊn tʊ / (BrE)
to consider that something is caused by a particular factor or reason; to attribute something to a specific cause
attribute something to a cause

Examples

Many experts put his rapid recovery down to his positive attitude.
She put her success down to years of hard work and determination.
The teacher put the students' confusion down to the unclear instructions.

Synset Examples

He put the delay down to bad weather.
The increase in sales can be put down to new marketing strategies.
Her success can be put down to hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attribute to - to regard something as being caused by
