to do well

1. VERB

/ duː wɛl / (AmE), / duː wɛl / (BrE)
to act in a way that is wise, prudent, or beneficial for oneself or others; to make a good decision or take appropriate action
act wisely or in one's best interest

Examples

You would do well to listen to her advice before making a decision.
He did well to apologize quickly after the argument.
They will do well to start saving money early for their retirement.

Synset Examples

You will do well to arrive on time tomorrow!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do wisely - to act with wisdom or prudence
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02729439-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ du wɛl / (AmE), / duː wɛl / (BrE)
to perform satisfactorily or be successful in an activity, job, or situation
perform satisfactorily; succeed

Examples

If you study hard, you will do well on the exam.
Despite limited resources, the company managed to do well last year.
After her surgery, she was doing well and recovering quickly.

Synset Examples

She said she was doing well despite the challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
