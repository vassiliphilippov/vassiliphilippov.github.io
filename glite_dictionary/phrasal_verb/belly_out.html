to belly out

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛli aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɛli aʊt / (BrE)
to swell or bulge outward, especially in a rounded shape
bulge or swell outward

Examples

The sails began to belly out as the wind picked up.
Her jacket bellied out in the strong breeze.
When the balloon was filled with air, it started to belly out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulge - to swell or stick out in a rounded shape
  • Antonym: to deflate - to lose fullness or swelling; to contract

Etymology

From the noun 'belly' (middle English, from Old English 'belig') meaning stomach, and the preposition 'out'.
