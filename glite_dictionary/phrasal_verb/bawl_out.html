to bawl out

1. VERB

/ bɔl aʊt / (AmE), / bɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to scold or reprimand someone loudly and severely
scold or reprimand loudly

Examples

The coach bawled out the team for their poor performance.
She was bawled out by her boss for missing the deadline.
It's never pleasant to be bawled out in front of your colleagues.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to angrily reprimand someone
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone

2. VERB

/ bɔl aʊt / (AmE), / bɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to reprimand or scold someone loudly and severely
reprimand or scold loudly

Examples

The manager bawled out the staff for not meeting the deadline.
I was bawled out by my parents when I got home late.
She bawled out her brother for making a mess in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

She bawled out her team for their poor performance.
He got bawled out by his boss yesterday.
The coach bawled him out for missing practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to angrily criticize or reprimand someone, especially a child
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From 'bawl' (to shout or cry loudly), likely of imitative origin, plus 'out' as an intensifier.
