to cloister away

1. VERB

/ ˈklɔɪstər əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to keep oneself or someone in a quiet, isolated place away from society; to withdraw from the world for privacy or contemplation
to isolate oneself from society

Examples

After losing his job, he decided to cloister away in his apartment and avoid his friends for a while.
The novelist would often cloister away in her study to focus on her writing without distractions.
They cloistered away the rare manuscripts in a secure room, away from public view.

Synset Examples

The monks choose to cloister away from the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seclude - to keep someone away from others

Etymology

From 'cloister', originally meaning a covered walk in a monastery or convent, from Latin 'claustrum' (enclosure).
