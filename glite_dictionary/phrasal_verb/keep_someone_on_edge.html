to keep someone on edge

1. VERB

/ kip ˈsʌmˌwʌn ɑn ɛdʒ / (AmE), / kiːp ˈsʌmwʌn ɒn ɛdʒ / (BrE)
to make someone feel excited, nervous, or uncertain, especially by creating suspense or tension
cause suspense or nervousness

Examples

The plot twists in the novel kept me on edge until the very end.
His unpredictable behavior always keeps his coworkers on edge during meetings.
The live news updates kept viewers on edge throughout the night.

Synset Examples

The movie was thrilling and kept us on edge.
The suspense in the movie kept us on edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep someone in suspense - cause someone to be uncertain or anxious while waiting for information
