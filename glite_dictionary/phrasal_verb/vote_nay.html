to vote nay

1. VERB

/ voʊt neɪ / (AmE), / vəʊt neɪ / (BrE)
to vote against a proposal or motion, especially by saying 'nay' in a formal setting
vote against a proposal

Examples

During the meeting, several members chose to vote nay on the budget proposal.
If you disagree with the motion, please vote nay when asked.
She surprised everyone when she decided to vote nay despite supporting it earlier.

Synset Examples

He voted nay on the proposed changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vote aye - to vote in favor of a proposal or motion
  • Synonym: to oppose - to disagree with or resist a proposal

Etymology

From 'vote' meaning to express a choice, and 'nay', an old word for 'no', especially in parliamentary or formal proceedings.
