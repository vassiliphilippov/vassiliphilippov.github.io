to dip into

1. VERB

/ dɪp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / dɪp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to read only certain passages or parts of a book, magazine, or other written material rather than reading it all the way through
read parts of a text selectively

Examples

She likes to dip into different cookbooks to find inspiration for dinner.
I sometimes dip into old novels when I have a few minutes to spare.
He dipped into the report just enough to get the main points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skim - to read quickly to get the main ideas
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00627478-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / dɪp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to use or take a small part of something, especially gradually or temporarily
use a small part of something

Examples

She had to dip into her emergency fund to cover the repairs.
I sometimes dip into my old journals for inspiration.
We try not to dip into our savings unless it's absolutely necessary.

Synset Examples

She decided to dip into her book collection.
I’ll dip into my savings for the trip.
He decided to dip into the paint for a new project.
