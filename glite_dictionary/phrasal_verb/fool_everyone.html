to fool everyone

1. VERB

/ fuːl ˈɛvriˌwʌn / (AmE), / fuːl ˈɛvriˌwʌn / (BrE)
to deceive or trick all the people present, often by making them believe something that is not true
deceive or trick all people present

Examples

He tried to fool everyone with his story, but eventually the truth came out.
You can't fool everyone all the time; someone will catch on sooner or later.
By using fake credentials, she managed to fool everyone at the conference.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone in order to achieve something
  • Synonym: to mislead - to give a wrong idea or impression to someone
