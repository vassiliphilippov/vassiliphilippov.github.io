to pull out of

1. VERB

/ pʊl aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to withdraw from a situation, activity, or commitment; to decide not to participate or to leave something that was previously joined or entered
withdraw from a situation or commitment

Examples

Due to budget cuts, the company decided to pull out of the partnership.
Several countries threatened to pull out of the trade agreement.
She chose to pull out of the race after spraining her ankle.

Synset Examples

She plans to pull out of the project if it fails.
He decided to pull out of the competition last minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to remove oneself from an activity or situation
  • Antonym: join - to become a member or participant
