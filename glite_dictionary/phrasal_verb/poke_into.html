to poke into

1. VERB

/ poʊk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pəʊk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to examine physically or investigate something, especially by prodding or probing.
physically or closely investigate (often by prodding)

Examples

The scientist used a stick to poke into the abandoned burrow.
She didn’t want anyone to poke into her personal affairs.
They warned the children not to poke into unknown holes in the ground.

Synset Examples

probe an anthill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - to physically examine or explore with a tool or device
  • Synonym: to inspect - to examine closely or officially
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01229439-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ poʊk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pəʊk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to enter a place briefly or casually, often out of curiosity
enter briefly or casually
informal

Examples

We decided to poke into a small cafe to escape the rain.
She likes to poke into antique shops when she travels.
He poked into the gym to see if anyone he knew was there.

Synset Examples

We poked into the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pop into - to visit or enter briefly, usually casually
