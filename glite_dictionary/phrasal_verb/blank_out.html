to blank out

1. VERB

/ blæŋk aʊt / (AmE), / blæŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to be suddenly unable to remember or think of something; to experience a mental block
temporarily forget something

Examples

I completely blanked out during the test and forgot everything I had studied.
Whenever she feels nervous, she tends to blank out on important facts.
He blanked out his address when asked by the police.

Synset Examples

I'm drawing a blank
You are blocking the name of your first wife!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forget - to be unable to recall or remember

2. VERB

/ blæŋk aʊt / (AmE), / blæŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or obscure words or information in a document, often for political or sensitive reasons
remove or obscure text in a document

Examples

The classified sections of the report were blanked out before publication.
Some names in the document have been blanked out for security purposes.
Officials decided to blank out sensitive information from the files.

Synset Examples

several lines in the report were blanked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to redact - edit or prepare official documents, often by removing sensitive information
  • Synonym: censor - examine and suppress unacceptable parts of publications
