to break the story

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə ˈstɔri / (AmE), / breɪk ðə ˈstɔːri / (BrE)
to be the first to report or publish news about an event
report news first

Examples

The newspaper was proud to break the story ahead of all its competitors.
She stayed up all night so she could break the story in the morning edition.
Several media outlets tried to break the story as soon as credible details emerged.

Synset Examples

Only a few journalists were able to break the story.
She worked late to break the story on time.
The journalist was eager to break the story first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoop - a news story reported or published before anyone else
