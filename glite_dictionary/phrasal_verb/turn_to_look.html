to turn to look

1. VERB

/ tɝn tə lʊk / (AmE), / tɜːn tə lʊk / (BrE)
to change direction or move one's body in order to look at someone or something
move to face and look at someone or something

Examples

As soon as he heard the noise, he turned to look behind him.
The crowd turned to look when the doors burst open.
She turned to look at the beautiful sunset before leaving the room.

Synset Examples

When she entered the scene, everyone turned to look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance - to look quickly at something or someone
  • Degree Higher: stare - to look at something or someone for a long time
