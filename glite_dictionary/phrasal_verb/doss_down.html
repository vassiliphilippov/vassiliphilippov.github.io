to doss down

1. VERB

/ dɑs daʊn / (AmE), / dɒs daʊn / (BrE)
to sleep somewhere, especially in a place that is not your usual bed or not very comfortable
sleep temporarily or in an unusual place
informal british

Examples

We had to doss down in the living room because all the hotels were full.
If you need a place to stay, you can doss down on my sofa tonight.
Backpackers often doss down wherever they can find a dry spot.

Synset Examples

You can crash here, though it's not very comfortable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - to sleep somewhere temporarily, often unexpectedly or informally

Etymology

From British slang 'doss' (to sleep or lodge), of uncertain origin, perhaps from Spanish 'dormir' (to sleep).
