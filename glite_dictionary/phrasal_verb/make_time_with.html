to make time with

1. VERB

/ meɪk taɪm wɪð / (AmE), / meɪk taɪm wɪð / (BrE)
to deliberately spend time with someone, especially giving them quality attention
spend quality time with someone

Examples

During his busy schedule, he always tries to make time with his family.
She encouraged him to make time with his old friends after moving back to town.
Even on business trips, he would make time with his children over video calls.

Synset Examples

She wants to make time with old friends this summer.
They plan to make time with their kids this weekend.
He tried to make time with her over coffee.
