to get back

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt bæk / (AmE), / ɡɛt bæk / (BrE)
to recover possession of something or someone that was lost or taken away
recover something or someone lost

Examples

She was thrilled to get back her lost necklace after so many years.
If you contact customer service, you might be able to get back your payment.
After hours of searching, we finally managed to get back our dog.

Synset Examples

We got back the money after we threatened to sue the company
He got back his son from the kidnappers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retrieve - to get something back after it has been lost or taken away
  • Antonym: to lose - to be deprived of or unable to find something or someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01113995-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt bæk / (AmE), / ɡɛt bæk / (BrE)
to take revenge on someone or to retaliate for something they have done
take revenge; retaliate

Examples

After he lied to me, I was determined to get back at him.
She plotted for months to get back at her coworker for the prank.
It's not worth your energy to get back at people who wrong you.

Synset Examples

I cannot accept the defeat — I want to get even

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - the act of hurting someone in return for something they have done
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01156228-v
Synset POS: v

take revenge

Although tempted to get back at them, he chose to move on instead.

I finally settled with my old enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retaliate - to take revenge for a wrong or injury
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling anger or resentment against someone for an offense
