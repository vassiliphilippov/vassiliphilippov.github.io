to tee up

1. VERB

/ ti ʌp / (AmE), / tiː ʌp / (BrE)
to make detailed arrangements or preparations for something so that it can happen
make arrangements or preparations

Examples

The marketing team will tee up the campaign launch for next month.
Let's tee up a meeting with the client to discuss the proposal.
She spent all morning teeing up the project so things would run smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • Synonym: to arrange - to organize or make plans for

Etymology

From the golf term 'tee up', meaning to place the ball on a tee ready for play.

2. VERB

/ ti ʌp / (AmE), / tiː ʌp / (BrE)
to place (a golf ball) on a tee in preparation for playing a shot
place a golf ball on a tee

Examples

He carefully teed up his ball before taking the first swing.
Before every drive, she tees up her ball and takes a deep breath.
The caddie teed up the ball for the golfer on the first hole.

Synset Examples

tee golf balls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tee - a small stand used to support a stationary ball in sports like golf

Etymology

From 'tee' (the stand used to hold a golf ball) plus 'up'.

3. VERB

/ ti ʌp / (AmE), / tiː ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare or arrange for something to happen, typically in a way that facilitates further action
prepare or arrange for something

Examples

Before the conference, please tee up all the necessary documents for the panelists.
The marketing team will tee up a campaign launch for the new product.
Could you tee up some talking points for tomorrow's interview?

Synset Examples

Can you tee up the next speaker for us?
We need to tee up a meeting to discuss the project.
She will tee up the presentation for tomorrow's meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set up - to organize or arrange something so that it is ready
  • Synonym: to arrange - to organize or make plans for something
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide or announce that something planned will not take place

Etymology

Originally from golf, where the ball is placed on a tee before being struck.
