to give off

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to produce or emit something, especially a smell, light, heat, or other by-product.
emit or produce (as a by-product)

Examples

The chemical reaction can give off dangerous fumes if performed incorrectly.
Flowers often give off a pleasant fragrance at night.
The old engine gives off a lot of smoke when started.

Synset Examples

The big cities gave off so many wonderful American qualities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emit - to send out (a substance, especially a gas or radiation)

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to produce or emit something such as heat, light, a smell, or a gas
emit or release (light, heat, gas, etc.)

Examples

The flowers give off a sweet fragrance in the evening.
That old heater gives off a lot of heat during winter nights.
When mixed together, these chemicals give off a harmless vapor.

Synset Examples

The ozone layer blocks some harmful rays which the sun emits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emit - to send out (light, heat, sound, etc.)
  • Synonym: release - to allow a substance to escape or be set free
  • Antonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (energy, light, or another substance)

3. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to emit or produce something such as a smell, light, sound, or feeling
emit or produce (smell, light, etc.)

Examples

The flowers gave off a wonderful sweet fragrance in the garden.
The lamp gives off a soft, warm glow at night.
His speech gave off an air of confidence that impressed the audience.

Synset Examples

Her roughspun dress gave off a vintage vibe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emit - to send out energy, sound, smell, etc.
  • Synonym: to give out - to emit or discharge (something), similar in meaning
  • Antonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (something), opposite of emit
