to come it strong

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɪt strɔŋ / (AmE), / kʌm ɪt strɒŋ / (BrE)
to speak or act in a forceful or confident manner, often exaggerating or boasting
speak or act forcefully; exaggerate or boast
informal

Examples

He tends to come it strong when speaking about his achievements.
Don't come it strong—just tell us the truth.
The candidate tried to come it strong during the debate, but it sounded like boasting.

Synset Examples

She tends to come it strong when defending her views.
Don’t come it strong; be honest about your skills.
He always comes it strong during negotiations.
