to think straight

1. VERB

/ θɪŋk streɪt / (AmE), / θɪŋk streɪt / (BrE)
to think clearly and logically, without confusion or distraction
to think clearly and logically

Examples

After a long day without sleep, I couldn't think straight during the meeting.
The noise was so loud that he found it difficult to think straight.
She needed a moment alone to think straight before making a decision.

Synset Examples

I can't think straight; my cumbrain is too foggy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to think clearly - to think without confusion or distraction
  • Synonym: think rationally - to reason or think in a logically sound manner
