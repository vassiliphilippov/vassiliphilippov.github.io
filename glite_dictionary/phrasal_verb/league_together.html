to league together

1. VERB

/ liɡ təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / liːɡ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to join or unite with others in order to achieve a common purpose or goal
join together for a common purpose

Examples

The smaller countries decided to league together against their powerful neighbor.
Several organizations leagued together to promote environmental awareness.
In the face of adversity, the villagers often league together to solve their problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ally - to unite or join with another for a common purpose
  • Synonym: to cooperate - to work together with others for a shared goal
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or be in conflict with

Etymology

From 'league' meaning alliance, from Old French 'ligue', from Italian 'lega', meaning bond or alliance, plus 'together'.
