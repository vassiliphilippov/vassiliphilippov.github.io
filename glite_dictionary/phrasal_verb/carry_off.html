to carry off

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˌɒf / (BrE)
to succeed in doing something difficult, especially in a skillful or impressive way
succeed in doing something difficult

Examples

Despite her nerves, she managed to carry off a perfect performance.
The architect's bold design was difficult to execute, but he carried off the project brilliantly.
It's not easy to make such a complex dish, but she really carries off the recipe.

Synset Examples

I managed to carry the box upstairs
She pulled it off, even though we never thought her capable of it
The pianist negociated the difficult runs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull off - to succeed in doing something difficult
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving something

2. VERB

/ ˈkæri ɔf / (AmE), / ˈkæri ɒf / (BrE)
(of diseases, disasters, etc.) to kill in large numbers.
cause death to many (disease, disaster)

Examples

Many lives were carried off by the epidemic last winter.
The flood carried off several entire villages in the region.
Pneumonia often carries off elderly patients during harsh winters.

Synset Examples

the plague wiped out an entire population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe out - to destroy or kill large numbers of a group
  • Synonym: to decimate - to kill or destroy a large part of something

3. VERB

/ ˈkæri ɒf / (AmE), / ˈkæri ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or transport someone or something from a place, or to move someone emotionally or mentally into a different state, often suddenly or unexpectedly
remove or transport; transfer emotionally or mentally

Examples

The strong current can carry off even the strongest swimmers.
Her imagination would often carry off her thoughts to distant places.
The ambulance quickly carried off the injured man to the hospital.

Synset Examples

Their dreams carried the Romantics away into distant lands

I'll take you away on a holiday
I got carried away when I saw the dead man and I started to cry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take away - to remove someone or something from a place
  • Antonym: to bring back - to return someone or something to a previous place or state
