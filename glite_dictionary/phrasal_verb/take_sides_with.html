to take sides with

1. VERB

/ teɪk saɪdz wɪð / (AmE), / teɪk saɪdz wɪð / (BrE)
to support or align oneself with one person or group in a disagreement or conflict
support one person/group in a conflict

Examples

It is difficult for the teacher to take sides with any student in the dispute.
Although the evidence was unclear, the manager decided to take sides with his assistant.
In family arguments, she tries not to take sides with either parent.

Synset Examples

she always takes his side, no matter how sound his argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or approval to someone or something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or resist someone or something
