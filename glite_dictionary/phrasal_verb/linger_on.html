to linger on

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪŋɡɚ ɑn / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡə ɒn / (BrE)
to spend more time discussing, thinking about, or remaining on a subject than is necessary or desirable
dwell on or prolong a topic

Examples

He preferred not to linger on the mistakes of the past.
The meeting dragged on because everyone kept trying to linger on minor issues.
It's healthier to acknowledge your feelings but not to linger on negative thoughts.

Synset Examples

He would rather not linger on the negative aspects.
Let's not linger on this topic any longer.
She tends to linger on past mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwell on - to think or talk about something at length
  • Antonym: to move on - to stop discussing or thinking about something and proceed
