to be adrift

1. VERB

/ bi əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / bi əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
to be carried along by air or water currents without being steered or anchored
carried along by currents

Examples

After the storm, the damaged yacht was adrift for days before being rescued.
The inflatable raft may be adrift somewhere beyond the bay by now.
We worried that our canoe would be adrift all night if we didn’t anchor it.

Synset Examples

The leaves were blowing in the wind
the boat drifted on the lake
The sailboat was adrift on the open sea
the shipwrecked boat drifted away from the shore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - to move slowly, especially as a result of outside forces such as wind or water
