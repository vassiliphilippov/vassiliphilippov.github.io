to catch it

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ɪt / (AmE), / kætʃ ɪt / (BrE)
to be punished or scolded for something that one has done wrong
be punished or scolded
informal

Examples

If my parents find out I broke the vase, I'm really going to catch it.
He caught it from his boss for being late to the meeting.
The kids knew they would catch it if they got caught sneaking out.

Synset Examples

I really caught it the other day!
You are going to get it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get it - to be punished or scolded (similar meaning)
