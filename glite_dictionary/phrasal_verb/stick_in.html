to stick in

1. VERB

/ stɪk ɪn / (AmE), / stɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to add or insert something, especially casually or unexpectedly, into speech, writing, or a situation
insert or include casually

Examples

He tried to stick in a joke during the serious presentation.
She managed to stick in a quick thank-you at the end of her remarks.
If you stick in one more comment, the meeting will run over time.

Synset Examples

She slipped in a reference to her own work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip in - to insert or include something unobtrusively or casually
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01027611-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɪk ɪn / (AmE), / stɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to place, fit, or thrust something into another thing, often by pushing
insert or put inside

Examples

She stuck in a note with the present so he would know who it was from.
Before you bake the cake, stick in a toothpick to check if it's cooked through.
He quickly stuck in the key and turned the lock.

Synset Examples

Insert your ticket here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - to put or place something into another thing
  • Antonym: extract - to remove something that has been inserted or placed inside
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00187671-v
Synset POS: v
