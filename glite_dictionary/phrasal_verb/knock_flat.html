to knock flat

1. VERB

/ nɑk flæt / (AmE), / nɒk flæt / (BrE)
to shock or overwhelm someone suddenly and greatly, often leaving them stunned or unable to react
to shock or overwhelm completely

Examples

The surprise announcement knocked flat everyone in the meeting.
He was knocked flat by the sudden news of his friend's departure.
The breathtaking view from the mountain knocked me flat.

Synset Examples

The news of her promotion knocked him flat with surprise.
A sudden revelation knocked him flat on his back.
The powerful storm knocked me flat as I walked home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stun - to shock or overwhelm someone so much they cannot react
  • Synonym: floor - to surprise or confound someone greatly
  • Degree Lower: to amaze - to surprise someone greatly
