to chimp out

1. VERB

/ tʃɪmp aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɪmp aʊt / (BrE)
To panic, lose control, or act irrationally, especially in a sudden or exaggerated manner.
lose control or panic suddenly (informal)
informal

Examples

Try not to chimp out if things get stressful at work today.
She chimped out when she saw the giant spider on her desk.
You can't just chimp out every time plans change unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

She warned him not to chimp out during the exam.
During the argument, he completely chimped out on her.
He tends to chimp out when things don't go his way.
When he lost the game, he started to chimp out.
Don't chimp out, just stay calm and think.
Don't chimp out; it’s just a harmless joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak out - to become very anxious, upset, or lose control suddenly
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become quiet and controlled after being upset
