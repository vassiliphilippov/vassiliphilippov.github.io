to ride high

1. VERB

/ raɪd haɪ / (AmE), / raɪd haɪ / (BrE)
to be very successful, confident, or popular, often for a period of time
be successful or confident

Examples

Ever since his promotion, Tom has been riding high at work.
The team was riding high after their third consecutive win.
Stock markets are riding high despite global uncertainty.

Synset Examples

She felt she was riding high in her new role.
After winning the award, he was riding high for weeks.
The company is riding high after their successful product launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soar - to rise high or increase quickly, especially in success or confidence
  • Antonym: struggle - to experience difficulty or lack of success
