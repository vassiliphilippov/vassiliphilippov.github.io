to line up

1. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to form or stand in a line or queue, especially while waiting for something
form a line; queue

Examples

People started to line up outside the theater hours before the doors opened.
Please line up in an orderly fashion before entering the classroom.
During the sale, shoppers would line up around the block.

Synset Examples

Customers lined up in front of the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - a line of people or vehicles waiting their turn
  • Degree Lower: wait - remain in a place until an expected event happens
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02040600-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to form a row or arrange oneself or things in a straight line; to be in alignment.
form or arrange in a row

Examples

We were asked to line up in front of the theater before entering.
The children lined up in pairs for their field trip.
All the cars lined up perfectly along the street.

Synset Examples

The buildings all line up neatly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to arrange in a straight line or into correct relative positions
  • Antonym: scatter - to separate and spread out people or things over an area
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02710073-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to place something or someone in a straight line or in parallel arrangement
arrange in a line or parallel

Examples

The teacher asked the students to line up in front of the classroom.
Please line up the chairs against the wall before the event starts.
He carefully lined up the photos on the shelf so they looked neat.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to place or arrange things in a straight line or in correct position
