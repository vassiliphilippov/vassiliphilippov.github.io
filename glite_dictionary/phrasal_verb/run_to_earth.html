to run to earth

1. VERB

/ rʌn tuː ɝːθ / (AmE), / rʌn tuː ɜːθ / (BrE)
to find or catch someone or something after a long and difficult search
track down after a long search

Examples

After weeks of investigation, the reporter finally managed to run to earth the elusive source of the leak.
It took the team months to run to earth the rare manuscript hidden in a private collection.
Detectives were determined to run to earth whoever was responsible for the mysterious disappearance.

Synset Examples

They finally managed to run him to earth after weeks of searching.
It took months, but they ran the fugitive to earth.
Detectives worked hard to run the suspect to earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to track down - to search for and find someone or something
  • Antonym: lose track of - to fail to keep informed about the whereabouts or activities of someone or something
