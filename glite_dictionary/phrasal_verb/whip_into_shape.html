to whip into shape

1. VERB

/ wɪp ˈɪntuː ʃeɪp / (AmE), / wɪp ˈɪntə ʃeɪp / (BrE)
to quickly improve the condition, performance, or organization of someone or something, especially by taking firm or effective action
bring to good condition quickly

Examples

The new manager was determined to whip the team into shape before the big project began.
A few weeks at the training camp should whip him into shape for the competition.
We need to whip this report into shape before the final submission.

Synset Examples

She had to whip her team into shape quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get into shape - to become physically fit or well-organized
