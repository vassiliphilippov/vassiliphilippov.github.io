to carry out

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæri aʊt / (BrE)
to do or complete something, especially something that you have said you would do or that you have been told to do
to perform or complete (a task or action)

Examples

The company will carry out a full investigation into the incident.
Scientists carried out experiments to test the new drug's effectiveness.
Please carry out these instructions exactly as written.

Synset Examples

Did he go through with the treatment?
He implemented a new economic plan
She followed up his recommendations with a written proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implement - to put (a decision, plan, or agreement) into effect
  • Antonym: abort - to stop something before it is finished, especially before it is successfully completed
