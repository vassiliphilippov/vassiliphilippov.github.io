to hoke up

1. VERB

/ hoʊk ʌp / (AmE), / həʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to invent a story, excuse, or explanation, especially in a way that is exaggerated or not truthful
fabricate or concoct a story or excuse
informal american

Examples

She tried to hoke up an excuse for being late, but no one believed her.
He managed to hoke up a story that explained his absence.
They always hoke up some wild tale to entertain their friends.

Synset Examples

She tried to hoke up an excuse for being late.
I can't believe you hoked up that story at dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make up - to invent a story or excuse
  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent or concoct, typically with deceitful intent

Etymology

Possibly from American slang 'hoke', meaning to fake or overdo, origin uncertain.
