to put up with

1. VERB

/ pʊt ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to accept or tolerate something or someone unpleasant or difficult without complaining
tolerate something unpleasant

Examples

I can't put up with this noise any longer.
She had to put up with rude customers all day.
How do you put up with his messy habits?

Synset Examples

I cannot bear his constant criticism
The new secretary had to endure a lot of unprofessional remarks
he learned to tolerate the heat
She stuck out two years in a miserable marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tolerate - to allow or accept something unpleasant or annoying
  • Synonym: to endure - to suffer patiently or without giving up
  • Synonym: bear - to be able to accept and deal with something unpleasant
