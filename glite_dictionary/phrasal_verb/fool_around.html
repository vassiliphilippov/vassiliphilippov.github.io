to fool around

1. VERB

/ ful əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(informal) To have a sexual relationship with someone who is not your spouse or regular partner; to commit adultery.
have an affair or commit adultery (informal)
informal

Examples

Rumors spread that he had fooled around with one of his colleagues.
She divorced him after learning he had fooled around during their marriage.
It's heartbreaking when someone finds out their spouse has been fooling around.

Synset Examples

he plays around a lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to have a sexual relationship with someone other than your partner
  • Antonym: be faithful - to remain loyal and not commit adultery

2. VERB

/ fuːl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a playful, silly, or mischievous way, often instead of doing something more serious
act in a playful or silly way

Examples

The children began to fool around instead of cleaning their room.
Stop fooling around and pay attention to your work.
During recess, the students were fooling around on the playground.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to horse around - to play roughly or behave in a silly way
  • Synonym: to mess around - to behave in a silly or aimless way
