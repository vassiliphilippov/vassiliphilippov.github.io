to help out

1. VERB

/ hɛlp aʊt / (AmE), / hɛlp aʊt / (BrE)
to assist someone, especially in a particular situation of need or difficulty
assist someone in need

Examples

Could you help out at the charity event this weekend?
Her neighbors always help out when she needs someone to watch her children.
I stayed after work to help out with the project deadline.

Synset Examples

Can you help out tonight with the dinner guests?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assist - to give support or aid to; help
  • Antonym: hamper - to hinder or impede the progress of
