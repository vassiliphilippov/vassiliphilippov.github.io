to unbridle emotion

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbraɪdəl ɪˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbraɪdəl ɪˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
to express feelings openly and without restraint
express feelings openly

Examples

She decided to unbridle emotion and spoke honestly about her fears.
Artists often unbridle emotion through their creative work.
During the ceremony, he allowed himself to unbridle emotion in front of the attendees.

Synset Examples

He wanted to unbridle his emotions during the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repress emotion - to keep feelings under control or hidden
  • Synonym: to express emotion - to show feelings in words or actions

Etymology

From 'unbridle' (remove restraints) + 'emotion'; metaphor from removing a bridle from a horse.
