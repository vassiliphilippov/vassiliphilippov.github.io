to play the edgelord

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ði ˈɛdʒˌlɔrd / (AmE), / pleɪ ði ˈɛdʒˌlɔːd / (BrE)
to deliberately adopt a provocative or controversial persona, often to attract attention or provoke a reaction, especially online
adopt a provocative persona online
informal slang

Examples

Some teenagers play the edgelord on social media to get a reaction from their peers.
She didn't actually believe those things—she just liked to play the edgelord for attention.
Whenever the debate gets heated, he can't resist playing the edgelord and pushing everyone's buttons.

Synset Examples

He loves to play the edgelord in online forums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edgelord - a person who adopts deliberately provocative opinions or behavior, especially online

Etymology

From 'edgelord', a slang blend of 'edge' (suggesting provocative or boundary-pushing) and 'lord'.
