to gather around

1. VERB

/ ˈɡæðər əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to come together and form a group or circle around someone or something
come together in a group or circle

Examples

The students gathered around the teacher to hear the exciting announcement.
Families often gather around the table for dinner each night.
Curious onlookers began to gather around after the street performer started playing music.

Synset Examples

They gathered around the hearth to tell stories.
They gathered around the campfire to sing Kumbaya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to come together as a group in one place

2. VERB

/ ˈɡæðər əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to come together in a group, often surrounding a person or object
come together in a group

Examples

After the announcement, employees gathered around to hear more details.
The children gathered around the storyteller, eager for the next tale.
Friends gathered around the campfire, sharing songs and laughter.

Synset Examples

The family gathered around the large salad bowl during dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to gather together in one place for a purpose
  • Antonym: to disperse - to break up and leave in different directions
