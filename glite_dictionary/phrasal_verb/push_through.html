to push through

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ θru / (AmE), / pʊʃ θruː / (BrE)
(of new growth, such as a tooth or plant) to break through a surface, especially as it emerges for the first time.
break out or emerge through a surface

Examples

The baby's first tooth finally pushed through after weeks of teething.
New shoots began to push through the soil in early spring.
A cactus will push through even the hardest, driest ground to reach the sun.

Synset Examples

The tooth erupted and had to be extracted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to break out or burst forth, especially through a surface (as of a tooth or volcano)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00549346-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ θru / (AmE), / pʊʃ θruː / (BrE)
to continue doing something or persevere despite obstacles, difficulty, or opposition
persevere despite obstacles

Examples

Despite feeling exhausted, she decided to push through and complete her shift.
It can be hard to push through setbacks, but resilience often pays off.
Even when the weather turned bad, the team managed to push through and finish the race.

Synset Examples

She pushed through the last mile with determination.
She managed to push through her fatigue to finish the project.
Despite the rain, we decided to push through with the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to continue steadily despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying; to quit
