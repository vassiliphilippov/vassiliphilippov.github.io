to wash down

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ daʊn / (AmE), / wɒʃ daʊn / (BrE)
(of something) to be carried along or to flow freely with a current of water, especially downstream.
be carried along by water

Examples

During the heavy rains, debris washed down the mountain into the valley below.
After the dam broke, entire trees washed down the river for miles.
When the tide came in, abandoned boats washed down the coastline.

Synset Examples

The body washed down the river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɑʃ daʊn / (AmE), / wɒʃ daʊn / (BrE)
To eat food and immediately swallow it with a drink; also, to metaphorically soothe or lessen something with a drink.
swallow food with drink; soothe with drink

Examples

She always tries to wash down spicy food with a cold glass of milk.
He needed a drink to wash down his dry sandwich at lunch.
After delivering the bad news, she washed down her worries with a cup of hot tea.

Synset Examples

She washed down her dinner with a bottle of red wine
He washes down his worries with a nightly glass of whisky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to quickly drink a beverage after taking medicine or food, or to soothe something with drink
