to lay claim

1. VERB

/ leɪ kleɪm / (AmE), / leɪ kleɪm / (BrE)
to assert that one has a right to something; to state formally or firmly that one owns or deserves something
assert one's right to something

Examples

Several countries lay claim to the disputed territory in the South China Sea.
He laid claim to the invention, arguing that he was the first to develop it.
By producing the proper documentation, she was able to lay claim to her inheritance.

Synset Examples

He claimed his suitcases at the airline counter
Mr. Smith claims special tax exemptions because he is a foreign resident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - to demand or assert something as one's own
