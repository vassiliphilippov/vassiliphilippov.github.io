to act on

1. VERB

/ ˈækt ɑn / (AmE), / ˈakt ɒn / (BrE)
to take action in response to something such as advice, information, or a request, in order to achieve a result
take action in response to something

Examples

The committee decided to act on the recommendations made in the report.
If you receive important information, you should act on it immediately.
She failed to act on his advice, which led to further problems.

Synset Examples

Can you act on this matter soon?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to follow up - to pursue or continue further action on something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately not pay attention to something or not take action on it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02381520-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ækt ɑn / (AmE), / ækt ɒn / (BrE)
to take action as a result of information, advice, or principles; to regulate one's behavior according to something
take action according to advice or information

Examples

She decided to act on her doctor's advice and started exercising regularly.
The government needs to act on the recommendations in the report.
He failed to act on the warning, which led to further problems.

Synset Examples

The Founding Fathers acted on certain moral principles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to follow - do what is suggested or recommended by someone or something
  • Antonym: to ignore - deliberately pay no attention to advice or information

Synset POS: v
