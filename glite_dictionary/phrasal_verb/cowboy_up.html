to cowboy up

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊbɔɪ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkaʊbɔɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to face a difficult or challenging situation with courage and determination, especially when urged to toughen up or take responsibility
face challenges bravely; toughen up
informal american

Examples

Whenever things get tough at work, you just have to cowboy up and keep going.
The coach told the team to cowboy up before the final quarter.
She realized it was time to cowboy up and confront the situation directly.

Synset Examples

She told him to cowboy up and take responsibility.
When challenges arise, you just have to cowboy up.
It's time to cowboy up and face your fears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to man up - show courage or toughness; behave in a more traditionally masculine way
  • Antonym: to buckle under - fail to withstand pressure; give up under stress

Etymology

Originally from American rodeo culture, referring to adopting the toughness or resilience associated with cowboys.
