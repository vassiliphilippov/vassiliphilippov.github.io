to knit together

1. VERB

/ nɪt təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / nɪt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to unite people, groups, or ideas closely; to bring separate things into a cohesive whole
bring or unite closely

Examples

The organization hopes to knit together diverse communities through collaboration.
Strong leadership can knit together a team facing difficult challenges.
After the merger, the new company worked hard to knit together its various departments.

Synset Examples

They aim to knit together various cultures through art.
The community will knit together during tough times.
The team needs to knit together their ideas for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to come or bring together for a common purpose
  • Synonym: combine - to join two or more things to form a single unit

Etymology

From 'knit,' meaning to join or combine, plus 'together' to indicate uniting.
