to turtle up

1. VERB

/ ˈtɝː.təl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.təl ʌp / (BrE)
to withdraw from social interaction or avoid engagement with others, often as a reaction to stress or discomfort.
withdraw from social interaction
informal

Examples

Whenever she feels overwhelmed at work, she tends to turtle up and stop talking to her colleagues.
After the argument, John just turtled up in his room for hours.
It's important not to turtle up during difficult conversations with friends.

Synset Examples

Don't turtle up; it's okay to share your feelings.
When stressed, he tends to turtle up and avoid socializing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to remove oneself from participation or social interaction
  • Antonym: to open up - to become more communicative and willing to share feelings

Etymology

Figurative use from 'turtle', referencing how turtles retreat into their shells.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɜr.t̬əl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.tl̩ ʌp / (BrE)
to withdraw emotionally or physically in response to difficulty, threat, or stress; to retreat or hide oneself like a turtle
withdraw or hide in response to stress
informal

Examples

When faced with criticism, he tends to turtle up instead of addressing the issue.
She always turtles up during tough times, avoiding friends and family.
Instead of turtling up when challenged, try to confront your fears.

Synset Examples

She always turtles up during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to move back or away, especially into privacy or isolation
  • Antonym: to open up - to become more communicative or less reserved

Etymology

From the behavior of turtles retracting into their shells for protection.
