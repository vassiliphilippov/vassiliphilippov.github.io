to bring forth

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ˈfɔrθ / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
to produce or present something for display or consideration, especially to make it known or available
produce or present for display

Examples

The lawyer brought forth new evidence during the trial.
She brought forth several options for the committee to consider.
During the meeting, he brought forth an idea that changed everyone's perspective.

Synset Examples

The proud father produced many pictures of his baby
The accused brought forth a letter in court that he claims exonerates him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: produce - to bring something into existence or present it
  • Synonym: show - to cause something to be seen; to display
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden or not make it known
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ fɔrθ / (AmE), / brɪŋ fɔːθ / (BrE)
(formal or literary) To give birth to or produce (offspring) by reproduction.
produce offspring; give birth (formal/literary)

Examples

The mare brought forth a healthy foal in the early morning.
She dreams of the day she will bring forth her own children.
According to the legend, the ancient queen brought forth twins destined to rule the land.

Synset Examples

Abraham begot Isaac
John fathered four daughters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beget - to father; to cause to exist or occur, especially in terms of offspring
  • Synonym: father - to become the father of children
  • Synonym: bear - to give birth to (a child)
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ brɪŋ fɔrθ / (AmE), / brɪŋ fɔːθ / (BrE)
to produce or create something; to make something appear or exist
produce or create

Examples

The discovery of water may bring forth new life on the planet.
The policy changes are expected to bring forth significant improvements.
Creativity often brings forth innovative solutions to complex problems.

Synset Examples

The new manager generated a lot of problems
The computer bug generated chaos in the office
The computer generated this image
The earthquake generated a tsunami

Related Terms

  • Synonym: produce - to make or create something
  • Synonym: to generate - to cause something to exist or happen
  • Antonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something
