to swear at

1. VERB

/ swɛr æt / (AmE), / swɛə æt / (BrE)
to use rude or offensive language directed at someone or something, typically in anger or frustration
use offensive language toward someone

Examples

He lost his temper and began to swear at the referee during the match.
Please don’t swear at people just because you are upset.
The driver swore at the pedestrian who stepped in front of his car.

Synset Examples

It's not polite to swear at someone in public.
She couldn't help but swear at the traffic jam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse at - to utter offensive words at someone in anger
  • Synonym: insult - to speak to or treat with disrespect or scornful abuse
