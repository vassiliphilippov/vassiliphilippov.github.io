to feel out

1. VERB

/ fil aʊt / (AmE), / fiːl aʊt / (BrE)
to try to discover someone's opinions or intentions, often in a careful or indirect way
try to discover opinions or intentions

Examples

We sent an email to feel out whether the clients were interested in the proposal.
She tried to feel out her colleagues' views before the meeting.
Before making an offer, he subtly felt out the vendor's flexibility.

Synset Examples

I have to sound out the new professor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sound out - to try to find out someone's views or opinions by indirect questioning

2. VERB

/ fil aʊt / (AmE), / fiːl aʊt / (BrE)
to carefully try to find out someone's opinion or the state of a situation without directly asking
gauge opinions or assess a situation discreetly

Examples

Before committing to the decision, she decided to feel out her colleagues' perspectives.
The politician sent an assistant to feel out the community's views on the new policy.
We should feel out the investors before making any big announcements.

Synset Examples

He tried to feel out the situation before reacting.
I'm going to feel out her opinion on the proposal.
Let's feel out the market before launching our product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sound out - to try to find out the opinions or intentions of someone by indirect questioning
