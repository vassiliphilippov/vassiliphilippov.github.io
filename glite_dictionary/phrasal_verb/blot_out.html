to blot out

1. VERB

/ blɑt aʊt / (AmE), / blɒt aʊt / (BrE)
to make something impossible to see, read, or remember by covering, obscuring, or concealing it
make impossible to see or remember

Examples

The thick fog completely blotted out the mountain from view.
She tried to blot out the memory of the accident from her mind.
The censorship board blotted out all sensitive information from the documents.

Synset Examples

a hidden message
a veiled threat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - to make unclear or difficult to see
  • Synonym: to erase - to remove completely so nothing remains
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make visible or known what was hidden

Etymology

From the verb 'blot' (to obscure or cover up) + 'out' (completely, to extinction).
