to bear off

1. VERB

/ bɛr ɔf / (AmE), / beə ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or carry someone or something away from a place to another location; to transport or take away.
to carry or remove away

Examples

The rescuers managed to bear off the injured man from the wreckage.
In ancient tales, heroes would bear off treasures from distant lands.
They tried to bear off all the valuable paintings before the fire spread.

Synset Examples

Their dreams carried the Romantics away into distant lands
The car carried us off to the meeting
I'll take you away on a holiday
I got carried away when I saw the dead man and I started to cry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry off - to take someone or something away, especially by force
  • Synonym: to take away - to remove something or someone from a place

Etymology

From the verb 'bear' (to carry) + 'off' (away, at a distance).
