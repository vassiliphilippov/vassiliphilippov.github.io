to stick to the plan

1. VERB

/ stɪk tə ðə plæn / (AmE), / stɪk tə ðə plan / (BrE)
to continue doing what was agreed upon or decided, without deviating or making changes
continue following the agreed plan

Examples

She always tries to stick to the plan, even when unexpected problems arise.
If we stick to the plan, we should finish the project on time.
It's important for everyone to stick to the plan so we can achieve our goals.

Synset Examples

You should stick to the plan we discussed.
Please stick to the plan for today's meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deviate from the plan - stop following the original plan
