to pile up

1. VERB

/ paɪl ʌp / (AmE), / paɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange objects into piles or stacks; to accumulate things in a heap.
arrange in piles or stacks

Examples

After doing laundry, I always pile up the clean clothes on my bed.
Snow began to pile up on the sidewalks during the storm.
She piled up boxes in the garage to make more space in the living room.

Synset Examples

She piled up her books in my living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stack - to arrange objects in a tidy pile
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01506930-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ paɪl ʌp / (AmE), / paɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or collect things in a large quantity, often so that they form a pile or increase in number over time
to accumulate; become numerous

Examples

Bills tend to pile up quickly if you ignore them for a month.
Snow began to pile up on the roads during the storm.
Dirty dishes often pile up in the sink after a big family dinner.

Synset Examples

I am accumulating evidence for the man's unfaithfulness to his wife
She is amassing a lot of data for her thesis
She rolled up a small fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect, especially over time
  • Synonym: to amass - to collect together or assemble, especially for oneself
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02309962-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ paɪl ʌp / (AmE), / paɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to increase in quantity or number by collecting or accumulating, often to an overwhelming degree.
accumulate or increase in quantity

Examples

After a week of neglect, the dirty dishes began to pile up in the sink.
Emails pile up quickly if you don't check your inbox regularly.
Snow started to pile up along the roads after the storm.

Synset Examples

Journals are accumulating in my office
The work keeps piling up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - gather together or amass over time
  • Antonym: to diminish - become less or decrease in amount
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00158612-v
Synset POS: v
