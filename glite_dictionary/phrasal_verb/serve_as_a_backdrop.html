to serve as a backdrop

1. VERB

/ sɜrv æz ə ˈbækˌdrɑp / (AmE), / sɜːv æz ə ˈbækˌdrɒp / (BrE)
to function as a scenic or situational background, often enhancing or framing the main subject or event
function as a scenic background

Examples

The historic castle serves as a backdrop for many local festivals throughout the year.
Snow-capped mountains served as a backdrop to the small alpine village.
A row of colorful flowers serves as a backdrop for wedding photographs taken in the garden.

Synset Examples

The city skyline served as a beautiful backdrop for photos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: background - the part of a scene behind the main subject
