to dial back

1. VERB

/ daɪəl bæk / (AmE), / daɪəl bak / (BrE)
to reduce the amount, level, or intensity of something
reduce or lessen something

Examples

After the complaints, the company had to dial back its advertising campaign.
You might want to dial back your expectations for the project deadline.
The chef decided to dial back the spices in the dish to suit milder tastes.

Synset Examples

We need to dial back our spending this month.
She had to dial back her ambitions to fit the project.
It's time to dial back on the caffeine intake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make smaller in amount or degree
  • Synonym: to cut back - to reduce the amount or use of something
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From the use of a dial (as on radios or telephones) to decrease levels or settings.
