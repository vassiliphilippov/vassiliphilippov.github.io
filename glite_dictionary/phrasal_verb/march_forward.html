to march forward

1. VERB

/ mɑrtʃ ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to progress steadily or proceed with determination, often in the face of difficulty
proceed determinedly

Examples

Despite numerous setbacks, the team chose to march forward and eventually succeeded.
She encouraged everyone to march forward with hope, regardless of the challenges.
Our company must march forward to stay competitive in the changing market.

Synset Examples

We need to march forward with our plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - move forward or make progress
  • Antonym: retreat - move back or withdraw, especially from difficulty
