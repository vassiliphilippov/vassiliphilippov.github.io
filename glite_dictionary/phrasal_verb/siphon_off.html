to siphon off

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪfən ɔf / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪfən ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or transfer liquid, resources, or money from one place or use to another, often gradually or secretly
remove or transfer something, often discreetly

Examples

The accountant was caught trying to siphon off funds into a private account.
Engineers used pumps to siphon off water from the flooded basement.
He was accused of siphoning off resources meant for the public project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to divert - to cause something to change direction or use

Etymology

From the noun 'siphon', which comes from Greek 'sīphōn' (a pipe or tube), combined with the particle 'off'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsaɪfən ɔf / (AmE), / ˈsaɪfən ɒf / (BrE)
to secretly take money or resources, especially dishonestly, for personal use or other unauthorized purposes
secretly take money for personal use

Examples

The accountant was caught trying to siphon off company funds into his personal account.
She used a network of fake invoices to siphon off money over several years.
Investigators discovered that thousands of dollars had been siphoned off from the local charity.

Synset Examples

He tried to siphon off some funds for his project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embezzle - to steal or misappropriate money placed in one's trust or belonging to one's employer
  • Synonym: to misappropriate - to dishonestly or unfairly take something, especially money, for one's own use

Etymology

From 'siphon', a tube for drawing off liquid, extended metaphorically to mean diverting money or resources.
