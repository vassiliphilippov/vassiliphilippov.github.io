to take sides

1. VERB

/ teɪk saɪdz / (AmE), / teɪk saɪdz / (BrE)
to support or align oneself with one person or group in a disagreement or conflict
support one in a conflict

Examples

It's never easy when friends ask you to take sides during a family argument.
He refused to take sides and tried to mediate between the two groups.
Teachers should avoid taking sides when students have a disagreement.

Synset Examples

It's better not to take sides in their dispute.
In every argument, she refuses to take sides.
Friends should never take sides during a breakup.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remain neutral - not supporting any side in a conflict
