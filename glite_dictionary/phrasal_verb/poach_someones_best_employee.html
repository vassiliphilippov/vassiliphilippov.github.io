to poach someone's best employee

1. VERB

/ poʊtʃ sʌmˈwʌnz bɛst ɛmˈplɔɪi / (AmE), / pəʊtʃ sʌmˈwʌnz bɛst ɪmˈplɔɪi / (BrE)
to try to persuade the most talented or valuable employee of another company or organization to leave their job and work for you instead
lure away another firm's top employee

Examples

The tech company tried to poach our best employee by offering her a higher salary and more benefits.
Rival firms often attempt to poach someone’s best employee during industry downturns when morale is low.
After hearing about his outstanding performance, they set out to poach their best employee with the promise of stock options.

Synset Examples

They tried to poach our best employee with a better offer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headhunt - to actively seek to recruit someone for a position, especially from another company

Etymology

From 'poach' (to illegally hunt or take) in reference to illegal recruitment.
