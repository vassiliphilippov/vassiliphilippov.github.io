to skip over

1. VERB

/ skɪp ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / skɪp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to move past or omit (something) intentionally when reading, speaking, or performing a series of actions
omit or bypass intentionally

Examples

When reading aloud, please do not skip over any words in the passage.
He tends to skip over the introduction and go straight to the main point.
If you skip over these crucial steps, the experiment might fail.

Synset Examples

He skipped a row in the text and so the sentence was incomprehensible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to omit - to leave out or fail to include
  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or set
