to put on the mat

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ðə mæt / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ðə mæt / (BrE)
to formally reprimand or confront (someone), especially for a mistake or wrongdoing
reprimand or confront someone

Examples

After the error was discovered, the coach put him on the mat in front of the team.
She was put on the mat for missing yesterday's deadline.
The boss often puts employees on the mat when they violate company policy.

Synset Examples

The manager put him on the mat for his mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: scold - to angrily or sharply reprimand
