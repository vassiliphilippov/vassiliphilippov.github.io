to tear off

1. VERB

/ tɛr ɔf / (AmE), / teə ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling or ripping it violently and forcefully
remove forcefully by pulling or ripping

Examples

The child tried to tear off the sticker from the window but couldn't.
A strong wind tore off several tiles from the roof during the storm.
She tore off the wrapping paper in excitement to see her birthday present.

Synset Examples

The passing bus tore off her side mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rip off - to remove something quickly and forcefully
  • Synonym: to tear away - to pull something apart or away violently
