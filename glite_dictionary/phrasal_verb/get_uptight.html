to get uptight

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ʌpˈtaɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ʌpˈtaɪt / (BrE)
to become anxious, tense, or nervous, especially in a stressful situation
become anxious or tense
informal

Examples

Try not to get uptight before your big presentation tomorrow.
He tends to get uptight whenever there's a lot of traffic.
Whenever the deadline approaches, Anna starts to get uptight and snaps at people.

Synset Examples

She gets uptight when things don't go as planned.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to relax - to become less tense or anxious
  • Synonym: get nervous - to become worried or anxious
