to drive away

1. VERB

/ draɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / draɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place, often by making them feel unwelcome, or to dispel a feeling or thought
force to leave; dispel something

Examples

The loud noise was enough to drive away the birds from the garden.
She tried to drive away her fears before the big exam.
Security guards were hired to drive away trespassers from the premises.

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispel - to make something disappear, especially a feeling or belief
  • Antonym: to attract - to draw someone or something towards a place

2. VERB

/ draɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / draɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to leave, usually by making them feel unwelcome or uncomfortable
make someone or something leave

Examples

The company’s strict policies may drive away valuable employees.
The smell of smoke can drive away birds from the garden.
Her unfriendly attitude tends to drive away new acquaintances.

Synset Examples

His rude behavior might drive away potential clients.
The loud music will drive away the neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repel - to drive back or keep away; synonym for 'drive away'
  • Antonym: to attract - to draw someone in; opposite of 'drive away'
