to sell someone out

1. VERB

/ sɛl ˈsʌmwʌn aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl ˈsʌmwʌn aʊt / (BrE)
to betray a person or group by revealing confidential information or failing to support them, especially for personal gain.
betray by revealing information

Examples

I can't believe you would sell me out to the competition for a little money.
He sold out his friends by telling the police everything they had done.
She felt betrayed when she learned her coworker had sold her out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal to someone by revealing secrets or supporting an enemy
