to pull ahead

1. VERB

/ pʊl əˈhɛd / (AmE), / pʊl əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to move into a leading or advantageous position, especially in a race, competition, or contest
move into the lead or gain advantage

Examples

The runner surged in the final lap to pull ahead of his rivals.
With a new marketing strategy, the company managed to pull ahead of its competitors this quarter.
As the votes were counted, the candidate began to pull ahead by a significant margin.

Synset Examples

The home team was gaining ground
After defeating the Knicks, the Blazers pulled ahead of the Lakers in the battle for the number-one playoff berth in the Western Conference
win points

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall behind - to move into a less advantageous or trailing position

2. VERB

/ pʊl əˈhɛd / (AmE), / pʊl əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to move into a leading position or gain an advantage over others, especially in a competition or race
to move into the lead

Examples

After a slow start, the runner began to pull ahead in the last lap.
If our team works harder, we can pull ahead of our rivals.
Sales figures show that the new product has started to pull ahead of competitors.

Synset Examples

They need to pull ahead to win the championship.
She managed to pull ahead in the race.
He started to pull ahead of the competition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall behind - to lose one's lead or lag in a competition
