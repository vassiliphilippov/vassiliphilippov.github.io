to saddle up

1. VERB

/ ˈsæd.əl ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl ˌʌp / (BrE)
to prepare for a long journey or a challenging task, especially by getting ready both mentally and physically
prepare for a journey or task

Examples

It's time to saddle up and face the challenge ahead.
Before the long hike, everyone made sure to saddle up with supplies and gear.
As the meeting drew near, she took a deep breath and mentally saddled up.

Synset Examples

They decided to saddle up for the long journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gear up - to prepare equipment or oneself for something
  • Synonym: get ready - to prepare oneself for something

Etymology

From the literal act of placing a saddle on a horse before riding.
