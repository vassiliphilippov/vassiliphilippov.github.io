to spiral up

1. VERB

/ ˈspaɪrəl ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəl ˈʌp / (BrE)
to move or rise in a spiral or helical path in an upward direction
move upward in a spiral path

Examples

We watched the colorful balloons spiral up into the clear morning sky.
Smoke began to spiral up from the chimney as the fire was lit.
The birds spiraled up on a thermal current, gaining altitude effortlessly.

Synset Examples

He watched the smoke spiral up into the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spiral down - to move in a spiral path downward
