to climb down

1. VERB

/ klaɪm daʊn / (AmE), / klaɪm daʊn / (BrE)
to descend from a higher place by using the hands and feet, especially by means of a ladder, tree, or similar object
descend from a height

Examples

She carefully climbed down the ladder after painting the top of the wall.
The cat got stuck in the tree and wouldn't climb down until we coaxed it.
We watched the hikers climb down the rocky slope as the sun was setting.

Synset Examples

the birds alighted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to descend - to move downward from a higher to a lower place
  • Antonym: to climb up - to go up something using hands and feet
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klaɪm daʊn / (AmE), / klaɪm daʊn / (BrE)
to retract or withdraw from a previously stated opinion, position, or claim, often under pressure or after reconsideration
to withdraw a previous opinion or position

Examples

After facing criticism, the politician was forced to climb down from her earlier remarks.
Despite his initial refusal, he eventually decided to climb down and accept responsibility.
The company had to climb down after customers complained about the new policy.

Synset Examples

They need to climb down from their rigid stance.
It's time for him to climb down and apologize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to back down - to withdraw or concede in a dispute
