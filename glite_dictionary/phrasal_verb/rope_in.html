to rope in

1. VERB

/ roʊp ɪn / (AmE), / rəʊp ɪn / (BrE)
to persuade someone to take part in an activity, especially when they may not want to
persuade to join or take part

Examples

She managed to rope in her friends to help decorate for the party.
The manager roped in several employees for the weekend shift.
I was initially hesitant, but they roped me in with promises of free food.

Synset Examples

The agent had roped in several customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coax - persuade someone gently to do something
  • Synonym: to entice - attract or tempt by offering pleasure or advantage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00770675-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ roʊp ɪn / (AmE), / rəʊp ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose, separate, or divide an area by placing a rope around it
enclose or separate with a rope

Examples

The organizers decided to rope in part of the park for the VIP guests.
Construction workers roped in the hazardous area to keep people out.
After the accident, police quickly roped in the entire street.

Synset Examples

The police roped off the area where the crime occurred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cordon off - to prevent access to an area by surrounding it with a barrier
  • Synonym: to fence in - to enclose an area with a fence
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
