to take huff

1. VERB

/ teɪk hʌf / (AmE), / teɪk hʌf / (BrE)
to become offended or upset, especially as a reaction to something perceived as a slight
become offended or upset

Examples

Please don’t take huff at my remarks; I didn’t mean to offend you.
He can easily take huff if he feels left out of the conversation.
When she was left out of the plans, she took huff and refused to join us later.

Synset Examples

Don't take huff; it was just a joke.
He tends to take huff over minor comments.
She took huff when he criticized her work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take offense - to feel insulted or hurt by something
  • Antonym: to shrug off - to disregard or not let something upset you
