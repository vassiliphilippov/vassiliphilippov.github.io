to call roll

1. VERB

/ kɔl roʊl / (AmE), / kɔːl rəʊl / (BrE)
to read aloud a list of names to check who is present, especially in a class or meeting
take attendance by reading names

Examples

The principal will call roll before the meeting starts.
Please stay quiet while I call roll so we don't miss anyone.
She called roll to ensure all students were present after the fire drill.

Synset Examples

The teacher will call roll at the beginning of class.
After I call roll, we can start the lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take attendance - to check who is present by recording names
