to stack away

1. VERB

/ stæk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / stæk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put something in a safe place for future use; to store or lay aside.
store for future use

Examples

During the harvest, farmers stack away hay in the barn for the winter.
She likes to stack away a portion of her salary each month.
Old photographs were stacked away in boxes in the attic.

Synset Examples

store grain for the winter
The bear stores fat for the period of hibernation when he doesn't eat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to store away - put something aside for future use; to store
