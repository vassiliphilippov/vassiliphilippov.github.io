to run down

1. VERB

/ rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to pursue someone or something until they are caught or stopped
pursue until caught

Examples

The police worked tirelessly to run down the escaped prisoner.
After hours of searching, they finally managed to run down the missing dog.
It took the authorities weeks to run down the fraudster hiding overseas.

Synset Examples

They ran down the fugitive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - to capture or seize, especially after a chase
  • Antonym: to let go - to release or stop pursuing someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02008241-v
Synset POS: v

/ rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to use up, reduce, or deplete a resource, supply, or amount until little or nothing remains
deplete; use up resources

Examples

We tried not to run down our food supplies before the next delivery arrived.
If you keep leaving the lights on, you'll run down the batteries much faster.
The charity began to run down its funds during the crisis.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to use up completely
