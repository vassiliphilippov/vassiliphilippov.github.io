to lose sight of

1. VERB

/ luːz saɪt əv / (AmE), / luːz saɪt ɒv / (BrE)
to no longer be able to see something or someone, usually because it is too far away or blocked from view
to no longer be able to see

Examples

We quickly lost sight of the boat as it sailed into the mist.
She lost sight of her friend in the crowded market.
As the plane climbed higher, the children lost sight of their parents standing below.

Synset Examples

We lost sight of the tower as we pulled out of the harbor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spot - to see, notice, or recognize someone or something
