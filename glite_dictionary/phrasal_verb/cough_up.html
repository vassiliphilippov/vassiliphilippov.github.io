to cough up

1. VERB

/ kɑf ʌp / (AmE), / kɒf ʌp / (BrE)
to expel phlegm or other substance from the lungs into the mouth by coughing
expel mucus by coughing

Examples

The patient began to cough up thick mucus during his illness.
If you cough up blood, see a doctor immediately.
She coughed up some phlegm and spit it into the tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expectorate - to eject matter from the throat or lungs by coughing

2. VERB

/ kɔf ʌp / (AmE), / kɒf ʌp / (BrE)
to give or pay something, especially money, unwillingly or with reluctance
give or pay unwillingly
informal

Examples

After much persuasion, he finally coughed up the money he owed us.
She didn't want to cough up the extra fee for express shipping.
Whenever there's a group bill, someone has to remind Tom to cough up his share.

Synset Examples

He coughed up some money for his children's tuition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fork out - to pay or give money reluctantly
  • Synonym: to shell out - to spend money, usually unwillingly
  • Synonym: to pony up - to pay money, especially reluctantly

informal

Examples

The company refused to cough up compensation until they were threatened with legal action.
