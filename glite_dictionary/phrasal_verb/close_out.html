to close out

1. VERB

/ kloʊz aʊt / (AmE), / kləʊz aʊt / (BrE)
To terminate by selling off all remaining stock or assets, often at reduced prices, or to bring an operation or account to an end.
sell off remaining stock; end an operation

Examples

The company decided to close out its remaining merchandise at huge discounts.
After 30 years, the store will close out its operations at the end of the month.
They had to close out the warehouse inventory before relocating to a new facility.

Synset Examples

He closed out his line of sports cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquidate - to sell assets to settle a debt or end a business
  • Synonym: to wind up - to bring a business to an end
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02357534-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kloʊs aʊt / (AmE), / kləʊz aʊt / (BrE)
to make something impossible, especially by taking anticipatory action; to prevent something from happening or being considered
prevent or make impossible beforehand

Examples

New evidence may close out the possibility of a retrial.
The new contract terms close out any further negotiations on the subject.
She tried to close out all doubts before making her decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preclude - to make impossible, especially beforehand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02635471-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kloʊz aʊt / (AmE), / kləʊz aʊt / (BrE)
to bring to an end; to finish or complete something, especially by concluding an account, arrangement, or process.
bring to an end, finish off

Examples

We decided to close out our savings account before moving to a new city.
The store will close out its inventory during the final sale next month.
After many years, the team finally closed out the project with a successful launch.

Synset Examples

We closed out our account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - to bring something to completion
  • Synonym: complete - to make something whole or finished
