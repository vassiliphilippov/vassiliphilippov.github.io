to fig out

1. VERB

/ fɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / fɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to put on special or elaborate clothes, especially to appear particularly appealing and attractive
dress in special or elaborate clothes
informal archaic

Examples

He likes to fig out before important meetings to impress his clients.
They figged out in their finest clothes for the annual gala.
She spent an hour figging out for her first big date.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes
  • Antonym: to dress down - to wear casual or less formal clothes

Etymology

Possibly from 'fig' (slang for dress or attire), of uncertain origin, + 'out' (completing the action).
