to shoot down

1. VERB

/ ʃut daʊn / (AmE), / ʃuːt daʊn / (BrE)
to reject or dismiss an idea, proposal, or suggestion, often abruptly or decisively
to decisively reject (proposal, idea)

Examples

The committee shot down the proposal after only a brief discussion.
Every time I suggest a new idea, my boss shoots down my contributions.
Her innovative plan was unfortunately shot down before it could be properly evaluated.

Synset Examples

kill a motion
he shot down the student's proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept or consider something
  • Antonym: to approve - to accept or agree to something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02479111-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʃuːt daʊn / (AmE), / ʃuːt daʊn / (BrE)
to fire at an aircraft or missile and force it to fall to the ground
bring down aircraft by shooting

Examples

The fighter pilot managed to shoot down two enemy planes during the battle.
They used surface-to-air missiles to shoot down the incoming drone.
The news reported that the military had shot down an unidentified aircraft near the border.

Synset Examples

the enemy landed several of our aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring down - to cause something, especially aircraft, to fall by force
  • Synonym: to take out - to destroy or disable, especially in a military context
