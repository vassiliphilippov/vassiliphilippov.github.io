to take any more

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˌɛni ˈmɔr / (AmE), / teɪk ˌɛni ˈmɔː / (BrE)
to be able to endure or tolerate something further; to withstand additional stress, difficulty, or trouble.
to endure or tolerate further

Examples

After hours of loud music, I just couldn't take any more and had to leave the party.
She was so frustrated with the delays that she felt she couldn't take any more.
If you take any more criticism, you might start to doubt your abilities.

Synset Examples

I can't take any more of this nonsense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endure - to suffer patiently; to tolerate
  • Synonym: to tolerate - to allow or accept something, especially something unpleasant
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying; to cease enduring or persisting
