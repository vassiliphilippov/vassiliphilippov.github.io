to prop up

1. VERB

/ prɑp ʌp / (AmE), / prɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to support something by placing it against a solid or rigid object
support by placing against something solid

Examples

We had to prop up the old fence with a few wooden planks.
She used some pillows to prop up her leg after the injury.
The workers propped up the collapsing wall to keep it from falling.

Synset Examples

shore and buttress an old building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shore up - to support or strengthen something that is weak or at risk of collapse

2. VERB

/ prɑp ʌp / (AmE), / prɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to support or prevent something from falling by placing something under or against it; to help or sustain something that is weak or about to fail
to support or stabilize

Examples

The government tried to prop up the financial sector during the crisis.
She used pillows to prop up her broken leg while resting.
International aid was used to prop up the struggling hospital.

Synset Examples

They tried to prop up the economy with new policies.
The volunteers worked to prop up the charity.
He propped his feet up on the footstool while reading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to bear the weight of something; to keep from falling
  • Antonym: to undermine - to weaken something or make it less effective
