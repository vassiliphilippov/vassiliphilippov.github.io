to drag up

1. VERB

/ dræɡ ʌp / (AmE), / dræɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to mention something unpleasant, embarrassing, or forgotten from the past, often when it would be better left unsaid
mention something unpleasant from the past

Examples

Please don't drag up that argument again; we've already moved on.
Every family gathering, my uncle drags up stories from when we were kids.
She hates it when people drag up her mistakes from years ago.

Synset Examples

Drag up old stories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring up - to mention or introduce a topic in conversation
  • Antonym: to bury - to deliberately not talk about or recall something from the past

2. VERB

/ dræɡ ʌp / (AmE), / dræɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to mention or recall a sensitive, unpleasant, or past subject, especially when it would be better left forgotten
mention or recall an unpleasant or past subject

Examples

She always drags up embarrassing stories at family gatherings.
Why did you drag up that argument from last year?
He tends to drag up old grievances whenever he's upset.

Synset Examples

She dragged up the topic at the worst moment.
He dragged up old memories during the conversation.
I hate when he drags up past mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring up - to raise or mention a topic in conversation
  • Antonym: to let go - to stop mentioning or thinking about something, especially past events
