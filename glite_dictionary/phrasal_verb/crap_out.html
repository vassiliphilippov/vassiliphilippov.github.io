to crap out

1. VERB

/ kræp aʊt / (AmE), / kræp aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) to stop functioning suddenly, or to quit or give up unexpectedly, especially due to exhaustion or failure.
stop working or give up unexpectedly
informal

Examples

My laptop crapped out just as I was about to save my work.
The old car finally crapped out halfway to the campsite.
He tried to finish the marathon but crapped out at mile twenty.

Synset Examples

The machine crapped out right before the deadline.
I thought I could finish, but I crapped out after the first mile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying; to surrender or quit
  • Synonym: to break down - to stop functioning, especially mechanically

2. VERB

/ kræp aʊt / (AmE), / kræp aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) to fail or stop working or trying, especially suddenly or unexpectedly.
fail or give up unexpectedly
informal

Examples

My old computer finally crapped out just as I was about to save my project.
He crapped out halfway through the marathon due to exhaustion.
The negotiations crapped out when neither side would compromise.

Synset Examples

He crapped out during the exam because he hadn't studied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying; surrender
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired result; to do well
