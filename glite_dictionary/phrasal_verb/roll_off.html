to roll off

1. VERB

/ roʊl ɑf / (AmE), / rəʊl ɒf / (BrE)
to recite or repeat something quickly, fluently, and often with little effort
recite or repeat fluently and easily

Examples

She can roll off statistics about the industry without hesitation.
During his presentation, he rolled off the company's achievements from the past decade.
He likes to roll off facts to impress people at parties.

Synset Examples

he could rattle off the names of all the chemical elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rattle off - to recite rapidly and easily, especially a list or sequence
  • Synonym: to reel off - to say or write a list of things quickly and easily
