to stand in

1. VERB

/ stænd ɪn / (AmE), / stænd ɪn / (BrE)
to take someone else's place, especially temporarily; to act as a substitute for someone or something
act as a substitute

Examples

When the main actor fell ill, his understudy had to stand in for him.
Could you stand in for me at tomorrow's meeting if I'm still unwell?
She often stands in for her boss when he is on vacation.

Synset Examples

The young teacher had to substitute for the sick colleague
The skim milk substitutes for cream — we are on a strict diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to take the place of another
