to box in

1. VERB

/ bɑks ɪn / (AmE), / bɒks ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose or confine someone or something so that movement is difficult or impossible
enclose or restrict movement

Examples

The parked cars boxed in my vehicle, making it impossible to leave.
She felt boxed in by the narrow walls of her office.
The new regulations box in small businesses, limiting their ability to compete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block in - to obstruct or trap by blocking movement
  • Antonym: free - to make free; release from confinement

2. VERB

/ bɑks ɪn / (AmE), / bɒks ɪn / (BrE)
to limit someone's ability to act freely or restrict movement or choice
restrict or confine someone or something

Examples

The new city laws will box in restaurants, making it harder for them to expand.
She didn't want her parents to box in her choices about her career.
With all those cars blocking us, we were completely boxed in and couldn't leave the parking lot.

Synset Examples

The new regulations will box in small businesses.
Don't let fear box you in; explore your options.
They felt boxed in by the strict company policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep within limits; restrict
  • Antonym: free - to allow to move or act freely; not restricted
