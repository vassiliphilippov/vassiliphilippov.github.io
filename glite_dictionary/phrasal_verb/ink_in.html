to ink in

1. VERB

/ ɪŋk ɪn / (AmE), / ɪŋk ɪn / (BrE)
to write or finalize details, especially by using ink or making something definite
write or finalize details

Examples

After sketching the plan, she inked in the final details to complete the drawing.
They will ink in the agreement once both parties approve the terms.
Once you ink in your answers, they cannot be changed.

Synset Examples

He decided to ink in the details after outlining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fill in - to complete by adding information
  • Antonym: to pencil in - to arrange tentatively; not finalized
