to partake in

1. VERB

/ pɑrˈteɪk ɪn / (AmE), / pɑːˈteɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to take part or participate in an activity or event
to participate in something

Examples

Many students choose to partake in extracurricular activities after school.
She was eager to partake in the city's annual festival.
New employees are encouraged to partake in team-building exercises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participate in - to take part in something

Etymology

From 'partake', ultimately from Middle English 'partaken', based on 'part' + 'take'.

2. VERB

/ pɑrˈteɪk ɪn / (AmE), / pɑːˈteɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to have a share in or participate in an activity, event, or experience
take part in; share in

Examples

All employees were invited to partake in the annual company picnic.
She chose not to partake in the heated discussion during the meeting.
Many students happily partake in the school's various extracurricular activities.

Synset Examples

We shared the cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to participate - take part in an activity or event
  • Antonym: to abstain - deliberately not take part in something

Etymology

From Middle English 'partaken', from part (portion) + take.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02300169-v
Synset POS: v
