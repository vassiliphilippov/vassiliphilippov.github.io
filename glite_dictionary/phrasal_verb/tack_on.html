to tack on

1. VERB

/ tæk ɑn / (AmE), / tæk ɒn / (BrE)
to add or attach something, especially as an extra or afterthought
add or attach as extra

Examples

The teacher decided to tack on an extra assignment at the end of the semester.
You can't just tack on fees without telling customers first.
She tacked on a brief disclaimer to her statement.

Synset Examples

append a charm to the necklace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to append - to add something as an attachment or supplement
  • Synonym: to attach - to fasten, join, or connect something

Etymology

From the verb "tack" (to fasten with tacks or short nails) + "on".

2. VERB

/ tæk ɑn / (AmE), / tæk ɒn / (BrE)
to add something, especially something extra or not originally planned, to a schedule, list, or activity
add something extra, especially not originally planned

Examples

They decided to tack on extra time at the end of the meeting.
The teacher agreed to tack on ten more minutes for questions.
We had to tack on another hour to finish the presentation.

Synset Examples

They had to tack on extra time for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add - to put something together with something else
  • Synonym: to append - to add as a supplement or attachment
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away

Etymology

From the verb 'tack', originally meaning to attach with tacks (small nails); figurative use to mean 'add on'.
