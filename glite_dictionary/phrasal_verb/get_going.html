to get going

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
to begin to be active or to start doing something; to set out or leave
start to be active; begin
informal

Examples

We should get going if we want to catch the early train.
It took her a while to get going this morning after staying up late.
Let's get going on that project before the deadline approaches.

Synset Examples

Get cracking, please!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get moving - start to move or become active
  • Synonym: to get started - begin doing something
  • Antonym: stop - to cease activity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00349195-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
to begin to move, start doing something, or set something in motion
begin to move or start action

Examples

We need to get going or we’ll be late for the meeting.
It took a bit of motivation, but I finally got going on my project.
Let’s get going before the weather gets worse.

Synset Examples

I start at eight in the morning
Ready, set, go!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin doing something or begin to happen
  • Antonym: stop - to cease movement or action
