to drive out

1. VERB

/ draɪv aʊt / (AmE), / draɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place or position, often using authority or effort
force to leave a place

Examples

The new regulations aim to drive out illegal operators from the market.
Rising rents threatened to drive out longtime residents of the neighborhood.
The villagers managed to drive out the invading wildlife using loud noises.

Synset Examples

The police routed them out of bed at 2 A.M.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place, especially officially
  • Synonym: to evict - to force someone to leave a property, especially through legal process
  • Antonym: welcome - to receive or accept someone in a friendly way

to force someone or something to leave a place or situation, often against their will; to expel or remove, especially by force or influence

Examples

A cup of tea can help drive out the chill on a cold morning.
She tried to drive out all negative thoughts before her big exam.

3. VERB

/ draɪv aʊt / (AmE), / draɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place, situation, or condition, often by persistent action
force to leave or remove

Examples

The protesters hoped their actions would drive out the corrupt officials.
Strong winds can drive out the clouds and bring clear skies.
The company implemented new measures to drive out inefficiency from its operations.

Synset Examples

The loud noise will drive out the quiet atmosphere.
They tried to drive out the pests from their home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone or something to leave a place
  • Synonym: to evict - to expel someone from a property, especially with legal force
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone or something to enter
