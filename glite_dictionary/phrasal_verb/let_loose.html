to let loose

1. VERB

/ lɛt lus / (AmE), / lɛt luːs / (BrE)
to make a sound or utterance freely or suddenly; to express something audibly, especially a sound or exclamation
emit or utter a sound suddenly

Examples

The children let loose a cheer when their team scored the winning goal.
He let loose a loud sigh after hearing the disappointing news.
During the performance, the singer suddenly let loose a wild, joyful laugh.

Synset Examples

She let out a big heavy sigh
He uttered strange sounds that nobody could understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utter - to say or make a sound with your voice
  • Synonym: to let out - to allow something, such as a sound, to escape or be expressed

2. VERB

/ lɛt lus / (AmE), / lɛt luːs / (BrE)
to release or set free from restraint or confinement
release; set free

Examples

During the celebration, the organizers let loose hundreds of balloons into the sky.
The scientist warned that the new virus could let loose a series of unpredictable consequences.
She accidentally let loose her secrets during the conversation.

Synset Examples

let loose mines
Loose terrible plagues upon humanity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to allow something to move freely, to let go
  • Antonym: to restrain - to hold back or keep under control
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ lɛt lus / (AmE), / lɛt luːs / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to become free, or to relax and act without restraint
allow to be free; behave freely

Examples

After the big exam, everyone wanted to let loose and have some fun.
They let loose the dogs into the garden to run freely.
At the party, she finally let loose and danced without worrying about anyone watching.

Synset Examples

After a long week, it's time to let loose and relax.
The coach decided to let the players loose for a friendly match.
At the concert, she let loose and enjoyed herself.
He decided to let loose during the weekend getaway.
They let the dog loose in the park to run.
He let one loose during the party and it caused a stir.
It's time to turn loose the dogs in the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to set someone or something free
  • Antonym: to restrain - to keep something under control or within limits
