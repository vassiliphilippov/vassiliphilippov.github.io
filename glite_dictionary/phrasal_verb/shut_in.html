to shut in

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt ɪn / (AmE), / ʃʌt ɪn / (BrE)
to surround or enclose something completely, preventing escape or movement
to surround or enclose completely

Examples

As the fog thickened, it seemed to shut in the whole village.
The tall buildings shut in the small park from sunlight.
They built a high stone wall to completely shut in the garden.

Synset Examples

Darkness enclosed him
They closed in the porch with a fence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to close in all sides; to surround
  • Antonym: release - to set free; to allow to escape
