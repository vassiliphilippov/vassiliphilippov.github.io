to bounce on

1. VERB

/ baʊns ɑn / (AmE), / baʊns ɒn / (BrE)
to take advantage of an opportunity quickly or eagerly, often before others do
seize an opportunity quickly
informal

Examples

As soon as the offer was announced, she bounced on it and made the purchase.
You have to bounce on these jobs before someone else applies.
Investors were quick to bounce on the opportunity before the market changed.

Synset Examples

If you see a good deal, bounce on it quickly.
When the opportunity arose, she decided to bounce on it.
He told me to bounce on it before someone else does.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jump on - to seize an opportunity quickly
