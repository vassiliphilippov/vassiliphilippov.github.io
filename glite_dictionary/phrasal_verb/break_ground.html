to break ground

1. VERB

/ breɪk ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / breɪk ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to begin work on a new construction project or to start an important undertaking
to begin a construction project or task

Examples

The city will break ground on the new library later this year.
Construction companies often hold ceremonies when they break ground for major buildings.
Innovative scientists regularly break ground in the field of medical research.

Synset Examples

This initiative will break ground for sustainable energy projects.
They plan to break ground on the new park next week.
The architect will break ground for the museum this summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commence - to begin or start something
  • Antonym: finish - to bring something to an end or completion
