to gad about

1. VERB

/ ɡæd əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ɡad əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to go from place to place aimlessly or for pleasure, often without a definite plan
go from place to place for pleasure or aimlessly

Examples

During her year abroad, she liked to gad about the city, discovering hidden cafes and shops.
He spent the summer gadding about the countryside with his friends instead of working.
Rather than stay indoors on weekends, she always finds a reason to gad about.

Synset Examples

She loves to gad and explore new places.
He decided to gad instead of staying home.
I gad around town looking for the best coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to move about with no fixed course or aim
  • Synonym: to roam - to travel or move about without a fixed plan or destination

Etymology

From the verb 'gad' (to wander), which may derive from Middle English 'gadden' (to hurry).
