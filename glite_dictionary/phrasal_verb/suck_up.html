to suck up

1. VERB

/ sʌk ʌp / (AmE), / sʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to take in or absorb a liquid, substance, or influence, both literally and figuratively
absorb, take in (liquid or metaphorically)

Examples

The roots suck up moisture from the soil after it rains.
He tried to suck up as much information as possible before the meeting.
The plant quickly sucked up all the nutrients from the fertilizer.

Synset Examples

The sponge absorbs water well
She drew strength from the minister's words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (a liquid, energy, or other substance)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01541851-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sʌk ʌp / (AmE), / sʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone by flattering them or acting in a servile manner
try to gain favor by flattering
informal

Examples

Everyone knows she's just trying to suck up to the manager for a promotion.
He spent the whole meeting sucking up to the boss, and it was embarrassing to watch.
You don't have to suck up to be appreciated for your hard work.

Synset Examples

He is always kowtowing to his boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kowtow - to act in an excessively subservient manner
  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise in an insincere way, often to gain favor
  • Antonym: snub - to treat someone with disdain or ignore them deliberately
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00882499-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sʌk ʌp / (AmE), / sʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone, especially in a way that is insincere or obsequious
try to ingratiate oneself insincerely
informal

Examples

He always tries to suck up to his boss whenever a promotion is mentioned.
Stop sucking up to the teacher and just do your work like everyone else.
She blatantly sucked up to her manager in hopes of getting a raise.

Synset Examples

She is playing up to the chairman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely
  • Synonym: to butter up - to be extra nice to someone to gain favor (informal)
  • Antonym: insult - to speak to or treat with disrespect or scornful abuse
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01808720-v
Synset POS: v
