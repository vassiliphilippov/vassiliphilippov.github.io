to be a wuss

1. VERB

/ bi ə wʊs / (AmE), / biː ə wʊs / (BrE)
to act in a cowardly or overly timid way; to avoid something out of fear or nervousness
act in a cowardly way
informal slang

Examples

Don't be a wuss; it's just a little rain.
He refused to be a wuss and tried the spicy food.
Whenever it's time to speak in public, she worries she'll be a wuss and back out.

Synset Examples

Don't be a wuss; it's just a little rain.
Stop being a wuss and try the roller coaster.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: be brave - to act courageously, the opposite of being timid or cowardly
  • Synonym: wimp out - to back out of doing something due to fear or nervousness

Etymology

From 'wuss', a slang term possibly blending 'wimp' and 'puss' (slang for 'cat' or 'coward'), attested in American English since the 1980s.
