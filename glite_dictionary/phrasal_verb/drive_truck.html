to drive truck

1. VERB

/ draɪv trʌk / (AmE), / draɪv trʌk / (BrE)
to operate or be employed to operate a large truck, especially as a profession or job
to operate a large truck as a job
american

Examples

After getting his commercial license, he began to drive truck for a local shipping company.
Many people drive truck across the country for freight companies.
She had always wanted to drive truck and see new places every week.

Synset Examples

They hired him to drive truck across state lines.
She decided to drive truck to make extra money.
After years of driving truck, he became a skilled operator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive lorry - to operate a large truck (British English usage)
