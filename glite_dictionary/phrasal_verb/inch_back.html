to inch back

1. VERB

/ ɪntʃ bæk / (AmE), / ɪntʃ bak / (BrE)
to move slowly and cautiously backwards, often in small increments
move backwards slowly and carefully

Examples

The crowd began to inch back as the flames grew higher.
She had to inch back along the narrow ledge.
Realizing the animal was agitated, he slowly inched back to a safe distance.

Synset Examples

The crowd started to inch back as the speaker arrived.
He had to inch back to avoid the oncoming car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw from a position
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or ahead
