to run away

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / rʌn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to escape from the control or restraint of someone or something; to become uncontrollable
escape from control or restraint

Examples

The project started well but quickly began to run away from the team's control.
Inflation can run away if the government doesn't intervene.
Sometimes technology seems to run away with our lives and reshape society faster than we expect.

Synset Examples

Industry is running away with us all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get free from something that holds or restricts
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02104754-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / rʌn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place or person suddenly and secretly, usually to escape from danger, trouble, or control
to flee or escape quickly

Examples

When the fire alarm sounded, everyone began to run away from the building.
The children tried to run away from home but came back by nightfall.
As soon as the dog saw the rain, it would run away and hide under the bed.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place where you are being held or kept
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in the same place instead of leaving
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02079296-v
Synset POS: v
