to scent out

1. VERB

/ sɛnt aʊt / (AmE), / sɛnt aʊt / (BrE)
to recognize or discover something, especially by or as if by sense of smell
detect or discover, as by smelling

Examples

The police dog was able to scent out the hidden package in minutes.
She has an uncanny ability to scent out a good bargain at any market.
Investigators managed to scent out the source of the trouble despite the confusion.

Synset Examples

He can smell out trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sniff out - to discover or detect something, especially using the sense of smell
