to stay in favor

1. VERB

/ steɪ ɪn ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / steɪ ɪn ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to continue to be liked, approved of, or supported by someone, especially a person in authority
remain liked or approved by someone

Examples

He always tries to stay in favor with his boss by volunteering for extra work.
Some officials changed their opinions to stay in favor with the new administration.
She remained neutral in order to stay in favor with both sides of the argument.

Synset Examples

The placeman always agreed with the boss to stay in favor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fall out of favor - lose someone's approval or support
  • Degree Lower: win favor - gain someone's approval or support
