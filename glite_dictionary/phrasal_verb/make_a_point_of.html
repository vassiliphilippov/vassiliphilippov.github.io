to make a point of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə pɔɪnt ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ə pɔɪnt ɒv / (BrE)
to be sure to do something because you think it is important or necessary
ensure to do something important

Examples

She always makes a point of greeting every guest personally.
I made a point of calling my parents on their anniversary.
They make a point of recycling all their waste at home.

Synset Examples

Let's make a point of discussing this in tomorrow's meeting.
