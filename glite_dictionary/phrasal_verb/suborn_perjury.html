to suborn perjury

1. VERB

/ səˈbɔrn ˈpɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / sʌˈbɔːn ˈpɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
to persuade or induce someone to commit perjury, especially by offering an incentive or coercion
induce someone to commit perjury
legal

Examples

The witness claimed he was offered money to suborn perjury in the high-profile case.
Any attorney who attempts to suborn perjury risks severe consequences, including loss of license.
He was convicted after the prosecution proved he had tried to suborn perjury during the trial.

Synset Examples

The lawyer was disbarred for trying to suborn perjury.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: perjury - the offense of willfully telling an untruth in a court after having taken an oath or affirmation

Etymology

From Latin *subornare* (to secretly furnish or equip) and *perjurium* (false oath).
