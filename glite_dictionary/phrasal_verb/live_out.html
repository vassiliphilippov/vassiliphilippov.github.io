to live out

1. VERB

/ lɪv aʊt / (AmE), / lɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to continue to live until the end of a period or one's life, often in a particular way or place
to continue living until the end

Examples

She hopes to live out her days in peace in the countryside.
He managed to live out his final years surrounded by his family.
Many people dream of living out their retirement by the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to survive - to remain alive; to continue to live or exist
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02625100-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɪv aʊt / (AmE), / lɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to work in someone's house or household but reside elsewhere, not living at the place of employment
work in a house but live elsewhere

Examples

After finding a new apartment, Anna decided to live out and commute to her job as a nanny.
Most domestic staff in the neighborhood live out and travel to their employers' homes each day.
He preferred to live out because it gave him more privacy and flexibility.

Synset Examples

our cook lives out; he can easily commute from his home

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to live in - to work and reside in the same house or household
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
