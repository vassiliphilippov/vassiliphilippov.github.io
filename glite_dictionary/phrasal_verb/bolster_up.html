to bolster up

1. VERB

/ ˈboʊlstər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlstə ʌp / (BrE)
to support or strengthen something, especially to make it more effective or to give added encouragement
support or strengthen

Examples

The CEO tried to bolster up the team's morale after the setback.
We need to bolster up our defenses against cyber attacks.
Additional training was provided to bolster up employees' skills before the launch.

Synset Examples

bolster morale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strengthen - make stronger or more effective
  • Antonym: to weaken - make less strong or effective

Etymology

From 'bolster' (Old English 'bolster', a cushion or support) + up (preposition).

2. VERB

/ ˈboʊlstər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlstə ʌp / (BrE)
to support or strengthen someone or something, especially in order to improve their condition or situation
support or strengthen

Examples

The government announced new measures to bolster up the economy after the recession.
She offered words of encouragement to bolster up his confidence before the interview.
They worked together to bolster up the team's morale during difficult times.

Synset Examples

He needed to bolster up his resume for the job.
She tried to bolster up his spirits after the loss.
Friends help to bolster up each other's morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance to; enable to function or act
  • Synonym: to strengthen - to make stronger or more powerful

Etymology

From the verb 'bolster,' which originally referred to providing with a bolster (a cushion or pillow), from Old English 'bolster'.
