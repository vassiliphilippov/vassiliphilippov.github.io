to bog down

1. VERB

/ bɑɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, person, or process) to become stuck or unable to progress, especially in mud or a difficult situation.
to become stuck or unable to progress

Examples

The truck bogged down in the thick mud, unable to move forward.
Negotiations bog down when participants can't agree on key points.
Progress on the project bogged down after several technical issues arose.

Synset Examples

The car bogged down in the sand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to stop running or moving, especially suddenly or unexpectedly

Etymology

From 'bog' (wet, muddy ground) + 'down'.

2. VERB

/ bɑɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to become stuck, as in mud, or unable to make progress
to become or cause to become stuck or slowed down

Examples

The heavy truck began to bog down in the wet field after the rain.
Disagreements over details can bog down a project for months.
We got bogged down in traffic during our drive to the airport.

Synset Examples

The mud mired our cart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mire - to cause to become stuck in mud or a difficult situation

Etymology

From 'bog' (wet, marshy ground) + 'down'.

/ bɔɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to cause to slow down, become stuck, or be unable to progress effectively, especially in a difficult situation
slow down or get stuck

Examples

The negotiations began to bog down after several weeks of debate.
Heavy rain can bog down even the best-prepared hikers on the trail.
Let’s not bog down this meeting with minor details.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From 'bog' (wet, muddy ground) + 'down', evoking the image of getting stuck in mud.

4. VERB

/ bɑɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to become stuck or unable to make progress in a task or activity
become stuck or make no progress

Examples

The project started well but soon bogged down in bureaucracy.
She often bogged down in the details and missed her deadlines.
Don't let yourself bog down with minor problems—stay focused on the main goal.

Synset Examples

She bogged down many times while she wrote her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to stop making progress; to be brought to a standstill
  • Antonym: move forward - to make progress; to proceed

Etymology

From 'bog' meaning a wet, muddy ground and 'down', metaphorically referring to becoming trapped like sinking in a bog.

5. VERB

/ bɑɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to become or cause to become stuck, delayed, or prevented from making progress by something
become stuck or hindered

Examples

The negotiations bogged down after several days with no agreement.
He tends to bog down in details and loses sight of the bigger picture.
Our plans were bogged down by unexpected complications.

Synset Examples

I felt bogged under all the work piled on my desk.
The project was bogged with too many revisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to come to a stop or be delayed
  • Synonym: to impede - to slow down or prevent progress
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or make progress

Etymology

Originally from 'bog' meaning wet, muddy ground where progress is difficult, figuratively applied to being hindered.
