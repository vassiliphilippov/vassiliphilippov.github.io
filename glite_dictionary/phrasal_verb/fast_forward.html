to fast forward

1. VERB

/ fæst ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / fɑːst ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to advance quickly through recorded video or audio, or skip quickly to a later part in a story or presentation
advance quickly through media or story

Examples

I used the remote to fast forward past the boring scenes.
He asked her to fast forward the recording to the main presentation.
Sometimes it's tempting to fast forward to the ending of a suspenseful movie.

Synset Examples

I had to fast forward through the commercials.
Can you fast forward to the end of the video?
Let’s fast forward to the best part of the story.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to rewind - to wind back to an earlier point in a video, audio, or narrative

Etymology

From 'fast' (quick) and 'forward' (toward the front, onward); originally referring to electronic tape devices.
