to talk down to

1. VERB

/ tɔk daʊn tu / (AmE), / tɔːk daʊn tuː / (BrE)
to speak to someone in a way that shows you think they are less intelligent or important than you
speak condescendingly to someone

Examples

Please don't talk down to me; I understand the situation perfectly well.
She hates it when her boss talks down to her during meetings.
Instead of talking down to students, he encourages open discussion and questions.

Synset Examples

Don't talk down to me like I'm a child.
He tends to talk down to his colleagues.
She felt insulted when he talked down to her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat someone with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Synonym: to belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
