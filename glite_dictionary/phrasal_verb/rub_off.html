to rub off

1. VERB

/ rʌb ɔf / (AmE), / rʌb ɒf / (BrE)
To be removed or worn away by repeated rubbing, friction, or contact.
be worn away by rubbing

Examples

The paint on the old door started to rub off after years of use.
If you scrub too hard, the label will rub off the bottle.
Some of the gold finish began to rub off where the ring touches the finger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear away - to gradually disappear or erode due to friction or use
  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away

2. VERB

/ ɹʌb ɑf / (AmE), / ɹʌb ɒf / (BrE)
(1) To remove something from a surface by rubbing it; (2) (of a quality, habit, or feeling) to be transferred to or influence another person through close contact or association.
become transferred by contact or remove by rubbing

Examples

She tried to rub off the ink stain from her shirt.
Her enthusiasm soon rubbed off on her classmates.
Make sure you rub off any dirt before painting the surface.

Synset Examples

He decided to rub himself off before heading to the party.
His passion for music rubbed off on his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influence - to affect someone or something
  • Antonym: to wear off - to gradually disappear
