to send away

1. VERB

/ sɛnd əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sɛnd əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to dismiss someone or end your association with them, often by telling them to leave
dismiss or end association with someone

Examples

After the scandal, the company decided to send away the manager responsible.
She was heartbroken when her friends sent away her from their group.
The club had to send away several members for violating the code of conduct.

Synset Examples

They dropped her after she had a child out of wedlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to order someone to leave their job or position
  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place or group
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet someone in a friendly way or invite to join
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02471362-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛnd əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sɛnd əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from their job or position; to terminate the employment of someone
dismiss from a job or position

Examples

After the scandal, the company decided to send away several senior executives.
He was heartbroken when they sent away his closest colleague without warning.
Management threatened to send away any employee caught violating company policy.

Synset Examples

The boss fired his secretary today
The company terminated 25% of its workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss from a job
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job
  • Antonym: hire - to employ someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02407967-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sɛnd əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sɛnd əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to dispatch or mail something to a particular destination
dispatch or mail something

Examples

You can send away the application form by post if you prefer.
After sorting the books, we sent away several old editions to charity.
She quickly sends away her packages every Monday morning.

Synset Examples

Please send away the documents by the end of the week.
I had to send away my old phone for recycling.
We decided to send away the invitations early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispatch - to send off to a destination
  • Synonym: mail - to send something by postal service
