to have done with

1. VERB

/ hæv dʌn wɪð / (AmE), / hæv dʌn wɪð / (BrE)
to finish or stop being involved with something, especially after a period of involvement or effort
finish or stop being involved

Examples

I wish you would have done with these endless complaints.
Once we have done with the paperwork, we can focus on the main project.
She wanted to have done with the argument and get on with her evening.

Synset Examples

Let's have done with this discussion and move on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish with - to bring something to an end or completion
  • Synonym: to give up - to stop doing something, to quit
