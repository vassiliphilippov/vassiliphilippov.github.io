to ebb down

1. VERB

/ ɛb daʊn / (AmE), / ɛb daʊn / (BrE)
(of water, particularly tides) to flow back, recede, or diminish after rising.
recede or diminish (especially of water/tides)

Examples

As the storm subsided, the floodwaters began to ebb down from the fields.
The excitement in the room seemed to ebb down after the announcement was made.
At sunset, we watched the waves ebb down along the quiet shore.

Synset Examples

the tides ebbed at noon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flow - move along in a stream; to move smoothly and continuously
  • Synonym: to recede - move back or further away from a previous position

Etymology

From Old English 'ebba' (the flowing back of the tide).
