to stand firm

1. VERB

/ stænd fɝm / (AmE), / stænd fɜːm / (BrE)
to refuse to change your decision or position, especially when faced with opposition or pressure
refuse to yield under pressure

Examples

She decided to stand firm on her beliefs despite criticism from others.
The protesters stood firm even when the police arrived to disperse the crowd.
He is standing firm against demands for budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yield - to give way or surrender to pressure

2. VERB

/ stænd fɝːm / (AmE), / stænd fɜːm / (BrE)
to refuse to change your opinion or decision, even when other people try to persuade you to do so
refuse to change opinion or position

Examples

Despite the criticism, she decided to stand firm in her beliefs.
We must stand firm if we want to effect real change in the organization.
They stood firm against the pressure to compromise their principles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
  • Synonym: stand one's ground - to maintain your position and not retreat

3. VERB

/ stænd fɝm / (AmE), / stænd fɜːm / (BrE)
to refuse to change your decision or position, especially in the face of opposition or difficulty
remain resolute; not yield

Examples

Despite the mounting pressure, she continued to stand firm in her decision.
The company will stand firm on its policy, regardless of criticism.
It's not always easy to stand firm when others disagree with you.

Synset Examples

They will stand firm until their demands are met.
It's important to stand firm in your beliefs.
He must stand firm against the criticism he faces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yield - to give way under pressure; to surrender
  • Synonym: stand one's ground - to refuse to retreat or change position
