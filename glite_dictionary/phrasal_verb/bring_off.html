to bring off

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ɔf / (AmE), / brɪŋ ɒf / (BrE)
to succeed in doing something difficult or unexpected
succeed in doing something difficult

Examples

It was a challenging negotiation, but she managed to bring off a successful agreement.
Few people thought the team could bring off such a stunning victory.
He wasn't sure he could bring off the stunt, but he gave it his best shot.

Synset Examples

I managed to carry the box upstairs
She pulled it off, even though we never thought her capable of it
The pianist negociated the difficult runs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull off - to succeed in achieving something difficult
  • Synonym: to carry off - to succeed in doing something despite difficulties
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
