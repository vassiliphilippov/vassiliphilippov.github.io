to leave off

1. VERB

/ liv ɔf / (AmE), / liːv ɒf / (BrE)
to stop using or wearing (an item of clothing or accessory)
stop using or wearing something

Examples

In warm weather, you can leave off your coat and enjoy the sunshine.
He decided to leave off his tie for the informal party.
If you prefer comfort, just leave off your shoes when you come inside.

Synset Examples

leave off your jacket — no need to wear it here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take off - to remove an item of clothing from one's body
  • Antonym: to put on - to begin wearing an item of clothing

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ liv ɔf / (AmE), / liːv ɒf / (BrE)
to exclude or omit something or someone from consideration, inclusion, or acceptance
omit or exclude

Examples

Please leave off unnecessary details when you write your summary.
They accidentally left off my name from the guest list.
If you leave off those ingredients, the recipe will be much healthier.

Synset Examples

The bad results were excluded from the report
Leave off the top piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately not include something
  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or whole
  • Synonym: to omit - to leave out or not include
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00617464-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ liv ɔf / (AmE), / liːv ɒf / (BrE)
to stop doing something or to come to an end; to cease, especially temporarily or at a certain point.
to stop or cease

Examples

Let's leave off work for today and continue tomorrow.
He left off reading at chapter five and went to bed.
Remind me where I left off so I can pick up from there.

Synset Examples

the road leaves off at the edge of the forest
leave off where you started

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to cease doing something
  • Antonym: to continue - to keep on doing something without stopping

to stop temporarily or omit
