to fasten on

1. VERB

/ ˈfæsən ɑn / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsən ɒn / (BrE)
to take up or seize eagerly and adopt (an idea, plan, etc.)
seize or adopt eagerly (idea, plan)

Examples

She tends to fasten on new ideas as soon as she hears them.
The committee quickly fastened on the proposal and made it the centerpiece of discussion.
Children often fasten on particular theories to explain what they don't understand.

Synset Examples

take up new ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - to accept or adopt willingly (an idea, belief, etc.)
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept (an idea, plan, etc.)
