to lick out

1. VERB

/ lɪk aʊt / (AmE), / lɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to clean the inside of something thoroughly by licking it, typically referring to animals or people licking containers to remove remaining food or liquid
clean by licking inside

Examples

The dog licked out the empty peanut butter jar until it was spotless.
She always lets her cat lick out the yogurt pot when she's finished.
After baking, the children asked if they could lick out the mixing bowl.

Synset Examples

She let the dog lick out her ice cream bowl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lick - to pass the tongue over something
