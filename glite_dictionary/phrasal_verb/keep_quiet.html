to keep quiet

1. VERB

/ kip ˈkwaɪət / (AmE), / kiːp ˈkwaɪət / (BrE)
to refrain from telling others; to avoid sharing sensitive or confidential information
avoid divulging information

Examples

He managed to keep quiet about the surprise party until the very end.
Please keep quiet about the changes until they're officially announced.
If you can't keep quiet about confidential matters, people will stop trusting you.

Synset Examples

Don't tell him any secrets — he cannot keep his mouth shut!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep one's mouth shut - to remain silent, especially about sensitive matters
  • Antonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret information
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00939649-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kip ˈkwaɪət / (AmE), / kiːp ˈkwaɪət / (BrE)
to remain silent or refrain from speaking
remain silent; not speak

Examples

The children tried to keep quiet so they wouldn't wake the baby.
If you can't contribute, please keep quiet during the discussion.
He managed to keep quiet about the surprise party until the last minute.

Synset Examples

Let's keep quiet until the meeting starts.
Please keep quiet during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay silent - to not make any sound or speak
  • Antonym: to speak up - to express one's opinion or speak loudly
