to deduct points

1. VERB

/ dɪˈdʌkt pɔɪnts / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌkt pɔɪnts / (BrE)
to subtract points from a score, usually as a penalty
subtract points from a score

Examples

If you are late submitting your assignment, the professor will deduct points from your grade.
The referee had to deduct points for repeated fouls during the match.
The judge decided not to deduct points because the mistake was minor.

Synset Examples

The teacher deducted ten points from Gryffindor for noise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: add points - to increase a score by granting extra points
