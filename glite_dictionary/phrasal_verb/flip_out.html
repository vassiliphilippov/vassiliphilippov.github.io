to flip out

1. VERB

/ flɪp aʊt / (AmE), / flɪp aʊt / (BrE)
To lose control of one's emotions; to become extremely angry, upset, or excited.
lose control emotionally
informal

Examples

He completely flipped out when he saw the mess in the kitchen.
Try not to flip out if they arrive late—they probably got stuck in traffic.
I almost flipped out when I realized I had lost my passport.

Synset Examples

He flipped when he heard that he was being laid off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak out - to become very anxious, upset, or angry
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become calmer and less agitated
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00585887-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ flɪp aʊt / (AmE), / flɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to react in an excited, delighted, or surprised way
react excitedly or in surprise
informal

Examples

She nearly flipped out when she saw the surprise party waiting for her.
The kids flipped out over the news that school was canceled.
He flipped out when he found out he had won the competition.

Synset Examples

he flipped when he heard that he was accepted into Princeton University

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak out - to become very excited, angry, or afraid
