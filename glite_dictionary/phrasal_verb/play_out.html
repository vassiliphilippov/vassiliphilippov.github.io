to play out

1. VERB

/ pleɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pleɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to develop or happen until a conclusion is reached, or to be performed until the end
develop or be performed to conclusion

Examples

We watched the tense negotiations play out on the news.
No one knows how this complex situation will play out.
The story played out over several dramatic weeks.

Synset Examples

How will the election drama be played out?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unfold - to gradually develop or become clear
  • Degree Higher: end - to come to a conclusion
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01719044-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pleɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pleɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to become spent, exhausted, or used up, especially over a period of time
become exhausted or used up

Examples

The team's energy played out in the final quarter, costing them the win.
After years of service, the old generator finally played out.
His patience played out before the negotiations could end.

Synset Examples

The champion's strength played out fast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear out - to become or cause to become unusable by long or hard use
  • Synonym: to run out - to become used up or exhausted
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00573865-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pleɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pleɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to continue playing a game, match, or contest until it finishes, especially to the end regardless of the outcome
continue playing until finished

Examples

Despite knowing they would lose, the team decided to play out the match to the very end.
The chess players agreed to play out the game even though it seemed obvious who would win.
We have to play out the last few minutes of the match, regardless of the score.

Synset Examples

We have got to play this game out, even thought it is clear that we have last

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - to bring something to an end or completion
  • Antonym: to quit - to stop or give up before something is finished
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01084045-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ pleɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pleɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to use up or exhaust a supply, resources, energy, or strength completely
use up or exhaust completely

Examples

After hours of hiking, our energy began to play out and we had to rest.
The company's funds finally played out during the lengthy legal battle.
Don't let your patience play out before the meeting ends.

Synset Examples

exhaust one's savings
We quickly played out our strength

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to use up completely; drain of resources or energy
  • Synonym: to deplete - to reduce or use up a supply of something
  • Antonym: to replenish - to fill something up again, especially after it has been used
Source: WordNet
