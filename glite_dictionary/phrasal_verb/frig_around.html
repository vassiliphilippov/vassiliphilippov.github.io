to frig around

1. VERB

/ frɪɡ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / frɪɡ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time aimlessly or be lazy; to idle or waste time without doing anything productive
be lazy or idle
informal

Examples

Stop frigging around and help me clean the garage.
He tends to frig around instead of getting his assignments done.
They spent the afternoon just frigging around in the park.

Synset Examples

Her son is just bumming around all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess around - to spend time doing unimportant things or being idle
  • Antonym: work - to engage in productive activity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645690-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ frɪɡ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / frɪɡ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
To waste time or behave in an irresponsible or unproductive manner, often instead of doing work.
waste time or act irresponsibly
informal

Examples

Stop frigging around and help me set the table.
If you frig around during class, you won’t finish your assignments.
The boss warned us not to frig around when there’s work to do.

Synset Examples

Don’t frig around; we need to finish this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess around - waste time or behave irresponsibly
  • Synonym: to fool around - spend time in a playful or unproductive way

Etymology

Alteration of the more vulgar phrase 'fuck around', with 'frig' as a milder substitute.
