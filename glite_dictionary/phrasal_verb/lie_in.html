to lie in

1. VERB

/ laɪ ɪn / (AmE), / laɪ ɪn / (BrE)
to have something as its source, cause, or basis; to originate in
have as its source or cause

Examples

The real difficulty lies in understanding the underlying concepts.
The answer to this question lies in careful research.
Much of the problem lies in poor communication between departments.

Synset Examples

The problems dwell in the social injustices in this country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to originate - to begin, arise, or derive from a source
  • Synonym: result from - to be caused by or as a consequence of

2. VERB

/ laɪ ɪn / (AmE), / laɪ ɪn / (BrE)
(of a woman) to be in bed during the period around childbirth, especially immediately after giving birth.
be in bed during childbirth or recovery
archaic

Examples

In former times, women would lie in for several weeks after giving birth.
During her recovery, she was expected to lie in and rest as much as possible.
The tradition of having relatives visit while a mother lies in still survives in some cultures.
