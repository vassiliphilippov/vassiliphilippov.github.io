to rake off

1. VERB

/ reɪk ɔf / (AmE), / reɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to take a share of money from a transaction, especially by dishonest or illegal means
take illicit share of money

Examples

The corrupt official tried to rake off a percentage from every contract.
Investigators suspect that he raked off thousands during the building project.
They discovered the manager had been quietly raking off cash from company sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skim off - take money or resources for oneself illicitly
  • Synonym: kickback - an illicit payment for services rendered
