to bracket out

1. VERB

/ ˈbræk.ɪt aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbræk.ɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to enclose a word, phrase, or passage in brackets, often to separate it from the surrounding text or to indicate modification
enclose text in brackets

Examples

Could you bracket out all personal comments in the first draft?
The editor decided to bracket out the disputed phrase for clarity.
In academic writing, it is common to bracket out clarifications added by the author.

Synset Examples

Please bracket this remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround something completely
