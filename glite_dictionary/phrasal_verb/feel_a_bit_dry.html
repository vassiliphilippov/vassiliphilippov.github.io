to feel a bit dry

1. VERB

/ fiːl ə bɪt draɪ / (AmE), / fiːl ə bɪt draɪ / (BrE)
to seem boring, lacking in interest, or without excitement
seem boring or uninteresting
informal

Examples

The lecture started to feel a bit dry after the first hour.
If your writing feels a bit dry, try adding some anecdotes to liven it up.
This documentary feels a bit dry compared to the director's earlier work.

Synset Examples

This conversation is feeling a bit dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - not interesting; dull
  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
