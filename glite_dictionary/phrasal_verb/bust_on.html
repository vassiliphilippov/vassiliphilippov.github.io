to bust on

1. VERB

/ bʌst ɑn / (AmE), / bʌst ɒn / (BrE)
to criticize or tease (someone), especially in a playful or informal way
criticize or tease, often playfully
informal american

Examples

My brother loves to bust on me whenever I make a silly mistake.
During the meeting, coworkers started to bust on Tim about his colorful tie.
You can't just bust on people for trying something new.

Synset Examples

She tends to bust on her friends when they slip up.
Don't bust on me for my choices; I know what I want.
They always bust on him for his outdated fashion sense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful way
  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone, often unkindly
