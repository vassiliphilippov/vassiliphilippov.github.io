to stir trouble

1. VERB

/ stɝ ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / stə ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
to deliberately cause conflict, disorder, or confusion in a situation or group
cause conflict or confusion

Examples

Some people just like to stir trouble wherever they go.
Be careful what you say around him; he's always trying to stir trouble.
She was accused of attempting to stir trouble among the employees.
