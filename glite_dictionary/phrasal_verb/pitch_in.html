to pitch in

1. VERB

/ pɪtʃ ɪn / (AmE), / pɪtʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to join others in contributing work, effort, or resources towards a common goal or task
contribute effort or resources

Examples

If everyone pitches in, the work will be done in no time.
They all decided to pitch in and buy a leaving gift for their colleague.
The neighbors pitched in to help rebuild the storm-damaged fence.

Synset Examples

If we all pitch in, we can finish the project faster.
She was happy to pitch in with the community clean-up.
Everyone agreed to pitch in for the holiday party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chip in - to contribute money or effort toward a cause or group activity

Etymology

Originally American English (mid-19th century), figurative use of 'pitch' meaning to throw, implying energetic involvement.
