to buttress a claim

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌtrəs ə kleɪm / (AmE), / ˈbʌtrɪs ə kleɪm / (BrE)
to support or strengthen an argument or assertion, usually by providing additional evidence or reasoning
support or strengthen an argument

Examples

She presented new data to buttress a claim that the policy was effective.
Experts often buttress a claim with references to previous research.
The lawyer tried to buttress a claim by citing relevant legal precedents.

Synset Examples

She needed to buttress her claims with evidence.
A solid argument can buttress your case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support a claim - to give support to an argument or assertion
  • Antonym: contradict a claim - to assert the opposite of a claim

Etymology

From Old French 'boterace' (a structure built against a wall for support); 'claim' from Latin 'clamare' (to call out).
