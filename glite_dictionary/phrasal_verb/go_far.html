to go far

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fɑr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fɑː / (BrE)
to achieve great success in life or in a particular activity
to achieve great success

Examples

If you keep working this hard, you will go far in your career.
Everyone knew she would go far after seeing her first performance.
He hasn't gone far yet, but he's determined to succeed.

Synset Examples

After he published his book, he had arrived
I don't know whether I can make it in science!
You will go far, my boy!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve what you intended or reach a high standard
  • Antonym: to fail - to not achieve success or reach a desired result

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fɑr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fɑː / (BrE)
to achieve great success, prominence, or influence; to have a strong impact or wide effect
achieve great success or wide influence

Examples

With talent and determination, she will go far in her career.
These innovative ideas could go far in shaping the future of technology.
His influence has gone far beyond the borders of his country.

Synset Examples

His accomplishments go far

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
  • Antonym: to fail - to not achieve success or the desired result
