to quest for

1. VERB

/ kwɛst fɔr / (AmE), / kwɛst fɔː / (BrE)
to go in search of or pursue something, especially something difficult to obtain
search or pursue something

Examples

Many scientists quest for new sources of renewable energy.
The hero set out to quest for the lost treasure.
She spent years questing for a sense of belonging.

Synset Examples

pursue a hobby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search for - to look for something
  • Synonym: hunt for - to pursue something in order to find it

Etymology

From Old French 'queste', from Latin 'quaestus' (search, seeking).
