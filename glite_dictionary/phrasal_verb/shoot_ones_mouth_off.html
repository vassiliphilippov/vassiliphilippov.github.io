to shoot one's mouth off

1. VERB

/ ʃut wʌnz maʊθ ɔf / (AmE), / ʃuːt wʌnz maʊθ ɒf / (BrE)
to speak carelessly or boastfully, often without considering the consequences
speak carelessly or boastfully

Examples

He tends to shoot his mouth off without thinking about whom he might offend.
Try not to shoot your mouth off during the meeting, or you'll cause trouble.
She realized she had shot her mouth off and regretted it immediately.

Synset Examples

She always shoots her mouth off and says things she later regrets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blab - to reveal secrets indiscreetly
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to remain silent or not disclose information
