to rely on

1. VERB

/ rɪˈlaɪ ɑn / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪ ɒn / (BrE)
to depend on someone or something for help, support, or to do what is expected
depend on for support or help

Examples

You can always rely on her to keep your secrets.
Farmers often rely on rain to grow their crops.
Many people rely on their smartphones for navigation every day.

Synset Examples

elderly parents often depend on their adult children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depend on - to need someone or something for support or help
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02669926-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈlaɪ ɑn / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪ ɒn / (BrE)
to trust or depend on someone or something to do what is needed or expected
depend or trust in something or someone

Examples

When faced with difficulties, I know I can rely on my closest friends.
Many students rely on scholarships to help pay for their education.
You shouldn't rely on public transport if you need to arrive on time.

Synset Examples

she is someone you can really rely on when times get rough
you can rely on his discretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depend on - to be determined or controlled by something or someone
  • Synonym: trust - to believe in the reliability or honesty of someone or something

Synset POS: v
