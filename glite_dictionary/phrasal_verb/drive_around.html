to drive around

1. VERB

/ draɪv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / draɪv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to take someone on a trip or for transport in a vehicle, usually for leisure or while showing places of interest
take someone for a ride in a vehicle

Examples

She offered to drive around her parents when they visited the city.
Tourists love to be driven around in open-top buses on warm days.
He drove around his friend to see the university campus before classes started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chauffeur - to drive someone else as a paid job or service
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02061973-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ draɪv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / draɪv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to drive a vehicle without any particular destination or purpose
drive without a specific destination

Examples

On weekends, we like to drive around the city just to relax and see what's new.
They spent the afternoon driving around the countryside, enjoying the fresh air.
When she felt bored, she would often drive around with no particular place in mind.

Synset Examples

we were driving around in the countryside on a fine Sunday morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruise - to drive or ride at a relaxed pace, especially without a specific destination
