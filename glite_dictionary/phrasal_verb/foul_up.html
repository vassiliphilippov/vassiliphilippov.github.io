to foul up

1. VERB

/ faʊl ʌp / (AmE), / faʊl ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mess of something; to destroy or ruin by making mistakes
make a mess of, ruin
informal

Examples

If you foul up the application, you might not get the job.
He really fouled up the experiment by skipping an important step.
Don't foul up this opportunity by being careless.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to do something badly or make mistakes, causing confusion or problems
  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or make something right

2. VERB

/ faʊl ʌp / (AmE), / faʊl ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mistake or spoil something through clumsiness or carelessness
make a mistake; spoil something
informal

Examples

Try not to foul up the application process this time.
She was worried she would foul up her lines during the performance.
They fouled up the entire schedule by arriving late.

Synset Examples

Don't foul up the project with careless mistakes.
He managed to foul up the simplest task.
I really fouled up the presentation yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make a mistake or spoil something
  • Synonym: to screw up - to make a serious mistake (informal)
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or put right something that has been spoiled
