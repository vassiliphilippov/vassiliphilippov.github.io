to tucker out

1. VERB

/ ˈtʌkər aʊt / (AmE), / ˈtʌkə aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone very tired; to exhaust completely
make someone very tired
informal american

Examples

The long hike really tuckered out the kids.
After a day of moving furniture, I was completely tuckered out.
Playing in the sun for hours will tucker out even the most energetic puppies.

Synset Examples

This kind of work exhausts me
I'm beat
He was all washed up after the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired
  • Antonym: to refresh - to make someone feel less tired or more energetic

Etymology

American English; origin uncertain, possibly from the verb 'tucker' (to tire, to exhaust), first recorded in the 19th century.
