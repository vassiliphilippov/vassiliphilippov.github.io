to stitch together a plan

1. VERB

/ stɪtʃ təˈɡɛðɚ ə plæn / (AmE), / stɪtʃ təˈɡɛðə ə plæn / (BrE)
to create a plan by combining different elements or ideas
to assemble a plan from parts

Examples

After the original idea failed, the team had to stitch together a plan using the remaining resources.
She quickly stitched together a plan to keep the event on schedule.
They spent the evening stitching together a plan from everyone's suggestions.

Synset Examples

He tried to stitch together a plan for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to invent or plan something carefully
  • Synonym: to formulate - to create or prepare something methodically
  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation, especially by combining available elements
