to think about

1. VERB

/ θɪŋk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / θɪŋk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to consider or take into account; to have in mind or reflect on something
to consider or reflect on

Examples

I need to think about what you said before making a decision.
She is thinking about applying for a new job next year.
Did you think about the consequences of your actions?

Synset Examples

He entertained the notion of moving to South America

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consider - to think carefully about something, especially before making a decision
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00713544-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ θɪŋk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / θɪŋk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to consider or have something in your mind; to reflect on or give attention to something mentally
consider or reflect on

Examples

I often think about my childhood when I visit my hometown.
She was thinking about changing her career to something more creative.
Before making a decision, he likes to think about all the possible outcomes.

Synset Examples

I'm thinking about my friends abroad
She always thinks about her children first

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to
