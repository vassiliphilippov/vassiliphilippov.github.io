to play for time

1. VERB

/ pleɪ fər taɪm / (AmE), / pleɪ fə taɪm / (BrE)
to deliberately delay or stall in order to gain more time before taking action or making a decision
delay deliberately to gain time

Examples

The negotiators tried to play for time until they could reach a better deal.
She started asking irrelevant questions just to play for time.
He often attempts to play for time when put on the spot in meetings.

Synset Examples

They needed to play for time until the report arrived.
She tried to play for time while making a decision.
He was just playing for time to think.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to delay taking action or speaking in order to gain time
  • Antonym: to hasten - to hurry or speed up
