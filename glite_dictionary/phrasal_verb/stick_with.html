to stick with

1. VERB

/ stɪk wɪð / (AmE), / stɪk wɪð / (BrE)
to continue to follow, support, or use something; to not change or abandon it
continue to follow or support

Examples

If you stick with the plan, you’re more likely to succeed.
She decided to stick with her original choice.
Even when it was hard, he stuck with his training routine.

Synset Examples

Stick to your principles
stick to the diet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abandon - to leave something behind or stop supporting it
  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly in a course of action
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00352060-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɪk wɪð / (AmE), / stɪk wɪð / (BrE)
to continue doing or supporting something, especially when it is difficult, or to remain committed to someone or something
continue doing or supporting; remain committed

Examples

Despite the challenges, she decided to stick with her original plan.
If you stick with him, you'll have a loyal friend for life.
Many students find it hard to stick with a new habit after the first week.

Synset Examples

I’ll stick with this project until it's finished.
He decided to stick with his current job for now.
You should stick with your workout routine to see results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly in an opinion or course of action despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something before finishing, usually because it is too difficult
