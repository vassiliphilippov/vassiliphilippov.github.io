to move out

1. VERB

/ muːv aʊt / (AmE), / muːv aʊt / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to leave a place or position
cause to leave a place

Examples

The principal asked the students to move out from the hallway.
The security guard moved out the protesters from the building.
During the drill, teachers were told to move out their classes quickly.

Synset Examples

The teacher took the children out of the classroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evacuate - to remove people from a place for safety
  • Synonym: remove - to take something or someone away from a place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02410046-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ muːv aʊt / (AmE), / muːv aʊt / (BrE)
to leave your home or office permanently and take your belongings with you
leave one's home or office permanently

Examples

After graduation, Sarah decided to move out and find her own apartment.
The company will move out of the old building next month.
It took them a week to move out and clean the entire house.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to move in - to begin living in a new home or office
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01860197-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ muːv aʊt / (AmE), / muːv aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a residence or home, especially to live somewhere else permanently
leave a residence or home

Examples

After getting a new job, he decided to move out and find his own apartment.
The landlord asked the tenants to move out by the end of the lease.
We're planning to move out next summer when the kids finish school.

Synset Examples

They plan to move out by the end of the month.
She decided to move out after college graduation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to move in - to begin living at a new residence
  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place
