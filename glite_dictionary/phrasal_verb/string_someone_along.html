to string someone along

1. VERB

/ strɪŋ ˈsʌmwʌn əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ ˈsʌmwən əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to deceive or mislead someone over a period of time, especially by making them believe something false, often to gain time or personal advantage
deceive or mislead someone over time

Examples

She was upset when she realized he had been stringing her along for months.
If you're not interested in the job, don't string them along.
Friends warned Lisa that Rob might just be stringing her along.

Synset Examples

She felt he was just stringing her along.
Stop stringing me along and tell me the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead someone on - to encourage someone to believe something false, often in romantic contexts
