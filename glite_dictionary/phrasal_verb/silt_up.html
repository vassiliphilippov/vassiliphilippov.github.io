to silt up

1. VERB

/ sɪlt ʌp / (AmE), / sɪlt ʌp / (BrE)
to become or cause to become blocked or filled with silt, especially in a river, harbor, or other waterway
become blocked with silt

Examples

Over time, the harbor began to silt up and was no longer accessible to large ships.
Neglected canals can quickly silt up after heavy rains.
Engineers had to dredge the area because the river had silted up.

Synset Examples

The river silted up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct or make passage difficult
