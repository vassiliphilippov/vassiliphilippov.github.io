to set out

1. VERB

/ sɛt aʊt / (AmE), / sɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to begin a journey; to leave one place in order to go somewhere else
begin a journey or leave for somewhere

Examples

We decided to set out early in the morning to avoid traffic.
The explorers set out on their expedition with high hopes.
She packed her bags and set out for Paris last week.

Synset Examples

The family took off for Florida

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially to start a journey
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛt aʊt / (AmE), / sɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or lay out objects, ideas, or information in an organized or logical manner
arrange or lay out in order

Examples

She set out the plates and cutlery before the guests arrived.
The report sets out the main points clearly and logically.
He set out the tools on the table so they were easy to find.

Synset Examples

lay out the clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay out - to arrange or spread something out
  • Antonym: to disorganize - to make something unorganized or disorderly

3. VERB

/ sɛt aʊt / (AmE), / sɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to begin a journey, project, or task; to start doing something with a particular aim
to begin a journey or activity

Examples

They set out on their hike before sunrise to avoid the afternoon heat.
After months of planning, she finally set out to start her own business.
The team set out to solve the complex problem with a clear strategy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin doing or experiencing something
  • Synonym: to begin - to start doing something
  • Antonym: finish - to bring an activity or journey to an end
