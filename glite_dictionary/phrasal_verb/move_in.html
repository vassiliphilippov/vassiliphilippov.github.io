to move in

1. VERB

/ muːv ɪn / (AmE), / muːv ɪn / (BrE)
to begin to occupy a place as a resident or start using a new location
begin to occupy a place

Examples

We're planning to move in next weekend after the renovations are complete.
As soon as the old tenants left, the new family moved in immediately.
The office is finally moving in to the new building downtown.

Synset Examples

The crowds are moving in

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to move out - to leave a place where one lived or stayed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01860082-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ muːv ɪn / (AmE), / muːv ɪn / (BrE)
to start living in a new house, apartment, or office; to take up residence in a new place
start living in a new home or office

Examples

They finally decided to move in together after dating for three years.
After the renovations were finished, we were able to move in last weekend.
New tenants will move in at the beginning of next month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to move out - to leave your home or office and stop living/working there
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01859968-v
Synset POS: v
