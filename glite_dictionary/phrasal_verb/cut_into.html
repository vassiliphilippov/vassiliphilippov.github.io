to cut into

1. VERB

/ kʌt ˈɪntu / (AmE), / kʌt ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to turn up, loosen, or remove earth, especially with a tool such as a spade or plow
turn or loosen earth (gardening/agriculture)

Examples

Before planting the seeds, gardeners need to cut into the soil to improve its aeration.
The tractor cut into the field, preparing the earth for the new crop.
She grabbed her spade and started cutting into the garden bed to loosen the dirt.

Synset Examples

Dig we must
turn over the soil for aeration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - break up and move earth
  • Synonym: plow - turn over soil to prepare for planting

2. VERB

/ kʌt ˈɪntu / (AmE), / kʌt ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to divide something into smaller pieces by cutting
divide into smaller pieces by cutting

Examples

She used a sharp knife to cut into the cake and serve slices to the guests.
Please cut into the cardboard to make smaller shapes for your project.
The chef cut into the vegetables to prepare them for the salad.

Synset Examples

Please cut the paper into smaller pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slice - to cut something into thin, flat pieces
