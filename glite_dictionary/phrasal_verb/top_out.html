to top out

1. VERB

/ tɑp aʊt / (AmE), / tɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave or abandon one's career just as one achieves a high level of success
leave career at peak success

Examples

After years of hard work, she decided to top out and focus on her family instead.
It's rare for athletes to top out when they're still winning championships.
He surprised everyone when he topped out at the very peak of his acting career.

Synset Examples

The financial consultant topped out at age 40 because he was burned out

2. VERB

/ tɑp aʊt / (AmE), / tɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to provide a structure with a top or to finish the topmost part of a building or object
finish or add the top to a structure

Examples

The builders will top out the new skyscraper next month with a decorative spire.
The church's steeple was topped out with an ornate cross.
Once they top out the dome, the construction will be nearly complete.

Synset Examples

the towers were topped with conical roofs

3. VERB

/ tɑp aʊt / (AmE), / tɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to reach the highest point or level; to attain the maximum possible value, intensity, or activity
reach the highest point or maximum level

Examples

After months of rapid growth, the market finally topped out in June.
Attendance at the festival topped out at over 100,000 people this year.
The skyscraper topped out at 80 stories, making it the tallest in the city.

Synset Examples

That wild, speculative spirit peaked in 1929
Bids for the painting topped out at $50 million

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peak - to reach a maximum or highest point, as in intensity or value
  • Synonym: to culminate - to reach the highest or a climactic point
