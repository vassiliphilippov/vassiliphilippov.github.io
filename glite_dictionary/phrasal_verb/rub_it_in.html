to rub it in

1. VERB

/ rʌb ɪt ɪn / (AmE), / rʌb ɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to emphasize or remind someone unnecessarily of their failure, mistake, or an unpleasant fact, often making them feel worse
remind someone harshly of a failure or error

Examples

I know I made a mistake, you don't have to rub it in every time.
Winning was enough, but he couldn't resist rubbing it in by reminding me of my loss.
Please stop rubbing it in; I'm already embarrassed about what happened.

Synset Examples

She always has to rub it in when she wins.
There's no need to rub it in; I already know I lost.
Please don't rub it in; I’m feeling bad enough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gloat - to dwell on one's own success or another's misfortune with smugness
