to lay siege

1. VERB

/ leɪ siːdʒ / (AmE), / leɪ siːdʒ / (BrE)
to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force its surrender by cutting off essential supplies
surround a place to capture it

Examples

The rebels tried to lay siege to the city, hoping to force a surrender.
During the war, the army laid siege to the castle for several months.
The general threatened to lay siege if the town refused to negotiate.

Synset Examples

They planned to lay siege until they surrendered.
The army decided to lay siege to the fortress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to besiege - to surround and attack a place in order to capture it
  • Antonym: retreat - to withdraw from enemy forces as a result of their superior power or after a defeat
