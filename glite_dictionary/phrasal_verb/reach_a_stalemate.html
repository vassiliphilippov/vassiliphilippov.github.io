to reach a stalemate

1. VERB

/ ritʃ ə ˈsteɪlmeɪt / (AmE), / riːtʃ ə ˈsteɪlmeɪt / (BrE)
to come to a situation in which neither side can make progress or win
reach a deadlock or impasse

Examples

After several attempts to resolve the issue, both parties finally reached a stalemate.
Talks between management and workers reached a stalemate when neither side would compromise.
The peace negotiations reached a stalemate, leaving the conflict unresolved.

Synset Examples

Negotiations reached a stalemate after hours of discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made
  • Synonym: impasse - a situation in which progress is impossible

Etymology

From 'stalemate', a chess term of French origin (estaler 'to bring to a stand', with 'mate' from Arabic 'māta', meaning 'died'), used figuratively since the 19th century.
