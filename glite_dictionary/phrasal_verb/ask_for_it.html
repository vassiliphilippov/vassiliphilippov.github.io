to ask for it

1. VERB

/ æsk fɔr ɪt / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə ɪt / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is likely to cause trouble or harm for oneself; to persist in actions that provoke negative consequences
provoke negative consequences through one's actions

Examples

If you drive that fast on icy roads, you're really asking for it.
He kept insulting the referee and eventually asked for it when he was sent off.
Ignoring the warning signs, she was asking for it by continuing to swim in dangerous waters.

Synset Examples

He is asking for trouble with his behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring it on oneself - cause trouble for oneself through one's actions
