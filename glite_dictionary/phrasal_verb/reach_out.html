to reach out

1. VERB

/ ritʃ aʊt / (AmE), / riːtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to attempt to communicate or make contact with someone, especially to offer help or support
attempt to contact or communicate

Examples

If you need any assistance, please reach out to our customer service team.
She decided to reach out to her old friend after many years apart.
The organization encourages people to reach out if they are struggling with stress.

Synset Examples

I try to reach out to my daughter but she doesn't want to have anything to do with me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact - to communicate with someone, especially by phone or letter
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately not pay attention or respond to someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00746558-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ritʃ aʊt / (AmE), / riːtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to extend or stretch outward in space or direction
extend or stretch outward

Examples

He tried to reach out and grab the branch above his head.
The cat reached out its paw to touch the toy.
A hand reached out from the darkness, startling everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

The awning extends several feet over the sidewalk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extend - to stretch out or make longer
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to pull back or move away
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02695935-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ritʃ aʊt / (AmE), / riːtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to extend or stretch forward or upward in order to touch or make contact; also, to attempt to communicate, offer help, or establish a connection, often in a metaphorical sense
extend to make contact or offer help

Examples

The organization encourages people to reach out if they need support.
She decided to reach out to her old friend after years of silence.
During the crisis, many volunteers reached out to help those affected.

Synset Examples

Government reaches out to the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact - the act of communicating or establishing connection
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
