to slick up

1. VERB

/ slɪk ʌp / (AmE), / slɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to dress and groom oneself with special care, especially for a special occasion
dress and groom carefully for an occasion

Examples

He always likes to slick up before important meetings at work.
You don't need to slick up just to go to the grocery store.
She slicked up nicely for her daughter's wedding.

Synset Examples

He spruced up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make oneself or something look neat and smart
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00043009-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ slɪk ʌp / (AmE), / slɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to make something or someone neat, smart, or more attractive in appearance
make neat or tidy; smarten up

Examples

Before the meeting, she decided to slick up her appearance with a new haircut.
He asked the kids to slick up their rooms before guests arrived.
The stylist will slick up your look for the photo shoot.

Synset Examples

Spruce up your house for Spring
titivate the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make neat, smart, or stylish in appearance
  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make neat or orderly
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make untidy or disorderly
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
