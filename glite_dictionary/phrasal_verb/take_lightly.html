to take lightly

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / teɪk ˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
to treat something as unimportant or not serious; to underestimate the significance of something
treat as unimportant; not take seriously

Examples

Despite the warnings, many residents continued to take lightly the risk of flooding.
She never takes lightly the trust her clients place in her.
You shouldn't take lightly the impact your words could have on others.

Synset Examples

Don't take this issue lightly; it's serious.
He never takes lightly the responsibility of leadership.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to take seriously - to regard something as important or significant
