to back out

1. VERB

/ bæk aʊt / (AmE), / bæk aʊt / (BrE)
to decide not to do something you had promised or agreed to do
withdraw from a commitment or agreement

Examples

She decided to back out of the trip at the last minute.
If you back out now, the whole plan will fall apart.
He promised to invest but later backed out without explanation.

Synset Examples

We'll have to crawfish out from meeting with him
He backed out of his earlier promise
The aggressive investment company pulled in its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - remove oneself from an activity or commitment
  • Synonym: to renege - go back on a promise or commitment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00801045-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bæk aʊt / (AmE), / bæk aʊt / (BrE)
to move out of a space by reversing, typically in a vehicle
reverse out of a space

Examples

She carefully backed out of the parking spot, checking for pedestrians.
The truck backed out of the narrow alley without hitting anything.
He forgot to look both ways before he backed out of the garage.

Synset Examples

He backed out of the driveway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to move backward, especially in a vehicle

Synset POS: v
