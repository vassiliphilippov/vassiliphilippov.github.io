to hang back

1. VERB

/ hæŋ bæk / (AmE), / hæŋ bæk / (BrE)
to stay behind or be slow to move forward, often because of hesitation or reluctance
to lag or linger behind

Examples

During the tour, some students tended to hang back and whisper quietly among themselves.
She wanted to speak up but decided to hang back out of shyness.
If you hang back at the start of the race, you might miss your chance to win.

Synset Examples

But in so many other areas we still are dragging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lag behind - to fall behind in movement or progress
  • Antonym: to move ahead - to advance forward more quickly
