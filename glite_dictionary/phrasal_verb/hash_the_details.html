to hash the details

1. VERB

/ hæʃ ðə dɪˈteɪlz / (AmE), / hæʃ ðə dɪˈteɪlz / (BrE)
to discuss or work out the specific aspects or finer points of something, especially by carefully considering different options or opinions
discuss or work out specific details

Examples

Let's hash the details before we sign the contract.
The two managers spent hours hashing the details of the agreement.
We need to hash the details out so that everyone is on the same page.

Synset Examples

Let’s hash the details of the project in our next meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to iron out - to resolve or clear up problems or differences, especially in detail
  • Synonym: to work out - to resolve or plan out details of something

Etymology

From 'hash', meaning to chop or mix, metaphorically extended to mean working out details by discussion.
