to blow off

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ɑf / (AmE), / bləʊ ɒf / (BrE)
(of an object) to become detached and carried away by an explosion or other powerful force.
become detached by force or explosion

Examples

The lid of the container was blown off in the accident.
Several roof tiles blew off during the hurricane last night.
Be careful or the pressure might blow off the valve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detach - to separate or remove from something else
  • Synonym: to blow up - to explode or cause to explode

WordNet ID: oewn-01293548-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bloʊ ɑf / (AmE), / bləʊ ɒf / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore, avoid, or fail to attend something, often out of lack of interest or a desire to do something else
deliberately ignore or skip (an event or duty)
informal

Examples

Tom decided to blow off the meeting and go to the movies instead.
She can't believe you would blow off such an important appointment.
Many students blow off class when the weather is nice outside.

Synset Examples

She blew off her responsibilities to enjoy the weekend.
Don't blow off your responsibilities just to have fun.
He decided to blow off the party and stay home.
He decided to blow off the meeting and go to lunch instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skip - to not attend or participate in something
  • Antonym: to attend - to be present at an event
