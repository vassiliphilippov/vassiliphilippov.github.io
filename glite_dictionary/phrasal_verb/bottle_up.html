to bottle up

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːt̬l ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɒtl ʌp / (BrE)
to deliberately keep emotions or feelings from being expressed or released
restrain emotions or feelings

Examples

He tends to bottle up his frustration instead of talking about it.
If you bottle up your feelings, they might eventually come out in unhealthy ways.
She realized it wasn't good to bottle up her sadness and started sharing her thoughts with friends.

Synset Examples

suppress a smile
he let his anger bottle up until he exploded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to prevent something, especially an emotion or information, from being expressed
  • Synonym: to repress - to not allow yourself to express feelings or desires
  • Antonym: express - to make feelings or thoughts known
