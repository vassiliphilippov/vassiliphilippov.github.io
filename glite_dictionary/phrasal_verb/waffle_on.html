to waffle on

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑːfəl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈwɒfəl ɒn / (BrE)
to be indecisive or speak in a vague, evasive way, especially when avoiding making a clear decision or statement
be indecisive or vague
informal

Examples

Whenever asked about his future plans, he tends to waffle on instead of giving a clear answer.
Please don't waffle on during the meeting; get straight to the point.
She started to waffle on when confronted about her responsibilities.

Synset Examples

Don't waffle on this issue; we need clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dither - to hesitate or be indecisive
  • Antonym: to decide - to reach a decision; to choose

Etymology

Probably from earlier noun 'waffle' (meaning: idle talk), origin uncertain, possibly imitative.
