to get wet

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wɛt / (AmE), / ɡet wɛt / (BrE)
to become covered or soaked with water or another liquid
become soaked with water

Examples

Be careful when you cross the stream, or you might get wet.
Despite the raincoat, she still managed to get wet on her walk home.
Kids love to get wet playing in the sprinklers during summer.

Synset Examples

They warned us not to get wet during the storm.
You might get wet if you forget your umbrella.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stay dry - avoid becoming wet
