to wave aside

1. VERB

/ weɪv əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / weɪv əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to dismiss or reject something as unimportant, often with a gesture
dismiss or reject something

Examples

He tried to wave aside their suggestions, insisting his plan was best.
She waved aside the criticism and continued with her work.
Despite the warnings, the manager waved aside all concerns from the team.

Synset Examples

It's time to wave aside distractions and focus.
He chose to wave aside their criticisms and move forward.
She attempted to wave away the negative thoughts.
She tried to wave away his doubts with a smile.
She waved aside his concerns with a dismissive gesture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide not to consider something
  • Synonym: to brush off - to reject or ignore someone or something
