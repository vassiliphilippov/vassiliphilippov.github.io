to take on faith

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɑn feɪθ / (AmE), / teɪk ɒn feɪθ / (BrE)
to accept or believe something without requiring proof or evidence
accept without proof or evidence

Examples

I had to take on faith their explanation for the company's sudden success.
Sometimes you just have to take on faith that things will improve.
She wasn't sure about the details, but she decided to take on faith what her mentor told her.

Synset Examples

They want us to take on faith their promises.
You have to take on faith that it will all work out.
I can't take on faith that he will change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to believe - to accept as true
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
