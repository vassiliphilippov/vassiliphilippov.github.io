to put to work

1. VERB

/ pʊt tə wɜrk / (AmE), / pʊt tə wɜːk / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to begin working; to assign work or tasks to someone or something.
cause to start working

Examples

After moving to the farm, they immediately put to work repairing the old barn.
The manager put to work every available employee to meet the deadline.
He found a stray dog and put it to work guarding the property.

Synset Examples

he is working his servants hard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employ - give someone a job to do; make use of
  • Antonym: idle - not working or not active

2. VERB

/ pʊt tə wɜrk / (AmE), / pʊt tə wɜːk / (BrE)
to cause someone to begin working or to assign tasks or duties to someone
assign someone to start working

Examples

After the meeting, the interns were put to work filing documents and answering phones.
New equipment was put to work to speed up production at the factory.
She hopes to be put to work on meaningful research during her summer internship.

Synset Examples

He was put to work on the new project right away.
The volunteers were put to work immediately after arriving.
She was eager to be put to work in the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a particular job or duty
  • Synonym: employ - to give work to someone and pay them for it
