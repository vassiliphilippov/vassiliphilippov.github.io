to go flat

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ flæt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ flæt / (BrE)
(of a tire, ball, or similar object) to lose air and become deflated.
lose air and become deflated

Examples

The bicycle tire went flat just as I was about to leave for work.
After a week without use, the soccer ball had gone flat and needed pumping up.
We had to stop on the highway because one of our tires went flat.

Synset Examples

The tire went flat on our road trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deflate - let air or gas out of; become flat
  • Antonym: to inflate - fill with air or gas; make larger
