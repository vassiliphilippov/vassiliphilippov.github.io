to focus on

1. VERB

/ ˈfoʊkəs ɑn / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəs ɒn / (BrE)
to give most of your attention or effort to one particular person, subject, or thing
concentrate attention or effort on

Examples

The speaker asked the audience to focus on the key issues of the presentation.
She needs to focus on finishing her project before the deadline.
The government should focus on improving public transportation systems.

Synset Examples

Her entire attention centered on her children
Our day revolved around our work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to concentrate on - to direct your attention or effort completely to something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone

2. VERB

/ ˈfoʊkəs ɑn / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəs ɒn / (BrE)
to direct your attention or efforts towards a particular subject, activity, or problem
direct attention or effort toward something

Examples

She tried to focus on her work despite the noise around her.
During meetings, it's important to focus on the key issues.
I need to focus on my studies to pass the upcoming exams.

Synset Examples

During yoga, focus on your resp to relax.
