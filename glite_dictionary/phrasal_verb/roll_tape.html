to roll tape

1. VERB

/ roʊl teɪp / (AmE), / rəʊl teɪp / (BrE)
to begin recording audio or video, especially on a tape recorder or video camera
to begin recording (audio or video)

Examples

The director shouted, "Roll tape!" and the actors got into their positions.
Before the interview could start, the technician had to roll tape to capture everything.
They rolled tape as soon as the band started their set.

Synset Examples

We need to roll tape before the sun sets.
Let's roll tape for the final scene.
He instructed the crew to roll tape on the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to make an audio or video recording of something

Etymology

Originally from the practice of starting the reels of magnetic tape recorders.
