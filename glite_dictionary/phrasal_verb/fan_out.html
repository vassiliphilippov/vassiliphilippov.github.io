to fan out

1. VERB

/ fæn aʊt / (AmE), / fan aʊt / (BrE)
to spread outwards from a central point in different directions
spread outward from a central point

Examples

The search party began to fan out across the forest.
As the parade approached, the crowd started to fan out along the street.
Birds fanned out overhead, forming a wide V in the sky.

Synset Examples

The soldiers fanned out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spread out - to extend or distribute over a larger area

Etymology

From the noun 'fan' (a device with spreading blades) + out, indicating outward movement.
