to swim upstream

1. VERB

/ swɪm ʌpˈstriːm / (AmE), / swɪm ʌpˈstriːm / (BrE)
to move against the current of water; figuratively, to persist in the face of opposition or difficulty
to move against the current or face difficulties

Examples

Many small businesses have to swim upstream to survive in a tough economy.
He felt he was swimming upstream against a tide of criticism.
Salmon swim upstream every year to reach their spawning grounds.

Synset Examples

It's tough to swim upstream in a competitive market.
She had to swim upstream to achieve her goals.
The salmon swim upstream to spawn every year.
They often feel like they’re swimming upstream in their careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go against the grain - to do something in opposition to what is generally accepted
  • Synonym: struggle - to make forceful or violent efforts; to strive to achieve or attain something in the face of difficulty
  • Antonym: go with the flow - to accept, conform to, or go along with prevailing attitudes or practices
