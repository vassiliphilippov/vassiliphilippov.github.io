to cave to

1. VERB

/ keɪv tu / (AmE), / keɪv tʊ / (BrE)
to surrender or give in to pressure or demands
yield under pressure or demands

Examples

Despite the criticism, she refused to cave to public pressure and maintained her decision.
The company finally caved to employee demands after weeks of protests.
Politicians often cave to popular opinion during election campaigns.

Synset Examples

She refused to cave to their demands during negotiations.
He had to cave under pressure and accept the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield to - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
  • Synonym: to succumb to - to fail to resist pressure, temptation, or some other negative force
