to close off

1. VERB

/ kloʊz ɔf / (AmE), / kləʊz ɒf / (BrE)
to block the entrance or passage through something, making it inaccessible or preventing movement
block passage; make inaccessible

Examples

Police had to close off the street after the accident.
They closed off the main entrance for repairs, so we used the side door.
Authorities are closing off several hiking trails due to dangerous conditions.

Synset Examples

We shut off the valve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to block off - to obstruct or close a passage, street, or area
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01480024-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kloʊz ɔf / (AmE), / kləʊz ɒf / (BrE)
to block or isolate an area, person, or thing from access, connection, or interaction; to separate entirely.
block or isolate; separate completely

Examples

Police decided to close off the street after the accident.
The manager will close off that section during renovations.
Sometimes people close off their feelings to protect themselves.

Synset Examples

She was shut off from the friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut off - to stop access or isolate; to block
  • Antonym: to open up - to make accessible or available; to remove barriers
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02035671-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kloʊz ɔf / (AmE), / kləʊz ɒf / (BrE)
to block or prevent the movement or flow of something, especially by shutting a passage or valve
block movement or flow

Examples

The road was closed off by police after the accident.
Make sure to close off the water supply before fixing the sink.
They had to close off part of the building due to safety concerns.

Synset Examples

shut off the gas when you leave for a vacation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut off - to stop the flow or operation of something, especially by turning a control
  • Antonym: to open up - to allow movement or flow where it was previously blocked
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02686341-v
Synset POS: v
