to wear thin

1. VERB

/ wɛr θɪn / (AmE), / wɛə θɪn / (BrE)
Gradually become weakened, less effective, or deteriorated due to continuous use, friction, or stress.
deteriorate through use or stress

Examples

After years of walking on the old carpet, it has started to wear thin in several places.
My patience is beginning to wear thin after hearing the same excuse repeatedly.
The paint on the benches will wear thin if not maintained properly.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear out - to use something until it is no longer usable

2. VERB

/ wɛr ˈθɪn / (AmE), / wɛə ˈθɪn / (BrE)
to become less strong, effective, acceptable, or believable; to lose its impact or patience over time
become less effective or believable

Examples

After hearing the same apology every day, his patience began to wear thin.
Their promises of improvement soon started to wear thin with customers.
The novelty of working from home can quickly wear thin if there are too many distractions.

Synset Examples

Her enthusiasm began to wear thin after several setbacks.
The excuses are starting to wear thin with the teacher.
His patience began to wear thin after the repeated delays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run out - to become used up; to be finished or depleted
  • Synonym: to diminish - to become or cause to become less in importance or value
