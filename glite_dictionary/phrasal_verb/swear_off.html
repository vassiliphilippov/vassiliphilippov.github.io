to swear off

1. VERB

/ swɛr ɔf / (AmE), / swɛə ɒf / (BrE)
to make a serious promise to stop doing or using something, especially a habit or substance
promise to stop doing or using something

Examples

After his health scare, he decided to swear off sugary drinks for good.
Many people try to swear off junk food at the start of the year.
She has sworn off social media to focus on her studies.

Synset Examples

I have sworn off cigarettes altogether

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop doing something, often a habit
  • Synonym: to abstain - to deliberately avoid or refrain from something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00886335-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ swɛr ɑf / (AmE), / swɛə ɒf / (BrE)
to make a serious decision to stop doing or using something, especially a habit or activity, often permanently
decide to stop or renounce a habit

Examples

After years of struggling with nicotine, he finally swore off smoking for good.
Many people swear off sweets after the holidays, hoping to eat healthier.
She swears off social media every summer to focus on outdoor activities.

Synset Examples

He swore off drinking after one too many visits to the porcelain god.
She swore off dating after a bad breakup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop doing something habitually
  • Antonym: resume - to start again after stopping

Etymology

From the verb "swear" (to make a solemn statement or promise) plus the preposition "off"; used from the late 1800s.

3. VERB

/ swɛr ɔf / (AmE), / swɛə ɒf / (BrE)
to make a serious decision or promise to stop doing or using something, especially a harmful habit
promise to stop a habit or behavior

Examples

After his doctor’s warning, he decided to swear off junk food for good.
She swore off junk food and started cooking healthier meals at home.
Many people try to swear off junk food at the start of each new year.

Synset Examples

He swore off junk food after his health scare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop doing or having something, often as a resolution or due to difficulty
  • Synonym: to quit - to stop doing something, especially a habit

Etymology

From Old English 'swerian' (to take an oath) + 'off'.
