to scrape up

1. VERB

/ skreɪp ʌp / (AmE), / skreɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to manage to collect or gather something, especially money or resources, with difficulty or over a period of time
manage to collect, often with difficulty

Examples

It took her months to scrape up enough money for the deposit.
We might be able to scrape up a few more volunteers if we ask around.
Despite their financial troubles, they managed to scrape up the funds for repairs.

Synset Examples

She had scraped together enough money for college
they scratched a meager living

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape together - to collect or gather gradually, especially with effort
