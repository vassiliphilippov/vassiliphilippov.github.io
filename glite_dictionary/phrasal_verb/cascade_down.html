to cascade down

1. VERB

/ ˌkæˌskeɪd ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌkasˌkeɪd ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
to flow, fall, or pour downward rapidly and in large quantities, like a waterfall
to rush down in large quantities

Examples

Water began to cascade down the mountainside after the heavy rains.
As the curtain was lifted, glitter seemed to cascade down from the ceiling onto the stage.
Her long hair would cascade down her back in shining waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour down - to rain or flow heavily downward
  • Degree Lower: trickle down - to flow or fall in small quantities

Etymology

From 'cascade' (French 'cascade', from Italian 'cascata' meaning 'waterfall') + 'down' (Old English 'dūn').
