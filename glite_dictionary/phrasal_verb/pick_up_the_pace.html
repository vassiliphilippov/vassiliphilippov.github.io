to pick up the pace

1. VERB

/ pɪk ʌp ðə peɪs / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp ðə peɪs / (BrE)
to go faster or increase the speed at which something is done
increase speed or progress

Examples

We need to pick up the pace if we want to catch the train.
Halfway through the race, she decided to pick up the pace.
The team must pick up the pace to finish before the deadline.

Synset Examples

To finish on time, we must pick up the pace.
We need to pick up the pace to meet the deadline.
They decided to pick up the pace during their training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speed up - to move or work faster
  • Antonym: to slow down - to move or work more slowly
