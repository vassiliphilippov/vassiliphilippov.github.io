to ooze from

1. VERB

/ uːz frəm / (AmE), / uːz frɒm / (BrE)
to leak or flow slowly out from a place or object
leak or flow out slowly

Examples

A thick sap began to ooze from the cut in the tree trunk.
Blood could ooze from a shallow wound if not cleaned properly.
After the rain, water would ooze from the cracks in the pavement.

Synset Examples

The honey began to ooze from the jar onto the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seep from - to flow or leak slowly from an object or place
  • Degree Higher: stream from - to flow continuously and steadily from a source
