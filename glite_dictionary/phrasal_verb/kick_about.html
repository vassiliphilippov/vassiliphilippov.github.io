to kick about

1. VERB

/ kɪk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / kɪk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
(informal) To remain alive or active, especially when expected not to.
remain alive or active
informal

Examples

Even though he's in his nineties, Grandpa still kicks about and enjoys his daily walks.
There are quite a few old traditions that still kick about in this town.
Some of those vintage cars are still kicking about in collectors' garages.

Synset Examples

Does the old man still kick around?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick around - be alive or active; remain present or extant
