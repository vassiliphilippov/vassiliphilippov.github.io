to roll in

1. VERB

/ roʊl ɪn / (AmE), / rəʊl ɪn / (BrE)
to arrive, appear, or flow in large numbers or quantities, especially steadily or continuously
arrive or flow steadily

Examples

The fog began to roll in from the lake as evening approached.
Donations continued to roll in after the charity event was televised.
As midnight neared, more guests started to roll in to the party.

Synset Examples

mist rolled in from the sea
tourists rolled in from the neighboring countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pour in - to arrive or come in large quantities
  • Degree Lower: trickle in - to arrive or appear in small numbers or quantities
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00585681-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ roʊl ɪn / (AmE), / rəʊl ɪn / (BrE)
to arrive or move in gradually and often in a noticeable or dramatic way, especially used for weather, fog, or groups of people
to arrive or move in gradually

Examples

As the sun set, thick fog began to roll in from the sea.
People watched from the pier as the clouds rolled in over the city skyline.
The cold front rolled in unexpectedly, catching everyone off guard.

Synset Examples

The fog rolled in, making the morning eerie and quiet.
We rushed inside as the thundercloud rolled in.
The fog rolled in like vapor from the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to roll out - to leave or depart gradually, especially in a dramatic way
