to kit out

1. VERB

/ kɪt aʊt / (AmE), / kɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to supply someone or something with the necessary equipment, tools, or clothing for a particular purpose
equip with necessary items
british

Examples

The team was kitted out with new uniforms for the tournament.
We need to kit out the kitchen before we can start cooking.
Before the climb, they kitted out each member with proper safety gear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equip - to provide with necessary items for a purpose
  • Antonym: strip - to remove equipment or gear from someone or something

Etymology

From British English 'kit' (set of equipment) + 'out' (completing the verb phrase)
