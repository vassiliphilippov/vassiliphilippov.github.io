to hot up

1. VERB

/ hɑt ʌp / (AmE), / hɒt ʌp / (BrE)
(of a place, object, or situation) to become hot or hotter; to gain heat.
become hot or hotter

Examples

As summer approached, the weather began to hot up considerably.
The debate will hot up once the results are announced.
Things really hotted up in the kitchen when the oven was turned on.

Synset Examples

The room heated up quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to heat up - to make or become hot or hotter
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00373318-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hɑt ʌp / (AmE), / hɒt ʌp / (BrE)
(chiefly British) To become or make more intense, active, or exciting.
to become or make more intense
british

Examples

The debate began to hot up as the election drew closer.
Things really hot up in the kitchen during the dinner rush.
Negotiations have hotted up in the last few days with both sides eager to reach a deal.

Synset Examples

Emotions were screwed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intensify - to make or become more intense
  • Antonym: to cool off - to become less intense or heated
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00228093-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hɑt ʌp / (AmE), / hɒt ʌp / (BrE)
to make a machine, especially an engine, more powerful or efficient, often by changing its parts
make a machine more powerful

Examples

He decided to hot up his car so it would go faster on the track.
Some mechanics specialize in hotting up old motorcycles for racing purposes.
The engine had been hotted up to deliver extra horsepower.

Synset Examples

he souped up the old cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soup up - to modify a vehicle or machine to increase its power or performance
