to wring from

1. VERB

/ rɪŋ frəm / (AmE), / rɪŋ frɒm / (BrE)
to obtain something from someone with great difficulty or effort, often through pressure or persuasion
obtain with difficulty or effort

Examples

The committee managed to wring from the company a promise to improve working conditions.
It took hours to wring from him the truth about what had happened.
She finally wrung from her parents permission to go on the trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract from - to obtain something by effort or force
