to persist in

1. VERB

/ pərˈsɪst ɪn / (AmE), / pəˈsɪst ɪn / (BrE)
to continue to do something, especially in a determined way and despite opposition or difficulty
continue determinedly despite difficulty

Examples

Despite criticism from colleagues, she persisted in advocating for environmental reforms.
He persisted in calling her every day, even after she asked for space.
They persisted in their efforts to complete the project on time despite many setbacks.

Synset Examples

We continued our research into the cause of the illness
The landlord persists in asking us to move

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to continue trying to do something despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something
