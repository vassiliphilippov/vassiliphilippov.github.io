to attach to

1. VERB

/ əˈtætʃ tu / (AmE), / əˈtatʃ tuː / (BrE)
to be present with or associated with a particular event, thing, or person
be present or associated with

Examples

A sense of nostalgia often attaches to places you visited as a child.
Great responsibility attaches to this position within the company.
A certain stigma attached to divorce in past generations.

Synset Examples

French fries come with the hamburger
heart attacks are accompanied by destruction of heart tissue
fish usually goes with white wine
this kind of vein accompanies certain arteries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to go somewhere with someone or to exist together with something
  • Synonym: to come with - to be included with or alongside something

Etymology

From French *attacher* (to attach), ultimately from Latin *attachiāre*; 'to' added as English particle to form the phrasal verb.
