to blazon out

1. VERB

/ ˈbleɪzən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbleɪzən aʊt / (BrE)
to proclaim or announce something publicly and conspicuously
proclaim publicly

Examples

The company decided to blazon out its achievements in a full-page newspaper ad.
Rumors about the celebrity's private life were blazoned out in the tabloids.
He likes to blazon out every detail of his success to anyone who will listen.

Synset Examples

before we had newspapers, a town crier would cry the news
He cried his merchandise in the market square

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proclaim - to announce officially or publicly
  • Synonym: broadcast - to make widely known by public announcement
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Old French blasoner (to display, proclaim), related to 'blazon' (coat of arms), meaning to make something visible or showy.
