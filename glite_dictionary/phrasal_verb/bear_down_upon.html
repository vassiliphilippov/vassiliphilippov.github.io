to bear down upon

1. VERB

/ bɛr daʊn əˈpɑn / (AmE), / beə daʊn əˈpɒn / (BrE)
(of a ship) to sail or move towards another vessel, usually with intent to approach closely.
sail towards another vessel (nautical)

Examples

The frigate began to bear down upon the merchant vessel, raising alarm among the crew.
As night fell, we saw a ship bearing down upon us through the fog.
The captain shouted orders as they bore down upon the enemy's flagship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to come near or nearer to something or someone
