to wreathe the door

1. VERB

/ rið ðə dɔr / (AmE), / riːð ðə dɔː / (BrE)
to decorate a door by placing a wreath or garland on it, especially for a celebration or holiday
decorate a door with a wreath

Examples

Each December, we wreathe the door with pine branches to welcome the holiday season.
She decided to wreathe the door in springtime flowers for the party.
Neighbors often wreathe the door as part of community celebrations.

Synset Examples

She plans to wreathe the door with garlands for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items or details

Etymology

From 'wreathe' (to encircle or adorn with a wreath), ultimately from Old English 'wrīthan' (to twist).
