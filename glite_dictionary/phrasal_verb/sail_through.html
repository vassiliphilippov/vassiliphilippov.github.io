to sail through

1. VERB

/ seɪl θru / (AmE), / seɪl θruː / (BrE)
to succeed in dealing with something, especially an exam or task, very easily
succeed easily at something

Examples

She managed to sail through the interview and got the job on her first try.
If you study hard, you'll sail through the final exam without any trouble.
Despite the challenges, our team sailed through the project and finished ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

She sailed through her exams
You will pass with flying colors
She nailed her astrophysics course

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to breeze through - to do something quickly and with little effort
  • Antonym: struggle through - to succeed with great difficulty
