to drag out

1. VERB

/ dræɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dræɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to make something continue for longer than is necessary or desirable
prolong unnecessarily

Examples

The meeting was dragged out for hours by unnecessary discussions.
She tends to drag out her stories, adding irrelevant details.
Negotiations were dragged out by both sides, delaying a final agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prolong - to make something last longer
  • Antonym: to shorten - to make something shorter or quicker
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02711288-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dræɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dræɡ aʊt / (BrE)
(of an event or process) to continue for a longer time than necessary or expected, often causing boredom or frustration.
continue for longer than necessary

Examples

The meeting started to drag out by the end, and people grew restless.
Negotiations can drag out for months without a clear resolution.
Let’s not drag out this discussion any longer than necessary.

Synset Examples

The speech dragged on for two hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prolong - to make something last longer
  • Antonym: to shorten - to make something shorter in time or length
