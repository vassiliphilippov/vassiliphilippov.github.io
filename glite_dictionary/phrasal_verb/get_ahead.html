to get ahead

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈhɛd / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to achieve an advantage or move into a better position than others, especially in a competition or contest
gain an advantage; move ahead competitively

Examples

If you want to get ahead in this company, you'll need to work very hard.
The team managed to get ahead in the final quarter and held on to win.
She studied every night to get ahead of her classmates.

Synset Examples

The home team was gaining ground
After defeating the Knicks, the Blazers pulled ahead of the Lakers in the battle for the number-one playoff berth in the Western Conference
win points

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull ahead - to move into a leading position, especially after being even or behind
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to lose a competitive position or be surpassed by others
