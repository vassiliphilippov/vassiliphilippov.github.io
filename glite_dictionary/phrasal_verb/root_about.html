to root about

1. VERB

/ rut əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ruːt əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to search through or rummage in a place or container, especially in a thorough or disorderly way
search or rummage thoroughly

Examples

He had to root about in his desk to find the missing receipt.
She spent the afternoon rooting about in the garage for her old bike.
Before breakfast, the dog roots about in the garden for buried toys.

Synset Examples

I had to root about in my bag for the keys.
She decided to root about in the attic for old photos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rummage - to search through something thoroughly, often in a disorderly way
