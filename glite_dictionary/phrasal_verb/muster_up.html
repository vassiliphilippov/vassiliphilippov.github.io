to muster up

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌstər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmʌstə ʌp / (BrE)
to gather or summon (energy, courage, support, etc.), especially with effort
gather or summon with effort

Examples

She had to muster up the courage to speak in front of the large audience.
He finally mustered up enough confidence to ask for a promotion.
It wasn't easy for him to muster up a smile after hearing the bad news.

Synset Examples

muster the courage to do something
she rallied her intellect
Summon all your courage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summon up - to call forth or bring to mind
  • Synonym: gather - to collect or bring together

Etymology

From Middle English 'muster' (to assemble), from Old French 'mostrer', from Latin 'monstrare' (to show).
