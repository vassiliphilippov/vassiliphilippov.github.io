to snag one's attention

1. VERB

/ snæɡ wʌnz əˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / snæɡ wʌnz əˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
to capture or attract someone's interest or notice
capture someone's interest

Examples

The bright colors of the advertisement snagged my attention as I walked by the store window.
Her unusual hairstyle immediately snagged my attention at the party.
A loud noise from the street snagged our attention during the meeting.

Synset Examples

He snagged my attention with a funny story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grab someone's attention - to attract or capture someone's interest
  • Antonym: lose someone's attention - to fail to keep someone interested

Etymology

From 'snag' meaning to catch or grab suddenly, combined with 'attention'.
