to part with

1. VERB

/ pɑrt wɪθ / (AmE), / pɑːt wɪð / (BrE)
to give up or relinquish something, especially something valuable or not strictly necessary
give up or let go of something

Examples

She was reluctant to part with her childhood toys, even though she no longer used them.
I can't believe you actually parted with your rare coin collection.
Would you be willing to part with your ticket for the concert?

Synset Examples

he asked if they could spare one of their horses to speed his journey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop having or keeping something; to surrender
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something
