to push around

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / pʊʃ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to treat someone in a domineering, bossy, or bullying way, especially by giving orders or using threats
treat someone in a bossy or bullying way

Examples

He hates it when his colleagues push around the new interns.
Nobody likes to be pushed around by their supervisor at work.
If you let her, she'll just push around everyone in the group.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to hurt or intimidate someone weaker repeatedly

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / pʊʃ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to treat someone in a domineering, aggressive, or unfair way; to bully or boss someone unnecessarily
bully or boss someone unfairly

Examples

You shouldn't let anyone push around you just because you're new at work.
He was tired of being pushed around by his older siblings.
Whenever there's a disagreement, she tries to push around everyone else in the group.

Synset Examples

Don’t let him push you around; stand up for yourself.
He’s always trying to push me around in meetings.
She felt tired of being pushed around by her boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone
  • Synonym: to dominate - to have control or power over someone
  • Antonym: stand up to - to resist someone who is bullying or dominating you
