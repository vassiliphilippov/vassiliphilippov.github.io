to hunker down

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋkɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋkə daʊn / (BrE)
to refuse to change one's position or opinion, especially by becoming more determined or resistant
refuse to change position; resist

Examples

Despite mounting pressure from her colleagues, she hunkered down and refused to compromise on her beliefs.
During the negotiations, the management hunkered down and would not accept any of the workers' demands.
He knew he was right, so he hunkered down and stuck by his decision.

Synset Examples

The wife hunkered down and the husband's resistance began to break down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dig in - to firmly resist or become entrenched in a position
  • Antonym: to give in - to yield or surrender one's position
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋ.kɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋ.kə daʊn / (BrE)
to take shelter or settle in a safe or secure position, especially during danger or difficult conditions
take shelter or assume a safe position

Examples

When the tornado warnings sounded, the whole family hunkered down in the basement.
During the blizzard, we decided to hunker down and wait for the roads to clear.
The soldiers hunkered down in their trenches as the enemy approached.

Synset Examples

During the sandstorm, they hunkered down in a small hut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take cover - to seek protection from danger, especially by physically hiding
  • Antonym: expose oneself - to place oneself in a position without protection

Etymology

Probably from Scots "hunker" meaning to squat or crouch.

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋkɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋkə daʊn / (BrE)
to lower your body close to the ground by bending your knees and sitting on your heels, often for shelter or protection
crouch low on heels

Examples

The children hunkered down behind a fallen tree to wait out the storm.
She hunkered down by the campfire, warming her hands in the cold night air.
During the drill, everyone was told to hunker down and stay still until the danger passed.

Synset Examples

In some cultures, the women give birth while squatting
The children hunkered down to protect themselves from the sandstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crouch - to lower the body close to the ground by bending the knees
  • Antonym: to stand up - to rise to a standing position

Etymology

Origin of "hunker" uncertain; possibly from Scots. "Down" added to form the phrasal verb.

4. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋkər daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋkə daʊn / (BrE)
to settle in and prepare for safety, survival, or a period of hard work, especially in the face of difficulty or danger
settle in for safety or difficult period

Examples

As the hurricane approached, the residents decided to hunker down and wait it out.
With deadlines looming, the team knew they had to hunker down and focus on the work.
During the power outage, we hunkered down with candles and blankets until morning.

Synset Examples

They had to hunker down in the cabin during the blizzard.
We decided to hunker down and wait for the storm to pass.
It's time to hunker down and finish this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunker down - (chiefly American) to take shelter or refuge, usually in anticipation of difficulty
