to send off

1. VERB

/ sɛnd ɔf / (AmE), / sɛnd ɒf / (BrE)
to dispatch something or someone to a particular destination or on a particular purpose.
to dispatch or send away

Examples

I need to send off this package before the post office closes.
She sent off her application forms last week.
The company regularly sends off samples to their overseas partners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispatch - to send someone or something to a place for a particular purpose
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01958986-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛnd ɔf / (AmE), / sɛnd ɒf / (BrE)
to dispatch something, such as a letter or package, to a particular destination; to transfer or transmit, often by post or electronically
dispatch or transmit to a destination

Examples

I need to send off my application before the deadline arrives.
After finishing the letter, she quickly sent off the documents to her lawyer.
The manager asked him to send off the report to the head office.

Synset Examples

The spy sent the classified information off to Russia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispatch - to send someone or something to a particular place for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: forward - to send something onward to another person or place
