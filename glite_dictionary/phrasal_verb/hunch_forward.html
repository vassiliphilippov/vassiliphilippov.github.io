to hunch forward

1. VERB

/ hʌntʃ ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / hʌntʃ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to bend your body forward and round your shoulders, often to hide or protect yourself or due to tiredness
bend forward, rounding the back and shoulders

Examples

She hunched forward in her chair, trying to warm herself near the fire.
The old man hunched forward as he shuffled down the street.
Whenever he feels anxious, he tends to hunch forward and avoid eye contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slouch - sit or stand with an awkward, drooping posture
