to stand up for oneself

1. VERB

/ stænd ʌp fɔr wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / stænd ʌp fə wʌnˈself / (BrE)
to defend or assert one's rights, opinions, or needs, especially in the face of opposition or challenge
defend or assert oneself

Examples

You need to stand up for oneself when you believe you are being treated unfairly.
It's important to teach children how to stand up for oneself in difficult situations.
After years of staying quiet, she decided to stand up for oneself at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert oneself - to express oneself confidently
  • Antonym: to give in - to yield or surrender, especially under pressure
