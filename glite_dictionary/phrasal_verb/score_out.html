to score out

1. VERB

/ skɔr aʊt / (AmE), / skɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to remove by drawing a line through written words; to cross out or erase something written, especially by rubbing or marking it.
to cross out or erase written words

Examples

He accidentally scored out the wrong answer on his test.
If you make a mistake on the form, just score out the incorrect word and write the correct one above it.
She scored out her ex-partner’s name from all her old diaries.

Synset Examples

Please erase the formula on the blackboard — it is wrong!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cross out - to draw a line through something written to show it should be ignored
  • Synonym: to erase - to remove written or drawn marks

Etymology

From the verb 'score' meaning 'to mark with lines', combined with 'out' indicating removal.
