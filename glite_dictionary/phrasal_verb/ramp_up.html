to ramp up

1. VERB

/ ræmp ʌp / (AmE), / ræmp ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or strengthen something, especially in intensity, amount, or effort
increase or strengthen

Examples

The government plans to ramp up security measures at all major airports.
After a slow start, the company began to ramp up production of the new model.
They will ramp up their marketing efforts ahead of the product launch.

Synset Examples

We worked up courage
build up confidence
ramp up security in the airports

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - make or become greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: to reduce - make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

2. VERB

/ ræmp ʌp / (AmE), / ræmp ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or enhance the amount, speed, or intensity of something, especially quickly or significantly
increase or intensify quickly

Examples

The company decided to ramp up production to meet rising demand.
We will ramp up our efforts ahead of the final deadline.
After hiring more staff, the team began ramping up their output.

Synset Examples

They need to ramp the marketing efforts before the launch.

Related Terms
