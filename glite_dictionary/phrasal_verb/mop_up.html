to mop up

1. VERB

/ mɑp ʌp / (AmE), / mɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to clean or wipe a surface using a mop or something similar, especially to remove liquid or dirt
clean or wipe with a mop

Examples

After the juice spill, he had to mop up the kitchen floor immediately.
She quickly mopped up the water with an old towel before it spread further.
The janitor mops up the hallways every evening after school.

Synset Examples

Mop the hallway now
He mopped her forehead with a towel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe up - to remove liquid from a surface by wiping it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01395993-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɑp ʌp / (AmE), / mɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to finish something completely, especially by dealing with the last parts or details
finish something completely

Examples

It took me all afternoon to mop up the paperwork left after the audit.
The team stayed late to mop up the remaining tasks before the deadline.
After the main project was done, she helped mop up the final details.

Synset Examples

I finally got through this homework assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finish off - to complete the last part of something
  • Synonym: to wrap up - to finish something, especially an activity or task

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'mop up' meaning to clean a surface by mopping, extended figuratively to mean 'complete' or 'clear up' remaining parts of a task.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00485823-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɑp ʌp / (AmE), / mɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to defeat an opponent completely, often in a decisive or overwhelming manner
defeat thoroughly

Examples

The home team mopped up their rivals with a score of 5-0.
She mopped up the competition at the chess tournament, winning every match easily.
Despite their efforts, the defending champions were mopped up in the final round.

Synset Examples

He mopped up the floor with his opponents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rout - to defeat someone completely
  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat easily and thoroughly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01105408-v
Synset POS: v
