to square up

1. VERB

/ skwɛr ʌp / (AmE), / skwɛə ʌp / (BrE)
to make the edges of a stack of paper or printed sheets even and aligned
make paper edges even

Examples

The printer stopped to square up the sheets before continuing the job.
Before binding, it's important to square up the pile of paper.
He carefully squared up the stack to ensure all the edges were perfectly aligned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to arrange in a straight line or correct relative positions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01991662-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skwɛr ʌp / (AmE), / skweə ʌp / (BrE)
to settle a debt or dispute; to come to terms or reach agreement
settle a debt or dispute

Examples

Before leaving the restaurant, let's square up the bill.
The two companies finally squared up after months of negotiations.
He promised to square up with me next week after getting paid.

Synset Examples

We finally settled the argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle - to resolve or come to an agreement about something

Etymology

From the verb 'square' meaning 'to make even or settle accounts,' combined with 'up'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00765444-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ skwɛr ʌp / (AmE), / skweə ʌp / (BrE)
to make something have square corners or edges; to shape or adjust so that it forms a square or right angle
make square in shape or angle

Examples

The carpenter used a file to square up the edges of the board.
You need to square up the frame so the corners fit properly.
She carefully measured before trying to square up the tiles on the wall.

Synset Examples

Square the circle
square the wood with a file

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rectify - to make straight or correct, especially removing irregularities
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
