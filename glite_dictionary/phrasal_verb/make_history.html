to make history

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / meɪk ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
to do something that is significant or likely to be remembered for a long time
achieve something significant or memorable

Examples

She hopes her invention will make history in the world of renewable energy.
The team made history by winning three championships in a row.
With this agreement, the leaders are determined to make history.

Synset Examples

They hope to make history with their groundbreaking technology.
The scientist's discovery will make history in medicine.
This match could make history for the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break new ground - to do something innovative or pioneering
  • Synonym: set a precedent - to establish an example to be followed in the future
