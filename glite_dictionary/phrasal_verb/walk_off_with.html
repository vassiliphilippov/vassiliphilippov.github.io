to walk off with

1. VERB

/ wɔk ɔf wɪð / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒf wɪð / (BrE)
to steal or take something quickly or easily, often without being noticed
steal or take something easily

Examples

Someone tried to walk off with my umbrella from the café.
The thief walked off with a valuable painting before anyone noticed.
She almost walked off with my phone by mistake.

Synset Examples

They thought he would walk off with the money.
She managed to walk off with the trophy.
He could easily walk off with the first prize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you without permission
  • Synonym: walk away with - to take something or win a prize easily or unexpectedly
