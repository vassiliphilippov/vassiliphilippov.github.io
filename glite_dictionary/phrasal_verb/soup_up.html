to soup up

1. VERB

/ suːp ʌp / (AmE), / suːp ʌp / (BrE)
to modify (a machine or vehicle), especially to increase its power or performance
make more powerful; enhance performance (informal)
informal american

Examples

He decided to soup up his motorcycle before the big race.
Many car enthusiasts love to soup up old engines in their garages.
The company souped up its laptop model by adding a faster processor.

Synset Examples

he souped up the old cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upgrade - raise to a higher standard
  • Synonym: tune - to adjust or modify for improved performance

Etymology

Originated in early 20th century US slang; 'soup' referred to narcotic or enhancing substances added to something for extra power.
