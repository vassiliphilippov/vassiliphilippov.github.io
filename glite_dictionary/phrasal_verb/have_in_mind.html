to have in mind

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪn maɪnd / (AmE), / hæv ɪn maɪnd / (BrE)
to be referring specifically to someone or something; to intend a particular person or thing when speaking or thinking about something
intend to refer to someone or something

Examples

I didn't say her name, but I definitely had in mind my colleague from accounting.
When you said 'the new manager,' whom did you have in mind?
During the interview, she had in mind several specific questions to ask.

Synset Examples

I'm thinking of good food when I talk about France
Yes, I meant you when I complained about people who gossip!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - to intend to refer to or designate someone or something
  • Synonym: refer (to) - to mention or allude to someone or something

2. VERB

/ hæv ɪn maɪnd / (AmE), / hæv ɪn maɪnd / (BrE)
to be thinking of or considering something or someone specific
consider or think of something specific

Examples

If you have in mind any suggestions, please let me know.
She had in mind a quiet evening at home.
They have in mind a new policy to improve customer service.

Synset Examples

Do you have in mind a specific color scheme?
I have in mind a great restaurant for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to think of - to have a particular idea or person in mind
  • Antonym: to forget - to not remember or consider something

Synset POS: None
