to put into practice

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˌɪntu ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / pʊt ˌɪntə ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
to implement or apply an idea, plan, or method in a real situation
implement or apply in reality

Examples

After months of planning, the company finally put into practice its new customer service policies.
The training will help you put into practice the skills you have learned.
She was excited to put into practice the theory from her university courses.

Synset Examples

They plan to put into practice their ideas at the conference.
He will put into practice the new strategies next week.
It's time to put into practice what we learned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implement - to carry out or put into effect
  • Synonym: to apply - to make use of or put into operation
