to skitter away

1. VERB

/ ˈskɪt̬ɚ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈskɪtə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move quickly and lightly, often in a sudden or frightened way
move quickly and lightly away

Examples

The lizard skittered away when it heard our footsteps on the path.
The leaves skittered away across the pavement in the wind.
As the lights flickered on, the cockroaches skittered away into the shadows.

Synset Examples

The mouse skittered away as I entered the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurry away - to move hurriedly with short quick steps away from something
  • Synonym: dart away - to move suddenly and quickly away

Etymology

From 'skitter' (to move lightly and quickly), likely imitative in origin, combined with 'away'.
