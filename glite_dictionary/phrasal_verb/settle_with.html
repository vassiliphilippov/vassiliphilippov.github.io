to settle with

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬l wɪð / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl wɪð / (BrE)
to reach an agreement with someone, especially to resolve a dispute or claim
reach an agreement to resolve a claim or dispute

Examples

After months of negotiation, they were finally able to settle with the insurance company.
The company offered compensation to settle with former employees who had filed complaints.
He refused to settle with the creditors until he reviewed every detail of the agreement.

Synset Examples

They decided to settle with the contractor amicably.
She had to settle with the insurance company after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - to have the same opinion or come to a shared decision
