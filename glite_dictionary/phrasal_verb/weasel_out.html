to weasel out

1. VERB

/ ˈwizəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwiːzl aʊt / (BrE)
to avoid doing something that one should do, especially by being dishonest or cunning
avoid responsibility or commitment
informal

Examples

Whenever there is extra work, he finds a way to weasel out of it.
The manager accused her of trying to weasel out of her obligations.
Don’t weasel out now that things are getting difficult.

Synset Examples

He tried to weasel out of his commitment again.
I won't let you weasel out of this discussion.
She couldn't weasel out of her responsibilities this time.
Don't weasel your way out of this responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodge - to avoid (something) by a sudden quick movement or trickery
  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery

Etymology

From the cunning and evasive nature attributed to weasels in folklore.
