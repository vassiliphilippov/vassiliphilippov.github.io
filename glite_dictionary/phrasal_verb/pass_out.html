to pass out

1. VERB

/ pæs aʊt / (AmE), / pɑːs aʊt / (BrE)
to distribute something to a number of people
distribute to several people

Examples

The volunteers helped pass out water bottles to the runners during the race.
Could you pass out these pamphlets to everyone in the audience?
At the meeting, she passed out copies of the agenda to all participants.

Synset Examples

The teacher handed out the exams

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hand out - to distribute something to several people

WordNet ID: oewn-02206426-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pæs aʊt / (AmE), / pɑːs aʊt / (BrE)
to become unconscious suddenly, especially because of illness, injury, or shock
lose consciousness suddenly

Examples

He was so exhausted during the hike that he suddenly passed out on the trail.
If you don't eat something soon, you might pass out from hunger.
People sometimes pass out at the sight of blood or after receiving shocking news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time
  • Antonym: to come to - to regain consciousness
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pæs aʊt / (AmE), / pɑːs aʊt / (BrE)
to lose consciousness, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
lose consciousness suddenly

Examples

He felt dizzy and suddenly passed out during the ceremony.
If you hold your breath for too long, you might pass out.
She passed out from exhaustion after running the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time
  • Antonym: to come round - to regain consciousness
