to get off at

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf ət / (BrE)
to leave a vehicle, such as a train or bus, at a particular stop or station
depart from a vehicle at a specified stop

Examples

Please remember to get off at the third stop for the museum.
She usually gets off at the main square to walk to her office.
We accidentally got off at the wrong station last night.

Synset Examples

Make sure to get off at Redfern for the concert.
You should get off at Redfern if you want to explore the area.
We need to get off at Redfern to switch trains.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to get on - to board a vehicle, such as a train or bus
