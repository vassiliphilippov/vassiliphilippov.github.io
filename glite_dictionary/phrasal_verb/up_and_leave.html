to up and leave

1. VERB

/ ʌp ənd liv / (AmE), / ʌp ənd liːv / (BrE)
to depart suddenly and without warning or explanation
depart suddenly without warning
informal

Examples

He didn't say a word—he just up and left in the middle of dinner.
Sometimes people up and leave even when things seem fine on the surface.
She couldn't believe he would up and leave after all those years together.

Synset Examples

He just up and left without telling anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to walk out - to leave suddenly, especially as a form of protest or disapproval
