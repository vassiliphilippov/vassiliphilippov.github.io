to play down

1. VERB

/ pleɪ daʊn / (AmE), / pleɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to try to make something seem less important or significant than it really is
make something seem less important

Examples

The company tried to play down the impact of the data breach in its statement.
She tends to play down her achievements, even though she works very hard.
Officials attempted to play down concerns about the new policy at the press conference.

Synset Examples

he played down his royal ancestry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or severe
  • Antonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
