to hang low

1. VERB

/ hæŋ loʊ / (AmE), / hæŋ ləʊ / (BrE)
to be positioned at a lower height than usual; (figurative) to feel or appear sad or depressed
to be at a low height; to feel sad

Examples

At the end of the long day, her spirits seemed to hang low.
Heavy branches hang low after the rain, almost touching the ground.
His eyes would often hang low whenever he thought about the past.

Synset Examples

His mood seemed to hang low all day.
They noticed the flag hung low in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to droop - to bend or hang downward limply
  • Synonym: slump - to sit, fall, or lean in a heavy or tired way; to decrease suddenly
