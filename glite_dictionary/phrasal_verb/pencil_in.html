to pencil in

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛnsl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl ɪn / (BrE)
to arrange or schedule something tentatively, knowing it may change later
arrange tentatively; schedule provisionally

Examples

I’ll pencil in a lunch meeting for Thursday, but let me know if that changes.
She asked her assistant to pencil in the conference on her calendar.
You can pencil in your vacation for July, just in case plans shift.

Synset Examples

Don't forget to pencil in your appointments.
Make sure to pencil in the meeting for next week.
I need to pencil in my vacation days soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - to arrange or plan an event for a particular time
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide or announce that a planned event will not happen

Etymology

From the noun 'pencil' (writing instrument) and the adverb 'in'; refers to writing an appointment in pencil to allow for easy changes.
