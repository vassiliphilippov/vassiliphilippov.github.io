to fly off

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ɑf / (AmE), / flaɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or upset, often without warning
suddenly become angry or upset

Examples

He tends to fly off the handle at the slightest provocation.
When she heard the news, she seemed to fly off in rage.
Try not to fly off if things don’t go as planned.

Synset Examples

He could fly off at any moment if provoked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to become suddenly angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm
