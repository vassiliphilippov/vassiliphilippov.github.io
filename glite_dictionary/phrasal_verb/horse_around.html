to horse around

1. VERB

/ hɔrs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / hɔːs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to play or behave in a silly, noisy, or boisterous way, especially when it is inappropriate
play or act in a boisterous way
informal

Examples

Stop horsing around and help me set the table, please.
The kids were horsing around in the backyard, laughing and shouting.
During the lecture, some students horsed around instead of paying attention.

Synset Examples

Enough horsing around — let's get back to work!
The bored children were fooling about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly way; to play instead of working
  • Synonym: to mess around - to spend time doing things that are not important; to behave playfully

Etymology

From the noun 'horse,' with reference to energetic or unruly movement, combined with 'around.'

2. VERB

/ hɔrs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / hɔːs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a silly, playful, or mischievous way, often making noise or causing minor trouble
act in a playful or silly way
informal

Examples

The kids were horsing around in the backyard, laughing and chasing each other.
During class, Jake kept horsing around instead of paying attention to the teacher.
She warned her friends to stop horsing around before someone got hurt.

Synset Examples

Stop horsing around and get back to work!
They were just horsing around, nothing serious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly or playful way
  • Synonym: to mess around - to behave in a playful or aimless manner

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to the lively or unruly behavior of horses.
