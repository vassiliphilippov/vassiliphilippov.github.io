to fall out

1. VERB

/ fɔl aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to stop being friendly with someone because of a disagreement or argument
stop being friendly due to a disagreement

Examples

They used to be close friends, but they fell out after a heated debate.
Siblings often fall out over small misunderstandings but usually reconcile.
I don't want to fall out with you over something so trivial.

Synset Examples

We fell out over a trivial question

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to make up - become friends again after a quarrel

2. VERB

/ fɔl aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
(of a situation, result, or conclusion) to happen or arise as a logical consequence; to follow logically.
to result or follow as a logical consequence

Examples

The conclusion of the proof falls out from the initial assumptions.
If these facts are correct, the inference simply falls out by itself.
In certain mathematical models, elegant results often fall out from the equations.

3. VERB

/ fɔl aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
(of hair, teeth, etc.) to become detached and drop from where it grew or was fixed.
become detached and drop (hair, teeth, etc.)

Examples

Many of his baby teeth began to fall out when he turned six.
After the stressful period, she noticed that more of her hair would fall out.
If screws fall out of the furniture, it could become unstable.

Synset Examples
