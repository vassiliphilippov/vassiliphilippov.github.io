to rally to support

1. VERB

/ ˈræli tə səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈræli tə səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to come together as a group in order to provide help or assistance to someone or a cause
join together to provide help

Examples

After the devastating storm, neighbors rallied to support those who had lost their homes.
Many volunteers rallied to support the fundraising event for cancer research.
The students rallied to support their classmate during his illness.

Synset Examples

The community rallied to support former drug users.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to band together - to unite as a group, especially for a cause
  • Synonym: to mobilize - to organize or prepare people to take action
  • Antonym: to disband - to break up or stop a group from acting together

Etymology

From 'rally' (to come together for a common purpose), ultimately from French 'rallier' (to gather again).
