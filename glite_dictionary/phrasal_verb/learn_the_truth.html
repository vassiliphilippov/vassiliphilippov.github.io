to learn the truth

1. VERB

/ lɝn ðə truθ / (AmE), / lɜːn ðə truːθ / (BrE)
to discover or find out what is actually true in a particular situation
discover what is true

Examples

It took her years to finally learn the truth about her family history.
The public demanded to learn the truth behind the political scandal.
He was devastated when he learned the truth about his friend’s betrayal.

Synset Examples

Despite the rumors, I never did learn the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discover the truth - to find out what is true
  • Synonym: uncover the truth - to reveal what is true
  • Antonym: be deceived - to accept something false as true
