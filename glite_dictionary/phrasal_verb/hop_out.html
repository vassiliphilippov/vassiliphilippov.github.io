to hop out

1. VERB

/ hɑp aʊt / (AmE), / hɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to get out of a vehicle or place quickly or energetically, often with a light or sudden movement
get out quickly or energetically

Examples

She hopped out of the taxi and ran towards the station.
As soon as the car stopped, the children hopped out and dashed inside.
He hopped out of bed the moment his alarm rang.

Synset Examples

The officer hopped out when he spotted an illegally parked car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jump out - to leave a vehicle or place by jumping, often quickly
