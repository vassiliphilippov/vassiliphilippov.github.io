to yack away

1. VERB

/ jæk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / jak əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to talk incessantly and in a tiresome or annoying way
talk continuously and tiresomely
informal

Examples

She can yack away for hours without ever running out of things to say.
The kids were yacking away in the back seat the entire drive.
Even after everyone was ready to leave, he just kept yacking away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly and continuously about unimportant things
  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • Antonym: be silent - not to speak or make any sound
