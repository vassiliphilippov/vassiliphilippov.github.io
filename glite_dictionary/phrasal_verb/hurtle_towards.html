to hurtle towards

1. VERB

/ ˈhɜr.tl̩ təˈwɔrdz / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.tl̩ təˈwɔːdz / (BrE)
to move quickly and violently in the direction of something, often with a sense of being out of control or unstoppable
move rapidly and forcefully toward something

Examples

The train began to hurtle towards the station at an alarming speed.
He watched the asteroid hurtle towards Earth on the computer screen.
With brakes failing, the car hurtled towards the intersection, unable to stop.

Synset Examples

They saw the rocks hurtle towards them during the avalanche.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush toward - to move quickly in the direction of something
  • Degree Lower: approach - to come nearer to something in distance

Etymology

From 'hurtle' (to move with great speed), originally Middle English, perhaps imitative; combined with the preposition 'towards'.
