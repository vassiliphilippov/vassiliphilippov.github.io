to play a joke on

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ə dʒoʊk ɑn / (AmE), / pleɪ ə dʒəʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to trick someone in a playful way as a joke, often to make others laugh
trick someone in a playful way

Examples

The students decided to play a joke on their teacher by switching seats before class.
It’s not very nice to play a joke on someone who doesn’t expect it.
Yesterday, my brother played a joke on me by putting salt in my coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank - a practical joke; a mischievous trick
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or try to provoke in a playful way

2. VERB

/ pleɪ ə dʒoʊk ɑn / (AmE), / pleɪ ə dʒəʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone for amusement, often as a prank
deceive or prank someone for fun

Examples

The children decided to play a joke on their teacher by switching the classroom signs.
Someone played a joke on me by putting salt in my coffee instead of sugar.
We played a joke on Tom by wrapping his desk in aluminum foil.

Synset Examples

We tricked the teacher into thinking that class would be cancelled next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play a trick on - deceive or prank someone, similar meaning
  • Synonym: prank - a practical joke or mischievous act
