to chill out

1. VERB

/ tʃɪl aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to relax or become calm, especially after being upset or excited
to relax or become calm
informal

Examples

After a stressful day at work, I like to chill out with some music.
You need to chill out and stop worrying so much about the little things.
He finally chilled out after his friends talked to him.

Synset Examples

After the fight both men need to cool off.
It took a while after the baby was born for things to settle down again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
  • Synonym: to calm down - to become less angry or excited
