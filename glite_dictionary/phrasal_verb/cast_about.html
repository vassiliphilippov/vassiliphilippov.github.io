to cast about

1. VERB

/ kæst əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / kɑːst əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to search or look around anxiously or with effort, especially for something needed or desired
search anxiously or with effort

Examples

After losing his job, he cast about for new opportunities in different industries.
She cast about in her mind for an excuse, but couldn’t think of one.
They cast about for ways to solve the ongoing budget crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seek - attempt to find or obtain something
  • Synonym: search - look for something carefully

Etymology

From the verb 'cast' (to throw, direct) + 'about', used since the 16th century in the sense of turning one's attention or efforts to something.
