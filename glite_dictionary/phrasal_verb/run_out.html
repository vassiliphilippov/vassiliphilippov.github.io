to run out

1. VERB

/ rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
to use up or exhaust the available supply of something, so that none remains
exhaust the supply of something

Examples

If we run out of milk, I'll go to the store and buy more.
The team ran out of energy in the final minutes of the game.
We must leave now before we run out of time.

Synset Examples

We ran out of time just as the discussion was getting interesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deplete - to use up the supply or resources of something
  • Degree Lower: to run short - to have nearly no more of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00562791-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow out from a container or enclosed space and be lost, usually unintentionally.
(of liquid) flow out and become lost

Examples

If you don't tighten the cap, the water might run out of the bottle.
Some oil ran out of the engine and stained the driveway.
She didn't notice the juice running out of the carton until it was too late.

Synset Examples

The milk spilled across the floor
The wine spilled onto the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - (of liquid) accidentally flow over the edge of its container
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02073816-v
Synset POS: v

/ rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)

become used up; be exhausted

Examples
