to separate from

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛpəˌreɪt frəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛpəˌreɪt frɒm / (BrE)
to keep apart or distinct from something else; to not mix or combine with
keep apart from; not mix with

Examples

It's important to separate from your emotions when making difficult decisions.
Good accounting practice requires you to separate from business and personal expenses.
She finds it hard to separate from her work during vacations.

Synset Examples

They keep their personal lives separate from work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinguish from - to recognize or mark as different from
  • Antonym: combine with - to mix together with
