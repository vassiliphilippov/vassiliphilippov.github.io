to run low

1. VERB

/ rʌn loʊ / (AmE), / rʌn ləʊ / (BrE)
to reach a level where there is very little of something left; to be nearly used up or exhausted
to become nearly used up

Examples

My patience is starting to run low after all these delays.
We need to buy more milk because we're running low at home.
Her energy ran low toward the end of the marathon.

Synset Examples

The money had gone after a few days
Gas is running low at the gas stations in the Midwest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run out - to have no more left of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01195306-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn loʊ / (AmE), / rʌn ləʊ / (BrE)
to have a decreasing amount of something so that there is not much left
to have little left; be nearly out

Examples

We're running low on milk, so I'll need to buy some more.
The team realized their energy was running low after a long day of work.
Our patience is running low with these endless delays.

Synset Examples

I volunteered for the beer run; we were running low.
During the siege, supplies were running dangerously low.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to run out - to use up all of something so that none remains

Synset POS: None
