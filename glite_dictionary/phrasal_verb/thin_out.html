to thin out

1. VERB

/ θɪn aʊt / (AmE), / θɪn aʊt / (BrE)
to make a solution or mixture less strong or concentrated, often by adding liquid
make a mixture less strong

Examples

If the soup tastes too salty, you can thin out the flavor by adding water.
She decided to thin out the paint to achieve a lighter shade on the wall.
Before serving, he would always thin out the cocktail with a splash of soda.

Synset Examples

cut bourbon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilute - make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding another substance
  • Antonym: concentrate - make (a solution or mixture) stronger or denser

2. VERB

/ θɪn aʊt / (AmE), / θɪn aʊt / (BrE)
to become less dense or numerous; to become sparser
become less dense or sparser

Examples

As we drove into the countryside, the buildings began to thin out.
The crowd started to thin out once the concert ended.
The forest thins out near the edge of the lake.

Synset Examples

Towards the end of town, the houses thinned out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thin - to make or become less dense
  • Antonym: to thicken - to become denser or more crowded

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ θɪn aʊt / (AmE), / θɪn aʊt / (BrE)
to make something less dense or numerous by removing some parts or members; to reduce the number or quantity so that what remains is spread more sparsely
make less dense by removing some

Examples

The gardener will thin out the seedlings to encourage healthy growth.
Traffic tends to thin out after the morning rush hour.
As the crowd began to thin out, the concert hall felt almost empty.

Synset Examples

thin out the young plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilute - make a substance thinner or weaker by adding something
  • Antonym: concentrate - to make denser or more crowded by reducing the volume of something else
