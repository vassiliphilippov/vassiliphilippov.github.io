to add up

1. VERB

/ æd ʌp / (AmE), / æd ʌp / (BrE)
to seem logical, reasonable, or likely; to make sense when considered carefully
make sense; be logical

Examples

Her explanation didn't add up, so I kept asking questions.
Something about his story just doesn't add up to me.
If you look at the facts, they simply don't add up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make sense - be reasonable or understandable
  • Antonym: make no sense - not be logical or comprehensible

2. VERB

/ æd ʌp / (AmE), / æd ʌp / (BrE)
to find the total of several numbers or amounts by combining them
determine the sum of several amounts

Examples

Please add up these numbers and write the total in the box.
After adding up all her expenses, she realized she had overspent.
The accountant added up the receipts at the end of the day.

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum - find the total amount by addition
  • Antonym: to subtract - to take one number or amount away from another

The numbers just don't add up—we must have made a mistake somewhere.
All his debts add up to a considerable sum.

4. VERB

/ æd ʌp / (AmE), / æd ʌp / (BrE)
(of facts, explanations, etc.) to be logical or make sense when considered together.
to make sense, be logical

Examples

Her explanation just doesn’t add up—there are too many inconsistencies.
If his story doesn’t add up, the police will become suspicious.
All these details finally add up and reveal the truth.

Synset Examples

His story didn't quite add up when I thought about it.
All her excuses just don't add up anymore.
The expenses just don't add up this month.
I can see through his excuses; they don't add up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make sense - to be reasonable and understandable
  • Synonym: to hold water - (of an argument) to be logically sound
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to fail to make sense or be logically consistent
