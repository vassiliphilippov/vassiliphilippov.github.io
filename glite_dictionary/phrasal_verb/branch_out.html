to branch out

1. VERB

/ bræntʃ aʊt / (AmE), / brɑːntʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to expand into new activities, areas, or businesses, especially in order to reduce risk or seek new opportunities
expand into new activities or areas

Examples

The tech company decided to branch out into renewable energy.
After years of selling only coffee, they began to branch out by offering pastries and sandwiches.
Once she had mastered painting, she wanted to branch out into sculpture.

Synset Examples

The company diversified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diversify - to make or become more varied, especially in business; to expand into new areas

2. VERB

/ bræntʃ aʊt / (AmE), / brɑːntʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to expand into new activities, interests, or areas of operation beyond one's usual limits
expand into new areas or activities

Examples

After years of teaching, she decided to branch out and start her own consulting business.
Our company is planning to branch out into the Asian market next year.
It's important to branch out beyond your comfort zone to achieve personal growth.

Synset Examples

It's time for him to branch out and explore.
She plans to branch out into new markets next year.
They encouraged students to branch out in their studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diversify - to develop a wider range of products, interests, or activities

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'branch' (from trees), meaning to spread out from a central point.
