to let out a yawn

1. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt ə jɔn / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt ə jɔːn / (BrE)
to yawn, especially making a sound or showing it openly
to yawn audibly or obviously

Examples

He let out a yawn during the long and boring meeting.
She quietly let out a yawn as midnight approached.
The child let out a yawn and rubbed his eyes before heading to bed.

Synset Examples

She let out a big yawn, signaling she was tired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yawn - to open the mouth wide and breathe in deeply, usually when tired or bored
