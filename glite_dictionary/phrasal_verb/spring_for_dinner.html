to spring for dinner

1. VERB

/ sprɪŋ fəɹ ˈdɪnɚ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ fə ˈdɪnə / (BrE)
to pay for dinner, especially as a treat for others
pay for dinner for others
informal american

Examples

I decided to spring for dinner at that fancy new Italian restaurant.
It was my birthday, so my friends offered to spring for dinner as a treat.
Whenever we have visitors, my parents usually spring for dinner at a nice place.

Synset Examples

I decided to spring for dinner at that fancy restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat - to pay for something as a gift for others

Etymology

From the verb 'spring' meaning 'to pay for (something expensive)', American slang, first attested early 20th century.
