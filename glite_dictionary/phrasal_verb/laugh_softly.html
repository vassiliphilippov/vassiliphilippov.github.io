to laugh softly

1. VERB

/ læf ˈsɔf(t)li / (AmE), / lɑːf ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
to laugh in a quiet or restrained manner
laugh in a quiet or restrained manner

Examples

She couldn't help but laugh softly at the unexpected joke.
He laughed softly so as not to disturb the sleeping child.
As the story ended, the room was filled with people laughing softly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself
  • Degree Higher: giggle - to laugh lightly in a silly or nervous way
  • Antonym: laugh loudly - to laugh with a lot of volume or noise
