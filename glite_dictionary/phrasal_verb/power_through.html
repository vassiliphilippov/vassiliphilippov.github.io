to power through

1. VERB

/ ˈpaʊɚ ˌθru / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ˌθruː / (BrE)
to continue or complete a task despite difficulties or fatigue
persevere through difficulties

Examples

Despite feeling exhausted, she managed to power through the final hours of her shift.
He had to power through his last semester of college even though he was unwell.
We will need to power through these challenges if we want to succeed.

Synset Examples

She relies on supercaffeine to power through her long shifts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to persist in doing something despite difficulty or delay
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or admit defeat

2. VERB

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ θru / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə θruː / (BrE)
to continue doing something with determination, especially during difficulty or adversity
persist despite difficulty

Examples

Despite the exhaustion, she powered through the last hour of her shift.
He powered through his cold and still made it to the meeting.
We powered through the dense paperwork to finish the project on time.

Synset Examples

She powered through the first mile with determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to persist steadfastly in pursuit of an undertaking or aim
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or discontinue an effort, usually due to difficulty
