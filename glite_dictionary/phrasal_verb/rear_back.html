to rear back

1. VERB

/ rɪr bæk / (AmE), / rɪə bæk / (BrE)
to react suddenly and strongly, often by drawing back physically or figuratively, especially in anger, resentment, or protest
suddenly draw back in anger or protest

Examples

The crowd began to rear back in outrage when the verdict was announced.
When criticized, she would often rear back and defend her actions fiercely.
He reared back in protest at the unfair decision.

2. VERB

/ rɪr ˈbæk / (AmE), / rɪə ˈbæk / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a horse) to suddenly raise the front part of the body off the ground, standing on the hind legs.
(of animals) rise on hind legs

Examples

The horse reared back suddenly, startled by the noise in the stable.
When threatened, a snake may rear back before striking.
The rider lost control as the stallion reared back on the trail.

Synset Examples

the frightened horse reared back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rear - (of a horse or animal) to rise up on hind legs
