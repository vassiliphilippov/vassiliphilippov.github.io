to leave at that

1. VERB

/ liv ət ðæt / (AmE), / liːv ət ðæt / (BrE)
to stop discussing or considering something further; to end the conversation or topic at the current point
stop discussing a topic

Examples

Let's agree to disagree and leave at that for now.
She saw he was getting upset, so she decided to leave at that.
After a brief explanation, the manager said they would leave at that.

Synset Examples

He decided to leave at that, not wanting to argue.
