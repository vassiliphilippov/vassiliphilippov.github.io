to have an accident

1. VERB

/ hæv ən əkˈsɪdənt / (AmE), / hæv ən əkˈsɪdənt / (BrE)
to experience an unexpected event, typically resulting in harm or injury
to be involved in an unexpected harmful event

Examples

He had an accident while crossing the street and broke his arm.
She had an accident at work but recovered quickly.
The child had an accident on the playground, requiring a visit to the nurse.

Synset Examples

He had an accident while riding his bike yesterday.
He had an accident on his way to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a mishap - to experience a minor unexpected problem or accident

2. VERB

/ ˈhæv ən əkˈsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈhav ən əkˈsɪdənt / (BrE)
to experience a crash, mishap, or an unintended event causing harm or damage
experience a crash or mishap

Examples

I hope you don’t have an accident on the slippery roads tonight.
She was nervous that her son might have an accident during his first driving lesson.
He had an accident last year, but thankfully no one was hurt.

Synset Examples

She was worried he would have an accident driving too fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - a collision, especially of vehicles
  • Synonym: mishap - an unlucky accident or minor misfortune
