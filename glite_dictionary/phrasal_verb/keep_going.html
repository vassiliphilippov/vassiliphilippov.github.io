to keep going

1. VERB

/ kip ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / kiːp ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a resource, supply, or support) to be sufficient for someone during a particular period or until a point is reached.
to be sufficient for a period

Examples

I'm not sure if there's enough food to keep going until payday.
A small loan helped the business keep going through tough times.
She used her savings to keep going until she found a new job.

Synset Examples

This money will keep us going for another year

2. VERB

/ kip ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / kiːp ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
to continue to do something without stopping or being interrupted
continue without stopping

Examples

Even when you feel tired, it's important to keep going if you want to reach your goal.
The bus broke down, but the driver managed to keep going until the next stop.
If we keep going at this pace, we'll finish before noon.

Synset Examples

The disease will run on unchecked
The party kept going until 4 A.M.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process
  • Antonym: stop - to cease moving or doing something
