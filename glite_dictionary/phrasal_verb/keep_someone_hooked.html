to keep someone hooked

1. VERB

/ kip ˈsʌmwʌn hʊkt / (AmE), / kiːp ˈsʌmwən hʊkt / (BrE)
to maintain someone's strong interest or attention; to make someone want to continue watching, reading, or engaging with something
maintain strong interest or attention

Examples

The suspenseful plot twists kept me hooked from beginning to end.
The teacher knows how to keep her students hooked during long lessons.
That documentary was so fascinating—it kept us hooked all night.

Synset Examples

I loved the ussun of the novel; it kept me hooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to hold the interest or attention of someone firmly
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or tired
