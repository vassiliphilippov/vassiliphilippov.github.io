to rout up

1. VERB

/ raʊt ʌp / (AmE), / raʊt ʌp / (BrE)
to obtain or discover something by searching carefully
find by searching

Examples

She managed to rout up some rare photographs from the attic.
He tried to rout up the necessary documents before the deadline.
After a long search, they finally routed up the missing keys.

Synset Examples

What did you rout out in the library?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look carefully for something
  • Synonym: to root out - to remove or find something by searching thoroughly

Etymology

From the verb 'rout' meaning 'to search or dig out', of Middle English origin, from Old French 'ruter'; paired with 'up' as a verbal particle.
