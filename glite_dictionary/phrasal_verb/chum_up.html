to chum up

1. VERB

/ tʃʌm ʌp / (AmE), / tʃʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to become friendly with someone; to act in a deliberately friendly or sociable way towards someone
become friends; act friendly towards
informal

Examples

The new student tried to chum up with his classmates on the first day of school.
She always manages to chum up to the boss at office parties.
It’s easy to chum up with people when you travel alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befriend - act as a friend to someone
  • Antonym: to alienate - make someone feel isolated or estranged

Etymology

From 'chum' (close friend, British slang), possibly from 'chamber fellow', plus the phrasal verb particle 'up'.
