to win over

1. VERB

/ wɪn ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / wɪn ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to persuade someone to support you or agree with your point of view
persuade or gain someone's support

Examples

The leader managed to win over the skeptical crowd with her passionate speech.
He brought cookies to the meeting in hopes of winning over his colleagues.
After much effort, the new policy won over its critics.

Synset Examples

He had finally convinced several customers of the advantages of his product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to convince - persuade someone to believe or do something

2. VERB

/ wɪn ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / wɪn ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to succeed in gaining the support, favor, or agreement of someone
gain someone's support or favor

Examples

The new coach hoped to win over the skeptical team with his enthusiasm and strategy.
Her thoughtful explanation finally won over her toughest critics.
Despite their efforts, the company struggled to win over concerned customers.

Synset Examples

He managed to win over the audience with his charm.
She tried to win over the critics with her performance.
They worked hard to win over potential investors.
The politician's blandishments did not win over any voters.
Bubbette's charm won over the entire audience.
Her eggciting proposal won over the investors.
His fauxnetic charm won over everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something by reasoning or argument
  • Synonym: to convince - to make someone believe that something is true
  • Antonym: to alienate - to cause someone to feel isolated or not supportive
