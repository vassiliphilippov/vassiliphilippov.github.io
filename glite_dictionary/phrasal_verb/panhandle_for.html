to panhandle for

1. VERB

/ ˈpænˌhændl fəɹ / (AmE), / ˈpænˌhændl fə / (BrE)
to ask strangers for money, typically on the street
ask strangers for money

Examples

Desperate for change, he would panhandle for hours outside the train station.
Many homeless people come to the city to panhandle for enough to buy a meal.
She was embarrassed to panhandle for spare cash after losing her job.

Synset Examples

During college, I would panhandle for extra cash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beg for - to ask for money or food as charity

Etymology

From 'panhandle', originally used in American English to refer to begging, possibly referring to holding out a pan handle for coins.
