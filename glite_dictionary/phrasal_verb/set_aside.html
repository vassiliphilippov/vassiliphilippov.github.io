to set aside

1. VERB

/ sɛt əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / sɛt əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to reserve or allocate something, such as time or money, for a specific purpose or person
reserve or allocate for a purpose

Examples

Each year, I set aside a portion of my salary for savings.
The government decided to set aside funds for disaster relief.
She always sets aside some time on Sundays to call her parents.

Synset Examples

I will earmark this money for your research
She sets aside time for meditation every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earmark - to designate something for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute resources for a specific purpose
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00726112-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛt əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / sɛt əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
(law) to annul or revoke a legal decision or judgment, making it no longer valid.
annul/revoke a legal decision
legal

Examples

The judge decided to set aside the earlier ruling due to new evidence.
If the appeal is successful, the court may set aside the conviction.
The lawyer filed a motion to set aside the verdict on procedural grounds.

Synset Examples

the court set aside the conviction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overturn - to reverse a legal decision so that it is no longer valid
  • Antonym: to uphold - to support or maintain a legal decision in force
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02483910-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sɛt əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / sɛt əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to stop something from operating or having effect, especially temporarily; to suspend or invalidate the application of something
temporarily stop or suspend; make inoperative

Examples

The judge decided to set aside the conviction due to new evidence.
The company agreed to set aside the current policy while it's under review.
In times of crisis, governments may set aside certain regulations to act swiftly.

Synset Examples

suspend payments on the loan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suspend - temporarily prevent from continuing or being in force or effect
  • Synonym: revoke - to officially cancel something so that it is no longer valid
  • Antonym: to enforce - ensure that a law or rule is carried out
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00543748-v
Synset POS: v
