to drift off

1. VERB

/ drɪft ɑf / (AmE), / drɪft ɒf / (BrE)
to gradually fall asleep, especially without intending to
to fall asleep gradually

Examples

I tend to drift off if I'm reading a boring book late at night.
She drifted off during the long car ride, her head resting against the window.
He sometimes drifts off while watching television after a long day.

Synset Examples

he always falls asleep during lectures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall asleep - to go from waking to sleeping
  • Antonym: to wake up - to stop sleeping and become awake
