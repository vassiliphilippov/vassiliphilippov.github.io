to tuck in

1. VERB

/ tʌk ɪn / (AmE), / tʌk ɪn / (BrE)
To eat a large amount of food eagerly and with enthusiasm.
eat a lot of food eagerly
informal

Examples

The children couldn’t wait to tuck in when they saw the feast on the table.
After a long day’s hike, we all tucked in to a hearty dinner at the campsite.
She was so hungry that she started tucking in as soon as the food arrived.

Synset Examples

My son tucked in a whole pizza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - To eat food quickly and eagerly
  • Antonym: nibble - To eat food by taking small bites
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01175505-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʌk ɪn / (AmE), / tʌk ɪn / (BrE)
to arrange bedding around someone securely in bed, especially to prepare them for sleep
arrange blankets around someone in bed

Examples

Every night, her mother would tuck in the children before turning off the light.
Could you tuck in Sam? He likes his blankets snug.
After reading a bedtime story, Dad came to tuck in his daughter.

Synset Examples

After reading a story, she tucked him in to go night-night.
He was sound asleep, tucked in the sack.
It's time to tucker in for the night.
