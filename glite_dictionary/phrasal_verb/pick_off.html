to pick off

1. VERB

/ pɪk ɑf / (AmE), / pɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to shoot, kill, or remove people or things one by one, especially from a group or crowd
to shoot or remove one by one

Examples

The sniper managed to pick off enemy soldiers from his hidden perch.
One by one, the predator would pick off the weakest members of the herd.
In the game, players tried to pick off their opponents before being spotted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - to aim at and try to hit
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from attacking or harming

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɪk ɔf / (AmE), / pɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling or plucking it away sharply, especially one at a time
remove by pulling or plucking sharply

Examples

She picked off the dead leaves from the plant so it would look healthier.
The children picked off the candies from the cake before they started eating.
I spent the morning picking off weeds from the flowerbed.

Synset Examples

pluck the flowers off the bush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck - to pull something quickly in order to remove it
