to take action

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈækʃən / (AmE), / teɪk ˈækʃən / (BrE)
to do something in order to achieve a change or deal with a problem
to do something to achieve a change

Examples

We must take action now to prevent further damage to the environment.
After months of discussion, the committee finally decided to take action.
If you see something suspicious, you should take action immediately.

Synset Examples

Stop being a sadfuck and start taking action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - to do something for a particular purpose
