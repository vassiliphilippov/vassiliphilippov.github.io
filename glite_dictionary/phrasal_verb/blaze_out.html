to blaze out

1. VERB

/ bleɪz aʊt / (AmE), / bleɪz aʊt / (BrE)
to move swiftly and conspicuously, as if emitting flames; to proceed rapidly and forcefully
move rapidly and conspicuously

Examples

The rocket blazed out of the atmosphere, leaving a trail of fire behind it.
As the starting gun sounded, the runners blazed out from the blocks, eager for victory.
The car blazed out of the garage and sped down the empty street.

Synset Examples

The spaceship blazed out into space

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Antonym: crawl - to move slowly; opposite of moving rapidly

Etymology

From 'blaze' (to burn brightly) + out (away, outward direction), forming a metaphor for moving flamboyantly or rapidly.
