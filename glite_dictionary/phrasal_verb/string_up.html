to string up

1. VERB

/ strɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / strɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange or link together items, ideas, or other things as if placing them in a sequence on a string
link or arrange in a sequence

Examples

The director decided to string up several short scenes to create one long sequence.
If you string up these anecdotes, you'll have the outline for your speech.
He managed to string up the memories of his travels into a compelling narrative.

Synset Examples

string these ideas together
string up these songs and you'll have a musical

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to string together - to arrange or connect in a series or sequence

2. VERB

/ strɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / strɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to kill (someone) by hanging them, especially as a form of punishment or execution
kill by hanging

Examples

The vigilante mob threatened to string up the horse thief outside the courthouse.
In those days, they would sometimes string up criminals without a fair trial.
The story described how the outlaws were strung up in the old town square.

Synset Examples

The murderer was hanged on Friday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hang - to kill by suspending by the neck

3. VERB

/ strɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / strɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to hang or attach something, especially in a decorative or prominent way
hang or attach decoratively

Examples

We should string up some lights across the backyard for tonight's barbecue.
She helped her father string up the new curtains in the living room.
They quickly strung up balloons and banners before the guests arrived.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang up - to suspend or attach something from above so that it hangs down
