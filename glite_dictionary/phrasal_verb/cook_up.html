to cook up

1. VERB

/ kʊk ʌp / (AmE), / kʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to invent or devise something, especially something false, dishonest, or artificial
devise or invent, often dishonestly

Examples

The company tried to cook up a reason for the sudden price increase.
She managed to cook up an elaborate story to explain her absence.
The suspect had clearly cooked up a fake alibi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent or concoct, typically with deceitful intent
  • Synonym: to make up - to invent a story, excuse, or explanation

2. VERB

/ kʊk ʌp / (AmE), / kʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare or make (a dish or meal) by mixing ingredients, often quickly or inventively
prepare or make by mixing ingredients

Examples

She managed to cook up a delicious meal from just a few leftovers.
Let's cook up something special for dinner tonight.
He quickly cooked up some soup to warm everyone after the hike.

3. VERB

/ kʊk ʌp / (AmE), / kʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to invent or devise something, especially a story, excuse, or plan, often dishonestly or imaginatively
invent or devise, often dishonestly

Examples

The politician tried to cook up a convincing explanation for his absence.
They managed to cook up a clever scheme to surprise their friend.
She claimed her brother had cooked up the story to avoid getting into trouble.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent something, often with the intent to deceive
