to bring out in a rash

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ aʊt ɪn ə ræʃ / (AmE), / brɪŋ aʊt ɪn ə ræʃ / (BrE)
to cause someone to develop a rash or skin irritation, often as an allergic reaction
cause to develop a skin rash

Examples

Some detergents can bring out in a rash if you have sensitive skin.
Wearing wool sweaters sometimes brings him out in a rash.
Being near cats brought her out in a rash as a child.

Synset Examples

Dust allergies can bring him out in a rash.
Certain soaps might bring her out in a rash.
Some flowers can bring me out in a rash.
