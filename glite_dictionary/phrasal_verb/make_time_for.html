to make time for

1. VERB

/ meɪk taɪm fɔr / (AmE), / meɪk taɪm fə / (BrE)
to arrange or find time to do something, even when busy
arrange or find time (when busy)

Examples

She always tries to make time for her family, no matter how busy she gets.
It's important to make time for relaxation and self-care every week.
Even with his hectic job, he managed to make time for volunteering at the shelter.

Synset Examples

He will make time for exercise despite his busy schedule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: find time for - to succeed in making time for something despite other commitments
