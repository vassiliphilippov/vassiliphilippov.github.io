to kick downstairs

1. VERB

/ kɪk daʊnˈstɛrz / (AmE), / kɪk daʊnˈsteəz / (BrE)
to assign someone to a lower position or reduce their rank, especially in a work or organizational context
demote; reduce in rank

Examples

After the reorganization, Mark was kicked downstairs to an entry-level role.
She feared her outspoken comments would get her kicked downstairs by her supervisor.
They kicked him downstairs instead of firing him outright after the incident.

Synset Examples

She was demoted because she always speaks up
He was broken down to Sergeant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demote - reduce someone to a lower rank or position
  • Antonym: to promote - raise someone to a higher rank or position
