to bandy about

1. VERB

/ ˈbændi əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbændi əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to discuss, mention, or pass around (an idea, rumor, or name) casually and often without careful consideration
discuss or circulate casually

Examples

The management team began to bandy about new strategies for improving productivity.
Her name was bandied about as a possible candidate for the position.
Several theories were bandied about during the meeting, though none were confirmed.

Synset Examples

bandy about an idea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to circulate - to pass from person to person; to be spread widely
  • Degree Lower: rumor - information or stories passed from person to person, often unverified

Etymology

From the verb 'bandy' (to toss to and fro), probably from French 'bander' (to fight, to bandy blows).
