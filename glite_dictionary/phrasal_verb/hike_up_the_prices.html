to hike up the prices

1. VERB

/ haɪk ʌp ðə ˈpraɪsɪz / (AmE), / haɪk ʌp ðə ˈpraɪsɪz / (BrE)
to increase prices considerably and often suddenly
raise prices significantly

Examples

The restaurant decided to hike up the prices during the holiday season to increase profits.
Many customers complained when the company hiked up the prices of essential goods.
If they continue hiking up the prices, people will start shopping elsewhere.

Synset Examples

It's time to hike up the prices for the tickets.
They decided to jack up the prices for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise prices - to make prices higher
  • Antonym: reduce prices - to make prices lower
  • Synonym: jack up the prices - to raise prices sharply

Etymology

From 'hike up' (to raise or lift), with 'hike' possibly of American origin, meaning to pull or raise sharply.
