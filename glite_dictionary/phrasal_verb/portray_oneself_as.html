to portray oneself as

1. VERB

/ pɔrˈtreɪ wʌnˈsɛlf æz / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ wʌnˈself æz / (BrE)
to present or describe oneself in a particular way, often to influence others' perceptions
present oneself in a particular way

Examples

He always tries to portray himself as a responsible leader during meetings.
Despite his flaws, Tom managed to portray himself as a trustworthy friend.
The politician attempted to portray himself as a champion of the people.

Synset Examples

He likes to portray himself as a tough guy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present oneself as - to behave in a way intended to create a certain impression
  • Degree Higher: disguise oneself as - to change one's appearance to hide one's identity or intentions
