to go back

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈbæk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈbæk / (BrE)
to return to a previous topic, point, or idea in conversation or thought
return to earlier topic or idea

Examples

I'd like to go back to what you said earlier about the project.
The author often goes back to this theme throughout the novel.
Can we go back to my original question for a moment?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revert - to return to a previous state or topic
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00961746-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈbæk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈbak / (BrE)
to return to a previous or former condition, especially financially, after a setback or loss
return to a former financial condition

Examples

The company's shares quickly went back to their pre-crisis value.
After several tough years, she managed to go back to her original income level.
It may take time for the market to go back after such a significant loss.

Synset Examples

We expect the stocks to recover to $2.90
The company managed to recuperate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after difficulty or loss
  • Synonym: to recuperate - to recover from financial loss or difficulty
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00529419-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈbæk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈbæk / (BrE)
to have existed or started at a particular time in the past; to belong to an earlier time
to originate or date from a previous time

Examples

This tradition can go back several centuries.
The origins of the festival go back to ancient times.
Some of these laws go back hundreds of years.

Synset Examples

This story dates back 200 years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to date back - to have existed since a particular time in the past
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02729826-v
Synset POS: v
