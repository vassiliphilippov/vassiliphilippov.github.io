to slip by

1. VERB

/ slɪp baɪ / (AmE), / slɪp baɪ / (BrE)
(of time) to pass or go by, often quickly and unnoticed.
(of time) pass quickly or unnoticed

Examples

The summer seemed to slip by before we knew it.
Hours can slip by when you're deeply focused on your work.
She watched the days slip by as she waited for news.

Synset Examples

three years elapsed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - (of time) to go by
  • Synonym: to fly by - (of time) to seem to go by very quickly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02076777-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ slɪp baɪ / (AmE), / slɪp baɪ / (BrE)
(of time or events) to pass without being noticed or without anyone paying attention.
pass unnoticed (of time or events)

Examples

The summer seemed to slip by before we even noticed.
Don't let this opportunity slip by without taking advantage of it.
Hours can slip by when you're doing something you enjoy.

Synset Examples

The deadline seemed to slip by unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pass by - to move past or go past without stopping or without being noticed
