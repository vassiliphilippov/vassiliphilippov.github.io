to pullulate with

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌljəˌleɪt wɪð / (AmE), / ˈpʌljʊleɪt wɪð / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) to contain or be filled with a large number of people or things; to teem with.
be filled or teeming with (something)

Examples

The forest pullulates with life during the spring months.
The market pullulated with eager shoppers as soon as it opened.
After dark, the city pullulates with activity and noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teem with - to be full of or swarming with
  • Synonym: overflow with - to be so full as to spill over

Etymology

From Latin 'pullulare' (to sprout).
