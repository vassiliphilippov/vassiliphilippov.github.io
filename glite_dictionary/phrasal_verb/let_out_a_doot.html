to let out a doot

1. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt ə duːt / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt ə duːt / (BrE)
to emit a short, often humorous or silly sound, typically imitating a horn or similar noise
emit a short, silly sound
informal slang

Examples

He couldn't help but let out a doot during the funny part of the song.
Whenever she's nervous, she sometimes lets out a doot without realizing it.
The toy makes children laugh when it lets out a doot as you squeeze it.

Synset Examples

He let out a doot when he accidentally stepped on the dog's paw.

Etymology

'Doot' is an onomatopoeic word representing horn-like sounds, popularized in internet memes.
