to butt against

1. VERB

/ bʌt əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / bʌt əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to collide violently or forcefully with an obstacle
collide violently with an obstacle

Examples

The car suddenly butted against the guardrail when the driver lost control.
She accidentally butted against the edge of the table while backing up.
The goat repeatedly butted against the fence, trying to escape.

Synset Examples

I ran into the telephone pole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collide with - to come into forceful contact with something

2. VERB

/ bʌt əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / bʌt əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
(of a structure or area) to be directly adjacent to or share a boundary with something else.
be adjacent to; share a boundary with

Examples

The old stone wall butts against the edge of the forest.
Our property butts against the river to the north.
This warehouse butts against the main road, making access easy.

Synset Examples

Canada adjoins the U.S.
England marches with Scotland

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjoin - to be next to or joined with
  • Synonym: border - to form a boundary with
  • Antonym: separate - to divide or keep apart

Etymology

From 'butt' (to strike or push against) + 'against'.
