to turn up the heat

1. VERB

/ tɝn ʌp ðə hit / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp ðə hiːt / (BrE)
to apply greater pressure or intensity to force someone to do something or to achieve a result
increase pressure or intensity

Examples

The coach decided to turn up the heat on the players after their poor performance.
If negotiations stall, management may turn up the heat with stricter deadlines.
During the interview, the reporter turned up the heat by asking tough questions.

Synset Examples

The Democrats turned up the heat on their candidate to concede the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step up the pressure - to increase the amount of pressure being applied
