to recloak oneself

1. VERB

/ riˈkloʊk wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / riːˈkləʊk wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to hide or disguise one's emotions or appearance again, especially after having briefly revealed them
hide or disguise emotions or appearance again

Examples

After briefly showing her vulnerability, she quickly recloaked herself in confidence.
The actor had to recloak himself in anonymity after the scandal.
During negotiations, he would sometimes recloak himself in indifference to hide his true position.

Synset Examples

She managed to recloak her true feelings during the meeting.
He decided to recloak himself after the surprise party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloak oneself - to disguise or conceal one's emotions or appearance

Etymology

From 're-' (again) + 'cloak' (to cover or conceal) + 'oneself' (reflexive pronoun).
