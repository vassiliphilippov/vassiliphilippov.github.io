to have a fit

1. VERB

/ hæv ə fɪt / (AmE), / hæv ə fɪt / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or agitated, often expressing this emotion strongly
suddenly become very angry

Examples

When he saw the mess in the kitchen, he began to have a fit right away.
Don’t have a fit; we’ll fix the problem as soon as possible.
If I come home late again, my parents will probably have a fit.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly and strongly express anger
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not get angry
