to lodge in

1. VERB

/ lɑdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / lɒdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to live or stay in a particular place, often temporarily
live or stay in a place

Examples

During the conference, I will lodge in a small hotel near the city center.
They used to lodge in a cottage by the lake every summer.
Several students lodge in the dormitory during the school year.

Synset Examples

She resides in Princeton
he occupies two rooms on the top floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reside in - to live permanently or for a long period in a place
  • Synonym: to occupy - to live in or use a place
