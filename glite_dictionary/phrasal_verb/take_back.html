to take back

1. VERB

/ teɪk bæk / (AmE), / teɪk bæk / (BrE)
to make someone remember or think about a time in the past
make remember the past

Examples

Listening to that song always takes back fond memories of my childhood.
Seeing her old school took back memories of her early years.
The scent of lavender instantly takes me back to summers spent at my grandmother’s house.

Synset Examples

This photo takes me back to the good old days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remind - to cause someone to remember something
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk bæk / (AmE), / teɪk bak / (BrE)
to return someone or something to the place where they started or came from
return to the starting point

Examples

Can you take back these books to the library for me?
After the hike, the bus will take back all the students to the school.
I offered to take back the borrowed equipment to its original owner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to go or bring back to a place or person
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02082541-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ teɪk bæk / (AmE), / teɪk bak / (BrE)
to admit that something you said was wrong and say you no longer believe it
retract a statement

Examples

I shouldn't have said that, and I take back my words.
She had to take back her accusation after realizing she was mistaken.
He refused to take back what he had said, even after learning the truth.

Synset Examples

He swallowed his words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retract - formally withdraw a statement or accusation

4. VERB

/ teɪk ˈbæk / (AmE), / teɪk ˈbæk / (BrE)
to resume a relationship with someone after it was ended or interrupted, especially by allowing them to return
resume a relationship after an interruption

Examples

After a long conversation, she decided to take back her ex-husband.
He apologized sincerely, and eventually, Sarah agreed to take back her old friend.
If you betray my trust, I may never take back our relationship.

Related Terms

5. VERB

/ teɪk bæk / (AmE), / teɪk bæk / (BrE)
to regain possession of something that was previously given, lent, or lost
regain possession of something

Examples

He decided to take back the books he had lent to his friend.
The company will take back old electronics for recycling.
After the misunderstanding, she wanted to take back her gift.

Related Terms
