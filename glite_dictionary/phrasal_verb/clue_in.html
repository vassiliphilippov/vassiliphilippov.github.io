to clue in

1. VERB

/ kluː ɪn / (AmE), / kluː ɪn / (BrE)
to inform someone about a situation, especially something they were previously unaware of
inform or update someone

Examples

Can you clue in the new team member about how things work here?
I wasn't clued in to the plan until the last minute.
She clued me in on what had happened while I was on vacation.

Synset Examples

Can you clue me in?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information to someone
  • Synonym: brief - to give essential information or instructions to someone

Etymology

From 'clue' (meaning a piece of evidence or information), extended to phrasal use in the 20th century.
