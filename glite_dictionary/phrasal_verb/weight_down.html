to weight down

1. VERB

/ weɪt daʊn / (AmE), / weɪt daʊn / (BrE)
To make someone or something heavy or burdened, especially by adding a physical load.
make heavy or burdened

Examples

The backpack was so full that it began to weight down her shoulders.
Heavy responsibilities can sometimes weight down even the most optimistic people.
The wet snow weighted down the branches until some of them snapped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load heavily or oppress
  • Antonym: to lighten - to make lighter or less heavy
