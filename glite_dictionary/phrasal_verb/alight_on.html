to alight on

1. VERB

/ əˈlaɪt ɑn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪt ɒn / (BrE)
(of a bird, insect, or object) to come down and settle or land lightly on a surface.
to land or settle on something

Examples

A butterfly alighted on the windowsill, basking in the morning sun.
She watched as a bird alighted on the branch just outside her window.
Tiny snowflakes alighted on his coat as he stepped outside.

Synset Examples

He watched the fireflies alight on the garden flowers.
The sun began to alight on the horizon, signaling dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land on - to come down onto a surface after moving through the air
  • Antonym: to take off - to leave the ground and begin flying

Etymology

From Middle English 'alighten', from Old English 'onlihtan' (to descend).
