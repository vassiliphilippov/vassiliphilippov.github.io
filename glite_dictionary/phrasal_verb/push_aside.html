to push aside

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / pʊʃ əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to refuse to consider or pay attention to someone or something; to dismiss from one's thoughts or consideration
dismiss or ignore someone or something

Examples

She tried to push aside her doubts and focus on the task.
He pushed aside the issue, preferring not to talk about it anymore.
The manager quickly pushed aside any objections to the new policy.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to refuse to consider something
  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00802592-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / pʊʃ əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to move someone or something out of the way by pushing
move something out of the way by pushing

Examples

She had to push aside the heavy curtains to let in the sunlight.
The new manager was determined not to push aside experienced staff.
He pushed aside his doubts and accepted the offer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shove aside - to move something out of the way with a shove
  • Antonym: pull aside - to move something out of the way by pulling
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
