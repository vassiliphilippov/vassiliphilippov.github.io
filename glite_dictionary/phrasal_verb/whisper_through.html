to whisper through

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪspɚ θru / (AmE), / ˈwɪspə θruː / (BrE)
(of wind, sounds, etc.) to move through something making a soft, quiet, murmuring noise.
move softly making a quiet sound

Examples

The wind began to whisper through the tall grass at dusk.
A soft breeze seemed to whisper through the open window, bringing the scent of rain.
As the orchestra quieted, a faint melody whispered through the auditorium.

Synset Examples

The wind seemed to whisper through the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rustle - make a soft, muffled crackling sound, like leaves or paper
