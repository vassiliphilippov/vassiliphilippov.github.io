to take the chair

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə tʃɛr / (AmE), / teɪk ðə tʃɛə / (BrE)
to assume the role of leader or presiding officer in a meeting or assembly
assume leadership of a meeting
british

Examples

After the lunch break, it was Dr. Smith's turn to take the chair and guide the discussion.
Would anyone like to take the chair for the next session of the conference?
She was honored when the committee asked her to take the chair during the annual meeting.

Synset Examples

It's your turn to take the chair at the meeting.
Please take the chair if you want to address the group.
He was asked to take the chair and lead the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chair - the person presiding over a meeting
