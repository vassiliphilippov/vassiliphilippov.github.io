to rest on

1. VERB

/ rɛst ɑn / (AmE), / rɛst ɒn / (BrE)
to be based on or dependent upon something, especially as a foundation for an idea, theory, or claim
be based on; depend on (something)

Examples

The validity of his argument rests on several questionable assumptions.
Much of Western philosophy rests on the ideas of ancient Greek thinkers.
Our conclusions rest on the data collected during the experiment.

Synset Examples

what is this new evidence built on?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depend on - to rely on or be determined by something
  • Synonym: base on - to use as a foundation for something

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛst ɑn / (AmE), / ˈrɛst ɒn / (BrE)
to depend on or use something or someone for support
depend on for support

Examples

You can rest on my shoulder if you need to take a break.
The argument rests on the assumption that everyone has equal access to resources.
His success rested on years of hard work and dedication.

Synset Examples

you can lean on me if you get tired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lean on - to rely on or use someone or something for support

3. VERB

/ rɛst ɑn / (AmE), / rɛst ɒn / (BrE)
to support part of your body or an object by putting it on something else
to support oneself or something on an object

Examples

She rested on the bench for a moment before continuing her walk.
The painting rested on a narrow shelf above the fireplace.
He rested on his elbows, gazing out of the window in silence.

Synset Examples
