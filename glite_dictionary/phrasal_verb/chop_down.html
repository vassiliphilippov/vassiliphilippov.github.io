to chop down

1. VERB

/ tʃɑp daʊn / (AmE), / tʃɒp daʊn / (BrE)
to cut through the trunk or base of something, especially a tree, so that it falls
cut down (a tree, etc.)

Examples

They decided to chop down the old oak tree in the backyard.
The workers chopped down dozens of trees to make room for the new road.
It took him all morning to chop down just one large pine.

Synset Examples

George chopped down the cherry tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fell - to cut down a tree

2. VERB

/ tʃɑp daʊn / (AmE), / tʃɒp daʊn / (BrE)
to cut through and bring to the ground, especially referring to trees or other plants
cut through and fell (a tree or plant)

Examples

They plan to chop down the old oak tree next week.
Farmers sometimes chop down wild bushes to clear land for crops.
Because the tree was diseased, it had to be chopped down.

Synset Examples

The axeman arrived to chop down the old trees.
He had to chop down the weeds in the garden.
The city decided to chop down the dead trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fell - to cut down (a tree)
