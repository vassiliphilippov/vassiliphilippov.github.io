to chime in

1. VERB

/ tʃaɪm ɪn / (AmE), / tʃaɪm ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt or join a conversation, especially by adding a comment or opinion
interrupt or join a conversation

Examples

If you have something to add, feel free to chime in at any time during the meeting.
She tends to chime in whenever someone is telling a story, often with her own opinions.
He waited for the right moment to chime in with his thoughts on the project.

Synset Examples

her husband always chimes in, even when he is not involved in the conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to break the continuity of a conversation or action

Etymology

Figurative extension of 'chime' (to make a ringing sound), with the sense of joining in harmoniously, from Middle English and Old French 'chimer'.

2. VERB

/ tʃaɪm ɪn / (AmE), / tʃaɪm ɪn / (BrE)
to join a conversation, especially by adding a comment or opinion that contributes to what is being discussed
join a conversation or discussion

Examples

If you have something to add, please chime in during the meeting.
She suddenly chimed in with her own opinion on the topic.
As the team discussed the plan, Mark couldn't help but chime in.

Synset Examples

Don't hesitate to chime in with your thoughts.
He always likes to chime in during discussions.
Feel free to chime in if you have any ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interject - to interrupt with a comment
  • Synonym: to butt in - to interrupt a conversation rudely or abruptly

Etymology

Metaphorically from the idea of musical chimes adding their sound to music.
