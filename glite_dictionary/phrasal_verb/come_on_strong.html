to come on strong

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɑn strɔŋ / (AmE), / kʌm ɒn strɒŋ / (BrE)
to behave in a very forceful, assertive, or intense manner; to make a powerful impression or approach with great energy
behave assertively or intensely

Examples

During the interview, he tried to come on strong to impress the panel.
Some people prefer not to come on strong when meeting new clients.
Her feedback came on strong, but it helped us improve.

Synset Examples

He really came on strong during the presentation.
The new marketing strategy will come on strong next quarter.
She tends to come on strong when she's passionate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be pushy - to behave in an unreasonably forceful way, especially in making demands
  • Antonym: to hold back - to be less assertive or restrained in one's actions or opinions
