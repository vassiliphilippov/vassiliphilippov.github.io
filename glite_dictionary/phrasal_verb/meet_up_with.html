to meet up with

1. VERB

/ mit ʌp wɪθ / (AmE), / miːt ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to meet someone, usually by arrangement, at a particular time and place
to meet someone by arrangement

Examples

Let's meet up with Sarah after work for coffee.
He met up with some old friends at the conference last week.
Are you free to meet up with me this weekend?

Synset Examples

She met up with her former lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together in the same place, often by arrangement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02026823-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mit ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / miːt ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to come together with someone, especially by arrangement, in order to spend time together or catch up
to gather or reunite with someone

Examples

I plan to meet up with my colleagues after work for dinner.
Last weekend, she met up with her friends from college.
Are you meeting up with anyone when you visit Paris?

Synset Examples

I met up with my old mate, wazzer!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together by arrangement or chance
