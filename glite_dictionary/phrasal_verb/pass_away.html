to pass away

1. VERB

/ pæs əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pɑːs əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually disappear or cease to exist
cease to exist; disappear gradually

Examples

Old traditions often pass away as new generations take over.
She hopes the controversy will eventually pass away and be forgotten.
With time, even the strongest memories may pass away.

Synset Examples

She hoped that the problem would eventually pass away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear completely and suddenly
  • Antonym: to persist - to continue to exist; to remain active or in force
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00352230-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pæs əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pɑːs əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to die; to cease living, especially expressed in a gentle or respectful way
to die (euphemistic)

Examples

Many people gathered to honor him after he passed away last month.
Her beloved pet passed away peacefully in its sleep at the age of fifteen.
When his grandmother passed away, he inherited the family home.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist as a living being
  • Synonym: to expire - to die; to come to an end of life
  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal, idiomatic)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00359085-v
Synset POS: v
