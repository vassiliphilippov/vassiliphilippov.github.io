to step out

1. VERB

/ stɛp aʊt / (AmE), / stɛp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a room or building briefly, especially for a short walk or for a particular purpose
leave a room/building for a short time

Examples

I'm going to step out for a moment to take this call.
She stepped out onto the patio to get some fresh air.
Please let me know if you need anything while I step out for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to step in - to enter a place, especially briefly or for a short time
