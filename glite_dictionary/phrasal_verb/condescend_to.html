to condescend to

1. VERB

/ ˌkɑːn.dɪˈsɛnd tu / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɪˈsɛnd tʊ / (BrE)
to behave towards someone as if you are more important or intelligent than they are, often in a patronizing way
behave as if superior to someone

Examples

She didn't want to condescend to explaining the obvious to her friend.
Please don't condescend to me; I am capable of understanding complex issues.
He tends to condescend to his juniors, which makes him unpopular at work.

Synset Examples

Don't condescend to your colleagues; treat them as equals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat someone with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Antonym: respect - to show regard or consideration for someone as an equal

Etymology

From Latin 'condescendere' (to stoop together, yield), via Old French and Middle English.
