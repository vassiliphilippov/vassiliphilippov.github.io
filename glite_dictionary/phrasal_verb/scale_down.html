to scale down

1. VERB

/ skeɪl daʊn / (AmE), / skeɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the size, amount, or extent of something in proportion to its original size
reduce in proportion to original size

Examples

Due to budget cuts, the company had to scale down its operations.
They decided to scale down the event and invite fewer people.
The architect scaled down the model to fit it on the table.

Synset Examples

The model is scaled down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - make smaller in amount, size, or number
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00429200-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skeɪl daʊn / (AmE), / skeɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the size, amount, or extent of something; to make smaller in scale or proportion
reduce the size or scope of something

Examples

The company decided to scale down its operations in response to declining demand.
We had to scale down our plans for the house renovation due to budget constraints.
The image can be scaled down before uploading to save storage space.

Synset Examples

reduce an image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something less or smaller in amount, degree, or size
  • Antonym: to enlarge - to make something bigger in size or extent
