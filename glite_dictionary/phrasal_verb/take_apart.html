to take apart

1. VERB

/ teɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / teɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to separate something into its individual pieces or parts, usually to understand it, repair it, or move it
disassemble into parts

Examples

The mechanic had to take apart the engine to find the problem.
She carefully took apart the old radio to see how it worked.
Before moving the furniture, we need to take apart the bed frame.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to assemble - to put together the parts of something
  • Synonym: to disassemble - to take something apart into its separate pieces

3. VERB

/ teɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / teɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to examine or analyze something by dividing it into its parts or essential features
analyze by breaking into parts

Examples

The professor asked us to take apart the argument and examine each point carefully.
In her essay, she takes apart the novel’s central themes, revealing their underlying meanings.
Can you take apart this paragraph and show me its grammatical structure?

Synset Examples

analyze a specimen
analyze a sentence
analyze a chemical compound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine methodically by separating into parts
  • Antonym: to synthesize - to combine parts to form a whole
