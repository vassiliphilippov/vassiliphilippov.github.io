to switch up

1. VERB

/ swɪtʃ ʌp / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to make a temporary or minor change to something; to vary or alter for a short period
make a temporary change
informal

Examples

To keep things interesting, the teacher likes to switch up the lesson format every week.
You should switch up your workout routine to avoid getting bored or hitting a plateau.
Sometimes it's refreshing to switch up your style and try something new.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - to make or become different
  • Synonym: switch - to change from one thing to another
