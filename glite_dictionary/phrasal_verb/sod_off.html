to sod off

1. VERB

/ sɑd ɔf / (AmE), / sɒd ɒf / (BrE)
to go away or leave someone alone, often used as a rude or forceful command
tell someone to go away rudely
informal british

Examples

He was being annoying, so I told him to sod off.
His boss shouted at him to sod off and stop complaining.
The kids wouldn't stop bothering us until we finally told them to sod off.

Synset Examples

I told him to sod off when he complained.
He was rude, so I told him to sod off.
When she asked for help, I said to sod off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bugger off - go away rudely (British slang)
  • Synonym: to clear off - leave immediately (mainly British, informal)
  • Antonym: come here - move nearer (opposite direction)

Etymology

British slang (mid-20th century), possibly derived from 'sod' as a mild expletive.
