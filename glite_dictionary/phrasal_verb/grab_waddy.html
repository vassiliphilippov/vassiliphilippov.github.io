to grab waddy

1. VERB

/ ɡræb ˈwɑdi / (AmE), / ɡræb ˈwɒdi / (BrE)
to take hold of a stick or tool, especially quickly or firmly
to seize a stick or tool
informal british

Examples

Before the dog ran outside, Sam had to grab waddy to defend the flock.
Every morning, the farmer would grab waddy before checking the sheep.
In the story, the old man grabbed waddy to scare off the intruders.

Synset Examples

He grabbed his waddy and headed out to the field.

Etymology

From 'grab' (seize) and Australian English 'waddy' (a heavy wooden club, from Wiradjuri 'wadi').
