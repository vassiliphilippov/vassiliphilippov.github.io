to take a chance

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə tʃæns / (AmE), / teɪk ə tʃɑːns / (BrE)
to do something risky in the hope that it will have a good result
risk something in hope of success

Examples

Sometimes you have to take a chance if you want to achieve your dreams.
She decided to take a chance and apply for the job, even though she wasn’t sure she would get it.
They were nervous about investing, but they chose to take a chance on the new company.

Synset Examples

When you buy these stocks you are gambling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take a risk - to do something that involves danger or uncertainty
