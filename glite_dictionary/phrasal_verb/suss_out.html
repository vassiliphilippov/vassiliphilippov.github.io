to suss out

1. VERB

/ sʌs aʊt / (AmE), / sʌs aʊt / (BrE)
to investigate or examine something in order to understand it or determine its quality, accuracy, or condition
investigate or figure out something
informal british

Examples

The mechanic will suss out the problem before suggesting any repairs.
It took us a while to suss out what was really happening in the company.
She quickly sussed out that the deal was too good to be true.

Synset Examples

check the brakes
Check out the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to figure out - to understand or find the solution to something
  • Synonym: to check out - to examine or inspect something carefully

Etymology

Probably from British slang 'suss', shortened from 'suspect' or possibly 'suspicion'.

2. VERB

/ sʌs aʊt / (AmE), / sʌs aʊt / (BrE)
to investigate or figure out something in order to understand it clearly
investigate or figure out
informal british

Examples

Before signing the contract, make sure you suss out all the terms and conditions.
It took him weeks to suss out what was really going on at the office.
Can you suss out whether this restaurant has any vegetarian options?

Synset Examples

I’ll suss out the details and let you know.
They need to suss out the facts before deciding.
I need to suss out the best route to the party.
He tried to suss out her true intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to figure out - to understand or solve something
  • Synonym: to investigate - to examine something carefully to discover the truth

Etymology

Probably from British slang 'sus' (suspect or suspicion), itself from 'suspicion'.
