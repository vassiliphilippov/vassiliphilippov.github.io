to come near

1. VERB

/ kʌm nɪr / (AmE), / kʌm nɪə / (BrE)
to move closer in distance or time to someone or something
move closer in distance or time

Examples

As the storm clouds came near, the sky grew darker and the wind picked up.
Don’t be afraid—the dog won’t bite if you come near.
The train comes near the station at exactly noon each day.

Synset Examples

We were approaching our destination
They are drawing near
The enemy army came nearer and nearer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - move closer to someone or something
  • Antonym: retreat - move away from someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02057865-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm nɪr / (AmE), / kʌm nɪə / (BrE)
To approach or draw close in time; to be about to happen.
approach in time

Examples

As the deadline comes near, everyone in the office works overtime to finish the project.
Winter came near, bringing shorter days and colder nights.
Her graduation day is coming near, and she's getting excited for the ceremony.

Synset Examples

Winter is approaching
approaching old age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to come closer in time, amount, quality, or number
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01853713-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kʌm nɪr / (AmE), / kʌm nɪə / (BrE)
to almost do or experience something; to be close to doing something without actually doing it
almost do or experience something

Examples

She came near to tears during the emotional speech.
The ball came near to hitting the window but missed by inches.
I came near to quitting my job last year when things got tough.

Synset Examples

She came near to screaming with fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly but not completely
  • Degree Lower: barely - only just; by a very small margin
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02602368-v
Synset POS: v
