to score points

1. VERB

/ skɔr pɔɪnts / (AmE), / skɔː pɔɪnts / (BrE)
to gain approval, favor, or an advantage through actions or remarks, especially in competitions, arguments, or social interactions
gain approval or advantage through actions or words

Examples

She always tries to score points with her boss by volunteering for extra work.
Politicians often score points by criticizing their opponents in public debates.
He wasn’t really interested in helping; he just wanted to score points with the teacher.

Synset Examples

You can score points by helping others in need.
He always knows how to score points in debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: win brownie points - to gain approval or favor by doing something to please someone
