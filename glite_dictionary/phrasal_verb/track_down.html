to track down

1. VERB

/ træk daʊn / (AmE), / træk daʊn / (BrE)
to pursue and follow wild animals, especially for food or sport, until caught or killed
pursue wild animals for hunting

Examples

The hounds were trained to track down deer through the dense forest.
Hunters would often track down wild boars at dawn in these mountains.
He learned how to track down game by reading footprints in the snow.

Synset Examples

Goering often hunted wild boars in Poland
The dogs are running deer
The Duke hunted in these woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunt - to chase and kill wild animals for food or sport
  • Synonym: chase - to pursue in order to catch

Etymology

From the noun 'track' (trail, footprint) and the preposition 'down', originally meaning to follow a trail.

2. VERB

/ træk daʊn / (AmE), / træk daʊn / (BrE)
to search for and find someone or something after some effort
find after searching

Examples

The police worked tirelessly to track down the criminal responsible for the theft.
It took him weeks to track down the original owner of the painting.
She managed to track down her childhood friend online after many years.

Synset Examples

Can you help me track down that missing report?
He spent hours trying to track down the rare book.
The cybercop tracked down the hacker in no time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to locate - to find something or someone by searching
  • Antonym: to lose - to be unable to find something or someone
