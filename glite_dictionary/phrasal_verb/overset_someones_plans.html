to overset someone's plans

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈsɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz plænz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz plænz / (BrE)
to disrupt or disturb someone's plans, causing them to fail or change unexpectedly
disturb or ruin someone's plans

Examples

The rain completely overset our plans for a picnic in the park.
His last-minute decision to resign overset their plans for the event.
She worried that the new policy might overset his plans for expansion.

Synset Examples

The unexpected news seemed to overset her plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upset - to cause plans or arrangements to fail; to disturb

Etymology

From 'overset' (meaning to overturn or upset) + 'plans'.
