to stand with

1. VERB

/ stænd wɪð / (AmE), / stænd wɪð / (BrE)
to support or align oneself with a person, group, or cause, especially during a difficult time
support or align with someone

Examples

Many citizens decided to stand with the protesters during the demonstration.
I will always stand with my friends, no matter what happens.
Several organizations are standing with the community in their time of need.

Synset Examples

She chose to stand with the victims of injustice.
We need to stand with each other in this fight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or approval to someone or something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to disagree with or resist someone or something
