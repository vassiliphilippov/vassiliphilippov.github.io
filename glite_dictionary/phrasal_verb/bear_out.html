to bear out

1. VERB

/ bɛr aʊt / (AmE), / beə aʊt / (BrE)
to support or confirm something by providing evidence or authority
support or confirm with evidence

Examples

The results of the experiment bear out his theory.
Recent studies have borne out her claim that exercise improves mental health.
If the facts bear out your story, I will believe you.

Synset Examples

The stories and claims were born out by the evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confirm - to make sure that something is true or correct
  • Antonym: to refute - to prove that something is not true
