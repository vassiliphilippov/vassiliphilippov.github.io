to strike work

1. VERB

/ straɪk wɝːk / (AmE), / straɪk wɜːk / (BrE)
to stop working as a form of protest, especially to demand better conditions or pay
stop working to protest

Examples

The union members voted to strike work if their demands were not met.
Thousands of teachers struck work during the nationwide protest.
When they decided to strike work, production at the factory came to a halt.

Synset Examples

Many employees decided to strike work for better conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to refuse to work as a form of protest
