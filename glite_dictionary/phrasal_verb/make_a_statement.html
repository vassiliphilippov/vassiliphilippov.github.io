to make a statement

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
to express an opinion, message, or attitude publicly or in a way that attracts attention, often to communicate one's values or beliefs
express an opinion publicly or conspicuously

Examples

The designer's new collection intends to make a statement about environmental responsibility.
She used vivid colors in her painting to make a statement about hope and resilience.
Wearing all black to the meeting was his way to make a statement without saying a word.

Synset Examples

The artwork was created to make a statement about climate change.
Her bold outfit was meant to make a statement.
He wore a t-shirt to make a statement about his beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an action or statement expressing disapproval or objection
  • Antonym: to blend in - to not stand out; to avoid attracting attention
