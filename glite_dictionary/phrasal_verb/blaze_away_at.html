to blaze away at

1. VERB

/ bleɪz əˈweɪ æt / (AmE), / bleɪz əˈweɪ æt / (BrE)
to work on something with intense energy or effort, often for a sustained period
work with great energy or effort

Examples

They blazed away at their assignments until late at night.
The workers blazed away at the repairs, finishing ahead of schedule.
Despite her fatigue, she blazed away at the project with remarkable determination.

Synset Examples

They blazed away at the tasks until they were all done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plug away at - to work steadily and persistently at something
  • Antonym: to slack off - to work with less effort or to become less active
