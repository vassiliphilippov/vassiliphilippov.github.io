to join forces

1. VERB

/ dʒɔɪn ˈfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / dʒɔɪn ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
to come together with another person or group in order to work towards a shared purpose or undertake a common project
work together with others

Examples

Several non-profit organizations decided to join forces to address homelessness in the city.
The two companies will join forces to release a new line of eco-friendly products.
Scientists from different countries are joining forces to develop a vaccine.

Synset Examples

The soprano and the pianist did not get together very well
We joined forces with another research group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collaborate - work jointly on an activity or project
  • Synonym: to cooperate - act jointly; work toward the same end

2. VERB

/ dʒɔɪn ˈfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / dʒɔɪn ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
to work together with someone or something in order to achieve a shared goal
to collaborate for a shared goal

Examples

The two charities decided to join forces to help the homeless in the city.
Rival political parties agreed to join forces to fight corruption.
As threats grew, the villages began joining forces for their mutual protection.

Synset Examples

If we join forces, we can tackle this challenge together.
The two companies decided to join forces for the project.
