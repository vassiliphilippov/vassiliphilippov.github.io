to leech onto

1. VERB

/ litʃ ˈɑntu / (AmE), / liːtʃ ˈɒntu / (BrE)
to attach oneself to someone in order to gain benefit or out of excessive admiration, often following them persistently and dependently
cling to someone for benefit or admiration

Examples

After his big win, dozens of people tried to leech onto him for fame and fortune.
Some fans tend to leech onto celebrities, hoping to be noticed or rewarded.
She warned her son not to let fake friends leech onto his success.

Synset Examples

the groupies leeched onto the rock star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cling to - to hold onto someone or something tightly
  • Degree Higher: to idolize - to admire or revere excessively

Etymology

From 'leech' (the bloodsucking worm), used figuratively since the 19th century to describe people who cling to and feed off others.
