to fade away

1. VERB

/ feɪd əˈweɪ / (AmE), / feɪd əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually become weaker or less distinct until disappearing completely
gradually disappear or diminish

Examples

As the music played, the melody slowly faded away into silence.
The pain in his shoulder eventually faded away after a few days of rest.
Her memory of those years has gradually faded away over time.

Synset Examples

The sound faded out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible; to stop existing
  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely

2. VERB

/ feɪd əˈweɪ / (AmE), / feɪd əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually disappear or become less distinct until no longer present or noticeable
gradually disappear or diminish

Examples

As the music played softly in the background, her worries began to fade away.
The old graffiti on the wall has slowly faded away over the years.
His voice started to fade away as he walked down the long hallway.

Synset Examples

The tradition of yelling 'gardyloo' has mostly faded away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or present
