to butt out

1. VERB

/ bʌt aʊt / (AmE), / bʌt aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) To stop involving oneself in someone else's affairs; to stop interfering or intruding.
stop interfering; withdraw from involvement
informal

Examples

He finally agreed to butt out after I asked for privacy several times.
If you don't butt out, you'll just make things worse between them.
She wished her parents would butt out of her personal life.

Synset Examples

Please butt out; this is a private conversation.
It's best to just butt out and let them handle it.
You need to butt out of my business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mind your own business - tell someone not to interfere
  • Antonym: to interfere - to involve oneself in something without invitation
