to rough up

1. VERB

/ rʌf ʌp / (AmE), / rʌf ʌp / (BrE)
to attack or treat (someone) violently, usually causing minor injuries or intimidation
treat violently; attack mildly

Examples

The gang threatened to rough up anyone who talked to the police.
He claimed the guards tried to rough up the prisoners after the escape attempt.
The journalist was roughed up while covering the protest.

Synset Examples

The police strong-armed the suspect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - to make a physical attack on someone
  • Synonym: to beat up - to attack someone and hit them repeatedly
