to jump into

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to start or become involved in something quickly and with enthusiasm
begin or engage in something energetically

Examples

She didn't hesitate to jump into the conversation when she had something to say.
Many investors are ready to jump into the market when prices drop.
Before you jump into a new job, make sure you know what's involved.

Synset Examples

She decided to jump into the new project.
Don't hesitate to jump into the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunge into - to start doing something suddenly and energetically
