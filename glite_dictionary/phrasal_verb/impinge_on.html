to impinge on

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɪndʒ ɑn / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪndʒ ɒn / (BrE)
to hit or come into sudden contact with something
to hit or come into contact with

Examples

The meteor impinged on the planet's surface, leaving a large crater.
The tennis ball impinged on the net before dropping onto the court.
Microscopic particles impinge on the sensor, triggering an alarm.

Synset Examples

The car hit a tree
He struck the table with his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
  • Synonym: to collide with - to come into forceful contact with

Etymology

From Latin 'impingere' (to push against), from 'in-' (in, into) + 'pungere' (to prick).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɪndʒ ɑn / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪndʒ ɒn / (BrE)
to have an effect on, especially a negative one; to limit or restrict something
affect or limit negatively

Examples

The new noise restrictions may impinge on our ability to host outdoor events.
Personal issues shouldn't impinge on your professional responsibilities.
The company's expansion plans could impinge on protected wildlife areas.

Synset Examples

The new regulations will impinge on our operations.
These changes will impinge on the project's timeline.
Her schedule may impinge on our meeting time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affect - to have an influence on something
  • Synonym: encroach on - to intrude on someone's territory, rights, or time

Etymology

From Latin 'impingere' (to strike against), from 'in-' (into) + 'pangere' (to fasten).
