to carry through

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri θruː / (AmE), / ˈkæri θruː / (BrE)
to help someone survive or escape a dangerous or difficult situation safely
help survive or escape safely

Examples

It was her determination that carried through the entire team after the accident.
The support of his friends carried him through a very rough period in his life.
Rescue workers carried through everyone trapped in the burning building.

Synset Examples

We pulled through most of the victims of the bomb attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull through - to survive or recover after a difficult or dangerous situation

2. VERB

/ ˈkæɹi θɹu / (AmE), / ˈkæɹi θɹuː / (BrE)
to complete something successfully or put something into effect, especially when it is difficult
complete or put into effect

Examples

Despite many obstacles, she managed to carry through her ambitious plan.
The government struggled to carry through the promised reforms within the year.
His determination helped them carry through the project to completion.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry out - to perform or complete a task, an order, etc.
  • Synonym: to execute - to put into effect or carry out a plan, order, or course of action

/ ˈkæri θruː / (AmE), / ˈkæri θruː / (BrE)
to complete or accomplish something, especially a plan, project, or promise, successfully
to complete or accomplish successfully

Examples

The new manager inspired the team to carry through their ambitious goals.
It takes determination to carry through with your promises.
