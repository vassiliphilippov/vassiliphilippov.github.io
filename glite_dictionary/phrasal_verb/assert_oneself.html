to assert oneself

1. VERB

/ əˈsɝt wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / əˈsɜːt wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to behave or speak confidently and forcefully so that people notice you, respect your views, or acknowledge your presence
speak or act with self-confidence

Examples

In meetings, she often finds it hard to assert herself among more outspoken colleagues.
It can be challenging to assert oneself in a new environment.
He slowly learned to assert himself and express his opinions at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up for oneself - defend one's own interests or opinions
  • Antonym: repress oneself - to restrain oneself from expressing ideas or emotions
