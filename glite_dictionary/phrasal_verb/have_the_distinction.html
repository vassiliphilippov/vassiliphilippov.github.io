to have the distinction

1. VERB

/ hæv ðə dɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / hæv ðə dɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
to be recognized as different from others because of a notable or noteworthy quality, often used for emphasizing something unique or special (sometimes positive, sometimes neutral or negative)
be recognized for a notable quality

Examples

Her novel has the distinction of winning three major literary awards in one year.
This city had the distinction of being the coldest place in the country last winter.
The museum has the distinction of housing the largest dinosaur skeleton ever discovered.

Synset Examples

it has the distinction of being the cheapest restaurant in town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be easily seen or noticed; prominent
