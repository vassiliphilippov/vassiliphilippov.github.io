to slip away

1. VERB

/ slɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / slɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly and secretly, so as not to be noticed
leave quietly and secretly

Examples

She tried to slip away from the party before anyone noticed her absence.
During the meeting, he managed to slip away for a quick phone call.
Several guests began to slip away as the evening wore on.

Synset Examples

The lecture was boring and many students slipped out when the instructor turned towards the blackboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sneak out - to leave a place secretly and quietly to avoid being noticed
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ slɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / slɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of time) to pass gradually or unnoticed.
(of time) pass gradually or unnoticed

Examples

The days seemed to slip away as we waited for news.
Summer has slipped away before we realized it.
He watched the hours slip away while working on his project.

3. VERB

/ slɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / slɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave quietly and unnoticed, or to gradually disappear or be lost
leave quietly or gradually disappear

Examples

She watched her dreams slowly slip away as challenges mounted.
During the commotion, he managed to slip away without anyone noticing.
The patient's strength seemed to slip away day by day.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
