to beat in

1. VERB

/ bit ɪn / (AmE), / biːt ɪn / (BrE)
to train someone by persistent drilling or repetition, typically to instil knowledge, discipline, or habits
teach by drills or repetition

Examples

The coach tried to beat in the rules of the game through endless practice sessions.
Their teacher would beat in the basics of grammar until everyone understood.
Discipline was beaten in to the recruits during their training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum in - to teach something by persistent repetition
  • Synonym: to inculcate - to instil an idea by persistent instruction
