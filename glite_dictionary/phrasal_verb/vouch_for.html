to vouch for

1. VERB

/ vaʊtʃ fɔr / (AmE), / vaʊtʃ fə / (BrE)
To give assurance or confirm the reliability, truth, or good character of someone or something.
guarantee reliability or character

Examples

I can vouch for his experience and dedication to the job.
She refused to vouch for the accuracy of the report without reading it first.
Can you vouch for everyone who was at the meeting last night?

Synset Examples

Can you vouch for the quality of this product?
I can vouch for her honesty and integrity.
He asked me to vouch for his character during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attest - to affirm the truth or accuracy of something
  • Synonym: guarantee - to provide assurance of something's reliability or truth

Etymology

From Old French 'vocher' (to call, invoke), ultimately from Latin 'vocare' (to call).
