to bugger off

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌɡər ˈɔf / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡə ˈɒf / (BrE)
(informal, British) used to tell someone to go away immediately, often in a rude or annoyed way.
tell someone rudely to go away
informal vulgar british

Examples

He told me to bugger off when I asked him to pay his share.
If you don't like it here, just bugger off and stop complaining.
She shouted at the reporters to bugger off and let her be.

Synset Examples

Scram!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go away - to leave a place
  • Synonym: to scram - to leave quickly, especially when told to do so

Etymology

From British English slang "bugger" (an impolite term for a person) plus phrasal verb construction.
