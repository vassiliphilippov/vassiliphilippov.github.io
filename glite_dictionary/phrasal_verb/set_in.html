to set in

1. VERB

/ sɛt ɪn / (AmE), / sɛt ɪn / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant or persistent) to begin and become established or noticeable.
to begin and become established

Examples

As soon as the rain set in, the streets quickly emptied.
Panic began to set in as the deadline approached.
Winter usually sets in around November in this region.

Synset Examples

winter has set in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start or commence
  • Antonym: end - to finish or come to a close

/ sɛt ɪn / (AmE), / sɛt ɪn / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant or undesirable) to begin and become likely to continue, often gradually or inevitably.

Examples

When the reality of her new job set in, she started to feel nervous.
