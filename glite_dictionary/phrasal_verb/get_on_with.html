to get on with

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn wɪð / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn wɪð / (BrE)
to have a friendly or harmonious relationship with someone
have a friendly relationship

Examples

Karen and her sister get on with each other despite their differences.
He doesn't get on with his new colleagues at work.
It is important to get on with people in a team environment.

Synset Examples

My boss and I get along very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get along with - to have a harmonious relationship with someone
  • Antonym: fall out with - to stop being friendly with someone after an argument
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02463998-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn wɪð / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn wɪð / (BrE)
to continue doing something, especially after an interruption, or to make progress with a task
continue with a task or activity

Examples

It’s time to get on with your homework instead of procrastinating all evening.
After the meeting ended, everyone tried to get on with their work as usual.
Let’s get on with the discussion before we run out of time.

Synset Examples

Let’s get on with you and finalize these plans.
I’m excited to get on with you and start this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry on - to continue especially after interruption
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop doing something before finishing it
Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
