to wipe away

1. VERB

/ waɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / waɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove something by wiping, especially tears, dirt, or marks from a surface
remove by wiping

Examples

She gently wiped away the tears from his cheeks.
The caretaker wiped away the dust on the old piano.
After spilling her drink, she quickly wiped away the liquid from the counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - remove all traces of something
  • Antonym: smudge - to make a dirty mark by rubbing
