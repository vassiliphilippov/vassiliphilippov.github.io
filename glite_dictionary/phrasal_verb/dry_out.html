to dry out

1. VERB

/ draɪ aʊt / (AmE), / draɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to become dry or drier, especially after being wet or damp
become dry or drier

Examples

If you leave the paint open, it will dry out and become unusable.
After the heavy rain, the ground finally started to dry out in the afternoon sun.
Be sure to store the cheese properly so it doesn't dry out.

Synset Examples

The laundry dries in the sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry - to remove moisture from; become free from moisture

2. VERB

/ draɪ aʊt / (AmE), / draɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove the moisture from something and make it completely dry
remove all moisture; make fully dry

Examples

After the rain, we hung our jackets outside to dry out in the sun.
It's important to dry out your tent before putting it away to prevent mildew.
The towels didn't dry out properly and remained damp.

4. VERB

/ draɪ aʊt / (AmE), / draɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to become dry or to make something dry by removing moisture, especially after being wet
become dry; remove moisture

Examples

If you leave the bread uncovered, it will dry out and become stale.
After the storm, it took several days for the fields to dry out.
She hung her tent in the sun to dry out before packing it away.
