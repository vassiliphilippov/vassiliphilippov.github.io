to make it up to

1. VERB

/ meɪk ɪt ʌp tu / (AmE), / meɪk ɪt ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
to do something good for someone in order to show that you are sorry for something you have done or failed to do
compensate for a mistake or wrongdoing

Examples

I know I hurt your feelings, but I promise I'll make it up to you.
After forgetting our anniversary, he tried to make it up to me with flowers and dinner.
She offered to babysit my kids to make it up to me for being late.

Synset Examples

He promised to make it up to her after the argument.
I’ll make it up to you for missing your birthday.
She wanted to make it up to him with a surprise dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compensate - to make amends for loss or damage
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense
