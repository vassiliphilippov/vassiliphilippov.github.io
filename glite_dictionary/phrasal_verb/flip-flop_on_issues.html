to flip-flop on issues

1. VERB

/ ˈflɪp flɑp ɑn ˈɪʃuːz / (AmE), / ˈflɪp flɒp ɒn ˈɪʃuːz / (BrE)
(of a person or organization) to change their opinion, policy, or position on important matters frequently, often causing confusion or a lack of trust.
change position on issues frequently

Examples

The senator was criticized for his flip-flop on issues during the campaign.
Voters were frustrated by the candidate's repeated flip-flop on issues.
She promised not to flip-flop on issues, but changed her views several times.

Synset Examples

The politician's flip-flop on issues confused voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waffle - to speak or write evasively or indecisively
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to maintain one's position without changing it

Etymology

From 'flip-flop' meaning to move back and forth, originally imitating the sound of a sandal.
