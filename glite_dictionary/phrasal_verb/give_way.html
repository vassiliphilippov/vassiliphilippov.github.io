to give way

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv weɪ / (BrE)
to move aside or be replaced by something or someone else; to make space for someone or something
move aside or be replaced

Examples

The old building will give way to a modern shopping center next year.
The crowd gave way as the parade approached.
Tradition sometimes has to give way to innovation in fast-changing industries.

Synset Examples

The park gave way to a supermarket
‘Move over,’ he told the crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make way - to move aside to allow progress
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv weɪ / (BrE)
to collapse, yield, or cease to resist under pressure or force
collapse or yield under pressure

Examples

The old bridge finally gave way after years of neglect and heavy rain.
He pushed against the locked door until it gave way beneath his weight.
Their determination never gave way, even in the face of overwhelming opposition.

Synset Examples

The door yielded to repeated blows with a battering ram

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to give in or surrender under pressure
  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the action of something; to oppose force
