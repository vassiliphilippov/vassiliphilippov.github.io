to face a headwind

1. VERB

/ feɪs ə ˈhɛdˌwɪnd / (AmE), / feɪs ə ˈhɛdˌwɪnd / (BrE)
to experience obstacles or resistance that make progress more difficult
encounter difficulties or resistance

Examples

The company will face a headwind as it enters a competitive market.
Despite their best efforts, the team continued to face a headwind from regulatory changes.
Many startups face a headwind when raising capital during economic downturns.

Synset Examples

The project faced a headwind due to budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcome obstacles - to succeed in dealing with difficulties
  • Antonym: face a tailwind - to experience favorable conditions that aid progress
