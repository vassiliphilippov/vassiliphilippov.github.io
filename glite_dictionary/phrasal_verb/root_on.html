to root on

1. VERB

/ rut ɑn / (AmE), / ruːt ɒn / (BrE)
to encourage or support someone, especially by shouting or cheering
encourage or support vocally

Examples

We all went to the basketball game to root on our friends.
Her parents love to root on her at dance competitions.
The fans were loudly rooting on their team during the final match.

Synset Examples

The crowd cheered the demonstrating strikers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer on - to encourage someone by shouting or cheering
  • Antonym: boo - to show disapproval by shouting or making noise
