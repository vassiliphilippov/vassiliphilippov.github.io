to spew out

1. VERB

/ spjuˈ aʊt / (AmE), / spjuˈ aʊt / (BrE)
to eject or send out something forcefully or in large quantities, especially suddenly and often in a way that is difficult to control; can be used both literally and figuratively.
eject or emit in large quantities

Examples

The factory chimneys spew out thick black smoke into the sky every morning.
After eating too quickly, he felt sick and almost spewed out his lunch.
During the meeting, she would often spew out statistics to support her argument.

Synset Examples

the volcano spews out molten rocks every day
The editors of the paper spew out hostile articles about the Presidential candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emit - to send out something such as light, heat, sound, or energy
  • Synonym: discharge - to send out a substance, especially gas or liquid, in large amounts
  • Antonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up a substance

Etymology

From Middle English 'spewen', from Old English 'spīowan', of Germanic origin, related to Old High German 'spiwan' (to spit).
