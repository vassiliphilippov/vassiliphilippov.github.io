to poke out

1. VERB

/ poʊk aʊt / (AmE), / pəʊk aʊt / (BrE)
to extend, stick, or project outward from a surface or space
extend or project outward

Examples

A branch began to poke out between the fence boards.
Her hat brim poked out just beyond the edge of the table.
Several wires poked out from the hole in the wall.

Synset Examples

The awning extends several feet over the sidewalk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stick out - to extend or protrude outward from something
  • Synonym: to jut out - to extend or project sharply outward
