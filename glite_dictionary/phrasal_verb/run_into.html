to run into

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈɪntu / (AmE), / rʌn ˈɪntə / (BrE)
to collide violently with something, usually by accident
collide with something by accident

Examples

He was so distracted that he ran into the wall.
The cyclist didn't see the parked car and ran into it.
If you run into anything while driving, stop and check for damage.

Synset Examples

I ran into the telephone pole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash into - to collide forcefully with something

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn ˈɪntu / (AmE), / rʌn ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to hit or collide with something unexpectedly or suddenly while moving
hit or collide with (phrasal verb)

Examples

The cyclist didn't see the wall and ran into it.
If you run into any obstacles on the way, let me know.
She accidentally ran into a parked car while reversing.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ ɹʌn ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ɹʌn ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to meet someone by chance, especially unexpectedly
meet by chance

Examples

I didn't expect to run into my old college roommate at the airport yesterday.
You never know whom you might run into at a big conference like this.
She was surprised to run into her former teacher while shopping downtown.
