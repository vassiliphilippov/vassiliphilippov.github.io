to dismiss as

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈmɪst æz / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪst æz / (BrE)
to regard or treat someone or something as having a particular negative quality, often without giving due consideration
regard or treat as (often negatively)

Examples

Many critics dismissed as unoriginal the artist's latest work.
Her concerns were quickly dismissed as exaggerations by the committee.
He dismissed as nonsense the rumors spreading online.

Synset Examples

They dismissed him as a midwit at the conference.
He dismissed the tabloid as unreliable and sensational.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - refuse to accept or consider
  • Antonym: accept as - regard or treat as acceptable or valid
