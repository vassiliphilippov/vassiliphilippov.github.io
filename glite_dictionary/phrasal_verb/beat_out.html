to beat out

1. VERB

/ biːt aʊt / (AmE), / biːt aʊt / (BrE)
to defeat someone or something in a competition, race, or conflict, often by being slightly better or faster
defeat in a competition

Examples

Our company managed to beat out several rivals for the contract.
He beat out dozens of applicants to get the job.
The underdog team beat out their competitors in a thrilling final match.

Synset Examples

Agassi beat Becker in the tennis championship
We beat the competition
Harvard defeated Yale in the last football game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a contest or battle
  • Antonym: lose to - to be defeated by someone in a competition or contest
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01104324-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ biːt aʊt / (AmE), / biːt aʊt / (BrE)
to produce a rhythm or sound by striking repeatedly, especially with a hand or instrument
produce a rhythm by striking repeatedly

Examples

The drummer beat out a steady rhythm that energized the crowd.
She beat out the melody on the tabletop with her fingers.
Marchers beat out a marching tune on their drums as they paraded through the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tap out - to make a sound or rhythm by tapping repeatedly
