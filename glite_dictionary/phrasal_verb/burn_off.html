to burn off

1. VERB

/ bɝːn ɔf / (AmE), / bɜːn ɒf / (BrE)
to use up energy or calories, especially through physical activity
use up energy or calories

Examples

She jogged every morning to burn off extra calories before breakfast.
After eating a big meal, he decided to go for a walk to burn off some energy.
The children need to burn off their excitement by playing outside.

Synset Examples

burn off calories through vigorous exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expend - to use or spend energy or resources
  • Antonym: conserve - to prevent the loss or waste of something such as energy

2. VERB

/ bɝn ɔf / (AmE), / bɜːn ɒf / (BrE)
to clear land of its vegetation by setting fire to it
clear vegetation from land by burning

Examples

Farmers often burn off the old crops to prepare the fields for planting.
The land was burned off to remove invasive plants before reforestation could begin.
After the forest was burnt off, the soil became more fertile for new growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - remove unwanted growth, objects, or obstacles
