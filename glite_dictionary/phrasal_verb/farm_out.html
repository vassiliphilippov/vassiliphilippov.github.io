to farm out

1. VERB

/ fɑrm aʊt / (AmE), / fɑːm aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange for someone else to do work or provide a service, especially for payment, or to lease out something for temporary use for a fee.
contract out work or lease for a fee

Examples

The company decided to farm out customer support to an agency overseas.
They often farm out specialized tasks to freelancers.
Our team has been farmed out to various departments during the busy season.

Synset Examples

We rent out our apartment to tourists every year
He hired himself out as a cook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rent out - to allow someone to use something in return for payment
  • Synonym: to hire out - to allow someone to use something, or to be employed, for a fee

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'farm' meaning 'to lease', with origins in Medieval Latin 'firma' (fixed payment).

2. VERB

/ fɑrm aʊt / (AmE), / fɑːm aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange for work or tasks to be done by other people, especially by contracting them to other organizations or individuals
contract work to others

Examples

The company decided to farm out its customer service operations to an overseas agency.
We often farm out major projects to specialized contractors for efficiency.
Instead of handling the entire process in-house, they farmed out parts of the job to freelancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier; synonym for farm out
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
