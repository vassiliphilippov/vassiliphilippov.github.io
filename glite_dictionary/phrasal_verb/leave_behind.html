to leave behind

1. VERB

/ liv bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / liːv bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to not take, alter, or interfere with something or someone; to leave something unchanged or undisturbed
not take or disturb; leave unchanged

Examples

Please leave behind any wildflowers you find in the park.
Sometimes it's best to leave behind old traditions that still have value.
The park ranger asked the visitors to leave behind any animal they encounter.

Synset Examples

leave it as is
leave the young fawn alone
leave the flowers that you see in the park behind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to leave alone - to refrain from disturbing or interfering with someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00136799-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ liv bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / liːv bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to depart from a place without taking someone or something with you
depart without taking something or someone

Examples

He decided to leave behind his old clothes when moving out.
Sometimes we have to leave behind painful memories to move forward.
They left behind a note for their friends explaining their absence.

Synset Examples

He left behind all his possessions when he moved to Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something completely and for good
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02085825-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ liv bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / liːv bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person) to be survived by (people, possessions, etc.) after one's death.
be survived by after death

Examples

She sadly left behind her beloved children and a devoted husband.
The late professor left behind a vast collection of rare books and manuscripts.
When he died unexpectedly, he left behind many friends who cherished his kindness.

Synset Examples

He left six children
At her death, she left behind her husband and 11 cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to survive - continue to live after someone else's death
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360745-v

4. VERB

/ liv bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / liːv bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to abandon something or someone, or to not take them with you when you go
to abandon or not take along

Examples

He decided to leave behind all his belongings and start a new life.
When you move to college, you may have to leave behind some childhood friends.
They quickly packed up, worried they would leave behind something important.

Synset Examples
