to cover up

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌvər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə ʌp / (BrE)
to hide the truth about something or prevent people from discovering it; to conceal from view or knowledge
hide or conceal the truth or something from view

Examples

The company tried to cover up the incident, but the press discovered the truth.
He wore a long scarf to cover up the scar on his neck.
Officials attempted to cover up evidence of their involvement in the scandal.

Synset Examples

The President covered the fact that he bugged the offices in the White House

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide or keep secret; not reveal
  • Antonym: to expose - to reveal the truth about something hidden

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌvɚ ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə ˌʌp / (BrE)
to hide or conceal something so that it cannot be seen or discovered
hide or conceal something

Examples

The company tried to cover up the mistake to avoid bad publicity.
He quickly put on long sleeves to cover up his injuries.
They attempted to cover up the truth, but the facts eventually came out.

Synset Examples

He wore a jacket to cover up his tattoo.
She used makeup to cover up her blemishes.
They tried to cover up the incident from the press.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • Antonym: to expose - to make something visible or reveal something hidden
