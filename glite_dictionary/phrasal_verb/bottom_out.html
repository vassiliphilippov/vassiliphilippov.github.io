to bottom out

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːtəm aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm aʊt / (BrE)
(of a vehicle) to reach such a low point that the underside touches the ground or another surface.
(vehicle) to hit the ground underneath

Examples

The old sedan bottomed out as it drove over the speed bump.
If you drive too fast over the dip, your car might bottom out.
After hitting the pothole, the truck bottomed out and scraped its chassis.

Synset Examples

the car bottomed out where the driveway meets the road

Etymology

From the noun 'bottom' (lowest part) + 'out', relating to reaching or touching the lowest point.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɑːtəm aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm aʊt / (BrE)
to reach the lowest or worst point before beginning to improve or rise again
reach lowest point before improving

Examples

Many experts believe the economy will bottom out before the end of the year.
Shares finally bottomed out after months of decline and began to recover.
His mood bottomed out in late winter but soon improved with the arrival of spring.

Synset Examples

Prices bottomed out and started to rise again after a while

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: plateau - to reach and stay at a steady level

3. VERB

/ ˈbɑtəm aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm aʊt / (BrE)
(of prices, values, activity, etc.) to reach the lowest or worst point before starting to improve.
reach the lowest point

Examples

After months of declining sales, the company finally bottomed out in March.
Experts predict that unemployment will bottom out in the second quarter of this year.
Housing prices continued to fall, but many analysts believe they will soon bottom out.

Synset Examples

Prices might bottom out this winter.
The stock is expected to bottom out soon.
After months of decline, the market finally bottomed out.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peak - to reach the highest point (opposite meaning)
