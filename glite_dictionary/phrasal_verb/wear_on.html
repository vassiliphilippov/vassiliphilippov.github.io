to wear on

1. VERB

/ wɛr ɑn / (AmE), / wɛə ɒn / (BrE)
(of time) to pass slowly or gradually, often with an implied sense of tediousness or slowness.
(of time) pass slowly or gradually

Examples

As the afternoon wore on, everyone grew increasingly restless.
Her anxiety increased as the hours wore on with no news.
The meeting seemed to wear on forever, never quite reaching a conclusion.

Synset Examples
