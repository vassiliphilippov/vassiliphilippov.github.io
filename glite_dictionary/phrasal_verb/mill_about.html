to mill about

1. VERB

/ mɪl əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / mɪl əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to move around with no clear direction, often in a confused or aimless manner, especially in a group
move aimlessly or confusedly

Examples

Shoppers began to mill about the mall as they waited for the doors to open.
After the meeting ended, people milled about the lobby, chatting and exchanging business cards.
Students were milling about in the hallway before class started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to move about without a fixed course or purpose
  • Antonym: to stand still - to not move or remain in one place
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɪl əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / mɪl əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to move around a place with no clear purpose or direction, usually in a group
move aimlessly in a group

Examples

As the concert ended, people began to mill about the exits, talking and laughing.
Shoppers milled about the market, browsing the stalls without really buying anything.
The students milled about the hallway, waiting for their next class to start.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - stand or wait around idly without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to hang around - spend time aimlessly in a place, often with no specific purpose

Etymology

Based on the movement of people likened to the turning of a mill.
