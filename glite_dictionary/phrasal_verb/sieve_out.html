to sieve out

1. VERB

/ sɪv aʊt / (AmE), / sɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or separate something by passing it through a sieve or by selecting and discarding unwanted items
separate or remove using a sieve

Examples

After washing the rice, she sieved out any remaining husks.
The scientist sieved out the larger particles from the solution.
You must carefully sieve out the impurities to produce pure sugar.

Synset Examples

The customer picked over the selection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to filter out - remove unwanted parts by passing through something
