to take time off

1. VERB

/ teɪk taɪm ɔf / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm ɒf / (BrE)
to stop working for a period of time, especially to rest or recover
temporarily stop working

Examples

Sarah decided to take time off after completing the big project.
He plans to take time off next month to travel with his family.
Sometimes you need to take time off to focus on your mental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - official permission to be absent from work or duty
  • Antonym: work - to be engaged in physical or mental activity for employment

2. VERB

/ teɪk taɪm ɑf / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm ɒf / (BrE)
to spend a period away from work or other duties for rest, personal matters, or other reasons
be absent from work for a period

Examples

After working nonstop for months, he decided to take time off and travel abroad.
She needs to take time off from work to care for her sick mother.
Many employees take time off during the summer to go on vacation.

Synset Examples

He needs to take time off to recharge.
I decided to take time off for my mental health.
She plans to take time off during the holidays.
Many young adults take time off to find themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - a period of absence from work for a specific reason
