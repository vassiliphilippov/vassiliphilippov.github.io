to go around

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move around the side of an opposing army, especially to avoid direct confrontation or to attack from the flank
move around an army's flank

Examples

The general ordered his troops to go around the enemy's left flank during the battle.
Instead of attacking head-on, they chose to go around and surprise the opponent from behind.
The cavalry managed to go around the main force and cut off their supply lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outflank - to move around the side of an enemy, especially to gain an advantage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02089693-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with something, especially an unpleasant or difficult task or rule, by finding another way
avoid dealing with something by finding another way

Examples

Some companies try to go around strict regulations by exploiting loopholes.
She always finds a way to go around the most difficult assignments at work.
Is there any way we can go around the lengthy approval process?

Synset Examples

You cannot bypass these rules!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass - avoid or go past something, especially an obstacle or rule
  • Synonym: to circumvent - find a way around an obstacle or rule
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move or turn in a circle around a point, axis, or center
move or turn circularly around a center

Examples

The ceiling fan goes around all day to keep the room cool.
When the Ferris wheel goes around, you can see the whole city from the top.
The planets go around the Sun in elliptical orbits.

Synset Examples

The Earth revolves around the Sun
The lamb roast rotates on a spit over the fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rotate - turn in a circle around an axis or center
  • Synonym: to revolve - move in a circular orbit around a central point
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to be enough for everyone in a group to have a share; to be sufficient for all concerned
be sufficient for everyone

Examples

5. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of information, a story, etc.) to become widely known and passed from person to person.
to be widely known or spread (of information)

Examples

There's a rumor going around that the company will be sold.
The story quickly went around the whole school by lunch time.
False information tends to go around on social media very fast.

(of something) to be enough for everyone in a group, or to circulate among people.
