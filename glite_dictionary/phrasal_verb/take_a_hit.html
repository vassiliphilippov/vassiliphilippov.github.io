to take a hit

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə hɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ə hɪt / (BrE)
to inhale smoke or vapor, especially from a cigarette, joint, or inhaling device
inhale smoke or vapor
informal

Examples

After lighting the cigar, he decided to take a hit and leaned back in his chair.
She passed the vape pen around, and everyone in the group took a hit.
Before answering, he takes a hit from his cigarette and exhales slowly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhale - to breathe in (air, gas, smoke, etc.)

2. VERB

/ teɪk ə hɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ə hɪt / (BrE)
to experience a loss, damage, or setback, especially in reputation, finances, or performance
suffer a loss or setback

Examples

After the new regulations were announced, the entire industry took a hit in the stock market.
His confidence took a hit when he failed the exam on his first try.
Several local restaurants took a hit during the economic downturn.

Synset Examples

The company took a hit after the scandal broke.
He took a hit to his reputation after the mistake.
She took a nervous hit before stepping on stage.
Investors took a hit when the stock prices fell.
Moscow Mitch's reputation took a hit last election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffer a setback - to experience a reversal or defeat
  • Antonym: to bounce back - to recover from a setback
