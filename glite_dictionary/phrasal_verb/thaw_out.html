to thaw out

1. VERB

/ θɔː aʊt / (AmE), / θɔː aʊt / (BrE)
(of food or other substances) to become unfrozen or to make something become unfrozen, usually by being exposed to warmth.
become or make unfrozen; defrost

Examples

Let the fish thaw out in the fridge overnight before you cook it.
The windshield finally thawed out after I turned on the car’s heater.
I forgot to thaw out the beef for dinner, so we had to eat something else.

Synset Examples

Leave the chicken out to thaw before cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defrost - to remove frost or ice from, especially by warming
