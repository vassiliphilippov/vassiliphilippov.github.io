to supervene upon

1. VERB

/ suː.pɚˈviːn əˌpɒn / (AmE), / suː.pəˈviːn əˌpɒn / (BrE)
(formal) To take the place of something or someone; to replace or succeed in a position or role.
to take the place of; to replace

Examples

Technological advances may supervene upon traditional methods, making them obsolete.
When the vice president supervened upon the president's duties, policies began to change rapidly.
In time, new customs supervened upon the old ones, altering the culture drastically.

Synset Examples

Smith replaced Miller as CEO after Miller left
the computer has supplanted the slide rule
Mary replaced Susan as the team's captain and the highest-ranked player in the school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replace - to take the place of something or someone
  • Synonym: to supplant - to take the place of something or someone, typically by force

Etymology

From Latin 'supervenire' (to come over, to happen unexpectedly), combined with 'upon'.
