to move up

1. VERB

/ muːv ʌp / (AmE), / muːv ʌp / (BrE)
to go or rise to a higher position or level
rise to a higher position

Examples

The manager asked us to move up a few steps so everyone could fit in the photo.
Prices are expected to move up as demand increases during the holiday season.
The elevator will move up to the top floor without stopping.

Synset Examples

The fog lifted
The smoke arose from the forest fire
The mist uprose from the meadows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ascend - to go up or climb
  • Antonym: to descend - to go or move downward
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01972576-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ muːv ʌp / (AmE), / muːv ʌp / (BrE)
to advance to a better job, position, or status in life
advance to a better job or position

Examples

She worked hard to move up within the company and eventually became a manager.
Many people hope to move up in their careers by gaining more experience and education.
After years at the entry-level, he finally moved up to a senior position.

Synset Examples

She ascended from a life of poverty to one of great renown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - to move forward in position or progress
  • Synonym: to get ahead - to be successful in one's career or life
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with progress
