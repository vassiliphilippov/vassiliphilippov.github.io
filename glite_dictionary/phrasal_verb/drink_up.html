to drink up

1. VERB

/ drɪŋk ʌp / (AmE), / drɪŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to drink all of something until there is nothing left
drink something completely

Examples

She told everyone to drink up before the bar closed.
He quickly drank up his coffee and headed to work.
Make sure you drink up; we don't want to waste any of this juice.

Synset Examples

drink up — there's more wine coming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - to bring something to an end or completion
  • Degree Lower: sip - to drink slowly in small amounts
  • Degree Higher: to gulp down - to swallow a drink quickly and often in large amounts
