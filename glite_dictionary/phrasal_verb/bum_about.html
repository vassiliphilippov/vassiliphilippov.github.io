to bum about

1. VERB

/ bʌm əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / bʌm əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to spend time lazily or without doing anything productive; to idle aimlessly
to be lazy or idle
informal slang british

Examples

I spent most of the summer just bumming about and watching TV.
After graduation, he decided to bum about Europe for a few months before starting work.
They bummed about the city, stopping at cafes and enjoying the sights.

Synset Examples

Her son is just bumming around all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laze around - to spend time in a relaxed, lazy way
  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing; avoiding work

Etymology

From informal 'bum' (to loaf, idle), of uncertain origin.
