to wreathe with

1. VERB

/ rið wɪð / (AmE), / riːð wɪð / (BrE)
to adorn or encircle something by placing or covering it with a circular arrangement of material (such as flowers or leaves)
adorn by encircling with something

Examples

The bride chose to wreathe with roses for her wedding hairstyle.
They wreathed with garlands, the old monument looked festive during the holiday.
Each doorway was wreathed with evergreen branches in preparation for the festival.

Synset Examples

They chose to wreathe the statue with flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - make something more attractive by adding ornamentation
  • Synonym: to encircle - surround in the form of a circle or ring

Etymology

From 'wreathe' (to twist or coil), ultimately from Old English 'wrīthan' (to twist), combined with 'with'.
