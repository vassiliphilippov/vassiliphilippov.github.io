to breathe in

1. VERB

/ brið ɪn / (AmE), / briːð ɪn / (BrE)
to draw air or another substance into the lungs through the nose or mouth
to inhale air or substance

Examples

She stepped outside to breathe in the crisp morning air.
Remember to breathe in slowly during your meditation practice.
He paused to breathe in the scent of blooming flowers.

Synset Examples

Inhale deeply
inhale the fresh mountain air
The patient has trouble inspiring
The lung cancer patient cannot inspire air very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhale - to breathe air or gas into the lungs
  • Antonym: to breathe out - to expel air from the lungs
