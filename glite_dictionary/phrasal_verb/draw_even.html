to draw even

1. VERB

/ drɔ ˈivən / (AmE), / drɔː ˈiːvən / (BrE)
to reach a state of equality with another, especially in terms of score, rank, or progress
reach equality in score or status

Examples

After a late goal, our team was able to draw even with the champions.
They hope to draw even by halftime despite the tough competition.
If we win this round, we’ll finally draw even in the series.

Synset Examples

After winning the last game, we finally draw even.
Let’s work together to draw even before the finals.
They managed to draw even with their rivals this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch up - to reach the same level or position as someone else
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to become less successful than others or have a lower score
