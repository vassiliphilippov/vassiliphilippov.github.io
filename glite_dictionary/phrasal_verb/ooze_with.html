to ooze with

1. VERB

/ uz wɪð / (AmE), / uːz wɪð / (BrE)
to slowly emit or be filled with a substance, quality, or feeling, as if seeping out
slowly emit or be filled with something

Examples

The cheese sandwich began to ooze with melted cheddar as soon as it was cut.
Her voice seemed to ooze with confidence during the interview.
The wound started to ooze with blood after the bandage was removed.

Synset Examples

The chocolate cake will ooze with melted fudge inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material
  • Synonym: to exude - to discharge slowly and steadily
