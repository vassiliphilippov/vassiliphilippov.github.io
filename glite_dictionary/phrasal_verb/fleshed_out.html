to fleshed out

1. VERB

/ flɛʃt aʊt / (AmE), / flɛʃt aʊt / (BrE)
given more substance or detail; developed and made more complete.
developed or made more detailed

Examples

The director fleshed out the character's backstory to make the role more believable.
Her proposal was only an outline until she fleshed out the details with research and statistics.
As the project neared completion, the design team fleshed out every aspect of the plan.

Synset Examples

did not spring full-clad from his imagination
a plan fleshed out with statistics and details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborated - made more detailed or complicated
  • Antonym: sketched - described in brief or general terms (provides an antonymic contrast in detail)

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'flesh out', originating from the idea of adding flesh to a skeleton to create a complete form.
