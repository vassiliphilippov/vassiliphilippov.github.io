to wind up

1. VERB

/ waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
to coil the spring of a mechanical device by turning a knob or stem, especially in order to make it operate
to coil a spring by turning a stem

Examples

I always wind up my old alarm clock before going to bed.
You need to wind up the toy car to make it move.
He forgot to wind up his watch, so it stopped overnight.

Synset Examples

wind your watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rewind - to wind again, especially in the opposite direction or to reset to the start

2. VERB

/ waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
to finally arrive in a particular situation, place, or state, especially despite initial intentions or plans
finally arrive in a situation

Examples

We didn't plan to stay so late, but we wound up talking for hours.
If you keep taking risks, you might wind up in trouble.
He studied engineering but wound up working as a chef.

  • Synonym: to end up - to reach or come to a particular situation at the end

3. VERB

/ waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
(in baseball, cricket, etc.) to move the throwing arm in a circular motion before pitching or throwing the ball.
prepare to throw by swinging the arm

Examples

The pitcher began to wind up before delivering the fastball.
You must wind up properly to get enough power behind the throw.
She carefully wound up and released a perfect pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch - to throw (a baseball or similar ball) toward a batter

/ waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
