to peddle crafts

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛdəl kræfts / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəl krɑːfts / (BrE)
to sell handmade or small-scale goods, typically in a personal or informal way, often by moving from place to place or using online platforms
sell handmade goods informally

Examples

Many artists peddle crafts at local markets on weekends.
She used social media to peddle crafts to a wider audience.
After retiring, he began to peddle crafts from his home workshop.

Synset Examples

She decided to peddle handmade crafts online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sell - to give something in exchange for money
  • Antonym: buy - to acquire by paying money

Etymology

From 'peddle' (to sell goods from place to place), itself from the noun 'peddler', with 'crafts' referring to handmade items.
