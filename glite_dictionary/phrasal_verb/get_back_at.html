to get back at

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈbæk æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈbak at / (BrE)
to do something in order to punish or get revenge on someone who has harmed you
retaliate or take revenge

Examples

She finally found a way to get back at her noisy neighbors.
After being betrayed, he vowed to get back at those responsible.
They plotted to get back at the team that cheated them out of victory.

Synset Examples

They found a way to get back at the bully.
He plotted to get back at his rival.
She wanted to get back at him for the prank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retaliate - to make an attack or assault in return for a similar attack
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense or mistake
