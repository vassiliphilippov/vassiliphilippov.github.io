to rip off

1. VERB

/ rɪp ɔf / (AmE), / rɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling or tearing it away violently and forcefully
remove by violent tearing

Examples

The impatient driver ripped off the side mirror when squeezing through the narrow street.
She ripped off the wrapping paper from her birthday present in excitement.
The old poster was so stuck that I had to rip off a few layers of paint with it.

Synset Examples

The passing bus tore off her side mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tear off - to remove by tearing, often suddenly or violently
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00179012-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪp ɔf / (AmE), / rɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to steal something, especially by taking it quickly or without the owner's knowledge
steal; take without consent
informal

Examples

Someone tried to rip off my bag while I was shopping in the market.
The thieves ripped off several laptops from the office last night.
He was caught ripping off expensive electronics from the store.

Synset Examples

Someone stole my wallet on the train
This author stole entire paragraphs from my dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take another person's property without permission
  • Synonym: to pilfer - to steal, especially small amounts or things of little value
  • Antonym: return - to give back something that was taken
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02326737-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪp ɔf / (AmE), / rɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to cheat someone, especially by charging too much money or by deceiving them in a transaction
deceive or cheat someone, especially financially
informal

Examples

That electronics store always tries to rip off unsuspecting tourists with high prices.
We realized too late that the tickets were fake and we'd been ripped off.
Don't let that contractor rip off your elderly parents by charging for unnecessary repairs.

Synset Examples

The con-man beat me out of $50
This salesman ripped us off!
we were cheated by their clever-sounding scheme
They chiseled me out of my money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: scam - deceive someone to get money, especially through fraud
  • Synonym: to defraud - illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: overcharge - charge someone too high a price for goods or a service
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02579332-v
Synset POS: v
