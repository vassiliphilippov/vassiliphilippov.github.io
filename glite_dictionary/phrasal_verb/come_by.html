to come by

1. VERB

/ kʌm baɪ / (AmE), / kʌm baɪ / (BrE)
to obtain or acquire something, especially by accident or with difficulty
obtain or acquire (esp. by chance or with effort)

Examples

Good jobs are hard to come by these days.
She was lucky to come by such rare coins at the market.
If you come by any extra tickets, please let me know.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acquire - to get or gain something
  • Synonym: to obtain - to get something, especially by effort
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02210796-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm baɪ / (AmE), / kʌm baɪ / (BrE)
to visit someone informally and often without prior arrangement
visit informally, without planning

Examples

Why don't you come by after work for a quick chat?
She often comes by her friend's place without calling ahead.
We're always happy when neighbors come by unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

We frequently drop by the neighbors' house for a cup of coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drop by - visit informally and spontaneously
