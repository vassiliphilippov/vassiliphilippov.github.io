to stand back

1. VERB

/ stænd bæk / (AmE), / stænd bæk / (BrE)
to move or remain at a distance from something or someone, often for safety or observation
move or stay away from something or someone

Examples

Please stand back from the edge of the platform for your safety.
The firefighters told the crowd to stand back while they worked.
He stood back to get a better view of the painting.

Synset Examples

He stood back to look at her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approach - to move closer to something or someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01549415-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stænd ˈbæk / (AmE), / stænd ˈbak / (BrE)
to move away or keep a distance from something, especially for safety or to avoid involvement
move away or keep a distance

Examples

Please stand back while the train is approaching the platform.
The police asked the crowd to stand back from the burning building.
If you can't help, it's best to stand back and let the experts handle it.

Synset Examples

Keep your hands off my wife!
Keep your distance from this man — he is dangerous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand away - to move to a distance; withdraw
  • Antonym: approach - to move closer to something
