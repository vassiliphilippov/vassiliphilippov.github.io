to liquidate investment

1. VERB

/ ˌlɪkwɪˌdeɪt ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪˌdeɪt ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
to convert an investment or assets into cash, typically by selling them
turn investments/assets into cash

Examples

Many retirees choose to liquidate investment accounts as they transition into retirement.
If the market falls sharply, you may need to liquidate investment holdings quickly to preserve capital.
He had to liquidate investment assets to pay for his children's college tuition.

Synset Examples

He had to liquidate his investments during the crisis.
The company decided to liquidate its assets quickly.
They plan to liquidate the inventory by year-end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cash in - to convert an investment or asset into cash
  • Antonym: to invest - to put money into something to make a profit
