to fall away

1. VERB

/ fɔl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to decrease or decline in quality, standard, or intensity
decline in standard or quality

Examples

Support for the policy began to fall away after the scandal.
Her enthusiasm has fallen away over the past few months.
If quality starts to fall away, customers will notice.

Synset Examples

My grades are slipping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become worse in quality or value
  • Antonym: to improve - to become or make better
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00204753-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɔl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of something) to decrease in amount, size, or intensity; to become less than before.
diminish in amount or intensity

Examples

Attendance at the club meetings began to fall away after the holidays.
Her enthusiasm never seemed to fall away, even in difficult times.
As the storm passed, the winds gradually fell away to a gentle breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to become or make less
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size or amount
