to lick into shape

1. VERB

/ lɪk ˈɪntu ˌʃeɪp / (AmE), / lɪk ˈɪntə ˌʃeɪp / (BrE)
to improve or refine something, especially by persistent effort, until it is in an acceptable or desired condition
make something acceptable by improving it

Examples

The new manager was hired to lick into shape the struggling department.
It took several attempts to lick into shape the final version of her thesis.
They worked all weekend to lick into shape the script for the school play.

Synset Examples

The coach helped the team lick their skills into shape.
He needed to lick the project into shape before the deadline.
With some practice, she managed to lick her performance into shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - to make something better or more refined
  • Degree Higher: perfect - to make something completely free from faults
  • Degree Lower: to rough up - to treat or work at something only superficially or quickly

Etymology

Refers to the old belief that mother animals literally licked their newborns into proper form; now used figuratively.
