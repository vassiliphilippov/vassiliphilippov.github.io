to run out of town

1. VERB

/ rʌn aʊt əv taʊn / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt ɒv taʊn / (BrE)
to leave a place quickly, often because of pressure, disgrace, or to escape trouble
leave a place quickly, often in disgrace

Examples

After the scandal, the mayor was run out of town by angry residents.
We decided to run out of town for a couple of days to relax by the lake.
He had to run out of town before anyone noticed his mistake.

Synset Examples

The thief was told to run out of town quickly.
They decided to run out of town for the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially in order to start a journey
  • Antonym: return - to come or go back to a place
