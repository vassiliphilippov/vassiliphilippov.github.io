to holler out

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑlər aʊt / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) to shout something loudly, especially to attract attention or call out to someone.
shout something loudly
informal

Examples

He hollered out for help when he saw the fire.
She hollered out the answer before anyone else could respond.
The coach hollered out instructions to the players across the field.

Synset Examples

He hollered out to surrender our weapons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shout out - to say something loudly
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very quietly
