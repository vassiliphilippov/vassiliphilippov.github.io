to wolf down

1. VERB

/ wʊlf daʊn / (AmE), / wʊlf daʊn / (BrE)
to eat something very quickly and eagerly, as if very hungry
eat quickly and eagerly

Examples

After skipping breakfast, Alan wolfed down his lunch the moment he got home.
She barely paused to breathe as she wolfed down her slice of cake.
We were so hungry after the hike that we wolfed down the entire pizza in minutes.

Synset Examples

The teenager wolfed down the pizza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gobble up - to eat something quickly and greedily
  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something eagerly and in large amounts

Etymology

From the noun 'wolf', referencing the animal's reputation for voracious eating.

2. VERB

/ wʊlf daʊn / (AmE), / wʊlf daʊn / (BrE)
To eat food very quickly and greedily, often because of hunger or impatience.
eat quickly and greedily

Examples

He was so hungry he wolfed down his dinner in five minutes.
The children wolf down their breakfast before running outside to play.
She barely took a breath as she wolfed down her sandwich between meetings.

Synset Examples

I watched him wolf down that burger in seconds.
He managed to wolf down three slices of cake.
After the hike, they wolfed down their packed lunch.
She was so hungry that she wolfed down the pasta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gobble - to eat something quickly and noisily
  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something hungrily and quickly

Etymology

From the verb 'wolf,' used figuratively to suggest devouring food as a wolf would.
