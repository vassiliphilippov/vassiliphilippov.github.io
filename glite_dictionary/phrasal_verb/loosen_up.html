to loosen up

1. VERB

/ ˈluːsən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ʌp / (BrE)
to become less tense or stressed; to relax, either mentally or physically
become more relaxed or less tense

Examples

After a long week at work, I just want to loosen up and watch a movie.
She found it hard to loosen up in front of the camera at first.
The coach told the players to loosen up before the big game.

Synset Examples

He relaxed in the hot tub
Let's all relax after a hard day's work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to become or make less tense or anxious

4. VERB

/ ˈluːsən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ʌp / (BrE)
to make one's body more relaxed or limber by stretching, especially before exercise or strenuous activity
become physically relaxed or limber

Examples

Make sure you loosen up your muscles to avoid injury during the workout.
She always loosens up by jogging lightly around the track for a few minutes.
