to impulse-buy

1. VERB

/ ˈɪmpʌls ˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪmpʌls ˌbaɪ / (BrE)
to purchase something spontaneously and without prior planning or careful consideration
buy something on impulse

Examples

She couldn't resist and decided to impulse-buy the dress she saw in the window.
Many shoppers tend to impulse-buy snacks while waiting at the checkout counter.
He regretted his decision to impulse-buy the latest smartphone without comparing prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splurge - spend money freely or extravagantly
