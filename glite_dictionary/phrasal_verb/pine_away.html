to pine away

1. VERB

/ paɪn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / paɪn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually lose strength, health, or vitality, especially due to grief or longing
gradually weaken from grief or longing

Examples

After her husband died, she just pined away for months, unable to regain her spirit.
Many prisoners pined away from homesickness during their long captivity.
The elderly dog pined away after his owner moved out, refusing to eat.

Synset Examples

After her husband died, she just pined away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to languish - to lose or lack vitality; grow weak or feeble

Etymology

From 'pine' (to waste away) + 'away'; 'pine' from Old English 'pīnian' (to suffer) and Latin 'poena' (punishment).

2. VERB

/ paɪn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / paɪn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to become weak or ill, or to suffer mentally, because of long-term sadness or longing for someone or something
to waste away from sadness or longing

Examples

After her best friend moved away, she began to pine away in her empty house.
He refused to pine away over lost opportunities and focused on the future instead.
Year after year, the old dog seemed to pine away waiting for its owner to return.

Synset Examples

Don't pine away; get out and meet new people.
She seemed to pine away during the long winter.
He watched her pine away after the breakup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to languish - to lose or lack vitality; to grow weak or feeble, often due to longing or neglect

Etymology

From 'pine' (to yearn), Old English 'pīnian' (to torment), influenced by 'pain'.
