to rule the school

1. VERB

/ ruːl ðə skuːl / (AmE), / ruːl ðə skuːl / (BrE)
to have authority or control over a school and its activities
have authority/control over a school
informal

Examples

As headteacher, Mrs. Grant really rules the school with fairness and kindness.
The twins act like they rule the school, ordering everyone around in the cafeteria.
When the coach retired, nobody knew who would rule the school sports teams next.

Synset Examples

The student council president really knows how to rule the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in charge - to have control or authority over something or someone
  • Synonym: to run the show - to be in charge of an activity, event, or organization
