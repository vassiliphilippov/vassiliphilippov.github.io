to chug down

1. VERB

/ tʃʌɡ daʊn / (AmE), / tʃʌɡ daʊn / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or machine) to move slowly and steadily, typically making a regular sound as it goes.
move slowly and steadily (vehicle, machine)

Examples

The old steamboat chugged down the river, leaving a trail of smoke behind it.
We stood by the highway watching the ancient truck chug down the road.
At dawn, the tourist train chugs down the mountain pass with a gentle, rhythmic clatter.

Synset Examples

They watched the train chug down the tracks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chug along - move slowly and with a steady rhythm
  • Antonym: speed down - move quickly along a path
