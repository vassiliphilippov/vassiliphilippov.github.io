to hole up

1. VERB

/ hoʊl ʌp / (AmE), / həʊl ʌp / (BrE)
(Of an animal) to take shelter in a safe place and spend the winter dormant or hibernating.
hibernate or shelter for winter

Examples

Bears often hole up in caves or dens during the cold winter months.
Many small creatures hole up underground to survive the harsh weather.
The hedgehogs will hole up for the winter as temperatures continue to drop.

Synset Examples

Bears must eat a lot of food before they hibernate in their caves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hibernate - to spend the winter in a dormant condition

Etymology

From 'hole' meaning a hiding place, and 'up' as an adverb; originally American English, early 19th century.

2. VERB

/ hoʊl ʌp / (AmE), / həʊl ʌp / (BrE)
to stay in a place and avoid leaving it, especially to hide or escape from something
stay hidden or secluded

Examples

After the robbery, the thieves decided to hole up in an abandoned warehouse.
During the snowstorm, we had to hole up at a roadside motel.
She likes to hole up in her cabin and write all weekend.

Synset Examples

He is writing his book and is holing up in his study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hide out - to stay somewhere secretly, often to avoid trouble

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'hole' (a hiding place for animals) and the verb 'up'; first used in the U.S. in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
