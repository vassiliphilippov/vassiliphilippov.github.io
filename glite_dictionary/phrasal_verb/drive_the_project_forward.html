to drive the project forward

1. VERB

/ draɪv ðə ˈprɑdʒɛkt ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / draɪv ðə ˈprɒdʒɛkt ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to actively promote, advance, or make progress with a project, especially through strong initiative or leadership
actively promote or advance a project

Examples

The new manager was brought in to drive the project forward and meet tight deadlines.
We need some fresh ideas to drive the project forward after months of slow progress.
Her determination and leadership skills helped drive the project forward despite several setbacks.

Synset Examples

She’s the dynamo driving the project forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - move forward, especially in progress or development
  • Antonym: to hold back - prevent progress or delay development
