to leaven the dough

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛvən ðə doʊ / (AmE), / ˈliːvən ðə dəʊ / (BrE)
to cause dough to rise and become light by adding yeast or another leavening agent
make dough rise with yeast

Examples

Before baking, it is important to leaven the dough to achieve a light texture.
She forgot to leaven the dough, so her bread turned out dense and flat.
Yeast is commonly used to leaven the dough in traditional bread recipes.

Synset Examples

We add yeast to leaven the dough before baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferment - to undergo or cause fermentation

Etymology

From Old French *levain* (leavening agent), from Latin *levamen* (alleviation, leaven), related to *levare* (to lift).
