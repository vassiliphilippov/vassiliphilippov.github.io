to make decisions

1. VERB

/ meɪk dɪˈsɪʒənz / (AmE), / meɪk dɪˈsɪʒənz / (BrE)
to choose between possible courses of action; to come to a conclusion or judgment after considering options
to choose or conclude between options

Examples

Leaders often need to make decisions quickly in times of crisis.
It's not easy to make decisions when the facts are unclear.
She struggled to make decisions about her future career path.

Synset Examples

It's risky to make decisions without consulting the host first.
You shouldn't make decisions when you're off your meds.
It's essential to make decisions that reflect all your life's worth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decide - to make a choice or come to a resolution
