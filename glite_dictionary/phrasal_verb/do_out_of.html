to do out of

1. VERB

/ du aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / duː aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to deprive someone of something they deserve or expect, usually by dishonest means
deprive someone of something by dishonest means
british

Examples

He was furious when he realized they had done him out of his inheritance.
She claimed the company tried to do her out of her final paycheck.
They accused the landlord of doing them out of their deposit.

Synset Examples

They tried to do me out of my commission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place
