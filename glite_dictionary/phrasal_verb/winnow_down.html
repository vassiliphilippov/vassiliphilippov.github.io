to winnow down

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪnoʊ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce a large group or list to a smaller, more select number by eliminating less suitable options
reduce a group by eliminating options

Examples

The committee will winnow down the candidates to just five for the final interview.
We managed to winnow down our vacation options to the three most affordable places.
She spent hours winnowing down the huge pile of applications to find the best ones.

Synset Examples

It's time to winnow the applicants down to three finalists.
We need to winnow out the weak proposals from the strong.
The team will winnow through the data for key insights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to narrow down - to make a list or group smaller by removing possibilities

Etymology

From the verb 'winnow' (to separate or sift), originally from Old English 'windwian', combined with 'down'.
