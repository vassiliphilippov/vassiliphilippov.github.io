to keep afloat

1. VERB

/ kip əˈfloʊt / (AmE), / kiːp əˈfləʊt / (BrE)
to manage to avoid financial failure; to remain in a stable financial position despite difficulties
remain financially stable

Examples

It was a struggle for the small business to keep afloat during the economic downturn.
She sold her car to keep afloat after losing her job.
Government loans helped many companies keep afloat during the crisis.

Synset Examples

She worked two jobs to keep her finances afloat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay afloat - avoid sinking or failing financially
