to play off

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɔf / (AmE), / pleɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to deliberately cause people or groups to compete or be in opposition to each other, often to one's own advantage
deliberately set in opposition

Examples

In negotiations, he would often play off one department against another to get what he wanted.
She tried to play off her friends to see who would help her move.
The manager was accused of playing off staff members against each other to boost productivity.

Synset Examples

let them match their best athletes against ours
pit a chess player against the Russian champion
He plays his two children off against each other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit - to set in direct opposition or competition

2. VERB

/ pleɪ ɔf / (AmE), / pleɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to use something as a basis for humor, performance, or action, especially by reacting to it creatively or cleverly
respond creatively to something in performance

Examples

The actor skillfully played off his co-star's unexpected ad-libs in the scene.
She plays off the audience's reactions to make her comedy routines more engaging.
During the interview, he cleverly played off the reporter's remarks to lighten the mood.

Synset Examples

The comedian's jokes played off the soundboard perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riff on - to speak or perform at length on a theme in a creative way
