to break it to someone

1. VERB

/ breɪk ɪt tu ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / breɪk ɪt tʊ ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to tell someone something unpleasant or unwelcome, often with care or tact
inform someone of bad news

Examples

I know it's upsetting, but I have to break it to you that your application was unsuccessful.
She found it difficult to break it to her parents that she was moving abroad.
Sometimes it's best to break it to someone gently rather than all at once.

Synset Examples

It’s hard to break it to them that the event is canceled.
Can you break it to him about the promotion?
I have to break it to her gently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: let someone know - to inform or give information to someone
  • Synonym: break the news to someone - to inform someone of important or bad news, usually gently
