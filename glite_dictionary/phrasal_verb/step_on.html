to step on

1. VERB

/ stɛp ɑn / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn / (BrE)
to treat someone badly, especially in order to gain an advantage or assert power
treat someone badly to gain advantage

Examples

His manager often steps on employees to climb the corporate ladder.
I refuse to step on others just to get a promotion.
She felt that her ideas were constantly stepped on during the meeting.

Synset Examples

This boss abuses his workers
She is always stepping on others to get ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to treat someone cruelly or violently
  • Synonym: to mistreat - to treat someone badly or unfairly
  • Antonym: respect - to treat someone with consideration and honor
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02522260-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɛp ɑn / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn / (BrE)
to place or press the foot onto something, often by accident or as part of movement
place the foot on something

Examples

Watch your feet so you don't step on the dog.
She accidentally stepped on his toes as they danced.
Be careful not to step on any sharp objects at the beach.

Synset Examples

He stepped on the hem of her long gown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tread on - to put your foot on something; especially accidentally
