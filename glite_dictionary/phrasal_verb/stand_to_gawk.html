to stand to gawk

1. VERB

/ stænd tu ɡɔk / (AmE), / stænd tə ɡɔːk / (BrE)
to stand and stare at something in amazement, often with open-mouthed curiosity or disbelief
stand and stare in amazement

Examples

A group of tourists stood to gawk at the enormous skyscraper rising above the city streets.
He stood to gawk at the celebrities walking down the red carpet.
Many children stopped and stood to gawk as the magician performed his final trick.

Synset Examples

They stood to gawk at the amazing street art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gawk - to stare openly and stupidly
