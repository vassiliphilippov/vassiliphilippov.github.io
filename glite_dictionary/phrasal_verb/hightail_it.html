to hightail it

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪˌteɪl ɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌteɪl ɪt / (BrE)
to leave or flee a place very quickly, especially in order to escape trouble or danger
leave or escape quickly
informal american

Examples

As soon as the alarm sounded, the thieves hightailed it out of the building.
If we don't hightail it, we'll miss the last train home.
She saw the angry dog and immediately hightailed it in the opposite direction.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary

Etymology

Originally from American English, likely from the image of an animal raising its tail when running away in alarm.
