to beat off

1. VERB

/ biːt ɔf / (AmE), / biːt ɒf / (BrE)
to succeed in repelling or driving away someone or something, especially when attacked or persistently bothered
repel or fend off an attacker or annoyance

Examples

The villagers managed to beat off the wild animals by making loud noises.
Despite being outnumbered, the guards beat off the attackers at the gate.
She used her coat to beat off the aggressive geese in the park.

Synset Examples

The stray dog kept bothering me, and I had to beat it off with a stick.
She managed to beat off her doubts and perform well.
She had to beat off with a stick the persistent flies.
He quickly beat off the attackers with his quick reflexes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repel - to push back or drive away, especially an attacker
  • Synonym: to fend off - to defend oneself against someone or something
  • Antonym: yield to - to give way or surrender to something or someone
