to let up

1. VERB

/ lɛt ʌp / (AmE), / lɛt ʌp / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant) to become less severe or intense; to decrease in amount or force.
become less intense or severe

Examples

The rain finally began to let up after several hours.
We waited until the traffic would let up before leaving the city.
Her headache didn't let up all day, making it difficult to concentrate.

Synset Examples

The storm abated
The rain let up after a few hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abate - become less strong or intense
  • Antonym: to intensify - become stronger or more intense

Examples
