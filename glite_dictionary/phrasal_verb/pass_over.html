to pass over

1. VERB

/ pæs ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to travel across or go over something, especially an area, surface, or boundary.
travel across; go over

Examples

The bird passed over the field as it searched for prey.
The hikers passed over the rocky terrain before reaching the lake.
A thick mist passed over the valley early in the morning.

Synset Examples

The caravan covered almost 100 miles each day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to go from one side to the other of something

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pæs ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or omit someone or something; to fail to consider.
deliberately ignore or omit

Examples

The committee decided to pass over her application despite her qualifications.
He was disappointed when they passed over his suggestion during the meeting.
If you pass over important details, you may make mistakes.

Synset Examples

He skipped a row in the text and so the sentence was incomprehensible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
  • Synonym: overlook - to fail to notice or consider something or someone
  • Antonym: to consider - to think about carefully before making a decision
