to bale out

1. VERB

/ beɪl aʊt / (AmE), / beɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to remove water from a boat by scooping and throwing it over the side
remove water from a boat

Examples

We had to bale out the dinghy before we could row it safely to shore.
As the storm worsened, everyone took turns baling out water from the boat.
She grabbed a bucket and began to bale out the leaking canoe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bail out - rescue someone from a difficult situation; also remove water from a boat

Etymology

From 'bale' (to scoop water) + 'out', originating from the noun 'bail' for a bucket or pail.
