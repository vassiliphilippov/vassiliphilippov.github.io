to resonate deeply

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛzəˌneɪt ˈdipli / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪt ˈdiːpli / (BrE)
to have a strong emotional impact or feeling of personal significance
have a strong emotional impact

Examples

The film's powerful ending resonated deeply with viewers around the world.
Her words resonated deeply with those who had faced similar struggles in their lives.
The poet's message resonated deeply, prompting many in the audience to reflect on their own experiences.

Synset Examples

The sermoner's message resonated deeply with the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to affect emotionally
  • Synonym: touch - to cause feelings of sympathy or emotion
