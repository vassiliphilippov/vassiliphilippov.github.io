to kick around

1. VERB

/ kɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to discuss an idea, suggestion, or subject informally or casually, often without reaching a decision
discuss informally or casually

Examples

The team will kick around a few ideas before making a final decision.
We kicked around the proposal over coffee yesterday afternoon.
Let's kick around some possible solutions during the meeting.

Synset Examples

We bandied around these difficult questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bandy about - to discuss or mention something frequently, often carelessly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00815313-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɪk əˈɹaʊnd / (AmE), / kɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to be alive or still active, especially after a long time or despite difficulties (informal)
remain alive or active (informal)
informal

Examples

My grandfather is ninety but still kicks around and tends his garden every day.
Some of those old beliefs still kick around in rural communities.
After all these years, that ancient law is still kicking around.

Synset Examples

Does the old man still kick around?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be around - to continue to exist or be alive
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02610312-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to treat someone badly or unfairly, often repeatedly; to abuse or mistreat
treat badly or unfairly

Examples

I won't let them kick around my little brother anymore.
She felt as though her coworkers only wanted to kick around someone weaker than them.
After years of being kicked around at work, he decided to stand up for himself.

Synset Examples

They won't have me to kick around any more!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mistreat - to treat someone badly or cruelly
  • Synonym: abuse - to use in a way that is harmful or morally wrong; treat cruelly
  • Antonym: to cherish - to care for and protect lovingly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02522644-v
Synset POS: v
