to keep out

1. VERB

/ kip aʊt / (AmE), / kiːp aʊt / (BrE)
to remain outside a particular place or not enter it
remain outside; not enter

Examples

A sign on the fence warned people to keep out.
Visitors are required to keep out of restricted areas at all times.
She told the children to keep out while she was cleaning the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to enter - to go or come into a place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00118243-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kip aʊt / (AmE), / kiːp aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from entering a place; to shut out
prevent entry; shut out

Examples

Please keep out of the construction area for your safety.
The thick curtains kept out most of the noise from the street.
Strict laws are used to keep out invasive species from the country.

Synset Examples

The trees were shutting out all sunlight
This policy excludes people who have a criminal record from entering the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately prevent from entering or participating
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow to enter; to permit entry

Synset POS: v
