to round off

1. VERB

/ raʊnd ɔf / (AmE), / raʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
to shape or finish something so that it has a smooth, curved, or rounded edge or surface
make something curved or rounded at the edge

Examples

He used sandpaper to round off the sharp corners of the table.
The sculptor carefully rounded off the edges of the statue for a softer look.
Before painting, make sure to round off any rough splinters on the wooden rail.

Synset Examples

round the edges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - make an object smooth
  • Antonym: to square off - to make the edges or corners square

2. VERB

/ raʊnd ɑf / (AmE), / raʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
to make something complete or refined by adding the finishing touches; to bring something to a highly developed or perfected state
bring to a finished or perfected state

Examples

She used a touch of gold paint to round off the frame perfectly.
The chef added a final garnish to round off the dish before serving.
He studied public speaking to round off his skills as a leader.

3. VERB

/ raʊnd ɔf / (AmE), / raʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
to change a number to the nearest whole number or a number ending in zero, in order to make it simpler or easier to use
make a number simpler by rounding

Examples

We decided to round off the total bill to the nearest dollar.
You should round off 4.57 to 5 in your final answer.
The accountant will round off the figures for simplicity.
