to chip away at

1. VERB

/ tʃɪp əˈweɪ æt / (AmE), / tʃɪp əˈweɪ at / (BrE)
to gradually remove, reduce, or weaken something, especially by persistent effort or small actions
gradually remove or weaken

Examples

The constant criticism began to chip away at his confidence.
Budget cuts will chip away at funding for vital services.
Over the years, water has chipped away at the stone foundation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away or diminish
  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less

Etymology

From the verb 'chip' (to cut small pieces from something) and the adverbial phrase 'away at'.

2. VERB

/ tʃɪp əˈweɪ æt / (AmE), / tʃɪp əˈweɪ æt / (BrE)
to gradually reduce, remove, or weaken something over a period of time
gradually reduce or weaken

Examples

After years of consistent effort, she managed to chip away at her student loan.
Criticism from his colleagues began to chip away at his confidence.
We need a solid plan if we're going to chip away at this mountain of paperwork.

Synset Examples

He will chip away at his debts over time.
We need to chip away at this project bit by bit.
They began to chip away at the old paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to slowly destroy or wear away
  • Synonym: to wear down - to gradually reduce the strength or effectiveness of something
