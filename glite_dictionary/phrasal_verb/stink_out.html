to stink out

1. VERB

/ stɪŋk aʊt / (AmE), / stɪŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to fill a place with a very unpleasant smell, usually by causing something to smell very bad
make a place smell very bad

Examples

The boys' dirty gym socks really stank out the locker room.
Someone must have left food in here overnight; it's completely stunk out the kitchen.
She was embarrassed when her dog's accident stank out the entire car.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fumigate - to disinfect or purify with fumes (opposite effect)
