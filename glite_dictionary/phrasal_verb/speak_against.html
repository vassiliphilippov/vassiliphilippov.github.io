to speak against

1. VERB

/ spiːk əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / spiːk əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to express opposition to or criticize something publicly
express opposition or criticism publicly

Examples

Many leaders chose to speak against the proposed law during the public forum.
She was not afraid to speak against injustice, even when others remained silent.
During the meeting, several members spoke against the changes suggested by management.

Synset Examples

He spoke against fascism during the rally.
Survivors spoke out against the atrocities of ethnic cleansing.
Activists spoke out against the rise of hetero-fascism in their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppose - to actively resist or refuse to comply with
  • Antonym: to speak for - to express support for something or someone publicly
