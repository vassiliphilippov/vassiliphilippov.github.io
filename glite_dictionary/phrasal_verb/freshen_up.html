to freshen up

1. VERB

/ ˈfrɛʃən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃən ʌp / (BrE)
to wash, tidy, or clean oneself to feel or appear more refreshed or presentable
to wash or make oneself look refreshed

Examples

After her flight, she decided to freshen up before the meeting.
He went to the restroom to freshen up after playing basketball.
I like to freshen up before going out to dinner.

Synset Examples

She freshened up after the tennis game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clean up - to wash or make something/someone clean
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024807-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɛʃən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃən ʌp / (BrE)
to make something look cleaner, brighter, or more attractive by improving or renovating it slightly
make cleaner or more attractive

Examples

We decided to freshen up the living room with a new coat of paint.
The hotel is planning to freshen up its lobby to attract more guests.
Before the party, she wanted to freshen up the guest bedroom with some flowers and new linens.

Synset Examples

we refurbished the guest wing
My wife wants us to renovate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore something to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: to refurbish - to clean, decorate, and repair something to improve its condition
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00163249-v
Synset POS: v
