to make fun of

1. VERB

/ meɪk fʌn ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk fʌn ɒv / (BrE)
to tease, mock, or laugh at someone or something in a way that is intended to be humorous or ridiculing
mock or ridicule someone or something

Examples

Please don't make fun of his accent; it's unkind.
The children were upset when their classmates made fun of their new shoes.
They kept making fun of the movie, even though I enjoyed it.

Synset Examples

The satirists ridiculed the plans for a new opera house
The students poked fun at the inexperienced teacher
His former students roasted the professor at his 60th birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to ridicule or make fun of someone or something
  • Synonym: ridicule - to make someone or something the object of scornful laughter
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration; opposite of ridicule

2. VERB

/ meɪk fʌn ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk fʌn ɒv / (BrE)
to laugh at or tease someone in an unkind way; to mock or ridicule someone.
to mock or ridicule someone

Examples

It is unkind to make fun of people because of their mistakes.
The students began to make fun of the teacher's accent during class.
Please stop making fun of your little brother; you're hurting his feelings.

Synset Examples

Don't make fun of her; she's trying her best.
They always make fun of his silly jokes.
It's not nice to make fun of others.
Making fun of someone's features is never acceptable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone or something, often by imitating them
  • Synonym: ridicule - to make someone or something the object of scornful laughter
  • Antonym: to flatter - to praise or compliment insincerely or excessively

3. VERB

/ meɪk fʌn əv / (AmE), / meɪk fʌn ɒv / (BrE)
to tease or mock someone in a way that may cause embarrassment or amusement
to tease or mock someone

Examples

The children made fun of his new haircut, but he laughed along with them.
She always makes fun of her brother when he tries to sing.
It's not kind to make fun of others for their mistakes.

Synset Examples

She made fun of me until I laughed the snot out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to laugh at or make fun of someone in a scornful or contemptuous manner
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful or unkind way
