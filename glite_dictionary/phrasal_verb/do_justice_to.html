to do justice to

1. VERB

/ du ˈdʒʌstɪs tu / (AmE), / duː ˈdʒʌstɪs tuː / (BrE)
to treat or represent someone or something in a fair, accurate, or appropriate way
treat or represent fairly; give due credit

Examples

The review didn't do justice to the complexity of the novel.
Photographs can never do justice to the beauty of the mountains.
No summary can do justice to her years of hard work.

Synset Examples

We must do justice to the victims' stories.
The documentary does justice to the artist's legacy.
This report does justice to the situation at hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to do right by - to treat fairly or properly
