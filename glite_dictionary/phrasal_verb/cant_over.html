to cant over

1. VERB

/ kænt ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / kænt ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to tilt or lean to one side, especially so as to be off balance
tilt or lean to one side

Examples

The old ship began to cant over as water filled the lower hull.
If you overload one side, the table will cant over and perhaps collapse.
The bookshelf wasn't fixed to the wall and started to cant over during the earthquake.

Synset Examples

The tower is tilting
The ceiling is slanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
  • Antonym: right - to restore to an upright position

Etymology

From 'cant' (to tilt or slant), possibly from Middle French 'cant' (edge, corner).
