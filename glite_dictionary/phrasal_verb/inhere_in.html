to inhere in

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɪr ɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪə ɪn / (BrE)
to exist as an essential or inherent part of something
exist as an essential part of

Examples

A certain degree of uncertainty inhere in all scientific research.
The power to make decisions inheres in the board of directors.
Risks always inhere in large investments.

Synset Examples

This problem inheres in the design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reside in - to exist or be found in something

Etymology

From Latin inhaerēre (to be attached to, cling to).
