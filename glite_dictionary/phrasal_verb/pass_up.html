to pass up

1. VERB

/ pæs ʌp / (AmE), / pɑːs ʌp / (BrE)
to fail to acknowledge or notice (someone), especially by ignoring them in passing
ignore or fail to acknowledge (someone)

Examples

I greeted her, but she passed up me without a word.
It's hurtful when old friends pass up each other on the street.
He passed up his colleague in the hallway, pretending not to see him.

Synset Examples

he passed me up in the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something
  • Antonym: notice - to pay attention to or recognize someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02123818-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pæs ʌp / (AmE), / pɑːs ʌp / (BrE)
to choose not to take advantage of an opportunity; to decline or forgo something desirable or advantageous
decline or forgo an opportunity

Examples

I didn't want to pass up the chance to meet the author.
She couldn't pass up such a generous offer.
You shouldn’t pass up the opportunity to study abroad.

Synset Examples

He decided to pass up the chance to travel.
She couldn't pass up the sale on shoes.
Don't pass up this great opportunity!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to miss out - to fail to take advantage of an opportunity
  • Antonym: to accept - to take or receive something offered

WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
