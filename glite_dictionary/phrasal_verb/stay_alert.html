to stay alert

1. VERB

/ steɪ əˈlɝt / (AmE), / steɪ əˈlɜːt / (BrE)
to remain vigilant or watchful, especially in order to notice danger or changes
remain vigilant or watchful

Examples

Drivers are urged to stay alert when approaching roadworks.
You have to stay alert during night shifts at the hospital.
It is important to stay alert when hiking in areas with wildlife.

Synset Examples

In politics, a week is a long time, so stay alert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on guard - to be watchful for possible danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: to relax - to become less vigilant or tense
