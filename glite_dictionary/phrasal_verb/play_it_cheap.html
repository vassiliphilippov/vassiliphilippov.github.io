to play it cheap

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɪt tʃip / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪt tʃiːp / (BrE)
to behave in a stingy, frugal, or low-quality manner, particularly to save money
act in a stingy or low-quality way

Examples

You shouldn't play it cheap when it comes to your health.
The company tried to play it cheap on materials, and the product suffered.
Instead of playing it cheap, they invested in the best equipment available.

Synset Examples

Don't play it cheap; invest in quality.
Sometimes, you can't afford to play it cheap.
He tried to play it cheap to save money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrimp - to economize or save money by being frugal
  • Antonym: splurge - to spend money freely or extravagantly
