to ferret out

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛrət aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɛrɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to search for and discover something that is hidden or difficult to find, especially through persistent investigation
search and discover through investigation

Examples

After hours of searching, the detective finally ferreted out the missing documents.
It's not easy to ferret out the facts in such a complicated case.
She has a talent for ferreting out interesting stories from old archives.

Synset Examples

She ferreted out the truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to uncover - to discover something hidden, secret, or unknown
  • Synonym: to expose - to make something visible that was hidden
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide something carefully

Etymology

From the use of 'ferret' (the animal) to drive out rabbits from burrows; later used figuratively for discovering hidden things.
