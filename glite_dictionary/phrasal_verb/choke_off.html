to choke off

1. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ɔf / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ɒf / (BrE)
to block or obstruct something so that it stops or is prevented from continuing
block or obstruct (something)

Examples

Heavy snowfall can choke off vital supply routes to remote villages.
A sudden drop in funding may choke off research into new treatments.
The pipe was choked off by a buildup of mineral deposits.

Synset Examples

The leaves clog our drains in the Fall
The water pipe is backed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement through or along something
  • Synonym: to obstruct - to block or hinder passage, progress, or activity

Etymology

From 'choke' (to block the airway) + 'off' (indicating separation or stoppage).

2. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ɔf / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ɒf / (BrE)
to stop or suppress something by cutting off its source or flow
suppress or stop by cutting off

Examples

A lack of funding may choke off the program's growth.
The government imposed regulations to choke off illegal trading.
Heavy snowfall choked off access to the mountain village.

Synset Examples

He choked down his rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to forcibly put an end to
  • Synonym: stifle - to restrain or stop something from developing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00392119-v
Synset POS: v
