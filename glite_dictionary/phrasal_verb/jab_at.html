to jab at

1. VERB

/ dʒæb æt / (AmE), / dʒæb æt / (BrE)
to make a critical or teasing remark, often in a quick or pointed way
make a teasing or critical remark

Examples

He couldn't resist a jab at his rival during the meeting.
Her article included a subtle jab at the politician's recent decision.
They all laughed when Tom took a lighthearted jab at Mary's singing.

Synset Examples

She couldn’t help but jab at her friend’s cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to poke fun at - to make fun of someone in a teasing way
  • Antonym: compliment - to praise or express approval
