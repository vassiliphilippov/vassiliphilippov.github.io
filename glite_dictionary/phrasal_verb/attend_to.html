to attend to

1. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd tu / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd tuː / (BrE)
to deal with or give attention to something or someone; to take care of
to deal with or pay attention to

Examples

Please attend to the guests as soon as they arrive.
She had several urgent matters to attend to before leaving the office.
The nurse will attend to you in a few minutes.

Synset Examples

Attend to your duties, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take care of - to look after or deal with someone or something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to take care of or pay attention to something

2. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd tu / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd tə / (BrE)
to act as a servant for someone; to take care of someone as part of one’s duties
serve or care for someone as a duty

Examples

The nurse attends to the elderly patients with great care every morning.
During his years at the manor, James attended to the needs of the household staff.
She graciously attended to her employer, preparing his meals and managing his appointments.

Synset Examples

May I serve you?
She attends the old lady in the wheelchair
Can you wait on our table, please?
Is a salesperson assisting you?
The minister served the King for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - to perform duties for someone as a servant
  • Synonym: to wait on - to act as a servant or provide service to someone

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' (to pay attention to); 'attend' meaning to wait upon or serve dates to Middle English.
