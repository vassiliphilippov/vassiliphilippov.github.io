to linger over

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪŋɡɚ ˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡə ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to spend more time than necessary on something, especially because you are enjoying it or reluctant to finish; to delay over something.
spend extra time on; delay over

Examples

Every morning, she would linger over her coffee, enjoying the quiet before work.
He tends to linger over his assignments, double-checking every detail.
They lingered over dinner, talking long after the plates had been cleared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwell on - spend time thinking or talking about something at length
  • Antonym: rush through - complete something quickly; do without delay

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪŋɡɚ ˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡə ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to spend time on something slowly and enjoyably, not hurrying to finish it
spend time slowly and enjoyably

Examples

He likes to linger over the Sunday paper with a cup of tea.
Tourists often linger over the paintings in the museum, admiring every detail.
She would linger over her dinner, savoring each bite and enjoying the conversation.

Synset Examples

I like to linger over coffee in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwell on - to spend a lot of time thinking or talking about something
  • Antonym: rush through - to do something quickly without taking time
