to keep company

1. VERB

/ kip ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / kiːp ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
to spend time with someone as a companion, especially regularly or socially
spend time as a companion

Examples

She often keeps company with her elderly neighbor to make sure he's not lonely.
During his recovery, friends and family kept company with him every evening.
It’s important to keep company with people who support and inspire you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to go somewhere with someone as a companion
  • Antonym: be alone - to be by oneself without company
