to run about

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / rʌn əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to move around energetically or busily, often in different directions without a fixed purpose
move around energetically

Examples

The children run about the yard until sunset every evening.
I could hear my dog running about the house, looking for his toy.
After the meeting, everyone was running about trying to finish their tasks.

Synset Examples

They often run about the beach on sunny days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush around - to move quickly and busily from place to place
  • Antonym: to stand still - to remain in one place without moving
