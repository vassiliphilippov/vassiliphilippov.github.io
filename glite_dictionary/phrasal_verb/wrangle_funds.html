to wrangle funds

1. VERB

/ ˈræŋɡəl fʌndz / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡl̩ fʌndz / (BrE)
to obtain or secure funds, especially through persistent effort or negotiation
obtain funds through effort

Examples

It took months of persistent lobbying to wrangle funds for the new community center.
The team managed to wrangle funds from several private donors for the expedition.
She often has to wrangle funds to keep her nonprofit organization running.

Synset Examples

They managed to wrangle the funds for the charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise funds - to collect or gather money for a purpose

Etymology

From 'wrangle' (to argue or struggle), possibly from Middle Low German 'wrangeln' (to wrestle), combined with 'funds'.
