to break through

1. VERB

/ breɪk θruː / (AmE), / breɪk θruː / (BrE)
to force a way through an obstacle or barrier; to penetrate something that is blocking the way.
to penetrate an obstacle

Examples

After hours of digging, the miners finally broke through to the trapped workers.
A shaft of light broke through the darkness in the abandoned tunnel.
Scientists are hoping to break through the barriers to a cure for the disease.

Synset Examples

The sun broke through the clouds
The rescue team broke through the wall in the mine shaft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to penetrate - to make a way into or through something

2. VERB

/ breɪk θruː / (AmE), / breɪk θruː / (BrE)
(of something covered or contained) to emerge suddenly or forcefully, especially by passing through a barrier or surface.
emerge suddenly or forcefully

Examples

The baby's first tooth finally broke through yesterday, much to her parents' delight.
Sunlight broke through the clouds after a long storm.
After weeks of trying, the plant broke through the soil.

Synset Examples

The tooth erupted and had to be extracted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to burst forth or emerge violently

3. VERB

/ breɪk θru / (AmE), / breɪk θruː / (BrE)
to successfully pass through or overcome a barrier or obstacle, often after a struggle or effort
pass through or overcome a barrier

Examples

The protestors managed to break through the police barricade and reach the main square.
After months of research, scientists finally broke through and discovered a new treatment.
She worked hard to break through the barriers in her industry and achieve success.

Synset Examples
