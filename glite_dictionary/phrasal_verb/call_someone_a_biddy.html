to call someone a biddy

1. VERB

/ kɔl ˈsʌmwʌn ə ˈbɪdi / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈsʌmwʌn ə ˈbɪdi / (BrE)
to refer to someone as an old woman, usually in a disparaging or mocking way
refer to someone as an old woman (derogatory)
informal

Examples

She angrily called her a biddy after hearing more gossip.
You shouldn't call someone a biddy, even if they're nosy.
He regretted calling his aunt a biddy during their argument.

Synset Examples

She called her neighbor a biddy for gossiping about her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old biddy - derogatory term for an old woman

Etymology

Possibly from the Irish personal name Biddy (diminutive of Bridget), used as a generic name for an old woman.
