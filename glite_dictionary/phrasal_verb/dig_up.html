to dig up

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ ʌp / (AmE), / dɪɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to find or discover something by digging in the ground
find by digging in the ground

Examples

Archaeologists hope to dig up more artifacts in this field.
We decided to dig up the flower bed and found an old coin.
The workers dug up several large rocks while building the foundation.

Synset Examples

I dug up an old box in the garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to dig out material from the ground, often to find something

2. VERB

/ dɪɡ ʌp / (AmE), / dɪɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to remove, harvest, or recover something from the ground by digging
remove or recover by digging

Examples

They plan to dig up the old road to lay new pipes underneath.
Farmers often dig up potatoes from the soil by hand.
The crew dug up the area to search for ancient relics.

  • Antonym: to bury - to put or hide under ground; opposite of uncovering by digging
