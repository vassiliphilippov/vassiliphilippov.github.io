to call on

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɑn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒn / (BrE)
to visit someone, usually for a short period of time
to visit briefly

Examples

I plan to call on my grandmother this afternoon to see how she's doing.
The doctor will call on several patients at their homes today.
We decided to call on our neighbors to invite them to the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visit - to go to see someone socially or for a purpose

2. VERB

/ kɔl ɑn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒn / (BrE)
to challenge someone to justify a statement or take responsibility for an action, especially by pointing out an inconsistency or offense
challenge someone to justify a statement

Examples

When he lied during the meeting, she called on him to provide evidence for his claim.
The journalist called on the politician about the inconsistency in his story.
He was called on his inappropriate comment by several members of the audience.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ kɔl ɑn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒn / (BrE)
to formally ask someone to do something or to appeal to someone for help or information
to request or appeal to someone

Examples

During the meeting, she called on her colleague to explain the report.
Citizens called on the mayor to address the housing crisis.
He calls on experts when he needs advice about complicated cases.
