to catch big air

1. VERB

/ kætʃ bɪɡ ɛr / (AmE), / katʃ bɪɡ eə / (BrE)
to achieve significant height while jumping, especially in sports such as skiing, snowboarding, or skateboarding
to jump to a great height (sport)
informal

Examples

During the competition, she managed to catch big air on the halfpipe and impress the judges.
If you want to catch big air, you need to build up plenty of speed before the jump.
Many snowboarders dream of catching big air at famous mountain resorts.

Synset Examples

The skier was able to catch big air during the competition.
He learned how to catch big air after many tries.
They were ecstatic to catch big air during their jumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get air - to jump and become airborne, especially in extreme sports
