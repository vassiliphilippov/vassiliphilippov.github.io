to bully off

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊli ɑf / (AmE), / ˈbʊli ɒf / (BrE)
(in field hockey) to start a game or restart play by a face-off, where two opposing players try to gain possession of the ball.
start a field hockey game with a face-off

Examples

The referee signaled for the teams to bully off at the start of the match.
After the first goal, the players gathered to bully off again in the center of the field.
He skillfully bullied off, quickly gaining control of the ball for his team.

Etymology

From 'bully,' a technical term in field hockey (possibly from English dialect 'bully' meaning 'noise' or 'confused fight'), combined with 'off.'
