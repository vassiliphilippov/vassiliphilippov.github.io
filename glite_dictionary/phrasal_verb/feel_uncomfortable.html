to feel uncomfortable

1. VERB

/ fil ʌnˈkʌmfərtəbl̩ / (AmE), / fiːl ʌnˈkʌmftəbl / (BrE)
to experience a sense of discomfort or unease, either physically or emotionally
experience discomfort or unease

Examples

She always feels uncomfortable speaking in front of large audiences.
Many people feel uncomfortable when they have to discuss personal topics.
If you feel uncomfortable at any point, please let us know.

Synset Examples

Many feel uncomfortable discussing vagenis openly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel uneasy - to experience a feeling of anxiety or restlessness
  • Antonym: feel comfortable - to experience comfort or ease
