to take a deep breath

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə diːp brɛθ / (AmE), / teɪk ə diːp brɛθ / (BrE)
to inhale deeply, especially to relax, calm oneself, or prepare for a difficult situation
inhale deeply to relax or prepare

Examples

She paused to take a deep breath before answering the difficult question.
Whenever you feel nervous, just take a deep breath and try to relax.
He told himself to take a deep breath and not panic during the interview.
