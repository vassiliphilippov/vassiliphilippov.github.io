to play fetch

1. VERB

/ pleɪ fɛtʃ / (AmE), / pleɪ fɛtʃ / (BrE)
to engage in a game, typically with a dog, where an object is thrown for the dog to retrieve and return.
throw-and-retrieve game with a dog

Examples

Every afternoon, Sarah takes her golden retriever to the park to play fetch.
The puppy quickly learned how to play fetch with a tennis ball.
Whenever we visit the beach, my dog insists we play fetch for hours.

Synset Examples

Her mutt loves to play fetch at the park.
The pit bull was friendly and loved to play fetch.
Buster loves to play fetch in the yard.
Her granddog loves to play fetch in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetch - bring back something that has been thrown
