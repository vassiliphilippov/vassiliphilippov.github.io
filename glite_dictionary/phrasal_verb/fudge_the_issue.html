to fudge the issue

1. VERB

/ fʌdʒ ði ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / fʌdʒ ði ˈɪʃuː / (BrE)
to avoid addressing a problem or question directly, often by being vague or evasive
avoid addressing a problem directly

Examples

The politician tried to fudge the issue when questioned about the policy.
Whenever difficult topics come up, he tends to fudge the issue instead of answering directly.
We shouldn't fudge the issue; it's better to face the facts honestly.

Synset Examples

He tends to fudge the issue to avoid confrontation.
Don't fudge the issue during the meeting; be clear.
Let's not fudge the issue; we need to address it directly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - avoid dealing with or responding to something directly
  • Antonym: to confront - to deal with a problem directly

Etymology

From 'fudge' meaning to be evasive or falsify, origin uncertain but used in English from the late 18th century.
