to hack it

1. VERB

/ hæk ɪt / (AmE), / hæk ɪt / (BrE)
to succeed or manage in a difficult or challenging situation
manage or succeed in tough circumstances
informal

Examples

After moving to the big city, she wondered if she could really hack it.
Not everyone is able to hack it in such a high-pressure environment.
He realized medical school was tough, but he was determined to hack it.

Synset Examples

I knew I could hack it if I really tried.
He believed he could hack it in the competitive industry.
She wasn't sure she could hack it in such a demanding job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make it - to succeed or survive, especially when it is difficult
  • Synonym: handle - to deal with or manage something successfully
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed; to be unsuccessful
