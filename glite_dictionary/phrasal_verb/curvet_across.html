to curvet across

1. VERB

/ ˈkɜrvɪt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ˈkɜːvɪt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to move across a space with a graceful, leaping motion, similar to a curvet performed in dance or by a horse
move across with a graceful leap

Examples

The ballerina seemed to curvet across the floor, mesmerizing the audience with her elegance.
The horse began to curvet across the paddock, displaying its agility for the judges.
With a light and joyful step, the child tried to curvet across the playground like her favorite dancer.

Synset Examples

He watched the dancer curvet across the stage with grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - to jump or spring a long way, to a great height, or with great force
  • Degree Lower: skip - to move along lightly, stepping from one foot to the other with a hop or bounce

Etymology

From 'curvet', from Old Italian 'corvetta' (leap of a horse).
