to hold fire

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / həʊld ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to stop shooting or attacking or to wait before taking action
to wait before attacking or acting

Examples

The commander shouted, 'Hold fire!' as the enemy approached the gate.
Sometimes it's wise to hold fire and gather more information before making a decision.
The negotiators agreed to hold fire until both sides could meet.

Synset Examples

The captain ordered everyone to hold their fire.
You should hold your fire until you have clear targets.
Hold your fire until I give the signal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to open fire - to begin shooting weapons or attacking
