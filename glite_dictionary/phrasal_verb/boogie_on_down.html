to boogie on down

1. VERB

/ ˈbuːɡi ɑn daʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʊɡi ɒn daʊn / (BrE)
to dance or move in a lively and energetic way, especially to music
dance or move energetically
informal slang

Examples

After hearing their favorite song, the crowd couldn't help but boogie on down at the party.
She loves to boogie on down whenever there's good music playing.
At the wedding, everyone got up to boogie on down and celebrate.

Synset Examples

They decided to boogie on down after dinner.
It's time to boogie on down and have some fun!
Let's boogie on down to the dance floor!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps

Etymology

From 'boogie' (to dance energetically), possibly influenced by boogie-woogie (a style of blues music).
