to talk up

1. VERB

/ tɔk ʌp / (AmE), / tɔːk ʌp / (BrE)
to speak about something in a way that makes it seem more important, impressive, or successful than it really is; to promote enthusiastically
to promote or exaggerate

Examples

During her job interview, she tried to talk up her previous experience.
The company is talking up its new product despite some criticism.
He always talks up his minor accomplishments as if they were major achievements.

Synset Examples

She always tries to talk up her achievements.
During the meeting, she talked up the new initiative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
