to frown at

1. VERB

/ fraʊn æt / (AmE), / fraʊn ət / (BrE)
to show displeasure or disapproval towards someone or something by frowning
show displeasure or disapproval by frowning

Examples

The teacher began to frown at students who were talking during the lesson.
She couldn't help but frown at the messy state of the kitchen.
Many people frown at the idea of working on weekends.

Synset Examples

The dog will frown at anyone who tries to take its toy.
He decided to frown at the rude customer for being disrespectful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl at - to look at someone or something with a very annoyed expression
  • Antonym: smile at - to show pleasure or approval by smiling at someone or something
