to plant your feet

1. VERB

/ plænt jʊr fit / (AmE), / plɑːnt jɔː fiːt / (BrE)
to stand firmly in place, especially in a determined or resolute manner; to assert oneself firmly
stand firmly or assert oneself

Examples

When challenged on her ideas, she chose to plant her feet and defend her perspective.
Coaches often tell athletes to plant their feet before making a big move.
It's not always easy to plant your feet in the face of strong opposition.

Synset Examples

You need to plant your feet if you want to make a stand.
It's time to plant your feet and assert your opinion.
She decided to plant her feet and not back down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand your ground - refuse to retreat; maintain one's position
  • Synonym: dig in your heels - refuse to change one's mind or position
