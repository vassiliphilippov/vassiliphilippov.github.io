to palp the pulse

1. VERB

/ pælp ðə pʌls / (AmE), / pælp ðə pʌls / (BrE)
to examine a person's heartbeat by feeling the pulse with the fingers, usually at the wrist or neck
examine heartbeat by touch
medical

Examples

During the examination, the nurse palped the pulse to check the patient's heart rate.
Doctors are trained to palp the pulse in various emergency situations.
She was able to palp the pulse accurately after just a few lessons.

Synset Examples

She learned to palp the pulse during her training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take the pulse - to measure the pulse rate by touch

Etymology

From Latin 'palpare' (to touch gently) and 'pulse' from Latin 'pulsus' (beating, striking).
