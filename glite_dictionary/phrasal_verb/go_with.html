to go with

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð / (BrE)
to be present or associated with something; to occur together or in combination with something else
to accompany or be associated with

Examples

French fries often go with hamburgers as a popular meal choice.
A sense of nostalgia can go with certain pieces of music.
White wine typically goes with seafood dishes at fancy restaurants.

Synset Examples

French fries come with the hamburger
heart attacks are accompanied by destruction of heart tissue
fish usually goes with white wine
this kind of vein accompanies certain arteries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to go somewhere with someone or something
  • Synonym: to come with - to be present or included with something
  • Antonym: be absent from - to not be present or associated with something

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð / (BrE)
to appear or occur together with something else; to accompany or be found together
occur together; accompany

Examples

Spicy food often goes with a glass of cold milk.
The color blue goes with almost everything in her wardrobe.
Economic downturns frequently go with rising unemployment rates.

Synset Examples

The word ‘hot’ tends to cooccur with ‘cold’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to be present or occur at the same time as something
  • Synonym: to coexist - to exist together or at the same time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02666897-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð / (BrE)
to choose or select something from a range of options
choose or select

Examples

After much thought, I've decided to go with the vegetarian option for dinner.
Are you planning to go with the classic design or something more modern?
We went with the cheaper hotel to save money on our trip.

Synset Examples

I think I'll go with the blue dress for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to choose - to pick out or select from a number of alternatives
  • Synonym: select - to carefully choose as being the best or most suitable
