to round up

1. VERB

/ raʊnd ʌp / (AmE), / raʊnd ʌp / (BrE)
to find and gather together people, animals, or things, often for a particular purpose
gather together; collect

Examples

The police managed to round up all the suspects before dawn.
Every autumn, the farmers round up their cattle for vaccination.
The volunteers will round up supplies for the disaster relief effort.

Synset Examples

round up some loyal followers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to bring together in one place
  • Antonym: to disperse - to break up and scatter in all directions

2. VERB

/ raʊnd ʌp / (AmE), / raʊnd ʌp / (BrE)
to gather together people, animals, or things; to collect or bring together in one place
gather or collect together

Examples

The teacher had to round up all the students after recess.
They worked together to round up stray dogs in the neighborhood.
Before starting the meeting, please round up everyone from the office.

Synset Examples

Let’s round up the kids for the game.
I need to round up some volunteers for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - bring or come together in a group
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or break up a group
