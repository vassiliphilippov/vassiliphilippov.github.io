to find shelter

1. VERB

/ faɪnd ˈʃɛltər / (AmE), / faɪnd ˈʃɛltə / (BrE)
to seek and obtain a place of safety or protection, especially from danger or bad weather
seek and obtain safety or protection

Examples

When the rain started pouring, we had to find shelter under a nearby bridge.
Many animals find shelter in caves during storms to stay safe.
Tourists often find shelter in local shops when sudden downpours strike the city.

Synset Examples

They fled the tornado and found shelter in a basement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take shelter - to go to or into a place of safety or protection
