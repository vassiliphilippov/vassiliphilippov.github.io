to level off

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛvəl ɑf / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl ɒf / (BrE)
to become flat, smooth, or even after rising or falling; to stop increasing, falling, or changing and remain steady
become flat or remain steady

Examples

After a steep climb, the trail finally levelled off near the summit.
House prices have started to level off after several years of rapid growth.
The pilot waited for the plane to level off before turning off the seatbelt sign.

Synset Examples

The ground levelled off

2. VERB

/ ˈlɛvəl ɑf / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl ɒf / (BrE)
to stabilize or stop increasing, especially after a period of change or growth
become stable after change

Examples

After years of rapid growth, the company's profits finally leveled off last year.
As the pilot guided the aircraft higher, she prepared to level off at cruising altitude.
Once the rain subsides, water levels in the river should level off and remain steady.

Synset Examples

Sales are expected to level off next quarter.
The plane began to level off after climbing.
The water level will level off once it stops raining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stabilize - to make or become steady or unlikely to change
  • Antonym: to fluctuate - to change frequently and unpredictably, opposite of leveling off
