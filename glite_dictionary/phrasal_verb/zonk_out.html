to zonk out

1. VERB

/ zɑŋk aʊt / (AmE), / zɒŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to lose consciousness suddenly, especially due to extreme tiredness, impact, or trauma
lose consciousness suddenly
informal

Examples

After staying awake for 36 hours straight, I zonked out the moment I hit the bed.
The medicine was so strong that he zonked out within minutes of taking it.
She zonks out almost every afternoon if she doesn't have her coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faint - to suddenly lose consciousness
  • Synonym: to pass out - to become unconscious suddenly
  • Antonym: to come to - to regain consciousness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00023861-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ zɑŋk aʊt / (AmE), / zɒŋk aʊt / (BrE)
To fall asleep very quickly, especially because of extreme tiredness.
fall asleep suddenly from fatigue
informal

Examples

I was so exhausted after the hike that I zonked out as soon as I lay down.
Sometimes he just zonks out in the middle of watching TV if he's tired enough.
She didn't even hear the thunderstorm last night because she zonked out right away.

Synset Examples

after the long drive, we zonked out and slept for 10 hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to doze off - to fall asleep, especially unintentionally
  • Synonym: to conk out - to fall asleep or lose consciousness suddenly
