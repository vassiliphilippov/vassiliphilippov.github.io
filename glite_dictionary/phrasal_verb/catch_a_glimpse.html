to catch a glimpse

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ə ɡlɪmps / (AmE), / kætʃ ə ɡlɪmps / (BrE)
to see something or someone for only a very short time, often briefly or partially
see briefly; spot momentarily

Examples

I managed to catch a glimpse of the singer as she left the building.
As the train sped by, he caught a glimpse of the countryside.
She was lucky to catch a glimpse of the rare bird in the treetop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimpse - a brief or quick view
