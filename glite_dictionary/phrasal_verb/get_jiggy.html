to get jiggy

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈd͡ʒɪɡi / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈd͡ʒɪɡi / (BrE)
to dance energetically or enjoy oneself in a lively, playful way
dance or have fun energetically
informal slang

Examples

Once the music started, everyone rushed to the floor to get jiggy.
At the wedding, even Grandpa decided to get jiggy with the rest of us.
It's Friday night, time to relax and get jiggy with your friends.

Synset Examples

They're always getting jiggy jiggy at the club.
After a few drinks, everyone got jiggy jiggy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps
  • Synonym: party - to enjoy oneself at a lively gathering, typically with drinking and dancing

Etymology

Popularized in the late 1990s, notably by Will Smith’s song 'Gettin’ Jiggy wit It'; 'jiggy' probably from 'jig,' a lively dance.
