to wind off

1. VERB

/ waɪnd ɑf / (AmE), / waɪnd ɒf / (BrE)
to remove by unrolling, or to complete something gradually and bring it to an end
to unroll or gradually finish

Examples

She asked him to wind off the rest of the ribbon for her.
Before you leave, make sure to wind off your current tasks.
Carefully wind off the tape so it doesn’t tangle.

Synset Examples

Please wind off the tape carefully.
It's time to wind off the lights and go home.
You should wind off your work before taking a break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wind up - to bring something to a conclusion; to finish
  • Synonym: finish - to complete an activity or task
