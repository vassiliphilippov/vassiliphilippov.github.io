to moon on

1. VERB

/ mun ɑn / (AmE), / muːn ɒn / (BrE)
to be idle in a dreamy or listless manner, often lost in thought
be idle in a dreamy or listless way

Examples

He would often moon on by the window, lost in his own thoughts for hours.
Instead of working, she spent the afternoon mooning on about her future.
Children sometimes moon on during class, not paying attention to the lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydream - to indulge in idle or absent-minded fantasy while awake
  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing; to be inactive
