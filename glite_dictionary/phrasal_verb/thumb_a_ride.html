to thumb a ride

1. VERB

/ θʌm ə ɹaɪd / (AmE), / θʌm ə ɹaɪd / (BrE)
to solicit a free ride in a passing vehicle by signaling with one's thumb, usually while standing by the roadside
to solicit a ride by hitchhiking

Examples

After his car broke down, he decided to thumb a ride to the nearest town.
We watched as she tried to thumb a ride along the highway during the rainstorm.
Tourists often thumb a ride when exploring the countryside with little money.

Synset Examples

He stood by the road trying to thumb a ride.
They had no car, so they thumbed a ride home.
I decided to thumb a ride to the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hitchhike - to travel by getting free rides in passing vehicles

Etymology

Refers to the gesture of sticking out one's thumb to signal for a ride, attested from mid-20th century US English.
