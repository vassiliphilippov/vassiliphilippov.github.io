to take time

1. VERB

/ teɪk taɪm / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm / (BrE)
to require a significant or considerable amount of time to be done or completed.
require a significant amount of time

Examples

Restoring the old painting will take time and patience.
Learning a new language takes time, so you shouldn't be discouraged early on.
She knew that recovering from the injury would take time.

Synset Examples

He explained the project details and all this took time.
