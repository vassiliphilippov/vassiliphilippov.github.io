to slur over

1. VERB

/ slɝː ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / slɜː ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to treat something superficially or hastily, avoiding proper or thorough attention
treat hastily or superficially

Examples

The report slurred over important facts that should have been discussed in detail.
He tends to slur over the more difficult issues during meetings.
Politicians often slur over their opponents' strongest arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skim over - to deal with quickly and without detail
  • Antonym: to dwell on - to focus attention on something persistently
