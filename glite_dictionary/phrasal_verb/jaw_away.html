to jaw away

1. VERB

/ dʒɑ ˈəˌweɪ / (AmE), / dʒɔː əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to talk continuously and usually casually or tediously
to talk continuously or idly

Examples

The two old friends would jaw away for hours every afternoon at the café.
He tends to jaw away during meetings, making it hard to stay focused.
We could hear the neighbors jawing away on their porch late into the night.

Synset Examples

The kids jaw away at the dinner table, ignoring their food.
They could jaw away for hours about their favorite shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk rapidly or incessantly about trivial matters
  • Synonym: gab - to talk at length, typically about trivial matters

Etymology

From 'jaw' meaning to chatter or talk, possibly imitative; 'away' as an adverb to indicate continuation.
