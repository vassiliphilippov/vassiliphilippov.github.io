to let out a gasp

1. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt ə ɡæsp / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt ə ɡɑːsp / (BrE)
to suddenly emit a short, sharp breath as a reaction to surprise, shock, or pain
suddenly emit a sharp breath in surprise

Examples

The audience collectively let out a gasp as the magician pulled the rabbit from the hat.
He let out a gasp of pain when he touched the hot stove.
She let out a gasp upon hearing the shocking news.

Synset Examples

She let out a gasp when the fireworks exploded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasp - a sudden, short intake of breath
