to luck through life

1. VERB

/ lʌk θruː laɪf / (AmE), / lʌk θruː laɪf / (BrE)
to succeed throughout one's life by chance rather than effort or planning
succeed by chance throughout life

Examples

Some people seem to luck through life while others struggle despite their best efforts.
He managed to luck through life, stumbling from one fortunate opportunity to another.
Although she rarely planned, she often lucked through life and ended up exactly where she wanted.

Synset Examples

He seemed to luck through life without any effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succeed by chance - to accomplish something due to luck rather than skill or effort
  • Antonym: work hard - to put in effort and diligence to achieve something, as opposed to relying on luck
