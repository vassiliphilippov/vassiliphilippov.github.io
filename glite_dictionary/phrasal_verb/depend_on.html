to depend on

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd ɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd ɒn / (BrE)
to need someone or something for support, help, or survival
require support or help from

Examples

Many endangered animals depend on forests for their habitat and food.
She knew she could always depend on her best friend for honest advice.
Farmers in this region depend on regular rainfall to grow their crops.

Synset Examples

elderly parents often depend on their adult children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rely on - to trust or depend on someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02669926-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd ɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd ɒn / (BrE)
to trust or place confidence in someone or something; to rely
trust; place confidence in

Examples

You can always depend on her to do the right thing in a crisis.
Many people depend on public transportation to get to work each day.
We depended on Tom for his expert advice during the project.

Synset Examples

she is someone you can really rely on when times get rough
you can rely on his discretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rely on - to have confidence or trust in

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere', meaning 'to hang from'; 'on' as preposition indicating basis or support.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02670095-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd ɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd ɒn / (BrE)
to be determined or influenced by something; to be contingent on
be determined by; be contingent on

Examples

Your success in this course will depend on how much effort you put in.
The timing of our picnic will depend on the weather forecast.
Many plants depend on bees for pollination.

Synset Examples

The outcomes rides on the results of the election
Your grade will depend on your homework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rely on - to trust or depend on someone or something
  • Antonym: be independent of - not contingent on; not depending on something

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang down), via Old French 'dependre'.

Source: WordNet
