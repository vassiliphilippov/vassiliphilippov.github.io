to rip out

1. VERB

/ rɪp aʊt / (AmE), / rɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something quickly or forcefully, especially by pulling; also used figuratively to mean remove or eliminate suddenly and completely.
remove suddenly or forcefully

Examples

The dentist had to rip out the broken tooth because it couldn't be saved.
They decided to rip out the old carpet before installing hardwood floors.
The new leadership wants to rip out inefficient practices from the company.

Synset Examples

pull weeds
extract a bad tooth
take out a splinter
extract information from the telegram

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tear out - to remove by tearing or pulling forcibly
  • Antonym: insert - to put something into something else
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01353852-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪp aʊt / (AmE), / rɪp aʊt / (BrE)
To utter or exclaim something suddenly and forcefully, often with anger or strong emotion.
say or shout suddenly and forcefully

Examples

Unable to control his temper, he ripped out a string of expletives.
She suddenly ripped out a furious protest at the unfair decision.
When the car wouldn't start, he ripped out a curse in frustration.

Synset Examples

ripped out a vicious oath
ripped out with an oath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly or forcefully
  • Synonym: to blurt out - to say something suddenly and without thinking
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or quietly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00932156-v
Synset POS: v
