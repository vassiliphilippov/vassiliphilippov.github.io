to come knocking

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈnɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈnɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
to appear, arrive, or present oneself, often unexpectedly or seeking attention, help, or opportunity
to arrive or present oneself unexpectedly

Examples

If trouble should come knocking, stay calm and handle it carefully.
He never expected fortune to come knocking twice in one year.
New opportunities may come knocking when you least expect them.

Synset Examples

Opportunities will come knocking if you work hard.
Don't wait for them to come knocking; reach out first.
When success comes knocking, be ready to answer.
