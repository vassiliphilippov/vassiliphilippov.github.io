to burn up

1. VERB

/ bɝn ʌp / (AmE), / bɜːn ʌp / (BrE)
to use energy, especially as a result of physical activity; to expend (calories, fuel, etc.) through activity or use
use up energy or fuel through activity

Examples

Intense cardio workouts can help you burn up more calories.
After climbing the hill, I could feel my body burning up energy quickly.
You burn up a lot of fuel walking several miles each day.

Synset Examples

burn off calories through vigorous exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expend - to use up (a resource, such as energy, time, or money)
  • Antonym: conserve - to use as little as possible; to avoid waste
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01207620-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɝːn ʌp / (AmE), / bɜːn ʌp / (BrE)
to burn with more intense brightness or flame, often suddenly or vividly
burn more brightly

Examples

As the dry wood caught, the campfire began to burn up and illuminate the night.
The fireworks made the sky burn up with color for a few brief seconds.
As dawn approached, the embers in the hearth started to burn up one last time.

Synset Examples

Every star seemed to flare with new intensity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flare - to burn with a sudden or intense brightness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02768764-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɝn ʌp / (AmE), / bɜːn ʌp / (BrE)
to be completely destroyed or consumed by fire
be destroyed by fire

Examples

The old barn burned up in the lightning storm last night.
If you don't watch the stove, the food might burn up and ruin the pot.
The documents burned up before anyone could save them from the flames.

Synset Examples

The hut burned down
The mountain of paper went up in flames

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burn down - (of a building or other structure) to be destroyed by fire
