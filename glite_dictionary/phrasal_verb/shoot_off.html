to shoot off

1. VERB

/ ʃut ɔf / (AmE), / ʃuːt ɒf / (BrE)
to send something, especially a message or note, quickly or without much preparation
send quickly or casually

Examples

I'll shoot off an email as soon as I get to my desk.
She managed to shoot off a few quick replies between meetings.
He forgot to shoot off the invitation to his colleagues.

Synset Examples

I need to shoot off a quick email before the meeting.
She decided to shoot off a few texts during lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dash off - to write or send something quickly
