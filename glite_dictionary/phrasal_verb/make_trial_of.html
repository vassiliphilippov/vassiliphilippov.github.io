to make trial of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈtraɪəl ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ˈtraɪəl əv / (BrE)
to test or evaluate something by trying it out
test or evaluate by trying

Examples

Before implementing the new system, the team decided to make trial of it in a controlled environment.
She prefers to make trial of any cleaning product before recommending it to her clients.
We should make trial of several solutions to determine which works best.

Synset Examples

Let's make trial of this method and see if it works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test - to try something in order to find out how good, effective, etc. it is
  • Synonym: to try out - to test something to see how useful or effective it is
