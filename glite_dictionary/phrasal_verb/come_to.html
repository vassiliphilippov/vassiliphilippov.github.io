to come to

1. VERB

/ kʌm tu / (AmE), / kʌm tʊ / (BrE)
to reach or attain (a particular condition, state, or result)
reach or attain a state or result

Examples

After hours of debate, the committee finally came to a decision.
It took me years to come to an understanding of the problem.
The patient slowly came to the realization that he needed help.

Synset Examples

The horse finally struck a pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrive at - to reach (a particular place or state)

2. VERB

/ kʌm tu / (AmE), / kʌm tʊ / (BrE)
(of a total) to add up to a particular amount or number when calculated.

Examples

With tax included, your bill comes to twenty-five dollars.
All the donations come to a significant sum for the charity.

Synset Examples
