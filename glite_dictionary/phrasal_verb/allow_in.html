to allow in

1. VERB

/ əˈlaʊ ɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to permit (someone or something) to enter a place
permit to enter

Examples

The security guard will not allow in anyone without proper identification.
The hotel does not allow in pets, except for service animals.
Fresh air is allowed in through the open windows during the summer.

Synset Examples

We cannot admit non-members into our club building
This pipe admits air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to allow entry; to let in
  • Antonym: bar - to prevent entry; to keep out
