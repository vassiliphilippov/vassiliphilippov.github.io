to hit out at

1. VERB

/ hɪt aʊt æt / (AmE), / hɪt aʊt ət / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something strongly and publicly
criticize or attack verbally

Examples

The athlete hit out at his coaches after the disappointing result.
She hit out at the unfair treatment she received at work.
During the interview, the politician hit out at her opponents' policies.

Synset Examples

He decided to hit out against the unfair policies.
They hit out at the critics during the press conference.
She hit out at the injustice she witnessed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to criticize or oppose strongly
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
