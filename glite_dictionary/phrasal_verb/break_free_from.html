to break free from

1. VERB

/ breɪk fri frəm / (AmE), / breɪk fri frɒm / (BrE)
to escape from limitations, restrictions, or control; to become independent or unrestrained.
escape limitations or restrictions

Examples

After years of working in the same job, she decided to break free from her routine and travel the world.
It took courage for him to break free from his family's expectations and choose his own path.
The country struggled to break free from the influence of its powerful neighbor.

Synset Examples

Traveling solo helped him break free from his comfort zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place or situation where you are confined or in danger
  • Synonym: break out of - to get out of a situation or place, especially a prison or entrapment
