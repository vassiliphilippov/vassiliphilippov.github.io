to come down to

1. VERB

/ kʌm daʊn tu / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn tə / (BrE)
to be reduced to, depend on, or be decided by a particular factor or point
be reduced to or decided by something

Examples

It all comes down to how much effort you are willing to put in.
The decision will come down to which candidate has more experience.
Success often comes down to being in the right place at the right time.

Synset Examples

Solutions need to come down to us quickly for implementation.
It always comes down to trust in a relationship.
It all comes down to how much effort you put in.
In the end, it comes down to your choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depend on - to rely on or be decided by something
  • Synonym: to hinge on - to depend entirely on something
