to hammer away at

1. VERB

/ ˈhæmɚ əˈweɪ æt / (AmE), / ˈhamə əˈweɪ at / (BrE)
to work persistently and with great effort on a task or problem, often over a long period
work persistently on something

Examples

She continued to hammer away at her thesis until every chapter was perfect.
Every morning, he would hammer away at the piano, practicing for hours.
Despite setbacks, the team kept hammering away at the project until they succeeded.

Synset Examples

He would hammer away at the keyboard all night.
They hammered away at the details until everything was perfect.
She continued to hammer away at her studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to continue in a course of action despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop making an effort; to quit
