to count on

1. VERB

/ kaʊnt ɑn / (AmE), / kaʊnt ɒn / (BrE)
to expect or rely on something, usually considering it likely or probable
expect or rely on as probable

Examples

You can always count on her to tell the truth.
I wouldn't count on the bus arriving on time.
They counted on good weather for the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rely on - depend confidently; trust in someone or something
  • Synonym: trust - believe in the reliability of someone or something

2. VERB

/ kaʊnt ɑn / (AmE), / kaʊnt ɒn / (BrE)
to rely on someone or something to do what is needed or expected
rely on; depend on

Examples

You can always count on her for honest feedback.
I wouldn't count on the weather staying dry all day.
They counted on their friends to help with the move.
