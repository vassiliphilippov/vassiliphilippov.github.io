to tell off

1. VERB

/ tɛl ɔf / (AmE), / tɛl ɒf / (BrE)
to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong; to reprimand.
reprimand verbally

Examples

She was told off by her boss for being late again.
If you don't do your homework, the teacher will tell off the whole class.
His mother told off the children for making so much noise.

Synset Examples

She told the misbehaving student off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - to tell someone officially or seriously that something they have done is wrong
  • Synonym: scold - to speak angrily to someone for doing something wrong
