to whip through

1. VERB

/ wɪp θruː / (AmE), / wɪp θruː / (BrE)
to deal with or complete something very quickly and efficiently
finish something quickly

Examples

I managed to whip through three chapters of the book before dinner.
She whipped through the paperwork in just half an hour.
They usually whip through their chores so they can go out and play.

Synset Examples

We whipped through the last papers that we had to read before the weekend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush through - to deal with something quickly, often superficially
  • Degree Lower: plow through - to work through something, often with effort, but steadily
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɪp θru / (AmE), / wɪp θruː / (BrE)
to read, do, or complete something very quickly and efficiently
complete quickly and efficiently

Examples

I can whip through this stack of paperwork before the meeting starts.
She whipped through her morning emails and moved on to the next task.
They whip through their chores every Saturday so they can enjoy the rest of the day.

Synset Examples

I can whip through this book in a day.
She managed to whip through her tasks before lunch.
He can whip through his emails in no time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush through - to do something quickly, often without enough attention to detail
  • Synonym: to breeze through - to accomplish something with ease and speed

Synset POS: None
