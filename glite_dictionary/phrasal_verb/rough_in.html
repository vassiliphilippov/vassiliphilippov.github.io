to rough in

1. VERB

/ rʌf ɪn / (AmE), / rʌf ɪn / (BrE)
to make a preliminary or unfinished version of something, especially as a basic outline or plan
make a basic or preliminary version

Examples

The architect will rough in the floor plan before meeting with the clients.
She roughed in the sculpture's shape using clay before adding the details.
We need to rough in the schedule so we have an idea of the project's timeline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sketch out - to outline or give a basic form to something
  • Antonym: to finalize - to complete or make final
