to scrape through

1. VERB

/ skreɪp θru / (AmE), / skreɪp θruː / (BrE)
to succeed in doing something, but only just; to barely manage to achieve a passing result or minimum standard
barely succeed; just pass

Examples

After a tough semester, she managed to scrape through her final exams.
The team scraped through to the next round by a single point.
He was relieved to scrape through the interview and get the job offer.

Synset Examples

I managed to scrape through the exam with a passing grade.
She scraped through by studying last minute.
He barely scraped through the interview process.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pass - to succeed in an exam or test
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving a passing result
