to slip out

1. VERB

/ slɪp aʊt / (AmE), / slɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly or secretly, especially to avoid being noticed
leave quietly or secretly

Examples

During the crowded party, Emily managed to slip out without anyone noticing her departure.
He tried to slip out of the office early so his boss wouldn't see him leave.
Several guests slipped out before the speeches even began.

Synset Examples

The lecture was boring and many students slipped out when the instructor turned towards the blackboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sneak out - leave a place secretly or quietly to avoid detection
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02080274-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ slɪp aʊt / (AmE), / slɪp aʊt / (BrE)
To leave a place quietly and unobtrusively, or for words or information to escape unintentionally.
leave quietly or be said unintentionally

Examples

She slipped out of the crowded room without anyone noticing.
The secret slipped out during the conversation before I could stop myself.
He slipped out after dinner to make a private phone call.

Synset Examples

He quietly slipped out of the meeting early.
His excuse slipped out like butter on a bald monkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sneak out - to leave a place quietly and secretly
  • Synonym: to blurt out - to say something suddenly and inadvertently
