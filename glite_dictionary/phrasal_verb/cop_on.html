to cop on

1. VERB

/ kɑp ɑn / (AmE), / kɒp ɒn / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British and Irish English) To become aware of or to understand something; to realize what is happening or expected.
realize or understand (informal)
informal british

Examples

It took her a while to cop on to the joke everyone was telling.
You need to cop on and take your responsibilities seriously.
He finally copped on when he saw everyone laughing.

Synset Examples

It took her a while to cop on to the joke.
You need to cop on and start studying harder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to understand what is meant or happening
  • Synonym: to wise up - to become more aware, especially of how things really are

Etymology

From slang use of 'cop' meaning 'to catch, notice' (possibly from Latin 'capere', to seize).

2. VERB

/ kɑp ɑn / (AmE), / kɒp ɒn / (BrE)
to become aware of or realize something, often belatedly
realize or become aware (informal)
informal british

Examples

He finally copped on to the fact that he was being used.
It took me a while to cop on that they were joking.
She didn't cop on until it was too late.

Synset Examples

He finally copped on to the fact that he was being used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wise up - become aware or more informed, especially about something unpleasant

Etymology

Colloquial use of 'cop' meaning 'to catch or seize,' with 'on' as a particle. Common in Irish and British English.
