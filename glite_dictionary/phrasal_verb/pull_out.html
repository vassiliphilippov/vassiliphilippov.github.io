to pull out

1. VERB

/ pʊl aʊt / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from a place or position, often with some force or effort; also to obtain something abstract, such as information, with effort
remove with force or effort

Examples

She had to pull out a stubborn nail from the wall.
The dentist will pull out your wisdom tooth if it's causing pain.
It's amazing what secrets journalists can pull out of public records.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
