to sound out

1. VERB

/ saʊnd aʊt / (AmE), / saʊnd aʊt / (BrE)
to try to discover someone's opinions or intentions, often by asking indirect questions
discreetly learn opinions or intentions

Examples

She decided to sound out her colleagues before proposing any changes.
The manager tried to sound out the new team member’s attitude towards remote work.
He spent weeks sounding out voters about their views on the policy.

Synset Examples

I have to sound out the new professor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - to investigate or explore, especially by asking questions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00810733-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ saʊnd aʊt / (AmE), / saʊnd aʊt / (BrE)
to pronounce the sounds of letters or syllables in a word in order, typically to help read or spell it
pronounce letters or syllables in sequence

Examples

Can you sound out this word for me?
The teacher asked the children to sound out each syllable of the new word.
She sounded out every letter to help her read the sentence.

Synset Examples

She pronounces French words in a funny way
I cannot say ‘zip wire’
Can the child sound out this complicated word?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pronounce - to make the sound of a word or letter

Synset POS: v
