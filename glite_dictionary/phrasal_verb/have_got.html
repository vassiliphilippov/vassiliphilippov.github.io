to have got

1. VERB

/ hæv ɡɑt / (AmE), / hæv ɡɒt / (BrE)
To own, possess, or hold something; to have or possess a quality, object, or amount.
possess or own something
informal

Examples

I've got a new car parked outside.
She has got a lot of experience in marketing.
They have got plenty of time to finish their assignment.

Synset Examples

She has $1,000 in the bank
He has got two beautiful daughters
She holds a Master's degree from Harvard
I have two years left
I don't have any money left

Related Terms
