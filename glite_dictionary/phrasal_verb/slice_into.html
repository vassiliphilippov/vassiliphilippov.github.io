to slice into

1. VERB

/ slaɪs ˈɪntu / (AmE), / slaɪs ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to move forcefully or smoothly into or through something with a slicing motion
move into/through with slicing motion

Examples

The diver sliced into the water with barely a splash.
The chef's knife sliced into the ripe tomato easily.
Her words sliced into him, revealing the sharpness of her anger.

Synset Examples

His hand sliced through the air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut into - move into by making a cut or incision
