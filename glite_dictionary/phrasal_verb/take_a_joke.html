to take a joke

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə dʒoʊk / (AmE), / teɪk ə dʒəʊk / (BrE)
to not be upset or offended when others make a joke about you
not be offended by a joke about oneself

Examples

She can always take a joke, no matter how silly it is.
You need to learn how to take a joke instead of getting upset.
He laughed and proved he could take a joke with good humor.

Synset Examples

Can't you take a joke?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to laugh it off - to treat something as unimportant or funny, especially criticism or an insult
  • Antonym: be offended - to feel hurt or upset by something said or done

2. VERB

/ teɪk ə ʤoʊk / (AmE), / teɪk ə ʤəʊk / (BrE)
to not be offended when someone makes a humorous or teasing remark about you
not be offended by a joke

Examples

She's able to take a joke and doesn't get upset when people tease her.
If you can't take a joke, maybe you shouldn't make them about others.
He was in a bad mood and couldn't take a joke last night.

Synset Examples

She laughed, knowing she could take a joke.
He can always take a joke, no matter what.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense of humor - the ability to appreciate or express what is amusing
  • Antonym: become offended - to react negatively to a comment or joke
