to jump on

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɑn / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒn / (BrE)
to get up onto the back of something, especially quickly or suddenly
get up on the back of

Examples

He quickly jumped on his horse and rode away.
The children tried to jump on the donkey for a ride.
She laughed as she jumped on the back of her friend's bike.

Synset Examples

mount a horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mount - to get up on something, especially an animal or bike
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01927367-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɑn / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒn / (BrE)
to take advantage of or respond to something very quickly
respond or act quickly to take advantage

Examples

Investors were quick to jump on the company’s new stock offering.
She tends to jump on every new trend that appears online.
Mark didn't jump on the job offer, preferring to think about it first.

Synset Examples

Don't jump on every opportunity that comes your way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize on - to take quick advantage of an opportunity or remark
  • Synonym: pounce on - to immediately take advantage of something
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to take advantage of an opportunity

Synset POS: None
