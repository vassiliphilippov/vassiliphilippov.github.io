to collaborate with

1. VERB

/ kəˈlæbəˌreɪt wɪθ / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪt wɪð / (BrE)
to work together with one or more people or organizations, especially on a specific project or activity
work jointly with others

Examples

The scientist decided to collaborate with researchers from other universities to broaden the study.
Many authors collaborate with illustrators to create children's books.
Our company is collaborating with several nonprofits on this community project.

Synset Examples

The rapper collaborated with several artists on his latest album.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperate with - to work together for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'collaborare' (to work together) + English preposition 'with'.
