to crank out

1. VERB

/ kræŋk aʊt / (AmE), / kræŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to produce (something) quickly and routinely, especially in large quantities, often without much attention to quality
produce routinely and quickly, often in quantity

Examples

The factory used to crank out thousands of toys every week during the holidays.
Some movie studios crank out sequels just to keep cash flowing in.
She was able to crank out three essays in one night before the deadline.

Synset Examples

We have to crank out publications in order to receive funding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to churn out - produce large quantities of something quickly, often without much quality

Etymology

From 'crank', referring to turning a mechanical handle, suggesting repetitive and mechanical action.
