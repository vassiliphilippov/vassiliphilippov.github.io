to overdose on

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊvərˌdoʊs ɑn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvədəʊs ɒn / (BrE)
to consume an excessive or dangerous amount of a substance, usually resulting in harmful effects
consume too much of something

Examples

Doctors warn not to overdose on vitamin supplements, as doing so may cause serious health problems.
He overdosed on caffeine and felt jittery for hours afterward.
It's possible to overdose on certain medicines if you don't follow the prescribed dosage.

Synset Examples

Don't overdose on supplements; they can be harmful.
She was careful not to overdose on painkillers after surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdose - to take an excessive and dangerous dose of a drug or substance

Etymology

Formed from 'overdose' (from 'over-' meaning excessively and 'dose' from Latin 'dosis'), plus the preposition 'on'.
