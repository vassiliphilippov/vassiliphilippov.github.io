to deck out

1. VERB

/ dɛk aʊt / (AmE), / dɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to dress or decorate oneself or someone else in special or elaborate clothing or accessories, often for an event or to look attractive
to dress or decorate elaborately

Examples

The children were decked out in colorful costumes for the parade.
He decided to deck out the living room with banners and balloons for her birthday.
She likes to deck out in her finest jewelry for special occasions.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or special clothes
  • Synonym: to adorn - to decorate or add beauty to
  • Antonym: to strip down - to remove clothing or decorations

Etymology

From 'deck' meaning 'to adorn', likely from Dutch 'dekken' (to cover), combined with 'out' as an intensifier.
