to cross out

1. VERB

/ krɔs aʊt / (AmE), / krɒs aʊt / (BrE)
to remove (a word, name, etc.) from a list or document by drawing a line through it
remove from a list by drawing a line through

Examples

Please cross out any items you no longer need from the list.
She carefully crossed out the incorrect answer on her exam.
Whenever a task is completed, we cross it out on the board.

Synset Examples

Cross the name of the dead person off the list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delete - to remove or erase (something written or printed)
  • Synonym: to erase - to rub or scrape out; remove written or drawn marks

2. VERB

/ krɔs aʊt / (AmE), / krɒs aʊt / (BrE)
to draw a line through a word, sentence, or item to show that it is incorrect, completed, or should not be considered
draw a line through to remove

Examples

Remember to cross out any outdated information before submitting the report.
If you make a mistake, simply cross out the wrong word and write the correct one above.
The teacher asked the students to cross out the answer they thought was incorrect.

Synset Examples

You should cross out any mistakes in the document.
He had to cross out several names from the list.
Please cross out the incorrect answer on the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delete - to remove written or printed matter
  • Synonym: to erase - to remove written marks from a surface

Synset POS: None
