to spring for

1. VERB

/ sprɪŋ fɔr / (AmE), / sprɪŋ fə / (BrE)
to pay for something, especially as a treat for others or as an unexpected gesture
pay for (something) as a treat
informal american

Examples

If you invite everyone, are you prepared to spring for dinner for the whole group?
She decided to spring for a new coffee machine for the office.
Whenever we go out, he always offers to spring for drinks.

Synset Examples

He'll likely spring for dessert if we ask nicely.
She agreed to spring for the tickets to the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay for - to give money in exchange for something
