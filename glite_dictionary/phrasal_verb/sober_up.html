to sober up

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊbər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbə ʌp / (BrE)
to become more realistic or sensible, especially after acting carelessly or fantasizing
become more realistic or sensible

Examples

After two failed business ventures, he finally sobered up and made a practical plan.
It took a serious warning from her doctor for her to sober up about her unhealthy habits.
Once the excitement faded, the team sobered up and realized how much work was still ahead.

Synset Examples

After thinking about the potential consequences of his plan, he sobered up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to one's senses - to regain mental clarity or become sensible
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊbɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbə ʌp / (BrE)
to become sober after having consumed too much alcohol or to help someone else become sober.
become sober after drinking alcohol

Examples

It took him hours to sober up after the party.
Drinking some water and resting can help you sober up quickly.
She waited at the café for her friend to sober up before driving home.

Synset Examples

Keep him in bed until he sobers up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sober - not affected by alcohol
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00148938-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsoʊbər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbə ʌp / (BrE)
to become less drunk or to recover from the effects of alcohol; to return to a normal state after drinking
become less drunk; recover from alcohol

Examples

He drank a lot last night, but after some coffee, he began to sober up.
It usually takes a few hours to sober up after a party.
You should sober up before trying to drive home.

Synset Examples

Keep him in bed until he sobers up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk
  • Antonym: to intoxicate - to make someone drunk; to cause intoxication
Source: WordNet
