to lay to rest

1. VERB

/ leɪ tu rɛst / (AmE), / leɪ tʊ rɛst / (BrE)
to bury someone who has died, usually as part of a funeral ceremony
bury a deceased person

Examples

After a moving ceremony, her family finally laid to rest their beloved grandmother.
He was laid to rest in the small cemetery near his childhood home.
Crowds gathered to lay to rest the soldier who had served his country.

Synset Examples

Stalin was buried behind the Kremlin wall on Red Square
The pharaohs were entombed in the pyramids
My grandfather was laid to rest last Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bury - to put a dead body into the ground
  • Synonym: to inter - to place a dead body in a grave or tomb
  • Antonym: to cremate - to burn the body of a person who has died

2. VERB

/ leɪ tə rɛst / (AmE), / leɪ tə rɛst / (BrE)
to finally stop a worry, rumor, or feeling by dealing with it, or to bury someone after death
settle concerns or bury someone

Examples

The new evidence helped lay to rest all speculation about the cause of the fire.
She hoped the memorial service would lay to rest her lingering grief.
His confession finally laid to rest the family's unanswered questions.

Synset Examples

The investigation aimed to lay the rumors to rest.
They held a ceremony to lay her memory to rest.
He finally managed to lay his doubts to rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put to bed - to bring something to an end, especially a problem or issue
  • Synonym: resolve - to find a solution to a problem or dispute
