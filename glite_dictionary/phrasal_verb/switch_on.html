to switch on

1. VERB

/ swɪtʃ ɑn / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to cause a device or system to start working by moving a switch or pressing a button
activate a device by switching

Examples

Could you switch on the heater? It's getting cold in here.
He switched on the computer and waited for it to start up.
She always switches on the lights as soon as she enters the room.

Synset Examples

switch on the light
turn on the stereo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn on - to cause to operate by flipping a switch
  • Antonym: to switch off - to stop a device or system by moving a switch

2. VERB

/ swɪtʃ ɑn / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to cause a device to begin operating by pressing a button or moving a switch
activate or turn on a device

Examples

Could you switch on the lights? It's getting dark in here.
He forgot to switch on his phone after the meeting.
She always switches on her computer as soon as she gets to work.

Synset Examples

Make sure to switch on the security system before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to switch off - to cause a device to stop operating by pressing a button or moving a switch
  • Synonym: to turn on - to start a device or machine
