to draw near

1. VERB

/ drɔː nɪr / (AmE), / drɔː nɪə / (BrE)
to move closer in space or time; to approach
move closer; approach

Examples

As midnight began to draw near, the crowd grew silent in anticipation.
Winter is drawing near, so the days are getting shorter and colder.
She felt nervous as the final exam drew near.

Synset Examples

We were approaching our destination
They are drawing near
The enemy army came nearer and nearer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to move closer to someone or something
