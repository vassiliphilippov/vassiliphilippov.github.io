to dwindle away

1. VERB

/ ˈdwɪndəl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈdwɪndəl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually become smaller, fewer, or less; to diminish or decrease slowly until nothing remains
decrease gradually until little remains

Examples

Over the years, their wealth dwindled away until they had nothing left.
If we keep spending at this rate, our resources will dwindle away quickly.
The enthusiasm for the project dwindled away as the months passed.

Synset Examples

Her savings dwindled down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwindle - to become gradually less or smaller
