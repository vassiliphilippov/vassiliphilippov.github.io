to give hell

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv hɛl / (AmE), / ɡɪv hɛl / (BrE)
to criticize, reprimand, or punish someone severely, often with anger or intensity
to criticize or punish severely
informal

Examples

If you miss another deadline, your boss will give hell to the whole team.
My parents really gave hell to my brother when he crashed the car.
The coach is known to give hell to players who don't put in enough effort.

Synset Examples

The teacher will give you hell for not studying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to speak angrily to someone for doing something wrong
  • Antonym: to let off - to punish someone less severely than expected
