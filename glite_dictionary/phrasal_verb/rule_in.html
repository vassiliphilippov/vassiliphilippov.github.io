to rule in

1. VERB

/ ruːl ɪn / (AmE), / ruːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of a judge, arbiter, or authority) to decide formally that something is to be included or considered, especially after applying proper procedures or rules.
to decide to include; judge in favor

Examples

The judge will rule in favor of the plaintiff if the evidence is convincing.
After much discussion, the committee decided to rule in the proposal for further consideration.
Doctors could not rule in a diagnosis of diabetes based on the initial tests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to rule out - to decide something is not possible or will not be included; to exclude after consideration
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01150028-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ruːl ɪn / (AmE), / ruːl ɪn / (BrE)
to accept or allow something, especially by including it as a possibility in a process of consideration or selection
accept or include as a possibility

Examples

The committee decided to rule in several of the proposals after much discussion.
Doctors could not rule in or rule out any specific diagnosis at this stage.
When reviewing the applications, we should rule in anyone who meets the minimum requirements.

Synset Examples

The committee decided to rule in several new proposals.
Experts will rule in the final decision next week.
We need to rule in all possible candidates for the job.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to rule out - to exclude as a possibility or reject from consideration
