to kick loose

1. VERB

/ kɪk lus / (AmE), / kɪk luːs / (BrE)
to free oneself from an obligation, commitment, or restriction
free oneself from obligations

Examples

Sometimes you need to kick loose from your daily routine and try something new.
She finally kicked loose from her stressful job and took a long vacation.
After years of commitments, he wanted to kick loose and live more freely.

Synset Examples

He managed to kick loose from his responsibilities for a day.
