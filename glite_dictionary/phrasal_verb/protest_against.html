to protest against

1. VERB

/ proʊˈtɛst əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / prəˈtɛst əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to publicly express strong objection or disapproval about something
to express strong objection or disapproval

Examples

Hundreds of students protested against the school's decision to cut funding for the arts program.
Environmental groups have protested against the construction of the new highway through the forest.
People around the world continue to protest against racial injustice.

Synset Examples

Many people protested against the use of 'kung flu' in media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: object to - to express or feel disapproval or opposition
