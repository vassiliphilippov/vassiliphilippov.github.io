to play big

1. VERB

/ pleɪ bɪɡ / (AmE), / pleɪ bɪɡ / (BrE)
to act confidently and assertively, often by taking significant risks or bold actions
act boldly or take major risks
informal

Examples

If you want real success, you have to play big and embrace new challenges.
She decided to play big by presenting her ambitious proposal to the board.
The company aims to play big in the international market this year.

Synset Examples

They always try to play big during negotiations.
He's decided to play big and invest in the new startup.
To succeed in this market, you need to play big.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to play it safe - to act cautiously and avoid risks
