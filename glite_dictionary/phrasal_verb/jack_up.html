to jack up

1. VERB

/ dʒæk ʌp / (AmE), / dʒæk ʌp / (BrE)
to lift something, especially a vehicle, off the ground using a jack or similar device
lift using a jack or similar device

Examples

Before changing the tire, make sure to jack up the car securely.
The mechanic jacked up the truck to inspect its undercarriage.
He forgot to jack up the vehicle before removing the flat tire.

Synset Examples

jack up the car so you can change the tire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - to raise to a higher position
  • Antonym: lower - to move or let something go down

Etymology

From the noun 'jack' (a mechanical lifting device) + 'up'.

2. VERB

/ dʒæk ʌp / (AmE), / dʒæk ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or raise something, especially by a large amount or suddenly
increase or raise significantly
informal

Examples

The landlord decided to jack up the rent without warning.
They often jack up the prices during the holidays.
Management plans to jack up production to meet increased demand.

Synset Examples

We need to jack up our marketing efforts this quarter.
I need to jack this up to make it more exciting.
He decided to jack up the prices for the concert tickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to increase the amount, level, or strength of something
  • Antonym: lower - to make something less in amount, level, or strength

Etymology

Originally from the physical act of using a jack (lifting device) to raise something; figurative use developed in 20th century.
