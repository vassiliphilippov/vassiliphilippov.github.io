to ride off

1. VERB

/ raɪd ɔf / (AmE), / raɪd ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place by riding, especially on a horse, bicycle, or motorcycle
depart by riding (horse, bike, etc.)

Examples

After saying goodbye, he rode off into the sunset.
The sheriff watched as the outlaws rode off down the dusty trail.
She quickly mounted her bicycle and rode off before anyone could stop her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ride away - to leave a place by riding (synonymous phrasal verb)
