to carry forward

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to transfer an amount or item from one time period to the next, especially in financial or accounting contexts
move an item to a later period

Examples

Any unused vacation days will be carried forward to the next calendar year.
The accountant needs to carry forward last quarter's losses to this year's records.
If the expense can't be claimed this year, you may be able to carry it forward and use it later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - to move something from one place, person, or period to another
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02017279-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to continue something or move it to a later time or situation, often using previous knowledge or results.
continue or move to a later time or situation

Examples

We decided to carry forward the remaining tasks to next week.
You should carry forward your experience from this project to future assignments.
Any unused vacation days will be carried forward to the next year.

Synset Examples

She decided to carry forward her ideas despite the criticism.
We can carry forward the lessons learned to future projects.
Let's carry forward this discussion to next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something to a later time
  • Synonym: to defer - to put off an action or event to a later time
  • Antonym: finish - to bring something to an end, opposite of carrying forward
