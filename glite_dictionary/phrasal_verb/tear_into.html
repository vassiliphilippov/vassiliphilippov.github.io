to tear into

1. VERB

/ tɛr ˈɪntu / (AmE), / tɪə ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to attack or criticize someone or something with great force or energy
attack or criticize violently

Examples

The critics really tore into the new movie, calling it a disaster.
As soon as he heard the news, he tore into his opponent with angry accusations.
She tears into her work each morning with incredible energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay into - to attack or criticize someone forcefully
  • Antonym: go easy on - to treat someone in a gentle way
