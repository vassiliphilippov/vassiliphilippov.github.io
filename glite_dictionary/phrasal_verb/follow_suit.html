to follow suit

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑloʊ ˈsut / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ˈsuːt / (BrE)
to do the same thing as someone else, especially after they have set an example or taken an action first
do the same as someone else

Examples

After the team introduced flexible work hours, other departments decided to follow suit.
When one student began recycling, many of her classmates chose to follow suit.
If the manager wears casual clothes on Friday, employees usually follow suit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - copy or mimic someone's actions or behavior
  • Antonym: to break the mold - to do something differently from what others are doing, not to conform

Etymology

Originally from card games, where following suit means playing a card of the same suit as the first card played in the trick.
