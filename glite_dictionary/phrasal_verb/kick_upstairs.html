to kick upstairs

1. VERB

/ kɪk ʌpˈstɛrz / (AmE), / kɪk ʌpˈstɛəz / (BrE)
to promote someone to a higher position, often as a way of removing them from an important role or sidestepping them
promote to a higher position (often to sideline)

Examples

After years in management, he was finally kicked upstairs to a board position.
Rumor has it they'll kick upstairs the department head to give someone else a chance.
She felt she was being kicked upstairs rather than genuinely rewarded with the new role.

Synset Examples

John was kicked upstairs when a replacement was hired
Women tend not to advance in the major law firms
I got promoted after many years of hard work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to raise someone to a higher position or rank
