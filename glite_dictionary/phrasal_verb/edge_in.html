to edge in

1. VERB

/ ɛdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / ɛdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to move gradually or carefully into a space, often by pushing or maneuvering oneself onward
move or push one's way into a space

Examples

She tried to edge in between the two passengers on the crowded train.
He managed to edge in a few words during the heated debate.
The cat edged in through the barely open door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeeze in - to manage to fit or enter into a limited space
  • Synonym: to push in - to enter a space by pushing
