to give notice

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
to formally inform someone of something, especially in a professional or official context
formally inform someone

Examples

She decided to give notice to her landlord before moving out.
Employees must give notice if they intend to leave the company.
You are required to give notice of any changes in your address.

Synset Examples

I advised him that the rent was due

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to notify - to formally tell someone about something
  • Synonym: to advise - to inform someone about something, especially officially

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
to formally inform an employee that their employment is being terminated; to dismiss from a job or position
formally dismiss from employment

Examples

After several warnings, the manager finally gave notice to the unproductive employee.
The company decided to give notice to several workers during the restructuring.
When he gave notice, he ensured all the paperwork was completed properly.

Synset Examples

The boss fired his secretary today
The company terminated 25% of its workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from their job
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job or position

Synset POS: v
