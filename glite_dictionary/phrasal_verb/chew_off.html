to chew off

1. VERB

/ tʃu ɔf / (AmE), / tʃuː ɒf / (BrE)
to remove, detach, or separate something by biting or gnawing
remove by biting or gnawing

Examples

The dog tried to chew off the leash when left alone in the yard.
She had to chew off the tip of the pen to remove the cap.
During the hike, he nearly chewed off a piece of rope stuck to his boot.

Synset Examples

He managed to chew off the stubborn label on the jar.
She tried to chew off the excess plastic from the package.
He had to chew off part of the tough steak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bite off - to sever by biting
