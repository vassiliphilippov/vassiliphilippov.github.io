to work back

1. VERB

/ wɝk bæk / (AmE), / wɜːk bæk / (BrE)
to plan or calculate steps in reverse order from a deadline or endpoint, or to revisit earlier items or stages in a process
plan or retrace steps in reverse order

Examples

We need to work back from the project deadline to schedule each stage appropriately.
She had to work back through the list of clients to see who was missing.
If we work back from today, we have just enough time to finish the proposal.

Synset Examples

Let’s work back from the deadline to plan our tasks.
I need to work back through my emails.
I’ll work back to the office after lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work through - to deal with or complete tasks methodically
