to carry on

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæri ɒn / (BrE)
to continue speaking after a pause or interruption
continue speaking after a pause

Examples

Despite the noise outside, she decided to carry on with her lecture.
He paused for a moment, then chose to carry on with his explanation.
Please carry on; I'm listening to what you have to say.

Synset Examples

‘I know it's hard,’ he continued, ‘but there is no choice’
carry on — pretend we are not in the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to keep doing something without stopping
  • Antonym: stop - to cease speaking or acting

2. VERB

/ ˈkæri ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæri ɒn / (BrE)
to behave in a silly, excited, or improper way; to misbehave or make a noisy fuss
behave in a silly or improper way
informal

Examples

The children began to carry on as soon as their parents left the room.
Stop carrying on and pay attention to the teacher, please.
She was carrying on so much at the party that people asked her to calm down.

Synset Examples

The children acted up when they were not bored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to act up - to behave badly or cause trouble
  • Antonym: to settle down - to become calm and behave properly

3. VERB

/ ˈkæri ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæri ɒn / (BrE)
to manage, continue, or conduct an activity, especially a business or other organized task
manage or conduct (an activity)

Examples

Despite the setbacks, she decided to carry on her family business.
They will carry on the negotiations until a solution is found.
The store has carried on its tradition for over a century.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct - to organize and carry out (an activity)
  • Synonym: run - to be in charge of or manage (a business or activity)
