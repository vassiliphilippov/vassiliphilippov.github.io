to weigh against

1. VERB

/ weɪ əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / weɪ əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to be considered as a disadvantage in comparison to something else
be a disadvantage compared to

Examples

His lack of formal education may weigh against him in the interview process.
The company's recent financial troubles could weigh against its chances of winning the contract.
While her skills are impressive, her limited experience might weigh against her in the selection criteria.

Synset Examples

Her experience will weigh against the other candidates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: count against - to be considered to someone’s disadvantage
