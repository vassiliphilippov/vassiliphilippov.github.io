to issue a subpoena

1. VERB

/ ˈɪʃu ə səˈpinə / (AmE), / ˈɪsjuː ə səˈpiːnə / (BrE)
to formally order someone to appear in court or provide evidence through a subpoena
formally order to appear in court
legal

Examples

The court decided to issue a subpoena to obtain the witness's testimony.
If necessary, the agency will issue a subpoena for financial records during the investigation.
Their lawyer threatened to issue a subpoena if the documents were not produced voluntarily.

Synset Examples

The company issued a subpoena for the missing documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summon - to officially call someone to appear, especially in court

Etymology

From Latin 'sub poena' (under penalty), via legal Latin phrase 'subpoena' (formal writ).
