to cheer up

1. VERB

/ tʃɪr ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to become or make someone feel happier or more cheerful
to become or make happier

Examples

He tried to cheer up after hearing the good news.
A funny movie always helps me cheer up when I'm feeling sad.
They brought her flowers to cheer up her spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brighten up - to become or make more cheerful or lively
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become or make less excited or agitated

2. VERB

/ tʃɪr ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone feel happier or become happier; to improve someone's mood
make happier; improve mood

Examples

He brought flowers to cheer up his sick friend.
After hearing the good news, she began to cheer up quickly.
Let's watch a funny movie tonight to cheer up everyone.

Synset Examples

She tried to cheer up the disappointed child when he failed to win the spelling bee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: console - to comfort someone who is unhappy
  • Synonym: to encourage - to give someone support and confidence
  • Antonym: to depress - to make someone feel sad or hopeless

3. VERB

/ tʃɪr ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone feel happier, or to become happier, especially after feeling sad or disappointed
to make or become happier

Examples

She brought him flowers to cheer up after his surgery.
A good laugh always helps me cheer up when I'm feeling down.
They played her favorite song to cheer up the mood in the room.

Synset Examples

Meg always knows how to cheer everyone up.
She always knew how to cheer him up.
My mater always knows how to cheer me up.
A good movie can really cheer me up.
Mum always knows how to cheer me up.
Her amio always knows how to cheer her up.
She tried to cheer up her friend after the breakup.
Let’s get ice cream to cheer you up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brighten up - to become or make happier or more cheerful
  • Synonym: lift (someone's) spirits - to make someone feel happier

Etymology

From 'cheer' (to make glad) + 'up'; the phrasal verb form dates from the 19th century.
