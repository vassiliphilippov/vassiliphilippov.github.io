to bridle at

1. VERB

/ ˈbraɪdəl æt / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdəl æt / (BrE)
to show anger or take offense at something; to react with indignation or resentment
show anger or indignation at something

Examples

She began to bridle at his repeated criticism, her patience wearing thin.
Many employees bridled at the new workplace restrictions imposed by management.
He tried not to bridle at her blunt response, but his annoyance was obvious.

Synset Examples

She bristled at his insolent remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bristle at - react with anger or defensiveness at something
  • Synonym: take offense - to feel insulted or upset by something

Etymology

From the noun 'bridle' (from Old English *brīdel*, a device for controlling a horse), used figuratively as a verb since the late Middle Ages.
