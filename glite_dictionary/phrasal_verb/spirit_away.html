to spirit away

1. VERB

/ ˈspɪrɪt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to take someone or something away quickly and secretly, especially in a mysterious or magical way
carry off secretly or mysteriously

Examples

It is said that the documents were spirited away before the police arrived.
The magician appeared to spirit away the rabbit from the hat, astonishing the children.
She was spirited away to a safe location under the cover of darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abduct - to take someone away illegally by force or deception
  • Antonym: return - to bring or send back; opposite of taking away

2. VERB

/ ˈspɪr.ɪt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈspɪr.ɪt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to take someone or something away quickly and secretly, especially in a mysterious manner
carry away quickly and secretly

Examples

The documents were spirited away before investigators arrived.
She was spirited away to a secret location by her protectors.
He spirits away the evidence every time trouble is near.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abduct - to take someone away illegally by force or deception
  • Synonym: to kidnap - to seize and carry away a person by force, often to obtain a ransom

Etymology

From the noun 'spirit' (meaning a supernatural being) and 'away'; originally referring to magical or mysterious removal.
