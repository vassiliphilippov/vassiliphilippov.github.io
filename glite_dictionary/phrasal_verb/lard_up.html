to lard up

1. VERB

/ lɑrd ʌp / (AmE), / lɑːd ʌp / (BrE)
to add fat or rich flavor to food, making it excessively fatty or rich
to make food excessively fatty or rich

Examples

Please don't lard up the potatoes with too much butter.
They decided to lard up the casserole with bacon to intensify the flavor.
The chef warned not to lard up the sauce or it would overpower the dish.

Synset Examples

He will lard up the meat for extra flavor.
They plan to lard up the cake with frosting.
Don't lard up the dish too much or it will be greasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrich - to improve the quality of something, especially food, by adding good ingredients
  • Degree Higher: grease - to make food greasy or oily

Etymology

From 'lard' (to cover or stuff with fat), from Old French 'larder', based on Latin 'laridum' (bacon, fat).
