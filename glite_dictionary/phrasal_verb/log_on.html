to log on

1. VERB

/ lɑɡ ɑn / (AmE), / lɒɡ ɒn / (BrE)
to enter a computer system or website by providing the necessary credentials, such as a username and password
enter a computer system or website

Examples

Please log on to your account using your username and password.
She forgot her password and couldn't log on to the network this morning.
After you log on, you will see the main dashboard.

Synset Examples

Have you logged in lately?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to log in - to enter a computer or system by identifying oneself
  • Antonym: to log off - to exit a computer system or website
  • Antonym: to log out - to exit a computer system or website

Etymology

Extension of 'log' (record of activity), from earlier use in computing jargon, alluding to recording a user's entry.
