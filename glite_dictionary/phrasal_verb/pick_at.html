to pick at

1. VERB

/ pɪk æt / (AmE), / pɪk æt / (BrE)
to eat only small amounts of food, often without interest or appetite
eat without appetite; nibble at food

Examples

He didn't seem hungry and just picked at his lunch for an hour.
She picks at her dinner whenever she's feeling upset.
The child was picking at his vegetables instead of eating them properly.

Synset Examples

The anorexic girl just picks at her food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nibble - to take small bites of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01175953-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɪk æt / (AmE), / pɪk æt / (BrE)
to repeatedly criticize or find fault with something or someone, often in a petty or unhelpful way
criticize repeatedly or in a petty way

Examples

She tends to pick at every little mistake I make, which is exhausting.
Instead of offering solutions, he just picks at everyone's ideas during meetings.
If you constantly pick at your friends' flaws, you'll push them away.

Synset Examples

She disparaged her student's efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disparage - to speak about someone or something in a derogatory way
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pɪk æt / (AmE), / pɪk æt / (BrE)
to pluck or pull at something with the fingers, especially repeatedly or nervously
pluck or pull at with the fingers

Examples

She nervously began to pick at the hem of her skirt during the interview.
Stop picking at that scab or it might get infected.
He absentmindedly picked at the label on his water bottle while he listened.

Synset Examples

She picked nervously at the buttons of her blouse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fiddle with - to touch or manipulate something, typically in a restless or nervous way
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ pɪk æt / (AmE), / pɪk æt / (BrE)
to eat only a small amount of food or to do something in a slow or reluctant way, often without enthusiasm
eat reluctantly; do something reluctantly

Examples

Whenever she feels upset, she only picks at her meals without really eating.
He kept picking at his project instead of finishing it quickly.
Stop picking at your food and eat something substantial.

Synset Examples

Don't pick at your assignments, just finish them.
He tends to pick at his food when he's nervous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nibble - to take small bites of food, usually in a hesitant or uninterested way
