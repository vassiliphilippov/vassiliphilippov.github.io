to run low on gas

1. VERB

/ rʌn loʊ ɑn ɡæs / (AmE), / rʌn ləʊ ɒn ɡæs / (BrE)
to have nearly exhausted one's supply of fuel, especially in a vehicle
have little fuel left
american

Examples

We need to stop soon because we run low on gas and there isn’t a station nearby.
If you run low on gas during your trip, it’s a good idea to fill up at the next opportunity.
She realized she was running low on gas just as the warning light came on.

Synset Examples

The car began to chug as it ran low on gas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: run out of gas - to completely use up one's supply of fuel
  • Antonym: to refuel - to fill up with fuel again
