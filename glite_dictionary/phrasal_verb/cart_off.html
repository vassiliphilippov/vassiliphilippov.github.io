to cart off

1. VERB

/ kɑrt ɑf / (AmE), / kɑːt ɒf / (BrE)
to take something or someone away, typically using a vehicle and often with little ceremony or reluctance
remove and transport using a vehicle

Examples

The city workers carted off the fallen branches after the storm.
Police carted off the evidence from the crime scene.
He was so exhausted at the end of the race that they had to cart off his bicycle for him.

Synset Examples

They carted off the old furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to haul away - to transport or carry off, especially with effort
  • Antonym: to bring - to take or carry to a place
