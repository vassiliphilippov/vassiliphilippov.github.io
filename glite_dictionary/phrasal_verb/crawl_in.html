to crawl in

1. VERB

/ krɔl ɪn / (AmE), / krɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to go to bed, especially in a slow or tired manner
go to bed (often tiredly)
informal

Examples

After a long day at work, I usually crawl in around midnight.
She was so exhausted that she could barely crawl in before falling asleep.
Most nights, he crawls in long after everyone else is asleep.

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn in - to go to bed
  • Synonym: to hit the hay - to go to bed (idiom)
