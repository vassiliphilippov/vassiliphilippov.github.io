to feel awkward

1. VERB

/ fil ˈɔk.wɚd / (AmE), / fiːl ˈɔː.kwəd / (BrE)
to experience discomfort or embarrassment, especially in a social situation
experience social discomfort or embarrassment

Examples

I always feel awkward when meeting new people at a party.
Her joke made everyone feel awkward for a moment.
You might feel awkward asking for help, but it's completely normal.

Synset Examples

His unnatural laugh made everyone feel awkward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel embarrassed - to experience self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness
  • Antonym: feel comfortable - to experience ease or relaxation in a situation
