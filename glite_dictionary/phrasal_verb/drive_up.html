to drive up

1. VERB

/ draɪv ʌp / (AmE), / draɪv ʌp / (BrE)
to approach a place or a person in a vehicle by driving
approach by driving

Examples

She watched as the taxi drove up to the front steps.
Can you drive up to the entrance so I can get out quickly?
Every morning, the delivery van drives up and leaves fresh bread at the door.

Synset Examples

The truck entered the driveway and drove up towards the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull up - to bring a vehicle to a stop, especially by the side of the road
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01854102-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ draɪv ʌp / (AmE), / draɪv ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or cause to increase, especially suddenly or to a higher level, often used for prices, values, or quantities
to increase (e.g., price, amount)

Examples

Rising demand can drive up housing prices in popular neighborhoods.
The new tax is likely to drive up the cost of imported goods.
Scarcity of raw materials can drive up production expenses for manufacturers.

Synset Examples

The price of gas is expected to drive up next week.
Her promotion will drive up her confidence significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to lift or increase something
  • Antonym: lower - to make something less in amount or value

Synset POS: None
