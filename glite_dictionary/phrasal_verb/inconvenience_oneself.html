to inconvenience oneself

1. VERB

/ ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to make an effort that causes trouble or discomfort to oneself; to take the trouble to do something
make an effort that causes oneself trouble

Examples

Please don't inconvenience yourself on my account; I can manage.
She never inconveniences herself to help others unless absolutely necessary.
He inconvenienced himself by staying late at the office to finish the project.

Synset Examples

He did not trouble to call his mother on her birthday
Don't bother, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put oneself out - to make a special effort, often at inconvenience to oneself
  • Synonym: to trouble oneself - to take the trouble to do something; to make an effort
