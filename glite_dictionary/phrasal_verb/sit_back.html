to sit back

1. VERB

/ sɪt bæk / (AmE), / sɪt bæk / (BrE)
to remain inactive or not get involved while something is happening, especially when action is expected
remain inactive or uninvolved

Examples

You can't just sit back and watch while injustice occurs.
Instead of helping, he chose to sit back and do nothing.
Many people sit back expecting someone else to solve the problem.

Synset Examples

Don't just sit by while your rights are violated!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to intervene - to become involved and take action in a situation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02379190-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɪt bæk / (AmE), / sɪt bak / (BrE)
to settle into a relaxed or comfortable sitting position, often to rest or observe without active participation
settle into a relaxed sitting position

Examples

After a long day, she likes to sit back and read a good book.
Just sit back and enjoy the performance; you don't need to do anything.
He sat back in his chair, looking satisfied with the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recline - to lean or lie back in a relaxed position
  • Antonym: to sit up - to move into or remain in a more upright seated position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00026934-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sɪt bæk / (AmE), / sɪt bæk / (BrE)
to relax and not become involved in an activity or take action
relax and not take action

Examples

After finishing dinner, he liked to sit back and enjoy a good book.
Sometimes you need to sit back and watch how events unfold.
Don’t just sit back—get involved if you want things to change.

Synset Examples

She decided to sit back and let others take charge.
At the beach, I could finally sit back and relax.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to take action - to do something in order to achieve a result
