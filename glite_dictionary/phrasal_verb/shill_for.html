to shill for

1. VERB

/ ʃɪl fɔr / (AmE), / ʃɪl fə / (BrE)
to promote or endorse someone or something, often insincerely or secretly, for personal gain or advantage.
promote something for personal gain
informal american

Examples

Several bloggers were paid to shill for the new smartphone without revealing their sponsorships.
Critics accused the TV host of shilling for the diet company during her program.
It's unethical for journalists to shill for companies they are writing about.

Synset Examples

The politician was accused of being a shill for big corporations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endorse - publicly declare support for
  • Antonym: to criticize - express disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

Originally American English; 'shill' first recorded in early 20th-century circus slang as a decoy or faux customer.
