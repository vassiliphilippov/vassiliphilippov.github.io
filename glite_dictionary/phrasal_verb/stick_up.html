to stick up

1. VERB

/ stɪk ʌp / (AmE), / stɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to defend or support someone or something, especially when they are being criticized or attacked
defend or support someone or something

Examples

She always sticks up for her little brother at school.
It's important to stick up for your beliefs, even if others disagree.
He stuck up for his colleague during the heated meeting.

Synset Examples

He stood up for his friend
She stuck up for the teacher who was accused of harassing the student

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up for - to defend or support someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00897237-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɪk ʌp / (AmE), / stɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to rob a person or place using a gun or the threat of violence
rob using a threat or weapon

Examples

The masked man tried to stick up the corner store last night.
They planned to stick up a bank but were caught before they could do it.
The gang stuck up several travelers on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold up - to rob using violence or threats, especially with a weapon
  • Synonym: mug - to attack and rob someone in a public place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02282293-v
Synset POS: v
