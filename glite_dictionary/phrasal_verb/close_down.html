to close down

1. VERB

/ kloʊz daʊn / (AmE), / kləʊz daʊn / (BrE)
to stop operating permanently, or to make something stop operating permanently, especially a business or organization
stop operating permanently (business, etc.)

Examples

After twenty years in business, the bookstore will close down next month due to low sales.
The government ordered all non-essential shops to close down during the crisis.
They had to close down their restaurant after failing to renew the lease.

Synset Examples

The owners decided to move and to close the factory
My business closes every night at 8 P.M.
close up the shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut down - to stop operating something or cause it to stop, especially a business, computer, or machine
  • Degree Lower: close up - to shut something, especially a shop, temporarily or at the end of the day

2. VERB

/ kloʊz daʊn / (AmE), / kləʊz daʊn / (BrE)
(of a business or organization) to stop operating permanently.
stop operating permanently (of a business, etc.)

Examples

The factory will close down at the end of the year due to falling profits.
Many small shops were forced to close down after the new mall opened.
They had to close down their restaurant when they could no longer afford the rent.

Synset Examples

Many businesses have closed down in the black zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut down - to cease operations, especially temporarily or for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: open - to begin or resume operation (of a business, etc.)
