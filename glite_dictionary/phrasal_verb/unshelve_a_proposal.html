to unshelve (a proposal)

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈʃɛlv ðə prəˈpoʊzəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɛlv ðə prəˈpəʊzəl / (BrE)
to bring a previously set-aside proposal back for reconsideration or active discussion
bring back a proposal for consideration

Examples

After several months, the board chose to unshelve the proposal and review its merits again.
Pressure from stakeholders led the committee to unshelve the proposal that had been ignored last year.
If new funding becomes available, they may unshelve the proposal and move forward with the project.

Synset Examples

The committee decided to unshelve the proposal for discussion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shelve (a proposal) - to set aside or postpone a proposal

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (to reverse an action) and shelve (to put aside or suspend, as books on a shelf).
