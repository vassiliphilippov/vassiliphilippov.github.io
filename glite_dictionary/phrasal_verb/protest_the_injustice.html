to protest the injustice

1. VERB

/ proʊˈtɛst ði ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / prəˈtɛst ði ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
to publicly show opposition to unfair treatment or conditions, often by demonstrating or speaking out
oppose unfairness publicly

Examples

Hundreds gathered outside the courthouse to protest the injustice they believed had occurred.
She joined the rally to protest the injustice faced by marginalized communities.
Activists used social media to organize events to protest the injustice in their country.

Synset Examples

They marched to protest the injustice in the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demonstrate - to take part in a public protest or show of support
  • Antonym: to condone - to accept or allow behavior that is morally wrong or offensive
