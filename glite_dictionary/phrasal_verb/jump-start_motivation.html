to jump-start motivation

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌmp stɑrt moʊˌtɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmp stɑːt məʊˌtɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
to cause someone's enthusiasm or drive to become active, intense, or renewed, especially after a period of low motivation
renew or boost enthusiasm

Examples

A new project can often jump-start motivation among employees who feel stuck in a routine.
The coach planned a surprise event to jump-start motivation before the big game.
Attending a workshop helped her jump-start motivation for her creative writing.

Synset Examples

He used a meeting to jump-start the team's motivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to motivate - provide someone with motivation
  • Antonym: demotivate - make someone lose motivation

Etymology

From 'jump-start' (to start the engine of a vehicle with an auxiliary power source), used metaphorically with 'motivation'.
