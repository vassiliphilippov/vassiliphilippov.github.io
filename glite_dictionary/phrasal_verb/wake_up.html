to wake up

1. VERB

/ ˌweɪk ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌweɪk ˈʌp / (BrE)
to stop sleeping and become awake; to cause someone to stop sleeping
stop sleeping; become awake

Examples

I usually wake up at 6 a.m. on weekdays.
He didn't wake up until the sun was high in the sky.
Could you wake up your brother for breakfast?

Synset Examples

She woke up to the sound of the alarm clock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to awaken - to stop sleeping; to become aware or conscious
  • Antonym: to fall asleep - to begin to sleep
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00018519-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ weɪk ʌp / (AmE), / weɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to cause someone to stop sleeping or to become alert or conscious
cause someone to become awake

Examples

Can you wake up your brother before we leave for school?
The loud thunder woke up everyone in the house last night.
She always wakes up her children gently in the morning.

Synset Examples

He was roused by the drunken men in the street
Please wake me at 6 AM.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rouse - to wake someone from sleep or bring them to consciousness
  • Antonym: to put to sleep - to cause someone to fall asleep
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00018806-v
Synset POS: v
