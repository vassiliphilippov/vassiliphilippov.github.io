to fill up

1. VERB

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to become full; to be filled completely
become full

Examples

The parking lot will fill up by noon on busy days.
If you don’t hurry, the train will fill up and you’ll have to stand.
My inbox tends to fill up quickly if I don’t check it every day.

Synset Examples

The pool slowly filled with water
The theater filled up slowly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empty - to become or make empty
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to make something full or stop up a space, often by adding material until nothing more can be added
make full; stop up

Examples

Please fill up the holes in the wall with plaster.
The workers filled up the cracks to prevent leaks.
After filling up the gaps, the window was properly sealed.

Synset Examples

Can you close the cracks with caulking?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fill - to make full; to put as much as possible into

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to make something completely full, either literally or metaphorically

The stadium quickly filled up as fans arrived for the concert.
Her heart filled up with joy when she saw her family waiting at the airport.

4. VERB

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to eat until one is completely full or satisfied
eat until full; eat to satiety

Examples

He filled up on pasta and couldn't eat another bite.
If you fill up on snacks, you won't be hungry for dinner.

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)

Examples
