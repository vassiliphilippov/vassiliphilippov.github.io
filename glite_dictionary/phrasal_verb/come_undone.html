to come undone

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʌnˈdʌn / (AmE), / kʌm ʌnˈdʌn / (BrE)
to fail, fall apart, or lose control emotionally or structurally
to fail or fall apart (literal or emotional)

Examples

She worried that everything she had built would come undone if one thing went wrong.
When his secret was revealed, his calm façade began to come undone.
The deal threatened to come undone after the investors pulled out.

Synset Examples

She feared her carefully arranged project would come undone.
The plan began to come undone when they missed the deadline.
His emotions started to come undone during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces or fail completely
  • Synonym: collapse - to suddenly fail or break down
  • Antonym: to hold together - to remain intact or maintain composure
