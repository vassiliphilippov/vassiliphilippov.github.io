to paper over

1. VERB

/ ˈpeɪpɚ ˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to try to hide or disguise faults, problems, or differences instead of dealing with them openly
conceal or hide faults or problems

Examples

Management tried to paper over the drop in sales by focusing on minor successes.
He hoped no one would notice if he papered over the cracks in his argument.
They cannot continue papering over their disagreements indefinitely.

Synset Examples

He attempted to paper over his mistakes with excuses.
They tried to paper over the issues during the discussion.
You can't just paper over the problems; they need solving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a mistake or wrongdoing
  • Synonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden or secret
  • Antonym: to expose - to make something visible that was hidden
