to become prickly

1. VERB

/ bɪˈkʌm ˈprɪkli / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm ˈprɪkli / (BrE)
to start reacting in an irritable or sensitive way, especially in response to criticism or provocation
to become irritable or sensitive

Examples

When questioned about the budget, the manager became prickly and avoided giving direct answers.
She often becomes prickly whenever someone mentions her work habits.
During the interview, he quickly became prickly when asked about his previous job.

Synset Examples

The subject became prickly after the last debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get defensive - to show a tendency to protect oneself emotionally when criticized
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less angry or irritated
