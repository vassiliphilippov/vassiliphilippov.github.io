to crack up

1. VERB

/ kræk ʌp / (AmE), / kræk ʌp / (BrE)
to laugh loudly and uncontrollably
to laugh uncontrollably
informal

Examples

The comedian’s joke made the whole audience crack up with laughter.
She always cracks up when she watches that movie.
I cracked up so hard I could barely breathe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burst out laughing - begin to laugh suddenly and loudly
  • Degree Lower: giggle - to laugh lightly in a silly way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00030359-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kræk ʌp / (AmE), / kræk ʌp / (BrE)
to suffer a mental or emotional breakdown due to stress or pressure
suffer a mental breakdown

Examples

After months of relentless pressure at work, he finally cracked up and had to take a leave of absence.
She was afraid she might crack up if things got any worse at home.
Many soldiers cracked up during the intense battles of the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break down - to lose control of one's emotions; to have a mental collapse
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01789398-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kræk ʌp / (AmE), / kræk ʌp / (BrE)
to suddenly start laughing a lot; to laugh uncontrollably, especially in reaction to something funny
to laugh uncontrollably
informal

Examples

Every time he tells that story, I just crack up.
The movie was so funny that the whole audience cracked up.
She tried to keep a straight face, but ended up cracking up anyway.

Synset Examples

They always crack up at his silly impressions.
His joke made everyone crack up at the party.
She couldn't help but crack up during the comedy show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burst out laughing - to suddenly start laughing
