to belt out

1. VERB

/ bɛlt aʊt / (AmE), / bɛlt aʊt / (BrE)
to sing or play music loudly and with a lot of energy
sing or play loudly and energetically

Examples

She stepped onto the stage and belted out her favorite song with impressive confidence.
The band belted out one hit after another at the concert last night.
Whenever he hears that tune, he can't resist belting out the chorus at the top of his lungs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to croon - to sing softly and gently

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'belt' meaning to hit or deliver forcefully (late 19th century).
