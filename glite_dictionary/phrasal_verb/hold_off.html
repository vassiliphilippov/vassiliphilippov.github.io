to hold off

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɔf / (AmE), / həʊld ɒf / (BrE)
to delay doing something; to wait before taking action or making a decision
delay taking action

Examples

They decided to hold off on buying a new car until next year.
Can you hold off making a decision for another week?
The rain held off just long enough for us to finish the game.

Synset Examples

the scientists held off announcing their results until they repeated the experiment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • Synonym: to defer - to put off to a future time
  • Antonym: to proceed - to begin or continue with an action
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02647547-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hoʊld ɔf / (AmE), / həʊld ɒf / (BrE)
to resist and prevent an opponent from succeeding or advancing, especially by maintaining one's position until the end
resist and prevent an opponent's success

Examples

The defense managed to hold off the attackers until reinforcements arrived.
She tried to hold off her rivals in the final lap of the race.
Despite heavy pressure, the team held off their opponents and won the match.

Synset Examples

Dallas had enough of a lead to hold the Broncos off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to restrain or prevent progress
  • Antonym: to give in - to stop resisting and yield to someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01119743-v
Synset POS: v
