to ravel out

1. VERB

/ ˈrævəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈrævəl aʊt / (BrE)
to disentangle or separate the threads, or to clarify something that is tangled or confusing
disentangle or clarify

Examples

She tried to ravel out the threads of the old sweater.
The detective worked tirelessly to ravel out the clues of the case.
It took hours to ravel out the tangled cords behind the television.

Synset Examples

can you unravel the mystery?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unravel - to undo or untangle something twisted or complicated
  • Antonym: to entangle - to twist together or entwine into a confusing mass
