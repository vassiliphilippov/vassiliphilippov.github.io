to stand up

1. VERB

/ stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stænd ʌp / (BrE)
to place something in an upright or vertical position
put in upright position

Examples

Please stand up the sign so everyone can see it.
Don't forget to stand up the mirror after cleaning it.
She carefully stood up the lamp next to the sofa.

Synset Examples

Can you stand the bookshelf up?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set up - to place or arrange something in position for use
  • Antonym: to lie down - to place something in a horizontal position
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stænd ʌp / (BrE)
to rise to an upright position on the feet; to be in a standing posture.
rise or be upright on the feet

Examples

Please stand up when the judge enters the courtroom.
She told everyone to stand up and stretch during the meeting.
After the long movie, he could barely stand up from his seat.

Synset Examples

We had to stand for the entire performance!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sit down - to move from a standing to a sitting position

4. VERB

/ stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stand ʌp / (BrE)
to remain strong or effective when subjected to wear, criticism, or other challenging situations
remain strong under challenge

Examples

The new evidence did not stand up to close scrutiny.
These shoes have really stood up well after years of use.
The theory doesn't stand up when you look at all the facts.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
