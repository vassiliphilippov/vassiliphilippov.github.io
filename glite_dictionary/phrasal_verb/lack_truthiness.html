to lack truthiness

1. VERB

/ læk ˈtruːθi.nəs / (AmE), / læk ˈtruːθi.nəs / (BrE)
to not have the appearance or quality of seeming true or genuine, despite possibly sounding convincing
not seem true or genuine

Examples

Although his story sounded believable, it lacked truthiness and left everyone suspicious.
The witness's testimony lacked truthiness, casting doubt on the entire case.
Her explanation was detailed but ultimately lacked truthiness in the eyes of the jury.

Synset Examples

His argument lacked truthiness despite sounding convincing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truthiness - the quality of seeming or being felt to be true, even if not necessarily true
  • Degree Higher: veracity - conformity to facts; accuracy
  • Degree Lower: falseness - the quality of being untrue or not genuine

Etymology

Formed from "lack" + "truthiness," the latter coined by American comedian Stephen Colbert in 2005.
