to take stock

1. VERB

/ teɪk stɑk / (AmE), / teɪk stɒk / (BrE)
to examine or assess a situation, oneself, or something carefully and thoroughly, often in order to make decisions or changes
examine or assess carefully

Examples

After a busy year, she decided to take stock of her achievements before planning ahead.
It’s important to take stock of your finances before making a big purchase.
The company paused to take stock and reconsider its strategy during the economic downturn.

Synset Examples

he scrutinized his likeness in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrutinize - to examine or inspect closely and thoroughly
  • Synonym: survey - to look carefully and thoroughly at someone or something

Etymology

Originally an allusion to checking stock (inventory) in business before making decisions.

2. VERB

/ teɪk stɑk / (AmE), / teɪk stɒk / (BrE)
to make an itemized record of goods, supplies, or assets, especially as part of an inventory; to assess or evaluate resources or a situation systematically
make an itemized record; assess resources

Examples

At the end of each month, the manager will take stock of all products in the warehouse.
Before launching the new project, the team decided to take stock of their available resources.
The shop assistant was busy taking stock after the busy holiday season.

Synset Examples

Inventory all books before the end of the year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - to make a complete list of items or goods, especially for recording or verification

Etymology

From 'take' (to capture or record) and 'stock' (referring to stored goods); stock from Old English 'stocc' (tree trunk, log, stocks, post).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02477918-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ teɪk stɑk / (AmE), / teɪk stɒk / (BrE)
to carefully consider or assess a situation or one's position before making a decision or taking action
assess or evaluate a situation

Examples

After the crisis, the management decided to take stock of the company's operations.
Every December, I like to take stock of my personal goals and achievements.
She paused to take stock before responding to the surprising news.

Synset Examples

Let's take stock before making any decisions.
I need to take stock of my finances this month.
It's time to take stock of our progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaluate - to judge or determine the significance, worth, or quality of something
  • Synonym: to assess - to judge or decide the amount, value, or importance of something

Etymology

Originally from the literal practice of counting stock or goods in storehouses before making decisions; 'stock' here refers to stored items or inventory.
