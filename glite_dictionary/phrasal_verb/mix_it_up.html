to mix it up

1. VERB

/ mɪks ɪt ʌp / (AmE), / mɪks ɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to add variety or bring change to an activity, routine, or situation for interest or freshness
add variety; change things for interest
informal

Examples

When planning meals for the week, she likes to mix it up to avoid repetition.
If the team keeps losing, maybe it's time to mix it up with a new strategy.
He tends to get bored easily, so he tries to mix it up in his daily workouts.

Synset Examples
