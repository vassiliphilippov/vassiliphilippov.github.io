to make over

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / meɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to use something again after altering or adapting it, especially in a new or different form
reuse after alteration

Examples

The director decided to make over the script from the original film for a modern audience.
She loves to make over old clothes into something fashionable and new.
The company plans to make over its classic model with updated technology.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to reuse - to use something again
  • Synonym: to recycle - convert waste into reusable material
