to spirit off

1. VERB

/ ˈspɪrɪt ɔf / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to carry away or remove quickly and secretly, as if by magic or supernatural means
carry away secretly or mysteriously

Examples

The documents were spirited off in the middle of the night before anyone noticed.
The missing child was believed to have been spirited off by strangers.
Someone had spirited off the valuable painting without tripping the alarm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to whisk away - to take or remove something quickly
  • Synonym: to abduct - to take someone away illegally by force or deception
  • Antonym: return - to give or send back

Etymology

From the noun 'spirit' (supernatural being or force) used as a verb, plus 'off'; used since the early 19th century.
