to knock off

1. VERB

/ nɑk ˈɔf / (AmE), / nɒk ˈɒf / (BrE)
to reduce the price of something, usually by a specific amount
reduce the price of something
informal

Examples

They're willing to knock off $50 if you buy today.
The store decided to knock off twenty percent from last season's stock.
Can you knock off a few dollars from the final price?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discount - to reduce the regular price of an item

2. VERB

/ nɑk ˈɔf / (AmE), / nɒk ˈɒf / (BrE)
(slang) To steal or take something by theft.
steal; take by theft (slang)
slang

Examples

Someone tried to knock off my bike outside the shop yesterday.
A gang managed to knock off several cars in the neighborhood last week.
She heard a rumor that someone was planning to knock off the electronics store.

Synset Examples

Someone snitched my wallet!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property illegally from a person or place
  • Antonym: return - to give something back to its owner; the opposite of steal

3. VERB

/ nɑk ɑf / (AmE), / nɒk ɒf / (BrE)
to write or produce something quickly and with little effort
write or produce quickly

Examples

He managed to knock off three short stories in one afternoon.
She can knock off a report in less than an hour when she's focused.
They asked me to knock off a quick summary before the meeting.

Synset Examples

She dashed off a note to her husband saying she would not be home for supper
He scratched off a thank-you note to the hostess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dash off - to write something quickly
  • Synonym: to scratch off - to write quickly and possibly carelessly

4. VERB

/ nɑk ɑf / (AmE), / nɒk ɒf / (BrE)
(informal, slang) to kill someone, especially as part of criminal activity or to remove a threat.
kill or murder (often in a criminal context)
informal slang

Examples

The gang planned to knock off the rival boss before the end of the week.
He hired a hitman to knock off his business competitor, but the police intervened.
Rumors spread quickly after the informant was knocked off last night.

Synset Examples

The mafia liquidated the informer
the double agent was neutralized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murder - to unlawfully kill someone with intent
  • Synonym: to assassinate - to kill someone, especially a prominent person, for political or money motives
  • Antonym: save - to prevent harm or death; opposite of 'kill'

stop doing something; cease an activity
informal

Examples
