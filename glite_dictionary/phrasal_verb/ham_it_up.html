to ham it up

1. VERB

/ hæm ɪt ʌp / (AmE), / hæm ɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to overact or exaggerate one's performance, especially in a theatrical or dramatic way
overact or exaggerate performance
informal

Examples

Whenever he's on stage, he loves to ham it up for the audience.
She couldn't resist hamming it up during the school play's funny scenes.
The actor really hammed it up in the comedy sketch, making everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overact - to act a role in an exaggerated way
  • Antonym: to underplay - to act a role in a restrained or subtle way

Etymology

From the noun "ham" (a performer who overacts), itself possibly from "hamfatter," an inferior actor, from the minstrel song "The Ham-fat Man."
