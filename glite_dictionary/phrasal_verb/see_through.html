to see through

1. VERB

/ si θru / (AmE), / siː θruː / (BrE)
to support someone financially or otherwise so that they can get through a period of difficulty or need
support through a difficult period

Examples

My parents agreed to see through my university expenses until I got a job.
He relied on his savings to see through the toughest months after being laid off.
A generous grant helped see through the charity's programs during the economic downturn.

Synset Examples

The scholarship saw me through college
This money will see me through next month

2. VERB

/ si θru / (AmE), / siː θruː / (BrE)
to recognize the true character or hidden motives of someone or something, not being deceived by a false appearance
recognize true character or motives

Examples

I could always see through his fake smile and spot the truth.
She tried to lie, but the police quickly saw through her story.
Even as a child, he was able to see through people's pretenses.

Synset Examples

We could see through her apparent calm

  • Synonym: to penetrate - to see or understand something despite obstacles
  • Antonym: be taken in - to be deceived by someone or something
