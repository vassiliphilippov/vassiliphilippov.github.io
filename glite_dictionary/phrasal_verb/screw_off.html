to screw off

1. VERB

/ skru ɑf / (AmE), / skruː ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone forcefully to go away or leave you alone; sometimes, to relax or take time off from work.
forcefully tell someone to go away; take time off
informal slang

Examples

If you're going to be rude, why don't you just screw off?
After a stressful week, she decided to screw off for the afternoon and go to the beach.
He told his brother to screw off when he wouldn't stop teasing him.

Synset Examples

If you don’t like it, you can just screw off.
He told his annoying coworker to screw off.
She decided to screw off and enjoy her weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lost - to go away, often used as an imperative command
  • Synonym: to buzz off - (informal) go away, leave someone alone
