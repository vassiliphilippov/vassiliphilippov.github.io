to peel off

1. VERB

/ pil ɔf / (AmE), / piːl ɒf / (BrE)
to remove the outer covering or layer from something, especially by stripping or pulling it away
remove outer layer by stripping

Examples

Carefully peel off the label from the jar so you can reuse it.
The old paint began to peel off the walls after years of neglect.
He used a knife to peel off the skin of the apple before eating it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - to remove a covering or layer from something

2. VERB

/ pil ɔf / (AmE), / piːl ɒf / (BrE)
to remove (something) from a surface or body, especially with effort or difficulty
remove with some difficulty

Examples

After the hike, she peeled off her muddy socks and threw them in the hamper.
He struggled to peel off the wet plaster from his arm after the cast was removed.
The children laughed as they tried to peel off sticky labels from their notebooks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strip off - to remove clothing or covering from someone or something

3. VERB

/ pil ɑf / (AmE), / piːl ɒf / (BrE)
(of a covering or surface) to come away or be removed in thin layers or small flakes.
come away in thin layers or flakes

Examples

After years of neglect, the old wallpaper began to peel off in large strips.
The sunburn caused the skin on his shoulders to peel off.
The paint will peel off if you don't apply a primer first.

Synset Examples

5. VERB

/ pil ɔf / (AmE), / piːl ɒf / (BrE)
(of a person or vehicle) to leave a group or formation and move away in a different direction.
leave a group or formation and move away

Examples

At the signal, the lead jet peeled off from the formation and flew west.
Several cars peeled off from the convoy as we entered the city.
During the marathon, one runner peeled off to take a different route.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break away - to leave or escape from a group or formation
  • Antonym: join up - to become part of a group or formation

Etymology

From 'peel' (to remove) + 'off,' reflecting the action of separating from a group.
