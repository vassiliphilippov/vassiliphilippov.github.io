to piddle away

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪdəl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɪdəl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to waste time by spending it idly or inefficiently, especially on trivial activities
waste time idly
informal

Examples

He tends to piddle away his afternoons scrolling through social media without getting much done.
Don’t piddle away your weekend—you should finish your project instead.
She realized she had piddled away two hours just chatting by the water cooler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to waste time - to spend time doing unproductive things
  • Synonym: kill time - to do something unimportant to make time pass

Etymology

From 'piddle', informal variant of 'peddle' or 'puddle', meaning to dawdle or engage in trivial activity, plus 'away'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪd.əl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɪd.əl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to spend time or resources aimlessly or wastefully; to waste time by being idle or unproductive
waste time or resources idly
informal

Examples

On rainy days, he tends to piddle away the hours doing nothing in particular.
She realized she had piddled away the morning scrolling through social media.
Many students piddle away their study time by chatting with friends.

Synset Examples

She decided to piddle away the afternoon at the café.
They piddled away the summer, not doing much at all.
He often piddles away his weekends instead of being productive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fritter away - to waste time or resources bit by bit on unimportant things
  • Synonym: waste - to use or expend carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose

Etymology

From the verb "piddle" (to waste time) + "away" (indicating continuation or dissipation).
