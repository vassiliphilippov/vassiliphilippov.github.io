to speak out against

1. VERB

/ spiːk aʊt əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / spiːk aʊt əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to publicly express strong opposition to something or someone
publicly oppose

Examples

Many activists speak out against environmental pollution at public rallies.
The senator was praised because he spoke out against government corruption.
Teachers are encouraged to speak out against bullying in schools.

Synset Examples

She spoke out against the anti-choice movement.
She spoke out against antiautistic rhetoric at the conference.
He spoke out against the ideologies of the AmeriKKKunt faction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppose - to be against or in conflict with something or someone
  • Antonym: speak up for - to publicly express support for someone or something
