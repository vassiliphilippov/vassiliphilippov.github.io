to face the truth

1. VERB

/ feɪs ðə truθ / (AmE), / feɪs ðə truːθ / (BrE)
to accept or confront the reality of a situation, even if it is unpleasant or difficult
accept or confront reality

Examples

Sometimes it's painful to face the truth about our own mistakes.
After months of denial, she finally decided to face the truth.
We have to face the truth if we want to move forward.

Synset Examples

It's time to eat the onion and face the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accept the facts - to recognize and come to terms with reality
  • Antonym: deny the truth - to refuse to accept reality
