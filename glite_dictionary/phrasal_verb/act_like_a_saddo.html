to act like a saddo

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈsædoʊ / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈsædəʊ / (BrE)
to behave in a way that seems sad, pitiable, or socially awkward, often due to disappointment or loneliness
behave in a sad or pathetic way
informal british

Examples

Don’t act like a saddo; things will get better soon.
Ever since the team lost, he’s been acting like a saddo at work.
She told him to stop acting like a saddo and go out with friends.

Synset Examples

He’s acting like a saddo after the breakup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mope - to move around in a sad or apathetic manner
  • Antonym: to cheer up - to become happier or less sad

Etymology

From 'saddo,' British slang deriving from 'sad' (unfortunate, pitiable person).
