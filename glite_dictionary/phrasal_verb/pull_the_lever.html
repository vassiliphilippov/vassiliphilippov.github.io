to pull the lever

1. VERB

/ pʊl ðə ˈliːvər / (AmE), / pʊl ðə ˈliːvə / (BrE)
to operate a device or mechanism by pulling its lever; to take action that causes a process or result, especially by using a control lever
operate something by pulling its lever

Examples

He shouted for help as he tried to pull the lever with all his strength.
You must pull the lever to release the emergency brake in case of trouble.
As soon as she pulled the lever, the doors slid open with a hiss.

Synset Examples

Just pull the lever to activate the system.
You need to pull the lever to start the machine.
He positioned himself to pull the lever effectively.
He pulled the lever and the lights turned on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flip the switch - to operate a device by moving its switch; to take decisive action
