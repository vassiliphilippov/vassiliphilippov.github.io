to rattle down

1. VERB

/ ˈrætl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈrætl daʊn / (BrE)
to recite something quickly, easily, and often at length, as if from memory, especially in a rapid or fluent manner
recite rapidly and fluently

Examples

During the quiz, she could rattle down the capitals of every European country.
He began to rattle down the rules without even pausing for breath.
They asked him about the project, and he rattled down all the key details without hesitation.

Synset Examples

he could rattle off the names of all the chemical elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rattle off - to recite a list or facts quickly and easily
