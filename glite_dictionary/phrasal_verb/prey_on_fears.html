to prey on fears

1. VERB

/ preɪ ɑn fɪrz / (AmE), / preɪ ɒn fɪəz / (BrE)
to exploit or take advantage of people's fears for personal gain or benefit
exploit or take advantage of fears

Examples

Scammers often prey on fears of financial loss to trick people into giving out personal information.
Certain politicians have been accused of preying on fears during election campaigns.
Advertisers sometimes prey on fears to sell unnecessary security products.

Synset Examples

They tend to prey on people's fears for profit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to make use of something or someone for one's own advantage
  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence someone cleverly or unfairly
