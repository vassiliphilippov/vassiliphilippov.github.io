to go away

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place or move away from someone or something
to leave or move away

Examples

She asked him to go away and never return.
The pain will go away after a few days of rest.
Every summer, we go away to the countryside for a holiday.

Synset Examples

Go away before I start to cry
The train departs at noon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place, often after traveling

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)

leave, often suddenly or without explanation

Examples

He tends to go away when things get difficult, often without warning anyone.
After a brief conversation, she suddenly went away and never contacted us again.
