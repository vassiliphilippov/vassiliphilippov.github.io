to cast off

1. VERB

/ kæst ɔf / (AmE), / kɑːst ɒf / (BrE)
to complete a piece of knitting by finishing the final row of stitches so that they do not unravel
finish the last row in knitting

Examples

To finish the scarf, simply cast off the last row of stitches.
She carefully cast off to make sure her sweater wouldn't unravel.
After knitting several inches, it's time to cast off and admire your work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bind off - finish the last row of knitting (American English)

2. VERB

/ kæst ɔf / (AmE), / kɑːst ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or get rid of something, especially something no longer wanted or needed
remove or get rid of something

Examples

When the weather warmed, she decided to cast off her heavy coat.
It's time to cast off old habits that no longer serve you.
He tried to cast off his reputation as a troublemaker.

Synset Examples

he shed his image as a pushy boss
shed your clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something no longer useful
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep or continue to have

Etymology

From the verb 'cast' (to throw, let go) + 'off' (away).
