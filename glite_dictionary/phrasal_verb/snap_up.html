to snap up

1. VERB

/ snæp ʌp / (AmE), / snæp ʌp / (BrE)
to buy or take something quickly and eagerly, especially before anyone else has a chance
buy or take quickly and eagerly

Examples

Tickets for the concert were snapped up within minutes of going on sale.
She managed to snap up a designer dress at a fraction of the price.
Properties in this area are often snapped up as soon as they come on the market.

Synset Examples

I snapped up all the good buys during the garage sale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take or seize suddenly
  • Antonym: to pass up - to decline or fail to take an opportunity

2. VERB

/ snæp ʌp / (AmE), / snæp ʌp / (BrE)
to acquire or take advantage of something quickly and eagerly, especially before it becomes unavailable
buy or take quickly and eagerly

Examples

Fans lined up outside the store to snap up the new limited-edition phones.
Smart investors were quick to snap up shares of the rising tech company.
You should snap up that job offer before someone else accepts it.

Synset Examples

She quickly snapped up the opportunity for a promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take something quickly, sometimes roughly
  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something suddenly or quickly
