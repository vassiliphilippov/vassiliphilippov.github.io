to live in a hovel

1. VERB

/ lɪv ɪn ə ˈhʌvəl / (AmE), / lɪv ɪn ə ˈhɒvəl / (BrE)
to reside in very poor or dilapidated accommodation, often lacking basic comforts or cleanliness
reside in very poor conditions

Examples

After losing his job, he was forced to live in a hovel on the outskirts of the city.
Many refugees had no choice but to live in a hovel until better housing became available.
She worked hard so her children would never have to live in a hovel again.

Synset Examples

She refused to live in that hovel any longer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live in squalor - to live in extremely dirty and unpleasant conditions
  • Antonym: live in luxury - to live with great comfort and wealth
