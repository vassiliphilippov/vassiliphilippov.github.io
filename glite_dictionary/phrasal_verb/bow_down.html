to bow down

1. VERB

/ baʊ daʊn / (AmE), / baʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to bend one's body or lower one's head, especially as a sign of respect, submission, or shame
lower the body or head as a sign of respect, etc.

Examples

Everyone in the hall bowed down as the emperor entered.
She bowed down her head in front of the audience, showing her gratitude.
You should never feel the need to bow down to unreasonable demands.

Synset Examples

He bowed before the King
She bowed her head in shame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kneel - to rest on one's knees, often as a gesture of respect or worship
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00900454-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ baʊ daʊn / (AmE), / baʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to bend one's body forward or to the ground, especially in respect, submission, or worship
to bend forward in submission or respect

Examples

The servants were required to bow down before the king when entering the throne room.
Many people bow down to tradition, even when they don't agree with it.
She refused to bow down to pressure from her peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostrate oneself - to lay oneself flat on the ground face downward, especially in reverence or submission
  • Antonym: stand tall - to behave in a proud and confident way, not submitting
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02067538-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ baʊ daʊn / (AmE), / baʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to submit or show deep respect or obedience to someone or something, often by physically lowering the head or body
submit or show deep respect to someone

Examples

In the face of overwhelming evidence, the company had to bow down to public pressure.
Some traditions require children to bow down before their elders during ceremonies.
She refused to bow down to his unreasonable demands.

Synset Examples

It's time to bow down and accept defeat.
He told his followers to bow down in gratitude.
You must bow down to the queen's authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submit - to yield or give in to governance or authority
  • Antonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
