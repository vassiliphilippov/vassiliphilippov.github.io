to rig out

1. VERB

/ rɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / rɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to dress someone or oneself in special or elaborate clothes, especially to look impressive or attractive
dress in special or elaborate clothes

Examples

The children rigged out in fancy costumes for the school play.
He rigged out in a tuxedo for the gala event last night.
We were rigged out in matching uniforms for the parade.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on special or formal clothes
  • Antonym: to dress down - to wear informal clothes compared to normal

Etymology

From 'rig' in the sense of 'to equip' (of uncertain origin) plus 'out' as a particle.

2. VERB

/ rɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / rɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to equip, furnish, or set up with the necessary accessories or equipment; to prepare (something or someone) for use or a specific purpose
equip or prepare with necessary items

Examples

We need to rig out the gym with chairs and tables for the event.
He rigged out his car with the latest sound system and speakers.
They spent the afternoon rigging out their campsite before dark.

Synset Examples

We need to rig out the stage before the concert starts.
She rigged out her phone with a new case and screen protector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equip - to provide with necessary items for a particular purpose

Etymology

From 'rig' (to equip) + 'out' (completely); origin traceable to nautical use in the 18th century.
