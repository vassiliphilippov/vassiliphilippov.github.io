to side with

1. VERB

/ saɪd wɪð / (AmE), / saɪd wɪð / (BrE)
to support or align oneself with a particular person or group in a dispute or argument
support or align with someone/group

Examples

During the debate, she chose to side with her colleague's proposals.
When arguments break out at home, he often sides with his younger sister.
Many citizens sided with the protesters in their call for reform.

Synset Examples

We all rooted for the home team
I'm pulling for the underdog
Are you siding with the defender of the title?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or approval to someone or something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or resist someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01151176-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ saɪd wɪð / (AmE), / saɪd wɪð / (BrE)
to support or agree with a person or group in an argument or dispute
support or take the part of someone or a group

Examples

Despite the controversy, she always sides with her colleagues.
During the meeting, John sided with the minority group in their proposal.
It's sometimes difficult to know which team to side with during family arguments.

Synset Examples

She always sides with the Whiggamores during discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to agree with or encourage someone or something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to disagree with or actively work against someone or something
