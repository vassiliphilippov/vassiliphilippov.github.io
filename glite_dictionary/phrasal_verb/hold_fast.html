to hold fast

1. VERB

/ hoʊld fæst / (AmE), / həʊld fɑːst / (BrE)
to remain tightly attached or committed to something; to stick to something firmly, either physically or figuratively
stick or stay firmly attached

Examples

Despite criticism, she continued to hold fast to her values.
The barnacles held fast to the ship's hull through the entire voyage.
They held fast during the storm, refusing to abandon their position.

Synset Examples

Will this wallpaper adhere to the wall?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - stick firmly to a surface or belief

2. VERB

/ hoʊld fæst / (AmE), / həʊld fɑːst / (BrE)
to grip something firmly or to remain firmly committed to or continue to believe in something
grip firmly or steadfastly maintain

Examples

If the wind picks up, hold fast to the rope until help arrives.
She learned to hold fast to her convictions, even when others doubted her.
During turbulent times, the team managed to hold fast to their original vision.

Synset Examples

In a storm, it's important to hold fast to the railing.
You need to hold fast to your dreams, no matter what.
They must hold fast to their principles in tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cling - to hold tightly or refuse to let go
  • Antonym: to let go - to stop holding or release something or a principle

Etymology

From Old English 'healdan' (to hold) and Old English 'fæst' (firm, strong).
