to lay into

1. VERB

/ leɪ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / leɪ ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to attack or hit someone or something forcefully and violently
attack or hit violently

Examples

The coach laid into the team for their poor performance after the game.
A group of kids laid into the old car with sticks until it was completely smashed.
She suddenly laid into him, hitting and kicking in a fit of anger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - make a physical attack on
  • Synonym: to beat up - to attack someone and injure them by hitting

2. VERB

/ leɪ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / leɪ ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to attack or criticize someone fiercely
attack or criticize fiercely

Examples

The teacher laid into the students for not completing their homework.
During the meeting, she laid into her colleague over his missed deadlines.
After hearing the criticism, he laid into his opponent with a passionate rebuttal.
