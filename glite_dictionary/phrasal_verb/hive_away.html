to hive away

1. VERB

/ haɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / haɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put something aside and store it for future use, often secretly or gradually
put aside for future use

Examples

Every month, she would hive away a little money for emergencies.
He hived away dozens of old coins in a tin box in the attic.
They have been hiving away canned food in preparation for winter.

Synset Examples

store grain for the winter
The bear stores fat for the period of hibernation when he doesn't eat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to save up - to keep money or resources for future use
  • Synonym: to put aside - to save something for future use

Etymology

From the practice of bees storing honey in a hive; 'hive' used as a verb in this sense since the 17th century.
