to take care

1. VERB

/ teɪk kɛr / (AmE), / teɪk keə / (BrE)
to deal with or be responsible for something; to handle a task or problem.
deal with or handle something

Examples

I'll take care of the arrangements for the meeting.
Can you take care of the emails while I'm away?
She quickly took care of the problem before it escalated.

Synset Examples

Could you see about lunch?
I must attend to this matter
She took care of this business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attend to - to deal with or give attention to something
  • Synonym: handle - to deal with or manage a situation or problem
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02555278-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk kɛr / (AmE), / teɪk keə / (BrE)
to be responsible for or deal with something or someone; to make necessary arrangements or provide for needs
be responsible for; deal with

Examples

Can you take care of the guests while I finish in the kitchen?
She always takes care of the paperwork for the team.
Don't worry, I'll take care of all the arrangements for the trip.

Synset Examples

She takes care of all the necessary arrangements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look after - to watch over or be responsible for someone or something
  • Synonym: to oversee - to supervise or be in charge of something

Synset POS: v
