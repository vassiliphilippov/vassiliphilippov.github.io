to feel like a schmuck

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ʃmʌk / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ʃmʌk / (BrE)
to feel foolish, stupid, or embarrassed, often as a result of one's actions or a social situation
feel foolish or embarrassed
informal slang

Examples

After spilling coffee all over the documents, I felt like a schmuck in my first meeting.
She felt like a schmuck when she realized everyone had pitched in except her.
He forgot their anniversary again and really felt like a schmuck about it.

Synset Examples

He felt like a schmuck after forgetting her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feel like a fool - to feel stupid or embarrassed
  • Antonym: feel proud - to feel pleased or honored

Etymology

Schmuck comes from Yiddish *shmok*, meaning 'penis', but in English has come to mean a foolish or contemptible person.
