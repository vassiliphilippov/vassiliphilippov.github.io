to weigh in

1. VERB

/ weɪ ɪn / (AmE), / weɪ ɪn / (BrE)
to give your opinion or input in a discussion or debate, especially when it is important or requested
to offer an opinion or input

Examples

During the meeting, she decided to weigh in with her thoughts on the project.
Many experts weighed in on the new policy's potential impact.
Feel free to weigh in if you have any suggestions or concerns.

Synset Examples

Feel free to weigh in on this discussion anytime.
Experts will weigh in on the issue during the conference.
He decided to weigh in after considering all viewpoints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contribute - to give (something, such as money, help, or ideas) to a common supply or effort
  • Synonym: to chime in - to join or interrupt a conversation with a comment

Etymology

Originally from boxing, where a competitor's weight is officially measured before a match; later extended figuratively to mean joining in by giving input.
