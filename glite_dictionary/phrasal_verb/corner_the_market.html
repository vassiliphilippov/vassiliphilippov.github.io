to corner the market

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔrnər ðə ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnə ðə ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
to gain enough control over the supply of a product or service to be able to fix its price or exclude competitors
gain control over a market

Examples

The company hoped to corner the market by acquiring all its competitors.
By offering unique designs, they quickly cornered the market in home decor.
The startup dreams of cornering the market for electric scooters in urban areas.

Synset Examples

With this innovation, they hope to corner the market on tech gadgets.
They aim to corner the market on organic snacks.
The new brand tried to corner the market on eco-friendly products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to monopolize - to obtain exclusive control over something
  • Antonym: to compete - to strive for superiority or control in a market
