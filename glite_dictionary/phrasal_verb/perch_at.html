to perch at

1. VERB

/ pɝtʃ æt / (AmE), / pɜːtʃ æt / (BrE)
to sit or settle at a particular location, often in a casual or unsteady way
sit or settle at a place

Examples

They decided to perch at the café for lunch after a long walk.
We perched at the back of the crowded lecture hall.
Tourists often perch at local street corners to watch the city come alive.

Synset Examples

They decided to perch at the café for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch on - to sit or rest on something, especially in a precarious position

Etymology

From 'perch' (to rest or alight), originally referring to a bird resting on something narrow.
