to rake up

1. VERB

/ reɪk ʌp / (AmE), / reɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to bring something unpleasant, forgotten, or past to public attention, especially unnecessarily
mention or revive something unpleasant from the past

Examples

Please don't rake up old arguments every time we talk.
Journalists sometimes rake up scandals from decades ago.
She regretted raking up painful memories at the reunion.

Synset Examples

He raked up the misdeeds of his predecessor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring back to consciousness or attention
