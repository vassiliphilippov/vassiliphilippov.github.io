to look through

1. VERB

/ lʊk θru / (AmE), / lʊk θruː / (BrE)
to examine or review something carefully, usually by reading or inspecting it
examine or review carefully

Examples

She took her time to look through all the applications before making a decision.
Before submitting your essay, you should look through it for any mistakes.
He promised to look through the report and provide feedback by tomorrow.

Synset Examples

Please look through the documents before signing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something carefully in order to learn more about it
  • Synonym: scan - to look over something quickly but thoroughly
