to lay an egg

1. VERB

/ leɪ ən ɛɡ / (AmE), / leɪ ən eɡ / (BrE)
(of a bird, reptile, insect, etc.) to produce and deposit an egg from the body.
to produce and deposit an egg

Examples

The hen laid an egg early this morning before sunrise.
Some turtles travel long distances to lay their eggs on sandy beaches.
Yesterday, the duck laid an egg in the grass near the pond.

Synset Examples

The hen laid three eggs in the coop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oviposit - to deposit or lay eggs, especially by insects
