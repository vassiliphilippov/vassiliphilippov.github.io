to jazzed up

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒæzd ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʒæzd ʌp / (BrE)
Made more exciting, interesting, or lively, especially by adding decorative or engaging features.
made more exciting or lively
informal

Examples

She jazzed up her old living room with some new cushions and artwork.
The chef jazzed up the recipe by adding extra spices.
He jazzed up his resume to make it stand out to employers.

Synset Examples

Her outfit was jazzed up with some bold accessories.
He jazzed up his presentation with colorful graphics.
The event was jazzed up with performances and lights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiced up - made more interesting or lively
  • Synonym: livened up - made more lively or energetic
  • Antonym: toned down - made less intense or exciting

Etymology

From 'jazz' (to enliven, to make lively), referencing the lively style of jazz music, plus 'up'.
