to stand in for

1. VERB

/ stænd ɪn fɔr / (AmE), / stænd ɪn fə / (BrE)
to substitute for or take the place of someone temporarily
substitute for someone temporarily

Examples

I was asked to stand in for the manager while he was away on business.
Can you stand in for me at the meeting tomorrow morning?
When the teacher was ill, her assistant stood in for her for two weeks.

Synset Examples

He agreed to stand in for his colleague during the meeting.
When she was sick, I had to stand in for her at work.
Could you stand in for me while I’m on vacation?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute for - to take the place of someone or something temporarily
  • Synonym: to replace - to take the place of someone or something
