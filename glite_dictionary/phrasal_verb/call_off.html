to call off

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɔf / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
to announce the movements or steps for dancers during a square dance.
give dancing instructions (square dance)

Examples

The caller will call off the steps so that all dancers know what to do next.
She learned to call off at the local square dance club every Friday night.
As the music began, he confidently called off each movement for the eager dancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompt - to give cues to dancers or actors

2. VERB

/ kɔl ɔf / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
To cancel or postpone something that was planned or scheduled, often with no intention to reschedule.
cancel or postpone an event

Examples

Due to the heavy rain, they had to call off the football match.
The CEO decided to call off the merger at the last minute.
If anyone gets sick, we may have to call off the party.

Synset Examples

Call off the engagement
cancel the dinner party
we had to scrub our vacation plans
scratch that meeting — the chair is ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to decide that an organized event will not happen
  • Antonym: proceed with - to continue as planned
