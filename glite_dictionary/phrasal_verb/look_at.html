to look at

1. VERB

/ lʊk ət / (AmE), / lʊk ət / (BrE)
to examine or consider something carefully, especially in order to understand it or make a decision
examine or consider carefully

Examples

Let me look at the contract before we sign it.
When you look at the problem from another angle, the solution becomes clearer.
The committee will look at all the available evidence before making a decision.

Synset Examples

view a problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or study something closely
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
