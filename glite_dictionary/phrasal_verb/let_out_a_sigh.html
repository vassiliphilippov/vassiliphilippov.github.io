to let out a sigh

1. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt ə saɪ / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt ə saɪ / (BrE)
to audibly express relief, fatigue, disappointment, or another emotion by sighing
audibly express emotion by sighing

Examples

After hearing the good news, she let out a sigh of relief.
He let out a sigh when the long meeting was finally over.
The students let out a sigh as the test papers were collected.

Synset Examples

She let out a sigh when she finished the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sigh - to emit a long, deep breath expressing emotion
