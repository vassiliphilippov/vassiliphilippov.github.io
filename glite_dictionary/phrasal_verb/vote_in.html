to vote in

1. VERB

/ voʊt ɪn / (AmE), / vəʊt ɪn / (BrE)
to elect or bring someone to a position by a voting process
elect to an office by voting

Examples

The citizens decided to vote in a new mayor during the recent election.
After a heated campaign, the party was voted in by a clear majority.
They will vote in new board members at next week's meeting.

Synset Examples

They voted in Clinton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elect - to choose someone for a public office by voting
