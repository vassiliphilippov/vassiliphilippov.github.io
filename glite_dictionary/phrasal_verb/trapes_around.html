to trapes around

1. VERB

/ treɪps əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / treɪps əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to walk or wander about in a casual, aimless, or leisurely manner
walk or wander aimlessly

Examples

Every Sunday, she likes to trapes around the city market, stopping at every stall.
We trapesed around the museum for hours without any particular plan.
The children trapesed around the playground, laughing and exploring new corners.

Synset Examples

He watched the dog trapes around the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wander around - to walk leisurely or without a fixed course
  • Synonym: to roam - to move about or travel aimlessly

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal variation of 'trapse' (to walk aimlessly), related to 'tramp'.
