to lock into

1. VERB

/ lɑk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / lɒk ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to be trapped in or bound by a situation, arrangement, or contract so that it is difficult or impossible to escape or change it
be trapped or bound by a situation or contract

Examples

Many students find themselves locked into long-term loans after graduation.
Once you sign the lease, you're locked into the agreement for a year.
He felt locked into a routine he could no longer enjoy.

Synset Examples

She felt locked into her job with no way out.
They were locked into a contract for another year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commit - to bind or obligate oneself to a course of action
  • Antonym: free - to release from a state of confinement or captivity
