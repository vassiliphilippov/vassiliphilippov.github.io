to shield against

1. VERB

/ ʃild əˈgɛnst / (AmE), / ʃiːld əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to protect someone or something from danger or harm
protect from harm or danger

Examples

A strong password can shield against most forms of cyber attack.
Vaccines shield against many infectious diseases.
He wore sunscreen to shield against the sun's harsh rays.

Synset Examples

He used his umbrella as a shield against the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to protect - keep safe from harm or injury
