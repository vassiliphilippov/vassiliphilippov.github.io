to two-time

1. VERB

/ ˈtuː taɪm / (AmE), / ˈtuː taɪm / (BrE)
to be romantically involved with two people at the same time, typically without the knowledge of either
be romantically involved with two people at once
informal

Examples

She was devastated when she discovered he had two-timed her for months.
It's wrong to two-time someone who trusts you completely.
He admitted to two-timing his girlfriend, and now he regrets it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to be unfaithful to one's partner
  • Antonym: faithful - loyal to one's partner; not having other romantic relationships
