to bunk off

1. VERB

/ bʌŋk ɑf / (AmE), / bʌŋk ɒf / (BrE)
to stay away from school or work without permission, usually in order to avoid something or to do something more enjoyable
stay away from school or work without permission
informal british

Examples

They decided to bunk off school and spend the day at the beach.
If you bunk off work again, you might get into trouble.
She was caught bunking off during her final year at college.

Synset Examples

The boy often plays hooky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play hooky - to stay away from school without permission (US informal)
  • Synonym: skip school - to miss school deliberately without permission
