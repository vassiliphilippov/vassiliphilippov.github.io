to tie in knots

1. VERB

/ taɪ ɪn nɑts / (AmE), / taɪ ɪn nɒts / (BrE)
to fasten or secure something, especially rope or string, by making one or more knots in it
to fasten by making knots

Examples

He quickly tied in knots to secure the tent to the stake.
Learning to tie in knots is essential for rock climbing safety.
She practiced how to tie in knots with a piece of string.

Synset Examples

He learned how to tie in knots during sailing camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - an interlacing, fastening, or entanglement made by tying a rope, string, or similar material
