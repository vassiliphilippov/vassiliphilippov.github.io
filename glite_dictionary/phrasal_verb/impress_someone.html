to impress someone

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈprɛs ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛs ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel admiration or respect for you
cause admiration or respect in someone

Examples

He tried hard to impress someone at the interview by talking about his achievements.
It can be difficult to impress someone who has seen it all before.
She wanted to impress someone with her cooking skills at the dinner party.

Synset Examples

He wanted to impress his boss with a great presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspire - to fill someone with the urge to do or feel something
  • Antonym: disappoint someone - to fail to meet someone's expectations or hopes
