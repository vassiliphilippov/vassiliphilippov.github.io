to cast up one's accounts

1. VERB

/ kæst ʌp wʌnz əˈkaʊnts / (AmE), / kɑːst ʌp wʌnz əˈkaʊnts / (BrE)
to calculate or add up financial records, usually to assess total income, expenses, or savings
calculate or summarize financial records
archaic

Examples

At the end of each month, she would always cast up her accounts to ensure nothing was missing.
Before meeting with his accountant, he decided to cast up his accounts for the year.
Merchants in the old days would regularly cast up their accounts to keep track of their trading.

Synset Examples

She cast up her accounts to assess her savings.
After the year ended, he cast up his accounts.
It's time to cast up my accounts and review my finances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add up - to calculate the total of a set of numbers
  • Synonym: to balance the books - to check that records of money taken in and paid out are correct

Etymology

From the verb 'cast' (to calculate or reckon, archaic usage) + 'up', meaning 'to total', and 'account', record of financial transactions.
