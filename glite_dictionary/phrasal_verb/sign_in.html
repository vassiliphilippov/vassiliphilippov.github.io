to sign in

1. VERB

/ saɪn ɪn / (AmE), / saɪn ɪn / (BrE)
to record or announce one's arrival by entering details in a register or system, especially at a hotel, airport, office, or event
record/announce arrival at a place

Examples

All employees must sign in at reception before entering the building.
Please sign in on the sheet at the front desk when you arrive.
After checking in at the airport, passengers are required to sign in at security.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sign out - to record or announce one's departure, especially from a hotel, office, or event
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00968184-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ saɪn ɪn / (AmE), / saɪn ɪn / (BrE)
to register one's arrival, especially by recording one's name in an official register or system when entering a place or starting a session
register one's arrival

Examples

Employees must sign in at the reception desk every morning.
Visitors are required to sign in before entering the laboratory.
I forgot to sign in to my online account before making a purchase.

Synset Examples

Please sign in before accessing the building.
Make sure to sign in using your employee ID.
I forgot to sign in at the front desk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sign out - to register one's departure, especially by recording it in a system or on a register

Synset POS: None
