to play it straight

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɪt streɪt / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪt streɪt / (BrE)
to act in a direct, honest, and straightforward way, without deception or trickery
to act honestly and straightforwardly

Examples

Even when faced with temptation, she always chooses to play it straight.
If you play it straight at work, you'll earn the trust of your colleagues.
During the interview, he decided to play it straight and answer every question honestly.

Synset Examples

You should always play it straight with your clients.
It's important to play it straight in business dealings.
He chose to play it straight during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be up front - to be honest and direct
