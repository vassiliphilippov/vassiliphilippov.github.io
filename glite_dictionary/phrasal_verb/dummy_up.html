to dummy up

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌmi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi ʌp / (BrE)
to refuse to speak or suddenly stop talking, especially when expected to give information
refuse to speak or fall silent
informal

Examples

When the police started asking questions, the suspect dummied up and wouldn't say a word.
Whenever I mention the missing money, he just dummies up and stares at the floor.
She tried to get him to talk about the plan, but he dummied up every time.

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut up - to stop talking or making noise
  • Synonym: to clam up - to suddenly stop talking, often from embarrassment or reluctance
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈdʌmi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi ʌp / (BrE)
to create a mock-up or model of something, especially as a prototype or for demonstration purposes
make a mock-up or prototype

Examples

The design team had to dummy up a brochure to show the client their ideas.
Before the magazine goes to print, the editors dummy up the final layout for approval.
She dummied up a new label so the company could see how it would look on the shelves.

Synset Examples

dummy up the books that are to be published

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mock up - to make a model or replica of something for demonstration or testing

Etymology

From 'dummy' (model or prototype) + 'up' (completing the action); used in printing and publishing.
