to turn against

1. VERB

/ tɝn əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to start to oppose, reject, or become hostile toward someone or something
oppose or become hostile to

Examples

After years of friendship, he decided to turn against her over a minor argument.
Many voters began to turn against the candidate after the scandal was revealed.
She turned against the project when she learned about its environmental impact.

Synset Examples

The community began to turn against the new policy.
It's disheartening when people turn against each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal to someone or something
  • Antonym: support - to give assistance or approval to someone or something
