to break a vow

1. VERB

/ breɪk ə vaʊ / (AmE), / breɪk ə vaʊ / (BrE)
to not do what you promised or committed to do, especially in a solemn or formal context
to violate a solemn promise

Examples

She was devastated when he broke a vow he had made to her years ago.
Monks are traditionally forbidden to break a vow once it is taken.
If you break a vow, you may lose the trust of those around you.

Synset Examples

Breaking a vow is a serious matter in their culture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: keep a vow - to fulfill or uphold a solemn promise or commitment
