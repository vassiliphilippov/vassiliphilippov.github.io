to grant land

1. VERB

/ ɡrænt lænd / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnt lænd / (BrE)
to give legal rights or ownership of land to someone, especially by official or legal means
give legal rights or ownership of land

Examples

The government decided to grant land to local farmers to encourage agriculture.
During medieval times, kings would grant land to loyal knights for their service.
Some charities grant land to wildlife organizations for conservation purposes.

Synset Examples

The king granted land to his vassal in exchange for military service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convey land - transfer ownership or rights to land
