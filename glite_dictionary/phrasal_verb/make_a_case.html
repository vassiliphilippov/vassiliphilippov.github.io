to make a case

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə keɪs / (AmE), / meɪk ə keɪs / (BrE)
to present arguments or reasoning in support of something
present arguments for something

Examples

During the meeting, she tried to make a case for investing in new technology.
If you want the board’s approval, you need to make a case with clear evidence.
He skillfully made a case against the proposed changes in his report.

Synset Examples

You can make a case for keeping the old policy.
She had to make a case for her proposal.
The lawyer will make a case in court next week.
I need to make a case for more funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to give reasons for or against something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to put forward reasons against something
