to feel tiddly

1. VERB

/ fil ˈtɪdli / (AmE), / fiːl ˈtɪdli / (BrE)
to feel slightly drunk or light-headed, especially from drinking alcohol
feel slightly drunk or tipsy
informal british

Examples

After one glass of wine, she began to feel tiddly and giggled at everything.
It's easy to feel tiddly at family parties with all the toasts.
He knew he'd feel tiddly if he had another pint.

Synset Examples

She danced around the room, feeling all tiddly.
After a few drinks, he felt a bit tiddly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tipsy - slightly drunk
