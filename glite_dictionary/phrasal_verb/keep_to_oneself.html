to keep to oneself

1. VERB

/ kip tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / kiːp tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to not share information, feelings, or thoughts with others; to withhold or keep something secret
not share thoughts or secrets

Examples

She prefers to keep to herself and rarely shares personal stories with her colleagues.
He decided to keep the news to himself to avoid worrying his family.
You shouldn't keep your feelings to yourself; talking about them can help.

Synset Examples

I kept your little secret to myself all these years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • Antonym: to divulge - to make known (something private or secret)

2. VERB

/ kip tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / kiːp tʊ wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to avoid socializing or sharing one's thoughts or feelings with others; to be reserved or withdrawn
avoid social contact or be reserved

Examples

After moving to a new city, she preferred to keep to herself for a while.
He tends to keep to himself at parties and rarely starts conversations.
Although friendly, I sometimes keep to myself when I'm feeling tired.

Synset Examples

The new student keeps to herself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdrawn - not wanting to communicate or be involved with other people
  • Antonym: outgoing - sociable and eager to mix with others

/ kip tu wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / kiːp tʊ wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to avoid sharing one's thoughts, feelings, or information with others; to remain private or reserved
avoid sharing thoughts or information

Examples

Tim tends to keep to himself at parties and rarely joins in conversations.
In difficult times, some people prefer to keep to themselves rather than seek support from friends.
She usually kept to herself during meetings, only speaking when necessary.
