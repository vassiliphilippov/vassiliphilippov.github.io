to overproduce for

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərprəˈdus fɔr / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈdjuːs fə / (BrE)
to produce more of something than is needed for a particular occasion, demand, or purpose
produce more than needed for a specific purpose

Examples

Many factories tend to overproduce for major shopping events like Black Friday.
The company did not want to overproduce for the summer sale and risk excess inventory.
If we overproduce for this project, we may waste valuable resources.

Synset Examples

They decided to overproduce for the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overproduce - to produce more than is needed in general
