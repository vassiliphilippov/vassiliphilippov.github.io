to give chase

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv tʃeɪs / (AmE), / ɡɪv tʃeɪs / (BrE)
to start chasing someone or something in order to catch them
begin to pursue or chase

Examples

The guards immediately gave chase when they saw the prisoners escaping.
As soon as the thief ran from the scene, the detective gave chase.
The children laughed as they gave chase to the dog around the garden.

Synset Examples

The policeman chased the mugger down the alley
the fox chased the rabbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow and try to catch or reach someone or something
  • Antonym: to flee - to run away from someone or something

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv tʃeɪs / (AmE), / ɡɪv tʃeɪs / (BrE)
to start pursuing someone or something in order to catch them
begin to pursue someone or something

Examples

When the thief ran from the shop, the security guard gave chase immediately.
The cat saw a bird and instinctively gave chase across the garden.
Several cyclists gave chase after the leader broke away from the pack.

Synset Examples

The police decided to give chase after the suspect.
He had to give chase to catch the runaway ball.
The dog decided to give chase after the squirrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow in order to catch or overtake
  • Antonym: to flee - to run away from a pursuer
