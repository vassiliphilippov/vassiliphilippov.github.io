to level to the ground

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛvəl tə ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl tə ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to completely destroy or flatten a building or structure so that nothing remains above ground
completely destroy or flatten a structure

Examples

The ancient fortress was leveled to the ground during the war.
Powerful winds leveled to the ground several houses in the village.
The developers plan to level to the ground the abandoned factory before starting new construction.

Synset Examples

The demolition crew leveled the structure to the ground.
They had to level the old building to the ground.
The storm leveled the trees to the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raze to the ground - to completely demolish or destroy a structure
