to howl through

1. VERB

/ haʊl θru / (AmE), / haʊl θruː / (BrE)
(of wind, animals, or people) to make a long, loud, wailing sound while moving or passing through a place.
make a wailing sound while passing through

Examples

The winter storm howled through the abandoned barn, rattling every loose windowpane.
Late at night, coyotes howled through the lonely canyons, echoing across the hills.
As midnight approached, a fierce wind howled through the city streets, keeping everyone awake.

Synset Examples

The wind howled through the trees all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, wailing sound
