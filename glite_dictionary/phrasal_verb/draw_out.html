to draw out

1. VERB

/ drɔː aʊt / (AmE), / drɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to encourage or cause someone to speak freely, especially when they are shy or reserved
encourage someone to speak

Examples

The teacher tried to draw out the quieter students during the discussion.
It took several weeks for her new friends to draw out her true personality.
He has a knack for drawing out even the most reserved people in conversation.

Synset Examples

Can you draw her out — she is always so quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - give support or confidence to someone to help them speak or act

/ drɔʊ aʊt / (AmE), / drɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from a place, usually with effort or difficulty; also, to elicit or extract something such as information or feelings
remove or extract, often with effort

Examples

The detective managed to draw out the truth during the interview.
She tried to draw out some information from her reluctant friend.
