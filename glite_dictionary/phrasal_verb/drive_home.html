to drive home

1. VERB

/ draɪv hoʊm / (AmE), / draɪv həʊm / (BrE)
to carry out or perform (an action, attack, or point) with force or emphasis so that it has its intended effect
do something with force for effect

Examples

The striker drove home the winning goal in the final minutes.
She drove home her criticism with a sharp tone that left no doubt about her opinion.
The lawyer drove home his argument with compelling evidence and strong words.

Synset Examples

deliver an attack
deliver a blow
The boxer drove home a solid left

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deliver - to perform or execute (an action, blow, or speech)

2. VERB

/ draɪv hoʊm / (AmE), / draɪv həʊm / (BrE)
to make something clear or forcefully understood, especially by emphasizing it repeatedly
emphasize to make clear

Examples

The teacher tried to drive home the importance of honesty to her students.
He used several examples to drive home his point during the meeting.
She repeated the instructions to drive home how crucial they were.

Synset Examples

drive home a point or an argument
I'm trying to drive home these basic ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - give special importance or prominence to something
  • Synonym: underscore - emphasize (something) or show its importance clearly
