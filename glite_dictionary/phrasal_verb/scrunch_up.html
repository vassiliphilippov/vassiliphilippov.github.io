to scrunch up

1. VERB

/ skrʌntʃ ʌp / (AmE), / skrʌntʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to lower oneself into a crouched position by bending the knees, often bringing the body close to the ground
to crouch down by bending the knees

Examples

The children scrunched up behind the bench to hide from their teacher.
He scrunched up on the floor to examine the piece of paper more closely.
When the rain started, they quickly scrunched up under the small umbrella.

Synset Examples

In some cultures, the women give birth while squatting
The children hunkered down to protect themselves from the sandstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crouch - to lower the body close to the ground by bending the knees
  • Antonym: to stand up - to be in an upright position on the feet

Etymology

From "scrunch" (variant of "crunch," imitative of a crushing sound) + "up".

2. VERB

/ skrʌntʃ ʌp / (AmE), / skrʌntʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to squeeze, fold, or contract something so that it becomes wrinkled or compacted
to squeeze or wrinkle up

Examples

She scrunched up the letter and tossed it in the bin.
The children scrunched up their faces when they tasted the sour candy.
He scrunched up the piece of paper before throwing it away.

Synset Examples

The dress got wrinkled
crease the paper like this to make a crane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crumple - to crush something so that it becomes wrinkled or creased
  • Antonym: to smooth out - to make a surface flat or free from wrinkles

Etymology

Probably imitative; based on the word "scrunch" + "up".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01280991-v
Synset POS: v
