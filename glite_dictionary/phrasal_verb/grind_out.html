to grind out

1. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd aʊt / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd aʊt / (BrE)
to produce something, especially in large quantities and without much enthusiasm or creativity, in a routine or monotonous way
produce routinely or monotonously

Examples

The author managed to grind out three novels last year, all with similar plots.
Some factories grind out products without caring much about quality.
He feels he’s just grinding out reports for his boss, day after day.

Synset Examples

We have to crank out publications in order to receive funding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crank out - to produce something in large quantities, often quickly and without much thought
