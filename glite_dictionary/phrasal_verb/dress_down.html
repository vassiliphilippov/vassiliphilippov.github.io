to dress down

1. VERB

/ drɛs daʊn / (AmE), / drɛs daʊn / (BrE)
to reprimand or scold someone severely, especially in anger
reprimand or scold harshly

Examples

The manager dressed down the employee for consistently arriving late.
She really dressed down her son after he broke the window.
The coach dressed down the entire team for their lack of effort.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to criticize angrily
  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

2. VERB

/ drɛs daʊn / (AmE), / drɛs daʊn / (BrE)
to wear informal or casual clothes, especially when a more formal style is usual or expected
wear informal or casual clothing

Examples

Many people choose to dress down when working from home.
The company allows employees to dress down on Fridays.
She decided to dress down for the casual dinner with friends.

Synset Examples

On Fridays, employees can underdress

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dress up - to wear formal or special clothes

3. VERB

/ drɛs daʊn / (AmE), / drɛs daʊn / (BrE)
1. To reprimand or scold someone severely. 2. To wear less formal or casual clothing than usual.
reprimand someone; wear casual clothing

Examples

The coach had to dress down the players after their poor performance.
On Fridays, employees are allowed to dress down and wear jeans to work.
She was embarrassed when her boss dressed down her in front of the whole team.

Synset Examples

She had to dress down the team after the mistake.
The manager decided to dress down for casual Friday.
He was told to dress down for the informal meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: to dress up - to wear more formal or special clothes
