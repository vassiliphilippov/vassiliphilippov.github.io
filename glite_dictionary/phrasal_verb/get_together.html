to get together

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ɡɛt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to bring people together for a meeting or activity
bring people together

Examples

She managed to get together all her old school friends for the reunion.
The teacher will get together the students to discuss the project.
He tried to get together everyone interested in joining the club.

Synset Examples

assemble your colleagues
get together all those who are interested in the project
gather the close family members

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to bring people or things together in one place for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: gather - to bring people together into a group

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ɡɛt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to meet or gather socially or for a specific purpose
meet or assemble socially

Examples

We should get together for coffee sometime and catch up.
The committee will get together next week to discuss the new plans.
Our friends got together last Friday for a barbecue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meet up - to meet with someone, especially by arrangement
  • Antonym: split up - to break up or stop meeting as a group
