to come on over

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɑn ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / kʌm ɒn ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to go to someone's home, usually by invitation, often for a social visit
visit someone's home
informal

Examples

If you're free this evening, you should come on over and watch the game with us.
She told her friends to come on over after work for some snacks and drinks.
Whenever I'm feeling down, my best friend always says I can come on over.

Synset Examples

Feel free to come on over anytime!
She invited me to come on over for coffee.
Why don't you come on over for dinner tonight?
You guys should come over for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drop by - to visit someone briefly and informally
  • Synonym: to come by - to visit someone's home or location
