to dredge up

1. VERB

/ drɛdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / drɛdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to mention or bring to attention an unpleasant or forgotten event, fact, or memory from the past, often causing discomfort
mention something unpleasant from the past

Examples

He didn't want to dredge up painful memories from his childhood.
Whenever we argue, she always dredges up mistakes I made years ago.
The documentary dredged up scandals that the company had hoped everyone would forget.

Synset Examples

Drag up old stories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring up - to introduce a subject or mention something from the past
  • Antonym: to suppress - to deliberately avoid mentioning or keeping something hidden

2. VERB

/ drɛdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / drɛdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to bring something forgotten, hidden, or unpleasant into notice or discussion again; to recall or mention a past event, issue, or memory
recall or bring up something from the past

Examples

The documentary managed to dredge up painful memories from her childhood.
Every family gathering seems to dredge up old arguments we'd rather forget.
He struggled to dredge up details from his university days for the reunion.

Synset Examples

It’s hard to dredge up enthusiasm for Mondays.
She tried not to dredge up past arguments during dinner.
I had to dredge up old memories for the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to remember something
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember something

Etymology

From 'dredge', meaning to scoop up from the bottom, combined with 'up'; figurative sense from original physical meaning.
