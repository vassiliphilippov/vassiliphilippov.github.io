to buckle to

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌkəl tu / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl tuː / (BrE)
to yield or give in to pressure or criticism
to yield to pressure or criticism

Examples

Despite mounting criticism, she refused to buckle to public pressure.
The company will not buckle to threats from competitors.
He finally buckled to his parents' wishes after months of arguing.

Synset Examples

She won't buckle to anyone's criticism.
He refused to buckle to peer pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give in - to yield, especially reluctantly, to pressure or force
  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the force of something; to not give in
