to walk all over someone

1. VERB

/ wɔk ɔl ˈoʊvɚ ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / wɔːk ɔːl ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to treat someone in a disrespectful way by taking advantage of them or ignoring their rights or feelings
treat someone disrespectfully or take advantage

Examples

If you don't set boundaries, people will walk all over you.
She was tired of letting her boss walk all over her during meetings.
He decided to stop being polite and refused to let them walk all over him.

Synset Examples

He felt like his colleagues were trying to walk all over him.
Don't let anyone walk all over you at work.
You need to stand up for yourself, or they'll walk all over you.
She realized he was trying to walk over her during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take advantage of - to exploit someone for personal gain
  • Antonym: stand up for yourself - to defend yourself against unfair treatment
