to filter up

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪltɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə ʌp / (BrE)
(of information or influence) to move from lower to higher levels within an organization, typically by being passed upward from subordinates to superiors.
to move upward in an organization (of information)

Examples

Concerns about workplace safety can filter up to the executive team through several channels.
It sometimes takes time for feedback from customers to filter up to decision-makers.
In many organizations, only major issues filter up to the top management for consideration.

Synset Examples

We hope our suggestions will filter up to the board.
Concerns from the team need to filter up to management.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to filter down - (of information or influence) to move from higher to lower levels within an organization
