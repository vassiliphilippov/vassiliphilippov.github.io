to cut in

1. VERB

/ kʌt ɪn / (AmE), / kʌt ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt a dancing couple in order to take one of them as one's own partner
interrupt a couple to dance with one partner

Examples

At formal dances, it was common for young men to cut in during slow songs.
She hoped no one would cut in before the end of the waltz.
Just as they started dancing, another guest tried to cut in and steal her away.

Synset Examples

Jim always cuts in!

3. VERB

/ kʌt ɪn / (AmE), / kʌt ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt someone who is speaking, especially by joining a conversation suddenly and without waiting your turn
interrupt a conversation

Examples

Please don't cut in while others are talking.
He annoyed everyone by repeatedly cutting in during the meeting.
Just as she started her story, John cut in with a question.

/ kʌt ɪn / (AmE), / kʌt ɪn / (BrE)

It's so frustrating when drivers cut in without signaling on the highway.
