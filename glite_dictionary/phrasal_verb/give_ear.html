to give ear

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪə / (BrE)
to listen carefully or pay close attention to someone or something, especially in a formal or literary context
listen carefully; pay attention (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

You would be wise to give ear to the advice of your elders.
He gave no sign that he would give ear to their complaints.
The crowd stood in silence, giving ear to the speaker's passionate words.

Synset Examples

The children in the audience attended the recital quietly
She hung on his every word
They attended to everything he said

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to listen - to give attention to sound or to someone speaking
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to

Etymology

From Middle English, literally 'give' + 'ear' (sense of 'listen'), based on Biblical and literary usage.
