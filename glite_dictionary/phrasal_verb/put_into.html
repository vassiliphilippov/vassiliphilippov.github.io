to put into

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pʊt ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to invest effort, time, emotion, or another resource into an activity or project
invest effort, time, or emotion

Examples

She really put into her studies this semester and improved her grades.
The team put into a lot of hard work to finish the project on time.
You can always tell when someone has put into their performance.

Synset Examples

She put her whole pussy into her dance performance.
It’s pitiful how little effort he puts into his work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devote - to give a large amount of time or resources to something
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove effort, emotion, or resources from an activity
