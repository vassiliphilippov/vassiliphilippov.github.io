to steer clear of

1. VERB

/ stɪr ˈklɪr ʌv / (AmE), / stɪə ˈklɪə ɒv / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid someone or something that may cause trouble or danger
deliberately avoid or keep away from

Examples

You should always steer clear of unfamiliar streets at night.
She tries to steer clear of arguments with her colleagues.
It's best to steer clear of that topic during family dinners.

Synset Examples

He advised me to steer clear of bad investments.
You should steer clear of that restaurant.
I always steer clear of confrontations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from or stop oneself from doing something
  • Antonym: approach - to come near or nearer to something
