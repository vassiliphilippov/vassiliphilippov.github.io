to get it

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪt / (BrE)
to understand something, often after some initial confusion or difficulty
understand (often after difficulty)
informal

Examples

It took me a while to get it, but now the instructions are clear.
He kept explaining the joke until everyone finally got it.
Sometimes she pretends not to get it to avoid answering.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to begin to understand
  • Synonym: grasp - to understand something fully
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to understand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00591761-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪt / (BrE)
to be punished, scolded, or reprimanded, especially as a result of one's actions
be punished or scolded
informal

Examples

If my parents find out I lied, I'll really get it.
The coach was furious, and the whole team got it after the game.
You'd better finish your homework, or you'll get it when your teacher checks.

Synset Examples

I really caught it the other day!
You are going to get it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch it - be punished or scolded for something one has done wrong
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02506138-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪt / (BrE)
to understand or comprehend something, especially after some difficulty or explanation
understand, comprehend

Examples

It took me a while, but now I finally get it.
If you don't get it, just ask the teacher for help.
She smiled when she realized he didn't get it at first.

Synset Examples

Did you get it? The answer was in the text.
I finally get it; the project needs more time.
Once he explained it, I began to get it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to understand - to grasp the meaning of something
  • Synonym: to comprehend - to fully understand or grasp something
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to understand
