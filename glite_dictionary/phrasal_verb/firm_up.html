to firm up

1. VERB

/ fɝm ʌp / (AmE), / fɜːm ʌp / (BrE)
to make something (such as an arrangement or plan) more definite or certain
make more definite or certain

Examples

We need to firm up the meeting date before sending out invitations.
She called the hotel to firm up their reservation for this weekend.
They are trying to firm up the contract details before the deadline.

Synset Examples

firm up one's plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finalize - to make final or complete
  • Synonym: settle - to resolve or decide upon

2. VERB

/ fɝːm ʌp / (AmE), / fɜːm ʌp / (BrE)
To make an agreement, plan, or decision more definite, complete, or final.
make definite or finalize (plan, agreement, etc.)

Examples

We need to firm up the schedule before booking the venue.
The negotiations will firm up after all parties agree on the terms.
She's working to firm up the proposal for submission next week.

Synset Examples
