to pass around

1. VERB

/ pæs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / pɑːs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to distribute something to each person in a group, usually by having each person hand it to the next
distribute to each person in a group

Examples

Please pass around the signup sheet so everyone gets a chance to write their name.
The teacher passed around the exam papers before starting the test.
At the meeting, someone passed around a basket for donations.

Synset Examples

This letter is being circulated among the faculty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to circulate - to distribute among a group so that everyone sees or receives it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02047080-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pæs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / pɑːs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to cause something, such as information or an object, to be distributed or shared widely among a group of people
distribute or share widely

Examples

Rumors about the new policy quickly began to pass around the office.
She didn't want her personal information to pass around the community.
The memo was passed around so that everyone could read it.

Synset Examples

spread information
circulate a rumor
broadcast the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to circulate - to spread or distribute widely
  • Synonym: spread - to make something known or available to more people
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00970243-v
Synset POS: v
