to bring round

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ raʊnd / (AmE), / brɪŋ raʊnd / (BrE)
to persuade someone to adopt a particular opinion or course of action
persuade to adopt an opinion or action

Examples

It took some effort, but I finally brought round my colleagues to support my proposal.
They tried hard to bring round their parents to their way of thinking.
The manager was skeptical at first, but Sarah successfully brought round him to the new strategy.

Synset Examples

His urgent letter finally brought me around to give money to the school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to convince - persuade someone to accept a point of view
  • Antonym: to dissuade - persuade someone not to do something
