to hark back to

1. VERB

/ hɑrk bæk tu / (AmE), / hɑːk bæk tʊ / (BrE)
to mention, remember, or be reminiscent of something from an earlier time
recall or refer to the past

Examples

The architecture of the new library harks back to classical Greek temples.
Whenever politics comes up, he tends to hark back to his days in office.
This tradition harks back to medieval festivals celebrated in the town square.

Synset Examples

This design seems to hark back to the 80s.
She likes to hark back to her childhood memories.
Their music tends to hark back to earlier rock styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reminisce - to recall or talk about past experiences with pleasure
  • Synonym: recall - to remember or bring to mind a past fact or event
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember or deliberately not think about the past

Etymology

From Middle English 'herken' (to listen), with 'back' suggesting a return to something earlier.
