to tick off

1. VERB

/ tɪk ɔf / (AmE), / tɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to mark with a check mark to show that something has been dealt with or completed
mark with a check or tick
british

Examples

Please tick off each box as you complete the steps.
She ticked off all the tasks on her to-do list before noon.
At the door, the staff will tick off your name when you arrive.

Synset Examples

Please check each name on the list
tick off the items
mark off the units

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to check off - to mark items as complete or verified, especially with a check mark

Etymology

From 'tick' meaning a check mark, plus 'off' indicating completion; chiefly British in this sense.
