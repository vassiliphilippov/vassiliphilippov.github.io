to wear away

1. VERB

/ wɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɛə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually reduce, remove, or erode something, especially by continuous use, rubbing, or friction
erode or diminish by friction or use

Examples

Years of footsteps have worn away the stone steps.
The paint on the old fence wore away over time.
Constant washing will wear away the fabric's softness.

Synset Examples

Erosion wore away the surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to wear away gradually, usually by natural forces
  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less; reduce

2. VERB

/ wɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɛə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually remove or erode the surface of something, usually by friction, use, or exposure to elements
gradually erode or remove

Examples

Over time, the constant flow of water began to wear away the stone surface.
The inscription on the old coin has worn away and is now difficult to read.
You must act quickly before rust wears away the metal completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually destroy or wear away (something) by natural forces
  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become progressively worse; to diminish in quality or value

3. VERB

/ wɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɛə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to become gradually reduced, damaged, or destroyed by continuous use or exposure
gradually erode or deteriorate

Examples

After years of wind and rain, the stone steps began to wear away.
Repeated criticism can wear away a person's confidence over time.
The paint on the old bench has worn away from years of use.

Synset Examples

Her confidence eroded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away or destroy
  • Synonym: decay - to break down or decline gradually
  • Antonym: to build up - to increase or accumulate over time (opposite of wear away)
