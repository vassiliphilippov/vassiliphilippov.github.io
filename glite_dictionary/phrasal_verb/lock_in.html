to lock in

1. VERB

/ lɑk ɪn / (AmE), / lɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose or secure something so that it cannot escape, often by sealing tightly to preserve quality or freshness
seal tightly to preserve or secure

Examples

This new packaging technology helps lock in moisture and flavor.
The chef quickly covered the dish to lock in the aromas.
Double-paned windows are designed to lock in heat during the winter.

Synset Examples

This vacuum pack locks in the flavor!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seal in - enclose firmly to prevent escape or loss
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01350695-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɑk ɪn / (AmE), / lɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to put something or someone in a place where removal or escape is not possible, often by securing with a lock or similar mechanism
confine or secure with no escape

Examples

He threatened to lock in the valuables every night for safety.
The caretaker accidentally locked in the cat before leaving the house.
Strict rules lock in workers during the night shift to prevent theft.

Synset Examples

The parents locked her daughter up for the weekend
She locked her jewels in the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lock up - to confine someone or something in a locked place, especially in prison or jail
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01350360-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lɑk ɪn / (AmE), / lɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to make something definite or secured, such as an investment, arrangement, or decision, so that it cannot easily be changed
secure or make definite a commitment or arrangement

Examples

We need to lock in these interest rates before they go up again.
Once you've locked in your plans, it can be hard to make changes.
She wants to lock in the date for her wedding as soon as possible.

Synset Examples

He decided to lock in his investment for five years.
We should lock in the meeting time early.
I need to lock in my travel plans by Friday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finalize - to complete all the details and make definite
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide not to proceed with a previously arranged plan or commitment
