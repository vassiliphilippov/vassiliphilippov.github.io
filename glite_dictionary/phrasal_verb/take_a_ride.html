to take a ride

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə raɪd / (AmE), / teɪk ə raɪd / (BrE)
to go for a casual drive or trip, especially for leisure rather than necessity
go for a casual drive or trip

Examples

Let's take a ride along the coast this afternoon and relax for a while.
Whenever she feels stressed, she likes to take a ride around the city to clear her mind.
We decided to take a ride out to the mountains over the weekend.

Synset Examples

We should take a ride to Tyburn one day.
She mentioned taking a ride to Tyburn after work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go for a ride - to travel somewhere for pleasure in a vehicle
