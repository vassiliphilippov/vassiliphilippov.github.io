to split up

1. VERB

/ splɪt ʌp / (AmE), / splɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to separate something into parts or portions
divide into parts

Examples

They decided to split up the inheritance among the three siblings.
We should split up the group into smaller teams for the project.
First, split up the dough into equal portions before baking.

Synset Examples

divide the cake into three equal parts
The British carved up the Ottoman Empire after World War I

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate something into parts; to split
  • Synonym: to carve up - to divide something into several parts, often with a sense of distribution

2. VERB

/ splɪt ʌp / (AmE), / splɪt ʌp / (BrE)
(of married or romantic partners) to end a marriage or relationship, especially by getting a divorce.
end a marriage or relationship

Examples

After years of arguments, they decided to split up and file for divorce.
Many couples split up when they realize they want different things in life.
Their parents split up when the kids were still very young.

Synset Examples

The couple divorced after only 6 months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divorce - to legally dissolve a marriage
  • Synonym: separate - to stop living together or being in a romantic relationship

/ splɪt ʌp / (AmE), / splɪt ʌp / (BrE)

Examples

The old tree eventually split up during the violent storm.

Synset Examples

The freshly baked loaf fell apart

Related Terms
