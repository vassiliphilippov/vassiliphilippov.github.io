to nod out

1. VERB

/ nɑd aʊt / (AmE), / nɒd aʊt / (BrE)
to fall asleep, especially unintentionally or from exhaustion
fall asleep, often from fatigue
informal

Examples

After the movie, I started to nod out in my chair.
She was so tired she would nod out during the lectures.
If you nod out at your desk, you'll miss the meeting.

Synset Examples

After the long day, he just wanted to nod out.
He was so exhausted, he nearly nodded out on the couch.
She tends to nod out if she sits too long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to doze off - to fall asleep, especially briefly or unintentionally
  • Synonym: to pass out - to lose consciousness or fall asleep suddenly
