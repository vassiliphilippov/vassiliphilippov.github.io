to tick over

1. VERB

/ tɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / tɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an engine or machine) to run slowly or idly without doing any useful work; to operate at a low, steady rate without stopping.
(of engines) run slowly without working hard

Examples

The old car's engine would tick over quietly while we waited for the mechanic.
On cold mornings, I let the generator tick over before starting work.
Even when not in use, the equipment must tick over to be ready for immediate operation.

Synset Examples

the engine is idling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - (of an engine) to run at low speed, disconnected

2. VERB

/ tɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / tɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a machine, business, or system) to operate or run in a steady, continuous, usually unremarkable way.
operate steadily or smoothly

Examples

The old generator still manages to tick over after all these years.
During the holidays, the office continued to tick over with a small staff.
It's important to let the car engine tick over for a few minutes after starting.

Synset Examples

Let the computer tick over while it updates.
The business continues to tick over with steady sales.
The engine will tick over smoothly if maintained well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run smoothly - to function without interruption or problems
  • Antonym: malfunction - to fail to operate properly
