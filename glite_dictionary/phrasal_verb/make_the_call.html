to make the call

1. VERB

/ meɪk ðə kɔl / (AmE), / meɪk ðə kɔːl / (BrE)
to make a decision or take action, especially when a judgment or choice must be made
make a decision or take action

Examples

As team leader, I had to make the call on whether to continue the project.
Sometimes you just have to trust your instincts and make the call.
After much debate, she finally made the call to relocate the office.

Synset Examples

If you're convinced, go on then and make the call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decide - to make a choice or judgment
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause before making a decision or acting
