to win back

1. VERB

/ wɪn bæk / (AmE), / wɪn bæk / (BrE)
to recover or regain something or someone that was previously lost
regain something lost

Examples

After a long campaign, she finally won back her supporters' trust.
The company hopes to win back customers by improving its services.
He is determined to win back the championship title this year.

Synset Examples

We got back the money after we threatened to sue the company
He got back his son from the kidnappers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to get something back that was lost or taken away
  • Antonym: to lose - to cease to have something because it has been taken or misplaced
