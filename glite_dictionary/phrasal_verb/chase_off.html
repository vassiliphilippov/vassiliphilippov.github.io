to chase off

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ɑf / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ɒf / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place by chasing, scaring, or threatening them
drive away; scare away

Examples

The farmer shouted to chase off the fox threatening his chickens.
We had to chase off some teenagers who were making noise in the park late at night.
The security guard quickly chased off anyone trying to loiter near the entrance.

Synset Examples

They tried to chase off the stray dogs from the yard.
She yelled to chase off the intruders.
The farmer used noise to chase off the birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drive away - to make someone or something leave a place
  • Synonym: to scare off - to make frightened so as to go away
