to tool around

1. VERB

/ tuːl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / tuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to drive or move about with no fixed destination, especially for enjoyment or leisure
drive or wander for pleasure with no set goal
informal american

Examples

On weekends, we like to tool around the city with no real plans.
After dinner, they decided to tool around the countryside in his old convertible.
Sometimes I just need to tool around for a while to clear my mind.

Synset Examples

We tooled down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruise - to drive casually for pleasure, especially without a destination

Etymology

From slang use of 'tool' meaning to drive or move in a vehicle, first recorded in early 20th-century American English.

2. VERB

/ tuːl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / tuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move around aimlessly or in a casual manner, often for enjoyment rather than a specific purpose
move or drive around casually for fun
informal

Examples

On Sundays, we like to tool around the countryside and see what we discover.
They spent the afternoon tooling around the shopping mall with no real plans.
Sometimes I tool around town just to clear my head.

Synset Examples

We decided to tool around the city this weekend.
Let’s tool around the park before heading home.
I love to tool around on my bike after work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruise - to travel or move leisurely, especially in a car or boat, without a specific destination
  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way
