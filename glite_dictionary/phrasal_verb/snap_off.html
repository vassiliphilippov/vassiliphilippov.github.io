to snap off

1. VERB

/ snæp ɔf / (AmE), / snæp ɒf / (BrE)
to break a piece from something, especially with a sharp or sudden movement
break a piece off suddenly

Examples

She tried to snap off a small twig for her bouquet.
If you twist the chocolate bar, it will snap off easily.
The mechanic had to snap off the rusty bolt to remove the part.

Synset Examples

break a branch from a tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break off - to detach a piece from something by breaking
