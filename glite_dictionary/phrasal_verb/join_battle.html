to join battle

1. VERB

/ dʒɔɪn ˈbætəl / (AmE), / dʒɔɪn ˈbætəl / (BrE)
to engage in a conflict or dispute, especially by actively participating in it
engage in conflict or dispute

Examples

After months of debate, the committee finally decided to join battle over the proposed changes.
Many countries were reluctant to join battle in the growing international dispute.
When she criticized his plan, he was quick to join battle and defend his ideas.

Synset Examples

The battle over health care reform was joined

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engage - to enter into conflict or participate actively in something
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to cease to participate in a conflict or dispute
