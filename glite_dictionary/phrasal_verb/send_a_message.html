to send a message

1. VERB

/ sɛnd ə ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / sɛnd ə ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
to give a clear signal or indication of a particular attitude, intention, or situation, often without stating it directly
to give a clear signal or indication

Examples

The manager's strict policy changes send a message that lateness will not be tolerated.
By refusing to participate, she hoped to send a message about the need for reform.
The new safety measures send a message to employees that their well-being is taken seriously.

Synset Examples

The lack of good teachers sends a strong message to all parents in the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - to convey information or instructions by means of a gesture, action, or sound
  • Synonym: to imply - to strongly suggest the truth or existence of something not explicitly stated
