to gather intel

1. VERB

/ ˈɡæðɚ ɪnˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə ɪnˈtɛl / (BrE)
to collect information, especially for military or strategic purposes
collect information (esp. military)

Examples

The team was sent behind enemy lines to gather intel on troop movements.
She used her contacts to gather intel before the mission began.
Journalists often gather intel before publishing investigative reports.

Synset Examples

We planned a sortie to gather intel on the enemy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect information - to gather data or facts for a purpose

Etymology

'Intel' is a shortening of 'intelligence' in the sense of information, especially military.
