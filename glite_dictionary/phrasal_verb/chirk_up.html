to chirk up

1. VERB

/ tʃɜrk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɜːk ʌp / (BrE)
to become or make oneself more cheerful or lively
become (more) cheerful

Examples

It took her a while to chirk up after hearing the good news.
Let’s put on some music to help the kids chirk up a bit.
He finally chirked up once his friends arrived at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cheer up - to become or make happier

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly imitative, related to 'cheer' and similar to 'chirp'.

2. VERB

/ tʃɝːk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɜːk ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone or something more cheerful or lively; to cheer up or enliven
make more cheerful or lively

Examples

They tried to chirk up the gloomy classroom with some colorful decorations.
The comedian's stories helped chirk up the crowd during the long event.
After hearing the sad news, it took a while for her friends to chirk up her spirits.

Synset Examples

Let's chirk up the meeting with some fun activities.
She tried to chirk up the atmosphere with jokes.
The music helped to chirk up the party mood.
He managed to chirk her up after a rough day.
He always knows how to chirk up his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cheer up - to become or make someone happier or less sad

Etymology

Perhaps a variant of 'cheer up,' influenced by dialectal 'chirk' (to become lively or cheerful).
