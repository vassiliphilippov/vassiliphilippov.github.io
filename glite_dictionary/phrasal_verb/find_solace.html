to find solace

1. VERB

/ faɪnd ˈsɑlɪs / (AmE), / faɪnd ˈsɒlɪs / (BrE)
to gain comfort or consolation in a time of distress or sadness
gain comfort in distress

Examples

After her loss, she tried to find solace in spending time with friends.
Many people find solace in nature when they are feeling overwhelmed by life.
He found solace in music, which helped him heal after the breakup.

Synset Examples

They found solace in the green tree … in the dry.
He found solace and was at peace with his choices.
She found solace in writing poetry.
He found solace and community within Quakerism's teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - a feeling of relief or consolation
