to drum up

1. VERB

/ drʌm ʌp / (AmE), / drʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to try to increase or generate something, such as interest, support, or business, often by making an effort to attract attention or persuade people
to try to generate or gather support or interest

Examples

The team worked hard to drum up support for their new project.
She handed out flyers downtown to drum up interest in the upcoming concert.
The company is looking for ways to drum up more business during the slow season.

Synset Examples

drum up support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solicit - to ask for or try to obtain something from someone
  • Synonym: rally - to bring people together in order to support a cause

Etymology

From the metaphor of beating a drum to draw attention, first used in the 17th century.

2. VERB

/ drʌm ʌp / (AmE), / drʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to try to increase or encourage support, interest, or business for something, often actively or energetically
actively generate interest or support

Examples

The marketing team worked hard to drum up interest in the new campaign.
She traveled across the country to drum up support for her project.
Social media can be a useful way to drum up excitement about an upcoming event.

Synset Examples

She managed to drum up interest in the new product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to generate - to cause something to exist; to create
  • Synonym: spark - to cause the start of something, especially excitement or interest
  • Antonym: to dampen - to make something less lively or intense; to reduce (opposite of generating excitement)

Etymology

Originally a figurative use of 'drum,' as in beating a drum to attract attention.
