to make a point

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / meɪk ə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to deliberately and purposefully do something, especially by giving it special attention
do something deliberately or intentionally

Examples

She always makes a point of greeting new colleagues on their first day.
I made a point to thank everyone who helped with the project.
If you want to improve, make a point of practicing every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a point of - to ensure that you always do something, emphasizing intention
