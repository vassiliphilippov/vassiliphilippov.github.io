to wear off

1. VERB

/ wɛr ɔf / (AmE), / wɛə ɒf / (BrE)
(of an effect or feeling) to gradually disappear or become less strong over time.
gradually diminish or disappear

Examples

The effects of the painkiller began to wear off after a few hours.
His enthusiasm for the new job started to wear off as the weeks went by.
Don't worry, the numbness from the dentist will wear off soon.

Synset Examples

Erosion wore away the surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade - to gradually disappear or become weaker
  • Synonym: to diminish - to become or make less
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01256888-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɛr ɔf / (AmE), / wɛə ɒf / (BrE)
to become gradually damaged, weakened, or less effective through continued use or stress
become less effective or deteriorate through use

Examples

The painkiller's effect began to wear off after a few hours.
The color on the old sign had started to wear off from years in the sun.
With repeated washing, the print on the shirt will eventually wear off.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear out - to use something so much that it is no longer usable
