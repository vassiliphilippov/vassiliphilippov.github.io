to sweat off

1. VERB

/ swɛt ɑf / (AmE), / swɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to lose weight or bodily fluid through sweating, especially as a result of physical activity or heat
lose weight by sweating

Examples

After running five miles, she claimed to have sweated off two pounds.
Boxers often sweat off extra weight before a fight by training in heated rooms.
He tried to sweat off his fever by wearing several layers of clothing and sitting by the heater.

Synset Examples

I sweated off 3 pounds in the sauna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweat away - to lose (something, especially weight) by sweating
