to make a motion

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
formally propose a suggestion or course of action, especially in a meeting or debate.
formally propose in a meeting

Examples

During the meeting, she made a motion to postpone the discussion until next week.
Any member of the committee may make a motion to amend the agenda.
Before we proceed, does anyone want to make a motion on this issue?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propose a motion - suggest a motion formally, esp. in a meeting
  • Synonym: second a motion - formally support a proposed motion
