to lose oneself in nature

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnˈsɛlf ɪn ˈneɪtʃər / (AmE), / luːz wʌnˈsɛlf ɪn ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
to become deeply absorbed or immersed in the natural environment, often feeling separated from daily concerns
become immersed in nature

Examples

She can lose herself in nature for hours, listening to the birds and watching the trees sway.
Many people lose themselves in nature as a way to escape the stresses of urban life.
During his vacation, he lost himself in nature while exploring the national park.

Synset Examples

He loves to lose himself in nature during hikes.
