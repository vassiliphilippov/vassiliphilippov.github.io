to come with

1. VERB

/ kʌm wɪð / (AmE), / kʌm wɪð / (BrE)
to be included with or to accompany something as a usual or expected part
to be included or accompany

Examples

All main dishes come with a side salad and bread.
This smartphone comes with a warranty and free technical support.
Risks come with every investment, so it's important to be prepared.

Synset Examples

French fries come with the hamburger
heart attacks are accompanied by destruction of heart tissue
fish usually goes with white wine
this kind of vein accompanies certain arteries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to go with or be present with someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02722040-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm wɪð / (AmE), / kʌm wɪð / (BrE)
to occur as a result of something, or to be a natural consequence of an action or situation.
occur as a result of something

Examples

Wisdom often comes with age and experience.
Satisfaction comes with completing a challenging project.
Greater responsibility comes with a promotion at work.

Synset Examples

The chef believes appetite comes with eating well-prepared dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result from - to be caused by something
  • Synonym: to follow - to happen as a consequence
  • Antonym: to precede - to come before something in time or order
