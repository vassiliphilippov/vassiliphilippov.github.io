to gimp around

1. VERB

/ ɡɪmp əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɪmp əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move awkwardly or slowly, often due to injury or stiffness
move awkwardly or slowly
informal

Examples

After spraining his ankle, he could only gimp around the house for a few days.
She didn’t want anyone to see her gimp around the office after her surgery.
Instead of gimping around in pain, why not visit a doctor?

Synset Examples

Don't gimp around; just ask for help!

Etymology

Sense of 'gimp' meaning 'to limp' dates from mid-19th century American English; origin unclear, possibly a variant of 'gammy.'
