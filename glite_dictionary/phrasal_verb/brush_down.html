to brush down

1. VERB

/ brʌʃ daʊn / (AmE), / brʌʃ daʊn / (BrE)
to reprimand someone, especially in a sharp or forceful manner
reprimand sharply

Examples

After the mistake was found, the manager brushed down the junior staff in front of everyone.
He was brushed down by his coach for not following instructions during practice.
If you keep talking during the meeting, you'll just get brushed down again.

Synset Examples

She told the misbehaving student off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tell off - to reprimand someone harshly
  • Synonym: scold - to criticize angrily
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00827309-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brʌʃ daʊn / (AmE), / brʌʃ daʊn / (BrE)
to clean a surface or object by brushing to remove dust, dirt, or debris
clean by brushing off dust or dirt

Examples

She used a soft cloth to brush down the dusty shelves before placing the books on them.
After riding, he took a few minutes to brush down his horse thoroughly.
Before visitors arrive, please brush down the hallway carpet to remove any debris.

Synset Examples

Make sure to brush down the furniture before polishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brush off - remove something from a surface by brushing
