to bond with

1. VERB

/ bɑnd wɪð / (AmE), / bɒnd wɪð / (BrE)
to develop a close and trusting relationship with someone or something
form a close relationship

Examples

It took a few weeks for the puppy to bond with its new owner.
During the trip, the coworkers had a chance to bond with each other outside the office.
Reading together is a great way for parents to bond with their children.

Synset Examples

He aims to -en the bond with his team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect with - to establish a rapport or understanding with someone
  • Antonym: detach from - to separate from; to lose emotional closeness
