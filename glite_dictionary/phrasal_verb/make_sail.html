to make sail

1. VERB

/ meɪk seɪl / (AmE), / meɪk seɪl / (BrE)
to begin sailing or start a journey by boat
to set out on a journey by boat

Examples

The captain shouted that it was time to make sail and head into the open sea.
We will make sail at sunrise to take advantage of the morning tide.
As soon as the storm cleared, the crew prepared to make sail.

Synset Examples

The captain shouted, 'It's time to make sail!'
They decided to make sail as the winds picked up.
We will make sail at dawn to catch the tide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set sail - to begin a voyage by boat or ship
