to fatten up

1. VERB

/ ˈfæt̬n ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfæt.ən ʌp / (BrE)
to make a person or animal fatter or plumper, often by providing more food
make someone or something fatter

Examples

The farmer tried to fatten up the pigs before selling them.
Doctors advised her to fatten up after a long illness left her underweight.
They are trying to fatten up the dog by giving it extra treats.

Synset Examples

We will plump out that poor starving child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to slim down - become thinner or make thinner

2. VERB

/ ˈfætn̩ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfætn̩ ʌp / (BrE)
to make or become heavier or plumper, especially in preparation for something such as sale, hibernation, or survival
make or become fatter

Examples

Farmers often fatten up pigs before taking them to market.
She needs to fatten up a little before the cold season arrives.
Wild animals instinctively fatten up as winter approaches.

Synset Examples

I plan to fatten up before the winter hibernation.
They need to fatten up the cattle before selling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to slim down - to make or become thinner
