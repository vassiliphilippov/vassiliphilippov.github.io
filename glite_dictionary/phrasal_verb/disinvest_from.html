to disinvest from

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛst frəm / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛst frəm / (BrE)
to withdraw financial investment from a business, industry, or project, typically for economic, ethical, or strategic reasons.
withdraw investment from something

Examples

Many universities have voted to disinvest from companies involved in fossil fuels.
Some shareholders are pushing to disinvest from industries with poor environmental records.
After years of losses, the firm decided to disinvest from its overseas subsidiary.

Synset Examples

The company plans to disinvest from its unprofitable ventures.
He urged the board to disinvest in fossil fuels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divest from - to rid oneself of an investment or asset
  • Antonym: to invest in - to put money into a business or project

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'invest', modeled on 'disinvest'.
