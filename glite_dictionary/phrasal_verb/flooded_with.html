to flooded with

1. VERB

/ ˈflʌdɪd wɪð / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɪd wɪð / (BrE)
filled or overwhelmed by a large quantity of something, especially more than can easily be handled
overwhelmed by a large amount

Examples

My inbox was flooded with messages after the new policy was announced.
The customer service lines were flooded with complaints following the outage.
During the holiday season, the store is often flooded with shoppers looking for discounts.

Synset Examples

The forum was flooded with posts from a spammy pornbot.
The online debate was flooded with wumao comments.
The forum was flooded with religitard opinions.
The internet is flooded with free porn these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamped with - overwhelmed with too much of something
  • Synonym: overrun with - infested or covered with an excessive amount

Etymology

Metaphorical use of the verb 'flood' (to cover or fill with water) plus 'with', indicating what is overflowing.
