to latch onto

1. VERB

/ lætʃ ˈɑn tu / (AmE), / lætʃ ˈɒn tʊ / (BrE)
to become very interested in, involved with, or attached to something, often adopting it eagerly or quickly
quickly become interested or attached to something

Examples

After the new policy was announced, many employees latched onto the idea with enthusiasm.
It's easy for fans to latch onto a celebrity's every move on social media.
He tends to latch onto trends before they become mainstream.

Synset Examples

He always latches onto the latest trends in fashion.
Children often latch onto their favorite toys quickly.
She tends to latch onto new ideas enthusiastically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - to accept or support something willingly or enthusiastically
  • Synonym: to cling to - to hold tightly or remain very close to something or someone
  • Antonym: to let go of - to stop holding or being attached to something

Etymology

From 'latch', meaning to fasten, with 'onto' indicating attachment; figurative use from the idea of grasping or holding firmly.
