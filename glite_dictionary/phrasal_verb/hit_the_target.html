to hit the target

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈtɑrɡət / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈtɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
to strike the intended object or to achieve the intended goal; to succeed in reaching an aim
strike an intended object or achieve an aim

Examples

She practiced archery until she could hit the target every time.
The company's new advertising campaign really hit the target with young audiences.
If our proposal is accepted, we will have truly hit the target this quarter.

Synset Examples

The arrow hit the target with a satisfying thwonk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miss the target - to fail to strike the intended object or achieve the intended aim
  • Synonym: hit the mark - to strike the intended object; to be accurate or correct
