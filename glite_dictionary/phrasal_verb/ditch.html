to ditch

1. VERB

/ dɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪtʃ / (BrE)
to get rid of or abandon something or plans, often suddenly or intentionally
abandon or get rid of (informal)
informal

Examples

He decided to ditch his old smartphone and buy the latest model.
They chose to ditch their dinner plans and stay home instead.
If you don't like those shoes anymore, just ditch them.

Synset Examples

He decided to ditch his old smartphone for a new one.
She wants to ditch her plans and go home.
They plan to ditch the meeting if it runs late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandon - to leave something behind or give up completely
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession or continue with something
