to bolt down

1. VERB

/ boʊlt daʊn / (AmE), / bəʊlt daʊn / (BrE)
to drink something quickly and completely, often without pausing
drink quickly and entirely

Examples

After the race, he bolted down a bottle of water in under a minute.
She bolted down her coffee before running out the door to catch the bus.
They bolted down their drinks so they wouldn't be late for the concert.

Synset Examples

He downed three martinis before dinner
She killed a bottle of brandy that night
They popped a few beers after work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down - to drink or swallow something quickly

2. VERB

/ boʊlt daʊn / (AmE), / bəʊlt daʊn / (BrE)
To eat a large amount of food quickly, often without chewing thoroughly.
eat food quickly, hurriedly

Examples

He was so hungry after work that he bolted down his dinner in five minutes.
Don’t just bolt down your breakfast—you’ll get indigestion.
She bolted down two slices of pizza before rushing out the door.

Synset Examples

The children gobbled down most of the birthday cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gobble down - to eat food quickly and greedily
  • Synonym: to wolf down - to eat something very quickly and greedily

3. VERB

/ boʊlt daʊn / (AmE), / bəʊlt daʊn / (BrE)
to run away quickly or suddenly, usually in a hurry or because of fear
run away quickly or suddenly
informal

Examples

As soon as the bell rang, the children bolted down the hallway to the playground.
He saw the police coming and bolted down the alley without looking back.
Startled by the loud noise, the cat bolted down the stairs and hid under the bed.

Synset Examples

She watched him bolt down the street in a hurry.
They had to bolt to the bran before it closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash - move quickly or suddenly
  • Synonym: to flee - run away from danger or pursuers
  • Antonym: to linger - stay in a place longer than necessary; move slowly

Etymology

From 'bolt' meaning to move suddenly, and 'down' as an adverb of direction.
