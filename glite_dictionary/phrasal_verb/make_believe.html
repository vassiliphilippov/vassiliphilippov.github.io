to make believe

1. VERB

/ meɪk bɪˈliv / (AmE), / meɪk bɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to pretend that something is true, especially for the sake of play or imagination; to act as if something is real when it is not
pretend; act as if

Examples

As children, we would make believe that our backyard was a pirate ship.
She likes to make believe she is a famous chef when she cooks dinner.
During the game, the kids make believe they are explorers in the jungle.

Synset Examples

She makes like an actress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when you know it is not
  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept of something not present

2. VERB

/ ˈmeɪk bəˌliv / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk bɪˌliːv / (BrE)
to pretend or imagine something, especially as part of a game or for fun
to pretend or imagine

Examples

When we were kids, we used to make believe we lived in a castle.
She likes to make believe that she is a famous singer when she's alone.
The children spent hours making believe they were pirates sailing the seas.

Synset Examples

Let’s make believe we are on a treasure hunt.
Children love to make believe they are superheroes.
She often likes to make believe she’s a princess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept
