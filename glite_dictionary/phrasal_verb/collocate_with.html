to collocate with

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.ləˌkeɪt wɪð / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.əˌkeɪt wɪð / (BrE)
(of words) to frequently occur together in language or text.
(of words) frequently occur together

Examples

In English, the word "commit" often collocates with "crime" or "sin".
Adjectives like "utter" rarely collocate with positive nouns.
In language teaching, it's important to learn which verbs collocate with which nouns.

Synset Examples

The word ‘hot’ tends to cooccur with ‘cold’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cooccur with - (of words) to occur together or at the same time

Etymology

From Latin 'collocare' (to place together), via modern usage in linguistics.
