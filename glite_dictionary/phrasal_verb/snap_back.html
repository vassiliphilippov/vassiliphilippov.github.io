to snap back

1. VERB

/ snæp bæk / (AmE), / snæp bæk / (BrE)
to recover quickly from a setback, illness, or difficulty
recover quickly

Examples

She was surprised by how quickly she could snap back after such a stressful week.
Children tend to snap back from illnesses much faster than adults.
Despite losing the match, the team managed to snap back with renewed determination.

Synset Examples

After the surgery, the patient snapped back in a few days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health or strength
