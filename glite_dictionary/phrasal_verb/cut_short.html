to cut short

1. VERB

/ kʌt ʃɔrt / (AmE), / kʌt ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to cause something to end or be interrupted before its expected or planned conclusion
end earlier than planned

Examples

The storm cut short our picnic plans.
His speech was cut short by technical problems.
The injury cut short her promising athletic career.

Synset Examples

The spontaneous applause cut the singer short

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of something
  • Antonym: to prolong - to make something last longer
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00521682-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌt ʃɔrt / (AmE), / kʌt ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to reduce the length or duration of something as if by removing part of it; to bring to an abrupt or premature end
reduce length or end prematurely

Examples

The meeting was cut short due to unexpected technical difficulties.
Her vacation was cut short by an urgent call from work.
Erosion has cut short the ridges of the mountains over time.

Synset Examples

truncate a word
Erosion has truncated the ridges of the mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truncate - shorten by cutting off a part
  • Antonym: to prolong - extend the duration or length of something

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kʌt ʃɔrt / (AmE), / kʌt ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to bring something to an end before its natural or planned conclusion
interrupt or end before completion

Examples

The meeting was cut short due to technical difficulties.
Her speech was cut short by a sudden power outage.
Unfortunately, illness cut short his promising career.

Synset Examples

We had to cut short our vacation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of an activity
  • Antonym: to prolong - to make something last longer than usual or planned
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kʌt ʃɔrt / (AmE), / kʌt ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to stop something before it is finished or planned, or to make it shorter than intended
end or interrupt before completion

Examples

The meeting was unexpectedly cut short due to technical issues.
Her vacation plans were cut short when a family emergency arose.
He tried to explain, but his answer was cut short by the bell.

Synset Examples

My speech was cut short
Personal freedom is curtailed in many countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to curtail - reduce in extent or quantity; impose a restriction on
  • Antonym: to prolong - make something last longer; extend in duration
