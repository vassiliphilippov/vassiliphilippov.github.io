to cannonball along

1. VERB

/ ˈkæn.ənˌbɔl əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkan.ənˌbɔːl əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to move very quickly or hurriedly, especially in a straight or forceful manner
move very quickly or hurriedly

Examples

The truck cannonballed along the empty highway, overtaking every vehicle in sight.
She cannonballed along the corridor, desperate not to miss her train.
As the clouds darkened, the children cannonballed along the street to get home before the rain started.

Synset Examples

He rushed down the hall to receive his guests
The cars raced down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Synonym: race - to move at great speed, compete in speed

Etymology

From 'cannonball' (a heavy ball fired from a cannon), evoking great speed and force.
