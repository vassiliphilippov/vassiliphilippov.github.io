to speak for

1. VERB

/ spiːk fɔr / (AmE), / spiːk fə / (BrE)
to act as a spokesperson for someone or something; to express the views or opinions on their behalf.
act as a spokesperson; represent views

Examples

She was chosen to speak for the entire class at the meeting.
As a lawyer, he speaks for his clients during negotiations.
I can't speak for everyone, but I support this decision.

Synset Examples

He speaks for the Government's position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to represent - to act or speak officially for someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02547838-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spiːk fɔɹ / (AmE), / spiːk fə / (BrE)
to express the opinions or wishes of another person or group, often officially or publicly
express the views of others

Examples

She was chosen to speak for the students at the conference.
I can't speak for everyone, but I think we should take a break.
As a manager, he often speaks for the whole department in meetings.

Synset Examples

She speaks for the entire team during meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to represent - to act or speak officially for someone
