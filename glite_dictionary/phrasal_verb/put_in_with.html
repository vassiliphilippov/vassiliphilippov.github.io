to put in with

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɪn wɪθ / (AmE), / pʊt ɪn wɪð / (BrE)
to join or become associated with a group of people or others in an activity
to become part of a group or activity

Examples

After moving to the city, he decided to put in with a group of musicians to find friends.
She hopes to put in with the new marketing team at her company.
Many students put in with campus clubs to meet people with similar interests.

Synset Examples

He decided to put in with the new team for the project.
She wants to put in with the volunteers at the shelter.
They put in with the local artists to organize the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to become a member or part of a group
  • Antonym: leave - to depart from or cease to be part of a group
