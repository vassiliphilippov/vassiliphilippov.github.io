to come home

1. VERB

/ kʌm hoʊm / (AmE), / kʌm həʊm / (BrE)
(of a fact, realization, etc.) to become fully clear or strongly felt; to be deeply understood or experienced emotionally.
become clear or deeply felt

Examples

It did not come home to her until years later just how serious the accident was.
The reality of his decision came home to him when he packed his bags to leave.
Her words finally came home to me after the meeting, and I realized their full meaning.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dawn on - to become apparent to someone; to be realized
  • Synonym: to sink in - to be fully understood or felt
