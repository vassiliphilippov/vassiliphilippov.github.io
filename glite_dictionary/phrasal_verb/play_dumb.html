to play dumb

1. VERB

/ pleɪ dʌm / (AmE), / pleɪ dʌm / (BrE)
to pretend not to know or understand something, especially to avoid responsibility or trouble
pretend ignorance to avoid notice or blame

Examples

When questioned about the missing report, he chose to play dumb and denied any knowledge.
If you keep playing dumb, they might stop asking you for help.
She sometimes plays dumb to avoid getting involved in office drama.

Synset Examples

She decided to play dumb to avoid confrontation.
Don't play dumb; we know you saw everything.
He likes to play dumb when asked tough questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feign ignorance - to pretend not to know about something
  • Synonym: to play along - to cooperate or act as if unaware, often to avoid conflict or trouble
