to slow up

1. VERB

/ sloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / sləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to move or progress more slowly
make proceed more slowly

Examples

Construction on the road will slow up traffic during rush hour.
Her injuries may slow up her recovery process.
The negotiations were slowed up by last-minute disagreements.

Synset Examples

The illness slowed him down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slow down - to reduce speed; to become or make slower
