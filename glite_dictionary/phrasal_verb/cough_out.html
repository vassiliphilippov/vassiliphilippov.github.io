to cough out

1. VERB

/ kɑf aʊt / (AmE), / kɒf aʊt / (BrE)
to expel (phlegm or sputum) from the lungs through the mouth by coughing
expel phlegm from lungs by coughing

Examples

The patient managed to cough out a large amount of mucus after the treatment.
He tried to cough out the phlegm that was making it hard to breathe.
She coughed out some sputum during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expectorate - to cough or spit out phlegm from the throat or lungs
