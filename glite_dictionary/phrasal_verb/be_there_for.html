to be there for

1. VERB

/ bi ðɛr fɔr / (AmE), / bi ðeə fə / (BrE)
to be available to support or help someone, especially during a difficult time
support or help someone

Examples

Whenever I went through hard times, my sister would always be there for me.
It's important to be there for your coworkers if they're struggling with a project.
I try to be there for my friends when they need someone to talk to.

Synset Examples

Make sure to be there for your friends when they need you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or encouragement to someone
