to run a marathon

1. VERB

/ rʌn ə ˈmærəˌθɑn / (AmE), / rʌn ə ˈmærəθən / (BrE)
to participate in a marathon race, typically a long-distance running event of 26.2 miles (42.195 kilometers)
participate in a long-distance race

Examples

Julie spent months training so she could run a marathon this spring.
After he turned forty, he decided to run a marathon to challenge himself.
Many people travel overseas just to run a marathon in famous cities like Boston or Berlin.

Synset Examples

She runs marathons, and how she trains every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete a marathon - to finish a marathon race
  • Antonym: run a sprint - to participate in a short-distance running race
