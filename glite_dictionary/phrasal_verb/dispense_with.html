to dispense with

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɛns wɪð / (AmE), / dɪˈspɛns wɪð / (BrE)
to manage without something or to decide that something is not necessary
do without; forgo

Examples

We decided to dispense with formalities and get straight to the point.
With modern technology, many offices have dispensed with paper records.
You can dispense with an umbrella if it isn't raining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forego - to go without; to do without something
  • Synonym: to omit - to leave out or exclude something
  • Antonym: to require - to need or demand; opposite of dispense with
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02308215-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈspɛns wɪθ / (AmE), / dɪˈspɛns wɪð / (BrE)
to manage without something or stop using something because it is no longer needed
do without; stop using or needing

Examples

In order to save time, we will dispense with the usual introductions.
Many offices have dispensed with paper files in favor of digital records.
The committee decided to dispense with traditional voting procedures this year.

Synset Examples

We are dispensing with formalities
relinquish the old ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do without - to manage or survive without what is desired or needed
  • Antonym: to retain - to continue to have or keep

Etymology

From Latin 'dispensare' (to distribute), with phrasal structure from English 'with' (meaning 'without' or 'exclude').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02539728-v

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈpɛns wɪθ / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛns wɪð / (BrE)
to stop using or doing something because it is not necessary; to manage without something
do without; forgo something unnecessary

Examples

The company decided to dispense with its paper catalogs and move sales online.
We can dispense with formalities and get straight to the point.
She managed to dispense with a car by living close to her workplace.

Synset Examples

he asked if they could spare one of their horses to speed his journey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do without - manage without something
  • Synonym: to forgo - to go without or give up (something); to do without
