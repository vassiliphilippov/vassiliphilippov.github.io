to stir up

1. VERB

/ stɝ ʌp / (AmE), / stɜː ʌp / (BrE)
to deliberately encourage or provoke strong feelings, reactions, or activity, especially negative ones, in other people or groups
deliberately provoke emotions or reactions

Examples

The politician tried to stir up public anger against the new law.
Rumors can easily stir up unnecessary panic in a community.
Activists hope to stir up debate on environmental issues with their campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up, especially unlawful or violent behavior
  • Antonym: to calm down - to make less agitated or angry
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɝː ʌp / (AmE), / stɜː ʌp / (BrE)
to cause strong emotions, feelings, or reactions, often deliberately; to provoke or incite.
provoke strong emotions or reactions

Examples

Her speech was designed to stir up support among the community.
Controversial articles often stir up heated debates online.
He warned that rumors could stir up unnecessary panic.

Synset Examples

The ostentatious way of living of the rich ignites the hatred of the poor
The refugees' fate stirred up compassion around the world
Wake old feelings of hatred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a particular reaction or feeling, especially anger
  • Antonym: to calm down - to make emotions or a situation less intense

3. VERB

/ stɝː ʌp / (AmE), / stɜː ʌp / (BrE)
to deliberately cause or provoke strong feelings, trouble, or activity
provoke or cause strong feelings or action

Examples

The politician's speech was meant to stir up anger among the crowd.
False rumors began to stir up panic in the community.
Social media can sometimes stir up controversy over minor issues.

Synset Examples

incite a riot
set off great unrest among the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up (violent or unlawful behavior)
  • Antonym: to calm down - to cause to become less agitated or excited
