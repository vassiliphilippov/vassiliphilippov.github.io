to cross off

1. VERB

/ krɒs ɔf / (AmE), / krɒs ɒf / (BrE)
to remove a name or item from a list by drawing a line through it
remove from a list by striking out

Examples

Remember to cross off each task as you complete it.
She quickly crossed off the incorrect answer from her checklist.
Once I buy milk, I’ll cross it off the shopping list.

Synset Examples

Cross the name of the dead person off the list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike off - remove from a list by drawing a line through
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00802412-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ krɔs ɑf / (AmE), / krɒs ɒf / (BrE)
to mark an item on a list as completed or to remove it by drawing a line through it
mark as done or remove from a list

Examples

She quickly crossed off the completed assignments from her list.
Please cross off any names that are no longer attending the event.
As I finished each chapter, I would cross off the title on my reading plan.

Synset Examples

Make sure to cross off completed tasks as you go.
I always cross off each grocery item after I shop.
I need to cross off the items on my to-do list.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tick off - to mark something on a list by putting a tick beside it
  • Antonym: to erase - to remove something written or drawn
