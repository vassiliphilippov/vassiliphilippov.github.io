to enthuse about

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈθuz əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ɪnˈθjuːz əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to speak with excitement or show enthusiastic approval about something
talk excitedly about something

Examples

Every time we visit, he loves to enthuse about his travels abroad.
Teachers often enthuse about their students' achievements in class.
She couldn't help but enthuse about the new movie she had just seen.

Synset Examples

She tends to enthuse about her favorite books.
It's hard not to enthuse over such a great achievement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rave about - to talk very enthusiastically about something
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or negative judgment

Etymology

Formed from 'enthuse' (derived from 'enthusiasm', from Greek 'enthousiasmos').
