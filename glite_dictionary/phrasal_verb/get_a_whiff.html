to get a whiff

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə wɪf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə wɪf / (BrE)
to smell something briefly or become aware of a smell, often one that is strong or distinctive
smell something briefly

Examples

As soon as I entered the kitchen, I got a whiff of freshly baked bread.
He got a whiff of perfume as she walked by.
Whenever the wind changed, we got a whiff of the sea air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch a whiff - to notice or smell something for a short time
