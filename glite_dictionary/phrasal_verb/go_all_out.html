to go all out

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔl aʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to make the greatest possible effort; to do something with maximum energy, enthusiasm, or resources
make the greatest possible effort
informal

Examples

Everyone decided to go all out for the charity event this year.
If we're going to win, we have to go all out from the very beginning.
The chef really went all out with tonight’s special menu.

Synset Examples

The cast gives full measure every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try one's best - to make as much effort as possible
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop making an effort; to quit
