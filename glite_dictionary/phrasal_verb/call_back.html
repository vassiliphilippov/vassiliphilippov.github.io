to call back

1. VERB

/ kɔl bæk / (AmE), / kɔːl bæk / (BrE)
to summon someone to return, especially after they have left or been sent away
summon to return

Examples

The company decided to call back some of its former employees for the busy season.
The ambassador was called back to his home country after the incident.
After the danger had passed, the authorities called back the evacuated residents.

Synset Examples

The ambassador was recalled to his country
The company called back many of the workers it had laid off during the recession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to formally order someone to return, especially to a place or position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00793168-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to remember or recollect something from the past; to bring a memory to mind.
remember or recall (something)

Examples

I tried hard to call back the details of our first meeting.
Whenever he hears that song, he calls back memories of his childhood.
She couldn't call back exactly what he had said.

Synset Examples

I can't remember saying any such thing
I can't think what her last name was
can you remember her phone number?
Do you remember that he once loved you?
call up memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to remember something; to bring a memory to mind
  • Antonym: to forget - to be unable to remember
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00609334-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kɔl bæk / (AmE), / kɔːl bæk / (BrE)
to telephone someone who has called you earlier or to return a telephone call
return a telephone call

Examples

If I'm not available, please call back later in the afternoon.
She forgot to call back her mother after missing the first call.
He promised he would call back as soon as he finished the meeting.

Synset Examples

I am busy right now — can you call back in an hour?
She left a message but the contractor never called back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring back - to return a phone call (British alternative)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00793426-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to order or request the return of something, especially a product that has been found to be defective or unsafe
order the return of goods

Examples

The company had to call back thousands of washing machines due to a safety issue.
After reports of contamination, the factory decided to call back all products from the market.
The government ordered the car manufacturer to call back the faulty vehicles to prevent accidents.

Synset Examples

recall the defective auto tires
The manufacturer tried to call back the spoilt yoghurt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to officially order the return of a product
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
