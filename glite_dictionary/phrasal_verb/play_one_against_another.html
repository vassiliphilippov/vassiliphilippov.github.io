to play one against another

1. VERB

/ pleɪ wʌn əˈgɛnst əˈnʌðər / (AmE), / pleɪ wʌn əˈɡɛnst əˈnʌðə / (BrE)
to manipulate two or more people or groups by encouraging conflict or competition between them, usually to gain an advantage for oneself
manipulate by encouraging conflict for one's advantage

Examples

In office politics, some people try to play one against another to climb the ladder.
It's unwise to play one against another in a close-knit team, as it can backfire.
He liked to play one against another when negotiating with suppliers for better terms.

Synset Examples

They tried to play one against another to gain leverage.
It's dangerous to play one against another in friendships.
He learned to play one against another for his benefit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set one against another - provoke conflict between people for advantage
  • Antonym: mediate - try to bring parties to agreement or peace
