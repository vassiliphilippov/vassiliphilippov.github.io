to start into

1. VERB

/ stɑrt ˈɪntu / (AmE), / stɑːt ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to begin to engage in something, especially a discussion or activity, often with sudden or energetic involvement
begin to engage in something

Examples

As soon as the meeting began, Jane started into her list of concerns.
He started into a detailed explanation before anyone could stop him.
Without hesitation, they started into a debate about politics.

Synset Examples

They started into the discussion about their plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enter into - to begin a discussion or activity
