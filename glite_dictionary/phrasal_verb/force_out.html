to force out

1. VERB

/ fɔrs aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːs aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone leave a position or office by using pressure or authority
compel someone to leave office or position

Examples

The board decided to force out the CEO after the scandal.
Several employees were forced out following the company's restructuring.
Rumors spread that he had been forced out due to disagreements with management.

  • Synonym: to oust - to remove someone from a position or place
