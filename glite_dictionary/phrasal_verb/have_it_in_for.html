to have it in for

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪt ɪn fər / (AmE), / hæv ɪt ɪn fə / (BrE)
to be determined to harm, criticize, or cause trouble for someone because of a grudge
hold a grudge against someone

Examples

Ever since I reported his mistake, my boss has had it in for me.
It's obvious the teacher has it in for Jake because she never calls on him.
Don't take it personally; she has it in for everyone who disagrees with her.

Synset Examples

She must have it in for me after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold a grudge - to maintain ill feelings or resentment toward someone
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone
