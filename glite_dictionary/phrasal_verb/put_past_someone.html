to put past someone

1. VERB

/ pʊt pæst ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / pʊt pɑːst ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to try to move on from previous mistakes or issues; to no longer let past problems affect oneself
move on from past mistakes or issues

Examples

It's important to put past someone and focus on making things better in the future.
After years of resentment, she finally managed to put past him and forgive.
He worked hard to put past himself and rebuild his relationships.

Synset Examples

Let's put the mistakes of the past past us.
He tried to put the argument past him and move on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to move on - to stop thinking or worrying about something bad that happened and continue with your life
