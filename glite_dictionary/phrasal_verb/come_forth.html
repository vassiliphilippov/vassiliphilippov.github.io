to come forth

1. VERB

/ kʌm fɔrθ / (AmE), / kʌm fɔːθ / (BrE)
to happen or occur as a result of something
happen or occur as a result

Examples

Several new ideas came forth during the brainstorming session.
After much discussion, a solution finally came forth.
Great changes often come forth from moments of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arise - to emerge or become apparent
  • Synonym: result - to happen as a consequence
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02631097-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm fɔrθ / (AmE), / kʌm fɔːθ / (BrE)
to appear or emerge from a place or source, especially suddenly or formally
emerge; appear from somewhere

Examples

As the mist cleared, the mountain began to come forth in the morning light.
New ideas can come forth during lively discussions.
Many volunteers came forth to help after the disaster.

Synset Examples

Water issued from the hole in the wall
The words seemed to come out by themselves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to become visible or come out into view
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to move back or away, especially from a place or a position
