to rot away

1. VERB

/ rɑt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / rɒt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to decay or be gradually destroyed, especially over a period of time; to diminish or waste away, often through neglect
decay or gradually disappear

Examples

The old wooden beams had begun to rot away after years of exposure to moisture.
If you don't use your skills, they'll eventually rot away.
Some ancient manuscripts were left to rot away in the damp cellar.

Synset Examples

Don't let your dreams rot away; pursue them actively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - to undergo decomposition or decline
  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become progressively worse
