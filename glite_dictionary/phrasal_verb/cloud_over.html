to cloud over

1. VERB

/ ˈklaʊd ˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈklaʊd ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) to become covered with clouds, making it less bright or sunny.
become covered with clouds

Examples

The weather was sunny at first, but it began to cloud over in the afternoon.
As soon as the hikers reached the summit, the sky started to cloud over.
It might cloud over later, so let's bring our raincoats just in case.

Synset Examples

The sky clouded over

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clear up - (of the sky) to become sunnier or less cloudy
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02776978-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klaʊd ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / klaʊd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of the sky) to become covered with clouds or overcast.
to become overcast (sky)

Examples

The sky began to cloud over as the afternoon wore on.
If it starts to cloud over, we might have to cancel the picnic.
It often clouds over in the late afternoon during the rainy season.

Synset Examples

the sky clouded over

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clear up - (of the sky) to become bright and free of clouds
