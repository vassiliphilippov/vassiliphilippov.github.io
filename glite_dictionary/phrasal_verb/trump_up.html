to trump up

1. VERB

/ trʌmp ʌp / (AmE), / trʌmp ʌp / (BrE)
to invent a false story, accusation, charge, or reason, usually with the intention to deceive or cause harm
invent a false charge or excuse

Examples

The government tried to trump up charges against the opposition leader to discredit him.
It is unethical to trump up evidence just to win a case.
Several officials were accused of trumping up reasons to fire the employee.

Synset Examples

trump up charges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent or concoct something, typically with deceitful intent
  • Synonym: forge - to produce a fraudulent copy or invent something falsely
