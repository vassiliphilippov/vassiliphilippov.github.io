to spiff up

1. VERB

/ spɪf ʌp / (AmE), / spɪf ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone or something look neat, smart, or more attractive, often with the addition of small improvements
make neat or more attractive
informal

Examples

She decided to spiff up her apartment with some new cushions and artwork.
We spiffed up the office before the big client meeting.
He's been spiffing up his resume to apply for new jobs.

Synset Examples

Spruce up your house for Spring
titivate the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make something tidy or smart in appearance
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make something untidy or disordered

Etymology

Possibly from 'spiff,' a 19th-century slang term for a dandy or well-dressed person.
