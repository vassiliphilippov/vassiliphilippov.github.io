to boom out

1. VERB

/ buːm aʊt / (AmE), / buːm aʊt / (BrE)
to make a deep, loud, and resonant sound, especially so as to be clearly heard at a distance
emit a deep, loud sound

Examples

The announcer's voice boomed out over the loudspeakers, grabbing everyone's attention.
Thunder boomed out across the valley, shaking the windows of the old house.
His name boomed out so loudly that everyone in the auditorium turned to look.

Synset Examples

Her voice booms out the words of the song

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blare out - to make a loud and harsh sound
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly
