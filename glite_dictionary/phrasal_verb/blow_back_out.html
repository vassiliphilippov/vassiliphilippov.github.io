to blow back out

1. VERB

/ bloʊ bæk aʊt / (AmE), / bləʊ bæk aʊt / (BrE)
to seriously injure one's back, often as a result of physical exertion or awkward movement
to seriously injure one's back
informal

Examples

If you lift that box the wrong way, you might blow back out and need medical attention.
He nearly blew back out at the gym yesterday because he didn't warm up.
She was worried she would blow back out trying to move the heavy furniture by herself.

Synset Examples

The intense workout might blow someone's back out.
That dance move could easily blow someone's back out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw out one's back - to strain or injure the muscles of one's back suddenly
