to take on water

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɑn ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / teɪk ɒn ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
(of a boat or ship) to begin to fill with water, often as a result of a leak or damage, potentially leading to sinking or failure.
(of a vessel) start filling with water

Examples

The old lifeboat began to take on water after being struck by debris.
We realized we were in trouble when the canoe started to take on water.
If the hull is damaged further, the ship will take on water quickly.

Synset Examples

The old ship began to take on water during the storm.
If it continues to take on water, we’ll sink.
The boat started to take on water after hitting the rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leak - (of a vessel or container) to let liquid in or out unintentionally
