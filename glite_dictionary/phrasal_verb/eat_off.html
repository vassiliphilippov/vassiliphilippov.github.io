to eat off

1. VERB

/ it ɔf / (AmE), / iːt ɒf / (BrE)
(of a surface) to be so clean that one could eat directly from it without concern for dirt or germs; often used in comparisons to emphasize cleanliness.
be extremely clean (said of a surface)

Examples

You could eat off these countertops, they're so clean.
The hotel room was spotless; I felt I could eat off the floor.
She keeps her kitchen so tidy, you could eat off any surface.

Synset Examples

His cleaning skills left a floor one could eat off.
The kitchen was so clean, it was a floor one could eat off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean, without any marks
  • Antonym: filthy - very dirty
