to wrest from

1. VERB

/ rɛst frʌm / (AmE), / rɛst frɒm / (BrE)
to forcibly take something away from someone, especially after a struggle
forcibly take away

Examples

The rebels managed to wrest from the government control of the radio station.
She tried to wrest from his hands the confidential files he was hiding.
After a lengthy legal battle, they finally wrested from the corporation the compensation they deserved.

Synset Examples

He had to wrest the ball from his opponent's grip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snatch from - to quickly grab something from someone
  • Antonym: to give up - to relinquish possession of something

Etymology

From Old English *wræstan* (to twist), related to Old Norse *reista* (to wrench).
