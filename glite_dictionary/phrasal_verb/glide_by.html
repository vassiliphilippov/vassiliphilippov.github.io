to glide by

1. VERB

/ ɡlaɪd baɪ / (AmE), / ɡlaɪd baɪ / (BrE)
(of time) to pass quickly and smoothly, often without being noticed.
(of time) pass smoothly and quickly

Examples

I didn't notice how the afternoon glided by while I was reading my book.
Years can glide by when you settle into a comfortable routine.
The summer glided by and suddenly autumn was upon us.

Synset Examples

three years elapsed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fly by - (of time) to seem to pass very quickly
  • Antonym: to drag on - (of time) to pass very slowly
