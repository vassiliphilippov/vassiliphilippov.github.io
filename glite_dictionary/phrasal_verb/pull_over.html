to pull over

1. VERB

/ pʊl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / pʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to steer a vehicle to the side of the road and stop, especially as directed by law enforcement or for safety reasons.
move a vehicle to the roadside and stop

Examples

The police officer signaled for him to pull over to check his documents.
We decided to pull over and take pictures of the beautiful sunset.
If you feel tired while driving, it's safer to pull over and rest.

Synset Examples

The car pulled over when the ambulance approached at high speed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to cause a vehicle to cease movement
  • Antonym: drive on - to continue driving rather than stopping at the roadside

2. VERB

/ pʊl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / pʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or its driver) to move to the side of the road and stop.
move a vehicle to the side and stop

Examples

The police officer told us to pull over because our taillight was out.
After driving for hours, she decided to pull over and rest for a while.
You should pull over immediately if you hear a siren behind you.

Synset Examples

I had to pull over to answer the phone.
The police officer signaled him to pull over.
The cop pulled him over for doing a California stop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to cease moving or bring to a halt
  • Antonym: drive on - continue driving without stopping
