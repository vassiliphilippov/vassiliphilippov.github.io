to check out

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to record, total, and receive payment for items purchased, especially at a store or supermarket
process payment for items in a store

Examples

The cashier will check out your groceries at the register.
After waiting in line, Sarah was ready to check out her items.
He learned how to check out customers quickly on his first day at work.

Synset Examples

She was checking out the apples that the customer had put on the conveyer belt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ring up - to enter the cost of goods on a cash register
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02368741-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to announce your departure and pay any outstanding charges before leaving a hotel or similar accommodation
leave and settle the bill at a hotel

Examples

We need to check out of the hotel by noon to avoid an extra charge.
Have you checked out yet, or are you staying another night?
She was busy checking out when her taxi arrived at the lobby.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to check in - to register upon arrival at a hotel or similar accommodation
