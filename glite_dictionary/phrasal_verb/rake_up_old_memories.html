to rake up old memories

1. VERB

/ reɪk ʌp oʊld ˈmɛməriz / (AmE), / reɪk ʌp əʊld ˈmɛməriz / (BrE)
to deliberately recall or revive memories from the past, especially those that may be better forgotten
deliberately recall past memories

Examples

Every time I visit my childhood home, I tend to rake up old memories that make me nostalgic.
She didn't want to rake up old memories by talking about her past relationships.
The documentary raked up old memories for many people who had lived through the events.

Synset Examples

He tried to rake up the old memories during the reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring up old memories - to recall or mention memories from the past
  • Antonym: to forget - to not remember or fail to recall something
