to churn up

1. VERB

/ tʃɝn ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɜːn ʌp / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel strong aversion, disgust, or moral offense
cause aversion or moral offense

Examples

The violent scene in the film really churned up the audience.
Graphic news reports can churn up strong feelings of disgust in viewers.
Some of his opinions churn up people’s moral sensibilities.

Synset Examples

The pornographic pictures sickened us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sicken - to cause to feel disgust or aversion
  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel upset or annoyed, typically with moral or social implications
  • Antonym: please - to give pleasure or satisfaction
