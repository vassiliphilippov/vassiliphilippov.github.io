to press down on

1. VERB

/ prɛs daʊn ɑn / (AmE), / prɛs daʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to exert downward force against something, usually with some weight or pressure
exert downward force

Examples

She had to press down on the lid to close the container properly.
The dentist asked me to press down on the gauze after my tooth was pulled.
Heavy machinery pressed down on the soil to flatten it.

Synset Examples

The snow bore down on the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bear down on - to move toward and then exert pressure on; similar forceful action
