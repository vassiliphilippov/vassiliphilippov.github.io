to measure up to

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛʒər ʌp tu / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒə ʌp tuː / (BrE)
to be good enough or as good as expected, required, or needed; to reach a particular standard
reach a required standard

Examples

He worked hard to measure up to his parents' expectations.
The new recruits must measure up to strict fitness requirements.
She never felt she could measure up to her talented brother.

Synset Examples

Can she measure up to the competition?
You need to measure up to the standards set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to live up to - to fulfill expectations or match a particular standard
  • Antonym: to fall short of - to fail to reach a required standard
