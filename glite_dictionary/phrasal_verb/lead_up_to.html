to lead up to

1. VERB

/ lid ʌp tu / (AmE), / liːd ʌp tuː / (BrE)
to gradually develop or progress towards a particular event or conclusion
develop in preparation for something

Examples

The negotiations led up to the signing of the historic treaty.
Months of training led up to her marathon debut.
Disagreements between the teams led up to the project's failure.

Synset Examples

Everything will lead up to the final decision.
The story will lead up to a surprising twist.
The events lead up to the big festival next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to precede - to come before in time, order, or position
  • Antonym: to follow - to come after in time or order
