to roll over

1. VERB

/ roʊl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / rəʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to reinvest funds from a previous investment into a similar fund or security, often to defer taxes or maintain an investment.
reinvest funds in a similar investment

Examples

She decided to roll over her 401(k) into a new retirement account.
You can roll over your savings certificate when it matures to avoid withdrawing the funds.
Before you roll over an investment, be sure to check for any penalties or tax implications.

Synset Examples

She rolled over her IRA
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ roʊl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / rəʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to turn one's body or an object over by rolling
turn by rolling

Examples

When commanded, the puppy would happily roll over for a treat.
He tried to roll over in bed, but his arm was stuck under the blanket.
The car hit a patch of ice and began to roll over on the highway.

Synset Examples

The dog rolled over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn over - to invert or rotate so that the lower side becomes the upper side

3. VERB

/ roʊl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / rəʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to negotiate the extension of a loan's term, so that repayment is postponed to a later date, usually for an additional fee
extend the repayment period of a loan

Examples

Due to cash flow issues, the company decided to roll over its short-term debt for another year.
We had to roll over the loan to avoid defaulting on our payments.
Many borrowers choose to roll over their loans rather than pay the full amount when due.

Synset Examples

roll over a loan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refinance - obtain new financing to pay off an existing loan
