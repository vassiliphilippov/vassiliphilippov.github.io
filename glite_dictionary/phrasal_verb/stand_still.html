to stand still

1. VERB

/ stænd stɪl / (AmE), / stænd stɪl / (BrE)
to remain stationary and not move; to hold oneself or something in a fixed position
remain stationary; not move

Examples

Please stand still while I take your picture.
The children were asked to stand still during the national anthem.
She tried to stand still, but she was too excited to keep from fidgeting.

Synset Examples

Traffic stood still when the funeral procession passed by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to become motionless suddenly or completely
  • Antonym: move - to change position or go from place to place

2. VERB

/ stænd stɪl / (AmE), / stænd stɪl / (BrE)
to remain in an upright position without moving; to not change one's position
remain motionless while standing

Examples

The children were told to stand still during the national anthem.
Please stand still so that I can adjust your tie properly.
She tried to stand still despite the biting wind.

Synset Examples

Please stand still while I take your picture.
You must stand still during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - suddenly stop moving and stay motionless
  • Antonym: move - change position or go from one place to another

Synset POS: None
