to cut across

1. VERB

/ kʌt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / kʌt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to travel across or go through something, rather than around, often as a shortcut
travel or pass across

Examples

We decided to cut across the field to save time on our walk.
The river cuts across the southern part of the city, dividing it in two.
They cut across the park instead of following the main road.

Synset Examples

The caravan covered almost 100 miles each day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to go or extend across something

2. VERB

/ kʌt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / kʌt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to go beyond or disregard traditional boundaries, rules, or distinctions
disregard traditional boundaries or rules

Examples

Her expertise cut across traditional academic disciplines.
The new initiative cuts across department boundaries and encourages collaboration.
The movement cut across age, gender, and social status.

Synset Examples

Opinions on bombing the Serbs cut across party lines

  • Synonym: to transcend - to go beyond the limits of something

3. VERB

/ kʌt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / kʌt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to cut something by making a diagonal line or movement across it
make a diagonal cut

Examples

To divide the cake evenly, she decided to cut across the round surface.
The tailor suggested I cut across the fabric to get a cleaner line.
He cut across the sheet of paper from one corner to the opposite diagonal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - to use a sharp tool to divide or remove part of something

4. VERB

/ kʌt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / kʌt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
(of a path, road, influence, etc.) to go across or through something, or to affect or involve different groups, areas, or categories.
to traverse or affect different areas

Examples

The highway cuts across several counties on its way to the coast.
This issue cuts across all levels of society, affecting everyone equally.
A narrow trail cuts across the park, making it easier to reach the lake.

Synset Examples

The path cuts across the field to the lake.
Their decision cuts across all departments equally.
The new policy cuts across traditional boundaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traverse - to travel across or through
  • Synonym: affect - to have an influence on
