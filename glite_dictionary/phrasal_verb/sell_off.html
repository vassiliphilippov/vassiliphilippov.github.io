to sell off

1. VERB

/ sɛl ɔf / (AmE), / sɛl ɒf / (BrE)
to sell something quickly, often at reduced prices, especially in order to dispose of surplus stock or assets
sell something quickly, often at a discount

Examples

The company decided to sell off its unprofitable division to focus on core business.
Many stores sell off seasonal items at a discount after the holidays.
During the crisis, investors began selling off their stocks to minimize losses.

Synset Examples

The store sold off the surplus merchandise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquidate - to convert assets into cash by selling them off
