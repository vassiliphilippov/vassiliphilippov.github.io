to warp time

1. VERB

/ wɔrp taɪm / (AmE), / wɔːp taɪm / (BrE)
to cause a change in the perception or passage of time, often making it seem to move faster, slower, or in a distorted manner
alter the perception of time

Examples

Intense focus can sometimes warp time, making hours feel like minutes.
In the story, the black hole was said to warp time around it.
The magical spell allowed the wizard to warp time and travel to the past.

Synset Examples

Time seemed to warp during their incredible adventure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distort time - to change or misrepresent the usual experience of time
