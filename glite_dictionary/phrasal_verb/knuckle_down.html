to knuckle down

1. VERB

/ ˈnʌkəl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈnʌkəl daʊn / (BrE)
to start working very hard, especially after a period of not doing much
apply oneself; begin working hard

Examples

It's time to knuckle down and finish this project before the deadline.
She knuckled down to study for her exams and improved her grades considerably.
If we all knuckle down now, we can complete the job by Friday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to buckle down - to start working seriously

Etymology

Originally a gambling term referring to pressing one’s knuckles to the ground before playing marbles.

2. VERB

/ ˈnʌkəl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈnʌkəl daʊn / (BrE)
to begin to work very hard, especially after a period of not working seriously
start working hard or seriously

Examples

If you want to get good grades, you'll have to knuckle down this semester.
After the holidays, the team knuckled down to meet the approaching deadlines.
She realized it was time to stop procrastinating and really knuckle down at work.

Synset Examples

It's time to knuckle down and finish this project.
He needs to knuckle down if he wants to pass the exam.
After a long break, she finally knuckled down to study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to buckle down - to start working hard or seriously

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang (mid-19th century), from knuckle (in marbles, to place the knuckles on the ground as a sign of readiness).
