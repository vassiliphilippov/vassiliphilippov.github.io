to get hold

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt hoʊld / (AmE), / ɡɛt həʊld / (BrE)
to obtain something or someone, especially for a particular purpose; to manage to acquire or contact.
obtain or acquire something/someone

Examples

I finally managed to get hold of the manager after several phone calls.
She tried to get hold of a copy of the book before her exam.
It took him hours to get hold of a plumber on the weekend.

Synset Examples

I found this gadget that will serve as a bottle opener
I got hold of these tools to fix our plumbing
The chairman got hold of a secretary on Friday night to type the urgent letter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acquire - to buy or obtain something for oneself
  • Synonym: to obtain - to get or gain something
