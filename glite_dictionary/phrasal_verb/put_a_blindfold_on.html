to put a blindfold on

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə ˈblaɪndˌfoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt ə ˈblaɪndfəʊld ɒn / (BrE)
to cover someone's or one's own eyes with a blindfold
cover eyes with a blindfold

Examples

Before the surprise, they asked her to put a blindfold on.
Magicians often put a blindfold on during their performances to impress the audience.
He nervously put a blindfold on before beginning the trust exercise.

Synset Examples

She put a blindfold on to heighten her other senses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blindfold - a piece of cloth that covers someone's eyes
