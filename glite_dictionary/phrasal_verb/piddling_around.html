to piddling around

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪdlɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpɪdlɪŋ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time in a relaxed or aimless way; to waste time doing unimportant things
waste time; be unproductive
informal

Examples

He spent the whole afternoon just piddling around in the garage.
Quit piddling around and help me finish this project.
On weekends, I like piddling around in the garden with no real plan.

Synset Examples

Stop piddling around and get to work!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fooling around - to behave in a silly or aimless way instead of working
  • Antonym: get down to business - to start doing serious or important work

Etymology

From the verb "piddle," likely a diminutive form of "piss" meaning to urinate; later generalized to mean dawdling or wasting time.
