to catalyze success

1. VERB

/ ˈkæt̬əˌlaɪz səkˈsɛs / (AmE), / ˈkætəlaɪz səkˈsɛs / (BrE)
to cause or accelerate the achievement of a successful result, often by initiating change or stimulating progress
to cause or speed up achievement of success

Examples

Her leadership helped catalyze success for the new product launch.
Their collaboration was key to catalyzing success in the competitive market.
Strong mentorship often catalyzes success among young professionals.

Synset Examples

Her innovative ideas helped catalyze the project's success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to facilitate - to make an action or process easier
  • Antonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to succeed
