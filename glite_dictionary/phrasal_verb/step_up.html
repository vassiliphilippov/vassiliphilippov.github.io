to step up

1. VERB

/ stɛp ʌp / (AmE), / stɛp ʌp / (BrE)
to increase the speed, amount, or intensity of something; to make something happen more quickly or at a higher level
increase speed, amount, or intensity

Examples

The company plans to step up production to meet higher demand.
We need to step up our efforts if we want to finish on time.
After the incident, security was stepped up around the building.

Synset Examples

let's rev up production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accelerate - to cause to happen faster or to move faster
  • Antonym: to slow down - to reduce speed or rate
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɛp ʌp / (AmE), / stɛp ʌp / (BrE)
to increase the amount, speed, intensity, or extent of something
increase intensity or extent

Examples

The company decided to step up production to meet the growing demand.
We need to step up our efforts if we want to finish the project on time.
After the warning, the police stepped up patrols in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

The Allies escalated the bombing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to escalate - to become or make greater in intensity
  • Antonym: decrease - to make or become less in amount or intensity
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ stɛp ʌp / (AmE), / stɛp ʌp / (BrE)
to take action or become more actively involved, especially by accepting responsibility or making oneself visible
take responsibility; become more involved

Examples

It's time for someone to step up and lead this project.
When the team needed help, he didn't hesitate to step up.
She decided to step up and address the issue directly.

Synset Examples

Young people should step to the fore and help their peers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step forward - to volunteer or come forward to help
  • Antonym: to hold back - to refrain from taking action or becoming involved
Source: WordNet
