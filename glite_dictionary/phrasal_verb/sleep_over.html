to sleep over

1. VERB

/ sliːp ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / sliːp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to stay overnight at someone else's house, especially as a guest and not as a planned accommodation
stay overnight as a guest

Examples

Sarah invited Emily to sleep over after the concert finished late.
When I was a kid, I loved to sleep over at my cousin's house on weekends.
If you miss the last train, you can always sleep over at my place.

Synset Examples

The boy's friends were allowed to sleep over after the birthday party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay over - to remain overnight at a place
