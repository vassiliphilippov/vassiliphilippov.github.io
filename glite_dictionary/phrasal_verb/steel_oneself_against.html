to steel oneself against

1. VERB

/ stil wʌnˈsɛlf əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / stiːl wʌnˈsɛlf əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to prepare yourself mentally or emotionally in order to deal with something unpleasant or difficult
mentally prepare for something unpleasant

Examples

She tried to steel herself against the pain of losing her childhood home.
He knew he would have to steel himself against criticism from his colleagues.
I steeled myself against the harsh winter wind as I stepped outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace oneself - prepare physically or mentally for something difficult
  • Synonym: fortify oneself - strengthen oneself mentally or emotionally

Etymology

From 'steel' meaning to make hard or strong, reflecting the idea of making oneself tough in preparation.
