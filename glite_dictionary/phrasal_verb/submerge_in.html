to submerge in

1. VERB

/ səbˈmɝrdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to immerse completely in a liquid or to deeply involve oneself in an activity or situation
immerse fully in liquid or activity

Examples

She likes to submerge in a good book and forget everything around her.
Make sure to submerge in the pasta completely so it cooks evenly.
After the scandal, he tried to submerge in his research and avoid public attention.

Synset Examples

He decided to submerge himself in his work.
She had to submerge the vegetables in boiling water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immerse in - to put completely into a liquid or engage deeply in something

Etymology

From Latin 'submergere' (to plunge under).
