to catch out

1. VERB

/ kætʃ aʊt / (AmE), / kætʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to expose someone by discovering a mistake or misdeed, often unexpectedly
expose a mistake or wrongdoing

Examples

The journalist tried to catch out the politician with difficult questions during the interview.
He was caught out when his lies were discovered by his colleagues.
They set a trap to catch out anyone breaking the company rules.

Synset Examples

He was caught out
She was found out when she tried to cash the stolen checks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find out - to discover information, especially something secret or hidden
  • Antonym: to let off - to excuse someone from punishment or exposure
