to mellow out

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈmɛləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to become more relaxed, less tense, or more easygoing
become more relaxed or easygoing
informal

Examples

After his vacation, he finally mellowed out and stopped stressing over work.
Sometimes listening to music helps me mellow out after a tough day.
She used to be strict, but she's really mellowed out since retiring.

Synset Examples

With age, he mellowed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - become less tense or anxious
  • Antonym: to tense up - become more tense or nervous
