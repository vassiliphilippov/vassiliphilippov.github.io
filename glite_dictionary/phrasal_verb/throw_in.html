to throw in

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to add something extra, especially as a bonus or at no additional cost
add something as a bonus or extra

Examples

If you buy the sofa, we'll throw in the cushions for free.
As a special offer, the mechanic threw in a free oil change with my service.
She throws in helpful tips whenever she explains a recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or set
  • Antonym: to omit - to leave out or exclude

2. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to insert something, especially a comment or remark, between other elements; to interject
to interject; insert a comment

Examples

She likes to throw in witty comments during meetings to keep everyone engaged.
If you want to make the story more interesting, throw in a few unexpected details.
He often throws in a joke when the conversation gets too serious.

Synset Examples

She interjected clever remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interject - to say something briefly, especially as an interruption
  • Synonym: insert - to put something into something else

3. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to admit defeat or stop trying, especially in a contest or difficult situation
admit defeat, stop trying

Examples

After realizing he couldn’t win, he finally decided to throw in and walk away.
Their team refused to throw in despite being behind by three goals.
She didn’t want to throw in until she had given it her absolute best.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying; to admit defeat
  • Synonym: surrender - to give in or admit defeat, especially formally
  • Antonym: to persist - to continue firmly despite difficulty

Etymology

Likely from the practice of throwing in the towel or sponge as a sign of surrender in boxing.
