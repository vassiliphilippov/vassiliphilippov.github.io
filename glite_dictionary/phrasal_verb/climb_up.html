to climb up

1. VERB

/ klaɪm ʌp / (AmE), / klaɪm ʌp / (BrE)
to move or go upward, especially gradually or with effort, often by using one's hands and feet
move upward with effort

Examples

The children love to climb up the old oak tree in the park.
She had to climb up the stairs because the elevator was broken.
We watched the hikers climb up the steep mountain trail.

Synset Examples

Did you ever climb up the hill behind your house?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ascend - to go up or move upward
  • Antonym: to descend - to go down or move downward
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01925957-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klaɪm ʌp / (AmE), / klaɪm ʌp / (BrE)
to rise to a higher position or level, especially in rank, status, or importance
rise in rank or status

Examples

She worked hard to climb up the corporate ladder and become a manager.
The song began to climb up the charts soon after its release.
He hopes to climb up in the organization through dedication and talent.

Synset Examples

Her new novel jumped high on the bestseller list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - move forward in position or rank
  • Antonym: to descend - to move to a lower rank or position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00155355-v
Synset POS: v
