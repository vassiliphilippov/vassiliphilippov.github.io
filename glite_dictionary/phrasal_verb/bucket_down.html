to bucket down

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌkɪt daʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪt daʊn / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall very heavily; to rain intensely.
rain very heavily
informal british

Examples

It began to bucket down just as the festival started, soaking everyone.
If it buckets down this afternoon, the match will likely be cancelled.
It was bucketing down all night, and the streets were flooded by morning.

Synset Examples

We got soaked when it began to bucket down unexpectedly.
Make sure to bring an umbrella; it's going to bucket down later.
It started to bucket down just as we left the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour down - (of rain) to fall very heavily
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - (of rain) to fall lightly in fine drops
