to fence in

1. VERB

/ fɛns ɪn / (AmE), / fɛns ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose (an area) by building a fence around it
enclose with a fence

Examples

They decided to fence in their vegetable garden to keep the rabbits out.
The new owner plans to fence in the entire property for privacy.
We had to fence in the playground before the school year started.

Synset Examples

we fenced in our yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround on all sides; to close in

/ fɛns ɪn / (AmE), / fens ɪn / (BrE)

Examples

3. VERB

/ fɛns ɪn / (AmE), / fɛns ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose an area with a fence or to restrict the movement or freedom of someone or something
enclose or restrict with a fence

Examples

They decided to fence in the backyard to keep their children safe.
The manager's strict rules seemed to fence in the team's creativity.
Before letting the sheep roam, we need to fence in the field.
