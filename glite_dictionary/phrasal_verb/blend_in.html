to blend in

1. VERB

/ blɛnd ɪn / (AmE), / blɛnd ɪn / (BrE)
to mix (something) with another substance so that the components are combined evenly
combine ingredients evenly by mixing

Examples

After adding the flour, blend in the eggs until the batter is smooth.
You should blend in the spices gently to avoid clumping.
Once the sauce is ready, blend in the cheese and stir well.

Synset Examples

At this stage of making the cake, blend in the nuts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mix in - add an ingredient to others by mixing
  • Synonym: stir in - incorporate an ingredient into a mixture by stirring

2. VERB

/ blɛnd ɪn / (AmE), / blend ɪn / (BrE)
to combine or harmonize well with other things so as not to be noticeable or to fit in naturally
become harmonious or unobtrusive

Examples

She tried to blend in with the locals by wearing traditional clothes.
The new curtains blend in perfectly with the rest of the décor.
He hoped his quiet behavior would help him blend in at his new school.

Synset Examples

This flavor will blend with those in your dish
This sofa won't go with the chairs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to stand out - to be very noticeable or prominent
  • Synonym: to merge - to combine or unite into a single entity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02706315-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ blɛnd ɪn / (AmE), / blɛnd ɪn / (BrE)
to appear similar to the surrounding people or things so as not to be easily noticed
become inconspicuous; not stand out

Examples

The spy used a disguise to blend in with the crowd.
Chameleons can blend in with their surroundings to avoid predators.
He tried to blend in at the party by wearing similar clothes to everyone else.

Synset Examples

The spy used a disguise to blend in.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to stand out - be easily noticed or prominent
