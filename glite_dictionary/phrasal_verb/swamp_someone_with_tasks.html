to swamp someone with tasks

1. VERB

/ swɑmp ˈsʌmwʌn wɪð tæsks / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈsʌmwʌn wɪð tɑːsks / (BrE)
to overwhelm someone by giving them more tasks or work than they can handle
overwhelm with too many tasks

Examples

My manager tends to swamp me with tasks when tight deadlines are approaching.
We shouldn't swamp new employees with tasks during their first week on the job.
If you swamp her with tasks, she might get stressed and make careless mistakes.

Synset Examples

Don't swamp me with too many tasks at once.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overload - to give too much of something, especially work or information
  • Synonym: burden - to cause difficulty or distress by giving too much to do

Etymology

Figurative use of "swamp" meaning to flood or overwhelm, from the noun 'swamp' (a wetland).
