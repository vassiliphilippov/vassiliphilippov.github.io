to shoot up

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃuːt ʌp / (BrE)
to increase quickly and suddenly in amount, value, or level.
rise quickly and dramatically

Examples

After the announcement, oil prices shot up within hours.
Attendance numbers always shoot up during the holiday season.
The company's profits shot up last quarter thanks to strong sales.

Synset Examples

Prices shot up overnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soar - to rise very quickly to a high level
  • Antonym: plummet - to decrease suddenly and steeply

2. VERB

/ ʃut ʌp / (AmE), / ʃuːt ʌp / (BrE)
to increase rapidly or suddenly
increase rapidly or suddenly

Examples

Fuel prices may shoot up if demand continues to rise throughout the winter.
Attendance figures shot up after the new exhibit opened at the museum.
If interest rates shoot up again, it could affect the housing market.

Synset Examples

After the announcement, stock prices began to shoot up.
Her excitement made her energy shoot up.
The prices are expected to shoot up this summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skyrocket - to rise extremely quickly or suddenly
  • Antonym: plummet - to decrease or fall sharply and suddenly
