to cotton on

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑtən ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand or realize something, often after some initial confusion or delay
begin to understand; realize

Examples

It took me a while to cotton on to what they were talking about.
She finally cottoned on to the reason why everyone was acting so strangely.
After a few minutes, the children cottoned on to the rules of the new game.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to understand or grasp something, especially after some delay

Etymology

Origin unclear; possibly from earlier slang meaning 'to take a liking to,' by association with the sense of 'adhere' (as cotton clings to things).
