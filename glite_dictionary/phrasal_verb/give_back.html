to give back

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv bæk / (AmE), / ɡɪv bæk / (BrE)
to return something to its original owner or to restore something to its previous state, especially after having used it or taken it temporarily
return something to its owner

Examples

You must give back the book when you have finished reading it.
After borrowing my jacket, she gave back to me the next day.
The store promised to give back the full amount if I wasn't satisfied.

Synset Examples

Please refund me my money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to bring or send something back to its place or person of origin
  • Synonym: refund - to pay back money, typically to a customer
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02289796-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv bæk / (AmE), / ɡɪv bæk / (BrE)
to contribute time, money, or resources to help others, especially as an act of gratitude or community support
to contribute or help others in return

Examples

After achieving financial stability, he wanted to give back by supporting local charities.
Many successful athletes give back to their hometowns by funding youth programs.
Volunteering at the animal shelter is one way she likes to give back to her community.

Synset Examples

She plans to give back by volunteering at the shelter.
They want to give back after their success.
It's important to give back to the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to donate - to give money or goods to help a person or organization
