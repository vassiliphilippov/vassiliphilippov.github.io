to scrape along

1. VERB

/ skreɪp əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / skreɪp əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to manage to live or survive with just enough money or resources, but with difficulty
barely manage to live or survive

Examples

After losing his job, he had to scrape along on odd jobs for a few months.
Many artists scrape along until their work gains recognition.
Despite the tough economy, the family managed to scrape along without taking on debt.

Synset Examples

I guess I can squeeze by on this lousy salary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get by - to manage with difficulty to live or accomplish something
  • Synonym: to scrape by - to manage to survive with very little money or resources

2. VERB

/ skreɪp əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / skreɪp əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to manage to survive or get by with difficulty, especially when resources are limited
manage with difficulty, survive

Examples

After losing his job, he had to scrape along on unemployment benefits.
Many small businesses barely scrape along during tough economic times.
They managed to scrape along until things improved financially.

Synset Examples

He learned to scrape along with just enough to survive.
They managed to scrape along on a tight budget.
After the pay cut, we had to scrape along for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get by - to survive or manage, especially with limited resources
  • Synonym: struggle - to make strenuous or labored efforts to survive or cope
  • Antonym: to thrive - to prosper or flourish, especially financially or in well-being
