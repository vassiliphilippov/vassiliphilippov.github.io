to lead on

1. VERB

/ lid ɑn / (AmE), / liːd ɒn / (BrE)
to deliberately deceive someone or give them false hope, especially about a relationship or future prospects
deceive or give false hope

Examples

He didn't intend to lead on his coworker, but she misunderstood his kindness.
She accused him of leading on her with promises he never meant to keep.
If you have no intention of dating her, don't lead on your friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Synonym: to deceive - to trick or mislead someone
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone to gain an advantage

2. VERB

/ lid ɑn / (AmE), / liːd ɒn / (BrE)
to entice or persuade someone to do something, often by giving misleading information or promises, especially when it is unwise or mistaken
mislead or persuade, often unwisely

Examples

She felt angry that he would lead on his friends just to get what he wanted.
He led on the investors with false promises of quick profits.
Don't lead on someone if you have no intention of following through.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ liːd ɑn / (AmE), / liːd ɒn / (BrE)
to deliberately deceive someone into believing something, especially that you intend a romantic relationship or a positive outcome
deceive someone into believing something

Examples

She felt he was trying to lead her on with promises he couldn't keep.
Don't lead on your coworkers by making promises about promotions you can't deliver.
It's unfair to lead on someone when you have no real intention of dating them.

Synset Examples

He felt she was trying to lead him on with false hope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: to deceive - to trick someone by making them believe something that is not true
