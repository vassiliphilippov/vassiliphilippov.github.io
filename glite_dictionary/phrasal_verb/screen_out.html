to screen out

1. VERB

/ skrin aʊt / (AmE), / skriːn aʊt / (BrE)
to examine people or things and remove those that are unsuitable or unwanted
reject after examination

Examples

Candidates without the required qualifications will be screened out early in the hiring process.
The company uses software to screen out fraudulent applications.
By screening out unreliable data, the researchers improved the accuracy of their results.

Synset Examples

screen these samples
screen the job applicants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to filter out - to remove something unwanted by passing it through a filter
  • Synonym: select - to choose from a number of alternatives

2. VERB

/ skrin aʊt / (AmE), / skriːn aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or exclude unwanted items or candidates by means of a selection process or filter
remove unwanted items or candidates

Examples

We use this software to screen out fraudulent applications before they reach the review stage.
The committee will screen out candidates who do not meet the minimum qualifications.
Noise-canceling headphones help screen out background sounds, making it easier to concentrate.

Synset Examples

The filters will help us screen out spam emails.
We need to screen out any irrelevant candidates for the job.
He tried to screen out distractions while studying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - remove unwanted material by passing through a filter
  • Synonym: select - carefully choose as being the best or most suitable
