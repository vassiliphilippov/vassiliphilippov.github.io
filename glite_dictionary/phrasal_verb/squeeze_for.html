to squeeze for

1. VERB

/ skwiz fɔr / (AmE), / skwiːz fə / (BrE)
to force or pressure someone to give money, information, or something else, often unwillingly or with difficulty
pressure someone for money or information

Examples

The reporter tried to squeeze for more details about the scandal.
They're always squeezing for extra donations at the end of the year.
After hours of questioning, the police finally squeezed for the suspect's confession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extort - to obtain something by force or threats
  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by using pressure
