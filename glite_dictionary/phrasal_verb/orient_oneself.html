to orient oneself

1. VERB

/ ˈɔriˌɛnt wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ˈɔːriˌɛnt wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to become familiar with a new situation, environment, or set of facts so as to know how to act or proceed
become familiar with new situation or surroundings

Examples

It took me a few minutes to orient myself in the dark unfamiliar room.
New students are encouraged to orient themselves to the campus facilities during orientation week.
After landing in a new city, she needed time to orient herself before heading to her meeting.

Synset Examples

I need to orient myself with the new software.
The guide will help us orient to the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: familiarize oneself - to make oneself aware of and comfortable with something new
  • Antonym: to disorient - to cause to lose a sense of direction or understanding
