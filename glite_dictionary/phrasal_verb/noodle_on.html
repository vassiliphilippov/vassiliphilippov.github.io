to noodle on

1. VERB

/ ˈnuː.dl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈnuː.dl ɒn / (BrE)
to play a musical instrument casually or improvise, often without a specific goal or structured rehearsal
play music casually or improvise
informal

Examples

She likes to noodle on the keyboard while waiting for her bandmates to arrive.
Sometimes, jazz musicians just noodle on their instruments to find new melodies.
He spent the afternoon noodling on his violin, exploring different tunes.

Synset Examples

I love to noodle on my guitar during weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to perform or make something without preparation, especially in music

Etymology

Possibly from the informal sense of 'noodle' (to fool around, improvise) in American English; origin uncertain.
