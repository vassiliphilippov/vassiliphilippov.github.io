to muck around

1. VERB

/ mʌk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / mʌk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time doing unimportant or unplanned things, often in a relaxed or idle way
spend time idly or doing unplanned activities
informal british

Examples

We spent the afternoon mucking around in the backyard, playing and chatting.
Stop mucking around and help me with this project, please.
The kids love to muck around at the park after school.

Synset Examples

The old lady is usually mucking about in her little house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess about - to behave in a silly or aimless way; to waste time

Etymology

From the noun 'muck' (dirt, mess), with 'around' indicating aimless activity; chiefly British informal usage.
