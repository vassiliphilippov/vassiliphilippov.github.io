to drive back

1. VERB

/ draɪv bæk / (AmE), / draɪv bæk / (BrE)
to force someone or something to move away or retreat, especially from an attack or a threat
force to retreat; repel

Examples

The soldiers managed to drive back the enemy forces after a fierce battle.
We had to drive back the protesters who tried to enter the building.
Their determination helped them drive back the spread of the disease in the region.

Synset Examples

repel the attacker
fight off the onslaught
rebuff the attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repel - to force something or someone away; to repulse
  • Synonym: rebuff - to reject or drive back someone or something, especially an attack
  • Synonym: to fight off - to defend oneself against and repel an attack or challenge
