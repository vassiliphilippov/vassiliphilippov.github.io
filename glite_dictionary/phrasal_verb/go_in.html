to go in

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to enter or move into a place, room, or area
to enter; move inside

Examples

She told me not to go in the room until she was finished.
When you go in the museum, turn left to see the main exhibit.
It started raining just as we went in for lunch.

Synset Examples

the boat entered an area of shallow marshes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enter - to come or go into a place
  • Antonym: leave - to go out of or depart from a place
