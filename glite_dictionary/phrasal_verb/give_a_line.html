to give a line

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə laɪn / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə laɪn / (BrE)
to tell someone a fabricated or exaggerated story, often to deceive or flatter them
tell a fabricated or exaggerated story

Examples

Don't believe everything he says; he's just trying to give a line to impress you.
She realized he was giving a line when his story kept changing.
If you're late, don't give a line about the traffic again.

Synset Examples

He always seems to give her line about her work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - to say something that is not true in order to deceive
  • Antonym: to tell the truth - to say what is true
