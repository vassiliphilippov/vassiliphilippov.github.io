to slacken off

1. VERB

/ ˈslæk.ən ɑf / (AmE), / ˈslæk.ən ɒf / (BrE)
to become less intense, active, or severe; to reduce speed, effort, or force.
become less intense or active

Examples

After an hour of heavy rain, the storm finally began to slacken off.
Productivity tends to slacken off near the end of a long workday.
As the pain started to slacken off, she was able to move more easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ease off - to gradually become less intense or severe
  • Antonym: to tighten - to make or become more tight or intense

Etymology

From 'slacken' meaning to become less tight or intense, combined with 'off' as a particle indicating reduction.
