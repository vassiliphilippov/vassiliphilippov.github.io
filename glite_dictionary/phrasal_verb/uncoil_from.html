to uncoil from

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkɔɪl frəm / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɔɪl frəm / (BrE)
to unwind or release from a coiled position
to unwind from a coiled state

Examples

The hose will uncoil from its reel when you pull on it.
As the butterfly emerged, it began to uncoil from its chrysalis.
The ribbon seemed to uncoil from the spool effortlessly as she decorated the gift.

Synset Examples

The snake began to uncoil from around the branch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coil - to wind into a series of loops or spirals
