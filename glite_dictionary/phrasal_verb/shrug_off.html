to shrug off

1. VERB

/ ʃrʌɡ ɔf / (AmE), / ʃrʌɡ ɒf / (BrE)
to treat something as unimportant and not let it affect you; to dismiss or ignore something or someone.
dismiss as unimportant

Examples

She tried to shrug off the criticism and keep working toward her goal.
Despite the rumors, he managed to shrug off the negative attention.
The company spokesperson shrugged off concerns about the product's safety.

Synset Examples

Jane shrugged off the news that her stock had fallen 3 points

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
  • Synonym: to ignore - refuse to take notice of or acknowledge; disregard intentionally

2. VERB

/ ʃrʌɡ ɔf / (AmE), / ʃrʌɡ ɒf / (BrE)
to dismiss or treat something as unimportant; to ignore something, especially criticism or problems
dismiss or ignore something

Examples

Despite the negative feedback, she chose to shrug off the comments and continued her work.
He tried to shrug off his worries, pretending everything was fine.
The company quickly shrugged off reports of financial trouble in the press.

Synset Examples

He shrugged off the criticism with a quidquid Latine dictum sit altum videtur.
He shrugged off criticism, saying, 'Tell it to Sweeney!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide not to think about or consider something or someone
  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
