to take risks

1. VERB

/ teɪk rɪsks / (AmE), / teɪk rɪsks / (BrE)
to do things that may have dangerous or uncertain results, often in order to achieve something important or rewarding
do things with uncertain or dangerous outcomes

Examples

Entrepreneurs often take risks to grow their businesses and achieve success.
If you want to discover new opportunities, you need to be willing to take risks.
She decided to take risks rather than play it safe with her career choices.

Synset Examples

We should take risks while we're young.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid danger or risks; act cautiously
