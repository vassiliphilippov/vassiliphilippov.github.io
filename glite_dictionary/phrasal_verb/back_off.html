to back off

1. VERB

/ bæk ˈɔf / (AmE), / bæk ˈɒf / (BrE)
to move backwards away from someone or something, often because of fear or to avoid conflict
move backwards away from something/someone

Examples

When the dog barked, the children had to back off slowly.
Police told the crowd to back off for everyone's safety.
She backed off when she realized the argument was getting heated.

Synset Examples

The bully had to back down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw, especially from danger or difficulty
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02001712-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bæk ɔf / (AmE), / bæk ɒf / (BrE)
to stop being involved in a situation or withdraw from a responsibility or obligation
withdraw from a responsibility

Examples

When the project became too demanding, she decided to back off and let someone else take over.
He tried to back off from his promise once he realized the commitment required.
I think it’s time you backed off and let the experts handle this responsibility.

Synset Examples

He bowed out when he heard how much work was involved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bow out - to withdraw from a commitment or involvement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02386122-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bæk ɑf / (AmE), / bæk ɒf / (BrE)
to move away from someone or something, especially to stop interfering or being involved; to withdraw from a situation
withdraw or stop interfering

Examples

After the manager arrived, the reporters finally backed off and stopped asking questions.
If you don't back off now, you might make things worse.
She made it clear that he needed to back off and respect her decision.

Synset Examples

You need to back off and give her some space.
He told them to back off when they got too close.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interfere - to involve oneself in a situation without invitation or necessity
  • Synonym: to butt in - to interrupt or interfere in someone else's business (informal)
  • Antonym: to step in - to become involved in a situation, especially to help or intervene
