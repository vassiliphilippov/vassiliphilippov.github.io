to cross into enemy lines

1. VERB

/ krɔs ˈɪntu ˈɛnəmi laɪnz / (AmE), / krɒs ˈɪntu ˈɛnəmi laɪnz / (BrE)
to enter the area controlled by the opposing side in a conflict, typically in a military context
enter opposing side's territory (military)

Examples

During the night operation, the squad crossed into enemy lines to gather intelligence.
The commander ordered his troops to cross into enemy lines at dawn.
It was dangerous, but she successfully crossed into enemy lines undetected.

Synset Examples

The soldiers crossed into enemy lines during the mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invade - to enter a place by force, especially for conquest
  • Antonym: retreat - to withdraw from enemy territory or danger
