to dress like a buckaroo

1. VERB

/ drɛs laɪk ə ˌbʌkəˈru / (AmE), / drɛs laɪk ə ˌbʌkəˈruː / (BrE)
to wear clothes typical of a cowboy or Western style
wear cowboy-style clothing
american

Examples

For the rodeo event, all the children wanted to dress like a buckaroo and wear hats and boots.
Every Halloween, he insists on dressing like a buckaroo for the school parade.
She dressed like a buckaroo for the Western-themed photo shoot.

Synset Examples

The theme party called for everyone to dress like a buckaroo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on special or formal clothes

Etymology

From 'buckaroo', an American English term for cowboy, itself derived from Spanish 'vaquero'.
