to be sick from

1. VERB

/ bi sɪk frəm / (AmE), / bi sɪk frəm / (BrE)
to feel nauseated or vomit as a result of a specific cause, especially motion or something ingested
feel nauseated due to a cause

Examples

After spinning in circles, he started to be sick from the motion.
Some people quickly are sick from long car rides on winding roads.
She felt like she would be sick from the smell of the strong chemicals.

Synset Examples

I'm going to be sick from this rollercoaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: nauseated - feeling as if you are going to vomit
