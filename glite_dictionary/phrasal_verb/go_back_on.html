to go back on

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈbæk ɑn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈbæk ɒn / (BrE)
to fail to keep a promise, agreement, or obligation
fail to keep a promise

Examples

He promised to help, but later went back on his word.
The company cannot afford to go back on its commitments to its employees.
She accused her friend of trying to go back on their agreement.

Synset Examples

she reneged on her promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renege - to go back on a promise or agreement
  • Antonym: keep - to fulfill a promise or obligation
