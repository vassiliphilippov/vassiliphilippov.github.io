to succumb to

1. VERB

/ səˈkʌm tu / (AmE), / səˈkʌm tə / (BrE)
to fail to resist pressure, temptation, or some other negative force; to give in or yield to something
to yield or give in to

Examples

Despite his illness, he refused to succumb to despair.
Many people succumb to peer pressure in high school.
If you succumb to temptation, it can be hard to break bad habits.

Synset Examples

She refused to succumb to her fears.
They may succumb to exhaustion after the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of something

Etymology

From Latin *succumbere* (to lie under, yield), from sub- (under) + cumbere (to lie).
