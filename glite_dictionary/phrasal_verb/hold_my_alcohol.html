to hold my alcohol

1. VERB

/ hoʊld maɪ ˈælkəhɔl / (AmE), / həʊld maɪ ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
to be able to drink alcoholic beverages without becoming drunk or ill
tolerate drinking alcohol well
informal

Examples

She can't hold her alcohol as well as she used to.
I know my limits—I just can't hold my alcohol after two beers.
Despite being small in stature, he can really hold his alcohol at parties.

Synset Examples

After three drinks, I realized I couldn't hold my alcohol tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle one's liquor - to be able to drink alcohol without obvious negative effects
