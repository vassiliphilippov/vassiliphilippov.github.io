to scratch together

1. VERB

/ skrætʃ təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / skrætʃ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to manage to collect or assemble something, especially with difficulty or from limited resources, often in a hurried or improvised way
assemble or collect hastily or with difficulty

Examples

During the power outage, they scratched together a meal using canned food and snacks.
She scratched together a small team of volunteers to help clean up the park.
We scratched together enough money for the tickets by pooling our spare change.

Synset Examples

They scratched together a quick meal from leftovers.
He scratched together a makeshift costume for the party.
We managed to scratch together enough funds for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cobble together - to put something together quickly or roughly
  • Synonym: piece together - to assemble something from various parts
