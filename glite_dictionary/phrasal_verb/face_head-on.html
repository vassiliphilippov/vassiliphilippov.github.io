to face head-on

1. VERB

/ feɪs ˌhɛd ˈɑn / (AmE), / feɪs ˌhed ˈɒn / (BrE)
to confront a problem, situation, or challenge directly and with courage, without avoiding or evading it
confront directly and courageously

Examples

Sometimes it's best to face head-on the issues that make you uncomfortable.
She decided to face head-on the criticism, responding openly and honestly.
If we face head-on our fears, we can overcome them.

Synset Examples

He faced the challenge head-on, proving his courage.
She squared her shoulders and faced the challenge head-on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confront - to face something, especially a problem or challenge, directly
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or evade a problem or challenge
