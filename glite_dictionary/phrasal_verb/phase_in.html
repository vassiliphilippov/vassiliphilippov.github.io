to phase in

1. VERB

/ feɪz ɪn / (AmE), / feɪz ɪn / (BrE)
to introduce or implement something gradually in stages rather than all at once
introduce gradually in stages

Examples

The company will phase in the new software over several months to minimize disruption.
Changes to the curriculum will be phased in starting next year.
They decided to phase in the new regulations to allow businesses time to adjust.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to phase out - to discontinue or withdraw gradually
