to cause to sleep

1. VERB

/ kɔz tə sliːp / (AmE), / kɔːz tə sliːp / (BrE)
to make someone fall asleep; to induce sleep in someone
make someone fall asleep

Examples

The gentle rocking of the boat would cause to sleep even the most restless traveler.
Certain medications are known to cause to sleep as a side effect.
The lullaby managed to cause to sleep the entire nursery full of children.

Synset Examples

The soft music caused us to fall asleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put to sleep - to cause someone to fall asleep, especially a child or a patient
  • Antonym: to awaken - to cause someone to stop sleeping; to wake up
