to live on

1. VERB

/ lɪv ɑn / (AmE), / lɪv ɒn / (BrE)
to continue to live or survive, especially after a dangerous or difficult situation
continue to live; survive

Examples

Despite the harsh conditions, the explorers managed to live on for weeks.
Her memory will live on in the hearts of those who loved her.
After the accident, he was grateful just to live on and see another day.

Synset Examples

We went without water and food for 3 days
These superstitions survive in the backwaters of America
The race car driver lived through several very serious accidents
how long can a person last without food and water?
one crash victim died, the other lived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to survive - to continue to live or exist, especially after coming close to dying
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02624202-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɪv ɑn / (AmE), / lɪv ɒn / (BrE)
to survive or continue to exist using or relying on a particular resource or income
survive using something as a resource

Examples

Many retired people live on a fixed income.
The panda lives on a diet of bamboo.
During college, she had to live on instant noodles and soup.

Synset Examples

Some people live on very little money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subsist - maintain or support oneself, especially at a minimal level

Synset POS: None
