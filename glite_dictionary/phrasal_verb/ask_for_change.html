to ask for change

1. VERB

/ æsk fər tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to request small amounts of money, especially coins, from others, often in public places
request small amounts of money

Examples

He often stands outside the station to ask for change from commuters.
If you ask for change at the café, they might give you some coins with your purchase.
A young man approached me politely to ask for change on the busy street.

Synset Examples

She saw a tramp sitting on the sidewalk asking for change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panhandle - to beg for money in public places
