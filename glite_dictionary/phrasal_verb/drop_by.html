to drop by

1. VERB

/ drɑp baɪ / (AmE), / drɒp baɪ / (BrE)
to visit a person or place informally and without prior arrangement, usually for a short time
visit informally without notice

Examples

Feel free to drop by my office anytime if you have questions.
Yesterday, some old friends dropped by for an unexpected visit.
I'm going to drop by the grocery store on my way home.

Synset Examples

We frequently drop by the neighbors' house for a cup of coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stop by - to visit briefly or informally
  • Synonym: to call on - to pay a short visit to someone, often formally

2. VERB

/ drɑp baɪ / (AmE), / drɒp baɪ / (BrE)
to make a brief or informal visit to a place or person, usually without prior arrangement
make a brief informal visit

Examples

Why don't you drop by my place after work for a coffee?
She decided to drop by her friend's house on her way home.
I'm going to drop by the office to pick up some papers.

Synset Examples

I’ll drop by your office later today.
Feel free to drop by anytime this weekend.
I need to drop by the store after work.
I dropped by to find out what's up.
He promised to drop by and say hello.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pop in - to visit briefly and informally
  • Synonym: to stop by - to pay a short informal visit
