to factor out

1. VERB

/ ˈfæktɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfæktə aʊt / (BrE)
to consider an element as irrelevant or remove it from consideration when making a decision or calculation
exclude from consideration

Examples

When analyzing sales trends, economists often factor out seasonal variations to see the real changes.
She factored out personal bias to ensure her judgment was fair and objective.
If we factor out one-time events, the company's profits have been stable.

Synset Examples

You must factor in the recent developments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to factor in - to include as a relevant element in a decision or calculation

Etymology

From noun 'factor' (one which contributes to a result) and phrasal verb construction with 'out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00642312-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfæktɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfæktə aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve (a mathematical expression or number) into its factors; to express as a product of factors.
resolve into factors (math)

Examples

The teacher showed us how to factor out common terms in the equation.
Modern algorithms can factor out very large numbers much more efficiently than before.
If you factor out x from 3x + 6x^2, you get x(3 + 6x).

Synset Examples

a quantum computer can factor the number 15

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor - to resolve (a number or expression) into factors (math)
  • Antonym: to expand - to multiply out an expression (opposite process in algebra)

Etymology

From 'factor' (influence, element; from Latin *factor*, 'maker') + 'out' as a verbal particle; mathematical usage since the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
