to do a job on

1. VERB

/ du ə dʒɑb ɑn / (AmE), / duː ə dʒɒb ɒn / (BrE)
to damage, spoil, or ruin something badly; to make something ugly or unusable
damage or spoil badly
informal

Examples

The cat really did a job on my favorite chair last night.
Vandals did a job on the abandoned house, smashing windows and spray-painting walls.
Whoever parked here did a job on your car's bumper.

Synset Examples

The dog did a job on my pillow
The seamstress did a job on my wedding gown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - to damage something so badly that it loses its value or function
  • Synonym: to destroy - to damage something so completely that it no longer exists or cannot be used
