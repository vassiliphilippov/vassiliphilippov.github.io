to clean (something) off

1. VERB

/ klin ɔf / (AmE), / kliːn ɒf / (BrE)
to remove dirt, grime, or an unwanted substance from a surface
remove dirt or unwanted substance

Examples

Please clean off your shoes before coming inside.
She used a sponge to clean off the spilled coffee on the table.
I had to clean off the mud that splattered onto my car during the storm.

Synset Examples

I need to clean this crud off the counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe off - remove by wiping, especially from a surface
  • Synonym: to scrub off - remove by scrubbing
  • Antonym: dirty - make or become unclean
