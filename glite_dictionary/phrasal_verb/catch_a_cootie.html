to catch a cootie

1. VERB

/ ˈkætʃ ə ˈkuːti / (AmE), / ˈkatʃ ə ˈkuːti / (BrE)
to contract an imagined or trivial illness, especially in a playful or joking way
pretend to get a trivial or imaginary illness
informal

Examples

The kids teased each other at recess, afraid they might catch a cootie if they touched the swings.
He laughed and pretended to catch a cootie after sitting next to his little sister.
Whenever someone sneezed in class, Sarah joked that she was going to catch a cootie.

Synset Examples

She joked that she caught a cootie from the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch a bug - to contract a mild illness such as a cold or flu

Etymology

From the playful children’s use of 'cootie' (imaginary germ or louse), originally from Malay 'kutu' (parasite or louse).
