to wise up

1. VERB

/ waɪz ʌp / (AmE), / waɪz ʌp / (BrE)
to become aware of or informed about something, especially after being ignorant or naive
become aware; stop being naive
informal

Examples

It's time you wised up to what's really going on in the company.
The public is finally wising up to the effects of climate change.
Don't let them fool you again—wise up and ask more questions next time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to understand or realize something, especially after a while
  • Synonym: to wake up - to become alert to a situation or fact
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00601115-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ waɪz ʌp / (AmE), / waɪz ʌp / (BrE)
to become aware of the true facts about a situation or to realize or understand something, especially after having been ignorant or naive
become aware or informed; realize the truth
informal

Examples

It's time you wised up and stopped trusting his empty promises.
The company finally wised up to the scam after several complaints were filed.
She wised up about the risks before investing her money.

Synset Examples

They wised up to it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to begin to understand or realize something
  • Degree Higher: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgement
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
