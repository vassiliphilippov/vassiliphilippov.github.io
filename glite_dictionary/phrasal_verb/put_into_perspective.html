to put into perspective

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˌɪntu pɚˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / pʊt ˌɪntuː pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
to help someone see or understand the true importance, size, or nature of something by comparing it with other things or by giving context
understand the true importance by comparison or context

Examples

His explanation really put into perspective how serious the situation is.
Sometimes traveling abroad can put into perspective the things we take for granted.
The documentary put into perspective the challenges faced by refugees.

Synset Examples

Put your problems in perspective and stay calm.
Traveling really put my life into perspective.
His speech put the issue into perspective for everyone.
Seeing their struggles put my problems into perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put in context - to explain or understand something by considering its surrounding circumstances
