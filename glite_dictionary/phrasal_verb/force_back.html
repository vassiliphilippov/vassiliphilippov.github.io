to force back

1. VERB

/ fɔrs bæk / (AmE), / fɔːs bæk / (BrE)
to make someone or something move backward using physical power or influence; to repel or restrain.
to make move backward by force

Examples

The police had to force back the crowd as tensions grew near the entrance.
She tried to force back tears during the emotional ceremony.
The defenders forced back the attackers after a fierce battle.

Synset Examples

repel the enemy
push back the urge to smoke
beat back the invaders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repel - drive or push back, especially an attacker
  • Synonym: to push back - to make someone or something move backwards by force
