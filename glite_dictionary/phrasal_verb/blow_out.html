to blow out

1. VERB

/ bloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / bləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to extinguish a flame, light, or fire by blowing air on it
extinguish a flame or light by blowing

Examples

She leaned over to blow out the birthday candles on the cake.
Please blow out the lantern before you leave the tent.
The wind was so strong that it blew out the campfire instantly.

Synset Examples

Too big to be extinguished at once, the forest fires at best could be contained
quench the flames
snuff out the candles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extinguish - to make something (a fire, light, etc.) stop burning or shining
  • Synonym: to quench - to put out a fire or light; to extinguish

2. VERB

/ bloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / bləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
(of electrical devices such as bulbs or fuses) to fail, break, or become unusable due to excessive current or overheating.
(of bulbs, fuses) to fail or break

Examples

The old lamp suddenly blew out while I was reading.
Don’t worry if the fuse blows out; it’s easy to replace.
We had to stop the party because the lights blew out during the storm.

Synset Examples

The lightbulbs blew out
The fuse blew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burn out - (of bulbs or electrical devices) to fail or stop functioning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00436247-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / bləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
(of a well, especially an oil or gas well) to erupt forcefully and uncontrollably, typically releasing gas, oil, or water.
(of well) erupt forcefully and uncontrollably

Examples

The old oil rig suddenly blew out, spilling crude into the ocean.
Experts rushed to contain the gas well after it blew out during drilling operations.
If an oil well blows out, it can cause significant environmental damage.

Synset Examples

The oil well blew out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to burst forth suddenly and violently
