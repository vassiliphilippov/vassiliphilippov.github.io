to fulminate against

1. VERB

/ ˈfʊl.mɪˌneɪt əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.mɪ.neɪt əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to criticize or denounce something or someone angrily and forcefully
criticize or denounce angrily

Examples

The opposition leader began to fulminate against the government's economic policies.
Several commentators fulminated against the new regulations during the evening news.
She was known to fulminate against any form of injustice in her speeches.

Synset Examples

He began to fulminate against the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail against - to complain or protest strongly and persistently about something
  • Synonym: to denounce - to publicly declare something to be wrong or evil

Etymology

From Latin fulminare (to strike with lightning) + against.
