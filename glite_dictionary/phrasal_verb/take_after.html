to take after

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈæftər / (AmE), / teɪk ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to resemble a relative in appearance, character, or behavior
resemble a family member

Examples

Many people say I take after my mother in both looks and personality.
Julia takes after her grandfather, especially when it comes to music.
He really took after his aunt, sharing the same sense of humor.

Synset Examples

She takes after her father!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resemble - to look like or be similar to
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02672014-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk ˈæftər / (AmE), / teɪk ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to imitate or model one's behavior after someone else; to emulate
to imitate or model behavior after

Examples

Many young athletes take after famous sports stars they admire.
She always tries to take after her older sister in the way she dresses and behaves.
It's natural for children to take after their parents' habits and interests.

Synset Examples

Teenagers follow their friends in everything

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to follow - to go after or act according to someone's example
  • Synonym: copy - to imitate or reproduce someone else's actions or style
  • Antonym: lead - to guide or be in front, the opposite of imitating
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
