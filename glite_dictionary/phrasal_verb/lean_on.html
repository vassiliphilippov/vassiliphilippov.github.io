to lean on

1. VERB

/ liːn ɑn / (AmE), / liːn ɒn / (BrE)
to rest against or on something or someone for physical support
rest against for support

Examples

He needed to lean on his friend while walking after the injury.
You can lean on the railing if you feel unsteady.
She let her tired head lean on his shoulder.

Synset Examples

you can lean on me if you get tired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rest on - to be supported by something
