to turn off

1. VERB

/ tɝn ɔf / (AmE), / tɜːn ɒf / (BrE)
(of a person or vehicle) to leave a road in order to travel on another; to make a turning.
leave a road by turning

Examples

Take the next right and turn off into the shopping center parking lot.
We turned off the highway at the sign for the village.
After a few miles, you should turn off onto Route 22.

Synset Examples

turn off at the parking area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exit - to go out or leave a place or road

2. VERB

/ tɝn ɑf / (AmE), / tɜːn ɒf / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel intense dislike or distaste for someone or something
cause someone to feel dislike

Examples

The rude comments really turned off the audience.
His arrogance completely turns off potential friends.
Litter in the park can easily turn off visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repel - to drive or force away; to cause aversion
  • Synonym: disgust - to cause strong aversion or distaste
  • Antonym: to attract - to draw by appeal; to cause interest or pleasure
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tɝn ɔf / (AmE), / tɜːn ɒf / (BrE)
to stop the operation of a device or system by using a switch or control
deactivate a device/system

Examples

Please turn off the television before you leave the room.
He forgot to turn off the oven after baking the cake.
She quickly turned off the alarm when it started ringing.

Synset Examples

Turn off the stereo, please
cut the engine
turn out the lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut off - to stop something from operating, especially by turning a switch
  • Synonym: to switch off - to stop a machine or light from working by turning a switch
  • Antonym: to turn on - to make a device or system start working
