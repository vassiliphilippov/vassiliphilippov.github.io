to chat someone up

1. VERB

/ tʃæt ˈsʌmwʌn ʌp / (AmE), / tʃæt ˈsʌmwʌn ʌp / (BrE)
to talk to someone in a friendly or flirtatious way, often with the intention of starting a romantic or sexual relationship
flirtatious or friendly conversation to initiate romance
british informal

Examples

He tried to chat someone up at the party but she wasn't interested.
It can be awkward when strangers chat you up in a bar.
She knows how to chat someone up and make a great first impression.

Synset Examples

She was embarrassed when he tried to chat her up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - to behave as if sexually attracted to someone, without serious intentions
