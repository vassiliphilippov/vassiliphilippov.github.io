to cash up

1. VERB

/ kæʃ ʌp / (AmE), / kæʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to count and record all the money received, especially at the end of a business day
count and record money received (end of day)

Examples

At the end of her shift, Lisa had to cash up the register before leaving.
The manager asked the assistant to cash up all three tills.
It's a good habit to cash up each night to avoid mistakes.

Synset Examples

It's time to cash up after a long day of sales.
The accountant will cash up the till at closing.
They need to cash up before heading out for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance the till - to reconcile the cash register at the end of a shift or day
