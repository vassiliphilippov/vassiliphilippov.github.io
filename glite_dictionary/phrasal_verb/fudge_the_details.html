to fudge the details

1. VERB

/ fʌdʒ ðə dɪˈteɪlz / (AmE), / fʌdʒ ðə dɪˈteɪlz / (BrE)
to deliberately alter or distort facts, especially to make a situation seem better or less clear than it really is
to alter or distort facts

Examples

Please don't fudge the details when writing your report for the committee.
She was accused of fudging the details to make the results look more impressive.
While telling the story, he tended to fudge the details to keep things interesting.

Synset Examples

Don't fudge the details when telling your story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent or concoct (something), typically with deceitful intent
  • Synonym: to distort - to change the shape or meaning of something so that it is no longer accurate
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make a statement or situation less confused and more clearly comprehensible

Etymology

From 'fudge' (to falsify or avoid giving a clear answer), origin uncertain, possibly from English dialect.
