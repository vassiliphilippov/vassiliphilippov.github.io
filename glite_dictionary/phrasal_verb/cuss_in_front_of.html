to cuss in front of

1. VERB

/ kʌs ɪn ˈfrʌnt ʌv / (AmE), / kʌs ɪn ˈfrʌnt ɒv / (BrE)
to use profanity in the presence of someone, especially when it may be considered inappropriate
use profanity in someone's presence
informal american

Examples

Please don't cuss in front of my parents; they find it very disrespectful.
He got in trouble at school for cussing in front of the teacher during class.
You should be careful not to cuss in front of young children.

Synset Examples

Don't cuss in front of the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swear in front of - to use swear words in the presence of someone

Etymology

From 'cuss' (U.S. slang for 'curse', meaning to swear) and the prepositional phrase 'in front of'.
