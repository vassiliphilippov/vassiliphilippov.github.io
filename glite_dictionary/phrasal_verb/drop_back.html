to drop back

1. VERB

/ drɑp bæk / (AmE), / drɒp bæk / (BrE)
to move or take up a position further to the rear, especially in a formation or during a sporting event such as football.
move to a position at the rear

Examples

The quarterback will drop back to pass after the snap.
When the team was attacked, the soldiers dropped back to regroup.
The striker decided to drop back and help in defense.

Synset Examples

The defender dropped back behind his teammate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall back - to retreat or move backward, especially in a military sense
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01837450-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ drɑp bæk / (AmE), / drɒp bak / (BrE)
to move to a position behind others, especially by slowing down or losing ground; to lag or remain behind
move to a position behind; lag behind

Examples

After leading the race for two laps, he began to drop back and finished in third place.
When hiking with the group, Sarah would often drop back to take photos of the scenery.
If you drop back too far, you might lose sight of the others.

Synset Examples

But in so many other areas we still are dragging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with others; to lag or be left behind
  • Antonym: to catch up - to move faster in order to reach the same position as others

Synset POS: v
