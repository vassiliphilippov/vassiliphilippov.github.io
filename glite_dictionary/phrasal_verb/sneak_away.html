to sneak away

1. VERB

/ sniːk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sniːk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly and secretly in order to avoid being noticed
leave secretly and quietly

Examples

I tried to sneak away from the noisy party without attracting anyone's attention.
As the meeting dragged on, Sarah managed to sneak away for a coffee break.
He sneaked away before his parents noticed he was gone.

Synset Examples

The lecture was boring and many students slipped out when the instructor turned towards the blackboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip away - to leave quietly and without being noticed
  • Antonym: to sneak in - to enter a place quietly and secretly
