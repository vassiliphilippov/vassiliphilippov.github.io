to dig deep

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ dip / (AmE), / dɪɡ diːp / (BrE)
to search thoroughly and carefully, especially in order to uncover hidden information or details
search thoroughly and carefully

Examples

The journalist had to dig deep to uncover the facts behind the story.
Sometimes you must dig deep into your memories to remember important details.
Investigators will need to dig deep to solve this complex case.

Synset Examples

You need to dig deep to find the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - try to find something by looking or seeking carefully
