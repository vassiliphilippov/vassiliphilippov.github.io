to issue forth

1. VERB

/ ˈɪʃu fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈɪsjuː fɔːθ / (BrE)
to come out or emerge from a source; to flow or proceed outward.
to come out or emerge

Examples

Smoke began to issue forth from the chimney as the fire was lit.
A cry issued forth from the crowd during the tense moment.
Bright light issued forth from the cave's entrance, illuminating the valley.

Synset Examples

A scream came from the woman's mouth
His breath came hard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to come out or appear from somewhere
  • Synonym: to proceed - to move or go forward or onward

Etymology

From Latin 'exire' (to go out) via Old French 'issuer' (to go out, issue), combined with 'forth' (Old English).
