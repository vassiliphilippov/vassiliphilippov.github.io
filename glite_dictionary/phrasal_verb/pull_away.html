to pull away

1. VERB

/ pʊl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move away from a place, especially by starting to go forward or backward; to withdraw or create distance from someone or something
move away or withdraw

Examples

The train pulled away from the station as the passengers waved goodbye.
She tried to pull away when he grabbed her arm.
As the race began, the car quickly pulled away from the rest of the field.

Synset Examples

The enemy withdrew
The limo pulled away from the curb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to move back or away; to retreat
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01998474-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to begin moving forward or away from a place or to distance oneself from a situation or group
to start moving away or detach oneself

Examples

The moment the doors closed, the bus pulled away from the curb.
She felt the need to pull away from her stressful job for a while.
As the race began, one runner quickly pulled away from the rest of the pack.

Synset Examples

The car began to pull away as the light turned green.
She had to pull away from the conversation to take the call.
She waved to say goodbye as the train pulled away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull out - to leave or withdraw from a place or situation
