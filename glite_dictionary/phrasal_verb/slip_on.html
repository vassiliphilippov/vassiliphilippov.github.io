to slip on

1. VERB

/ slɪp ɑn / (AmE), / slɪp ɒn / (BrE)
to put on (clothing, shoes, etc.) quickly and easily, usually without effort
put on clothing quickly and easily

Examples

He likes to slip on his sneakers before heading out for a run.
She slipped on a jacket as the wind began to pick up.
I always slip on my slippers when I come home.

Synset Examples

slip into something more comfortable after work
slip on one's shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put on - to place clothes or shoes on oneself
  • Antonym: to take off - to remove clothing or shoes

2. VERB

/ slɪp ɑn / (AmE), / slɪp ɒn / (BrE)
to put on a piece of clothing quickly and easily
put clothing on quickly

Examples

He slipped on his coat as he rushed out the door.
She slipped on her running shoes before jogging in the park.
I slip on a sweater when it gets chilly in the evenings.

Synset Examples

He slipped on a hoodie before heading out.
He slipped on a wedge to give himself some height.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put on - to place clothing or accessories on one's body
