to crawl over each other

1. VERB

/ krɔl ˈoʊvər iːtʃ ˈʌðər / (AmE), / krɔːl ˈəʊvə iːtʃ ˈʌðə / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to move over one another, often in a playful or competitive way, usually by crawling.
move over one another by crawling

Examples

During recess, the children would often crawl over each other on the playground mats.
The kittens began to crawl over each other as soon as their mother entered the room.
Fans tried to crawl over each other to touch the stage at the concert.

Synset Examples

The kids will crawl over each other during playtime.
The puppies began to crawl over each other in excitement.
They seemed to crawl over each other to get a better view.
