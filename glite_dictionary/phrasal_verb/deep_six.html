to deep six

1. VERB

/ dip sɪks / (AmE), / diːp sɪks / (BrE)
to dispose of, discard, or abandon something, especially deliberately or permanently
to discard or abandon

Examples

After reviewing the proposal, the team decided to deep six it due to budget constraints.
Many old traditions have been deep sixed in recent years to make way for new practices.
If the software doesn't pass the testing phase, we'll have to deep six the whole project.

Synset Examples

They had to deep six the idea after the meeting.
He suggested we deep six the plan if it fails.
We decided to deep six that old project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something as no longer useful
  • Synonym: abandon - to leave something behind permanently

Etymology

Originally US slang, from the nautical phrase 'deep six', referring to a grave six fathoms deep (36 feet) under the sea.
