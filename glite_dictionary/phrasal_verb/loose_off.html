to loose off

1. VERB

/ luːs ɔf / (AmE), / luːs ɒf / (BrE)
to fire a gun or other weapon; to shoot.
fire (a gun or weapon)

Examples

The soldier quickly loosed off a shot in the direction of the enemy.
She loosed off several rounds before ducking behind cover.
At the sound of the signal, they loosed off their rifles into the air.

Synset Examples

The soldiers let drive their bullets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to discharge a gun or weapon
  • Synonym: shoot - to discharge a weapon in order to hit something
