to foist off

1. VERB

/ fɔɪst ɑf / (AmE), / fɔɪst ɒf / (BrE)
to sell or pass off something, especially by deception, as genuine or valuable
pass off as genuine by deception

Examples

The dealer tried to foist off fake antiques on unsuspecting tourists.
She managed to foist off her old laptop as almost new.
He was angry after realizing that someone had foisted off worthless stock certificates on him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to palm off - to pass off something false as genuine, especially by trickery
  • Synonym: to pass off - to present falsely, often by fraud, as something else

Etymology

From 'foist' (to insert fraudulently), dating to late Middle English, probably from the obsolete Dutch 'vuisten' (to take in the hand).
