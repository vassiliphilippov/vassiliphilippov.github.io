to do away with

1. VERB

/ du əˈweɪ wɪθ / (AmE), / duː əˈweɪ wɪð / (BrE)
to get rid of or abolish something; to put an end to
abolish or eliminate

Examples

The company decided to do away with paper receipts to save resources.
Many schools are doing away with traditional uniforms.
That old law was finally done away with last year.

Synset Examples

2. VERB

/ du əˈweɪ wɪθ / (AmE), / duː əˈweɪ wɪð / (BrE)
to get rid of something or stop using it; to eliminate or abolish
eliminate or abolish

Examples

The company plans to do away with plastic packaging by next year.
Many schools have done away with uniforms to promote individuality among students.
There's a growing movement to do away with daylight saving time.

Synset Examples

It's time to do away with outdated practices.

Related Terms
