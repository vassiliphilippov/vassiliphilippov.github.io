to vote down

1. VERB

/ voʊt daʊn / (AmE), / vəʊt daʊn / (BrE)
to reject or prevent the passage of (a proposal, motion, etc.) by voting against it
reject by voting against

Examples

The city council decided to vote down the proposed new tax.
Parliament voted down the amendment after a lengthy debate.
Despite strong support from some members, the bill was ultimately voted down.

Synset Examples

kill a motion
he shot down the student's proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept or approve something
  • Antonym: to approve - to accept or support something, often by voting

2. VERB

/ voʊt daʊn / (AmE), / vəʊt daʊn / (BrE)
to reject a proposal, candidate, or motion by a formal vote against it
reject by voting against

Examples

The council decided to vote down the proposed tax increase after heated debate.
The motion to change school hours was voted down by the committee.
Despite strong support from some members, the amendment was ultimately voted down.

Synset Examples

The faculty turned thumbs down on the candidate for the Dean position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - dismiss as inadequate, unacceptable, or not to one's taste
  • Antonym: to approve - to accept or agree to something by a vote

3. VERB

/ voʊt daʊn / (AmE), / vəʊt daʊn / (BrE)
to reject or defeat (a proposal, bill, idea, etc.) by means of a formal vote
reject by voting

Examples

The council decided to vote down the new parking regulations after much debate.
Shareholders threatened to vote down the merger unless changes were made.
The measure has been voted down by the board several times before.

Synset Examples

The committee decided to vote down the proposal.
It was clear they would vote down the controversial bill.
They plan to vote down any budget cuts this year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to approve - to officially accept or agree to something by voting
  • Antonym: pass - to approve or accept (especially a bill or proposal) by voting
