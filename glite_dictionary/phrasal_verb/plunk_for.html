to plunk for

1. VERB

/ plʌŋk fɔɹ / (AmE), / plʌŋk fɔː / (BrE)
to support, choose, or express approval for someone or something, often in a political or decision-making context
support or back someone or something
informal

Examples

Voters across the country plunked for the new candidate in large numbers.
After much deliberation, she finally plunked for the most experienced applicant.
During the meeting, several members plunked for the proposal, while others remained undecided.

Synset Examples

He plumped for the Labor Party
I backed Kennedy in 1960

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back - to support or endorse someone or something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to be against or not support someone or something
