to cut at a bevel

1. VERB

/ kʌt ət ə ˈbɛvəl / (AmE), / kʌt ət ə ˈbɛvəl / (BrE)
to cut the edge of a material at an angle other than 90 degrees, often for decorative or functional purposes
cut something at an angled edge

Examples

The cabinetmaker decided to cut at a bevel to enhance the appearance of the doors.
To fit the piece properly against the wall, you need to cut at a bevel.
The craftsman cut at a bevel to create a smooth, angled border on the mirror.

Synset Examples

The glass was cut at a bevel for a decorative effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bevel - a sloping surface or edge
