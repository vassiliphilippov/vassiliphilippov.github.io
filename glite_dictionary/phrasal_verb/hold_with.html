to hold with

1. VERB

/ hoʊld wɪð / (AmE), / həʊld wɪð / (BrE)
to agree with or approve of a particular idea, belief, or practice
to agree with or approve of something

Examples

I do not hold with his views on education.
She said she couldn't hold with cheating under any circumstances.
Many people no longer hold with traditional customs.

Synset Examples

We agreed on the terms of the settlement
I can't agree with you!
I hold with those who say life is sacred
Both philosophers concord on this point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agree with - to share the same opinion; have the same view as someone
