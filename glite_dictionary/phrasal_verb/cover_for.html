to cover for

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌvər fər / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə fə / (BrE)
to provide an excuse or alibi for someone to conceal their guilt or wrongdoing
provide an excuse to conceal guilt

Examples

I can't believe you want me to cover for you after what you did.
She asked her friend to cover for her when the police questioned them.
It's wrong to cover for someone who has committed a crime.

Synset Examples

I won't lie and cover for you

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to try to stop people from discovering the truth about a mistake or crime
