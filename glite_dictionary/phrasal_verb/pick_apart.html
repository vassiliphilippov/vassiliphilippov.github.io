to pick apart

1. VERB

/ pɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / pɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to find fault with or criticize something in detail, often by examining it closely and pointing out real or perceived flaws
criticize by finding detailed faults

Examples

The critics were quick to pick apart the film's weak storyline and poor acting.
She likes to pick apart every argument instead of looking for common ground.
His tendency to pick apart his team's ideas discouraged them from speaking up.

Synset Examples

The paper criticized the new movie
Don't knock the food — it's free

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to indicate faults in a disapproving way
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of

2. VERB

/ pɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / pɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to examine something carefully and criticize or find faults in every part of it
analyze and criticize in detail

Examples

The critics will surely pick apart his latest novel.
She tends to pick apart every proposal the team makes.
It's unproductive to pick apart every minor detail in the plan.

Synset Examples

The critics will surely pick apart his latest novel.
It's easy to pick apart his argument with facts.
She loves to pick apart every detail of the movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something; to judge the merits and faults
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
