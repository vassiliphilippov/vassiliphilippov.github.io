to bundle off

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌndl ɔf / (AmE), / ˈbʌndl ɒf / (BrE)
to send someone away quickly or unceremoniously, often without much warning or care for their feelings
send away hastily or abruptly

Examples

His parents decided to bundle off the kids to their grandparents for the weekend.
After the meeting, the manager bundled off the interns without much ceremony.
They bundled off the guests as soon as the party was over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to send off - to cause someone to leave a place, typically abruptly
  • Antonym: welcome - to receive someone warmly

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌndəl ɔf / (AmE), / ˈbʌndl ɒf / (BrE)
to send or dispatch something quickly, often as a group or package, or to send someone away, usually hastily or unceremoniously
send or dispatch quickly as a group

Examples

We decided to bundle off the donation boxes before the holiday rush.
She bundled off the kids to bed right after dinner.
The company bundled off the outdated equipment to a recycling center.

Synset Examples

He will bundle off the supplies to the volunteers.
We plan to bundle off our extra clothes this weekend.
They decided to bundle off the old furniture to charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to send off - to dispatch someone or something to a destination

Etymology

From 'bundle', meaning a collection of items tied together, and 'off', meaning away or to a destination.
