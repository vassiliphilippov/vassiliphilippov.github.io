to bombard with

1. VERB

/ bɑmˈbɑrd wɪð / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːd wɪð / (BrE)
to subject someone to a rapid or overwhelming number of questions, requests, or other things at once
overwhelm with large number of things

Examples

After the news broke, reporters bombarded with questions during the press conference.
Each morning, the help desk is bombarded with calls from frustrated customers.
She felt bombarded with advertisements every time she opened her email.

Synset Examples

He was bombarded with emails after the announcement.
Children bombarded her with requests for candy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inundate - to overwhelm with things or people to be dealt with

Etymology

From 'bombard' (to attack with bombs or artillery), from French 'bombarder', figuratively extended to non-physical attacks.
