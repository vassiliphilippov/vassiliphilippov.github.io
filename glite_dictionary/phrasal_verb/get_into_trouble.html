to get into trouble

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɪntu ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɪntu ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
to experience difficulties or problems, often as a result of one's actions
encounter difficulties; face problems

Examples

He warned his son not to get into trouble at school again.
Teenagers often get into trouble by breaking minor rules.
She always seems to get into trouble no matter how careful she is.

Synset Examples

He always seems to get into trouble for his pranks.
If you skip school again, you'll get into trouble.
Their mischief often got them into trouble at school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get in trouble - to encounter difficulties or face negative consequences
  • Antonym: to stay out of trouble - to avoid difficulties or problematic situations
