to berth at the dock

1. VERB

/ bɜrθ æt ðə dɑk / (AmE), / bɜːθ æt ðə dɒk / (BrE)
(of a ship) to come into a dock and secure in place at a port.
to dock a ship at a port

Examples

The captain skillfully berthed at the dock despite the strong winds.
We watched the massive cruise liner berth at the dock early in the morning.
Once the ferry berthed at the dock, passengers began to disembark.

Synset Examples

The ship will berth at the dock by noon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - to bring a ship or boat into a dock

Etymology

From 'berth' (to moor, provide a place for a ship), origin uncertain but possibly from Old English 'beran' (to carry) or related nautical terms.
