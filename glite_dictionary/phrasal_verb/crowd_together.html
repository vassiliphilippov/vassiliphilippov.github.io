to crowd together

1. VERB

/ kraʊd təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / kraʊd təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to gather closely in a group, especially in large numbers
gather closely in large numbers

Examples

Fans began to crowd together outside the stadium hours before the concert started.
The children crowded together under the tree to stay out of the rain.
People crowd together in the city square every year for the New Year's festivities.

Synset Examples

men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to congregate - to come together in a group, assemble
  • Antonym: to disperse - to break up and move in different directions
