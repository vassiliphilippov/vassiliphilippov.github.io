to let it be

1. VERB

/ ˌlɛt ɪt ˈbi / (AmE), / ˌlɛt ɪt ˈbiː / (BrE)
to accept a situation as it is without attempting to change or interfere with it
accept without interfering

Examples

Sometimes it's best to just let it be rather than cause more trouble.
If you can't fix the problem right now, you might as well let it be.
She wanted to argue, but chose to let it be and move on.

Synset Examples

When things get tough, I just let it be.
She decided to let it be and stop worrying.
He learned to let it be when facing challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to leave alone - to not disturb or interfere with someone or something
  • Antonym: to interfere - to involve oneself in a situation where one is not wanted or needed
