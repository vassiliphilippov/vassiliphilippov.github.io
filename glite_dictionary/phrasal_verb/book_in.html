to book in

1. VERB

/ bʊk ɪn / (AmE), / bʊk ɪn / (BrE)
to register or arrange in advance to be present at an event, stay, or accommodation
register or arrange in advance (event, stay)

Examples

We decided to book in at a small guesthouse for the night.
You should book in as soon as registration opens to avoid disappointment.
All participants must book in online before attending the workshop.

Synset Examples

They decided to book in for the weekend retreat.
Please book in early to secure your spot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to check in - to register upon arrival, especially at a hotel or airport
  • Synonym: book - to arrange for or reserve in advance
