to cull out

1. VERB

/ kʌl aʊt / (AmE), / kʌl aʊt / (BrE)
to select and remove the best or most suitable items from a larger group or list
select and remove the best items from a group

Examples

The editor had to cull out the most relevant articles for the report.
They culled out weak seedlings to focus resources on the strongest plants.
After reviewing the applications, she culled out the top candidates for interviews.

Synset Examples

cull out the interesting letters from the poet's correspondence
winnow the finalists from the long list of applicants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to choose from a group according to preference or criteria
  • Synonym: to choose - to pick out from a number of alternatives

Etymology

From "cull," meaning 'to select,' ultimately from Latin *colligere* (to gather together), combined with "out" indicating separation.
