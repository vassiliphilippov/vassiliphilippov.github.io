to trim back

1. VERB

/ trɪm bæk / (AmE), / trɪm bæk / (BrE)
to reduce the size, amount, or extent of something by cutting or removing parts
reduce the size or amount by cutting

Examples

We need to trim back our spending this month to save money.
Gardeners often trim back shrubs in the spring to encourage new growth.
Due to budget cuts, the company had to trim back several projects.

Synset Examples

reduce your daily fat intake
The employer wants to cut back health benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut back - to reduce something, especially expenses or consumption
  • Synonym: to reduce - make smaller in amount, degree, or size
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size or amount
