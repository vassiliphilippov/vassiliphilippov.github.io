to peg away

1. VERB

/ pɛg əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pɛg əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to work steadily and persistently, especially at something difficult or tedious
to work persistently at something

Examples

Even when the work seemed endless, she continued to peg away until it was finished.
Writers must peg away every day if they want to complete a novel.
The team pegged away at the project despite repeated setbacks.

Synset Examples

She keeps plugging away at her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plug away - to keep working hard at something
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop making an effort; to quit

Etymology

From 'peg', meaning to move or proceed energetically (origin in 19th-century slang), combined with 'away' indicating continuation.
