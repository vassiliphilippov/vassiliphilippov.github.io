to thimblerig your way out

1. VERB

/ ˈθɪmbəlˌrɪɡ jʊr weɪ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈθɪmbəlˌrɪɡ jɔː weɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to escape a difficult or compromising situation by deceiving or tricking others
deceive to escape a situation

Examples

You can't thimblerig your way out of this situation.
He tried to thimblerig his way out of paying the fine, but it didn't work.
Instead of admitting the mistake, she attempted to thimblerig her way out with clever excuses.

Synset Examples

You can't thimblerig your way out of this situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat your way out - to escape by cheating or deception
  • Antonym: to face the music - to accept the consequences of your actions

Etymology

From 'thimblerig', a 19th-century word for a cheating game with cups or thimbles; extended to mean trickery.
