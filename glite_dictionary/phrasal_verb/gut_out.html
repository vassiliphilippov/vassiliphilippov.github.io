to gut out

1. VERB

/ ɡʌt aʊt / (AmE), / ɡʌt aʊt / (BrE)
to endure or persevere through a difficult or unpleasant situation, especially by using determination or courage
endure a difficult situation with determination
informal

Examples

Despite his injury, he managed to gut out the entire match and finish strong.
Sometimes you have to gut out the hardest moments in life to succeed.
She knew she just needed to gut out the final exams to graduate.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you just need to gut out the hard times.
He had to gut out the last few miles of the race.
She decided to gut out the tough conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough out - to endure something difficult without giving up
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying; to quit

Etymology

From the noun 'gut' (courage, resolve) + 'out'; American English colloquial from mid-20th century.
