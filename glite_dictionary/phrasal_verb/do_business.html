to do business

1. VERB

/ du ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / duː ˈbɪznəs / (BrE)
(informal, euphemistic) To urinate or defecate, especially used when referring to animals or children.
urinate or defecate (euphemism)
informal

Examples

Our puppy needs to go outside to do business before bedtime.
She took her son to the restroom so he could do business.
We often say 'go do business' when we walk our dogs in the park.

Synset Examples

The dog went outside to do its business.
I stepped outside to do my business quickly.
