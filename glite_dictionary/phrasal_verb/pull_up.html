to pull up

1. VERB

/ pʊl ʌp / (AmE), / pʊl ʌp / (BrE)
to straighten oneself to a more upright or formal posture, often to appear more dignified or confident
straighten oneself upright or formally

Examples

When the principal entered, she pulled up and greeted him with a firm nod.
He pulled up when he realized everyone was watching him speak.
The officer pulled up to his full height during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

He drew himself up when he talked to his superior

2. VERB

/ pʊl ʌp / (AmE), / pʊl ʌp / (BrE)
to remove something, often with effort or force; can also be used to mean removing or extracting something in an abstract sense
remove with effort or force

Examples

She had to pull up all the weeds from the garden before planting new flowers.
The dentist decided to pull up the damaged tooth to stop the pain.
Investigators tried to pull up hidden details from the encrypted files.

Synset Examples

take out a splinter
extract information from the telegram

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - to remove something, especially by effort
  • Synonym: remove - to take something away or off

3. VERB

/ pʊl ʌp / (AmE), / pʊl ʌp / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or its driver) to stop or come to a halt after driving somewhere.
stop a vehicle after driving

Examples

She watched as the taxi pulled up outside her house.
The bus pulled up at the next stop to let passengers off.
He pulled up in front of the café and waved at us.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to bring a vehicle to a halt
