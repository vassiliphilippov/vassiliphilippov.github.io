to shake up

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to disturb, unsettle, or profoundly affect someone or something emotionally or socially
disturb or unsettle emotionally or socially

Examples

The tragic news really shook up the whole community.
Her sudden resignation shook up the entire company.
The documentary shook up many viewers and made them rethink their opinions.

Synset Examples

These stories shook the community
the civil war shook the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disturb - to interrupt the normal state or peace of something
  • Antonym: calm - to make (someone or something) peaceful or tranquil again

2. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to shock or disturb someone or something physically, often causing a temporary upset or discomfort
shock or disturb physically

Examples

After the car accident, he was visibly shaken up but not seriously hurt.
The turbulence shook up passengers throughout the flight.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ʌp / (BrE)

make something fluffier by shaking

Examples

She shook up the comforter to give it more volume and softness.
Could you shake up the flour bag to loosen it before measuring?

/ ʃeɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ʌp / (BrE)
