to act out

1. VERB

/ æktaʊt / (AmE), / ækt aʊt / (BrE)
to perform or represent something, such as a situation or event, as if in a play or as part of a role-play exercise
perform or represent something as if in a play

Examples

The teacher asked the students to act out a scene from the story.
He likes to act out funny situations to entertain his friends.
During therapy, children are encouraged to act out their feelings through role-play.

Synset Examples

She reenacted what had happened earlier that day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reenact - to perform again as in a reenactment or role-play
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01726324-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ækt aʊt / (AmE), / ækt aʊt / (BrE)
to express or represent an incident, emotion, or idea through physical action, especially as part of a performance or role-play.
express through physical action (esp. on stage)

Examples

In drama class, the students were asked to act out scenes from their favorite movies.
Therapists sometimes have children act out their feelings using puppets or toys.
She was able to act out the character’s sorrow with remarkable realism.

Synset Examples

She could act neurotic anxiety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enact - to perform or represent something, often in a theatrical setting

3. VERB

/ ækˈt aʊt / (AmE), / ækt ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to express emotions, especially negative ones, through behavior that is often inappropriate or disruptive, rather than talking about them
express emotions through inappropriate behavior

Examples

When children feel neglected, they may act out to gain attention from adults.
He tends to act out his anger instead of talking about it.
During times of stress, some people act out in unexpected ways.

Synset Examples

She tends to act out when she's frustrated.
Children often act out to express their feelings.
He had to act out the scene during the audition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misbehave - to behave badly or improperly
  • Antonym: to internalize - to keep feelings or emotions inside rather than expressing them
