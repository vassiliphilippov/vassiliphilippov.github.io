to throw off balance

1. VERB

/ ˈθroʊ ɔf ˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ ɒf ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel confused or unsettled, especially by a sudden change or unexpected event
to confuse or unsettle someone

Examples

The unexpected question threw off balance the entire panel during the interview.
She was thrown off balance by the abrupt change in his mood.
A sudden power outage can easily throw off balance even the most prepared event planner.

Synset Examples

The news of her departure threw everyone off balance.
The surprise announcement threw him off balance.
He was thrown off balance by her sudden change in attitude.
Her sudden departure has thrown everyone off balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unsettle - to make someone feel worried or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less worried or more confident
