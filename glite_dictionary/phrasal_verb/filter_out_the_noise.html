to filter out the noise

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪltɚ aʊt ðə nɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə aʊt ðə nɔɪz / (BrE)
to remove distractions or irrelevant information in order to focus on what is important
remove distractions or irrelevant information

Examples

Before starting her study session, Maria tried to filter out the noise by closing her windows.
This new algorithm helps filter out the noise from large data sets, allowing for clearer results.
He put on headphones with white noise to filter out the noise of the busy cafe.

Synset Examples

You need to filter out the noise to focus better.
