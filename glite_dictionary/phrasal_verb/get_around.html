to get around

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of news, information, etc.) to become widely known or spread among people.
(of news) become widely known

Examples

The rumor about his resignation quickly got around the office.
It didn't take long for the news of their engagement to get around.
Word got around that the restaurant was under new management.

Synset Examples

News of her death broke in the morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to leak out - (of information) to become known to the public
  • Synonym: break - (of news) to become known or published for the first time

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with something difficult, unpleasant, or required, often by finding an indirect or clever way
avoid something difficult or unpleasant

Examples

He always finds a way to get around doing his chores.
They tried to get around the new regulations with a loophole.
You can't get around the fact that honesty is expected.

Synset Examples

You cannot bypass these rules!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to or deal with a problem or situation directly
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(informal) To socialize widely or go to a lot of different places; to be active in many social situations.
socialize widely; be very sociable
informal

Examples

Jake really likes to get around and meet new people at every opportunity.
She used to get around a lot more before she started working full-time.
If you want to know what's happening in town, just get around and talk to different groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mingle - to move among and talk to people at a social event
  • Antonym: stay in - to remain at home and not go out socially
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move from place to place, especially within a particular area or in general; to travel or circulate.
move or travel from place to place

Examples

After breaking her leg, she found it difficult to get around the house.
Many people use bicycles to get around in the city.
Despite his age, he still manages to get around quite easily.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel - to go from one place to another, typically over a distance
  • Antonym: to stay put - to remain in one place and not move around
