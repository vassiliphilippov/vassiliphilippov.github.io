to can't stand

1. VERB

/ kænt stænd / (AmE), / kɑːnt stænd / (BrE)
to dislike something or someone very much; to not be able to tolerate
to dislike intensely; be unable to tolerate
informal

Examples

I can't stand loud music when I'm trying to concentrate.
She can't stand people who are always late to meetings.
He said he couldn't stand the smell of cigarette smoke.

Synset Examples

She can't stand rude behavior in public.

I can't stand that nugger; he's so arrogant.
I can't stand that asshole at work.
I can't stand that fucktwit in our group.
I can't stand that pricklouse; he never stops whining.
He couldn't stand the swill they served at the bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hate - to intensely dislike
  • Antonym: love - to have a deep affection for
  • Synonym: dislike - to not like or approve of
