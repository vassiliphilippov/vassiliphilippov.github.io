to clock up

1. VERB

/ klɑk ʌp / (AmE), / klɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to reach or accumulate a particular total or amount, especially in terms of distance, speed, or time, as a result of repeated action or activity
accumulate a total amount; record (distance/time)

Examples

The pilot managed to clock up over 10,000 flying hours during his career.
Our old car has clocked up 150,000 miles so far.
She hopes to clock up enough experience to apply for a promotion next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - collect or gather over time

2. VERB

/ klɑk ʌp / (AmE), / klɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to reach or accumulate a particular amount or total, especially of distance, time, or experience
to accumulate a total (distance, time, etc.)
british

Examples

She hopes to clock up 1,000 hours of volunteering by next year.
The company has clocked up impressive profits this quarter.
After years of training, he finally clocked up enough experience to become a coach.

Synset Examples

We need to clock up more experience to improve our skills.
They managed to clock up over 100 miles on the road trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect over time
  • Synonym: to pile up - to accumulate in a large quantity

Etymology

From the noun 'clock' (measuring device) and 'up'; usage attested from mid-20th century, likely from recording time or mileage on a clock or meter.

3. VERB

/ klɑk ʌp / (AmE), / klɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or record (especially time, distance, or an amount) in the course of an activity
accumulate or record a total (e.g., hours)

Examples

She clocked up over a hundred hours volunteering at the animal shelter.
The new trainee has already clocked up three months of experience.
He clocked up a record number of miles on his bicycle last year.

Synset Examples

She clocked up several hours of overtime last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - gather or acquire an increasing number or quantity of something
  • Synonym: to rack up - to accumulate or achieve a number, score, amount, etc.

Etymology

From the use of 'clock' as a verb meaning to record time, originating in reference to time clocks used to record workers' hours.
