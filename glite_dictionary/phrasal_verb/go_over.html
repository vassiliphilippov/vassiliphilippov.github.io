/ ɡoʊ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a commander) to review or inspect troops formally, often as a ceremonial or evaluative act.

Examples

Every year, the president would go over the assembled forces to boost morale.
The commanding officer went over the soldiers, inspecting their uniforms and weapons.
