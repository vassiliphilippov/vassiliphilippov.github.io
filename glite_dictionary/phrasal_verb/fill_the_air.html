to fill the air

1. VERB

/ fɪl ði ˈɛr / (AmE), / fɪl ði ˈeə / (BrE)
to spread through and become noticeable in the surrounding atmosphere, often creating a strong or pervasive presence
to permeate the atmosphere; to be very noticeable

Examples

The scent of blooming flowers filled the air as we walked through the garden.
Laughter filled the air at the family's holiday reunion.
Tension filled the air as the final exam results were announced.

Synset Examples

The sweet smell of incense filled the air.
The barbecue's smoke filled the air with delicious aromas.
The concert's music filled the air with excitement.
The smell of manure filled the air near the farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to permeate - to spread throughout something; to penetrate
