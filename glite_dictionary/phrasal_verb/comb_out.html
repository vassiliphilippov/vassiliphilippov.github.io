to comb out

1. VERB

/ koʊm aʊt / (AmE), / kəʊm aʊt / (BrE)
to remove unwanted elements or parts from something, especially after careful examination
remove unwanted elements or parts

Examples

The editors carefully combed out errors from the manuscript before publication.
The team spent hours combing out unnecessary regulations from the policy.
During the audit, the agency combed out outdated procedures to improve efficiency.

Synset Examples

The company weeded out the incompetent people
The new law weeds out the old inequities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weed out - to remove people or things that are not wanted or are less good than the rest
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02228412-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ koʊm aʊt / (AmE), / kəʊm aʊt / (BrE)
to smooth, untangle, or neaten (hair, fibers, etc.) using a comb or a similar tool
make neat or untangle using a comb

Examples

She took her time to comb out every tangle in her daughter's hair.
You should comb out the wool before spinning it into yarn.
He carefully combed out the knots to make his hair look neat for the interview.

Synset Examples

comb your hair before dinner
comb the wool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - make smooth, neat, or tidy using a brush
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00038078-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ koʊm aʊt / (AmE), / kəʊm aʊt / (BrE)
to use a comb to remove tangles or knots from hair
remove tangles from hair with a comb

Examples

She carefully tried to comb out the knots after her shower.
It can take a long time to comb out curly hair without breaking it.
Before the haircut, the stylist will comb out any tangles.

Synset Examples

I need to comb out the knots in my hair.
Make sure to comb out any tangles before styling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to untangle - to remove tangles or knots from something
  • Synonym: brush out - to remove tangles from hair using a brush
