to specialize in

1. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz ɪn / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz ɪn / (BrE)
to concentrate on and become an expert in a particular subject, field, or activity
focus expertise on a subject or field

Examples

After completing medical school, she decided to specialize in pediatric medicine.
Many law firms specialize in intellectual property cases to serve specific client needs.
He is specializing in renewable energy engineering at his university.

Synset Examples

She plans to specialize in environmental law.
She decided to specialize in neurology during medical school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to focus on - to direct attention or effort toward something
  • Antonym: to generalize - to make general rather than specific or detailed

Etymology

From Latin specialis (particular, belonging to a species); the phrasal form arises from use of 'specialize' with the preposition 'in.'
