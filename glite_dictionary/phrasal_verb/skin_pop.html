to skin pop

1. VERB

/ skɪn pɑp / (AmE), / skɪn pɒp / (BrE)
to inject drugs just beneath the skin rather than into a vein or muscle
inject drugs under the skin
informal

Examples

Some users prefer to skin pop rather than inject the drug intravenously.
He admitted to skin popping heroin regularly as part of his routine.
Because of repeated skin popping, she developed abscesses on her arms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mainline - to inject drugs directly into a vein
  • Synonym: to inject - to force a substance into something, especially the body, using a syringe
