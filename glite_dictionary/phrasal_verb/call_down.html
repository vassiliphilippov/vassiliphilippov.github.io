to call down

1. VERB

/ kɔl daʊn / (AmE), / kɔːl daʊn / (BrE)
to reprimand or criticize someone severely or angrily
reprimand or scold severely

Examples

The teacher called down the students for their disruptive behavior.
She was called down by her boss after missing the deadline.
He calls down anyone who doesn’t follow the safety rules.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to angrily reprimand someone, especially a child
  • Synonym: to dress down - to reprimand or scold someone severely

2. VERB

/ kɔl daʊn / (AmE), / kɔːl daʊn / (BrE)
to summon into action or bring into existence, often by invoking supernatural or magical powers
summon or invoke, often supernaturally

Examples

The villagers believed the shaman could call down rain during a drought.
During the ceremony, they tried to call down the spirits of their ancestors.
He claimed he could call down a curse upon anyone who wronged him.

Synset Examples

raise the specter of unemployment
he conjured wild birds in the air
call down the spirits from the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invoke - to call on (a deity or spirit) in prayer as a witness or for inspiration
  • Synonym: to conjure - to call upon or command (a spirit or devil) by invocation or spell
