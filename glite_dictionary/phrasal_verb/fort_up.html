to fort up

1. VERB

/ fɔrt ʌp / (AmE), / fɔːt ʌp / (BrE)
to gather together for protection or defense, as if inside a fort
gather for protection or defense

Examples

The family decided to fort up in their cabin during the snowstorm.
During the invasion, the villagers forted up in the old stone church.
We could fort up here until help arrives if the situation gets worse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barricade - to block or defend a place by building a barrier
