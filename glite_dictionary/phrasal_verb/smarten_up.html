to smarten up

1. VERB

/ ˈsmɑːrtən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsmɑːtən ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone or something look neat, tidy, or more stylish; to improve in appearance or dress
make neat or more stylish

Examples

Before the guests arrived, she decided to smarten up the living room.
You should smarten up before your job interview tomorrow.
The teacher asked the students to smarten up their appearance for the assembly.

Synset Examples

Spruce up your house for Spring
titivate the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make something tidier or more attractive
  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make something clean and orderly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00294739-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsmɑɹtən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsmɑːtn ʌp / (BrE)
to make oneself look neat and tidy, especially by dressing and grooming carefully for a special occasion
dress and groom neatly

Examples

Please smarten up before the job interview to make a good impression.
She always smartens up when there is a special event at work.
He smartened up for the dinner party, wearing a crisp shirt and polished shoes.

Synset Examples

He spruced up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make oneself or something look neat and smart
