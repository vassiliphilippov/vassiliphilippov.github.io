to single out

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋɡl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl aʊt / (BrE)
to choose or pick someone or something from a group for special attention, often in a distinctive or noticeable way
select for special attention

Examples

The head coach decided to single out Maria for her leadership on the team.
Reporters often single out experts to interview during major events.
Don't single out one person for blame when many were at fault.

Synset Examples

She was singled out for her outstanding performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to choose from among a group
  • Synonym: to pick out - to choose someone or something from a group
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00681158-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl aʊt / (BrE)
to choose or treat one person or thing from a group differently, especially to give special attention or discrimination.
choose/treat one differently from a group

Examples

The coach tends to single out the most talented players for extra training.
She felt singled out for criticism because of her background.
The teacher singled out one student for special praise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to separate or set apart from others
  • Synonym: discriminate - to treat a person or group differently, especially unfairly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02517971-v
Synset POS: v
