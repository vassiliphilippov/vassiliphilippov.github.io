to come close

1. VERB

/ kʌm kloʊs / (AmE), / kʌm kləʊs / (BrE)
to nearly do or experience something; to be almost successful at something
nearly do something

Examples

She came close to quitting her job after the difficult week.
The team came close to winning the championship, but lost in the final seconds.
I come close to forgetting your birthday every year, but I always remember just in time.

Synset Examples

She came close to quitting her job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly, but not quite
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to achieve or reach something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02379065-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm kloʊs / (AmE), / kʌm kləʊs / (BrE)
to be nearly equal to or almost achieve something; to be similar or very near in amount or degree
be nearly equal or similar

Examples

The team's performance came close to breaking the national record.
No other proposal comes close to matching this one in quality.
Her painting comes close to capturing the beauty of the original scene.

Synset Examples

Her results approximate my own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approximate - to be similar to but not exactly the same as something
