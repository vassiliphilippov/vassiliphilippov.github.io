to build in

1. VERB

/ bɪld ɪn / (AmE), / bɪld ɪn / (BrE)
to make something an integral or permanent part of something else
make an integral part of something

Examples

The software developers built in new security features to protect users' data.
We need to build in enough time for questions at the end of the presentation.
The architect built in extra storage space under the stairs.

Synset Examples

we can build in a special clause to make the contract better for you

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorporate - to include something as part of a larger whole
  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave something out or not include it
