to make ground

1. VERB

/ meɪk ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / meɪk ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to gain progress, advantage, or move forward in achieving something
gain progress or advantage

Examples

The company hopes to make ground on its competitors by launching a new product.
If we work together, we can make ground towards our goal.
Despite the early setback, the team managed to make ground before halftime.

Synset Examples

It's time to make ground in this negotiation.
We're hoping to make ground on our project this week.
They need to make ground before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lose ground - to fall behind or lose progress or advantage
