to flag down

1. VERB

/ flæɡ daʊn / (AmE), / flæɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to signal to a driver or vehicle to stop, typically by waving or gesturing
signal to stop a vehicle

Examples

He tried to flag down a taxi but they were all full.
The cyclist flagged down a passing motorist for help.
We flagged down the bus just as it was pulling away from the stop.

Synset Examples

Let's flag down a cab — it's starting to rain
The policeman flagged down our car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave down - to signal to stop by waving

Etymology

From 'flag' meaning to signal or direct, combined with 'down' indicating the action of causing something to stop.

2. VERB

/ flæɡ daʊn / (AmE), / flæɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to signal a vehicle or person to stop, especially by waving your hand or an object
signal to stop (vehicle/person)

Examples

We had to flag down a taxi when it started to rain heavily.
The cyclist managed to flag down a passing motorist for help.
She stood by the roadside trying to flag down a bus.

Synset Examples

She waved her arm to flag down the bus.
We need to flag down help if the car breaks down.
I had to flag down a taxi in the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hail - to signal to a vehicle, especially a taxi, to attract the driver's attention
