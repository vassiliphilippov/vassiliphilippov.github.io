to plonk down

1. VERB

/ plɑŋk daʊn / (AmE), / plɒŋk daʊn / (BrE)
to put or drop something or oneself heavily and carelessly onto a surface
drop or place heavily
informal

Examples

She plonked down her bag on the table and sighed in relief.
After a long walk, he plonked down onto the sofa and closed his eyes.
The child plonked down his toys in the middle of the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plunk down - to put or drop something or oneself heavily

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound made when something is dropped heavily.

2. VERB

/ plɑŋk daʊn / (AmE), / plɒŋk daʊn / (BrE)
to sit down heavily or abruptly, often with little care or grace
sit down heavily or abruptly
informal

Examples

After a long run, he plonked down on the nearest bench to catch his breath.
As soon as I got home, I plonked down in front of the television with a snack.
She plonked down beside her friend, sighing loudly from exhaustion.

Synset Examples

She decided to plonk down on the couch after work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flop down - to sit or lie down in a relaxed or heavy way

Etymology

Imitative; probably from the sound 'plonk' representing something falling or being put down heavily.
