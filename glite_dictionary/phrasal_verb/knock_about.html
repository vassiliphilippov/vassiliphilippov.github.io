to knock about

1. VERB

/ nɑk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / nɒk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
(informal) To continue to be alive, present, or active, especially in an unremarkable way.
remain alive or active
informal

Examples

My grandfather is still knocking about at ninety.
There are a few old cars knocking about in the garage.
That rumor has been knocking about for years.

Synset Examples

Does the old man still kick around?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick around - to be alive or existent, often used informally about someone continuing to live or exist
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ nɑk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / nɒk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to strike or hit something repeatedly and forcefully, often causing it to move or become damaged
strike or hit repeatedly and forcefully

Examples

The old boat was knocked about by the storm all night long.
After years of use, the suitcase had clearly been knocked about during travel.
The children knocked about the ball in the backyard until sunset.

Synset Examples

Winds buffeted the tent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffet - to strike repeatedly and forcefully, especially of wind or waves
  • Synonym: pound - to hit repeatedly with great force
