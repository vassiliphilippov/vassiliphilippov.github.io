to add on

1. VERB

/ æd ɑn / (AmE), / æd ɒn / (BrE)
to include or attach something extra; to make an addition to something
make an addition or attachment

Examples

They decided to add on a new room to their house.
If you want dessert, you can add on a slice of cake for $2.
When the project grew, we had to add on more features.

Synset Examples

Let's add on to this

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to append - attach as an addition or supplement
  • Antonym: remove - take something away or off
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00183902-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ æd ɑn / (AmE), / æd ɒn / (BrE)
to attach or include something extra, especially at the end of something else
to add as an extra part, especially at the end

Examples

We decided to add on an extra bedroom to the house.
Could you add on any information you have to this report?
The company plans to add on a new feature in the next update.

Synset Examples

He appended a glossary to his novel where he used an invented language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to append - to add something to the end of a written document or text
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331387-v
Synset POS: v
