to chance on

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃæns ɑn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːns ɒn / (BrE)
to find or meet something or someone unexpectedly, usually by luck
find or encounter unexpectedly

Examples

While cleaning the attic, I chanced on a box of old photographs.
We chanced on a small café tucked away in a quiet alley.
She chanced on an interesting article while browsing the internet.

Synset Examples

the archeologists chanced upon an old tomb
she struck a goldmine
The hikers finally struck the main path to the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come across - find or discover by chance
  • Synonym: stumble upon - find or encounter unexpectedly
  • Antonym: seek - try to find something, often intentionally (opposite of accidental discovery)
