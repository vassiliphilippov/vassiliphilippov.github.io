to glam up

1. VERB

/ ɡlæm ʌp / (AmE), / ɡlæm ʌp / (BrE)
to make oneself or someone else look more glamorous, especially by wearing fashionable clothes or applying makeup
make oneself look glamorous
informal

Examples

She decided to glam up before heading out to the party.
You don't need to glam up for a casual dinner at my place.
He loves to glam up for special occasions, complete with flashy accessories.

Synset Examples

She dolled herself up for the night out with her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to doll up - to dress or make oneself look smart or attractive
  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes

Etymology

From 'glamorous', from English 'glamour' (enchantment, magic) + 'up', phrasal verb particle.
