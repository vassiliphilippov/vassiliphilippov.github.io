to tee off

1. VERB

/ ti ˈɔf / (AmE), / ti ˈɒf / (BrE)
to strike a golf ball from the teeing ground at the start of a hole
hit a golf ball from the tee

Examples

She was nervous as she prepared to tee off in the championship match.
We plan to tee off at sunrise to avoid the crowds.
After teeing off on the first hole, he felt ready for the rest of the round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - to hit a golf ball with a full swing, typically as the first stroke from the tee
