to kill the mood

1. VERB

/ kɪl ðə muːd / (AmE), / kɪl ðə muːd / (BrE)
to suddenly disrupt or end a pleasant atmosphere, often by saying or doing something inappropriate, awkward, or negative
disrupt a pleasant atmosphere

Examples

I'm sorry if my joke killed the mood at the party last night.
Bringing up politics really kills the mood during family dinners.
Her harsh criticism killed the mood in the room instantly.

Synset Examples

He didn't mean to kill the mood with his comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil the mood - to make an atmosphere less pleasant or enjoyable
