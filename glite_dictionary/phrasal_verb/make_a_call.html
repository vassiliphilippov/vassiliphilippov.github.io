to make a call

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə kɔl / (AmE), / meɪk ə kɔːl / (BrE)
to telephone someone; to contact someone by telephone
telephone someone

Examples

I'll make a call to the doctor to schedule an appointment.
She stepped outside to make a call because the room was too noisy.
If you have any questions, don't hesitate to make a call to our office.

Synset Examples

Can you make a call to confirm the reservation?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to contact someone by telephone
  • Synonym: phone - to make a telephone call to someone

2. VERB

/ meɪk ə kɔl / (AmE), / meɪk ə kɔːl / (BrE)
to contact someone by using a telephone
phone someone

Examples

She stepped outside to make a call in the quiet hallway.
Before dinner, please make a call to your grandmother.
He made a call to confirm the reservation at the restaurant.

Synset Examples

He made a call from the vintage phone box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to speak or try to contact someone by telephone
