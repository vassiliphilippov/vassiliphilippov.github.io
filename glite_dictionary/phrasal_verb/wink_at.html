to wink at

1. VERB

/ wɪŋk æt / (AmE), / wɪŋk æt / (BrE)
to pretend not to notice something, especially something bad, in order to give silent approval or encouragement
pretend not to notice; silently approve

Examples

The authorities often wink at small acts of corruption in the region.
She chose to wink at her friend's bad behavior rather than confront her.
Many companies wink at minor breaches of protocol to keep things running smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn a blind eye to - to deliberately ignore something wrong or undesirable
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02422489-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɪŋk æt / (AmE), / wɪŋk æt / (BrE)
to signal discreetly to someone or show playful interest by briefly closing one eye
signal or flirt with a wink

Examples

During the conversation, he would often wink at her to make her laugh.
She caught her brother wink at their friend from across the table.
Sometimes, a simple wink at someone can say more than words.

Synset Examples

I saw him wink at me during the presentation.
She tried to wink at him across the crowded room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wink - close one eye briefly as a signal
