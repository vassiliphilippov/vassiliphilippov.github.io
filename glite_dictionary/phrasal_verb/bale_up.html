to bale up

1. VERB

/ beɪl ʌp / (AmE), / beɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to pack into bales by compressing and bundling tightly, especially for storage or transport
pack into bales tightly

Examples

Every autumn, the workers bale up tons of hay for storage in the barn.
We should bale up the leftover cardboard to make recycling easier.
After the harvest, the team will bale up the straw and stack it in the shed.

Synset Examples

We need to bale up these leaves for the compost.
It's time to bale up the hay before the rain comes.
Farmers often bale up straw for winter feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bale - to make something into a bale
