to live off

1. VERB

/ lɪv ɑf / (AmE), / lɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to depend on someone or something for the money or food needed to live, especially over a period of time
depend on for income or support

Examples

Many students struggle to live off their part-time job incomes during college.
After retirement, she plans to live off her investments.
Some wildlife can live off only what they find in the forest.

Synset Examples

Many artists live off their creative works.
He managed to live off his savings for years.
She lives off her parents while studying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depend on - to rely on; require as a necessity
  • Synonym: to live on - to have a particular amount of money to support yourself
