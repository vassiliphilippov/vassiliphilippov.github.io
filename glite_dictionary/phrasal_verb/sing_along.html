to sing along

1. VERB

/ sɪŋ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / sɪŋ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to sing together with a group, such as a choir or orchestra, or to join in singing with others
sing together with others

Examples

The children were encouraged to sing along with the music during the show.
At the concert, the whole crowd started to sing along to the chorus.
She always likes to sing along when her favorite song comes on the radio.

Synset Examples

Every year the local orchestra and choir perform the ‘Messiah’ and the audience is invited to sing along

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to join in - to participate in an activity with others

2. VERB

/ sɪŋ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / sɪŋ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to sing together with music or a performer, especially when the music or song is playing.
to sing with music or a performer

Examples

Whenever my favorite tune comes on, I can't help but sing along.
At the concert, the crowd started to sing along during the chorus.
She was embarrassed at first, but soon she sang along with everyone else.

Synset Examples

It felt natural to sing along with the song.
I love to sing along with my favorite songs.
They encouraged the audience to sing along.
Everyone began to sing along with the band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to join in - to participate with others in an activity
