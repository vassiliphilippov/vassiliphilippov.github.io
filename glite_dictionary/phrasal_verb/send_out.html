to send out

1. VERB

/ sɛnd aʊt / (AmE), / sɛnd aʊt / (BrE)
to cause something to be delivered, transmitted, or dispatched to a different place or person, often formally or in quantity
to dispatch or transmit something to another place/person

Examples

Please send out the invitations by Friday so everyone receives them on time.
The company regularly sends out newsletters to keep its customers informed.
Firefighters were quickly sent out to control the blaze in the nearby forest.

Synset Examples

He had sent the dispatches downtown to the proper people and had slept

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispatch - to send someone or something somewhere quickly, especially for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: recall - to order something that has been sent out to return
