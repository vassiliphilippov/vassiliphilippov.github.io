to wave away

1. VERB

/ weɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / weɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to dismiss or reject something, such as a question, problem, or suggestion, as unimportant or not worth considering
dismiss or reject something as unimportant

Examples

Whenever I brought up our concerns, he would simply wave away the issue.
The manager waved away the issue, saying it was nothing to worry about.
She tried to wave away the issue, but the board insisted on a discussion.

Synset Examples

He waved away the issue, insisting it wasn't important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide something or someone is not worth considering or giving attention to
  • Antonym: to consider - to think about carefully, especially in decisions
