to plump for

1. VERB

/ plʌmp fɔr / (AmE), / plʌmp fɔː / (BrE)
to choose or support someone or something, often after considering various options
choose or support after consideration

Examples

After much debate, she finally plumped for the blue dress over the red one.
Many voters are expected to plump for the independent candidate in the upcoming election.
Despite his initial hesitation, he eventually plumped for the vegetarian option at dinner.

Synset Examples

He plumped for the Labor Party
I backed Kennedy in 1960

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back - to support or endorse someone or something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to be against or act in opposition to someone or something
