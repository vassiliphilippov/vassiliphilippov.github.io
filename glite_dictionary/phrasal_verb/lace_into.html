to lace into

1. VERB

/ leɪs ˈɪntuː / (AmE), / leɪs ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to attack or criticize someone or something forcefully or violently
attack or criticize forcefully

Examples

The coach really laced into the team after their poor performance.
He suddenly laced into his opponent with a series of punches.
The critic laced into the film, calling it a waste of time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay into - to attack someone physically or verbally
  • Synonym: to beat up - to attack and injure someone by hitting them repeatedly
