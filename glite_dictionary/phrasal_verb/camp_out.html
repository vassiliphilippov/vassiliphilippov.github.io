to camp out

1. VERB

/ kæmp aʊt / (AmE), / kæmp aʊt / (BrE)
to sleep or live outdoors for a short time, especially in a tent or under the open sky
sleep or live outdoors temporarily

Examples

We decided to camp out in the mountains for the weekend.
Many fans camped out overnight to buy tickets for the concert.
If you don't book a hotel, you'll have to camp out on the beach.

Synset Examples

Can we go camping again this summer?
The circus tented near the town
The houseguests had to camp in the living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - to stay in a tent or temporary shelter outdoors

2. VERB

/ kæmp aʊt / (AmE), / kæmp aʊt / (BrE)
to sleep outdoors, often in a tent or other temporary shelter, usually for enjoyment or as a temporary arrangement
sleep outdoors, often in a tent

Examples

Every summer, we camp out beside the lake for a weekend of fun.
When the electricity went out, the children wanted to camp out in the living room.
Some fans chose to camp out overnight to get tickets for the concert.
