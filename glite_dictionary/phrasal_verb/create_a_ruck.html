to create a ruck

1. VERB

/ kriˈeɪt ə rʌk / (AmE), / kriˈeɪt ə rʌk / (BrE)
to form a ruck during a rugby match, typically causing a contested, chaotic situation over the ball on the ground
form a ruck in rugby

Examples

The forwards worked together to create a ruck near the try line.
He managed to create a ruck and slow the attack's momentum.
Coaches train players to create a ruck effectively in pressure situations.

Synset Examples

They scrummed and created a ruck on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruck - a loose scrum in rugby formed around a tackled player
