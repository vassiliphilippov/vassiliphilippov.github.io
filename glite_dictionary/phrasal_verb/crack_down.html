to crack down

1. VERB

/ kræk daʊn / (AmE), / kræk daʊn / (BrE)
to take strong or severe measures to repress or suppress undesirable or illegal activity
take strong action to suppress something

Examples

The government has promised to crack down on tax evasion this year.
After several complaints, the school began to crack down on bullying.
Authorities will crack down on illegal parking in the downtown area starting next week.

Synset Examples

The police clamped down on illegal drugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clamp down - to take strict action in order to prevent or limit something
  • Antonym: to let up - to become less strict or severe

2. VERB

/ kræk daʊn / (AmE), / kræk daʊn / (BrE)
to take strong or severe action to deal with or stop illegal or unacceptable behavior
take strong action to stop something

Examples

The government plans to crack down on tax evasion by introducing stricter penalties.
Police have started to crack down on illegal street racing in the city.
The school will crack down on cheating during exams to ensure fairness.

Synset Examples

The university cracked down on essay mills this semester.
Authorities are cracking down on granny dumping cases.
The police are cracking down on cases of sextortion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clamp down - to take strict action to prevent something, similar in meaning
