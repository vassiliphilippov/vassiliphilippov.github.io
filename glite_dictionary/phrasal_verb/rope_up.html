to rope up

1. VERB

/ roʊp ʌp / (AmE), / rəʊp ʌp / (BrE)
(in climbing) to attach oneself to others in a group using ropes, for safety.
to tie climbers together for safety

Examples

Before they began the glacier crossing, the team decided to rope up for extra security.
It is standard practice for beginners to rope up in climbing classes.
They always rope up before attempting any difficult or risky section of the climb.

Synset Examples

The mountaineers roped up when they started the final ascent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tie in - to attach by tying, especially in climbing contexts
