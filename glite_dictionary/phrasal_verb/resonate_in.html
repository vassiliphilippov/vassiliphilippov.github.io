to resonate in

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛzəˌneɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪt ɪn / (BrE)
(of a sound, idea, or feeling) to continue to have a strong presence or lasting impact in a place or situation.
have a lasting impact or effect

Examples

The themes of the novel still resonate in modern discussions about society.
Her voice would resonate in the quiet room long after she finished singing.
His words resonated in my mind for days after the meeting.

Synset Examples

Her laughter always seemed to resonate in the hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - (of a sound, idea, or sentiment) to be repeated or continue to be heard; similar figurative sense

Etymology

From Latin resonare (to resound) plus the preposition 'in'.
