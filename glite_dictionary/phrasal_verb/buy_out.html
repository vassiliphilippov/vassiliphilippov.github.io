to buy out

1. VERB

/ baɪ aʊt / (AmE), / baɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to purchase a company, business, or shareholder's interest in order to gain control or full ownership
take over a business by purchasing it

Examples

The company was bought out by a multinational corporation last year.
He plans to buy out his partner and run the business alone.
After months of negotiations, they successfully bought out their main competitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acquire - to come to possess or own, especially a business
  • Antonym: to sell off - to sell all or part of a business or asset

2. VERB

/ baɪ aʊt / (AmE), / baɪ aʊt / (BrE)
To purchase someone's share or ownership, especially to gain total control of a business.
purchase all of someone's share or ownership

Examples

The company agreed to buy out its partner for $10 million.
She managed to buy out her siblings' interest in the family business.
After years of negotiations, they finally bought out their competitor.

Synset Examples

Investors can buy out small businesses for expansion.
He hopes to buy out the remaining shares soon.
The company plans to buy out its competitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acquire - to buy or obtain for oneself, especially a company
  • Antonym: to sell out - to sell all or one's share of ownership, often to another party
