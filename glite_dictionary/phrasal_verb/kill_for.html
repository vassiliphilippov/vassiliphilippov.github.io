to kill for

1. VERB

/ kɪl fɔr / (AmE), / kɪl fə / (BrE)
to want something very much; to desire something strongly, often used for emphasis
to desire something strongly
informal

Examples

After hiking all day, I would kill for a cold drink right now.
He said he would kill for a chance to travel the world.
Many students kill for an internship at a prestigious company.

Synset Examples

She felt like she could kill for a slice of cake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crave - to feel a powerful desire for something
  • Synonym: long for - to have a strong desire or wish for something
