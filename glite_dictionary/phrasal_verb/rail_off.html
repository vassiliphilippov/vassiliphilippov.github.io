to rail off

1. VERB

/ reɪl ɔf / (AmE), / reɪl ɒf / (BrE)
to separate or enclose an area or people by putting up a railing or barrier
separate with a railing or barrier

Examples

The museum staff decided to rail off the fragile exhibits to protect them from visitors.
For safety reasons, contractors railed off the construction zone from public access.
They railed off a section of the park for the upcoming event.

Synset Examples

rail off the crowds from the Presidential palace
