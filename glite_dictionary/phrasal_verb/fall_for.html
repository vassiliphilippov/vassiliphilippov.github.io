to fall for

1. VERB

/ fɔl fər / (AmE), / fɔːl fə / (BrE)
to be deceived by someone or something, especially by believing something that is not true
be deceived or duped by

Examples

I can't believe you fell for that old trick again.
Many people fall for online scams without realizing it.
She almost fell for his story, but then she noticed the inconsistencies.

Synset Examples

He fell for her charms
He fell for the con man's story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be taken in - to be deceived or tricked by someone
  • Antonym: to see through - to not be deceived by something or someone
