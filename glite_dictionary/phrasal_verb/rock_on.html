to rock on

1. VERB

/ rɑk ɑn / (AmE), / rɒk ɒn / (BrE)
to continue enjoying or participating in something with enthusiasm, especially in a lively or spirited manner
continue with enthusiasm or enjoyment
informal

Examples

Even after hours of dancing, the crowd continued to rock on late into the night.
No matter what happens, just rock on and have fun!
The DJ encouraged everyone to rock on with his energetic music selection.

Synset Examples

When the band started playing, everyone began to rock on.
She told him to rock on and enjoy his concert.
With a cheer, they shouted, 'Rock on!' during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep going - to continue; to persist

Etymology

Derived from 'rock' (the music genre or verb meaning 'to move rhythmically') and the adverb 'on', popularized in music culture in the late 20th century.
