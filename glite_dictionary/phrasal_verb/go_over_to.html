to go over to

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈoʊvɚ tu / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvə tuː / (BrE)
to change allegiance or support from one person, group, or viewpoint to another
change allegiance or support

Examples

Many politicians have chosen to go over to the new party leader.
During the debate, she decided to go over to the opposing side.
When he realized his team was losing, he quietly went over to their rivals.

Synset Examples

He decided to go over to the majority for a better chance of success.
It was time to go over to the majority and align with their goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defect - to abandon allegiance to one group in favor of another
  • Synonym: switch sides - to change allegiance or support from one side to another
