to spread out

1. VERB

/ sprɛd aʊt / (AmE), / sprɛd aʊt / (BrE)
to distribute or scatter something over an area so that it covers a surface more widely
distribute over an area

Examples

Please spread out the photos on the table so we can all see them.
She spread out the picnic blanket beneath the tree.
The documents were spread out across his desk for easy access.

Synset Examples

He spread fertilizer over the lawn
scatter cards across the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - to throw things loosely about in different directions
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

/ sprɛd aʊt / (AmE), / sprɛd aʊt / (BrE)
to move outward so as to cover a larger area or to become less concentrated
move outward or become more dispersed

Examples

The children began to spread out across the playground as soon as the bell rang.
Troops were ordered to spread out and cover more ground during their search.
Picnic blankets were soon spread out over the green lawn.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
