to get into a tizz

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɪntu ə tɪz / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɪntə ə tɪz / (BrE)
to become overly anxious, agitated, or flustered, especially about minor details
become anxious or flustered
informal british

Examples

Try not to get into a tizz before your big presentation tomorrow.
She tends to get into a tizz whenever things don't go exactly as planned.
There’s no need to get into a tizz about missing one deadline.

Synset Examples

Don't get into a tizz; everything will be fine.
He got into a tizz over the minor details.
She was in a tizz trying to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: panic - to experience sudden overwhelming fear
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become or make someone become calm or less agitated

Etymology

Likely from British English 'tizz', meaning a state of agitation, origin uncertain but attested from early 20th century slang.
