to pump up

1. VERB

/ pʌmp ʌp / (AmE), / pʌmp ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or enhance the energy, excitement, or enthusiasm of someone or something
increase energy or excitement

Examples

The DJ knows how to pump up the atmosphere and get everyone dancing.
Before the game, the coach tried to pump up the players with an inspiring speech.
Her infectious enthusiasm really pumped up the whole office.

Synset Examples

I always pump up my motivation with a good playlist.
We need to pump up the crowd before the concert starts.
The team needs to pump up their energy for the final match.
He likes to pump up the energy in the room with his jokes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to energize - to give energy or enthusiasm to someone or something
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become or make less excited or energetic
