to make a face

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə feɪs / (AmE), / meɪk ə feɪs / (BrE)
to contort the muscles of the face, usually in disapproval, annoyance, or another emotion, often to express discomfort or dislike
contort one's face to express emotion

Examples

The boy made a face when he tasted the sour lemon.
She makes a face every time she hears that annoying song on the radio.
He was making a face at his sister to make her laugh during dinner.

Synset Examples

He grimaced when he saw the amount of homework he had to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimace - to twist the face in an expression of pain, disgust, or wry amusement
