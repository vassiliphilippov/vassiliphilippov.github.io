to bleed out

1. VERB

/ bliːd aʊt / (AmE), / bliːd aʊt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to lose so much blood from an injury that there is a risk of death or serious harm, especially if untreated.
lose a large amount of blood

Examples

The medic rushed over before the victim could bleed out from the deep wound.
If they had arrived any later, she might have bled out on the sidewalk.
We need to apply pressure here so he doesn't bleed out before the ambulance gets here.

Synset Examples

The soldier managed not to bleed out after the injury.
He was afraid he would bleed out before help arrived.
They had to stop the wound from bleeding out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exsanguinate - to drain or lose blood; medical term
