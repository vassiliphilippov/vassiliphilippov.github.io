to clear out

1. VERB

/ klɪr aʊt / (AmE), / klɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to remove everything from a place so that it is empty
empty completely

Examples

We need to clear out the garage before we move.
She cleared out her old files to make space on her computer.
The landlord asked the tenants to clear out by the end of the month.

Synset Examples

We cleaned out all the drawers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clean out - to remove all contents from a place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00449819-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klɪr aʊt / (AmE), / klɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place and remove all personal belongings or contents, often permanently
leave a place, taking everything with you

Examples

After the tenants cleared out, the apartment was left completely empty.
We're planning to clear out by the end of the week and hand over the keys.
She cleared out her office when she decided to quit her job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to move out - to leave one's place of residence and take possessions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01860336-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ klɪr aʊt / (AmE), / klɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to make the chest or lungs free of mucus or other unwanted substances, usually by expectoration
remove mucus from chest or lungs

Examples

The new medicine helped to clear out my lungs after I got the flu.
Doctors sometimes prescribe inhalants to clear out the chest of patients with respiratory illnesses.
The coughing fit finally managed to clear out all the mucus from his chest.

Synset Examples

This drug expectorates quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expectorate - to cough up and eject mucus from the throat or lungs
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00577305-v
Synset POS: v
