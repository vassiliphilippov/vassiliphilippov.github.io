to look for

1. VERB

/ lʊk fɔr / (AmE), / lʊk fə / (BrE)
to anticipate or be excited or anxious about something that is expected to happen
anticipate with excitement or anxiety

Examples

I always look for the holidays with great excitement.
Children look for their birthdays months in advance.
We look for your visit next weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something with pleasure
  • Antonym: dread - to anticipate with fear or anxiety

2. VERB

/ lʊk fɔr / (AmE), / lʊk fə / (BrE)
to try to find or seek someone or something; to attempt to discover or locate
attempt to find; seek

Examples

I'm going to look for my keys; I think I dropped them in the kitchen.
Many people look for new opportunities at the start of the year.
She spent hours looking for the right gift for her friend.

Synset Examples

The police are searching for clues
They are searching for the missing man in the entire county

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search for - to try to find something by looking carefully
  • Synonym: hunt for - to search determinedly for something or someone

3. VERB

/ lʊk fər / (AmE), / lʊk fə / (BrE)
to try to find or search for something or someone
try to find; search for

Examples

I've been looking for my keys all morning but can't find them.
She always looks for ways to improve her work.
If you look for inspiration, try visiting an art museum.

Synset Examples

You should look for the best deals online.
He suggested we look for another place to eat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search for - to try to find something by looking carefully
  • Synonym: hunt for - to look for something or someone, especially carefully
  • Antonym: to ignore - to intentionally not look for or pay attention to something
