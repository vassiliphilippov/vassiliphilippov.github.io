to holla at

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑːlə æt / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə æt / (BrE)
to shout or call out to someone, often to get their attention
shout or call out to someone
informal american

Examples

Whenever I see my friend across the street, I holla at him to say hello.
She doesn't like it when strangers holla at her from their cars.
He tried to holla at the waitress to get her attention, but she didn't hear him.

Synset Examples

He loves to holla at his friends during games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout at - to speak loudly or yell at someone

Etymology

Variant pronunciation and spelling of 'holler at', American English slang; 'holler' from 'holler' (to shout).
