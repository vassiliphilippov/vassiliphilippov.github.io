to torque off

1. VERB

/ tɔrk ɑf / (AmE), / tɔːk ɒf / (BrE)
to tighten or loosen (a fastener or part) by applying a specified amount of torque, usually with a torque wrench
tighten/loosen using torque

Examples

The mechanic made sure to torque off every bolt to the correct specification.
Remember to torque off the wheel nuts evenly to avoid damage.
She always torques off the screws according to the manufacturer's guidelines.

Synset Examples

Make sure to torque off the screws before moving on.
He forgot to torque off the engine parts before leaving.
You need to torque off those bolts to secure the frame.

Etymology

From 'torque' (force causing rotation), from Latin 'torquere' (to twist), combined with 'off' phrasal verb formation.
