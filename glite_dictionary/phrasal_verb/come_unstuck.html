to come unstuck

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʌnˈstʌk / (AmE), / kʌm ʌnˈstʌk / (BrE)
to become detached or separated from a surface or position
become detached or separated

Examples

After a few days, the bandage began to come unstuck from his arm.
One of the posters in the hallway has come unstuck and is hanging loosely.
Over time, cheap wallpaper may come unstuck at the corners.

Synset Examples

The puzzle pieces seemed to come unstuck after a while.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detach - to separate or unfasten something
  • Antonym: stick - to become fixed in a particular position
