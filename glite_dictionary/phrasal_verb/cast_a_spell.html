to cast a spell

1. VERB

/ kæst ə spɛl / (AmE), / kɑːst ə spel / (BrE)
to use magic words or actions to produce a supernatural effect; to perform magic
perform magic using words or actions

Examples

The magician cast a spell that made the rabbit disappear.
In many fairy tales, witches cast a spell over the entire kingdom.
She whispered the ancient words to cast a spell of protection.

Synset Examples

He used his wand to cast a spell on the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enchant - to put someone or something under a spell
  • Antonym: curse - to cause misfortune by a magical spell
