to voice concern

1. VERB

/ vɔɪs kənˈsɜrn / (AmE), / vɔɪs kənˈsɜːn / (BrE)
to express worries or criticisms about something, usually publicly or officially
express worries or criticisms

Examples

Several employees voiced concern about the safety measures in the factory.
Parents have repeatedly voiced concern over the lack of healthy lunch options at school.
During the meeting, she did not hesitate to voice concern about the proposed budget cuts.

Synset Examples

Critics often voice their concerns about ObamaCare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: express concern - to state worries or anxieties
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears
