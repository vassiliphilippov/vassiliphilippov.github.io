to catch up

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / kætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to reach the same standard, level, or position as others after having fallen behind; to do tasks that one should have already finished
reach level after falling behind

Examples

After missing a week of school, it took me several days to catch up on all my assignments.
She worked late into the night to catch up with her colleagues.
If you don't study now, it will be hard to catch up later.

Synset Examples

I caught up on my homework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep up - to remain at the same level or speed as someone or something
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to remain at the same level or pace as others
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02002825-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / kætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to learn about something or become up to date with events after having missed information or developments
become up to date with news or events

Examples

I need to catch up on all the emails I missed during my vacation.
She spent the evening catching up with the news after her trip.
He finally caught up with the latest market trends after reading several reports.

Synset Examples

I'm trying to catch up with the latest developments in molecular biology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get up to speed - to reach the current level of knowledge or skill
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ kætʃ ˈʌp / (AmE), / katʃ ˈʌp / (BrE)
to talk to someone after a period of time to exchange news or updates about each other's lives
talk after absence to share news

Examples

Let’s get coffee this week so we can catch up on each other’s lives.
It was great to catch up with old friends at the reunion.
They spent the afternoon catching up after not seeing each other for years.
