to get over with

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈoʊvɚ wɪθ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈəʊvə wɪð / (BrE)
to finish or complete something, especially something unpleasant, as quickly as possible so that you do not have to worry about it any longer
to finish something unpleasant quickly

Examples

Let's just get over with this exam so we can relax afterward.
She wanted to get over with the difficult conversation as soon as possible.
He finally decided to get over with cleaning out the garage.

Synset Examples

He told his friend to just spank it and get it over with.
I just want to get the presentation over with.
Let's get this meeting over with quickly.
I need to get my dental appointment over with.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - to bring something to an end or completion
  • Antonym: to put off - to delay doing something unpleasant
