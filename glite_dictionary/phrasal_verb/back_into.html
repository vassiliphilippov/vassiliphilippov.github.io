to back into

1. VERB

/ bæk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / bæk ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to move a vehicle in reverse so that it enters a space or position rear-first
reverse a vehicle into a space

Examples

It's often easier to back into a parking spot than to drive in forwards.
Janet quickly backed into the narrow driveway without hitting the fence.
Learning to back into tight spaces is a useful driving skill.

Synset Examples

They had to back into the garage to fit the car.
He had to back into the parking space carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to move backward, especially a vehicle
