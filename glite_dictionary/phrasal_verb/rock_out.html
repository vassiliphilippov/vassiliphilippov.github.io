to rock out

1. VERB

/ rɑk aʊt / (AmE), / rɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to perform or enjoy music, especially rock music, with great energy and enthusiasm
perform or enjoy music energetically
informal

Examples

The band really rocked out during the encore, getting the whole crowd dancing.
Whenever her favorite song comes on, she loves to rock out in her living room.
They invited a few friends over to rock out with their guitars all night.

Synset Examples

They decided to rock out with their cock out at the beach.
She’s known to rock out with her cock out at festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - to play music informally with others
