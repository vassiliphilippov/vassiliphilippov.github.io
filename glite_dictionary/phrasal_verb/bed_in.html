to bed in

1. VERB

/ bɛd ɪn / (AmE), / bɛd ɪn / (BrE)
to gradually make something, such as a machine or system, work effectively by using it over time or allowing it to settle after installation
install or adapt to effective use

Examples

The engineers will bed in the new control system over the next month.
It's important to bed in new shoes before a long hike to avoid discomfort.
The company needs time to bed in the latest software update across all departments.

Synset Examples

The engineers will bed in the new equipment next week.
We need to bed in the new tires before the trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break in - to make something ready for use by using it gradually
