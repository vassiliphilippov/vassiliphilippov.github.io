to get one up on

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌn ʌp ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌn ʌp ɒn / (BrE)
to achieve an advantage over someone; to outdo or surpass another person in a competitive situation
gain an advantage over someone
informal

Examples

She's determined to get one up on her rival at any opportunity.
Whenever he loses, he can't wait to get one up on his opponent next time.
Marketing teams are always looking for ways to get one up on the competition.

Synset Examples

She managed to get one up on her competitors.
He always tries to get one up on his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to lag or be at a disadvantage in comparison to others
