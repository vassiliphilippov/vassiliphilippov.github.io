to turn a loss

1. VERB

/ tɝn ə lɔs / (AmE), / tɜːn ə lɒs / (BrE)
(of a business, investment, or venture) to result in a financial loss rather than a profit.
incur a financial loss

Examples

After a challenging quarter, the company turned a loss for the first time in years.
Despite their efforts to cut costs, they still turned a loss last year.
If the project turns a loss again, we may have to shut it down.

Synset Examples

I lost thousands of dollars on that bad investment!
The company turned a loss after the first year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to turn a profit - to result in a financial gain
