to deck up

1. VERB

/ dɛk ʌp / (AmE), / dɛk ʌp / (BrE)
to put on special or elaborate clothes in order to look particularly appealing or attractive
dress in special or elaborate clothes
informal

Examples

She likes to deck up for special occasions such as weddings.
All the contestants decked up in colorful costumes for the parade.
Before the big dinner, he was busy decking up in his finest suit.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to wear formal or elaborate clothes

Etymology

Possibly from the nautical verb 'deck' (to adorn), originally from Dutch 'dekken' (to cover).
