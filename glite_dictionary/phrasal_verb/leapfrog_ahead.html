to leapfrog ahead

1. VERB

/ ˈliːp.frɑɡ əˈhɛd / (AmE), / ˈliːp.frɒɡ əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to make a sudden, significant advance that surpasses others, especially by overtaking competitors or previously leading positions
advance rapidly beyond others

Examples

The new smartphone could leapfrog ahead of its competitors with its cutting-edge features.
By investing in research, the company hopes to leapfrog ahead in renewable energy.
Many Asian cities have managed to leapfrog ahead in transportation technology.

Synset Examples

They plan to leapfrog ahead in technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outpace - to move or develop faster than someone or something
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to lag or be slower than others

Etymology

From 'leapfrog' (to jump over) + 'ahead', referencing the children's game of leapfrog and used figuratively.
