to sell out

1. VERB

/ sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to abandon one's principles, ideals, or associates, often for personal gain or advantage, especially financial
abandon principles for gain

Examples

Many fans accused the band of having sold out when they switched to a more commercial sound.
She vowed never to sell out her beliefs for money.
Politicians often face criticism if people feel they have sold out to powerful lobbyists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal or unfaithful, especially by breaking trust
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to refuse to change your beliefs or principles
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00615215-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to sell all of the available goods or tickets so that nothing remains; to dispose of all merchandise
to sell all available merchandise

Examples

The concert tickets sold out within two hours of going on sale.
We expect the new model to sell out before the end of the week.
By noon, the bakery had sold out of its famous chocolate croissants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run out - to use up all of something so that none is left
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02252366-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to betray one's principles or integrity for personal gain, profit, or advantage
compromise principles for gain

Examples

Many fans accused the artist of selling out after she signed a major label deal.
He promised never to sell out his beliefs, no matter how tempting the offer.
Politicians are often criticized for selling out when they put personal ambition above public interest.

Synset Examples

She refused to sell out her values for fame.
The band decided not to sell out for a record deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - to accept standards that are lower than is desirable
