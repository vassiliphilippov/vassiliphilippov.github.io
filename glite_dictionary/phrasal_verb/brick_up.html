to brick up

1. VERB

/ brɪk ʌp / (AmE), / brɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to seal or close an opening by building a wall of bricks across it
seal with bricks

Examples

After the old window was removed, the workers bricked up the opening completely.
They decided to brick up the fireplace to prevent drafts during the winter.
The abandoned tunnel had been bricked up for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wall up - to seal an opening with a wall, often of bricks or another material
