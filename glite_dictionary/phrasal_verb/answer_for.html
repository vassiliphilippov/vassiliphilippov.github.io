to answer for

1. VERB

/ ˈæn.sɚ fɔr / (AmE), / ˈɑːn.sə fə / (BrE)
to be responsible or held accountable for something, especially wrongdoing or mistakes
be liable or accountable for something

Examples

As manager, you will have to answer for the team's performance.
He refused to answer for his friend's actions.
The politician will answer for the corruption charges in court.

Synset Examples

She must answer for her actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to account for - give a satisfactory record or explanation of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02751892-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæn.sɚ fər / (AmE), / ˈɑːn.sə fə / (BrE)
to explain or give a reason for something, especially to justify or be responsible for it
explain or justify; be responsible for

Examples

He had to answer for every item of expenditure during the audit.
I can't answer for what happened before I arrived at the company.
She was unable to answer for her colleague's mistakes during the interview.

Synset Examples

I can't account for the missing money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to account for - to give an explanation for; to justify
