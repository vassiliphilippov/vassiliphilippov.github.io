to raise the rent

1. VERB

/ reɪz ðə rɛnt / (AmE), / reɪz ðə rɛnt / (BrE)
to increase the amount of money required to rent a property
increase rental price

Examples

The landlord decided to raise the rent after renovating the building.
They threatened to move out if the company tried to raise the rent again this year.
After property taxes increased, the owner was forced to raise the rent on all his apartments.

Synset Examples

The slumlord raised the rents without making any repairs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lower the rent - to decrease the rental price
