to catapult into the spotlight

1. VERB

/ ˈkædəˌpʌlt ˈɪntu ðə ˈspaɪtˌlɑɪt / (AmE), / ˈkatəˌpʌlt ˈɪntuː ðə ˈspaɪtˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to suddenly become the center of public attention or interest
suddenly become widely noticed

Examples

Her breakthrough role in the movie would catapult her into the spotlight overnight.
The viral video catapulted the unknown musician into the spotlight within days.
Controversy can catapult a brand into the spotlight, for better or worse.

Synset Examples

The marketing campaign will catapult the brand into the spotlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust into the limelight - make someone/something suddenly the center of attention
  • Antonym: fade into obscurity - gradually lose public attention or recognition

Etymology

From 'catapult' (a device for hurling objects; from Latin 'catapulta') and 'spotlight' (focus of public attention).
