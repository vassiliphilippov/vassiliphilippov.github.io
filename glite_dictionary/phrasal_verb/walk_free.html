to walk free

1. VERB

/ wɔk fri / (AmE), / wɔːk friː / (BrE)
to be allowed to leave a place or situation, especially a court or prison, without receiving punishment or further restriction
be released without punishment

Examples

After the charges were dropped, he was able to walk free from court.
The press criticized the judge for letting the suspect walk free.
She never thought she would walk free after the long trial.

Synset Examples

The judge decided to let him walk free after the hearing.
She was relieved to see her friend walk free from the courtroom.
After serving his time, he finally walked free.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be acquitted - to be found not guilty by a court
