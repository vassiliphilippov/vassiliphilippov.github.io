to cash out

1. VERB

/ kæʃ aʊt / (AmE), / kaʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a demanding career or lifestyle, often to pursue a simpler or more fulfilling way of living, especially after reassessing personal and career goals.
leave demanding life for simpler one

Examples

After years in the corporate world, he decided to cash out and travel the world on a sailboat.
She finally cashed out and bought a quiet farmhouse in the countryside.
Many professionals dream of cashing out for a simpler life, but few actually do it.

Synset Examples

After 3 decades in politics, she cashed out and moved to Polynesia

Etymology

From the idea of 'cashing out' in gambling or investment, meaning to take your gains and leave, metaphorically extended to life choices.
