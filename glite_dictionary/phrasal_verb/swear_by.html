to swear by

1. VERB

/ swɛr baɪ / (AmE), / swɛə baɪ / (BrE)
to have great confidence in or strongly believe in the value or effectiveness of something
trust or firmly believe in something's value

Examples

My grandmother swears by honey and lemon for soothing a sore throat.
Many runners swear by stretching before a workout to prevent injury.
He swore by the power of meditation to reduce stress.

Synset Examples

He swears by this herbal tonic for boosting energy.

Some gamers swear by RNGesus for their wins.
Many athletes swear by yoga for flexibility.
I swear by this brand of vitamins for energy.
She swears by the dormitive virtue of herbal remedies.
She swears by herbal tea for relaxation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust - to have confidence in the reliability or truth of someone or something
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about; to lack belief in
