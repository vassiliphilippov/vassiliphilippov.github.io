to zap the bug

1. VERB

/ zæp ðə bʌɡ / (AmE), / zæp ðə bʌɡ / (BrE)
to quickly eliminate a problem or error in software, especially by fixing a bug in computer code
quickly fix a software bug
informal

Examples

After several hours, the developer was finally able to zap the bug causing the crash.
It's always satisfying to zap the bug that was breaking your code.
They worked late into the night to zap the bug in the new application.

Synset Examples

She managed to zap the bugs in the software quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debug - to find and remove errors from computer code

Etymology

"Zap" comes from American comic books to represent sudden action or destruction; "bug" is long used in computing to mean an error.
