to backed up

1. VERB

/ ˌbækt ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌbækt ˈʌp / (BrE)
used to describe a situation where traffic or movement is delayed or halted due to congestion or obstruction
congested or delayed (e.g., traffic)

Examples

The highway was completely backed up after the morning accident.
My emails are backed up because the server crashed.
Traffic is often backed up near the city center during rush hour.

Synset Examples

After the car crash, traffic was backed up for miles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congested - crowded or obstructed, especially referring to traffic
  • Antonym: cleared - no longer blocked or congested
