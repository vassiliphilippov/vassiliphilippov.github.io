to push out

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ aʊt / (AmE), / pʊʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to move or cause to move outward by applying force; to thrust away from a central point or place.
to thrust outward; to move out by force

Examples

The factory uses machines to push out newly made products on the assembly line.
He tried to push out the stuck window, but it wouldn't budge.
Roots can sometimes push out the pavement as they grow under a sidewalk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to pull in - to move inward by applying force
