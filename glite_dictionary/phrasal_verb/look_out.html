to look out

1. VERB

/ lʊk aʊt / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt / (BrE)
to be vigilant or careful; to watch for danger or trouble
be vigilant; stay alert

Examples

You should always look out when crossing a busy street.
Look out for falling branches after the storm.
I told the children to look out for cars while playing near the road.

Synset Examples

Watch out for pickpockets!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to watch out - be vigilant or cautious about danger
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02156396-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lʊk aʊt / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt / (BrE)
to protect someone or their interests; to ensure that someone is safe or gets what they need
protect someone's interests

Examples

You have to look out for yourself in this business.
Older siblings often look out for their younger brothers and sisters.
She promised to look out for her friend's interests during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

A man's gotta look out for his family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take care of - to look after or provide for someone
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or protect someone properly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01131490-v
Synset POS: v
