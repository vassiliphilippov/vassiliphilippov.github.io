to carry on with

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ɑn wɪð / (AmE), / ˈkæri ɒn wɪð / (BrE)
to continue doing something, especially after a pause or interruption
continue with an activity or task

Examples

Despite the difficulties, we decided to carry on with the project.
She encouraged her team to carry on with their research after the setback.
If you want to succeed, you must carry on with your efforts, no matter what.

Synset Examples

Even after setbacks, we must carry on with our plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process
  • Antonym: stop - to cease doing something
