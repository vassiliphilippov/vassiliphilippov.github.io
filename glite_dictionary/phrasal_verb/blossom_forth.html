to blossom forth

1. VERB

/ ˈblɑːsəm fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈblɒsəm fɔːθ / (BrE)
to develop, flourish, or come to a promising or successful stage
develop or flourish successfully

Examples

She truly began to blossom forth after moving to the city and starting her new career.
New businesses often blossom forth during periods of economic growth.
His artistic talent blossomed forth in the supportive environment of the college.

Synset Examples

Youth blossomed into maturity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flourish - to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way
  • Antonym: to wither - to become dry and shrunken, often used as an opposite when speaking metaphorically

Etymology

From 'blossom' (Middle English, from Old English blōstm, flower) + 'forth' (Old English, forward).

2. VERB

/ ˈblɑːsəm fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈblɒsəm fɔːθ / (BrE)
To develop or emerge in a noticeable and positive way.
develop or emerge positively

Examples

New friendships can blossom forth quickly in a supportive environment.
Her artistic abilities truly blossomed forth after she enrolled in art school.
Innovative ideas often blossom forth when people feel inspired.

Synset Examples

Creative solutions will blossom forth if we collaborate.
The new ideas began to blossom forth in the meeting.
Her confidence started to blossom forth after the workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flourish - to grow or develop in a healthy way
  • Antonym: to wither - to gradually decline or fade away
