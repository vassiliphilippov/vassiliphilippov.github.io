to hold down

1. VERB

/ hoʊld daʊn / (AmE), / həʊld daʊn / (BrE)
to restrain or keep something within limits; to prevent from increasing or becoming excessive
restrain; keep within limits

Examples

Please hold down the noise so that the neighbors can sleep.
The company managed to hold down costs despite rising prices.
She struggled to hold down her excitement during the meeting.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: limit - to restrict the amount or size of something

2. VERB

/ hoʊld daʊn / (AmE), / həʊld daʊn / (BrE)
to manage to keep a job or position, especially in difficult circumstances or over a period of time
manage to keep a job or position

Examples

It's difficult to hold down a stressful job and study at the same time.
She managed to hold down the manager position for five years.
After his illness, he struggled to hold down steady work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep - to continue to have or hold onto something
  • Antonym: to lose - to cease to have something, especially a job or position
