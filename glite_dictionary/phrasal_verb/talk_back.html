to talk back

1. VERB

/ tɔk bæk / (AmE), / tɔːk bæk / (BrE)
to reply to someone, especially a person in authority, in a rude or disobedient way
reply disrespectfully to authority

Examples

If you talk back to your boss, you might get in trouble.
Children sometimes talk back when they're feeling frustrated or misunderstood.
The student was reprimanded for talking back during the lesson.

Synset Examples

Don't talk back; you'll just get into trouble.
She doesn’t like it when kids talk back.
Don't talk back to your teacher like that.
He talked back with a lip that surprised everyone.
He was punished for talking back during class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: answer back - to reply disrespectfully, especially to someone in authority
