to wear round

1. VERB

/ wɛr raʊnd / (AmE), / wɛə raʊnd / (BrE)
(nautical) To turn a boat's head away from the wind so that it moves with the wind on the opposite side.
(nautical) turn a boat away from the wind

Examples

As the wind shifted, the captain ordered the crew to wear round and change direction.
The yacht had to wear round to avoid the oncoming storm.
She learned how to wear round quickly during her sailing lessons.

Synset Examples

The sailors decided to tack the boat
The boat tacked

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tack - turn a boat's head through the wind toward the opposite tack

Etymology

From nautical use of 'wear' meaning to bring a ship's head round by turning away from the wind, combined with 'round'.
