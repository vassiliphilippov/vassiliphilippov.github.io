to slobber over

1. VERB

/ ˈslɑbɚ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɒbə ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to show excessive envy or desire for something or someone, often in an overt, unrestrained, or embarrassing way
envy or desire something excessively

Examples

He couldn't help but slobber over his colleague's promotion and new office.
The critics seemed to slobber over every detail of the celebrity's outfit last night.
She tried not to slobber over the designer shoes in the shop window, but it was difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drool over - to show great admiration or desire for someone or something
