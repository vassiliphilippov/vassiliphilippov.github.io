to scope out

1. VERB

/ skoʊp aʊt / (AmE), / skəʊp aʊt / (BrE)
to look at or examine something carefully, especially to get information before taking action
investigate or examine closely
informal

Examples

Before signing the lease, we should scope out the neighborhood and see what it's really like.
He spent an hour scoping out the store's security system before attempting the heist.
At the conference, she scoped out networking opportunities with key industry leaders.

Synset Examples

I’ll scope out the best deals online.
They decided to scope out the venue first.
She went to scope out potential clients.
We need to scope out the competition first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to check out - to look at something to see what it is like
  • Synonym: to investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry
