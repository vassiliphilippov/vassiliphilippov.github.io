to edit out

1. VERB

/ ˈɛdɪt aʊt / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate a part from a film, recording, or text during the editing process
remove during editing

Examples

The director decided to edit out the controversial scene from the final cut.
Mistakes were edited out of the podcast before it was released to the public.
She spent hours editing out unnecessary sentences from her article.

Synset Examples

edit film
cut recording tape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut out - to remove a part of something by cutting
