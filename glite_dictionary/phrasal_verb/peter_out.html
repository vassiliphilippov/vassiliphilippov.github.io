to peter out

1. VERB

/ ˈpiːtər aʊt / (AmE), / ˈpiːtə aʊt / (BrE)
(of something) to gradually become weaker or smaller and then end or disappear
gradually diminish and end

Examples

Our conversation petered out after an hour, and we both fell silent.
The fundraising campaign petered out before reaching its goal.
Interest in the project gradually petered out over the summer.

Synset Examples

The music just petered out — there was no proper ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fizzle out - to gradually stop happening, especially after starting with excitement

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the slang use of 'peter' meaning to run out or diminish (19th century).

2. VERB

/ ˈpiːtɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈpiːtə aʊt / (BrE)
to gradually decrease and come to an end; to dwindle or fade away, especially after a gradual loss of strength or intensity
gradually come to an end

Examples

The excitement around the project began to peter out after several months.
Their conversation petered out as both grew tired.
The river peters out before reaching the coast during dry seasons.

Synset Examples

At the end of the march, I pooped out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwindle - to become steadily less; to diminish in size, amount, or strength
  • Synonym: fade - to gradually disappear or lose strength

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from American English mid-19th century mining slang, related to the depletion of resources.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00099271-v
Synset POS: v
