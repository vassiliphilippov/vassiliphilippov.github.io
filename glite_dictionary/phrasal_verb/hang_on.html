to hang on

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ɑn / (AmE), / hæŋ ɒn / (BrE)
to remain connected on a telephone line; to wait without ending the call
wait on the telephone line

Examples

Could you hang on a minute while I transfer your call?
If you hang on, I'll find out if she's available to speak with you.
He asked the caller to hang on while he checked the schedule.

Synset Examples

Please hang on while I get your folder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold on - to remain connected on a telephone line; to wait without ending the call
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00792171-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hæŋ ɑn / (AmE), / hæŋ ɒn / (BrE)
to continue doing something determinedly, especially in difficult circumstances; to persist and refuse to give up
persist; refuse to give up

Examples

Even when the project seemed impossible, she decided to hang on and finish it.
They told him to quit, but he continued to hang on despite the obstacles.
You have to hang on if you want to reach your goals, no matter how tough it gets.

Synset Examples

he persisted to call me every night
The child persisted and kept asking questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly in a course of action despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying; to surrender
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00351115-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hæŋ ɑn / (AmE), / hæŋ ɒn / (BrE)
to wait for a short time; to persevere or endure a difficult situation
wait briefly; persevere

Examples

Can you hang on a minute while I grab my keys?
Despite the challenges, she managed to hang on until things improved.
Just hang on; the doctor will see you shortly.

Synset Examples

Just hang on a little longer, help is coming.
Hang on, I'll be right back!
You need to hang on during the rough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold on - to wait for a short time
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying; to surrender
