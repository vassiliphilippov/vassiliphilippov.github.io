to eagle up

1. VERB

/ ˈiːɡəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈiːɡl̩ ʌp / (BrE)
to rise to a challenge; to face a task with courage or determination
rise to a challenge
informal

Examples

When the team faced setbacks, their captain urged everyone to eagle up and keep pushing forward.
You need to eagle up if you want to overcome these obstacles and succeed.
Despite her nerves, she eagled up and delivered a confident speech.

Synset Examples

He managed to eagle up and make a great presentation.
