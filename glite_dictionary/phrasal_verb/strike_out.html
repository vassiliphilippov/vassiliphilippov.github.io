to strike out

1. VERB

/ straɪk aʊt / (AmE), / straɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to remove someone or something from a list, record, or document, usually by drawing a line through it
remove from a list or record

Examples

After reviewing the document, the manager decided to strike out several unnecessary names from the list.
You need to strike out any items that have already been completed.
Her name was struck out because she did not attend the meeting.

Synset Examples

Cross the name of the dead person off the list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cross off - to remove from a list by drawing a line through a name or item
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00802412-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ straɪk aʊt / (AmE), / straɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to fail to achieve success in an attempt or activity
fail to succeed in an attempt

Examples

After trying to start his own business, he struck out and ended up returning to his old job.
The team struck out in their attempt to win the championship this year.
She struck out on her proposal, but learned a lot from the experience.

Synset Examples

The candidate struck out with his health care plan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result

Etymology

Originally from baseball terminology, where 'strike out' means to be put out after failing to hit the ball three times.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02534499-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ straɪk aʊt / (AmE), / straɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to make a sudden movement towards someone or something, often in an attempt to hit or kick
make a sudden movement to hit or kick

Examples

When threatened, the snake will strike out at anything that moves.
He struck out with his fist, narrowly missing his opponent's jaw.
Startled by the noise, the horse struck out with its hind legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lash out - to suddenly attempt to hit or attack someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01940220-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ straɪk aʊt / (AmE), / straɪk aʊt / (BrE)
(in baseball) to dismiss a batter by getting them to miss or not hit three pitches, resulting in an out.
get a batter out in baseball by three strikes

Examples

The pitcher managed to strike out the final batter and win the game.
If you strike out three times in a row, it can be very discouraging.
She was determined not to strike out during her first at-bat.

Synset Examples

The pitcher retired three batters
the runner was put out at third base

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to cause a batter or player to be out in baseball or softball
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01156641-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ straɪk aʊt / (AmE), / straɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to begin a new course of action, especially independently or with determination
begin a new course of action

Examples

After graduation, she decided to strike out on her own and start a business.
When his ideas were dismissed, he chose to strike out in a new direction.
Many young people strike out from home in search of independence and adventure.

Synset Examples

He struck out on his own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set out - to begin a journey or a course of action
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00347368-v
Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ straɪk aʊt / (AmE), / straɪk aʊt / (BrE)
(of a batter) to be put out after failing to hit three pitched balls, or (of a pitcher) to cause a batter to be put out in this way.
be put out in baseball by three strikes

Examples

The rookie pitcher managed to strike out the league's top hitter.
She was disappointed when she struck out with the bases loaded.
He has struck out twelve batters so far in this game.

Synset Examples

Oral struck out three batters to close the inning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: walk - to reach first base on four balls (baseball)
