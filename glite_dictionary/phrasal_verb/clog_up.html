to clog up

1. VERB

/ klɑg ʌp / (AmE), / klɒg ʌp / (BrE)
to (cause to) become blocked so that movement is difficult or impossible
become blocked or obstructed

Examples

Hair can clog up the shower drain if not removed regularly.
The traffic tends to clog up the main street during rush hour.
Leaves in the gutter will eventually clog up the drainage system.

Synset Examples

The leaves clog our drains in the Fall
The water pipe is backed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to cause something to become obstructed
  • Antonym: to unclog - to remove an obstruction or blockage

2. VERB

/ klɑg ʌp / (AmE), / klɒg ʌp / (BrE)
to become or cause to become blocked so that movement or flow is hindered
become blocked or obstructed

Examples

Hair and soap can easily clog up the shower drain.
Leaves in the gutter may clog up your downspouts if not removed.
The printer tends to clog up if it isn't cleaned regularly.

Synset Examples

Traffic tends to clog up during rush hour.
If you don’t clean it, the pipes will clog up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement or flow by obstruction
  • Antonym: to unblock - to remove an obstruction and allow movement or flow

Etymology

From 'clog' (to hinder or obstruct), of uncertain origin, plus 'up' as an intensifying particle.
