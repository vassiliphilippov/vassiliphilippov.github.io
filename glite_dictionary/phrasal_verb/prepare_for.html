to prepare for

1. VERB

/ prɪˈpɛr fər / (AmE), / prɪˈpeə fə / (BrE)
to make oneself ready mentally or emotionally for something, especially something unpleasant or challenging
get ready mentally or emotionally

Examples

She tried to prepare for the bad news by staying calm.
You should prepare for disappointment just in case things don't go as planned.
He mentally prepared for the long, difficult meeting ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace for - to get oneself ready for something difficult or unpleasant
