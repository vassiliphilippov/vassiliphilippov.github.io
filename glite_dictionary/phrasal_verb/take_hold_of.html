to take hold of

1. VERB

/ teɪk hoʊld əv / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld əv / (BrE)
to grasp or seize something or someone deliberately with your hands
grasp or seize with hands

Examples

She took hold of her son's hand tightly while crossing the busy street.
The firefighter took hold of the hose and aimed it at the flames.
As panic took hold of the crowd, people began to rush for the exits.

Synset Examples

The sales clerk quickly seized the money on the counter
She clutched her purse
The mother seized her child by the arm
Birds of prey often seize small mammals
A crazy idea took hold of him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something quickly and firmly
  • Synonym: grab - to take hold of something or someone suddenly and roughly
  • Antonym: release - to stop holding or to let go of something or someone
