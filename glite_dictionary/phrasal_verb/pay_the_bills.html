to pay the bills

1. VERB

/ peɪ ðə bɪlz / (AmE), / peɪ ðə bɪlz / (BrE)
to provide enough money to cover regular expenses or financial obligations, such as rent, utilities, and groceries
cover regular living expenses

Examples

She took a second job just to pay the bills each month.
No matter how much he earns, it never seems enough to pay the bills.
Writing novels doesn't always pay the bills, so he tutors on the side.

Synset Examples

I need to work extra hours to pay the bills.
He struggles each month to pay the bills on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet expenses - to have enough money to cover costs
  • Antonym: go broke - to lose all one's money or become unable to pay bills
