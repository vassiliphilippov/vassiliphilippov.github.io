to set up a bivvy

1. VERB

/ sɛt ʌp ə ˈbɪvi / (AmE), / sɛt ʌp ə ˈbɪvi / (BrE)
to put up a temporary shelter or sleeping cover outdoors, typically used for camping or survival purposes
erect a temporary outdoor shelter

Examples

We decided to set up a bivvy just before the rain started.
After hiking all day, they found a clearing to set up a bivvy for the night.
It took her only a few minutes to set up a bivvy using her lightweight gear.

Synset Examples

We set up a bivvy by the lake for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pitch a tent - to put up a tent for shelter outdoors
  • Antonym: take down a bivvy - to dismantle or remove a temporary shelter outdoors

Etymology

From 'set up' (to establish) and 'bivvy', short for 'bivouac' (from French 'bivouac', temporary camp).
