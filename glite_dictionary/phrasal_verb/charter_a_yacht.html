to charter a yacht

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɑrt̬ɚ ə jɑt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə ə jɒt / (BrE)
to hire a yacht for private or personal use, usually for a specified period.
hire a yacht for private use

Examples

They decided to charter a yacht for the weekend and explore the nearby islands.
It can be expensive to charter a yacht during the peak summer months.
She has always dreamed of chartering a yacht for a Mediterranean vacation.

Synset Examples

They decided to charter a yacht for the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent a yacht - to pay for the use of a yacht for a period
  • Antonym: buy a yacht - to purchase a yacht and own it

Etymology

From Old French *chartre*, from Latin *charta* (paper, document); 'charter' in the sense of hiring a vessel dates from the 17th century.
