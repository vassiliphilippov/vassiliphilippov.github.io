to black out the memory

1. VERB

/ blæk aʊt ðə məˈmɛɹi / (AmE), / blæk aʊt ðə məˈmɛɹi / (BrE)
to intentionally suppress or block a memory, often in order to avoid recalling something distressing
deliberately suppress a memory

Examples

After the accident, she tried to black out the memory of what had happened.
Some people black out the memory of traumatic events as a coping mechanism.
He found it nearly impossible to black out the memory of his childhood hardships.

Synset Examples

He tried to black out the memory of that day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repress - to subdue or hold back memories, feelings, or desires
  • Synonym: to suppress - to consciously inhibit or prevent from being expressed, especially memories or feelings
