to high-tail

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪ teɪl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ teɪl / (BrE)
to leave or retreat very quickly, often to avoid trouble or danger
leave or escape quickly
informal

Examples

We'd better high-tail it out of here before the storm gets worse.
When the alarm sounded, the thieves high-tailed down the alley to avoid the police.
She high-tailed to the exit as soon as she saw her ex enter the party.

Synset Examples

The actress high-tailed to her villa when reporters began to follow her to the restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary

Etymology

Possibly from the image of animals, such as deer, running with their tails raised.
