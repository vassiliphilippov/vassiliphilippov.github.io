to cohere into

1. VERB

/ koʊˈhɪr ˈɪntu / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪə ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
(of ideas, elements, etc.) to come together and form a unified or organized whole.
come together as a unified whole

Examples

After much discussion, the team's thoughts began to cohere into a single proposal.
Over the months, scattered policies would cohere into a comprehensive reform.
The images and memories in her mind cohered into a vivid dream.

Synset Examples

Their ideas began to cohere into a solid plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coalesce - to come together and form one mass or whole
  • Antonym: dissolve - to break apart or disintegrate, to lose unity
