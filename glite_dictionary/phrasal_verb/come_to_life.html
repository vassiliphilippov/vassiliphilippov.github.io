to come to life

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə laɪf / (AmE), / kʌm tə laɪf / (BrE)
to begin to exist, show activity, or become lively and animated.
to begin to exist or become lively

Examples

When the sun rises, the forest seems to come to life with the sounds of birds and animals.
The city really comes to life at night, with music and laughter filling the streets.
After months of planning, the festival finally came to life on the first day.

Synset Examples

All these flowers come to life when the rains come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come alive - to become lively, active, or exciting
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00361861-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm tə laɪf / (AmE), / kʌm tə laɪf / (BrE)
(of a work of art or similar object) to appear lifelike or animated; to seem as if alive.
appear lifelike or animated

Examples

The characters in the mural come to life when the light changes throughout the day.
Under the artist's skillful hand, the statue seemed to come to life.
As I gazed at the painting, the landscape appeared to come to life before my eyes.

Synset Examples

If you look at it long enough, this portrait comes to life!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spring to life - to suddenly become lively or active

Synset POS: v
