to obtrude upon

1. VERB

/ əbˈtruːd əˈpɑn / (AmE), / əbˈtruːd əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to force oneself or something uninvitedly and unwelcome into someone else's space, attention, or affairs; to intrude upon.
to intrude or force oneself uninvited

Examples

It is rude to obtrude upon people’s private conversations without invitation.
She disliked colleagues who would obtrude upon her workspace without asking.
Modern advertisements often obtrude upon our online experiences in unpleasant ways.

Synset Examples

This new colleague invades my territory
The neighbors intrude on your privacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intrude on - to enter or force oneself in without welcome
  • Synonym: encroach on - to intrude gradually, often violating boundaries

Etymology

From Latin obtrudere (to thrust upon), from ob- (toward) + trudere (to thrust).
