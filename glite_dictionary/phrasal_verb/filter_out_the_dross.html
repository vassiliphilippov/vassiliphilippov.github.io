to filter out the dross

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪltɚ aʊt ðə drɑs / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə aʊt ðə drɒs / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate unwanted or worthless elements from a collection, situation, or process, leaving only what is valuable or useful
remove unwanted elements; keep only what is valuable

Examples

The editor's job is to filter out the dross and publish only the best articles.
It's important to filter out the dross when reviewing feedback from a large audience.
Researchers must filter out the dross to focus on relevant and high-quality data.

Synset Examples

Only the best ideas remain after filtering out the dross.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weed out - to remove unwanted items from a group
