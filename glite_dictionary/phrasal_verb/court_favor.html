to court favor

1. VERB

/ kɔrt ˈfeɪvɚ / (AmE), / kɔːt ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to try to gain someone's approval or goodwill by flattery or obsequious behavior
seek approval by flattery

Examples

She tried to court favor with her new boss by complimenting his every decision.
Politicians often court favor with powerful donors during election campaigns.
It was clear that he was courting favor with his supervisor to get promoted.

Synset Examples

This employee is currying favor with his superordinates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to curry favor - to seek someone's approval by excessive flattery
  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively or insincerely

Etymology

From 'court' (to seek the favor of someone, as in a royal court) and 'favor.'
