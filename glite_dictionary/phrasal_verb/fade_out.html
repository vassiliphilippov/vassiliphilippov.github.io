to fade out

1. VERB

/ feɪd aʊt / (AmE), / feɪd aʊt / (BrE)
to gradually become less distinct or disappear, especially until no longer visible or audible
gradually disappear or become less distinct

Examples

As the song ended, the music faded out until there was only silence.
The image on the screen slowly faded out and was replaced by a new scene.
His voice faded out as he walked away from the microphone.

Synset Examples

The sound faded out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or exist
  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely

2. VERB

/ feɪd aʊt / (AmE), / feɪd aʊt / (BrE)
to gradually become less visible, audible, or intense until disappearing or ceasing
become gradually less visible or audible

Examples

As the credits rolled, the movie soundtrack began to fade out gently.
Over time, the paint on the old sign started to fade out until it was barely readable.
The announcer's voice will fade out as the commercial begins.

Synset Examples

They decided to fade out the background noise in the recording.
The music will fade out as the lights dim.
She watched the sunset fade out behind the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade - to lose brightness or strength or become less visible
