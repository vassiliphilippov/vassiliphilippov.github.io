to stand fast

1. VERB

/ stænd fæst / (AmE), / stænd fɑːst / (BrE)
to refuse to change your opinion or belief, especially when faced with opposition
refuse to abandon one's opinion or belief

Examples

She decided to stand fast in her beliefs despite mounting criticism.
He stood fast against the pressure to compromise his principles.
The committee members are standing fast on their decision, refusing to change their minds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold firm - refuse to change one's viewpoint or position
  • Antonym: to give in - to yield or surrender (an opinion or belief)
