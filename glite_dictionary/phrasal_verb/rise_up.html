to rise up

1. VERB

/ raɪz ʌp / (AmE), / raɪz ʌp / (BrE)
to take part in a rebellion or revolt; to rebel against authority or a previous allegiance
rebel against authority

Examples

The people decided to rise up against the oppressive regime.
Workers threatened to rise up if their demands were ignored.
Throughout history, many have risen up to fight for their freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - to refuse allegiance or oppose authority
  • Antonym: to submit - to accept authority or yield to control
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02589224-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ raɪz ʌp / (AmE), / raɪz ʌp / (BrE)
to move upwards and come to the surface of a liquid or other medium
come to the surface (phrasal verb)

Examples

Bubbles rise up to the surface of the boiling water.
After a few minutes, the sediment began to rise up from the bottom of the tank.
Oil droplets will rise up through the water and form a layer at the top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - to rise to the surface of a liquid
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01994295-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ raɪz ʌp / (AmE), / raɪz ʌp / (BrE)
(of a four-legged animal) to stand up on its hind legs, often suddenly or as a reaction to fear or excitement.
(of animals) stand on hind legs

Examples

The frightened stallion began to rise up as the thunder echoed.
If you wave a flag, the pony might rise up and rear back.
Suddenly, the circus elephant tried to rise up on its hind legs to reach the fruit.

Synset Examples

The horse reared in terror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rear - (of a horse) to rise up on its hind legs
