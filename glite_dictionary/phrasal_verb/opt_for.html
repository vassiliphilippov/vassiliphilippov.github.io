to opt for

1. VERB

/ ɑpt fɔr / (AmE), / ɒpt fə / (BrE)
to choose or decide in favor of a particular option from among several possibilities
to choose or select something

Examples

Many students opt for online courses due to their flexibility.
After much consideration, she opted for the vegetarian meal at the restaurant.
Companies often opt for remote work to reduce overhead costs.

Synset Examples

They opted for a vanilla wedding cake for simplicity.
Some people are opting for Novax due to personal beliefs.
After discussing, we opted for ritux as the treatment.
He always opts for cheap meals to save money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to choose - to select from a number of possibilities
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or choose

Etymology

From Latin 'optare' (to choose, desire); 'opt' entered English in the 19th century, with 'opt for' as a phrasal verb developing later.
