to force down

1. VERB

/ fɔrs daʊn / (AmE), / fɔːs daʊn / (BrE)
to impose or make someone accept ideas, beliefs, or opinions against their will or inclination
impose ideas or beliefs on someone

Examples

The government tried to force down new regulations without public debate.
She resented her parents' attempts to force down their religious views on her.
Teachers should not force down opinions, but encourage open discussion.

Synset Examples

He felt they were trying to force down their beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impose - to force someone to accept something
  • Degree Lower: to instill - gradually but firmly introduce (ideas, attitudes)
