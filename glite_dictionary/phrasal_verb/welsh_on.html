to welsh on

1. VERB

/ wɛlʃ ɑn / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to fail to honor or fulfill a promise, obligation, or bet, especially by not paying money owed
fail to fulfill a promise or bet

Examples

If you welsh on a bet, you may lose your friends' trust.
He promised to help with the move but tried to welsh on it later.
She hates when people welsh on their commitments, especially at work.

Synset Examples

He tends to welsh on his promises to help.
Don't welsh on the bet; pay up!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renege - to go back on a promise, agreement, or contract

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly derived from the offensive stereotype associating breach of promise with Welsh people. Now considered by some as potentially offensive.
