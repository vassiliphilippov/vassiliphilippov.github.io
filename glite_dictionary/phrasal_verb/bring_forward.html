to bring forward

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to move something to a position farther forward or closer to the front
move something forward

Examples

Could you please bring forward your chair so we have more space behind you?
The manager asked to bring forward the meeting table by a few feet.
She brought forward her suitcase to make room for others boarding the train.

Synset Examples

Can you move the car seat forward?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move forward - to advance in position or make progress
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01997958-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to introduce or present something for consideration or discussion, especially in a formal setting
present for consideration

Examples

She decided to bring forward the proposal at the next meeting.
The committee will bring forward several new suggestions for discussion.
The finance report was brought forward by the chairman for immediate review.

Synset Examples

The case was called up in court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submit - to formally present something for consideration or judgment
  • Synonym: to propose - to suggest an idea or plan for consideration
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove or take back something from consideration
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01027464-v
Synset POS: v
