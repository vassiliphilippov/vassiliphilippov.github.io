to chop off

1. VERB

/ tʃɑp ɑf / (AmE), / tʃɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove (something) by cutting, especially with a sharp blow
remove by cutting

Examples

The chef decided to chop off the ends of the carrots before slicing them.
He accidentally chopped off a piece of his finger while using the saw.
The gardener chopped off the dead branches to help the tree grow.

Synset Examples

cut off the ear
lop off the dead branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - to remove or detach by cutting
