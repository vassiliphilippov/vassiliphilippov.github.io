to con someone

1. VERB

/ kɑn ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / kɒn ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone, especially for personal gain
deceive or trick someone
informal american

Examples

He managed to con someone out of their savings with a fake investment scheme.
Tourists are often warned not to let strangers con someone with street games.
She discovered too late that she had been conned by a clever scam artist.

Synset Examples

The lowlife tried to con everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: scam - to deceive someone, often for money

Etymology

Short for 'confidence', from 'confidence trick' or 'confidence man', originally American slang.
