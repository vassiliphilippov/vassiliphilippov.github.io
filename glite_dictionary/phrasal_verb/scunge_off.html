to scunge off

1. VERB

/ skʌndʒ ɑf / (AmE), / skʌndʒ ɒf / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially help or things, from others by taking advantage of their generosity, usually without giving anything in return
obtain things by exploiting others' generosity
informal

Examples

He’s always scunging off his friends for free rides.
Don't scunge off my notes again; do your own work this time.
She hated scunging off others, but her situation left her no choice.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooch - to ask for or obtain something without paying or working for it
  • Synonym: spunge off - to live at someone else's expense, often without repaying
