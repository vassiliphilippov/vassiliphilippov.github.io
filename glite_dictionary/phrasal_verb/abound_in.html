to abound in

1. VERB

/ əˈbaʊnd ɪn / (AmE), / əˈbaʊnd ɪn / (BrE)
to exist in large quantities or numbers; to have something in abundance
to exist in large quantities

Examples

The region abounds in natural beauty, attracting tourists from all over the world.
Her stories abound in rich detail and vivid imagery.
Libraries abound in resources that can help students with their research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abound with - to have something in abundance
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ əˈbaʊnd ɪn / (AmE), / əˈbaʊnd ɪn / (BrE)
to exist in large quantities within something; to be plentiful or well supplied with something
exist in great quantities within

Examples

The region abounds in natural beauty and breathtaking landscapes.
This book abounds in useful information for beginner gardeners.
Their garden abounded in colorful flowers every spring.

Synset Examples

He seemed to abound in his own sense of confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abound with - to have something in large quantities; alternate form of 'abound in'
