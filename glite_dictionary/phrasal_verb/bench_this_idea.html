to bench this idea

1. VERB

/ bɛntʃ ðɪs aɪˈdiə / (AmE), / bɛntʃ ðɪs aɪˈdɪə / (BrE)
to postpone discussion or consideration of an idea, typically until a later time
postpone consideration of an idea
informal

Examples

After a lengthy discussion, the team decided to bench this idea until the next meeting.
When resources ran low, management chose to bench this idea rather than proceed immediately.
Sometimes it's best to bench this idea and revisit it when the timing is better.

Synset Examples

Let's bench this idea until we gather more data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table this idea - postpone consideration of an idea

Etymology

From the noun 'bench' (as in sports, meaning to remove from active play), used figuratively.
