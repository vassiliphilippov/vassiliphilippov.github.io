to feel poxy

1. VERB

/ fil ˈpɑksi / (AmE), / fiːl ˈpɒksi / (BrE)
to feel ill or generally unwell, especially in an indefinite or minor way
to feel ill or unwell (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

I always feel poxy after pulling an all-nighter for work.
After eating that street food, she started to feel poxy and went straight to bed.
Do you feel poxy today, or are you coming to the football match?

Synset Examples

He felt poxy after that long, tiring hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel sick - to feel ill or nauseous
  • Antonym: feel fine - to feel well or healthy

Etymology

From British slang 'poxy' (worthless, diseased), related to 'pox', a historical disease.
