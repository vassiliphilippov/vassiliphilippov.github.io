to drowse off

1. VERB

/ draʊz ɔf / (AmE), / draʊz ɒf / (BrE)
to gradually fall asleep, especially without intending to
fall asleep unintentionally

Examples

She often drowses off while watching television late at night.
The heat in the classroom made him drowse off during the lecture.
I was so tired I nearly drowsed off at my desk.

Synset Examples

he always falls asleep during lectures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nod off - to fall asleep briefly or unintentionally

Etymology

From "drowse" (to be sleepy) + off (indicating separation or transition).
