to move ahead

1. VERB

/ muːv əˈhɛd / (AmE), / muːv əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to progress or advance towards a destination, goal, or point in time
progress or advance forward

Examples

Now that we've solved this issue, let's move ahead with the project.
As the months move ahead, the days become longer and warmer.
Despite the setbacks, the committee decided to move ahead with the plan.

Synset Examples

We proceeded towards Washington
She continued in the direction of the hills
We are moving ahead in time now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proceed - to move forward, especially after stopping
  • Synonym: advance - to move forward or make progress
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with progress

2. VERB

/ muːv əˈhɛd / (AmE), / muːv əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to make progress or advance, especially towards a goal or after a delay
progress or advance

Examples

After months of planning, the team can finally move ahead with the project.
Despite several setbacks, she was determined to move ahead with her studies.
Let's move ahead to the next item on the agenda.

Synset Examples

It's time to move ahead after the long discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - to move forward in position or progress
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with others; to lag in progress
