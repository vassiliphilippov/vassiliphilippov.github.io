to come back

1. VERB

/ kʌm bæk / (AmE), / kʌm bæk / (BrE)
to be restored or return to a previous state or condition
be restored; return to a prior state

Examples

After months of illness, her strength finally came back.
When the lights came back on, everyone cheered.
The tradition is starting to come back in modern society.

Synset Examples

Her old vigor returned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to come or go back to a place, state, or activity

/ kʌm bæk / (AmE), / kʌm bæk / (BrE)
to return to a previous subject, topic, or state that was mentioned earlier
return to a previous subject or state

Examples

Let me come back to the previous point after we discuss this issue.
Whenever the topic of childhood comes up, she tends to come back to her favorite memory.
In his speech, the president would often come back to the theme of unity.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ kʌm bæk / (AmE), / kʌm bæk / (BrE)
to reply quickly or sharply, especially in response to criticism or a remark
reply quickly or sharply

Examples

When criticized, she would always come back with a clever response.
He tried to insult Tom, but Tom came back twice as hard.
Whenever the teacher scolded her, she couldn't help but come back.

Related Terms

4. VERB

/ kʌm bæk / (AmE), / kʌm bæk / (BrE)
to reach an equal score after being behind in a sports contest; to make up a deficit in points or goals
regain an equal score in sports

Examples

The team managed to come back from a two-goal deficit to tie the game.
If we come back in the second half, we might still have a chance to win.
She was thrilled when her favorite player helped the team come back and draw the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rally - to recover in performance or spirit, especially after a setback
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to lose a lead or lag in score or position
