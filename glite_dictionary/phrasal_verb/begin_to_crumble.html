to begin to crumble

1. VERB

/ bəˈɡɪn tə ˈkrʌmbəl / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə ˈkrʌmbəl / (BrE)
to start to deteriorate, decay, or fall apart, either literally or figuratively.
start to deteriorate or fall apart

Examples

After losing key players, the team began to crumble and lost several games in a row.
The relationship began to crumble when trust was broken.
As the rain poured in, the ancient walls began to crumble before our eyes.

Synset Examples

The old building began to crumble after years of neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - break into pieces or collapse
  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become worse in condition or value
