to sweat it out

1. VERB

/ swɛt ɪt aʊt / (AmE), / swet ɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to endure a period of discomfort, anxiety, or hardship, or to exercise intensely in order to relieve stress or tension
endure hardship; exercise intensively
informal

Examples

She had to sweat it out until the test results finally arrived.
They decided to sweat it out at the gym after a stressful day at work.
During the blackout, the villagers just had to sweat it out until the power returned.

Synset Examples

He decided to sweat it out instead of calling in sick.
Sometimes, you just have to sweat it out and wait.
Let's sweat it out at the gym together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough it out - to endure something difficult without giving up
  • Synonym: to ride out - to survive or endure (a period of trouble or difficulty)
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or surrender in a difficult situation
