to gang up on

1. VERB

/ ɡæŋ ʌp ɑn / (AmE), / ɡæŋ ʌp ɒn / (BrE)
(of people) to join together as a group to criticize, attack, or oppose someone.
join together to criticize or oppose someone

Examples

The whole team decided to gang up on their coach when they disagreed with the strategy.
Sometimes children gang up on one another in the playground, which can be very hurtful.
Whenever there's a controversial decision, employees tend to gang up on their manager.

Synset Examples

They always gang up on him during group projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to intimidate or mistreat someone weaker or vulnerable
