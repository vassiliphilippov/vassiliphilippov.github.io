to blast off

1. VERB

/ blæst ɑf / (AmE), / blɑːst ɒf / (BrE)
(of a rocket or spacecraft) to launch with great force, especially upwards into the air or space.
to launch with great force (rocket, spacecraft)

Examples

The crowd watched as the rocket blasted off into the clear morning sky.
At exactly 9 a.m., the shuttle blasted off from the launch pad.
Scientists cheered when their experiment successfully blasted off aboard the spacecraft.

Synset Examples

the rockets were blasted off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to set in motion, especially a rocket or spacecraft

2. VERB

/ blæst ˈɔf / (AmE), / blɑːst ˈɒf / (BrE)
(of a rocket or spacecraft) to launch or take off from the ground into the air or space.
(of rockets) to launch or take off

Examples

Crowds gathered to watch as the rocket prepared to blast off into orbit.
The countdown reached zero, and the spacecraft blasted off smoothly from the launch pad.
Children cheered as they saw the simulation of a spaceship blasting off on the big screen.

Synset Examples

The rocket is set to blast off at noon.
They watched the shuttle blast off into the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lift off - (of a spacecraft or rocket) to rise off the ground and begin flight
