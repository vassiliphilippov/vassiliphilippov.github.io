to strike down

1. VERB

/ straɪk daʊn / (AmE), / straɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to fall by hitting, attacking, or with a sudden force
cause to fall by a blow or force

Examples

The old oak tree was struck down during the violent storm.
A mysterious illness struck down several members of the expedition.
Lightning struck down the power lines, leaving the area without electricity.

Synset Examples

strike down a tree
Lightning struck down the hikers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock down - to make someone or something fall to the ground by hitting them
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01260866-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ straɪk daʊn / (AmE), / straɪk daʊn / (BrE)
(of a disease, disaster, etc.) to cause (someone) to die suddenly or unexpectedly.
cause to die suddenly

Examples

Many soldiers were struck down by the mysterious illness during the winter.
The epidemic struck down entire families within a matter of weeks.
He was struck down in his prime by a sudden heart attack.

Synset Examples

The disease struck down many young men in the village

Etymology

From the verb 'strike' (Old English strīcan) + 'down', indicating sudden force or bringing low.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01328616-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ straɪk daʊn / (AmE), / straɪk daʊn / (BrE)
(of a court or authority) to declare that a law, rule, or order is invalid and no longer in effect.
declare a law or rule invalid

Examples

The Supreme Court decided to strike down the controversial legislation as unconstitutional.
Several provisions of the new policy were struck down after a lengthy legal battle.
Activists cheered when the court moved to strike down the discriminatory law.

Synset Examples

Cancel the election results
strike down a law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invalidate - to make or declare something no longer legally acceptable or correct
  • Antonym: to uphold - to support or confirm a law, decision, or principle, often by a court
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02482757-v
Synset POS: v
