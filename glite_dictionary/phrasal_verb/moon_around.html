to moon around

1. VERB

/ mun əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / muːn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time in a listless or dreamy way, often appearing distracted or absent-minded
be idle or dreamy; appear distracted

Examples

He spent the afternoon just mooning around the house without doing anything productive.
She's been mooning around ever since her friend moved away.
Instead of studying, he kept mooning around in the garden, lost in his thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydream - to be lost in pleasant thoughts, usually while awake
  • Synonym: to mope - to act in a depressed or listless way

Etymology

From 'moon' in the sense of behaving dreamily, combined with 'around' as a particle.

2. VERB

/ mun əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / muːn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time aimlessly in an apathetic, gloomy, or dazed manner
move or act aimlessly in a gloomy mood

Examples

She would often moon around the house after a long, rainy afternoon.
He tends to moon around the office when he's feeling uninspired.
Instead of working on his assignment, Tom just mooned around all evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mope - to move around in a sad or listless manner

Etymology

From the verb 'moon' (to behave in a dreamy or listless way), with the particle 'around' emphasizing wandering or idleness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01809214-v
Synset POS: v
