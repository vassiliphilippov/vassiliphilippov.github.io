to beg off

1. VERB

/ bɛɡ ɔf / (AmE), / beɡ ɒf / (BrE)
to ask to be excused from an engagement or commitment
ask to be excused from a commitment

Examples

I'm sorry, but I have to beg off from the dinner party tonight.
She begged off attending the meeting due to a family emergency.
He tried to beg off the project, saying he was too busy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse oneself - to politely remove oneself from a responsibility or obligation
  • Antonym: to commit - to pledge or bind to a certain course or policy

2. VERB

/ bɛɡ ɔf / (AmE), / bɛɡ ɒf / (BrE)
to withdraw from a commitment, obligation, or planned activity by making an excuse
excuse oneself from a duty or plan

Examples

I have to beg off dinner tonight because I'm not feeling well.
He respectfully begged off joining the committee due to his busy schedule.
She's always quick to beg off when extra work is assigned.

Synset Examples

She decided to beg off from the party.
He tried to beg off his responsibilities.
I had to beg off the meeting due to illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse oneself - to give a reason not to participate in something
