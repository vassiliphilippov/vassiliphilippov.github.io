to feel sleepy

1. VERB

/ fil ˈsliːpi / (AmE), / fiːl ˈsliːpi / (BrE)
to begin to experience drowsiness or the desire to sleep
to become drowsy or wanting sleep

Examples

I always feel sleepy after having a large lunch.
It's normal to feel sleepy late at night when your body is ready to rest.
During the lecture, she started to feel sleepy and struggled to keep her eyes open.

Synset Examples

I always feel sleepy around noon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drowsy - feeling sleepy or half-asleep
  • Antonym: awake - not feeling sleepy; fully conscious
