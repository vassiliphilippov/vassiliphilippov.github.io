to get onto

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɑntu / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɒntu / (BrE)
to begin to understand something, usually after some difficulty or confusion
begin to understand

Examples

It took me a while to get onto what the instructions meant, but now it's clear.
Once she started paying attention, she quickly got onto the main idea of the lecture.
He finally got onto the joke after everyone else started laughing.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to understand or realize something, especially after some time
