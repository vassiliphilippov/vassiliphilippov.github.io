to mess up

1. VERB

/ mɛs ʌp / (AmE), / mɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to make something untidy or disturb its smoothness or order
make untidy or disturb smoothness

Examples

A sudden breeze messed up the smooth surface of the lake.
Don't mess up the papers on my desk by shuffling them around.
She carefully ironed her shirt, only to mess up the collar when she put it on.

Synset Examples

ruffle the surface of the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disarrange - to put out of order; to disturb the arrangement of
  • Antonym: tidy - arranged neatly and in order

2. VERB

/ mes ʌp / (AmE), / mes ʌp / (BrE)
to spoil, damage, or ruin something; to make a mistake that causes a problem
make a mistake; ruin something
informal

Examples

If you mess up the experiment, we will have to start all over again.
She was afraid she would mess up her big presentation at work.
Don't mess up your room right after I just cleaned it!

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to do something badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: to screw up - to make a mistake or do something badly (informal)
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or correct something that is wrong or broken
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɛs ʌp / (AmE), / mɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to make something dirty, untidy, or disordered; to cause confusion or mistakes in a situation or process
make disorderly or untidy; cause confusion

Examples

Don't mess up the kitchen right after I cleaned it.
He always seems to mess up his chances by being careless.
She realized she had messed up the final calculation.

Synset Examples

He messed up his room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - to spoil or destroy something completely
  • Antonym: to clean up - to make something clean or tidy
