to swing over

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / swɪŋ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to influence someone decisively to change their opinion, allegiance, or decision
influence decisively to change opinion or allegiance

Examples

The announcement was enough to swing over the undecided voters.
Her passionate speech swung over many members of the committee.
They tried hard, but nothing could swing over his decision.

Synset Examples

This action swung many votes over to his side

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to convince - persuade someone to do or believe something
  • Synonym: to persuade - cause someone to do something by reasoning or argument
