to hold the line

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə laɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to keep a telephone line open and wait, usually while being connected or as someone retrieves information
wait on the telephone line

Examples

Could you hold the line while I transfer your call to the manager?
She was asked to hold the line as the receptionist looked up her records.
Please hold the line; your call is very important to us.

Synset Examples

Please hang on while I get your folder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang on - to wait briefly, especially on the telephone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00792171-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə laɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to keep prices, spending, or another variable at the current level without allowing an increase
keep prices or rates unchanged

Examples

Despite rising costs, the company promised to hold the line on prices for the coming year.
The government is trying to hold the line on public spending during the economic downturn.
They have held the line on tuition fees for the past five years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raise prices - to increase the amount charged for goods or services

Synset POS: v
