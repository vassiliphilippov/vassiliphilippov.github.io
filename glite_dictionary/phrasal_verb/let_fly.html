to let fly

1. VERB

/ lɛt flaɪ / (AmE), / lɛt flaɪ / (BrE)
to shoot or throw something suddenly and forcefully, especially a weapon or projectile.
suddenly shoot or throw something

Examples

As soon as he saw the target, he let fly with his arrow.
The angry player let fly a rock across the field.
During the battle, the troops let fly a barrage of bullets at the enemy.

Synset Examples

The soldiers let drive their bullets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to shoot a gun or similar weapon
  • Synonym: launch - to send or catapult something forcefully

2. VERB

/ lɛt flaɪ / (AmE), / lɛt flaɪ / (BrE)
to send or express something abruptly and forcefully, such as words, objects, or gestures
to launch or express forcefully

Examples

During the argument, she suddenly let fly a string of harsh criticisms.
At the archery contest, he drew his bow and let fly the arrow.
When he heard the news, he couldn't help but let fly with a loud cheer.

Synset Examples

She let fly a series of jokes that had everyone laughing.
When the music started, they let fly and danced wildly.
He decided to let fly with his opinions during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurl - to throw something forcefully
  • Antonym: to restrain - to hold back or prevent from doing something
