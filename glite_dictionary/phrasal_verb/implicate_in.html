to implicate in

1. VERB

/ ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to show or suggest that someone is involved in a crime or wrongdoing.
to show involvement in a crime or wrongdoing

Examples

The documents clearly implicate in several senior officials in the corruption scandal.
She denied any intention to implicate in her colleague during the investigation.
New evidence could implicate in other members of the organization.

Synset Examples

The evidence may implicate him in the crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incriminate - to make someone appear guilty of a crime
  • Antonym: to exonerate - to officially state that someone is not guilty

Etymology

From Latin implicare (to entangle, involve), from in- (in) + plicare (to fold).
