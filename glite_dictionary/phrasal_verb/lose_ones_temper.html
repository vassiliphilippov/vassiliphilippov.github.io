to lose one's temper

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry and unable to control yourself
become suddenly very angry

Examples

He tried to stay calm, but eventually he lost his temper and shouted at the group.
It's important not to lose your temper during stressful situations at work.
She apologized after losing her temper with her children.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to keep one's temper - to remain calm and not become angry
  • Synonym: to blow up - to suddenly become very angry; lose control of one's anger
