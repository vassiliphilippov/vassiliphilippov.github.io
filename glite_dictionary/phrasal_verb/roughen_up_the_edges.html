to roughen up the edges

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌf.ən ʌp ði ˈɛdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.ən ʌp ði ˈɛdʒɪz / (BrE)
to make something appear or feel less smooth, perfect, or polished by creating irregularities along its surface or perimeter
make less smooth or perfect at the edges

Examples

She used sandpaper to roughen up the edges of the wooden shelf.
The designer chose to roughen up the edges of the poster for a vintage look.
He liked to roughen up the edges of his sketches so they felt less formal.

Synset Examples

He decided to roughen up the edges of the photo for effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to smooth out - to make smooth or even
