to spell out

1. VERB

/ spɛl aʊt / (AmE), / spɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to say or recite each letter in a word or name individually, usually to clarify its spelling
say each letter in a word or name

Examples

Could you please spell out your last name for me?
The teacher asked each student to spell out their assigned word aloud.
He had to spell out the address over the phone to make sure they understood.

Synset Examples

How do you spell this word?
We had to spell out our names for the police officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spell - to say or write the letters of a word in order
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00939909-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spɛl aʊt / (AmE), / spɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to say or write the letters of a word in their correct order, especially one at a time, without abbreviating
say or write letters of a word fully

Examples

Please spell out your last name so I can write it down correctly.
When filling out official forms, you must spell out abbreviations.
She patiently spelled out each word for the child.

Synset Examples

Can you spell out your middle name instead of just giving the initial?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to abbreviate - to shorten a word or phrase by leaving out letters
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01704008-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ spɛl aʊt / (AmE), / spɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to state or explain something clearly and in detail
explain clearly and in detail

Examples

Could you spell out exactly what you expect from us?
The contract spells out all the terms and conditions in detail.
She patiently spelled out the instructions for the new employees.

Synset Examples

You should spell out your demands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - to make something clear or easy to understand
  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something less confused and more comprehensible
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01007949-v
Synset POS: v
