to turn up the pressure

1. VERB

/ tɝn ʌp ðə ˈprɛʃɚ / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp ðə ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
to apply greater or increased pressure, often to try to influence someone's actions or decisions
increase the pressure (on someone)

Examples

Under public scrutiny, the committee decided to turn up the pressure on the company to improve safety standards.
The manager turned up the pressure on the team after their disappointing performance last season.
Negotiators are turning up the pressure ahead of the deadline to reach a new agreement.

Synset Examples

The Democrats turned up the heat on their candidate to concede the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn up the heat - to increase the force or intensity of pressure
