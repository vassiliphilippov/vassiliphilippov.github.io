to give a smooch

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə smuːtʃ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə smuːtʃ / (BrE)
to kiss someone affectionately, often with a sense of warmth or playfulness
kiss someone affectionately
informal

Examples

She leaned over to give a smooch to her daughter before leaving.
It's our anniversary—you have to give a smooch to me!
Before the train departed, he stopped to give a smooch to his wife.

Synset Examples

She gave him a smooch before heading to school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiss - to touch someone with your lips as a sign of love or greeting

Etymology

From 'give' + 'smooch' (informal term for a kiss, 20th-century American slang).
