to gun down

1. VERB

/ ɡʌn daʊn / (AmE), / ɡʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to shoot someone, especially killing or seriously injuring them, typically in a deliberate and violent attack
shoot and kill or injure someone

Examples

The police officer was gunned down while responding to a robbery call.
The gang members gunned down two rivals in broad daylight.
He was gunned down outside his home in what appeared to be a targeted attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoot - to fire a gun at someone or something

2. VERB

/ ɡʌn daʊn / (AmE), / ɡʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to shoot someone or something, usually resulting in serious injury or death
to shoot and wound or kill

Examples

In broad daylight, the criminal tried to gun down a rival in front of witnesses.
The soldiers were ordered not to gun down unarmed civilians.
She survived the attempt by thieves to gun down her during the robbery.

Synset Examples

They feared the predator would gun down anything that moved.
In the movie, heroes often gun down their enemies in dramatic battles.
The police had to gun down the suspect after he threatened them.

  • Synonym: shoot - to fire a gun or other weapon at someone or something
