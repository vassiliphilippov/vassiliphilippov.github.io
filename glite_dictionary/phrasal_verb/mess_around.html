to mess around

1. VERB

/ mɛs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / mɛs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time doing random, unplanned activities, often in an idle or aimless way
engage in idle or unplanned activities

Examples

Stop messing around and finish your homework before dinner.
He spent the afternoon messing around in the garage, fixing his bike.
They love to mess around at the beach, playing games and swimming.

Synset Examples

The old lady is usually mucking about in her little house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to muck about - to spend time idly or waste time
  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a playful, silly, or idle way
  • Antonym: work - to engage in productive activity or labor
