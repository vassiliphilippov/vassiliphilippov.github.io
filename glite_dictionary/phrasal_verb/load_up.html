to load up

1. VERB

/ loʊd ʌp / (AmE), / ləʊd ʌp / (BrE)
to fill or put a load onto something, especially a vehicle or container
to fill or put a load onto

Examples

Before leaving for the trip, we loaded up the car with supplies.
The workers loaded up the truck with boxes all morning.
She loads up her shopping cart every time there's a sale.

Synset Examples

load a car
load the truck with hay

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unload - to remove a load from something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01492646-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ loʊd ʌp / (AmE), / ləʊd ʌp / (BrE)
to fill a vehicle, container, or oneself with items, food, or supplies, often until full or abundant
fill a vehicle or oneself abundantly

Examples

We need to load up the truck with boxes before it starts raining.
At parties, he likes to load up his plate with food from the buffet.
Be sure to load up on snacks for the road trip.

Synset Examples

Make sure to load up the car before we leave.
Let's load up our plates at the buffet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fill up - to make full; occupy completely

Synset POS: None
