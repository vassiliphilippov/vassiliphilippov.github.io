to nose out

1. VERB

/ noʊz aʊt / (AmE), / nəʊz aʊt / (BrE)
to recognize, detect, or discover something, especially something hidden, as if by a sense of smell
discover or detect as if by smelling

Examples

The journalists managed to nose out the real story despite attempts to hide it.
Her keen intuition helped her nose out the source of the problem quickly.
Detectives finally nosed out the truth after weeks of investigation.

Synset Examples

He can smell out trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sniff out - to discover or detect by smelling or searching carefully

Etymology

From the noun 'nose' (the organ of smell) + 'out', emphasizing detection as by scent.

2. VERB

/ noʊz aʊt / (AmE), / nəʊz aʊt / (BrE)
to discover or uncover information, especially by searching carefully or in a secretive way
discover or uncover information secretly

Examples

The journalist managed to nose out the truth behind the politician's resignation.
It took weeks to nose out the details of the confidential deal.
Detectives finally nosed out the source of the anonymous tips.

Synset Examples

He managed to nose out the secret project details.
It's hard to nose out information in this office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ferret out - to search out and discover something hidden

Etymology

From the verb 'nose' (to search with one's nose, as an animal), figuratively used for prying or searching.

3. VERB

/ noʊz aʊt / (AmE), / nəʊz aʊt / (BrE)
to discover or uncover the truth or a fact, especially after searching for it carefully
discover or uncover through investigation

Examples

The journalist nosed out the corruption scandal despite attempts to hide it.
After weeks of research, the detective finally nosed out the real culprit.
Reporters quickly nosed out the facts that officials tried to keep secret.

Synset Examples

She nosed out the truth behind the rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discover - to find out something previously unknown
  • Synonym: to detect - to notice or find something, especially something not easily noticed

Etymology

From the verb 'nose', meaning to search or investigate with the nose (like an animal), plus 'out' to indicate finding or uncovering.
