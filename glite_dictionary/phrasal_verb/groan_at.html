to groan at

1. VERB

/ ɡroʊn æt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊn æt / (BrE)
to make a low sound or complain to show annoyance or disapproval of something
express annoyance or disapproval

Examples

The students groaned at the teacher's announcement of a surprise quiz.
Everyone in the room groaned at the corny joke.
She couldn't help but groan at the sight of her overflowing inbox.

Synset Examples

The audience began to groan at the long speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moan about - to complain or grumble about something
  • Antonym: to applaud - to express approval or praise, often by clapping
