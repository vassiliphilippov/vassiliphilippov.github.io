to thrash out

1. VERB

/ θræʃ aʊt / (AmE), / θræʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss a problem or issue thoroughly and forcefully in order to reach a solution or agreement
discuss thoroughly to reach agreement

Examples

We spent several hours trying to thrash out the details of the contract.
The parties finally thrashed out their differences and agreed on a compromise.
They are still thrashing out a solution to the budget crisis.

Synset Examples

The leaders of the various Middle Eastern countries are trying to hammer out a peace agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hammer out - discuss and negotiate a solution in detail
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ θræʃ aʊt / (AmE), / θræʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss something thoroughly and resolve its details or issues, especially in order to reach an agreement
discuss in detail to resolve issues

Examples

The parties spent hours trying to thrash out a suitable compromise.
Before launching the project, the committee will thrash out the plan in detail.
They met late into the night to thrash out their remaining disagreements.

Synset Examples

We need to thrash out the details before signing.
Let's thrash out our differences in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work out - to find a solution to a problem by thinking or discussing
  • Synonym: to iron out - to resolve difficulties or disagreements by discussion
