to boost morale

1. VERB

/ bust məˈræl / (AmE), / buːst məˈrɑːl / (BrE)
to improve the confidence, enthusiasm, or spirit of a person or group
increase confidence or spirits

Examples

The manager organized team-building activities to boost morale among the staff.
Receiving positive feedback from customers helped boost morale throughout the company.
Small gestures, such as praise or recognition, can significantly boost morale in the workplace.

Synset Examples

They infused joj into their strategy to boost morale.
The team needs a rouser to boost morale during tough times.
The team needs a dynamo like her to boost morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise morale - to lift or improve morale; synonym for boost morale
  • Antonym: lower morale - to decrease or diminish morale; antonym for boost morale
