to mill around

1. VERB

/ mɪl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / mɪl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of people) to move around a place in a confused, aimless, or disorganized way, often as part of a crowd.
move about in a confused manner (people)

Examples

Shoppers began to mill around the entrance as the store prepared to open.
Students milled around the hallway waiting for the bell to ring.
Police officers watched as the crowd milled around after the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mingle - to move among others in a group, often talking to them
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01906119-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɪl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / mɪl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move about with no particular purpose or direction, often in a group or crowd
wander aimlessly, usually in groups

Examples

After the assembly ended, students began to mill around the auditorium, chatting and waiting for their friends.
We watched the tourists mill around the entrance to the museum, unsure which way to go.
Security became concerned as a large crowd started to mill around outside the concert venue.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to hang around - spend time in a place doing nothing in particular

Etymology

From 'mill' (to move around slowly or aimlessly), likely referencing the movement of people resembling the turning of a mill.
