to frown on

1. VERB

/ fraʊn ɑn / (AmE), / fraʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to disapprove of something, especially in an official or socially accepted way
disapprove of

Examples

Many companies frown on employees using social media at work.
Smoking is generally frowned on in public places these days.
Parents may frown on staying out late on school nights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disapprove of - regard with disapproval

2. VERB

/ fraʊn ɑn / (AmE), / fraʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to disapprove of or criticize something, especially officially or publicly
disapprove of; criticize

Examples

Most teachers frown on using mobile phones during class.
Some cultures frown on public displays of affection.
The company strongly frowns on late submissions of reports.

Synset Examples

Many parents frown on excessive screen time for their kids.
Experts generally frown on skipping breakfast.
Many employers frown upon degrees from diploma mills.
The community frowns on littering in public parks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disapprove of - to have a negative opinion about something
  • Antonym: approve of - to have a positive opinion about something
