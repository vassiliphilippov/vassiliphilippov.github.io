to hold back

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈbæk / (AmE), / həʊld ˈbæk / (BrE)
to prevent something, such as a feeling or information, from being revealed or noticed by others
to conceal or restrain something

Examples

She tried to hold back her tears during the sad movie.
It's hard to hold back your true feelings when you're upset.
He decided not to hold back any information from his colleagues.

Synset Examples

She conceals her anger well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - prevent something from being seen or known; keep secret
  • Antonym: to reveal - make known to others; allow to be seen
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hoʊld bæk / (AmE), / həʊld bæk / (BrE)
to stop yourself from doing something; to restrain a reaction, impulse, or feeling
refrain from doing something

Examples

She tried to hold back her laughter during the serious meeting.
Despite the pain, he managed to hold back his tears.
Sometimes it's better not to hold back your true feelings.

Synset Examples

she forbore a snicker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to hold something or someone back physically or emotionally
  • Synonym: to forbear - to refrain from an action or expression
  • Antonym: to let go - to stop holding something back; to release control
Source: WordNet

/ hoʊld ˈbæk / (AmE), / həʊld ˈbæk / (BrE)

Examples

Examples
