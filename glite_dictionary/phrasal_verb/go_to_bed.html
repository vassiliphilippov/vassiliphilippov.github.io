to go to bed

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə bɛd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə bɛd / (BrE)
to get into bed in order to sleep
get into bed to sleep

Examples

The children usually go to bed at eight o'clock on school nights.
After watching the late movie, I finally went to bed around 1 a.m.
She felt tired and decided to go to bed early for once.

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn in - to go to bed, esp. to sleep
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00017858-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə bɛd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə bɛd / (BrE)
to lie down in bed with the intention of sleeping
to lie down to sleep

Examples

I'm tired, so I'm going to go to bed early tonight.
Children should go to bed at the same time every night for better sleep.
She usually drinks some tea before she goes to bed.

Synset Examples

He decided to go to bed with a book instead of watching TV.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to get up - to rise from bed after sleeping
