to take someone to

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈsʌmwʌn tuː / (AmE), / teɪk ˈsʌmwən tʊ / (BrE)
to accompany and bring a person to a particular place, usually by leading or guiding them there
bring someone to a place

Examples

Can you take someone to the hospital if they feel unwell?
Teachers often take someone to the library for reading sessions.
He offered to take someone to the airport early in the morning.

Synset Examples

Daddy promised to take us to the zoo this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring someone to - to cause someone to come to a place
