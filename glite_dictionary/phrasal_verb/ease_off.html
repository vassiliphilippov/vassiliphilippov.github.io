to ease off

1. VERB

/ iːz ɔf / (AmE), / iːz ɒf / (BrE)
to become less intense, severe, or strong; to decrease gradually
become less intense

Examples

The storm is expected to ease off by this evening, making travel safer.
Once the pain began to ease off, she was able to get out of bed.
The pressure from my boss finally eased off after I finished the project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to intensify - to become more intense; to increase in intensity
  • Synonym: to subside - to become less intense, violent, or severe
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00156293-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ iz ɔf / (AmE), / iːz ɒf / (BrE)
to gradually reduce pressure, intensity, or speed; to become less severe or forceful.
reduce pressure, intensity, or speed

Examples

As the pain began to ease off, she felt more comfortable moving her arm.
You should ease off the brakes when driving on icy roads to avoid skidding.
The rain finally started to ease off after several hours of heavy downpour.

Synset Examples

he eased off the gas pedal and the car slowed down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slacken - make or become slower or less active
  • Antonym: to intensify - to become stronger or more intense
