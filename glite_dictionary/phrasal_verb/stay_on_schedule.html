to stay on schedule

1. VERB

/ steɪ ɑn ˈskɛdʒuːl / (AmE), / steɪ ɒn ˈʃɛdjuːl / (BrE)
to continue to follow a planned timetable without delay
keep to a planned timetable

Examples

It's important for construction projects to stay on schedule to avoid extra costs.
Despite the heavy traffic, the train managed to stay on schedule.
She worked late every night to stay on schedule with her assignments.

Synset Examples

Make sure to stay on schedule for the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with a schedule or deadline
