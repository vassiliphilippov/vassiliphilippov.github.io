to step forward

1. VERB

/ stɛp ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / stɛp ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to move into a position where one is noticed or can take responsibility for something; to volunteer or take action
come forward; volunteer or take action

Examples

During the meeting, she was the first to step forward and offer a solution.
Police are asking witnesses to step forward with any information about the incident.
Many volunteers have stepped forward to help after the disaster.

Synset Examples

Young people should step to the fore and help their peers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come forward - to present oneself, especially voluntarily
  • Antonym: to hang back - to be unwilling to take action or become involved
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02093441-v

2. VERB

/ stɛp ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / stɛp ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to offer to help or take responsibility, especially when others have not
offer to help or take responsibility

Examples

When nobody else would help, John decided to step forward and lead the team.
Several witnesses stepped forward to give their statements to the police.
We're looking for someone to step forward and organize the charity event.

Synset Examples

She decided to step forward and share her story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volunteer - to offer to do something willingly without being forced or paid
  • Antonym: to hold back - to hesitate or not offer oneself for something
