to look squarely at

1. VERB

/ lʊk ˈskwɛrli æt / (AmE), / lʊk ˈskweəli ət / (BrE)
to confront or address directly, without avoidance or hesitation
confront or address directly

Examples

He looked squarely at his opponent and calmly stated his opinion.
It is important to look squarely at your mistakes in order to learn from them.
The committee finally decided to look squarely at the financial problems facing the organization.

Synset Examples

He looked squarely at the issue without hesitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face up to - to confront something difficult or unpleasant directly
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or not confront
