to rush out

1. VERB

/ rʌʃ aʊt / (AmE), / rʌʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to jump or move quickly out from a place of hiding, often to surprise or attack someone
jump or move suddenly from hiding

Examples

The robber rushed out from behind the door and startled the security guard.
As soon as the bell rang, the children rushed out from their hiding spots.
Suddenly, a cat rushed out from under the car, nearly scaring me to death.

Synset Examples

The attackers leapt out from the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to leap out - to jump out suddenly from hiding
