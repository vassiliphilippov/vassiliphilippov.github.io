to fetch up

1. VERB

/ fɛtʃ ʌp / (AmE), / fɛtʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to finally arrive, end up, or reach a particular state, especially unexpectedly or after a series of events
to end up or arrive somewhere unexpectedly
british

Examples

After days of wandering through the countryside, they finally fetched up at a small inn.
She never planned to live abroad, but somehow she fetched up in Paris.
We took the wrong turn, and fetched up miles from where we intended to be.

Synset Examples

He ended up marrying his high school sweetheart
he wound up being unemployed and living at home again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to end up - to finally be in a particular place or state
  • Synonym: to wind up - to eventually arrive in a situation, place, or state

Etymology

Related to the verb 'fetch' (to go and bring back), extended figuratively by the addition of 'up' to mean arriving at a result or place.

2. VERB

/ fɛtʃ ʌp / (AmE), / fɛtʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to find or recover something, especially unexpectedly or by bringing it to light
find or recover something unexpectedly

Examples

After searching the attic, she managed to fetch up a box of forgotten letters.
They always seem to fetch up something valuable during their explorations.
I hope to fetch up that missing document before the meeting.

Synset Examples

He managed to fetch up some interesting stories.
I hope to fetch up some old photos.
They always fetch up strange artifacts on their digs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unearth - to discover or dig up something hidden, especially from the ground
  • Synonym: to recover - to find or regain possession of something lost or hidden
