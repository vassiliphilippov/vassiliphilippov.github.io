to bowl over

1. VERB

/ boʊl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / bəʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to cause something to overturn from an upright or normal position, usually by hitting or striking it
knock over something

Examples

The strong wind bowled over several trash cans on the street.
She accidentally bowled over a stack of books while cleaning.
The football player bowled over the cones during practice.

Synset Examples

The cat knocked over the flower vase
the clumsy customer turned over the vase
he tumped over his beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock over - to overturn something by pushing or hitting it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01913971-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ boʊl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / bəʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to astonish or greatly impress someone; to overwhelm with amazement
to astonish or greatly impress

Examples

The director's new film really bowled over the critics.
I was completely bowled over by the generosity of our hosts.
Her incredible singing voice bowls over everyone who hears it.

Synset Examples

This boggles the mind!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amaze - to surprise someone greatly; to fill with astonishment
  • Synonym: to stun - to shock or overwhelm someone, especially with surprise
  • Antonym: underwhelm - to fail to impress or excite someone

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'bowl over' (to knock over, as in bowling), used figuratively to mean 'overwhelm' or 'astonish'.
