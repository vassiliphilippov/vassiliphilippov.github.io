to act like a dorkus

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈdɔrkəs / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈdɔːkəs / (BrE)
to behave in an awkward, silly, or foolish way, often in a manner that attracts mild ridicule
behave awkwardly or foolishly
informal slang american

Examples

Try not to act like a dorkus at the party tonight.
He always acts like a dorkus when he's nervous.
Samantha realized she had acted like a dorkus during her presentation.

Synset Examples

He acted like a dorkus when he met his idol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a fool of oneself - to behave in a way that makes one look silly

Etymology

Variant of 'dork', US slang for a socially awkward person, combined with the mock-suffix '-us' for humor.
