to get even

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈivən / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈiːvən / (BrE)
to take revenge on someone, especially for a perceived wrong; to retaliate or settle a score
take revenge; retaliate

Examples

After losing the match, she was determined to get even with her opponent next time.
He played a practical joke on me, so I decided to get even by hiding his phone.
It's never healthy to try to get even, but sometimes the temptation is strong.

Synset Examples

I cannot accept the defeat — I want to get even

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take revenge - to inflict harm in return for a wrong
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈivən / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈiːvən / (BrE)
to take revenge on someone, especially after being wronged or harmed by them
take revenge; retaliate

Examples

After years of bullying, Jill finally found a way to get even with her tormentors.
He was determined to get even after his coworker sabotaged his project.
They plotted together, each eager to get even for the insult.

Synset Examples

She plotted a way to get even after the betrayal.
He just wants to get even with those who wronged him.
They vowed to get even for the unfair treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retaliate - to make an attack in return for a similar attack; take revenge
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense; the opposite of taking revenge
