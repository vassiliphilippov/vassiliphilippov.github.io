to sit up

1. VERB

/ sɪt ʌp / (AmE), / sɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to stay awake and not go to bed, often later than usual or expected
stay awake past usual bedtime

Examples

We decided to sit up and wait for our daughter to come home.
She often sits up late reading her favorite books.
He sat up all night to finish his assignment.

Synset Examples

Don't stay up so late — you have to go to work tomorrow
We sat up all night to watch the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay up - to remain awake and not go to bed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00020252-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɪt ʌp / (AmE), / sɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to move from a lying or leaning position to an upright sitting position
move to an upright sitting position

Examples

He woke up suddenly and sat up in bed.
When the nurse entered, the patient tried to sit up unaided.
The toddler giggled as she sat up for the first time.

Synset Examples

He sat up in bed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lie down - to place oneself in a horizontal position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02102958-v
Synset POS: v
