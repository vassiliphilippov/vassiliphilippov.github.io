to stand up against

1. VERB

/ stænd ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / stand ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to resist or oppose someone or something, especially in the face of unfairness, pressure, or hostility
resist or oppose

Examples

She was the only one who stood up against the new company policy.
Many people have stood up against discrimination in their communities.
He is known for standing up against corruption throughout his career.

Synset Examples

She stood up against the baldist comments in the group.
She stood up against homophobic bullying in her school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand up to - to confront or refuse to accept unfair treatment from someone
