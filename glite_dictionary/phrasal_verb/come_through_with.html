to come through with

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈθru wɪθ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈθruː wɪð / (BrE)
to deliver or provide something that was promised or expected
deliver as promised

Examples

Despite his doubts, he managed to come through with the information we needed.
We were worried the supplier wouldn't come through with the shipment, but it arrived on time.
She always comes through with her promises, no matter how difficult.

Synset Examples

They promised to come through with the funds soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deliver - to bring or hand over as promised or expected
  • Antonym: to fail - to not do or provide something that was expected
