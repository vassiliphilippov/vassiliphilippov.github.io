to carry over

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to move something from one place, time, or condition to another, especially when it is continued or transferred beyond its original limits
transfer from one place, time, or state to another

Examples

Unfinished tasks from Monday will be carried over to Tuesday's schedule.
Some traditions are carried over from one generation to the next.
The positive balance will carry over into the next financial year.

Synset Examples

Adam would have been carried over into the life eternal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - to move from one place to another
  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay or move to a later time

2. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to keep goods unsold in one season for sale in the next season
keep goods for sale in next season

Examples

The store decided to carry over its unsold winter coats to the next year.
It is common for retailers to carry over surplus inventory into the following season.
Due to weak sales, they had to carry over many items from last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold over - to keep something for future use or consideration

/ ˈkæri ˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
