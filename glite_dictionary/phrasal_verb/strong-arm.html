to strong-arm

1. VERB

/ ˈstrɔŋ ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋ ɑːm / (BrE)
to use force or threats to make someone do something; to bully or coerce someone
use force or threats to coerce

Examples

The company tried to strong-arm its supplier into lowering prices.
He attempted to strong-arm his way into the leadership position.
She refused to be strong-armed into signing the agreement.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to intimidate or mistreat someone with force or threats
  • Synonym: to coerce - to make someone do something by force or threats

Etymology

From 'strong' + 'arm', referring metaphorically to using physical power or force.

2. VERB

/ ˈstrɔŋ ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋ ɑːm / (BrE)
to use force or threats to make someone do something
use force or threats to coerce

Examples

The union claimed the company tried to strong-arm workers into signing the new contract.
She refused to be strong-armed into making a decision she wasn't comfortable with.
Politicians sometimes strong-arm their opponents to gain an advantage.

Synset Examples

He was strong-armed by the policemen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by use of power or threats
  • Antonym: to persuade - to cause someone to do something by reasoning or argument
