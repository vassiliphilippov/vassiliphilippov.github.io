to settle down

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬l daʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl daʊn / (BrE)
to become quiet, calm, or peaceful after being agitated, excited, or disturbed
become calm after disturbance

Examples

It took the children a few minutes to settle down after playing outside.
The crowd began to settle down once the speaker took the stage.
After moving into their new home, things finally started to settle down.

Synset Examples

After the fight both men need to cool off.
It took a while after the baby was born for things to settle down again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - become calmer or less agitated
  • Antonym: get worked up - become agitated or excited

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛtl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl daʊn / (BrE)
(of a substance) to rest, come to rest, or accumulate in a position, typically on a surface or the ground.
come to rest on a surface

Examples

After the storm, a fine layer of dust settled down on the parked cars.
The snow settled down quietly over the streets during the night.
Tiny particles from the construction site eventually settled down on every surface in the house.

Synset Examples

dust settled on the roofs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sink - to move downward, especially slowly or by gravity

3. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬l daʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl daʊn / (BrE)
to begin to live a quiet and steady life by getting a permanent job, getting married, or establishing a home
begin a stable or permanent life

Examples

After years of traveling, he decided it was time to settle down and start a family.
Many people feel pressure to settle down by a certain age, but it's different for everyone.
She bought a house because she was ready to settle down in one place.

Synset Examples

He finally settled down

Related Terms
