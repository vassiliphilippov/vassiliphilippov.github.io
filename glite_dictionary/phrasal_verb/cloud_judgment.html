to cloud judgment

1. VERB

/ klaʊd ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / klaʊd ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
to make someone unable to think clearly or make good decisions, often due to emotions or external influences
impair clear thinking or decision-making

Examples

Don't let anger cloud judgment when making important decisions.
Her personal feelings threatened to cloud judgment during the negotiations.
Alcohol can easily cloud judgment and lead to poor choices.

Synset Examples

Idolatry can cloud judgment and distort reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impair - to weaken or damage something, especially a faculty or function
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand or free from confusion
