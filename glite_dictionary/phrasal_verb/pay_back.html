to pay back

1. VERB

/ peɪ bæk / (AmE), / peɪ bæk / (BrE)
to take vengeance on someone or get even for a wrong or injury
take revenge or get even

Examples

After years of rude treatment, she finally decided to pay back her mean colleague.
He vowed to pay back those who had betrayed him.
They plotted together to pay back the team that had beaten them last season.

Synset Examples

We'll get them!
That'll fix him good!
This time I got him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get even - take revenge or retaliate
  • Antonym: to forgive - stop feeling angry or resentful; not seek revenge

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ peɪ bæk / (AmE), / peɪ bæk / (BrE)
to act in return for someone's behavior, often by giving similar treatment or recognition; to reciprocate or retaliate
to reciprocate or retaliate

Examples

She decided to pay back his kindness by helping him when he needed it.
He swore he would pay back anyone who had wronged him.
It took her years to finally pay back all the favors she had received.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repay - to give back money that is owed
  • Synonym: revenge - to inflict harm in return for harm received
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02349377-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ peɪ bæk / (AmE), / peɪ bæk / (BrE)
to return money that you have borrowed from someone
return borrowed money

Examples

I'll pay back the $50 you lent me next week.
She always makes sure to pay back her debts promptly.
He forgot to pay back the money he borrowed from his friend.

Synset Examples

I promise to pay back the money I borrowed.
She forgot to pay back the loan she took.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repay - to pay back money that you have borrowed
