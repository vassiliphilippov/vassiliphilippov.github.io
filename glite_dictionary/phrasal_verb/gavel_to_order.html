to gavel to order

1. VERB

/ ˈɡævəl tə ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈɡævəl tʊ ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to officially start a meeting or session by striking a gavel, typically to call for attention or commence proceedings
open a meeting using a gavel

Examples

The chairman gaveled to order at precisely 9 a.m. to begin the annual conference.
As soon as the mayor gavels to order, all discussion must cease and the agenda starts.
The judge gaveled to order the hearing, signaling that the proceedings were officially underway.

Synset Examples

The councilman gavel to order the session promptly.
Once the speaker gavel to order, the meeting began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call to order - to officially begin a meeting, often by speaking or using a gavel

Etymology

From 'gavel,' meaning a ceremonial mallet, combined with 'to order,' meaning to bring to an ordered state or begin officially.
