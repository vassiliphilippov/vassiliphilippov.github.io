to catch a cold

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ə koʊld / (AmE), / katʃ ə kəʊld / (BrE)
to become ill with a cold, a mild viral infection that affects the nose and throat
become ill with a cold

Examples

If you don't wear a hat in winter, you might catch a cold.
She stayed home all week after she caught a cold.
He was worried that swimming in the rain would make him catch a cold.

Synset Examples

Make sure to wash your hands to not catch a cold.
In winter, he couldn't catch a cold even outdoors.
He always seems to catch a cold in winter.
Don’t go out without a jacket, or you’ll catch a cold.
She said he couldn't catch a cold if he tried.
With his immune system, he couldn't catch a cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come down with - to start to suffer from an illness
