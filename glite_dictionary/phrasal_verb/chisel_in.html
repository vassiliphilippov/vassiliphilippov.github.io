to chisel in

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪzəl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪzəl ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt or break into a conversation, often abruptly or without invitation
interrupt or break into a conversation
informal

Examples

It's rude to chisel in when others are talking without waiting for a pause.
She didn't hesitate to chisel in with her opinion during the heated debate.
No one likes it when you chisel in on their private conversation.

Synset Examples

her husband always chimes in, even when he is not involved in the conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of an activity or process, especially speech
  • Synonym: to butt in - to interrupt a conversation or activity rudely or unwisely
