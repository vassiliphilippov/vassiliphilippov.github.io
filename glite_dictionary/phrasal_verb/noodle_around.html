to noodle around

1. VERB

/ ˈnudl̩ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈnuːdl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to play casually or improvise without a serious purpose, especially on a musical instrument
play or improvise casually
informal

Examples

He likes to noodle around on his guitar in the evenings after work.
Instead of practicing scales, she spent the afternoon noodling around on the piano.
The band members would often noodle around before their rehearsal officially began.

Synset Examples

He spent hours just noodling around on the piano.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess around - to behave in a playful or aimless way
  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously without preparation

Etymology

Probably from US slang 'noodle', meaning to play or fool around, origin uncertain.
