to feed back

1. VERB

/ fid bæk / (AmE), / fiːd bæk / (BrE)
to submit information or data again to a program or automatic system for further processing or analysis.
resubmit information to a system

Examples

After making corrections, she had to feed back the updated file into the database.
The system asks you to feed back your answer for further analysis.
Engineers will feed back the sensor data to improve the model's accuracy.

2. VERB

/ fid ˈbæk / (AmE), / fiːd ˈbæk / (BrE)
to give a response or share information in reaction to a query, result, or process
give a response or reaction

Examples

Please feed back any comments you have about the new website.
The system allows employees to feed back their suggestions directly to management.
We fed back our initial observations to the research team after the first round of testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to respond - to say or do something as a reaction to something that has been said or done
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone
