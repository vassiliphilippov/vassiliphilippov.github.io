to watch over

1. VERB

/ wɑtʃ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / wɒtʃ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to observe closely or pay careful attention to someone or something, often to ensure safety or progress
observe or monitor closely

Examples

Can you watch over my bag while I go to the restroom?
She was assigned to watch over the children on the field trip.
Security cameras watch over the entrance day and night.

Synset Examples

Keep an eye on the baby, please!
The world is watching Sarajevo
She followed the men with the binoculars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor - observe and check the progress or quality of something over a period of time
  • Synonym: to oversee - supervise or watch over work or people

2. VERB

/ wɑtʃ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / wɒtʃ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to take care of someone or something and make sure that nothing bad happens to them; to supervise or look after
take care of or supervise

Examples

Could you watch over my laptop while I step out for a minute?
Parents are expected to watch over their children during the field trip.
The security guard watches over the building throughout the night.

Synset Examples

She promised to watch over the kids at the park.
Can you watch over my bag while I shop?
The dog will watch over the house at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look after - to take care of someone or something
  • Synonym: to supervise - to oversee and direct the work or actions of others
