to go on

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɑn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒn / (BrE)
to continue moving forward or to progress, especially in a metaphorical sense; to persist or proceed
continue or move forward

Examples

As the meeting dragged on, she hoped the discussion would go on in a more productive direction.
Despite several setbacks, the project must go on until it is finished.
Seasons go on regardless of what happens in our lives.

Synset Examples

Time marches on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process without stopping
  • Antonym: stop - to cease movement or activity; the opposite of continuing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01996535-v
Synset POS: v

/ ɡoʊ ɑn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒn / (BrE)
