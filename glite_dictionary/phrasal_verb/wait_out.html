to wait out

1. VERB

/ weɪt aʊt / (AmE), / weɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to stay where you are or in a situation until something unpleasant or unwanted has ended or passed
remain until something ends

Examples

We decided to wait out the storm in a nearby café.
Some investors prefer to wait out market downturns rather than sell their stocks.
Let's wait out the rain before heading back to the car.

Synset Examples

They will wait out the traffic before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endure - to patiently withstand something difficult
  • Antonym: to give in - to stop resisting and allow something to happen
