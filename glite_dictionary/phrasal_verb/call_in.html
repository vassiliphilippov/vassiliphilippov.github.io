to call in

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to request the return of something, especially a product, often because it is faulty or unsafe
request the return of a product

Examples

The company decided to call in all the toys after receiving complaints about safety issues.
The bank may call in the loan if the conditions are not met.
After discovering a fault, the manufacturer had to call in thousands of cars for inspection.
