to tell on

1. VERB

/ tɛl ɑn / (AmE), / tɛl ɒn / (BrE)
to have a noticeable or damaging effect, especially by causing physical or emotional strain
have a damaging or noticeable effect

Examples

The harsh winter weather began to tell on the health of the elderly.
Years of heavy work eventually told on his back, causing constant pain.
The stress of the job really tells on her after a long week.

Synset Examples

Each step told on his tired legs
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00137889-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɛl ɑn / (AmE), / tɛl ɒn / (BrE)
to inform an authority or another person about someone's wrongdoing, usually resulting in that person getting in trouble
report someone's misdeed to authority

Examples

If you don't stop teasing me, I'll tell on you to the teacher.
He told on his little brother for breaking the window.
Children sometimes hesitate to tell on their friends, even if they know it's the right thing to do.

Synset Examples

He told on his classmate who had cheated on the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitch - to secretly inform on someone
  • Synonym: to betray - to reveal someone's secret or wrongdoing to others
