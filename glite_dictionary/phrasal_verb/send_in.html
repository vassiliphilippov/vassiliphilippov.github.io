to send in

1. VERB

/ sɛnd ɪn / (AmE), / send ɪn / (BrE)
to submit or deliver something, especially by mail or electronically, to an organization or authority
submit or deliver (esp. by mail)

Examples

Please send in your completed application forms by Friday.
Contestants are encouraged to send in their entries as soon as possible.
She sent in her resignation letter last week.

Synset Examples

Send in your comments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submit - to present or propose something for consideration, decision, or action
  • Antonym: to send out - to distribute or dispatch to others

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛnd ɪn / (AmE), / sɛnd ɪn / (BrE)
to present one's name or calling card to a servant when making a formal visit, as a way of announcing oneself to the host
announce oneself by presenting a name or card on a formal visit
archaic

Examples

In Victorian times, it was customary to send in your calling card before being admitted.
She asked the footman to send in Mr. Brown's name to the hostess.
Before entering the drawing room, guests would send in their cards to announce their presence.
