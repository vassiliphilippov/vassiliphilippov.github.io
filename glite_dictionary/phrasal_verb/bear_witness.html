to bear witness

1. VERB

/ bɛr ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / beə ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
to give evidence or confirm the truth of something; to testify to a fact, often based on personal experience or knowledge
give evidence; testify to something

Examples

Many villagers bore witness to the remarkable event in the square.
His calm demeanor bears witness to his years of meditation practice.
The scars on the walls bear witness to the building's turbulent past.

Synset Examples

The blood test showed that he was the father
Her behavior testified to her incompetence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to testify - to give evidence as a witness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01017253-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɛr ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / bɛə ˈwɪtnɪs / (BrE)
to give formal testimony or evidence, especially in a court of law.
give legal testimony

Examples

Several experts bear witness during the trial to support the prosecution's case.
The doctor bore witness to the victim's injuries in court.
She has borne witness on behalf of dozens of clients over her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to testify - to give evidence as a witness in court
