to withdraw support

1. VERB

/ wɪðˈdrɔ səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔː səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to stop providing help, approval, or backing for someone or something
cease providing help or backing

Examples

Several key donors decided to withdraw support after the controversy surfaced.
If we withdraw support now, the program may not survive the year.
The company threatened to withdraw support for the event unless changes were made.

Synset Examples

They plan to withdraw their support for the project.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: support - to give help or approval to someone or something
