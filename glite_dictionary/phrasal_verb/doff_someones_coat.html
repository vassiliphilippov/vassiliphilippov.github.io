to doff someone's coat

1. VERB

/ dɑf sʌmz wʌnz koʊt / (AmE), / dɒf sʌmz wʌnz kəʊt / (BrE)
to take off or remove one's coat
remove one's coat

Examples

She doffed her coat as soon as she got inside.
After arriving at the party, he politely doffed his coat and handed it to the attendant.
The guests doffed their coats and settled into the warm living room.

Synset Examples

She doffed her coat as soon as she got inside.

Etymology

From 'doff', contracted form of 'do off'; 'doff' dates from Middle English.
