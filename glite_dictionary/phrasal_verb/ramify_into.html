to ramify into

1. VERB

/ ˈræməˌfaɪ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈramɪfʌɪ ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to separate or spread into multiple related branches, areas, or issues
divide or spread into branches or areas

Examples

The investigation began to ramify into various unexpected fields of research.
Her responsibilities ramified into project management and team leadership after her promotion.
Economic impacts of the policy may ramify into diverse sectors of society over time.

Synset Examples

The issues began to ramify into several smaller problems.
Their plan will ramify into different departments soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to branch out - to extend in new directions or areas

Etymology

From Latin ramificare (to form branches), with 'into' indicating the direction of branching or division.
