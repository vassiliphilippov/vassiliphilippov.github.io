to conjure up

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑndʒər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkʌndʒə ʌp / (BrE)
to bring something into existence or to mind, often as if by magic or with imagination
bring into existence or to mind as if by magic

Examples

The smell of fresh bread can conjure up memories of childhood for many people.
The magician seemed to conjure up a rabbit out of thin air.
Reading that poem always conjures up a sense of nostalgia in me.

Synset Examples

raise the specter of unemployment
he conjured wild birds in the air
call down the spirits from the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
  • Synonym: to summon - to call forth or bring something into existence

Etymology

From Middle English 'conjuren', from Old French 'conjurer', from Latin 'conjurare' (to swear together).
