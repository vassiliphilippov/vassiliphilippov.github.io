to bridge over

1. VERB

/ brɪdʒ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / brɪdʒ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to make a connection between two things or people, or to reduce the gap or differences between them
make a connection or reduce a gap

Examples

Efforts to bridge over the cultural differences took months of open discussion.
The new highway will bridge over the river, making travel much easier.
His diplomatic skills helped to bridge over the disagreements between the two companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join together or link
  • Antonym: separate - to keep apart or set apart

Etymology

From 'bridge' (noun, a structure for crossing) used as a verb, plus the preposition 'over'.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brɪdʒ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / brɪdʒ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to provide or be enough to cover a gap or difficult period between two times or conditions
suffice to cover an interim period

Examples

The extra funding will bridge over the company’s finances until the next quarter.
We had to bridge over a difficult period before the new project began.
Her temporary job helped bridge over the gap between graduation and full-time employment.

Synset Examples

This money will keep us going for another year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tide over - to help someone through a difficult period by providing what they need

Etymology

From 'bridge' (to span a gap) + 'over', modeling the idea of creating a connection across a period of need.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02629427-v
Synset POS: v
