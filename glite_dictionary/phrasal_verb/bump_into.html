to bump into

1. VERB

/ bʌmp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / bʌmp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to collide with someone or something unexpectedly, often with some force
collide accidentally with someone or something

Examples

She wasn't looking where she was going and bumped into the doorframe.
The cyclist lost control and bumped into a parked car.
He apologized after he accidentally bumped into me on the crowded sidewalk.

Synset Examples

I ran into the telephone pole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash into - collide violently with something
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from hitting or colliding with something

2. VERB

/ bʌmp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / bʌmp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to meet someone by chance or unexpectedly, especially somewhere in public
meet someone by chance

Examples

I was surprised to bump into my old teacher at the grocery store.
You never know who you might bump into while traveling abroad.
She bumped into her childhood friend during the conference.
