to brick in

1. VERB

/ brɪk ɪn / (AmE), / brɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to close off or enclose a space by building a wall of bricks across it
close off with bricks

Examples

After the renovations, they decided to brick in the old fireplace to modernize the room.
The window was bricked in years ago, leaving no natural light in the basement.
He spent the weekend bricking in the doorway to create a new storage space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wall up - to close or seal with a wall; to block off by building a wall

2. VERB

/ brɪk ɪn / (AmE), / brɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to fill in or close an opening with bricks, especially by building them across it; to enclose or incorporate something in a fixed position using bricks.
fill or enclose with bricks

Examples

After the renovations, they decided to brick in the old fireplace for added insulation.
The architect wanted to brick in the unused window to match the style of the new extension.
Workers will brick in the archway to create a more private room.
