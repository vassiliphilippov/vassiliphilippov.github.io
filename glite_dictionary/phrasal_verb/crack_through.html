to crack through

1. VERB

/ kræk θruː / (AmE), / kræk θruː / (BrE)
to work quickly and efficiently on a task, especially to complete it within a short time or under pressure
work quickly and efficiently
informal

Examples

We have a lot to do, so let's crack through these reports as quickly as possible.
She managed to crack through all her assignments before the weekend.
If we crack through this work now, we can finish early and relax.

Synset Examples

Let's crack through this project to meet our deadline.
We need to crack through the paperwork before the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to power through - to continue doing something difficult or strenuous with determination
