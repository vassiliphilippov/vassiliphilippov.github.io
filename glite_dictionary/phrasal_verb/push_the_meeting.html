to push the meeting

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ðə ˈmiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / pʊʃ ðə ˈmiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
to reschedule a meeting for a later time or date
reschedule a meeting later

Examples

Due to a scheduling conflict, we need to push the meeting to Friday afternoon.
Let's push the meeting back an hour so everyone can attend.
They decided to push the meeting to next week because several team members were unavailable.
