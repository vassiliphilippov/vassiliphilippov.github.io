to impose a fine

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpoʊz ə faɪn / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊz ə faɪn / (BrE)
to officially order someone to pay a financial penalty as punishment for breaking a rule or law
officially order someone to pay a penalty

Examples

The council decided to impose a fine on those who park illegally.
The referee may impose a fine for any unsportsmanlike behavior.
Failure to comply with the rules could lead authorities to impose a fine on the organization.

Synset Examples

They decided to impose a fine for late submissions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levy a fine - to officially demand the payment of a fine
