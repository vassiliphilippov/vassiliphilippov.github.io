to crowd out

1. VERB

/ kraʊd aʊt / (AmE), / kraʊd aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something out of a place or position by taking up too much space or by being too numerous or strong
force out by taking up space

Examples

Rising rents may crowd out small businesses in the city center.
Invasive species can crowd out native plants in an ecosystem.
Her loud voice seemed to crowd out all other conversation in the room.

Synset Examples

The weeds crowded out the flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to displace - to take the place of something or someone
  • Synonym: to push out - to force something or someone out

2. VERB

/ kraʊd aʊt / (AmE), / kraʊd aʊt / (BrE)
to force something or someone out of a place or situation by being more numerous or prominent; to displace or eliminate by overwhelming presence
displace or force out by overwhelming presence

Examples

The new shopping mall may crowd out local businesses in the area.
Invasive plant species often crowd out native vegetation in the ecosystem.
Rising property prices can crowd out young families from the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

New developments often crowd out green spaces.
High rents can crowd out local businesses.
The loud music seemed to crowd out our conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to displace - to take the place of something or someone
  • Synonym: to replace - to take the place of or substitute for
  • Antonym: to allow in - to permit the presence or entry of
