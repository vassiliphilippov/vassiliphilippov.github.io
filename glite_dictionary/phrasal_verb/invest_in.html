to invest in

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɛst ɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛst ɪn / (BrE)
to put money, time, or effort into something in the hope of achieving a profit or benefit
put resources into for profit or benefit

Examples

Many people choose to invest in real estate as a way to build wealth.
She decided to invest in new equipment to expand her business.
Tech companies often invest in research and development to stay competitive.

Synset Examples

His stupidy led him to invest in a failing company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invest - to put money, effort, or time into something to get a return
