to catch a wave

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ə weɪv / (AmE), / katʃ ə weɪv / (BrE)
to ride on a moving wave, especially as part of the sport of surfing
to surf or ride a wave

Examples

Every morning, she wakes up early to catch a wave before work.
Do you know how to catch a wave when the tide is right?
He tried for hours but couldn't catch a wave until the wind picked up.

Synset Examples

She loves to catch a wave every summer.
They all waited to catch a wave at the beach.
He managed to catch a wave just before sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surf - to ride on ocean waves using a surfboard
