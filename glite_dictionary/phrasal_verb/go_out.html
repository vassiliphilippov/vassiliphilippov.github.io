to go out

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to leave your home or another building, especially in order to do something social or for leisure
leave home to go somewhere

Examples

Let's go out for dinner tonight instead of cooking at home.
She barely went out during the winter because of the cold.
They enjoy going out with friends on weekends.

Synset Examples

We never went out when our children were small

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stay in - to remain at home instead of going out
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

/ ɡoʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
(of a light, fire, etc.) to stop burning or shining; to become extinguished.

Examples
