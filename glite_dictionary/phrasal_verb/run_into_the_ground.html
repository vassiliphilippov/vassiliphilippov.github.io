to run into the ground

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈɪntu ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / rʌn ˈɪntuː ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to ruin or exhaust something through excessive use, mismanagement, or overexploitation
exhaust or ruin through overuse

Examples

The manager ran into the ground a once-successful company within two years.
She warned her team not to run into the ground a promising new strategy.
If you keep using that old laptop, you're going to run it into the ground.

Synset Examples

They ran the project into the ground with poor management.
He ran the car into the ground before selling it.
Don't run the idea into the ground before it even starts.
