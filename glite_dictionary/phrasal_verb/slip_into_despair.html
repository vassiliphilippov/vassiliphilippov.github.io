to slip into despair

1. VERB

/ slɪp ˈɪntu dɪˈspɛr / (AmE), / slɪp ˈɪntə dɪˈspɛə / (BrE)
to gradually begin to feel hopeless or lose hope, often without realizing it immediately
to gradually fall into hopelessness

Examples

After losing his job, he began to slip into despair despite support from friends.
It's easy to slip into despair during difficult times if you don't reach out for help.
She realized she was slipping into despair and decided to seek professional guidance.

Synset Examples

She tried not to slip into despair after the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall into despair - to become hopeless
  • Antonym: recover hope - to regain hope after losing it
