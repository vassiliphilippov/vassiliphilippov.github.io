to beat on

1. VERB

/ bit ɑn / (AmE), / biːt ɒn / (BrE)
to continue or persist with an activity, especially in the face of difficulty; or to keep making a steady, often rhythmic, noise.
continue with something; keep making a rhythmic noise

Examples

Despite the cold, the hikers decided to beat on toward the summit.
The rain beat on the roof for hours, making sleep difficult.
Even when faced with setbacks, she chose to beat on and pursue her dreams.

Synset Examples

Despite the rain, they chose to beat on with their plans.
The drums continued to beat on throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly in an activity or opinion
  • Antonym: to cease - to stop or bring to an end
