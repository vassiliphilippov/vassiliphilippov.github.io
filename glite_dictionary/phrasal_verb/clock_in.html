to clock in

1. VERB

/ klɑk ɪn / (AmE), / klɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to record the time of one's arrival at work by using a special machine or system
register arrival time at work

Examples

All employees must clock in as soon as they arrive at the factory.
She forgot to clock in this morning and had to inform her supervisor.
New staff members are trained on how to clock in using the automated system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clock out - to register the time of leaving work

Etymology

From 'clock' (referring to a time-recording device) + 'in' (indicating entry). Usage dates from late 19th–early 20th century workplace practices.

2. VERB

/ klɑk ɪn / (AmE), / klɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to record the time of one's arrival at work, typically using a clock or electronic system
record time of arrival at work

Examples

All employees must clock in before starting their shift.
She forgot to clock in this morning and had to tell her supervisor.
Many companies now use an app for staff to clock in and out.

Synset Examples
