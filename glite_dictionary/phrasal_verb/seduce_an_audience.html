to seduce an audience

1. VERB

/ sɪˈdus ən ˈɑdiəns / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːs ən ˈɔːdiəns / (BrE)
to captivate or attract the attention and admiration of an audience, often through charm, skill, or performance
captivate or charm an audience

Examples

The charismatic speaker knew exactly how to seduce an audience with her powerful words.
His humor and warmth helped him seduce an audience every time he stepped on stage.
She practiced for hours to learn how to seduce an audience with her violin performances.

Synset Examples

She knew how to seduce an audience with her storytelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the attention of
  • Antonym: bore an audience - to cause an audience to lose interest

Etymology

From Latin 'seducere' (to lead aside); figurative use of 'seduce' to mean attract or captivate.
