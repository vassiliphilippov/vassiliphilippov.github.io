to keep apart

1. VERB

/ kip əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / kiːp əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to prevent two or more people or things from coming together or mixing; to separate or isolate.
prevent from mixing; separate

Examples

The teacher tried to keep apart the two students who were arguing.
Please keep apart the clean and dirty laundry.
They were advised to keep apart during the quarantine period.

Synset Examples

The dentist sequesters the tooth he is working on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to cause to move or be apart; to divide
  • Synonym: to isolate - to cause something or someone to be alone or kept apart from others
  • Antonym: bring together - to cause people or things to come together
