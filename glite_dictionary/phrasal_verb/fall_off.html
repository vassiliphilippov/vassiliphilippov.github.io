to fall off

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɔf / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
to decrease or decline sharply or unexpectedly; to drop off in amount, quality, or quantity.
decline sharply or drop off

Examples

Sales usually fall off after the holiday season.
Attendance at the club meetings has fallen off in recent months.
The quality of the service began to fall off after the restaurant changed ownership.

Synset Examples

The real estate market fell off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - to become smaller, fewer, or less; decrease
  • Synonym: to drop off - to decrease or fall sharply
  • Antonym: increase - to become greater in amount, number, or degree

2. VERB

/ fɔl ɑf / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
to become smaller, fewer, or less intense; to decrease or diminish
diminish or decrease

Examples

Sales usually fall off in the winter months due to lower demand.
Interest in the project has fallen off since the initial excitement.
After a strong start, attendance began falling off at the weekly meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrease - to become smaller or less
  • Antonym: increase - to become larger or greater

3. VERB

/ fɔl ɔf / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
to become detached or separated from something by dropping or breaking away
become detached or come off

Examples

The label kept peeling until it finally fell off the bottle.
Be careful with that chair leg; it might fall off if you sit down too hard.
The leaves fall off the trees every autumn.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
