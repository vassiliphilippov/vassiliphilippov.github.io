to bring back

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ bæk / (AmE), / brɪŋ bæk / (BrE)
to cause someone to remember something; to recall to mind
recall to mind; make someone remember

Examples

Hearing that song always brings back memories of my childhood.
The old book brought back vivid images of her school days.
Looking at those photos brings back so many emotions for him.

Synset Examples

These pictures bring back sad memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to bring a memory into the mind; to remember
  • Antonym: to forget - to be unable to remember; fail to recall
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00022092-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ bæk / (AmE), / brɪŋ bæk / (BrE)
to return someone or something to the place where they started
return to original place

Examples

Please bring back the books to the library when you are finished.
The tour guide promised to bring back all the tourists to the starting point safely.
After the party, she offered to bring back the borrowed chairs to their owners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to go or come back to a place
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
