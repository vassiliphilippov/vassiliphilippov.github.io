to stay up

1. VERB

/ steɪ ʌp / (AmE), / steɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to not go to bed at the usual time; to remain awake later than usual
remain awake past usual bedtime

Examples

If you stay up too late, you'll feel tired in the morning.
She likes to stay up and read novels long after everyone else has gone to bed.
We stayed up all night talking about old times.

Synset Examples

Don't stay up so late — you have to go to work tomorrow
We sat up all night to watch the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sit up - to remain awake and not go to bed, especially later than usual
