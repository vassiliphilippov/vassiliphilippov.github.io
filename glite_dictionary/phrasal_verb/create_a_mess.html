to create a mess

1. VERB

/ kriˈeɪt ə mɛs / (AmE), / kriˈeɪt ə mɛs / (BrE)
to make a place or situation untidy or disorganized
make a place or situation untidy

Examples

The children always create a mess when they play with paints.
Please try not to create a mess in the kitchen this time.
It's easy to create a mess if you cook several dishes at once.

Synset Examples

The kids created a mess with their art supplies.
She made a mess while baking cookies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a mess - to cause disorder or untidiness
  • Antonym: to clean up - to make a place or situation tidy again
