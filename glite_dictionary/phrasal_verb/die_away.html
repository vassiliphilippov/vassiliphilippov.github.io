to die away

1. VERB

/ daɪ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / daɪ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually become less strong and eventually disappear, especially a sound, feeling, or effect
become weaker or less; fade away

Examples

After a few minutes, the applause began to die away and everyone returned to their seats.
The sound of the bells died away in the distance as the procession moved on.
As the storm passed, the wind gradually died away and it became calm again.

Synset Examples

The storm abated
The rain let up after a few hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fade away - to gradually disappear or become less distinct
  • Synonym: to diminish - to become less in size, importance, or intensity
  • Antonym: to intensify - to become stronger or more intense
