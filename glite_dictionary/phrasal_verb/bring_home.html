to bring home

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ hoʊm / (AmE), / brɪŋ həʊm / (BrE)
to earn (a salary or wage) for one's household, especially as regular income
earn a salary or wage

Examples

He spends much of his time at work to bring home enough money for the family.
She is the primary earner and brings home most of the household income.
After joining the new company, he started bringing home a larger paycheck each month.

Synset Examples

How much does your wife take home after taxes and other deductions?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take home - to earn (a salary or wage) after deductions

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ hoʊm / (AmE), / brɪŋ həʊm / (BrE)
to make (something) clearly understood or fully realized by someone, especially by emphasizing its importance or impact
make clear or easily understood

Examples

The tragic story brought home the reality of the crisis.
The teacher used statistics to bring home the importance of regular attendance.
His experience in the hospital really brought home how serious the illness can be.

Synset Examples

This brings home my point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something clearer or easier to understand
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something unclear or difficult to understand

3. VERB

/ brɪŋ hoʊm / (AmE), / brɪŋ həʊm / (BrE)
to make someone realize or understand something clearly; to emphasize the importance or reality of something
make someone realize something clearly

Examples

Her illness brought home the importance of regular health check-ups.
The tragic news brought home just how quickly life can change.

Synset Examples
