to weasel into

1. VERB

/ ˈwizəl ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈwiːzl ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to enter or gain access to a place, group, or situation by sly or sneaky means
sneakily gain access to something
informal

Examples

She tried to weasel into the exclusive event without an invitation.
He managed to weasel into the discussion by pretending to know the topic.
They attempted to weasel into the restricted area but were quickly spotted by security.

Synset Examples

She managed to weasel into the meeting at the last minute.
He tried to weasel into the conversation unnoticed.
They weaseled into the club despite not having a ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak into - to enter a place secretly or without permission

Etymology

From the cunning behavior attributed to the animal 'weasel' in English idiom.
