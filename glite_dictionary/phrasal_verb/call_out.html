to call out

1. VERB

/ kɔl aʊt / (AmE), / kɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to announce or say something loudly, especially names or numbers, so that people can hear clearly
announce loudly (names, numbers, etc.)

Examples

The teacher began to call out the students’ names for attendance.
Please call out the winning numbers clearly so everyone can hear.
She called out each item on the list as she checked them off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly

2. VERB

/ kɔl aʊt / (AmE), / kɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
(historical) To challenge someone to a duel, especially in response to an insult or perceived wrongdoing.
challenge to a duel (historical)
archaic

Examples

The offended gentleman decided to call out his rival on the field of honor.
It was not uncommon in the 18th century for men to call out one another over perceived insults.
Aaron Burr famously called out Alexander Hamilton, leading to their notorious duel.

Synset Examples

Aaron Burr called out Alexander Hamilton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to invite someone to compete or take part, especially in a contest

3. VERB

/ kɔl aʊt / (AmE), / kɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to say something loudly and clearly, especially because you want to attract someone's attention or express an emotion such as surprise, horror, or joy.
say something loudly and clearly

Examples

She called out his name, hoping he would hear her over the noise.
The teacher called out the answer so everyone could hear.
He called out in surprise when he saw the results.

Synset Examples

‘I won!’ he exclaimed
‘Help!’ she cried
‘I'm here,’ the mother shouted when she saw her child looking lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Synonym: to cry out - to make a loud sound or say something suddenly out of strong emotion
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
