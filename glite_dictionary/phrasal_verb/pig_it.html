to pig it

1. VERB

/ pɪɡ ɪt / (AmE), / pɪɡ ɪt / (BrE)
(informal, British) To live in a very dirty or untidy way, like a pig.
live in squalor or messily
informal british

Examples

After his roommate left, he really started to pig it and stopped cleaning the apartment.
The boys pigged it all summer, leaving dirty dishes everywhere and never making their beds.
She hated to pig it but the plumbing was out and the house quickly turned into a mess.

Etymology

From the noun 'pig' (the animal), used metaphorically for messiness, with the verb 'it' meaning to act as such.
