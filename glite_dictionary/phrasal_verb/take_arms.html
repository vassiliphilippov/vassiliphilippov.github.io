to take arms

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɑrmz / (AmE), / teɪk ɑːmz / (BrE)
to begin fighting, especially by preparing for war or hostilities
to begin fighting or war

Examples

Citizens were urged to take arms against the invading forces.
Quick to anger, he took arms at the first sign of betrayal.
Throughout history, people have taken arms to defend their homeland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to war - to become involved in war or hostilities
