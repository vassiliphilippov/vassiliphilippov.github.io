to fob off

1. VERB

/ fɑb ɑf / (AmE), / fɒb ɒf / (BrE)
to sell or pass off something as genuine or valuable, typically with the intention to deceive; to palm off a fake or inferior item
deceive by passing off something as genuine

Examples

The shopkeeper tried to fob off a fake watch as a luxury brand to unsuspecting tourists.
She was angry after being fobbed off with a shoddy product that was advertised as premium quality.
They attempted to fob off the old software as the latest edition, but customers quickly noticed the difference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to palm off - to dispose of something undesirable by trickery, often as something of value
  • Synonym: to pass off - to present something falsely, often with intent to deceive

Etymology

Originates from obsolete 'fob' (to cheat or trick), likely from German dialect 'foppen' (to jeer at, make a fool of).
