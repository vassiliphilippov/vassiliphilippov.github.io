to get glumpy

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɡlʌmpi / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɡlʌmpi / (BrE)
to feel sad or gloomy; to become moody or downhearted
become sad or gloomy
informal

Examples

On rainy days, I tend to get glumpy and stay indoors reading novels.
Whenever he loses a game, he can't help but get glumpy for the rest of the day.
Don't get glumpy over small setbacks; things will get better soon.

Synset Examples

He always gets glumpy when it rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feel blue - to feel sad or depressed
  • Antonym: to cheer up - to become happier or less sad
