to wipe up

1. VERB

/ waɪp ʌp / (AmE), / waɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to clean a surface by wiping, especially to remove a spill or liquid
clean by wiping, especially spilt liquid

Examples

After the juice spilled, she quickly wiped up the mess on the floor.
Could you wipe up the counter before dinner, please?
He used a towel to wipe up the spilled water from the table.

Synset Examples

Mop the hallway now
He mopped her forehead with a towel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mop up - to clean or soak up liquid with a cloth or mop
  • Synonym: wipe - to clean or dry by rubbing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01395993-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ waɪp ʌp / (AmE), / waɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to clean a surface by removing liquid, dirt, or a mess, usually with a cloth, towel, or similar item
clean by removing liquid or dirt

Examples

She quickly grabbed a sponge to wipe up the spilled juice on the floor.
After dinner, he helped wipe up the crumbs from the table.
Please wipe up any water you see on the bathroom counter.

Synset Examples

Don't forget to wipe up after cooking.
She used a towel to wipe up the mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clean up - to make something clean or tidy by removing dirt or mess
