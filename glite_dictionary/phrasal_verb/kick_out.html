to kick out

1. VERB

/ kɪk aʊt / (AmE), / kɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place or organization; to expel or eject someone.
expel or force to leave

Examples

The principal decided to kick out several students for breaking the rules.
After the argument, Sarah was kicked out of the club.
If you don't pay your rent, the landlord might kick out the tenants.

Synset Examples

He was expelled from his native country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from a job or position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02507387-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɪk aʊt / (AmE), / kɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a job, position, or place, especially suddenly or unceremoniously
remove someone from a job or place

Examples

After the scandal, the board decided to kick out the CEO immediately.
He was kicked out of the club for violating the rules repeatedly.
If you keep missing deadlines, they might kick out your entire team.

Synset Examples

The chairman was ousted after he misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to officially remove someone from their job
  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from their job
  • Antonym: hire - to give someone a job
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02406951-v
Synset POS: v
