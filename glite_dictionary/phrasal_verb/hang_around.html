to hang around

1. VERB

/ hæŋ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / hæŋ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to remain in or near a place without any specific purpose or activity
stay in a place without purpose

Examples

We used to hang around the park after school, just talking and relaxing.
There is no point to hang around if the shop is closed for the day.
He spent the afternoon hanging around the mall, waiting for his friends.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - stand or wait around idly or without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to linger - stay in a place longer than necessary, typically because of reluctance to leave

2. VERB

/ hæŋ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / hæŋ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time in a particular place without any specific purpose or activity
spend time idly in a place

Examples

After school, the kids like to hang around the playground before going home.
There's no reason to hang around if the meeting is over.
We spent the afternoon hanging around the city center, just people-watching.

Synset Examples

He likes to hang around after class to chat.
Let's hang around the cafe for a while.
Don't just hang around; join us for the game.
Eshays often hang around the mall looking for trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary
  • Synonym: to loiter - to stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • Antonym: leave - to depart from a place
