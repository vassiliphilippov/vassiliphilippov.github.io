to go tutti-frutti

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˌtʌti ˈfruːti / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˌtʌti ˈfruːti / (BrE)
(informal) To mix a variety of flavors, elements, or styles together, often in a creative or playful way.
mix various flavors or styles
informal

Examples

When it comes to decorating, she likes to go tutti-frutti and blend bright colors and patterns.
At the party, everyone decided to go tutti-frutti with their outfit choices, resulting in a vibrant crowd.
Don’t be afraid to go tutti-frutti with your pizza toppings for a unique flavor experience.

Synset Examples

He loves to go tutti-frutti with his ice cream toppings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or mix together
  • Antonym: separate - to keep apart; not mix

Etymology

From Italian 'tutti frutti' meaning 'all fruits,' referring to a mixture; here used figuratively in an English phrase.
