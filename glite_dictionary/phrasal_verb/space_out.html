to space out

1. VERB

/ speɪs aʊt / (AmE), / speɪs aʊt / (BrE)
to become distracted, inattentive, or lost in thought, failing to pay attention to what is happening around you
become inattentive or lost in thought
informal

Examples

Sometimes I just space out and forget what I was doing.
During the meeting, she began to space out and missed most of the discussion.
After staring at the screen for hours, he realized he had completely spaced out.

Synset Examples

It's easy to space out when you're tired.
I tend to space out during long lectures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zone out - to stop paying attention or lose awareness for a period
