to plumb the depth

1. VERB

/ plʌm ðə dɛpθ / (AmE), / plʌm ðə dɛpθ / (BrE)
to investigate or explore something thoroughly or deeply, especially to understand all its complexities or hidden aspects.
explore or investigate thoroughly

Examples

The scientists set out to plumb the depth of the ancient ruins, searching for long-lost secrets.
Before approving the merger, the committee needed to plumb the depth of the financial records.
His novel attempts to plumb the depth of human emotion in times of crisis.

Synset Examples

They decided to plumb the depths of the ocean.
We need to plumb the issue further before making a decision.
He will plumb the mysteries of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explore - to examine or investigate systematically
  • Antonym: skim the surface - to deal with only the superficial aspects

Etymology

From the verb 'plumb', originally referring to measuring water depth with a lead weight; now used figuratively.
