to cast on

1. VERB

/ kæst ɑn / (AmE), / kɑːst ɒn / (BrE)
to make the first row of stitches on a needle when beginning to knit
make first row of knitting stitches

Examples

Before you start the sweater, you need to cast on thirty stitches.
She carefully cast on the edge of her scarf using the long-tail method.
After learning to cast on, he found knitting much easier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to cast off - to finish knitting by binding off stitches
