to zoom along

1. VERB

/ zuːm əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / zuːm əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to move or travel very quickly, especially along a route or path
move very quickly

Examples

The sports car zoomed along the empty highway, leaving all the other traffic behind.
Children on bicycles zoom along the sidewalk after school ends.
We watched the train zoom along the tracks as it approached the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed along - move quickly along a route
  • Antonym: creep along - move slowly along a route
