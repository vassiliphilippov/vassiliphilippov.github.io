to drive off

1. VERB

/ draɪv ɑf / (AmE), / draɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave, often by using threats, actions, or deterrents; can refer to both people and things in physical and figurative contexts.
force to go away

Examples

The barking dog managed to drive off the intruders before they could break in.
She tried to drive off her worries by focusing on her work.
The loud noise was meant to drive off the birds from the crops.

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drive away - to repel or force someone or something to leave
  • Synonym: to dispel - to make a feeling, doubt, or belief disappear
  • Antonym: to attract - to draw someone or something towards oneself
