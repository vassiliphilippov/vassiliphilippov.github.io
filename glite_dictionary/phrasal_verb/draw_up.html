to draw up

1. VERB

/ drɔː ʌp / (AmE), / drɔː ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare and write out plans, documents, or detailed instructions; to compose or draft something official or formal.
prepare or compose (a plan or document)

Examples

The committee will draw up a proposal for the new project by next week.
She was asked to draw up the contract before the meeting began.
Lawyers often draw up wills and other legal documents for their clients.

Synset Examples

frame a policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft - to compose or prepare a preliminary version of a document
  • Synonym: to formulate - to create or devise methodically (a strategy or proposal)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00708633-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ drɔː ʌp / (AmE), / drɔː ʌp / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or its driver) to come to a stop after moving or driving somewhere.
stop a vehicle after moving

Examples

The limo drew up outside the theater, waiting for the guests to arrive.
Our bus drew up at the station right on time.
As soon as the taxi drew up, she hurried outside to meet it.

Synset Examples

The chauffeur hauled up in front of us

  • Synonym: to pull up - stop a vehicle after moving
