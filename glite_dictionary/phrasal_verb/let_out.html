to let out

1. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to leave a place, condition, or state; to release.
allow to leave or be released

Examples

She promised to let out the dog before leaving for work.
After weeks of rain, the sun finally let out and brightened the sky.
The teacher let out the students early due to the assembly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - set free from confinement or control
  • Antonym: to confine - keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01477931-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to express a sound, such as a cry, sigh, or other audible utterance, sometimes involuntarily or with strong feeling
make a sound audibly

Examples

She couldn't help but let out a laugh at the joke.
The patient suddenly let out a loud groan during the procedure.
He let out a deep breath, relieved the interview was over.

Synset Examples

She let out a big heavy sigh
He uttered strange sounds that nobody could understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utter - to say or make a sound with the voice
  • Synonym: to emit - to produce or discharge (a sound, noise, etc.)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00985856-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to make an item of clothing larger by sewing so as to allow more room
make clothing larger by sewing

Examples

I had to let out my skirt because it was too tight after washing.
The tailor can let out your jacket if it feels snug around the shoulders.
After his son grew quickly, they let out all his school trousers.

Synset Examples

Let out that dress — I gained a lot of weight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to take in - to make clothes smaller by sewing

/ lɛt aʊt / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to make known information that was previously secret or only known to a few people
make secret information known

Examples

He accidentally let out the surprise party plans before the big day.
The journalist refused to let out the source of her information.
Don't let out any details about the confidential agreement.

Synset Examples

bring out the truth

unwrap the evidence in the murder case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disclose - to make something secret public
