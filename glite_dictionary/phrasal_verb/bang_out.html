to bang out

1. VERB

/ bæŋ aʊt / (AmE), / bæŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to play a piece of music loudly and usually with little subtlety or finesse
play music loudly or forcefully

Examples

The pianist began to bang out a lively tune to get the party going.
She loves to bang out pop songs on her old upright piano.
He banged out the national anthem before the football match started.

Synset Examples

They banged out ‘The star-spangled banner’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belt out - to sing or play music loudly and forcefully

2. VERB

/ bæŋ aʊt / (AmE), / bæŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to produce something quickly or with little effort, especially in a mechanical or casual way
produce quickly or with little effort
informal

Examples

She managed to bang out three assignments before lunch.
I can bang out this report in an hour if I focus.
He likes to bang out a few songs on his guitar when he's bored.

Synset Examples

She managed to bang out three assignments before lunch.
I can bang out this report in an hour.
He loves to bang out a few songs on his guitar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock out - to produce or complete something quickly, especially without care; informal
  • Synonym: to crank out - to produce large quantities of something quickly, often with less care
