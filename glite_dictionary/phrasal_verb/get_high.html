to get high

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt haɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt haɪ / (BrE)
to experience a state of euphoria or altered consciousness, especially as a result of taking drugs or alcohol
experience euphoria, often from drugs
informal

Examples

Many teenagers are tempted to get high at parties, but it's important to be aware of the risks.
She used to get high to escape her problems, but now she finds healthier ways to cope.
Some people get high just once out of curiosity, while others may do so more frequently.

Synset Examples

We might get high if the weather is nice.
He decided to get high with friends after exams.
They plan to get high at the music festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get drunk - to become intoxicated from alcohol
