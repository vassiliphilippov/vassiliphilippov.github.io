to boot out

1. VERB

/ buːt aʊt / (AmE), / buːt aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a position, job, or place, often suddenly or unceremoniously
remove or dismiss from a position

Examples

After the scandal, the board decided to boot out the CEO immediately.
They threatened to boot out anyone who continued to break the rules.
Several employees were booted out for repeated misconduct.

Synset Examples

The chairman was ousted after he misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oust - to remove or expel someone from a position or place
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job
  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job

2. VERB

/ buːt aʊt / (AmE), / buːt aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place or a position, especially in a sudden or unceremonious way
force someone to leave or be expelled
informal

Examples

The coach decided to boot out the player after repeated rule violations.
She was booted out of the club for breaking the code of conduct.
If you don't pay the rent, the landlord might boot out all the tenants.

Synset Examples

The unruly student was excluded from the game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: to kick out - to force someone to leave a place or position

Etymology

From 'boot' (to kick) + 'out', from the idea of kicking someone out of a place.

3. VERB

/ buːt aʊt / (AmE), / buːt aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place, organization, or job, or to remove something forcefully or unceremoniously
force out or remove suddenly

Examples

The unruly passenger was booted out of the theater for causing a disturbance.
After missing three meetings, she was booted out of the club.
I had to boot out the outdated program to install the latest version.

Synset Examples

The manager will boot out anyone who is late.
I had to boot out the old software from my laptop.
They decided to boot out the disruptive player.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick out - to force someone to leave
  • Synonym: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: to evict - to legally force someone to leave their home or property
