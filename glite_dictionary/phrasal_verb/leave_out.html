to leave out

1. VERB

/ liv aʊt / (AmE), / liːv aʊt / (BrE)
to fail to include or mention something or someone; to omit.
omit; fail to include or mention

Examples

Please do not leave out any important details in your report.
She accidentally left out his name from the guest list.
If you leave out the eggs, the cake will not rise properly.

Synset Examples

How could I miss that typo?
The workers on the conveyor belt miss one out of ten

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to omit - to leave out or exclude
  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or list
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ liv aʊt / (AmE), / liːv aʊt / (BrE)
to not include someone or something; to omit
omit; not include

Examples

Please do not leave out any important details in your summary.
She felt upset when her friends left out her name from the list.
You can leave out the onions if you don’t like them in the salad.

Synset Examples

The bad results were excluded from the report
Leave off the top piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to omit - to leave out or exclude, either intentionally or forgetfully
  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or set
