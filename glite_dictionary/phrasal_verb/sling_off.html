to sling off

1. VERB

/ slɪŋ ɔf / (AmE), / slɪŋ ɒf / (BrE)
to discard or remove something quickly, carelessly, or with little regard
remove or discard quickly, carelessly

Examples

After a long day, he would sling off his coat as soon as he got home.
She slung off her doubts and decided to take the risk.
The children sling off their backpacks the moment they step inside.

Synset Examples

She decided to sling off her responsibilities for the weekend.
They slung off their shoes before entering the house.
He quickly slung off his jacket as the room heated up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw away - to dispose of something one no longer wants or needs
  • Synonym: toss aside - to put something down quickly, carelessly, or without regard

Etymology

From 'sling' (to throw or cast) + 'off', common phrasal verb formation.
