to kick back

1. VERB

/ kɪk ˈbæk / (AmE), / kɪk ˈbæk / (BrE)
to make an illicit payment in return for a favor or to secure special treatment; to pay a kickback
make an illegal payment

Examples

The company was accused of trying to kick back money to government officials to win the contract.
Several executives were arrested after they were caught kicking back to secure the building permit.
He denied ever having kicked back a portion of his earnings to his supervisors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - to give money or favors to influence someone dishonestly
  • Synonym: kickback - an illicit payment made to someone for facilitating a transaction

Etymology

From 'kickback', originally US slang for a secret or illegal payment.

2. VERB

/ kɪk bæk / (AmE), / kɪk bæk / (BrE)
(of a machine or device) to suddenly recoil or spring backward, usually as a result of a forceful thrust or impact.
recoil or spring backward suddenly

Examples

When he fired the shotgun, it kicked back and nearly knocked him off his feet.
The chainsaw can kick back if it hits a hard knot in the wood.
Be careful, because that old rifle tends to kick back pretty hard.

Synset Examples

The gun kicked back into my shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recoil - to spring back upon firing or release; to suddenly move back
