to sweep the deck

1. VERB

/ swip ðə dɛk / (AmE), / swiːp ðə dɛk / (BrE)
to clean the surface of a deck by sweeping; to tidy up the deck of a boat or outdoor area
to clean or tidy a deck by sweeping

Examples

Before we set sail, let's sweep the deck to make sure it's clean.
He asked his crew to sweep the deck after the storm had passed.
Every morning, she enjoys sweeping the deck while listening to the sea.

Synset Examples

We need to sweep the deck before the guests arrive.
Can you sweep the deck while I prepare dinner?
It's time to sweep the deck and tidy up the boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clean up - to make tidy by removing dirt or mess
  • Synonym: to sweep the floor - to clean the floor using a broom
