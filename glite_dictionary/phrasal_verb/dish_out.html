to dish out

1. VERB

/ dɪʃ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to give or distribute something, especially in portions, often repeatedly or in a casual way
give or distribute in portions

Examples

The coach didn't hesitate to dish out criticism whenever the team made a mistake.
Every Saturday, volunteers dish out meals at the local shelter.
She loves to dish out advice, even when no one asks for it.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present
dole out some money
shell out pocket money for the children
deal a blow to someone
the machine dispenses soft drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dole out - to give out small amounts of something, often as charity or in a measured way
  • Synonym: to hand out - to give something to each person in a group

2. VERB

/ dɪʃ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to serve food to people; to give or distribute something, often in large amounts or without much thought
to serve or distribute (often food or other things)

Examples

Every evening, volunteers dish out hot meals at the shelter.
The teacher was quick to dish out praise for good work.
He loves to dish out advice, even when no one asks for it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - to give food or drink to someone at a meal
  • Synonym: to hand out - to distribute something to people

3. VERB

/ dɪʃ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to give or distribute something, especially in large amounts or freely, often used for comments, criticism, or food
distribute or give out (esp. freely or in large amounts)
informal

Examples

She loves to dish out advice to her coworkers, whether they ask for it or not.
The cafeteria staff quickly dished out lunch to the hungry students.
He can easily criticize others but can't take it when it's dished out to him.

Synset Examples

She can dish out compliments like nobody else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hand out - to distribute something to a group of people
