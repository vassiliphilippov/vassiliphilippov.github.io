to take care of (someone)

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈkɛr əv ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / teɪk ˈkeə əv ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to look after or provide for the needs of someone; to ensure someone's well-being or safety
look after or provide for someone

Examples

When her parents were away, Maya had to take care of her younger brother.
The nurse will take care of you during your recovery at the hospital.
It is important to take care of yourself when you are feeling stressed.

Synset Examples

They need to learn to take care of themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look after - to care for or be responsible for someone or something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for someone or something
