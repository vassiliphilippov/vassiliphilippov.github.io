to bounce ideas off

1. VERB

/ baʊns aɪˈdiəz ɔf / (AmE), / baʊns aɪˈdɪəz ɒf / (BrE)
to present ideas to someone in order to receive feedback, suggestions, or help in refining them
discuss ideas for feedback or improvement

Examples

Before starting the new campaign, she wanted to bounce ideas off her team.
He always likes to bounce ideas off trusted friends before making big decisions.
Let's bounce ideas off each other to make this project better.

Synset Examples

We can bounce off each other's thoughts to refine our project.
She decided to bounce off her ideas with her colleagues first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brainstorm - to generate ideas spontaneously, often in a group
  • Synonym: to consult - to seek advice or information from someone
