to turn to

1. VERB

/ tɝn tu / (AmE), / tɜːn tuː / (BrE)
to address or speak to someone, often by turning one's attention or body toward them
address or speak to someone

Examples

She turned to her colleague and whispered a question.
When he finished reading, he turned to the class to explain the answer.
Suddenly, the manager turned to me and asked for my opinion.

Synset Examples

He addressed the crowd outside the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: address - to speak to a person or group directly

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɝn tu / (AmE), / tɜːn tuː / (BrE)
to go to someone or something for help, advice, or guidance
seek help or guidance from

Examples

When she needed advice, she would always turn to her older sister for guidance.
During difficult times, many people turn to religion for comfort.
If you have a technical problem, you can turn to our support team for assistance.

Synset Examples

Many people turn to MSDNC for their political news.
Everyone turns to the tech guru for advice on gadgets.
Everyone turned to Teague for advice on office matters.
Everyone turned to Tom for advice during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consult - to seek advice or information from someone
  • Synonym: to rely on - to depend on someone or something for support
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something
