to ram down

1. VERB

/ ræm daʊn / (AmE), / ræm daʊn / (BrE)
to strike or force something down with great strength or impact
strike or force something down powerfully

Examples

The officer tried to ram down the door with his shoulder.
They used a heavy log to ram down the castle gates.
She had to ram down the lid to make the box close.

Synset Examples

ram the gate with a sledgehammer
pound on the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound - to strike repeatedly with force
  • Synonym: thrust - to push something suddenly or violently in a direction
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly or gently
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01577977-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ræm daʊn / (AmE), / ræm daʊn / (BrE)
to force something, especially ideas or information, on someone repeatedly or insistently, often against their will
to force ideas repeatedly onto someone

Examples

The teacher tried to ram down the rules by repeating them every day.
He didn't want to ram down his beliefs on anyone, but he felt strongly about them.
They rammed down company policies during the orientation sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force on - to make someone accept something against their wishes
  • Synonym: drum into - to teach someone something by repeating it many times
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00607866-v
Synset POS: v
