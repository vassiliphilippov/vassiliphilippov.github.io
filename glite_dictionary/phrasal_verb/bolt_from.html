to bolt from

1. VERB

/ boʊlt frəm / (AmE), / bəʊlt frəm / (BrE)
to leave or run away from a place suddenly and unexpectedly
leave suddenly and unexpectedly

Examples

When the fire alarm sounded, everyone chose to bolt from the building immediately.
She tried to bolt from the meeting before anyone could ask her questions.
At the first sign of trouble, the suspect attempted to bolt from the scene.

Synset Examples

He decided to bolt from the party early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from danger or a place
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary
