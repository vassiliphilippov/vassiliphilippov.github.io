to die hard

1. VERB

/ daɪ hɑrd / (AmE), / daɪ hɑːd / (BrE)
to continue to exist or be believed for a long time, despite opposition or attempts to change or eliminate it
persist despite attempts to remove

Examples

Old habits die hard, even when people try to change them.
The myth about the haunted house died hard in the small town.
Superstitions sometimes die hard, lasting for generations.

Synset Examples

These stories die hard
The legend of Elvis endures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue to exist or endure over a prolonged period
  • Antonym: to fade away - to gradually disappear or become weaker
