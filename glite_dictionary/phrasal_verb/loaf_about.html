to loaf about

1. VERB

/ loʊf əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ləʊf əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to spend time doing nothing in particular, often in a lazy or idle manner
spend time idly or lazily

Examples

Sometimes it feels good to loaf about on a Sunday afternoon with nothing to do.
The students were caught loafing about outside the library instead of attending class.
After all his exams, he just wanted to loaf about and watch TV for a few days.

Synset Examples

He prefers to loaf about rather than do chores.
On weekends, I love to loaf about and relax.
On weekends, I love to loaf around the house.
Instead of studying, he decided to loaf around with friends.
They spent the afternoon loafing about the park.
They spent the afternoon loafing around at the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing or being inactive
  • Synonym: to laze - to relax and avoid work or effort

Etymology

From the noun 'loaf' meaning a lazy person, itself from slang use related to 'loaf of bread' (head, in Cockney rhyming slang), and 'about' as an adverb of place.
