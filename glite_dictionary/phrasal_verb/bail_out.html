to bail out

1. VERB

/ beɪl aʊt / (AmE), / beɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or pay for the release of someone from prison by providing bail
release someone from jail by paying bail

Examples

Her father had to bail out her after she was arrested last night.
If you get arrested, who would you call to bail out you?
He couldn't afford to bail out his friend from jail this time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release on bail - to allow someone to leave jail while awaiting trial after bail is paid

Etymology

From 'bail' (to obtain release from custody by providing security) + 'out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02499908-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ beɪl aʊt / (AmE), / beɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to remove water from a boat by repeatedly scooping it out and throwing it over the side
remove water from a boat

Examples

After the storm, they had to bail out the boat to keep it from sinking.
She grabbed a bucket to bail out the water accumulating inside the dinghy.
We took turns bailing out as quickly as possible during the leak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pump out - to remove water from a boat using a pump

Etymology

From 'bail' meaning to scoop water out, from Old French 'baille' (bucket).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00450381-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ beɪl aʊt / (AmE), / beɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to give financial help or other assistance to prevent a person, company, or organization from failing or getting into serious trouble
provide financial or emergency help

Examples

The government had to bail out the airline to prevent thousands of layoffs.
She asked her parents to bail out the family restaurant when revenue dropped suddenly.
After the storm damaged their business, the owners hoped that someone would bail out their company.

Synset Examples

The government decided to bail out the failing bank.
The city plans to bail out struggling businesses after the storm.
He had to bail out his friend after the car broke down.
I had to go bail my brother out of jail last night.
They had to bail out the company to save jobs.
They agreed to bail out the struggling company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - to save someone or something from danger or harm
  • Synonym: bail out (literal) - to remove water from something (like a boat) with a container

Etymology

Originally from the literal sense of 'bail' (to remove water with a container) applied figuratively to rescuing from trouble.
