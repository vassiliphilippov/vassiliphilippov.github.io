to embark on

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑrk ɑn / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːk ɒn / (BrE)
to begin a new project, activity, or journey, especially one that is significant or challenging
begin a significant project or activity

Examples

After graduating, she decided to embark on a career in medicine.
The company will embark on a new project next year.
Before you embark on this adventure, make sure you are well prepared.

Synset Examples

Who started this company?
We embarked on an exciting enterprise
I start my day with a good breakfast
We began the new semester
The afternoon session begins at 4 PM
The blood shed started when the partisans launched a surprise attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start doing something
  • Synonym: to commence - to begin or start something
  • Antonym: finish - to bring something to an end or completion

Etymology

From French *embarquer* (to go on board a ship), from Spanish or Portuguese *embarcar*, with figurative use developing in English.
