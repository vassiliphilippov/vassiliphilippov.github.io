to tog out

1. VERB

/ tɑɡ aʊt / (AmE), / tɒɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to dress in special or attractive clothes for a particular occasion
dress in special or attractive clothes
british informal

Examples

He likes to tog out in his best suit for special occasions.
The children were togged out in costumes for the school play.
Before the award ceremony, she togs out in an elegant dress.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on clothes that are more formal or elaborate than usual

Etymology

Possibly from 'tog', 18th-century slang for clothing, from Latin 'toga'.
