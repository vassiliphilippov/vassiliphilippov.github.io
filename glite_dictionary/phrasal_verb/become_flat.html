to become flat

1. VERB

/ bɪˈkʌm flæt / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm flæt / (BrE)
(of a drink, especially wine or beer) to lose its sparkle, effervescence, or freshness; to become stale or lifeless in taste.
lose sparkle or freshness (in drinks)

Examples

If you leave champagne open overnight, it will become flat by morning.
The beer had become flat, so nobody wanted to drink it anymore.
Red wine can quickly become flat if left uncorked on the table.

Synset Examples

wine and beer can pall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go flat - (of a drink) lose its effervescence or freshness
