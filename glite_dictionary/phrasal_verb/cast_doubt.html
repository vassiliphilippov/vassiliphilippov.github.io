to cast doubt

1. VERB

/ kæst daʊt / (AmE), / kɑːst daʊt / (BrE)
to cause people to feel uncertain about something or to suspect that it may not be true, honest, or valid
cause uncertainty or suspicion

Examples

The allegations have cast doubt on the politician's integrity.
New scientific evidence casts doubt on the theory's accuracy.
The witness's contradictory statements cast doubt over the entire case.

Synset Examples

A sense of doubt was cast over the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise doubt - to bring up reasons to feel uncertain
  • Antonym: dispel doubt - to remove uncertainty or suspicion
