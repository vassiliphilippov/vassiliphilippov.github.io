to read lips

1. VERB

/ rid lɪps / (AmE), / riːd lɪps / (BrE)
to understand what someone is saying by studying the movements of their lips, without hearing the actual words
understand speech by watching lip movements

Examples

Because she was hard of hearing, she learned to read lips to follow conversations.
He tried to read lips from across the room when the microphone failed.
It takes practice to read lips accurately in noisy environments.

Synset Examples

He could read lips well, even from afar.
She learned to read lips while watching movies.
He tried to read lips during the quiet lecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lip-read - to understand speech by observing lip movements
