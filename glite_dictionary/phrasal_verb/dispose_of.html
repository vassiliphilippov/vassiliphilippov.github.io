to dispose of

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈpoʊz əv / (AmE), / dɪsˈpəʊz əv / (BrE)
to deal with or settle a problem, task, or matter effectively so that it is no longer a concern
deal with or settle a matter

Examples

The manager needed to dispose of several urgent matters before noon.
She efficiently disposed of all the paperwork by the deadline.
The committee disposed of old business before starting on new topics.

Synset Examples

He disposed of these cases quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - find a solution to a problem or argument
  • Synonym: address - deal with an issue or problem

Etymology

From Latin 'disponere' (to arrange), via Old French 'disposer'.
