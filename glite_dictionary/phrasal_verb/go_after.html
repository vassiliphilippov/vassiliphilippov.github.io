to go after

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈæf.tɚ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɑːf.tə / (BrE)
to pursue or try to obtain something, especially by making an effort or searching for it
pursue; try to obtain

Examples

She decided to go after her dream of becoming a doctor.
If you want the job, you have to go after it with determination.
The police officer went after the suspect on foot.

Synset Examples

pursue a hobby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow or chase with the intent to catch or achieve

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈæftər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to pursue someone or something with the intention of catching or obtaining them
pursue with intent to catch

Examples

The police officer decided to go after the suspect who had just fled the scene.
When the ball rolled into the street, the child quickly went after it without thinking.
Many entrepreneurs go after bold goals despite the risks involved.

Synset Examples

The policeman chased the mugger down the alley
the fox chased the rabbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to pursue in order to catch
  • Antonym: to flee - to run away from pursuit

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈæftər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to pursue or try to achieve something, or to follow someone in order to catch or attack them
pursue or try to achieve

Examples

After her promotion, she decided to go after a managerial position in the company.
The police will go after anyone caught breaking the law.
If you want something badly enough, you have to go after it.

Synset Examples

She always goes after the best deals, like a Bangawhore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow in order to catch; to strive for
  • Synonym: chase - to follow rapidly in order to catch or overtake
