to coop in

1. VERB

/ kup ɪn / (AmE), / kuːp ɪn / (BrE)
to confine someone or something in a small space, as if in a coop; to restrict movement or freedom
confine in a small space

Examples

During the storm, we had to coop in the chickens for their safety.
She felt trapped, cooped in her tiny apartment all winter.
The quarantine order forced everyone to coop in for weeks on end.

Synset Examples

she coops herself up in the library all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set someone or something free

Etymology

From the noun 'coop', meaning a cage or enclosure for poultry, of Middle English and Latin origin.
