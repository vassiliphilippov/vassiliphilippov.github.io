to sugarcoat

1. VERB

/ ˈʃʊɡɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡəˌkəʊt / (BrE)
To make something appear better, more acceptable, or less harsh than it really is, often by softening the truth or omitting negative details.
make something seem better than it is

Examples

It's important not to sugarcoat anything when giving performance feedback.
Please don't sugarcoat the situation—I need to know the real facts.
He tends to sugarcoat bad news to avoid upsetting people.

Synset Examples

She told him to keep it real and not sugarcoat anything.
It's better to be honest than to sugarcoat the situation.
Don't sugarcoat the truth; just tell me what happened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften the blow - to make something unpleasant seem less severe
  • Synonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or serious than it actually is
  • Antonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as greater or more serious than it really is

Etymology

Figurative use of 'sugarcoat', originally meaning to coat with sugar to improve flavor or appearance.
