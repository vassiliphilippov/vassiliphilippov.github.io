to get excited

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
to become enthusiastic or eager, often in response to something happening or anticipated
become enthusiastic or eager

Examples

It's hard not to get excited when you hear such good news.
The children always get excited before their birthdays.
She tries not to get excited about the results until they're official.

Synset Examples

He gets excited every time the phone rings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: get agitated - to become troubled or nervous
