to beat the crowd

1. VERB

/ bit ðə kraʊd / (AmE), / biːt ðə kraʊd / (BrE)
to arrive at a place before most others in order to avoid large groups of people
arrive early to avoid crowds

Examples

If you want to find a parking spot, it pays to beat the crowd.
She likes to shop early in the morning to beat the crowd and avoid long lines.
They left for the airport at dawn to beat the crowd and have a smoother journey.

Synset Examples

We left early to beat the crowd at the concert.
She arrived at the store early to beat the crowd.
To avoid traffic, we tried to beat the crowd home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miss the rush - to avoid busy times by arriving early or late
  • Antonym: join the crowd - to go along with the main group of people, not arriving early
