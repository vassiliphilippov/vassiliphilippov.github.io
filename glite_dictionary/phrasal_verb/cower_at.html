to cower at

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊ.ɚ æt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.ə æt / (BrE)
to shrink back or move away in fear or apprehension of something
shrink back in fear of something

Examples

The child would often cower at the sight of large, barking dogs.
Many people cower at the thought of public speaking.
She couldn’t help but cower at his sudden, angry outburst.

Synset Examples

The dog began to cower at the sound of thunder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cower - to crouch down in fear
