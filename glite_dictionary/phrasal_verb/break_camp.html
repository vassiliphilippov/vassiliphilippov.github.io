to break camp

1. VERB

/ breɪk kæmp / (AmE), / breɪk kæmp / (BrE)
to pack up belongings and leave a campsite, especially at the end of a stay
leave a campsite after packing up

Examples

We plan to break camp early tomorrow to avoid the heat.
Once the rain stopped, they quickly broke camp and continued their hike.
It took less than an hour for the group to break camp and load the van.

Synset Examples

The hikers decamped before dawn

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pitch camp - set up a tent or establish a temporary camp
