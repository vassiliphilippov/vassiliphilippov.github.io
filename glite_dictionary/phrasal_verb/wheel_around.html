to wheel around

1. VERB

/ wil əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / wiːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to turn or move someone or something around by rolling, especially using wheels; to turn around quickly or change direction suddenly.
to turn or move on wheels, or turn suddenly

Examples

He wheeled around and glared at me when I called his name.
The nurse wheeled around the patient to face the window.
Startled by the noise, the dog wheeled around to see what had happened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn around - to move in a circular direction to face the opposite way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02050390-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wiːl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / wiːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to turn or change direction quickly, as if revolving on a pivot
turn sharply or suddenly

Examples

The startled bird suddenly wheeled around and flew off in the opposite direction.
At the sound of his name, she wheeled around to see who had called her.
The general wheeled around on his horse to address the gathered soldiers.

Synset Examples

They wheeled their horses around and left

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn around - to move so as to face the opposite direction
