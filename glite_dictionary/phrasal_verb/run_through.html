to run through

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈθru / (AmE), / rʌn ˈθruː / (BrE)
to use up or spend resources, materials, or money quickly and sometimes wastefully
use up resources rapidly

Examples

They managed to run through their entire budget in just a week.
She ran through her inheritance on lavish vacations and shopping sprees.
If you run through all your supplies now, there will be nothing left for later.

Synset Examples

this car consumes a lot of gas
We exhausted our savings
They run through 20 bottles of wine a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to use up - to consume all of something so that none remains
  • Antonym: conserve - to use resources carefully and avoid waste

2. VERB

/ rʌn ˈθru / (AmE), / rʌn ˈθruː / (BrE)
to examine, practice, or think about something thoroughly, usually as preparation or review
examine or rehearse thoroughly

Examples

Let's run through the plan one more time to make sure everything's clear.
Before the presentation, she ran through her speech to check for mistakes.
He likes to run through his schedule for the day over breakfast.

Synset Examples

We worked through an example

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work through - to deal with something methodically, especially a problem or task

3. VERB

/ rʌn ˈθru / (AmE), / rʌn ˈθruː / (BrE)
to review, rehearse, or practice something quickly, often to check for errors or improve performance
review or practice quickly

Examples

Let's run through the report before submitting it to the manager.
The director asked the actors to run through the scene one last time.
She likes to run through her notes before every exam.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rehearse - to practice or go over something before a performance
  • Synonym: review - to look over or inspect something again
