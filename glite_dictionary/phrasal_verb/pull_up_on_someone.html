to pull up on someone

1. VERB

/ pʊl ʌp ɑn ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / pʊl ʌp ɒn ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
(informal) To unexpectedly arrive or confront someone, especially in a surprising or challenging way.
unexpectedly confront or arrive at someone
informal

Examples

He was just hanging out on the corner when the guys pulled up on him out of nowhere.
Don’t be surprised if she pulls up on you to confront you about what happened.
They pulled up on the group to ask what was going on.

Synset Examples

She didn't expect him to pull up on her during lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll up on someone - to approach or arrive at someone unexpectedly, often by vehicle
