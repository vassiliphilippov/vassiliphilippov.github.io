to melt off

1. VERB

/ mɛlt ɑf / (AmE), / mɛlt ɒf / (BrE)
To lose body weight, especially gradually or noticeably, as if the weight were melting away.
lose body weight

Examples

After months of regular exercise, the pounds began to melt off and he felt healthier than ever.
She was amazed at how quickly the extra weight seemed to melt off once she changed her diet.
Many people hope the holiday weight will simply melt off after January.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slim down - to become thinner
  • Antonym: gain weight - to increase in body weight
