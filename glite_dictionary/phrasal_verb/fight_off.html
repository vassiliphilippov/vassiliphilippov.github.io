to fight off

1. VERB

/ faɪt ɑf / (AmE), / faɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to succeed in repelling or resisting something, especially an attack, threat, or unwanted feeling.
repel or resist an attack or threat

Examples

The soldiers managed to fight off the enemy forces until reinforcements arrived.
She tried to fight off a bad cold by getting plenty of rest and drinking water.
Despite feeling sleepy, he struggled to fight off his drowsiness during the long meeting.

Synset Examples

repel the attacker
fight off the onslaught
rebuff the attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repel - to drive or force something back or away
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to an opponent
