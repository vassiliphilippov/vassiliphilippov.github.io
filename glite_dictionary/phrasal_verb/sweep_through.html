to sweep through

1. VERB

/ swip θru / (AmE), / swiːp θruː / (BrE)
to complete or succeed at something easily and with little effort
succeed or finish something easily

Examples

She managed to sweep through all her interviews and received several job offers.
The team swept through the early rounds of the tournament with ease.
He swept through the training course, finishing ahead of everyone else.

Synset Examples

She sailed through her exams
You will pass with flying colors
She nailed her astrophysics course

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sail through - to succeed at something easily
  • Antonym: struggle through - to have difficulty succeeding at something
