to get heated

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈhitɪd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈhiːtɪd / (BrE)
to become intense or passionate, especially in discussion or argument
become intense or passionate

Examples

The conversation started to get heated when politics were brought up.
Discussions on the internet can quickly get heated if people disagree strongly.
Try not to let meetings get heated when debating important issues.

Synset Examples

Debates often get heated over the woke mind virus in society.
Fanpol discussions often get heated on social media.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less intense or agitated
