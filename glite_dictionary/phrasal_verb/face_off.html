to face off

1. VERB

/ feɪs ˈɔf / (AmE), / feɪs ˈɒf / (BrE)
(in sports such as ice hockey or lacrosse) to start play by a face-off, where two opposing players try to gain possession as the referee drops the puck or ball.
to start play with a face-off (sports)

Examples

The referee dropped the puck to face off at the start of the game.
Both teams lined up to face off after the opening whistle.
They faced off for possession as the crowd cheered.
