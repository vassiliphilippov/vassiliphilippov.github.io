to jump on the offer

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɑn ði ˈɔfɚ / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒn ði ˈɒfə / (BrE)
to accept or seize an opportunity quickly and eagerly
accept an opportunity quickly

Examples

She decided to jump on the offer before someone else accepted the job.
Investors were quick to jump on the offer when the share price dropped suddenly.
If you see a great deal, don't hesitate to jump on the offer.

Synset Examples

He quickly jumped on the offer before it expired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab the opportunity - to act quickly to take advantage of an opportunity
