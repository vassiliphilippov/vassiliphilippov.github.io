to furbish up

1. VERB

/ ˈfɝː.bɪʃ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.bɪʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to restore or renovate something, making it look fresh or like new, especially by repairing or cleaning it
restore or renovate to look new

Examples

They decided to furbish up their old cottage before inviting guests to stay.
The company spent thousands to furbish up the conference room.
He managed to furbish up the antique desk, making it look almost new again.

Synset Examples

She repaired her TV set
Repair my shoes please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to original condition
  • Synonym: repair - to mend or fix something that is broken

Etymology

From Middle English 'furbish', from Old French 'forbir' (to polish, clean).

2. VERB

/ ˈfɜr.bɪʃ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfəː.bɪʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to renovate, restore, or improve something so it is in good condition or appears better
renovate or restore to good condition

Examples

We decided to furbish up the apartment before moving in.
It took several days to furbish up the old family photos for the exhibition.
They hired professionals to furbish up the historic mansion.

Synset Examples

She decided to furbish up her old furniture with new paint.
I need to furbish up my resume before applying.
They furbish up the garden every spring with fresh flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore something to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: to refurbish - to make clean, fresh or like new again

Etymology

From Middle English 'furbishen', from Old French 'forbir' (to polish), combined with 'up' to indicate improvement.
