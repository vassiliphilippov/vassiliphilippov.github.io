to drink in

1. VERB

/ drɪŋk ɪn / (AmE), / drɪŋk ɪn / (BrE)
to listen to, look at, or experience something with great attention and enjoyment; to absorb mentally or emotionally
absorb mentally with keen attention

Examples

The audience drank in every word of the inspiring speech.
She stood on the balcony, drinking in the beautiful sunset.
He drinks in every detail of her stories with childlike wonder.

Synset Examples

The mother drinks in every word of her son on the stage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (information, experience, etc.)
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

2. VERB

/ drɪŋk ɪn / (AmE), / drɪŋk ɪn / (BrE)
to look at, listen to, or experience something with great attention or enjoyment; to absorb eagerly
absorb or pay close attention to

Examples

She stood on the hilltop and drank in the breathtaking view.
The students drank in every word of their inspiring teacher.
He drank in the silence of the early morning before the city woke up.

Synset Examples

The mother drinks in every word of her son on the stage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savor - to enjoy something completely
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something
