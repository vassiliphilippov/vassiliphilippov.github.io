to put down a deposit

1. VERB

/ pʊt daʊn ə dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / pʊt daʊn ə dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to make an initial payment towards the purchase or rental of something, often to reserve it
make an initial payment

Examples

We had to put down a deposit before moving into the flat.
Before signing the contract, she put down a deposit to secure the car.
Many venues require you to put down a deposit to book a date for an event.

Synset Examples

They put down a deposit for the new apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay a deposit - to pay a sum of money in advance as security
