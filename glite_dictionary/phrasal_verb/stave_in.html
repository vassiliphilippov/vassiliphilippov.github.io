to stave in

1. VERB

/ steɪv ɪn / (AmE), / steɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to break or smash a hole in (something), especially breaking through the side of a container made with staves, such as a barrel or cask.
break a hole in, esp. a cask or barrel

Examples

The ship struck a rock, which staved in the hull.
Angry rioters staved in the shop window with metal bars.
He accidentally staved in the side of the old barrel while moving it.

Synset Examples

stave in a cask

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break in - to enter forcibly or damage by breaking

Etymology

From 'stave' (a thin strip of wood forming the sides of a barrel) + 'in'.

2. VERB

/ steɪv ɪn / (AmE), / steɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to break or force a hole into something; to crush or cave in part of an object
break or cave in a surface

Examples

The heavy blow caused the door to stave in, letting in a rush of cold air.
During the storm, some of the windows were staved in by flying debris.
He used a hammer to stave in the old barrel, breaking it apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash in - to violently break through or destroy a surface
  • Synonym: break - to damage or separate into pieces by force

Etymology

From 'stave' (a stick or narrow piece of wood), originally referring to breaking the staves of a barrel.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00311056-v
Synset POS: v
