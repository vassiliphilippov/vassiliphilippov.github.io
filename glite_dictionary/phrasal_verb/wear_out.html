to wear out

1. VERB

/ wɛr aʊt / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt / (BrE)
(of an object) to become unusable or break down from long or repeated use.
become unusable or break from use

Examples

After years of daily use, the washing machine finally wore out and stopped working.
The soles of his shoes wore out after months of hiking on rough terrain.
If you don’t replace the brake pads, they will wear out completely and cause damage.

Synset Examples

The lawn mower finally broke
The gears wore out
The old chair finally fell apart completely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break down - (of a machine or structure) to stop functioning or fall apart
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or apart, often due to structural failure
  • Synonym: to fall apart - to come apart or break into pieces

2. VERB

/ wɛr aʊt / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt / (BrE)
(of a thing) to become unusable or damaged because of repeated use, friction, or stress.
to deteriorate from use or stress

Examples

After years of heavy use, the engine finally wore out and had to be replaced.
If you don't rotate your tires, they will wear out much faster.
Her favorite jeans have started to wear out at the knees.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear down - to gradually reduce the strength or effectiveness of
  • Degree Higher: to destroy - to damage something so much that it no longer exists or works
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00470295-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ wɛr aʊt / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone or something very tired or no longer able to function by using too much or for too long
make exhausted or unusable through use

Examples

The children wore out their shoes playing soccer all summer.
After cleaning the house for hours, I was completely worn out.
Long meetings always seem to wear out the whole team.

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired
  • Antonym: to rejuvenate - to make someone feel younger or more lively again
