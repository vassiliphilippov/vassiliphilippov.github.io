to hunch over

1. VERB

/ hʌntʃ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / hʌntʃ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to bend forward with the shoulders and upper back rounded, often as a result of poor posture, concentration, or fatigue
to bend forward with rounded shoulders

Examples

After hours at the computer, she would hunch over and rub her back.
The old man tended to hunch over as he walked down the street.
Don't hunch over your desk or you'll strain your neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slouch - to sit or stand with a drooping posture

2. VERB

/ hʌntʃ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / hʌntʃ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to bend the upper part of your body forwards and down, typically with a rounded back and shoulders
bend forward; slouch

Examples

After hours of working, she began to hunch over her laptop, barely noticing her poor posture.
The old man would hunch over his cane as he walked down the street.
Children often hunch over their books while reading at their desks.

Synset Examples

Don't hunch over your desk; it’s bad for your back.
He had to hunch over to fit under the low doorway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slouch - to sit, stand, or walk with an awkward, drooping posture
