to get along with

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈlɔŋ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈlɒŋ wɪð / (BrE)
to have a friendly or harmonious relationship with someone
have a good relationship with someone

Examples

I really get along with my neighbors—they're friendly and helpful.
It's important to get along with your colleagues at work.
She doesn't get along with her brother, even though she tries.

Synset Examples

My boss and I get along very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get on with - have a good relationship with someone
  • Antonym: fall out with - to stop being friendly with someone after an argument
