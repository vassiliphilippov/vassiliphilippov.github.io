to chuck out

1. VERB

/ tʃʌk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃʌk aʊt / (BrE)
to throw something away or get rid of it
throw away; dispose of
informal

Examples

We decided to chuck out all the old magazines from the attic.
Jenny chucked out the leftovers after the dinner party.
It's time to chuck out those broken chairs in the garage.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw away - to get rid of something you do not want or need
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something

2. VERB

/ tʃʌk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃʌk aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place; to get rid of something by throwing it away
expel from a place; throw away
informal

Examples

They decided to chuck out all the old magazines cluttering the office.
The club will chuck out anyone who causes trouble during the event.
After the argument, he was chucked out of the restaurant by security.

Synset Examples

The unruly student was excluded from the game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw out - to expel or dispose of
  • Antonym: to let in - to allow to enter a place

Etymology

'Chuck' as a verb meaning to throw, possibly from Middle English 'chukken' (to throw).
