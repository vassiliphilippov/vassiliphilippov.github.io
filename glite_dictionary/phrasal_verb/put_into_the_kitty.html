to put into the kitty

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˈɪntu ðə ˈkɪti / (AmE), / pʊt ˈɪntə ðə ˈkɪti / (BrE)
to contribute money to a shared or communal fund, especially for a specific purpose
contribute to a shared fund
informal british

Examples

Everyone agreed to put into the kitty to cover the pizza costs.
She always forgets to put into the kitty when we go out for lunch.
Before starting the game, each player was asked to put into the kitty.

Synset Examples

He put money into the kitty for the group dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chip in - to contribute money or effort to a group cause

Etymology

From the noun 'kitty,' slang for a pool or fund of money, possibly from the card game term for a collective stake.
