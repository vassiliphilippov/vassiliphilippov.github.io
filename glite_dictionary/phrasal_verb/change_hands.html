to change hands

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ hændz / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ hændz / (BrE)
to pass into the possession of a new owner
become owned by someone else

Examples

The old mansion finally changed hands after years on the market.
Several valuable paintings change hands at the annual auction.
It's not uncommon for small businesses to change hands during economic downturns.

Synset Examples

This restaurant changed hands twice last year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - to move from one person or place to another
  • Antonym: to remain - to stay in the same ownership or condition

2. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ hændz / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ hændz / (BrE)
to pass into the possession of a different person or group; to be transferred or sold to a new owner
be transferred to new ownership

Examples

That antique vase will likely change hands at the auction tonight.
The popular restaurant has changed hands several times over the years.
As soon as the property changes hands, the new owners plan to renovate it.

Synset Examples

The company will change hands next week.
It’s exciting when properties change hands at auctions.
After years, the painting finally changed hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - move from one owner or place to another
  • Antonym: to acquire - to buy or gain possession of something
