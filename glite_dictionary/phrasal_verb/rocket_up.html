to rocket up

1. VERB

/ ˈrɑːkɪt ʌp / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt ʌp / (BrE)
(of a quantity or value) to increase rapidly or sharply.
rise or increase sharply

Examples

Housing prices continue to rocket up in major cities across the country.
Demand for the new smartphone has begun to rocket up since its release last month.
If costs rocket up again, many small businesses may struggle to survive.

Synset Examples

His popularity started to rocket up after that performance.
Sales are expected to rocket up during the holiday season.
The stock prices began to rocket up after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soar - to rise quickly or increase rapidly
  • Antonym: plummet - to fall or decrease sharply

Etymology

From 'rocket' (to move swiftly upward) + 'up', alluding to the rapid ascent of rockets.
