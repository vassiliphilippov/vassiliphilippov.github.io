to come down heavily

1. VERB

/ kʌm daʊn ˈhɛvɪli / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn ˈhɛvɪli / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall with great force; to rain very hard.
to rain very hard

Examples

It started to come down heavily just as we were leaving the office.
If it begins to come down heavily, the streets can flood very quickly.
The rain continued to come down heavily throughout the night, making it hard to sleep.

Synset Examples

The rain is expected to come down heavily tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pour (down) - to rain very heavily
