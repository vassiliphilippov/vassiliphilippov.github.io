to bundle up

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌndəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʌndl ʌp / (BrE)
to put on warm clothes; to dress oneself or someone else warmly, especially for cold weather
dress warmly

Examples

You should bundle up before going outside; it's freezing today.
The children bundled up in scarves and hats for the snowstorm.
Before leaving, she made sure everyone was bundled up against the chilly wind.

Synset Examples

Mother bundled up the children for the long way to school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wrap up - to dress warmly by putting on lots of clothing

Etymology

From 'bundle' (to wrap together) + 'up,' reflecting the act of wrapping oneself in clothing.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌndəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʌndl ʌp / (BrE)
to tie or wrap several things together into a bundle
wrap or tie things together

Examples

Before moving, she carefully bundled up her books with string.
He bundled up the old newspapers and set them by the door.
They bundled up their camping gear for the long hike.

Synset Examples

he bundled up his few possessions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to collect several things together
  • Antonym: to unwrap - to remove something from a wrapped bundle
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01489634-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌnd(ə)l ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.dl ʌp / (BrE)
to put on warm clothing, especially several layers, to protect oneself from cold weather
dress warmly in layers

Examples

Don't forget to bundle up before you go outside; it's freezing this morning.
The children bundled up in their coats and scarves for the snowball fight.
She always tells her kids to bundle up when the temperature drops.

Synset Examples

She looked so bundled up, I asked, 'Is your head cold?'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wrap up - to dress warmly by putting on extra clothing
