to pan out

1. VERB

/ pæn aʊt / (AmE), / pæn aʊt / (BrE)
to wash earth or gravel in a pan to separate out gold or other precious minerals
wash dirt to separate gold/minerals

Examples

The old prospector hoped that this riverbed would pan out enough gold to make him rich.
She spent hours panning out silt from the creek but found only a few flecks of gold.
They learned how to pan out minerals using simple equipment at the mining museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice - to wash gold-bearing material in a sluice box to extract gold
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ pæn aʊt / (AmE), / pæn aʊt / (BrE)
to develop in a particular way, especially to be successful or to have a good result
turn out successfully or as planned

Examples

We don't know if the investment will pan out as we hope.
After months of planning, the event finally panned out perfectly.
They're waiting to see how the experiment will pan out before making a decision.

Synset Examples

The idea panned out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work out - to have a good result or solution
  • Antonym: to fail - to not achieve the desired result

Etymology

Originally from gold panning, where 'panning out' refers to washing gravel to find gold.

3. VERB

/ pæn aʊt / (AmE), / pæn aʊt / (BrE)
to develop or turn out in a particular way, especially to be successful or satisfactory
develop or turn out successfully

Examples

We made several investments, but only a few actually panned out as we hoped.
The new strategy didn’t pan out and had to be revised last quarter.
If the weather pans out, we’ll have the picnic in the park.

Synset Examples

I hope this plan will pan out successfully.
Not all ideas pan out in the end.
Let's see how the project will pan out next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work out - to have a successful result; to be resolved positively
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful; to not turn out well

Etymology

Originally from mining jargon in the nineteenth century, referring to using a pan to separate gold from gravel.
