to begin to sprout

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɪn tə spraʊt / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə spraʊt / (BrE)
to start to grow or emerge, especially of plants or seeds
start to grow or emerge

Examples

After planting the seeds, they began to sprout within a few days.
The idea began to sprout in her mind as she walked through the museum.
Tiny leaves begin to sprout on the trees each spring.

Synset Examples

The garden began to sprout after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprout - to begin to grow; to send out new growth
