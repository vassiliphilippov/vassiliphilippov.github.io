to make a blunder

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈblʌndɚ / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
to commit a serious or embarrassing mistake
commit a serious mistake

Examples

He didn't check the details and made a blunder during the presentation.
If you make a blunder at work, it’s important to admit it quickly.
Politicians risk their careers if they make a blunder in public.

Synset Examples

She made a blunder by forgetting the client's name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a serious or embarrassing mistake

Etymology

From the noun 'blunder' (a mistake), which is of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'blundra' (to shut one's eyes).
