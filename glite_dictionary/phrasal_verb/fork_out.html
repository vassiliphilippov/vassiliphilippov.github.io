to fork out

1. VERB

/ fɔrk aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) to pay or hand over money, especially unwillingly or with reluctance.
pay money reluctantly
informal

Examples

After months of saving, she finally had to fork out for a new laptop.
He wasn't happy about having to fork out so much money for repairs.
They expect us to fork out extra charges every time we use the service.

Synset Examples

the guard delivered the criminal to the police
render up the prisoners
render the town to the enemy
fork over the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shell out - to pay money for something, usually reluctantly
  • Synonym: to cough up - to reluctantly give or pay money

Etymology

Probably derived from the idea of handing something over by using a forked instrument, metaphorically extended to money.
