to creep in

1. VERB

/ krip ɪn / (AmE), / kriːp ɪn / (BrE)
to enter or become noticeable gradually or surreptitiously, especially in a way that is not immediately obvious
enter or become noticeable gradually or secretly

Examples

Doubts began to creep in as the project deadline approached.
She tried to concentrate, but thoughts of her family would always creep in.
During the meeting, several unexpected issues managed to creep in unnoticed.

Synset Examples

He sneaked in under cover of darkness
In this essay, the author's personal feelings creep in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sneak in - to enter a place secretly or without being noticed
  • Synonym: to slip in - to move or enter somewhere quietly or quickly, often without being noticed
