to feel jit

1. VERB

/ fil dʒɪt / (AmE), / fiːl dʒɪt / (BrE)
To feel nervous or anxious, especially in anticipation of something.
to feel nervous or anxious (slang)
slang

Examples

I always feel jit before speaking in front of a large crowd.
There's no reason to feel jit about your interview—you'll do great!
She admitted she was feeling jit as the exam day approached.

Synset Examples

He's feeling jit about the big presentation tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - feeling worried or anxious about something
  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness
