to begin to mushroom

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɪn tə ˈmʌʃruːm / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə ˈmʌʃrʊm / (BrE)
to start growing or expanding very quickly and dramatically
start growing or expanding rapidly

Examples

After its initial success, the company began to mushroom across the continent, opening new branches everywhere.
Rumors began to mushroom following the politician's unexpected resignation last week.
As word spread online, interest in the art exhibit began to mushroom far beyond the local community.

Synset Examples

The business began to mushroom after the successful campaign.
Her popularity started to mushroom after the viral video.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - to increase suddenly and rapidly
  • Synonym: boom - to experience a sudden period of growth or success
  • Antonym: contract - to decrease in size, number, or extent

Etymology

From the noun 'mushroom', referencing the fast way mushrooms appear and spread.
