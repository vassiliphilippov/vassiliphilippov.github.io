to give away

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to reveal secret or confidential information about someone, often unintentionally or as a betrayal
reveal secret information about someone

Examples

She accidentally gave away his surprise party plans.
Under pressure, he gave away the names of his accomplices.
Don't give away my secret to anyone.

Synset Examples

He told on his classmate who had cheated on the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to reveal information that should be kept secret, especially to an enemy
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to not say anything about something, especially a secret
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00843665-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to make a gift of something; to give something to someone without expecting payment or anything in return
to give something as a gift

Examples

She decided to give away most of her books before moving to a smaller apartment.
The bakery will give away free cupcakes to the first fifty customers.
He didn't want to give away his old guitar, but he knew his friend would appreciate it.

Synset Examples

She gave away her antique furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to donate - to give something, especially to a charity or for a good cause, often formally
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02206637-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of a bride) to formally hand over to the bridegroom in marriage, traditionally performed by the father of the bride.
formally hand over bride at wedding

Examples

It was an emotional moment when her father gave away the bride.
Traditionally, the father gives away his daughter at the start of the ceremony.
When her brother gave her away, everyone in the audience was moved to tears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hand over - to give something to someone else, often formally
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to make information known to the public that was previously secret or only known by a few people; to reveal or disclose unintentionally or deliberately
reveal or disclose information

Examples

He accidentally gave away the surprise party plans to his wife.
Please don't give away any details about the movie's ending.
The journalist refused to give away her sources.

5. VERB

/ ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)

reveal or disclose unintentionally

Examples

Don't let your face give away the surprise party we're planning.

Synset Examples
