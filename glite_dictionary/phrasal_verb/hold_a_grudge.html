to hold a grudge

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ə ɡrʌdʒ / (AmE), / həʊld ə ɡrʌdʒ / (BrE)
to continue feeling angry or resentful toward someone for a past offense or slight
continue feeling resentment about a past offense

Examples

Despite the years that had passed, he still held a grudge against his old friend.
It's better to move on than to hold a grudge over something minor.
She tried not to hold a grudge, even when people disappointed her.

Synset Examples

She tends to hold a grudge against anyone who wrongs her.
It's not healthy to hold a grudge for so long.
She believes life is too short to hold grudges.
It's petty to hold grudges over small mistakes.
We should make peace instead of holding grudges.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone
