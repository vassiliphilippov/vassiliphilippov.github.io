to check through

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk θru / (AmE), / tʃɛk θruː / (BrE)
to examine or review something carefully and thoroughly, especially by looking at all parts or items
examine thoroughly

Examples

I will check through all the files to make sure nothing is missing.
You should check through your work before submitting it to the teacher.
He spent the afternoon checking through old letters in the attic.

Synset Examples

She will check through the documents before the meeting.
Make sure to check through the report for errors.
I need to check through my emails for important updates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go through - to examine all parts of something carefully
  • Degree Lower: scan - to look at something carefully in order to find something specific
