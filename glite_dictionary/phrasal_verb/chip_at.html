to chip at

1. VERB

/ tʃɪp æt / (AmE), / tʃɪp æt / (BrE)
to remove small pieces from the surface of something, especially by repeated small blows or cuts, often to shape or engrave it
remove small pieces from a surface

Examples

He used a knife to chip at the bark and carve his initials into the tree.
The sculptor spent weeks chipping at the marble block to reveal the figure within.
Please do not chip at the paint on the wall, or it will start to peel.

Synset Examples

carve one's name into the bark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carve - to cut into a surface to make a design or shape
  • Synonym: chisel - to shape or cut with a chisel
