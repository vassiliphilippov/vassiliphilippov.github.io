to take issue

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / teɪk ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
to disagree or express a different opinion, especially when challenging a statement or viewpoint
to disagree or express a differing opinion

Examples

I must take issue with your interpretation of the data.
She did not hesitate to take issue with the proposal during the meeting.
Many experts take issue with the new policy, arguing it will be ineffective.

Synset Examples

I beg to differ!
She disagrees with her husband on many questions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to agree - to have the same opinion; to concur
