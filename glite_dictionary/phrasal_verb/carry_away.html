to carry away

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæri əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or transport someone or something from one place, state, or situation to another, often emotionally or physically
remove or transport to another place or state

Examples

Hearing the news, she was completely carried away with excitement.
The river carried away several trees during the flood.
Sometimes, good books carry us away to another world.

Synset Examples

Their dreams carried the Romantics away into distant lands
The car carried us off to the meeting
I'll take you away on a holiday
I got carried away when I saw the dead man and I started to cry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take away - to remove something from a place
  • Antonym: to bring back - to return something to its original place or state
