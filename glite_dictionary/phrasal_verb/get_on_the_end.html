to get on the end

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn ði ɛnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ðiː ɛnd / (BrE)
to take a position at the end of a line, row, or queue; to join as the last person or thing
join the last position in a line or queue

Examples

If you want to buy a ticket, you need to get on the end of the line.
The coach told the latecomers to get on the end of the bench.
She always tries to get on the end when there's a group photo.

Synset Examples

Make sure you get on the end of the table.
You need to get on the end of that line for tickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join the queue - to become part of a line or queue
