to keep off

1. VERB

/ kip ɔf / (AmE), / kiːp ɒf / (BrE)
to avoid entering or stepping onto a particular place or area
avoid entering an area

Examples

Please keep off the newly planted grass while it grows.
They put up a sign to warn hikers to keep off private property.
Children are reminded to keep off the construction site for their safety.

Synset Examples

keep off the grass
stay off the premises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay off - to avoid going onto something or somewhere

2. VERB

/ kip ɔf / (AmE), / kiːp ɒf / (BrE)
to avoid eating, drinking, or using a particular food, beverage, or substance
avoid consuming something

Examples

My doctor told me to keep off sugar to improve my health.
He has managed to keep off alcohol for six months now.
During Lent, many people keep off meat on Fridays.

Synset Examples

I keep off drugs
During Ramadan, Muslims avoid tobacco during the day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abstain - to deliberately avoid doing or consuming something, especially for moral or religious reasons
  • Synonym: to give up - to stop doing or using something, often as a resolution

3. VERB

/ kip ɔf / (AmE), / kiːp ɒf / (BrE)
to avoid or stay away from someone or something
avoid or stay away from

Examples

The doctor told me to keep off fatty foods for the sake of my health.
Wild animals tend to keep off areas where humans frequently gather.
Please keep off the grass while it is being reseeded.

Synset Examples

He advised her to keep off the streets late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to stay away from or prevent oneself from doing something
  • Antonym: approach - to come near or nearer to something or someone
