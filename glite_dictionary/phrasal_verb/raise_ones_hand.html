to raise one's hand

1. VERB

/ reɪz wʌnz hænd / (AmE), / reɪz wʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to lift one's hand, especially to signal a desire to speak, ask a question, or draw attention
lift one's hand as a signal

Examples

If you have a question, please raise your hand before speaking.
During the vote, every member raised his hand in agreement.
She raised her hand to get the teacher's attention.

Synset Examples

He raised his palm to signal for silence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put up one's hand - to lift one's hand as a signal
  • Antonym: lower one's hand - to bring one's hand down after raising it
