to punch in

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ ɪn / (AmE), / pʌntʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to record the time of one's arrival at work by using a special machine or system
register time of arrival at work

Examples

Every morning, employees are required to punch in as soon as they arrive.
I always forget to punch in when my shift starts, and my manager reminds me.
She punched in late today because her train was delayed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to punch out - to record the time of leaving work

2. VERB

/ pʌntʃ ɪn / (AmE), / pʌntʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to record the time of arrival at work by clocking in, typically using a time clock or electronic system
register arrival at work (time clock)

Examples

Employees are required to punch in before starting their shift each morning.
She forgot to punch in and had to ask her manager to correct her hours.
If you don't punch in on time, your pay could be docked.

Synset Examples

He was late and forgot to punch in his hours.
Don't forget to punch in when you arrive at work.
Make sure to punch in before the shift starts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clock in - to record one's arrival at work (synonym)
  • Antonym: to punch out - to record the time of departure when leaving work (antonym)

Etymology

From 'punch' (to operate a mechanical device) and 'in', referring to activating the time record upon arrival.
