to magic up

1. VERB

/ ˈmædʒɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to create or produce something quickly or unexpectedly, as if by magic
produce/create suddenly or unexpectedly

Examples

She managed to magic up a delicious meal from almost nothing in the fridge.
If you can't find a taxi, I'll try to magic up a ride for you.
He seemed to magic up energy whenever it was really needed.

Synset Examples

Can you magic up a solution for this problem?
We need to magic up some extra chairs for tonight.
He managed to magic up a fantastic idea at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conjure up - to bring to mind or create as if by magic
  • Synonym: to rustle up - to quickly gather or make something, especially food or resources
  • Antonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something
