to draw back

1. VERB

/ drɔ ˈbæk / (AmE), / drɔː ˈbak / (BrE)
to use a surgical instrument to hold open the edges of a wound or an organ during a procedure
hold open edges of a wound with an instrument
medical

Examples

The surgeon instructed the nurse to draw back the tissue using the retractor.
Special forceps were used to draw back the edges of the wound for better visibility.
Once the area was exposed, the assistant carefully drew back the skin to allow access to the organ.

2. VERB

/ drɔː ˈbæk / (AmE), / drɔː ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move away or backwards, especially because of fear, surprise, or a change of mind
move away or backwards

Examples

She instinctively drew back when the dog barked loudly.
He drew back his hand before touching the hot surface.
As the curtain drew back, the actors took their places on stage.

Synset Examples

The enemy withdrew
The limo pulled away from the curb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to move back or away; to remove oneself from a place
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or ahead
