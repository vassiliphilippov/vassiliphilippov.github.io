to factor in

1. VERB

/ ˈfæk.tɚ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ɪn / (BrE)
to include something as a relevant element when making a decision or calculation
take into account

Examples

Did you factor in the possibility of delays when planning your schedule?
It's important to factor in all expenses before setting the final budget.
She hadn't factored in how much time the project would actually take.

Synset Examples

You must factor in the recent developments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take into account - to consider or remember something when judging a situation
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately not consider something

Etymology

From 'factor' (noun: an element that influences a result) + 'in' (preposition).

Examples
