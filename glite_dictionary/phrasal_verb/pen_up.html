to pen up

1. VERB

/ pɛn ʌp / (AmE), / pɛn ʌp / (BrE)
to confine (an animal or person) in a small enclosure or space
confine in an enclosure

Examples

The shepherd had to pen up the flock before nightfall.
During the storm, the cows were penned up in the barn for safety.
Don't pen up the children all day; let them play outside.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: release - to set free from confinement

Etymology

From 'pen' (verb, to confine in an enclosure), ultimately from Old English 'penn'.
