to hammer out

1. VERB

/ ˈhæmɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈhamə aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss something forcefully and thoroughly in order to reach a decision or agreement
reach agreement after thorough discussion

Examples

The two companies spent months trying to hammer out the terms of their merger.
After a long debate, the committee finally hammered out a compromise.
Diplomats are working late into the night to hammer out a ceasefire agreement.

Synset Examples

The leaders of the various Middle Eastern countries are trying to hammer out a peace agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - try to reach an agreement by discussion
  • Degree Lower: to agree - have the same opinion or come to a mutual decision
