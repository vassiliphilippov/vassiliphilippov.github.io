to dig out

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from beneath earth, snow, or a similar covering by digging
remove by digging up from beneath

Examples

After the storm, it took hours to dig out the car from under the thick snow.
The workers had to dig out the old pipe buried deep in the garden.
They dug out the trapped hikers who were buried by the avalanche.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to make a hole or remove earth carefully
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ dɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to create or form something by removing earth or other material with a tool, hands, or a machine
create by digging

Examples

The workers had to dig out a trench for the new pipeline.
Farmers often dig out irrigation channels to water their crops.
They plan to dig out a pond in the backyard this summer.

Synset Examples

dig out a channel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to make a hole or channel by digging; to remove earth carefully

3. VERB

/ dɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove, harvest, or recover something from the ground by digging
remove or recover by digging

Examples

The workers had to dig out the coal from deep underground.
Every autumn, farmers dig out the potatoes before the ground freezes.
Archaeologists carefully dug out the ancient pottery from the ruins.

Synset Examples

dig coal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to dig out material or objects, especially for archaeological or construction purposes
  • Antonym: to bury - to place something in the ground and cover it, the opposite of digging out

4. VERB

/ dɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to find or retrieve something that has been buried, hidden, or forgotten, or to remove something from being covered or trapped
find or remove something hidden or buried

Examples

I had to dig out my passport from a box in the closet.
They used shovels to dig out the car buried under the snow.
She finally dug out her childhood letters from the bottom drawer.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unearth - to dig up or discover something buried; synonym of 'dig out'
