to wave off

1. VERB

/ weɪv ɑf / (AmE), / weɪv ɒf / (BrE)
To dismiss something as unimportant or not worthy of consideration.
dismiss as unimportant

Examples

The CEO waved off reporters' questions about the controversy.
She tried to apologize, but he waved off her concerns as unnecessary.
Despite warnings from his colleagues, he waved off any fears about the plan.

Synset Examples

He waved off suggestions of impropriety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - refuse to consider or accept
  • Synonym: to ignore - refuse to take notice of
