to clean out

1. VERB

/ klin aʊt / (AmE), / kliːn aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or eject someone or something completely from a place, especially as a result of being unwanted
remove or eject entirely from a place

Examples

After the scandal, the company cleaned out several corrupt executives.
The landlord threatened to clean out tenants who didn't pay their rent on time.
A new manager was hired to clean out the department of underperforming staff.

Synset Examples

The new boss cleaned out the lazy workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evict - to force someone to leave a place, especially legally
  • Synonym: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02408550-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klin aʊt / (AmE), / kliːn aʊt / (BrE)
to deprive someone completely of money or possessions, often by theft, spending, or loss
deprive completely of money or goods

Examples

The burglars managed to clean out the entire house while the owners were away.
After losing at the casino, he was completely cleaned out and had to borrow money.
The new tax bill nearly cleans out my savings every year.

Synset Examples

The robbers cleaned us out in a couple of hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place
  • Synonym: empty - to remove all contents from something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02319764-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ klin aʊt / (AmE), / kliːn aʊt / (BrE)
to empty something completely, especially by removing all its contents
empty completely by removing all contents

Examples

I need to clean out the garage before we move.
She cleaned out her desk on her last day at work.
We spent the afternoon cleaning out the attic.

Synset Examples

We cleaned out all the drawers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty - to remove all the contents from something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00449819-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ klin aʊt / (AmE), / kliːn aʊt / (BrE)
to remove all unwanted or unnecessary items from a place, such as a container, room, or piece of furniture
remove unwanted items

Examples

Every spring, I clean out the garage to get rid of old tools and boxes.
She spent the afternoon cleaning out her desk at work.
We really need to clean out the refrigerator before buying more groceries.

Synset Examples

He decided to clean out his wardrobe this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clear out - to remove the contents of something, especially unwanted items
