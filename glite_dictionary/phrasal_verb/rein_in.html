to rein in

1. VERB

/ reɪn ɪn / (AmE), / reɪn ɪn / (BrE)
to control or limit something or someone, especially to stop it from becoming too extreme
control or limit; restrain

Examples

The government has promised to rein in public spending this year.
If you don't rein in your excitement, you might scare the animals away.
She struggled to rein in her impatience during the long meeting.

Synset Examples

He reined in his horses in front of the post office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to prevent someone or something from doing something; to control
  • Antonym: to let loose - to allow to be free or uncontrolled

Etymology

From the noun 'rein', meaning the straps used to control a horse, and the preposition 'in'.
