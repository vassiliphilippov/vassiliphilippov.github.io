to stand guard

1. VERB

/ stænd ˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / stænd ˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
to remain in a position to watch over and protect a person, place, or thing
watch over and protect

Examples

At the entrance, two soldiers stand guard throughout the night.
I volunteered to stand guard while the others slept in the tent.
The dog would stand guard by the gate, alert to any intruders.

Synset Examples

We must stand sentinel to protect ourselves
The jewels over which they kept guard were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep watch - to remain alert and observe, especially for protection
  • Synonym: to stand sentinel - to act as a guard standing in place to watch for danger
