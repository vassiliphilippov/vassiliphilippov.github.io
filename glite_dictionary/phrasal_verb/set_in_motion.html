to set in motion

1. VERB

/ sɛt ɪn ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / sɛt ɪn ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
to cause something to begin or to start a process or series of events
to cause something to start happening

Examples

The president's speech set in motion a series of reforms across the country.
Their investment set in motion the development of the new shopping center.
A simple idea can sometimes set in motion significant changes in society.

Synset Examples

launch a career
Her actions set in motion a complicated judicial process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to begin or start something
  • Antonym: stop - to bring something to an end or halt
