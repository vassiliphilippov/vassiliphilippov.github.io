to turn away

1. VERB

/ tɝn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to refuse to allow someone to enter a place or become a member of a group or organization
refuse entrance or membership

Examples

The doorman had to turn away several visitors who didn't have tickets.
Many applicants were turned away because the program was already full.
They turn away anyone who cannot provide proper identification.

Synset Examples

They turned away hundreds of fans
Black people were often rejected by country clubs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - refuse to accept or consider someone or something
  • Antonym: to accept - to agree to admit or receive someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02508565-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɝn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move your body or head so as not to face someone or something
move so as not to face

Examples

She had to turn away because the sight was too painful to bear.
Whenever he passed the mirror, he would turn away quickly.
The children turned away from the television when the scary scene began.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to turn around - move so as to face the opposite direction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01912802-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tɝn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move from a straight course, fixed direction, or line of interest; to divert or swerve from continuing forward.
move or divert from a straight course

Examples

The pilot had to turn away from the storm to ensure everyone's safety.
She tried to turn away from the distractions and focus on her work.
At the last moment, the driver turned away to avoid hitting the animal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to veer - to change direction suddenly
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make straight or move in a straight direction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02038145-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ tɝn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move in a different direction, especially to avoid looking at something
to move or look in another direction

Examples

She turned away so she wouldn't have to watch the scary scene.
Please turn away if you feel uncomfortable looking at the surgery.
He quickly turned away when his name was called.

Synset Examples

They averted their eyes when the King entered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avert - to turn away (one's eyes or thoughts)
