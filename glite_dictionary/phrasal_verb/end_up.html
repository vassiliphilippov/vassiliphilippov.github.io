to end up

1. VERB

/ ɛnd ʌp / (AmE), / ɛnd ʌp / (BrE)
to finally reach a particular place, condition, or situation, often without having planned it
reach a final state or situation

Examples

He didn't study for the test and ended up failing it.
After getting lost, we ended up at a tiny café by the river.
If you keep spending like this, you'll end up with nothing left in your account.

Synset Examples

He ended up marrying his high school sweetheart
he wound up being unemployed and living at home again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wind up - to arrive in a specified situation, condition, or position, usually unexpectedly
