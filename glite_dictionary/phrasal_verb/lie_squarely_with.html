to lie squarely with

1. VERB

/ laɪ ˈskwɛrli wɪð / (AmE), / laɪ ˈskwɛəli wɪð / (BrE)
(of responsibility, blame, etc.) to rest firmly or unequivocally with a particular person, group, or thing.
be firmly assigned to someone

Examples

The blame for the error lies squarely with the supervisor who approved the report.
In this situation, the obligation lies squarely with the company to ensure safety measures are implemented.
Responsibility for the project’s failure lies squarely with the management team.

Synset Examples

The responsibility lies squarely with the management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rest with - (of responsibility or decision) to be with someone
