to close in

1. VERB

/ kloʊz ɪn / (AmE), / kləʊz ɪn / (BrE)
to surround something or someone completely; to enclose or hem in.
surround or enclose completely

Examples

As the mist began to close in, visibility dropped sharply across the fields.
They decided to close in the patio to protect it from the wind.
The police were able to close in on the suspects by blocking all the exit routes.

Synset Examples

Darkness enclosed him
They closed in the porch with a fence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround, shut in on all sides
  • Synonym: to hem in - to surround and restrict the movement of
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01589983-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kloʊz ɪn / (AmE), / kləʊz ɪn / (BrE)
to move nearer to someone or something in order to surround or catch them; to advance closer, especially in pursuit.
move nearer to surround or catch

Examples

The police were about to close in on the suspect when he slipped away.
As the storm clouds began to close in, we quickly packed up our picnic.
Enemy troops started to close in from every direction, leaving the defenders with little room to maneuver.

Synset Examples

The police were closing in on him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to come nearer in distance
  • Antonym: retreat - to move away from someone or something
