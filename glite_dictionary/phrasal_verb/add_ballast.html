to add ballast

1. VERB

/ æd ˈbæl.əst / (AmE), / æd ˈbæl.əst / (BrE)
to put weight into a vehicle, vessel, or structure in order to improve its stability and balance
put weight in for stability

Examples

To prevent the vessel from capsizing, they decided to add ballast before departure.
Engineers had to add ballast to the rail tracks for added support.
The airship's crew needed to add ballast to maintain the desired altitude.

Synset Examples

They added ballast to stabilize the ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballast - heavy material placed for stability
