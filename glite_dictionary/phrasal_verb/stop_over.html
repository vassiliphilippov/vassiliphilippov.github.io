to stop over

1. VERB

/ stɑp ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / stɒp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to interrupt a journey temporarily, especially to stay somewhere overnight before continuing to your final destination
temporarily interrupt journey, often overnight

Examples

We decided to stop over in Paris for a night before heading to Rome.
Their flight stopped over in Dubai, giving them a chance to see the city.
Many travelers stop over in Singapore on their way to Australia.

Synset Examples

We had to stop over in Venezuela on our flight back from Brazil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layover - a short stay somewhere between two parts of a journey
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02659017-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈstɑp ˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈstɒp ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to interrupt a journey by staying somewhere temporarily before continuing to your destination
interrupt a journey with a temporary stay

Examples

We decided to stop over in Paris for two nights on our way to Rome.
Many international flights stop over in Dubai before continuing to Asia.
She prefers not to stop over and takes direct flights whenever possible.

Synset Examples

we stopped at Aunt Mary's house
they stopped for three days in Florence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay over - to make a short stay somewhere during a journey
