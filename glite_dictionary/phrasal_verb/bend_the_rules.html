to bend the rules

1. VERB

/ bɛnd ðə ruːlz / (AmE), / bɛnd ðə ruːlz / (BrE)
to deviate from established guidelines, regulations, or norms, often to accommodate a special circumstance
deviate from guidelines or norms

Examples

Sometimes managers need to bend the rules to help their employees succeed.
The teacher agreed to bend the rules just this once due to the unique situation.
If we bent the rules for everyone, the system wouldn't work at all.

Synset Examples

As a strict rule-follower, she never bends the rules.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: break the rules - to violate or disobey rules completely
  • Antonym: follow the rules - to obey or adhere to rules or guidelines
