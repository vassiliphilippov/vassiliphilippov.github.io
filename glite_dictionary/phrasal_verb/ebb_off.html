to ebb off

1. VERB

/ ɛb ɔf / (AmE), / ɛb ɒf / (BrE)
to decrease or recede, especially by flowing back gradually, as in the tide or in intensity or strength
recede or decrease gradually

Examples

As the excitement of the game faded, the crowd's energy began to ebb off.
After the storm passed, the floodwaters gradually ebbed off from the fields.
Interest in the new product started to ebb off after the initial launch.

Synset Examples

the tides ebbed at noon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subside - to become less intense, violent, or severe
  • Synonym: to recede - to move back or away, withdraw

Etymology

From 'ebb' (Old English 'ebba', meaning 'receding tide') + 'off'.
