to con out of

1. VERB

/ kɑn aʊt əv / (AmE), / kɒn aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to trick someone into giving you something, especially money, by deceiving them
trick someone into giving something

Examples

She was conned out of her life savings by an online scammer.
Tourists are often conned out of their money by clever pickpockets in the city.
He realized too late that he had been conned out of his watch at the card table.

Synset Examples

The grifter conned several people out of their savings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: cheat out of - to deprive someone of something by deception

Etymology

From "con," meaning to swindle or deceive, itself a shortening of "confidence trick."
