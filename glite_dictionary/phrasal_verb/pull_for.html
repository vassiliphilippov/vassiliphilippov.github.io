to pull for

1. VERB

/ pʊl fɔr / (AmE), / pʊl fə / (BrE)
to support or root for someone or something, especially in a competition or difficult situation
support or root for (someone or something)

Examples

The whole class was pulling for their teammate during the spelling bee.
I'm really pulling for you to get the job you applied for.
Despite the odds, she knew her family would pull for her all the way.

Synset Examples

We all rooted for the home team
I'm pulling for the underdog
Are you siding with the defender of the title?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to root for - to support or cheer for someone or something
  • Synonym: to side with - to support or align oneself with a person or group
