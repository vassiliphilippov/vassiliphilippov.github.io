to make a speech

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə spiːtʃ / (AmE), / meɪk ə spiːtʃ / (BrE)
to deliver a formal talk to an audience, especially on a public or ceremonial occasion
deliver a formal talk

Examples

The president will make a speech at the opening ceremony tonight.
She was nervous before she had to make a speech in front of her colleagues.
After winning the award, he stood up to make a speech to the audience.

Synset Examples

He stood upright, ready to make his speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give a speech - to deliver a formal talk
  • Synonym: to deliver a speech - to present a formal talk to an audience
