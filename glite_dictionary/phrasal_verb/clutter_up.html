to clutter up

1. VERB

/ ˈklʌt̬ɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈklʌtə ʌp / (BrE)
to fill a space with too many things, making it untidy or disorganized
make a space untidy and full

Examples

Too many decorations can clutter up a small apartment and make it feel cramped.
Please don’t clutter up the kitchen counters with unnecessary appliances.
Old newspapers and magazines often clutter up the living room until someone organizes them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make untidy or disorganized
  • Antonym: to clear out - to remove things to make a place tidy
