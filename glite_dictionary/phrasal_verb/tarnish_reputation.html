to tarnish reputation

1. VERB

/ ˈtɑr.nɪʃ ˌrɛp.jəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.nɪʃ ˌrɛp.jʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
to damage or reduce the respect and good opinion that people have for someone or something
damage someone's good name

Examples

The scandal could tarnish reputation of the entire organization.
One careless remark is enough to tarnish reputation built over years.
She feared the rumors would tarnish reputation among her colleagues.

Synset Examples

His lies will tarnish his reputation forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage reputation - to harm someone's good name or public opinion
  • Antonym: restore reputation - to improve or recover one's good name
