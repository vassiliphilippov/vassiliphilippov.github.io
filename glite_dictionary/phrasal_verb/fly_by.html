to fly by

1. VERB

/ flaɪ baɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ baɪ / (BrE)
(of an aircraft, bird, or other flying object) to move past something or someone while flying.
pass by while flying

Examples

The jet flew by the crowd during the airshow, leaving everyone in awe.
Several geese flew by our window early this morning.
I watched as the helicopter flew by on its way to the hospital.

Synset Examples

An enemy plane flew by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly past - (of a flying object) move past while flying
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02056282-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ flaɪ baɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ baɪ / (BrE)
(of time or events) to seem to pass very quickly.
to pass very quickly (of time/events)

Examples

The summer seemed to fly by and soon it was time for school again.
Those two hours really flew by while we were watching the movie.
When you're having fun, the afternoon often just flies by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to zip by - to pass quickly (of time or objects)
  • Antonym: to drag on - to seem to take a long time

Synset POS: v
