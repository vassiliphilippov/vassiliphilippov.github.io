to wrap a meeting

1. VERB

/ ræp ə ˈmitɪŋ / (AmE), / ræp ə ˈmiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
to bring a meeting to an end; to conclude the proceedings of a meeting
conclude a meeting
informal

Examples

Let's wrap the meeting so everyone can get to lunch on time.
We need to wrap the meeting in the next five minutes because another team needs the room.
After discussing all the points, the manager suggested they wrap the meeting.

Synset Examples

They decided to wrap the meeting early today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjourn - to formally end or suspend a meeting or session
  • Antonym: begin a meeting - to start a meeting

Etymology

From the use of 'wrap up' meaning 'to conclude or finish', originating from the idea of wrapping something to finish with it.
