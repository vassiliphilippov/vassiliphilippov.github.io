to crack on

1. VERB

/ kræk ɑn / (AmE), / kræk ɒn / (BrE)
to continue doing something, especially energetically or with renewed effort
continue with energy or effort
british informal

Examples

Once the meeting ended, we decided to crack on with our work.
If you want to finish by noon, you'd better crack on.
Despite the setbacks, the team managed to crack on and complete the project.

Synset Examples

Let’s crack on with the project before the deadline.
We need to crack on if we want to finish early.
He urged everyone to crack on with the tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to keep doing something without stopping
  • Synonym: to press on - to continue moving forward or making progress, despite difficulties
