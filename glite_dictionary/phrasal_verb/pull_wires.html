to pull wires

1. VERB

/ pʊl waɪɚz / (AmE), / pʊl waɪəz / (BrE)
to use secret or devious means to influence or control a situation or the actions of others, especially for personal gain
secretly influence or control events

Examples

He managed to get the job because he knew how to pull wires behind the scenes.
Some politicians are accused of pulling wires to secure favors for their supporters.
She tried to pull wires in order to get her friend out of trouble with the authorities.

Synset Examples

He manipulated public opinion in his favor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull strings - use one's influence secretly, especially for personal advantage

Etymology

From the imagery of manipulating puppet strings or wires to control movements behind the scenes.
