to make up one's mind

1. VERB

/ meɪk ʌp wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / meɪk ʌp wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to decide on something after thinking about it; to come to a decision
to decide; come to a decision

Examples

She couldn't make up her mind about which college to attend.
After hours of discussion, he finally made up his mind.
I wish you would make up your mind so we can move forward.

Synset Examples

We finally decided after lengthy deliberations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decide - to make a choice or come to a conclusion
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause before deciding or acting

2. VERB

/ meɪk ʌp wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / meɪk ʌp wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to decide on something after considering various possibilities; to reach a conclusion
decide after consideration

Examples

It took her days to make up her mind about the new car.
Have you made up your mind where you want to eat tonight?
He can't make up his mind between the two universities.

Synset Examples

I need to make up my mind about the job offer.
It's time to make up your mind about the vacation plans.
She finally made up her mind to move abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decide - to choose after consideration
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to be slow to decide or uncertain
