to let in

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɪn / (AmE), / lɛt ɪn / (BrE)
to allow someone to become a member of a group, organization, or profession; to permit participation or admittance.
permit to join or participate

Examples

Only a few students were let in to the advanced math club after a rigorous selection process.
After years of hard work, she was finally let in to the engineering society.
He hopes they will let in more international members to broaden the group's perspective.

Synset Examples

admit someone to the profession
She was admitted to the New Jersey Bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to allow someone to enter a place or join a group
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02454712-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɛt ɪn / (AmE), / lɛt ɪn / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to enter a place
allow to enter

Examples

Please let in the delivery driver when he arrives.
The security guard refused to let in anyone without a ticket.
Can you let in some fresh air by opening the window?

Synset Examples

We cannot admit non-members into our club building
