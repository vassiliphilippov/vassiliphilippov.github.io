to lock up

1. VERB

/ lɑk ʌp / (AmE), / lɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to secure something, such as a building or valuables, by fastening with a lock, typically to prevent unauthorized access
secure by fastening with a lock

Examples

Please lock up the house before you leave for work.
She always makes sure to lock up her jewelry in the safe.
Don't forget to lock up the office at the end of the day.

Synset Examples

lock up the house before you go on vacation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to make something safe from danger or threat
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01351134-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɑk ʌp / (AmE), / lɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a place that cannot be left or removed, usually to keep them safe or to prevent escape
to confine securely

Examples

The police decided to lock up the suspect overnight for questioning.
Make sure you lock up your valuables before you leave the hotel room.
They threatened to lock up anyone found trespassing after dark.

Synset Examples

The parents locked her daughter up for the weekend
She locked her jewels in the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep someone or something within limits
  • Antonym: release - to set someone or something free
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01350360-v
Synset POS: v
