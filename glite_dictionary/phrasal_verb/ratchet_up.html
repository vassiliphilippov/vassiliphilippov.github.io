to ratchet up

1. VERB

/ ˈrætʃ.ɪt ʌp / (AmE), / ˈrætʃ.ɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or escalate something gradually, especially in a way that is difficult to reverse
to increase or escalate gradually

Examples

The company plans to ratchet up production to meet the rising demand.
Tensions between the two countries continued to ratchet up over the dispute.
They decided to ratchet up security measures after the recent breach.

Synset Examples

a ratcheting lopping tool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - to make or become larger in amount or number
  • Antonym: decrease - to make or become smaller in amount or number

Etymology

From 'ratchet', a device allowing movement in one direction only, plus 'up'.
