to build on

1. VERB

/ bɪld ɑn / (AmE), / bɪld ɒn / (BrE)
to use something as a foundation or starting point for further development, argument, or progress
use as a basis for further development

Examples

The new theory builds on previous research in the field.
You should build on your strengths when approaching this project.
Her argument builds on several established scientific principles.

Synset Examples

what is this new evidence built on?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: base on - to use something as the main part or reason for something else
