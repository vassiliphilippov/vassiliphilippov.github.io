to run aground

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a ship or boat) to hit the sea bottom or shore and become stuck or unable to move.
(of a vessel) become stuck on ground or shore

Examples

The ferry ran aground during last night's storm, causing a major rescue operation.
If the yacht runs aground in shallow water, it may take hours to refloat it.
Many old ships have run aground on these treacherous reefs over the centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go aground - of a ship, to go onto the bottom or shore and become stuck

2. VERB

/ rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a ship or boat) to be brought onto the shore or into shallow water so that it cannot move off without assistance.
(of a ship) be brought onto shore or shallow water

Examples

The ship ran aground during last night's storm and could not be refloated.
Several fishing boats have run aground in the shallow waters of the bay this year.
The captain took quick action to avoid running aground on the hidden rocks.

Synset Examples

the storm grounded the ship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground - (of a ship) to bring a vessel onto shore or shallow water so it cannot move

3. VERB

/ rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a ship or boat) to become stuck on land or in shallow water and unable to move.
become stuck on shore (of a ship)

Examples

The cargo ship ran aground during the storm and could not be freed for days.
Fishermen had to be rescued after their boat ran aground on the rocky coast.
If you don't pay attention to the tide, you might run aground in shallow water.

Synset Examples

The ship was marooned after running aground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beach - to run or be driven onto the shore (of a ship)
