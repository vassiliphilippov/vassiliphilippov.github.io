to get in front of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪn ˈfrʌnt ʌv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ˈfrʌnt əv / (BrE)
to proactively address, manage, or anticipate an issue, challenge, or situation before it becomes problematic or urgent
act proactively to address an issue

Examples

We need to get in front of any potential problems before they escalate.
It's important to get in front of negative publicity by releasing a statement early.
The company is trying to get in front of the latest technology trends.

Synset Examples

We must get in front of the changes to stay relevant.
If we want to lead, we must get in front of the issue.
To succeed, we must get in front of our competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to foresee and act before something happens
  • Antonym: to react - to respond to something after it has happened
