to jot down

1. VERB

/ dʒɑt daʊn / (AmE), / dʒɒt daʊn / (BrE)
to write something quickly and briefly, often as a short note
write something quickly and briefly

Examples

She quickly jotted down the phone number before she forgot it.
During the meeting, he jotted down a few important points in his notebook.
Whenever I have a new idea, I try to jot down my thoughts instantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note down - to write something so that you do not forget it
  • Synonym: to write down - to put something on paper from memory or speech

Etymology

From 'jot' (meaning 'a very small amount', from Latin 'iota'), combined with 'down' meaning 'to note'.

2. VERB

/ dʒɑt daʊn / (AmE), / dʒɒt daʊn / (BrE)
to write something quickly, often as a note or reminder
write something quickly

Examples

Whenever an idea pops into her head, she jots down a quick note in her phone.
Can you jot down your contact details for me before you leave?
He jotted down the directions to the new restaurant on a napkin.

Synset Examples

I always jot down ideas when they come to me.
Please jot down your thoughts in this notebook.
Don't forget to jot down the address before you leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note down - to write something as a note
  • Synonym: to write down - to record information by writing it

Etymology

From 'jot' (meaning: to write briefly), likely from Latin 'iota' (the Greek letter, used metaphorically for something very small).
