to make space

1. VERB

/ meɪk speɪs / (AmE), / meɪk speɪs / (BrE)
to create room or an area for something by removing obstacles or rearranging things
create room for something

Examples

We need to make space in the garage for the new car.
She moved the books to make space for the vase on the shelf.
Can you make space at the table for another guest?

Synset Examples

I had to throw out the old furniture to make space.
He had to wheel out the old furniture to make space.
They had to move furniture to make space for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear space - to remove items to make an area available
