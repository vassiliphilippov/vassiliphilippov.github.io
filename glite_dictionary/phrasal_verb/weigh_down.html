to weigh down

1. VERB

/ weɪ daʊn / (AmE), / weɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to make something heavier or cause it to bend or sink by placing weight on it
make heavy or press down

Examples

Heavy books can weigh down the shelves over time.
The thick snow began to weigh down the tree branches dangerously.
She felt her backpack weighing down her as she walked up the hill.

Synset Examples

The snow bore down on the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load heavily or trouble someone with a burden
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01600017-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ weɪ daʊn / (AmE), / weɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to make someone feel sad, anxious, or oppressed; to cause a feeling of burden or discouragement
oppress or cause discouragement

Examples

The stress of his new job began to weigh down on him.
She felt weighed down by the responsibilities of caring for her family.
Don't let negative thoughts weigh down your spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppress - keep (someone) in hardship, especially by the unjust exercise of authority
  • Antonym: to cheer up - make or become less sad or anxious
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01818233-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ weɪ daʊn / (AmE), / weɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to make someone or something heavy, or to cause them to feel burdened, either physically or emotionally
make heavy or cause to feel burdened

Examples

The extra equipment began to weigh down the rescue team as they climbed the hill.
She felt that her responsibilities at work were starting to weigh down her spirits.
The branches were weighed down with heavy snow after the storm.

Synset Examples

The backpack was too heavy and began to weigh him down.
Her worries started to weigh down on her mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load heavily or cause worry; to weigh down
  • Antonym: to lighten - to make lighter or less heavy; to relieve a burden

4. VERB

/ weɪ daʊn / (AmE), / weɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to make someone feel worried, sad, or burdened, especially emotionally
make someone feel burdened or weighed emotionally

Examples

Don’t let your past mistakes weigh you down and stop you from moving forward.
She felt her responsibilities at work weighing her down more each day.
After the argument, guilt seemed to weigh him down for weeks.

Synset Examples

Don't let your doubts weigh you down during the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to trouble someone with worries or responsibilities
