to gloss over

1. VERB

/ ɡlɑs ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡlɒs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to try to make a fault, mistake, or problem seem less serious than it really is, often by ignoring or lightly mentioning it
minimize faults or errors

Examples

The company tried to gloss over the poor financial results in its annual report.
Politicians often gloss over their past mistakes when speaking to voters.
She attempted to gloss over her role in the incident, but the truth eventually came out.

Synset Examples

Let's not whitewash the crimes of Stalin
She tried to gloss over her mistakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitewash - to deliberately conceal unpleasant facts about someone or something
  • Synonym: to cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a mistake or crime

Etymology

Originally refers to applying a shiny coating ('gloss') to mask imperfections.

2. VERB

/ ɡlɑs ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡlɒs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to deal with a problem, error, or unpleasant fact hastily or superficially so as to avoid paying attention to it
treat superficially; avoid full discussion

Examples

She tried to gloss over the mistakes in her report, but they were too obvious to ignore.
The politician attempted to gloss over the scandal during his speech.
Don't gloss over the facts—tell us exactly what happened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skim over - to deal with something only superficially or briefly
  • Synonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

From 'gloss' (to explain or comment on a text) + 'over', meaning to make something appear better or less serious than it is.

3. VERB

/ ɡlɑs ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡlɒs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to deliberately try to make something seem unimportant or to avoid giving details about it, especially when discussing problems or faults
avoid discussing in detail; minimize problems

Examples

The company spokesperson tried to gloss over the recent security breach at the press conference.
It's tempting to gloss over the negative aspects when sharing your achievements.
Politicians sometimes gloss over their past mistakes during election campaigns.

Synset Examples

His report was just a gloss over the main issues.
He tried to gloss over his mistakes during the presentation.
Don't gloss over the details; they matter.
She tends to gloss over the difficult topics in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skirt - to avoid dealing with or talking about something directly
  • Synonym: to downplay - to make something appear less important than it really is
  • Antonym: highlight - to emphasize or draw attention to something

Etymology

From the verb 'gloss' (to explain) and 'over' indicating covering up; sense developed in the late 17th century.
