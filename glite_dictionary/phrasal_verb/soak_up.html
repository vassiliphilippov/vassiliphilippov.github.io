to soak up

1. VERB

/ soʊk ʌp / (AmE), / səʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to absorb a liquid or, metaphorically, to take in information, experience, or atmosphere completely
absorb (liquid or experience)

Examples

The towel will soak up any spills on the counter.
She likes to soak up the atmosphere at local cafes.
He soaked up every word of advice his mentor gave him.

Synset Examples

The sponge absorbs water well
She drew strength from the minister's words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (liquid, energy, or information)

2. VERB

/ soʊk ʌp / (AmE), / səʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to devote oneself fully to something or become deeply absorbed in it
devote oneself fully; become absorbed

Examples

She decided to soak up everything she could about the new culture.
He loves to soak up knowledge in every book he reads.
During the internship, she tried to soak up as much experience as possible.

Synset Examples

He immersed himself into his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immerse oneself - to involve oneself deeply in a particular activity or interest

3. VERB

/ soʊk ʌp / (AmE), / səʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to absorb liquid completely, especially by using a material like a sponge or cloth
absorb liquid fully

Examples

The paper towels quickly soak up the spilled milk on the counter.
She used a cloth to soak up the water from the floor.
These sponges are designed to soak up large amounts of liquid efficiently.

Synset Examples

The sponge can soak up all the spilled water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up a liquid or other substance
