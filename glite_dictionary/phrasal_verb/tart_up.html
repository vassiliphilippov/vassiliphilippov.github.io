to tart up

1. VERB

/ tɑrt ʌp / (AmE), / tɑːt ʌp / (BrE)
to decorate or dress something in a way that is showy, flashy, and often lacking in good taste
to decorate or dress in a showy, flashy way
informal british

Examples

They decided to tart up the shop with neon signs to attract more customers.
The old theater was tarted up for the festival, but the decorations looked rather cheap.
She spent hours tarting up the living room before the party.

Synset Examples

the small-town bar was all tarted up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes

Etymology

From 'tart' (slang for prostitute or a woman of flashy appearance) + 'up'.

2. VERB

/ tɑrt ʌp / (AmE), / tɑːt ʌp / (BrE)
to dress or decorate in a way that is cheap, vulgar, or meant to attract attention, often in a provocative or tasteless manner
dress or decorate in a flashy, vulgar way
informal british

Examples

She decided to tart up the living room with some brightly colored cushions and cheap ornaments.
The critics complained that the film tried to tart up history with sensational details.
He tarted up his old car before putting it up for sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or special clothes
  • Antonym: to tone down - to make less showy or less extreme in style or appearance
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
