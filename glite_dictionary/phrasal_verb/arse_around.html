to arse around

1. VERB

/ ɑrs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɑːs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a silly or aimless way; to waste time by not being serious
fool about; waste time
informal british

Examples

Stop arsing around and help me with this assignment.
They were arsing around in the hallway after class and nearly got caught.
If you arse around all day, you'll never finish your work.

Synset Examples

Enough horsing around — let's get back to work!
The bored children were fooling about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess around - to behave in a playful or idle way
  • Synonym: horsing around - to indulge in playful or silly behavior
  • Antonym: work - to exert effort for a purpose

Etymology

From British slang 'arse' (buttocks) + 'around'.

2. VERB

/ ɑrs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɑːs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a lazy or aimless way; to waste time doing nothing productive
be lazy or waste time
informal british

Examples

Stop arsing around and help me with these boxes.
He spent the weekend arsing around with his friends instead of finishing his assignment.
They were arsing around in the park all afternoon.

Synset Examples

Her son is just bumming around all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess around - spend time doing unimportant or trivial things
  • Synonym: to fool around - behave in a silly or aimless way
  • Antonym: work hard - put in effort and diligence

Etymology

From British English slang 'arse' (buttocks) + 'around'; similar to American 'goof around.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645690-v
Synset POS: v
