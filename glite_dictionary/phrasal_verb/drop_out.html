to drop out

1. VERB

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to stop participating in a competition, activity, or group, usually because of difficulty, defeat, or lack of hope
stop participating (esp. after difficulty)

Examples

After injuring his leg, he had to drop out of the race.
Many students drop out when the course becomes too challenging.
She didn't want to drop out, but her health gave her no choice.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something because it is too difficult
  • Antonym: to continue - to keep doing something without stopping
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01085437-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave school, college, or an educational program before finishing it, especially without obtaining a qualification
leave school or program before finishing

Examples

After failing several classes, Mark decided to drop out of college.
Many students drop out because they cannot afford the tuition anymore.
She regretted her decision to drop out years later when job opportunities were limited.

Synset Examples

Many students drop out because they are not prepared for our challenging program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to leave a course or program by choice
  • Antonym: graduate - to successfully complete a course of study
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02388080-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to withdraw from society or stop participating in conventional life, especially because of disillusion with its values
withdraw from conventional life or society

Examples

Many young people chose to drop out of mainstream society in the 1960s.
After years of stress, he decided to drop out and live a simpler life in the countryside.
She felt disconnected from conventional values, so she eventually dropped out.

Synset Examples

She hasn't heard from her brother in years — he dropped out after moving to California

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to opt out - to choose not to participate in something
  • Antonym: to conform - to behave in accordance with convention or expectations
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave school, college, or a competition before finishing it, especially before completing a course of study
to leave school or a competition before finishing

Examples

After his father lost his job, Tom had to drop out of university to help support the family.
Several athletes were forced to drop out of the race because of injuries.
She regretted her decision to drop out before completing her final semester.

Synset Examples

Many students drop out due to financial issues.
She had to drop out of the competition last minute.
He decided to drop out of college to start a business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to remove oneself from participation in something
  • Antonym: to enroll - to sign up for a course or institution (opposite sense)
