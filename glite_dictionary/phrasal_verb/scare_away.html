to scare away

1. VERB

/ skɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / skeə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to make someone or something go away or stay away because of fear or intimidation
make flee due to fear

Examples

The loud noise scared away the birds from the garden.
Rising prices may scare away potential buyers.
She tried not to make any sudden moves that would scare away the stray cat.

Synset Examples

dashed by the refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten off - to make someone or something go away by causing fear
  • Antonym: to attract - to draw someone or something toward rather than repel

2. VERB

/ skɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / skeə(r) əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to leave or stay away because of fear or intimidation
cause to leave due to fear

Examples

The loud noise was enough to scare away the birds from the garden.
Rising crime rates may scare away potential investors from the area.
If you shout too much, you might scare away your friends.

Synset Examples

His ghost stories were enough to scare the hoes away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten off - to make someone or something go away by making them feel afraid
  • Antonym: to attract - to draw someone or something nearer or encourage their approach
