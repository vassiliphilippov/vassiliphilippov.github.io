to strike up

1. VERB

/ straɪk ʌp / (AmE), / straɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to begin a conversation, relationship, or activity, often in a sudden or informal way
to begin something, such as a conversation

Examples

It can be hard to strike up a conversation with strangers at a party.
They quickly struck up a friendship after meeting at university.
Whenever the band strikes up a lively tune, everyone starts to dance.

Synset Examples

strike up a conversation
strike up a friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin something
  • Antonym: end - to bring to a conclusion or finish
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01645853-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ straɪk ʌp / (AmE), / straɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to begin to play music or start singing, especially by a group or band
begin to play music or sing

Examples

As soon as the guests arrived, the band struck up a lively tune.
During the ceremony, the orchestra will strike up the national anthem.
Whenever he entered the room, the pianist would strike up his favorite song.

Synset Examples

The musicians struck up a tune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start doing something
  • Antonym: to cease - to stop doing something; to bring to an end
