to urge on

1. VERB

/ ɝdʒ ɑn / (AmE), / ɜːdʒ ɒn / (BrE)
to encourage, force, or impel someone or something to move forward or take action, especially with determination or persistence
encourage or drive someone/something forward

Examples

She tried to urge on her team even though they were losing.
The coach's words seemed to urge on the exhausted runners.
Fans lined the route to urge on the cyclists during the race.

Synset Examples

I urged him to finish his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Antonym: to discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɜrdʒ ɑn / (AmE), / ɜːdʒ ɒn / (BrE)
to encourage someone or something to move forward or to try harder, especially by words or gestures
encourage to greater effort

Examples

The coach continued to urge on the runners even when they were tired.
She tried to urge on her teammates by clapping and shouting their names.
The excited fans urged on their team to victory with loud cheers.

Synset Examples

The crowd cheered the demonstrating strikers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Synonym: spur on - to urge someone to try harder or to take action
  • Antonym: to hold back - to restrain or prevent someone from moving forward or trying harder
