to sniff out

1. VERB

/ snɪf aʊt / (AmE), / snɪf aʊt / (BrE)
to discover, recognize, or detect something, especially by searching or through intuition, sometimes by smelling
detect or discover, often by searching or intuition

Examples

The journalist managed to sniff out the truth behind the company’s financial scandal.
Dogs are trained to sniff out drugs and explosives at airports.
It didn’t take long for her to sniff out that something was wrong.

Synset Examples

He can smell out trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detect - to discover or identify the presence of something
  • Synonym: sense - to perceive or become aware of something
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or detect something

2. VERB

/ snɪf aʊt / (AmE), / snɪf aʊt / (BrE)
to discover or detect something, especially by searching or investigating
detect or discover by investigation

Examples

The reporter managed to sniff out the truth behind the company's financial problems.
Our dog can easily sniff out hidden treats in the garden.
She was able to sniff out the source of the gossip quickly.

Synset Examples

He was able to sniff out the deceit in her words.
The detective can sniff out a lie instantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detect - to discover or identify the presence of something
  • Synonym: to uncover - to find out about something hidden or secret
