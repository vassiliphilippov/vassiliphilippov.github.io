to speak in tongues

1. VERB

/ spiːk ɪn tʌŋz / (AmE), / spiːk ɪn tʌŋz / (BrE)
to utter speech or sounds, typically unintelligible, as a result of religious ecstasy or spiritual experience, especially in some Christian traditions
utter unintelligible speech in religious ecstasy

Examples

During the revival service, several people began to speak in tongues as part of the worship.
Some churches believe that the ability to speak in tongues is a sign of the Holy Spirit.
He claimed that he could speak in tongues when he felt moved by spiritual energy.

Synset Examples

The parishioners spoke in tongues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossolalia - the phenomenon of speaking in an unknown language, especially in religious worship
