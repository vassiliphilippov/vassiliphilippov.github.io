to set apart

1. VERB

/ sɛt əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / sɛt əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to separate or distinguish someone or something from others; to keep something isolated or reserved for a specific purpose.
separate or distinguish from others

Examples

Her exceptional artistic style set apart her work from that of her peers.
The company sets apart a portion of its profits for charity every year.
Strict hygiene protocols set apart this hospital from others in the area.

Synset Examples

The dentist sequesters the tooth he is working on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to cause to move or be apart
  • Synonym: to distinguish - to recognize or treat as different
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00496744-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛt əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / sɛt əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to select someone or something for a special purpose or duty
select for a special purpose

Examples

The school decided to set apart one student to represent the class at the ceremony.
She was set apart from the rest of the team to handle customer service.
They set apart these funds especially for emergency repairs.

Synset Examples

The teacher assigned him to lead his classmates in the exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to choose - to pick out or select someone or something
  • Synonym: to set aside - to reserve something for a specific purpose
