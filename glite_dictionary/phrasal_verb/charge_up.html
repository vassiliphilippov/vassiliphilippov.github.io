to charge up

1. VERB

/ tʃɑrdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to cause (someone or something) to become highly excited, agitated, or emotionally roused
make excited or emotionally stirred

Examples

The coach managed to charge up the team just before the final match.
An energetic song can really charge up a crowd at a concert.
Her passionate speech charged up the audience and inspired them to take action.

Synset Examples

The speaker charged up the crowd with his inflammatory remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excite - to cause strong feelings of enthusiasm or eagerness
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become or make less agitated or excited

2. VERB

/ tʃɑrdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to restore electrical power to a battery or electronic device by connecting it to a power source
restore electrical power to a device

Examples

I forgot to charge up my electric toothbrush last night, so the battery died this morning.
She needs to charge up her phone before leaving for the day.
Don't forget to charge up the camera batteries before the hike.

Synset Examples

Make sure to charge up your laptop overnight.
Let's charge up the batteries for the camera.
You need to charge up your phone before the trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recharge - to restore electrical energy, especially to a battery
