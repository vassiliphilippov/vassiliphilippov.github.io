used to

1. AUX

/ ˈjuːst tə / (AmE), / ˈjuːst tə / (BrE)
to indicate that something was regularly done or was the case in the past but is no longer true
did something regularly in the past

Examples

I used to live in New York before moving to London.
She used to play tennis every weekend with her friends.
They used to believe that the company would never close.

Synset Examples

She used to call her mother every week but now she calls only occasionally
I used to get sick when I ate in that dining hall
They used to vacation in the Bahamas
she used to be his best friend and now they are enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: would - auxiliary verb for repeated past actions (e.g. 'When I was a child, I would play outside')
