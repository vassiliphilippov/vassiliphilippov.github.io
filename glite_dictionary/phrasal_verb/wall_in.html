to wall in

1. VERB

/ wɔl ɪn / (AmE), / wɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose something or someone by building a wall or walls around them
enclose with or by a wall

Examples

The old garden was completely walled in to keep out intruders.
They plan to wall in the patio to create a sunroom.
The prisoner felt walled in after years in the small cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround or close off on all sides
  • Synonym: wall off - to separate or divide with or as if with a wall
