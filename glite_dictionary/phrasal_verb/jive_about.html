to jive about

1. VERB

/ dʒaɪv əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / dʒaɪv əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to talk or discuss something casually, often in a lively or informal way
talk casually or informally
informal american

Examples

We sat around the kitchen table and jived about our favorite childhood memories.
They spent the whole afternoon jiving about music and movies.
Whenever they get together, they love to jive about their travel adventures.

Synset Examples

They decided to jive about their travel plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: banter - exchange remarks in a good-humored teasing way

Etymology

Originally American slang from the 1920s, 'jive' meaning 'deceptive or playful talk', of uncertain origin.
