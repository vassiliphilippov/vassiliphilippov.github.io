to scrape together

1. VERB

/ skreɪp təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / skreɪp təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to manage to collect or obtain something, especially money or resources, with difficulty or great effort
gather with difficulty

Examples

After months of saving, they finally managed to scrape together enough money for a deposit.
We had to scrape together supplies from several different sources to finish the project.
He could only scrape together a few dollars for lunch.

Synset Examples

They managed to scrape together enough funds for the trip.
She scraped together her savings to buy a new car.
She managed to scrape together some slopdosh for dinner.
We could only scrape together a small team for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrounge - seek to obtain something with difficulty or by persuasion
