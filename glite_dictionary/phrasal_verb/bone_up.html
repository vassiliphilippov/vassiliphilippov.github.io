to bone up

1. VERB

/ boʊn ʌp / (AmE), / bəʊn ʌp / (BrE)
to study intensively, especially for a short period before an exam or important event
study intensively before an exam
informal

Examples

She had to bone up on chemistry before her big test.
I usually bone up on my Spanish vocabulary before traveling to Mexico.
Let’s bone up on the company history for tomorrow’s interview.

Synset Examples

I had to bone up on my Latin verbs before the final exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cram - to study intensively over a short period of time, especially before an exam

Etymology

Probably from the use of 'Bohn's Classical Library' study guides in the 19th century, and the idea of cramming knowledge from books.

2. VERB

/ boʊn ʌp / (AmE), / bəʊn ʌp / (BrE)
to study something intensively, especially in preparation for an examination or important event
study intensively (especially for exams)
informal

Examples

I need to bone up on physics before tomorrow's test.
She spent the weekend boning up on legal terminology for her interview.
He quickly boned up on the company's history before his presentation.

Synset Examples

I had to bone up on my Latin verbs before the final exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cram - to study hard in a short time, especially before an exam

Etymology

Probably from 'bone', slang for 'hard work or study', origin uncertain.
