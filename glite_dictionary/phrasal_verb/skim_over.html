to skim over

1. VERB

/ skɪm oʊvɚ / (AmE), / skɪm əʊvə / (BrE)
to move or pass swiftly and lightly just above or across the surface of something
move swiftly over a surface

Examples

A flock of birds skimmed over the lake at sunrise.
The stone skimmed over the water before finally sinking.
She watched the dragonfly skimming over the surface of the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - move smoothly and continuously along a surface
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01250114-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skɪm ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / skɪm ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to read or examine something quickly and superficially, without paying close attention to detail
read quickly and superficially

Examples

She decided to skim over the report before the meeting, just to get the main points.
I usually skim over the headlines in the morning instead of reading every article in detail.
He skimmed over the contract but missed some important clauses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scan - to look through something quickly to find specific information
  • Antonym: read thoroughly - to read with close attention to every detail
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00629486-v
Synset POS: v
