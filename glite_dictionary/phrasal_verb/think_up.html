to think up

1. VERB

/ θɪŋk ʌp / (AmE), / θɪŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to devise or invent (a plan, idea, solution, etc.) by thinking
devise or invent by thinking

Examples

She managed to think up a clever excuse for being late.
The marketing team will think up new ideas for the campaign.
He tried to think up a way to solve the difficult problem.

Synset Examples

He thought up a plan to get rich quickly
no-one had ever thought of such a clever piece of software

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: to brainstorm - to generate ideas or solutions by thinking creatively

2. VERB

/ θɪŋk ʌp / (AmE), / θɪŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to invent or devise (an idea, plan, excuse, etc.), usually suddenly or creatively
devise or invent (an idea, plan, etc.)

Examples

She managed to think up a clever excuse for being late.
The marketing team needs to think up a new slogan for the product.
It took him hours to think up the perfect birthday surprise.

Synset Examples

Can you think up a creative solution for our problem?
It’s hard to think up new content every week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to invent or plan something, especially something difficult or complicated
  • Synonym: to conceive - to form an idea or a plan in the mind
