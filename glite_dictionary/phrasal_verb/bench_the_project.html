to bench the project

1. VERB

/ bɛntʃ ðə ˈprɑːdʒɛkt / (AmE), / bɛntʃ ðə ˈprɒdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to suspend or delay work on a project, typically temporarily or until certain conditions are met
suspend or delay a project

Examples

Due to budget constraints, the company had to bench the project until next year.
Management decided to bench the project when new information came to light.
We should not bench the project unless we have a clear alternative plan.

Synset Examples

They will bench the project until next quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put on hold - to postpone or temporarily stop something
  • Antonym: resume the project - to restart work on a delayed or paused project

Etymology

From the sports usage of 'bench', meaning to remove a player from active participation.
