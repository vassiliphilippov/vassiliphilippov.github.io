to play up

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ʌp / (AmE), / pleɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to emphasize or make something more noticeable or prominent
emphasize or give prominence to

Examples

The company tried to play up its environmental achievements in the annual report.
He tends to play up his experience when applying for jobs.
During the meeting, she played up the team's recent successes to impress the board.

Synset Examples

The introduction highlighted the speaker's distinguished career in linguistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out or draw attention to it
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pleɪ ʌp / (AmE), / pleɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone, often in an insincere or flattering way
behave insincerely to gain favor

Examples

He always tries to play up to his boss in meetings to get promoted.
She was playing up to the new manager, laughing at all his jokes.
Don't play up to the teacher just to get a better grade.

Synset Examples

She is playing up to the chairman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise someone insincerely to please them
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01808720-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pleɪ ʌp / (AmE), / pleɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to exaggerate the importance or significance of something, often to gain attention or advantage
emphasize or exaggerate something

Examples

Politicians often play up certain achievements to gain voters' trust.
The media tends to play up minor incidents into big stories.
She tried to play up her experience during the interview.

Synset Examples

He likes to play up his achievements to impress others.
She tends to play up her role in the project.
Don't play up the situation; it's not that serious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as being larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
