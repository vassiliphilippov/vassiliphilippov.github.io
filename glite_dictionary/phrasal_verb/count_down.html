to count down

1. VERB

/ kaʊnt daʊn / (AmE), / kaʊnt daʊn / (BrE)
to count backwards to zero, especially in preparation for something happening, such as the start of an event or the detonation of a bomb
count backwards, especially before starting something

Examples

We will count down from ten before launching the rocket.
The children counted down as the new year approached.
They were counting down to the big moment when the fireworks began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: count - to recite or list numbers in order
  • Antonym: count up - to count numbers in ascending order
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00949749-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kaʊnt daʊn / (AmE), / kaʊnt daʊn / (BrE)
To count numbers in reverse order to indicate the time remaining before an event begins.
count backward to a starting point

Examples

The children gathered around to count down from ten before the fireworks began.
We always count down the last few seconds before midnight on New Year's Eve.
As the rocket prepared for launch, engineers counted down to zero.

Synset Examples

She counted down the seconds like a tic tac clock.
The team counted down to liftoff with excitement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: count up - to count numbers forward, usually from zero or one upward
