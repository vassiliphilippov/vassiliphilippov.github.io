to spin up a meeting

1. VERB

/ spɪn ʌp ə ˈmitɪŋ / (AmE), / spɪn ʌp ə ˈmiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
to organize or arrange a meeting, especially quickly or in response to a new need
organize or arrange a meeting
informal

Examples

Let me spin up a meeting so we can align on the new priorities.
We had to spin up a meeting with the engineering team after the outage occurred.
She offered to spin up a meeting with the client to clarify expectations.

Synset Examples

I’ll spin up a meeting to discuss the changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange a meeting - to organize a meeting
  • Antonym: cancel a meeting - to call off or remove a planned meeting

Etymology

From technical jargon 'spin up' (to start or initialize a process or machine), adapted to meetings in business/tech contexts.
