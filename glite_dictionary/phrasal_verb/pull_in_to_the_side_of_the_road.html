to pull in to the side of the road

1. VERB

/ pʊl ɪn tu ðə saɪd əv ðə roʊd / (AmE), / pʊl ɪn tu ðə saɪd əv ðə rəʊd / (BrE)
to steer a vehicle towards and stop near the edge of the road
stop a vehicle at the roadside

Examples

The driver pulled in to the side of the road to let the ambulance pass.
She noticed a strange noise and decided to pull in to the side of the road to check her tyres.
When it started raining heavily, we pulled in to the side of the road and waited for the storm to pass.

Synset Examples

The officer signaled to pull in to the side of the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull over - to drive a vehicle to the roadside and stop
