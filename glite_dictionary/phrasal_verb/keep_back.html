to keep back

1. VERB

/ kip bæk / (AmE), / kiːp bæk / (BrE)
to prevent oneself or someone else from expressing an emotion or reaction openly
prevent the expression of emotion

Examples

She tried to keep back her tears during the emotional speech.
He couldn't keep back his frustration when he heard the news.
They struggled to keep back their laughter at the joke.

Synset Examples

hold back your anger
keep your cool
she struggled to restrain her impatience at the delays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to restrain oneself from acting or expressing something
  • Synonym: to restrain - to prevent someone from doing something, especially by physical force or by restricting freedom
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kip ˈbæk / (AmE), / kiːp ˈbak / (BrE)
to reserve or retain something for future use or application, not using or giving all of it at once.
to reserve or retain for later use

Examples

Each year, the company keeps back some profits as an emergency fund.
Please keep back enough cake for the guests who arrive late.
They kept back several tickets for VIPs.

Synset Examples

The landlord retained the security deposit
I reserve the right to disagree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve - to keep something for a particular use or future time
  • Synonym: to set aside - to keep something separate for a special purpose
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02288169-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kip ˈbæk / (AmE), / kiːp ˈbæk / (BrE)
to withhold or refuse to give or share something; to prevent something from being made known or distributed
withhold or refuse to share

Examples

The company decided to keep back important details about the project until the official announcement.
She tried to keep back her emotions during the farewell ceremony.
The teacher kept back some test results until all the students had finished.

Synset Examples

The father is withholding the allowance until the son cleans his room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired
  • Antonym: to give away - to make something known or let something go to others
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02218472-v
Synset POS: v
