to issue a ukase

1. VERB

/ ˈɪʃu ə juˈkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪsjuː ə juˈkeɪs / (BrE)
to give or announce an authoritative or absolute order or decree
give an absolute order or decree

Examples

When the CEO decided to issue a ukase, all company policies changed overnight.
The principal issued a ukase banning phones on school grounds.
He is known to issue a ukase the moment he senses any disorder.

Synset Examples

The manager issued a ukase about the new dress code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decree - an official order with the force of law
  • Synonym: edict - an official public order or command

Etymology

From Russian 'ukaz' (imperial decree), via French 'ukase'.
