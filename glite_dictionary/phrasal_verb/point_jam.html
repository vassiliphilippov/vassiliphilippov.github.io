to point jam

1. VERB

/ pɔɪnt dʒæm / (AmE), / pɔɪnt dʒam / (BrE)
to deliberately jam or interfere with a narrow band of radio frequencies, usually for tactical or electronic warfare purposes
jam a specific narrow radio band

Examples

Military communications can become unreliable if the enemy attempts to point jam vital frequency bands.
The new device can point jam enemy transmitters with remarkable accuracy.
Engineers developed a technique to point jam only the channels most commonly used for coordination.

Synset Examples

We can counter point-jamming effectively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - to deliberately interfere with radio signals
