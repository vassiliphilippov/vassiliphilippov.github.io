to press down

1. VERB

/ prɛs daʊn / (AmE), / prɛs daʊn / (BrE)
to push or apply force downward onto something
push downward

Examples

Please press down firmly on the stamp to get a clear impression.
She had to press down the lid to close the box properly.
Use your fingers to press down on the dough until it's flat.

Synset Examples

Depress the space key

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depress - to press down or lower physically
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01577597-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prɛs daʊn / (AmE), / prɛs daʊn / (BrE)
to apply pressure to make something flatter, lower, or more compact, or to close it tightly
apply pressure to flatten or close

Examples

She used her palm to press down the wet shirt onto the paper towels.
You have to press down gently when closing the sandwich maker.
With both hands, he began to press down on the suitcase to fit everything inside.

Synset Examples

You need to press down harder to make the dough.
He had to press down the lid to seal the jar.
Make sure to press down on the brake pedal firmly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatten - to make or become flat or flatter
