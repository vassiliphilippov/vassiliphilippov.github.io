to push one's way through

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ wʌnz weɪ θru / (AmE), / pʊʃ wʌnz weɪ θruː / (BrE)
to move forward by forcing a passage through people or obstacles
move forward by forcing passage

Examples

She had to push her way through the crowded market.
The firefighter pushed his way through the smoke to reach the trapped family.
Tourists often find themselves pushing their way through busy city streets during festivals.

Synset Examples

She had to push her way through the crowded market.
He managed to push his way to the front of the line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elbow one's way - to make one's way forward using one's elbows
  • Synonym: force one's way - to make progress by using force
