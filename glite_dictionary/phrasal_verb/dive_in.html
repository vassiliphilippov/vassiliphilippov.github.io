to dive in

1. VERB

/ daɪv ɪn / (AmE), / daɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to start doing something with enthusiasm and energy, often without hesitation
start something eagerly or energetically

Examples

When the new project was announced, she couldn't wait to dive in and get started.
The buffet was ready, and everyone was eager to dive in.
He decided to dive in and learn a new language over the summer.

Synset Examples

It's time to dive in and start the project.
Don't hesitate, just dive in and join the fun!
He was excited to dive in and explore the new game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump in - to begin or interrupt with sudden enthusiasm
  • Antonym: to hold back - to hesitate or refrain from acting
