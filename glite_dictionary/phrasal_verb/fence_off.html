to fence off

1. VERB

/ fɛns ɔf / (AmE), / fɛns ɒf / (BrE)
to enclose an area by putting up a fence, typically to prevent access or separate it from surrounding areas
enclose an area with a fence

Examples

They decided to fence off the playground to keep the children safe.
Farmers often fence off their fields to protect crops from animals.
A portion of the park was fenced off during construction work.

Synset Examples

She decided to fence off the garden to protect her plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to close off on all sides
  • Antonym: to open up - to make accessible or remove enclosure
