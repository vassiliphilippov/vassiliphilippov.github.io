to sign up

1. VERB

/ saɪn ʌp / (AmE), / saɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to register or put one's name on a list to become a member of a group, participate in an activity, or receive a service
register to participate or join

Examples

I decided to sign up for the photography workshop next weekend.
They encouraged employees to sign up for the new health insurance plan.
Have you signed up for the school newsletter yet?

Synset Examples

Sign up for yoga classes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enroll - to officially register as a member of an institution or course
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01099720-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ saɪn ʌp / (AmE), / saɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to agree to become involved in an activity, job, or service, often by signing a written agreement
agree to participate or engage formally

Examples

I decided to sign up for piano lessons after seeing the school advertisement.
She signed up as a volunteer at the local community center last year.
Many athletes sign up for marathons months before the actual event.

Synset Examples

They signed two new pitchers for the next season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enlist - to enroll or sign up, often for military service
  • Synonym: register - to officially sign up or enroll
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove oneself from participation or enrollment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02415071-v
Synset POS: v
