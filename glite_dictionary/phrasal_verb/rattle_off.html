to rattle off

1. VERB

/ ˈræt̬l ɔf / (AmE), / ˈræt.əl ɒf / (BrE)
to say or recite something quickly and easily, often without pausing or much thought
say or list quickly and easily

Examples

She could rattle off all the capitals of Europe in less than a minute.
During the interview, he rattled off his achievements without hesitation.
The teacher rattled off the exam instructions in a hurry before class ended.

Synset Examples

he could rattle off the names of all the chemical elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reel off - to say, recite, or list a series of things quickly and easily

Etymology

From the verb 'rattle' (to make rapid sounds) + 'off', indicating separation or completion. The phrase uses the idea of things coming out as rapidly as objects from a rattling container.
