to snip off

1. VERB

/ snɪp ɑf / (AmE), / snɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to cut or remove something quickly and neatly with scissors or a similar tool
cut or remove with scissors

Examples

She used garden shears to snip off the dead branches.
Make sure to snip off any loose threads before wearing the shirt.
He carefully snipped off the price tag from the gift.

Synset Examples

nip off the flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - to remove something by cutting
  • Synonym: trim - to make something neat by cutting away unwanted parts
