to futz with

1. VERB

/ fʌts wɪθ / (AmE), / fʌts wɪð / (BrE)
to fiddle or tinker with something in an aimless or unnecessary way
tinker or mess aimlessly with
informal american

Examples

Please don't futz with my computer while I'm away.
He spent all afternoon futzing with his old radio, but it still doesn't work.
If you futz with the thermostat too much, you'll break it.

Synset Examples

Don't futz with the settings; it works fine as it is.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinker with - to make small or experimental changes to something
  • Synonym: mess with - to interfere with or handle without care

Etymology

Probably from Yiddish *arafutsn* (to waste time), picked up in American English in the 20th century.
