to clean house

1. VERB

/ klin haʊs / (AmE), / kliːn haʊs / (BrE)
to make a house clean and tidy by removing dirt and putting things in order
tidy and clean the house

Examples

Every Saturday morning, we clean house before any guests arrive.
After the party, it took us hours to clean house properly.
She likes to clean house while listening to her favorite music.

Synset Examples

She housecleans every week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make a place neat by arranging things and cleaning

2. VERB

/ klin haʊs / (AmE), / kliːn haʊs / (BrE)
to reorganize or eliminate unwanted items, people, or problems, often to improve efficiency or effectiveness
remove unwanted items or problems

Examples

The company hired a new CEO to clean house and restructure the business.
Before the move, she decided to clean house and donate things she no longer needed.
After a series of losses, the coach plans to clean house and rebuild the team.

Synset Examples

They had to clean house to improve team dynamics.
It's time to clean house and get rid of old stuff.
The manager wants to clean house before the new quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purge - remove undesirable people or things
  • Synonym: declutter - remove unnecessary items from a space
