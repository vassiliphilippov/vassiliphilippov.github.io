to hash out

1. VERB

/ hæʃ aʊt / (AmE), / hæʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss something in detail, especially in order to resolve differences or reach an agreement
discuss in detail to resolve

Examples

Let's hash out the details of the project before we get started.
The two parties spent hours hashing out their differences in the meeting.
They finally hashed out an agreement after several rounds of talks.

Synset Examples

We discussed our household budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discuss - to talk about something with others
  • Synonym: debate - to argue about something in detail

Etymology

From 'hash' (to chop or mix), used figuratively for working through all parts of a topic.

2. VERB

/ hæʃ aʊt / (AmE), / hæʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss something thoroughly in order to reach an agreement or find a solution
discuss thoroughly to reach agreement

Examples

We spent hours trying to hash out the final details of the contract.
The management and staff will hash out their concerns at the meeting tomorrow.
Let's sit down and hash out our plans for the weekend.

Synset Examples

Let’s hash out our differences and find a solution.
We need to hash out the agreement before signing.
He decided to hash out the plan over lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work out - find a solution or agree after discussion

Etymology

Possibly from 'hash' meaning 'to chop up', used figuratively for vigorous discussion.

3. VERB

/ hæʃ aʊt / (AmE), / hæʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss something thoroughly in order to reach an agreement or to clarify details
discuss in detail to clarify or agree

Examples

The committee needs to hash out the final details of the proposal before the deadline.
We spent all afternoon hashing out our ideas for the project.
They hashed out a compromise everyone could accept after a long discussion.

Synset Examples

She needs to hash her ideas before presenting them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - argue or discuss formally or in detail
  • Synonym: to negotiate - discuss to reach an agreement

Etymology

From the verb 'hash' meaning to chop or mix, from French 'hacher' (to chop).
