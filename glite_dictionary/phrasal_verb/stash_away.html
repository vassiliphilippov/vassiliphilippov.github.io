to stash away

1. VERB

/ stæʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / stæʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put something in a secret or safe place for future use
hide or store for future use
informal

Examples

She likes to stash away a little money every month for emergencies.
Many squirrels stash away nuts to eat during the winter.
He stashed away old love letters in a box under his bed.

Synset Examples

store grain for the winter
The bear stores fat for the period of hibernation when he doesn't eat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to keep or accumulate something for future use
  • Synonym: hide - to put or keep out of sight
  • Antonym: to dispose of - to get rid of something

Etymology

Probably from the slang verb "stash" (to conceal), origin unknown, first recorded in the 18th century.
