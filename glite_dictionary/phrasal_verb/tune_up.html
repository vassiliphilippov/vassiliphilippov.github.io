to tune up

1. VERB

/ tun ʌp / (AmE), / tjuːn ʌp / (BrE)
to adjust a machine or instrument so that it works more effectively or efficiently
adjust for better functioning

Examples

Before the road trip, I need to tune up my car to make sure it's running smoothly.
The technician will tune up the air conditioner before the summer heat sets in.
She always tunes up her guitar before a performance to get the best sound.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - to change something slightly to improve its function or fit
  • Synonym: to calibrate - to set or correct the settings of an instrument

2. VERB

/ tun ʌp / (AmE), / tjuːn ʌp / (BrE)
to adjust the pitch or tone of a musical instrument so that it is in the correct or desired tuning
adjust musical instrument's pitch

Examples

He hired a professional to tune up his grand piano before the concert.
Every guitarist knows it’s important to tune up before rehearsals begin.
The cello was out of tune, so she stopped to tune up during the intermission.

3. VERB

/ tuːn ʌp / (AmE), / tjuːn ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare, adjust, or service something to achieve optimal performance, especially a musical instrument or mechanical device
adjust for optimal performance

Examples

The mechanic will tune up your car to ensure it runs smoothly.
It's important to tune up your bike before a long ride.
The orchestra gathered on stage to tune up their instruments before the concert.

Synset Examples
