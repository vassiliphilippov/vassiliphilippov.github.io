to fall into old habits

1. VERB

/ fɔl ˈɪntu oʊld ˈhæbɪts / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɪntuː əʊld ˈhæbɪts / (BrE)
to begin behaving in the same way as before, especially after trying to change or improve one's behavior
return to former behavior patterns

Examples

Whenever under pressure, he tends to fall into old habits and procrastinate.
After a few months of exercise, she started to fall into old habits of skipping the gym.
Despite his best intentions, John fell into old habits once the holidays arrived.

Synset Examples

She tends to fall into old habits when stressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state or habit, especially a bad one
