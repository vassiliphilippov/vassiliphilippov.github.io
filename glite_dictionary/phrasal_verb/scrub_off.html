to scrub off

1. VERB

/ skrʌb ɔf / (AmE), / skrʌb ɒf / (BrE)
to clean something by scrubbing its surface to remove dirt, stains, or unwanted material
clean by scrubbing to remove something

Examples

She had to scrub off the muddy footprints from the kitchen floor.
It took me an hour to scrub off all the graffiti from the wall.
You should scrub off any old paint before applying a new coat.

Synset Examples

She tried to scrub off the permanent dirt from her shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean off - to remove dirt or something unwanted from a surface
  • Synonym: to wipe off - to remove from a surface by wiping
