to scrunge some money

1. VERB

/ skrʌndʒ səm ˈmʌni / (AmE), / skrʌndʒ səm ˈmʌni / (BrE)
to gather or obtain money with difficulty, often by asking others or finding small amounts
obtain money with difficulty
informal

Examples

He had to scrunge some money from his friends to pay the rent.
After losing his wallet, Tom tried to scrunge some money for lunch.
She managed to scrunge some money to buy a train ticket home.

Synset Examples

He managed to scrunge some money for the bus fare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrounge - to obtain (something) by begging or borrowing
