to pan off

1. VERB

/ pæn ɔf / (AmE), / pæn ɒf / (BrE)
to wash earth or gravel in a pan to separate out valuable minerals, especially gold.
wash to separate minerals

Examples

The prospectors would often pan off the riverbed to search for gold nuggets.
After several hours, she managed to pan off enough dust to find a few tiny gems.
He spent the day panning off the silt, hoping to uncover something valuable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pan - to wash earth or gravel to find valuable minerals
