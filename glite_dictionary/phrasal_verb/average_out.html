to average out

1. VERB

/ ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ aʊt / (BrE)
to calculate the average of several amounts, or to become equal to the average amount when combined with others
calculate or become the average of several amounts

Examples

When the test scores were combined, they averaged out to a B grade.
The rainfall may vary each month, but over the year it tends to average out.
After all the ups and downs, our expenses averaged out to about $100 a week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - find the mean of data values

2. VERB

/ ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ aʊt / (BrE)
to result in an average value, especially after considering all gains and losses; to reach a level that represents a typical or mean amount over a period of time or across cases
to reach or calculate an average

Examples

If you look at my expenses, they average out to about $500 per month.
The fluctuations in temperature usually average out over the course of a year.
Our profits may be uneven month to month, but they average out in the long run.

Synset Examples

The number of hours I work per week averages out to 40

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to even out - become or make level or consistent, especially after fluctuations
