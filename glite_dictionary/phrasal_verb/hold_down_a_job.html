to hold down a job

1. VERB

/ hoʊld daʊn ə dʒɑb / (AmE), / həʊld daʊn ə dʒɒb / (BrE)
to manage to keep a job over a period of time, especially in challenging circumstances
maintain regular employment

Examples

After his illness, he struggled to hold down a job for more than a month.
Many parents hold down a job while raising young children.
It's not easy to hold down a job when you're dealing with chronic pain.

Synset Examples

It's tough to hold down a job and study full-time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep a job - to remain employed in a position
