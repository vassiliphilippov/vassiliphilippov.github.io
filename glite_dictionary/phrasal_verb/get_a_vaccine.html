to get a vaccine

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə vækˈsiːn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə vækˈsiːn / (BrE)
to receive a vaccination, typically by injection, to protect against a disease
receive a vaccination

Examples

It's important to get a vaccine before traveling to certain countries.
Many people choose to get a vaccine every year to protect against the flu.
Children should get a vaccine according to the recommended schedule.

Synset Examples

She urged everyone to get their vaccine this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vaccinate - to administer a vaccine to someone
