to get in

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place, vehicle, or enclosed space; to come or go inside.
to enter a place or vehicle

Examples

She asked me to get in the car quickly.
What time did you get in last night?
He couldn’t get in because the door was locked.

Synset Examples

the boat entered an area of shallow marshes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enter - to go or come into a place
  • Antonym: to get out - to leave a place or vehicle
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02020375-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn / (BrE)
to be accepted or admitted to a college, university, or other institution after applying
be accepted to institution

Examples

She was thrilled to get in to her first-choice university.
If you study hard, you have a better chance to get in to medical school.
He didn't get in to any of the colleges he applied to.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be admitted - to be allowed entrance to a place or group
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02244187-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn / (BrE)
(of trains, buses, or other vehicles) to arrive or reach a station or destination.
(of trains, etc.) arrive at a station

Examples

The train is due to get in at 8 a.m. sharp.
We waited on the platform for the express to get in.
What time does your bus get in to the station?

Synset Examples

The bullet train drew into Tokyo Station

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey or trip
  • Antonym: to depart - to leave a place, especially at the start of a journey
