to get to

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tu / (AmE), / ɡɛt tʊ / (BrE)
to reach a particular goal, destination, or state; to achieve something after effort
reach a goal or achieve something

Examples

It took her years of training to finally get to the Olympic team.
When you get to the top of the mountain, the view is breathtaking.
After all that hard work, I can't believe we actually got to this point.

Synset Examples

make the first team
We made it!
She may not make the grade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to achieve - to successfully bring about or reach a desired objective
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving a goal

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt tu / (AmE), / ɡɛt tʊ / (BrE)
to annoy or disturb someone, especially through minor irritations; to make someone feel upset or bothered
annoy or disturb emotionally

Examples

The loud noise from the construction site is starting to get to me.
Don't let her negative comments get to you.
After a while, the constant criticism began to get to him.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bother - to annoy or disturb someone
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone annoyed or impatient

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt tə / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə / (BrE)
to reach the point of becoming or starting to do something, often used with an activity, emotion, or state
reach the point of doing something

Examples

She began to get to worrying about the results during the night.
If you get to thinking about it too much, you may become anxious.
He got to shouting before anyone could calm him down.

Synset Examples

She gets to fretting if I stay away from home too long
