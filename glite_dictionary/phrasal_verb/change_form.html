to change form

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ fɔrm / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ fɔːm / (BrE)
to assume a different shape or form; to transform or be transformed in appearance or structure
to transform in shape or structure

Examples

Some animals can change form to blend into their environment.
After being heated, the metal will change form and become more malleable.
In the story, the wizard could change form at will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change in form, appearance, or structure
  • Antonym: remain the same - to keep the same form or not change
