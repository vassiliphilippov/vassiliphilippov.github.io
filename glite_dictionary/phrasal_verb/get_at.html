to get at

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt æt / (BrE)
to reach or gain access to something
reach or gain access to

Examples

I couldn't get at the lightbulb without a ladder.
The files are locked in a drawer and I can't get at them right now.
She tried to get at the cookies hidden at the back of the shelf.

Synset Examples

How does one access the attic in this house?
I cannot get to the T.V. antenna, even if I climb on the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: access - to be able to reach, approach, or use something
  • Synonym: reach - to arrive at or get to a place or thing

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt æt / (BrE)
To annoy or disturb someone, especially through repeated minor irritations.
annoy or disturb repeatedly

Examples

The constant criticism from her boss really gets at her.
Little noises like the ticking clock can get at me when I'm trying to concentrate.
Don't let his teasing get at you; he's only joking.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irritate - to cause annoyance or discomfort
  • Synonym: bother - to disturb or cause trouble to someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01791774-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ət / (BrE)
to attempt to influence or corrupt someone, especially by bribery or improper means
influence or corrupt; tamper with

Examples

Several witnesses were allegedly got at before the trial began, raising concerns about a fair verdict.
There are rumors that the referee was got at by gamblers seeking to fix the match.
The reporter suspected that the official had been got at, as his statements kept changing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - to persuade someone to act in one's favor, typically illegally or dishonestly, by a gift of money or other inducement
  • Synonym: tamper with - to interfere with something in order to cause damage or make unauthorized alterations; can also mean to try to corrupt or influence dishonestly
Source: WordNet
