to get away

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to escape from a place where you are being held or confined, especially secretly or by force
escape from confinement

Examples

The prisoners managed to get away before the guards noticed.
She tried to get away from her captors by climbing through a window.
The thief got away during the confusion after the alarm went off.

Synset Examples

The convicted murderer escaped from a high security prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - break free from confinement or control
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02078906-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave your usual environment or routine, especially in order to relax or take a break for pleasure or diversion
leave for relaxation or a break

Examples

It's important to get away from work sometimes and recharge your energy.
She hopes to get away to the countryside this weekend for some rest.
After a busy few months, they finally got away for a holiday.

Synset Examples

We escaped to our summer house for a few days
The president of the company never manages to get away during the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get free from a place where you are being kept or from a dangerous situation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02605001-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to avoid being caught or punished for something wrong or illegal that you have done
avoid being caught or punished

Examples

He managed to get away without anyone noticing his mistake.
Sometimes students get away with turning in late assignments if the teacher is lenient.
She often tries to get away with bending the rules in her favor.

Synset Examples

She gets away with murder!
I couldn't get out from under these responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get away with - to succeed in avoiding punishment for something
  • Antonym: to face the music - to accept punishment or unpleasant consequences
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00812391-v
Synset POS: v
