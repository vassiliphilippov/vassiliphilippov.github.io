to chew on

1. VERB

/ tʃu ɑn / (AmE), / tʃu ɒn / (BrE)
to think carefully about something for a period of time before forming an opinion or making a decision
think carefully about

Examples

She needed time to chew on the proposal before responding.
He chewed on the idea for days before making a decision.
I like to chew on complex problems during my morning walks.

Synset Examples

He chewed on his favorite dogbone while watching TV.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: to consider - to think about something carefully, especially in making decisions
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
