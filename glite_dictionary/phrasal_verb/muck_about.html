to muck about

1. VERB

/ mʌk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / mʌk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to spend time doing things that are not important or serious, often in an aimless or idle way
spend time idly or aimlessly
british informal

Examples

The children spent the afternoon mucking about in the garden, making up games.
Stop mucking about and help me with the chores, please.
I wasted half the morning just mucking about instead of getting my work done.

Synset Examples

The old lady is usually mucking about in her little house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess around - behave in a silly or aimless way
  • Antonym: work - do purposeful activity

Etymology

Likely derived from 'muck' (dirt, mess) plus 'about', common in British English for aimless activity.
