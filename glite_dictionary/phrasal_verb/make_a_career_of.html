to make a career of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə kəˈrɪr ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ə kəˈrɪə əv / (BrE)
to choose to pursue a particular activity or profession as one's main paid occupation over a long period.
pursue as a main profession

Examples

She hopes to make a career of scientific research after graduating from university.
Many athletes make a career of their sport, dedicating years to training and competition.
He wanted to make a career of acting, despite the challenges in the industry.

Synset Examples

She aims to make a career of journalism.
He decided to make a career of teaching.
Many musicians want to make a career of their passion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow or engage in a particular activity as a profession
  • Antonym: to dabble - to take part in an activity in a casual or superficial way
