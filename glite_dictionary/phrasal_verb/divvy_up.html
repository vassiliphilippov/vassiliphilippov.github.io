to divvy up

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪvi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdɪvi ʌp / (BrE)
to divide or distribute something among several people; to share out.
divide or share out

Examples

Let's divvy up the bill so everyone pays their fair share.
After the fundraising event, the group divvied up the proceeds evenly.
They spent an hour divvying up the camping supplies among the hikers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give (something) out to several people or places
  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute resources or duties for a particular purpose

Etymology

From "divvy" (to divide), an alteration of "divide," with "up" added as a particle to form the phrasal verb.

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪvi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdɪvi ʌp / (BrE)
to divide or distribute something among several people
divide or distribute among people
informal

Examples

Let's divvy up the pizza so everyone gets two slices.
The manager divvied up the tasks among the new employees.
After the project ended, they had to divvy up the remaining funds.

Synset Examples

It’s only fair to divvy the chores equally.
They decided to divvy the winnings among the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: share out - to give a portion of something to each member of a group
  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute something for a particular purpose or to particular people
  • Antonym: hoard - to save and store up something instead of sharing

Etymology

From 'divvy', a slang shortening of 'divide', originally from Latin 'dividere'.
