to squeak by

1. VERB

/ skwik baɪ / (AmE), / skwiːk baɪ / (BrE)
to manage to achieve something or survive with only just enough; to barely get by
manage to survive or succeed by a narrow margin
informal

Examples

I didn’t have much money in college, but I managed to squeak by.
With only one vote to spare, the bill squeaked by in the Senate.
They're just squeaking by each month on his salary.

Synset Examples

I guess I can squeeze by on this lousy salary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape by - to manage to survive or succeed with minimal resources
  • Synonym: to get by - to manage to cope or survive with what one has
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02594620-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skwik baɪ / (AmE), / skwiːk baɪ / (BrE)
to manage to escape from a situation or achieve something by a very narrow margin; to barely succeed or survive
barely escape or narrowly succeed

Examples

She was worried about the exam but managed to squeak by with a passing grade.
The underdog team squeaked by their rivals in the final minutes of the game.
I didn't have enough money for rent last month, but I somehow squeaked by.

Synset Examples

She squeaked by me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape by - to succeed with the bare minimum or just manage to get through a situation
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving a goal or passing a standard
Source: WordNet
