to gang up

1. VERB

/ ɡæŋ ʌp / (AmE), / ɡæŋ ʌp / (BrE)
(of people) to join together as a group, usually in order to criticize or oppose someone or something.
join together to oppose someone

Examples

The other kids gang up on him whenever he makes a mistake.
If we gang up together, we can finish this project faster.
She felt that her friends were starting to gang up against her during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to band together - to unite as a group for a common purpose
  • Antonym: stand alone - to act without help or support from others
