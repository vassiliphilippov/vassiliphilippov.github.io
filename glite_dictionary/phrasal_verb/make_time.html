to make time

1. VERB

/ meɪk taɪm / (AmE), / meɪk taɪm / (BrE)
to find or allocate time to do something important, often by rearranging other commitments
find or schedule time (for something)

Examples

It's essential to make time for regular exercise, even with a busy schedule.
She always tries to make time for her friends, no matter how hectic her life gets.
Despite his demanding job, he managed to make time to attend his son's recital.

Synset Examples

We should make time to catch up soon.
I need to make time for my family this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: find time - to have or manage to have enough time for something
