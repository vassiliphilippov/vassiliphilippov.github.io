to whitewash the issues

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃ ði ˈɪʃuz / (AmE), / ˈwʌɪtwɒʃ ði ˈɪʃuːz / (BrE)
to deliberately conceal, gloss over, or minimize problems or faults, especially in order to present a more favorable or innocent appearance
conceal or gloss over problems

Examples

The government tried to whitewash the issues by releasing a carefully edited report.
It's not helpful to whitewash the issues when addressing public concerns.
Instead of acknowledging their mistakes, the company chose to whitewash the issues in their press statement.

Synset Examples

They decided to whitewash the issues instead of addressing them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to hide or conceal something, especially wrongdoing
  • Antonym: to expose - to reveal or make something known that was hidden

Etymology

Whitewash originally referred to a paint mixture used to cover walls and later became a metaphor for concealing faults or problems.
