to pipe up

1. VERB

/ paɪp ʌp / (AmE), / paɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to begin to speak or make a sound, especially suddenly, loudly, or in a shrill voice
to speak up loudly or suddenly

Examples

The child suddenly piped up with a question during the meeting.
As soon as the teacher asked a question, James piped up with the answer.
She piped up in a high voice from the back of the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speak up - to speak more loudly or assertively
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to remain silent

Etymology

From the verb 'pipe' (to play a pipe or speak in a high voice) plus 'up', suggesting a sudden start.

2. VERB

/ paɪp ʌp / (AmE), / paɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to begin to play or sing, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
begin to play or sing

Examples

Just as the silence settled over the room, the children suddenly piped up with a cheerful song.
When the band piped up, everyone turned to face the stage.
As we approached the church, the choir piped up with a beautiful hymn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start singing - to begin singing
  • Antonym: fall silent - to stop making noise or singing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01052437-v
Synset POS: v
