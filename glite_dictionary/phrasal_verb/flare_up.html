to flare up

1. VERB

/ flɛr ʌp / (AmE), / fleə ʌp / (BrE)
(of conflict, emotion, or activity) to begin or become more intense suddenly or unexpectedly.
erupt or intensify suddenly

Examples

Tensions between the two groups can often flare up without warning.
My allergies tend to flare up in the spring when pollen levels rise.
An argument flared up during the meeting over the new policy.

Synset Examples

Unrest erupted in the country
Tempers flared at the meeting
The crowd irrupted into a burst of patriotism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to burst forth or break out suddenly
  • Antonym: to die down - to become gradually less intense or active

2. VERB

/ flɛr ʌp / (AmE), / fleə ʌp / (BrE)
(of a fire, light, or emotion) to suddenly increase in intensity after having decreased or faded.
suddenly increase in intensity

Examples

Everyone panicked when the campfire flared up unexpectedly during the night.
Tempers flared up again despite attempts to calm the group.
The old wound flared up after he started running regularly.

Synset Examples

the fire flared up and died down once again

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to die down - to become less intense, strong, or active
  • Synonym: to ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
