to smoke out

1. VERB

/ smoʊk aʊt / (AmE), / sməʊk aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a hiding place by sending smoke into it
drive out by using smoke

Examples

The beekeeper tried to smoke out the bees from the hive.
Authorities smoked out the fugitives who were hiding in the cave.
They used burning branches to smoke out the animals from their burrows.

Synset Examples

smoke out the bees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drive out - to force someone or something to leave a place

2. VERB

/ smoʊk aʊt / (AmE), / sməʊk aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something out of hiding, or to make information known or revealed, often through persistent effort
force out from hiding; reveal or expose

Examples

The police managed to smoke out the criminals hiding in the warehouse.
Journalists tried to smoke out the facts behind the company's sudden bankruptcy.
She used clever questioning to smoke out the real reasons for his decision.

Synset Examples

They used a searchlight to smoke out the intruders.
The detective tried to smoke out the suspect.
We need to smoke out the truth from him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flush out - to force people or animals to leave a hiding place
  • Synonym: to expose - to make something visible or known that was hidden
