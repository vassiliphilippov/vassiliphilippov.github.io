to switch off

1. VERB

/ swɪtʃ ɔf / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to stop a machine, light, or other device from operating by moving a switch to the off position
stop a device or machine by using a switch

Examples

Don’t forget to switch off the oven before you leave the house.
She switched off her phone so she wouldn’t be disturbed during the meeting.
Could you switch off the lights in the living room, please?

Synset Examples

Turn off the stereo, please
cut the engine
turn out the lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn off - to stop something from operating by using a switch or similar device
  • Antonym: to switch on - to start a device by moving a switch to the on position

2. VERB

/ swɪtʃ ɔf / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to turn off a device or piece of electrical equipment by operating a switch
turn off a device or equipment

Examples

Please switch off the lights before you leave the room.
He always switches off his computer at the end of the day.
You should switch off your phone during the meeting.

Synset Examples

She decided to switch off her phone for the weekend.
Don't forget to switch off the lights when you leave.
It's time to switch off your devices and relax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn off - to deactivate a device by turning a switch to the off position
  • Antonym: to switch on - to activate a device or equipment by operating a switch
