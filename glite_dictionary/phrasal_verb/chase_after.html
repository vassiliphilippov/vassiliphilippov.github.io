to chase after

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to run or go after someone or something in order to catch them
pursue with intent to catch

Examples

The children began to chase after the stray puppy in the park.
Police had to chase after the suspect when he ran from the scene.
Tourists sometimes chase after famous actors hoping to get a photograph.

Synset Examples

The policeman chased the mugger down the alley
the fox chased the rabbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow or chase in order to catch or attain
  • Antonym: to flee - to run away from someone or something, typically to escape
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ˈæftər / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to pursue someone with the intention of starting a romantic or sexual relationship
pursue romantically or sexually

Examples

He spent all summer chasing after his new neighbor, hoping she would go out with him.
She's tired of men chasing after her for superficial reasons.
You shouldn't chase after someone who isn't interested in you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow or try to attract someone in a romantic way
  • Antonym: to ignore - to refuse to pay attention or show romantic interest
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02540759-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to run or hurry to catch up with someone or something; to pursue or actively seek something
to pursue or run after

Examples

He decided to chase after his childhood dreams and enrolled in art school.
I had to chase after the bus this morning because I was running late.
She realized it was pointless to chase after people who didn't value her.
