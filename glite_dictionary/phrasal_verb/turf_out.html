to turf out

1. VERB

/ tɝf aʊt / (AmE), / tɜːf aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place or organization; to expel or eject someone
expel or remove someone

Examples

The manager threatened to turf out anyone who broke the rules.
They turfed out the tenants for not paying their rent.
Security turfed out the protesters from the building.

Synset Examples

The unruly student was excluded from the game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: to eject - to force someone to leave, remove from a place

Etymology

From 'turf' (grass surface/lawn); figuratively used in sense of being thrown out onto the ground.
