to wrestle with

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛsəl wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈrɛsəl wɪð / (BrE)
to struggle to deal with or try to find a solution to a difficult problem or situation
struggle to deal with something difficult

Examples

She had to wrestle with her conscience before making the final decision.
Many scientists wrestle with the ethical implications of their research.
After the announcement, the company continued to wrestle with financial difficulties for months.

Synset Examples

They often wrestle with difficult decisions at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grapple with - struggle to cope with or deal with something

Etymology

From 'wrestle' (to struggle physically), here used figuratively with 'with' to indicate mental struggle.
