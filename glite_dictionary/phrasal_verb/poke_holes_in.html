to poke holes in

1. VERB

/ poʊk hoʊlz ɪn / (AmE), / pəʊk həʊlz ɪn / (BrE)
to find faults in or criticize the weaknesses of an argument, idea, or proposal
criticize or find faults in something

Examples

In the meeting, several team members tried to poke holes in the new marketing strategy.
Lawyers are trained to poke holes in the opposing side's case during a trial.
She managed to poke holes in my theory with just a few well-placed questions.

Synset Examples

It’s easy to poke holes in someone else’s ideas.
The critics began to poke holes in the proposed plan.
She tried to poke holes in his argument, but he was prepared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval by pointing out faults
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for
