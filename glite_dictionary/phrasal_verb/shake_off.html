to shake off

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ɔf / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to get rid of something or someone that is unwanted or causing difficulty
get rid of something or someone

Examples

After weeks of trying, I finally managed to shake off my cold.
She sped up to shake off the reporters following her car.
He couldn't shake off his feeling of unease.

Synset Examples

I couldn't shake the car that was following me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get rid of - to remove or free oneself from something unwanted
