to rake in

1. VERB

/ reɪk ɪn / (AmE), / reɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to earn or receive large amounts of money, especially with little effort
earn large amounts of money
informal

Examples

The company continues to rake in millions from its popular smartphone app.
She managed to rake in a fortune in just a few years of investing.
They raked in a lot of money during the holiday sales.

Synset Examples

Since she accepted the new position, she has been raking it in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to earn - to receive money in return for work or services
  • Antonym: to lose - to be deprived of money or not gain profit
