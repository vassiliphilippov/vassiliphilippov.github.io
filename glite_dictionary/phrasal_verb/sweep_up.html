to sweep up

1. VERB

/ swip ʌp / (AmE), / swiːp ʌp / (BrE)
to be carried or involved in a situation, often without intending or being able to resist it
become involved in a situation unintentionally

Examples

Many young people were swept up in the excitement of the demonstration.
She found herself swept up in office politics despite her best efforts to avoid them.
He was swept up by the momentum of the project and didn't notice how much work he was doing.

Synset Examples

They were swept up by the events
don't drag me into this business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get caught up - to become involved in something without intending to
  • Synonym: to draw in - to involve someone in a situation, often unwillingly
