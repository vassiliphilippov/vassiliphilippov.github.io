to snatch up

1. VERB

/ snætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / snætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to seize or take something quickly, suddenly, or eagerly
seize quickly or eagerly

Examples

She managed to snatch up the last ticket before they sold out.
The child snatched up his toy and ran outside to play.
During the sale, shoppers snatched up bargains as quickly as they appeared.

Synset Examples

Before I could stop him the dog snatched the ham bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take hold of something suddenly or roughly
  • Synonym: snatch - to seize something quickly, often by force
