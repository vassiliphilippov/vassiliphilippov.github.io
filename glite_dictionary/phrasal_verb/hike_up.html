to hike up

1. VERB

/ haɪk ʌp / (AmE), / haɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to pull something, especially a piece of clothing, up to a higher position
pull up (clothing, etc.)

Examples

He quickly hiked up his jeans before stepping into the cold water.
She hiked up her skirt so she could climb over the fence.
During the hike, he stopped to hike up his backpack straps.

Synset Examples

He hitched up his socks and pants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull up - to raise something to a higher position

2. VERB

/ haɪk ʌp / (AmE), / haɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to increase (a price, rate, or amount) sharply or suddenly
increase sharply, especially cost or rate

Examples

The city plans to hike up parking fees next year.
After the announcement, the company hiked up its subscription prices overnight.
If you hike up the interest rates, fewer people may apply for loans.

Synset Examples

The landlord hiked up the rents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to increase the amount or level of something
  • Antonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01979926-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ haɪk ʌp / (AmE), / haɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to walk or climb up a hill, mountain, or slope, often for exercise or enjoyment
to climb up a hill or mountain

Examples

We decided to hike up the trail early in the morning to avoid the heat.
It took us two hours to hike up the rocky hillside, but the view was worth it.
She loves to hike up mountains whenever she travels to new countries.

Synset Examples

We need to hike up the mountain before sunset.
The hike up the steep hill was exhausting.
We hiked up to see the snow on the mountaintop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climb - go up something, usually using the feet and hands

Etymology

From 'hike' (to walk a long distance) + 'up' (direction upward).
