to beaver away

1. VERB

/ ˈbiːvər əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to work hard and persistently at something, especially for a long period of time
work hard persistently

Examples

She continued to beaver away at her thesis every night until it was finished.
They beavered away in the kitchen all morning to prepare the feast.
He's been beavering away on that project for months without complaint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toil - work extremely hard or incessantly
  • Antonym: to slack off - to reduce effort or stop working hard

Etymology

From the industrious behavior of beavers, combined with the verbal particle 'away' indicating continued action.

2. VERB

/ ˈbiːvər əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to work hard and continuously, especially on something that requires great effort or concentration
work hard and diligently

Examples

She continued to beaver away at her homework even after midnight.
The whole team had to beaver away to finish the project on time.
He beavered away quietly in the corner, oblivious to the noise around him.

Synset Examples

They beaver away at their tasks without complaint.
She will beaver away all night to finish her report.
He had to beaver away to meet the project deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work hard - to put in much effort at a task
  • Antonym: to slack off - to do less work or become less diligent

Etymology

From the noun 'beaver', an animal noted for its industrious behavior, used figuratively in the expression.
