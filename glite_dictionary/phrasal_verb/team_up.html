to team up

1. VERB

/ tim ʌp / (AmE), / tiːm ʌp / (BrE)
to join with one or more people to form a team in order to achieve a common goal
join with others to form a team

Examples

The two scientists decided to team up to conduct the research.
Several companies will team up to create the new product.
She asked her friend to team up with her for the competition.

Synset Examples

We teamed up for this new project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collaborate - work jointly on an activity
  • Antonym: to compete - strive against others for victory or superiority
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01091696-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tim ʌp / (AmE), / tiːm ʌp / (BrE)
to join with another person or group in order to do something together
work together with someone

Examples

The two companies decided to team up to develop a new product.
If you team up with others, the task will be easier.
We teamed up for the science fair and won first prize.

Synset Examples

She decided to team up with her friend for the competition.
I'll team up with you for the upcoming project.
They can team up with local charities to make a difference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work together with another person or group
  • Synonym: to cooperate - to act jointly or work together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: to compete - to strive against others to achieve something
