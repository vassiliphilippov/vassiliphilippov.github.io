to excel at

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsɛl æt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛl æt / (BrE)
to be very good at or proficient in a particular subject, activity, or skill
be very good at something

Examples

He truly excels at public speaking and often leads presentations at work.
Many children excel at sports when given encouragement and support.
Sarah always excels at problem solving during group projects.

Synset Examples

She shines at math

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shine at - to be very good at something
  • Antonym: struggle with - to have difficulty with something

Etymology

From Latin 'excellere' meaning 'to rise, surpass'.
