to land hard

1. VERB

/ lænd hɑrd / (AmE), / lænd hɑːd / (BrE)
to fall or come down with great force or impact, often causing pain or damage
fall or arrive with a strong impact

Examples

After slipping on the icy stairs, she landed hard on her back and groaned in pain.
The gymnast missed his step and landed hard during his floor routine.
The skateboarder jumped off the rail but landed hard, scraping his knee.

Synset Examples

He fell and landed hard on his kneecap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - to move and hit something with great force and noise
  • Antonym: land softly - to come down in a gentle or controlled way
