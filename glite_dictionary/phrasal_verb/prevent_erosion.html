to prevent erosion

1. VERB

/ priˈvɛnt ɪˈroʊʒən / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛnt ɪˈrəʊʒən / (BrE)
to stop soil or land from being worn away by natural forces such as wind or water
stop soil or land from being worn away

Examples

Farmers plant cover crops to prevent erosion on their fields during the winter months.
Terracing the hillside can help prevent erosion after heavy rainfall.
The construction project includes steps to prevent erosion along the riverbank.

Synset Examples

The park implemented measures to prevent erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to wear away gradually
