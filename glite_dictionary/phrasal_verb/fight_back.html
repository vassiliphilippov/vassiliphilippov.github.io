to fight back

1. VERB

/ faɪt bæk / (AmE), / faɪt bæk / (BrE)
to defend oneself by resisting or retaliating against an attack or challenge
defend oneself or resist an attack

Examples

The protesters decided to fight back when the new law threatened their rights.
Despite being outnumbered, she refused to give up and chose to fight back.
When criticized unfairly at the meeting, he started to fight back with strong arguments.

Synset Examples

The senator said he would oppose the bill
Don't fight it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resist - to withstand or oppose something strongly
  • Antonym: to give in - to yield or surrender

2. VERB

/ faɪt ˈbæk / (AmE), / faɪt ˈbæk / (BrE)
to defend oneself against an attack or criticism; to resist or retaliate when threatened or challenged
defend oneself; retaliate

Examples

The villagers decided to fight back against the bandits who threatened their homes.
During the debate, she managed to fight back against her critics’ harsh remarks.
He refused to be bullied any longer and finally fought back with confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of something
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting and yield
