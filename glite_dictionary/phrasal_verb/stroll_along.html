to stroll along

1. VERB

/ stroʊl əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / strəʊl əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to walk in a slow, relaxed way along a route or in a place, especially for pleasure
walk leisurely along a route

Examples

Every evening, she likes to stroll along the riverside, watching the city lights reflect on the water.
Tourists often stroll along the cobblestone streets, enjoying the historic views.
We used to stroll along the park paths, talking about our day.

Synset Examples

We decided to stroll along the beach at sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amble - to walk at a slow, relaxed pace
  • Antonym: rush - to move quickly with urgency
