to go wrong with

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ rɔŋ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ rɒŋ wɪð / (BrE)
to make a poor choice when selecting or choosing something; to choose unsuccessfully
make a poor choice; choose badly

Examples

You really can't go wrong with the chef's recommendation tonight.
Whenever in doubt, she never seems to go wrong with a classic black dress.
If you want a safe investment, you won't go wrong with government bonds.

Synset Examples

You can't go wrong with a McCar for reliability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choose poorly - to make a bad choice
  • Antonym: go right with - to make a good or successful choice
