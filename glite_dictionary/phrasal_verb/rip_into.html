to rip into

1. VERB

/ rɪp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / rɪp ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to criticize or attack someone or something with great force or enthusiasm
criticize or attack vigorously
informal

Examples

After the disappointing performance, the manager ripped into the entire team during the meeting.
The critic didn't hold back and ripped into the book in her latest column.
She rips into anyone who is lazy or careless at work.

Synset Examples

He didn’t hesitate to rip into the argument.
She decided to rip into the problem head-on.
The coach will rip into the team after that loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay into - attack or criticize strongly
  • Synonym: to tear into - attack or criticize passionately
