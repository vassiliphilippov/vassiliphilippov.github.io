to turn around

1. VERB

/ tɝn əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to turn and face the opposite direction, either physically or in attitude or action
to face the opposite direction

Examples

She suddenly turned around when she heard her name called from behind.
After realizing his mistake, he decided to turn around and apologize.
Please turn around so I can zip up the back of your dress.

Synset Examples

He turned around to face his opponent
My conscience told me to turn around before I made a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn back - to reverse direction and go back

2. VERB

/ tɝn əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to improve significantly, especially after being in a poor or unsuccessful state
cause dramatic improvement

Examples

The new CEO managed to turn around the struggling company within a year.
Strong leadership helped turn around the team's poor performance this season.
It took a lot of effort to turn around his failing grades at school.

Synset Examples

The new strategy turned around sales
The tutor turned around my son's performance in math

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to make or become better
  • Antonym: to worsen - to become or make worse

3. VERB

/ tɝn əˈɹaʊnd / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a situation, organization, or person) to improve significantly or go from a bad state to a good one.
improve significantly; recover from a bad state

Examples

The company managed to turn around its finances and avoid bankruptcy.
After a slow start, the team was able to turn around the game and win.
Her attitude has really turned around since she changed schools.

Synset Examples

Her performance in school picked up

Related Terms
