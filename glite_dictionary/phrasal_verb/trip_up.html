to trip up

1. VERB

/ trɪp ʌp / (AmE), / trɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mistake or error, often unintentionally or through carelessness
make an error

Examples

He tends to trip up when speaking in public because of his nerves.
The complicated instructions might trip up even experienced users.
She tripped up during the interview and answered one question incorrectly.

Synset Examples

She slipped up and revealed the name

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip up - to make a mistake, often through carelessness
  • Synonym: blunder - to make a serious or careless mistake
  • Antonym: get right - to perform something correctly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00619747-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ trɪp ʌp / (AmE), / trɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to cause someone to make a mistake, often by catching them in a blunder or by detecting a misstep in what they say or do
cause someone to make a mistake

Examples

The interviewer tried to trip up the politician with a tricky question.
It's easy to trip up during a complex presentation if you're not well-prepared.
She almost tripped up her opponent by pointing out a contradiction in his statement.

Synset Examples

The reporter tripped up the senator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch out - to detect someone in a mistake, especially by surprise
  • Degree Higher: to mislead - to guide someone into error; to cause to make a mistake
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00601230-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ trɪp ʌp / (AmE), / trɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to cause someone to stumble or to make a mistake; to catch someone out or cause them to falter, especially unintentionally
cause someone to stumble or make a mistake

Examples

She tried to trip up her opponent with a tricky question during the debate.
The uneven carpet could trip up anyone not watching their step.
It's easy to trip up on these complex calculations if you're not careful.

Synset Examples

The questions on the test tripped him up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stumble - to trip and almost fall
  • Synonym: to mislead - cause someone to go wrong or make a mistake
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01904753-v
Synset POS: v
