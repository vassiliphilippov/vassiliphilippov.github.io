to tie up

1. VERB

/ taɪ ʌp / (AmE), / taɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to cause something to be delayed or prevented from functioning normally; to restrain from moving, acting, or progressing
to delay or prevent normal function

Examples

Road construction can tie up traffic for hours during rush hour.
Several legal issues have tied up the completion of the project.
His accident tied up emergency services all morning.

Synset Examples

Traffic is tied up for miles around the bridge where the accident occurred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - to make something late or slower than expected
  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement or progress
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01479644-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ taɪ ʌp / (AmE), / taɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to secure a boat or ship in or as if in a berth or dock, typically by fastening it with ropes.
secure a boat or ship at a dock

Examples

We decided to tie up the boat at the marina before sunset.
Can you help me tie up the dinghy so it doesn't drift away?
After a long day at sea, they tied up their yacht and went ashore.

Synset Examples

tie up the boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moor - to fasten a boat or ship with cables or anchors
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ taɪ ʌp / (AmE), / taɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to fasten or secure something by using rope, string, or a similar material, often so that it cannot move or be used
fasten or secure with rope or string

Examples

Please tie up those boxes with string before moving them.
The boat was tied up at the dock overnight for safety.
Could you help me tie up these loose wires so no one trips over them?

Synset Examples

tie down the prisoners
tie up the old newspapers and bring them to the recycling shed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bind - to tie or fasten something tightly
  • Antonym: to untie - to undo or release something that has been tied

4. VERB

/ taɪ ʌp / (AmE), / taɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to invest money so that it is not readily available for other uses
invest money so it's unavailable

Examples

Most of our capital is tied up in property, so we can't access it easily.
If your savings are tied up in stocks, it may be hard to liquidate quickly.
She regrets tying up all her money in a single investment fund.

Synset Examples

All my money is tied up in long-term investments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: free up - to make available for use; opposite of tie up (in this sense)
