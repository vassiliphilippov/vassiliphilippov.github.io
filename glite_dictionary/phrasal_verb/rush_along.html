to rush along

1. VERB

/ rʌʃ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / rʌʃ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to move quickly and hurriedly in a particular direction
move quickly; hurry

Examples

The train began to rush along the tracks as it left the station.
We had to rush along the corridor to make it to the meeting on time.
Children rush along the beach, excited to reach the water.

Synset Examples

He rushed down the hall to receive his guests
The cars raced down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or act with haste
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move slowly or waste time
