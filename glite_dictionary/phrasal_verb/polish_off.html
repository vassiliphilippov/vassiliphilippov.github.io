to polish off

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑlɪʃ ɔf / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to finish or complete something, especially a task, quickly and easily
finish something quickly and completely
informal

Examples

She managed to polish off the entire report before lunch.
After dinner, he decided to polish off his remaining work emails.
They quickly polished off the final chapter of the group project.

Synset Examples

I finally got through this homework assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finish off - to complete or bring something to an end
