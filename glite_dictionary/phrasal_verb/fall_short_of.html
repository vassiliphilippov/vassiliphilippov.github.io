to fall short of

1. VERB

/ fɔl ʃɔrt əv / (AmE), / fɔːl ʃɔːt ɒv / (BrE)
to fail to reach the standard or level that was expected or required
not reach expected level

Examples

The new product fell short of our expectations and received mixed reviews.
Despite his efforts, his performance fell short of what was needed to win the prize.
Her explanation fell short of convincing the skeptical audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meet expectations - to satisfy the standard or requirements expected
  • Antonym: to exceed - to go beyond or surpass a standard or expectation

2. VERB

/ fɔl ʃɔrt ʌv / (AmE), / fɔːl ʃɔːt ɒv / (BrE)
to fail to reach a desired level or standard; to not achieve what was expected or required
to not reach a standard or expectation

Examples

Despite his best efforts, his proposal still fell short of what the committee wanted.
If our sales fall short of the target again this quarter, we may face cutbacks.
Her explanation fell short of convincing the panel of her innocence.

Synset Examples

His performance fell short of our expectations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meet expectations - to achieve the level that was anticipated or required
  • Degree Higher: to exceed - to go beyond a particular standard or expectation
