to play along

1. VERB

/ pleɪ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / pleɪ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to cooperate with someone or pretend to do so, often in order to avoid trouble or deception
cooperate or pretend to cooperate

Examples

Sometimes it's easier to just play along rather than start an argument.
She decided to play along with their plan until she could find a way out.
He didn't believe the story, but he played along to see what would happen.

Synset Examples

He decided to play along with the burglars for the moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cooperate - to work jointly towards the same end
  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work together with someone, especially on a project
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02422308-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pleɪ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / pleɪ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to perform music as an accompaniment to another performer or group
accompany musically

Examples

The guitarist likes to play along with recorded tracks at home.
At the recital, the pianist will play along while the children sing.
She enjoyed playing along to her favorite songs on the violin.

Synset Examples

The orchestra could barely follow the frequent pitch changes of the soprano

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to play a musical part that supports another performer
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01732234-v
Synset POS: v
