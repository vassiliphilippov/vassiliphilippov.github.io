to palm off

1. VERB

/ pɑm ɔf / (AmE), / pɑːm ɒf / (BrE)
to persuade someone to accept or buy something that is not genuine, especially by deception
deceive into accepting something false

Examples

He tried to palm off fake watches as the real thing.
She was angry when she realized she had been palmed off with an imitation bag.
The company attempted to palm off outdated stock onto unsuspecting customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to foist - to force someone to accept something unwanted
  • Synonym: to pass off - to present something falsely as being genuine

Etymology

From the noun 'palm' (the inside of the hand); originally meaning to hide something in the palm of the hand and thus deceive.

2. VERB

/ pɑm ɔf / (AmE), / pɑːm ɒf / (BrE)
to persuade someone to accept something that is untrue, inferior, or unwanted, often by deception
to pass off something unwanted, often by deceit

Examples

He tried to palm off the counterfeit bill as genuine to the cashier.
The company attempted to palm off outdated electronics onto unsuspecting customers.
Don't let them palm off their responsibilities on you.

Synset Examples

He managed to palm off the blame onto his colleague.
She tried to palm off the broken watch as new.
Don't palm off your old clothes on me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to foist - to force someone to accept something unwanted or inferior

Etymology

From the act of concealing something in the palm of one's hand, suggesting trickery.
