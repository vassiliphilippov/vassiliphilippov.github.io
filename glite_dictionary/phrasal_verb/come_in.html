to come in

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɪn / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place, especially a room or building
to enter a place

Examples

Please come in and have a seat while you wait for the doctor.
She knocked on the door and then decided to come in when she heard her name.
As soon as the bell rang, the students began to come in to the classroom.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go in - to enter; to go inside

/ kʌm ɪn / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn / (BrE)
to achieve a particular position or rank in a competition, race, or contest, usually stated with an ordinal number
to place or rank in a competition

Examples

He was disappointed to come in second after leading the entire race.
Our team managed to come in fifth at the national championships.
Despite her injury, she came in fourth in the tournament.
