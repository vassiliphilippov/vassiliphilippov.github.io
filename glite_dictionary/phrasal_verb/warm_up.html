to warm up

1. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to perform or cause to perform gentle exercises or activities before a more intense activity, especially to prepare the muscles and reduce the risk of injury
do gentle exercises before physical activity

Examples

The team warmed up before heading out onto the field.
She always warm up her muscles to prevent injury during her workouts.
The coach asked everyone to warm up with a light jog before practice.

Synset Examples

The coach warmed up the players before the game

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to cool down - to perform gentle exercises after strenuous activity to help the body recover
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to become warm or warmer, or to make something warmer
become warm or warmer

Examples

The room began to warm up as the sun rose.
Stir the soup occasionally as it warms up on the stove.
She warmed up her hands by holding them near the fire.

Synset Examples

The soup warmed slowly on the stove

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to heat up - to make or become hot or hotter

3. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare the body for exercise or strenuous physical activity by stretching or performing light movements
prepare body before exercise

Examples

It's important to warm up before going for a run to prevent injury.
The coach reminded the team to warm up thoroughly before the match.
She always spends ten minutes warming up with some light stretches.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to cool down - to perform gentle exercises after intense activity to gradually lower the heart rate

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
