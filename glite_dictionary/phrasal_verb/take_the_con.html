to take the con

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə kɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ðə kɒn / (BrE)
to assume control or leadership, especially in a discussion, meeting, or group activity
assume control or leadership

Examples

After a brief pause, she decided to take the con and guide the discussion to a conclusion.
Who will take the con if our team leader is absent during the meeting?
He prefers to take the con when complex decisions need to be made.

Synset Examples

You should take the con during the presentation.
To succeed, you need to take the con in meetings.
He decided to take the con and lead the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take charge - to assume control or responsibility
  • Antonym: hand over the con - to give up leadership or control to someone else

Etymology

From 'con', a nautical term for controlling the steering of a ship, from Middle English 'connen' (to direct).
