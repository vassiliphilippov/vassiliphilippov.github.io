to swing out

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / swɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to move or cause to move outward or away in a wide, sweeping motion
move outward in a sweeping motion

Examples

The large door swung out, letting a rush of cold air into the room.
She swung out her arm to point at the distant mountain.
The crane swung out over the river to move the heavy cargo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - to move back and forth or in a curve
