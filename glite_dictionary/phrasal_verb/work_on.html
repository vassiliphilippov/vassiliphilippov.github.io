to work on

1. VERB

/ wɝːk ɑn / (AmE), / wɜːk ɒn / (BrE)
to make an effort to accomplish, develop, or improve something over a period of time
exert effort to accomplish or improve something

Examples

She needs to work on her math skills before the exam.
The team will work on the new software update this week.
I'm working on my communication skills to become a better leader.

Synset Examples

the child worked at the multiplication table until she had it down cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work at - to expend effort in order to accomplish something (especially over time)
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying to achieve or improve something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02420169-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɝk ɑn / (AmE), / wɜːk ɒn / (BrE)
to shape, form, or improve a material by applying effort, skill, or treatment to it
to shape or improve a material

Examples

The sculptor worked on the marble for months to create the statue.
We need special tools to work on this type of metal.
Artisans work on leather to make it softer and more pliable.

Synset Examples

work stone into tools
process iron
work the metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: process - to subject a material to a series of actions or steps to change or preserve it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01672498-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ wɜrk ɑn / (AmE), / wɜːk ɒn / (BrE)
to spend time and effort doing, repairing, or improving something; to engage in a particular task or project.
engage in a task or project

Examples

She worked on her thesis until she perfected every chapter.
Engineers are currently working on a solution to the power outage.
He's worked on several major films as a sound technician.

Synset Examples

We worked on the project til midnight last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work at - to make an effort in order to achieve or improve something
  • Synonym: work for - to be employed by or labour on behalf of someone or something
