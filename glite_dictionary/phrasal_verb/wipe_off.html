to wipe off

1. VERB

/ waɪp ɑf / (AmE), / waɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something from a surface by rubbing it with a cloth, paper, or hand
to remove by wiping

Examples

Please wipe off the table after dinner so it stays clean.
She quickly wiped off the fingerprints from the phone's screen.
After painting, he had to wipe off the excess paint from his hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe away - to remove something from a surface by wiping, especially with effort
  • Synonym: to scrub off - to remove something from a surface by scrubbing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01395572-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ waɪp ɔf / (AmE), / waɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something from a surface by rubbing or erasing
remove by rubbing or erasing

Examples

Please wipe off the fingerprints from the screen before returning the device.
He wiped off the spilled juice from the kitchen counter.
After rain, the players had to wipe off the benches before sitting down.

Synset Examples

Please erase the formula on the blackboard — it is wrong!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove written or drawn marks
  • Synonym: to rub out - to remove by rubbing, especially writing
  • Antonym: to write on - to mark or inscribe on a surface
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01551500-v
Synset POS: v
