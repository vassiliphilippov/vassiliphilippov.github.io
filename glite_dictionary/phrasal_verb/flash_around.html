to flash around

1. VERB

/ flæʃ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / flæʃ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to display or show off something, especially expensive or impressive possessions, in a conspicuous or boastful manner
show off possessions ostentatiously
informal

Examples

He loves to flash around his expensive watch at every opportunity.
Instead of saving money, she prefers to flash around her latest purchases.
Some people flash around their achievements to make others feel inferior.

Synset Examples

She likes to flash around her designer handbag.
He would flash around his new phone to impress friends.
Stop trying to flash around your wealth; it’s unnecessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - behave in a way meant to attract attention or admiration
