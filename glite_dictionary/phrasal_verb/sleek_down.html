to sleek down

1. VERB

/ slik daʊn / (AmE), / sliːk daʊn / (BrE)
to make (hair, fur, or another surface) smooth and glossy by applying pressure or a substance
make smooth and glossy (esp. hair, fur)

Examples

She used a little gel to sleek down the stray hairs around her face.
Before the photo, the groomer sleeked down the dog's fur to enhance its shine.
He quickly sleeked down his hair with water before heading out the door.

Synset Examples

slick one's hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to smooth down - to make (something) flat, even, or smooth by pressing it
  • Antonym: ruffle - to disturb the smoothness or evenness of (especially hair or feathers)
