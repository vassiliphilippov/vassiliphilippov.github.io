to lend itself to

1. VERB

/ lɛnd ˈɪtˌsɛlf tu / (AmE), / lɛnd ˈɪtˌsɛlf tʊ / (BrE)
to be suitable or adaptable for a particular purpose or situation
be suitable/adaptable for

Examples

Her writing style lends itself to detailed historical analysis.
The material is flexible and lends itself to various design applications.
That topic doesn't lend itself to a quick explanation.

Synset Examples

This story would lend itself well to serialization on television
The current system lends itself to great abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be suited to - to be appropriate or fitting for a certain purpose

2. VERB

/ lɛnd ˈɪtˌsɛlf tu / (AmE), / lɛnd ˈɪtˌsɛlf tuː / (BrE)
to be suitable for or easily adaptable to a particular purpose, situation, or use
to be suitable or adaptable for something

Examples

This idea really lends itself to a practical solution for busy families.
The flexible schedule lends itself to students who have part-time jobs.
Not every topic lends itself to discussion in a large group.

Synset Examples

The recipe lends itself to many variations.
This design does not lend itself to easy updates.
Her style lends itself to a relaxed atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be suited to - to be appropriate or fitting for a purpose or person
