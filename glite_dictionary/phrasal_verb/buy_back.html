to buy back

1. VERB

/ baɪ bæk / (AmE), / baɪ bæk / (BrE)
to purchase something again after having sold, lost, or given it away
re-purchase something previously owned

Examples

After losing his favorite guitar, he managed to buy back the exact same model from a collector.
The company decided to buy back its shares from public investors.
She regretted selling her childhood home and later tried to buy back the property.

Synset Examples

He bought back the house that his father sold years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to redeem - to buy back, recover, or pay off
  • Antonym: sell - to give something in exchange for money
