to vamp up

1. VERB

/ væmp ʌp / (AmE), / væmp ʌp / (BrE)
to repair, improve, or update something by adding new parts or elements, especially to make it more attractive or suitable
update or improve by adding new parts

Examples

We're planning to vamp up the living room with new furniture and lighting.
The company decided to vamp up its website to attract more customers.
She managed to vamp up her old speech by adding a few humorous anecdotes.

Synset Examples

vamp up an old speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revamp - to renovate, update, or make improvements
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring something back to a former condition

Etymology

From 'vamp', originally meaning to patch up old footwear, from Middle English 'vampe' (upper part of a shoe), from Old French, from Latin 'vampes'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01593310-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ væmp ʌp / (AmE), / væmp ʌp / (BrE)
to invent or concoct (something), especially quickly or superficially; to improvise or fabricate.
improvise or fabricate

Examples

She tried to vamp up an excuse for her tardiness.
The manager had to vamp up a plan when the original one failed.
They quickly vamped up a story to explain the missing funds.

Synset Examples

vamp up an excuse for not attending the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make up - to invent or fabricate, especially a story or excuse
  • Synonym: to conceive - to form (a plan, idea, etc.) in the mind

Etymology

From 'vamp' (verb: to patch or improvise), originally from Old French 'avantpie' (forefoot of a stocking or shoe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01743426-v
Synset POS: v
