to come into

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈɪntu / (AmE), / kʌm ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to receive or acquire something, especially by chance or inheritance
to acquire, often by chance or inheritance

Examples

After her uncle passed away, she came into a large fortune.
When he came into possession of the old letters, he discovered a family secret.
They unexpectedly came into some money after winning the lottery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inherit - to receive property, money, or a title as an heir at the death of the previous holder
