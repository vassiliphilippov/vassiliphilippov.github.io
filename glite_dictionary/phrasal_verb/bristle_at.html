to bristle at

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɪsəl æt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl at / (BrE)
to react with anger or indignation to something said or done
react with anger or indignation

Examples

She bristled at the suggestion that she was unprepared for the meeting.
Many readers bristled at the tone of the editorial.
He bristles at any criticism of his leadership style.

Synset Examples

She bristled at his insolent remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take offense - to feel hurt or insulted by something
  • Antonym: to shrug off - to dismiss or ignore something, often negative

2. VERB

/ ˈbrɪsəl æt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl æt / (BrE)
to react suddenly with anger or irritation, especially in response to something said or done
react with anger or irritation

Examples

She tends to bristle at any suggestion that she is not doing her job well.
Many people bristle at unfair criticism, even if it's meant to be constructive.
It's important not to bristle at honest feedback from your colleagues.

Synset Examples

She could see him bristle at the criticism.
He began to bristle when she challenged his ideas.
Don't bristle at feedback; it's meant to help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take offense - to feel insulted or hurt by something
  • Antonym: to shrug off - to disregard something without annoyance

Etymology

From 'bristle' (to become stiff with anger), likely related to the way animal fur stands up in agitation; 'at' indicating cause.
