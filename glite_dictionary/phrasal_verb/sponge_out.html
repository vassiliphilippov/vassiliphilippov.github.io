to sponge out

1. VERB

/ spʌndʒ aʊt / (AmE), / spʌndʒ aʊt / (BrE)
to absorb or remove liquid from a surface or container using a sponge or cloth
remove liquid with a sponge

Examples

After the soup spilled, I quickly sponged out the mess from the table.
You should sponge out the excess oil before it soaks in.
He carefully sponged out the water from his shoes after the rain.

Synset Examples

Remember to sponge out the spills before they stain.
You should sponge out the excess paint for a cleaner finish.
She used a cloth to sponge out the water on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe up - to clean a surface by wiping, often with a cloth or sponge
  • Synonym: mop up - to clean or absorb a spill using a mop or cloth
