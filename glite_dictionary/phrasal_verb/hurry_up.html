to hurry up

1. VERB

/ ˈhɜri ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhʌri ˌʌp / (BrE)
to do something more quickly or to move or act with increased speed
move or act more quickly

Examples

You need to hurry up or you'll miss your appointment.
Please hurry up and finish your homework before dinner.
She told everyone to hurry up because the movie was about to start.

Synset Examples

Hurry up and get in the car before it rains.
She asked the baker to hurry up the cakes for the party.
Hurry up and get ready, or else we'll miss the train.
We need to hurry up the cakes before the oven runs out of time.
Can you hurry up the cakes? The guests will arrive soon?
We need to hurry up and get to the fireworks factory before it closes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move or do something quickly
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move or act slowly, wasting time
