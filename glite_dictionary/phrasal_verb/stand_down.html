to stand down

1. VERB

/ stænd daʊn / (AmE), / stænd daʊn / (BrE)
to withdraw from a position or duty, or to stop taking action, especially after being ordered to do so
withdraw from duty or action

Examples

After the emergency was over, the captain ordered everyone to stand down.
She decided to stand down as committee chairperson after five years of service.
Troops were told to stand down once the threat had passed.

Synset Examples

After the drill, they were told to stand down.
It's time to stand down and let others take over.
The officer was instructed to stand down during the protest.
