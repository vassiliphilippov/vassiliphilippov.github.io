to shut out

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt aʊt / (AmE), / ʃʌt aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from entering or being included
prevent from entering or being included

Examples

The company tried to shut out new competitors by raising prices.
Heavy curtains can shut out the early morning light.
Many felt the new policy would shut out people from disadvantaged backgrounds.

Synset Examples

The trees were shutting out all sunlight
This policy excludes people who have a criminal record from entering the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately not include something or someone
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone to enter a place or become part of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02454205-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʃʌt aʊt / (AmE), / ʃʌt aʊt / (BrE)
To prevent someone or something from entering a place, being included, or being considered.
prevent from entering or participating

Examples

The new security gates shut out anyone without proper ID.
Heavy curtains can shut out unwanted noise from the street.
Many immigrants felt the country’s policies shut out people like them.

Synset Examples

The trees were shutting out all sunlight
This policy excludes people who have a criminal record from entering the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately not include something or someone
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone or something to enter
