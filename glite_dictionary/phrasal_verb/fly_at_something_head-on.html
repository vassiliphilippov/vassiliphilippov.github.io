to fly at something head-on

1. VERB

/ flaɪ æt ˈsʌmθɪŋ hɛd ˈɑn / (AmE), / flaɪ æt ˈsʌmθɪŋ hɛd ˈɒn / (BrE)
to confront a challenge, problem, or difficulty directly and decisively without avoiding it
confront directly and decisively

Examples

Despite the difficulties, she decided to fly at the project head-on and solve each problem as it arose.
He prefers to fly at challenges head-on rather than procrastinate or avoid them.
The team flew at the competition head-on and emerged victorious despite tough odds.

Synset Examples

He didn't hesitate to fly at the challenge head-on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face something head-on - to meet or confront something directly and resolutely
