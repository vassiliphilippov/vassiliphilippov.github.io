to hold cheap

1. VERB

/ hoʊld tʃip / (AmE), / həʊld tʃiːp / (BrE)
to consider someone or something to be of little worth; to undervalue or disregard
consider of little worth; undervalue
archaic

Examples

Despite their dedication, the company continued to hold cheap the efforts of its employees.
We must never hold cheap the value of education in building a better society.
Some people hold cheap the traditions that have shaped our culture for centuries.

Synset Examples

I won't hold cheap on my health; I invest in good food.
Despite its quality, they decided to hold cheap on the price.
She tends to hold cheap when it comes to her fashion choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undervalue - to underestimate the worth or importance of something
  • Antonym: to appreciate - to recognize the full worth of something or someone

Etymology

From the verb 'hold' (regard, consider) and 'cheap' (inexpensive, of little value), as in the older sense 'good cheap' = 'good buy'.
