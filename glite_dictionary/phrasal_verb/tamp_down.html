to tamp down

1. VERB

/ tæmp daʊn / (AmE), / tamp daʊn / (BrE)
to press or pack something down firmly, usually to make it more compact or stable
press or pack down firmly

Examples

After adding the soil, he used his foot to tamp down the earth around the roots.
It's important to tamp down the coffee evenly before brewing an espresso.
She tamped down the trash in the bin to make room for more.

Synset Examples

tamp the coffee grinds in the container to make espresso

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compact - to press something together to make it firm or dense

Etymology

From 'tamp,' likely of Dutch or Low German origin, meaning 'to press down' or 'ram', plus the adverb 'down'.

2. VERB

/ tæmp daʊn / (AmE), / tamp daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the intensity of something or suppress it, especially emotions, rumors, or unrest
reduce or suppress (intensity, emotions, rumors, etc.)

Examples

The government acted swiftly to tamp down public unrest after the announcement.
She tried her best to tamp down her excitement before entering the meeting.
News outlets worked to tamp down the speculation by reporting verified facts only.

Synset Examples

The manager had to tamp down employee dissatisfaction.
They tried to tamp down the rumors spreading online.
It's important to tamp down any negativity in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to the activities of; to subdue
  • Synonym: to quell - to put an end to (a rebellion or disorder)
  • Antonym: to amplify - to make something larger, greater, or stronger

Etymology

From 'tamp', meaning to pack down firmly, likely from French 'tamper' (to stamp), combined with 'down'.
