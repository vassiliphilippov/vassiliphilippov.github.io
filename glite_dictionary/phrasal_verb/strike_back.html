to strike back

1. VERB

/ straɪk bæk / (AmE), / straɪk bæk / (BrE)
to retaliate or make a counterattack, especially in response to an attack or wrongdoing
retaliate; make a counterattack

Examples

After being criticized in the meeting, she decided to strike back with her own remarks.
The champion lost the first round but managed to strike back and win the match.
Countries sometimes strike back when threatened by their rivals.

Synset Examples

The Empire strikes back
The Giants struck back and won the opener
The Israeli army retaliated for the Hamas bombing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retaliate - to make an attack in return for a similar attack
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling anger or resentment towards someone for an offense
