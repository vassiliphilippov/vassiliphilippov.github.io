to reek of

1. VERB

/ rik əv / (AmE), / riːk ɒv / (BrE)
to have a very strong and usually unpleasant smell of something; to be strongly or noticeably full of a particular quality, especially something negative
have a strong (unpleasant) smell or clear presence of something

Examples

The kitchen reeked of burnt toast after the failed breakfast attempt.
Her explanation reeks of dishonesty, and nobody believed her.
Every time it rains, the basement reeks of mold.

Synset Examples

The fish market would sometimes reek of seafood.
The old building seemed to reek of history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smell of - to have the scent or aroma of something
