to get out of here

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt aʊt əv hɪr / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt əv hɪə / (BrE)
to leave or go away from a place, especially quickly or suddenly
leave or go away from a place

Examples

It's getting late, so we should get out of here before traffic starts.
After the fire alarm went off, everyone tried to get out of here as quickly as possible.
If you're uncomfortable, just get out of here and call me later.

Synset Examples

When I heard that noise, I thought, I need to get out of here!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place, the opposite of leaving
