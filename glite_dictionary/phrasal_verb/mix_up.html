to mix up

1. VERB

/ mɪks ʌp / (AmE), / mɪks ʌp / (BrE)
to confuse someone or make them unable to think clearly; to cause to be perplexed or confounded
confuse or perplex

Examples

The complicated instructions mixed up the new employees on their first day.
I always mix up their names and can't remember who is who.
She was completely mixed up by the technical jargon in the report.

Synset Examples

This problem stumped her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or to mistake one thing for another
  • Synonym: to bewilder - to make someone very confused
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00622051-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɪks ʌp / (AmE), / mɪks ʌp / (BrE)
to combine things in a way that produces disorder or confusion; to assemble without order or sense
assemble or combine things disorderly

Examples

The teacher warned the students not to mix up the answer sheets.
Please do not mix up the documents on my desk.
He tends to mix up his facts when telling the story.

Synset Examples

She jumbles the words when she is supposed to write a sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumble - to mix things in a confused or disordered way
  • Antonym: sort - to arrange things in order
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01660937-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɪks ʌp / (AmE), / mɪks ʌp / (BrE)
to confuse two or more people or things, or to mistake one for another
confuse or mistake things for each other

Examples

I often mix up the twins because they look so alike.
Don't mix up the documents, or we will lose track of the originals.
She accidentally mixed up the salt and sugar when baking the cake.

Synset Examples

I tend to mix up their names during introductions.
She always mixes up her left and right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to mistake one thing or person for another
  • Synonym: jumble - to mix things together in a confused or untidy way
