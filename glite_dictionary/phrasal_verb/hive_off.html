to hive off

1. VERB

/ haɪv ɔf / (AmE), / haɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to separate part of a group, organization, or company and make it independent or separate
separate part of a group or company

Examples

The company decided to hive off its electronics division into a separate entity.
Several non-core businesses will be hived off over the next few years.
After restructuring, they hived off the publishing group to focus on digital products.

Synset Examples

The unit was hived off from its parent company

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spin off - create a new company by separating part of an existing one

Etymology

From analogy to bees separating from a larger hive to form a new colony.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02473888-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ haɪv ɔf / (AmE), / haɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to secretly withdraw or transfer money or resources, often with dishonest intentions
withdraw money/resources secretly or dishonestly

Examples

The manager tried to hive off large sums of money into offshore accounts.
They discovered that he had hived off funds from the charity for personal use.
Regulators became suspicious when assets were quietly hived off during the audit.

Etymology

From the noun 'hive' (beehive) + 'off', originally referring to bees forming new colonies; later figuratively extended to separating or diverting resources.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02316994-v
Synset POS: v
