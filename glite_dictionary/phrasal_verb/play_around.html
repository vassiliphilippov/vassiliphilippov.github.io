to play around

1. VERB

/ pleɪ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / pleɪ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to engage in an activity casually or experimentally, without serious intent or expertise
to do something casually or experimentally

Examples

She likes to play around with different recipes when she cooks.
I decided to play around with the new software to see what it could do.
They spent the afternoon playing around with ideas for their next project.

Synset Examples

She dabbles in astronomy
He plays around with investments but he never makes any money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dabble - to take part in an activity in a casual or superficial way

2. VERB

/ pleɪ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / pleɪ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(informal) To have romantic or sexual relations outside of one's committed relationship; to commit adultery.
have affairs; commit adultery (informal)
informal

Examples

Rumors spread that he would often play around despite being married for years.
She left her husband after discovering he had been playing around with several coworkers.
If you play around in a relationship, you risk losing the trust of your partner.

Synset Examples

he plays around a lot

3. VERB

/ pleɪ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / pleɪ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to engage in playful activities, behave in a lighthearted or unserious way, or experiment with something without serious intent
engage in playful or experimental activity

Examples

You shouldn't play around with electrical wires unless you know what you're doing.
The children played around in the garden while their parents talked.
She likes to play around with different ingredients when cooking.

Synset Examples

The kids love to play around in the park.
He likes to play around with new recipes on weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly or playful way
  • Synonym: experiment - to try out new ideas or methods
