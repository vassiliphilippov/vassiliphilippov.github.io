to drive in

1. VERB

/ ˈdraɪv ɪn / (AmE), / ˈdraɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to arrive at a place by car or other motor vehicle
arrive by car or motor vehicle

Examples

She plans to drive in from the suburbs each morning for work.
They drove in just as the sun was setting over the city skyline.
We drive in every weekend to visit our friends downtown.

Synset Examples

The star and her manager drive in today from their motor tour across the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrive - to reach a place, especially at the end of a journey
  • Antonym: to depart - to leave a place or begin a journey

/ draɪv ɪn / (AmE), / draɪv ɪn / (BrE)

/ draɪv ɪn / (AmE), / draɪv ɪn / (BrE)

/ draɪv ɪn / (AmE), / draɪv ɪn / (BrE)

force (nail, screw, etc.) into a surface

Examples

Be sure to drive in the nails straight so the wood doesn't split.
