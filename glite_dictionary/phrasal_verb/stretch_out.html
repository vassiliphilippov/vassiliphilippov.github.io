to stretch out

1. VERB

/ strɛtʃ aʊt / (AmE), / strɛtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to extend one's neck in order to see something better
extend neck to see better

Examples

The children stretched out their necks to catch a glimpse of the parade.
She stretched out to see over the heads in front of her.
He stretched out as far as he could to try and spot his friends in the crowd.

Synset Examples

The women craned their necks to see the President drive by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craned - to stretch the neck in order to see better

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ strɛtʃ aʊt / (AmE), / strɛtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to extend your body or limbs to their full length, especially in order to relax or relieve stiffness
extend body or limbs fully

Examples

After the long flight, she needed to stretch out on the hotel bed.
He likes to stretch out his arms and legs before going for a run.
The cat stretched out in a patch of sunlight on the floor.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stretch - to straighten or extend one's body or a part of one's body to its full length
