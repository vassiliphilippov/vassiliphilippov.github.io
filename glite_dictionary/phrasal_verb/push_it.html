to push it

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ɪt / (AmE), / pʊʃ ɪt / (BrE)
to make a special effort or try very hard, especially to go beyond what is usual or expected
make a special effort; go to the limit

Examples

She decided to push it and study for two more hours before the exam.
If we push it, we might finish this project by Friday.
He's really pushing it at the gym lately, training twice a day.

Synset Examples

He decided to push it and finish the race.
They always push it during the last week of work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go the extra mile - to make a special effort beyond what is required
  • Antonym: to take it easy - to relax and not exert extra effort
