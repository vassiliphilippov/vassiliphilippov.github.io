to pull about

1. VERB

/ pʊl əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / pʊl əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to maneuver or navigate something, especially to change position or direction; to move or handle something in a casual or aimless way
maneuver, navigate, or handle casually

Examples

We had to pull about several times before docking safely at the pier.
He likes to pull about with his old car in the countryside on weekends.
The children pulled about the toy train until it broke.

Synset Examples

We can pull about to find the best route.
She likes to pull about in her spare time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuver - to move or guide something carefully or skillfully
  • Synonym: handle - to touch or use something with your hands
