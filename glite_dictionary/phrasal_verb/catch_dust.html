to catch dust

1. VERB

/ kætʃ dʌst / (AmE), / kætʃ dʌst / (BrE)
to accumulate dust or dirt from not being used or from being neglected
accumulate dust from disuse

Examples

The souvenirs on the top shelf just catch dust all year long.
He left his bike in the garage, and now it's starting to catch dust.
Many unused kitchen appliances tend to catch dust after a few months.

Synset Examples

The old books on the shelf catch dust.
I need to clean the corner; it’s starting to catch dust.
If you leave it there, it will just catch dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather dust - to accumulate dust from being unused
