to sow seed

1. VERB

/ soʊ sid / (AmE), / səʊ siːd / (BrE)
to plant seeds in the ground in order for them to grow
to plant seeds for growth

Examples

In March, it's time to sow seed for your vegetable garden.
Farmers sow seed in the fields after the last frost has passed.
He learned how to properly sow seed to ensure a healthy crop.

Synset Examples

They plan to sow seeds for the spring garden next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - to put seeds or plants in the ground so they can grow
