to provide for

1. VERB

/ prəˈvaɪd fɔr / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪd fə / (BrE)
to make arrangements or take necessary measures in anticipation of something, especially to ensure that needs are met or future events are addressed
make arrangements for future needs

Examples

It is important to provide for your children's education by saving early.
The new policy was designed to provide for those who are unable to work.
They have carefully provided for any unexpected emergencies during the trip.

Synset Examples

provide for the proper care of the passengers on the cruise ship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready or get ready for something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or attend to
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00407888-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prəˈvaɪd fər / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪd fə / (BrE)
to make arrangements or give the means so that something is possible or will happen; to allow or plan for something to occur
make possible or arrange for

Examples

The new contract provides for annual salary increases based on performance.
The law provides for emergency funding in case of natural disasters.
The schedule provides for several breaks throughout the day.

Synset Examples

This leaves no room for improvement
The evidence allows only one conclusion
allow for mistakes
leave lots of time for the trip
This procedure provides for lots of leeway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allow for - to make allowances or plans for something
  • Synonym: leave room for - to permit the possibility or opportunity for something
