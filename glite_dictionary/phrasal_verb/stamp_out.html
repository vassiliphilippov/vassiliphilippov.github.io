to stamp out

1. VERB

/ stæmp aʊt / (AmE), / stæmp aʊt / (BrE)
to eliminate or put an end to something, especially by using strong or forceful action
eliminate or end by force

Examples

The government hopes to stamp out corruption through strict new laws.
Health officials are working hard to stamp out the spread of the disease.
They launched a campaign to stamp out smoking in public places.

Synset Examples

Stamp out poverty!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eradicate - to completely destroy or eliminate something harmful
  • Synonym: to suppress - to forcibly put an end to something
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone (opposite of suppress)
