to stand out

1. VERB

/ stænd aʊt / (AmE), / stænd aʊt / (BrE)
to be very noticeable or conspicuous compared to the surrounding people or things
be very noticeable

Examples

Her bright red coat made her stand out in the crowd.
Among all the candidates, he really stood out with his creative ideas.
The painting's vivid colors stand out against the plain white wall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to blend in - to not be noticeable or to be similar to the surrounding people or things

2. VERB

/ stænd aʊt / (AmE), / stænd aʊt / (BrE)
to be very noticeable or to be much better than others in a group; to distinguish oneself
be clearly noticeable or exceptional

Examples

Her bright dress made her stand out at the party.
In a competitive market, a business must stand out to succeed.
His talent really stood out among the other contestants.

Synset Examples

She excelled in math

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excel - to do extremely well or be outstanding at something
  • Antonym: to blend in - to not be noticeable, to merge with the surroundings

/ stænd aʊt / (AmE), / stænd aʊt / (BrE)

be noticeable or prominent

She always tries to stand out by wearing bright, unusual clothing.
His achievements in science really stand out among his peers.
