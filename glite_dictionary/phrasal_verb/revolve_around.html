to revolve around

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɑlv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move in a circular path around a central point or object
move in a circular path around something

Examples

The Earth revolves around the Sun in about 365 days.
Several moons revolve around Jupiter, forming a complex system.
The planets in our solar system all revolve around the Sun.

Synset Examples

The Earth revolves around the Sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orbit - to move in a curved path around something, especially in space
  • Synonym: to rotate - to turn around an axis or center

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɑlv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to have something or someone as the main focus or most important element in an activity, situation, or life
have as main focus or center

Examples

Much of her life seemed to revolve around her children.
Discussions at work often revolve around upcoming projects and deadlines.
For many people, daily routines revolve around meal times.
