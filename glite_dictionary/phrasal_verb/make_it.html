to make it

1. VERB

/ ˈmeɪk ɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk ɪt / (BrE)
to succeed in passing a test or being chosen in a selection process
succeed in a test or selection process

Examples

After years of studying, she finally made it through her medical exams.
Only ten out of a hundred candidates will make it to the final round.
He was worried he wouldn't make it, but he passed the test with flying colors.

Synset Examples

She passed the new Jersey Bar Exam and can practice law now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - to be successful in an examination or test
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in a test or selection process

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmeɪk ɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk ɪt / (BrE)
to succeed in surviving or enduring after adversity, danger, or hardship
to survive after adversity

Examples

Despite all the difficulties she faced, she managed to make it through college.
The doctors weren’t sure he would make it, but he pulled through after the surgery.
Many businesses don’t make it past their first year of operation.

Synset Examples

He survived the cancer against all odds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to survive - to continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship

3. VERB

/ ˈmeɪk ɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk ɪt / (BrE)
to become very successful, especially in one's career or ambitions
become successful

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally made it as a professional musician.
He left his hometown determined to make it in the big city.
Their parents are proud because both children have really made it in their fields.

Synset Examples

After he published his book, he had arrived
I don't know whether I can make it in science!
You will go far, my boy!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - achieve the result you wanted or become successful
  • Antonym: to fail - not achieve success; be unsuccessful
