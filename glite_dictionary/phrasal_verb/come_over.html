to come over

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to be communicated in a particular way; to make a certain impression when expressed or presented
communicate a certain impression

Examples

Her message didn't come over as she had hoped, leaving the audience confused.
He tried to sound confident, but it didn't come over that way in his speech.
The joke didn't come over well at all, and no one laughed.

Synset Examples

He came across very clearly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come across - to be understood or perceived in a certain way
