to serve in the army

1. VERB

/ sɝv ɪn ði ˈɑrmi / (AmE), / sɜːv ɪn ði ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
to be a member of a country's armed forces, especially as a job or during a period of conscription
to be a member of the armed forces

Examples

After finishing college, he decided to serve in the army for a few years.
Both her grandfathers served in the army during World War II.
Many young people in that country are required to serve in the army for two years.

Synset Examples

He served in the army for four years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enlist - to join the armed forces
  • Antonym: desert - to leave the armed forces without permission
