to stay down with

1. VERB

/ steɪ daʊn wɪð / (AmE), / steɪ daʊn wɪð / (BrE)
to remain relatable or connected to younger people or contemporary trends, especially by adopting their attitudes or language
remain relatable to young people or trends
informal slang

Examples

He tries to stay down with the kids by using their slang.
To stay down with the students, she keeps up with the latest memes.
Some politicians attempt to stay down with younger voters by referencing pop culture.
