to eat away

1. VERB

/ it əˈweɪ / (AmE), / iːt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of a natural force) to gradually destroy or erode something, especially soil or rock.
gradually erode; wear away

Examples

Over time, the constant flow of water ate away the riverbank.
Acid rain can eat away at limestone buildings, causing structural damage.
The ocean waves have eaten away the base of the cliff for centuries.

Synset Examples

Rain eroded the terraces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away soil, rock, or land
  • Synonym: weather - to change and break down by long exposure to the elements

2. VERB

/ it əˈweɪ / (AmE), / iːt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually wear away or erode something, often by chemical action or persistent force
wear away or erode gradually

Examples

The constant dripping of water began to eat away the stone foundation over the years.
Rust can eat away at metal if it is left unprotected.
Over time, doubt started to eat away at his confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away, especially by natural forces
  • Synonym: to corrode - to destroy or damage (metal, stone, etc.) slowly by chemical action
