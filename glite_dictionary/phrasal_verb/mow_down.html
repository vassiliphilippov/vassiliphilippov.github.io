to mow down

1. VERB

/ moʊ daʊn / (AmE), / məʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to kill or injure many people suddenly and without discrimination, especially using gunfire or other weapons
kill or injure many people suddenly

Examples

The attackers mowed down dozens of unarmed villagers before escaping into the night.
In the movie, the villain mows down his enemies without mercy.
The troops mowed down the advancing soldiers with machine guns.

Synset Examples

The Hutus massacred the Tutsis in Rwanda

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - to kill many people in a violent and cruel way

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from 'mow', meaning to cut down with a scythe.
