to keep guard

1. VERB

/ kip ɡɑrd / (AmE), / kiːp ɡɑːd / (BrE)
to remain on watch in order to protect or defend someone or something
remain on watch to protect

Examples

Two soldiers were assigned to keep guard at the entrance of the camp.
He volunteered to keep guard while the others slept.
Throughout the night, the dog kept guard over the property.

Synset Examples

We must stand sentinel to protect ourselves
The jewels over which they kept guard were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand guard - to keep watch in order to protect
  • Synonym: guard - to watch over and protect
