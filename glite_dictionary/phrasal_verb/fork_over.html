to fork over

1. VERB

/ fɔrk ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / fɔːk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to give something, especially money, to someone, often unwillingly or after being asked or pressured
to hand something over, especially reluctantly
informal

Examples

After much arguing, he finally forked over the cash he owed.
The company refused to fork over compensation until they were threatened with legal action.
Don't expect me to fork over my savings just because you asked.

Synset Examples

the guard delivered the criminal to the police
render up the prisoners
render the town to the enemy
fork over the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hand over - to give something to someone else, especially in a position of authority
  • Synonym: to cough up - to give money or something, especially when reluctant
