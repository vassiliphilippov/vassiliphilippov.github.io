to come short

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʃɔrt / (AmE), / kʌm ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to fail to reach a required standard or to achieve what was expected
fail to meet expectations or standards

Examples

Despite his efforts, he came short of qualifying for the finals.
Their proposal comes short of what we had hoped for.
The team worked hard but still came short in the championship match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall short - to fail to reach a desired amount or standard
