to tootle around

1. VERB

/ ˈtuːtəl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtuːtəl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move or travel about in a leisurely or aimless way
move casually or aimlessly
informal

Examples

On weekends, we like to tootle around the countryside in our old car.
Instead of rushing home, he decided to tootle around downtown for a while.
They spent the afternoon tootling around the city, enjoying the sights.

Synset Examples

She decided to tootle around the park for some fresh air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to move about without a fixed course
  • Synonym: stroll - to walk in a slow, relaxed way

Etymology

From 'tootle', which imitates the sound of a musical instrument and came to mean move leisurely, plus 'around'.
