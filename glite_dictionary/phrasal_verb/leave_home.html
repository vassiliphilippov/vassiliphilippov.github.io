to leave home

1. VERB

/ liv hoʊm / (AmE), / liːv həʊm / (BrE)
to move out of one's family house to live independently
move out from family house

Examples

Many young people choose to leave home when they turn eighteen.
He decided to leave home in search of better opportunities in the city.
It can be difficult for parents when their children finally leave home.

Synset Examples

It's hard to leave home after all these years.
She plans to leave home for college soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to move out - to stop living in a particular home
