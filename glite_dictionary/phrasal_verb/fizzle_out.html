to fizzle out

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪzəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɪzəl aʊt / (BrE)
to come to a weak, disappointing, or ineffective end; to fail or lose strength gradually and stop
end weakly or fail gradually

Examples

After a promising start, the project began to fizzle out due to lack of interest.
Their enthusiasm for the new club quickly fizzled out over the following months.
The concert seemed exciting at first, but it eventually fizzled out with no real conclusion.

Synset Examples

The music just petered out — there was no proper ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to peter out - to gradually decrease and stop

Etymology

From "fizzle" (to fail or die out, originally meaning 'to break wind quietly'), probably of imitative origin, plus out.

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪzəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɪzəl aʊt / (BrE)
to gradually come to an end or fail after starting off strongly or with promise
gradually fail or come to an end

Examples

Interest in the new product began to fizzle out after a few months.
Their relationship slowly fizzled out when they realized they had nothing in common.
Despite high hopes, the protest movement quickly fizzled out by the end of the year.

Synset Examples

His enthusiasm seemed to fizzle as the meeting dragged on.
Their plans to start a band began to fizzle out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to die down - to become less strong or intense; to subside
  • Synonym: to fade away - to slowly disappear or become weaker

Etymology

From 'fizzle' (to fail ingloriously), likely imitative of a hissing or sputtering sound, combined with 'out'.
