to beetle off

1. VERB

/ ˈbiːtəl ɒf / (AmE), / ˈbiːtl ɒf / (BrE)
to leave quickly and suddenly, usually without attracting attention
leave quickly or suddenly

Examples

As soon as the meeting ended, he beetled off to catch his train.
The children beetled off the moment they heard their mother calling.
She beetled off without saying goodbye, leaving everyone puzzled.

Synset Examples

The listeners bolted when he discussed his strange ideas
When she started to tell silly stories, I ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run off - to leave a place or person quickly and unexpectedly
  • Synonym: to dash off - to leave somewhere in a hurry

Etymology

Possibly from the movement of beetles, which often scurry away rapidly when disturbed.
