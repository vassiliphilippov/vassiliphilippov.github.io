to bring through

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ θruː / (AmE), / brɪŋ θruː / (BrE)
to help someone survive or recover from a difficult or dangerous situation; to bring someone into safety
help survive a dangerous or difficult situation

Examples

The nurse's quick actions brought through several patients who were in critical condition.
Despite the harsh conditions, the guide managed to bring through everyone in the hiking group safely.
Her determination and care brought through her brother after his serious accident.

Synset Examples

We pulled through most of the victims of the bomb attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull through - to recover from a serious illness or survive a difficult situation
  • Synonym: rescue - to save from danger or harm
  • Antonym: to endanger - to put someone or something at risk or in danger
