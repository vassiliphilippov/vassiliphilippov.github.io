to butt in

1. VERB

/ bʌt ɪn / (AmE), / bʌt ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt a conversation or activity, especially when not invited to do so
interrupt a conversation unexpectedly

Examples

It's rude to butt in when others are speaking.
She hates it when people butt in during meetings.
Please don't butt in; let him finish his story.

Synset Examples

her husband always chimes in, even when he is not involved in the conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from continuing what they are saying or doing
  • Synonym: to cut in - to interrupt someone while they are speaking

2. VERB

/ bʌt ɪn / (AmE), / bʌt ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt or intrude in a conversation or activity, often rudely or without invitation
interrupt or intrude in a conversation

Examples

I'm sorry to butt in, but I think you're making a mistake.
She wished her neighbor wouldn't always butt in to their private conversations.
It's rude to butt in when others are talking.

Synset Examples

I hate it when people butt in on my plans.
Please don’t butt in while I’m speaking.
He tends to butt in during conversations uninvited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop someone in the middle of speaking or doing something
  • Synonym: to cut in - to interrupt a conversation or activity, especially suddenly
