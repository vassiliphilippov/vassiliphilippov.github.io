to go past

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ pæst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ pɑːst / (BrE)
to be superior or better than a particular standard or expectation; to surpass or exceed
surpass; exceed a standard or expectation

Examples

This year's profits go past all previous records.
She managed to go past the limitations of her training.
The movie's success went past what anyone had predicted.

Synset Examples

She exceeded our expectations
She topped her performance of last year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exceed - to go beyond a particular limit or standard
  • Synonym: to surpass - to be better or greater than someone or something else
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach a required or expected standard
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02674870-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ pæst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ pɑːst / (BrE)
to move beyond or pass by someone or something
move beyond; pass by

Examples

She watched the train go past her window as she finished her coffee.
Don’t let the opportunity go past without taking advantage of it.
We had to step aside to let the runners go past during the marathon.

Synset Examples

A black limousine passed by when she looked out the window
He passed his professor in the hall
One line of soldiers surpassed the other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - move past or beyond something
  • Synonym: to overtake - move past something or someone ahead, especially in a vehicle or race
  • Antonym: stop - to cease movement or progress
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02055618-v
Synset POS: v
