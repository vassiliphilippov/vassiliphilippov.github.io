to go under

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈʌndər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to sink below the surface, especially of water, and often be submerged completely
sink below the surface

Examples

The old fishing boat began to go under as water filled the hull.
If you don't keep swimming, you might go under in the deep lake.
After the storm, several small dinghies went under near the dock.

Synset Examples

The raft sank and its occupants drowned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sink - to go down below the surface of water or another liquid
  • Antonym: float - to remain on the surface of a liquid and not go under

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈʌndər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to be known or called by a particular name
be called by a particular name

Examples

The writer goes under several different pen names.
He now goes under the name of Michael Dean.
The shop used to go under another name before it was renovated.

Synset Examples

She goes by her maiden name again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go by - to use a particular name or title; be known as

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈʌndər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈʌndə / (BrE)
(of a business, person, or other entity) to fail, especially financially, or to sink or become submerged.
to fail (esp. financially) or sink

Examples

The company began to go under after the recession hit.
If the lifeboats don't arrive soon, the boat will go under.
Several restaurants in the area had to go under during the pandemic.

Synset Examples

Many startups go under within the first year.
If sales don't improve, the business might go under.
The ship began to go under during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often used for financial failure
  • Synonym: sink - to go below the surface of water and become submerged
