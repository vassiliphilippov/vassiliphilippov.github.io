to dress up

1. VERB

/ drɛs ʌp / (AmE), / drɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to put on formal or special clothes, often for a particular occasion; to wear more elegant or elaborate clothing than usual
put on formal or special clothes

Examples

He decided to dress up for the interview, wearing his best suit.
Children often like to dress up as their favorite characters on Halloween.
You don't need to dress up for dinner; casual clothes are fine.

Synset Examples

She dresses in the latest Paris fashion
he dressed up in a suit and tie

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dress down - to wear more casual or less formal clothing than usual

5. VERB

/ drɛs ʌp / (AmE), / drɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to make something seem more attractive, impressive, or appealing than it really is, often in a superficial or misleading way
make something seem more attractive than it is

Examples

The company tried to dress up its quarterly report to hide the losses.

It's not wise to dress up the facts just to please the audience.

Synset Examples
