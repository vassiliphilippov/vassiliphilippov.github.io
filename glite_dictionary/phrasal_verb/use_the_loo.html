to use the loo

1. VERB

/ juz ði lu / (AmE), / juːz ðə luː / (BrE)
to go to the toilet; to use the bathroom, especially in British English
go to the toilet (British)
informal british

Examples

Excuse me for a moment, I need to use the loo.
She quickly ran upstairs to use the loo before the movie started.
If you need to use the loo, it's down the hall to the left.

Synset Examples

I’ll be right back; I need to use the loo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to use the bathroom - go to the toilet (American English expression)
  • Synonym: loo - British informal word for a toilet
  • Synonym: toilet - a fixture for defecation and urination

Etymology

'Loo' is British slang for toilet, origin uncertain, possibly from French 'lieu' (place) or 'Gardez l'eau!'
