to be a snob

1. VERB

/ bi ə snɑb / (AmE), / bi ə snɒb / (BrE)
to behave in an unfriendly or superior way because you believe you belong to a higher social class or have better taste than others
act in a superior or snobbish way

Examples

I can't stand people who be a snob about wine and refuse to try anything new.
She used to be a snob in high school, but now she's much more down-to-earth.
Try not to be a snob when meeting people with different backgrounds.

Synset Examples

Don't be a snob; try the local cuisine.
Don't be a snoot; try the local food instead of sticking to the familiar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be a snoot - to act superior to others about tastes or status
  • Antonym: be humble - to not view oneself as better than others
