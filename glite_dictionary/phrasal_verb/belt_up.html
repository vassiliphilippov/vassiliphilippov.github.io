to belt up

1. VERB

/ bɛlt ʌp / (AmE), / bɛlt ʌp / (BrE)
(British, informal) To stop talking; to be quiet or fall silent.
stop talking; be quiet (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

Could you please belt up while I'm on the phone?
The teacher told the noisy class to belt up and listen.
If you don't belt up, you'll have to leave the room.

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut up - to stop talking; to be quiet

2. VERB

/ bɛlt ʌp / (AmE), / bɛlt ʌp / (BrE)
to fasten a seatbelt, especially when travelling in a vehicle
fasten a seatbelt

Examples

Before we start driving, please belt up for your own safety.
I always belt up as soon as I get in the car.
She told her children to belt up before the plane took off.

Synset Examples

He reminded everyone to belt up before leaving the parking lot.
You should belt up before the ride starts.
Make sure to belt up for safety's sake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to buckle up - to fasten one's seatbelt
