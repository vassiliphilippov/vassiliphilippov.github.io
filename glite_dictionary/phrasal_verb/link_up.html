to link up

1. VERB

/ lɪŋk ʌp / (AmE), / lɪŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to make a logical or causal connection between things or ideas
make a logical connection

Examples

The detective tried to link up the new evidence with the previous clues.
It's difficult to link up his strange behavior to any known motive.
Can you link up those facts to support your argument?

Synset Examples

I cannot connect these two pieces of evidence in my mind
colligate these facts
I cannot relate these events at all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join or associate things or ideas in the mind
  • Synonym: to relate - to show or make a connection between two or more things
  • Antonym: disconnect - to break or not establish a connection between things
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ lɪŋk ʌp / (AmE), / lɪŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to join or connect with someone or something, or to become joined or connected
join or become connected

Examples

After several years apart, the cousins decided to link up for a family reunion.
The river and the canal link up just before entering the city.
Researchers around the world link up to share their findings at this conference.

Synset Examples

The two streets connect to become a highway
Our paths joined
The travelers linked up again at the airport

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join or be joined with something else
  • Antonym: separate - to move apart or become disconnected
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02628315-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lɪŋk ʌp / (AmE), / lɪŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to connect or fasten two or more things together; to join parts so that they form a single unit
connect or join together

Examples

The two trains will link up at the next station.
Engineers managed to link up several computers to create a network.
She linked up the wires to complete the circuit.

Synset Examples

Can you connect the two loudspeakers?
Tie the ropes together
Link arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join together so as to provide access or communication
  • Synonym: join - to bring or put together
  • Antonym: to detach - to disconnect or separate something
