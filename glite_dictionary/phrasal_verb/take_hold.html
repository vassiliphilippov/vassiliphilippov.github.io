to take hold

1. VERB

/ teɪk hoʊld / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld / (BrE)
to assume control or to become dominant in a situation or process
assume control; become dominant

Examples

When fear began to take hold, the team lost its focus.
A sense of calm took hold as she entered the room.
As soon as the new manager arrived, her leadership style took hold in the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize control - to take control quickly or forcefully
  • Antonym: lose control - to cease having control over something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02445635-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk hoʊld / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld / (BrE)
to grasp or seize something deliberately with your hands
grasp or seize something deliberately

Examples

He reached out to take hold of the rope before he slipped.
The nurse gently took hold of the patient's arm to examine it.
She quickly took hold of her bag when the phone rang.

Synset Examples

The sales clerk quickly seized the money on the counter
She clutched her purse
The mother seized her child by the arm
Birds of prey often seize small mammals
A crazy idea took hold of him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
  • Synonym: grab - to take or grasp something suddenly
  • Antonym: release - to let go of something held or seized
