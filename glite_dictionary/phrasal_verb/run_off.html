to run off

1. VERB

/ rʌn ɔf / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place or person suddenly and unexpectedly, often in a hurry
leave suddenly and unexpectedly

Examples

She suddenly ran off without saying goodbye to anyone.
Don't run off just because you hear a bit of bad news.
The dog ran off as soon as it saw the open gate.

Synset Examples

The listeners bolted when he discussed his strange ideas
When she started to tell silly stories, I ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - leave suddenly and unexpectedly
  • Synonym: to flee - run away quickly, often from danger or responsibility
  • Antonym: to linger - stay in a place longer than necessary
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

Examples
