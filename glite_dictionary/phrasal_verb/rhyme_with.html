to rhyme with

1. VERB

/ raɪm wɪð / (AmE), / raɪm wɪð / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) to have the same or similar ending sound as another word or phrase, especially in poetry or song.
have the same ending sound as another word

Examples

The word 'cat' rhymes with 'hat' and 'bat'.
Can you think of another word that rhymes with 'blue'?
Her favorite poem uses lines that rhyme with each other throughout.

Synset Examples

Many words in English rhymes-with-rich, like 'switch'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhyme - to have or produce a rhyme
