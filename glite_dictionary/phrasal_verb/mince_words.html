to mince words

1. VERB

/ mɪns wɝdz / (AmE), / mɪns wɜːdz / (BrE)
to speak in a restrained or indirect way, especially to avoid being blunt or offending
speak indirectly or with restraint

Examples

He never minces words when discussing important issues with his team.
Rather than mincing words, she said exactly what everyone was thinking.
If you want the honest truth, don't expect him to mince words.

Synset Examples

She doesn't mince words when giving feedback.
Let's not mince words; this project is failing.
He tends to mince his words when he’s nervous.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: be blunt - speak in a direct, straightforward way
  • Antonym: speak plainly - to communicate without softening or avoiding the truth
