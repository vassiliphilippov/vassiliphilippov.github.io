to sweep away

1. VERB

/ swip əˈweɪ / (AmE), / swiːp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to overwhelm someone emotionally, so that they are carried away by strong feelings
overwhelm emotionally

Examples

She was completely swept away by his declaration of love.
At the funeral, many people were swept away by emotion.
He found himself swept away by the beauty of the music.

Synset Examples

Her swept her away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to affect someone so strongly they cannot think clearly
  • Synonym: move - to affect emotionally

2. VERB

/ swip əˈweɪ / (AmE), / swiːp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate something completely, often leaving no trace
eliminate or remove completely

Examples

The new government vowed to sweep away corruption and inefficiency.
Years of tradition were suddenly swept away by the reforms.
Heavy rains can sweep away bridges and roads in a matter of hours.

Synset Examples

The old values have been wiped out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe out - to destroy or remove completely
  • Synonym: to eradicate - to destroy completely; to put an end to

3. VERB

/ swip əˈweɪ / (AmE), / swiːp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or destroy completely, so that nothing remains
remove or eliminate completely

Examples

The heavy rains threatened to sweep away the bridge during the flood.
Many traditions were swept away by the rapid modernization of the city.
A sense of relief swept away her anxiety after hearing the good news.

Synset Examples

The storm will sweep away the old debris.
She hopes to sweep away the past mistakes.
Time will eventually sweep away their worries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove completely, especially from memory or existence
  • Synonym: to obliterate - to destroy completely, leaving no trace
