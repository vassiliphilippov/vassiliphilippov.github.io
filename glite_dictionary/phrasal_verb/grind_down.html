to grind down

1. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd daʊn / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd daʊn / (BrE)
to treat people cruelly or oppressively, especially by abusing power; to rule as a tyrant
oppress or rule tyrannically

Examples

The dictator continued to grind down his people, denying them basic freedoms and rights.
For decades, the regime has ground down any opposition that threatens its power.
She spoke out against leaders who grind down citizens with harsh laws and relentless surveillance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppress - to keep someone in subservience, especially by unjust use of authority
  • Synonym: to tyrannize - to rule or treat someone in a cruel, autocratic way
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set free from a situation, especially oppression

2. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd daʊn / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd daʊn / (BrE)
to gradually weaken or exhaust someone, or to make a surface smooth by rubbing or abrasion
weaken someone or make smooth by rubbing

Examples

The coach's demands began to grind down the young athletes over the season.
Carpenters often grind down sharp edges for a smoother finish.
Years of relentless workloads can grind down even the most enthusiastic employees.

Synset Examples

They had to grind down the rough edges of the wood.
We need to grind down our differences for peace.
The constant criticism can grind anyone down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear down - to make someone gradually weaker or to make something thinner through use or effort
  • Degree Higher: polish - to make smooth and shiny by rubbing
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make or become stronger
