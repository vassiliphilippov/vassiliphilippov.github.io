to gain traction

1. VERB

/ ɡeɪn ˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ɡeɪn ˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
to begin to have more acceptance, influence, or popularity; to start becoming successful or established.
become more accepted or popular

Examples

The new mobile payment system is starting to gain traction among small business owners.
Her ideas on environmental policy have recently gained traction in the local community.
The social media campaign quickly gained traction after being shared by several influencers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to become popular or fashionable
  • Antonym: fade - to lose popularity or strength gradually
