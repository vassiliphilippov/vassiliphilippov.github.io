to stack up

1. VERB

/ stæk ʌp / (AmE), / stæk ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange things in a neat pile or stack, one on top of another
arrange in a pile or stack

Examples

Could you please stack up the boxes by the door?
The magazines in the waiting room were stacked up neatly on the table.
Every morning, the bakery stacks up fresh loaves on the shelves.

Synset Examples

She piled up her books in my living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pile up - to form a heap or collection by putting things on top of each other
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01506930-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stæk ʌp / (AmE), / stæk ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or save things, especially resources or debts, usually so that they form a larger amount over time
accumulate or save (resources or debts)

Examples

If you don't clear your emails, they will stack up quickly.
She managed to stack up enough vacation days for a long trip.
Unpaid invoices can stack up and cause financial stress.

Synset Examples

Make sure to stack up your savings for emergencies.
The bills will stack up if you don't pay them on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect, especially over time
  • Synonym: to pile up - to become larger in quantity; to accumulate
Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
