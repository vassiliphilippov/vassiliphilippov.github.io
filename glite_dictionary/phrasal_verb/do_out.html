to do out

1. VERB

/ du aʊt / (AmE), / duː aʊt / (BrE)
to clean, organize, or prepare (a room or space) thoroughly for use
clean or organize thoroughly
british

Examples

Every spring, I do out the kitchen to get rid of unused items and dust.
The caretaker was hired to do out the guest rooms before the visitors arrived.
After their holiday, they decided to do out the garage together.

Synset Examples

She plans to do out her closet this weekend.
We need to do out the garden before spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tidy up - to make a place clean and orderly
