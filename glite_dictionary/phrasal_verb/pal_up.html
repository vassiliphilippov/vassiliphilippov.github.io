to pal up

1. VERB

/ pæl ʌp / (AmE), / pæl ʌp / (BrE)
to become friends with someone or start acting in a friendly way toward them
become friends with someone

Examples

At summer camp, she quickly palled up with the other girls in her cabin.
He always tries to pal up with the popular kids at school.
They seemed to pal up after working together on the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befriend - to become friends with someone

Etymology

From 'pal' (friend), of Romani origin, with 'up' indicating the initiation of the action.
