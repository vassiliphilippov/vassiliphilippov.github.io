to go along with the gag

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈlɔŋ wɪð ðə ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈlɒŋ wɪð ðə ɡæɡ / (BrE)
to cooperate or participate in a humorous act, prank, or joke, often by pretending to believe or join in
participate in a joke or prank
informal

Examples

She decided to go along with the gag and pretended not to recognize her friend in disguise.
Even when the prank got a bit out of hand, he continued to go along with the gag.
It's more fun when everyone chooses to go along with the gag rather than spoil the surprise.

Synset Examples

He loved to go along with the gag during their prank wars.
Even though it was silly, he chose to go along with the gag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play along - to cooperate or pretend to cooperate for the sake of appearances
