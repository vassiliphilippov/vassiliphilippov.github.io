to enter upon

1. VERB

/ ˈɛntɚ əˌpɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛntə əˌpɒn / (BrE)
to take formal possession of something, especially property or land
take formal possession (property, land)
legal

Examples

After years of litigation, she finally entered upon her inheritance.
The new landlord will enter upon the property next week after all the papers are signed.
Once you enter upon the premises, you are responsible for its upkeep.

Synset Examples

She entered upon the estate of her rich relatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take possession of - to gain ownership or control of something
