to come to nothing

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / kʌm tʊ ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
to fail to produce any result or success; to end in failure
to result in failure

Examples

After months of negotiations, the merger finally came to nothing.
If all our efforts come to nothing, we will have to try a different approach.
Her hopes for promotion came to nothing when the company restructured.

Synset Examples

Their plans came to nothing after the budget cuts.
All their hard work came to nothing in the end.
Despite their efforts, the project came to nothing.
All his ambitions came to nought when he lost his job.
His dreams came to nought after the accident.
Their negotiations came to nought and led to conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving something
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve a desired result
