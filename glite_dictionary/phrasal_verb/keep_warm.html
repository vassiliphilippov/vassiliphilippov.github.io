to keep warm

1. VERB

/ kip wɔrm / (AmE), / kiːp wɔːm / (BrE)
to prevent yourself from becoming cold; to maintain body heat
maintain body heat, not get cold

Examples

She wore extra layers to keep warm on the chilly evening.
They huddled by the fire to keep warm while camping.
Drinking hot tea helped him keep warm during the winter storm.

Synset Examples

She wore a hood to keep warm during winter.
They built a fire to keep warm during the night.
Every wintard, I struggle to stay warm in the cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay warm - to remain at a comfortable temperature, not get cold
  • Antonym: to cool down - to become cooler or cause to become cooler
