to date back

1. VERB

/ deɪt bæk / (AmE), / deɪt bæk / (BrE)
to have existed since a particular time in the past; to originate from an earlier period
originate from a past time

Examples

The village church dates back to the 12th century.
Some of these traditions date back hundreds of years.
This artifact dates back to the Roman Empire.

Synset Examples

This story dates back 200 years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to originate - to begin to exist; to come from somewhere
