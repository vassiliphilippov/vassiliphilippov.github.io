to blunder out

1. VERB

/ ˈblʌndɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə aʊt / (BrE)
to say something impulsively or clumsily, often without thinking
utter something impulsively or awkwardly

Examples

During the meeting, he blundered out an inappropriate joke that embarrassed everyone.
She realized too late that she had blundered out the confidential details to the press.
Trying to help, the child blundered out the answer before anyone else had a chance.

Synset Examples

He blurted out the secret
He blundered his stupid ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blurt out - to say something suddenly and without thinking
