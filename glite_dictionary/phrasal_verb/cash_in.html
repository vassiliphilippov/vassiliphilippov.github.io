to cash in

1. VERB

/ kæʃ ɪn / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to exchange something, such as a check or bonds, for cash
exchange something for cash

Examples

I went to the bank to cash in some bonds.
She cashed in her traveler's checks for local currency.
After winning the raffle, he was eager to cash in his prize certificate.

Synset Examples

I cashed the check as soon as it arrived in the mail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash - pay in cash or obtain money for an instrument such as a check
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02261162-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kæʃ ɪn / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to convert something into cash or realize a profit, often by taking advantage of an opportunity
convert to cash or profit

Examples

She decided to cash in her bonds when rates dropped.
Many investors cash in their shares at the end of the year.
He plans to cash in on his success by selling his company.

Synset Examples

He decided to cash-in his stocks during the market peak.
She wants to cash in before the market drops.
She plans to cash-in her savings for a new car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquidate - to convert assets into cash

Synset POS: None
