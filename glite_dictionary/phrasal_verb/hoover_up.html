to hoover up

1. VERB

/ ˈhuːvɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈhuːvə ʌp / (BrE)
to clean or remove dirt, dust, or debris using a vacuum cleaner, often quickly or thoroughly
vacuum clean dirt or debris
british

Examples

After the kids left, she hoovered up all the cookie crumbs from the carpet.
Could you hoover up the living room before our guests arrive?
He quickly hoovered up the mess after the party.

Synset Examples

He will hoover up the crumbs after dinner.
Don't forget to hoover up before guests arrive.
She decided to hoover up the mess quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum - to clean something using a vacuum cleaner

Etymology

From Hoover, a trademark for a brand of vacuum cleaner, used in British English as a verb.
