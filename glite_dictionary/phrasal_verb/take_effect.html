to take effect

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / teɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to begin to apply or be in force; to become operative or valid
to become operative or valid

Examples

The new regulations will take effect at the start of the year.
It often takes a few minutes for the medicine to take effect.
The changes to the contract took effect immediately after both sides signed.

Synset Examples

The new law will take effect next month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come into force - to become valid or operative (synonym)
