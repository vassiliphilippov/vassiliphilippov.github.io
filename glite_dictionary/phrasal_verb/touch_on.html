to touch on

1. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɑn / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to be relevant to or deal briefly with a particular subject or issue
mention or relate to briefly

Examples

The professor did not go into detail but did touch on the main points of the theory.
Her speech barely touched on the subject of climate change.
The article touches on several important issues relevant to our discussion.

Synset Examples

There were lots of questions referring to her talk
My remark pertained to your earlier comments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refer to - to mention or allude to
  • Synonym: pertain to - to be relevant or appropriate to

WordNet ID: oewn-02681865-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɑn / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to mention or discuss something briefly without going into detail
mention or discuss briefly

Examples

In her speech, she touched on the topic of climate change without providing many details.
The article touches on several important issues but doesn't explore them fully.
He briefly touched on his childhood during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mention - to refer to something briefly or without detail
  • Antonym: elaborate - to explain or discuss in more detail
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɑn / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to have an effect upon or influence something or someone, even if only slightly
have an effect or influence (slightly)

Examples

The new law will touch on every aspect of our daily lives.
Rising fuel prices touch on all sectors of the economy, not just transportation.
Her decisions as a manager often touch on the work of several different departments.

Synset Examples

Will the new rules affect me?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affect - to have an influence on; to make a difference to
  • Synonym: impact - to have a strong effect on something or someone
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɑn / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to mention or discuss something briefly in speech or writing
briefly mention or discuss

Examples

During the interview, the speaker touched on the challenges faced by young entrepreneurs.
The author only touches on the issue in the introduction of the book.
We will touch on this topic again in our next meeting.

Synset Examples

Let's touch on the main points before we wrap up.
The lecture will touch on climate change issues.
She will touch on her experience during the meeting.

Related Terms
