to fly at each other

1. VERB

/ flaɪ æt iːtʃ ˈʌðɚ / (AmE), / flaɪ ət iːtʃ ˈʌðə / (BrE)
(of two or more people) to argue or fight with sudden and intense aggression.
argue or fight aggressively (of people, mutually)

Examples

The children began to fly at each other the moment their parents left the room.
They would often fly at each other whenever a disagreement arose.
Instead of talking calmly, the two colleagues flew at each other over the project.

Synset Examples

They couldn't help but fly at each other over the misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - to argue angrily with someone
  • Synonym: fight - to engage in physical conflict
