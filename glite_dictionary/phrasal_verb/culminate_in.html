to culminate in

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌlmɪˌneɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌlmɪneɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to reach a final or climactic point, ending in a specific event or result
come to a climax or final result

Examples

Months of training will culminate in a final exam at the end of the course.
The negotiations culminated in a successful peace agreement.
All their hard work culminated in the launch of a new product.

Synset Examples

The project will culminate in a grand presentation next week.
The election will culminate in a grand celebration of democracy and unification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result in - to have a specified result or outcome
  • Synonym: to lead to - to cause or result in a particular outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'culminare' (to crown, top), from 'culmen' (peak, summit).
