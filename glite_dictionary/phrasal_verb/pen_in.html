to pen in

1. VERB

/ pɛn ɪn / (AmE), / pɛn ɪn / (BrE)
to write down or record something, especially quickly or informally
write down; record in writing

Examples

During the workshop, she likes to pen in interesting ideas that come up.
He made sure to pen in the appointment as soon as he received the email.
Many writers pen in their thoughts at the end of each day.

Synset Examples

He likes to pen in his ideas during meetings.
She decided to pen in her thoughts before bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write down - to put something in writing; record
  • Synonym: to jot down - to quickly write something; make a quick note

2. VERB

/ pɛn ɪn / (AmE), / pɛn ɪn / (BrE)
to write down (goals, plans, etc.) for greater clarity or commitment
write down goals or plans

Examples

She likes to pen in her goals at the beginning of each month.
If you pen in what you want to achieve, it feels more real.
After the workshop, everyone was told to pen in their top three objectives.

Synset Examples

It's best to pen in your goals for clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write down - to put in writing

Etymology

From 'pen' (verb: to write) + 'in' (adverbial particle indicating inclusion or enclosure).
