to stay put

1. VERB

/ steɪ pʊt / (AmE), / steɪ pʊt / (BrE)
to remain in the same place and not move or leave
remain in one place

Examples

You should stay put while the doctor examines you.
We decided to stay put during the storm rather than seek shelter elsewhere.
If lost in the woods, it's often safest to stay put and wait for help.

Synset Examples

We are staying in Detroit; we are not moving to Cincinnati
Stay put in the corner here!
Stick around and you will learn something!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than expected
  • Antonym: move - to change location or position

2. VERB

/ steɪ pʊt / (AmE), / steɪ pʊt / (BrE)
to remain in the same position; to not move from a place
remain in the same place

Examples

If there's an emergency, just stay put until help arrives.
I told the children to stay put while I went to get the car.
During the storm, everyone was advised to stay put indoors.

Synset Examples

Just stay put until I come back.
The dog was told to stay put and not move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remain - to stay in the same place or condition
  • Antonym: move - to go from one place to another
