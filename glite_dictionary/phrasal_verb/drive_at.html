to drive at

1. VERB

/ ˈdraɪv æt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪv æt / (BrE)
to be trying to suggest, imply, or get at something indirectly in speech or writing
imply or suggest indirectly

Examples

I'm not sure what you're driving at—could you make your point clearer?
She kept giving me clues but I couldn't tell what she was driving at.
He smiled knowingly, as if he expected everyone to understand what he was driving at.

Synset Examples

What are you driving at?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - to suggest something without saying it directly
