to renege on

1. VERB

/ rɪˈnɛɡ ɑn / (AmE), / rɪˈniːɡ ɒn / (BrE)
to fail to do something that you promised or agreed to do
fail to keep a promise or agreement

Examples

The company reneged on its agreement to provide health benefits.
He was upset when his friend reneged on their deal at the last minute.
Several countries have reneged on promises to reduce carbon emissions.

Synset Examples

she reneged on her promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - fail to keep (a promise, one's word)
  • Antonym: to fulfill - to carry out (a promise, an obligation)

Etymology

From Latin renegare (to deny, reject).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈnɛg ɑn / (AmE), / rɪˈniːg ɒn / (BrE)
to fail to carry out a promise, agreement, or obligation
fail to keep a promise or agreement

Examples

The company decided to renege on its agreement to provide additional resources.
He promised to help but later reneged on his word.
If you renege on your commitments, it can damage your reputation.

Synset Examples

she reneged on her promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break a promise - to not do what one has promised
  • Antonym: to keep a promise - to do what one has promised

Etymology

From Latin renegare (to deny), via Medieval French renegher.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00801904-v
Synset POS: v
