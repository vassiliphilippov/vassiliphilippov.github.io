to calm down

1. VERB

/ kɑm daʊn / (AmE), / kɑːm daʊn / (BrE)
to become quieter, less excited, or less intense; to relax after being angry, excited, or agitated
become quieter or less agitated

Examples

After the argument, everyone needed a few minutes to calm down.
The storm will calm down soon, so we can go outside.
She took deep breaths to calm down before giving her speech.

Synset Examples

the fighting lulled for a moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
  • Antonym: to agitate - to make someone nervous or upset

3. VERB

/ kɑm daʊn / (AmE), / kɑːm daʊn / (BrE)
to become or make someone become quieter, less angry, or less excited after a period of agitation or stress
become less agitated or excited

Examples

Please calm down before we talk about what happened.
After a few deep breaths, he managed to calm down and explain the situation.
It took her a while to calm down after receiving the upsetting news.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
