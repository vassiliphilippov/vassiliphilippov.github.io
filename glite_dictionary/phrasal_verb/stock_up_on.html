to stock up on

1. VERB

/ stɑk ʌp ɑn / (AmE), / stɒk ʌp ɒn / (BrE)
to buy or collect a large quantity of something for future use
buy or collect a large quantity in advance

Examples

We need to stock up on groceries before the storm hits.
Many people stocked up on masks during the pandemic.
She always stocks up on snacks for the holidays.

Synset Examples

Don’t forget to stock up on bathroom tissue.
We prepared for the hurricane by stocking up on supplies.
He stocked up on ammunition before the hunting trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stockpile - to accumulate a large supply of something
