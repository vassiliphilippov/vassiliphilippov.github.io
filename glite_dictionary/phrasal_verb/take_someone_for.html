to take someone for

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈsʌmwʌn fər / (AmE), / teɪk ˈsʌmwən fə / (BrE)
to believe or assume that someone is a particular type of person, often mistakenly
assume someone is a certain type

Examples

I didn't take her for someone who would lie.
He always takes people for fools, but he's usually wrong.
Did you take me for your assistant? I'm actually the manager.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake someone for - to wrongly identify someone as another person
