to mete out

1. VERB

/ mit aʊt / (AmE), / miːt aʊt / (BrE)
to give or administer something, especially justice, punishment, or portions, usually in a measured or controlled way
to administer or distribute (usually punishment or justice)

Examples

The judge decided to mete out a harsh sentence to those found guilty of fraud.
Teachers sometimes have to mete out punishment when rules are broken.
It is not his responsibility to mete out justice; that is for the courts.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present
dole out some money
shell out pocket money for the children
deal a blow to someone
the machine dispenses soft drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispense - to distribute or provide (a service or item) to a number of people
  • Synonym: to hand out - to distribute something to people
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired

Etymology

From Old English *metan* (to measure, allot).
