to chat up

1. VERB

/ tʃæt ʌp / (AmE), / tʃæt ʌp / (BrE)
to talk to someone in a friendly or romantic way, often with the intention of attracting them; to flirt with someone.
talk flirtatiously to attract someone
informal british

Examples

He was trying to chat up the bartender all night.
Whenever we went out, Tom would chat up everyone he met.
She caught him chatting up a girl at the party.

Synset Examples

The guys always try to chat up the new secretaries
My husband never flirts with other women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - to behave as if romantically interested in someone without serious intent

2. VERB

/ tʃæt ʌp / (AmE), / tʃæt ʌp / (BrE)
to talk to someone in a friendly or flirtatious way, often with the intention of attracting them romantically or sexually
flirtatiously talk with someone
british

Examples

He tried to chat up the bartender but she wasn't interested.
Sophie was chatted up by a stranger at the party last night.
Pete spent the evening chatting up his coworker in hopes of asking her out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - behave as if attracted to someone, usually playfully
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ tʃæt ʌp / (AmE), / tʃæt ʌp / (BrE)
to start a conversation with someone in a friendly or flirtatious way, often because you are attracted to them
talk to someone in a flirtatious way
british informal

Examples

He always tries to chat up the new colleagues at parties.
Sophie was embarrassed when her friend attempted to chat up the bartender.
Do you think it's easy to chat up strangers in this kind of place?

Synset Examples

It's not easy to chat up someone in a crowded room.
He tried to chat up the girl at the cafe.

Related Terms
