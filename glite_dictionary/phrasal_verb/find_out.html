to find out

1. VERB

/ faɪnd aʊt / (AmE), / faɪnd aʊt / (BrE)
to discover or establish information as a result of calculation, investigation, research, or study
discover or establish through investigation

Examples

We need to find out who is responsible for the error.
After some research, she finally found out the answer to her question.
Scientists are trying to find out how the disease spreads.

Synset Examples

find the product of two numbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discover - to find something that was hidden or unknown
  • Synonym: to ascertain - to find out something with certainty through investigation
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention or remain unaware
