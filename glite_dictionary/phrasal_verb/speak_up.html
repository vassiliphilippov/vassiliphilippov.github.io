to speak up

1. VERB

/ spiːk ʌp / (AmE), / spiːk ʌp / (BrE)
to express one's opinion openly and without fear or hesitation
express one's opinion openly

Examples

If you disagree with the plan, you should speak up during the discussion.
She finally spoke up about the unfair treatment at work.
We encourage all employees to speak up if they notice any problems.

Synset Examples

John spoke up at the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voice - to express in words, state openly (verb)
  • Antonym: stay silent - to not express an opinion

2. VERB

/ spiːk ʌp / (AmE), / spiːk ʌp / (BrE)
to speak more loudly so that people can hear you
to speak more loudly

Examples

Could you please speak up so everyone at the back can hear you?
The manager asked her to speak up during the meeting if she had any concerns.
He had to speak up because the microphone wasn't working properly.

Synset Examples

The audience asked the lecturer to please speak up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speak - to say something using your voice
  • Degree Higher: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Degree Lower: whisper - to speak very quietly
