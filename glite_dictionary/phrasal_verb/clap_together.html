to clap together

1. VERB

/ klæp təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / klæp təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to make or assemble something quickly and carelessly, often using whatever materials are immediately available
assemble hastily and carelessly

Examples

He managed to clap together a rough shelter before the storm hit.
The committee clapped together a report in just two days.
She clapped together a meal from whatever was left in the fridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw together - to assemble or create something quickly without careful planning
