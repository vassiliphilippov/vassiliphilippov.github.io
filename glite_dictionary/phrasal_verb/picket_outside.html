to picket outside

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪk.ɪt ˌaʊt.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.ɪt ˌaʊt.saɪd / (BrE)
to stand or march outside a place, especially as part of a protest, to influence others or express opposition
stand in protest outside a location

Examples

Workers threatened to picket outside the company headquarters if their demands were not met.
A group of students began to picket outside the administration building early in the morning.
Environmental activists plan to picket outside the factory to raise awareness about pollution.

Synset Examples

They decided to picket outside the factory for better wages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an organized public demonstration expressing opposition or support
