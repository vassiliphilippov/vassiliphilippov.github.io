to set ashore

1. VERB

/ sɛt əˈʃɔɹ / (AmE), / sɛt əˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to bring (someone or something) onto the shore from a ship or boat; to land on shore
bring onto shore from a ship or boat

Examples

The crew set ashore the passengers safely after the storm.
We were set ashore on a small island at dawn.
The explorers set ashore to search for fresh water.

Synset Examples

The ship landed in Pearl Harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to bring or come to shore from a ship or boat
