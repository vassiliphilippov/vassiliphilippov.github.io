to chip off

1. VERB

/ tʃɪp ɑf / (AmE), / tʃɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to break off a small piece from a whole, especially by impact or pressure
break off a small piece

Examples

Be careful not to chip off any paint when you move the chair.
The workman used his chisel to chip off a piece of stone.
A small corner of the plate chipped off when it was dropped.

Synset Examples

Her tooth chipped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break off - to separate a part from a whole by force
