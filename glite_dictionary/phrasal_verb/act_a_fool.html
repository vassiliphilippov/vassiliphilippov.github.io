to act a fool

1. VERB

/ ækt ə ful / (AmE), / ækt ə fuːl / (BrE)
to behave in a foolish or inappropriate way, especially so as to attract attention or amuse others
behave foolishly or inappropriately
informal

Examples

Please don't act a fool during the meeting; we need to make a good impression.
Whenever he drinks too much, he tends to act a fool in front of his friends.
She told her children not to act a fool while visiting their grandparents.

Synset Examples

Don’t act a fool at the party; just be yourself.
He tends to act a fool when he's nervous.
Stop acting a fool and take this seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to act up - to behave badly or in a disruptive way
  • Synonym: to act out - to behave badly, especially by expressing emotions through actions
