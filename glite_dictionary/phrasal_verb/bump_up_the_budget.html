to bump up the budget

1. VERB

/ bʌmp ʌp ðə ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / bʌmp ʌp ðə ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
to increase the amount of money allocated for a particular purpose or project
increase the amount of a budget
informal

Examples

After reviewing the initial estimates, the committee decided to bump up the budget for the event.
Due to unexpected expenses, we had to bump up the budget halfway through the project.
She asked the finance team if they could bump up the budget for marketing this quarter.

Synset Examples

They decided to bump up the budget for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase the budget - to make a budget larger
  • Antonym: reduce the budget - to make a budget smaller
