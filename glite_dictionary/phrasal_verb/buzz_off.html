to buzz off

1. VERB

/ bʌz ɔf / (AmE), / bʌz ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone to go away immediately.
informal command to go away
informal

Examples

I told him to buzz off when he started asking personal questions.
Why don’t you just buzz off and leave me alone?
She angrily shouted, "Buzz off!" at the reporters crowding her doorway.

Synset Examples

Scram!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scram - (slang) go away or leave immediately
  • Synonym: to clear off - (informal, British) go away quickly
  • Antonym: come here - move closer or approach
