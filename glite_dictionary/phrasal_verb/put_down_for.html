to put down for

1. VERB

/ pʊt daʊn fɔr / (AmE), / pʊt daʊn fə / (BrE)
to reserve a place, time, or opportunity for someone in an event, activity, or service
to reserve or book something for someone

Examples

I've put down for the early morning yoga class next week.
They put down for a double room at the hotel months ago.
Are you going to put down for the charity run this year?

Synset Examples

They put down for a team-building retreat this summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign up for - to enroll or register for something
  • Synonym: book - to arrange in advance to have or use something at a particular time
