to pig out

1. VERB

/ pɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / pɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to eat a large amount of food, especially greedily or to excess
eat excessively; overeat
informal

Examples

We sometimes pig out on pizza during movie night.
After the race, she pigged out on burgers and fries without guilt.
They promised not to pig out at the buffet, but couldn't help themselves.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overeat - to eat more food than your body needs
  • Antonym: restrain oneself - to limit one's behavior, especially regarding eating

Etymology

Informal expression, likely derived from the behaviour attributed to pigs and the phrase "make a pig of oneself" (mid-20th century).
