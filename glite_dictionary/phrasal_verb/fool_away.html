to fool away

1. VERB

/ ful əˈweɪ / (AmE), / fuːl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to spend or use (money, time, opportunity, etc.) wastefully or frivolously
spend or use wastefully

Examples

He managed to fool away his entire savings on unnecessary gadgets.
Don't fool away your time when you should be studying for exams.
She regretted having fooled away her inheritance on lavish parties.

Synset Examples

Fritter away one's inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fritter away - to waste or squander (especially time or money) little by little

2. VERB

/ ful əˈweɪ / (AmE), / fuːl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to waste or squander (time, money, or an opportunity) through careless or frivolous behavior
waste or squander

Examples

He fooled away his entire inheritance before he turned thirty.
Don't fool away your chance to study abroad because of hesitation.
She realized too late that she'd fooled away her best years on pointless arguments.

Synset Examples

Don't fool away your time on trivial matters.
He fooled away his money on gambling.
She regretted fooling away her youth on distractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - to use or expend carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose
  • Synonym: to squander - to spend or use (money, time, etc.) wastefully

Etymology

From "fool" (to act foolishly) + "away" (to cause to go to waste).
