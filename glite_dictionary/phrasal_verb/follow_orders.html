to follow orders

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑːloʊ ˈɔrdərz / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ˈɔːdəz / (BrE)
to do what someone in authority tells you to do, especially without questioning it
obey instructions without question

Examples

Soldiers are trained to follow orders without hesitation, even in dangerous situations.
She didn't agree with the company's decision, but she continued to follow orders.
It's important in some jobs to follow orders exactly as given.

Synset Examples

He was just a puppet, following orders without question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obey - to do what you are told or required to do by someone in authority
  • Antonym: to defy - to refuse to obey someone or something
