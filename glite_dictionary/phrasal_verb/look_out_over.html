to look out over

1. VERB

/ lʊk aʊt ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to have a view of something from a particular position, especially when elevated; to face or overlook a scene or area
to have a view over something

Examples

The hotel room looks out over the entire city skyline.
From the balcony, we could look out over the vast green fields.
Her office looks out over the river, offering a relaxing view during the day.

Synset Examples

The house looks out on a tennis court
The apartment overlooks the Hudson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlook - to have a view from above
