to sweep aside

1. VERB

/ swip əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / swiːp əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to dismiss or disregard something, especially an idea, concern, or objection, as unimportant
dismiss or disregard something

Examples

The committee chose to sweep aside all objections and continue with their plan.
He tried to sweep aside the criticism, insisting his decision was correct.
We can't just sweep aside their concerns without a proper discussion.

Synset Examples

She chose to sweep aside the negativity and focus.
Don't sweep aside your responsibilities; face them head-on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
  • Antonym: to consider - to think about carefully
