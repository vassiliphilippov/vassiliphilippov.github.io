to live with

1. VERB

/ lɪv wɪð / (AmE), / lɪv wɪð / (BrE)
to accept or tolerate something unpleasant or difficult, often by making adjustments to one's attitude or circumstances
tolerate or adapt to something unpleasant

Examples

After the accident, he had to live with constant pain every day.
I can't change the past, so I try to live with my mistakes.
She found it difficult to live with the noise from the busy street.

Synset Examples

I shall have to accept these unpleasant working conditions
I swallowed the insult
She has learned to live with her husband's little idiosyncrasies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accept - to consent to receive or endure; to tolerate
  • Synonym: to endure - to suffer something painful or difficult patiently
  • Synonym: to put up with - to tolerate or accept an unpleasant situation or person

2. VERB

/ lɪv wɪð / (AmE), / lɪv wɪð / (BrE)
to accept or tolerate something difficult or undesirable, often because it cannot be changed
accept or tolerate something difficult

Examples

After the accident, he had to live with constant pain in his leg.
We can't change the rules, so we'll just have to live with them.
She found a way to live with the disappointment and move on.

Synset Examples

I can live with that decision for now.
She learned to live with her mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put up with - to tolerate or endure something annoying or unpleasant

Synset POS: None
