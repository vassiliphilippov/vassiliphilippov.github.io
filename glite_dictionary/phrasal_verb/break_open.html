to break open

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / breɪk ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to open something by using force, especially something that is shut, locked, or sealed
to open by force

Examples

Someone had to break open the locked door to rescue the puppy.
The police decided to break open the safe during the investigation.
He tried to break open the stubborn jar lid with a knife.

Synset Examples

He broke open the picnic basket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force open - to open something by applying force
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01349112-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ breɪk ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / breɪk ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
(of an emotion, conflict, or situation) to erupt or intensify suddenly and forcefully.
erupt or intensify suddenly

Examples

Tensions in the city broke open after the controversial decision was announced.
A long-standing family feud broke open at the holiday gathering, surprising everyone.
Violence broke open between the rival groups, despite attempts at negotiation.

Synset Examples

Unrest erupted in the country
Tempers flared at the meeting
The crowd irrupted into a burst of patriotism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to break out or burst forth suddenly
  • Synonym: to flare up - to intensify or suddenly become more severe

/ breɪk ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / breɪk ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to come open suddenly and violently, as if as a result of internal pressure
come open suddenly and violently

Examples

The pipe did not just leak; it broke open and water gushed everywhere.
During the experiment, the container suddenly broke open and released gas into the room.
As the pressure increased, the bottle broke open, startling everyone in the lab.

Synset Examples

The bubble burst

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst - to break open or apart suddenly and violently

to open suddenly or with force

Examples
