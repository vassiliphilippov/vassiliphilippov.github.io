to feel down

1. VERB

/ fil daʊn / (AmE), / fiːl daʊn / (BrE)
to feel unhappy, sad, or depressed
to feel sad or depressed

Examples

I've been feeling down since the weather turned gloomy.
She tends to feel down after long, stressful weeks at work.
Whenever you feel down, remember that things will get better.

Synset Examples

He was feeling down about his recent job loss.
She felt down after hearing the disappointing news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depressed - feeling very sad and without hope
  • Antonym: cheerful - feeling or showing happiness
