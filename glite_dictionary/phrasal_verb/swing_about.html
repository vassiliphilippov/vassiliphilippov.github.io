to swing about

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / swɪŋ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to turn or move abruptly to face in another direction, either physically or in attitude
turn abruptly to face another way

Examples

The captain ordered the ship to swing about and return to port immediately.
He swung about in surprise when he heard his name called from behind.
During the meeting, her opinion swung about after hearing the new evidence.

Synset Examples

He turned around to face his opponent
My conscience told me to turn around before I made a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn around - to move so as to face in the opposite direction; to reverse attitude
