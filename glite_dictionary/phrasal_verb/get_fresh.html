to get fresh

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt frɛʃ / (AmE), / ɡɛt frɛʃ / (BrE)
to act in an overly familiar, forward, or disrespectful manner, often in a flirtatious or presumptuous way
behave in an overly familiar or disrespectful manner
informal

Examples

If you get fresh with the teacher, you'll be sent out of the classroom.
Don't get fresh with me just because you finished your work early.
He started to get fresh at the party, so I left.

Synset Examples

She told him not to get fresh or he'd regret it.
He tends to get fresh when he's in a good mood.
