to flatten out

1. VERB

/ ˈflætn̩ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈflætn aʊt / (BrE)
to become flat or smoother, or to make something flat or smoother
become (more) flat

Examples

The hills gradually flatten out as you move toward the coast.
After being stored under heavy books, the paper finally flattened out.
Prices have started to flatten out after months of rapid growth.

Synset Examples

The landscape flattened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatten - make or become flat
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00464382-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈflætən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈflætən aʊt / (BrE)
to become level, smooth, or steady after having been uneven or unstable
become level or stable

Examples

Once the initial excitement faded, the team's performance began to flatten out.
As we reached the valley, the steep trail started to flatten out.
If interest rates flatten out, the housing market may stabilize.

Synset Examples

The road begins to flatten out after the hill.
The graph will flatten out if sales continue to decline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to level off - to become level or steady after rising or falling
