to burn away

1. VERB

/ bɝn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / bɜːn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to be consumed by fire or to gradually disappear, especially as a result of heat or light
be consumed by fire or disappear gradually

Examples

The morning mist will burn away once the sun is fully up.
We watched the candle slowly burn away until only a pool of wax remained.
With time, his anger began to burn away, replaced by understanding.

Synset Examples

We watched the leaves burn away in the autumn sun.
The fog will burn away as the sun rises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burn up - to destroy or be destroyed by fire
