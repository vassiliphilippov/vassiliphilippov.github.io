to bluff out

1. VERB

/ blʌf aʊt / (AmE), / blʌf aʊt / (BrE)
to deceive an opponent into withdrawing from competition, especially by pretending to have a stronger position than one actually does
deceive to force withdrawal (esp. in games)

Examples

She managed to bluff out her opponent in the final round of poker.
He tried to bluff out the other bidders, pretending he had a higher offer.
The negotiator hoped to bluff out the competition by exaggerating her company's resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluff - to mislead or deceive, especially by a show of confidence
