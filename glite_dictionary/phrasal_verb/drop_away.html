to drop away

1. VERB

/ drɑp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / drɒp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to decrease or fall to a lower level, standard, or value, often gradually or steadily
fall to a lower standard or level

Examples

Attendance at the lectures began to drop away after the midterm exam.
Support for the new policy will drop away if the results are disappointing.
My energy tends to drop away in the late afternoon.

Synset Examples

My grades are slipping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - to become smaller, fewer, or less; decrease
  • Antonym: to improve - to become or make better
