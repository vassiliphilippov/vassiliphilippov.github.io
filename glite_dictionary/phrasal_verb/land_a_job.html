to land a job

1. VERB

/ lænd ə dʒɑb / (AmE), / lænd ə dʒɒb / (BrE)
to obtain a job, especially one that is desirable or difficult to get
successfully obtain a job

Examples

After months of searching, he finally managed to land a job at a prestigious law firm.
She was thrilled to land a job straight out of college.
Many people struggle to land a job in today's competitive market.

Synset Examples

She managed to land her dream job after several interviews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get a job - to find employment, usually any job
  • Antonym: lose a job - to no longer have employment, usually as a result of being fired or laid off

Etymology

From the verb "land" meaning to secure or achieve something, figuratively extended from its original sense of coming to shore.
