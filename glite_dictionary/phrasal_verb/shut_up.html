to shut up

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to confine someone or something in a place so that escape or removal is not possible
confine someone or something securely

Examples

They shut up the valuables in the safe before leaving for vacation.
The rebels were shut up in the old fortress for weeks.
During the quarantine, the animals were shut up in their stalls to prevent disease spread.

Synset Examples

The parents locked her daughter up for the weekend
She locked her jewels in the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lock up - to confine someone or something in a secure place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01350360-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to command someone to stop talking or to make someone become quiet, often abruptly or rudely
command to stop speaking

Examples

She told her noisy brother to shut up during the movie.
The teacher asked the class to shut up so the lesson could begin.
He couldn't help but shut up when his boss walked into the room.

Synset Examples

Please silence the children in the church!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - to make someone or something quiet
  • Antonym: to speak - to say words; to talk

Etymology

From the verb 'shut' (to close) and 'up', expressing the act of metaphorically closing one's mouth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00462448-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to refuse to talk or to stop talking; to become silent
stop talking; become silent

Examples

As soon as the teacher entered the room, the students shut up and paid attention.
He angrily told his friend to shut up during the movie.
When they heard footsteps in the hallway, they quickly shut up and listened.

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be quiet - to make no sound or stop talking
  • Antonym: to speak - to talk; to use one's voice to say something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01042933-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to stop talking or making noise, especially as a command to be quiet
command to stop talking
informal

Examples

Could you please shut up for a moment so I can concentrate?
She told her little brother to shut up during the movie.
Even after being asked to shut up, he continued to complain.

Synset Examples

Sometimes it’s better to just shut up and listen.
He told her to shut up when she started complaining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be quiet - to stop making noise or talking
