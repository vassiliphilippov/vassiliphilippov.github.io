to suck down

1. VERB

/ sʌk daʊn / (AmE), / sʌk daʊn / (BrE)
To eat or drink something quickly or greedily, often with enthusiasm or without pause.
eat or drink quickly and greedily
informal

Examples

He managed to suck down his coffee in less than a minute before the meeting.
After the marathon, she was eager to suck down a bottle of water.
They would suck down burgers and fries while watching the game.

Synset Examples

She can easily suck down a large pizza by herself.
They love to suck down cold beers on a hot day.
He decided to suck down a smoothie before the gym.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wolf down - to eat food very quickly
  • Synonym: to gulp down - to swallow food or drink quickly
