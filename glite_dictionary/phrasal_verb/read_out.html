to read out

1. VERB

/ rid aʊt / (AmE), / riːd aʊt / (BrE)
to say or announce something aloud so that people can hear it, especially from a written source
announce or recite aloud

Examples

The teacher will read out the names of the students who passed the exam.
She was asked to read out the instructions to the class before starting the activity.
Please read out your answers one by one so everyone can hear.

Synset Examples

The teacher will read out the results shortly.
He was asked to read out the names of the winners.
Please read out your answers during the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to announce - to make something known publicly
