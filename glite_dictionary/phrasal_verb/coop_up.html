to coop up

1. VERB

/ kup ʌp / (AmE), / kuːp ʌp / (BrE)
to confine (someone or something) in a small or restricted space, as if in a cage
confine in a small space

Examples

After the surgery, he was cooped up at home for several weeks.
She felt restless after being cooped up during the snowstorm.
Don't let the dog be cooped up in the garage all day.

Synset Examples

she coops herself up in the library all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep or restrict within certain limits

Etymology

From 'coop', meaning a small enclosure for animals, especially poultry, from Middle English, from Old English *cype* (basket).
