to box out

1. VERB

/ bɑks aʊt / (AmE), / bɒks aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent an opponent from gaining an advantageous position, especially by using one's body to block them, such as when competing for a rebound in sports; also, to prevent competitors or distractions from gaining an advantage
block an opponent or competitor from advantage

Examples

Rebounding is easier if you box out your opponent before jumping for the ball.
In the boardroom, she managed to box out her rivals to secure the contract.
Players are taught to box out as soon as a shot goes up.

Synset Examples

They managed to box out the competition effectively.
We need to box out distractions to focus better.
He tried to box out his opponents during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct or impede an opponent's action, especially in sports
