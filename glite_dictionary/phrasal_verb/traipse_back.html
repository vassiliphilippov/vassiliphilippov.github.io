to traipse back

1. VERB

/ ˈtreɪps bæk / (AmE), / ˈtreɪps bak / (BrE)
to walk or return somewhere in a light, carefree, or sometimes tired manner, often implying a lack of rush or purpose
walk back in a light or leisurely way

Examples

After the movie ended, we traipsed back to our hotel under the starry sky.
The children traipsed back to the classroom after recess, dragging their feet.
She traipsed back into the kitchen to fetch the forgotten loaf of bread.

Synset Examples

They traipsed back home after a long day at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to traipse - walk aimlessly or without obvious purpose, often in a carefree or leisurely way
