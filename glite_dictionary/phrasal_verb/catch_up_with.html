to catch up with

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / kætʃ ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to reach someone or something ahead of you, and sometimes to overtake them
reach or overtake someone ahead

Examples

As I slowed down, the group behind me finally caught up with me.
He ran faster, trying to catch up with the ice cream truck before it left.
If you don't hurry, you'll never catch up with the rest of the team.

Synset Examples

The Rolls Royce caught us near the exit ramp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overtake - to catch up with and pass something or someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01157556-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kætʃ ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / kætʃ ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to do work or tasks that one has missed, especially after an absence, in order to reach the same level as others
make up missed work or progress

Examples

After missing a week of classes, I had to catch up with all my assignments.
She stayed late to catch up with the work she missed while she was sick.
It's important to catch up with the latest updates before the meeting.

Synset Examples

I have to make up a French exam
Can I catch up with the material or is it too late?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make up - to do or complete something that was missed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02526396-v
Synset POS: v
