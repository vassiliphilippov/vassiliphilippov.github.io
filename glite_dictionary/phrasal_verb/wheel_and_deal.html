to wheel and deal

1. VERB

/ wiːl ənd diːl / (AmE), / wiːl ənd diːl / (BrE)
to negotiate, make deals, or trade energetically and often shrewdly to achieve personal advantage, especially in business or politics
to negotiate deals energetically
informal

Examples

She loves to wheel and deal when searching for the best travel deals.
Politicians often wheel and deal behind closed doors to gain support for their proposals.
You have to wheel and deal if you want to succeed in the stock market.

Synset Examples

She’s known to wheel and deal her way through negotiations.
In business, you need to wheel and deal to get the best contracts.
He loves to wheel and deal at the flea market for rare finds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - to negotiate over the terms of a purchase or agreement
  • Synonym: haggle - to argue persistently over the cost of something

Etymology

Likely from the image of manipulating a wheel (control) and dealing cards (trade), popularized in 20th-century American English.
