to pick at scabs

1. VERB

/ pɪk ət skæbz / (AmE), / pɪk ət skæbz / (BrE)
to touch or disturb scabs repeatedly, preventing healing; sometimes used figuratively to mean reopening old emotional wounds or issues.
repeatedly disturb healing scabs (literal/figurative)

Examples

You should try not to pick at scabs so your injuries can heal properly.
She keeps picking at scabs, both literally and when talking about past mistakes.
It's best not to pick at scabs—let things heal on their own.

Synset Examples

She will pick at her scabs instead of letting them heal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reopen old wounds - to bring up past emotional pain
