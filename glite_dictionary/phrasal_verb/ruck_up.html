to ruck up

1. VERB

/ rʌk ʌp / (AmE), / rʌk ʌp / (BrE)
(of fabric, skin, etc.) to become wrinkled, folded, or drawn together in ridges or creases.
become wrinkled or creased

Examples

The fabric tends to ruck up at the seams after several washes.
My socks always ruck up inside my boots when I walk long distances.
If the sheet rucks up on the bed, you won't sleep comfortably.

Synset Examples

her lips puckered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pucker - to become gathered into wrinkles or small folds

Etymology

From 'ruck', a noun meaning 'a wrinkle or fold', of uncertain origin, likely from Scandinavian.

2. VERB

/ rʌk ʌp / (AmE), / rʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare for action or to gather one's belongings, especially quickly or in order to move on
gather belongings or get ready for action

Examples

It's almost time to ruck up and head out for the expedition.
She told everyone to ruck up their stuff before the campfire ended.
The soldiers quickly rucked up and moved to the next checkpoint.

Synset Examples

It's time to ruck up and get this job done.
They had to ruck up their gear before the hike.
