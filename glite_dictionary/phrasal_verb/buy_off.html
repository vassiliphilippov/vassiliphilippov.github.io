to buy off

1. VERB

/ baɪ ˈɔf / (AmE), / baɪ ˈɒf / (BrE)
to give someone money or a reward so that they will do something for you, especially something dishonest, or so that they will not take action against you
bribe to prevent action

Examples

The corrupt official tried to buy off the inspectors with expensive gifts.
They attempted to buy off witnesses in order to avoid prosecution.
No amount of money could buy off the journalist from publishing the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - to give someone money or a gift to persuade them to do something dishonest
  • Synonym: to buy out - to purchase the whole of someone's share, especially to gain control or prevent competition
