to sort oneself out

1. VERB

/ sɔrt wʌnˈsɛlf aʊt / (AmE), / sɔːt wʌnˈsɛlf aʊt / (BrE)
to organize or resolve one's personal problems, thoughts, or life in general
organize or resolve personal issues

Examples

After the move, he needed a few weeks to sort himself out before starting his new job.
Sometimes taking a walk helps me sort myself out when I'm stressed or confused.
She went on a retreat to sort herself out after a difficult year.

Synset Examples

It's time for her to sort herself out and focus.
I had to sort myself out before the big meeting.
He really needs to sort himself out after the breakup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull oneself together - to regain control of one's emotions or behavior
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to lose one's emotional or mental composure
