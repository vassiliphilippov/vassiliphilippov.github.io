to double down

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
to strengthen one's commitment to a course of action or decision, especially despite risk or opposition
commit more strongly; reinforce commitment

Examples

When criticized for her views, she chose to double down rather than apologize.
After the first attempt failed, the company decided to double down on their new approach.
Instead of retreating, he doubled down and worked even harder to achieve his goals.

Synset Examples

She chose to double down on her investment despite the risks.
If you believe in it, you should double down.
The team decided to double down on their marketing strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recommit - to commit again to something
  • Antonym: to back down - to withdraw from a commitment or position

Etymology

Originally from the card game blackjack, meaning to double one's bet and receive only one more card.
