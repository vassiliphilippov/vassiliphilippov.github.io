to cobble together

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːbəl təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ˈkɒbəl təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to make or assemble something quickly and roughly, often from available parts or resources
to assemble hastily or roughly

Examples

We managed to cobble together a meal from the leftovers in the fridge.
The report was cobbled together at the last minute and contained several errors.
They cobbled together a temporary solution until a permanent fix could be found.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation
  • Synonym: to assemble - to fit together the parts of something

Etymology

From the verb 'cobble' (to repair or put together), here used figuratively with 'together' to emphasize hasty assembly.
