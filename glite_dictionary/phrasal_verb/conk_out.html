to conk out

1. VERB

/ kɑŋk aʊt / (AmE), / kɒŋk aʊt / (BrE)
(of a machine or person) to suddenly stop working or functioning, especially due to exhaustion or mechanical failure.
stop working suddenly (informal)
informal

Examples

My old laptop conked out right before I could save my document.
The generator conked out during the storm, leaving us without power.
After running the marathon, she conked out on the couch from exhaustion.

Synset Examples

The engine finally went
The car died on the road
The bus we travelled in broke down on the way to town
The coffee maker broke
The engine failed on the way to town
her eyesight went after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break down - cease to function due to failure
  • Synonym: die - (of a machine) to stop working; to break down
  • Antonym: work - to function properly

2. VERB

/ kɑŋk aʊt / (AmE), / kɒŋk aʊt / (BrE)
(of a person) to use up all one's strength and energy and stop working or functioning, typically due to exhaustion.
stop working from exhaustion
informal

Examples

I was so tired after the hike that I conked out on the couch as soon as I got home.
The old engine finally conked out after years of service, leaving us stranded on the road.
She usually stays up late, but last night she conked out before nine o'clock.

Synset Examples

At the end of the march, I pooped out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, typically from exhaustion
  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness temporarily due to lack of strength

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century American slang, possibly imitative or from 'conk' meaning 'head' (as in collapse).

3. VERB

/ kɑŋk aʊt / (AmE), / kɒŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to suddenly fall asleep or lose consciousness, especially from tiredness or exhaustion
suddenly fall asleep or lose consciousness
informal

Examples

I was so tired after work that I conked out on the couch within minutes.
During the boring lecture, he nearly conked out at his desk.
She conked out before she could finish reading the first chapter.

Synset Examples

She felt like she could conk any moment from exhaustion.
He decided to conk out early after the long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pass out - to faint or lose consciousness unexpectedly
  • Antonym: to wake up - to stop sleeping or become conscious again
