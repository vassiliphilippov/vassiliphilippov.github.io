to keep up

1. VERB

/ kip ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp / (BrE)
to maintain something in good condition or protect it from harm, decay, or loss
maintain or preserve in good condition

Examples

The landlord struggled to keep up the old house during the winter.
It's important to keep up historic monuments for future generations.
Without regular repairs, it became impossible to keep up the building.

Synset Examples

We preserve these archeological findings
The old lady could not keep up the building
children must be taught to conserve our national heritage
The museum curator conserved the ancient manuscripts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition
  • Synonym: conserve - to protect something from harm or destruction; to save
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or maintain something

3. VERB

/ kip ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp / (BrE)
to continue at the same rate, level, or standard as others or as required
maintain the required pace or level

Examples

It's hard to keep up with all the changes in technology nowadays.
She walked so quickly that I struggled to keep up.
If you want to do well in this class, you must keep up with the assignments.

Synset Examples

He could not keep up and dropped out of the race

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall behind - to not be able to maintain the same pace or level as others
  • Antonym: to catch up - to reach the same level, standard, or place as someone else

/ kip ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp / (BrE)

Related Terms
