Reason

Although Thiry refers to a specific historical figure (likely Jean Meslier, sometimes referred to as Meslier-Thiry), the synset is a proper noun representing a person. Names of individual people, even notable philosophers, are not included as headwords in standard English dictionaries unless they have become common nouns or adjectives (e.g., 'Newtonian', 'Shakespearean'), which is not the case here.
