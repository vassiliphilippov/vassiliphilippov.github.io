(of a clause) capable of standing syntactically alone as a complete sentence

Synset Examples

the main (or independent) clause in a complex sentence has at least a subject and a verb

Reason

This 'main' refers specifically to 'main clause' in grammar. The standalone adjective 'main' meaning 'of a clause capable of standing alone' is not used outside the set phrase 'main clause.' The headword in this sense is not specific enough for inclusion; instead, 'main clause' would be the proper entry. This concept is too contextually bound and not generally used as an adjective with that precise grammatical sense independently.
