Synset Examples

the in party

Reason

The concept 'in' as 'holding office' is too general and context-dependent to warrant a separate entry under this sense in a standard English-English dictionary. The example 'the in party' references a political usage that is not commonly found as a lexicalized sense of the word; 'in' meaning 'currently in power' is covered under the adjective and preposition senses and does not constitute a distinct dictionary headword. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
