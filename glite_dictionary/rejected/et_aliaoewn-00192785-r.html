et alia

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00192785-r
Synset POS: r
Internal ID: et alia|oewn-00192785-r
and others (‘et al.’ is used as an abbreviation of ‘et alii’ (masculine plural) or ‘et aliae’ (feminine plural) or ‘et alia’ (neuter plural) when referring to a number of people)

Synset Examples

the data reported by Smith et al.

Reason

Although 'et alia' is a Latin phrase used in the abbreviation 'et al.' in academic contexts, the full form 'et alia' is extremely rarely used in modern English (the abbreviation 'et al.' is standard). There is little evidence that 'et alia' (neuter plural) itself is current in English usage, and its direct definition as such would not appear as a primary entry in major English dictionaries—only the abbreviation 'et al.' has wide currency as a dictionary entry. Thus, it does not warrant a separate entry.
