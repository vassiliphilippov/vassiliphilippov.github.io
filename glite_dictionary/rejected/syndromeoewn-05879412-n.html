Reason

This sense of 'syndrome' as 'a complex of concurrent things' is highly general and not used in modern standard English. The typical meaning of 'syndrome' relates to a group of medical or psychological symptoms. The general sense here would not appear in major dictionaries like Oxford, as it does not correspond to common usage.
