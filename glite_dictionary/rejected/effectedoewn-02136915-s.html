Synset Examples

Reason

The synset 'settled securely and unconditionally' under 'effected' is too broad and does not correspond specifically to the headword 'effected' as an adjective. In standard dictionaries, 'effected' is primarily recognized as the past participle of 'effect' (verb), not as an adjective meaning settled or accomplished. Additionally, the example sentence uses 'accomplished,' not 'effected,' and this adjective sense does not naturally occur in general English usage for 'effected.' Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
