reckon

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00714537-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: reckon|oewn-00714537-v
have faith or confidence in

Synset Examples

you can count on me to help you any time
Look to your friends for support
You can bet on that!
Depend on your family in times of crisis

Reason

This sense of "reckon" as 'have faith or confidence in' is not commonly used in modern or standard English and is largely obsolete or dialectal. In contemporary English, 'reckon' more commonly means 'to think' or 'to calculate.' The examples given actually use other verbs (count on, depend on, look to, bet on), not 'reckon' itself, and this meaning would not be expected in a standard dictionary like Oxford in this sense.
