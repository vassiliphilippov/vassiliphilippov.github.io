resort

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01847439-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: resort|oewn-01847439-v
move, travel, or proceed toward some place

Synset Examples

He repaired to his cabin in the woods

Reason

This verb sense of 'resort' (meaning 'to move, travel, or proceed toward some place') is archaic and extremely rare in contemporary English. Modern usage of 'resort' as a verb primarily refers to 'turning to or adopting a course of action' (e.g., 'resort to violence'), not physical movement toward a place. The example given uses 'repair' in its archaic sense, not 'resort.' Therefore, this concept should be excluded as it does not reflect a current or independently meaningful usage that would appear in a modern dictionary.
