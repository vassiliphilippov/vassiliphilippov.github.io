capable of melting

Reason

The concept 'disintegrable' as 'capable of melting' is extremely rare, nonstandard, and not attested in standard dictionaries like Oxford. The more common sense of 'disintegrable' is 'capable of being disintegrated' (broken into parts), not 'capable of melting.' The 'capable of melting' sense is obscure and not widely recognized or used, so it should not be included in a general-purpose English dictionary.
