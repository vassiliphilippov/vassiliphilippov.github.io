inexplicit

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00944754-a
Synset POS: a
Internal ID: inexplicit|oewn-00944754-a
implied though not directly expressed; inherent in the nature of something

Synset Examples

an implicit agreement not to raise the subject
there was implicit criticism in his voice
anger was implicit in the argument
the oak is implicit in the acorn

Reason

The concept 'inexplicit' is simply an uncommon synonym for 'implicit'. Its primary sense is already captured under the standard term 'implicit', which is the widely used and dictionary-standard headword for this sense. 'Inexplicit' is extremely rare in modern usage, barely occurs in real English, and is not included in Oxford Dictionary or mainstream reference works as an independent entry. Learners are better served by the entry for 'implicit'. Therefore, it should not be included as a main headword.
