deuce-ace

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13766184-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: deuce-ace|oewn-13766184-n
the cardinal number that is the sum of one and one and one

Reason

The sense for 'deuce-ace' as 'the cardinal number that is the sum of one and one and one' refers to the number three. This use is extremely rare and archaic, and the term is almost never used outside of very specific, obsolete contexts (such as old dice games). It is not a standard number word nor in contemporary use, so it should not be included as a headword in a modern English dictionary like Oxford.
