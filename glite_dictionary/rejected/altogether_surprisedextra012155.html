Synset Examples

Reason

The concept 'altogether surprised' is a compositional phrase (adverb + adjective) whose meaning is directly predictable from its parts ('altogether' meaning 'completely' and 'surprised'). Such transparent adverb-adjective combinations are generally not included as headwords in major dictionaries unless they have an idiomatic meaning or are frequently used as fixed collocations, which is not the case here.
