Reason

This sense of 'even' as an adverb meaning 'to the full extent' is highly restricted, literary, and almost archaic (e.g., 'even unto death'). In modern English dictionaries such as Oxford, this sense is not generally listed separately from the common adverb usage of 'even' (indicating emphasis, contrast, or surprise). Including this meaning as a separate entry would be unnecessarily granular and potentially confusing for learners, as it is not commonly used or recognized. Therefore, it should be excluded from the dictionary.
