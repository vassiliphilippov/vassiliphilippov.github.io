bring up from infancy

Reason

The verb sense of 'cradle' meaning 'bring up from infancy' (to rear or nurture) is rare, archaic, and not commonly used in modern English except in highly literary or poetic contexts. This sense does not meet the inclusion criteria for contemporary English dictionaries such as the Oxford Dictionary, and could be confusing for learners. The far more common verb sense is 'to hold or support as in a cradle', which is already covered.
