Reason

The concept refers to 'Anderson' as a specific person (Carl David Anderson), but the synset ('physicist', 'person') is too general. Dictionary entries only include proper nouns for people if the specific individual is of the type commonly included in English dictionaries (for example, historical figures of vast cultural significance like 'Shakespeare'). 'Anderson' alone is not specific or distinctive enough for inclusion as a dictionary headword; the entry would more properly be 'Carl Anderson' or 'Carl David Anderson'. Thus, this concept should be filtered out.
