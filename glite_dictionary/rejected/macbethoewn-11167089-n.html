king of Scotland (died in 1057)

Reason

This sense refers to 'Macbeth' solely as 'king of Scotland (died in 1057),' which is too generic and simply identifies the individual as a person. In dictionaries like Oxford, entries for historical individuals focus on notable persons with descriptions, not generic sense synsets such as 'person' or 'king.' Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
