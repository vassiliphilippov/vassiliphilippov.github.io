head-to-head

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00553636-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: head-to-head|oewn-00553636-s
inconclusive as to outcome; close or just even in a race or comparison or competition

Synset Examples

as they approached the finish line they were neck and neck
the election was a nip and tuck affair

Reason

The concept 'head-to-head' with the sense 'inconclusive as to outcome; close or just even in a race or competition' is not the primary or standard usage of 'head-to-head.' In English dictionaries like Oxford, 'head-to-head' is included primarily as an adjective or adverb meaning 'involving direct competition between two people or teams.' The sense described here (as an adjective meaning 'neck and neck', 'nip and tuck') is more idiomatically expressed by other phrases such as 'neck and neck' or 'nip and tuck.' Therefore, this sense should be excluded as it is not a standard or commonly accepted usage of 'head-to-head.'
