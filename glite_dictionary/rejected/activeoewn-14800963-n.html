Reason

The noun sense 'active' meaning 'chemical agent capable of activity' is rare and highly technical; it is not common enough in general or even most specialized English usage to appear as a dictionary headword. In chemistry, such substances are typically called 'active agents' or 'actives' (as plural noun), but 'active' as a countable noun for a chemical agent is not standard in major English dictionaries. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
