colorful

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00395623-a
Synset POS: a
Internal ID: colorful|oewn-00395623-a
having color or a certain color; sometimes used in combination

Synset Examples

colored crepe paper
amber-colored heads of grain

Reason

This synset refers to the general meaning 'having color or a certain color', sometimes as a combining form (e.g. amber-colored, red-colored). In modern dictionaries, these general adjectival formations are not typically given their own entries, since 'colored' as a participle or combining form is covered under 'color' and its derived forms. The actual adjective 'colorful' has a specific meaning (full of color, vivid, interesting), but this concept is for 'colored' as descriptive; 'colored' used to refer to 'having color' is already listed in dictionaries as a participial adjective. Thus, this sense is too grammatically general to warrant a separate entry.
