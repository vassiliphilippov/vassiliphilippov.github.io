how's your father

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: how's your father|extra.005151
a phrase used to inquire about someone's well-being

Synset Examples

During the call, I remembered to ask, how's your father?
After a long time, I asked her, how's your father doing?

Reason

The phrase 'how's your father' in this sense functions as a literal, compositional question using standard English, rather than an established idiom or fixed expression. It's not a set phrase with a distinct meaning apart from its parts, and thus wouldn't be included as a dictionary entry in Oxford or similar dictionaries unless referencing the unrelated British euphemistic slang meaning. Therefore, it is too general and does not warrant its own dictionary entry.
