deflower

Synset POS: v
Internal ID: deflower|oewn-00478872-v
make imperfect

Synset Examples

nothing marred her beauty

Reason

The verb 'deflower' in the sense of 'make imperfect' is extremely rare and archaic. Modern dictionaries primarily record 'deflower' as removing someone's virginity or, in a much less common and literal sense, removing the flowers from (a plant). The broad generic sense of 'make imperfect' is too general and not associated with the main current usage of 'deflower' and does not warrant a dictionary entry.
