hooks

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05572825-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: hooks|oewn-05572825-n
large strong hand (as of a fighter)

Synset Examples

wait till I get my hooks on him

Reason

The synset refers to a very specific, figurative, and archaic sense of 'hooks' meaning 'large strong hands,' most notably in the phrase 'get my hooks on.' This is not a standard, independently recognized meaning or headword in modern English and would not warrant a separate entry in a dictionary like Oxford. If relevant, this figurative use could be briefly mentioned at the entry for 'hook' or in an entry for an idiom such as 'get one's hooks on.'
