Reason

This sense of 'plunger' (meaning 'someone who dives into water') is archaic and extremely rare in modern English. The primary, commonly used sense of 'plunger' is a tool for clearing drains, which would be included, but not this obsolete sense. This sense would not normally appear in a contemporary English dictionary.
