mayidism

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14224355-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: mayidism|oewn-14224355-n
a disease caused by deficiency of niacin or tryptophan (or by a defect in the metabolic conversion of tryptophan to niacin); characterized by gastrointestinal disturbances and erythema and nervous or mental disorders; may be caused by malnutrition or alcoholism or other nutritional impairments

Reason

The concept 'mayidism' does not correspond with established medical or general English usage. The standard and widely recognized term for this disease is 'pellagra,' and 'mayidism' does not appear as a headword in reputable English dictionaries (e.g. Oxford, Merriam-Webster). There is no substantial evidence for 'mayidism' being used or recognized in English.
