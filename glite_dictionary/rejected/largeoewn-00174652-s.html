Reason

This concept is an archaic, context-specific sense of 'large' meaning 'in an advanced stage of pregnancy,' but in contemporary English, 'large' is not used in this sense independently; the expressions 'big with child' or 'great with child' are idiomatic and archaic. The sense is better covered as an idiom or under 'with child.' The use of 'large' alone to mean 'pregnant' would be misleading or unclear to modern learners. Therefore, it should not be included as an independent dictionary entry for 'large.'
