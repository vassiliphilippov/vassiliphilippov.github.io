set

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02502978-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: set|oewn-02502978-s
being below the horizon

Synset Examples

the moon is set

Reason

This concept refers to a participial adjective ('set') meaning 'below the horizon' as applied to celestial bodies. However, this usage functions almost entirely as a predicative complement (e.g. 'the moon is set') and is not independently lexicalized in English dictionaries except as a past participle of the verb 'set.' Comprehensive dictionaries do not list 'set' as an adjective with this meaning, as it is instead derived straightforwardly from the verb 'set' (as in 'the sun has set'). Therefore, it is too narrow and redundant for a separate dictionary entry.
