entree

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03295682-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: entree|oewn-03295682-n
something that provides access (to get in or get out)

Synset Examples

they waited at the entrance to the garden
beggars waited just outside the entryway to the cathedral

Reason

The synset here ('entree' as 'something that provides access, to get in or get out') is too general and not meaningfully distinct from broader terms like 'entrance' or 'entryway.' In standard usage, 'entree' in English refers to a main course in a meal or, more rarely, the right to enter. The general 'means of access' sense with this wording would not be included as a separate dictionary entry.
