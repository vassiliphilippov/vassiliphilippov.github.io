nothingness

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07151419-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: nothingness|oewn-07151419-n
empty rhetoric or insincere or exaggerated talk

Synset Examples

that's a lot of wind
don't give me any of that jazz

Reason

This sense refers to 'nothingness' as empty talk or meaningless rhetoric, but this is not a standard or widely recognized meaning of the noun 'nothingness' in contemporary English. 'Nothingness' is primarily used to refer to the state of being nothing or nonexistence, not empty rhetoric or insincere talk. This sense seems too colloquial/idiomatic and not specific to the headword; it is better captured by words like 'wind', 'hot air', or 'jazz' in this sense. Thus, this concept should not be included.
