Reason

The sense 'change the order or arrangement of' for 'commute' is extremely rare and not found in standard dictionaries such as Oxford or Merriam-Webster for this verb. The usual dictionary meanings focus on travel or reducing a judicial sentence. This mathematical or technical sense (to change order, as in 'commute operators') is usually labeled as 'commute' in mathematics, not as a general verb for 'change the order or arrangement of.' The example given ('Dyslexics often transpose letters') better fits the verb 'transpose.' This sense would not appear as a main entry in standard dictionaries and is not distinct for general English learners.
