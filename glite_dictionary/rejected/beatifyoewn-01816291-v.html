fill with sublime emotion

Synset Examples

The children were thrilled at the prospect of going to the movies
He was inebriated by his phenomenal success

Reason

This verb sense ('fill with sublime emotion') is not a current or widely accepted use of 'beatify' in contemporary English. Standard dictionaries define 'beatify' primarily in the religious sense ('to declare someone blessed') or rarely as 'to make supremely happy'. The meaning 'fill with sublime emotion' is obscure, extremely rare, and not established enough for inclusion in a general English dictionary like Oxford. Therefore, it should not be included.
