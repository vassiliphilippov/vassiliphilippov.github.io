Reason

The concept provided is 'subsequence' with the sense 'following in time.' However, in general English usage and reputable dictionaries, 'subsequence' is not used to mean 'the act of following in time'; it is a mathematical or technical term referring to a sequence derived from another sequence. The general meaning 'following in time' is already covered by words like 'subsequent' or 'succession.' Therefore, this sense of the concept is too general, not strongly linked to the headword, and should not be included.
