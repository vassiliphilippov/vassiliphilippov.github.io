English theologian whose objections to Roman Catholic doctrine anticipated the Protestant Reformation (1328-1384)

Reason

The sense of 'Wickliffe' here is as a person (John Wycliffe, also spelled Wickliffe), and the synset is simply 'person', making it too general. Modern English-English dictionaries would not include every individual person, unless they are of extreme prominence, and then it would be as a proper noun under 'Wycliffe' as the accepted spelling. Also, the main spelling variant is 'Wycliffe', and encyclopedic entries are more appropriate for such figures. Thus, this concept should be excluded based on guidelines.
