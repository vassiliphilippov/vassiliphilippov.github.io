in the frame

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: in the frame|extra.018227
included in a visual display or context

Synset Examples

She was delighted to see her photo in the frame.

Reason

The phrase 'in the frame' in this context simply indicates being within the borders of a picture or visual display. This meaning is compositional (the literal meaning of being inside a physical or figurative frame) and does not have a distinct idiomatic or widely recognized non-literal meaning requiring a dictionary entry. Oxford and comparable dictionaries do not typically include such transparent combinations, unless there is an idiomatic or specific technical use, which is not the case here. Therefore, it should not be included as a separate entry.
