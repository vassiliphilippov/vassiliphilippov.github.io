cranial nerve that supplies facial muscles

Reason

The synset for 'facial' in this sense (as a noun referring to the cranial nerve) is too general and technical. In standard English dictionaries like Oxford, 'facial' is typically listed as an adjective (relating to the face) or as a noun meaning a beauty treatment for the face. The use of 'facial' to refer to the cranial nerve (facial nerve) is not standard for general English; the term 'facial nerve' is used instead. Therefore, this concept should not be included as a standalone dictionary entry for 'facial'.
