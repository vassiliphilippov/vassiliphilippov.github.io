knight of the post

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: knight of the post|extra.005807
someone who serves the community honorably, often in a dependable role

Synset Examples

He serves the community well, a true knight of the post.
The knight of the post is known for his reliability.
As a knight of the post, he delivers messages promptly.

Reason

The phrase 'knight of the post' in historical English actually refers to a dishonest person, especially a professional false witness or cheat. The WordNet synset here incorrectly attributes a positive, honorable meaning that is not attested in standard English usage. Furthermore, this meaning is not established or notable in contemporary or historical English sources, and would not appear as such in a reputable dictionary like Oxford.
