Reason

The synset is for an ordinal number ('the ordinal number of one hundred eighty-five in counting order') but the lemma given is '190th', which would be 'the ordinal number of one hundred ninety'. Therefore, the synset definition does not match the lemma, and ordinal numbers above basic, clear figures (such as 'first', 'second', 'hundredth') are typically not included as individual entries in major dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary. Such numbers are better understood as compositional forms rather than as lexical entries requiring a separate definition.
