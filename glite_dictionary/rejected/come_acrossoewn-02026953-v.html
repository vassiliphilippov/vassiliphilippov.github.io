Synset Examples

Reason

The synset describes the general meaning 'come together' (i.e., 'meet'), but the phrase 'come across' is not used in this sense in standard English usage. In English, 'come across' means 'to find by chance' or 'to give a certain impression,' not 'meet' or 'come together.' Therefore, this sense does not warrant a dictionary entry for 'come across' and would not appear in the Oxford Dictionary.
