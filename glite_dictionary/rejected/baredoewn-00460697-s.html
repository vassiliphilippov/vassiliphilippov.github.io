Reason

The synset 'bared' here refers to 'having the head uncovered,' but in this sense, 'bared' is only used in the set phrase 'with bared head.' It is not commonly used on its own with this meaning and is not a standalone dictionary word for 'bareheaded.' The more standard form is 'bareheaded,' which would be the headword, not 'bared.' This concept is too limited and does not warrant a separate dictionary entry.
