Reason

The synset 'serving for wages in a foreign army' is historically related to the origin of 'free-lance' but is not a recognized or current sense of the modern term in English dictionaries. Contemporary dictionaries treat 'freelance' as relating to self-employed work, not as a direct term for mercenary soldiers. This sense is archaic and too specific to the etymological history rather than to contemporary or even historical English usage. Dictionaries would not list this distinct sense under 'free-lance'.
