The synset for 'uncertainly' as 'in an unsteady manner' is not idiomatic or commonly used in modern English. The standard adverb is 'unsteadily.' While 'uncertainly' is a valid word, it is primarily used to mean 'with uncertainty' (without confidence or sureness), not specifically 'in an unsteady manner.' Therefore, this sense is either too rare, obsolete, or a misuse, and would not merit its own entry in a general-purpose English-English dictionary.
