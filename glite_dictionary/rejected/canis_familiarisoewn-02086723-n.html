Canis familiaris

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02086723-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: Canis familiaris|oewn-02086723-n
a member of the genus Canis (probably descended from the common wolf) that has been domesticated by man since prehistoric times; occurs in many breeds

Synset Examples

the dog barked all night

Reason

The concept 'Canis familiaris' is the scientific (Latin) name for the domestic dog, and while sometimes encountered in technical or academic contexts, standard English dictionaries such as Oxford would include the common noun 'dog' rather than the taxonomic designation itself. This synset corresponds to the biological species classification rather than standard English usage, so it is not appropriate for inclusion as a headword in an English-English dictionary.
