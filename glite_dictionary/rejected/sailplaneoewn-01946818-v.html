Reason

The verb sense 'sailplane' meaning 'to fly a plane without an engine' is extremely rare in modern English and is not standard; the noun 'sailplane' (the glider aircraft itself) is the usual dictionary entry. This verb sense would not appear in major English dictionaries such as Oxford or Merriam-Webster.
