put a seat on a chair

Reason

This sense ('put a seat on a chair') is not in standard use and would not appear in major English dictionaries. It refers to the act of attaching or installing a seat to a chair, which is better described by words like 'upholster' or 'assemble.' 'Seat' as a verb in standard usage means to cause someone to sit or to provide with seats, not to put a physical seat on a chair.
