begin to thrum

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: begin to thrum|extra.012803
started to make a low, continuous sound

Synset Examples

The engine began to thrum as it warmed up.

Reason

The phrase 'begin to thrum' is a transparent combination of the verb 'begin' and the verb 'thrum.' It is not an idiom, fixed expression, or phrasal verb with a special meaning beyond the sum of its parts. Only 'thrum' as a verb (meaning to make a low, continuous sound) merits a dictionary entry. 'Begin to thrum' does not meet criteria for inclusion as a standalone entry.
