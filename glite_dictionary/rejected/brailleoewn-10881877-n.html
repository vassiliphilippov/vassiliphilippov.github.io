Braille

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10881877-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: Braille|oewn-10881877-n
French educator who lost his sight at the age of three and who invented a system of writing and printing for sightless people (1809-1852)

Reason

This synset refers to 'Braille' as a specific person (Louis Braille), but in general English dictionaries, 'Braille' primarily refers to the tactile writing system, not the person. The person's name ('Louis Braille') would generally not have its own dictionary entry, as it functions as a proper noun for a historical figure, not a broadly used term.
