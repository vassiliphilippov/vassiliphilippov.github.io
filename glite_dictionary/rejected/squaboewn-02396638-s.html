Reason

This sense of 'squab' as an adjective meaning 'short and fat' is archaic and extremely rare in modern English; it is not commonly encountered and unlikely to be looked up. The main contemporary English uses of 'squab' are as a noun (a young pigeon or plump chicken, or the flesh thereof). Therefore, this sense does not merit inclusion in a modern learner's dictionary.
