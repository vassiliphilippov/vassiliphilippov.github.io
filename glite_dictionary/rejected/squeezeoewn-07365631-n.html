Reason

This concept refers specifically to a 'twisting squeeze' as in the act of wringing something out, but the primary and common noun sense of 'squeeze' does not mean 'twisting squeeze.' The example given, 'gave the wet cloth a wring,' shows that the synset is about 'wring' rather than a unique noun sense of 'squeeze.' Therefore, this sense of 'squeeze' does not warrant a separate dictionary entry as used in Oxford Dictionary.
