tilt

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07197811-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: tilt|oewn-07197811-n
a contentious speech act; a dispute where there is strong disagreement

Synset Examples

they were involved in a violent argument

Reason

This sense of 'tilt' as a 'contentious speech act' or 'argument' is very rare and archaic. It is not used in contemporary English and would not appear in modern dictionaries like Oxford except possibly as a highly archaic usage. The primary meanings of 'tilt' are mechanical ('to incline') or related to jousting. Therefore, this sense should be excluded from the dictionary.
