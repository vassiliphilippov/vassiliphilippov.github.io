Reason

The synset describes an island but 'Mona' as a headword in this context is simply an alternative ancient name for Anglesey, and is treated as a proper noun referring to a geographical location. Unless 'Mona' is the commonly used modern English name (which it is not; Anglesey is the standard), it does not warrant a separate dictionary entry. Therefore, it's too specific and archaic for a general dictionary and overlaps with the 'place' concept. Excluding as per guidelines.
