tip of the ice cube

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: tip of the ice cube|extra.010647
a small part of a larger issue

Synset Examples

Her complaint was only the tip of the ice cube in the larger issue.
What we've seen is just the tip of the ice cube.
That was just the tip of the ice cube in terms of the problem.

Reason

The phrase 'tip of the ice cube' is not an established idiom or expression in English. The standard and widely recognized form is 'tip of the iceberg,' which refers to a small, visible part of a much larger, hidden problem. 'Tip of the ice cube' appears to be a mistaken or nonstandard variant and would not appear in reputable dictionaries such as Oxford.
