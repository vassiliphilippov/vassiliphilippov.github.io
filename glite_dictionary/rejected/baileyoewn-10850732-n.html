English lexicographer who was the first to treat etymology consistently; his work was used as a reference by Samuel Johnson (died in 1742)

Reason

Although 'Bailey' refers to a notable English lexicographer, this use is as a proper noun designating an individual person. In modern dictionaries such as the Oxford Dictionary, such entries are generally reserved for terms of general usage, not for specific biographical names unless the person is extremely famous and the name is widely used as a common noun or adjective (e.g., 'Darwinian'). Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
