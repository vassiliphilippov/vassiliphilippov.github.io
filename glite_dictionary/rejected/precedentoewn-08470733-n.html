precedent

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08470733-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: precedent|oewn-08470733-n
a system of jurisprudence based on judicial precedents rather than statutory laws

Synset Examples

common law originated in the unwritten laws of England and was later applied in the United States

Reason

This sense refers to 'precedent' as a type or system of law (specifically the doctrine of case law or common law), but the word 'precedent' is almost never used in that way in standard English dictionaries. Instead, 'precedent' typically appears as a noun meaning 'an earlier event or decision used as an example' or 'a previous court decision serving as an authoritative rule.' Since the use of 'precedent' for an entire system of jurisprudence is not standard or dictionary-worthy, this sense should not be included.
