the scar left following inoculation with a vaccine

Reason

This sense ('vaccination' meaning 'the scar left following inoculation with a vaccine') is extremely rare and not current usage in modern English. The primary meaning of 'vaccination' refers to the act of inoculating with a vaccine, not the resulting scar. There is no evidence this sense would be included as a separate entry in Oxford or similar dictionaries.
