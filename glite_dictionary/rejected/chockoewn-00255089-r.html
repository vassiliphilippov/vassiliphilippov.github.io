Synset Examples

Reason

This sense of 'chock' is an adverb meaning 'as completely as possible', but in contemporary English, this usage is almost exclusively found in the fixed informal phrase 'chock-a-block' or 'chock-full.' The standalone adverb 'chock' in this way is rare and archaic, and not used independently in modern English. As a dictionary entry, 'chock' as an adverb meaning 'completely' would not typically appear separately in major modern dictionaries. The concept is too marginal for inclusion.
