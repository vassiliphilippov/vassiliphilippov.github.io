disadvantage by prejudice

Reason

The synset for 'prejudice' as a verb meaning 'to disadvantage by prejudice' is extremely rare and not in common usage. In modern English, 'prejudice' as a verb is almost exclusively used to mean 'to influence with prejudice or bias.' This rare legalistic meaning ('to disadvantage or cause harm by prejudice') is not well attested in general usage and would not normally appear in main English dictionaries like Oxford, especially as a distinct verb sense. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
