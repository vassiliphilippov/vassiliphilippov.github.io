Reason

The sense of 'check out' as 'trace' (e.g., 'running down a few tips') is not an independent or widely recognized dictionary sense of 'check out.' In major dictionaries, 'check out' typically means to examine, verify, look at, or leave a hotel; the specialized sense of 'trace' is rare, idiomatic, and more naturally expressed by verbs such as 'trace' or 'track down.' This usage is too narrow and not standard enough to merit its own dictionary entry.
