soft-boiled

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01161960-a
Synset POS: a
Internal ID: soft-boiled|oewn-01161960-a
easily moved to pity or sorrow

Synset Examples

a softhearted judge

Reason

The sense described ('easily moved to pity or sorrow') is uncommon and not a standard or widely recognized meaning of 'soft-boiled' in modern English. The typical usage of 'soft-boiled' refers to eggs cooked lightly so that the yolk remains runny, which is separate from the emotional sense described here. Since this figurative sense does not appear in authoritative dictionaries and is not attested in current usage, it should be excluded.
