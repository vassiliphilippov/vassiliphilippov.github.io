scuffle

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01921542-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: scuffle|oewn-01921542-v
walk by dragging one's feet

Synset Examples

he shuffled out of the room
We heard his feet shuffling down the hall

Reason

The verb 'scuffle' in standard contemporary English does not commonly mean 'to walk by dragging one's feet'; that sense is rare and usually associated with 'shuffle.' The primary verb sense of 'scuffle' is 'to engage in a short, confused fight.' The usage given here is not standard and would not appear in mainstream dictionaries for this meaning.
