Internal ID: drool|oewn-06623500-n
pretentious or silly talk or writing

Reason

The noun sense of 'drool' as 'pretentious or silly talk or writing' is rare and not standard in contemporary English; it does not appear in major dictionaries or standard usage. The common noun 'drool' refers to saliva, and the verb means 'to let saliva run from the mouth.' This sense is too marginal and would not appear in a standard dictionary like Oxford.
