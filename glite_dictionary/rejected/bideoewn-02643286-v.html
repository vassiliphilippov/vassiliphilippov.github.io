Reason

The sense of "bide" meaning "to dwell" is archaic and extremely rare in modern English, and even in historical contexts is much less common than "stay" or "live." It would not appear as a primary sense in a modern learner's dictionary, and the main current use of "bide" is in the phrase "bide one's time" (to wait patiently). Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
