Reason

The sense 'a mistake in printed matter resulting from mechanical failures of some kind' for 'literal' is not relevant to normal English usage and does not meaningfully relate to the headword. 'Literal' in modern English does not mean 'a printing error'; that is an obsolete sense, sometimes 'literal' was once used for a typographical error, but this is now extremely archaic and almost entirely replaced by 'typo' or 'misprint'. The word 'literal', as commonly understood, should not have this sense in a present-day dictionary.
