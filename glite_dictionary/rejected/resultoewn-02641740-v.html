Reason

The synset 'produce as a result or residue' mapped to 'result' as a verb is not a standard or established sense of 'result' in modern English. The examples instead fit the senses of 'leave', 'cause', or 'produce'. 'Result' as a verb is only commonly used to mean 'to happen because of something', not 'to produce something as a result.' Therefore, this sense should be excluded from the dictionary.
