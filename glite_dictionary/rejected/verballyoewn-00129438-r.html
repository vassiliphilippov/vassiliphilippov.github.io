Synset Examples

Reason

The synset refers to the adverb 'verbally' in the very narrow and technical sense 'as a verb,' which is not distinct or widespread enough to warrant a separate dictionary entry. In standard usage, 'verbally' generally means 'in words' or 'by means of speech,' which is already covered in major dictionaries. The specialized grammatical use ('as a verb' as in 'to noun something verbally') is not common or established as a separate dictionary sense.
