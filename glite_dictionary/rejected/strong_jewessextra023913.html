Reason

The synset 'strong Jewess' is a descriptive phrase combining 'strong' and 'Jewess' (an old-fashioned/dated term for a Jewish woman), referring specifically to a powerful female Jewish character in a narrative. Dictionaries do not typically include such compositional, ad hoc descriptive noun phrases unless they are idiomatic or have special cultural significance. 'Jewess' itself may be included (often marked archaic or sometimes offensive), and 'strong' is an entry, but 'strong Jewess' as a fixed term does not warrant a separate dictionary entry in the style of the Oxford Dictionary.
