Reason

This sense of 'prehensile' meaning 'having a keen intellect' is archaic and extremely rare. The prevalent and dictionary-recognized meaning of 'prehensile' refers to 'adapted for seizing or grasping', especially in reference to an animal's tail, limb, or appendage. The figurative sense described here is almost obsolete and would not appear in standard modern dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary. Therefore, it should not be included.
