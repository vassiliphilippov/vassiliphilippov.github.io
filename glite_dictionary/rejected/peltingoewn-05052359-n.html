pelting

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05052359-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: pelting|oewn-05052359-n
anything happening rapidly or in quick successive

Synset Examples

a rain of bullets
a pelting of insults

Reason

The concept describes a general phenomenon (things happening rapidly or in quick succession) rather than a distinct lexical item; 'pelting' is not commonly used as a noun in contemporary English for this sense, and this use is not standard enough to merit a dictionary entry. The synonymous noun forms ('shower', 'barrage', 'flurry') are preferred in modern usage.
