Reason

The sense of 'mediate' described here as 'being neither at the beginning nor at the end in a series' is extremely rare and largely obsolete. 'Mediate' in modern English is not used as an adjective with this meaning; the standard dictionary entries focus on its use as a verb (to act as an intermediary) or, rarely, as an adjective meaning 'indirect.' This sense is too rare and archaic to merit inclusion in a modern English-English dictionary.
