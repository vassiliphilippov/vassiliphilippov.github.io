cause to feel shame; hurt the pride of

Synset Examples

He humiliated his colleague by criticising him in front of the boss

Reason

This verb sense of 'chagrin' (meaning 'to cause to feel shame; hurt the pride of') is quite rare in modern English usage and would not appear as a common present-day headword verb in standard dictionaries like Oxford. The common use of 'chagrin' is as a noun meaning 'distress or embarrassment at having failed or been humiliated.' The verb sense is archaic and practically unattested in contemporary English.
