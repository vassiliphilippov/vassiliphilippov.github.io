blow someone's brains out

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: blow someone's brains out|extra.001193
to shock, surprise, or confuse greatly

Synset Examples

The plot twist at the end was sure to blow someone's brains out.
The horror movie was so intense, it felt like it would blow someone's brains out.
His wild theories could blow someone's brains out.

Reason

The synset description given here ('to shock, surprise, or confuse greatly') is not the standard, dictionary-recognized meaning of 'blow someone's brains out.' In established dictionaries, this phrase primarily refers to killing someone by shooting them in the head, a graphic and violent sense (sometimes used figuratively to mean extreme mental impact, but rarely and not standard). The sense provided here is not a common or standard enough idiomatic use to merit its own dictionary entry; it's likely a confusion or a nonstandard extension. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
