Reason

The concept 'untraded on' is not a standard dictionary headword or established phrasal verb; it is a compositional use of the prefix 'un-' and the word 'traded,' combined with the preposition 'on.' This usage is too specific and depends on context, and would not appear as an entry in the Oxford Dictionary. Dictionaries would cover 'untraded' and 'trade on' separately, but not 'untraded on' as a fixed phrase.
