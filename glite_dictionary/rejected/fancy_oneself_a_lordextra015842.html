fancy oneself a lord

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: fancy oneself a lord|extra.015842
to consider oneself superior or important

Synset Examples

He fancied himself a lord among his friends.

Reason

The concept 'fancy oneself a lord' is a specific instance of the more general phrase 'fancy oneself' meaning to have a high opinion of oneself or consider oneself superior. The 'a lord' part is just one possible object and not an idiom or fixed phrase in its own right. Therefore, this is too specific and would not appear as a separate entry in standard dictionaries; users should be directed to 'fancy oneself'.
