pot

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13796604-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: pot|oewn-13796604-n
(often followed by ‘of’) a large number or amount or extent

Synset Examples

a batch of letters
a deal of trouble
a lot of money
he made a mint on the stock market
see the rest of the winners in our huge passel of photos
it must have cost plenty
a slew of journalists
a wad of money

Reason

The sense described here refers to 'pot' as a quantifier meaning 'a large number or amount', but this usage is not common or idiomatic in standard English. Unlike 'batch', 'lot', or 'slew', 'pot' is not typically used in this way in contemporary or even archaic English. Including this sense would be misleading, as it would not appear in reputable English dictionaries for this meaning.
