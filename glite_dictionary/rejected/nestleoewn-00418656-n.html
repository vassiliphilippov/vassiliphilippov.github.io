Reason

The synset described ('a close and affectionate (and often prolonged) embrace') does not correspond to the canonical usage of 'nestle' as a noun in standard English. 'Nestle' is not commonly used as a noun with this meaning in modern English; it is primarily used as a verb meaning 'to settle or lie comfortably within or against something.' Therefore, this noun sense should be excluded since it would not appear in major English dictionaries such as Oxford.
