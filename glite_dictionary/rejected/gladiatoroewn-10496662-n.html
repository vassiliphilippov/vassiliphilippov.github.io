Reason

This sense ('a professional boxer') for 'gladiator' is archaic/metaphorical and not current or standard usage in modern English. The primary and dictionary-worthy meaning of 'gladiator' refers to the armed combatant in ancient Rome. The sense as 'professional boxer' would not appear as a main definition in contemporary dictionaries and is too metaphorical/general for inclusion.
