dowdy

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07638972-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: dowdy|oewn-07638972-n
deep-dish apple dessert covered with a rich crust

Reason

Although 'dowdy' can refer to a specific kind of apple dessert in dialectal American English ('apple dowdy'), this sense is now extremely rare and not widely attested in modern usage. The word 'dowdy' is overwhelmingly known as an adjective meaning unattractive or unfashionable. The noun sense referring to a dessert is too obscure for modern standard English dictionaries like Oxford to include, unless in a historical or regional supplement.
