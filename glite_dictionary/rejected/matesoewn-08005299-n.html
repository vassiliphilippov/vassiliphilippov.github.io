Reason

This sense of "mates"—as a noun meaning "a pair of people who live together," especially implying a couple—is not established or common in English. In standard dictionaries, "mates" typically refers to friends, partners, or animals paired for breeding, but not to a couple living together as a distinct noun. Therefore, this sense does not warrant a dictionary entry.
