moor

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01308021-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: moor|oewn-01308021-v
come into or dock at a wharf

Synset Examples

the big ship wharfed in the evening

Reason

The synset described refers to the verb 'moor' with the rare meaning 'to come into or dock at a wharf' (i.e., 'wharf' as a verb). While 'moor' as a verb meaning 'to secure a boat or ship to a fixed object' is standard and should be included, this particular sense (synonymous with 'wharf' as a verb) is too specialized, rare, and not widely attested in general English usage. Reputable dictionaries like Oxford focus on the ordinary sense 'to secure a boat'. Therefore, this narrow verb sense should not be included.
