Reason

The sense 'bad' as 'reproduced fraudulently' (e.g., a bad penny, a forged bill) is a specialized, extended use relating to forgery or counterfeit. However, Oxford Dictionary and similar dictionaries fold this meaning under the general 'bad' adjective sense (inferior, fraudulent, criminal, etc.) without a separate entry. Since the core synset (adjective 'bad') covers this usage, a distinct entry for 'bad' in the narrower sense of 'reproduced fraudulently' is not warranted. This synset is too narrow/specialized for a standalone entry and is covered by the broad adjectival meanings.
