King of England from 1272 to 1307; conquered Wales (1239-1307)

Reason

This sense of 'Edward' refers to an individual person (specifically, King Edward I of England), and the synset is 'person', which is too general. Proper names for individuals with only a generic 'person' sense are not typically included in general dictionaries like Oxford unless the definition is specific to their historical or cultural significance; instead, they tend to appear in encyclopedias.
