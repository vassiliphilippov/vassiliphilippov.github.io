owner who lets another person use something (housing usually) for hire

Reason

The sense 'letter' meaning 'an owner who lets another person use something (usually housing) for hire' is archaic and extremely rare in modern English; it would not appear as a primary sense in Oxford or current general dictionaries. Contemporary usage for this concept is 'landlord' or 'lessor', not 'letter'. Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
