be all bullocks

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: be all bullocks|extra.018413
to be nonsense or untrue

Synset Examples

He talks about his plans, but it's all bullocks.

Reason

The phrase 'be all bullocks' is not an established or recognized idiom or phrasal verb in standard English dictionaries. It appears to be a mishearing or adaptation of 'bollocks', which is a British slang term for nonsense, but 'bullocks' means young bulls and is unrelated. Therefore, this concept should not be included as is.
