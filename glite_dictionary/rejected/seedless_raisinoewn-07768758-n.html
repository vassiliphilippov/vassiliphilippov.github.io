Reason

The concept 'seedless raisin' is a compositional phrase describing a 'raisin' (which is already defined as a dried grape) that happens to be seedless. It is not a term with a specialized or idiomatic meaning and would not appear as a separate entry in major dictionaries like Oxford; rather, it is formed from 'seedless' and 'raisin,' both of which have their own entries.
