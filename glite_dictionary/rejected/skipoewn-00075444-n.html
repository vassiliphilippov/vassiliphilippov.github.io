Reason

The sense 'skip' as 'a mistake resulting from neglect' is not an established or commonly recognized noun usage in contemporary English. Most speakers understand 'skip' primarily as a verb ('to move along lightly' or 'to omit something'), not as a noun meaning 'mistake.' This sense is too obscure and would not appear as a standalone noun entry in standard dictionaries like Oxford.
