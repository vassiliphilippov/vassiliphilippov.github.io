censure severely or angrily

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Reason

This sense of 'jaw' as a verb meaning 'censure severely or angrily' is extremely rare and archaic, and does not appear in contemporary English dictionaries such as the Oxford Dictionary in this sense. Its primary verb meaning relates to talking or chatting, not to scolding. Including this sense would be misleading or confusing for most learners.
