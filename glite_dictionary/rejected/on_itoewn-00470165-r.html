Reason

The synset for 'on it' here refers to the very general, compositional meaning 'on that', where 'on' is simply the preposition and 'it' is the pronoun. This usage is not idiomatic or a phrasal verb, and does not have a unique meaning that would justify a separate dictionary entry. It is fully predictable from the meanings of its parts, so it would not appear as its own entry in a dictionary like Oxford.
