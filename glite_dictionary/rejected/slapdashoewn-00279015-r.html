Synset Examples

Reason

The adverbial sense of 'slapdash' meaning 'directly' is very rare, obsolete, and not in standard current use. It is not found as an adverb with this sense in major English dictionaries like Oxford; the primary modern meanings of 'slapdash' are as an adjective or adverb meaning 'done carelessly.' The sense here, as seen in examples 'ran slap into her,' is covered by a different entry for 'slap' (adverb) meaning 'directly.' Therefore, it should not be included as 'slapdash' in the dictionary.
