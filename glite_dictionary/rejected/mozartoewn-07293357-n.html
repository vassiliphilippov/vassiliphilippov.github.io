Synset Examples

Reason

The synset is 'the music of Mozart' using 'Mozart' metonymically to refer to his works. This is a referential use of the proper noun, not a distinct lexical sense suitable for a general English dictionary entry. 'Mozart' as a proper noun referring to the composer would be included, but its metonymic use for his music is not treated as a separate dictionary headword in major dictionaries like Oxford.
