detain

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00461855-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: detain|oewn-00461855-v
stop or halt

Synset Examples

Please stay the bloodshed!

Reason

This sense of 'detain' as 'stop or halt' is extremely rare and not current in modern English; in standard dictionaries, 'detain' commonly means to keep someone in official custody or to delay. The example ('Please stay the bloodshed!') actually uses 'stay' in the sense of 'stop', not 'detain'. Therefore, this sense should not be included in the dictionary.
