Synset Examples

Reason

Although 'out of' can mean 'motivated by' in certain contexts, this use is rare and highly context-dependent. This sense is highly compositional, combining the preposition 'out of' with a following noun to express a cause or motive (e.g., 'out of curiosity', 'out of fear'), but it does not constitute a standalone lexical item suitable for a dictionary entry. Major English dictionaries seldom list 'out of' as a fixed phrase specifically meaning 'motivated by'. Therefore, this phrase should not be included as a separate dictionary entry.
