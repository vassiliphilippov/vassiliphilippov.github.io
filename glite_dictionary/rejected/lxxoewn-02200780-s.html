Reason

The concept 'lxx' refers to the Roman numeral for 70 (ten more than sixty); however, as an adjective in this form and sense, it is not generally used in modern English and would not appear as a standard headword in dictionaries like Oxford. Such entries typically appear as 'LXX' within the set of Roman numerals, or are subsumed under 'seventy' and 'Roman numeral' entries. Therefore, it's too obscure and rarely used in this adjectival sense for inclusion.
