Reason

This sense of 'marvelous' ('too improbable to admit of belief') is not a distinct or commonly recognized meaning in modern usage; it is a rare or figurative extension not found in standard dictionaries. The primary sense of 'marvelous' relates to something wonderful or astonishing, not specifically to being unbelievable or far-fetched. The example given ('a tall story') is better served by entries like 'incredible' or 'fantastic' in the sense of 'not credible.' Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
