Reason

This sense of 'major' is an archaic and highly specialized usage, referring to distinguishing the elder of two boys in the same family, often as part of a name (e.g., Jones major). It is not commonly used in modern English and would not typically appear as a separate sense in contemporary dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary. The concept is thus too narrow, dated, and rare for inclusion.
