In modern English dictionaries, individual Roman numerals with their numeric meanings are often not included unless they have significant independent usage as words. 'LI' functions strictly as a numeral, not as a standard word or phrase in English, and would not have an entry in a traditional dictionary such as Oxford unless used as an abbreviation or with special significance. Therefore, it is excluded.
