freeze out

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00446666-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: freeze out|oewn-00446666-v
change from a liquid to a solid when cold

Synset Examples

Water freezes at 32 degrees Fahrenheit

Reason

The synset refers to the basic process of 'freezing' (changing from a liquid to a solid), but the phrasal verb 'freeze out' is not used in standard English to mean this. The standard verb for this sense is 'freeze.' 'Freeze out' has other specific meanings (e.g., excluding someone socially), but not this literal physical change. Therefore, this concept should be excluded as it is not dictionary-attestable for this meaning.
