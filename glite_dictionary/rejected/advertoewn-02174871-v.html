advert

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02174871-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: advert|oewn-02174871-v
give heed (to)

Synset Examples

The children in the audience attended the recital quietly
She hung on his every word
They attended to everything he said

Reason

The sense 'advert' as a verb meaning 'give heed (to)' is archaic and extremely rare in modern English, not appearing in current dictionaries in this sense. The common modern use of 'advert' is as a noun (short for 'advertisement') or rarely as a verb meaning 'to refer to' or 'mention.' Therefore, this sense is excluded.
