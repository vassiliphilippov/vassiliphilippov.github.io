Synset Examples

She visited her used-to-be hometown last week.

Reason

The concept 'used to be' with the meaning 'formerly; in the past' is represented here as a noun phrase (e.g. 'her used-to-be hometown'), which is not standard or widely recognized in formal English. The correct and established form 'used to' (as a phrasal verb or auxiliary for past habits or states) is already in the dictionary, but this noun use ('used-to-be' as in 'former') is not sufficiently attested or standard to merit an entry in a general English dictionary like Oxford. The concept is too nonstandard and marginal.
