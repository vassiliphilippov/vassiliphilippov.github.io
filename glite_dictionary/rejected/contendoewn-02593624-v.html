contend

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593624-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: contend|oewn-02593624-v
succeed in doing, achieving, or producing (something) with the limited or inadequate means available

Synset Examples

We got by on just a gallon of gas
They made do on half a loaf of bread every day

Reason

The synset described ("succeed in doing, achieving, or producing with limited means") is more accurately represented by phrasal verbs like 'get by' or 'make do', not by the verb 'contend'. This sense does not directly relate to how 'contend' is used in contemporary English, and including it under 'contend' would be misleading.
