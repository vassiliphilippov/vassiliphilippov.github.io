Synset POS: n

troops trained to fight on horseback

Synset Examples

500 horse led the attack

Reason

The synset refers to a specific, historical collective use of 'horse' as meaning cavalry or mounted troops. This use is now rare and falls outside general modern English usage for the word 'horse,' which primarily refers to the animal. As such, it would not typically be included as a main entry sense in a current learner's dictionary like Oxford. The usual dictionary entry for 'horse' is the animal, and this sense as 'cavalry' is archaic and specialized, not in regular use.
