at a draught

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: at a draught|extra.000539
in a place where drinks are served, often on tap or served from a barrel or keg

Synset Examples

She enjoyed her cider at a draught while relaxing outdoors.
The pub serves their ales at a draught for authenticity.

Reason

The phrase 'at a draught' in this sense is not attested or common in modern or historical English dictionaries. The idiomatic, fixed expressions related to drinking (such as 'on tap', 'on draught', 'draft beer') are widely used, but 'at a draught' is not an established, recognizable form in reference to being served from a barrel or keg. Therefore, this concept does not meet dictionary inclusion criteria.
