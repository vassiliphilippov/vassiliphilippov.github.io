Reason

This sense of 'head' as 'forward movement' (as in 'making headway') is too distant from how 'head' is primarily understood as a noun; it is used almost exclusively in fixed phrases like 'make head' or 'headway,' and would not be included as an independent entry in a standard English dictionary. The general noun 'head' has many senses, but 'head' meaning simply 'forward movement' is obsolete or only used within specific phrases, so is too broad/general here.
