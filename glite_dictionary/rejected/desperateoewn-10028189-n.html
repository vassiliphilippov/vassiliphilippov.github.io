Reason

This sense of 'desperate' as a noun ('a desperate' meaning a person who is frightened and in need of help) is extremely rare and not standard in contemporary English. Major English dictionaries do not list 'desperate' as a countable noun with this meaning, and it would not appear as a separate entry in authoritative dictionaries such as Oxford. The word 'desperate' is primarily used as an adjective. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
