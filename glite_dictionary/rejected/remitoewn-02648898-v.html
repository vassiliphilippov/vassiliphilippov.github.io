remit

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02648898-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: remit|oewn-02648898-v
hold back to a later time

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Reason

The verb 'remit' in the sense of 'hold back to a later time' is archaic and very rarely used in modern English. In current usage, 'remit' most commonly means 'to send money' or 'to cancel a penalty or punishment.' The sense of 'postpone' is obsolete and would not appear as an active entry in general-use English dictionaries like Oxford in this meaning.
