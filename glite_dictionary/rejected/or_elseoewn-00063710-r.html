Reason

The concept presented here is 'or else' as an adverb meaning 'alternatively' or 'instead.' The standard usage of 'or else' in English is to indicate a threat or consequence (e.g., 'Do it, or else!'), rather than 'alternatively' or 'instead.' The example sentences given do not match common or dictionary-recognized usage for 'or else' in general English. As such, this synset is not appropriate for dictionary inclusion as it is not representative of how 'or else' is used in modern English. The sense is too artificial or not generally attested.
