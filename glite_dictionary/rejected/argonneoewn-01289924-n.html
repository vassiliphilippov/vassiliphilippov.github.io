Argonne

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01289924-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: Argonne|oewn-01289924-n
an American operation in World War I (1918); American troops under Pershing drove back the German armies which were saved only by the armistice on November 11

Reason

The concept 'Argonne' here refers specifically to a World War I military operation, but the proper noun 'Argonne' in English is better known as a place name (the Argonne Forest) or as part of the compound 'Meuse-Argonne Offensive.' As a standalone dictionary entry, 'Argonne' as an operation is too narrow and not independently used with this meaning in general English; it would not appear in Oxford Dictionary under this definition.
