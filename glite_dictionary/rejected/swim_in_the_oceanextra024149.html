Reason

The concept 'swim in the ocean' is a transparent, compositional phrase built from the basic verb 'swim' and the prepositional phrase 'in the ocean.' Such literal verb-preposition combinations are not typically included as dictionary headwords unless they are idiomatic or have a special, non-obvious meaning. Here, it simply means to swim in the sea and does not qualify as a dictionary entry.
