sulky

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00984894-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: sulky|oewn-00984894-s
slow or slothful

Synset Examples

a sluggish stream

Reason

This sense of 'sulky' meaning 'slow or slothful' is archaic and not found in modern English. The commonly used meaning of 'sulky' is 'morose or sullen,' referring to mood or demeanor. The usage referring to slowness is obsolete and would not appear as a current main entry or sense in standard dictionaries like Oxford.
