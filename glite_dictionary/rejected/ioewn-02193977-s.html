used of a single unit or thing; not two or more

Synset Examples

‘ane’ is Scottish

Reason

The concept 'i' here refers to the archaic or dialectal form used as a variant of 'one' (the number or pronoun), and the sense description given applies to the numeral 'one.' The example sentence references 'ane,' a Scottish term for 'one.' 'i' is not conventionally used in modern English for this meaning and would not appear as this entry in a modern English-English dictionary such as Oxford. Therefore, it should be excluded.
