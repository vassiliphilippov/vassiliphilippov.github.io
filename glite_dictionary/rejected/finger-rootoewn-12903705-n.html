Internal ID: finger-root|oewn-12903705-n
tall leafy European biennial or perennial having spectacular clusters of large tubular pink-purple flowers; leaves yield drug digitalis and are poisonous to livestock

Reason

The synset described here is for the plant commonly known as 'foxglove' (Digitalis purpurea), not for 'finger-root.' 'Finger-root' is typically used for Boesenbergia rotunda, a Southeast Asian herb, and is not commonly used as a synonym for foxglove. The concept as described is too general and does not match the specific headword, so it should be excluded.
