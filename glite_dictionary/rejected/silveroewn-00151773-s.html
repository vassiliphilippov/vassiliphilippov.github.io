Reason

The synset 'expressing yourself readily, clearly, effectively' with the example 'silver speech' refers to an attributive use of 'silver' as a metaphor for eloquence (i.e., 'silver-tongued'). However, as an adjective 'silver' in this sense is only used as part of idiomatic compounds (e.g., 'silver tongue', 'silver-tongued') and is not a standalone adjective with this meaning. Therefore, it is too general and not a standalone dictionary headword for this sense.
