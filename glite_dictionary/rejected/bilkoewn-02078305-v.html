bilk

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02078305-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: bilk|oewn-02078305-v
escape, either physically or mentally

Synset Examples

The thief eluded the police
This difficult idea seems to evade her
The event evades explanation

Reason

The synset describes a very broad sense ('escape, either physically or mentally'), but 'bilk' is not used with this meaning in contemporary or even historical English. In current usage, 'bilk' means to cheat, defraud, or evade payment, not simply to escape physically or mentally like 'elude' or 'evade.' This sense is too broad and not associated with 'bilk' as found in reputable dictionaries. Therefore, this concept should not be included as a separate entry.
