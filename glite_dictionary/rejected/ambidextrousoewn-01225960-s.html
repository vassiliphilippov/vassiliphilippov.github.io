ambidextrous

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01225960-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: ambidextrous|oewn-01225960-s
marked by deliberate deceptiveness especially by pretending one set of feelings and acting under the influence of another

Synset Examples

she was a deceitful scheming little thing
a double-dealing double agent
a double-faced infernal traitor and schemer

Reason

This sense of 'ambidextrous' as 'marked by deliberate deceptiveness' is rare and archaic. In modern usage, 'ambidextrous' primarily refers to being able to use both hands equally well. The deceptive sense would not typically appear in a contemporary English-English dictionary except as a rare or obsolete usage order, and is no longer familiar to the vast majority of users.
