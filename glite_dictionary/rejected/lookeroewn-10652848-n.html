looker

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10652848-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: looker|oewn-10652848-n
a close observer; someone who looks at something (such as an exhibition of some kind)

Synset Examples

the spectators applauded the performance
television viewers
sky watchers discovered a new star

Reason

The synset defines 'looker' as a generic 'observer' or 'someone who looks at something,' which is too broad and does not reflect standard dictionary usage. 'Looker' in common English primarily means 'an attractive person,' especially in informal contexts, rather than 'a close observer.' Therefore, this sense is too general and not appropriate for inclusion as a primary dictionary entry.
