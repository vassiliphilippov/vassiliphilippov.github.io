sewer

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14499576-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: sewer|oewn-14499576-n
misfortune resulting in lost effort or money

Synset Examples

his career was in the gutter
all that work went down the sewer
pensions are in the toilet

Reason

This sense of 'sewer' as 'misfortune resulting in lost effort or money' is rare, metaphorical, and not common or standard enough to warrant a main entry in an English-English dictionary such as the Oxford Dictionary. The predominant meaning of 'sewer' is a conduit for waste water, which does belong in dictionaries. This figurative use only appears in idiomatic phrases and is not an accepted standalone noun with this meaning.
