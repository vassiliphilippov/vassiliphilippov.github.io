all over

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01006696-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: all over|oewn-01006696-s
having come or been brought to a conclusion

Synset Examples

the harvesting was complete
the affair is over, ended, finished
the abruptly terminated interview

Reason

The concept 'all over' in the sense of 'having come or been brought to a conclusion' is not a standalone meaning or phrasal usage. The sense described is more properly covered by the adjective 'over' or 'finished' in this context. 'All over' typically means 'everywhere' or 'completely covered,' but not 'ended.' Therefore, this concept should not be included.
