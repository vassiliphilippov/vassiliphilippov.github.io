Reason

The synset refers to the abstract noun 'success in satisfying a test or requirement,' but 'qualifying' is not used as a standard noun in this meaning in modern English. The noun 'qualification' or phrases like 'passing' or 'success' are more appropriate for this sense. As such, this use of 'qualifying' is too uncommon or awkward to include as its own noun entry in a general dictionary.
