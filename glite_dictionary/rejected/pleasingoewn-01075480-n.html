the act of one who pleases

Reason

The noun sense 'pleasing' as 'the act of one who pleases' is extremely rare, archaic, and not used in modern English. The current meaning of 'pleasing' is as an adjective (giving pleasure, attractive, agreeable). The noun use would not appear as a headword in a contemporary English dictionary such as Oxford; it is excessively marginal and would confuse learners.
