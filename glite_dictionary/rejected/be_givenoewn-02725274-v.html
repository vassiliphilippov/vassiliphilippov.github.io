be given

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02725274-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: be given|oewn-02725274-v
have a tendency or disposition to do or be something; be inclined

Synset Examples

She tends to be nervous before her lectures
These dresses run small
He inclined to corpulence

Reason

The sense of 'be given' here is an auxiliary construction meaning 'to have a disposition or tendency', rather than a distinct lexical phrasal verb. This is not a standard dictionary entry on its own; Oxford and other dictionaries would not list 'be given' in this sense as a headword entry, but rather describe this usage under 'given' as an adjective (e.g. 'given to'), or under entries like 'tend', 'inclined'. The synset is too general and constructional.
