hitch

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01888376-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: hitch|oewn-01888376-v
jump vertically, with legs stiff and back arched

Synset Examples

the young filly bucked

Reason

This sense of "hitch" is a very rare and obscure usage (to jump vertically with legs stiff and back arched, as in a horse 'bucking'), not commonly found in standard modern English or reflected in major dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary. The verb 'buck' is the normal term for this action. Therefore, this sense does not merit a standalone entry in a general English-English dictionary.
