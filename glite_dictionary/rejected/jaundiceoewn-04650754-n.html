a rough and bitter manner

Reason

This sense of 'jaundice' (meaning a rough and bitter manner) is obsolete and not current in contemporary English. The primary and widely recognized sense of 'jaundice' relates to the medical condition. This figurative sense would not appear as a distinct entry in a modern dictionary like the Oxford Dictionary.
