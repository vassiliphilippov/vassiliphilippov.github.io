Reason

This sense describes a very narrow, context-specific usage (to rinse one's mouth and throat with mouthwash) and is commonly referred to as 'gargle'. The main verb 'rinse' is widely used and should be included in the dictionary, but this highly specific sense does not warrant a separate entry, as it overlaps with 'gargle', which is the standard term for this action. This meaning is too specific and not a general sense or distinct phrasal unit for 'rinse'.
