Synset Examples

Reason

The synset refers to an extremely general sense of 'move forward' (e.g., time marches on), and the phrase 'pass on' is not generally used in standard English to mean simply 'move forward' in this generic or metaphorical way. In this sense, 'pass on' is not a distinct dictionary entry; it's not idiomatic or commonly found as an independent phrase meaning 'move forward.' Therefore, it should not be included.
