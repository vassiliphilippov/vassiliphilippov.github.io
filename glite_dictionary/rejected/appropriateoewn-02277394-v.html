appropriate

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02277394-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: appropriate|oewn-02277394-v
take possession of by force, as after an invasion

Synset Examples

the invaders seized the land and property of the inhabitants
The army seized the town
The militia captured the castle

Reason

This sense of 'appropriate'—meaning to take possession by force—is rare and not commonly associated with the word in modern standard English. The standard dictionary meaning of 'appropriate' as a verb relates to taking something for one’s own use, often without permission, but not necessarily by force following invasion (the given sense overlaps more with 'seize' or 'capture'). Therefore, this narrow and unusual sense does not warrant separate inclusion as a dictionary entry.
