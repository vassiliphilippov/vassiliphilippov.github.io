rectify

Synset POS: v

bring, lead, or force to abandon a wrong or evil course of life, conduct, and adopt a right one

Synset Examples

The Church reformed me
reform your conduct

Reason

This synset interpretation for 'rectify' ('bring, lead, or force to abandon a wrong or evil course of life, conduct, and adopt a right one') is not a standard, widely attested meaning of 'rectify' in general English. Mainstream dictionaries use 'rectify' to mean 'to put right, correct, or fix.' The given sense aligns with 'reform' rather than 'rectify,' and would not appear as a separate sense under 'rectify' in Oxford or similar dictionaries. Therefore, it should not be included.
