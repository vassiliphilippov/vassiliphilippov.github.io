phlegm

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07498922-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: phlegm|oewn-07498922-n
apathy demonstrated by an absence of emotional reactions

Reason

This sense of 'phlegm'—meaning apathy or absence of emotional reactions—is archaic and is practically never used in modern English. The overwhelming primary meaning of 'phlegm' today relates to respiratory mucus. The archaic use derived from humoral theory, but would not appear in a modern general dictionary such as Oxford, except perhaps with a historical/archaic label and even then rarely. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
