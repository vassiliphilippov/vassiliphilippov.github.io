English paleontologist whose account of fossil discoveries in Tanzania changed theories of human evolution (1903-1972)

Reason

The synset is 'person', which is too general. While 'Leakey' refers to a notable individual (Louis Leakey), dictionary entries do not generally include proper names unless they have extended lexical significance beyond being a personal name. Most English dictionaries, including Oxford, do not include such biographical entries unless the name has become a common noun or adjective (e.g., 'Newtonian' or 'Shakespearean'). Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
