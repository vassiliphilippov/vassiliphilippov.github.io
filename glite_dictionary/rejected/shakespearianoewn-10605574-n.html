Reason

The synset 'a Shakespearean scholar' is overly specific and refers to a person who studies Shakespeare, which is not a standard sense of 'Shakespearian'; rather, it is a derived professional label. The headword 'Shakespearian' as a noun for a scholar would not be included as a separate entry in major dictionaries because it is simply the regular adjectival form used substantively and not in widespread use as a noun.
