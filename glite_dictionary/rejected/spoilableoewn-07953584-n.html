Reason

The word 'spoilable' is an adjective describing something that can spoil (decay or become bad), but the WordNet synset here refers to it as a noun meaning food that will decay rapidly if not refrigerated. This noun sense is not standard and is not found in major dictionaries such as Oxford or Merriam-Webster. 'Spoilable' in its adjective form is legitimate, but as a noun it is too rare or nonstandard for inclusion.
