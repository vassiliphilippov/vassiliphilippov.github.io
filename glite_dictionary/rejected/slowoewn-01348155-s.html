slow

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01348155-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: slow|oewn-01348155-s
so lacking in interest as to cause mental weariness

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Reason

The synset described here refers to 'slow' meaning 'so lacking in interest as to cause mental weariness,' but this sense is better covered by adjectives such as 'boring,' 'dull,' 'tedious,' etc. The use of 'slow' in this way is not common, and established dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary do not mark 'slow' as standardly meaning 'boring' or 'tedious.' Therefore, this sense should not be included as an independent headword meaning in the dictionary.
