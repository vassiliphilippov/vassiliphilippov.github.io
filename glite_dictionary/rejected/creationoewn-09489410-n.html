creation

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09489410-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: creation|oewn-09489410-n
everything that exists anywhere

Synset Examples

they study the evolution of the universe
the biggest tree in existence

Reason

The concept here is the very general sense of 'everything that exists anywhere', i.e., the totality of existence or the universe. While 'creation' can mean this in religious or philosophical contexts, its most common dictionary definitions focus on the act of creating or something that has been created. This extremely broad use as an absolute synonym for 'the universe' or 'all existence' is too general and would not be included as a separate sense in a standard learner's dictionary like the Oxford Dictionary. Main entries cover 'creation' as act/result/product, not as 'the universe' per se.
