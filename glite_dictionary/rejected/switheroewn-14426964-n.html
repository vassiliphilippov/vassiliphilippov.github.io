swither

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14426964-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: swither|oewn-14426964-n
agitation resulting from active worry

Synset Examples

don't get in a stew
he's in a sweat about exams

Reason

The WordNet synset here uses 'swither' for a meaning related to a state of worry or agitation, but in standard British and American English, 'swither' is primarily a verb meaning to be indecisive or to waver between possibilities. The noun form meaning 'agitation resulting from active worry' is nonstandard and extremely rare, and does not correspond to a commonly recognized or dictionary-worthy usage. The typical dictionary entry for 'swither' is as a verb, not a noun, so the noun sense should be excluded.
