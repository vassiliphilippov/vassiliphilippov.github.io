Swedish chemist and physicist noted for his theory of chemical dissociation (1859-1927)

Reason

The synset is 'person', which is too general and refers to any individual human being. Personal names with only the synset 'person' are not included in standard English dictionaries such as Oxford, unless the person is extremely iconic and their name has become a word or term in the language (e.g., 'Einstein' as a term for a genius). Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
