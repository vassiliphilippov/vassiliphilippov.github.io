Reason

This concept describes 'intelligent' in the specific sense of 'possessing sound knowledge' (i.e., being well-informed), but 'intelligent' is not commonly or standardly defined this way in major dictionaries. The standard meaning of 'intelligent' relates to mental ability, reasoning, or intellect, not specifically being knowledgeable or well-informed. The synset overlaps more with 'well-informed' or 'knowledgeable' rather than the canonical sense of 'intelligent.' Therefore, this specific sense should not be included as a dictionary entry under 'intelligent.'
