shape (a material like stone or wood) by whittling away at it

Synset Examples

She is sculpting the block of marble into an image of her husband

Reason

The concept of 'grave' meaning 'to shape (a material like stone or wood) by whittling away at it' is very rare and obsolete in contemporary English. This sense does not appear in modern dictionaries like Oxford, nor is it in common usage, and in any case is usually covered under 'carve', 'engrave', or 'sculpt'. Therefore, this sense should not be included.
