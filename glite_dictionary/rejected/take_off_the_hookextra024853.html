take off the hook

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: take off the hook|extra.024853
removed from a hanging position

Synset Examples

She took the jackets off the hooks before leaving.

Reason

The concept 'take off the hook' in this sense is simply a compositional phrase that means to remove something from a hook, and is not an established phrasal verb or idiom. It would not have its own entry in a standard dictionary like Oxford; instead, the individual words 'take' and 'hook' would be defined, and the construction is transparent. Therefore, it should not be included as a separate dictionary entry.
