at the roundabout

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: at the roundabout|extra.012452
a location or junction where roads meet

Synset Examples

If you go left at the roundabout, you'll find the cafe.

Reason

The concept 'at the roundabout' is a common prepositional phrase indicating location, but it lacks lexical distinctiveness and is purely compositional. It describes a location using 'at' (preposition) and 'the roundabout' (noun), both of which are standard entries. Such fully compositional phrases are generally not included in dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary unless they have idiomatic meaning, which is not the case here.
