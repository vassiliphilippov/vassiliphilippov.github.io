radiation

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14331899-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: radiation|oewn-14331899-n
syndrome resulting from exposure to ionizing radiation (e.g., exposure to radioactive chemicals or to nuclear explosions); low doses cause diarrhea and nausea and vomiting and sometimes loss of hair; greater exposure can cause sterility and cataracts and some forms of cancer and other diseases; severe exposure can cause death within hours

Synset Examples

he was suffering from radiation

Reason

This concept refers to 'radiation' in the sense of 'radiation sickness' or 'acute radiation syndrome,' which is a specific medical condition. However, the headword 'radiation' is not commonly or correctly used in standard English to mean the syndrome itself; instead, 'radiation' refers to the process of emitting energy as waves or particles. The name for the syndrome is 'radiation sickness' or 'acute radiation syndrome.' Therefore, this sense is too specialized and not appropriate under the headword 'radiation' for a general English dictionary.
