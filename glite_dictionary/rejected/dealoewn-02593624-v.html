deal

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593624-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: deal|oewn-02593624-v
succeed in doing, achieving, or producing (something) with the limited or inadequate means available

Synset Examples

We got by on just a gallon of gas
They made do on half a loaf of bread every day

Reason

This sense of 'deal' is not specific to the headword 'deal' but instead conveys the meaning of phrases like 'get by' or 'make do.' 'Deal' does not naturally carry this particular sense in contemporary English without additional words (such as 'deal with'). Therefore, this meaning is too general or not directly and naturally associated with 'deal' as a stand-alone verb in modern usage. It should not be included.
