consist

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02664128-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: consist|oewn-02664128-v
be consistent in form, tenor, or character; be congruous

Synset Examples

Desires are to be satisfied only so far as consists with an approved end

Reason

This sense of 'consist' ('be consistent in form, tenor, or character; be congruous') is archaic and virtually never encountered in modern English and would not appear as a main meaning in contemporary dictionaries. It is not the primary use of the verb 'consist', which more commonly means 'to be composed of'.
