Reason

The concept 'above ground' in this context refers to a metaphorical extension (constructed and open to the public), which is not a standard dictionary sense. The usual senses of 'above ground' (physically located above the surface, visible, not underground) would be included, but not this specific usage. It is too context-specific and not established enough for a general dictionary entry.
