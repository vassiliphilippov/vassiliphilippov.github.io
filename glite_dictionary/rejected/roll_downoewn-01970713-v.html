gather into a huge mass and roll down a mountain, of snow

Reason

The synset is a very narrow, context-specific sense (to gather into a huge mass and roll down a mountain, of snow) and does not reflect an established, widely recognized meaning of 'roll down' in general dictionaries. Typically, 'roll down' means to descend by rolling, or to move down by turning over and over, or (of a window, sleeve, etc.) to be rolled to a lower position. The meaning here is both highly specific and not established as a standard sense in English, so it would not appear in Oxford or most general dictionaries as a separate entry.
