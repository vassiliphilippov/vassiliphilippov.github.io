throw overboard

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02308311-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: throw overboard|oewn-02308311-v
lose (something) or lose the right to (something) by some error, offense, or crime

Synset Examples

you've forfeited your right to name your successor
forfeited property

Reason

The concept 'throw overboard' in this sense is not used in modern or standard English with the meaning 'to lose something by error or offense'. This is a metaphorical extension corresponding to 'forfeit', and not an established phrasal verb in this meaning. It would not appear as a stand-alone entry in Oxford dictionaries for this sense.
