make a blueprint of

Reason

The verb sense of 'draught' meaning 'to make a blueprint of' is extremely rare, archaic, and not found in contemporary English dictionaries such as Oxford. The standard verb is 'draft,' especially in American English, and even in British English 'draught' is not commonly used as a verb in this way. Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
