son of Edward II and King of England from 1327-1377; his claim to the French throne provoked the Hundred Years' War; his reign was marked by an epidemic of the Black Plague and by the emergence of the House of Commons as the powerful arm of British Parliament (1312-1377)

Reason

This sense of 'Edward' is a specific historical individual (Edward III), but the synset is just a person's name without additional distinguishing content. English dictionaries do not normally include such entries for personal names unless they are used as common nouns or adjectives. Proper nouns for historical figures are covered in encyclopedias rather than dictionaries, so this concept should be excluded.
