Reason

The adjective 'experient', meaning 'having experience', is considered archaic and is not in common use in modern English. Contemporary dictionaries like Oxford do not include it except in historical contexts. Modern usage prefers 'experienced'. This concept is too obsolete to warrant inclusion for general English learners.
