raining

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02496475-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: raining|oewn-02496475-s
falling in drops or as if falling like rain

Synset Examples

watched the raining apple blossoms

Reason

The synset refers to the participial adjective form meaning 'falling in drops or as if falling like rain', which is used rarely and almost always figuratively. The primary sense 'raining' as a verb is already included as the present participle of 'rain'. This adjectival sense is specialized, dated, and unlikely to be needed as a separate lemma in a general-purpose dictionary like the Oxford Dictionary. It would ordinarily be covered under figurative uses or in example sentences, not as a standalone entry.
