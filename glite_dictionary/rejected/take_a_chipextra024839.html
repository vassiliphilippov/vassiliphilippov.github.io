take a chip

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: take a chip|extra.024839
to eat a snack (often crisp)

Synset Examples

He took a chip from the bowl during the movie.

Reason

The phrase 'take a chip' is a compositional and transparent phrase, simply meaning to pick up or eat a chip (snack/crisp), and is not an established phrasal verb or idiom. It does not have a special, non-literal meaning or warrant its own dictionary entry, as would be found in the Oxford Dictionary.
