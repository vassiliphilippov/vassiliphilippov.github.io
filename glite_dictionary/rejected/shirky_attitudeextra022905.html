shirky attitude

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: shirky attitude|extra.022905
a noncommittal or evasive demeanor

Synset Examples

His shirky attitude made meetings unbearable.

Reason

The phrase 'shirky attitude' is not an established or widely recognized term in English. It is a nonce formation based on the adjective 'shirky', which itself is rare and not standard in major dictionaries. The underlying concept is simply a noncommittal or evasive attitude, which is adequately covered by established words such as 'evasive' or 'noncommittal attitude'. Therefore, this concept should not be included as a separate dictionary entry.
