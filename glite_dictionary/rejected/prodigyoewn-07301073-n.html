prodigy

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07301073-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: prodigy|oewn-07301073-n
a sign of something about to happen

Synset Examples

he looked for an omen before going into battle

Reason

This sense of 'prodigy' as 'a sign of something about to happen' is archaic and no longer in general or even specialist current use. In modern English, 'prodigy' is overwhelmingly used to mean a person, especially a young one, endowed with exceptional qualities or abilities. The sense here corresponds to historical usage better rendered today by 'omen' or 'portent.' Such a definition would not appear as a current headword sense in Oxford or similar dictionaries.
