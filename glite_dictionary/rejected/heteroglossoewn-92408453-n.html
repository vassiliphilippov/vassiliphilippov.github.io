heterogloss

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92408453-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: heterogloss|oewn-92408453-n
the locative boundary of a certain linguistic feature, such as the pronunciation of a vowel, the meaning of a word, or use of some syntactic feature.

Synset Examples

A major isogloss in American English has been identified as the North-Midland isogloss, which demarcates numerous linguistic features, including the Northern Cities vowel shift.

Reason

The concept described is actually 'isogloss,' not 'heterogloss.' 'Heterogloss' is not an established English word recognized in dictionaries like Oxford, and the synset provided confuses it with the well-known linguistic term 'isogloss.' Therefore, this concept should not be included in the dictionary.
