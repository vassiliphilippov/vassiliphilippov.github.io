Reason

The sense described ('a solemn pledge of fidelity') for 'plight' as a noun is archaic and extremely rare in modern English. 'Plight' is not commonly used with this meaning and it does not warrant a primary entry in a current English-English dictionary. Most modern dictionaries focus on 'plight' as a noun meaning 'a difficult situation' or as a verb meaning 'to pledge', especially in the phrase 'plight one's troth'.
