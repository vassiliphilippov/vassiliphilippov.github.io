premise

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00903562-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: premise|oewn-00903562-v
furnish with a preface or introduction

Synset Examples

She always precedes her lectures with a joke
He prefaced his lecture with a critical remark about the institution

Reason

The verb sense of 'premise' meaning 'to furnish with a preface or introduction' is rarely, if ever, used in contemporary English. The standard verbs are 'preface' or 'precede.' This sense is not common enough to merit its own dictionary entry and does not appear in contemporary major dictionaries under 'premise' as a verb.
