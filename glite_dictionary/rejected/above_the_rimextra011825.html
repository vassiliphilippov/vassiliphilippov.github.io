above the rim

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a

Internal ID: above the rim|extra.011825
a basketball term for shooting over the hoop

Synset Examples

She shot above the rim, securing the win for her team.

Reason

The phrase 'above the rim' in this sense is a specialized basketball term referring to actions (typically shooting or playing) at a level higher than the basketball hoop. However, it is not an established idiom or set phrase widely recognized beyond basketball context, nor would it appear as a headword in major dictionaries like Oxford. Rather, it is a compositional phrase describing the physical location relative to the rim in basketball terminology.
