Reason

The word 'unmutilated' does not accurately match the synset definition or examples provided (which are for 'unblemished'). While 'unmutilated' could, in theory, mean 'not mutilated,' the actual synset refers to being free of spots or moral stains, a sense associated with 'unblemished.' Therefore, including 'unmutilated' for this meaning would be misleading and does not align with standard dictionary practice. 'Unblemished' should be included for this sense, but not 'unmutilated.'
