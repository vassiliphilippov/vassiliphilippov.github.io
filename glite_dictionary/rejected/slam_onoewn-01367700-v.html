Reason

The sense for 'slam on' as 'apply carelessly' is not a standard or widely recognized phrasal verb in English, nor is it commonly found in reputable dictionaries like Oxford. The provided example, 'slap some paint onto the wall,' uses 'slap on,' a recognized phrasal verb meaning 'to apply something quickly or carelessly.' 'Slam on' is not used in this way; in common usage, 'slam on' is usually found in expressions like 'slam on the brakes.' Therefore, this sense should not be included.
