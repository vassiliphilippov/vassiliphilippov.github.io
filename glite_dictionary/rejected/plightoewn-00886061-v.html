Internal ID: plight|oewn-00886061-v
promise solemnly and formally

Synset Examples

I pledge that I will honor my wife

Reason

The sense of 'plight' as a verb meaning 'to promise solemnly and formally' is archaic and virtually obsolete in modern English. In contemporary use, 'plight' is overwhelmingly used as a noun meaning 'a dangerous, difficult, or otherwise unfortunate situation.' The verbal usage exists only in historical or literary texts, so it does not warrant inclusion as a headword in a current dictionary, except perhaps in an extended or archaic appendix.
