nineteen hand high

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: nineteen hand high|extra.006991
a horse measurement of height (19 hands)

Synset Examples

I couldn't believe the horse was nineteen hand high.
You need a strong rider for a nineteen hand horse.

Reason

The concept 'nineteen hand high' refers to a specific numerical measurement of a horse, but is not a standard lexical entry; instead, it is a compositional phrase using the regular measurement system of 'hands' for equine height. Such specific numeral+unit constructions (e.g., 'nineteen hand high', 'seventeen hand high') are not dictionary headwords. Instead, the term 'hand' as a measurement is the dictionary-worthy entry. Therefore, it should be excluded.
