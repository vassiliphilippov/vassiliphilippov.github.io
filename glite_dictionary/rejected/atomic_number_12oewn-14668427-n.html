atomic number 12

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14668427-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: atomic number 12|oewn-14668427-n
a light silver-white ductile bivalent metallic element; in pure form it burns with brilliant white flame; occurs naturally only in combination (as in magnesite and dolomite and carnallite and spinel and olivine)

Reason

The term 'atomic number 12' is a generic scientific designation referring to the element magnesium, rather than a lexical entry in itself. The appropriate headword for a dictionary is 'magnesium,' not its atomic number. Such numerical designations are not included as standalone entries in English dictionaries like Oxford.
