unmoved

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00354705-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: unmoved|oewn-00354705-s
being in the original position; not having been moved

Synset Examples

the archeologists could date the vase because it was in situ
an in-situ investigator

Reason

This concept describes the state of being 'in the original position' or 'not having been moved,' but the standard and widely used term for this is 'in situ.' The adjective 'unmoved' is possible in other senses (such as 'not emotionally affected'), but the usage as an adjective specifically meaning 'being in the original position' is very rare or technical and is much better captured by 'in situ.' Therefore, this sense of 'unmoved' would not appear as a separate sense in a major English dictionary and should be excluded.
