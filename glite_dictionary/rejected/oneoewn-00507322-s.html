Reason

The synset is describing a highly exceptional or unmatched quality (eminent beyond or above comparison), but the use of 'one' in this sense is not a standard dictionary entry as an adjective; rather, phrases like 'one and only' or 'one in a million' express this sense idiomatically. Including 'one' as an adjective meaning 'peerless' or 'matchless' is too ambiguous and does not correspond to a common, isolated dictionary entry. Instead, synonyms like 'peerless' or idioms such as 'one and only' cover this concept in dictionaries. Therefore, this sense should not be included as a headword 'one' in this meaning.
