Synset Examples

the world is constantly changing

Reason

The synset sense described as 'invariably' corresponds to a very rare or almost obsolete adverbial use of 'forever' to mean 'always, in every case.' In contemporary English, 'forever' as an adverb nearly always means 'for all time; perpetually.' The 'invariably' sense does not occur naturally for the word 'forever' and is not included as a main meaning in major dictionaries like Oxford or Merriam-Webster. Therefore, it should not be included as a separate entry.
