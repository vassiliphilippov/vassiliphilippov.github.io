Synset Examples

a woman of breeding and refinement

Reason

This sense of 'education'—meaning 'the result of good upbringing (especially knowledge of correct social behavior)'—is outdated and extremely rare in contemporary English. In modern use, 'education' overwhelmingly refers to formal learning, schooling, or the process of being educated, rather than social breeding or refinement. This archaic sense would not commonly appear in Oxford Dictionary as a distinct entry today.
