regain

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02290474-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: regain|oewn-02290474-v
come upon after searching; find the location of something that was missed or lost

Synset Examples

Did you find your glasses?
I cannot find my gloves!

Reason

The sense described is essentially 'find' or 'locate', but 'regain' is not used in modern English to mean 'find after searching'; its meaning is to get something back that was lost, especially after effort, not simply to locate. The synset sense overlaps with 'find', not the actual usage of 'regain'. Therefore, this sense should not be entered as a dictionary headword for 'regain'.
