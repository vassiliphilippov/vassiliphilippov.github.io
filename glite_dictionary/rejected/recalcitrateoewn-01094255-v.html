recalcitrate

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01094255-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: recalcitrate|oewn-01094255-v
show strong objection or repugnance; manifest vigorous opposition or resistance; be obstinately disobedient

Synset Examples

The Democratic senators recalcitrated against every proposal from the Republican side

Reason

The word 'recalcitrate' is extremely rare and virtually unused in modern or historical English. It does not appear in major dictionaries such as Oxford or Merriam-Webster, likely because it is almost never used in practice; instead, the adjective form 'recalcitrant' and the noun 'recalcitrance' are standard. Therefore, this concept should not be included in the dictionary.
