acquit

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00010428-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: acquit|oewn-00010428-v
behave in a certain manner; show a certain behavior; conduct or comport oneself

Synset Examples

You should act like an adult
Don't behave like a fool
What makes her do this way?
The dog acts ferocious, but he is really afraid of people

Reason

This sense of 'acquit' as 'to behave or conduct oneself' is highly archaic and not commonly used in modern English. Modern usage of 'acquit' overwhelmingly refers to declaring someone not guilty in a court of law. The examples provided are more relevant to general verbs like 'act' or 'behave,' not the typical use of 'acquit.' Therefore, this sense does not warrant a dictionary entry.
