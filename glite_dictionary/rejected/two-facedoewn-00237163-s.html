having two faces — one looking to the future and one to the past

Synset Examples

Janus the two-faced god

Reason

This sense of 'two-faced' as 'having two faces — one looking to the future and one to the past' is specific to the literal description of Janus, a Roman god, and is not a general English word meaning. It does not correspond to a sense that would appear in an English-English dictionary (such as Oxford), which would instead focus on the figurative sense (deceitful or hypocritical). Therefore, it should not be included.
