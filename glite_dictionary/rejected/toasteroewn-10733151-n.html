someone who who drinks to the health of someone or the success of some venture

Reason

The sense of 'toaster' as 'someone who drinks to the health of someone or the success of some venture' is rare and outdated. In modern English, 'toaster' almost exclusively refers to the kitchen appliance, not a person making a toast. This usage would not typically appear as a distinct entry in a current Oxford-style dictionary.
