infinite

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01012335-a
Synset POS: a
Internal ID: infinite|oewn-01012335-a
(of verbs) not having tense, person, or number (as a participle or gerund or infinitive)

Synset Examples

infinite verb form

Reason

The concept 'infinite' as defined here refers specifically to a grammatical sense ('not having tense, person, or number'), which is not a standard or attested use of 'infinite' in English grammar. In English, the standard term for verb forms not marked for person, number, or tense is 'non-finite.' 'Infinite' in this sense is either an erroneous or exceedingly rare usage, so it should not be included in the dictionary.
