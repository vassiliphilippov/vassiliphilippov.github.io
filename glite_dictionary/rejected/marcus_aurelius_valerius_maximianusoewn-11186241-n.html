Reason

Such proper names with only the 'person' sense are not typically included in general English dictionaries like Oxford unless the individual is of major, universally recognized historical prominence (e.g., Julius Caesar, Napoleon). Maximianus, while a Roman Emperor, is not universally referenced enough in modern English to warrant dictionary entry status, unless included as a major historical figure. The description does not indicate unique lexical significance beyond being the name of a person.
