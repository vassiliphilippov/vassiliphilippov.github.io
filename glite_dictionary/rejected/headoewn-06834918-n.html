head

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06834918-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: head|oewn-06834918-n
a V-shaped mark at one end of an arrow pointer

Synset Examples

the point of the arrow was due north

Reason

This concept uses 'head' to refer to a general 'point' or 'end' of an arrow or pointer, which is too broad and does not represent a distinct English sense that is specific to the word 'head.' The core noun sense of 'head' in the context of arrows (meaning the tip or leading part) is already covered in dictionaries, but describing it as a 'V-shaped mark' at one end of an 'arrow pointer' is overly technical and not a regular dictionary meaning.
