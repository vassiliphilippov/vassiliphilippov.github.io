readiness

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05682428-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: readiness|oewn-05682428-n
(psychology) being temporarily ready to respond in a particular way

Synset Examples

the subjects' set led them to solve problems the familiar way and to overlook the simpler solution
his instructions deliberately gave them the wrong set

Reason

This sense of 'readiness' refers to a specific psychological concept where a person is temporarily predisposed to respond in a particular way, also known as 'set' in psychology. However, this specialized sense is not widely recognized or used in general English, and most dictionaries would not include this technical meaning of 'readiness' under its general senses. Therefore, it should not be included as a standalone entry in a general English-English dictionary.
