hale

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02510211-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: hale|oewn-02510211-v
to cause to do through pressure or necessity, by physical, moral or intellectual means

Synset Examples

She forced him to take a job in the city
He squeezed her for information

Reason

This sense of 'hale' (as a verb meaning to force or cause someone to do something by pressure or necessity) is extremely rare, archaic, and not found in contemporary or standard English usage. The usual verb 'hale' means to drag or pull forcibly, not to compel through pressure. For the described sense, standard verbs are 'force,' 'compel,' or 'coerce.' This meaning would not appear in a modern dictionary like Oxford, so it should be excluded.
