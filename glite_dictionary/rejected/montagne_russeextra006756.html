montagne russe

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: montagne russe|extra.006756
a situation with many highs and lows, akin to a rollercoaster experience

Synset Examples

Their relationship was a true montagne russe of ups and downs.
Life has been a montagne russe of emotions lately.
The stock market feels like a montagne russe this week.

Reason

The term 'montagne russe' is not established in standard English and does not appear in major English dictionaries, nor is it a widely adopted loanword. It is a French phrase occasionally used in English contexts as a stylistic or metaphorical variant for 'roller coaster.' English dictionaries would instead prefer the term 'roller coaster' for such a metaphorical meaning. Therefore, it should not be included as its own English dictionary entry.
