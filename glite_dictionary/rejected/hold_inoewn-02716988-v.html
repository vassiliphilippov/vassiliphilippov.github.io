Reason

The synset 'close in' is too general and does not directly correspond to the phrasal verb 'hold in'. The meaning here ('enclose' or 'surround') is already represented by more standard verbs like 'enclose' or 'surround', not by the phrasal verb 'hold in' in typical usage. Therefore, this sense does not warrant a separate dictionary entry for 'hold in' in this context.
