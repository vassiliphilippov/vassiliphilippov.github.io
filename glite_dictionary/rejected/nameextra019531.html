Synset Examples

She made a name for herself as a pro-shitter in the film industry.

Reason

This concept refers to the noun 'name' used in the idiomatic sense of 'make a name for oneself' (i.e., to establish a reputation). However, 'name' by itself with the sense 'established a reputation' does not stand alone in modern English dictionaries; it is typically found in fixed expressions such as 'make a name for oneself.' The noun 'name' as a standalone entry means 'a word by which a person or thing is known,' not 'reputation.' Therefore, this synset is too context-specific and not appropriate for an independent dictionary entry for 'name.'
