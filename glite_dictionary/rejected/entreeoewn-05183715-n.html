the right to enter

Reason

This sense of 'entree' referring to 'the right to enter' is highly formal and rarely used in modern English outside of very specific contexts. The main current meaning in English is 'main course' (in American English) or a literal French borrowing for 'entrance.' The 'right to enter' sense would not commonly appear as a separate Oxford Dictionary entry relevant for modern learners.
