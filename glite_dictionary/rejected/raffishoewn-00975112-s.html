Reason

The sense described as 'marked by up-to-dateness in dress and manners' aligns more closely with words like 'dapper' or 'jaunty', not with the primary and widely attested meaning of 'raffish'. 'Raffish' usually means 'slightly disreputable but attractive or charmingly unconventional', not simply stylish or up-to-date. Since the concept here represents an extremely rare or unattested usage and would not be expected to appear in Oxford or similar dictionaries under 'raffish', it should be excluded.
