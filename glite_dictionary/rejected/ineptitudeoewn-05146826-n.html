ineptitude

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05146826-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: ineptitude|oewn-05146826-n
having no qualities that would render it valuable or useful

Synset Examples

the drill sergeant's intent was to convince all the recruits of their worthlessness

Reason

The concept described here corresponds to the overly generic sense of 'ineptitude' as having no value or usefulness (i.e., 'worthlessness'), which is not the primary or common dictionary sense. The headword's main accepted meaning is 'lack of skill or ability.' This questionable sense does not merit a separate full entry, as it's not a distinct usage and would not appear as such in the Oxford Dictionary.
