Reason

The provided concept 'precipitant' is given as an adjective meaning 'done with very great haste and without due deliberation.' However, in standard English dictionaries (like Oxford or Merriam-Webster), 'precipitant' is almost exclusively used as a NOUN in chemistry ('a substance that causes precipitation'), not as an adjective meaning 'hasty' or 'rash.' The adjectival meaning is very rare, archaic, or not current in modern usage. The commonly used adjective with this sense is 'precipitate.' Therefore, this specific adjectival sense of 'precipitant' should not be included as a separate entry in a modern English-English dictionary.
