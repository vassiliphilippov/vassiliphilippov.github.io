Reason

The verb 'visit' in the sense of 'talk socially without exchanging too much information' is highly informal, non-standard, and rarely attested in modern English usage. This sense overlaps more naturally with phrasal verbs and idioms such as 'shoot the breeze,' 'chat,' or 'make small talk.' Such a usage of 'visit' would not typically appear as a separate entry or sense in a standard dictionary like the Oxford Dictionary. Including this sense may cause confusion, as 'visit' is overwhelmingly used to mean 'go to see' someone or a place, not 'chat socially.'
