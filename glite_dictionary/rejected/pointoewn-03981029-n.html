a wall socket

Reason

This sense of 'point' as 'a wall socket' is a regional or technical usage, most likely British or Australian English. However, in standard contemporary English dictionaries such as Oxford, 'point' as 'wall socket' is either very rare or not included as a main meaning. Instead, 'wall socket,' 'outlet,' or 'power point' are used. This use is too narrow and not widespread/general enough to warrant inclusion under 'point' in a general dictionary.
