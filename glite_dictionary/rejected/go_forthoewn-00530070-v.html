Reason

The sense 'go forth' as 'come out of' in this context is a very general, archaic or literary construction and not commonly found in contemporary English dictionaries under the phrasal verb 'go forth.' Modern usage rarely employs 'go forth' to mean 'to come out of' (e.g., as water comes forth from a spring); this is typically expressed as 'issue from' or 'emerge.' This sense is too obscure and uncommon for inclusion according to standard dictionary practices.
