Reason

This sense of 'unsealed' ('not established or confirmed') is highly specific, literary, and archaic; it is not a common or recognizable meaning in modern English dictionaries. The main, standard sense of 'unsealed' is 'not sealed' or 'having had a seal removed.' This abstract, metaphorical usage would not typically warrant a separate main entry in a general dictionary.
