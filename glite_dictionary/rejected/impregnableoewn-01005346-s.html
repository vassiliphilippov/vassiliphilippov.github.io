Reason

The synset described ('capable of conceiving') is an extremely rare and obsolete biological or medical sense of the word 'impregnable' (more commonly spelled 'impregnable'), which is almost never used in modern English in this way. The standard and universally understood meaning of 'impregnable' is 'unable to be captured or defeated' (especially of a fortress or position), which is included in major dictionaries. This rare, technical sense ('capable of being impregnated,' i.e., made pregnant) does not meet inclusion criteria for a learner's dictionary and is not current or widely relevant.
