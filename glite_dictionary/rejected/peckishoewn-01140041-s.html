peckish

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01140041-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: peckish|oewn-01140041-s
easily irritated or annoyed

Synset Examples

an incorrigibly fractious young man
not the least nettlesome of his countrymen

Reason

The synset provided for 'peckish' corresponds to the sense of being easily irritated or annoyed, but in standard modern usage, 'peckish' most commonly means 'slightly hungry.' The usage indicating 'irritable' is rare, archaic, and not found in contemporary dictionaries as a primary sense. Therefore, this sense should be excluded from a modern dictionary entry.
