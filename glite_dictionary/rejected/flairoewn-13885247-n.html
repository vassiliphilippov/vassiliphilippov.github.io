Reason

This concept refers to "flair" as a shape that spreads outward, based on a misreading or confusion with "flare" (which is the correct word for a shape that spreads outward). In standard English dictionaries, "flair" means talent or a special instinct, while "flare" describes something spreading outward (e.g., a flared skirt). This sense is not a valid entry for "flair" in an English dictionary and should be excluded.
