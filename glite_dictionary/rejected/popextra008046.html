pop

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: pop|extra.008046
express extreme joy or excitement

Synset Examples

He popped his cork when he won the lottery.
He popped his cork after the big game victory.

Reason

The sense described is for the idiom 'pop one's cork', not the word 'pop' in isolation. The headword 'pop' alone does not directly mean 'to express extreme joy or excitement'; this meaning applies to the idiom. Therefore, as 'pop' alone with this sense is not dictionary-worthy according to Oxford-like standards, it should be excluded.
