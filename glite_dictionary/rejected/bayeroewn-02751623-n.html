Bayer

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02751623-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: Bayer|oewn-02751623-n
the acetylated derivative of salicylic acid; used as an analgesic anti-inflammatory drug (trade names Bayer, Empirin, and St. Joseph) usually taken in tablet form; used as an antipyretic; slows clotting of the blood by poisoning platelets

Reason

The concept 'Bayer' here refers to a trade name (brand name) for acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin) rather than the word itself with an independent meaning. The dictionary would include 'aspirin', but 'Bayer' as a proprietary drug name is specific to branding and is not an independent lexical word or common noun. Therefore, it should not have a standalone entry except as a brand or proper noun referring to the company, which is not the sense described.
