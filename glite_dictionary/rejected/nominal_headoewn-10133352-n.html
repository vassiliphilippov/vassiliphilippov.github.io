Reason

This sense of 'nominal head' refers to a generic concept (a person serving as a cover/figurehead), but the phrase 'nominal head' itself is not commonly used with this specific sense in standard English. Instead, more common terms include 'front man' or 'figurehead'. The phrase 'nominal head' is chiefly used to mean 'a person who holds a title but has no real power', which would be included, but not this particular synset which is too general and not well-attested as a discrete entry.
