Gorki

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09030500-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: Gorki|oewn-09030500-n
an industrial city in the European part of Russia; birthplace of Maksim Gorky

Reason

This sense refers to 'Gorki' as an industrial city in Russia, but the current, widely recognized English usage is 'Nizhny Novgorod.' The name 'Gorki' was used from 1932 to 1990, so as a geographic name in general usage, it's historical and potentially ambiguous. Without the sense of a distinct, enduring usage in present-day general English, it does not meet the criteria for inclusion as a dictionary entry under 'Gorki.'
