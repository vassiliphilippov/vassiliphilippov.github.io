resembling starch

Reason

The concept 'amyloid' as 'resembling starch' is an attributive/adjective sense that is rarely encountered outside technical or historical scientific writings. In modern English, 'amyloid' is almost exclusively used as a noun/adjective referring to a protein deposit related to certain diseases, not its original meaning of 'starch-like.' This rare, obsolete sense would not appear as a separate entry in a general dictionary like Oxford. Therefore, it should be excluded.
