jut

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13917127-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: jut|oewn-13917127-n
something that bulges out or is protuberant or projects from its surroundings

Synset Examples

the gun in his pocket made an obvious bulge
the hump of a camel
he stood on the rocky prominence
the occipital protuberance was well developed
the bony excrescence between its horns

Reason

The synset refers to the concept of 'jut' as a noun meaning something that bulges out or projects from its surroundings. However, 'jut' is rarely or never used as a noun in contemporary English in this sense. The commonly used noun forms for this concept are 'bulge,' 'hump,' 'prominence,' or 'protuberance.' 'Jut' as a verb (to stick out) is dictionary-worthy, but the noun form is not in standard usage and would not appear as a main headword in Oxford or similar dictionaries.
