English navigator who explored the Arctic while searching for the Northwest Passage (1550-1605)

Reason

The synset is 'person', which is too general. Most English dictionaries do not include biographical entries for people whose notability is not extremely high, and this specific sense refers to a historical person (John Davis, English navigator), which is not handled in standard dictionaries unless overwhelmingly famous (e.g., Shakespeare, Napoleon).
