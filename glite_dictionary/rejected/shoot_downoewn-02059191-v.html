shoot down

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
Internal ID: shoot down|oewn-02059191-v
move quickly and violently

Synset Examples

The car tore down the street
He came charging into my office

Reason

The phrasal verb 'shoot down' in this sense ('move quickly and violently') is not a standard or commonly accepted usage in modern English; the examples actually correspond to verbs like 'tear down' or 'charge.' Including this sense for 'shoot down' would misrepresent its most accepted meanings, which are 'to bring down by shooting,' 'to reject,' etc. Therefore, this concept should be excluded from the dictionary.
