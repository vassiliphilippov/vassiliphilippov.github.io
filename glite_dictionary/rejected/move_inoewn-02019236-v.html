move in

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02019236-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: move in|oewn-02019236-v
of trains; move into (a station)

Synset Examples

The bullet train drew into Tokyo Station

Reason

The concept refers specifically to trains entering a station and is a subsense of the phrasal verb 'move in,' which in this context is a very narrow and technical collocation. The primary dictionary entry for 'move in' typically covers meanings such as 'to begin to inhabit a place' or 'to take action,' and this specialized railway sense is not widely recognized as a stand-alone dictionary sense in general English dictionaries. Thus, this concept is too narrow to merit its own entry.
