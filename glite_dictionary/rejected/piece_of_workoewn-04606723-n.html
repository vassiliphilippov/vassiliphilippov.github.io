piece of work

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04606723-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: piece of work|oewn-04606723-n
a product produced or accomplished through the effort or activity or agency of a person or thing

Synset Examples

it is not regarded as one of his more memorable works
the symphony was hailed as an ingenious work
he was indebted to the pioneering work of John Dewey
the work of an active imagination
erosion is the work of wind or water over time

Reason

The synset refers to a very general sense of 'work' (product or result of an effort), but the headword is 'piece of work', which normally has a different and more specific idiomatic meaning in English (often a person who is unusual or difficult). The general 'product/accomplishment' sense forms core meanings of 'work', not 'piece of work', so this concept does not represent an actual phrase used in this sense in modern English. Therefore, it should not be included as an entry for 'piece of work'.
