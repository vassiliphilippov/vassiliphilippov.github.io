perpendicular

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03975985-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: perpendicular|oewn-03975985-n
a cord from which a metal weight is suspended pointing directly to the earth's center of gravity; used to determine the vertical from a given point

Reason

The synset describes a highly technical and archaic use of 'perpendicular' as a physical object, which is rarely used or recognized in modern English outside of specialist historical or surveying contexts. In modern English, 'perpendicular' is primarily used as an adjective to describe lines or planes at right angles, and this noun usage is obsolete and too specialized for a general dictionary.
