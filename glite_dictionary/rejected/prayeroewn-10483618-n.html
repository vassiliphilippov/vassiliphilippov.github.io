someone who prays to God

Reason

The given sense of 'prayer' as 'someone who prays to God' is extremely rare and not current in modern English usage. The standard sense of 'prayer' as 'a spoken or mental communication with God' (i.e., the act or words of praying) is correct, but 'prayer' as 'a person who prays' does not appear in major dictionaries as a primary, contemporary noun and would not be included in the Oxford Dictionary. The corresponding term for a person is usually 'worshipper' or 'supplicant,' not 'prayer.' Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
