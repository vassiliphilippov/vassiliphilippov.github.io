St. Joseph

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02751623-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: St. Joseph|oewn-02751623-n
the acetylated derivative of salicylic acid; used as an analgesic anti-inflammatory drug (trade names Bayer, Empirin, and St. Joseph) usually taken in tablet form; used as an antipyretic; slows clotting of the blood by poisoning platelets

Reason

The synset refers to 'St. Joseph' as a trade name (brand) for acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin). While 'St. Joseph' is a historical brand name for aspirin, dictionary entries typically focus on the generic drug name 'aspirin' or relevant uses of the brand in language. Including individual brand names of common drugs, especially if their use is not widespread or genericized in language, is generally outside standard dictionary scope unless the brand has become a generic term (e.g., 'Xerox'). Therefore, it should not be included.
