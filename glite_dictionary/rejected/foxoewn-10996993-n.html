Reason

The synset describes 'Fox' as an individual person ('English statesman'), which is too general because its synset is simply 'person' or 'statesman'. Dictionary entries for proper nouns of this kind—unless extraordinarily famous and a household word—are typically excluded from standard dictionaries such as Oxford and appear in encyclopedias instead.
