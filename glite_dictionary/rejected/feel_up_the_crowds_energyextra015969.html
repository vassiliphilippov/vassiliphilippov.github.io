feel up the crowd's energy

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: feel up the crowd's energy|extra.015969
to gauge or sense the crowd's enthusiasm

Synset Examples

They wanted to feel up the crowd's energy before the concert.

Reason

The phrase 'feel up the crowd's energy' appears to be a nonstandard or mistaken wording for the much more common expression 'feel out the crowd's energy' or 'read the crowd's energy.' The verb 'feel up' is not standard in this sense (rather, it most commonly means to grope someone inappropriately); therefore, this concept does not merit inclusion as a dictionary entry. It would not appear in reputable dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary.
