canvass

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14537091-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: canvass|oewn-14537091-n
the setting for a narrative or fictional or dramatic account

Synset Examples

the crowded canvas of history
the movie demanded a dramatic canvas of sound

Reason

This concept should be excluded because the synset refers not to the main meaning of 'canvass,' but to a metaphorical or transferred sense of 'canvas' (meaning setting or background, especially in art or literature). The correct headword for this sense is 'canvas' (the fabric or, metaphorically, the scene or setting), not 'canvass.' 'Canvass' is a different word altogether (usually a verb meaning to solicit votes or opinions). This entry confuses the two homophones and thus is too general and improper for 'canvass.'
