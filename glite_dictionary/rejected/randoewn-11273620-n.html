Reason

The synset is 'person,' which is overly general and not specific to the headword. Although 'Rand' refers to Ayn Rand, a proper noun (person), this general sense does not warrant a dictionary entry unless her works, philosophy, or concepts derived from her are specifically included. Oxford-style dictionaries typically only include biographical entries for highly notable individuals, and they do not provide entries for synsets like 'person.'
