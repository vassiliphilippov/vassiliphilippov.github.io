Reason

The adverb 'viscerally' in the sense 'in the viscera' is extremely rare, technical, and not standard contemporary English usage. The common use of 'viscerally' is as an adverb meaning 'in a way that relates to deep inward feelings rather than intellect,' not literally 'in the viscera.' The literal, medical sense 'in the viscera' would be covered by technical medical language, but is not attested as a current or general-use dictionary entry. Therefore, it should not be included as a distinct entry in a general dictionary.
