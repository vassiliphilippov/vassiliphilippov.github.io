Synset Examples

Reason

The synset's primary meaning is 'quietly in concealment,' but the headword 'out of sight' does not naturally or idiomatically mean this in English. 'Out of sight' more commonly means 'not visible,' not specifically 'in concealment.' The example 'he lay doggo' does not correspond to standard usage of 'out of sight.' Therefore, this concept is too general or mismatched, and should not be included as a dictionary entry for 'out of sight' in this sense.
