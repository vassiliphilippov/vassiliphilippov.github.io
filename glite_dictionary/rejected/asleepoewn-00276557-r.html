Reason

This concept refers to an archaic or poetic use of 'asleep' meaning 'dead', which is not commonly understood or used in modern English. Such metaphorical uses are now rare and typically only found in literature or religious texts. The standard and relevant senses of 'asleep' as 'sleeping, not awake' should be included, but not this specific extended usage.
