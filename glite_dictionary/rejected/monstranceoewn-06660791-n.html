Synset POS: n

proof by a process of argument or a series of proposition proving an asserted conclusion

Reason

This sense of 'monstrance' as 'proof by a process of argument' is not used in contemporary or standard English and would not appear as a headword with this meaning in modern dictionaries. 'Monstrance' is almost exclusively used to mean a religious vessel for displaying the consecrated host in Christian tradition. The philosophical or logical sense is obscure or obsolete and not relevant for a general English dictionary.
