straightened out

Reason

The concept 'loosened' with the sense 'straightened out' is not correct, as 'loosened' does not mean 'straightened out' in standard English usage. 'Loosened' is the past participle of 'loosen', which means to make something less tight or firm. Since this sense is erroneous and would not appear in a reputable dictionary like Oxford, it should be filtered out.
