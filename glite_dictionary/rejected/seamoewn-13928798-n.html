seam

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13928798-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: seam|oewn-13928798-n
a slight depression or fold in the smoothness of a surface

Synset Examples

his face has many lines
ironing gets rid of most wrinkles

Reason

The concept described here refers to a very general sense: 'a slight depression or fold in the smoothness of a surface.' This is too broad and generic, and is not a distinct or usual meaning of 'seam' found in standard English dictionaries. The standard meaning of 'seam' relates to a line where two pieces of fabric or material are joined, not simply any depression or fold. Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
