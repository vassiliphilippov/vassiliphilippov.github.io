Reason

The synset 'call to mind' connected to 'bring round' is not an idiomatic or standard meaning of the phrasal verb 'bring round.' In standard English, 'bring round' typically means to persuade, to make someone conscious again, or to bring something to someone's place. The sense 'call to mind' is standard for 'bring back' or 'remind.' Therefore, this concept should be excluded as it does not represent a meaningful, widely accepted sense of 'bring round.'
