fume

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00068398-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: fume|oewn-00068398-v
be wet with sweat or blood, as of one's face

Reason

This sense of 'fume'—meaning 'to be wet with sweat or blood'—is extremely rare, archaic, and not attested in modern English usage. It would not be included in standard dictionaries such as the Oxford Dictionary as an active meaning. 'Fume' in current English means to emit smoke, gas, or vapor, or to show anger. This obsolete sense is not relevant for general learners and should be excluded.
