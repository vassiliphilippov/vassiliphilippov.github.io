a young tree or plant

Synset Examples

The garden is full of young saplings.

Reason

The concept 'young sapling' is a descriptive noun phrase consisting of 'young' (an adjective) and 'sapling' (a noun), where 'sapling' already means a young tree or plant. The phrase is tautological and would not be included as a separate entry in a standard dictionary like Oxford; only 'sapling' is a dictionary headword.
