rail

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01505900-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: rail|oewn-01505900-v
lay with rails

Synset Examples

hundreds of miles were railed out here

Reason

This sense ('rail' meaning 'lay with rails') is highly technical, rare, and not widely recognized as a common English verb. It is not attested in standard dictionaries like Oxford. The usual verb for installing rails is 'to lay' or 'to lay rail/rails,' not 'to rail.' Therefore, this concept need not be included in the general English dictionary.
