Reason

The verb 'overleap' in the sense of 'leave undone or leave out' is extremely rare and obsolete in modern English. In current usage, 'overleap' primarily means 'to leap over something.' The sense described here does not appear in major modern dictionaries like Oxford or Merriam-Webster, and the provided examples actually use the verbs 'miss' or 'leave out,' which are the standard expressions. Therefore, this concept should be filtered out.
