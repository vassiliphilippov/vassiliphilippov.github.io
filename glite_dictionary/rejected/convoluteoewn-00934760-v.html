convolute

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00934760-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: convolute|oewn-00934760-v
practice sophistry; change the meaning of or be vague about in order to mislead or deceive

Synset Examples

Don't twist my words

Reason

The sense of 'convolute' as a verb meaning 'practice sophistry; change the meaning of or be vague about in order to mislead or deceive' is not standard in modern English. This usage is extremely rare or unattested in reputable dictionaries such as Oxford or Merriam-Webster; the standard dictionary sense is 'to coil up' or 'to make something complex or twisted.' The example sentence ('Don't twist my words') corresponds to the verb 'twist' or 'distort,' not to 'convolute.' Therefore, this sense should not be included in the dictionary.
