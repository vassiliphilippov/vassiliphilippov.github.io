Reason

This sense of 'clemency' (meaning good weather with comfortable temperatures) is archaic and extremely rare in modern English. The overwhelming and current usage of 'clemency' refers to mercy or leniency, especially in legal contexts. This weather-related sense would not appear in a standard modern dictionary such as Oxford.
