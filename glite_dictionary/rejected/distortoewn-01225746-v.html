form into a spiral shape

Synset Examples

The cord is all twisted

Reason

This specific sense of 'distort' as 'form into a spiral shape' is already more accurately captured by more common verbs such as 'twist' or 'coil.' While 'distort' can mean to deform or change the shape of something in general, its primary modern usages refer to making something misshapen or giving a false or misleading account, not specifically to forming a spiral shape. Therefore, this sense is too narrow and infrequent to warrant a separate dictionary entry.
