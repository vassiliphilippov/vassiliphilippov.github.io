head-the-ball

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: head-the-ball|extra.004766
to take initiative or lead in discussions or responsibilities

Synset Examples

She always tries to head-the-ball in meetings.
To excel, you must head-the-ball in your responsibilities.

Reason

The phrase 'head-the-ball' as described here (meaning to take initiative or lead in discussions or responsibilities) is not recognized as standard, widely-used English. It does not appear in major dictionaries with this meaning. Also, the phrase 'head-the-ball' is more commonly known in Irish or British slang as an insult meaning a foolish person. Therefore, this sense should not be included in a standard English dictionary.
