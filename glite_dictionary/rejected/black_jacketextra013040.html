a jacket that is dark in color

Synset Examples

He bought a blacky jacket for the winter.

Reason

The synset 'black jacket' simply denotes a jacket that is black in color, which is a compositional phrase (adjective + noun) and does not have a distinct meaning beyond its parts. Such color+noun phrases are too general to merit an individual entry in a dictionary like Oxford, unless they refer to a specific established concept (e.g. 'Black Jack' the game, or 'black tie'). Therefore, this phrase should not be included.
