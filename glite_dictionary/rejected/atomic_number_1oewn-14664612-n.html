atomic number 1

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14664612-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: atomic number 1|oewn-14664612-n
a nonmetallic univalent element that is normally a colorless and odorless highly flammable diatomic gas; the simplest and lightest and most abundant element in the universe

Reason

The headword 'atomic number 1' represents a technical designation (the atomic number of hydrogen) rather than a commonly used term. The proper dictionary entry for the described sense is 'hydrogen,' which is already a lexical headword. 'Atomic number 1' on its own is too technical and would not appear as a main entry in a standard English dictionary.
