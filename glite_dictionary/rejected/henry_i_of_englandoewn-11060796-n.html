King of England from 1100 to 1135; youngest son of William the Conqueror; conquered Normandy in 1106 (1068-1135)

Reason

Although 'Henry I of England' was a specific historical figure, its synset is 'person', which is too general. English dictionaries usually do not include biographical entries for specific people, except for very select cases. His name functions as a proper noun referring to an individual, not as a general-English headword.
