Kong the Master

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10926574-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: Kong the Master|oewn-10926574-n
Chinese philosopher whose ideas and sayings were collected after his death and became the basis of a philosophical doctrine known a Confucianism (circa 551-478 BC)

Reason

The synset is for a person ('Chinese philosopher'), and the headword 'Kong the Master' refers to Confucius. Dictionary entries as in the Oxford Dictionary would use 'Confucius' as the standard entry; 'Kong the Master' is not widely recognized or used in English to refer to this philosopher, and the synset is primarily identifying the person, not a unique lexical concept. This does not warrant a separate dictionary entry.
