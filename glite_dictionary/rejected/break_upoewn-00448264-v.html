Reason

This concept uses the phrase 'break up' in a very technical or extended sense ('cause to go into a solution'), which is not a common usage of this phrasal verb in standard or even scientific English dictionaries. The usual verb for this meaning is 'dissolve.' 'Break up' in the sense of dissolving a substance in a liquid is not idiomatic or standard, so it should be excluded.
