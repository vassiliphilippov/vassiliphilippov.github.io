downfall

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11515038-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: downfall|oewn-11515038-n
the falling to earth of any form of water (rain or snow or hail or sleet or mist)

Reason

Although 'downfall' in general is a valid dictionary word, this specific sense (meaning 'any form of precipitation') is archaic and rarely, if ever, used in modern English. It would not appear as a current meaning in major dictionaries like Oxford. The contemporary and primary meaning of 'downfall' is 'a loss of power, prosperity, or status.' Therefore, this precipitation sense should not be kept.
