Reason

The concept uses 'fall' in the sense of 'the fall of night' as an idiomatic phrase meaning 'the onset of night' or 'twilight.' However, this sense of 'fall' is not a standalone noun commonly listed in modern English dictionaries for 'the time of day immediately following sunset'; it's only found in the set phrase 'the fall of night.' Thus, this usage is too narrow and not directly associated with the headword 'fall' as a dictionary entry by itself.
