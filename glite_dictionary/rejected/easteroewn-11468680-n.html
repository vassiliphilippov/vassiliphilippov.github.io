Reason

The synset refers to 'easter' as a wind from the east, which is an extremely archaic or obscure usage not found in most contemporary English dictionaries. The common and dictionary-relevant sense of 'Easter' is as a religious festival; this specific sense would not be included in modern general dictionaries.
