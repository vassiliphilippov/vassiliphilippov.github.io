err

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01929647-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: err|oewn-01929647-v
wander from a direct course or at random

Synset Examples

The child strayed from the path and her parents lost sight of her
don't drift from the set course

Reason

The sense of 'err' as 'to wander from a direct course or at random' is extremely archaic and almost never used in this way in modern English. In contemporary dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary, 'err' typically means 'to make a mistake.' The meaning of physically wandering from a path does not appear in standard modern usage, so it should be excluded.
