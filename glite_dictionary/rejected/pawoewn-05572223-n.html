paw

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05572223-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: paw|oewn-05572223-n
the (prehensile) extremity of the superior limb

Synset Examples

he had the hands of a surgeon
he extended his mitt

Reason

This sense refers generally to a 'prehensile extremity of the superior limb,' which is too broad and refers to general categories like 'hand.' The given sense ('paw' as 'hand') is also nonstandard and only used informally/metaphorically. The standard sense of 'paw' referring to the foot of an animal is the common dictionary entry; this sense is more about using 'paw' (informally or as slang) to refer to a human hand, which is redundant with 'hand' and not a standalone synset worthy of its own dictionary entry unless specifically marked as a slang term. This concept is too general and not sufficiently distinct for a main dictionary entry.
