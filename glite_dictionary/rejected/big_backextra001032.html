WordNet ID: n/a

a muscular or strong back

Synset Examples

He has a big back from years of lifting weights.
I admire his big back; he works hard at the gym.

Reason

The sense described ('big back' meaning a muscular or strong back) is a compositional phrase and not a set expression or distinct lexeme. It is simply the adjective 'big' modifying the noun 'back', and could refer literally to the size or muscularity of the back in context. Such adjective-noun combinations are generally not included as separate entries in major dictionaries unless they are idiomatic or have a fixed/non-compositional meaning. 'Big back' does not meet these criteria.
