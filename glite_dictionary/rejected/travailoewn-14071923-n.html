travail

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14071923-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: travail|oewn-14071923-n
concluding state of pregnancy; from the onset of contractions to the birth of a child

Synset Examples

she was in labor for six hours

Reason

The synset refers to 'travail' meaning 'labor' in the context of childbirth, but in modern English, 'travail' is very rarely used for this meaning. The common term is 'labor' (or 'labour' in British English). 'Travail' as a noun is primarily used for 'hard work' or 'ordeal', and the childbirth-specific sense is archaic and exceedingly rare, making it unnecessary for most dictionary users. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
