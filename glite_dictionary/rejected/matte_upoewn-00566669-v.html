matte up

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00566669-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: matte up|oewn-00566669-v
change texture so as to become matted and felt-like

Synset Examples

The fabric felted up after several washes

Reason

The phrase 'matte up' as a verb meaning to become matted or felt-like is extremely rare and not attested in standard or widely-used dictionaries such as Oxford. The example given ('The fabric felted up after several washes') suggests 'felted up' is more idiomatic. 'Matte up' is not commonly used in this context, so it should be excluded from a general English-English dictionary.
