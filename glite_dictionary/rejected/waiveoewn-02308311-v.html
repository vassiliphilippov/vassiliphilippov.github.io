waive

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02308311-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: waive|oewn-02308311-v
lose (something) or lose the right to (something) by some error, offense, or crime

Synset Examples

you've forfeited your right to name your successor
forfeited property

Reason

This concept expresses a sense related to 'waive' being defined as to lose (something) or lose the right to (something) by error, offense, or crime, but the actual English verb 'waive' means to voluntarily refrain from exercising a right, not to lose it through error or offense. The sense described and its examples ('forfeited property') refer to 'forfeit', not 'waive'. Therefore, this sense does not correspond to the standard meaning of 'waive' and should not be included.
