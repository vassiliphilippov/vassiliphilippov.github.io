arouse

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00018519-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: arouse|oewn-00018519-v
stop sleeping

Synset Examples

She woke up to the sound of the alarm clock

Reason

This sense of 'arouse' (meaning 'stop sleeping' or 'wake up') is not commonly used in modern English; instead, 'arouse' is most often used in the sense of 'stimulate' or 'provoke' emotions, interest, or sexual feelings. The usage 'arouse someone from sleep' is archaic or very rare, and would not merit a separate entry in a contemporary dictionary for this meaning.
