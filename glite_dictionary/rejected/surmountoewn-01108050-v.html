surmount

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01108050-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: surmount|oewn-01108050-v
be or do something to a greater degree

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Reason

This sense of 'surmount' is not correct for typical English dictionaries. In standard usage, 'surmount' means to overcome or prevail over (an obstacle, difficulty, etc.). The synset meaning here ('be or do something to a greater degree') refers to verbs like surpass, exceed, outdo, or outperform, which are different words. Therefore, this sense should not be included as 'surmount' in the dictionary.
