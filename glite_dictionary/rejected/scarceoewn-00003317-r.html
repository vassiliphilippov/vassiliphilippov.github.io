Reason

The adverbial sense of 'scarce' meaning 'almost not' is no longer in common use and is considered archaic or obsolete; the modern equivalents are 'scarcely' or 'barely'. This adverbial use of 'scarce' does not appear in contemporary dictionaries as an active entry and would not be included in a current Oxford-style dictionary. Instead, 'scarcely' covers this sense.
