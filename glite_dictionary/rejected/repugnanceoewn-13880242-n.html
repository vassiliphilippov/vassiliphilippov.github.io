repugnance

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13880242-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: repugnance|oewn-13880242-n
the relation between propositions that cannot both be true at the same time

Reason

The concept described is from formal logic (mutual incompatibility of propositions), but this sense of 'repugnance' is archaic and not used in contemporary English except possibly in highly specialized philosophical or logical contexts. Modern dictionaries do not include this meaning, focusing on the primary meaning of 'repugnance' as a feeling of disgust or strong dislike. Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
