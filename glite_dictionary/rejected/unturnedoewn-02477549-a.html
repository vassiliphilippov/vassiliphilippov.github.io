Reason

The adjective 'unturned' is only used in set phrases (e.g., 'no stone unturned') and is not independently meaningful outside of such expressions. It is not commonly used as a general adjective; its use is almost entirely idiomatic. Therefore, as a standalone term, it would not be included in a dictionary like Oxford except possibly as part of the idiom 'leave no stone unturned'.
