Synset Examples

the trailer rode piggyback across the country

Reason

The sense 'pig-a-back' as an adverb specifically meaning 'on a railroad flatcar' is too technical and not widely used in general English. The general meaning 'piggyback' (carried on the back or shoulders) is relevant, but this technical adverbial sense does not merit a separate dictionary entry for most learners. The expression is rare and would not appear as such in a standard English dictionary like the Oxford Dictionary.
