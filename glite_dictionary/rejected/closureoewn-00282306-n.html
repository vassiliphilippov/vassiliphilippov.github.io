closure

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00282306-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: closure|oewn-00282306-n
approaching a particular destination; a coming closer; a narrowing of a gap

Synset Examples

the ship's rapid rate of closing gave them little time to avoid a collision

Reason

This sense of 'closure' refers to the process of approaching a destination or narrowing a gap, which is a rare and highly technical usage, found mainly in nautical or military jargon. In general English dictionaries, 'closure' overwhelmingly refers to the act of closing or the state of being closed (e.g., closing of a road, emotional closure). This technical sense is too specific and uncommon for standard dictionary inclusion.
