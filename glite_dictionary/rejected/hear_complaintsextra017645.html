to listen to grievances or issues raised

Synset Examples

Reason

The phrase 'hear complaints' is a compositional and literal usage of the words 'hear' and 'complaints'; it does not have a distinct lexicalized meaning or idiomatic usage. It simply means to listen to complaints, which can be understood directly from the meanings of 'hear' and 'complaint'. Such phrases are typically not included in a quality English-English dictionary like Oxford, as there is nothing idiomatic or specialized about their meaning.
