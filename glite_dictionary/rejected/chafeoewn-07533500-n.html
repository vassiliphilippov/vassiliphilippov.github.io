Reason

This sense of 'chafe' as a noun meaning 'anger produced by some annoying irritation' is obsolete or extremely rare, and is not commonly encountered in modern English dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary. The primary and recognized uses of 'chafe' are as a verb (relating to rubbing) or a noun (referring to the act/result of rubbing). Therefore, this concept should not be included as a standard entry.
