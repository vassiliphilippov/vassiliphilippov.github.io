an expression of dismissal or rejection, often used in a dismissive context

Synset Examples

He always says, "chuck you Farley" when things go wrong.
After the argument, she exclaimed, "chuck you Farley!"
When he heard the news, he just muttered, "chuck you Farley."

Reason

The phrase 'chuck you' in this context is not an established English lexeme with independent usage; the examples all refer to the fixed, informal, and culturally-specific phrase 'chuck you Farley,' which is a colloquial dismissive expression. As 'chuck you' alone does not have dictionary-level independent meaning, it should not be included.
