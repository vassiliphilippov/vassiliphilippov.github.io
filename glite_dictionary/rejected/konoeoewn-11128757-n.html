Japanese statesman who set Japan's expansionist policies and formed an alliance with Germany and Italy (1891-1945)

Reason

'Konoe' refers to a Japanese statesman (a specific person's surname) and as such is a proper noun indicating an individual. Dictionary entries for general dictionaries like Oxford typically do not include biographical proper nouns unless the individual is extremely significant and known universally by a single name (such as 'Shakespeare' or 'Napoleon'), which 'Konoe' is not widely recognized as in English usage. Therefore, this concept is excluded.
