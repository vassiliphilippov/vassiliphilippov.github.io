the cardinal number that is the sum of five and one

Reason

This sense of 'sestet' as the cardinal number that is the sum of five and one is not accurate and does not correspond to how the word 'sestet' is used in modern English. 'Sestet' properly refers to a stanza or group of six lines in poetry, especially the last six lines of a sonnet, and not to the number six itself. The synset here appears to be too general or incorrect for the headword.
