Italian anatomist who first described the Fallopian tubes (1523-1562)

Reason

The synset is used as a proper noun referring specifically to an individual person (Gabriello Fallopius), whose primary sense is simply 'person'. Such biographical entries are typically excluded from general English dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary, unless the name is eponymous for something else (e.g., 'Fallopian tube' is included, but not Fallopius as a headword).
