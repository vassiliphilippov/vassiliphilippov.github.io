Reason

The synset definition here describes "heady" as 'marked by the exercise of good judgment or common sense in practical matters,' which is actually the sense of 'judicious' or 'wise,' not commonly associated with 'heady.' In standard English usage, 'heady' refers to something having a strong effect (e.g., intoxicating, exciting, stimulating), not to good judgment. This synset does not align with a recognized meaning of 'heady' in contemporary English dictionaries and should be excluded.
