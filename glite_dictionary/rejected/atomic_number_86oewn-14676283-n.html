atomic number 86

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14676283-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: atomic number 86|oewn-14676283-n
a radioactive gaseous element formed by the disintegration of radium; the heaviest of the inert gasses; occurs naturally (especially in areas over granite) and is considered a hazard to health

Reason

The concept 'atomic number 86' is too general and refers to the chemical element radon. A proper dictionary entry would be under 'radon,' not its atomic number, as Oxford and similar dictionaries do not include chemical elements by their atomic numbers but rather by their names.
