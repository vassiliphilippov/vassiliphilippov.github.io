posit

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01578506-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: posit|oewn-01578506-v
put (something somewhere) firmly

Synset Examples

She posited her hand on his shoulder
deposit the suitcase on the bench
fix your eyes on this spot

Reason

The verb 'posit' in standard contemporary English is primarily used in the sense of 'to assume or suggest as a fact or principle,' not to mean 'put something somewhere firmly.' This physical placement sense is archaic, rare, or possibly confused with other verbs such as 'place,' 'deposit,' or 'pose.' Therefore, this specific sense should not be included in a modern English-English dictionary.
