may not leave; expressing prohibition

Synset Examples

She warned that we mayn't leave before the meeting ends.

Reason

The phrase 'may not leave' in this context reflects a general use of 'may not' to express prohibition, rather than a fixed phrase or idiom. This is a compositional phrase (modal 'may' + 'not' + verb) rather than a lexical entry in its own right. As such, it would not appear as a separate entry in a standard dictionary such as the Oxford Dictionary, which instead covers 'may' as a modal verb and gives its relevant senses (permission and prohibition).
