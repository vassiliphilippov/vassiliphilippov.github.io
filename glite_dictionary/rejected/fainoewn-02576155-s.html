Reason

The sense 'fain' as 'having made preparations' does not reflect standard or modern usage, and is not relevant as an adjective meaning 'prepared' in contemporary English. 'Fain' is better known in an archaic sense meaning 'willing' or 'gladly,' not 'prepared.' Thus, this sense is too obscure and misleading for inclusion in a modern dictionary.
