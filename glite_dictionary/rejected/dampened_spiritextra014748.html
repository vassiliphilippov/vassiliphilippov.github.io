Synset Examples

Reason

The phrase 'dampened spirit' is an ordinary combination of 'dampened' (past participle of the verb 'dampen') and 'spirit', meaning 'weakened morale'. It is not an established idiom, compound, or lexical phrase that merits its own dictionary entry. Instead, this meaning is covered by the dictionary entry for 'dampen' (as in 'to dampen sb's spirits'). Therefore, it should not be included as a separate entry.
