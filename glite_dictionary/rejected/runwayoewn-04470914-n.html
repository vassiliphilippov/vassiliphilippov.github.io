runway

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04470914-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: runway|oewn-04470914-n
a bar or pair of parallel bars of rolled steel making the railway along which railroad cars or other vehicles can roll

Reason

This sense of 'runway' refers to a technical usage meaning a rail or track bar, which is very uncommon. In modern and general usage, 'runway' refers almost exclusively to the strip at an airport for aircraft takeoff and landing or as a passage for models. The sense described is outdated and not sufficiently relevant for inclusion as a discrete dictionary entry for the general learner. The standard dictionary would not include this technical, now-archaic usage.
