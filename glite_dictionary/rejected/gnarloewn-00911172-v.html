gnarl

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00911172-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: gnarl|oewn-00911172-v
make complaining remarks or noises under one's breath

Synset Examples

she grumbles when she feels overworked

Reason

This sense of 'gnarl' (to make complaining remarks or noises under one's breath) is not standard usage in contemporary English and does not appear in major dictionaries like Oxford in this meaning. The standard meaning of 'gnarl' as a verb refers to making twisted or knotted shapes, especially in reference to wood. The sense here is a rare or mistaken attribution, so it should not be included.
