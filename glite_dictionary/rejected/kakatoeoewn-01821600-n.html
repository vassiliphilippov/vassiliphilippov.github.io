a genus of Psittacidae containing the cockatoos, found from the Philippines and Wallacea east to the Solomon Islands and south to Australia (Kakatoe, Cacatua)

Reason

The concept 'Kakatoe' refers to a genus classification and is a scientific/technical term (taxonomic name). The commonly used term in English is 'cockatoo.' 'Kakatoe' is not used as an English headword and would not be found as an entry in standard English dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary except, perhaps, as a scientific or taxonomic synonym, which is outside the scope. Therefore, it should be excluded.
