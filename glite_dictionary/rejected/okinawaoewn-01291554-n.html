Okinawa

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01291554-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: Okinawa|oewn-01291554-n
a campaign in the closing days of World War II in the Pacific (April to June 1945); in savage close-quarter fighting United States marines and regular army troops took the island from the Japanese; considered the greatest victory of the Pacific campaign for the Americans

Reason

The sense described refers to the historical 'Battle of Okinawa,' but the headword given is 'Okinawa,' which is primarily a place name (island, prefecture) rather than the specific battle. Place names without a more specific or unique usage beyond being a proper noun or general geographic term are generally excluded from dictionaries like Oxford beyond brief gazetteer use. The battle itself would appear as 'Battle of Okinawa, n.'
