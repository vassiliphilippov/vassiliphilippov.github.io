as a signal

Synset Examples

a term that is used signally rather than symbolically

Reason

The sense 'signally' meaning 'as a signal' is not standard or commonly used in contemporary English. Dictionaries (including Oxford) do not record this adverb in this sense, and it appears to be overly technical or rare, possibly even misconstrued. The commonly accepted adverb 'signally' means 'in a remarkable or significant manner,' which is separate. Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
