Reason

The synset describes 'betting' as an adjective meaning preoccupied with games of chance, but this usage is extremely rare and not standard in modern English. 'Betting' as an adjective with this specific sense does not appear in Oxford or major dictionaries. The commonly recognized senses of 'betting' are as a noun ('the action of betting') or, rarely, as an attributive adjective ('betting shop'), not as describing someone's character or lifestyle. Therefore, this sense does not merit a separate entry.
