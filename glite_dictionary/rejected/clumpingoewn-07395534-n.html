the sound of a horse's hoofs hitting on a hard surface

Reason

The concept describes a very specific sense of 'clumping' (the sound of a horse's hoofs), but in common English usage, 'clumping' typically means forming into clumps or moving with heavy steps; it's not a standard noun referring specifically to hoof sounds. This sense is too narrow and not widely recognized in general English dictionaries like the Oxford. Therefore, this concept is filtered out.
