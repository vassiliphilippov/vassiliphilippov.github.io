put on top

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: put on top|extra.021700
to place something above another

Synset Examples

He suggested we put the bottom rail on top to fix the fence.

Reason

The synset 'put on top' refers to the very general and compositional meaning of physically placing one item above another, which is directly inferred from the meanings of 'put' and 'on top'. It is not an idiom or set phrase with a unique or non-obvious meaning, nor is it an established phrasal verb. Such constructions do not warrant a separate entry in an English-English dictionary like Oxford.
