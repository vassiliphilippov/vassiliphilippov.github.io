purloin intellectual property

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: purloin intellectual property|extra.021643
to steal or use someone else's creative work

Synset Examples

It's illegal to purloin someone else's intellectual property.

Reason

The phrase 'purloin intellectual property' is a free combination of the verb 'purloin' (meaning to steal) and the noun phrase 'intellectual property'; it is not a set phrase or idiom. Dictionaries would include 'purloin' and 'intellectual property' separately, but not the combination. Therefore, this concept should be excluded from the dictionary.
