contact action

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13465304-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: contact action|oewn-13465304-n
acceleration of a chemical reaction induced by the presence of material that is chemically unchanged at the end of the reaction

Synset Examples

of the top 50 commodity chemicals, 30 are created directly by catalysis and another 6 are made from raw materials that are catalytically produced

Reason

The concept 'contact action' in this sense refers to the process of catalysis in chemistry. However, the commonly used and accepted term in both scientific and general English is 'catalysis', not 'contact action'. 'Contact action' as a term for catalysis is archaic or extremely rare and would not be included in a contemporary English-English dictionary such as Oxford. Thus, it should be excluded.
