from off of the floor

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: from off of the floor|extra.016642
to retrieve something from the ground

Synset Examples

Can you get that toy from off of the floor?

Reason

The phrase 'from off of the floor' is an informal, regional variant of 'off the floor' or 'from the floor,' and is not treated as a standard lexical entry in established dictionaries like Oxford. Its meaning is fully compositional and not idiomatic or fixed; it's a combination of prepositions that would not have a separate entry. It is therefore too general for a dictionary entry.
