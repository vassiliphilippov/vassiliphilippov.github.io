battery

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00989949-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: battery|oewn-00989949-n
the heavy fire of artillery to saturate an area rather than hit a specific target

Synset Examples

they laid down a barrage in front of the advancing troops
the shelling went on for hours without pausing

Reason

The synset given refers to the general concept of concentrated artillery fire ('barrage' or 'shelling'), not specifically to the headword 'battery.' While 'battery' can refer to a group of artillery guns, using it to refer to the heavy fire itself is extremely rare or nonexistent in contemporary English. The canonical Oxford-style meaning for 'battery' in a military context is a group of guns or missile launchers operated together, not the act of firing. This synset is too general and not relevant for 'battery' as a headword.
