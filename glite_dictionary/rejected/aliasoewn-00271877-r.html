Synset Examples

Reason

The concept 'alias' as an adverb meaning 'as known or named at another time or place' is not in contemporary English use as an adverb; instead, 'alias' is used as a noun ('an assumed name'). This adverbial sense is archaic/legal and extremely rare in general usage, so it would not typically appear in a modern dictionary. The normal dictionary entry is for 'alias' as a noun.
