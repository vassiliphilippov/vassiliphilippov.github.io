Reason

This concept refers to 'Chablis' as a town in France, and the synset is just 'town' or 'place,' which is too general. While 'Chablis' is notable as a wine region and the name of a type of wine, the town as a general place is not included in most English-English dictionaries outside of a gazetteer context. Therefore, it does not meet the criteria for inclusion as a dictionary entry.
