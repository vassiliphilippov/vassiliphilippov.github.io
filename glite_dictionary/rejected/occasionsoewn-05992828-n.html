Reason

The synset definition, 'something you have to do,' is extremely obscure and does not correspond to the standard or widely recognized usage of 'occasions' as a noun. This sense is both poorly attested and not specific to the headword; it would not be included in a standard English dictionary such as Oxford. The typical meaning of 'occasion' relates to an event or a special time, not 'something you have to do.' Therefore, this sense should not be included in the dictionary.
