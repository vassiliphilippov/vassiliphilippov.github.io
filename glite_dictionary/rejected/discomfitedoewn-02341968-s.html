Reason

The sense 'discomfited' here refers to a general state of being 'disappointingly unsuccessful', corresponding to participles like 'thwarted', 'frustrated', and 'foiled', but 'discomfited' is not widely or commonly used in modern English in this exact sense compared to 'disappointed' or 'frustrated'. Moreover, the headword 'discomfited' would more usefully be covered under 'discomfit' as a verb or its usual adjective/participle meanings (e.g., 'embarrassed' or 'confused'). The meaning here is too broad/general and doesn't precisely match the common dictionary definition of 'discomfited', so including this participle form with this sense is unnecessary and could mislead learners.
