Synset Examples

Reason

The synset refers to a specific, limited adverbial use of 'pickaback' in the sense of 'on a railroad flatcar', which is both highly technical and rare. The more general and common meanings of 'pickaback' (adv. or n., meaning carried on the back/shoulders) should be included, but this technical adverbial use is too narrow and would not appear as a standalone entry in a general-purpose dictionary like Oxford. Additionally, the synset seems to conflate 'piggyback' (rail transport sense) and 'pickaback', and 'piggyback' is the more standard term for freight transport by rail. Therefore, this particular sense should be excluded.
