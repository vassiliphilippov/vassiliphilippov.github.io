a territorial military unit

Reason

The sense described, 'a territorial military unit', refers to a specific use of 'territorial' as a noun. In general English dictionaries, 'territorial' is most commonly listed as an adjective. The noun sense is specialized and almost exclusively used in British military contexts to describe a member or unit of the Territorial Army. Since this is an extremely specialized meaning that is rarely encountered outside military or historical contexts, and not general enough to meet main dictionary inclusion, it is best omitted for a general English-English dictionary. The main adjective sense ('related to territory') would be included, but not the noun sense as described here.
