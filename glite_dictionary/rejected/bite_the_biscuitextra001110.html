bite the biscuit

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: bite the biscuit|extra.001110
to face a difficult situation bravely or act decisively

Synset Examples

She decided to bite the biscuit and accept the new job offer.
We need to bite the biscuit and finish this project.
I guess it's time to bite the biscuit and move on.

Reason

The phrase 'bite the biscuit' is not a widely recognized or established idiom in standard English. The commonly accepted idiom is 'bite the bullet' with this meaning. 'Bite the biscuit' does not appear in Oxford or other major dictionaries and seems to be a mistaken or less common variant.
