troika

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13766184-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: troika|oewn-13766184-n
the cardinal number that is the sum of one and one and one

Reason

This sense of 'troika' as a cardinal number meaning 'three' is not standard in English. 'Troika' is used in English primarily to refer to a group of three people or things, or a Russian carriage drawn by three horses abreast, not as a cardinal number. The concept of 'three' is covered by the English word 'three', not 'troika'. Therefore, this sense is too general and does not reflect actual English usage.
