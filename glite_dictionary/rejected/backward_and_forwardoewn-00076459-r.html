backward and forward

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076459-r
Synset POS: r
Internal ID: backward and forward|oewn-00076459-r
moving from one place to another and back again

Synset Examples

he traveled back and forth between Los Angeles and New York
the treetops whipped to and fro in a frightening manner
the old man just sat on the porch and rocked back and forth all day

Reason

The synset describes a general adverbial meaning ('backward and forward') but the requested concept is 'backward and forward' as a phrase. The canonical form in standard English is 'back and forth', which is the idiom commonly found in dictionaries. 'Backward and forward' is not a standard idiom and is rarely included as a primary entry. It is better to cover 'back and forth' as the dictionary entry.
