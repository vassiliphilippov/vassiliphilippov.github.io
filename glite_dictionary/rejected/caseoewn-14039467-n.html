a specific state of mind that is temporary

a case of the jitters

Reason

This concept corresponds to a very specific, idiomatic, and rare use of 'case' meaning a temporary state of mind, as in 'a case of the jitters.' However, this sense is not general or distinct enough from the main dictionary senses of 'case' to warrant a separate entry. Oxford and similar dictionaries incorporate this usage under broader entries for 'case' without a distinct sense, and it does not represent an independent, commonly-used meaning. Therefore, it should not be included as a standalone dictionary entry.
