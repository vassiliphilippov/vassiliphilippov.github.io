just north of

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: just north of|extra.005638
a phrase indicating a geographical location or specific directional location

Synset Examples

He lives in a town just north of here.
The restaurant is located just north of the river.

Reason

'just north of' is a general compositional phrase formed from 'just' (meaning 'very close') and 'north of'. It does not have an idiomatic, non-compositional, or fixed meaning requiring its own dictionary entry. Such locational constructions are productive in English and would not appear as a separate entry in a dictionary like Oxford.
