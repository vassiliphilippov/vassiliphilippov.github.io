renig on

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: renig on|extra.022113
to go back on a commitment or agreement

Synset Examples

If you decide to renig on our agreement, it could lead to trouble.
It's unwise to renig on a deal made with friends.

Reason

The word 'renig' is a common misspelling of 'renege,' which is the correct verb meaning to go back on a promise or commitment. Dictionaries use the standard spelling 'renege.' Therefore, the concept 'renig on' should not be included as a main entry; instead, users should be directed to the correct spelling 'renege.'
