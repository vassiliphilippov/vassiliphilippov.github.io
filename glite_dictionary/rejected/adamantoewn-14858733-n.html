very hard native crystalline carbon valued as a gem

Reason

This sense of 'adamant' as a noun meaning 'very hard native crystalline carbon valued as a gem' refers to 'diamond.' In contemporary English, 'adamant' as a noun in this meaning is obsolete and no longer in regular use. It would not appear as a main entry in modern dictionaries and is considered archaic. The standard use of 'adamant' today is as an adjective meaning 'refusing to be persuaded.' Therefore, this concept should be excluded.
