Reason

The headword 'asleep' in the sense of 'dead' is archaic or poetic and not used in contemporary English outside of highly figurative expressions. While 'asleep' is commonly used to mean 'in a state of sleep', its use as a synonym for 'dead' is extremely rare and typically only found in older or religious texts. Therefore, this sense does not warrant a separate entry in a modern general-purpose dictionary.
