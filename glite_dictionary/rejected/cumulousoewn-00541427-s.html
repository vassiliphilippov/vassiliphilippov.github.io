cumulous

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00541427-s
Synset POS: s
Internal ID: cumulous|oewn-00541427-s
thrown together in a pile

Synset Examples

a desk heaped with books
heaped-up ears of corn
ungraded papers piled high

Reason

The word 'cumulous' as 'thrown together in a pile' is an attributive/adjective sense that is archaic and now almost entirely replaced by 'cumulus' in meteorology or by 'heaped', 'piled', etc. The form 'cumulous' with this meaning does not appear as a current English adjective in general usage or in modern dictionaries like Oxford. The provided examples use 'heaped', 'heaped-up', and 'piled', not 'cumulous'. Therefore, this concept should be excluded from the dictionary.
