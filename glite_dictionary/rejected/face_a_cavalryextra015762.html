face a cavalry

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: face a cavalry|extra.015762
faced a significant challenge or hardship

Synset Examples

He faced his calvary when he lost his job.

Reason

The concept 'face a cavalry' appears to be a confusion with 'face a calvary' (which itself is a rare idiomatic usage) or more commonly with 'face one's calvary', referring metaphorically to a period of suffering (from Calvary, the site of Christ's crucifixion). Additionally, the example sentence is likely a typographical or semantic error. 'Face a cavalry' would literally mean to confront a unit of soldiers on horseback, which is not idiomatic or widely used with the sense of 'facing a challenge or hardship'. Therefore, this concept should not be included in the dictionary.
