Guffmanesque style

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: Guffmanesque style|extra.004509
style reminiscent of comedian Martin Guffman or playwright David Kaufman (Guffman)

Synset Examples

His performance had a Guffmanesque flair that captivated everyone.
She appreciated the Guffmanesque style of the playwright.

Reason

The concept 'Guffmanesque style' is derived from a personal name (Guffman) and refers only to a style reminiscent of that individual, which is not a broadly established or widely recognized term in English. Such 'X-esque' formations are typically dictionary-worthy only if well-attested and in significant general use. There is no evidence that 'Guffmanesque' has attained this status. Therefore, it should not be included as a standalone dictionary entry.
