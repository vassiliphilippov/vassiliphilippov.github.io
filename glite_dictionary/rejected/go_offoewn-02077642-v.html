go off

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02077642-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: go off|oewn-02077642-v
run away; usually includes taking something or somebody along

Synset Examples

The thief made off with our silver
the accountant absconded with the cash from the safe

Reason

The phrase 'go off' in the sense of 'run away, usually taking something or somebody along' is not a primary or widely recognized sense of 'go off' in contemporary English dictionaries. The more common phrasal verb for this meaning is 'make off' or 'abscond.' Therefore, this particular sense does not need its own entry for 'go off' in a standard English-English dictionary.
