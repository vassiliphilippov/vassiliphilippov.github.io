finish

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00353212-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: finish|oewn-00353212-v
finally be or do something

Synset Examples

He ended up marrying his high school sweetheart
he wound up being unemployed and living at home again

Reason

The synset here is not distinctive to the ordinary verb 'finish', but refers to a broad, general sense of 'finally be or do something', as in 'end up' or 'wind up'. This is not a distinct meaning of 'finish', but rather a general semantic overlap that is better reflected under 'end up' or similar verbs. This sense would not be listed as a primary or separate meaning of 'finish' in an Oxford-style dictionary.
