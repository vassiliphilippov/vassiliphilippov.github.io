an extremely steep face

Reason

This sense of 'perpendicular' as 'an extremely steep face' is extremely rare and not in regular use in English. The noun form for 'perpendicular' typically means a straight line at a right angle to another line, not a steep face. This usage does not meet the criteria for inclusion, as it would not appear in a major dictionary like Oxford under this meaning.
