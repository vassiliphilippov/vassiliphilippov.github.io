Synset Examples

Reason

The synset refers to extremely general terms for the human body ('form') used as alternative names for the body of a human being, but 'form' in this sense is rare and highly context-dependent. Common English usage does not employ 'form' as a primary or alternative word for 'the human body' except in very poetic, archaic, or technical contexts. Its use to mean 'human body' is not significant enough to merit a standalone dictionary sense, and more common anthropological terms ('body', 'physique', 'flesh') cover this meaning. Therefore, this sense is too general and indirect for an Oxford-style dictionary entry.
