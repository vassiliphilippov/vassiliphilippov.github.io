French physicist for whom the Pitot tube was named (1695-1771)

Reason

The concept refers to 'Pitot' only as 'a person' (the French physicist Henri Pitot), which is too general for inclusion as a dictionary entry in the sense used for typical biographical names in dictionaries like the Oxford Dictionary. Biographical entries for individuals who are not extremely influential or whose names have not become common nouns/adjectives (e.g., 'Newtonian') are generally excluded. The synset is simply 'person.'
