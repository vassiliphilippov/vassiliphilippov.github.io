Reason

This sense refers to a specialized use of 'sharp' as a noun (a type of sewing needle). While 'sharp' can mean 'a sewing needle with a sharp point' in British English sewing terminology, this usage is highly specialized and not common enough to appear as a main entry in general English dictionaries like Oxford (it may appear in specialized sewing glossaries). The typical noun senses of 'sharp' in general dictionaries relate to musical notes or sharp objects in general, not specifically to this sense.
