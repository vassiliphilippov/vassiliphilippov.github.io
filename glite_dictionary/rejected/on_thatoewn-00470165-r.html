Reason

The synset for 'on that' in this case refers to a very general, context-dependent adverbial phrase (as in 'thereon'), which would not merit an entry as 'on that' in a standard English dictionary. Its use is legalistic/formulaic and almost always appears as part of a larger phrase. This specific sense does not function as an independent lexical item outside specialized contexts.
