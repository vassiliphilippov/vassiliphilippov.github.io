a naval battle in the Spanish-American War (1898); the United States fleet bottled up the Spanish ships in the harbor of Santiago de Cuba and destroyed them when they tried to escape

Reason

This concept refers to a specific historical event ('the Battle of Santiago de Cuba') but is labeled with the ambiguous headword 'Santiago', which is a proper noun referring to multiple places and people as well. The specific event would more properly be listed under its full name 'Battle of Santiago de Cuba' or similar, and the general term 'Santiago' as 'naval battle' is too ambiguous and unlikely to appear as a standalone dictionary entry in major English dictionaries without further specification.
