labeled a skunk

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS:
Internal ID: labeled a skunk|extra.018762
called disloyal or treacherous

Synset Examples

She was labeled a skunk after betraying her friends.

Reason

The phrase "labeled a skunk" is not a fixed idiom or widely recognized set phrase in English; it is simply a literal use of 'labeled' plus the noun 'skunk' (which itself is an existing metaphor for a contemptible or disloyal person). Such a compositional, transparent phrase would not appear as a separate entry in Oxford Dictionary or similar references.
