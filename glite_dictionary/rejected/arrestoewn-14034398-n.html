arrest

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14034398-n
Synset POS: n
Internal ID: arrest|oewn-14034398-n
the state of inactivity following an interruption

Synset Examples

the negotiations were in arrest
held them in check
during the halt he got some lunch
the momentary stay enabled him to escape the blow
he spent the entire stop in his seat

Reason

This sense of 'arrest' as 'the state of inactivity following an interruption' is extremely rare and not standard in contemporary English. In modern usage, 'arrest' is overwhelmingly used for the act of detaining someone, particularly by police, or in a medical context (e.g., cardiac arrest). This meaning as a general state of pause or halt does not appear in major dictionaries like Oxford, and is not encountered in everyday usage; more common terms are 'pause', 'halt', or 'suspension'. Therefore, this sense should be excluded.
