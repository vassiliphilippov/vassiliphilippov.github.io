lots

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
Internal ID: lots|oewn-00059709-r
to a very great degree or extent

Synset Examples

I feel a lot better
we enjoyed ourselves very much
she was very much interested
this would help a great deal

Reason

The synset 'lots' here refers to the use of the phrase 'a lot' or 'lots' as an adverbial intensifier (meaning 'very much'). While 'a lot' (and sometimes 'lots') is commonly used adverbially in informal English, standard dictionaries primarily record 'a lot' as the main adverbial form. The adverbial use of 'lots' in this sense is not standard, and is generally absorbed under entries for 'a lot'. Therefore, a standalone entry for 'lots' as an adverb is unnecessary in a formal dictionary; this sense is better handled under 'a lot (adverb)'.
