Reason

The sense of 'douse' meaning 'slacken' (as in 'douse a rope') is highly technical, archaic, and nearly obsolete except in certain nautical contexts. It does not have general currency and would not typically appear as a distinct sense in standard modern English dictionaries like the Oxford, unless in an unabridged historical or nautical supplement.
