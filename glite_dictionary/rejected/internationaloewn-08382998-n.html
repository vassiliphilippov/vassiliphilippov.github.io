Reason

The synset here ('any of several international socialist organizations') refers to a specific meaning of 'International' as a noun in socialist and labor history, often referring to organizations like the First International, Second International, etc. However, this noun sense is highly specialized, usually capitalized, and refers to specific organizations rather than being a general noun. Most major English dictionaries either do not treat it as a common noun, or only in the context of proper names. Thus, it does not represent a general noun sense suitable for a general-purpose English dictionary entry for 'international.'
