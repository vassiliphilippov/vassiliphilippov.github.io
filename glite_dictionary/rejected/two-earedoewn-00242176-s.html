Reason

The word 'two-eared' is simply a compositional adjective formed from 'two' and 'eared', meaning 'having two ears'. This is a predictable and transparent phrase, and would not appear as a distinct entry in most dictionaries, including the Oxford Dictionary. It is not idiomatic or lexicalized, nor does it have special meaning beyond the sum of its parts. Therefore, it should be excluded.
