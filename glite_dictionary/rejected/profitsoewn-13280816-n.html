Reason

The sense 'profits' as 'something won (especially money)' is not distinct or standard in modern English usage. In contemporary English, 'profit' primarily refers to financial gain from business or investment, not specifically 'something won.' The concept overlaps with 'winnings' but 'profits' is not commonly used for this meaning, so it would not be included as a separate dictionary entry in a standard English-English dictionary.
