Reason

This concept assigns a sense of 'tasteless showiness' to the noun 'loudness,' but this is an extended, figurative, and rare meaning. In standard Oxford-style dictionaries, 'loudness' is defined as the quality of being loud (usually relating to sound), and its figurative extension to visual display (i.e., 'showiness' or 'garishness') is not a primary or generally recognized sense. This metaphorical usage of 'loudness' for showiness is not common enough to merit a separate dictionary entry.
