knell

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02185984-v
Synset POS: v
Internal ID: knell|oewn-02185984-v
make (bells) ring, often for the purposes of musical edification

Synset Examples

Ring the bells
My uncle rings every Sunday at the local church

Reason

This sense of 'knell' as a verb meaning simply 'to make (bells) ring' is not standard in modern English and does not appear in authoritative dictionaries. The common use of 'knell' as a verb is to refer specifically to the sound of a bell rung solemnly, especially to mark death or a funeral. The generic verb sense 'to ring bells' would be covered by 'ring', not 'knell'. Therefore, this usage does not warrant a dictionary entry.
