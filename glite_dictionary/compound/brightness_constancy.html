brightness constancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪtnəs ˈkɑnstənsi / (AmE), / ˈbraɪtnəs ˈkɒnstənsi / (BrE)
the tendency for an object to be perceived as having the same brightness even when the lighting conditions change significantly
perception of constant brightness despite changing light

Examples

The phenomenon of brightness constancy allows us to recognize objects in both sunlight and shade.
Despite clouds changing the outdoor lighting, brightness constancy helps us judge the true shade of objects.
Young children may develop brightness constancy as their visual perception matures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color constancy - the tendency to perceive the color of objects as constant under varying illumination
