ethanoyl radical

1. NOUN

/ əˈθænɔɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / iːˈθænɔɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
the acyl group derived from acetic acid, with the structure CH3CO-
acyl group from acetic acid (CH3CO-)

Examples

The ethanoyl radical is a key component in many important biochemical reactions.
A common example of an acyl group in chemistry is the ethanoyl radical.
Researchers studied the reactivity of the ethanoyl radical in various organic syntheses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetyl - the acyl group CH3CO-, especially as a substituent

Etymology

From 'ethanoic' (systematic name for acetic acid) and 'yl', denoting a radical or substituent group in chemistry.
