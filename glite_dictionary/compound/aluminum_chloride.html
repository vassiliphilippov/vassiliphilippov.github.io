aluminum chloride

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːmɪnəm klaɪˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪnɪəm klaɪˈraɪd / (BrE)
a white or yellowish chemical compound used as a catalyst in chemical reactions and as a wood preservative
chemical compound used as a catalyst

Examples

The laboratory stored several jars of aluminum chloride for use in chemical synthesis.
Aluminum chloride is commonly added as a catalyst in the production of certain plastics.
As a wood preservative, aluminum chloride can help protect timber from decay.
