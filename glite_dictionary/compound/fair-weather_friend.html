fair-weather friend

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr ˌwɛðər ˈfrɛnd / (AmE), / ˈfeə ˌwɛðə ˈfrɛnd / (BrE)
a person who is only supportive or loyal when times are good, but disappears or withdraws support during difficulties
friend supportive only in good times

Examples

When her business failed, she discovered who her real friends were and who were just fair-weather friends.
You can't rely on fair-weather friends when you really need help.
It's easy to mistake someone for a close friend when things are good, but hardship reveals the fair-weather friends.

Synset Examples

She realized he was just a fair-weather friend.
He turned out to be a fair-weather friend during tough times.
It's hard to find true friends; most are fair-weather friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true friend - a friend who sticks with you in all circumstances

Etymology

From 'fair-weather' (pleasant weather), figuratively describing someone present only in favorable circumstances.
