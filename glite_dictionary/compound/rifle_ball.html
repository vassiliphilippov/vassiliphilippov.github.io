rifle ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəl bɔl / (AmE), / ˈraɪfəl bɔːl / (BrE)
a bullet designed to be fired from a rifle, originally spherical but later made elongated
bullet for a rifle (historically spherical)

Examples

The soldier loaded a rifle ball into his musket before taking aim at the target.
Early rifle balls were made spherical, but later designs improved accuracy with an elongated shape.
Collectors value authentic Civil War-era rifle balls for their historic significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullet - a projectile fired from a gun
