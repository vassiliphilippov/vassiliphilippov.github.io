neuromuscular junction

1. NOUN

/ nuˌroʊˌmʌskjəˈlɚ ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / njuˌərəʊˌmʌskjʊˈlə ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the synapse or site of connection where a nerve cell communicates with a muscle fiber to transmit nerve impulses, causing muscle contraction
connection point between nerve and muscle
medical

Examples

The neuromuscular junction plays a crucial role in transmitting signals that cause muscles to contract.
Certain toxins can disrupt the neuromuscular junction, leading to paralysis or muscle weakness.
Researchers are studying diseases that impair the neuromuscular junction, such as myasthenia gravis.

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves) + muscular (relating to muscles) + junction (point of joining).
