musical scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪkəl skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl skeɪl / (BrE)
A set of musical notes ordered by ascending or descending pitch according to a particular pattern, usually within an octave.
series of musical notes by pitch

Examples

Practicing the musical scale daily can help improve your finger technique on the piano.
The major musical scale is often the first one beginners learn in music lessons.
Composers use different musical scales to create distinct moods in their songs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - (music) a sequence of notes in a fixed order of pitches
