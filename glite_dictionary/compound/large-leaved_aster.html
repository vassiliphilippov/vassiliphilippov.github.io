large-leaved aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrdʒ ˌlivd ˈæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ ˌliːvd ˈæstə / (BrE)
A perennial wildflower of North America with tufts of broad leaves, belonging to the aster family, now naturalized in Europe.
North American wildflower with broad leaves

Examples

We found a cluster of large-leaved aster growing along the forest trail.
The large-leaved aster is prized for its broad foliage and delicate flowers.
Gardeners in Europe often cultivate the large-leaved aster as an ornamental plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the genus Aster, bearing star-shaped flowers
