First Battle of Chattanooga

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɝst ˈbætəl əv ˌtʃætəˈnuːɡə / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈbætəl ɒv ˌtʃætəˈnuːɡə / (BrE)
a major 1863 battle of the American Civil War where Union forces led by Ulysses S. Grant decisively defeated Confederate troops near Chattanooga, Tennessee
1863 Civil War battle near Chattanooga

Examples

The First Battle of Chattanooga marked a turning point in the Union's campaign in the western theater.
Many historians study the strategies used in the First Battle of Chattanooga to understand Civil War tactics.
A memorial commemorates those who fought in the First Battle of Chattanooga.
