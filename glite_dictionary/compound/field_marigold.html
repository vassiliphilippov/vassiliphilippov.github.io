field marigold

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌmær.ɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌmær.ɪˌɡəʊld / (BrE)
A European herb with bright yellow flowers, often found as a common weed in grain fields.
European herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The field marigold brightens up the countryside in early spring with its yellow blossoms.
Farmers often find field marigold growing among their cereal crops as a weed.
She picked a bouquet that included field marigold from the edge of the meadow.
