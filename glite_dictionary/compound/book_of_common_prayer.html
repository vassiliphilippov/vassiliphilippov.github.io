Book of Common Prayer

1. PROPN

/ bʊk əv ˈkɑmən ˈprɛr / (AmE), / bʊk əv ˈkɒmən ˈpreə / (BrE)
The official service book of the Church of England, used for Anglican worship and notable for its dignified and beautiful language.
Anglican service book

Examples

Many Anglican churches still use the Book of Common Prayer in their services today.
The language of the Book of Common Prayer has influenced English literature for centuries.
A revised version of the Book of Common Prayer was published in the twentieth century.

Etymology

Title introduced in the mid-16th century for the English Reformation; 'book' (text), 'common' (shared), 'prayer' (worship).
