vector product

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛktər ˈprɑdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈvɛktə ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
the vector resulting from the cross product of two vectors, calculated such that it is perpendicular to both and whose magnitude equals the area of the parallelogram formed by the original vectors
cross product of two vectors

Examples

The vector product of two vectors is perpendicular to the plane containing them.
In physics, torque is calculated using the vector product of force and position vectors.
To find the area of the parallelogram, compute the vector product of the given vectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross product - an alternative term for vector product in mathematics and physics
  • Antonym: dot product - a scalar product of two vectors, producing a scalar result

Etymology

From 'vector' (from Latin 'vehere', to carry) and 'product' (from Latin 'productum', something produced), used in mathematical terminology since the late 19th century.
