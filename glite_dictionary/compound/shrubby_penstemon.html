shrubby penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrʌbi pɛnˈstiːmən / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌbi pɛnˈstiːmən / (BrE)
A low, bushy plant with large showy pale lavender or blue-violet flowers in narrow clusters at the ends of its stems.
a low bushy plant with showy violet flowers

Examples

The shrubby penstemon added a splash of color to the garden with its violet flowers.
Gardeners recommend shrubby penstemon for dry, sunny spots where other plants struggle to thrive.
We spotted a beautiful shrubby penstemon along the walking trail last spring.

Etymology

From 'shrubby', meaning bush-like, and 'penstemon', the genus name of the plant, from Greek 'pente' (five) and 'stēmōn' (thread, referring to the stamens).
