dilawan belief

1. NOUN

/ diˈlɑːwɑːn bəˈliːf / (AmE), / dɪˈlɑːwæn bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
Political beliefs supportive of liberal or 'yellow' politics characteristic of a segment of opposition in the Philippines, often associated with democratic reform and social justice.
liberal Filipino political belief

Examples

Her dilawan belief influenced her advocacy for press freedom and government transparency.
Many in the community accused him of holding dilawan beliefs during the heated election.
Discussions about history often reveal whether someone holds a dilawan belief or aligns elsewhere politically.

Synset Examples

His dilawan beliefs shaped his views on social justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow politics - Liberal or opposition Philippine politics associated with the color yellow
  • Antonym: pro-administration belief - Beliefs supportive of the current government or ruling party in the Philippines

Etymology

From Filipino 'dilawan' meaning 'yellow,' referring to the yellow-themed political opposition in the Philippines.
