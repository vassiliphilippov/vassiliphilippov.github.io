tenor voice

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnər vɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnə vɔɪs / (BrE)
the adult male singing voice with a range above baritone and below countertenor
male singing voice above baritone

Examples

He sang the solo beautifully, his tenor voice soaring above the choir.
The opera company is looking for a strong tenor voice for their next production.
Many pop singers are known for their impressive tenor voices that captivate audiences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: baritone voice - the adult male singing voice between bass and tenor
  • Degree Lower: bass voice - the lowest adult male singing voice
  • Degree Higher: countertenor - the adult male singing voice above tenor

Etymology

From Italian 'tenore', from Latin 'tenor' (holder, course), originally referring to the part that held the melody.
