common mallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmaləʊ / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant (Malva sylvestris) native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, with clusters of pink or white flowers, now naturalized in other regions including the United States.
species of mallow with pink or white flowers

Examples

The leaves of the common mallow are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
In summer, the common mallow produces clusters of delicate pink blossoms in the garden.
We found common mallow growing wild along the edge of the old field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - any of several plants of the genus Malva

Etymology

From Latin 'malva' (mallow), 'common' to distinguish it from other species.
