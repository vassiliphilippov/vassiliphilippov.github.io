exclamation point

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the punctuation mark (!) used to indicate an exclamation or strong feeling at the end of a sentence
punctuation mark: !
american

Examples

You should use an exclamation point to show excitement or surprise.
The sentence ended with an exclamation point to emphasize her anger.
Some people think too many exclamation points make writing seem unprofessional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclamation mark - the punctuation mark (!) used after an exclamation (British term)

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the punctuation mark (!) used to show strong feelings such as excitement or surprise
punctuation mark for strong emotion
american

Examples

He finished the sentence with an exclamation point to show his excitement.
Please avoid using more than one exclamation point in formal writing.
Her name was written in big letters with an exclamation point at the end.

Synset Examples

She ended her speech with an enthusiastic exclamation point.
The kid jumped up with an exclamation point of joy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclamation mark - the punctuation mark (!) used to express strong emotions, chiefly British English

Etymology

Formed from 'exclamation' (from Latin exclamare, to cry out) plus 'point' (punctuation mark).
