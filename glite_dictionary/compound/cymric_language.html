Cymric language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪmrɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɪmrɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an alternative name for the Welsh language, a member of the Celtic language family spoken primarily in Wales
alternative term for the Welsh language

Examples

The Cymric language has a rich literary tradition dating back over a thousand years.
Efforts to preserve the Cymric language have increased in recent decades throughout Wales.
He decided to learn the Cymric language to connect with his Welsh heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welsh - the Celtic language spoken in Wales

Etymology

From Cymric, meaning 'Welsh', derived from 'Cymru', the Welsh name for Wales.
