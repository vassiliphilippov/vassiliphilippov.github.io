lawn tennis

1. NOUN

/ lɔn ˈtɛnɪs / (AmE), / lɔːn ˈtɛnɪs / (BrE)
A game played on a rectangular court divided by a net, in which two or four players use rackets to hit a ball back and forth over the net.
tennis played on a court with a net

Examples

He has played lawn tennis since he was a teenager and competes in local tournaments.
In Victorian England, lawn tennis became a popular pastime among the upper classes.
The rules of lawn tennis have evolved since the sport was first played on grass courts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tennis - The common modern game played with rackets over a net on a court

Etymology

Originally distinguished from 'real tennis', with 'lawn' referring to the grass surfaces on which the game was first played.
