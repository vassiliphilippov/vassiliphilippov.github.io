boundary condition

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊndəri kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbaʊndəri kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a condition given at the boundary of a domain, used to determine the solution of a set of differential equations
requirement at the edge of a domain in maths/physics

Examples

To solve the heat equation, you must specify a boundary condition for the temperature at each end of the rod.
The simulation failed because the boundary condition was not properly defined for the fluid's edge.
Changing the boundary condition can dramatically affect the solution of a physical problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initial condition - a condition specifying the state of a system at the beginning of a process
