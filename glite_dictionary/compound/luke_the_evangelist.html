Luke the Evangelist

1. PROPN

/ luːk ði ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst / (AmE), / luːk ði ɪˈvan.dʒə.lɪst / (BrE)
the Apostle closely associated with St. Paul and traditionally regarded as the author of the Gospel of Luke in the New Testament
author of the Gospel of Luke; Apostle in the New Testament

Examples

Many scholars believe that Luke the Evangelist was a physician as well as a writer.
The feast day of Luke the Evangelist is celebrated on October 18th in many Christian traditions.
According to tradition, Luke the Evangelist also authored the Acts of the Apostles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Luke - one of the four Evangelists, author of the Gospel of Luke

Etymology

From Latin Lucas, from Ancient Greek Loukas; 'Evangelist' from Greek euangelistēs (bringer of good news).
