creatio ex nihilo

1. NOUN

/ kriːˈɑːtioʊ ɛks ˈniːhɪloʊ / (AmE), / kriːˈɑːtɪəʊ ɛks ˈniːhɪləʊ / (BrE)
The doctrine in Judeo-Christian theology that God created the world out of nothing.
creation of the world out of nothing

Examples

The doctrine of creatio ex nihilo is fundamental to many Christian theological perspectives.
Scholars have debated the origins of creatio ex nihilo within early Judaic thought.
Many religious traditions contrast creatio ex nihilo with creation from pre-existing matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creationism - the belief that the universe was created by a divine being

Etymology

From Latin creatio (creation) + ex nihilo (from nothing).
