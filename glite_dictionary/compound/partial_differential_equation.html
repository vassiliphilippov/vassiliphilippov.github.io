partial differential equation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrʃəl dɪˌfɛrənʃl iˌkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl dɪˌfɛrənsɪəl ɪˌkweɪʒən / (BrE)
A mathematical equation that relates the partial derivatives of a function of more than one variable.
math equation involving partial derivatives

Examples

The heat equation is an example of a partial differential equation used in physics.
Solving a partial differential equation often requires advanced mathematical techniques.
Many phenomena in engineering are modeled using partial differential equations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: differential equation - an equation involving derivatives of a function

Etymology

From 'partial' (referring to partial derivatives in calculus) and 'differential equation' (equation involving derivatives).
