linear leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɚ lif / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə liːf / (BrE)
a long, narrow leaf with sides that are nearly parallel and considerably longer than wide
a long, narrow leaf

Examples

The grass in the field was easily identified by its linear leaf structure.
Many aquatic plants have a linear leaf to reduce water resistance.
The botanist noted the linear leaf form during her survey of the flora.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lanceolate leaf - a leaf shaped like a lance, wider in the middle and tapering to both ends
