groined vault

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɔɪnd vɔlt / (AmE), / ɡrɔɪnd vɔːlt / (BrE)
An architectural vault formed by the intersection at right angles of two barrel vaults, creating a 'groin' or edge where they meet.
vault formed by intersecting barrel vaults

Examples

The cathedral's ceiling features a magnificent series of groined vaults spanning the nave.
Medieval architects commonly used the groined vault to allow for large, open interior spaces.
A groined vault distributes the weight more efficiently than a simple barrel vault.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: barrel vault - a simple, continuous vault in the shape of a half-cylinder

Etymology

From 'groin' (the curved edge formed by the intersection), and 'vault'.
