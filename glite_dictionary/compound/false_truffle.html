false truffle

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈtrʌfəl / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈtrʌfəl / (BrE)
any of various fungi of the genus Scleroderma having hard-skinned, subterranean fruiting bodies that resemble true truffles, but are not closely related
a fungus resembling a true truffle

Examples

The false truffle can be mistaken for genuine truffles due to its similar appearance.
Some foragers avoid collecting false truffles because they are inedible or mildly toxic.
Researchers studied the false truffle to understand its effect on local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truffle - an edible subterranean fungus prized as a delicacy

2. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈtrʌfəl / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈtrʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
Any of several fungi of the family Rhizopogonaceae, which have underground fruiting bodies resembling true truffles but are botanically distinct.
fungus with subterranean fruiting body like a truffle

Examples

Some species of false truffle are mistaken for edible truffles due to their similar appearance.
Ecologists study the role of false truffles in forest ecosystems, as they form symbiotic relationships with trees.
Unlike true truffles, the false truffle is not highly valued in culinary traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truffle - an edible underground fungus, valued as a delicacy
