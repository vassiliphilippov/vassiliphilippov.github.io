zonal pelargonium

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.nəl ˌpɛl.ɑrˈɡoʊ.ni.əm / (AmE), / ˈzəʊ.nəl ˌpɛl.əˈɡəʊ.ni.əm / (BrE)
an upright cultivated geranium with scalloped leaves featuring a broad colored band inside the margin and typically white, pink, or red flowers
type of upright cultivated geranium

Examples

The zonal pelargonium is a popular choice for window boxes due to its vibrant flowers and striking leaves.
Gardeners prize the zonal pelargonium for its colorful foliage and long-lasting blooms.
She planted several zonal pelargoniums along the path, creating a cheerful border of pink and red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a plant with rounded leaves and various colored flowers, often grown as an ornamental

Etymology

Pelargonium from Greek 'pelargos' meaning 'stork', referring to the stork-bill shape of the fruit; zonal referring to the band (zone) of color on the leaves.
