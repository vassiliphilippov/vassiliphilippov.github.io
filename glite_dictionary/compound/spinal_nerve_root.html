spinal nerve root

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnəl nɜːrv ruːt / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl nɜːv ruːt / (BrE)
either of the two nerve roots (dorsal or ventral) that emerge from the spinal cord and join to form a spinal nerve
nerve root of the spinal cord
medical

Examples

A herniated disc may compress a spinal nerve root and cause pain or numbness.
Inflammation of a spinal nerve root can result in significant neurological symptoms.
Each spinal nerve root carries sensory or motor signals to and from the spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve root - a root of a nerve, especially of a spinal nerve
