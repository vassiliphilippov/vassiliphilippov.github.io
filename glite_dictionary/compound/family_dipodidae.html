family Dipodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli dɪˈpɑdəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dɪˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, long-legged rodents known as jerboas, native to the Old World, especially arid regions of Asia and Africa.
rodent family including jerboas

Examples

The family Dipodidae includes various species of jerboas adapted to desert life.
Biologists study the family Dipodidae to understand the evolution of jumping rodents.
Few mammals are as well adapted to arid environments as those in the family Dipodidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerboa - small jumping rodent found in deserts

Etymology

From Modern Latin Dipodidae, based on Greek di- (two) + pous (foot), referencing the animal's jumping feet.
