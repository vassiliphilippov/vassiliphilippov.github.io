peptic ulceration

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛptɪk ˌʌlsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɛptɪk ˌʌlsəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
An ulcer in the mucous membrane lining the digestive tract, typically caused by stomach acid.
ulcer in the lining of the digestive tract
medical

Examples

Prolonged use of certain medications can increase the risk of peptic ulceration in patients.
Symptoms of peptic ulceration may include abdominal pain and indigestion.
Doctors often prescribe proton pump inhibitors to treat peptic ulceration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peptic ulcer - an ulcer occurring in the lining of the stomach or duodenum

Etymology

From Greek 'peptikos' (digestive) and Latin 'ulceratio' (ulceration).
