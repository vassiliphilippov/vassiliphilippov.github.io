armed bullhead

1. NOUN

/ ɑrmd ˈbʊlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ɑːmd ˈbʊlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a small northern Atlantic fish of the sea poacher family, known for its bony armor and spines
a northern Atlantic armored fish

Examples

The armed bullhead is known for the protective spines on its head and body.
Fishermen occasionally catch an armed bullhead while trawling the northern Atlantic.
The distinctive armor of the armed bullhead helps it avoid predators in its rocky habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea poacher - a fish of the family Agonidae, found in cold northern waters
