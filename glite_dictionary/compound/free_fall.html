free fall

1. NOUN

/ fri fɔl / (AmE), / friː fɔːl / (BrE)
A sudden and sharp decrease in the value or amount of something, especially in financial or economic contexts.
sudden sharp decrease (esp. financial)

Examples

After the bad earnings report, the company's stock went into free fall and lost 30% in one day.
Oil prices are in free fall due to oversupply and decreased demand.
The athlete's reputation went into free fall following the scandal.

Synset Examples

a drop of 57 points on the Dow Jones index
there was a drop in pressure in the pulmonary artery
a dip in prices
when that became known the price of their stock went into free fall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunge - a rapid and steep fall in value or amount
  • Antonym: rebound - an increase in value after a decline

2. NOUN

/ fri fɔl / (AmE), / friː fɔːl / (BrE)
the motion of a body when it is only under the influence of gravity, with no other forces acting upon it
falling motion under gravity alone

Examples

Astronauts in orbit experience free fall because they are constantly falling towards the Earth.
During a free fall, there is no air resistance to slow down the object.
The experiment was designed to study the effect of gravity during free fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - the force that attracts a body toward the center of the earth
  • Degree Higher: weightlessness - the state of being without measurable weight, as in free fall
