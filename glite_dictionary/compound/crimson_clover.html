crimson clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmzən ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmzən ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
a European annual plant with crimson flower heads, widely grown in the United States for forage
a plant with crimson flowers used as forage

Examples

Farmers often plant crimson clover to improve soil fertility and provide grazing for livestock.
The field was bright with the red blossoms of crimson clover in early spring.
Crimson clover is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - a plant with trifoliate leaves and dense flower heads, used as forage
