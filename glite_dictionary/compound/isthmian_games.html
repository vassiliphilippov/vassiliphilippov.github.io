Isthmian Games

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪsmiən ˈɡeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈɪsmiən ˈɡeɪmz / (BrE)
one of the major ancient Greek Panhellenic festivals, held every two years on the Isthmus of Corinth, featuring athletic and musical competitions
ancient Greek festival games at Corinth

Examples

The Isthmian Games attracted athletes from all over ancient Greece for competition and celebration.
Scholars have found references to the Isthmian Games in many classical texts.
During the Isthmian Games, victors were crowned with wreaths of wild celery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympic Games - major ancient and modern international athletic competitions held every four years
  • Synonym: Pythian Games - ancient Greek games held at Delphi
  • Synonym: Nemean Games - ancient Greek games held at Nemea

Etymology

From Greek Isthmia, referring to the Isthmus of Corinth, where the games were held.
