columnar epithelial cell

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌm.nɚ ˌɛp.əˈθi.li.əl sɛl / (AmE), / kəˈlʌm.nə ˌɛp.ɪˈθiː.li.əl sɛl / (BrE)
an epithelial cell that is shaped like a column, typically longer than it is wide; some types have cilia
a column-shaped epithelial cell
medical

Examples

The lining of the stomach is made up of columnar epithelial cells that secrete mucus and digestive enzymes.
Under the microscope, columnar epithelial cells can be easily distinguished from other epithelial cell types by their elongated shape.
Respiratory passages contain ciliated columnar epithelial cells that help move mucus and trapped particles out of the lungs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: squamous epithelial cell - an epithelial cell that is flat and scale-like
  • Degree Lower: cuboidal epithelial cell - an epithelial cell that is cube-shaped

Etymology

From Latin 'columna' (column) and Greek 'epithelial' (from 'epi-' upon + 'thēlē' nipple).
