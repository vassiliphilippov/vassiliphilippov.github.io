horse chestnut

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree with palmate leaves and showy clusters of white to red flowers, producing shiny brown inedible seeds
tree with large leaves and shiny inedible seeds

Examples

The horse chestnut is a common sight along city streets and parks in Europe.
Children collected shiny horse chestnut seeds to play traditional games in the autumn.
The large white flowers of the horse chestnut tree appear in late spring, creating a spectacular display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conker - the seed of the horse chestnut

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its seeds to chestnuts and the belief that they were fed to horses.
