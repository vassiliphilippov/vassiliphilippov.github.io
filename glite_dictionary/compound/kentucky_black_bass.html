Kentucky black bass

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtʌki blæk bæs / (AmE), / kənˈtʌki blæk bɑːs / (BrE)
a variety of black bass (Micropterus) found in the United States, especially in Kentucky, also known as the spotted bass
a variety of black bass fish

Examples

Anglers in the region often seek out the Kentucky black bass because of its spirited fight.
The Kentucky black bass is known for its distinctive spotted pattern along the lateral line.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of the Kentucky black bass in freshwater rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted bass - a species of black bass also called Kentucky black bass
