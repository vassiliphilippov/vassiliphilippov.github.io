detention basin

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛnʃən ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən ˈbeɪsən / (BrE)
An artificial structure or reservoir designed to temporarily store stormwater runoff and release it slowly to prevent flooding and erosion downstream.
structure for temporary stormwater storage

Examples

The new housing development includes a detention basin to manage excess rainwater from heavy storms.
Engineers inspected the detention basin to ensure it was functioning properly during the rainy season.
A detention basin helps reduce the risk of downstream flooding by temporarily holding stormwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retention pond - an artificial pond for storing stormwater permanently

Etymology

From 'detention' (from Latin 'detentio', meaning holding back) and 'basin'.
