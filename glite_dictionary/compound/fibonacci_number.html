Fibonacci number

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːbəˈnɑtʃi ˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / ˌfɪbəˈnɑːtʃi ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
Any number in the sequence where each number is the sum of the previous two, starting with 0 and 1.
number in the Fibonacci sequence

Examples

The fifth Fibonacci number is 5, since it follows 2 and 3 in the sequence.
Many natural patterns, like spiral shells, reflect arrangements related to the Fibonacci number.
She demonstrated how every Fibonacci number can be generated by adding the previous two numbers together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fibonacci sequence - the sequence where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones

Etymology

Named after Leonardo Fibonacci (c. 1170–c. 1250), an Italian mathematician.
