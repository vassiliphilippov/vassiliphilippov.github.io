family Cariamidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kəˌriəˈmaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kæˌrɪəˈmaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of crane-like South American wading birds, including the seriemas.
family of South American wading birds

Examples

The family Cariamidae comprises only two living species, both native to South America.
Members of the family Cariamidae are known for their long legs and terrestrial habits.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of the family Cariamidae to understand their relationship to other birds.

Etymology

From New Latin *Cariamidae*, based on the genus *Cariama*.
