driver's license

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvɚz ˌlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvəz ˌlaɪsəns / (BrE)
An official document authorizing a person to operate a motor vehicle.
license to drive a motor vehicle

Examples

You must carry your driver's license with you at all times while operating a car.
She finally passed her test and received her driver's license last week.
Police officers may ask to see your driver's license during a traffic stop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driving licence - British term for driver's license
