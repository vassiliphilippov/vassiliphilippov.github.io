pinnate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪneɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈpɪneɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf with leaflets arranged on either side of a central axis, resembling a feather.
leaf with feather-like arrangement

Examples

The rose has a pinnate leaf with several oval-shaped leaflets along each side of its stem.
Many species of ferns display a classic pinnate leaf pattern.
When identifying trees, noticing a pinnate leaf can help distinguish ash from maple.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: compound leaf - a leaf consisting of multiple leaflets

Etymology

From Latin 'pinnatus' meaning 'feathered'.
