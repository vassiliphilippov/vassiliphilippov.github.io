valvular heart disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælvjələr hɑrt dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈvælvjʊlə hɑːt dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
heart disease caused by malfunction of one or more of the heart valves, often due to stenosis or regurgitation
disease affecting heart valves
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with valvular heart disease after showing symptoms of shortness of breath and fatigue.
Certain infections can increase the risk of developing valvular heart disease later in life.
Treatment for valvular heart disease may include medication or surgery to repair the affected valve.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heart disease - any disorder that affects the heart
  • Synonym: valve stenosis - narrowing of a heart valve

Etymology

From Latin 'valvula' (diminutive of 'valva', leaf of a door) and 'heart disease'.
