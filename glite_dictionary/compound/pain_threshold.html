pain threshold

1. NOUN

/ peɪn ˈθreʃhoʊld / (AmE), / peɪn ˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
the minimum level of stimulus at which a person begins to feel pain
minimum intensity at which pain is felt
medical

Examples

Some people's pain threshold is much higher than others, making them less sensitive to discomfort.
After the surgery, his pain threshold seemed to be much lower than before.
Doctors often assess a patient's pain threshold to help determine the right dosage of medication.

Synset Examples

some people have much higher pain thresholds than do other people
