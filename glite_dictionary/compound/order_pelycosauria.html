order Pelycosauria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌpɛlɪkoʊˈsɔriə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌpɛlɪkəˈsɔːrɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of early, extinct, primitive reptiles, including genera such as Edaphosaurus and Dimetrodon, known for their sail-backed appearances
order of extinct early reptiles

Examples

The extinct order Pelycosauria includes some of the earliest known synapsids, such as Dimetrodon.
Fossils from the order Pelycosauria are crucial for understanding vertebrate evolution.
Researchers discovered a new species within the order Pelycosauria in the Texas fossil beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dimetrodon - a genus of sail-backed synapsid reptiles in Pelycosauria
  • Synonym: Edaphosaurus - a genus of sail-backed plant-eating reptiles in Pelycosauria

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'pelycos' (cup or bowl) and 'sauros' (lizard), referring to the sail-like structure on their backs.
