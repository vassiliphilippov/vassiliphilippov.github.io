square millimetre

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr ˈmɪlɪˌmitɚ / (AmE), / skwɛə ˈmɪlɪˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A unit of area equal to the area of a square with sides each measuring one millimetre.
metric unit of area (mm²)

Examples

The scratch on the lens measured less than one square millimetre in area.
Printed circuit board features can be smaller than a single square millimetre.
Scientists found only three square millimetres of corrosion on the metal sample.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: square centimetre - unit of area equal to a square with sides of one centimetre
  • Degree Higher: square metre - unit of area equal to a square with sides of one metre

Etymology

From 'square' (referring to area) + 'millimetre', ultimately from French 'millimètre', based on Latin 'mille' (thousand) and Greek 'metron' (measure).
