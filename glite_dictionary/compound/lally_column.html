lally column

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæli ˌkɑləm / (AmE), / ˈlæli ˌkɒləm / (BrE)
A vertical support column made from a steel tube filled with concrete, typically used in building construction.
steel and concrete support column

Examples

The basement renovation required a new lally column to support the added load.
Before removing the wall, the contractor installed a temporary lally column for safety.
Old lally columns may rust over time and need to be replaced in older homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column - an upright pillar, typically cylindrical, supporting a structure

Etymology

Named after John Lally, an American innovator who patented the design in the late 19th century.
