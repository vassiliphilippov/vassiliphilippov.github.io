wall germander

1. NOUN

/ wɔl dʒərˈmændɚ / (AmE), / wɔːl dʒəˈmændə / (BrE)
A European perennial subshrub with red-purple or bright rose flowers marked with red and white spots; grown as an ornamental plant.
a European perennial subshrub with showy flowers

Examples

The wall germander is often planted in herb gardens for its attractive foliage.
Gardeners admire the wall germander for its bright, long-lasting flowers.
A low hedge of wall germander can help define the edges of garden beds.

Etymology

From Middle English 'germandre', from Old French, from Late Latin 'germandra', from Greek 'khemarrhion' (a kind of plant), with 'wall' due to its common growth near walls.
