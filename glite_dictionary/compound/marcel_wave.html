marcel wave

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˈsɛl weɪv / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɛl weɪv / (BrE)
a hairstyle characterized by deep, regular waves, typically created using a heated curling iron
hair with deep, regular waves

Examples

She styled her hair in a classic marcel wave for the 1920s-themed party.
The actress's marcel wave added elegance to her red carpet look.
Many vintage fashion enthusiasts love experimenting with the marcel wave hairstyle.

Synset Examples

He reminisced about the marcel hairstyles of the past.
The vintage look was completed with marcel waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a hair wave style

Etymology

Named after French hairdresser Marcel Grateau, who invented the technique in the late 19th century.
