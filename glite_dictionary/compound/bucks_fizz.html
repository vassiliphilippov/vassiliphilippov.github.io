buck's fizz

1. NOUN

/ bʌks fɪz / (AmE), / bʌks fɪz / (BrE)
A cocktail made by mixing champagne and orange juice, typically served as a celebratory drink.
cocktail of champagne and orange juice

Examples

They served buck's fizz at the wedding brunch to toast the newlyweds.
For a light breakfast cocktail, I like to make buck's fizz with freshly squeezed orange juice.
At New Year's brunch, he prepared a large pitcher of buck's fizz for the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimosa - a similar cocktail of champagne and orange juice, sometimes with added liqueur

Etymology

Named after the Buck's Club in London where the drink is said to have originated in the 1920s.
