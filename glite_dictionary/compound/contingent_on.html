contingent on

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪndʒənt ɑn / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒɪnt ɒn / (BrE)
dependent on something else; determined by particular conditions or circumstances.
dependent on conditions or circumstances

Examples

Admission to the advanced course is contingent on passing the preliminary exam.
The manager’s approval was contingent on all paperwork being completed correctly.
Bonuses this year are contingent on company profits meeting certain targets.

Synset Examples

arms sales contingent on the approval of Congress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependent on - determined or decided by something else

Etymology

From Latin contingentem, present participle of contingere (to happen, befall). 'Contingent' meaning 'dependent on' developed in English in the 16th century.
