Arabian Desert

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪbiən ˈdɛzɚt / (AmE), / əˈreɪbiən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a large desert region in Egypt, situated between the Nile River and the Red Sea, known for its arid landscape and historical significance
desert in Egypt between the Nile and the Red Sea

Examples

The Arabian Desert stretches for hundreds of miles across Egypt, presenting a harsh and beautiful landscape.
Ancient trade routes often passed through the Arabian Desert, connecting different civilizations.
Rare species of wildlife have adapted to survive in the extreme conditions of the Arabian Desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sahara - the largest hot desert in the world, located in North Africa

Etymology

From 'Arabian', relating to Arabia or the Arab world, and 'desert', from Latin 'desertum' (desert, wilderness).

2. PROPN

/ əˈreɪ.bi.ən ˈdɛz.ɚt / (AmE), / əˈreɪ.bi.ən ˈdez.ət / (BrE)
a vast desert region on the Arabian Peninsula in southwestern Asia, known for its arid climate and extensive sand dunes
desert in southwestern Asia, on the Arabian Peninsula

Examples

The Arabian Desert stretches over much of the Arabian Peninsula, covering several countries.
Camels are well adapted to survive in the harsh environment of the Arabian Desert.
Archaeologists have discovered ancient settlements beneath the sands of the Arabian Desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sahara - the largest hot desert in the world, in North Africa
  • Synonym: Gobi Desert - a large desert in East Asia, covering parts of northern China and southern Mongolia
