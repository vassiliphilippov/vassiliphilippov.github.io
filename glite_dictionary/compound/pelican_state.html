Pelican State

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛlɪkən steɪt / (AmE), / ˈpelɪkən steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of Louisiana in the southern United States, especially referencing its state bird, the brown pelican
nickname for Louisiana, USA

Examples

Many tourists visit the Pelican State to experience its vibrant music scene and delicious cuisine.
The Pelican State is famous for its Mardi Gras celebrations in New Orleans.
Louisiana, also known as the Pelican State, has a rich cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Louisiana - a state in the southern United States on the Gulf of Mexico

Etymology

Named after the brown pelican, the state bird of Louisiana.
