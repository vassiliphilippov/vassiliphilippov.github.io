thermal insulation

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝːməl ˌɪnsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməl ˌɪnsjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
Material or methods used to reduce the transfer of heat between objects or spaces, helping to keep temperatures stable.
material or method that reduces heat transfer

Examples

Adding thermal insulation to your attic can significantly reduce heating costs during winter.
The new windows have improved thermal insulation, keeping the building warmer in cold weather.
Engineers tested different types of thermal insulation to find the most effective solution.

Synset Examples

There are a number of products available which are designed to improve the thermal insulation of homes, including thermal glass such as Planitherm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insulation - material that reduces transfer of heat, electricity, or sound

Etymology

From 'thermal' (relating to heat) and 'insulation' (protection from transmission), both from Latin origins.
