Soviet Socialist Republic

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈviɛt ˈsoʊʃəlɪst rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈsəʊviət ˈsəʊʃəlɪst rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
any of the constituent states that made up the former Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR) from 1922 to 1991.
constituent state of the former USSR

Examples

Ukraine was once a Soviet Socialist Republic before gaining independence in 1991.
Each Soviet Socialist Republic was represented in the Supreme Soviet of the USSR.
The dissolution of the USSR led to the independence of 15 former Soviet Socialist Republics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: USSR - the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, a former federation in Eurasia

Etymology

From Russian 'Sovetskaya Sotsialisticheskaya Respublika'.
