heavy fine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi faɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi faɪn / (BrE)
a large amount of money charged as a penalty for breaking a law or regulation
large monetary penalty

Examples

The judge imposed a heavy fine on the company for violating safety regulations.
Drivers who are caught speeding in that area face a heavy fine.
Smoking in public places can result in a heavy fine in many cities.

Synset Examples

Scalphunting is illegal, and it attracts heavy fines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fine - a sum of money exacted as a penalty by a court of law or other authority
