quantum physics

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːntəm ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
The branch of physics that studies the behavior of matter and energy at the scale of atoms and subatomic particles, based on quantum theory.
branch of physics based on quantum theory

Examples

Many technological advancements, such as lasers and semiconductors, are based on principles of quantum physics.
He decided to pursue a PhD in quantum physics after being fascinated by the mysterious nature of subatomic particles.
The professor explained how quantum physics differs from classical physics when describing the behavior of electrons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum mechanics - branch of physics dealing with quantum phenomena, often treated as synonymous

Etymology

From Latin quantum (how much) and Greek physika (natural things), referencing quantum theory.
