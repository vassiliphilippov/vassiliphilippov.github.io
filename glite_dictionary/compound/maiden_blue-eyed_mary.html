maiden blue-eyed Mary

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdən bluː aɪd ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdn bluː aɪd ˈmɛri / (BrE)
a small, branching western North American wildflower with tiny blue-and-white flowers, found from British Columbia to California and Colorado
a blue-and-white-flowered wild plant of western North America

Examples

We spotted several maiden blue-eyed Mary blooming along the trail in early spring.
The delicate flowers of the maiden blue-eyed Mary add a splash of color to the woodland understory.
Gardeners sometimes plant maiden blue-eyed Mary to attract native pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue-eyed Mary - a wildflower with blue-and-white flowers (Collinsia species)
