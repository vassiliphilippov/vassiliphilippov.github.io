vascular strand

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæskjələr strænd / (AmE), / ˈvaskjʊlə strænd / (BrE)
a strand within the stem or leaf of higher plants, consisting of xylem and phloem, that forms part of the plant's vascular system
a unit of xylem and phloem in plants

Examples

The vascular strand in each leaf transports water and nutrients to the cells.
Scientists examined the vascular strand structure to better understand plant growth.
Damage to the vascular strand can disrupt the flow of sap and affect plant health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vascular bundle - a bundle of vascular tissue in plants containing xylem and phloem
