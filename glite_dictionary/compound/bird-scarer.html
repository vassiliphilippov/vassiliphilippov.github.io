bird-scarer

1. NOUN

/ bɝd ˈskɛrɚ / (AmE), / bɜːd ˈskeərə / (BrE)
an object, often shaped like a person, placed in fields to scare birds away from crops or seeds
object to frighten birds from crops

Examples

The farmer placed a bird-scarer in the field to protect his newly sown seeds.
They built a homemade bird-scarer using old clothes and sticks.
A brightly colored bird-scarer can be surprisingly effective against crows and pigeons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarecrow - an object shaped like a human to scare birds from fields
