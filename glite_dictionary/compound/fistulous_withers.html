fistulous withers

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪstʃu·ləs ˈwɪðɚz / (AmE), / ˈfɪstjʊləs ˈwɪðəz / (BrE)
a chronic inflammatory disease of the withers in a horse, resulting in swelling and discharge
chronic inflammation of a horse's withers
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with fistulous withers after noticing swelling and persistent discharge.
Treatment for fistulous withers often requires antibiotics and surgical intervention.
If left untreated, fistulous withers can severely impair the animal's ability to work or perform.

Etymology

From 'fistulous' (having fistulas) + 'withers' (part of a horse's back).
