neat-freak

1. NOUN

/ ˈnit ˌfrik / (AmE), / ˈniːt ˌfriːk / (BrE)
a person who is excessively concerned with cleanliness and order
person overly focused on cleanliness
informal

Examples

My brother is such a neat-freak that he cleans his room twice a day.
As a neat-freak, she couldn't stand even a single crumb on the kitchen counter.
Being a neat-freak means you always notice the tiniest mess others overlook.

Synset Examples

Her obsession with cleanliness turned her into a neat-freak-zilla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean freak - person obsessively concerned with cleanliness

Etymology

From 'neat' (orderly, clean) + 'freak' (someone unusually fixated on something), popularized in late 20th-century American English.
