communicating artery

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmjuː.nɪˈkeɪ.tɪŋ ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / kəˌmjuː.nɪˈkeɪ.tɪŋ ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
any of three arteries in the brain that form part of the circle of Willis, connecting major cerebral arteries to ensure blood flow between them.
artery in the brain, part of circle of Willis
medical

Examples

The communicating artery plays a crucial role in maintaining consistent blood flow within the brain.
Aneurysms can sometimes occur on the posterior communicating artery, leading to neurological symptoms.
In medical imaging, a blocked communicating artery may be identified during a stroke diagnosis.

Etymology

From Latin 'communicare' (to share, to communicate) + 'arteria' (artery).
