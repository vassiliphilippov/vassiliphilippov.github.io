Virgin Birth

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrdʒɪn ˌbɝθ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn ˌbɜːθ / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the doctrine that Jesus Christ was born to Mary without a human father, by the power of the Holy Spirit.
belief that Jesus was born without a human father

Examples

The doctrine of the Virgin Birth is central to many Christian denominations.
Scholars have debated historical interpretations of the Virgin Birth for centuries.
At Christmas, many churches celebrate the Virgin Birth with special services and readings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nativity - the birth of Jesus Christ, especially as celebrated at Christmas
  • Synonym: Immaculate Conception - Roman Catholic doctrine that Mary was conceived without original sin (distinct from Virgin Birth)
