Santiago de Cuba

1. PROPN

/ ˌsæn.tiˈɑːɡoʊ də ˈkuːbə / (AmE), / ˌsan.tiˈɑː.ɡəʊ də ˈkuː.bə / (BrE)
a major naval battle of the Spanish-American War in 1898, where the United States fleet trapped and destroyed the Spanish fleet in the harbor of Santiago de Cuba.
1898 naval battle in the Spanish-American War

Examples

The Battle of Santiago de Cuba marked a decisive victory for the United States Navy.
Many historians consider Santiago de Cuba the turning point in the Spanish-American War.
The destruction of the Spanish fleet at Santiago de Cuba effectively ended Spain's colonial rule in the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Manila Bay - Another major naval battle of the Spanish-American War

Etymology

From Spanish, named after the city of Santiago de Cuba, Cuba.
