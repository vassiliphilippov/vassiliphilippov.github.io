packed cell volume

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækt sɛl ˈvɑl.jum / (AmE), / ˈpækt sɛl ˈvɒl.juːm / (BrE)
the ratio of the volume occupied by packed red blood cells to the volume of whole blood, measured by a hematocrit
ratio of red blood cells in blood (hematology)
medical

Examples

A low packed cell volume may indicate anemia in a patient.
The laboratory technician measured the packed cell volume using a centrifuge.
Changes in packed cell volume can help monitor dehydration levels in animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematocrit - the proportion of blood volume that is occupied by red blood cells
