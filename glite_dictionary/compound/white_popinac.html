white popinac

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈpoʊpɪˌnæk / (AmE), / waɪt ˈpɒpɪˌnæk / (BrE)
a low, scrubby tree native to tropical and subtropical North America, with white flowers tinged with yellow and long flattened pods, resembling mimosa
scrubby tree with white-yellow flowers, tropical America

Examples

The garden features several white popinac trees, which attract bees with their fragrant blossoms.
Farmers have planted white popinac as a shade tree for their crops in tropical regions.
The long pods of the white popinac make it easy to identify among the other shrubs.

Etymology

From Spanish 'popenac', originally from Nahuatl (Mexican indigenous language), referring to a kind of mimosa-like tree.
