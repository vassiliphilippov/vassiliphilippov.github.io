Gordian knot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔr.di.ən nɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡɔː.di.ən nɒt / (BrE)
A very complex or unsolvable problem; originally, an intricate knot tied by King Gordius, cut by Alexander the Great, symbolizing a difficult problem solved by bold action.
a complex or unsolvable problem

Examples

The political crisis quickly became a Gordian knot that no one could untangle.
Instead of trying to untie the Gordian knot, she made a decisive move to resolve the issue.
The merger talks turned into a Gordian knot of conflicting interests and regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enigma - something that is mysterious or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: solution - an answer to a problem

Etymology

From the legend of Gordius of Phrygia, whose knot was cut by Alexander the Great.
