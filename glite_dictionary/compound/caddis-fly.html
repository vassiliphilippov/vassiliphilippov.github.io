caddis-fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædɪs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkadɪs flaɪ / (BrE)
A small, moth-like insect with two pairs of hairy, membranous wings; its larvae are aquatic and often build protective cases from debris.
small moth-like aquatic insect

Examples

The riverbank was alive with caddis-flies fluttering above the water at dusk.
Aquatic larvae of the caddis-fly create protective cases out of sand, twigs, and leaves.
Anglers often imitate the appearance of a caddis-fly when tying fishing flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trichoptera - the scientific order to which caddis-flies belong

Etymology

From 'caddis', possibly from obsolete English for ribbon or strip of cloth, plus 'fly'.
