hydrated oxide

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪtɪd ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdreɪtɪd ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed by the combination of an oxide with water, often resulting in a substance containing hydroxide ions
compound of an oxide with water
medical

Examples

Rust is a common example of a hydrated oxide formed from iron and water.
The chemist analyzed the sample and identified it as a hydrated oxide.
Some pigments used in painting contain hydrated oxide compounds for their vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroxide - a compound containing the hydroxyl group (OH−)

Etymology

From 'hydrate' (from Greek hydōr, water) + 'oxide'.
