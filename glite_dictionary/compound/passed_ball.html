passed ball

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæst ˈbɔl / (AmE), / ˌpɑːst ˈbɔːl / (BrE)
(in baseball) a pitch that the catcher should have caught but failed to, allowing a base runner to advance to the next base.
baseball: pitch missed by catcher allowing runner to advance

Examples

The runner advanced to second base on a passed ball in the seventh inning.
A passed ball can be costly if there are runners in scoring position.
The pitcher was credited with a strikeout, but a passed ball allowed the batter to reach first.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wild pitch - a pitch that is so high, low, or wide that the catcher cannot stop it, allowing base runners to advance
