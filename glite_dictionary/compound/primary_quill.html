primary quill

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri kwɪl / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri kwɪl / (BrE)
one of the main flight feathers projecting along the outer edge of a bird's wing, crucial for flight
main flight feather on a bird's wing

Examples

The eagle lost a primary quill during its last molt.
A broken primary quill can affect a bird's ability to fly efficiently.
Scientists collected a primary quill for DNA analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primary feather - main large flight feather on a bird's wing
