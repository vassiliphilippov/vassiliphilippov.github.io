desertic soil

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɝːtɪk sɔɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːtɪk sɔɪl / (BrE)
soil that forms in arid or desert regions, characterized by low organic matter and limited water retention
soil found in desert regions

Examples

Desertic soil lacks nutrients and organic material, making plant growth extremely difficult.
Farming in areas with desertic soil requires careful irrigation and soil management.
Scientists study how desertic soil responds to rainfall to understand desert ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arid soil - soil found in regions with very low rainfall

Etymology

From 'desert' (arid region) + Latinic '-ic' (adjective-forming suffix); 'soil' from Old French 'sol'.
