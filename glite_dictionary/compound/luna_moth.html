luna moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːnə mɑːθ / (AmE), / ˈluːnə mɒθ / (BrE)
a large pale-green North American moth with long-tailed hind wings and a yellow crescent-shaped mark on each forewing
large pale-green North American moth

Examples

We spotted a beautiful luna moth resting on the garden wall last night.
The delicate wings of the luna moth shimmered in the light of the porch lamp.
A luna moth is rarely seen during the day because it is primarily nocturnal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: moth - an insect related to the butterfly, typically nocturnal

Etymology

From Latin 'lūna' (moon), referring to the moon-like shape of the markings.
