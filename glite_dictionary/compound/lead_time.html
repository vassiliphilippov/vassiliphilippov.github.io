lead time

1. NOUN

/ lid taɪm / (AmE), / liːd taɪm / (BrE)
the period of time between the initiation of a process and its completion, especially in manufacturing, business, or project management.
time between starting and finishing a process

Examples

Because of the short lead time, the company was able to deliver orders quickly.
Manufacturers strive to minimize lead time to improve efficiency and customer satisfaction.
Planning ahead helps reduce the lead time for major projects.

Synset Examples

the lead times for many publications can vary tremendously
planning is an area where lead time can be reduced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnaround time - the time taken to complete a process or fulfill a request

2. NOUN

/ lid taɪm / (AmE), / liːd taɪm / (BrE)
the period of time needed to complete a process or prepare for something, especially between the initiation and completion of an order or project
the time needed to complete a process

Examples

The supplier requires a lead time of three weeks before shipment.
Accurate forecasting helps reduce the lead time on new product launches.
Due to high demand, the lead time for this item may be longer than usual.

Synset Examples

Ensure you account for lead time in your plans.
We need a lead time of two weeks for orders.
The project has a lead time of six months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnaround time - the time taken to complete a process or fulfill a request
  • Synonym: delivery time - the time it takes for goods to reach a destination after ordering

Etymology

From 'lead' meaning 'to precede'; used in manufacturing and logistics since the early 20th century.
