shock wave

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑk weɪv / (AmE), / ʃɒk weɪv / (BrE)
a sharp change in pressure moving through a gas, liquid, or solid, usually caused by an explosion, impact, or other rapid energy release
sudden pressure wave from explosion or impact

Examples

The explosion sent a powerful shock wave through the surrounding buildings.
A meteor entering the atmosphere at high speed produced a visible shock wave.
The scientists measured the shock wave created by the supersonic jet.

Synset Examples

the explosion created a shock wave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure wave - a wave in which the disturbance is a variation in pressure

2. NOUN

/ ʃɑk weɪv / (AmE), / ʃɒk weɪv / (BrE)
a sudden, powerful wave of energy, especially one created by an explosion, earthquake, or other force; also, a strong reaction or disturbance caused by a surprising event
sudden, powerful wave or reaction

Examples

The explosion produced a massive shock wave that shattered nearby windows.
The scandal sent a shock wave through the entire organization.
A shock wave travels faster than the speed of sound in air.

Synset Examples

The earthquake sent a shock wave across the city.
The news of his departure caused a shock wave in the industry.
Her announcement created a shock wave among the employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blast wave - a wave of pressure caused by an explosion
