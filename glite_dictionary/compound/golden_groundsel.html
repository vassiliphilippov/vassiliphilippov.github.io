golden groundsel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈɡraʊndsəl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈɡraʊndsl̩ / (BrE)
a weedy herb native to the eastern United States and Texas, with golden-yellow flowers; sometimes considered invasive and occasionally classified in the genus Senecio
weedy North American herb with golden-yellow flowers

Examples

The golden groundsel produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in spring.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control the spread of golden groundsel in their flower beds.
In some regions, golden groundsel is appreciated for its early blooms but can become invasive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senecio - a large genus of plants in the daisy family, including many groundsels and ragworts
