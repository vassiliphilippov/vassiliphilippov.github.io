semiautomatic pistol

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˌɔtəˈmætɪk ˈpɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌɔːtəˈmætɪk ˈpɪstl̩ / (BrE)
A pistol that reloads itself after each shot and can fire one round per trigger pull until the magazine is empty.
pistol that reloads automatically after each shot

Examples

He purchased a semiautomatic pistol for target shooting at the range.
The police recovered the suspect's semiautomatic pistol at the crime scene.
Many law enforcement officers carry a semiautomatic pistol as their standard sidearm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolver - a pistol with a revolving cylinder holding several cartridges
