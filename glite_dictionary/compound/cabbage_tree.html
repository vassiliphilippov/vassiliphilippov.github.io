cabbage tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪdʒ triː / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪdʒ triː / (BrE)
an elegant tree native to New Zealand, either with a single trunk or branching trunks, each bearing terminal clusters of long, narrow leaves and large panicles of fragrant white, yellow, or red flowers
New Zealand tree with fragrant clustered flowers

Examples

The cabbage tree is instantly recognizable by its long, spiky leaves and tall, slender trunk.
In New Zealand, many gardens feature a mature cabbage tree as a striking focal point.
Several birds nest in the branches of the cabbage tree throughout the year.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪdʒ tri / (AmE), / ˈkabɪdʒ triː / (BrE)
a tropical tree with rough, unpleasant-smelling, toxic bark and strong, durable wood; its bark and seeds are used as a purgative, vermifuge, and narcotic
tropical tree with toxic bark used medicinally

Examples

The bark of the cabbage tree was once used to prepare traditional medicines.
Farmers warned that the cabbage tree is toxic to livestock if consumed.
Many indigenous communities have long valued the cabbage tree for its durable wood and medicinal properties.
