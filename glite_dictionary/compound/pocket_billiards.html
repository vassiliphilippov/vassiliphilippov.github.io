pocket billiards

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪt ˈbɪljɚdz / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ˈbɪliədz / (BrE)
any of various games, such as pool, played on a billiard table with six pockets into which balls are struck with a cue
cue games played on a table with six pockets

Examples

He enjoys playing pocket billiards at the local recreation center every weekend.
The rules of pocket billiards vary depending on the specific game, like eight-ball or nine-ball.
Pocket billiards requires skillful aim and strategic thinking to win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pool - a common form of pocket billiards played with 16 balls
  • Antonym: carom billiards - billiard games played without pockets
