smalltooth sawfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlˌtuθ ˈsɔˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlˌtuːθ ˈsɔːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large tropical sawfish (Pristis pectinata) with a long, narrow snout edged with small teeth, found in bays and estuaries
a species of sawfish found in tropical waters

Examples

The smalltooth sawfish is critically endangered due to habitat loss and fishing.
Researchers spotted a juvenile smalltooth sawfish near the mouth of the river.
Unlike other species, the smalltooth sawfish is not considered dangerous to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawfish - a family of large rays with long, saw-like snouts
