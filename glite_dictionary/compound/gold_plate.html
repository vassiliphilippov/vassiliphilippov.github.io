gold plate

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld pleɪt / (AmE), / ɡəʊld pleɪt / (BrE)
A piece of tableware or other object made of a base metal and coated with a thin layer of gold.
tableware coated with gold

Examples

The royal family dined on gold plate during the ceremony.
He inherited a collection of antique gold plate from his grandmother.
The opulent table was set with fine china and gold plate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: silver plate - tableware coated with silver

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld pleɪt / (AmE), / ɡəʊld pleɪt / (BrE)
a thin layer of gold applied to the surface of another material, typically for decoration or protection
thin layer of gold coated on a surface

Examples

The antique watch was covered in a thin gold plate that had started to wear away.
You can add elegance to cutlery by applying a gold plate to the handles.
Over time, the gold plate on the jewelry may fade with frequent use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gilding - the application of gold leaf or gold paint to a surface
