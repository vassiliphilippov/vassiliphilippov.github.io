sound judgment

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
the ability to make sensible and well-considered decisions
ability to make sensible decisions

Examples

Her sound judgment helped the company avoid a costly mistake.
Good leaders are often recognized for their sound judgment in difficult situations.
It's important to trust your sound judgment when making important life choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudence - the quality of being prudent; cautiousness
  • Antonym: recklessness - the quality of being without caution or concern for consequences
