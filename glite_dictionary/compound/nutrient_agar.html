nutrient agar

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtriənt ˈæɡɑr / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrɪənt ˈɑːɡɑː / (BrE)
a gelatinous culture medium containing nutrients and agar, used for growing microorganisms in laboratory settings
gelatinous medium for culturing microorganisms
medical

Examples

The scientist poured the bacteria onto the nutrient agar to observe colony growth.
Samples were cultured on nutrient agar plates to test for contamination.
Sterile technique is important when transferring organisms to nutrient agar.

Etymology

From Latin 'nutriens' (nourishing) and Malay 'agar-agar' (gelatinous substance from seaweed).
