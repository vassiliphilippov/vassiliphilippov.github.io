Blackwall hitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌwɔl hɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɔːl hɪtʃ / (BrE)
A simple knot used for temporarily attaching a rope or line to a hook, commonly in nautical contexts.
knot for attaching a line to a hook

Examples

The sailor used a Blackwall hitch to quickly secure the rope to the crane's hook.
When speed was essential, the crew relied on a Blackwall hitch for loading cargo.
You must remove the Blackwall hitch as soon as tension is released, or the line will slip.

Etymology

Named after Blackwall, a district in London associated with docks and shipping.
