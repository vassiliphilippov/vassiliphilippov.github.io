solid geometry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑlɪd dʒiˈɑmɪtri / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd dʒiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the branch of geometry that deals with three-dimensional figures such as cubes, spheres, and pyramids
geometry of three-dimensional figures

Examples

In high school, we studied solid geometry to learn about volumes and surface areas of shapes.
The architect used principles from solid geometry when designing the building's curved roof.
Her research focuses on new developments in solid geometry and their applications in engineering.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plane geometry - the branch of geometry dealing with two-dimensional figures
