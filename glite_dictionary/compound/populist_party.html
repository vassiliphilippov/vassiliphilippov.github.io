Populist Party

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑː.pjə.lɪst ˈpɑːr.ti / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.pjʊ.lɪst ˈpɑː.ti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, formed in 1891 to promote policies such as currency expansion and government control of railroads
U.S. political party (1891–1908)

Examples

The Populist Party gained widespread support from farmers in the Midwest during the 1890s.
Many economic reforms championed by the Populist Party were later adopted by other political groups.
Historians often study the rise and fall of the Populist Party to understand American populism.

Etymology

From 'populist', meaning supporter of the rights and power of the people, and 'party'; named for its appeal to common citizens.
