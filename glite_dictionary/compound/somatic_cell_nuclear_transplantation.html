somatic cell nuclear transplantation

1. NOUN

/ səˈmætɪk sɛl ˈnuːkliɚ trænsˌplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk sɛl ˈnjuːklɪə trænsˌplɑːnˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of transferring the nucleus and its genetic material from a somatic cell into an egg cell whose nucleus has been removed, commonly used in cloning and genetic research
transfer of a somatic cell nucleus to an egg cell
medical

Examples

Scientists used somatic cell nuclear transplantation to create genetically identical animals in the laboratory.
Somatic cell nuclear transplantation is a key technique for cloning mammals such as sheep and cows.
Advances in somatic cell nuclear transplantation have contributed to stem cell research and regenerative medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloning - the process of making an identical copy of an organism or cell

Etymology

Somatic from Greek sōmatikos (of the body), nuclear from Latin nucleus (kernel), transplantation from Latin transplantare (to plant across).
