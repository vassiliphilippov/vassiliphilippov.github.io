phase II

1. NOUN

/ feɪz tu / (AmE), / feɪz tuː / (BrE)
a stage in a clinical trial in which a treatment is given to a larger group of people to assess its efficacy and monitor possible side effects
second stage of a clinical trial
medical

Examples

The cancer drug is currently being tested in a phase II clinical trial.
During phase II, the researchers focus on whether the new treatment actually works.
Patient enrollment for the phase II study was completed last month.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phase I - first stage of a clinical trial assessing safety, dosage, and side effects
  • Degree Higher: phase III - third stage of a clinical trial evaluating effectiveness in large groups
