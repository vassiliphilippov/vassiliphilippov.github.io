cellular communication

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljələr kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which cells exchange information through chemical or electrical signals, enabling coordination of functions within an organism
exchange of information between cells
medical

Examples

The discovery of new molecules involved in cellular communication advanced our understanding of immune system responses.
Disruptions in cellular communication can lead to diseases such as cancer or diabetes.
Recent research focuses on how hormones facilitate cellular communication in the brain.

Synset Examples

This peptide plays a crucial role in cellular communication.

Etymology

From Latin 'cellula' (small room, cell) and Latin 'communicatio' (sharing, imparting).
