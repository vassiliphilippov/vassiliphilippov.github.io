Dow-Jones Industrial Average

1. NOUN

/ daʊ ˈdʒoʊnz ˌɪn.dəˈstrɪl ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / daʊ ˈdʒəʊnz ˌɪn.dəˈstrɪəl ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
an index that measures the stock performance of 30 prominent companies listed on the New York Stock Exchange and NASDAQ, used as a key indicator of overall market health
major U.S. stock market index

Examples

Many investors watch the Dow-Jones Industrial Average to gauge the overall health of the stock market.
When the Dow-Jones Industrial Average fell sharply, it signaled widespread concerns about the economy.
Analysts often compare company performance with movements in the Dow-Jones Industrial Average.

Synset Examples

the Dow Jones Industrial Average is the most widely cited indicator of how the stock market is doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock market index - an indicator measuring a section of the stock market

Etymology

Named after Charles H. Dow and Edward D. Jones, its creators, and the term 'industrial average' for the original focus on industrial stocks.
