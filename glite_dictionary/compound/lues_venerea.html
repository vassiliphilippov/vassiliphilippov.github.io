lues venerea

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.iːz vəˈnɪriə / (AmE), / ˈluː.iːz vɛˈnɪərɪə / (BrE)
A historical and medical term for syphilis, a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum, which progresses through several stages and can be transmitted from mother to child during pregnancy.
historical term for syphilis
medical archaic

Examples

The physician recognized the symptoms as those of lues venerea, common in the late 19th century.
Historical medical records make frequent references to patients suffering from lues venerea.
Congenital lues venerea can result in serious complications for newborns if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted disease caused by Treponema pallidum

Etymology

From Latin lues (plague, disease) + venerea (of Venus, i.e. of sexual love), referencing the association with sexual transmission.
