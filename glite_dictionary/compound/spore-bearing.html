spore-bearing

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɔr ˌbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɔː ˌbɛərɪŋ / (BrE)
Producing or bearing spores instead of seeds, especially in reference to certain plants, fungi, or microorganisms.
producing spores instead of seeds

Examples

Many ferns are spore-bearing plants, unlike flowering plants that reproduce using seeds.
The spore-bearing surface of the mushroom is found on the underside of its cap.
Mosses, as spore-bearing organisms, thrive in damp environments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seed-bearing - producing seeds, not spores
