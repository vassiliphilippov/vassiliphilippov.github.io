bestial behavior

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛstiəl bəˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / ˈbiːstɪəl bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
extremely cruel, savage, or brutal behavior; actions resembling those of a wild animal
cruel or savage conduct

Examples

The news article described the bestial behavior of the criminals during the attack.
Her outrage was fueled by the bestial behavior captured on camera.
The court condemned the defendant's bestial behavior towards his victims.

Synset Examples

His bestial behavior shocked everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutal - savagely violent or harsh; cruel
  • Synonym: inhuman - lacking human qualities of compassion and mercy; cruel
  • Antonym: humane - having or showing compassion or benevolence

Etymology

From Latin 'bestia' (beast) + 'behavior', literally 'beast-like behavior'.
