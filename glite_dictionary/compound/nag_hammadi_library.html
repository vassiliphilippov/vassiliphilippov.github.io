Nag Hammadi Library

1. PROPN

/ nɑɡ hɑˈmɑdi ˈlaɪbrɛri / (AmE), / næɡ hɑːˈmɑːdi ˈlaɪbrəri / (BrE)
a collection of 13 ancient papyrus codices discovered near Nag Hammadi in 1945, containing early Christian and Gnostic texts, mostly written in Coptic
collection of Gnostic and early Christian manuscripts

Examples

Scholars have studied the Nag Hammadi Library to gain insights into early Christian beliefs.
The discovery of the Nag Hammadi Library was a turning point in the study of Gnosticism.
Many texts in the Nag Hammadi Library were previously unknown to historians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dead Sea Scrolls - ancient Jewish religious manuscripts discovered near the Dead Sea
  • Synonym: Gnosticism - a collection of ancient religious ideas and systems

Etymology

Nag Hammadi is the Egyptian town near which the manuscripts were found; 'library' from Latin 'libraria' (book room).
