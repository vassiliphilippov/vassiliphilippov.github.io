foetal movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtəl ˈmuːv.mənt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.təl ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
the motion of a fetus within the uterus, usually detected by the 16th week of pregnancy
movement of a fetus in the womb
medical british

Examples

The doctor asked if she had felt any foetal movement yet during her pregnancy.
Increased foetal movement can sometimes indicate that the baby is healthy and active.
Most women become aware of foetal movement between the 16th and 25th week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quickening - the first noticeable movements of the fetus felt by the mother
  • Antonym: stillbirth - the birth of an infant that has died in the womb

Etymology

From Latin 'fetus' (offspring) and 'movement' from Latin 'movimentum'.
