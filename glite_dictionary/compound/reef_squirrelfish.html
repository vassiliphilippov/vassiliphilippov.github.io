reef squirrelfish

1. NOUN

/ riːf ˈskwɝːrlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / riːf ˈskwɪrəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a brightly colored fish of the squirrelfish family (Holocentridae), commonly found on coral reefs from Bermuda and Florida to northern South America
a type of tropical reef fish

Examples

The reef squirrelfish hides in crevices during the day and emerges at night to feed.
Divers were excited to spot a school of reef squirrelfish darting among the corals.
A bright red reef squirrelfish added a splash of color to the underwater scene.
