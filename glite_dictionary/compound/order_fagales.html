order Fagales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər fəˈɡeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə fəˈɡeɪliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of dicotyledonous trees in the subclass Hamamelidae, including beeches, oaks, birches, and related families.
an order of dicotyledonous trees in Hamamelidae
medical

Examples

Oak, beech, and birch trees all belong to the order Fagales.
The order Fagales contains several economically important timber species.
Botanists often study the characteristics that define the order Fagales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fagaceae - the beech family, one of the families within Fagales
  • Antonym: order Rosales - another taxonomic order of dicotyledonous plants

Etymology

From Latin 'Fagus' (beech) + '-ales' (order suffix in taxonomy).
