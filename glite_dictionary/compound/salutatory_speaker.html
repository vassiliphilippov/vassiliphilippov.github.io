salutatory speaker

1. NOUN

/ səˈluːtəˌtɔri ˈspiːkər / (AmE), / səˈljuːtətəri ˈspiːkə / (BrE)
a graduating student with the second highest academic rank, often chosen to deliver the opening address at graduation ceremonies.
graduation speaker with second highest rank

Examples

Our school's salutatory speaker gave an inspiring address to open the graduation ceremony.
The selection process for salutatory speaker was highly competitive this year.
After her years of hard work, Maria was named salutatory speaker for the class of 2022.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: valedictorian - graduating student with the highest academic rank

Etymology

From 'salutatory', meaning a welcoming or greeting speech, combined with 'speaker'. 'Salutatory' derives from Latin 'salutatorius', related to greeting.
