tactical intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tɪ.kəl ɪnˈtɛl.ə.d͡ʒəns / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tɪ.kəl ɪnˈtel.ɪ.dʒəns / (BrE)
Information about an enemy or environment used in the planning and execution of short-term military or security operations.
military information for tactical planning

Examples

The general relied on tactical intelligence to coordinate the troops during the surprise attack.
Accurate tactical intelligence can determine the outcome of a battlefield engagement.
They gathered tactical intelligence from drone surveillance before launching the mission.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: strategic intelligence - intelligence needed for planning and policy at the highest levels
