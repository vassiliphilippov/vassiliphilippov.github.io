visible horizon

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪzəbəl həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / ˈvɪzəbl həˈraɪzən / (BrE)
the apparent line in the distance where the earth and sky seem to meet
the line where earth and sky meet visually

Examples

From the mountaintop, the visible horizon stretched for miles across the open landscape.
As the sun set below the visible horizon, the sky turned shades of orange and red.
Sailors often use the visible horizon to help navigate when at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horizon - the line at which the earth's surface and the sky appear to meet
