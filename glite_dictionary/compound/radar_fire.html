radar fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪˌdɑr ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈreɪˌdɑː ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
Gunfire directed at a target that is being tracked using radar.
gunfire using radar tracking

Examples

The ship responded to the incoming aircraft with heavy radar fire.
To avoid radar fire, the pilot flew at a very low altitude.
Modern defense systems rely heavily on radar fire to intercept enemy missiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunfire - the shooting of guns or artillery
