rocker arm

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkər ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈrɒkə ɑːm / (BrE)
A lever pivoted at its center, especially used to open or close a valve in an internal combustion engine.
lever in engines to actuate valves

Examples

The mechanic inspected the rocker arm for signs of wear after hearing an unusual noise.
A broken rocker arm can prevent an engine valve from opening properly.
Many modern cars use an improved rocker arm design to increase efficiency and reduce friction.
