ancien régime

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑn.si.æ̃ ʁeɪˈʒiːm / (AmE), / ˌɒ̃.si.ɑ̃ ʁeɪˈʒiːm / (BrE)
The political and social system in France before the French Revolution; more generally, any former system of government that has been replaced.
former political/social system (esp. pre-Revolutionary France)

Examples

Historians often compare the ancien régime to the revolutionary government that replaced it.
After the revolution, supporters of the ancien régime were exiled or executed.
The reforms dismantled many institutions dating back to the ancien régime.

Etymology

From French 'ancien régime' meaning 'old rule.'

2. NOUN

/ ɑ̃.si.ɛ̃ ɹeɪˈʒiːm / (AmE), / ɒ̃.sjɛ̃ ʁeɪˈʒiːm / (BrE)
the political and social system in France before the French Revolution, or any similar outdated system
pre-revolutionary French social and political system

Examples

Many nobles benefited from privileges under the ancien régime.
The collapse of the ancien régime paved the way for modern democracy in France.
Some historians compare the company’s rigid hierarchy to an ancien régime.

Synset Examples

Historians debate the impact of the ancien régime.
Life under the ancien régime was marked by inequality.
The ancien régime in France led to the revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old order - the previous social or political structure, especially before a revolution

Etymology

From French 'ancien régime' meaning 'former rule', with 'ancien' meaning 'old, former' and 'régime' meaning 'system'.
