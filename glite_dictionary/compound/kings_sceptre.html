king's sceptre

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋz ˈsɛptər / (AmE), / kɪŋz ˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a ceremonial staff carried by a king as a symbol of authority and sovereignty
royal ceremonial staff of authority

Examples

The ornate king's sceptre was displayed in the royal treasury for all to see.
At the coronation, the monarch held the king's sceptre as a sign of his rule.
A golden king's sceptre was passed down through generations of royalty.

Synset Examples

In the play, the king's sceptre symbolized his power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sceptre - a staff carried by rulers as a symbol of authority
