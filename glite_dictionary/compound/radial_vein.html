radial vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl veɪn / (BrE)
A superficial vein running along the radial (thumb) side of the forearm, which unites with the ulnar vein to form the brachial vein.
vein on the radius side of the forearm
medical

Examples

The radial vein runs parallel to the radius bone on the lateral side of the forearm.
During the procedure, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the radial vein.
Blood from the radial vein eventually flows into the brachial vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulnar vein - superficial veins ascending the ulnar side of the forearm
  • Degree Higher: brachial vein - the vein formed by the union of the radial and ulnar veins in the arm

Etymology

From 'radial,' referring to the radius bone of the forearm, and 'vein' from Latin 'vena.'
