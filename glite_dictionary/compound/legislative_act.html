legislative act

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒ.ɪˌsleɪ.tɪv ækt / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒ.ɪ.slə.tɪv ækt / (BrE)
a law that has been formally passed by a legislative body such as a parliament or congress
law passed by a legislature

Examples

The new legislative act aims to improve environmental protection nationwide.
Each legislative act must be signed by the president before becoming law.
The assembly debated the implications of the legislative act for over a week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
