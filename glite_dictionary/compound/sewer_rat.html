sewer rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.ɚ ˌræt / (AmE), / ˈsjuː.ə ˌræt / (BrE)
A brown rat, especially one commonly found living in sewers or filthy urban environments.
brown rat living in sewers

Examples

The city's health department is working to control the population of sewer rats downtown.
After the heavy rain, a sewer rat was seen scurrying along the curb.
Stories about giant sewer rats have circulated among residents for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown rat - common wild rat species, also known as Norway rat
  • Antonym: black rat - a different species of rat, often found in drier places
