dwarf elm

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ɛlm / (AmE), / dwɔːf ɛlm / (BrE)
A fast-growing shrubby Asian tree, often cultivated in the United States as a shelter or ornamental plant.
a shrubby Asian elm tree used as ornament or shelter

Examples

The dwarf elm is often chosen for landscaping due to its compact size and rapid growth.
Many parks have planted dwarf elm as a natural windbreak along walking paths.
Horticulturists admire the dwarf elm for its resilience and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of tree in the genus Ulmus
