Korean Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ kəˈriən pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / kəˈriːən pəˈnɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
A peninsula in East Asia, situated between the Yellow Sea and the Sea of Japan, occupied mostly by North and South Korea.
peninsula in East Asia between the Yellow Sea and Sea of Japan

Examples

The Korean Peninsula has been divided into North and South Korea since 1945.
Tensions on the Korean Peninsula often have implications for global security.
The climate of the Korean Peninsula varies significantly between its northern and southern regions.
