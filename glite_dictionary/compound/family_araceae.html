family Araceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌɛəˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌæˈræsiː / (BrE)
A large family of flowering plants, also known as the arum family, that includes anthuriums, calla lilies, jack-in-the-pulpits, and philodendrons.
botanical family of arums (flowering plants)

Examples

Many popular houseplants, such as the philodendron, belong to the family Araceae.
The family Araceae is known for its distinctive spathe-and-spadix flowers.
Botanists have recently classified several new genera within the family Araceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arum - any plant of the Araceae family

Etymology

From New Latin Araceae, based on the type genus Arum.
