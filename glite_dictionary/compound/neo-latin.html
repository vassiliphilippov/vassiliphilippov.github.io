Neo-Latin

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.oʊˈlæt.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈlæt.ɪn / (BrE)
the form of Latin used since the Renaissance, especially for scientific, scholarly, and technical purposes
Latin language since the Renaissance

Examples

Many scientific names are derived from Neo-Latin to maintain consistency across languages.
The study of Neo-Latin literature reveals much about Renaissance intellectual life.
Botanists often use Neo-Latin terms when classifying new plant species.

Etymology

From Greek 'neos' (new) and Latin 'Latinus' (Latin).
