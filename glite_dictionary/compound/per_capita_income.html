per capita income

1. NOUN

/ pər ˈkæpətə ˈɪnˌkʌm / (AmE), / pə ˈkapɪtə ˈɪnˌkʌm / (BrE)
the average income earned per person in a given area in a specified year, calculated by dividing the total national income by the population
average income per person in a country

Examples

The country's per capita income rose significantly over the past decade.
Economists use per capita income to compare the prosperity of different nations.
Low per capita income often indicates limited economic development in a region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gross domestic product - total value of goods and services produced within a country
  • Synonym: per capita - for each person; per person

Etymology

From Latin 'per capita' meaning 'by heads' plus 'income'.

2. NOUN

/ pɚ ˈkæpɪtə ˈɪn.kʌm / (AmE), / pə ˈkæpɪtə ˈɪn.kʌm / (BrE)
The average income earned per person in a given area within a specified period, calculated by dividing the total income by the population.
average income per person

Examples

The country's per capita income increased steadily over the last decade.
Many economists use per capita income as an indicator of a nation's standard of living.
Regional differences in per capita income highlight economic disparities across the nation.

Synset Examples

The per capita income in this area has risen significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average income - the mean income within a group or area

Etymology

From Latin 'per capita' meaning 'by heads', and 'income'.
