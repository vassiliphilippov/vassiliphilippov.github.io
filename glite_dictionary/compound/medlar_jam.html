medlar jam

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛd.lɚ dʒæm / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lə dʒam / (BrE)
A sweet spread made by cooking the fruit of the medlar tree with sugar, commonly used as a preserve.
jam made from medlar fruit

Examples

I bought a jar of medlar jam at the farmers' market last weekend.
Her homemade medlar jam has a unique, slightly tart flavor that everyone loves.
They served warm scones with medlar jam and clotted cream for tea.

Synset Examples

He brought medlar jam to share at breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - a sweet spread made from fruit and sugar

Etymology

From 'medlar' (the fruit of Mespilus germanica) and 'jam' (preserved fruit spread).
