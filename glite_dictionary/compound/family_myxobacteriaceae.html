family Myxobacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌmɪksoʊˌbæktɪriəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌmɪksəʊˌbæktɪrɪəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of bacteria that primarily inhabit soil and dung, known for their complex social behavior and ability to form fruiting bodies.
family of soil-dwelling bacteria

Examples

Many members of the family Myxobacteriaceae exhibit cooperative behaviors unusual for bacteria.
Scientists discovered several new species within the family Myxobacteriaceae in soil samples from the rainforest.
The family Myxobacteriaceae is studied for its role in decomposing organic matter.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Myxobacteria' (from Greek 'myxa' meaning slime, and 'bacteria'), with '-ceae' denoting a family in taxonomy.
