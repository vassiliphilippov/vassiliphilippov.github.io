dead nettle

1. NOUN

/ dɛd ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / dɛd ˈnɛtl̩ / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Pilea, characterized by drooping green flower clusters and smooth, translucent stems and leaves.
a plant of the genus Pilea

Examples

The garden was filled with lush dead nettle growing along the shady paths.
Unlike true nettles, dead nettle does not sting when touched.
She planted some dead nettle in her flowerbed to add greenery and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pilea - a genus of plants with delicate stems and leaves

2. NOUN

/ dɛd ˈnɛtəl / (AmE), / dɛd ˈnɛtl / (BrE)
a foul-smelling perennial Eurasian herb with a green creeping rhizome, unrelated to true nettles, often with nettle-like leaves but no stinging hairs
a perennial Eurasian herb with nettle-like leaves

Examples

The garden was overrun with dead nettle growing between the flower beds.
Unlike true nettles, dead nettle does not sting when touched.
Botanists studied the distinctive flowers of the dead nettle for pollination research.

3. NOUN

/ dɛd ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / dɛd ˈnɛt.əl / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Lamium, characterized by clusters of small, usually purplish, two-lipped flowers, often resembling stinging nettles but without stinging hairs.
plant of the genus Lamium; non-stinging nettle

Examples

Many gardens feature the variegated dead nettle because it is easy to grow and non-invasive.
Unlike true nettles, dead nettle does not sting when touched.
The purple dead nettle bloomed early in the spring, carpeting the ground with color.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nettle - plant with stinging hairs; genus Urtica

Etymology

From 'dead' (meaning lacking stinging properties) + 'nettle' (from Old English netele).

4. NOUN

/ dɛd ˈnɛtl̩ / (AmE), / dɛd ˈnɛtl / (BrE)
a coarse, bristly Eurasian plant of the genus Lamium, with white or reddish flowers and leaves resembling a nettle, commonly found as a weed in the United States
Eurasian plant with nettle-like leaves and flowers

Examples

The garden was overrun with dead nettle after the spring rains.
Unlike stinging nettle, dead nettle does not irritate the skin on contact.
She planted dead nettle along the border for its attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle - any plant of the genus Urtica, known for stinging hairs

Etymology

So called because its leaves resemble stinging nettle but do not sting ('dead' meaning 'inactive').
