oral vowel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈɒrəl ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A vowel sound produced with airflow only through the mouth, without nasalization.
vowel made in the mouth without nasal airflow

Examples

French distinguishes between oral vowels and nasal vowels in its pronunciation.
In English, all vowel sounds are typically oral vowels.
You can identify an oral vowel by the lack of airflow through the nose during its articulation.

Synset Examples

An oral vowel is produced with the soft palate in a raised position, so that there is no airflow through the nasal cavity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nasal vowel - a vowel sound produced with airflow through the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'os, oris' (mouth) + English 'vowel'.
