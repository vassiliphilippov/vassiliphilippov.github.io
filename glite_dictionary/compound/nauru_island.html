Nauru Island

1. PROPN

/ naʊˈruː ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / naʊˈruː ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
a small island country in the central Pacific Ocean, southwest of Hawaii, in Micronesia west of the Gilbert Islands; the world’s third smallest country by area
island country in the central Pacific Ocean

Examples

Tourists rarely visit Nauru Island due to its remote location in the Pacific Ocean.
The phosphate mines on Nauru Island were once a major source of income for the country.
After a brief flight from Australia, we landed on Nauru Island and explored its unique landscape.

Etymology

From native Nauruan, possibly meaning 'I go,' and English 'island.'
