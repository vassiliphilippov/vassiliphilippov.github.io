sputum smear

1. NOUN

/ ˈspjuː.təm smɪr / (AmE), / ˈspjuː.təm smɪə / (BrE)
a laboratory test in which a sample of sputum is smeared on a glass slide for microscopic examination to aid in diagnosing lung diseases such as tuberculosis and cancer
test of sputum for lung disease diagnosis
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a sputum smear to test for tuberculosis.
A positive sputum smear can indicate the presence of mycobacteria in the lungs.
Technicians prepared the sputum smear and examined it under a microscope.
