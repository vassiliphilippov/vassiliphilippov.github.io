boutonneuse fever

1. NOUN

/ buːˌtɑːnˈuz ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / buːˌtɒnˈuːz ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a disease caused by a rickettsia transmitted by ticks, marked by fever and a characteristic rash, common in India and around the Mediterranean
tick-borne rickettsial disease with fever and rash
medical

Examples

boutonneuse fever is most commonly transmitted to humans through the bite of an infected tick.
Doctors in the Mediterranean often diagnose boutonneuse fever after patients present with a fever and spotted rash.
The spread of boutonneuse fever is closely linked to the presence of ticks on local dogs.

Etymology

From French 'boutonneuse' (with pustules), referring to the rash; first described in France.
