alpha privative

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ˈprɪvətɪv / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˈprɪvətɪv / (BrE)
a negative prefix, especially 'a-' or 'an-' used in words derived from Greek, expressing negation or absence
negative prefix, especially in Greek-derived words

Examples

The word 'atheist' contains an alpha privative indicating the absence of belief in deities.
Linguists often analyze the use of the alpha privative in classical Greek vocabulary.
In 'anarchy,' the prefix 'an-' functions as an alpha privative to signify 'without government.'

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha privativum', referring to the letter alpha used as a privative (negating) prefix.
