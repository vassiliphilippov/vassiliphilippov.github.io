forward-leaning strategy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwɚd ˌlinɪŋ ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd ˌliːnɪŋ ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
an approach characterized by proactive planning and actions to achieve progress or anticipate future developments, rather than reacting to events as they occur.
proactive approach or plan

Examples

The company adopted a forward-leaning strategy to stay ahead of market trends.
Her forward-leaning strategy positioned the organization for future growth.
By implementing a forward-leaning strategy, the team was prepared for upcoming industry changes.

Synset Examples

They adopted a forward-leaning strategy for growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proactive - taking action in advance rather than reacting later
  • Antonym: reactive - responding to events after they happen
