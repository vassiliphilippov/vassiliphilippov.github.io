alpine bearberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn ˈberˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn ˈbɛəˌbɛri / (BrE)
a low, creeping deciduous shrub found in alpine and circumpolar regions, known for its bright red autumn foliage and black or blue-black berries
creeping shrub with black berries, alpine regions

Examples

The alpine bearberry turns brilliant red in autumn, adding color to mountain slopes.
Hikers spotted several alpine bearberries growing along the rocky trail.
Birds often feed on the berries produced by the alpine bearberry.

Etymology

From 'alpine' (relating to high mountains) + 'bearberry' (so named because bears eat its berries).
