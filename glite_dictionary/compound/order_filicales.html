order Filicales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər fɪˈlaɪkəliz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə fɪˈlaɪkəliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of true, leptosporangiate ferns, comprising the largest group of living ferns distinguished by their unique spore production.
a taxonomic order of true ferns

Examples

Many of the most familiar ferns belong to the order Filicales.
The order Filicales is characterized by leptosporangiate spore development.
Botanists have long debated the evolutionary origins of the order Filicales.

Etymology

From Latin 'Filix' (fern) and scientific taxonomic suffix '-ales'.
