undirected graph

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dəˈrɛk.tɪd ɡræf / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈrɛk.tɪd ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A graph in which the edges have no direction; each edge simply connects two vertices without any orientation.
graph with edges lacking direction

Examples

A tree is an example of an undirected graph in graph theory.
In an undirected graph, connections between nodes do not have a set direction.
The algorithm works for both directed and undirected graphs but behaves differently in each case.

Synset Examples

In mathematics, and more specifically in graph theory, a tree is an undirected graph in which any two vertices are connected by exactly one path.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directed graph - a graph in which the edges have a direction

Etymology

Formed from 'undirected' (not directed) and 'graph' (from Greek 'graphos', meaning writing or diagram).
