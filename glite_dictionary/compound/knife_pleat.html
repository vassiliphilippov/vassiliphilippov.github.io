knife pleat

1. NOUN

/ naɪf pliːt / (AmE), / naɪf pliːt / (BrE)
A narrow, sharply pressed pleat in fabric, all turned in the same direction, often used in skirts and other garments.
narrow, sharp pleat turned in one direction

Examples

The dress featured a row of knife pleats along the hem, giving it elegant movement.
She carefully ironed each knife pleat to keep them crisp and defined.
The uniform skirt was designed with neat knife pleats for a classic look.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: box pleat - a double pleat made by folding fabric under at each side to form a box-like shape

Etymology

From 'knife' + 'pleat', named for its sharp, knife-like fold.
