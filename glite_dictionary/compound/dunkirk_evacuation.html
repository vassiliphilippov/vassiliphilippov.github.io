Dunkirk evacuation

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌn.kɝk ˌɛv.æk.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋ.kɜːk ˌiː.væk.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the evacuation of Allied troops from the beaches of Dunkirk, France, in 1940 during World War II, when over 300,000 soldiers were rescued under enemy fire
Allied evacuation from Dunkirk, 1940 (WWII)

Examples

The Dunkirk evacuation is remembered as one of the most daring rescue operations in military history.
Many families had relatives who were saved during the Dunkirk evacuation.
Documentaries have explored the personal stories of soldiers involved in the Dunkirk evacuation.

Etymology

Named after Dunkirk (French: Dunkerque), the French port city; 'evacuation' from Latin 'evacuare.'
