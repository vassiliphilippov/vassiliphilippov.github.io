geologic process

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑlədʒɪk ˈprɑsɛs / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒlədʒɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a natural process in which geological features of the Earth are created, altered, or destroyed
natural process affecting Earth's features

Examples

Erosion is a common geologic process that gradually wears away rocks and soil.
Volcanic eruptions are dramatic examples of a geologic process altering the landscape.
Over millions of years, plate tectonics act as a powerful geologic process shaping entire continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geological process - an alternative form of 'geologic process'
