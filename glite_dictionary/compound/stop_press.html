stop press

1. NOUN

/ stɑp prɛs / (AmE), / stɒp prɛs / (BrE)
news or information inserted into a newspaper at the last possible moment before printing, often of urgent or late-breaking nature
urgent late news in a newspaper

Examples

The editors added a stop press about the election results before the final print run.
A major accident just occurred, so the headline was changed in the stop press.
The stop press section often contains the latest updates unavailable in earlier editions.

2. NOUN

/ stɑp prɛs / (AmE), / stɒp prɛs / (BrE)
a late announcement or item of urgent news added to a newspaper or publication at the last moment before printing
urgent late news in publications

Examples

The editor ordered a stop press to cover the unexpected election results.
A stop press was added at the last minute to report the storm warning.
Don't miss the stop press at the bottom of the front page for urgent updates.
