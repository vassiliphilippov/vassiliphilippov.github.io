torsion bar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrʒən bɑr / (AmE), / ˈtɔːʃən bɑː / (BrE)
a metal bar that functions as a torsional spring, commonly used in the suspension systems of some vehicles to absorb shocks and support the chassis
metal spring bar for vehicle suspension

Examples

The front suspension of the car uses a torsion bar instead of traditional coil springs.
During the repair, the mechanic adjusted the torsion bar to correct the vehicle's ride height.
Many off-road vehicles rely on a torsion bar setup for their rugged suspension systems.

Synset Examples

Over-rotating the torsion bars can cause the suspension to hit the bump-stop prematurely, causing a harsh ride.

Etymology

From 'torsion' (twisting) and 'bar' (rod); used in mechanical engineering since the early 20th century.
