Egyptian jackal

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈdʒækəl / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈdʒækəl / (BrE)
a wild canid native to the deserts of northern Africa, closely related to the golden jackal and the grey wolf
wild canid of northern Africa

Examples

The Egyptian jackal has adapted to survive in harsh desert climates with little water.
Scientists are studying the genetic differences between the Egyptian jackal and other canids.
The Egyptian jackal is rarely seen during the day, preferring to hunt at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden jackal - a medium-sized canid found in Eurasia and Africa
  • Synonym: grey wolf - a large canid native to Eurasia and North America
