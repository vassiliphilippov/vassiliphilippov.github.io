Low German

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
a group of German dialects spoken mainly in northern Germany and parts of the Netherlands and Denmark
German dialects of northern Germany

Examples

Many older residents in the region still speak Low German at home.
The stories were translated from Low German into standard German for publication.
Linguists study Low German to trace the history of northern European languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: High German - a group of German dialects spoken mainly in southern Germany, Austria, and Switzerland

Etymology

From English 'low' (referring to the lowland regions) and 'German'; after German 'Niederdeutsch'.
