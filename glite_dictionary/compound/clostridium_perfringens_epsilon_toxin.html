Clostridium perfringens epsilon toxin

1. NOUN

/ klɑːˈstrɪdiəm pərˈfrɪndʒənz ˈɛpsɪlɒn ˈtɑksən / (AmE), / klɒˈstrɪdiəm pəˈfrɪndʒənz ˈɛpsɪlɒn ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a potent bacterial toxin produced by Clostridium perfringens, responsible for causing severe abdominal cramps and diarrhea after ingestion of contaminated food
toxin from Clostridium perfringens causing food poisoning
medical

Examples

Food poisoning caused by Clostridium perfringens epsilon toxin can result in severe diarrhea and abdominal pain.
Scientists are studying how Clostridium perfringens epsilon toxin affects the intestines of animals and humans.
After the outbreak, tests confirmed the presence of Clostridium perfringens epsilon toxin in several food samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms

Etymology

Clostridium from Latin 'klōstēr' (spindle); epsilon from Greek 'ἒ ψιλόν' (the letter E); toxin from Latin 'toxicum' (poison).
