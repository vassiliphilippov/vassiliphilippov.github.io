U.S. Congress

1. NOUN

/ juː ˈɛs ˈkɑːŋɡrəs / (AmE), / juː ˈɛs ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
the legislature of the United States, consisting of the Senate and the House of Representatives
legislature of the United States
american

Examples

The U.S. Congress passed a new law on environmental protection last week.
Members of the U.S. Congress debated the proposed budget for several hours.
The president addressed the U.S. Congress during his annual State of the Union speech.
