pellitory-of-the-wall

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪˌtɔri əv ðə ˈwɔl / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˌtɒri əv ðə ˈwɔːl / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant (Parietaria judaica) with long narrow leaves and small pink, petal-less flowers, often found growing in wall crevices
a herbaceous plant found in walls

Examples

The old garden wall was covered in pellitory-of-the-wall, its leaves shimmering in the sunlight.
Botanists noted that pellitory-of-the-wall thrives in urban environments where mortar decay provides ideal crevices.
She recognized the pellitory-of-the-wall by its tiny pink flowers and its position growing between bricks.

Etymology

The name refers to the plant's habit of growing on walls; 'pellitory' from Old French 'peletre', from Latin 'parietaria'.
