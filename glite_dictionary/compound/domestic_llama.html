domestic llama

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɛstɪk ˈjɑmə / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪk ˈlɑːmə / (BrE)
A domesticated South American mammal used as a pack animal and for its wool, descended from the guanaco.
a domesticated South American pack animal

Examples

The domestic llama has been bred for centuries to carry loads across mountainous terrain.
Farmers in Peru still depend on the domestic llama for wool and transportation.
Unlike its wild relatives, the domestic llama is gentle and accustomed to human contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guanaco - wild South American camelid, ancestor of the llama
  • Synonym: alpaca - a South American mammal related to the llama, valued for its wool

Etymology

From Spanish 'llama', from Quechua 'lama'; 'domestic' from Latin 'domesticus'.
