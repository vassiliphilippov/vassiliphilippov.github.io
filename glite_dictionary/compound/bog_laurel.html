bog laurel

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
a small, evergreen shrub of boggy areas in northwestern North America, known for its purple flowers and pale, glaucous leaves
evergreen shrub with purple flowers in bogs

Examples

We spotted a beautiful bog laurel blooming by the lake's edge.
The pale leaves of the bog laurel have a glaucous underside.
Hikers are warned not to touch the bog laurel as it can be toxic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laurel - any of various shrubs or trees of the family Lauraceae
