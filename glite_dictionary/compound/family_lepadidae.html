family Lepadidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli lɪˈpædɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli lɛˈpædɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of stalked barnacles known as goose barnacles, found attached to floating objects in the sea.
a family of stalked goose barnacles

Examples

The family Lepadidae includes many species of goose barnacles commonly seen on driftwood.
Researchers discovered a new member of the family Lepadidae attached to a ship's hull.
Members of the family Lepadidae have long, flexible stalks and shell plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose barnacle - a stalked barnacle often found attached to ships or driftwood

Etymology

From New Latin *Lepadidae*, based on *Lepas*, the type genus.
