banker's acceptance

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkərz əkˈsɛptəns / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkəz əkˈsɛptəns / (BrE)
a bill of exchange guaranteed by a bank, promising payment at a future date
bill of exchange promised by a bank

Examples

The company used a banker's acceptance to finance its import transaction.
With a banker's acceptance, the exporter receives payment guaranteed by the buyer's bank.
Many international trade deals rely on banker's acceptances for secure payment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill of exchange - a written order to pay a particular amount to a named person or the bearer
