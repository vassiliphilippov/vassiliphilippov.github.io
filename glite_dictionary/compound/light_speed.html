light speed

1. NOUN

/ laɪt spiːd / (AmE), / laɪt spiːd / (BrE)
the speed at which light travels in a vacuum, defined as exactly 299,792,458 meters per second
the speed of light in a vacuum

Examples

Many scientists believe nothing can travel faster than light speed.
Spacecraft propulsion systems are far from reaching light speed.
Einstein's theory of relativity relies on the constancy of light speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed of light - the speed at which light propagates through a vacuum
