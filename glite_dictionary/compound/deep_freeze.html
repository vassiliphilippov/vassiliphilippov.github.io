deep freeze

1. NOUN

/ ˈdip ˌfriz / (AmE), / ˈdiːp ˌfriːz / (BrE)
a state in which progress or activity is temporarily halted or suspended, especially for an extended period
a period of inactivity or suspension

Examples

The proposal remains in the deep freeze until further notice.
The awards program was taken out of the deep freeze after several years of inactivity.
Many projects went into a deep freeze during the budget crisis.

Synset Examples

the legislation has now been revived after ten years in the deep freeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiatus - a pause or break in continuity
  • Synonym: suspension - the action of temporarily stopping something

Etymology

Originally a term for a device for freezing food, later used figuratively for inactivity.

2. NOUN

/ diːp friːz / (AmE), / diːp friːz / (BrE)
A period of extremely cold or freezing temperatures, especially one lasting for several days.
period of extreme cold

Examples

The entire region was hit by a deep freeze that lasted more than a week.
After the deep freeze, pipes burst in several homes due to the intense cold.
Meteorologists warned that another deep freeze was approaching the northern states.

Synset Examples

They needed a day to thaw after the deep freeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold snap - a short period of cold weather
  • Antonym: heatwave - a prolonged period of excessively hot weather
