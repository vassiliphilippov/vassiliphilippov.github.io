cotton-seed tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtənˌsid tri / (AmE), / ˈkɒtənˌsiːd triː / (BrE)
a shrub native to salt marshes of eastern and south central North America and the West Indies, whose fruit is surrounded by white, plumelike hairy tufts
marsh shrub with tufted fruit

Examples

The cotton-seed tree is easy to spot in salt marshes because of its fluffy fruit.
During their hike, they identified several cotton-seed trees growing near the water's edge.
The white tufts on the cotton-seed tree help its seeds disperse on the wind.
