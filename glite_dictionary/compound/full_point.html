full point

1. NOUN

/ fʊl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / fʊl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the punctuation mark (.) used to indicate the end of a declarative sentence or after abbreviations; called 'period' in American English.
punctuation mark (.) at sentence end
british

Examples

Please place a full point at the end of each sentence.
In British English, a period is usually called a full point.
Abbreviations such as 'etc.' are followed by a full point in formal writing.

Synset Examples

in England they call a period a ‘stop’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - the punctuation mark (.) used in American English for the same function
