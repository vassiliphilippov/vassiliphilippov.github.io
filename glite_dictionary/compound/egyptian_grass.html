Egyptian grass

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ɡræs / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a creeping grass with finger-like spikes, often found in tropical and subtropical regions
a creeping grass with finger-like spikes

Examples

Farmers in arid regions often cultivate Egyptian grass for its drought resistance.
The field was covered in thick, green Egyptian grass, providing excellent grazing for the cattle.
We examined the unique, finger-like spikes of the Egyptian grass during our botany expedition.
