wall pellitory

1. NOUN

/ wɔl ˈpɛlɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / wɔːl ˈpɛlɪtəri / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant that grows in crevices, with long narrow leaves and small pink apetalous flowers, commonly found on old walls and rocks
herb with narrow leaves and pink flowers, grows in crevices

Examples

The old stone fence was covered in wall pellitory thriving between the cracks.
We noticed wall pellitory growing along the abandoned building’s walls.
Gardeners often remove wall pellitory because it spreads quickly in rocky areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pellitory - a name for several plants of the genus Parietaria or Anacyclus

Etymology

From 'pellitory', ultimately from Latin *parietaria* (wall-growing), referring to its tendency to grow on walls.
