first-aid station

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝːst ˈeɪd ˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈeɪd ˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
A place where emergency care or treatment is provided to people before they can receive regular medical aid.
place for emergency medical care

Examples

She went to the first-aid station after spraining her ankle at the park.
Medical volunteers staffed a first-aid station near the start of the marathon.
The festival organizers set up a first-aid station in case anyone needed immediate help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infirmary - a place for the care of the sick or injured; especially a hospital or clinic
  • Synonym: hospital - an institution for medical and surgical treatment
