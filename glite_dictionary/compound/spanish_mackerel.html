Spanish mackerel

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈmækərəl / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ ˈmakərəl / (BrE)
a large marine fish of the genus Scomberomorus, valued as food and found in warm ocean waters
a large edible marine fish

Examples

The waters off the coast are well known for their abundant Spanish mackerel.
Grilled Spanish mackerel is a popular dish in many seaside restaurants.
Fishermen hope to catch large Spanish mackerel during the summer season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a species of fast-swimming marine fish with a streamlined body
