Ohio buckeye

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈhaɪ.oʊ ˈbʌk.aɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈhaɪ.əʊ ˈbʌk.aɪ / (BrE)
a deciduous tree (Aesculus glabra) with scaly grey bark, native to the central United States, especially Ohio.
a tree native to central U.S.

Examples

The Ohio buckeye is the state tree of Ohio and is known for its distinctive nuts.
In autumn, the leaves of the Ohio buckeye turn a brilliant yellow.
The park is home to several mature Ohio buckeye trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckeye - a tree of the genus Aesculus, especially Aesculus glabra
