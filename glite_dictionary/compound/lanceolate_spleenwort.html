lanceolate spleenwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænsiəˌleɪt ˈspliːnwɜːrt / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnsiəˌleɪt ˈspliːnwɜːt / (BrE)
a species of spleenwort fern native to western Europe, known for its lance-shaped fronds
a species of spleenwort fern

Examples

The lanceolate spleenwort grows on rocky slopes in parts of western Europe.
Botanists identified a rare lanceolate spleenwort in the limestone crevice.
Leaves of the lanceolate spleenwort are characteristically long and narrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spleenwort - a type of fern in the genus Asplenium

Etymology

From Latin 'lanceolatus' (lance-shaped) and 'spleenwort', referring to the shape of the fern's fronds.
