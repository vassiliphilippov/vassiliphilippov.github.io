genus Lasiurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs læs.iˈjʊər.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs las.iˈjʊə.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, known as hairy-tailed bats.
genus of hairy-tailed bats

Examples

The genus Lasiurus includes several species of hairy-tailed bats found throughout the Americas.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Lasiurus during their field expedition.
The distinguishing feature of the genus Lasiurus is the fur that extends along the tail membrane.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Vespertilionidae - the family of vesper bats to which genus Lasiurus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Lasiurus', from Greek 'lasios' (hairy) and 'oura' (tail).
