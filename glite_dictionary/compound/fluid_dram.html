fluid dram

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪd dræm / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪd dræm / (BrE)
a unit of liquid capacity in the British imperial system, equal to 60 minims or approximately 3.55 milliliters
British unit of liquid capacity

Examples

The pharmacist measured out a fluid dram of the tincture for the patient.
Recipes from the 19th century sometimes call for a fluid dram of a particular ingredient.
Each fluid dram contains precisely 60 minims, making it useful for small doses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fluid ounce - a larger unit of liquid capacity in the imperial and US customary systems
  • Degree Lower: minim - a smaller unit of liquid capacity, used in pharmacy

Etymology

From 'fluid', relating to liquid, and 'dram', from Late Latin 'dragma', a unit of weight or volume.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪd dræm / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪd dræm / (BrE)
a unit of liquid capacity in the apothecary system, equal to one eighth of a fluid ounce (approximately 3.55 milliliters)
unit of liquid volume (1/8 fluid ounce)

Examples

The prescription required exactly one fluid dram of the medication.
In old recipes, a fluid dram was sometimes used to measure liquid ingredients.
A fluid dram is about 3.55 milliliters in the metric system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fluid ounce - a unit of liquid capacity in the US and British systems
  • Synonym: milliliter - metric unit of volume, one thousandth of a liter

Etymology

From Latin 'dragma', from Greek 'drakhmē' meaning a drachm or a handful.
