pelican crossing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlɪkən ˈkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛlɪkən ˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of pedestrian crossing with traffic lights controlled by pedestrians, commonly found in the UK
pedestrian crossing with pedestrian-controlled lights
british

Examples

When the light turns green at the pelican crossing, pedestrians can safely cross the road.
A pelican crossing makes it easier for people to stop traffic and cross busy streets.
Drivers must stop when the signal changes at a pelican crossing near schools.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zebra crossing - a pedestrian crossing marked with white stripes, no pedestrian-controlled lights

Etymology

Acronym from ‘PEdestrian LIght CONtrolled’ crossing.
