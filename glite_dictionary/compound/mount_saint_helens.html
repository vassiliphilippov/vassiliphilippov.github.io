Mount Saint Helens

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt seɪnt ˈhɛlənz / (AmE), / maʊnt seɪnt ˈhɛlənz / (BrE)
an active volcano in the Cascade Range in southwestern Washington State, known for its major eruption in 1980
active volcano in Washington State, USA

Examples

The eruption of Mount Saint Helens in 1980 was one of the most significant volcanic events in U.S. history.
Scientists continue to monitor Mount Saint Helens for signs of future volcanic activity.
Hiking trails around Mount Saint Helens attract thousands of outdoor enthusiasts every year.

Etymology

Named after British diplomat Alleyne Fitzherbert, Baron St Helens.
