megaloblastic anemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəloʊˈblæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊˈblæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A type of anemia characterized by the presence of unusually large, immature, and dysfunctional red blood cells (megaloblasts) in the bone marrow, usually due to vitamin B12 or folic acid deficiency.
anemia with large, abnormal red blood cells
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with megaloblastic anemia after presenting with fatigue and laboratory findings of large red blood cells.
Vitamin B12 deficiency is a common cause of megaloblastic anemia in older adults.
Treatment for megaloblastic anemia typically includes supplementation of vitamin B12 or folic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pernicious anemia - a severe type of anemia caused by vitamin B12 deficiency

Etymology

From Greek 'megas' (large), 'blastos' (germ, sprout), and 'anemia' (lack of blood).
