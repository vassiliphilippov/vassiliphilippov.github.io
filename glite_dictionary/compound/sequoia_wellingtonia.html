Sequoia Wellingtonia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə ˌwɛlɪŋˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə ˌwɛlɪŋˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A very tall evergreen tree native to the southern end of the western Sierra Nevada foothills in California, known as the largest living organism.
giant evergreen tree of California

Examples

The Sequoia Wellingtonia is the largest tree and one of the oldest living things on Earth.
Many tourists visit California to marvel at the imposing Sequoia Wellingtonia groves in national parks.
A single Sequoia Wellingtonia can grow over 80 meters tall and live for thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant sequoia - another common name for Sequoia Wellingtonia
  • Synonym: redwood - tall evergreen trees related to Sequoia Wellingtonia, including both coastal redwoods and giant sequoias

Etymology

Sequoia: named after Sequoyah, a Cherokee leader; Wellingtonia: named in honor of Arthur Wellesley, Duke of Wellington.
