Isere River

1. PROPN

/ iˈzɛr ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / iˈzɛə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southeastern France that flows into the Rhone
river in southeastern France

Examples

The Isere River winds through beautiful Alpine valleys before reaching the Rhone.
During spring, the melting snow swells the Isere River and raises its water level.
Many charming towns are located along the banks of the Isere River in France.

Etymology

From French Isère, from Latin Isara, of pre-Latin (Gaulish) origin.
