picture plane

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪktʃər pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɪktʃə pleɪn / (BrE)
the imaginary flat surface in a drawing or painting that is perceived as closest to the viewer, often coinciding with the literal surface of the artwork
imaginary plane perceived at the surface of an artwork

Examples

The artist sketched a vase that appeared to float just in front of the picture plane.
Cubist painters often played with the picture plane to create a sense of spatial ambiguity.
She instructed her students to imagine the picture plane as a window through which to view the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - the outside layer of something
