Scotch fir

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ faɪr / (AmE), / skɒtʃ faɪə / (BrE)
a medium to large pine tree (Pinus sylvestris) of northern Europe and Asia, known for its two-needled leaves and flaking red-brown bark
a pine tree of northern Europe and Asia

Examples

A towering Scotch fir stood alone at the edge of the field.
The timber from the Scotch fir is valued for its strength and durability.
She planted a young Scotch fir in her garden last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scots pine - medium to large pine tree native to Europe and Asia

Etymology

From 'Scotch' (Scottish) and 'fir', though the tree is a pine, not a true fir.
