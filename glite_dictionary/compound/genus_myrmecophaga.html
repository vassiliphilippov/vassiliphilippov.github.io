genus Myrmecophaga

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs mɪrˌmiːkəˈfeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs mɜːˌmiːkəˈfeɪɡə / (BrE)
The genus of mammals in the family Myrmecophagidae, comprising the giant anteater native to South America.
genus of South American anteaters

Examples

The genus Myrmecophaga includes the well-known giant anteater found throughout Central and South America.
Scientific studies of the genus Myrmecophaga have provided important insight into the evolution of ant-eating mammals.
The distribution of animals belonging to the genus Myrmecophaga is limited mostly to tropical forests and grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myrmecophagidae - the family comprising anteaters

Etymology

From Latin 'myrmecophaga,' meaning 'ant-eater', from Greek 'myrmēx' (ant) + 'phagein' (to eat).
