satin bowerbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæt̬.ɪn ˈbaʊ.ɚ.bɝd / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ɪn ˈbaʊ.ə.bɜːd / (BrE)
A species of bowerbird found in southeast Australia, known for the male's glossy violet-blue plumage and elaborate courtship displays.
Australian bird; male is glossy blue

Examples

The male satin bowerbird decorates his bower with blue objects to attract a mate.
A satin bowerbird was spotted in the forest, its feathers shimmering in the sunlight.
In documentaries, the elaborate courtship rituals of the satin bowerbird are often featured.
