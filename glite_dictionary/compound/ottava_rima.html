ottava rima

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɑvə ˈrimə / (AmE), / ɒˌtɑːvə ˈriːmə / (BrE)
A stanza of eight lines of heroic verse with a rhyme scheme of abababcc, especially used in Italian and English epic poetry.
eight-line stanza with abababcc rhyme

Examples

Byron frequently used ottava rima in his narrative poems.
The poet experimented with ottava rima to add structure to his epic.
Writing in ottava rima requires careful attention to rhyme and meter.

Etymology

From Italian 'ottava rima', meaning 'eighth rhyme'.
