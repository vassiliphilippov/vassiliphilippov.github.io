Standard and Poor's Index

1. NOUN

/ ˌstændərd ən ˈpʊrz ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˌstændəd ænd ˈpɔːz ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
an index measuring the performance of a broad selection of stocks, widely used as a benchmark for the stock market
major stock market index

Examples

Many investors use the Standard and Poor's Index to gauge overall market performance.
The Standard and Poor's Index closed at a record high today, signaling optimism in the economy.
Mutual funds often aim to match the returns of the Standard and Poor's Index.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: S&P 500 - index of 500 leading U.S. publicly traded companies
  • Synonym: Dow Jones Industrial Average - another major U.S. stock market index

Etymology

Named after the financial services company Standard & Poor's, which created and maintains the index.
