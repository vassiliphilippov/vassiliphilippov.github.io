heath family

1. NOUN

/ hiːθ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / hiːθ ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Ericaceae) that includes heathers, blueberries, and rhododendrons, typically found in acidic soils
family of plants including heathers and rhododendrons

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate plants from the heath family for their colorful blooms and evergreen foliage.
Azaleas, which belong to the heath family, are popular ornamental shrubs in many regions.
The heath family thrives in acidic soils, making it common in moorlands and peat bogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ericaceae - the botanical name for the heath family of plants
