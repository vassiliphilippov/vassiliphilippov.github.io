order Stereospondyli

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌstɪrioʊˈspɒndɪlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌstɪərɪəʊˈspɒndɪlaɪ / (BrE)
a formerly recognized group of extinct amphibians in which the vertebrae have component elements fused into a single piece; classified as a suborder of Stegocephalia.
extinct amphibian group with fused vertebrae

Examples

Fossils of the order Stereospondyli provide critical clues about the evolution of early amphibians.
The order Stereospondyli is known for its unique solid vertebral structure among Stegocephalian amphibians.
Many species in the order Stereospondyli lived during the Triassic period and are now extinct.

Etymology

From Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'spondylos' (vertebra), referring to the fused vertebrae characteristic of the group.
