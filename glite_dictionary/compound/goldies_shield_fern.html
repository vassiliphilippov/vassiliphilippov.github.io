Goldie's shield fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldiːz ˈʃiːld fɝːn / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldiːz ˈʃiːld fɜːn / (BrE)
a large North American fern with a blackish, glossy stipe, scientifically known as Dryopteris goldiana
a North American fern species

Examples

The shady ravine was filled with Goldie's shield fern and other native plants.
She identified the glossy stipe as a feature of Goldie's shield fern during her hike.
Botanists study Goldie's shield fern for its unique adaptation to moist woodland environments.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist John Goldie (1793–1886).
