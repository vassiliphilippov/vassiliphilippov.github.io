course of action

1. NOUN

/ kɔrs əv ˈækʃən / (AmE), / kɔːs əv ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a way of acting; a plan or procedure to achieve a particular result
plan or method of acting

Examples

After careful consideration, they decided on a new course of action for the project.
Changing circumstances forced the company to alter its course of action.
The committee debated which course of action would lead to the best outcome.

Synset Examples

if you persist in that course you will surely fail
once a nation is embarked on a course of action it becomes extremely difficult for any retraction to take place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term aim
  • Synonym: approach - a way of dealing with something

2. NOUN

/ kɔrs əv ˈækʃən / (AmE), / kɔːs əv ˈækʃən / (BrE)

plan or strategy to follow

Examples

She recommended a new course of action to solve the ongoing issue.
The committee will outline its course of action at the next meeting.

Synset Examples
