vinyl record

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.nəl ˈrɛk.ərd / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.nɪl ˈrɛk.ɔːd / (BrE)
an analog sound storage medium in the form of a flat disc made of vinyl, commonly used for playing music
analog music disc made of vinyl

Examples

He found a rare vinyl record at the local flea market.
Many audiophiles prefer the sound quality of vinyl records over digital music.
My father still plays his old vinyl records on a turntable every weekend.

Synset Examples

The musicmonger had an impressive collection of vinyl records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonograph record - analog sound storage medium, general term

Etymology

From 'vinyl', referring to the material polyvinyl chloride (PVC), and 'record', from Latin 'recordari' (to recall).
