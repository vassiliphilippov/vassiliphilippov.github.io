family Stichaeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli staɪˈkiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli stɪˈkiːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated marine fishes, commonly known as pricklebacks, found primarily in the North Pacific Ocean.
a family of marine prickleback fishes

Examples

The family Stichaeidae includes a variety of elongated marine fish known as pricklebacks.
Marine biologists study the family Stichaeidae to understand their unique adaptations to cold waters.
Many species within the family Stichaeidae are characterized by their spiny dorsal fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickleback - a common name for fishes in the Stichaeidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Stichaeidae, based on the type genus Stichaeus.
