thick-billed murre

1. NOUN

/ θɪk bɪld mɜr / (AmE), / θɪk bɪld mɜː / (BrE)
a species of large auk (Uria lomvia) of Arctic seas, with a short, thick bill and black-and-white plumage
a species of Arctic seabird, Uria lomvia

Examples

During their summer breeding season, thick-billed murres nest in dense colonies on remote Arctic cliffs.
A thick-billed murre can dive to great depths in search of fish and crustaceans.
Scientists track the migration routes of thick-billed murre populations as climate change alters their environment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: common murre - a related species of murre, Uria aalge
