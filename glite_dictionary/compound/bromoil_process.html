bromoil process

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.mɔɪl ˈprɑː.sɛs / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.mɔɪl ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
an early photographic printing technique, based on the oil print process, in which a silver bromide print is bleached and replaced with an oil-based ink image
early photographic printing technique

Examples

The bromoil process was popular among photographers seeking painterly effects in the early 20th century.
He created stunning images by mastering the intricate bromoil process.
Many historical exhibitions feature prints made using the bromoil process.

Etymology

Formed from 'bromide' (for silver bromide paper) and 'oil' (for the ink used), referring to the materials involved.
