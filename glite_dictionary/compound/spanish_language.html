Spanish language

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæn.ɪʃ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspan.ɪʃ ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in most of Spain and many countries in Latin America and elsewhere, descended from Latin
Romance language of Spain and Latin America

Examples

She studied Spanish language for three years before traveling to South America.
Many schools offer courses in the Spanish language starting in elementary grades.
Learning the Spanish language can open doors to new cultures and business opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - belonging to or relating to Spain or its people or language

Etymology

From Latin 'Hispania' (Spain) and English 'language'.
