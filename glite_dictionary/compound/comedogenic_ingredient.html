comedogenic ingredient

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːməˌdoʊˈdʒɛnɪk ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒməˌdəʊˈdʒɛnɪk ɪŋˈɡriːdiənt / (BrE)
an ingredient in cosmetics or skincare products that tends to clog pores, potentially leading to acne or blackheads
ingredient that clogs pores

Examples

People with acne-prone skin should avoid products that contain comedogenic ingredients.
She checked the label carefully to make sure there were no comedogenic ingredients in her moisturizer.
Using a cleanser without comedogenic ingredients can help prevent breakouts.

Synset Examples

The efficiency of comedogenic ingredients is determined by one's skin type.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-comedogenic - not tending to clog pores

Etymology

From 'comedogenic', which derives from Latin comedo (worm, glutton; in medicine, blackhead) + -genic (producing).
