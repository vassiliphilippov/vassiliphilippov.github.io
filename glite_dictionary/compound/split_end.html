split end

1. NOUN

/ splɪt ɛnd / (AmE), / splɪt ɛnd / (BrE)
(in American football) an offensive end who lines up some distance away from the other linemen, usually to receive passes.
football wide receiver position
american

Examples

The coach decided to move their fastest player to split end for the upcoming game.
As a split end, she was known for her precise route running and reliable hands.
In the new formation, the split end lined up wide to stretch the defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wide receiver - an offensive player who catches passes in football
  • Antonym: tight end - a football end who lines up close to the offensive line
