scoop shovel

1. NOUN

/ skuːp ˈʃʌvəl / (AmE), / skuːp ˈʃʌv(ə)l / (BrE)
a large, deep shovel or bucket, especially as used on a dredge or backhoe, designed for lifting and moving loose material
large shovel or bucket on machinery

Examples

The operator maneuvered the scoop shovel to remove debris from the construction site.
With its powerful scoop shovel, the backhoe made quick work of the heavy dirt.
During the project, the dredge’s scoop shovel was essential for clearing silt from the riverbed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovel - a tool with a broad blade and typically upturned sides
