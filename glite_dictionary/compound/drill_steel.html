drill steel

1. NOUN

/ drɪl stiːl / (AmE), / drɪl stiːl / (BrE)
A strong carbon steel used to make rods or bits for rock drills and dowels.
carbon steel for drill rods or bits

Examples

The construction crew replaced the worn drill steel to improve the performance of their rock drill.
For safety reasons, always inspect your drill steel before beginning any blasting operation.
Some mining operations require a specialized drill steel to penetrate particularly hard rock formations.
