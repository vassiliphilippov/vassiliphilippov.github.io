shank-nag

1. NOUN

/ ʃæŋk næɡ / (AmE), / ʃæŋk næɡ / (BrE)
an old or worn-out horse, especially one considered past its prime or unfit for regular work
old or worn-out horse
archaic

Examples

After years of faithful service, his shank-nag could no longer keep up with the younger horses.
The children laughed as the shank-nag trotted slowly down the lane.
Even though it was just a shank-nag, he loved his old horse dearly.

Synset Examples

He decided to take his shank-nag to the market today.
After a long ride, my shank-nag deserves a good rest.
The old shank-nag is still faster than my car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jade - an old or worn-out horse
