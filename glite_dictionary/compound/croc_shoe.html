croc shoe

1. NOUN

/ krɑk ʃu / (AmE), / krɒk ʃuː / (BrE)
A type of lightweight, comfortable, and casual shoe made of molded resin, typically with ventilation holes, produced by the brand Crocs.
lightweight shoe made by Crocs

Examples

Many nurses prefer croc shoes at work for their support and breathability.
She bought bright yellow croc shoes for gardening.
At the water park, every child seemed to be wearing croc shoes.

Synset Examples

He wore croc shoes for their comfort at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandal - open footwear worn for comfort or fashion
  • Synonym: clog - a shoe with a thick, often wooden sole

Etymology

From the brand name Crocs, first introduced in 2002.
