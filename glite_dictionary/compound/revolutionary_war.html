Revolutionary War

1. PROPN

/ ɹɪˌvoʊluˈʃəˌnɛɹi wɔɹ / (AmE), / ɹɪˌvɒljuˈʃənəri wɔː / (BrE)
the war of 1775–1783 in which the thirteen American colonies fought for independence from Britain
American War of Independence (1775–1783)

Examples

Many families can trace their heritage back to a soldier who served in the Revolutionary War.
The Revolutionary War marked a turning point in world history and the rise of the United States as a nation.
Students are required to write an essay on the causes of the Revolutionary War for their history class.

Synset Examples

His ancestor fought bravely in the Revolutionary War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Revolution - the period and conflict when the American colonies gained independence from Britain
  • Antonym: Civil War - a different major war in American history, between North and South, 1861–1865
