West Nile encephalitis

1. NOUN

/ wɛst naɪl ɪnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / wɛst naɪl ɪnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A form of encephalitis (inflammation of the brain) caused by the West Nile virus, which can be fatal, particularly in humans, horses, and birds.
brain inflammation due to West Nile virus
medical

Examples

Several people in the area were hospitalized with West Nile encephalitis after a summer outbreak of the virus.
Veterinarians warn that horses are at increasing risk of West Nile encephalitis during mosquito season.
Recent research has improved treatment options for patients diagnosed with West Nile encephalitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encephalitis - inflammation of the brain tissue

Etymology

From 'West Nile' (virus first identified in the West Nile district of Uganda) and 'encephalitis' (from Greek enkephalos, 'brain', and -itis, 'inflammation').
