right ascension

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪt əˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌraɪt əˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The angular distance of a celestial object measured eastward along the celestial equator from the vernal equinox; used, with declination, to specify positions on the celestial sphere, and usually expressed in hours, minutes, and seconds.
astronomical coordinate defining a sky object's position

Examples

Sirius has a right ascension of about 6 hours and 45 minutes.
To locate a star, you need its right ascension and declination.
Astronomers measured the right ascension to determine the position of the comet.

Synset Examples

one hour of right ascension equals fifteen degrees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declination - the angular distance of a celestial object north or south of the celestial equator

Etymology

From Latin 'rectus' (right) and 'ascensio' (a rising), referring to rising points of stars.
