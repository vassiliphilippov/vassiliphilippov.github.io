oxaloacetic acid

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˌsæloʊəˈsɛtɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɒkˌsæləʊəˈsiːtɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a four-carbon dicarboxylic acid, important as an intermediate in the metabolic processes of cells, such as the citric acid cycle
intermediate compound in cell metabolism
medical

Examples

Oxaloacetic acid plays a crucial role in the Krebs cycle, enabling cellular energy production in most organisms.
Doctors may refer to levels of oxaloacetic acid when studying certain metabolic disorders.
During exercise, oxaloacetic acid concentrations can affect how fats and carbohydrates are metabolized.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: malic acid - a related acid in cellular metabolism
  • Degree Lower: fumaric acid - another intermediate in the citric acid cycle

Etymology

From oxalo- (relating to oxalic acid) + acetic acid, reflecting its chemical structure.
