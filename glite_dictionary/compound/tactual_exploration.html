tactual exploration

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tʃu.əl ˌɛk.spləˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tʃu.əl ˌɛk.spləˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a method of examination in which the examiner feels the size, shape, firmness, or location of something, especially body parts, by touch
medical examination by touch
medical

Examples

The doctor used tactual exploration to detect any unusual lumps in the patient's abdomen.
Early diagnosis of tumors often relies on careful tactual exploration by a skilled physician.
Through tactual exploration, the nurse assessed the swelling in the patient's wrist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palpation - examination of the body by touch

Etymology

From Latin 'tactus' (touch) and 'exploratio' (investigation).
