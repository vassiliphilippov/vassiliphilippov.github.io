herpes simplex 2

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɝːpiːz ˈsɪmplɛks tuː / (AmE), / ˌhɜːpiːz ˈsɪmpleks tuː / (BrE)
A virus that can cause genital herpes, typically transmitted through sexual contact.
virus causing genital herpes
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that herpes simplex 2 is a common sexually transmitted infection.
Most genital herpes cases are caused by herpes simplex 2 rather than type 1.
Antiviral medications can help manage outbreaks of herpes simplex 2.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpes simplex 1 - virus causing cold sores, related to herpes simplex 2

Etymology

From Greek 'herpēs' (creeping) and Latin 'simplex' (simple), with '2' denoting the specific virus type.
