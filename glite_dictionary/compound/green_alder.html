green alder

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈɔldɚ / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
A North American shrub with light green leaves and small, winged nuts, commonly found in moist, cool regions.
North American shrub with winged nuts

Examples

The green alder is often found along riverbanks in northern forests.
Botanists studied how the green alder adapted to the colder climate.
We identified the tree by its distinctive winged nuts characteristic of the green alder.
