sheepdog

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːp.dɒɡ / (BrE)
A dog of a breed developed for herding and guarding sheep, often characterized by a long-haired coat.
dog bred for herding sheep

Examples

The farmer relies on his sheepdog to round up the flock every evening.
Among the most famous tasks for a sheepdog is guiding sheep through gates or into pens.
During the show, the sheepdog demonstrated impressive agility and discipline guiding the herd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herding dog - dog bred for herding livestock

Etymology

From 'sheep' + 'dog', referring to a dog used for managing sheep.
