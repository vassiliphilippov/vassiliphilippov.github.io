foley sound

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊli saʊnd / (AmE), / ˈfəʊli saʊnd / (BrE)
A sound effect specially created and recorded for film, television, or other media productions to enhance the audio experience and match on-screen actions.
custom sound effect for film/media

Examples

The film's realistic foley sound made the chase scene much more intense.
She specializes in creating foley sound for animated movies.
Adding the right foley sound can transform an ordinary film into an immersive experience.

Synset Examples

Creating foley sounds can be quite challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound effect - an artificially created or enhanced sound used in media

Etymology

Named after Jack Foley (1891–1967), a pioneering American sound effects artist.
