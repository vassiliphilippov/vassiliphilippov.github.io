capital expenditure

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.ɪ.tl ɪkˌspen.dɪ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkap.ɪ.təl ɪkˌspen.dɪ.tʃə / (BrE)
money spent by a business or organization to acquire, upgrade, or maintain long-term assets such as property, equipment, or infrastructure
spending on long-term assets or improvements

Examples

The company increased its capital expenditure to upgrade its manufacturing facilities.
Investing in new IT systems is considered a capital expenditure, not an operating cost.
The budget allocates a significant amount for capital expenditure on infrastructure projects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: operating expense - money spent for day-to-day running of a business
