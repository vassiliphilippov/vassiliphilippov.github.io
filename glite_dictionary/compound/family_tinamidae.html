family Tinamidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɪˈnæməˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɪˈnæmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of ground-dwelling birds known as tinamous, native to Central and South America.
a bird family: tinamous

Examples

The family Tinamidae includes several species of elusive, ground-dwelling birds found in South America.
Ornithologists study the family Tinamidae to understand the evolution of ratite birds.
Members of the family Tinamidae are known for their secretive behavior and distinctive calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinamou - Any bird of the family Tinamidae; a ground-dwelling bird native to the Americas.

Etymology

From modern Latin Tinamidae, based on the type genus Tinamus.
