passenger pigeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsəndʒɚ ˈpɪdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪndʒə ˈpɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
a species of migratory pigeon (Ectopistes migratorius) once abundant in North America, now extinct
extinct North American pigeon species

Examples

The passenger pigeon once numbered in the billions but disappeared in the early 20th century.
Naturalists often study the extinction of the passenger pigeon to understand human impact on wildlife.
Martha, the last known passenger pigeon, died in captivity in 1914.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodo - an extinct flightless bird from Mauritius

Etymology

Named for its migratory habits; 'passenger' from French 'passager', meaning 'passing by'.
