North Platte River

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ ˈplæt ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / nɔːθ ˈplæt ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the central United States that rises in northern Colorado, flows through Wyoming and Nebraska, and joins the South Platte River to form the Platte River
river in central United States

Examples

The North Platte River passes through three different states before joining the South Platte River.
Fishing along the North Platte River is popular with both locals and tourists.
Heavy snowfall in the Rockies often leads to high water levels in the North Platte River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: South Platte River - a river in the central United States that joins the North Platte River to form the Platte River
  • Degree Higher: Platte River - the river formed by the North and South Platte rivers
