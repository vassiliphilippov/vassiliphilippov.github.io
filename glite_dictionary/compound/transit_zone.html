transit zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænzɪt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtrænzɪt zəʊn / (BrE)
a designated geographic area commonly used as a route for transporting goods or people, notably for drug smuggling between regions such as the Caribbean, Gulf of Mexico, and eastern Pacific Ocean
area used as a route for transport, esp. smuggling

Examples

Law enforcement agencies increased patrols in the transit zone to curb drug trafficking activities.
The Caribbean is a major part of the transit zone for narcotics smuggled to North America.
International cooperation is essential to monitor the vast transit zone used by smugglers.
