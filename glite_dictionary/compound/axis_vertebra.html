axis vertebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksɪs vərˈtɛbrə / (AmE), / ˈæksɪs vəˈtiːbrə / (BrE)
the second cervical vertebra (C2) in the spine, serving as a pivot that allows the head to turn from side to side
the second cervical vertebra (C2) in the spine
medical

Examples

The axis vertebra allows the head to rotate from side to side.
A fracture of the axis vertebra can lead to serious spinal injuries.
The dentist explained that the axis vertebra plays a crucial role in neck mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atlas vertebra - the first cervical vertebra (C1) that supports the skull

Etymology

From Latin 'axis' (axle, pivot) and 'vertebra' (joint, spinal bone).
