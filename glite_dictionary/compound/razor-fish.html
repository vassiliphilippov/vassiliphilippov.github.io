razor-fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small fish found in the West Indies and Mediterranean, known for its compressed, sharp-edged head; any of several wrasses of this type
a type of small sharp-headed fish

Examples

Divers in the Caribbean often spot the elusive razor-fish hiding among the coral.
The local fisherman caught several razor-fish off the coast of Sicily yesterday.
The sharp-edged head of the razor-fish helps it slip between rocky crevices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razorfish - a similar spelling and alternative form
