Palaic language

1. NOUN

/ pəˈleɪɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / pəˈleɪɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language once spoken in northwestern Anatolia, present-day Turkey
extinct Anatolian language of northwest Turkey

Examples

The Palaic language was spoken by a small community in ancient Anatolia.
Scholars study the Palaic language to learn more about the early Indo-European languages.
Many texts in the Palaic language have been found on clay tablets in Turkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hittite - extinct Indo-European Anatolian language of ancient Hittites

Etymology

From Pala, the name of an ancient Anatolian people, and English 'language'.
