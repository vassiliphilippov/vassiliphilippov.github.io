law of large numbers

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv lɑrdʒ ˈnʌmbɚz / (AmE), / lɔː əv lɑːdʒ ˈnʌmbəz / (BrE)
a principle in statistics stating that as the size of a sample increases, its mean will get closer to the average of the whole population
statistical principle about sample averages

Examples

The law of large numbers explains why casinos can reliably profit over time.
In practice, the law of large numbers assures that the average outcome becomes predictable as the sample size grows.
By conducting many trials, the scientist relied on the law of large numbers to support her hypothesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central limit theorem - statistical theorem related to sample distributions approaching normality
  • Antonym: small sample bias - statistical phenomenon where estimates from small samples deviate from population values

Etymology

Statistical term describing the behavior of sample means, coined in the late 18th century from mathematical usage of 'law' and 'large numbers'.
