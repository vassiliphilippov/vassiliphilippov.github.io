Manx language

1. NOUN

/ mæŋks ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / mæŋks ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Gaelic language historically spoken on the Isle of Man, now mostly used on ceremonial occasions
Gaelic language of the Isle of Man

Examples

She is studying the Manx language to help preserve its cultural heritage.
The last native speaker of the Manx language passed away in the 20th century.
Some schools on the Isle of Man now offer lessons in the Manx language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaelic - Celtic languages of Scotland, Ireland, and the Isle of Man

Etymology

From Manx, relating to the Isle of Man, and English 'language'.
