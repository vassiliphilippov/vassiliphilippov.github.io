slack tide

1. NOUN

/ slæk taɪd / (AmE), / slæk taɪd / (BrE)
The period when the water is relatively still at the turning point between ebb and flow of the tide.
period of still water at tidal turn

Examples

Fishermen often wait for slack tide to cross the sandbar safely.
Marine biologists collected samples during slack tide, when the current was minimal.
The boat drifted gently at slack tide, before the water began to move again.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high tide - the state of the tide when at its highest level
  • Antonym: low tide - the state of the tide when at its lowest level

Etymology

From 'slack' (slow, inactive) + 'tide' (Old English 'tīd', time, season).
