orbital point

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrbɪt̬l̩ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪtl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the craniometric point located at the lowest part of the lower edge of the eye socket in the skull
anatomical point at base of eye socket
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the orbital point as a reference during the cranial reconstruction procedure.
Measurement of the orbital point is important in anthropological studies of human skulls.
Radiologists use the orbital point to help assess the alignment of facial bones in medical imaging.

Etymology

From 'orbit' (eye socket) and 'point' (specific spot); technical anatomical term.
