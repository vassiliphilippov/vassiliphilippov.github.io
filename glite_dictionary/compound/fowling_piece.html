fowling piece

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊlɪŋ piːs / (AmE), / ˈfaʊlɪŋ piːs / (BrE)
a light shotgun, especially one used for shooting wildfowl
light shotgun for shooting birds

Examples

The hunter carried his trusty fowling piece through the misty marsh at dawn.
In the old painting, a gentleman is depicted cleaning his fowling piece after a day's hunt.
She inherited a beautifully engraved fowling piece from her grandfather's collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shotgun - a firearm for firing a number of small pellets

Etymology

From 'fowling', the act of hunting wildfowl, and 'piece', an old term for a firearm.
