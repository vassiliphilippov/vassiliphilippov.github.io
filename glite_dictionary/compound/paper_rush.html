paper rush

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ rʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə rʌʃ / (BrE)
a tall sedge native to the Nile valley, valued historically for its fiber, which was used in making paper and various goods
tall Nile sedge yielding fiber for papermaking

Examples

Ancient Egyptians cultivated the paper rush along the Nile for various uses.
Fibers from the paper rush were once essential for making papyrus scrolls.
The paper rush is still found growing in marshy areas near the Nile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papyrus - plant used to make ancient Egyptian paper
