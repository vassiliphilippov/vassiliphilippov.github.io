Mother Goose

1. PROPN

/ ˈmʌðər ɡus / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə ɡuːs / (BrE)
an imaginary old woman traditionally credited as the author of English nursery rhymes and fairy tales
imaginary author of nursery rhymes

Examples

Many English children's stories are attributed to Mother Goose.
My grandmother used to read Mother Goose rhymes to me before bed.
The book featured classic tales from Mother Goose and colorful illustrations.

Etymology

First used in France as 'Mère l'Oye', later adopted in English to refer to the collector or narrator of nursery rhymes.
