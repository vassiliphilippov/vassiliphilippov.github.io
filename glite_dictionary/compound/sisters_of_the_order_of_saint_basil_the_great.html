Sisters of the Order of Saint Basil the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪstərz əv ði ˈɔrdər əv ˈseɪnt ˈbeɪzəl ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəz əv ði ˈɔːdə əv ˈseɪnt ˈbæzɪl ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic religious order of women, particularly affiliated with the Ukrainian Greek Catholic Church, dedicated to religious service and education
female Catholic religious order (Ukrainian Greek Catholic)

Examples

The Sisters of the Order of Saint Basil the Great established several schools throughout Ukraine and North America.
Many young women have joined the Sisters of the Order of Saint Basil the Great to dedicate their lives to service and spirituality.
The Sisters of the Order of Saint Basil the Great play a vital role in preserving Ukrainian cultural traditions within the church.

Etymology

Named after Saint Basil the Great, a 4th-century Christian bishop; the order originated in Eastern Europe.
