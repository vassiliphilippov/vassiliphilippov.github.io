strange particle

1. NOUN

/ streɪndʒ ˈpɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / streɪndʒ ˈpɑːtɪkl̩ / (BrE)
An elementary particle that possesses the property of non-zero strangeness, produced in high-energy physics reactions.
elementary particle with non-zero strangeness
medical

Examples

Physicists observed a strange particle during the collision experiment at the accelerator.
A strange particle decays more slowly than an ordinary particle because of its strangeness.
The discovery of the first strange particle helped physicists understand the strong interaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particle - a minute portion of matter

Etymology

'Strange' refers to 'strangeness,' a quantum property named by physicists due to the unexpected behavior of these particles.
