slime eel

1. NOUN

/ slaɪm iːl / (AmE), / slaɪm iːl / (BrE)
A jawless, eellike marine animal (hagfish) with a round, tentacled mouth and the ability to secrete slime, feeding on dead or trapped fish by boring into their bodies.
jawless, slimy marine fish (hagfish)

Examples

The slime eel defends itself by secreting large amounts of mucus when threatened.
Researchers observed a group of slime eels feeding on a decaying whale carcass on the ocean floor.
Some fishermen consider slime eels a nuisance because they invade fish traps and eat the catch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hagfish - a jawless marine fish known for producing slime
