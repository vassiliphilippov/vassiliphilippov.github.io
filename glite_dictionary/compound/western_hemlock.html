western hemlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈhɛmlɑk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree (Tsuga heterophylla) native to western North America, valued for its softwood timber
evergreen tree of western North America

Examples

The western hemlock thrives in the moist forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Builders often use lumber from the western hemlock because of its strength and workability.
Conservationists are working to protect old-growth stands of western hemlock from logging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - any of several trees of the genus Tsuga with soft needle-like leaves
