matrix operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.trɪks ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks ˌɒp.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical process involving matrices, such as addition, multiplication, or inversion
mathematical operation with matrices

Examples

The most common matrix operation is multiplication, which combines two matrices to form a new one.
Every matrix operation must follow specific rules based on the dimensions of the matrices.
Learning each matrix operation is essential for understanding linear algebra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matrix multiplication - the operation of multiplying two matrices
  • Synonym: matrix addition - the operation of adding two matrices

Etymology

Matrix from Latin 'matrix' (womb, source), operation from Latin 'operatio' (working).
