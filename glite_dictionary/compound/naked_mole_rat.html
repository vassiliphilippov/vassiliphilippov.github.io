naked mole rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪkɪd ˈmoʊl ˌræt / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪd ˈməʊl ˌræt / (BrE)
a small, nearly hairless burrowing rodent of East Africa (Heterocephalus glaber), known for its eusocial behavior and wrinkled, fetal-like appearance
hairless East African burrowing rodent

Examples

The naked mole rat is famous for its resistance to cancer and unusually long lifespan.
Scientists study the naked mole rat to understand how its colonies function like insect societies.
Children were fascinated by the wrinkled skin of the naked mole rat at the zoo exhibit.
