drawing lots

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪŋ lɑts / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːɪŋ lɒts / (BrE)
the act of deciding something by chance, typically by selecting straws, pebbles, or similar objects
making a decision by chance

Examples

To decide who would go first, they suggested drawing lots.
After much debate, the committee resolved the tie by drawing lots.
In some cultures, important decisions were often made by drawing lots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casting lots - deciding by chance, especially in biblical or historical contexts
