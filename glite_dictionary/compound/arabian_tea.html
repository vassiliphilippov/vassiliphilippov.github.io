Arabian tea

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪ.bi.ən tiː / (AmE), / əˈreɪ.bi.ən tiː / (BrE)
the leaves of the Catha edulis shrub, chewed or brewed as tea for their euphoric, stimulant effects, especially in East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula
leaves of Catha edulis with stimulant effect

Examples

In some regions, Arabian tea is consumed socially for its stimulating effects.
Yemeni traders often chew Arabian tea during long market days.
The leaves known as Arabian tea are a traditional stimulant in the Horn of Africa.
