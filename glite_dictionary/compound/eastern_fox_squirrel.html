eastern fox squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn fɑks ˈskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˈiːstən fɒks ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a large tree-dwelling squirrel native to the eastern United States, known for its bushy tail and reddish-gray fur
large arboreal squirrel of eastern US

Examples

The eastern fox squirrel is often seen gathering acorns in suburban parks.
Unlike the gray squirrel, the eastern fox squirrel has a distinct reddish tint to its fur.
Researchers tracked the movements of several eastern fox squirrels in the deciduous forests of Ohio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrel - a small rodent with a bushy tail, often found in trees
