bane of

1. NOUN

/ beɪn əv / (AmE), / beɪn ɒv / (BrE)
A cause of great distress or annoyance to someone or something.
cause of great distress or annoyance

Examples

Mosquitoes are the bane of summer evenings in this region.
Paperwork can be the bane of running a small business.
For some students, calculus is the bane of their college experience.

Synset Examples

The rain was the bane of our picnic plans.
Traffic jams are the bane of my daily commute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scourge - a person or thing that causes great trouble or suffering
  • Antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or benefit

Etymology

From Middle English 'bane' (poison), from Old English 'bana' (destroyer).
