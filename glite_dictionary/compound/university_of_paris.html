University of Paris

1. PROPN

/ juːˌnɪvərˈsɪti əv ˈpærɪs / (AmE), / juːˌnɪvəˈsɪti əv ˈparɪs / (BrE)
a former university in Paris, established in the 12th century, noted as a leading intellectual center in France and Europe until its division in 1970.
historical university in Paris, France

Examples

The University of Paris was once renowned across Europe for its theological studies.
She pursued her doctorate at the University of Paris in the 1960s before it was reformed.
Medieval scholars often traveled great distances to attend the University of Paris.
