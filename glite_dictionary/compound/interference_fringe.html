interference fringe

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns frɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns frɪndʒ / (BrE)
one of the alternating light or dark bands formed as a result of the interference and diffraction of light waves.
band produced by light interference

Examples

The scientist adjusted the apparatus to observe the interference fringes clearly.
Thin films result in colorful interference fringes due to the varying thickness of the material.
The pattern of interference fringes revealed information about the wavelength of the light used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringe - the border or edge stitched or woven so as to stand out from the main fabric
