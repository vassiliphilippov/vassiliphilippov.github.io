to get up early

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ʌp ˈɜrli / (AmE), / ɡɛt ʌp ˈɜːli / (BrE)
to rise from bed before the usual or expected time in the morning
rise from bed before usual time

Examples

She tries to get up early every day to have a calm breakfast.
On weekends, I don’t usually get up early unless I have plans.
He always has more energy when he gets up early in the morning.

Synset Examples

He decided to get up early for the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sleep in - to stay in bed later than usual in the morning
