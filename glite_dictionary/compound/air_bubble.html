air bubble

1. NOUN

/ ɛr ˈbʌbəl / (AmE), / eə ˈbʌbəl / (BrE)
a small pocket of air trapped within a liquid or solid
small pocket of air in liquid/solid

Examples

Small air bubbles can cause imperfections in glass products.
Make sure there is no air bubble in the syringe before injecting the medicine.
If you trap an air bubble under the sticker, press it out toward the edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - a thin sphere of liquid enclosing air or gas
