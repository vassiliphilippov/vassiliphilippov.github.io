to fall into a routine

1. VERB

/ fɔl ˈɪntu ə ruˈtin / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɪntuː ə ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
to begin to follow a regular and often unvaried pattern of behavior, especially one that develops unconsciously over time
begin a regular pattern of behavior

Examples

After moving to the new city, she fell into a routine of jogging every morning.
It was easy to fall into a routine during the long winter months.
They didn't realize they had fallen into a routine until their days all started to look the same.

Synset Examples

He fell into a routine that was hard to break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle into a routine - adopt a regular pattern of behavior
  • Antonym: break the routine - end or disrupt a regular pattern of behavior
