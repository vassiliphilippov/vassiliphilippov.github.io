antihaemophilic factor

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌhiːməˈfɪlɪk ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˌantiˌhiːməˈfɪlɪk ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a protein in the blood that helps with clotting; its deficiency causes hemophilia A
blood-clotting protein; deficiency causes hemophilia A
medical

Examples

Replacement of the antihaemophilic factor is crucial in treating patients with hemophilia A.
The laboratory tested his blood for antihaemophilic factor levels after he experienced unexplained bleeding.
Modern therapies include the injection of antihaemophilic factor concentrates to prevent bleeding episodes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor VIII - another name for the antihaemophilic factor
  • Degree Lower: hemophilia A - a hereditary bleeding disorder caused by deficiency of antihaemophilic factor

Etymology

From anti- (against) + haemophilic (referring to hemophilia) + factor (substance with specific function).
