cinder track

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndɚ ˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈsɪndə ˌtræk / (BrE)
a running track with a surface made of fine cinders, once commonly used for athletics before artificial surfaces became standard
running track surfaced with cinders

Examples

The old stadium still has a cinder track instead of a modern synthetic one.
Sprinters found the cinder track challenging after it rained heavily the night before the meet.
Many world records were set on the cinder track before synthetic surfaces became common.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: all-weather track - a running track made of synthetic materials, suitable in all weather conditions
