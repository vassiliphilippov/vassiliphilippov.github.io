red Bordeaux

1. NOUN

/ rɛd bɔrˈdoʊ / (AmE), / rɛd bɔːˈdɔː / (BrE)
a dry red wine produced in the Bordeaux region of France, or a wine made in a similar style elsewhere
dry red wine from Bordeaux

Examples

The restaurant has an impressive selection of red Bordeaux to pair with your meal.
He prefers a glass of red Bordeaux with his steak.
We toured several vineyards famous for their red Bordeaux blends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claret - another term for red Bordeaux wine, especially in British English

Etymology

From Bordeaux, the name of a wine-producing region in southwestern France.
