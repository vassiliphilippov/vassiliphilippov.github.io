retinal detachment

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtɪnəl dɪˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪnəl dɪˈtætʃm(ə)nt / (BrE)
A serious eye condition in which the retina separates from the layer of blood vessels at the back of the eye, leading to impaired vision and requiring urgent medical treatment.
separation of retina from the eye's back surface
medical

Examples

If left untreated, retinal detachment can lead to permanent vision loss.
The specialist diagnosed him with retinal detachment after he reported seeing flashes of light and floaters.
Laser surgery is commonly used to repair a retinal detachment and restore vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retina - the light-sensitive membrane lining the inner eyeball
  • Synonym: photocoagulation - a medical procedure to treat retinal detachment

Etymology

From 'retina', the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye (from Medieval Latin), and 'detachment', meaning separation.
