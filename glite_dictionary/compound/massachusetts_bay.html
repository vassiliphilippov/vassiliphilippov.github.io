Massachusetts Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæsətʃuˈsɛts beɪ / (AmE), / ˌmæsətʃuːˈsɛts beɪ / (BrE)
an inlet of the Atlantic Ocean off eastern Massachusetts, extending from Cape Ann in the north to Cape Cod in the south.
bay off eastern Massachusetts

Examples

The early settlers established their colony along the shores of Massachusetts Bay.
Oil tankers are strictly regulated when entering Massachusetts Bay to prevent pollution.
A number of seabirds can be seen migrating through Massachusetts Bay every spring.

Etymology

Named after the Massachusetts tribe; 'bay' from Old French 'baee' (opening).
