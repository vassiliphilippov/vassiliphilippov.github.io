SA node

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs ˈeɪ ˌnoʊd / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˈeɪ ˌnəʊd / (BrE)
A specialized group of cells in the right atrium of the heart that initiates and regulates the heartbeat.
heart tissue that controls heartbeat
medical

Examples

The SA node is often referred to as the heart's natural pacemaker.
An irregularity in the SA node can lead to arrhythmias.
Doctors examined the function of his SA node after he experienced heart palpitations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinoatrial node - full form of 'SA node', refers to the same structure
  • Degree Lower: AV node - atrioventricular node, another node controlling heart rhythm

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'sinoatrial node', from Latin 'sinus' (curve, cavity) + 'atrium' (hall).
