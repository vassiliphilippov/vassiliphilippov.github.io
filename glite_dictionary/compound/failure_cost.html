failure cost

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪljər kɔst / (AmE), / ˈfeɪljə kɒst / (BrE)
the costs incurred when products or services fail to meet specified requirements, including internal and external failures
cost from failing to meet requirements

Examples

The company invested heavily in quality control to reduce failure costs in their manufacturing process.
High failure costs can damage a brand's reputation if defective products reach customers.
Regular staff training aims to lower failure costs by minimizing errors in service delivery.

Synset Examples

Inspection is never completely effective, so appraisal costs stay high as long as the failure costs stay high.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraisal cost - the costs of measuring, evaluating, or auditing products or services to ensure quality standards
  • Antonym: prevention cost - the costs incurred to prevent defects or failures in products or services
