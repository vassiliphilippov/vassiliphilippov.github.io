financial aid

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl eɪd / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl eɪd / (BrE)
money or other resources given to help support a person, group, or cause, often to assist with educational or living expenses.
monetary or material support

Examples

Many students rely on financial aid to afford college tuition each year.
The government announced increased financial aid for families affected by the disaster.
Without financial aid, it would have been impossible for her to complete her studies.

Synset Examples

economic assistance to depressed areas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - money given for a particular purpose, often for education or research
  • Synonym: scholarship - money awarded to a student to help pay for education, often based on merit
  • Synonym: loan - money lent that must be paid back, sometimes part of financial aid
