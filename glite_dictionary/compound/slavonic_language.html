Slavonic language

1. NOUN

/ sləˈvɑːnɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / sləˈvɒnɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European family of languages that includes Russian, Polish, Czech, and other languages spoken in Eastern Europe and parts of Asia
Indo-European language branch including Russian, Polish, etc.

Examples

Russian is a Slavonic language spoken by millions across eastern Europe and Asia.
The similarities between Polish and Czech show their shared Slavonic language roots.
Old Church Slavonic was the earliest written Slavonic language used by the Slavs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic language - another term for Slavonic language

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Slavonicus, from Slavic self-designation 'Slavŭ'.
