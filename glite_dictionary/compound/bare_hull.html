bare hull

1. NOUN

/ bɛr hʌl / (AmE), / beə hʌl / (BrE)
the basic structure of a ship, boat, or vessel without any internal or external fittings, equipment, or installations
basic structure of a ship without fittings

Examples

The shipbuilder delivered only the bare hull, and the client would finish outfitting it.
During restoration, the crew reduced the yacht to its bare hull before making repairs.
Insurance typically does not cover a bare hull until vital equipment is installed.

Synset Examples

The razee was stripped down to its bare hull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hull - the main body of a ship or boat
