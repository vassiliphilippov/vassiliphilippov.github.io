Wassermann test

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːsərˌmæn tɛst / (AmE), / ˈwɒsəmən tɛst / (BrE)
a blood test formerly used to detect syphilis by identifying antibodies to the causative organism
blood test for syphilis (historical)
medical

Examples

The Wassermann test played a crucial role in diagnosing syphilis in the early twentieth century.
Doctors no longer rely on the Wassermann test due to the availability of more accurate methods.
A positive Wassermann test required further clinical evaluation and confirmation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syphilis test - general test for the detection of syphilis infection
  • Synonym: Treponema pallidum - the bacterium that causes syphilis

Etymology

Named after August von Wassermann, German bacteriologist who developed the test in 1906.
