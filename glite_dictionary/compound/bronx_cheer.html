Bronx cheer

1. NOUN

/ brɑŋks tʃɪr / (AmE), / brɒŋks tʃɪə / (BrE)
an insulting noise made by blowing through closed lips, used to show scorn or dislike; a raspberry
insulting noise made to show contempt

Examples

When the unpopular decision was announced, the crowd responded with a loud Bronx cheer.
She gave him a Bronx cheer when he struck out in the ninth inning.
The comedian received nothing but a Bronx cheer after his joke fell flat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a similar sound made to express contempt or derision

Etymology

Named after The Bronx, a borough of New York City, where the gesture gained notoriety.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑŋks tʃɪr / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋks tʃɪə / (BrE)
a loud, mocking sound made by blowing through closed lips, used to show disapproval or scorn, typically by an audience
mocking or derisive sound of disapproval

Examples

The crowd let out a loud Bronx cheer when the referee made a bad call.
At the end of his off-key song, the audience responded with a long Bronx cheer.
The disappointed fans gave the players a Bronx cheer as they left the field.

Synset Examples

He received a Bronx cheer for his poor performance.
The crowd gave him a Bronx cheer after his mistake.
The comedian got a Bronx cheer from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a similar mocking noise made by blowing through closed lips

Etymology

Named after the Bronx, a borough of New York City, where the practice was said to be popular.
