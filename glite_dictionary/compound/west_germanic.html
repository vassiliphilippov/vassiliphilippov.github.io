West Germanic

1. NOUN

/ wɛst dʒərˈmænɪk / (AmE), / wɛst dʒɜːˈmænɪk / (BrE)
A branch of the Germanic language family that includes German, English, Dutch, and others.
branch of the Germanic language family

Examples

English, German, and Dutch are all part of the West Germanic language group.
Linguists often compare the grammar of various West Germanic languages.
The West Germanic family split from North and East Germanic branches centuries ago.
