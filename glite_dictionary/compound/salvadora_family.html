Salvadora family

1. NOUN

/ sæl.vəˈdɔr.ə ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / sæl.vəˈdɔː.rə ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family of Old World shrubs and trees in the order Gentianales, related to the olive family, characterized by having four stamens and four petals
family of Old World shrubs and trees

Examples

The Salvadora family includes the toothbrush tree, known for its medicinal properties.
Botanists study the Salvadora family to understand plant adaptation in arid environments.
Several shrubs in the Salvadora family are used in traditional remedies across Africa and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oleaceae - olive family of plants

Etymology

Named after the genus Salvadora, itself named in honor of Spanish apothecary Juan Salvador.
