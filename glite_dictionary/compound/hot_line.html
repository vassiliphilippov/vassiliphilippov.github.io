hot line

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒt ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a telephone line providing direct and immediate information or advice about a particular subject, often for emergencies or support
direct telephone advice or support line

Examples

If you need immediate support, call the medical hot line at any time, day or night.
The school set up a hot line for parents concerned about the new policy changes.
After the storm, a dedicated emergency hot line was established to assist residents in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helpline - telephone service offering help or information

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒt ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a direct and exclusive telephone line set up for emergency or urgent communication between officials, especially between heads of government.
direct emergency telephone link between officials

Examples

The two presidents spoke over the hot line to prevent the crisis from escalating.
A hot line has been established between the police headquarters and emergency services.
During the summit, diplomats relied on a hot line for quick and private communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct line - a telephone line with direct connection, without intermediaries

Etymology

Formed from 'hot' (urgent) + 'line' (telephone line); became widely known during the Cold War.
