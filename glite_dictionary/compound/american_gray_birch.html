American gray birch

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ɡreɪ bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ɡreɪ bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A medium-sized birch tree of eastern North America with white or pale grey bark and low-value wood, often found in second-growth forests.
birch tree of eastern North America

Examples

The American gray birch is commonly seen in abandoned fields across the northeastern United States.
Due to its quick growth, the American gray birch often colonizes areas after a forest fire.
Unlike other birches, the American gray birch has wood that is not highly valued by the timber industry.
