Polaroid Land camera

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.ləˌrɔɪd lænd ˈkæm.ɚ.ə / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ləˌrɔɪd lænd ˈkæm.ər.ə / (BrE)
A camera that develops and produces a positive print within seconds after taking a picture.
instant-print camera

Examples

She still owns a vintage Polaroid Land camera that produces instant photos.
The Polaroid Land camera revolutionized photography by allowing images to develop right before your eyes.
He demonstrated the Polaroid Land camera for his students, impressing them with the immediate prints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instant camera - camera that produces finished photographs moments after taking the picture

Etymology

From 'Polaroid', the company name, and 'Land', after Edwin Land, its inventor.
