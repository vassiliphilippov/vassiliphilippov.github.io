triphosphopyridine nucleotide

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌfɑs.foʊ.pɪˈrɪ.din ˈnu.kli.oʊˌtaɪd / (AmE), / trɪˌfɒs.fəʊ.pɪˈrɪ.diːn ˈnjuː.kli.ə.taɪd / (BrE)
a coenzyme involved in several enzymatic reactions, also known as NADP, important in metabolism
a coenzyme, also called NADP
medical

Examples

The enzyme requires triphosphopyridine nucleotide as a cofactor to catalyze the reaction.

Researchers measured the concentration of triphosphopyridine nucleotide in liver cells for their study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NADP - nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate, the common name for triphosphopyridine nucleotide

Etymology

From pyridine (a nitrogenous base) and triphospho- (having three phosphate groups), describing its chemical structure.
