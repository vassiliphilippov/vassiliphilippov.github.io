Tasman dwarf pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæzmən dˈwɔrf paɪn / (AmE), / ˈtazmən dˈwɔːf paɪn / (BrE)
a small shrub native to Tasmania, characterized by short, stiff branches; a common name for certain species in the genus Microcachrys or related conifers.
small shrub from Tasmania with stiff branches

Examples

The Tasman dwarf pine thrives in the cold, mountainous regions of central Tasmania.
Botanists are studying the growth habits of the Tasman dwarf pine in its natural habitat.
Many gardeners admire the unique beauty of the Tasman dwarf pine for rock gardens.

Etymology

Named after Tasmania, the Australian island, and its dwarf pine appearance.
