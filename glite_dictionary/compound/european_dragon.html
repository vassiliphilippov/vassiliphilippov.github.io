European dragon

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / jʊˈrəʊpiən ˈdræɡən / (BrE)
A mythical creature from European folklore, typically depicted as a large, winged, fire-breathing reptile.
mythical fire-breathing creature of Europe

Examples

Legends of the European dragon often describe it as hoarding treasure in deep caves.
The European dragon differs from its Asian counterpart in both appearance and symbolism.
In medieval art, the European dragon is usually shown breathing fire and terrorizing villages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon - mythical reptilian creature in folklore
  • Antonym: Chinese dragon - mythical creature in Chinese folklore, typically without wings
