Doctor of Medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə ɒv ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
A degree awarded to medical school graduates qualifying them to practice medicine.
advanced degree in medical practice

Examples

After years of study, she finally received her Doctor of Medicine degree.
Many universities require candidates to defend a thesis before awarding the Doctor of Medicine.
He proudly displayed his Doctor of Medicine diploma on the office wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: M.D. - abbreviation for Doctor of Medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'doctor medicinae', meaning 'teacher of medicine'.
