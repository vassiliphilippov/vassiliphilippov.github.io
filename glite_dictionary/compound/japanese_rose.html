Japanese rose

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs roʊz / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs rəʊz / (BrE)
a vigorous rose plant, typically with clusters of small flowers, originally from East Asia and often used for hedges or grafting stock
a species of rose from East Asia, often used in horticulture

Examples

The Japanese rose is often planted in gardens for its hardiness and lovely blooms.
She decided to use a Japanese rose hedge to add privacy to her yard.
Many gardeners use the Japanese rose as a rootstock for grafting other types of roses.

Etymology

Named for its origin in Japan and its classification as a rose.
