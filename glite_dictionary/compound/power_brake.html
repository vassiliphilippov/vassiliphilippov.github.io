power brake

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər breɪk / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə breɪk / (BrE)
a brake system in a vehicle that uses power assistance to increase the force applied by the driver, making braking easier and more effective
brake system with power assistance in vehicles

Examples

The mechanic replaced the faulty power brake unit to improve the car's stopping performance.
Modern cars are usually equipped with power brakes for safer and easier driving.
If the power brake system fails, stopping the vehicle may require much more effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brake - a device for slowing or stopping a vehicle
