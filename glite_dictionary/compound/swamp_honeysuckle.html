swamp honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to swamps in the eastern United States, with small white to pinkish flowers that resemble those of honeysuckle.
shrub with honeysuckle-like flowers, found in eastern U.S. swamps

Examples

The swamp honeysuckle blooms in late spring, filling the air with a delicate fragrance.
Gardeners admire the swamp honeysuckle for its ability to thrive in wet, boggy soils.
We spotted a cluster of swamp honeysuckle plants growing along the edge of the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - any of various shrubs or vines of the genus Lonicera, known for their fragrant flowers
