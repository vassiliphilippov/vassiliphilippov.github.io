Norwich terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔːrwɪtʃ ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˈnɒrɪdʒ ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
A small English breed of terrier with short legs, a wiry straight coat that can be red, grey, or black-and-tan, and erect ears.
small English terrier breed

Examples

The Norwich terrier is known for its friendly and fearless temperament.
Sophie adopted a playful Norwich terrier from the local animal shelter last year.
At the dog show, the judge praised the Norwich terrier's distinctive wiry coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norfolk terrier - a similar small English breed of terrier distinguished by its drop ears

Etymology

Named after Norwich, a city in England where the breed originated.
