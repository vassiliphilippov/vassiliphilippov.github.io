springer spaniel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋər ˈspæniəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋə ˈspæniəl / (BrE)
a breed of large spaniel with a wavy, silky coat, typically black or liver and white, known for its energy and ability in hunting and retrieving
breed of large spaniel dog

Examples

The springer spaniel quickly located the bird and waited for its owner's command.
She adopted a young springer spaniel with a gentle temperament.
A springer spaniel requires plenty of exercise and mental stimulation each day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocker spaniel - a smaller breed of spaniel, also used in hunting

Etymology

From 'springer' (from spring, referring to its role in springing game) and 'spaniel' (from Old French 'espaignol', Spanish), referring to Spanish origin of the breed group.
