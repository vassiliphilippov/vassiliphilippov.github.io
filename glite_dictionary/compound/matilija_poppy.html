matilija poppy

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɪliə ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / məˈtɪliə ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
a tall perennial subshrub native to California and Mexico, cultivated for its silvery-blue foliage and large fragrant white flowers
tall Californian/Mexican subshrub with white flowers

Examples

The garden's most stunning feature is the row of matilija poppies along the fence line.
She chose to plant a matilija poppy because of its showy white blossoms and drought tolerance.
Each spring, the matilija poppy fills the yard with a sweet, delicate fragrance and striking beauty.

Etymology

Named after the Matilija region in California, where the plant is native, and 'poppy' for its poppy-like flowers.
