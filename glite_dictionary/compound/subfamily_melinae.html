subfamily Melinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌfæməli məˈlaɪni / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌfæmɪli mɛˈliːniː / (BrE)
A zoological subfamily that includes the badgers, though not always recognized in all classifications.
zoological subfamily including badgers

Examples

The European badger belongs to the subfamily Melinae of the family Mustelidae.
Researchers debated whether to classify certain species within the subfamily Melinae.
Field guides sometimes omit the subfamily Melinae in their classifications of carnivorous mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a burrowing mammal of the family Mustelidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Melinae*, based on *Meles* (genus for badgers) and -inae (zoological subfamily suffix).
