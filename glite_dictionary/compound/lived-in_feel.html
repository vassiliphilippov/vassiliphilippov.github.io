lived-in feel

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvd ɪn fil / (AmE), / ˈlɪvd ɪn fiːl / (BrE)
a cozy and welcoming atmosphere, often suggesting a place is comfortably used and inhabited
cozy, welcoming atmosphere

Examples

The new apartment has a lived-in feel that makes everyone feel instantly at home.
She decorated her studio to give it a lived-in feel rather than a showroom look.
Visitors often compliment the lived-in feel of their cozy living room.

Synset Examples

Their home had a warm, lived-in feel.
He prefers a lived-in look for his furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homey - cozy and comfortable like home
  • Antonym: sterile - lacking warmth or lived-in atmosphere
