Ewe language

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪweɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈiːwɛ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A member of the Kwa branch of Niger-Congo languages, spoken by the Ewe people in Ghana, Togo, and Benin.
language spoken in Ghana, Togo, and Benin

Examples

More than three million people speak the Ewe language across Ghana, Togo, and Benin.
She is fluent in both the Ewe language and English, which helps her work as a translator.
Academic researchers have published several books on the grammar of the Ewe language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Twi - another language spoken in Ghana
  • Synonym: Hausa - a major African language spoken in Nigeria and surrounding countries

Etymology

From the name of the Ewe people, who are native speakers of the language.
