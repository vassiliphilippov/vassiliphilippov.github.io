sand blackberry

1. NOUN

/ sænd ˈblækˌbɛri / (AmE), / sand ˈblækbəri / (BrE)
a stiff, shrubby blackberry plant native to the eastern United States, especially from Connecticut to Florida
shrubby blackberry of eastern U.S.

Examples

The sand blackberry grows abundantly in the sandy soils of the southeastern United States.
Wildlife often feed on the fruit of the sand blackberry during the summer.
She planted a sand blackberry bush along the edge of her garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - a soft black or dark purple fruit; the bush producing it
