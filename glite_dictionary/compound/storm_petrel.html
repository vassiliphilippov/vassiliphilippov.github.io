storm petrel

1. NOUN

/ stɔrm ˈpɛtrəl / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈpɛtrəl / (BrE)
a small seabird with predominantly dark feathers and paler underparts, belonging to several species in the family Hydrobatidae, often seen out at sea and associated with stormy weather
a small seabird with dark plumage

Examples

The storm petrel is rarely seen near land except during the breeding season.
Fishermen believe that the appearance of a storm petrel foretells rough weather.
A flock of storm petrels darted across the surface of the waves at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrel - any seabird of the order Procellariiformes, typically small with long wings

Etymology

From 'storm' (rough weather) and 'petrel', from Italian 'Petrillo' (little Peter), after St Peter walking on the water, referring to the bird's habit of appearing to walk on the waves.
