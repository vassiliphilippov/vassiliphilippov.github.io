slave driver

1. NOUN

/ sleɪv ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / sleɪv ˈdrʌɪvə / (BrE)
a person, especially an employer, who treats workers very harshly or forces them to work excessively hard
very harsh, demanding employer

Examples

The new manager is a real slave driver, expecting us to work late every night.
My friends warned me that their coach was a slave driver during training sessions.
He quit his job because he couldn't stand working for a slave driver any longer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taskmaster - a person who imposes hard or burdensome work
  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler or employer

Etymology

Originally referred to someone who forced slaves to work, later figuratively applied to demanding employers.

2. NOUN

/ sleɪv ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / sleɪv ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
a person who supervises and pushes slaves or workers to work hard, especially one who is harsh or oppressive
oppressive supervisor of slaves or workers

Examples

The slave driver demanded that everyone work from sunrise to sunset.
History books describe the cruelty of the slave driver on the plantation.
He acted like a slave driver, giving his workers no rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overseer - a person who supervises others, especially workers
  • Synonym: taskmaster - a person who imposes hard or continuous work

Etymology

From 'slave' (a person held in servitude) + 'driver' (one who drives or urges others to work); originally referred to overseers on plantations.
