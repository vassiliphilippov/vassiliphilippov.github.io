house martin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊs ˌmɑrtɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs ˌmɑːtɪn / (BrE)
a small migratory European bird of the swallow family, noted for building its nests under the eaves of houses
small European bird of the swallow family

Examples

A pair of house martins built their mud nest under our roof last spring.
The arrival of house martins each year marks the start of summer in our village.
Many people hang special boxes to encourage house martins to nest on their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small, fast-flying songbird of the family Hirundinidae
