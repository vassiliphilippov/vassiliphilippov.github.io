surgical operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝdʒɪkəl ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a doctor makes an incision in the body, usually to repair, remove, or replace tissue or organs
medical procedure involving surgery
medical

Examples

The patient needed a surgical operation to repair a damaged artery after the accident.
Advances in technology have made many surgical operations less invasive and safer than before.
After her surgical operation, she required several weeks of rest and physical therapy.

Synset Examples

they will schedule the operation as soon as an operating room is available
he died while undergoing surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surgery - the treatment of injuries or disorders by cutting open the body
  • Degree Lower: procedure - a series of actions conducted in a certain manner; in medicine, a medical intervention

Etymology

From Latin 'chirurgiae operatio', where 'chirurgia' means surgery and 'operatio' means operation.
