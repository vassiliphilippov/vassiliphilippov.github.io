chamois cress

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæm.wɑː ˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈʃam.wɑː ˌkrɛs / (BrE)
a small tufted perennial herb native to the mountains of central and southern Europe, known for very small flowers on usually leafless stems; sometimes classified in the genus Lepidium
a small European mountain herb

Examples

The chamois cress thrives in rocky areas at high altitudes.
Botanists studied the chamois cress for its adaptation to harsh mountain climates.
Hikers are sometimes surprised by the delicate flowers of the chamois cress in Alpine meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lepidium - a genus of plants including cresses
