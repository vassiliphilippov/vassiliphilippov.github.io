saffron crocus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfrən ˈkroʊkəs / (AmE), / ˈsæfrən ˈkrəʊkəs / (BrE)
a Mediterranean crocus (Crocus sativus) with purple or white flowers, whose orange stigmas are harvested to produce saffron used as a spice
Mediterranean crocus used for saffron spice

Examples

The saffron crocus blooms in autumn and is meticulously harvested by hand.
Only the stigmas of the saffron crocus are used to make the prized spice saffron.
Farmers in Spain cultivate fields of saffron crocus for both culinary and medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saffron - the spice made from the dried stigmas of Crocus sativus
  • Synonym: crocus - a genus of flowering plants in the iris family

Etymology

Saffron from Arabic *zaʿfarān* via Old French, crocus from Latin *crocus* and Greek *krokos*.
