flycatching warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌkætʃɪŋ ˈwɔrlbɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌkatʃɪŋ ˈwɔːlbə / (BrE)
Any of numerous American wood warblers that catch insects while flying.
American wood warbler that catches insects in flight

Examples

The yellow-rumped flycatching warbler is often seen darting through the air in pursuit of insects.
Many flycatching warblers migrate long distances to take advantage of abundant insect populations.
Unlike other warblers, the flycatching warbler frequently snaps up its prey in midair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird known for its trilling song
