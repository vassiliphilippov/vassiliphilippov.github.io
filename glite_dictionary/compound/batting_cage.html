batting cage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾɪŋ keɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbætɪŋ keɪdʒ / (BrE)
a large screen or enclosed area, often made of netting, used to catch baseballs during batting practice and to keep balls and people safe
enclosure or screen for baseball batting practice

Examples

The batting cage allows players to practice hitting without worrying about chasing stray balls.
Before the game, the team warmed up in the batting cage.
Many parks have public batting cages where anyone can improve their swing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batting practice - period during which baseball batters practice hitting
