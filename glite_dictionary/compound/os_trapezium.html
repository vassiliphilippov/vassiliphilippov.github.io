os trapezium

1. NOUN

/ ɑs træˈpiːziəm / (AmE), / ɒs trəˈpiːzɪəm / (BrE)
the wrist bone on the thumb side of the hand that articulates with the first and second metacarpal bones
wrist bone near thumb
medical

Examples

In a healthy hand, the os trapezium moves smoothly against the surrounding carpal bones.
Arthritis can cause pain in the os trapezium, especially when gripping objects.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's os trapezium for any signs of fracture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapezium - the wrist bone at the base of the thumb
  • Degree Higher: carpal bone - any of the small bones forming the wrist

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) and trapezium, from Greek trapezion (little table).
