brass section

1. NOUN

/ bræs ˈsɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / brɑːs ˈsɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the group of musicians in a band or orchestra who play brass instruments, such as trumpets and trombones
section of brass instrument players

Examples

The brass section played a powerful fanfare to open the concert.
During rehearsal, the conductor asked the brass section to play more softly.
The brass section gives the symphony its bold, distinctive sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woodwind section - the group of musicians who play woodwind instruments in an orchestra or band
  • Antonym: strings - the section of an orchestra that plays stringed instruments
