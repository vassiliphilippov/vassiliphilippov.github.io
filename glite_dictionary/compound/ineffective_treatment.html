ineffective treatment

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tɪv ˈtriːt.mənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tɪv ˈtriːt.mənt / (BrE)
a medical or other treatment that does not achieve the desired or expected results
treatment that does not work
medical

Examples

After several months of ineffective treatment, the patient's symptoms continued to worsen.
Doctors warn that antibiotic misuse can result in ineffective treatment of infections.
He sought a second opinion after realizing the initial therapy was an ineffective treatment.

Synset Examples

An underdose can lead to ineffective treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effective treatment - treatment that produces the desired results
