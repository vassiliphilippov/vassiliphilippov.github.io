spring cleavers

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ ˈkliːvərz / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈkliːvəz / (BrE)
an annual plant with stems covered in curved prickles, found in North America, Europe, and Asia
prickly-stemmed annual plant

Examples

The field was filled with spring cleavers growing among the grasses.
Gardeners often remove spring cleavers to prevent them from overtaking other plants.
The prickly stems of spring cleavers can stick to clothing and animal fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleavers - a common name for Galium aparine, a similar prickly plant
