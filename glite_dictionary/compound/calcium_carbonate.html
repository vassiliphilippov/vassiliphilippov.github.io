calcium carbonate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm ˈkɑːr.bəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæl.si.əm ˈkɑː.bə.neɪt / (BrE)
a white insoluble solid found in rocks as minerals such as chalk, limestone, and marble, used in making lime and cement
a mineral found in chalk, limestone, and marble

Examples

Farmers often add calcium carbonate to soil to reduce its acidity.
Seashells are mainly composed of calcium carbonate.
When heated, calcium carbonate decomposes to form calcium oxide and carbon dioxide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalk - soft, white, porous sedimentary rock, a form of limestone composed of calcium carbonate
  • Synonym: limestone - sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate

Etymology

Calcium from Latin 'calx' (lime) + carbonate, from 'carbonic' (containing carbon).
