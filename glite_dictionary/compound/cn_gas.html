CN gas

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈɛn ɡæs / (AmE), / siː ˈɛn ɡæs / (BrE)
a type of tear gas, less potent but longer lasting than CS gas, used in riot control
a type of tear gas used in riot control

Examples

During the protests, the police dispersed the crowd with CN gas.
Exposure to CN gas caused severe irritation to the eyes and skin.
Unlike CS gas, CN gas lingers in the area for a longer time.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: CS gas - a stronger type of tear gas than CN gas

Etymology

From the chemical abbreviation for chloroacetophenone (CN), the active compound in this gas.
