marsh bellflower

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈbɛlˌflaʊər / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈbɛlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A bellflower found in marshes of eastern North America, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small whitish flowers.
wetland bellflower of eastern North America

Examples

During our hike, we spotted a cluster of marsh bellflowers growing along the wetland path.
The delicate white blooms of the marsh bellflower stood out against the green grasses.
Botanists often study the marsh bellflower to understand wetland ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - plant with bell-shaped flowers, genus Campanula
