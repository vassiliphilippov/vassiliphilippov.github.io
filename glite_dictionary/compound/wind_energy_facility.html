wind energy facility

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd ˈɛnɚdʒi fəˈsɪləti / (AmE), / wɪnd ˈɛnədʒi fəˈsɪlɪti / (BrE)
A power plant that uses wind turbines to generate electricity.
power plant using wind turbines

Examples

Several states are investing in a new wind energy facility to boost their renewable power supply.
The construction of a large wind energy facility created hundreds of local jobs.
Tourists often visit the wind energy facility to learn about sustainable electricity generation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind farm - an area of land with a group of wind turbines used to produce electricity
