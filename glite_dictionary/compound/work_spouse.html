work spouse

1. NOUN

/ wɝːk spaʊs / (AmE), / wɜːk spaʊs / (BrE)
a colleague with whom one has a close, supportive, and usually platonic relationship similar to that of a spouse
close, supportive colleague at work

Examples

Her work spouse helped her manage the stress of a big project deadline.
He confided in his work spouse about the challenges he faced with their manager.
People often notice how well the two work spouses collaborate on team assignments.

Synset Examples

She has a great work spouse who supports her daily.
My work spouse always knows how to lighten the mood.
He joked that his work spouse was the best part of his job.

Etymology

Formed from 'work' and 'spouse'; popularized in the late 20th century to describe close workplace relationships.
