endocentric compound

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈkɑmpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
A compound word in which the whole has the same grammatical function as one of its parts, usually called the 'head.'
compound word with same function as its head

Examples

In linguistics, a endocentric compound like "blackbird" functions as a noun, just as "bird" does.
A typical endocentric compound follows the structure where one element is the head and determines its category.
"Wheelchair" is an endocentric compound because its meaning and grammatical function come primarily from the word "chair."

Synset Examples

‘greenhouse’ is an endocentric compound, since it is a noun as is its head house.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exocentric compound - compound word whose meaning is not determined by one of its parts

Etymology

From endo- (within) + centric (centered) and compound (from Latin componere, to put together).
