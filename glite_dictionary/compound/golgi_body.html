Golgi body

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldʒi ˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈɡɒldʒi ˌbɒdi / (BrE)
a netlike structure in the cytoplasm of animal or plant cells, involved in the processing and packaging of proteins and other substances, especially in cells that produce secretions; also called the Golgi apparatus
structure in cells that processes and packages proteins
medical

Examples

The Golgi body modifies and packages proteins before they are transported out of the cell.
Under the microscope, the Golgi body appears as a stack of flattened sacs within the cytoplasm.
Dysfunction of the Golgi body can affect the secretion of enzymes and hormones in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Golgi apparatus - another name for the Golgi body, a cellular organelle

Etymology

Named after Italian physician Camillo Golgi, who discovered the structure in 1898.
