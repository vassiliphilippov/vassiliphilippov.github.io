malaria parasite

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪriə ˈpɛrəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / məˈlɛːriə ˈparəsʌɪt / (BrE)
a parasitic protozoan of the genus Plasmodium that infects humans and causes malaria
protozoan causing malaria
medical

Examples

The malaria parasite is transmitted to humans by the bite of an infected mosquito.
Scientists are developing new drugs to target the malaria parasite at different stages of its life cycle.
He contracted the disease after being bitten by a mosquito carrying the malaria parasite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plasmodium - genus of protozoa that causes malaria
