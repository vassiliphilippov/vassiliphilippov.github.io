needle blight

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəl blaɪt / (AmE), / ˈniːdl̩ blaɪt / (BrE)
A disease affecting coniferous trees, causing their needles to fall prematurely.
disease causing conifer needles to fall

Examples

The spruce trees in the forest were severely affected by needle blight this year.
Foresters are researching ways to prevent needle blight from spreading to other conifers.
Early detection of needle blight is essential for saving young pine plantations.
