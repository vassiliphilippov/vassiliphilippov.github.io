hanging offence

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɪŋ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈhæŋɪŋ əˈfɛns / (BrE)
a crime considered serious enough to be punishable by death, especially by hanging; often used figuratively to describe a severe wrongdoing
crime punishable by death, often figurative

Examples

In the early 19th century, stealing a horse was considered a hanging offence.
Insider trading is treated as a hanging offence in some financial circles, though not literally punished by death.
Criticizing the team's strategy was a hanging offence among the dedicated fans.

Synset Examples

In this sport, doping is a hanging offence.
Failing to report for duty is a hanging offence.
Cheating on the test is a hanging offence here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capital offense - a crime punishable by death
  • Antonym: petty offense - a minor crime, not serious

Etymology

From the practice in English law of death by hanging for certain crimes; 'offence' meaning crime.
