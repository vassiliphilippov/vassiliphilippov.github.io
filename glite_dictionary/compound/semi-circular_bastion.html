semi-circular bastion

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈsɝkjələr ˈbæs.tʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈsɜːkjʊlə ˈbæs.tʃən / (BrE)
a type of bastion with a curved, semicircular shape, used in 16th-century fortifications but later abandoned due to its inefficiency in concentrating gunfire.
16th-century curved fortification structure

Examples

The old fortress still stands, featuring a prominent semi-circular bastion overlooking the valley.
Engineers abandoned the semi-circular bastion design in favor of more angular fortifications.
Archaeologists studied a ruined semi-circular bastion dating back to the Elizabethan era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification

Etymology

From Latin 'semi-' (half) and 'circulus' (circle), joined with 'bastion' from French 'bastille' (fortification).
