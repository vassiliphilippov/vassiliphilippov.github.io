knitting stitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪtɪŋ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈnɪtɪŋ stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A single loop or unit of yarn created by making a specific movement with the needles while knitting.
a loop or unit in knitting

Examples

She dropped a knitting stitch and had to unravel several rows to fix it.
Changing the type of knitting stitch can create different patterns in a sweater.
The basic knitting stitch most beginners learn is called the knit stitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitch - a loop of thread or yarn resulting from a single pass or movement of the needle in sewing, knitting, or crocheting
