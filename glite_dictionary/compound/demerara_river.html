Demerara River

1. PROPN

/ ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə ˈrɪv.ər / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river in northern Guyana that flows northward through the country to the Atlantic Ocean
river in northern Guyana

Examples

The Demerara River is a crucial waterway for transporting goods in Guyana.
Fishing villages line the banks of the Demerara River along its route to the ocean.
Many bridges cross the Demerara River, connecting communities on both sides.

Etymology

Named after the Demerara region of Guyana, itself from Dutch colonial origins.
