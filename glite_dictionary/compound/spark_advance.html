spark advance

1. NOUN

/ spɑrk ədˈvæns / (AmE), / spɑːk ədˈvɑːns / (BrE)
the timing of the ignition of the fuel-air mixture in an internal-combustion engine, measured relative to the piston's position
timing of ignition in an engine

Examples

Adjusting the spark advance can improve an engine’s performance and efficiency.
Incorrect spark advance may cause knocking or poor acceleration in cars.
Modern engines use sensors to automatically control spark advance for optimal operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition timing - the timing of the spark in relation to piston position in an engine
