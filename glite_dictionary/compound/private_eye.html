private eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət aɪ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət aɪ / (BrE)
a private detective; a person who is hired to investigate and collect information, often for criminal cases
private detective
informal american

Examples

The private eye followed the suspect through the city streets, gathering evidence for his client.
She hired a private eye to find out who was sending her anonymous letters.
Classic films often feature a private eye with a sharp wit and a knack for solving mysteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person whose job is to investigate crimes and find information
  • Synonym: sleuth - another term for a detective, often used informally

Etymology

From 'private' (not official, personal) and 'eye' (slang for detective or observer), originally American English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət aɪ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət aɪ / (BrE)
A private investigator, especially one hired to solve crimes or uncover information confidentially.
private investigator; detective

Examples

The private eye lingered outside the restaurant, hoping to catch a glimpse of the target.
Hiring a private eye was their last resort when the police had no leads.
The novel is about a cynical private eye who unravels a complex mystery.

Synset Examples

He plays a private eye in the latest crime series.
The private eye followed the suspect for days.
The private eye uncovered the truth behind the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private investigator - a person who is licensed to undertake investigations
  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes and catch criminals

Etymology

Formed from 'private' and 'eye', alluding to a vigilant personal detective.
