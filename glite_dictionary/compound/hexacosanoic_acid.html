hexacosanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛk.sə.kəˈsæn.oʊ.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛk.sə.kəˈsæn.əʊ.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a white solid fatty acid found in natural waxes, such as beeswax; also known as cerotic acid
white solid fatty acid in waxes

Examples

Hexacosanoic acid is commonly extracted from beeswax for laboratory analysis.
Researchers studied the role of hexacosanoic acid in the protection of plant leaves.
Because of its high melting point, hexacosanoic acid is valuable in the manufacturing of certain waxes.

Etymology

From Greek 'hex' (six), 'eikos' (twenty), and '-anoic' (related to carboxylic acids), reflecting the 26 carbon atoms in its structure.
