changeling child

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒlɪŋ tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒlɪŋ tʃaɪld / (BrE)
(in folklore) a child believed to have been secretly substituted by fairies for the parents' real child.
mythical child substituted by fairies

Examples

The villagers whispered that the strange boy was a changeling child left by the fairies.
Old stories describe families discovering a changeling child after their baby mysteriously changed behavior.
In some legends, a changeling child could only be revealed through special rituals.

Synset Examples

The old folklore tells tales of a changeling child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: changeling - a child believed to have been secretly exchanged by fairies for the parents' real child

Etymology

'Changeling' from Middle English changeling (one who has been changed), from change + -ling (diminutive suffix).
