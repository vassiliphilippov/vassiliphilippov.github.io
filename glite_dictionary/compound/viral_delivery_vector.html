viral delivery vector

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪrəl dɪˈlɪvəri ˈvɛktər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪərəl dɪˈlɪvəri ˈvɛktə / (BrE)
a virus-based carrier used to deliver genetic material into cells, especially for gene therapy or research purposes
virus-based carrier for gene delivery
medical

Examples

Researchers used a viral delivery vector to introduce the modified gene into the patient's cells.
The safety of each viral delivery vector must be thoroughly tested before clinical use.
Advances in viral delivery vector technology have improved the efficiency of gene therapy treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vector - a carrier that transfers genetic material into a cell

Etymology

From Latin virus (poison) and Latin vector (carrier).
