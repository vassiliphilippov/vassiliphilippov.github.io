Himantopus stilt

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəpəs ˈstɪlt / (AmE), / hɪˌmæn.tə.pəs ˈstɪlt / (BrE)
a long-legged black-and-white wading bird of the genus Himantopus, found in inland ponds, marshes, and brackish lagoons
wading bird of the genus Himantopus

Examples

A Himantopus stilt was spotted feeding in the shallow waters of the marsh.
The black-and-white plumage of the Himantopus stilt makes it easy to identify.
Conservationists are monitoring the breeding sites of the Himantopus stilt to protect the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilt - a type of long-legged wading bird

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'himantopous' meaning 'strap foot', describing the bird's long legs; 'stilt' refers to its leggy appearance.
