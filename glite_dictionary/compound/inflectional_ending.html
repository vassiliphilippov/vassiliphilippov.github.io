inflectional ending

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɛkʃənəl ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃənəl ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
a letter or group of letters added to the end of a word to show its grammatical function, such as tense, number, or case
grammatical ending added to words

Examples

The inflectional ending '-ed' in 'walked' shows that the action is in the past.
Adding an inflectional ending changes the word's grammatical form without changing its core meaning.
English nouns often take the inflectional ending '-s' to form plurals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffix - a letter or group of letters added at the end of a word
