genus Borago

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈreɪ.ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈreɪ.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs native to the Mediterranean region, including the common borage plant
a genus of Mediterranean perennial herbs

Examples

The genus Borago is characterized by hairy leaves and star-shaped blue flowers.
Several species within the genus Borago are cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.
Researchers are studying the genus Borago for its potential use in natural medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borage - an annual herb of the genus Borago, often grown for its edible leaves and flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Borago,' the name of the common borage plant, from Medieval Latin.
