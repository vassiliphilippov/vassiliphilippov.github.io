iridaceous plant

1. NOUN

/ ɪr.əˈdeɪ.ʃəs plænt / (AmE), / ɪr.ɪˈdeɪ.ʃəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
Any bulbous plant belonging to the family Iridaceae, including irises, crocuses, and gladioli.
bulbous plant of the Iridaceae family

Examples

The iridaceous plant in her garden bloomed early in the spring.
Crocuses are a type of iridaceous plant prized for their vibrant flowers.
Many gardeners prefer an iridaceous plant like the iris for its striking colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant of the genus Iris, a well-known member of Iridaceae
  • Synonym: crocus - a plant of the genus Crocus, another member of Iridaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'Iridaceae', based on 'iris' (the plant) + '-aceous' meaning 'of the nature of'.
