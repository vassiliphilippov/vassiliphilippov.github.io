sleepy sickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpi ˌsɪknɪs / (AmE), / ˈsliːpi ˌsɪknɪs / (BrE)
A form of encephalitis, especially the epidemic that occurred between 1915 and 1926, characterized by paralysis of the eye muscles and severe muscular weakness.
historical epidemic encephalitis

Examples

The outbreak of sleepy sickness in the early 20th century baffled doctors and scientists alike.
Some survivors of sleepy sickness suffered from long-term neurological problems.
Historical records describe sleepy sickness as causing sudden sleep, weakness, and paralysis in its victims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encephalitis lethargica - an epidemic form of encephalitis causing lethargy and other symptoms, mainly between 1915–1926
