Australian magpie

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˈmæɡˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˈmæɡpaɪ / (BrE)
a large black-and-white songbird native to Australia, known for its melodious call and for resembling the European magpie
black-and-white Australian songbird

Examples

We spotted an Australian magpie singing in the garden early this morning.
The Australian magpie is famous for its beautiful, flute-like warbling call.
Many Australians are wary of Australian magpies during nesting season, as they can become aggressive.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: magpie - a long-tailed black-and-white bird related to the crow
