sabre rattling

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbər ˌræt̬lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə ˌrætlɪŋ / (BrE)
the showy display of military power intended to threaten or intimidate others
threatening display of military power

Examples

The government's sabre rattling worried neighboring countries and raised fears of conflict.
Critics accused the president of engaging in sabre rattling rather than genuine diplomacy.
Recent sabre rattling by both countries has increased tensions in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brinkmanship - the practice of pursuing a dangerous policy to the limits of safety
  • Antonym: diplomacy - the profession or skill of managing international relations

Etymology

From 'sabre' (a type of sword) and 'rattling' (making shaking noise); metaphorical use since early 20th century.
