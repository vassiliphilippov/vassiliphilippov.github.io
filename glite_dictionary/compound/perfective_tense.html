perfective tense

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛktɪv tɛns / (AmE), / pəˈfɛktɪv tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense that denotes an action or event that has been completed, especially one that emphasizes the result or completion of the action
verb tense showing completed action

Examples

In Russian, the perfective tense is used to emphasize the completion of an action.
The perfective tense contrasts with the imperfective aspect in describing ongoing versus finished actions.
She explained how the perfective tense changes the meaning of verbs in that language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfective aspect - aspect indicating completed action, regardless of tense

Etymology

From Latin perfectivus (tending to complete) and tense, meaning time (from Latin tempus).
