Mt. Rushmore

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaʊnt ˌrʌʃˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈmaʊnt ˌrʌʃˌmɔː / (BrE)
a mountain in the Black Hills of South Dakota, famous for the colossal carved likenesses of four U.S. presidents: Washington, Jefferson, Lincoln, and Roosevelt
U.S. monument with presidents' carvings

Examples

The faces of four presidents are carved into Mt. Rushmore, attracting millions of visitors each year.
Our family trip included a stop at Mt. Rushmore National Memorial in South Dakota.
Photographs of Mt. Rushmore are often used to symbolize American history and leadership.
