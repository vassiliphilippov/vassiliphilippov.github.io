Plumbago europaea

1. NOUN

/ plʌmˈbeɪɡoʊ juˈroʊpiə / (AmE), / plʌmˈbeɪɡəʊ jʊˈrəʊpiə / (BrE)
a species of flowering plant in the genus Plumbago, native to southern Europe, with blue flowers
a species of blue-flowered Plumbago plant

Examples

The gardens featured several Plumbago europaea plants in full bloom by late spring.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Plumbago europaea in traditional European remedies.
The blue flowers of Plumbago europaea attract many pollinators throughout the summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'plumbago' (leadwort) and 'europaea' (of Europe), referring to its native region.
