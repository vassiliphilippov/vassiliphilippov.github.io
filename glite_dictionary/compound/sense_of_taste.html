sense of taste

1. NOUN

/ sɛns əv teɪst / (AmE), / sɛns əv teɪst / (BrE)
the faculty by which a person distinguishes sweet, sour, bitter, and salty properties in the mouth
the ability to perceive flavors

Examples

After the accident, he lost his sense of taste and couldn't enjoy food as before.
Pregnancy can sometimes heighten a woman's sense of taste.
Some medications may dull your sense of taste temporarily.

Synset Examples

his cold deprived him of his sense of taste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taste - the sensation produced when a substance in the mouth reacts chemically with taste receptor cells
