mountain tea

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tən tiː / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn tiː / (BrE)
A creeping shrub native to eastern North America, with white bell-shaped flowers, spicy red berrylike fruit, and shiny aromatic leaves that yield wintergreen oil.
creeping shrub yielding wintergreen oil

Examples

Hikers often spot mountain tea growing along shaded forest trails in the Appalachian Mountains.
The aromatic leaves of mountain tea are sometimes used to make herbal infusions.
Native Americans traditionally used mountain tea for its medicinal properties.
