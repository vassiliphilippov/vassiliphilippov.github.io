white clover

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
a low-growing plant with rounded leaves and clusters of small white or pinkish flowers, commonly found in grasslands and used as forage
a creeping plant with white or pinkish flower clusters

Examples

The field was covered with white clover blossoming in the summer sun.
Farmers often plant white clover to improve soil fertility and provide forage for livestock.
Children wove garlands from the sweet-smelling flowers of white clover in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red clover - similar clover species with reddish flowers
