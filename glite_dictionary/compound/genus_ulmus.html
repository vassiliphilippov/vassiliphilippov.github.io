genus Ulmus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈʌl.məs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈʌl.məs / (BrE)
the botanical genus comprising the elms, deciduous trees with simple serrated leaves, widely distributed in temperate regions
botanical genus of elms

Examples

Many species within the genus Ulmus are susceptible to Dutch elm disease.
The genus Ulmus includes both native and introduced types of elm trees.
Researchers study the genus Ulmus to understand the diversity of elm species worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a deciduous tree of the genus Ulmus

Etymology

From Latin 'Ulmus' (elm), the classical name for the elm tree.
