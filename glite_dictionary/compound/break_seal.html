break seal

1. NOUN

/ breɪk sil / (AmE), / breɪk siːl / (BrE)
a seal designed to be irreversibly broken upon first use, indicating whether a container or package has been opened
tamper-evident seal broken at first use

Examples

The medicine was packaged with a break seal to ensure its contents remained uncontaminated.
If the break seal is missing or damaged, do not use the product.
Each laboratory sample was secured with a break seal to maintain its integrity.

Synset Examples

it was stored in a tube with a break seal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamper-evident seal - a seal that shows clear evidence if accessed or removed
  • Antonym: resealable seal - a seal that can be closed again after opening
