Kafiri language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈfɪri ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈfɪəri ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Dardic language historically spoken by the Kafir people in northeastern Afghanistan, now usually known as Nuristani languages
Dardic language of northeastern Afghanistan

Examples

The Kafiri language is now more commonly referred to as Nuristani, reflecting changes in regional identity.
Few written records exist of the ancient Kafiri language, making its study challenging for linguists.
Anthropologists are interested in the cultural practices preserved through the Kafiri language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nuristani language - any of several languages spoken in Nuristan, Afghanistan, formerly called Kafiri

Etymology

From Persian 'Kafir' (infidel), the ethnonym used for the local population; now replaced by 'Nuristani'.
