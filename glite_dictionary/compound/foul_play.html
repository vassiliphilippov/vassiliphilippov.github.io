foul play

1. NOUN

/ faʊl pleɪ / (AmE), / faʊl pleɪ / (BrE)
unlawful, dishonest, or violent behavior, especially when causing suspicion of a crime or unfairness
unlawful or violent dishonest behavior

Examples

The police suspect foul play after discovering unusual marks at the scene.
There is no evidence of foul play in his sudden disappearance.
The referee called foul play when one player deliberately tripped another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an illegal act; an action punishable by law
  • Antonym: fair play - honest and just behavior or treatment
