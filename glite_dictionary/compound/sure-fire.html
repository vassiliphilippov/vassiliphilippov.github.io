sure-fire

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃʊrˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːˌfaɪə / (BrE)
Certain to be effective or successful; guaranteed to work or happen as intended.
certain to be successful

Examples

This new marketing strategy is a sure-fire way to increase our sales.
He gave me a sure-fire recipe for making the perfect bread every time.
Her idea was a sure-fire success with the judges.

Synset Examples

a sure-fire way to get rich

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guaranteed - certain or promised, especially to succeed
  • Synonym: fail-safe - designed to go right or not fail
  • Antonym: risky - involving risk or uncertainty of success

Etymology

Formed from 'sure' + 'fire', originally referring to guns that were certain to discharge properly.
