Catawba language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtɔbə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈtɔːbə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language historically spoken by the Catawba people of the southeastern United States
Siouan language of the Catawba people

Examples

The Catawba language is considered critically endangered, with only a few speakers remaining.
Scholars have compiled dictionaries to help preserve the Catawba language.
He studied the phonetics of the Catawba language for his linguistic research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - family of languages spoken by the Sioux and related tribes

Etymology

From Catawba, the name of the indigenous people, combined with 'language'.
