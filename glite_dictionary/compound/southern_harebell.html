southern harebell

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn ˈhɛrˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈhɛəbɛl / (BrE)
A bellflower native to the southeastern United States, from Maryland to Georgia, with pale blue flowers.
bellflower of the southeastern US

Examples

The southern harebell blooms each spring in woodlands across Georgia and Maryland.
Hikers were delighted to spot a patch of southern harebell along the forest trail.
Botanists have documented the slow decline of the southern harebell due to habitat loss.
