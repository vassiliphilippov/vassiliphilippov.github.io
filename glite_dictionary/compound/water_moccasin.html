water moccasin

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈmɑkəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈmɒkəsɪn / (BrE)
A venomous North American snake, typically found in freshwater habitats; also called cottonmouth.
a venomous North American water snake

Examples

The water moccasin is often found near swamps and slow-moving streams in the southeastern United States.
Despite their reputation, water moccasins will usually avoid humans if left undisturbed.
We spotted a large water moccasin basking on a log beside the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottonmouth - another name for the water moccasin, a venomous snake

Etymology

The name likely derives from the Algonquian word for 'shoe' or 'slipper,' in reference to the Native American footwear called moccasins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˈmɑkəsən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈmɒkəsɪn / (BrE)
a large venomous semiaquatic snake (Agkistrodon piscivorus) found in swamps, marshes, and slow-moving streams in the southern United States; also called cottonmouth
a venomous aquatic snake of the southern US

Examples

The water moccasin is often found sunning itself on logs by the bayou.
People should take caution when swimming in areas known to be inhabited by water moccasins.
She recognized the distinctive pattern of the water moccasin as it glided silently across the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottonmouth - common name for Agkistrodon piscivorus, same species as water moccasin

Etymology

Moccasin from Algonquian language, originally referring to a type of shoe, transferred to the snake for its coloration.
