electrical relay

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
an electrical device that uses a current in one circuit to open or close a switch in another circuit
device controlling a circuit by another circuit

Examples

An electrical relay can isolate and control high voltage equipment from a low voltage circuit.
When the sensor detects a fault, it activates the electrical relay to cut the power supply.
Modern cars use an electrical relay to switch on the headlights and other major systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relay - device for opening or closing a circuit by an electric signal
