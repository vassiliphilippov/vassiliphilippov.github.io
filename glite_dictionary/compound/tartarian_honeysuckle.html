Tartarian honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ tɑːrˈtɛriən ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / tɑːˈtɛːrɪən ˈhʌniˌsʌkl / (BrE)
A species of honeysuckle shrub (Lonicera tatarica) native to southern Russia and central Asia, often grown as an ornamental plant.
species of honeysuckle shrub

Examples

The Tartarian honeysuckle produces clusters of pink flowers in late spring.
Gardeners often plant Tartarian honeysuckle to attract birds and butterflies.
Some regions consider Tartarian honeysuckle invasive due to its rapid growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a shrub or climbing plant of the genus Lonicera with fragrant flowers

Etymology

Tartarian, referring to Tartary (a historical region in central and northern Asia), and honeysuckle, from Middle English 'hony-sucle'.
