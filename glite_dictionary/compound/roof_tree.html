roof tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːf ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈruːf ˌtriː / (BrE)
the main beam supporting the roof of a building, or a tree growing on a roof or at a great height
main roof beam or tree on a roof

Examples

The old cottage still had its original roof tree supporting the entire structure.
After the storm, the roof tree was badly damaged and needed replacement.
Birds nested in the roof tree, hidden high above the ground.

Synset Examples

The children climbed the roof tree to watch the sunset.
A roof tree is essential for the stability of a house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridgepole - the horizontal beam at the top of a roof

Etymology

From 'roof' + 'tree', where 'tree' is an old term for a timber beam.
