whitebarked pine

1. NOUN

/ ˌwaɪtˌbɑrkt ˈpaɪn / (AmE), / ˌwaɪtˌbɑːkt ˈpaɪn / (BrE)
a small pine tree native to western North America, known for its smooth grey-white bark and soft, brittle wood
a pine tree with white bark from western North America

Examples

The whitebarked pine thrives at high elevations in the Rocky Mountains.
Many animals rely on the seeds of the whitebarked pine for food.
Researchers are studying the impact of climate change on whitebarked pine populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limber pine - a similar pine tree native to western North America
