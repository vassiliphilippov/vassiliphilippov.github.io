trenchant wit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛntʃənt wɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛntʃənt wɪt / (BrE)
the ability to express humor in a sharp, incisive, and often critical way
sharp, incisive humor

Examples

The critic's trenchant wit made her reviews both entertaining and thought-provoking.
He disarmed his opponents with his trenchant wit and quick comebacks.
Her trenchant wit enlivened many dinner conversations, often leaving guests in stitches.

Synset Examples

He has a trenchant wit that makes him stand out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting wit - humor that is sharp and capable of hurting feelings
  • Degree Lower: blunt wit - humor delivered in a direct, sometimes harsh manner
  • Degree Higher: caustic wit - wit that is even more biting and corrosive than trenchant wit
