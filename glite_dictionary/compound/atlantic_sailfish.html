Atlantic sailfish

1. NOUN

/ əˈtlæn.tɪk ˈseɪl.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ətˈlæn.tɪk ˈseɪl.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large game fish of the Atlantic Ocean, known for its long bill and distinctive sail-like dorsal fin
large Atlantic game fish with a sail-like dorsal fin

Examples

The Atlantic sailfish is prized by sport fishermen for its speed and agility.
We spotted a large Atlantic sailfish leaping out of the water near the boat.
Researchers track the migration patterns of the Atlantic sailfish to study changes in ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailfish - large marine fish with a prominent sail-like dorsal fin
