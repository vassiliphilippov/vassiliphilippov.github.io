gray sole

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ soʊl / (AmE), / ɡreɪ səʊl / (BrE)
The flesh of a type of flatfish, typically grayish-white in color, used as food.
flesh of a flatfish used as food

Examples

The restaurant special tonight features gray sole with lemon and capers.
She prefers the delicate flavor of gray sole over stronger-tasting fish.
At the market, fresh gray sole was displayed on ice alongside cod and haddock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sole - any flatfish of the family Soleidae
