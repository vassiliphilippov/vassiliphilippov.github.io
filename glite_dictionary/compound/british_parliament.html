British Parliament

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈpɑːr.ləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.ɪʃ ˈpɑː.lɪ.mənt / (BrE)
the supreme legislative body in the United Kingdom, consisting of the House of Commons and the House of Lords
the legislative body of the United Kingdom
british

Examples

The British Parliament passed several important laws last year.
Debates in the British Parliament can last for hours, especially on controversial topics.
Tourists often visit the British Parliament to see its historic buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Parliament - the national legislative body of a country
  • Antonym: Congress - the national legislative body in the United States
