Bessemer process

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛsəmər ˌproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˈbɛsɪmə ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
an industrial method of producing steel by blowing air through molten iron to remove carbon and other impurities, enabling mass production at low cost
steel-making by air-blast method

Examples

The invention of the Bessemer process revolutionized steel production in the nineteenth century.
Because of the Bessemer process, steel became much more affordable and widely used in construction.
Many old factories still display equipment once used for the Bessemer process.

Etymology

Named after Sir Henry Bessemer (1813–1898), English engineer and inventor.
