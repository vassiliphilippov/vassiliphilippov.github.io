love-lies-bleeding

1. NOUN

/ ˌlʌv laɪz ˈbliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌlʌv laɪz ˈbliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A tropical plant (Amaranthus caudatus) with long, drooping red flower spikes; its young leaves are used as vegetables and its seeds as cereal.
tropical plant, edible leaves and seeds

Examples

The love-lies-bleeding in her garden added a splash of dramatic color with its cascading red flowers.
Many traditional dishes in South America use the leaves of love-lies-bleeding as a nutritious green.
Farmers are beginning to appreciate love-lies-bleeding for its hardy seeds, which can be used as a cereal.

Etymology

Named for the long, drooping red flower clusters, resembling bleeding, and associated with the sentiment of unrequited love.
