mad money

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæd ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈmad ˌmʌni / (BrE)
A small amount of money set aside for discretionary spending or unexpected enjoyment.
money set aside for fun spending
informal

Examples

She always keeps some mad money in her wallet for unexpected treats.
After paying her bills, she put aside a little mad money to go to the movies with friends.
He dipped into his mad money for an unplanned weekend getaway.

Synset Examples

She uses her mad money to buy gifts for friends.
He saved some mad money for a spontaneous trip.
The couple had some mad money for fun activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spending money - money set aside for daily or personal expenses

Etymology

From the idea of money kept for 'mad' (impulsive or out-of-the-ordinary) uses, US slang origin early 20th century.
