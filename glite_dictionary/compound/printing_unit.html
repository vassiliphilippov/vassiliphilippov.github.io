printing unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntɪŋ ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɪntɪŋ ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement used in the printing industry to specify size, spacing, or other print-related dimensions
unit of measurement in printing

Examples

The brochure's margins were set to one printing unit on each side.
Many designers use points as a standard printing unit when laying out text.
Precision is essential, so every printing unit must be calculated accurately.
