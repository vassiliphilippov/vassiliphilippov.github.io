antipernicious anemia factor

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.pərˈnɪʃ.əs əˈniː.mi.ə ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.pəˈnɪʃ.əs əˈniː.mi.ə ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
a former name for vitamin B12, the vitamin used to treat pernicious anemia
old term for vitamin B12
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with pernicious anemia, the patient began receiving injections of antipernicious anemia factor.
Historically, antipernicious anemia factor was identified as the key substance missing in the disease.
Research showed that antipernicious anemia factor is essential for the production of healthy red blood cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B12 - an essential vitamin also known as cobalamin

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against), 'pernicious anemia' (a disease), and 'factor' (substance); refers to its role in preventing pernicious anemia.
