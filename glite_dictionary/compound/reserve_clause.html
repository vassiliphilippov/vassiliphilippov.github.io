reserve clause

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɝv klɔz / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv klɔːz / (BrE)
a clause formerly included in professional athletes' contracts, allowing teams to extend the contract for an additional year beyond its expiration, thus binding the athlete to the team
historical contract clause in professional sports
legal

Examples

The controversial reserve clause kept many baseball players tied to a single team throughout their career.
The legal battle over the reserve clause changed the future of free agency in sports.
Before free agency, the reserve clause was a standard part of every professional player's contract.

Synset Examples

the reserve clause was used to bind players to a particular ball club
