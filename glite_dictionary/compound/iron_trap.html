iron trap

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚn træp / (AmE), / ˈaɪən træp / (BrE)
a trap that is impossible or extremely difficult to escape from
inescapable trap

Examples

The criminal found himself caught in the iron trap of the law, with no way to flee.
Once inside the bureaucratic process, he felt as if he were stuck in an iron trap.
The spy feared falling into an iron trap set by the enemy agents.
