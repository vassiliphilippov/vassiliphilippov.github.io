order Podicipediformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrdər poʊˌdɪsɪpɪˈdɪfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə pəʊˌdɪsɪpɪˈdɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of aquatic, diving birds known as grebes
order of aquatic birds (grebes)

Examples

Scientists classified the little grebe under the order Podicipediformes due to its distinctive features.
The order Podicipediformes includes birds that are expert divers and swimmers.
All members of the order Podicipediformes are known for their lobed toes and aquatic lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - an aquatic diving bird of the family Podicipedidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Podicipediformes, based on Latin podicipes (grebe genus: 'rump-footed') + -formes (having the form of).
