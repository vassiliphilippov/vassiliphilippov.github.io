World Council of Churches

1. PROPN

/ wɝld ˈkaʊn.səl əv ˈtʃɝ.tʃɪz / (AmE), / wɜːld ˈkaʊn.səl əv ˈtʃɜː.tʃɪz / (BrE)
an international ecumenical organization of Protestant and Eastern churches founded to promote unity and cooperation in religious and secular matters
international Christian ecumenical organization

Examples

The World Council of Churches encourages dialogue among different Christian denominations.
In 1948, the World Council of Churches was established to foster Christian unity worldwide.
She attended a conference organized by the World Council of Churches to discuss social justice issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenical - promoting unity among the world's Christian churches
