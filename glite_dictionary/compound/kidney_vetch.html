kidney vetch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdni vɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni vɛtʃ / (BrE)
a perennial Eurasian herb (Anthyllis vulneraria) with heads of red or yellow flowers, common in meadows and pastures, formerly used in medicine for kidney disorders
herb with red or yellow flower heads, formerly medicinal

Examples

We spotted kidney vetch growing along the coastal cliffs during our hike.
The bright yellow flowers of kidney vetch attract bees and butterflies to the garden.
In the past, herbalists used kidney vetch as a remedy for various kidney complaints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - any of a group of climbing plants from the pea family

Etymology

Named for its historical use in treating kidney ailments and resemblance to vetch plants; vetch from Old French 'veche', from Latin 'vicia'.
