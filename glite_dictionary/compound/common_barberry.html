common barberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈbɑrbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbɑːbəri / (BrE)
An upright deciduous shrub (Berberis vulgaris), native to Europe and widely naturalized in the United States, known for its clusters of juicy red berries.
a European shrub with edible red berries

Examples

The bright red berries of the common barberry attract many species of birds in the autumn.
Gardeners often plant common barberry as an ornamental shrub due to its attractive foliage.
Researchers studied the spread of common barberry in North American woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barberry - any shrub of the genus Berberis with spiny shoots and yellow flowers
