ricinoleic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪsɪˈnoʊliɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌrɪsɪˈnəʊlɪɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an oily unsaturated fatty acid found in castor oil, used especially in making soaps and lubricants
fatty acid from castor oil, used in soaps
medical

Examples

Ricinoleic acid is the main component responsible for the laxative effect of castor oil.
The soap industry often uses ricinoleic acid for its cleansing properties.
Researchers studied the effects of ricinoleic acid on bacterial growth in laboratory settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oleic acid - another unsaturated fatty acid found in various animal and vegetable fats

Etymology

From ricinus (Latin for 'castor oil plant') + -oleic (from oleic acid) + acid.
