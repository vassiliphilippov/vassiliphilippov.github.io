coefficient of mutual induction

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv ˈmjuːtʃuəl ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv ˈmjuːtʃuəl ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
a measure of the ability of one electric circuit to induce an electromotive force in another circuit, expressed as the ratio of the induced e.m.f. in one circuit to the rate of change of current in the neighboring circuit, usually measured in henries.
measure of electromagnetic induction between circuits

Examples

The coefficient of mutual induction determines how much voltage is induced in the secondary coil.
In transformer design, engineers calculate the coefficient of mutual induction to optimize energy transfer.
A change in the orientation of the coils can significantly affect the coefficient of mutual induction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual inductance - the property quantifying mutual induction between circuits

Etymology

From Latin 'coefficient' (co- 'together' + facere 'to do') and Latin 'mutuus' (mutual) + 'inductio' (induction).
