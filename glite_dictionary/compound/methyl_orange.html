methyl orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːθɪl ˈɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈmiːθɪl ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
an orange azo dye used as an acid-base indicator in titrations, especially with weak bases
orange azo dye; acid-base indicator
medical

Examples

Methyl orange is commonly used in laboratories to determine the pH of a solution.
The color of methyl orange changes from orange to red as the solution becomes more acidic.
We added methyl orange to the mixture to observe when the titration reached its endpoint.

Etymology

From 'methyl' (chemical group derived from methane) and 'orange' (its color as a dye).
