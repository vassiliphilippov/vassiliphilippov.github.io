green hellebore

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈhɛlɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈhɛlɪˌbɔː / (BrE)
A deciduous plant with large, deep green, divided leaves and nodding, saucer-shaped green flowers, native to Europe.
a European plant with green flowers

Examples

The gardener planted green hellebore to add color to the winter landscape.
Green hellebore is known for its toxic properties, so children and pets should be kept away.
The green hellebore blooms early in the spring, often before many other flowers appear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellebore - a genus of perennial plants with toxic roots and flowers

Etymology

From Middle English 'hellebor', via Old French, from Latin 'helleborus', from Greek 'helleboros'.
