limited liability

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmɪtəd laɪəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˈlɪmɪtɪd laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
A legal condition in which a company's owners are responsible for its debts only up to the amount they have invested.
owners' liability limited to investment
legal

Examples

The structure of a corporation provides limited liability to its shareholders.
Because of limited liability, personal assets are protected if the business fails.
Investors are attracted to companies offering limited liability arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlimited liability - owners are personally responsible for all business debts

Etymology

From 'limited' (restrained) and 'liability' (legal responsibility); used in business law since the 19th century.
