Mexican husk tomato

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən hʌsk təˈmeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən hʌsk təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a small, edible fruit similar to a tomato, yellow to purple in color, enclosed in a papery husk and native to Mexico; often used in Mexican cuisine
small fruit in husk, used in Mexican cooking

Examples

Salsa verde is made with Mexican husk tomatoes instead of regular tomatoes.
The market had a fresh batch of Mexican husk tomatoes for making sauces.
After peeling the papery covering, she chopped the Mexican husk tomato for the stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomatillo - another name for the Mexican husk tomato

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən hʌsk təˈmeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən hʌsk təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
An annual plant native to Mexico and the southern United States, with edible purplish sticky fruits resembling small tomatoes, often used in cooking.
plant with edible tomato-like fruit

Examples

The salsa was made with fresh Mexican husk tomato for a distinctive tangy flavor.
Farmers in southern Texas often cultivate the Mexican husk tomato for local markets.
You can substitute a Mexican husk tomato for regular tomatoes in many recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomatillo - another name for the Mexican husk tomato, especially in culinary contexts
