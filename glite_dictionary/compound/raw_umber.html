raw umber

1. NOUN

/ rɔ ˈʌmbɚ / (AmE), / rɔː ˈʌmbə / (BrE)
a natural brown or greenish-brown earth pigment used in painting, consisting of clay containing iron and manganese oxides
a greenish-brown earth pigment

Examples

The artist mixed raw umber with white to achieve a subtle earthy tone.
You can use raw umber to create natural-looking shadows in landscape paintings.
He purchased a tube of raw umber for his next watercolor project.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: burnt umber - a reddish-brown pigment obtained by heating raw umber

Etymology

From Italian 'ombra' (shade, shadow), via French 'ombre', referring to its use for shading in painting.
