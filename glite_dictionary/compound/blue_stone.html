blue stone

1. NOUN

/ bluː stoʊn / (AmE), / bluː stəʊn / (BrE)
A hydrated blue crystalline form of copper sulfate, used especially as a fungicide or in chemistry.
hydrated blue copper sulfate crystal

Examples

The farmer treated his crops with blue stone to prevent fungal diseases.
In chemistry class, we examined crystals of blue stone under the microscope.
Old recipes for ink sometimes called for blue stone as an ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper sulfate - a common blue chemical compound, CuSO₄
