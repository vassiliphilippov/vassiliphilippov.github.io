REM sleep

1. NOUN

/ ɑr i ɛm slip / (AmE), / ɑː iː ɛm sliːp / (BrE)
A stage of sleep characterized by rapid movement of the eyes, vivid dreaming, and increased brain activity.
a stage of sleep with rapid eye movement and dreaming
medical

Examples

Most intense dreaming takes place during REM sleep, when the brain is highly active.
A typical adult cycles through several periods of REM sleep each night.
Researchers study REM sleep to understand its role in memory and mood regulation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: NREM sleep - sleep stages without rapid eye movement, contrasting with REM sleep

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'rapid eye movement', describing the characteristic motion observed during this sleep stage.
