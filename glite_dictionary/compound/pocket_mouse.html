pocket mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪt maʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt maʊs / (BrE)
A small nocturnal burrowing rodent found in deserts, noted for its external cheek pouches and long hind legs and tail.
small desert rodent with cheek pouches

Examples

The pocket mouse uses its cheek pouches to carry seeds back to its burrow.
Scientists observed the behavior of the pocket mouse during the night.
Several species of pocket mouse have adapted to arid environments in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - desert rodent with long hind legs, similar but larger

Etymology

Named for the cheek pouches that resemble pockets.
