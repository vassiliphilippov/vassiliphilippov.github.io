power unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a standard quantity used to measure electrical power, such as the watt.
measure of electric power

Examples

In most countries, the power unit for electricity is the watt.
To compare appliances, check how many power units they consume per hour.
The generator is rated at 5,000 power units for continuous operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watt - the SI unit of power, equal to one joule per second
