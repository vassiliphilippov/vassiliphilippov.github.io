deodar cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiː.oʊˈdɑːr ˈsiː.dɚ / (AmE), / ˌdiː.əʊˈdɑː ˈsiː.də / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to the Himalayas, valued for its graceful appearance and durable timber
Himalayan evergreen tree valued for timber

Examples

The deodar cedar is often planted in parks for its beautiful, sweeping branches.
Carpenters prize deodar cedar wood for its resistance to decay and insects.
A mature deodar cedar can grow to over 40 meters in height in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - any of several coniferous trees that yield fragrant, durable wood

Etymology

From Hindi 'devadār,' from Sanskrit 'devadāru' (timber of the gods).
