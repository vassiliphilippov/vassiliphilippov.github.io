thin-shelled mussel

1. NOUN

/ θɪn ʃɛld ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / θɪn ʃɛld ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
A mussel species characterized by thin, fragile shells with only rudimentary hinge teeth.
mussel with thin, fragile shells

Examples

The thin-shelled mussel is particularly vulnerable to predators due to its fragile shell.
Researchers collected several thin-shelled mussels from the estuary for examination.
Unlike its thick-shelled relatives, the thin-shelled mussel can be easily crushed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a type of edible bivalve mollusc with a shell
