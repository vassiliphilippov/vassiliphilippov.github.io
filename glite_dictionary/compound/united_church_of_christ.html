United Church of Christ

1. PROPN

/ juˈnaɪt̬ɪd tʃɝːtʃ əv kraɪst / (AmE), / juˈnaɪtɪd tʃɜːtʃ əv kraɪst / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination in the United States formed in 1957 by the merger of the Congregational Christian Churches and the Evangelical and Reformed Church.
major U.S. Protestant denomination

Examples

The United Church of Christ is known for its progressive stance on social issues.
He was ordained as a minister in the United Church of Christ in 1984.
Many congregations affiliated with the United Church of Christ are active in community outreach.
