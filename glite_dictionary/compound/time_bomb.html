time bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪm bɑm / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb equipped with a device that allows it to explode at a set time
bomb set to detonate at a certain time

Examples

Police found a time bomb hidden in the warehouse.
The discovery of a time bomb forced the evacuation of the building.
Setting a time bomb requires careful planning to ensure it detonates at the right moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - an explosive weapon designed to cause destruction
  • Synonym: landmine - an explosive device placed on the ground

2. NOUN

/ taɪm bɑm / (AmE), / taɪm bɒm / (BrE)
A situation or problem that is likely to become very serious or dangerous in the future, especially if not dealt with soon.
problem that will become dangerous

Examples

The lack of regular maintenance is a time bomb waiting to explode.
Unchecked inequality could turn the city into a social time bomb.
Environmental neglect is a time bomb for future generations.

Synset Examples

India is a demographic time bomb
the refugee camp is a ticking bomb waiting to go off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticking time bomb - a situation that is about to become very dangerous
  • Synonym: powder keg - a situation likely to become violent or dangerous

Synset Examples

Related Terms
