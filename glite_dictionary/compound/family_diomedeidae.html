family Diomedeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli daɪˌɑːməˈdiː.aɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli daɪˌɒmɪˈdiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large seabirds known as albatrosses, found mainly in the Southern Ocean and North Pacific.
biological family of albatrosses
medical

Examples

The family Diomedeidae comprises all species of albatross, some of the largest flying birds.
Researchers have observed that members of the family Diomedeidae can glide for hours without flapping their wings.
Conservationists are concerned about declining populations within the family Diomedeidae due to fishing practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albatross - large seabird of the family Diomedeidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Diomedeidae', based on the genus Diomedea (albatrosses).
