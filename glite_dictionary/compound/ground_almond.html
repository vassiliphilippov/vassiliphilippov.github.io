ground almond

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈɑlmənd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈɑːlmənd / (BrE)
a European sedge (Cyperus rotundus) with small edible, nutlike tubers
a sedge with edible tubers

Examples

The ancient Egyptians cultivated ground almond for its nutritious tubers.
You can eat the tubers of ground almond raw or roasted.
Foragers in southern Europe often collect ground almond from the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earth almond - another common name for Cyperus rotundus, the same tuberous sedge
