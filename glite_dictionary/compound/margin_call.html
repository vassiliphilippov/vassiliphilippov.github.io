margin call

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪn kɔl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn kɔːl / (BrE)
an official demand by a broker requiring an investor to deposit additional funds or securities to bring a margin account up to the minimum required level
demand for more funds to maintain a margin account

Examples

When the value of his stocks dropped, he received a margin call from his broker.
She had to sell part of her portfolio to meet a sudden margin call.
Investors may face a margin call if their account balance falls below the required threshold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: margin account - an account that allows investors to borrow funds from a broker
  • Degree Higher: liquidation - the process of closing positions when a margin call is unmet

Etymology

From 'margin' in finance (money on deposit with a broker) + 'call' (demand for payment).
