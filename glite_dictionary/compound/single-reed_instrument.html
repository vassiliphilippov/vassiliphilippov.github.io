single-reed instrument

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˌɹid ˌɪn.strəˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˌriːd ˌɪn.strəˈmɛnt / (BrE)
A musical instrument that produces sound by sending air past a single reed, such as a clarinet or saxophone.
musical instrument with one vibrating reed

Examples

The clarinet is a well-known single-reed instrument often featured in orchestras.
Many jazz musicians choose the saxophone, a popular single-reed instrument, for its expressive sound.
Unlike the oboe, which is a double-reed, the bass clarinet is classified as a single-reed instrument.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: double-reed instrument - A musical instrument that uses two reeds vibrating against each other, such as an oboe or bassoon
