directional mike

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkʃənəl maɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃənəl maɪk / (BrE)
A microphone designed to pick up sound primarily from a specific direction, minimizing noise from other sources.
microphone receiving sound from one direction

Examples

The interviewer used a directional mike to capture the speaker's voice clearly amid the crowd.
A directional mike helps to reduce background noise in outdoor recordings.
For the film shoot, the audio team selected a directional mike to isolate dialogue from ambient sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directional microphone - microphone designed to pick up sound from a specific direction
  • Antonym: omnidirectional microphone - microphone that picks up sound equally from all directions

Etymology

From 'directional' (having a specific direction) and 'mike' (informal for 'microphone').
