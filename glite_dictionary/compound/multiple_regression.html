multiple regression

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌl.tɪ.pəl rɪˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈmʌl.tɪ.pəl rɪˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
a statistical method used to estimate the relationship between one dependent variable and two or more independent variables
statistical method predicting one variable from several others

Examples

The researcher used multiple regression to analyze the impact of age, income, and education on health outcomes.
In statistics, multiple regression is often applied to determine how several factors affect a single result.
We learned how to conduct multiple regression analysis to predict housing prices based on various property features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression - statistical technique modeling relationships between variables

Etymology

From 'multiple' (many) + 'regression' (statistical relationship), used since early 20th century in statistics.
