scarlet oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑrlət oʊk / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its deeply lobed leaves that turn bright scarlet in autumn
North American tree with scarlet autumn leaves

Examples

The scarlet oak is easily recognized by its bright red foliage in the fall.
We planted a scarlet oak in the front yard for its vibrant autumn colors.
Many parks feature scarlet oaks because of their striking appearance in October.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red oak - a related oak species group with reddish wood and leaves
  • Degree Lower: pin oak - another oak species known for deeply lobed leaves, but smaller and with less dramatic autumn color
