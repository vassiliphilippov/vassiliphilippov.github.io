writ of mandamus

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪt əv mænˈdeɪməs / (AmE), / ˌrɪt ɒv mænˈdeɪməs / (BrE)
a court order requiring a government official to perform a duty that is mandated by law and not subject to their discretion, issued when all other remedies have been exhausted
court order compelling official action
legal

Examples

The judge issued a writ of mandamus compelling the city clerk to certify the election results.
The petitioner sought a writ of mandamus when the agency failed to fulfill its legal duty.
Only after exhausting all other remedies did the lawyer apply for a writ of mandamus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injunction - a court order requiring a person to do or cease doing a specific action

Etymology

From Latin mandāmus (we command), used historically in English legal procedure.
