grammar Nazism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmər ˈnɑːzɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə ˈnɑːzɪzəm / (BrE)
an excessive or rigid insistence on following grammar rules, often in a way that stifles creativity or communication
excessively strict adherence to grammar rules
informal offensive

Examples

Her grammar Nazism often discourages others from participating in group discussions.
Many writers worry that grammar Nazism can limit creative expression in their work.
Online forums sometimes suffer from grammar Nazism when minor errors are harshly criticized.

Synset Examples

His grammar Nazism makes discussions less enjoyable.
Grammar Nazism can stifle creativity in writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedantry - excessive concern with formal rules and trivial points of learning
  • Synonym: prescriptivism - belief in strict adherence to established language rules

Etymology

Based on the term 'Nazism', used metaphorically to describe extreme behavior; considered informal and potentially offensive.
