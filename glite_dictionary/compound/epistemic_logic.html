epistemic logic

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈstiːmɪk ˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈstiːmɪk ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
A branch of modal logic concerned with formalizing reasoning about knowledge, uncertainty, and ignorance.
branch of logic about knowledge and uncertainty

Examples

Epistemic logic is often used to analyze reasoning processes in artificial intelligence research.
Philosophers employ epistemic logic to discuss problems like the paradox of the knower.
Understanding epistemic logic requires familiarity with concepts from modal and propositional logic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modal logic - a type of logic dealing with necessity and possibility

Etymology

From Greek 'epistēmē' (knowledge) and English 'logic'.
