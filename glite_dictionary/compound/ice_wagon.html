ice wagon

1. NOUN

/ aɪs ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / aɪs ˈwaɡən / (BrE)
A horse-drawn wagon, formerly used to deliver blocks of ice door to door.
horse-drawn wagon for delivering ice

Examples

In the early 1900s, the ice wagon was a common sight on city streets during the summer.
Children would often run behind the ice wagon to collect small chips of ice on hot days.
Before electric refrigeration, families relied on the daily delivery of ice from the ice wagon.
