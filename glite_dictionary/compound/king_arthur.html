King Arthur

1. PROPN

/ kɪŋ ˈɑrθər / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˈɑːθə / (BrE)
a legendary British king, reputed to have led the Knights of the Round Table at Camelot and a central figure in Arthurian legends
legendary king of Britain in Arthurian legends

Examples

Stories about King Arthur and his Knights of the Round Table have fascinated people for centuries.
Some historians debate whether King Arthur was based on a real person from the 6th century.
The legend of King Arthur has inspired countless books, films, and works of art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Camelot - the legendary castle and court of King Arthur

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Arthurus, legendary king of the Britons, popularized by Geoffrey of Monmouth's 12th-century work.
