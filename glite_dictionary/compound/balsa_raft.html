balsa raft

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔl.sə ræft / (AmE), / ˈbælsə rɑːft / (BrE)
A light raft constructed from balsa wood, typically used for transport or exploration on water.
raft made from balsa wood

Examples

The explorers crossed the ocean on a balsa raft to prove their theory about ancient travel.
Building a balsa raft requires lightweight wood and careful construction to ensure buoyancy.
The museum displayed a famous balsa raft used in a historic expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raft - a flat structure for support or transportation over water
