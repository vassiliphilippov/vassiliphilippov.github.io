Culver's root

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlvərz ˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈkʌlvəz ˌruːt / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb of eastern North America, having spikes of small white or purple flowers; also known as Veronicastrum virginicum.
North American perennial herb with flower spikes

Examples

In the wildflower garden, Culver's root adds vertical interest with its tall flower spikes.
Early settlers used Culver's root for its purported medicinal properties.
You can find Culver's root blooming in moist meadows during the summer months.

Etymology

Named after Dr. Culver, an American physician of the 18th century, combined with 'root' for its medicinal use.

a tall perennial herb native to eastern North America, with spikes of small white or purple flowers, scientifically known as Veronicastrum virginicum
tall North American perennial herb

Examples

Gardeners often plant Culver's root for its tall, elegant flower spikes and medicinal reputation.
Native Americans used Culver's root in herbal remedies centuries before it became known in Western medicine.
In midsummer, Culver's root blooms attract bees and butterflies to woodland gardens.

Etymology

Named after an 18th-century American physician, Dr. Culver, who promoted its medicinal use.
