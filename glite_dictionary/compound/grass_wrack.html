grass wrack

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs ˈræk / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs ˈræk / (BrE)
A submerged marine plant with very long, narrow leaves, found in abundance along North Atlantic coasts.
marine plant with long narrow leaves

Examples

The coastal marsh was filled with dense beds of grass wrack swaying gently underwater.
Biologists study grass wrack habitats to monitor the health of marine ecosystems.
After the storm, piles of grass wrack washed up along the sandy shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelgrass - a submerged marine plant with long ribbon-like leaves
