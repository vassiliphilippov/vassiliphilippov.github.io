hawk's-beard

1. NOUN

/ hɔksˌbɪrd / (AmE), / hɔːksˌbɪəd / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Crepis, with loose clusters of yellow flowers on long, branched leafy stems, found in the Northern Hemisphere
plant of the genus Crepis

Examples

The field was dotted with bright yellow hawk's-beard blooming in the summer sun.
She studied the unique structure of the hawk's-beard for her botany class project.
Gardeners sometimes mistake hawk's-beard for dandelion because of its similar yellow flowers.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the plant's bristly seed head to the beard of a hawk; 'hawk' from Old English 'hafoc' and 'beard' from Old English 'beard'.
