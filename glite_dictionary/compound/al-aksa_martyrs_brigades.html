al-Aksa Martyrs Brigades

1. PROPN

/ æl ˈæksə ˈmɑrtɚz brɪˈgeɪdz / (AmE), / æl ˈæksə ˈmɑːtəz brɪˈgeɪdz / (BrE)
a militant Palestinian organization and armed faction, originally an offshoot of al-Fatah, active primarily in the West Bank and known for carrying out attacks against Israel, especially in the early 2000s.
militant Palestinian faction active in the West Bank

Examples

The al-Aksa Martyrs Brigades claimed responsibility for the attack in the city center.
Members of the al-Aksa Martyrs Brigades were arrested by security forces in 2003.
International observers have often condemned the violent tactics of the al-Aksa Martyrs Brigades during the conflict.

Etymology

Named after the al-Aqsa Mosque in Jerusalem; 'Martyrs Brigades' refers to those who die for a cause.
