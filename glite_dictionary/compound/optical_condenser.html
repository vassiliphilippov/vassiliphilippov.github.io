optical condenser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a lens or system of lenses used to concentrate light onto an object, especially in optical instruments such as microscopes
lens or system for focusing light

Examples

The optical condenser was adjusted to provide optimal illumination for the slide.
Make sure the optical condenser is clean before starting your microscopy work.
A high-quality optical condenser improves image clarity under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condenser - a device that concentrates or condenses something (e.g. light, vapor)

Etymology

From 'optical' (relating to sight or vision) and 'condenser' (device for concentrating).
