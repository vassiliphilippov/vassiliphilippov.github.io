genus Loranthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs loʊˈrænθəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ləˈranθəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Loranthaceae family, containing a single species of parasitic plants known as Loranthus.
type genus of the Loranthaceae family

Examples

The genus Loranthus is well-known for its parasitic habits on trees in tropical regions.
Botanists often study the genus Loranthus to understand plant parasitism.
This specimen belongs to the genus Loranthus, which is common in some Asian forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loranthus - a genus of parasitic plants in the Loranthaceae family

Etymology

From Latin 'loranthus', from Greek 'loron' (strap) + 'anthos' (flower).
