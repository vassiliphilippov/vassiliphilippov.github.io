bell founder

1. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈfaʊndɚ / (AmE), / bɛl ˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is casting or making metal bells, typically by pouring molten metal into a mold
person who casts metal bells

Examples

The bell founder used ancient techniques to create a new church bell.
Historically, bell founders were highly respected for their craftsmanship.
The old city records mention a renowned bell founder from the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: founder - a person who manufactures articles by casting metal

Etymology

From 'bell' (metal musical instrument) + 'founder' (person who casts metal objects), from Middle English 'foundour', from Old French 'fondeur'.
