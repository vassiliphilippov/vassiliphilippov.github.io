Johnson grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑnsən ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒnsən ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall perennial grass (Sorghum halepense) that spreads by creeping rhizomes; grown for fodder but considered a noxious weed in some regions, especially the southern United States
a weedy grass species, often a pest

Examples

Large patches of Johnson grass have invaded the soybean fields this year.
Farmers often struggle to control Johnson grass because of its aggressive growth.
Livestock sometimes feed on Johnson grass, but it can become toxic under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sorghum - a genus of grasses including Johnson grass
  • Synonym: weed - an unwanted plant, often invasive

Etymology

Named after Colonel William Johnson, who introduced it to the United States in the 19th century.
