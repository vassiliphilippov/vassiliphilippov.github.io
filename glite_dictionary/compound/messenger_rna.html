messenger RNA

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsəndʒər ɑr ɛn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmesɪndʒə ɑːr en ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a type of RNA molecule that carries genetic information from DNA in the cell nucleus to the ribosomes, where proteins are made
RNA molecule that carries genetic information for protein synthesis
medical

Examples

Researchers studied how messenger RNA delivers genetic instructions from the nucleus to the ribosomes.
The COVID-19 vaccines utilize messenger RNA to teach cells how to make a protein that triggers an immune response.
She explained that without messenger RNA, the process of synthesizing proteins in cells would not occur.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: transfer RNA - RNA molecule that helps decode mRNA into protein
  • Degree Higher: ribosomal RNA - RNA component of ribosomes, essential for protein synthesis

Etymology

Formed from 'messenger' (carrier of a message) + the initialism RNA (ribonucleic acid).
