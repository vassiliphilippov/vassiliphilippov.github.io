shellac varnish

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈlæk ˈvɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ʃəˈlæk ˈvɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
a transparent or colored thin varnish made by dissolving shellac in alcohol, used for finishing wood and other surfaces
varnish made from shellac for wood finishing

Examples

The antique dresser was finished with a shellac varnish to highlight its natural wood grain.
After sanding the table, she applied a coat of shellac varnish for a glossy, protective finish.
Artists sometimes use shellac varnish to seal their paintings and add shine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varnish - a liquid that is spread on surfaces to form a hard, shiny, transparent coating

Etymology

From 'shellac' (from French 'laque', from Persian 'lak', meaning resin) plus 'varnish' (from Medieval Latin 'vernix').
