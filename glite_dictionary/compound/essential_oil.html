essential oil

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈsɛnʃəl ɔɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃl ɔɪl / (BrE)
An aromatic oil obtained from a plant, containing its characteristic fragrance or flavor, used in perfumes, flavorings, and aromatherapy.
aromatic oil from plants for fragrance or flavor

Examples

Lavender essential oil is popular for its calming scent and therapeutic uses.
Some people add a few drops of essential oil to a diffuser to freshen up a room.
She bought peppermint essential oil to use in homemade soap and lotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Synonym: perfume - a fragrant liquid typically made from essential oils
  • Antonym: carrier oil - a neutral oil used to dilute essential oils for application

Etymology

From 'essential', meaning 'containing the essence', and 'oil'.
