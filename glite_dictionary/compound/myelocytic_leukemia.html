myelocytic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˌloʊˈsɪtɪk luːˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪələˌsɪtɪk ljuːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A type of cancer affecting the blood-forming tissues, characterized by an abnormal increase of myelocytes in the blood.
type of blood cancer with excess myelocytes
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with myelocytic leukemia after a series of blood tests revealed abnormal white cell counts.
Treatment options for myelocytic leukemia have improved significantly in recent decades.
Researchers are studying new therapies to help patients with myelocytic leukemia achieve remission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - general term for cancers of the blood-forming tissues

Etymology

From Greek 'myelos' (marrow) and 'kytos' (cell), combined with 'leukemia' from 'leukos' (white) and 'haima' (blood).
