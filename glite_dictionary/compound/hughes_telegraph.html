Hughes telegraph

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːz ˈtɛləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhjuːz ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an early form of telegraph that printed text on a paper tape, invented by David Edward Hughes in the 19th century
early telegraph printing text, invented by Hughes

Examples

The Hughes telegraph revolutionized communication by printing text directly onto paper tape.
Historians often study the Hughes telegraph to understand advances in 19th-century telecommunications.
A working model of the Hughes telegraph is displayed in the museum's technology section.

Etymology

Named after David Edward Hughes, its inventor.
