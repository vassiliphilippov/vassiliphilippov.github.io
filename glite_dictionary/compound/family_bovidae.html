family Bovidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli boʊˈvɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bəʊˈvɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of hoofed mammals including cattle, sheep, goats, and true antelopes.
family of hoofed mammals (cattle, sheep, goats, antelopes)

Examples

Cattle, sheep, and goats are all members of the family Bovidae.
Unlike deer, species in the family Bovidae do not shed their horns annually.
The family Bovidae is one of the most diverse families of large mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bovid - any member of the family Bovidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Bovidae', from Latin 'bos' (ox, cow) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
