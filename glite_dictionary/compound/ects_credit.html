ECTS credit

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː siː tiː ˈɛs ˌkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌiː siː tiː ˈɛs ˌkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
A unit used in European higher education to measure student workload, awarded upon completion of a course, module, or program.
unit of student workload in European higher education

Examples

Each course at the university is worth six ECTS credits toward graduation.
To complete the master's program, students must earn at least 90 ECTS credits.
She transferred her ECTS credits from a German university to continue her degree in France.

Synset Examples

One academic year corresponds to 60 ECTS credits that are equivalent to 1500-1800 hours of study in all countries respective of standard or qualification type and is used to facilitate transfer and progression throughout the Union.

Etymology

Abbreviation of European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System, coined by the European Union.
