bilevel railcar

1. NOUN

/ baɪˌlɛvəl ˈreɪlˌkɑr / (AmE), / baɪˌlɛvəl ˈreɪlˌkɑː / (BrE)
A rail car with two levels of passenger accommodation, designed to increase passenger capacity compared to single-level cars.
rail car with two passenger levels

Examples

The new train features a bilevel railcar design, allowing more passengers to be seated comfortably during rush hour.
Many commuter lines have switched to bilevel railcars to handle increased ridership without adding extra trains.
From the platform, you could see the distinctive tall profile of the bilevel railcar as it pulled into the station.

Synset Examples

When double-deck carriages (bilevel cars) were introduced on North American railways (railroads) they identified a problem in that it would be physically difficult - if not impossible - for the conductor to verify, collect payment and sell tickets to such a large concentration of passengers in one car, especially in suburban passenger service, owing to the short distance between stops.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-level railcar - a railcar with only one level of passenger accommodation

Etymology

Formed from 'bi-' (meaning two) and 'level', combined with 'railcar'.
