conductance unit

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌktəns ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktəns ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a standard unit used to measure a material's ability to conduct an electric current
unit measuring electrical conductance

Examples

The electrical engineer measured the wire's efficiency in conductance units for accuracy.
Laboratory reports frequently express results using conductance units such as the siemens.
Conversion between resistance units and conductance units is essential in circuit analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siemens - the SI unit of electrical conductance
