jap clover

1. NOUN

/ dʒæp ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / dʒap ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
An annual herb native to tropical Asia, naturalized in the United States, commonly used as forage or ground cover.
annual herb of tropical Asia, forage plant

Examples

The field was covered in jap clover after the summer rains.
Farmers often plant jap clover to improve soil quality.
Jap clover thrives in warm, humid climates and requires little maintenance.
