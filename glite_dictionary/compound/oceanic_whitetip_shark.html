oceanic whitetip shark

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌʃænɪk ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑrk / (AmE), / əʊˌʃænɪk ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large deep-water shark (Carcharhinus longimanus) with a white-tipped dorsal fin, found in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide, known for being one of the most dangerous sharks to humans
large predatory shark with white-tipped dorsal fin

Examples

The oceanic whitetip shark is often seen cruising at the surface of open oceans.
Due to overfishing, populations of oceanic whitetip sharks have declined significantly in recent decades.
Marine biologists study the behavior of the oceanic whitetip shark to better understand its role in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large predatory fish of the order Selachii
