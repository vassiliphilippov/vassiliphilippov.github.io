Black Death

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈdɛθ / (AmE), / blæk ˈdɛθ / (BrE)
the epidemic of bubonic plague that spread through Europe in the mid-14th century, killing millions of people
devastating bubonic plague epidemic (14th century)

Examples

The Black Death radically changed the course of European history in the 14th century.
Many villages were abandoned as the Black Death swept through the countryside.
Historians still study the impact of the Black Death on medieval society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubonic plague - a serious infectious disease caused by Yersinia pestis, affecting lymph nodes
