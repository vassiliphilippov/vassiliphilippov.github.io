computational linguist

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpjutˈeɪʃənl lɪŋˈɡwɪst / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpjʊˈteɪʃənl lɪŋˈɡwɪst / (BrE)
a person skilled in both computer science and linguistics who applies computational methods to the analysis and processing of natural language
expert in computer-based language analysis

Examples

As a computational linguist, she develops algorithms to help computers understand human language.
The hiring committee is looking for a computational linguist with experience in natural language processing.
Many language apps rely on the expertise of a computational linguist to improve their accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computational linguistics - the field concerned with using computers to analyze and process language

Etymology

From 'computational', relating to computation, and 'linguist', from Latin 'lingua' (tongue, language).
