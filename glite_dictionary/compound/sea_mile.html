sea mile

1. NOUN

/ siː maɪl / (AmE), / siː maɪl / (BrE)
a former British unit of length used in navigation, equal to 6,000 feet (1,828.8 meters)
former British unit of length (6,000 feet)
archaic british

Examples

Before the adoption of the international nautical mile, distances at sea were measured in sea miles.
The old British charts listed distances using the sea mile rather than the nautical mile.
A sea mile was shorter than today's standard nautical mile, causing potential confusion in historical records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautical mile - unit of length used in navigation (1,852 meters)
