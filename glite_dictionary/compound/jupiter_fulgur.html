Jupiter Fulgur

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtər ˈfʌlɡər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə ˈfʌlɡə / (BrE)
An epithet of Jupiter in Roman mythology, signifying his aspect as the bringer of lightning.
epithet of Jupiter as bringer of lightning

Examples

The temple was dedicated to Jupiter Fulgur in hopes of protection from storms.
Ancient texts often invoke Jupiter Fulgur when describing dramatic thunderstorms.
In Roman religion, offerings were given to Jupiter Fulgur after lightning struck sacred sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - The chief god of Roman mythology, equivalent to Zeus in Greek mythology.

Etymology

Latin, from 'Fulgur' meaning 'lightning.'
