last gasp

1. NOUN

/ læst gæsp / (AmE), / lɑːst ɡɑːsp / (BrE)
the final moment of existence or activity before death, exhaustion, or completion; a final effort or occurrence before coming to an end.
final stage before ending or death

Examples

The company made a last gasp attempt to avoid bankruptcy by launching a new product.
His victory was the last gasp of a once-dominant athlete facing retirement.
The negotiations represented the last gasp of the failing peace process.

Synset Examples

the last gasp of the cold war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finale - the last part of something, especially a performance or event
  • Synonym: end - the final part or finish of something

2. NOUN

/ læst ɡæsp / (AmE), / lɑːst ɡɑːsp / (BrE)
a final attempt or effort made at the point of imminent failure or defeat
final effort before failure

Examples

The team mounted a last gasp attack in the closing minutes of the match.
In a last gasp effort, the candidate appealed directly to undecided voters.
Despite a last gasp negotiation, the deal ultimately fell through.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last-ditch - a final, desperate attempt or effort
