nuclear explosion

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliər ɪkˈsploʊʒən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːklɪə ɪkˈspləʊʒən / (BrE)
an extremely powerful explosion caused by the sudden release of energy from a nuclear reaction, typically from an atomic or hydrogen bomb
explosion from a nuclear reaction

Examples

The city was devastated by the nuclear explosion that occurred in the early morning hours.
Scientists have studied the long-term effects of a nuclear explosion on the environment.
Fears of a nuclear explosion led to strict international arms control agreements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic explosion - an explosion resulting from the release of nuclear energy, especially from an atomic bomb
