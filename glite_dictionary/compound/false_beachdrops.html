false beachdrops

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈbitʃˌdrɑps / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈbiːtʃdrɒps / (BrE)
A fleshy, tawny or reddish saprophytic herb resembling the Indian pipe, found in woodland humus of eastern North America; sometimes placed in the separate genus Hypopitys.
a saprophytic woodland herb of North America

Examples

We spotted a cluster of false beachdrops growing among the leaf litter in the forest.
The botanist explained how false beachdrops differ from the closely related Indian pipe.
Because false beachdrops lack chlorophyll, they rely on decaying matter for their nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian pipe - a similar-looking saprophytic herb also found in woodlands
