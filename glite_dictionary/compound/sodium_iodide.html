sodium iodide

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈaɪədaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈʌɪədaɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline salt (NaI) used in medicine and chemical analysis, similar in use to potassium iodide
a white crystalline salt (NaI), used in medicine and chemistry

Examples

The laboratory technician prepared a solution of sodium iodide for the chemical test.
Doctors sometimes use sodium iodide as a source of iodine in medical treatments.
Crystals of sodium iodide were dissolved in water to produce the reagent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium iodide - a white crystalline salt (KI) used similarly in medicine and analysis
