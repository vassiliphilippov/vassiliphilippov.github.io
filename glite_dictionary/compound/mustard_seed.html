mustard seed

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstɚd siːd / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd siːd / (BrE)
A small seed, usually black or white, from the mustard plant, used whole or ground to make mustard condiments or powders.
seed used for making mustard

Examples

Chefs often grind mustard seed to prepare their own spicy condiments.
A single mustard seed can add a sharp flavor to pickled vegetables.
She scattered mustard seed on top of the salad for extra crunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard - condiment made from ground mustard seeds

Etymology

From Middle English mustarde (mustard), from Old French mostarde, from Latin mustum (new wine), because mustard was originally prepared with grape must; seed referring to the botanical origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstɚd siːd / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd siːd / (BrE)
A very small seed often used as a metaphor for a small amount of faith or an idea that has the potential to grow significantly.
tiny seed; symbol for faith or potential

Examples

The idea began as a mustard seed but soon blossomed into a successful business.
Even a mustard seed of hope can lead to great changes in your life.
She held onto her dreams, no matter how much like a mustard seed they seemed.

Synset Examples

The idea started as a mustard seed in his mind.
With faith as small as a mustard seed, anything is possible.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seed - the grain or ripened ovule of a flowering plant used for sowing
  • Degree Higher: tree - a large plant with a trunk and branches

Etymology

From the use of the mustard plant's seed, noted in biblical parables for its small size yet potential for great growth.
