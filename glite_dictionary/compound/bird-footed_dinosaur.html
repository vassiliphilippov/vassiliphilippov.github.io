bird-footed dinosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːd ˌfʊtɪd ˈdaɪnəˌsɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːd ˌfʊtɪd ˈdaɪnəˌsɔː / (BrE)
any of various carnivorous dinosaurs from the Triassic to Cretaceous periods, characterized by short forelimbs and strong hind legs used for walking or running
carnivorous dinosaurs with bird-like feet

Examples

The museum's new exhibit features a reconstructed skeleton of a bird-footed dinosaur from the Jurassic period.
Researchers believe that many bird-footed dinosaurs were swift predators due to their powerful hind legs.
A bird-footed dinosaur left fossilized footprints that provide clues about its speed and behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theropod - member of a group of bipedal saurischian dinosaurs, including most carnivorous species
