nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliəˌsaɪd rɪˈvɜrs trænˈskrɪptəˌs eɪt ɪnˈhɪbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːklɪəˌsaɪd rɪˈvɜːs trænˈskrɪptəˌeɪt ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a class of antiviral drugs used to treat HIV infection; these drugs are incorporated into viral DNA and block the reverse transcriptase enzyme, preventing the virus from replicating
antiviral drugs that block HIV replication
medical

Examples

Treatment for HIV often includes a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor as part of the drug regimen.
The doctor explained how a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor works to block the HIV virus from multiplying.
Researchers are developing new nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors with improved resistance profiles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor - another class of reverse transcriptase inhibitors acting by a different mechanism

Etymology

Formed from 'nucleoside', 'reverse transcriptase', and 'inhibitor', highlighting its action in blocking the enzyme reverse transcriptase.
