Black September Movement

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsɛptəmbɚ ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / blæk ˈsɛptəmbə ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant organization responsible for high-profile attacks in the early 1970s, including the murder of 11 Israeli athletes at the 1972 Munich Olympics.
Palestinian militant group active in the 1970s

Examples

The Black September Movement was responsible for the attack on Israeli athletes at the Munich Olympics.
After merging with the Fatah Revolutionary Council, the influence of the Black September Movement diminished.
The assassination of Jordan's Prime Minister was carried out by members of the Black September Movement.

Etymology

Named after the events of September 1970, known as Black September in Jordan, when the Jordanian government expelled Palestinian groups.
