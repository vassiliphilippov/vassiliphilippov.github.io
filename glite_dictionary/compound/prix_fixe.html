prix fixe

1. NOUN

/ pri ˈfiks / (AmE), / pri ˈfiːks / (BrE)
a meal offered in a restaurant at a fixed total price, usually with several courses and limited selection for each course
fixed-price multi-course meal

Examples

The restaurant offers a prix fixe dinner, which includes an appetizer, entrée, and dessert.
We enjoyed a delicious prix fixe lunch during Restaurant Week downtown.
Many French bistros feature a prix fixe menu for special occasions.

Etymology

From French 'prix fixe' (fixed price).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06509224-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ priː ˈfɪks / (AmE), / priː ˈfiːks / (BrE)
(of a meal, especially in a restaurant) offered as a complete meal with limited choices at a fixed price.
fixed-price complete meal

Examples

Many upscale restaurants offer a prix fixe menu during special events.
The prix fixe dinner included an appetizer, main course, and dessert.
She opted for the prix fixe option, knowing it was a better deal than ordering separately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: à la carte - meals priced and ordered separately, not as a set menu

Etymology

From French 'prix fixe', meaning 'fixed price'.

Synset POS: a
