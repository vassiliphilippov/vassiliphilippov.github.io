nim tree

1. NOUN

/ nɪm tri / (AmE), / nɪm triː / (BrE)
a large semi-evergreen tree native to the East Indies, with bitter bark used as a tonic and seeds yielding aromatic oil
semi-evergreen tree; source of medicinal bark and oil

Examples

The leaves and bark of the nim tree have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
Farmers often plant nim trees around their fields to act as a natural pesticide.
An oil extracted from nim tree seeds is used in various herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neem tree - a tree known for its medicinal properties, same species as nim tree
