east-west direction

1. NOUN

/ ist wɛst dəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / iːst wɛst dɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
a direction that runs parallel to lines of latitude, from east to west or vice versa
direction parallel to lines of latitude

Examples

The railway runs in an east-west direction across the entire state.
Wind patterns in this region typically move in an east-west direction.
The main street was constructed to follow an exact east-west direction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: north-south direction - direction parallel to lines of longitude (from north to south or vice versa)
