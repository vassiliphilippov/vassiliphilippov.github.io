wet nurse

1. NOUN

/ wɛt nɜrs / (AmE), / wɛt nɜːs / (BrE)
a woman employed to breastfeed and care for another person's child
woman hired to breastfeed another's child

Examples

In Victorian times, wealthy families often employed a wet nurse for their newborns.
She worked as a wet nurse after losing her own child.
The royal child was raised by a devoted wet nurse until he was three years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nursemaid - woman employed to care for children

Etymology

From 'wet' (with milk) + 'nurse'; used since Middle English to denote a breastfeeding nurse.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛt nɜrs / (AmE), / ˈwɛt nɜːs / (BrE)
a woman who breastfeeds and cares for another person's child, typically because the child's own mother is unable or unwilling to do so
woman who breastfeeds another's child

Examples

In the royal household, a wet nurse was often employed to care for infants.
She hired a wet nurse after complications prevented her from breastfeeding.
The role of a wet nurse was once common in many cultures around the world.

Synset Examples

The wet nurse cared for the newborn lovingly.
In the past, a wet nurse was common for wealthy families.
She played the role of a wet nurse for her sister's child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nursemaid - a woman employed to look after children

Etymology

From 'wet' (with milk) + 'nurse', used since the 16th century in English.
