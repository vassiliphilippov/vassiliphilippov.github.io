mansard roof

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.sɑrd ruːf / (AmE), / ˈman.sɑːd ruːf / (BrE)
A roof with two slopes on each of its four sides, the lower slope being steeper than the upper.
roof with two slopes on each side

Examples

The house was easily recognizable by its distinctive mansard roof and ornate dormer windows.
Adding a mansard roof provided extra living space in the attic of the old building.
Many Parisian apartments are famous for their elegant mansard roofs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hip roof - a roof with all sides sloping down toward the walls

Etymology

Named after the French architect François Mansart (1598–1666).
