saddle tramp

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædəl træmp / (AmE), / ˈsædəl træmp / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place, especially a cowboy or drifter who wanders, often on horseback, without a permanent home or steady employment
wandering cowboy or drifter

Examples

He spent years as a saddle tramp, never staying long in any one town.
The legend tells of a mysterious saddle tramp who helped strangers across the prairie.
Stories of saddle tramps are common in American folk literature and cowboy songs.

Synset Examples

Being a saddle tramp suits his adventurous spirit.
The old cowboy was a true saddle tramp at heart.
He lived as a saddle tramp, wandering from town to town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drifter - a person who moves from place to place without steady employment
  • Synonym: nomad - a member of a community without fixed habitation

Etymology

From 'saddle' (seat for a rider) and 'tramp' (wanderer or vagrant); common in American West slang.
