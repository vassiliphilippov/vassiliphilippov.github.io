Acorus calamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈækərəs ˈkæləməs / (AmE), / ˈækərəs ˈkæləməs / (BrE)
A perennial marsh plant with swordlike leaves and aromatic roots, commonly known as sweet flag.
marsh plant with aromatic roots

Examples

The wetlands are home to Acorus calamus, valued for its fragrant roots and medicinal uses.
Researchers study extracts from Acorus calamus for their potential health benefits.
In traditional herbal medicine, Acorus calamus is often used as a remedy for digestive issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet flag - another common name for Acorus calamus

Etymology

From Latin 'acorus', from Greek 'ákoros' (a plant with aromatic roots), and Latin 'calamus' (reed).
