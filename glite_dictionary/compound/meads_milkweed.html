Mead's milkweed

1. NOUN

/ miz ˈmɪlkwid / (AmE), / miːdz ˈmɪlkwiːd / (BrE)
A rare, perennial milkweed species (Asclepias meadii) native to central North America, now considered threatened.
rare North American milkweed species

Examples

Conservationists are working to protect Mead's milkweed from extinction in its native habitats.
Prairie restoration often includes planting Mead's milkweed to support native pollinators.
The sighting of Mead's milkweed indicated a healthy remnant prairie ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - any plant of the genus Asclepias, known for milky sap

Etymology

Named after Samuel Barnum Mead, American botanist.
