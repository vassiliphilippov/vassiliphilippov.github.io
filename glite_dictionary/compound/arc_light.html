arc light

1. NOUN

/ ɑrk laɪt / (AmE), / ɑːk laɪt / (BrE)
a lamp that produces light by an electric arc between two electrodes, typically used in early lighting and film projectors.
lamp producing light by electric arc

Examples

The theater was illuminated by an old arc light hanging from the ceiling.
In early cinema, filmmakers often relied on the intense brightness of arc lights.
The technician replaced the carbon rods inside the arc light to maintain its performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arc lamp - a lamp in which light is produced by an electric arc

Etymology

Derived from 'arc' (referring to the luminous electrical discharge between two points) + 'light'.
