Portland cement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔr(t)lənd səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtlənd sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
A type of cement that hardens under water, made by heating limestone and clay together and then grinding the result into a fine powder.
main type of construction cement

Examples

Builders often use Portland cement as the primary ingredient in concrete mixtures.
The durability of Portland cement makes it suitable for construction projects exposed to water.
The manufacturing process of Portland cement involves heating limestone and clay at high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cement - a binding material used in construction

Etymology

Named after Portland, England, due to the similarity of its color to Portland stone.
