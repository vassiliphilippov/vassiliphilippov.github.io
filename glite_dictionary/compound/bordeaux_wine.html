Bordeaux wine

1. NOUN

/ bɔrˈdoʊ waɪn / (AmE), / bɔːˈdəʊ waɪn / (BrE)
Any of several red or white wines produced in the Bordeaux region of France, or wines made in a similar style elsewhere.
wine from Bordeaux region, France

Examples

He ordered a bottle of Bordeaux wine to accompany their dinner.
The aroma of the Bordeaux wine was rich and complex, hinting at dark berries and oak.
Many collectors consider vintage Bordeaux wines to be among the finest in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claret - British term for red Bordeaux wine

Etymology

From French Bordeaux (the city and region) + English wine.
