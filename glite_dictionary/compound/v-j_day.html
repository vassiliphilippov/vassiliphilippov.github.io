V-J Day

1. NOUN

/ vi dʒeɪ deɪ / (AmE), / viː dʒeɪ deɪ / (BrE)
the day marking the Allied victory over Japan in World War II, celebrated on August 15 or September 2, 1945
Allied victory over Japan, WWII

Examples

Every year, veterans gather to commemorate V-J Day and remember those who served in the Pacific.
The formal surrender ceremony marking V-J Day took place on the deck of the USS Missouri.
Many people celebrated V-J Day with parades and street parties at the end of World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: V-E Day - the day marking the Allied victory in Europe in World War II

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Victory over Japan Day.'
