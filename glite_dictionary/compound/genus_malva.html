genus Malva

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmæl.və / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmæl.və / (BrE)
A group of flowering plants in the mallow family (Malvaceae), which includes various species of mallows found in temperate regions.
group of plants in the mallow family
medical

Examples

The genus Malva includes plants commonly known as mallows, which are often cultivated for their colorful flowers.
Several species within the genus Malva have medicinal uses dating back centuries.
Gardeners appreciate the resilience of the genus Malva in a variety of soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - a common name for plants in the genus Malva

Etymology

From Latin 'malva' (mallow), the type genus of the mallow family.
