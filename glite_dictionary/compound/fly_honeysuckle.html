fly honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈhʌnɪˌsʌkl / (BrE)
A deciduous North American shrub with upright stems and yellow-white flowers, belonging to the honeysuckle family.
deciduous North American shrub with yellow-white flowers

Examples

The fly honeysuckle blooms in late spring, attracting bees and other pollinators to its yellow-white flowers.
With proper pruning, the fly honeysuckle can form a dense hedge along garden borders.
Botanists studied the fly honeysuckle to learn more about native North American plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a genus of shrubs with sweet-smelling flowers
