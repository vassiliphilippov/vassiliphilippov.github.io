headlong into

1. ADV

/ ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlɒŋ ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
rushing into something eagerly and without considering the risks or consequences
rushing eagerly and recklessly into

Examples

He jumped headlong into the debate without knowing all the facts.
The company dived headlong into new markets, hoping to outpace its competitors.
She often throws herself headlong into projects, regardless of the possible difficulties.

Synset Examples

She ran headlong into the crowd to catch her friend.
They fell headlong into the adventure of a lifetime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessly - without thinking about the risks or consequences

Etymology

From 'headlong' meaning 'with the head foremost,' combined with preposition 'into.'
