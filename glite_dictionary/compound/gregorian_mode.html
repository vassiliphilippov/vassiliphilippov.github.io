Gregorian mode

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈɡɔriən moʊd / (AmE), / ɡrəˈɡɔːriən məʊd / (BrE)
any of the system of eight musical modes used in Gregorian chant, derived from earlier Greek modes and used until about 1600
musical mode used in Gregorian chant

Examples

Each Gregorian mode gives a distinct atmosphere to the chant.
The composer studied the structure of every Gregorian mode for his liturgical piece.
Musicologists debate how the Gregorian mode evolved from earlier Greek traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek mode - any of the modes in ancient Greek music theory
  • Degree Higher: Gregorian chant - plainsong used in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Gregorian, after Pope Gregory I, with influence from Greek musical theory.
