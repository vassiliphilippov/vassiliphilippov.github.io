Vogul language

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊɡəl ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvəʊɡʊl ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Ugric language, related to Hungarian, traditionally spoken by the Vogul (Mansi) people of western Siberia.
Ugric language of the Vogul (Mansi) people

Examples

The Vogul language is one of the few surviving Ugric languages besides Hungarian.
Efforts are being made to preserve the endangered Vogul language among minority communities.
She wrote her thesis on the phonetic structure of the Vogul language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mansi language - another name for the Vogul language, Ugric language of western Siberia
  • Synonym: Hungarian - Ugric language of Hungary, related to Vogul language

Etymology

'Vogul' from Russian 'Vogul,' referring to the Mansi people; language itself is of Ugric origin related to Hungarian.
