cattle drive

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬l̩ draɪv / (AmE), / ˈkætl draɪv / (BrE)
the process of moving a large herd of cattle, especially on horseback, from one place to another, often over long distances
movement of a herd of cattle over distance

Examples

The annual cattle drive attracted tourists eager to witness ranching traditions.
After days on the trail, the cattle drive finally reached the railhead.
Participating in a cattle drive was a challenging but unforgettable experience for the new ranch hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundup - the act of gathering together cattle or other domestic animals
