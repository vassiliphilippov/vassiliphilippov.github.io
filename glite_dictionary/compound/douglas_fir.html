Douglas fir

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌɡləs fɜr / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs fɜː / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to western North America, valued for its strong, resinous timber and short needles
tall North American evergreen tree

Examples

The lumber from the Douglas fir is prized for its strength and durability.
A mature Douglas fir can reach heights of over 70 meters in its natural habitat.
Many Christmas trees are varieties of Douglas fir due to their symmetrical shape and pleasant fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - a type of evergreen coniferous tree with needle-like leaves and cones

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist David Douglas (1799–1834), who introduced it from North America to Europe.
