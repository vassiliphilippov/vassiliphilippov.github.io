bear's breeches

1. NOUN

/ bɛrz ˈbriːtʃɪz / (AmE), / bɛəz ˈbriːtʃɪz / (BrE)
A southern European perennial plant (Acanthus mollis) with large, lobed leaves and tall spikes of whitish, purple-veined flowers, widely cultivated as an ornamental.
ornamental plant (Acanthus mollis) with white-purple flowers

Examples

We planted bear's breeches along the path for their striking flower spikes.
The leaves of bear's breeches inspired many classical architectural designs.
In early summer, bear's breeches produces tall, dramatic blooms that attract bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acanthus - a genus of herbaceous perennial plants, including bear's breeches

Etymology

The common name refers to the large, bristly leaves that resemble a bear's leg coverings ('breeches').
