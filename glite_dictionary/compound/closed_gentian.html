closed gentian

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A plant of eastern North America with tubular blue or white flowers that remain mostly closed.
North American plant with closed tubular flowers

Examples

The closed gentian blooms in late summer along the edges of wetlands and meadows.
Many pollinators rely on the closed gentian as a late-season nectar source.
Her field guide helped her identify the striking blue flowers as closed gentian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - a plant of the genus Gentiana, often with blue flowers

2. NOUN

/ kloʊzd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A North American wildflower of the gentian family (Gentiana clausa), known for its bottle-shaped, closed blue or purple flowers that do not open fully.
a North American wildflower with closed blue or purple flowers

Examples

We discovered a cluster of closed gentian growing beside the woodland path.
The unique buds of the closed gentian attract certain species of bumblebees that force their way inside.
Many gardeners admire the closed gentian for its late-season blooms and striking color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle gentian - another name for closed gentian (Gentiana andrewsii)
  • Synonym: Gentiana andrewsii - a similar species of bottle gentian

Etymology

From 'gentian', after the genus Gentiana, with 'closed' referring to the never-opening flower.
