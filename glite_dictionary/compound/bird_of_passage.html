bird of passage

1. NOUN

/ bɝd əv ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɜːd əv ˈpæsɪdʒ / (BrE)
A person who moves frequently from place to place or does not settle permanently in one location.
person who moves from place to place

Examples

After years as a bird of passage, he finally decided to settle down in one city.
The countryside was familiar with birds of passage who arrived every harvest season.
As a journalist, she's become something of a bird of passage, traveling wherever the news takes her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migrant - a person who moves from one place to another, especially for work

Etymology

Patterned after the literal sense of migrating birds passing through an area.

2. NOUN

/ bɝd əv ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɜːd əv ˈpæsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a bird that migrates from one region to another according to the season
migratory bird

Examples

Many species, such as the swallow, are well-known birds of passage that return each spring.
The wetlands provide a crucial stopover for birds of passage during their long migration.
Scientists track birds of passage to study the effects of climate change on migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migratory bird - a bird that travels from one region to another at regular times each year
