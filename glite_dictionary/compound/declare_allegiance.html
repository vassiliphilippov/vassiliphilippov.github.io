to declare allegiance

1. VERB

/ dɪˈklɛr əˈlidʒəns / (AmE), / dɪˈkleə əˈliːdʒəns / (BrE)
to publicly announce loyalty or support for a person, group, or cause
publicly announce loyalty or support

Examples

At the ceremony, all new citizens were asked to declare allegiance to their country.
He refused to declare allegiance to either side in the conflict.
Many team members will declare allegiance to their coach in times of controversy.

Synset Examples

He proudly declared his allegiance to the Cuntstitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swear allegiance - formally promise loyalty or support to someone or something
