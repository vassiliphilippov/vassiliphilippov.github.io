family Hydrophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli haɪˌdrɑːfəˈlɛsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli hʌɪˌdrɒfɪˈliːsiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly perennial woodland herbs, primarily native to North and South America, known collectively as waterleafs.
a family of woodland herbs (waterleafs)

Examples

Many wildflowers in North America belong to the family Hydrophyllaceae.
The botanist specialized in studying the family Hydrophyllaceae in temperate forests.
Plants in the family Hydrophyllaceae often thrive in moist woodland environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterleaf - a common name for plants in the family Hydrophyllaceae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Hydrophyllaceae, from Greek hydor (water) + phyllon (leaf), referring to waterleaf plants.
