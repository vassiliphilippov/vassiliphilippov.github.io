holding paddock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldɪŋ ˈpædək / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ ˈpadək / (BrE)
an enclosure where livestock are kept temporarily, often before being moved or sold
temporary livestock enclosure

Examples

The sheep were moved into the holding paddock before shearing began.
After transport, the cattle spend an hour in the holding paddock to recover from the journey.
Farm workers checked the fences of the holding paddock to ensure the animals were secure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stockyard - a large enclosed yard where livestock are kept temporarily before slaughter or shipment
