mathematical proof

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl pruːf / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl pruːf / (BrE)
a logical argument that demonstrates the truth of a mathematical statement or theorem, based on accepted mathematical principles
logical argument proving a mathematical theorem

Examples

The researcher presented a mathematical proof to support the new theorem.
Every student must learn how to write a mathematical proof in their first-year mathematics course.
After hours of discussion, they finally found a mathematical proof for the conjecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth of a statement
