biological warfare

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
the use of bacteria, viruses, or toxins as weapons to harm or kill humans, animals, or plants, or to contaminate food and water supplies
use of biological agents as weapons

Examples

International treaties have been established to prohibit the use of biological warfare.
The threat of biological warfare has raised concerns about global security and public health.
During the war, there were fears that biological warfare agents might be deployed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical warfare - the use of chemical substances as weapons in war
