nihil obstat

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.hɪl ˈɒb.stat, ˌnaɪ.hɪl ˈɑːb.stɑːt / (AmE), / ˌniː.hɪl ˈɒb.stat, ˌnaɪ.hɪl ˈɒb.stat / (BrE)
an official declaration, especially by a Roman Catholic censor, that a publication has been examined and contains nothing contrary to faith or morals
official Church declaration of no objection to publication

Examples

The manuscript received a nihil obstat before proceeding to publication.
Before printing, the book was granted a nihil obstat by Church authorities.
A nihil obstat is required for Catholic religious texts to ensure doctrinal accuracy.

Etymology

From Latin nihil obstat (nothing hinders), used as a formula in official approval.
