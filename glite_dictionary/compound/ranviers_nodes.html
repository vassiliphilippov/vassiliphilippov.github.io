Ranvier's nodes

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑn.vi.eɪz noʊdz / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.vieɪz nəʊdz / (BrE)
small gaps in the myelin sheath of medullated nerve axons, which facilitate rapid conduction of nerve impulses
gaps in nerve myelin sheath
medical

Examples

The action potential jumps from one Ranvier's node to the next, speeding up the transmission along the nerve.
Demyelinating diseases can damage Ranvier's nodes, disrupting nerve signal conduction.
Researchers studied how the spacing of Ranvier's nodes affects the efficiency of electrical impulses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: node of Ranvier - another form for Ranvier's nodes, referring to the same anatomical feature

Etymology

Named after French histologist Louis-Antoine Ranvier (1835–1922).
