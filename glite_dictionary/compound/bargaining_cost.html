bargaining cost

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrɡənɪŋ kɑst / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡənɪŋ kɒst / (BrE)
the expenses incurred in reaching an agreement between parties, including negotiation and contract preparation costs
costs of negotiation and agreement

Examples

The bargaining cost can be significant when negotiating complex contracts between corporations.
Reducing the bargaining cost is crucial for businesses wanting to lower overall transaction expenses.
Before finalizing the deal, both sides considered whether the bargaining cost was justified by the expected benefits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: transaction cost - the total cost of making an economic exchange, which includes bargaining costs
  • Synonym: negotiation cost - expenses specifically associated with negotiating a deal
