corn marigold

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈmɛr.ɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈmær.ɪ.ɡəʊld / (BrE)
A European herb with bright yellow flowers, commonly found as a weed in grain fields.
European weed with yellow flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with bright corn marigold every summer.
Farmers often consider corn marigold a troublesome weed among their wheat crops.
She picked a bouquet of corn marigold and placed it on the kitchen table.
