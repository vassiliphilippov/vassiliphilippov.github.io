mother of thyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ əv ˌθaɪm / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə əv ˌtaɪm / (BrE)
a fragrant European mint plant (Thymus serpyllum) with clusters of small violet-and-white flowers, often naturalized in North America
a fragrant European mint plant

Examples

Mother of thyme is often used as a ground cover in rock gardens due to its fragrant foliage.
The small violet-and-white flowers of mother of thyme attract butterflies and bees in the summer.
Gardeners appreciate how drought-tolerant mother of thyme is compared to other mints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild thyme - another name for mother of thyme (Thymus serpyllum)
  • Synonym: thyme - a genus of aromatic herbs similar to mother of thyme
