suborder Passeres

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌɔrdər pəˈsɪəriːz / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɔːdə pəˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of birds that comprises the typical songbirds.
taxonomic suborder of songbirds

Examples

The suborder Passeres includes many well-known songbirds found throughout the world.
Scientists have long debated the evolutionary relationships within the suborder Passeres.
Robins and sparrows are common members of the suborder Passeres.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Passeriformes - the order of perching birds, including Passeres
  • Synonym: Oscines - songbirds; traditional name for Passeres

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Passeres, based on Latin passer (sparrow).
