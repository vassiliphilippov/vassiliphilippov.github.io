jug wine

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌɡ waɪn / (AmE), / dʒʌɡ waɪn / (BrE)
Inexpensive wine that is sold in large bottles or jugs, typically of lower quality.
inexpensive wine sold in large bottles

Examples

Many college students buy jug wine for parties because it is cheap and comes in large containers.
She brought a bottle of jug wine to the picnic to share with friends.
The store advertised a special on local jug wine for the holiday weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table wine - wine for everyday drinking, not of high quality
