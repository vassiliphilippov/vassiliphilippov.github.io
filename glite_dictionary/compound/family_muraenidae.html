family Muraenidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli mʊˌriːˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli mjʊəˌriːˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of elongated, predatory marine fish known as moray eels, found in tropical and subtropical seas
family of moray eels

Examples

The family Muraenidae includes more than 200 species of moray eels distributed around the world.
Marine biologists study the family Muraenidae to understand the diversity of reef-dwelling predators.
The distinctive jaws and teeth of the family Muraenidae help them capture slippery prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moray eel - a type of elongated predatory fish from the family Muraenidae

Etymology

From New Latin Muraenidae, from Muraena (a type of eel) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
