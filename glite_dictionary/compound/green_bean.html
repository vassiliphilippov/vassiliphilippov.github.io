green bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrin ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn ˌbiːn / (BrE)
the seed of the mung bean plant, commonly used as food in various dishes
seed of the mung bean plant used as food

Examples

Stir-fried green beans are popular in many Asian cuisines.
She added soaked green beans to the soup for extra protein.
Dried green beans are often used to make sweet desserts in Chinese cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - seed of Vigna radiata, used as food

2. NOUN

/ ɡrin bin / (AmE), / ɡriːn biːn / (BrE)
an immature bean pod, typically eaten as a vegetable while still tender and green
immature bean pod eaten as a vegetable

Examples

She added fresh green beans to the salad for extra crunch.
We served steamed green beans with garlic as a side dish.
The recipe calls for a pound of green beans trimmed and cut in half.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap bean - another term for a green bean, emphasizing its crisp texture
  • Antonym: runner bean - a different type of bean grown for its edible pods or seeds

3. NOUN

/ ɡriːn biːn / (AmE), / ɡriːn biːn / (BrE)
a type of common bean plant grown for its long, slender green edible pods, often eaten as a vegetable
a slender green edible bean pod

Examples

She added fresh green beans to the salad for extra crunch.
The farmer harvested baskets full of green beans early in the morning.
Roasted green beans make a healthy and delicious side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap bean - another term for green bean, especially when eaten whole
  • Synonym: string bean - another common term for green bean
