hydrogen ion concentration

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.drə.dʒən ˌaɪ.ɑn ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.drɪ.dʒən ˌaɪ.ɒn ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the amount of hydrogen ions, measured in moles, present per cubic decimeter of a solution; used to determine acidity
measure of hydrogen ions in a solution
medical

Examples

Chemists often measure the hydrogen ion concentration to determine a solution's acidity.
The hydrogen ion concentration of blood is crucial to proper body function.
Changes in hydrogen ion concentration can have significant effects on cellular processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pH - scale used to specify the acidity or basicity of a solution
