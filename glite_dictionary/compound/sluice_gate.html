sluice gate

1. NOUN

/ sluːs ɡeɪt / (AmE), / sluːs ɡeɪt / (BrE)
A gate or barrier that can be opened or closed to control the flow of water, especially in a channel, river, or reservoir.
gate controlling water flow

Examples

Engineers raised the sluice gate to let excess water flow out after the heavy rain.
The town installed a new sluice gate to manage the river's water level and reduce flood risk.
When the sluice gate is closed, water is trapped in the canal for irrigation.

Synset Examples

The engineer adjusted the sluice gate for better drainage.
The sluice gate controls the water flow in the river.
They opened the sluice gate to prevent flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floodgate - a gate for shutting out or releasing water, especially in a dam or levee

Etymology

From 'sluice' (from Old French *escluse*, from Latin *exclusa*, 'barrier') and 'gate'.
