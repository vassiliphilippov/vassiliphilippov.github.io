tobacco thrips

1. NOUN

/ təˈbækoʊ ˈθrɪps / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ ˈθrɪps / (BrE)
A species of small insect (Frankliniella fusca) that feeds on and damages tobacco and peanut plants.
insect pest of tobacco and peanuts

Examples

Tobacco thrips can cause serious damage to young tobacco plants if not managed early.
Farmers monitor their crops regularly to check for signs of tobacco thrips infestation.
In recent years, tobacco thrips have also been found affecting peanut fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - a small slender insect that feeds on plants, sometimes damaging crops
