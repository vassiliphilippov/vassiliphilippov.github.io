box office

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈɑfɪs / (AmE), / bɒks ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
The place in a theatre, cinema, or other venue where tickets are sold to the public.
ticket sales counter at a venue

Examples

I waited in line at the box office to buy tickets for the concert.
Tickets are available online or from the box office at the theater.
The movie was such a hit that the box office sold out within hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticket office - place where tickets are sold

2. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈɑfɪs / (AmE), / bɒks ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
the amount of money earned from ticket sales for a film, performance, or event
total ticket sales revenue

Examples

The movie broke records at the box office during its opening weekend.
Despite positive reviews, the play was a box office disappointment.
They track box office numbers to measure a film's commercial success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gross - total earnings before deductions
  • Antonym: net - earnings after all deductions

Etymology

Originally from the literal 'box office', referring to a booth selling tickets at theaters; later extended to mean total ticket receipts.
