congress gaiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡrəs ˈɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs ˈɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a type of ankle-high shoe or boot with elastic gussets at the sides, typically worn in the 19th and early 20th centuries
ankle-high shoe with elastic sides
archaic

Examples

He wore a pair of black congress gaiters polished to a high shine.
The shop specialized in vintage footwear, including congress gaiters from the Victorian era.
Fashion began to change, and the congress gaiter gradually fell out of style by the 1920s.

Etymology

The term 'congress' refers to its reported popularity during sessions of the U.S. Congress in the 19th century; 'gaiter' from French 'guêtre', meaning a covering for the ankle and shoe.
