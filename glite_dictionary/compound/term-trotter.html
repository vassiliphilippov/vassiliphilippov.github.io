term-trotter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrmˌtrɑtər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmˌtrɒtə / (BrE)
a person who travels frequently, especially for academic or work-related reasons
person who travels frequently for work or study

Examples

He's a real term-trotter, always traveling for work.
As a term-trotter, she has visited dozens of universities worldwide.
Being a term-trotter means spending a lot of time on planes and in hotels.

Synset Examples

He's a real term-trotter, always traveling for work.
Being a term-trotter has its perks and challenges.
As a term-trotter, she visits new cities frequently.

Etymology

From 'term' (as in academic term or work term) + 'trotter' (one who trots or travels about).
