skin graft

1. NOUN

/ skɪn ɡræft / (AmE), / skɪn ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
a piece of healthy skin taken from one part of the body or a donor and surgically attached to another area to replace damaged skin
a surgical transplant of skin
medical

Examples

The patient received a skin graft to treat the severe burn on his leg.
After the accident, a skin graft was needed to aid the healing process.
Recovery from a skin graft can take several weeks, depending on the size of the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graft - a surgical operation where tissue is transplanted
