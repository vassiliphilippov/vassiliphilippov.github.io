black bindweed

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈbaɪndˌwiːd / (AmE), / blak ˈbaɪndwiːd / (BrE)
a common European climbing plant (Fallopia convolvulus) with tuberous roots, heart-shaped leaves, and small red berries
a European climbing plant with red berries

Examples

The hedgerow was covered in black bindweed during late summer.
Farmers often struggle to control black bindweed because of its rapid spread.
The heart-shaped leaves of black bindweed make it easy to identify in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bindweed - a climbing or twining plant of the morning glory family
