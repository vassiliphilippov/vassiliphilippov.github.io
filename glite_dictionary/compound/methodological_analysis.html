methodological analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəˈdɑlədʒɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛθəˈdɒlədʒɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the study or examination of the principles and methods used for inquiry and investigation in a particular discipline
analysis of methods and principles of inquiry

Examples

The philosopher's methodological analysis of science challenged widely accepted research practices.
In his article, she presents a rigorous methodological analysis of linguistic theories.
A thorough methodological analysis can reveal biases in previous studies.

Etymology

From 'methodological' (relating to methods, from Greek 'methodos') + 'analysis' (from Greek 'analusis', a breaking up).
