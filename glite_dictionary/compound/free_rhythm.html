free rhythm

1. NOUN

/ fri ˈrɪðəm / (AmE), / friː ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
A type of musical rhythm lacking recurring accents or fixed time values, not conforming to regular metrical patterns.
musical rhythm without recurring accents

Examples

The ancient chant was performed with free rhythm, creating a floating and timeless feel.
Many modern composers use free rhythm to break away from traditional metric structures.
The singer interpreted the melody with free rhythm, allowing each phrase to flow naturally.

Synset Examples

Some chants and recitative vocal pieces may be considered as instances of a free rhythm.
