Nullarbor Plain

1. PROPN

/ ˈnʌlɑrbɔr pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈnʌləbɔː pleɪn / (BrE)
a vast, almost treeless arid plain in southern Australia, extending inland from the Great Australian Bight, noted for its sparse vegetation and lack of surface water
arid plain in southern Australia

Examples

The sheer emptiness of the Nullarbor Plain can be both mesmerizing and intimidating for first-time travelers.
Driving across the Nullarbor Plain is considered a classic Australian road trip adventure.
Very few people live on the Nullarbor Plain due to its harsh, arid conditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'nullus arbor' meaning 'no tree', referring to the plain's lack of trees.
