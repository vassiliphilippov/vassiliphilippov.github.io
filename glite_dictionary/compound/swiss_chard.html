Swiss chard

1. NOUN

/ swɪs tʃɑrd / (AmE), / swɪs tʃɑːd / (BrE)
a leafy green vegetable with large dark green leaves and thick white, red, or yellow stalks, often used in cooking
leafy vegetable with thick stalks

Examples

I sautéed the Swiss chard with garlic for a healthy side dish.
The farmer's market had fresh Swiss chard among other leafy greens.
Add chopped Swiss chard to your soup for extra nutrition.

Etymology

Named for its supposed origin in Switzerland and 'chard' from French 'carde' meaning artichoke thistle.

2. NOUN

/ swɪs tʃɑrd / (AmE), / swɪs tʃɑːd / (BrE)
A leafy vegetable related to beetroot, grown for its large green leaves and fleshy, often colorful stalks, which are eaten as a vegetable.
leafy vegetable related to beetroot

Examples

I like to sauté Swiss chard with garlic for a quick and healthy side dish.
The farmers’ market had fresh bundles of Swiss chard in a variety of colors.
You can use Swiss chard in soups, salads, or as a substitute for spinach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chard - a leafy vegetable with edible stalks and leaves
  • Synonym: beet - the plant Beta vulgaris, related to Swiss chard

Etymology

From Swiss, indicating origin in Switzerland, and chard, from French 'carde'.
