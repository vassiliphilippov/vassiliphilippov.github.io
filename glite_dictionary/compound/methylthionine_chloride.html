methylthionine chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθɪlˌθaɪ.əˌnin ˈklɔraɪd / (AmE), / ˌmiːθɪlˌθaɪ.əˌniːn ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a dark green dye used in staining, as an antiseptic, a chemical indicator, and an antidote for cyanide poisoning
a dye and chemical indicator, medical and lab use
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician added methylthionine chloride to the slide to stain the tissues for examination.
Treatment for cyanide poisoning may involve administering methylthionine chloride as an antidote.
In microbiology, methylthionine chloride is often used as a chemical indicator during certain tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methylene blue - a widely-used synthetic dye with similar applications

Etymology

From 'methyl' (chemical prefix), 'thionine' (a thiazine dye), and 'chloride' (a salt of hydrochloric acid).
