cacao moth

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkaʊ ˌmɑθ / (AmE), / kəˈkaʊ ˌmɒθ / (BrE)
a small moth whose larvae feed on dried plant products such as tobacco and cacao beans, often causing damage to stored goods
moth whose larvae feed on dried plant products

Examples

The storage facility suffered an infestation of cacao moth larvae that damaged several bags of tobacco.
Proper hygiene and sealing practices are essential to prevent cacao moth infestations in warehouses.
A cacao moth was discovered in the shipment of dried herbs, prompting immediate inspection.
