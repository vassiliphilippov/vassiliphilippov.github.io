Chinese restaurant syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz ˈrɛs.təˌrɑːnt ˈsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪˌniːz ˈrɛs.tər.ɒnt ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A group of symptoms such as headache, tingling, burning sensations, and sweating, allegedly triggered by eating food containing monosodium glutamate (MSG).
symptoms after eating MSG-containing food

Examples

After eating at the new buffet, he believed he was experiencing Chinese restaurant syndrome due to a sudden headache.
Some doctors question whether Chinese restaurant syndrome is actually caused by MSG or if it is a myth.
The article discussed how Chinese restaurant syndrome is less commonly reported today than in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MSG symptom complex - group of symptoms linked to MSG (monosodium glutamate) intake

Etymology

Named after reports linking symptoms to Chinese restaurant food containing MSG in the 1960s.
