siege of Yorktown

1. NOUN

/ siːdʒ əv ˈjɔrk.taʊn / (AmE), / siːdʒ əv ˈjɔːk.taʊn / (BrE)
the final major battle of the American Revolutionary War in 1781, in which British forces under Cornwallis surrendered to American and French troops after a three-week siege
final battle of American Revolutionary War (1781)

Examples

The siege of Yorktown marked a decisive victory for the American and French forces.
Many historians consider the siege of Yorktown the event that effectively ended the American Revolution.
Cornwallis surrendered to Washington after the siege of Yorktown in 1781.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Yorktown - alternative historical name for the same event
