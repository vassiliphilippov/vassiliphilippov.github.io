descensus uteri

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈsɛnsəs ˈjuːtəraɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnsəs ˈjuːtɪraɪ / (BrE)
prolapse or downward displacement of the uterus from its normal position
prolapse of the uterus
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with descensus uteri after experiencing pelvic discomfort and a feeling of heaviness.
Surgical intervention is sometimes necessary to correct advanced descensus uteri.
Risk factors for descensus uteri include childbirth, aging, and chronic straining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uterine prolapse - condition in which the uterus descends into or outside of the vaginal canal

Etymology

From Latin: 'descensus' (a descending), 'uteri' (of the uterus).
