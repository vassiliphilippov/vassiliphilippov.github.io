extragalactic nebula

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkstrəɡəˈlæktɪk nəˈbjuːlə / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəɡəˈlæktɪk nɪˈbjuːlə / (BrE)
(historical) A galaxy; an astronomical system comprising many stars, nebulae, and dust beyond the Milky Way, a term formerly used for galaxies.
historical term for galaxy

Examples

In early 20th-century texts, the Andromeda Galaxy was often called an extragalactic nebula.
The term extragalactic nebula has largely been replaced by 'galaxy' in modern astronomy.
Astronomers once debated whether each extragalactic nebula was part of the Milky Way or a separate island universe.

Synset Examples

‘extragalactic nebula’ is a former name for ‘galaxy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galaxy - A massive system of stars, gas, and dust bound together by gravity

Etymology

From 'extra-' (outside), 'galactic' (of the galaxy), and Latin 'nebula' (mist, cloud).
