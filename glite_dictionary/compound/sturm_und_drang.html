Sturm und Drang

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃtʊrm ʊn ˈdrɑːŋ / (AmE), / ˌʃtʊəm ʊn ˈdraŋ / (BrE)
A period or state of tumultuous upheaval, intense conflict, or emotional turbulence, especially in cultural, artistic, or political contexts.
a period of upheaval or emotional turbulence

Examples

The company's restructuring led to a period of Sturm und Drang within the organization.
Her adolescence was marked by Sturm und Drang as she struggled to find her identity.
Historians often refer to the 1960s as a decade of social Sturm und Drang in the West.

Synset Examples

the industrial revolution was a period of great turbulence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • Synonym: upheaval - violent or sudden change or disruption to something

Etymology

German for 'storm and stress', originally denoting a late 18th-century German literary movement characterized by emotional unrest and individualism.
