barium oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəm ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbæriəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a whitish, toxic powder that is an oxide of barium, used in ceramics, glassmaking, and as a chemical reagent
a toxic oxide of barium (chemical)

Examples

Lab technicians must handle barium oxide with care due to its toxicity.
Barium oxide is often used in glassmaking for its unique chemical properties.
When heated, barium oxide reacts readily with water to form barium hydroxide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: barium peroxide - an oxide of barium containing more oxygen
  • Synonym: barium - a soft, silvery-white metallic element

Etymology

From 'barium' (element name from New Latin, after Barilla) + 'oxide' (from Greek 'oxys' meaning 'sharp, acid' and '-ide', indicating binary compounds).
