jack salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæk ˌsæmən / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk ˌsamən / (BrE)
A pike-like freshwater fish of the perch family, especially referring to young salmon or certain species of pike-perch.
pike-like freshwater fish; young salmon or pike-perch

Examples

Many anglers try to catch jack salmon during the early fishing season.
The river's population of jack salmon has increased due to conservation efforts.
We grilled jack salmon over the campfire for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike-perch - a predatory freshwater fish also called zander or walleye
  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish found in both Atlantic and Pacific Oceans

2. NOUN

/ dʒæk ˈsæm.ən / (AmE), / dʒæk ˈsæm.ən / (BrE)
a common name for certain young or small salmon, especially male Pacific salmon that return to spawn after only one year at sea; also used regionally for other species such as the walleye
a type of young or regional salmon

Examples

Local anglers say the river is full of jack salmon in early fall.
We grilled some fresh jack salmon we caught on our trip.
In some regions, people refer to walleye as jack salmon.

Synset Examples

Jack salmon is my favorite type of fish.
We had jack salmon for dinner last night.

Etymology

From 'jack' meaning 'young male' in animal names and 'salmon'; regional usage in North America.
