black willow

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / blæk ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a North American willow tree (Salix nigra) with dark bark and narrow leaves, typically growing near streams and lakes
a North American willow tree

Examples

The black willow thrives in wet soils along riverbanks and lakeshores.
A large black willow shaded the anglers as they waited patiently by the stream.
In spring, the leaves of the black willow emerge before most other trees in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically with slender branches
