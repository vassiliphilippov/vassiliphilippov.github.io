storm centre

1. NOUN

/ stɔrm ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
the central area of a storm where the barometric pressure is lowest
central low-pressure area of a storm

Examples

Meteorologists tracked the storm centre as it moved across the Gulf Coast.
High winds were recorded near the storm centre during the hurricane.
The barometric pressure dropped sharply at the storm centre of the approaching cyclone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye - the calm region at the center of a tropical cyclone

2. NOUN

/ stɔrm ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈsentə / (BrE)
The focal point of trouble, disturbance, or controversy.
center of trouble or disturbance

Examples

The political campaign quickly became the storm centre of national debate.
As the CEO, she often found herself at the storm centre of company controversies.
The disputed border region is once again the storm centre of international tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epicenter - the central point of something, typically a difficult or unpleasant situation
  • Synonym: flashpoint - a place, event, or time at which trouble, such as violence or anger, flares up
