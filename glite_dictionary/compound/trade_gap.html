trade gap

1. NOUN

/ treɪd ɡæp / (AmE), / treɪd ɡap / (BrE)
the difference between the value of a country's imports and exports over a specific period, often used to indicate a trade deficit or surplus
difference between imports and exports

Examples

The government is trying to reduce the trade gap through new export policies.
A widening trade gap can lead to concerns about a country's economic stability.
Last year, the nation's trade gap reached its highest level in a decade.

Synset Examples

a nation's balance of trade is favorable when its exports exceed its imports

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance of trade - the difference in value between a country's imports and exports
