Greek War of Independence

1. NOUN

/ ɡrik wɔr ʌv ˌɪndəˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / ɡriːk wɔːr əv ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
the war fought by the Greeks between 1821 and 1829 to gain independence from Ottoman Turkish rule, resulting in the establishment of a sovereign Greek state
war for Greek independence (1821–1829)

Examples

The Greek War of Independence marked a turning point in European history.
During the Greek War of Independence, several European powers provided crucial military support.
Many works of art and literature were inspired by the Greek War of Independence.
