antitrust legislation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈtrʌst ˌlɛdʒɪsˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌantiˈtrʌst ˌlɛdʒɪsˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
Law enacted to promote free competition by preventing monopolies and unfair business practices.
law preventing monopolies

Examples

Recent antitrust legislation has made it harder for large companies to merge without oversight.
The government relies on antitrust legislation to prevent unfair business practices.
Critics argue that current antitrust legislation needs to be updated for the digital economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antitrust law - law that prevents unfair monopolies and promotes competition

Etymology

antitrust: from anti- ('against') + trust ('large business monopoly'); legislation: from Latin 'legis latio', 'the proposing of a law'.
