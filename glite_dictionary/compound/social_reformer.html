social reformer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl rɪˈfɔːrmər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃl rɪˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a person who works to improve society by advocating or implementing social reforms
person who advocates social reform

Examples

Florence Nightingale was a renowned social reformer who changed the way hospitals operated.
Many social reformers dedicate their lives to fighting for equality and justice.
The policies proposed by the social reformer drew widespread public attention and debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reformer - a person who makes changes to something in order to improve it
