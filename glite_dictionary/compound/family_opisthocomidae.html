family Opisthocomidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli oʊˌpɪsθoʊˈkɒmɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli əʊˌpɪsθəʊˈkɒmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds that comprises the hoatzin, a unique South American bird species.
bird family comprising the hoatzin

Examples

The family Opisthocomidae contains only one living species, the hoatzin, found in South America.
Researchers debated the evolutionary origins of the family Opisthocomidae for decades.
The digestive system of birds in the family Opisthocomidae is unique among all birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoatzin - a South American bird known for its unique digestive system, found in the family Opisthocomidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Opisthocomidae', the taxonomic name formed from 'Opisthocomus' (the genus of the hoatzin) and the suffix '-idae' indicating a family.
