blue vitriol

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈvɪtriəl / (AmE), / bluː ˈvɪtriəl / (BrE)
a blue crystalline compound, copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate, used in electroplating, as a fungicide, and in chemistry
hydrated blue crystalline copper sulfate

Examples

The laboratory technician carefully measured out the blue vitriol for the experiment.
Blue vitriol was once widely used to control fungal diseases in crops.
Crystals of blue vitriol formed at the bottom of the beaker after the solution cooled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper sulfate - a blue chemical compound containing copper and sulfur

Etymology

From 'vitriol', an old term for sulfates, combined with 'blue' describing its color.
