Black race

1. NOUN

/ blæk reɪs / (AmE), / blæk reɪs / (BrE)
A group of people traditionally identified by dark skin color, often referring to people of African ancestry; the term is now considered outdated and is rarely used in modern contexts except historically or anthropologically.
people traditionally identified by dark skin color

Examples

The term Black race was commonly used in historical demographic studies.
Sociologists now rarely use Black race, preferring terms like 'Black people' or 'people of African descent'.
She researched the portrayal of the Black race in nineteenth-century literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black people - people of African or other dark-skinned descent
  • Antonym: White race - group traditionally identified by light skin color
