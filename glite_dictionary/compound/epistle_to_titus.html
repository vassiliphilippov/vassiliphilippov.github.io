Epistle to Titus

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl tu ˈtaɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tʊ ˈtaɪtəs / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament containing Saint Paul's letter to Titus, offering advice on church leadership and pastoral duties
New Testament book; letter from Paul to Titus

Examples

The Epistle to Titus outlines the qualifications for church elders and leaders.
Many scholars have analyzed the structure of the Epistle to Titus in their studies of the New Testament.
She referenced the Epistle to Titus during her sermon on pastoral responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Titus - Paul's companion; recipient of the New Testament letter
  • Synonym: Epistle to Timothy - New Testament letters from Paul to Timothy, similar in content

Etymology

Epistle from Latin 'epistola' (letter); Titus is a Greek name, referring to the addressee of Paul's letter.
