uninominal system

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪˈnɑːmɪnəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈnɒmɪnəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
An electoral system in which only one representative is elected from each constituency or district.
electoral system with one member per district

Examples

Many countries use a uninominal system to elect their members of parliament.
The uninominal system tends to favor larger political parties over smaller ones.
Debates about electoral reform often compare the uninominal system with proportional representation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proportional representation - an electoral system in which parties gain seats in proportion to the number of votes cast for them

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'nomin-' (name) + 'system'.
