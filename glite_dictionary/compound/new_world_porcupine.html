New World porcupine

1. NOUN

/ nuː wɜrld ˈpɔːrkjupaɪn / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld ˈpɔːkjupaɪn / (BrE)
a porcupine of the family Erethizontidae, native to North, Central, and South America, usually living in trees
porcupine from the Americas, often arboreal

Examples

The New World porcupine spends most of its life in the branches of trees.
Unlike its Old World relatives, the New World porcupine has a prehensile tail to help it climb.
Biologists observed a New World porcupine feeding on bark during the night.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Old World porcupine - porcupine native to Europe, Asia, and Africa (family Hystricidae)
