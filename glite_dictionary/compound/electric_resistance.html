electric resistance

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
A material's opposition to the flow of electric current, measured in ohms.
opposition to electric current flow

Examples

Copper has very low electric resistance, making it an excellent material for wiring.
The electric resistance of a wire increases as its length increases.
Engineers measure electric resistance to ensure circuits operate safely and efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conductance - a measure of how easily electricity flows through a material, the reciprocal of resistance
  • Synonym: resistor - an electrical component used to provide resistance in a circuit

Etymology

From 'electric' (relating to electricity) and 'resistance' (opposition), the latter from Latin 'resistentia'.
