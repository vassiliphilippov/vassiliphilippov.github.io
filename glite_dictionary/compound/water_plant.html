water plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that lives partly or entirely in water, either rooted in the soil or floating freely
aquatic plant

Examples

The pond was filled with water plants such as lilies and reeds.
Some water plants float on the surface, while others are rooted in the mud below.
Removing invasive water plants can help restore the balance of the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aquatic plant - a plant adapted to living in or on water
