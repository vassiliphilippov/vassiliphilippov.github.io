strelitzia family

1. NOUN

/ strəˈlɪtsiə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / strɛˈlɪtsiə ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of tropical woody plants with erect stems, native to South America, South Africa, and Madagascar, sometimes classified with the bananas (Musaceae)
family of tropical woody plants

Examples

The strelitzia family includes the well-known bird of paradise plant.
Many members of the strelitzia family are cultivated for their striking flowers.
Botanists debate whether the strelitzia family should be grouped with the banana family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Musaceae - the banana family

Etymology

Named after the genus Strelitzia, itself named for Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz.
