bistable relay

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪˈsteɪbəl rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˌbɪˈsteɪbəl rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
a relay that remains in either of two positions without continuous power, switching positions when briefly energized
relay remaining in two states without power

Examples

The circuit uses a bistable relay to remember its last state even when the power is cut.
A bistable relay is ideal for applications where power consumption must be minimized.
Technicians prefer bistable relays in alarm systems due to their reliability and low energy usage.

Synset Examples

A latching relay allows remote control of building lighting without the hum that may be produced from a continuously (AC) energized coil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latching relay - relay that maintains its position after being actuated

Etymology

From 'bi-' meaning 'two' and 'stable,' indicating two stable states; 'relay' from French 'relayer' (to relieve).
