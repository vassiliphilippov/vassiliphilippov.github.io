drawing card

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪ.ɪŋ kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdrɔː.ɪŋ kɑːd / (BrE)
an item or attraction offered to entice customers, especially something sold at a loss to increase store traffic
attraction or item used to draw customers

Examples

The supermarket used a cheap turkey as a drawing card to attract more Thanksgiving shoppers.
The new arcade game became the biggest drawing card at the amusement center.
Special discounts on electronics serve as a drawing card for customers during holiday sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loss leader - an item sold at a loss to attract customers

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔ.ɪŋ ˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdrɔː.ɪŋ ˌkɑːd / (BrE)
a person, performance, or feature that attracts many people to an event or place, especially an entertainer who draws large audiences
person or thing attracting large crowds

Examples

The band’s new lead singer is a major drawing card for live shows.
This year’s festival has no real drawing card to attract big crowds.
She was the main drawing card of the evening, and tickets sold out quickly.

Synset Examples

he was the biggest drawing card they had

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - something that draws people to it
  • Synonym: crowd-puller - person or thing likely to attract a large audience (esp. British)

Etymology

From the notion of a 'card' (as in a performer listed on a playbill) that 'draws' interest or attendance.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪŋ ˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːɪŋ ˌkɑːd / (BrE)
a person, feature, or event that attracts a lot of interest or attention
something that attracts interest or attention

Examples

The new skyscraper quickly became the city's main drawing card for tourists.
Her reputation as a chef was the restaurant's biggest drawing card.
The festival's headline act is always a huge drawing card for music lovers.

Synset Examples

The new product design is our main drawing card.
Education is a drawing card for many job applicants.
His charm was his best drawing card in negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - something that draws attention or interest
  • Synonym: crowd-puller - something that attracts large audiences or groups

Etymology

Originally from card games, where cards are drawn, and later used figuratively to mean an attraction.
