migratory locust

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪɡrəˌtɔri ˈloʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈmɪɡrəˌtəri ˈləʊkəst / (BrE)
an Old World species of locust that moves in large swarms, consuming crops and vegetation over wide areas
a locust species swarming in large groups

Examples

During drought years, the migratory locust destroyed crops across entire regions.
Scientists are studying the swarming behavior of the migratory locust to better predict outbreaks.
The sudden invasion of migratory locusts caused severe food shortages in the affected villages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locust - a type of grasshopper that migrates in large swarms
