forest god

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.əst ɡɑd / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪst ɡɒd / (BrE)
a mythological deity associated with forests and woods, often depicted as an attendant of Bacchus and linked to Roman fauns
mythological woodland deity

Examples

In the ancient myths, the forest god was often depicted playing a pipe among the trees.
The villagers believed a forest god protected the woods from harm.
Legends describe the forest god as a playful spirit who loved wine and dancing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faun - a Roman woodland deity with goat-like features
  • Synonym: satyr - a Greek woodland deity, companion of Dionysus, lustful and playful
