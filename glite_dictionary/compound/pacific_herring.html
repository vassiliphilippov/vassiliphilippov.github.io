Pacific herring

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
a species of herring (Clupea pallasii) found in the northern Pacific Ocean, important as a food fish
a species of herring in the northern Pacific

Examples

Fishermen in Alaska rely heavily on the Pacific herring during the spring season.
The population of Pacific herring has been declining due to overfishing and environmental changes.
Smoked Pacific herring is considered a delicacy in many coastal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silver fish found in the temperate waters of the northern hemisphere
