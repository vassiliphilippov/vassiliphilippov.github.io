inflation therapy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfleɪʃən ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪʃən ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which water, oxygen, or a drug is introduced into the respiratory tract along with inhaled air
medical treatment for respiratory tract
medical

Examples

The patient underwent inflation therapy to help open his airways and improve oxygen levels.
Doctors sometimes use inflation therapy as part of the treatment for severe respiratory conditions.
Recently, advances in inflation therapy have reduced complications associated with traditional ventilation.
