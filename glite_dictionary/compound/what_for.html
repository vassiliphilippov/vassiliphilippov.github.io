what for

1. NOUN

/ wʌt fɔr / (AmE), / wɒt fɔː / (BrE)
an act of giving someone a strong verbal reprimand; a scolding
strong reprimand; scolding
informal

Examples

If you don't finish your homework, you'll get a real what for from your teacher.
She gave him the what for after discovering the mess he left in the kitchen.
Their coach gave the team the what for following the disappointing game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scolding - an angry rebuke or reprimand
