Transylvanian Alps

1. PROPN

/ trænˌsɪlˈveɪniən ælps / (AmE), / trænˌsɪlˈveɪniən ælps / (BrE)
a mountain range forming the southern part of the Carpathian Mountains, stretching across central Romania
mountain range in central Romania

Examples

The Transylvanian Alps are known for their rugged peaks and beautiful scenery.
We drove through the Transylvanian Alps on our way to southern Romania.
Several rare animal species can be found in the forests of the Transylvanian Alps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carpathian Mountains - a major mountain system in Central and Eastern Europe

Etymology

From 'Transylvania', a historical region in Romania, and 'Alps', by analogy to the European mountain range.
