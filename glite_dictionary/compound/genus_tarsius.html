genus Tarsius

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtɑːr.si.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtɑː.si.əs / (BrE)
the sole genus of the family Tarsiidae, comprising small primates known as tarsiers found in Southeast Asia
genus of tarsier primates

Examples

The genus Tarsius includes several species of nocturnal primates native to Southeast Asia.
Researchers conducted a study on the behavior of primates in the genus Tarsius.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitats of the genus Tarsius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarsier - a small nocturnal primate found in Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'tarsius', the type genus name, from 'tarsus' (ankle), referring to their elongated feet.
