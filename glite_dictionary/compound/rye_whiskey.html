rye whiskey

1. NOUN

/ raɪ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / raɪ ˈwɪski / (BrE)
A type of whiskey distilled from rye grain or a mixture of rye and malt.
whiskey made from rye grain

Examples

He ordered a glass of rye whiskey to go with his meal.
The distillery is famous for its award-winning rye whiskey.
She prefers rye whiskey in her Manhattan cocktail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bourbon - a type of American whiskey made primarily from corn

Etymology

From 'rye' (the grain) and 'whiskey', itself from Irish 'uisce beatha' meaning 'water of life'.
