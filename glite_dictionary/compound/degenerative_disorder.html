degenerative disorder

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by progressive deterioration of tissue or function in the body, often worsening over time
progressively worsening disease
medical

Examples

Alzheimer's disease is a well-known example of a degenerative disorder affecting the brain.
Early diagnosis can be crucial in managing symptoms of a degenerative disorder.
Advances in medicine have improved treatment options for people with degenerative disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disease - an abnormal condition affecting an organism
  • Degree Higher: neurodegenerative disease - a type of degenerative disorder affecting nerve cells

Etymology

From Latin *degenerare* ('to degenerate') + 'disorder'.
