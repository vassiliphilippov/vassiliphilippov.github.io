genus Tetrao

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs tɛˈtraʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs tɪˈtrɑːəʊ / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Tetraonidae, which includes birds known as capercaillies.
type genus of Tetraonidae (capercaillies)

Examples

The genus Tetrao includes several species of large woodland grouse found in northern Europe and Asia.
Ornithologists often study the genus Tetrao to understand capercaillie behavior and habitat.
The capercaillie, belonging to the genus Tetrao, is renowned for its elaborate courtship displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capercaillie - a large woodland grouse found in Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'Tetrao', the classical name for a type of grouse.
