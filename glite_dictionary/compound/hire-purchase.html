hire-purchase

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪərˈpɜr.tʃəs / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəˈpɜː.tʃəs / (BrE)
A system by which a buyer pays for a product in regular installments while having the use of it, and ownership is transferred only after all payments are made.
system of paying for goods in installments
british legal

Examples

Many people bought televisions on hire-purchase in the 1960s.
She decided to get her furniture through a hire-purchase agreement.
If you miss payments on a hire-purchase contract, the goods can be repossessed.

Synset Examples

we bought a car on the never-never

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment plan - a method of paying for something in parts over time

Etymology

First recorded in late 19th century; 'hire' and 'purchase' from Old English and Anglo-Norman origins, respectively.
