mud-beplastered

1. ADJ

/ mʌd bɪˈplæstɚd / (AmE), / mʌd bɪˈplɑːstəd / (BrE)
Covered with or appearing as if covered with mud.
covered with or as if with mud

Examples

After the rainstorm, the children returned home mud-beplastered and laughing.
The mud-beplastered boots left tracks all over the clean kitchen floor.
Her mud-beplastered coat was a testament to her trek through the flooded fields.

Synset Examples

mud-beplastered arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddy - Covered with or full of mud

Etymology

From 'mud' + 'beplastered', where 'beplastered' means 'covered thickly' and originates from the prefix 'be-' and 'plaster'.
