hard to justify

1. ADJ

/ hɑrd tə ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / hɑːd tə ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
difficult to defend or explain as reasonable or fair
difficult to explain as reasonable

Examples

The committee found the expense report hard to justify given the project’s limited results.
Some business practices, while legal, are hard to justify ethically.
It is hard to justify spending so much money on unnecessary upgrades.

Synset Examples

The costs of the project were astronomic and hard to justify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indefensible - impossible to defend or justify
  • Synonym: unjustifiable - impossible to justify; not able to be shown to be right
