rust inhibitor

1. NOUN

/ rʌst ɪnˈhɪbətər / (AmE), / rʌst ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
A chemical substance that prevents or slows the formation of rust on metal surfaces.
chemical preventing rust

Examples

Applying a rust inhibitor to the metal tools can significantly extend their lifespan.
The manufacturer recommends using a rust inhibitor on exposed steel parts.
Before painting, he coated the frame with a rust inhibitor to protect it from corrosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antioxidant - a substance that inhibits oxidation, especially one used to counteract the deterioration of stored food products or prevent rust
