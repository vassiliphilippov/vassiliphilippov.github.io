pencil lead

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsl lɛd / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsl lɛd / (BrE)
the mixture of graphite and clay that forms the marking core of a pencil, available in different degrees of hardness
core material of a pencil

Examples

She replaced the broken pencil lead in her mechanical pencil.
The pencil lead snapped just as he started to draw the outline.
Different grades of pencil lead are used for sketching and writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphite - a soft, black, carbon-based mineral used as the main substance in pencil lead

Etymology

The term 'lead' arises from the historical use of lead styluses, though modern pencil 'lead' is made from graphite and clay.
