shot put

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑt pʊt / (AmE), / ʃɒt pʊt / (BrE)
a track and field event in which competitors throw a heavy spherical object as far as possible
athletics event: throwing a heavy ball

Examples

She broke the national record in the women's shot put at the championship.
The shot put requires both strength and precise technique.
He trained for months to qualify for the Olympic shot put final.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammer throw - athletics event: throwing a heavy weight attached to a handle
  • Synonym: discus throw - athletics event: throwing a heavy disc
  • Synonym: javelin throw - athletics event: throwing a long spear-like object

Etymology

From 'shot' (projectile) + 'put' (to throw), referring to putting (thrusting) a heavy ball.
