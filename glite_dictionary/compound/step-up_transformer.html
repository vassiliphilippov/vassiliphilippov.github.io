step-up transformer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛp ʌp trænsˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ˈstɛp ʌp trɑːnsˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
A transformer designed to increase the voltage of an alternating current.
transformer that increases voltage

Examples

The voltage from the generator was increased using a step-up transformer.
A step-up transformer is used in power stations to transmit electricity over long distances.
Engineers installed a new step-up transformer to improve the efficiency of the power grid.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: step-down transformer - a transformer that decreases voltage
