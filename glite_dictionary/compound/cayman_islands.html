Cayman Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈkeɪ.mən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.mən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of three islands in the Caribbean Sea, a British Overseas Territory, known for its beaches and status as an international financial center
Caribbean British Overseas Territory

Examples

Many tourists flock to the Cayman Islands every year for their pristine beaches and clear blue waters.
The Cayman Islands are famous for their thriving financial services industry.
She dreamed of scuba diving in the Cayman Islands during her next vacation.

Synset Examples

They vacationed in the Cayman Islands last summer.
The Cayman Islands are known for their stunning beaches.
He invested in properties in the Cayman Islands.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Caimán' (caiman, a type of crocodile), referring to the local crocodiles observed by early explorers.
