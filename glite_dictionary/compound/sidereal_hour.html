sidereal hour

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈdɪriəl aʊər / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪəriəl aʊə / (BrE)
A unit of time equal to one twenty-fourth of a sidereal day, used in astronomy.
1/24 of a sidereal day (astronomy)

Examples

In astronomy, a sidereal hour is used to divide the sidereal day into equal parts.
The telescope's position was adjusted every sidereal hour to track the star.
Unlike a solar hour, a sidereal hour is slightly shorter since the sidereal day is about four minutes less than a solar day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidereal day - A day measured by Earth's rotation relative to the stars

Etymology

From Latin sidereus (starry) + hour; relating to measurements based on the stars.
