western chokecherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈtʃoʊkˌtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈtʃəʊkˌtʃɛri / (BrE)
a species of chokecherry native to the western United States, known for its small, astringent fruit and use in preserves and wildlife habitats
chokecherry species of western United States

Examples

The western chokecherry thrives in the dry soils of the Rocky Mountains.
Birds are attracted to the fruit of the western chokecherry in late summer.
Many Native American tribes used the western chokecherry for making jams and medicinal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - a North American wild cherry tree with astringent fruit
