locking pliers

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkɪŋ ˈplaɪɚz / (AmE), / ˈlɒkɪŋ ˈplaɪəz / (BrE)
A type of pliers designed with a locking mechanism that allows them to be clamped and held in place without constant pressure from the user.
pliers that lock in place

Examples

He used his locking pliers to grip the rusted bolt securely.
Make sure the locking pliers are tightly clamped before you start turning.
Locking pliers can be a lifesaver when you need an extra pair of hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vice grips - another term for locking pliers, especially in North America
