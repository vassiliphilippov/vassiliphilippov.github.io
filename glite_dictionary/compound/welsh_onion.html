Welsh onion

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
A species of onion (Allium fistulosum) with slender bulbs, commonly used as green onions in cooking.
species of onion with slender bulbs

Examples

She sprinkled chopped Welsh onion on top of the soup for extra flavor.
In many Asian dishes, Welsh onion is preferred over ordinary onions for its milder taste.
He grows Welsh onion in his garden every spring for use in salads and stir-fries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring onion - young onion with a small bulb, used in salads
  • Synonym: scallion - a long-necked onion, used as a green vegetable

Etymology

Despite the name, it is not from Wales; 'Welsh' in this sense comes from an Old English word meaning 'foreign.'
