chief of staff

1. NOUN

/ tʃiːf əv stæf / (AmE), / tʃiːf əv stɑːf / (BrE)
the senior officer in charge of a branch of the armed forces, responsible for overseeing operations and administration
senior military officer in charge of a service branch

Examples

The president met with the chief of staff to discuss military strategy.
General Martin was appointed as the new chief of staff of the army last year.
It is the responsibility of the chief of staff to coordinate all branches of the service.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: commander in chief - the supreme commander of a nation's armed forces

Etymology

From 'chief' (leader) and 'staff' (group of officers assisting a commander).
