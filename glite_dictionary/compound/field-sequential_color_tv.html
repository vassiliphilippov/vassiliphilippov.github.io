field-sequential color TV

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld səˈkwɛn.ʃəl ˈkʌlər tiː ˈviː / (AmE), / ˈfiːld sɪˈkwen.ʃəl ˈkʌlə ˌtiː ˈviː / (BrE)
an early type of color television in which each frame is broadcast in sequence using separate fields for the three primary colors, creating a full-color image when combined rapidly by the eye.
early color TV using sequential color fields

Examples

The demonstration of a field-sequential color TV amazed the audience with its vivid images.
Early field-sequential color TV systems struggled with motion artifacts compared to later technologies.
Researchers studied field-sequential color TV to understand the challenges in early color broadcasting.
