sense of equilibrium

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛns əv iˌkwɪlɪˈbriəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛns əv iˌkwɪlɪˈbriːəm / (BrE)
the sensory system in the inner ear that detects the position and movement of the head, helping to maintain balance
system for balance in the inner ear
medical

Examples

A disturbance in the sense of equilibrium can cause dizziness and confusion.
The inner ear is crucial for maintaining our sense of equilibrium.
Pilots must have an excellent sense of equilibrium to respond to rapid changes in movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense of balance - the ability to maintain bodily equilibrium
