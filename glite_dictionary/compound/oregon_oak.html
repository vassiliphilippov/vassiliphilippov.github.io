Oregon oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrəɡən oʊk / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən əʊk / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of western North America with crooked branches and pale grey bark, also known as Garry oak.
a North American deciduous tree

Examples

The Oregon oak is a vital part of the native landscape in the Pacific Northwest.
A mature Oregon oak can live for several centuries, providing habitat for wildlife.
We hiked through a forest dominated by Oregon oak and wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Garry oak - Another name for Oregon oak
