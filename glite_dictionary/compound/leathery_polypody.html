leathery polypody

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðəri pəˈlɪpədi / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəri pəˈlɪpədi / (BrE)
a stiff, leathery-leaved fern of western North America, with ovate fronds divided to the midrib
a stiff, leathery-leaved western North American fern

Examples

The rugged hillsides were dotted with clusters of leathery polypody thriving in the shade.
Hikers often spot leathery polypody along woodland trails in California.
This species, known as leathery polypody, easily withstands dry and rocky environments.
