call girl

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔl ˌɡɝl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːl ˌɡɜːl / (BrE)
A female prostitute who is hired by clients via telephone, rather than working on the street or in brothels.
female prostitute available by phone

Examples

The wealthy businessman was arrested for hiring a call girl during his trip to the city.
She led a double life as a university student by day and a call girl by night.
The documentary explored the lives of call girls working in major metropolitan areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - a person, typically a woman, who is hired to accompany someone socially, sometimes with sexual services implied

2. NOUN

/ kɔl ɡɝl / (AmE), / kɔːl ɡɜːl / (BrE)
a woman who is paid to have sex with clients, often arranging meetings by telephone or online rather than soliciting on the street
female sex worker who arranges clients by phone or online
informal

Examples

The detective suspected the woman was a call girl involved in a larger operation.
Many call girls arrange meetings with their clients online these days.
He read a novel about a young call girl trying to start a new life.

Synset Examples

He was shocked to discover she was a call girl.
She shared her experience as a call girl in the city.
In the story, the protagonist meets a call girl unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a person who engages in sexual activity for payment
  • Synonym: escort - a person who is paid to spend time with clients, sometimes for sex

Etymology

From 'call' (referring to arranging appointments by telephone) + 'girl' (woman), first attested mid-20th century.
