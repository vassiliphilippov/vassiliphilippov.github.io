Mayan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of indigenous languages spoken by the Maya peoples of Mesoamerica, primarily in southern Mexico, Guatemala, and Belize
family of indigenous languages of the Maya

Examples

Archaeologists often study ancient texts written in the Mayan language.
There are still many speakers of Mayan language varieties in Guatemala today.
The Mayan language family has a complex system of hieroglyphic writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mayan - relating to the Maya people, culture, or language

Etymology

From Maya, the name of the indigenous Mesoamerican people.
