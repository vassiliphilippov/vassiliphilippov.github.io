book-length

1. ADJ

/ bʊk lɛŋkθ / (AmE), / bʊk lɛŋkθ / (BrE)
having a length typical of a book; as long or nearly as long as a standard book
of a length typical of a book

Examples

The committee rejected his book-length manuscript because it was too detailed for their journal.
Her master's thesis was almost book-length, surprising her advisor with its depth.
Many modern biographies are book-length, offering exhaustive coverage of their subjects.

Synset Examples

I prefer short stories over book-length novels.
The novel was surprisingly book-length for a debut.
Her essay was nearly book-length and very detailed.
