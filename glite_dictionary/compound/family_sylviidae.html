family Sylviidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪlˈvɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪlˈvɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small insectivorous songbirds, commonly known as Old World warblers, and related species.
the family of Old World warblers (birds)

Examples

The family Sylviidae includes many species of Old World warblers.
Recent studies propose that kinglets and gnatcatchers may be related to the family Sylviidae.
Birdwatchers often classify certain warblers within the family Sylviidae based on their song and plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird often found in the family Sylviidae

Etymology

From New Latin Sylviidae, from genus Sylvia + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
