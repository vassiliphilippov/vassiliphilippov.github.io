mass spectroscopy

1. NOUN

/ mæs spɛkˈtrɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / mæs spɛkˈtrɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the use of spectroscopy to determine the masses of small electrically charged particles
analytical method for measuring particle masses
medical

Examples

The laboratory specializes in mass spectroscopy to analyze complex mixtures of proteins.
By using mass spectroscopy, researchers can identify trace elements in chemical compounds.
She wrote her thesis on the application of mass spectroscopy in environmental science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass spectrometry - the analytical technique for determining masses of particles, often used synonymously with 'mass spectroscopy'

Etymology

Formed from 'mass' and 'spectroscopy', the latter from Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image) and Greek 'skopein' (to look at).
