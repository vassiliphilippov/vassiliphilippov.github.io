yellow sweet clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ swiːt ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ swiːt ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A Eurasian biennial plant with yellow flowers and aromatic leaves, used as a carminative or flavoring agent, and often cultivated as green manure or a cover crop.
a yellow-flowered Eurasian clover used as green manure

Examples

Farmers often plant yellow sweet clover to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
The aromatic leaves of yellow sweet clover are sometimes used to flavor certain foods.
Bees are attracted to the yellow sweet clover when it blooms in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet clover - a group of plants in the genus Melilotus with fragrant foliage
