fluid drachm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪd dræm / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪd dræm / (BrE)
a unit of capacity in the apothecary system equal to one eighth of a fluid ounce, formerly used in pharmacy
apothecary unit of liquid measure
archaic medical

Examples

The old prescription called for one fluid drachm of tincture to be mixed with water.
Doses were measured in fluid drachms before the metric system became standard.
A fluid drachm is equivalent to approximately 3.7 milliliters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluid dram - alternative spelling for the same apothecary measure
  • Degree Higher: fluid ounce - a unit of liquid capacity in the apothecary system

Etymology

From 'fluid' and Greek 'drakhmē' (a unit of weight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪd dræm / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪd dræm / (BrE)
a unit of capacity in the British imperial system, equal to 60 minims or 3.5516 cubic centimeters, used in measuring liquids, especially in pharmacy
British imperial liquid measure, 3.5516 ml
british

Examples

The pharmacist carefully measured one fluid drachm of the medicine for the prescription.
In old British recipes, ingredients might be listed in fluid drachms rather than millilitres.
He was puzzled by the instruction to use a fluid drachm of tincture, as the unit was unfamiliar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluid dram - alternative spelling for the same liquid measure

Etymology

From 'fluid' (liquid) + 'drachm', from Latin 'drachma', from Greek 'drakhmē' (a weight or coin).
