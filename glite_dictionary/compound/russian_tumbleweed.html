Russian tumbleweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃən ˈtʌmbəlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃən ˈtʌmbəlwiːd / (BrE)
A prickly, bushy plant native to Eurasia that is a troublesome weed in central and western United States, known for its tumbleweed motion.
prickly weed plant; forms tumbling masses

Examples

The Russian tumbleweed quickly took over large areas of farmland, making it difficult to cultivate crops.
Many western movies feature scenes with a Russian tumbleweed rolling across the dusty landscape.
As autumn winds picked up, clusters of Russian tumbleweeds blew across the deserted highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumbleweed - plant whose rounded bushy form is blown by the wind
