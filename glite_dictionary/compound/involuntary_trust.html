involuntary trust

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɑːlənˌtɛri trʌst / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlənˌtɛri trʌst / (BrE)
A trust imposed by a court, regardless of the parties' intentions, to benefit someone who has been wrongfully deprived of their rights.
court-imposed trust for remedial purposes
legal

Examples

The court imposed an involuntary trust to prevent the company from profiting from its wrongful actions.
When property was acquired fraudulently, an involuntary trust ensured the rightful owner was protected.
An involuntary trust may be created to remedy cases where a party is unjustly enriched.
