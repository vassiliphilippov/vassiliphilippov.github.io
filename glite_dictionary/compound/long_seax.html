long seax

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ˈseɪ.æks / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈsiː.æks / (BrE)
a type of large seax characterized by a long hilt and a blade 50 cm or longer, often pattern-welded and featuring multiple fullers and grooves
large historical Germanic knife or sword

Examples

The warrior displayed a finely crafted long seax at his belt during the ceremony.
Archaeologists uncovered a long seax with distinct pattern welding and several fullers.
Collectors often seek out a long seax due to its historical significance and unique blade design.

Synset Examples

The edge of a long seax is generally straight, or curved slightly towards the tip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seax - a short sword or large knife used by the Germanic peoples

Etymology

Seax from Old English 'seax' meaning ‘knife’ or ‘sword’.
