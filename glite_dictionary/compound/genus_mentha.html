genus Mentha

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmɛn.θə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmɛn.θə / (BrE)
a group of aromatic plants in the mint family, commonly known as mints, including species such as spearmint and peppermint
group of plants including mints

Examples

The genus Mentha contains well-known herbs such as spearmint and peppermint.
Several species within the genus Mentha are used for their aromatic leaves in cooking and medicine.
Scientists are studying the genus Mentha for potential medicinal benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - an aromatic herb used for flavoring, belonging to the genus Mentha

Etymology

Mentha: from Latin *mentha*, from Greek *minthē*, meaning mint (the plant).
