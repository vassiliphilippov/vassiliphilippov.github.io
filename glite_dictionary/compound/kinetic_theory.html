kinetic theory

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈθiəri / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
The scientific theory that gases are composed of small particles moving in random motion, explaining properties such as pressure and temperature.
theory that gases consist of moving particles

Examples

The kinetic theory explains why heating a gas increases its pressure.
Many physics courses introduce the kinetic theory to describe the behavior of molecules in a gas.
According to the kinetic theory, gas particles are in constant and random motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinetic theory of gases - the scientific theory specifically applied to gases

Etymology

From Greek *kinētikos* (relating to motion) and English 'theory'; 'kinetic' from Greek *kinein* (to move).
