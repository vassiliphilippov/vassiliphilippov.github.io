undivided interest

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dəˈvaɪdɪd ˈɪn.trəst / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈvaɪdɪd ˈɪn.trəst / (BrE)
a share in property ownership where each owner has equal rights to the whole property, not just a specific part
equal ownership right to entire property
legal

Examples

Each heir received an undivided interest in the family farm after their mother passed away.
As tenants in common, the siblings held an undivided interest in the apartment building.
Selling an undivided interest can be complex since all owners must agree to transfer the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint tenancy - a type of ownership where two or more people hold equal shares to the whole property and right of survivorship
  • Antonym: tenancy in common - a type of ownership where each owner has a distinct, divisible share of property
