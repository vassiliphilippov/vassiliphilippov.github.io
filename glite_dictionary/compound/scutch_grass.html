scutch grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌtʃ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈskʌtʃ ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A creeping grass native to Europe and now widely found in warm regions; commonly used for lawns and pastures, especially in the southern United States and India.
a trailing grass used for lawns and pastures

Examples

Scutch grass is often used on golf courses because it forms a dense, resilient turf.
Farmers sometimes struggle to remove scutch grass from their fields due to its rapid spread.
Lawn care professionals recommend specific herbicides to control scutch grass infestations.

Etymology

Possibly from 'scutch', an old word meaning to beat, possibly relating to how the plant is removed due to its invasive nature.
