intercontinental ballistic missile

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərkənˌtɪnəˈnɛntl bəˈlɪstɪk ˈmɪsaɪl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkɒnˌtɪnəˈnɛntl bəˈlɪstɪk ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
a missile with a ballistic trajectory capable of traveling from one continent to another, typically designed for delivering nuclear warheads.
long-range missile between continents

Examples

The country successfully launched an intercontinental ballistic missile during the test.
Many nations consider intercontinental ballistic missiles as part of their nuclear deterrent strategy.
Engineers improved the guidance systems of the intercontinental ballistic missile to increase its accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile - a weapon that is forcibly propelled at a target

Etymology

Ballistic from Greek 'ballistikos', and missile from Latin 'missilis' (that may be thrown).
