pocketed freetail bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪtɪd ˈfriˌteɪl bæt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪtɪd ˈfriːteɪl bæt / (BrE)
a small, brown bat found in California and northern Mexico, known for the distinctive pockets near its tail
species of small brown bat native to California and northern Mexico

Examples

The pocketed freetail bat is rarely seen outside of its native habitat in California.
Researchers identified a pocketed freetail bat during their nighttime survey of the area.
Unlike other bats, the pocketed freetail bat has small pockets near its tail membrane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal with leathery wings
