primary coil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri kɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri kɔɪl / (BrE)
the coil in an electrical circuit in which a varying current produces a magnetic field that induces a current in a nearby secondary coil, typically in transformers.
coil inducing current in a transformer

Examples

The engineer measured the voltage across the primary coil to ensure proper operation of the transformer.
When alternating current passes through the primary coil, it creates a magnetic field that affects the secondary coil.
If the primary coil is damaged, the transformer will not be able to transfer energy efficiently.

Synset Examples

current through the primary coil induces current in the secondary coil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary coil - the coil in which induced current is produced by the primary coil in a transformer

Etymology

From 'primary' (first or original) and 'coil' (looped wire), used in electromagnetism.
