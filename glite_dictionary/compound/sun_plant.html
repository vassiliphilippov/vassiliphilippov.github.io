sun plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌn ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈsʌn ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a widely grown ornamental plant with succulent, moss-like foliage and abundant, brightly colored flowers, typically of the genus Portulaca
ornamental plant with bright flowers

Examples

The sun plant brightened the garden with its vibrant blooms all summer long.
Many gardeners use sun plants as ground cover due to their drought tolerance and colorful appearance.
She planted a row of sun plants along the edge of the walkway for an eye-catching display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portulaca - a genus of flowering plants often called purslane or sun plant
