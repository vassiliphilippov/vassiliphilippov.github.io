order Exocycloida

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊrdər ˌɛksoʊsaɪˈklɔɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌɛksəʊsaɪˈklɔɪdə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order comprising flat sea urchins distinguished by their morphology.
order of flat sea urchins

Examples

The order Exocycloida includes several species of flattened sea urchins found in deep ocean habitats.
Marine biologists are studying the evolutionary adaptations of the order Exocycloida to better understand echinoid diversity.
Fossils attributed to the order Exocycloida have been discovered in Cretaceous geological formations.

Etymology

From Greek 'exo' (outside) + 'kyklos' (circle), used in New Latin taxonomy.
