imported input

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɔrtɪd ˈɪnpʊt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtɪd ˈɪnpʊt / (BrE)
a material, component, or resource brought from another country to be used in the production of goods or services
material or component sourced from abroad for production

Examples

Many factories rely on imported input to keep their production lines running smoothly.
The smartphone industry sources a significant portion of its components as imported input.
Rising tariffs increased the cost of imported input for local manufacturers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: import - to bring goods or services into a country from abroad
  • Antonym: exported input - a material, component, or resource sent to another country for use in production
