southern white cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn waɪt ˈsiːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən waɪt ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a slow-growing, medium-sized cedar tree native to the east coast of the United States, resembling the American arborvitae
a cedar tree of eastern United States

Examples

The swamp was dominated by ancient southern white cedar trees.
Many homeowners choose southern white cedar for its resistance to decay.
We identified the towering southern white cedar by its scaly leaves and fibrous bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic white cedar - a similar cedar tree growing in eastern North America
  • Degree Higher: northern white cedar - a related cedar tree found in more northern regions of North America
