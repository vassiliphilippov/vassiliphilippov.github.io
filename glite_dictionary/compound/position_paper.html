position paper

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzɪʃ.ən ˌpeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / pəˈzɪʃ.ən ˌpeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a written document that states an organization's or individual's stance or perspective on a particular issue, often used to summarize agreed points, especially in diplomatic or policy contexts
written statement of an official stance

Examples

The ambassador presented a position paper outlining his country's response to the proposed agreement.
Each committee was asked to submit a position paper before the international conference began.
The government released a position paper explaining its policy on climate change.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈzɪʃ.ən ˈpeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / pəˈzɪʃ.ən ˈpeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a written report that outlines and argues for a particular policy or viewpoint, often used to explain, justify, or recommend a course of action
report stating and justifying a policy or stance

Examples

The committee submitted a position paper to clarify their stance on climate change.
Students were asked to write a position paper on the ethics of artificial intelligence.
Her position paper effectively convinced the board to adopt stricter safety measures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: policy statement - a formal declaration of an organization's position on a specific matter
