domoic acid

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈmoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / dəʊˈməʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a neurotoxin produced by certain marine algae, which is poisonous to humans and animals
poisonous neurotoxin from marine algae
medical

Examples

Shellfish contaminated with domoic acid can cause serious illness if consumed by humans.
Scientists are monitoring coastal waters for increased levels of domoic acid produced by algal blooms.
Symptoms of poisoning from domoic acid include memory loss and seizures.

Etymology

From Japanese ‘domoi’ (the seaweed genus Dōmoe) + English ‘acid’.
