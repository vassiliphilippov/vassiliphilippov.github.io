large white

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ waɪt / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ waɪt / (BrE)
A large, common, mainly white Eurasian butterfly (Pieris brassicae), whose larvae are a pest of brassica crops.
a species of white Eurasian butterfly

Examples

The large white is a familiar sight in European gardens during the summer months.
Farmers often consider the large white a pest due to its larvae feeding on cabbages.
She photographed a large white resting on a kale leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage white - another name for the large white butterfly
