prickly pear cactus

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli pɛr ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli peə ˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A cactus with flat, spiny segments and oval, often edible fruit, especially of the genus Opuntia, found in warm regions.
a cactus with flat, spiny joints and edible fruit

Examples

The prickly pear cactus thrives in arid regions and is commonly found in the deserts of the Americas.
Ranchers sometimes feed the fruit of the prickly pear cactus to livestock during droughts.
She carefully removed the spines before slicing the prickly pear cactus fruit for dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a plant with thick, fleshy stems adapted to store water
