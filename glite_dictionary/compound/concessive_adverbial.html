concessive adverbial

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛsɪv ædˈvɝːbiəl / (AmE), / kənˈsɛsɪv ədˈvɜːbɪəl / (BrE)
An adverbial word or phrase that introduces a contrast, implying something unexpected or contrary to the main part of the sentence.
adverbial expressing contrast or concession

Examples

Although it was raining, the concessive adverbial shows that the picnic continued as planned.
'Even though' is commonly used as a concessive adverbial in English sentences.
The student struggled to identify the concessive adverbial in the complex sentence.

Synset Examples

A number of them deal with marked forms within the verb phrase, but at least downtoners, concessive adverbials and pied piping constructions do not fall in this category.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverbial - a word or phrase functioning like an adverb

Etymology

From 'concessive', from Latin concedere (to concede), and 'adverbial', from Latin adverbium (adverb).
