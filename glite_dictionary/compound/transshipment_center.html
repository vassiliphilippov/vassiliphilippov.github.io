transshipment center

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈʃɪpmənt ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / trænzˈʃɪpmənt ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
A port where goods can be imported and then exported without paying import duties.
port for duty-free import and export of goods

Examples

Singapore is a major transshipment center for goods moving between Asia and the rest of the world.
Many shipping companies use Rotterdam as a transshipment center to distribute cargo across Europe.
Becoming a transshipment center boosted the city's economic growth and international profile.

Synset Examples

Bahrain has been an entrepot of trade between Arabia and India since the second millennium BC

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrepot - a port or city where goods are imported, stored, and exported
