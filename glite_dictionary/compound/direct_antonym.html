direct antonym

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈæn.təˌnɪm / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈæn.tə.nɪm / (BrE)
a word that means the exact opposite of another word in a direct and core sense, such as 'wet' and 'dry'
a word with the exact opposite meaning

Examples

'Tall' and 'short' are often cited as a classic pair of direct antonyms.
In language studies, understanding direct antonyms helps clarify meaning and context.
The teacher asked students to list some common direct antonyms they encounter in everyday speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antonym - a word with the opposite meaning to another word
  • Antonym: synonym - a word with the same or nearly the same meaning as another word
  • Degree Lower: gradable antonym - an antonym pair that admits intermediate degrees (e.g., 'hot'/'cold')

Etymology

Formed from 'direct' (from Latin directus) + 'antonym' (from Greek anti- 'against' + -onym 'name').
