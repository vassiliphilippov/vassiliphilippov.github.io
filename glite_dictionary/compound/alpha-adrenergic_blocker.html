alpha-adrenergic blocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə əˌdrɛnərdʒɪk ˈblɑkər / (AmE), / ˈælfə əˌdrɛnəˈdʒɪk ˈblɒkə / (BrE)
A drug that blocks alpha-adrenergic receptors, used to treat conditions such as high blood pressure and benign prostatic hyperplasia by relaxing certain muscles.
drug blocking alpha-adrenergic receptors
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an alpha-adrenergic blocker to help relieve his symptoms of an enlarged prostate.
Some patients with high blood pressure may benefit from taking an alpha-adrenergic blocker.
Research into new alpha-adrenergic blockers is ongoing to improve treatment options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta-blocker - drug that blocks beta-adrenergic receptors

Etymology

Formed from 'alpha' (the first letter of Greek alphabet), 'adrenergic' (relating to adrenaline), and 'blocker' (something that blocks).
