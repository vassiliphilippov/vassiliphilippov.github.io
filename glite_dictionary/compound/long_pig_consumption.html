long pig consumption

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ pɪɡ kənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / lɒŋ pɪɡ kənˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act or practice of eating human flesh, used as a euphemism for cannibalism
euphemism for cannibalism

Examples

Anthropologists have studied historical instances of long pig consumption in remote island cultures.
The explorer's diary contained chilling descriptions of long pig consumption.
The taboo surrounding long pig consumption remains strong in modern society.

Synset Examples

Stories of long pig consumption are often sensationalized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannibalism - the practice of eating the flesh of one's own species; in particular, humans eating humans

Etymology

From 'long pig,' a slang term reportedly used in Pacific Islander pidgin, referring to human flesh as food.
