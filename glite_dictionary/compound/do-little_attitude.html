do-little attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈduˌlɪtəl ˈætɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈduːˌlɪtl ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a tendency to be lazy or to avoid making an effort
tendency toward laziness

Examples

Her do-little attitude made it difficult for the group to finish the project on time.
A do-little attitude in the workplace often results in poor performance and low morale.
The coach warned the players against adopting a do-little attitude during practice.

Synset Examples

His do-little attitude frustrated the entire team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laziness - lack of willingness to work or use energy
  • Antonym: work ethic - principle that hard work is valuable and important
