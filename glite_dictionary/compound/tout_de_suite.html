tout de suite

1. ADV

/ tuː də ˈswit / (AmE), / tuː də ˈswiːt / (BrE)
immediately; at once; without delay
immediately; at once

Examples

The manager asked for the documents tout de suite, leaving no time to prepare.
If you want a seat, you must reserve tout de suite or they will be sold out.
When the alarm rang, everyone evacuated the building tout de suite.

Synset Examples

I need your answer tout de suite to proceed.
Please send me the report tout de suite.
She left for the airport tout de suite after getting the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without any delay
  • Synonym: right away - at once; immediately
  • Antonym: later - at a time in the future; not now

Etymology

From French 'tout de suite' (literally 'all at once').
