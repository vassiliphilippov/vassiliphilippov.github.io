outrigger canoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌrɪɡər kəˈnu / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌrɪɡə kəˈnuː / (BrE)
a type of narrow canoe with one or more lateral support floats (outriggers) fastened to one or both sides for stability, especially used in the South Pacific and other tropical regions.
a canoe with a stabilizing outrigger float

Examples

The fishermen paddled their outrigger canoe across the lagoon at sunrise.
Tourists were given a chance to ride in a traditional Hawaiian outrigger canoe.
An outrigger canoe is known for its stability in choppy waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canoe - a lightweight narrow boat pointed at both ends and open on top, propelled by one or more paddles

Etymology

From 'outrigger' (from English 'out' + 'rig') and 'canoe' (from Spanish 'canoa', from Taíno).
