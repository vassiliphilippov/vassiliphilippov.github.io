ambulatory plague

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbjəˌleɪtɔri ˈpleɪɡ / (AmE), / ˌambjʊˈleɪtəri ˈpleɪɡ / (BrE)
A mild form of bubonic plague in which the patient is able to walk about; a less severe manifestation of the disease.
mild form of bubonic plague
medical

Examples

The doctor recognized the symptoms as those of ambulatory plague rather than the more severe form.
During the outbreak, several patients were diagnosed with ambulatory plague and recovered quickly.
Unlike its deadly counterpart, ambulatory plague allows sufferers to remain active and mobile.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bubonic plague - a severe infectious disease caused by Yersinia pestis

Etymology

From 'ambulatory' (from Latin 'ambulare', to walk) and 'plague' (from Latin 'plaga', stroke or wound).
