umbrella fern

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbɹɛl.ə fɝn / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɹɛl.ə fɜːn / (BrE)
a large fern native to Australasia, noted for its fan-shaped, repeatedly forked fronds; sometimes classified in the genus Gleichenia
large Australasian fern with fanlike fronds

Examples

The umbrella fern is a striking addition to any greenhouse collection.
Many hikers in New Zealand encounter the distinctive fronds of the umbrella fern along forest trails.
Botanists sometimes classify the umbrella fern within the genus Gleichenia due to its unique foliage.
