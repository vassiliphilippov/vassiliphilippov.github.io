chromosomal mutation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊ.məˈsoʊ.məl mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊ.məˈsəʊ.məl mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a change in the structure or number of chromosomes, resulting in an alteration of the genetic material of an organism
change in chromosome structure or number
medical

Examples

A chromosomal mutation can lead to genetic disorders such as Down syndrome.
Scientists discovered a rare chromosomal mutation in the population that affected eye color.
Some cancers are associated with specific chromosomal mutations that disrupt cell regulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gene mutation - change in the sequence of a single gene

Etymology

From chromosome (from Greek khrōma 'color' + sōma 'body') and mutation (from Latin mutatio 'change').
