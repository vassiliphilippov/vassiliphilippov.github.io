U.S. Mint

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː ˈɛs mɪnt / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˈɛs mɪnt / (BrE)
the government agency responsible for producing and distributing coins of the United States, as well as processing precious metals
United States coin-manufacturing agency
american

Examples

The U.S. Mint has released a new series of commemorative coins this year.
All circulating coins in the United States are produced by the U.S. Mint.
The U.S. Mint also manages large reserves of gold and silver bullion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Royal Mint - the official mint of the United Kingdom

Etymology

U.S., abbreviation of United States, plus mint, from Old English mynet (coin, money).
