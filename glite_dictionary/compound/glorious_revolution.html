Glorious Revolution

1. PROPN

/ ɡlɔːriəs ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːriəs ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the overthrow of King James II of England in 1688–89 by William of Orange, which established parliamentary supremacy and limited monarchy in Britain
the 1688–89 overthrow of James II in England

Examples

Historians often debate the long-term consequences of the Glorious Revolution for modern constitutional democracy.
The Glorious Revolution marked the end of absolute monarchy in England and the rise of parliamentary power.
Many textbooks highlight the Glorious Revolution as a turning point in British and European history.

Etymology

Named for its perceived lack of violence and the establishment of constitutional monarchy, from 'glorious' (splendid, honorable) and 'revolution' (from Latin revolutio).
