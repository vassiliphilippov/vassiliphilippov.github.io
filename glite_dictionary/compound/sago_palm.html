sago palm

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪɡoʊ pɑm / (AmE), / ˈseɪɡəʊ pɑːm / (BrE)
a dwarf, palm-like cycad native to Japan, known for yielding sago starch
dwarf Japanese cycad yielding sago

Examples

The sago palm is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens despite its slow growth.
Starch extracted from the sago palm is an important food source in some cultures.
Although called a palm, the sago palm is actually a type of cycad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - tropical or subtropical plant with a stout trunk and large leaves

Etymology

Sago from Malay *sagu* (a starch), palm from Latin *palma* (palm tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪɡoʊ ˌpɑm / (AmE), / ˈseɪɡəʊ ˌpɑːm / (BrE)
any of several tropical Asian palm trees whose trunks yield sago, a starchy food substance
tropical Asian palm tree yielding sago

Examples

The trunk of the sago palm is harvested to extract sago for cooking.
Many traditional Asian dishes use starch from the sago palm as a thickener.
The sago palm thrives in tropical climates and is an important source of food.

Etymology

From Malay 'sagu', the name for the starch, plus 'palm'.
