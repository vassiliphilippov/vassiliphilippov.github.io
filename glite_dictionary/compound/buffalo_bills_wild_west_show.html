Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəloʊ bɪlz waɪld wɛst ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ bɪlz waɪld west ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a famous American traveling show, organized in 1883 by William F. Cody, featuring displays of horseback riding, marksmanship, and reenactments of frontier life; it toured widely in the United States and Europe
19th-century American traveling show by Buffalo Bill

Examples

Many historians credit Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show with popularizing the myths of the American frontier.
During its European tour, Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show drew huge crowds eager to see the spectacle.
Annie Oakley rose to fame as a sharpshooter in Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show.

Etymology

Named after its creator, William F. Cody, known as 'Buffalo Bill', and the popular imagery of the American 'Wild West'.
