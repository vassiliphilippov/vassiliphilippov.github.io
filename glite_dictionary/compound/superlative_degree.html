superlative degree

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɜrlətɪv dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / sjuːˈpɜːlətɪv dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
the form of an adjective or adverb that expresses the highest or most extreme degree of a quality, typically formed by adding '-est', 'most', or 'least'
highest degree of adjective or adverb

Examples

In the sentence 'She is the smartest student in the class,' 'smartest' is in the superlative degree.
The superlative degree of 'good' is 'best.'
When describing three or more items, we often use the superlative degree to highlight the most extreme option.

Synset Examples

‘fastest’ is the superlative of the adjective ‘fast’
‘least famous’ is the superlative degree of the adjective ‘famous’
‘most surely’ is the superlative of the adverb ‘surely’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comparative degree - the form of an adjective or adverb that compares two things

Etymology

From Latin superlativus (exaggerated, utmost), from super (above) + latus (carried).
