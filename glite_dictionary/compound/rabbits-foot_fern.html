rabbit's-foot fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪts fʊt fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈræbɪts fʊt fɜːn / (BrE)
a tropical fern with brown, scaly rhizomes, cultivated for its large, deeply lobed bluish-green fronds, native to the Americas
tropical fern with scaly rhizomes and bluish-green fronds

Examples

The rabbit's-foot fern is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and furry rhizomes.
She placed a rabbit's-foot fern on her windowsill to add a touch of greenery to the room.
Gardeners often admire the unique creeping rhizomes of the rabbit's-foot fern.
