aqueous humour

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪkwiəs ˈhjuːmər / (AmE), / ˈeɪkwɪəs ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
the clear, watery fluid that fills the space between the cornea and the lens of the eye
clear fluid in the eye between cornea and lens
medical british

Examples

The aqueous humour maintains the intraocular pressure in a healthy eye.
An imbalance in the production or drainage of aqueous humour can lead to glaucoma.
The surgeon carefully checked the clarity of the aqueous humour during the eye procedure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vitreous humour - the clear gel that fills the space between the lens and the retina of the eyeball

Etymology

From Latin 'aqueous' (watery) and 'humour' (fluid, from Latin 'humor')
