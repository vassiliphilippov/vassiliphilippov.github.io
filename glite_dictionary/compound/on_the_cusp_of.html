on the cusp of

1. ADP

/ ɑn ðə kʌsp ʌv / (AmE), / ɒn ðə kʌsp ɒv / (BrE)
at the point of transition from one state to another, or very close to achieving something significant
at the point of transition or verge of change

Examples

The company is on the cusp of launching a revolutionary new product.
Many scientists believe we are on the cusp of major medical breakthroughs in the next decade.
After years of preparation, she finally felt on the cusp of success.

Synset Examples

He stood on the cusp of a new adventure.
He felt he was on the cusp of adulthood.
The team is on the cusp of winning the championship.
They are on the cusp of a major breakthrough.
The project is on the cusp of completion.
She felt on the cusp of something big happening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the verge of - very close to experiencing something or being in a new situation
  • Synonym: on the brink of - about to experience something, often something significant or dangerous

Etymology

From 'cusp', meaning 'point or edge', originally from Latin 'cuspis' (point, tip).
