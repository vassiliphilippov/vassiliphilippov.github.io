class Taxopsida

1. NOUN

/ klæs tæksˈɒpsɪdə / (AmE), / klɑːs tækˈsɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
a class of gymnosperms comprising the yews, used in some taxonomic systems
a plant class of yews (Taxaceae)
medical

Examples

The class Taxopsida encompasses all species of yews found worldwide.
Some older classifications of gymnosperms include the class Taxopsida as a major division.
Botanists often debate whether class Taxopsida should be considered a separate group from other conifers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxaceae - the yew family of conifers
  • Degree Higher: Gymnospermophyta - the division of seed-producing plants that includes conifers, cycads, and ginkgoes

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Taxus' (the genus of yews) and the suffix '-opsida' (used for plant classes).
