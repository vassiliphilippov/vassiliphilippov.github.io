rheumatic fever

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmætɪk ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ruːˈmatɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a serious disease, mainly affecting children, involving painful inflammation of the joints and often causing lasting damage to the heart valves
disease causing joint and heart inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with rheumatic fever after she developed joint pain and a persistent fever.
Early treatment of strep throat can prevent rheumatic fever in children.
He was hospitalized as a child for complications related to rheumatic fever.

Etymology

From 'rheumatic' (from Greek *rheumatikos*: pertaining to flux or flow) and 'fever'.
