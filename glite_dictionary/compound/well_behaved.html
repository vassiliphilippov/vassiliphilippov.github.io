well behaved

1. ADJ

/ wɛl bɪˈheɪvd / (AmE), / wɛl bɪˈheɪvd / (BrE)
(especially of children) behaving in a polite or proper way, as expected or desired by adults or authorities.
behaving in a polite or proper way

Examples

The teacher praised the well behaved students after the assembly.
We were pleasantly surprised by how well behaved their dog was during the visit.
Parents often worry that their children won't be well behaved at birthday parties.

Synset Examples

a well-behaved child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ill behaved - behaving in a rude or improper way
  • Synonym: polite - having or showing good manners and respect for others
