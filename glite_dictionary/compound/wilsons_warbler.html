Wilson's warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlsənz ˈwɔr.blɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪlsənz ˈwɔː.blə / (BrE)
A small North American wood warbler with yellow plumage and a distinctive black crown, found mainly in shrubby habitats.
small North American yellow warbler

Examples

I saw a bright yellow Wilson's warbler hopping among the shrubs near the stream.
During migration season, many birdwatchers hope to spot a Wilson's warbler in local parks.
The male Wilson's warbler is easy to identify by its small black cap and cheerful song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird known for its singing

Etymology

Named after Alexander Wilson, Scottish-American ornithologist.
