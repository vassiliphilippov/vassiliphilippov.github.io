field emission

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the release of electrons from a material's surface caused by a strong electric field, typically occurring in a vacuum
emission of electrons by strong electric field

Examples

The microscope utilizes field emission to achieve extremely high resolution imaging of samples.
Engineers studied field emission to develop more efficient display technologies.
Unwanted field emission from electrodes can interfere with sensitive electronic measurements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thermionic emission - emission of electrons from heated materials

Etymology

From 'field' (referring to electric field) + 'emission' (release), used in physics since early 20th century.
