fairy godmother

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri ˈɡɑdˌmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfeəri ˈɡɒdˌmʌðə / (BrE)
A woman in fairy tales who uses magical powers to help the protagonist or grant wishes, often bringing unexpected good fortune.
magical helper in fairy tales

Examples

In the story, the fairy godmother transformed a pumpkin into a carriage for Cinderella.
Every child wishes for a fairy godmother to grant their secret dreams.
She often acted like a fairy godmother to students in need, offering guidance and support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godmother - a woman who sponsors a child at baptism

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri ˈɡɑdˌmʌðər / (AmE), / ˈfeəri ˈɡɒdˌmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman who acts as a generous benefactor, often by providing help or support in a difficult situation
female generous benefactor

Examples

My aunt acted as a fairy godmother, supporting me through every difficult moment.
The new sponsor became a fairy godmother to the struggling art program.
Sometimes all you need is a fairy godmother to believe in your dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives money or help to a person or cause
  • Synonym: patron - a person who gives financial or other support to a person, organization, or cause

Etymology

From fairy tales, referring to magical guardians, especially in 'Cinderella'.
