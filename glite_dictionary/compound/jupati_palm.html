jupati palm

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːˈpɑːti pɑːm / (AmE), / dʒuːˈpɑːti pɑːm / (BrE)
a tall Brazilian feather palm (Raphia taedigera) with a crown of very large, leathery, divided leaves; its long, strong stems are used for structural purposes
a tall Brazilian palm with large divided leaves

Examples

The villagers used the strong stems of the jupati palm to build their shelters.
We admired the crown of leathery, arching leaves atop the jupati palm during our trek.
The baskets were expertly woven from fibers harvested from the jupati palm.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jupati', of indigenous origin, referring to the type of palm.
