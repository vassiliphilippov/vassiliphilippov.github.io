stone root

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊn ˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈstəʊn ˌruːt / (BrE)
A strong-scented, erect perennial herb with serrated, pointed leaves and loose clusters of yellowish flowers, native to eastern North America (Collinsonia canadensis).
a perennial herb native to eastern North America

Examples

The stone root blooms in late summer, attracting various pollinators to its yellowish flowers.
Herbalists value stone root for its traditional medicinal properties.
While hiking in the Appalachians, we spotted several clumps of stone root growing near the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Collinsonia canadensis - the botanical name for stone root
