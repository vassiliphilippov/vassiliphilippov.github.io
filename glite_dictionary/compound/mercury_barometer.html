mercury barometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːkjəri bəˈrɑːmətər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊri bəˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a barometer that measures atmospheric pressure by the height of a column of mercury in a glass tube
barometer using mercury column to show pressure

Examples

The scientist used a mercury barometer to monitor changes in atmospheric pressure during the experiment.
Before digital instruments, weather stations primarily relied on the mercury barometer for accurate readings.
A mercury barometer consists of a glass tube immersed in a reservoir of mercury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aneroid barometer - barometer that measures pressure without liquid
