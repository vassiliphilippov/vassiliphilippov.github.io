genus Burhinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs bɜːrˈhaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bjʊəˈhɪn.əs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Burhinidae, comprising stone curlews, a group of large wading birds.
type genus of stone curlews (Burhinidae)

Examples

The genus Burhinus includes the Eurasian stone curlew and its relatives found in various continents.
Birdwatchers often search for members of the genus Burhinus in dry grasslands and open country.
Recent studies have provided new insights into the evolutionary history of the genus Burhinus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Burhinus - the genus of stone curlews

Etymology

From Latin 'Burhinus', the scientific name assigned in taxonomy.
