arrow-grass family

1. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊ ɡræs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈærəʊ ɡrɑːs ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of monocotyledonous bog herbs in the order Najadales, including arrow-grasses (genus Triglochin) found in wet habitats
family of bog herbs (genus Triglochin)

Examples

The arrow-grass family consists of herbaceous plants commonly found in marshy areas.
Some species within the arrow-grass family can be toxic to livestock grazing in wetlands.
The botanist specialized in identifying members of the arrow-grass family across Europe.
