family Upupidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli juːˈpjuːpɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli juːˈpjuːpɪdiː / (BrE)
A small family of birds known as hoopoes, characterized by a distinctive crown of feathers.
a family of birds: hoopoes

Examples

The family Upupidae is noted for its only living member, the hoopoe.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a rare species within the family Upupidae.
Members of the family Upupidae are easily recognized by their distinctive crown of feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoopoe - a colorful bird with a prominent crest, member of Upupidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Upupidae, from Upupa (the type genus), itself from Latin upupa (hoopoe).
