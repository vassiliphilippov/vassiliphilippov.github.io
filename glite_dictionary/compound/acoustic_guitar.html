acoustic guitar

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk ɡɪˈtɑr / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk ɡɪˈtɑː / (BrE)
A guitar that produces sound acoustically without electrical amplification, using a hollow body to project the sound of vibrating strings.
guitar without electrical amplification

Examples

She prefers to play her acoustic guitar at small, intimate gatherings.
The rich tones of the acoustic guitar filled the room during the performance.
He learned to play songs on an acoustic guitar before switching to electric.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electric guitar - a guitar that requires electrical amplification to be heard at normal performance volumes

2. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk ɡɪˈtɑr / (AmE), / əˈkjuːstɪk ɡɪˈtɑː / (BrE)
A guitar that produces sound naturally through its hollow body, without electronic amplification.
guitar producing sound without amplification

Examples

He strummed his acoustic guitar by the campfire, entertaining everyone with folk songs.
She prefers the warm tone of an acoustic guitar over an electric one.
The band features both an electric and an acoustic guitar in their performances.
