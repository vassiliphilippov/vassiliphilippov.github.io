Kuiper belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪpər bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪpə bɛlt / (BrE)
A region of the Solar System beyond Neptune, containing many small, icy bodies and dwarf planets.
region past Neptune with icy bodies

Examples

Many astronomers believe the Kuiper belt is the source of some short-period comets.
Pluto is one of the largest known objects in the Kuiper belt.
Scientists use powerful telescopes to study bodies in the distant Kuiper belt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asteroid belt - region of space between Mars and Jupiter with many asteroids

Etymology

Named after Dutch-American astronomer Gerard Kuiper, who theorized its existence.
