temple orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəl ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A large, sweet, deep-orange citrus fruit that is easily peeled, grown mainly in Florida.
variety of sweet citrus fruit

Examples

The temple orange is prized for its sweetness and easy-to-peel skin.
Growers in Florida harvest temple oranges from late winter through early spring.
I bought a bag of fresh temple oranges at the farmers' market.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛm.pəl ˈɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.pəl ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a large, sweet orange grown in Florida, known for its deep orange rind and easy-to-peel skin
a sweet, easily peeled Florida orange

Examples

The temple orange is prized for its rich flavor and bright color.
She brought home a bag of temple oranges from the Florida farmers' market.
Many people enjoy a temple orange as a refreshing snack in the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - a small citrus fruit with a loose skin, similar to an orange
  • Degree Higher: orange - a round citrus fruit with a tough bright reddish-yellow rind

Etymology

Named after William Chase Temple, a Florida fruit grower.
