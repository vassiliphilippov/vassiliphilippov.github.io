Sertoli cell

1. NOUN

/ sərˈtoʊli sɛl / (AmE), / səˈtəʊli sɛl / (BrE)
an elongated cell found in the seminiferous tubules of the testis that supports and nourishes developing spermatids
supporting cell in the testis
medical

Examples

The function of the Sertoli cell is essential for the development of healthy sperm.
Damage to Sertoli cells can lead to impaired fertility in males.
Researchers are studying how Sertoli cells regulate the hormonal environment of the testes.

Etymology

Named after Enrico Sertoli, Italian physiologist (1842–1910), who first described the cell.

2. NOUN

/ sərˈtoʊli sɛl / (AmE), / səˈtəʊli sɛl / (BrE)
A type of elongated cell found in the seminiferous tubules of the testis, responsible for supporting and nourishing developing sperm cells.
cell in testes that nourishes sperm
medical

Examples

The Sertoli cell plays a crucial role in supporting developing sperm within the seminiferous tubules.
Damage to the Sertoli cell can impair male fertility by disrupting sperm development.
Researchers are studying how hormones influence the function of the Sertoli cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leydig cell - a cell in the testis that produces testosterone

Etymology

Named after Enrico Sertoli, Italian physiologist (1842–1910), who first described the cell.
