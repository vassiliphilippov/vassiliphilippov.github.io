indoleacetic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndoʊləˌsiːtɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊliːəˌsiːtɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a plant hormone that promotes the elongation of stems and roots, important in regulating plant growth
plant growth hormone
medical

Examples

Researchers measured the levels of indoleacetic acid in the seedlings to determine its effect on growth.
Many commercial rooting powders contain indoleacetic acid to encourage root formation in cuttings.
The production of indoleacetic acid is crucial for normal development of leaves and stems in plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxin - a class of plant hormones that includes indoleacetic acid

Etymology

From 'indole' (a chemical compound) and 'acetic acid' (a simple carboxylic acid), referring to its molecular structure.
