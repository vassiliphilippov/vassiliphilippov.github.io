safety valve

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti ˌvælv / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ˌvɑːlv / (BrE)
A device on a container or system that automatically releases pressure when it becomes too high, to prevent accidents or explosions.
device that releases excess pressure automatically

Examples

The safety valve released steam automatically when the pressure in the boiler became too high.
Engineers check the safety valve regularly to maintain safe operating conditions.
Without a functioning safety valve, the system could explode under excess pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure relief valve - a valve that relieves pressure, especially in engineering contexts

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti vælv / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti vɑːlv / (BrE)
A device that automatically releases pressure in a system when it becomes too high, or a means of relieving excess emotion or stress.
device or means to relieve pressure or stress

Examples

The safety valve released steam to prevent the boiler from exploding.
Taking regular exercise acts as a safety valve for many people struggling with stress.
The government introduced new policies as a safety valve for public dissatisfaction.

Synset Examples

The pressure gauge acts as a safety valve in machinery.
A good relationship can serve as a safety valve for stress.
A safety-valve is crucial for any high-pressure system.
They installed a safety-valve to prevent accidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief valve - a device for letting out excess pressure from a system
