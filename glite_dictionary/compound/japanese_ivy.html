Japanese ivy

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs ˈaɪvi / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈaɪvi / (BrE)
An Asiatic climbing vine with three-lobed leaves and purple berries, often grown as an ornamental plant.
Asiatic climbing vine with lobed leaves and berries

Examples

The wall of the old building was completely covered in Japanese ivy.
Gardeners often choose Japanese ivy for its attractive foliage and fast growth.
In autumn, the leaves of Japanese ivy turn a brilliant shade of red before falling.
