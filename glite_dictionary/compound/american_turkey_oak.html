American turkey oak

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈtɜːrki oʊk / (AmE), / əˈmerɪkən ˈtɜːki əʊk / (BrE)
A small, slow-growing deciduous shrubby tree native to dry sandy barrens of the southeastern United States, with bristle-tipped lobed leaves resembling turkey's toes.
a shrubby oak tree found in the southeastern US

Examples

The dry sandy soils of the region are often dotted with American turkey oak.
Leaves of the American turkey oak are shaped somewhat like a turkey's foot.
Botanists conducted a survey of American turkey oak populations in southeastern woodlands.
