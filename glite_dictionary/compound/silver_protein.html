silver protein

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər ˈproʊˌtin / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a colloidal preparation of protein (such as albumin or gelatin) and silver oxide, used in aqueous solution as an antibacterial agent
colloid of protein and silver used as antibacterial agent
medical

Examples

The nurse applied a silver protein solution to the wound to prevent infection.
Historically, silver protein was commonly used as a topical antibacterial treatment.
Some pharmaceutical companies still produce silver protein for use in specific medical applications.
