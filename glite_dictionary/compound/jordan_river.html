Jordan River

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdn ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the Middle East, flowing from the slopes of Mount Hermon through the Sea of Galilee to the Dead Sea, significant in biblical history
river between Sea of Galilee and Dead Sea

Examples

Many pilgrims visit the Jordan River to see the site of Jesus's baptism.
The Jordan River is an important source of water for Israel and Jordan.
Environmentalists are worried about pollution levels in the Jordan River.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Yarden', possibly meaning 'the descender'.
