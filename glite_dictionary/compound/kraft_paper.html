kraft paper

1. NOUN

/ kræft ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / krɑːft ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
a strong, brown wrapping paper made from wood pulp processed with a sulfur solution, used especially for packaging
strong brown wrapping paper

Examples

The package was wrapped securely in kraft paper and tied with string.
Many grocery bags are made from recycled kraft paper.
Artists often use kraft paper for sketching because of its texture and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrapping paper - paper used for wrapping packages or gifts

Etymology

From German Kraft (strength, power), referring to the process that produces strong paper.
