Epistle to the Galatians

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl tu ðə ɡəˈleɪʃənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tʊ ðə ɡəˈleɪʃənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament consisting of a letter written by Saint Paul to the Christian community in Galatia
New Testament book; Paul's letter to Galatians

Examples

Biblical scholars have written extensively on interpretations of the Epistle to the Galatians.
The Epistle to the Galatians addresses issues of faith versus law in early Christianity.
She cited a passage from the Epistle to the Galatians during her sermon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible

Etymology

'Epistle' from Latin 'epistula' (letter), 'Galatians' from Galatia, an ancient region of Asia Minor.
