benzoyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnzoʊˌɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛnzɔɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a monovalent group (C6H5CO–) derived from benzoic acid by removal of the hydroxyl group
radical derived from benzoic acid
medical

Examples

The benzoyl radical is important in organic synthesis involving aromatic compounds.
Researchers isolated a benzoyl radical during photochemical reactions in the laboratory.
Formation of the benzoyl radical occurs when benzoic acid loses its hydroxyl group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoyl group - the monovalent group C6H5CO–

Etymology

From 'benzoyl', formed from 'benzoic acid' + '-yl' (radical), and 'radical' (reactive group).
