Black Hills

1. PROPN

/ blæk hɪlz / (AmE), / blæk hɪlz / (BrE)
a range of mountains in southwestern South Dakota and northeastern Wyoming, sacred to the Sioux people and known for their historical significance and landmarks such as Mount Rushmore.
mountain range in South Dakota and Wyoming

Examples

The Black Hills are a popular tourist destination known for their natural beauty and landmarks.
The Sioux regard the Black Hills as a sacred and historic place.
Mount Rushmore is located in the Black Hills of South Dakota.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mount Rushmore - monument carved into the Black Hills of South Dakota

Etymology

Named for the dark appearance of the pine-covered hills; from Lakota 'Paha Sapa' meaning 'the hills that are black'.
