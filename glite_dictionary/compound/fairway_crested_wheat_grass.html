fairway crested wheat grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrˌweɪ ˈkrɛstɪd wiːt ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈfeəweɪ ˈkrɛstɪd wiːt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A Eurasian grass species cultivated in the United States Great Plains for use as forage and for controlling soil erosion.
forage and erosion-control grass species

Examples

Farmers often plant fairway crested wheat grass to restore fields after heavy erosion.
The fairway crested wheat grass was chosen for its resilience in the unpredictable climate.
Researchers studied how fairway crested wheat grass improved soil quality over several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crested wheat grass - a related grass species used for forage and erosion control

Etymology

Named 'crested wheat grass' for its wheat-like seed heads and growth habit; 'fairway' refers to its use on golf course fairways and managed grasslands.
