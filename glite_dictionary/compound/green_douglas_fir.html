green douglas fir

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈdʌɡləs ˌfɪr / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈdʌɡləs ˌfɜː / (BrE)
A tall species of Douglas fir native to northwestern North America, characterized by short needles and egg-shaped cones.
species of Douglas fir tree

Examples

The green douglas fir is commonly found in the forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Lumber from the green douglas fir is prized for its strength and durability.
We identified the tree by its short needles and the distinctive cones of the green douglas fir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: douglas fir - a large North American coniferous tree
