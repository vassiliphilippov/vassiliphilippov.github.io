Siegfried Line

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiɡˌfrid laɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːɡfriːd laɪn / (BrE)
A line of German fortifications built along the western border of Germany, opposite France's Maginot Line, before and during World War II.
German WWII defensive fortifications

Examples

The Allied forces faced fierce resistance when they attacked the Siegfried Line in late 1944.
Historians often compare the Siegfried Line with its French counterpart, the Maginot Line.
Remnants of the Siegfried Line can still be seen along the German border today.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Maginot Line - French line of defensive fortifications opposite the Siegfried Line

Etymology

Named after Siegfried, a hero in Germanic legend, to evoke strength and protection.
