barrage fire

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɑːʒ ˌfaɪr / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A heavy, continuous artillery fire directed over a wide area to saturate it, rather than aimed at a specific target.
heavy, area-saturating artillery fire

Examples

The army unleashed a barrage fire to protect the advancing infantry from enemy positions.
The noise of the barrage fire echoed across the valley for hours.
Before the assault, a barrage fire was used to weaken enemy defenses.

Synset Examples

they laid down a barrage in front of the advancing troops
the shelling went on for hours without pausing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a concentrated artillery bombardment over a wide area

Etymology

From French 'barrage' (barrier).
