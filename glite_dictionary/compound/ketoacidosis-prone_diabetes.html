ketoacidosis-prone diabetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːtoʊəˌsɪdoʊsɪs ˈproʊn daɪəˈbiːtiz / (AmE), / ˌkiːtəʊəˌsɪdəʊsɪs ˈprəʊn dʌɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
a form of diabetes mellitus, usually with early onset, characterized by a tendency to develop ketoacidosis, and requiring diet and insulin injections for control
a severe diabetes type prone to ketoacidosis
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with ketoacidosis-prone diabetes, she began daily insulin injections to control her blood sugar.
Children with ketoacidosis-prone diabetes often present with weight loss and increased thirst.
Managing ketoacidosis-prone diabetes requires careful attention to both diet and medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 1 diabetes - a chronic condition in which the pancreas produces little or no insulin

Etymology

From 'ketoacidosis' (medical term for a type of metabolic acidosis caused by ketone bodies) + 'prone' + 'diabetes'.
