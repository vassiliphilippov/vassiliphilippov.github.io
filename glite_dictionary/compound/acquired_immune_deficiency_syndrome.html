acquired immune deficiency syndrome

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwaɪərd ɪˈmjun dɪˈfɪʃnsi ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪəd ɪˈmjuːn dɪˈfɪʃnsi ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A serious, often fatal, disease in which the immune system is severely weakened, making the body vulnerable to infections and certain cancers, and primarily transmitted through blood, sexual contact, or contaminated needles.
serious disease weakening the immune system
medical

Examples

Many patients diagnosed with acquired immune deficiency syndrome require specialized medical care to manage their symptoms.
The campaign aimed to educate the public about the prevention of acquired immune deficiency syndrome.
She works for an organization that supports people living with acquired immune deficiency syndrome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: AIDS - abbreviation of acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Etymology

Formed from 'acquired' + 'immune deficiency' + 'syndrome'; coined in the early 1980s after the identification of the disease.
