wall fern

1. NOUN

/ wɔl fɝn / (AmE), / wɔːl fɜːn / (BrE)
a mat-forming fern with creeping rootstocks and divided fronds, found on rocks or soil in North America, Europe, Africa, and East Asia
a mat-forming fern species

Examples

A patch of wall fern grew between the stones of the old garden wall.
The wall fern thrives in shaded, rocky crevices where moisture collects.
Botanists discovered a new species of wall fern in the mountains of East Asia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fern - a flowerless plant with feathery or leafy fronds
