Dobereiner's lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊbəˈraɪnərz læmp / (AmE), / ˌdəʊbəˈraɪnəz læmp / (BrE)
an early type of lighter invented in 1823 by the German chemist Johann Wolfgang Döbereiner, based on the Fürstenberger lighter, using hydrogen to ignite a flame
historical hydrogen-based lighter

Examples

The Dobereiner's lamp played an important role in the history of chemical appliances.
Collectors of antique scientific instruments often seek out the Dobereiner's lamp for their rarity.
Before matches became common, the Dobereiner's lamp provided a reliable method for producing fire.

Etymology

Named after Johann Wolfgang Döbereiner, its inventor, a German chemist.
