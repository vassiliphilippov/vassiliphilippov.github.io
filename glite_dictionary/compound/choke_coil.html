choke coil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊk ˌkɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊk kɔɪl / (BrE)
A coil with low resistance and high inductance used in electrical circuits to allow the passage of direct current while reducing or blocking alternating current.
electrical coil that blocks AC but lets DC pass

Examples

The engineer installed a choke coil to reduce electrical noise in the circuit.
A choke coil is often used to prevent unwanted alternating current from entering sensitive equipment.
Replacing the faulty choke coil restored normal operation to the power supply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inductor - a passive electric component that stores energy in its magnetic field

Etymology

From 'choke' (to block or restrict) and 'coil' (a wound conductor); named for its function of restricting alternating current.
