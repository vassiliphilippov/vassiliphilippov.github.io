via media

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːə ˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / ˌviː.ə ˈmiː.dɪ.ə / (BrE)
a middle way or course between two extremes; moderation or compromise
a middle way between extremes

Examples

The committee chose a via media to satisfy both parties involved in the dispute.
Some philosophers argue that the best approach is a via media rather than adopting radical views.
The architect found a via media between modernity and tradition in his design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - an agreement reached by each side making concessions
  • Antonym: extreme - the furthest end of a range; excessive position

Etymology

From Latin 'via media' meaning 'middle road' or 'middle way'.
