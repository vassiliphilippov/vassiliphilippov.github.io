tooth socket

1. NOUN

/ tuːθ ˈsɑːkɪt / (AmE), / tuːθ ˈsɒkɪt / (BrE)
A bony hollow in the jawbone in which a tooth is set.
bony hollow for a tooth in the jaw

Examples

The dentist examined the tooth socket for signs of infection after the extraction.
If a tooth socket does not heal properly, it may cause pain or swelling.
During surgery, care was taken to avoid damaging the surrounding tooth socket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alveolus - the bony socket in the jaw that holds a tooth
