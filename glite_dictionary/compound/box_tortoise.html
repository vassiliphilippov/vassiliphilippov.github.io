box tortoise

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈtɔrtəs / (AmE), / bɒks ˈtɔːtəs / (BrE)
a chiefly terrestrial North American turtle whose shell can be tightly closed
North American turtle with a closable shell

Examples

The box tortoise can withdraw into its shell for protection from predators.
We observed a box tortoise slowly crossing the hiking trail yesterday.
Unlike other turtles, the box tortoise is able to close its shell completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box turtle - terrestrial turtle of North America; shell can be closed tightly
