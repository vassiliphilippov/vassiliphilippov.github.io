seed vessel

1. NOUN

/ sid ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / siːd ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
the part of a plant that develops from the ovary and encloses or contains the seeds, often modified in form when ripe
part of a plant enclosing seeds

Examples

After flowering, the rose's seed vessel develops and eventually scatters the seeds.
Botanists examined the structure of the seed vessel to classify the plant.
The seed vessel of the poppy bursts open, releasing hundreds of tiny seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit - the mature ovary of a flowering plant, often edible
