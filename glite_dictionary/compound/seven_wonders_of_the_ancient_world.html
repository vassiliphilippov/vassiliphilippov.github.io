Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvən ˈwʌndɚz əv ði ˈeɪnʃənt wɝld / (AmE), / ˈsɛvən ˈwʌndəz əv ði ˈeɪnʃənt wɜːld / (BrE)
the seven remarkable constructions of classical antiquity admired for their extraordinary architecture and engineering, traditionally listed as the Great Pyramid of Giza, Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Statue of Zeus at Olympia, Temple of Artemis at Ephesus, Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, Colossus of Rhodes, and Lighthouse of Alexandria.
ancient world's most famous monuments

Examples

Many tourists dream of seeing all the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World on their travels.
The Lighthouse of Alexandria was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World but no longer exists today.
Students learned about the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World in their history class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - a marvel or phenomenon that inspires awe
