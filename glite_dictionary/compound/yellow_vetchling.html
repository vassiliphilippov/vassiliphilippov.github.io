yellow vetchling

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈvɛtʃlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈvɛtʃlɪŋ / (BrE)
A scrambling perennial Eurasian wild pea with yellowish flowers and compressed seed pods, cultivated for forage.
Eurasian wild pea with yellow flowers

Examples

Farmers sometimes grow yellow vetchling as a forage crop for its nutritional seeds.
The meadows were dotted with yellow vetchling, adding a splash of color to the greenery.
Botanists identified the plant as yellow vetchling due to its distinctive yellow flowers and pea-like pods.
