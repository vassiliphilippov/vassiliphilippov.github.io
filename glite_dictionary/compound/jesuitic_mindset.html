Jesuitic mindset

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛz.juˈɪ.tɪk ˈmaɪnd.set / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛz.juˈɪ.tɪk ˈmaɪnd.set / (BrE)
A way of thinking characterized by cleverness, subtle reasoning, or, sometimes, cunning.
clever or cunning way of thinking

Examples

With a Jesuitic mindset, she navigated the complicated negotiations with ease.
The lawyer’s Jesuitic mindset allowed him to find loopholes others missed.
Critics accused him of using a Jesuitic mindset to justify questionable actions.

Synset Examples

She approached the issue with a Jesuitic mindset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion
  • Synonym: cleverness - the quality of being quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas

Etymology

From 'Jesuit', referring to the Society of Jesus, often associated historically with subtle or shrewd reasoning.
