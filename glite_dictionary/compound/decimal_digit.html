decimal digit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪməl ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪməl ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
any of the digits from 0 to 9 used in decimal notation
digit from 0 to 9 in decimal system

Examples

Each decimal digit in the number represents a power of ten.
A serial number typically consists of a sequence of decimal digits.
Children learn to recognize each decimal digit before moving on to more complex mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digit - a numeral from 0 to 9
  • Degree Lower: binary digit - a digit in the binary system, 0 or 1
  • Degree Higher: hexadecimal digit - a digit in the hexadecimal system, 0–9 or A–F

Etymology

From Latin decimalis (of tenths) and Latin digitus (finger, used for counting).
