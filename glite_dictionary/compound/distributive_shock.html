distributive shock

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɪbjətɪv ʃɑk / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjʊtɪv ʃɒk / (BrE)
A form of shock caused by abnormal distribution of blood flow in the smallest blood vessels, resulting in inadequate supply to body tissues.
shock from poor blood distribution
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed her with distributive shock after observing a dramatically low blood pressure and warm skin.
Certain severe infections can lead to distributive shock, which requires urgent treatment to restore blood circulation.
In cases of distributive shock, blood flow fails to deliver enough oxygen to the body's vital organs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypovolemic shock - shock caused by low blood volume
  • Degree Higher: septic shock - shock caused by severe infection and blood vessel dilation
  • Degree Lower: cardiogenic shock - shock caused by failure of the heart to pump blood
  • Degree Higher: anaphylactic shock - shock caused by a severe allergic reaction

Etymology

From 'distributive', relating to distribution, and 'shock' (medical sense: state of inadequate blood flow).
