Steller's sea eagle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlɚz si ˈiːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstɛləz siː ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a large sea eagle native to the coasts of the northwestern Pacific, especially northeastern Asia, known for its striking appearance and powerful build
large sea eagle of the northwestern Pacific

Examples

The Steller's sea eagle is one of the heaviest eagles in the world, with an impressive wingspan.
Wildlife photographers travel to Hokkaido to catch a glimpse of the rare Steller's sea eagle.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the Steller's sea eagle along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea eagle - any of several large eagles that live near water and feed mainly on fish

Etymology

Named after German naturalist Georg Wilhelm Steller (1709–1746), who first described the species.
