saltwater fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltˌwɔtɚ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltˌwɔːtə fɪʃ / (BrE)
the flesh of fish that live in the sea, used as food
flesh of sea fish used as food

Examples

She prefers to cook saltwater fish because of its unique flavor.
Most sushi is made with saltwater fish like tuna and salmon.
During Lent, many families eat saltwater fish as their main source of protein.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freshwater fish - flesh of fish from rivers or lakes used as food
