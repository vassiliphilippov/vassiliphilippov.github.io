Sargasso Sea

1. PROPN

/ sɑrˈɡæsoʊ ˌsi / (AmE), / sɑːˈɡæsəʊ ˌsiː / (BrE)
a region of the North Atlantic Ocean, east of the West Indies and west of the Azores, characterized by floating masses of gulfweed (Sargassum) and relatively calm, clear water
region of the North Atlantic Ocean

Examples

Christopher Columbus was among the first Europeans to record his passage through the Sargasso Sea.
The Sargasso Sea is known for its clear blue water and thick mats of floating seaweed.
Several species of eel migrate thousands of miles to spawn in the Sargasso Sea.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Sargaço', referring to the sargassum (a type of seaweed) found in the area.

2. PROPN

/ sɑrˈɡæsoʊ si / (AmE), / sɑːˈɡæsəʊ siː / (BrE)
a region of the North Atlantic Ocean distinguished by its calm waters and abundance of floating sargassum seaweed
region of the North Atlantic Ocean with floating seaweed

Examples

Sailors often remarked on the dense mats of seaweed in the Sargasso Sea.
The Sargasso Sea is famous for its clear blue water and unique ecosystem.
Many species of eel spawn in the Sargasso Sea before migrating to distant rivers.

Synset Examples

The boat drifted aimlessly in the sargasso sea.

Etymology

Named after the sargassum seaweed found there; 'Sargasso' from Spanish 'sargazo' (seaweed).
