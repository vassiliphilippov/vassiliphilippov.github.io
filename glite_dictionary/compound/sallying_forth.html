sallying forth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæliɪŋ fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈsæliɪŋ fɔːθ / (BrE)
an adventurous venture or journey away from one's usual place or routine, often with a sense of purpose or curiosity
adventurous excursion or venture

Examples

On a sunny morning, they decided on a spontaneous sallying forth into the countryside.
Her first sallying forth beyond the city limits was thrilling and a bit daunting.
The documentary follows a group's sallying forth in search of rare wildlife in the rainforest.

Synset Examples

a sally into the wide world beyond his home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken for a specific purpose

Etymology

From the verb 'sally' meaning to leap or rush out, from Old French 'saillie' (a rush), from Latin 'salire' (to jump) combined with 'forth' (forward, out).
