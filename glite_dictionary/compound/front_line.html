front line

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt laɪn / (AmE), / frʌnt laɪn / (BrE)
the area or position where opposing armies meet and fighting takes place; a place of direct engagement in a conflict
area where opposing armies meet

Examples

Many brave soldiers lost their lives on the front line during the war.
Reporters risked their safety to cover the news from the front line.
He was transferred to the front line after months of training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefront - the region where fighting is taking place

2. NOUN

/ frʌnt laɪn / (AmE), / frʌnt laɪn / (BrE)
the most important or active position in a particular activity, often where risk, action, or new developments occur
most important or active position

Examples

During the health crisis, nurses worked tirelessly on the front line to care for patients.
The reporter wanted to be where the action was, right on the front line of the protests.
Many volunteers joined the front line of the campaign to help those in need.

Synset Examples

He works on the front line of customer service.
The soldiers were on the front line during the battle.
She’s always been on the front line of innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanguard - the leading position or people in a movement or field
  • Antonym: rear guard - a position or group protecting behind the main force, or the least active/advanced position

Etymology

Originally a military term referring to the foremost line in battle; figurative senses developed in the 20th century.
