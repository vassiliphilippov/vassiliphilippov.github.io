Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic

1. NOUN

/ səˈprim ˈælaɪd kəˈmændər ətˈlæntɪk / (AmE), / sjuːˈpriːm ˈælaɪd kəˈmændə ætˈlæntɪk / (BrE)
the highest-ranking officer in charge of the Allied Command Atlantic (ACLANT), usually a U.S. Army general appointed by the President of the United States and approved by the North Atlantic Council
senior officer of Allied Command Atlantic

Examples

The Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic played a central role in NATO's defense strategy during the Cold War.
At the conference, the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic addressed the importance of transatlantic naval cooperation.
He was appointed Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic after a distinguished military career.
