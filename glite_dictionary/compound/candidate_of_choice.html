candidate of choice

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændɪˌdeɪt əv tʃɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdət əv tʃɔɪs / (BrE)
the person who is most preferred or favored for a position or role among a group of candidates
preferred candidate for a position

Examples

The board agreed that she was the candidate of choice for the CEO position.
Among all the applicants, Mark quickly became the candidate of choice.
He knew he was not the candidate of choice, but he still prepared a strong application.

Synset Examples

He was the candidate of choice for the role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front-runner - the leading or most likely candidate to win a competition or position
  • Antonym: underdog - a candidate or competitor thought to have little chance of winning
