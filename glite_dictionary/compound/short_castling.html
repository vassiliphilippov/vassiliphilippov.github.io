short castling

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ˈkæsəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈkɑːslɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of castling on the kingside in chess, moving the king two squares towards the rook on its original square and then placing the rook on the square over which the king crossed.
kingside castling in chess

Examples

He chose short castling to protect his king and quickly connect his rooks.
After short castling, she began her kingside attack without worrying about her king's safety.
In most modern games, players prefer short castling as it is considered safer and faster.

Synset Examples

Since a positional player will always keep his options open and his king safe, castling short would be a good option, and is played most of the time at top level.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long castling - castling on the queenside in chess

Etymology

Formed from 'short' (referring to the kingside, which is the shorter distance for the rook to move) and 'castling', a chess move.
