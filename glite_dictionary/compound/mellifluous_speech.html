mellifluous speech

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪfluəs spiːtʃ / (AmE), / mɛˈlɪflʊəs spiːtʃ / (BrE)
pleasant-sounding or musical speech that is smooth and sweet to hear
pleasant-sounding or musical speech

Examples

The politician's mellifluous speech won over even the most skeptical members of the crowd.
With his mellifluous speech, the poet captured the hearts of listeners.
She was admired for her mellifluous speech, which made every presentation enjoyable.

Synset Examples

His mellifluous speech enchanted the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euphonious - pleasing to the ear, especially of sound
  • Antonym: harsh speech - speech that is unpleasant or grating to hear

Etymology

From Latin mellifluus (flowing with honey), from mel (honey) + fluere (to flow).
