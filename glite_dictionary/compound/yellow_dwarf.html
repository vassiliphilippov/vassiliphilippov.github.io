yellow dwarf

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ dɔrf / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ dɔːf / (BrE)
Any of several viral diseases in plants characterized by stunted growth and yellowing of the leaves.
plant virus disease causing yellowing and stunting

Examples

Barley crops infected with yellow dwarf often show severe leaf yellowing and reduced yield.
Scientists are developing resistant varieties to combat yellow dwarf in wheat fields.
Symptoms of yellow dwarf usually appear in early spring, stunting plant growth.
