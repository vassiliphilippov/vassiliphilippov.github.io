Appian Way

1. PROPN

/ ˈæpiən weɪ / (AmE), / ˈæpɪən weɪ / (BrE)
an ancient Roman road extending from Rome to Brindisi, built beginning in 312 BC and famous for its historical importance
ancient Roman road from Rome to Brindisi

Examples

The Appian Way was one of the earliest and most important roads of the Roman Republic.
Tourists can still walk sections of the Appian Way outside of Rome today.
Many battles and historic events took place along the Appian Way during ancient times.

Etymology

From Latin Via Appia, named after the Roman official Appius Claudius Caecus who began its construction.
