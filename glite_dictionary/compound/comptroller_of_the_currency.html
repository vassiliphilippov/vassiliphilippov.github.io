Comptroller of the Currency

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈtroʊlɚ əv ðə ˈkɝːrənsi / (AmE), / kəmˈtrəʊlə əv ðə ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
the head of the United States Office of the Comptroller of the Currency, responsible for regulating and supervising national banks
US federal official regulating national banks
american

Examples

The Comptroller of the Currency issued new regulations to ensure the stability of national banks.
After a thorough investigation, the Comptroller of the Currency released a formal report on the bank's operations.
Congress called the Comptroller of the Currency to testify about changes in the financial industry.

Etymology

From 'comptroller' (medieval French 'compte') and 'currency'; official US title since 1863.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈtroʊlɚ əv ðə ˈkɝːrənsi / (AmE), / kəmˈtrəʊlə əv ðə ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
the chief bank regulator in the United States, heading the federal agency responsible for chartering, regulating, and supervising national banks and federal savings associations
U.S. federal bank regulator agency head

Examples

The Comptroller of the Currency announced new regulations for national banks this year.
She worked for the Comptroller of the Currency before joining the private sector.
Congress held hearings with the Comptroller of the Currency on changes to banking oversight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - person or agency that manages or supervises financial matters

Etymology

From French 'contrôleur' (controller), influenced by English 'compter' (to count); agency established by the U.S. National Currency Act of 1863.
