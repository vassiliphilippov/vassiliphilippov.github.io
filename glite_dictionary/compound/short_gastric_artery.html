short gastric artery

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ˈɡæs.trɪk ˈɑr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈɡæs.trɪk ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
any of several small arteries branching from the splenic artery to supply the greater curvature of the stomach
artery supplying stomach's greater curvature
medical

Examples

During surgery, the short gastric artery was carefully ligated to prevent bleeding.
Damage to the short gastric artery can result in gastric ischemia.
The anatomical course of the short gastric artery can vary between individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splenic artery - major artery branching from the celiac trunk to the spleen

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria gastrica breves', with 'gastric' from Greek 'gaster' (stomach).
