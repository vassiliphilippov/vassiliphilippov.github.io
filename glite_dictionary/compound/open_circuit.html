open circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.pən ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electrical circuit in which the path is incomplete, preventing current from flowing
an incomplete electrical circuit with no current flow

Examples

If the wires are not connected properly, you will have an open circuit and the device won't work.
The technician tested the system and found an open circuit in the power supply line.
A blown fuse creates an open circuit, stopping electricity from reaching the appliance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed circuit - an electrical circuit with a complete path for current to flow
