genus Chauna

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs tʃɑːˈuː.nə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs tʃɑːˈuː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of large South American birds, known as crested screamers, characterized by a distinctive crest and loud call.
a genus of crested South American birds

Examples

The genus Chauna includes birds commonly known as crested screamers, found throughout South America.
Ornithologists often study the genus Chauna to learn about the unique vocalizations of screamers.
Both species in the genus Chauna inhabit wetlands and marshy areas.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Chauna', the genus name assigned in taxonomy, possibly from Greek meaning 'gaping' or 'yawning'.
