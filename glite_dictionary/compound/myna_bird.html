myna bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnə bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə bɜːd / (BrE)
A tropical Asian bird of the starling family, known for its glossy black plumage and ability to mimic sounds.
tropical Asian starling

Examples

We saw a myna bird perched on the fence, imitating the sound of passing cars.
The myna bird is prized in some cultures for its talking ability.
Several myna birds have adapted well to city life, often feeding on scraps in markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - a family of small to medium-sized birds

Etymology

From Hindi mainā, ultimately from Sanskrit madanā (a species of bird).
