coordination compound

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɔːrdəˈneɪʃən kəmˌpaʊnd / (AmE), / kəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n kəmˌpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which a central atom, usually a metal, is bonded to surrounding groups of atoms or molecules, called ligands, through coordinate bonds
chemical substance with a central atom bonded to ligands
medical

Examples

A coordination compound consists of a central metal ion surrounded by several ligands.
The bright color of the solution is due to the presence of a coordination compound.
Chemists often study how ligands bond to metals in a coordination compound.

Etymology

From 'coordinate' in the sense of 'to bring into relationship', referencing the binding of ligands to a central atom.
