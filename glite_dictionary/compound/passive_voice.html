passive voice

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv vɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv vɔɪs / (BrE)
a grammatical construction in which the subject of a sentence receives the action rather than performing it, typically formed with a form of 'be' and a past participle
grammatical voice where subject receives action

Examples

Many scientific papers are written using the passive voice to emphasize the process rather than the researcher.
Teachers often recommend using the passive voice less frequently in essays for clearer writing.
In the sentence 'The cake was eaten by Tom', the passive voice is used.

Synset Examples

‘The ball was thrown by the boy’ uses the passive voice
‘The ball was thrown’ is an abbreviated passive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active voice - grammatical voice where subject performs the action

Etymology

From Latin 'passivus' (capable of suffering or feeling) + 'voice' in the grammatical sense.
