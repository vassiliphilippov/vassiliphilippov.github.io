wild angelica

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / waɪld ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
A European herb (Angelica sylvestris) with compound leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers, sometimes found growing wild elsewhere.
a European herb with white flowers

Examples

We spotted wild angelica growing along the edge of the forest trail.
The flowers of wild angelica are attractive to bees and butterflies.
Wild angelica thrives in damp meadows and woodland clearings across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelica - a genus of aromatic plants, including wild angelica
