land mine

1. NOUN

/ lænd maɪn / (AmE), / lænd maɪn / (BrE)
An explosive device concealed under or on the ground, designed to detonate when pressure is applied, typically by a person or vehicle passing over it.
explosive device hidden in the ground

Examples

Many countries are still affected by land mines left over from past wars.
The military is working to clear land mines from the area to ensure safety.
He lost his leg after stepping on a land mine in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an explosive device placed in the ground or sea

Etymology

From 'land', referring to the ground, and 'mine', from Middle French 'mine' (explosive device).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlænd ˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlænd ˌmaɪn / (BrE)
an explosive device concealed just under or on the ground, designed to detonate when disturbed, commonly used as a weapon in military conflicts; also used metaphorically for a hidden danger
concealed explosive device

Examples

Many countries are still working to remove old land mines from former conflict zones.
He accidentally stepped on a land mine while walking through the field.
The negotiations were full of political land mines that could derail the process.

Synset Examples

The project was a land mine waiting to explode.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booby trap - an explosive or other device hidden to harm someone unexpectedly
  • Synonym: improvised explosive device - a homemade bomb constructed and deployed in unconventional ways

Etymology

Formed from 'land' (ground) and 'mine' (explosive device), first used in military contexts in the late 19th century.
