Sepoy Mutiny

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːpɔɪ mjuːˈtɪni / (AmE), / ˈsiːpɔɪ mjuːˈtɪni / (BrE)
a major revolt against British rule in India in 1857–1858, initiated by Indian soldiers (sepoys) in the service of the British East India Company
1857–1858 revolt of Indian soldiers against British rule

Examples

The Sepoy Mutiny marked a turning point in the history of British-ruled India.
Historians often debate the causes and consequences of the Sepoy Mutiny.
The British responded to the Sepoy Mutiny with sweeping changes in their administration of India.

Etymology

From 'sepoy', meaning an Indian soldier under British (from Portuguese 'sipai', from Persian 'sipāhī'), and 'mutiny', meaning rebellion.
