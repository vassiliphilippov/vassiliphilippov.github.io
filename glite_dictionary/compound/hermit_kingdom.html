Hermit Kingdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrmɪt ˈkɪŋdəm / (AmE), / ˈhɜːmɪt ˈkɪŋdəm / (BrE)
a country, especially North Korea, that deliberately isolates itself from the rest of the world
an isolated country, especially North Korea

Examples

Many journalists refer to North Korea as the Hermit Kingdom due to its extreme isolation.
The Hermit Kingdom has been resistant to international engagement and reform.
Outsiders rarely get a glimpse into daily life within the Hermit Kingdom.

Synset Examples

North Korea is often referred to as the Hermit Kingdom.
The Hermit Kingdom maintains strict control over its citizens.
Living in the Hermit Kingdom, they rarely interact with the outside world.

Etymology

Formed from 'hermit' (a person living in solitude) + 'kingdom'; used in the 19th century for Korea's isolationist period.
