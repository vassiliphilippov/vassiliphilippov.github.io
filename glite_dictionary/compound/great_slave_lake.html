Great Slave Lake

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡreɪt ˈsleɪv ˌleɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈsleɪv ˌleɪk / (BrE)
a large lake in the Northwest Territories of northwestern Canada, drained by the Mackenzie River
lake in Northwest Territories, Canada

Examples

The expedition crossed the ice of Great Slave Lake during the harsh winter months.
Fishing is a popular activity on Great Slave Lake, especially in the summer.
The Mackenzie River flows northward, draining the waters of Great Slave Lake into the Arctic Ocean.

Etymology

Named after the Slavey First Nations people in the area.
