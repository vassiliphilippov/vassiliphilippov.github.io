Soviet Russia

1. PROPN

/ soʊˈviː.ət ˈrʌʃ.ə / (AmE), / səʊˈviː.ət ˈrʌʃ.ə / (BrE)
the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic, the largest constituent republic of the former USSR, occupying eastern Europe and northern Asia
the largest republic of the former USSR

Examples

During the Cold War, Soviet Russia was seen as a global superpower rivaling the United States.
Many scientific breakthroughs in the 20th century were credited to researchers working in Soviet Russia.
The collapse of Soviet Russia led to major political changes across Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: USSR - the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, former federation of communist states in Eurasia
