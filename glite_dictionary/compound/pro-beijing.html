pro-Beijing

1. ADJ

/ proʊ bəˈdʒeɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / prəʊ bɪˈdʒeɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
politically supporting or aligned with the Chinese Communist Party, especially in the context of Hong Kong and China.
supporting the Chinese Communist Party

Examples

The government was criticized for its appointment of several pro-Beijing officials.
Many pro-Beijing groups organized rallies in support of the proposed legislation.
She is widely regarded as one of the most influential pro-Beijing politicians in the region.

Synset Examples

Pro-Beijing DAB's Starry Lee and Holden Chow were successfully elected in this constituency, while pro-Beijing veteran politician Wong Kwok-hing of FTU was defeated by 10,694 votes by James To.
In the Hong Kong Island geographical constituency, pro-Beijing New People Party's Regina Ip was re-elected with 60,760 votes.
In traditional functional constituencies, the pan-democracy camp gained a seat from the pro-Beijing camp.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro-democracy - supporting democratic principles, often in opposition to pro-Beijing stances

Etymology

Formed from 'pro-' (in favor of) + 'Beijing', the capital of China.
