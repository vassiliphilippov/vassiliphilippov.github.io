bald-faced hornet

1. NOUN

/ bɔld feɪst ˈhɔrnət / (AmE), / bɔːld feɪst ˈhɔːnɪt / (BrE)
A large black-and-white hornet found in North America, known for its aggressive defense of its nest and its paper-like nests in trees and shrubs.
North American black-and-white hornet

Examples

A bald-faced hornet built a large nest in the corner of our backyard last summer.
The sting of a bald-faced hornet can be very painful if you accidentally disturb its nest.
Scientists observed a colony of bald-faced hornets constructing their papery hive high in the trees.
