vegetable sheep

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛdʒtəbl ʃiːp / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒtəbəl ʃiːp / (BrE)
a cushion-forming New Zealand herb with leaves densely covered in tawny hairs, resembling the appearance of a sheep from a distance
cushion-shaped New Zealand herb resembling a sheep

Examples

From a distance, the vegetable sheep dotting the hillside resemble grazing animals.
During the hike, we spotted a rare vegetable sheep nestled among the rocks.
Scientists study the vegetable sheep to understand how plants adapt to harsh alpine environments.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to sheep when seen on New Zealand hillsides.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛdʒtəbl ʃip / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒtəb(ə)l ʃiːp / (BrE)
a perennial mat-forming herb with dense, woolly foliage, native to New Zealand, resembling a sheep from a distance
New Zealand mat-forming herb with woolly leaves

Examples

From a distance, the vegetable sheep looked like a flock grazing on the alpine slopes.
Botanists are fascinated by the vegetable sheep due to its unique adaptation to harsh environments.
Hikers in New Zealand's mountains often mistake vegetable sheep for real animals.

Etymology

So named because its cushion-like clumps resemble sheep from afar.
