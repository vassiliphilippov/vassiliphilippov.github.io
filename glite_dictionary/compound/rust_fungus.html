rust fungus

1. NOUN

/ rʌst ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / rʌst ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
a type of fungus that causes rust disease in plants, characterized by reddish or brownish spots
fungus causing rust disease in plants

Examples

The wheat crop was severely damaged by a rust fungus this year.
Gardeners are advised to watch for signs of rust fungus on their rose bushes.
Researchers are developing new methods to control the spread of rust fungus in soybean fields.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fungus - a group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter
  • Synonym: rust disease - a plant disease marked by reddish or brownish spots

Etymology

Formed from 'rust' (the reddish coating) and 'fungus' (from Latin 'fungus', meaning mushroom).
