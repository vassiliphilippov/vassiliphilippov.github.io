salutatory oration

1. NOUN

/ səˈluːtəˌtɔri ɔˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈluːtətəri ɔˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an opening or welcoming speech, especially one delivered at a graduation ceremony to welcome attendees
welcoming speech at graduation

Examples

The class president delivered a heartfelt salutatory oration to open the graduation ceremony.
Her salutatory oration welcomed new students and their families to the campus.
Every year, a different student is chosen to give the salutatory oration at commencement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: valedictory oration - farewell speech usually given at graduation

Etymology

From Latin salutatorius (of greeting), from salutare (to greet), combined with oration (formal speech).
