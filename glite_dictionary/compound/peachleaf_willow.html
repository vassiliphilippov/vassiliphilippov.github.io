peachleaf willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːtʃˌlif ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpiːtʃˌliːf ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a willow tree of the western United States, distinguished by its long, narrow leaves resembling those of the peach or almond tree
a willow tree with peach-like leaves

Examples

The peachleaf willow grows along stream banks in the western United States.
Botanists often note the leaf shape of the peachleaf willow when identifying it.
In spring, the peachleaf willow displays slender green leaves that resemble those of a peach tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub with slender branches and narrow leaves
