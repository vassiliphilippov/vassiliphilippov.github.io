oil heater

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈhiːtər / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈhiːtə / (BrE)
a heater that burns oil, typically kerosene, to provide warmth or for cooking
heater burning oil for heat or cooking

Examples

We used an oil heater to keep the house warm during the winter power outage.
The oil heater needs to be refilled with kerosene before it will work.
Some campers prefer an oil heater for cooking when they are outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kerosene heater - a heater that burns kerosene as fuel for heat
