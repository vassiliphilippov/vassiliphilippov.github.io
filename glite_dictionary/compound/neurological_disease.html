neurological disease

1. NOUN

/ nuˌɹɑːlədʒɪkəl dɪˈziz / (AmE), / njuːˌɹɒlədʒɪkəl dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease that affects the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, or nerves
disease affecting the nervous system
medical

Examples

Multiple sclerosis is a neurological disease that can affect movement and vision.
Research into Alzheimer's, a progressive neurological disease, is advancing rapidly.
She specializes in treating patients with rare neurological diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurological disorder - a disorder of the nervous system

Etymology

neurological: from Greek neuron (nerve) + -logia (study); disease: from Old French desaise (discomfort, disease)
