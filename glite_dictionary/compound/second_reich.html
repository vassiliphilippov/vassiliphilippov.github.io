Second Reich

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈraɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈraɪk / (BrE)
the period of the German Empire under Hohenzollern rule, from 1871 to 1919.
German Empire under Hohenzollern rule (1871–1919)

Examples

Many historians regard the Second Reich as a period of significant industrial and military expansion in Germany.
The collapse of the Second Reich after World War I led to the establishment of the Weimar Republic.
Kaiser Wilhelm II was the last emperor to rule during the Second Reich.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: First Reich - the Holy Roman Empire (962–1806), predecessor to the Second Reich
  • Degree Higher: Third Reich - Nazi Germany (1933–1945), successor to the Second Reich

Etymology

From German 'Zweites Reich', meaning 'Second Empire', with 'Reich' meaning 'realm' or 'empire'.
