funnel web

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌn.əl wɛb / (AmE), / ˈfʌn.əl wɛb / (BrE)
A spider web shaped like a funnel, often inhabited by certain spiders such as funnel-web spiders, which wait in the center to catch prey.
a funnel-shaped spider web

Examples

The spider spun a funnel web at the corner of the shed, waiting patiently for insects.
Australian gardens are sometimes home to the dangerous Sydney funnel web spider.
He was fascinated by the intricate design of the funnel web tucked between the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cobweb - a spider web, especially an old or dusty one, not of the funnel type
