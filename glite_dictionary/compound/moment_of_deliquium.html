moment of deliquium

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt əv dɪˈlɪkwiəm / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt əv dɪˈlɪkwɪəm / (BrE)
a period of mental confusion or delirium
period of confusion or delirium
archaic

Examples

During a moment of deliquium, she could not remember her own name.
He wrote the letter in a moment of deliquium, full of wild and confused thoughts.
The doctor recognized his patient was experiencing a moment of deliquium and called for assistance.

Synset Examples

In moments of deliquium, he often found clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delirium - a disturbed state of mind, marked by confusion

Etymology

From Latin 'deliquium' meaning 'fainting' or 'swoon'.
