revolutions per minute

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənz pɚ ˈmɪnət / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənz pə ˈmɪnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement expressing the number of complete rotations made by an object in one minute, commonly used to describe the rotational speed of engines and motors
unit for rotational speed (rotations per minute)

Examples

When the car reaches 3,000 revolutions per minute, it's time to shift to a higher gear.
The fan operates at 1,200 revolutions per minute to provide optimal airflow.
The engine's revolutions per minute increased as the driver accelerated.

Synset Examples

the engine was doing 6000 revs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rpm - abbreviation for revolutions per minute
