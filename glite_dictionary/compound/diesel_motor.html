diesel motor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːzəl ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈdiːzəl ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
an internal combustion engine that uses heavy oil (diesel fuel) instead of gasoline
engine using diesel fuel

Examples

The ship was powered by a sturdy diesel motor that could run for days without refueling.
Many trucks and buses rely on a diesel motor for their superior torque and fuel efficiency.
After years of use, the old tractor’s diesel motor still started up every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diesel engine - an engine using diesel fuel rather than gasoline
  • Antonym: gasoline engine - an engine that uses gasoline as fuel

Etymology

From the name of German engineer Rudolf Diesel, inventor of the engine.
