dyspeptic mood

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpɛptɪk muːd / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛptɪk muːd / (BrE)
A bad-tempered, irritable, or gloomy state of mind.
bad-tempered or irritable state of mind

Examples

Belinda's dyspeptic mood cast a shadow over the entire office meeting.
After hours of delays, he was in a particularly dyspeptic mood by the time he got home.
You might want to avoid talking to her when she’s in a dyspeptic mood.

Synset Examples

Her dyspeptic mood made it hard to enjoy the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: good mood - a state of happiness or contentment

Etymology

From Greek 'dyspeptos' (hard to digest) + English 'mood'.
