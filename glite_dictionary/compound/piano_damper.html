piano damper

1. NOUN

/ piˈænoʊ ˈdæmpər / (AmE), / piˈanəʊ ˈdæmpə / (BrE)
a part of a piano consisting of a small felted block that rests on a string to stop its vibration when a key is released
felted block in a piano that stops string vibration

Examples

When the key is released, the piano damper falls back onto the string, silencing the note.
The technician replaced a worn piano damper to improve the instrument's sound quality.
A malfunctioning piano damper can cause unwanted ringing or sustain in a piano.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damper - device for deadening vibrations or sounds in instruments or machines
