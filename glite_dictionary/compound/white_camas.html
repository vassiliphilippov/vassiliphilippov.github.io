white camas

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkæməs / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkaməs / (BrE)
a plant of eastern and central North America with creamy white, often brown- or purple-tinged flowers, poisonous especially to grazing animals
a poisonous North American plant with white flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with white camas blooming in early summer.
Farmers must be careful as white camas can be toxic to livestock.
We identified several clumps of white camas along the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death camas - any of several closely related poisonous plants with white or cream flowers
