dusky salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌski səˈlæməndɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌski səˈlæməndə / (BrE)
a common North American salamander, mottled with dull brown or greyish-black coloring
a North American salamander species

Examples

During our hike in the Appalachian woods, we spotted a dusky salamander beneath a mossy log.
The dusky salamander relies on its natural camouflage to blend in with its damp surroundings.
Conservationists are studying the habitat of the dusky salamander to protect this sensitive species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a tailed amphibian of the order Caudata
