genus Anethum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈniː.θəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈniː.θəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbaceous plants in the parsley family, chiefly known for dill (Anethum graveolens).
genus of herbs including dill

Examples

The species in the genus Anethum are widely used for culinary and medicinal purposes.
Botanists identified the plant as belonging to the genus Anethum.
Dill, the best known member of the genus Anethum, is popular in many cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dill - an aromatic herb, Anethum graveolens

Etymology

From Latin 'Anethum', originally from Greek 'ánēthon' (dill).
