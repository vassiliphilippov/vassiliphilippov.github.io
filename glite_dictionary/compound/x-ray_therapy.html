X-ray therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛks reɪ ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˈɛks reɪ ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the use of X-rays to treat diseases, especially cancer
use of X-rays for medical treatment
medical

Examples

The patient underwent X-ray therapy to shrink the tumor.
Advances in X-ray therapy have improved cancer treatment outcomes.
Some side effects of X-ray therapy can include fatigue and skin irritation.

Etymology

From 'X-ray', denoting the type of radiation, and 'therapy', from Greek therapeia (healing).
