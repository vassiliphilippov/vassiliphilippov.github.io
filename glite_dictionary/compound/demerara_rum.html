Demerara rum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.əˈrɛr.ə ˌrʌm / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə ˌrʌm / (BrE)
A type of dark rum produced in Guyana, typically made from Demerara sugar and known for its rich flavor.
dark rum from Guyana

Examples

She prefers to use Demerara rum when making fruitcake for its deep, rich flavor.
The distillery boasts an impressive selection of Demerara rum aged in wooden casks.
He poured a generous splash of Demerara rum into the glass, savoring its aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rum - an alcoholic spirit distilled from sugarcane products

Etymology

Named after the Demerara region and river in Guyana, where the rum is produced.
