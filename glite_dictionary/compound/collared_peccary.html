collared peccary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlɚd ˈpɛkəri / (AmE), / ˈkɒləd ˈpɛkəri / (BrE)
a dark grey, pig-like mammal with an indistinct white collar, native to semi-desert areas of Mexico and the southwestern United States
a pig-like mammal of Mexico and the US Southwest

Examples

The collared peccary is often seen traveling in small herds throughout the arid regions of Arizona.
Unlike domestic pigs, the collared peccary has a coarse, bristly coat and a distinctive collar marking.
We spotted a group of collared peccaries rooting for food near the desert trail.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: peccary - a pig-like mammal of the Americas

Etymology

From Spanish 'pecarí', from a Cariban language; 'collared' refers to its pale neck band.
