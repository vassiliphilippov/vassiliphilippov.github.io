blank check

1. NOUN

/ blæŋk tʃɛk / (AmE), / blæŋk tʃɛk / (BrE)
A check that has been signed but has the amount left blank, allowing the holder to fill in any value.
signed check with no amount filled in

Examples

The thief cashed the blank check that he found in the drawer.
It's risky to give someone a blank check because they can write any amount.
She accidentally left a blank check in her notebook.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: signed check - a check with the signature of the account holder

2. NOUN

/ blæŋk tʃɛk / (AmE), / blæŋk tʃɛk / (BrE)
complete freedom to act as one wishes, especially to spend money or make decisions without restriction
complete freedom to act or decide

Examples

The new manager was given a blank check to implement whatever changes he thought necessary.
Congress refused to give the president a blank check for military spending.
With a blank check from investors, she expanded her startup rapidly.

Synset Examples

many have doubts about giving him a free hand to attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free rein - unrestricted freedom to act or decide
  • Synonym: carte blanche - complete freedom or authority to act
  • Antonym: restriction - a rule or limit on freedom or actions

Etymology

From the literal 'blank check', meaning a check with no specified amount, metaphorically extended to mean unrestricted authority.

3. NOUN

/ blæŋk tʃɛk / (AmE), / blæŋk tʃɛk / (BrE)
complete freedom to act or spend as one wishes, usually provided by someone else, especially with regard to financial support or authority.
unlimited financial authority or support

Examples

The committee gave the new director a blank check to implement her vision for the company.
Some politicians worry about giving the military a blank check for defense spending.
With a blank check from his parents, he was able to start his own business.

Synset Examples
