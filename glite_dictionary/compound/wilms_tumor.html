Wilms' tumor

1. NOUN

/ wɪlmz ˈtumɚ / (AmE), / wɪlmz ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
a malignant tumor of the kidney occurring primarily in young children, often presenting with high blood pressure, blood in the urine, and an abdominal mass
malignant kidney tumor in children
medical

Examples

A four-year-old was diagnosed with Wilms' tumor after presenting with a large abdominal mass.
Successful treatment of Wilms' tumor often involves surgery and chemotherapy.
Frequent screening is recommended for children at risk of developing Wilms' tumor.

Etymology

Named after Max Wilms, a German surgeon who first described the tumor.
