charge account credit

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑrdʒ əˈkaʊnt ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ əˈkaʊnt ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
a line of consumer credit that allows purchases up to a certain limit and can be paid down at any time
a flexible consumer credit line

Examples

The department store offered a charge account credit to frequent shoppers, letting them pay for purchases later.
She checked her charge account credit balance before making another large purchase.
Many retailers restrict the spending limit on a charge account credit based on a buyer's creditworthiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit line - the maximum amount of credit that a financial institution extends to a client
