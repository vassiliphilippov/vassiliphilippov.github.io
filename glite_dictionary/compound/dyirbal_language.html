Dyirbal language

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪrbəl ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɪəbəl ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an Aboriginal Australian language traditionally spoken in northeast Queensland
an Australian Aboriginal language

Examples

Linguists have documented the Dyirbal language to preserve its unique grammatical patterns.
She is one of the few remaining speakers of the Dyirbal language.
The university offers a course in the Dyirbal language and its cultural significance.

Etymology

From the name 'Dyirbal', the people and their language, used by Aboriginal communities in Queensland, Australia.
