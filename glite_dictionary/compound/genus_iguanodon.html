genus Iguanodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈɡwɑː.nəˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈɡwɑː.nəˌdɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the Iguanodontidae, a group of herbivorous dinosaurs from the Early Cretaceous period.
type genus of Iguanodontidae (herbivorous dinosaurs)

Examples

The genus Iguanodon is known for its distinctive thumb spikes and was among the first dinosaurs to be discovered.
Scientists have unearthed many fossils belonging to the genus Iguanodon across Europe.
The classification of the genus Iguanodon has been revised several times as new discoveries are made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iguanodon - a large herbivorous dinosaur of the genus Iguanodon

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Iguanodon', from Spanish 'iguana' (a type of lizard) and Greek 'odous, odont-' (tooth).
