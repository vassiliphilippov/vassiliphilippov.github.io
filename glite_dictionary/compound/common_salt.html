common salt

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən sɔlt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən sɒlt / (BrE)
ordinary salt used in food and industry, consisting mainly of sodium chloride (NaCl) as a white crystalline substance.
sodium chloride; table salt

Examples

Many people consume too much common salt in their diets.
The chemist explained that common salt is essential for human health in small quantities.
Sea water contains a high concentration of common salt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table salt - refined sodium chloride used for seasoning food

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən sɔlt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən sɒlt / (BrE)
white crystalline substance, especially sodium chloride, used to season and preserve food
sodium chloride used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled a pinch of common salt over the salad to enhance its flavor.
During winter, common salt is sometimes spread on icy roads to melt the ice.
For centuries, people have used common salt to preserve meat and fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table salt - refined salt suitable for use at the table or in cooking
  • Degree Lower: rock salt - natural, unrefined, or minimally processed salt in large crystalline form
