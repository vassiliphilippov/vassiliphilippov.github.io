sunflower-seed oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌflaʊɚ sid ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌflaʊə siːd ɔɪl / (BrE)
a pale yellow oil extracted from sunflower seeds, commonly used in cooking and as a salad oil
oil extracted from sunflower seeds

Examples

She prefers to use sunflower-seed oil when baking cakes because of its light flavor.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of sunflower-seed oil instead of olive oil.
Doctors recommend sunflower-seed oil for people looking to lower their cholesterol levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable oil - any oil derived from plants, used in cooking
