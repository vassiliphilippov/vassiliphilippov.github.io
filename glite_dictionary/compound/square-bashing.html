square-bashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɛr ˌbæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɛː ˌbaʃɪŋ / (BrE)
repetitive drill or marching exercises performed by soldiers on a barracks parade ground, especially during initial training
military drill on a parade ground
british

Examples

The recruits spent hours doing square-bashing every morning during basic training.
He complained that square-bashing was the most tedious part of army life.
After weeks of square-bashing, the soldiers finally mastered their marching techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - systematic military training involving marching and exercises
