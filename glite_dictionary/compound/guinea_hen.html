guinea hen

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni hɛn / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni hɛn / (BrE)
a female guinea fowl
female guinea fowl

Examples

The farmer raised several guinea hens to help control insects on his property.
A guinea hen will often be more vocal than her male counterpart.
We watched as the guinea hen led her chicks through the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea fowl - an African bird with spotted plumage, often domesticated

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni hɛn / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni hɛn / (BrE)
The meat or flesh of a guinea fowl, especially that of hens, used as food.
meat or flesh of the guinea fowl

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious stew using fresh guinea hen.
Roasted guinea hen is often served with seasonal vegetables in gourmet restaurants.
The recipe calls for one whole guinea hen, cleaned and cut into pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea fowl - bird native to Africa, often domesticated for meat or eggs

Etymology

From 'guinea' (referring to West Africa) and 'hen' (female fowl).
