subphylum Urochorda

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfaɪləm ˌjʊroʊˈkɔrdə / (AmE), / sʌbˌfʌɪləm ˌjʊərəˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
A major subdivision of the phylum Chordata, comprising tunicates or sea squirts, marine animals characterized by a sac-like body and a notochord during some stage of development.
taxonomic group of tunicates

Examples

Scientists study members of the subphylum Urochorda to learn more about chordate evolution.
Larvae in the subphylum Urochorda have a notochord that disappears as they mature.
The subphylum Urochorda includes various tunicates found in oceans around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunicate - marine animal with sac-like body; member of Urochorda

Etymology

From New Latin: 'subphylum', from Latin 'sub-' (under) + Greek 'phylon' (race, tribe); 'Urochorda' from Greek 'oura' (tail) + 'chorda' (cord, string), referring to the tail cord or notochord.
