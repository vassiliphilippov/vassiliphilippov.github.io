bearing wall

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrɪŋ wɔl / (AmE), / ˈbeərɪŋ wɔːl / (BrE)
A wall in a building that supports a floor or the roof above it.
wall that supports floor or roof

Examples

The architect determined that the living room's bearing wall could not be removed without adding extra support.
During renovations, we discovered a hidden bearing wall supporting the second floor.
It's important to know which is the bearing wall before making any structural changes to the house.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partition wall - a wall that divides spaces but does not support structural loads
