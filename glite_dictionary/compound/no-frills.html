no-frills

1. ADJ

/ ˌnoʊˈfrɪlz / (AmE), / ˌnəʊˈfrɪlz / (BrE)
Without unnecessary or extra features; simple and basic in design or service.
lacking unnecessary features; basic

Examples

The airline offers a no-frills service with no free meals or entertainment.
He bought a no-frills phone that just calls and texts.
Our no-frills fitness club is perfect for people who want to focus on working out.

Synset Examples

he got a no-frills introduction to the job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basic - simple and involving only the most essential elements
  • Antonym: luxurious - providing great comfort or elegance, often with unnecessary extra features

2. ADJ

/ noʊ ˈfrɪlz / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈfrɪlz / (BrE)
Simple and basic; without unnecessary features, decorations, or extras.
simple, basic, lacking extras

Examples

The airline offers a no-frills ticket that covers just the flight and nothing more.
He bought a no-frills phone that can only make calls and send texts.
Many budget hotels provide only no-frills accommodation for travelers on a tight budget.

Synset Examples

The new restaurant has no frills, just good food.
I prefer a no-frills approach to my workouts.
She chose a no-frills package for her vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barebones - very basic, with only the most essential features
  • Antonym: luxury - state of great comfort or extravagant living
