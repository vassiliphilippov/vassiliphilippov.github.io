empty words

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpti wɝdz / (AmE), / ˈɛmpti wɜːdz / (BrE)
words or statements that have no real meaning, value, or substance; talk that is insincere or unproductive
statements lacking meaning or substance

Examples

Politicians are often accused of making promises that are just empty words.
His apology felt like empty words because he repeated the same mistakes.
The speech was full of empty words, offering no real solutions to the problem.

Synset Examples

mere rhetoric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetoric - language that is persuasive but often insincere or empty
  • Synonym: platitude - a remark or statement, especially one with moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful
