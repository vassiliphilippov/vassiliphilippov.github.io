ape-man

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪpˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈeɪpˌman / (BrE)
A person who is believed to have been raised by apes, especially as imagined in fiction or mythology.
person raised by apes (fictional)

Examples

The story of Tarzan is about an ape-man who grows up in the jungle.
Some scientists once speculated about the possibility of an ape-man existing in the distant past.
The movie portrayed an ape-man struggling to adapt to human society.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪpˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈeɪpˌmæn / (BrE)
a hypothetical or prehistoric human ancestor, especially one considered to be intermediate in form or characteristics between apes and modern humans
prehistoric human ancestor between apes and humans

Examples

Scientists once searched for the elusive ape-man believed to bridge the gap between apes and humans.
Discoveries of ancient skulls prompted debates about the existence of a true ape-man.
In early 20th-century literature, the character of the ape-man symbolized the dawn of human evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hominid - any member of the biological family Hominidae (great apes and humans)
  • Degree Higher: human - modern member of the species Homo sapiens
  • Degree Lower: ape - a primate, especially one without a tail, such as gorillas or chimpanzees
