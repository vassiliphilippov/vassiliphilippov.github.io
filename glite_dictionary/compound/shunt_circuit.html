shunt circuit

1. NOUN

/ ʃʌnt ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
A closed electrical circuit in which current divides into two or more branches before recombining to complete the circuit.
electrical circuit with divided current paths

Examples

In a shunt circuit, the current flows through multiple paths, allowing for redundancy if one pathway fails.
The electrician explained that a shunt circuit can prevent the overheating of components.
If too much current is drawn in a shunt circuit, it may cause the fuse to blow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallel circuit - an electrical circuit with branches so the current divides and recombines

Etymology

From 'shunt', meaning to turn aside or divert, combined with 'circuit'.
