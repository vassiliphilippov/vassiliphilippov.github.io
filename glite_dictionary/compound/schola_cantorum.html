schola cantorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌskoʊlə kænˈtɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌskəʊlə kænˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
A choir school, especially one attached to a cathedral or monastery, where boys with singing ability receive a general education.
choir school in a cathedral or monastery

Examples

The famous schola cantorum trained generations of choristers in the cathedral.
Many European cities established a schola cantorum during the Middle Ages to educate choirboys.
Membership in the schola cantorum was highly sought after by musically talented boys.

Etymology

From Latin 'schola cantorum' meaning 'school of singers'.
