chamber orchestra

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbər ˈɔrkəstrə / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə ˈɔːkɪstrə / (BrE)
A small orchestra, typically consisting of 10–40 musicians, that usually performs classical music.
small classical music orchestra

Examples

The chamber orchestra performed an intimate concert in the historic church.
She is the principal violinist of a renowned chamber orchestra.
Many composers have written pieces specifically for chamber orchestra due to its unique sound.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: orchestra - a large group of musicians who play classical music together

Etymology

From 'chamber' (a room) and 'orchestra' (from Greek 'orkhēstra', the space for the chorus in a theater).
