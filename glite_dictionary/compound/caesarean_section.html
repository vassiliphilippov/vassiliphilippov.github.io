caesarean section

1. NOUN

/ siˈzɛriən ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / siˈzɛːriən ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for delivering a baby through incisions in the mother's abdominal wall and uterus
surgical delivery of a baby
medical

Examples

After complications during labor, the doctors decided to perform a caesarean section.
A caesarean section can help ensure the safety of both mother and child in certain cases.
Her recovery from the caesarean section took several weeks, but she and the baby were healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: C-section - informal abbreviation of caesarean section

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesarea' (belonging to Caesar), from the belief that Julius Caesar was born this way.
