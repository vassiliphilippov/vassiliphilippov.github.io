alginic acid

1. NOUN

/ ælˈdʒɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ælˈdʒɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a gelatinous polysaccharide obtained from brown seaweeds, used especially as a thickener or emulsifier in food and pharmaceuticals
gum from seaweed used as thickener

Examples

Ice cream manufacturers often add alginic acid to improve texture and prevent ice crystals from forming.
The pharmaceutical industry uses alginic acid as a binding and stabilizing agent in tablets.
As a naturally derived thickener, alginic acid is widely accepted in vegan food products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agar - a gelatinous substance from seaweed used as a thickener
  • Synonym: gelatin - a colorless, flavorless thickening agent derived from collagen

Etymology

From 'alga' (seaweed) + '-inic' (from chemical nomenclature), referring to its origin from algae.
