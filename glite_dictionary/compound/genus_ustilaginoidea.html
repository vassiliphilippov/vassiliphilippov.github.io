genus Ustilaginoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːstɪˌlædʒɪˈnɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌjuːstɪˌlædʒɪˈnɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A genus of imperfect fungi that cause plant diseases such as smut.
fungus genus causing plant diseases
medical

Examples

Rice crops are susceptible to infection by genus Ustilaginoidea during the rainy season.
Scientists are studying genus Ustilaginoidea to develop disease-resistant plants.
Smut caused by genus Ustilaginoidea can severely reduce agricultural yields.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ustilago' (smut) and the standard genus suffix '-oidea'.
