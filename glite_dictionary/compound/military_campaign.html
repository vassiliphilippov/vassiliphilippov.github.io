military campaign

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪləˌtɛri kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
A series of coordinated military operations intended to achieve a specific objective within a defined area or period.
coordinated series of military operations

Examples

The Normandy military campaign was crucial to the Allied victory in World War II.
He studied the tactics used during the military campaign in North Africa.
The general planned a new military campaign to capture the enemy's stronghold.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: operation - a single organized activity in warfare
  • Degree Higher: war - a large-scale armed conflict between countries or groups
  • Degree Lower: battle - a single fight between armed forces
