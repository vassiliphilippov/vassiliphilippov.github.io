shroud-waving coverage

1. NOUN

/ ʃraʊd ˈweɪvɪŋ ˈkʌv(ə)rɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʃraʊd ˈweɪvɪŋ ˈkʌv(ə)rɪdʒ / (BrE)
Sensationalized reporting of tragic events, often focusing excessively on grief and tragedy.
sensational reporting of tragic events

Examples

The news channel came under fire for its shroud-waving coverage of the disaster.
Many viewers found the shroud-waving coverage disrespectful to the victims' families.
Critics argue that shroud-waving coverage does little to inform and instead fuels public anxiety.

Synset Examples

Experts criticized the media for their shroud-waving coverage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensationalism - use of shocking stories or language to provoke public interest

Etymology

From 'shroud,' referring to a burial cloth, and 'waving,' conveying a display; originally figurative in media critique.
