district line

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪs.trɪkt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.trɪkt laɪn / (BrE)
the boundary that separates two districts or administrative areas.
boundary between two districts

Examples

Residents on either side of the district line pay different rates of council tax.
The new school was built close to the district line separating the city from the suburbs.
Disputes often arise over which side of the district line certain properties belong to.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - a line separating two political or geographical areas
  • Synonym: boundary - a real or imaginary line marking the edge of a state or territory
