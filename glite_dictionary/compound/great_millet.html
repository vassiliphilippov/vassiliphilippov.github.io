great millet

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈmɪlət / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a tall cereal grass of tropical Africa cultivated for its edible grain, used in food for humans and animals
a tropical cereal grass, edible grain

Examples

Farmers in parts of Africa grow great millet as a staple food crop.
The leaves of great millet are long and have sawtooth edges, similar to Indian corn.
Both livestock and people rely on great millet for nutrition in many rural communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearl millet - a related species of millet with small, round grains
  • Synonym: sorghum - another important cereal crop related to millet
