exothermic reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.soʊˈθɜr.mɪk riˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səʊˈθɜː.mɪk riˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which heat is released to the surroundings
chemical reaction releasing heat
medical

Examples

Combustion of gasoline is an exothermic reaction that powers car engines.
When an exothermic reaction occurs, the temperature of the surroundings usually rises.
Mixing water and quicklime produces an exothermic reaction that releases a lot of heat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endothermic reaction - a chemical reaction that absorbs heat from its surroundings

Etymology

From Greek 'exo-' (outside) and 'thermos' (heat).
