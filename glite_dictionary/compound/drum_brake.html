drum brake

1. NOUN

/ drʌm breɪk / (AmE), / drʌm breɪk / (BrE)
A type of brake in which friction is applied to the inside of a rotating drum by brake shoes, commonly used in vehicles.
type of automotive brake using a drum

Examples

The car was fitted with drum brakes on all four wheels.
Many older vehicles use drum brakes instead of disc brakes.
The mechanic explained that drum brakes require regular inspection to ensure safe operation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disc brake - brake using a disc and caliper instead of a drum
