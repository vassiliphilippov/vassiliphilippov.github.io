Mexican cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
a tall, spreading evergreen tree native to Mexico, recognized for its drooping branches; often grown as an ornamental tree and also known as Cupressus lusitanica
a tall evergreen tree native to Mexico

Examples

The Mexican cypress is frequently planted in parks for its elegant, drooping branches.
Botanists discovered an ancient Mexican cypress growing near the village.
She chose a Mexican cypress for her garden due to its evergreen foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any coniferous tree or shrub of the genus Cupressus
