biodegradable pollution

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.dɪˈɡreɪ.də.bəl pəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊ.dɪˈɡreɪ.də.bəl pəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
Pollution consisting of substances that can be broken down by natural processes and therefore do not cause permanent harm to the environment.
pollution that can be naturally decomposed

Examples

Local authorities are encouraging industries to reduce biodegradable pollution by improving waste treatment methods.
Although biodegradable pollution eventually breaks down, it can still harm rivers if present in large quantities.
The impact of biodegradable pollution is often less severe than that of plastics or toxic chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-biodegradable pollution - pollution that does not break down naturally and is long-lasting

Etymology

From 'biodegradable', meaning capable of being broken down by living organisms, and 'pollution', from Latin 'pollutio'.
