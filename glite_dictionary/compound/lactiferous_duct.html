lactiferous duct

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtɪfərəs dʌkt / (AmE), / lækˈtɪfərəs dʌkt / (BrE)
a duct in the mammary gland that carries milk from the lobules to the nipple
milk-carrying duct in the mammary gland
medical

Examples

Blockage of a lactiferous duct can lead to discomfort and swelling in the breast.
Each lactiferous duct carries milk toward the nipple during breastfeeding.
Doctors examined the lactiferous ducts for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammary gland - the gland in female mammals that produces milk
  • Synonym: milk duct - general term for ducts carrying milk in the breast

Etymology

From Latin *lac* (milk) and *ferre* (to bear), meaning 'milk-carrying'.
