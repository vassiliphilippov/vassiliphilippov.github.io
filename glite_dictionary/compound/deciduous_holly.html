deciduous holly

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɪdʒuəs ˈhɑːli / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪdjʊəs ˈhɒli / (BrE)
a North American holly tree (Ilex decidua) that loses its leaves in winter and produces bright red berries; also called possumhaw or swamp holly
North American holly tree with red berries

Examples

The bright red berries of the deciduous holly are especially striking in winter.
Gardeners often use deciduous holly to add winter interest to their landscapes.
Unlike its evergreen relatives, deciduous holly sheds its leaves in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possumhaw - another name for deciduous holly
  • Synonym: swamp holly - another name for deciduous holly
  • Antonym: evergreen holly - holly species that keep their leaves year-round

Etymology

From Latin 'deciduus' (falling off) and Old English 'holegn' (holly).
