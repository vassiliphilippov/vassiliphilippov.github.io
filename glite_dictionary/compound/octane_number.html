octane number

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːk.teɪn ˈnʌm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒk.teɪn ˈnʌm.bə / (BrE)
A numerical measure of a fuel's resistance to knocking in spark-ignition engines, based on a standard scale.
measure of a fuel's antiknock properties

Examples

The gasoline at this station has a high octane number, making it suitable for performance vehicles.
Many drivers are advised to use fuel with the correct octane number for their car's engine.
Racing cars often require gasoline with a higher octane number to prevent engine knocking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octane rating - alternative term for octane number

Etymology

From 'octane', a hydrocarbon, and 'number' referring to the measurement scale in fuel chemistry.
