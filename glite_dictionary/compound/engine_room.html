engine room

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒɪn ˌrum / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn ˌruːm / (BrE)
a room on a ship, train, or industrial building where the engine or main mechanical equipment is housed and operated
room for housing engines (esp. on a ship)

Examples

The mechanic spent hours repairing equipment in the engine room.
During the tour, we were shown how the engine room keeps the ship running smoothly.
A warning light alerted the crew to a problem deep within the engine room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boiler room - room in a building or ship where boilers are located

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒɪn ˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn ˌruːm / (BrE)
the part of a ship or other vehicle where the engines and related machinery are operated
area where engines are operated on a ship

Examples

The tour included a visit to the engine room of the cruise ship.
Strict safety protocols must be followed in the engine room at all times.
Noise from the engine room could be heard throughout the lower decks.

Synset Examples

He was promoted after his work in the engine room impressed the captain.
The engineers worked tirelessly in the engine room to keep everything running.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machinery space - area on a ship where machinery is housed
