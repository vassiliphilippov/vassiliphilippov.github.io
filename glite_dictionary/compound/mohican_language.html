Mohican language

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhiːkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məʊˈhiːkən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language historically spoken by the Mohican people of the northeastern United States
Algonquian language of the Mohican people

Examples

The Mohican language was once spoken throughout the Hudson River Valley.
Linguists have worked to document and preserve the endangered Mohican language.
He studied the structure and vocabulary of the Mohican language for his doctoral research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian language - a family of Native American languages spoken in North America

Etymology

From 'Mohican', the name of the indigenous people, combined with 'language'.
