melanocyte-stimulating hormone

1. NOUN

/ məˌlænoʊˌsaɪt ˈstɪmjəˌleɪtɪŋ ˌhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / mɪˌlanəˌsaɪt ˈstɪmjʊˌleɪtɪŋ ˌhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that regulates the amount of pigment in skin cells called melanocytes
hormone regulating skin pigmentation
medical

Examples

The pituitary gland releases melanocyte-stimulating hormone to influence the color of the skin.
An excess of melanocyte-stimulating hormone can cause skin darkening in some medical conditions.
Researchers are studying how melanocyte-stimulating hormone affects pigmentation disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MSH - abbreviation for melanocyte-stimulating hormone

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) and 'kytos' (cell), referring to melanocytes, plus 'stimulating hormone.'
