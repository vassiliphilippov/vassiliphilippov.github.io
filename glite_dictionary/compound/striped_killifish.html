striped killifish

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪpt ˈkɪlɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈstraɪpt ˈkɪlɪfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, black-barred fish (Fundulus majalis) found in bays and coastal marshes along the Atlantic and Gulf Coast of the United States
a North American coastal fish with black bars

Examples

The striped killifish can often be seen darting among the grasses in tidal marshes.
Researchers studied a population of striped killifish along the Gulf shoreline.
Juvenile striped killifish are an important food source for coastal birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killifish - a small, egg-laying fish of the family Fundulidae
