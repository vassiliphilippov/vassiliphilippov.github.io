bluefin tuna

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌfɪn ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈbluːfɪn ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
a large species of tuna found in temperate seas, known for its size and value as a food fish
a large species of tuna fish

Examples

The chef prepared sushi using fresh bluefin tuna flown in that morning.
Overfishing has made bluefin tuna a threatened species in many parts of the world.
A single bluefin tuna can weigh more than 600 kilograms and fetch a high price at auction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - a large marine fish in the mackerel family

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːfɪn ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈbluːfɪn ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
the flesh of a very large species of tuna, prized as food, especially in sushi and sashimi
flesh of a large tuna used as food

Examples

The restaurant is famous for serving the freshest bluefin tuna in its sushi rolls.
She ordered a salad topped with seared bluefin tuna and a tangy vinaigrette.
Due to overfishing, bluefin tuna is considered a delicacy and commands high prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - flesh of tuna fish used as food
