Innocents' Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnəsənts deɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsənts deɪ / (BrE)
December 28, a Christian feast day commemorating the killing of the children of Bethlehem by King Herod as described in the Bible.
Christian feast on December 28

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Innocents' Day to remember the children of Bethlehem.
In some countries, Innocents' Day is marked by traditions similar to April Fools' Day.
The story behind Innocents' Day comes from the Gospel of Matthew in the New Testament.

Etymology

Named for the biblical 'innocents' (children) killed by Herod, as recounted in the Gospel of Matthew.
