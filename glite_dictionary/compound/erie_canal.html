Erie Canal

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪri kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈɪəri kəˈnæl / (BrE)
an artificial waterway in New York State, connecting the Hudson River at Albany with Lake Erie at Buffalo, built in the early 19th century
historic waterway in New York State

Examples

The construction of the Erie Canal transformed New York into a major trade hub.
Many towns along the Erie Canal flourished due to increased commerce.
Tourists often visit the remnants of the historic Erie Canal during the summer.

Etymology

Named after Lake Erie, which was named after the Erie people, a Native American tribe.
