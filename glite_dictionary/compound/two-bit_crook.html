two-bit crook

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuː bɪt ˈkrʊk / (AmE), / ˌtuː bɪt ˈkrʊk / (BrE)
A petty or insignificant criminal, especially one involved in minor wrongdoing or small-time scams.
petty or insignificant criminal
informal american

Examples

The police quickly dismissed him as just a two-bit crook with no real influence.
She refused to work with a two-bit crook after her last experience.
Everyone knew he was nothing more than a two-bit crook hustling on the streets.

Synset Examples

He was just a two-bit crook in the scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petty criminal - a criminal who commits minor crimes
  • Antonym: mastermind - a person who plans and controls a complex crime or activity

Etymology

From US slang 'two-bit' meaning of little value, referencing the 25-cent piece ('two bits').
