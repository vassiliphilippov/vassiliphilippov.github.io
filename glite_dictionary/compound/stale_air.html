stale air

1. NOUN

/ steɪl ɛr / (AmE), / steɪl eə / (BrE)
air that is unpleasant because it is old, stagnant, or lacking in freshness
old, unpleasant or stagnant air

Examples

After being closed for months, the house was full of stale air and dust.
She opened the window to let out the stale air and bring in a breeze.
The stale air in the subway made everyone feel tired.

Synset Examples

The air in the room felt stale and needed refreshing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh air - air that is clean or recently circulated
