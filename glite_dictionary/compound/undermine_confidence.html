to undermine confidence

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈmaɪn kənˈfɪdəns / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈmaɪn kənˈfɪdəns / (BrE)
to gradually weaken someone's self-assurance or trust in themselves
weaken someone's self-assurance

Examples

Negative feedback from a manager can undermine confidence in an employee's abilities.
Frequent setbacks may undermine confidence in the success of a new project.
Spreading false rumors is a tactic often used to undermine confidence in political opponents.

Synset Examples

His constant criticism served to undermine her confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake confidence - to make someone's confidence less certain or secure
