acyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.sɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.sɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a chemical group formed by removing one hydroxyl group from a carboxylic acid, represented as RCO− where R is an organic group
chemical group derived from acids
medical

Examples

The acetyl group is a common acyl group in organic chemistry.
In the reaction, the enzyme transfers the acyl group to the substrate.
Carboxylic acids form derivatives by replacing the hydroxyl with an acyl group.

Synset Examples

an example of the acyl group is the acetyl group

Etymology

From French acyle, from Latin acidum (acid) + -yl (chemical suffix for groups).
