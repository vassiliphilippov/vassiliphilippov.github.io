cesarean section

1. NOUN

/ səˈsɛriən ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈzɛəriən ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for delivering a baby through incisions made in the mother's abdominal wall and uterus
surgical childbirth procedure
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a cesarean section because of complications during labor.
She recovered quickly after undergoing a cesarean section last month.
Some women choose a planned cesarean section for medical or personal reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: C-section - abbreviation for cesarean section

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesareus' (relating to Caesar), due to the belief that Julius Caesar was delivered by this method.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈzɛri.ən ˈsɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈzɛəri.ən ˈsɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a baby is delivered through an incision made in the mother's abdominal wall and uterus
surgical delivery of a baby
medical

Examples

After hours of labor, the doctors decided that a cesarean section was necessary for the safety of both mother and baby.
She recovered well after her cesarean section and was able to return home within a few days.
Cases of cesarean section have been increasing in many countries due to various medical and personal reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: C-section - abbreviation for cesarean section

Etymology

From Latin caesarea (relating to Caesar); named from the belief that Julius Caesar was born by this procedure.
