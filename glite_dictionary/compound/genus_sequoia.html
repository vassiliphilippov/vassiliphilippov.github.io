genus Sequoia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of the redwood trees, formerly classified in the family Taxodiaceae, whose members are among the largest trees in the world.
botanical genus of redwood trees

Examples

The genus Sequoia contains the famous coast redwood, known for its great height.
Botanists debated the classification of the genus Sequoia for many years.
Only one living species remains in the genus Sequoia, making it unique among conifers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sequoia - type genus of the redwood trees
  • Synonym: redwood - common name for tall coniferous trees of the genus Sequoia

Etymology

From Latin 'Sequoia', the type genus, named after Sequoyah, a Cherokee silversmith.
