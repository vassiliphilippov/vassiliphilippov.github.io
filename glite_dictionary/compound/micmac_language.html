Micmac language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪkˌmæk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪkˌmak ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Mi'kmaq people of northeastern Canada and parts of the northeastern United States
Algonquian language spoken by the Mi'kmaq

Examples

The Micmac language is still taught in some schools in Nova Scotia.
Many elders are fluent speakers of the Micmac language.
Linguists have written dictionaries to help preserve the Micmac language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - family of Native American languages including Micmac

Etymology

From Mi'kmaq *Míkmawísimk* (their language), anglicized as 'Micmac'.
