madia oil plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdiə ɔɪl plænt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdɪə ɔɪl plɑːnt / (BrE)
A South American herb with sticky glandular foliage that is cultivated as a source of madia oil.
South American oil-producing herb

Examples

The madia oil plant is valued for the quality of its extracted oil.
Farmers in the region have begun cultivating madia oil plant for industrial purposes.
The sticky leaves of the madia oil plant help protect it from herbivores.

Etymology

From 'Madia', the genus name, derived from the Chilean plant name 'madi'.
