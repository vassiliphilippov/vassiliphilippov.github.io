Alcaic verse

1. NOUN

/ ælˈkeɪɪk vɜrs / (AmE), / ælˈkeɪɪk vɜːs / (BrE)
A form of verse in Greek and Latin poetry characterized by stanzas of four lines in a specific metrical pattern, reputedly invented by Alcaeus.
type of ancient Greek/Latin verse form

Examples

The ancient poet composed in Alcaic verse to emulate the style of Alcaeus.
Many English poets have experimented with Alcaic verse in their translations of classical works.
Learning to write in Alcaic verse requires a deep understanding of classical meters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sapphic verse - Another classical Greek metrical form associated with Sappho

Etymology

From Alcaeus, a Greek poet of the 6th century BC, combined with Latin 'Alcaicus' (of Alcaeus).
