abrading stone

1. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪdɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / əˈbreɪdɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
a primitive tool made from stone, typically sandstone, used to grind, smooth, or sharpen other objects by abrasion
primitive stone tool for grinding or smoothing

Examples

Archaeologists discovered an abrading stone at the ancient campsite, indicating the inhabitants worked with bone and wood tools.
The craftsman used an abrading stone to smooth the rough edges of his handmade arrowheads.
Many museums display abrading stones to illustrate the daily life of prehistoric people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whetstone - a stone used for sharpening cutting tools
