southern cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌð.ɚn ˈsaɪ.prəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌð.ən ˈsaɪ.prəs / (BrE)
the wood of a cypress tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its expanded trunk base and found in coastal swamps or river bottoms
wood from a southeastern U.S. cypress tree

Examples

The deck was constructed from southern cypress because of its durability in wet climates.
Furniture made of southern cypress can withstand high humidity and is popular in the South.
They harvested southern cypress from the swamps to use in boat building.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərn ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
a common cypress tree native to the southeastern United States, especially found in coastal swamps and river bottoms, with a trunk expanded at the base
cypress tree native to southeastern U.S. swamps

Examples

The southern cypress thrives in the wetlands of Louisiana and Mississippi.
Many swamps in the southeastern United States are dominated by southern cypress trees.
The distinctive flared trunk of the southern cypress helps it adapt to flooded environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - another term for the southern cypress tree, Taxodium distichum
