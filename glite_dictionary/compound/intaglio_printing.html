intaglio printing

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtælioʊ ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæliəʊ ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a printing technique in which an image is incised into a surface, and the incised area holds the ink used to make prints
printing method using incised plates

Examples

The artist specializes in intaglio printing to create her limited edition artworks.
Many banknotes use intaglio printing to add texture and prevent counterfeiting.
In the workshop, students learned the basics of intaglio printing with copper plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - process of incising a design onto a hard surface for printing
  • Antonym: relief printing - printing process where the image is raised above the background

Etymology

From Italian 'intaglio' (engraving, incision), from 'intagliare' (to engrave or carve).
