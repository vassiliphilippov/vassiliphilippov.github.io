roguish flair

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊɡɪʃ flɛr / (AmE), / ˈrəʊɡɪʃ fleə / (BrE)
a distinctive and attractive quality marked by mischievous charm or playfulness
charmingly mischievous quality or style

Examples

He told the story with a roguish flair that had everyone laughing.
Her roguish flair made her stand out among the more serious candidates.
The actor brought a roguish flair to the otherwise straightforward role.

Synset Examples

The character had a roguish flair that captivated the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flair - a natural ability or distinctive style
