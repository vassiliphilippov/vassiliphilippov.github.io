copra oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊprə ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒprə ɔɪl / (BrE)
edible oil extracted from the dried kernel (copra) of coconuts, widely used in cooking and industry
oil made from dried coconut kernel

Examples

The factory processes large quantities of copra oil for export each year.
Many cosmetic products list copra oil as a moisturizing ingredient.
Chefs in tropical regions often use copra oil for frying due to its high smoke point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coconut oil - oil obtained from the flesh of coconuts

Etymology

From Spanish and Portuguese 'copra', from Malayalam 'koppara' (dried coconut), combined with 'oil'.
