Sublime Porte

1. NOUN

/ səˈblaɪm ˈpɔrt / (AmE), / səˈblaɪm ˈpɔːt / (BrE)
the Ottoman government or court at Constantinople, especially in diplomatic contexts.
the Ottoman court/government in Constantinople

Examples

European ambassadors were received at the Sublime Porte to discuss matters of diplomacy.
The Sublime Porte was synonymous with the central authority of the Ottoman Empire.
Many treaties between Western powers and the Sublime Porte shaped international relations in the eighteenth century.

Etymology

From French 'Sublime Porte', translating Ottoman Turkish 'Bāb-ı ʿĀlī' (High Gate), from the gate of the palace where state affairs were conducted.

2. NOUN

/ səˌblaɪm ˈpɔrt / (AmE), / səˌblaɪm ˈpɔːt / (BrE)
the central government of the Ottoman Empire, especially as the authority dealing with foreign affairs
Ottoman Empire's central government
archaic

Examples

During the 19th century, European powers often sent ambassadors to the Sublime Porte in Istanbul.
Many reform edicts were issued by the Sublime Porte during the Tanzimat period.
The Sublime Porte was responsible for negotiating several major international treaties.

Synset Examples

The diplomats negotiated terms with the Sublime Porte in Istanbul.
The Sublime Porte played a crucial role in European politics.
Many treaties were signed under the authority of the Sublime Porte.

Etymology

From French 'Sublime Porte', translation of Ottoman Turkish 'Bâb-ı Âli' meaning 'High Gate', referring to the gate of the Ottoman government complex in Istanbul.
