Postal Rate Commission

1. PROPN

/ ˈpoʊstəl ˈreɪt kəˌmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstl ˈreɪt kəˌmɪʃən / (BrE)
an independent U.S. federal agency responsible for recommending changes in postal rates and overseeing the U.S. postal service's pricing policies
U.S. agency regulating postal rates
american

Examples

The Postal Rate Commission reviewed the proposal to increase first-class mail prices.
After months of analysis, the Postal Rate Commission issued its recommendations to Congress.
The Postal Rate Commission plays an important role in ensuring fair postal rates nationwide.
