cosmological constant

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːzməˈlɑːdʒɪkəl ˈkɑːnstənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a constant added by Einstein to the equations of general relativity, representing a force causing the universe's expansion
constant in relativity theory causing universe's expansion

Examples

The value of the cosmological constant influences the rate at which the universe expands.
Einstein originally introduced the cosmological constant to achieve a static universe model.
Modern observations suggest the cosmological constant may explain the acceleration in cosmic expansion.

Etymology

Cosmological from 'cosmology' (the study of the universe) and constant, referring to its fixed value in equations.
