south wind

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ wɪnd / (AmE), / saʊθ wɪnd / (BrE)
a wind that blows from the south
wind from the south

Examples

Farmers welcomed the arrival of the south wind that brought warmer temperatures to the region.
The scent of the ocean drifted inland on the gentle south wind.
As the south wind increased, the sailing became much easier for the crew.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: north wind - a wind from the north
  • Synonym: east wind - a wind from the east
  • Degree Higher: southwesterly - a wind from the southwest
