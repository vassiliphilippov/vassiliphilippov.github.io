Remembrance Sunday

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɛm.brəns ˈsʌn.deɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛm.brəns ˈsʌn.deɪ / (BrE)
The Sunday closest to November 11, observed in the UK and Commonwealth nations to commemorate those who died in World War I and World War II.
UK memorial day for war dead
british

Examples

The country falls silent at 11 a.m. on Remembrance Sunday to honor those who gave their lives in war.
Wreaths of poppies are laid at memorials across the nation on Remembrance Sunday.
Television broadcasts often feature ceremonies and parades that take place on Remembrance Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Armistice Day - Commemorative day on November 11 for the end of World War I
