Hudson Bay collared lemming

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌdsən beɪ ˈkɑːlɚd ˈlɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌdsən beɪ ˈkɒləd ˈlɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
a species of lemming found in northern Canada, notable for its distinctive collar-like fur marking
a lemming species native to northern Canada

Examples

The Hudson Bay collared lemming is well adapted to the harsh Arctic environment.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of the Hudson Bay collared lemming through the tundra.
Populations of Hudson Bay collared lemmings fluctuate dramatically depending on food supply and predation.

Etymology

Named after Hudson Bay, Canada, where it is commonly found, and its collar-like fur marking.
