Kaw River

1. PROPN

/ kɔ ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / kɔː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in northeastern Kansas, USA, that flows eastward and joins the Missouri River; also known as the Kansas River
river in northeastern Kansas, USA

Examples

The Kaw River flooded several neighborhoods after heavy rainfall last spring.
Many local communities rely on water from the Kaw River for municipal supplies.
Scientists are studying the biodiversity found along the Kaw River in northeastern Kansas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kansas River - alternative name for the Kaw River
  • Degree Higher: Missouri River - major US river to which the Kaw River is a tributary

Etymology

Named after the Kaw (Kansa) Native American tribe.
