little by little

1. ADV

/ ˈlɪt̬.əl baɪ ˈlɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl baɪ ˈlɪt.əl / (BrE)
Gradually; by small amounts at a time instead of all at once.
gradually, in small increments

Examples

Little by little, the city recovered from the effects of the storm.
If you practice little by little each day, you'll make steady progress.
She saved money little by little until she could afford a new laptop.

Synset Examples

the research structure has developed piecemeal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradually - slowly over time
  • Antonym: suddenly - happening quickly or without warning
