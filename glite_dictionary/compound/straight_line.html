straight line

1. NOUN

/ streɪt laɪn / (AmE), / streɪt laɪn / (BrE)
a line that does not curve, traced by a point moving in a constant direction; a line with zero curvature
a line with no curve; zero curvature

Examples

The artist used a ruler to draw a perfectly straight line across the page.
Light travels in a straight line unless it passes through a different medium.
He followed the straight line of the road all the way to the horizon.

Synset Examples

the shortest distance between two points is a straight line

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curve - a line that deviates from being straight and bends continuously
