stationary stochastic process

1. NOUN

/ ˌsteɪʃəˌneri stəˈkæstɪk ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌsteɪʃənəri stəˈkæstɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a stochastic process whose statistical properties, such as the distribution of random variables, do not change over time
stochastic process with time-invariant distribution
medical

Examples

The model assumes the underlying data is generated by a stationary stochastic process.
Economists often analyze whether time series exhibit the characteristics of a stationary stochastic process.
A white noise sequence is a classic example of a stationary stochastic process in statistics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonstationary process - a stochastic process whose statistical properties do change over time

Etymology

stationary from Latin 'stationarius' (standing at a station) + stochastic from Greek 'stochastikos' (skilled in aiming) + process from Latin 'processus' (advance, progress).
