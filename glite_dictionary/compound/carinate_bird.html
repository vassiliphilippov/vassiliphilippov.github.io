carinate bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrɪneɪt bɝd / (AmE), / ˈkæɹɪneɪt bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird with a keeled breastbone, which allows for the attachment of strong flight muscles
bird with a keeled breastbone

Examples

Penguins are an example of a carinate bird, as they possess a distinct keel on their breastbone.
Unlike ostriches, every carinate bird is adapted for flight due to its keeled sternum.
The presence of a keel distinguishes a carinate bird from a ratite bird.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ratite - flightless bird with an unkeeled breastbone

Etymology

From Latin 'carinatus' (keeled), from 'carina' (keel).
