conjugal right

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑndʒəɡəl raɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒʊɡəl raɪt / (BrE)
a right possessed by a spouse, especially to the companionship, support, and intimacy with their partner as part of marriage, including living together and sharing property
right of spouses to marital companionship and intimacy
legal

Examples

After several months apart, she filed a petition to restore her conjugal right to live with her husband.
The law recognizes certain conjugal rights that spouses may claim against each other.
He argued in court that his conjugal right to companionship was being denied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marital right - legal rights of marriage partners

Etymology

From Latin 'conjugalis' (of marriage), from 'conjugare' (to join together).
