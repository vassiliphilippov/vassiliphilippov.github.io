coenzyme Q

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɛnzaɪm ˈkju / (AmE), / kəʊˌɛnzaɪm ˈkjuː / (BrE)
any of a group of quinone compounds found in living cells that act as coenzymes, transferring electrons during cellular respiration.
quinone compound involved in cell respiration
medical

Examples

Coenzyme Q is essential for the proper functioning of the electron transport chain in mitochondria.
Doctors sometimes recommend coenzyme Q supplements for people with heart conditions.
Researchers studied how coenzyme Q levels affect cellular energy production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ubiquinone - another name for coenzyme Q

Etymology

Formed from 'coenzyme' and the letter 'Q', denoting its quinone structure.
