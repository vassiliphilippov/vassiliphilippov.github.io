genus Zostera

1. NOUN

/ ˈd͡ʒiːnəs zoʊˈstɛrə / (AmE), / ˈd͡ʒiːnəs zɒˈstɪərə / (BrE)
A small genus of widely distributed marine plants, commonly known as eelgrasses, sometimes classified in the family Zosteraceae.
a genus of marine plants (eelgrasses)

Examples

Researchers are studying the decline of genus Zostera populations in coastal waters.
The genus Zostera provides crucial habitat for many marine species.
Restoration projects often involve planting members of the genus Zostera to improve ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelgrass - common name for plants in the genus Zostera

Etymology

From Latin 'zostera', meaning seaweed or eelgrass; used in scientific taxonomy.
