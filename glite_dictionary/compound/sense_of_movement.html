sense of movement

1. NOUN

/ sɛns əv ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / sɛns əv ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
the ability to perceive the movement of one's limbs or body, usually through internal sensory mechanisms
awareness of body or limb movement
medical

Examples

After her injury, her sense of movement in the right arm was significantly reduced.
Physical therapists assess a patient's sense of movement to determine nerve function and coordination.
The drug is known to affect the sense of movement, making walking more difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense that detects position and movement of the body
