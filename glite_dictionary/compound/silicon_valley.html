Silicon Valley

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪlɪkən ˈvæli / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkən ˈvæli / (BrE)
a region in California, south of San Francisco, renowned for its concentration of high-technology companies
high-tech region in California

Examples

Many of the world's leading software firms are based in Silicon Valley.
She moved to Silicon Valley to start her career in tech.
Silicon Valley attracts entrepreneurs and engineers from around the globe.

Etymology

Named after the silicon used in semiconductor computer chips and 'valley' from Santa Clara Valley.

2. PROPN

/ ˌsɪlɪkən ˈvæli / (AmE), / ˌsɪlɪkən ˈvæli / (BrE)
an area in northern California notable as a center for high-tech industries and innovation, especially in computing and information technology
California tech industry region

Examples

Many entrepreneurs dream of moving to Silicon Valley to launch their tech startups.
Major technology companies like Google and Apple are headquartered in Silicon Valley.
The influence of Silicon Valley extends globally, shaping how we use technology every day.

Synset Examples

Tech innovators flock to Silicon Valley, the Mecca of startups.
The tech bro culture thrives in Silicon Valley.

Etymology

Named after silicon, used in semiconductors, reflecting the area's early focus on computer chips.
