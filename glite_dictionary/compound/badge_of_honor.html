badge of honor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbædʒ əv ˈɑnər / (AmE), / ˈbædʒ əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
A symbol, mark, or act regarded as a source of pride, achievement, or distinction.
symbol of pride or distinction

Examples

For many artists, being banned was a badge of honor that showed their work was impactful.
She wears her first rejection letter as a badge of honor in the publishing world.
Years of hard work and dedication are now his badge of honor.

Synset Examples

He wore that scar as a badge of honor.
Surviving the tough times became his badge of honor.
He joked that being called a ukrop was a badge of honor.
Completing the marathon was her badge of honor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark of distinction - a distinguishing sign of excellence or honor
  • Antonym: dishonor - lack or loss of honor or reputation
