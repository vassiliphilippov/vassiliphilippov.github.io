Ashanti pepper

1. NOUN

/ əˈʃɑːn.ti ˈpɛp.ɚ / (AmE), / əˈʃɑːn.ti ˈpɛp.ə / (BrE)
A spice made from the dried fruit of Piper guineense, similar to cubeb pepper but less bitter and with a fresher, more herbaceous flavour.
West African spice from Piper guineense

Examples

Ashanti cuisine often features Ashanti pepper as a key ingredient in spicy soups.
The chef imported Ashanti pepper to achieve an authentic flavour in the dish.
Because Ashanti pepper is so rare, it is used sparingly in traditional West African recipes.

Synset Examples

Even in West Africa, Ashanti pepper is an expensive spice and is used sparingly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubeb pepper - a related spice from Piper cubeba, with a more bitter taste

Etymology

Named after the Ashanti people of Ghana; 'pepper' from Old English 'pipor', from Latin 'piper'.
