principle of equivalence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ɪˈkwɪvələns / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ɪˈkwɪvələns / (BrE)
in physics, the principle stating that the effects observed in a uniform gravitational field are indistinguishable from those in an accelerated frame of reference
physics: indistinguishability of gravity and acceleration

Examples

Einstein's principle of equivalence is fundamental to the general theory of relativity.
According to the principle of equivalence, gravity and acceleration are locally indistinguishable.
In the classroom, the professor illustrated the principle of equivalence with thought experiments involving elevators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivalence principle - alternative phrasing for the same concept in physics
