intracapsular surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˈkæp.sjə.lɚ ˈsɝ.dʒə.ri / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈkæp.sjʊ.lə ˈsɜː.dʒəri / (BrE)
a type of cataract surgery in which the entire lens of the eye is removed, including its surrounding capsule
eye surgery removing lens and capsule
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended intracapsular surgery to treat her advanced cataract.
Advances in technology have made intracapsular surgery less common today.
Recovery from intracapsular surgery can require more care than with newer procedures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extracapsular surgery - cataract surgery where the lens capsule is left in place

Etymology

From 'intra-' (Latin for 'within') + 'capsular' (relating to a capsule), describing the removal within the lens capsule.
