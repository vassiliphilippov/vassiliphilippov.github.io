Ten Commandments

1. NOUN

/ tɛn kəˈmændmənts / (AmE), / tɛn kəˈmɑːndmənts / (BrE)
the set of biblical laws or rules given by God to Moses on Mount Sinai, forming a core ethical code in Judaism and Christianity
biblical laws given to Moses

Examples

Many people look to the Ten Commandments as a foundation for their moral beliefs.
The film about Moses depicted the giving of the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai.
Debate continues over whether the Ten Commandments should be displayed in public buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Decalogue - another name for the Ten Commandments, especially in religious or scholarly contexts

Etymology

From the biblical account of Moses receiving the commandments from God on Mount Sinai (Hebrew: עֲשֶׂרֶת הַדִּבְּרוֹת).
