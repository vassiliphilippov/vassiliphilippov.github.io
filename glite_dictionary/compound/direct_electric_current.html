direct electric current

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkɝrənt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
An electric current that flows in one direction steadily, without reversing its direction.
electric current flowing in one direction

Examples

A battery supplies direct electric current to power small electronic devices.
Solar panels generate direct electric current that can be used or converted for household use.
The experiment required a steady direct electric current to produce accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alternating current - an electric current that reverses direction periodically
