New Norwegian

1. NOUN

/ nuː nɔˈwiːʒən / (AmE), / njuː nɔˈwiːʒən / (BrE)
one of the two official written standards of the Norwegian language, based on rural Norwegian dialects and used especially in western Norway
official Norwegian written language variant

Examples

Many students in western Norway study New Norwegian as part of their curriculum.
Government documents are available in both Bokmål and New Norwegian.
She chose to write her novel in New Norwegian to reflect her heritage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Bokmål - the other, more commonly used standard of written Norwegian

Etymology

Translation of Norwegian 'nynorsk', from 'ny' (new) + 'norsk' (Norwegian).
