lupus erythematosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːpəs ˌɛr.ɪ.ˌθiː.məˈtoʊ.səs / (AmE), / ˈluː.pəs ˌɛr.ɪ.θiː.məˈtəʊ.səs / (BrE)
A chronic autoimmune disease causing inflammation and damage to skin, joints, and other connective tissue.
chronic autoimmune disease affecting skin and joints
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected lupus erythematosus after seeing the patient's distinctive skin rash.
Early diagnosis of lupus erythematosus can improve the effectiveness of treatment.
Many support groups exist for people living with lupus erythematosus.

Etymology

From Latin 'lupus' (wolf) and Greek 'erythēma' (redness) + '-tosus' (full of).
