fibrocystic disease of the pancreas

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪbroʊˈsɪstɪk dɪˈziz ʌv ðə pænˈkriəs / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbrəʊˈsɪstɪk dɪˈziːz əv ðə pæŋˈkriːəs / (BrE)
a hereditary disease in which thick mucus clogs the lungs, intestines, and pancreas, often leading to chronic respiratory and digestive problems; also known as cystic fibrosis
hereditary disease causing thick mucus in organs
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of fibrocystic disease of the pancreas was confirmed after several genetic tests.
Children with fibrocystic disease of the pancreas often require lifelong therapy to manage their symptoms.
Advancements in treatment have improved the life expectancy of people living with fibrocystic disease of the pancreas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cystic fibrosis - hereditary disease with thick mucus in organs

Etymology

From fibro- (relating to fiber), cystic (referring to cysts), and pancreas (glandular organ), reflecting the fibrous and cystic changes in the pancreas typical of the disease.
