Rayleigh disk

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪli dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈreɪli dɪsk / (BrE)
an acoustic instrument consisting of a light disk suspended on a thread, used to measure the velocity of sound waves by detecting torque caused by sound.
instrument for measuring sound wave velocity

Examples

The scientist used a Rayleigh disk to analyze the velocity profile of the acoustic field.
A Rayleigh disk demonstrates the relationship between sound wave pressure and particle velocity.
Because of its sensitivity, the Rayleigh disk became a fundamental tool in early acoustic research.

Etymology

Named after Lord Rayleigh, the British physicist (1842–1919), who developed the device.
