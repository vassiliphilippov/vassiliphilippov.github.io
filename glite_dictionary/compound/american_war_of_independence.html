American War of Independence

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən wɔɹ əv ˌɪn.dəˈpɛn.dəns / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən wɔːr əv ˌɪn.dɪˈpɛn.dəns / (BrE)
the war (1775–1783) in which the American Colonies fought for and gained independence from Great Britain
war for American independence, 1775–1783

Examples

The American War of Independence began in 1775 with the Battles of Lexington and Concord.
Many historians study the causes and outcomes of the American War of Independence.
France played a crucial role as an ally during the American War of Independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Revolution - another name for the conflict by which the American Colonies won independence
