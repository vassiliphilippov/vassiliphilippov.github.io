foreign devil

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrən ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
An offensive term used to refer to a foreigner, especially a Westerner, perceived as dangerous or deceitful; chiefly used in East Asian contexts.
offensive term for a foreigner, especially a Westerner
offensive

Examples

During his travels in 19th-century China, he was frequently called a foreign devil by the locals.
The villagers distrusted the newcomers, whispering that they might be foreign devils.
Literature from the era often portrayed Westerners as foreign devils, reflecting deep-seated suspicion.

Synset Examples

The novel depicted the protagonist as a foreign devil in a new land.
They called him a foreign devil for his strange accent.
She felt like a foreign devil in her new neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreigner - a person from another country

Etymology

Calque of Chinese 洋鬼子 (yáng guǐzi, “ocean devil/foreign devil”), historically used to describe Westerners.
