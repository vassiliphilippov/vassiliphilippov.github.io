area 17 of Brodmann

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛriə sɛˌvɛntiˈɛn əv ˈbroʊdˌmɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛəriə sɛv(ə)nˈtiːn əv ˈbrɒdˌman / (BrE)
the part of the occipital cortex in the brain that serves as the primary visual receiving area, processing input from the optic radiation
primary visual area of the occipital cortex
medical

Examples

Damage to the area 17 of Brodmann can lead to partial or complete loss of vision.
Researchers studied neuronal activity in the area 17 of Brodmann to understand visual perception.
The area 17 of Brodmann is crucial for interpreting signals received from the eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primary visual cortex - part of the cerebral cortex that processes visual information

Etymology

Named after German neurologist Korbinian Brodmann, who classified cerebral cortex regions.
