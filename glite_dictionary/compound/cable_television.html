cable television

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.bəl ˌtɛl.əˈvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl ˌtɛl.ɪˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
A system of delivering television programming to consumers via coaxial or fiber-optic cables, rather than traditional broadcast or satellite signals.
television delivered by cable

Examples

Many people prefer cable television because it offers a greater variety of channels.
The new apartment complex includes free cable television as part of the rent.
With the rise of streaming services, some viewers are cancelling their cable television subscriptions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satellite television - television transmitted by satellite directly to homes
  • Antonym: broadcast television - television transmitted via radio waves over the air

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.bəl ˌtɛl.əˈvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl ˌtɛl.ɪˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
A system of delivering television programs to viewers by transmitting signals through cables rather than by broadcast airwaves.
TV transmitted via cables, not airwaves

Examples

Many rural communities rely on cable television for access to a wider range of channels.
The popularity of cable television began to surge in the 1980s.
She subscribed to cable television to watch her favorite sports channels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broadcast television - traditional television transmitted via radio waves

Etymology

Formed from 'cable' and 'television'; first used in the early 20th century as the technology developed.
