white-rumped shrike

1. NOUN

/ ˌwaɪt ˈrʌmpt ʃraɪk / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˈrʌmpt ʃraɪk / (BrE)
a species of shrike found in western North America, noted for its grey upperparts and white underparts, including a distinctive white rump; also known as a type of butcherbird
a shrike species with a white rump

Examples

The white-rumped shrike is easy to recognize by the flash of white on its tail during flight.
A white-rumped shrike perched on the fence, scanning the field for insects.
Birdwatchers in the region often hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive white-rumped shrike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a songbird of the family Laniidae known for impaling prey
