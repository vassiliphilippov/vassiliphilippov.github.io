sadistic tendency

1. NOUN

/ səˈdɪstɪk tɛnˈdɛnsi / (AmE), / səˈdɪstɪk tɛnˈdɛnsi / (BrE)
a tendency or inclination to derive pleasure from inflicting pain or suffering on others
desire to cause others pain

Examples

The detective suspected the criminal had a sadistic tendency that drove his violent crimes.
Her novel explores the sadistic tendency present in some authoritarian figures.
Counselors discussed ways to help patients manage a sadistic tendency in therapeutic settings.

Synset Examples

The villain's sadistic tendencies made the story intense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadism - the tendency to derive pleasure from inflicting pain or humiliation on others

Etymology

From 'sadistic', derived from the name of Marquis de Sade, and 'tendency', from Latin tendentia.
