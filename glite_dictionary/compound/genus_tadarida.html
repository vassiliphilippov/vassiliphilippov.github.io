genus Tadarida

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs təˈdærɪdə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs təˈdɑːrɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of free-tailed bats found in many parts of the world, known for their long tails that extend beyond the tail membrane.
genus of free-tailed bats

Examples

The genus Tadarida includes several species of bats commonly known as free-tailed bats.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Tadarida in South America.
Members of the genus Tadarida are known for their distinctive tail structure.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Tadarida', the genus name; common in biological nomenclature.
