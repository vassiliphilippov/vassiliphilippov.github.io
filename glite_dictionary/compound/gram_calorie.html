gram calorie

1. NOUN

/ ɡræm ˈkæl.ə.ri / (AmE), / ɡræm ˈkæl.ə.ri / (BrE)
A unit of heat equal to the amount of energy needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1°C at standard atmospheric pressure.
unit of heat energy (1g water, 1°C, 1 atm)

Examples

The scientist measured the heat released as exactly one gram calorie.
Nutrition labels often refer to kilocalories rather than the smaller gram calorie.
One gram calorie is a very small amount of energy, much less than a dietary Calorie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calorie - unit of energy; also known as 'small calorie'
  • Degree Higher: kilocalorie - unit of heat energy equal to 1,000 gram calories

Etymology

From 'gram' (a metric unit of mass) and 'calorie' (from Latin calor, 'heat').
