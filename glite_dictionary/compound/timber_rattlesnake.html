timber rattlesnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ ˈrætəlˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə ˈrætəlˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a large venomous snake (Crotalus horridus) found chiefly in the eastern United States, recognized by its rattling tail and distinctive markings
a North American rattlesnake species

Examples

Hikers in the Appalachian Mountains should watch out for the timber rattlesnake.
The forested hills are home to a thriving population of timber rattlesnakes.
She studied the habitat of the timber rattlesnake for her biology project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - any venomous snake with a rattling tail, native to the Americas
