arum lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛːrəm ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈɑːrəm ˌlɪli / (BrE)
A South African plant, Zantedeschia aethiopica, widely cultivated for its large, showy white spathe and yellow spadix.
South African plant with white spathe

Examples

The elegant arum lily is a popular choice for bridal bouquets.
We planted an arum lily beside the pond, and its white bloom stands out beautifully.
Although native to South Africa, the arum lily thrives in many temperate gardens around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calla lily - an alternative common name for Zantedeschia aethiopica
