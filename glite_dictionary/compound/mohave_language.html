Mohave language

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Mohave people of the southwestern United States
the Yuman language of the Mohave people

Examples

Few people today are fluent in the Mohave language, making revitalization efforts essential.
Linguists study the Mohave language to better understand Yuman language structures.
She is proud to teach her grandchildren the basics of the Mohave language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman language - a group of languages spoken by indigenous peoples of the southwestern US and northern Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Mohave people and 'language.'
