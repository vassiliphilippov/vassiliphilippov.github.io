monophonic music

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈfɑːnɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈfɒnɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
music that consists of a single melodic line or vocal part, typically without harmonic accompaniment
music with a single melodic line

Examples

Gregorian chant is one of the best-known forms of monophonic music.
Early Western church music was primarily monophonic music before the development of harmony.
Students in music history class studied examples of monophonic music from medieval times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyphonic music - music with two or more independent melodic lines

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'phōnē' (sound, voice).
