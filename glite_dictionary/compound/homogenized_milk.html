homogenized milk

1. NOUN

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪzd mɪlk / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒɪnaɪzd mɪlk / (BrE)
milk processed so that the fat particles are broken up and distributed evenly, preventing cream from separating and rising to the top
milk with evenly distributed fat

Examples

Many people prefer homogenized milk because it has a smooth, uniform texture.
You can find both homogenized milk and non-homogenized milk at the grocery store.
The process of making homogenized milk ensures the cream does not separate and rise to the top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole milk - milk from which no fat has been removed
  • Antonym: skim milk - milk from which the cream has been removed

Etymology

From 'homogenize' (to make uniform), based on Greek 'homogenēs' (of the same kind); 'milk' from Old English 'meolc'.
