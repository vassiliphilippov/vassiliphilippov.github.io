cedar tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsidɚ tri / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə triː / (BrE)
A large evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Cedrus, native to mountainous regions and known for its fragrant, durable wood.
large evergreen tree (genus Cedrus)

Examples

The ancient cedar tree in the forest has stood for hundreds of years.
We planted a young cedar tree at the edge of the garden.
The wood from a cedar tree is highly valued for making furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - the wood or tree of genus Cedrus

Etymology

From Old French 'cedre', from Latin 'cedrus', from Greek 'kedros'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdər triː / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə ˌtriː / (BrE)
Any of various coniferous trees of the family Cupressaceae that are commonly known as cedars.
a coniferous tree resembling a cedar

Examples

The cedar tree in their backyard provided shade throughout the summer.
Cedar chests are often made from the wood of the cedar tree because of its pleasant scent.
After the storm, branches from the old cedar tree were scattered across the lawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - wood or tree of the genus Cedrus

Etymology

From Old French 'cedre', from Latin 'cedrus', from Greek 'kedros' (cedar).
