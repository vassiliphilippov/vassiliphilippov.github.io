Epstein-Barr virus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpstaɪn ˈbɑr ˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌepstaɪn ˈbɑː ˌvaɪrəs / (BrE)
a herpesvirus that causes infectious mononucleosis and is associated with certain cancers, mainly in Africa and China
herpesvirus causing mononucleosis; linked to some cancers
medical

Examples

The Epstein-Barr virus is one of the most common human viruses worldwide.
Doctors discovered that her symptoms were caused by the Epstein-Barr virus.
Research shows a link between the Epstein-Barr virus and certain types of lymphomas.

Etymology

Named after British researchers Michael Epstein and Yvonne Barr, who discovered the virus in 1964.
