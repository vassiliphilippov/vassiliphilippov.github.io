grey wolf

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ wʊlf / (AmE), / ɡreɪ wʊlf / (BrE)
A large wild canine with a brindled grey coat, native to forested northern regions of North America; also known as the timber wolf.
a large wild wolf of North America

Examples

The grey wolf is known for its intelligence and complex social structure.
Conservation efforts have helped the grey wolf population recover in some areas.
We spotted a pack of grey wolves moving through the snowy forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf - a wild carnivorous mammal of the dog family, native to Eurasia and North America
