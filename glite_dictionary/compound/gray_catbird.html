gray catbird

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈkætbɝd / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈkatbɜːd / (BrE)
A North American songbird (Dumetella carolinensis) known for its slate-gray color and a call that resembles a cat's mewing.
North American songbird with catlike call

Examples

We spotted a gray catbird perched on the fence in the backyard.
The gray catbird is easily recognized by its slate-gray plumage and catlike cry.
Each spring, the melodious song of the gray catbird fills the garden.
