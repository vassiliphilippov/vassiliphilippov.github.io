record album

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˌælbəm / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌælbəm / (BrE)
a book or case designed to hold and protect phonograph records
book for storing phonograph records

Examples

My grandfather still keeps his old jazz records in a record album on the shelf.
She found a vintage record album at the flea market, filled with rare vinyls.
The record album had spaces for her to carefully store each disc and its sleeve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photo album - a book whose pages contain photographs for storage or display

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˈæl.bəm / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.əd ˈæl.bəm / (BrE)
a collection of audio recordings issued together, especially as a 12-inch vinyl record, cassette, or compact disc, typically with a distinct cover
collection of audio recordings issued together

Examples

He spent the afternoon listening to his favorite record album from the 1970s.
The Beatles' first record album broke sales records around the world.
She inherited a large collection of vintage record albums from her uncle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: album - a collection of audio recordings issued as a single item

Etymology

From 'record' (audio recording) and 'album' (book for keeping collections); usage became standard with the introduction of vinyl LPs in the mid-20th century.
