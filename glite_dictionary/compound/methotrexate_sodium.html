methotrexate sodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθ.oʊˈtrɛk.seɪt ˈsoʊ.di.əm / (AmE), / ˌmɛθ.əʊˈtrɛk.seɪt ˈsəʊ.di.əm / (BrE)
a toxic drug used as a chemotherapy agent and to treat autoimmune diseases, by inhibiting cellular reproduction as an antagonist to folic acid
drug used for cancer and autoimmune diseases
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed methotrexate sodium to help manage her rheumatoid arthritis symptoms.
Low doses of methotrexate sodium can be effective in controlling severe psoriasis.
Patients undergoing chemotherapy may receive methotrexate sodium as part of their treatment regimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methotrexate - drug used as an antimetabolite and antifolate in medicine

Etymology

From 'methotrexate', derived from chemical roots (methyl + folic acid derivative), and 'sodium', from Latin 'sodanum'.
