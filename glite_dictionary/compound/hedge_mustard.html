hedge mustard

1. NOUN

/ hɛdʒ ˈmʌstɚd / (AmE), / hɛdʒ ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
A stiffly branching annual plant with pale yellow flowers, originally from the Old World, now common in North America; formerly used in traditional medicine.
a yellow-flowered annual plant, formerly medicinal

Examples

The hedge mustard grew wild along the roadside, its yellow blooms standing out against the grass.
Some herbal remedies in the past used hedge mustard to treat throat ailments.
Gardeners sometimes remove hedge mustard because it can spread quickly and compete with native plants.
