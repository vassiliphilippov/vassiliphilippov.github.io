outer ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt̬ɚ ɪr / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə ɪə / (BrE)
the external part of the ear, consisting of the auricle (pinna) and the ear canal, which collects and directs sound waves toward the eardrum
external part of the ear

Examples

He accidentally pierced the cartilage of his outer ear.
The doctor explained that an infection in the outer ear is called swimmer's ear.
Sunscreen should be applied to the outer ear to avoid sunburn.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inner ear - the innermost part of the ear, involved in hearing and balance
  • Antonym: middle ear - the part of the ear between the eardrum and the inner ear
