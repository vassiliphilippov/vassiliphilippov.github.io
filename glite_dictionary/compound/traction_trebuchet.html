traction trebuchet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrækʃən trɛbjuˌʃɛt / (AmE), / ˈtrækʃən ˌtrɛbjʊˈʃɛt / (BrE)
a type of catapult powered by people pulling cords attached to a lever and sling, used to launch projectiles
manpowered catapult with a lever and sling

Examples

During the siege, the army used a traction trebuchet to breach the city walls.
The museum's display includes a replica of a medieval traction trebuchet.
By coordinating their efforts, dozens of soldiers could operate the traction trebuchet efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trebuchet - a large medieval siege weapon for hurling heavy stones
  • Synonym: catapult - a general device for hurling objects

Etymology

From 'traction' (Latin tractio, act of drawing) and 'trebuchet' (Old French trebuchet, war engine).
