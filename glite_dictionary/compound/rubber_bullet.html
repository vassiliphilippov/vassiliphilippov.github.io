rubber bullet

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbər ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
a bullet made of hard rubber, designed to be non-lethal and used in crowd control situations
non-lethal crowd control bullet

Examples

The police fired rubber bullets to disperse the protesting crowd.
Several people were injured when rubber bullets were used during the demonstration.
He was hit in the leg by a rubber bullet but was not seriously hurt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live round - an actual bullet fired from a gun, as opposed to non-lethal ammunition
  • Synonym: tear gas - a chemical weapon used for crowd control
