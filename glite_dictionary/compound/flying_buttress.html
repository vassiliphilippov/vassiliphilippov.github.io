flying buttress

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈbʌtrɪs / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈbʌtrɪs / (BrE)
An external support consisting of a buttress standing apart from a wall and connected to it by an arch, often found in Gothic architecture.
arched external support (architecture)

Examples

Many Gothic cathedrals are famous for their flying buttresses that provide structural support.
The engineer studied the old church's flying buttress to understand its stability.
Tourists admired the elegant flying buttress arching gracefully from the nave to the outer wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttress - a projecting support built against a wall

Etymology

From Middle French 'bouteresse', related to 'buter' (to strike or support).
