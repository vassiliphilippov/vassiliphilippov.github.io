giant hives

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt haɪvz / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt hʌɪvz / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by large, swollen areas of the skin (wheals) that appear suddenly and usually subside within 24 hours, often as an allergic reaction.
large swollen skin welts; sudden onset; allergy-related
medical

Examples

The patient developed giant hives after taking penicillin for her infection.
Stress or certain foods can sometimes trigger episodes of giant hives.
Her doctor diagnosed the sudden swelling as giant hives and prescribed antihistamines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urticaria - medical term for hives; itchy, raised skin rash
