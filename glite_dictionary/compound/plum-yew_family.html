plum-yew family

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmˌju ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈplʌmˌjuː ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of evergreen shrubs and small trees, Cephalotaxaceae, native to East Asia and known for their plum-like seeds and yew-like foliage.
family of evergreen shrubs and trees (Cephalotaxaceae)

Examples

The plum-yew family is distinguished by its unique seed structure and evergreen leaves.
Many members of the plum-yew family are cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
Botanists recently discovered a new species belonging to the plum-yew family in China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cephalotaxaceae - the botanical family known as the plum-yew family
