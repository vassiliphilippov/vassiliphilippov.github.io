English lavender

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋglɪʃ ˈlævəndər / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈlavəndə / (BrE)
an aromatic Mediterranean shrub (Lavandula angustifolia) commonly cultivated for its fragrant lilac flowers, which are often dried and used in sachets and perfumes
aromatic shrub with fragrant lilac flowers

Examples

She grows English lavender in her garden for its beautiful fragrance and lovely blooms.
Many people place dried English lavender in closets to keep their clothes smelling fresh.
The essential oil from English lavender is popular in aromatherapy for its calming scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - a type of aromatic plant with purple flowers
