Honduras mahogany

1. NOUN

/ hɒnˈdʊrəs məˈhɑɡəni / (AmE), / hɒnˈdjʊərəs məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
a species of mahogany tree native to Central America, valued for its high-quality reddish-brown timber
Central American mahogany tree species

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Honduras mahogany, giving it a rich, deep color.
Many historic ships were built using durable Honduras mahogany for their decks.
Deforestation has reduced the number of wild Honduras mahogany trees in Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - any of several tropical hardwood trees or their wood

Etymology

Named after Honduras, the country, and 'mahogany' from Spanish 'mahogani', referring to this type of wood.
