grid metal

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪd ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ɡrɪd ˈmetl̩ / (BrE)
a hard alloy of lead, typically containing small amounts of antimony, used for making the grids in storage batteries
lead alloy for battery grids

Examples

Lead-antimony grid metal increases the lifespan of lead-acid batteries.
The performance of the battery depends partly on the quality of the grid metal used.
Engineers are researching alternatives to traditional grid metal to improve battery safety.
