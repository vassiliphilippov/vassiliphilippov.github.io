lily of the Nile

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪli əv ðə naɪl / (AmE), / ˈlɪli əv ðə naɪl / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Agapanthus, known for its umbels of showy blue or purple flowers, often grown as an ornamental
ornamental plant with blue or purple flowers

Examples

The lily of the Nile produces striking blue blossoms in midsummer.
She planted several lilies of the Nile along the garden path for a touch of color.
Gardeners often choose lily of the Nile for borders due to its attractive flowers and foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agapanthus - plant genus featuring clusters of blue or purple flowers
