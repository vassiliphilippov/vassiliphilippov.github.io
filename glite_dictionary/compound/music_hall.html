music hall

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuː.zɪk hɔl / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.zɪk hɔːl / (BrE)
a type of entertainment popular in Britain in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, featuring a variety show with songs, comic acts, and other performances
variety show with songs and comedy (British, historical)
british

Examples

Many famous comedians got their start performing in music hall shows across London.
The old theater was once a bustling music hall filled with laughter and song.
She collected posters from the heyday of British music hall entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaudeville - a similar variety show popular in the United States and Canada

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪk hɔl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪk hɔːl / (BrE)
a theater or venue where popular entertainment, including comedy, song, and variety acts, is performed, especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
theater for variety entertainment

Examples

The city is famous for its historic music hall where many great performers got their start.
We watched a vaudeville show at the old music hall last weekend.
Many music halls were converted into cinemas during the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaudeville - a type of variety entertainment popular in the early 20th century

Etymology

From 'music' + 'hall'; refers to public halls used for musical entertainment in the 19th century.
