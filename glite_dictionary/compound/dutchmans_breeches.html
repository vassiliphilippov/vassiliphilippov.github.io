Dutchman's breeches

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌtʃmənz ˈbriːtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʌtʃmənz ˈbriːtʃɪz / (BrE)
A delicate spring-flowering plant of the eastern United States with white flowers bearing double spurs, scientific name Dicentra cucullaria.
spring-flowering plant with white double-spurred flowers

Examples

In early April, the forest was dotted with blooming Dutchman's breeches among the fallen leaves.
She photographed a patch of Dutchman's breeches during her hike in the Appalachian Mountains.
Gardeners prize Dutchman's breeches for their unique, pantaloon-shaped white flowers.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the flowers to traditional Dutch trousers ('breeches').
