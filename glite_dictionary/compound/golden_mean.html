golden mean

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈmin / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈmiːn / (BrE)
a desirable moderate position between two extremes, especially in conduct, philosophy, or decision-making
the moderate course between extremes

Examples

The philosopher believed that true happiness comes from living according to the golden mean.
She always tries to find the golden mean between work and leisure.
In art, the golden mean represents balance and harmony rather than excess or deficiency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extreme - a point farthest from the center or most severe position
  • Synonym: moderation - the quality of being moderate and avoiding excess

Etymology

From the Latin 'aurea mediocritas' (golden moderation), used by Aristotle and later philosophers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈmin / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈmiːn / (BrE)
the proportional relationship in which the ratio of the smaller part to the larger is the same as the ratio of the larger part to the whole, often considered aesthetically pleasing and expressed mathematically as the golden ratio
the golden ratio; harmonious proportion

Examples

The Parthenon's façade is said to be designed according to the golden mean for visual harmony.
Many artists believe that using the golden mean in their work results in more pleasing compositions.
He explained the mathematics behind the golden mean to illustrate its importance in nature and design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden ratio - the mathematical ratio approximately equal to 1.618:1, considered especially pleasing to the eye

Etymology

From the mathematical and philosophical term 'mean,' referring to a middle point or proportion; 'golden' denotes its esteemed status.
