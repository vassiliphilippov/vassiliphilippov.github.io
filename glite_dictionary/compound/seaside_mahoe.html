seaside mahoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌsaɪd məˈhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsiːsaɪd məˈhəʊ / (BrE)
A tropical tree found near seacoasts, noted for its rounded heart-shaped leaves, showy yellow and purple flowers, and valuable reddish wood.
pantropical coastal tree with showy flowers and valuable wood

Examples

The seaside mahoe thrives along tropical beaches where it protects the shoreline from erosion.
Carpenters prize the wood of the seaside mahoe for its strength and reddish color.
Gardeners often plant seaside mahoe as an ornamental because of its attractive flowers and foliage.

Etymology

From 'seaside', referring to its typical coastal habitat, and 'mahoe', from Arawak (Caribbean indigenous language).
