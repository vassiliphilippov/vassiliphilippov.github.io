feeling of movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈfilɪŋ əv ˈmuvmənt / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪŋ əv ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
the perception of the position and movement of one's body and muscular tensions.
perception of body movement and position

Examples

A dancer develops a strong feeling of movement to perform graceful routines with precision.
After the surgery, he lost his feeling of movement in his right leg, making walking difficult.
Proprioception provides us with a feeling of movement even when our eyes are closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinesthesia - the sense that detects bodily position, weight, or movement of the muscles, tendons, and joints
