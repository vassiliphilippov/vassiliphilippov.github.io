parquet circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.keɪt ˈsɝː.kəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.keɪ ˈsɜː.kəl / (BrE)
the section of seating at the rear of the main floor in a theater, located beneath the balconies
theater seating beneath the balconies

Examples

We found seats in the parquet circle for the evening performance.
Tickets for the parquet circle were nearly sold out by noon.
The view from the parquet circle was ideal for watching the entire stage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: balcony - seating area located above the main floor in a theater
  • Degree Lower: orchestra - seating section nearest the stage in a theater

Etymology

From French parquet (wooden floor) + English circle, referring to the arrangement and location of the seats.
