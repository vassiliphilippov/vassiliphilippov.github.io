Assyrian language

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language once spoken by the Assyrians in ancient Mesopotamia
extinct ancient language of Assyria

Examples

The Assyrian language was once widely spoken in the ancient cities of Mesopotamia.
Scholars have studied clay tablets written in the Assyrian language to understand ancient cultures.
As a branch of the Akkadian dialects, the Assyrian language played a vital role in historical records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Akkadian - an extinct Semitic language of Mesopotamia, to which Assyrian belonged

Etymology

From Assyria, the ancient Mesopotamian kingdom, and language, from Latin 'lingua'.
