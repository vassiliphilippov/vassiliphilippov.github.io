great Solomon's-seal

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈsɒləmənz siːl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈsɒləmənz siːl / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with smooth leaves and drooping, tubular greenish flowers, commonly found in woodlands.
North American perennial herb with drooping greenish flowers

Examples

In the shade of the forest, great Solomon's-seal blooms with its distinctive drooping greenish flowers.
Gardeners appreciate great Solomon's-seal for its elegant arching stems and smooth foliage.
The herbalist identified the plant as great Solomon's-seal growing beside the woodland trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solomon's-seal - A group of plants in the genus Polygonatum with arching stems and hanging flowers

Etymology

Named after King Solomon, referring to a scar on the root resembling a seal; 'great' distinguishes it from similar species.
