Guadalcanal Campaign

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡwɑdəlkəˈnæl kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡwæd(ə)lkəˈnæl kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
a significant military campaign during World War II (1942–1943) in which Allied forces fought to recapture Guadalcanal Island from Japanese occupation in the Pacific theater.
World War II battle in the Pacific (1942–1943)

Examples

The Guadalcanal Campaign marked a turning point in the Pacific War during World War II.
Many Allied soldiers lost their lives during the fierce battles of the Guadalcanal Campaign.
The victory in the Guadalcanal Campaign was crucial for gaining control of strategic islands in the Pacific.
