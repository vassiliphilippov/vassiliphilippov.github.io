Bermuda rig

1. NOUN

/ bərˈmjuːdə rɪg / (AmE), / bəˈmjuːdə rɪg / (BrE)
A sailing rig using triangular sails set fore and aft, typically used on yachts.
type of triangular sail rig for yachts

Examples

The sleek sloop featured a modern Bermuda rig for optimal speed and maneuverability.
Most competitive yachts today use the Bermuda rig, which is known for its efficiency.
Sailors admired the simplicity of the Bermuda rig compared to older sail plans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gaff rig - A sailing rig with a four-sided sail, as opposed to the Bermuda rig's triangular sail.

Etymology

Named after Bermuda, where the rig was developed and popularized in the 17th century.
