lambda hyperon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.də ˈhaɪ.pɚˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈlæm.də ˈhaɪ.pərˌɒn / (BrE)
an electrically neutral subatomic particle (baryon) with isotopic spin 1, found in high-energy physics and containing a strange quark.
subatomic baryon particle with isotopic spin 1

Examples

Scientists detected a lambda hyperon in the particle collision experiment.
The lambda hyperon decays quickly into a proton and a pion.
Research on the lambda hyperon provides insight into the behavior of strange quarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baryon - a class of subatomic particles including protons, neutrons, and hyperons
  • Degree Lower: proton - a positively charged baryon
  • Degree Higher: sigma hyperon - a related baryon with different quark content

Etymology

From Greek 'lambda', the 11th letter of the Greek alphabet, and 'hyperon', from Greek 'hyper' (above) + '-on' (particle).
