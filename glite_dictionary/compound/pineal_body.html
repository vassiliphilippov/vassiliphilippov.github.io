pineal body

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪniəl ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈpɪniəl ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
A small endocrine gland in the brain, located beneath the posterior part of the corpus callosum, that produces melatonin and regulates various biological rhythms.
small endocrine gland in the brain
medical

Examples

The pineal body plays a crucial role in regulating the sleep-wake cycle by secreting melatonin.
Doctors are studying how the pineal body responds to changes in light exposure.
In some animals, the pineal body is involved in seasonal breeding behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineal gland - another term for pineal body
  • Synonym: pituitary gland - another important endocrine gland in the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'pinealis', meaning 'like a pine cone', referring to its shape.
