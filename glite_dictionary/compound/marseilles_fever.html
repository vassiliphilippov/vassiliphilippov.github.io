Marseilles fever

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈseɪlz ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / mɑːˈseɪlz ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by a rickettsia, transmitted to humans by a reddish-brown tick living on dogs and other mammals, common in India and around the Mediterranean area
infectious disease transmitted by ticks
medical

Examples

Marseilles fever is prevalent in some areas near the Mediterranean Sea.
The doctor explained that the patient’s symptoms matched those of Marseilles fever.
Ticks that transmit Marseilles fever often live on domestic dogs in affected regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mediterranean spotted fever - another name for Marseilles fever; a similar tick-borne rickettsial disease

Etymology

Named after the city of Marseilles, France, where the disease was first described.
