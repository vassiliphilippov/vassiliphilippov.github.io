Muslim calendar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊz.lɪm ˈkæl.ən.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʊz.lɪm ˈkæl.ən.də / (BrE)
a lunar calendar used by Muslims, beginning from 622 AD (the year of the Hegira), with its New Year retrogressing through the solar year every 32 years
lunar calendar used in Islam

Examples

Religious festivals like Ramadan are observed according to the Muslim calendar.
The Muslim calendar is based on the lunar cycle, making its months shorter than those in the Gregorian calendar.
He learned that the year 2023 corresponds to 1444–1445 in the Muslim calendar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islamic calendar - the same calendar system used in Islamic tradition
