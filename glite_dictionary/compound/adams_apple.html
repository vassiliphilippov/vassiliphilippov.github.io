Adam's apple

1. NOUN

/ ˈædəmz ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈædəmz ˌæp(ə)l / (BrE)
the prominent projection of the thyroid cartilage of the larynx, typically more visible in males, in the front of the neck
prominent laryngeal cartilage in the neck

Examples

He nervously swallowed, and his Adam's apple moved up and down.
The doctor examined his throat and pointed out the prominent Adam's apple.
Some people are self-conscious about having a large Adam's apple.

Etymology

From the biblical Adam, with reference to the forbidden fruit thought to have got stuck in his throat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈædəmz ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈædəmz ˌapəl / (BrE)
A tropical shrub with glossy leaves and fragrant night-blooming flowers with crimped or wavy corollas, native to northern India to Thailand.
tropical shrub with fragrant, crimped flowers

Examples

The garden featured a rare Adam's apple among the other tropical plants.
Botanists often study the fragrant blooms of the Adam's apple for their unique structure.
She planted an Adam's apple shrub to add an exotic touch to her backyard.
