tepary bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛpəri ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈtɛpəri ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its small, roundish beans that vary in color from white to yellow, brown, or black.
climbing bean plant of SW US and Mexico

Examples

The drought-resistant tepary bean is a traditional crop among Native American communities.
Farmers in arid regions are beginning to grow tepary beans for their resilience and nutritional value.
A southwestern stew might include tepary bean as its main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - an edible seed, typically kidney-shaped, of various plants in the legume family

Etymology

From Tohono O'odham (a Native American language) 'típari,' referring to the plant.
