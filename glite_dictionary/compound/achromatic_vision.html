achromatic vision

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈmætɪk vɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈmatɪk ˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
vision in which only shades of gray are perceived, without any colour; typically associated with the use of rod cells in the eye.
vision without colour, only shades of gray

Examples

Some animals rely mostly on achromatic vision for navigating in low-light conditions.
People with certain visual disorders may experience the world through achromatic vision rather than seeing colors.
The test showed that his achromatic vision was functioning normally, but he had difficulty distinguishing colors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: color vision - the ability to perceive differences between light composed of different wavelengths (i.e., color)

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'khrōma' (color) + '-ic', meaning without color.
