nodding thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑdɪŋ ˌθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒdɪŋ ˌθɪsəl / (BrE)
a thistle with spiny, cottony leaves and drooping crimson flower heads, native to Eurasia and naturalized in North America
a thistle with drooping crimson flowers

Examples

Bees are often attracted to the nodding thistle for its rich nectar supply.
The fields near the old farmhouse were filled with blooming nodding thistle in late summer.
Some gardeners remove nodding thistle to prevent its rapid spread, despite its attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a plant with prickly leaves and flowers, typical of the family Asteraceae
