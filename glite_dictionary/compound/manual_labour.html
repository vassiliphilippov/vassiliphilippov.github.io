manual labour

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænjuəl ˈleɪbər / (AmE), / ˈmænjuəl ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
work that involves using your hands or physical effort, rather than machines or intellectual skill
work done by hand, physical work

Examples

Construction often requires a lot of manual labour to complete projects.
The company reduced costs by replacing manual labour with automated machines.
Farmers still rely heavily on manual labour during the harvest season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical labor - work involving bodily effort; similar to manual labour

Etymology

From Latin 'manualis' (of the hand) and Middle English 'labour' (work).
