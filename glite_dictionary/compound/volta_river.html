Volta River

1. PROPN

/ ˈvoʊltə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈvɒltə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in Ghana that flows south into the Gulf of Guinea, forming part of Lake Volta
major river in Ghana

Examples

The Volta River plays a crucial role in Ghana's hydroelectric power generation.
Fishermen rely on the Volta River for their daily catch.
Lake Volta, formed by the damming of the Volta River, is one of the largest artificial lakes in the world.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'volta' (turn), referring to the river's bends; named by Portuguese explorers.
