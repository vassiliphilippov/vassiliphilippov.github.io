Pawnee language

1. NOUN

/ pɔːˈni ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / pɔːˈniː ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the native American language of the Pawnee people, belonging to the Caddoan language family
native American language of the Pawnee people

Examples

Few people today speak the Pawnee language fluently, as the number of native speakers has declined.
Linguists are working on resources to help preserve the Pawnee language for future generations.
She learned common greetings and phrases in the Pawnee language during her research.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Caddoan - a family of Native American languages including Pawnee
  • Synonym: Pawnee - a member of a Native American people originally from Nebraska and Kansas

Etymology

Pawnee, the name of the people, from their own autonym; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
