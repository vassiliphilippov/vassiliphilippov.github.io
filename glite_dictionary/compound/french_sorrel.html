French sorrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛntʃ ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɛntʃ ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
A perennial herb with small, tart, oval to pointed leaves, used especially in salads and preferred to common sorrel.
herb with tart edible leaves

Examples

She added fresh French sorrel to the salad for a sharp, lemony flavor.
The chef garnished the dish with finely chopped French sorrel leaves.
Gardeners value French sorrel for its vibrant green leaves and tangy taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - a leafy herb with a tart flavor

2. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a perennial herb with small silvery-green, ovate or arrow-shaped leaves, used in salads and cooking for its tart flavor
herb with tart, silvery-green leaves

Examples

Fresh salads often include French sorrel for its lemony tang.
She grows French sorrel in her herb garden to use in soups and sauces.
The silvery-green leaves of French sorrel add both flavor and decoration to dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - a plant with acidic-tasting leaves, used as a salad herb or vegetable
