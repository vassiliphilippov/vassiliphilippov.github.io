casting couch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstɪŋ kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstɪŋ kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
the practice, especially in the entertainment industry, where sexual favors are solicited or expected from job seekers in exchange for employment or roles
soliciting sexual favors for job opportunities

Examples

Rumors of a casting couch culture have surrounded several prominent production companies.
She was determined to succeed on her own talent, avoiding the casting couch at all costs.
His exposé shed light on the prevalence of the casting couch in Hollywood.

Synset Examples

The film industry has had a long-standing issue with the casting couch.
He warned her about the dangers of the casting couch.

Etymology

From 'casting' (choosing actors for roles) + 'couch' (suggesting a location for the behavior); in use since the early to mid-20th century.
