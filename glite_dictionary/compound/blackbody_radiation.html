blackbody radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɑdi ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɒdi ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation emitted by an ideal black body, with a spectrum dependent only on its temperature
radiation emitted by an ideal black body

Examples

The spectrum of blackbody radiation shifts to higher frequencies as temperature increases.
Physicists use the concept of blackbody radiation to understand how stars emit energy.
Planck's law accurately describes blackbody radiation across different wavelengths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermal radiation - electromagnetic radiation generated by the thermal motion of particles
