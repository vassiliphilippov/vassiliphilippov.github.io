sea snot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsi ˌsnɑt / (AmE), / ˈsi ˌsnɒt / (BrE)
A thick, sticky, gelatinous substance formed by the overgrowth of certain algae or marine microorganisms, often appearing as floating masses in the sea.
gelatinous marine substance from algae

Examples

After weeks of hot weather, large patches of sea snot accumulated along the coastline.
Fishermen complained that sea snot clogged their nets and made fishing difficult.
Researchers believe that pollution has contributed to the recent outbreaks of sea snot.

Synset Examples

The beach was covered in sea snot after the storm.
