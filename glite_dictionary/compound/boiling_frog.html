boiling frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlɪŋ frɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ frɒɡ / (BrE)
a person or group that fails to react to subtle, harmful changes until it is too late to prevent damage
unnoticed gradual danger scenario

Examples

The climate crisis is often compared to a boiling frog, as changes occur too slowly for people to notice their severity.
The company's steady decline was a classic boiling frog situation that went unaddressed until it was too late.
Many employees acted like a boiling frog, adapting to increasing workloads without realizing the impact on their well-being.

Synset Examples

He was like a boiling frog, unaware of the danger.
The company faced a boiling frog scenario with gradual budget cuts.
Ignoring small problems can lead to a boiling frog situation.

Etymology

From the anecdote that a frog placed in slowly heated water will not perceive danger and will be cooked alive.
