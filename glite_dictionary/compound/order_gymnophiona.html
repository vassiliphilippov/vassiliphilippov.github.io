order Gymnophiona

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌdʒɪmnəˈfaɪənə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌdʒɪmnəˈfʌɪənə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of limbless, burrowing amphibians known as caecilians
order of limbless, burrowing amphibians

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the order Gymnophiona in the rainforest.
The order Gymnophiona is distinguished by its limbless, elongated bodies.
Few people are familiar with the unique adaptations of the order Gymnophiona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caecilian - limbless, burrowing amphibian of the order Gymnophiona

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' meaning 'naked' and 'ophis' meaning 'snake', referencing the appearance of caecilians.
