folk poet

1. NOUN

/ foʊk ˈpoʊɪt / (AmE), / fəʊk ˈpəʊɪt / (BrE)
A poet who composes verse in the tradition of folk literature, often reflecting the customs, stories, and language of ordinary people.
poet of folk tradition

Examples

The stories of the countryside were preserved by the folk poet in his vivid verses.
As a celebrated folk poet, she turned everyday life into memorable songs.
Many regions honor at least one folk poet for keeping their oral traditions alive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folksinger - a person who sings folk songs
  • Synonym: bard - a poet, traditionally one reciting epics and associated with a particular oral tradition
