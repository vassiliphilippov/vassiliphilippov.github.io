agada tantra

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑːdə ˈtʌntrə / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːdə ˈtʌntrə / (BrE)
A traditional branch of Ayurveda concerned with toxicology, including the treatment of poisoning from food, snakebites, animal bites, and insect stings.
Ayurvedic science of toxicology
medical

Examples

He specialized in agada tantra, treating cases of poisoning and animal bites.
The ancient texts of agada tantra describe remedies for snake and insect poisons.
Courses on agada tantra are offered in many traditional Ayurvedic colleges.

Synset Examples

Agada Tantra (Toxicology) was so highly developed during the early ages that it was given prime status, as one among the eight branches of Classical Ayurveda.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'agada' (antidote) and 'tantra' (treatise, system).
