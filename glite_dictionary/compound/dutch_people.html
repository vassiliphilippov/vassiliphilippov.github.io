Dutch people

1. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ ˈpipəl / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈpiːpl / (BrE)
the people native to or citizens of the Netherlands, especially those sharing the Dutch language and culture
people of the Netherlands

Examples

The Dutch people have a long tradition of building windmills to manage water levels.
Many Dutch people speak English fluently in addition to their native language.
The art and architecture of the Dutch people are admired around the world.

Synset Examples

the Dutch are famous for their tulips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Netherlands - a country in western Europe
  • Synonym: Dutch - the language and culture of the Netherlands

Etymology

Dutch from Middle Dutch 'duutsch' (of the people), ultimately from Proto-Germanic. 'People' from Latin 'populus'.
