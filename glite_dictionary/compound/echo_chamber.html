echo chamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkoʊ ˌtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ ˌtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a room or enclosure designed to create reverberation of sound, often used in acoustic testing or audio recording to enhance sound effects.
room for sound reverberation

Examples

The musicians created unusual effects by playing their instruments inside an echo chamber.
Engineers use an echo chamber to study how sound waves behave in different environments.
The studio was equipped with a large echo chamber to add natural reverberation to recordings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverberation room - a room specially constructed to create a diffuse sound field for acoustic measurements

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛkoʊ ˌtʃeɪmbər / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ ˌtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
an environment in which people encounter only opinions or information that reflect and reinforce their own beliefs
environment reinforcing one's own beliefs

Examples

Social media can easily become an echo chamber where diverse perspectives are rarely heard.
The members of the group lived in an echo chamber, repeating the same ideas to one another.
He left the online forum because it felt like nothing but an echo chamber.

Synset Examples

He realized his beliefs were shaped by an echo chamber.
The debate turned into an echo chamber with no new ideas.
Social media often becomes an echo chamber for opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - a situation where one is isolated from opposing views
  • Antonym: debate - discussion involving opposing viewpoints

Etymology

Originally referred to a room designed to produce echoes; figurative use dates from the late 20th century.
