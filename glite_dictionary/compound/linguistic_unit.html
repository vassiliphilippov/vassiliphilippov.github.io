linguistic unit

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a segment of language, such as a sound, word, or phrase, regarded as a discrete entity for analysis or study
a segment of language used in analysis

Examples

A phoneme is a linguistic unit that distinguishes one word from another in a particular language.
The sentence can be broken down into smaller linguistic units for grammatical analysis.
Every linguistic unit carries meaning or function within a language system.

Etymology

From 'linguistic', relating to language, and 'unit', something regarded as a single entity.
