scientific agriculture

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈəntɪfɪk ˌæɡrɪˈkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / saɪˈɒntɪfɪk ˌæɡrɪˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the application of scientific principles, especially soil and plant sciences, to the management of land and the production of crops
science-based approach to farming

Examples

The adoption of scientific agriculture transformed farming practices in the 20th century.
He studied scientific agriculture at university to learn more about crop improvement techniques.
Investment in scientific agriculture has led to increased food production worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agriculture - the practice or science of farming
