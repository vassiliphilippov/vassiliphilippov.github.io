Pinot blanc

1. NOUN

/ piːnoʊ ˈblɑːŋk / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəʊ ˈblɒ̃ / (BrE)
a variety of white wine grape, especially grown in California and France, used to produce dry white wines
variety of white wine grape

Examples

The winery specializes in Pinot blanc and other crisp white wines.
They paired the seafood platter with a glass of chilled Pinot blanc.
Many California vineyards produce excellent Pinot blanc wines.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pinot noir - black wine grape variety used for red wines

Etymology

From French, literally 'white Pinot', where 'Pinot' is a type of grape and 'blanc' means 'white'.

2. NOUN

/ piːˌnoʊ ˈblɑːŋk / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəʊ ˌblɒ̃ / (BrE)
A dry white wine made from a variety of white Pinot grapes, or the grape variety itself, often associated with regions such as California and Alsace.
a dry white wine or white grape variety

Examples

We ordered a bottle of Pinot blanc to start our meal.
The Pinot blanc from Alsace has a crisp, refreshing flavor.
She prefers Pinot blanc over Chardonnay for its lighter body and subtle fruit notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinot gris - another white grape variety and wine, related to Pinot blanc
  • Degree Higher: Chardonnay - a common white wine grape variety
  • Antonym: Pinot noir - a red grape variety closely related to Pinot blanc

Etymology

French, literally 'white Pinot', from 'Pinot' (a grape variety) and 'blanc' (white).
