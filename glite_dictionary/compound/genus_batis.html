genus Batis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbeɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbaː.tɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of low, straggling, dioecious shrubs, constituting the family Batidaceae.
genus of low straggling shrubs (Batidaceae)

Examples

The genus Batis includes salt-tolerant shrubs found in coastal marshes.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Batis.
Plants in the genus Batis are characterized by their small, dioecious flowers and spreading growth.

Etymology

From Latin 'Batis', the name of the type genus.
