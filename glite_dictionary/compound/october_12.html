October 12

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtoʊbɚ twɛlfθ / (AmE), / ɒkˈtəʊbə twɛlfθ / (BrE)
A date observed in some countries as a holiday to commemorate the discovery of America by Christopher Columbus in 1492.
holiday marking Columbus's discovery of America

Examples

Many countries celebrate October 12 as Columbus Day, honoring the explorer's arrival in the Americas.
Parades and official ceremonies are held on October 12 in parts of Latin America and Spain.
Some schools remain closed on October 12 due to its status as a public holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Columbus Day - A holiday commemorating Christopher Columbus's landing in the Americas
