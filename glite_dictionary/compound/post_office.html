post office

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊst ˌɑfɪs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊst ˌɒfɪs / (BrE)
a public building or office where mail is received, sorted, and sent out, and where postal services such as selling stamps and handling packages are provided.
building or office for mail and postal services

Examples

I went to the post office to send a package overseas.
She collects stamps from every post office she visits during her travels.
The local post office closes at 5 p.m. every weekday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mailbox - a box into which mail is delivered or dropped for collection

Etymology

From 'post', meaning mail, and 'office', meaning a place of business; first used in English in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊst ˌɑːfɪs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊst ˌɒfɪs / (BrE)
A children's game in which participants pretend to send and deliver letters, exchanging kisses for the letters.
children's game involving exchanging kisses

Examples

At the party, the children played post office and giggled every time someone had to deliver a letter.
She remembered her first kiss during a game of post office at summer camp.
The old-fashioned game called post office was once a popular pastime among schoolchildren.
