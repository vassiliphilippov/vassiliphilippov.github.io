Post Office

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊst ˌɑːfɪs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊst ˌɒfɪs / (BrE)
the public department or agency responsible for mail collection and delivery, especially in the United States
U.S. mail delivery agency
american

Examples

I went to the Post Office to mail a package this morning.
The nearest Post Office is closed on Sundays and federal holidays.
Many people rely on the Post Office for important government documents and parcels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: USPS - United States Postal Service; current official name for U.S. mail agency
  • Synonym: Royal Mail - the UK national mail delivery service

Etymology

From post (from Latin 'positus', placed) + office; originally referring to the place where mail was sorted and dispatched.
