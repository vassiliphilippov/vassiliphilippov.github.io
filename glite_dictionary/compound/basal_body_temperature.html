basal body temperature

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsəl ˈbɑdi ˌtɛmpɚˈtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪs(ə)l ˈbɒdi ˌtɛmp(ə)ˈtʃə / (BrE)
the body temperature measured immediately after waking and before any physical activity, such as rising, moving, or eating
body temperature upon waking
medical

Examples

Tracking your basal body temperature can help determine your ovulation cycle.
Doctors often advise patients to record their basal body temperature every morning for a month.
A woman's basal body temperature may slightly increase after ovulation.

Etymology

From 'basal' (relating to a base or foundation) and 'body temperature'.
