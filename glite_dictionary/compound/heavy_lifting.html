heavy lifting

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈlɪftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈlɪftɪŋ / (BrE)
hard or challenging work that requires significant effort or responsibility, often used figuratively.
difficult or laborious work

Examples

She does most of the heavy lifting when it comes to managing the team's workload.
In this project, the software will do the heavy lifting for you.
He wasn't afraid to take on the heavy lifting while others preferred the easier tasks.

Synset Examples

the boss hoped the plan would succeed but he wasn't willing to do the heavy lifting

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛvi ˈlɪftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌhɛvi ˈlɪftɪŋ / (BrE)
physically demanding work or effort, often requiring strength or significant exertion; sometimes used figuratively for hard or difficult tasks
physically or mentally demanding work

Examples

After hours of heavy lifting, the workers finally completed moving all the boxes.
The team appreciated anyone who volunteered to do the heavy lifting on the project.
Thanks to the new equipment, much of the heavy lifting no longer had to be done by hand.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easy task - something not difficult or strenuous to do
