polysyllabic word

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːli.sɪˈlæbɪk wɜrd / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪ.sɪˈlæbɪk wɜːd / (BrE)
A word that has more than three syllables.
word with more than three syllables

Examples

Many scientific terms are polysyllabic words that can be difficult for children to pronounce.
In her essay, she avoided polysyllabic words to make the text more readable.
My students often struggle with spelling polysyllabic words in English.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monosyllabic word - word of one syllable
  • Antonym: disyllabic word - word of two syllables
  • Synonym: polysyllabic - having more than one syllable (general adjective)

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'syllabē' (syllable).
