order Urodella

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər juːˈrɒdələ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə juːˈrɒdɪlə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of amphibians that includes salamanders, newts, and congo snakes, characterized by elongated bodies and tails.
order of amphibians; salamanders and newts

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the order Urodella in the remote mountain streams.
Unlike frogs, members of the order Urodella retain their tails throughout life.
Some biologists debate the evolutionary relationships within the order Urodella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caudata - taxonomic order of tailed amphibians; synonym for Urodella

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Urodela, from Greek 'oura' (tail) and 'delos' (visible), referring to the visible tails of these amphibians.
