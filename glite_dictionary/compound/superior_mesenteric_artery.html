superior mesenteric artery

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɪriɚ ˌmesənˈterɪk ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˌmɛsənˈterɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the upper part of the aorta and supplies blood to the small intestine, cecum, and part of the colon
artery supplying the intestines
medical

Examples

The superior mesenteric artery plays a crucial role in supplying blood to most of the small intestine.
A blockage in the superior mesenteric artery can lead to severe abdominal pain and digestive issues.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully identified the superior mesenteric artery to avoid accidental injury.

Etymology

From Latin: 'superior' (higher), 'mesentericus' (of the mesentery), 'arteria' (artery).
