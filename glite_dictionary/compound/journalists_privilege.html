journalist's privilege

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːnəlɪsts ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːnəlɪsts ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the legal right of a journalist to refuse to reveal confidential information or sources in certain circumstances
legal right of journalists to keep sources confidential
legal

Examples

Several courts have debated the extent of journalist's privilege regarding confidential sources.
She refused to disclose her source, citing journalist's privilege as her defense.
Some states recognize journalist's privilege by statute, while others do not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reporter's privilege - legal protection allowing reporters to withhold confidential sources
