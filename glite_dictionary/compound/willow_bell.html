willow bell

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊ bɛl / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ bɛl / (BrE)
A perennial European bellflower (Campanula persicifolia) with racemes of white or blue flowers.
a European bellflower with white or blue flowers

Examples

The willow bell thrives in both sunny and partially shaded areas of the garden.
She planted several willow bell flowers to add a touch of blue to her borders.
The delicate white blooms of the willow bell attracted many pollinators in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - any plant of the genus Campanula with bell-shaped flowers
