County of Artois

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaʊnti əv ɑrˈtwɑ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnti əv ɑːˈtwɑː / (BrE)
a historical province of northern France near the English Channel, situated between Picardy and Flanders
historical province in northern France

Examples

The County of Artois played a significant role in medieval European history.
Many cultural traditions from the County of Artois can still be found in the region today.
The treaty transferred control of the County of Artois to the French crown.

Etymology

From French 'Artois', itself from Old Flemish and Latin 'Artesia'.
