rhythm method

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪðəm ˌmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈrɪðəm ˌmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method of natural family planning that attempts to predict ovulation and avoid conception by abstaining from sexual intercourse during the woman's most fertile period, estimated by calendar calculations
natural family planning method using menstrual cycle
medical

Examples

Some couples rely on the rhythm method to avoid pregnancy, tracking the woman's menstrual cycles carefully.
The doctor explained how the rhythm method works and its limitations compared to other contraceptive methods.
While the rhythm method can be natural and inexpensive, it requires discipline and accurate record-keeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calendar method - a method of birth control based on the timing of the menstrual cycle
  • Synonym: contraception - the deliberate prevention of pregnancy
  • Antonym: birth control pill - oral medication used for preventing pregnancy

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɪðəm ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈrɪðəm ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
A form of natural family planning in which a woman abstains from unprotected sex during the days around ovulation, based on calculating the fertile period in her menstrual cycle.
contraceptive method based on fertility timing
medical

Examples

The couple decided to use the rhythm method as their primary form of contraception.
Some doctors caution that the rhythm method is less reliable than other methods of birth control.
By carefully tracking her menstrual cycle, she practiced the rhythm method to avoid pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural family planning - contraceptive methods based on fertility awareness and cycle tracking

Etymology

Named for the 'rhythm' or regular intervals of the menstrual cycle; 'rhythm' from Greek 'rhythmos' (measured motion, time).
