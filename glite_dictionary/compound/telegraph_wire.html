telegraph wire

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌɡræf waɪr / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf waɪə / (BrE)
a wire used to carry telegraph and telephone signals over distances
wire for carrying telegraph/telephone signals

Examples

The old telegraph wire stretched across the countryside for miles.
A broken telegraph wire disrupted communication between the two towns.
During the storm, several telegraph wires were knocked down by falling trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wire - a thin, flexible strand of metal
