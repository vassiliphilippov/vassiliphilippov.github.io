canola oil

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnoʊlə ɔɪl / (AmE), / kəˈnəʊlə ɔɪl / (BrE)
A light-colored vegetable oil made from specially bred rapeseed, high in monounsaturated fats, commonly used in cooking and food preparation.
light vegetable oil from rapeseed

Examples

Heat some canola oil in the pan before adding the vegetables.
Many people prefer canola oil for its mild flavor and health benefits.
She always checks if her salad dressing contains canola oil instead of other fats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapeseed oil - oil extracted from the seeds of the rape plant

Etymology

From 'Canola,' a trademark derived from 'Canadian oil, low acid,' referring to the origin and breeding of the rapeseed plant.
