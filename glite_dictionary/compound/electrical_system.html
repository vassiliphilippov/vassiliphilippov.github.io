electrical system

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the set of components in a vehicle or building that provides and distributes electricity for various functions such as starting the engine, lighting, and powering equipment
system that distributes electricity in a vehicle or building

Examples

The mechanic checked the electrical system after the car wouldn’t start.
A fault in the electrical system caused the building’s lights to flicker.
Upgrading the electrical system is often necessary in older homes to meet modern safety standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuit - a complete and closed path through which electric current can flow

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a network or infrastructure that supplies electricity for use in buildings, vehicles, or an area
utility that provides electricity

Examples

The city upgraded its electrical system to prevent future blackouts.
A reliable electrical system is essential for modern hospitals to function safely.
Many homes lost power when the storm damaged the electrical system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power grid - network that delivers electricity from producers to consumers
  • Synonym: distribution system - system for delivering electricity to end users
