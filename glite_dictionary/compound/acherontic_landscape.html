Acherontic landscape

1. NOUN

/ ˌækəˈrɑn.tɪk ˈlænd.skeɪp / (AmE), / ˌak.əˈrɒn.tɪk ˈlan(d).skeɪp / (BrE)
A place or scene that is dark, gloomy, or reminiscent of hell, especially in a literary or poetic context.
gloomy or hellish place or scene

Examples

The novel's Acherontic landscape gave every scene an eerie, unsettling feeling.
As the mist rolled in over the dark river, the valley took on an Acherontic landscape.
Artists often depict the underworld with an Acherontic landscape of twisted trees and shadowy figures.

Synset Examples

The Acherontic landscape in the book was haunting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infernal - resembling hell; extremely evil or cruel
  • Antonym: paradisiacal - resembling paradise; heavenly or blissful

Etymology

From Acherontic, relating to Acheron, a river of the underworld in Greek mythology, and landscape.
