foot soldier

1. NOUN

/ fʊt ˈsoʊldʒɚ / (AmE), / fʊt ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
a soldier who fights on foot, typically armed with small weapons rather than mounted or in vehicles
infantry soldier who fights on foot

Examples

The foot soldier marched alongside the tanks as they advanced into the city.
During the medieval era, the majority of armies were made up of foot soldiers.
As a foot soldier, he carried only a rifle and minimal gear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantryman - a soldier belonging to an infantry unit

2. NOUN

/ fʊt ˈsoʊldʒɚ / (AmE), / fʊt ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
A person who does routine or subordinate work for an organization, often under the authority of others.
routine or subordinate worker

Examples

The campaign relied on hundreds of foot soldiers to distribute flyers to every house.
She started as a foot soldier in the company before working her way up to management.
Most of the research was done by graduate students, the foot soldiers of the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underling - a subordinate or junior worker
  • Antonym: superior - someone in a position of higher authority

Etymology

Originally referred to soldiers who fought on foot in contrast to cavalry; later extended figuratively to subordinate workers.

3. NOUN

/ fʊt ˈsoʊl.dʒɚ / (AmE), / fʊt ˈsəʊl.dʒə / (BrE)
A soldier who fights on foot rather than on horseback, or a low-ranking, ordinary member of an organization or movement.
ordinary infantry or low-ranking member

Examples

Every foot soldier followed orders without question.
He started as a foot soldier in the company, learning the ropes.
The campaign relied on dedicated foot soldiers to spread the message.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantryman - a soldier belonging to the infantry
  • Antonym: officer - a member of the armed forces holding a position of authority

Etymology

From 'foot' (referring to movement on foot) and 'soldier' (from Old French 'soudier', one who serves for pay).
