north forty

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ ˈfɔr.ti / (AmE), / nɔːθ ˈfɔː.ti / (BrE)
A tract of land, usually forty acres, on the northern section of a farm or property, especially in rural America.
forty-acre plot on the north side (agriculture)
american

Examples

We planted alfalfa on the north forty this season.
During the drought, the north forty was the only field that still produced a crop.
He inherited the north forty after his parents retired.

Synset Examples

The north forty is perfect for grazing cattle.
They bought a north forty for farming.

Etymology

From the practice of dividing farmland into forty-acre plots in 19th-century United States; 'north' indicates the location on the property.
