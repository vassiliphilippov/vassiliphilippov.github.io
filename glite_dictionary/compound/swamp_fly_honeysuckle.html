swamp fly honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp flaɪ hʌnɪˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / swɒmp flaɪ hʌnɪˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
a variety of fly honeysuckle (Lonicera oblongifolia) that grows in swampy or wetland areas
a wetland variety of fly honeysuckle

Examples

Swamp fly honeysuckle thrives in the moist, acidic soils of northern bogs and swamps.
The berries of the swamp fly honeysuckle are an important food source for certain bird species.
Botanists surveyed several wetland regions and catalogued numerous swamp fly honeysuckle specimens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly honeysuckle - a type of honeysuckle shrub of the genus Lonicera
