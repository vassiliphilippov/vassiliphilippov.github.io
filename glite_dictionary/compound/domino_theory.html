domino theory

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːməˌnoʊ ˈθɪri / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəʊ ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the political theory that if one country falls under communist control, neighboring countries will also fall like a row of dominoes
political theory about the spread of communism

Examples

During the Cold War, many American policymakers believed strongly in the domino theory.
The domino theory was often used to justify U.S. intervention in Southeast Asia.
Some historians now question whether the domino theory was ever a valid explanation for the spread of communism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: containment - the policy of preventing the expansion of a hostile power or ideology

Etymology

Named by analogy with falling dominoes, first used in the 1950s to describe the spread of communism.
