Old Testament

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈtɛstəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / əʊld ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
the collection of books forming the first part of the Christian Bible, comprising the sacred scriptures of the Hebrew people
first part of the Christian Bible

Examples

Many stories from the Old Testament are frequently referenced in literature and art.
She studied the laws and prophecies found in the Old Testament.
The church service included a reading from the Old Testament.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible, focusing on the life and teachings of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin 'Vetus Testamentum', translating Greek 'Hē Palaia Diathēkē' meaning 'the old covenant'.

2. PROPN

/ oʊld ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / əʊld ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
the first part of the Christian Bible, comprising books written before the life of Jesus Christ and central to both Jewish and Christian traditions
first part of the Christian Bible

Examples

Many early laws and customs in Western society have roots in the Old Testament.
She spent a year studying the prophets of the Old Testament at university.
The Old Testament is regarded as sacred scripture in both Judaism and Christianity.

Synset Examples

The Old Testament has many lessons relevant today.
He often quotes the Old Testament in his sermons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible, describing the life and teachings of Jesus and the early church

Etymology

From Latin 'Vetus Testamentum', translating Greek 'hē palaia diathēkē' (the old covenant/testament).
