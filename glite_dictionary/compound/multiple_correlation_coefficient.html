multiple correlation coefficient

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltəpəl kəˌrɛləˈʃən koʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl kəˌrɛləˈʃən kəʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a statistical measure estimating the combined effect of two or more independent variables on a dependent variable
statistical measure of combined variable effects

Examples

The multiple correlation coefficient indicated how well the model predicted student performance based on several variables.
When adding new predictors, the multiple correlation coefficient noticeably increased, showing improved model accuracy.
Researchers reported a multiple correlation coefficient of 0.92 for their combined factors affecting patient recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation coefficient - a statistical measure of the relationship between two variables

Etymology

Formed from 'multiple' + 'correlation coefficient', originating in early 20th-century statistical theory.
