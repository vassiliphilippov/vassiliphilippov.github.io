face angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪs ˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs ˌæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the angle formed by two edges that meet at a vertex on the face of a polyhedron or polyhedral angle
angle between two edges of a polyhedron's face

Examples

A regular tetrahedron has each face angle equal to 60 degrees.
The face angle at each vertex of the cube is a right angle.
Calculating the face angle helps determine the shape and symmetry of a polyhedron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dihedral angle - the angle between two planes, especially faces of a polyhedron
