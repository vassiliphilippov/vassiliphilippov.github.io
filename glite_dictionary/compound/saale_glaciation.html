Saale glaciation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.lə ɡleɪ.siˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.lə ɡleɪ.sɪˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the next-to-last major glaciation of the Pleistocene epoch in northern Europe, and the geological deposits formed during that period
Pleistocene glaciation in northern Europe

Examples

Geologists discovered evidence of the Saale glaciation in the sediment layers beneath the North German Plain.
The Saale glaciation significantly shaped the landscape of northern Europe through ice movement and deposition.
Studies of the Saale glaciation help scientists reconstruct Pleistocene climate patterns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Würm glaciation - the last glacial period in the Alps and central Europe
  • Degree Lower: Elster glaciation - the preceding Pleistocene glaciation in northern Europe

Etymology

Named after the Saale River in Germany, near which deposits from this glaciation were first studied.
