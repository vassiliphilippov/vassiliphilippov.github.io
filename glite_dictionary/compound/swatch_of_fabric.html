swatch of fabric

1. NOUN

/ swɑtʃ əv ˈfæbrɪk / (AmE), / swɒtʃ əv ˈfæbrɪk / (BrE)
a small sample piece of fabric used to show the color, texture, or pattern before making a larger purchase or decision
sample piece of fabric

Examples

The designer handed me a swatch of fabric so I could feel the material.
Each customer may request a swatch of fabric before making their choice.
Several swatches of fabric were laid out on the table for comparison.

Synset Examples

She selected a swatch of fabric for her new dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a small part or quantity intended to show what the whole is like

Etymology

From 'swatch', possibly related to Scots 'swats' (sip, taste), and 'fabric' from Latin 'fabrica' (a workshop).
