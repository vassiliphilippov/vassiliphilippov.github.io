miner's lettuce

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚz ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnəz ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
A succulent annual herb of western North America, sometimes grown as a salad or pot herb, with tender round leaves and small white flowers.
a succulent leafy herb used in salads

Examples

In early spring, miner's lettuce carpets the forest floor with its delicate green leaves.
She added fresh miner's lettuce to the salad for a mild, refreshing flavor.
During the hike, they foraged for miner's lettuce growing along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purslane - a type of succulent herb used in salads and cooking

Etymology

Named for its historical use as a food source by California gold miners in the 19th century.
