far-fetched

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɑrˈfɛtʃt / (AmE), / ˌfɑːˈfɛtʃt / (BrE)
(of an idea or story) very unlikely and difficult to believe; improbable or exaggerated.
improbable, hard to believe

Examples

The plot of the movie was so far-fetched that I couldn't take it seriously.
She dismissed his explanation as too far-fetched to be true.
Some of his theories sound far-fetched, but they are supported by evidence.

Synset Examples

He was always full of far-fetched plans, none of which ever worked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implausible - not seeming reasonable or probable; failing to convince
  • Antonym: believable - able to be believed; credible

Etymology

From 'far' + 'fetched', meaning 'brought from a distance' (i.e., forced or exaggerated), dating from the 16th century.
