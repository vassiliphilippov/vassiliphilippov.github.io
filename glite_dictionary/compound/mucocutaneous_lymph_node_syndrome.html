mucocutaneous lymph node syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːkoʊkjuːˈteɪniəs ˈlɪmf noʊd ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkəʊkjuːˈteɪniəs ˈlɪmf nəʊd ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
an acute illness of young children, marked by fever, rash, and swollen lymph nodes; also known as Kawasaki disease.
childhood illness with fever, rash, and swollen lymph nodes
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with mucocutaneous lymph node syndrome after noticing the rash and swollen glands.
Children suffering from mucocutaneous lymph node syndrome typically present with persistent fever and red eyes.
Early treatment of mucocutaneous lymph node syndrome is crucial to prevent long-term heart complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kawasaki disease - an alternative name for mucocutaneous lymph node syndrome

Etymology

From Latin 'mucosa' (mucous membrane) + 'cutaneus' (of the skin) + 'lymph node' + Greek 'syndromē' (syndrome).
