quick march

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / kwɪk mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a marching pace used in military drill that is faster than walking but slower than running, typically at about 120 steps per minute
military marching pace (about 120 steps/min)

Examples

At the command of quick march, the soldiers began to move smartly forward.
After the parade, the troops performed a quick march around the square.
The recruits practiced quick march until their steps were perfectly in sync.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: double march - a military marching pace faster than quick march
