Rocky Mountain spotted fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑki ˈmaʊntn ˈspɑt̬ɪd ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɒki ˈmaʊntɪn ˈspɒtɪd ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease caused by rickettsial bacteria, transmitted to humans by the bite of infected ticks, and characterized by fever, headache, and a distinctive rash
tick-borne bacterial disease
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed him with Rocky Mountain spotted fever after he developed a high fever and rash.
Rocky Mountain spotted fever is most commonly transmitted by the bite of an infected tick.
If left untreated, Rocky Mountain spotted fever can become life-threatening within days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhus - an infectious disease caused by rickettsiae, with fever and rash

Etymology

Named after the Rocky Mountains in North America, where it was first identified.
