Beta Centauri

1. PROPN

/ ˌbeɪtə sɛnˈtɔːraɪ / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə sɛnˈtʊəriː / (BrE)
the second brightest star in the constellation Centaurus
star in Centaurus constellation

Examples

Many travelers in the Southern Hemisphere can spot Beta Centauri in the night sky.
Beta Centauri is often used as a guide star for locating other celestial objects.
Astronomers have measured the distance from Earth to Beta Centauri with great precision.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Alpha Centauri - the brightest star in Centaurus constellation

Etymology

From Greek letter 'Beta' (the second in the Greek alphabet) and Latin 'Centaurus' (the constellation name).
