deontic logic

1. NOUN

/ diˈɑntɪk ˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / diˈɒntɪk ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
a branch of modal logic concerned with the formalization of concepts like obligation and permissibility
modality branch for obligation/permissibility

Examples

Philosophers often use deontic logic to analyze ethical dilemmas involving duty and permission.
The formalization of legal systems can benefit from tools developed in deontic logic.
Courses in advanced philosophy sometimes include modules on deontic logic and its applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modal logic - a type of logic dealing with modes such as necessity and possibility

Etymology

From Greek deon, deont- (that which is obligatory/duty) and logic.
