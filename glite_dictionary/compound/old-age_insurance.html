old-age insurance

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊld ˈeɪdʒ ɪnˌʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈeɪdʒ ɪnˌʃɔːrəns / (BrE)
insurance that provides financial benefits or payments to individuals after they reach a specified age, typically during retirement.
insurance for the elderly after retirement

Examples

Social Security is a form of old-age insurance provided by the government.
Many workers contribute to old-age insurance throughout their careers to support themselves after retirement.
The country introduced old-age insurance to reduce poverty among the elderly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pension - a regular payment made during retirement from an investment fund or government
