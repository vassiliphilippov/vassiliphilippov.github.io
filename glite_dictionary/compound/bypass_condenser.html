bypass condenser

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌpæs kənˈdɛnsɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌpɑːs kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
A capacitor used in electronic circuits to provide a low-impedance path for high-frequency signals, thereby bypassing them around certain components.
capacitor for bypassing high frequencies

Examples

A bypass condenser is installed across the power supply terminals to reduce noise in the circuit.
The engineer replaced the faulty bypass condenser to restore the amplifier's performance.
Proper use of a bypass condenser prevents high-frequency interference in sensitive equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass capacitor - A capacitor used to bypass AC signals around components or to ground

Etymology

From 'bypass' (to go around or circumvent) and 'condenser', an older term for 'capacitor'.
