rapid transit

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd ˈtrænzɪt / (AmE), / ˈræpɪd ˈtrænsɪt / (BrE)
A system of public transportation in a city, typically using underground or elevated trains, designed for fast, frequent service.
urban mass transit system

Examples

Many major cities rely on rapid transit systems to reduce traffic congestion and pollution.
The new rapid transit line will connect the suburbs to the downtown area.
Improvements in rapid transit help make commuting more efficient for urban residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subway - an underground urban railway
  • Synonym: metro - an underground or elevated electric railway system in a city
  • Degree Lower: light rail - a form of urban rail transit with lower capacity and speed than traditional rapid transit

Etymology

From rapid (fast) + transit (passage or transportation); adopted in early 20th century for high-speed urban railways.
