Karakoram Range

1. PROPN

/ ˌkær.əˈkɔː.rəm reɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌkær.əˈkɔː.rəm reɪndʒ / (BrE)
a mountain range in northern Kashmir, extending from the Hindu Kush, which contains K2, the world's second highest peak
mountain range in northern Kashmir

Examples

The Karakoram Range is renowned for its challenging climbing routes and stunning glaciers.
Researchers conducted a wildlife survey in the remote areas of the Karakoram Range.
K2, the second tallest mountain in the world, is part of the Karakoram Range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindu Kush - mountain range west of the Karakoram Range

Etymology

From Turkic *kara* (black) and *kurum* (gravel, rocky), referencing the rugged terrain.
