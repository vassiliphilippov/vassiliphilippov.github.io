head crash

1. NOUN

/ hɛd kræʃ / (AmE), / hɛd kræʃ / (BrE)
An accident in which the read/write head of a hard disk drive comes into contact with the surface of the magnetic disk, often causing data loss or hardware failure.
hard disk failure caused by head contact

Examples

A sudden jolt caused a head crash, destroying years of important data.
Engineers examined the hard drive and confirmed that a head crash had occurred.
The risk of a head crash increases if you move your computer while it's running.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard disk crash - failure of a hard disk drive
