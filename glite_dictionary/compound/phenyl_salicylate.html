phenyl salicylate

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːnəl səˈlɪsɪleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɪl səˈlɪsɪleɪt / (BrE)
a white, crystalline powder used as a light-absorbing agent in sun creams, and as a preservative, antiseptic, or coating for enteric-release pills
a chemical used in lotions and enteric pills

Examples

Some sunscreens contain phenyl salicylate to help protect the skin from ultraviolet light.
The tablets are coated with phenyl salicylate to ensure the medicine reaches the intestines before dissolving.
Researchers are studying the antiseptic properties of phenyl salicylate in pharmaceutical preparations.

Etymology

From phenyl (a chemical group from benzene) and salicylate (from salicylic acid).
