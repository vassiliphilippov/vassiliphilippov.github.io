priest-doctor

1. NOUN

/ ˈprist ˌdɑktɚ / (AmE), / ˈpriːst ˌdɒktə / (BrE)
In certain societies, a person who acts as an intermediary between the physical and spiritual worlds, practicing rituals or sorcery for healing or divination.
shamanic healer and spiritual intermediary

Examples

The village priest-doctor performed a ritual to cure the sick child.
During the ceremony, the priest-doctor communicated with ancestral spirits on behalf of the community.
Anthropologists studied the role of the priest-doctor in traditional healing practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaman - a person regarded as having access to, and influence in, the world of spirits
