house of ill fame

1. NOUN

/ haʊs əv ɪl feɪm / (AmE), / haʊs əv ɪl feɪm / (BrE)
a place, especially a brothel, known for immoral or disreputable activities
brothel or disreputable establishment
archaic

Examples

The detective suspected that the criminal operated out of a nearby house of ill fame.
In Victorian novels, a house of ill fame often appears as a symbol of moral decline.
In the 19th century, visiting a house of ill fame was grounds for public scandal.

Synset Examples

That street is known for its house of ill fame.
He warned them to stay away from the house of ill fame.
The novel featured a mysterious house of ill fame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brothel - a house of prostitution; a place where people pay to have sex
  • Synonym: house of ill repute - another term for a brothel or similarly disreputable place
