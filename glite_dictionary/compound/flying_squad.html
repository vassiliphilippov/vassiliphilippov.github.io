flying squad

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ skwɑd / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ skwɒd / (BrE)
a mobile group of specially trained police or officials able to respond quickly to emergencies
mobile emergency-response police or official unit

Examples

The city established a flying squad to tackle sudden outbreaks of crime.
A specialized flying squad was dispatched to manage the emergency.
The flying squad responded within minutes to the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapid response team - a team trained to respond quickly to emergencies

Etymology

From 'flying' (moving rapidly) and 'squad' (a small organized group); first used in early 20th-century British policing.
