electronic deception

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk dɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk dɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the deliberate use of electromagnetic energy to transmit misleading information, typically for military or security purposes
use of electromagnetic signals to mislead

Examples

The military used electronic deception to confuse enemy radar systems during the exercise.
Sophisticated electronic deception techniques can mislead opponents about the real location of forces.
Advancements in electronic deception are critical for modern defense strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electronic warfare - military action involving electromagnetic energy to control or attack the enemy
