factor XII

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɚ ˈtwɛl.v / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ˈtwɛlv / (BrE)
a coagulation protein in blood plasma whose deficiency causes increased clotting time; also known as Hageman factor
blood coagulation protein (Hageman factor)
medical

Examples

A deficiency in factor XII can lead to abnormal clotting test results.
Doctors measured the levels of factor XII to investigate the patient's unexplained bleeding.
Factor XII plays a role in initiating the blood coagulation cascade.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: factor XI - a coagulation protein involved in blood clotting, preceding factor XII
  • Degree Higher: factor XIII - a blood coagulation factor that acts after factor XII

Etymology

Named using Roman numerals as part of the system for classifying blood coagulation factors; also called Hageman factor after the patient in whom it was first discovered.
