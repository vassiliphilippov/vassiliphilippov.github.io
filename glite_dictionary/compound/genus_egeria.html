genus Egeria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs eɪˈdʒɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈdʒɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical aquatic plants that are dioecious, commonly known for containing species such as Brazilian waterweed.
genus of tropical aquatic plants

Examples

The aquarium contains several specimens of genus Egeria to help oxygenate the water.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of plants in the genus Egeria for their ecological impact.
Members of the genus Egeria are popular in aquatic landscaping due to their rapid growth.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Egeria, a nymph in Roman mythology.
