complete darkness

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplit ˈdɑrknəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːt ˈdɑːknəs / (BrE)
a state in which there is a total absence of light
total absence of light

Examples

The power outage plunged the entire house into complete darkness.
She waited in complete darkness until her eyes adjusted to the room.
Even in complete darkness, some animals can find their way by using other senses.

Synset Examples

Infrared cameras can capture images in complete darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch darkness - extreme or total darkness
  • Synonym: blackness - the state of being very dark or without light
  • Antonym: brightness - the quality of being full of light or shining strongly
