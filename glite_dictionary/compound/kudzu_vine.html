kudzu vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊd.zu vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʊd.zuː vaɪn / (BrE)
a fast-growing vine native to eastern Asia, with hairy trifoliate leaves, purple flower racemes, and long hairy pods, widely spread in the southern United States and used for forage and starch
fast-growing Asian vine, invasive in US

Examples

The kudzu vine can quickly overtake trees and buildings if left unchecked.
Farmers once planted kudzu vines for erosion control throughout the southeastern United States.
The roots of the kudzu vine are sometimes used to make starch in Asian cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pueraria lobata - the botanical name for the kudzu plant

Etymology

From Japanese 'kudzu' (the name of the plant).
