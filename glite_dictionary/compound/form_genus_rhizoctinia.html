form genus Rhizoctinia

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌzoʊkˈtɪniə / (AmE), / rɪˌzɒkˈtɪniə / (BrE)
a form genus of imperfect fungi, some species of which are now classified under Pellicularia and Corticium after their perfect stages were identified
a genus of imperfect fungi (Rhizoctinia)
medical

Examples

Many plant pathologists study form genus Rhizoctinia for its role in causing crop diseases.
Recent classification changes have reassigned some form genus Rhizoctinia species to new genera.
The imperfect fungi in the form genus Rhizoctinia are of significant interest in mycology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pellicularia - a genus of fungi formerly including species of Rhizoctinia
  • Degree Higher: Corticium - a genus of fungi now including some Rhizoctinia species

Etymology

From New Latin 'Rhizoctinia', derived from Greek 'rhiza' (root) + 'ktínein' (to kill).
