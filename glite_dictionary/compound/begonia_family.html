begonia family

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡoʊni.ə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊni.ə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of monoecious succulent herbs or shrubs, primarily native to tropical and warm regions, especially in the Americas
family of tropical succulent plants

Examples

The begonia family includes a wide variety of species with ornamental foliage.
Some members of the begonia family are popular as houseplants due to their attractive leaves.
Botanists classify begonias in the begonia family, primarily native to tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Begoniaceae - the botanical family comprising begonias
  • Synonym: begonia - a plant of the begonia genus, commonly grown for its decorative flowers and leaves

Etymology

From New Latin *Begonia* (the genus name) + English 'family'.
