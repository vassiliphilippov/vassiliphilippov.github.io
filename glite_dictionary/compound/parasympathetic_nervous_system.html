parasympathetic nervous system

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk ˈnɜrvəs ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌparəˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk ˈnɜːvəs ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
The part of the autonomic nervous system that originates in the brainstem and lower spinal cord, primarily responsible for conserving energy by slowing the heart rate, stimulating digestive activity, and relaxing blood vessels.
part of the autonomic nervous system that conserves energy
medical

Examples

The parasympathetic nervous system helps your body relax after stressful situations.
Activation of the parasympathetic nervous system slows the heart rate and increases digestive activity.
Yoga and deep breathing stimulate the parasympathetic nervous system, promoting a sense of calm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympathetic nervous system - part of the autonomic nervous system that prepares the body for action

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside) + 'sympathetic' (relating to the sympathetic nervous system).
