imperative verb

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrətɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrətɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
a verb form used to express a command, request, or instruction, typically in the imperative mood
verb expressing a command or request

Examples

Please note that 'Sit' is an imperative verb in the sentence, giving a direct order.
Teachers often use imperative verbs at the beginning of instructions, such as 'Open your books.'
In the phrase 'Be quiet!', 'be' functions as an imperative verb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperative mood - the grammatical mood used to express commands or requests
  • Antonym: indicative verb - a verb used in the indicative mood, expressing statements or facts
  • Antonym: subjunctive verb - a verb used in the subjunctive mood, expressing wishes, suggestions, or hypothetical situations

Etymology

From Latin *imperativus* (commanding) and *verbum* (word).
