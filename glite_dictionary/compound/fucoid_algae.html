fucoid algae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːkɔɪd ˈældʒi / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəkɔɪd ˈældʒiː / (BrE)
Algae of the family Fucaceae, typically found in marine environments and characterized by brownish color due to the pigment fucoxanthin.
marine brown algae of the family Fucaceae

Examples

Marine biologists are studying fucoid algae to better understand coastal ecosystems.
Several types of fucoid algae dominate the rocky shores of temperate regions.
The brown pigment in fucoid algae helps them absorb light efficiently underwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - general term for large algae growing in the sea or on rocks below the high-water mark

Etymology

From New Latin 'Fucus' (a genus of brown seaweed) and Greek 'eidos' (form, shape).
