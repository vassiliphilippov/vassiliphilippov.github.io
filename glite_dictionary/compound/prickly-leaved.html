prickly-leaved

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪk.li liːvd / (AmE), / ˈprɪk.li liːvd / (BrE)
(of a plant or leaf) having leaves that are covered with sharp spines or points.
having leaves with sharp spines or points

Examples

The prickly-leaved shrub made it difficult to walk through the garden barefoot.
Some holly species are well-known for their prickly-leaved foliage, which deters animals from eating them.
Botanists often note whether a plant is prickly-leaved when describing new species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - covered with spines or thorns
  • Synonym: thorny - having many thorns or sharp points
