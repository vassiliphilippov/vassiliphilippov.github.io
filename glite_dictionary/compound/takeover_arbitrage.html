takeover arbitrage

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkoʊvər ˈɑrbɪˌtrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈteɪkəʊvə ˈɑːbɪtrɑːʒ / (BrE)
an investment strategy involving the simultaneous purchase of shares in a target company and the sale of shares in its potential acquirer, used to profit from takeover bids; carries significant risk if the deal fails
risky trading strategy in merger and acquisition deals

Examples

Some hedge funds specialize in takeover arbitrage, aiming to profit from merger announcements.
Engaging in takeover arbitrage can result in significant losses if the deal falls through.
She studied several cases where takeover arbitrage produced both remarkable gains and dramatic failures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitrage - simultaneous buying and selling of assets to profit from price differences

Etymology

arbitrage from French, 'takeover' from English business terminology.
