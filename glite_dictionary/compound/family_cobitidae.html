family Cobitidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli koʊˈbɪtɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kəʊˈbɪtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small freshwater fishes commonly known as loaches, found mainly in Eurasia and recognized for their elongated bodies and barbels around the mouth.
family of freshwater fishes (loaches)

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the family Cobitidae in Southeast Asian rivers.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep fish from the family Cobitidae due to their unique feeding habits.
The family Cobitidae is known for its members' ability to tolerate low-oxygen environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loach - a small freshwater fish of the family Cobitidae

Etymology

From New Latin ‘Cobitidae’, derived from genus ‘Cobitis’.
