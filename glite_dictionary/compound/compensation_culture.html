compensation culture

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑmpənˈseɪʃən ˈkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃən ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
A situation in which a society is seen to be increasingly prone to seek monetary compensation for any perceived wrong or injury, often by making legal claims.
tendency to seek compensation through legal claims

Examples

Some believe the compensation culture has led to an unnecessary increase in lawsuits.
The company introduced waivers to protect itself from the growing compensation culture.
Journalists often debate whether the UK's compensation culture is as widespread as reported.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that compensation culture encourages frivolous lawsuits.
He's tired of the compensation culture affecting his industry.
The rise of compensation culture has changed workplace policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litigious - prone to taking legal action to settle disputes
  • Synonym: claim culture - a culture focused on making claims for compensation, especially in Britain
