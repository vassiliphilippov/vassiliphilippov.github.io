torture chamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtʃɚ ˌtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃə ˌtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a room where people are subjected to torture, especially as an act of punishment or interrogation
room for inflicting torture

Examples

The prisoners were led into the torture chamber during the darkest days of the regime.
Stories about the abandoned castle often mention a hidden torture chamber beneath its floors.
Historians uncovered evidence of a medieval torture chamber used for interrogations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dungeon - an underground prison cell, often in a castle
