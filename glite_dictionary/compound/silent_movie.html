silent movie

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlənt ˈmuvi / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt ˈmuːvi / (BrE)
A film made without synchronized recorded sound or spoken dialogue.
film without sound or dialogue

Examples

We watched a classic silent movie from the 1920s last night.
Many silent movies relied heavily on exaggerated facial expressions to tell their stories.
Charlie Chaplin became famous during the era of the silent movie.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: talkie - a movie with sound and spoken dialogue
