molecular genetics

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈlɛkjələr dʒəˈnɛtɪks / (AmE), / məˈlɛkjʊlə dʒəˈnɛtɪks / (BrE)
the branch of genetics that studies the structure and function of genetic material at the molecular level
branch of genetics focused on molecular processes
medical

Examples

Many recent breakthroughs in medicine are the result of research in molecular genetics.
She specializes in molecular genetics, focusing on how genes are regulated in cancer cells.
The conference will cover topics in molecular genetics and their applications to disease treatment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: genetics - the study of heredity and the variation of inherited characteristics
  • Synonym: genomics - the study of genomes, the complete set of genetic material in an organism

Etymology

From 'molecular', relating to molecules, and 'genetics', from Greek 'genesis' (origin, birth).
