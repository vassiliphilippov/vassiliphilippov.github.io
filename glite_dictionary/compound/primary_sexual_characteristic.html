primary sexual characteristic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈsɛkʃuəl ˌkɛrəktəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ˈsɛkʃuəl ˌkærəktəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
a sex characteristic present at birth, directly related to the reproductive organs and essential for reproduction, such as the genitals and internal reproductive organs
biological sex trait involving reproductive organs
medical

Examples

At birth, the doctor examined the baby's primary sexual characteristics to determine the biological sex.
Unlike secondary traits, primary sexual characteristic development occurs during fetal growth.
Testes and ovaries are each considered a primary sexual characteristic in human anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary sexual characteristic - trait distinguishing the sexes of a species, not directly involved in reproduction
