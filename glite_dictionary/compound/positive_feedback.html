positive feedback

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzɪtɪv ˈfidˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv ˈfiːdbæk / (BrE)
a process in which the effects of a small disturbance on a system include an increase in the magnitude of the disturbance; feedback that augments or amplifies changes in a system
feedback that amplifies changes in a system

Examples

The microphone picked up positive feedback, causing an increasingly loud screeching noise.
In climate science, positive feedback loops can accelerate global warming.
The concept of positive feedback is essential for understanding some biological control mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: negative feedback - feedback that reduces or counteracts changes in a system

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːzətɪv ˈfiːdbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv ˈfiːdbak / (BrE)
comments or responses that affirm, praise, or encourage someone's performance, work, or behavior
affirming or praising comments

Examples

Her manager gave positive feedback on her recent presentation, which boosted her confidence.
Students are more motivated when they regularly receive positive feedback on their work.
The team meeting ended with plenty of positive feedback for everyone's efforts during the project.

Synset Examples

He appreciated the positive feedback from his peers.
The manager gave her positive feedback on the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something based on perceived faults or mistakes
