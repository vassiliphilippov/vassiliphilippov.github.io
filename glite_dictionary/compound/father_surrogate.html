father surrogate

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːðər səˈrɔːɡət / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə səˈrʊɡət / (BrE)
a man who assumes the role and responsibilities of a real father in someone's life
man acting as a father figure

Examples

After his father passed away, his uncle became a father surrogate to him.
Many children in foster care look for a father surrogate to guide them through life.
The coach acted as a father surrogate for several boys on the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: father figure - a man regarded as an emotional substitute for a father

Etymology

From 'father' (Old English fæder) + 'surrogate' (from Latin surrogatus, 'substituted').
