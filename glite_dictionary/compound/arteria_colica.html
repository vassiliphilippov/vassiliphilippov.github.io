arteria colica

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪriə koʊˈlaɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə kəʊˈlɪkə / (BrE)
Any of several arteries that supply blood to the colon.
arteries supplying the colon
medical

Examples

The arteria colica branches off the superior mesenteric artery.
Damage to the arteria colica can compromise blood supply to the large intestine.
Surgeons must carefully identify the arteria colica during colon resection.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'colon artery.'
