principal axis

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpəl ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
an imaginary straight line passing through the center of curvature of a lens or mirror, along which light passes without being refracted or reflected
axis of symmetry in optics

Examples

The principal axis of the lens must be aligned for the clearest focus.
Light entering parallel to the principal axis passes through the focal point of the mirror.
In the experiment, we marked the principal axis before adjusting the optical equipment.

Synset Examples

in a normal eye the optic axis is the direction in which objects are seen most distinctly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optical axis - the axis of an optical system such as a lens or the eye
