spectroscopic analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛktrəˈskɑpɪk əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌspɛktrəˈskɒpɪk əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the examination of substances or materials by studying the spectra produced when they interact with electromagnetic radiation.
analysis using spectroscopy
medical

Examples

Astronomers use spectroscopic analysis to determine the composition of distant stars.
Spectroscopic analysis revealed the presence of trace metals in the sample.
The identification of chemical compounds was accomplished through spectroscopic analysis in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectroscopy - the study of spectra produced when matter interacts with energy
  • Degree Lower: chemical analysis - analysis to determine the chemical composition of a substance

Etymology

From 'spectroscopic' (relating to spectroscopy, from Latin spectrum ‘image, apparition’ + -scopic) + 'analysis' (from Greek analusis, ‘a breaking up’).
