wood poppy

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / wʊd ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to woodland areas of the eastern United States, with bright yellow flowers; also called celandine poppy.
yellow-flowered woodland plant

Examples

The shady garden bed was brightened by the blooming wood poppy each spring.
A wood poppy needs moist, rich soil to thrive in a home landscape.
Botanists studied the wood poppy for its role in native forest ecosystems.
