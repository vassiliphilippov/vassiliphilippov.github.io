lichtenoid eczema

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪktəˌnɔɪd ˈɛk.sɪ.mə / (AmE), / ˌlɪktɪˌnɔɪd ˈɛk.sɪ.mə / (BrE)
A type of eczema marked by thickening of the skin and pronounced skin lines.
eczema with thickened, lined skin
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed the rash as lichtenoid eczema, noting the thickened and accentuated skin lines.
Patients with lichtenoid eczema often experience severe itching alongside the characteristic skin changes.
Topical steroids can help reduce inflammation caused by lichtenoid eczema.
