unique biotope

1. NOUN

/ juˈnik baɪəˌtoʊp / (AmE), / juːˈniːk ˈbaɪ.ə.təʊp / (BrE)
An ecological area with distinct and singular characteristics different from other environments.
an ecological area with distinct features

Examples

The national park was established to protect the unique biotope found only in that region.
Researchers study each unique biotope to better understand how biodiversity is supported.
Pollution threatens the delicate balance within this unique biotope.

Synset Examples

The conservation efforts focused on preserving the unique biotope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitat - the natural home or environment of an organism
  • Synonym: ecosystem - a biological community interacting with its environment

Etymology

Biotope from Greek bios (life) + topos (place); unique from Latin unicus (only, single).
