phenylic acid

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / fəˈnɪlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A toxic white crystalline acidic substance derived from benzene, used in manufacturing, disinfectants, and as an antiseptic; poisonous if ingested.
toxic crystalline acidic benzene derivative
medical

Examples

Industrial workers must handle phenylic acid with care due to its toxic nature.
The use of phenylic acid as a disinfectant has declined because of safety concerns.
Exposure to large amounts of phenylic acid can cause severe health problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenol - another name for phenylic acid

Etymology

From 'phenyl', referencing the chemical group from benzene, and 'acid'.
