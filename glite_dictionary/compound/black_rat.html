black rat

1. NOUN

/ blæk ræt / (AmE), / blæk ræt / (BrE)
a small, dark-colored rat, originally from Asia, now found worldwide and known as a common household pest (Rattus rattus)
a widespread species of rat (Rattus rattus)

Examples

The black rat is a major carrier of diseases such as the plague.
Many ships unknowingly transported the black rat to new continents.
Unlike the larger Norway rat, the black rat is more agile and prefers higher places.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Norway rat - a common brown rat species, also known as brown rat
