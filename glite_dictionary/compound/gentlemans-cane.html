gentleman's-cane

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛntəlmənz keɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntlmənz keɪn / (BrE)
A tall, showy tropical American annual plant with hairy stems, long spikes of usually red flowers, and leaves deeply flushed with purple; its seeds are sometimes used as cereal.
tropical American annual plant with red flowers

Examples

The colorful gentleman's-cane stood out in the botanical garden with its vibrant red blossoms.
Farmers sometimes harvest the seeds of gentleman's-cane to use as a cereal grain.
The leaves of the gentleman's-cane were strikingly purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.
