methadone hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəˌdoʊn haɪˌdrɑːkləˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɛθədəʊn haɪˌdrɒklaɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic narcotic drug, less habit-forming than morphine, used in the treatment and maintenance therapy of opioid addiction, particularly heroin dependency
synthetic narcotic for opioid addiction treatment
medical

Examples

The clinic prescribes methadone hydrochloride as part of its treatment program for opioid dependence.
Many patients receive daily doses of methadone hydrochloride to help suppress withdrawal symptoms.
The government has strict regulations regarding the distribution of methadone hydrochloride to prevent misuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methadone - synthetic narcotic drug used to treat opioid dependence
  • Degree Lower: morphine - a powerful narcotic drug derived from opium

Etymology

From 'methadone', coined in German (Methadon) from chemical elements, and 'hydrochloride', indicating the salt form.
