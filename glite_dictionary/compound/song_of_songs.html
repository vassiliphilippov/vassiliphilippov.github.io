Song of Songs

1. PROPN

/ sɔŋ əv sɔŋz / (AmE), / sɒŋ əv sɒŋz / (BrE)
an Old Testament book consisting of a collection of love poems, traditionally attributed to Solomon, included in the Hebrew Bible and Christian Old Testament
book of the Old Testament; collection of love poems

Examples

Many scholars study the Song of Songs to analyze its poetic and allegorical meanings.
The Song of Songs is sometimes read at weddings because of its celebration of love.
Her essay on the Song of Songs explores its historical and religious significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Song of Solomon - alternative name for the Song of Songs, Old Testament love poetry

Etymology

From Hebrew *Shir Hashirim*, meaning 'Song of Songs'.
