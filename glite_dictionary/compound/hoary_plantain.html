hoary plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔri ˈplænˌteɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɔːri ˈplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
A North American annual or biennial plant with long, soft hairs on its leaves, belonging to the plantain family.
hairy North American plantain

Examples

The leaves of the hoary plantain are covered in distinctive soft hairs.
Many gardeners appreciate the resilience of hoary plantain in wildflower meadows.
During the summer, you can often spot hoary plantain growing along the roadside.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔri plænˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɔːri ˈplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial plant (Plantago media) with finely hairy leaves and small white fragrant flowers, naturalized in North America
a perennial Eurasian plant with hairy leaves

Examples

The hoary plantain thrives in meadows and grasslands across Europe.
Botanists noted that the hoary plantain is easily identified by its softly hairy leaves.
We found a patch of hoary plantain growing along the old stone wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - any plant of the genus Plantago

Etymology

From 'hoary', meaning white or gray with age (referring to the hairy leaves), and 'plantain' (the genus Plantago).
