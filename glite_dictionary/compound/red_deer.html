red deer

1. NOUN

/ rɛd dɪr / (AmE), / rɛd dɪə / (BrE)
A large species of deer native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, with reddish-brown fur and branched antlers in males.
large Eurasian deer species

Examples

The red deer is one of the largest deer species found in Europe and parts of Asia.
We spotted a group of red deer grazing quietly in the early morning mist.
The male red deer sheds its antlers each winter and regrows them during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elk - large species of deer found in North America and eastern Asia (also called wapiti)
