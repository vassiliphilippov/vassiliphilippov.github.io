blind landing

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
A landing of an aircraft made using only instruments, without outside visual references, typically due to poor visibility such as clouds or mist.
aircraft landing using only instruments

Examples

The pilot performed a blind landing after heavy fog covered the runway.
Advances in technology have made blind landings much safer than in the past.
Bad weather forced the crew to attempt a blind landing at the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instrument landing - landing an aircraft using navigational instruments
