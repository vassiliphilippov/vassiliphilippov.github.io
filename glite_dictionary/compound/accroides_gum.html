accroides gum

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɔɪdiːz ˌɡʌm / (AmE), / əˈkrɔɪdiːz ˌɡʌm / (BrE)
a yellowish resin obtained from certain Australian trees, used in varnishes and paper manufacturing
resin from Australian trees used in varnish and paper

Examples

The varnish was made with accroides gum to give it a glossy finish.
Some specialty papers are manufactured using accroides gum as an adhesive agent.
Accroides gum is also valued for its distinctive yellow color in crafts.

Etymology

From 'accroides,' a name for the resinous exudate of Australian Xanthorrhoea species, combined with 'gum'.
