to smatter paint

1. VERB

/ ˈsmætər peɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsmætə peɪnt / (BrE)
to apply paint in a light or careless way; to paint in a scattered or uneven manner
apply paint carelessly or lightly

Examples

She tried to smatter paint on the canvas, but the colors ran together messily.
It's better to plan your strokes rather than just smatter paint across the surface.
The child would often smatter paint on his drawings without much thought.

Synset Examples

She tried to smatter some paint on the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dab - to touch or apply with quick light strokes
  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to strike or fall in drops
