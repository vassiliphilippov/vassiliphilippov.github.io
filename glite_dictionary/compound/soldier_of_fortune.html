soldier of fortune

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊldʒɚ əv ˈfɔːrtʃən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒə əv ˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
a person who fights for pay in wars for countries other than their own; a mercenary.
mercenary fighter

Examples

He lived as a soldier of fortune, fighting in conflicts across several continents.
The novel tells the story of a daring soldier of fortune in 16th-century Europe.
Seeking adventure and profit, he became a soldier of fortune after leaving his country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercenary - a professional soldier hired to serve in a foreign army

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊldʒər əv ˈfɔrtʃən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒə əv ˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
a person who fights for any country or group that will pay them, especially one who is not concerned with the cause but with personal gain; a mercenary or adventurer
mercenary or adventurous fortune-seeker

Examples

He made his living as a soldier of fortune in South America.
The novel tells the story of a soldier of fortune caught between loyalties.
As a young man, he dreamed of becoming a soldier of fortune and seeing the world.

Synset Examples

He worked as a soldier of fortune in various countries.
Her father was a soldier of fortune during the war.
The movie featured a classic soldier of fortune storyline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercenary - a professional soldier hired to serve in a foreign army
