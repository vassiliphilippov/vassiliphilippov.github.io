smalleye hammerhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlˌaɪ ˈhæmərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈsmɒlˌaɪ ˈhæməhɛd / (BrE)
a species of hammerhead shark (Sphyrna tudes), notable for its comparatively small eyes, found in western Atlantic waters and fished for its hide and vitamin-rich liver
a species of hammerhead shark

Examples

The smalleye hammerhead is often caught for its liver, which is rich in vitamins.
Researchers are studying the habitat of the smalleye hammerhead along the South American coast.
Due to overfishing, the population of smalleye hammerhead sharks has declined in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammerhead shark - a group of sharks with distinctive hammer-shaped heads
