rape oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪp ˌɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈreɪp ɔɪl / (BrE)
An edible light yellow to brown oil obtained from rapeseed, used in cooking, as a lubricant, or as an illuminant.
oil from rapeseed used in food and industry

Examples

Traditional lamps in rural areas were often fueled with rape oil.
Production of rape oil has increased due to its use as a cooking oil.
Some machines require rape oil as a lubricant for optimal performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canola oil - edible oil from a breed of rapeseed with reduced erucic acid; synonym in North America

Etymology

From 'rape', short for 'rapeseed', from Latin *rapum* (turnip).
