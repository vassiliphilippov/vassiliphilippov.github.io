horse thistle

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A European annual wild lettuce with prickly stems, considered a troublesome weed in parts of the United States.
a prickly annual wild lettuce weed

Examples

The farmer spent hours pulling horse thistle from the edges of his fields.
Horse thistle often invades neglected gardens and can be difficult to eradicate.
In early summer, the yellow flowers of horse thistle are a familiar sight along rural roadsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow thistle - a similar annual or perennial weed with prickly leaves and yellow flowers
