pyralid moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəlɪd mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪrəlɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
a slender-bodied, long-legged moth, often found in tropical regions, whose larvae are notorious crop pests
crop pest moth (Pyralidae family)

Examples

Farmers often struggle with infestations of pyralid moth larvae damaging their crops each year.
The pyralid moth is recognizable by its long legs and slender body.
Some biological control programs target pyralid moth populations to protect agricultural yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snout moth - another name for moths of the family Pyralidae

Etymology

From New Latin Pyralidae (name of the family), from genus Pyralis.
