LDL cholesterol

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl di ː ˈɛl kəˌlɛstɚˌɔl / (AmE), / ˌɛl diː ˈɛl kəˌlɛstərɒl / (BrE)
cholesterol contained in low-density lipoproteins, often called 'bad' cholesterol because high levels in the blood are associated with increased risk of heart disease and other conditions
cholesterol in low-density lipoproteins; 'bad' cholesterol
medical

Examples

A diet high in saturated fat can increase your LDL cholesterol levels.
Her doctor recommended regular exercise to help lower her LDL cholesterol.
The blood test showed that his LDL cholesterol was above the recommended range.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: HDL cholesterol - cholesterol in high-density lipoproteins; the 'good' cholesterol

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'low-density lipoprotein cholesterol.'
