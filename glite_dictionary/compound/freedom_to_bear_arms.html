freedom to bear arms

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdəm tu bɛr ɑrmz / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm tuː beə ɑːmz / (BrE)
the right, especially in the United States, for individuals to own and carry weapons, as guaranteed by the Second Amendment to the US Constitution
the right to own and carry weapons
legal american

Examples

The debate over the freedom to bear arms continues to spark controversy in the United States.
Many Americans consider the freedom to bear arms essential to their personal liberty.
Laws regulating firearms often raise questions about the limits of the freedom to bear arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right to bear arms - the legal right to possess and carry weapons
