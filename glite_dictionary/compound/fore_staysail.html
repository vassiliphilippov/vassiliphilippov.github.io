fore staysail

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ˈsteɪˌseɪl / (AmE), / fɔː ˈsteɪseɪl / (BrE)
the innermost jib sail set just ahead of the foremast on a large square-rigged ship.
innermost jib sail on a square-rigged ship

Examples

The crew hoisted the fore staysail to take advantage of the changing wind direction.
During the storm, the captain ordered the fore staysail to be reefed for safety.
A tear in the fore staysail forced the sailors to switch to a spare sail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jib - a triangular sail set at the front of a ship

Etymology

Formed from 'fore' (in front) and 'staysail' (a sail set on a stay line).
