language area

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ ˌɛriə / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ ˌeəriə / (BrE)
a large region of the cerebral cortex, usually in the left hemisphere, containing centers involved in language processing and production
region of the brain for language functions
medical

Examples

The language area of the brain is crucial for speech and comprehension.
Damage to the language area can result in serious communication difficulties.
Studies have shown that the language area develops rapidly in early childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Broca's area - region of the brain involved in speech production
  • Synonym: Wernicke's area - region of the brain associated with understanding language
