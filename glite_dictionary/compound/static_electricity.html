static electricity

1. NOUN

/ ˌstætɪk ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌstætɪk ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
electricity produced by the accumulation of electric charges on the surface of a material, typically caused by friction
electricity from accumulated surface charges

Examples

When you comb your hair in winter, static electricity can make it stand on end.
The crackling sound you hear when removing a sweater is caused by static electricity.
Lightning is a dramatic natural example of static electricity being discharged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electricity - form of energy resulting from charged particles
