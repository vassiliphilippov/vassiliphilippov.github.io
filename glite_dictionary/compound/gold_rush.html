gold rush

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld rʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊld rʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden movement of people to an area where gold has been discovered, especially in hopes of making a fortune
sudden migration to a new gold discovery

Examples

The California gold rush of 1849 attracted thousands of fortune seekers from around the world.
Many towns sprang up overnight during the gold rush era, only to disappear just as quickly.
Stories of instant wealth fueled the gold rush in Alaska at the turn of the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold fever - intense excitement and rush for gold

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld rʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊld rʌʃ / (BrE)
a situation in which many people suddenly move to a place or begin an activity in search of quick wealth or success, especially as a result of a new opportunity
sudden rush for quick wealth

Examples

The tech industry experienced a gold rush when smartphones first became popular.
After oil was discovered in the region, there was a modern-day gold rush with investors pouring in.
His startup joined the gold rush to develop new artificial intelligence applications.

Synset Examples

the demand for testing has created a boom for those unregulated laboratories where boxes of specimen jars are processed like an assembly line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boom - a period of sudden economic growth or activity
  • Antonym: bust - a period of sudden economic decline following a boom or rush

Etymology

Originally referred to the migrations of people to areas with newly discovered gold deposits in the 19th century, especially in California and Australia.

3. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld rʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊld rʌʃ / (BrE)
A period of rapid migration to an area where gold has been discovered, often accompanied by feverish activity and economic opportunity.
mass migration after gold discovery

Examples

The California gold rush changed the state's history and attracted people from all over the world.
During the Australian gold rush, entire towns sprang up overnight as miners searched for fortune.
Countless families moved west in hopes of striking it rich during the gold rush.

Synset Examples

The excitement of the gold rush is legendary in history.
The gold rush brought thousands to the region.
The Klondike region was famous during the gold rush.
During the gold rush, many fortunes were made and lost.
