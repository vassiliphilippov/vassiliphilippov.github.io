emergent evolution

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒənt ˌɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənt ˌiːvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the theory that entirely new properties or forms can arise at certain critical points in the process of evolution, not predictable from earlier stages
appearance of new properties in evolution

Examples

The concept of emergent evolution attempts to explain how consciousness appeared as a novel property.
Some biologists have debated whether emergent evolution can fully account for the complexity of life.
His research paper discusses the implications of emergent evolution for understanding the human mind.

Etymology

From 'emergent' (from Latin 'emergere', to arise) and 'evolution' (from Latin 'evolutio', unrolling).
