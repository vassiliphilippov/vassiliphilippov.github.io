surface condenser

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːfɪs kənˈdɛnsɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a heat exchanger that condenses steam from a gaseous to a liquid state at a pressure below atmospheric pressure, typically used in power plants to improve efficiency
heat exchanger condensing steam in power plants

Examples

The power plant relies on a surface condenser to convert exhaust steam from the turbine back into water.
Regular maintenance of the surface condenser is essential to ensure efficient operation of the steam cycle.
Engineers inspected the surface condenser to check for any signs of tube leakage.

Synset Examples

In thermal power plants, the purpose of a surface condenser is to condense the exhaust steam from a steam turbine to obtain maximum efficiency, and also to convert the turbine exhaust steam into pure water (referred to as steam condensate) so that it may be reused in the steam generator or boiler as boiler feed water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condenser - device for condensing gas to a liquid
