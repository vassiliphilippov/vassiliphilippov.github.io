confervoid algae

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɜr.vɔɪd ˈæl.dʒi / (AmE), / kənˈfɜː.vɔɪd ˈæl.dʒiː / (BrE)
Algae that resemble confervae, especially those with branching filaments.
algae with branching, conferva-like filaments

Examples

The scientist examined several species of confervoid algae under the microscope.
Pond water is often teeming with confervoid algae that form dense, green mats.
Branching filaments are a key feature of confervoid algae found in many freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conferva - a genus of threadlike green freshwater algae

Etymology

From Latin 'conferva' (a green filamentous freshwater alga) with '-oid' (resembling), plus 'algae' (plural of alga).
