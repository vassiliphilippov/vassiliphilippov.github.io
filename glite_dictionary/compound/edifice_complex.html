edifice complex

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdɪˌfɪs ˈkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪfɪs ˈkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
an obsession or fixation with building or creating grand and impressive structures, especially as a way of demonstrating power or importance
fixation with constructing grand buildings

Examples

The mayor's edifice complex led to a series of costly city projects.
Critics accused the tycoon of having an edifice complex after unveiling his latest skyscraper.
Her edifice complex became apparent in the blueprints for her extravagant mansion.

Synset Examples

He has an edifice complex, always building bigger.
His edifice complex led to extravagant projects.
The architect's edifice complex drove her to fame.

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'Oedipus complex', combining 'edifice' (large building) and 'complex' (psychological fixation).
