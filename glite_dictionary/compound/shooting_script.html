shooting script

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːtɪŋ skrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈʃuːtɪŋ skrɪpt / (BrE)
the final and detailed script used in the production of a movie or television program, specifying scenes, camera directions, and other production details
final script for film or TV production

Examples

The director requested a copy of the shooting script before the first day of filming.
Major changes were made to the shooting script after the initial rehearsal.
Each department relied on the shooting script to coordinate their work during production.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: script - written text of a play, movie, or broadcast
  • Synonym: screenplay - script for a movie, including dialogue and instructions
