Battle of Wake

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv weɪk / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv weɪk / (BrE)
a battle in December 1941 during World War II, in which Wake Island was captured by Japanese forces after a defense by a small number of United States marines
World War II battle on Wake Island (1941)

Examples

The Battle of Wake demonstrated the resilience of the outnumbered U.S. Marines.
Historians often discuss the strategic importance of the Battle of Wake during the early months of World War II.
A memorial on the island commemorates those who fought in the Battle of Wake.
