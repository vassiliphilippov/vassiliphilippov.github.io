spare change

1. NOUN

/ spɛr tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / spɛə tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
small coins or money that is left over, especially money that is not needed for regular expenses and often carried casually
small coins or money left over

Examples

I don't have any spare change for the parking meter today.
She emptied her coat pockets and found a handful of spare change.
Street performers often rely on travelers for spare change.

Synset Examples

Do you have any spare change for the vending machine?
She collected spare change to donate to charity.
He always keeps some spare change in his pocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - small coins as opposed to paper money
