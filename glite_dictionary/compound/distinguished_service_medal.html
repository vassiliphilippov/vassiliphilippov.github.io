Distinguished Service Medal

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt ˈsɜrvɪs ˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt ˈsɜːvɪs ˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
A United States military decoration awarded for exceptionally meritorious service in a duty of great responsibility during wartime.
U.S. military decoration for outstanding wartime service

Examples

General Smith was awarded the Distinguished Service Medal for his leadership during the conflict.
She displayed such bravery and dedication that she earned the Distinguished Service Medal.
Receiving the Distinguished Service Medal is considered one of the highest honors in the U.S. Army.
