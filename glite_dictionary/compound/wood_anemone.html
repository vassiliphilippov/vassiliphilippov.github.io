wood anemone

1. NOUN

/ wʊd əˈnɛmoʊni / (AmE), / wʊd əˈnɛməni / (BrE)
a small perennial European plant with solitary white flowers, commonly found in deciduous woodlands
European woodland flower with white blooms

Examples

In early spring, the forest floor is carpeted with wood anemone blooms.
We spotted several wood anemones while hiking through the old woodland.
The delicate white petals of the wood anemone open to the morning sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemone - a plant of the buttercup family with bright flowers

2. NOUN

/ wʊd əˈnɛməˌni / (AmE), / wʊd əˈnɛməni / (BrE)
a perennial wildflower of eastern North America, Anemone quinquefolia, with solitary pink-tinged white flowers, commonly found in woodland areas
North American woodland wildflower

Examples

Every spring, the wood anemone carpets the forest floor with delicate white blossoms.
The botanist identified the solitary flower as a wood anemone during her hike.
After the snow melted, patches of wood anemones appeared along the shaded woodland path.
