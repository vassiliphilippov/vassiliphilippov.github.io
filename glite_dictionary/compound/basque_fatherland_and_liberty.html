Basque Fatherland and Liberty

1. PROPN

/ bæsk ˈfɑðərlænd ənd lɪˈbɝti / (AmE), / bask ˈfɑːðəlænd ənd lɪˈbɜːti / (BrE)
a Basque nationalist organization founded in 1959 advocating for an independent Basque homeland, known for carrying out violent attacks in Spain and France
Basque nationalist militant group (ETA)

Examples

Basque Fatherland and Liberty claimed responsibility for several high-profile attacks during the 1980s.
The activities of Basque Fatherland and Liberty influenced both Spanish and French politics for decades.
Security forces increased patrols after threats from Basque Fatherland and Liberty were reported.

Synset Examples

in 1968 ETA launched a campaign of political assassinations of government officials

Etymology

Translation of Spanish 'Euskadi Ta Askatasuna' (ETA) meaning 'Basque Country and Freedom.'
