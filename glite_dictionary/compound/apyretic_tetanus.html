apyretic tetanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪpaɪˈrɛtɪk ˈtɛtənəs / (AmE), / ˌeɪpɪˈrɛtɪk ˈtɛtənəs / (BrE)
A clinical neurological syndrome marked by muscle twitching, cramps, and sometimes seizures, without fever; typically linked to calcium or vitamin D deficiency, or alkalosis.
muscle cramps/seizures without fever (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with apyretic tetanus after presenting with muscle cramps and no fever.
In cases of severe calcium deficiency, apyretic tetanus may develop, leading to dangerous muscle spasms.
Unlike typical tetanus, apyretic tetanus occurs without fever but with similar neurological symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + pyretos (fever) + tetanos (tetanus, tension).
