bissextile day

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈsɛkstaɪl deɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈsɛkstaɪl deɪ / (BrE)
the extra day, February 29th, that is inserted into the calendar during a leap year
February 29th in a leap year

Examples

In 2020, the bissextile day fell on a Saturday, making the year one day longer.
Some people like to celebrate their birthdays only on the bissextile day if they were born on February 29th.
Historically, the bissextile day was added to keep the calendar year synchronized with the astronomical year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap day - February 29th, the intercalary day in a leap year

Etymology

From Latin 'dies bissextilis', meaning 'bissextile day', from 'bis' (twice) + 'sextus' (sixth), referring to the intercalated day in the Julian calendar.
