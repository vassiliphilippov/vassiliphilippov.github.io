borrowing cost

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːroʊɪŋ kɑst / (AmE), / ˈbɒrəʊɪŋ kɒst / (BrE)
the amount charged or paid for the use of money or assets that are borrowed, typically in the form of interest or fees
cost incurred for borrowing money or assets

Examples

The company's borrowing cost increased after the bank raised interest rates.
Reducing your borrowing cost can save a significant amount over the life of a loan.
The government is concerned about the rising borrowing cost for businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest rate - the percentage charged for borrowing money
  • Antonym: principal - the amount of money borrowed or lent, excluding interest
