renal corpuscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːnəl ˈkɔːrpəsəl / (AmE), / ˈriːnəl ˈkɔːpəsl / (BrE)
A structure in the kidney consisting of Bowman's capsule and a glomerulus, found at the enlarged end of a nephron and responsible for filtering blood.
kidney structure for blood filtration
medical

Examples

The renal corpuscle plays a crucial role in the first step of urine formation.
Each nephron contains a renal corpuscle that filters blood plasma.
Damage to the renal corpuscle can impair kidney function and waste removal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glomerulus - cluster of capillaries in the renal corpuscle
  • Degree Lower: nephron - the functional unit of the kidney

Etymology

From Latin 'renalis' (of the kidney) and 'corpusculum' (small body), diminutive of 'corpus' (body).
