Battle of Granicus River

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv ˈɡrænɪkəs ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈɡranɪkəs ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the first major battle won by Alexander the Great against the Persian Empire in 334 BC, marking the beginning of his conquest of Asia Minor
first major victory of Alexander over Persians (334 BC)

Examples

The Battle of Granicus River marked the beginning of Alexander's campaign in Asia.
Historians often study the tactics Alexander used at the Battle of Granicus River.
After the Battle of Granicus River, Persian resistance in Asia Minor was significantly weakened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Issus - another major battle won by Alexander against the Persians
