safety school

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti skul / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti skuːl / (BrE)
a college or university with a high acceptance rate, to which a student applies because they are confident of being admitted
college or university with likely admission

Examples

She applied to a safety school to ensure she had a guaranteed college option.
It's smart to include at least one safety school when making a college application list.
After getting accepted by all her safety schools, she finally relaxed.

Synset Examples

He applied to a safety school in case he didn't get in.
Everyone should have a safety school on their list.
Choosing a safety school eased her college anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reach school - a college where admission is less likely for the applicant
  • Antonym: target school - a college where admission is reasonably likely for the applicant
