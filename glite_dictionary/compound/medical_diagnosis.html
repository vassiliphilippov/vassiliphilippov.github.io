medical diagnosis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪkəl daɪəɡˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl daɪəɡˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the identification of a disease or condition by examining a patient's symptoms and signs, often with the help of medical tests
identification of a disease from symptoms
medical

Examples

After several tests, the doctor gave a medical diagnosis of pneumonia.
Receiving a medical diagnosis early can significantly improve treatment outcomes.
Advances in technology have made medical diagnosis more accurate and efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnosis - the identification of the nature of an illness or problem

Etymology

From Greek 'diagnōsis' (discernment, distinguishing), via Latin, combined with 'medical'.
