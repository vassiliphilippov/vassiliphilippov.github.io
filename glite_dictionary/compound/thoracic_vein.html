thoracic vein

1. NOUN

/ θɔˈræsɪk veɪn / (AmE), / θɔːˈræsɪk veɪn / (BrE)
any of the veins that drain blood from the walls of the thorax (chest) into larger veins or directly into the heart
vein draining the chest wall
medical

Examples

In medical school, we studied the anatomy of the thoracic vein and its role in venous return.
The surgeon carefully avoided injuring the thoracic vein during the chest procedure.
Imaging revealed a blockage in the patient's thoracic vein, requiring further intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - blood vessel carrying blood towards the heart

Etymology

From Greek 'thōraks' (breastplate, chest) and Latin 'vena' (vein).
