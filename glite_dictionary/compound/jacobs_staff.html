Jacob's staff

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪkəbz stæf / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪkəbz stɑːf / (BrE)
a desert shrub of the southwestern United States and Mexico with slender, naked spiny branches that produce foliage and clusters of red flowers after the rainy season
desert shrub with spiny branches and red flowers

Examples

The vibrant red flowers of the Jacob's staff appear soon after the rains in the desert.
Botanists studied the adaptive features of Jacob's staff to survive arid conditions.
During spring hikes, we spotted several Jacob's staff shrubs blooming along the trail.
