Gilbert Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɪlbərt ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlbət ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in Micronesia, formerly part of the British colony of Gilbert and Ellice Islands, now forming part of the Republic of Kiribati
group of islands in Micronesia (Kiribati)

Examples

The Gilbert Islands gained independence from British colonial rule in 1979.
Coral reefs in the Gilbert Islands support a rich variety of marine life.
Many tourists visit the Gilbert Islands for their beautiful beaches and unique culture.

Etymology

Named after British sea captain Thomas Gilbert, who visited the islands in 1788.
