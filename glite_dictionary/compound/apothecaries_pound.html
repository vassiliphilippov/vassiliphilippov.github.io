apothecaries' pound

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑθəˌkɛriz paʊnd / (AmE), / əˈpɒθəkərɪz paʊnd / (BrE)
a historical unit of weight used in pharmacy, equal to 12 ounces or about 373.242 grams
historical pharmacy weight (12 ounces)
archaic medical

Examples

In old medical texts, dosages were often given in the apothecaries' pound rather than the avoirdupois system.
The chemist carefully measured out the herbs using a scale marked in apothecaries' pounds.
Historians researching early pharmacy must understand how the apothecaries' pound compares to modern metric units.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: avoirdupois pound - standard unit of weight equal to 16 ounces or about 453.592 grams

Etymology

From 'apothecary', via Old French *apotecaire*, from Latin *apothecarius* (storekeeper), and 'pound' (unit of weight).
