small fry

1. NOUN

/ smɔl fraɪ / (AmE), / smɔːl fraɪ / (BrE)
an informal term for a child or young person.
child; young person (informal)
informal

Examples

All the small fry went outside to play after the party.
The camp is mostly designed for small fry, so activities are geared toward young children.
Don't worry about the small fry; we'll have plenty of games for them.

Synset Examples

she writes books for children
they're just kids
‘tiddler’ is a British term for youngster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kid - child; young person
  • Synonym: child - a young human being below the age of puberty

2. NOUN

/ smɔl fraɪ / (AmE), / smɔːl fraɪ / (BrE)
People or things regarded as unimportant or insignificant members of a group.
unimportant or insignificant people/things

Examples

Although he started out as small fry, he quickly rose through the ranks.
The authorities are after the leaders, not the small fry involved in the scheme.
In the world of finance, independent investors are often dismissed as small fry.

Synset Examples

Don't worry about the small fry; aim for the big fish.
In the corporate world, they considered him small fry.
The small fry in this industry often get overlooked.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: big fish - an important or influential person

Etymology

Originally a reference to small young fish; figurative sense dates from the 19th century.
