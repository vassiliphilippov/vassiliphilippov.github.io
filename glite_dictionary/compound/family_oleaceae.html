family Oleaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli oʊliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli əʊliˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of trees and shrubs, including olives, ashes, jasmines, privets, and lilacs, bearing berries, drupes, or capsules as fruits
plant family: olives, ashes, jasmines

Examples

The family Oleaceae includes well-known plants such as olives, lilacs, and jasmines.
Researchers studied the evolution of floral structures in the family Oleaceae.
Many ornamental shrubs in gardens belong to the family Oleaceae.

Etymology

From Latin 'Oleaceae', from 'olea' meaning olive.
