areolar tissue

1. NOUN

/ əˈriː.ə.lɚ ˈtɪʃ.uː / (AmE), / əˈriː.lə ˈtɪʃ.uː / (BrE)
A type of connective tissue consisting of a meshwork of fibers and cells, providing support and elasticity in various parts of the body.
fibrous connective tissue with a mesh-like structure
medical

Examples

Areolar tissue forms a loose network beneath the skin, cushioning underlying structures.
Doctors examined the areolar tissue to identify signs of inflammation.
The flexibility of areolar tissue allows organs to move and expand within the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connective tissue - tissue that supports, connects, or separates different tissues and organs

Etymology

From Latin 'areola' (small open space), referring to its mesh-like structure.
