shallow fording

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæloʊ ˈfɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊ ˈfɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of crossing a body of water at a shallow point, especially where it is safe to do so on foot or by vehicle
crossing water at a shallow spot

Examples

The soldiers performed a shallow fording to cross the stream quickly and avoid the deep water.
During our hike, a shallow fording allowed us to reach the other side of the river safely.
Heavy rains sometimes make shallow fording impossible, forcing travelers to find a bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ford - a shallow place in a river or stream allowing one to walk or drive across
