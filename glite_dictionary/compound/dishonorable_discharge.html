dishonorable discharge

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɑnərəbəl dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəbl dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a formal removal from the armed forces for a serious offense, typically involving misconduct or criminal activity
military dismissal for serious misconduct
legal

Examples

After the investigation, he received a dishonorable discharge from the navy for his actions.
Being given a dishonorable discharge can have serious consequences for a veteran's future.
The court-martial recommended a dishonorable discharge because of the severity of the misconduct.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honorable discharge - a formal departure from the armed forces due to satisfactory service
