Jewish rye bread

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːɪʃ raɪ brɛd / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːɪʃ raɪ brɛd / (BrE)
A type of rye bread made with rye flour, often containing caraway seeds, traditionally associated with Jewish cuisine.
rye bread with caraway seeds, Jewish tradition

Examples

The deli served freshly baked Jewish rye bread with every meal.
My grandmother always used Jewish rye bread to make the best pastrami sandwiches.
You can recognize Jewish rye bread by its distinctive tang and caraway seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rye bread - bread made with rye flour
