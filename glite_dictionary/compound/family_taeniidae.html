family Taeniidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli taɪˈniːɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli taɪˈniːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tapeworms (class Cestoda) that includes medically important parasites such as Taenia and Echinococcus species.
family of parasitic tapeworms
medical

Examples

Members of the family Taeniidae are known for causing infections in both humans and animals.
The life cycle of tapeworms in the family Taeniidae often involves multiple hosts.
Diagnosis of diseases caused by family Taeniidae tapeworms requires both clinical and laboratory expertise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taenia - a genus of tapeworms within the family Taeniidae

Etymology

From the genus name Taenia (from Latin 'taenia' meaning 'ribbon' or 'band'), plus New Latin family suffix -idae.
