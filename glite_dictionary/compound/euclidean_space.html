Euclidean space

1. NOUN

/ juˈklidiən speɪs / (AmE), / juːˈkliːdiən speɪs / (BrE)
A space in mathematics with a finite number of dimensions, where the geometric rules and axioms formulated by Euclid apply.
mathematical space with Euclidean geometry

Examples

The geometry of our physical world is often modeled as a Euclidean space.
Vectors can be added and scaled in Euclidean space using familiar rules.
In two-dimensional Euclidean space, the shortest distance between two points is a straight line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-Euclidean space - a space where Euclid's axioms do not all apply

Etymology

Named after Euclid, the ancient Greek mathematician (c. 300 BC).
