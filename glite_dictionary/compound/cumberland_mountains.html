Cumberland Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈkʌmbərlənd ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbələnd ˈmaʊntənz / (BrE)
a mountain range in the southwestern part of the Appalachian Mountains, extending from southern West Virginia through eastern Kentucky to northeastern Tennessee
mountain range in the southwestern Appalachians

Examples

Many hikers dream of exploring the rugged beauty of the Cumberland Mountains.
Coal mining once played a major role in the economy of the Cumberland Mountains region.
The Cumberland Mountains are renowned for their biodiversity and scenic landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Appalachian Mountains - a major mountain system in eastern North America

Etymology

Named after the English county of Cumberland.
