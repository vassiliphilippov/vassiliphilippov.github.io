calico bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪkoʊ bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ bʊʃ / (BrE)
a North American evergreen shrub (Kalmia latifolia) with glossy leaves and white or pinkish rose-colored flowers, also known as mountain laurel
North American evergreen shrub; mountain laurel

Examples

The calico bush is well known for its vibrant clusters of flowers in late spring.
Gardeners often plant calico bush to attract bees and butterflies to their yards.
In the Appalachian Mountains, calico bush flourishes in rocky, acidic soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain laurel - another name for calico bush; evergreen shrub (Kalmia latifolia)
