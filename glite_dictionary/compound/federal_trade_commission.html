Federal Trade Commission

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl treɪd kəmˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl treɪd kəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
A US government agency that enforces antitrust laws and maintains fair and free competition, also educating the public about issues like identity theft.
US agency regulating competition and consumer protection
american

Examples

The Federal Trade Commission investigates complaints of false advertising by companies.
In 2020, the Federal Trade Commission issued new regulations to combat online scams.
Before merging, the companies had to get approval from the Federal Trade Commission.
