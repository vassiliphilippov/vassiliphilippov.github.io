human immunodeficiency virus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhjuːmən ɪˌmjuːnəʊdɪˈfɪʃənsi ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌhjuːmən ɪˌmjuːnəʊdɪˈfɪʃənsi ˈvaɪrəs / (BrE)
a virus that attacks the immune system and causes acquired immune deficiency syndrome (AIDS) by destroying helper T cells
the virus that causes AIDS
medical

Examples

The discovery of the human immunodeficiency virus was a turning point in the fight against AIDS.
Medical researchers are still studying how the human immunodeficiency virus interacts with the immune system.
She tested positive for the human immunodeficiency virus during a routine health screening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: HIV - abbreviation for human immunodeficiency virus

Etymology

From human + immunodeficiency (from immune and deficiency) + virus; coined in late 20th century after identification of the virus.
