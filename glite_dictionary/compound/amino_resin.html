amino resin

1. NOUN

/ əˈminoʊ ˈrɛzən / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəʊ ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
A synthetic resin made from amino compounds, used as an adhesive and in coatings for paper and textiles.
synthetic resin used as adhesive or coating
medical

Examples

The manufacturer chose an amino resin for its strong adhesive properties in the furniture assembly.
Many decorative laminates are coated with an amino resin to enhance their durability and appearance.
Paper treated with amino resin becomes more resistant to moisture and wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urea-formaldehyde resin - a specific type of amino resin used as glue and insulator
  • Antonym: phenolic resin - synthetic resin made from phenol and formaldehyde

Etymology

From 'amino', referring to the amino group in chemistry, and 'resin', from Latin 'resina' (resin).
