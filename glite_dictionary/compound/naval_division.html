naval division

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl dɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
a group of warships of similar type, forming a unit within a navy's organizational structure
group of similar warships in a navy

Examples

The fleet was organized into several naval divisions for the upcoming exercise.
Each naval division was commanded by an experienced rear admiral.
During the operation, the naval division patrolled the northern waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squadron - a principal division of a fleet, usually containing two or more divisions
