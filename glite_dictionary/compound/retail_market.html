retail market

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːteɪl ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪl ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a market where goods or services are sold directly to consumers rather than to producers or intermediaries
market selling to consumers

Examples

The retail market has experienced rapid growth with the rise of e-commerce platforms.
Many companies focus on the retail market to build strong brand awareness among consumers.
Government policies can significantly impact the retail market by affecting pricing and competition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale market - the market for sales to retailers or professionals in large quantities

Etymology

From 'retail', from Old French 'retaillier' (to cut up, divide), and 'market', from Latin 'mercatus' (trade, market).
