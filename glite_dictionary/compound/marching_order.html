marching order

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtʃɪŋ ˌɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ ˌɔːdə / (BrE)
the equipment, uniform, and kit prepared or worn by soldiers when ready to march or move out
military gear for marching

Examples

Before leaving camp, all soldiers checked their marching order to ensure nothing was missing.
The regiment assembled early, each member in full marching order for the parade ground inspection.
His boots and backpack were part of the standard marching order issued to every infantryman.

Synset Examples

the company was dressed in full marching order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle dress - standard military uniform worn in combat situation
  • Antonym: parade dress - formal military attire for parade or ceremonial events

Etymology

marching from 'march' (to walk steadily in a military manner) + order (arrangement, readiness).
