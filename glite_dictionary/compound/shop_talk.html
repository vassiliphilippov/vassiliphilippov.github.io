shop talk

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑp tɑk / (AmE), / ʃɒp tɔːk / (BrE)
conversation about work or business, especially using technical terms that are only understood by those in the same field
work-related technical conversation

Examples

During lunch, the engineers couldn't resist lapsing into shop talk about their latest project.
She asked them to stop the shop talk and discuss something everyone could understand.
At the party, their constant shop talk left the non-lawyers feeling excluded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - specialized language used by a particular profession
  • Antonym: small talk - polite conversation about unimportant or non-technical matters

Etymology

Refers to the tradition of workers talking in their 'shop' (workplace) about trade matters.
