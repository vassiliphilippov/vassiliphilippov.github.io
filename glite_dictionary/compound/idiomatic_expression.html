idiomatic expression

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪdiəˈmætɪk ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈmætɪk ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (BrE)
A phrase or expression whose meaning is different from the literal meanings of the individual words.
phrase with non-literal meaning

Examples

The phrase 'spill the beans' is an idiomatic expression meaning to reveal a secret.
Many idiomatic expressions are challenging for language learners to understand.
She tried to explain the idiomatic expression to her students using simple examples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiom - A group of words with a special meaning different from the literal meanings.

Etymology

From 'idiom' (Greek idiōma, 'special property, peculiar phrase'), combined with 'expression' (from Latin expressio, 'expression').
