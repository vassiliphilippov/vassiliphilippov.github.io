Shetland sheepdog

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛtlənd ˈʃipˌdɔg / (AmE), / ˈʃɛtlənd ˈʃiːpdɒg / (BrE)
a small dog breed with a long, thick coat, originally bred in the Shetland Islands for herding sheep and resembling a small collie
small herding dog breed from the Shetland Islands

Examples

The Shetland sheepdog is known for its intelligence and friendly nature.
Many families choose the Shetland sheepdog as a loyal and gentle companion.
Her Shetland sheepdog won first prize at the local dog show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collie - a herding dog breed with a long, narrow head and a flowing coat

Etymology

Named after the Shetland Islands, Scotland, where the breed originated.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛtlənd ˈʃip dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛtlənd ˈʃiːp dɒɡ / (BrE)
A small breed of herding dog resembling a collie, originally developed in the Shetland Islands, Scotland.
small herding dog breed, like a collie

Examples

The Shetland sheepdog is known for its intelligence and agility.
She entered her Shetland sheepdog in the national dog show, where he won several prizes.
Many families choose a Shetland sheepdog as a loyal companion and watchdog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collie - a breed of herding dog with a long, narrow face and thick fur

Etymology

Named after the Shetland Islands, Scotland, where the breed originated.
