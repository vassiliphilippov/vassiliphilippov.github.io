box camera

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈkæm.ɚ / (AmE), / bɒks ˈkæm.ərə / (BrE)
a simple type of camera, typically shaped like a rectangular box, usually using a fixed-focus lens and limited settings
simple rectangular camera

Examples

My grandfather took his first photographs with a box camera in the 1940s.
The museum displays an exhibit of vintage box cameras from the early 20th century.
Even today, some artists use a box camera for its unique aesthetic effect.
