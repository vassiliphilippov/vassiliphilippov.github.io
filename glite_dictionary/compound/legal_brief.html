legal brief

1. NOUN

/ ˈliɡəl briːf / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl briːf / (BrE)
a written document presented to a court, setting out the facts and points of law relevant to a particular case
written statement of a case for court
legal

Examples

The attorney spent hours preparing the legal brief for tomorrow's court hearing.
Each side submitted a legal brief outlining their arguments to the judge.
The Supreme Court reviewed the legal brief before making its decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brief - a written summary of facts and legal arguments in a case
