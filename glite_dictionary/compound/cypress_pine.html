cypress pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprɪs paɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪprəs pʌɪn / (BrE)
The wood from any of several evergreen trees or shrubs native to Australia and northern New Caledonia, especially from the genus Callitris.
wood of certain Australian evergreens

Examples

The furniture was crafted from cypress pine, giving it a warm, golden hue.
Australian builders often use cypress pine for its natural resistance to termites.
The distinctive scent of cypress pine filled the workshop as the carpenter sanded the wood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprəs paɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪprəs paɪn / (BrE)
any of several evergreen trees or shrubs native to Australia and northern New Caledonia, belonging to the genus Callitris, valued for their timber and drought resistance
Australian evergreen tree or shrub (Callitris genus)

Examples

The durable wood of the cypress pine is often used for flooring and fencing in Australia.
Many cypress pine species thrive in dry, sandy soils and can withstand harsh climates.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect native cypress pine forests from over-harvesting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Callitris - genus of evergreen trees and shrubs native to Australia and New Caledonia
