theme song

1. NOUN

/ θim sɔŋ / (AmE), / θiːm sɒŋ / (BrE)
a piece of music or melody that is closely associated with and identifies a particular performer, radio or television program, or series
melody identifying a performer or TV/radio program

Examples

The show's theme song became an instant classic and is still recognized today.
Every time I hear that theme song, I think of my childhood.
The band played their theme song at every concert to open the show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signature tune - distinctive tune identifying a radio or TV program, chiefly British

WordNet ID: oewn-07042575-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ θim sɔŋ / (AmE), / θiːm sɒŋ / (BrE)
a piece of music that recurs in and is associated with a particular film, television program, or musical, often serving as its signature melody
recurring signature music of a show or movie

Examples

Every time the theme song played, the audience cheered in recognition.
The composer won an award for creating the memorable theme song of the series.
She hummed the movie's theme song all the way home after watching it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soundtrack - the recorded music accompanying a film or show
