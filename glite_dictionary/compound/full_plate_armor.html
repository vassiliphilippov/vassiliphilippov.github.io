full plate armor

1. NOUN

/ fʊl pleɪt ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / fʊl pleɪt ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
a type of personal armor made from metal plates that completely encase the body, used especially in Europe during the late Middle Ages and Renaissance
historical body armor of metal plates

Examples

The knight wore full plate armor as he rode into battle, shining in the sunlight.
During the tournament, only those who could afford full plate armor competed in the joust.
Because of its weight, full plate armor required great strength and endurance to wear for long periods.

Synset Examples

Full plate armour was expensive to produce and remained therefore restricted to the upper strata of society; lavishly decorated suits of armour remained the fashion with 18th-century nobles and generals long after they had ceased to be militarily useful on the battlefield due to the advent of inexpensive muskets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suit of armor - a protective covering made of metal, formerly worn by soldiers
