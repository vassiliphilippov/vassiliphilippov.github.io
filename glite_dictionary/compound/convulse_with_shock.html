to convulse with shock

1. VERB

/ kənˈvʌls wɪð ʃɑk / (AmE), / kənˈvʌls wɪð ʃɒk / (BrE)
to react suddenly and violently in response to unexpected or distressing news or events
react violently to shock

Examples

She began to convulse with shock after hearing the terrible news.
The whole audience seemed to convulse with shock at the sudden announcement.
He convulsed with shock when he witnessed the accident in front of him.

Synset Examples

The sudden news made him convulse with shock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recoil in shock - to move back suddenly because of shock
  • Synonym: be stunned - to be extremely surprised or shocked
