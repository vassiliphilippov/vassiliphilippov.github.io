better off

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛtɚ ɔf / (AmE), / ˈbɛtə ɒf / (BrE)
in a more fortunate, prosperous, or advantageous condition than before or compared to someone else
in a more fortunate or advantageous state

Examples

Many people believe they would be better off living in the countryside.
After the promotion, he was financially better off than ever before.
You’re better off telling the truth from the start.

Synset Examples

she would have been better off if she had stuck with teaching
is better off than his classmate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worse off - in a less fortunate or disadvantageous condition
