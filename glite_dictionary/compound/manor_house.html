manor house

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænər haʊs / (AmE), / ˈmænə haʊs / (BrE)
a large country house that was historically the residence of a lord of the manor or a wealthy landowner
large country house of a lord or wealthy person

Examples

The ancient manor house stood at the edge of the village, surrounded by rolling fields.
Tours of the restored manor house are offered every weekend throughout the summer.
She dreamed of living in a grand manor house with beautiful gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Antonym: cottage - a small, simple house, usually in the countryside

Etymology

From Middle English 'manor' (estate) and 'house'. 'Manor' derives from Old French 'maneir'; 'house' from Old English 'hūs'.
