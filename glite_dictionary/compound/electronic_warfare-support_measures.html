electronic warfare-support measures

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈwɔrfɛr səˈpɔrt ˈmɛʒərz / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈwɔːfɛə səˈpɔːt ˈmɛʒəz / (BrE)
operations in electronic warfare conducted to detect and locate sources of electromagnetic energy in order to recognize immediate threats, typically under the direct control of a commander
military detection of electromagnetic threats

Examples

The military unit relied on electronic warfare-support measures to detect hostile radar installations.
Modern battlefields increasingly depend on electronic warfare-support measures for immediate threat identification.
Through sophisticated electronic warfare-support measures, the fleet was able to avoid a surprise attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electronic countermeasures - the use of electronic devices to deceive or block enemy detection and communication
