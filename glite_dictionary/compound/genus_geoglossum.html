genus Geoglossum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌdʒiː.əˈɡlɒs.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌdʒiː.əˈɡlɒs.əm / (BrE)
The type genus of the Geoglossaceae family, comprising fungi commonly known as earthtongues.
type genus of Geoglossaceae (earthtongues)

Examples

The genus Geoglossum includes species often found in grassy meadows and woodlands.
Members of the genus Geoglossum are notable for their dark, tongue-shaped fruiting bodies.
Researchers studied the ecological role of several fungi within the genus Geoglossum.

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' (earth) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the tongue-like shape of the fungi.
