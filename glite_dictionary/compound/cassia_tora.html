Cassia tora

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiə ˈtɔrə / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə ˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
a tropical herb or subshrub with yellow flowers and slender curved pods, often considered a weed; sometimes used in herbal medicine
tropical herb with yellow flowers, sometimes a weed

Examples

Cassia tora is widely used in traditional medicine across Asia.
In some regions, Cassia tora is regarded as an invasive weed.
The slender pods of Cassia tora are a distinctive feature of this tropical herb.

Etymology

Cassia: from Latin, from Greek 'kasia' (cinnamon-like tree); tora: from Hindi 'tora' (the plant), ultimately from Sanskrit.
