wicket door

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkɪt dɔr / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪt dɔː / (BrE)
a small door or gate, especially one built into a larger door or gate for individual access
small door within a larger door

Examples

The castle’s massive gates included a wicket door for the guards to pass through without opening the main entrance.
Please use the wicket door at the side if you arrive after hours.
Medieval cathedrals often had a wicket door so visitors could enter quietly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicket - a small gate or door, especially one in a larger gate

Etymology

From Old Northern French *wiket* (a small gate or window).
