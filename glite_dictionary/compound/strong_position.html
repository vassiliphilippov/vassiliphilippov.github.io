strong position

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / strɒŋ pəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
an advantageous or favorable situation, especially in negotiations or competition, giving someone greater power or leverage
advantageous situation in negotiation or competition

Examples

After the merger, the company was in a strong position to dominate the market.
Her years of experience put her in a strong position during the salary negotiations.
With so much evidence, the lawyer had a strong position in court.

Synset Examples

He was in a strong position to negotiate the deal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak position - a disadvantageous or less powerful situation in negotiations or competition
