Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement

1. PROPN

/ ˈtuːˌpɑk əˈmɑru ˌrɛvəˌluːˈʃəˌnɛri məˈvmənt / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌpak əˈmaru ˌrɛvəˌluːˈʃənəri məˈvmənt / (BrE)
a Marxist-Leninist rebel organization in Peru, formed in 1983 with the aim of overthrowing the Peruvian government and establishing a Marxist regime; also known for its connections to other revolutionary groups in South America
Peruvian Marxist-Leninist rebel organization

Examples

The Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement was involved in several high-profile hostage situations in Peru during the 1990s.
Authorities linked the Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement to other militant groups in South America.
Scholars have studied the origins and impact of the Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement on Peruvian politics.

Etymology

Named after Túpac Amaru II, an 18th-century indigenous Peruvian revolutionary leader.
