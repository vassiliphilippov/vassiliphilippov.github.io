epidural anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdʊrəl ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdjʊərəl anaesˈθiːziə / (BrE)
a type of regional anesthesia produced by injecting an anesthetic into the epidural space of the spinal cord, causing loss of sensation in the lower body, often used during childbirth and certain surgeries
regional anesthesia via epidural injection
medical

Examples

Many women choose to have an epidural anesthesia during labor to manage pain.
The doctor explained the risks and benefits of epidural anesthesia before the procedure.
After receiving epidural anesthesia, the patient felt numbness in her lower body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal anesthesia - anesthesia produced by injection into the spinal canal
  • Antonym: general anesthesia - anesthesia that affects the whole body and induces unconsciousness

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) + Latin 'durus' (hard), referring to the dura mater, and 'anesthesia' from Greek 'anaisthēsia' (lack of sensation).
