family Guttiferae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ˌɡʌtəˈfɪriː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ˌɡʌtəˈfɪəriː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines that produce oils, resins, and some timber, widely distributed in the tropics
tropical plant family producing oils and resins

Examples

Trees belonging to the family Guttiferae are known for producing valuable resins and oils.
Many members of the family Guttiferae can be found in tropical rainforests across the globe.
Botanists study the family Guttiferae for its economic importance and ecological diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clusiaceae - alternative family name for Guttiferae

Etymology

From New Latin Guttiferae, from Latin 'gutta' meaning 'drop', referring to resin drops produced by some species.
