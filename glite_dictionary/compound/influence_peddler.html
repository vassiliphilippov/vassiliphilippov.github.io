influence peddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnfluəns ˌpɛdlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪnflʊəns ˌpɛdlə / (BrE)
a person who uses their position or connections to gain favors or advantages for others in return for payment, often through improper or illegal means
person gaining favors for payment, often illegally

Examples

The investigation uncovered that the senator had close ties to an influence peddler implicated in several scandals.
Journalists revealed how the influence peddler arranged meetings between businessmen and high-ranking officials.
Laws were created to prevent influence peddlers from exploiting their connections for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixer - a person who arranges matters for others, often by illicit means
  • Degree Lower: lobbyist - a person who tries to influence legislation on behalf of a special interest
  • Synonym: corrupt official - someone in authority who behaves dishonestly for personal gain
