transversus abdominis muscle

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɜrsəs æbˈdɑmənɪs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜːsəs æbˈdɒmɪnɪs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a flat muscle with transverse fibers that forms the anterior and lateral walls of the abdominal cavity, playing a key role in core stability and abdominal compression
abdominal wall muscle
medical

Examples

Strengthening the transversus abdominis muscle can help improve core stability and reduce lower back pain.
The transversus abdominis muscle is activated during deep abdominal breathing exercises.
Injury to the transversus abdominis muscle can compromise the strength of the abdominal wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectus abdominis muscle - a long muscle of the anterior abdominal wall
  • Synonym: external oblique muscle - a broad muscle of the lateral abdomen, superficial to the transversus

Etymology

From Latin 'transversus' (crosswise) and 'abdominis' (of the abdomen).
