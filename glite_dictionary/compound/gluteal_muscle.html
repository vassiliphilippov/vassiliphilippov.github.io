gluteal muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːtiəl ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈɡluːtɪəl ˈmʌsl / (BrE)
any of the three large muscles in the buttock that move the thigh and form the bulk of the buttocks
one of the three major buttock muscles
medical

Examples

Injury to a gluteal muscle can make it painful to walk or climb stairs.
Strengthening your gluteal muscles is important for runners and athletes.
The physical therapist recommended exercises that target each gluteal muscle individually.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gluteus maximus - the largest of the gluteal muscles
  • Degree Lower: gluteus medius - the middle gluteal muscle
  • Degree Lower: gluteus minimus - the smallest of the gluteal muscles

Etymology

From Latin 'glutaeus' (buttock) + 'musculus' (muscle).
