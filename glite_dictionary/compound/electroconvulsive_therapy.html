electroconvulsive therapy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊkənˈvʌlsɪv ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊkənˈvʌlsɪv ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
A medical treatment for certain mental illnesses in which seizures are electrically induced in patients to provide relief from psychiatric symptoms.
psychiatric treatment with electrically induced seizures
medical

Examples

After years of depression, her doctor recommended electroconvulsive therapy as a last resort.
The use of electroconvulsive therapy requires careful monitoring by medical professionals.
Some patients experience rapid improvement in symptoms after receiving electroconvulsive therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ECT - abbreviation for electroconvulsive therapy

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + convulsive (causing convulsions) + therapy (treatment).
