air sac

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌsæk / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌsæk / (BrE)
A thin-walled extension of the tracheae in insects that helps with respiration and air circulation within the body.
thin-walled respiratory extension in insects

Examples

Many flying insects rely on air sacs to efficiently deliver oxygen throughout their bodies.
Damage to the air sac system can impair an insect's ability to breathe.
The structure of an air sac allows it to expand and contract with changing pressure.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌsæk / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌsæk / (BrE)
a small, thin-walled sac in the lungs or respiratory system that holds air and facilitates gas exchange
small sac in lungs holding air

Examples

Oxygen passes through the walls of the air sac and into the bloodstream.
The infection caused several air sacs in her lungs to become inflamed.
Birds have a system of air sacs that helps them breathe efficiently during flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alveolus - a tiny air sac in the lungs where gas exchange occurs

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛr sæk / (AmE), / ˈeə sæk / (BrE)
A membranous, air-filled extension of the lungs found in birds, aiding in respiration and buoyancy.
membranous, air-filled extension of a bird's lung

Examples

Birds rely on their air sacs to increase oxygen exchange during flight.
The anatomy of a bird includes several air sacs connected to the lungs.
When studying respiration in birds, the function of air sacs is crucial to understand.
