umbrageous nature

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌmˈbreɪdʒəs ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌʌmˈbreɪdʒəs ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
the quality of being shady or providing shelter from sunlight, typically due to foliage or natural cover
shady or sheltered quality

Examples

The umbrageous nature of the ancient trees made the garden a perfect spot for a summer picnic.
Visitors enjoyed the umbrageous nature of the woodland trails, especially during the heat of the day.
The architect designed the courtyard to have an umbrageous nature, offering relief from direct sunlight.

Synset Examples

He appreciated the umbrageous nature of the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shady - providing or full of shade

Etymology

From 'umbrageous' (from Latin 'umbra' meaning 'shade') + 'nature'.
