green peafowl

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈpiːˌfaʊl / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈpiː.faʊl / (BrE)
a large peafowl species native to Southeast Asia, known for the vibrant green and blue plumage of the male
a peafowl species from Southeast Asia

Examples

The green peafowl is a striking bird found in the forests of Southeast Asia.
Unlike its cousin, the Indian peafowl, the green peafowl has a metallic green breast and long, elegant tail feathers.
Due to habitat loss, the green peafowl is now considered a vulnerable species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacock - the male of any peafowl species, famous for its showy tail feathers
  • Degree Lower: Indian peafowl - a related peafowl species also known for colorful plumage, native to the Indian subcontinent
