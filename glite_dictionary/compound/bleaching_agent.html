bleaching agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːtʃɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈbliːtʃɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A substance used to make things white or colorless by removing or altering their color.
substance that makes things white or colorless

Examples

Hydrogen peroxide is a common bleaching agent used in hair dyes.
Textile industries often rely on chlorine as a powerful bleaching agent.
Always use gloves when handling any kind of bleaching agent to protect your skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - substance used to whiten or remove color
