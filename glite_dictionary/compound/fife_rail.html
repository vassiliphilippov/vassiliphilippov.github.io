fife rail

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪf reɪl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪf reɪl / (BrE)
a horizontal railing surrounding the mast of a sailing vessel, used to secure lines or provide support
railing around a ship's mast

Examples

The sailors coiled the ropes neatly around the fife rail after raising the sails.
A sturdy fife rail is essential for managing the lines on a traditional tall ship.
He leaned against the fife rail while keeping a watchful eye on the horizon.

Etymology

From 'fife' (nautical term; of uncertain origin) + 'rail' (from Old French reille, Latin regula meaning 'straight stick, ruler').
