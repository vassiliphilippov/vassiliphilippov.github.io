skin-deep

1. ADJ

/ skɪnˈdip / (AmE), / skɪnˈdiːp / (BrE)
(of a quality or feeling) superficial; not deep or lasting.
superficial; not deep or lasting

Examples

Her apparent confidence was only skin-deep and faded under pressure.
Their friendship proved to be skin-deep when they argued over money.
The politician's concern for the poor was merely skin-deep and lacked real action.

Synset Examples

Her beauty is only skin-deep; kindness counts more.
His charm is skin-deep; he lacks true substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough, deep, or complete; surface-level
  • Antonym: profound - very great or intense; deeply felt or understood

Etymology

From the literal phrase describing something only as deep as the skin, used figuratively in English since the 16th century.
