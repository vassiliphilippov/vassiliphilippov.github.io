maidenhair fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdənˌheə fɜːn / (BrE)
A small to large terrestrial fern of the genus Adiantum, noted for its delicate, palmately branched fronds.
fern of the genus Adiantum

Examples

The maidenhair fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant.
She admired the lacy appearance of the maidenhair fern in the botanical garden.
After researching indoor plants, he decided to add a maidenhair fern to his collection.

Etymology

So named for the delicate, hair-like appearance of its fronds.
