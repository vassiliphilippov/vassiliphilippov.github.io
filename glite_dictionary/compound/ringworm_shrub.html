ringworm shrub

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌwɜrm ʃrʌb / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋˌwɜːm ʃrʌb / (BrE)
a tropical shrub, especially of the Americas, with yellow flowers and large leaves whose juice is used as a remedy for ringworm and poisonous bites; sometimes classified in the genus Cassia
tropical shrub used to treat ringworm

Examples

The juice of the ringworm shrub is traditionally used to treat skin infections in rural communities.
We planted a ringworm shrub in our garden for its medicinal properties and bright yellow flowers.
The herbalist recommended using leaves from the ringworm shrub to make a paste for rashes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassia - a genus of tropical flowering plants, some used medicinally
