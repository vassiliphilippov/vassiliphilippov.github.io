carcinoma in situ

1. NOUN

/ kɑːr.sɪˈnoʊ.mə ɪn ˈsaɪ.tu / (AmE), / kɑː.sɪˈnəʊ.mə ɪn ˈsɪ.tjuː / (BrE)
A cluster of cancerous cells confined to the site of origin, not yet spread to deeper tissues or other parts of the body.
early-stage localized cancer
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed a carcinoma in situ, meaning the malignant cells had not yet invaded deeper tissues.
Early detection of carcinoma in situ greatly improves the chance of a successful treatment.
Treatment options for carcinoma in situ often differ from those for invasive cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malignant - dangerously cancerous; tending to spread uncontrollably
  • Antonym: benign - not cancerous; not harmful in effect

Etymology

From Latin: 'carcinoma' (cancer) + 'in situ' (in its original place).
