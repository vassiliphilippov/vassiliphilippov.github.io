grammatical construction

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈmætɪkəl kənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmatɪkl̩ kənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
a group of words arranged together in a sentence according to the rules of grammar, regarded as a single unit with a particular function or meaning
group of words forming a unit in a sentence

Examples

This textbook explains each grammatical construction with plenty of examples and exercises.
The passive voice is a common grammatical construction in English.
She struggled to understand the complex grammatical construction in the sentence.

Synset Examples

I concluded from his awkward constructions that he was a foreigner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrase - a small group of words forming a meaningful unit within a clause
  • Synonym: clause - a group of words containing a subject and predicate, forming part of a sentence
