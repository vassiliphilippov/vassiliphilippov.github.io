area of cardiac dullness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛriə əv kɑrˈdiæk ˈdʌlnəs / (AmE), / ˈeəriə əv kɑːˈdɪæk ˈdʌlnəs / (BrE)
a triangular area on the front of the chest, identified by percussion, corresponding to the part of the heart not covered by the lungs
triangular chest area over heart uncovered by lungs
medical

Examples

The doctor tapped gently to locate the area of cardiac dullness during the physical examination.
An enlarged heart can increase the size of the area of cardiac dullness detected by percussion.
Knowledge of the area of cardiac dullness can assist in diagnosing certain cardiac conditions.
