inferior mesenteric artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˌmesənˈtɛrɪk ˈɑrti / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˌmesənˈterɪk ˈɑːti / (BrE)
an artery that branches off near the lower end of the abdominal aorta and supplies blood to the large intestine
artery supplying the large intestine
medical

Examples

During surgery, the inferior mesenteric artery must be located and carefully preserved.
A blockage in the inferior mesenteric artery can lead to serious intestinal problems.
The inferior mesenteric artery supplies blood to the descending colon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior mesenteric artery - artery supplying the small intestine and part of the large intestine

Etymology

From Latin: 'inferior' (lower) + 'mesentericum' (of the mesentery) + 'arteria' (artery).
