Hohenzollern empire

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊənˈzɔlɚn ˈɛmˌpaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌhəʊənˈzɒlən ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
the German Empire during the period when the Hohenzollern dynasty ruled, from 1871 to 1919.
German Empire under Hohenzollern rule (1871–1919)

Examples

During the late nineteenth century, the Hohenzollern empire experienced rapid industrial growth.
Historians often analyze the political structure of the Hohenzollern empire to understand modern Germany's roots.
After World War I, the Hohenzollern empire was replaced by the Weimar Republic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German Empire - the historical nation-state from 1871 to 1918 in Germany
  • Antonym: Weimar Republic - the German state following the Hohenzollern empire, from 1919 to 1933

Etymology

Hohenzollern from the name of the German royal dynasty, and empire from Latin 'imperium' (command, power).
