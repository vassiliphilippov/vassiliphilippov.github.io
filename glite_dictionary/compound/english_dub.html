English dub

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ dʌb / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ dʌb / (BrE)
an audio version of a film, television show, or other media in which the original dialogue is replaced with a translation spoken in English
audio translation of media into English

Examples

Many anime fans prefer to watch an English dub rather than reading subtitles.
The film was only available in an English dub when it was first released in the US.
Some viewers think the original version has more emotion than the English dub.

Synset Examples

The network aired English dubs of the popular drama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dub - to provide new dialogue in a different language for a film or show

Etymology

Formed from 'English' (the language) and 'dub' (a shortened form of 'double', meaning to substitute audio).
