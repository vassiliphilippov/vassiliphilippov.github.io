Montgomery's tubercle

1. NOUN

/ mʌnˈɡʌməri(z) ˈtʌbərkəl / (AmE), / mʌnˈɡʌməri(z) ˈtjuːbəkəl / (BrE)
one of the small sebaceous glands on the areola of the breast that secrete lubricating fluid during breastfeeding
small gland on areola of breast
medical

Examples

During her pregnancy, she noticed the Montgomery's tubercles around her nipples became more prominent.
The doctor explained that Montgomery's tubercle serves an important function in breastfeeding mothers.
Enlarged Montgomery's tubercles can be one of the early signs of pregnancy.

Etymology

Named after William Fetherstone Montgomery, Irish obstetrician who described the glands.
