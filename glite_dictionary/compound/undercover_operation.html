undercover operation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈkʌvər ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəkˈʌvə ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a covert activity carried out by law enforcement or other authorities where identity and purpose are concealed to gather information or evidence
secret activity by authorities to gather information

Examples

The police launched an undercover operation to infiltrate the drug ring.
Several officers participated in the undercover operation for months, posing as gang members.
An undercover operation led to the arrest of three major suspects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting operation - deceptive operation to catch someone committing a crime
