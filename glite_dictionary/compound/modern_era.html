modern era

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdɚn ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən ˈɪərə / (BrE)
the present or recent period of history, especially as distinct from earlier times
the present or recent historical period

Examples

In the modern era, technology has transformed the way we communicate and work.
Many historians consider the modern era to have begun after the Industrial Revolution.
Art from the modern era often reflects significant social and political changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemporary era - the time period roughly corresponding to the modern era
  • Antonym: ancient era - the distant past, preceding the modern era
