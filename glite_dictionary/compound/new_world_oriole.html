New World oriole

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈwɝːld ˈɔːriˌoʊl / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld ˈɔːriˌəʊl / (BrE)
A brightly colored songbird native to the Americas, especially known for the males' black and orange or yellow plumage.
American songbird with bright plumage

Examples

The Baltimore New World oriole is admired for its vivid orange and black feathers.
Many New World oriole species build hanging, woven nests in tall trees.
Unlike their Old World counterparts, New World orioles are native to the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - A colorful songbird with predominantly yellow or orange plumage
