large-flowered fiddleneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrdʒ ˌflaʊɚd ˈfɪdəlˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ ˌflaʊəd ˈfɪd(ə)lˌnɛk / (BrE)
an annual plant of the western United States, known for its large, coiled flower spikes; considered a threatened species
a threatened western US annual plant

Examples

The large-flowered fiddleneck blooms in spring along the hills of California.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the large-flowered fiddleneck.
Wildflower enthusiasts often search for the rare large-flowered fiddleneck during hiking trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiddleneck - any plant of the genus Amsinckia, with coiled flower spikes
