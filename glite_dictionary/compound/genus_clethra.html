genus Clethra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈklɛθrə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkliːθrə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees, also known as white alder or summer-sweet, and the sole genus of the Clethraceae family.
a genus of shrubs or small trees (Clethraceae family)

Examples

The genus Clethra includes plants commonly known as summersweet and white alder.
One notable species within the genus Clethra is Clethra alnifolia, valued for its fragrant flowers.
Botanists recently discovered a new population belonging to the genus Clethra in the region.

Etymology

Clethra, from Greek klēthra (alder tree), used as a genus name in botanical Latin.
