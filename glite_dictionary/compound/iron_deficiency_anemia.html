iron deficiency anemia

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚn dɪˈfɪʃənsi əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈaɪən dɪˈfɪʃənsi əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
a type of anemia caused by insufficient iron in the body, typically due to poor diet or chronic blood loss, resulting in reduced red blood cell production
anemia caused by too little iron
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with iron deficiency anemia after noticing she was frequently tired and pale.
Vegetarians are sometimes at risk of iron deficiency anemia if they do not consume enough iron-rich foods.
Chronic blood loss can eventually lead to iron deficiency anemia if not properly treated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemia - a condition in which the blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells
