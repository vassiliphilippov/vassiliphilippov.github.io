Big Brother

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
a person or organization exercising total control and surveillance over people's lives, especially in an intrusive and oppressive way
totalitarian leader or controlling authority

Examples

Many people fear that government surveillance is creating a modern Big Brother situation.
Critics warn that social media companies could become a kind of Big Brother monitoring our daily lives.
The new policy was attacked as a step toward Big Brother control over personal information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained control by force
  • Synonym: oppressor - a person or group that exercises authority or power over others in a harsh, burdensome, or unjust manner
  • Antonym: free society - a society where citizens have freedom and privacy and are not subject to pervasive government control

Etymology

Popularized by George Orwell's novel "1984" (1949), in which Big Brother is the dictatorial leader of a totalitarian state.
