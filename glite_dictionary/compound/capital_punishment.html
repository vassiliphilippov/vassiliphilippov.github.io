capital punishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəl ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the legally authorized killing of someone as punishment for a crime
legally authorized execution of a person

Examples

Many countries have abolished capital punishment in favor of alternative sentences.
The debate over capital punishment remains passionate and unresolved in many societies.
Opponents argue that capital punishment does not effectively deter crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the act of killing someone as a legal penalty
  • Antonym: life imprisonment - punishment by imprisonment for the remainder of a person's life

Etymology

From the adjective 'capital' (from Latin capitālis, concerning the head or life) + 'punishment'.
