Maldive Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈmælˌdiːv ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈmældɪv ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of about 1,200 small coral islands in the Indian Ocean, forming the country of Maldives
group of coral islands in the Indian Ocean

Examples

The Maldive Islands are renowned for their stunning white-sand beaches and vibrant coral reefs.
Tourists from around the world visit the Maldive Islands for their luxury resorts and crystal-clear waters.
The Maldive Islands are vulnerable to the effects of rising sea levels due to climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maldives - an island country in the Indian Ocean made up of small coral islands

Etymology

From 'Maldives', ultimately from Sanskrit 'Malādvīpaḥ' (garland of islands).
