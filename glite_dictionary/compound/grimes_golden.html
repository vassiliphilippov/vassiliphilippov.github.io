Grimes' golden

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪmz ˈɡoʊldən / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪmz ˈɡəʊldən / (BrE)
A yellow apple variety that ripens in late autumn and is typically eaten raw.
variety of yellow apple

Examples

I found a basket of Grimes' golden apples at the farmer's market.
Grimes' golden is prized for its sweet flavor and crisp texture.
She packed a Grimes' golden in her lunchbox for a healthy snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Golden Delicious - another popular yellow apple variety

Etymology

Named after Thomas Grimes, who discovered the cultivar in West Virginia, USA, in the early 19th century.
