spirit of turpentine

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪt əv ˌtɜrpənˈtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪt əv ˌtɜːpənˈtaɪn / (BrE)
a volatile liquid distilled from turpentine oleoresin, used as a paint thinner, solvent, and sometimes in medicine
volatile solvent distilled from turpentine

Examples

The artist used spirit of turpentine to thin her oil paints before beginning the portrait.
Always ensure good ventilation when working with spirit of turpentine in your studio.
Pharmacists once used spirit of turpentine for medicinal preparations, though this is now rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turpentine - volatile pungent oil distilled from resin of pine trees

Etymology

From 'spirit' (meaning distilled essence) and 'turpentine', which comes from Old French 'terebenthine', ultimately from Latin 'turpentinus'.
