electrical condenser

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
an electrical device used to store electric charge, now usually called a capacitor
device for storing electric charge (capacitor)

Examples

The electrical condenser in the circuit stores energy that can be released when needed.
Early radios often used an electrical condenser to tune into different frequencies.
Before modern terminology, what we now call a capacitor was referred to as an electrical condenser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacitor - an electrical device used to store energy electrostatically, also called a condenser

Etymology

From 'condenser', referring to concentrating (condensing) electric charge; term largely replaced by 'capacitor'.
