electrostatic unit

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈstætɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈstætɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of electricity defined by the forces between electric charges, especially in the CGS system
unit of electricity (CGS system)

Examples

The electrostatic unit was widely used in scientific literature before the SI system became standard.
Scientists measured the charge using the electrostatic unit in early electrical experiments.
The electrostatic unit is now largely of historical interest, replaced by modern units.

Etymology

Formed from 'electrostatic' (relating to stationary electric charges) and 'unit'.
