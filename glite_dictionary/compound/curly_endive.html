curly endive

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝli ˈɛndaɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɜːli ˈɛndaɪv / (BrE)
A variety of endive with crisp, spiky, curly leaves and a somewhat bitter taste, often used in salads.
leafy vegetable with curly, bitter leaves

Examples

She added some curly endive to the salad for a crisp texture and a touch of bitterness.
Farmers markets in spring often sell fresh curly endive alongside spinach and lettuce.
The chef garnished the dish with finely chopped curly endive for added color and flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frisée - another name for curly endive
  • Degree Lower: escarole - a broad-leaved variety of endive
