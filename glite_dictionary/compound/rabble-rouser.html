rabble-rouser

1. NOUN

/ ˈræb.əl ˌraʊ.zər / (AmE), / ˈræb.əl ˌraʊ.zə / (BrE)
A person, especially a political leader, who seeks to gain support or stir up trouble by appealing to the emotions, passions, or prejudices of the public, often using inflammatory rhetoric.
person who incites crowds by appealing to emotion

Examples

The politician was labeled a rabble-rouser after his fiery speech incited protests across the city.
Many saw her as a courageous reformer, while others dismissed her as merely a rabble-rouser.
He was accused of being a rabble-rouser who stirred up unrest rather than seeking constructive solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to people's desires and prejudices rather than rational argument

Etymology

From 'rabble' (a disorderly crowd) + 'rouser' (one who stirs up).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræb.əl ˌraʊ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈræb.əl ˌraʊ.zə / (BrE)
A person who stirs up public emotions or incites a crowd, especially for political reasons.
person who agitates or incites a crowd

Examples

The opposition accused him of being a rabble-rouser trying to destabilize the government.
Her reputation as a rabble-rouser made her both feared and admired in activist circles.
Many saw the young leader as a rabble-rouser who could inspire crowds with his speeches.

Synset Examples

He acted like a rabble rouser, stirring up the crowd.
As a rabble rouser, she always encouraged protests.
The politician was known as a rabble rouser during rallies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitator - person who urges others to protest or rebel
  • Synonym: firebrand - person who incites unrest or strife

Etymology

From 'rabble' (disorderly crowd) and 'rouser' (one who wakes or excites), first attested in English in the mid-19th century.
