ginkgo family

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪŋkoʊ ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈɡɪŋkəʊ ˌfæməli / (BrE)
A family of gymnospermous plants, comprising the genus Ginkgo and related extinct forms, belonging to the order Ginkgoales.
plant family including Ginkgo

Examples

The ginkgo family is notable for having only a single living species, Ginkgo biloba.
Fossils from the ginkgo family have been discovered in rock layers dating back millions of years.
Most members of the ginkgo family are extinct, leaving Ginkgo biloba as the sole survivor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgoales - the plant order containing the ginkgo family
  • Antonym: conifer - a group of gymnosperms distinct from ginkgo family

Etymology

From the genus name Ginkgo, itself from Japanese *ginkyō*, and the English word 'family' as used in botanical classification.
