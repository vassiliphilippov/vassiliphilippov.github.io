Irtish River

1. PROPN

/ ɪrˈtɪʃ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ɪəˈtɪʃ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in Asia that flows from the Altai Mountains in northern China northwest through Kazakhstan and Russia, serving as a tributary of the Ob River.
major river in Asia, tributary of the Ob

Examples

The Irtish River supports a variety of ecosystems along its journey through China, Kazakhstan, and Russia.
Many cities along the Irtish River rely on it for water transport and fishing.
During the spring thaw, the Irtish River often experiences significant flooding in its lower reaches.

Etymology

From Russian Иртыш (Irtysh), of Turkic origin.
