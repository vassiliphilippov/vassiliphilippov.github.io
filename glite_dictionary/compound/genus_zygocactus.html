genus Zygocactus

1. NOUN

/ ˈziːɡoʊˌkæktəs / (AmE), / ˈzaɪɡəʊˌkæktəs / (BrE)
A small genus of Brazilian cacti characterized by flat, fleshy, usually branched joints and showy red or pink flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits.
genus of Brazilian cacti with jointed stems and showy flowers

Examples

The genus Zygocactus is known for its colorful holiday-flowering cacti.
Horticulturists often cross species within the genus Zygocactus to create new cultivars.
Botanical gardens display several varieties from the genus Zygocactus in their greenhouse collections.

Etymology

From Greek 'zygon' (yoke) and modern Latin 'cactus,' referring to the shape of the plant's joints.
