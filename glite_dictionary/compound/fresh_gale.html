fresh gale

1. NOUN

/ frɛʃ ɡeɪl / (AmE), / frɛʃ ɡeɪl / (BrE)
A wind with a speed of 39–46 knots (45–53 mph or 72–85 km/h), classified as number 8 on the Beaufort scale.
strong wind (Beaufort scale 8)

Examples

The ship was forced to change course due to a fresh gale sweeping across the sea.
A fresh gale caused significant delays at the harbor yesterday afternoon.
Meteorologists warned of a fresh gale approaching the coastline overnight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gale - a very strong wind
  • Degree Higher: strong gale - wind stronger than a fresh gale (Beaufort scale 9)
