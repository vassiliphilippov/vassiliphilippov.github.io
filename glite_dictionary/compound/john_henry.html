John Henry

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈhɛnri / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈhɛnri / (BrE)
A hero of American folk tales, depicted as a giant, strong Black man who worked on railroads and, according to legend, died from exhaustion after defeating a steam drill in a contest of strength and endurance.
heroic figure in American folklore

Examples

The story of John Henry has inspired countless songs and poems about strength and perseverance.
Many children learned about John Henry in school while studying American folklore.
The legend of John Henry embodies the struggle between man and machine during the Industrial Revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paul Bunyan - legendary American folk hero, a giant lumberjack
