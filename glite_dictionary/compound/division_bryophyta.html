division Bryophyta

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən braɪˈɒfɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən braɪˈɒfɪtə / (BrE)
A division of nonflowering plants characterized by rhizoids instead of true roots, limited or no vascular tissue, and alternation of generations; includes mosses, liverworts, and hornworts.
division of nonflowering plants (mosses, liverworts, hornworts)
medical

Examples

Scientists study the division Bryophyta to better understand the evolution of land plants.
True mosses, liverworts, and hornworts all belong to the division Bryophyta.
The division Bryophyta is distinguished by its lack of true roots and complex vascular tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bryophyte - a nonvascular plant in the division Bryophyta
  • Degree Higher: division Tracheophyta - division of vascular plants

Etymology

From Greek 'bryon' (moss) + '-phyta' (plants), used in botanical taxonomy.
