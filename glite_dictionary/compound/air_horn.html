air horn

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈeə hɔːn / (BrE)
the air intake opening or passage of a carburetor, through which air enters before mixing with fuel
carburetor air intake opening

Examples

A blocked air horn can cause poor engine performance and reduced fuel efficiency.
Inspection of the air horn revealed a buildup of dust restricting airflow to the carburetor.
Technicians recommend cleaning the air horn regularly to maintain optimal engine operation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈeə hɔːn / (BrE)
A horn that produces a loud sound using compressed air, commonly used for signaling, especially on vehicles and ships.
loud pneumatic horn for signaling

Examples

The ship blasted its air horn as it entered the harbor.
He startled everyone by suddenly blowing an air horn during the celebration.
Many trucks are equipped with a loud air horn for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siren - a device that makes a loud warning sound
  • Synonym: klaxon - an electric horn that produces a loud warning sound
