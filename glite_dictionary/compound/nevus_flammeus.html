nevus flammeus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnivəs ˈflæmiəs / (AmE), / ˈniːvəs ˈflæmiːəs / (BrE)
a flat, pink to purple birthmark present at birth, also known as a port-wine stain
flat, pink to purple birthmark
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the nevus flammeus on the infant's cheek was harmless and could be treated if desired.
Her nevus flammeus was clearly visible on her left arm but did not cause any health problems.
Many people choose to have a nevus flammeus removed for cosmetic reasons, though it is typically benign.

Etymology

From Latin 'naevus' (mole, birthmark) and 'flammeus' (flame-colored).
