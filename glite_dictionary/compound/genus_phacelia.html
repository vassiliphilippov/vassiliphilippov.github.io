genus Phacelia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs fəˈsiːliə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fəˈsiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of American herbs, usually with pinnatifid leaves and blue, purple, or white flowers arranged in scorpioid cymes.
genus of American herbs with showy flowers

Examples

The genus Phacelia includes many wildflowers found throughout North America.
Botanists studied the floral structures unique to the genus Phacelia.
Several members of the genus Phacelia are cultivated in gardens for their attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phacelia - any plant of the Phacelia genus

Etymology

From New Latin Phacelia, the genus name, possibly from Greek phakelos (bundle), in reference to the clustered flowers.
