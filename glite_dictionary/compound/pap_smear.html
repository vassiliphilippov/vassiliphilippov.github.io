Pap smear

1. NOUN

/ pæp smɪr / (AmE), / pap smɪə / (BrE)
A medical test in which a sample of cells is taken from a woman's cervix to check for abnormalities, especially signs of cervical cancer.
test for cervical abnormalities
medical

Examples

A Pap smear is recommended every three years for most women.
The doctor explained why getting a regular Pap smear is important for early detection of cervical cancer.
Her last Pap smear came back normal, giving her peace of mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pap test - alternative name for Pap smear

Etymology

Named after Dr. George Papanicolaou, who developed the test.
