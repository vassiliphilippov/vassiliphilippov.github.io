trans-Alaska pipeline

1. NOUN

/ trænz əˈlæskə ˈpaɪplaɪn / (AmE), / tranz əˈlɑːskə ˈpaɪplaɪn / (BrE)
A major oil pipeline running approximately 800 miles (1,287 km) from Prudhoe Bay in northern Alaska to the port of Valdez.
Alaskan oil pipeline from Prudhoe Bay to Valdez

Examples

The trans-Alaska pipeline was completed in 1977 and remains a vital part of the state's infrastructure.
Environmental groups have monitored the trans-Alaska pipeline for possible oil spills and wildlife impacts.
Tourists often visit sections of the trans-Alaska pipeline to learn about its engineering and history.
