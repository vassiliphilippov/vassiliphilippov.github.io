genus Limenitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs lɪˈmɛn.ɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs lɪˈmɛn.ɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A genus of mainly dark butterflies found in northern regions, characterized by white wing bars.
genus of dark butterflies with white wing bars

Examples

The genus Limenitis includes several species admired for their striking wing patterns.
Researchers observed the behavior of butterflies from the genus Limenitis in the northern forests.
One member of the genus Limenitis, the white admiral, is commonly seen in Europe and North America.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Limenitis' is a neo-Latin genus name for certain butterflies.
