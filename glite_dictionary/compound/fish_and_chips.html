fish and chips

1. NOUN

/ fɪʃ ənd tʃɪps / (AmE), / fɪʃ ən tʃɪps / (BrE)
a dish consisting of fried fish and French-fried potatoes, traditionally served hot and often eaten as a casual takeaway meal, especially in Britain
fried fish and French-fried potatoes dish

Examples

We bought fish and chips for dinner at the seaside.
Many British pubs serve excellent fish and chips with homemade tartar sauce.
On Fridays, the school cafeteria always offers fish and chips as a menu option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chips - thin strips or slices of potato fried until crisp (British sense)

2. NOUN

/ fɪʃ ənd tʃɪps / (AmE), / fɪʃ ən tʃɪps / (BrE)
A hot dish consisting of fried fish, typically cod or haddock, served with fried potatoes or fries, often eaten as a takeaway meal.
fried fish with chips (fries)

Examples

We decided to have fish and chips for dinner at the local seaside shop.
Nothing tastes better on a cold day than hot fish and chips wrapped in paper.
She ordered fish and chips with extra vinegar and tartar sauce.

Synset Examples

The leftovers were just yesterday's fish and chips.
She complained that yesterday's fish and chips were soggy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fried fish - fish cooked by frying in oil or fat
  • Synonym: chips - thin slices of potato that are deep-fried (British usage; fries)

Etymology

First recorded in Britain in the 19th century as a pairing of 'fish' and 'chips' (from French 'chipe', a small piece).
