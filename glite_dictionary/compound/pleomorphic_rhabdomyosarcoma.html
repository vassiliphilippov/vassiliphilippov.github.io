pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ pliː.oʊˈmɔr.fɪk ræbˌdoʊ.maɪ.oʊ.sɑːrˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / pliː.əʊˈmɔː.fɪk ræbˌdəʊ.maɪ.əʊ.sɑːˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A rare form of rhabdomyosarcoma that affects the limb muscles of older adults, characterized by cells with varying shapes and sizes.
rare muscle cancer subtype in adults
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma in his left thigh.
Researchers are studying new treatments for pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma in older adults.
Unlike other types, pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma primarily affects limb muscles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rhabdomyosarcoma - a malignant tumor of muscle tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'pleos' (more) + 'morphē' (form) and 'rhabdomyosarcoma', from Greek 'rhabdos' (rod), 'mys' (muscle), 'sarx' (flesh), '-oma' (tumor).
