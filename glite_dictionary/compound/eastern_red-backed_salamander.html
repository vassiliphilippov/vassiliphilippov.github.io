eastern red-backed salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈistərn rɛd ˈbækt ˈsæləˌmændɚ / (AmE), / ˈiːstən rɛd ˈbækt ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
a small salamander native to eastern North America, typically with a distinctive reddish stripe along its back
a small North American salamander species

Examples

The eastern red-backed salamander is often found hiding under logs in damp forests.
Scientists studied how climate change affects the habitat of the eastern red-backed salamander.
A child carefully lifted a rock and discovered an eastern red-backed salamander underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - an amphibian of the order Caudata, resembling a lizard
