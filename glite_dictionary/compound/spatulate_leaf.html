spatulate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈspætʃəˌleɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈspatjʊleɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf that is shaped like a spatula, with a broad, rounded tip and a narrow base.
leaf with a broad tip and narrow base

Examples

The plant can be easily identified by its spatulate leaf structure.
A spatulate leaf tapers at the base and widens toward the tip, resembling a kitchen spatula.
You can distinguish this species by the unique shape of its spatulate leaf.

Etymology

From Latin 'spatula' (a broad, flat tool) and 'leaf'.
