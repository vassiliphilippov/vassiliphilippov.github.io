South Frigid Zone

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ ˈfrɪdʒɪd zoʊn / (AmE), / saʊθ ˈfrɪdʒɪd zəʊn / (BrE)
the area of the Earth's surface surrounding the South Pole, including Antarctica and the nearby southern oceans, located within the Antarctic Circle.
region around the South Pole

Examples

Very few species are able to survive the extreme cold of the South Frigid Zone.
Scientists travel to the South Frigid Zone to study climate change and its effects on Antarctica.
The South Frigid Zone is characterized by icy landscapes and long periods of darkness during winter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North Frigid Zone - the region around the North Pole; Arctic Circle area

Etymology

From Latin frigidus (cold) + zone (belt, region); referring to Earth's polar areas.
