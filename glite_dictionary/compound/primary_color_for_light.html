primary color for light

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈkʌlər fər laɪt / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ˈkʌlə fə laɪt / (BrE)
any of three basic colors of light (red, green, and blue) that can be combined to produce all other colors by additive mixing
one of three key colors of light (red, green, blue)

Examples

Red is a primary color for light, along with green and blue.
Screens use primary colors for light to create every shade you see.
Understanding how primary colors for light work is fundamental in digital imaging.

Synset Examples

the primary colors for light are red, blue, and green

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secondary color for light - a color formed by mixing two primary colors of light
