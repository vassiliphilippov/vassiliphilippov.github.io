stepped line

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɛpt laɪn / (BrE)
A headline or line of text with the first line flush left and each succeeding line indented further to the right.
headline with each line further indented

Examples

The magazine used a stepped line to emphasize the headline on the cover.
She preferred the clean look of a stepped line for the article titles.
Designers often choose a stepped line format for longer headlines to improve readability.
