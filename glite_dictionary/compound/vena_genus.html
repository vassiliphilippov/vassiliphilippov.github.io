vena genus

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ˈdʒiː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ˈdʒiː.nəs / (BrE)
Veins that drain blood from tissues around the knee, which flow into the popliteal vein.
veins draining the knee area
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the vena genus during the procedure to ensure proper blood flow from the knee.
Damage to the vena genus can impair circulation in the lower leg.
Radiological images clearly showed the vena genus draining into the popliteal vein.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: popliteal vein - a deep vein behind the knee

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'genus' (knee).
