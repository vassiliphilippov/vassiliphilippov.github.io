reflex epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.flɛks ˌɛp.əˈlɛp.si / (AmE), / ˈriː.flɛks ˌɛp.ɪˈlɛp.si / (BrE)
A type of epilepsy in which seizures are triggered by specific external stimuli, such as flashing lights or noises.
epilepsy triggered by external stimuli
medical

Examples

Photogenic stimuli like flickering lights can provoke seizures in individuals with reflex epilepsy.
She was diagnosed with reflex epilepsy after her seizures repeatedly occurred during video games.
Certain types of reflex epilepsy are more common in children than in adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilepsy - neurological disorder with recurrent seizures

Etymology

From 'reflex' (involuntary response to a stimulus) and 'epilepsy' (from Greek 'epilēpsia', seizure).
