Gulf of Bothnia

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡʌlf əv ˈboʊθniə / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlf əv ˈbɒθniə / (BrE)
the northern arm of the Baltic Sea, situated between Sweden and Finland.
northern arm of the Baltic Sea

Examples

The Gulf of Bothnia is frozen over much of the winter, making navigation difficult.
The ferry crosses the Gulf of Bothnia between Sweden and Finland.
Environmental concerns have increased regarding pollution in the Gulf of Bothnia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baltic Sea - a sea in Northern Europe, of which the Gulf of Bothnia is an arm

Etymology

From Bothnia, a Latinized form deriving from Old Norse 'Botn', meaning 'bottom' or 'innermost part'.
