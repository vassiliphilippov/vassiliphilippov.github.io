colonic irrigation

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlɑːnɪk ˌɪrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɒnɪk ˌɪrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a procedure in which water is introduced into the colon, typically via the rectum, to flush out its contents.
procedure flushing colon with water
medical

Examples

Some people seek colonic irrigation as a detoxification procedure at health spas.
The doctor explained both the benefits and risks of colonic irrigation before the patient agreed.
Many experts question whether colonic irrigation has real health benefits beyond conventional treatments.

Etymology

From 'colon' (the large intestine) and 'irrigation' (from Latin 'irrigare', to water).
