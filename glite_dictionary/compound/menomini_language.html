Menomini language

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˈmiːni ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmɛnəˈmiːni ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Menomini people of northeast Wisconsin.
Native American Algonquian language

Examples

The Menomini language is taught in some schools to preserve the tribe's heritage.
Linguists have published new dictionaries of the Menomini language.
Fewer young people speak the Menomini language fluently nowadays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian language - a member of the Algonquian language family

Etymology

From Menomini, the name of the Native American people who speak the language.
