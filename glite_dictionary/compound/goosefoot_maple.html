goosefoot maple

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːsfʊt ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsfʊt ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A maple tree native to eastern North America, distinguished by its striped bark and large, two-lobed leaves that turn clear yellow in autumn.
North American maple with striped bark and two-lobed leaves

Examples

The goosefoot maple is especially beautiful when its leaves turn yellow each autumn.
We spotted a goosefoot maple along the hiking trail, easily recognizable by its striped bark.
Botanists often study the distinct leaf shape of the goosefoot maple in eastern forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped maple - another name for goosefoot maple; a maple with striped bark
