high jinks

1. NOUN

/ haɪ dʒɪŋks / (AmE), / haɪ dʒɪŋks / (BrE)
playful or boisterous activity; lively and mischievous merrymaking
noisy, playful activity

Examples

The party was full of high jinks that went on until dawn.
Their high jinks got them in trouble with the neighbors.
There were plenty of high jinks at the summer camp talent show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merrymaking - cheerful, festive celebration or activity
  • Synonym: revelry - lively and noisy festivities
  • Antonym: solemnity - formal, serious ceremony or behavior

Etymology

Originally Scots 'high jinks', a drinking game popular in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ haɪ dʒɪŋks / (AmE), / haɪ dʒɪŋks / (BrE)
noisy, playful, and mischievous behaviour, especially at a party or celebration
noisy, playful mischief or revelry

Examples

The children’s high jinks at the party lasted long into the night.
There was plenty of high jinks among the wedding guests after the speeches.
After the exam, the students indulged in some harmless high jinks outside the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shenanigans - mischievous or deceitful activity
  • Synonym: merrymaking - the process of enjoying oneself with others, especially with dancing and drinking

Etymology

From Scots 'high jinks', originally a 17th-century drinking game.
