Brenner Pass

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɛnər pæs / (AmE), / ˈbrɛnə pɑːs / (BrE)
an Alpine mountain pass connecting Innsbruck in Austria with Bolzano in Italy, historically important for trade and invasions
Alpine mountain pass between Austria and Italy

Examples

The Brenner Pass has been a crucial route for centuries, linking northern and southern Europe.
Heavy snowfall sometimes closes the Brenner Pass in winter.
Many travelers cross the Brenner Pass on their way from Austria to Italy.
