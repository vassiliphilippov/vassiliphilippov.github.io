car boot sale

1. NOUN

/ kɑr buːt seɪl / (AmE), / kɑː buːt seɪl / (BrE)
an outdoor market where people sell goods from the trunks of their cars, typically in a parking lot or open space
outdoor sale from car trunks (UK)
british

Examples

We found some vintage records at the car boot sale yesterday.
Every Sunday, there's a large car boot sale in the school parking lot.
He cleared out his attic and sold the unwanted items at a car boot sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garage sale - a sale of used items held at someone's home, typically in the garage or yard, common in North America

Etymology

From British English 'car boot' (trunk of a car) and 'sale'.
