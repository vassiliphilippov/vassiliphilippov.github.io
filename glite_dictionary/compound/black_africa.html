Black Africa

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈæf.rɪ.kə / (AmE), / blak ˈaf.rɪ.kə / (BrE)
the parts of Africa that lie south of the Sahara Desert, typically inhabited by Black African peoples
the region of Africa south of the Sahara Desert

Examples

European colonial powers had a significant impact on Black Africa during the late nineteenth century.
Many international aid organizations focus their efforts on Black Africa because of ongoing development needs.
The music of Black Africa is known for its rich rhythms and diverse traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sub-Saharan Africa - the area of Africa situated south of the Sahara Desert
