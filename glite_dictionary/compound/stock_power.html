stock power

1. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / stɒk ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
a legal document authorizing the transfer of ownership of a registered security from the owner to another party
legal document for transfer of securities
legal

Examples

The investor signed a stock power to transfer the shares to her daughter.
A stock power must be endorsed properly to be valid.
Without a stock power, the broker cannot complete the securities transfer.
