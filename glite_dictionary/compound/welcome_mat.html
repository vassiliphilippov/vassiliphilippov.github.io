welcome mat

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlkəm mæt / (AmE), / ˈwɛlkəm mæt / (BrE)
a mat placed outside a door for people to wipe their shoes on before entering a building
mat for wiping shoes at an entrance

Examples

He brushed the dirt from his boots on the welcome mat before stepping inside.
The new welcome mat outside their door reads 'Home Sweet Home.'
Please leave your shoes on the welcome mat when you come in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doormat - a mat placed in front of a door for wiping shoes

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlkəm mæt / (AmE), / ˈwɛlkəm mæt / (BrE)
A mat placed outside an entrance, often with a welcoming message, to greet visitors or symbolize hospitality.
mat at entrance to greet guests

Examples

You can wipe your shoes on the welcome mat before coming in.
Every visitor was greeted by a cheerful welcome mat at the entrance.
Putting out the welcome mat is a simple way to make guests feel at home.

Synset Examples

Her invitation felt like a welcome mat to new friendships.
The welcome mat at the door was frayed but inviting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doormat - a mat placed in front of a door for wiping feet
