prehistoric culture

1. NOUN

/ priː.hɪˈstɔːr.ɪk ˈkʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / priː.hɪˈstɒr.ɪk ˈkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
the period or society in human history before the development of written records
period before written history

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered tools that offer insights into prehistoric culture across Europe.
Burial sites can reveal much about the beliefs and practices of prehistoric culture.
The exhibition focuses on the art and technology of prehistoric culture before the dawn of writing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ancient civilization - early complex societies with writing systems
  • Antonym: modern culture - contemporary ways of life and society

Etymology

From 'prehistoric', from Latin 'prae' (before) + 'historicus' (historical); 'culture' from Latin 'cultura' (cultivation, care).
