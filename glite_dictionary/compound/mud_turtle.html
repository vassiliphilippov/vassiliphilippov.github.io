mud turtle

1. NOUN

/ mʌd tɝːtəl / (AmE), / mʌd tɜːtəl / (BrE)
A small, bottom-dwelling freshwater turtle that inhabits muddy rivers and streams in North and Central America.
freshwater turtle of muddy rivers

Examples

The children spotted a mud turtle basking on a log in the riverbank.
Due to its camouflage, the mud turtle can be difficult to find in muddy waters.
Scientists are monitoring the mud turtle population to track environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: turtle - A reptile with a bony or leathery shell, especially one living in water
