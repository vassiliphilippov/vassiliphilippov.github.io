amphibicidal treatment

1. NOUN

/ æmˌfɪbɪˈsaɪdəl ˈtrit.mənt / (AmE), / æmˌfɪbɪˈsaɪdəl ˈtriːt.mənt / (BrE)
A chemical treatment or agent intended to kill or control amphibians, such as frogs or salamanders.
chemical agent that kills amphibians

Examples

The use of amphibicidal treatment is controversial due to its impact on local ecosystems.
Regulations strictly control the application of amphibicidal treatments near protected wetlands.
Scientists are studying the long-term effects of amphibicidal treatment on amphibian populations.

Synset Examples

Farmers use amphibicidal treatments to protect crops from frogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbicidal treatment - chemical agent that kills plants or weeds
  • Synonym: insecticidal treatment - chemical agent that kills insects

Etymology

From 'amphibi-' (relating to amphibians) and '-cidal' (from Latin 'cida' meaning killer).
