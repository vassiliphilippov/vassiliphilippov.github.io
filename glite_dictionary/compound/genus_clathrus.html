genus Clathrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈklæθ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈklæθ.rəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Clathraceae, comprising fungi with distinctive lattice-like fruiting bodies.
a genus of fungi (Clathraceae family)

Examples

Species in the genus Clathrus are known for their unusual, net-like fruiting bodies.
The genus Clathrus includes fungi commonly called lattice stinkhorns.
Mycologists discovered a new species within the genus Clathrus in the rainforest.

Etymology

From Latin 'Clathrus' meaning lattice, referring to the fruiting body's structure.
