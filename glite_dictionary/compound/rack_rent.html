rack rent

1. NOUN

/ ræk rɛnt / (AmE), / ræk rɛnt / (BrE)
An excessively high or extortionate rent charged for property, especially land or a house.
extortionate rent

Examples

The tenants could not afford the rack rent imposed by the landlord.
Protests erupted in the village because of increasing rack rent on farmland.
She was forced to leave her home when the rack rent made it impossible to stay.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fair rent - a reasonable or legally regulated rent amount
  • Synonym: extortionate rent - an extremely high and unreasonable rent

Etymology

From 'rack' (to stretch or strain) + 'rent'; originally referred to rents stretched to the highest possible amount.
