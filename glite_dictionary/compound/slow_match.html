slow match

1. NOUN

/ sloʊ mætʃ / (AmE), / sləʊ mætʃ / (BrE)
A cord or fuse treated to burn slowly and steadily, formerly used to ignite firearms or explosives.
cord or fuse that burns slowly

Examples

The soldier lit the cannon using a slow match to safely ignite the powder.
Early musketeers carried a slow match to fire their weapons during battle.
The slow match burned steadily as the artillerymen prepared to fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - a cord or tube for igniting explosives
