vermillion rockfish

1. NOUN

/ vərˈmɪljən ˈrɑːkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / vəˈmɪljən ˈrɒkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a commercially important bright red fish found along the Pacific coast of North America
a Pacific coast red fish, commercial value

Examples

Fishermen along the coast frequently catch vermillion rockfish for the seafood market.
The vibrant red color makes the vermillion rockfish easy to spot among other species.
Grilled vermillion rockfish is a local delicacy in several West Coast restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockfish - a general term for several kinds of marine fishes with firm flesh

Etymology

From vermillion, referring to its bright red color, + rockfish.
