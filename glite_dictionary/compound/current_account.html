current account

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrənt əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənt əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
the part of a country's balance of payments that records exports and imports of goods and services, along with transfer payments
balance of payments record for trade and transfers

Examples

The country's current account showed a significant surplus last year due to strong exports.
A deficit in the current account can indicate that a nation is spending more on foreign trade than it is earning.
Economists analyze the current account to assess a country's international economic position.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capital account - the part of the balance of payments recording capital transfers and the acquisition or disposal of non-produced, non-financial assets

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.ənt əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈkʌr.ənt əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
A bank account from which money can be withdrawn at any time, typically by check; the British equivalent of a checking account.
bank account for everyday transactions
british

Examples

She opened a current account to manage her daily expenses.
Most employers pay salaries directly into employees' current accounts.
You can write checks and use a debit card with a current account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checking account - a bank account from which checks can be drawn; used in American English
