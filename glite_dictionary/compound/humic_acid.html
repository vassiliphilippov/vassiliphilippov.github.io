humic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A dark brown organic compound found in soil, formed by the decomposition of plant and animal material, and soluble in water at pH values above 2.
organic substance in soil, dark brown

Examples

The farmer added humic acid to the fields to improve the soil fertility.
Research shows that humic acid can enhance water retention in sandy soils.
Composted organic matter is a source of humic acid in garden soil.

Synset Examples

the half-life of humic acid is measured in centuries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulvic acid - another type of humic substance, more soluble in water

Etymology

Formed from the Latin 'humus' (earth, ground).
