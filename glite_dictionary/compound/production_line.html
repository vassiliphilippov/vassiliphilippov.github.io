production line

1. NOUN

/ prəˈdʌk.ʃən laɪn / (AmE), / prəˈdʌk.ʃən laɪn / (BrE)
a series of machines and workers in a factory through which a product passes, with each person or machine performing a particular step until the product is finished
factory process where products are assembled in stages

Examples

The new production line has doubled the factory's output this year.
Automated robots were introduced to the production line to improve efficiency.
He works on the production line, assembling car doors all day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly line - a manufacturing process in which parts are added sequentially to create a finished product
