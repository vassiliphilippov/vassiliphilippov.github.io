artisan's lien

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.tə.zənz liːn / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.zənz liːn / (BrE)
a legal right allowing an artisan to retain possession of a finished item until payment for their work has been received
right to keep work until paid (legal)
legal

Examples

The jeweler exercised an artisan's lien to keep the ring until her client paid the full amount.
Under law, a mechanic can claim an artisan's lien on a repaired vehicle pending payment.
Having completed the sculpture, he used his artisan's lien to ensure he was compensated before releasing it.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lien - the right to keep possession of property until a debt is paid

Etymology

From 'artisan' (a skilled manual worker) and 'lien' (from Old French, meaning 'a bond or tie').
