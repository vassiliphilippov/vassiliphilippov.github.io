French lavender

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈlævəndɚ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈlævəndə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant (Lavandula stoechas) with pale purple flowers, often cultivated for its aromatic spike lavender oil.
Mediterranean aromatic plant with purple flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the calming scent of French lavender in full bloom.
She uses oil from French lavender to make her handmade soaps.
A field of French lavender stretched along the hillside, attracting bees and visitors alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - A genus of aromatic plants with purple flowers

2. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈlævəndər / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈlævəndə / (BrE)
A shrubby greyish lavender native to southwestern Europe, usually bearing reddish-purple flowers; often grown as an ornamental plant.
a shrubby lavender plant with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

French lavender is prized for its fragrant, reddish-purple flowers in both gardens and floral arrangements.
The gardener planted a row of French lavender along the walkway to add color and fragrance.
Unlike other species, French lavender thrives in warmer, drier climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - a small aromatic evergreen shrub with narrow leaves and bluish-purple flowers
