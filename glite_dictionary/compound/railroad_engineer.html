railroad engineer

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌrɑd ɛnˈdʒɪnɪr / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌrəʊd ˌɛnʤɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to drive and operate a railway locomotive
operator of a railway locomotive
american

Examples

After years of training, he finally became a railroad engineer and drove freight trains across the country.
The railroad engineer sounded the horn as the train approached the crossing.
As a railroad engineer, she was responsible for the safety of both passengers and crew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train driver - person who operates a railway locomotive (chiefly British English)
