Pacific walrus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk ˈwɔl.rəs / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈwɔːl.rəs / (BrE)
a large marine mammal found in the Bering Sea and northern Pacific, known for its long tusks and whiskers
walrus species of the northern Pacific

Examples

Scientists are studying how climate change affects the habitat of the Pacific walrus.
A group of Pacific walruses was spotted resting on an ice floe near Alaska.
The Pacific walrus is easily recognized by its long tusks and thick blubber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walrus - large Arctic marine mammal with tusks and whiskers
