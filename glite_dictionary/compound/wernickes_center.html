Wernicke's center

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛrnɪkəz ˈsɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪkəz ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
an area in the posterior part of the superior temporal gyrus of the brain, involved in the comprehension of spoken and written language
area of brain for language comprehension
medical

Examples

Damage to the Wernicke's center can result in difficulties understanding spoken language.
Researchers have identified the Wernicke's center as crucial for auditory word recognition.
A stroke affecting the Wernicke's center may lead to a condition known as receptive aphasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Broca's area - area of the brain involved in speech production
  • Synonym: Wernicke's area - region of the brain for language comprehension, typically the left superior temporal gyrus

Etymology

Named after Carl Wernicke, a German neurologist, who first described the area in the 19th century.
