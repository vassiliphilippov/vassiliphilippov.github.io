big yus

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈjuːs / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈjuːs / (BrE)
a letter in the early Cyrillic and Glagolitic alphabets representing a Common Slavonic nasalized back vowel
early Cyrillic/Glagolitic letter for a nasal vowel

Examples

The big yus was used to represent a nasal vowel in Old Church Slavonic texts.
Many early manuscripts feature both big yus and little yus in various words.
Linguists study how the big yus gradually disappeared from Slavic alphabets.

Synset Examples

The names of the letters do not imply capitalization, as both little and big yus exist in majuscule and minuscule variants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little yus - another letter in the early Cyrillic and Glagolitic alphabets for a nasalized vowel

Etymology

From Old Church Slavonic юсъ (jusŭ), name for the nasalized vowel letter.
