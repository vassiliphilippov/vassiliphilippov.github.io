Battle of Brunanburh

1. PROPN

/ ˈbætəl əv ˈbruːnənbɜːr / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈbruːnənbəː / (BrE)
a battle fought in 937 in which the English king Athelstan defeated an alliance of Scots, Vikings, and others, establishing English dominance in Britain
historic 937 battle between English and Scots

Examples

The Battle of Brunanburh is often cited as a key moment in the formation of England.
Historians still debate the exact location of the Battle of Brunanburh.
King Athelstan emerged victorious at the Battle of Brunanburh, changing the future of Britain.
