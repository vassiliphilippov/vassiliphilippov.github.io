Cross-Florida Waterway

1. NOUN

/ krɒs ˌflɔːrɪdə ˈwɔːtərˌweɪ / (AmE), / krɒs ˌflɒrɪdə ˈwɔːtəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a navigable water route across Florida, allowing small boats to travel between the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico via Lake Okeechobee and connecting canals and rivers
navigable water route across Florida

Examples

The Cross-Florida Waterway provides a shortcut for boaters traveling between the Atlantic and the Gulf of Mexico.
Many fishing enthusiasts use the Cross-Florida Waterway to access different coastal regions of Florida.
Repairs to the Cross-Florida Waterway have affected small boat traffic this season.
