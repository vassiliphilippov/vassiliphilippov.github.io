Bloomsbury Group

1. PROPN

/ ˈbluːmzˌbɛri ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈblʊmz.bəri ɡruːp / (BrE)
A group of English writers, thinkers, and artists who lived or worked in the Bloomsbury area of London in the early 20th century, known for their influence on literature and art and their unconventional attitudes.
group of early 20th-century London intellectuals

Examples

The Bloomsbury Group played a significant role in shaping modern British literature and art.
Virginia Woolf was one of the most famous members of the Bloomsbury Group.
The legacy of the Bloomsbury Group continues to influence writers and artists today.

Etymology

Named after the Bloomsbury district in London, where members lived or gathered.
