fossil fool

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑsəl ˌfuːl / (AmE), / ˈfɒsəl ˌfuːl / (BrE)
a person who is outdated or stubbornly resists change, especially by clinging to old ideas or technologies
someone resistant to change; outdated person
informal

Examples

My grandfather can be a real fossil fool when it comes to new technology.
Don't act like a fossil fool; try embracing new ideas instead.
The company risks becoming a fossil fool by ignoring modern trends in the market.

Synset Examples

Don't be a fossil fool; keep up with the latest trends.
Being a fossil fool won't help you in this fast-paced world.
He's a fossil fool if he thinks old tech will work forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick-in-the-mud - a person who is old-fashioned or resists change
  • Degree Lower: fossil fuel - natural fuel formed from the remains of living organisms; wordplay component

Etymology

Wordplay on 'fossil fuel' and 'fool', blending the two to create a term for someone stuck in the past.
