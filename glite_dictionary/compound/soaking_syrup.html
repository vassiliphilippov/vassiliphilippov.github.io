soaking syrup

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.kɪŋ ˈsɪr.əp / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.kɪŋ ˈsɪr.əp / (BrE)
a sweet liquid made from water, sugar, and sometimes other flavorings or alcohol, used to moisten and infuse cakes and pastries
sweet liquid for moistening cakes

Examples

The pastry chef brushed the cake layers with soaking syrup to ensure they stayed moist.
A dash of rum in the soaking syrup added a delightful kick to the dessert.
If your cake seems dry, try adding some soaking syrup before serving.

Synset Examples

A simple trick to keep you cake nice and moist is brushing it with soaking syrup right after you take it out of the oven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simple syrup - a basic syrup of sugar and water used in drinks and desserts
