Abkhazian language

1. NOUN

/ æbˈkɑːziən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / æbˈkɑːziən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Northwest Caucasian language spoken by the Abkhaz people, primarily in Abkhazia and parts of Turkey and Russia
the language of the Abkhaz people

Examples

The Abkhazian language uses a unique Cyrillic-based alphabet.
She is studying the grammar of the Abkhazian language as part of her linguistics research.
Many traditional songs are performed in the Abkhazian language at local festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abkhaz - the language spoken by the Abkhaz people

Etymology

Abkhazian, referring to Abkhazia, a region in the South Caucasus; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
