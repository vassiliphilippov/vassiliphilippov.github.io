Tangier peavine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtænʤər ˈpiˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtænʤɪə ˈpiːvaɪn / (BrE)
A North African annual plant related to the sweet pea, with showy but odorless flowers.
North African plant with showy, scentless flowers

Examples

The Tangier peavine is often grown for its colorful but unscented blossoms.
Gardeners value the Tangier peavine for its resemblance to the sweet pea.
Unlike some related species, the Tangier peavine does not have a strong fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet pea - a climbing plant with fragrant flowers, related to the peavine
