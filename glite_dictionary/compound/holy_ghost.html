Holy Ghost

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ɡəʊst / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the third person of the Trinity; the spiritual presence of God as promised by Jesus to his followers, also known as the Holy Spirit.
third person of the Christian Trinity

Examples

Many Christians believe that the Holy Ghost guides and comforts them in their daily lives.
They prayed for the Holy Ghost to descend upon the congregation during the service.
The doctrine of the Trinity includes God the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Spirit - another name for the Holy Ghost, third person of the Christian Trinity

Etymology

From Old English 'hālig gāst' (holy spirit).
