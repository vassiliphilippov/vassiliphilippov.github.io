common meter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmitər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
A poetic metre consisting of four lines alternating between iambic tetrameter and iambic trimeter, typically used in ballads and hymns.
poetic metre with alternating lines, used in ballads

Examples

Many traditional hymns are written in common meter, making them easy to sing to a variety of tunes.
Emily Dickinson often used common meter in her poetry, giving her verses a familiar rhythm.
Because the song follows common meter, its lyrics fit effortlessly to several well-known melodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballad meter - a similar metrical pattern used in ballads
