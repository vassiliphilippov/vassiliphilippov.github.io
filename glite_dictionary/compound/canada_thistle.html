Canada thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænədə ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈkanədə ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A European origin thistle (Cirsium arvense), now common in North America, known as an invasive weed with spiny leaves and purple flower heads.
invasive weed with spiny leaves and purple flowers

Examples

Canada thistle spreads rapidly and can be difficult to control in gardens and agricultural fields.
The pretty purple blooms of the Canada thistle mask its invasive nature.
Farmers often struggle with Canada thistle choking out their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a plant with prickly leaves and stalks of the genus Cirsium or Carduus

Etymology

Named after its widespread presence in Canada, despite being native to Europe; 'thistle' from Old English 'thistel'.
