Western civilization

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the culture, values, and social institutions characteristic of Western Europe and North America, especially as developed from ancient Greece and Rome through the modern era
culture of Western Europe and North America

Examples

Many philosophers regard the concept of democracy as a cornerstone of Western civilization.
Some believe technological innovation is one of the major achievements of Western civilization.
The influence of Western civilization can be seen in global art, science, and political systems.

Synset Examples

when Ghandi was asked what he thought of Western civilization he said he thought it would be a good idea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Western culture - the shared customs, beliefs, arts, and institutions of Western societies
  • Antonym: Eastern civilization - the cultures and societies of eastern Asia, often contrasted with Western civilization
