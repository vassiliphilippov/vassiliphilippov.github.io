treasurer's cheque

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒərz ˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒərəz ˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
a cheque issued by a bank officer, drawn on the bank's own account rather than a customer's account
cheque drawn by bank itself, not an individual

Examples

The company paid the supplier with a treasurer's cheque for added security.
To buy the car, she requested a treasurer's cheque from her bank.
Most large transactions require a treasurer's cheque rather than a personal cheque.

Synset Examples

cashier's checks are as good as cash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashier's check - a check issued by a bank on its own funds

Etymology

From 'treasurer', meaning the bank officer responsible for funds, and 'cheque' (from Old French 'eschequier', meaning a bill of exchange).
