Mount Logan

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈloʊɡən / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈləʊɡən / (BrE)
the highest mountain in Canada, located in the St. Elias Range of the southwestern Yukon Territory, with an elevation of 5,959 meters (19,551 feet)
highest mountain in Canada, in Yukon

Examples

Climbers from around the world travel to Mount Logan to take on its challenging ascent.
The weather on Mount Logan can change rapidly, making it a difficult peak to climb.
Mount Logan is renowned for its massive icefields and remote location in the Yukon.

Etymology

Named after Sir William Edmond Logan, a Canadian geologist.
