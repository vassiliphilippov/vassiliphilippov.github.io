American watercress

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwɔtərˌkrɛs / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwɔːtəkres / (BrE)
a mat-forming perennial plant found in cold springs in the eastern United States, related to watercress
mat-forming perennial plant of eastern US

Examples

The stream bank was covered with lush American watercress thriving in the cool water.
Researchers studied how American watercress purifies natural spring water in the region.
She added fresh American watercress to her salad for a peppery flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - a fast-growing, aquatic plant used as a salad green

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwɔtərˌkrɛs / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwɔːtəkres / (BrE)
a plant native to southwestern Europe and cultivated in parts of the United States, especially Florida, known for its edible, peppery leaves
a plant with edible, peppery leaves

Examples

The chef garnished the dish with freshly picked American watercress for a spicy finish.
She grows American watercress in her backyard pond to add to salads and sandwiches.
The peppery taste of American watercress makes it a popular choice in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - an aquatic plant with peppery-flavored leaves, often used in salads
