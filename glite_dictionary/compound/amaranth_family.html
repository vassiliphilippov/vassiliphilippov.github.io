amaranth family

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.əˌrænθ ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈam.ərænθ ˈfam.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a large and widespread family of mostly herbaceous plants and shrubs, including amaranths, characterized by clusters of small flowers and often found in temperate and tropical regions
family of plants including amaranths

Examples

The amaranth family includes several important crops and ornamental plants worldwide.
Many species in the amaranth family are valued for their nutrition and resilience in harsh conditions.
The botanist specialized in studying weeds belonging to the amaranth family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amaranthaceae - the scientific name for the amaranth family

Etymology

From ‘amaranth’, the type genus, which comes from Greek ‘amarantos’ (unfading), referencing the long-lasting flowers.
