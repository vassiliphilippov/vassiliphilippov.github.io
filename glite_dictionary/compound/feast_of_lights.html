Feast of Lights

1. NOUN

/ fiːst əv laɪts / (AmE), / fiːst əv laɪts / (BrE)
an eight-day Jewish holiday commemorating the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC, also known as Hanukkah
Jewish festival, another name for Hanukkah

Examples

The family celebrated the Feast of Lights by lighting candles each night.
Many people give gifts to children during the Feast of Lights.
The Feast of Lights is a time for remembering miracles and rededication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanukkah - Jewish festival marking the rededication of the Temple
