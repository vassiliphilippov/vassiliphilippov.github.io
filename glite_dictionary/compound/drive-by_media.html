drive-by media

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪv baɪ ˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / ˈdraɪv baɪ ˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
Media organizations or outlets that provide superficial or rapid coverage of events, often without adequate investigation or fact-checking, potentially spreading misinformation.
media providing superficial or hasty coverage

Examples

Many critics argue that the drive-by media focuses on speed rather than accuracy.
The senator blamed the drive-by media for misrepresenting his remarks in their reports.
People are increasingly skeptical of the drive-by media and its tendency to sensationalize news.

Synset Examples

In today's world, drive-by media often spreads misinformation quickly.
The politician criticized the drive-by media for their biased coverage.
He felt frustrated with the drive-by media's shallow reporting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press - collective term for news media and journalists
  • Synonym: yellow journalism - journalism that is sensationalized and not well-researched

Etymology

Derived from 'drive-by' (suggesting a quick, passing action), modeled after 'drive-by shooting'; popularized in political commentary in the late 20th century.
