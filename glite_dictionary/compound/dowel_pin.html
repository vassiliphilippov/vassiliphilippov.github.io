dowel pin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊəl pɪn / (AmE), / ˈdaʊəl pɪn / (BrE)
a cylindrical fastener inserted into holes in two adjacent pieces to hold them together securely
cylindrical fastener for joining pieces

Examples

The two wooden boards were held firmly together by a dowel pin.
He used a steel dowel pin to align the machine parts precisely.
When assembling the furniture, insert a dowel pin into each pre-drilled hole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowel - a wooden or metal rod used for joining pieces together
  • Synonym: peg - a small cylindrical object used to fasten things together

Etymology

From 'dowel' (Middle English, of uncertain origin) and 'pin' (Old English 'pinn')
