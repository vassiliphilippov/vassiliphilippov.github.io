radio-frequency spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊ ˈfrikwənsi ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊ ˈfriːkwənsi ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the range of electromagnetic frequencies used for wireless communication, including radio, radar, and television transmissions
range of electromagnetic frequencies for wireless communication

Examples

The government regulates the radio-frequency spectrum to prevent interference between different broadcasters.
Mobile phones rely on access to the radio-frequency spectrum to connect users wirelessly.
Allocating the radio-frequency spectrum efficiently is crucial for new technologies like 5G and satellite communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio spectrum - portion of electromagnetic spectrum allocated to radio communication
