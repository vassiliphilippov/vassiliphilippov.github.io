Soledad pine

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊləˈdæd paɪn / (AmE), / ˌsəʊlɪˈdad paɪn / (BrE)
A medium-sized pine tree of southwestern California with five-needled bundles and long cylindrical cones.
pine tree native to southwestern California

Examples

The Soledad pine is known for thriving in the dry hills of southwestern California.
We saw a rare Soledad pine along the ridge during our hike.
The long cones of the Soledad pine make it easy to distinguish from other pines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - a type of evergreen tree with needle-shaped leaves

Etymology

Named after Soledad, a region in California, USA.
