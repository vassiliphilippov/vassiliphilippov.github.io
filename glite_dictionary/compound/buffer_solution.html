buffer solution

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfɚ səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
A solution that resists changes in its pH when small amounts of acid or base are added, typically containing a weak acid and its conjugate base or a weak base and its conjugate acid.
solution that resists pH change
medical

Examples

A buffer solution helps maintain a stable pH in biological experiments.
She prepared a buffer solution to prevent drastic changes in acidity during the reaction.
The effectiveness of a buffer solution depends on the concentration of its components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffer - a substance that resists changes in pH when acid or base is added
