yellow-eyed grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ aɪd ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ aɪd ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of several grass-like plants, especially found in the pine barrens of the southern United States, known for their yellow, eye-shaped flower centers
rushlike plant with yellow center flowers

Examples

The sandy soils of the region are home to rare yellow-eyed grass species.
Botanists identified several clusters of yellow-eyed grass near the wetlands.
The bright yellow centers of yellow-eyed grass make it easy to recognize in the wild.
