Shahaptian language

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈhæpʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʃəˈhæptiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a branch of the Penutian language family comprising languages traditionally spoken by the Shahaptian peoples of the Pacific Northwest, chiefly in present-day Oregon, Washington, and Idaho.
a group of indigenous Penutian languages

Examples

The Shahaptian language family includes several dialects spoken across the Pacific Northwest.
Few native speakers of the Shahaptian language remain today, making preservation efforts crucial.
Researchers have published a new dictionary of the Shahaptian language for academic and cultural use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nez Percé - an indigenous language of the Shahaptian branch
  • Degree Higher: Penutian language - a family of indigenous North American languages including Shahaptian

Etymology

From the name 'Shahaptian,' relating to the indigenous peoples of the Pacific Northwest, plus 'language.'
