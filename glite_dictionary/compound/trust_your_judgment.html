to trust your judgment

1. VERB

/ trʌst jʊr ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / trʌst jɔː ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
to rely on someone's ability to make good decisions or form sound opinions
rely on someone's decision-making

Examples

I always trust your judgment when it comes to important decisions.
You should trust your judgment instead of second-guessing yourself all the time.
Even when we disagree, I still trust your judgment in most matters.

Synset Examples

I trust your judgment completely; you're the doctor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doubt your judgment - feel uncertain about someone's ability to make good decisions
