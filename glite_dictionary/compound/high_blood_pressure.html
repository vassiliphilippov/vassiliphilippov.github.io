high blood pressure

1. NOUN

/ haɪ blʌd ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / haɪ blʌd ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
a condition in which the force of the blood against artery walls is consistently higher than normal, often defined as a reading of 140/90 mm Hg or more
abnormally high blood pressure; hypertension
medical

Examples

Regular exercise can help prevent high blood pressure and improve overall heart health.
People with high blood pressure should monitor their diet and reduce sodium intake.
Untreated high blood pressure increases the risk of heart attack and stroke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypertension - abnormally high blood pressure

2. NOUN

/ haɪ blʌd ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / haɪ blʌd ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is consistently too high, increasing health risks.
condition of elevated blood pressure
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with high blood pressure after several routine checkups.
Stress and poor diet can contribute to high blood pressure.
Medication may be required to control high blood pressure in some patients.

Synset Examples
