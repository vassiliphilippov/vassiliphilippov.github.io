tail fin

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪl fɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪl fɪn / (BrE)
a decorative projection above the rear fenders of an automobile, especially popular in mid-20th century car designs
decorative projection on car's rear fender

Examples

The classic Cadillac from the 1950s had dramatically large tail fins that made it instantly recognizable.
Restoring the car to its original condition required repairing the damaged tail fin on the left side.
Many collectors admire the bold design of vehicles with prominent tail fins.

2. NOUN

/ teɪl fɪn / (AmE), / teɪl fɪn / (BrE)
the flattened fin at the rear end of a fish or aquatic animal, used for propulsion and steering
rear fin of a fish or aquatic animal

Examples

The shark's powerful tail fin helps it swim swiftly through the water.
Colorful fish often have a beautiful tail fin that fans out behind them.
If a fish injures its tail fin, it may have trouble swimming properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caudal fin - the anatomical term for the tail fin of a fish

3. NOUN

/ ˈteɪl ˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪl ˌfɪn / (BrE)
the vertical stabilizing surface at the tail of an aircraft, which helps to maintain stability and control during flight
vertical stabilizing surface on an aircraft

Examples

The plane's tail fin was damaged during the turbulent landing.
Modern jetliners often have a large tail fin to improve directional stability.
Inspectors checked the tail fin for signs of wear before the next flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertical stabilizer - the vertical surface on the tail of an aircraft that provides stability
