International System of Units

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnt̬ɚˈnæʃənəl ˈsɪstəm əv ˈjuːnɪts / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl ˈsɪstəm əv ˈjuːnɪts / (BrE)
the modern, globally adopted system of measurement based on seven fundamental units, including meter, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin, mole, and candela
the metric system of scientific measurement

Examples

The International System of Units defines the meter, kilogram, and second as standard units of measurement.
Many scientific journals require data to be presented using the International System of Units.
Switching to the International System of Units makes global collaboration much easier.

Synset Examples

Today the United States is the only country in the world not totally committed to the Systeme International d'Unites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SI - abbreviation for International System of Units
  • Synonym: metric system - decimal-based system of measurement units

Etymology

From French 'Système International d’Unités', formalized by international agreement in 1960.
