to inflame tensions

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfleɪm ˈtɛnʃənz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm ˈtɛnʃənz / (BrE)
to make a situation more hostile or likely to result in conflict
make conflict or hostility worse

Examples

Political leaders were careful not to inflame tensions during the protests.
Irresponsible media coverage can inflame tensions between different communities.
The manager warned that such remarks would only inflame tensions in the already divided team.

Synset Examples

His words could inflame tensions among the group.
Don't inflame the situation with harsh comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exacerbate - to make a problem or situation worse
  • Antonym: calm - to make a situation less tense or hostile
