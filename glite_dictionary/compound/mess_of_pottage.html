mess of pottage

1. NOUN

/ mɛs əv ˈpɑtɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɛs əv ˈpɒtɪdʒ / (BrE)
something of little or trivial value, especially when accepted at the expense of something more valuable or important
something of trivial value (often accepted at great cost)

Examples

He traded his promising career for a mess of pottage.
Don’t give up your principles for a mess of pottage.
She realized too late that she had exchanged happiness for a mess of pottage.

Synset Examples

Esau sold his birthright to Jacob for a mess of pottage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifle - something of little value or importance
  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy, but worthless thing

Etymology

From the Biblical story of Esau, who sold his birthright to Jacob for a meal of lentil stew ('pottage').
