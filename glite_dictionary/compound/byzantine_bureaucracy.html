Byzantine bureaucracy

1. NOUN

/ bəˈzaɪntiːn bjuːˌrɑːkrəˌsi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzəntiːn bjʊəˈrɒkrəsi / (BrE)
an extremely complex and intricate administrative or governmental system, often characterized by excessive paperwork and inefficiency
overly complicated administrative system

Examples

Dealing with the Byzantine bureaucracy of the university delayed her graduation paperwork by several months.
Foreign businesses are often discouraged by the Byzantine bureaucracy they encounter when setting up operations.
He spent weeks trying to unravel the Byzantine bureaucracy involved in securing a building permit.

Synset Examples

Navigating the Byzantine bureaucracy took longer than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red tape - excessive bureaucracy or adherence to official rules and formalities
  • Degree Lower: bureaucracy - a system of government or management with many complicated rules and ways of doing things

Etymology

Refers to the bureaucratic system of the Byzantine Empire, noted for its complexity and rigidity; 'Byzantine' now often signifies anything highly complicated or intricate.
