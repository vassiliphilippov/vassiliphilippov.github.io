Old English poetry

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / əʊld ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
poetry composed in the Old English language, typically dating from the 7th to the 11th centuries, including works such as Beowulf and The Seafarer.
poetry in the Old English language

Examples

She specialized in Old English poetry during her graduate studies in medieval literature.
Many students find Old English poetry challenging due to its unique language and style.
The course will explore themes and techniques found in Old English poetry.

Synset Examples

His fascination with Old English poetry began in college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Beowulf - an epic poem and one of the most famous works of Old English poetry

Etymology

Old English refers to the earliest historical form of the English language, used from about the 5th to the 12th century.
