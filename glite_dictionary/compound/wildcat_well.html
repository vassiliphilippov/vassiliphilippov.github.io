wildcat well

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪldˌkæt wɛl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldkæt wɛl / (BrE)
An exploratory oil well drilled in an area not previously known to contain oil.
exploratory oil well in unknown area

Examples

A wildcat well was drilled in hopes of discovering a new oil field in West Texas.
The company's risky investment paid off when their latest wildcat well struck oil.
Drilling a wildcat well requires extensive geological surveys and significant financial risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploratory well - an oil well drilled for exploration purposes

Etymology

From 'wildcat' (unpredictable, speculative venture) and 'well' (a drilled hole to extract fluids).
