Margaret Thatcher

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrɡərət ˈθætʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːɡərət ˈθætʃə / (BrE)
(1925–2013) British stateswoman; the first woman to serve as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, from 1979 to 1990.
British Prime Minister (1979–1990)

Examples

Margaret Thatcher became the first woman to hold the office of British Prime Minister in 1979.
Many credit Margaret Thatcher with transforming the British economy during her time in government.
The legacy of Margaret Thatcher continues to influence British politics today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime minister - the head of government in the United Kingdom
