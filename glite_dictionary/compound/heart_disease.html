heart disease

1. NOUN

/ hɑrt dɪˈziz / (AmE), / hɑːt dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
Any condition that affects the normal functioning of the heart, including various disorders such as coronary artery disease, arrhythmias, and heart failure.
disease affecting the heart
medical

Examples

Smoking significantly increases the risk of developing heart disease.
Doctors are working on new treatments to prevent heart disease.
A healthy diet and regular exercise can help lower your chance of heart disease.
