camphorated phenol

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.fəˌreɪ.tɪd ˈfiː.nɒl / (AmE), / ˈkæm.fər.eɪ.tɪd ˈfiː.nɒl / (BrE)
A solution containing phenol, camphor, and liquid petrolatum, used as a local anesthetic and for the relief of toothache.
phenol-camphor solution for toothache relief
medical

Examples

The dentist applied camphorated phenol to the cavity to ease the patient's pain.
You should never ingest camphorated phenol as it can be toxic if swallowed.
Camphorated phenol is commonly used in dentistry as a local anesthetic for toothaches.

Synset Examples

Don't swallow camphorated phenol — ingestion of two teaspoonfuls can result in adverse neurological effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenol - an aromatic organic compound often used as an antiseptic
