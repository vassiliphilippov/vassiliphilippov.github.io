field day

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
A day set aside for military exercises, maneuvers, and displays, often for training or demonstration purposes.
day for military exercises and displays

Examples

The army organized a field day to showcase their new equipment and tactics.
During the annual field day, soldiers participated in a series of challenging drills.
Spectators attended the military field day to watch demonstrations of precision and discipline.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld deɪ / (AmE), / ˈfiːld deɪ / (BrE)
A day organized for outdoor activities, often including games, competitions, or social gatherings, typically held by a school or community group.
day for outdoor social event or games

Examples

The school held its annual field day with races and games for the students.
We all packed a picnic and enjoyed a sunny field day in the park.
Every summer, the neighborhood association organizes a field day full of activities for families.

Etymology

Originally a military term from the 18th century for a day spent in field exercises; later adopted for school or community activity days.
