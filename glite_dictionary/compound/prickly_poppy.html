prickly poppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
An annual plant of the poppy family, native to the Old World, with orange-red flowers and spiny fruits.
spiny-flowered poppy plant

Examples

The prickly poppy has distinctive orange-red flowers and sharp spines on its fruit.
Gardeners often avoid touching the prickly poppy because of its bristly seed pods.
In the wild, the prickly poppy grows along dry roadsides and fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poppy - a flowering plant of the genus Papaver, known for its showy flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Argemone, characterized by large white or yellow flowers and prickly leaves, stems, and seed pods, mainly found in tropical America.
plant of the genus Argemone with prickly features

Examples

The desert landscape was dotted with prickly poppy, their yellow flowers bright under the sun.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of prickly poppy extracts.
She carefully avoided the prickly poppy plants while hiking, not wanting to touch their spiny stems.
