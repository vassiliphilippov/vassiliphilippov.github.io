lily-of-the-valley tree

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪli əv ðə ˈvæli tri / (AmE), / ˌlɪli əv ðə ˈvæli triː / (BrE)
a broad-leaved evergreen shrub native to Asia, with glossy leaves and drooping clusters of fragrant white flowers
Asian evergreen shrub with white flowers

Examples

The lily-of-the-valley tree produces beautiful white blooms each spring.
Gardeners often plant the lily-of-the-valley tree for its year-round glossy foliage.
The lily-of-the-valley tree thrives in partial shade and acidic soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pieris japonica - the botanical name for the lily-of-the-valley tree
  • Degree Lower: lily of the valley - a different plant with similar-looking flowers
