clinical anatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪnɪkəl əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
The branch of anatomy concerned with the practical application of anatomical knowledge to diagnosis and treatment of diseases.
applied anatomical study in medicine
medical

Examples

Medical students are required to study clinical anatomy to better understand patient care.
A strong foundation in clinical anatomy is essential for aspiring surgeons.
The lecture highlighted the importance of clinical anatomy in diagnosing abdominal pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the study of the structure of living things

Etymology

From 'clinical', relating to medical practice, and 'anatomy', from Greek anatomē (dissection).
