fly orchid

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ ˈɔrkɪd / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈɔːkɪd / (BrE)
a small European orchid with flowers that resemble flies, typically with slender stalks and hairy petals
a European orchid with insect-like flowers

Examples

The fly orchid gets its name from its flowers that mimic the appearance of flies to attract pollinators.
We spotted a rare fly orchid growing along the woodland trail in early summer.
Botanists are fascinated by how the fly orchid tricks insects into helping with pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee orchid - a European orchid whose flowers resemble bees

2. NOUN

/ flaɪ ˈɔrkɪd / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈɔːkɪd / (BrE)
A European orchid (Ophrys insectifera) whose brown and yellowish flowers resemble flies.
European orchid with fly-like flowers

Examples

The fly orchid attracts insects by mimicking the appearance and scent of a female fly.
We spotted several fly orchids growing wild in the meadow during our hike.
Botanists are fascinated by the unusual pollination strategy of the fly orchid.
