dirty word

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːti wɝːd / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti wɜːd / (BrE)
a word that is regarded as offensive, taboo, or inappropriate to mention or use
offensive or taboo word

Examples

He was scolded by his parents for using a dirty word at the dinner table.
To some politicians, "tax" has become a dirty word.
Teachers always remind students that a dirty word is not acceptable in class.

Synset Examples

‘failure’ is a dirty word to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscenity - an offensive or indecent word or phrase
  • Synonym: expletive - an offensive or swear word

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːti wɝːd / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti wɜːd / (BrE)
an offensive or indecent word or expression, especially one considered rude or obscene
offensive or indecent word or phrase

Examples

Please don't use a dirty word in front of the children.
He was reprimanded at work for accidentally saying a dirty word during the meeting.
Some people consider 'damn' a dirty word, while others use it casually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse word - offensive or obscene word used for emphasis or swearing
  • Synonym: profanity - words or expressions considered disrespectful, rude, or obscene
  • Antonym: euphemism - a mild or indirect word substituted for one considered too harsh or blunt
