electrical power

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛk.trɪ.kəl ˈpaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trɪ.kəl ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the rate at which electrical energy is transferred by an electric circuit, calculated as the product of voltage and current
rate of energy transfer in an electric circuit

Examples

The generator supplies electrical power to the entire building during a blackout.
Measuring electrical power is crucial in designing safe electrical systems.
Increasing the voltage will increase the electrical power delivered to the device.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - rate at which work is done or energy is transferred
