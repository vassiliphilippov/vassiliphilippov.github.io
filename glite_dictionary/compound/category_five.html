category five

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˌɡɔri ˈfaɪv / (AmE), / ˌkatɪˌɡɒri ˈfaɪv / (BrE)
the highest classification on the hurricane scale, indicating a hurricane with sustained winds of 157 mph (252 km/h) or higher, signifying extreme intensity and catastrophic potential
most severe hurricane classification

Examples

The storm was upgraded to a category five hurricane overnight, alarming residents across the region.
Experts warn that a category five hurricane can cause catastrophic damage to anything in its path.
After the devastation caused by the category five storm, rebuilding efforts took years.

Synset Examples

The hurricane reached category five, causing widespread destruction.
This storm is classified as a category five disaster.
They evacuated the coast ahead of the category five storm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: category four - the hurricane scale rating just below category five
