Franciscan order

1. NOUN

/ frænˈsɪskən ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / frænˈsɪskən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic religious order founded by Saint Francis of Assisi in the 13th century, dedicated to principles of poverty, humility, and charity
Roman Catholic order founded by St. Francis

Examples

The Franciscan order played a significant role in the spread of Christianity during the Middle Ages.
She dedicated her life to service after joining the Franciscan order.
Members of the Franciscan order are known for their vows of poverty and devotion to the poor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dominican order - a Roman Catholic order founded by Saint Dominic in the 13th century
  • Synonym: Benedictine - member of a Roman Catholic monastic order following the Rule of St. Benedict

Etymology

From the name of Saint Francis of Assisi, the order's founder.
