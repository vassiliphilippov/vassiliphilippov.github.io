New Greek

1. NOUN

/ nju ˈɡrik / (AmE), / njuː ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the Greek language as it is currently spoken and written, especially as distinguished from Ancient Greek and Medieval Greek
modern Greek language

Examples

Many young people in Greece speak New Greek as their first language.
She is fluent in both New Greek and its ancient counterpart.
Linguists study New Greek to understand how the language has evolved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Modern Greek - the modern form of the Greek language
  • Antonym: Ancient Greek - the Greek language used from ancient times to about the 6th century AD
