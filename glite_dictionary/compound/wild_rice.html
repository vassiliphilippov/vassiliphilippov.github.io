wild rice

1. NOUN

/ waɪld raɪs / (AmE), / waɪld raɪs / (BrE)
the grains of certain aquatic grasses native to North America, cooked and eaten as a food similar to rice
grain from North American aquatic grasses

Examples

Many people enjoy wild rice as a healthy alternative to white rice.
The chef prepared a delicious salad with roasted vegetables and wild rice.
Harvesting wild rice is an important tradition for some Native American communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rice - the starchy seeds of a cereal grass used as food

2. NOUN

/ waɪld raɪs / (AmE), / waɪld raɪs / (BrE)
a grain obtained from certain North American aquatic grasses (genus Zizania), used as food, or the plant itself
North American aquatic grain plant

Examples

Native Americans have harvested wild rice from lakes and rivers for centuries.
The chef prepared a delicious salad with organic wild rice and seasonal vegetables.
Wild rice grows naturally in shallow waters across parts of the northern United States and Canada.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rice - common cereal grain, staple food
