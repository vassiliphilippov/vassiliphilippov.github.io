coonskin cap

1. NOUN

/ ˈkunˌskɪn kæp / (AmE), / ˈkuːnskɪn kap / (BrE)
A hat made from the skin of a raccoon, typically with the tail hanging down the back.
hat made from raccoon skin with tail

Examples

He wore a coonskin cap just like the pioneers did.
The museum displayed an authentic coonskin cap from the early 1800s.
Children dressed up as Davy Crockett with a coonskin cap and a toy rifle.

Etymology

From 'coon', colloquial form of 'raccoon', and 'skin', meaning pelt, plus 'cap'.
