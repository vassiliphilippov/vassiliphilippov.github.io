fall armyworm

1. NOUN

/ fɔl ˈɑːrmiˌwɜrm / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɑːmiˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the larval stage of a migratory American moth (Spodoptera frugiperda), known for destroying grasses and cereal crops
larva of a destructive American moth

Examples

Farmers are worried about an invasion of fall armyworm in their corn fields this year.
If not controlled quickly, the fall armyworm can destroy entire crops of maize and sorghum.
Researchers are developing new methods to prevent fall armyworm outbreaks in affected regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armyworm - larva of various species of moths that move in groups and can devastate crops
