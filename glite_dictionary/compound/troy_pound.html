troy pound

1. NOUN

/ trɔɪ paʊnd / (AmE), / trɔɪ paʊnd / (BrE)
a unit of weight used for precious metals, equal to 12 troy ounces or approximately 373.24 grams
apothecary weight, 12 troy ounces

Examples

Gold is typically weighed in troy pounds and ounces, rather than the standard avoirdupois system.
The old coin collection was listed as exceeding one troy pound in total weight.
A troy pound is lighter than an avoirdupois pound, which is commonly used for most other goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troy ounce - a unit of weight (1⁄12 of a troy pound) for precious metals

Etymology

From the city of Troyes in France, where the system was first used.
