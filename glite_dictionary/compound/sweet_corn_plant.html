sweet corn plant

1. NOUN

/ swit kɔrn plænt / (AmE), / swiːt kɔːn plɑːnt / (BrE)
a variety of corn grown for its sweet, edible kernels, harvested when young and tender
corn plant grown for sweet, edible kernels

Examples

The sweet corn plant thrives in warm, well-drained soil.
Farmers harvested several rows of sweet corn plants early in the summer.
You can identify a sweet corn plant by its tender, sugary kernels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet corn - corn with sweet, edible kernels, harvested while immature
  • Antonym: field corn - variety of corn grown primarily for livestock feed or industrial use, not for fresh eating
