American agave

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən əˈɡɑːveɪ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən əˈɡɑːveɪ / (BrE)
a large, succulent plant native to the Americas, known for its tall flowering stalk, greenish-white flowers, and the tendency to bloom once after many years before dying
large, long-lived succulent flowering plant

Examples

The American agave blooms just once in its lifetime, after which the plant dies.
Gardeners often admire the towering stalk of the American agave when it finally flowers.
The American agave is a striking addition to xeriscaped gardens due to its dramatic foliage and rare flower show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agave - succulent plant genus with spiny leaves and tall flower stalks

Etymology

From American (referring to its native region) + Spanish 'agave', from Latin 'Agave', from Greek 'Agauē', mythological name.
