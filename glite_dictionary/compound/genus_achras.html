genus Achras

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈækrəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈækrəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees with papery leaves and large fruit, including the sapodilla.
genus of tropical fruit trees

Examples

The genus Achras is known for producing the sapodilla fruit enjoyed in many tropical regions.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Achras in Central America.
Leaves from trees in the genus Achras are often papery and distinctively shaped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapodilla - A tropical fruit tree of the genus Achras

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Achras', the genus name given in scientific Latin.
