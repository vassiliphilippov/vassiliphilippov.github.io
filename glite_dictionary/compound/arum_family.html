arum family

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrəm ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈɑːrəm ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants (Araceae) including anthurium, calla lily, jack-in-the-pulpit, and philodendron, typically with distinctive spadix and spathe flowers.
family of flowering plants (Araceae)

Examples

Many common houseplants, such as the peace lily, belong to the arum family.
The arum family includes plants with unusual, often striking flowers and foliage.
Florists often use members of the arum family for their dramatic appearance in arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Araceae - the botanical family name for arum family
  • Antonym: lily family - a different family of plants, Liliaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Arum, from Latin arum, a type of lily, from Greek aron.
