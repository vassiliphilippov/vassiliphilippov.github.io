low-sudsing

1. ADJ

/ ˌloʊˈsʌd.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌləʊˈsʌd.zɪŋ / (BrE)
(of detergents) producing few suds or bubbles when used with water.
producing few suds (detergent)

Examples

This low-sudsing detergent is ideal for high-efficiency washing machines.
If you have soft water, a low-sudsing formula helps prevent excess bubbles.
Most liquid laundry soaps today are low-sudsing to avoid residue buildup.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-sudsing - (of detergents) producing a lot of suds or bubbles
