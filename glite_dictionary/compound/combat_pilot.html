combat pilot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmˌbæt ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbæt ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
A military aviator trained to engage in aerial combat missions against enemy forces.
military aviator who fights in aerial combat

Examples

The combat pilot completed his final mission before returning to base.
She trained for years to become a combat pilot in the Air Force.
During the operation, each combat pilot followed strict protocols to ensure mission success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fighter pilot - a pilot specifically trained to fly military fighter aircraft
