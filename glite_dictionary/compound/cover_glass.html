cover glass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvɚ ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a thin, flat piece of glass used to cover a specimen on a microscope slide for examination under a microscope
thin glass for covering microscope specimens

Examples

The technician carefully placed a cover glass over the sample before viewing it under the microscope.
Accidentally dropping the cover glass can damage both the slide and the specimen.
Make sure the cover glass lies flat to prevent air bubbles from forming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coverslip - a small, thin piece of glass used to cover specimens on microscope slides; synonym for 'cover glass'
