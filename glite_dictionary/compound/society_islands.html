Society Islands

1. PROPN

/ səˈsaɪəti ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / səˈsaɪəti ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
an island group in French Polynesia in the South Pacific, east of Samoa, including Tahiti and Bora Bora
group of islands in French Polynesia

Examples

The Society Islands are renowned for their breathtaking scenery and vibrant coral reefs.
Tahiti, the largest of the Society Islands, attracts tourists from all over the world.
Paul Gauguin found inspiration for his paintings while living in the Society Islands.

Etymology

Named by Captain James Cook in 1769 in honour of the Royal Society.
