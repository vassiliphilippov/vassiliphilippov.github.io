biological terrorism

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈtɛrəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈtɛrərɪzəm / (BrE)
Terrorist acts involving the deliberate use of biological agents, such as bacteria or viruses, as weapons to cause harm or fear.
use of biological agents in terrorism

Examples

The government increased security measures to prevent biological terrorism at major public events.
Experts warn that biological terrorism poses a significant threat to public health and safety.
After the anthrax attacks, awareness of biological terrorism grew among policymakers and the general public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bioterrorism - terrorism involving biological weapons
