genus Natrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈneɪtrɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈneɪtrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of water snakes found widely across the world, commonly called grass snakes or water snakes.
genus of water snakes

Examples

Species in the genus Natrix are commonly found near rivers and lakes.
Researchers studied several specimens belonging to the genus Natrix in Eastern Europe.
Unlike vipers, snakes in the genus Natrix are non-venomous and pose little threat to humans.

Etymology

From Latin natrix (water snake).
