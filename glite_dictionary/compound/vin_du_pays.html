vin du pays

1. NOUN

/ vɛ̃ dy pɛ / (AmE), / vɛ̃ dy pɛ / (BrE)
A wine produced in a specific region of France, typically of lesser prestige than those classified as 'appellation contrôlée'.
French regional table wine

Examples

The restaurant serves a delightful vin du pays selection that pairs well with rustic dishes.
She opted for a glass of vin du pays instead of a more expensive wine.
Many tourists enjoy sampling the local vin du pays during their stay in the French countryside.

Synset Examples

The restaurant serves a delightful vin du pays selection.
He prefers vin du pays over expensive imports.
We enjoyed a bottle of vin du pays with dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table wine - wine of ordinary quality, intended for everyday drinking
  • Degree Higher: appellation contrôlée - a wine of higher French classification based on strict regional laws

Etymology

From French 'vin du pays', meaning 'wine of the country/region'.
