external orifice

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɝːnəl ˈɔrɪfɪs / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈɔːrɪfɪs / (BrE)
the opening through which urine is discharged from the body; usually refers to the urethral opening
opening for urine discharge
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the external orifice for signs of infection.
A blockage at the external orifice can cause urinary retention.
Proper hygiene around the external orifice is important to prevent urinary tract infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urethral meatus - the opening at the end of the urethra

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outside) and 'orificium' (opening).
