hand-to-mouth

1. ADJ

/ ˌhænd tə ˈmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˌhænd tə ˈmaʊθ / (BrE)
having only enough money or resources to meet immediate or basic needs, with no savings or security
living with just enough for immediate needs

Examples

After losing his job, he was forced to live a hand-to-mouth existence for several months.
Many artists live hand-to-mouth until their work gains recognition.
She escaped her hand-to-mouth upbringing by securing a well-paying job.

Synset Examples

They lived hand-to-mouth, struggling to make ends meet.
The hand-to-mouth lifestyle left them with no savings.
She was tired of living hand-to-mouth every month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverished - lacking sufficient financial means; poor
  • Degree Higher: destitute - without basic necessities of life

Etymology

From the literal action of taking food directly from one's hand to one's mouth, signifying having just enough to survive.
