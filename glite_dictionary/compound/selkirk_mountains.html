Selkirk Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛl.kɜrk ˈmaʊn.tənz / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.kɜːk ˈmaʊn.tɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in southeastern British Columbia, Canada, forming part of the Columbia Mountains and lying west of the Rocky Mountains
mountain range in southeastern British Columbia, Canada

Examples

The Selkirk Mountains are famous for their dramatic peaks and deep valleys.
Heavy snowfall is common throughout the Selkirk Mountains in winter.
Many hikers travel to the Selkirk Mountains each summer for the stunning scenery.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Douglas, 5th Earl of Selkirk (1771–1820), a Scottish philanthropist.
