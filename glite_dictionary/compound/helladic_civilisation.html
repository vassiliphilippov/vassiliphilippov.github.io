Helladic civilisation

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈlædɪk ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / hɛˈlædɪk ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The Bronze Age culture of mainland Greece, flourishing from around 2500 to 1100 BC.
Bronze Age culture of mainland Greece

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable artifacts from the Helladic civilisation at several sites in mainland Greece.
The Helladic civilisation is known for its characteristic pottery and monumental architecture.
Scholars often compare the Helladic civilisation with the Minoan and Cycladic cultures of the Bronze Age Aegean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minoan civilisation - Bronze Age culture of Crete, parallel to the Helladic period
  • Synonym: Cycladic civilisation - Early Bronze Age culture of the Cycladic islands, related to Helladic

Etymology

From Greek Hellas (Ἑλλάς), the ancient name for Greece, with 'Helladic' used to denote the Greek mainland period.
