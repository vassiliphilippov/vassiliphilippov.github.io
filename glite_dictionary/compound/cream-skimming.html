cream-skimming

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːm ˌskɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkriːm ˌskɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of selectively taking only the best or most profitable resources, opportunities, or people, leaving the rest behind
selective taking of the best resources

Examples

The new regulations are intended to prevent banks from cream-skimming only the wealthiest clients.
Critics say that cream-skimming by private companies can undermine the quality of public services.
Many insurance providers have been accused of cream-skimming by targeting only low-risk policyholders.

Synset Examples

He accused the company of cream-skimming the best resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry-picking - selecting only the best or most desirable from a group

Etymology

From the metaphor of removing the cream (the best part) from milk; skimming meaning to remove from the surface.
