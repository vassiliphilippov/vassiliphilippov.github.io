dirty bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːti bɑm / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti bɒm / (BrE)
A bomb designed to disperse radioactive material upon explosion, causing contamination rather than a large nuclear blast.
bomb that spreads radioactive contamination

Examples

Authorities feared that terrorists might try to use a dirty bomb to contaminate a city center.
News reports confirmed that a dirty bomb had never been successfully detonated in a major urban area.
After the threats were made, the city increased its surveillance for materials used in building a dirty bomb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiological weapon - a weapon intended to spread radioactive material
  • Antonym: nuclear bomb - a bomb that releases energy through nuclear reactions
