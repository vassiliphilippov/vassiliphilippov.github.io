Anglo-Saxon language

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡloʊ ˈsæksən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡləʊ ˈsæksən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The English language as it was spoken and written in England before approximately 1100; also known as Old English.
Old English language (before 1100)

Examples

Many early English texts, such as Beowulf, were written in Anglo-Saxon language.
The Anglo-Saxon language has a grammar and vocabulary quite different from modern English.
Scholars study the Anglo-Saxon language to better understand England’s medieval past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old English - the English language in its oldest historical form (c. 450–1150)

Etymology

From Old English, spoken in England after the Anglo-Saxon settlement (5th–11th centuries); 'Anglo-' from Latin Angli (the Angles), 'Saxon' from Latin Saxones (the Saxons).
