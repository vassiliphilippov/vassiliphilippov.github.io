black tongue

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈtʌŋ / (AmE), / blæk ˈtʌŋ / (BrE)
a temporary medical condition in which the tongue appears dark or black due to overgrowth of papillae, often as a side effect of certain antibiotics or other causes
medical condition: dark discoloration of the tongue
medical

Examples

The patient developed black tongue after several days of antibiotic therapy.
A black tongue can sometimes be alarming, but it is usually harmless and temporary.
Doctors reassured him that his black tongue would disappear without treatment.
