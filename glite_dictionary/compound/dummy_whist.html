dummy whist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmi wɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi wɪst / (BrE)
A variation of the card game whist for three players, where four hands are dealt and the hand opposite the dealer is placed face up as a 'dummy.'
three-player variant of whist card game

Examples

We decided to play dummy whist since only three of us were available for cards.
In dummy whist, the dealer's partner's hand lies face up for everyone to see.
She explained the rules of dummy whist before we started our game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whist - a classic English trick-taking card game for four players in partnerships
