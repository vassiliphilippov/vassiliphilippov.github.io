antimonial lead

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈmoʊ.ni.əl lɛd / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈməʊ.ni.əl lɛd / (BrE)
a lead alloy containing about 5% antimony, used for its hardness and corrosion resistance
lead alloy with antimony

Examples

Many battery grids are made from antimonial lead to enhance durability.
The use of antimonial lead is common in the manufacture of cable sheathing.
Due to its improved strength, antimonial lead is ideal for some industrial applications.

Etymology

Derived from 'antimony' and 'lead', describing the alloy's composition.
