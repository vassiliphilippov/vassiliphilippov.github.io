harlequin snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrləˌkwɪn ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːlɪkwɪn ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a brightly colored, venomous snake of the New World, typically banded in red, black, and yellow or white, found in Central and South America
venomous, brightly banded New World snake

Examples

The harlequin snake is easily identified by its bold, colorful bands and timid nature.
Some species of the harlequin snake are highly venomous and should not be handled without caution.
During our trip to the Amazon, we spotted a harlequin snake slithering through the underbrush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - a venomous, brightly colored snake often confused with the harlequin snake

Etymology

From 'harlequin', referring to the snake's resemblance to the colorful costume of the traditional comic character.
