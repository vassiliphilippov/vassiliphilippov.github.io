umbrella pine

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbrɛl.ə paɪn / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛl.ə paɪn / (BrE)
a medium-sized pine tree of southern Europe with a broad, spreading crown, cultivated for its edible seeds that resemble almonds
European pine tree with broad crown and edible seeds

Examples

The umbrella pine is a striking feature of Mediterranean landscapes, easily recognized by its flat-topped crown.
Edible seeds from the umbrella pine are valued in many culinary traditions.
Several umbrella pines have been planted in the city park for their ornamental beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone pine - another name for the umbrella pine, especially known for its edible seeds
