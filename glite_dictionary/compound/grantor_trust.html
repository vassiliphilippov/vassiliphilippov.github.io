grantor trust

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn.tɔɹ tɹʌst / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.tɔː tɹʌst / (BrE)
A trust in which the person who creates the trust, the grantor, retains certain powers or benefits, causing the trust's income to be taxed to the grantor.
trust in which grantor retains tax liability
legal

Examples

A grantor trust allows the creator to retain certain powers, resulting in the trust’s income being taxable to them.
Experts recommend reviewing the tax implications before establishing a grantor trust.
Transferring assets into a grantor trust may provide flexibility in estate planning.

Etymology

From 'grantor', meaning a person who creates a trust, and 'trust', from Old Norse 'traust' (confidence, protection).
