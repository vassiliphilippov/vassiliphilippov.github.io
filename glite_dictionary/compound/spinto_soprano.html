spinto soprano

1. NOUN

/ spiːn.toʊ səˈprɑː.noʊ / (AmE), / ˈspiːntəʊ səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
An operatic soprano voice that combines the clear, high notes of a lyric soprano with the ability to sing with dramatic intensity without strain.
type of operatic soprano voice

Examples

The company cast a spinto soprano for the challenging role in the opera.
Her voice developed into a true spinto soprano, allowing her to sing both lyric and dramatic parts.
As a spinto soprano, she excels in roles that demand both lyrical beauty and dramatic power.

Synset Examples

The more dramatic sister of the lyric soprano is the spinto soprano, not quite a full-on dramatic soprano, but possessing a combination of qualities of both the lyric and the dramatic sopranos.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lyric soprano - a light operatic soprano voice with a bright tone
  • Degree Higher: dramatic soprano - a powerful operatic soprano voice for more dramatic roles

Etymology

From Italian 'spinto' (pushed) + 'soprano' (soprano).
