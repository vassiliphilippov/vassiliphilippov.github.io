Medal of Honor

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛd.əl əv ˈɑː.nɚ / (AmE), / ˌmed.əl əv ˈɒn.ə / (BrE)
the highest United States military decoration, awarded for personal acts of valor above and beyond the call of duty
highest U.S. military award for valor
american

Examples

He received the Medal of Honor for his extraordinary bravery in battle.
Only a handful of soldiers have ever been awarded the Medal of Honor.
The President presented the Medal of Honor during a solemn ceremony at the White House.
