soylent green

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪlənt ˈɡriːn / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪlənt ˈɡriːn / (BrE)
a fictional food product made from people, originally from the 1973 film 'Soylent Green'; often used allusively to refer to food of mysterious or dubious origin
fictional food from a dystopian film

Examples

Few people realize that the name soylent green comes from a famous science fiction movie.
When the cafeteria served an unidentifiable casserole, someone joked, 'Is this soylent green?'
The phrase soylent green is often used to express distrust of processed food.

Synset Examples

They joked about making a soylent green smoothie for dinner.
The phrase 'soylent green' has become a pop culture reference.
In the film, the shocking twist revealed that soylent green was made from humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soylent - branded meal replacement drink inspired by 'Soylent Green'

Etymology

From the title of the 1973 science fiction film 'Soylent Green'.
