electric cord

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk kɔrd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk kɔːd / (BrE)
a flexible, insulated wire used to carry electricity to an appliance or device
flexible insulated wire for electricity

Examples

The television stopped working because its electric cord was damaged.
Please make sure the electric cord is not plugged in before cleaning the appliance.
A frayed electric cord can be a serious safety hazard in any home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power cord - a cable supplying electrical power to a device
