isogonic line

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsɑɡənɪk laɪn / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒɡənɪk laɪn / (BrE)
An imaginary line on a map connecting points with equal magnetic declination at a given time.
line connecting points of equal magnetic declination

Examples

The navigator charted the ship's course using an isogonic line to correct for magnetic variation.
Maps often display isogonic lines to help pilots determine true directions.
As you move across an isogonic line, the magnetic declination remains unchanged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isogon - a line connecting points of equal magnetic declination

Etymology

From iso- (equal) + Greek gōnia (angle) + line.
