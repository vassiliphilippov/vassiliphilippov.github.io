pharyngeal recess

1. NOUN

/ fəˈrɪndʒiəl rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / fəˈrɪndʒiəl rɪˈsɛs / (BrE)
a small cavity or depression located in the wall of the pharynx, specifically near the opening of the Eustachian tube
small cavity in the pharynx wall
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's pharyngeal recess for signs of infection.
Tumors may sometimes develop in the pharyngeal recess, making early detection essential.
A deep pharyngeal recess can occasionally collect debris, causing discomfort.

Etymology

From Greek 'pharynx' (throat) and Latin 'recessus' (a going back, recess).
