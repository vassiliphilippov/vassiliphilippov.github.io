piked whale

1. NOUN

/ paɪkt weɪl / (AmE), / paɪkt weɪl / (BrE)
A small species of finback whale found in coastal waters of the Atlantic and Pacific, also known as the minke whale.
a small finback whale; minke whale

Examples

The piked whale is frequently observed in the coastal waters during the summer migration.
Unlike larger whales, the piked whale is known for being agile and elusive.
Scientists have been studying the feeding habits of the piked whale along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minke whale - common name for Balaenoptera acutorostrata, a small baleen whale
