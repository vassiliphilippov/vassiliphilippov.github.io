isochoric process

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈkɔrɪk ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈkɔːrɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a thermodynamic process in which the volume of the system remains constant
thermodynamic process at constant volume

Examples

In an isochoric process, the volume of the gas does not change while heat is added or removed.
The pressure inside the vessel increased during the isochoric process.
Cooling a gas at constant volume is an example of an isochoric process.

Synset Examples

In practice, a gas that receives heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume heating and at the same time it is a constant volume pressurization; a gas that looses heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume cooling and at the same time it is a constant volume depressurization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isobaric process - a thermodynamic process at constant pressure
  • Antonym: isothermal process - a thermodynamic process at constant temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'chōra' (space, place), plus 'process'.
