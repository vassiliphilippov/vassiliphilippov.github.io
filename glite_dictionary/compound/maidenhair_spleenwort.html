maidenhair spleenwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr ˈspliːnwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdənheə ˈspliːnwɜːt / (BrE)
a small fern found in northern temperate regions and Hawaii, with pinnate fronds, often growing on rocks
a small rock-inhabiting fern

Examples

The shady stone walls in the garden were covered with maidenhair spleenwort.
Hikers in Hawaii often spot maidenhair spleenwort growing among the rocks along the trails.
Botanists collected samples of maidenhair spleenwort to study its unique fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a type of non-flowering plant with feathery leaves

Etymology

From 'maidenhair' (referring to a delicate appearance, from similar ferns) and 'spleenwort', from Old English 'splegnwyrt', named for its supposed medicinal use for spleen ailments.
