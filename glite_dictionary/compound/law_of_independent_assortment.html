law of independent assortment

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˌɪndəˈpɛndənt əˈsɔrtmənt / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt əˈsɔːtmənt / (BrE)
the genetic principle stating that each pair of homologous chromosomes separates independently of other pairs during the formation of gametes, resulting in random combinations of traits
genetic principle about random trait inheritance

Examples

The law of independent assortment helps explain why siblings with the same parents can have different traits.
Gregor Mendel first formulated the law of independent assortment through his experiments with pea plants.
Biology students often study the law of independent assortment when learning about genetics and inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mendel's second law - another term for the law of independent assortment

Etymology

Principle described by Gregor Mendel based on his experiments with pea plants in the 19th century.
