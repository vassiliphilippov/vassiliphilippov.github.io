Brazilian potato tree

1. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪljən pəˈteɪtoʊ triː / (AmE), / brəˈzɪljən pəˈteɪtəʊ triː / (BrE)
a South American shrub or small tree, often cultivated in tropical regions, that is not a true potato
South American shrub; not a true potato

Examples

The Brazilian potato tree is popular among gardeners in tropical climates for its vibrant purple flowers.
Although it's called the Brazilian potato tree, it is not related to the common potato plant.
The Brazilian potato tree thrives in well-drained soil and warm temperatures.
