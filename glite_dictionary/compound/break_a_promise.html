to break a promise

1. VERB

/ breɪk ə ˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / breɪk ə ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
to fail to do what you have promised to do
fail to keep a promise

Examples

He swore he would never break a promise to his children.
Politicians often face criticism when they break a promise made during their campaigns.
If you break a promise, it can be hard to regain someone's trust.

Synset Examples

Breaking a promise can damage your reputation.
He didn't want to break a promise to his friend.
She feared she would break her promise to help.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to keep a promise - to do what you said you would do
