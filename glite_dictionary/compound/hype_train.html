hype train

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪp treɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪp treɪn / (BrE)
A surge of collective excitement, promotion, or enthusiastic support for an event, product, or trend, often fueled by media and public anticipation.
collective enthusiasm for a trend or event
informal

Examples

Everyone hopped on the hype train for the latest smartphone release.
As soon as the teaser dropped, the hype train for the series went full speed ahead.
She avoided the hype train, preferring to judge the game after its actual launch.

Synset Examples

She jumped on the hype train for the movie release.
Don't miss the hype train for the upcoming festival.
The new game has everyone on the hype train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandwagon - an activity or cause that is currently fashionable or popular and attracting increasing support

Etymology

Blend of 'hype' (extravagant or intensive publicity) and 'train' (a connected series).
