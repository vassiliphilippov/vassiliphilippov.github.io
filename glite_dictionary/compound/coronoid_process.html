coronoid process

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəˌnɔɪd ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənɔɪd ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a sharp, triangular projection from a bone, notably found in the mandible and the ulna in humans and other animals
sharp projection from a bone (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the fracture involved the coronoid process of the ulna.
On the X-ray, the coronoid process of the mandible was clearly visible.
Damage to the coronoid process can impact jaw movement significantly.

Etymology

From Greek korōnē (hook, crow) + -oid (form, resembling) + process (projection).
