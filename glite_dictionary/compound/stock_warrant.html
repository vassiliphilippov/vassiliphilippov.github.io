stock warrant

1. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈwɔɹənt / (AmE), / stɒk ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
a written certificate that gives the holder the right to buy shares of a company's stock at a specific price before a set date
certificate granting right to buy stock at set price

Examples

She invested in a stock warrant that allowed her to buy shares at a lower price if the market rose.
The company issued a stock warrant as an incentive for its employees.
Investors often use a stock warrant to speculate on the future price of a company's shares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock option - contract granting the right to buy or sell stock at a specific price

2. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈwɔɹənt / (AmE), / stɒk ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
a security issued by a company that gives the holder the right to buy shares of the company's stock at a specified price before a set expiration date
right to buy company stock at set price

Examples

The company issued stock warrants to attract more investors to their bond offering.
He exercised his stock warrant to purchase 100 shares at the agreed price.
Stock analysts often discuss the potential value of a stock warrant in a company's capital structure.

Synset Examples

as a sweetener they offered warrants along with the fixed-income securities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: option - right to buy or sell a security at a set price
  • Degree Lower: bond - fixed-income investment representing a loan made by an investor to a borrower
  • Degree Higher: share - a unit of ownership in a company or financial asset
