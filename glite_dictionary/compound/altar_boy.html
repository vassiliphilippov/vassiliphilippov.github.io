altar boy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔltɚ bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːltə bɔɪ / (BrE)
a boy who assists a priest during services, especially in the Roman Catholic Church, by performing various ceremonial duties at the altar.
boy assisting in church services

Examples

The altar boy prepared the candles before Mass began.
As a child, he served as an altar boy every Sunday.
The priest praised the altar boy for his dedication to the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acolyte - a person assisting in religious services, especially in liturgical churches
