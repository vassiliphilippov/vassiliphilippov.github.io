genus Mikania

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs mɪˈkaɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs mɪˈkaɪ.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A large genus of evergreen climbing plants (lianas) native to tropical America, belonging to the family Asteraceae.
genus of tropical American evergreen climbing plants

Examples

Several species in the genus Mikania are known for their rapid growth and ability to smother other plants.
The genus Mikania includes the notorious invasive weed known as mile-a-minute vine.
Botanists are still discovering new members of the genus Mikania in South American rainforests.

Etymology

Named after the Czech botanist Johann Christian Mikan (1769–1844).
