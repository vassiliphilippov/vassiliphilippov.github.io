house of detention

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaʊs əv dɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaʊs əv dɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
an institution where people, especially juvenile offenders, are held in custody, usually under court supervision, for a temporary period
institution for temporary detention, often of juveniles

Examples

The young offender was sent to a house of detention while awaiting his court hearing.
Local officials debated whether to build a new house of detention to address overcrowding.
After his arrest, he was placed in a house of detention for two weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detention center - facility where people are held in custody, especially juveniles
