water polo

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌpoʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌpəʊləʊ / (BrE)
A team sport played in a swimming pool, where two teams compete to throw a ball into the opponents' goal.
team sport played in a swimming pool

Examples

The university has a very competitive water polo team that travels nationwide for tournaments.
She started playing water polo in high school and quickly fell in love with the sport.
Watching the Olympic water polo finals has become a family tradition for us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polo - a team sport played on horseback in which the objective is to score by hitting a small ball into the opposing team's goal

Etymology

From 'water' and 'polo', adapting the horseback game 'polo' to a swimming pool.
