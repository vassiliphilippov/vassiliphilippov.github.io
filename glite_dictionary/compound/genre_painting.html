genre painting

1. NOUN

/ ˈʒɑnrə ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʒɒnrə ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
A style of painting that depicts scenes of ordinary people engaged in everyday activities.
painting style showing everyday life

Examples

Dutch artists excelled at genre painting in the seventeenth century.
The exhibition featured several fine examples of genre painting from the Victorian era.
Unlike historical works, genre painting focuses on scenes of everyday domestic life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: portrait painting - Painting focused on representing a person or group of people
  • Degree Higher: history painting - Painting of historical, religious, or mythological subjects

Etymology

From French 'genre' (kind, category) and English 'painting'.
