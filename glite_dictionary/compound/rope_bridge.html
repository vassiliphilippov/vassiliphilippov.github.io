rope bridge

1. NOUN

/ roʊp brɪdʒ / (AmE), / rəʊp brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a bridge made with ropes supporting its walkway, typically suspended and used to cross valleys or rivers
bridge made from ropes

Examples

The hikers crossed the river by walking over a narrow rope bridge.
In remote villages, a rope bridge is often the only way to reach the other side of the gorge.
The old rope bridge swayed dangerously in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspension bridge - a type of bridge in which the deck is hung below suspension cables
