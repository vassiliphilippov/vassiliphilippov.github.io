muscat grape

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kət ˌɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kæt ˌɡreɪp / (BrE)
a sweet, aromatic grape variety used for making wine and raisins
sweet, aromatic grape for wine and raisins

Examples

The vineyard is famous for its delicious muscat grapes, which are harvested in late summer.
She prefers desserts made with muscat grapes because of their floral aroma.
Many sweet wines are crafted from the muscat grape due to its high sugar content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscatel - sweet wine made from muscat grapes

Etymology

From French 'muscat', from Italian 'moscato', from Late Latin 'muscatus' ('perfumed with musk'), referring to the grape's aroma.
