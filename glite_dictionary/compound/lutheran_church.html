Lutheran Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːθərən tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈluːθərən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a Protestant Christian denomination based on the teachings of Martin Luther
Protestant denomination based on Luther's teachings

Examples

She grew up attending a Lutheran Church in her hometown.
The Lutheran Church celebrates Reformation Day every October.
Many communities in Minnesota have a strong Lutheran Church presence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Protestantism - the faith, practice, and church order of the Protestant churches
  • Antonym: Roman Catholic Church - the Christian church headed by the Pope in Rome

Etymology

Named after Martin Luther (1483–1546), German theologian and religious reformer.
