metronome marking

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrəˌnoʊm ˈmɑrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrənəʊm ˈmɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
an indication of the tempo of a piece of music, given as the number of beats per minute
tempo indication in beats per minute

Examples

The conductor adjusted the metronome marking to ensure a brisker pace for the orchestra.
Always check the metronome marking at the top of the sheet music before you start playing.
The composer specified a new metronome marking to match the energetic style of the piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempo - the speed at which a passage of music is or should be played
