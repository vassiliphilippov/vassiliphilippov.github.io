floating dock

1. NOUN

/ ˈfloʊtɪŋ dɑk / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtɪŋ dɒk / (BrE)
A large floating structure that can be submerged to allow a vessel to enter and then raised to lift the vessel out of the water for repairs or maintenance.
submersible structure for lifting vessels

Examples

The damaged ship was moved into a floating dock for urgent repairs.
Engineers inspected the floating dock before submerging it to take in a new vessel.
A floating dock allows shipyards to work on vessels without permanent dock facilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry dock - enclosed basin for repairing ships, drained of water
