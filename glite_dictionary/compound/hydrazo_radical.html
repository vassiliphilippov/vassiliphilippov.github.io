hydrazo radical

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdræzoʊ ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdrɑːzəʊ ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a bivalent chemical group (-HNNH-) derived from hydrazine, commonly occurring as part of organic compounds
bivalent chemical group from hydrazine
medical

Examples

In organic chemistry, the hydrazo radical acts as a key intermediate during certain synthesis reactions.
The properties of the hydrazo radical are crucial in the formation of some azo compounds.
Researchers studied how the hydrazo radical reacts under different laboratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrazine - a colorless, fuming, corrosive liquid used in chemical synthesis

Etymology

From 'hydrazo', itself related to 'hydrazine' (chemical origin), and 'radical' (a group of atoms behaving as a unit).
