hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.pɚˌtroʊ.fɪk ˌkɑr.di.oʊ.maɪˈɑ.pə.θi / (AmE), / haɪ.pəˌtrɒ.fɪk ˌkɑː.di.əʊ.maɪˈɒ.pə.θi / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the heart muscle becomes abnormally thick, making it harder for the heart to pump blood effectively
heart disorder with thickened muscle
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy after noticing unusual thickening of his heart muscle.
Athletes are sometimes screened for hypertrophic cardiomyopathy because it can cause sudden cardiac events.
Her family history put her at higher risk for developing hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cardiomyopathy - any disease of the heart muscle

Etymology

From Greek: 'hyper' (over), 'trophē' (nourishment), 'kardia' (heart), and 'myopathy' (muscle disease).
