terry towel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛri ˌtaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛri ˌtaʊəl / (BrE)
A towel made from terry cloth, featuring a rough, looped pile that is highly absorbent, commonly used for bathing.
absorbent towel with looped pile

Examples

She wrapped herself in a soft terry towel after her shower.
All the hotel bathrooms are stocked with thick terry towels for guests.
I prefer using a terry towel because it dries me off quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bath towel - a large towel used for drying the body after bathing

Etymology

From 'terry', a type of cloth derived from French 'tiré' (drawn, pulled), indicating the looped fabric; 'towel' from Old English 'twāl'.
