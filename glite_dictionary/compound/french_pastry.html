French pastry

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈpeɪstri / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈpeɪstri / (BrE)
a sweet, often filled pastry, especially one made with puff pastry, originating from or typical of French baking
sweet pastry typical of French baking

Examples

The bakery's display was filled with delicate French pastry creations, tempting every customer who walked in.
She learned how to make a traditional French pastry during her culinary course in Paris.
My favorite dessert is a flaky French pastry filled with rich chocolate cream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Danish pastry - type of sweet, flaky pastry, often filled, originating from Denmark

2. NOUN

/ ˌfrɛntʃ ˈpeɪstri / (AmE), / ˌfrɛntʃ ˈpeɪstri / (BrE)
A type of sweet baked good or dessert that originated in France, known for its delicate texture, rich flavor, and elaborate preparation.
sweet baked good from France

Examples

She ordered a French pastry and a cup of coffee for breakfast.
The bakery is famous for its wide selection of French pastries, such as éclairs and croissants.
Learning to make a traditional French pastry requires patience and attention to detail.

Synset Examples
