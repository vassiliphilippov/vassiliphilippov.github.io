rock hopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌhɑpər / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌhɒpə / (BrE)
a small species of penguin found on the Falkland Islands and New Zealand, known for its distinctive crest and jumping movement over rocks
a small crested penguin of southern islands

Examples

We spotted a rock hopper making its way up the steep cliffs.
The rock hopper stands out with its bright yellow crest and red eyes.
Researchers studied the habits of the rock hopper in the Falklands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - a flightless seabird of the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

Named for its habit of hopping over rocks; from 'rock' + 'hopper'.
