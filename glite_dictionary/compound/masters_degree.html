master's degree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɚz dɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stəz dɪˌɡriː / (BrE)
An academic degree awarded by a university or college after completion of a course of study beyond a bachelor's degree and below a doctorate.
academic degree above a bachelor's

Examples

After completing her master's degree, she decided to pursue a PhD.
A master's degree can help you advance your career in many fields.
He earned his master's degree in engineering from a prestigious university.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bachelor's degree - an undergraduate academic degree awarded by colleges and universities
  • Degree Higher: doctorate - the highest academic degree awarded by a university

Etymology

From 'master', indicating a person who has attained proficiency, and 'degree' (from Latin 'gradus', meaning step or stage).
