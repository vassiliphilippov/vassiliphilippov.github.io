approach trench

1. NOUN

/ əˈproʊtʃ trɛntʃ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃ trɛntʃ / (BrE)
A trench built to provide protected passage between the rear and front lines of a defensive position, especially in military fortifications.
military trench linking rear and front lines

Examples

The soldiers advanced through the approach trench to reach the front lines safely.
Engineers spent days digging a new approach trench during the siege.
A well-constructed approach trench reduced casualties from enemy fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communication trench - a trench used to move between positions safely in war
