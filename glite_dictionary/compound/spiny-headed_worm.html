spiny-headed worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni ˈhɛdɪd wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈhɛdɪd wɜːm / (BrE)
a parasitic worm living in the intestines of vertebrates, characterized by a retractable proboscis covered with hooked spines
parasitic worm with a spined proboscis
medical

Examples

The veterinarian discovered a spiny-headed worm infection in the dog's intestines.
Certain species of fish can be susceptible to spiny-headed worm parasites.
The retractable proboscis is a distinctive feature of every spiny-headed worm.
