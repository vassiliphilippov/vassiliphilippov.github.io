pneumatic hammer

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmætɪk ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / njuːˈmætɪk ˈhamə / (BrE)
A heavy tool powered by compressed air, used for breaking up hard materials such as concrete or rock.
tool powered by compressed air

Examples

The construction workers used a pneumatic hammer to break up the old road surface.
Operating a pneumatic hammer requires wearing ear protection due to the loud noise it generates.
A pneumatic hammer can quickly demolish concrete walls in demolition projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackhammer - a portable pneumatic hammer used for breaking up concrete, pavement, etc.

Etymology

From Greek pneuma (air, breath) via French pneumatique, and hammer (tool for striking).
