common hop

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑmən ˈhɑp / (AmE), / ˌkɒmən ˈhɒp / (BrE)
A European climbing plant (Humulus lupulus) whose flowers are used primarily to flavor beer and other malt liquors; also cultivated in America.
climbing plant used in brewing

Examples

The common hop is essential for brewing beer, giving it its characteristic bitterness and aroma.
Farmers in the region grow acres of common hop for use by local breweries.
Brewers select the finest common hop flowers to ensure the best flavor in their ales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - flowers of the Humulus lupulus plant used in brewing
