New Look

1. NOUN

/ nuː lʊk / (AmE), / njuː lʊk / (BrE)
A style of women's clothing introduced by Christian Dior in 1947, featuring a tight bodice, narrow waist, and full, pleated skirt.
Dior's 1947 women's fashion style

Examples

The fashion world was transformed overnight by Dior's New Look in 1947.
Her dress, inspired by the New Look, had a cinched waist and a voluminous skirt.
Many designers tried to imitate the iconic New Look throughout the late 1940s.

Etymology

From the phrase 'new look', coined by the press to describe Christian Dior's influential 1947 fashion collection.
