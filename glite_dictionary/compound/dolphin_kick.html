dolphin kick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlfɪn kɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɒlfɪn kɪk / (BrE)
a swimming technique in which both legs kick up and down together in a fluid, wave-like motion, resembling the movement of a dolphin's tail
swimming kick with both legs moving together

Examples

The swimmer used a powerful dolphin kick to accelerate off the wall.
Perfecting your dolphin kick is essential for success in the butterfly stroke.
He practiced his dolphin kick to improve his speed underwater.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flutter kick - a swimming kick where the legs alternate up and down

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the motion to the way dolphins use their tails to swim.
