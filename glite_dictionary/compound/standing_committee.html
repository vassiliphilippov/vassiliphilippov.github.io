standing committee

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɪŋ kəˈmɪti / (AmE), / ˈstændɪŋ kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
A permanent committee, especially within a legislative body, that continues in existence from one session to another.
permanent committee in a legislature or organization

Examples

The standing committee on finance meets every month to review the budget.
A new issue was referred to the standing committee for further investigation.
Membership on the standing committee rotates every two years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ad hoc committee - a committee formed for a specific, temporary purpose

Etymology

Formed from 'standing' (remaining in force) + 'committee'.
