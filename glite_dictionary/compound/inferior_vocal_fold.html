inferior vocal fold

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˈvoʊkəl foʊld / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˈvəʊkəl fəʊld / (BrE)
either of the two lower bands of tissue in the larynx that come together to form the glottis and produce vocal sounds when air passes between them
lower band of tissue in the larynx; produces vocal sounds
medical

Examples

Damage to one inferior vocal fold can result in a hoarse or weak voice.
The surgeon carefully avoided the inferior vocal folds during the procedure.
Vibration of the inferior vocal fold is essential for producing clear speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocal cord - one of the folds of tissue in the larynx involved in sound production
  • Antonym: superior vocal fold - the upper band of tissue in the larynx, not directly involved in sound production

Etymology

From Latin 'inferior' meaning 'lower', and 'vocal', from Latin 'vox' meaning 'voice', plus 'fold', meaning 'a band or layer'.
