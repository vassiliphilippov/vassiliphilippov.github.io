American flagfish

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈflæɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈflæɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of the eastern United States, especially Florida, with a dark-blue back and whitish sides marked with red stripes (Jordanella floridae)
a North American freshwater fish with red and blue stripes

Examples

The American flagfish is popular with aquarium hobbyists because of its distinctive coloration.
We saw several American flagfish swimming in the shallow waters of a Florida stream.
Scientists study the American flagfish to understand its unique reproductive habits.
