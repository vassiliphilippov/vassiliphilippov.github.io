skunk cabbage

1. NOUN

/ skʌŋk ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / skʌŋk ˈkæbɪdʒ / (BrE)
A low-growing, foul-smelling perennial plant of swampy areas in eastern North America, with minute flowers inside a hood-like spathe.
foul-smelling swamp plant of eastern North America

Examples

The skunk cabbage releases a strong odor to attract pollinators in early spring.
We found skunk cabbage growing along the edge of the marsh.
Despite its smell, the leaves of skunk cabbage are an important food for some animals.

Etymology

Named for its skunk-like odor and cabbage-like leaves.

2. NOUN

/ skʌŋk ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / skʌŋk ˈkæbɪdʒ / (BrE)
A clump-forming perennial plant native to swampy areas of western North America, with a distinctive yellow spathe and strong odor, similar to Symplocarpus foetidus.
malodorous swamp plant of North America

Examples

Hikers spotted a patch of skunk cabbage growing beside the wetland trail.
The pungent smell of skunk cabbage is unmistakable in early spring.
In botany class, we learned that skunk cabbage emerges even before the snow melts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Symplocarpus foetidus - eastern North American plant related to skunk cabbage

Etymology

Named for its pungent, skunk-like odor and the cabbage-like appearance of its leaves.
