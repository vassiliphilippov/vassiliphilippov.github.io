squirreltail grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːl.teɪl ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈskwɪəl.teɪl ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of barley with highly ornamental flower heads and long, silky awns, native to North America and northeastern Asia.
ornamental barley with silky awns

Examples

The squirreltail grass in the meadow shimmered in the breeze, its silky awns catching the sunlight.
Gardeners often plant squirreltail grass for its attractive, feathery flower heads.
Native to North America, squirreltail grass is valued both ornamentally and ecologically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a major cereal grain, member of the grass family
