mountain grape

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ɡreɪp / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, with dark green, pinnate leaves, yellow flower clusters, and blue-black berries
evergreen shrub of Pacific North America

Examples

The mountain grape is often planted in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
Birds are attracted to the blue-black berries of the mountain grape.
You can find mountain grape growing wild along the Pacific coast.
