flood control

1. NOUN

/ flʌd kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / flʌd kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
The methods or techniques used to prevent or reduce the occurrence of floods, often by managing rivers and using dams and barriers.
techniques for preventing or reducing floods

Examples

The city invested heavily in flood control measures after the last major storm.
Building dams is a common method of flood control along major rivers.
Experts debated the effectiveness of current flood control systems during the public forum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood prevention - actions or systems to stop floods from occurring
  • Degree Higher: flood - an overflow of water onto normally dry land
