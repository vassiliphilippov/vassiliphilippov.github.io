Old Frisian

1. NOUN

/ oʊld frɪˈziːən / (AmE), / əʊld frɪˈziːən / (BrE)
the West Germanic language spoken in Friesland until the 16th century, ancestral to modern Frisian
historical West Germanic language of ancient Frisia

Examples

Many legal documents from medieval Friesland were written in Old Frisian.
Scholars compare Old Frisian texts to better understand the history of West Germanic languages.
Some modern Frisian words are directly descended from their Old Frisian ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Frisian - the modern West Germanic language group spoken in Friesland and nearby regions

Etymology

Old refers to its status as an early form; Frisian from the people of Frisia, itself from Latin Frisii.
