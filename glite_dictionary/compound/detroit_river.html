Detroit River

1. PROPN

/ dɪˈtrɔɪt ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / dɪˈtrɔɪt ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a short river on the border between the United States and Canada, flowing from Lake St. Clair to Lake Erie
river between Lake St. Clair and Lake Erie, US/Canada border

Examples

Cargo ships travel along the Detroit River every day, carrying goods between the Great Lakes.
The Detroit River forms part of the international boundary between Michigan and Ontario.
Many species of fish can be found in the waters of the Detroit River.

Etymology

Named after the city of Detroit, from French 'détroit' (strait).
