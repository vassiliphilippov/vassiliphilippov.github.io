bladder senna

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædər ˈsɛnə / (AmE), / ˈblædə ˈsɛnə / (BrE)
A yellow-flowered European shrub, cultivated for its bright yellow flowers and large, inflated pods, and also used as a source of food for wildlife.
European shrub with yellow flowers and inflated pods

Examples

The gardener planted bladder senna along the fence to add bright yellow color to the garden in summer.
Many birds are attracted to the seeds of bladder senna during autumn.
The distinctive pods of bladder senna make it easy to identify among other shrubs.

Etymology

From 'bladder' referring to the plant's inflated seed pods and 'senna' from Arabic 'sanā', denoting certain leguminous plants.
