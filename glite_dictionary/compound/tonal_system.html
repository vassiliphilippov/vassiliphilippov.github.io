tonal system

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.nəl ˌsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.nəl ˌsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the set of pitch patterns or tones used in a particular language or dialect to distinguish meaning between words or syllables
system of tones in a language

Examples

Mandarin Chinese uses a complex tonal system to distinguish the meanings of words.
Language learners often find the tonal system challenging when studying Vietnamese.
The tonal system in Yoruba consists of three level tones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone language - a language in which pitch is used to distinguish word meaning

Etymology

From 'tonal' (relating to tones) + 'system' (organized set).
