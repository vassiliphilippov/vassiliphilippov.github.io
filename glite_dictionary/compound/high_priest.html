high priest

1. NOUN

/ haɪ prist / (AmE), / haɪ priːst / (BrE)
a senior clergyman or dignitary, especially one holding the highest religious office in certain faiths or rituals.
senior religious leader; chief priest

Examples

He was appointed as the high priest of the temple at a young age.
The high priest led the ceremony with dignity and authority.
In many cultures, the high priest held significant political and spiritual power.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: priest - a person authorized to perform sacred rituals and mediate between humans and the divine
  • Synonym: archbishop - the chief bishop responsible for an ecclesiastical province

2. NOUN

/ haɪ prist / (AmE), / haɪ priːst / (BrE)
a person who is the leading authority or most prominent advocate of a particular movement, trend, or doctrine, especially in a non-religious context
leading authority or proponent in a field

Examples

Miles Davis was often called the high priest of modern jazz.
She is considered the high priest of avant-garde fashion.
As the high priest of minimalism, his influence extended throughout the art world.

Synset Examples

he's the high priest of contemporary jazz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
  • Antonym: follower - a person who supports and admires a particular person or set of ideas

Etymology

Figurative use based on the Biblical and Jewish high priest, a chief religious leader.
