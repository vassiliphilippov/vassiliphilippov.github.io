sorbic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrbɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɔːbɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline carboxylic acid, widely used as a food preservative to inhibit the growth of mold, yeast, and fungi
a food preservative chemical

Examples

Many processed foods contain sorbic acid to extend their shelf life.
The bakery uses sorbic acid as a preservative in its bread products.
Scientists recommend monitoring the levels of sorbic acid in packaged food.

Etymology

From New Latin sorbus (rowan tree), because it was first isolated from unripe berries of the mountain ash (Sorbus aucuparia).
