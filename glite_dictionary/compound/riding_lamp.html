riding lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdɪŋ læmp / (AmE), / ˈraɪdɪŋ læmp / (BrE)
a lamp hung in the rigging of a ship when it is anchored, used to signal its position at night
lamp used on anchored ships

Examples

The crew hung a riding lamp to ensure the anchored ship was visible through the fog.
Every vessel at anchor is required to display a riding lamp overnight for safety.
The old riding lamp swayed gently in the breeze, casting light across the deck.

Etymology

From 'ride' (to be at anchor) + 'lamp'.
