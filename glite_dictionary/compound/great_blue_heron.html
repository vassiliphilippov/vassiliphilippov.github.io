great blue heron

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt blu ˈhɛr.ən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt bluː ˈhe.rən / (BrE)
a large North American heron with bluish-grey plumage, long legs, and a long neck, commonly found near shores and wetlands
large North American heron

Examples

The great blue heron stood motionless by the water, waiting to catch a fish.
Birdwatchers spotted a great blue heron soaring gracefully over the marshlands.
A great blue heron nested in the tall reeds near the lake last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - a long-legged wading bird
