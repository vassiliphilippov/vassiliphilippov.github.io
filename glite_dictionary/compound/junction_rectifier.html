junction rectifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkʃən ˈrɛktəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a semiconductor device with a single p-n junction, used to convert alternating current to direct current
semiconductor with a p-n junction for rectification

Examples

The junction rectifier is widely used in power supply circuits to convert AC to DC.
A faulty junction rectifier can cause the device to malfunction.
Technicians replaced the old junction rectifier to restore the circuit's performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diode - a semiconductor device with two terminals allowing current to flow in one direction
  • Synonym: rectifier - an electrical device that converts alternating current to direct current
