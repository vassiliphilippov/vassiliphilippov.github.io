instantaneous sound pressure

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstənˈteɪniəs saʊnd ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˌɪnstənˈteɪnɪəs saʊnd ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the difference between the pressure at a specific moment at a point in a sound field and the average pressure at that point
momentary pressure deviation in a sound field

Examples

The microphone captures the instantaneous sound pressure variations in the room for accurate audio measurement.
In acoustics, instantaneous sound pressure helps in analyzing how sound waves affect different materials.
The engineer monitored the instantaneous sound pressure to detect sudden changes in the noise level.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sound pressure - the pressure created by a sound wave, above or below atmospheric pressure
