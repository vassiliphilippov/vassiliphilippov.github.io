genus Welwitschia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs wɛlˈwɪtʃiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs wɛlˈwɪtʃɪə / (BrE)
The only genus of the family Welwitschiaceae, consisting of the unique African desert plant Welwitschia mirabilis.
sole plant genus in Welwitschiaceae

Examples

The genus Welwitschia is represented by a single species native to the Namib Desert.
Botanists consider genus Welwitschia to be a living fossil due to its ancient lineage.
The unusual leaf structure of the genus Welwitschia fascinates plant researchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welwitschia mirabilis - the sole species in the genus Welwitschia

Etymology

From the surname of Friedrich Welwitsch, the Austrian botanist who discovered the plant, combined with Latin taxonomic tradition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs wɛlˈwɪtʃ.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs wɛlˈwɪtʃ.ɪ.ə / (BrE)
The only genus in the family Welwitschiaceae, containing the unique plant species Welwitschia mirabilis, native to southwestern Africa.
botanical genus of Welwitschia

Examples

The genus Welwitschia is renowned for its single living species, Welwitschia mirabilis, found in the Namib Desert.
Researchers study the unique adaptations of plants within the genus Welwitschia to survive extreme aridity.
Botanists regard the genus Welwitschia as a living fossil due to its ancient evolutionary lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welwitschia mirabilis - the only species in the genus Welwitschia

Etymology

Genus name from Dr. Friedrich Welwitsch, the Austrian botanist who discovered the plant, plus standard Latin taxonomic suffix.
