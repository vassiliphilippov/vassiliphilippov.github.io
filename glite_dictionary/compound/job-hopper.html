job-hopper

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑb ˈhɑpɚ / (AmE), / dʒɒb ˈhɒpə / (BrE)
a person who changes jobs frequently, especially as a pattern or habit
a person who changes jobs often

Examples

Being labeled a job-hopper can make it difficult to find stable employment.
Recruiters may hesitate to interview someone who appears to be a job-hopper.
As a job-hopper, she has gained experience in many different industries.

Synset Examples

He was labeled a job-hopper after switching roles frequently.
Being a job-hopper can be risky for career growth.
She's a job-hopper, always seeking new challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careerist - a person whose main concern is for advancement in their career

Etymology

From 'job' (occupation) and 'hopper' (someone who shifts or moves rapidly between things).
