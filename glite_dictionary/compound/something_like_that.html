something like that

1. X

/ ˈsʌmθɪŋ laɪk ðæt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmθɪŋ laɪk ðæt / (BrE)
used to refer to things that are similar to what has just been mentioned, without being more specific.
referring to similar things
informal

Examples

He told me to submit the form online or by mail, or something like that.
She usually wears jeans, a sweater, or something like that when she goes out.
We need a screwdriver, a hammer, or something like that to fix this.

Synset Examples

The recipe requires garlic, onions, and something like that.
