diapason stop

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈpeɪ.sən stɑp / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈpɑː.sən stɒp / (BrE)
either of the two main stops on a pipe organ, producing a rich, fundamental tone characteristic of the instrument
main pipe organ stop

Examples

The organist adjusted the diapason stop to produce a fuller, richer sound for the hymn.
Many traditional pipe organs feature both an open and a stopped diapason stop.
He explained that the diapason stop gives the organ its characteristic foundation tone.

Etymology

From 'diapason', from Greek 'diapasōn' (through all), referring to the musical interval of an octave, and 'stop', an organ control mechanism.
