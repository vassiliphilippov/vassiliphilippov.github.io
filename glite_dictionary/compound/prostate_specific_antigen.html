prostate specific antigen

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌsteɪt spəˈsɪfɪk ˈæn.ti.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.teɪt spəˈsɪf.ɪk ˈæn.tɪ.dʒən / (BrE)
a protein produced exclusively by the prostate gland, often measured in blood tests to help diagnose prostate disorders such as benign prostatic hyperplasia and prostate cancer
protein marker for prostate disorders
medical

Examples

Your doctor may order a blood test to check your prostate specific antigen levels.
An elevated prostate specific antigen reading can be a sign of prostate cancer or other issues.
Regular screening includes monitoring prostate specific antigen to detect any early signs of disease.

Etymology

Formed from 'prostate', 'specific', and 'antigen', commonly abbreviated as PSA in medicine.
