chalcis fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsɪs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkælsɪs flaɪ / (BrE)
A wasp-like insect belonging to the family Chalcididae, known for its metallic coloration and parasitic larvae that develop in other insect hosts.
a parasitic wasp of the Chalcididae family

Examples

The chalcis fly is often studied for its role in controlling pest populations naturally.
Scientists observed a chalcis fly deposit its eggs inside the larvae of another insect.
Farmers appreciate the presence of chalcis flies in their fields due to their parasitic habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalcid - a tiny parasitic wasp of the superfamily Chalcidoidea

Etymology

From scientific Latin Chalcis, genus name, from Greek khalkís (a kind of wasp), from khalkos (copper, referring to metallic color).
