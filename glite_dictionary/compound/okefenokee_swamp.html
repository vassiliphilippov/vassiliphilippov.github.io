Okefenokee Swamp

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊkifəˈnoʊki swɑmp / (AmE), / ˌəʊkɪfəˈnəʊki swɒmp / (BrE)
a large, swampy area in northeastern Florida and southeastern Georgia, known for its unique wildlife and ecology
large swamp in the southeastern United States

Examples

The Okefenokee Swamp is home to many species of birds, reptiles, and plants native to the region.
We took an airboat tour through the Okefenokee Swamp and saw alligators up close.
Conservationists are working to protect the delicate ecosystem of the Okefenokee Swamp.

Etymology

From Creek (Muskogean language) words meaning 'land of trembling earth.'
