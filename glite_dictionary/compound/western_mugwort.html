western mugwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈmʌɡˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈmʌɡˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a perennial cottony-white herb native to the southwestern United States, known for its aromatic leaves
cottony-white herb of the southwestern US

Examples

In the high desert, western mugwort is commonly found growing alongside sagebrush and rabbitbrush.
Researchers studied how western mugwort affects the soil composition in arid regions.
Gardeners value western mugwort for its distinctive appearance and ability to thrive with little water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mugwort - a group of aromatic plants in the genus Artemisia
