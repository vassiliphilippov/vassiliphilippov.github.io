requiem shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkwiəm ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈrɛkwɪəm ʃɑːk / (BrE)
any of various medium to large sharks of the family Carcharhinidae, including both harmless and dangerous species found in marine environments.
a type of shark in the family Carcharhinidae

Examples

The bull shark is one of the most well-known requiem sharks found in coastal waters.
Scientists study requiem sharks to learn more about marine ecosystems and predator behavior.
Some requiem sharks, like the tiger shark, can be dangerous to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - a large group of predatory fish with cartilaginous skeletons

Etymology

From French 'requiem', named in reference to the genus 'Requiem', possibly via folk etymology.
