eau de Javelle

1. NOUN

/ oʊ də ʒaˈvɛl / (AmE), / əʊ də ʒaˈvɛl / (BrE)
a solution of sodium hypochlorite in water, used as a bleach and disinfectant
bleaching solution; sodium hypochlorite in water

Examples

They used eau de Javelle to disinfect the lab surfaces before the experiment.
A diluted solution of eau de Javelle is effective for whitening laundry.
The janitor prepared a bucket with eau de Javelle to clean the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical used to whiten or disinfect

Etymology

From French 'eau de Javelle' ('water from Javelle', a district in Paris where it was first made).
