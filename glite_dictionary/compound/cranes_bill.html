crane's bill

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪnz ˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈkreɪnz ˌbɪl / (BrE)
Any of various flowering plants of the genus Geranium, known for their long, slender seed pods that resemble a crane's beak.
a type of geranium plant

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming crane's bill in early summer.
She planted several varieties of crane's bill to attract bees and butterflies.
Botanists admire the distinctive seed pods of the crane's bill species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - any of a genus (Geranium) of plants with divided leaves and usually pink, purple, or white flowers

Etymology

So called because the shape of the seed pod resembles a crane's beak.
