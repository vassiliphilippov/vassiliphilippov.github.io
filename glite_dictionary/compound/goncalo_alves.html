goncalo alves

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔnˈsɑːloʊ ˈælviːz / (AmE), / ɡɔnˈsɑːləʊ ˈælviːz / (BrE)
A tall tropical tree native to South America, especially Brazil, valued for its hard, durable wood with distinctive dark stripes on a lighter ground, used in furniture, veneer, and heavy construction.
tropical wood-yielding tree of Brazil

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from goncalo alves, showcasing its striking striped pattern.
Due to its durability, goncalo alves is a popular choice for flooring in luxury homes.
Many guitar makers prefer goncalo alves for its beauty and strength in instrument construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebrawood - striped, decorative wood from various tropical trees used in woodworking

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Gonçalo Alves', the local name for the tree, likely named after a personal name.
