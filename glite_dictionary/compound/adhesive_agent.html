adhesive agent

1. NOUN

/ ədˈhivs ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ədˈhiːsɪv ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a substance that is used to unite or bond surfaces together by adhesion
substance for bonding surfaces

Examples

An adhesive agent is essential for mounting dental crowns securely in the mouth.
The tiles were fixed in place using a strong adhesive agent that dried within hours.
Scientists developed a new adhesive agent that works even on wet surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive - a substance used for sticking objects together
  • Synonym: glue - an adhesive substance used for sticking objects together

Etymology

From Latin 'adhaesivus' (tending to stick to) + agent, from Latin 'agens' (doing).
