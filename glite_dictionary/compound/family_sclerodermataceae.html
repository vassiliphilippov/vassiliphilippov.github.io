family Sclerodermataceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsklɛroʊˌdɜrməˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsklɛərəʊˌdɜːməˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi, belonging to the order Sclerodermatales, characterized by a single-layered peridium and including earthballs.
a family of fungi including earthballs
medical

Examples

The family Sclerodermataceae includes various species of earthballs found in woodland areas.
Research into the family Sclerodermataceae has revealed new insights into fungal biodiversity.
Members of the family Sclerodermataceae are known for their tough, thick-skinned fruiting bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scleroderma - a genus of fungi with hard-skinned fruiting bodies (earthballs)

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sclerodermataceae', based on the type genus 'Scleroderma' (from Greek 'skleros' meaning hard, and 'derma' meaning skin) plus '-aceae', a standard suffix for plant and fungal family names.
