relapsing fever

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlæpsɪŋ ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈlæpsɪŋ ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an infectious disease marked by recurring episodes of high fever, chills, headache, and muscle pain, typically transmitted by lice or ticks
infectious disease with recurrent fever
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected relapsing fever after noticing a pattern of recurring high fevers in the patient.
Outbreaks of relapsing fever are most commonly associated with areas where tick populations are high.
Prompt diagnosis and treatment are important to manage relapsing fever and prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhus - infectious disease causing fever, often transmitted by lice

Etymology

From Latin 'relapsus' (a falling back) + English 'fever'.
