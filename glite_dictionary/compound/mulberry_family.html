mulberry family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlˌbɛri ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmʌlbəri ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of trees or shrubs (Moraceae) that have a milky sap, including the mulberry, fig, and breadfruit plants; in some classifications, it also includes the genus Cannabis.
family of trees or shrubs with milky sap (Moraceae)

Examples

The fig tree belongs to the mulberry family, along with breadfruit and mulberry itself.
Many members of the mulberry family produce a characteristic milky latex.
Botanists have debated whether Cannabis should be included in the mulberry family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Moraceae - the botanical family also known as the mulberry family
