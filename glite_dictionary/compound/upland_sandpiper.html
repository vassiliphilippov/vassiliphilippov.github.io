upland sandpiper

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌp.lənd ˈsænˌdaɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌp.lənd ˈsæn.daɪ.pə / (BrE)
a large, plover-like sandpiper (Bartramia longicauda) native to North American fields and uplands
large North American sandpiper species

Examples

We spotted an upland sandpiper walking through the tall grass of the prairie.
The upland sandpiper is easily identified by its long neck and yellow legs.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats favored by the upland sandpiper.
