distributor point

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɪbjətər pɔɪnt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjʊtə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a contact in an automobile distributor that is touched by the rotor arm, allowing current to flow to the spark plugs
electrical contact in a car distributor

Examples

A worn distributor point can cause the engine to misfire or run poorly.
Older vehicles often require periodic adjustment of the distributor point gap.
The mechanic inspected the distributor point when diagnosing the ignition problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact breaker - mechanical switch in an ignition system that interrupts the current
