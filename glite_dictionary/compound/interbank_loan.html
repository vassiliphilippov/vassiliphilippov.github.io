interbank loan

1. NOUN

/ ɪntərˌbæŋk loʊn / (AmE), / ɪntəˌbæŋk ləʊn / (BrE)
a loan that one bank makes to another, typically for short-term funding needs
loan from one bank to another

Examples

The central bank monitors interbank loans to maintain stability in the financial system.
During a liquidity crisis, interbank loan rates often spike sharply.
Many financial institutions rely on interbank loans to meet their short-term funding needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bank loan - a loan provided by a bank to an individual or business
