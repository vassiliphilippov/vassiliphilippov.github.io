self-rule

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈruːl / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈruːl / (BrE)
the government of a country or region by its own people, especially after having been a colony or subject to external control
government by one’s own people

Examples

The region has been seeking self-rule since gaining partial autonomy a decade ago.
Many former colonies struggled to establish effective self-rule after independence.
The campaigners organized rallies demanding self-rule and democratic reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomy - independence or self-government
  • Synonym: independence - the state of being independent, self-government
  • Antonym: colonial rule - government by a foreign or imperial power

Etymology

From 'self' (one's own person) + 'rule' (governance).
