Battle of Philippi

1. PROPN

/ ˈbætəl əv fɪˈlɪpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv fɪˈlɪpʌɪ / (BrE)
a decisive battle in 42 BC near Philippi in Macedonia, where the forces of Octavian and Mark Antony defeated the armies of Brutus and Cassius
decisive 42 BC battle in Macedonia

Examples

The outcome of the Battle of Philippi changed the course of Roman history.
Historians often cite the Battle of Philippi as the end of the Roman Republic.
After losing the Battle of Philippi, Brutus and Cassius took their own lives.
