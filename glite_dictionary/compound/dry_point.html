dry point

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
A steel needle used for engraving directly onto a copper plate without the use of acid.
engraving tool; steel needle for copper plates

Examples

The artist expertly used a dry point to etch fine details into the copper plate.
Unlike traditional etching, dry point allows for direct engraving without acid.
A worn dry point can create softer, more expressive lines in the print.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burin - A tool for engraving in metal, similar in use to a dry point

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A print made by engraving a design into a plate with a hard pointed needle, creating characteristic rich and soft lines.
a print from dry point engraving

Examples

The artist created a beautiful dry point that captured the subtle textures of the landscape.
This gallery specializes in rare prints, including several Rembrandt dry points.
She learned the dry point technique in her printmaking class at university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etching - a print made from a metal plate covered with acid-resistant ground and etched with acid

Etymology

From 'dry' + 'point', referring to the technique of engraving without acid.
