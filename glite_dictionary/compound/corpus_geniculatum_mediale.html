corpus geniculatum mediale

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrpəs dʒɛnɪˌkjuːlɑːtəm miːˈdeɪli / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpəs ˌdʒɛnɪkjʊˈlɑːtəm mɛˈdɪeɪli / (BrE)
a structure in the brain's thalamus that acts as the primary relay center for auditory information on its way to the cerebral cortex
brain structure relaying auditory signals
medical

Examples

The corpus geniculatum mediale is a crucial relay point for sensory information related to hearing.
Damage to the corpus geniculatum mediale can result in deficits in auditory processing.
Scientists mapped auditory pathways from the cochlea through the corpus geniculatum mediale to the cortex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medial geniculate body - the main auditory relay nucleus in the thalamus
  • Antonym: corpus geniculatum laterale - brain structure in the thalamus for visual information relay

Etymology

From Latin: 'corpus' (body), 'geniculatum' (with little knees or bends), 'mediale' (medial, in the middle).
