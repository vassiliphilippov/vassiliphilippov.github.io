more-mover

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr ˌmuːvər / (AmE), / ˈmɔː ˌmuːvə / (BrE)
(in chess composition) A type of problem stipulating that White must checkmate Black in no more than a specified number of moves (greater than three), regardless of Black's defense.
chess problem: mate in more than three moves

Examples

This challenging puzzle is a more-mover requiring careful calculation to find the forced mate.
He specializes in composing more-movers that demand deep and creative solutions from solvers.
Solvers found the more-mover intriguing because it allowed for a wide range of defensive responses.

Synset Examples

This week's problem is a more-mover by a composer better known for his endgame study and theory work.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three-mover - chess problem: mate in three moves
  • Degree Lower: two-mover - chess problem: mate in two moves

Etymology

From 'more' + 'mover', by analogy with chess terms like 'three-mover' and 'two-mover'.
