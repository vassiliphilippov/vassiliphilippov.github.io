striped bass

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪpt bæs / (AmE), / ˈstraɪpt bæs / (BrE)
a large North American marine and freshwater fish with dark horizontal stripes along its body, valued as a food and game fish
a large fish with dark horizontal stripes

Examples

Anglers flock to the coast each spring to catch striped bass during their migration.
Grilled striped bass is a popular dish in many seafood restaurants.
The river is known for its large populations of striped bass this time of year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockfish - another common name for the striped bass

2. NOUN

/ straɪpt bæs / (AmE), / straɪpt bæs / (BrE)
a large fish of the temperate bass family, with horizontal dark stripes running along its body, found especially along the Atlantic coast of North America and valued as a game and food fish
large North American fish with horizontal stripes

Examples

Anglers often search for striped bass along the coastal waters each spring.
The restaurant specializes in grilled striped bass served with fresh vegetables.
A huge striped bass was caught during last weekend's fishing tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockfish - another name for striped bass, especially in some regions
