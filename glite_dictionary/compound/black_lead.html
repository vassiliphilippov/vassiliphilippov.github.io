black lead

1. NOUN

/ blæk lɛd / (AmE), / blæk lɛd / (BrE)
A soft, black form of carbon, also known as graphite, used as a lubricant and as a moderator in nuclear reactors.
graphite used as lubricant or moderator

Examples

Engineers sometimes use black lead as a dry lubricant in machinery.
The core of a traditional pencil is actually made of black lead, not real lead.
Black lead plays a crucial role as a moderator in certain types of nuclear reactors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphite - A soft, black, crystalline form of carbon used in pencils, lubricants, etc.

Etymology

From the mistaken belief that graphite was a form of lead.
