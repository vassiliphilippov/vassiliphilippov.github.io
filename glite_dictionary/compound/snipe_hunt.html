snipe hunt

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnaɪp ˌhʌnt / (AmE), / ˈsnaɪp ˌhʌnt / (BrE)
A practical joke, typically involving sending someone on a fool's errand to hunt for a non-existent creature, intended to trick the victim.
elaborate practical joke involving a fake hunt

Examples

The boys convinced their friend to go on a snipe hunt in the woods.
She realized too late that the snipe hunt was just a prank by her older cousins.
Many campers have fond memories of being tricked with the traditional snipe hunt.

Synset Examples

in the South a snipe hunt is practically a rite of passage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild-goose chase - a futile or hopeless pursuit

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnaɪp ˌhʌnt / (AmE), / ˈsnaɪp ˌhʌnt / (BrE)
A trick or prank in which someone is sent on a futile, pointless, or imaginary search or task, usually as a joke.
prank involving a pointless search

Examples

The new recruits quickly realized the assignment was just a snipe hunt organized by their amused colleagues.
After three hours searching for the non-existent 'left-handed wrench,' I knew I'd been sent on a snipe hunt.
Their friends tricked them into a snipe hunt at the campfire, sending them off into the woods at night.

Synset Examples

The kids were sent on a snipe hunt for fun.
He felt foolish after realizing it was a snipe hunt.
We went on a snipe hunt as a prank on the newbies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild-goose chase - a hopeless or pointless pursuit

Etymology

Originated in North America, originally referring to a real or imagined hunt for the elusive snipe bird, later used as a practical joke.
