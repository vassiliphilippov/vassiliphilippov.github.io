profit taker

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfɪt ˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt ˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person who sells stock or an asset to realize a profit, especially in the context of a rising market
person selling assets to realize a profit

Examples

Several profit takers cashed out after the stock market rally.
When prices peaked, profit takers quickly moved to sell their holdings.
Heavy selling by profit takers can sometimes cause a temporary dip in prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investor - a person who puts money into financial schemes, shares, or property with the expectation of achieving a profit
