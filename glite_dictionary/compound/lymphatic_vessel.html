lymphatic vessel

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈfætɪk ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / lɪmˈfætɪk ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
a thin-walled tube in the lymphatic system that carries lymph and returns it to the blood circulation
tube carrying lymph in the body
medical

Examples

The doctor explained how a blocked lymphatic vessel could cause swelling in the leg.
The dye made the lymphatic vessel visible during the scan.
Infections can sometimes spread through a lymphatic vessel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood vessel - a tube that carries blood through the body

Etymology

From Greek 'lympha' (water) and Latin 'vascellum' (small vessel).
