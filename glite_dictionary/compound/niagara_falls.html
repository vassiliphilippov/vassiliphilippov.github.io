Niagara Falls

1. PROPN

/ naɪˈæɡrə fɔlz / (AmE), / naɪˈæɡərə fɔːlz / (BrE)
A famous large waterfall on the border between Canada and the United States, consisting of the Horseshoe Falls and the American Falls.
famous large waterfall on US-Canada border

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit Niagara Falls each year to witness its powerful beauty.
Our road trip included a stop at Niagara Falls on the Canadian side.
The mist from Niagara Falls is visible from miles away on a sunny day.

Etymology

From Iroquoian origin, perhaps meaning 'thunder of waters'.

2. PROPN

/ naɪˈæɡərə fɔlz / (AmE), / naɪˈæɡərə fɔːlz / (BrE)
A city in western New York State, United States, located at the site of the Niagara River's renowned waterfalls; a popular tourist attraction and honeymoon destination.
city in western New York, tourist site

Examples

Many couples visit Niagara Falls each year for their honeymoon.
The city of Niagara Falls is famous for its breathtaking scenery and vibrant tourism industry.
We took a weekend trip to Niagara Falls and enjoyed both the city and the waterfalls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Niagara Falls - the famous waterfalls on the Niagara River

Etymology

Named for the Niagara River and its falls, from indigenous Iroquoian origin, possibly meaning 'thunder of waters'.

a large, famous waterfall on the border between Canada and the United States, known for its impressive size and scenic beauty
famous waterfall between Canada and the USA

Examples

Many tourists visit Niagara Falls every year to admire its power and beauty.
The boat ride brought us very close to Niagara Falls, and we could feel the mist on our faces.
At night, Niagara Falls is illuminated with colorful lights, creating a spectacular view.

Synset Examples

Niagara Falls is breathtaking during the summer.

Etymology

Named after the Niagara River; 'Niagara' likely from an Iroquoian word meaning 'thunder of waters' or 'neck'.
