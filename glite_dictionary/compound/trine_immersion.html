trine immersion

1. NOUN

/ traɪn ɪˈmɝːʒən / (AmE), / traɪn ɪˈmɜːʃən / (BrE)
a method of baptism in which a person is immersed three times, each in the name of a different person of the Trinity
baptism by triple immersion

Examples

The church practices trine immersion, with the candidate submerged three times during the ceremony.
Some denominations insist on trine immersion as a reflection of the doctrine of the Trinity.
Historical records show that trine immersion was common in early Christian communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baptism - a Christian sacrament of initiation and purification

Etymology

From Latin 'trinus' (threefold) + English 'immersion'.
