cherry tomato

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛri təˈmeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a small, round, red or yellow tomato, typically eaten whole in salads or as a snack
small, round salad tomato

Examples

She added sliced cherry tomato to the salad for a burst of color and flavor.
A bowl of fresh cherry tomatoes sat on the kitchen counter, ready for snacking.
He prefers cherry tomato because they are sweet and easy to eat whole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato - a round, red fruit eaten as a vegetable

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛri təˈmeɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a small, round variety of tomato, typically red or yellow, with a sweet taste, often eaten whole in salads or as a snack
small, sweet tomato variety

Examples

She added a handful of cherry tomatoes to the salad for extra color and sweetness.
The garden's cherry tomato plant produced fruit all summer long.
Roasted cherry tomatoes make a delicious addition to pasta dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato - the round, red fruit of the tomato plant

Etymology

Named for its small, cherry-like size and resemblance; 'cherry' (fruit) + 'tomato'.
