basil thyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæzəl ˌθaɪm / (AmE), / ˈbæzəl ˌtʌɪm / (BrE)
a fragrant European herb of the mint family, with small violet-and-white flowers, often naturalized in eastern North America
fragrant European mint herb

Examples

The garden was filled with the scent of basil thyme in the early summer.
Cooks sometimes add fresh basil thyme to their dishes for an aromatic flavor.
We planted basil thyme along the pathway to attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinopodium - genus including basil thyme
