Chinookan language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈnʊkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊkən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of closely related Native American languages of the Penutian family, spoken by the Chinook people of the Pacific Northwest
language of the Chinook people

Examples

Researchers have documented the grammar of the Chinookan language in detail.
Only a few elders still speak the traditional Chinookan language today.
The Chinookan language holds many stories and oral traditions of the Chinook people.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Chinook jargon - trade language based on Chinookan and other languages

Etymology

From Chinook, the name of the indigenous people, plus 'an' (language forming suffix).
