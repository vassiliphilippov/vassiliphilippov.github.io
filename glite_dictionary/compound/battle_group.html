battle group

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl ˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl ˌɡruːp / (BrE)
a military unit usually composed of several companies, organized for combat operations
military unit composed of several companies

Examples

The commander led his battle group toward the front lines at dawn.
Each battle group was responsible for securing its assigned sector during the operation.
The army reorganized its units, forming more flexible battle groups for rapid deployment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battalion - a large body of troops; larger than a company, often similar in size to a battle group
