flying opossum

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ əˈpɑsəm / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ əˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
a small nocturnal marsupial of Australia and New Guinea that glides between trees using folds of skin along its sides
gliding nocturnal marsupial

Examples

The flying opossum leaps from tree to tree using the skin flaps along its body to glide.
Many families in Australia have spotted a flying opossum gliding through their backyard at night.
Unlike regular opossums, the flying opossum can travel long distances without touching the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar glider - a type of small gliding possum found in Australia and New Guinea
