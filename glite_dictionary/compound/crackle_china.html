crackle china

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrækəl ˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
Glazed china with a network of fine, decorative cracks on the surface, produced intentionally as part of the finish.
china with a fine crackled glaze

Examples

The collector displayed her favorite pieces of crackle china in the glass cabinet.
Antique crackle china is prized for its distinctive patterns and craftsmanship.
He inherited a set of crackle china dishes from his grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crackleware - ceramic ware with a crackled glaze

Etymology

From 'crackle' (to make a series of small cracks or lines) and 'china' (porcelain), referring to the characteristic crackled glaze.
