Regency era

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənsi ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənsi ˈɪərə / (BrE)
the period in British history from 1811 to 1820 when the future George IV ruled as Prince Regent during George III's incapacity
British historical period (1811–1820)
british

Examples

Many novels set in the Regency era feature lavish balls and strict social codes.
The architecture from the Regency era is still admired for its elegance and symmetry.
Scholars often study the Regency era to understand shifts in British fashion and politics.

Etymology

From 'Regency', relating to the rule of a regent, and 'era', from Latin 'aera' (era).
