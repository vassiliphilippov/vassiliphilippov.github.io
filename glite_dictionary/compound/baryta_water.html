baryta water

1. NOUN

/ bəˈraɪtə ˌwɔtər / (AmE), / bəˈraɪtə ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
an aqueous solution of barium hydroxide, used mainly as a chemical reagent
aqueous solution of barium hydroxide

Examples

In the laboratory, baryta water is used to test for carbon dioxide due to its reactivity.
Accidental ingestion of baryta water can be extremely dangerous and should be avoided.
The chemist prepared baryta water to use as a reagent in the upcoming experiment.

Synset Examples

The monohydrate (x =1) is known as baryta, or baryta-water, it is one of the principal compounds of barium.

Etymology

From 'baryta', derived from New Latin, relating to barium compounds, and 'water'.
