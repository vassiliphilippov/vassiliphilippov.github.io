neutralisation reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which an acid and a base combine to form a salt and water
reaction of acid and base to form salt
medical

Examples

A neutralisation reaction occurs when hydrochloric acid reacts with sodium hydroxide.
The laboratory experiment demonstrated a classic neutralisation reaction between an acid and a base.
In a neutralisation reaction, water and a salt are usually produced as end products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutralization reaction - alternative spelling (American English) for 'neutralisation reaction'

Etymology

From 'neutralisation', from Latin 'neutralis' (of neuter gender), and 'reaction', from Latin 'reactio'.
