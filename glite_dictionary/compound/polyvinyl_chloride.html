polyvinyl chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˌvaɪnəl ˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌvaɪnɪl ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic plastic polymer made from vinyl chloride, widely used for making pipes, cable insulation, and many other products
synthetic plastic polymer (PVC)

Examples

Most household pipes are made of polyvinyl chloride because it is durable and resistant to corrosion.
The electrician replaced the cable sheath with polyvinyl chloride for improved insulation.
Products such as credit cards and vinyl flooring are often manufactured using polyvinyl chloride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PVC - abbreviation of polyvinyl chloride

Etymology

From poly- (many) + vinyl (from Latin vinum ‘wine’, relating to ethylene) + chloride, referring to its chemical structure.
