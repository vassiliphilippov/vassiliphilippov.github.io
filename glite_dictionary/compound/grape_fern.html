grape fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪp fɝn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪp fəːn / (BrE)
A fern of the genus Botrychium, with a fertile frond that bears small, grapelike clusters of spore cases.
a fern with grapelike spore clusters

Examples

The grape fern can be identified by the small, grapelike spore clusters on its fronds.
We spotted a rare grape fern growing at the edge of the forest trail.
Several species of grape fern thrive in moist, shady woodlands.

Etymology

Named for the appearance of its spore clusters, which resemble small grapes.
