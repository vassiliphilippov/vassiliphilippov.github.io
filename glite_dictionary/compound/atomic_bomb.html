atomic bomb

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk bɑm / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk bɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon that releases enormous destructive energy through the fission of heavy atomic nuclei such as uranium-235 or plutonium-239.
nuclear weapon using nuclear fission

Examples

The United States dropped an atomic bomb on Hiroshima in 1945.
The threat of an atomic bomb changed the course of international relations during the Cold War.
Scientists debated the ethical implications of developing the atomic bomb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear weapon - A weapon whose destructive power comes from nuclear reactions.
  • Degree Higher: hydrogen bomb - A more powerful nuclear weapon based on nuclear fusion.

Etymology

Compound of 'atomic' (from Greek atomos, indivisible) and 'bomb' (from Italian bomba).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk bɑm / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk bɒm / (BrE)
a type of nuclear weapon that releases an enormous amount of energy by splitting the nuclei of atoms, causing massive destruction
nuclear weapon causing enormous destruction

Examples

The devastating effects of the atomic bomb were felt for decades after its detonation.
She studied the history of the atomic bomb and its impact on international relations.
Many people around the world campaign for the abolition of the atomic bomb.

Synset Examples

Survivors of the atomic bomb shared their stories at the memorial.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hydrogen bomb - a more powerful type of nuclear weapon using fusion
  • Synonym: nuclear weapon - a weapon deriving destructive force from nuclear reactions

Etymology

From 'atomic', relating to atoms (from Greek atomos, indivisible), and 'bomb'.
