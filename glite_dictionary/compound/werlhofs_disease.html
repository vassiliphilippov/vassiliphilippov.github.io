Werlhof's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛr.lhɔfs dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈvɛː.lhɒfs dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by purpura and a reduced number of circulating blood platelets, often resulting in easy bruising and bleeding
medical condition causing purpura and low platelets
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Werlhof's disease after presenting symptoms of unexplained bruising and bleeding.
Treatment options for Werlhof's disease often focus on increasing platelet counts and managing bleeding risks.
Early recognition of Werlhof's disease can significantly improve patient outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura - another name for Werlhof's disease; a blood disorder with low platelets causing purpura

Etymology

Named after German physician Paul Gottlieb Werlhof (1699–1767), who first described the condition.
