Demotic language

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the modern vernacular form of the Greek language, used in everyday speech and writing
modern Greek vernacular

Examples

Many novels of the twentieth century in Greece were written in Demotic language for broader accessibility.
The debate between using Demotic language and Katharevousa shaped Greek education policy for decades.
He studied the transition of official documents from ancient Greek to Demotic language in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Katharevousa - a purist, archaic form of modern Greek language

Etymology

From Greek 'dēmotikos' (popular, of the people).
