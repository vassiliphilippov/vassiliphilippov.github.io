conenose bug

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊnˌnoʊz bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊnˌnəʊz bʌɡ / (BrE)
A large bloodsucking bug, especially any of several species of the genus Triatoma, known to feed on humans and animals and sometimes transmit disease.
large bloodsucking insect

Examples

The conenose bug can sometimes transmit Chagas disease to humans through its bite.
Researchers collected several conenose bugs during their field study in rural Arizona.
A bite from a conenose bug may cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kissing bug - another name for conenose bug, large bloodsucking bug of the genus Triatoma

Etymology

From 'cone' + 'nose', referring to the shape of the insect's head.
