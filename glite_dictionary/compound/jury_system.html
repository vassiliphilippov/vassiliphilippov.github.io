jury system

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜri ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊəri ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A legal system in which a group of citizens forms a jury to decide the facts and render a verdict in a trial.
system using juries to decide trials
legal

Examples

The jury system is a fundamental part of many democratic countries' legal proceedings.
Debates about the fairness of the jury system continue to arise in the media.
Some people argue that the jury system allows ordinary citizens to participate in justice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bench trial - a trial by judge rather than by jury
