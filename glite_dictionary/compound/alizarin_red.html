alizarin red

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzərɪn rɛd / (AmE), / əˈlɪzərɪn rɛd / (BrE)
any of several synthetic acid dyes, derived from alizarin, used especially to dye wool a scarlet red color.
a synthetic red dye for wool

Examples

The fabric was dyed with alizarin red to achieve a bright scarlet hue.
In the laboratory, alizarin red is used to stain calcium deposits in biological samples.
The artist preferred alizarin red for its vivid, long-lasting color in her textiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alizarin - an orange-red dye originally obtained from madder root

Etymology

Derived from 'alizarin', itself from Arabic 'al-‘usāra' (the juice), with 'red' referring to the color produced.
