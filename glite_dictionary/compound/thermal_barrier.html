thermal barrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜːrməl bəˈrɪər / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməl bəˈrɪə / (BrE)
a limit to the speed of aircraft or spacecraft imposed by aerodynamic heating at high velocities
speed limit due to aerodynamic heating

Examples

The new jet was designed to overcome the thermal barrier and achieve greater speeds.
Engineers had to develop materials that could withstand the heat generated when approaching the thermal barrier.
Breaking through the thermal barrier was a major milestone in the advancement of supersonic flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound barrier - limit to flight speed imposed by the speed of sound
