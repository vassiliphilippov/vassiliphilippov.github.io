to beat off with a stick

1. VERB

/ bit ɔf wɪð ə stɪk / (AmE), / biːt ɒf wɪð ə stɪk / (BrE)
To repel or push away someone or something forcefully, especially when there are many of them; often used humorously to suggest being overwhelmed by attention or presence.
repel forcefully; fend off numerous things/people

Examples

The bakery owner said she has to beat off with a stick all the eager customers in the morning.
Rumor has it he's so popular he needs to beat off with a stick all his admirers.
Sometimes it feels like you need to beat off with a stick the constant stream of emails.

Synset Examples

He joked about needing to beat off with a stick the negativity around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fight off - to defend oneself against attack or unwanted advances
