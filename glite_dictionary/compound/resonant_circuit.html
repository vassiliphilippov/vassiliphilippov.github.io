resonant circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzənənt ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzənənt ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electrical circuit containing both inductance and capacitance, arranged so that it will produce the strongest possible oscillations at a particular frequency
electrical circuit with maximum oscillation at certain frequency

Examples

The radio uses a resonant circuit to tune into specific frequencies.
By adjusting the components in the resonant circuit, the engineers achieved optimal signal amplification.
A malfunction in the resonant circuit caused the device to stop oscillating properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank circuit - an electrical resonant circuit typically consisting of a capacitor and inductor
  • Degree Lower: filter circuit - an electric circuit that removes unwanted frequencies

Etymology

From 'resonant' (relating to resonance) and 'circuit' (from Latin 'circuitus', a going around).
