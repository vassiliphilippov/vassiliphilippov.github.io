Dalmatian pyrethrum

1. NOUN

/ dælˈmeɪʃən paɪˈriːθrəm / (AmE), / dælˈmeɪʃən pɪˈriːθrəm / (BrE)
A white-flowered plant native to the Balkan region, with pinnate, white, and silky-hairy leaves on the underside, used as a source of insecticide; sometimes classified in the genus Chrysanthemum.
Balkan plant used for insecticide

Examples

Dalmatian pyrethrum is a natural source of insect-repellent compounds widely used in organic farming.
Scientists studied the properties of Dalmatian pyrethrum to develop safer pesticides.
Many gardeners prefer to plant Dalmatian pyrethrum for its beautiful flowers and practical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrethrum - plant genus used as source of insecticides

Etymology

Dalmatian from Dalmatia, a region in the Balkans; pyrethrum from Greek 'pyrethron' (feverfew).
