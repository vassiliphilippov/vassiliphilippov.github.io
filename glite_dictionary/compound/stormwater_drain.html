stormwater drain

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔrmˌwɔtər dreɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɔːmwɔːtə dreɪn / (BrE)
A pipe or channel designed to carry away excess rainwater from streets, buildings, and other surfaces.
system for carrying away excess rainwater

Examples

Heavy rainfall can cause the stormwater drain to overflow if it is blocked.
Leaves and debris should be cleared from the stormwater drain to prevent flooding.
The city's stormwater drain system channels water safely into nearby rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewer - an underground conduit for waste water and refuse
