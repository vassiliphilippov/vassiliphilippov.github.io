genus Aeschynanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌiːskɪˈnænθəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌiːskɪˈnænθəs / (BrE)
a large genus of ornamental woody epiphytic plants native to East India, known for their brightly colored flowers
genus of ornamental East Indian epiphytic plants

Examples

The genus Aeschynanthus includes several species popularly known as lipstick plants.
Botanists have studied the genus Aeschynanthus for its unique epiphytic adaptations.
Many gardeners cultivate varieties from the genus Aeschynanthus for their ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aeschynanthus - a plant of the genus Aeschynanthus

Etymology

From Greek 'aischynē' (shame) and 'anthos' (flower), said to refer to the flower's drooping habit.
