in chains

1. ADJ

/ ɪn tʃeɪnz / (AmE), / ɪn tʃeɪnz / (BrE)
being physically restrained or confined by chains, often used to describe prisoners or captives
physically restrained by chains

Examples

The prisoners were led in chains through the courtyard.
After the rebellion, the defeated king was paraded in chains before the crowd.
The novel describes a hero who refuses to live in chains, even in the face of danger.

Synset Examples

enchained demons strained in anger to gnaw on his bones
prisoners in chains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chained - restrained or fastened with chains
  • Antonym: free - not confined or restrained
