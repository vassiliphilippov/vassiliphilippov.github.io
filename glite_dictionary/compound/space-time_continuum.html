space-time continuum

1. NOUN

/ ˌspeɪsˌtaɪm kənˈtɪnuəm / (AmE), / ˌspeɪsˌtaɪm kənˈtɪnjuːəm / (BrE)
The four-dimensional framework that combines three dimensions of space and one of time in which all physical events occur.
four-dimensional space (space + time in physics)

Examples

According to Einstein's theory, gravity results from the curvature of the space-time continuum.
Science fiction often features characters traveling through the space-time continuum to visit different eras.
A black hole is a region where the space-time continuum is so warped that nothing can escape its gravity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: space-time - The union of space and time into a single idea as a four-dimensional continuum.

Etymology

From Latin 'continuum' (a continuous thing) and 'space-time', coined in early 20th-century physics.
