solar cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər sɛl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə sɛl / (BrE)
A device that converts sunlight directly into electrical energy by means of the photovoltaic effect.
device converting sunlight to electricity

Examples

The roof of the building is covered with solar cells to generate clean electricity.
Engineers are working to make solar cells more efficient and affordable.
A single solar cell can power small devices in remote locations where electricity is not available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photovoltaic cell - another term for solar cell
