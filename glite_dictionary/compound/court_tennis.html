court tennis

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt ˈtɛnɪs / (AmE), / kɔːt ˈtɛnɪs / (BrE)
an ancient form of tennis played indoors on a four-walled court, regarded as the predecessor of modern lawn tennis
indoor ancestor of modern tennis

Examples

He traveled to England to watch a court tennis match in an old royal palace.
Unlike modern tennis, court tennis is played with asymmetrical rackets and a sloping roof.
Many players find the history and unique rules of court tennis fascinating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: real tennis - the original racquet sport from which lawn tennis is derived, synonymous with court tennis
  • Degree Lower: lawn tennis - the modern outdoor game of tennis played on grass, clay, or hard courts

Etymology

Derived from the term 'court' for an enclosed playing area and 'tennis', dating to medieval ball games in France and England.
