caste system

1. NOUN

/ kæst ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / kɑːst ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a social structure in which a person's class or status is determined by birth and is fixed for life, especially as practiced in some societies such as India
a social class system based on heredity

Examples

The rigid caste system in India historically limited people's opportunities based on their birth.
Some societies have moved away from using a caste system to determine social status.
His research focused on how the caste system affects economic mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class system - a system of social stratification based on social or economic status

Etymology

From Portuguese 'casta' (race, lineage); 'system' from Greek 'systēma' (whole, organized body).
