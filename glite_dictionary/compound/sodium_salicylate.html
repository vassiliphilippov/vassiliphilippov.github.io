sodium salicylate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm səˈlɪsɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm səˈlɪsɪˌleɪt / (BrE)
A white crystalline salt used as an analgesic and antipyretic, often in the treatment of pain and fever.
analgesic and antipyretic salt
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed sodium salicylate to help manage her mild joint pain.
Researchers are studying how sodium salicylate can reduce inflammation in certain chronic conditions.
Some over-the-counter pain relievers contain sodium salicylate as an active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain
  • Synonym: antipyretic - a drug used to reduce fever

Etymology

From 'sodium', a chemical element, and 'salicylate', derived from Latin 'salix' (willow), the original source of salicylic compounds.
