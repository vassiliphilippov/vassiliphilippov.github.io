to owe loyalty

1. VERB

/ oʊ ˈlɔɪəlti / (AmE), / əʊ ˈlɔɪəlti / (BrE)
to have a duty or obligation to be loyal to someone or something
have a duty to be loyal

Examples

Employees are expected to owe loyalty to their company as part of their contract.
She felt she did not owe loyalty to someone who had betrayed her trust.
In a monarchy, subjects may owe loyalty to the crown as a lifelong commitment.

Synset Examples

In medieval times, a vassal owed loyalty to their lord.
