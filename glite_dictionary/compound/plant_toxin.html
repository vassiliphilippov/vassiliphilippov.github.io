plant toxin

1. NOUN

/ plænt ˈtɑksən / (AmE), / plɑːnt ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous substance produced by a plant, similar in its properties to toxins made by bacteria
a poisonous substance made by a plant

Examples

Some species produce a plant toxin that is deadly to livestock if ingested.
Researchers discovered a new plant toxin in the leaves of the shrub.
The plant toxin affects the nervous system and can cause paralysis in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phytotoxin - a toxin produced by a plant
  • Antonym: animal toxin - a toxin produced by an animal
