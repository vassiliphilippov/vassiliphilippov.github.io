intracranial cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈkreɪniəl ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈkreɪniəl ˈkævɪti / (BrE)
the space within the skull that is enclosed by the cranium, containing the brain.
space inside the skull containing the brain
medical

Examples

The brain is protected by the bones of the skull and sits within the intracranial cavity.
Increased pressure in the intracranial cavity can be a medical emergency.
Tumors may develop in the intracranial cavity, affecting brain function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranial cavity - the space in the skull that houses the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'intra-' (within) + 'cranium' (the skull).
