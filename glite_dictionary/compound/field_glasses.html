field glasses

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌɡlæsɪz / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌɡlɑːsɪz / (BrE)
a pair of binoculars, especially ones designed for use outdoors to view distant objects
portable binoculars for outdoor use

Examples

He scanned the distant hills with his field glasses for any sign of movement.
The wildlife observer always carried field glasses during her research trips.
She raised her field glasses to watch the birds nesting in the far trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binoculars - an optical instrument with two lenses for viewing distant objects

Etymology

Formed from 'field' (open land) and 'glasses' (lenses), referring to their outdoor viewing use.
