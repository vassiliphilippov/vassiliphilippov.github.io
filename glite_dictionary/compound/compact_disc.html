compact disc

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmˌpækt ˈdɪsk / (AmE), / ˌkɒmˌpækt ˈdɪsk / (BrE)
A small, round optical disc used to store digital audio, video, or data, readable by a laser.
digital optical storage disc

Examples

She bought her favorite album on a compact disc at the record store.
The library still has a collection of classical music compact discs available for borrowing.
Many computers now omit the compact disc drive in favor of digital downloads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CD - Abbreviation for 'compact disc'

Etymology

From 'compact' (small, closely packed) and 'disc', introduced in the 1980s for the digital audio format.
