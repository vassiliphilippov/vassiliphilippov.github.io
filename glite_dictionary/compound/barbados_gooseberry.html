Barbados gooseberry

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈbeɪdoʊs ˈɡusˌbɛri / (AmE), / bɑːˈbeɪdɒs ˈɡuːsbəri / (BrE)
a woody climbing plant native to the West Indies, with spiny stems, fragrant white flowers, and small yellow to orange fruit
West Indian climbing plant with edible fruit

Examples

The Barbados gooseberry produces clusters of fragrant white flowers followed by orange-yellow fruits.
Locals often use the ripe Barbados gooseberry in jams and desserts.
A tangle of Barbados gooseberry vines covered the old garden wall.

2. NOUN

/ bɑrˈbeɪdoʊs ˈɡuːsbɛri / (AmE), / bɑːˈbeɪdəʊs ˈɡuːsbəri / (BrE)
A small yellow to orange berry produced by a species of cactus (Pereskia aculeata), used in desserts, preserves, and jellies.
edible fruit of a cactus

Examples

The Barbados gooseberry makes a delicious addition to homemade jams and jellies.
She planted a Barbados gooseberry vine in her garden for its vibrant fruit.
Local markets often sell Barbados gooseberry for use in tropical desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pereskia aculeata - the cactus species that produces Barbados gooseberry

Etymology

Named after Barbados (an island in the Caribbean) and gooseberry, due to its resemblance to gooseberry fruits.
