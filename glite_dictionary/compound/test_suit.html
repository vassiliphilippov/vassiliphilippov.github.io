test suit

1. NOUN

/ tɛst suːt / (AmE), / tɛst suːt / (BrE)
a legal case brought primarily to set a precedent by clarifying a point of law or establishing its validity
a legal case to set a precedent
legal

Examples

The court's decision in the test suit influenced many similar cases nationwide.
Several plaintiffs joined together to file a test suit challenging the constitutionality of the policy.
Lawyers often use a test suit to clarify the interpretation of new legislation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test case - a legal action brought to test the validity of a law or principle
