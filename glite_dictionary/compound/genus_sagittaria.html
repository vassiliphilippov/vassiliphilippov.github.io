genus Sagittaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sæˌdʒɪˈtɛəri.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sæˌdʒɪˈtɛː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic herbs found in temperate and tropical regions, known for their arrow-shaped leaves and white stemless flowers.
genus of aquatic herbs with arrow-shaped leaves

Examples

The genus Sagittaria includes several species commonly referred to as arrowheads.
Many aquatic gardeners choose plants from the genus Sagittaria for their decorative ponds.
Scientists studied the ecological role of the genus Sagittaria in wetland habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'sagittaria', meaning arrow-shaped, alluding to the leaf shape.
