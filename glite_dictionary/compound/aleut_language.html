Aleut language

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈluːt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Aleut people, native to the Aleutian Islands and parts of Alaska and eastern Siberia
language of the Aleut people

Examples

She is studying the Aleut language as part of her research into indigenous Alaskan cultures.
There are only a few fluent speakers of the Aleut language remaining today.
The Aleut language has contributed several words to the English vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aleut - a member of the native people inhabiting the Aleutian Islands

Etymology

Aleut, the self-designation of the people, from Russian Aleut, and language, from Latin lingua.
