fatigue duty

1. NOUN

/ fəˈtiɡ ˈduːti / (AmE), / fəˈtiːɡ ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
duties of a nonmilitary nature assigned to soldiers, such as cleaning, digging, or other manual labor, often as routine or punishment.
nonmilitary labor assigned to soldiers

Examples

After the long march, the platoon was assigned fatigue duty cleaning equipment.
Private Jones complained about having to do fatigue duty in the mess hall every morning.
The captain rotated the soldiers through various fatigue duties, including digging trenches and collecting trash.

Synset Examples

the soldiers were put on fatigue to teach them a lesson
they were assigned to kitchen fatigues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatigue - wearisome labor, especially in a military context

Etymology

From French 'fatigue' (weariness, labor) and English 'duty'.
