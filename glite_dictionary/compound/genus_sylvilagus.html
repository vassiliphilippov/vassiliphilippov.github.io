genus Sylvilagus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.vɪˌleɪ.ɡəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.vɪˌleɪ.ɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American rabbits commonly known as cottontails.
genus of North American cottontail rabbits

Examples

The genus Sylvilagus includes several species of cottontail rabbit native to North America.
Scientists conducted a study on the reproductive habits of rabbits in the genus Sylvilagus.
Unlike hares, members of the genus Sylvilagus build nests for their young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottontail - common name for rabbits in the genus Sylvilagus

Etymology

From Latin sylva (forest) + lagos (Greek for hare).
