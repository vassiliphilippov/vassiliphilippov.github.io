creeping snowberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈsnoʊˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈsnəʊˌbɛri / (BrE)
A slow-growing, low evergreen shrub native to northern North America and Japan, with white flowers and numerous white, rough, hairy seeds.
small evergreen shrub with white flowers and berries

Examples

The creeping snowberry thrives in cool, moist forests of northern North America.
Gardeners appreciate the creeping snowberry for its delicate white flowers and low maintenance needs.
During autumn, the creeping snowberry produces clusters of distinctive white berries.
