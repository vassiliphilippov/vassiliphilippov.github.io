genus Solandra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs soʊˈlændrə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs səˈlandrə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubby climbing plants native to tropical America, known for their large, trumpet-shaped flowers.
genus of tropical American climbing plants

Examples

Several species within the genus Solandra are cultivated for their striking trumpet-shaped flowers.
The genus Solandra thrives in warm, humid environments across Central and South America.
Botanists often study the genus Solandra for its unique climbing habit and ornamental value.

Etymology

Solandra: named after Daniel C. Solander (1733–1782), Swedish botanist.
