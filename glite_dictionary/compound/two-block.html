two-block

1. NOUN

/ tuː blɑk / (AmE), / tuː blɒk / (BrE)
a distance equivalent to two city blocks, used to describe how far one location is from another, especially in urban areas
distance of two city blocks

Examples

The café is located two-block down from the station.
My office is just a two-block walk from here.
They parked the car a two-block distance from the concert venue.

Synset Examples

The delivery was just two-blocks away from here.
The café is located two-blocks down from the station.
She lives only two-blocks from the main street.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: block - a distance from one cross street to the next in a city or town
  • Degree Higher: three-block - a distance of three city blocks
