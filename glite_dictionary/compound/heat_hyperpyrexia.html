heat hyperpyrexia

1. NOUN

/ hit ˌhaɪpərpaɪˈrɛksiə / (AmE), / hiːt ˌhaɪpəpaɪˈrɛksiə / (BrE)
Collapse or severe illness caused by exposure to excessively high temperatures.
collapse from excessive heat
medical

Examples

Several athletes were hospitalized with heat hyperpyrexia after running the marathon in extreme temperatures.
The risk of heat hyperpyrexia increases during heat waves and for those working outdoors.
Immediate treatment is crucial for someone showing signs of heat hyperpyrexia to prevent severe complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heatstroke - a condition caused by overheating of the body, often leading to collapse

Etymology

From 'heat' and 'hyperpyrexia', the latter from Greek 'hyper-' (above) and 'pyrexia' (fever).
