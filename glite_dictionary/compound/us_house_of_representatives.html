US House of Representatives

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː ˈɛs ˌhaʊs əv ˌrɛprɪˌzɛntəˈtɪvz / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˈɛs ˌhaʊs əv ˌrɛprɪˌzɛntəˈtɪvz / (BrE)
the lower legislative chamber of the United States Congress, consisting of elected representatives from each state, responsible for making federal laws
lower legislative chamber of US Congress
american

Examples

She was elected to the US House of Representatives at the age of 32.
The US House of Representatives passed the bill after hours of debate.
There are 435 members in the US House of Representatives, each representing a congressional district.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: US Senate - the upper legislative chamber of the United States Congress
