mast cell

1. NOUN

/ mæst sɛl / (AmE), / mɑːst sɛl / (BrE)
a type of large cell found in connective tissue that contains granules rich in histamine, heparin, and serotonin, released during allergic reactions or inflammation
cell releasing histamine in allergy/infection
medical

Examples

Mast cells play a key role in allergic reactions such as hay fever and asthma.
When activated, a mast cell releases substances that contribute to inflammation and swelling.
Scientists are studying how mast cells affect the immune response in various diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basophil - a type of white blood cell that also releases histamine during immune responses

Etymology

From German *Mastzelle* (lit. 'fattened cell'); named for their appearance in stained tissue samples.
