Holland gin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːlənd dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒlənd dʒɪn / (BrE)
A type of gin distilled in the Netherlands, particularly known for its malty flavor and traditional production methods.
Dutch-style gin; genever

Examples

Traditional cocktails sometimes call for Holland gin instead of London dry gin.
The distinctive taste of Holland gin sets it apart from other types of gin.
While traveling in Amsterdam, she sampled authentic Holland gin at a local bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genever - traditional juniper-flavored Dutch gin

Etymology

From 'Holland', the region of the Netherlands, and 'gin', shortened from 'genever', Dutch for juniper.
