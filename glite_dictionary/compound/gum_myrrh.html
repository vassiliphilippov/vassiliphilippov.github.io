gum myrrh

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ˈmɜr / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈmɜː / (BrE)
an aromatic resin obtained from certain trees, burned as incense and used in perfume
aromatic resin used in incense and perfume

Examples

The altar was fragrant with the scent of gum myrrh burning in the censer.
She mixed gum myrrh into the perfume to give it a rich, spicy aroma.
Ancient Egyptians valued gum myrrh for its use in religious ceremonies and embalming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myrrh - aromatic resin from certain trees, used in perfumes and incense
