genus Trogon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtroʊgɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtrəʊgɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Trogonidae, comprising various species of colorful arboreal birds found mainly in tropical forests.
type genus of the family Trogonidae (birds)

Examples

The genus Trogon includes several species known for their brilliant plumage and distinctive calls.
Scientists studying tropical birds often focus on members of the genus Trogon due to their ecological importance.
Many birdwatchers travel to Central America to observe the colorful species of the genus Trogon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trogonidae - family of birds to which genus Trogon belongs

Etymology

From Latin *trogon*, from Greek *trōgōn* (gnawer), referring to the birds' feeding habits.
