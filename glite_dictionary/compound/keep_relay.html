keep relay

1. NOUN

/ kip rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / kiːp rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
A relay that maintains its contact position indefinitely after the coil is de-energized, requiring no continuous power to hold the state.
relay that holds contact position without power

Examples

The technician installed a keep relay to ensure the lights stayed on during a power interruption.
Unlike standard relays, a keep relay does not consume power to maintain its state.
In industrial controls, a keep relay is often used for safety circuits requiring memory.

Synset Examples

A latching relay allows remote control of building lighting without the hum that may be produced from a continuously (AC) energized coil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latching relay - a relay that stays in the last position until triggered again
