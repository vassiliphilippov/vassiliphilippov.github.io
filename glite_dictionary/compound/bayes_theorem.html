Bayes' theorem

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪz ˌθɪərəm / (AmE), / ˈbeɪz ˌθɪərəm / (BrE)
a mathematical theorem describing how to calculate the probability of a cause based on prior knowledge and observed evidence, widely used in statistics and probability theory
theorem for updating probabilities based on evidence

Examples

Using Bayes' theorem, the analyst recalculated the likelihood of various scenarios given the new data.
Many machine learning algorithms rely on Bayes' theorem to make predictions based on incomplete information.
Understanding Bayes' theorem is essential for interpreting results in Bayesian statistics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditional probability - probability of an event given that another event has occurred

Etymology

Named after English mathematician Thomas Bayes (1701–1761), who formulated the principle.
