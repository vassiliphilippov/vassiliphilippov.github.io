heterocercal fin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈsɜr.kəl fɪn / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈsɜː.kəl fɪn / (BrE)
A tail fin in fish where the upper lobe is larger than the lower and the vertebral column extends into the upper lobe, as seen in sharks.
fish tail fin with unequal lobes

Examples

The shark’s heterocercal fin helps it maintain lift as it swims through the ocean.
Fossil evidence shows that many ancient fish species had a heterocercal fin rather than the more balanced type seen today.
A heterocercal fin is a distinguishing feature in the anatomy of many cartilaginous fish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homocercal fin - A tail fin with roughly equal-sized lobes on top and bottom

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + kerkos (tail), referring to the unequal lobes of the fin.
