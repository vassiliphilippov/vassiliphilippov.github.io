handful of

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌfʊl əv / (AmE), / ˈhænd.fʊl ɒv / (BrE)
an amount that can be held in one hand or a small number or quantity of something
a small amount or quantity, as much as a hand can hold

Examples

She grabbed a handful of sand and let it run through her fingers.
Just add a handful of chopped parsley to brighten the dish.
He offered me a handful of coins as payment for my help.

Synset Examples

He ate a handful of nut for a healthy snack.
I picked a handful of ripe raspberry from the bush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: few - a small number of
  • Antonym: lot of - a large amount or quantity of
