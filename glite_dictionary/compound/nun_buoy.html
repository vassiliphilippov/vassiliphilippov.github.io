nun buoy

1. NOUN

/ nʌn ˈbɔɪ / (AmE), / nʌn ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
A conical buoy used as a navigational marker, typically to mark channels or hazards at sea.
conical navigational buoy

Examples

The harbor was marked by a series of red nun buoys lining the channel.
Sailors are trained to recognize a nun buoy by its distinctive conical shape.
The coast guard replaced the damaged nun buoy to ensure safe navigation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: can buoy - a type of navigational buoy with a cylindrical shape

Etymology

Nun buoys are so named because their shape is reminiscent of a nun's habit.
