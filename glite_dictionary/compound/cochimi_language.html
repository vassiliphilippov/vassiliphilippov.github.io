Cochimi language

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtʃiːmi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈtʃiːmi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Yuman language once spoken by the Cochimi people of Baja California, Mexico
extinct Yuman language of Baja California

Examples

There are no fluent speakers of the Cochimi language remaining today.
Anthropologists have studied the Cochimi language to learn about the culture of the Cochimi people.
The Cochimi language is part of the larger Yuman language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of Native American languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From Spanish 'Cochimí', referring to the indigenous people of Baja California.
