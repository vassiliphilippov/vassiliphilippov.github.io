genus Lophophora

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ləˈfɒf.ə.rə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs lɒˈfɒf.ə.rə / (BrE)
A genus consisting of two species of small cacti native to northeastern Mexico and the southwestern United States, characterized by rounded stems with jointed tubercles; includes the peyote cactus.
genus of small, tubercled cacti (peyote group)

Examples

The genus Lophophora includes the well-known peyote cactus, used in traditional rituals.
Researchers discovered a new property in the alkaloids produced by members of the genus Lophophora.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting wild populations of the genus Lophophora in their native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peyote - A small, spineless cactus known for its hallucinogenic properties, scientifically named Lophophora williamsii.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek lophos (crest) and phora (bearing), referring to the plant's appearance.
