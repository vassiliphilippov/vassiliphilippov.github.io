Fauci ouchie

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊtʃi ˈaʊtʃi / (AmE), / ˈfaʊtʃi ˈaʊtʃi / (BrE)
an informal, playful term for the COVID-19 vaccine or the act of receiving a COVID-19 vaccination, often referencing a vaccination sticker
playful term for COVID-19 vaccine or shot
informal american

Examples

After getting my Fauci ouchie, I posted a selfie with my vaccination sticker.
People made memes about their Fauci ouchie on Twitter during the vaccination rollout.
She wore her Fauci ouchie sticker to encourage others to get vaccinated.

Synset Examples

Many people joked about their Fauci ouchie on social media.
She proudly showed off her Fauci ouchie sticker.
After getting the Fauci ouchie, he felt relieved.

Etymology

Blend of the surname 'Fauci' (Dr. Anthony Fauci, U.S. immunologist) and 'ouchie' (child's word for pain from a needle or injury).
