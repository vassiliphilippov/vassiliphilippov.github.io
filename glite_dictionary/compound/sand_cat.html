sand cat

1. NOUN

/ sænd kæt / (AmE), / sænd kæt / (BrE)
a small wildcat (Felis margarita) native to desert regions of Africa and Asia, adapted to live in sandy, arid environments
desert wildcat species

Examples

The sand cat is known for its ability to survive without free-standing water in the desert.
Researchers have observed the elusive sand cat hunting at night in the Sahara.
Unlike other felines, the sand cat has thick fur on its paws to protect against hot sand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildcat - a small to medium-sized wild member of the cat family
