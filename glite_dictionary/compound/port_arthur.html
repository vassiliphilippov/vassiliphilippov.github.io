Port Arthur

1. PROPN

/ pɔrt ˈɑrθɚ / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈɑːθə / (BrE)
A port in northeastern China, notable for being the site of a major battle in the First Sino-Japanese War (1894), when Japanese forces captured it from China.
Chinese port, site of 1894 battle

Examples

The capture of Port Arthur marked a turning point in the First Sino-Japanese War.
Many historians discuss the strategic importance of Port Arthur in East Asian military history.
The Japanese army faced stiff resistance while advancing toward Port Arthur.

2. PROPN

/ pɔrt ˈɑrθɚ / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈɑːθə / (BrE)
a major port city in northeastern China on the Liaodong Peninsula, historically significant and now part of the city of Luda (Dalian)
major port city in northeastern China

Examples

The siege of Port Arthur was a critical event in the Russo-Japanese War.
Many historical accounts describe the strategic importance of Port Arthur in early 20th-century conflicts.
Today, Port Arthur is part of the urban area known as Dalian.

Etymology

From English 'port' (harbor) + 'Arthur' (personal name), the city's name used in English during periods of foreign influence.
