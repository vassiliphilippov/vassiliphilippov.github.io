coming into court

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmɪŋ ˈɪntu ˈkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkʌmɪŋ ˈɪntuː ˈkɔːt / (BrE)
the formal appearance of a party before a court or at a hearing in a legal action
formal appearance in court
legal

Examples

The defendant's coming into court marked the start of the trial.
Failure in coming into court may result in a default judgment.
A lawyer can manage the process of coming into court on behalf of the client.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appearance - formal presence before a court
