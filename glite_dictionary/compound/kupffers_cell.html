Kupffer's cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊp.fɚz sɛl / (AmE), / ˈkʊp.fəz sɛl / (BrE)
a specialized cell in the liver that engulfs and destroys bacteria, foreign particles, and old blood cells
specialized immune cell in the liver
medical

Examples

Kupffer's cell plays a crucial role in filtering harmful substances from the bloodstream in the liver.
Scientists observed a significant increase in Kupffer's cell activity following liver injury.
The destruction of old red blood cells is one of the key functions of the Kupffer's cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macrophage - a type of white blood cell that engulfs and digests foreign substances

Etymology

Named after Karl Wilhelm von Kupffer, the German anatomist who discovered the cells.
