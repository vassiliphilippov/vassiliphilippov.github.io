heat of condensation

1. NOUN

/ hit ʌv ˌkɑndənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv ˌkɒndənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount of heat released when a unit mass of a gas at its boiling point condenses to a liquid
heat released during condensation

Examples

The heat of condensation plays a vital role in weather phenomena like cloud formation.
Engineers calculate the heat of condensation when designing efficient heat exchangers.
The heat of condensation must be considered when steam condenses on cold surfaces.

Synset Examples

the heat of condensation is equal to the heat of vaporization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heat of vaporization - the quantity of heat required to convert a unit mass of liquid into vapor without a temperature change
