Bloody Mary

1. PROPN

/ ˈblʌdi ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi ˈmeəri / (BrE)
Queen Mary I of England (1516–1558), known for her persecution of Protestants and restoration of Roman Catholicism during her reign from 1553 to 1558.
Queen Mary I of England, 1516–1558

Examples

Queen Bloody Mary is remembered for her attempt to restore Catholicism in England.
During the reign of Bloody Mary, many Protestants were executed for heresy.
The legacy of Bloody Mary remains controversial among historians today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mary I - the same monarch, Queen of England from 1553 to 1558
  • Antonym: Elizabeth I - Queen of England who succeeded Mary I

Etymology

Nickname from her violent persecution of Protestants during her reign.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdi ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi ˈmɛːri / (BrE)
a cocktail made with vodka, tomato juice, and various spices and flavorings, often garnished with celery
cocktail with vodka and tomato juice

Examples

She ordered a Bloody Mary at brunch to go with her eggs benedict.
The bartender mixed a spicy Bloody Mary with extra horseradish for the customer.
A traditional garnish for a Bloody Mary is a stalk of celery or a slice of lemon.

Etymology

Named after Mary I of England (1516–1558), nicknamed 'Bloody Mary' due to the execution of Protestants during her reign.
