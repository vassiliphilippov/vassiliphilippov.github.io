pelvic girdle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlvɪk ˈɡɜrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɛlvɪk ˈɡɜːdl̩ / (BrE)
the set of bones forming the structure that supports the lower limbs in humans and the hind limbs in other vertebrates
bone structure supporting lower limbs
medical

Examples

The fracture in her pelvic girdle required immediate medical attention.
During evolution, the pelvic girdle of bipedal animals adapted for upright walking.
The anatomy textbook contains detailed diagrams of the pelvic girdle and its connections to the spine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvis - the lower part of the trunk in the human body

Etymology

From Latin pelv- (basin, relating to the pelvis) + girdle (Old English gyrdel).
