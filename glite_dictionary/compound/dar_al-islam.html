Dar al-Islam

1. NOUN

/ dɑɹ æl ɪsˈlɑm / (AmE), / dɑː æl ɪsˈlɑːm / (BrE)
the part of the world under Islamic rule or where Muslims are in the majority
regions under Islamic rule or majority

Examples

Throughout history, the borders of the Dar al-Islam have shifted with changing empires and treaties.
The scholar explained that Muslims living outside the Dar al-Islam might face different religious obligations.
Many travelers in the Middle Ages sought safe passage within the Dar al-Islam for religious and commercial reasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Dar al-Harb - areas not under Islamic rule

Etymology

From Arabic دار الإسلام‎ (dār al-islām), literally “house/abode of Islam”.
