Lord of the Flies

1. NOUN

/ lɔrd əv ðə flaɪz / (AmE), / lɔːd əv ðə flaɪz / (BrE)
A reference to the novel by William Golding, often used to symbolize chaos, savagery, or the breakdown of societal order.
symbol of chaos or savagery (from Golding's novel)

Examples

Many critics use Lord of the Flies to analyze the darkest aspects of human nature.
The classroom descended into a Lord of the Flies scenario when the teacher left.
In politics, some warn against a Lord of the Flies situation if rules are ignored.

Synset Examples

In discussions, we often reference the lord of the flies for its social commentary.
The children in the story became the lord of the flies on the island.
That novel explores the theme of chaos, like the lord of the flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Beelzebub - a demon, sometimes referred to as 'lord of the flies'
  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is in its correct place

Etymology

From the title of the 1954 novel by William Golding; itself a translation of 'Beelzebub' from Hebrew, meaning 'lord of the flies.'
