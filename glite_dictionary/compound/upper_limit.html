upper limit

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ ˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
the highest possible or allowed point, value, or boundary of something
the highest possible or allowed boundary

Examples

There is an upper limit to the amount of sugar you should eat each day.
The speedometer shows the upper limit of the car's speed capacity.
Scientists established an upper limit for safe radiation exposure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lower limit - the lowest possible or allowed point, value, or boundary of something

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ ˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
the maximum quantity, level, or value that is allowed or possible
maximum allowed or possible amount

Examples

The scientists set an upper limit for acceptable radiation exposure.
You cannot exceed the upper limit of 100 participants in the study.
If the temperature goes above the upper limit, the experiment must be stopped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maximum - the greatest quantity or highest degree possible
  • Antonym: lower limit - the minimum quantity or value that is allowed or possible
