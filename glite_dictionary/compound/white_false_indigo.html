white false indigo

1. NOUN

/ waɪt fɔls ˈɪndoʊ / (AmE), / waɪt fɔːls ˈɪndɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
an erect or spreading perennial herb native to the eastern United States, with racemes of creamy white flowers, belonging to the genus Baptisia.
North American herb with white flowers

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a blooming white false indigo that attracted many bees.
She planted white false indigo along the fence to create a natural border with summer interest.
Botanists in the region frequently encounter white false indigo in open meadows and woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baptisia - genus of plants including wild indigos and false indigos
  • Degree Higher: blue false indigo - related herb with blue flowers
