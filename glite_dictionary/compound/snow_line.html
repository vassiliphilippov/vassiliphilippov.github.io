snow line

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊ ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊ ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the imaginary line on a mountain or in a region above which snow remains on the ground throughout the year
altitude above which snow is perennial

Examples

Climate change is causing the snow line to retreat higher up the mountains each year.
Above the snow line, only hardy plants and lichens manage to survive.
The hikers stopped to rest just below the snow line where the air was noticeably cooler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree line - altitude above which trees do not grow
