US Trade Representative

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː ˈɛs treɪd ˌɹɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˈɛs treɪd ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv / (BrE)
the government official and agency responsible for developing and coordinating the United States' international trade, commodity, and direct investment policy, and overseeing trade negotiations with other countries
US official/agency handling international trade policy
american

Examples

The US Trade Representative announced a new round of talks with China regarding tariffs.
Each year, the US Trade Representative submits a report to Congress detailing trade policy objectives.
The US Trade Representative is often involved in negotiating complex trade agreements on behalf of the United States.
