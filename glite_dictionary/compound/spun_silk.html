spun silk

1. NOUN

/ spʌn sɪlk / (AmE), / spʌn sɪlk / (BrE)
yarn made from the shorter fibers or waste produced during silk manufacturing, rather than from the long, continuous filaments of raw silk.
yarn made from silk waste

Examples

The dress was made from fine spun silk that felt soft against her skin.
Factories often use spun silk to create affordable silk blends for clothing.
Unlike expensive raw silk, spun silk is valued for its durability and matte texture.

Synset Examples

It is the spinner's business to straighten out these fibres, with the aid of machinery, and then to so join them that they become a thread, which is known as spun silk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raw silk - silk in its natural state as produced by the silkworm, before processing
