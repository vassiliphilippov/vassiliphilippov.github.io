primary school

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri skuːl / (AmE), / ˈpraɪməri skuːl / (BrE)
a school for young children, typically between the ages of 5 and 11, where the first stages of formal education are provided
elementary school for young children

Examples

All children in the area attend the local primary school.
She has taught at a primary school for ten years.
In the UK, children start primary school at the age of five.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elementary school - a school for the first part of a child's education, typically ages 5–11
