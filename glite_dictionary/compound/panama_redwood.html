Panama redwood

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænəmə ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˌpænəmə ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
a large shrub native to Colombia with large pinnate leaves and clusters of fragrant yellow flowers
shrub with yellow flowers, native to Colombia

Examples

The botanical garden features a stunning Panama redwood in its South American collection.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of extracts taken from the Panama redwood.
The Panama redwood is prized for its tall stature and showy yellow flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpænəˌmɑː ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˌpænəmə ˈrɛdwʊd / (BrE)
A hard, heavy reddish wood obtained from a quira tree, valued for its strength.
hard red wood from a quira tree

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Panama redwood, giving it a distinctive deep color and durability.
Carpenters prize Panama redwood for its exceptional hardness and resistance to decay.
Export regulations restrict the logging of Panama redwood due to its ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quira - a tropical tree whose wood is called Panama redwood
