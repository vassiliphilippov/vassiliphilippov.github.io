mineral jelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
a semisolid mixture of hydrocarbons derived from petroleum, used in medicinal ointments and as a lubricant
semisolid petroleum-based mixture

Examples

He applied a layer of mineral jelly to the cut to keep it moist.
Mineral jelly is commonly found in many household ointments and moisturizers.
The mechanic recommended using mineral jelly to lubricate the machine's moving parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petroleum jelly - a semisolid mixture of hydrocarbons used as ointment or lubricant
