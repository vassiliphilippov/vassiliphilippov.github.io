broom snakeroot

1. NOUN

/ bruːm ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / bruːm ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
A low-growing, sticky subshrub of the southwestern United States, characterized by narrow linear leaves, many slender branches, and numerous tiny yellow flower heads.
Southwestern U.S. subshrub with yellow flower heads

Examples

Broom snakeroot is often found growing in dry, sandy soils across the American Southwest.
Botanists are studying the sticky leaves of broom snakeroot to understand its adaptation to arid environments.
The yellow flower heads of broom snakeroot make it easy to spot in late summer.
