to feel a hypo

1. VERB

/ fil ə ˈhaɪpoʊ / (AmE), / fiːl ə ˈhaɪpəʊ / (BrE)
to experience an episode of low blood sugar (hypoglycemia), often characterized by symptoms such as shakiness, sweating, or confusion
experience low blood sugar
informal medical

Examples

After missing lunch, he suddenly felt a hypo coming on and needed to eat something quickly.
She always carries glucose tablets in case she feels a hypo during the day.
If you feel a hypo, it's important to treat it right away to prevent complications.

Synset Examples

He felt a hypo after skipping breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypoglycemia - abnormally low levels of sugar in the blood

Etymology

Shortened from 'hypoglycemia', with 'hypo' used in medical slang for low blood sugar episodes.
