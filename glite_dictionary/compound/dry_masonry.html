dry masonry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˈmeɪsən.i / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˈmɑːsən.i / (BrE)
A method of building structures, especially walls, by laying stones without using mortar to bind them together.
masonry built without mortar

Examples

Traditional mountain fences were often built using dry masonry methods.
The retaining wall, constructed of dry masonry, has stood for over a century.
Restorers aimed to preserve the original dry masonry in the ancient ruins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry stone - construction technique using stones without mortar
