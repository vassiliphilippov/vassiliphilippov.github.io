fundamental frequency

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
the lowest frequency of a periodic waveform, determining the pitch of a sound; the first and main tone in a harmonic series
lowest frequency in a harmonic series

Examples

The fundamental frequency of a vibrating string determines the note that is heard.
Scientists measured the fundamental frequency of the sound to analyze the vocalization.
When tuning an instrument, musicians often listen for the fundamental frequency to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonic - an overtone whose frequency is a whole-number multiple of the fundamental frequency
