calcium blocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm ˈblɑː.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.si.əm ˈblɒ.kə / (BrE)
a drug that reduces the movement of calcium into heart and blood vessel muscle cells, used to treat conditions such as high blood pressure, angina, or arrhythmias
drug that blocks calcium flow in cells
medical

Examples

His doctor prescribed a calcium blocker to help control his high blood pressure.
The patient responded well to the calcium blocker during treatment for irregular heart rhythms.
Some migraine sufferers find that a calcium blocker can reduce the frequency of their headaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcium channel blocker - drug that blocks calcium channels, same class as calcium blocker
