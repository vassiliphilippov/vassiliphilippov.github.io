Acherontic theme

1. NOUN

/ ˌækəˈrɑːntɪk θiːm / (AmE), / ˌakəˈrɒntɪk θiːm / (BrE)
A theme in literature or art characterized by profound sorrow, gloom, or darkness, often evoking images of the underworld.
dark or sorrowful theme

Examples

The novel's Acherontic theme is reflected in its portrayal of lost hope and endless night.
Many critics noted the Acherontic theme running through the artist's latest series of paintings.
Despite the Acherontic themes, the opera ends with a glimmer of redemption.

Synset Examples

His Acherontic themes explored the depths of despair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness
  • Antonym: cheerful - full of happiness or joy

Etymology

From Greek *Acheron*, a river in the underworld, symbolic of death and sorrow, with the English suffix -ic.
