common stock equivalent

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən stɑk ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən stɒk ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
a security, such as preferred stock, convertible bonds, or warrants, that can be converted into common stock of a company
security convertible into common stock

Examples

The company issued common stock equivalents to attract more investors and improve its capital structure.
When analyzing earnings per share, accountants must factor in outstanding common stock equivalents.
Convertible bonds are a type of common stock equivalent that can potentially dilute existing shareholders' ownership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convertible security - a security that can be converted into another form, usually shares of common stock
