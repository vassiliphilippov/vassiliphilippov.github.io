massasauga rattler

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈsɔɡə ˈrætlɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈsɔːɡə ˈrætlə / (BrE)
A small species of rattlesnake (Sistrurus catenatus) found in moist habitats from the Great Lakes region to Mexico, noted for its venomous bite.
a pygmy rattlesnake species of North America

Examples

The massasauga rattler is rarely seen but plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
Park rangers warned hikers to watch out for the massasauga rattler in the wetlands.
Despite its reputation, the massasauga rattler is generally shy and avoids human contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a rattle at the end of its tail

Etymology

From Ojibwe *massa:sa:ga:*, meaning 'great river-mouth', used to name the region where the snake was first identified.
