resplendent quetzal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsplɛndənt kɛtˈsɑl / (AmE), / rɪˈsplɛndənt kɛtˈsɑːl / (BrE)
a rare, brightly colored bird (Pharomachrus mocinno) native to Central America, known for its striking green and red plumage and long tail feathers; the national bird of Guatemala
rare Central American bird; Guatemala's national bird

Examples

The resplendent quetzal is admired for its brilliant green and red feathers.
Birdwatchers travel to Costa Rica in hopes of spotting a resplendent quetzal in the cloud forests.
The resplendent quetzal features prominently on the Guatemalan flag and coat of arms.

Etymology

Quetzal from Nahuatl *quetzalli* (tail feather of the quetzal); 'resplendent' from Latin *resplendentem* (shining brightly).
