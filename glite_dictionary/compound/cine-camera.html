cine-camera

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪni ˌkæmərə / (AmE), / ˈsɪni ˌkæmərə / (BrE)
a camera designed to take a sequence of photographs on film for motion pictures, creating the illusion of movement when played back rapidly
motion picture film camera

Examples

He used an old cine-camera to film his family's holiday in black and white.
The museum displays a vintage cine-camera from the early days of filmmaking.
Before digital technology, every movie was shot with a cine-camera using reels of film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movie camera - a camera for photographing moving images on film; synonym for cine-camera
  • Degree Higher: camcorder - an electronic device for recording video and audio digitally; modern counterpart

Etymology

From 'cine', short for 'cinematograph', from Greek 'kinein' (to move), and 'camera', from Latin 'camera' (chamber).
