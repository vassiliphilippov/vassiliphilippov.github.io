optical density

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl dɛnˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl dɛnˈsɪti / (BrE)
a measure of the degree to which a substance reduces the intensity of light or other electromagnetic radiation passing through it
measure of light absorption in a material

Examples

The optical density of the sample was measured using a spectrophotometer.
A higher concentration of pigment results in a greater optical density.
Filters with high optical density block most of the incident light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbance - the amount of light absorbed by a sample
  • Antonym: transmittance - the fraction of light passing through a substance

Etymology

From 'optic' (relating to sight or light) and 'density'; used in physics from early 20th century.
