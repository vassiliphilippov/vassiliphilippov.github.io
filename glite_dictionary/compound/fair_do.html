fair do

1. NOUN

/ fɛr duː / (AmE), / feə duː / (BrE)
used to express that someone deserves fair treatment or acknowledgment for their actions or contributions
fair treatment; due acknowledgment
informal british

Examples

I have to say, fair do to Sam for handling the project so well.
It’s only fair do to give her credit for the idea.
If he apologizes, then that’s fair do and we should accept it.

Synset Examples

In this situation, fair dos means acknowledging everyone's contributions.
I think it’s only fair dos to give them credit for their work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fair play - respect for rules or equal treatment in competition or life
