speech intelligibility

1. NOUN

/ spiːtʃ ɪnˌtɛlədʒəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / spiːtʃ ɪnˌtɛlɪdʒəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which spoken language can be understood, especially when there is background noise or distortion
clarity of spoken language

Examples

The speech intelligibility in the crowded restaurant was poor, making it hard to follow conversations.
Engineers tested the speech intelligibility of the new public address system in the subway.
Hearing aids are designed to improve speech intelligibility for people with hearing loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligibility - the quality of being understandable
