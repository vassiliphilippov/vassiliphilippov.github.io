neutral spirits

1. NOUN

/ ˈnutɹəl ˈspɪɹɪts / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtɹəl ˈspɪɹɪts / (BrE)
A highly concentrated alcoholic distillate, usually at 95 percent alcohol by volume (190 proof), that is tasteless and colorless and used for blending with other spirits or in the production of gin and liqueurs.
highly pure, flavorless alcohol used in blending spirits

Examples

The distillery uses neutral spirits as a base for making its flavored vodkas.
Many types of gin are made by redistilling neutral spirits with botanicals.
When blending whiskey, small amounts of neutral spirits can adjust the final taste and strength.

Etymology

From 'neutral' (not having distinct flavor) + 'spirits' (distilled alcoholic liquors).
