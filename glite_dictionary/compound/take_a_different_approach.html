to take a different approach

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈdɪf(ə)rənt əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈdɪf(ə)r(ə)nt əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to adopt a new method, strategy, or way of handling a situation, especially when previous attempts have failed or proved unsatisfactory
try a new method or strategy

Examples

After several failed attempts, we decided to take a different approach to the project.
If negotiations stall, it may be wise to take a different approach to reach an agreement.
Sometimes you need to take a different approach when old solutions no longer work.

Synset Examples

They decided to take a different approach this time.
Let’s try a different approach to solve this problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change tactic - to alter one's strategy or method
  • Antonym: stick to the same approach - to maintain the current method or strategy
