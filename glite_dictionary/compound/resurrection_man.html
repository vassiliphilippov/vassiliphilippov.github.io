resurrection man

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən mæn / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən mæn / (BrE)
a person who steals dead bodies from graves, especially for medical study or dissection
person who steals corpses for dissection

Examples

The notorious resurrection man prowled cemeteries after dark, searching for fresh graves.
In Victorian England, a resurrection man could earn a living selling bodies to medical schools.
The character was rumored to be a resurrection man, stealing corpses for profit.

Synset Examples

The story featured a notorious resurrection man from history.
The novel's villain was a clever resurrection man.
He was labeled a resurrection man for stealing bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body snatcher - a person who steals corpses from graves

Etymology

From 'resurrection' (rising from the dead) and 'man'; historical term linked to medical body snatching in 18th–19th centuries.
