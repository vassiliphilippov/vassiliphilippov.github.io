dashed hope

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæʃt hoʊp / (AmE), / ˈdæʃt həʊp / (BrE)
a hope or expectation that has been destroyed or disappointed
a ruined or disappointed hope

Examples

The latest setback was yet another dashed hope in her search for a new job.
Years of hard work ended in dashed hopes after the team lost the final match.
He tried not to dwell on his dashed hope of ever owning his own business.

Synset Examples

The bad news dashed her hopes for a promotion.
The storm dashed their hopes for an outdoor wedding.
His comments dashed my hopes of a peaceful resolution.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hope - a feeling of desire for a certain thing to happen
  • Synonym: wish - a strong desire or hope for something not easily attainable
  • Antonym: despair - complete loss or absence of hope
