marsh gentian

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈdʒɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈdʒɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
a perennial plant of damp, open heaths in Eurasia, with sky-blue, funnel-shaped flowers; Gentiana pneumonanthe
a Eurasian plant with blue flowers

Examples

The marsh gentian blooms early in the summer across the heathlands.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the rare marsh gentian.
We spotted a beautiful marsh gentian while hiking near the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - a plant of the genus Gentiana, typically with blue flowers

Etymology

From 'marsh' (a wetland) and 'gentian', from Latin 'gentiana', the name of the plant family.
