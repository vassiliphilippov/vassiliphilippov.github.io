Siberian pea tree

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈbɪriən pi ˌtri / (AmE), / sɪˈbɪəriən pi ˌtriː / (BrE)
A large spiny shrub native to eastern Asia, with clusters of yellow flowers, commonly grown in shelterbelts and hedges.
spiny Asian shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The Siberian pea tree is often planted as a windbreak in rural gardens.
Clusters of yellow flowers cover the Siberian pea tree each spring, attracting pollinators.
Farmers value the Siberian pea tree for its hardiness and decorative appearance.
