cracked wheat

1. NOUN

/ krækt wiːt / (AmE), / krækt wiːt / (BrE)
wheat grains that have been crushed into small pieces, typically used in cooking and baking.
crushed wheat grains used in cooking

Examples

The recipe calls for a cup of cracked wheat to give the salad its hearty texture.
Some breads are made with cracked wheat to add extra fiber and a nutty flavor.
You can find cracked wheat in the bulk food section of most grocery stores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulgur - parboiled cracked wheat often used in Middle Eastern cuisine
