Daphne laureola

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæfni lɔːˈriːələ / (AmE), / ˈdæfni lɔːˈriːələ / (BrE)
a bushy Eurasian shrub with glossy, leathery, oblong leaves and yellow-green flowers, often found in woodlands
a Eurasian shrub with glossy leaves and yellow-green flowers

Examples

The woodland was dotted with the glossy leaves of Daphne laureola.
Gardeners sometimes grow Daphne laureola for its attractive foliage.
Daphne laureola can be toxic if ingested by humans or animals.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Daphne' after the mythological nymph Daphne, 'laureola' meaning 'little laurel'.
