chinquapin oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪŋkwəpɪn oʊk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪŋkwəpɪn əʊk / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree (Quercus muehlenbergii) of the eastern United States, valued for its strong durable wood.
deciduous oak tree of eastern US

Examples

The leaves of the chinquapin oak are similar in shape to those of the chestnut.
A mature chinquapin oak can provide significant shade in a woodland garden.
Carpenters prize the wood of the chinquapin oak for furniture and flooring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree in the genus Quercus

Etymology

Chinquapin from Virginia Algonquian *chechinquamin* (a type of nut), referencing similarity to the chinquapin chestnut; oak from Old English *āc*.
