left stage

1. NOUN

/ lɛft steɪdʒ / (AmE), / lɛft steɪdʒ / (BrE)
The part of a theatre stage that is to the left of an actor facing the audience.
area of stage to actor's left

Examples

The main character entered from left stage and paused in the spotlight.
You should exit left stage after your final line in Act II.
The director asked the dancer to wait at left stage before her solo began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage left - the left side of the stage as viewed by the actor facing the audience
  • Antonym: right stage - the part of the stage on the actor's right as the actor faces the audience
