prairie coneflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈkoʊnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri ˈkəʊnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a North American plant of the daisy family, with drooping yellow or red petals and a prominent cone-shaped center, found from British Columbia to New Mexico
a North American coneflower plant

Examples

The prairie coneflower blooms beautifully in summer meadows across North America.
Gardeners often plant prairie coneflowers to attract bees and butterflies.
We spotted a cluster of prairie coneflower along the hiking trail in New Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican hat - a similar coneflower plant species

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈkoʊnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈkəʊnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a tall, yellow-flowered coneflower native to central and southwestern regions of the United States
yellow-flowered plant native to central/southwestern U.S.

Examples

The prairie coneflower blooms in mid-summer across the grasslands of Texas and Oklahoma.
Gardeners often choose prairie coneflower for its drought tolerance and bright yellow petals.
A field full of prairie coneflowers creates a striking landscape during the hottest months of the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coneflower - any of several plants with cone-shaped flower heads
