devil dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛv.əl dɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈdɛv.əl dɒɡ / (BrE)
a nickname for a member of the United States Marine Corps, especially noted for bravery and toughness
US Marine Corps member (nickname)

Examples

Ever since he became a devil dog, his discipline and determination have inspired others.
The film tells the story of a group of devil dogs facing impossible odds during the war.
She proudly displayed the photograph of her grandfather in his devil dog uniform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marine - a member of a military force trained for service at sea and on land

Etymology

Said to originate from the German 'Teufelshunde', a nickname allegedly given to US Marines by German soldiers during World War I.
