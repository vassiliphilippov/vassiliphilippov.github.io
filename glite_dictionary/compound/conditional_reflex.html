conditional reflex

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənəl rɪˈflɛks / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
An automatic, learned response that occurs in reaction to a specific stimulus as a result of prior conditioning.
a learned automatic response to a stimulus

Examples

The dog's salivation when hearing the bell is a classic example of a conditional reflex.
With enough repetition, a conditional reflex can become almost automatic in response to the stimulus.
Researchers studied how quickly a conditional reflex develops in young children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditioned response - a learned response to a conditioned stimulus
  • Antonym: unconditioned reflex - an inborn, automatic response to a stimulus

Etymology

From Latin 'conditio' (condition, agreement) and Latin 'reflexus' (bent back).
