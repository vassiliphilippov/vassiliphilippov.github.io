Wesleyan Methodists

1. PROPN

/ ˌwɛzliən ˈmɛθədɪsts / (AmE), / ˌwɛzliən ˈmɛθədɪsts / (BrE)
members of the original Methodist Church founded on the teachings of John Wesley; specifically, the Methodist tradition emphasizing Wesley's doctrines and practices.
members of the original Methodist Church

Examples

The Wesleyan Methodists played an important role in the religious revival of 18th-century England.
Many Wesleyan Methodists were active in social reform movements, including the abolition of slavery.
My great-grandparents were devout Wesleyan Methodists who attended church every Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Methodists - members of the Methodist Church
  • Antonym: Anglicans - members of the Anglican Church

Etymology

From the name of John Wesley (1703–1791), founder of Methodism, plus Methodist.
