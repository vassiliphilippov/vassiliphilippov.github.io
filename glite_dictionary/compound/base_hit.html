base hit

1. NOUN

/ beɪs hɪt / (AmE), / beɪs hɪt / (BrE)
A successful act in baseball where the batter strikes the ball and reaches base safely without an error or fielder's choice.
successful hit allowing reach of base (baseball)

Examples

The player celebrated his first base hit of the season with a smile.
Jackson’s base hit in the eighth inning broke the tie.
She led the team with three base hits in yesterday’s game.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single - a base hit that allows the batter to reach first base
  • Degree Higher: double - a base hit that allows the batter to reach second base
  • Degree Higher: triple - a base hit that allows the batter to reach third base
  • Degree Higher: home run - a base hit where the batter rounds all bases to score
