rules lawyer

1. NOUN

/ ruːlz ˈlɔɪ.ər / (AmE), / ruːlz ˈlɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who insists on the strict application of rules or laws, often to gain an advantage or to point out technicalities, sometimes to the detriment of common sense or enjoyment
person who insists on strict interpretation of rules

Examples

As a rules lawyer, she often complicates simple situations that should be straightforward.
The rules lawyer kept interrupting the game to point out every minor infraction.
No one enjoys playing with a rules lawyer who takes the fun out of friendly competition.

Synset Examples

As a rules lawyer, she often complicates simple situations.
He acted like a rules lawyer, citing clauses during negotiations.
The rules lawyer made the game far less enjoyable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedant - a person excessively concerned with minor details or rules
  • Synonym: stickler - a person who insists on a certain quality or type of behavior, especially adherence to rules
