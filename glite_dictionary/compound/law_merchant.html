law merchant

1. NOUN

/ lɔː ˈmɜr.tʃənt / (AmE), / lɔː ˈmɜː.tʃənt / (BrE)
a body of rules governing commercial transactions, originating from the customs and practices of merchants rather than formal legal systems
rules for commercial transactions from merchant customs
legal

Examples

Many medieval courts recognized the law merchant as a separate body of rules for resolving trade disputes.
The adoption of the law merchant helped standardize international commerce in the Middle Ages.
Modern commercial law has its roots in the principles of the law merchant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercial law - the body of law that governs business and commercial transactions

Etymology

From Old French 'loi marchande' (merchant law), relating to customary rules among medieval European merchants.
