gymnospermous yellowwood

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nəˌspɝː.məs ˈjɛl.oʊˌwʊd / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˌspɜː.məs ˈjɛl.əʊˌwʊd / (BrE)
Any of various gymnospermous trees that have yellow wood.
gymnosperm tree with yellow wood

Examples

The gymnospermous yellowwood is prized for its durable timber in some regions.
Botanists studied the characteristics of a gymnospermous yellowwood in the forest.
Many gymnospermous yellowwoods are important members of ancient ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek *gymnos* (naked) + *sperma* (seed) and English 'yellowwood'.
