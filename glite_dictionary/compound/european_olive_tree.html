European olive tree

1. NOUN

/ juˌroʊpiən ˈɑlɪv tri / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiən ˈɒlɪv triː / (BrE)
an evergreen tree (Olea europaea) native to the Mediterranean region and widely cultivated elsewhere for its edible fruit and oil
Mediterranean tree grown for olives and oil

Examples

The ancient European olive tree in their yard is believed to be over three hundred years old.
Farmers across Spain planted rows of European olive trees to harvest olives for oil production.
The European olive tree thrives in dry, rocky soil and warm, sunny climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive - the small oval fruit of the olive tree, eaten as food or used for oil
