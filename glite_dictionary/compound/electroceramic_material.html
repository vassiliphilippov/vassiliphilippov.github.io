electroceramic material

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊsəˈræmɪk məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊsəˈræmɪk məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a ceramic material with electrical, optical, or magnetic properties, used in insulators, electronic components, and similar applications
ceramic with functional electrical or magnetic properties
medical

Examples

Advances in electroceramic materials have led to more efficient capacitors and sensors.
Researchers are developing new electroceramic material compositions for high-frequency applications.
You can find electroceramic materials in a range of electronic devices, from smartphones to medical imaging equipment.

Synset Examples

Examples of electroceramic materials include: ferroelectrics — high dielectric capacitors, non-volatile memories; ferrites — data and information storage; solid electrolytes — energy storage and conversion; piezoelectrics — sonar; semiconducting oxides — environmental monitoring.

Etymology

From 'electro-' (relating to electricity) + 'ceramic' (from Greek keramikos, pottery).
