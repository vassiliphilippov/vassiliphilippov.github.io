Admiralty brass

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.mɚ.əl.ti ˈbræs / (AmE), / ˌæd.mɪ.rəl.ti ˈbrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of brass alloy containing copper, zinc, and tin, known for its resistance to sea water corrosion.
a corrosion-resistant brass alloy

Examples

Shipbuilders often choose Admiralty brass for condenser tubes because of its superior resistance to seawater.
The chemical composition of Admiralty brass includes tin for increased corrosion resistance.
Our supplier recommended Admiralty brass due to its durability in marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brass - a metal alloy of copper and zinc

Etymology

Named after the British Admiralty, which used this alloy in naval applications.
