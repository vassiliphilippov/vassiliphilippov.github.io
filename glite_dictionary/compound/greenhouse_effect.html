greenhouse effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnˌhaʊs ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌhaʊs ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
the warming of the Earth's atmosphere that occurs when certain gases trap heat from solar radiation, preventing it from escaping into space
atmospheric warming caused by trapped heat

Examples

Scientists warn that the greenhouse effect is a significant factor in climate change.
Some gases, such as carbon dioxide and methane, contribute to the greenhouse effect.
Without the greenhouse effect, the Earth would be too cold to support most current forms of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: global warming - a gradual increase in the average temperature of the Earth's atmosphere

Etymology

Named by analogy to the heating in a greenhouse, where glass allows sunlight in but traps heat.
