grand total

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈtoʊt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈtəʊtəl / (BrE)
the final sum obtained by adding the totals of several groups of numbers together
the final overall sum

Examples

The grand total for the evening came to nearly $5,000.
After counting all the receipts, the grand total was confirmed by the accountant.
If you add up each section's expenses, the grand total exceeds expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: total - the whole number or amount

2. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈtoʊt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈtəʊtəl / (BrE)
the final amount reached after all additions and deductions have been made
final sum after all calculations

Examples

After adding the taxes and discounts, the grand total came to exactly $75.
The grand total of votes was announced at the end of the night.
We waited for the cashier to reveal the grand total before splitting the bill.

Synset Examples

He calculated the grand total before finalizing the order.
The grand total of the bill was surprisingly low.
