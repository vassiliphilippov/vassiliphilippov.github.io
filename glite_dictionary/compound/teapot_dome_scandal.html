Teapot Dome scandal

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːˌpɑt doʊm ˈskændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌpɒt dəʊm ˈskændl̩ / (BrE)
A major U.S. political scandal in the early 1920s, in which government officials secretly leased federal oil reserves in Wyoming to private companies, resulting in widespread corruption during the Harding administration.
U.S. political scandal (1920s oil leases)

Examples

The Teapot Dome scandal severely damaged public trust in the Harding administration.
Historians often cite the Teapot Dome scandal as one of the greatest political corruption cases in U.S. history.
The exposure of the Teapot Dome scandal led to several government reforms on handling public resources.

Etymology

Named after Teapot Dome, a geological formation in Wyoming where the scandal's oil reserve was located.
