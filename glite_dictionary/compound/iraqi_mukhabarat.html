Iraqi Mukhabarat

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈrɑki muːˈxɑːbɑːˌrɑt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɑːki mʊˈxɑːbərɑːt / (BrE)
the intelligence service and secret police agency of Iraq, especially during Saddam Hussein's regime, known for its role in state security and covert operations
Iraq's former intelligence agency

Examples

The Iraqi Mukhabarat operated with secrecy and was feared by many citizens.
International reports have accused the Iraqi Mukhabarat of various human rights abuses during the 1980s and 1990s.
After the fall of Saddam Hussein, documents from the Iraqi Mukhabarat were made public.

Synset Examples

the Iraqi Mukhabarat has been involved in numerous terrorist activities

Etymology

Arabic مُخَابَرَات‎ (mukhābarāt, 'intelligence services'), from the root kh-b-r relating to news or information.
