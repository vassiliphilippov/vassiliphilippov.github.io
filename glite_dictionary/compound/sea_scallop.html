sea scallop

1. NOUN

/ si ˈskæləp / (AmE), / si ˈskæləp / (BrE)
a large edible marine scallop (Placopecten magellanicus), especially the adductor muscle of this species, often used as food
large edible marine scallop

Examples

Many seafood restaurants serve grilled sea scallop as a delicacy.
She ordered a dish made with fresh sea scallop and lemon sauce.
The sea scallop is prized for its large, tender muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - an edible marine bivalve mollusc with a radially ribbed shell

2. NOUN

/ si ˈskæləp / (AmE), / si ˈskælɒp / (BrE)
a large scallop found in deep waters of the Atlantic coast of North America, valued for its edible muscle
large Atlantic edible scallop

Examples

The chef pan-seared the sea scallop and served it with lemon butter sauce.
Fishermen in New England harvest sea scallops from offshore beds every season.
Sea scallops are larger and meatier than their bay scallop cousins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - a marine bivalve mollusk with a fan-shaped shell
