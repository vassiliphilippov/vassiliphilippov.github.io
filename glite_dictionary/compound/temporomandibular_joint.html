temporomandibular joint

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛmpəroʊˌmændɪˈbjʊlər dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəʊˌmændɪˈbjuːlə dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
the joint connecting the lower jawbone (mandible) to the temporal bone of the skull, enabling movement of the jaw
jaw joint connecting mandible and skull
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that my jaw pain was due to inflammation in the temporomandibular joint.
Injuries to the temporomandibular joint can cause difficulty in chewing and speaking.
A clicking sound when opening the mouth may indicate a problem with the temporomandibular joint.

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (temple) + 'mandibula' (jaw) + 'joint'.
