2019-nCoV acute respiratory disease

1. NOUN

/ tu ˈθaʊzənd ˈnaɪnti ˈɛn ˈsiː ˈoʊ ˈviː əˈkjuːt rɪˌspɪrəˈtɔːri dɪˈziz / (AmE), / tuː ˈθaʊzənd ˈnaɪntiːn ˈɛn ˈsiː ˈəʊ ˈviː əˈkjuːt rɪˌspɪrəˈtɔːri dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a viral respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2, responsible for the global pandemic in 2020; also known as COVID-19
viral disease caused by SARS-CoV-2
medical

Examples

The onset of 2019-nCoV acute respiratory disease led to widespread public health measures worldwide.
Many early cases of 2019-nCoV acute respiratory disease were linked to travel from Wuhan, China.
Doctors are researching effective treatments for patients suffering from 2019-nCoV acute respiratory disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: COVID-19 - the disease caused by SARS-CoV-2, responsible for the 2020 pandemic

Etymology

2019-nCoV abbreviates '2019 novel coronavirus'; the term was used before the official naming of COVID-19.
