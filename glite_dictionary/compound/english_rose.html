English rose

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ roʊz / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ rəʊz / (BrE)
a woman regarded as a classic example of English beauty, typically having a fair complexion and delicate features
traditional English beauty (woman)

Examples

She was the very image of an English rose, with her fair skin and gentle manner.
Many consider the actress to be an English rose because of her timeless elegance.
His poem was dedicated to an English rose who had captured his heart.

Synset Examples

Her grace and beauty made her the English rose of the ball.
She wore a beautiful dress adorned with English roses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty - a combination of qualities that pleases the senses

Etymology

From the association of England with the rose as a national emblem, symbolizing delicate and traditional beauty.
