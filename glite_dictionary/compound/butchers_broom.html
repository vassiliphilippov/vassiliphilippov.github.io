butcher's broom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃərz ˌbruːm / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəz ˌbruːm / (BrE)
a low shrub (Ruscus aculeatus) with stiff, flattened stems resembling leaves, traditionally used for making brooms
shrub with stiff, leaf-like stems

Examples

The spiky branches of butcher's broom were once used to sweep butcher shops clean.
Gardeners often plant butcher's broom for its hardiness and attractive red berries.
Traditional herbalists value butcher's broom for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

So named because its stems were once used by butchers to make brooms for cleaning.
