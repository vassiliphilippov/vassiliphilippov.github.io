unslaked lime

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˈsleɪkt laɪm / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈsleɪkt laɪm / (BrE)
a white, caustic, crystalline substance (calcium oxide) used especially in making mortar and cement, and for industrial purposes
calcium oxide used in industry

Examples

The workers handled the unslaked lime with care, wearing protective gloves and goggles.
Unslaked lime reacts violently with water, producing heat and forming slaked lime.
To prepare mortar, architects often mix unslaked lime with sand and water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quicklime - calcium oxide as used in industry
  • Antonym: slaked lime - calcium hydroxide, formed when unslaked lime reacts with water

Etymology

From 'unslaked', meaning not combined with water, and 'lime', from Old English līm (limestone, sticky substance).
