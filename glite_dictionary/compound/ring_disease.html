ring disease

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / rɪŋ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A bacterial disease affecting tomatoes, potatoes, tobacco, and other plants, caused by Pseudomonas solanacearum, characterized by dark rings in the stem tissue.
plant disease caused by bacteria

Examples

The farmer noticed ring disease affecting several rows of his potato plants.
Ring disease can devastate tomato crops if not managed early.
Researchers are developing new methods to detect ring disease in tobacco fields.
