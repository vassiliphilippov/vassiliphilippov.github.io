Bougainville Trench

1. PROPN

/ ˌbuːɡənˈvɪl ˈtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˌbuːɡənˈvɪl ˈtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
a deep depression in the floor of the Pacific Ocean, located between New Guinea and the Solomon Islands
undersea trench in the Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Bougainville Trench is one of the deepest parts of the South Pacific Ocean.
Scientists have studied the Bougainville Trench to understand oceanic plate movement.
The Bougainville Trench lies between New Guinea and the Solomon Islands.

Etymology

Named after the nearby island of Bougainville, itself named after the French explorer Louis Antoine de Bougainville.
