gustatory modality

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌstəˌtɔri məˈdæləti / (AmE), / ˈɡʌstətəri məˈdælɪti / (BrE)
the faculty or ability to perceive and distinguish tastes such as sweet, sour, bitter, and salty in the mouth
the ability to taste; sense of taste
medical

Examples

A head injury can sometimes impair the gustatory modality, making flavors seem less intense or absent entirely.
Research into the gustatory modality has helped scientists understand why people have different food preferences.
Diseases affecting the tongue may have a direct impact on one's gustatory modality.

Synset Examples

his cold deprived him of his sense of taste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense of taste - the ability to perceive and identify flavors

Etymology

From Latin 'gustare' (to taste) and 'modus' (measure, manner).
