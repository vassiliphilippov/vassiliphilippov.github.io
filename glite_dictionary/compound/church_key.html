church key

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɝːtʃ ˌkiː / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃ ˌkiː / (BrE)
a can opener with a pointed end used to pierce the tops of cans
pointed can opener for piercing cans

Examples

He used a church key to punch a hole in the can of tomato juice.
Every bartender keeps a church key handy for opening cans.
The old-style church key was lying in the kitchen drawer.

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly humorous, suggesting a key to a 'church' (alcohol) by analogy.
