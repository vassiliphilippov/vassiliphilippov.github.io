nosh-up

1. NOUN

/ nɑːʃ ʌp / (AmE), / nɒʃ ʌp / (BrE)
A large and satisfying meal, especially one that is enjoyed informally with others.
large, satisfying meal (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

After the long hike, they treated themselves to a hearty nosh-up at the local pub.
We invited friends over for a Sunday nosh-up with roast beef and all the trimmings.
Nothing beats a proper nosh-up on a cold winter's evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feast - a large meal, typically a celebratory one
  • Antonym: snack - a small amount of food eaten between meals

Etymology

From 'nosh' (food, to eat, from Yiddish 'nashn' – to nibble) + 'up' (indicating fullness); British slang, mid-20th century.
