harvest fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrvəst flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvɪst flaɪ / (BrE)
A cicada, especially one heard commonly in July and August, known for its distinctive song.
a cicada; insect with a distinctive summer song

Examples

The distinctive song of the harvest fly fills the air during the warm days of August.
Children tried to spot a harvest fly among the tree branches as it sang its buzzing tune.
In rural areas, the sound of the harvest fly often signals the peak of summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - an insect with transparent wings, known for its loud song
