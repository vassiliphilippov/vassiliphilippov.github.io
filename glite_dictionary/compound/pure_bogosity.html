pure bogosity

1. NOUN

/ pjʊr boʊˈɡɑsɪti / (AmE), / pjʊə bəʊˈɡɒsɪti / (BrE)
Complete nonsense; utter lack of truth or credibility.
complete nonsense or falsehood
informal slang

Examples

His explanation was pure bogosity from start to finish.
You can spot pure bogosity in online scams if you pay close attention.
The article was criticized for being based on pure bogosity rather than facts.

Synset Examples

Don't believe everything; it might be pure bogosity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Antonym: truth - the quality of being true

Etymology

From 'bogosity' (informal, meaning nonsense or falsehood), which is a playful extension of 'bogus'.
