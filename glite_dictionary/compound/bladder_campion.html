bladder campion

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædɚ ˈkæmp.i.ən / (AmE), / ˈblædə ˈkæmp.i.ən / (BrE)
a perennial herbaceous plant with bluish-green leaves, sticky stems, and clusters of white, balloon-like flowers that open in the evening; native to Europe and commonly found in meadows and roadsides
herb with inflated white flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with the delicate white blooms of bladder campion swaying gently in the evening breeze.
Botanists identify bladder campion by its characteristic inflated calyx and pale flowers.
Gardeners sometimes plant bladder campion for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Etymology

From 'bladder' referring to the inflated calyx and 'campion' used for various species in the Caryophyllaceae family.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblædɚ ˈkæmpiən / (AmE), / ˈblædə ˈkæmpiən / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to Arctic Europe, with large white flowers and an inflated, bladder-like calyx
white-flowered perennial plant with an inflated calyx

Examples

The wild bladder campion can be found growing in meadows across northern Europe.
We noticed the distinctive, inflated calyx of the bladder campion during our hike.
Gardeners often plant bladder campion for its attractive white flowers and drought tolerance.

Etymology

From 'bladder' describing the swollen calyx, and 'campion' from Latin 'campionem', a name given to several flowering plants.
