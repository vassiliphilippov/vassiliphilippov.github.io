moss phlox

1. NOUN

/ mɑs flɑks / (AmE), / mɒs flɒks / (BrE)
a low-growing, evergreen perennial plant with needle-like leaves and pink or white flowers, used as a ground cover and native to the United States
low evergreen plant with pink or white flowers

Examples

Moss phlox blooms in early spring, covering the rock garden with vibrant color.
Gardeners often use moss phlox to create a beautiful, low-maintenance ground cover.
The pink flowers of moss phlox attract butterflies to the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlox - a genus of flowering plants in the family Polemoniaceae, known for colorful blooms
