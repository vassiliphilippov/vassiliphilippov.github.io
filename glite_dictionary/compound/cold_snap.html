cold snap

1. NOUN

/ koʊld snæp / (AmE), / kəʊld snæp / (BrE)
a short period of sudden, unusually cold weather
brief period of sudden cold weather

Examples

A sudden cold snap in January caused power outages across the region.
Farmers worried that the cold snap might damage their crops.
The weather forecast warns of another cold snap hitting this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold spell - a period of colder than normal weather
  • Antonym: heat wave - a prolonged period of excessively hot weather

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊld snæp / (AmE), / ˈkəʊld snæp / (BrE)
a sudden, brief period of unusually cold weather
brief period of sudden cold weather

Examples

Last January, a cold snap caused widespread frost damage to crops across the region.
The sudden cold snap forced the city to open emergency shelters for the homeless.
After weeks of mild temperatures, a cold snap brought snow to the area overnight.

Synset Examples

We experienced a cold snap that surprised everyone.
The cold snap caught many off guard this winter.
During the cold snap, temperatures dropped significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold spell - a period of cold weather, often longer than a cold snap
  • Antonym: heat wave - a prolonged period of excessively hot weather
