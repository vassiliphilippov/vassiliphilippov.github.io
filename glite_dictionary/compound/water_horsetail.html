water horsetail

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈhɔrˌsteɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈhɔːsteɪl / (BrE)
a species of horsetail (Equisetum fluviatile) found in wetlands of Eurasia and northern North America, with hollow stems and whorled branches
species of horsetail plant, found in wetlands

Examples

Water horsetail grows abundantly along the edges of northern lakes and ponds.
The dense stands of water horsetail provide shelter for aquatic wildlife.
Botanists studying wetland ecology often encounter water horsetail in their fieldwork.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum, with hollow, jointed stems
