mother-hive of artisans

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ haɪv əv ɑrˈtɪzənz / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə hʌɪv əv ɑːˈtɪzənz / (BrE)
A central or original place known for being the source or hub of skilled craftsmen.
center for skilled craftsmen

Examples

During the Renaissance, Florence was a mother-hive of artisans in Europe.
The region became a mother-hive of artisans after centuries of craftsmanship tradition.
That workshop is the mother-hive of artisans who trained many of the city's famous sculptors.

Synset Examples

The old town was known as the mother-hive of artisans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center of excellence - a place where highly skilled work is concentrated

Etymology

From 'mother-hive' (the original hive in a colony, source of new swarms) used metaphorically; 'artisan' from Italian 'artigiano.'
