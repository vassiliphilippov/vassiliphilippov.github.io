Andromeda galaxy

1. PROPN

/ ænˈdrɑːmədə ˈɡæləksi / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə ˈɡæləzi / (BrE)
the large spiral galaxy in the constellation Andromeda, visible to the naked eye and the nearest major galaxy to the Milky Way
nearest major spiral galaxy to the Milky Way

Examples

The Andromeda galaxy is expected to collide with the Milky Way in several billion years.
With clear skies, you can see the Andromeda galaxy without a telescope.
Astronomers have studied the Andromeda galaxy to understand the structure of spiral galaxies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Milky Way - the galaxy containing our solar system

Etymology

Named after the constellation Andromeda, in which the galaxy appears.
