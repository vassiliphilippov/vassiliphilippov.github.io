metacarpophalangeal joint

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˌkɑrpoʊfəˈlændʒiəl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəkɑːpəfəˈlændʒɪəl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
The joint between a metacarpal bone of the hand and the corresponding proximal phalanx of a finger or thumb.
joint between hand bone and finger bone
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the swollen metacarpophalangeal joint on his patient's index finger.
Arthritis often affects the metacarpophalangeal joints in people with rheumatoid arthritis.
Proper stretching can help maintain flexibility in each metacarpophalangeal joint.

Etymology

From meta- (after) + carpus (wrist) + phalanx (finger bone) + -eal (relating to) + joint.
