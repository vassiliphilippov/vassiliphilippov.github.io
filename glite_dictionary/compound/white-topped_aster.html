white-topped aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt tɑpt ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt tɒpt ˈæstə / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant with clusters of white, daisy-like flowers, scaly bracts, and silky, non-splitting fruits
herb with white flower clusters

Examples

The white-topped aster blooms in late summer along woodland edges and meadows.
Gardeners prize the white-topped aster for its delicate white petals and hardy nature.
We identified several white-topped asters during our hike through the preserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a genus of flowering plants with star-shaped flower heads
