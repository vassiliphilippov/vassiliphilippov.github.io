cafe royale

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæfeɪ roʊˈjaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæfeɪ rəʊˈjal / (BrE)
A drink made from black coffee mixed with Cognac, garnished with lemon peel and sugar.
black coffee with Cognac, lemon peel, and sugar

Examples

He ended his meal with a cafe royale to savor the perfect combination of coffee and Cognac.
Would you like a cafe royale after dessert, or do you prefer something non-alcoholic?
The bartender prepared a classic cafe royale, expertly flaming the Cognac before adding it to the coffee.

Etymology

French origin: 'café' (coffee) + 'royale' (royal), referring to the luxurious ingredients.
