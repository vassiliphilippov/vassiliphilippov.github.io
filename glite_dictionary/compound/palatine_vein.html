palatine vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.ə.taɪn veɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.tiːn veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the palate and empties into the facial vein
vein draining the palate
medical

Examples

The palatine vein plays an essential role in draining blood from the palate region.
Damage to the palatine vein during surgery can lead to significant bleeding.
The dentist explained how the palatine vein connects to the larger facial vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial vein - a vein that collects blood from the face and empties into the internal jugular vein

Etymology

From Latin *palatinus* (of the palate) + *vein*.
