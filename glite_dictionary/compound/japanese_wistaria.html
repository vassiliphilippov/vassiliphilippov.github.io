Japanese wistaria

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæn ɪz wɪˈstɛriə / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn iːz wɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
a climbing plant of East Asian origin with showy clusters of pink, mauve, or violet-blue flowers, often grown for ornament
climbing plant with pink or violet-blue flowers

Examples

The Japanese wistaria bloomed beautifully along the garden trellis this spring.
Many traditional Japanese gardens feature Japanese wistaria for their vibrant, cascading flowers.
Botanists admire the Japanese wistaria for its fragrant blossoms and vigorous growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese wistaria - a similar climbing plant native to China

Etymology

Wistaria from genus *Wisteria*, named after Caspar Wistar; 'Japanese' refers to its origin in Japan.
