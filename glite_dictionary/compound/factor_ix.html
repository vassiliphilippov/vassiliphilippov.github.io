factor IX

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɚ naɪn / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə naɪn / (BrE)
a protein in the blood necessary for normal clotting, whose deficiency causes hemophilia B
blood clotting protein, deficiency causes hemophilia B
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a deficiency of factor IX after experiencing frequent nosebleeds.
Replacement therapy for factor IX is used to treat people with hemophilia B.
Genetic testing revealed a mutation in the gene responsible for producing factor IX.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hemophilia B - genetic disorder caused by a lack of factor IX
  • Degree Lower: factor VIII - another blood clotting factor, deficiency causes hemophilia A
