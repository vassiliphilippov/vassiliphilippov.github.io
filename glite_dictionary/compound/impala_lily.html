impala lily

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɑːlə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːlə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a South African shrub (Adenium multiflorum) with a swollen, succulent stem, producing showy pink and white flowers after the leaves fall; widely grown as an ornamental in tropical regions
South African ornamental shrub with pink and white flowers

Examples

The impala lily blooms in a stunning display of pink and white when its leaves have just fallen.
Gardeners love to cultivate the impala lily in warm, sunny spots for its unique appearance.
During the dry season, the impala lily is covered with beautiful flowers, attracting many visitors.
