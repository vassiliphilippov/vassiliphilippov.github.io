work song

1. NOUN

/ wɝːk sɔŋ / (AmE), / wɜːk sɒŋ / (BrE)
a usually rhythmic song sung to accompany repetitive or physical work, often to coordinate effort or relieve boredom
song to accompany repetitive work

Examples

The sailors sang a work song to keep time as they pulled in the heavy nets.
African American communities have a rich history of work songs sung in cotton fields.
The rhythm of the work song helped the laborers coordinate their movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk song - traditional song passed orally among people of a region
