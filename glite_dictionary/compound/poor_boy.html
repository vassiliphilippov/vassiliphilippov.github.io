poor boy

1. NOUN

/ pɔr bɔɪ / (AmE), / pɔː bɔɪ / (BrE)
a large sandwich made with a long crusty roll, typically filled with meats, cheese, and other ingredients, known in some regions of the United States
large sandwich on a crusty roll, US regional
american

Examples

I ordered a delicious poor boy for lunch at the local deli.
The restaurant's signature dish is a shrimp poor boy stuffed with lettuce and tomato.
Across Louisiana, the poor boy sandwich has become a beloved culinary tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a large sandwich made from a long roll split lengthwise
  • Synonym: hoagie - another US regional term for a similar sandwich
  • Synonym: po' boy - Louisiana-origin sandwich similar to 'poor boy'

Etymology

Originally from Louisiana, possibly referring to sandwiches given to 'poor boys' during streetcar strikes.
