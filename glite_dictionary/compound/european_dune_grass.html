European dune grass

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən dun ɡræs / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən djuːn ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A grass native to Europe, commonly used as a sand binder along the Pacific seacoast to prevent dune erosion.
coastal grass used to bind sand dunes

Examples

European dune grass was planted to stabilize the shifting sand along the coastline.
Many conservationists prefer European dune grass for its effectiveness in dune restoration projects.
The long roots of European dune grass help anchor the dunes during storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beach grass - a general term for grasses growing on sand dunes and beaches, used to prevent erosion
