heat of vaporization

1. NOUN

/ hit əv ˌvæpəɹəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv ˌvapəɹʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount of heat required to convert a unit mass of a liquid into vapor without a change in temperature
heat needed to turn liquid into vapor

Examples

The heat of vaporization of water is much higher than that of most other liquids.
To boil the liquid, you must provide enough energy to reach its heat of vaporization.
Scientists measured the heat of vaporization to study the properties of the new compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latent heat - heat absorbed or released during a phase change without temperature change
  • Degree Lower: heat of fusion - heat required to melt a solid at its melting point
