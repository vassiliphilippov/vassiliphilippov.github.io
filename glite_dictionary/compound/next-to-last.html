next-to-last

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɛkst tə ˈlæst / (AmE), / ˌnɛkst tə ˈlɑːst / (BrE)
Immediately preceding the last in a series; second from the end.
second from the end

Examples

He finished in the next-to-last position in the race.
Please check the next-to-last page of the report for the summary.
She lives in the next-to-last house on the street, right before the corner.

Synset Examples

the author inadvertently reveals the murderer in the penultimate chapter
the figures in the next-to-last column

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penultimate - immediately before the last; second last
