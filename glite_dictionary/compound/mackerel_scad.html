mackerel scad

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækərəl skæd / (AmE), / ˈmakərəl skad / (BrE)
a small, silvery marine fish (Decapterus macarellus) found in the Atlantic Ocean from Nova Scotia to Brazil, valued for food and as bait.
a small Atlantic fish used for food and bait

Examples

Fishermen often use mackerel scad as bait when targeting larger fish such as tuna.
The waters off the coast of Florida are home to abundant schools of mackerel scad.
Grilled mackerel scad is a popular dish in parts of the Caribbean.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to both mackerel and scad fish.
