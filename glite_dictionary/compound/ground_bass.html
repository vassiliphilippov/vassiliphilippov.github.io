ground bass

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd bæs / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd bɑːs / (BrE)
A short phrase or melody in the bass part that is repeated continually while the upper parts vary, typically used as a structural device in music.
repeated bass melody in music

Examples

The composer used a ground bass to give the piece a sense of unity and continuity.
Many Baroque compositions feature a ground bass as a foundation for elaborate melodic variations.
The repeating ground bass in the song creates a hypnotic effect for the listener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostinato - a continually repeated musical phrase or rhythm

Etymology

From 'ground' (basis, foundation) and 'bass' (lowest musical part); used in English music since the 17th century.
