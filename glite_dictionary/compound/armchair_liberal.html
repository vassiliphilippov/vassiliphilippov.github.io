armchair liberal

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmˌtʃɛr ˈlɪbərəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːmtʃɛː ˈlɪbərəl / (BrE)
a person who espouses liberal views but does not actively pursue or support them through action
person with unacted liberal beliefs

Examples

He's often called an armchair liberal because he talks about social justice but never actually volunteers or donates.
Some critics dismiss celebrities as mere armchair liberals who only speak out when it's fashionable.
Rather than remaining an armchair liberal, she decided to join a local activist group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champagne socialist - a person who espouses socialist ideals while enjoying a wealthy lifestyle

Etymology

From 'armchair', meaning 'from a position of comfort or inaction', and 'liberal' (politically progressive). Modeled on 'armchair critic'.
