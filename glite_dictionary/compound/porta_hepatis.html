porta hepatis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːrtə ˈhɛpətɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtə ˈhɛpətɪs / (BrE)
the transverse fissure on the liver's underside where major blood vessels and ducts enter or leave the organ
opening in the liver for vessels and ducts
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the porta hepatis before proceeding with the liver transplant.
Blockage in the porta hepatis can lead to serious complications in liver function.
Several important structures pass through the porta hepatis, including the hepatic artery and portal vein.

Etymology

From Latin 'porta' (gate) and 'hepatis' (of the liver).
