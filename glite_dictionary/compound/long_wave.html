long wave

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ weɪv / (AmE), / lɒŋ weɪv / (BrE)
a radio wave with a wavelength longer than one kilometer, corresponding to a frequency below 300 kilohertz
radio wave with wavelength > 1 km

Examples

The BBC still broadcasts some programs on long wave frequencies that can be received across Europe.
Emergency information was transmitted over long wave radio during the power outage.
Early maritime communication often relied on long wave signals for their long-distance coverage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short wave - a radio wave with a wavelength shorter than that of long waves, typically used for different radio communications
  • Antonym: medium wave - a radio wave with a wavelength between long wave and short wave, often used for AM broadcasting
