fibroid tumor

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.brɔɪd ˈtu.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.brɔɪd ˈtjuː.mə / (BrE)
a benign tumor composed mainly of fibrous tissue, especially occurring in the uterus
benign tumor of fibrous tissue, esp. in uterus
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the fibroid tumor was benign and recommended regular monitoring.
Many women experience symptoms such as heavy bleeding when they have a fibroid tumor.
After the surgery, her fibroid tumor was sent for laboratory analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leiomyoma - a benign tumor of smooth muscle, especially of the uterus
  • Antonym: malignant tumor - a cancerous growth with potential to spread

Etymology

From 'fibroid', meaning 'resembling fiber', and 'tumor', from Latin 'tumor' (swelling).
