hognose snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑɡˌnoʊs sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡˌnəʊz sneɪk / (BrE)
a harmless North American snake with an upturned nose, noted for spreading its head and neck or playing dead when disturbed
harmless North American snake with upturned nose

Examples

When threatened, the hognose snake will often play dead to fool predators.
We found a hognose snake in the woods, easily recognized by its upturned nose.
The hognose snake is harmless, despite its dramatic defensive displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adder - a type of snake, to which hognose snakes are sometimes compared
  • Antonym: venomous snake - a snake that produces venom; hognose snakes are non-venomous

Etymology

Named for its upturned, hog-like snout.
