difference of opinion

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪfɚəns əv əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / ˈdɪfərəns əv əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
a situation in which two or more people disagree about something important, often leading to argument or debate
disagreement about something important

Examples

There was a difference of opinion among the team members about how to solve the problem.
A serious difference of opinion can create tension in a relationship if not handled carefully.
Despite their difference of opinion, they remained good friends.

Synset Examples

he had a dispute with his wife
there were irreconcilable differences
the familiar conflict between Republicans and Democrats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disagreement - a lack of consensus or harmony; disapproval
  • Antonym: accord - agreement; harmony between people or groups
