olde worlde charm

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈwɜrld tʃɑrm / (AmE), / əʊld ˈwɜːld tʃɑːm / (BrE)
an appealing quality that is reminiscent of or evocative of a romanticized, old-fashioned past; nostalgic quaintness or attractiveness
nostalgic, quaint old-fashioned appeal
british

Examples

The hotel was full of olde worlde charm, with wooden beams and antique furnishings.
Many tourists are drawn to villages that still retain their olde worlde charm.
The cottage’s olde worlde charm made it a perfect location for the film.

Synset Examples

The restaurant had an olde worlde charm that attracted tourists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nostalgia - a sentimental longing for the past
  • Antonym: modernity - the quality of being modern or up to date

Etymology

A pseudo-archaic spelling of 'old world', used to evoke the past; 'olde' and 'worlde' imitate archaic English forms.
