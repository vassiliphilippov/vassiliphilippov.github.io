Xerox machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪrɑks məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈzɪərɒks məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
A machine that makes copies of documents and images by using light and electrostatic charges; a photocopier, originally a trademark name.
photocopier; document copying machine

Examples

The office just bought a new Xerox machine to speed up printing tasks.
She scanned the report using the Xerox machine near her desk.
Old Xerox machines often jam if overloaded with paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocopier - a machine that makes paper copies of documents and images

Etymology

From the trademark 'Xerox', based on Greek 'xeros' (dry) and American English 'machine'.
