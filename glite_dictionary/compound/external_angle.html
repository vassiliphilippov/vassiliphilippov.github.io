external angle

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəl ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
An angle formed outside a polygon when one side is extended, equal to the supplement of the adjacent interior angle.
angle outside a polygon

Examples

The sum of all the external angles of a polygon is always 360 degrees.
To find the external angle of a regular pentagon, divide 360 by 5.
Each external angle in an equilateral triangle measures 120 degrees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interior angle - an angle inside a polygon formed by two adjacent sides

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outside) and 'angulus' (angle).
