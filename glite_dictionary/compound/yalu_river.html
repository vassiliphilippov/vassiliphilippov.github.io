Yalu River

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɑːlu ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈjɑːlu ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in East Asia forming part of the border between North Korea and China, notable for its historical significance, especially during the Korean War.
river on China–North Korea border

Examples

The Yalu River played a strategic role during the Korean War.
Chinese forces crossed the Yalu River to enter North Korea in 1950.
Bridges across the Yalu River connect China and North Korea.

2. PROPN

/ ˈjɑːlu ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈjɑːlu ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in eastern Asia that rises in North Korea and flows southwest to Korea Bay, forming part of the border between North Korea and China
river forming part of the China–North Korea border

Examples

The Yalu River serves as a natural border between China and North Korea.
During the Korean War, United Nations forces advanced to the banks of the Yalu River.
Pollution levels in the Yalu River have been a concern for both countries.
