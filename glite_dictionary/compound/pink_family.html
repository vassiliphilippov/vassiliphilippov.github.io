pink family

1. NOUN

/ pɪŋk ˈfæməli / (AmE), / pɪŋk ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, mostly herbs or subshrubs, typically having stems swollen at the nodes; includes carnations and campions (family Caryophyllaceae)
family of flowering plants (Caryophyllaceae)

Examples

Many popular garden flowers, such as carnations, belong to the pink family.
The pink family is characterized by opposite leaves and swollen nodes on the stems.
Botanists have documented over 2,000 species within the pink family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caryophyllaceae - the botanical name for the pink family of plants

Etymology

Named for the genus Dianthus (‘pinks’) within the family.
