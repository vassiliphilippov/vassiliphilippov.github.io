road metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊd ˌmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd ˌmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
broken stone or rock used in making and repairing roads
broken stone for road construction

Examples

The construction crew spread road metal evenly across the new stretch of highway.
After the heavy rains, much of the road metal was washed into the ditch.
Lorries delivered tons of road metal for resurfacing the rural lanes.

Etymology

From 'road', meaning a thoroughfare, and 'metal', from Middle English, meaning material or substance.
