rabbiteye blueberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈræb.i.taɪ ˈbluːˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈræb.ɪt.aɪ ˈbluːˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southeastern United States, cultivated for its small, edible blueberries, especially used in commercial canning.
a southeastern US blueberry shrub and its fruit

Examples

Many growers in Georgia cultivate rabbiteye blueberry for both fresh and canned markets.
The rabbiteye blueberry thrives in sandy, acidic soils of the southeastern United States.
Her farm specializes in rabbiteye blueberry varieties prized for their sweet flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - an edible blue-black berry and the plant that bears it

Etymology

Named for its pale, pinkish fruit resembling a rabbit's eye; 'blueberry' from Middle English 'blewbiry'.
