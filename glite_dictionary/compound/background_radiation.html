background radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡraʊnd ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
radiation from natural or incidental sources present in an environment, other than radiation from the specific source being studied
ambient radiation from non-target sources

Examples

Scientists must account for background radiation when measuring radioactive samples in the laboratory.
High levels of background radiation can affect sensitive equipment in hospitals and research centers.
The Earth is constantly exposed to background radiation from cosmic rays and natural radioactive materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiation - the emission of energy as electromagnetic waves or particles
