sheet bend

1. NOUN

/ ʃit bɛnd / (AmE), / ʃiːt bɛnd / (BrE)
A type of knot used to temporarily join a rope to the middle of another rope or to an eye, often used in sailing or climbing.
type of knot for joining ropes

Examples

The sheet bend is ideal for joining two ropes of unequal thickness.
He secured the tarp using a sheet bend to fasten the cord to the grommet.
Sailors often rely on a sheet bend because it is quick and easy to tie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowline - a knot making a fixed loop at the end of a rope

Etymology

From 'sheet' (a rope on a sailing vessel) and 'bend' (a knot for joining ropes).
