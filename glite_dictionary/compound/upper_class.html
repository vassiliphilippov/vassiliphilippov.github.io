upper class

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpər klæs / (AmE), / ˌʌpə ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
the social group that has the highest status, wealth, or power in a society
the highest social class

Examples

She was born into the upper class and inherited a large fortune.
In the 19th century, the British upper class held significant political influence.
Despite the upper class lifestyle, he always felt out of place among the elite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the class of people holding exceptional rank and privileges
  • Antonym: working class - the social group consisting of people who are employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
  • Degree Lower: middle class - the social group between the upper and working classes
