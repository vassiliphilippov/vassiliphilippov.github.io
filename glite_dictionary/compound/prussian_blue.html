Prussian blue

1. NOUN

/ ˈprʌʃən blu / (AmE), / ˈprʌʃən bluː / (BrE)
a deep greenish-blue color, often used in art and pigments
a dark greenish-blue color

Examples

The artist chose Prussian blue for the evening sky in her painting.
My bedroom walls are painted in a rich shade of Prussian blue.
This fabric is available in both Prussian blue and teal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: navy blue - a very dark shade of blue
  • Degree Higher: indigo - a deep blue color between blue and violet

Etymology

Named after Prussia, where the pigment was first made in the early 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprʌʃən bluː / (AmE), / ˈprʌʃən bluː / (BrE)
a deep blue pigment, originally made by oxidizing ferrous ferrocyanide salts, used in paints and dyes
a deep blue pigment

Examples

The artist mixed a bit of Prussian blue with white to create a striking sky.
Early chemists discovered Prussian blue while experimenting with iron salts.
Her dress was dyed a vibrant shade of Prussian blue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultramarine - a brilliant blue pigment made from powdered lapis lazuli
  • Synonym: indigo - a deep blue dye obtained from plants

Etymology

Named after Prussia, where the pigment was first discovered in the early 18th century.
