Twelfth Night

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɛlfθ ˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtwɛlfθ ˌnaɪt / (BrE)
The evening of January 5th, marking the end of the Christmas season and celebrated as the eve of the Epiphany in some Christian traditions.
eve of January 6 (Epiphany); end of Christmas season

Examples

We always celebrate Twelfth Night with a special family dinner and traditional games.
Many people take down their Christmas decorations on Twelfth Night to avoid bad luck.
The local church hosts a festive service every year on Twelfth Night.

Etymology

Named for being the twelfth night after Christmas Day, preceding Epiphany.
