space station

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪs ˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈspeɪs ˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
A large artificial structure in space where people can live and work for extended periods, usually for scientific research.
manned artificial satellite for research

Examples

The International space station orbits the Earth about every 90 minutes.
Scientists performed groundbreaking experiments aboard the space station.
She dreamed of becoming an astronaut and living on a space station someday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satellite - an artificial body placed in orbit around the earth or another planet
  • Synonym: spacecraft - a vehicle used for traveling in space
