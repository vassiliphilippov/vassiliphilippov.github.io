grass roots

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs ruːts / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs ruːts / (BrE)
the most basic level of an organization or activity; the ordinary people regarded as the main support or foundation
basic level or foundation of an organization

Examples

The organization decided to consult the grass roots before making any drastic changes.
True reform must begin at the grass roots to have lasting impact.
Political campaigns often focus on mobilizing grass roots support.

Synset Examples

the problem was attacked at the grass roots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part or base of something
  • Antonym: hierarchy - a system in which people or things are arranged according to their importance

Etymology

Originally from agriculture, referring to the roots of grass, metaphorically extended to mean the foundation or origin.

2. NOUN

/ ɡræs ruːts / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs ruːts / (BrE)
the ordinary people in a community, especially as distinct from the leadership or elite, particularly in politics or social movements.
the ordinary people in a community

Examples

The new political party gained widespread support from the grass roots.
Many social changes have started at the grass roots level before spreading nationwide.
He believes that real reform must come from the grass roots rather than the government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: establishment - the dominant group or elite holding power or authority in a nation or organization
  • Synonym: populace - the people living in a particular country or area; the general public

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to the roots of grass, symbolizing the basic level of an organization or society.
