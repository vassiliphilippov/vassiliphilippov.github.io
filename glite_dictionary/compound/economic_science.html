economic science

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪk ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
the study of the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services, and the management of these activities
study of goods, services, and management

Examples

She chose to major in economic science because she was interested in how markets operate.
Many universities offer advanced degrees in economic science to prepare students for research careers.
The conference brought together leading scholars in economic science to discuss global trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economics - the social science concerned with the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services
