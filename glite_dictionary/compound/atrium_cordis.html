atrium cordis

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.tri.əm ˈkɔr.dɪs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əm ˈkɔː.dɪs / (BrE)
the upper chamber of each half of the heart, which receives blood before passing it to the ventricle
upper chamber of the heart
medical

Examples

The left atrium cordis receives oxygenated blood from the lungs.
Doctors examined the structure of the atrium cordis in the patient's heart.
A clot found in the atrium cordis can lead to serious complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrium - the upper chamber of the heart that receives blood returning to the heart
  • Antonym: ventricle - the lower chamber of the heart that pumps blood out

Etymology

From Latin: 'atrium' (entrance hall, central court) + 'cordis' (of the heart).
