rooting interest

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtɪŋ ˌɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪŋ ˌɪntrəst / (BrE)
support or enthusiasm for a particular cause, team, or outcome, often due to personal investment or emotional attachment
personal support or investment in a cause
american

Examples

She didn't have a rooting interest in the game, but she still enjoyed watching it.
His rooting interest in the election was clear from his passionate discussions.
The fans' rooting interest shifted after their home team was eliminated.

Synset Examples

His rooting interest in the project kept him motivated.
Her rooting interest in the charity made her volunteer.
Their rooting interest in the outcome was evident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a group or cause
  • Synonym: enthusiasm - strong excitement or interest in a subject or cause

Etymology

From the verb 'root' meaning 'to cheer for,' plus 'interest'. Usage popularized in American English from mid-20th century.
