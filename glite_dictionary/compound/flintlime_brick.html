flintlime brick

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪntˌlaɪm brɪk / (AmE), / ˈflɪntˌlaɪm brɪk / (BrE)
A type of brick made with sand and slaked lime instead of clay, usually light gray or off-white in color.
brick made with sand and slaked lime

Examples

The old factory was constructed using flintlime bricks that gave the walls a distinctive pale color.
When renovating historic buildings, matching the original flintlime brick can be a challenge.
Unlike traditional clay bricks, flintlime brick is known for its smooth surface and light shade.

Synset Examples

The finished calcium-silicate bricks are very accurate and uniform, although the sharp arrises need careful handling to avoid damage to brick and bricklayer.
