internasal suture

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈneɪzəl ˈsuːtʃər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈneɪzəl ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
the line of junction or suture between the two nasal bones of the skull
suture between nasal bones
medical

Examples

A fracture at the internasal suture may affect the stability of the nose.
The surgeon carefully examined the internasal suture before proceeding with the procedure.
In newborns, the internasal suture is sometimes more visible during physical examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal suture - the suture related to the nose or nasal bones

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) and 'nasalis' (of the nose), plus 'suture' from Latin 'sutura' (a seam).
