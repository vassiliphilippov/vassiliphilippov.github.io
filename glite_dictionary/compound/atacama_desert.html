Atacama Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˌætəˈkɑmə ˈdɛzərt / (AmE), / ˌætəˈkɑːmə ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a desert in northern Chile, known as one of the driest places on Earth, noted for its mineral resources, especially nitrate and copper
desert in northern Chile, extremely dry

Examples

Scientists from around the world travel to the Atacama Desert to study its extreme dryness and unique ecosystem.
The Atacama Desert is famous for its clear night skies, making it a prime location for astronomical observatories.
Copper mining is a major industry in the Atacama Desert due to its rich mineral deposits.

Etymology

From the Atacama region in Chile; 'Atacama' is a local native name.
