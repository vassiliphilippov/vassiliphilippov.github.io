Turko-Tatar

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɜrkoʊ ˈtɑːtɑr / (AmE), / ˌtɜːkəʊ ˈtɑːtɑː / (BrE)
A subfamily of Altaic languages traditionally including Turkic and Tatar languages.
subfamily of Altaic languages

Examples

The Turko-Tatar languages are spoken across a vast region of Central Asia.
Linguists often study Turko-Tatar influences in Russian and Mongolian.
The Turko-Tatar peoples have shaped the history of Eurasia for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - relating to the Turkic languages, a broader language family

Etymology

From 'Turko-' (referring to Turkic peoples/languages) + 'Tatar' (an ethnic group and their language).
