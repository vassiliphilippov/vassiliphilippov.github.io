vestibular system

1. NOUN

/ vɛˈstɪbjələr ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪbjʊlə ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of organs in the inner ear that is responsible for maintaining balance and sensing spatial orientation
system of organs for balance and orientation
medical

Examples

Damage to the vestibular system can result in severe balance disorders.
The vestibular system helps people maintain their equilibrium while moving.
Scientists are studying how space travel affects the vestibular system of astronauts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: inner ear - the part of the ear responsible for balance and hearing

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance hall) referring to the vestibule of the inner ear and 'systema' (system)
