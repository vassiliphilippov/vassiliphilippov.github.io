dismal science

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪzməl ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪzməl ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
a term used to refer to economics, especially regarding its reputation for focusing on bleak or gloomy aspects such as scarcity and hardship
term for economics highlighting its perceived bleakness

Examples

Many still refer to economics as the dismal science because of its association with hardship and scarcity.
The professor explained why the study of incentives makes the dismal science so relevant today.
Some believe the nickname dismal science comes from economics' focus on negative predictions.

Synset Examples

Economics is often called the dismal science for good reason.
Many view economics as a dismal science full of gloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economics - the social science dealing with production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services

Etymology

First used in the mid-19th century by Thomas Carlyle to criticize economics and its focus on grim realities.
