Saami language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmi ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uralic language spoken by the Saami people in northern Scandinavia and the Kola Peninsula
language of the Saami people

Examples

The Saami language is spoken by indigenous communities across northern Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Saami language among younger generations.
She is fluent in both Norwegian and the Saami language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnish - the Uralic language spoken in Finland

Etymology

From Saami (self-designation), a Uralic language family.
