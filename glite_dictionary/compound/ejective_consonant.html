ejective consonant

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɛktɪv ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛktɪv ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced by the sudden release of air pressure from the closed glottis, found in certain languages such as Hausa.
consonant produced by glottal air release
medical

Examples

In some Caucasian languages, every stop can be pronounced as an ejective consonant.
The Hausa word for 'milk' begins with an ejective consonant not found in English.
Linguists study how ejective consonants affect syllable structure and pronunciation.

Synset Examples

The vast majority of ejective consonants noted in the world's languages consists of stops or affricates, and all ejective consonants are obstruents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: implosive consonant - A consonant produced with an inward airflow, contrasting with ejective

Etymology

From 'ejective', derived from Latin 'ejectus' (cast out), and 'consonant', from Latin 'consonans' (sounding together).
