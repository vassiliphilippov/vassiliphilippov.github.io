glottal plosive

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑt̬.əl ˈploʊ.sɪv / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒt.əl ˈpləʊ.sɪv / (BrE)
A type of stop consonant produced by closing and then rapidly releasing the vocal cords (glottis), as in the abrupt start of some vowel sounds.
phonetic stop sound at the glottis
medical

Examples

The glottal plosive often occurs at the beginning of words that start with a vowel in some dialects.
When teaching pronunciation, the instructor highlighted the use of the glottal plosive in English.
Some languages use the glottal plosive as a distinct consonant between vowels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glottal stop - Another term for the glottal plosive sound
  • Degree Higher: voiced plosive - A stop consonant produced with vocal cord vibration
  • Degree Lower: voiceless plosive - A stop consonant produced without vocal cord vibration

Etymology

From 'glottal' (referring to the glottis) and 'plosive' (from Latin 'plosivus', to explode).
