jasper stone

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæspər stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaspə stəʊn / (BrE)
an opaque variety of quartz, typically red, yellow, brown, or green, used as a gemstone or for ornamentation
opaque mineral used as a gemstone

Examples

The pendant was inlaid with a polished jasper stone set in silver.
Collectors admire the unique colors and patterns found in jasper stones.
She bought a bracelet made from red jasper stone at the craft fair.

Synset Examples

The artist used jasper stones in her latest sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agate - a banded variety of chalcedony, used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Old French jaspre, from Latin iaspis, from Greek iaspis; ultimately from a Semitic source (cf. Hebrew yashpeh).
