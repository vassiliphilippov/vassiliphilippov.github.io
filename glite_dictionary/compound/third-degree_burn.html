third-degree burn

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝd dɪˌɡri bɝn / (AmE), / ˈθɜːd dɪˌɡriː bɜːn / (BrE)
A severe burn in which both the epidermis and dermis layers of the skin are destroyed, often resulting in numbness and requiring medical attention.
severe burn destroying both skin layers
medical

Examples

The firefighter suffered a third-degree burn on his arm after rescuing the child from the blaze.
Treating a third-degree burn often requires skin grafts and intensive medical care.
Because a third-degree burn destroys nerves, the area might feel numb despite the injury's seriousness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: second-degree burn - burn affecting both the epidermis and part of the dermis
