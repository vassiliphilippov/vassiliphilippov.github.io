Bodoni font

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈdoʊni fɑnt / (AmE), / bəˈdəʊni fɒnt / (BrE)
a serif typeface with high contrast between thick and thin strokes, hairline serifs, and a regular, geometric shape, designed by Giambattista Bodoni in the late 18th century
a high-contrast serif typeface by Bodoni

Examples

The magazine's headlines were set in Bodoni font to give a classic and elegant appearance.
Graphic designers often choose Bodoni font for projects that require a sense of sophistication.
She recognized the Bodoni font by its sharp serifs and dramatic contrast between thick and thin strokes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Didot - a similar high-contrast serif typeface developed in France in the late 18th century

Etymology

Named after Italian typographer Giambattista Bodoni (1740–1813), who designed the original typeface.
