vegetable ivory

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛdʒtəbl̩ ˈaɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒtəbəl ˈaɪvəri / (BrE)
the hard, white, nut-like seed of certain South American palms, especially the tagua palm, used as a substitute for ivory in making buttons, carvings, and other small items
hard palm seed used as ivory substitute

Examples

Buttons made from vegetable ivory were popular before the invention of plastic.
The intricate carving was done on vegetable ivory from the tagua palm.
Collectors value antique chess pieces crafted from vegetable ivory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory - hard, creamy-white substance from elephant tusks, used for carving

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to animal ivory; 'vegetable' from Latin 'vegetabilis' (animating), 'ivory' from Latin 'eboreus' (of ivory).
