C clef

1. NOUN

/ si klɛf / (AmE), / si klɛf / (BrE)
a movable clef in musical notation that designates middle C on one of the lines of a staff
movable musical clef for middle C

Examples

The viola part was written using the C clef to indicate middle C.
Many vocal scores employ the C clef for different voices, such as alto or tenor.
Before reading the music, she had to remind herself where the C clef was positioned on the staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: F clef - a musical clef indicating F below middle C
  • Synonym: G clef - a musical clef indicating G above middle C
