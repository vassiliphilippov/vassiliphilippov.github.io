huckleberry oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri oʊk / (AmE), / ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri əʊk / (BrE)
a low, spreading or prostrate shrub of the southwestern United States with small acorns and leaves resembling those of the huckleberry
Southwestern U.S. shrub with huckleberry-like leaves

Examples

The huckleberry oak is well adapted to dry, rocky slopes in California.
Botanists often recognize huckleberry oak by its distinctive small acorns and shiny leaves.
We found huckleberry oak growing alongside manzanita on our mountain hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quercus vaccinifolia - Scientific name for huckleberry oak
