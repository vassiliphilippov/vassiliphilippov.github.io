thermal conductivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɝːməl kənˌdʌktɪˈvɪɾi / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməl kənˌdʌktɪˈvɪti / (BrE)
The property of a material that measures its ability to conduct heat.
ability of a material to conduct heat

Examples

Copper has a high thermal conductivity, making it useful for heat exchangers.
Engineers consider thermal conductivity when choosing materials for insulation.
The thermal conductivity of glass is lower than that of most metals.

Synset Examples

When a material undergoes a phase change from solid to liquid or from liquid to gas the thermal conductivity may change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conductivity - the ability of a material to conduct something, such as electricity or heat
  • Degree Lower: electrical conductivity - the property of a material that measures its ability to conduct electricity

Etymology

From 'thermal' (relating to heat) and 'conductivity' (ability to conduct); ultimately from Greek 'thermos' (hot).
