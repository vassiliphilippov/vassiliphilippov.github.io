genus Grison

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡrɪzən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡrɪzən / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the weasel family (Mustelidae), comprising the grisons.
a genus of mustelids (grisons)
medical

Examples

The genus Grison consists of small, carnivorous mammals native to Central and South America.
Biologists compared several species within the genus Grison to study their evolutionary relationships.
The genus Grison is notable for its members' elongated bodies and agile hunting behavior.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mustelidae - the family of carnivorous mammals including weasels, otters, and badgers
  • Degree Lower: grison - a species within the genus Grison, a small carnivorous mammal

Etymology

From New Latin genus (kind, group) and 'Grison', the common name for members of the genus.
