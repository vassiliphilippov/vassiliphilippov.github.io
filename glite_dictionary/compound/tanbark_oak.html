tanbark oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈtænˌbɑrk oʊk / (AmE), / ˈtanˌbɑːk əʊk / (BrE)
an evergreen tree of the Pacific coast of North America, with large leathery leaves, valued for its bark used in tanning leather
evergreen tree yielding bark for tanning

Examples

The tanbark oak is easily recognized by its thick, leathery leaves and rough bark.
Early settlers used the bark of the tanbark oak for tanning leather.
Many native animals find shelter among the roots of the tanbark oak in the coastal forests.
