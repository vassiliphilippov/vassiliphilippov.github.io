line of vision

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / laɪn əv ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
an imaginary straight line along which a person looks or sees
direction or path of a person's sight

Examples

The monument was directly in his line of vision, blocking his view of the park.
Please keep your hands out of my line of vision while I'm driving.
A sudden movement in her line of vision caught her attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line of sight - an imaginary line from an observer's eye to a viewed object
