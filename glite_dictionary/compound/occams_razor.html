Occam's Razor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑkəmz ˈreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɒkəmz ˈreɪzə / (BrE)
the principle that the simplest solution or explanation is most likely to be correct, and that unnecessary assumptions should be avoided
principle favoring simpler explanations

Examples

When faced with several explanations for the data, scientists often apply Occam's Razor to select the simplest one.
In solving mysteries, detectives frequently invoke Occam's Razor to rule out unlikely scenarios.
Philosophers debate whether Occam's Razor truly leads to truth or just to convenient simplicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principle of parsimony - the scientific idea that simpler explanations are preferable

Etymology

Named after William of Ockham, an English medieval philosopher (c. 1287–1347).
