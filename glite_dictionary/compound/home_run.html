home run

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm rʌn / (AmE), / həʊm rʌn / (BrE)
a hit in baseball that allows the batter to round all the bases and score a run
baseball hit allowing a run to score

Examples

The crowd erupted in cheers when he hit a home run in the final inning.
She broke the school's record with her fifth home run of the season.
He dreamed of hitting a home run at his first professional baseball game.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: grand slam - a home run hit when all bases are occupied, scoring four runs

2. NOUN

/ hoʊm ˈrʌn / (AmE), / həʊm ˈrʌn / (BrE)
A result or action that is outstandingly successful or achieves its intended objective.
outstanding success or achievement

Examples

The company's latest product launch was a home run with both critics and customers.
Her suggestion turned out to be a home run for the team, solving many problems at once.
The new policy is a home run for improving workplace efficiency.

Synset Examples

the new advertising campaign was a bell ringer

hit the mark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull's eye - an achievement that precisely hits the goal; perfect success
  • Synonym: hit the mark - to achieve exactly what was intended or aimed for
  • Antonym: to miss the mark - to fail to achieve the intended result

Etymology

Figurative use from the baseball term 'home run', meaning a hit allowing the batter to round all bases and score.
