gastrointestinal hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌgæstroʊˌɪntaɪˈstɪnəl ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌgæstrəʊˌɪntɛˈstɪnəl ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone that is produced in the gastrointestinal tract and regulates its function, such as digestion and motility
hormone affecting the digestive system
medical

Examples

Gastrin is a type of gastrointestinal hormone that stimulates acid production in the stomach.
Doctors test levels of gastrointestinal hormones in patients with certain digestive disorders.
The release of a gastrointestinal hormone can influence how quickly food moves through the intestines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone - a regulatory substance produced in an organism to stimulate specific cells or tissues

Etymology

From gastro- (relating to the stomach) + intestinal (relating to the intestine) + hormone (from Greek hormōn, 'stimulating').
