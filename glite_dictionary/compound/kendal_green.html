Kendal green

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛndəl ɡriːn / (AmE), / ˈkɛndəl ɡriːn / (BrE)
a green dye or the cloth colored with it, traditionally made from woad and associated with the town of Kendal in England
green dye or cloth colored with woad

Examples

In medieval England, Robin Hood was often described as wearing Kendal green.
The actor’s costume included a cloak dyed in Kendal green for authenticity.
Textiles colored with Kendal green were highly prized in the 16th century.

Etymology

Named after Kendal, a town in Cumbria, England, historically known for producing green-dyed cloth.
