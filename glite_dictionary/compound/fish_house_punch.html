fish house punch

1. NOUN

/ fɪʃ haʊs pʌntʃ / (AmE), / fɪʃ haʊs pʌntʃ / (BrE)
a traditional punch made with rum, brandy, water or tea, and sweetened with sugar syrup
punch made with rum, brandy, and sweeteners

Examples

He prepared a large bowl of fish house punch for the summer party.
The recipe for fish house punch has been passed down through generations.
Guests enjoyed glasses of fish house punch at the festive gathering.

Etymology

Named after the Schuylkill Fishing Company (also known as the 'Fish House'), an 18th-century social club in Philadelphia where the drink originated.
