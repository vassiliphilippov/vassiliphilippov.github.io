ramification of

1. NOUN

/ ˌræmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən ʌv / (AmE), / ˌræmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən ɒv / (BrE)
the consequences or outcomes resulting from a particular decision or action
consequences of a decision or action

Examples

The CEO carefully considered the ramification of expanding the company overseas.
You should think about the ramification of breaking the agreement before making a decision.
They underestimated the ramification of introducing such a high tax on consumers.

Synset Examples

They discussed the ramification of the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: result - something that happens as an outcome of an event or process

Etymology

From 'ramification' (from French 'ramification', from Latin 'ramificatio', branching), plus the preposition 'of'.
