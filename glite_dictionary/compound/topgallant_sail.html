topgallant sail

1. NOUN

/ tɑpˌgæl.ənt seɪl / (AmE), / tɒpˌɡæl.ənt seɪl / (BrE)
A sail set above the topsail on the topgallant mast of a square-rigged ship.
sail above the topsail on a square-rigged ship

Examples

The crew quickly climbed the rigging to furl the topgallant sail as the storm approached.
With a fresh wind, the captain ordered the topgallant sail to be set for greater speed.
The topgallant sail fluttered as the ship changed course to port.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: topsail - a sail set above the lowest sail on a mast

Etymology

From 'topgallant' (referring to the mast above the topmast) + 'sail'.
