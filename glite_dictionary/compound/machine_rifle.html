machine rifle

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃin ˈraɪfəl / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn ˈraɪfl̩ / (BrE)
a self-loading rifle capable of automatic fire, able to fire multiple rounds with a single pull of the trigger
automatic self-loading rifle

Examples

The soldier carried a machine rifle equipped with a high-capacity magazine.
During the demonstration, engineers tested the new machine rifle under various conditions.
Most modern armies provide troops with a standard-issue machine rifle for increased firepower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submachine gun - a lightweight, automatic gun firing pistol ammunition
  • Synonym: assault rifle - a lightweight rifle capable of selective fire
  • Degree Lower: semi-automatic rifle - a self-loading rifle that fires one shot per trigger pull

Etymology

Formed from 'machine' and 'rifle'; 'machine' in the sense of mechanical automation and 'rifle' from French 'rifler', to groove.
