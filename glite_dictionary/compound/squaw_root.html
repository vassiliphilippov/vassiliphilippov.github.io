squaw root

1. NOUN

/ skwɔ ˌruːt / (AmE), / skwɔː ˌruːt / (BrE)
a tall herb native to eastern North America and Asia, with blue, berrylike fruit and a thick, knotty rootstock formerly used medicinally
herb with blue fruit and medicinal root

Examples

Many indigenous communities once used squaw root for medicinal purposes.
The botanist collected a specimen of squaw root during his field trip.
In the forest, squaw root is easily recognized by its blue, berrylike fruit.
