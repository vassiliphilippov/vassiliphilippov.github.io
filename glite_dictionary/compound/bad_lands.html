Bad Lands

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæd ˌlændz / (AmE), / ˈbæd ˌlændz / (BrE)
A region of heavily eroded, barren land in southwestern South Dakota and northwestern Nebraska, notable for its striking landscapes.
eroded, barren region in the US Midwest

Examples

The rugged beauty of the Bad Lands attracts thousands of visitors each year.
Many early settlers avoided the Bad Lands because of its harsh, unforgiving terrain.
Photographers love capturing sunrise over the dramatic rock formations in the Bad Lands.

Etymology

From 'bad' (unfavorable) + 'lands', referring to the area's difficulty for travel and agriculture; popularized by early French trappers as 'les mauvaises terres.'
