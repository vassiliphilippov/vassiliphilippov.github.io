endangered species

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdeɪndʒɚd ˈspiʃiz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdeɪndʒəd ˈspiːʃiːz / (BrE)
A species of animal or plant that is seriously at risk of extinction because its population is very small or declining.
species at serious risk of extinction

Examples

The giant panda is considered an endangered species due to habitat loss and low birth rates.
Laws protect endangered species from hunting and habitat destruction.
Conservationists work tirelessly to save endangered species around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: extinct species - species that no longer exists
  • Degree Lower: threatened species - species likely to become endangered in the near future
