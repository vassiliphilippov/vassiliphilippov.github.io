brake disk

1. NOUN

/ breɪk dɪsk / (AmE), / breɪk dɪsk / (BrE)
A flat, circular metal plate attached to the wheel of a vehicle, against which brake pads press to slow down or stop the wheel's rotation.
metal plate in disc brake system

Examples

The mechanic replaced the worn brake disk to ensure safe stopping power.
If you hear a screeching noise, it might be a sign that your brake disk is damaged.
Some high-performance cars use ventilated brake disks to improve cooling during heavy braking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brake pad - a component that presses against the brake disk to create friction and slow the wheel
