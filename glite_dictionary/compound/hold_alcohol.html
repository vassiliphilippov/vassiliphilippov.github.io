to hold alcohol

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈælkəhɔl / (AmE), / həʊld ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
to be able to drink alcoholic beverages without becoming noticeably drunk or incapacitated
to tolerate drinking alcohol well

Examples

Despite drinking several glasses of wine, she was able to hold alcohol and remained composed all evening.
Some people cannot hold alcohol and become tipsy very quickly.
He boasts that he can hold alcohol better than anyone else in the group.

Synset Examples

She prides herself on being able to hold her alcohol well.
She struggles to hold her liquor after just two drinks.
He can hold his liquor better than anyone I know.
You need to learn how to hold your liquor at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold one's liquor - to be able to drink alcohol without becoming drunk
