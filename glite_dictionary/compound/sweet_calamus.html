sweet calamus

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈkæləməs / (AmE), / swiːt ˈkæləməs / (BrE)
a perennial marsh plant with sword-shaped leaves and aromatic roots, used in herbal remedies and flavorings
aromatic marsh plant with swordlike leaves

Examples

The herbalist recommended using sweet calamus for its aromatic properties.
We found sweet calamus growing along the edge of the marshy pond.
Some traditional recipes call for dried roots of sweet calamus as a flavouring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamus - a marsh plant with aromatic roots, also called sweet flag
