immunoglobulin G

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmjunoʊɡləˌbjuːlɪn ˈdʒi / (AmE), / ˌɪmjuːnəʊˌɡlɒbjʊlɪn ˈdʒi / (BrE)
the most common type of antibody in the blood, important in fighting bacterial and viral infections
the main antibody in the blood
medical

Examples

A blood test revealed high levels of immunoglobulin G in the patient.
Immunoglobulin G plays a crucial role in defending against bacterial infections.
Most vaccines are designed to stimulate the production of immunoglobulin G.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens
  • Degree Lower: immunoglobulin M - the first type of antibody formed in response to an infection
  • Degree Higher: immunoglobulin A - an antibody playing a crucial role in immune function of mucous membranes

Etymology

From 'immunoglobulin' (immune globule protein) and the designation 'G' for its class.
