Santa Lucia fir

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæn.tə luˈsiː.ə fɝː / (AmE), / ˌsæn.tə luˈsiː.ə fɜː / (BrE)
a species of fir tree native to southwestern California, distinguished by its pyramidal shape, sharp-pointed leaves, and cones with long spines
a fir tree species from southwestern California

Examples

The Santa Lucia fir is considered one of the rarest firs in the world.
Hikers admired the distinctive spiny cones of the Santa Lucia fir along the trail.
Efforts are underway to preserve the endangered Santa Lucia fir in its natural habitat.

Etymology

Named after the Santa Lucia Mountains in California, where the tree is native.
