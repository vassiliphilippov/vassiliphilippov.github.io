two-toed anteater

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː ˌtoʊd ˈæntiˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtuː ˌtəʊd ˈæntiˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a small South American anteater with long, silky golden fur and two prominent claws on each forefoot
small South American anteater species

Examples

The two-toed anteater spends much of its time curled up in trees, hidden among the leaves.
Scientists recently discovered a new population of two-toed anteaters in the Amazon rainforest.
The two-toed anteater uses its long tongue to feed on ants and termites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky anteater - another common name for the two-toed anteater
