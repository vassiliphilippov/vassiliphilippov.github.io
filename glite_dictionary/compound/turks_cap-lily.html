Turk's cap-lily

1. NOUN

/ tɝks kæp ˈlɪli / (AmE), / tɜːks kæp ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A lily native to eastern North America, with orange to red flowers marked with maroon spots, typically bearing petals curved backward.
a North American lily with spotted, recurved flowers

Examples

The garden was enhanced by the striking blooms of the Turk's cap-lily beside the pond.
We spotted a rare Turk's cap-lily during our hike in the Appalachian Mountains.
Horticulturists prize the Turk's cap-lily for its dramatic, downward-facing flowers and spotted petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant with large, prominent flowers in the genus Lilium

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its recurved petals to a Turkish turban or cap.
