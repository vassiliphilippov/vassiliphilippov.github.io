Greek cross

1. NOUN

/ ɡrik krɑs / (AmE), / ɡriːk krɒs / (BrE)
a cross with four arms of equal length, often used as a symbol in Christian art and architecture
cross with four equal arms

Examples

The museum displayed a Greek cross from the Byzantine era.
In Eastern Orthodox churches, the floor plan often resembles a Greek cross.
She wore a pendant shaped like a Greek cross on her necklace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Latin cross - a cross with a longer descending arm than the other three

Etymology

From Greek use in Christian symbolism; distinguishes from 'Latin cross.'
