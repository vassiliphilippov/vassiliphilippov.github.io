zinc carbonate

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈkɑrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound (ZnCO₃) used as a mild astringent and in dusting powders, as well as in chemical applications
a white crystalline chemical compound
medical

Examples

The pharmacist recommended using zinc carbonate powder to relieve skin irritation.
During the experiment, we observed the reaction of zinc carbonate with hydrochloric acid.
Many dusting powders for infants contain zinc carbonate as a gentle astringent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc oxide - a white powder used in ointments and creams for skin protection

Etymology

From 'zinc', ultimately from German 'Zink', and 'carbonate' from Latin 'carbonas' (charcoal, coal, carbon).
