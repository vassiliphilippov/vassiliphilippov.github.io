Jerusalem artichoke sunflower

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈruːsələm ˈɑːrtɪˌtʃoʊk ˈsʌnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / dʒəˈruːsələm ˈɑːtɪˌtʃəʊk ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant (Helianthus tuberosus) with hairy stems and leaves, cultivated for its large, irregular, edible tubers; also known as Jerusalem artichoke
plant with edible tubers, Jerusalem artichoke

Examples

The Jerusalem artichoke sunflower is cultivated for its knobby, edible tubers in many regions.
She harvested several Jerusalem artichoke sunflower tubers from her garden for making soup.
Farmers appreciate the Jerusalem artichoke sunflower for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerusalem artichoke - edible tuber of the plant Helianthus tuberosus

Etymology

The name 'Jerusalem artichoke' is a misinterpretation of the Italian 'girasole' (sunflower); 'sunflower' refers to its botanical genus Helianthus.
