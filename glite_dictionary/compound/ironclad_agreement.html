ironclad agreement

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚnˌklæd əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌklæd əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
an agreement that is firm, secure, and very difficult to break or alter
a very strong, binding agreement

Examples

The two companies entered into an ironclad agreement to collaborate for the next decade.
She insisted on an ironclad agreement before sharing her proprietary information.
Negotiating an ironclad agreement ensured both parties felt legally protected.

Synset Examples

They made an ironclad agreement to protect their interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binding agreement - an agreement that is legally or morally obligating
  • Antonym: verbal agreement - an agreement made orally rather than in writing

Etymology

From 'ironclad' (covered or protected with iron) used figuratively to mean 'strong; unbreakable', + 'agreement'.
