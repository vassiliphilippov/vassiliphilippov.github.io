order Myxobacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ ˌmɪksəboʊˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌmɪksəʊbəˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of higher bacteria characterized by their ability to move in swarms and produce fruiting bodies
order of higher bacteria
medical

Examples

Scientists have discovered several new species within the order Myxobacteria.
The order Myxobacteria is noted for its complex life cycle involving the formation of fruiting bodies.
Research on the order Myxobacteria has advanced our understanding of bacterial social behavior.

Etymology

From Greek myxa (mucus) + bacteria, indicating the slimy nature of these bacteria.
