field poppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˈpɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
an annual European poppy (Papaver rhoeas) with bright red flowers, commonly found in grain fields and often cultivated as an ornamental plant
annual European poppy with red flowers

Examples

The field poppy is easily recognized by its vivid red petals scattered across the countryside.
Many farmers consider the field poppy a beautiful but troublesome weed in their wheat fields.
Each spring, field poppies bloom in abundance, creating a sea of red among the golden crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn poppy - another common name for Papaver rhoeas
