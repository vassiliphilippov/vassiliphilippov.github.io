happiness incarnate

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæpinəs ɪnˈkɑrnət / (AmE), / ˈhæpinəs ɪnˈkɑːnət / (BrE)
the embodiment or perfect example of happiness, as if happiness were made flesh
embodiment of happiness

Examples

Her smile was pure happiness incarnate and brightened everyone's day.
He walked into the room, radiating happiness incarnate with every step.
Some people are so cheerful, they seem to be happiness incarnate.

Synset Examples

She considered love to be happiness incarnate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joy incarnate - the embodiment of joy
  • Antonym: misery incarnate - the embodiment of misery

Etymology

From Latin 'incarnatus' (made flesh), used in English for something embodied in human form.
