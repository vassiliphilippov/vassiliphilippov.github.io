rifleman bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəlmən bɝd / (AmE), / ˈraɪflmən bɜːd / (BrE)
a very small green-and-bronze songbird native to New Zealand, known for its thin bill and active nature
tiny New Zealand green-and-bronze songbird

Examples

We spotted a rifleman bird darting among the branches during our walk in New Zealand.
The rifleman bird is notable for its tiny size and bronze-green feathers.
Many birdwatchers travel just to see the rare rifleman bird in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acanthisitta - genus including the rifleman bird

Etymology

Named for its resemblance in coloring to the uniforms of colonial riflemen in New Zealand, combined with 'bird'.
