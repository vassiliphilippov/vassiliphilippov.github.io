chlorous acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːɹəs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəs ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A strong oxidizing acid (HClO₂) known only in solution; an unstable compound formed by dissolving chlorine dioxide in water.
a strong oxidizing acid (HClO₂) known only in solution
medical

Examples

Chlorous acid is an intermediate in the production of certain chlorine-based disinfectants.
The instability of chlorous acid makes it difficult to isolate in pure form.
Researchers study the reactions of chlorous acid due to its strong oxidizing properties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chloric acid - A related oxyacid of chlorine (HClO₃) with a higher oxidation state
  • Degree Lower: hypochlorous acid - A related oxyacid of chlorine (HClO) with a lower oxidation state

Etymology

From 'chlorous' (referring to chlorine in a +3 oxidation state) and 'acid'.
