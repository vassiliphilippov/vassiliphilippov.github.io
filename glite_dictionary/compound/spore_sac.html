spore sac

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔr ˌsæk / (AmE), / ˈspɔː ˌsæk / (BrE)
an organ or structure in which spores are formed or contained, especially in fungi or ferns
organ that contains or produces spores

Examples

Many fungi release their spores when the spore sac bursts open.
The fern's spore sacs can be seen as small clusters under its leaves.
Scientists observed the development of the spore sac in the microscopic images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sporangium - a structure in which spores are produced
