Molisch's test

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlɪʃɪz tɛst / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪʃɪz tɛst / (BrE)
a chemical test for detecting the presence of carbohydrates in a solution, which gives a violet ring when carbohydrates react with alpha-naphthol and sulfuric acid
chemical test for carbohydrates
medical

Examples

Using Molisch's test, the scientists confirmed the presence of carbohydrates in the sample.
The violet ring in Molisch's test is a positive indication of sugar.
Many biology labs teach students to perform Molisch's test during experiments involving plant extracts.

Etymology

Named after Hans Molisch, Austrian botanist (1856–1937), who developed the test.
