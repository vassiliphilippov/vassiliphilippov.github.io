coal size

1. NOUN

/ koʊl saɪz / (AmE), / kəʊl saɪz / (BrE)
the grade or category of coal determined by the dimensions of its pieces after being broken and sorted for commercial use
category of coal by piece size

Examples

The shipment was rejected because the coal size did not meet the power plant's requirements.
Operators adjust the machinery to ensure uniform coal size for specific industrial applications.
Each grade of coal size is destined for a different type of consumer.

Synset Examples

The fuel would move over sorting bars, with the various sizes of coal going down different chutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain size - the size of individual particles in a material
