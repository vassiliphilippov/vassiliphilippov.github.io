menstrual flow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnstruəl floʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛnstrʊəl fləʊ / (BrE)
the flow of blood from the uterus during menstruation, occurring about once a month in women of reproductive age
monthly discharge of blood during menstruation
medical

Examples

Irregular menstrual flow can sometimes be a sign of hormonal imbalance.
She kept track of her menstrual flow in a diary for her doctor.
The average duration of menstrual flow is between three and seven days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menstruation - the monthly process where blood is discharged from the uterus
  • Synonym: period - informal term for menstrual flow or menstruation

Etymology

From Latin 'menstruus' (monthly), relating to the menstrual cycle, and 'flow'.
