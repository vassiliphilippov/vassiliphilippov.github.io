solar plexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
A complex network of sympathetic nerves in the abdomen located behind the stomach, playing a major role in the autonomic nervous system.
nerve network in the abdomen
medical

Examples

A blow to the solar plexus can knock the wind out of a person.
Doctors explained that the solar plexus controls many involuntary functions, such as digestion.
He doubled over in pain after being struck in the solar plexus during the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coeliac plexus - another name for the solar plexus

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (braid, network) and 'solar' referencing its radiating structure resembling the sun.
