gold foil

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld fɔɪl / (AmE), / ɡəʊld fɔɪl / (BrE)
Extremely thin sheets of gold, used for decoration, art, dentistry, and scientific experiments.
very thin sheets of gold

Examples

The artist applied gold foil to the sculpture to enhance its beauty.
Scientists used gold foil in the famous Rutherford experiment to study atomic structure.
Sheets of gold foil are often used for gilding picture frames and religious icons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold leaf - very thin sheet of gold used for gilding
