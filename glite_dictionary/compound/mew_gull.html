mew gull

1. NOUN

/ mju ˈɡʌl / (AmE), / mjuː ˈɡʌl / (BrE)
a medium-sized gull (Larus canus) found in Eurasia and northeastern North America, with a grey back and white underparts
a medium-sized Eurasian and North American gull

Examples

The mew gull is often seen along coastal regions during migration season.
I spotted a flock of mew gulls resting by the lake.
Unlike other gulls, the mew gull has a softer call and a less aggressive demeanor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common gull - another name for the same species (Larus canus)
