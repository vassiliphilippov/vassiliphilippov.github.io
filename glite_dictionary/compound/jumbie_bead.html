jumbie bead

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmbi biːd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmbi biːd / (BrE)
A West Indian tree that resembles Ormosia monosperma but is larger, with smaller leaflets and smaller seeds.
West Indian tree with distinctive seeds

Examples

Children in the village collect jumbie beads to make colorful necklaces.
The seeds of the jumbie bead are often used as ornaments in local crafts.
I found a jumbie bead tree growing along the riverbank during our hike.

Etymology

From Caribbean English, probably influenced by 'jumbie' (spirit or ghost) and 'bead' (referring to the bright red seeds).
