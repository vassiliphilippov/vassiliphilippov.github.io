auditory system

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdəˌtɔri ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtəri ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the sensory system responsible for hearing, including the ear and related neural pathways
the sensory system for hearing
medical

Examples

The auditory system is essential for processing and interpreting sounds in our environment.
Damage to the auditory system can result in partial or complete hearing loss.
Scientists study the auditory system to better understand how we perceive music and speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearing - the sense by which sound is perceived

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear) and 'systema' (system).
