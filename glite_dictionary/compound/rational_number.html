rational number

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃənəl ˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəl ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number that can be expressed as a fraction or the ratio of two integers
a number expressible as a fraction

Examples

Every rational number can be written as a simple fraction like 3/4 or 7.
Zero is also considered a rational number because it can be written as 0/1.
All repeating decimals represent a rational number in fractional form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrational number - a number that cannot be expressed as a ratio of two integers
  • Degree Lower: integer - a whole number, positive, negative, or zero, without fractions
  • Degree Higher: real number - any number on the number line, including rational and irrational numbers

Etymology

From Latin rationalis (rational) + number.
