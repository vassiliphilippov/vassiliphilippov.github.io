belt of the crozier

1. NOUN

/ bɛlt əv ðə ˈkroʊʒər / (AmE), / bɛlt əv ðə ˈkrəʊziə / (BrE)
A ceremonial belt associated with a bishop's staff, symbolizing authority in church ceremonies.
ceremonial church belt symbolizing authority

Examples

The bishop carefully adjusted the belt of the crozier before the service began.
During the ceremony, the ornate belt of the crozier was admired by all present.
The tradition of wearing the belt of the crozier dates back centuries in the church.

Synset Examples

Tradition dictates that the belt of the crozier signifies authority.
The bishop wore the belt of the crozier during the ceremony.
