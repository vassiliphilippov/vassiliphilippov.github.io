valuation reserve

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæljuˈeɪʃən rɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / ˌvæljuˈeɪʃən rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
an amount of money set aside from profits to cover possible changes in the value of a company's assets
fund for asset value changes

Examples

The company increased its valuation reserve to protect against potential losses in asset value.
Auditors recommended establishing a valuation reserve for the firm's real estate holdings.
Changes in asset prices led to an adjustment of the valuation reserve at year-end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve fund - money set aside for a specific future purpose
