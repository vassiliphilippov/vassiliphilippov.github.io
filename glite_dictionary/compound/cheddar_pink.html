cheddar pink

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛdər pɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛdə pɪŋk / (BrE)
a mat-forming perennial plant of central Europe, known for its large, fragrant pink or red flowers, especially in gardens
a fragrant perennial flowering plant

Examples

The cheddar pink thrives in rocky gardens and adds vibrant color in late spring.
She planted several cheddar pinks along the garden border for their fragrance.
The cheddar pink is admired for its hardy nature and beautiful blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dianthus - a genus of flowering plants that includes carnations and pinks

Etymology

Named after Cheddar Gorge, Somerset, England, where the plant was first found, and 'pink,' the traditional name for flowers in the genus Dianthus.
