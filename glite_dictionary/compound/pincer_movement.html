pincer movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnsɚ ˌmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪnsə ˌmuːvmənt / (BrE)
A military or strategic maneuver in which forces attack an enemy simultaneously from two sides, encircling or trapping them.
military maneuver attacking from two sides

Examples

The army executed a classic pincer movement to surround the enemy forces.
Her negotiating team performed a pincer movement, applying pressure from both management and employees.
During the chess match, he set up a clever pincer movement that cornered his opponent's queen.

Synset Examples

The pincer movement in the game caught my opponent off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envelopment - a military movement to surround the enemy

Etymology

From 'pincer', referring to a grasping or pinching tool, describing the action of closing from two sides.
