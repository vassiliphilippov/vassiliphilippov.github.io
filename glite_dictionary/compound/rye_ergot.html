rye ergot

1. NOUN

/ raɪ ˈɜrɡət / (AmE), / raɪ ˈɜːɡət / (BrE)
a hard, compact mass (sclerotium) of fungal mycelium forming on rye and other cereals, often associated with the fungus Claviceps purpurea
fungal sclerotium on rye plants

Examples

Rye ergot can be toxic to humans if ground into flour and consumed in bread.
Botanists collected samples of rye ergot from the infected grain fields for laboratory analysis.
Historically, outbreaks of rye ergot poisoning led to severe health problems in rural communities.

Etymology

From 'rye' (the cereal grain) and 'ergot,' from French 'ergot' (spur), referring to the spur-like shape of the sclerotium.
