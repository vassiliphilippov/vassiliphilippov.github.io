Mexican War

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛk.sɪ.kən wɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɛk.sɪ.kən wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought between the United States and Mexico from 1846 to 1848, resulting in the U.S. acquisition of territories including California, Arizona, and New Mexico.
war between the U.S. and Mexico, 1846–1848

Examples

The Mexican War significantly expanded the territory of the United States in the 19th century.
Many historians argue that the causes of the Mexican War were rooted in the annexation of Texas.
The treaty that ended the Mexican War was called the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican–American War - alternative name for the Mexican War
