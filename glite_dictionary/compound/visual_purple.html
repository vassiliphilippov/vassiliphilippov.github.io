visual purple

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl ˈpɜːpl / (BrE)
a reddish-purple pigment found in the retinal rods of vertebrates, that is sensitive to light and is involved in vision in dim light
pigment in the retina aiding dim light vision
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered that visual purple is essential for seeing in low-light conditions.
The decomposition of visual purple by light is important in the process of vision.
Researchers study how vitamin A affects the regeneration of visual purple in the retina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhodopsin - the scientific name for visual purple
