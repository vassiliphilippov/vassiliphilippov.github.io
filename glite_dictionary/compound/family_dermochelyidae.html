family Dermochelyidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dɜrˌmoʊˌkiːliˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dɜːˌmɒkiːlɪˈiːdaɪ / (BrE)
A family of large, typically leathery-shelled sea turtles, known as the leatherback turtles.
family of sea turtles (leatherbacks)

Examples

The family Dermochelyidae includes the largest of all living turtles, the leatherback turtles.
Marine biologists are studying threats to species within the family Dermochelyidae.
Unlike hard-shelled sea turtles, members of the family Dermochelyidae have flexible, leathery shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherback turtle - large sea turtle with leathery shell (Dermochelys coriacea)

Etymology

From New Latin Dermochelyidae, based on the type genus Dermochelys (from Greek derma: skin, and chelys: turtle).
