freak show

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrik ˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfriːk ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
An event or performance featuring people, acts, or situations that are unusual, bizarre, or sensational, often presented for entertainment or shock value. Sometimes used figuratively to describe something strange or chaotic.
event or display of bizarre acts or situations

Examples

The reality competition quickly devolved into a freak show as contestants performed increasingly bizarre stunts.
Some critics called the tabloid's coverage of the scandal a media freak show.
He felt uncomfortable at the carnival's freak show, finding it exploitative and unsettling.

Synset Examples

Reality TV often feels like a freak show to me.
Her stories about her travels sounded like a freak show.
The circus was a real freak show last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideshow - a minor, secondary show at a fair, often featuring unusual acts

Etymology

From 'freak' (a person or thing that is unusual) and 'show' (a public entertainment).
