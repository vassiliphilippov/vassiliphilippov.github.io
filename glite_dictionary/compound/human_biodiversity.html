human biodiversity

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən ˌbaɪoʊdaɪˈvɜːrsəti / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ˌbʌɪəʊdaɪˈvɜːsɪti / (BrE)
the range of genetic differences and cultural traits among humans
variety of human genetic and cultural traits

Examples

Understanding human biodiversity helps scientists study how populations adapt to different environments.
Preserving human biodiversity is essential for maintaining a rich variety of languages and traditions.
Educational programs on human biodiversity promote respect for both genetic and cultural differences.

Synset Examples

Human biodiversity enriches our global community.
Protecting human biodiversity ensures the survival of traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biodiversity - the variety of life in the world or in a particular habitat

Etymology

From 'human' (relating to people) + 'biodiversity' (from 'biological' and 'diversity').
