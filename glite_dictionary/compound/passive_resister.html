passive resister

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv rɪˈzɪstər / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv rɪˈzɪstə / (BrE)
a person who advocates or practices passive resistance as a method to bring about social or political change
person engaging in passive resistance

Examples

As a well-known passive resister, she inspired others to join the nonviolent protest.
The passive resister was arrested for refusing to leave the demonstration site.
Gandhi is perhaps the most famous passive resister in modern history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifist - a person who believes that war and violence are unjustifiable
  • Synonym: activist - a person who campaigns for social or political change

Etymology

From 'passive' + 'resister'; 'passive resistance' dates to the late 19th century, especially in reference to Gandhi's methods.
