nasal twang

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.zəl twæŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.zəl twæŋ / (BrE)
a distinctive, exaggerated nasal quality in the sound of a person's speech, often characteristic of certain regional accents
exaggerated nasal quality in speech

Examples

Her nasal twang made it easy to guess she was from the Midwest.
Some comedians exaggerate a nasal twang to mimic certain accents on stage.
After living in the region for years, he picked up a slight nasal twang in his speech.
