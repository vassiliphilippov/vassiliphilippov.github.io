magnesium hydroxide

1. NOUN

/ mægˌniːziəm haɪˈdrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / mægˌniːziəm haɪˈdrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline powder used mainly as an antacid and laxative
white powder used as an antacid or laxative
medical

Examples

Magnesium hydroxide is commonly used to relieve symptoms of indigestion and heartburn.
The doctor recommended taking magnesium hydroxide as a mild laxative for occasional constipation.
Magnesium hydroxide appears as a white powder and is barely soluble in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milk of magnesia - a suspension of magnesium hydroxide used as an antacid and laxative

Etymology

magnesium: from New Latin *magnesium*, from Magnesia (a district in Thessaly, Greece); hydroxide: from 'hydro-' (water) and '-oxide' (a compound with oxygen).
