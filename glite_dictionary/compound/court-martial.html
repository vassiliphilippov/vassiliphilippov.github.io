court-martial

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrt ˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːt ˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
a military court that tries members of the armed forces accused of breaking military law, or the trial itself conducted by such a court
a military trial or the court holding it

Examples

The captain faced a court-martial for disobeying direct orders.
Three soldiers were found guilty at their court-martial last week.
If convicted by court-martial, he could be dishonorably discharged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military tribunal - a court judging military legal cases
  • Antonym: civilian court - a non-military court dealing with civilian matters

Etymology

From 'court' (judicial tribunal) and 'martial' (relating to war, from Latin 'martialis').
