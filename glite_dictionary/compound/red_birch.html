red birch

1. NOUN

/ rɛd bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / rɛd bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a birch tree with reddish-brown bark, commonly found in swamps and river bottoms in the eastern United States
birch tree with reddish-brown bark

Examples

The forest was dotted with red birch rising beside the marshy riverbank.
We identified the tree as a red birch by its distinctive reddish-brown bark.
Many birds nest in the branches of the red birch during spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a type of deciduous tree of the genus Betula
