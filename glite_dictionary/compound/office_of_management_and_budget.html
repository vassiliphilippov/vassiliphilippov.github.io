Office of Management and Budget

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfɪs əv ˈmænɪdʒmənt ənd ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪs əv ˈmænɪdʒmənt ənd ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
A U.S. executive agency responsible for assisting the President in preparing the federal budget and overseeing its administration in executive branch agencies.
U.S. agency managing federal budget
american

Examples

The Office of Management and Budget issued new guidelines for government spending.
Congress consults the Office of Management and Budget when drafting budget proposals.
The director of the Office of Management and Budget presented the annual report to the President.
