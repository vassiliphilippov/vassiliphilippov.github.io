disseminated multiple sclerosis

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsɛm.əˌneɪ.tɪd ˈmʌl.tə.pəl skləˈroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛm.ɪˌneɪ.tɪd ˈmʌl.tɪ.pl̩ sklɪˈrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A chronic, progressive disease of the nervous system characterized by scattered patches of loss of myelin sheath around certain nerve fibers.
chronic nervous disorder causing scattered myelin loss
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that disseminated multiple sclerosis causes symptoms that vary according to the areas affected.
She was diagnosed with disseminated multiple sclerosis after experiencing numbness and muscle weakness.
New treatments have improved the quality of life for patients with disseminated multiple sclerosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiple sclerosis - a chronic disease involving damage to the myelin in the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin *disseminatus* (scattered) and New Latin *sclerosis* (hardening).
