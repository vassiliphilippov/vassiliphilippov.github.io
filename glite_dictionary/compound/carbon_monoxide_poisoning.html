carbon monoxide poisoning

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrbən məˈnɑksaid ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən məˈnɒksaɪd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
A potentially fatal illness caused by inhaling and absorbing carbon monoxide gas, leading to a lack of oxygen in the body.
illness from carbon monoxide exposure
medical

Examples

Firefighters rescued several people suffering from carbon monoxide poisoning after a faulty heater leaked gas overnight.
Early symptoms of carbon monoxide poisoning include headache, dizziness, and nausea, which can easily be mistaken for the flu.
The hospital treated a family for carbon monoxide poisoning caused by using a portable generator indoors during a power cut.

Synset Examples

Smoke inhalation and carbon monoxide poisoning killed 91 miners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon dioxide poisoning - a toxic condition caused by exposure to carbon dioxide
