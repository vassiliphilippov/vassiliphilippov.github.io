Kebecois cuisine

1. NOUN

/ kɛbɛˈkwɑː kwiˈzin / (AmE), / kɛbɛˈkwɑː kwiˈziːn / (BrE)
the traditional foods and culinary practices of Quebec, Canada, especially those reflecting its French heritage.
traditional food of Quebec, Canada

Examples

We sampled a variety of Kebecois cuisine during our stay in Montreal.
Her grandmother is famous for her traditional Kebecois cuisine, especially tourtière and sugar pie.
Many restaurants in Quebec City offer authentic Kebecois cuisine using local ingredients.

Synset Examples

The Kebecois cuisine was a highlight of our trip.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Canadian cuisine - the traditional food of Canada as a whole

Etymology

Kebecois from French Québécois (of Quebec), cuisine from French cuisine (kitchen, cooking).
