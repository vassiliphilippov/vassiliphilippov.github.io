terra cotta

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈkɑtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ˈkɒtə / (BrE)
a hard, usually unglazed, brownish-red earthenware used for pottery, architectural decoration, and sculptures
brownish-red earthenware

Examples

The statue was made entirely of terra cotta and had a warm, earthy color.
They used terra cotta tiles for the flooring in the old villa.
Several ancient civilizations crafted pottery and figurines from terra cotta.

Etymology

From Italian 'terra cotta', literally 'baked earth'.
