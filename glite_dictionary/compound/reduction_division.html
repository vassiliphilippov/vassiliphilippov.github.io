reduction division

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʌkʃən dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌkʃən dɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
(genetics) the type of cell division by which reproductive cells (gametes or spores) are formed, in which the number of chromosomes is halved, resulting in four nuclei, each with half the chromosome number of the original cell; also called meiosis.
cell division producing gametes with half chromosome number
medical

Examples

The process of reduction division ensures that offspring inherit the correct number of chromosomes from each parent.
During reduction division, homologous chromosomes separate into different cells.
Biology students observed the stages of reduction division under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meiosis - the process of reduction division in cell biology

Etymology

From Latin reducere (to lead back) and Late Latin divisio (division).
