watt second

1. NOUN

/ wɑt ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / wɒt ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
A unit of electrical energy equal to the energy transferred when one watt of power is used for one second; equivalent to one joule.
unit of electrical energy (one watt for one second)

Examples

The battery stores a total of 3,600 watt seconds of energy.
To calculate the total energy consumed, multiply the power in watts by the time in watt seconds.
A camera's flash may output a few hundred watt seconds of energy when activated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joule - the SI unit of energy, equal to a watt second

Etymology

Named for Scottish engineer James Watt (1736–1819) and from Latin 'secunda' (second, following).
