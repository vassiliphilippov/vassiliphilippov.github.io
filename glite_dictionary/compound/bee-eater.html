bee-eater

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiˌitər / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌiːtə / (BrE)
a brightly colored, mainly tropical Old World bird of the family Meropidae, known for catching and eating bees and other insects in flight
tropical bird that eats bees

Examples

The bee-eater perched gracefully on a branch, scanning the air for insects.
Several species of bee-eater can be seen along the riverbanks during the summer months.
A bee-eater swooped down and snatched a wasp from midair with remarkable precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roller - colorful Old World bird related to bee-eaters
  • Synonym: kingfisher - brightly colored bird related to bee-eaters, often found near water
