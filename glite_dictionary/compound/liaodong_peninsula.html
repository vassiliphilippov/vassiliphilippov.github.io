Liaodong Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ liˌaʊˈdʊŋ pəˈnɪnsələ / (AmE), / liˌaʊˈdɒŋ pəˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
a large peninsula in northeastern China that extends into the Yellow Sea, between the Bohai Sea and Korea Bay
peninsula in northeastern China

Examples

Many historical battles have taken place on the Liaodong Peninsula due to its strategic importance.
The Liaodong Peninsula separates the Bohai Sea from Korea Bay along China's northeast coast.
Tourists often visit the coastal cities on the Liaodong Peninsula for their beautiful beaches and seafood.

Etymology

From Mandarin Liaodong (辽东, literally 'east of Liao'), referring to its position east of the Liao River.
