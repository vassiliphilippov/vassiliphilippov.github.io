Blue Peter

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈpiːtər / (AmE), / bluː ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
a blue flag with a white square in the center, flown by a ship to signal that it is ready to sail
blue flag signaling a ship is ready to sail

Examples

When the Blue Peter is raised, it means the ship is about to leave port.
Sailors hurried to return onboard as soon as they saw the Blue Peter flying.
The captain gave the order to hoist the Blue Peter at sunrise.

Etymology

From the French 'bleu parti' (divided blue), referencing its color and pattern.
