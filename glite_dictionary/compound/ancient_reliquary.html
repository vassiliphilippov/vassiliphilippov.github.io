ancient reliquary

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃənt ˈrɛlɪˌkwɛri / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt ˈrɛlɪkwəri / (BrE)
a container, especially a historical one, for holding sacred relics or remains, often of religious significance
container for sacred relics (historical)

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient reliquary adorned with precious stones and intricate carvings.
Pilgrims traveled for miles to see the ancient reliquary believed to contain the saint's remains.
Historians studied the ancient reliquary to learn more about medieval religious practices.

Synset Examples

The ancient reliquary held the remains of a saint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliquary - a container for relics, especially in a religious context

Etymology

Ancient: from Latin 'ante' (before) + Reliquary: from French 'reliquaire', from Medieval Latin 'reliquiarium' (container for relics).
