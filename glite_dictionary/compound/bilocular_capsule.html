bilocular capsule

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈlɑːkjələr ˈkæp.səl / (AmE), / bɪˈlɒkjʊlə ˈkæp.sjuːl / (BrE)
a botanically classified capsule fruit that is divided into two distinct chambers or compartments
botanical capsule with two compartments
medical

Examples

The poppy produces a bilocular capsule containing numerous seeds in each compartment.
During dissection, the bilocular capsule was carefully opened to study its internal structure.
Many species in the nightshade family bear fruit in the form of a bilocular capsule.

Etymology

From Latin bi- (two) + loculus (compartment) + capsule.
