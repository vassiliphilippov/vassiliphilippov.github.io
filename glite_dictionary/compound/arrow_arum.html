arrow arum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊ ˈɑːrəm / (AmE), / ˈærəʊ ˈɑːrəm / (BrE)
A North American aquatic plant of the genus Peltandra, with arrow-shaped leaves and greenish flowering spikes.
aquatic plant of the genus Peltandra

Examples

The wetlands were dominated by stands of arrow arum and cattails.
In early summer, the leaves of the arrow arum provide shelter for young amphibians.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare species of arrow arum in the reserve.

Etymology

From 'arrow,' referencing the leaf shape, and 'arum,' a plant family from Latin 'arum' (arum plant).
