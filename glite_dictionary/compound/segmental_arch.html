segmental arch

1. NOUN

/ sɛɡˈmɛntəl ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / sɛɡˈmɛntəl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch whose curve is less than a semicircle, typically used in architecture for its shallow span
a shallow architectural arch

Examples

The brick bridge was constructed with a segmental arch to allow for a lower profile across the stream.
Many traditional doorways in Europe feature a segmental arch for both strength and aesthetic appeal.
Engineers chose a segmental arch for the culvert due to its ability to distribute loads efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: semicircular arch - an arch forming a half-circle

Etymology

From 'segment' (a part of a circle) + 'arch', referring to the shape of the structure.
