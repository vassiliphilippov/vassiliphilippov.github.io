vacuum flask

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm flæsk / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm flɑːsk / (BrE)
A container consisting of a double-walled flask with a vacuum between the walls, designed to keep liquids or other substances hot or cold for extended periods by minimizing heat transfer.
insulated container for keeping liquids hot or cold

Examples

She poured hot coffee into a vacuum flask before leaving for the hike.
The vacuum flask kept the soup warm until lunchtime.
Remember to fill the vacuum flask with ice water when you go to the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermos - a brand name commonly used for a vacuum flask

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuum' (empty space) and 'flask'; invented by Sir James Dewar in 1892.
