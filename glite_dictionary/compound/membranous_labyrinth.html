membranous labyrinth

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛmbɹənəs ləˈbɹɪnθ / (AmE), / ˌmɛm.brə.nəs ləˈbɹɪnθ / (BrE)
the soft, membrane-bound system of ducts and sacs within the inner ear, including the labyrinthine receptors and the cochlea, contained within the bony labyrinth
membrane-bound part of the inner ear
medical

Examples

The membranous labyrinth is essential for maintaining balance and hearing in humans.
Damage to the membranous labyrinth can result in vertigo and loss of hearing.
During the dissection, the scientist carefully exposed the membranous labyrinth inside the temporal bone.

Etymology

From Latin membrana (membrane) and Greek labyrinthos (labyrinth).
