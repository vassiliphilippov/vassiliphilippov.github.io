mental deficiency

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.t̬l dɪˈfɪʃ.ən.si / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təl dɪˈfɪʃ.ən.si / (BrE)
historically, a condition in which a person has below average intellectual ability, especially referring to mild mental retardation
historical term for mild intellectual disability
medical archaic

Examples

Medical reports from the early 20th century often used the term mental deficiency for children with learning difficulties.
He was diagnosed with a mild mental deficiency as a child, but received special education support.
Most professionals today avoid the term mental deficiency, using 'intellectual disability' instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual disability - condition of below average intellectual functioning
  • Synonym: mental retardation - outdated term for significant impairment of intellectual functioning
