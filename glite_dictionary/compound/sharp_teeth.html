sharp teeth

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrp tiθ / (AmE), / ʃɑːp tiːθ / (BrE)
teeth that are pointed or able to cut or pierce easily, often found in animals or used metaphorically for humans.
pointed or cutting teeth

Examples

The shark's sharp teeth gleamed as it approached its prey.
She avoided eating hard foods because her sharp teeth could easily cut her tongue.
Many carnivores have evolved sharp teeth to help them catch and eat their food.

Synset Examples

That gnasher is known for its sharp teeth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blunt teeth - teeth that are not pointed or cutting, opposite of sharp teeth
