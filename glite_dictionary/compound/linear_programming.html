linear programming

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɚ proʊˌɡræmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə ˈprəʊɡramɪŋ / (BrE)
a mathematical technique used to find the maximum or minimum value of a linear function of several variables, subject to linear constraints
mathematical optimization method
medical

Examples

The company used linear programming to optimize its supply chain operations.
A basic linear programming problem involves maximizing profit under budget and staffing constraints.
Students learn about linear programming in advanced mathematics and economics classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimization - the process of making something as effective or functional as possible
  • Degree Lower: integer programming - an optimization technique where solutions are required to be integers

Etymology

From 'linear' indicating use of linear functions and 'programming' in the sense of mathematical planning, first used in the mid-20th century.
