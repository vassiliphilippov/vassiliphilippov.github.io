Yugoslavian dinar

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːɡəˈslɑːviən dɪˈnɑːr / (AmE), / ˌjuːɡəˈslɑːviən dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Yugoslavia before its dissolution
former currency unit of Yugoslavia

Examples

When traveling through the Balkans in the 1980s, you needed to exchange your dollars for Yugoslavian dinar.
After the breakup of Yugoslavia, the Yugoslavian dinar was replaced by new national currencies.
I have an old Yugoslavian dinar banknote as a souvenir from my trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinar - the name of various currency units in some countries

Etymology

From Yugoslavia + dinar (from Arabic 'dīnār', originally from Latin 'denarius').
