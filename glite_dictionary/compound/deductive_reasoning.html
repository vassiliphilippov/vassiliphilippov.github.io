deductive reasoning

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʌktɪv ˈrizənɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktɪv ˈriːzənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of reaching a conclusion by applying general principles or rules to specific cases, reasoning from the general to the particular.
reasoning from general to specific

Examples

Scientists use deductive reasoning to apply broad scientific laws to individual cases.
Good detectives often rely on deductive reasoning to solve mysteries from the clues before them.
In mathematics, deductive reasoning is used to prove theorems from accepted axioms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inductive reasoning - reasoning from specific cases to general principles

Etymology

From Latin 'deducere' (to lead down or derive), via 'deductive' + 'reasoning'.
