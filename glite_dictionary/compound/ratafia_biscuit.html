ratafia biscuit

1. NOUN

/ ˌræ.təˈfiː.ə ˈbɪs.kɪt / (AmE), / ˌræt.əˈfiː.ə ˈbɪs.kɪt / (BrE)
A small crisp biscuit flavored with or originally made using ratafia liqueur or essence of bitter almonds.
a small almond-flavored biscuit

Examples

I served the coffee with a plate of ratafia biscuits on the side.
The recipe calls for ground almonds to make authentic ratafia biscuits.
She bought a box of ratafia biscuits from the bakery to try with her tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaroon - a type of small cake or biscuit made with ground almonds, sugar, and egg whites

Etymology

From French 'ratafia' (a type of liqueur), indicating the flavoring used in the biscuit.
