clandestine operation

1. NOUN

/ klænˈdɛstɪn ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / klænˈdɛstɪn ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a secret activity planned and carried out to ensure it remains hidden, especially in intelligence or military contexts
a secret intelligence or military activity

Examples

The agency denied any involvement in the clandestine operation that was uncovered last week.
Only a handful of agents knew the real purpose of the clandestine operation.
News reports revealed that a clandestine operation had been underway for several months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covert operation - a military or intelligence operation intended to conceal the identity of its sponsor

Etymology

From Latin 'clandestinus' (secret, hidden) + English 'operation' (from Latin 'operatio').
