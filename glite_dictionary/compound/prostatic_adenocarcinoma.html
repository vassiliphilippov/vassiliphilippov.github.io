prostatic adenocarcinoma

1. NOUN

/ prəˈstætɪk ˌædɪnoʊˌkɑrsɪˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / prɒˈstatɪk ˌadɪnəʊˌkɑːsɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor originating in the glandular tissue of the prostate gland; the most common form of prostate cancer.
type of prostate cancer
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with prostatic adenocarcinoma after a series of tests and a biopsy.
Recent advances in treatment have improved the prognosis for men with prostatic adenocarcinoma.
Routine screening is recommended for the early detection of prostatic adenocarcinoma in older men.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostate cancer - cancer that occurs in the prostate gland

Etymology

From 'prostatic' (relating to the prostate) and 'adenocarcinoma,' from Greek 'adēn' (gland) and 'karkinoma' (cancerous tumor).
