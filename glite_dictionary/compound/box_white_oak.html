box white oak

1. NOUN

/ bɑks waɪt oʊk / (AmE), / bɒks waɪt əʊk / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to eastern and central United States, known for its dark green, deeply lobed leaves and tough, moisture-resistant wood used especially for fence posts.
a small, tough-wooded American oak tree

Examples

The cabin was built using strong box white oak for its frame.
Farmers often choose box white oak because its wood resists rot in damp conditions.
A mature box white oak can provide dense shade during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white oak - a species of oak tree with pale bark and wood
