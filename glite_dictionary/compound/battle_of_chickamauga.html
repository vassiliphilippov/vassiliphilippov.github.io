Battle of Chickamauga

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv ˌtʃɪk.əˈmɔː.ɡə / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ˌtʃɪk.əˈmɔː.ɡə / (BrE)
a major battle of the American Civil War fought in 1863, resulting in a Confederate victory under General Braxton Bragg over Union forces
Civil War battle in 1863 (Confederate victory)

Examples

The Battle of Chickamauga was one of the bloodiest battles of the American Civil War.
Many history books analyze the strategies used during the Battle of Chickamauga.
After their defeat at the Battle of Chickamauga, Union forces retreated to Chattanooga.

Etymology

Named after Chickamauga Creek in Georgia, from a Cherokee word meaning 'river of death'.
