swaddling clothes

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑd.lɪŋ kloʊðz / (AmE), / ˈswɒd.lɪŋ kləʊðz / (BrE)
pieces of cloth, or a gown, used to wrap an infant snugly, especially in historical or biblical contexts
cloths used to wrap an infant

Examples

The newborn was wrapped tightly in swaddling clothes to help him feel secure.
The museum displayed replicas of ancient swaddling clothes used in biblical times.
She gently placed the baby, still in his swaddling clothes, into the cradle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to swaddle - to wrap a baby in swaddling clothes

Etymology

From Old English 'swæþeling', related to 'swathe' (to wrap) and 'clothes'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɑdlɪŋ kloʊðz / (AmE), / ˈswɒdlɪŋ kləʊðz / (BrE)
restrictions or limitations placed on someone or something at an early or undeveloped stage, often used figuratively
restrictions on the immature (figurative)

Examples

Many believe bureaucracy acts as swaddling clothes that stifle new businesses before they can grow.
The poet lamented the swaddling clothes of tradition that constrained innovation in art.
Education sometimes imposes swaddling clothes on creative minds rather than encouraging their independence.

Etymology

From 'swaddle' (to wrap an infant in cloth) + 'clothes'. Originally referenced infant wrappings, later used metaphorically.
