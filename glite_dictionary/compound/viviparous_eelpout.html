viviparous eelpout

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈvɪpərəs ˈilˌpaʊt / (AmE), / vaɪˈvɪpərəs ˈiːlpaʊt / (BrE)
A small fish of northern European waters (Zoarces viviparus) that gives birth to live young rather than laying eggs.
European fish that gives birth to live young

Examples

The viviparous eelpout is known for giving birth to live offspring rather than laying eggs.
Researchers observed a decline in the population of viviparous eelpout along the coastal waters.
The unique reproductive strategy of the viviparous eelpout sets it apart from many other European fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelpout - a fish in the family Zoarcidae

Etymology

From Latin 'viviparus' (bearing live young) + 'eelpout' (a type of fish).
