potassium permanganate

1. NOUN

/ pəˌtæsiəm pərˈmæŋɡəˌneɪt / (AmE), / pəˌtasiəm pəˈmaŋɡəneɪt / (BrE)
a poisonous compound, made of dark purple crystals, used as an oxidizing agent, disinfectant, and antiseptic
a poisonous oxidizing compound and disinfectant

Examples

The laboratory kept a stock of potassium permanganate for chemical experiments.
Doctors sometimes use potassium permanganate to treat certain skin conditions.
When potassium permanganate dissolves in water, it produces a deep purple solution.

Etymology

From potassium (element name) and permanganate (from manganate with prefix per-, indicating higher oxidation state).
