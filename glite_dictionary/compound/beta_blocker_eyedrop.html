beta blocker eyedrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtə ˈblɑkər ˈaɪˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə ˈblɒkə ˈaɪdrɒp / (BrE)
an eyedrop medication containing a beta blocker, used to lower intraocular pressure, especially in the treatment of glaucoma
eyedrop with beta blocker for glaucoma
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a beta blocker eyedrop to help manage her glaucoma.
Some patients may experience side effects from using a beta blocker eyedrop daily.
If you forget your beta blocker eyedrop dose, use it as soon as you remember.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timolol - a common beta blocker used in eyedrops for glaucoma
  • Degree Higher: glaucoma medication - any drug used to treat glaucoma

Etymology

From 'beta blocker', a class of drugs, and 'eyedrop'.
