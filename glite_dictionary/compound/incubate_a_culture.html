to incubate (a) culture

1. VERB

/ ˈɪŋkjəˌbeɪt ˈkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbeɪt ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
to maintain a culture of microorganisms or cells under controlled conditions to encourage growth
grow organisms in controlled conditions

Examples

To ensure optimal bacterial growth, the technician will incubate the culture at 37°C for 24 hours.
It is necessary to incubate the culture in a sterile environment to prevent contamination.
Before analyzing the results, she had to incubate the culture overnight.

Synset Examples

The scientist will incubate the cultures overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culture - microorganisms or cells grown for study, especially in laboratory conditions

Etymology

From Latin incubare (to lie upon), used scientifically for fostering growth of organisms in culture.
