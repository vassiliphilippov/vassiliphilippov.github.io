nautical linear unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔtɪkəl ˈlɪniɚ ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪkəl ˈlɪniə ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of distance used in navigation, especially at sea, such as the nautical mile or cable length
unit of distance for navigation

Examples

The ship's journey was measured in nautical linear units to ensure accurate navigation.
Pilots and sailors often use nautical linear units when recording distances at sea.
Understanding nautical linear units is essential for maritime navigation and chart reading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautical mile - a unit of length used in marine navigation, equal to 1,852 meters
