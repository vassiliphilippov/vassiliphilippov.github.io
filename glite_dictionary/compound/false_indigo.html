false indigo

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈɪndoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈɪndɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a shrub of the pea family with racemes of purple or blue flowers, native to North America, often grown for ornament; especially of the genera Baptisia or Amorpha.
North American shrub with blue-purple flowers

Examples

The garden was bordered with false indigo that bloomed every spring.
We planted false indigo for its vibrant purple flowers and hardiness.
Bees are often attracted to the striking clusters of false indigo in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo - a tropical plant used for blue dye

2. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈɪndoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈɪndɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
any of several North American plants of the genus Baptisia, with bluish flowers, that resemble true indigo but are not used for dyeing
a plant of the genus Baptisia

Examples

The field was dotted with blooming false indigo in early summer.
Gardeners often plant false indigo for its vibrant blue flowers and hardiness.
Unlike true indigo, false indigo is not used to produce dye.

3. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈɪndɪˌgoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈɪndɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A dense shrub native to moist areas of eastern North America, noted for its fragrant leaves and clusters of dark purple flowers.
a shrub with purple flowers, native to eastern North America

Examples

The riverbank was lined with false indigo, its purple flowers attracting bees in spring.
Gardeners value false indigo for its ability to thrive in wet soils and its showy blooms.
We identified the shrub as false indigo by its distinctive fragrant foliage.

Etymology

Named as a 'false' version of indigo due to resemblance but not being true indigo (Indigofera tinctoria).
