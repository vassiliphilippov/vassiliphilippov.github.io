racial immunity

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪʃəl ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəl ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
natural immunity to certain diseases or conditions that is shared by all members of a particular race or ethnic group
natural immunity in a specific race
medical

Examples

Some diseases are less prevalent in certain populations due to racial immunity passed down through generations.
Researchers studied whether racial immunity played a role in the spread of the virus among different groups.
The concept of racial immunity has been discussed in historical medical literature, though it is now considered controversial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunity - the ability to resist a particular infection or toxin
