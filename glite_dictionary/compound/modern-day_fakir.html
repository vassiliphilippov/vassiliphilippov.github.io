modern-day fakir

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdərn deɪ fəˈkɪr / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən deɪ fəˈkɪə / (BrE)
A person in contemporary times who resembles or behaves like a mystic, ascetic, or religious figure, especially in adopting a life of simplicity or austerity.
contemporary mystic-like person

Examples

Many viewed the eccentric philosopher as a modern-day fakir due to his minimalist lifestyle.
The documentary followed a modern-day fakir living in the heart of a bustling city.
She was described as a modern-day fakir for her dedication to meditative practices and simple living.

Synset Examples

He was often referred to as a modern-day fakir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fakir - a Muslim (or sometimes Hindu) religious ascetic and mendicant

Etymology

From 'modern-day' (contemporary) + 'fakir,' from Arabic fakīr (poor man, ascetic).
