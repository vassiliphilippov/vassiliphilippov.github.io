hydrogen bromide

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrədʒən ˈbroʊmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrədʒən ˈbrəʊmaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent gas that forms hydrobromic acid when dissolved in water
a colorless gas, forms hydrobromic acid in water

Examples

The laboratory kept hydrogen bromide in secure, sealed containers due to its reactivity.
When hydrogen bromide is dissolved in water, it yields hydrobromic acid used in chemical syntheses.
Engineers monitor the release of hydrogen bromide gas in industrial processes to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hydrobromic acid - the acid formed when hydrogen bromide dissolves in water

Etymology

Formed from 'hydrogen' and 'bromide' (bromide from French 'brome' and '-ide').
