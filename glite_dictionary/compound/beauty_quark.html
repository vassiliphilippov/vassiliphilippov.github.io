beauty quark

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːti kwɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbjuːti kwɑːk / (BrE)
a type of elementary particle (quark) with an electric charge of -1/3 and a mass about 10,000 times that of an electron, also known as the bottom quark
type of elementary particle (quark)

Examples

The beauty quark is an important part of the Standard Model of particle physics.
Scientists detected the presence of a beauty quark in the results of the experiment.
Finding the beauty quark helped confirm theoretical predictions about the behavior of subatomic particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottom quark - an elementary particle, another name for the beauty quark

Etymology

Named for the convention of assigning whimsical names to quark types, also known as bottom quark.
