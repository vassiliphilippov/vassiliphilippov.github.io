Indian cholera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈkoʊlərə / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪən ˈkɒlərə / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease of the intestines, caused by ingestion of contaminated water or food, also known as cholera
acute intestinal infection; cholera
medical

Examples

The spread of Indian cholera in the 19th century caused devastating epidemics across Europe.
Scientists traced the source of Indian cholera outbreaks to contaminated water supplies.
Historical records show that Indian cholera was a frequent cause of death among travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholera - an acute intestinal infection caused by Vibrio cholerae bacteria

Etymology

Named for the disease's historical association with outbreaks in India, where cholera was first extensively identified and described in the 19th century.
