nuclear family

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family group consisting only of parents and their children, considered as a single unit
parents and their children as a family unit

Examples

They come from a nuclear family with two children living at home.
The nuclear family has become less common in many societies.
Her nuclear family moved frequently due to her father's job.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extended family - a family that includes relatives such as grandparents, aunts, and uncles

Etymology

Formed from 'nuclear' (central, basic) + 'family', dating from mid-20th century sociology.
