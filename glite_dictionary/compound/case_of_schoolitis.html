case of schoolitis

1. NOUN

/ keɪs əv skuːˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / keɪs əv skuːˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
(humorous, informal) A bout of stress, fatigue, or lack of motivation associated with school, typically affecting students.
humorous term for school-related stress
informal

Examples

With finals approaching, Emily claimed she had a case of schoolitis and begged for a day off.
Every Monday, Tim joked that his case of schoolitis was acting up again.
Teachers often hear students blaming a case of schoolitis for their late assignments.

Synset Examples

She developed a case of schoolitis before finals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burnout - mental and physical exhaustion due to prolonged stress
  • Degree Higher: school phobia - fear or aversion to attending school

Etymology

Formed from "school" + "-itis" (a suffix denoting inflammation, used humorously).
