Foreign Service

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrən ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
the branch of the United States Department of State responsible for staff and diplomats posted to embassies and consulates abroad
U.S. State Department diplomatic service
american

Examples

He spent thirty years in the Foreign Service, representing the United States in various countries.
After passing the exam, she began her training for the Foreign Service.
The Foreign Service plays a crucial role in U.S. diplomacy and international relations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diplomatic service - the body of diplomats representing a country abroad
