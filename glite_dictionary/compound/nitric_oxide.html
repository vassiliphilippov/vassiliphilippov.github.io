nitric oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrɪk ɑkˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrɪk ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless or pale brown gas (NO), used in industry and produced naturally in the body as a signaling molecule
a poisonous gas (NO), chemical compound
medical

Examples

The reaction between ammonia and oxygen produces nitric oxide as an intermediate product.
Scientists discovered that nitric oxide acts as a powerful signaling molecule in the human body.
Inhaling high concentrations of nitric oxide can be extremely dangerous to your health.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nitrogen dioxide - a poisonous brown gas (NO₂), related oxides of nitrogen
  • Degree Lower: nitrous oxide - a gas (N₂O), another oxide of nitrogen, known as laughing gas

Etymology

From 'nitric' (relating to niter, from Latin 'nitrum') and 'oxide' (from oxygen).
