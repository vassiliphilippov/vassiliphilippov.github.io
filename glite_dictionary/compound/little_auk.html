little auk

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ɔk / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ɔːk / (BrE)
a small, short-billed seabird (Alle alle) abundant in Arctic regions
small Arctic seabird

Examples

During their Arctic expedition, researchers observed several little auks nesting along the rocky cliffs.
The little auk feeds mainly on zooplankton, diving underwater to catch its prey.
Each summer, thousands of little auks migrate to the northern coasts to breed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: auk - a bird of the family Alcidae, including seabirds such as puffins and guillemots
