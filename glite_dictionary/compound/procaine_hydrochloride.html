procaine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkeɪn haɪˌdrɑːkləˌraɪd / (AmE), / prəʊˈkeɪn haɪˌdrɒkləˌraɪd / (BrE)
A white crystalline powder used as a local anesthetic, commonly known by the trade name Novocain.
local anesthetic compound
medical

Examples

The dentist injected procaine hydrochloride to numb the area before the procedure.
Some patients may have allergic reactions to procaine hydrochloride during minor surgeries.
Procaine hydrochloride, also known as Novocain, was once the most popular local anesthetic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novocaine - trade name for procaine hydrochloride

Etymology

From procaine (from Latin pro- ‘before’ + caine, modelled on cocaine) and hydrochloride, indicating the hydrochloride salt form.
