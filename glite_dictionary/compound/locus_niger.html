locus niger

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəs ˈnaɪ.dʒər / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəs ˈnaɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
a layer of deeply pigmented grey matter in the midbrain, associated with movement control and affected in Parkinson's and Huntington's diseases
pigmented brain layer, related to movement
medical

Examples

Damage to the locus niger can result in movement disorders such as Parkinson's disease.
Researchers studied the locus niger to better understand the effects of dopamine loss in the brain.
In Huntington's disease, the locus niger may show metabolic abnormalities similar to those seen in Parkinson's.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantia nigra - part of the midbrain involved in motor control

Etymology

From Latin 'locus' (place) and 'niger' (black), referring to its dark appearance.
