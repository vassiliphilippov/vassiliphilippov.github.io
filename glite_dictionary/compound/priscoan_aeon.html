Priscoan aeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪs.koʊ.ən ˈi.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈprɪs.kəʊ.ən ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
the earliest eon in Earth's history, lasting from the planet's formation around 4,600 million years ago to about 3,800 million years ago, before the appearance of the oldest known rocks and with no evidence of life
earliest eon in Earth's history

Examples

Geologists debate whether the Priscoan aeon should be recognized as a distinct chapter in Earth's history.
During the Priscoan aeon, the Earth was forming from cosmic dust and debris.
The Priscoan aeon predates any known rocks or evidence of life on our planet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hadean eon - the first geologic eon of Earth, similar in timespan to the Priscoan aeon

Etymology

From Latin 'priscus' (ancient, former) and Greek 'aiōn' (age, eon).
