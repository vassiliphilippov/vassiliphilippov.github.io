double bassoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl bəˈsun / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl bəˈsuːn / (BrE)
a large woodwind instrument in the bassoon family, pitched an octave lower than the standard bassoon
very large, low-pitched bassoon

Examples

The orchestra included a double bassoon to add depth to the low register.
He learned to play the double bassoon after mastering the regular bassoon.
A double bassoon is sometimes used in symphonic and opera music for its rich, deep tones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrabassoon - a very large bassoon; synonym of double bassoon
  • Degree Lower: bassoon - a woodwind instrument of the oboe family
