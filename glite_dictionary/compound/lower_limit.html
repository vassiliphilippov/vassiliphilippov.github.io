lower limit

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊər ˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / ˈləʊə ˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
the smallest possible amount, value, or boundary that something can reach or be reduced to
minimum possible amount or value

Examples

The lower limit for passing the exam is 60%.
There is a lower limit to the temperature that these plants can survive.
You must specify both the upper and lower limit for the measurement range.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper limit - the greatest possible amount or value that something can reach

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.ɚ ˈlɪm.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə ˈlɪm.ɪt / (BrE)
the smallest amount or value that is possible or allowed in a particular situation
smallest possible quantity

Examples

There is a lower limit to how much the machine can compress the material.
The lower limit for the recommended dosage is five milligrams per day.
Safety regulations set a lower limit on the temperature for storing the chemical.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper limit - the greatest amount or value that is possible or allowed
