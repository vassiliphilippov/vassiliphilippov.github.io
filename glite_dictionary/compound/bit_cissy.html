bit cissy

1. ADJ

/ bɪt ˈsɪsi / (AmE), / bɪt ˈsɪsi / (BrE)
Slightly effeminate or excessively delicate in manner, style, or appearance, often used disparagingly.
slightly effeminate or overly delicate (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

Some classmates teased him for being a bit cissy when he chose ballet over football.
He felt his floral shirt was a bit cissy, but he wore it anyway to the party.
The decor of the room was described as a bit cissy by some of the guests.

Synset Examples

Some thought his fashion choices were a bit cissy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sissy - A boy or man regarded as effeminate or cowardly

Etymology

'Cissy' is a variant of 'sissy', possibly influenced by the female name 'Cissy', from 'sister'.
