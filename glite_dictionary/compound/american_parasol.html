American parasol

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈpærəˌsɑl / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈpærəsɒl / (BrE)
A type of agaric mushroom characterized by a pale cap and a stalk that is swollen near the base.
a pale agaric mushroom with a swollen-based stalk

Examples

The American parasol is often found growing on grassy lawns during late summer.
He picked a beautiful American parasol mushroom for his collection of fungi.
Experts warn that the American parasol can be easily confused with similar but toxic species.
