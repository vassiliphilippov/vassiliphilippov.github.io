childbed fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌbɛd ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldbɛd ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A serious infectious disease contracted by women during childbirth or abortion, usually caused by unsanitary conditions; historically a common cause of maternal death, now rare.
infection after childbirth or abortion
medical

Examples

In the 19th century, childbed fever claimed the lives of many women after childbirth.
Doctors now know that childbed fever is caused by bacterial infection, often due to poor hygiene.
The introduction of antiseptic practices led to a dramatic decline in cases of childbed fever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puerperal fever - another term for childbed fever
  • Degree Higher: septicemia - systemic infection caused by bacteria in the blood
