family Crangonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kræŋˈɡoʊnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kræŋˈɡəʊnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small shrimps found in marine and estuarine environments, noted for their commercial and ecological importance.
a family of small shrimps
medical

Examples

The family Crangonidae includes many species of commercially important shrimps.
Scientists discovered a new member of the family Crangonidae along the Pacific coast.
Many marine biologists study the family Crangonidae to understand estuarine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimp - small, slender marine crustacean

Etymology

From New Latin Crangonidae, from Crangon (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
