living rock

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ rɑk / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ rɒk / (BrE)
a highly succulent, stemless, clump-forming plant, native to South Africa, with grey-green leaves resembling granite; especially from the genus Lithops
South African succulent plant resembling stone

Examples

Many collectors admire the unique appearance of living rock succulents in their gardens.
Because living rock plants blend with stones, they are less likely to be eaten by grazing animals.
She bought several living rocks to create a miniature desert landscape on her windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lithops - Genus of plants known as living stones or living rocks

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ rɑk / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ rɒk / (BrE)
a type of small, usually spineless cactus (genus Ariocarpus) with warty tubercles, magenta flowers, and white or green fruit, found in northeastern Mexico and the southwestern United States
a type of small, warty Mexican-US cactus

Examples

The living rock is prized among collectors for its unusual warty surface and beautiful flowers.
In its native habitat, the living rock blends in so well with its surroundings that it is hard to spot.
Botanists have studied the living rock to understand how it survives in such arid regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a plant with thick, fleshy stems that typically bear spines and lack leaves
