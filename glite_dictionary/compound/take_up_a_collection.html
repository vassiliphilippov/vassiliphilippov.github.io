to take up a collection

1. VERB

/ teɪk ʌp ə kəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp ə kəˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
to gather money or other donations from a group of people, usually for a charitable or communal cause
collect money or donations

Examples

During the meeting, someone suggested we take up a collection for the local food bank.
After hearing about the emergency, the neighbors decided to take up a collection for the family.
The teacher helped the students take up a collection to buy a farewell gift.

Synset Examples

The school decided to take up a collection for charity.
We should take up a collection for her birthday gift.
They took up a collection to help the family in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to gather together, especially money or donations
