Nubian queen

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːbiən kwiːn / (AmE), / ˈnjuːbiən kwiːn / (BrE)
A royal woman from ancient Nubia, or a term used to symbolize beauty, strength, and elegance, especially of women of African descent.
royal woman from ancient Nubia; symbol of beauty and strength

Examples

The museum displayed stunning jewelry once worn by a Nubian queen.
At the pageant, she embodied the grace of a Nubian queen.
Many describe her presence as reminiscent of a Nubian queen.

Synset Examples

The art exhibit featured a sculpture of a Nubian queen.
She dressed like a Nubian queen for the gala.
Her confidence made her feel like a Nubian queen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queen - a female monarch or a woman noted for her beauty or power

Etymology

From Nubia, an ancient region along the Nile in what is now southern Egypt and northern Sudan.
