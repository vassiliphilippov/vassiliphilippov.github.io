gymnosperm family

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnoʊspɜrm ˈfæməli / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnɒspɜːm ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A taxonomic family within the group of gymnosperms, comprising related species of non-flowering, seed-producing plants, such as conifers or cycads.
group of related gymnosperm plants

Examples

The pine tree belongs to the gymnosperm family Pinaceae, which includes many evergreen species.
Scientists studied the diversity within each gymnosperm family to understand their evolutionary history.
Some of the oldest plants on Earth come from the gymnosperm family Cycadaceae.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: angiosperm family - a family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'sperma' (seed), referring to plants whose seeds are not enclosed in an ovary; 'family' from Latin 'familia'.
