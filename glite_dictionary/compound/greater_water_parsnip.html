greater water parsnip

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈwɔtər ˈpɑr.snɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈwɔːtə ˈpɑː.snɪp / (BrE)
a large, stout, white-flowered perennial plant growing wild in shallow fresh water, native to Europe; known for its clustered umbels and divided leaves
a European aquatic perennial plant species

Examples

The greater water parsnip thrives in ditches and along the banks of European rivers.
While hiking, we spotted a cluster of greater water parsnip growing in the marshy shallows.
Because it can be mistaken for other species, the greater water parsnip should be identified with care.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: water parsnip - a general term for several aquatic plants in the genus Sium
