to kick up excitement

1. VERB

/ kɪk ʌp ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
to generate or increase enthusiasm or interest in something
generate or increase enthusiasm

Examples

The organizers hope to kick up excitement with a surprise announcement at the festival.
A flashy new marketing campaign helped the company kick up excitement before the product launch.
Celebrities were invited to kick up excitement and attract media attention to the charity event.

Synset Examples

They plan to kick up excitement for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arouse excitement - to cause enthusiasm or interest
  • Antonym: dampen excitement - to reduce enthusiasm or interest
