underground tunnel

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈɡraʊnd ˈtʌnəl / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɡraʊnd ˈtʌnəl / (BrE)
A passageway built beneath the surface of the ground, usually for transportation, utilities, or access.
tunnel below ground surface

Examples

During the construction project, workers discovered a hidden underground tunnel dating back to the 19th century.
The city's metro system relies on a vast network of underground tunnels to transport commuters efficiently.
Rescue teams entered the underground tunnel to search for survivors after the earthquake.

Synset Examples

They found an underground tunnel during the renovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunnel - an artificial underground passage
  • Synonym: subway - underground railway system or its tunnel
