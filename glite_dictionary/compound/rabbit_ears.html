rabbit ears

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ɪrz / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ɪəz / (BrE)
the long ears characteristic of a rabbit, notable for their upright shape and sensitivity
the long ears of a rabbit

Examples

The rabbit ears helped the animal detect predators approaching from a distance.
Children love to draw rabbit ears on their bunny pictures at school.
He wore a headband with pink rabbit ears for the costume party.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ɪrz / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ɪəz / (BrE)
an indoor television antenna consisting of two extendible metal rods angled to form a V-shape, used to receive TV signals
indoor TV antenna with extendible rods

Examples

We had trouble getting a clear picture, so we adjusted the rabbit ears on top of the TV.
Old televisions often relied on rabbit ears to pick up local broadcasts.
She covered the rabbit ears with aluminum foil to try to improve the reception.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the long, upright ears of a rabbit.
