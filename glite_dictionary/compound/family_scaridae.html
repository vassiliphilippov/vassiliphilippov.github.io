family Scaridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli skəˈraɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli skəˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored marine fishes, commonly known as parrotfishes, found in tropical and subtropical oceans.
a family of parrotfishes

Examples

Marine biologists often study the family Scaridae to understand reef ecosystems.
The family Scaridae includes many species important for their role in coral reef health.
Colorful members of the family Scaridae are popular among snorkelers and divers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrotfish - a brightly colored marine fish of the family Scaridae

Etymology

From New Latin Scaridae, from Greek skaros (parrotfish).
