alkylating agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌleɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈæl.kɪ.leɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance, especially a drug, that treats certain cancers by attaching alkyl groups to DNA, thereby interfering with cell growth
drug that attacks DNA to treat cancer
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an alkylating agent as part of her cancer treatment regimen.
Some alkylating agents can cause significant side effects, including damage to healthy cells.
Researchers are developing new alkylating agents with fewer toxicities for patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemotherapy - the treatment of disease, especially cancer, using chemical substances
  • Synonym: antineoplastic - an agent that prevents the development or growth of tumors

Etymology

From 'alkyl,' a chemical group derived from alkanes, and 'agent,' meaning something that acts.
