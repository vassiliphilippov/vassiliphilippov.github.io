neat's-foot oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈnitsˌfʊt ˌɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈniːtsˌfʊt ˌɔɪl / (BrE)
a pale yellow oil made from the feet and legs of cattle, used especially for conditioning and dressing leather
oil from cattle feet for leather care

Examples

She applied neat's-foot oil to her riding boots to keep the leather supple.
Store your saddle in a dry place after treating it with neat's-foot oil.
Many craftsmen recommend neat's-foot oil for restoring old leather goods.

Etymology

From 'neat' (archaic word for cattle) + 'foot' + 'oil'.
