quantitative chemical analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑn.t̬əˌteɪ.t̬ɪv ˌkɛm.ɪ.kəl əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌkwɒn.tɪˌteɪ.tɪv ˌkɛm.ɪ.kəl əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
the process of determining the amount or concentration of each element or compound present in a substance through chemical methods
determining amounts of chemical components
medical

Examples

Accurate quantitative chemical analysis is crucial for quality control in the pharmaceutical industry.
The laboratory performed quantitative chemical analysis to measure the metal content in the water samples.
Students learn techniques of quantitative chemical analysis in their advanced chemistry courses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualitative chemical analysis - chemical analysis to determine the kinds of elements or compounds present, without regard to quantity

Etymology

From Latin 'quantitativus' (relating to quantity), and modern Latin 'analyis' via Greek 'analusis' (a loosening).
