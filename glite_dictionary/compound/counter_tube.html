counter tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tə tjuːb / (BrE)
a device that counts individual ionizing events, typically used to detect radiation by measuring particles passing through a gas-filled tube.
device for detecting ionizing events

Examples

The laboratory uses a counter tube to monitor background radiation levels.
Each counter tube must be carefully calibrated to ensure accurate readings.
Technicians replaced the old counter tube after it stopped detecting alpha particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geiger counter - an instrument that detects and measures ionizing radiation
