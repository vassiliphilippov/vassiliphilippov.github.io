valley pocket gopher

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæli ˈpɑkɪt ˈgoʊfər / (AmE), / ˈvæli ˈpɒkɪt ˈgəʊfə / (BrE)
a burrowing rodent native to valleys and mountain meadows in the western United States, known for its fur-lined cheek pouches
rodent of western U.S. valleys and meadows

Examples

The valley pocket gopher creates extensive tunnel systems beneath meadows in California.
Biologists studied the habits of the valley pocket gopher to understand its impact on local vegetation.
Many predators, such as hawks and foxes, rely on the valley pocket gopher as a source of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket gopher - a burrowing rodent with fur-lined cheek pouches
