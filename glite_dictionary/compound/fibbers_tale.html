Fibber's tale

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪbərz teɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪbəz teɪl / (BrE)
A fabricated or invented story; a lie or tall tale told by someone who is not telling the truth.
a fabricated story

Examples

He spun a Fibber's tale about catching a giant fish, but nobody believed him.
Her explanation sounded like just another Fibber's tale made to get out of trouble.
It turned out the story of the haunted house was a Fibber's tale spread by kids in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

That sounds like a Fibber's tale to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - an intentionally false statement
  • Antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact

Etymology

From 'fibber' (one who tells fibs, i.e. small lies) + 'tale' (story).
