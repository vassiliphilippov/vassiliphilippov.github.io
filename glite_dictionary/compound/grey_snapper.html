grey snapper

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈsnæpər / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈsnæpə / (BrE)
A species of snapper fish with a greyish body, found in shallow coastal waters, especially off the coast of Florida.
a species of greyish snapper fish

Examples

Fishermen often catch grey snapper near the coral reefs off southern Florida.
The restaurant serves grilled grey snapper as one of its seafood specialties.
Marine biologists study the habitat of the grey snapper to support conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a type of fish found in warm seas
