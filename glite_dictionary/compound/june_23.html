June 23

1. NOUN

/ dʒun twɛnti θɜrd / (AmE), / dʒuːn ˈtwenti ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
the night before Midsummer Day, often observed in some cultures with traditional celebrations
the night before Midsummer Day

Examples

Many European countries celebrate June 23 with bonfires and festivals.
We stayed up late on June 23, waiting for the sunrise of Midsummer Day.
Traditionally, June 23 is a time for gathering herbs thought to be most potent at this time.
