high-hat cymbal

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌhæt ˈsɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌhæt ˈsɪmbəl / (BrE)
A pair of cymbals mounted on a stand and operated by a foot pedal, commonly used in drum sets.
pair of cymbals on a stand, operated by foot pedal

Examples

The drummer played a quick rhythm on the high-hat cymbal during the jazz solo.
She adjusted the height of her high-hat cymbal before starting the performance.
Many beginners find it tricky to coordinate the foot pedal with the high-hat cymbal while drumming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hi-hat - pair of cymbals on a stand, operated by a foot pedal
