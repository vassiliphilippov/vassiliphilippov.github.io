Abbe vertical metroscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbi ˈvɜrtɪkəl ˈmɛtroʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈæbi ˈvɜːtɪkəl ˈmɛtrəˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A measuring device that applies Abbe's principle, used for precise dimensional measurements vertically.
a device for vertical dimensional measurement

Examples

The laboratory technician used the Abbe vertical metroscope to ensure the components' heights met specifications.
Precision measurements were possible thanks to the Abbe vertical metroscope and its adherence to Abbe's principle.
An Abbe vertical metroscope is often found in quality control departments for its reliable dimensional readings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metroscope - an instrument for measuring dimensions

Etymology

Named after the German physicist Ernst Abbe, who formulated Abbe's principle.
