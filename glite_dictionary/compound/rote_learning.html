rote learning

1. NOUN

/ roʊt ˈlɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / rəʊt ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
learning or memorization achieved by repeating information over and over, often without understanding its meaning
memorization by repetition

Examples

Many students rely on rote learning to pass their exams, repeating facts until they remember them.
Critics argue that rote learning does not encourage deep understanding or creative thinking.
The language course used songs and games instead of rote learning to teach new vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorization - the process of committing something to memory
  • Synonym: learning by heart - the act of memorizing something word-perfect

Etymology

From 'rote', meaning routine or mechanical repetition, with origins in Middle English, possibly from Old French.

2. NOUN

/ roʊt ˈlɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / rəʊt ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
Learning or memorizing facts by repetition without necessarily understanding their meaning.
memorization without understanding

Examples

Many schools still rely heavily on rote learning for examinations.
Critics argue that rote learning does not foster critical thinking skills.
Instead of rote learning, teachers encouraged students to understand the underlying concepts.

Synset Examples

They focused on education rather than just rote learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorization - the process of learning something so it can be remembered

Etymology

From 'rote' (mechanical repetition or routine), of uncertain origin, combined with 'learning'.
