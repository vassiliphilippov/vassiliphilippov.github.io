new jazz

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈdʒæz / (AmE), / njuː ˈdʒæz / (BrE)
any of various styles of jazz music that developed after 1940, often featuring innovation and departures from traditional forms
post-1940 innovative jazz styles

Examples

Many musicians in the 1950s were drawn to new jazz as a way to express evolving ideas.
The festival will feature prominent artists who specialize in new jazz styles.
His collection includes rare recordings from the early era of new jazz.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bebop - a complex jazz style developed in the 1940s
  • Degree Higher: avant-garde jazz - experimental jazz style developed from the 1950s onward
