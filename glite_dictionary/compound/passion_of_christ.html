Passion of Christ

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃən əv kraɪst / (AmE), / ˈpæʃən əv kraɪst / (BrE)
the suffering and death of Jesus Christ during his Crucifixion, especially as recounted in the Gospels
the suffering and death of Jesus at Crucifixion

Examples

Many artists have depicted the Passion of Christ in paintings and sculptures throughout history.
During Holy Week, Christians commemorate the Passion of Christ with special services and rituals.
The play dramatically portrayed the events of the Passion of Christ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crucifixion - the execution of Jesus by nailing him to a cross

Etymology

From Latin 'passio' (suffering) and 'Christus' (Christ).
