single cream

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl kriːm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl kriːm / (BrE)
a type of cream containing about 18% butterfat, commonly used in British cooking
cream with about 18% butterfat (British)
british

Examples

I poured single cream over the strawberries for dessert.
The recipe calls for 200ml of single cream to make the sauce extra smooth.
In the UK, single cream is often used in place of milk to enrich coffee.

Synset Examples

in England they call light cream ‘single cream’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light cream - cream with a similar butterfat content, mainly US term
