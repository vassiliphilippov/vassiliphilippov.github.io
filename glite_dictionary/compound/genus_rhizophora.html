genus Rhizophora

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs raɪˈzɒfərə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs raɪˈzɒfərə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Rhizophoraceae, consisting of small tropical trees and shrubs commonly known as mangroves.
tropical tree or shrub genus (mangroves)

Examples

The genus Rhizophora includes several species of mangroves found in tropical coastal areas.
Botanists studied the adaptive roots of the genus Rhizophora to understand how they thrive in saline environments.
Restoration projects often focus on planting members of the genus Rhizophora to stabilize coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangrove - A type of tree or shrub that grows in coastal saline soil; members of Rhizophora

Etymology

From Greek 'rhiza' (root) and 'phora' (bearing), referring to the root structures of these plants.
