Old Dominion

1. PROPN

/ oʊld dəˈmɪnjən / (AmE), / əʊld dəˈmɪnjən / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of Virginia, one of the original 13 American colonies; often used in historical or cultural contexts
nickname for Virginia (US state)

Examples

Many historians refer to Virginia as the Old Dominion because of its colonial heritage.
The University of Old Dominion is located in Norfolk, Virginia.
During the Civil War, the Old Dominion played a significant role as part of the Confederacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia - a state in the eastern United States; one of the original 13 colonies

Etymology

Refers to Virginia's status as the first permanent English colony in America to remain loyal to the English crown ('the dominion').
