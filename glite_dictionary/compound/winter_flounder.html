winter flounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ ˈflaʊn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə ˈflaʊn.də / (BrE)
a species of flounder (Pseudopleuronectes americanus) found in the northwestern Atlantic, valued for its white flesh and commonly fished during the winter
a North American flatfish, Pseudopleuronectes americanus

Examples

Fishermen caught several winter flounder off the coast of Massachusetts.
The white, delicate flesh of the winter flounder makes it a popular choice for seafood dishes.
During the winter, local markets often feature freshly caught winter flounder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a flatfish species found in coastal waters

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntər ˈflaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈflaʊndə / (BrE)
a flatfish found in the northwestern Atlantic, valued as an American food fish and typically caught in the winter
a flatfish valued as a food fish in North America

Examples

Fishermen caught a large winter flounder off the coast of Massachusetts.
The mild flavor of winter flounder makes it popular in seafood dishes during the colder months.
Regulations now limit how many winter flounder can be harvested each year.
