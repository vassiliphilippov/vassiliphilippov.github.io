mare's tail

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrz teɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɛəz teɪl / (BrE)
A long, narrow, wispy cirrus cloud resembling a horse's tail.
long, wispy cirrus cloud

Examples

High in the sky, a single mare's tail drifted gracefully in the afternoon sunlight.
Sailors often say that a mare's tail signals an approaching change in weather.
The artist painted mare's tail clouds stretching across the blue morning sky.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the flowing tail of a mare (female horse).
