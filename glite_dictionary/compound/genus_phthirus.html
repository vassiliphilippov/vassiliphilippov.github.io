genus Phthirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈθaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈθaɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of true lice, commonly known as crab lice, which are external parasites of mammals including humans.
genus of crab lice (parasites)

Examples

The genus Phthirus includes parasites with short, broad bodies adapted for clinging to coarse hair.
Research on the genus Phthirus has provided insights into the evolution of host-specific parasites.
Only two species belong to the genus Phthirus, one of which infests humans and is known as the crab louse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phthirus pubis - species of crab louse that infests humans

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Phthirus' from Greek 'phthir' meaning 'louse'.
