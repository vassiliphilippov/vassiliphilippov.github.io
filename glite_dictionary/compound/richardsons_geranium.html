Richardson's geranium

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃərdsənz dʒəˈreɪniəm / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃədˌsənz dʒəˈrəːnɪəm / (BrE)
A geranium native to western North America, characterized by branched clusters of white or pale pink flowers.
North American wild geranium

Examples

During our hike, we spotted a patch of Richardson's geranium blooming along the trail.
Gardeners appreciate Richardson's geranium for its delicate white and pink flowers.
The Richardson's geranium is commonly found in meadows and open woods across western North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - A plant genus with various species including Richardson's geranium

Etymology

Named after Sir John Richardson (1787–1865), a Scottish naturalist and Arctic explorer.
