GI tract

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː ˈtrækt / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː ˈtrækt / (BrE)
the entire passage in the body, extending from the mouth to the anus, where food is digested and waste is eliminated
digestive passage from mouth to anus
medical

Examples

The GI tract plays a crucial role in breaking down food and absorbing nutrients.
Doctors examined her GI tract to identify the cause of her discomfort.
Certain medications may disrupt the natural balance of bacteria in the GI tract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastrointestinal tract - the full digestive passage from mouth to anus

Etymology

Abbreviation: GI from 'gastrointestinal' (from Greek 'gastro-' meaning 'stomach' and Latin 'intestinum' meaning 'intestine').
