freeze-dried

1. ADJ

/ ˈfriːzˌdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈfriːzˌdraɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially food or biological material) dried by freezing and then removing moisture in a vacuum, preserving structure and composition.
dried by freezing and vacuum; preserved

Examples

The laboratory used freeze-dried serum samples to ensure accurate testing.
Many campers prefer freeze-dried meals because they are lightweight and easy to prepare.
The museum preserves rare plant specimens in a freeze-dried state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyophilized - dried by freeze-drying; especially of biological material

Etymology

Formed from 'freeze' and 'dried', referring to the process of freeze-drying (lyophilization).
