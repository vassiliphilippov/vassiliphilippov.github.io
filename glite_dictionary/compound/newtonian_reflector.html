Newtonian reflector

1. NOUN

/ nuˈtoʊniən rɪˈflɛktər / (AmE), / njuːˈtəʊniən rɪˈflɛktə / (BrE)
a type of reflecting telescope in which the image is viewed through an eyepiece set perpendicular to the main axis of the telescope
a reflecting telescope with a perpendicular eyepiece

Examples

The Newtonian reflector is popular among amateur astronomers for its simple design and effectiveness.
He purchased a large Newtonian reflector to observe distant galaxies from his backyard.
Unlike a refractor, a Newtonian reflector uses mirrors instead of lenses to collect light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflecting telescope - a telescope that forms an image using a mirror

Etymology

Named after Isaac Newton, who invented this telescope design in the 17th century.
