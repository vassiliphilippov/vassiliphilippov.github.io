rush nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃ nʌt / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃ nʌt / (BrE)
a European sedge plant (Scirpus or Cyperus species) having small, edible, nut-like tubers
European sedge with edible tubers

Examples

In some regions, rush nut tubers are collected and eaten as a delicacy.
Botanists have identified the rush nut growing near the wetlands in spring.
The small rush nut is often overlooked despite its nutritious tubers.
