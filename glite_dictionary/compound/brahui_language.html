Brahui language

1. NOUN

/ brəˈhuː.i ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / brəˈhuː.i ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an isolated Dravidian language spoken by the Brahui people in Pakistan, especially in Balochistan
Dravidian language of the Brahui in Pakistan

Examples

Linguists are fascinated by the Brahui language because of its Dravidian roots in a non-Dravidian region.
The Brahui language is primarily spoken in parts of Balochistan, Pakistan.
Despite being surrounded by Indo-Iranian languages, the Brahui language has retained its unique identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dravidian language - a member of the Dravidian language family

Etymology

From 'Brahui', the name of the ethnic group, likely of local origin.
