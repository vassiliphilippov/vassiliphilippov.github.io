Johns Hopkins University

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒɑnz ˈhɑpkɪnz ˌjunəˈvɝsəti / (AmE), / ˌdʒɒnz ˈhɒpkɪnz ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsɪti / (BrE)
a major private research university located in Baltimore, Maryland, known for its programs in medicine, science, and international studies
research university in Baltimore, Maryland

Examples

She completed her medical degree at Johns Hopkins University.
Johns Hopkins University is renowned for its research in public health.
Many international students apply to Johns Hopkins University each year.

Etymology

Named after the American philanthropist Johns Hopkins (1795–1873), whose bequest founded the university in 1876.
