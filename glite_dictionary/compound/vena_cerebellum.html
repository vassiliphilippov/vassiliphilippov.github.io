vena cerebellum

1. NOUN

/ ˈviː.nə sɛr.əˈbɛl.əm / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə sɛr.ɪˈbɛl.əm / (BrE)
Any of the veins that drain blood from the cerebellum.
veins draining the cerebellum
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the vena cerebellum was carefully protected to prevent excessive bleeding.
The radiologist noted an abnormality in the drainage of the vena cerebellum on the MRI scan.
Damage to the vena cerebellum can result in impaired blood flow to the cerebellar region.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'cerebellum' (little brain).
