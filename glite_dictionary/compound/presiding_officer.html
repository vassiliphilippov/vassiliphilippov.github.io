presiding officer

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈzaɪdɪŋ ˈɑːfəsər / (AmE), / prɪˈzaɪdɪŋ ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
the person who leads or is in charge of a meeting, organization, or assembly, ensuring that it is conducted properly
person in charge of a meeting or assembly

Examples

The presiding officer called the meeting to order promptly at 9 a.m.
If there is any dispute, it is the presiding officer who makes the final decision.
She was elected as the presiding officer for the annual shareholders’ meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairperson - person who leads a meeting or organization

Etymology

From 'preside' (to be in charge) and 'officer' (person holding office); both from Latin roots.
