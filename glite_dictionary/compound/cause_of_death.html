cause of death

1. NOUN

/ kɔz əv dɛθ / (AmE), / kɔːz əv dɛθ / (BrE)
the factor, condition, or event responsible for causing a person's death
factor or event causing death

Examples

The official cause of death was determined to be heart failure.
Doctors are required to list a cause of death on the death certificate.
Understanding the cause of death helps with public health statistics and prevention.

Synset Examples

heart disease is the biggest killer in the United States

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manner of death - the way in which death occurred (e.g., natural, accidental, homicidal, suicidal)

2. NOUN

/ kɔz əv dɛθ / (AmE), / kɔːz əv dɛθ / (BrE)
the specific reason or event that directly led to a person's death, as determined by investigation or examination.
reason or event leading to death

Examples

The autopsy identified a heart attack as the cause of death.
Medical examiners are responsible for determining the cause of death in suspicious deaths.
The doctor listed pneumonia as the official cause of death on the certificate.

Synset Examples

The autopsy revealed the cause of death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cause - something that brings about a result
  • Synonym: manner of death - the circumstances or classification of how a death occurred
