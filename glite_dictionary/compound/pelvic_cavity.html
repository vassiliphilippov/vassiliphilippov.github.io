pelvic cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlvɪk ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈpɛlvɪk ˈkævəti / (BrE)
the space within the pelvis, surrounded by the pelvic bones, that contains the lower abdominal organs such as the bladder, rectum, and reproductive organs
the space within the pelvis containing pelvic organs
medical

Examples

The bladder is located within the pelvic cavity and is protected by the surrounding bones.
Doctors examined the patient's pelvic cavity for signs of internal bleeding after the accident.
The pelvic cavity contains important organs such as the uterus, prostate, and rectum.

Etymology

From Latin 'pelvis' (basin, bowl) and Late Latin 'cavitas' (hollowness, cavity).
