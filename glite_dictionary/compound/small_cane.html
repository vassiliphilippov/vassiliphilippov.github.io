small cane

1. NOUN

/ smɔl keɪn / (AmE), / smɔːl keɪn / (BrE)
a type of cane (reed-like grass) that grows in watery or moist areas, especially in the southern United States
reed grass of wet southern U.S. areas

Examples

The wetlands were filled with small cane growing along the edges of the ponds.
Local wildlife uses the dense stands of small cane as shelter from predators.
During the summer, small cane forms a lush green border around the marshlands.
