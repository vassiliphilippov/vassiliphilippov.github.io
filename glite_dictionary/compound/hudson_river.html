Hudson River

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌdsən ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈhʌdsən ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in eastern New York, flowing south into New York Bay and known for its historical and economic significance
major river in eastern New York

Examples

Many artists in the 19th century found inspiration along the Hudson River.
The Hudson River separates New York City from New Jersey.
We took a scenic boat tour up the Hudson River last summer.

Etymology

Named after the English explorer Henry Hudson (c.1565–1611), who explored it in the early 17th century.
