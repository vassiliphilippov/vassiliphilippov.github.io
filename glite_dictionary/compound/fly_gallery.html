fly gallery

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ ˈɡæl.ə.ri / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈɡæl.əri / (BrE)
a narrow elevated platform at the side of a theater stage from which stagehands operate ropes and other equipment in the flies
theater platform for operating stage equipment

Examples

Only authorized crew members are allowed in the fly gallery during a performance.
The old theater's fly gallery was constructed from wood and accessed by a narrow staircase.
From the fly gallery, technicians skillfully managed the scenery changes throughout the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flies - the space above a stage where scenery and equipment are kept out of view

Etymology

From theatrical terminology: 'fly' refers to the space above the stage, and 'gallery' to a raised platform.
