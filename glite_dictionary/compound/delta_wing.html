delta wing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛltə wɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛltə wɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of airplane wing shaped like a triangle, resembling the Greek letter delta (Δ), commonly used in supersonic aircraft designs
triangular-shaped aircraft wing

Examples

The fighter jet was designed with a delta wing to improve its speed and maneuverability.
Several experimental aircraft in the 1950s featured a delta wing configuration for stability at high speeds.
The Concorde's distinctive delta wing allowed it to travel at supersonic speeds.

Etymology

Named after the Greek letter delta (Δ), due to the triangular shape.
