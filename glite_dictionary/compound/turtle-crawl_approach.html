turtle-crawl approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːtl̩ krɔl əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːtl̩ krɔːl əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a method or manner of doing something that is extremely slow or inefficient
very slow or ineffective method

Examples

We need to avoid the turtle-crawl approach if we want to stay competitive.
The company suffered from a turtle-crawl approach to innovation for years.
Relying on a turtle-crawl approach won't help us meet the urgent deadline.

Synset Examples

We can't afford a turtle-crawl approach to deadlines.
Their turtle-crawl method frustrated the entire team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow approach - a method characterized by little speed or progress
  • Antonym: rapid approach - a method characterized by great speed or progress

Etymology

Formed by metaphorical use of 'turtle-crawl' (turtle's slow movement) and 'approach' (method).
