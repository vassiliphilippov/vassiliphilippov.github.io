to glean information

1. VERB

/ ɡlin ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡliːn ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
to collect or discover information gradually, often by piecing together small amounts from various sources
gather information gradually

Examples

The detective tried to glean information from the suspect's body language during the interview.
Journalists often have to glean information from confidential sources to uncover major stories.
Analysts glean information by carefully reviewing data from multiple reports.

Synset Examples

He tried to glean information from their conversation.
She managed to glean insights from the report.
We can glean some knowledge from their mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather information - collect information, typically more actively or directly
  • Synonym: mine information - extract information, often from a large amount of data
