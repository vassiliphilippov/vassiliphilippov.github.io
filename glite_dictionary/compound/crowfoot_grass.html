crowfoot grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊˌfʊt ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊˌfʊt ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a creeping grass with finger-like spikes, often found in warm regions and sometimes considered a weed
a creeping grass with finger-like spikes

Examples

Crowfoot grass can spread quickly and overtake garden beds if not managed.
Farmers often regard crowfoot grass as a troublesome weed in their fields.
The spiky heads of crowfoot grass resemble the shape of a bird’s foot.

Etymology

Named for its inflorescence, which resembles a bird's foot (crow's foot).
