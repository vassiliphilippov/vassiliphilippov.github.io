legal ouster

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːɡəl ˈaʊstər / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈaʊstə / (BrE)
the expulsion of a person from the possession of property or land through legal process
expulsion from property by law
legal

Examples

The court ruled in favor of a legal ouster after the tenant refused to vacate the premises.
A landlord cannot achieve a legal ouster without proper notice and a court order.
Following the legal ouster, the property was returned to its rightful owner.
