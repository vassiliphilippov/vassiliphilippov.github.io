full stop

1. NOUN

/ fʊl stɑp / (AmE), / fʊl stɒp / (BrE)
A punctuation mark (.) used at the end of a sentence to indicate its conclusion, or after abbreviations.
period (punctuation mark)
british

Examples

Please remember to put a full stop at the end of every sentence.
In British English, a period is called a full stop.
He forgot the full stop after the abbreviation in his essay.

Synset Examples

in England they call a period a ‘stop’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - The punctuation mark (.) at the end of a sentence (primarily in American English)
