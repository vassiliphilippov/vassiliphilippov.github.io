to feel twitchy

1. VERB

/ fil ˈtwɪtʃi / (AmE), / fiːl ˈtwɪtʃi / (BrE)
to feel anxious, nervous, or unable to stay still, often because of anticipation or worry
to feel anxious or restless
informal

Examples

I always feel twitchy before giving a big presentation.
He started to feel twitchy after waiting for over an hour.
Whenever she has too much coffee, she tends to feel twitchy all day.

Synset Examples

She felt twitchy waiting for the interview results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - feeling worried or anxious
  • Antonym: calm - free from anxiety or agitation
