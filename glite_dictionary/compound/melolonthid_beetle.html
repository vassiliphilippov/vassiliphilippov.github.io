melolonthid beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈlɒnθɪd ˈbiːtl̩ / (AmE), / ˌmiːləˈlɒnθɪd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
Any of various beetles belonging to the family Melolonthidae, commonly known as May beetles or cockchafers.
beetle of the family Melolonthidae

Examples

The garden was infested with melolonthid beetles every spring.
Entomologists study melolonthid beetles to understand their life cycle and ecological impact.
Damage to the crops was attributed to the larvae of melolonthid beetles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockchafer - a large European beetle of the Melolonthidae family
  • Synonym: May beetle - beetle of the Melolonthidae family, often seen in spring

Etymology

From New Latin Melolonthidae, the scientific name for the family, from Greek melolonthē (a kind of beetle).
