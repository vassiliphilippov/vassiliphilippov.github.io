bullet train

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlət treɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt treɪn / (BrE)
a high-speed passenger train, especially one operating on dedicated tracks and capable of very fast travel, as in Japan.
high-speed passenger train

Examples

The bullet train from Tokyo to Osaka travels at incredible speeds, reducing travel time dramatically.
Japan's bullet train system is known for its punctuality and comfort.
Tourists were amazed by how smooth and quiet the bullet train ride was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shinkansen - the Japanese network of high-speed railway lines

Etymology

Modelled after the Japanese term 'shinkansen', the English name refers to the train's bullet-like shape and speed.
