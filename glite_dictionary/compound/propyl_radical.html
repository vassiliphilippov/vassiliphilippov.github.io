propyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.pɪl ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.pɪl ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a monovalent organic group (C3H7–) derived from propane, occurring as a substituent in many organic compounds
monovalent group from propane (C3H7–)
medical

Examples

The propyl radical is often encountered as a substituent in organic synthesis reactions.
Scientists compared the reactivity of the propyl radical and the methyl radical in the experiment.
When propane loses a hydrogen atom, a propyl radical is formed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ethyl radical - a monovalent group C2H5– derived from ethane
  • Degree Higher: butyl radical - a monovalent group C4H9– derived from butane

Etymology

From 'propane' and 'radical', referring to the molecular structure and reactivity.
