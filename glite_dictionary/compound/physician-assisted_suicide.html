physician-assisted suicide

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈzɪʃən əˈsɪstɪd ˈsuːə.saɪd / (AmE), / fɪˈzɪʃ(ə)n əˈsɪstɪd ˈsjuːɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
the act of intentionally ending one's own life with the assistance of a physician, typically by means of prescribed medication
suicide with aid of a physician
medical

Examples

Debates about the legality of physician-assisted suicide continue in many countries around the world.
The patient requested physician-assisted suicide due to her terminal illness and severe pain.
Ethical questions often arise in discussions of physician-assisted suicide within the medical community.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: assisted suicide - suicide carried out with help from another person
