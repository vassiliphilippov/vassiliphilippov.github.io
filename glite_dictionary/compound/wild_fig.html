wild fig

1. NOUN

/ waɪld fɪɡ / (AmE), / waɪld fɪɡ / (BrE)
a clusia tree of the West Indies with fig-shaped fruit
West Indies tree with fig-shaped fruit

Examples

The wild fig is native to several Caribbean islands.
Researchers studied the unique fruits of the wild fig for their ecological importance.
Local communities sometimes use the wood of the wild fig for making small crafts.

2. NOUN

/ waɪld fɪɡ / (AmE), / waɪld fɪɡ / (BrE)
a species of strangler tree native to southern Florida and the West Indies, which begins as an epiphyte and develops numerous thick aerial roots, eventually covering large areas
a tropical strangler tree; Ficus species

Examples

The wild fig can be found growing high in the branches of other trees before sending roots to the ground.
Over time, a wild fig may completely envelop its host tree with its aerial roots.
Botanists studied the rapid spread of wild fig across the tropical forest region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangler fig - a type of tropical fig starting as an epiphyte and enveloping host trees
