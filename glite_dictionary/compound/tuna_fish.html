tuna fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːnə fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːnə fɪʃ / (BrE)
A large, fast-swimming marine fish of the genus Thunnus, valued for its firm, edible flesh, often served as steaks or canned.
large edible marine fish; food

Examples

She ordered a grilled tuna fish steak at the restaurant.
Many people enjoy making sandwiches with canned tuna fish.
The chef specializes in dishes featuring fresh tuna fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - edible marine fish of the genus Thunnus

Etymology

From Spanish 'atún', via Portuguese 'atum', from Arabic 'al-tūn', ultimately from Latin 'thunnus'.
