temporal change

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a change that takes place over a period of time
change over time

Examples

Scientists are measuring temporal change in sea level caused by global warming.
The researchers studied temporal change in the brain's structure during early childhood.
Detecting temporal change in a population requires long-term observation.

Synset Examples

The temporal changes in climate are alarming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolution - the gradual development or change of something over time
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being unchanging over time

Etymology

From Latin temporalis (of time) + English change.
