atom smasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈætəm ˌsmæʃər / (AmE), / ˈætəm ˌsmæʃə / (BrE)
a large device used to accelerate charged particles to high speeds in order to cause collisions, often for scientific research in nuclear or particle physics
device for accelerating particles in physics

Examples

Scientists use an atom smasher to study the properties of subatomic particles.
The construction of a new atom smasher will enable more advanced experiments in particle physics.
Visitors to the science museum were fascinated by the massive atom smasher on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particle accelerator - a device that increases the speed of charged particles

Etymology

Formed from 'atom' (from Greek 'atomos', indivisible) and 'smasher' (one who smashes); popularized in the early 20th century.
