visual disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an impairment or abnormality of the sense of sight, affecting the ability to see clearly or normally
impairment or abnormality of sight
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with a visual disorder after struggling to see objects at a distance.
Certain visual disorders can be treated with corrective lenses or surgery.
Children with a visual disorder may have difficulty reading and need special support in school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual impairment - partial or complete loss of vision
