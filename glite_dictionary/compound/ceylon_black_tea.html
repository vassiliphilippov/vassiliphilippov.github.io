Ceylon black tea

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɑn blæk ti / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒn blæk tiː / (BrE)
A variety of black tea produced in Sri Lanka (formerly Ceylon), known for its bright color and distinctive flavor.
tea from Sri Lanka with a unique flavor

Examples

He enjoys a cup of Ceylon black tea every morning to start his day.
The bright, citrusy notes of Ceylon black tea make it a favorite among tea connoisseurs.
At the café, she ordered Ceylon black tea served with a splash of milk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black tea - Fully oxidized tea with a strong flavor
  • Degree Lower: green tea - Unoxidized tea with a more delicate flavor

Etymology

Ceylon, the former name of Sri Lanka, where this tea is grown.
