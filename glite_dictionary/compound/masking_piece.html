masking piece

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskɪŋ pis / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ piːs / (BrE)
a piece of scenery used in a theatre to block the audience's view of parts of the stage not meant to be seen
theatre scenery used to block stage areas

Examples

The stage manager adjusted the masking piece to ensure the audience couldn't see the wings.
A tall masking piece was placed at the side of the stage to hide the lighting equipment.
Designers debated where to position the masking piece for the best concealment of scenery changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masking - scenery used on stage to hide backstage areas
