cursive script

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜr.sɪv skrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.sɪv skrɪpt / (BrE)
A style of handwriting in which letters are joined together in a flowing manner without lifting the writing implement.
handwriting with joined letters

Examples

Many schools have stopped teaching cursive script as part of the standard curriculum.
Her grandmother's letter was written in beautiful cursive script that was hard to read at first.
Learning cursive script helped him improve both his handwriting speed and style.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: printing - handwriting with unjoined letters

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cursivus (running), relating to running hand in writing.
