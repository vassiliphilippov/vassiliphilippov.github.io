spot jamming

1. NOUN

/ spɑt ˈdʒæmɪŋ / (AmE), / spɒt ˈdʒæmɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate electronic interference with a specific channel or frequency to disrupt communications
targeted electronic frequency jamming

Examples

During the conflict, the army resorted to spot jamming to block enemy transmissions on critical channels.
Effective spot jamming requires precisely locating the frequency to be jammed.
The technician developed new equipment to improve spot jamming capabilities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: barrage jamming - simultaneous jamming of a wide range of frequencies
