genus Canella

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈnɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical flowering plants in the family Canellaceae, consisting mainly of the species Canella winterana.
genus of tropical plants (Canellaceae)

Examples

The genus Canella is best known for its only species, Canella winterana.
Researchers discovered that the genus Canella is native to the Caribbean region.
Floral extracts from the genus Canella have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canella winterana - the main species in the genus Canella

Etymology

From New Latin, using Latin genus (kind) and Canella, the genus name.
