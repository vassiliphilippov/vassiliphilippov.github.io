orthophosphoric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθoʊfəˈsfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊfɒsˈfɒrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless, and syrupy inorganic acid (H3PO4) used especially in fertilizers, detergents, and some foods and beverages
a common inorganic acid (H3PO4)
medical

Examples

Many soft drinks contain orthophosphoric acid as an additive to provide tartness and preserve freshness.
The production of fertilizers relies heavily on industrial quantities of orthophosphoric acid.
Always handle orthophosphoric acid with gloves and eye protection because it can cause burns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphoric acid - a broader term including orthophosphoric and other phosphoric acids

Etymology

From Greek 'ortho' (straight, correct) and 'phosphoros' (bringing light); refers to the simplest form of phosphoric acid.
