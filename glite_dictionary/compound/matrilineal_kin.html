matrilineal kin

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætɹəˈlɪniəl kɪn / (AmE), / ˌmatɹɪˈlɪnɪəl kɪn / (BrE)
a relative who is related through one's mother's line rather than the father's
relative on the mother's side

Examples

In traditional societies, inheritance often passes through matrilineal kin rather than the paternal line.
She maintained close ties with her matrilineal kin, especially her mother's sisters and their children.
Some cultures grant property rights exclusively to matrilineal kin to preserve lineage through the female side.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patrilineal kin - a relative related through one's father's line

Etymology

From Latin mater (mother) + linea (line).
