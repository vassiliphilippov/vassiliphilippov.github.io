great snipe

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt snaɪp / (AmE), / ɡreɪt snaɪp / (BrE)
a large, dark species of snipe found in parts of Europe and Asia
a large Old World snipe species

Examples

The great snipe prefers marshy meadows and grassy wetlands as its habitat.
Birdwatchers travel to Eastern Europe each year to observe the spectacular mating display of the great snipe.
Unlike the common snipe, the great snipe is bulkier and has a bolder pattern on its feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird with a long straight bill
