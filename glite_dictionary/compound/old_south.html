Old South

1. NOUN

/ oʊld saʊθ / (AmE), / əʊld saʊθ / (BrE)
the southern region of the United States as it existed before the American Civil War, especially associated with plantation-based society and slavery
the antebellum southern United States

Examples

Many writers have explored the culture and traditions of the Old South.
Plantation life was a defining feature of the Old South.
The legacy of slavery continues to affect the descendants of those who lived in the Old South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Deep South - the southeastern region of the United States, especially known for its strong cultural ties to the Old South
