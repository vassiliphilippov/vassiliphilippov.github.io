ethyl alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːθɪl ˈælkəhɑl / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
a colorless volatile flammable liquid that is the intoxicating agent in alcoholic drinks, also used as a solvent and in industrial applications
alcoholic liquid; solvent and fuel

Examples

Most hand sanitizers contain ethyl alcohol as the primary active ingredient.
The laboratory uses ethyl alcohol for cleaning and disinfecting equipment.
As a fuel additive, ethyl alcohol can help reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethanol - another name for ethyl alcohol

Etymology

From International Scientific Vocabulary, combining 'ethyl' and 'alcohol'; 'ethyl' from German 'Aether' (ether) + '-yl', 'alcohol' from Arabic 'al-kuḥl' (the kohl).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːθɪl ˈælkəhɔl / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
alcohol of high purity (usually 95 percent), used in blending spirits and in the manufacture of gin and liqueurs
pure alcohol used in spirits and manufacturing

Examples

Many distilleries use ethyl alcohol as a neutral base for their products.
The laboratory stored bottles of ethyl alcohol for use in cleaning and experiments.
Some gins are made by redistilling botanicals with high-purity ethyl alcohol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethanol - the chemical name for ethyl alcohol
  • Antonym: methanol - a different type of alcohol (methyl alcohol)

Etymology

From ethyl (from French éthyle, from Greek aithēr 'upper air') + alcohol (from Arabic al-kuḥl).
