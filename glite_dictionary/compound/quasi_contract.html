quasi contract

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːzi ˈkɑːnˌtrækt / (AmE), / ˈkwɑːzi ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
A legal obligation imposed by a court, in the absence of an actual agreement, to prevent unjust enrichment of one party at the expense of another.
legal obligation imposed without actual contract
legal

Examples

The court imposed a quasi contract to ensure the company paid for the services it received.
Even though there was no formal agreement, a quasi contract was recognized to prevent unjust enrichment.
A quasi contract can be enforced by law when one party benefits unfairly at the expense of another.

Etymology

From Latin quasi (as if, almost) and contractus (agreement).
