dihybrid cross

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈhaɪbrɪd krɔs / (AmE), / daɪˈhʌɪbrɪd krɒs / (BrE)
A genetic cross involving individuals that differ in two observed traits, each with two alleles, to study the inheritance of both traits together.
genetic cross with two traits
medical

Examples

The dihybrid cross revealed the inheritance patterns of both seed shape and seed color in peas.
Students used a dihybrid cross to predict the ratios of offspring with different trait combinations.
A classic dihybrid cross demonstrates how genes for different traits assort independently.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monohybrid cross - a genetic cross involving a single pair of traits

Etymology

From 'di-' (meaning two) and 'hybrid' (from Latin 'hybrida'); used in genetics.
