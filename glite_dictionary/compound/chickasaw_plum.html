Chickasaw plum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ə.sɑː ˈplʌm / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ə.sɔː ˈplʌm / (BrE)
a small North American shrubby tree (Prunus angustifolia) that bears small, edible yellow to reddish fruit
North American shrubby tree with edible fruit

Examples

The orchard has several Chickasaw plum trees by the fence line.
Wildlife often feed on the ripe Chickasaw plum fruit in late summer.
Jams made from Chickasaw plum are popular in some Southern cuisines.

Etymology

Named after the Chickasaw people, with 'plum' from Old English 'plūme'.
