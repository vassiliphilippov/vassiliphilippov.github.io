antihemophilic globulin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.hiː.məˈfɪl.ɪk ˈɡlɑː.bjə.lɪn / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌhiːməˈfɪlɪk ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a protein in the blood (factor VIII) essential for blood clotting, whose deficiency causes hemophilia A
blood clotting protein; deficiency causes hemophilia A
medical

Examples

Researchers developed a synthetic version of antihemophilic globulin to treat hemophilia A in children.
A deficiency of antihemophilic globulin leads to prolonged bleeding after minor injuries.
Thanks to advances in medicine, people can now receive antihemophilic globulin injections to manage their condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor VIII - another name for antihemophilic globulin, a blood clotting factor
  • Antonym: hemophilia A - a genetic disorder caused by factor VIII deficiency

Etymology

From 'antihemophilic' (preventing hemophilia) and 'globulin' (a type of protein), referring to its function in coagulation.
