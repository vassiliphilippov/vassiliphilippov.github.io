genus Ictalurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪkˈtæl.jʊ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪkˈtæl.jʊ.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American freshwater catfish, commonly known as channel catfishes.
a genus of North American catfish
medical

Examples

The genus Ictalurus includes several species commonly found in North American rivers and lakes.
Scientists studied the reproductive habits of fish in the genus Ictalurus.
The channel catfish, a member of the genus Ictalurus, is popular among anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'ailouros' (cat).
