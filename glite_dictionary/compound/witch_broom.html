witch broom

1. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ bruːm / (AmE), / wɪtʃ bruːm / (BrE)
an abnormal dense cluster of twigs or branches growing from a single point on a tree or shrub, caused by disease, insects, or other disturbances
dense, abnormal twig cluster on trees or shrubs

Examples

The old elm in the garden developed a large witch broom after an outbreak of fungus.
Gardeners prune away any witch brooms to prevent further damage to the tree.
Certain insects are known to cause witch broom formations on fruit trees.
