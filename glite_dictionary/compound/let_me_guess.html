let me guess

1. X

/ lɛt mi ɡɛs / (AmE), / lɛt mi ɡɛs / (BrE)
used to indicate that you are about to make a guess or assumption, often in response to something just said or implied
phrase for making a guess aloud
informal

Examples

Let me guess, you forgot your keys again, didn't you?
Before you tell me, let me guess—you missed the train.
He looked at the weather and said, "Let me guess, it's going to rain today."

Synset Examples

Lemme guess, you're going to say yes!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - to form an opinion or give an answer about something without enough evidence
