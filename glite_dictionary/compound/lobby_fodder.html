lobby fodder

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːbi ˌfɑdər / (AmE), / ˈlɒbi ˌfɒdə / (BrE)
information or materials intended to influence lawmakers or sway political opinion, often presented as facts or arguments.
material meant to sway political decisions

Examples

The report was filled with lobby fodder disguised as facts.
Many policy briefings include lobby fodder to steer debates in a particular direction.
He dismissed the statistics as mere lobby fodder created by interest groups.

Synset Examples

The report was filled with lobby fodder disguised as facts.
Don't believe that; it's merely lobby fodder to sway public opinion.
The senator's speech was just lobby fodder for big corporations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propaganda - biased or misleading information used to promote a political cause
