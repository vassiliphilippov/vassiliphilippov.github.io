Wilson's snipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlsənz snaɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɪlsənz snaɪp / (BrE)
A North American species of snipe, a medium-sized wading bird with a long straight bill and brown mottled plumage (Gallinago delicata).
North American species of snipe (bird)

Examples

We spotted a Wilson's snipe near the edge of the marsh early this morning.
During migration, the Wilson's snipe travels thousands of miles between its breeding and wintering grounds.
The distinctive call of the Wilson's snipe is often heard at dusk in wet grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common snipe - a related Eurasian snipe species (Gallinago gallinago)

Etymology

Named after Alexander Wilson (1766–1813), a Scottish-American ornithologist.
