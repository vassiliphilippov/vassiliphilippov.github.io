peptic ulcer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛptɪk ˈʌlsər / (AmE), / ˈpɛptɪk ˈʌlsə / (BrE)
A sore that develops on the lining of the stomach, upper small intestine, or esophagus, caused by the erosion of the mucous membrane due to stomach acid.
ulcer in the stomach or digestive tract
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a peptic ulcer after complaining of persistent stomach pain.
Certain medications and infections can increase your risk of developing a peptic ulcer.
A peptic ulcer may cause symptoms such as burning pain or nausea after eating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastric ulcer - ulcer specifically in the stomach lining
  • Synonym: duodenal ulcer - ulcer in the first part of the small intestine (duodenum)

Etymology

From Greek 'peptikos' (related to digestion) and Latin 'ulcus' (sore).
