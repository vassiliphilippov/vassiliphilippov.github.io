neural arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʊrəl ɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the curved part of a vertebra enclosing the spinal cord and arising from the back of the vertebral body
part of a vertebra enclosing the spinal cord
medical

Examples

A fracture of the neural arch can put pressure on the spinal cord.
The surgeon carefully examined the neural arch for signs of damage.
Most vertebrae are composed of a centrum and a neural arch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertebral arch - the bony arch formed by the pedicles and laminae of a vertebra

Etymology

From Latin 'neuralis' (pertaining to nerves) and 'arch' (curved structure).
