alveolar rhabdosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ælˈvioʊlɚ ræbˌdoʊsɑrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ælˈviːələ ræbˌdəʊsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A rare form of rhabdomyosarcoma, a type of cancer that mainly affects adolescents and young adults, characterized by the formation of tumor cells in alveolar patterns.
rare cancer mainly in young adults
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed that the tumor was an alveolar rhabdosarcoma, requiring intensive treatment.
Doctors explained that alveolar rhabdosarcoma typically affects teenagers and young adults.
Research into alveolar rhabdosarcoma has led to new treatment protocols for this aggressive cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhabdomyosarcoma - a malignant tumor of skeletal muscle tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'alveolus' (small cavity) and Greek 'rhabdos' (rod) + 'sarcoma' (fleshy tumor).
