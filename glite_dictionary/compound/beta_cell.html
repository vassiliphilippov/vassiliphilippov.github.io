beta cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtə sɛl / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə sɛl / (BrE)
a cell in the islets of Langerhans in the pancreas that produces and secretes insulin
insulin-producing pancreatic cell
medical

Examples

Damage to beta cells can lead to insulin deficiency and type 1 diabetes.
Researchers are developing ways to regenerate beta cells in diabetic patients.
The pancreas contains clusters of beta cells responsible for regulating blood sugar levels.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alpha cell - a pancreatic cell that produces glucagon

Etymology

Beta from the Greek letter 'beta', referring to the cell type's position in classification; 'cell' from Latin 'cella' (small room).
