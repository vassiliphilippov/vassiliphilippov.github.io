genus Raphanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ræˈfeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs rəˈfeɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae) that includes the radish.
botanical genus containing radishes

Examples

The genus Raphanus includes several species, most notably the cultivated radish.
Botanists debate whether wild radishes belong to the genus Raphanus or are separate species.
Recent studies have explored the evolutionary history of the genus Raphanus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radish - an edible root vegetable of the species Raphanus sativus

Etymology

From Latin 'Raphanus', from Ancient Greek 'raphanos' (radish).
