pit of the stomach

1. NOUN

/ pɪt əv ðə ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / pɪt əv ðə ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
the slight hollow in the middle of the upper abdomen, just below the breastbone (sternum), often associated with feelings or physical sensations like anxiety or pain
upper abdominal hollow below the sternum

Examples

He felt a sharp pain in the pit of the stomach after being hit by the ball.
Anxiety often creates a fluttering sensation in the pit of the stomach.
Sarah clutched the pit of her stomach as the news began to sink in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epigastrium - the upper central region of the abdomen
