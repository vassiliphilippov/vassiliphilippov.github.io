chink in someone's argument

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪŋk ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / tʃɪŋk ɪn ˈsʌmwənz ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
a weakness or flaw in someone's reasoning or logic
weakness in reasoning

Examples

The lawyer searched for a chink in their argument to challenge the prosecution's case.
Even the most solid theories can have a chink in someone's argument if examined closely.
During the debate, she deftly exposed a chink in his argument.

Synset Examples

Finding a chink in their argument was crucial to winning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak point - a weakness in someone's position or argument
