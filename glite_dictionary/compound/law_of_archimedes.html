law of Archimedes

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ɑːrkɪˈmidiz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˌɑːkɪˈmiːdiːz / (BrE)
the scientific principle stating that a body immersed in a fluid experiences an apparent loss of weight equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces
principle about buoyancy in fluids

Examples

The law of Archimedes explains why ships float despite their massive weight.
Students demonstrated the law of Archimedes by measuring the water displaced by a submerged object.
Engineers use the law of Archimedes to design submarines and other vessels that operate in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archimedes' principle - the scientific law describing buoyancy in fluids

Etymology

Named after Archimedes, the ancient Greek mathematician and physicist.
