vase vine

1. NOUN

/ veɪs vaɪn / (AmE), / vɑːz vaɪn / (BrE)
A climbing subshrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by large red-purple bell-shaped flowers with leathery recurved sepals.
climbing subshrub with red-purple bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The vase vine blossoms added a vivid pop of color to the garden fence.
Botanists studied the unique leathery sepals of the vase vine in its native habitat.
She trained the vase vine to climb up the trellis on her porch.
