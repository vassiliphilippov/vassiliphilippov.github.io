secondary dentition

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ənˌdɛr.i dɛnˈtɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.əndəri dɛnˈtɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the set of permanent teeth that replace the primary teeth in humans and some animals
set of permanent teeth
medical

Examples

Children typically develop their secondary dentition between the ages of six and twelve.
Proper care of your secondary dentition can help prevent dental diseases and complications.
Orthodontic treatment often begins after the eruption of secondary dentition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: primary dentition - the set of first (baby) teeth in humans and some animals

Etymology

From Latin 'dentitio' (teething); 'secondary' refers to the second set after primary (baby) teeth.
