carline thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrˌlaɪn ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːlɪn ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
a European thistle of the genus Carlina, typically having spiny leaves and a large, often silvery flower head
a thistle of the genus Carlina

Examples

The carline thistle is often found growing in dry, rocky habitats across Europe.
Many herbalists use dried carline thistle roots for traditional remedies.
The spiky appearance of the carline thistle makes it easily recognizable in the field.

Etymology

From Latin 'Carlina', the genus name, combined with 'thistle'.
