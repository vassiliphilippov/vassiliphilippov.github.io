tortricid moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.trɪ.sɪd mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.trɪ.sɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
A small moth of the family Tortricidae, whose larvae are leaf rollers or live in fruits and galls.
small moth, leaf roller larva

Examples

The apple orchards suffered severe damage due to an infestation of tortricid moth larvae.
Some species of tortricid moth are considered major agricultural pests across the world.
The researcher collected several tortricid moth specimens for her entomology study.

Etymology

From modern Latin Tortricidae, the family name, based on Latin torquere (to twist).
