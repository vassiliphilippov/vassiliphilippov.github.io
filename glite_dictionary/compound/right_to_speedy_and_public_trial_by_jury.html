right to speedy and public trial by jury

1. NOUN

/ raɪt tə spiːdi ənd ˈpʌblɪk ˈtraɪəl baɪ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / raɪt tə spiːdi ənd ˈpʌblɪk ˈtraɪəl baɪ ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
The legal right of an accused person to have a prompt and open trial before an impartial jury, as guaranteed by the Sixth Amendment to the US Constitution.
legal right to a prompt and public jury trial
legal american

Examples

Every citizen has the right to speedy and public trial by jury if they are charged with a crime.
The Sixth Amendment guarantees the right to speedy and public trial by jury for all criminal defendants.
Legal reform advocates emphasize the importance of preserving the right to speedy and public trial by jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due process - fair treatment through the normal judicial system
  • Synonym: habeas corpus - a writ requiring a person to be brought before a court
