pelagic bird

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlædʒɪk bɝd / (AmE), / pəˈlædʒɪk bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird that lives on or travels over the open sea, far from land
bird living on the open sea

Examples

The albatross is one of the most iconic pelagic birds in the world.
Many pelagic birds spend months at sea without ever touching land.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of several pelagic birds across the Pacific Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seabird - a bird that frequents the sea or coast

Etymology

From Greek pelagos (sea); 'pelagic' denotes open sea, applied to birds in scientific contexts.
