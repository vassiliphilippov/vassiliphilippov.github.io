angler fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlɚ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlə fɪʃ / (BrE)
a predatory marine fish with a large mouth and a wormlike filament on its head used to lure prey
marine fish with lure filament

Examples

The angler fish attracts prey with a bioluminescent lure on its head.
Deep-sea explorers were fascinated by the gruesome appearance of the angler fish.
In an aquarium display, a preserved angler fish showed its characteristic jaw and filament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkfish - a type of angler fish also known as Lophius
  • Synonym: angler - a person who fishes with a rod and line

Etymology

From 'angle' (to fish with a hook) and 'fish'; named for its fishing lure-like filament.
