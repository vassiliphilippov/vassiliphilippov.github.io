off-limits

1. ADJ

/ ɑfˈlɪmɪts / (AmE), / ɒfˈlɪmɪts / (BrE)
Not allowed to be entered or used; prohibited to a particular person or group.
not allowed to enter or use

Examples

The laboratory is off-limits to unauthorized personnel.
Certain topics were declared off-limits during the meeting.
The construction site is strictly off-limits after working hours.

Synset Examples

that area is off-limits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forbidden - not allowed or prohibited by law or authority
  • Antonym: accessible - able to be reached or entered

2. ADJ

/ ˌɔfˈlɪmɪts / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈlɪmɪts / (BrE)
not allowed to be entered, used, or discussed; forbidden or restricted.
forbidden or restricted; not permitted

Examples

The laboratory is strictly off-limits to unauthorized personnel.
Political topics were considered off-limits during the meeting.
Certain rooms in the museum are off-limits while they are being renovated.

Synset Examples

His personal life is off-limits; it's none of your business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forbidden - not permitted, especially by law or authority
  • Synonym: restricted - limited in extent, number, or action
  • Antonym: permitted - allowed or authorized

Etymology

Formed from the preposition 'off' and noun 'limit', originally used in military contexts to indicate areas forbidden to personnel.
