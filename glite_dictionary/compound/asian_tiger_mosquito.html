Asian tiger mosquito

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪʒən ˈtaɪɡər məˈskiːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃən ˈtaɪɡə məˈskiːtəʊ / (BrE)
A mosquito species native to Asia, recognizable by its striped appearance, now found in many regions worldwide and known as a potential carrier of diseases like dengue and Zika.
a striped invasive mosquito species, disease vector

Examples

The Asian tiger mosquito can transmit viruses including dengue, chikungunya, and Zika to humans.
Scientists are monitoring the spread of the Asian tiger mosquito in temperate regions due to its rapid population growth.
Residents were advised to remove standing water to help control Asian tiger mosquito breeding sites.

Etymology

Named for its native region (Asia) and tiger-like striped appearance.
