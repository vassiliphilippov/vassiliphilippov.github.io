blue crab

1. NOUN

/ blu kræb / (AmE), / bluː kræb / (BrE)
a bluish edible crab found along the Atlantic and Gulf Coasts of North America, valued for its sweet meat
edible crab of Atlantic and Gulf Coasts, USA

Examples

The fisherman caught a dozen blue crabs in the Chesapeake Bay this morning.
Many restaurants serve steamed blue crab as a regional delicacy.
The blue crab population is crucial to the local ecosystem and economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab - a crustacean with a broad, flat body, pincers, and a short tail

2. NOUN

/ blu kræb / (AmE), / blu kræb / (BrE)
a large Atlantic crab (Callinectes sapidus), known for its blue claws and valued as a source of fresh crabmeat
an Atlantic edible crab species

Examples

The Chesapeake Bay is famous for its blue crab harvests each summer.
She prepared a delicious meal using fresh blue crab meat.
Fishermen checked their traps every morning for blue crab along the coast.
