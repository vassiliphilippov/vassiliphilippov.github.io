thirty-second note

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜːrti ˈsɛkənd noʊt / (AmE), / ˌθɜːti ˈsɛkənd nəʊt / (BrE)
a musical note played for one thirty-second the duration of a whole note
musical note, 1/32 of a whole note

Examples

The pianist played several thirty-second notes in a rapid, intricate passage.
A thirty-second note is often used in fast, virtuosic music.
He struggled to count the thirty-second notes in the new piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demisemiquaver - musical note, 1/32 the duration of a semibreve (British term)
