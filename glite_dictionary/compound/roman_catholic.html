Roman Catholic

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.mən ˈkæθ.ə.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.mən ˈkæθ.ə.lɪk / (BrE)
A member of the branch of Christianity based in the Vatican and led by the Pope and a hierarchy of bishops; also, relating to this Church.
member of the Catholic Church under the Pope

Examples

My grandmother has been a devout Roman Catholic her entire life.
The city has a large Roman Catholic community that organizes many social events.
He converted to Roman Catholicism after studying different Christian traditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Protestant - a member of any of the Western Christian churches separate from the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin Romanus (of Rome) and Catholicus (universal), referring to the universal Christian church centered in Rome.

2. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.mən ˈkæθ.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.mən ˈkæθ.lɪk / (BrE)
a member of the Roman Catholic Church, which is governed by the Pope and based in Rome
member of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

As a Roman Catholic, she attends Mass every Sunday with her family.
The new president is the first Roman Catholic elected in the country's history.
Many Roman Catholics observe Lent as a time of reflection and fasting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Catholic - a member of the Catholic Church in general, not necessarily Roman

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (of Rome) and 'catholicus' (universal); referring to the Christian church in Rome.
