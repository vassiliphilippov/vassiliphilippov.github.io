scarlet lychnis

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːr.lɪt ˈlɪk.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈskɑː.lɪt ˈlɪk.nɪs / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial garden plant with bright scarlet flowers clustered in dense terminal heads, often grown for ornamental purposes.
a Eurasian garden plant with scarlet flowers

Examples

The scarlet lychnis in her garden bloomed with vibrant red flowers every summer.
Gardeners often recommend planting scarlet lychnis for a bold splash of color.
Clusters of scarlet lychnis brightened the entrance to the historic estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maltese cross - another name for scarlet lychnis

Etymology

From 'scarlet' for the flower's color, and 'Lychnis', the former genus name from Greek 'lychnos' (lamp), referencing the brightness of the flowers.
