mountain sumac

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
a nonpoisonous shrub of eastern North America with compound leaves, green paniculate flowers, and red berries
a North American shrub with red berries

Examples

The mountain sumac blooms in the summer, adding splashes of green and red to the landscape.
Birds are often attracted to the bright red berries of the mountain sumac.
Hikers should learn to identify mountain sumac, which is common along many forest trails in the eastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Rhus
