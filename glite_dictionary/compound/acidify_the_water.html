to acidify the water

1. VERB

/ əˈsɪdəˌfaɪ ðə ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / əˈsɪdɪfaɪ ðə ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to make water more acidic by increasing its acid content
make water more acidic

Examples

Environmentalists warn that certain pollutants can acidify the water in lakes and rivers.
You should not acidify the water in your aquarium without proper knowledge, as it can harm fish.
They used a weak acid to acidify the water for the plant experiment.
