heavy stodge

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi stɑdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi stɒdʒ / (BrE)
heavy, filling food that is unappetizing or lacks refinement
heavy, unappetizing food

Examples

The cafeteria often serves heavy stodge that leaves me feeling sluggish for the rest of the day.
During winter, traditional meals tend to be heavy stodge rather than light or fresh dishes.
He was disappointed when the banquet consisted mostly of heavy stodge instead of elegant cuisine.

Synset Examples

He complained that all the food was heavy stodge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stodge - heavy, filling, often unappetizing food
  • Antonym: delicacy - a food that is considered highly refined or choice

Etymology

From 'stodge', British informal term for stodgy food, possibly from dialectal 'stodge' (refuse, mud).
