lion's foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪənz ˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪənz ˌfʊt / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to the southern and eastern United States, known for its drooping clusters of pink or purple flowers and thick basal leaves resembling a lion's foot; sometimes classified in the genus Prenanthes
a perennial herb with lion-paw-shaped leaves

Examples

The gardener planted several lion's foot near the garden path for their attractive foliage.
Wild lion's foot can often be found growing along woodland edges in the southeastern United States.
The pinkish flowers of lion's foot droop gracefully above the broad, lobed leaves.

Etymology

So named because the basal leaves resemble the shape of a lion's paw.
