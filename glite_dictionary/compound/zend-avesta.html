Zend-Avesta

1. NOUN

/ zɛnd əˈvɛstə / (AmE), / zɛnd əˈvɛstə / (BrE)
the sacred text of Zoroastrianism, consisting of a collection of writings compiled during the 4th or 6th centuries
Zoroastrian sacred scriptures

Examples

Scholars have studied the Zend-Avesta to understand ancient Persian religious beliefs.
The Zend-Avesta contains hymns, rituals, and teachings central to Zoroastrian faith.
A rare manuscript of the Zend-Avesta was displayed at the museum's special exhibition.

Etymology

From Middle Persian 'Zend' (commentary) and 'Avesta' (the holy book of Zoroastrianism).
