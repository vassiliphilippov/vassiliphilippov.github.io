calcium sulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsiəm ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a white, odorless, inorganic salt (CaSO₄) used in construction, food, and medicine; occurs naturally as gypsum and anhydrite
a white inorganic salt, CaSO₄

Examples

The laboratory technician measured the amount of calcium sulfate in the water sample.
Plaster of Paris is made by heating calcium sulfate dihydrate to drive off some of the water content.
Farmers may add calcium sulfate to soil to improve its structure and fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsum - a soft sulfate mineral composed of calcium sulfate dihydrate
  • Synonym: plaster of paris - a form of calcium sulfate hemihydrate used for casts and sculptures

Etymology

From Latin 'calcium' (lime) + 'sulfas' (salt of sulfur).
