mountain cranberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tən ˈkrænˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˈkræn.bəri / (BrE)
A small, tart red berry similar to the American cranberry, but smaller, found especially in northern and alpine regions.
a small, tart red berry (plant)

Examples

The tartness of the mountain cranberry gives jams a distinctive flavor.
We found clusters of mountain cranberries while hiking in the alpine meadows.
She used mountain cranberry juice as a unique ingredient in her recipe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cranberry - a larger, tart red berry commonly used in sauces and juices

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈkrænˌbɛri / (BrE)
A low evergreen shrub of northern temperate regions, bearing small red edible berries; also called lingonberry.
low evergreen shrub with edible red berries

Examples

The tart flavor of mountain cranberry makes it popular in jams and preserves.
We hiked through fields dotted with mountain cranberry bushes in Scandinavia.
Bears in the region often eat mountain cranberry to supplement their diets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lingonberry - another name for the mountain cranberry, especially in Europe and Scandinavia
