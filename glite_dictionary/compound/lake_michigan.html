Lake Michigan

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈmɪʃəɡən / (AmE), / leɪk ˈmɪʃɪɡən / (BrE)
the third largest of the Great Lakes, and the largest freshwater lake entirely within the United States.
one of the Great Lakes in North America

Examples

Chicago sits on the southwestern shore of Lake Michigan and is known for its stunning skyline.
Many people enjoy sailing and fishing on Lake Michigan during the summer months.
The beaches along Lake Michigan are popular tourist destinations in the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lake Superior - the largest of the Great Lakes
  • Synonym: Lake Huron - a Great Lake connected to Lake Michigan by the Straits of Mackinac

Etymology

Named after the Ojibwe word 'mishigami,' meaning 'great water.'
