ferroelectric material

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛroʊɪˈlɛktrɪk məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a material that exhibits a spontaneous electric polarization which can be reversed by an applied electric field
material with reversible electric polarization

Examples

Research on ferroelectric material has led to advances in non-volatile memory devices.
A ferroelectric material can retain its polarization state after the external field is removed.
Engineers use ferroelectric materials in sensors and actuators due to their unique electrical properties.

Synset Examples

In addition to being nonlinear, ferroelectric materials demonstrate a spontaneous nonzero polarization (after entrainment, see figure) even when the applied field E is zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dielectric - an insulating material that can be polarized by an electric field

Etymology

From ferro- (relating to iron), though most ferroelectric materials do not contain iron; modeled after 'ferromagnetic'.
