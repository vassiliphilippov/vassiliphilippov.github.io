genus Aralia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs əˈreɪliə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈreɪlɪə / (BrE)
the type genus of the Araliaceae family, including a large, widely distributed group of shrubs, trees, and vines such as spikenard and Hercules'-club
type genus of the Araliaceae plant family

Examples

The genus Aralia includes a variety of shrubs and trees valued for their ornamental foliage.
Botanists identified the plant as a member of the genus Aralia due to its distinctive leaves.
Several species in the genus Aralia are used in traditional medicine across Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Araliaceae - the botanical family including the genus Aralia
  • Degree Lower: spikenard - a plant species within the genus Aralia

Etymology

From New Latin 'Aralia', the scientific name introduced by Linnaeus.
