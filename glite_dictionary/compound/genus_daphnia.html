genus Daphnia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs ˈdæfniə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs ˈdæfniə / (BrE)
A genus of small, planktonic, freshwater crustaceans commonly known as water fleas.
a genus of small freshwater crustaceans
medical

Examples

Scientists observed several species within the genus Daphnia in the pond's water sample.
The genus Daphnia is often used in ecological and toxicology studies due to its sensitivity.
Freshwater ecosystems frequently include members of the genus Daphnia as key components of the food web.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water flea - common name for small aquatic crustaceans like those in genus Daphnia

Etymology

From New Latin Daphnia, the genus name, derived from Greek Daphnē (a nymph in Greek mythology).
