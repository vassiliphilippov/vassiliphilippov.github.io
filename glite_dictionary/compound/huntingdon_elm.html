Huntingdon elm

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntɪŋdən ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋdən ɛlm / (BrE)
an erect, vigorous hybrid ornamental elm tree, typically grown for its attractive form and foliage
hybrid ornamental elm tree

Examples

The Huntingdon elm is popular in parks due to its symmetrical shape and fast growth.
Many streets in the city are lined with Huntingdon elm trees for their ornamental value.
Botanists praised the resilience of the Huntingdon elm compared to other hybrids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of tree with serrated leaves, known for its tall stature

Etymology

Named after Huntingdon, a town in England where the hybrid was first cultivated.
