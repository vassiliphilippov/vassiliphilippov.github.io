human right

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən raɪt / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən raɪt / (BrE)
A basic right or freedom to which all people are entitled, such as the right to life, liberty, and equality, usually recognized and protected by law.
fundamental right or freedom of all people
legal

Examples

Every individual is entitled to certain human rights regardless of nationality or background.
Activists have been protesting against violations of human rights around the world.
Access to clean water is increasingly recognized as a fundamental human right by many nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil right - a right protected by law, especially regarding personal liberty and equality
  • Synonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think freely
  • Antonym: privilege - a special right or advantage available to a particular person or group
