depth gauge

1. NOUN

/ dɛpθ ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / dɛpθ ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the depth of grooves, holes, or other recesses
instrument for measuring depth

Examples

The mechanic used a depth gauge to check the depth of the tire tread.
Before drilling further, she measured the hole with a depth gauge to ensure accuracy.
A depth gauge is essential when precise measurements are required in engineering projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliper - an instrument for measuring external or internal dimensions
