military blockade

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri blɑːˈkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri blɒˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a military action in which armed forces surround and isolate a place, usually to force surrender, often while continuing to attack
armed force surrounding and isolating a place

Examples

The navy enforced a military blockade to cut off supplies to the enemy city.
During the war, the military blockade lasted for months, causing severe shortages inside the fortress.
International law has specific rules governing the establishment of a military blockade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siege - the surrounding and blockading of a place to force surrender
  • Synonym: embargo - an official ban on trade or other commercial activity with a particular country
