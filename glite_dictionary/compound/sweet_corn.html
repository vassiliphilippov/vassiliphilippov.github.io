sweet corn

1. NOUN

/ swit kɔrn / (AmE), / swiːt kɔːn / (BrE)
a type of corn with kernels that are high in sugar and eaten as a vegetable when young and tender
corn with sweet, edible kernels

Examples

We had fresh sweet corn from the farmers' market for dinner last night.
She prefers to eat sweet corn off the cob, seasoned with a little butter and salt.
The recipe calls for a cup of canned sweet corn to add sweetness and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn - a tall cereal plant (Zea mays) grown for its edible grain

2. NOUN

/ ˈswit ˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈswiːt ˌkɔːn / (BrE)
a type of maize with kernels that are eaten as a vegetable while still young, tender, and sweet
a variety of corn eaten as a vegetable

Examples

We had grilled sweet corn at the barbecue last night.
She added fresh sweet corn to the salad for extra flavor.
Canned sweet corn is convenient for making chowders and soups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maize - a cereal plant that produces large grains set in rows on a cob, also called corn
