Illinois River

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪlɪˌnɔɪ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˌnɔɪ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in Illinois that flows southwest and joins the Mississippi River
river in Illinois, USA

Examples

Many towns along the Illinois River have a rich history dating back to pioneer days.
The Illinois River provides an important waterway for commercial transportation in the Midwest.
After heavy rainfall, the banks of the Illinois River can flood nearby fields.

Etymology

Named after the state of Illinois, itself from a French rendering of Native American (Algonquian) word for the local people.
