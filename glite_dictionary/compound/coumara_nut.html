coumara nut

1. NOUN

/ kuˈmɑrə nʌt / (AmE), / kuˈmɑːrə nʌt / (BrE)
The fragrant, black, nutlike seed of the tonka bean tree, used in perfumes, medicines, and as a substitute for vanilla.
seed of the tonka bean tree, vanilla substitute

Examples

The chef added a hint of coumara nut to the dessert for a unique vanilla flavor.
Perfume makers often use coumara nut extract to create sweet, fragrant scents.
In traditional medicine, the coumara nut is valued for its aromatic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonka bean - the seed of the Dipteryx odorata tree, used as flavoring

Etymology

From French 'coumara', a variant of 'coumarou', from a South American indigenous language, relating to the tonka bean tree.
