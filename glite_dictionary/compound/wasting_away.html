wasting away

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstɪŋ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪŋ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
a gradual decrease in size or strength of an organ or tissue, typically due to disease or lack of use
loss of organ or tissue size
medical

Examples

The patient's muscles experienced significant wasting away after months of immobility.
Doctors were concerned about the wasting away of his liver due to chronic illness.
Prolonged malnutrition can result in the wasting away of tissues throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrophy - gradual wasting or decrease in size of a body part
