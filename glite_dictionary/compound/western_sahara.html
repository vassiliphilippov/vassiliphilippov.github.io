Western Sahara

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɛstərn səˈhɑrə / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən səˈhɑːrə / (BrE)
A disputed territory in northwestern Africa, rich in phosphate deposits, largely under Moroccan control since 1992.
disputed territory in northwestern Africa

Examples

The United Nations continues to discuss the political status of Western Sahara.
Phosphate mining is a major industry in Western Sahara.
Many Sahrawis live in refugee camps outside Western Sahara due to the ongoing conflict.

2. PROPN

/ ˈwɛstərn səˈhɑrə / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən səˈhɑːrə / (BrE)
a region in Northwest Africa, regarded as a partially recognized state, located in the western Maghreb on the Atlantic coast
partially recognized state in Northwest Africa

Examples

The United Nations considers Western Sahara a non-self-governing territory.
The climate of Western Sahara is mostly desert and extremely arid.
There has been ongoing conflict over the status of Western Sahara for decades.
