lemon grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛm ən ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈlɛm ən ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tropical grass native to South Asia, especially India and Sri Lanka, with a strong lemon scent, used in cooking and herbal remedies
a tropical, lemon-scented grass used in cooking

Examples

She added chopped lemon grass to the soup for a fresh, citrus flavor.
Growing lemon grass in your garden can help repel mosquitoes naturally.
Some teas are flavored with lemon grass to create a soothing drink.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A tropical grass (Cymbopogon citratus) with fragrant leaves used in cooking, herbal teas, and traditional medicine.
fragrant tropical herb used in cooking

Examples

She added freshly chopped lemon grass to the soup for a citrusy aroma.
Thai curries often include lemon grass as a key ingredient.
Drinking tea made from lemon grass is a traditional remedy in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemongrass - alternative spelling for lemon grass

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tropical grass with a lemon scent, whose stalks and oil are used in Asian cooking, perfumes, and traditional medicine
aromatic tropical grass used in cooking and perfumes

Examples

She added chopped lemon grass to the soup for extra flavor.
The essential oil from lemon grass is used in many natural remedies.
I love the fresh, citrusy aroma of lemon grass in Thai curry dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citronella - an aromatic oil also derived from lemon grass, used in perfumery and as an insect repellent
