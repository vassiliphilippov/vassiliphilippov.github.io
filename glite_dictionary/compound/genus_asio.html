genus Asio

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪsiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪsiːəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of owls that includes several species, such as the long-eared owl and short-eared owl, found in Europe and other regions
genus of owls including long-eared and short-eared owls

Examples

The genus Asio includes the well-known long-eared owl commonly found in Europe.
Scientists recently discovered new species within the genus Asio during their fieldwork in Asia.
Both the long-eared and short-eared owls belong to the genus Asio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with a large head and forward-facing eyes

Etymology

From Latin 'Asio', a kind of horned owl.
