Tyrian purple

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪriən ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪriən ˈpɜːpl / (BrE)
a vivid purple dye ranging from red-purple to deep purple, originally obtained from certain sea snails and historically valuable for its rarity and richness
ancient purple dye from sea snails

Examples

The royal robes were dyed with the rare Tyrian purple reserved for emperors.
Archaeologists discovered traces of Tyrian purple in the ancient pottery.
By the Middle Ages, cheaper alternatives had replaced Tyrian purple in most textiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue
  • Synonym: murex - a genus of sea snails used to produce Tyrian purple

Etymology

From Tyre, an ancient Phoenician city famous for producing the dye, plus 'purple'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪriən ˈpɜːrpl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɪəriən ˈpɜːpl / (BrE)
a vivid purplish-red dye and color, historically associated with royalty and opulence, especially in ancient Rome and Byzantium
a vivid purplish-red color, ancient royal dye

Examples

The emperor's robes were dyed in Tyrian purple, signifying his supreme power.
In ancient times, only the wealthiest citizens could afford Tyrian purple.
The museum displayed a fragment of cloth still showing traces of Tyrian purple.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: purple - a color between red and blue

Etymology

From Tyre, an ancient Phoenician city, where the dye was first produced.
