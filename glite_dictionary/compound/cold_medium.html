cold medium

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A means of communication that provides little sensory information and requires active audience participation to interpret or fill in details, as with speech or cartoons.
communication medium requiring audience participation

Examples

Unlike a newspaper, a cold medium such as television demands that viewers actively interpret what they see and hear.
Speech is considered a cold medium because listeners must pay close attention to grasp its meaning.
Cartoons are an example of a cold medium, as they require the audience’s imagination to fully understand the message.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hot medium - a medium that is high in definition and requires less audience participation

Etymology

Coined by Marshall McLuhan (1911–1980) in the 1960s; 'medium' from Latin 'medium' (middle, means of communication).
