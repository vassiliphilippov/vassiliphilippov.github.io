Clinton's lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪn.tənz ˈlɪl.i / (AmE), / ˈklɪn.tənz ˈlɪl.i / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Clintonia, which are temperate liliaceous plants with broad basal leaves, white, yellowish, or purplish flowers, and blue or black berries.
plant of the genus Clintonia

Examples

We found a cluster of Clinton's lily blooming in the shady woods.
The berries of Clinton's lily turn a deep blue as they ripen.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Clinton's lily in North America.

Etymology

Named after American botanist De Witt Clinton (1769–1828).
