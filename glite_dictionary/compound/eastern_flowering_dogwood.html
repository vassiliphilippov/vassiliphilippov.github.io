eastern flowering dogwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn ˈflaʊərɪŋ ˈdɔːɡwʊd / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈflaʊərɪŋ ˈdɒɡwʊd / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree of eastern North America, noted for its showy white or pink flower bracts in spring, brilliant autumn foliage, and clusters of red berries
North American tree with showy flowers and red berries

Examples

The eastern flowering dogwood is a favorite ornamental tree in many gardens due to its beautiful spring blossoms.
In autumn, the leaves of the eastern flowering dogwood turn vibrant shades of red and purple.
Birds are attracted to the bright red berries produced by the eastern flowering dogwood in late fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - any tree or shrub of the genus Cornus, especially with showy bracts
