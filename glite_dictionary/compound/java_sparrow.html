Java sparrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːvə ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːvə ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
a small finch-like weaverbird (native to Indonesia) with a stout bill, often found in rice fields
small finch-like Indonesian weaverbird

Examples

Many bird enthusiasts keep the Java sparrow as a pet for its attractive plumage.
Rice farmers in Indonesia sometimes consider the Java sparrow a pest.
You can recognize a Java sparrow by its distinctive pink bill and black head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaverbird - bird known for weaving elaborate nests
