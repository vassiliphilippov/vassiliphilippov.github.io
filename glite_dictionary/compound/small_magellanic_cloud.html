Small Magellanic Cloud

1. NOUN

/ smɔl məˌdʒɛlɪnɪk klaʊd / (AmE), / smɔːl məˌdʒelɪnɪk klaʊd / (BrE)
a small, irregular galaxy near the Milky Way, visible from the Southern Hemisphere as one of the Magellanic Clouds
a small, irregular galaxy near the Milky Way

Examples

The Small Magellanic Cloud is a prominent feature in the night sky of the Southern Hemisphere.
Astronomers have discovered several star clusters within the Small Magellanic Cloud.
From certain locations in Australia, the Small Magellanic Cloud is visible to the naked eye.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Large Magellanic Cloud - the larger of the two Magellanic Clouds, another nearby irregular galaxy
  • Synonym: galaxy - a system of millions or billions of stars, together with gas and dust, held together by gravity

Etymology

Named after explorer Ferdinand Magellan, whose ship's voyage first recorded observations of it by Europeans.
