feijoa bush

1. NOUN

/ feɪˈʒoʊ.ə bʊʃ / (AmE), / feɪˈʒəʊ.ə bʊʃ / (BrE)
A South American shrub (Acca sellowiana) with edible greenish, plum-like fruit, often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
South American shrub with edible greenish fruit

Examples

The feijoa bush in our backyard produces sweet, aromatic fruit every autumn.
Gardeners recommend planting a feijoa bush in a sunny, sheltered spot for best results.
After a few years, our feijoa bushes grew high enough to become a natural hedge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feijoa - the edible fruit of the feijoa bush

Etymology

From Portuguese 'feijoa', named after João da Silva Feijó, a Brazilian botanist.
