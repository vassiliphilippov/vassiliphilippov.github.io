woolly bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊli bɛr / (AmE), / ˈwʊli beə / (BrE)
A caterpillar of various moths that is covered with dense, woolly hairs, sometimes found as a pest on plants.
hairy caterpillar of certain moths

Examples

A woolly bear crawled slowly across the garden path.
Children love to collect woolly bears in the fall, believing they predict the coming winter.
The woolly bear is known for its fuzzy appearance and black-and-brown bands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caterpillar - larval stage of butterflies and moths
