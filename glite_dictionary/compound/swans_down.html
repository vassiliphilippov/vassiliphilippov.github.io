swan's down

1. NOUN

/ swɑːnz daʊn / (AmE), / swɒnz daʊn / (BrE)
a very soft woolen fabric, used especially for making baby clothes or delicate garments
soft woolen fabric, esp. for babies

Examples

This blanket is made of swan's down and feels incredibly soft against the baby's skin.
She bought a swan's down jacket for her newborn because it was gentle and warm.
The dress was trimmed with swan's down to give it a delicate finish.

Etymology

Named after the soft down of a swan, the fabric mimics the texture and softness of real swan down.

2. NOUN

/ swɑːnz daʊn / (AmE), / swɒnz daʊn / (BrE)
The soft, fine feathers found under the outer feathers of a swan, often used as a filling for clothing or bedding.
soft under-feathers of a swan

Examples

The luxurious pillow was filled with swan's down for unmatched softness.
In the 18th century, garments lined with swan's down were highly prized among the nobility.
She touched the blanket, amazed by how light and warm swan's down could be.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down - soft fine fluffy feathers
