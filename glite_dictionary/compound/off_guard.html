off guard

1. ADJ

/ ɔf ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒf ɡɑːd / (BrE)
not ready or prepared for something; not alert or vigilant
not alert or unprepared

Examples

She was caught off guard by the sudden question and didn't know how to respond.
The unexpected news took him off guard during the meeting.
If you let your attention wander, life can catch you off guard.

Synset Examples

the blow caught him napping
caught in an off-guard moment
found him off his guard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off one's guard - not being vigilant or on alert
  • Synonym: unprepared - not ready for something; taken by surprise
  • Antonym: on guard - alert and ready to deal with danger or problems

2. ADJ

/ ɑf ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒf ɡɑːd / (BrE)
not prepared or ready for something unexpected; unprepared or surprised by something
unprepared; taken by surprise

Examples

The sudden announcement caught everyone off guard.
She was completely off guard when the interviewer asked an unexpected question.
Security was off guard, so the intruder entered unnoticed.

Synset Examples

The prank was about sugma, catching everyone off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprepared - not ready or equipped for something; lacking preparation
  • Antonym: on guard - watchful and prepared for danger or surprise
