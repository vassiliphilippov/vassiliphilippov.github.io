Alaskan brown bear

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskən ˈbraʊn bɛr / (AmE), / əˈlɑːskən ˈbraʊn beə / (BrE)
a large subspecies of brown bear found along the coasts of Alaska and British Columbia
large coastal brown bear of Alaska

Examples

During our trip to Alaska, we spotted an Alaskan brown bear fishing in the river.
The Alaskan brown bear is one of the largest bear subspecies in the world.
Wildlife biologists study the diet and behavior of the Alaskan brown bear along the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grizzly bear - a large North American brown bear (inland populations)
  • Antonym: polar bear - large bear species native to the Arctic
