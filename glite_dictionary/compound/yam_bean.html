yam bean

1. NOUN

/ jæm bin / (AmE), / jæm biːn / (BrE)
A twining plant native to Central America, grown for its large, edible tuberous roots and pods; also valued for producing rotenone and oils.
edible Central American tuberous plant

Examples

The yam bean can be eaten raw or cooked and has a sweet, crunchy texture.
Farmers cultivate yam beans both for their edible tubers and for the rotenone found in their pods.
The young pods of the yam bean must be thoroughly cooked before consumption.

2. NOUN

/ jæm ˌbiːn / (AmE), / jam ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to the Americas, especially the Amazon basin, with large edible tuberous roots.
tropical plant with edible roots

Examples

The yam bean is widely cultivated for its crisp, sweet-tasting roots.
Farmers in South America grow yam bean as both a food source and a soil-enriching crop.
Sliced yam bean makes a refreshing addition to salads and spring rolls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jicama - edible root of the yam bean plant; also the plant itself
