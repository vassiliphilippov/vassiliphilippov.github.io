basic assumption

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪk əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪk əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
a fundamental idea or belief that is taken for granted and forms the foundation of an argument, theory, or reasoning
fundamental presumption in an argument

Examples

The entire theory relies on a basic assumption that may not actually be true.
You must first identify the basic assumption behind their reasoning to understand their conclusions.
Every scientific experiment is built upon at least one basic assumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - something taken for granted or accepted as true without proof
