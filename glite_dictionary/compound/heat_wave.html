heat wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːt weɪv / (AmE), / ˈhiːt weɪv / (BrE)
A prolonged period of unusually hot weather, often significantly above average for the region or season.
period of unusually hot weather

Examples

A severe heat wave swept across Europe, breaking temperature records in several countries.
During the heat wave, many people were advised to stay indoors and drink plenty of water.
The crops suffered extensive damage due to an unexpected heat wave last summer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold snap - a brief period of very cold weather

2. NOUN

/ ˈhit ˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈhiːt ˌweɪv / (BrE)
A prolonged period of abnormally high temperatures, often with high humidity.
period of excessive heat

Examples

Last summer's heat wave broke several temperature records in the city.
Residents were urged to conserve water during the unexpected heat wave.
The heat wave caused power outages as people relied on air conditioning.

Synset Examples

During the heat wave, we stayed indoors with fans running.
The heat wave forced us to cancel our outdoor plans.
The city is experiencing a heat wave this week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold snap - a short period of unusually cold weather
