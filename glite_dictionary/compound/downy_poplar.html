downy poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊni ˈpɑplər / (AmE), / ˈdaʊni ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
A species of North American poplar tree with large, rounded, scalloped leaves and brownish bark and wood.
North American poplar tree species

Examples

The leaves of the downy poplar are soft to the touch and have a rounded outline.
A row of downy poplars grew along the riverbank, their bark showing a distinct brown hue.
Botanists surveyed the forest for downy poplar, noting their large, scalloped leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a type of tree in the genus Populus
