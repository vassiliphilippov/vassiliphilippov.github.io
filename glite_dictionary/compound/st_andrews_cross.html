St. Andrew's cross

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪnt ˈændruz krɔs / (AmE), / ˌsənt ˈændruːz krɒs / (BrE)
a cross in the shape of the letter X, with two diagonal bars of equal length; traditionally associated with Saint Andrew and used as a symbol in flags and heraldry
a cross shaped like an X

Examples

The Scottish flag features a white St. Andrew's cross on a blue background.
The road sign warned drivers with a red St. Andrew's cross at the railway crossing.
The historian explained the origin of the St. Andrew's cross in European heraldry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltire - a cross in the form of an X, especially as a heraldic symbol

Etymology

Named after Saint Andrew, a Christian apostle said to have been martyred on a cross of this shape.
