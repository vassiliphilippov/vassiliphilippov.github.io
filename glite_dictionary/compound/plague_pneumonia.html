plague pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ pleɪɡ nuːˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / pleɪɡ njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A severe and often fatal form of plague affecting the lungs, characterized by rapid progression and the ability to spread between people through the air.
severe, contagious lung form of plague
medical

Examples

Doctors identified plague pneumonia in several patients during the outbreak.
The rapid transmission of plague pneumonia makes it particularly dangerous in crowded conditions.
If left untreated, plague pneumonia can quickly become fatal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pneumonic plague - contagious form of plague that attacks the lungs
