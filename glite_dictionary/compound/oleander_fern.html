oleander fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊliˌændɚ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈəʊliˌændə fɜːn / (BrE)
a tropical fern with leathery fronds resembling oleander leaves, found from Asia to Polynesia
tropical fern with oleander-like leaves

Examples

The oleander fern thrives in the humid climate of Southeast Asia.
Gardeners prize the oleander fern for its elegant, leathery fronds.
Visitors admired the lush oleander fern in the conservatory's tropical section.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the oleander plant (Nerium oleander) in leaf shape.
