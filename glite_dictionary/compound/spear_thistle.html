spear thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪr ˌθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪə ˌθɪsəl / (BrE)
a European thistle (Cirsium vulgare) with large flower heads and spiny leaves, widely naturalized as a weed in North America.
a weedy European thistle with spiny leaves

Examples

The spear thistle is often found growing along roadsides and in pastures.
Farmers consider the spear thistle a troublesome weed because of its rapid spread.
Its sharp spines make the spear thistle difficult to handle without gloves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - any of various prickly, flowering plants in the family Asteraceae
