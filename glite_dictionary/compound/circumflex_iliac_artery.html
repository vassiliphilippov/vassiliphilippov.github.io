circumflex iliac artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːkəmˌflɛks ˈɪliˌæk ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌflɛks ˈɪliˌæk ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
an artery in the pelvis that supplies blood to the lower abdominal wall, skin, and the sartorius muscle
artery of the lower abdomen and pelvis
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the circumflex iliac artery was carefully preserved to maintain proper blood flow.
Injury to the circumflex iliac artery can result in significant bleeding in the pelvic region.
The circumflex iliac artery supplies oxygenated blood to parts of the abdominal wall and thigh.

Etymology

From Latin: 'circumflexus' (bent around) and 'iliacus' (pertaining to the ilium).
