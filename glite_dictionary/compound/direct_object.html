direct object

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈɑbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈɒbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
a noun, noun phrase, or pronoun that receives the action of a verb directly in a sentence
grammar: object directly affected by a verb

Examples

In the sentence 'She reads a book,' 'a book' is the direct object.
The direct object answers the question 'what?' or 'whom?' after the verb.
When you rewrite the sentence in passive voice, the direct object becomes the subject.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indirect object - object that indicates to whom or for whom the action is done
