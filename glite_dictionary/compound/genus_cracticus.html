genus Cracticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kræɡˈtiːkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs krækˈtɪkəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Cracticidae, comprising the Australian butcherbirds.
type genus of butcherbirds (Cracticidae)

Examples

The genus Cracticus includes several species known as Australian butcherbirds.
Researchers studied the vocalizations specific to the genus Cracticus.
Most species in the genus Cracticus are native to Australia and New Guinea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcherbird - any of several predatory birds in the family Cracticidae

Etymology

From Latin 'cracticus', used in scientific naming for the genus.
