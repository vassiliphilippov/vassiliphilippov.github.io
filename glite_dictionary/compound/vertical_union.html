vertical union

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːtɪkəl ˈjuːniən / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl ˈjuːniən / (BrE)
a labor union that organizes all workers in a particular industry regardless of their specific job or craft
union for all workers in an industry

Examples

The vertical union included all employees at the plant, from machine operators to office staff.
In contrast to craft unions, a vertical union seeks to unite everyone in the same industry.
The rise of vertical unions in the early 20th century changed the structure of labor movements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: horizontal union - a union organizing workers on the basis of their craft or trade
