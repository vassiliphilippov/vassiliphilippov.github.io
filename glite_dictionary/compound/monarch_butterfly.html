monarch butterfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnərk ˈbʌtərˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnək ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
a large migratory American butterfly with deep orange wings, marked with black and white, whose larvae feed on milkweed plants
large migratory American butterfly

Examples

Each fall, millions of monarch butterflies migrate from North America to central Mexico.
The monarch butterfly is easily recognized by its striking orange and black wings.
Children at the park watched a monarch butterfly rest on a milkweed plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect with large, often brightly colored wings
