dry humor

1. NOUN

/ ˌdraɪ ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / ˌdraɪ ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
a style of humor expressed with a serious, unemotional tone, often making it seem unintentional or deadpan
humor delivered in an unemotional or deadpan way

Examples

He told the joke with such dry humor that no one knew if he was serious or not.
Her dry humor often leaves people guessing whether she’s joking.
I appreciate shows that use dry humor instead of slapstick comedy.

Synset Examples

Her humor is often dry but clever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadpan - deliberately impassive or expressionless in humor
