biting midge

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪtɪŋ ˌmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪtɪŋ ˌmɪdʒ / (BrE)
a tiny flying insect of the family Ceratopogonidae that bites and feeds on the blood of mammals, birds, and other insects
tiny bloodsucking insect

Examples

The camp was infested with biting midges, making it impossible to spend time outdoors at dusk.
Some people experience severe allergic reactions to the bites of biting midges.
Farmers often use repellents to protect their livestock from biting midges in the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midge - small fly; typically non-biting, but some species bite
