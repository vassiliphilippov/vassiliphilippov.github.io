wooly lip fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊli lɪp fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈwʊli lɪp fɜːn / (BrE)
A small North American evergreen fern whose stalks and lower surfaces of the fronds are densely covered with wool-like hairs.
North American fern with woolly fronds

Examples

The wooly lip fern prefers to grow in rocky woodlands and shaded cliffs.
I spotted a wooly lip fern while hiking in the Appalachians last summer.
Because of its woolly texture, the wooly lip fern is easy to identify among other native ferns.
