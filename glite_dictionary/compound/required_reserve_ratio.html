required reserve ratio

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwaɪɚd rɪˈzɜrv ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪəd rɪˈzɜːv ˈreɪʃiəʊ / (BrE)
the minimum percentage of a bank's total deposits that must be held in reserve and not lent out, as specified by regulation.
minimum reserve percentage for banks

Examples

The central bank raised the required reserve ratio to limit excessive lending practices.
A low required reserve ratio gives banks more flexibility in loan creation.
Changes to the required reserve ratio can influence the overall money supply in an economy.

Synset Examples

The required reserve ratio is sometimes used as a tool in monetary policy, influencing the country's borrowing and interest rates by changing the amount of funds available for banks to make loans with.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve requirement - the amount of funds banks are required to hold in reserve
