champagne bubble

1. NOUN

/ ʃæmˈpeɪn ˈbʌbəl / (AmE), / ʃamˈpeɪn ˈbʌbl / (BrE)
A small bubble formed by the carbonation in champagne, typically seen rising in the drink or sparkling at the surface.
bubble in carbonated champagne

Examples

The fine champagne bubbles rose steadily to the top of the flute.
Guests admired the way champagne bubbles sparkled in the candlelight during the celebration.
She sipped her drink slowly, watching champagne bubbles dance along the glass.

Synset Examples

They celebrated with champagne bubbles sparkling in glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - a small globule of gas in a liquid
