imaginative comparison

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmædʒənətɪv kəmˈpɛrəsən / (AmE), / ɪˌmadʒɪnətɪv kəmˈparɪsən / (BrE)
a mental comparison in literature where unlike things are likened through similes, metaphors, or allegories
comparison using similes or metaphors

Examples

The author uses imaginative comparison to make ordinary events seem magical.
Teachers often encourage students to incorporate imaginative comparison in their writing.
Through imaginative comparison, the poem brings abstract emotions to vivid life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech involving a direct comparison
  • Synonym: simile - a comparison using 'like' or 'as'
