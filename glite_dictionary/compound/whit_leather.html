whit leather

1. NOUN

/ wɪt ˈlɛðər / (AmE), / wɪt ˈlɛðə / (BrE)
A type of leather that has been tanned using alum and/or salt, resulting in a pale and flexible material.
leather tanned with alum or salt

Examples

The gloves were made of fine whit leather, prized for its softness.
Artisans often used whit leather in bookbinding during the medieval period.
She carefully cleaned the old whit leather saddle to preserve its quality.

Etymology

From 'whit' meaning 'white', referring to its pale color.
