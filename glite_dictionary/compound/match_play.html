match play

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈmatʃ pleɪ / (BrE)
A golf competition format where the winner is determined by the number of holes won, rather than total strokes taken.
golf scoring by holes won

Examples

The tournament used match play instead of stroke play to determine the champion.
In match play, losing a hole by two shots is the same as losing it by one.
Some golfers prefer match play because each hole is a separate contest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stroke play - golf scoring by total strokes
