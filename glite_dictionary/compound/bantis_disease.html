Banti's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːntiz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈbæntiz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a disease marked by congestion and enlargement of the spleen, often accompanied by anemia or cirrhosis of the liver
disease causing enlarged spleen, anemia, or cirrhosis
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Banti's disease after presenting symptoms of an enlarged spleen and anemia.
Banti's disease is considered rare but can lead to serious complications if left untreated.
Doctors monitor liver function carefully in cases of Banti's disease due to the risk of cirrhosis.

Etymology

Named after Italian physician Guido Banti (1852–1925), who first described the disease.
