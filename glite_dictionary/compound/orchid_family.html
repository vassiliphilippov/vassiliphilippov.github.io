orchid family

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.kɪd ˌfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kɪd ˌfæm.əl.i / (BrE)
a large, worldwide family of perennial plants (Orchidaceae), known for their fleshy tubers or rootstocks and unique, often showy flowers
family of flowering plants (orchids)

Examples

The orchid family includes thousands of species found on every continent except Antarctica.
Many botanists agree that the orchid family is one of the largest families of flowering plants.
Members of the orchid family are prized for their intricate and colorful blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orchidaceae - the scientific family name of orchids
