linguistic context

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈkɑːntɛkst / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈkɒntɛkst / (BrE)
the surrounding words, sentences, or discourse that help to determine the meaning or interpretation of a language unit
surrounding discourse of a language unit

Examples

The linguistic context of the sentence clarified the ambiguous word.
Understanding the linguistic context is essential for accurate translation.
She used the linguistic context to infer the meaning of the unfamiliar term.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: context - the parts of something written or spoken that immediately precede and follow a word or passage and clarify its meaning
