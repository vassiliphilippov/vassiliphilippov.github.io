congenital heart defect

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl hɑrt dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl hɑːt dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a structural abnormality of the heart present at birth
heart abnormality present at birth
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with a congenital heart defect shortly after birth.
Some congenital heart defects require surgery, while others may heal on their own.
Routine screening has improved outcomes for babies with congenital heart defects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart defect - an abnormality or problem with the heart's structure or function

Etymology

From Latin 'congenitus' (born together, innate) + 'heart' + 'defect'.
