congo snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡoʊ sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ sneɪk / (BrE)
A species of aquatic, eel-shaped salamander with two pairs of very small feet, found in still muddy waters of the southern United States.
aquatic eel-shaped salamander of the southern US

Examples

We spotted a congo snake gliding silently through the murky water near the riverbank.
The biology textbook includes an illustration of the congo snake and its unique small limbs.
During the field trip, researchers carefully captured a congo snake for detailed observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphiuma - a genus of aquatic salamanders, also called congo eels or congo snakes
