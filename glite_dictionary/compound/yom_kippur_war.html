Yom Kippur War

1. PROPN

/ joʊm kɪˈpʊr wɔr / (AmE), / jəʊm kɪˈpʊə wɔː / (BrE)
A war fought in October 1973 when Egypt and Syria launched a surprise attack on Israel on Yom Kippur, leading to heavy fighting and significant territorial changes.
1973 war between Israel, Egypt, and Syria

Examples

The Yom Kippur War had a profound impact on Middle Eastern politics and global energy markets.
Many historians study the Yom Kippur War to understand the dynamics of modern warfare.
On the anniversary of the Yom Kippur War, ceremonies are held to remember those who served.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Yom Kippur' (Day of Atonement), marking the Jewish holiday on which the war began.
