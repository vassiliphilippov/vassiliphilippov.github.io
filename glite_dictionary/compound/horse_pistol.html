horse pistol

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈpɪstl̩ / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈpɪstl̩ / (BrE)
a large pistol, typically kept in a holster, that was formerly carried by horsemen
large pistol used by horsemen
archaic

Examples

The cavalry soldier drew his horse pistol as he charged across the field.
In the old west, a horse pistol was a common sidearm for mounted troops.
Collectors prize the ornate designs found on antique horse pistols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistol - a small firearm designed to be held in one hand
