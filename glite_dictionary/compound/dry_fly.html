dry fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌflaɪ / (BrE)
an artificial fishing lure designed to float on the surface of the water and resemble an insect
artificial floating fishing lure

Examples

He tied a dry fly to his line to attract the rising trout.
Many anglers prefer using a dry fly in clear, slow-moving streams.
The delicate presentation of a dry fly can often tempt wary fish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wet fly - an artificial fishing lure designed to sink below the water's surface
