bit player

1. NOUN

/ bɪt ˈpleɪɚ / (AmE), / bɪt ˈpleɪə / (BrE)
a person who has a minor role or influence in a particular activity, especially in a film, play, or project
person with a minor role or influence

Examples

Despite his experience, he was still treated as a bit player in the company.
She started her career as a bit player before landing bigger roles.
In the grand scheme of things, I was merely a bit player in the negotiations.

Synset Examples

He’s just a bit player in this large project.
She played a bit player in the big movie.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leading actor - the most important actor in a production
  • Degree Higher: supporting actor - an actor in a secondary role

Etymology

From 'bit' (small part) + 'player' (actor or participant), originally from theater and film usage.
