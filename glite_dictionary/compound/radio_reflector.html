radio reflector

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ rɪˈflɛktər / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ rɪˈflɛktə / (BrE)
an astronomical telescope that collects electromagnetic waves in the radio-frequency range from extraterrestrial sources
telescope for collecting radio waves from space

Examples

The scientists used a radio reflector to observe distant galaxies emitting radio waves.
After repairs, the radio reflector was able to detect weak signals from a nearby pulsar.
A radio reflector can reveal cosmic objects that are invisible in optical telescopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio telescope - astronomical instrument that detects radio waves from space

Etymology

Formed from 'radio' (relating to radio waves) and 'reflector' (device that reflects waves or light).
