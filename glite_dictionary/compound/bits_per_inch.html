bits per inch

1. NOUN

/ bɪts pɚ ˈɪntʃ / (AmE), / bɪts pə ˈɪntʃ / (BrE)
a measure of the density of information packed onto a storage medium, expressed as the number of bits recorded in each linear inch
information density per inch on storage media

Examples

The old magnetic tape drive had a recording density of 1600 bits per inch.
Increasing the bits per inch improves storage capacity but can also increase data errors.
Engineers measured the quality of the recording by the number of bits per inch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dots per inch - a measure of printing or screen resolution; number of dots in a linear inch
