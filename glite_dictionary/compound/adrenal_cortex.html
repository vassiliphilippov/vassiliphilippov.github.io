adrenal cortex

1. NOUN

/ əˈdriː.nəl ˈkɔr.tɛks / (AmE), / əˈdriː.nəl ˈkɔː.tɛks / (BrE)
the outer layer of the adrenal gland, responsible for producing steroid hormones such as corticosterone and sex hormones
outer layer of the adrenal gland
medical

Examples

The adrenal cortex produces hormones that help regulate metabolism and immune response.
Damage to the adrenal cortex can result in serious endocrine disorders.
Doctors studied the effects of various drugs on the adrenal cortex of laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adrenal medulla - the inner part of the adrenal gland that produces adrenaline and noradrenaline

Etymology

From Latin 'ad' (to), 'renal' (kidney), and 'cortex' (bark, outer layer).
