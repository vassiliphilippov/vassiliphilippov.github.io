suborder Lari

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌɔrdər ˈlɑri / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːdə ˈlɑːri / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of birds that includes gulls, terns, jaegers, and skimmers.
taxonomic group of seabirds (gulls, terns, etc.)

Examples

The suborder Lari includes many familiar seabirds such as gulls and terns.
Recent studies in ornithology have revised the classification within the suborder Lari.
Jaegers and skimmers are lesser-known members of the suborder Lari.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a type of seabird in the family Laridae
  • Synonym: tern - a slender seabird in the suborder Lari

Etymology

From Latin 'Larus', meaning 'gull', used as the type genus, and 'suborder', a rank in biological classification.
