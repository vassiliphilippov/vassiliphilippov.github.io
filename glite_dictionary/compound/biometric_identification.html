biometric identification

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛtrɪk aɪˌdɛntəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əˈmɛtrɪk aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of automatically identifying individuals using unique physiological or behavioral characteristics, such as fingerprints or facial features
automatic identification using biological traits

Examples

Many airports now use biometric identification systems to speed up security checks.
Unlike passwords, biometric identification relies on a person's unique physical traits.
The company upgraded its security by introducing biometric identification for all employees.

Synset Examples

negative identification can only be accomplished through biometric identification
if a pin or password is lost or forgotten, it can be changed and reissued but a biometric identification cannot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fingerprint recognition - identification using a person's fingerprints
  • Synonym: facial recognition - identification using a person's facial features

Etymology

From 'biometric' (bio- relating to life + metric, from Greek metron 'measure') and 'identification'.
