gold medal

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld ˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
a medal made of or resembling gold, awarded as the highest prize in a competition, especially in sports
medal awarded for first place

Examples

She proudly displayed her gold medal after winning the race.
He has trained for years to earn a gold medal at the Olympics.
The team celebrated their victory when they were awarded the gold medal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: silver medal - medal awarded for second place in a competition
  • Degree Lower: bronze medal - medal awarded for third place in a competition

2. NOUN

/ goʊld ˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
A medal made of or colored like gold, awarded to the first-place winner in a competition, especially in sports.
top award in a competition (first place)

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally won a gold medal at the championship.
He dreams of bringing home a gold medal for his country one day.
The gymnast celebrated her gold medal victory with her teammates.

Synset Examples

The Olympian proudly displayed her gold medal.
That Spanish athlete has won multiple gold medals.

  • Degree Lower: silver medal - medal awarded to the second-place winner in a competition
