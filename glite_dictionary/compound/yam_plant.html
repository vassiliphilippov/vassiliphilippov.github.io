yam plant

1. NOUN

/ jæm plænt / (AmE), / jæm plɑːnt / (BrE)
Any of several tropical vines of the genus Dioscorea, many of which have edible tuberous roots commonly known as yams.
tropical vine with edible tuberous roots

Examples

The yam plant thrives in tropical climates and is a major food source in West Africa.
Farmers cultivated several acres of yam plants for their nutritious roots.
Botanists are studying the growth cycle of the yam plant to improve crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - the edible starchy tuber of several plants of the genus Dioscorea
