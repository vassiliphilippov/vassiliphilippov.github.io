Killarney fern

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈlɑːrni fɜrn / (AmE), / kɪˈlɑːni fɜːn / (BrE)
a large, robust fern (Trichomanes speciosum) native to the damp, shaded areas of extreme western Europe, especially Ireland
a large fern found in western Europe

Examples

The Killarney fern is considered one of Ireland's rarest and most treasured plants.
Conservation efforts have helped preserve the natural habitat of the Killarney fern in western Europe.
Botanists traveled to remote woodlands to study the growth patterns of the Killarney fern.

Etymology

Named after Killarney, a region in Ireland, where it is notably found.
