Doctor of Theology

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv θiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv θɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A doctoral degree awarded for advanced study and research in theology.
advanced degree in theology

Examples

After years of dedicated study, she was finally awarded the Doctor of Theology degree.
He pursued a Doctor of Theology to deepen his understanding of religious texts.
Many seminarians aspire to earn a Doctor of Theology at a prestigious university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doctor of Divinity - a doctorate in divinity, often honorary and similar in scope
  • Degree Lower: Doctor of Philosophy - a doctorate degree in philosophy or related academic fields

Etymology

From Latin 'doctor' (teacher) and 'theologia' (study of God/religion).
