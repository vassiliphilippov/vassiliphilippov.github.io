kit fox

1. NOUN

/ kɪt fɑks / (AmE), / kɪt fɒks / (BrE)
a small, grayish fox native to the southwestern United States, known for its large ears and nocturnal habits
small fox of the southwestern US

Examples

The kit fox is well adapted to arid environments and is rarely seen during the day.
Researchers tracked the movements of several kit foxes across the desert.
Unlike the red fox, the kit fox has much larger ears in proportion to its head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift fox - a small North American fox, closely related to the kit fox

2. NOUN

/ kɪt fɑks / (AmE), / kɪt fɒks / (BrE)
a small, slender grey fox (Vulpes macrotis) native to the plains and deserts of western North America, known for its large ears and nocturnal habits
small North American fox species

Examples

The kit fox is well adapted to arid desert environments, using its large ears to dissipate heat.
We spotted a kit fox darting between the sagebrush at dusk.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered kit fox in California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift fox - a closely related small fox species native to North American prairies
