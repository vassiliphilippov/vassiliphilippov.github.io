family Clathraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kləˈθreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli klæˈθreɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of fleshy fungi in the order Phallales, characterized by their resemblance to stinkhorns.
family of fleshy fungi resembling stinkhorns

Examples

The family Clathraceae includes several species of fungi that are often found in decaying wood.
Biologists researching tropical fungi discovered a new specimen belonging to the family Clathraceae.
Some mushrooms in the family Clathraceae emit a strong odor to attract insects for spore dispersal.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Clathraceae', based on the type genus 'Clathrus' (from Latin clathratus, meaning latticed).
