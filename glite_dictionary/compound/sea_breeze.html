sea breeze

1. NOUN

/ siː briːz / (AmE), / siː briːz / (BrE)
A cooling breeze that blows from the sea toward the land, typically during the daytime as the land heats up.
cooling breeze from the sea

Examples

The sea breeze provided welcome relief from the afternoon heat on the beach.
As evening approached, the sea breeze grew stronger and cooled the coastal town.
Sailors relied on the regular sea breeze to help them navigate along the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: land breeze - a breeze blowing from the land toward the sea, usually at night
