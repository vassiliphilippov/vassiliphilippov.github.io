dark ground illumination

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk ɡraʊnd ɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɑːk ɡraʊnd ɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A method in microscopy where specimens appear bright against a dark background, achieved by illuminating the sample with scattered light.
microscopic method using scattered light
medical

Examples

The presence of live spirochetes was confirmed using dark ground illumination.
Many transparent specimens can be visualized clearly only with dark ground illumination.
Researchers observed the cells under dark ground illumination to highlight their movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark field microscopy - Microscopy technique using dark background and scattered light
