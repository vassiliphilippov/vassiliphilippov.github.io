Aleppo boil

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛpoʊ bɔɪl / (AmE), / əˈlɛpəʊ bɔɪl / (BrE)
A form of leishmaniasis affecting the skin, marked by ulcerative lesions.
skin disease with ulcerative lesions
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with an Aleppo boil after traveling in the Middle East.
Treatment for Aleppo boil typically involves medication to target the underlying parasite.
Doctors were researching new therapies for cutaneous leishmaniasis, commonly called Aleppo boil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - disease caused by protozoan parasites of the genus Leishmania

Etymology

Named after Aleppo, a city in Syria where the disease was once common.
