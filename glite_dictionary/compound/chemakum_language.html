Chemakum language

1. NOUN

/ t͡ʃɛˈmɑːkəm ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / t͡ʃɛˈmɑːkəm ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Chemakuan language formerly spoken by the Chemakum people of Washington State, USA.
extinct Native American language of the Chemakum

Examples

There are no remaining fluent speakers of the Chemakum language today.
Linguists have studied the Chemakum language to learn more about Chemakuan languages.
Many words in the Chemakum language are preserved only in historical records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chemakuan - a family of Native American languages including Chemakum and Quileute
  • Synonym: Quileute language - another Chemakuan language spoken by the Quileute people

Etymology

Chemakum, from the name of the indigenous Chemakum people of the Pacific Northwest.
