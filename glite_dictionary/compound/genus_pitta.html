genus Pitta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpɪtə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpɪtə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pittidae, comprising a group of brightly colored birds found in southern Asia, Australia, and nearby islands.
the type genus of Pittidae; birds of Asia and Australia

Examples

Researchers discovered an undocumented species within the genus Pitta in the Indonesian rainforests.
The colorful plumage of birds in the genus Pitta makes them popular among birdwatchers.
The genus Pitta includes several species known for their distinctive calls and hopping movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pittidae - Family of birds including pittas

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Pitta,' a Modern Latin genus name, possibly from Telugu 'pitta' (small bird).
