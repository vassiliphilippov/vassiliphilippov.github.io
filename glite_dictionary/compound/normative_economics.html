normative economics

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrmətɪv ɪˌkɑnəmɪks / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmətɪv iːˌkɒnəmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of economics that involves value judgments about what the economy should be like or what particular policy actions should be recommended
branch of economics involving value judgments
medical

Examples

Many policy debates rely heavily on normative economics to argue for what governments should do.
Unlike positive economics, normative economics considers ethical perspectives when evaluating economic policies.
Discussions of minimum wage laws often belong to the domain of normative economics.

Synset Examples

Normative economics, in contrast, becomes arbitrary and unmotivated unless it attributes largely self-interested preferences to people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positive economics - the branch of economics focused on objective analysis and facts, not value judgments

Etymology

From 'normative' (relating to norms or standards) and 'economics' (from Greek oikonomikos, relating to management of a household).
