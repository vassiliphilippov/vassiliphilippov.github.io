tracked vehicle

1. NOUN

/ trækt ˈviːɪkəl / (AmE), / trækt ˈviːɪkl̩ / (BrE)
A vehicle that is self-propelled and moves using continuous tracks instead of wheels, especially used on rough or uneven terrain.
vehicle with continuous tracks

Examples

The army deployed a tracked vehicle to navigate the muddy battlefield.
Bulldozers are often tracked vehicles designed to work on rough construction sites.
A tracked vehicle can cross snow and sand more easily than one with wheels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - an armored fighting vehicle that moves on tracks and typically has a large gun
  • Antonym: wheeled vehicle - a vehicle that moves using wheels rather than tracks
