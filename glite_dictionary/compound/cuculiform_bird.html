cuculiform bird

1. NOUN

/ kuˈkjuːlɪˌfɔrm bɝd / (AmE), / kjuˈkjuːlɪˌfɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
A bird belonging to the order Cuculiformes, characterized by having zygodactyl feet, including cuckoos, roadrunners, and related species (except for touracos).
bird of the order Cuculiformes

Examples

The cuculiform bird is distinguished by its unique foot structure, which helps it perch securely on tree branches.
Many species of cuculiform birds, such as cuckoos, are known for their brood parasitism.
A roadrunner is a well-known example of a North American cuculiform bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a type of bird in the family Cuculidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Cuculus' (cuckoo) + '-form' (having the form of).
