recombinant DNA

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑmbənənt ˌdiːɛnˈeɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒmbɪnənt ˌdiːɛnˈeɪ / (BrE)
DNA that has been experimentally created by combining genetic material from different sources, often from different species, to produce new genetic combinations.
genetically engineered DNA from different sources

Examples

Scientists used recombinant DNA technology to produce insulin for diabetic patients.
The creation of recombinant DNA has revolutionized modern genetics and biotechnology.
Concerns have been raised about the use of recombinant DNA in genetically modified crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic engineering - the manipulation of an organism's genes using biotechnology

Etymology

From 'recombinant', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'combinare' (to combine); 'DNA' is an acronym for deoxyribonucleic acid.
