Koplik's spots

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊplɪks spɑts / (AmE), / ˈkɒplɪks spɒts / (BrE)
small red spots with white centers on the mucous membranes of the mouth, appearing as an early symptom of measles
spots in mouth, early measles sign
medical

Examples

The doctor noticed Koplik's spots on the patient's inner cheek, suggesting the early stage of measles.
Before the rash appeared, she developed Koplik's spots in her mouth, confirming the diagnosis.
Medical students are trained to recognize Koplik's spots as a critical sign of measles infection.

Etymology

Named after Dr. Henry Koplik (1858–1927), American pediatrician who first described them.
