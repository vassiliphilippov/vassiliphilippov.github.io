erectile dysfunction

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛktaɪl dɪsˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛktaɪl dɪsˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the inability of a man to achieve or maintain an erection sufficient for satisfactory sexual performance
inability to achieve or maintain an erection
medical

Examples

Many men experience erectile dysfunction at some point in their lives, especially as they age.
There are several medical treatments available for erectile dysfunction today.
Doctors encourage patients to discuss erectile dysfunction openly, as it can signal other health issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impotence - inability to achieve or maintain an erection

Etymology

From Latin erectus (upright) and Latin dysfunction (abnormal functioning).
