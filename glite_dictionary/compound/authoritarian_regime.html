authoritarian regime

1. NOUN

/ əˌθɔːr.əˈtɛr.i.ən rɪˈʒim / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɒr.ɪˈteə.ri.ən rɪˈʒiːm / (BrE)
A government in which political power is concentrated in the hands of a leader or small elite not accountable to the public.
government with concentrated, unaccountable power

Examples

The country has been ruled by an authoritarian regime for decades, denying citizens basic rights.
International observers criticized the authoritarian regime for suppressing free speech and political opposition.
After years under an authoritarian regime, the nation finally held its first open elections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictatorship - a form of government with a single ruler holding absolute power
  • Antonym: democracy - a government controlled by the people through elected representatives

Etymology

From 'authoritarian' (from authority + -arian) and French 'régime' (system of rule).
