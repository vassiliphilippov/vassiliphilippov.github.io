performance bond

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɔːrməns bɑnd / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːməns bɒnd / (BrE)
A bond issued by a surety company guaranteeing that the terms of a contract will be carried out, providing compensation if they are not.
bond guaranteeing contract fulfillment
legal

Examples

The contractor was required to provide a performance bond before beginning construction on the building.
A performance bond protects the client if the contractor fails to meet the project's terms.
Without a performance bond, the company risked significant financial losses due to non-completion of the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surety bond - a bond guaranteeing the performance of an obligation
  • Degree Lower: insurance - an arrangement providing compensation for loss or liability
