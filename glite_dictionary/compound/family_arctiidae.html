family Arctiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɑːrkˈtiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɑːkˈtiːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of moths commonly known as tiger moths, belonging to the order Lepidoptera and known for their bright colors and diverse patterns
family of moths (tiger moths)

Examples

The family Arctiidae includes species with striking wing patterns and vivid coloration.
Recent studies have revealed new genera within the family Arctiidae from South America.
Many members of the family Arctiidae are known for their chemical defenses against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger moth - a moth of the family Arctiidae, often brightly colored

Etymology

From New Latin 'Arctiidae,' based on Greek 'arktios' (of a bear), referencing the hairy caterpillars (woolly bears) of this family.
