optical pyrometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑp.tɪ.kəl paɪˈrɑː.mə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tɪ.kəl paɪˈrɒm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring high temperatures by comparing the color of light emitted by a hot object to a calibrated standard
temperature-measuring device using color comparison

Examples

The engineer used an optical pyrometer to determine the temperature of the furnace.
An optical pyrometer can accurately measure heat without touching the metal surface.
Older optical pyrometers rely on matching the color of a filament to that of the hot object.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrometer - an instrument for measuring high temperatures

Etymology

From 'optical' (relating to sight or light) and Greek *pyrometer* (pyro- 'fire' + -meter 'measuring device').
