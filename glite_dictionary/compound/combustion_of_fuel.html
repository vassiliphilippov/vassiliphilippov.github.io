combustion of fuel

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbʌs.tʃən əv ˈfjuːl / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌs.tʃən ɒv ˈfjʊəl / (BrE)
the chemical process in which a fuel reacts with oxygen to produce heat and light, typically used to generate energy
chemical reaction of fuel with oxygen

Examples

The combustion of fuel in power plants is a major source of electricity worldwide.
Proper ventilation is important to ensure the safe combustion of fuel in indoor heaters.
Scientists study the combustion of fuel to develop more efficient engines and reduce emissions.

Synset Examples

The combustion of fuel generates energy for the engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burning - the process of something being consumed by fire
