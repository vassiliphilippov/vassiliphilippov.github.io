mass debate

1. NOUN

/ mæs dɪˈbeɪt / (AmE), / mæs dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)
a large public discussion or argument involving many participants, often held to address significant issues
large-scale public discussion or argument

Examples

The mass debate on gun control drew hundreds of community members to the town hall.
A televised mass debate gave voters the chance to hear arguments from all the political candidates.
Universities often organize a mass debate to encourage students to engage with current affairs.

Synset Examples

The mass debate attracted many passionate speakers.
There was a mass debate on climate change yesterday.
They held a mass debate about education reform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion on a particular topic
  • Synonym: panel discussion - a structured conversation on a topic among selected participants in front of an audience
