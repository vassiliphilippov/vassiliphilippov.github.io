conditional reaction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənəl riˈækʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a learned response that only occurs when a specific stimulus is present, typically as a result of conditioning
a learned response to a specific stimulus

Examples

A dog salivating when it hears a bell is an example of a conditional reaction.
The laboratory measured the subject's conditional reaction to different sounds.
Gaining a conditional reaction to certain smells can result from repeated experiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditioned response - a learned reaction to a conditioned stimulus
  • Antonym: unconditioned response - an automatic, unlearned reaction to a stimulus

Etymology

From 'condition' (to train to respond to a stimulus) + 'reaction' (a response to something).
