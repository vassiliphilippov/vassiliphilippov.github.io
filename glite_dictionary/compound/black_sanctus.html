black sanctus

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsæŋktəs / (AmE), / blæk ˈsaŋktʊs / (BrE)
A hymn or choral composition, sometimes sung during a service, often with a solemn or mocking tone.
a hymn or choral piece in a service

Examples

During the service, the choir sang a moving black sanctus.
He was familiar with the tradition of performing the black sanctus at certain ceremonies.
The sound of the black sanctus echoed throughout the cathedral, leaving the congregation in awe.

Synset Examples

During the service, we heard the beautiful black sanctus.
The choir sang the black sanctus with deep emotion.
The black sanctus resonated through the old cathedral.

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctus' (holy), with 'black' referring to its somber or satirical character.
