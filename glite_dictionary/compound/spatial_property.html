spatial property

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪ.ʃəl ˈprɑː.pɚ.ti / (AmE), / ˈspeɪ.ʃəl ˈprɒ.pə.ti / (BrE)
a characteristic or attribute relating to, or determined by, the arrangement, dimensions, or occupation of space
attribute related to space or spatial arrangement

Examples

The spatial property of an object affects how it fits into its surroundings.
Researchers are studying the spatial properties of molecules to design better drugs.
Understanding the spatial property known as volume is essential in geometry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatial characteristic - attribute determined by location or arrangement in space

Etymology

From Latin spatium (space) and property (from Latin proprietas, ownership, quality).
