actual possession

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.tʃu.əl pəˈzɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃu.əl pəˈzɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Law) Immediate and direct physical control over property.
direct physical control over property; legal term
legal

Examples

He was charged with theft because he had actual possession of the stolen goods.
The court distinguished between actual possession and constructive possession in its ruling.
To prove illegal ownership, the prosecution needed evidence of actual possession of the firearm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constructive possession - legal possession without physical control
