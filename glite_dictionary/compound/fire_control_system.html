fire control system

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ kənˈtroʊl ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə kənˈtrəʊl ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system used to detect, track, and control the firing of weapons at military targets, especially in naval or military operations.
system for controlling weapon fire

Examples

The new warship is equipped with an advanced fire control system for improved targeting accuracy.
Upgrades to the fire control system have greatly enhanced the submarine's ability to engage multiple targets.
Effective training ensures that the crew can operate the fire control system under combat conditions.
