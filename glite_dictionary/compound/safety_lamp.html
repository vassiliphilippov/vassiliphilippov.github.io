safety lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti læmp / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti læmp / (BrE)
an oil lamp designed to prevent the ignition of flammable gases, especially used by miners in explosive atmospheres.
an oil lamp that prevents ignition of flammable gases

Examples

The miners carried a safety lamp to protect themselves from dangerous methane explosions underground.
A safety lamp was vital equipment in coal mines during the 19th century.
The design of the safety lamp allowed workers to detect the presence of flammable gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Davy lamp - a type of safety lamp for use in flammable atmospheres
