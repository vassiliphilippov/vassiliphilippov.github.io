major suit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒər sjuːt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə sjuːt / (BrE)
(in bridge) a suit with the highest scoring value, either spades or hearts.
bridge term: spades or hearts

Examples

Players often try to win the contract in a major suit because of the higher score.
If you have a strong hand in a major suit, consider bidding it early.
He explained that hearts is an example of a major suit in bridge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor suit - (in bridge) a suit with lower scoring value, either clubs or diamonds
