narrow-leaved plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛroʊ livd plænˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ liːvd ˈplæntɪn / (BrE)
a plantain species (Plantago lanceolata) with long, narrow ribbed leaves, commonly found in temperate regions worldwide
a plantain with long, narrow leaves

Examples

The meadow was dotted with narrow-leaved plantain and wildflowers after the spring rain.
Farmers often consider narrow-leaved plantain a persistent weed in their fields.
Researchers studied how narrow-leaved plantain adapts to different soil types across temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plantago lanceolata - scientific name for narrow-leaved plantain
  • Antonym: broad-leaved plantain - a related plantain species with broader leaves
