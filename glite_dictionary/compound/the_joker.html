the Joker

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˈdʒoʊkɚ / (AmE), / ðə ˈdʒəʊkə / (BrE)
A notorious supervillain from DC Comics, the nemesis of Batman; also, a person who behaves in an unpredictable or humorous way.
infamous comic villain or unpredictable person

Examples

In the film, the Joker orchestrates chaos throughout Gotham City.
Some people thought he was funny, but others found him as unpredictable as the Joker.
After playing a series of elaborate pranks, he was jokingly called the Joker of the group.

Synset Examples

His antics made him seem like he would become the Joker.
In this chaotic environment, it's easy to become the Joker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - a person who behaves in a comical or foolish way
  • Synonym: villain - an evil or wicked person, especially a fictional antagonist

Etymology

From the comic book character created by Bill Finger, Bob Kane, and Jerry Robinson in 1940 for DC Comics.
