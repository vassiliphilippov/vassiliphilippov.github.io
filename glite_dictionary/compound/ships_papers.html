ship's papers

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪps ˈpeɪpərz / (AmE), / ʃɪps ˈpeɪpəz / (BrE)
official documents that a ship must carry by law, providing details about ownership, cargo, crew, and voyage
legal documents required on a ship
legal

Examples

The coast guard checked the ship's papers before allowing the vessel to dock.
All ship's papers must be presented to authorities upon entering a foreign port.
The captain kept the ship's papers locked in his cabin for security.
