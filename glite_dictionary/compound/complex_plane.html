complex plane

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛks pleɪn / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛks pleɪn / (BrE)
the plane used to graphically represent complex numbers, with the horizontal axis for real parts and the vertical axis for imaginary parts
plane for representing complex numbers

Examples

Each point in the complex plane corresponds to a unique complex number.
The function's behavior is easier to understand when plotted on the complex plane.
Rotating a vector in the complex plane is achieved by multiplying by a complex number of modulus one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Argand diagram - graphical representation of complex numbers, similar to complex plane
