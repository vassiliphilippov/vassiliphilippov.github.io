letter bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtər bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə bɒnd / (BrE)
A bond that has not been registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission and cannot be sold to the general public.
unregistered, privately sold bond

Examples

The company raised cash by issuing several letter bonds to a select group of investors.
Because it was a letter bond, it could not be freely traded on the open market.
Only qualified buyers were allowed to purchase the letter bond under private placement rules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: registered bond - a bond that has been registered with the SEC and may be sold to the general public
