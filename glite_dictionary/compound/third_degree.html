third degree

1. NOUN

/ θɝd dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / θɜːd dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
intense questioning or interrogation, often involving pressure or coercion, to obtain information or a confession
intense interrogation or questioning

Examples

The suspect was given the third degree until he finally confessed to the crime.
Whenever I come home late, my parents give me the third degree about where I've been.
Journalists accused the police of using the third degree during their investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grilling - intense or thorough questioning
  • Synonym: interrogation - formal or forceful questioning, usually by police

Etymology

Originally American slang, possibly from the Freemasons' third degree as a ceremony of initiation; early 20th century.
