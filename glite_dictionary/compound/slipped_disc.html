slipped disc

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpt dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈslɪpt dɪsk / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a disc between the bones of the spine ruptures or moves out of place, often causing pain, especially in the lower back
back injury involving a spinal disc
medical

Examples

After lifting a heavy box, he found out he had a slipped disc and needed physical therapy.
The doctor explained that a slipped disc can put pressure on nearby nerves, causing intense back pain.
Because of her slipped disc, she had to take time off work to recover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herniated disc - a disc in the spine that has ruptured or bulged out of position
