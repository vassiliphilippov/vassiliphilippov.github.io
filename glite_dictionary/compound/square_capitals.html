square capitals

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr ˈkæp.ɪ.tl̩z / (AmE), / skwɛə ˈkæp.ɪ.tlz / (BrE)
an ancient Roman style of writing with upright, block-like capital letters, which served as the basis for modern capital letters
ancient Roman uppercase writing style

Examples

The museum displayed inscriptions written in square capitals from the first century AD.
Latin scholars often study square capitals to better understand the development of modern alphabets.
Stone monuments in Rome frequently feature text carved in square capitals for clarity and durability.

Synset Examples

Square capitals were used to write inscriptions, and less often to supplement everyday handwriting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capital letters - large letters used at the start of sentences or for names
