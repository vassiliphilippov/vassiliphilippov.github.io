color vision deficiency

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚ ˈvɪʒən dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˈvɪʒən dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
a condition, often genetic, in which a person has difficulty distinguishing between certain colors, typically due to deficiencies in the eye's color-detecting cells
inability to distinguish certain colors
medical

Examples

People with color vision deficiency may find it difficult to distinguish between reds and greens.
A routine eye exam can detect color vision deficiency in children before it affects their learning.
Many traffic signals are designed with shapes to help those with color vision deficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - the inability or decreased ability to see color or perceive color differences
