variable density concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriəbl ˈdɛnsəti kənˈkriːt / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbl ˈdɛnsɪti kɒnˈkriːt / (BrE)
a type of concrete in which the density is adjusted by adding air-entraining agents or lightweight aggregates such as expanded clay, cork granules, or vermiculite
concrete with adjustable density using additives

Examples

Builders often use variable density concrete for insulation purposes in modern construction projects.
The properties of variable density concrete can be customized by altering the amount of lightweight aggregate.
When reducing building weight is a priority, engineers recommend variable density concrete.

Synset Examples

Do not confuse cellular concrete with aerated autoclaved concrete, which is manufactured off-site using an entirely different method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerated concrete - concrete made by introducing air or gas bubbles
  • Degree Lower: lightweight concrete - concrete made with light aggregates for reduced weight
