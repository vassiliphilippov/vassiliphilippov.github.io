dog mercury

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡ ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / dɒɡ ˈmɜːkjəri / (BrE)
a European perennial weedy plant (Mercurialis perennis) with greenish flowers, commonly found in woodland and shady places
a European perennial weedy plant

Examples

We found a patch of dog mercury growing beneath the trees in the old forest.
Gardeners often consider dog mercury a nuisance because it spreads quickly in shaded areas.
The greenish flowers of dog mercury are inconspicuous but easy to identify for botanists.

Etymology

So-called by analogy with 'mercury' (the herb); 'dog' denotes its lesser value compared to true mercury.
