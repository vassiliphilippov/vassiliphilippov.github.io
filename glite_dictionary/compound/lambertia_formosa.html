Lambertia formosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæmˈbɝːti.ə fərˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌlæmˈbɜːti.ə fəˈməʊsə / (BrE)
an erect, bushy shrub native to eastern Australia, known for clusters of red, nectar-rich flowers
Australian shrub with red flowers

Examples

The Lambertia formosa brightened the garden with its vivid red blooms.
Many native birds are attracted to the nectar produced by Lambertia formosa.
Bushwalkers often spot Lambertia formosa growing in rocky areas of New South Wales.

Etymology

Genus Lambertia named after English botanist Aylmer Bourke Lambert; species formosa means 'handsome' in Latin.
