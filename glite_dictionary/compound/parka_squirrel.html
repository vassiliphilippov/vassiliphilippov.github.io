parka squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrkə ˌskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkə ˌskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a large ground squirrel native to the far northern regions of North America, noted for its thick fur
large northern North American ground squirrel

Examples

During the summer, parka squirrels can often be seen foraging above the tree line.
The thick fur of the parka squirrel helps it survive in frigid temperatures.
Researchers tracked several parka squirrels to study their hibernation patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arctic ground squirrel - a ground squirrel species inhabiting the Arctic regions, closely related to the parka squirrel

Etymology

From 'parka' (a type of fur coat) + 'squirrel,' possibly due to the animal's thick fur resembling a parka.
