genus Heliopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs hɛl.iˈɑːp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs hɛl.ɪˈɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as oxeyes, native to the Americas
genus of flowering plants (oxeyes)

Examples

The genus Heliopsis includes several species prized for their bright yellow flowers.
Gardeners often plant members of the genus Heliopsis for their long-lasting summer blooms.
In botanical studies, the genus Heliopsis is recognized for its resemblance to sunflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxeye - a type of daisy-like flower, common name for plants in the genus Heliopsis

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + opsis (appearance), referring to the sun-like appearance of the flowers.
