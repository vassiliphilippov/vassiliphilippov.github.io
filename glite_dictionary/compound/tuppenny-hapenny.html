tuppenny-ha'penny

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌpəni ˈheɪpəni / (AmE), / ˈtʌpəni ˈheɪpəni / (BrE)
Of little value or importance; trivial or insignificant.
trivial; of little value or importance
british

Examples

She dismissed the rumor as just a tuppenny-ha'penny piece of gossip.
The politician's tuppenny-ha'penny promises failed to impress the public.
He threw away the tuppenny-ha'penny souvenirs he bought on vacation.

Synset Examples

Their tuppenny-ha'penny arguments wasted everyone's time.
He found the antique shop filled with tuppenny-ha'penny trinkets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Degree Higher: worthless - having no value at all

Etymology

From British coins 'tuppenny' (two pence) and 'ha'penny' (half penny), meaning something worth almost nothing.
