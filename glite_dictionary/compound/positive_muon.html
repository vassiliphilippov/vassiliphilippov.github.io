positive muon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzətɪv ˈmjuːɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv ˈmjuːɒn / (BrE)
the antiparticle of a muon, a subatomic particle with a positive electric charge that decays into a positron, a neutrino, and an antineutrino
antiparticle of a muon (subatomic particle)
medical

Examples

A positive muon is produced in some particle accelerator experiments to study fundamental interactions.
The decay of a positive muon results in the emission of a positron, a neutrino, and an antineutrino.
Scientists track the path of a positive muon using advanced detectors in physics laboratories.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: muon - an elementary subatomic particle of the lepton family

Etymology

From 'positive' (referring to electric charge) and 'muon', from Greek 'mu', name for the particle in physics nomenclature.
