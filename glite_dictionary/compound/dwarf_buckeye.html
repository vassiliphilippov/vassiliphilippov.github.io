dwarf buckeye

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈbʌk.aɪ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈbʌk.aɪ / (BrE)
a spreading shrub native to the southeastern United States, known for its pink flowers; a species of buckeye
spreading shrub with pink flowers (Southeastern US)

Examples

The garden's dwarf buckeye bloomed early in the spring, displaying clusters of bright pink flowers.
A dwarf buckeye can add vibrant color to a shaded landscape in the southeastern United States.
Botanists often study the dwarf buckeye for its unique adaptation to wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckeye - a tree or shrub of the genus Aesculus, especially Aesculus glabra or Aesculus pavia
