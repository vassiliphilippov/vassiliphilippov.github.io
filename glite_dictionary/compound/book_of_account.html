book of account

1. NOUN

/ bʊk əv əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / bʊk əv əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
a book or register in which financial transactions or commercial accounts are systematically recorded and maintained
register for recording financial transactions

Examples

The company kept every book of account in meticulous order for the annual audit.
Her lawyer requested access to the books of account during the lawsuit.
A book of account can serve as critical evidence in financial disputes.

Synset Examples

they got a subpoena to examine our books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ledger - a book or digital record used to record financial transactions
  • Synonym: journal - a book in which daily transactions are recorded before being posted to the ledger
