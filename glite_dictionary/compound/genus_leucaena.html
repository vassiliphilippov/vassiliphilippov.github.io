genus Leucaena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs luːˈsiːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs luːˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs, native to the Americas, with seed pods resembling those of the acacia.
genus of tropical evergreen trees/shrubs

Examples

The genus Leucaena includes several fast-growing trees valued for their use as fodder and shade.
Botanists studied members of the genus Leucaena to understand their nitrogen-fixing properties.
Farmers often plant species from the genus Leucaena to improve soil fertility in tropical regions.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on the Mexican Spanish 'leucaena', from Greek 'leukos' (white), referring to the flowers.
