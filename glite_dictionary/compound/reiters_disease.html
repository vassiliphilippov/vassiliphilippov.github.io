Reiter's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtərz dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / ˈraɪtəz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A medical condition, mainly affecting males, characterized by inflammation including arthritis, conjunctivitis, and urethritis; now more commonly known as reactive arthritis.
inflammatory syndrome causing arthritis, conjunctivitis, and urethritis
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with Reiter's disease after noticing the combination of arthritis and eye inflammation.
Cases of Reiter's disease are now frequently referred to as reactive arthritis in medical literature.
She experienced symptoms typical of Reiter's disease shortly after a bacterial infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactive arthritis - an alternative term for Reiter's disease

Etymology

Named after Hans Reiter, a German physician who described the syndrome in 1916.
