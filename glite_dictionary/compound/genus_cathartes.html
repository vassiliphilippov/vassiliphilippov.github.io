genus Cathartes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kæˈθɑːrtiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kæˈθɑːtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of New World vultures in the family Cathartidae, including the turkey vulture and related species.
a genus of New World vultures

Examples

The genus Cathartes contains several species commonly known as turkey vultures.
Biologists studied the feeding habits of birds within the genus Cathartes.
The most widespread member of the genus Cathartes is the turkey vulture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cathartidae - the family of New World vultures to which Cathartes belongs
  • Synonym: turkey vulture - a common species in the genus Cathartes

Etymology

From Latin 'cathartes' (purifier), used in zoological naming.
