Soviet Canuckistan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊviət kəˈnʌkɪˌstæn / (AmE), / ˈsəʊvɪət kəˈnʌkɪstan / (BrE)
a humorous or satirical term referring to a fictional version of Canada imagined as having Soviet-style policies or characteristics.
humorous term for fictional Soviet-style Canada
informal slang

Examples

The comedian used Soviet Canuckistan to poke fun at government bureaucracy in Canada.
Political satire shows often reference Soviet Canuckistan when discussing Canadian policies.
In online forums, some users jokingly refer to their country as Soviet Canuckistan during political debates.

Synset Examples

The movie depicted life in Soviet Canuckistan with humor and nostalgia.
His stories about Soviet Canuckistan always make us laugh.

Etymology

Blend of 'Soviet' (pertaining to the former USSR) and 'Canuckistan' (from 'Canuck,' a slang term for Canadian, with '-stan,' a suffix meaning 'land').
