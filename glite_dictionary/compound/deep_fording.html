deep fording

1. NOUN

/ diːp ˈfɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / diːp ˈfɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or place of crossing a river or stream at a location where the water is deep
crossing a deep part of a stream

Examples

The travelers hesitated before attempting the deep fording after heavy rain.
The map marked a deep fording that required skilled navigation.
Early pioneers often risked their lives at deep fording points along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ford - a shallow place in a river or stream where one can cross
