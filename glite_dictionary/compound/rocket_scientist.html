rocket scientist

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkɪt ˌsaɪəntɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt ˌsaɪəntɪst / (BrE)
an engineer who designs, builds, or tests rockets and spacecraft
engineer specializing in rockets

Examples

After years of study, she became a respected rocket scientist at NASA.
People often joke that you don't have to be a rocket scientist to solve simple problems.
The young boy dreamed of becoming a rocket scientist and exploring space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineer - a person who designs, builds, or maintains engines, machines, or public works

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt ˌsaɪəntɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt ˌsaɪəntɪst / (BrE)
a person who is very intelligent or clever, especially in solving complex problems; often used ironically to suggest that something does not require much intelligence
very clever or intelligent person (often ironic use)

Examples

You don't have to be a rocket scientist to understand how this software works.
She may not be a rocket scientist, but she knows how to solve tough problems.
It's not exactly rocket scientist stuff—anyone could do it with a bit of effort.

Synset Examples

you do not have to be a rocket scientist to figure that out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual ability
  • Antonym: idiot - a person of very low intelligence; used as an antonym in ironic phrases

Etymology

From 'rocket' and 'scientist', referring originally to scientists specializing in rocket technology; later used figuratively for intelligence.
