family Lophiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli loʊˈfaɪiːdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli lɒˈfɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large-headed marine fishes known as anglers, characterized by their distinctive appearance and mode of predation.
family of angler fishes

Examples

The family Lophiidae includes several bizarre-looking deep-sea creatures.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Lophiidae near the Atlantic Ridge.
Members of the family Lophiidae are known for their extraordinary ability to lure prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - Common name for fishes in the family Lophiidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Lophiidae*, based on Greek *lophios* (of a crest, relating to the type genus *Lophius*).
