key evidence

1. NOUN

/ ki ˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / ki ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
information or proof that is crucial in proving a case or supporting an argument
crucial proof or information

Examples

The prosecutor presented the key evidence that linked the defendant to the crime.
Her confession served as key evidence in securing the conviction.
Investigators searched the house for any key evidence that might have been overlooked.

Synset Examples

The suspect's unique murder mitten became the key evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or objects used to prove something
