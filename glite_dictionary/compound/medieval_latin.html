Medieval Latin

1. NOUN

/ məˈdiviəl ˈlætən / (AmE), / mɪˈdiːviəl ˈlatɪn / (BrE)
the form of Latin used for writing, scholarship, and liturgy in Europe during the Middle Ages (roughly 6th to 15th centuries)
Latin used in the Middle Ages

Examples

Many important philosophical works were written in Medieval Latin during the Middle Ages.
Scholars study Medieval Latin to better understand European history and religious texts.
The Catholic Church still uses some prayers originally composed in Medieval Latin.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Classical Latin - the form of Latin used in ancient Rome
  • Synonym: Ecclesiastical Latin - the form of Latin used by the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin 'medius aevum' (middle age) + Latin 'latinus' (Latin).

2. NOUN

/ məˌdiviəl ˈlæt.ən / (AmE), / mɪˌdiːvɪəl ˈlæt.ɪn / (BrE)
the form of the Latin language used during the Middle Ages, especially for religious and scholarly purposes
Latin used in the Middle Ages

Examples

Scholars often study Medieval Latin to understand documents from the Middle Ages.
Many monasteries preserved their knowledge in Medieval Latin manuscripts.
The influence of Medieval Latin can still be found in modern scientific terminology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Classical Latin - the form of Latin used in ancient Rome
  • Synonym: Church Latin - Latin as used in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

Latin 'mediaevalis' (of the Middle Ages) + Latin 'Latinus' (Latin language)
