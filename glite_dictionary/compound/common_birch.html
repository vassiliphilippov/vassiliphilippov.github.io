common birch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən bɝtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A European birch tree (Betula pendula) with silvery white peeling bark and distinctive drooping branches.
European birch with white bark and drooping branches

Examples

A stand of common birch provided a lovely contrast to the dark pines.
The silvery bark of the common birch peels off in thin, papery layers.
Several common birches line the edge of the meadow, their branches drooping gracefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - deciduous tree with thin bark and slender branches
