Great Bear

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡreɪt ˈbɛr / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈbɛə / (BrE)
a large constellation in the northern sky, also known as Ursa Major, which contains the star formation called the Big Dipper.
a northern constellation, Ursa Major

Examples

The Great Bear is easily recognizable in the northern hemisphere night sky.
Many ancient cultures created myths about the stars of the Great Bear.
The Big Dipper forms part of the constellation known as the Great Bear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursa Major - the Latin name for the Great Bear constellation
  • Synonym: Big Dipper - the prominent star pattern that is part of the Great Bear constellation
