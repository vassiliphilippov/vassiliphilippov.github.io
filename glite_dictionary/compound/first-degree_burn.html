first-degree burn

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst dɪˈɡri bɝn / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst dɪˈɡriː bɜːn / (BrE)
A mild burn affecting only the outer layer of the skin, causing redness and pain but no blistering.
mild burn affecting skin's surface
medical

Examples

She treated her first-degree burn with cool water and aloe vera gel.
A first-degree burn usually heals within a week without permanent scarring.
Sunburn is a common example of a first-degree burn.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: second-degree burn - burn affecting both the outer and underlying layers of the skin
  • Synonym: burn - an injury to skin or tissue caused by heat, chemicals, or radiation
