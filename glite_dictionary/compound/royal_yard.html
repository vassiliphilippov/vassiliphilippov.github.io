royal yard

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl jɑrd / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl jɑːd / (BrE)
the horizontal spar on the royalmast of a sailing ship, from which the royal sail is set, located above the topgallant yard.
yard on a ship's royalmast above the topgallant

Examples

The sailors climbed up to the royal yard to adjust the highest sail.
During storms, it was risky work to furl the sails on the royal yard.
The royal yard must be hoisted carefully above the topgallant before setting the royal sail.

Synset Examples

Prince William's royal yards are the highest and smallest yards on the ship, are made of wood, and are ‘lifting yards’ that can be raised along a section of the mast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yard - a horizontal spar on a ship from which a sail is set

Etymology

From 'royal' (relating to the uppermost sail) and 'yard' (nautical term for a horizontal spar).
