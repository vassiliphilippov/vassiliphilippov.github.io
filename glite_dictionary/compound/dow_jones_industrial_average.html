Dow Jones Industrial Average

1. NOUN

/ daʊ ˈdʒoʊnz ɪnˈdʌstriəl ˈævərɪdʒ / (AmE), / daʊ ˈdʒəʊnz ɪnˈdʌstriəl ˈævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
an American stock market index that measures the performance of 30 large publicly owned companies listed mostly on the New York Stock Exchange
US stock market index of 30 major companies

Examples

The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose significantly after the announcement of strong quarterly earnings.
Many investors follow the Dow Jones Industrial Average to gauge the overall health of the stock market.
Analysts predicted that the Dow Jones Industrial Average would reach new highs by the end of the year.

Synset Examples

the Dow Jones Industrial Average is the most widely cited indicator of how the stock market is doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: S&P 500 - another major US stock market index
  • Synonym: stock market - a market in which shares are bought and sold

Etymology

Named after Charles Dow and Edward Jones, American financial journalists, who created the index in 1896.
