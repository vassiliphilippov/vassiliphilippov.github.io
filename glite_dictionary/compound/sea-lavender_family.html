sea-lavender family

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈlævəndɚ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / siː ˈlævəndə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of perennial herbs, shrubs, and lianas, found worldwide especially in saltwater areas; also known as Plumbaginaceae.
family of salt-tolerant flowering plants

Examples

The sea-lavender family includes many salt-tolerant plants found in coastal regions worldwide.
Botanists have studied the adaptability of species within the sea-lavender family to saline environments.
Members of the sea-lavender family are popular in gardens for their attractive flowers.
