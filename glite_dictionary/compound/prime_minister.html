prime minister

1. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
the head of government in parliamentary systems or certain countries, usually responsible for running the government and implementing policy
the head of government in some countries

Examples

The prime minister addressed the nation in a televised speech last night.
After the election, she became the youngest prime minister in the country's history.
The prime minister met with foreign leaders to discuss economic cooperation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: president - the elected head of a republican state
  • Synonym: chancellor - senior state official, especially in some European countries

Etymology

From Latin 'primus' (first) and 'minister' (servant, official), meaning 'chief minister.'
