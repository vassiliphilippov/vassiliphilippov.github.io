flight strip

1. NOUN

/ flaɪt strɪp / (AmE), / flaɪt strɪp / (BrE)
a simple airfield lacking normal airport facilities, often used for emergency or military landings
basic airfield without normal facilities

Examples

During the war, many temporary flight strips were constructed for emergency landings.
The small town had only a flight strip, with no terminal or hangars.
Pilots practiced touch-and-go landings at the isolated flight strip outside the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airfield - an area of land set aside for the takeoff, landing, and maintenance of aircraft
  • Degree Higher: airport - a complex for the arrival and departure of aircraft, usually with full facilities
