rent-a-quote

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnt ə ˌkwoʊt / (AmE), / ˈrent ə ˌkwəʊt / (BrE)
a person who is frequently quoted in the media, often giving superficial or predictable remarks
person often quoted in the media
informal

Examples

As a well-known rent-a-quote, she appeared in almost every newspaper article about politics.
Critics accused the actor of being a rent-a-quote more interested in publicity than real issues.
Journalists often rely on rent-a-quote experts to fill space in stories.

Synset Examples

He became famous as a rent-a-quote for news outlets.
The politician was often seen as a rent-a-quote during interviews.
Her expertise made her a sought-after rent-a-quote among journalists.
