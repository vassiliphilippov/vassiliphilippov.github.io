meuniere butter

1. NOUN

/ məʊˈnjɛər ˈbʌtər / (AmE), / məʊnˈjɛː ˈbʌtə / (BrE)
Butter that has been clarified, slowly browned, and seasoned with lemon juice and parsley, often used as a sauce for fish or vegetables.
brown butter with lemon and parsley

Examples

The chef poured a generous amount of meuniere butter over the pan-fried sole.
Her recipe calls for finishing the vegetables with meuniere butter and a sprinkle of fresh parsley.
He expertly browned the meuniere butter before drizzling it atop the cooked asparagus.

Etymology

From French 'beurre meunière', relating to 'meunière' style preparations (meaning 'miller's wife'), in classic French cuisine.
