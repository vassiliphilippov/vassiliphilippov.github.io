false lupine

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈluːpaɪn / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈluːpaɪn / (BrE)
A bushy herb of the western United States with yellow, pea-like flowers, resembling true lupine plants.
western US bushy herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The false lupine brightens the hills each spring with its yellow blossoms.
Gardeners sometimes mistake false lupine for true lupine due to their similar appearance.
During our hike, we spotted several clusters of false lupine near the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - a plant with spikes of blue, purple, or white flowers in the pea family
