nuclear resonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliər rɪˈzɔnəns / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə rɪˈzɒnəns / (BrE)
the resonance absorption of a gamma ray by a nucleus that is identical to the one that emitted the gamma ray
resonance absorption of gamma rays by nuclei

Examples

The discovery of nuclear resonance greatly advanced our understanding of atomic nuclei.
Researchers used nuclear resonance techniques to study the properties of various isotopes.
Precise measurements were made using nuclear resonance to analyze the interaction between gamma rays and nuclei.
