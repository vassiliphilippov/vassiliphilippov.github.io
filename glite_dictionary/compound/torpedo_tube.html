torpedo tube

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˈpidoʊ tuːb / (AmE), / tɔːˈpiːdəʊ tjuːb / (BrE)
A tube installed in a naval vessel, typically near the waterline, from which a torpedo is launched.
tube for firing torpedoes from a vessel

Examples

The submarine fired a torpedo tube to launch its weapon at the enemy ship.
Maintenance crews inspected each torpedo tube to ensure they were functioning properly.
Older warships often had a torpedo tube mounted on the deck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torpedo - self-propelled underwater missile
