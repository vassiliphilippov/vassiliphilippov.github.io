assumed name

1. NOUN

/ əˈsumd neɪm / (AmE), / əˈsjuːmd neɪm / (BrE)
a name that someone adopts instead of their real name, often to conceal their identity; an alias
name adopted instead of one's real name

Examples

She wrote the novel under an assumed name to protect her privacy.
The witness testified in court using an assumed name for safety reasons.
Many criminals are known to travel under an assumed name to evade capture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alias - a false or assumed name used to conceal one's identity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06349528-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈsuːmd neɪm / (AmE), / əˈsjuːmd neɪm / (BrE)
(law) a name under which a business or corporation operates that is different from its legal, registered name.
business name used instead of legal name
legal

Examples

ABC Corporation filed paperwork to conduct business under the assumed name "Main Street Books."
Many states require companies to register their assumed names for transparency.
He opened a restaurant using an assumed name instead of his own company name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade name - the name a business uses to identify itself to the public
  • Synonym: alias - another name used by someone or something
