battle of Thermopylae

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l̩ əv θərˈmɑːpɪˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv θəˈmɒpɪli / (BrE)
a famous battle in 480 BC in which a small Greek force led by Leonidas resisted a much larger Persian army at Thermopylae, ultimately being defeated
historic Greek-Persian battle, 480 BC

Examples

The battle of Thermopylae is remembered for the heroic last stand of the Spartans.
Historians study the battle of Thermopylae to understand ancient military tactics and alliances.
The battle of Thermopylae became a symbol of courage against overwhelming odds.

Etymology

Thermopylae from Greek 'Thermopylai' meaning 'hot gates', referring to the narrow coastal passage where the battle occurred.
