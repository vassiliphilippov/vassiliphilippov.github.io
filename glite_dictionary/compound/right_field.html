right field

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌfiːld / (BrE)
the position in baseball of the outfielder who plays in the right side of the outfield when viewed from home plate
baseball outfield position on the right

Examples

He caught the long fly ball in right field to secure the win.
The coach moved her from left field to right field for this game.
A powerful batter often tries to hit the ball into right field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left field - the position in baseball of the outfielder who plays in the left side of the outfield
  • Synonym: center field - the position in baseball of the outfielder who plays in the center of the outfield

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌfiːld / (BrE)
the part of the outfield in baseball located to the right from the perspective of the catcher, typically behind first and second base
area of baseball outfield to catcher's right

Examples

He made an impressive catch in right field during last night's game.
The coach moved her to right field because of her strong throwing arm.
A home run sailed over right field, thrilling the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left field - the part of the outfield on the catcher's left in baseball

3. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈraɪt fiːld / (BrE)
the area of the outfield in baseball that is to the right from the perspective of the batter facing the field; the section of the field behind first and second base.
part of a baseball outfield to right of batter

Examples

The ball soared into right field for a home run.
The coach moved her to right field after noticing her strong throw.
Spectators in right field caught several foul balls during the game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left field - the part of the baseball outfield to the batter's left
  • Degree Lower: center field - the central part of a baseball outfield
