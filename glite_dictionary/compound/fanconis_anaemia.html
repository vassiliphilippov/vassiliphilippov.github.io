Fanconi's anaemia

1. NOUN

/ fænˈkoʊniz əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / fanˈkəʊniz əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder characterized by decreased production of all types of blood cells (pancytopenia) and underdevelopment of the bone marrow.
rare inherited blood disorder
medical british

Examples

Following the diagnosis of Fanconi's anaemia, the child underwent bone marrow testing.
Fanconi's anaemia is often detected in early childhood during routine blood tests.
Researchers are working to find new treatments for Fanconi's anaemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aplastic anemia - a condition in which the bone marrow does not produce sufficient new cells

Etymology

Named after Swiss pediatrician Guido Fanconi, who first described the disease.
