Boston Tea Party

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑstən ti ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈbɒstən ti ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
A political protest that took place in Boston in 1773, in which American colonists, disguised as Native Americans, dumped British tea into the harbor to protest taxes imposed by the British government.
1773 protest against British tea tax in Boston

Examples

The Boston Tea Party played a crucial role in rallying support for the American Revolution.
Many schoolchildren learn about the Boston Tea Party and its impact on American history.
A new documentary explores how the Boston Tea Party influenced colonial resistance.

Etymology

Named after Boston, Massachusetts, and the protest over British tea taxes in 1773.
