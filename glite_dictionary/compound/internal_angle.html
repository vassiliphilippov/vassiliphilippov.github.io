internal angle

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
The angle formed inside a polygon where two adjacent sides meet.
angle inside a polygon

Examples

Each internal angle of a regular pentagon measures 108 degrees.
To find the sum of the internal angles in a polygon, use the formula (n-2) × 180.
The internal angle increases as the number of sides in a regular polygon increases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external angle - the angle formed outside a polygon by one side and the extension of an adjacent side

Etymology

From Latin 'internus' (inward, internal) and 'angulus' (angle).
