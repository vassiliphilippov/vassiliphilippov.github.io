Hell's Kitchen

1. PROPN

/ hɛlz ˈkɪtʃən / (AmE), / hɛlz ˈkɪtʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a district in Manhattan, New York City, formerly known for its poverty, crime, and slum conditions, now a residential and commercial area
historic Manhattan district, NYC

Examples

She grew up in Hell's Kitchen before it became a trendy neighborhood.
The history of Hell's Kitchen includes stories of notorious gangs and tough living conditions.
Tourists now visit Hell's Kitchen for its restaurants and lively nightlife.

Etymology

The name's origin is uncertain, possibly from 19th-century slang for a rough or lawless area.
