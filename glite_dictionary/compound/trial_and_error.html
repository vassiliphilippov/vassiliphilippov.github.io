trial and error

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪəl ənd ˈɛrɚ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl ənd ˈɛrə / (BrE)
a method of solving problems by trying various solutions until one is successful
problem-solving by trying alternatives

Examples

The engineers used trial and error to develop a working prototype of the device.
Learning to cook often involves trial and error as you discover what works best.
Through trial and error, she finally solved the difficult puzzle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guesswork - the process of making guesses instead of using calculated methods
  • Antonym: systematic - done or acting according to a fixed plan or system; methodical
