sacramental manduction

1. NOUN

/ ˌsækrəˈmɛntəl mænˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəl mænˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
The act of receiving the bread and wine during the celebration of the Eucharist in Christian worship.
receiving bread and wine in the Eucharist

Examples

His first experience of sacramental manduction was deeply moving and solemn.
During Easter, the congregation gathers for sacramental manduction to commemorate the Last Supper.
The church emphasized the importance of sacramental manduction in the life of every believer.

Synset Examples

the governor took Communion with the rest of the congregation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communion - the Christian sacrament in which bread and wine are consumed
  • Synonym: Eucharist - the Christian ceremony commemorating the Last Supper

Etymology

From Latin sacramentum (a solemn oath, sacred rite) and Medieval Latin manductio (the act of leading or bringing to the mouth, from manus (hand) + ducere (to lead)).
