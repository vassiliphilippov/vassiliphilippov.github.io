basivertebral vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪsɪˈvɜrtɪbrəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌbeɪsɪˈvɜːtɪbrəl veɪn / (BrE)
A vein that drains the spongy substance of a vertebra and empties into the anterior internal vertebral venous plexus.
vein draining spongy vertebral bone
medical

Examples

The basivertebral vein plays an important role in draining blood from the vertebral body.
Damage to the basivertebral vein can lead to complications after spinal surgery.
In medical imaging, the basivertebral vein may be visible within the vertebral body.

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base) and 'vertebra' (joint of the spine) combined with 'vein'.
