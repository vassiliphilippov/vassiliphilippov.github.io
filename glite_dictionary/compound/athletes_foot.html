athlete's foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈæθˌlit͡s fʊt / (AmE), / ˈæθliːts fʊt / (BrE)
a fungal infection of the skin on the feet, especially between the toes, causing itching, scaling, and sometimes blistering
fungal infection of the feet
medical

Examples

Athlete's foot is a common problem among swimmers and athletes who use communal showers.
If left untreated, athlete's foot can become a persistent and uncomfortable condition.
He bought an over-the-counter cream to treat his athlete's foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringworm - fungal skin infection caused by dermatophytes
