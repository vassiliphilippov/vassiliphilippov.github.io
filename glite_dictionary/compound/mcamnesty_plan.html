McAmnesty plan

1. NOUN

/ məkˈæmnəsti plæn / (AmE), / məkˈæmnəsti plan / (BrE)
A controversial immigration reform proposal in the United States, especially referencing proposals associated with Senator John McCain that were criticized as granting 'amnesty' to undocumented immigrants.
controversial U.S. immigration reform proposal
informal american

Examples

Many politicians debated the details of the McAmnesty plan before the vote.
Critics argue that the McAmnesty plan would encourage more illegal immigration.
The McAmnesty plan was a frequent topic during the presidential campaign.

Synset Examples

Many voters opposed the McAmnesty plan during the election.

Etymology

Blend of 'Mc' from 'McCain' (Senator John McCain) and 'amnesty', used derisively in U.S. political discourse.
