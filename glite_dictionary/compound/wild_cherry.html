wild cherry

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
the fruit of a wild cherry tree, typically small, tart, and red or black
fruit of the wild cherry tree

Examples

The wild cherry is smaller and more tart than most cultivated varieties.
Birds often feast on wild cherries in the late summer.
She made a delicious tart using fresh wild cherry from the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry - the small, round fruit with a pit

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A cherry tree that grows naturally without cultivation, or its fruit.
uncultivated cherry tree or its fruit

Examples

The wild cherry flourished at the edge of the old wood, its branches laden with small red fruit.
Birds love to feed on the fruit of the wild cherry in late summer.
A botanist explained how the bark of the wild cherry can be used for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet cherry - a cultivated cherry tree producing large, sweet fruit
  • Antonym: sour cherry - a cherry tree producing sour, edible fruit; often cultivated
