undulatory theory

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dʒəˈleɪ.tɔːr.i ˈθɪə.ri / (AmE), / ˌʌn.djʊˈleɪ.tər.i ˈθɪə.ri / (BrE)
the scientific theory that light is transmitted in the form of waves rather than particles
theory that light is transmitted as waves

Examples

The undulatory theory of light was widely accepted in the 19th century before quantum theory emerged.
According to the undulatory theory, light exhibits wave-like behaviors such as interference and diffraction.
Newton's particle theory competed with the undulatory theory for acceptance in the scientific community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave theory - the theory that light behaves as a wave
  • Antonym: particle theory - the theory that light is transmitted as particles

Etymology

From Latin undulatus (wavy), relating to the propagation of waves.
