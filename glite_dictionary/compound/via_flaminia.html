Via Flaminia

1. PROPN

/ ˌviː.ə fləˈmɪni.ə / (AmE), / ˌviː.ə flaˈmɪni.ə / (BrE)
an ancient Roman road in Italy, built by Gaius Flaminius in 220 BC, stretching north from Rome to Cisalpine Gaul
an ancient Roman road in Italy

Examples

Thousands of travelers once marched along the Via Flaminia during the Roman Empire.
Archaeologists have uncovered several milestones on the ancient Via Flaminia.
The Via Flaminia connected Rome with the north, facilitating trade and military movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Appian Way - another famous ancient Roman road

Etymology

Latin via (road) + Flaminia (from the name Flaminius, the Roman magistrate who built it).
