army brat

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmi bræt / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi bræt / (BrE)
a child of a career officer in the United States Army, often raised on military bases and familiar with military life
child of a U.S. Army officer

Examples

Growing up as an army brat, she moved to a new city almost every two years.
As an army brat, he attended schools in five different countries.
Many army brats find it easy to adapt to new environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military brat - a child of a parent in any branch of the military

Etymology

From 'army' + 'brat' (originally meaning 'child' in British slang); 'brat' later adopted a mildly pejorative sense.
