Chin language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪn ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a group of Kamarupan languages spoken in western Myanmar (Burma), Bangladesh, and easternmost India, primarily by the Chin people
language group spoken in western Myanmar, Bangladesh, and India

Examples

Many ethnic groups in Myanmar communicate using a Chin language.
He grew up speaking a Chin language before learning Burmese at school.
Linguists have conducted studies on the grammar of various Chin language dialects.

Etymology

Named after the Chin people, the primary speakers of these languages.
