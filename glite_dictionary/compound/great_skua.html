great skua

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪt ˈskjuːə / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪt ˈskjuːə / (BrE)
a large, brown seabird of the northern Atlantic known for its aggressive behavior and predation of other birds' eggs and chicks
a large brown seabird of the northern Atlantic

Examples

The great skua is notorious for attacking other seabirds to steal their food.
We spotted a flock of great skuas while sailing along the Icelandic coast.
Conservationists are monitoring the population of the great skua in Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skua - a group of predatory seabirds
