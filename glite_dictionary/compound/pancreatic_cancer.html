pancreatic cancer

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæŋkriˈætɪk ˈkænsər / (AmE), / ˌpaŋkrɪˈatɪk ˈkænsə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor of the pancreas, characterized by uncontrolled growth of cells in the pancreatic tissue.
cancer of the pancreas
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that pancreatic cancer is often difficult to detect in its early stages.
Her father underwent surgery after being diagnosed with pancreatic cancer.
Researchers are seeking new treatments to improve survival rates for pancreatic cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - A disease caused by abnormal cell growth with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body

Etymology

From 'pancreatic' (relating to the pancreas) + 'cancer' (malignant tumor); 'pancreatic' from Greek 'pan' (all) + 'kreas' (flesh).
