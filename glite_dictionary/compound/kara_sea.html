Kara Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːrə ˌsiː / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrə ˌsiː / (BrE)
an arm of the Arctic Ocean north of Siberia and east of the Barents Sea, noted for being icebound much of the year
sea north of Siberia in the Arctic Ocean

Examples

The Kara Sea remains frozen for much of the year due to its harsh Arctic climate.
Several important Russian rivers, such as the Ob and Yenisei, flow into the Kara Sea.
Explorers have long been challenged by the unpredictable ice conditions of the Kara Sea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Barents Sea - part of the Arctic Ocean west of the Kara Sea

Etymology

Named after the Kara River in Russia; 'Kara' is of Turkic origin, meaning 'black'.
