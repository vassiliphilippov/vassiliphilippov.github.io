potato peelings

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪɾoʊ ˈpiːlɪŋz / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ ˈpiːlɪŋz / (BrE)
thin strips of potato skin, especially when fried and eaten as a snack or garnish
fried strips of potato skin

Examples

The restaurant serves potato peelings as a crunchy appetizer with dipping sauce.
She fried the potato peelings until they were golden and crisp.
Instead of throwing away the potato peelings, he turned them into a tasty snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato skin - the outer skin of a potato, sometimes fried and eaten as a snack
