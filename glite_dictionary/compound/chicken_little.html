Chicken Little

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɪkən ˈlɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn ˈlɪtl̩ / (BrE)
A fictional character in folk tales who panics easily, believing disaster is imminent after being hit on the head by an acorn.
panicky fictional character in folk tales

Examples

My grandmother always called me Chicken Little whenever I worried too much about small things.
In business meetings, we avoid playing Chicken Little and panicking at the first sign of trouble.
The story of Chicken Little is often used to warn against spreading unfounded fear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Henny Penny - another panicky character in related folk tales
