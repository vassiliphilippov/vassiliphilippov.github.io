glass teat

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs tit / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs tiːt / (BrE)
A feeding nipple made of glass, typically used as part of a baby bottle.
feeding nipple made of glass
archaic

Examples

Antique baby bottles often featured a glass teat instead of a rubber one.
The midwife examined the glass teat for any cracks before use.
Collectors sometimes seek out old bottles with original glass teats.

Synset Examples

She preferred using a bottle with a glass teat.
The bottle is designed with a glass teat for safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nipple - the rubber or plastic teat on a baby's bottle
