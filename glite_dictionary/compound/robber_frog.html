robber frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑbər frɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈrɒbə frɒɡ / (BrE)
A small terrestrial frog native to tropical America, belonging to certain genera in the family Craugastoridae.
small terrestrial frog of tropical America

Examples

The robber frog is commonly found hiding beneath leaf litter in rainforests.
Scientists have discovered a new species of robber frog in Central America.
Unlike tree frogs, robber frogs tend to live entirely on the forest floor.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːbər frɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈrɒbə frɒɡ / (BrE)
a small frog of the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its bark-like call resembling a dog's bark
a southwestern U.S. and Mexican frog with a bark-like call

Examples

At night, the distinctive call of the robber frog echoed through the desert canyon.
Many hikers were surprised to learn that the robber frog's croak sounds like a barking dog.
Researchers studied the habitat of the robber frog in the arid regions of northern Mexico.
