cheese soufflé

1. NOUN

/ tʃiz suːˈfleɪ / (AmE), / tʃiːz suːˈfleɪ / (BrE)
A light, fluffy baked dish made with cheese, eggs, and other ingredients, typically served as a savory entrée or side.
light baked dish with cheese

Examples

The restaurant's signature cheese soufflé is known for its exquisite flavor and airy texture.
Impressed by the rise of her first cheese soufflé, she took a photo to share with friends.
For their anniversary, they cooked a special dinner featuring a homemade cheese soufflé.

Synset Examples

The chef prepared a delicious cheese soufflé for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soufflé - a light, fluffy baked dish

Etymology

Soufflé from French 'souffler' (to blow, to puff), introduced into English culinary vocabulary; 'cheese' from Old English 'cēse'.
