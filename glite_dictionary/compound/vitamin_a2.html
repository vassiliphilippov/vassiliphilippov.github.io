vitamin A2

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪtəmɪn eɪ tuː / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn eɪ tuː / (BrE)
a viscous alcohol found in some fish and amphibians, less active in mammals than vitamin A1, and related to vision and growth
a form of vitamin A found in some animals
medical

Examples

Research has shown that vitamin A2 is present in the retinas of some freshwater fish.
Compared to vitamin A1, vitamin A2 is less efficient in supporting mammalian vision.
Scientists are studying the nutritional roles of vitamin A2 in various animal species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vitamin A1 - a primary form of vitamin A, more active in mammals
