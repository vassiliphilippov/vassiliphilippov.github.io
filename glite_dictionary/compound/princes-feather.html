prince's-feather

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsɪz ˌfɛðər / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪz ˌfɛðə / (BrE)
an annual plant with broad ovate leaves and slender, drooping spikes of crimson flowers, native to southeastern Asia and Australia and naturalized in North America
a plant with crimson flower spikes

Examples

The striking crimson flowers of the prince's-feather made a bold statement in the summer garden.
She added several prince's-feather plants to her flower arrangement for a dramatic effect.
Gardeners appreciate prince's-feather for its long-lasting ornamental spikes and ease of cultivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - a plant genus including species with showy flowers and edible seeds

Etymology

Named for the plume-like appearance of its flower spikes, reminiscent of feathers used for princely decoration.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsɪz ˌfɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪz ˌfɛðə / (BrE)
A tall, showy annual plant (Amaranthus species), native to the Americas, with long, red flower spikes and purplish leaves; its seeds are sometimes used as cereal.
tall plant with red spikes (Amaranthus)

Examples

The vibrant red spikes of the prince's-feather made it a striking addition to the summer garden.
She harvested seeds from her prince's-feather plants to use as a cereal grain.
A row of prince's-feather stood tall along the fence, their crimson plumes waving in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - a genus of plants with brightly colored flower spikes, including prince's-feather

Etymology

Named for its feathery, plume-like flower spikes, suggestive of the ornamental plumes worn by princes.
