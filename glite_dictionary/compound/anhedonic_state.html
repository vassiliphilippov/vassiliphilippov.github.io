anhedonic state

1. NOUN

/ ænˌhiːˈdɑnɪk steɪt / (AmE), / ænˌhiːˈdɒnɪk steɪt / (BrE)
a psychological condition in which a person is unable to experience pleasure from activities usually found enjoyable
inability to feel pleasure
medical

Examples

The patient’s anhedonic state persisted even after weeks of therapy and medication.
Living in an anhedonic state can make daily activities seem meaningless and tiring.
A prolonged anhedonic state is often associated with major depressive disorder.

Synset Examples

His anhedonic state made it hard to enjoy life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anhedonia - inability to experience pleasure

Etymology

From Greek an- (not) + hēdonē (pleasure).
