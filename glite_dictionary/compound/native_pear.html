native pear

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪtɪv pɛr / (AmE), / ˈneɪtɪv pɛə / (BrE)
A tree that bears pear-shaped fruit with a thick, woody outer layer, especially one of certain species occurring in Australia or Africa.
tree with pear-shaped woody fruit

Examples

The native pear is often found growing wild in sandy soils across the countryside.
Birds and other wildlife consume the fruit of the native pear during the dry season.
The thick skin of the native pear protects its seeds from harsh environmental conditions.
