wood frog

1. NOUN

/ wʊd frɑɡ / (AmE), / wʊd frɒɡ / (BrE)
a small, light-brown frog native to moist woodlands in North America, especially common in spruce forests
a brown frog found in North American woodlands

Examples

We spotted a wood frog near the edge of the forest during our hike.
The wood frog is known for its ability to survive freezing temperatures in winter.
Researchers study the wood frog because of its unique adaptation to cold climates.

2. NOUN

/ wʊd frɑg / (AmE), / wʊd frɒg / (BrE)
A small, light-brown frog (Rana sylvatica) found in moist woodlands of North America, especially in forests with spruce trees.
small North American woodland frog

Examples

The wood frog can survive in some of the coldest regions of North America.
Scientists study the ability of the wood frog to freeze and thaw during winter.
We spotted a wood frog near the edge of the forest after the rain.
