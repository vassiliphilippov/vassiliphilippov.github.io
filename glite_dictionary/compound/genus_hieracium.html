genus Hieracium

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs haɪˈræsiəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs hɪəˈræsɪəm / (BrE)
A large genus of perennial hairy herbs, commonly known as hawkweeds, native to Europe, western Asia, northwestern Africa, and North America; sometimes considered to include Pilosella.
genus of perennial herbs (hawkweeds)

Examples

The genus Hieracium includes hundreds of species of hawkweeds found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Many botanists debate the classification of certain species within the genus Hieracium.
The flowers of the genus Hieracium are typically yellow and daisy-like.

Etymology

From Latin *hieracium* (hawkweed), itself from Greek *hierax* (hawk).
