penny pincher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃə / (BrE)
a person who is extremely careful or stingy about spending money
a very frugal or stingy person

Examples

My grandfather is such a penny pincher that he reuses tea bags to save money.
You don't have to be a penny pincher to manage your finances wisely.
The boss is known as a penny pincher who questions even minor expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miser - a person who hoards money and spends as little as possible
  • Antonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money recklessly or wastefully

Etymology

From 'penny' (a small coin) and 'pincher' (one who pinches); refers to extreme frugality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃə / (BrE)
a person who is very unwilling to spend money; someone who is extremely frugal or stingy
person who is very frugal or stingy

Examples

My dad is a real penny pincher and always uses coupons when shopping.
Even her friends tease her for being such a penny pincher at restaurants.
Being a penny pincher helped him save enough money to buy a house.

Synset Examples

As a penny pincher, she never buys anything full price.
He’s such a penny pincher, always looking for discounts.
Being a penny pincher has helped him save for a vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheapskate - a person who is unwilling to spend money
  • Synonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible
  • Antonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money in an extravagant, irresponsible way

Etymology

From 'penny' (a coin of small value) + 'pincher' (one who pinches or holds tightly), referring to someone who holds onto their money tightly.
