moral value

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl ˈvælju / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl ˈvæljuː / (BrE)
A principle or standard that distinguishes right from wrong and guides ethical behavior.
ethical principle or standard

Examples

Parents often try to teach their children moral values from a young age.
The story emphasized the importance of moral values in making decisions.
Different cultures may hold different moral values regarding honesty and respect.

Synset Examples

Christianity plays a significant role in shaping moral values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethical value - a value relating to right and wrong; similar concept
  • Antonym: immoral - not conforming to accepted standards of morality; opposite quality
