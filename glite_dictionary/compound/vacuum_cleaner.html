vacuum cleaner

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm ˌkliː.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm ˌkliː.nə / (BrE)
An electrical appliance used for cleaning floors, carpets, and other surfaces by suction.
electrical appliance for cleaning by suction

Examples

She ran the vacuum cleaner over the carpets before the guests arrived.
This vacuum cleaner picks up pet hair better than any I've tried.
Our old vacuum cleaner finally broke after ten years of use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hoover - a vacuum cleaner (mainly British English)

Etymology

From 'vacuum' referring to empty space and 'cleaner', indicating its function.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm ˌkliː.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm ˌkliː.nə / (BrE)
An electrical appliance that uses suction to remove dust and dirt from floors and other surfaces.
an appliance for cleaning floors

Examples

She bought a new vacuum cleaner to help keep the house tidy.
The vacuum cleaner was clogged and needed to be fixed before use.
Every Saturday, he runs the vacuum cleaner through all the bedrooms.

Synset Examples

He hates using the vacuum cleaner on weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hoover - a vacuum cleaner (especially in British English) or the act of vacuuming; synonymous with vacuum cleaner

Etymology

From 'vacuum' (meaning an absence of matter) and 'cleaner', indicating a device that cleans by creating a vacuum.
