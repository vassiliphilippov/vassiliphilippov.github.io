keyhole limpet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːˌhoʊl ˈlɪmpɪt / (AmE), / ˈkiːhəʊl ˈlɪmpɪt / (BrE)
a marine mollusk with a conical shell that has a hole at the apex
marine mollusk with apex hole in shell

Examples

The keyhole limpet is easily recognized by the opening at the top of its shell.
Researchers studied the keyhole limpet to learn more about marine biodiversity.
Several keyhole limpets were found attached to the rocks at low tide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpet - a type of marine mollusk with a conical shell

Etymology

Named for the keyhole-shaped opening at the shell's apex.
