brain fluid circulation

1. NOUN

/ breɪn ˈfluɪd ˌsɝːkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / breɪn ˈfluːɪd ˌsɜːkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the movement of fluid, especially cerebrospinal fluid, within and around the brain
movement of fluid in the brain
medical

Examples

Disorders can develop when brain fluid circulation is disrupted, leading to symptoms such as headaches and dizziness.
Proper brain fluid circulation is essential for removing waste products from neural tissues.
Hydrocephalus is a condition caused by abnormal brain fluid circulation.

Synset Examples

Too much pressure can affect the brain fluid circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebrospinal fluid - the clear fluid found in the brain and spinal cord
