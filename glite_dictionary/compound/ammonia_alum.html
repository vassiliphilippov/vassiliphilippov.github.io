ammonia alum

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniə ˈæləm / (AmE), / əˈməʊniə ˈaləm / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound, the ammonium double sulfate of aluminum, used in dyeing and water purification
ammonium double sulfate of aluminum

Examples

The textile industry uses ammonia alum as a mordant in the dyeing process.
When dissolved in water, ammonia alum yields a clear, acidic solution.
Ammonia alum can be identified by its white crystalline appearance and solubility in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alum - a class of double sulfate salts with various metal ions
