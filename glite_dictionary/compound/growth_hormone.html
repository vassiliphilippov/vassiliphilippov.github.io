growth hormone

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊθ ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊθ ˈhɔːmən / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates growth and cell reproduction, especially in humans and other animals
hormone that promotes growth
medical

Examples

Some athletes have used growth hormone to try to enhance their performance.
Children with a deficiency in growth hormone may experience delayed physical development.
Doctors sometimes prescribe growth hormone therapy for patients with certain medical conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somatotropin - another term for growth hormone, especially in scientific contexts
