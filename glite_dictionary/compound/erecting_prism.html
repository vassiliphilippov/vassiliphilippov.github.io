erecting prism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛktɪŋ ˌprɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛktɪŋ ˌprɪzəm / (BrE)
a right-angled optical prism used to invert an upside-down image so it appears upright
right-angled prism that makes images upright

Examples

Binoculars often use an erecting prism to display the image right side up.
The camera's viewfinder contains an erecting prism to correct the inverted image from the lens.
Without an erecting prism, the image would appear upside-down to the user.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prism - a transparent optical element with flat, polished surfaces that refract light

Etymology

From 'erect' (upright) + 'prism' (geometric optical element), used in optical instruments.
