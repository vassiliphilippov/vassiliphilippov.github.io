marginal placentation

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪnəl ˌpleɪsənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəl ˌplæsənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a type of placentation in which ovules are attached in a row along the wall (ventral suture) of a simple ovary, typically seen in legumes
ovule arrangement along one side of a simple ovary
medical

Examples

In legumes such as peas and beans, marginal placentation is commonly observed within their pods.
The botanist explained that marginal placentation is characteristic of simple, unilocular ovaries.
When studying flower anatomy, students learned to identify marginal placentation in various plant families.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: axile placentation - placentation where ovules are attached to a central column in a compound ovary
  • Antonym: parietal placentation - placentation where ovules are attached to the inner wall or periphery of the ovary
  • Antonym: basal placentation - placentation with ovules attached at the base of the ovary

Etymology

From 'marginal', meaning at the edge, and 'placentation', from Latin 'placenta' (cake, referring to the placenta's shape in animals); botanical meaning relates to the arrangement of ovules.
