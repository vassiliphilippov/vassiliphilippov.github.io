physical distancing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈdɪstənsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈdɪstənsɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of maintaining a safe physical space between individuals, especially to prevent the spread of infectious diseases by avoiding close contact and crowded places.
maintaining space to prevent infection spread

Examples

Many public places required physical distancing during the pandemic to keep everyone safe.
The company encouraged physical distancing by rearranging office desks and limiting the number of people in meeting rooms.
Through physical distancing, communities have slowed the transmission of contagious illnesses.

Synset Examples

To practice social or physical distancing, stay at least 6 feet (about 2 arm lengths) from other people who are not from your household in both indoor and outdoor spaces.
Physical distancing can prevent the virus from transferring to one another; it doesn't mean that socially we have to disconnect from our loved ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social distancing - Maintaining a set physical distance from others to prevent disease spread
