guard ship

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑrd ʃɪp / (AmE), / ɡɑːd ʃɪp / (BrE)
A warship, either anchored or moving, that is kept at a higher state of readiness than others in its group to respond quickly to emergencies.
warship kept in high readiness

Examples

The guard ship remained anchored in the harbor, ready to respond at a moment's notice.
As the only guard ship on duty, it maintained a high level of vigilance throughout the night.
During the naval exercise, the fleet assigned one guard ship to stand ready in case of emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warship - a ship equipped for warfare
