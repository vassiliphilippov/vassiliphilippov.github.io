azido radical

1. NOUN

/ əˈziːdoʊ ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / əˈziːdəʊ ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group (N₃⁻) derived from hydrazoic acid, consisting of three nitrogen atoms
chemical group N₃⁻ from hydrazoic acid
medical

Examples

The decomposition of the azido radical releases nitrogen gas in the reaction.
Researchers studied how the azido radical reacts with organic substrates.
Formation of the azido radical occurs during certain photochemical processes.

Etymology

From 'azido', relating to nitrogen (from French 'azote' for nitrogen), and 'radical', meaning a group of atoms; 'azido' is from the azide group.
