tumor necrosis factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːmɚ nəˈkroʊsɪs ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmə nɪˈkrəʊsɪs ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a protein produced by certain white blood cells that promotes inflammation and can destroy tumor cells, but may also cause harmful inflammation in diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis
inflammatory protein involved in immune response
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that tumor necrosis factor plays a role in the progression of inflammatory diseases.
New therapies targeting tumor necrosis factor have improved treatment for patients with rheumatoid arthritis.
Researchers study tumor necrosis factor to develop more effective cancer treatments.

Etymology

From Latin tumor (swelling), necrosis (death of tissue), and factor (agent) – refers to its action in causing death of tumor cells.
