deadstick landing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌstɪk ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌstɪk ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
a landing of an aircraft made without the use of engine power
aircraft landing without engine power

Examples

The pilot managed a deadstick landing after both engines failed mid-flight.
Achieving a successful deadstick landing requires training and quick decision-making.
During the test flight, a simulated deadstick landing was carried out safely.

Synset Examples

The pilot performed a perfect deadstick landing.
A deadstick landing requires immense skill and composure.
In an emergency, he executed a deadstick landing on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency landing - a landing made in response to an emergency situation

Etymology

From 'deadstick' (an aircraft with a stopped engine, i.e., the propeller is not turning and thus a 'dead' stick) + 'landing'.
