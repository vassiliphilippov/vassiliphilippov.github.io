the h-word

1. NOUN

/ ði ˈeɪtʃ wɝːd / (AmE), / ði ˈeɪtʃ wɜːd / (BrE)
a euphemistic way of referring to the word 'hate', especially when the term itself is considered too strong or sensitive to use directly
euphemism for 'hate'

Examples

In front of the children, we never use the h-word.
She was shocked that her teacher uttered the h-word during the lesson.
To avoid conflict, he refrained from saying the h-word in the meeting.

Synset Examples

The h-word was taboo in their family discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the f-word - a euphemism for a vulgar word beginning with 'f', especially 'fuck'

Etymology

Modelled on similar phrases like 'the f-word', using the initial letter of the avoided word.
