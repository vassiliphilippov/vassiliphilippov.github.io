Mother Seton

1. PROPN

/ ˈmʌðɚ ˈsitən / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə ˈsiːtən / (BrE)
(1774–1821) The first person born in the United States to be canonized as a saint; founder of the first American Catholic religious order, the Sisters of Charity.
first U.S.-born saint; religious leader

Examples

Many Catholic schools and churches across the country are named after Mother Seton.
The life and work of Mother Seton continue to inspire many people today.
Mother Seton was canonized by the Catholic Church for her dedication to charity and education.

Etymology

Seton is the family name of Elizabeth Ann Seton, known as Mother Seton.
