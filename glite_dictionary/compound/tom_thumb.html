Tom Thumb

1. NOUN

/ tɑm ˈθʌm / (AmE), / tɒm ˈθʌm / (BrE)
a very small person, especially one who is notably or unusually short; originally the name of a legendary tiny character in English folklore
a very small or tiny person

Examples

Despite his age, the child was a real Tom Thumb compared to his taller classmates.
Their new kitten is such a Tom Thumb that it fits in the palm of your hand.
The actor played a modern-day Tom Thumb in the stage adaptation.

Etymology

From the English fairy tale character Tom Thumb, whose adventures date back to at least the 16th century.
