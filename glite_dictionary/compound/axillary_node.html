axillary node

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksəˌleri noʊd / (AmE), / ˈæksɪləri nəʊd / (BrE)
any of the lymph nodes located in the armpit, responsible for fighting infections in the neck, chest, and arm regions
lymph node in the armpit
medical

Examples

The patient had a swollen axillary node that needed to be examined.
Surgeons removed several axillary nodes during the breast cancer operation.
A tender axillary node can be a sign of infection or inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymph node - a small swelling in the lymphatic system where lymph is filtered and lymphocytes are formed

Etymology

From Latin 'axilla' (armpit) + 'node' (knot, node).
