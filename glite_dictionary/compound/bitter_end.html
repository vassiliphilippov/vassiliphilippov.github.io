bitter end

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtər end / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə end / (BrE)
(Nautical) The inboard end of a line or cable, especially the end that is secured to a bitt on a ship.
end of a cable attached to a ship's bitt

Examples

The sailor made sure the bitter end was securely fastened to the deck.
During the storm, only the bitter end of the rope remained attached to the ship.
He released the chain until the bitter end came into view.

Etymology

From 'bitt', a post on a ship for securing lines, plus 'end'.
