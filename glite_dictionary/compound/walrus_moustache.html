walrus moustache

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑl.rəs məˈstæʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːl.rəs ˈmʌstæʃ / (BrE)
a thick, bushy moustache that droops downward, resembling the whiskers of a walrus
large, drooping bushy moustache

Examples

He was famous for his walrus moustache, which covered his upper lip completely.
The old professor twirled his walrus moustache thoughtfully while pondering the question.
In the photograph, her grandfather sports a thick walrus moustache and a friendly smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handlebar moustache - a moustache with the ends curled upward

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the whiskers of a walrus, a large marine mammal.
