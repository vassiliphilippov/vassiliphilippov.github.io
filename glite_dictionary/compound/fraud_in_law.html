fraud in law

1. NOUN

/ frɔd ɪn lɔ / (AmE), / frɔːd ɪn lɔː / (BrE)
A type of fraud presumed from the circumstances, regardless of the fraudster's intent.
presumed fraud regardless of intent (legal)
legal

Examples

The court determined it was fraud in law even though there was no evidence of malicious intent.
Certain transactions can constitute fraud in law if they appear deceptive by their nature.
He was found liable for fraud in law after transferring property to avoid creditors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fraud in fact - fraud that requires proof of deceitful intent
