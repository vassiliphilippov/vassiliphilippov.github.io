lateral geniculate

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæt̬.ɚ.əl dʒəˈnɪk.jə.lət / (AmE), / ˌlæt.ər.əl dʒɪˈnɪk.jə.lət / (BrE)
a relay center in the thalamus of the brain that receives visual information from the retina and transmits it to the occipital lobe of the cerebral cortex
brain structure relaying visual signals
medical

Examples

Damage to the lateral geniculate can result in visual field deficits.
Researchers studied the activity of the lateral geniculate in response to changes in light intensity.
The optic nerve transmits signals from the retina to the lateral geniculate before they reach the visual cortex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lateral geniculate nucleus - the full anatomical term for lateral geniculate structure in the thalamus

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (lateral, of the side) and 'geniculatus' (kneelike, bent at an angle), referring to its position and shape.
