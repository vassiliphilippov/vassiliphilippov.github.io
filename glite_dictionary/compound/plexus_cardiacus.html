plexus cardiacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs kɑrˈdiːəkəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs kɑːˈdiːəkəs / (BrE)
a network of nerves supplying the heart and nearby structures
network of nerves near the heart
medical

Examples

The plexus cardiacus transmits nerve signals that regulate heart rate and cardiac function.
Damage to the plexus cardiacus may result in abnormal heart rhythms or chest pain.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid injuring the plexus cardiacus.

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network, braid) and 'cardiacus' (of the heart).
