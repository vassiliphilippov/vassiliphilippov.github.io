white noise

1. NOUN

/ waɪt nɔɪs / (AmE), / waɪt nɔɪz / (BrE)
a continuous noise containing all audible frequencies at equal intensity, often used to mask other sounds
continuous noise with equal intensity across all frequencies

Examples

Some people use white noise machines to help them sleep by blocking out background sounds.
The steady hum of the fan created a white noise effect in the office.
Researchers often use white noise in experiments to mask unwanted auditory stimuli.

Synset Examples

white noise is a good masking agent

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pink noise - noise with intensity that decreases as frequency increases
  • Degree Lower: brown noise - noise with even more energy at lower frequencies than pink noise

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'white light', which contains all visible wavelengths.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt nɔɪs / (AmE), / waɪt nɔɪz / (BrE)
a steady sound containing all frequencies of equal intensity, often used to mask other noises or promote concentration and sleep
steady background sound that masks other noises

Examples

Many people use white noise machines to help them sleep in noisy environments.
The sound of falling rain creates a natural white noise that many find soothing.
Some offices play white noise to reduce distractions and increase productivity.

Synset Examples

The white noise helped drown out the traffic outside.
I fell asleep to the white noise of the fan.
She used an app that generated white noise for better focus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pink noise - sound with equal energy per octave, often used for audio testing or relaxation

Etymology

From the analogy to 'white light', which contains all visible wavelengths.
