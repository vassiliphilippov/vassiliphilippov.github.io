greenhouse warming

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌhaʊs ˈwɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhaʊs ˈwɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the increase in Earth's temperature caused by atmospheric gases that trap solar radiation, allowing sunlight in but preventing heat from escaping
rise in Earth's temperature from trapped heat

Examples

Scientists have warned that greenhouse warming could have serious consequences for global weather patterns.
The increase in carbon dioxide has accelerated greenhouse warming over the past century.
Experts agree that reducing emissions is crucial to slowing greenhouse warming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse effect - the process by which heat is trapped in the Earth's atmosphere
  • Degree Higher: global warming - the ongoing increase in the Earth's average surface temperature

Etymology

From 'greenhouse,' a glass building for growing plants, referencing how heat is trapped inside, and 'warming.'
