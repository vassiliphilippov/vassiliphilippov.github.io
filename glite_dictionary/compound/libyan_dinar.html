Libyan dinar

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbiən dɪˈnɑr / (AmE), / ˈlɪbiən dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
the official currency and basic monetary unit of Libya
currency of Libya

Examples

The price of oil is often quoted in Libyan dinar when dealing with North African markets.
Tourists should exchange their money for Libyan dinars before traveling to Libya.
He converted his euros into Libyan dinar at the airport exchange counter.

Etymology

From Arabic 'dīnār', itself from Latin 'denarius', combined with 'Libyan' for the country of issue.
