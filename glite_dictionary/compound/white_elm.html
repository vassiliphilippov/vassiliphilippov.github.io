white elm

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ɛlm / (AmE), / waɪt ɛlm / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree of eastern North America (Ulmus americana), valued for its graceful branching and use as an ornamental shade tree.
a North American elm tree

Examples

The old white elm in our backyard provides deep shade throughout the summer.
Several white elms lined the avenue, their spreading branches arching gracefully overhead.
Dutch elm disease has threatened many white elm populations in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American elm - another name for the white elm (Ulmus americana)
