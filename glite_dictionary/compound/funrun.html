funrun

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌnˌrʌn / (AmE), / ˈfʌnˌrʌn / (BrE)
A non-competitive footrace held primarily for enjoyment, often to raise money for charity.
non-competitive charity footrace

Examples

The annual funrun attracted hundreds of participants this year, raising money for local charities.
She decided to join the funrun with her colleagues to support cancer research.
Many families signed up for the city's funrun and enjoyed a morning of exercise and community spirit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: marathon - long-distance running race of 26.2 miles
  • Degree Higher: race - a competition of speed, usually over a set distance

Etymology

Formed from 'fun' and 'run', first used in late 20th century.
