hog sucker

1. NOUN

/ hɑɡ ˈsʌkər / (AmE), / hɒɡ ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
A freshwater fish found in warm, clear, shallow streams in North America, noted for its large mouth and sucker-like lips.
freshwater fish of shallow streams

Examples

The hog sucker is well-adapted to life at the bottom of fast-flowing streams.
Anglers often spot a hog sucker foraging among the rocks in the river.
Biologists study the diet of the hog sucker to understand stream ecosystems.
