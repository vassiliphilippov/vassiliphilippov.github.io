alpine clubmoss

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn ˈklʌbˌmɑs / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn ˈklʌbˌmɒs / (BrE)
a species of clubmoss (Lycopodium alpinum) found in northern latitudes of the Northern Hemisphere, noted for its creeping stems and scale-like leaves
a northern clubmoss species (Lycopodium alpinum)

Examples

The alpine clubmoss is commonly found across the tundra and mountainous regions of the Arctic.
Botanists collected several samples of alpine clubmoss to study its adaptation to cold environments.
Some hikers admire the carpet-like spread of alpine clubmoss along high-altitude trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clubmoss - any of a group of primitive, evergreen, mosslike plants

Etymology

From 'alpine', relating to high mountains, and 'clubmoss', a group of primitive plants resembling mosses.
