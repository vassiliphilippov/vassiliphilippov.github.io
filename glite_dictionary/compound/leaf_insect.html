leaf insect

1. NOUN

/ lif ˈɪn.sɛkt / (AmE), / liːf ˈɪn.sɪkt / (BrE)
A tropical insect of southern Asia and the East Indies, known for its flattened, leaf-like body that serves as camouflage.
tropical insect resembling a leaf

Examples

The leaf insect blended perfectly into the foliage, making it nearly invisible to predators.
Many visitors to the rainforest exhibit are amazed by the leaf insect's incredible mimicry.
Researchers studied how the camouflage of the leaf insect helps it avoid being eaten.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking stick - an insect with a body resembling a twig or stick
