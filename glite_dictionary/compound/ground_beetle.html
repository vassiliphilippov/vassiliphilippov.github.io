ground beetle

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a predatory, usually shiny black or metallic beetle found on the ground, known for feeding on harmful insects
predatory ground-dwelling beetle

Examples

The ground beetle is often found under rocks, hunting for its prey at night.
Farmers value the ground beetle because it helps control crop-damaging pests.
Some species of ground beetle have a metallic green sheen on their backs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carabid - another term for ground beetle, especially in scientific contexts
