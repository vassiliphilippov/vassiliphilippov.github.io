genus Agama

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈɡɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈɡɑː.mə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Agamidae, comprising a group of lizards widespread in Africa and parts of Asia
type genus of the family Agamidae (lizards)

Examples

The genus Agama includes several brightly colored lizard species found in sub-Saharan Africa.
Taxonomists reclassified certain species under the genus Agama after new genetic evidence emerged.
Males from the genus Agama are known for their striking coloration during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Agamidae - the family of lizards to which Agama belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Agama', a genus of lizards named by naturalists.
