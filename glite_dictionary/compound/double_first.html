double first

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
A first-class honours degree awarded in two separate subjects, especially at some British universities such as Oxford and Cambridge.
first-class honours in two subjects
british

Examples

He graduated with a double first from Cambridge in history and English.
Achieving a double first is considered a rare academic distinction.
She was delighted to learn she had earned a double first after years of hard work.
