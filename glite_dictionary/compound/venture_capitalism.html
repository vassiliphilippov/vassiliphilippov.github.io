venture capitalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛntʃɚ ˌkæpɪtəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɛntʃə ˌkapɪtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A form of capitalism in which investors provide funding to innovative or high-risk businesses, especially in the technology sector, with the expectation of large potential profits.
investment in innovative high-risk businesses

Examples

Many Silicon Valley startups owe their success to venture capitalism.
The rapid growth of the tech sector is driven by venture capitalism and innovation.
During the 1990s, venture capitalism funded many internet companies that later became global giants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venture capital - money invested in new or emerging businesses

Etymology

From 'venture capital' (investment in new businesses) and 'capitalism'.
