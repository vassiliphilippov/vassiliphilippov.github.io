intrauterine device

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˌjuː.tə.ˈraɪ.nɪn dɪˈvaɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˌjuː.tjʊˈraɪ.nɪn dɪˈvaɪs / (BrE)
A small contraceptive device, usually made of plastic or metal, that is inserted into the uterus through the vagina to prevent pregnancy.
contraceptive device inserted into the uterus
medical

Examples

After consulting with her doctor, she decided to have an intrauterine device fitted for long-term birth control.
The clinic offers information on different types of intrauterine devices and their possible side effects.
An intrauterine device can be removed at any time if pregnancy is desired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IUD - abbreviation for intrauterine device

Etymology

From Latin 'uterus' (womb), with 'intra-' meaning 'within', and 'device' from Latin 'divisus' (divided, arranged).
