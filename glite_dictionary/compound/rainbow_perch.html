rainbow perch

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌboʊ pɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbəʊ pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored fish of the Pacific coast of North America, known for its iridescent hues; species Hypsurus caryi
colorful Pacific coast fish

Examples

The biologist studied the vibrant scales of the rainbow perch along the California coast.
Snorkelers often spot the rainbow perch darting among kelp forests.
Local anglers prize the rainbow perch for its striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surfperch - a family of fishes (Embiotocidae) that includes the rainbow perch
