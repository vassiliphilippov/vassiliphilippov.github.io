drop arch

1. NOUN

/ drɑp ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / drɒp ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch with a slightly pointed, shallow curve formed from two centers within the span, commonly used in architecture
a shallow, slightly pointed architectural arch

Examples

The cathedral features a beautiful drop arch above its main doorway.
Architects in the Gothic period often used the drop arch for its distinctive appearance.
A drop arch distributes weight differently from a traditional round arch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - a curved structure supporting the weight above an opening
