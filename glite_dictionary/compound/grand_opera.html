grand opera

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈɑpɚə / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈɒpərə / (BrE)
an opera in which all the dialogue is sung, typically on a large or serious scale
opera with all-sung text

Examples

The Metropolitan Opera is famous for staging impressive grand opera productions each season.
She prefers grand opera to lighter musical theatre because of its emotional intensity.
Verdi’s "Aida" is considered a masterpiece of grand opera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opera - a dramatic work in which all or most of the text is sung to music
