combining weight

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbaɪnɪŋ weɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌɪnɪŋ weɪt / (BrE)
the atomic weight of an element that corresponds to the amount needed to combine with a standard quantity of another element, often with oxygen as the standard (set at 8)
atomic weight relative to combining capacity

Examples

The combining weight of hydrogen is much lower than that of oxygen.
Scientists calculated the combining weight of sulfur based on its reaction with oxygen.
Early chemists used the combining weight to compare the reactivity of different elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivalent weight - weight of a substance that combines with a fixed amount of another
