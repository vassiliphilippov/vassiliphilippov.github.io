isotonic solution

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈtɑnɪk səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈtɒnɪk səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a solution that has the same osmotic pressure as body fluids such as blood, preventing net movement of water across cell membranes
solution with same osmotic pressure as blood
medical

Examples

Saline is often used as an isotonic solution in hospitals for intravenous therapy.
When placed in an isotonic solution, red blood cells maintain their normal shape.
The scientist prepared an isotonic solution to study its effects on plant cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertonic solution - a solution with higher osmotic pressure than blood
  • Antonym: hypotonic solution - a solution with lower osmotic pressure than blood

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + tonos (tension) + solution.
