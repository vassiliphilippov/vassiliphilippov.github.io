fenestra of the cochlea

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛstrə əv ðə koʊˈkliə / (AmE), / fəˈnɛstrə əv ðə ˈkɒkliə / (BrE)
A small anatomical opening or window leading into the cochlea of the inner ear.
anatomical opening to the cochlea
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully inspected the fenestra of the cochlea during the ear operation.
A blockage at the fenestra of the cochlea can impact hearing ability.
Reconstruction of the fenestra of the cochlea may be necessary in cases of severe damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval window - the membrane-covered opening from the middle ear to the cochlea
  • Synonym: round window - another membrane-covered opening leading from the middle ear to the cochlea

Etymology

From Latin 'fenestra' (window) and 'cochlea' (snail, referring to the spiral shape of the ear structure).
