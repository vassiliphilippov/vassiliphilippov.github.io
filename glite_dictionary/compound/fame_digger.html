fame digger

1. NOUN

/ feɪm ˈdɪɡər / (AmE), / feɪm ˈdɪɡə / (BrE)
a person who seeks attention or fame, often by associating with celebrities or engaging in publicity-seeking behaviors
person who seeks fame or attention
informal

Examples

The tabloids accused her of being a fame digger after she started dating the movie star.
Many reality TV contestants are dismissed as nothing more than fame diggers by the public.
He insisted he was not a fame digger, but genuinely interested in the project.

Synset Examples

Everyone knew she was just a fame digger.
He was labeled a fame digger after his latest antics.
The reality show was full of fame diggers looking for attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold digger - a person who forms relationships to exploit others financially

Etymology

Modeled after 'gold digger'; fame + digger, indicating someone who pursues fame the way a 'gold digger' pursues wealth.
