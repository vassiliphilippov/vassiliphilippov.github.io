parabolic mirror

1. NOUN

/ pɛrəˈbɑlɪk ˈmɪrər / (AmE), / ˌparəˈbɒlɪk ˈmɪrə / (BrE)
A mirror with a surface shaped as a paraboloid, designed to focus parallel rays of light to a single point.
mirror shaped as a paraboloid

Examples

The telescope uses a large parabolic mirror to gather and focus light from distant stars.
A solar cooker concentrates sunlight onto a small area with a parabolic mirror.
The headlights were designed with a parabolic mirror to improve the focus of the beam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parabolic reflector - A reflective surface shaped like a paraboloid, often used for focusing waves
  • Degree Lower: concave mirror - A mirror with a concave surface used to focus light

Etymology

From 'parabolic', relating to a parabola (from Greek 'parabolē'), and 'mirror' (from Latin 'mirare', to look at).
