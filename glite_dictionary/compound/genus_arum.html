genus Arum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɛərəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɑːrəm / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Araceae, consisting of tuberous perennial herbs native to Europe and Asia, typically bearing heart-shaped leaves.
botanical genus of Araceae family

Examples

The genus Arum includes the well-known cuckoo-pint found in woodland areas.
Several species within the genus Arum are cultivated for their unusual flowering structures.
Botanists identified the specimen as belonging to the genus Arum due to its tuberous roots and leaf shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Araceae - the plant family to which genus Arum belongs

Etymology

From Latin *Arum*, the classical name for the plant; 'genus' from Latin meaning 'kind' or 'group'.
