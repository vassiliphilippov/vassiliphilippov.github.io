WATS line

1. NOUN

/ wæts laɪn / (AmE), / wæts laɪn / (BrE)
A telephone line providing long-distance service at fixed rates for specified geographic zones, especially used for business purposes.
long-distance telephone service line

Examples

The company installed a WATS line to handle its growing volume of customer service calls.
Before the advent of the internet, businesses relied heavily on the WATS line for long-distance communication.
Our office cut costs by switching from multiple phone lines to a single WATS line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leased line - a private telecommunication circuit between two or more locations

Etymology

Acronym from 'Wide Area Telephone Service.'
