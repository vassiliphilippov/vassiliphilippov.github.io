genus Chordospartium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkɔːrdəʊˈspɑːrtiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkɔːdəˈspɑːtiəm / (BrE)
A small genus of leguminous trees native to New Zealand, including two species known as weeping tree broom, some of which are endangered.
small genus of New Zealand trees

Examples

The genus Chordospartium is notable for its rare and endangered weeping tree brooms found only in New Zealand.
Botanists are working to conserve the genus Chordospartium due to its threatened status.
Members of the genus Chordospartium are unique among leguminous trees for their elegant, drooping branches.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'Chordo-' (referring to string or cord) and 'Spartium' (Latin for 'broom').
