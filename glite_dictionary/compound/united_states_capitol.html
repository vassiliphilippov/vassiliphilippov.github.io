United States Capitol

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː.nɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts ˈkæp.ɪ.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts ˈkæp.ɪ.təl / (BrE)
The government building in Washington, D.C., where the United States Senate and the House of Representatives meet.
the seat of the U.S. Congress in Washington, D.C.

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit the United States Capitol every year to admire its architecture and history.
A major debate was held inside the United States Capitol regarding the new healthcare legislation.
Security was heightened around the United States Capitol during the presidential inauguration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Capitol - a building where a legislative body meets
  • Synonym: United States Congress - the legislative branch of the U.S. federal government

Etymology

From Latin 'capitoleum', the temple of Jupiter in ancient Rome; 'Capitol' refers to the hill on which the Roman temple stood.
