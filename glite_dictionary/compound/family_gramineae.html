family Gramineae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɡræˈmɪniː.iː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɡræˈmɪniː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of chiefly herbaceous but sometimes woody plants, including grasses, cereals, bamboo, reeds, and sugar cane
the botanical family of grasses

Examples

The family Gramineae includes staple crops such as wheat, rice, and corn.
Bamboo, although woody, also belongs to the family Gramineae.
Scientists study the family Gramineae to improve cereal crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poaceae - the alternate and now more widely accepted scientific name for the grass family

Etymology

From Latin 'Gramineae', from 'gramen' meaning grass; scientific name for the grass family.
