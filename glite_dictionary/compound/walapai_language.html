Walapai language

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləˌpaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɒləˌpaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Walapai people of northwestern Arizona.
the language of the Walapai people

Examples

The preservation of the Walapai language is crucial for maintaining the tribe's cultural heritage.
Linguists have documented many traditional stories told in Walapai language.
There are educational programs to help young people learn the Walapai language in Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Havasupai language - a closely related Yuman language spoken by the Havasupai people

Etymology

Walapai is from the tribal name of the people in northwestern Arizona; language from Latin lingua.
