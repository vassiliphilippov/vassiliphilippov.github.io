to check bogie

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk ˈboʊɡi / (AmE), / tʃɛk ˈbəʊɡi / (BrE)
to inspect the bogie (wheel assembly) of a vehicle, especially for damage or wear
inspect a vehicle's bogie

Examples

The mechanic had to check bogie before the train could depart.
As part of daily maintenance, engineers regularly check bogie for signs of damage.
She was trained to check bogie thoroughly to ensure passenger safety.

Synset Examples

He checked the bogie for any signs of wear.
