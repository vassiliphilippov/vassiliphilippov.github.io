trisomy 21

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈsoʊmi ˌtwɛnti ˈwʌn / (AmE), / traɪˈsəʊmi ˌtwenti ˈwʌn / (BrE)
a congenital disorder caused by the presence of an extra 21st chromosome, resulting in characteristic physical and cognitive features; also known as Down syndrome
genetic disorder (Down syndrome) caused by an extra 21st chromosome
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of trisomy 21 was confirmed after genetic testing of the newborn.
Children with trisomy 21 may require additional medical and educational support.
Prenatal screening can sometimes detect the presence of trisomy 21 before birth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Down syndrome - a genetic disorder caused by trisomy 21, characterized by developmental and physical differences

Etymology

From 'trisomy' (abnormality with three chromosomes instead of a pair) and '21' referring to chromosome 21.
