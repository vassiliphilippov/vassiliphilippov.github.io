field winding

1. NOUN

/ ˈfild ˌwaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌwaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
an electric coil wound around a field magnet, producing the magnetic force required for operation in electrical machines
coil creating magnetism in a machine

Examples

The generator's field winding had to be replaced after the machine failed to produce enough magnetic force.
In electric motors, the field winding is energized to create the necessary magnetic field for rotation.
Proper insulation of the field winding extends the life of electrical machines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armature winding - the coil in which the electromotive force is induced in a machine
