rough trot

1. NOUN

/ rʌf trɑt / (AmE), / rʌf trɒt / (BrE)
A period or experience that is difficult or challenging; also, a difficult or uncomfortable ride or journey, especially relating to horseback riding.
a difficult experience or challenging ride
informal british

Examples

After losing his job and getting ill, he really had a rough trot last year.
The team's losing streak has been a rough trot for their loyal fans.
The horse stumbled a few times, making it a rough trot for the rider.

Synset Examples

We had a rough trot through the forest after the rain.
The hike started with a rough trot up the hill.
The horse had a rough trot on the uneven terrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard time - a period of difficulty or struggle
  • Antonym: easy ride - an experience free from difficulty or challenge
