daisy-bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪzi ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Olearia, native mostly to Australia, grown for its evergreen foliage and a profusion of daisy-like flowers with white, purple, or blue petals
Australian Olearia shrub with daisy-like flowers

Examples

We planted a daisy-bush in the garden, and its purple blooms attracted butterflies.
The daisy-bush is known for its hardy nature and beautiful evergreen leaves.
Some species of daisy-bush have fragrant flowers that brighten up any landscape.
