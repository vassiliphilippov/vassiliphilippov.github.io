osage orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊseɪdʒ ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈəʊseɪdʒ ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree (Maclura pomifera) native to south central United States, having spiny branches, glossy dark green leaves, and large, inedible greenish-yellow fruit; its hard, orange-colored wood was traditionally used for making bows.
spiny tree with inedible fruit and hard wood

Examples

The osage orange is often planted as a natural fence because of its dense, thorny branches.
Native Americans traditionally used osage orange wood to craft strong and flexible bows.
Although the osage orange fruit resembles an orange, it is not edible and has a rough, bumpy surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maclura - the genus containing osage orange

Etymology

Named after the Osage Nation, a Native American tribe, and for its orange-like fruit.
