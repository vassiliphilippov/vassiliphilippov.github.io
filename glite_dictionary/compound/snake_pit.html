snake pit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪk pɪt / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk pɪt / (BrE)
A place or situation of great confusion, distress, or turmoil.
place of extreme turmoil or distress

Examples

Working in that overcrowded office felt like a snake pit during tax season.
The debate quickly turned into a snake pit of accusations and shouting.
After the scandal broke, the company became a snake pit of rumors and distrust.

Synset Examples

the hell of battle
the inferno of the engine room
when you're alone Christmas is the pits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellhole - an extremely unpleasant or chaotic place
  • Synonym: bedlam - a scene of uproar and confusion
  • Antonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety

Etymology

Originally referred to an enclosure for snakes; later used figuratively for institutions, especially mental asylums, then more generally for chaotic or traumatic environments.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪk ˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk ˌpɪt / (BrE)
A place or situation of extreme disorder, chaos, or mental distress; formerly, a pejorative term for an insane asylum.
disorderly place; dated term for asylum
informal

Examples

The old hospital was often described as a snake pit due to its terrible conditions.
Her new job felt like a snake pit—everyone was constantly arguing and plotting.
In the past, people feared ending up in a snake pit rather than receiving real help for their mental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane asylum - institution for the care of people with mental illness
  • Synonym: bedlam - a scene of uproar and confusion; a madhouse

Etymology

Originally referring to literal pits of snakes; figurative use dates from mid-20th century after the novel and film 'The Snake Pit' (1946/1948).
