abatement of a nuisance

1. NOUN

/ əˈbeɪtmənt əv ə ˈnuːsəns / (AmE), / əˈbeɪtmənt əv ə ˈnjuːsəns / (BrE)
(Law) The removal, termination, or destruction of something that has been determined to be a nuisance, especially by legal means.
legal removal of a nuisance
legal

Examples

The city ordered the abatement of a nuisance after neighbors complained about the abandoned property.
Legal action was taken to ensure the abatement of a nuisance caused by excessive noise.
The court may authorize the abatement of a nuisance if public welfare is at risk.

Etymology

From 'abatement' (from Old French abattre, to beat down) and 'nuisance' (from Old French nui(s)ance, harm).
