seventeen-year locust

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛv.ənˌtiːn ˈjɪr ˈloʊ.kəst / (AmE), / ˌsɛv.ənˌtiːn ˈjɪə ˈləʊ.kəst / (BrE)
A North American cicada that emerges in large numbers once every 13 to 17 years, due to its long nymph stage.
periodical North American cicada

Examples

Every summer, the arrival of the seventeen-year locust fascinates scientists and nature lovers alike.
The noise made by the seventeen-year locust can be overwhelming in some areas.
Farmers often prepare for the emergence of the seventeen-year locust to protect their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - a type of large insect known for its distinct sound
  • Synonym: periodical cicada - a cicada emerging at long, fixed intervals, especially 13 or 17 years

Etymology

Named for its 17-year life cycle before emergence as an adult.
