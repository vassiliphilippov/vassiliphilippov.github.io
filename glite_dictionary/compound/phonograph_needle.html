phonograph needle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊnəˌɡræf ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəɡrɑːf ˈniːdl / (BrE)
A small, pointed stylus that produces sound by tracing the grooves of a phonograph record.
stylus for a phonograph record player

Examples

The phonograph needle needs to be replaced to improve sound quality.
He gently placed the phonograph needle onto the spinning record.
Vintage record players often require a specific type of phonograph needle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylus - a pointed device that makes contact with a record or surface to produce sound or data
