the pillar of

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈpɪlər əv / (AmE), / ðə ˈpɪlə əv / (BrE)
a fundamental principle, support, or source of strength on which something depends or is built
a fundamental principle or main support

Examples

Education is often considered the pillar of a thriving democracy.
Trust is the pillar of any strong relationship.
Innovation forms the pillar of our company's success.

Synset Examples

Education is the pillar of a successful society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornerstone - an essential or foundational element
  • Synonym: foundation - the basis or groundwork of something

Etymology

From 'pillar', meaning a tall vertical structure providing support, used figuratively to indicate foundational importance.
