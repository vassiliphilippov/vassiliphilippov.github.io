conveyance of title

1. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪ.əns əv ˈtaɪ.təl / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ.əns əv ˈtaɪ.təl / (BrE)
the act of legally transferring ownership of property from one person to another
legal transfer of property ownership
legal

Examples

The conveyance of title was finalized when both parties signed the legal documents.
Proper documentation is required for the conveyance of title in real estate transactions.
Disputes often arise when the conveyance of title is not clearly established.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer of ownership - the act of passing legal rights to property from one person to another

Etymology

From 'conveyance' (Middle English, from Old French 'conveier', to carry) and 'title' (from Latin 'titulus', inscription, label).
