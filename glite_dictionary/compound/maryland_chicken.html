Maryland chicken

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪˌlænd ˈtʃɪkən / (AmE), / ˈmɛːrɪlənd ˈtʃɪkɪn / (BrE)
A dish of chicken that is first fried, then baked in the oven, and typically served with milk gravy.
fried and baked chicken with milk gravy
american

Examples

For Sunday dinner, she made Maryland chicken with creamy mashed potatoes and green beans.
The restaurant's specialty is Maryland chicken, served with a rich milk gravy.
He learned how to cook Maryland chicken from his grandmother's old recipe book.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Maryland, where the dish originated.
