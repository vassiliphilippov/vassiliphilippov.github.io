wild garlic

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / waɪld ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
a strong-smelling European plant with broad leaves and white flowers, often found in woods, and sometimes used in cooking
a pungent European woodland plant

Examples

In spring, the forest floor is covered with wild garlic in bloom.
She picked a handful of wild garlic leaves to add to the salad.
The strong scent of wild garlic filled the woodland air after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramsons - another name for wild garlic
  • Degree Lower: garlic - a closely related cultivated plant with a strong odor

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / waɪld ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
A plant of the onion family, especially Allium ursinum, with broad leaves and a strong garlicky smell, found in woods and damp places in Europe and Asia.
a pungent wild plant of the onion family

Examples

The woods were filled with the scent of wild garlic in the spring.
Chopped wild garlic adds a subtle flavor to salads and soups.
She went foraging for wild garlic during her hike in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramsons - alternative name for wild garlic, Allium ursinum
  • Synonym: garlic - a cultivated plant with a strong flavor, used in cooking
