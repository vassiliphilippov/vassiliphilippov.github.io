European quaking aspen

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈkweɪkɪŋ ˈæspən / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈkweɪkɪŋ ˈaspən / (BrE)
a species of aspen tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, with a broad, much-branched crown; Populus tremula
tree species native to Europe and Asia

Examples

The European quaking aspen is known for its fluttering, rounded leaves that shimmer in the breeze.
In northern forests, the European quaking aspen provides habitat for many bird species.
The wood from the European quaking aspen is valued for making matches and paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspen - a type of poplar tree with trembling leaves
  • Degree Lower: quaking aspen - a related North American species; Populus tremuloides
