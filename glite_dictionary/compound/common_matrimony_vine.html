common matrimony vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈmætrɪmoʊni vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmætrɪməni vaɪn / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub with spiny branches, violet-purple flowers, and orange-red berries, native from southeastern Europe to China.
deciduous shrub with spiny branches and colorful berries

Examples

The common matrimony vine is often grown for its nutritious berries and ornamental value.
Gardeners in temperate climates sometimes plant the common matrimony vine to attract pollinators.
Some herbal teas are made from the dried fruit of the common matrimony vine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goji - another common name for the same shrub (Lycium barbarum)
