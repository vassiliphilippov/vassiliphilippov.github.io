genus Lepisma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ləˈpɪzmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lɪˈpɪzmə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Lepismatidae, comprising silverfish and related insects.
genus of insects including silverfish

Examples

The genus Lepisma includes the common household silverfish.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Lepisma in South America.
Characteristics of the genus Lepisma are important for classifying primitive insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverfish - a small, wingless insect known for its silvery scales

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'genus' means kind and 'Lepisma' is the genus name introduced by Linnaeus, from Greek 'lepisma' meaning 'a peeling' or 'scale'.
