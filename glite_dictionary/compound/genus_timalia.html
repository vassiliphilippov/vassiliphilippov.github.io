genus Timalia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tɪˈmeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tɪˈmɑːliə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Timaliidae, comprising certain species of babblers.
type genus of Timaliidae (babblers)

Examples

The genus Timalia is notable for its gregarious species of babblers found in Asia.
Researchers identified the new bird as belonging to the genus Timalia based on its morphological traits.
Many ornithologists study the evolutionary relationships within the genus Timalia and its relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Timaliidae - family of passerine birds known as babblers

Etymology

From New Latin 'Timalia', used in taxonomy; 'genus' from Latin (kind, type).
