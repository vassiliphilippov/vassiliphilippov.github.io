Brazilian pepper tree

1. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪliən ˈpɛpər tri / (AmE), / brəˈzɪliən ˈpɛpə triː / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Brazil, known for its glossy leaves, clusters of small red berries, and aromatic resin; often grown as an ornamental or invasive plant in warm climates
tree or shrub native to Brazil

Examples

The Brazilian pepper tree has become an invasive species in many parts of Florida.
Gardeners appreciate the fragrant foliage of the Brazilian pepper tree.
Birds are often attracted to the bright red berries of the Brazilian pepper tree.
