stimulus generalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪmjələs ˌdʒɛnərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊləs ˌdʒɛnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychology) The process by which a response that has been learned to one stimulus is elicited by similar but different stimuli.
transfer of learned response to similar stimuli (psychology)
medical

Examples

The experiment demonstrated stimulus generalisation when the dog salivated to bells of different tones.
Children often show stimulus generalisation by reacting to similar situations in the same way.
Due to stimulus generalisation, a person who fears one type of dog may fear all dogs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discrimination learning - learning to respond differently to different stimuli

Etymology

From Latin 'stimulus' (goad, incentive) + generalisation (from French 'généralisation').
