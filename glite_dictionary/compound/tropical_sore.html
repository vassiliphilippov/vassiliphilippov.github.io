tropical sore

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːpɪkəl sɔr / (AmE), / ˈtrɒpɪkəl sɔː / (BrE)
A skin disease caused by leishmaniasis, marked by ulcerative skin lesions, typically occurring in tropical regions.
skin disease with ulcerative lesions
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a tropical sore after returning from South America.
Doctors treated the tropical sore with a course of antiparasitic medication.
A tropical sore can take months to heal if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - a disease caused by protozoan parasites, often leading to skin sores
