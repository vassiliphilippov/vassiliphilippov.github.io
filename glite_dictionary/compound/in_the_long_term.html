in the long term

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə lɔŋ tɜrm / (AmE), / ɪn ðə lɒŋ tɜːm / (BrE)
over an extended period of time; considering future results or consequences rather than immediate effects
over an extended period; considering future effects

Examples

Taking care of your health now will pay off in the long term.
Environmental policies may be unpopular at first, but they help in the long term.
Regular exercise is good for you in the long term.

Synset Examples

This strategy will be beneficial in the long term.
Investing in education pays off in the long term.
Saving money now helps us in the long term.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in the short term - considering only a short period or immediate effects
