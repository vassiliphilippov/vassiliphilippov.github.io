silver sagebrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
A low, bushy perennial plant of the western United States, recognized by its silvery leaves and significance as a browse and shelter plant.
a silvery-leaved shrub of western U.S.

Examples

The silver sagebrush thrives in the arid plains of Montana and Wyoming.
Cattle often seek shelter under the silver sagebrush during hot summer days.
The leaves of silver sagebrush are soft and have a distinct silvery color.
