witch alder

1. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ ˈældɚ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ ˈældə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of the genus Fothergilla, native to the southeastern United States, known for its brushlike white spring flowers and bright red or orange autumn leaves.
deciduous shrub with showy flowers, genus Fothergilla

Examples

The witch alder brightens up the garden each spring with clusters of fragrant white flowers.
In autumn, the leaves of the witch alder turn stunning shades of red and orange.
We planted a witch alder near the patio for its colorful seasonal display.
