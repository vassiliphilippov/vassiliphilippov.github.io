cat's foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæts ˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæts ˌfʊt / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial herb with leaves covered in whitish down and clusters of small white flowers, commonly found in temperate regions.
a low-growing perennial herb with white flowers

Examples

The meadows were dotted with cat's foot blooming among the grasses.
Gardeners often plant cat's foot to add a soft ground cover.
I recognized the silvery foliage of cat's foot during my nature walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pussytoes - another common name for the same plant

Etymology

Named for the soft, paw-like appearance of the flower clusters resembling a cat's foot.
