memento mori

1. NOUN

/ məˌmɛntoʊ ˈmɔri / (AmE), / məˌmɛn.təʊ ˈmɔː.ri / (BrE)
an object or symbol serving as a reminder of the inevitability of death
object symbolizing a reminder of death

Examples

The skull on his desk served as a memento mori, reminding him that life is fleeting.
Throughout art history, painters have used vanitas images as a memento mori for viewers.
She wore a ring inscribed with a Latin phrase as her personal memento mori.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reminder - something that makes you remember something else

Etymology

From Latin 'memento' (remember) + 'mori' (to die), meaning 'remember you must die'.
