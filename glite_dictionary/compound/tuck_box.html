tuck box

1. NOUN

/ tʌk bɑks / (AmE), / tʌk bɒks / (BrE)
A small box used to store snacks or food, especially by schoolchildren at boarding schools.
box for storing snacks, esp. at school
british

Examples

At boarding school, every student kept their favorite treats in a tuck box under the bed.
His mother packed homemade biscuits into his tuck box for him to enjoy during the term.
She opened her tuck box to share sweets with her classmates after lights out.

Etymology

From British English 'tuck' (food, especially sweets or snacks) and 'box.'
