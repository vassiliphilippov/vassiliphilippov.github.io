Engrish translation

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡrɪʃ tɹænzˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡrɪʃ tɹænsˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a humorous or incorrect use of English, often seen in translations, especially in East Asian contexts
humorous or incorrect English usage
informal offensive

Examples

We spent the evening laughing at the Engrish translation on the instruction manual.
The hotel's welcome brochure contained an amusing Engrish translation of its services.
She collected photos of signs with Engrish translations from her trip to Japan.

Synset Examples

The menu was filled with amusing Engrish translations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinglish - English as used by Chinese speakers, often marked by errors
  • Synonym: Japlish - Japanese-influenced English with humorous mistakes

Etymology

Formed from 'Engrish', an imitation of the Japanese pronunciation of 'English', and 'translation'. 'Engrish' originated as a term to describe flawed English in East Asian contexts.
