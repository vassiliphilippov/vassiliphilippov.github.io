swage block

1. NOUN

/ ˈsweɪdʒ ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈsweɪdʒ ˌblɒk / (BrE)
a heavy iron block with various holes and grooves used to shape, form, or support metal during forging or cold working
iron block for shaping metal

Examples

The blacksmith used a swage block to form the rounded ends of the metal bar.
She carefully positioned the hot iron over the swage block before hammering it into shape.
A well-equipped forge often includes at least one large swage block for various metalworking tasks.

Etymology

From 'swage', referring to a tool for shaping metal, and 'block'.
