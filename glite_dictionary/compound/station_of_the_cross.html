Station of the Cross

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪʃən əv ðə krɑs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪʃən əv ðə krɒs / (BrE)
any of the 14 images or representations depicting the stages of Christ's journey to Calvary, typically displayed in churches for devotional purposes
one of the 14 stages of Christ's journey to Calvary

Examples

Each Station of the Cross represents a significant event during Jesus's final journey.
On Good Friday, the congregation moved from one Station of the Cross to the next, praying at each one.
The artist was commissioned to paint a new Station of the Cross for the chapel.

Etymology

From Latin 'statio' (standing place), in reference to the pauses in Christ's journey; 'Cross' refers to the crucifixion.
