hand waving

1. NOUN

/ ˈhænd ˌweɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhænd ˌweɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making vague or insubstantial gestures or explanations, especially to distract or avoid giving a clear answer
vague or insubstantial gesture or explanation

Examples

The manager's hand waving during the meeting didn't address the team's concerns.
Critics dismissed the proposal as mere hand waving lacking detailed evidence.
Her response was all hand waving and failed to answer the key questions.

Synset Examples

The professor's hand waving didn't convince anyone.
Stop the hand waving and get to the point.
His explanations were just hand waving, lacking substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivocation - use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid commitment
  • Synonym: evasiveness - the act of avoiding giving a direct answer
