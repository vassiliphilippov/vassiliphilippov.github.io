wood tick

1. NOUN

/ wʊd tɪk / (AmE), / wʊd tɪk / (BrE)
a type of tick commonly found on animals and humans, capable of transmitting diseases such as Rocky Mountain spotted fever and tularemia
disease-carrying tick species

Examples

While hiking, Sophia found a wood tick attached to her ankle and removed it promptly.
The wood tick can spread serious illnesses if not detected early.
Park rangers advise wearing long sleeves to avoid wood ticks in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a small, bloodsucking arachnid
