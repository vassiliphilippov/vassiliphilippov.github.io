water rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ræt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ræt / (BrE)
any of several species of rat that live in or near water and are adapted to an amphibious lifestyle
amphibious rat species

Examples

The water rat is often seen swimming along the riverbank at dusk.
Scientists are studying the habitats of the water rat to better understand wetland ecosystems.
Unlike many rodents, the water rat is well adapted to an aquatic environment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌræt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌræt / (BrE)
a large semi-aquatic Eurasian vole (Arvicola amphibius) that typically lives near water
large Eurasian semi-aquatic vole

Examples

The water rat spends much of its life burrowing along riverbanks.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats for the endangered water rat.
We caught a glimpse of a water rat swimming across the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small rodent resembling a mouse but with a stouter body and shorter tail

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ræt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ræt / (BrE)
a person who enjoys spending time in or on the water, especially swimming or boating
enthusiast for water activities

Examples

Ever since she was a child, Emily has been a real water rat, always swimming and surfing at the beach.
As soon as the pool opened, the water rat in him couldn't stay away any longer.
His friends started calling him a water rat because he spends more time on his boat than at home.
