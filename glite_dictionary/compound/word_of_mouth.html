word of mouth

1. NOUN

/ wɝd əv maʊθ / (AmE), / wɜːd əv maʊθ / (BrE)
the passing of information, news, or recommendations from one person to another by spoken communication rather than written or formal means
information spread by spoken communication

Examples

Much of the restaurant's success can be attributed to word of mouth publicity.
The story spread quickly through word of mouth in the small community.
They relied on word of mouth to advertise their new business.

Synset Examples

the news of their affair was spread by word of mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - informal talk about the private affairs of others

2. NOUN

/ wɝd əv maʊθ / (AmE), / wɜːd əv maʊθ / (BrE)
the process by which information or recommendations are spread informally from person to person, typically through speech rather than written or formal communication
informal spoken communication or recommendation

Examples

The movie's popularity spread quickly by word of mouth before it even had an official trailer.
Most of our new clients find us through word of mouth rather than advertisements.
Relying on word of mouth can be a powerful marketing strategy for small businesses.

Synset Examples

The restaurant gained popularity through word of mouth.
They relied on word of mouth to promote their business.
Her success story spread by word of mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - informal talk or rumors about people
  • Synonym: rumor - unverified information spread among people
  • Antonym: advertising - the activity of promoting products or services formally
