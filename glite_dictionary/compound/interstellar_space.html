interstellar space

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈstɛlɚ speɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɛlə speɪs / (BrE)
the region of space that exists between the stars within a galaxy, containing gas, dust, and cosmic rays
space between stars in a galaxy

Examples

Scientists study interstellar space to learn about the origins of our galaxy.
Voyager 1 was the first spacecraft to enter interstellar space after leaving the solar system.
Dust and particles in interstellar space can affect the light that travels between stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interplanetary space - the region of space between planets within a solar system

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + stellar (of stars)
