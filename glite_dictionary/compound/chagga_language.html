Chagga language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑɡə ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɡə ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken by the Chaga people in northern Tanzania
Bantu language of northern Tanzania

Examples

The Chagga language is studied by linguists interested in Bantu languages.
Many older members of the community still speak Chagga language at home.
She wrote her dissertation on verb structure in the Chagga language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu language - a group of related African languages spoken in central and southern Africa

Etymology

From the name of the Chaga (or Chagga) people of northern Tanzania.
