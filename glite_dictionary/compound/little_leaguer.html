little leaguer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˌliːɡər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˌliːɡə / (BrE)
a child, typically between 8 and 12 years old, who plays in an organized Little League baseball team
child on a Little League baseball team
american

Examples

The little leaguer practiced his swing every afternoon after school.
As a proud little leaguer, she wore her team uniform everywhere she went.
Many famous athletes started out as a little leaguer in their hometown.

Etymology

From 'Little League', the name of the baseball organization for children, plus '-er' denoting a person associated with an activity.
