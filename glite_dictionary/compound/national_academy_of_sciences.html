National Academy of Sciences

1. PROPN

/ ˌnæʃənəl əˈkædəmi əv ˈsaɪənsɪz / (AmE), / ˌnæʃənəl əˈkædəmi ɒv ˈsaɪənsɪz / (BrE)
A prestigious American organization of distinguished scientists, established in 1863 to provide independent, objective advice to the nation on scientific matters.
prestigious American scientific society

Examples

She was elected to the National Academy of Sciences because of her groundbreaking research in genetics.
The National Academy of Sciences publishes influential reports on issues ranging from climate change to healthcare.
Founded during the Civil War, the National Academy of Sciences has shaped American scientific policy for over a century.
