fool's bargain

1. NOUN

/ fulz ˈbɑːrɡən / (AmE), / fuːlz ˈbɑːɡən / (BrE)
an agreement or deal that seems beneficial at first but ultimately proves disadvantageous or unwise
seemingly good but actually bad deal

Examples

Signing the contract without reading the fine print turned out to be a fool's bargain.
He thought he was getting a great price, but it was actually a fool's bargain.
Many investors fell for the scheme, only to realize it was a fool's bargain.

Synset Examples

His low offer was a fool's bargain in disguise.
I realized that accepting a fool's bargain cost me later.
Beware of a fool's bargain; it rarely pays off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad deal - an arrangement that is not beneficial or advantageous
  • Antonym: wise bargain - an agreement or deal that is truly advantageous
