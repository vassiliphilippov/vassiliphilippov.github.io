tomato ketchup

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ ˈkɛtʃəp / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ ˈkɛtʃəp / (BrE)
a thick, spicy sauce made from tomatoes, commonly used as a condiment
thick tomato-based condiment

Examples

He squeezed some tomato ketchup onto his fries before eating them.
Many children love tomato ketchup on their burgers and sandwiches.
There was a large bottle of tomato ketchup on the picnic table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ketchup - a thick, sweet-and-sour sauce often made from tomatoes
  • Antonym: brown sauce - a common British condiment similar to ketchup but not tomato-based

Etymology

'Ketchup' likely from Malay 'kecap' (a kind of sauce); 'tomato' from Spanish 'tomate'.
