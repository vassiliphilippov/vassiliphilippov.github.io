doorjamb

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌdʒæm / (AmE), / ˈdɔːˌdʒæm / (BrE)
the vertical portion of the frame onto which a door is secured
vertical side of a door frame

Examples

The hinges are attached to the doorjamb to support the door.
She painted the doorjamb white to match the rest of the frame.
The lock was damaged, so the doorjamb needed to be repaired as well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorframe - the whole frame into which a door fits
  • Synonym: lintel - a horizontal support across the top of a door or window

Etymology

Compound of 'door' and 'jamb', from Old French jambe (leg).
