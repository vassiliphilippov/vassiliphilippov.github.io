Aeolic dialect

1. NOUN

/ iˈɑlɪk ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / iˈɒlɪk ˈdaɪ.ə.lɛkt / (BrE)
the dialect of Ancient Greek that was spoken in Thessaly, Boeotia, and Aeolis.
ancient Greek dialect of Thessaly, Boeotia, Aeolis

Examples

The Aeolic dialect was used in many ancient Greek poems and inscriptions.
Linguists study the Aeolic dialect to better understand the evolution of Greek language.
Sappho wrote most of her poetry in the Aeolic dialect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doric dialect - another major dialect of Ancient Greek
  • Synonym: Ionic dialect - another major dialect of Ancient Greek
  • Synonym: Attic dialect - the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Athens

Etymology

From Greek Aiolikos, relating to Aeolis, a region of Asia Minor.
