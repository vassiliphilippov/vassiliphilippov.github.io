Saint Ulmo's light

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈʌlmoʊz laɪt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈʌlməʊz laɪt / (BrE)
An electrical discharge in the atmosphere, appearing as a luminous blue or violet glow, often seen on ship masts or airplane wings during storms, caused by ionization of the air.
atmospheric luminous electrical discharge

Examples

The sailors were both awed and comforted when they saw Saint Ulmo's light dancing on the mast.
During the storm, a faint blue glow known as Saint Ulmo's light appeared on the ship's rigging.
Pilots have sometimes reported seeing Saint Ulmo's light on the wings of aircraft during intense thunderstorms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Elmo's fire - luminous electrical discharge on pointed objects during storms

Etymology

Variant of 'St. Elmo's fire,' named after St. Erasmus (Elmo), patron saint of sailors.
