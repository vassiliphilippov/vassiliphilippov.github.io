Taimyr Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ taɪˈmɪr ˌpɛnɪnˈsjuːlə / (AmE), / taɪˈmɪə ˌpɛnɪnˈsjʊlə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in northern Siberia, Russia, extending into the Arctic Ocean and known as the northernmost part of the Eurasian continent.
peninsula in northern Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Taimyr Peninsula experiences some of the harshest winters in the northern hemisphere.
Scientists discovered ancient mammoth remains preserved in the permafrost of the Taimyr Peninsula.
Many migratory birds travel to the Taimyr Peninsula to nest during the summer months.
