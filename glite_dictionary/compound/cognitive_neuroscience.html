cognitive neuroscience

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑɡnɪtɪv ˌnʊroʊsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv ˌnjʊərəʊˌsaɪəns / (BrE)
The scientific study of the biological processes and neural mechanisms underlying mental activities and behaviour.
study of biology underlying mental processes
medical

Examples

She decided to pursue a PhD in cognitive neuroscience after being inspired by a lecture on brain function.
Research in cognitive neuroscience often involves both behavioural experiments and brain imaging techniques.
Advances in cognitive neuroscience have greatly enhanced our understanding of memory and learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuropsychology - the study of how brain function affects psychological processes and behaviour
  • Degree Lower: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behaviour

Etymology

Formed from 'cognitive' (from Latin 'cognitio', meaning knowledge) and 'neuroscience' (study of the nervous system).
