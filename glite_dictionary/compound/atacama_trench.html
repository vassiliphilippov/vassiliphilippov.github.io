Atacama Trench

1. PROPN

/ ˌætəˈkɑːmə trɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˌætəˈkɑːmə trɛntʃ / (BrE)
a long, deep depression in the Pacific Ocean floor off the coast of Chile, forming one of the world's major oceanic trenches
deep oceanic trench off Chile

Examples

The Atacama Trench is the deepest part of the southeastern Pacific Ocean.
Scientists have explored the biodiversity of the Atacama Trench using deep-sea submersibles.
Earthquakes are common near the Atacama Trench due to tectonic plate activity.

Etymology

Named after the Atacama region in northern Chile.
