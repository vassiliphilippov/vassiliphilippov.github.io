half-baked excuse

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæf ˈbeɪkt ɪkˈskjus / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ˈbeɪkt ɪkˈskjuːs / (BrE)
an excuse or justification that is poorly thought out or unconvincing
poorly thought-out excuse

Examples

His half-baked excuse for missing the meeting failed to convince anyone.
Tired of her son's half-baked excuses, she insisted on the real reason for his bad grades.
The manager was unimpressed with the employee's half-baked excuse for arriving late again.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his half-baked excuses for being late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretext - a reason given in justification of a course of action that is not the real reason
  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation given to justify a fault or offense

Etymology

From 'half-baked', meaning insufficiently thought out, used figuratively since the 19th century.
