alpha-adrenergic blocking agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfə əˌdrɛnərˈdʒɪk ˈblɑkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌælfə əˌdrɛnəˈdʒɪk ˈblɒkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
Any of a class of drugs that block alpha-adrenergic receptors, used to treat conditions such as benign prostatic hyperplasia by relaxing the muscles of the prostate and bladder.
drug that blocks alpha-adrenergic receptors
medical

Examples

After being prescribed an alpha-adrenergic blocking agent, his symptoms of an enlarged prostate improved considerably.
Doctors may recommend an alpha-adrenergic blocking agent to help patients with high blood pressure or urinary difficulties.
The pharmacist explained the potential side effects of taking an alpha-adrenergic blocking agent alongside other medications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beta-blocker - a drug that blocks beta-adrenergic receptors

Etymology

From 'alpha' (relating to α-adrenergic receptors) and 'adrenergic', from 'adrenaline' + '-ergic' (working on or produced by), combined with 'blocking agent'.
