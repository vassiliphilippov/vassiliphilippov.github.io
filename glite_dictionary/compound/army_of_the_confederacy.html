Army of the Confederacy

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑrmi əv ðə kənˈfɛdərəsi / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi əv ðə kənˈfɛdərəsi / (BrE)
the military forces of the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War (1861–1865)
Confederate army in the American Civil War

Examples

General Robert E. Lee commanded the Army of the Confederacy during key battles of the American Civil War.
The Army of the Confederacy was ultimately defeated in 1865, bringing an end to the conflict.
Many soldiers in the Army of the Confederacy came from Southern states such as Virginia and Georgia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union Army - the northern army during the American Civil War
