type B

1. NOUN

/ taɪp ˈbi / (AmE), / taɪp ˈbiː / (BrE)
the blood group whose red cells have the B antigen and lack the A antigen
a human blood group with B antigen
medical

Examples

The donor was found to have type B blood, which was compatible with the recipient.
Patients with type B require blood from donors with the same group or type O.
They conducted a study on the global distribution of type B blood.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: type A - the blood group with A antigen
  • Degree Higher: type AB - the blood group with both A and B antigens
  • Degree Lower: type O - the blood group with neither A nor B antigens
