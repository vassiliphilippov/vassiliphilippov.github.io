genus Illicium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈlɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈlɪsiəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees with aromatic leaves, including species such as star anise, found mainly in East and Southeast Asia and North America.
genus of evergreen aromatic trees (anise trees)

Examples

Many spices are derived from species within the genus Illicium, most notably star anise.
Botanists studied the distinctive aromatic leaves of trees in the genus Illicium.
Some members of the genus Illicium are cultivated both for their fragrance and their ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star anise - a spice from the fruit of Illicium verum

Etymology

From Latin 'illicium' meaning 'entice, allure', referring to the attractive nature of the aroma.
