spherical trigonometry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪr.ɪ.kəl ˌtrɪg.əˈnɑ.mə.tri / (AmE), / ˈsfɪə.rɪ.kəl ˌtrɪg.əˈnɒ.mə.tri / (BrE)
the branch of trigonometry that deals with spherical triangles and the relationships between their sides and angles
trigonometry of spherical triangles

Examples

The navigational calculations relied heavily on spherical trigonometry to determine the ship's precise position.
Students learned about spherical trigonometry as part of their advanced mathematics curriculum.
Astronomers often use spherical trigonometry to calculate the positions of stars on the celestial sphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigonometry - branch of mathematics that studies the relationships between side lengths and angles of triangles

Etymology

From Greek sphaira (sphere) and trigonon (triangle) + -metry (measuring).
