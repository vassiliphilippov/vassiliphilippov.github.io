Gaussian curve

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsiən kɜrv / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊsiən kɜːv / (BrE)
A symmetrical, bell-shaped curve representing the normal distribution in statistics and probability theory.
bell-shaped statistical curve (normal distribution)

Examples

The data, when plotted, closely followed a Gaussian curve centered around the mean value.
Many natural phenomena, such as heights and test scores, tend to approximate a Gaussian curve.
After analysis, the experiment's results produced a nearly perfect Gaussian curve on the graph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normal distribution - A probability distribution symmetrical about the mean, showing that data near the mean are more frequent in occurrence.

Etymology

Named after German mathematician Carl Friedrich Gauss.
