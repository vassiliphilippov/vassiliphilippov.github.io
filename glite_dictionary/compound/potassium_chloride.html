potassium chloride

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtæsiəm klaɪˈraɪd / (AmE), / pəˈtæsɪəm klɔːˈraɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt (KCl) used as a potassium supplement and in various industrial applications
a salt used as a potassium supplement
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed potassium chloride tablets to address her electrolyte imbalance.
Industrial-grade potassium chloride is commonly used in the production of fertilizers.
Too much potassium chloride can be dangerous if not taken as directed by a physician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt - a compound formed from acid and base reactions

Etymology

Formed from 'potassium' (element name) and 'chloride' (from Greek 'chloros', green/yellow, for chlorine).
