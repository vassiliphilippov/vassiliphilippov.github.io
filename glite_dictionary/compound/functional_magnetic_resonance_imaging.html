functional magnetic resonance imaging

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənl ˌmæɡˈnɛtɪk rɪˈzɔnəns ˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənl ˌmaɡˈnetɪk rɪˈzɒnəns ˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
a technique for measuring and mapping brain activity by detecting changes in blood flow using magnetic resonance imaging
brain imaging technique measuring brain activity
medical

Examples

The researchers used functional magnetic resonance imaging to observe changes in brain activity during the experiment.
Advances in functional magnetic resonance imaging have improved our understanding of neurological disorders.
She volunteered for a study that involved spending time inside a functional magnetic resonance imaging scanner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic resonance imaging - technique for imaging internal structures of the body using magnetic fields and radio waves

Etymology

Formed from 'functional' + 'magnetic resonance imaging,' itself from magnetic and resonance (physical principles) and imaging (visualization of internal body structures).
