Lappish language

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlapɪʃ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the languages spoken by the Sami people (also called Lapps), generally classified as part of the Uralic language family
language of the Sami (Lapp) people

Examples

Few people outside Scandinavia have studied the Lappish language in depth.
The Lappish language has many dialects, some of which are mutually unintelligible.
She wrote her dissertation on the grammar of a Lappish language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sami language - the languages spoken by the Sami people, also known as Saami languages

Etymology

From 'Lapp', an older term for the Sami people, plus '-ish' and 'language'.
