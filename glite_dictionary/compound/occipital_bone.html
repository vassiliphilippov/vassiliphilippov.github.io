occipital bone

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɪpɪtəl boʊn / (AmE), / əkˈsɪpɪtəl bəʊn / (BrE)
the curved bone at the lower back part of the skull, which encloses the foramen magnum and forms the back of the head
bone at the back of the skull
medical

Examples

The occipital bone protects the back part of the brain and supports the skull.
A fracture in the occipital bone can result from a severe blow to the back of the head.
During the anatomy lesson, the professor pointed to the occipital bone on the human skull model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parietal bone - either of two bones forming the sides and roof of the skull
  • Degree Lower: frontal bone - bone that forms the forehead and upper part of the eye sockets
  • Synonym: temporal bone - a paired bone at the sides and base of the skull

Etymology

From Latin 'occiput' (back of the head) + 'bone'.
