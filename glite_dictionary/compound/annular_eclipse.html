annular eclipse

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jʊ.lɚ ɪˈklɪps / (AmE), / ˈæn.jʊ.lə ɪˈklɪps / (BrE)
An eclipse of the sun in which the moon covers all but a bright ring or annulus of the sun's disk.
solar eclipse leaving a ring of sunlight

Examples

During an annular eclipse, a brilliant ring of sunlight encircles the dark moon.
Millions traveled to see the rare annular eclipse visible from South America this year.
Unlike a total solar eclipse, an annular eclipse leaves a glowing 'ring of fire' in the sky.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: total eclipse - an eclipse in which the sun is completely covered
  • Degree Lower: partial eclipse - an eclipse in which only part of the sun is covered

Etymology

From Latin 'annulus' (ring) and 'eclipse'.
