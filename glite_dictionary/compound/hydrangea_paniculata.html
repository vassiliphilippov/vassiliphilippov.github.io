Hydrangea paniculata

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪndʒə ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / haɪˈdrændʒɪə ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous shrub or small tree, known for its pyramidal clusters of white to pink flowers.
species of hydrangea with pyramidal flower clusters

Examples

Hydrangea paniculata thrives in full sun and is often used as a specimen plant in gardens.
Gardeners appreciate Hydrangea paniculata for its large, showy flower heads that bloom in late summer.
Many new cultivars of Hydrangea paniculata offer improved colors and extended flowering periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrangea - genus of flowering plants with large flower heads

Etymology

From genus name Hydrangea (from Greek 'hydor' water + 'angeion' vessel) and species epithet paniculata (from Latin 'panicula' meaning tuft or panicle).
