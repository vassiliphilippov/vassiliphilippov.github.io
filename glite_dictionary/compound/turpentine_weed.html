turpentine weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːpənˌtaɪn wid / (AmE), / ˈtɜːpəntaɪn wiːd / (BrE)
A low-growing, sticky subshrub native to the southwestern United States, with narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small yellow flower heads.
a sticky subshrub of the southwestern U.S.

Examples

We found turpentine weed growing in abundance along the dusty roadside in Arizona.
The sticky leaves of turpentine weed are easily identified by their strong, resinous smell.
Local wildlife often avoids turpentine weed because of its pungent odor and taste.
