abdominal actinomycosis

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdɑmɪnəl ˌæktɪnoʊmaɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / æbˈdɒmɪnəl ˌæktɪnəʊmaɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A severe form of actinomycosis infection that occurs in the abdomen, often resulting in abscesses and chronic inflammation.
severe abdominal bacterial infection
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with abdominal actinomycosis after months of unexplained abdominal pain.
Surgical intervention is often needed to treat complicated cases of abdominal actinomycosis.
Early recognition of abdominal actinomycosis can prevent severe complications and unnecessary surgeries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actinomycosis - a chronic bacterial infection characterized by abscess formation

Etymology

From 'abdominal' (relating to the abdomen) and 'actinomycosis' (from Greek aktis, aktinos 'ray' + mykes 'fungus' + -osis).
