pneumococcal pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːməˌkɑkəl nuːˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˌkɒkəl njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a type of pneumonia caused by infection with bacteria of the genus Streptococcus pneumoniae (pneumococcus)
pneumonia caused by pneumococcus bacteria
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with pneumococcal pneumonia after he developed a high fever and difficulty breathing.
Vaccination is an effective way to prevent pneumococcal pneumonia in young children and the elderly.
She recovered from pneumococcal pneumonia after a course of antibiotics and several days of rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pneumonia - an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs

Etymology

From 'pneumococcal', relating to the bacterium pneumococcus, and 'pneumonia', from Greek 'pneumōn' (lung).
