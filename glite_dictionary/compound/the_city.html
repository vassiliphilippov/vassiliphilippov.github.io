the City

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ðə ˈsɪti / (BrE)
the part of London within the ancient boundaries, known as its commercial and financial center
financial district of London
british

Examples

International banks maintain offices in the City near the Bank of England.
Many people commute daily to work in the City from the suburbs.
Property prices in the City are among the highest in the United Kingdom.

2. NOUN

/ ðə ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ðə ˈsɪti / (BrE)
the financial and commercial institutions of the City of London, especially with reference to the securities industry of Great Britain
UK financial district; securities industry
british

Examples

Many international banks are headquartered in the City.
After the crisis, confidence in the City was seriously shaken.
She landed a job in the City straight out of university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wall Street - the securities industry of the United States

Etymology

Refers to the historic City of London, the traditional core of Britain's financial industry.
