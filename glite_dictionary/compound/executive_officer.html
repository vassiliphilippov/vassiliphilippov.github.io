executive officer

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an officer who is second in command, especially in a military or organizational hierarchy
officer second in command

Examples

As the executive officer, she managed daily operations in the absence of the captain.
The executive officer ensures that all orders from the commanding officer are carried out.
He was promoted from executive officer to commanding officer after two years of service.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: commanding officer - the officer in command, especially the senior officer in a unit
