renegade state

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnɪˌɡeɪd steɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛnɪɡeɪd steɪt / (BrE)
A state that deliberately disregards the accepted laws or norms of international relations.
state acting against international norms

Examples

The international community imposed sanctions on the renegade state after repeated violations of treaties.
Some analysts warn that a renegade state could destabilize the entire region.
Diplomats are struggling to negotiate with the renegade state to prevent further conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rogue state - a nation that acts in defiance of international norms or treaties

Etymology

Renegade from Latin 'renegatus' (to deny or renounce), adopted into English to mean a traitor or rebellious individual; here combined with 'state'.
