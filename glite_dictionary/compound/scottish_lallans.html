Scottish Lallans

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑtɪʃ ˈlælənz / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈlalənz / (BrE)
a dialect of English traditionally spoken in the Lowlands of Scotland, characterized by distinct vocabulary, pronunciation, and grammar
dialect of English in Lowland Scotland
british

Examples

Robert Burns wrote many poems in Scottish Lallans, capturing the essence of Lowland Scots life.
Some words in Scottish Lallans can be difficult for English speakers from other regions to understand.
The play was performed in Scottish Lallans to preserve its original cultural flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scots - the Germanic language variety spoken in Scotland, closely related to Lallans

Etymology

From Scots 'lallans', meaning 'lowlands', referring to the geographical area where the dialect is spoken.
