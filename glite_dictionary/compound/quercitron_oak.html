quercitron oak

1. NOUN

/ kwɜːrˈsɪtrən oʊk / (AmE), / kwɜːˈsɪtrən əʊk / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous tree native to eastern North America, with dark outer bark and yellow inner bark used in tanning and dye-making.
a North American oak tree used in tanning and dye-making

Examples

The bark of the quercitron oak was once widely exported for use in tanning leather.
Botanists identified the ancient tree as a quercitron oak due to its yellow inner bark.
Traditional dyes made from quercitron oak are still prized by artisans today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black oak - a closely related species and alternative name for 'quercitron oak'

Etymology

From Latin 'Quercus' (oak) and 'citron', referring to the yellow dye extracted from its bark.
