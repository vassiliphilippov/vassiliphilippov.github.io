ataxic aphasia

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæksɪk əˈfeɪziə / (AmE), / əˈtaksɪk əˈfeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A severe form of aphasia in which communication through speech or writing is greatly impaired.
severe impairment of speech and writing (aphasia)
medical

Examples

After his stroke, the patient was diagnosed with ataxic aphasia and struggled to communicate.
The neurologist explained that ataxic aphasia affects both spoken and written language.
Support groups can help individuals living with ataxic aphasia regain confidence in daily interactions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: aphasia - Loss or impairment of the power to use or comprehend words, usually as a result of brain damage

Etymology

From Greek ataxia (lack of order) and aphasia (speechlessness) via medical Latin.
