foul line

1. NOUN

/ faʊl laɪn / (AmE), / faʊl laɪn / (BrE)
a line across a bowling alley that a player must not cross when delivering the ball
line on a bowling alley; not to be crossed

Examples

If your foot crosses the foul line, your throw is not counted.
The foul line is clearly marked to ensure fair play in bowling tournaments.
She carefully approached the foul line before releasing the ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowling alley - a long, narrow lane where bowling is played

2. NOUN

/ faʊl laɪn / (AmE), / faʊl laɪn / (BrE)
In basketball, a line on the court from which players take free throws after certain fouls are committed.
basketball line for free throws

Examples

He stepped up to the foul line and calmly sank both free throws.
The referee signaled for the player to shoot from the foul line.
Practicing at the foul line helped her improve her free throw percentage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free throw line - the line from which free throws are taken in basketball
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ faʊl laɪn / (AmE), / faʊl laɪn / (BrE)
either of the lines extending past first and third base that mark the boundaries of the playing field in baseball
boundary line in baseball field

Examples

The ball landed just inside the foul line, so it was counted as fair.
If a batter hits the ball beyond the foul line, it's considered a foul ball.
White chalk marks are used to draw the foul lines before each baseball game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fair territory - the area inside the foul lines on a baseball field
  • Synonym: foul ball - a baseball hit outside the foul lines
