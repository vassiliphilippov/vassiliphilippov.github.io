bad taste

1. NOUN

/ bæd teɪst / (AmE), / bæd teɪst / (BrE)
a lingering feeling of displeasure or disgust caused by something unpleasant or offensive
lingering feeling of displeasure or disgust

Examples

His arrogant apology left a bad taste in my mouth.
Her insensitive remarks gave everyone a bad taste about the whole meeting.
The movie's ending left viewers with a bad taste that lingered for days.

Synset Examples

His rude comments left a bad taste in everyone's mouth.
His mordicative remarks always left a bad taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - a strong feeling of dislike or disapproval
  • Synonym: offense - a feeling of being upset or displeased by something rude or insulting
