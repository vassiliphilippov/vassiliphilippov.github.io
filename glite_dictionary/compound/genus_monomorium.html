genus Monomorium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs məˌnɔːˈmɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məˌnɒˈmɔːriəm / (BrE)
A genus of ants in the family Formicidae, which includes species such as the pharaoh ant.
a genus of ants including the pharaoh ant

Examples

The genus Monomorium includes ants that are both widespread and highly adaptable.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Monomorium in South America.
Households may encounter pests from the genus Monomorium, especially the notorious pharaoh ant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharaoh ant - a small, yellow-brown ant species commonly found indoors

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Monomorium' (genus name) from Greek 'monos' (single) + 'morion' (a little part).
