behemoth of a project

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhiːməθ əv ə ˈprɑːdʒɛkt / (AmE), / bɪˈhiːməθ əv ə ˈprɒdʒɛkt / (BrE)
an extremely large and difficult project requiring significant effort or resources
very large and difficult project

Examples

Taking on the behemoth of a project nearly overwhelmed the small team of engineers.
Managing that behemoth of a project required input from multiple departments over several years.
The new bridge was a behemoth of a project, taking a decade to construct.

Synset Examples

They struggled to manage the behemoth of a project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colossal task - an extremely large or difficult undertaking
  • Synonym: Herculean effort - requiring great strength or effort

Etymology

From 'behemoth', a Hebrew word used in the Bible to describe a huge beast, now metaphorically used for anything of great size or power.
