what's with

1. X

/ wʌts wɪð / (AmE), / wɒts wɪð / (BrE)
used to ask for the reason or explanation for something, often expressing confusion, curiosity, or mild annoyance.
inquiring about the reason for something
informal

Examples

What's with all the excitement in the office today?
She wondered, 'what's with the sudden change in his attitude?'
What's with these messages popping up on my phone every minute?

Synset Examples

Can you tell me what's up with the meeting?
I asked him, what's with the new hairstyle?
What's with all the noise outside?
