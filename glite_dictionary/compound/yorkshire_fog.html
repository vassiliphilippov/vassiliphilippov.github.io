Yorkshire fog

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɔrkʃɪr fɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːkʃə fɒɡ / (BrE)
a tall European perennial grass (Holcus lanatus) with velvety stems, naturalized in North America and used for forage
a species of tall grass used for forage

Examples

The field was covered with Yorkshire fog, creating a soft, silvery carpet in the early morning light.
Farmers often plant Yorkshire fog because it provides reliable forage for livestock.
We learned to identify Yorkshire fog by its velvety stems and distinctive flowering heads.
