salicylate poisoning

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪsɪˌleɪt ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪˌleɪt ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
poisoning resulting from consuming an excessive amount of salicylates, typically aspirin, leading to toxic effects on the body
poisoning from excessive salicylates (e.g., aspirin)
medical

Examples

The patient was admitted to the emergency room with signs of salicylate poisoning after taking too much aspirin.
Early diagnosis of salicylate poisoning is crucial to prevent serious complications.
Children are particularly at risk of salicylate poisoning due to accidental ingestion of medications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspirin - a common drug containing acetylsalicylic acid

Etymology

salicylate from Latin 'salix' (willow), as willow bark was historically a source of salicylates. 'Poisoning' from Middle English 'poison'.
