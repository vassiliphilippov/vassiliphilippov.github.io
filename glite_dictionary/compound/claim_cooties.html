to claim cooties

1. VERB

/ kleɪm ˈkuːtiz / (AmE), / kleɪm ˈkuːtiz / (BrE)
to say, usually jokingly, that one has an imaginary contagious illness (cooties), especially in children's games or banter
pretend to have imaginary germs (children's usage)

Examples

During recess, several children began to claim cooties after touching the playground railings.
She pretended to claim cooties to make her friends laugh at lunch.
When one boy sneezed, the others jokingly claimed cooties to avoid sitting near him.

Synset Examples

The kids giggled, claiming cooties were real.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have cooties - to be considered as having imaginary germs (children's slang)

Etymology

Cooties: American English slang (early 20th century), possibly from Malay 'kutu' (louse); 'claim' from Old French 'clamer', from Latin 'clamare' (to cry out).
