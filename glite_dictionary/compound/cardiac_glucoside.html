cardiac glucoside

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdiæk ˈɡluːkoʊsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiæk ˈɡluːkəsaɪd / (BrE)
a compound obtained from certain plants, used to stimulate the heart in cases of heart failure
heart-stimulating plant compound
medical

Examples

The medication prescribed for his heart failure contained a cardiac glucoside derived from foxglove.
Doctors closely monitor the dosage of cardiac glucosides due to their potent effects on the heart.
Some plants produce cardiac glucoside compounds that are valuable in the treatment of certain cardiac conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitalis - plant-derived drug containing cardiac glucosides for heart conditions
  • Synonym: cardiotonic - substance that increases the strength of the heart's contractions

Etymology

From 'cardiac', relating to the heart (from Greek kardiakos), and 'glucoside', a class of glycosides derived from glucose (from Greek glykys, sweet).
