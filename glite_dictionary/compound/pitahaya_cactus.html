pitahaya cactus

1. NOUN

/ pɪtəˈhaɪ.ə ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / pɪtəˈhaɪ.ə ˈkæktəs / (BrE)
a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its edible, juicy fruit
cactus with edible fruit from the Southwest US and Mexico

Examples

The pitahaya cactus thrives in arid deserts, producing beautiful flowers and sweet fruit.
Farmers in northern Mexico cultivate the pitahaya cactus for both its fruit and ornamental value.
You can identify a pitahaya cactus by its thick stems and bright, spiky fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon fruit - common name for the edible fruit of several pitahaya cacti

Etymology

From Spanish 'pitaya', of Taíno origin, referring to the fruit; 'cactus' from Greek 'kaktos'.
