hidden clue

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪdən kluː / (AmE), / ˈhɪdən kluː / (BrE)
a clue that is not immediately obvious or is concealed, often requiring careful observation or investigation to discover
a concealed or overlooked clue

Examples

The detective searched the crime scene carefully for any hidden clue that might explain the robbery.
Sometimes, a hidden clue can change the entire direction of an investigation.
The author cleverly planted a hidden clue in the first chapter that readers often miss.

Synset Examples

His detective work uncovered hidden clues to the mystery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: clue - a piece of evidence or information to help solve a problem or mystery
  • Degree Lower: obvious clue - a clue that is easy to notice or recognize
