nonesuch clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌnˌsʌtʃ ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / ˈnʌnsʌtʃ ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A prostrate European herb (Trifolium dubium) with small yellow flowers and curved black seed pods, commonly found in grasslands and naturalized in North America.
a small European yellow-flowered clover

Examples

The field was dotted with nonesuch clover in early spring.
Gardeners often use nonesuch clover as ground cover because of its resilience.
She studied the curved black pods of the nonesuch clover during her botany class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop clover - another common name for related yellow-flowered Trifolium species
