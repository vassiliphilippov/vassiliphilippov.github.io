egg fu yung

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡ fu ˈjʌŋ / (AmE), / ɛɡ fu ˈjʌŋ / (BrE)
A Chinese-style omelet made with beaten eggs, onions, celery, and usually chopped meat or fish, often served with a sauce.
Chinese omelet with vegetables and meat or fish

Examples

She ordered egg fu yung with shrimp at her favorite Chinese restaurant.
The chef prepared a delicious egg fu yung packed with sliced mushrooms and chicken.
My grandmother taught me how to make traditional egg fu yung for family dinners.

Etymology

From Cantonese 芙蓉蛋 (*fu4 jung4 daan6*), meaning 'hibiscus egg'; adapted into English from Chinese restaurant cuisine.
