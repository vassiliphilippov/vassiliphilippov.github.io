family Casuarinaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli kəˌwɛrɪˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ˌkæsjʊˌærɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, consisting of one genus (Casuarina), known for trees and shrubs commonly called she-oaks or beefwoods.
plant family with genus Casuarina

Examples

The trees belonging to the family Casuarinaceae are often planted to stabilize sandy soils.
Species in the family Casuarinaceae are commonly found in Australia and Southeast Asia.
The unique appearance of the family Casuarinaceae makes them easily recognizable among other plant families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Casuarina - the only genus in the family Casuarinaceae; trees and shrubs called she-oaks

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Casuarinaceae, after the type genus Casuarina, named for its resemblance to the cassowary (Casuarius).
