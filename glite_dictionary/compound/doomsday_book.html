Doomsday Book

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːmz.deɪ bʊk / (AmE), / ˈduːmz.deɪ bʊk / (BrE)
an official record of the great survey of England completed in 1086, detailing land ownership and resources, commissioned by William the Conqueror
English land survey and census of 1086
british

Examples

Historians still refer to the Doomsday Book when studying medieval England's economy.
The Doomsday Book lists hundreds of villages and their landholders.
William the Conqueror's Doomsday Book was a crucial administrative achievement.

Etymology

From Middle English 'Domesday', meaning 'day of judgment', reflecting the book's definitive authority.
