penny dreadful

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ˈdrɛdfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˈdrɛdfəl / (BrE)
a cheaply produced and sensational storybook, especially one published in 19th-century Britain, featuring lurid and melodramatic tales intended to entertain the masses
cheap sensational storybook (19th-century Britain)
british

Examples

Victorian children were often captivated by the thrilling tales in the latest penny dreadful.
Many critics of the time considered the penny dreadful a corrupting influence on young readers.
He built a collection of rare penny dreadfuls from the 1800s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dime novel - cheap popular novel in 19th-century America, often sensational in style

Etymology

From the low price (a penny) and the lurid, shocking content considered 'dreadful.'
