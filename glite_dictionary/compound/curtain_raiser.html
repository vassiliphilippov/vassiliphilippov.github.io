curtain raiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrtən ˌreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtən ˌreɪzə / (BrE)
an event or activity that precedes and introduces a main event or performance; a preliminary to the main business
preliminary event before the main one

Examples

The charity match served as a curtain raiser to the football season.
The opening speech was just a curtain raiser before the main conference began.
The exhibit acted as a curtain raiser for the much-anticipated art show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelude - an action or event serving as an introduction to something more important
  • Antonym: finale - the last part of a performance or event

Etymology

Originally referred to a short play performed before the main theatrical production, from 'curtain' + 'raiser'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.tən ˌreɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tən ˌreɪ.zə / (BrE)
A short play or performance presented before the main play or event.
short play before the main performance

Examples

The festival began with a delightful curtain raiser that set the mood for the evening.
Before the main show, a brief curtain raiser entertained the audience.
The comedy troupe performed a special curtain raiser to warm up the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening act - performance beginning an event, usually before the main act

Etymology

From 'curtain' (referring to the theater curtain) + 'raiser' (something that raises or opens), as this item is performed before the main curtain rises.
