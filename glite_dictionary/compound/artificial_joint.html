artificial joint

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A metal or plastic part surgically implanted to replace a natural joint, such as a hip or knee.
surgically implanted replacement joint
medical

Examples

After his hip replacement surgery, John had to learn how to walk with his new artificial joint.
Doctors recommend physical therapy to help patients recover mobility after receiving an artificial joint.
The development of the artificial joint has greatly improved the quality of life for many people with arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosthesis - an artificial device that replaces a missing body part
