neutral zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtrəl zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəl zəʊn / (BrE)
an area or region in which no conflict, fighting, or competition occurs, especially between opposing sides.
area with no conflict or competition

Examples

The referee signaled that the puck had entered the neutral zone in the hockey match.
During the negotiations, the leaders met in a neutral zone to ensure that no side had an advantage.
The children established a neutral zone on the playground where no team could tag each other.

Synset Examples

The beige flag indicated a neutral zone during the game.
