dittany of Crete

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪtəni əv ˈkriːt / (AmE), / ˈdɪtəni əv ˈkriːt / (BrE)
a dwarf aromatic shrub (Origanum dictamnus) native to Crete, known for its soft, hairy leaves and pink-purple flowers, often used in herbal remedies
aromatic shrub from Crete

Examples

The dittany of Crete is famous for its use in ancient herbal medicine.
Gardeners admire the soft leaves of the dittany of Crete for their pleasant aroma.
Some teas are flavored with extracts from the dittany of Crete.

Etymology

From Greek 'diktamnon' via Latin 'dictamnus', referring to a plant growing on Mount Dicte in Crete.
