adductor muscle

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌktər ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / əˈdʌktə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
A muscle that draws a body part toward the median line of the body or of a limb.
muscle that draws a part toward the body's midline
medical

Examples

The adductor muscle in your thigh helps pull your legs together.
Injury to the adductor muscle can make walking difficult.
The doctor explained the role of the adductor muscle during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abductor muscle - a muscle that moves a body part away from the median line

Etymology

From Latin adducere (to bring toward).
