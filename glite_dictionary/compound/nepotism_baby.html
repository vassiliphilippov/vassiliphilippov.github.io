nepotism baby

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛpəˌtɪzəm ˈbeɪbi / (AmE), / ˈnɛpətɪzəm ˈbeɪbi / (BrE)
a person who receives opportunities or advantages—especially in a career—because of family connections rather than individual merit
person benefiting from family connections
informal

Examples

Many celebrities in Hollywood are accused of being nepotism babies.
She resented being labeled a nepotism baby, even though her parents were famous actors.
He acknowledged that his success as a nepotism baby opened many doors for him.

Synset Examples

She’s a nepotism baby, thanks to her father’s connections.
Many consider him a nepotism baby in the industry.
As a nepotism baby, he landed the job easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nepotism - favoritism shown to relatives, especially by giving them jobs

Etymology

From 'nepotism' (favoritism toward relatives) and 'baby' (slang for beneficiary or product of), popularized in late 2010s internet culture.
