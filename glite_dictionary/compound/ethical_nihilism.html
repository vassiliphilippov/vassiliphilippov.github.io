ethical nihilism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪkəl ˈnaɪəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪkəl ˈnaɪɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
The philosophical belief that nothing is inherently moral or immoral.
belief that nothing is inherently moral or immoral

Examples

Some philosophers argue that ethical nihilism undermines the basis for moral judgment.
His writings are often criticized for promoting ethical nihilism in society.
Contemporary debates on morality frequently touch on the implications of ethical nihilism.

Synset Examples

The moral nihilism of celebrity culture is played out on reality television shows, most of which encourage a dark voyeurism into other people's humiliation, pain, weakness, and betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moral nihilism - the belief that no moral truths exist

Etymology

From 'ethical,' relating to morals, and 'nihilism,' from Latin 'nihil' (nothing).
