genus Leontopodium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs liˌɒntəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs liˌɒntəˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A small genus of plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), including the edelweiss and related alpine species.
genus of alpine plants, including edelweiss

Examples

The genus Leontopodium is known for its woolly white flowers found in mountain regions.
Botanists classify edelweiss under the genus Leontopodium, which is native to Europe and Asia.
Many alpine plants in the genus Leontopodium are adapted to harsh, cold environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edelweiss - a well-known alpine flower with white star-shaped petals

Etymology

From Greek leon (lion) + podion (little foot), referring to the shape of the plant's inflorescence.
