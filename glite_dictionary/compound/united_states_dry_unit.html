United States dry unit

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.naɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts ˈdraɪ ˌjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts ˈdraɪ ˌjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement for the volume of dry substances as defined in the United States Customary System, such as the dry pint or dry quart.
US unit for measuring dry volume

Examples

A common United States dry unit is the dry gallon, used for measuring agricultural products.
The dry quart is a United States dry unit equal to approximately 1.101 liters.
Farmers often use the United States dry unit bushel to measure grain harvests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: US liquid unit - a unit for measuring liquid volume in the US Customary System
