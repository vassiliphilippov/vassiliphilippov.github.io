Mormon family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːrmən ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmɔːmən ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family whose members belong to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
family belonging to the LDS Church

Examples

She grew up in a Mormon family in Utah.
Many traditions in a Mormon family revolve around church activities.
Being part of a Mormon family often means participating in weekly religious events.

Synset Examples

She was raised in a Mormon family.

Etymology

Mormon: From the Book of Mormon, a sacred text of the LDS Church; family: from Latin familia (household).
