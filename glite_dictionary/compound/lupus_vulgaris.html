lupus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːpəs vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈluːpəs vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A chronic form of tuberculosis that affects the skin, usually appearing first on the face and healing slowly with deep scars.
chronic skin tuberculosis; causes deep scars
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with lupus vulgaris after developing persistent skin ulcers on her cheek.
Histological tests confirmed the presence of lupus vulgaris in the affected tissue.
Early treatment is important to prevent the deep scarring associated with lupus vulgaris.

Etymology

From Latin 'lupus' (wolf) and 'vulgaris' (common), referring to the ulcerative, destructive nature of the lesions.
