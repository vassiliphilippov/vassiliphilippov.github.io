respiratory system

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛspəˌrɛˌtɔri ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrətəri ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The system of organs and structures in the body responsible for taking in oxygen and expelling carbon dioxide, primarily through breathing.
body system for breathing

Examples

The respiratory system is essential for supplying oxygen to the body's cells.
Smoking can severely damage your respiratory system over time.
Doctors examined the patient's respiratory system after he complained of shortness of breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circulatory system - the system responsible for circulating blood and lymph through the body
  • Synonym: digestive system - the system by which ingested food is broken down

Etymology

From Latin 'respirare' (to breathe) + English 'system'.
