Payne's grey

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪnz ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnz ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
a dark bluish-grey color, originally referring to a pigment of this hue used in painting and art
a dark bluish-grey pigment or color

Examples

The artist used Payne's grey to create subtle shadows in the watercolor painting.
Many painters prefer Payne's grey over black because it produces softer, more natural tones.
She mixed a little Payne's grey with white to achieve a misty blue atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue-gray - a color intermediate between blue and gray
  • Synonym: slate - a grayish-blue color, similar in shade to slate rock

Etymology

Named after William Payne, an 18th-century English watercolorist, who introduced the pigment.
