sardine oil

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdin ɔɪl / (AmE), / sɑːˈdiːn ɔɪl / (BrE)
oil extracted from sardines, used mainly as a lubricant or in the production of soap
oil from sardines, used in industry or soap

Examples

The factory produces sardine oil for use in making high-quality soap.
Much of the industrial lubricant was once made from sardine oil extracted from processed fish.
Exports of sardine oil rose significantly during the early twentieth century due to its demand in manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish oil - oil derived from the bodies of oily fish, used as a dietary supplement and in industry
