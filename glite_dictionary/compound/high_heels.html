high heels

1. NOUN

/ haɪ hilz / (AmE), / haɪ hiːlz / (BrE)
A pair of women's or dress shoes with tall, raised heels that lift the heel of the foot higher than the toes.
shoes with tall, raised heels

Examples

She wore her favorite high heels to the party despite knowing they would hurt her feet.
Many people consider high heels to be fashionable but sometimes uncomfortable.
The actress walked gracefully down the red carpet in sparkling high heels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilettos - shoes with very high, thin heels
  • Antonym: flats - shoes with no heel or a very low heel

2. NOUN

/ haɪ hilz / (AmE), / haɪ hiːlz / (BrE)
women's shoes with tall or raised heels that elevate the wearer's heels significantly above the toes
shoes with tall or raised heels

Examples

She wore high heels to the party despite knowing she would have to dance all night.
Many people find it challenging to walk quickly in high heels.
Some offices still require women to wear high heels as part of the dress code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stiletto - a type of high-heeled shoe with a very thin, high heel
  • Antonym: flat shoes - shoes with little or no heel
