lacewing fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪsˌwɪŋ flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈleɪswɪŋ flaɪ / (BrE)
A delicate insect belonging to either of two families (Chrysopidae and Hemerobiidae), characterized by gauzy wings; its larvae feed on insect pests such as aphids.
insect with gauzy wings; larvae eat pests

Examples

A lacewing fly can often be found in gardens, where its larvae help control aphid populations.
The delicate green wings of the lacewing fly shimmer in the sunlight.
Many farmers encourage the presence of lacewing flies as a natural form of pest control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacewing - an insect with delicate, lace-like wings, especially of the family Chrysopidae

Etymology

From 'lacewing', describing the lacy appearance of the wings, and 'fly', referring to its order of insects.
