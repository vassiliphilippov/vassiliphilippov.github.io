interplanetary dust

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈplænəˌtɛri dʌst / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈplænɪtəri dʌst / (BrE)
microscopic particles found in space between the planets, forming part of the interplanetary medium
microscopic particles in space between planets

Examples

The solar wind affects the movement of interplanetary dust throughout the solar system.
Telescopes have detected interplanetary dust as a faint, glowing cloud near Earth.
Scientists study interplanetary dust to learn more about the formation of planets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmic dust - tiny solid particles found in space, including interplanetary and interstellar dust
