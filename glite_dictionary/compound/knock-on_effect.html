knock-on effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑk ɑn ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈnɒk ɒn ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
an indirect or secondary effect that is caused by an initial event and influences other situations or events
an indirect result or consequence
british

Examples

The closure of the factory had a significant knock-on effect on the local economy.
Rising oil prices have a knock-on effect on transportation costs and consumer goods.
Any changes to the schedule will have a knock-on effect throughout the entire project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripple effect - a continuing and spreading result of an event or action
  • Synonym: domino effect - a situation in which one event sets off a chain of similar events

Etymology

From British English 'knock-on', originally a rugby term for a ball knocked forward, later figuratively extended.
