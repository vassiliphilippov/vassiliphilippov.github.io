creeping buttercup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈbʌtərˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈbʌtəkʌp / (BrE)
a perennial European herb (Ranunculus repens) with long, creeping stolons and yellow flowers, often found in damp grounds
a perennial herb with creeping stolons

Examples

Creeping buttercup spreads quickly across lawns, making it difficult to remove.
The bright yellow flowers of the creeping buttercup stand out in the wet meadow.
Gardeners often struggle to control creeping buttercup in their flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - a plant of the genus Ranunculus, typically with yellow flowers
