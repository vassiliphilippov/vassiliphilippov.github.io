bible leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪbəl liːf / (AmE), / ˈbaɪbəl liːf / (BrE)
a tansy-scented Eurasian perennial herb with buttonlike yellow flowers, used as a potherb, salad green, potpourri, tea, or flavoring; sometimes classified in the genus Chrysanthemum
tansy-scented Eurasian perennial herb

Examples

She sprinkled chopped bible leaf into her salad for a unique, slightly bitter taste.
Historically, bible leaf was placed between the pages of books to keep them smelling fresh.
The herbal tea contained dried bible leaf along with other fragrant ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: costmary - an alternative name for bible leaf, a perennial herb used in cooking and medicine
