loan translation

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊn trænsˌleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈləʊn trænzˌleɪʃən / (BrE)
a word or phrase introduced into a language by translating it component by component from a foreign language, preserving its meaning
word-for-word translation from another language

Examples

The English word skyscraper is not a loan translation from another language, but some other words are.
The term 'flea market' is a loan translation of the French 'marché aux puces'.
Linguists often study how loan translation influences vocabulary development in different languages.

Synset Examples

‘superman’ is a calque for the German ‘Ubermensch’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calque - a word or phrase borrowed by literal translation from another language

Etymology

Modeled on the practice of translating the parts of a foreign term directly into the target language.
