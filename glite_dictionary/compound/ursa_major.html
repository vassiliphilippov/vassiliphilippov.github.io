Ursa Major

1. PROPN

/ ˌɝːsə ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˌɜːsə ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
a prominent constellation in the northern sky, also known as the Great Bear, located near the North Star and visible throughout the year in most of the northern hemisphere
constellation visible in northern sky

Examples

Ursa Major is one of the most recognizable constellations in the night sky.
Ancient cultures saw the pattern of Ursa Major as a great bear.
The Big Dipper is part of the larger constellation called Ursa Major.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Bear - another name for the Ursa Major constellation
  • Degree Lower: Ursa Minor - a smaller constellation near Ursa Major, also known as the Little Bear

Etymology

From Latin 'Ursa Major' meaning 'Greater Bear'.
