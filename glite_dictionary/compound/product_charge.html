product charge

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːdʌkt tʃɑːrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɒdʌkt tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a tax or fee imposed on products considered harmful to the environment, designed to discourage their use.
tax on environmentally harmful products

Examples

The government has introduced a product charge on plastic bags to reduce pollution.
A product charge is often used to encourage companies to find greener alternatives.
Imposing a product charge on certain chemicals can help protect natural resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmental tax - a tax levied with the aim of reducing environmental harm
