driving axle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvɪŋ ˈæksəl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ ˈæksəl / (BrE)
the axle in a vehicle that transmits power from the engine to the wheels, causing them to turn
axle that transmits power to wheels

Examples

A train's driving axle is responsible for moving the locomotive forward.
When the driving axle failed, the entire truck came to a halt.
The mechanic explained how the driving axle transfers power to the wheels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axle - a rod or spindle passing through the center of a wheel or group of wheels
