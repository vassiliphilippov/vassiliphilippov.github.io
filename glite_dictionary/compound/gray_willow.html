gray willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A Eurasian shrubby willow (Salix cinerea) with whitish, woolly twigs, commonly found in damp habitats.
Eurasian shrubby willow with whitish twigs

Examples

The gray willow grows abundantly along the riverbanks in eastern Europe.
Gardeners often choose the gray willow for wetland restoration projects.
The twigs of the gray willow have a distinctive whitish, woolly appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, usually found near water
