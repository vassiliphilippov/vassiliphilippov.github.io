trace detector

1. NOUN

/ treɪs dɪˈtɛktɚ / (AmE), / treɪs dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
a device used to screen for small amounts of explosives, especially in security settings such as airports
device for detecting traces of explosives

Examples

The airport security officer used a trace detector to check passengers' luggage for any residues of explosives.
Modern trace detectors are highly sensitive and can analyze even tiny amounts of chemical substances.
A trace detector can provide results within seconds, making it ideal for busy security checkpoints.
