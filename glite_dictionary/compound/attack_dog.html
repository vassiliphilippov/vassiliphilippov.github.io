attack dog

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæk dɔg / (AmE), / əˈtæk dɒg / (BrE)
A dog that has been specially trained to attack people or other animals on command, often used for security or protection.
dog trained to attack on command

Examples

The security company uses highly trained attack dogs to patrol the premises at night.
He was bitten by an attack dog during a police demonstration.
Some people argue that owning an attack dog carries serious legal responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard dog - a dog trained to guard property or people
  • Degree Lower: watchdog - a dog kept to watch for intruders

2. NOUN

/ əˈtæk dɔɡ / (AmE), / əˈtæk dɒɡ / (BrE)
A dog trained to protect people or property by attacking on command or showing aggression to intruders.
dog trained for attack or protection

Examples

The police often use an attack dog to help apprehend suspects.
After the break-in, the company hired a security team with an attack dog on patrol.
He warned trespassers that his attack dog would not hesitate to defend the property.

Synset Examples

The lawyer unleashed her attack dog during the cross-examination.
He trained his dog to be an effective attack dog.
The security team used an attack dog for protection.
