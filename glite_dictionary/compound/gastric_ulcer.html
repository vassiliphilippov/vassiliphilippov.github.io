gastric ulcer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrɪk ˈʌlsər / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk ˈʌlsə / (BrE)
A sore that develops on the lining of the stomach.
stomach lining ulcer
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a gastric ulcer after complaining of persistent stomach pain.
Stress and certain medications can increase the risk of developing a gastric ulcer.
The doctor prescribed medication to help heal her gastric ulcer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peptic ulcer - an ulcer of the digestive tract caused by gastric acid
  • Degree Lower: duodenal ulcer - an ulcer located in the duodenum

Etymology

From Latin 'gastricus' (of the stomach) and Latin 'ulcus' (sore, ulcer).
