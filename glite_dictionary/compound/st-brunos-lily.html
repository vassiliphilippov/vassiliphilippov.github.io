St.-Bruno's-lily

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪnt ˈbruːnoʊz ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ˈbruːnəʊz ˌlɪli / (BrE)
a perennial herbaceous plant of the genus Paradisea, known for its white, lily-like flowers, commonly called St.-Bruno's-lily.
white-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The garden boasted a beautiful cluster of St.-Bruno's-lily in full bloom.
He identified the rare St.-Bruno's-lily among the alpine flora.
Botanists value the delicate white flowers of St.-Bruno's-lily for their ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paradisea - the botanical genus to which St.-Bruno's-lily belongs

Etymology

Named after Saint Bruno, founder of the Carthusian order.
