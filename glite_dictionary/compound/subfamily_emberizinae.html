subfamily Emberizinae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæməli ˌɛmbəˈraɪzɪniː / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæmɪli ˌɛmbəˈraɪzɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds within the family Emberizidae, including buntings and certain New World sparrows.
bird subfamily including buntings and sparrows

Examples

Many species within the subfamily Emberizinae are known for their melodious songs.
The biologist studied the plumage differences among birds in the subfamily Emberizinae.
New World sparrows once classified under subfamily Emberizinae have recently been reassigned.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Emberizidae - the family including buntings and related birds
  • Synonym: bunting - a small songbird of the subfamily Emberizinae
  • Synonym: sparrow - a small bird included in part of Emberizinae, especially New World sparrows

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Emberizinae', derived from the genus Emberiza (buntings) + -inae (zoological subfamily suffix).
