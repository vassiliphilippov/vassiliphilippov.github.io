Chenin blanc

1. NOUN

/ ʃɛnən ˈblɑ̃ / (AmE), / ˌʃɛn.æ̃ ˈblɒ̃ / (BrE)
a white grape variety originally from the Loire Valley in France, widely grown for making white wines, especially in France and California
a white grape variety for wine

Examples

The winery specializes in both Chenin blanc and Sauvignon blanc wines.
We enjoyed a crisp glass of Chenin blanc with our seafood dinner.
Many vineyards in California have started planting more Chenin blanc due to its refreshing acidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sauvignon blanc - another major white grape variety used for wine
  • Antonym: Cabernet Sauvignon - a prominent red grape variety, contrasted with white Chenin blanc

Etymology

French, named after the region of Chenin in the Loire Valley.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˌnæn ˈblɑŋk / (AmE), / ʃəˌnɛ̃ ˈblɒ̃ / (BrE)
a white grape variety originating from the Loire Valley in France, also used to make white wines in California and other regions
white grape variety; white wine

Examples

The Chenin blanc from the Loire Valley is known for its crisp acidity and floral aromas.
Many California vineyards now produce high-quality Chenin blanc wines.
Would you like to try a glass of Chenin blanc with your seafood?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sauvignon blanc - another major white grape variety and wine
  • Antonym: red wine - wine made from dark-colored grape varieties, contrasted with white wine

Etymology

French, from the name of Mont Chenin in the Loire region, where the grape is believed to have originated.
