mental imagery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntəl ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈmɛntəl ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (BrE)
the ability to form pictures or images in the mind of objects, scenes, or events that are not present
the ability to form images in the mind

Examples

Athletes often use mental imagery to visualize success before a competition.
Good mental imagery can help writers describe scenes more vividly in their stories.
The psychologist taught her patients relaxation techniques and mental imagery exercises.

Synset Examples

he could still hear her in his imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty or action of forming new ideas or images not present to the senses
