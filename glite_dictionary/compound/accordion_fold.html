accordion fold

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɔr.di.ən foʊld / (AmE), / əˈkɔː.di.ən fəʊld / (BrE)
a series of parallel folds alternately made in opposite directions, forming a zig-zag pattern like the bellows of an accordion
series of zig-zag parallel folds

Examples

The brochure was designed with an accordion fold to make it compact yet easy to expand.
She used an accordion fold technique to create decorative streamers for the party.
The map featured an accordion fold so that it could be unfolded quickly when needed.

Synset Examples

Seen from above, concertina folds resemble a Z or M or series of zigs and zags.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concertina fold - another term for accordion fold, used especially in printing and paper folding

Etymology

From 'accordion' (the musical instrument, describing the similarity in appearance) + 'fold'.
