pea crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈpi kræb / (AmE), / ˈpiː krab / (BrE)
a very small, soft-bodied crab that lives as a commensal inside the mantles of certain bivalve mollusks
small crab living in bivalves

Examples

A pea crab was discovered living inside the oyster by the marine biologist.
The pea crab forms a symbiotic relationship with certain clams and mussels.
Fishermen sometimes find a pea crab when they open up a freshly harvested oyster.
