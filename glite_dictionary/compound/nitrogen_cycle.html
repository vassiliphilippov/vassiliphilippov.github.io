nitrogen cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtɹə.dʒən ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.tɹɪ.dʒən ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
the natural process by which nitrogen is converted between its various chemical forms in the environment, involving processes such as absorption by plants, consumption by animals, and return to the soil through decay.
the circulation of nitrogen in nature

Examples

The nitrogen cycle is essential for maintaining healthy ecosystems and supporting plant growth.
Fertilizer can disrupt the natural nitrogen cycle by adding excess nitrates to the soil.
Scientists study the nitrogen cycle to understand how pollution affects the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon cycle - the natural circulation of carbon through the environment
