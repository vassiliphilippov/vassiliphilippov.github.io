normal dwarf

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔːrməl dwɔrf / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməl dwɔːf / (BrE)
A person of unusually short stature due to a genetic condition, whose physical proportions, mental, and sexual development are otherwise typical.
person of short stature with typical development
medical

Examples

The scientist explained that a normal dwarf has average mental and sexual development despite their short stature.
Advocacy groups work to ensure that individuals like a normal dwarf are not unfairly stereotyped in society.
Research into the genetics of the normal dwarf has contributed to advances in bone growth disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achondroplastic dwarf - a person with achondroplasia, causing short stature with distinctive physical features
  • Degree Lower: dwarf - a person who is much smaller than average size
