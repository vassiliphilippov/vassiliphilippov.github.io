pencil pusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.səl ˌpʊʃ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl ˌpʊʃ.ə / (BrE)
an office worker, especially one whose job consists mainly of routine paperwork and administrative tasks, often regarded as unexciting or mundane
office worker doing routine paperwork
informal

Examples

He didn't want to spend his life as a pencil pusher trapped in a cubicle.
The company needs more creative thinkers, not just pencil pushers following rules.
She prefers action-packed jobs over being a pencil pusher in an office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerk - an office worker who handles records or office tasks

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.səl ˌpʊʃ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl ˌpʊʃ.ə / (BrE)
an office worker who performs routine or boring clerical tasks, especially as a main part of their job
person who does monotonous office work
informal

Examples

He got tired of being a pencil pusher and decided to pursue a more creative career.
After years as a pencil pusher, she longed for work that was more meaningful.
The company was filled with pencil pushers who rarely left their desks.

Synset Examples

My dad was a pencil pusher at the bank.
She became a pencil pusher after college.
Being a pencil pusher doesn’t fulfill me creatively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper-pusher - office worker who only does routine paperwork
  • Synonym: bureaucrat - official in an administrative system, often seen as concerned with procedural correctness

Etymology

From 'pencil' (writing instrument) and 'pusher' (one who pushes); metaphorically for repetitive paperwork.
