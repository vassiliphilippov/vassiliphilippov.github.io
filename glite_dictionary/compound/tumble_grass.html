tumble grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmbəl ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəl ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A North American grass with slender, brush-like panicles, often found as a weed in cultivated fields.
a North American grass weed

Examples

The farmer spent hours pulling tumble grass from his wheat field.
In dry seasons, tumble grass can quickly take over abandoned lots.
Researchers studied how tumble grass affects soil quality in agricultural areas.
