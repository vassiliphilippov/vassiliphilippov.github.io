fee simple absolute

1. NOUN

/ fiː ˈsɪmpəl ˈæbsəˌlut / (AmE), / fiː ˈsɪmpəl ˈæbsəluːt / (BrE)
an estate in land that provides the owner with complete ownership rights and no limitations, except those imposed by law or private agreement
unlimited property ownership estate
legal

Examples

After years of tenancy, he finally acquired the land in fee simple absolute.
The house was left to her as a fee simple absolute in her grandfather's will.
Owning property in fee simple absolute allows you to sell or bequeath it without restrictions.

Synset Examples

The property was transferred as a fee simple absolute in possession.
He holds a fee simple absolute in possession of the land.
She inherited the house as a fee simple absolute in possession.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: life estate - an estate limited to the duration of someone's life
  • Degree Lower: fee simple defeasible - an estate in land that can be voided if certain conditions are met

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'fee' (feud, estate in land) and Latin 'absolutus' (unrestricted).
