attic salt

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬ɪk sɔlt / (AmE), / ˈætɪk sɒlt / (BrE)
subtle, refined, and elegant wit or humor, especially of a classical or literary kind
refined or delicate wit; subtle humor
archaic

Examples

The dinner conversation sparkled with attic salt, delighting all the guests.
Critics admired the author's attic salt, which gave his essays an enduring charm.
Her letters were always lively, seasoned with just enough attic salt to amuse her friends.

Synset Examples

The humor in her speech had just the right amount of attic salt.
Her comments were filled with attic salt, making them amusing.
He always added attic salt to his stories, making them lively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - clever humor and intelligence

Etymology

From Attic, referring to the region around Athens, Greece, renowned for the wit of its inhabitants, combined with 'salt' meaning wit or liveliness.
