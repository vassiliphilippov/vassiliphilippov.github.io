War of American Independence

1. NOUN

/ wɔr əv əˌmɛrɪkən ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / wɔːr əv əˌmɛrɪkən ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
The war (1775–1783) in which the thirteen American Colonies fought to gain independence from Great Britain, resulting in the formation of the United States of America.
Revolutionary war between American colonies and Britain (1775–1783)

Examples

Historians have long debated the causes of the War of American Independence.
Several key battles during the War of American Independence were fought on the eastern seaboard.
The War of American Independence led to the birth of a new nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Revolution - the political upheaval during which the Thirteen Colonies broke away from British rule
