suborder Scorpaenoidea

1. NOUN

/ səbˈɔrdər ˌskɔrpiəˈnɔɪdiə / (AmE), / sʌbˈɔːdə ˌskɔːpiəˈnɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of mail-cheeked fishes that includes scorpionfishes and gurnards.
taxonomic group of mail-cheeked fishes

Examples

The suborder Scorpaenoidea includes many species known for their venomous spines.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of the suborder Scorpaenoidea.
Fishes in the suborder Scorpaenoidea are often found in rocky and coral reef habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorpaenid - any fish of the family Scorpaenidae
  • Synonym: gurnard - a type of fish with bony heads and armored plates, found in the same suborder

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'suborder' (taxonomic rank) and 'Scorpaenoidea' (from genus 'Scorpaena').
