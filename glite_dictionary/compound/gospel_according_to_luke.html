Gospel According to Luke

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɑspəl əˈkɔrdɪŋ tuː luːk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl əˈkɔːdɪŋ tuː luːk / (BrE)
the third book of the New Testament, recounting the life, teachings, and works of Jesus Christ, with special emphasis on his birth and early life
third book of the New Testament

Examples

The parable of the Good Samaritan appears in the Gospel According to Luke.
Scholars analyze the unique focus on compassion in the Gospel According to Luke.
Many Christmas traditions draw inspiration from the nativity story in the Gospel According to Luke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel According to Matthew - the first Gospel in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Gospel According to Mark - the second Gospel in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Gospel According to John - the fourth Gospel in the New Testament

Etymology

From Greek 'euangelion kata Loukan', meaning 'good news according to Luke'.
