double dipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌdɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌdɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of receiving two incomes from the same source, such as holding a government job while also drawing a government pension.
receiving two incomes from the same source

Examples

The city introduced new rules to prevent double dipping among retired officials who return to government jobs.
Critics argue that double dipping unfairly benefits public employees at taxpayer expense.
Investigations revealed widespread double dipping in the agency over the last decade.
