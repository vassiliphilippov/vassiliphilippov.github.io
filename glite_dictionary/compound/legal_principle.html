legal principle

1. NOUN

/ ˈliɡəl ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
a fundamental rule or concept forming the basis of law or judicial decisions
fundamental rule or concept in law
legal

Examples

The judge based her ruling on a well-established legal principle.
Our constitution is founded on fundamental legal principles of justice and equality.
Understanding the underlying legal principle is essential for interpreting the law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jurisprudence - the theory or philosophy of law
  • Synonym: legal precedent - an earlier court decision serving as a rule for future cases
