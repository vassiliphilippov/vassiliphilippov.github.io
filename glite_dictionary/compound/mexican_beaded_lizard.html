Mexican beaded lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən ˈbiːdɪd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən ˈbiːdɪd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A large venomous lizard of western Mexico, recognized by its black and yellow beadlike scales.
venomous lizard of western Mexico

Examples

The Mexican beaded lizard is one of only a few venomous lizards in the world.
Herpetologists studied the unique scales of the Mexican beaded lizard in their natural habitat.
A bite from a Mexican beaded lizard requires immediate medical attention due to its potent venom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gila monster - venomous lizard of the southwestern US and northwestern Mexico
