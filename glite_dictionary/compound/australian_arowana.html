Australian arowana

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˌæroʊˈɑːnə / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˌærəʊˈɑːnə / (BrE)
a species of large freshwater fish (Scleropages leichardti) native to Australian rivers, noted for a red or pink spot on each scale
large Australian freshwater fish species

Examples

The Australian arowana is prized by aquarium enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity.
Researchers observed the Australian arowana in its native river habitat in Queensland.
A single Australian arowana can grow over half a meter in length in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arowana - a family of freshwater bony fish known for their elongated bodies and scales
