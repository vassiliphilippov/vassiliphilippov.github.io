genus Plasmodiophora

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs plæzˌmoʊ.diˈɒf.ə.rə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs plæzˌməʊ.diˈɒf.ə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of minute, plant-parasitic fungi of the family Plasmodiophoraceae, sometimes classified among the slime molds.
type genus of Plasmodiophoraceae, plant-parasitic fungi

Examples

The genus Plasmodiophora includes organisms responsible for the clubroot disease in cabbages.
Researchers studied the life cycle of organisms within the genus Plasmodiophora to better understand plant infections.
Specimens from the genus Plasmodiophora are often found in agricultural soils impacting root crops.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Plasmodiophoraceae - family of parasitic fungi related to slime molds

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Plasmodiophora' from Greek 'plasma' (something molded) + 'diophoros' (bearing).
