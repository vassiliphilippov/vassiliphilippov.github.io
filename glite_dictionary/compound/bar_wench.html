bar wench

1. NOUN

/ bɑr wɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɑː wɛn(t)ʃ / (BrE)
a female server in a bar or tavern, especially in a historical or playful context
female server in a bar (historical)
archaic

Examples

In the lively inn, the bar wench served travelers hearty ale and warm meals.
He dressed as a pirate, and his friend came as a bar wench for the costume party.
The old play features a charming bar wench who outsmarts the rude customers.

Synset Examples

They referred to her affectionately as the bar wench.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barmaid - a female bartender or server in a bar
  • Antonym: barman - a male bartender or server in a bar

Etymology

From 'bar' (establishment serving alcohol) + 'wench' (Middle English wenc(e), meaning girl or young woman).
