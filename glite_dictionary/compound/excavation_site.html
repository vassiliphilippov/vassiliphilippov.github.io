excavation site

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.skəˈveɪ.ʃən saɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛk.skəˈveɪ.ʃən saɪt / (BrE)
a place where archaeologists dig to uncover historical remains or artifacts
archaeological digging location

Examples

The excavation site provided invaluable insights into the ancient civilization.
Archaeologists carefully catalogued every item they found at the excavation site.
Heavy rains delayed work at the main excavation site for several days.

Synset Examples

The excavation site attracted many curious tourists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig site - location where an archaeological dig is conducted

Etymology

excavation: from Latin excavatio (hollowing out); site: from Latin situs (site, position).
