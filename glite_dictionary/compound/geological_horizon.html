geological horizon

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑlədʒɪkəl həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈlɒdʒɪkəl həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
a distinct layer of rock, especially one identifiable by its composition or fossil content, used to date and correlate strata
distinct rock layer for dating strata

Examples

The fossils found within this geological horizon helped to determine its age.
Each geological horizon in the core sample was analyzed for mineral composition.
A sudden change in sediment type marks a new geological horizon in the rock sequence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratum - a layer of sedimentary rock or soil
  • Synonym: bed - a layer of rock or sediment
