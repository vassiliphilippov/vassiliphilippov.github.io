Das Kapital

1. NOUN

/ dɑs kɑːˈpɪtəl / (AmE), / dæs ˈkæpɪtæl / (BrE)
a major work by Karl Marx, published in 1867, outlining his theories of capitalism and economics
Karl Marx's influential 1867 book on economics

Examples

Many scholars still study Das Kapital to understand Marxist economic theory.
The arguments in Das Kapital have influenced generations of economists and political thinkers.
She wrote her dissertation on the historical impact of Das Kapital in modern society.

Etymology

German, literally 'Capital', title of Karl Marx's treatise.
