unwritten law

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈrɪtən lɔ / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪtən lɔː / (BrE)
A law that is not formally enacted by a legislative body but is observed through custom or tradition; a customary rule governing behavior.
customary rule not formally enacted

Examples

The community followed the unwritten law that everyone helped their neighbors in times of need.
In some cultures, the unwritten law of hospitality is as binding as any statute.
Juries have sometimes acquitted defendants under the so-called unwritten law of self-defense.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
