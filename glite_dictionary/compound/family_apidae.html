family Apidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈæpɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈæpɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of bees that includes honeybees, carpenter bees, and bumblebees.
bee family including honeybees and bumblebees

Examples

The family Apidae includes many species important for pollination.
Scientists are studying the diversity within the family Apidae.
Honey production largely depends on bees from the family Apidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee - a winged insect that collects nectar and pollen
  • Antonym: wasp - a similar flying insect but not part of Apidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Apidae', from 'Apis' meaning 'bee'.
