family Ceratopsidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɛˌrætəpˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɛˌrætɒpˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of horned, herbivorous dinosaurs, including Triceratops and its relatives, native to North America during the Late Cretaceous period.
family of horned dinosaurs (Ceratopsidae)

Examples

Some of the most famous members of the family Ceratopsidae include Triceratops and Styracosaurus.
Paleontologists have discovered many fossils belonging to the family Ceratopsidae in North America.
The large frills and facial horns are characteristic features of dinosaurs in the family Ceratopsidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceratopsian - horned, beaked dinosaurs of the group Ceratopsia
  • Synonym: Triceratops - a well-known genus within Ceratopsidae

Etymology

From New Latin Ceratopsidae, based on Ceratops (type genus) + suffix -idae (family).
