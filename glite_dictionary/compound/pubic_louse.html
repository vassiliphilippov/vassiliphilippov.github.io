pubic louse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːbɪk laʊs / (AmE), / ˈpjuːbɪk laʊs / (BrE)
a parasitic insect (Phthirus pubis) that infests the pubic hair region of humans, causing irritation and itching
parasitic insect infesting pubic hair
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with an infestation of pubic louse after examining the irritated area.
A pubic louse is commonly spread through close physical contact.
Treatments for pubic louse infestations are available over the counter at most pharmacies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head louse - a louse that infests the scalp hair of humans
  • Synonym: body louse - a louse that infests the body and clothing of humans

Etymology

From Latin 'pubicus' (of the groin) and 'louse' from Old English 'lūs'.
