traddy music

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrædi ˌmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈtrædi ˌmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Informal term for traditional music, especially folk music, typically performed in a style rooted in local or historical customs.
informal term for traditional music
informal slang

Examples

At the pub last night, everyone danced to the traddy music played by the local band.
She prefers traddy music over modern pop songs at family gatherings.
Many young musicians are rediscovering traddy music and blending it with contemporary styles.

Synset Examples

They love the traddy music at the folk festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traditional music - music passed down through generations, often folk music

Etymology

From 'traddy', a slang shortening of 'traditional', plus 'music'.
