jam session

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæm ˌsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒam ˌsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
an informal gathering of musicians, especially jazz players, who improvise and play music together without prior rehearsal
informal improvised music gathering

Examples

The musicians decided to host a jam session after the concert ended.
Anyone can join the jam session as long as they bring an instrument.
At the club, a spontaneous jam session broke out among the local jazz artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvisation - the act of creating and performing music spontaneously

Etymology

From 'jam' (to improvise music) and 'session' (period spent doing a particular activity), popularized in jazz culture in the 1930s.
