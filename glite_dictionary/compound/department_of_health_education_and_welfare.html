Department of Health, Education, and Welfare

1. PROPN

/ dɪˈpɑːrtmənt əv hɛlθ ˌɛdʒʊˈkeɪʃən ənd ˈwɛlfɛr / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtmənt əv hɛlθ ˌedjʊˈkeɪʃən ənd ˈwɛlfɛə / (BrE)
a former executive department of the United States government, established in 1953 to oversee health, education, and welfare programs and reorganized in 1979.
former U.S. government department (1953–1979)
american

Examples

The Department of Health, Education, and Welfare played a major role in shaping U.S. policy after World War II.
In 1979, the Department of Health, Education, and Welfare was split into separate agencies.
Many federal programs we know today originated in the Department of Health, Education, and Welfare.
