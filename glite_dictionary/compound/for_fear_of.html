for fear of

1. ADP

/ fər fɪr ʌv / (AmE), / fə fɪəʳ əv / (BrE)
to avoid the risk of something happening or because one is worried that something might happen
to avoid the risk of or due to worry about

Examples

She stayed silent for fear of upsetting anyone.
He spoke quietly for fear of waking the baby.
Many people refuse to travel alone at night for fear of being robbed.

Synset Examples

She stayed silent for fear of upsetting anyone.
They left early for fear of missing the last train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lest - for fear that (literary or formal alternative)
