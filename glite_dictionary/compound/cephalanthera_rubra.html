Cephalanthera rubra

1. NOUN

/ sɛf.əˌlænˈθɛr.ə ˈruː.brə / (AmE), / sɛf.əˌlænˈθɪə.rə ˈruː.brə / (BrE)
A species of orchid found in the Mediterranean region and Asia, characterized by loosely arranged spikes of bright rose-pink flowers.
Mediterranean/Asian orchid with pink flowers

Examples

The woods were filled with the delicate blooms of Cephalanthera rubra during the early summer months.
Botanists were excited to find a rare specimen of Cephalanthera rubra in the national park.
Many photographers visit the region each year hoping to capture images of the striking Cephalanthera rubra orchid.

Etymology

From Latin, with Cephalanthera from Greek 'kephalē' (head) + 'anthera' (anther) and 'rubra' meaning 'red'.
