dark side

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk saɪd / (AmE), / dɑːk saɪd / (BrE)
the negative, evil, or morally questionable aspects of a person, thing, or situation, often hidden or less visible
evil or negative aspect of something

Examples

The investigation uncovered the dark side of the company's business practices.
She struggled with the dark side of fame and its constant scrutiny.
Many stories explore the dark side of human nature.

Synset Examples

The scandal revealed the dark side of crony capitalism.
Don’t look on the dark side; it's not that bad.
The villain embraced the dark side of the Force.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: good side - the positive or virtuous aspect of something
