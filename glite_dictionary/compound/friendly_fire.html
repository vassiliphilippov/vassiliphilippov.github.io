friendly fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛndli ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛndli ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
gunfire or attack that unintentionally injures or kills one's own forces or allies, rather than the enemy
unintentional attack on one's own forces

Examples

The tragedy was caused by friendly fire during the confusion of battle.
Several soldiers were injured due to friendly fire in the early morning hours.
The military is investigating the friendly fire incident that occurred last week.
