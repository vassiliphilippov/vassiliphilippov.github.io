family Calliphoridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkælɪfɔːˈraɪəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkælɪfəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of metallic-colored flies commonly known as blowflies, many of which are associated with decaying matter.
a family of flies; blowflies

Examples

The larvae of the family Calliphoridae are often used in forensic investigations to estimate time of death.
Members of the family Calliphoridae are attracted to decaying animal matter.
Researchers studied the life cycle of several species within the family Calliphoridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowfly - a common name for any member of Calliphoridae

Etymology

From New Latin Calliphoridae, based on Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the shiny appearance of many species.
