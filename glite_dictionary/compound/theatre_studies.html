theatre studies

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiətər ˈstʌdiz / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə ˈstʌdiz / (BrE)
the academic study of theatre, including the analysis of theatrical performance within its literary, physical, psychological, sociological, and historical contexts
the academic study of theatre and performance

Examples

She decided to major in theatre studies because of her passion for live performance.
Many universities offer theatre studies as part of their arts curriculum.
Her background in theatre studies helped her understand the cultural impact of drama.

Synset Examples

Its wide citation in a range of scholarly contexts, in the UK, Australasia and North America and in geography, urban and theatre studies, suggests its reception as a contribution to research in theatre and urban cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drama - a composition intended for performance in the theatre
  • Degree Higher: performance studies - the interdisciplinary study of performance in all its forms
