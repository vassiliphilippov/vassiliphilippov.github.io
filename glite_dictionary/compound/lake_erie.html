Lake Erie

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈɪəri / (AmE), / leɪk ˈɪəri / (BrE)
the fourth largest of the Great Lakes of North America, located on the border between the United States and Canada.
the fourth largest Great Lake in North America

Examples

The city of Cleveland sits on the southern shore of Lake Erie.
Many storms form suddenly over Lake Erie during the fall and winter months.
Fishermen flock to Lake Erie for its abundant walleye population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lake Michigan - one of the other Great Lakes in North America
  • Synonym: Lake Ontario - another of the Great Lakes bordering Canada and the United States

Etymology

Named after the Erie people, a Native American tribe who once lived along its southern shore.
