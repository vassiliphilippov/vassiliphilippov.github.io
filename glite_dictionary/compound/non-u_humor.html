non-U humor

1. NOUN

/ nɑn ˈju ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / nɒn ˈjuː ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
humor that is not considered upper-class, typically seen as less sophisticated or more middle- or working-class in style
humor not considered upper-class
british

Examples

The comedian's non-U humor drew laughter from everyday working families.
Many British sitcoms rely on non-U humor to connect with a broad audience.
His jokes had a distinctive non-U humor that set him apart from more traditional performers.

Synset Examples

The film's charm lay in its non-U humor and relatable characters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: U humor - humor considered upper-class or sophisticated
  • Synonym: popular humor - humor appealing to or characteristic of the general populace

Etymology

'Non-U' from 'non-upper class,' popularized by Alan S. C. Ross in 1950s Britain to distinguish between linguistic and social class markers.
