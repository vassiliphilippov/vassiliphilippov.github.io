bill of sale

1. NOUN

/ bɪl əv seɪl / (AmE), / bɪl əv seɪl / (BrE)
a written document that officially transfers ownership of personal property from one party to another
legal document transferring ownership
legal

Examples

He provided a bill of sale as proof he had legally purchased the car.
A bill of sale is often required when transferring used equipment between companies.
Without a bill of sale, establishing rightful ownership can be difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deed - a legal document that is signed and delivered, especially one regarding ownership of property
