Kenyan shilling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛniən ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɛnjən ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Kenya, equal to 100 cents.
currency of Kenya

Examples

She exchanged her dollars for Kenyan shillings before traveling to Nairobi.
The price of lunch was 350 Kenyan shillings at the local cafe.
Foreign investors monitor the exchange rate of the Kenyan shilling closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shilling - former currency unit of the UK and several other countries

Etymology

From 'Kenya', the country, and 'shilling', originally from Old English *scilling*.
