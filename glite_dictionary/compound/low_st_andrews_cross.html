low St Andrew's cross

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˌsɛnt ˈændruːz krɑs / (AmE), / ləʊ ˌsənt ˈændruːz krɒs / (BrE)
A low, shrubby plant native to Bermuda, the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and eastern Mexico, with yellow flowers that have four petals arranged in the shape of a cross.
low shrubby plant with cross-shaped yellow flowers

Examples

The garden features a section devoted to native plants, including the low St Andrew's cross.
Botanists observed the distinctively arranged flowers on the low St Andrew's cross during their field survey.
The low St Andrew's cross thrives in sandy soils along the coast of the southeastern United States.

Etymology

From the reference to St Andrew's cross, relating to the cross-like arrangement of the flower's petals.
