Gregorian calendar

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈɡɔriən ˈkæləndər / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːrɪən ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the calendar in general use internationally, first introduced in 1582 by Pope Gregory XIII to correct inaccuracies in the Julian calendar; it standardized leap years and adjusted the date to align with the solar year
widely-used solar calendar introduced in 1582

Examples

Many countries adopted the Gregorian calendar at different times throughout history.
The Gregorian calendar replaced the Julian calendar to provide a more accurate measurement of the year.
Our current year numbering is based on the Gregorian calendar system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Julian calendar - the predecessor calendar replaced by the Gregorian calendar

Etymology

Named after Pope Gregory XIII, who introduced it in 1582.
