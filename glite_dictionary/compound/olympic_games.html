Olympic Games

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪmpɪk ɡeɪmz / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪk ɡeɪmz / (BrE)
an international sports competition held every four years in which athletes from many countries participate in a variety of sporting events
international multi-sport event held every four years

Examples

Athletes around the world train for years to compete in the Olympic Games.
The city was honored to be chosen as the host of the Olympic Games.
The Olympic Games unite nations through the spirit of sportsmanship and friendly competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympics - another term for the Olympic Games

Etymology

From Greek 'Olympiakos' (of Olympus), after Olympia, site of the original games in ancient Greece.

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɪmpɪk ˈɡeɪmz / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪk ˈɡeɪmz / (BrE)
the ancient Panhellenic festival of athletic and sporting competitions held at Olympia every four years in honor of Zeus, beginning in 776 BC
ancient Greek athletic festival in Olympia

Examples

The ancient Olympic Games were held to honor the Greek god Zeus every four years.
Historians believe that athletes from all over Greece traveled to compete in the Olympic Games.
Women were not allowed to participate in the original Olympic Games of ancient Greece.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: modern Olympic Games - the international sporting event first held in 1896

Etymology

From Olympia, the site in Greece; Greek Ολυμπιακοὶ Ἀγῶνες (Olympiakoi Agones).
