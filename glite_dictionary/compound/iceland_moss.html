Iceland moss

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪslənd mɑs / (AmE), / ˈaɪslənd mɒs / (BrE)
a lichen with a branched, flattened, partly upright thallus, found in mountainous and Arctic regions, used as a medicine or food source
a medicinal and edible Arctic lichen

Examples

Iceland moss was traditionally used as a remedy for coughs and colds in northern Europe.
The hikers noticed patches of Iceland moss growing along the rocky slopes of the mountain.
Livestock in Arctic regions often rely on Iceland moss as an essential part of their winter diet.
