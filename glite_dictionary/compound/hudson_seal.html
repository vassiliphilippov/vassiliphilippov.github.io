hudson seal

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌdsən siːl / (AmE), / ˈhʌdsən siːl / (BrE)
Muskrat fur that has been processed and dyed to resemble sealskin.
muskrat fur made to look like sealskin

Examples

The coat was made of hudson seal, a popular choice in the early twentieth century.
She inherited a vintage hudson seal muff from her grandmother.
Many shoppers could not afford real seal fur, so they opted for hudson seal instead.

Etymology

From Hudson Seal Company, a trade name originating in North America, where muskrat fur was processed to imitate real seal fur.
