nodding groundsel

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑdɪŋ ˈɡraʊndsəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒdɪŋ ˈɡraʊndsəl / (BrE)
A plant with upright leafy stems and bent stalks bearing clusters of small, yellow, rayless flower heads, found in moist areas of the southwestern United States.
plant with yellow rayless flower heads

Examples

The nodding groundsel is often found in meadows after spring rains.
Botanists noted the distinctive bent stalks of the nodding groundsel in the field guide.
She photographed the bright yellow flower heads of the nodding groundsel during her hike.

Etymology

From 'nodding' (bending forward) and 'groundsel', from Old English 'grundeswelge' (ground swallower), a traditional name for plants in the genus Senecio.
