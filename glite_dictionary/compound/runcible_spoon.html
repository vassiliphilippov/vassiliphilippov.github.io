runcible spoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnsəbəl spuːn / (AmE), / ˈrʌnsɪbəl spuːn / (BrE)
a fork-like spoon with a cutting edge, originally coined by Edward Lear; often used humorously or fancifully
fork-like spoon with cutting edge

Examples

He ate his green peas carefully with a runcible spoon.
The runcible spoon was featured in the whimsical poem by Edward Lear.
Collectors treasure rare examples of the Victorian runcible spoon.

Etymology

Coined by Edward Lear in the late 19th century; origin uncertain, invented word.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnsəbəl spuːn / (AmE), / ˈrʌnsɪbəl spuːn / (BrE)
a spoon with a broad, flat, often curved blade, sometimes with sharp edges or tines, originally coined as a nonsense term but sometimes used for certain types of cutlery
whimsical type of spoon (nonsense term)

Examples

The waiter brought out the exotic fruit salad and presented it with a runcible spoon.
At the quirky café, every dessert was served with a runcible spoon, delighting the customers.
Her grandmother collected unusual cutlery, and her favorite piece was a runcible spoon.

Synset Examples

He used a runcible spoon to serve the dessert.

Etymology

Coined by Edward Lear in the 19th century as a nonsense word in his poems.
