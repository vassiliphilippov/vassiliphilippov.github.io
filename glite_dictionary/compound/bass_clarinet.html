bass clarinet

1. NOUN

/ beɪs ˌklɛrɪˈnɛt / (AmE), / beɪs ˌklærɪˈnɛt / (BrE)
a large woodwind instrument similar to a clarinet but with a lower pitch, sounding an octave below the standard B-flat clarinet
a low-pitched woodwind instrument

Examples

She plays the bass clarinet in the school orchestra.
The rich, deep sound of the bass clarinet set the mood for the piece.
A bass clarinet can add warmth to any wind ensemble.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clarinet - a woodwind instrument with a single-reed mouthpiece

Etymology

From 'bass', meaning low in pitch, and 'clarinet', from French 'clarinette'.
