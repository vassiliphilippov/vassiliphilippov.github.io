cynthia moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnθiə mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθiə mɒθ / (BrE)
A large moth native to Asia, introduced into the United States, whose larvae feed on the ailanthus tree.
large Asiatic moth, feeds on ailanthus

Examples

The cynthia moth was introduced to the United States to help produce silk.
Larvae of the cynthia moth primarily feed on ailanthus, also known as the tree of heaven.
Scientists are studying the spread of the cynthia moth across different regions of North America.

Etymology

Named after Cynthia, a genus name given by the Swedish naturalist Carl Linnaeus.
