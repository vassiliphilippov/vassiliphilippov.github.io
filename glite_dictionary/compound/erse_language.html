Erse language

1. NOUN

/ ɜːrs ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɜːs ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Goidelic Celtic language group spoken in Ireland (Irish Gaelic) and Scotland (Scottish Gaelic), especially referred to historically as 'Erse'
historic term for Gaelic languages of Ireland and Scotland
archaic

Examples

The Erse language was once widely spoken across both Ireland and the Scottish Highlands.
Early poets in the Erse language left a rich tapestry of oral tradition.
There are efforts today to preserve the Erse language and its cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaelic - the Celtic languages spoken in Ireland and Scotland

Etymology

From Scots 'Erische' (Irish), from Middle Irish 'Ériu' (Ireland).
