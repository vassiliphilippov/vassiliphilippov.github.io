potato bean

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ biːn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ biːn / (BrE)
A North American climbing plant with fragrant flowers and edible tubers, formerly an important food source for Native Americans.
North American vine with edible tubers

Examples

The roots of the potato bean can be eaten after cooking, much like potatoes.
Early settlers learned from Native Americans how to prepare potato bean dishes.
The potato bean thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
