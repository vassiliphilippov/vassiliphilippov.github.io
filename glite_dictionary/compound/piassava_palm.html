piassava palm

1. NOUN

/ piˌɑːˈsɑːvə pɑːm / (AmE), / piˌæsəˈvɑː pɑːm / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree (Attalea funifera or Leopoldinia piassaba) whose stiff, resilient fibers are used to make ropes, mats, and brushes.
Brazilian palm yielding strong fiber

Examples

The piassava palm is cultivated in Brazil for its valuable, water-resistant fibers.
Workers harvested the long fibers from the piassava palm to weave durable brooms and mats.
Exporters ship products made from piassava palm fibers to various markets around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffia palm - African palm yielding similar strong fibers

Etymology

From Portuguese 'piaçaba' or 'piassava', from Tupi language.
