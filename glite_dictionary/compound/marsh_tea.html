marsh tea

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ti / (AmE), / mɑːʃ tiː / (BrE)
a bog shrub (Ledum palustre) found in northern and central Europe, Siberia, Korea, and Japan, known for its aromatic leaves
aromatic bog shrub of northern Eurasia

Examples

The herbalist collected marsh tea from the bog for its medicinal properties.
In Scandinavia, marsh tea has traditionally been used to flavor beer.
Hikers should be cautious when picking marsh tea, as its leaves contain toxic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild rosemary - common synonym for Ledum palustre
