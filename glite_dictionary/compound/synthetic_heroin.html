synthetic heroin

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈθɛtɪk hɛˈroʊɪn / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk hɛˈrəʊɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic narcotic drug similar to morphine but less habit-forming; used to aid in the detoxification and maintenance therapy of heroin addiction
synthetic narcotic for heroin treatment
medical

Examples

The clinic provides synthetic heroin to help patients manage their withdrawal symptoms.
Many recovering addicts are prescribed synthetic heroin as part of their treatment plan.
Studies suggest that synthetic heroin is less likely to cause dependency than traditional opiates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methadone - a synthetic opioid used in the treatment of heroin addiction
  • Antonym: heroin - a highly addictive narcotic drug derived from morphine
