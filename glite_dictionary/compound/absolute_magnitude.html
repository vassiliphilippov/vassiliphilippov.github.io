absolute magnitude

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbsəˈluːt ˈmæɡnɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˌabsəˈluːt ˈmaɡnɪtjuːd / (BrE)
(Astronomy) the measure of the intrinsic brightness of a celestial object, expressed as the apparent magnitude it would have if observed from a standard distance of 10 parsecs (32.62 light years) from Earth.
astronomical measure of intrinsic stellar brightness
medical

Examples

The absolute magnitude of the Sun is about +4.8.
Astronomers use absolute magnitude to compare the true brightness of stars regardless of their distance.
A star's absolute magnitude can be calculated using its apparent magnitude and distance from Earth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apparent magnitude - the observed brightness of a celestial object as seen from Earth
