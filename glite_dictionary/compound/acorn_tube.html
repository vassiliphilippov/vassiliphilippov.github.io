acorn tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.kɔrn tuːb / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.kɔːn tjuːb / (BrE)
A small vacuum tube designed for use at high frequencies, characterized by its acorn-like shape.
small, high-frequency vacuum tube

Examples

Early radar systems often used acorn tubes for their high-frequency performance.
The distinctive shape of the acorn tube made it easy to spot in old radio equipment.
Collectors sometimes seek out acorn tubes as rare examples of vacuum tube technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum tube - an electronic device that controls electric current through a vacuum in a sealed container

Etymology

Named for its resemblance in shape to an acorn.
