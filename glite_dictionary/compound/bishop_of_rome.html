Bishop of Rome

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪʃəp əv roʊm / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəp əv rəʊm / (BrE)
the pope; the head of the Roman Catholic Church
the pope, head of the Catholic Church

Examples

The Bishop of Rome leads Catholics throughout the world.
In history, many influential decisions have been made by the Bishop of Rome.
A new Bishop of Rome is chosen by the College of Cardinals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pope - the bishop of Rome and leader of the worldwide Catholic Church
  • Degree Lower: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, typically in charge of a diocese
