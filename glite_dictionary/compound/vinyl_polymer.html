vinyl polymer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪnəl pəˈlɪmər / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪnɪl ˈpɒlɪmə / (BrE)
a thermoplastic material formed by polymerizing compounds containing the vinyl group, used in a variety of plastics and resins
type of thermoplastic polymer

Examples

Most pipes in modern homes are made from vinyl polymer due to its durability.
Researchers developed a new vinyl polymer that is stronger and more flexible than previous materials.
The recycling process for vinyl polymer products requires special equipment to break them down safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyethylene - a common type of vinyl polymer used in plastics
  • Synonym: polystyrene - another example of a vinyl polymer used in packaging and containers

Etymology

From 'vinyl', from Latin 'vinum' (wine) due to the ethanol derivative, and 'polymer', from Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'meros' (part).
