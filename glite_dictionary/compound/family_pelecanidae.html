family Pelecanidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pɛˌlɛkəˈnaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pɛˌlɛkəˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large water birds comprising all species of pelicans.
biological family of pelicans

Examples

The family Pelecanidae is found on every continent except Antarctica.
All eight species in the family Pelecanidae are known for their large throat pouches.
Scientists recently studied the evolutionary history of the family Pelecanidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelican - large water bird with a distinctive pouch under its beak

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Pelecanidae' (pelican family), based on Greek 'pelekan' (pelican).
