Indian pony

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.di.ən ˈpoʊ.ni / (AmE), / ˈɪn.di.ən ˈpəʊ.ni / (BrE)
A small, hardy horse bred and used by Native Americans, especially in western North America.
small horse bred by Native Americans

Examples

The Indian pony played a vital role in the daily lives of many Plains tribes.
Her painting depicted an Indian pony galloping across the prairie.
Historians studied how the Indian pony changed Native American culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustang - wild or free-roaming horse of the western United States
