laser beam

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzər biːm / (AmE), / ˈleɪzə biːm / (BrE)
a narrow, intense beam of light produced by a laser device, typically coherent and monochromatic
intense light beam from a laser

Examples

The surgeon used a laser beam to make a precise incision.
You should never look directly into a laser beam because it can damage your eyes.
During the concert, colorful laser beams lit up the stage and thrilled the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laser - a device that emits a powerful beam of coherent light

Etymology

Formed from 'laser' (an acronym for 'light amplification by stimulated emission of radiation') + 'beam'.
