stamping ground

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæmpɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈstæmpɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a place that someone visits regularly or spends a lot of time in
a frequently visited place

Examples

The old café was his favorite stamping ground during college.
Artists have long made this vibrant district their stamping ground.
Every summer, the lake becomes our family's main stamping ground for relaxation and fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haunt - a place frequently visited by a particular person

Etymology

Originally from the practice of animals stamping the ground where they gathered; later used figuratively for human habitual gathering places.
