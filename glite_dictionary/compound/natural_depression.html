natural depression

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃɚəl dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəl dɪˈprɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a sunken or hollow area in the landscape, formed by natural geological processes
sunken geological formation

Examples

The lake formed in a natural depression left behind by a glacier thousands of years ago.
Farmers often find rich soil in the natural depression near the riverbed.
After the heavy rains, water collected in the natural depression in the center of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basin - a large or small natural depression in the surface of the land or in the ocean floor
