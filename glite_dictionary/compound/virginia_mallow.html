Virginia mallow

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪnjə ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪnɪə ˈmaləʊ / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, with maple-like leaves and white flowers
perennial herb with white flowers, US

Examples

The Virginia mallow grows wild along riverbanks in the southeast.
Gardeners prize Virginia mallow for its showy white blossoms and attractive leaves.
Several butterflies were seen feeding on the blossoms of the Virginia mallow in late summer.
