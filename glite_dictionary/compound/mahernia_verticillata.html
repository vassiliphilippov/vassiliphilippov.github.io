Mahernia verticillata

1. NOUN

/ məˈhɜr.ni.ə vərˌtɪs.ɪˈleɪ.tə / (AmE), / məˈhɜː.nɪ.ə vəˌtɪs.ɪˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
An African shrub with trailing stems and slender, yellow, honey-scented flowers, found singly or in pairs.
African shrub with honey-scented yellow flowers

Examples

Mahernia verticillata is often cultivated for its delicate yellow flowers and sweet scent.
Botanists discovered several new specimens of Mahernia verticillata in the South African shrublands.
The nursery specializes in rare plants such as Mahernia verticillata for enthusiasts and collectors.

Etymology

From the genus name Mahernia and species epithet verticillata in modern botanical Latin.
