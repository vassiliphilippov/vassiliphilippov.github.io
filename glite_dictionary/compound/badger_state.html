Badger State

1. PROPN

/ ˈbædʒɚ steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbædʒə steɪt / (BrE)
A nickname for the US state of Wisconsin, located in the north-central region of the Midwest.
nickname for Wisconsin (U.S. state)

Examples

Many tourists visit the Badger State to enjoy its lakes and forests.
She moved to the Badger State for a new job opportunity.
The Badger State is famous for its cheese production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wisconsin - A state in the north-central United States

Etymology

Refers to early lead miners in Wisconsin who lived in tunnels like badgers.
