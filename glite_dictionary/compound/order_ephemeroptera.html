order Ephemeroptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ɪˌfɛmərəˈptɛrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ɪˌfiːmərəˈptɛrə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of insects comprising the mayflies or shadflies, characterized by delicate transparent wings and aquatic larvae
order of mayflies/shadflies (insect order)

Examples

The order Ephemeroptera is known for species whose adults live only a few hours or days.
Biologists study the order Ephemeroptera to understand aquatic insect life cycles.
Larvae of the order Ephemeroptera are important indicators of water quality in rivers and streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - a slender, short-lived insect of the order Ephemeroptera

Etymology

From Greek *ephemeros* (short-lived) + *pteron* (wing), referring to the brief adult life stage and winged form.
