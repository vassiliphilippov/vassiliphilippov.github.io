family Astacidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæstəˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌæstəˈsaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater crayfish native to Europe and parts of western North America, forming part of the order Decapoda.
taxonomic family of freshwater crayfish

Examples

The family Astacidae contains several well-known species of European crayfish.
Researchers are studying diseases that affect members of the family Astacidae in freshwater habitats.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered crayfish within the family Astacidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - a freshwater crustacean of the family Astacidae

Etymology

From New Latin Astacidae, based on Astacus (the type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
