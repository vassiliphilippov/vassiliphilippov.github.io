home ground

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / həʊm ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
the natural environment where an organism or group normally lives or occurs; a familiar setting.
natural environment; habitat; familiar setting

Examples

The rainforest is the home ground of countless unique species.
She always performs best on her home ground where she feels confident.
The wolves returned to their home ground after several years away.

Synset Examples

a marine habitat
he felt safe on his home grounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitat - the natural home or environment of an organism

2. NOUN

/ hoʊm ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / həʊm ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
the field, stadium, or area where a sports team regularly plays or where someone feels most comfortable and confident
team's regular venue; familiar area

Examples

Our team rarely loses a match on its home ground.
She always feels more confident performing on her home ground.
The visiting players seemed nervous on the unfamiliar home ground of their opponents.

Synset Examples

The stadium is our team's home ground for the season.
He always plays better on his home ground.
She feels confident at her home ground during competitions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: away ground - a venue or field that is not one's regular or familiar location; the opposite of home ground
