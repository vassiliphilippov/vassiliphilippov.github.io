banksia rose

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋk.si.ə roʊz / (AmE), / ˈbaŋk.sɪ.ə rəʊz / (BrE)
A Chinese climbing rose with evergreen foliage, bearing yellow or white single flowers.
species of climbing rose with yellow or white flowers

Examples

The banksia rose climbs the garden wall, producing clusters of pale yellow flowers in spring.
She planted a banksia rose by the terrace to add color and fragrance to the backyard.
Unlike most roses, the banksia rose is nearly thornless and ideal for covering arches.

Etymology

Named after the English botanist Sir Joseph Banks (1743–1820) and 'rose'.
