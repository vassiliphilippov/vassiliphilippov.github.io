security review

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkjʊrəti rɪˈvju / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊərəti rɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
the act of examining information, documents, or communications to ensure that sensitive material is not disclosed to unauthorized parties, often for counterintelligence purposes
examination to prevent security leaks

Examples

The government conducted a security review before releasing the report to the public.
All outgoing emails must pass a security review to prevent any sensitive data leaks.
Following the breach, the company implemented a stricter security review process for internal communications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of information considered sensitive or harmful
