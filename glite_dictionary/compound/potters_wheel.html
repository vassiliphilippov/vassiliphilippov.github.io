potter's wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑt̬ɚz ˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈpɒtəz ˌwiːl / (BrE)
a machine with a flat, circular, horizontal rotating surface, used by potters to shape clay into pottery
rotating device for shaping clay

Examples

The artisan skillfully shaped the clay vase on the potter's wheel.
Learning to control the potter's wheel took hours of practice and patience.
Many traditional pottery workshops still use a manual potter's wheel for crafting ceramics.

Synset Examples

the potter's wheel was invented in Asia Minor around 6500 BC

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheel - circular object that rotates on an axle
  • Synonym: lathe - machine used to shape materials by rotating them
