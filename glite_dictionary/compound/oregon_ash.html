Oregon ash

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪɡən æʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən æʃ / (BrE)
a deciduous timber tree (Fraxinus latifolia) native to western North America, valued for its hard, light wood and closely related to the red ash.
a timber tree of western North America

Examples

The Oregon ash thrives in moist lowlands and along streams in its native range.
Wood from the Oregon ash is often used for furniture and sports equipment.
Unlike some other species, the Oregon ash can tolerate seasonal flooding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red ash - a species of ash tree closely related to Oregon ash
