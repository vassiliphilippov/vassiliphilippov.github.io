piggyback plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡiˌbæk plænt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡibæk plɑːnt / (BrE)
a vigorous perennial herb (Tolmiea menziesii) with flowers in upright racemes and young plants growing at the junction of the leaf blade and stalk
perennial herb with new plants growing on leaves

Examples

The piggyback plant is popular in gardens for its unique ability to grow new plants from its leaves.
She propagated a new piggyback plant by separating a plantlet from the mother.
The piggyback plant thrives in shaded indoor environments with moderate moisture.

Etymology

Named for the way new plants grow at leaf joints, appearing to 'piggyback' on the parent plant.
