Zaar language

1. NOUN

/ zɑːr ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / zɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken primarily in northern Nigeria, especially in Bauchi State's Tafawa Balewa area
Chadic language of northern Nigeria

Examples

The Zaar language is spoken by thousands of people in Bauchi State.
Linguists have documented the grammar of the Zaar language for preservation purposes.
She grew up bilingual, speaking both Hausa and the Zaar language at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chadic language - a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family
