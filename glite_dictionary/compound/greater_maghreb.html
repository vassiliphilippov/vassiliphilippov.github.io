Greater Maghreb

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡreɪtər mæˈɡrɛb / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə mæˈɡrɛb / (BrE)
a region of northwest Africa, including the Atlas Mountains and the coastal areas of Morocco, Algeria, and Tunisia
region of northwest Africa (Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia)

Examples

Many historians study the ancient civilizations that thrived in the Greater Maghreb region.
Trade across the Greater Maghreb has influenced the culture and languages of North Africa.
Environmental policies in the Greater Maghreb are shaped by its unique climate and geography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maghreb - the region of northwest Africa including Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, and sometimes Libya and Mauritania

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Maghrib' meaning 'the west' or 'place of sunset', referring to northwest Africa.
