jewels-of-opar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːəlz əv oʊˈpɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəlz əv ˈəʊpɑː / (BrE)
A tropical perennial plant with tuberous roots and clusters of small red to yellow flowers, native to southwestern North America and Central America and widely grown as an ornamental.
ornamental plant with small red or yellow flowers

Examples

The jewels-of-opar added a brilliant splash of red and yellow to the corner of the garden.
She enjoys collecting uncommon plants, and her latest addition is the vibrant jewels-of-opar.
Because jewels-of-opar can thrive in poor soil, they're often recommended for urban container gardening.

Etymology

The name combines 'jewels' (referring to the appearance of the flowers) with 'Opar', from the fictional lost city in Edgar Rice Burroughs's Tarzan novels.
