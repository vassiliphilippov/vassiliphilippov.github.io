genus Pterois

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ptɛˈrɔɪ.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ptɛˈrɔɪ.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous marine fishes known as lionfish, characterized by their striking stripes and long, spiny fins, native to the Indo-Pacific region.
a genus of venomous lionfishes

Examples

The genus Pterois is known for its spectacular, venomous fins and striking appearance.
Marine biologists study the spread of the genus Pterois in non-native waters.
Several species within the genus Pterois are considered invasive in the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lionfish - venomous fish with distinctive fins and stripes in the genus Pterois

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing, fin), referring to their wing-like fins.
