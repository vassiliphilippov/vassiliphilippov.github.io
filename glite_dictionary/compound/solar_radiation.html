solar radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the electromagnetic radiation emitted by the Sun, including visible light, ultraviolet, and infrared radiation, which reaches the Earth
radiation from the Sun

Examples

Many plants rely on solar radiation to carry out photosynthesis efficiently.
The Earth's atmosphere absorbs and reflects some of the solar radiation before it reaches the surface.
Measuring solar radiation is essential for designing efficient solar power systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunlight - light from the sun
  • Degree Lower: infrared radiation - electromagnetic radiation with longer wavelengths than visible light
  • Degree Higher: ultraviolet radiation - electromagnetic radiation with shorter wavelengths than visible light

Etymology

solar from Latin sol (sun) + radiation from Latin radiatio (a shining, radiation)
