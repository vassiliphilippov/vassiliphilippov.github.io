sandy mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændi ˈmʌʃruːm / (AmE), / ˈsandi ˈmʌʃrʊm / (BrE)
an edible mushroom that grows in clusters, especially in sandy soil under cottonwood trees
edible mushroom found in sandy soil

Examples

During our hike, we found several sandy mushrooms growing under the cottonwood trees.
The chef added chopped sandy mushrooms to the risotto for an earthy flavor.
Because sandy mushrooms thrive in loose, sandy soil, they're often found near riverbanks.
