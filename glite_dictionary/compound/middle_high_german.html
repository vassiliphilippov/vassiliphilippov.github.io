Middle High German

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl haɪ ˈdʒɝː.mən / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl haɪ ˈdʒɜː.mən / (BrE)
the form of the High German language used roughly from 1100 to 1500
historical stage of the German language (1100–1500)

Examples

Many epic poems were written in Middle High German during the medieval period.
Students of historical linguistics often study texts in Middle High German.
The Nibelungenlied is a famous work composed in Middle High German.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Old High German - the predecessor stage of High German before about 1100
  • Degree Higher: Early New High German - the transitional stage of High German after 1500

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl haɪ ˈdʒɜr.mən / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl haɪ ˈdʒɜː.mən / (BrE)
the form of the German language spoken and written in the High German area from about 1100 to 1500
historical stage of German (1100–1500)

Examples

Many medieval epics were originally composed in Middle High German.
Students of literature often need to translate texts from Middle High German.
The famous "Nibelungenlied" was written in Middle High German.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Old High German - earlier stage of High German, until about 1100
  • Degree Higher: Early New High German - later stage of High German, after about 1500

Etymology

Middle English, translation of German 'Mittelhochdeutsch': 'mittel' (middle) + 'hoch' (high) + 'deutsch' (German).
