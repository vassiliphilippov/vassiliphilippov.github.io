to organize union

1. VERB

/ ˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz ˈjunjən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪz ˈjuːnjən / (BrE)
to form or establish a labor union in order to represent and protect the interests of workers
form a labor union

Examples

The workers decided to organize union after repeated disputes with management.
She helped her colleagues organize union efforts across multiple departments.
Fearing unfair treatment, employees began to organize union meetings in secret.

Synset Examples

They organized a union to protect employee rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unionize - form or join a labor union; organize a union
