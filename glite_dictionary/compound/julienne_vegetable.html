julienne vegetable

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈliːən ˈvɛdʒtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʒuː.liˈɛn ˈvɛdʒ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
A vegetable that has been cut into thin, matchstick-like strips, typically for use in salads, garnishes, or cooking.
vegetable cut into thin strips

Examples

She added julienne vegetables to the stir-fry for extra color and texture.
The soup was garnished with a handful of fresh julienne vegetables.
Carefully preparing julienne vegetables gives the dish an elegant presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: julienne - to cut food into thin, matchstick strips

Etymology

From French 'julienne', a culinary term referring to thin strips, possibly named after a chef or derived from the French personal name 'Julienne'.
