rape card

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪp kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈreɪp kɑːd / (BrE)
an offensive and controversial term used to refer to the act of invoking one's experience of sexual assault, especially in debate or public settings, to seek sympathy, deflect criticism, or gain advantage
invoking sexual assault for sympathy or advantage
offensive

Examples

Some accused her of playing the rape card to influence public opinion.
The use of the rape card is widely condemned for minimizing real trauma.
Journalists cautioned against deploying the rape card in political debates.

Synset Examples

Using the 'rape card' trivializes real victims' experiences.
The phrase 'rape card' should never be used lightly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: race card - invoking race or accusations of racism for advantage
