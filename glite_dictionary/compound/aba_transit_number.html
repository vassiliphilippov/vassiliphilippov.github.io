ABA transit number

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.biːˈeɪ ˈtræn.sɪt ˌnʌm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.biːˈeɪ ˈtræn.zɪt ˌnʌm.bə / (BrE)
an identification number consisting of a two-part code assigned to banks and savings associations in the United States; the first part indicates the location and the second identifies the bank itself
US bank identification code

Examples

Every check issued by a bank in the United States shows its ABA transit number in the upper right corner.
To process wire transfers accurately, you need to provide the correct ABA transit number.
Many online payment forms will request your bank's ABA transit number to ensure funds are routed properly.

Etymology

ABA from the American Bankers Association, which developed the numbering system.
