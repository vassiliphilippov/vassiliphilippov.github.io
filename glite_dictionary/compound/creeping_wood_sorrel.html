creeping wood sorrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ wʊd ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ wʊd ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a small, mat-forming perennial weed with clover-like leaves and yellow flowers, found worldwide
a mat-forming perennial weed

Examples

The lawn was covered with patches of creeping wood sorrel after the spring rains.
Gardeners often struggle to control creeping wood sorrel because of its rapid growth.
You can recognize creeping wood sorrel by its clover-like leaves and small yellow flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxalis - genus of plants to which creeping wood sorrel belongs
