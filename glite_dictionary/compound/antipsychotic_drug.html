antipsychotic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.t̬i.saɪˈkɑː.tɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.saɪˈkɒ.tɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a type of tranquilizer used to treat psychotic conditions, having a calming effect
drug for treating psychosis
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antipsychotic drug to help manage her symptoms.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking an antipsychotic drug.
Research continues to improve the effectiveness of antipsychotic drugs with fewer adverse reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - drug used to manage psychosis

Etymology

anti- (against) + psychotic (relating to psychosis), from Greek psychosis.
