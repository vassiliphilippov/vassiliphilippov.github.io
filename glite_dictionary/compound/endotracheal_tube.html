endotracheal tube

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈtreɪkiəl tuːb / (AmE), / ˌɛndəˈtreɪkiəl tjuːb / (BrE)
A flexible tube inserted through the mouth or nose into the trachea to maintain an open airway, deliver oxygen, remove secretions, or prevent aspiration.
tube placed into the trachea to manage airway
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist inserted an endotracheal tube to secure the patient's airway before surgery.
Proper placement of the endotracheal tube was confirmed using a chest X-ray.
Doctors removed the endotracheal tube after the patient regained the ability to breathe independently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catheter - a thin tube inserted into the body for medical purposes

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'tracheia' (windpipe) + English 'tube'.
