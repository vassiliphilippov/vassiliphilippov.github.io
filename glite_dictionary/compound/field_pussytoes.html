field pussytoes

1. NOUN

/ fiːld ˈpʌsitoʊz / (AmE), / fiːld ˈpʌsitəʊz / (BrE)
a species of low-growing, white or pink-flowered wildflower (Antennaria neglecta), native to North America, commonly found in fields and open areas.
a wildflower species (Antennaria neglecta)

Examples

We spotted a patch of field pussytoes blooming along the edge of the meadow.
The soft, silvery leaves of field pussytoes make them easy to identify in the wild.
Gardeners sometimes plant field pussytoes for ground cover in sunny areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pussytoes - common name for various Antennaria species with soft, woolly flower heads
