angiotensin II

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒioʊtɛnˈsɪn tu / (AmE), / ˌandʒiəʊtɛnˈsɪn tuː / (BrE)
a peptide hormone formed from angiotensin I that acts as a powerful vasoconstrictor, increasing blood pressure
peptide hormone regulating blood pressure
medical

Examples

Angiotensin II is a key hormone involved in the regulation of blood pressure and fluid balance.
Doctors may use medications to block the effects of angiotensin II in patients with hypertension.
When blood volume drops, the body increases production of angiotensin II to constrict blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: angiotensin I - precursor peptide hormone converted into angiotensin II
  • Degree Lower: angiotensinogen - precursor protein from which angiotensin I and II are ultimately derived

Etymology

From 'angio-' (vessel) + 'tensin' (tension), indicating its role in blood vessel tension; 'II' denotes its subtype.
