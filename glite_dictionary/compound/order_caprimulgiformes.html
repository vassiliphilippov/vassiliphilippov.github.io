order Caprimulgiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər kæprɪˌmʌlʤɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌkæprɪˌmʌldʒɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of nocturnal birds including goatsuckers, frogmouths, and oilbirds
an order of nocturnal birds

Examples

The order Caprimulgiformes includes fascinating nocturnal birds such as nightjars and frogmouths.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of species within the order Caprimulgiformes.
Oilbirds, the only nocturnal fruit-eating birds, belong to the order Caprimulgiformes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caprimulgidae - the family including the goatsuckers or nightjars

Etymology

From Latin 'Caprimulgus' (goatsucker) + '-formes' (having the form of).
