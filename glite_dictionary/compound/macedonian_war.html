Macedonian War

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniən wɔr / (AmE), / ˌmæsɪˈdəʊniən wɔː / (BrE)
any of the four wars fought between Macedonia and Rome during the 3rd and 2nd centuries BC, which resulted in the defeat of Macedonia and its annexation by Rome.
wars between Macedonia and Rome (3rd–2nd centuries BC)

Examples

The defeat in the Macedonian War marked the end of Macedon's independence.
Historians still debate the causes and consequences of the Macedonian War.
After the last Macedonian War, the region became a Roman province.
