active immunity

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪv ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
Immunity that results from the production of antibodies by the immune system in response to the presence of an antigen.
immunity developed through body's own antibody production
medical

Examples

Vaccination is a common method of stimulating active immunity against various diseases.
Individuals who recover from an infection often develop active immunity to that particular pathogen.
The doctor explained that active immunity takes time to develop but usually lasts for years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passive immunity - immunity acquired by receiving antibodies from another source

Etymology

From Latin 'immunitas' (exemption), combined with 'active' to indicate the body's own response.
