stringybark pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪŋ.i.bɑrk paɪn / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋ.i.bɑːk paɪn / (BrE)
an Australian cypress pine tree with fibrous inner bark, especially of the genus Callitris
Australian cypress pine with fibrous bark

Examples

The stringybark pine is often used for timber and windbreaks on Australian farms.
Its distinctive fibrous bark makes the stringybark pine easy to identify in the wild.
Conservation efforts have increased to protect the native stringybark pine from deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Callitris - genus of Australian cypress pines
  • Synonym: cypress pine - common name for several trees in the genus Callitris

Etymology

From 'stringybark' (describing the bark's texture) and 'pine'.
