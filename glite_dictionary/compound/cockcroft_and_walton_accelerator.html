Cockcroft and Walton accelerator

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑk.krɑft ənd ˈwɔl.tən əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.krɒft ənd ˈwɒl.tən əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a type of high-voltage machine that uses rectifiers and capacitors to accelerate charged particles through a tube
particle accelerator using rectifiers and capacitors

Examples

The laboratory installed a Cockcroft and Walton accelerator to conduct new experiments in nuclear physics.
A Cockcroft and Walton accelerator uses a series of capacitors and rectifiers to generate high voltages.
Many early research facilities relied on the Cockcroft and Walton accelerator to provide beams of accelerated particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Van de Graaff generator - a type of electrostatic particle accelerator

Etymology

Named after physicists John Cockcroft and Ernest Walton, who invented the accelerator in the 1930s.
