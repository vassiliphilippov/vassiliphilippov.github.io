yellow adder's tongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈædərz tʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈædəz tʌŋ / (BrE)
A North American plant (Erythronium americanum) of the lily family, having solitary yellow flowers marked with brown or purple and spotted interiors.
North American plant with yellow, marked flowers

Examples

While hiking in the spring, we spotted several yellow adder's tongue blooming by the creek.
The botanist collected a specimen of yellow adder's tongue for her research.
Many gardeners appreciate yellow adder's tongue for its delicate yellow flowers and attractive foliage.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the plant's flower or leaves to a snake's tongue, with 'adder' meaning 'snake' in archaic English.
