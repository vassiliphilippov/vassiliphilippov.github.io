Boston ivy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑstən ˈaɪvi / (AmE), / ˈbɒstən ˈaɪvi / (BrE)
a climbing Asiatic vine (Parthenocissus tricuspidata) with three-lobed leaves and purple berries, often grown as an ornamental plant on buildings
climbing ornamental vine with three-lobed leaves

Examples

The old brick buildings were covered in Boston ivy, turning red in the autumn.
She planted Boston ivy along the fence for a touch of greenery.
Boston ivy is a favorite among gardeners for its ability to quickly cover walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia creeper - a North American climbing plant similar to Boston ivy
