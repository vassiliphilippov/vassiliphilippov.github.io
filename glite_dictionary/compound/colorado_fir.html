Colorado fir

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːləˈrædoʊ fɝ / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˈrædəʊ fɜː / (BrE)
a medium to tall fir tree native to central and western United States, known for its narrow upright crown and soft wood
a fir tree of the western US

Examples

The forest was dominated by tall Colorado fir trees reaching towards the sky.
Soft wood from the Colorado fir is often used in construction and paper production.
Researchers studied the resilience of Colorado fir in regions affected by drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - an evergreen coniferous tree with needle-shaped leaves
