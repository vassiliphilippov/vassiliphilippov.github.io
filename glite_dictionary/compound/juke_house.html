juke house

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːk haʊs / (AmE), / dʒuːk haʊs / (BrE)
a small, informal establishment in the southeastern United States where people can eat, drink, and dance to music from a jukebox
Southern establishment with food, drink, and dancing

Examples

We spent Saturday night at the local juke house, dancing and listening to blues.
That old juke house at the crossroads serves the most amazing fried catfish.
Many famous musicians got their start performing in a juke house before making it big.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juke joint - an informal bar with music and dancing, common in the southern US

Etymology

From 'juke' (from Gullah, meaning disorderly or rowdy) and 'house'.
