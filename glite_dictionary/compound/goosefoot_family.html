goosefoot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːsfʊt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsfʊt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including spinach and beets, formerly classified as Chenopodiaceae
plant family including spinach and beets

Examples

The goosefoot family contains many edible plants, such as spinach and beets.
Many weeds found in gardens also belong to the goosefoot family.
Plants in the goosefoot family are often tolerant of saline soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chenopodiaceae - the botanical family formerly known as the goosefoot family
  • Synonym: Amaranthaceae - the plant family now including the former goosefoot family

Etymology

So named due to the characteristic shape of the leaves in some species resembling a goose's foot.
