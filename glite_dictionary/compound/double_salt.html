double salt

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl sɑlt / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl sɒlt / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed when two different simple salts crystallize together as a single substance, retaining some properties of both but existing as a distinct entity
chemical compound formed from two salts

Examples

Alum is a common example of a double salt used in water purification.
When dissolved in water, a double salt can break down into its individual ions.
The chemist prepared a double salt by crystallizing two simple salts together.
