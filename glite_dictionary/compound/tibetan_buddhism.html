Tibetan Buddhism

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈbɛt̬.ən ˈbʊd.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / tɪˈbɛt.ən ˈbʊd.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
A form of Buddhism practiced in Tibet and neighboring regions, incorporating Indian Buddhist teachings and elements of native Tibetan religion.
major branch of Buddhism in Tibet

Examples

Many Westerners travel to Tibet to learn about Tibetan Buddhism and its meditation practices.
The Dalai Lama is one of the most prominent leaders of Tibetan Buddhism.
Art and architecture influenced by Tibetan Buddhism can be found in monasteries across the Himalayan region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhism - A religion based on the teachings of the Buddha
