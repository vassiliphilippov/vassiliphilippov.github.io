intramuscular injection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈmʌskjələr ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈmʌskjʊlə ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
An injection administered directly into a muscle, typically to deliver medication quickly into the bloodstream.
injection directly into a muscle
medical

Examples

The flu vaccine is usually given as an intramuscular injection in the upper arm.
After the accident, she received an intramuscular injection of pain medication.
Some antibiotics are administered via intramuscular injection for faster absorption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subcutaneous injection - an injection into the tissue beneath the skin
  • Synonym: intravenous injection - an injection directly into a vein

Etymology

From Latin 'intra-' (within) and 'muscular' (of the muscles), combined with 'injection' (from Latin 'injectio').
