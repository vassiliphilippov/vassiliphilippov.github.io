coquilles Saint-Jacques

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkiːl sæ̃ ˈʒɑk / (AmE), / kɒˈkiːl sæ̃ ˈʒak / (BrE)
A French dish of scallops baked in a creamy white wine sauce, typically served in scallop shells.
scallops in white wine sauce, served in shells

Examples

She ordered the coquilles Saint-Jacques as her appetizer at the French bistro.
The chef prepared his signature coquilles Saint-Jacques with a rich, buttery sauce.
I followed a traditional recipe to make coquilles Saint-Jacques for our dinner party.

Etymology

French, meaning 'shells Saint James', referring to the scallop shell symbol of Saint James.
