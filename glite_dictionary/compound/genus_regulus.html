genus Regulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈrɛɡ.jʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈrɛɡ.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the family Sylviidae, which includes the kinglets.
genus of birds including kinglets

Examples

The genus Regulus comprises several species of kinglets found across Europe, Asia, and North America.
Recent studies have helped clarify the evolutionary history of birds in the genus Regulus.
Birdwatchers often delight in spotting members of the genus Regulus due to their vibrant colors and energetic behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinglet - a small, active songbird of the genus Regulus

Etymology

From Latin 'regulus', meaning 'little king', used in zoological nomenclature for kinglets.
