short temper

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
the tendency to become angry quickly or easily; a disposition to lose patience readily
tendency to become angry quickly

Examples

He is known for his short temper and often loses his cool in stressful situations.
Her short temper made it difficult for her to work with others who disagreed with her.
If you have a short temper, it's important to find healthy ways to manage frustration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irascibility - the quality of being easily angered
  • Antonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay or trouble without getting angry

/ ʃɔrt ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
