black squirrel

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈskwɝː.əl / (AmE), / blæk ˈskwɪr.əl / (BrE)
a fox squirrel or gray squirrel that has black fur due to a genetic color variation
squirrel with black fur (color variant)

Examples

A black squirrel darted across the park path, startling a group of joggers.
Several black squirrels can be seen gathering acorns in the fall.
The presence of a black squirrel in the neighborhood is considered unusual by many local residents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gray squirrel - a squirrel with gray fur, especially Sciurus carolinensis or Sciurus griseus
  • Degree Higher: albino squirrel - a squirrel with white fur due to lack of pigment
