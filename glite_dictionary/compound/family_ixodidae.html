family Ixodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɪkˈsɑdəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɪkˈsɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of hard-bodied ticks that includes many species of parasites found on animals and humans.
family of hard ticks

Examples

Scientists studied several species from the family Ixodidae to understand tick-borne diseases.
The family Ixodidae is responsible for transmitting Lyme disease to humans.
Veterinarians must be aware of the family Ixodidae when treating pets for tick infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ixodes - A genus of hard ticks

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ixodidae', based on the type genus 'Ixodes' and the suffix '-idae' (denoting a zoological family).
