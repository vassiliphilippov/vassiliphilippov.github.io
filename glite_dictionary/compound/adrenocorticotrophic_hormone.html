adrenocorticotrophic hormone

1. NOUN

/ əˌdrinoʊˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈtroʊfɪk ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / əˌdriːnəʊˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈtrɒfɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the adrenal cortex to secrete corticosteroids
hormone from anterior pituitary stimulating adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to measure adrenocorticotrophic hormone levels in order to diagnose the patient's symptoms.
Elevated adrenocorticotrophic hormone can indicate certain types of adrenal gland disorders.
Researchers are studying how adrenocorticotrophic hormone affects the body's response to stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ACTH - abbreviation for adrenocorticotrophic hormone
  • Synonym: corticotropin - another term for adrenocorticotrophic hormone

Etymology

From adreno- (adrenal), cortico- (cortex), trophic (growth-stimulating), and hormone.
