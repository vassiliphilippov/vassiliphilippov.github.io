bottle grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑt̬l ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbɒtl ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European species of foxtail grass, now naturalized in North America, often considered a troublesome weed.
a European foxtail grass species, a weed

Examples

The farmer struggled to control the spread of bottle grass in his wheat fields.
Bottle grass can quickly invade disturbed areas, making it difficult for native plants to establish.
Many gardeners find bottle grass particularly persistent once it takes root in a lawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail - a type of grass with bushy seed heads resembling a fox's tail
