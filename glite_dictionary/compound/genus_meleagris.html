genus Meleagris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs məˈliːəɡrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs mɛliˈaːɡrɪs / (BrE)
The genus of birds that includes wild and domestic turkeys.
genus of turkeys

Examples

The genus Meleagris contains both the wild turkey and its domesticated descendants.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the genus Meleagris to learn more about bird domestication.
The most well-known member of the genus Meleagris is the North American turkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turkey - large bird native to North America, domesticated for food

Etymology

From Latin *Meleagris*, meaning 'guinea fowl', used in scientific naming.
