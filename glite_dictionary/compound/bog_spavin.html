bog spavin

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ ˈspævɪn / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈspævɪn / (BrE)
A swelling of the hock joint in horses, caused by the accumulation of fluid.
swelling of a horse's hock joint due to fluid

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with bog spavin after noticing swelling in the hock joint.
Proper management can help reduce the symptoms of bog spavin in show horses.
Owners are advised to consult a vet if their horse shows signs of bog spavin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spavin - swelling of a horse's joint

Etymology

From 'bog' (in the sense of soft swelling) and 'spavin' (from Old French *espavain*, swelling in horses).
