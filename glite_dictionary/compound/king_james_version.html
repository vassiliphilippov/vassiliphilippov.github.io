King James Version

1. PROPN

/ kɪŋ dʒeɪmz ˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / kɪŋ dʒeɪmz ˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the most famous English translation of the Bible, first published in 1611 under the commission of King James I of England
English Bible translation (1611)

Examples

Many literary works quote the King James Version for its poetic language.
She received a beautifully bound King James Version as a confirmation gift.
Theologians often compare modern translations to the King James Version for accuracy and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Authorized Version - another name for the King James Version of the Bible

Etymology

Named after King James I of England, who commissioned the translation.
