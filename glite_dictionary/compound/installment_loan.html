installment loan

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɔlmənt loʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɑːlmənt ləʊn / (BrE)
A loan that is repaid with interest through a series of regular, equal payments over a set period of time.
loan repaid in regular payments

Examples

He decided to take out an installment loan to pay for his new car.
With an installment loan, you make equal payments every month until the debt is cleared.
Many people prefer an installment loan because it offers predictable payment schedules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortgage - a type of loan secured by real property, repaid in installments
  • Antonym: revolving credit - a type of credit that is not repaid in fixed payments
