intestinal obstruction

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl əbˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstɪnl əbˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
A blockage in the intestine that prevents the normal passage of its contents, especially affecting the ileum.
blockage of the intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon diagnosed an intestinal obstruction after reviewing the patient's symptoms and test results.
If left untreated, an intestinal obstruction can lead to serious complications such as tissue death and infection.
Severe abdominal pain and vomiting are common signs of intestinal obstruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowel obstruction - blockage of the bowel that prevents passage of contents
