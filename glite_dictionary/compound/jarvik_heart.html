Jarvik heart

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑrvɪk hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːvɪk hɑːt / (BrE)
A type of artificial heart used to replace a human heart, named after its inventor, Dr. Robert Jarvik.
type of artificial heart
medical

Examples

The Jarvik heart was first implanted in a patient in 1982, revolutionizing cardiac surgery.
Patients with a Jarvik heart require careful medical monitoring after the operation.
Research continues to improve the long-term performance of the Jarvik heart and other artificial hearts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial heart - a mechanical device that replaces the heart

Etymology

Named after Dr. Robert Jarvik, the American scientist who led its development in the late 20th century.
