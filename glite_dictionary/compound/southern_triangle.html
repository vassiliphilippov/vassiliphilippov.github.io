Southern Triangle

1. PROPN

/ ˈsʌðɚn ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈtraɪæŋɡl / (BrE)
a small bright constellation in the southern celestial hemisphere, located near Circinus and Apus
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

The Southern Triangle is best viewed from the Southern Hemisphere during the winter months.
Astronomers use the Southern Triangle as a reference point for locating nearby constellations.
We identified three bright stars forming the Southern Triangle in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Triangulum - a small northern constellation, also called the Triangle
