cloven hoof

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊvən hʊf / (AmE), / ˈkləʊvən hʊf / (BrE)
A hoof split into two parts at the front, as found in animals such as cattle, sheep, or pigs.
a split hoof found in some animals

Examples

The goat's cloven hoof left a clear print in the soft mud.
Many ruminant animals, like cows and sheep, have a cloven hoof.
Swine are notable for their distinctive cloven hoof, which sets them apart from animals like horses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid hoof - an undivided, single hoof as in horses

Etymology

From 'cloven,' the past participle of 'cleave' (to split or divide).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊ.vən huːf / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.vən huːf / (BrE)
A split hoof; in mythology and popular culture, a symbol or mark associated with the Devil or Satan.
the mark of Satan, split hoof

Examples

Many believed that the witch's birthmark was a cloven hoof and proof of her pact with the Devil.
In old folktales, the Devil often appears with a cloven hoof instead of a human foot.
Superstitions in some cultures hold that a cloven hoof is the unmistakable sign of evil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil's mark - a distinguishing mark supposedly left by the Devil
  • Degree Lower: hoof - the horny part of an animal's foot

Etymology

From Old English 'clofen' (split) and 'hof' (hoof); 'cloven hoof' became associated with the Devil in medieval European folklore.
