herpes varicella zoster virus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːpiz vəˌrɪsələ ˈzɑstɚ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz vəˌrɪsɪlə ˈzɒstə ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a herpes virus that causes chickenpox and shingles in humans
the virus causing chickenpox and shingles
medical

Examples

The herpes varicella zoster virus is responsible for both chickenpox in children and shingles in adults.
After recovering from chickenpox, the herpes varicella zoster virus can remain dormant in the body for years.
Doctors confirmed that her painful rash was caused by the herpes varicella zoster virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varicella-zoster virus - another name for the herpes virus causing chickenpox and shingles

Etymology

From Greek 'herpēs' (creeping), Latin 'varicella' (diminutive of 'variola', smallpox), and 'zoster' (belt, referring to shingles).
