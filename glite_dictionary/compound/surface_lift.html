surface lift

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːfɪs lɪft / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs lɪft / (BrE)
a ski tow that pulls skiers up a slope without lifting them off the ground
ski lift pulling riders without leaving the ground

Examples

The beginner slope has a surface lift that's easy for new skiers to use.
Many ski resorts have replaced old surface lifts with chairlifts for added comfort.
Children often prefer the surface lift because it moves slowly up the gentle hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairlift - a ski lift with chairs suspended above the ground
  • Synonym: gondola - an enclosed ski lift cabin suspended from a cable
