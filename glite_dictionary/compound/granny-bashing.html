granny-bashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræni ˌbæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrani ˌbaʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Mocking, criticizing, or disparaging elderly people, sometimes involving abuse or prejudice.
mocking or criticizing elderly people

Examples

The media was accused of granny-bashing after publishing ageist remarks.
Many advocate for stronger laws to prevent granny-bashing in care homes.
She spoke out against the culture of granny-bashing on social media.

Synset Examples

Granny-bashing jokes are hurtful and should stop.
The article criticized the trend of granny-bashing in media.
They condemned the granny-bashing culture in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ageism - prejudice or discrimination against elderly people
  • Antonym: respect - polite regard and consideration for someone

Etymology

Formed from 'granny' (informal for grandmother) and 'bashing' (verbally attacking).
