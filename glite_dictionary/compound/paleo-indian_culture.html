Paleo-Indian culture

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˈɪndiən ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˈɪndiən ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the culture of the earliest human inhabitants of North and South America during the late Pleistocene period, characterized by distinctive stone tools and a hunter-gatherer lifestyle
prehistoric culture of earliest Americans

Examples

Many archaeologists study the Paleo-Indian culture to understand the migration patterns of early humans in the Americas.
Artifacts from the Paleo-Indian culture have been found across both North and South America.
The use of distinctive spear points is a hallmark of the Paleo-Indian culture.

Etymology

From 'paleo-', meaning 'ancient', combined with 'Indian', denoting indigenous peoples of the Americas.
