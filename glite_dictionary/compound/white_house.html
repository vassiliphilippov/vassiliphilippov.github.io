White House

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
the executive branch and official residence of the President of the United States, often used metonymically to refer to the administration or its actions
U.S. president's residence; executive branch (metonym)

Examples

The White House announced new policies on climate change today.
Congress is waiting for the White House to respond to the proposed legislation.
Journalists gathered outside the White House for the daily briefing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administration - the government in power, especially in the United States
  • Synonym: Oval Office - the official office of the President of the United States in the White House

2. PROPN

/ ˈwaɪt ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
the government building in Washington, D.C. that serves as the official residence and workplace of the President of the United States
residence and office of the US President

Examples

The President addressed the nation from the White House last night.
Hundreds of tourists visit the White House each day to learn about its history.
A press conference was scheduled at the White House following the summit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Capitol - the building where the US Congress meets

Etymology

Named for its white-painted neoclassical facade; the term has been used since the early 19th century.
