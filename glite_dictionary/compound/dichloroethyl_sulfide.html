dichloroethyl sulfide

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌklɔːroʊˌɛθəl ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / daɪˌklɔːrəʊˌiːθɪl ˈsʌlfʌɪd / (BrE)
a highly toxic chemical compound, also known as mustard gas, used as a chemical weapon; causes severe blisters and damages eyes and respiratory system
toxic chemical warfare agent (mustard gas)

Examples

Even a small release of dichloroethyl sulfide can have devastating effects on people and the environment.
The use of dichloroethyl sulfide during World War I led to countless injuries among soldiers.
Researchers are developing better protective clothing to shield against dichloroethyl sulfide exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard gas - chemical warfare agent known for causing severe blisters and lung damage

Etymology

From the chemical constituents: di- (two), chloro- (chlorine), ethyl, and sulfide.
