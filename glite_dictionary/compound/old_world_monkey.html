Old World monkey

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊld ˈwɝld ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈwɜːld ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
any of a group of monkeys native to Africa and Asia, characterized by nonprehensile tails and closely set nostrils
monkey native to Africa or Asia with nonprehensile tail

Examples

The baboon is an example of an Old World monkey found in Africa.
Unlike New World monkeys, Old World monkeys have tails that are not adapted for grasping objects.
Scientists study the social behavior of Old World monkeys to understand primate evolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: New World monkey - monkey native to Central or South America, often with prehensile tails

Etymology

From the classification of monkeys in the Old World (Africa, Europe, Asia), as distinct from the New World (Americas).
