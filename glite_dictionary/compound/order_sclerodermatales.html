order Sclerodermatales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər skleˌroʊdərˈmeɪtəliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə skleˌrəʊdəmɑːˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi characterized by a peridium enclosing a gleba, sometimes classified under the subclass Homobasidiomycetes.
order of fungi with peridium and gleba
medical

Examples

Many earthball fungi are classified within the order Sclerodermatales by mycologists.
The order Sclerodermatales is distinguished from other fungal orders by its tough outer layer.
Recent studies have debated whether the order Sclerodermatales should remain in its current subclass.

Etymology

From New Latin: Sclerodermat-, from Greek 'skleros' (hard) + 'derma' (skin) + '-ales' (order suffix in taxonomy).
