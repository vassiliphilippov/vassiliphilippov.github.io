forest red gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrɪst rɛd ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪst rɛd ɡʌm / (BrE)
a tall eucalyptus tree (Eucalyptus tereticornis) native to Queensland, New South Wales, and Victoria, known for its hard timber and smooth bark
a tall Australian eucalyptus tree

Examples

The wood from the forest red gum is highly valued for its strength and durability.
Many koalas feed on the leaves of the forest red gum in eastern Australia.
Planting a forest red gum can help provide habitat for native wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucalyptus tereticornis - scientific name of the forest red gum
