philharmonic pitch

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪlhɑrˈmɑnɪk pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌfɪlhɑːˈmɒnɪk pɪtʃ / (BrE)
the standard pitch used to tune musical instruments for concert performances, typically assigning 440 hertz to the A above middle C.
standard musical tuning pitch (usually A = 440 Hz)

Examples

Most orchestras today tune their instruments to philharmonic pitch before performances.
The violinist adjusted her strings to match the philharmonic pitch of A at 440 Hz.
Disagreements about philharmonic pitch have occurred in music history, with different standards in various eras.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concert pitch - the standard pitch to which instruments are tuned for concert performance

Etymology

From 'philharmonic' (relating to a musical society) and 'pitch' (musical frequency).
