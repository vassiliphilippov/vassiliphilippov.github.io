threshold level

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊld ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃ.həʊld ˈlɛv.əl / (BrE)
the minimum intensity or value of a stimulus that is just detectable by the senses or instruments
minimum detectable intensity

Examples

The noise was just above the threshold level and could be heard by most participants.
Doctors monitor the threshold level of pain to determine effective treatments.
The threshold level for light detection varies among different species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshold - the point or level at which something begins or changes; especially in sensory perception
  • Synonym: limen - the point at which a stimulus is strong enough to produce a response
