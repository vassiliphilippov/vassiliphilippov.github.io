ell-wand

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlˌwɑnd / (AmE), / ˈɛlˌwɒnd / (BrE)
A rod of one ell in length, historically used as an official standard of measurement.
historical measuring rod
archaic

Examples

The town's officials displayed the ell-wand in the marketplace to measure cloth accurately.
During medieval times, the ell-wand served as an official reference for traders and merchants.
The guild insisted that all measurements be taken with a certified ell-wand.

Synset Examples

Edward I of England required that every town has at least one ell-wand.

Etymology

From 'ell', an old unit of length (from Old English 'eln'), and 'wand', meaning rod.
