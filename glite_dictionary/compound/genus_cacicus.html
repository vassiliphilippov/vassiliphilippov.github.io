genus Cacicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈsiːkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈsiːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orioles, belonging to the family Icteridae, known for their bright plumage and gregarious nesting habits.
genus of tropical American orioles

Examples

The nests of the genus Cacicus hang like woven baskets from tree branches in the rainforest.
Several species within the genus Cacicus are known for their elaborate vocalizations.
Ornithologists studied the social structures of birds in the genus Cacicus during their fieldwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a colorful passerine bird, often yellow and black, found in the Old and New Worlds

Etymology

From Latin *cacicus*, meaning 'chieftain', also the Spanish word for an indigenous tribal chief; adopted as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.
