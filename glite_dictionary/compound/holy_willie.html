Holy Willie

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈwɪli / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈwɪli / (BrE)
A person who is hypocritically self-righteous or piously judgmental.
hypocritically pious or self-righteous person

Examples

Don't be such a Holy Willie; nobody likes being constantly judged.
Ever since she joined the committee, she's become the group's Holy Willie.
He was known as a Holy Willie for his holier-than-thou attitude.

Synset Examples

He acted like a Holy Willie, judging everyone around him.
That Holy Willie never misses a chance to preach.
Her strict views made her the Holy Willie of the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prig - a self-righteously moralistic person
  • Synonym: sanctimonious - making a show of being morally superior
  • Synonym: hypocrite - a person pretending to have virtues they do not possess

Etymology

Refers to the character 'Holy Willie' in the 1785 satirical poem 'Holy Willie's Prayer' by Robert Burns.
