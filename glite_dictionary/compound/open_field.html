open field

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən fiːld / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən fiːld / (BrE)
an area of land that is unobstructed by buildings, trees, or other barriers
unobstructed area of land

Examples

Cows grazed peacefully in the open field behind their farmhouse.
After a week in the city, she longed for the quiet of an open field.
The soccer team practiced drills on the open field away from the main stadium.

Synset Examples

The kids ran freely in the open field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow - a field of grass, especially one used for hay
  • Antonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
