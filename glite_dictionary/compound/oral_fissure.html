oral fissure

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
the opening of the mouth through which food is taken in and sounds emerge
the opening of the mouth
medical

Examples

The dentist examined the oral fissure for signs of infection or abnormality.
In some developmental disorders, the oral fissure may be abnormally shaped or positioned.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the tissues around the oral fissure.

Synset Examples

he stuffed his mouth with candy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouth - the opening in the lower part of the human face

Etymology

From Latin 'os' (mouth) and 'fissura' (cleft, opening).
