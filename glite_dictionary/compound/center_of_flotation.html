center of flotation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntər əv floʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv fləʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the point on the waterline of a floating object through which all changes of the waterplane pass as the vessel trims, considered the center of gravity of the waterplane area
center of gravity of a floating object's waterplane

Examples

When the ship's load was adjusted, engineers recalculated the center of flotation to ensure stability.
The center of flotation moves as cargo is redistributed along the deck.
Accurately knowing the center of flotation helps in designing safer vessels.
