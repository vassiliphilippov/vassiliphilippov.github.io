American twinflower

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈtwɪnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈtwɪnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant of the species Linnaea borealis found in North America, similar to the twinflower of northern Europe and Asia.
North American plant, similar to twinflower

Examples

The American twinflower carpets the forest floor with its delicate pink blossoms each spring.
Botanists study the differences between the European twinflower and the American twinflower.
Hikers were delighted to spot a patch of American twinflower along the woodland trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twinflower - Small creeping evergreen subshrub with aromatic leaves and pairs of pinkish flowers; Linnaea borealis
