suborder Alcyonacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌɔrdɚ ˌæl.si.əˈneɪ.si.ə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːdə ˌæl.sɪ.əˈneɪ.sɪə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of marine cnidarians comprising the soft corals.
taxonomic group of soft corals
medical

Examples

The suborder Alcyonacea includes a wide variety of vividly colored marine organisms found in coral reefs.
Scientists study the suborder Alcyonacea to better understand coral biodiversity and reef health.
Many aquarium enthusiasts seek out members of the suborder Alcyonacea for their unique beauty and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft coral - marine corals with fleshy, flexible bodies, members of Alcyonacea

Etymology

From New Latin 'Alcyonacea', from Greek 'alkyon', meaning 'kingfisher', possibly referring to the bright colors of some species.
