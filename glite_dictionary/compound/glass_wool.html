glass wool

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs wʊl / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs wʊl / (BrE)
a mass of fine glass fibers spun together to resemble wool, used as an insulating material
fibrous glass insulation material

Examples

The attic was insulated with glass wool to keep the house warm in winter.
Workers wore protective gear while installing the glass wool panels.
Glass wool is commonly used in construction for thermal and acoustic insulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiberglass - a material made from extremely fine glass fibers; often used as insulation or reinforcement
