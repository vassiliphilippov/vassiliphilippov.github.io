atlas moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈætləs mɔθ / (AmE), / ˈatləs mɒθ / (BrE)
A very large Asian moth (Attacus atlas) known for its impressive size and patterned wings, sometimes cultivated for its silk.
very large Asian moth

Examples

The atlas moth is one of the largest moths in the world, with wingspans reaching up to 25 centimeters.
She visited a butterfly house and was amazed by the bright colors of the atlas moth.
In some regions, the atlas moth is raised for its strong silk, which is used in textiles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: moth - an insect related to the butterfly, generally nocturnal
  • Synonym: silkworm - larva of a silk-producing moth, especially Bombyx mori
