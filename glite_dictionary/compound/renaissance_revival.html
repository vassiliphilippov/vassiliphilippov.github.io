Renaissance revival

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈneɪsəns rɪˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / rɪˈneɪsəns rɪˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
A 19th-century architectural style that draws inspiration from a variety of classic Italian Renaissance forms, distinct from Grecian or Gothic revivals.
19th-century architectural style based on Italian Renaissance

Examples

Many public buildings in the 19th century were constructed in the Renaissance revival style, emphasizing grandeur and symmetry.
The Renaissance revival movement influenced architects throughout Europe and North America.
His library was designed according to Renaissance revival principles, featuring elaborate columns and arches.

Synset Examples

By the time Italy had developed its obsession with the neo-Renaissance in the 1870s, collectors and scholars in the rest of Europe had been excited by Renaissance taste and style for several decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neo-Renaissance - Another term for Renaissance revival, particularly emphasizing the newer adaptation of Renaissance styles

Etymology

From Renaissance (French for 'rebirth', from Italian rinascimento) + revival (meaning a renewed interest in or use of something).
