Chief Constable

1. NOUN

/ tʃif ˈkɑnstəbəl / (AmE), / tʃiːf ˈkɒnstəbl / (BrE)
the head of the police force in a county or similar administrative area, especially in the United Kingdom
head of a county police force (UK)
british

Examples

The Chief Constable announced a new initiative to tackle crime in the region.
She was promoted to Chief Constable after decades of dedicated police service.
The meeting was attended by the Chief Constable and other senior officers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police chief - the person in charge of a city police department

Etymology

Chief: from Old French 'chef' (head); Constable: from Latin 'comes stabuli' (count of the stable), via Old French.
