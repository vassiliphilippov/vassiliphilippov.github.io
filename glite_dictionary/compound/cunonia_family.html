cunonia family

1. NOUN

/ kuˈnoʊniə ˌfæməli / (AmE), / kjuˈnəʊnɪə ˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of trees, shrubs, or climbers, mainly found in the Southern Hemisphere, known scientifically as Cunoniaceae.
botanical family of mainly Southern Hemisphere trees and shrubs

Examples

The cunonia family includes many evergreen trees native to the Southern Hemisphere.
Scientists discovered a new species within the cunonia family in New Zealand's rainforests.
Several garden shrubs belong to the cunonia family due to their attractive flowers and foliage.

Etymology

From the scientific genus name Cunonia, which is the type genus of the family, combined with 'family'.
