pteroylglutamic acid

1. NOUN

/ təˌrɔɪəlˌɡluːˈtæmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / təˌrɔɪəlˌɡluːˈtæmɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a B vitamin, also known as folic acid, essential for cell growth and reproduction
B vitamin for cell growth (folic acid)
medical

Examples

Many prenatal vitamins contain pteroylglutamic acid to support healthy fetal development.
A deficiency in pteroylglutamic acid can lead to certain types of anemia.
Researchers studied the effects of pteroylglutamic acid supplementation on rapid cell division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folic acid - a B vitamin essential for healthy growth (synonym)

Etymology

From 'pteroyl-' (from pterin, a chemical compound group) and 'glutamic acid', reflecting its chemical structure.
