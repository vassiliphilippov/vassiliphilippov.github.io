latent heat

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪtənt hiːt / (AmE), / ˈleɪtənt hiːt / (BrE)
heat absorbed or released during a change of phase of a substance at constant temperature and pressure, such as during melting or boiling
heat in a phase change (constant temperature)
medical

Examples

When water boils, it absorbs latent heat without increasing its temperature.
The concept of latent heat explains why sweating cools the body as sweat evaporates.
Scientists measured the latent heat released during the freezing of the solution.

Etymology

From Latin 'latens' (lying hidden) + English 'heat'.
