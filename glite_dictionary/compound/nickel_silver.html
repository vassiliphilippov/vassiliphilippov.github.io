nickel silver

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkəl ˈsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
a silver-white alloy consisting mainly of copper, zinc, and nickel, often used for decorative objects and tableware
a silver-white alloy of copper, zinc, and nickel

Examples

The cutlery set was made of nickel silver, giving it a bright, silvery sheen.
Many musical instruments use nickel silver for their durability and aesthetic appeal.
Despite its name, nickel silver does not actually contain any real silver.

Etymology

Named for its silvery appearance, not its silver content; earlier known as German silver.
