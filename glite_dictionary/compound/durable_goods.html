durable goods

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊrəbəl ɡʊdz / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəbəl ɡʊdz / (BrE)
goods intended for long-term use and not consumed quickly when used, such as appliances, cars, or furniture.
goods lasting for a long time

Examples

Household appliances like refrigerators and washing machines are classified as durable goods.
The demand for durable goods often rises when consumer confidence is high.
Cars, computers, and furniture are all examples of durable goods purchased by families.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consumable - goods that are intended to be used up quickly
