Massachusetts language

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsətʃuːsɪts ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmæsətʃuːsɪts ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Algonquian language formerly spoken by the Massachusett people of eastern Massachusetts
extinct Algonquian language of the Massachusett people

Examples

The Massachusetts language played a crucial role in the early missionary efforts in New England.
Some early colonial documents were translated into the Massachusetts language for indigenous communities.
Although no longer spoken, the Massachusetts language has influenced several place names in the region.

Etymology

Named after the Massachusett people; 'Massachusett' from an Algonquian word meaning 'at the large hill'.
