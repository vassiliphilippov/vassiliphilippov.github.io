instrument of punishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstrəmənt əv ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnstrʊmənt əv ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
a device or implement specifically designed and used to administer punishment, particularly to people who have been condemned for a crime
device used to punish condemned persons

Examples

The stocks were a common instrument of punishment in medieval times.
The guillotine is a notorious instrument of punishment from the French Revolution.
Some societies have banned any instrument of punishment considered cruel or inhumane.
