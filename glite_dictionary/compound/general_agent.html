general agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnərəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person authorized to handle all types of business for another person (the principal), typically with broad powers to act on their behalf
person with broad authority to act for another
legal

Examples

The company appointed a general agent to oversee its international operations.
As a general agent, she was empowered to sign contracts and make decisions on behalf of her client.
A general agent differs from a special agent in the breadth of their authority.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: special agent - an agent with authority limited to a specific act or type of act

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
an agent authorized to sell insurance and manage business generally for an insurance company, often with wide authority in a region
insurance agent with regional authority

Examples

The company appointed a general agent to oversee its operations in the Midwest.
As a general agent, she managed multiple local offices and handled major client accounts.
He worked his way up from a sales representative to become the general agent for the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurance agent - a person who sells insurance policies
