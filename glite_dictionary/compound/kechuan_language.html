Kechuan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the indigenous language of the Quechua people, historically spoken by the Inca civilization and still used in parts of South America
language of the Quechua people, spoken by the Incas

Examples

The Kechuan language is still taught in schools across parts of Peru and Bolivia.
Many ancient Incan stories have survived thanks to preservation in the Kechuan language.
Linguists study the Kechuan language to help reconstruct the history of Andean cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - the indigenous people for whom the Kechuan language is named
  • Synonym: Inca - the ancient civilization that spoke this language

Etymology

From Spanish 'quechua', from Quechua 'qichwa' (temperate valley, language name).
