brown rot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊn ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn ˌrɒt / (BrE)
a fungal disease of plants in which tissues turn brown and decay, commonly affecting fruits such as peaches, plums, and apples
fungal disease causing browning and decay of plants

Examples

The orchard lost most of its crop to brown rot this year.
Peach trees are especially susceptible to brown rot when the weather is warm and humid.
Proper pruning and removal of infected fruit can help prevent the spread of brown rot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry rot - a fungal decay in wood, often confused with brown rot but distinct
