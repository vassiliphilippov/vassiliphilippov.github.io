family Scolytidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli skoʊˈlɪtəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli skɒˈlɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of bark-boring or wood-boring short-beaked beetles, many of which are destructive to forest and fruit trees.
family of bark-boring beetles
medical

Examples

Several species in the family Scolytidae can devastate pine forests if left uncontrolled.
The family Scolytidae includes many beetles that threaten commercial fruit orchards worldwide.
Entomologists study the life cycles of the family Scolytidae to develop pest management strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark beetle - A common name for beetles of the family Scolytidae, known for boring into bark

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Scolytus' (type genus) and the family suffix '-idae'.
