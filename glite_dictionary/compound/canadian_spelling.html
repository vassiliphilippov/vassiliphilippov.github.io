Canadian spelling

1. NOUN

/ kəˈneɪdiən ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the system of English spelling conventions used in Canada, which often combines elements of British and American orthography
English spelling conventions used in Canada

Examples

Many Canadian publications follow Canadian spelling even when using American grammar.
The word 'colour' is an example of Canadian spelling that differs from American usage.
Schools in Canada teach children Canadian spelling as part of the curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: British spelling - English orthography as used in Britain
  • Degree Lower: American spelling - English orthography as used in the United States
