rusty woodsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌsti ˈwʊdziə / (AmE), / ˈrʌsti ˈwʊdziə / (BrE)
A common fern of northern temperate regions, typically found on rocks, with rusty-brown stalks and lance-shaped, divided fronds.
a rock-inhabiting temperate fern with rusty-brown stalks

Examples

The rusty woodsia thrives in rocky crevices throughout the northern mountains.
We spotted several rusty woodsia ferns clinging to the stone outcrop during our hike.
Botanists study rusty woodsia for its remarkable adaptation to harsh, rocky environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a flowerless, seedless vascular plant with fronds that produces spores
