ivory tower

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊər / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
a state of intellectual or academic detachment from the practical concerns of everyday life, often associated with universities or scholars
detached or aloof intellectual state

Examples

Many critics claim that university professors often remain in their ivory tower, disconnected from society's problems.
He left his ivory tower to work directly with disadvantaged communities.
Some politicians accuse experts of offering solutions from their ivory tower instead of practical experience.

Synset Examples

he lived in the ivory tower of speculation
they viewed universities as ivory towers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real world - the practical, everyday world; opposite of 'ivory tower'

Etymology

Originated in the 19th century from the French 'tour d’ivoire', used as a symbol of noble purity and later of academic aloofness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
a state or place of privileged seclusion or detachment from practical concerns, especially intellectual or academic isolation
state of privileged isolation or detachment

Examples

Many critics accused the policy makers of making decisions from their ivory tower without understanding ordinary people's lives.
As a writer, she tries not to live in an ivory tower but to stay in touch with the public.
His ivory tower attitude made collaboration with others difficult.

Synset Examples

Academics sometimes retreat to their ivory tower, ignoring practical concerns.
He often criticized others from his ivory tower.
Living in an ivory tower, she was unaware of the issues outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - a state of being isolated from real-world problems or experiences
  • Antonym: real world - practical, everyday realities as opposed to theoretical or academic ideas

Etymology

From the French phrase 'tour d'ivoire', originally symbolic of noble purity, later used to criticize academic or intellectual detachment.
