line of gab

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv gæb / (AmE), / laɪn əv gæb / (BrE)
persuasive or plausible talk, especially designed to impress or influence others, often used by salespeople
persuasive or slick talk
informal

Examples

The salesman had a line of gab that was hard to resist.
She wasn’t fooled by his smooth line of gab and asked for more details.
He tried every line of gab he knew, but I still said no.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - a persuasive or glib sequence of words
  • Synonym: gift of gab - the ability to speak persuasively

Etymology

From 'line' (a sequence of words spoken) + 'gab' (slang for talk, from Scottish).
