mung bean

1. NOUN

/ mʌŋ ˌbin / (AmE), / mʌŋ ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an edible seed of an Asian plant (Vigna radiata), commonly used in cooking and sprouting
edible seed of an Asian plant

Examples

I added soaked mung beans to the salad for extra protein.
Stir-fried noodles often include sprouts made from mung bean seeds.
The recipe calls for two cups of cooked mung beans as the main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bean - an edible seed, typically kidney-shaped, of various plants in the legume family

Etymology

From Hindi 'moong' (मूँग), from Sanskrit 'mudga' (मुद्ग).

2. NOUN

/ mʌŋ bin / (AmE), / mʌŋ biːn / (BrE)
a small, green seed of an Asian plant (Vigna radiata) that is eaten as food and often used in sprouting
edible green seed from the mung bean plant

Examples

She made a salad using fresh mung beans and herbs.
Mung beans are often sprouted and added to stir-fries or soups.
The recipe called for soaking mung beans overnight before cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adzuki bean - a small, red bean commonly used in East Asian cuisine

Etymology

From Hindi moong, ultimately from Sanskrit mudga (a type of bean).

3. NOUN

/ mʌŋ ˌbin / (AmE), / mʌŋ ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a small, green, edible bean widely cultivated in warm regions, used chiefly for sprouting and in Asian cooking
small edible green bean, source of bean sprouts

Examples

Our chef added fresh mung beans to the salad for a crunchy texture.
In many Asian dishes, mung bean sprouts are a common ingredient.
She bought a bag of mung beans to grow her own sprouts at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean sprout - a sprout from a mung bean, commonly used in cooking

Etymology

From Hindi मुण्ग (mung), from Sanskrit मुद्ग (mudga, 'mung bean').
