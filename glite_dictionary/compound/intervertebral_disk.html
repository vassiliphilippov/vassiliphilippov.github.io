intervertebral disk

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚvɝˈteɪbrəl dɪsk / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɜːtɪbrəl dɪsk / (BrE)
A fibrocartilaginous disc that acts as a cushion between the vertebrae of the spinal column, except between the first two vertebrae.
disc between the bones of the spine
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a herniated intervertebral disk can cause severe back pain and nerve problems.
An MRI revealed degeneration of an intervertebral disk in his lower spine.
Regular exercise and good posture can help keep your intervertebral disks healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal disc - alternative term for intervertebral disk
  • Degree Lower: vertebra - each of the bones forming the spinal column

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + vertebra (joint of the spine) + disk (from Greek diskos, plate).
