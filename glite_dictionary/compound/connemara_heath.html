Connemara heath

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈmɑːrə hiːθ / (AmE), / ˌkɒnɪˈmɑːrə hiːθ / (BrE)
A low, spreading evergreen shrub native to western Europe, especially known for its varieties with white to rose-purple flowers.
evergreen shrub with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The hillside was blanketed with blooming Connemara heath in spring.
Gardeners often choose Connemara heath for its year-round greenery and attractive flowers.
The delicate pink blooms of the Connemara heath added color to the rocky landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a low-growing shrub of open land, related plant species

Etymology

Connemara is a region in western Ireland; 'heath' is from Old English 'hæth' (heathland).
