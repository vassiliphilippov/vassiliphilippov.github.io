family Gasterosteidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɡæstəˈrɒstiˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɡæstəˈrɒstiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of small marine and freshwater fish known as sticklebacks, characterized by their slender bodies and armored plates
family of stickleback fish

Examples

Species in the family Gasterosteidae are often found in both marine and freshwater habitats.
Researchers study the evolution of armor plates in fish from the family Gasterosteidae.
Sticklebacks, which belong to the family Gasterosteidae, are common in northern hemisphere waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickleback - a small fish with spines on its back

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Gasterosteidae', from Greek 'gaster' (stomach) and 'osteon' (bone), referring to anatomical features of the fish.
