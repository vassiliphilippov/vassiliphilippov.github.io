poster color

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstər ˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə ˌkʌlə / (BrE)
a type of paint consisting of pigment mixed with water-soluble glues or binders, used especially for posters and educational art projects
water-based paint used for posters

Examples

She used poster color to create bright and bold artwork for her project.
The art teacher recommended poster color for the classroom because it dries quickly and is easy to clean up.
The vivid hues of poster color made the school play's scenery stand out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempera - paint made with pigment and a water-soluble binder like egg yolk
