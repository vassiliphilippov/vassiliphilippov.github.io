to run around town

1. VERB

/ ɹʌn əˈɹaʊnd taʊn / (AmE), / ɹʌn əˈɹaʊnd taʊn / (BrE)
to move quickly from one place to another within a town, often to complete various tasks or errands
move quickly around town to do errands

Examples

She spent all morning running around town gathering the supplies for the party.
After work, he had to run around town to finish his errands before the stores closed.
I've been running around town trying to get everything ready for our trip.

Synset Examples

She spent the day running around town shopping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush about - to move hurriedly from place to place
