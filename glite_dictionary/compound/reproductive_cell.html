reproductive cell

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.prəˈdʌk.tɪv sɛl / (AmE), / ˌriː.prəˈdʌk.tɪv sɛl / (BrE)
A cell, such as a sperm or an egg, that carries genetic material and is involved in the creation of a new organism.
cell involved in reproduction; sperm or egg
medical

Examples

The reproductive cell contains half the genetic material of a typical body cell.
A mutation in a reproductive cell can be passed to future generations.
Sperm is a male reproductive cell, while the egg represents the female counterpart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamete - a mature reproductive cell (sperm or egg)

Etymology

From Latin "re-" (again) + "producere" (to lead forth) + cella (small room).
