Hadean eon

1. NOUN

/ heɪˈdiː.ən ˈiː.ɒn / (AmE), / heɪˈdiː.ən ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
the earliest eon in Earth's history, spanning from the planet's formation about 4.6 billion years ago to roughly 4 billion years ago, before the earliest known rocks and any evidence of life
earliest eon in Earth's history

Examples

Scientists believe the Hadean eon was marked by intense volcanic activity and a molten surface.
There is no direct geological evidence from the Hadean eon due to the lack of surviving rocks.
The formation of the Moon is thought to have occurred during the Hadean eon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Archean eon - the eon following the Hadean, when the Earth's crust cooled and life began

Etymology

From 'Hades', the Greek underworld, reflecting the Earth's hot and inhospitable state at that time. 'Eon' from Greek 'aiōn' (age, vital force).
