Holy Week

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˌwiːk / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˌwiːk / (BrE)
the week preceding Easter, observed by Christians as a time of devotion commemorating the last week of Christ's life
the week before Easter in the Christian calendar

Examples

Many churches hold special services during Holy Week to commemorate the events leading up to Easter.
In Spain, vibrant processions mark each day of Holy Week.
She always takes part in community outreach activities scheduled for Holy Week.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Easter - the Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus

Etymology

From 'holy' meaning sacred and 'week,' denoting the seven-day period before Easter; used in Christian tradition.
