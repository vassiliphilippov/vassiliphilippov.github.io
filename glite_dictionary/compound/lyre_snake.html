lyre snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪər sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪə snɛɪk / (BrE)
a mildly venomous snake of the genus Trimorphodon, distinguished by a lyre-shaped mark on its head and found in rocky areas from the southwestern United States to Central America
a mildly venomous snake with a lyre-shaped head mark

Examples

The lyre snake can be identified by the distinctive pattern on its head.
Researchers discovered a lyre snake hiding among the rocks in the Arizona desert.
Although the lyre snake is mildly venomous, it rarely poses a threat to humans.

Etymology

From the lyre-shaped (stringed instrument) marking on the snake's head.
