autogenic therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a technique that teaches individuals to induce relaxation and reduce stress through self-suggestion and mental exercises
relaxation technique using self-suggestion
medical

Examples

Many people find autogenic therapy helpful in managing anxiety and promoting relaxation.
A therapist may recommend autogenic therapy as part of a stress reduction program.
Through regular autogenic therapy, she learned to calm her mind and body during tense situations.

Etymology

From Greek 'autos' (self) + 'genos' (origin, production), indicating self-generated effects.
