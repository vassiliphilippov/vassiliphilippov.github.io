edge of the world

1. NOUN

/ ɛdʒ əv ðə wɜrld / (AmE), / ɛdʒ əv ðə wɜːld / (BrE)
a place or viewpoint that is extremely remote, distant, or dramatic, often evoking the sense of being at the outermost limits of the known or inhabited world
extremely remote or dramatic location

Examples

After days of travel, they finally reached the edge of the world and gazed out over the endless sea.
The tiny village felt so isolated it might as well be at the edge of the world.
Standing atop the mountain, she marveled at the breathtaking view from the edge of the world.

Synset Examples

They believed they were at the edge of the world.
Standing at the edge of the world felt surreal.
The cliff seemed like the edge of the world.
