genus Acinonyx

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæs.ɪˈnoʊ.nɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæs.ɪˈnəʊ.nɪks / (BrE)
A genus of large felines within the family Felidae, consisting of cheetahs.
the genus of cheetahs

Examples

The genus Acinonyx is best known for its only living species, the cheetah.
Unlike other big cats, members of the genus Acinonyx have non-retractable claws.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the genus Acinonyx to understand cheetah adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheetah - a large African cat known for its speed

Etymology

From Greek akínon (unmoved, unmoving, a reference to the non-retractile claws) and ōnyx (claw).
