Altai Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ælˌtaɪ ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ælˌtaɪ ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in central Asia, stretching about a thousand miles from Kazakhstan east into western Mongolia and northern China
mountain range in central Asia

Examples

The snowcaps of the Altai Mountains can be seen from miles away.
Many rare animal species inhabit the remote Altai Mountains.
The Altai Mountains form a natural border between Russia and Mongolia.

Etymology

From Mongolian and Turkic 'Altai', meaning 'golden'.
