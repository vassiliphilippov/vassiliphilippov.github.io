ecclesiastical calendar

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl ˈkælɪndɚ / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
a calendar of the Christian year showing the dates of religious fasts and festivals
Christian calendar of religious observances

Examples

The ecclesiastical calendar determines the dates of major Christian festivals like Easter and Christmas.
Religious communities often consult the ecclesiastical calendar to observe traditional fasts and holy days.
The ecclesiastical calendar differs slightly between Western and Eastern branches of Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liturgical year - the cycle of seasons in the Christian church calendar

Etymology

From Latin ecclesiasticus (of the church) and Latin calendarium (account book, calendar).
