sickle medick

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəl ˈmɛdɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəl ˈmɛdɪk / (BrE)
a European plant of the clover family, naturalized in North America, with yellow flowers and curved, sickle-shaped seed pods (Medicago falcata)
plant with yellow flowers and sickle-shaped pods

Examples

The field was dotted with sickle medick blooming brightly in the summer sun.
Farmers often use sickle medick as a cover crop to improve soil quality.
The sickle-shaped pods make sickle medick easy to identify among other wild plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alfalfa - a related plant, also called lucerne, grown as forage

Etymology

From 'sickle' (the curved farming tool) and 'medick', from Latin 'medica' (a type of clover).
