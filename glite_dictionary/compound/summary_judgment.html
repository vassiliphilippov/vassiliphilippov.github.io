summary judgment

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌməri ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌməri ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
a judgment by a court decided without a full trial when there is no dispute about the key facts of the case
court decision without a trial
legal

Examples

The company was granted a summary judgment, ending the lawsuit before it went to trial.
Her lawyer filed a motion for summary judgment, arguing that the facts were not in dispute.
The judge concluded that a summary judgment was appropriate since all evidence favored the defendant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌməri ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌməri ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
a court decision made without a full trial, rendered when there are no disputed facts and one party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law
court decision without full trial
legal

Examples

The defendant filed a motion for summary judgment, arguing that there were no facts in dispute.
After hearing the arguments, the judge granted summary judgment in favor of the plaintiff.
The case never went to trial because the court issued a summary judgment.

Etymology

From legal use of 'summary' (Latin 'summarius', of summary nature) + 'judgment' (Old French 'jugement').
