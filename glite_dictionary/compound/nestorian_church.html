Nestorian Church

1. PROPN

/ nɛˈstɔriən tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / nɛˈstɔːrɪən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a Christian church in the Middle East, historically associated with Nestorianism, a doctrine emphasizing the disunion between the human and divine natures of Jesus Christ.
historical Christian church of Nestorianism

Examples

The Nestorian Church played a significant role in spreading Christianity along the Silk Road.
Today, only a small community associated with the Nestorian Church remains in Iraq.
Scholars have studied the theological debates that shaped the Nestorian Church in the early centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Church of the East - Eastern Christian church historically linked to Nestorianism
  • Synonym: Nestorianism - the doctrine stressing the separation of Christ's human and divine natures

Etymology

From Nestorius, 5th-century Archbishop of Constantinople, whose teachings inspired the doctrine.

2. NOUN

/ nɛˈstɔriən tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / nɛˈstɔːriən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a Christian sect founded in the 5th century, emphasizing the independence of the divine and human natures of Jesus Christ, prominent in ancient Persia and Asia
historic Christian sect in Persia and Asia

Examples

The Nestorian Church played a significant role in the spread of Christianity in the East.
Many ancient manuscripts in Syriac were preserved by the Nestorian Church.
The influence of the Nestorian Church extended from Persia to parts of China.

Synset Examples

The Nestorian church was prominent in ancient Persia.

Etymology

Named after Nestorius (c.386–c.451), Patriarch of Constantinople, whose teachings founded the sect.
