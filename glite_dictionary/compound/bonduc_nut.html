bonduc nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑn.dʌk nʌt / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.dʊk nʌt / (BrE)
the hard, shiny, gray seed of a bonduc tree, often used for making jewelry or adornments
seed of a bonduc tree used in jewelry

Examples

The craftsman used a bonduc nut as the centerpiece of the necklace.
Children enjoy playing traditional games using bonduc nuts as play pieces.
Collectors sought out jewelry featuring the distinctive shape of the bonduc nut.

Etymology

From the name of the bonduc tree, ultimately from Arabic *bunduq* (hazelnut, pellet, ball).
