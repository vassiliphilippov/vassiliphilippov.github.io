yellow gurnard

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈɡɜrnərd / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈɡɜːnəd / (BrE)
a species of gurnard fish (Trigla lucerna), noted for its yellowish coloring, found in eastern Atlantic waters and the Mediterranean Sea
a yellowish gurnard fish species

Examples

Fishermen along the coast often catch yellow gurnard in their nets.
The yellow gurnard is recognized by its distinctive yellow fins and head.
Chefs prize the yellow gurnard for its firm, white flesh in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurnard - a fish of the family Triglidae with bony heads and armored scales
