crucifix fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːsəˌfɪks fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfɪks fɪʃ / (BrE)
a sea catfish of the Caribbean region, known for the bony cross-shaped structure found in its head
a Caribbean sea catfish with a cross-shaped bone

Examples

Fishermen in the Caribbean sometimes find a crucifix fish among their catch and save its bony cross for good luck.
The crucifix fish gets its name from the unique cross-shaped bone found inside its head.
Tour guides often show tourists the dried remains of a crucifix fish at local markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea catfish - a type of catfish inhabiting marine waters

Etymology

The name refers to the cross-shaped bony structure found in the fish's head.
