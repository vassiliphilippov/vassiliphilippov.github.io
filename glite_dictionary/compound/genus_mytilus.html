genus Mytilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs mɪˈtaɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs mɪˈtɪl.əs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Mytilidae, comprising certain smooth-shelled marine mussels
type genus of marine mussels (Mytilidae)

Examples

The genus Mytilus includes several species commonly known as blue mussels.
Scientists studied the distribution patterns of genus Mytilus along the Atlantic coast.
Shells from the genus Mytilus are often found in coastal archaeological sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a marine or freshwater bivalve mollusc with a dark elongated shell

Etymology

From Latin 'Mytilus' (mussel), used as a taxonomic genus name in biological classification.
