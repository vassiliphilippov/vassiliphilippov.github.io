half-relief

1. NOUN

/ hæf rɪˈlif / (AmE), / hɑːf rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
a style of sculptural relief in which the forms project about halfway from the background, intermediate between low relief and high relief
intermediate sculptural relief

Examples

The ancient frieze was carved in half-relief to give depth without overwhelming the wall.
Many Renaissance artists preferred half-relief for depicting figures with subtle modeling.
You can see the difference between high relief and half-relief on the museum's sculpture tour.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bas-relief - sculptural relief where the projection from the surrounding surface is slight
  • Degree Higher: high relief - sculptural relief where forms project strongly from the background

Etymology

From Italian 'mezzo-rilievo' (mezzo 'half' + rilievo 'relief'), calqued into English.
