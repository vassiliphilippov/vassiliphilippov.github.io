dative case

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtɪv keɪs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtɪv keɪs / (BrE)
The grammatical case used for nouns that function as the indirect object of a verb.
case for indirect object of verb

Examples

In German, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a sentence.
Some ancient languages, like Latin, have a distinct dative case for nouns and pronouns.
The student struggled to remember when to use the dative case in her Russian homework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusative case - grammatical case for the direct object of a verb
  • Synonym: nominative case - grammatical case for the subject of a verb

Etymology

From Latin 'casus dativus', meaning 'case for giving'.
