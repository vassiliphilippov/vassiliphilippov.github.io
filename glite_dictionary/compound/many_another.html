many another

1. DET

/ ˈmɛni əˈnʌðər / (AmE), / ˈmɛni əˈnʌðə / (BrE)
Used to refer to each of a large number of people or things individually, especially in literary or formal contexts.
each of many, individually (literary)

Examples

Many another challenge awaited the young scientist after her first discovery.
He has faced many another difficult decision in his long career.
We spent many another afternoon reminiscing about our travels.

Synset Examples

many a man
many another day will come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: many a - used to mean 'a large number of'; each considered individually
