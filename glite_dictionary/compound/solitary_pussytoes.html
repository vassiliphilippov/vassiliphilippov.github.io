solitary pussytoes

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪˌtɛri ˈpʊsitoʊz / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪtəri ˈpʊsɪtəʊz / (BrE)
A North American plant (Antennaria solitaria) in the daisy family, noted for its small, woolly flower heads and preference for solitary growth.
a plant with small, woolly flowers (Antennaria solitaria)

Examples

The meadow was dotted with delicate solitary pussytoes blooming in the spring sunlight.
Botanists observed a patch of solitary pussytoes thriving at the forest's edge.
Hikers admired the soft, white clusters of solitary pussytoes along the trail.
