trespass quare clausum fregit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛsˌpæs ˈkwɛəri ˈklaʊsəm ˈfrɛdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəs ˈkwɪəri ˈklaʊsəm ˈfriːdʒɪt / (BrE)
A legal action in which a person claims damages because another unlawfully entered their land; commonly called 'trespass to land' in modern law.
legal action for unlawful entry onto land
legal

Historically, trespass quare clausum fregit allowed landowners to protect their boundaries through legal proceedings.
The defendant was found liable for trespass quare clausum fregit when he entered the neighbor’s field without permission.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin, meaning 'trespass whereby the enclosure was broken.'
