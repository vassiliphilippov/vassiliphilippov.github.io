sea puss

1. NOUN

/ siː pʊs / (AmE), / siː pʊs / (BrE)
A seaward undercurrent formed after waves have broken on the shore, sometimes causing strong movement away from the beach.
seaward undercurrent after waves break

Examples

Swimmers are advised to avoid the sea puss near the jetty, as it can pull you out to deeper water.
Lifeguards posted warnings about a dangerous sea puss forming along the eastern shore.
After the giant waves crashed, a strong sea puss developed, making it risky for surfers to return to the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rip current - a strong, narrow current flowing outward from the beach through the surf zone
