cortical area

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.tɪ.kəl ˈɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.tɪ.kəl ˈeə.ri.ə / (BrE)
any of the distinct regions of the cerebral cortex, each associated with particular functions
region of the cerebral cortex
medical

Examples

The primary visual cortical area processes information received from the eyes.
Damage to a specific cortical area can affect speech and language abilities.
Scientists are mapping each cortical area to better understand brain function.

Etymology

From Latin cortex (bark, rind) + area (open space, region).
