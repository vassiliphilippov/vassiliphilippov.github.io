crow's feet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɹoʊz ˌfit / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊz ˌfiːt / (BrE)
Wrinkles at the outer corners of the eyes, typically caused by aging or frequent smiling.
wrinkles at the eye corners

Examples

Laughter and years in the sun had given her noticeable crow's feet around her eyes.
He applied cream daily, hoping to reduce his crow's feet.
The actor embraced his crow's feet as a sign of wisdom and experience.

Etymology

The name refers to the resemblance of the wrinkles to a crow's foot with splayed toes.
