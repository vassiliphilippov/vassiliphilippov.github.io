audited account

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdɪtɪd əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtɪd əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
a financial account that has been formally examined and verified by a qualified accountant or auditor to ensure accuracy and compliance with regulations
financial account examined by an auditor

Examples

The company submitted its audited account to the regulatory authorities for review.
An audited account gives investors confidence in the accuracy of a business's financial statements.
All charities are required to prepare at least one audited account annually for public disclosure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audit - an official inspection of an organization's accounts

Etymology

From 'audit' (from Latin audire, to hear) and 'account' (from Old French acunt, from Latin computus).
