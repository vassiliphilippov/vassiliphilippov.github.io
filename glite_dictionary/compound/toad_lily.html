toad lily

1. NOUN

/ toʊd ˈlɪli / (AmE), / təʊd ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A perennial plant with strikingly spotted flowers, native to moist areas of East Asia or North America, often cultivated for its ornamental value.
spotted-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The delicate blooms of the toad lily brighten shady gardens in late summer.
Gardeners often prize the toad lily for its unique, orchid-like appearance.
In the wild, toad lilies thrive in damp, wooded areas near streams.
