family Lorisidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli lɔːˈrɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli lɒˈrɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
a group of slow-moving, usually tailless, omnivorous nocturnal primates found in tropical Asia
a family of nocturnal Asian primates
medical

Examples

Most members of the family Lorisidae are nocturnal and feed on fruit, insects, and small animals.
The slow loris, a member of the family Lorisidae, is found in Southeast Asian forests.
Researchers are studying the conservation status of several family Lorisidae species due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loris - a small, slow-moving nocturnal primate of tropical Asia, member of Lorisidae
  • Antonym: bush baby - a small, agile, nocturnal primate; not a member of Lorisidae but a related group (Galagidae)

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Lorisidae,' based on 'Loris' (type genus) + '-idae', a suffix denoting animal families.
