dispersion medium

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɝːʒən ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːʃən ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
(in chemistry) the substance in which particles are dispersed to form a colloid.
substance in which colloidal particles are dispersed

Examples

Water is commonly used as the dispersion medium in many colloidal systems.
The properties of a colloid depend heavily on both the dispersed phase and the dispersion medium.
Air can serve as a dispersion medium for aerosols such as fog or smoke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloid - a mixture where one substance is dispersed in another
