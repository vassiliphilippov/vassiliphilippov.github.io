patriarchal cross

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪ.triˈɑrk.əl krɔs / (AmE), / ˌpeɪ.triˈɑː.kəl krɒs / (BrE)
a cross with two horizontal crossbars, the upper one shorter than the lower; used as a symbol in Eastern Christian traditions
a cross with two crossbars; Christian symbol

Examples

The patriarchal cross is a prominent symbol in many Eastern Orthodox churches.
The flag featured a large, golden patriarchal cross at its center.
He studied the meaning behind the double bars of the patriarchal cross for his art project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - an object or design consisting of an upright and a transverse piece

Etymology

From 'patriarch', referring to high-ranking bishops in the Eastern Orthodox Church, and 'cross'.
