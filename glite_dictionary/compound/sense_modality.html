sense modality

1. NOUN

/ sɛns moʊˈdæləti / (AmE), / sɛns məʊˈdælɪti / (BrE)
a particular system or form of sensation, such as sight, hearing, smell, taste, or touch
a particular sense (e.g., sight, hearing)

Examples

Each sense modality provides unique information about the environment.
Humans rely on the visual sense modality for spatial orientation.
Research often compares how different sense modalities process stimuli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense - a faculty by which the body perceives an external stimulus

Etymology

From Latin 'modus' (measure, manner) and 'sensus' (perception).
