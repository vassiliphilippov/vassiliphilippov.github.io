only too

1. ADV

/ ˈoʊnli tuː / (AmE), / ˈəʊnli tuː / (BrE)
(used before an adjective or adverb) very; to a greater degree than might be expected or wished, often implying excess or regret.
to a high or excessive degree

Examples

I would be only too happy to help you with your project.
She was only too aware of the risks involved in her decision.
He is only too willing to volunteer for extra work.

Synset Examples

she is all too ready to accept the job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all too - to a greater degree than desired; regrettably much
