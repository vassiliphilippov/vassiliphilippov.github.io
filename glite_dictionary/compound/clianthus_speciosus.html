Clianthus speciosus

1. NOUN

/ kliˈænθəs spiˈʃoʊsəs / (AmE), / klaɪˈænθəs spɪˈʃəʊsəs / (BrE)
A sprawling shrubby perennial plant native to dry regions of Australia, known for its scarlet, black-marked flowers.
Australian perennial with scarlet flowers

Examples

The garden featured a stunning display of Clianthus speciosus blooming in early summer.
Many botanists admire the unique flowers of Clianthus speciosus for their vivid scarlet color.
Clianthus speciosus thrives in the dry, sandy soils of Western Australia.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Clianthus' and the Latin 'speciosus' meaning 'showy' or 'splendid'.
