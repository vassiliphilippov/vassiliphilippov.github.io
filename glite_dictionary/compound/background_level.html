background level

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡraʊnd ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
the amplitude or intensity of unwanted background noise in an environment or recording
amplitude of background noise

Examples

The microphone picked up too much background level during the interview, making it hard to hear the speaker.
Technicians reduced the background level to improve the clarity of the audio recording.
It's important to measure the background level before starting any sensitive experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal-to-noise ratio - the ratio of desired signal power to background noise power
