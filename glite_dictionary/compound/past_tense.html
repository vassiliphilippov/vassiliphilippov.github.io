past tense

1. NOUN

/ pæst tɛns / (AmE), / pɑːst tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense that refers to an action or state that occurred in the past
grammatical tense for past actions

Examples

Regular verbs in English usually add '-ed' to form the past tense.
She always confuses the past tense with the present tense when speaking English.
In the sentence 'He walked to school,' 'walked' is in the past tense.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present tense - a verb tense expressing current or habitual actions
  • Antonym: future tense - a verb tense expressing actions that will happen

Etymology

Formed from 'past' (from Latin 'passus', meaning 'step, track, passage') and 'tense' (from Latin 'tempus', meaning 'time').
