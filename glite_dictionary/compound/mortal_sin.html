mortal sin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.tl̩ sɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tl̩ sɪn / (BrE)
A grave sin, regarded in Christian theology as leading to the total loss of grace unless repented; an unpardonable sin.
grave sin causing loss of grace

Examples

According to Catholic doctrine, committing a mortal sin without repentance endangers one's salvation.
He confessed to what he believed was a mortal sin, seeking forgiveness for his actions.
Pride is often cited as an example of a mortal sin in Christian teaching.

Synset Examples

theologians list seven mortal sins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: venial sin - a lesser sin that does not result in a total loss of grace

Etymology

From Latin 'mortalis' (mortal) + 'peccatum' (sin), used in Christian theology since at least the Middle Ages.
