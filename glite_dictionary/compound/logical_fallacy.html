logical fallacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl ˈfæləsi / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈfæləsi / (BrE)
an error in reasoning that renders an argument invalid or unsound
error in reasoning or argument

Examples

The politician's argument contained a logical fallacy that weakened his entire case.
It's important to recognize a logical fallacy when analyzing debates or persuasive essays.
He avoided using any logical fallacy to ensure that his reasoning was sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallacy - a mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound arguments
