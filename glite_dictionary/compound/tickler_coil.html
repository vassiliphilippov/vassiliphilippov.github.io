tickler coil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪk.lɚ kɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɪk.lə kɔɪl / (BrE)
a small coil in series with the anode of a vacuum tube, coupled to the grid to provide feedback, typically in radio circuits
feedback coil in vacuum tube circuits

Examples

The engineer adjusted the tickler coil to achieve optimal feedback in the radio circuit.
Early regenerative receivers often used a tickler coil to amplify weak signals.
If the tickler coil is too loosely coupled, the feedback may not be sufficient for oscillation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feedback coil - a coil used to provide feedback in an electrical circuit
