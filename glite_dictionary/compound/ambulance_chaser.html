ambulance chaser

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmbjələns ˌtʃeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈæmbjələns ˌtʃeɪsə / (BrE)
an informal, often derogatory term for a lawyer who seeks out accident victims in order to encourage them to file lawsuits, especially for personal gain.
an unethical lawyer seeking accident cases
informal offensive american

Examples

The newspaper ran an expose on the practices of an ambulance chaser who pressured clients to sue.
Some people think hiring an ambulance chaser only worsens the suffering of accident victims.
She refused to work for a firm with a reputation for being full of ambulance chasers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal injury lawyer - a lawyer specializing in representing accident victims, usually in civil lawsuits

Etymology

Refers figuratively to lawyers who supposedly chase ambulances to find clients among accident victims. The phrase dates from early 20th-century America.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.bjə.ləns ˌʧeɪ.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈæm.bjə.ləns ˌtʃeɪ.sə / (BrE)
a lawyer who seeks clients by searching for accident victims, often viewed negatively as acting unethically
lawyer who solicits accident victims
informal offensive american

Examples

After the crash, the media accused the attorney of being an ambulance chaser hungry for business.
His reputation as an ambulance chaser made clients and colleagues distrust him.
She resented being called an ambulance chaser just because she handled personal injury cases.

Synset Examples

He criticized her for acting like an ambulance chaser after the incident.
She felt uncomfortable being called an ambulance chaser in her profession.
The lawyer was known as an ambulance chaser, always lurking at accident scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shyster - a lawyer acting in a dishonest or unethical way

Etymology

Originating in early 20th-century American slang, from the idea of lawyers literally pursuing ambulances to accident scenes.
