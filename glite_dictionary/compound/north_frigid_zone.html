North Frigid Zone

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ ˈfrɪdʒɪd zoʊn / (AmE), / nɔːθ ˈfrɪdʒɪd zəʊn / (BrE)
the region of the Earth north of the Arctic Circle, centered on the North Pole, characterized by extremely cold climate
region north of the Arctic Circle

Examples

Explorers have ventured into the North Frigid Zone to study polar climates and wildlife.
The North Frigid Zone experiences long periods of darkness during the winter months.
Sea ice in the North Frigid Zone is a critical habitat for polar bears and seals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arctic Circle - imaginary circle around the Earth at latitude 66.5° north
