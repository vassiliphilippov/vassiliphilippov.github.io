early warning radar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrli ˈwɔrnɪŋ ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˈɜːli ˈwɔːnɪŋ ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
a radar system designed to detect threats or targets at a great distance, providing advance notice for defensive or protective action
radar system for advance threat detection

Examples

The military installed an early warning radar on the coast to detect incoming missiles.
Advancements in early warning radar technology have greatly enhanced national security.
An early warning radar can provide valuable time to respond to aerial threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - a device that uses radio waves to detect and locate objects
