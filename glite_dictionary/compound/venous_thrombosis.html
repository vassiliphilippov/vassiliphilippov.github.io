venous thrombosis

1. NOUN

/ ˈvinəs θrɑmˈboʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs θrɒmˈbəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a blood clot forms in a vein, not caused by prior inflammation, often linked to sluggish blood flow or increased coagulability.
blood clot in a vein
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with venous thrombosis after experiencing swelling and pain in his leg.
Surgery and prolonged bed rest can increase the risk of venous thrombosis in some individuals.
Doctors monitor for venous thrombosis in patients who have risk factors such as obesity and advanced age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deep vein thrombosis - a specific type of venous thrombosis occurring mainly in the deep veins of the legs

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and Greek 'thrombosis' (clotting).
