kind of

1. ADV

/ ˈkaɪnd əv / (AmE), / ˈkaɪnd əv / (BrE)
To some extent; somewhat; used to indicate a moderate degree or a slight quality.
to some extent; somewhat
informal

Examples

I'm kind of tired, so I think I'll go to bed early.
The movie was kind of disappointing, but I still enjoyed it.
She looked kind of nervous before giving her presentation.

Synset Examples

it was rather cold
the party was rather nice
the knife is rather dull
I rather regret that I cannot attend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sort of - to some (great or small) extent; somewhat
  • Synonym: rather - to some degree; used for mild emphasis
