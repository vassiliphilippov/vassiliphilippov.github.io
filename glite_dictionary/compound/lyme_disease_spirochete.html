Lyme disease spirochete

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪm dɪˈziz spaɪˈroʊˌkit / (AmE), / ˈlaɪm dɪˈziːz spaɪˈrəʊˌkiːt / (BrE)
the bacterium that causes Lyme disease, transmitted primarily by ticks of the genus Ixodes
bacterium causing Lyme disease
medical

Examples

The Lyme disease spirochete is typically transmitted through the bite of an infected tick.
Researchers studied how the Lyme disease spirochete invades human tissues.
Testing identified the presence of the Lyme disease spirochete in the patient's blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Borrelia burgdorferi - the specific species of spirochete bacterium that causes Lyme disease

Etymology

Lyme disease named after Lyme, Connecticut, where it was first recognized; 'spirochete' from Greek 'speira' (coil) and 'khaitē' (hair).
