genus Naias

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈnaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈneɪ.əs / (BrE)
the sole genus of the family Naiadaceae, comprising aquatic plants commonly known as water nymphs or naiads.
genus of aquatic plants (Naiadaceae)

Examples

The aquatic plants in the genus Naias thrive in both fresh and brackish waters.
Botanists are studying the reproductive habits of the genus Naias to better understand aquatic ecosystems.
Several species within the genus Naias are important for maintaining water quality in lakes and ponds.

Etymology

From Latin 'Naias', derived from Greek 'Naiás', meaning water nymph.
