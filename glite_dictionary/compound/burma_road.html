Burma Road

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɝːmə roʊd / (AmE), / ˈbɜːmə rəʊd / (BrE)
a historic route built during World War II to transport military supplies from Burma (Myanmar) into China, playing a crucial role in wartime logistics against Japanese forces
historic WWII military supply route

Examples

The Allied forces relied on the Burma Road to deliver supplies to China during World War II.
Restoration efforts are underway to preserve sections of the historic Burma Road.
His grandfather recounted stories of traveling along the Burma Road during the war.

Synset Examples

The expedition followed the historic Burma Road for supplies.
The Burma Road played a crucial role in wartime logistics.

Etymology

Named after Burma (now Myanmar), the country through which much of the road passed.
