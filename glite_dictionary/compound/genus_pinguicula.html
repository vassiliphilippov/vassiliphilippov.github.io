genus Pinguicula

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiŋɡwɪˌkjuːlə / (AmE), / ˌpɪŋɡwɪˈkjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of almost stemless carnivorous bog plants, commonly known as butterworts, found in Europe, America, and Antarctica.
genus of carnivorous bog plants (butterworts)

Examples

The genus Pinguicula contains plants known for their sticky, insect-trapping leaves.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the genus Pinguicula in South America.
Gardeners appreciate members of the genus Pinguicula for their unusual insectivorous habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterwort - common name for plants of genus Pinguicula

Etymology

From Latin *pinguicula*, diminutive of *pinguis* meaning 'fat', referring to the greasy leaves.
