value judgement

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælju ˌdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈvalju ˌdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
an opinion or assessment that is based on personal or cultural values, rather than objective facts
assessment based on personal values

Examples

Making a value judgement about art is always influenced by personal taste.
Her comments about the new policy were clearly a value judgement.
Try to avoid value judgements when analyzing historical events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opinion - a belief or view about something, not necessarily based on fact
  • Antonym: fact - something that is known or proven to be true

Etymology

From 'value' (from Latin valere, to be worth) + 'judgement' (from Latin judicium, act of judging).
