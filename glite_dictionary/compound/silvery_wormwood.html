silvery wormwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvəri ˈwɜːrmwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəri ˈwɜːmwʊd / (BrE)
A silver-haired shrub native to central and southern United States and Mexico, often considered a troublesome weed on rangelands.
silver-haired shrub of US and Mexico

Examples

The rancher struggled to control silvery wormwood spreading across his grazing land.
Silvery wormwood is known for its distinctive silver leaves and resilience in dry climates.
Many farmers consider silvery wormwood a nuisance weed due to its rapid growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - any plant of the genus Artemisia, especially Artemisia absinthium; a plant with bitter-tasting leaves
