proboscis monkey

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈbɑːsɪs ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / prəˈbɒsɪs ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
A large monkey native to Borneo, noted for its long, bulbous nose, especially in adult males.
Borneo monkey with a large nose

Examples

The proboscis monkey is easily recognized by its unusually large nose.
We saw a group of proboscis monkeys swinging through the mangrove trees.
Researchers are studying the diet and behavior of the endangered proboscis monkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nasalis larvatus - scientific name of the proboscis monkey

Etymology

From Latin 'proboscis' (meaning 'trunk' or 'long nose') and 'monkey'.
