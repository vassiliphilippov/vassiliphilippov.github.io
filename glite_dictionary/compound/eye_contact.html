eye contact

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ ˌkɑn.tækt / (AmE), / ˈaɪ ˌkɒn.tækt / (BrE)
the act of looking directly into another person's eyes; direct visual engagement between people
looking directly into another's eyes

Examples

Maintaining eye contact during an interview shows confidence and attentiveness.
She avoided eye contact because she was feeling shy.
Good eye contact is important when giving a public speech.

Synset Examples

a teacher should make eye contact with the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaze - a long, steady look
  • Antonym: avert one's eyes - to avoid looking directly at someone

2. NOUN

/ aɪ ˈkɑnˌtækt / (AmE), / aɪ ˈkɒn.tækt / (BrE)
the act of two people looking directly into each other's eyes, often communicating emotion or intent without words
direct mutual gaze between two people

Examples

She felt nervous during the interview and struggled to maintain eye contact with the manager.
Good eye contact is important when giving a public speech.
He avoided eye contact, which made him seem untrustworthy.

Synset Examples

it was a mere glance, but the eye contact was enough to tell her that he was desperate to leave

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glance - a brief or hurried look
