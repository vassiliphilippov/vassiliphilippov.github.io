red lead

1. NOUN

/ rɛd lɛd / (AmE), / rɛd lɛd / (BrE)
a bright red or orange-red powder, consisting of lead tetroxide (Pb3O4), used as a pigment and in the manufacture of glass and ceramics
a red lead-based pigment

Examples

The rusty machinery was coated with red lead to prevent further corrosion.
Historic artists often prepared their own red lead pigment for use in paintings.
The production of high-quality glass sometimes requires the addition of red lead as an ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minium - the chemical compound Pb3O4, also known as red lead
