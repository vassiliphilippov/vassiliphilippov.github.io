nonmalignant neoplasm

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.məˈlɪɡ.nənt ˈni.oʊˌplæz.əm / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.məˈlɪɡ.nənt ˈniː.əʊˌplæz.əm / (BrE)
A tumor that is not cancerous and does not invade surrounding tissues or spread to other parts of the body.
tumor that is not cancerous
medical

Examples

After surgery, the doctor confirmed that the growth was a nonmalignant neoplasm and not a cancer.
A nonmalignant neoplasm typically grows slowly and does not spread to other parts of the body.
The biopsy results showed that the mass was a nonmalignant neoplasm, relieving the patient and her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benign tumor - a tumor that is not malignant and does not spread to other parts of the body
  • Antonym: malignant neoplasm - a cancerous tumor capable of invading and spreading

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'non-' (not), 'malignus' (bad or evil), and Greek 'neoplasma' (new formation).
