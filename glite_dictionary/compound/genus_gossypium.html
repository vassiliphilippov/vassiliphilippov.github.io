genus Gossypium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinoʊs ˌɡɒˈsɪpiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɡɒˈsɪpɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of herbs, shrubs, and small trees that includes the cotton plants
genus of cotton plants

Examples

The species within the genus Gossypium are cultivated for their valuable cotton fibers.
Researchers study the genus Gossypium to improve resistance to pests and diseases.
There are over 50 species classified in the genus Gossypium worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft, white, fibrous substance from the cotton plant

Etymology

From Latin 'Gossypium' (cotton), used in scientific classification.
