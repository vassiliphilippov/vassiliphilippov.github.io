Maori language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊ.ri ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊəri ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Oceanic language spoken by the Maori people of New Zealand
language of the Maori people of New Zealand

Examples

The Maori language is taught in schools throughout New Zealand to preserve the culture.
Many place names in New Zealand come from the Maori language.
She is fluent in both English and the Maori language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: te reo - the Maori term for the Maori language

Etymology

From Maori 'Māori' (the indigenous people or their language) + English 'language'.
