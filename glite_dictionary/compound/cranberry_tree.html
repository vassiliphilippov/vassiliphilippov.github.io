cranberry tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrænˌbɛri tri / (AmE), / ˈkrænˌbɛri triː / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub of the Old World, Viburnum opulus, with clusters of white flowers and small bright red berries
deciduous shrub; Viburnum opulus

Examples

The cranberry tree produces attractive clusters of bright red berries in the fall.
Gardeners often plant the cranberry tree for its ornamental flowers and berries.
Birds are attracted to the cranberry tree because of its abundant fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrænˌbɛri tri / (AmE), / ˈkrænˌbɛri triː / (BrE)
A deciduous North American shrub or small tree with three-lobed leaves and clusters of red berries.
North American shrub or small tree with red berries

Examples

In autumn, the leaves of the cranberry tree turn a brilliant red.
The cranberry tree in the garden is full of clusters of bright red berries.
Many birds are attracted to the berries of the cranberry tree during the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viburnum - a genus of shrubs and small trees including the cranberry tree
  • Antonym: cranberry - a small, red, tart berry of a different plant, not to be confused with cranberry tree berries
