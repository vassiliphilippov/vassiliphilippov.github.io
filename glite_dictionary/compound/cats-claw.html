cat's-claw

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæts klɔ / (AmE), / ˈkats klɔː / (BrE)
an erect shrub native to the West Indies and Florida, having few or no spines, racemes of white to yellow flowers, and curved pointed pods with black shiny seeds
a shrub with curved pods, West Indies and Florida

Examples

The cat's-claw in her garden bloomed with clusters of yellow flowers this summer.
Botanists studying the cat's-claw noted its distinctive curved seed pods and glossy black seeds.
In Florida, you can often find the cat's-claw growing wild along sunny roadsides.

Etymology

So named for the shape of its curved pods, resembling a cat's claw.
