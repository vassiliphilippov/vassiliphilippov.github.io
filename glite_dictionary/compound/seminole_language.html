Seminole language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmɪˌnoʊl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnəʊl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Muskogean language spoken by the Seminole people of Florida and Oklahoma
language of the Seminole people

Examples

Few people today are fluent in the Seminole language, but revitalization efforts are ongoing.
She has been studying the Seminole language to better understand her heritage.
The Seminole language is taught in some schools on tribal lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogean - a family of Native American languages spoken in the southeastern United States

Etymology

Seminole, from Creek 'simanóli' meaning 'runaway', and language, from Latin 'lingua'.
