harmonic law

1. NOUN

/ hɑːrˈmɑːnɪk lɔː / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒnɪk lɔː / (BrE)
a law in astronomy stating that the ratio of the square of a planet's orbital period to the cube of its orbital axis is constant for all planets
astronomical law relating planets' orbits

Examples

Kepler's harmonic law helps astronomers predict planetary movements with remarkable accuracy.
The harmonic law shows a precise relationship between a planet’s orbital period and its distance from the sun.
Students studied the harmonic law to better understand the structure of our solar system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kepler's third law - the law relating the squares of the periods and cubes of the semimajor axes of orbits

Etymology

Named after the concept of harmony, relating to Kepler's view of planetary motion; see also Kepler's third law.
