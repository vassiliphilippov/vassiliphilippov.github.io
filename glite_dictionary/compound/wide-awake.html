wide-awake

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪd əˈweɪk / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd əˈweɪk / (BrE)
fully alert and attentive, not sleepy or sluggish
fully alert and attentive

Examples

After drinking a strong cup of coffee, I felt wide-awake and ready to start my day.
She stayed wide-awake throughout the long, boring meeting.
The noise outside kept him wide-awake all night.

Synset Examples

played heads-up ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to danger or problems
  • Antonym: drowsy - feeling tired and ready to sleep

2. ADJ

/ waɪd əˈweɪk / (AmE), / waɪd əˈweɪk / (BrE)
fully awake, especially when unusually alert or unable to sleep
fully awake and alert

Examples

Despite the late hour, he felt wide-awake and ready to work.
She was so nervous before the exam that she stayed wide-awake all night.
The thunderstorm kept the children wide-awake until early morning.

Synset Examples

the unsleeping city
so excited she was wide-awake all night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awake - not asleep; conscious and alert
  • Antonym: asleep - in the state of sleep; not awake

Etymology

Formed by combining "wide" (in the sense of fully) and "awake," first attested in the 18th century.
