Japanese red pine

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz rɛd paɪn / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz rɛd paɪn / (BrE)
a pine tree native to Japan and Korea, with a wide-spreading irregular crown when mature, often cultivated as an ornamental
pine tree native to Japan and Korea

Examples

The Japanese red pine is prized in traditional Japanese gardens for its striking appearance.
A mature Japanese red pine can develop a beautiful, irregular canopy that provides shade and visual interest.
Many bonsai enthusiasts use the Japanese red pine because of its distinctive needles and attractive growth habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scots pine - a species of pine native to Europe and Asia, known for its reddish bark
