somatotrophic hormone

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌmætəˈtroʊfɪk ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / səˌmatəˈtrəʊfɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates growth and cell reproduction in humans and other animals
growth-promoting hormone from the pituitary gland
medical

Examples

The somatotrophic hormone plays a crucial role in childhood development and growth.
Insufficient secretion of somatotrophic hormone can result in growth disorders.
Doctors may prescribe synthetic somatotrophic hormone to treat certain hormonal deficiencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growth hormone - a hormone that stimulates growth, also called somatotropin

Etymology

From Greek sōma (body) and trophē (nourishment).
