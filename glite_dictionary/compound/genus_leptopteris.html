genus Leptopteris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs lɛpˈtɒp.tə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs lɛpˈtɒp.tə.rɪs / (BrE)
A genus of ferns that includes some species sometimes classified in the genus Todea.
a genus of ferns (botany)

Examples

The genus Leptopteris is recognized for its finely divided fronds and delicate structure.
Some botanists argue that members of genus Leptopteris should be placed in the genus Todea.
Research on the genus Leptopteris has provided new insights into fern evolution.

Etymology

From Greek ‘leptos’ (slender) + Greek ‘pteris’ (fern).
