aphis lion

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪfɪs ˌlaɪən / (AmE), / ˈeɪfɪs ˌlaɪən / (BrE)
the carnivorous larva of a lacewing fly, which feeds on aphids and other small insects
carnivorous larva of a lacewing fly

Examples

The aphis lion is an effective natural enemy of garden pests such as aphids.
Farmers often encourage aphis lions to thrive as a form of biological pest control.
If you look closely, you can spot an aphis lion feeding on aphids under the leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacewing - an insect known for its delicate wings and predatory larvae

Etymology

From 'aphis' (a genus of aphids) and 'lion', describing its predatory behavior.
