supratentorial lesion

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.prə.tɛnˈtɔːr.i.əl ˈliː.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌsuː.prə.tɛnˈtɔː.rɪ.əl ˈliː.ʒən / (BrE)
a lesion located in the region of the brain above the tentorium cerebelli, often referring to tumors, hemorrhages, or other abnormal tissue in the cerebral hemispheres
lesion above the tentorium cerebelli in the brain
medical

Examples

The MRI revealed a supratentorial lesion in the left parietal lobe.
Patients with a supratentorial lesion may present with seizures or cognitive changes.
The location of a supratentorial lesion can affect the treatment approach and prognosis.

Synset Examples

Research on supratentorial lesions is crucial for neurology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infratentorial lesion - a lesion located below the tentorium cerebelli

Etymology

From Latin 'supra' (above) and 'tentorium cerebelli' (tent-like covering of the cerebellum).
