genus Pinguinus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pɪŋˈɡwaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pɪŋˈɡwiːnəs / (BrE)
A former genus of flightless birds, known for the great auk, now extinct.
extinct bird genus (great auk)

Examples

The genus Pinguinus included the great auk, a bird that once inhabited the North Atlantic.
Extinction of the genus Pinguinus is often cited in discussions about human impact on wildlife.
Researchers studied fossils of the genus Pinguinus to learn about its evolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great auk - an extinct flightless bird once found in the North Atlantic
  • Degree Higher: penguin - a group of flightless seabirds found in the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

From Latin 'pinguinus', the genus name assigned in taxonomy, related to 'penguin'.
