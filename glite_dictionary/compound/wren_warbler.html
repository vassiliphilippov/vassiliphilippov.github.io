wren warbler

1. NOUN

/ ɹɛn ˈwɔːɹbəlɚ / (AmE), / ɹɛn ˈwɔːbələ / (BrE)
a small bird found in Asia and Africa, known for building nests similar to those of tailorbirds.
small Asiatic and African bird

Examples

The wren warbler is known for its distinctive whistling call in the early mornings.
Many birdwatchers travel to India to catch a glimpse of the elusive wren warbler.
Unlike most birds, the wren warbler constructs elaborate nests that hang from tree branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prinia - another name for a wren warbler, small warbler found in the Old World tropics
