interplanetary medium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚpləˈnɛtɛri ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəpləˈnɛtəri ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
the matter and energy, including gases, dust, and cosmic rays, that exists in the space between the planets of a solar system
matter and energy in space between planets

Examples

Scientists study the interplanetary medium to understand how space weather affects spacecraft.
The solar wind is a major component of the interplanetary medium in our solar system.
Dust particles in the interplanetary medium can create spectacular meteor showers on Earth.

Etymology

From 'interplanetary' (between planets) and 'medium' (substance through which something is transmitted).
