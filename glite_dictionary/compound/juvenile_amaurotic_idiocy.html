juvenile amaurotic idiocy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl əˌmɔːˈrɑːtɪk ɪdˈiːəsi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvɪnaɪl əˌmɔːˈrætɪk ˌɪdɪˈɒsi / (BrE)
a rare congenital disorder of lipid metabolism, typically beginning in childhood, characterized by progressive blindness, dementia, and early death
rare childhood disorder marked by blindness and dementia
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with juvenile amaurotic idiocy after presenting with progressive vision loss and cognitive decline.
Medical researchers have been studying juvenile amaurotic idiocy to better understand its genetic causes.
Families affected by juvenile amaurotic idiocy often seek support from specialized healthcare providers.

Etymology

From Latin juvenilis (youthful), Greek amaurosis (darkening), and Greek idiōteia (peculiarity, used here for idiocy).
