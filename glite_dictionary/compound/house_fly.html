house fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊs ˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs ˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
a common fly (Musca domestica) typically found in human habitations, known for spreading diseases
common disease-spreading fly

Examples

A house fly landed on my sandwich before I could stop it.
House flies are known to transmit a variety of diseases to humans.
We set up screens to keep house flies out of the kitchen during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly - a small insect with two wings

Etymology

Formed from 'house' and 'fly', denoting a fly commonly found in homes.
