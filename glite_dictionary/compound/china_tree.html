China tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.nə ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.nə ˌtriː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree of tropical America whose pulpy fruit contains saponin and was used as soap by Native Americans.
evergreen tree with saponin-rich fruit

Examples

The Native Americans used the fruit of the China tree to make a natural soap.
A China tree grows in the garden, thriving in the tropical climate.
The saponin-rich fruit of the China tree has long been valued for its cleansing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapberry - tree with fruit used to make soap; another name for the China tree

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnə triː / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə triː / (BrE)
a deciduous tree native to northern India and China, with purple flowers and small yellow inedible fruit, often grown as a shade tree in the southern United States
deciduous tree with purple flowers, grown for shade

Examples

The large China tree in the backyard provides excellent shade during the summer months.
In the spring, the China tree bursts into clusters of fragrant purple flowers.
Many southern gardens feature the China tree for its attractive foliage and distinctive yellow fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Melia azedarach - the scientific name for China tree
