mydriatic drug

1. NOUN

/ maɪdriˈætɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / mɪdrɪˈatɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that causes the pupil of the eye to dilate, used especially to aid eye examinations.
drug used to dilate the pupil
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist administered a mydriatic drug before examining her retina.
Use of a mydriatic drug can make it difficult to see clearly for several hours.
A mydriatic drug is often necessary for thorough eye examinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pupil-dilating drug - another term for mydriatic drug
  • Antonym: miotic drug - a drug that causes the pupil to contract (opposite effect)

Etymology

From Greek mydriasis (dilation of the pupil) + -ic; mydriatic describes something causing pupil dilation.
