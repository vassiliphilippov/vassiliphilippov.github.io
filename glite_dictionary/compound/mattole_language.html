Mattole language

1. NOUN

/ məˈtoʊl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məˈtəʊl ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Athapaskan language historically spoken by the Mattole people of northern California.
Athapaskan language of the Mattole people

Examples

Fewer and fewer people today can speak the Mattole language fluently.
Scholars are working to document the grammar and vocabulary of the Mattole language.
The Mattole language is an important part of the region’s cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athapaskan - a family of indigenous North American languages

Etymology

From the name Mattole, referring to the indigenous people of northern California.
