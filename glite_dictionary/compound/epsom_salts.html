Epsom salts

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpsəm sɔlts / (AmE), / ˈɛpsəm sɒlts / (BrE)
A crystalline compound of hydrated magnesium sulfate, used as a laxative, to treat heartburn, prevent seizures, and for bathing.
hydrated magnesium sulfate compound
medical

Examples

She added a handful of Epsom salts to her bath to help soothe her sore muscles.
Doctors sometimes recommend Epsom salts as a mild laxative for short-term relief of constipation.
The nurse administered Epsom salts intravenously to prevent seizures in the patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnesium sulfate - a sulfate salt of magnesium, used in medicine and industry

Etymology

Named after the town of Epsom in Surrey, England, where the mineral was originally obtained.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛpsəm sɔlts / (AmE), / ˈɛpsəm sɔːlts / (BrE)
hydrated magnesium sulfate, often used as a laxative or in baths to soothe sore muscles
hydrated magnesium sulfate; medicinal/bath salt

Examples

She soaked her feet in a warm solution of Epsom salts to relieve the pain.
The doctor recommended taking Epsom salts to help with occasional constipation.
Gardeners often add Epsom salts to the soil to improve magnesium content.

Etymology

Named after Epsom, a town in Surrey, England, where the compound was first discovered in mineral waters.
