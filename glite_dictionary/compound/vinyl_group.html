vinyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.nəl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈvɪ.nᵊl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group derived from ethylene, with the formula –CH=CH2, found in many polymers and chemicals.
univalent chemical group (–CH=CH2)
medical

Examples

The vinyl group in the compound makes it highly reactive in polymerization reactions.
Many plastics are produced using monomers containing a vinyl group.
Chemists often study the reactivity of molecules that feature a vinyl group for creating new materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkyl group - a univalent group derived from an alkane by removing a hydrogen atom

Etymology

From 'vinyl', which comes from Latin 'vinum' (wine), referring to ethyl alcohol, and 'group' in the chemical sense.
