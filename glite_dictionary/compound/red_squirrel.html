red squirrel

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈskwɪrəl / (AmE), / rɛd ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a small tree-dwelling squirrel with reddish fur, found in forests of northern North America and Eurasia
a squirrel species with reddish fur

Examples

A red squirrel darted across the trail, its bushy tail flickering in the sunlight.
Conservationists are working hard to protect the native red squirrel population.
I spotted a red squirrel gathering acorns beneath the pine trees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gray squirrel - a common tree squirrel with gray fur, native to North America

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / rɛd ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a small squirrel with reddish-brown fur, native to Europe and parts of Asia
small reddish-brown squirrel of Europe and Asia

Examples

The red squirrel is declining in some areas due to competition from the gray squirrel.
We spotted a red squirrel darting through the pine trees during our hike.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the red squirrel in Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gray squirrel - large gray squirrel common in North America
