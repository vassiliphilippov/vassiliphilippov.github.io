plate tectonics

1. NOUN

/ pleɪt tɛkˈtɑnɪks / (AmE), / pleɪt tɛkˈtɒnɪks / (BrE)
the scientific theory explaining the movement and interaction of Earth's lithospheric plates, leading to phenomena such as earthquakes, mountain formation, and continental drift
theory explaining Earth's crust movement

Examples

The theory of plate tectonics has revolutionized our understanding of earthquakes and volcanic activity.
Scientists study plate tectonics to learn how continents drift over millions of years.
The Himalayas were formed as a result of plate tectonics pushing land masses together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tectonics - study of the structure and movement of Earth's crust

Etymology

From 'plate', meaning a rigid segment, and 'tectonics', from Greek 'tektonikos' (of building).
