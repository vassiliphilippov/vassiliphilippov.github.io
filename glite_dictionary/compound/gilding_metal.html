gilding metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪldɪŋ ˌmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪldɪŋ ˌmɛtəl / (BrE)
a type of brass rich in copper, used for making articles to be gilded or for medals and other items requiring a reddish-gold appearance
copper-rich brass used for gilding

Examples

The craftsman selected gilding metal for the decorative vase to ensure a smooth gold finish.
Bullets are sometimes made from gilding metal because of its durability and appearance.
She learned how to shape gilding metal sheets in her jewelry class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - a metal alloy made of copper and zinc

Etymology

From 'gilding' (to overlay with gold) and 'metal'; refers to metal suitable for gilding.
