foxtail millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːks.teɪl ˈmɪl.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɒks.teɪl ˈmɪl.ɪt / (BrE)
a coarse, drought-resistant annual grass cultivated for its grain and as hay or forage, especially in Europe and Asia
a type of millet grass grown for grain or fodder

Examples

Foxtail millet is an important staple crop in some parts of India and China.
Farmers often plant foxtail millet because it thrives in areas with limited rainfall.
Livestock feed can include foxtail millet hay during dry seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millet - small seeded grass cultivated as a cereal crop
