a lot of buzz

1. NOUN

/ ə ˈlɑt əv ˈbʌz / (AmE), / ə ˈlɒt əv ˈbʌz / (BrE)
widespread excitement or interest about something, often attracting public attention or media coverage
widespread excitement or interest
informal

Examples

There was a lot of buzz surrounding the candidate's announcement.
The new smartphone generated a lot of buzz before its official launch.
Critics agreed that the movie lived up to a lot of buzz it had received.

Synset Examples

There's a lot of buzz around the new restaurant opening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hype - promotional publicity or excitement around something
  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest, concern, or excitement
