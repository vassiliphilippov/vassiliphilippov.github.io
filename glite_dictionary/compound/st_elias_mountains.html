St. Elias Mountains

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt iˈlaɪ.əs ˈmaʊn.tənz / (AmE), / seɪnt iˈlaɪ.əs ˈmaʊn.tɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range located between southeastern Alaska and the Yukon territory, known for some of the highest peaks in North America.
mountain range between Alaska and Yukon

Examples

The St. Elias Mountains contain some of the tallest peaks in North America, including Mount Logan.
Scientists conducted climate research in the remote St. Elias Mountains.
The border between Alaska and Canada runs through the St. Elias Mountains.

Etymology

Named after Cape St. Elias, which was named by explorer Vitus Bering after the feast day of St. Elias (Elijah).
