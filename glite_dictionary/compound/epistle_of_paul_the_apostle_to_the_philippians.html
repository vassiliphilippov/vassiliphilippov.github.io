Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Philippians

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ðə fɪˈlɪpiənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsl əv ˈpɔːl ði əˈpɒsl tuː ðə fɪˈlɪpɪənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament consisting of a letter written by Saint Paul to the Christian community at Philippi in Macedonia
New Testament book; Paul's letter to Philippians

Examples

The Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Philippians is often studied for its teachings on joy and humility.
During her sermon, she quoted from the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Philippians.
Scholars debate the historical context of the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Philippians.
