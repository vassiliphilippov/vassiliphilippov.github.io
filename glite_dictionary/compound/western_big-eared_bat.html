western big-eared bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn bɪɡ ˈɪrd bæt / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən bɪɡ ˈɪəd bæt / (BrE)
a bat of western North America with extremely large ears, scientifically known as Corynorhinus townsendii
bat species with large ears, western North America

Examples

The western big-eared bat is known for its distinctive, oversized ears.
Scientists are studying the habitat preferences of the western big-eared bat.
Many caves in California provide a home for the western big-eared bat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Townsend's big-eared bat - another common name for Corynorhinus townsendii
