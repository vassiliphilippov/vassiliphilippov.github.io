Eurasian badger

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈreɪʒən ˈbædʒər / (AmE), / jʊəˈreɪʒən ˈbadʒə / (BrE)
a species of badger native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its black and white striped face and burrowing habits
European and Asian badger species

Examples

The Eurasian badger is known for its distinctive black and white facial stripes.
Farmers sometimes try to protect crops from Eurasian badger activity in rural Europe.
Researchers observed a family of Eurasian badgers burrowing near the forest edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a burrowing, nocturnal mammal with a broad body and short legs
