gray sage

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A low, much-branched perennial plant of the western United States with silvery leaves, important as browse and shelter for wildlife.
a western US perennial plant with silvery leaves

Examples

The gray sage provided excellent cover for the deer in the open field.
Botanists identify gray sage by its silvery leaves and woody stems.
Many birds build their nests in clusters of gray sage across the western plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagebrush - a common shrub found in arid regions of North America; similar plant
