class Lycopodiate

1. NOUN

/ klæs laɪˌkɒpəˈdaɪət / (AmE), / klɑːs lɪˌkɒpəˈdaɪət / (BrE)
a botanical class comprising club mosses and related plants, including Lycopodiales, Isoetales, Selaginellales, and extinct Lepidodendrales; sometimes regarded as a subdivision of Tracheophyta
class of club mosses and related plants

Examples

The class Lycopodiate includes many ancient and modern club mosses found worldwide.
Paleobotanists study fossils of the class Lycopodiate to understand plant evolution.
Some researchers classify Isoetes within the broader class Lycopodiate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lycopodiales - an order of club mosses within class Lycopodiate

Etymology

From Modern Latin Lycopodiatae, based on Lycopodium (a genus of club mosses) + -atae, a botanical suffix.
