cave myotis

1. NOUN

/ keɪv maɪˈoʊtɪs / (AmE), / keɪv maɪˈəʊtɪs / (BrE)
a small bat (Myotis velifer) native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for inhabiting caves and similar sheltered locations
a small cave-dwelling bat species

Examples

The cave myotis usually forms large colonies in limestone caves throughout Texas.
Researchers observed a population of cave myotis feeding near the entrance of a cave at sunset.
The distinctive ear shape helps the cave myotis navigate in complete darkness.

Etymology

From Latin 'myotis', meaning 'mouse-eared' (myo- 'mouse' + -otis 'ear'), referencing its genus.
