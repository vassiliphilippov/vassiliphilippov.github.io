genus Craspedia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kræˈspiːdiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kræˈspiːdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering herbs native to Australia and New Zealand, known for their spherical yellow flower heads.
Australian/New Zealand genus of flowering herbs

Examples

The genus Craspedia includes several striking species commonly found in the grasslands of New Zealand.
Scientists are studying the unique pollination strategies of the genus Craspedia.
Gardeners value members of the genus Craspedia for their distinctive globe-shaped yellow flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Craspedia', the genus name assigned by botanists.
