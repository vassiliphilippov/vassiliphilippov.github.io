Levant garlic

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvænt ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / lɪˈvɑːnt ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
a coarse perennial plant native to the Old World, with a large bulb and tall stalk bearing greenish, purple-tinged flowers; widely naturalized
a coarse perennial garlic plant

Examples

The Levant garlic produces clusters of purple-tinged flowers in late spring.
Botanists have studied the adaptability of Levant garlic in various climates.
Some chefs prefer Levant garlic for its robust flavor in Mediterranean dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic - a plant with a strong-smelling bulb used as seasoning

Etymology

From 'Levant', referring to the eastern Mediterranean region, and 'garlic', from Old English *gārlēac*.
