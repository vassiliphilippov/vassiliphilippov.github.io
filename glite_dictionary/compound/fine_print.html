fine print

1. NOUN

/ faɪn prɪnt / (AmE), / faɪn prɪnt / (BrE)
The detailed conditions or limitations of a contract, usually presented in small type and often overlooked.
small print with contract details

Examples

Always read the fine print before agreeing to any subscription service.
The company hid additional fees in the fine print of the contract.
She was surprised by several clauses in the fine print that she hadn't noticed.

Synset Examples

don't sign a contract without reading the fine print

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small print - text in a document printed in small type, often with important details

2. NOUN

/ faɪn ˈprɪnt / (AmE), / faɪn ˈprɪnt / (BrE)
printed text in a document that is in a small font and often contains important or restrictive details, especially in legal or commercial contexts
small printed text in documents, esp. legal or contractual

Examples

Be sure to read the fine print before signing any contract.
The warranty details are hidden in the fine print at the bottom of the page.
Many people overlook the fine print and are surprised by unexpected fees.

Synset Examples

he needed his glasses in order to read the fine print

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small print - text in contracts or agreements printed in small type with important information
