unit of measurement

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.nɪt əv ˈmɛʒ.ər.mənt / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪt əv ˈmɛʒ.ə.mənt / (BrE)
a fixed quantity used as a standard of measurement for a particular physical quantity, such as length, mass, or volume
fixed standard quantity for measurement

Examples

The meter is a widely recognized unit of measurement for length.
Temperature can be expressed in various units of measurement, such as Celsius or Fahrenheit.
Scientists must always specify the unit of measurement when reporting their results.

Synset Examples

the dollar is the United States unit of currency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit - a single, complete thing that may be used as a standard

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.nɪt əv ˈmɛʒ.ər.mənt / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪt əv ˈmɛʒ.ə.mənt / (BrE)
a quantity used as a standard to express and compare amounts of the same kind, such as length, mass, or volume
standard quantity for measurement

Examples

The kilogram is the international unit of measurement for mass.
Temperature is often measured using the Celsius unit of measurement.
Historians sometimes mention the 'cubit' as an ancient unit of measurement.

Synset Examples

The smoot became a quirky unit of measurement.
