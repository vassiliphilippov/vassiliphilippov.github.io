federal government

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəl ˈɡʌvərnmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
A national government in which power is shared between a central authority and constituent political units, often with strong central powers.
national government with strong central powers

Examples

The federal government is responsible for regulating interstate commerce and national defense.
Many citizens believe that the federal government should invest more in education.
The federal government enacted new policies to address climate change across the nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central government - the main governing authority of a country
