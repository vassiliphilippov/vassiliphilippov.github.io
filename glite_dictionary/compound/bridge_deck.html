bridge deck

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪdʒ ˌdɛk / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdʒ ˌdɛk / (BrE)
an upper deck on a ship from which it is steered and where the captain is often stationed
upper control deck of a ship

Examples

The captain stood on the bridge deck, scanning the horizon for approaching vessels.
After the storm, the crew inspected the bridge deck for any damage.
From the bridge deck, you have a commanding view of the entire ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridge - the raised platform on a ship from which it is navigated
