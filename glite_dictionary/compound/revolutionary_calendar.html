Revolutionary calendar

1. NOUN

/ rɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri ˈkæləndər / (AmE), / rɛvəˈluːʃənəri ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the calendar introduced by the first French Republic in 1793, calculated from 22 September 1792, and used until 1805
French calendar used 1793–1805

Examples

During the French Revolution, the Revolutionary calendar replaced the Gregorian calendar for over a decade.
Historians often refer to the Revolutionary calendar when discussing events in late eighteenth-century France.
Conversion tables are sometimes needed to compare the Revolutionary calendar dates to modern dates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Gregorian calendar - the calendar currently used internationally
  • Antonym: Julian calendar - a calendar system used before the Gregorian calendar

Etymology

Named after the French Revolution, during which it was introduced.
