potato murrain

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ mɜːˈreɪn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ mʌˈreɪn / (BrE)
A disease or blight affecting potatoes, especially referring to the potato blight caused by Phytophthora infestans.
disease affecting potatoes

Examples

The Irish Famine was caused by a devastating potato murrain that wiped out crops for several years.
Researchers studied the spread of potato murrain to better protect future harvests.
Farmers feared the return of potato murrain after an unusually wet summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato blight - an infectious disease of potatoes, especially caused by a fungus-like organism; synonym of potato murrain
  • Degree Lower: blight - a plant disease, especially one caused by fungi, resulting in wilting and death of parts

Etymology

From 'potato' + 'murrain', the latter from Middle English, meaning an infectious disease of cattle or plants.
