Mid-Atlantic states

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪd əˈtlæntɪk steɪts / (AmE), / ˌmɪd əˈtlæntɪk steɪts / (BrE)
the region of the eastern United States that includes New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Delaware, and Maryland
eastern US region (NY, NJ, PA, DE, MD)
american

Examples

Many major cities, including New York and Philadelphia, are located in the Mid-Atlantic states.
The Mid-Atlantic states experience all four seasons, with hot summers and cold winters.
He moved from California to the Mid-Atlantic states to pursue a new job opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Northeast - region of the northeastern United States
  • Degree Higher: New England - region of the northeastern United States including six states north of the Mid-Atlantic

Etymology

From 'Mid-' meaning middle + 'Atlantic', referencing the central portion of the US Atlantic coast.
