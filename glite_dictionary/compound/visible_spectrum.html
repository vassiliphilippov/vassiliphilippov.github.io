visible spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪzəbəl ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪbəl ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the range of wavelengths of light that are visible to the human eye, appearing as a distribution of colors when light is dispersed, such as by a prism
range of visible light wavelengths

Examples

A rainbow is a natural display of the visible spectrum in the sky.
When white light passes through a prism, the visible spectrum fans out into distinct colors.
The visible spectrum includes all the colors that our eyes can perceive, from red to violet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: electromagnetic spectrum - the entire range of electromagnetic radiation
  • Degree Lower: infrared - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light
  • Degree Higher: ultraviolet - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light

Etymology

From Latin 'visibilis' (that can be seen) and 'spectrum' (appearance, image).
