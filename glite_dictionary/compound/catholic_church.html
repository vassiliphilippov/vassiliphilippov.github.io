Catholic Church

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæθəlɪk tʃɝtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkæθlɪk tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
any of several Christian churches claiming to have maintained unbroken historical continuity with the original Church established by Jesus Christ, especially the Roman Catholic Church.
principal Christian church with historical continuity claims

Examples

The Catholic Church has played a major role in shaping Western civilization.
She was raised in the Catholic Church before exploring other faiths.
Controversial reforms have sometimes divided opinion within the Catholic Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roman Catholic Church - the largest and most widely recognized branch of the Catholic Church
  • Antonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - another church claiming apostolic continuity, distinct from the Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin catholicus, meaning ‘universal’, used since early Christianity; ‘church’ from Greek kyriakon, meaning ‘the Lord’s (house)’.
