wood violet

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈvaɪəlɪt / (AmE), / wʊd ˈvaɪəlɪt / (BrE)
a common violet of the eastern United States, characterized by large pale blue or purple flowers that resemble pansies
a flowering plant with pale blue or purple flowers

Examples

A patch of wood violets bloomed along the forest edge in early spring.
She pressed a single wood violet into her journal as a keepsake.
The wood violet is the state flower of Wisconsin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple or blue flowers, genus Viola

2. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / wʊd ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
a common European violet with purple or blue flowers, typically found in woodland and hedgerows
a woodland European violet

Examples

We found a patch of wood violets blooming near the edge of the forest.
The wood violet is known for its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers.
In spring, wood violets carpet the woodland floor with color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple, blue, or white flowers
