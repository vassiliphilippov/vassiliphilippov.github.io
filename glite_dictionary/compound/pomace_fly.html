pomace fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌməs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpʌmɪs flaɪ / (BrE)
A small fly whose larvae feed on fruit, especially decaying or fermenting fruit; commonly known as fruit fly.
small fly with larvae feeding on fruit

Examples

The pomace fly is a common pest in kitchens where fruit is stored.
Researchers studied the genetics of the pomace fly to understand heredity.
Cleaning up ripe bananas reduced the number of pomace flies in the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit fly - a small fly often found around decaying fruit; another common name for pomace fly

Etymology

From 'pomace' (the pulpy residue after fruit is pressed) and 'fly'.
