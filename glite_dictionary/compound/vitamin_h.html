vitamin H

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.tə.mɪn ˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌvɪ.tə.mɪn ˈeɪtʃ / (BrE)
another name for biotin, a B-complex vitamin important for growth and metabolism
biotin; B-complex vitamin
medical

Examples

Doctors sometimes recommend vitamin H supplements to help with hair and skin health.
Egg yolks, nuts, and liver are rich sources of vitamin H.
A deficiency of vitamin H can lead to developmental problems in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biotin - a B-complex vitamin also known as vitamin H

Etymology

The letter H is from German 'Haar' (hair), referring to its association with hair health.
