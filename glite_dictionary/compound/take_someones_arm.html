to take someone's arm

1. VERB

/ teɪk sʌmwʌnz ɑrm / (AmE), / teɪk sʌmwʌnz ɑːm / (BrE)
to hold someone's arm, typically for support or guidance
hold an arm for support or guidance

Examples

As the crowd grew, she took his arm so they wouldn't get separated.
He politely took her arm while crossing the busy street.
She takes my arm whenever the ground is icy.

Synset Examples

She took his arm to steady him on the uneven path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take - to acquire possession or control; to grasp
