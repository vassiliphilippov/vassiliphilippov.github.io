low-fat milk

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ ˈfæt mɪlk / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈfæt mɪlk / (BrE)
Milk from which most of the fat has been removed, containing a lower percentage of fat than whole milk.
milk with reduced fat content

Examples

She always buys low-fat milk for her breakfast cereal.
The recipe calls for either whole or low-fat milk, depending on your preference.
Switching to low-fat milk can help reduce your daily intake of saturated fat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: skim milk - milk from which all the cream and almost all the fat has been removed
  • Degree Higher: whole milk - milk containing all of its original fat content
