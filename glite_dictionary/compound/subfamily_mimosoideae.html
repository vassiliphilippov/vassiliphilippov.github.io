subfamily Mimosoideae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæməli ˌmaɪməˌsoʊˈideɪ / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæmɪli ˌmɪməˌsɔɪˈdiːiː / (BrE)
a subfamily within the legume family (Fabaceae), including plants such as acacias and mimosas; formerly treated as a separate family (Mimosaceae) in some classifications
legume plant subfamily (acacias, mimosas)
medical

Examples

Many species in the subfamily Mimosoideae are well-known for their sensitive leaves that fold when touched.
The subfamily Mimosoideae includes economically important trees such as acacias and mesquites.
Taxonomists sometimes debate the placement of the subfamily Mimosoideae within the larger Fabaceae family.

Etymology

From Modern Latin: 'sub-' (under, below) + 'family' + 'Mimosoideae', derived from 'Mimosa', a genus in this group.
