common fig tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən fɪɡ triː / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən fɪɡ triː / (BrE)
A Mediterranean tree (Ficus carica) widely cultivated for its edible fruit, the fig.
Mediterranean tree producing figs

Examples

The common fig tree thrives in warm, dry climates and is prized for its sweet fruit.
Ancient Romans cultivated the common fig tree both for food and for shade in their gardens.
The leaves of the common fig tree are also used in some traditional Mediterranean recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig tree - a tree producing figs, especially Ficus carica
