parietal bone

1. NOUN

/ pəˈraɪ.ət̬əl boʊn / (AmE), / pəˈraɪ.ə.təl bəʊn / (BrE)
either of the two bones forming the top and sides of the cranium, located between the frontal and occipital bones
bone forming sides and top of the skull
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the parietal bone for signs of fracture.
Damage to the parietal bone can cause serious head injuries.
An X-ray showed that the parietal bone on his left side was intact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frontal bone - the bone forming the front part of the skull
  • Synonym: occipital bone - the bone forming the back and base of the skull

Etymology

From Latin parietalis (of walls), referring to the wall of the cranium.
