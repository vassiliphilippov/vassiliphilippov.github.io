Labor Department

1. PROPN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ dɪˌpɑːrt.mənt / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə dɪˌpɑːt.mənt / (BrE)
the federal department of the United States government responsible for promoting and improving the working conditions, opportunities, and welfare of wage earners; established in 1913
U.S. federal agency for labor and workforce issues
american

Examples

The Labor Department released its latest unemployment figures this morning.
Congress discussed funding levels for the Labor Department in next year's federal budget.
The new policy was announced jointly by the Labor Department and the Department of Education.
