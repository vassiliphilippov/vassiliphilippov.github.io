cirrostratus cloud

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪroʊˈstrætəs klaʊd / (AmE), / ˌsɪrəʊˈstrætəs klaʊd / (BrE)
A thin, uniform, hazy cloud layer found at high altitudes, often covering the entire sky and sometimes producing a halo around the sun or moon.
thin, high-altitude hazy cloud layer

Examples

A thin cirrostratus cloud stretched across the afternoon sky, creating a faint halo around the sun.
Pilots reported encountering a vast layer of cirrostratus cloud at 25,000 feet during their descent.
When a cirrostratus cloud covers the sky, it's often a sign that rain or snow may arrive within the next day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cirrus cloud - wispy high-altitude cloud type
  • Degree Lower: altostratus cloud - mid-altitude cloud forming a continuous layer
  • Synonym: cirrocumulus cloud - high-altitude cloud with small, white patches

Etymology

From Latin 'cirrus' (curl of hair) + 'stratus' (layered), describing its appearance and structure.
