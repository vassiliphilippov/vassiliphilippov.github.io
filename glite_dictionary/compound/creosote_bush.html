creosote bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːəˌsoʊt bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈkriːəsəʊt bʊʃ / (BrE)
a desert shrub of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with aromatic resinous leaves and small yellow flowers
aromatic desert shrub of the Southwest US

Examples

The creosote bush thrives in the arid landscapes of the Mojave Desert.
Early settlers used the resin of the creosote bush for medicinal purposes.
A strong scent rises from the creosote bush after a rare desert rain.

Etymology

From 'creosote', referencing the plant's resinous scent, and 'bush'.
