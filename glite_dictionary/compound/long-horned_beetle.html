long-horned beetle

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ hɔrn̩d ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / lɒŋ hɔːnd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A beetle with a long body and very long antennae, belonging to the family Cerambycidae.
beetle with long antennae

Examples

The long-horned beetle is famous for its exceptionally long antennae, often longer than its body.
Scientists discovered a new species of long-horned beetle in the rainforests of South America.
Gardeners sometimes find long-horned beetles on decaying wood in their backyards.
