hook wrench

1. NOUN

/ hʊk ɹɛntʃ / (AmE), / hʊk rɛntʃ / (BrE)
a wrench with a hook-shaped end designed to fit over and turn a nut or bolt head
wrench with a hooked end

Examples

The mechanic used a hook wrench to loosen the stubborn nut.
This toolbox contains a variety of tools, including a hook wrench for special fasteners.
A hook wrench is useful for turning round nuts with no flat sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spanner - a tool used for gripping and turning nuts, bolts, and other objects
  • Degree Lower: pipe wrench - an adjustable wrench for gripping and turning pipes
