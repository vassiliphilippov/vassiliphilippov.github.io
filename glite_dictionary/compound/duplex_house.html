duplex house

1. NOUN

/ ˈduː.plɛks haʊs / (AmE), / ˈdjuː.pleks haʊs / (BrE)
A house divided into two separate living units, typically sharing a common wall or floor.
house with two separate living units

Examples

They decided to buy a duplex house so they could live in one unit and rent out the other.
The duplex house has a shared driveway and a common wall between the two homes.
She grew up in a duplex house with her grandparents living next door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semi-detached house - a house joined to another by a shared wall
  • Antonym: detached house - a house that stands alone and is not joined to another

Etymology

From Latin 'duplex' (double), referring to something composed of two parts.
