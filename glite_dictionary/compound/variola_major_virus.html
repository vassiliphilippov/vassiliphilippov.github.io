variola major virus

1. NOUN

/ vəˈraɪ.ələ ˈmeɪ.dʒər ˈvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / vəˈraɪ.ələ ˈmeɪ.dʒə ˈvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
a highly virulent type of smallpox virus with a fatality rate of up to 25 percent
a type of deadly smallpox virus
medical

Examples

Before the eradication of smallpox, variola major virus caused devastating epidemics worldwide.
Researchers studied the variola major virus to understand what made it so deadly compared to other strains.
The discovery of a vaccine significantly reduced the threat posed by the variola major virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - an infectious disease caused by the variola virus
  • Degree Lower: variola minor virus - a milder version of the smallpox virus

Etymology

From Latin variola (smallpox) and major (greater), denoting the more severe form of the virus.
