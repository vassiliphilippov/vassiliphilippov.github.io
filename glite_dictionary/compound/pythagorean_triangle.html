Pythagorean triangle

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌθæɡəriən ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / paɪˌθaɡəriːən ˈtraɪaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
A right triangle whose side lengths are positive integers satisfying the equation a² + b² = c².
right triangle with integer sides

Examples

The (3, 4, 5) triangle is a classic example of a Pythagorean triangle.
Mathematicians study the properties of Pythagorean triangles for number theory research.
She showed how to construct a new Pythagorean triangle with different integer side lengths.

Synset Examples

Conversely, if (x, y, z) is a Pythagorean triangle, then (x/t, y/t, z/t) is a primitive Pythagorean triangle provided (x,y) = t.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right triangle - triangle with one right angle
  • Degree Lower: primitive Pythagorean triple - set of integers that are coprime and satisfy a² + b² = c²

Etymology

From Pythagoras, ancient Greek mathematician, and 'triangle'.
