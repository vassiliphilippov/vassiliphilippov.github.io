Chukchi Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃʊk.tʃi pəˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈtʃʊk.tʃi pəˈnɪn.sɪ.lə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in northeastern Siberia, Russia, extending into the Bering Sea and lying across the Bering Strait from Alaska
peninsula in northeastern Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Chukchi Peninsula forms the easternmost part of mainland Russia.
Researchers studied the effects of climate change on the Chukchi Peninsula.
A narrow stretch of water separates Alaska from the Chukchi Peninsula of Siberia.

Etymology

Named after the Chukchi people, the indigenous inhabitants of the region.
