four-in-hand

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ɪn hænd / (AmE), / fɔː ɪn hænd / (BrE)
a carriage pulled by four horses driven by a single driver
carriage drawn by four horses

Examples

The museum displayed a restored four-in-hand from the Victorian era.
We watched as the four-in-hand made its way down the country lane, the driver expertly guiding the team.
In the parade, the grandest entry was a splendid four-in-hand harnessed with white horses.

Etymology

Refers to 'four' horses managed 'in hand' by one driver.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr ɪn ˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːr ɪn ˌhænd / (BrE)
A long necktie tied with a slipknot so that one end hangs in front of the other.
type of necktie or necktie knot

Examples

He wore a four-in-hand to the wedding for a classic look.
Choosing a silk four-in-hand can make any suit appear more elegant.
My grandfather always preferred a four-in-hand over a bow tie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Windsor knot - a wide, triangular necktie knot
  • Antonym: bow tie - a necktie in the form of a bow

Etymology

From the earlier use referring to a four-horse carriage, then to the knot resembling reins held in one hand.
