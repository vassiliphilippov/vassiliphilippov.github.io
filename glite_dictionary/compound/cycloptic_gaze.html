cycloptic gaze

1. NOUN

/ saɪkˈlɑptɪk ɡeɪz / (AmE), / sɪkˈlɒptɪk ɡeɪz / (BrE)
an intense, single-minded look, especially one that seems to focus with all attention on one point
single, intense look

Examples

The teacher fixed a cycloptic gaze on the noisy students, silencing the room at once.
He felt unsettled under the cycloptic gaze of the ancient statue.
A cycloptic gaze from the interviewer made her hesitate before answering.

Synset Examples

Her cycloptic gaze seemed to penetrate my thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - a long, fixed, and intense look
  • Antonym: glance - a brief or hurried look

Etymology

From 'cycloptic', relating to a Cyclops (a mythical giant with a single eye) + 'gaze'.
