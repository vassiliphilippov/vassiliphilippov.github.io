live-and-die

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪv ən ˈdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪv ən ˈdaɪ / (BrE)
a prostrate or semi-erect shrub of tropical America and Australia, noted for its sensitive leaflets that fold and droop when touched or at night, typically armed with recurved thorns.
sensitive-leaved shrub of tropical America and Australia

Examples

Children love watching the live-and-die because its leaves fold instantly when touched.
The live-and-die is often planted in gardens for its fascinating leaf movement.
Researchers studied the defensive mechanisms of the live-and-die in response to environmental stimuli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitive plant - plant whose leaflets fold when touched (Mimosa pudica)
