Castilian language

1. NOUN

/ kæˈstɪljən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kaˈstɪlɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the variety of Spanish spoken in Castile, often used to refer to standard Spanish
standard variety of Spanish (from Castile)

Examples

Many linguists consider the Castilian language to be the standard form of Spanish.
He learned the Castilian language before traveling to northern Spain.
The pronunciation in the Castilian language can differ from Spanish spoken in Latin America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in most of Spain and Latin America

Etymology

From Latin 'Castilla,' the region in Spain, plus 'language.'
