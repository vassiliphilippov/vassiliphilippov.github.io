coagulation factor

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌæɡjəˈleɪʃən ˈfæktər / (AmE), / kəʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən ˈfæktə / (BrE)
any of a group of substances in the blood, such as proteins, that are essential for blood clotting
blood-clotting substance (protein)
medical

Examples

A deficiency in a key coagulation factor can lead to excessive bleeding after injury.
Hemophilia is caused by a genetic lack of a specific coagulation factor.
Doctors measured his coagulation factor levels to diagnose the bleeding disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clotting factor - substance in blood necessary for clot formation
