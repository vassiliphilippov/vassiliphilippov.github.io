Norfolk Island pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔr.fək ˈaɪ.lənd paɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.fək ˈaɪ.lənd paɪn / (BrE)
an evergreen tree (Araucaria heterophylla) native to Norfolk Island and parts of Australia, often grown as an ornamental plant
an evergreen tree native to Norfolk Island

Examples

The living room was decorated with a Norfolk Island pine for the holidays.
Many homeowners choose the Norfolk Island pine as an indoor ornamental plant.
In the wild, a mature Norfolk Island pine can grow over fifty meters tall.

Etymology

Named after Norfolk Island, a territory in the South Pacific, and 'pine' due to its pine-like appearance.
