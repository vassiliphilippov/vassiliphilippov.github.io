lumber room

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚ ruːm / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbə ruːm / (BrE)
a room in a house used for storing unused furniture and other miscellaneous items
room for storing unwanted items
british

Examples

They found an old painting hidden in the lumber room while cleaning the house.
The lumber room was stacked high with broken chairs and dusty trunks.
She liked to explore the lumber room for forgotten treasures from her childhood.

Etymology

From 'lumber' (discarded or stored items) + 'room'; 'lumber' in this sense from British English meaning odds and ends.
