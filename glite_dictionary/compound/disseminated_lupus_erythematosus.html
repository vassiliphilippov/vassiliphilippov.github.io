disseminated lupus erythematosus

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsɛm.əˌneɪ.tɪd ˈluː.pəs ˌɛr.ɪ.θiː.məˈtoʊ.səs / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛm.ɪˌneɪ.tɪd ˈluː.pəs ˌɛr.ɪ.θiː.məˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
an inflammatory disease of connective tissue characterized by fever, weakness, fatigue, joint pain, and distinctive skin lesions, especially on the face, neck, or arms; commonly known as systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)
connective tissue disease with diverse symptoms
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with disseminated lupus erythematosus after developing a butterfly-shaped rash on her face.
Symptoms of disseminated lupus erythematosus often include joint pain, fatigue, and skin lesions.
Treatment for disseminated lupus erythematosus typically involves immunosuppressive medications and careful monitoring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systemic lupus erythematosus - an alternative term for disseminated lupus erythematosus, emphasizing its systemic nature

Etymology

From Latin lupus (wolf) and Greek erythema (redness); medical term dating from the late 19th century.
