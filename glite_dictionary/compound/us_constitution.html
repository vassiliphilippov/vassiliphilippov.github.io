U.S. Constitution

1. NOUN

/ juː ɛs ˌkɑnstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / juː ɛs ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the fundamental law of the United States, established in 1787, outlining the structure, functions, and limits of government
the foundational law of the United States

Examples

The U.S. Constitution guarantees certain rights and liberties to American citizens.
Scholars often study the U.S. Constitution to understand the country's legal foundation.
The Supreme Court interprets the U.S. Constitution in its decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitution - a system of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state is governed
