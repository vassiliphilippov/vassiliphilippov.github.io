US Constitution

1. PROPN

/ ju ɛs ˌkɑn.stɪˈtu.ʃən / (AmE), / juː ɛs ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the fundamental law of the United States, adopted in 1787, establishing the framework of the federal government and the rights of citizens
foundational law of the United States

Examples

The US Constitution guarantees freedom of speech as a fundamental right.
Legal scholars often debate the original meaning of the US Constitution.
The US Constitution was ratified by all thirteen original states by 1790.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitution - the system of fundamental laws and principles by which a country is governed
  • Degree Lower: Bill of Rights - the first ten amendments to the US Constitution
