course correction

1. NOUN

/ kɔrs kəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / kɔːs kəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
an adjustment to a plan or strategy intended to improve performance or results
adjustment to improve a plan or strategy

Examples

After realizing profits were declining, the board decided a course correction was necessary.
Her leadership was marked by an early course correction that turned the project around.
Sometimes a timely course correction can prevent a small problem from becoming a crisis.

Synset Examples

The company implemented a course correction after poor sales.
We need a course correction to meet our project goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustment - a small change to improve something
  • Synonym: revision - the act of revising or altering a plan

Etymology

Adapted from nautical terms where 'course' refers to a ship's direction and 'correction' means change to reach the intended destination.
