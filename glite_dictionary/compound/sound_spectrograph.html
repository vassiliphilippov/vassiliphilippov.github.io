sound spectrograph

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd spɛktɹəˌɡræf / (AmE), / saʊnd spɛktɹəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument used to produce a visual representation of the spectrum of frequencies in a sound signal
device displaying sound frequency spectrum

Examples

The sound spectrograph is commonly used in phonetics research to analyze speech patterns.
A sound spectrograph can reveal hidden frequencies in a musical recording.
They used a sound spectrograph to compare the acoustic differences between bird calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrograph - an instrument for dispersing radiation into a spectrum and recording it
