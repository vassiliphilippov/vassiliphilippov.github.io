picric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkrɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪkrɪk ˌasɪd / (BrE)
a yellow, highly toxic and explosive acid used in high explosives, dyes, and some chemical reactions
a yellow, explosive acid used in explosives and dyes
medical

Examples

The laboratory stored picric acid in a special containment area because of its explosive nature.
Early dyes often contained picric acid to achieve vivid yellow colors.
Because picric acid is highly toxic, safety protocols must be strictly followed during its handling.

Etymology

From French 'picrique', from Greek 'pikros' (bitter), referring to its bitter taste.
