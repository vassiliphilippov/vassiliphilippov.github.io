constant of gravitation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnstənt əv ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt əv ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the universal physical constant (G) that relates the force of gravitational attraction between two bodies to their masses and the distance between them, as stated in Newton's law of gravitation.
universal constant in Newton's gravitation law
medical

Examples

The value of the constant of gravitation is crucial for calculations involving planetary motion.
Astronomers use the constant of gravitation when determining the masses of distant stars.
A small error in the constant of gravitation can lead to inaccurate predictions in physics experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravitational constant - the universal constant (G) in Newton's law of gravitation
