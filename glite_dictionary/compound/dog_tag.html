dog tag

1. NOUN

/ dɑɡ tæɡ / (AmE), / dɒɡ tæg / (BrE)
a small metal tag worn on a chain around the neck by military personnel, bearing identification information
military identification tag worn on a chain

Examples

Each soldier is required to wear a dog tag at all times during active duty.
During the war, the dog tag was essential for identification in emergencies.
She kept her grandfather's dog tag as a memento of his service.

Etymology

Originally from the resemblance to tags worn by pets for identification.

2. NOUN

/ dɔɡ tæɡ / (AmE), / dɒɡ tӕɡ / (BrE)
a small metal plate attached to a dog's collar that typically shows the dog's registration number and owner's contact information
metal plate with registration on a dog's collar

Examples

Every responsible pet owner should make sure their dog wears a dog tag in case it gets lost.
She engraved her phone number on the dog tag before attaching it to Max's collar.
The stray was identified by the dog tag hanging from its collar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog license - official permit to keep a dog, often linked to registration number
