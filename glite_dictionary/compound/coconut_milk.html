coconut milk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌkʌt mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌkʌt mɪlk / (BrE)
The milky or whitish liquid obtained from the grated flesh of a mature coconut, commonly used in cooking and beverages.
liquid extracted from coconut flesh

Examples

Add coconut milk to the curry to give it a rich, creamy texture.
Many vegan desserts use coconut milk as a substitute for dairy.
She poured coconut milk into her smoothie for extra flavor.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.kə.nʌt mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.kə.nʌt mɪlk / (BrE)
A white liquid extracted from the grated pulp of mature coconuts, used in cooking and beverages.
white liquid from grated coconut

Examples

The recipe calls for one cup of coconut milk to add creaminess to the curry.
She drinks iced coffee made with coconut milk for a dairy-free alternative.
They used coconut milk to make a traditional Thai dessert.
