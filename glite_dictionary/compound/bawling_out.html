bawling out

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
a severe scolding or reprimand, especially given loudly or harshly
a loud severe scolding

Examples

He got a bawling out from his boss for missing the deadline.
After the mistake, she gave her team a real bawling out.
Nobody likes receiving a bawling out in front of their colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval or scolding
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
