synovial fluid

1. NOUN

/ səˈnoʊviəl ˈfluɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊviəl ˈfluːɪd / (BrE)
a thick, slippery fluid secreted by the membrane lining joints and tendon sheaths, serving to lubricate and reduce friction
lubricating fluid in joints
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that synovial fluid is essential for smooth joint movement.
Arthritis can lead to changes in the composition of synovial fluid.
Lab tests showed an abnormal buildup of synovial fluid in the patient's knee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - a structure in the body where two bones are connected
  • Synonym: bursa - a small fluid-filled sac around joints

Etymology

From Greek 'syn' (together) + Latin 'ovum' (egg), referring to its egg-white-like consistency.
