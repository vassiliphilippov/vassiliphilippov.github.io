cargo cult

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrɡoʊ kʌlt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːɡəʊ kʌlt / (BrE)
A religious group, especially found in Melanesia, that believes in the imminent arrival of material wealth ('cargo') brought by spiritual or supernatural means, often in response to colonial influences.
Melanesian religious movement awaiting miraculous goods

Examples

The villagers developed a cargo cult after seeing Western supplies arrive by airplane.
Anthropologists have studied the rituals and beliefs of various cargo cults in the South Pacific.
The concept of a cargo cult is used metaphorically in discussions of imitating external features without understanding underlying principles.

Etymology

From 'cargo' (goods transported) and 'cult', arising from Melanesian reactions to Western contact.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrɡoʊ kʌlt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːɡəʊ kʌlt / (BrE)
A religious movement among Melanesian communities characterized by the belief that ancestral spirits or divine beings would deliver material wealth ('cargo'), often associated with Western goods, leading followers to imitate Western practices in anticipation of this event.
Melanesian belief in divine delivery of Western goods

Examples

Anthropologists have studied the rituals of the cargo cult to understand its origins in Melanesian culture.
The villagers began building imitation airstrips, hoping the cargo cult prophecy would attract planes loaded with goods.
The concept of the cargo cult highlights how cultural contact can result in new religious movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millenarianism - belief in a coming major transformation or salvation, often religious

Etymology

From 'cargo' (goods transported by ship or aircraft) and 'cult' (system of religious veneration), referring to a focus on Western material goods.
