infantile paralysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fən.taɪl pəˈræl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fəntaɪl pəˈral.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease, chiefly of children, caused by the poliovirus and characterized by inflammation of nerve cells in the brain stem and spinal cord, often resulting in paralysis; also known as poliomyelitis or polio.
poliomyelitis (polio), especially affecting children
medical

Examples

Many families lived in fear of infantile paralysis before the polio vaccine became available.
He contracted infantile paralysis as a child and used a wheelchair for much of his life.
The outbreak of infantile paralysis in the 1950s prompted widespread public health campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polio - abbreviated term for poliomyelitis, a viral disease causing paralysis
  • Synonym: poliomyelitis - medical term for the viral disease causing paralysis, especially in children

Etymology

From infantile (relating to infants) + paralysis (Greek *paralysis*, meaning 'looseness, palsy').
