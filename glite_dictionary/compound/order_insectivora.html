order Insectivora

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ɪnˌsɛktɪˈvɔrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ɪnˌsɛktɪˈvɔːrə / (BrE)
a formerly recognized order of small, insect-eating mammals including shrews, moles, hedgehogs, and tenrecs
former mammal order: shrews, moles, etc.

Examples

The order Insectivora once included animals such as shrews and hedgehogs.
Some mammals now placed in other orders were previously classified under order Insectivora.
The classification of order Insectivora has mostly been replaced by more accurate groups based on genetic evidence.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Insectivora, from Latin insectum (insect) + vorare (to devour).
