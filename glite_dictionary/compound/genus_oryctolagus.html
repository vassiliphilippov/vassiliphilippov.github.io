genus Oryctolagus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɔːˌrɪkˈtɒləɡəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɒrɪkˈtɒləɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of rabbits native to the Old World, including the European rabbit.
a genus of Old World rabbits

Examples

The genus Oryctolagus includes the widely known European rabbit.
Biologists have studied members of the genus Oryctolagus to understand rabbit behavior.
Domesticated rabbits are descended from species in the genus Oryctolagus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small, furry mammal with long ears

Etymology

From Greek oryktos (dug, burrowed) + lagōs (hare).
