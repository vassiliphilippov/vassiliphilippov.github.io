alpha test

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə tɛst / (AmE), / ˈælfə tɛst / (BrE)
a preliminary test of an experimental product, especially computer software, conducted by the developers to identify bugs before wider release
preliminary internal product test

Examples

The development team conducted an alpha test to detect major bugs before releasing the software to beta testers.
Only internal staff participate in the alpha test phase of our new application.
The purpose of an alpha test is to fix critical issues early in the development cycle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beta test - secondary test of a product, typically involving external users

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha', the first letter of the Greek alphabet, indicating the first stage of testing.
