Wilkes Land

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪlks lænd / (AmE), / ˈwɪlks land / (BrE)
a coastal region of Antarctica bordering the Indian Ocean, situated south of Australia; most of the area is claimed by Australia
region of Antarctica south of Australia

Examples

The research team established a base on Wilkes Land to study climate change.
Most of Wilkes Land remains covered by thick ice throughout the year.
Australia claims sovereignty over Wilkes Land in Antarctica.

Etymology

Named after Charles Wilkes, an American explorer and naval officer.
