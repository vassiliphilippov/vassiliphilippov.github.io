European wolf spider

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən wʊlf ˈspaɪdɚ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən wʊlf ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
a large southern European spider once thought to cause tarantism, characterized by uncontrollable bodily movement
southern European spider linked to tarantism

Examples

It was once believed that the European wolf spider could cause uncontrollable dancing in those it bit.
A specimen of the European wolf spider was found during a research expedition in southern Italy.
Legends about the European wolf spider gave rise to the fear of tarantism in medieval Europe.
