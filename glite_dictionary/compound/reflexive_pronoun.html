reflexive pronoun

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛksɪv ˈproʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun formed with ‘-self’ or ‘-selves’ (such as ‘myself’, ‘yourself’, ‘themselves’) that refers back to the subject of the clause and indicates that the subject and object of the verb are the same person or thing
pronoun referring back to the subject

Examples

She blamed herself for the mistake she made.
Please help yourself to some coffee if you like.
The cat cleaned itself after eating.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: personal pronoun - a pronoun used to refer to a particular person or thing

Etymology

From Latin 'reflexivus' (bending back) + 'pronoun'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛksɪv ˈproʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv ˈprəʊˌnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun that refers back to the subject of the same clause or sentence, such as 'myself,' 'yourself,' or 'themselves'
pronoun referring to the subject itself

Examples

In the sentence 'She taught herself French,' the word 'herself' is a reflexive pronoun.
Please check your work yourself before submitting it to the teacher.
They blamed themselves for the accident.

Synset Examples

The reflexive pronoun emphasizes the subject's action.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: personal pronoun - a pronoun representing a particular person
  • Degree Higher: intensive pronoun - a pronoun used for emphasis that refers back to a noun or pronoun

Etymology

From Latin 'reflexivus' (bent back) and 'pronomen' (pronoun).
