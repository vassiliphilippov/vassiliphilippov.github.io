moral hazard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl ˌhæzərd / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl ˌhæzəd / (BrE)
In economics, the situation where someone has less incentive to avoid risk because they are protected from its consequences, often by insurance.
economic risk-taking due to protection from consequences

Examples

After the government bailout, critics worried that a moral hazard would lead banks to take greater risks.
Comprehensive insurance can create a moral hazard if policyholders act less carefully with their property.
The company implemented stricter monitoring to reduce moral hazard among its employees.

Synset Examples

insurance companies are exposed to a moral hazard if the insured party is not honest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverse selection - the problem of attracting high-risk individuals when offering insurance
