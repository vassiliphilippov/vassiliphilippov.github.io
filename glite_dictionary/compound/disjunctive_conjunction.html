disjunctive conjunction

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdʒʌŋktɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒʌŋktɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a conjunction used to connect words or phrases that present alternative or mutually exclusive options, such as 'or' or 'either...or'
a conjunction indicating alternatives

Examples

'Or' is the most common disjunctive conjunction in English, used to offer choices between two options.
When you use a disjunctive conjunction, you typically present alternatives, such as in the phrase 'tea or coffee'.
In grammar, 'either...or' is an example of a disjunctive conjunction presenting mutually exclusive possibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunction - a word used to connect clauses or sentences

Etymology

From Latin disjunctus (separated) and conjunction (from Latin coniugere, to join together).
