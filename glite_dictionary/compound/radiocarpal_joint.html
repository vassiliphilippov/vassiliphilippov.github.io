radiocarpal joint

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈkɑrpəl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪəʊˈkɑːpəl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
the joint between the distal end of the radius bone and the proximal row of carpal bones, forming the main wrist joint
main wrist joint between radius and carpal bones
medical

Examples

A fracture near the radiocarpal joint can significantly affect wrist mobility.
The doctor examined the patient's radiocarpal joint for signs of swelling and tenderness.
Proper alignment of the radiocarpal joint is essential for normal hand function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrist joint - the joint connecting the forearm and the hand
  • Antonym: elbow joint - the joint connecting the upper arm and the forearm

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (spoke, forearm bone) and 'carpus' (wrist), with 'joint' meaning the place where bones meet.
