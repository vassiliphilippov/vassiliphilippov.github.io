Argentine hemorrhagic fever

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdʒɛnˌtin ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑː.dʒənˌtiːn ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
A severe infectious disease caused by the Junin virus, characterized by fever, bleeding, and neurological symptoms, found mainly in Argentina.
viral hemorrhagic disease in Argentina
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of Argentine hemorrhagic fever have been reported in rural areas of the country.
Researchers are developing vaccines to prevent Argentine hemorrhagic fever among at-risk populations.
Symptoms of Argentine hemorrhagic fever include high fever, bleeding, and neurological complications.
