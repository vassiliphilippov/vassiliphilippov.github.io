ring-tailed cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋ teɪld kæt / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ teɪld kæt / (BrE)
a raccoon-like mammal of Mexico and the southwestern United States with a long bushy tail marked by black and white rings
omnivorous mammal with a ringed bushy tail

Examples

A ring-tailed cat was spotted searching for food near the campsite in Arizona.
The ring-tailed cat is known for its agility and striking black-and-white tail.
Despite its name, the ring-tailed cat is more closely related to raccoons than to domestic cats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bassarisk - another name for the ring-tailed cat
  • Synonym: raccoon - a similar omnivorous mammal with a ringed tail, related to the ring-tailed cat
