Roman alphabet

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
the alphabet developed by the ancient Romans, used for writing most languages of western Europe, including English
writing system from ancient Rome, used widely in Europe

Examples

The English language is written using the Roman alphabet.
Many European languages, such as French and Spanish, also use the Roman alphabet.
The Roman alphabet has 26 letters in modern English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin alphabet - another term for the Roman alphabet, especially in linguistic contexts

Etymology

From Latin litterae Romanae, referring to the writing system of ancient Rome.
