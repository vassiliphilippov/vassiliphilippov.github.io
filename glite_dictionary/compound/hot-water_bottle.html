hot-water bottle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt ˌwɔtɚ ˌbɑtəl / (AmE), / ˈhɒt ˌwɔːtə ˌbɒtl̩ / (BrE)
a rubber or plastic container, usually with a stopper, designed to be filled with hot water and used to provide warmth to a bed or to soothe parts of the body
rubber container for hot water to provide warmth

Examples

She placed a hot-water bottle at her feet before going to sleep on the chilly night.
The nurse gave him a hot-water bottle to help ease his stomach pain.
My grandmother always travels with her hot-water bottle during the winter months.
