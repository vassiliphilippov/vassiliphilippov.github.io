to break the cycle of poverty

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə ˈsaɪkəl əv ˈpɑvɚti / (AmE), / breɪk ðə ˈsaɪkl əv ˈpɒvəti / (BrE)
to end the persistent pattern in which poverty continues from one generation to the next
end generational poverty

Examples

Many social programs aim to break the cycle of poverty by providing better education and job opportunities.
She hopes her children will break the cycle of poverty that affected her family for generations.
Investments in early childhood education are crucial to breaking the cycle of poverty.

Synset Examples

We need to break the cycle of poverty in our community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end poverty - bring an end to the state of being poor
