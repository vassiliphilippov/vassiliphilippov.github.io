Hokkienese language

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈkjɛniz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɒˈkjɛnɪz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of Chinese dialects spoken mainly in the Fujian (Fukien) province of southeastern China and by overseas Chinese communities; also called Hokkien or Minnan
Chinese dialect group of Fujian province

Examples

Many people in Taiwan speak Hokkienese language in their daily lives.
She grew up in a family that uses both Mandarin and Hokkienese language at home.
The Hokkienese language has a rich vocabulary influenced by centuries of trade and migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hokkien - the main dialect of the Minnan language family spoken in Southeast China and among overseas Chinese
  • Degree Lower: Cantonese language - Chinese language variety spoken in Guangdong province and Hong Kong
  • Degree Higher: Mandarin Chinese - the standard and most widely spoken variety of Chinese

Etymology

From 'Hokkien', local pronunciation of 'Fujian' (福建) province in southeastern China; '-ese' denotes a language or people.
