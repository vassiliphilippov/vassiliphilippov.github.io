California black walnut

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈfɔrnjə blæk ˈwɔlnʌt / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈfɔːniə blæk ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
a medium-sized tree native to California, with aromatic compound leaves and edible nuts, scientifically known as Juglans hindsii
a tree species native to California

Examples

The California black walnut is valued both for its nuts and its striking appearance in the landscape.
Many restoration projects use California black walnut to support local wildlife and preserve native plant communities.
The leaves of the California black walnut release a subtle fragrance when crushed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black walnut - any walnut tree with dark wood and edible nuts, esp. Juglans nigra or similar species
