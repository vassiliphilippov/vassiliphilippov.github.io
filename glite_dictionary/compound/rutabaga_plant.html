rutabaga plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːtəˈbeɪɡə plænt / (AmE), / ˌruːtəˈbɑːɡə plɑːnt / (BrE)
a cruciferous plant (Brassica napus Napobrassica group) with a thick, bulbous, edible yellow root, commonly grown as a root vegetable
plant yielding the rutabaga root

Examples

The rutabaga plant is often harvested in autumn for its sweet, yellow root.
Farmers rotate cabbage, rutabaga plant, and broccoli to prevent disease in the soil.
He carefully weeded his garden, making sure the rutabaga plant had enough space to grow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rutabaga - the edible yellow root of the rutabaga plant
  • Degree Lower: turnip - a similar root vegetable from a closely related plant

Etymology

From Swedish 'rotabagge', from 'rot' (root) + 'bagge' (lump).
