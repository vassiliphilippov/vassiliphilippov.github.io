United States Customary System

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnəˈteɪd ˈsteɪts ˈkʌstəmɛri ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtɪd ˈsteɪts ˈkʌstəməri ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of weights and measures used in the United States, based chiefly on units such as the foot, pound, and pint, differing in some respects from the British Imperial System.
system of US weights and measures

Examples

American recipes often give ingredient quantities using the United States Customary System.
The United States Customary System uses feet and inches instead of meters and centimeters for length.
Many tools sold in the US are marked with both metric and United States Customary System measurements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: British Imperial System - the system of measurement used in the United Kingdom and some other countries
  • Antonym: International System of Units - the modern form of the metric system, known as SI
