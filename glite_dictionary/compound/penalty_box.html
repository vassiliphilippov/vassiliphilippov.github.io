penalty box

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəlti bɑks / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti bɒks / (BrE)
in ice hockey, a specially designated area, usually enclosed, where players serve time for penalties
area where penalized ice hockey players sit

Examples

After the fight, both players were sent to the penalty box for five minutes.
The team had to kill a penalty while their captain sat in the penalty box.
He watched the rest of the game from the penalty box after a hard foul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sin bin - informal term for the penalty box in ice hockey and other sports

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəlti bɑks / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti bɒks / (BrE)
an area in sports such as ice hockey where players sit to serve penalty time after committing an infraction
area for penalized players in some sports

Examples

The referee sent him to the penalty box for high-sticking.
After spending five minutes in the penalty box, she returned to the game with renewed energy.
He watched his team score a goal while he was still in the penalty box.

Synset Examples

After the foul, the player was sent to the penalty box.
She watched nervously from the penalty box as her team played.
He spent two minutes in the penalty box for roughing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sin bin - a box or area for penalized players, especially in sports; informal term
