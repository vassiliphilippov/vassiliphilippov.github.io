European smelt

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən smɛlt / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən smɛlt / (BrE)
a small silvery fish (Osmerus eperlanus) native to European waters, known for its delicate flavor and seasonal appearance.
a small silvery fish native to Europe

Examples

Fishermen catch European smelt in large numbers during their spring migration to freshwater rivers.
The delicate taste of European smelt makes it a popular dish in some European countries.
Biologists studied the spawning habits of the European smelt in coastal estuaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - a small silvery fish of the family Osmeridae found in northern waters

2. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən smɛlt / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən smɛlt / (BrE)
A small, slender fish native to European rivers and coastal waters, known for its silvery body and delicate flavor.
a small silvery fish of European waters

Examples

The European smelt migrates upstream to spawn each spring.
Fishermen in the Netherlands often catch European smelt for local markets.
The delicate taste of European smelt is prized in many traditional European dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - a small, silvery fish found in northern waters
