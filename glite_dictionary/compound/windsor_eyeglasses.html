Windsor eyeglasses

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnzər ˈaɪˌɡlæsɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɪnzə ˈaɪˌɡlɑːsɪz / (BrE)
A style of eyeglasses with small, round frames made of thin wire or tortoiseshell, popularized in the early 20th century and associated with figures such as John Lennon.
eyeglasses with round wire or tortoiseshell frames

Examples

He wore classic Windsor eyeglasses that gave him a vintage look.
Vintage shops often carry Windsor eyeglasses similar to those made famous by John Lennon.
Many fashion enthusiasts prefer the timeless appearance of Windsor eyeglasses.

Synset Examples

Windsors are very chic this season.
