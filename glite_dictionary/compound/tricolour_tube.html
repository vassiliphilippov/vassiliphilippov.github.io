tricolour tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌkʌlɚ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌkʌlə tjuːb / (BrE)
a type of colour television tube that combines the three primary colours to produce the full spectrum of colours on the screen
colour TV tube using three primary colours

Examples

The old television set uses a tricolour tube to display images in full colour.
Engineers worked to improve the brightness and clarity of the tricolour tube.
Many vintage TVs with a tricolour tube are now collectors' items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathode ray tube - a vacuum tube in which images are produced when an electron beam strikes a phosphorescent surface
