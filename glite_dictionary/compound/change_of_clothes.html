change of clothes

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪndʒ əv kloʊðz / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ əv kləʊðz / (BrE)
an extra set of clothes to be worn instead of one's current clothing, typically brought in case of need
an extra set of clothes

Examples

After spilling coffee on his shirt, he was glad to have a change of clothes at the office.
Campers are advised to bring at least one change of clothes in case they get wet.
She always keeps a change of clothes in her locker for emergencies.

Synset Examples

I always keep a change of clothes in my car.
She packed a change of clothes for the trip.
