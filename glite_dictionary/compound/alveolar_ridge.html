alveolar ridge

1. NOUN

/ ælˈviələr rɪdʒ / (AmE), / alˈviːələ rɪdʒ / (BrE)
the bony ridge that forms the borders of the upper and lower jaws and contains the sockets of the teeth
ridge in the jaw holding teeth sockets
medical

Examples

The dentist examined the patient's alveolar ridge to check for any signs of bone loss.
Some speech sounds are produced by touching the tongue to the alveolar ridge.
After tooth extraction, the alveolar ridge may gradually change shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum - the soft tissue covering the alveolar ridge

Etymology

From Latin alveolus (small cavity) and ridge (raised strip).
