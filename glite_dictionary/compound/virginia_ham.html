Virginia ham

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə hæm / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniə hæm / (BrE)
A type of lean, dry-cured ham from Virginia, United States, known for its distinctive hickory-smoked flavor and dark red meat.
lean, hickory-smoked ham from Virginia
american

Examples

We served Virginia ham and biscuits at the holiday brunch.
The smoky flavor of Virginia ham makes it perfect for sandwiches and salads.
During her trip to the South, she toured a farm famous for its Virginia ham.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Virginia, where this style of ham was developed.
