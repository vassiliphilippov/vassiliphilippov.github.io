first lady

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
The most prominent or influential woman in a particular field, group, or profession.
leading woman in a field

Examples

In jazz, Ella Fitzgerald is often dubbed the first lady of song.
She is regarded as the first lady of American theater for her decades of outstanding performances.
The first lady of finance delivered the keynote speech at the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queen - the woman considered best or most important in a particular sphere

Etymology

Modeled after the term for the spouse of the head of state, extended figuratively to other domains.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
the wife of a president or other head of state, especially in a republic; also sometimes used for the wife of a governor or other high-ranking official.
wife of a president or leader

Examples

The first lady attended the opening ceremony of the new hospital.
As first lady, she championed several important social causes.
Many people admire the charitable work done by the first lady of the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: president - an elected head of a republic, especially in government
