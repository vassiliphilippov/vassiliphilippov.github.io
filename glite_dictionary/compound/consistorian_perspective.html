consistorian perspective

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪstɔriən pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / kənˌsɪstɔːriən pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
a viewpoint or position based on the principles or practices of church governance, especially those associated with a church consistory
viewpoint based on church governance

Examples

Her consistorian perspective shaped the committee's decisions.
The report reflected a consistorian perspective influenced by centuries of ecclesiastical tradition.
Many policies were developed from a distinctly consistorian perspective within the church synod.

Synset Examples

Her consistorian perspective shaped the committee's decisions.

Etymology

From 'consistory' (council or governing body in some Christian churches) and 'perspective.'
