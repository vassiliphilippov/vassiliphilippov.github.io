resistance thermometer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstəns θərˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A thermometer that measures temperature by detecting changes in the electrical resistance of a platinum wire coil.
thermometer using electrical resistance to measure temperature

Examples

A resistance thermometer is often used in laboratories for precise temperature measurements.
The accuracy of the resistance thermometer makes it valuable in industrial applications.
Technicians calibrated the resistance thermometer before starting the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermistor - a type of resistor used as a thermal sensor by measuring resistance to infer temperature
