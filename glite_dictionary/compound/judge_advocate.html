judge advocate

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒ ˈædvəkət / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒ ˈædvəkət / (BrE)
an officer in the armed forces who acts as a legal adviser and is responsible for the administration of military justice
military legal officer
legal

Examples

The judge advocate provided legal counsel during the court-martial.
Each unit appoints a judge advocate to oversee military justice procedures.
After consulting the judge advocate, the commander proceeded with the inquiry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military lawyer - a lawyer who practices law within the military justice system

Etymology

From 'judge' and 'advocate'; 'advocate' from Latin 'advocatus', one called to aid.

2. NOUN

/ dʒʌdʒ ˈædvəkət / (AmE), / dʒʌdʒ ˈadvəkət / (BrE)
A staff officer who serves as a legal adviser to a military commander, responsible for military legal matters and courts-martial.
military legal officer/adviser
legal medical

Examples

The judge advocate explained the legal implications of the operation to the commanding officer.
During the court-martial, the judge advocate ensured all procedures were properly followed.
She was appointed as a judge advocate in the Army after completing law school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military lawyer - an attorney specializing in legal matters within the armed forces

Etymology

From 'judge' (Middle English from Old French) and 'advocate' (from Latin advocatus, 'one called to aid').
