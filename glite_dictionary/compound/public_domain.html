public domain

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌblɪk doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪk dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
the state of belonging or being available to the public as a whole, especially when property rights have expired, been forfeited, or are inapplicable
freely available to the public; not privately owned

Examples

The works of Shakespeare are in the public domain and can be freely performed by anyone.
When the patent expired, the invention entered the public domain.
Many classic films are now part of the public domain and available for free download.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: copyright - exclusive legal right to reproduce or publish a work
