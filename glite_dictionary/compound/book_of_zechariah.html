Book of Zechariah

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Zechariah, focused mainly on the renewal of Israel after the Babylonian captivity
Old Testament prophetic book

Examples

The Book of Zechariah contains visions that encouraged the Israelites to rebuild the Temple.
Many scholars study the Book of Zechariah for its apocalyptic imagery and messianic themes.
He referenced the Book of Zechariah during his lecture on post-exilic prophets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zechariah - the prophet whose visions form the basis of the book
  • Synonym: Book of Haggai - another prophetic book closely related in period and theme

2. NOUN

/ ˌbʊk əv ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (AmE), / ˌbʊk əv ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Zechariah, mainly focused on the renewal of Israel after the Babylonian Captivity
Old Testament book of prophecies by Zechariah

Examples

The Book of Zechariah contains visions about the restoration of Jerusalem.
Scholars have debated the authorship of the Book of Zechariah for centuries.
During the service, several passages from the Book of Zechariah were read aloud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old Testament - the first part of the Christian Bible, corresponding to the Hebrew Scriptures
  • Degree Higher: Book of Malachi - the last book of the Old Testament, following Zechariah

Etymology

Zechariah from Hebrew Zəḵaryāh meaning 'Yahweh has remembered'.
