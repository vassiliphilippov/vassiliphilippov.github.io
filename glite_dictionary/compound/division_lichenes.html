division Lichenes

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən lɪˈkiːniːz / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒ.ən lɪˈkiːniːz / (BrE)
a former taxonomic division comprising lichens, which are symbiotic associations between fungi and algae or cyanobacteria; sometimes treated as an independent group alongside algae and fungi
former taxonomic group of lichens

Examples

Botanists no longer classify lichens under the division Lichenes in modern taxonomy.
The division Lichenes once grouped all lichen-forming organisms together, regardless of their fungal partners.
Historical texts often refer to the division Lichenes as a major group alongside fungi and algae.

Etymology

From Latin 'lichenes', plural of 'lichen', meaning lichen (a type of symbiotic organism).
