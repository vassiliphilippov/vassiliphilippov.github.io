stem ginger

1. NOUN

/ stɛm ˈdʒɪndʒər / (AmE), / stɛm ˈdʒɪndʒə / (BrE)
fresh young rhizomes of the ginger plant, often preserved in syrup and used in cooking or baking
young ginger rhizome; often preserved for cooking

Examples

My grandmother loves to use stem ginger in her fruitcakes for an extra kick of flavor.
The recipe calls for three pieces of chopped stem ginger in syrup.
You can often find jars of preserved stem ginger in the baking section of the supermarket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - the rhizome of the ginger plant used as spice or flavoring
