family Bacteroidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bækˌtɪroɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bækˌtɪərəʊɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of bacteria usually found in the digestive tract or on mucous surfaces of warm-blooded animals, sometimes linked to acute infections.
a family of bacteria found in animals
medical

Examples

The family Bacteroidaceae plays a significant role in the gut microbiome of mammals.
Researchers studied the antibiotic resistance patterns within the family Bacteroidaceae.
Some species belonging to the family Bacteroidaceae can cause infections under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bacteroides - a genus of bacteria within the family Bacteroidaceae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Bacteroides' (genus name) + '-aceae', a standard suffix for bacterial families.
