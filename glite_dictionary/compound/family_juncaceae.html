family Juncaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒʌŋˈkeɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dʒʌŋˈkeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of tufted, grass-like herbs commonly known as rushes, found in damp habitats worldwide.
plant family of rushes

Examples

The family Juncaceae includes about 400 species of rushes found worldwide.
Botanists distinguish the family Juncaceae from grasses by their solid stems and simple flowers.
Many wetlands are dominated by plants from the family Juncaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a grass-like plant of wet places in the family Juncaceae
  • Degree Lower: family Poaceae - the botanical family of true grasses

Etymology

From Latin 'Juncus' (rush) + botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
