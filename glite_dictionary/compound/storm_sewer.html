storm sewer

1. NOUN

/ stɔrm ˈsuː.ər / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈsjuː.ə / (BrE)
an underground system of drains designed to carry off rainwater and surface water from streets, roofs, and other surfaces
drainage system for rainwater

Examples

After the heavy rain, water flowed directly into the storm sewer to prevent street flooding.
Leaves and debris blocking the storm sewer caused significant water buildup during the storm.
City workers inspected the storm sewer system to ensure it was functioning properly before hurricane season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm drain - a channel or pipe that carries away surplus rainwater
