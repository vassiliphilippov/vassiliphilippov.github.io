great horned owl

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt hɔːrnd aʊl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt hɔːnd aʊl / (BrE)
a large North American owl with prominent ear tufts and mottled brown plumage
large North American owl with ear tufts

Examples

At dusk, a great horned owl called from the trees near the cabin.
Farmers appreciate the great horned owl for its role in controlling rodent populations.
The feathers of the great horned owl provide excellent camouflage in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with large forward-facing eyes
