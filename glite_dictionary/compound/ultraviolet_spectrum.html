ultraviolet spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the range of electromagnetic wavelengths just shorter than visible light, known as ultraviolet radiation
range of ultraviolet electromagnetic wavelengths

Examples

The ultraviolet spectrum is used in astronomy to study stars that emit high-energy radiation.
Certain materials fluoresce when exposed to light in the ultraviolet spectrum.
Scientists use spectrometers to measure the intensity of light across the ultraviolet spectrum.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: visible spectrum - the portion of the electromagnetic spectrum visible to the human eye

Etymology

ultraviolet from Latin ultra (beyond) + violet; spectrum from Latin spectrum (appearance, image)
