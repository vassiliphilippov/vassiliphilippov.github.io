1. ADJ

/ ə ˈkʌpəl əv / (AmE), / ə ˈkʌpl əv / (BrE)
a small, indefinite number of (typically two but sometimes a few)
a small, indefinite number

Examples

Could you hand me a couple of pens from the desk?
She waited a couple of minutes before replying to the email.
There are a couple of things I need to discuss with you.

2. DET

/ ə ˈkʌpəl əv / (AmE), / ə ˈkʌpəl ɒv / (BrE)
a small number of things or people, usually two, but sometimes a few
two or a few of something

Examples

She invited a couple of friends to join us for dinner.
I have a couple of questions about the schedule before we start.
Could you please wait a couple of minutes while I finish this call?

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: several - more than two but not many
