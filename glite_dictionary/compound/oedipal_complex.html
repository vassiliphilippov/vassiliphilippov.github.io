Oedipal complex

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdɪpəl kəmˈplɛks / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪpəl ˈkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
in psychoanalysis, a child's unconscious sexual desire for the opposite-sex parent and hostility toward the same-sex parent, especially as experienced by boys toward their mothers and fathers
psychoanalytic concept of child-parent desire/conflict
medical

Examples

Freud's theory of the Oedipal complex has significantly influenced how we understand early childhood development.
The film explores the protagonist's conflicted feelings, which seem rooted in an unresolved Oedipal complex.
Some psychologists believe that the Oedipal complex can affect adult relationships if not properly resolved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Electra complex - psychoanalytic concept: girl's sexual attraction to her father and rivalry with her mother

Etymology

From the name Oedipus, a character in Greek mythology who unwittingly killed his father and married his mother, plus 'complex'.
