light intensity

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (AmE), / laɪt ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (BrE)
the measurable amount of visible light, usually expressed in candelas, emitted from a source in a particular direction
measurable amount of visible light

Examples

The light intensity in the laboratory needs to be adjusted for accurate experiments.
Plants grow best when exposed to a suitable light intensity for their species.
He measured the light intensity in the room using a photometer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brightness - the quality of being bright; how much light is emitted or reflected
  • Synonym: luminous intensity - the amount of visible light emitted in a particular direction, measured in candelas
