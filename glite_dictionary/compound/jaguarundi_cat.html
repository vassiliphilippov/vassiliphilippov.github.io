jaguarundi cat

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæɡwəˈrʌndi kæt / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɡwəˈrʌndi kæt / (BrE)
a long-bodied, long-tailed wildcat native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas
a tropical American wildcat

Examples

The jaguarundi cat is known for its sleek, weasel-like appearance and secretive behavior.
Unlike most felines, the jaguarundi cat is active during the day and travels widely in search of food.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats critical to the survival of the jaguarundi cat in Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaguarundi - the same wildcat species; alternative headword

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jaguarundi', from Old Tupi 'îaguarundí', referring to this wildcat.
