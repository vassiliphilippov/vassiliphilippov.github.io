Cumberland Plateau

1. PROPN

/ ˈkʌmbərlənd pləˈtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbələnd plæˈtəʊ / (BrE)
a plateau forming the southwestern part of the Appalachian Mountains, extending through parts of Kentucky, Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia.
plateau in the southwestern Appalachians

Examples

The Cumberland Plateau is known for its scenic beauty and extensive cave systems.
Many geologists study the rock formations of the Cumberland Plateau to learn about Earth's history.
Hikers enjoy exploring the rugged trails throughout the Cumberland Plateau region.

Etymology

Named after the Cumberland River and Cumberland region, itself derived from the English county of Cumberland.
