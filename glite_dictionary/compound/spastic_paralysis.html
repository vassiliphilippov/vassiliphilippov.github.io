spastic paralysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæstɪk pəˈræləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈspæstɪk pəˈraləsɪs / (BrE)
A form of paralysis characterized by a loss or deficiency of motor control with involuntary muscle spasms, typically caused by permanent brain damage present at birth.
a type of paralysis with muscle spasms
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with spastic paralysis after showing persistent muscle stiffness and involuntary movements.
Modern therapies aim to improve quality of life for patients with spastic paralysis.
Physical therapy can help manage the symptoms of spastic paralysis in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralysis - loss of ability to move one or more muscles

Etymology

From Greek spasmos (spasm) and paralysis (loss of muscle function), via Latin.
