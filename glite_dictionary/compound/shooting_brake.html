shooting brake

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːtɪŋ breɪk / (AmE), / ˈʃuːtɪŋ breɪk / (BrE)
a car with an extended rear cargo area and a rear door, similar to a station wagon; originally referring to a vehicle for carrying shooting parties and their equipment.
car similar to a station wagon

Examples

The classic Aston Martin shooting brake was a rare and luxurious vehicle.
He bought a vintage shooting brake for road trips with the family.
Many European carmakers now offer stylish shooting brakes based on their sports models.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station wagon - a car with an extended rear cargo area and a rear door

Etymology

Originally referred to horse-drawn vehicles used for shooting parties in Britain; 'brake' meant a type of open carriage.
