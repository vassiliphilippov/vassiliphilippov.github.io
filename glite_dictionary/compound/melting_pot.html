melting pot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛltɪŋ pɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ pɒt / (BrE)
A vessel made of material that does not melt easily, used for high-temperature chemical reactions and for melting or fusing substances.
vessel for high-temperature chemical reactions

Examples

The chemist carefully placed the metal samples in the melting pot before starting the experiment.
Ceramic melting pots are commonly used in laboratories because they can withstand extreme temperatures.
After hours in the melting pot, the mixture had fused into a single alloy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucible - a container used for melting or subjecting substances to high temperatures

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛltɪŋ pɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ pɒt / (BrE)
A place or environment in which people of different backgrounds, cultures, or races are assimilated into a cohesive whole.
place of cultural assimilation

Examples

New York City is often described as a melting pot because of its diverse population.
The classroom became a melting pot where students from all over the world learned together.
America has been called a melting pot for its history of embracing immigrants from many countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salad bowl - a society in which distinct cultural groups remain separate but coexist

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛltɪŋ pɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ pɒt / (BrE)
A place or environment in which people of different backgrounds, cultures, or ideas blend and mix together, often forming a new, unified whole.
place where cultures blend

Examples

New York has long been celebrated as a melting pot of different cultures and traditions.
Her classroom was a melting pot of students from all over the world.
Modern cities often serve as a melting pot, blending languages, foods, and beliefs.

Synset Examples

New York City is a true melting pot of cultures.
The restaurant is a melting pot of global cuisines.
In a melting pot, diverse ideas thrive together.
He described Islamsterdam as a melting pot of cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salad bowl - a place where diverse cultures coexist but retain their distinctiveness

Etymology

From the literal use of 'melting pot' (a container used to melt substances) as a metaphor for social blending, first popularized in the early 20th century.
