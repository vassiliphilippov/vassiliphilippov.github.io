straight arrow

1. NOUN

/ streɪt ˈæroʊ / (AmE), / streɪt ˈærəʊ / (BrE)
A person who is honest, trustworthy, and follows conventional moral standards.
person of integrity and high morals

Examples

Everyone trusts Sarah because she's a real straight arrow in both her work and personal life.
As a politician, he built his reputation as a straight arrow who refused to take bribes.
Despite the pressure to cheat, she remained a straight arrow and followed the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goody-goody - a person who behaves extremely well to please authority
  • Antonym: crook - a dishonest person, especially a criminal

2. NOUN

/ streɪt ˈæroʊ / (AmE), / streɪt ˈærəʊ / (BrE)
A person regarded as honest, ethical, and straightforward.
an honest, ethical person

Examples

Despite pressure from his colleagues, Mark remained a straight arrow and refused to compromise his values.
The company is looking for a straight arrow to manage its finances honestly.
Everyone trusts her because she's a real straight arrow in business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straight shooter - a person who is honest and direct
