Jarvik artificial heart

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːrvɪk ɑːrˈtɪfɪʃəl hɑːrt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːvɪk ɑːˈtɪfɪʃəl hɑːt / (BrE)
a type of artificial heart, developed by Dr. Robert Jarvik, that can temporarily replace the function of a human heart
type of artificial heart device
medical

Examples

The patient received a Jarvik artificial heart as a temporary measure while waiting for a transplant.
The invention of the Jarvik artificial heart marked a breakthrough in cardiac surgery.
Doctors closely monitored the first person fitted with a Jarvik artificial heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial heart - a mechanical device that replaces the human heart

Etymology

Named after Dr. Robert Jarvik, the biomedical engineer who led its development in the late 20th century.
