time out

1. NOUN

/ taɪm aʊt / (AmE), / taɪm aʊt / (BrE)
a short period when you stop doing something so that you can rest or do something else
short pause for rest or other activity

Examples

After working for three hours, she took a time out to stretch and relax.
The coach called a time out to discuss tactics with the players.
I need a quick time out before we continue this meeting.

Synset Examples

we took a 10-minute break
he took time out to recuperate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - a short period of rest from work or activity

2. NOUN

/ taɪm aʊt / (AmE), / taɪm aʊt / (BrE)
a short break from an activity or period of work, often to rest or recover
short break from activity

Examples

After a long meeting, the team took a time out to recharge.
The teacher gave the children a time out to calm down after the argument.
Sometimes it's important to take a time out from your busy schedule.

Synset Examples

The coach called for a time out to regroup the team.
She took a time out from work to focus on her health.
