boulder clay

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊldər kleɪ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldə kleɪ / (BrE)
A type of unstratified glacial deposit consisting of a mixture of clay, sand, gravel, and boulders.
mixed glacial deposit of clay, sand, and boulders

Examples

The fields in this region are covered by thick layers of boulder clay.
The boulder clay contains large stones left behind by retreating glaciers.
Geologists analyzed the boulder clay to better understand the history of the last ice age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: till - unstratified glacial drift including clay, sand, gravel, and boulders
