Canada moonseed

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnædə ˈmuːnˌsiːd / (AmE), / kəˈnædə ˈmuːnsiːd / (BrE)
a woody vine native to eastern North America, with large oval leaves, small white flowers, and purple to blue-black fruits
North American woody vine with blue-black fruits

Examples

The fruit of the Canada moonseed is easily mistaken for wild grapes but is poisonous.
Botanists studied how Canada moonseed climbed through the forest understory.
You can recognize Canada moonseed by its distinctive moon-shaped seeds.
