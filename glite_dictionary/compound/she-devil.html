she-devil

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiˌdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃiːˌdɛv.əl / (BrE)
A woman considered to be cruel, vicious, or wicked; a female who behaves in a markedly malicious or spiteful way.
a cruel or wicked woman

Examples

Many people in the office called her a she-devil because of her ruthless management style.
In the story, the villain is a she-devil who stops at nothing to get her way.
His ex-wife was unfairly labeled a she-devil by the local tabloids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil - an evil spirit; a cruel or wicked man
  • Antonym: angel - a kind or good person, especially a woman

Etymology

Formed in English from 'she' (female) + 'devil'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃi ˌdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃiː ˌdɛvəl / (BrE)
A woman regarded as evil, malicious, or fiercely cruel.
an evil or fiercely malicious woman

Examples

Rumors spread quickly that the new boss was a she-devil who tolerated no mistakes.
He warned his friends that his ex-wife could turn into a she-devil during arguments.
Many fairy tales feature a she-devil as the main antagonist, feared by all.

Synset Examples

He called her a she-devil after their breakup.
Everyone knows she’s a she-devil when angry.
Watch out for her; she's a real she-devil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil - a wicked or cruel person (male or generic)
  • Antonym: angel - a kind or good person, especially a woman

Etymology

Formed from 'she' + 'devil', attested in English since the 17th century.
