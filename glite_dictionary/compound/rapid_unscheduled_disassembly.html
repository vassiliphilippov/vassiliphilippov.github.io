rapid unscheduled disassembly

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd ˌʌnˈskɛdʒuəld dɪsəmˈbli / (AmE), / ˈræpɪd ˌʌnˈʃɛdjuəld dɪsˈæmbli / (BrE)
a humorous or technical euphemism for a sudden and catastrophic failure, especially an explosion, often used in aerospace or engineering contexts
sudden and catastrophic failure (euphemism)

Examples

After the rocket experienced a rapid unscheduled disassembly, engineers began an immediate investigation.
The press conference addressed yesterday's rapid unscheduled disassembly during the launch sequence.
They joked that the satellite's rapid unscheduled disassembly was not part of the mission plan.

Synset Examples

His report highlighted the risk of rapid unscheduled disassembly in their design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent expansion or blowing up
  • Synonym: failure - the act or state of failing to function
