Doctor of Divinity

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv dɪˈvɪnɪti / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv dɪˈvɪnɪti / (BrE)
a higher university degree in theology, usually awarded to scholars in religion or divinity
advanced academic degree in theology

Examples

After many years of study, he was awarded the Doctor of Divinity by the university.
The new archbishop holds a Doctor of Divinity degree from Oxford.
She published several books a decade after receiving her Doctor of Divinity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doctor of Theology - an advanced degree in theology

Etymology

From Latin 'divinitatis doctor', literally 'teacher of divinity'.
