tinfoil hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnˌfɔɪl hæt / (AmE), / ˈtɪnfɔɪl hæt / (BrE)
A hat made of tinfoil, typically worn (or imagined to be worn) by someone believing it protects against mind control, surveillance, or other perceived threats; often used as a symbol of paranoia or belief in conspiracy theories.
hat made of tinfoil, symbol of conspiracy belief

Examples

People joked that only someone wearing a tinfoil hat would believe such a story.
The internet forum was filled with tinfoil hat theories about government surveillance.
He made a tinfoil hat as a prank for the costume party.

Synset Examples

She always wore a tinfoily hat to ward off aliens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy theory - an explanation suggesting secret, often unlawful, plots by groups or organizations
