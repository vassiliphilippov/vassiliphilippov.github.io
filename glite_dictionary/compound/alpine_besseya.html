Alpine besseya

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn bəˈsiːjə / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn bəˈsiːjə / (BrE)
a small pale plant with dense spikes of pale bluish-violet flowers, found in high cold meadows from Wyoming and Utah to New Mexico
mountain plant with bluish-violet flowers

Examples

The rare Alpine besseya blooms briefly each summer in the Rocky Mountain meadows.
Hikers in Utah sometimes spot clusters of Alpine besseya along the ridgelines.
Botanists study the Alpine besseya to understand plant adaptation to cold, high-altitude environments.
