family Myrmeleontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli mɪrˌmiːliˈɒntaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli mɪˌmɪliːˈɒntaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects within the order Neuroptera, commonly known as antlions, characterized by their slender bodies and delicate wings.
insect family including antlions
medical

Examples

The family Myrmeleontidae is best known for species whose larvae construct pitfall traps to capture ants.
Several members of the family Myrmeleontidae are found in sandy environments worldwide.
Researchers studied the wing structure variation across the family Myrmeleontidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antlion - insect known for its predatory larvae, member of Myrmeleontidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Myrmeleontidae*, based on Greek *myrmēx* (ant) and *leōn* (lion).
