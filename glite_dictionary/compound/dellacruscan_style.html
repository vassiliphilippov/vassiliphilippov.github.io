Dellacruscan style

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛləˈkrʌskən staɪl / (AmE), / ˌdɛləkˈrʌskən staɪl / (BrE)
a literary style marked by pedantry and narrow-minded attitudes toward art, especially associated with the Dellacruscan poets
pedantic, narrow-minded literary style

Examples

His poetry was criticized as being written in the Dellacruscan style, with excessive ornamentation and little substance.
Literary critics of the time often dismissed works in the Dellacruscan style for being overly intellectual and constrained.
Modern readers seldom encounter the Dellacruscan style outside of academic discussions on 18th-century literature.

Synset Examples

The Dellacruscan style of writing is rarely appreciated today.
She criticized his Dellacruscan views on art and literature.

Etymology

From 'Dellacruscan', referring to the 18th-century English literary group influenced by the Italian poet 'Della Crusca'.
