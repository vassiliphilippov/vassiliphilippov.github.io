glans clitoridis

1. NOUN

/ ɡlænz klɪˈtɔrɪdɪs / (AmE), / ɡlanz klɪˈtɒrɪdɪs / (BrE)
the small mass of sensitive erectile tissue at the end of the body of the clitoris
sensitive tissue at tip of the clitoris
medical

Examples

During the examination, the doctor inspected the glans clitoridis for signs of irritation.
The glans clitoridis is highly sensitive and plays an important role in sexual response.
Anatomy textbooks often include detailed diagrams of the glans clitoridis and surrounding tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clitoris - female erectile sexual organ

Etymology

From Latin *glans* (acorn, gland) and *clitoridis* (genitive of clitoris).
