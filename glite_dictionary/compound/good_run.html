good run

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd rʌn / (AmE), / ɡʊd rʌn / (BrE)
a period of success or positive performance, especially in sports, business, or life
period of success or positive performance

Examples

After three years of profit, the company finally ended its good run last quarter.
The tennis player had a good run at the tournament, making it to the semifinals.
Despite their good run, the team narrowly missed the championship title.

Synset Examples

She had a good run at the marathon last weekend.
That was a good run for our team this season.
He enjoyed a good run in the park every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winning streak - a series of wins or successes
  • Antonym: losing streak - a series of losses or bad results
