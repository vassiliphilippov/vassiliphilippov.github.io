macular area

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækjələr ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊlə ˈeəriə / (BrE)
a small yellowish central region of the retina, rich in cone cells, responsible for detailed and clear vision
central retinal area responsible for detailed vision
medical

Examples

The macular area is essential for tasks that require sharp vision, such as reading.
Diseases affecting the macular area can result in significant loss of central vision.
Ophthalmologists carefully examine the macular area during routine eye check-ups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macula lutea - the yellowish central area of the retina, also known as the macular area

Etymology

From Latin 'macula' (spot, stain), referring to the spot-like structure.
