ivory-thumper

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.vəɹ.i ˌθʌm.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.vəri ˌθʌm.pə / (BrE)
An informal, often jazz-influenced term for a pianist, especially one who plays with energy and flair.
informal term for a pianist
informal

Examples

The ivory-thumper played a lively solo that brought the crowd to its feet.
As a renowned ivory-thumper, she was invited to headline the city jazz festival.
Every Sunday, the club features a different ivory-thumper entertaining the late-night patrons.

Synset Examples

The ivory-thumper’s latest album has received rave reviews.
The ivory-thumper was invited to play at the charity event.
He’s an ivory-thumper who loves performing for large crowds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pianist - a person who plays the piano

Etymology

From 'ivory,' referring to piano keys (once made of ivory), and 'thumper,' someone who strikes repeatedly.
