hearty rumbullion

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrti rʌmˈbʌljən / (AmE), / ˈhɑːti rʌmˈbʌljən / (BrE)
A lively and noisy celebration or feast, often involving much eating and drinking.
lively, noisy celebration or feast
archaic

Examples

The village hosted a hearty rumbullion to mark the end of winter.
Families gathered for a hearty rumbullion filled with laughter, music, and dancing.
After the victory, the soldiers enjoyed a hearty rumbullion in the great hall.

Synset Examples

They celebrated with a hearty rumbullion after the harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revelry - lively and noisy festivities
  • Synonym: celebration - an event to mark a special occasion

Etymology

Rumbullion is an old word of uncertain origin, historically used to mean a noisy commotion or revelry.
