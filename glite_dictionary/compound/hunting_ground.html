hunting ground

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an area where wild animals are hunted, either for sport or food
area where animals are hunted

Examples

The forest has long been a favorite hunting ground for local hunters.
Ancient tribes defended their hunting grounds fiercely from outsiders.
The savannah serves as a rich hunting ground for lions and other predators.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌn.tɪŋ ˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.tɪŋ ˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a place or area where there are many opportunities to achieve something, especially where someone frequently seeks success or advantage
place with many opportunities

Examples

Silicon Valley is a hunting ground for ambitious tech entrepreneurs.
The job fair proved to be a fertile hunting ground for talented graduates.
Collectors see the market as a rich hunting ground for rare books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomping ground - a place where someone regularly spends time, often for fun or opportunities

Etymology

From the literal sense of an area used for hunting; figurative use dates from the 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌn.tɪŋ ˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.tɪŋ ˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a place where people regularly search for or pursue something they desire, such as opportunities, objects, or prey
place sought for opportunities or desired objects

Examples

The antique market is a popular hunting ground for collectors seeking rare items.
Many recruiters consider online forums a new hunting ground for fresh talent.
During the sale, the shop became a hunting ground for bargain hunters.

Synset Examples

The city park serves as a hunting ground for wildlife photographers.
This neighborhood is a great hunting ground for vintage furniture.
The old bookstore became a hunting ground for rare finds.

Etymology

From the literal use referring to land where animals are hunted, now also used figuratively.
