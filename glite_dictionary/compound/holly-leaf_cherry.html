holly-leaf cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːli lif ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈhɒli liːf ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A California evergreen shrub or small tree (Prunus ilicifolia) with spiny, leathery leaves and clusters of white flowers, related to the wild plum.
California evergreen shrub with spiny leaves

Examples

The holly-leaf cherry thrives in the chaparral regions of California.
Birds are often attracted to the fruit of the holly-leaf cherry in late summer.
She planted a holly-leaf cherry in her garden for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prunus ilicifolia - Scientific name for the holly-leaf cherry

Etymology

Named for its holly-like leaves and cherry-like fruit; scientific name Prunus ilicifolia.
