President Van Buren

1. PROPN

/ ˌprɛzɪdənt væn ˈbjʊrən / (AmE), / ˌprɛzɪdənt van ˈbjʊərən / (BrE)
Martin Van Buren, the 8th President of the United States, who served from 1837 to 1841.
8th President of the United States (1837–1841)

Examples

President Van Buren faced significant economic challenges during his time in office.
The political career of President Van Buren influenced the development of the Democratic Party.
Historians often debate the legacy of President Van Buren as America's eighth president.

Related Terms

Etymology

Van Buren is of Dutch origin; Martin Van Buren (1782–1862) was the 8th U.S. President.
