tree martin

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈmɑrtən / (AmE), / triː ˈmɑːtɪn / (BrE)
a species of swallow found in Australia and Polynesia that nests in tree cavities
an Australian and Polynesian swallow

Examples

The tree martin is commonly seen perching on wires in rural Australia.
Many tree martins nest in the cavities of dead trees near rivers.
Unlike other swallows, the tree martin prefers to use natural hollows for breeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small songbird with long pointed wings and a forked tail
