southern flying squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈskwɝl / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a small nocturnal squirrel of eastern North America, with large eyes and a membrane for gliding between trees
North American nocturnal gliding squirrel

Examples

The southern flying squirrel is known for its ability to glide between trees in the forest.
We spotted a southern flying squirrel in the oak woods after sunset.
Biologists study the habitats of the southern flying squirrel to understand its nocturnal behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern flying squirrel - a related flying squirrel species found in northern areas
