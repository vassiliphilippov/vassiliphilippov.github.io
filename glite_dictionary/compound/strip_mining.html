strip mining

1. NOUN

/ strɪp ˈmaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪp ˈmaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of removing soil and rock from the surface to extract ore or coal from near the earth's surface
surface mining technique for ore or coal

Examples

Strip mining has caused significant environmental damage in some regions.
The company switched from underground mining to strip mining to increase efficiency.
Many communities oppose strip mining because it scars the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-pit mining - a method of extracting rock or minerals from the earth by removal from an open pit
  • Antonym: underground mining - the process of mining underground deposits of ore or coal

Etymology

Formed from 'strip' (to remove a covering) and 'mining'.
