tomato blight

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ blaɪt / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ blaɪt / (BrE)
a disease affecting tomato plants, typically caused by fungal or mold pathogens, resulting in the deterioration of leaves, stems, or fruit
disease of tomato plants

Examples

Many farmers lost their crops due to tomato blight spreading rapidly through the region.
Proper crop rotation can help prevent tomato blight in home gardens.
Signs of tomato blight include dark spots on leaves and rotting fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blight - a plant disease, especially one caused by fungi
