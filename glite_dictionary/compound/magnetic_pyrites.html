magnetic pyrites

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk paɪˈraɪtiːz / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk paɪˈriːtiːz / (BrE)
a brownish iron sulfide mineral (FeS) with weak magnetic properties, also known as pyrrhotite
iron sulfide mineral with weak magnetism

Examples

The geologist identified the brownish specimen as magnetic pyrites due to its faint magnetic attraction.
Magnetic pyrites is often found associated with other iron-containing minerals in metamorphic rocks.
Because of its weak magnetism, magnetic pyrites can be distinguished from ordinary pyrite during mineral tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrrhotite - an iron sulfide mineral with weak magnetism (alternative name)
  • Degree Lower: pyrite - a yellow iron sulfide mineral, also called fool’s gold
