Trojan Horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒən hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒən hɔːs / (BrE)
A large hollow wooden horse used by the Greeks to secretly enter the city of Troy during the Trojan War.
wooden horse from Greek legend

Examples

The legend of the Trojan Horse has fascinated historians and writers for centuries.
They built a replica of the Trojan Horse for the museum exhibit.
The expression 'beware of Greeks bearing gifts' comes from the story of the Trojan Horse.

Etymology

From the story in ancient Greek mythology, where Greeks concealed soldiers in a wooden horse to enter Troy.
