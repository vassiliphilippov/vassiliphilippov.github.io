cordon bleu

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrˌdɑn ˈbluː / (AmE), / ˌkɔːdɒ̃ ˈblɜː / (BrE)
a chef who is renowned for their great skill and excellence in cooking
a chef of outstanding skill

Examples

After years of training, he was finally recognized as a cordon bleu by his peers.
Her reputation as a cordon bleu attracted diners from all over the city.
Only a true cordon bleu could prepare such an exquisite meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master chef - a highly skilled and experienced chef

Etymology

French, literally ‘blue ribbon’, from the blue ribbon worn by knights of the Order of the Holy Spirit (L’Ordre du Saint-Esprit), later associated with culinary excellence.
