Molisch test

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlɪʃ tɛst / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪʃ tɛst / (BrE)
a biochemical test for the presence of carbohydrates in a solution, in which a violet ring forms when a sample is treated with alpha-naphthol and sulfuric acid
test for detecting carbohydrates
medical

Examples

The Molisch test produced a distinct violet ring, confirming the presence of carbohydrates in the sample.
First-year biochemistry students often learn the Molisch test as part of carbohydrate analysis.
A positive Molisch test indicated that the unknown solution contained a significant amount of sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fehling's test - chemical test for detecting reducing sugars
  • Synonym: Benedict's test - chemical test for detecting reducing sugars

Etymology

Named after Austrian botanist Hans Molisch (1856–1937), who developed the test.
