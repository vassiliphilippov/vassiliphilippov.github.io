genus Ptilocercus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ptaɪloʊˈsɜrkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ptaɪləʊˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal, tree-dwelling mammals known as pentails, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of Southeast Asian pentail mammals

Examples

The genus Ptilocercus contains only one living species, the pen-tailed treeshrew.
Researchers discovered a new habitat of the genus Ptilocercus in Malaysia.
Members of the genus Ptilocercus are noted for their unique feather-like tails.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Ptilo-' (from Greek 'ptilon' meaning feather) and 'cercus' (from Greek 'kerkos' meaning tail).
