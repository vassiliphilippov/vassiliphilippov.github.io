pathologic process

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪk ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
an organic process in the body that occurs as a result of disease
organic process caused by disease
medical

Examples

Inflammation is a common pathologic process seen in many chronic diseases.
Cancer can trigger multiple pathologic processes in different organs of the body.
Understanding the pathologic process helps doctors determine the best treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathology - the study of disease or disease processes

Etymology

From Greek 'pathos' (suffering, disease) and Latin 'processus' (advance, procedure).
