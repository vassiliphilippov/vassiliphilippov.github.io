backhand stroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌhænd ˈstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbakhænd ˈstrəʊk / (BrE)
A stroke in tennis, table tennis, or similar sports made with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke.
stroke made with back of the hand facing forward

Examples

He hit a powerful backhand stroke that caught his opponent off guard.
Her backhand stroke has improved significantly since last season.
During the match, his winning point came from a flawless backhand stroke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forehand stroke - a stroke made with the palm of the hand facing the direction of the stroke
