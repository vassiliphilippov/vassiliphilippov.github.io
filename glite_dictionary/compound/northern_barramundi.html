northern barramundi

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɹðɚn ˌbæɹəˈmʊndi / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˌbærəˈmʊndi / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish native to northern Australian rivers, recognized by several reddish crescent-shaped spots on each scale
a large spotted Australian river fish

Examples

The northern barramundi is prized by anglers for its impressive size and fighting ability.
Researchers observed the unique crescent-shaped spots on the scales of the northern barramundi.
During our trip to northern Australia, we tasted grilled northern barramundi at a local restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barramundi - a related large Australian river fish species, Lates calcarifer

Etymology

From Australian English, 'barramundi' from an Aboriginal language of Queensland meaning 'large-scaled river fish'.
