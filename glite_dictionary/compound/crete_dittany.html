Crete dittany

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːt ˈdɪtəni / (AmE), / ˈkriːt ˈdɪtəni / (BrE)
a small aromatic shrub (Origanum dictamnus) native to Crete, with woolly leaves and pale purple flowers, used in herbal remedies
aromatic shrub native to Crete

Examples

Many traditional recipes from Crete use Crete dittany as a key ingredient for flavor and aroma.
For centuries, people have valued Crete dittany in herbal medicine for its healing properties.
The rocky slopes of the island are dotted with wild Crete dittany during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dittany - an aromatic herb in the mint family, especially various Origanum species

Etymology

From Crete, the Greek island where it is native, and dittany, from Old French ditanee, from Latin dictamnum.
