unemployment compensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪmənt ˌkɑmpənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪmənt ˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
money paid by a government agency to people who are unemployed, especially in the United States
government payments to the unemployed
american

Examples

After losing his job, he applied for unemployment compensation to help cover his expenses.
The new legislation made it harder to qualify for unemployment compensation.
Many families rely on unemployment compensation during periods of economic downturn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unemployment benefit - payments made by a government to unemployed people, chiefly British usage
