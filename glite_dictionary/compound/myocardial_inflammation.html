myocardial inflammation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈkɑːr.di.əl ɪnˌflæm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈkɑː.di.əl ɪnˌflæm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Inflammation of the myocardium, the muscular tissue of the heart.
inflammation of heart muscle
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected myocardial inflammation after reviewing the patient's symptoms and ECG results.
Viral infections are a common cause of myocardial inflammation in young adults.
Prompt treatment of myocardial inflammation can help prevent serious complications such as heart failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myocarditis - inflammation of the myocardium

Etymology

From Greek 'myo-' (muscle) + 'kardia' (heart) + Latin 'inflammatio' (setting on fire, inflammation).
