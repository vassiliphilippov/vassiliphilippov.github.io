butterfly plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬.ɚ.flaɪ ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ə.flaɪ ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
Any orchid of the genus Oncidium, known for its slender, branching sprays of small yellow and brown flowers and often grown as a houseplant.
orchid of the genus Oncidium

Examples

The butterfly plant thrives in bright, indirect sunlight and needs only moderate watering.
Gardeners often choose the butterfly plant for its decorative sprays of colorful flowers.
My grandmother keeps a butterfly plant on her windowsill to brighten up the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oncidium - genus of orchids known as butterfly orchids

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪ plænt / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəflaɪ plɑːnt / (BrE)
An orchid with large, elliptic to obovate fleshy leaves and fragrant pink-and-white flowers spotted with red.
a type of orchid with spotted pink-and-white flowers

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the butterfly plant for its delicate, fragrant flowers.
The butterfly plant thrives in warm, humid environments similar to its native habitat.
She bought a butterfly plant to add a touch of exotic beauty to her greenhouse.
