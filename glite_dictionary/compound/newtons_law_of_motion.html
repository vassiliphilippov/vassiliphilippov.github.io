Newton's law of motion

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtənz lɔː əv moʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtənz lɔː əv məʊʃən / (BrE)
any of three fundamental laws formulated by Sir Isaac Newton that describe the motion of objects and form the basis of classical mechanics.
fundamental laws of motion by Newton

Examples

Every physics student studies Newton's law of motion as a foundation of classical mechanics.
The explanation of a car's acceleration relies on Newton's law of motion.
Rocket launches can be analyzed using Newton's law of motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical mechanics - branch of physics dealing with the motion of bodies under forces

Etymology

Named after Sir Isaac Newton (1642–1727), English mathematician and physicist.
