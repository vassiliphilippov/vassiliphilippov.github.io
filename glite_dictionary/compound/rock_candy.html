rock candy

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈkændi / (AmE), / rɒk ˈkændi / (BrE)
A type of hard candy made in large, bright-colored crystals or sticks, often flavored with peppermint or other flavorings.
hard, crystalline sugar candy

Examples

As a child, I loved sucking on rock candy at summer fairs.
The brightly colored rock candy sparkled in the shop window.
She gave each of us a stick of peppermint rock candy as a treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candy - a sweet food made with sugar or syrup

2. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈkændi / (AmE), / rɒk ˈkændi / (BrE)
a sweet made of large, hard, transparent sugar crystals formed on a stick or string
large sugar crystals formed into a sweet

Examples

My grandmother always kept a jar of rock candy on her kitchen counter.
The science project involved growing rock candy crystals at home.
Children love to watch the colorful rock candy form slowly on wooden sticks.
