sheep plant

1. NOUN

/ ʃip plænt / (AmE), / ʃiːp plɑːnt / (BrE)
a cushion-forming herb native to New Zealand, with leaves densely covered in tawny hairs
New Zealand cushion-forming herb

Examples

The sheep plant thrives in alpine regions of New Zealand.
Botanists studied the distinctive hairy leaves of the sheep plant.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining populations of the sheep plant in its native habitat.

2. NOUN

/ ʃip plænt / (AmE), / ʃiːp plɑːnt / (BrE)
A perennial prostrate herb with dense, woolly, hoary foliage, often forming mats on the ground.
mat-forming woolly-leaved herb

Examples

The sheep plant thrives in rocky, alpine regions where it forms dense mats.
Gardeners prize the sheep plant for its silvery, woolly leaves and low-growing habit.
During summer, the sheep plant produces small flowers hidden among its fuzzy foliage.
