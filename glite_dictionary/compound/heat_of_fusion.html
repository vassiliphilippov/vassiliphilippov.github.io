heat of fusion

1. NOUN

/ hit əv ˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / hiːt əv ˈfjuːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the amount of heat required to convert a unit mass of a solid into a liquid at its melting point without changing its temperature
amount of heat to melt unit mass of solid

Examples

The heat of fusion for ice is an important value in thermodynamics.
Scientists measured the heat of fusion to understand phase changes in different substances.
The heat of fusion must be supplied to melt a block of lead at its melting point.

Synset Examples

the heat of fusion is equal to the heat of solidification

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heat of solidification - the amount of heat released when a unit mass of liquid turns into solid at the same temperature
