to carve a path

1. VERB

/ kɑrv ə pæθ / (AmE), / kɑːv ə pɑːθ / (BrE)
to create a route or open a way forward, either literally by making a passage or figuratively by advancing or making progress
create a route or make progress forward

Examples

The explorers managed to carve a path through the dense jungle.
Her determination helped her carve a path to success in a male-dominated field.
The river slowly carved a path through the rocky canyon over thousands of years.

Synset Examples

They want to carve a path through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pave the way - to make progress easier for others; to create the conditions for something
  • Synonym: to blaze a trail - to be the first to do something and show others the way
