colloidal solution

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɔɪdəl səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɔɪdəl səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
A mixture in which very small solid particles are evenly suspended throughout a continuous liquid phase without settling out.
mixture of suspended solids in liquid

Examples

Milk is an example of a colloidal solution where fat particles are dispersed in water.
The chemist prepared a colloidal solution of silver for the experiment.
Many medicines are delivered as a colloidal solution to increase their bioavailability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloid - a substance consisting of particles dispersed throughout another substance

Etymology

From 'colloid', from Greek kolla (glue) + '-oid' (like), and 'solution' from Latin solutio (a loosening, solution).
