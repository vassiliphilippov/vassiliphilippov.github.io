ostensive definition

1. NOUN

/ ɑˈstɛnsɪv ˌdɛfəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ɒˈstɛnsɪv ˌdɛfɪˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a definition that explains a word or concept by indicating examples or instances rather than by stating a verbal description
definition by example or indication

Examples

The teacher gave an ostensive definition of 'red' by pointing to several red objects.
An ostensive definition can be helpful when words fail to fully capture a concept.
By showing real coins, the lecturer provided an ostensive definition for the term 'currency'.

Etymology

From Latin 'ostendere' (to show) and 'definition'.
