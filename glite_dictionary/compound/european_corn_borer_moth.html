European corn borer moth

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən kɔrn ˈbɔr ər mɑθ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən kɔːn ˈbɔːr ə mɒθ / (BrE)
a species of moth native to Europe, whose larvae infest and bore into the stems of corn and other plants, causing agricultural damage
moth species; pest of corn plants

Examples

The European corn borer moth has become a major pest for farmers in the Midwest.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of the European corn borer moth to improve pest control methods.
Infestation by the European corn borer moth can lead to significant crop losses in cornfields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ostrinia nubilalis - scientific name for European corn borer moth
