honey mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni ˈmʌʃˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ˈmʌʃˌrʊm / (BrE)
a honey-colored edible mushroom (Armillaria mellea), often found near tree roots in late summer and fall; should not be eaten raw
edible honey-colored mushroom (Armillaria) found near tree roots

Examples

We found several honey mushrooms growing at the base of an old oak tree.
Before cooking, always make sure to identify the honey mushroom correctly, as some wild mushrooms are toxic.
The earthy flavor of the honey mushroom makes it a favorite in autumn recipes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mushroom - a fungal fruiting body, typically with a stem and cap
