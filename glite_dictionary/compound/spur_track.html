spur track

1. NOUN

/ spɝ ˈtræk / (AmE), / spɜː ˈtræk / (BrE)
a short railway line that branches from a main line, typically providing access to an industrial site or facility
a railway line branching from a main line

Examples

The factory receives its raw materials by rail via a spur track connected to the main line.
A new spur track was built to provide direct train service to the industrial park.
Maintenance crews inspected the spur track for safety before allowing freight operations to resume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch line - a secondary railway line branching off a main line
  • Antonym: main line - the principal railway line of a network
