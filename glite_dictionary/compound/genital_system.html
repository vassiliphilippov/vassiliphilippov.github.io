genital system

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the organs and tissues involved in the production, maturation, and union of reproductive cells and the development of offspring
reproductive organs and tissues
medical

Examples

The genital system develops differently in males and females during puberty.
Certain infections can affect the genital system and impact fertility.
Scientists study the genital system to understand reproductive health and diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproductive system - organs and tissues involved in reproduction

Etymology

From Latin 'genitalis' (pertaining to generation) and 'systema' (system).
