Louisiana Purchase

1. PROPN

/ luˌiːziˈænə ˈpɝːtʃəs / (AmE), / luˌiːziˈænə ˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
the acquisition by the United States of a large territory from France in 1803, extending from the Mississippi River to the Rocky Mountains and from the Gulf of Mexico to Canada
1803 US acquisition of western territory from France

Examples

The Louisiana Purchase doubled the size of the United States overnight.
History classes often highlight the significance of the Louisiana Purchase in early American expansion.
Without the Louisiana Purchase, the country's western border might look very different today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gadsden Purchase - another major US land acquisition from Mexico in 1853

Etymology

Named after the territory of Louisiana, itself named for King Louis XIV of France, and the transaction of purchase.
