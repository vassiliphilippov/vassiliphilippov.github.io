pound-shop

1. NOUN

/ paʊnd ʃɑp / (AmE), / paʊnd ʃɒp / (BrE)
A shop selling a wide range of items at low prices, typically for one pound each.
British shop selling low-priced goods, usually for one pound
british

Examples

I picked up cleaning supplies for just £1 each at the pound-shop.
The new pound-shop in town attracts a lot of bargain hunters.
Many students rely on the pound-shop for affordable household items.

Synset Examples

I found some great deals at the pound-shop yesterday.
She loves browsing the pound-shop for hidden treasures.
The pound-shop had everything I needed for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dollar store - A shop selling goods at very low prices, typically one dollar each (mainly North America)

Etymology

From 'pound' (the British currency unit) and 'shop'.
