Eranthis hyemalis

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrænθɪs haɪˈɛməlɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈrænθɪs hɪˈɛmælɪs / (BrE)
A small perennial herb native to Europe, grown for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the snow melts.
European spring-flowering perennial herb

Examples

The bright yellow blooms of Eranthis hyemalis often appear before the last snow has melted.
Gardeners favor Eranthis hyemalis for adding early color to spring borders.
Clusters of Eranthis hyemalis can create a cheerful display beneath leafless trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'er' meaning 'spring' and 'anthos' meaning 'flower', and Latin 'hyemalis' meaning 'of winter'.
