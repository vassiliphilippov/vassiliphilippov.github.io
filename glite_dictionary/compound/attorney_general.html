attorney general

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɝːni dʒəˈnɛrəl / (AmE), / əˈtɜːni dʒəˈnɛrəl / (BrE)
the chief legal officer of a country or state, responsible for representing the government in legal matters and overseeing the administration of justice
chief legal officer of a country or state
legal

Examples

The attorney general announced new measures to combat corruption in government agencies.
After a thorough investigation, the attorney general filed charges against the company for violating environmental laws.
Every state has its own attorney general responsible for enforcing state law.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: solicitor general - deputy to the attorney general, typically representing the government in court

Etymology

From Latin 'attornatus' (appointed) and 'general' (universal, relating to all).
