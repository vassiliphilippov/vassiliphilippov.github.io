Attorney General

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɝːni dʒəˈnɝːəl / (AmE), / əˌtɜːni dʒəˈnɜːrəl / (BrE)
the principal legal officer who represents a government in legal matters and is often the head of the Department of Justice
principal legal officer of a government

Examples

The Attorney General announced new measures to combat cybercrime today.
Each state has its own Attorney General responsible for upholding the law within its jurisdiction.
After a long investigation, the Attorney General filed charges against several officials.

Synset Examples

the post of Attorney General was created in 1789

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solicitor general - a law officer below the attorney general, often representing the government in court
  • Synonym: prosecutor - a legal officer who conducts criminal prosecutions on behalf of the state

Etymology

From 'attorney' (legal representative) and 'general' meaning 'chief' or 'principal'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɝni dʒəˈnɪrəl / (AmE), / əˈtɜːni dʒəˈnɛrəl / (BrE)
the principal legal officer who represents a country or state in legal matters and heads the Justice Department or equivalent
chief legal officer of a government

Examples

The Attorney General announced a new policy on cybercrime enforcement.
Each state in the U.S. has its own Attorney General responsible for legal affairs.
As Attorney General, she led the investigation into government corruption.

Synset Examples

Edmund Randolph was the first Attorney General, appointed by President Washington

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solicitor general - deputy to the Attorney General or principal assistant in some countries

Etymology

From 'attorney' (legal representative) and 'general' (in the sense of 'chief' or 'principal')

Synset POS: n
