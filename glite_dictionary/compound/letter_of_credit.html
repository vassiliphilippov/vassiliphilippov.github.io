letter of credit

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtər əv ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə əv ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
A document issued by a bank guaranteeing payment to a seller on behalf of a buyer, provided certain conditions are met.
bank document guaranteeing payment
legal

Examples

The importer secured a letter of credit from her bank before ordering the goods.
A letter of credit provides reassurance to sellers when dealing with overseas buyers.
Without a letter of credit, the supplier refused to ship the merchandise.
