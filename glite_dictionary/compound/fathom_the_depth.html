to fathom the depth

1. VERB

/ ˈfæθəm ðə dɛpθ / (AmE), / ˈfaðəm ðə dɛpθ / (BrE)
to understand or grasp the full extent or seriousness of something, often something abstract or profound
to fully understand the extent of something

Examples

It’s difficult to fathom the depth of his disappointment after losing the championship.
Only after hearing her story did I truly fathom the depth of her courage.
Scientists are still trying to fathom the depth of the ocean’s mysteries.

Synset Examples

It’s hard to fathom the depth of her sadness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to comprehend - to understand fully
  • Synonym: grasp - to seize and understand mentally
  • Antonym: to misunderstand - to fail to understand

Etymology

From 'fathom,' originally a nautical term for measuring water depth, used figuratively with 'depth' for extent of feeling or meaning.
