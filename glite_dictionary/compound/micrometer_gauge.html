micrometer gauge

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑmɪtər ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / maɪˈkrɒmɪtə ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a precise instrument used to measure small distances or thicknesses, typically with a calibrated screw
precision instrument for measuring small distances

Examples

The engineer used a micrometer gauge to check the thickness of the sheet metal.
For accurate results, the technician recommends calibrating your micrometer gauge regularly.
A micrometer gauge is essential for measuring tiny components in precision engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliper - instrument for measuring internal or external dimensions

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'metron' (measure), combined with 'gauge', meaning a measuring instrument.
