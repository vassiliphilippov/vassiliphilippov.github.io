lift bridge

1. NOUN

/ lɪft brɪdʒ / (AmE), / lɪft brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a type of movable bridge in which a section can be raised vertically to allow ships or boats to pass underneath
a bridge that can be raised for water traffic

Examples

The city replaced the old lift bridge with a modern structure to facilitate river traffic.
We had to wait ten minutes while the lift bridge was raised for a passing cargo ship.
Photographers gathered to capture the impressive sight of the lift bridge in motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawbridge - a bridge that can be raised, lowered, or moved aside to allow passage beneath or across
