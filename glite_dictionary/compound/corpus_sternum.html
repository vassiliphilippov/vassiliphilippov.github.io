corpus sternum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrpəs ˈstɝːnəm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpʊs ˈstɜːnəm / (BrE)
the large central part of the breastbone (sternum) between the manubrium and the xiphoid process
central part of the sternum
medical

Examples

In medical school, students learn to identify the corpus sternum during anatomy labs.
A fracture in the corpus sternum can be quite serious and requires attentive care.
The surgeon made an incision near the corpus sternum to access the patient's heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sternum - the long flat bone in the center of the chest, breastbone

Etymology

From Latin corpus (body) and sternum (breastbone).
