judicial branch

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl bræntʃ / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
the branch of a government that interprets laws and administers justice, especially the courts and judges.
branch of government responsible for justice

Examples

The judicial branch plays a crucial role in interpreting the constitution.
The separation of powers divides the government into the executive, legislative, and judicial branch.
Supreme Court decisions illustrate the importance of the judicial branch in shaping law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: executive branch - branch of government responsible for enforcing laws
  • Antonym: legislative branch - branch of government responsible for making laws
