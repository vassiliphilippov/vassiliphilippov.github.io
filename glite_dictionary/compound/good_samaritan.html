Good Samaritan

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd səˈmɛrətən / (AmE), / ɡʊd səˈmærətən / (BrE)
a person who helps others, especially strangers, selflessly and without expecting anything in return
person who helps others selflessly

Examples

After witnessing the accident, a Good Samaritan stopped to offer first aid until help arrived.
She was praised as a Good Samaritan for returning the lost wallet with all its contents.
He played the Good Samaritan by helping an elderly neighbor with her groceries.

Synset Examples

Being a Good Samaritan is rewarding.
She acted like a Good Samaritan by volunteering at the shelter.
The Good Samaritan helped the injured cyclist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or money to others

Etymology

From the biblical parable of the Good Samaritan in the Gospel of Luke, where a Samaritan helps a wounded traveler.
