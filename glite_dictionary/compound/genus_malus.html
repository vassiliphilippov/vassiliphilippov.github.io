genus Malus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmeɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmeɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the family Rosaceae, including apple trees, found throughout temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of trees including apple trees

Examples

The genus Malus includes many species of apple trees found in orchards worldwide.
Botanists have identified dozens of wild and cultivated varieties within the genus Malus.
The crabapple is a member of the genus Malus and is valued for its ornamental flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple tree - tree that bears apples; main species of Malus

Etymology

From Latin 'malus' meaning 'apple tree'; used in scientific classification.
