dressing sacque

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛsɪŋ sæk / (AmE), / ˈdrɛsɪŋ sæk / (BrE)
A woman's loose-fitting jacket, typically worn while dressing or preparing for the day.
loose woman's dressing jacket

Examples

She slipped a dressing sacque over her nightgown before going downstairs for breakfast.
In Victorian times, a dressing sacque was a common item in many women's wardrobes.
The soft cotton dressing sacque kept her warm as she prepared for the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressing gown - a loose robe worn after getting up or before dressing

Etymology

From French 'sacque', meaning a loose-fitting coat or jacket, from 'sac' (sack).
