Western balsam poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈbælsəm ˈpɑplɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈbɑːlsəm ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
a cottonwood tree native to western North America, with dark green, glossy leaves and rusty or silvery undersides
tree of western North America with glossy leaves

Examples

The Western balsam poplar thrives along riverbanks and in moist lowlands across western Canada and the United States.
In spring, the leaves of the Western balsam poplar shimmer in the sunlight due to their glossy surface.
You can easily identify a Western balsam poplar by checking for silvery undersides on its leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam poplar - a species of poplar found in North America
