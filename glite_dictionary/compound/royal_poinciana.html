royal poinciana

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl pɔɪnˈsiː.ə.nə / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl pɔɪnˈsiː.ə.nə / (BrE)
a tropical tree native to Madagascar, widely grown for its large clusters of bright scarlet and orange flowers; also known as flamboyant or Delonix regia
tropical tree with bright scarlet flowers

Examples

The royal poinciana blooms spectacularly each summer, covering the tree in brilliant red flowers.
A large royal poinciana shades the front yard with its spreading branches and dense foliage.
Many tropical streets are lined with royal poinciana trees, creating a vivid display during flowering season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamboyant - another name for Delonix regia, especially in horticulture
