carboxymethyl cellulose

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrboksimeθɪl ˈsɛljuloʊs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbɒksimeθɪl ˈsɛljʊləʊs / (BrE)
a water-soluble derivative of cellulose in which carboxymethyl groups are substituted for some of the hydroxyl groups, used as a thickener, stabilizer, or emulsifier in food and industrial products
water-soluble cellulose derivative used as thickener
medical

Examples

Many food products use carboxymethyl cellulose as a thickening agent to improve texture.
Pharmaceutical companies often add carboxymethyl cellulose to tablets to control their dissolution rate.
The addition of carboxymethyl cellulose in toothpaste helps maintain a smooth consistency.

Etymology

From carboxy- (referring to the carboxyl group), methyl, and cellulose, indicating its chemical composition.
