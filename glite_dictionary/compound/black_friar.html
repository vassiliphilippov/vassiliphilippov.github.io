Black Friar

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk ˌfraɪər / (AmE), / ˈblæk ˌfraɪə / (BrE)
a member of the Dominican order of Roman Catholic friars, distinguished by wearing a black mantle
Dominican friar wearing a black mantle

Examples

The monastery was once home to a community of Black Friars renowned for their scholarship.
During the procession, the Black Friar led the prayers, dressed in his traditional habit.
Many medieval cities had districts named after the Black Friars who lived and worked there.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: White Friar - a Carmelite friar, known for wearing a white habit
  • Antonym: Grey Friar - a Franciscan friar, known for wearing a grey habit

Etymology

Refers to the black mantle traditionally worn by Dominican friars.
