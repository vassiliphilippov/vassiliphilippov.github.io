index finger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛks ˌfɪŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪndɛks ˌfɪŋɡə / (BrE)
the finger next to the thumb, used for pointing, counting, or pressing
the finger next to the thumb

Examples

She raised her index finger to signal for silence.
He cut his index finger while chopping vegetables.
Using her index finger, she pressed the doorbell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forefinger - the finger next to the thumb, used for pointing
  • Degree Higher: middle finger - the finger between the index finger and ring finger
  • Degree Lower: thumb - the short, thick first digit of the hand

Etymology

From Latin 'index' (forefinger, pointer), referring to indicating or pointing, combined with 'finger'.
