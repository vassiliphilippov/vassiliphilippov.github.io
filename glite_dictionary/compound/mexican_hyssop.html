Mexican hyssop

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən ˈhɪsəp / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən ˈhɪsəp / (BrE)
An erect perennial plant native to Mexico, having rose to crimson flowers, often used as an ornamental or in herbal remedies.
a perennial plant of Mexico with rose-red flowers

Examples

The gardener planted Mexican hyssop along the walkway for its striking crimson blossoms.
Herbalists prize Mexican hyssop for its aromatic leaves and medicinal uses.
I saw a cluster of Mexican hyssop growing wild near the mountains in central Mexico.
