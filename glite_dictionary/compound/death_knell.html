death knell

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ nɛl / (AmE), / dɛθ nɛl / (BrE)
A bell rung to announce a death, or an event or sign marking the end or destruction of something.
bell rung to signal a death

Examples

The closing of the local factory sounded the death knell for the town's economy.
The rise of digital media was the death knell for many print newspapers.
He heard the death knell from the church tower as he passed by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death bell - a bell tolled to signify a death

2. NOUN

/ dɛθ nɛl / (AmE), / dɛθ nɛl / (BrE)
an event, fact, or sign that signals the end or destruction of something
signal of end or destruction

Examples

Many saw the closure of the factory as the death knell for the town's economy.
The scandal was the death knell of his political career.
Some believe that streaming services will sound the death knell for traditional television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omen - a sign of something about to happen
  • Synonym: harbinger - a person or thing that signals the approach of another

Etymology

From the use of a bell tolled to announce a death, with 'knell' from Old English 'cnyll' (a bell, tolling bell).
