thoracic medicine

1. NOUN

/ θɔˈræsɪk ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / θəˈræsɪk ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of diseases affecting the chest, including the lungs and respiratory system
branch of medicine for chest diseases
medical

Examples

After months of persistent coughing, Emily was referred to the thoracic medicine department for further tests.
Specialists in thoracic medicine often treat patients with pneumonia, asthma, and lung cancer.
The hospital's thoracic medicine unit is equipped with advanced imaging technology for chest diagnostics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulmonology - medical specialty focused on lung diseases
  • Synonym: respiratory medicine - branch of medicine dealing with respiratory tract diseases

Etymology

From thoracic, relating to the thorax (chest), and medicine, from Latin medicina (the art of healing).
