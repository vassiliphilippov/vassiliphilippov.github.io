Ellice Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛlɪs ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪs ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a former name for a group of coral islands in the Pacific Ocean, now known as Tuvalu, located in Micronesia to the southwest of Hawaii
former name for Tuvalu, Pacific island group

Examples

The Ellice Islands gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1978, becoming the nation of Tuvalu.
During World War II, the Ellice Islands were used as bases by the Allied forces.
Many people still use the name Ellice Islands when discussing historical events in the Pacific.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tuvalu - country in the Pacific formerly known as Ellice Islands

Etymology

Named after English politician Edward Ellice.
