Leboyer method

1. NOUN

/ ləˈbwaɪ.ər ˈmɛθ.əd / (AmE), / ləˈbwɑː.jeɪ ˈmɛθ.əd / (BrE)
a method of childbirth that aims to reduce trauma for the newborn by delivering the baby in a quiet, dimly lit room without pulling the head and encouraging immediate bonding with the mother
childbirth method minimizing trauma
medical

Examples

The hospital offers the Leboyer method for parents who want a gentler birth experience.
Many parents credit the Leboyer method with creating a calm environment for both mother and child.
Interest in the Leboyer method has grown among expectant mothers seeking alternatives to traditional childbirth practices.

Etymology

Named after French obstetrician Frederick Leboyer (1918–2017), who developed the technique.
