Guinea worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni wɜːm / (BrE)
a parasitic roundworm (Dracunculus medinensis) found in Africa and India that lives beneath the skin or in the abdomen of humans and other vertebrates, causing the disease dracunculiasis
parasitic roundworm causing dracunculiasis
medical

Examples

The eradication campaign against the Guinea worm has greatly reduced the number of cases worldwide.
A Guinea worm infection can cause severe pain and discomfort as the worm emerges through the skin.
Travelers to certain regions are warned about the risk of contracting Guinea worm disease from contaminated water.

Etymology

Named after the Guinea coast of West Africa, where the disease was common.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni wɜːm / (BrE)
a parasitic worm (Dracunculus medinensis) that causes a painful disease in humans, contracted by drinking water contaminated with its larvae; the adult worm emerges from the skin causing severe discomfort
parasitic worm causing painful skin disease
medical

Examples

The eradication campaign aims to eliminate Guinea worm disease worldwide.
After drinking untreated water during her travels, she contracted a Guinea worm infection.
The presence of the Guinea worm in rural communities has been a major public health concern.

Etymology

From the country name Guinea in West Africa, where the disease was commonly observed, and 'worm'.
