solid-propellant motor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪd prəˈpɛl(ə)nt ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd prəˈpɛl(ə)nt ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A rocket engine that uses a solid chemical mixture as its fuel and oxidizer.
type of rocket engine using solid fuel

Examples

The missile is powered by a solid-propellant motor that ensures rapid ignition and acceleration.
Engineers chose a solid-propellant motor for its reliability and simplicity in design.
A solid-propellant motor requires careful storage because the fuel is cast directly into the casing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquid-propellant motor - A rocket engine that uses liquid fuel and oxidizer
