india paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiə ˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈɪndiə ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a very thin, tough, and opaque paper, traditionally used in printing fine books, especially bibles and dictionaries
thin, tough paper for fine books

Examples

Many classic bibles are printed on india paper to reduce the book's thickness.
The collector was pleased to find a first edition with original india paper pages.
India paper is valued for its strength and ability to prevent ink from bleeding through.

Etymology

Named for its original source of manufacture in India.
