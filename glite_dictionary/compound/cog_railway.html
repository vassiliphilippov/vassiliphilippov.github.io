cog railway

1. NOUN

/ kɑɡ ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / kɒɡ ˈreɪl.weɪ / (BrE)
A railway designed for steep slopes, in which a cogwheel on the train engages with a rack rail to ensure traction.
railway with cogwheel mechanism for steep tracks

Examples

The Mount Washington cog railway is famous for its scenic route up the mountain.
Engineers built a cog railway to handle the region’s challenging inclines.
Many tourists ride the cog railway to reach the summit for the spectacular views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rack railway - railway with a rack rail for cogwheel engagement

Etymology

From 'cog', meaning a tooth on a gear, and 'railway'.
