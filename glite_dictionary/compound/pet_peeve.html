pet peeve

1. NOUN

/ pɛt piv / (AmE), / pɛt piːv / (BrE)
something that a person finds especially annoying or irritating, more so than most people do.
thing someone finds particularly annoying

Examples

One of my biggest pet peeves is when people chew loudly.
Leaving dirty dishes in the sink is her pet peeve.
He listed traffic jams as a common pet peeve among city dwellers.

Synset Examples

grammatical mistakes are his pet peeve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - something that causes irritation or bother
  • Synonym: irritation - the state of feeling annoyed or something that causes annoyance

Etymology

Formed from "pet" (favorite, special) and "peeve" (annoyance). "Peeve" is derived from the noun "peevish."

2. NOUN

/ pɛt piv / (AmE), / pɛt piːv / (BrE)
a particular thing that annoys or irritates someone more than it does most people
personal annoyance; minor grievance

Examples

One of my pet peeves is when people chew loudly during meals.
Her biggest pet peeve is when meetings start late.
Leaving your socks on the floor is a common pet peeve for many parents.

Synset Examples

Her pet peeve is being late for appointments.
Her biggest pet peeve is people chewing loudly.
My pet peeve is when people interrupt me.
One of my peeves is when people interrupt.
Leaving the dishes undone is my major pet peeve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - something that causes slight irritation
  • Synonym: grievance - a real or imagined cause for complaint

Etymology

Formed from 'pet' (special, personal) and 'peeve' (annoyance); 'peeve' from noun sense first attested in 1911.
