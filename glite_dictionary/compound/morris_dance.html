morris dance

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.ɪs dæns / (AmE), / ˈmɒr.ɪs dɑːns / (BrE)
A traditional English folk dance performed by groups of dancers wearing costumes and often using props such as sticks, handkerchiefs, or bells.
English folk group dance
british

Examples

The village holds a festival every year where performers showcase the traditional morris dance.
He joined a group to learn the steps and history of the morris dance.
The children watched with delight as the morris dance was performed in the town square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk dance - A traditional dance of a particular people or area

Etymology

Perhaps from the Middle English 'Morys dance', an altered form of 'Moorish dance'.
