Sitka willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtkə ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪtkə ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small shrubby willow tree (Salix sitchensis) native to western North America, especially from Alaska to Oregon.
shrubby willow tree of western North America

Examples

The Sitka willow thrives in the moist streambanks of Alaska and northern California.
Birds often use the dense branches of the Sitka willow for nesting sites.
Botanists identified the shrub as a Sitka willow due to its slender, pliant stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

Named after Sitka, a city in Alaska, where the species is common.
