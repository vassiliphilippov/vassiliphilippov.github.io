day laborer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ ˌleɪbərɚ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ˌleɪb(ə)rə / (BrE)
a person who is employed and paid on a daily basis, typically for manual or unskilled work
worker paid by the day

Examples

Many day laborers gather at the marketplace each morning, hoping for work.
The construction company hires day laborers during busy seasons to meet deadlines.
After losing his job, he worked as a day laborer to support his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migrant worker - a person who moves from place to place to do seasonal work
