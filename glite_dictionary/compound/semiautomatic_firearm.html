semiautomatic firearm

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˌɔtəˈmætɪk ˈfaɪɚˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌɔːtəˈmætɪk ˈfaɪəˌɑːm / (BrE)
A firearm that automatically reloads after each shot but fires only one round per pull of the trigger.
gun that fires one shot per trigger pull and reloads automatically

Examples

Police officers are often equipped with a semiautomatic firearm for duty.
A semiautomatic firearm allows shooters to fire rapidly while maintaining control.
Unlike revolvers, a semiautomatic firearm uses the energy of the fired round to cycle the action.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: automatic firearm - a gun that continues firing as long as the trigger is pressed
  • Degree Lower: manual firearm - a gun that must be manually reloaded after each shot
