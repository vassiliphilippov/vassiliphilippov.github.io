academic art

1. NOUN

/ əˌkædəmɪk ɑrt / (AmE), / əˌkadəmɪk ɑːt / (BrE)
a style of painting and sculpture characterized by formal techniques and standards established by European academies of art, especially during the 18th and 19th centuries
European academic style of painting and sculpture

Examples

Many 19th-century painters produced academic art that adhered strictly to the rules taught by art schools.
The museum’s new exhibition explores the transition from academic art to Impressionism.
Some critics regard academic art as lacking innovation despite its technical skill.

Synset Examples

The setting and treatment of the subject here are typical of his artistic approach, which lies between the idealized academicism of Bouguereau and the social realism of Daumier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academicism - adherence to academic art conventions
  • Antonym: modern art - art produced from late 19th century onwards, rejecting traditional techniques
