2. NOUN

/ ˈkæntərˌbɛl ˈbɛl / (AmE), / ˈkæntəbəri ˈbɛl / (BrE)
A European biennial plant, Campanula medium, widely grown for its showy blue, violet, or white bell-shaped flowers.
garden plant with bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The Canterbury bell is a popular addition to many cottage gardens for its vibrant display.
She planted a row of Canterbury bells along the garden path to add color in early summer.
The blue and violet blooms of the Canterbury bell attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - any plant of the genus Campanula, to which Canterbury bell belongs

Etymology

Named after Canterbury, England, likely for its prevalence or association with the region, and 'bell' for the bell-like shape of its flowers.
