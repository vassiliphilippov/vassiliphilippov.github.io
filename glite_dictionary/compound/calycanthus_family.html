calycanthus family

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈkænθəs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈkanθəs ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family of shrubs or small trees with aromatic bark, native to the eastern United States and eastern Asia; also known as sweetshrub family.
family of aromatic shrubs or small trees

Examples

The calycanthus family includes several species prized for their aromatic bark and ornamental flowers.
Gardeners in the southern United States often plant members of the calycanthus family for their fragrant blooms.
Botanists have discovered new medicinal properties in the bark of shrubs belonging to the calycanthus family.

Etymology

From the genus name Calycanthus, from Greek 'kalyx' (calyx) + 'anthos' (flower).
