Granite State

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡrænɪt steɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡrænɪt steɪt / (BrE)
the U.S. state of New Hampshire; used as a nickname referring to its extensive granite formations
nickname for New Hampshire (U.S. state)

Examples

The Granite State attracts tourists with its scenic mountains and lakes.
She grew up in the Granite State and later moved to Boston for college.
New Hampshire, known as the Granite State, boasts a rich history dating back to colonial times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Hampshire - a state in the northeastern United States, known as the Granite State

Etymology

Nickname referencing New Hampshire's many granite rock formations.
