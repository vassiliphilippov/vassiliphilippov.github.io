Hindoostani language

1. NOUN

/ hɪnˌduːˈstɑːni ˈlæŋgwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɪnˌduːˈstɑːni ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an early form of Hindi spoken around Delhi, considered a historical basis of modern Hindi and Urdu
early form of Hindi spoken near Delhi

Examples

During the 19th century, many poets wrote in Hindoostani language, blending Hindi and Urdu vocabulary.
The British administration often taught their officers basic Hindoostani language to communicate with locals.
Linguists trace the evolution of standard Hindi and Urdu back to the Hindoostani language spoken around Delhi.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hindi - the official language of India, derived from the Hindustani spoken in Delhi region
  • Degree Higher: Urdu - a standardised register of Hindustani, with Persian and Arabic influence
  • Synonym: Hindustani - the historical Indo-Aryan language resulting from the fusion of Hindi and Urdu

Etymology

From Persian 'Hindūstānī' (of Hindustan), referring to the northern region of the Indian subcontinent.
