temporal property

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl ˈprɑːpərti / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
a property or characteristic that relates to time or the passage of time
property relating to time

Examples

The duration of an event is a classic example of a temporal property.
Scientists often measure the temporal property of decay in radioactive substances.
In computer science, a process's execution time is considered a key temporal property of an algorithm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: timeless - not affected by the passage of time
  • Antonym: spatial property - a property relating to space or position

Etymology

From Latin *temporalis* (of time) and *proprietas* (ownership, quality).
