antisocial tendency

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈsoʊ.ʃəl ˈtɛn.dən.si / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈsəʊ.ʃəl ˈtɛn.dən.si / (BrE)
a pattern of behavior that opposes social norms or indicates avoidance of social interaction
behavior contrary to social norms or social withdrawal

Examples

His antisocial tendency made it difficult for him to work in teams at the office.
Psychologists study what may lead to an antisocial tendency during adolescence.
Repeated antisocial tendency can be a sign of a deeper behavioral disorder.

Synset Examples

The character displayed antisocial tendencies throughout the film.
Her asocial tendencies often leave her feeling lonely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asocial - avoiding social interaction; indifferent to society
  • Synonym: antisocial behaviour - actions that harm or lack consideration for others

Etymology

From anti- (against) + social (relating to society) + tendency (inclination).
