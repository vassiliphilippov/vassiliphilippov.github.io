poison-pen letter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən pɛn ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzn pɛn ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
an anonymous letter written in order to upset or insult someone; a letter that contains malicious or abusive statements, often sent to harm the recipient.
malicious anonymous letter

Examples

The newspaper received a poison-pen letter criticizing its staff in harsh language.
She was deeply upset after finding a poison-pen letter in her mailbox.
A series of poison-pen letters sowed distrust within the small company.

Synset Examples

The scandal began with a poison-pen letter to the editor.
He was hurt by the poison-pen letter he received.
The poison-pen letter caused a rift in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anonymous letter - a letter sent without revealing the sender's identity

Etymology

From 'poison pen,' referring to the figurative 'poisoning' of words; first recorded in early 20th-century English.
