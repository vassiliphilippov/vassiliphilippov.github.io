dry battery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌbætəri / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌbatəri / (BrE)
A battery consisting of two or more dry cells, commonly used to supply electric power to devices without the use of liquid electrolytes.
battery made of dry cells

Examples

He replaced the old dry battery in his flashlight before going camping.
Many portable radios use a dry battery as their primary power source.
A dry battery is preferred for small electronic devices because it requires no maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wet battery - a battery using a liquid electrolyte
