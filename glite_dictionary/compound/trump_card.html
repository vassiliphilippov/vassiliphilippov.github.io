trump card

1. NOUN

/ trʌmp kɑrd / (AmE), / trʌmp kɑːd / (BrE)
a playing card that belongs to the suit chosen as trumps in a particular game, which outranks the other suits
a card in the declared trump suit

Examples

He played his trump card and won the round easily.
Holding the king as a trump card gave her a major advantage.
You must follow suit unless you have a trump card in your hand.

Synset Examples

the ace of trumps is a sure winner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump - the suit that outranks others in a card game

Etymology

From 'trump', alteration of 'triumph', meaning a suit that overrides others, plus 'card'.

2. NOUN

/ trʌmp kɑrd / (AmE), / trʌmp kɑːd / (BrE)
an advantage or resource that gives someone the decisive edge in a situation
decisive advantage or resource

Examples

Her ability to speak five languages is her trump card in job interviews.
When negotiations stalled, the investor played his trump card.
The team's strong defense could be their trump card this season.

Synset Examples

Her experience was her trump card in negotiations.
Having a mentor is my trump card in this industry.
He played his trump card when he shared exclusive data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace up one's sleeve - a secret or hidden advantage

Etymology

From the game of cards, where a 'trump' is a suit that temporarily outranks the others.
