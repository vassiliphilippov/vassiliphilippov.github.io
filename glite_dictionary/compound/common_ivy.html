common ivy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈaɪvi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈaɪvi / (BrE)
an evergreen climbing vine (Hedera helix) native to Europe and western Asia, with lobed leaves and black, berrylike fruits
a species of evergreen climbing vine

Examples

Common ivy can cover walls and fences, giving gardens a lush, green appearance year-round.
Gardeners often plant common ivy to provide ground cover and prevent soil erosion.
Birds are attracted to common ivy because of its dense foliage and small, black fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivy - evergreen climbing or ground-creeping plant of the genus Hedera
