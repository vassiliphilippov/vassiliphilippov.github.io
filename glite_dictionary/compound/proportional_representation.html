proportional representation

1. NOUN

/ prəˌpoʊrʃənl ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌpɔːʃənl ˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an electoral system in which parties or groups are represented in a legislative body in proportion to the number of votes they receive.
electoral system based on vote proportion

Examples

Many countries in Europe use proportional representation to form their parliaments.
The debate over proportional representation versus majority systems is ongoing.
With proportional representation, smaller parties have a better chance of gaining seats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first-past-the-post - electoral system where the candidate with most votes wins
