lizard orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪzərd ˈɔːrkɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪzəd ˈɔːkɪd / (BrE)
A European orchid (Himantoglossum hircinum) with greenish flowers that resemble lizards.
European orchid with lizard-shaped flowers

Examples

The lizard orchid can be found growing wild in southern England.
Botanists are fascinated by the unusual scent of the lizard orchid.
Photographers often seek out the striking flowers of the lizard orchid in the summer months.

Etymology

Named for the lizard-like appearance of its flowers; orchid from Greek 'orkhis' (testicle), referring to the plant's root shape.
