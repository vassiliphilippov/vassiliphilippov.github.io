Midsummer's Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌsʌmɚz deɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsʌməz deɪ / (BrE)
(in England, Wales, and Ireland) The day traditionally celebrated as the middle of summer, falling on 24 June and recognized as one of the quarter days in the calendar.
quarter day at the middle of summer (24 June)
british

Examples

Many traditional events and fairs are held on Midsummer's Day throughout the countryside.
Rent payments and contracts used to be settled on Midsummer's Day in older English custom.
The family planned a picnic to celebrate Midsummer's Day in the village green.

Etymology

From 'midsummer' (middle of summer) and 'day'; traditionally dating to Old English and medieval customs.
