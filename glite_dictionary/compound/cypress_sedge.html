cypress sedge

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprəs ˌsɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪprəs ˌsɛdʒ / (BrE)
A tufted sedge plant found in temperate regions worldwide, resembling miniature cypresses in appearance.
a tufted sedge plant of temperate regions

Examples

The wet meadow was filled with cypress sedge thriving among the reeds.
Botanists catalogued several specimens of cypress sedge in the nature reserve.
Many gardeners use cypress sedge for ground cover in damp areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge - a grass-like plant of wet ground
