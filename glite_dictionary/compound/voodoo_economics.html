voodoo economics

1. NOUN

/ ˈvuːduː ɪˌkɑnəˈmɪks / (AmE), / ˈvuːduː iːkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
a term used to criticize economic policies considered unrealistic, deceptive, or lacking scientific validity, often implying that such policies rely on wishful thinking rather than sound economics.
unrealistic economic policies
informal

Examples

Many critics dismiss the proposal as voodoo economics that won't deliver the promised growth.
The candidate accused his opponent of relying on voodoo economics instead of realistic fiscal plans.
After several failed predictions, their theories were widely branded as voodoo economics.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that his policies are just voodoo economics.
Many believe that tax cuts without spending cuts are voodoo economics.
She dismissed his financial forecasts as mere voodoo economics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickle-down economics - theory that tax breaks for the wealthy benefit the broader economy
  • Antonym: sound economics - economic principles considered well-founded and reliable

Etymology

Coined in the 1980 United States presidential campaign, blending 'voodoo' (magical practice) with 'economics' to suggest implausible economic policy.
