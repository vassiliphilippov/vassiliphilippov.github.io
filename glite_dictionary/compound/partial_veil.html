partial veil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrʃəl veɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl veɪl / (BrE)
a temporary membrane in immature mushrooms, extending from the edge of the cap to the stem, which breaks as the mushroom matures and may leave a ring (annulus) on the stem
membrane in developing mushrooms

Examples

The young mushroom was protected by a partial veil that later broke to leave a ring around the stem.
Mycologists use the presence and type of partial veil as a key feature for identifying mushrooms.
After the partial veil ruptured, an annulus was clearly visible on the stalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annulus - a ring-like structure on the stem of a mature mushroom, remnant of the partial veil
