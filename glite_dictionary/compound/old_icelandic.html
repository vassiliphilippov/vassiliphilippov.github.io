Old Icelandic

1. NOUN

/ oʊld aɪsˈlændɪk / (AmE), / əʊld aɪsˈlændɪk / (BrE)
the extinct form of Old Norse spoken in Iceland until about 1600
extinct dialect of Old Norse in Iceland

Examples

Many medieval sagas were originally written in Old Icelandic.
Linguists study Old Icelandic to better understand Norse mythology and culture.
He can read Old Icelandic manuscripts with remarkable fluency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old Norse - the North Germanic language spoken by Scandinavians until the 14th century

Etymology

From Old Norse roots; 'old' + 'Icelandic' referring to early Iceland language.
