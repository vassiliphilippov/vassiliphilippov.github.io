shore patrol

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔr pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ʃɔː pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
members of a navy assigned to police duties on land, especially in ports and shore leave areas
naval personnel acting as military police on land

Examples

The shore patrol was called in to deal with the disturbance outside the navy base.
Sailors on leave were closely watched by the shore patrol.
After a night out in town, he was escorted back by members of the shore patrol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military police - soldiers responsible for law enforcement duties within the military
