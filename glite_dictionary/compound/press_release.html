press release

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛs rɪˌlis / (AmE), / ˈprɛs rɪˌliːs / (BrE)
an official statement or announcement given to the media to provide information or make a public statement
official statement for the media

Examples

The company issued a press release announcing its new product line.
After the incident, the mayor's office sent a press release to clarify the facts.
Journalists received a press release detailing the schedule of the upcoming festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news release - an official written statement given to the press

2. NOUN

/ prɛs rɪˈlis / (AmE), / prɛs rɪˈliːs / (BrE)
an official statement or announcement given to newspapers and other media outlets to provide information or make a public statement
official statement issued to the media

Examples

The company issued a press release detailing its plans for the new product launch.
After the scandal, the government spokesperson read a press release to reporters.
We learned about the event when a press release was circulated to all major newspapers.

Synset Examples

The company issued science by press release to highlight breakthroughs.
The company announced its new policy by press release.
They criticized the government for operating through policy by press release.
Critics argue against using science by press release for transparency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news release - an official statement issued to the media

Etymology

From 'press' (referring to news media) and 'release' (meaning to make available), dating from the early 20th century.
