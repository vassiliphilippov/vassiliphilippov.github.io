naval unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl ˈjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a military unit that forms part of a navy, such as a ship, fleet, or squadron
military group in a navy

Examples

The aircraft carrier is the largest naval unit in active service.
Several naval units participated in the international maritime exercise.
A new naval unit was commissioned to patrol the coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: army unit - a military unit that is part of an army

Etymology

naval from Latin navalis (of ships), unit from Latin unitas (oneness, unity).
