acid rock

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪd ˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˌrɒk / (BrE)
A style of rock music from the 1960s characterized by improvisation and experimental sounds, often associated with psychedelic or drug-influenced experiences.
psychedelic rock music style of the 1960s

Examples

The band's debut album is considered a classic of acid rock from the late 1960s.
Many acid rock songs feature long guitar solos and surreal lyrics.
He became interested in acid rock after attending a music festival in San Francisco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychedelic rock - rock music inspired by psychedelic culture and drug experiences

Etymology

Formed from 'acid', referencing LSD (a psychedelic drug), and 'rock' (music genre); emerged in 1960s counterculture.
