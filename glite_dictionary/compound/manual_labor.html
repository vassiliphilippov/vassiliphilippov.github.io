manual labor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.ju.əl ˈleɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæn.ju.əl ˈleɪ.bə / (BrE)
work that is done by hand or with physical effort rather than by machines or intellectual means
work involving physical effort

Examples

Construction work often requires a lot of manual labor during the building process.
He found manual labor exhausting but rewarding, as it kept him active.
As automation increases, fewer people are needed for manual labor jobs in factories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical labor - labor that involves bodily strength
  • Antonym: skilled labor - work requiring specialized training or expertise

Etymology

From Latin 'manualis' (of the hand) and Middle English 'labour' (work).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.ju.əl ˈleɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæn.ju.əl ˈleɪ.bə / (BrE)
work that involves using physical strength rather than mental effort, typically using the hands
physical work done by hand

Examples

Many construction jobs require hours of manual labor each day.
He found manual labor more rewarding than sitting behind a desk.
The economy still depends greatly on the availability of people willing to perform manual labor.

Synset Examples

She respects those who do manual labor for a living.
After a day of manual labor, he was exhausted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical labor - work involving bodily effort, not mental effort
  • Antonym: desk job - a job involving mainly mental/clerical work, not physical labor

Etymology

From Latin 'manualis' (of the hand) and Middle English 'labor' (work).
