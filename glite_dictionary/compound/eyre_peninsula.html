Eyre Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪɚ pəˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈaɪə pəˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in southern Australia, bordered by the Spencer Gulf, the Great Australian Bight, and the Gawler Ranges
peninsula in southern Australia

Examples

The Eyre Peninsula is famous for its beautiful coastline and fresh seafood.
Many tourists explore the Eyre Peninsula for its unique landscapes and wildlife.
She spent her holiday driving around the Eyre Peninsula in South Australia.

Etymology

Named after the explorer Edward John Eyre (1815–1901).
