water fennel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̮ɚ ˈfɛnəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈfɛnəl / (BrE)
a European poisonous herb with fibrous roots, commonly found near water; also known as Oenanthe aquatica
a poisonous European waterside herb

Examples

Cattle have occasionally been poisoned after eating water fennel growing near rivers.
Botanists warn that water fennel should not be confused with edible fennel species.
The pond was lined with water fennel and other marsh plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic flowering plant used as an herb
