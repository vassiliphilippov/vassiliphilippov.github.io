silver pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə paɪn / (BrE)
a timber tree native to New Zealand, known for its shiny white wood
New Zealand timber tree with shiny wood

Examples

The dense forests contained several majestic silver pines prized for their valuable timber.
The furniture was crafted from silver pine, giving it a distinct pale sheen.
Conservationists are working to protect the native silver pine from deforestation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree of western North America with stout blue-green needles and grey-brown bark that forms rectangular plates when mature
pine tree of western North America

Examples

The hikers rested beneath the tall silver pine trees, enjoying the cool shade.
A grove of silver pine stood majestically against the backdrop of the mountains.
The silver pine is prized for its durable timber and striking blue-green needles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - a type of evergreen tree with long needles and cones
