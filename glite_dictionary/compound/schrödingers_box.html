Schrödinger's box

1. NOUN

/ ʃroʊˈdɪŋərz bɑks / (AmE), / ʃrəʊˈdɪŋəz bɒks / (BrE)
a thought experiment in quantum mechanics illustrating the concept of superposition, where a particle can exist in multiple states until observed
quantum mechanics thought experiment

Examples

Many students use Schrödinger's box to explain the paradoxes of quantum theory.
Debates about the decision resembled Schrödinger's box, with outcomes unresolved until revealed.
The classic Schrödinger's box scenario demonstrates the principle that observation affects reality.

Synset Examples

The situation felt like Schrödinger's box, uncertain and ambiguous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Schrödinger's cat - the original form of the thought experiment using a cat

Etymology

Named after Austrian physicist Erwin Schrödinger (1887–1961), who proposed the experiment in 1935.
