heron's bill

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrənz ˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɛrənz ˌbɪl / (BrE)
any of various small flowering plants of the genus Erodium, having beak-like fruit resembling a heron's bill
plant of the genus Erodium

Examples

The seeds of the heron's bill are dispersed by the wind, thanks to their unique shape.
Gardeners sometimes plant heron's bill for its delicate, fern-like leaves and pink flowers.
Some species of heron's bill are considered weeds in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storksbill - any plant of the genus Erodium, also called heron's bill

Etymology

Named for the shape of the fruit, which resembles the bill of a heron.
