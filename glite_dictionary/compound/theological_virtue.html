theological virtue

1. NOUN

/ ˌθiːəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈvɝtʃu / (AmE), / ˌθiːəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈvɜːtʃuː / (BrE)
In Christian theology, any of the three virtues—faith, hope, and charity—given by God and necessary for salvation, complementing the natural virtues.
Christian virtue: faith, hope, and charity

Examples

Charity, as a theological virtue, is central to Christian moral teaching.
Hope is one of the three theological virtues that guide believers' lives.
Many theologians have written about how faith, a theological virtue, shapes human existence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cardinal virtue - any of the four principal moral virtues in classical philosophy: prudence, justice, fortitude, and temperance

Etymology

From Late Latin *virtutes theologicae*, meaning 'virtues pertaining to theology'.
