contributory negligence

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtrɪb.jəˌtɔr.i ˈnɛɡ.lə.dʒəns / (AmE), / kənˌtrɪb.jʊˌtɔː.ri ˈnɛɡ.lɪ.dʒəns / (BrE)
(Law) failure of an injured party to act prudently, considered to have contributed to the injury or loss suffered, which can reduce or bar recovery of damages from another party.
plaintiff's fault contributing to their own injury (law)
legal

Examples

The court ruled that contributory negligence barred the plaintiff from receiving any compensation.
Because of her contributory negligence, her damages were significantly reduced.
In some states, even a small amount of contributory negligence can prevent you from recovering damages.

Synset Examples

in common law any degree of contributory negligence would bar the plaintiff from collecting damages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comparative negligence - partial legal defense reducing damages based on plaintiff's share of fault

Etymology

From 'contributory', from Latin 'contributus' (contributed), and 'negligence', from Latin 'negligentia' (carelessness).
