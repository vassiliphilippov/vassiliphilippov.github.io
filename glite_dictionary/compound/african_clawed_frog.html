African clawed frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.rɪ.kən klɔd frɑg / (AmE), / ˈæf.rɪ.kən klɔːd frɒg / (BrE)
a species of tongueless aquatic frog native to Africa, often found in laboratories and sometimes established in the wild outside its native range
a tongueless frog species native to Africa

Examples

The African clawed frog is often used in scientific research due to its hardy nature.
Several African clawed frogs escaped from a laboratory and established a population in local ponds.
Unlike most frogs, the African clawed frog lacks a tongue and uses its claws for feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xenopus laevis - scientific name for the African clawed frog
