Goa powder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.ə ˌpaʊ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.ə ˌpaʊ.də / (BrE)
a bitter yellow powder obtained from the bark of certain trees, formerly used to treat skin diseases
bitter yellow medicinal powder
medical

Examples

The physician recommended an application of Goa powder for the persistent skin rash.
Historically, Goa powder was valued for its efficacy against certain skin conditions.
He read about the use of Goa powder in nineteenth-century medical treatments.

Etymology

Named after Goa, a region in India where it was first obtained.
