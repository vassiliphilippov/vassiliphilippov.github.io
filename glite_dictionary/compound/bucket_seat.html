bucket seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkɪt siːt / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪt siːt / (BrE)
A low, single seat with a contoured shape, especially used in cars or airplanes to hold one person securely.
single contoured seat in vehicles

Examples

Many sports cars feature bucket seats to provide better support during sharp turns.
The airplane's bucket seat was comfortable for the long flight.
She replaced the old bench with a modern bucket seat in her vintage car.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bench seat - a long seat for more than one person, especially in vehicles

Etymology

From 'bucket' (a round, deep container) + 'seat', describing the seat's shape.
