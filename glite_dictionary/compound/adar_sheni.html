Adar Sheni

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˌdɑːr ʃeˈni / (AmE), / ɑːˌdɑː ʃəˈniː / (BrE)
the additional month added to the Jewish calendar in leap years, falling after Adar and occurring seven times in every 19-year cycle
second Adar; extra month in Jewish leap year

Examples

During leap years, Jews observe Adar Sheni to align the lunar and solar calendars.
The festival of Purim is celebrated in Adar Sheni during a leap year.
Some religious events are postponed to Adar Sheni to maintain their position in the calendar.

Etymology

From Hebrew אֲדָר שֵׁנִי ('Adar Sheni), meaning 'second Adar'.
