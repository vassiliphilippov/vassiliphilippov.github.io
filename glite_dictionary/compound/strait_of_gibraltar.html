Strait of Gibraltar

1. PROPN

/ streɪt əv dʒɪˈbrɔltər / (AmE), / streɪt əv dʒɪˈbrɒltə / (BrE)
A narrow strait that separates southern Spain from northern Africa and connects the Atlantic Ocean with the Mediterranean Sea.
strait between Spain and Africa

Examples

Many ships pass through the Strait of Gibraltar on their way to the Mediterranean.
The distance across the Strait of Gibraltar is just 14 kilometers at its narrowest point.
Scientists have studied marine life in the Strait of Gibraltar because of its unique water flow.

Etymology

From Gibraltar, the name of the British Overseas Territory, itself from Arabic 'Jabal Ṭāriq' (Mountain of Tariq).
