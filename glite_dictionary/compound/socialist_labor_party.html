Socialist Labor Party

1. PROPN

/ ˈsoʊʃəlɪst ˈleɪbər ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪst ˈleɪbə ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a political party in the United States, founded in 1874, that advocated for the peaceful establishment of socialism
U.S. socialist political party, founded 1874

Examples

The Socialist Labor Party played a significant role in early American labor movements.
He became a member of the Socialist Labor Party after reading about its principles.
Debates about worker rights in the late nineteenth century often referenced the Socialist Labor Party.
