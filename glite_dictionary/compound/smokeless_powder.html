smokeless powder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkləs ˈpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkləs ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
A type of propellant used in firearms and artillery that produces little smoke when burned, typically containing nitrocellulose or similar substances.
propellant that produces little smoke

Examples

Early cartridges used black powder, but most modern ammunition contains smokeless powder.
The switch to smokeless powder greatly improved visibility on battlefields.
You should store smokeless powder in a cool, dry place for safety.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black powder - an older form of gunpowder that produces a lot of smoke

Etymology

Formed from 'smokeless' + 'powder'; 'smokeless' signifies its reduced smoke compared to black powder.
