working capital

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ ˈkapɪtəl / (BrE)
the amount of a company's assets that are available and used to carry out its day-to-day operations, calculated as current assets minus current liabilities
available assets for daily business operations

Examples

The company's working capital increased after it collected outstanding invoices from clients.
Insufficient working capital can make it difficult for a business to cover its short-term expenses.
Good management of working capital is crucial for a firm's financial health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capital - wealth in the form of money or assets owned by a business
