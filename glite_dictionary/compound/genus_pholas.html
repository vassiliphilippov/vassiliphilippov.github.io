genus Pholas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfoʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfəʊ.ləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pholadidae, consisting of marine bivalve mollusks known as piddocks.
genus of marine bivalves (piddocks)

Examples

The genus Pholas includes several species of marine bivalves that bore into wood and rock.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Pholas along the coast.
Specimens of genus Pholas are often studied for their unique boring behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pholadidae - the family of marine bivalve mollusks known as piddocks

Etymology

From Latin 'Pholas', the classical name for a type of burrowing bivalve, and 'genus' (kind, type).
