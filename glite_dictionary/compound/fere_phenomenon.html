Fere phenomenon

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr i fəˌnɑːməˌnɑːn / (AmE), / ˈfeəri fəˈnɒmɪnən / (BrE)
a change in the electrical properties of the skin, especially its resistance or currents, in response to stress or anxiety; measured by monitoring skin's electrical activity
change in skin’s electrical activity from stress or anxiety
medical

Examples

Researchers measured the Fere phenomenon to assess subjects' stress responses during the experiment.
A sudden increase in the Fere phenomenon indicated heightened anxiety in the patient.
The Fere phenomenon is often used as a physiological indicator of emotional arousal.

Etymology

Named after Charles Féré (1852–1907), French neurologist, who studied this phenomenon.
