plutonium pit

1. NOUN

/ pluˈtoʊniəm pɪt / (AmE), / pluːˈtəʊniəm pɪt / (BrE)
A steel or beryllium sphere containing plutonium-239, used as the core of a nuclear weapon to trigger nuclear fission when compressed by explosives.
core component of a nuclear weapon

Examples

The plutonium pit is a critical component in the design of modern nuclear weapons.
Scientists inspected the plutonium pit for signs of corrosion or degradation.
Modern disarmament efforts often involve securely storing or destroying each plutonium pit from decommissioned warheads.
