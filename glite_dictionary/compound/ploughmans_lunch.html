ploughman's lunch

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊmənz ˌlʌntʃ / (AmE), / ˈplaʊmənz ˌlʌntʃ / (BrE)
a traditional British meal typically consisting of bread, cheese, and salad, sometimes with pickles or cold meat
traditional British meal of bread, cheese, and salad
british

Examples

At the pub, I ordered a ploughman's lunch with cheddar cheese and a crunchy apple.
The menu featured a classic ploughman's lunch, served with crusty bread and pickles.
A ploughman's lunch is a popular choice during summer picnics in England.

Etymology

From 'ploughman' (farm worker) and 'lunch'; promoted as a pub meal in Britain from the mid-20th century.
