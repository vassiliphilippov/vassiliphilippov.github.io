Hebrew language

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːbruː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Semitic language of the ancient Hebrews, revived as the official language of modern Israel
official language of Israel; ancient Semitic language

Examples

Many Jewish prayers are recited in Hebrew language during religious ceremonies.
After it was revived in the 20th century, the Hebrew language became the main language spoken in Israel.
He decided to learn the Hebrew language to read the Old Testament in its original form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yiddish - a High German-derived language historically spoken by Ashkenazi Jews

Etymology

From Latin 'Hebraeus', from Greek 'Hebraios', from Hebrew 'ʿIvrit' (language of the Hebrews).
