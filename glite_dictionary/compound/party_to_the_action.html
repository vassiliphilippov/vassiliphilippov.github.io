party to the action

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrti tə ði ˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti tə ði ˈakʃən / (BrE)
a person or group directly involved in legal proceedings as a plaintiff or defendant
person or group involved in a lawsuit
legal

Examples

The judge asked each party to the action to present their evidence clearly.
Both parties to the action agreed to settle the dispute out of court.
A notice must be delivered to every party to the action before the hearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litigant - a person involved in a lawsuit
  • Synonym: defendant - an individual or group being sued or accused in a court of law
  • Synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
