American elm

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ɛlm / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ɛlm / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree (Ulmus americana) native to eastern North America, known for its graceful, spreading branches and commonly used as an ornamental shade tree.
a large North American elm tree

Examples

The American elm was once a popular choice for city streets due to its wide, arching canopy.
Many American elm trees were lost to Dutch elm disease in the twentieth century.
An old American elm stands at the center of the park, providing shade for picnickers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of tree with serrated leaves and a distinct fan-shaped crown
