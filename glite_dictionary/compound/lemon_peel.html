lemon peel

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən piːl / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən piːl / (BrE)
the yellow outer rind of a lemon, often grated or cut for use in cooking or baking
yellow outer rind of a lemon

Examples

She added some lemon peel to the batter for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of grated lemon peel.
He decorated the cocktail with a twist of lemon peel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon zest - the outer colored part of the peel of a citrus fruit, used as flavoring

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən pil / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən piːl / (BrE)
strips of lemon skin, especially when candied by cooking them in sugar and coating with more sugar, used as an ingredient or for decoration in desserts and confectionery
candied strips of lemon skin

Examples

She decorated the cake with finely chopped lemon peel for extra flavor.
The bakery uses homemade lemon peel in their scones and muffins.
I love the sweet and tangy taste of lemon peel dipped in chocolate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candied peel - fruit rind preserved by cooking with sugar
