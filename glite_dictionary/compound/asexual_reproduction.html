asexual reproduction

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˈsɛkʃuəl ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈsɛksjʊəl ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
reproduction that occurs without the fusion of gametes, resulting in offspring genetically identical to the parent
reproduction without gamete fusion
medical

Examples

Many plants can reproduce through asexual reproduction, producing clones of themselves.
Bacteria multiply quickly because they use asexual reproduction to create new generations.
The advantages of asexual reproduction include rapid population growth in favorable environments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexual reproduction - reproduction involving the fusion of gametes from two parents

Etymology

From the prefix 'a-' meaning 'without', Latin 'sexus' (sex), and 'reproduction'.
