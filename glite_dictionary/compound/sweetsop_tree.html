sweetsop tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈswiːtˌsɑp triː / (AmE), / ˈswiːtsɒp triː / (BrE)
A tropical American tree that produces sweet, pulpy fruit with a thick, scaly rind and shiny black seeds; also known as sugar apple.
tropical American fruit tree; sugar apple

Examples

The sweetsop tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its delicious fruit.
Farmers in the region have been planting more sweetsop trees to meet growing demand in local markets.
A mature sweetsop tree can yield dozens of fruits each season, prized for their unique flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar apple - another name for sweetsop tree
