Shirodkar's operation

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈroʊdkɑrz ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ʃɪˈrɒdkɑːz ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a suture is placed around the cervix to prevent premature opening during pregnancy, typically performed when the cervix has failed to retain previous pregnancies
surgical procedure to suture cervix during pregnancy
medical

Examples

The obstetrician decided to perform a Shirodkar's operation to prevent another premature birth.
After undergoing a Shirodkar's operation, she was closely monitored throughout her pregnancy.
A Shirodkar's operation can improve the chances of carrying a baby to full term in women with cervical incompetence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cervical cerclage - a surgical treatment for cervical incompetence using a suture to close the cervix

Etymology

Named after V. N. Shirodkar, Indian obstetrician who developed the technique in the 20th century.
