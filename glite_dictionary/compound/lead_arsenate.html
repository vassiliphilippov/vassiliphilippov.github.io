lead arsenate

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˈɑːrsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / lɛd ˈɑːsəneɪt / (BrE)
a poisonous white solid chemical compound (Pb3[AsO4]2) formerly used as an insecticide
poisonous insecticidal chemical

Examples

The use of lead arsenate on apple orchards was banned due to health concerns.
Old pesticide residues of lead arsenate can still be found in some agricultural soils.
Researchers studied the environmental impact of lead arsenate contamination in groundwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenic - a poisonous metallic element
  • Synonym: insecticide - a substance used to kill insects

Etymology

From 'lead' (the metal) and 'arsenate' (a salt or ester of arsenic acid).
