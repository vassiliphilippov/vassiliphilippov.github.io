phone-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊn ɪn / (AmE), / ˈfəʊn ɪn / (BrE)
a radio or television program in which listeners or viewers participate by telephone, often to express opinions or ask questions
broadcast program with telephone participation

Examples

She voiced her opinion on the local politics phone-in last night.
The radio station hosts a daily phone-in about health and wellness.
Many listeners enjoy joining the phone-in to ask questions directly to the experts.

Etymology

Formed from 'phone', short for 'telephone', and 'in', indicating participation by calling in.
