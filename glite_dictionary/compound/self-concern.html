self-concern

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.kənˈsɜrn / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.kənˈsɜːn / (BrE)
Concern for one's own interests and welfare.
concern for one's own interests

Examples

His self-concern sometimes made him seem indifferent to others' problems.
A healthy degree of self-concern is important for maintaining personal boundaries.
Therapists encourage clients to balance self-concern with empathy for others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-interest - regard for one's own advantage or well-being
  • Antonym: altruism - unselfish concern for the welfare of others
