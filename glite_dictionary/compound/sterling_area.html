sterling area

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɜr.lɪŋ ˌɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈstɜː.lɪŋ ˌeə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a group of countries whose currencies were closely linked to the British pound sterling, especially in the mid-20th century
countries linked by currency to pound sterling

Examples

After World War II, several former colonies remained part of the sterling area.
India withdrew from the sterling area in 1949 to establish its currency independence.
The economic policies of the sterling area aimed to stabilize exchange rates among member countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Commonwealth - an association of sovereign states, many of which were former territories of the British Empire

Etymology

From 'sterling', denoting the British pound, and 'area', referring to a group of linked countries.
