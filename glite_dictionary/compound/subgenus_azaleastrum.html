subgenus Azaleastrum

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈdʒiːnəs ˌeɪzeɪliˈæstrəm / (AmE), / sʌbˈdʒiːnəs ˌeɪzəliˈæstrəm / (BrE)
a group of evergreen or deciduous shrubs, formerly considered a separate genus, now classified within the genus Rhododendron
a Rhododendron subgenus of shrubs

Examples

Many horticulturists now classify subgenus Azaleastrum as part of the broader Rhododendron group.
Species in subgenus Azaleastrum exhibit a range of evergreen and deciduous foliage.
The reclassification of subgenus Azaleastrum clarified its close relationship with other rhododendrons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhododendron - a genus of woody plants that includes azaleas and rhododendrons

Etymology

From New Latin 'Azaleastrum', based on 'Azalea' and the suffix '-astrum' (diminutive or 'like').
