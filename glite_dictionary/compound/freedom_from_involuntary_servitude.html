freedom from involuntary servitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdəm frəm ɪnˌvɑːlʌnˈtɛri ˈsɜːrvəˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm frəm ɪnˌvɒlənˈtɛri ˈsɜːvɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
The civil right of not being forced to work against one's will, as guaranteed by the 13th amendment to the US Constitution.
right not to be forced into labor
legal american

Examples

The 13th amendment guarantees freedom from involuntary servitude to all citizens in the United States.
Civil rights activists fought for freedom from involuntary servitude and the abolition of slavery.
Legal scholars often discuss the historical impact of freedom from involuntary servitude in the context of American law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slavery - the state of being owned by another person and forced to work for them
  • Synonym: emancipation - the process of being set free from legal, social, or political restrictions; liberation
