genus Triglochin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˈɡloʊkɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˈɡlɒkɪn / (BrE)
A genus of perennial or annual bog or marsh plants, including arrow grass.
genus of bog/marsh plants (arrow grass)

Examples

The genus Triglochin includes several species commonly found in wetland environments.
Botanists conducted a survey of the local marsh and identified members of the genus Triglochin.
Arrow grass, belonging to the genus Triglochin, is adapted to both freshwater and saltwater marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrow grass - a common name for plants of the genus Triglochin

Etymology

From New Latin Triglochin, the genus name, from Greek triglokhinos, meaning 'three-pointed' (referring to the fruit).
