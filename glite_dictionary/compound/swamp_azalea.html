swamp azalea

1. NOUN

/ swɒmp əˈzeɪliə / (AmE), / swɒmp əˈzeɪliə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to swamps in the eastern United States, with small white to pinkish, honeysuckle-like flowers.
deciduous shrub with fragrant white to pink flowers

Examples

The swamp azalea blooms in late spring, filling the wetlands with its fragrance.
Botanists found a new population of swamp azalea along the riverbank.
The delicate pink flowers of the swamp azalea attract many pollinators during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azalea - any of several shrubs of the genus Rhododendron with showy flowers
