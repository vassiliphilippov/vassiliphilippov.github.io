rapid change

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈræpɪd tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a quick or sudden alteration in condition, appearance, or state
quick or sudden alteration

Examples

There was a rapid change in the company's fortunes after the new manager took over.
Global warming has led to a rapid change in many ecosystems.
Her decision to move abroad was made after a rapid change in her personal life.

Synset Examples

He noticed a rapid change in the weather this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sudden change - an abrupt or unexpected alteration
  • Antonym: gradual change - a slow and continuous alteration over time
