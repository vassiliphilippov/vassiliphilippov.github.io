Battle of Eniwetok

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˌɛniˈwiːtɑk / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌɛniˈwiːtɒk / (BrE)
a major World War II battle in February 1944, in which American forces captured the Japanese-held atoll of Eniwetok in the Marshall Islands
World War II battle at Eniwetok, 1944

Examples

The Battle of Eniwetok was a pivotal moment in the Pacific campaign during World War II.
Historians often study the strategies used in the Battle of Eniwetok to understand amphibious warfare.
After the Battle of Eniwetok, American forces secured a vital airbase for future operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Kwajalein - World War II battle in the Marshall Islands preceding Eniwetok
