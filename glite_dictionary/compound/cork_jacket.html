cork jacket

1. NOUN

/ kɔrk ˈdʒækət / (AmE), / kɔːk ˈdʒækɪt / (BrE)
a sleeveless jacket made of cork or containing cork used as a life preserver, especially in the past
historical life-preserver jacket filled with cork

Examples

In the 19th century, sailors often relied on a cork jacket for safety at sea.
The museum displayed an old cork jacket alongside other maritime artifacts.
He survived the shipwreck thanks to his trusty cork jacket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life jacket - a sleeveless buoyant jacket for keeping a person afloat in water

Etymology

Named for its cork filling, as cork is a lightweight buoyant material.
