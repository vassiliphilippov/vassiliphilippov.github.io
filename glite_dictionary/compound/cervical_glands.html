cervical glands

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrvɪkəl ɡlændz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪkəl ɡlændz / (BrE)
Small glands located in the mucous membrane of the uterine cervix that secrete mucus, playing a role in reproductive health.
glands in the uterine cervix secreting mucus
medical

Examples

The cervical glands produce mucus that helps protect the uterus from infection.
Hormonal changes can affect the activity of the cervical glands during the menstrual cycle.
A blockage of the cervical glands may sometimes cause the formation of a nabothian cyst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bartholin's gland - pair of glands at the vaginal opening that secrete mucus
