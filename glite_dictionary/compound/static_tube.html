static tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæt̬ɪk tuːb / (AmE), / ˈstætɪk tjuːb / (BrE)
a tube-shaped instrument used to measure the static pressure in a flow of fluid, such as air or water
instrument for measuring static fluid pressure

Examples

The engineer installed a static tube to monitor the air pressure inside the wind tunnel.
By comparing readings from the static tube and a Pitot tube, the velocity of the fluid can be determined.
Careful placement of the static tube is essential for accurate measurements in experimental setups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pitot tube - instrument measuring total and static pressure in fluids
