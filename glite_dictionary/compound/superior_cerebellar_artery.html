superior cerebellar artery

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɪriɚ sərəˈbɛlər ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə səˌrɛbɪˈlɑː ɒːtəri / (BrE)
a branch of the cerebellar artery that supplies blood to the upper part of the cerebellum and parts of the midbrain
artery supplying the upper cerebellum
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the superior cerebellar artery was carefully isolated to prevent any damage to the brainstem.
An aneurysm in the superior cerebellar artery can result in severe neurological deficits.
Detailed imaging revealed a blockage in the patient's superior cerebellar artery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cerebellar artery - major artery supplying the cerebellum
  • Degree Lower: posterior inferior cerebellar artery - a branch of the cerebellar artery supplying the lower posterior part of the cerebellum
  • Degree Lower: anterior inferior cerebellar artery - a branch supplying the anterior inferior part of the cerebellum

Etymology

From Latin 'superior' (higher), 'cerebellum' (the little brain), and 'arteria' (artery).
