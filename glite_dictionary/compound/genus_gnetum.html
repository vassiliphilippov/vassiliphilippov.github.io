genus Gnetum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡniːtəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈnɛtəm / (BrE)
A genus of the Gnetaceae family, consisting of small trees or shrubs usually with climbing jointed stems and terminal spikes of flowers with orange-red seeds clustered in rough cones.
genus of climbing trees or shrubs (Gnetaceae)

Examples

The genus Gnetum is notable for its unique reproductive structures among gymnosperms.
Many species within the genus Gnetum are found in tropical rainforests.
Researchers have studied the seeds of the genus Gnetum for their potential nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gnetaceae - the plant family that includes genus Gnetum

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Gnetum', the scientific name assigned in botanical classification.
