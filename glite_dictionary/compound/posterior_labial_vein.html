posterior labial vein

1. NOUN

/ pəˈstɪriɚ ˈleɪbiəl veɪn / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə ˈleɪbɪəl veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins that run backward from the labia majora and drain into the internal pudendal vein.
vein from the labia majora to internal pudendal vein
medical

Examples

The posterior labial veins are important for venous drainage in the female perineal region.
A blockage in the posterior labial vein can lead to localized swelling or discomfort.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the posterior labial veins.

Etymology

From Latin: 'posterior' meaning 'behind,' 'labialis' meaning 'of the lip'.
