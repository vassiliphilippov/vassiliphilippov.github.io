order Eubacteriales

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊrdər ˌjuːˌbæktəˈraɪəliːz / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə ˌjuːˌbæktəˈraɪəliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of true bacteria, usually Gram-positive and spherical or rod-shaped, which may be motile; sometimes classified under Schizomycetes.
a bacterial taxonomic order (Eubacteriales)
medical

Examples

Many of the soil bacteria belong to the order Eubacteriales and play a vital role in nutrient cycling.
The laboratory identified the organism as a Gram-positive rod in the order Eubacteriales.
In older classifications, some bacteria grouped under order Eubacteriales were included with Schizomycetes.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'eu-' (good, true) and 'bacterium' (little rod).
