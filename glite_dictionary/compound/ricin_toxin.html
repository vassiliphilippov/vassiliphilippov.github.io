ricin toxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsɪn ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪsɪn ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a highly toxic protein obtained from castor beans, often used as a chemical reagent and known for its potential use as a bioweapon
highly toxic protein from castor beans
medical

Examples

Accidental ingestion of ricin toxin can result in severe organ failure or even death.
Authorities found evidence of ricin toxin being prepared for use in a potential biological attack.
Scientists must handle ricin toxin with extreme caution due to its lethal nature.

Synset Examples

one milligram of ricin can kill an adult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ricin - a highly poisonous protein from castor bean seeds

Etymology

From 'ricin,' named after the castor bean plant genus Ricinus.
