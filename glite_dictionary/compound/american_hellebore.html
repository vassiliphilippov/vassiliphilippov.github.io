American hellebore

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈhɛlɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈhɛlɪˌbɔː / (BrE)
a North American plant (Veratrum viride) with large leaves and racemes of yellowish-green flowers, known for yielding a toxic alkaloid used in medicine
a North American poisonous plant

Examples

Researchers studied the medicinal properties of American hellebore despite its high toxicity.
The large leaves and racemes of greenish flowers make the American hellebore easy to identify in wet meadows.
Caution is needed when handling American hellebore, as all parts of the plant are poisonous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false hellebore - another common name for Veratrum viride
