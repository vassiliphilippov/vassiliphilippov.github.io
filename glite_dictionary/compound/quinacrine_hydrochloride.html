quinacrine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ kwɪˈnækrɪn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔraɪd / (AmE), / kwɪˈnækrɪn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a drug used to treat certain worm infestations and formerly used to treat malaria
an antiparasitic and antimalarial drug
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed quinacrine hydrochloride to treat the parasitic infection.
For many years, quinacrine hydrochloride was used as a front-line treatment for malaria.
Researchers are investigating new uses for quinacrine hydrochloride beyond its original medical purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atabrine - trade name for quinacrine hydrochloride

Etymology

From 'quinacrine', based on the quinoline group, and 'hydrochloride', denoting its salt form with hydrochloric acid.
