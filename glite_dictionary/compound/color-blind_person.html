color-blind person

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər ˌblaɪnd ˈpɜrsn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˌblaɪnd ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a person who is unable to distinguish certain colors, usually due to a deficiency in the retina
a person with color vision deficiency

Examples

As a color-blind person, he finds it difficult to tell the difference between red and green traffic lights.
Being a color-blind person hasn't stopped her from becoming a talented artist.
The test was designed to identify whether someone is a color-blind person or has normal vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish certain colors
