Karagiozis performance

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrəˈjoʊzɪs pərˈfɔːrməns / (AmE), / kəˌræɡiˈɒzɪs pəˈfɔːməns / (BrE)
A traditional Greek shadow puppet show featuring the character Karagiozis, known for comedic storytelling and satire.
traditional Greek shadow puppet show

Examples

Growing up in Athens, my grandparents often took me to see a Karagiozis performance in the summer.
The festival included a special Karagiozis performance that delighted both children and adults.
Many schools in Greece invite puppeteers to give a Karagiozis performance for cultural education.

Synset Examples

The play featured a hilarious Karagiozis performance.

Etymology

Karagiozis comes from Turkish Karagöz, meaning 'black eye', the name of the central character in the show.
