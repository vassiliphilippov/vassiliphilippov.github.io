terrestrial time

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɛstriəl taɪm / (AmE), / təˈrɛstriəl taɪm / (BrE)
(astronomy) time measured by Earth's orbital motion, defined as mean solar time corrected for the irregularities in Earth's movements.
astronomical time scale based on Earth's orbit

Examples

Modern astronomers synchronize their clocks to terrestrial time for precise measurements.
The discrepancy between universal time and terrestrial time affects calculations of celestial events.
Atomic clocks are used to maintain the accuracy of terrestrial time standards.

Etymology

From Latin 'terra' (earth) + English 'time'.
