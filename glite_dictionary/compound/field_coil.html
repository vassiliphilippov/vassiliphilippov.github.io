field coil

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌkɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌkɔɪl / (BrE)
an electric coil wound around a field magnet, used to produce a magnetic field in an electric machine such as a motor or generator
coil generating a magnetic field in motors/generators

Examples

The field coil in the generator was damaged and needed replacement before the machine could work again.
In many motors, the field coil is energized to create the required magnetic field.
Proper maintenance of the field coil ensures efficient performance in electric machines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armature - the rotating part of an electric machine that carries current
  • Antonym: rotor - the rotating part of an electric generator or motor
  • Antonym: stator - the stationary part of an electric motor or generator

Etymology

From the engineering terms 'field' (magnetic field) and 'coil'.
