amitriptyline hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈtrɪptɪˌlin ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌæmɪˈtrɪptɪliːn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat depression and certain types of pain; known for its significant side effects and interactions with other medications
tricyclic antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amitriptyline hydrochloride to help manage her chronic nerve pain.
Patients taking amitriptyline hydrochloride should be aware of possible side effects and drug interactions.
He researched amitriptyline hydrochloride before starting the medication for his depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amitriptyline - the active ingredient, a tricyclic antidepressant

Etymology

From amitriptyline, the drug name, and hydrochloride, indicating the hydrochloride salt form used in medication.
