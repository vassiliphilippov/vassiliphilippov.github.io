free central placentation

1. NOUN

/ fri ˈsɛntrəl ˌplænˌteɪˈʃən / (AmE), / friː ˈsɛntrəl ˌplænˌteɪˈʃən / (BrE)
A type of placentation in plants in which ovules develop on a central column within a compound ovary that lacks internal dividing walls, or has them only at the base.
botanical ovule arrangement on a central column
medical

Examples

In primroses, free central placentation can be observed in the mature ovary.
Carnation flowers display free central placentation, where ovules cluster around a central column.
Botanists use free central placentation as a key trait to classify certain plant families.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: axile placentation - ovules attached to a central axis in an ovary with septa
  • Antonym: parietal placentation - ovules attached to the wall of the ovary

Etymology

From botanical terminology: 'placentation' from Latin 'placenta' (cake, used for 'afterbirth'), describing the arrangement of ovules within the ovary.
