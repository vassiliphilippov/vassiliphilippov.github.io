prairie wagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˌwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˌwæɡən / (BrE)
a large wagon with wide wheels and an arched canvas cover, used by American pioneers to cross the prairies in the 19th century.
wagon used by pioneers in the US

Examples

A group of settlers traveled for weeks in a prairie wagon across the plains.
The prairie wagon was packed with supplies for the long journey westward.
Children peeked out from beneath the canvas of their family’s prairie wagon as it rolled along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covered wagon - a wagon with a canvas cover, especially as used on the American frontier

Etymology

Formed from 'prairie' (from French *prairie*, meaning 'meadow') and 'wagon' (from Dutch *wagen*).
