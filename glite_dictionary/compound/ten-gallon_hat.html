ten-gallon hat

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛn ˈɡælən hæt / (AmE), / ˌtɛn ˈɡælən hæt / (BrE)
a broad-brimmed hat with a high, soft crown, traditionally worn by American cowboys and ranchers
broad-brimmed cowboy hat
american

Examples

The sheriff tipped his ten-gallon hat as he greeted the townsfolk.
He wore a dusty ten-gallon hat that shaded his eyes from the desert sun.
Tourists love posing for photos in a ten-gallon hat at the rodeo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stetson - a brand of felt cowboy hat often called a ten-gallon hat

Etymology

Origin uncertain; 'ten-gallon' may derive from Spanish 'galón' (braid), referring to hatbands rather than capacity.
