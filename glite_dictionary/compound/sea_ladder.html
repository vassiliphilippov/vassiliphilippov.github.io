sea ladder

1. NOUN

/ si ˈlædɚ / (AmE), / si ˈlædə / (BrE)
(Nautical) a ladder that is lowered over the side of a ship to allow people to come aboard from the sea or from another vessel.
nautical ladder for boarding a ship

Examples

The sailors quickly lowered the sea ladder so the pilot could board the vessel.
During the drill, each crew member took a turn climbing up the sea ladder.
A rope sea ladder was used when the ship anchored far from the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangway - a movable bridge or ramp used for boarding or leaving a ship
  • Synonym: jacob's ladder - a kind of flexible ladder, often used on ships
