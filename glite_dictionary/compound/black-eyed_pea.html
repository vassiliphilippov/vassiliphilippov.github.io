black-eyed pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk.aɪd piː / (AmE), / ˈblæk.aɪd piː / (BrE)
a small, pale bean with a black spot, eaten fresh or dried, especially common in Southern US cooking
a pale bean with a black spot

Examples

She added chopped black-eyed peas to the salad for extra protein.
Traditional Southern dishes often feature black-eyed peas, especially on New Year's Day.
I bought a bag of dried black-eyed peas for tonight's stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowpea - another name for black-eyed pea or its broader species

3. NOUN

/ ˌblækˈaɪd pi / (AmE), / ˌblækˈaɪd piː / (BrE)
a type of legume with a pale seed and a distinctive black spot, often eaten as food, especially in the southern United States.
a legume with a black spot on the seed

Examples

She served a bowl of black-eyed peas as a side dish with dinner.
Many traditional Southern recipes call for black-eyed peas on New Year's Day for good luck.
The farmer grew black-eyed peas both for their edible seeds and to enrich the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowpea - a leguminous plant to which the black-eyed pea belongs
