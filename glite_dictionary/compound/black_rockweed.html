black rockweed

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈrɑkˌwid / (AmE), / blæk ˈrɒkˌwiːd / (BrE)
a common species of seaweed often used in making kelp and as a fertilizer
a common species of seaweed

Examples

The harvesters collected black rockweed from the tidal rocks early in the morning.
Farmers sometimes spread black rockweed over their fields as a natural fertilizer.
The nutrient-rich black rockweed is also used in preparing kelp products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockweed - a type of seaweed found attached to rocks
