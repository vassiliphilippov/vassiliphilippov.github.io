big shellbark hickory

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈʃɛlˌbɑrk ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈʃɛlˌbɑːk ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
a species of hickory tree of the eastern United States, resembling the shagbark hickory but bearing much larger nuts
a hickory tree with large nuts native to eastern US

Examples

The big shellbark hickory is prized for its exceptionally large edible nuts.
Several big shellbark hickory trees grow along the riverbank, standing out from other species.
We collected fallen nuts from the big shellbark hickory during our nature hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - hickory with shaggy bark and edible nuts
