First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Timothy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpɑl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ˈtɪməθi / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpɔːl ði əˈpɒsl̩ tuː ˈtɪməθi / (BrE)
the first of two letters in the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint Paul and addressed to Timothy, offering guidance on pastoral matters and church leadership
New Testament letter from Paul to Timothy

Examples

The First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Timothy contains instructions on the qualifications for church leaders.
Many pastors study the First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Timothy for its counsel on congregation management.
In Sunday school, we read passages from the First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Timothy about faith and perseverance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Second Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Timothy - the second letter from Paul to Timothy in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Epistle - a letter, especially a formal or didactic one; often found in the Bible

Etymology

Epistle from Greek 'epistolē' (letter); Timothy from Greek 'Timotheos'.
