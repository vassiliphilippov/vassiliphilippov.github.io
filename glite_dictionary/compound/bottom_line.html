bottom line

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtəm laɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm laɪn / (BrE)
the most important or decisive point; the final outcome or result
the decisive or most important point

Examples

The bottom line is that we need to increase sales if we want to stay in business.
No matter what happens, the bottom line is that everyone should be treated with respect.
The bottom line was clear: the project could not continue without more funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crux - the most important or decisive point
  • Synonym: main point - the central or most important idea
  • Antonym: side issue - a minor or less important point

Etymology

Originally from bookkeeping, where the 'bottom line' shows the final profit or loss on a financial statement.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːɾəm laɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm laɪn / (BrE)
the final total of an account, balance sheet, or other financial document, showing net profit or loss; the most important outcome or result
final net profit or loss; key result

Examples

The company's bottom line improved significantly after the new strategy was implemented.
Ultimately, your expenses will affect your bottom line more than you think.
When the audit was complete, the bottom line showed a healthy profit for the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: net income - total profit after all expenses
  • Antonym: gross income - total earnings before deductions

Etymology

Originally from the literal 'bottom line' of financial statements showing the net result.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtəm laɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm laɪn / (BrE)
the most important fact or conclusion in a situation; the main point that needs attention or consideration
the most important point or conclusion

Examples

The bottom line is that we need to finish the project by Friday.
No matter how you look at it, the bottom line is customer satisfaction.
After a long discussion, the bottom line was that we couldn't afford the new equipment.
