volume-detonation bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːljum ˌdɛtəˈneɪʃən bɑːm / (AmE), / ˈvɒljuːm ˌdɛtəˈneɪʃən bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb that disperses fuel in the air and detonates it to produce a powerful explosion, typically using a fuel-air explosive
bomb using fuel-air explosive

Examples

The military deployed a volume-detonation bomb to clear the heavily fortified area.
A volume-detonation bomb produces a massive blast wave by igniting a dispersed fuel cloud.
Some experts consider the volume-detonation bomb more destructive than conventional explosives in open areas.

Synset Examples

a thermobaric bomb can create overpressures equal to an atomic bomb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermobaric bomb - a bomb that uses fuel-air explosives to generate high temperatures and pressure
