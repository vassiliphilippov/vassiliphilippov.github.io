Michaelmas daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkəlməs ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈmɪkəlməs ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant in the aster family, bearing small purple, pink, or white flowers that bloom in autumn, often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
autumn-blooming perennial plant with daisy-like flowers

Examples

The Michaelmas daisy adds bright color to gardens in late September.
She planted several Michaelmas daisies along the garden path for autumn blooms.
Bees are attracted to the purple flowers of the Michaelmas daisy each fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the genus Aster, related group of flowering perennials

Etymology

Named for Michaelmas (the feast of St Michael on September 29), when the flowers typically bloom.
