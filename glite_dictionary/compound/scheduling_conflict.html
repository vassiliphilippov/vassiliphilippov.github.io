scheduling conflict

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛdʒʊlɪŋ ˈkɑnflɪkt / (AmE), / ˈʃɛdjuːlɪŋ ˈkɒnflɪkt / (BrE)
A situation in which two or more planned appointments or events overlap, making it impossible to attend or participate in all of them.
overlapping appointments or events

Examples

I missed the seminar because of a scheduling conflict with another meeting.
Due to a scheduling conflict, the appointment had to be moved to next week.
Her scheduling conflict prevented her from joining the conference call.

Synset Examples

They decided to delay their meeting due to scheduling conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clash - an instance of two events happening at the same time
