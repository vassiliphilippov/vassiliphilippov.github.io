council tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊnsəl tæks / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnsəl tæks / (BrE)
A tax collected by local authorities in the UK, based on the value of a property and, in some cases, the number of people living there.
UK local property-based tax
british

Examples

I pay my council tax by direct debit every month.
The local authority increased council tax rates this year.
Many students are exempt from paying council tax.
