nerve block anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ nɝv blɑk ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / nɜːv blɒk ænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
local anesthesia of a specific area achieved by injecting an anesthetic agent near a nerve, blocking sensation in the region supplied by that nerve.
anesthesia by blocking a nerve
medical

Examples

The surgeon administered nerve block anesthesia to numb the patient's arm before the procedure.
Regional pain control was achieved through nerve block anesthesia, eliminating the need for general anesthesia.
After receiving nerve block anesthesia, the patient reported complete loss of sensation in the affected area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local anesthesia - loss of sensation in a specific area of the body, typically induced by medication
  • Antonym: general anesthesia - complete loss of sensation and consciousness in the whole body
