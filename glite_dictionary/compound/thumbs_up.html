thumbs up

1. NOUN

/ θʌmz ʌp / (AmE), / θʌmz ʌp / (BrE)
a gesture or symbol indicating approval, agreement, or satisfaction, typically made by raising the thumb upward
gesture indicating approval or satisfaction

Examples

She gave a thumbs up to the proposal during the meeting.
The movie received two thumbs up from the critics.
He flashed a thumbs up as he crossed the finish line.

Synset Examples

The phrase ‘two thumbs up’ has come to be used as an indication of very high quality or unanimity of praise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thumbs down - an indication of disapproval or rejection

2. NOUN

/ θʌmz ʌp / (AmE), / θʌmz ʌp / (BrE)
a gesture in which the thumb is extended upward from a closed fist, used to indicate approval, agreement, or encouragement
gesture indicating approval

Examples

After hearing her idea, everyone in the meeting gave her a thumbs up.
The coach signaled a thumbs up to show his satisfaction with the team's performance.
Posting a thumbs up emoji is a quick way to express approval online.

Synset Examples

I gave the video a thumbs up with my e-thumb.
He gave the movie a thumbs up after watching it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thumbs down - gesture indicating disapproval
