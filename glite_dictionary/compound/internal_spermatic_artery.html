internal spermatic artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl spərˈmæt̬ɪk ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl spəˈmatɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a branch of the aorta that supplies blood to the testicles
artery supplying the testicles
medical

Examples

The internal spermatic artery branches off the abdominal aorta and reaches the testicles through the inguinal canal.
Damage to the internal spermatic artery during surgery can affect blood flow to the testes.
A blockage in the internal spermatic artery may lead to testicular atrophy if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testicular artery - an artery supplying blood to the testicles; another term for internal spermatic artery

Etymology

From Latin spermaticus (pertaining to seed; sperm) and artery.
