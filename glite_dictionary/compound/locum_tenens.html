locum tenens

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkəm ˈtenənz / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəm ˈtɛnɛnz / (BrE)
A person, especially a physician or clergyman, who temporarily takes the place of another in their professional duties.
temporary substitute (doctor, clergyman)

Examples

After the senior doctor went on leave, a locum tenens was hired to handle his patients.
The church appointed a locum tenens to cover services during the vicar's sabbatical.
Several hospitals rely on locum tenens to manage staff shortages throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person who takes the place of another

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'one holding a place'.
