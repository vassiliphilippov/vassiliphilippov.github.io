house mouse

1. NOUN

/ haʊs maʊs / (AmE), / haʊs maʊs / (BrE)
a small brownish-grey mouse (Mus musculus) that is native to the Old World, now widespread and commonly found in human dwellings as a pest
common household mouse species (Mus musculus)

Examples

The laboratory used the house mouse to study genetic mutations.
We discovered a house mouse nesting behind the kitchen cabinets.
Because the house mouse can carry diseases, it is important to keep food sealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse - a small rodent, typically with a pointed snout and long tail
  • Degree Higher: rat - a larger rodent similar to a mouse, also often found in houses

Etymology

Formed from 'house' and 'mouse', referring to its frequent presence in human homes.
