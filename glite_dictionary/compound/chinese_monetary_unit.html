Chinese monetary unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniːz məˈnɛtəri ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪˌniːz məˈnɛtəri ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in the People's Republic of China, commonly known as the yuan or renminbi.
unit of currency in China

Examples

One Chinese monetary unit is currently worth about fifteen US cents.
The exchange rate for the Chinese monetary unit fluctuates daily against the dollar.
Many prices in China are listed in the Chinese monetary unit known as the yuan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yuan - the principal unit of the Chinese currency
  • Synonym: renminbi - the official name for the Chinese currency
