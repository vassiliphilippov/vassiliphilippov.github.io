bulldog ant

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌdɔg ænt / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.dɒɡ ænt / (BrE)
a large and aggressive Australian ant of the genus Myrmecia, known for its powerful sting and strong mandibles
large aggressive Australian ant

Examples

The bulldog ant is feared in Australia for its aggressive behavior and painful sting.
Several bulldog ants built a nest near the hiking trail, and hikers were advised to avoid the area.
Despite its size, the bulldog ant can move remarkably fast when provoked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack jumper - a common species of Myrmecia ant, known for its jumping ability

Etymology

Named for its fierce nature and strong jaws, likened to those of a bulldog.
