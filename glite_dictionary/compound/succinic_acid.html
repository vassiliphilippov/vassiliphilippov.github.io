succinic acid

1. NOUN

/ səkˈsɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / səkˈsɪnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A dicarboxylic acid (C4H6O4) found in living organisms, involved in metabolic processes such as the citric acid cycle.
a dicarboxylic acid involved in metabolism
medical

Examples

Succinic acid is a key intermediate in the citric acid cycle, essential for cellular respiration.
The laboratory synthesized succinic acid from renewable plant sugars.
Researchers measured levels of succinic acid in the blood to assess metabolic function.

Etymology

From Latin succinum (amber), as it was first obtained from amber.
