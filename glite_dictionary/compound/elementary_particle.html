elementary particle

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈmɛn.t̬ɚi ˈpɑːr.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌɛl.ɪˈmɛn.tər.i ˈpɑː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a particle in physics that is more fundamental than an atom and is considered a building block of matter, such as an electron or quark
fundamental particle in physics

Examples

The physicist explained that the electron is an elementary particle with a negative charge.
Research into the behavior of each elementary particle has led to major breakthroughs in quantum physics.
Quarks are an example of an elementary particle that cannot be broken down into smaller components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subatomic particle - a particle smaller than an atom; includes elementary particles and composite particles

Etymology

From Latin 'elementarius' (elementary) + 'particle' from Latin 'particula' (small part).
