aminoalkanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊˌælkəˈnoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˌalkəˈnəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an organic compound containing both an amino group (–NH₂) and a carboxylic acid group (–COOH), forming a principal type of amino acid
an organic compound with amino and carboxylic acid groups
medical

Examples

The structure of an aminoalkanoic acid includes both an amino and a carboxylic acid group.
Glycine, the simplest aminoalkanoic acid, is found in most proteins.
Scientists synthesized a new aminoalkanoic acid to study its potential for drug development.

Synset Examples

proteins are composed of various proportions of about 20 common amino acids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - an organic compound with both amino and carboxylic groups, the common name for aminoalkanoic acid

Etymology

From 'amino' (containing an amino group) and 'alkanoic acid,' a systematic name for saturated carboxylic acids (alkanes substituted by –COOH).
