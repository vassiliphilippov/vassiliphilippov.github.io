family Hydrophidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli haɪˌdrɑːˈfɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli haɪˌdrɒˈfɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of venomous sea snakes chiefly found in the Indo-Pacific region, known for their aquatic adaptations.
a family of venomous sea snakes

Examples

The family Hydrophidae includes about 60 species of highly venomous sea snakes.
Members of the family Hydrophidae are well adapted to life in saltwater.
Researchers observed breeding behavior unique to the family Hydrophidae during the marine survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea snake - a venomous aquatic snake found in the Indian and Pacific Oceans

Etymology

From New Latin Hydrophidae, based on Greek hydros (water) and ophis (snake).
