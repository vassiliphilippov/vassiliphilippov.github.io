germander speedwell

1. NOUN

/ dʒɚˈmændɚ ˈspiːdˌvɛl / (AmE), / dʒɜːˈmændə ˈspiːdˌvɛl / (BrE)
An Old World perennial plant (Veronica chamaedrys) with small blue-and-white flowers borne in racemes, commonly found in meadows and grassy places.
Old World plant with blue-and-white flowers

Examples

The germander speedwell is easily recognized by its vivid blue petals in the springtime.
Wildflower enthusiasts often seek out germander speedwell during their walks in the countryside.
She planted germander speedwell along the garden path to add a splash of color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speedwell - any plant of the genus Veronica

Etymology

From 'germander', a plant name of uncertain origin, and 'speedwell', a common name for several plants in the genus Veronica.
