Book of Deuteronomy

1. PROPN

/ ˌbʊk əv ˌduːtəˈrɑnəmi / (AmE), / ˌbʊk əv ˌdjuːtəˈrɒnəmi / (BrE)
the fifth book of the Old Testament, containing a second statement of Mosaic law and Moses' final speeches to the Israelites.
fifth book of the Old Testament

Examples

Many of the laws in the Book of Deuteronomy emphasize compassion for the poor.
In his sermon, the pastor referenced the Book of Deuteronomy to illustrate his point.
Scholars have debated the historical origins of the Book of Deuteronomy for centuries.

Etymology

From Greek Deuteronomion (second law), from deuteros (second) and nomos (law).
