Salic law

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪk lɔ / (AmE), / ˈsalɪk lɔː / (BrE)
the code of laws of the Salian Franks, especially noted for prohibiting female succession to thrones or property in certain European countries
legal code of the Salian Franks
legal

Examples

Under Salic law, women were barred from inheriting the French throne.
The historian presented a detailed analysis of Salic law and its influence on European monarchies.
Debates over Salic law shaped succession disputes in several medieval kingdoms.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *lex Salica*, referring to the laws of the Salian Franks.
