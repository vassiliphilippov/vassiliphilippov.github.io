holly family

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːli ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈhɒli ˌfæməli / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed shrubs and trees, including holly, belonging to the family Aquifoliaceae
family of shrubs and trees including holly

Examples

The holly family includes numerous species known for their glossy, evergreen leaves and bright red berries.
Botanists have documented the distribution of the holly family across different continents and climates.
Many plants in the holly family are prized for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aquifoliaceae - the botanical family to which holly belongs
  • Synonym: holly - a type of shrub or tree with glossy leaves and red berries

Etymology

From 'holly' (the type genus Ilex) and 'family' referring to a taxonomic rank in botany.
