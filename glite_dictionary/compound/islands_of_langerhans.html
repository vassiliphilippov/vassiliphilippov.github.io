islands of Langerhans

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪləndz əv ˈlæŋɡɚˌhænz / (AmE), / ˈaɪləndz əv ˈlæŋɡəhænz / (BrE)
groups of specialized cells in the pancreas that produce insulin and other hormones regulating blood sugar
pancreatic cell clusters producing insulin
medical

Examples

Damage to the islands of Langerhans can lead to serious metabolic problems such as diabetes.
Scientists study the islands of Langerhans to better understand how hormones regulate blood sugar levels.
A biopsy revealed changes in the islands of Langerhans that were consistent with early-stage diabetes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancreas - a large gland behind the stomach that secretes digestive enzymes and hormones
  • Synonym: insulin - a hormone produced in the pancreas that regulates blood sugar

Etymology

Named after German pathologist Paul Langerhans (1847–1888), who discovered them.
