ground cable

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a heavy cable used to secure a buoy to a mooring anchor on the seabed
mooring cable from buoy to anchor

Examples

The buoy was held in place by a sturdy ground cable attached to an anchor.
Divers inspected the ground cable to ensure it remained secure after the storm.
Before installing the buoy, workers laid out the ground cable along the seafloor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooring cable - a cable used to secure a vessel, buoy, or structure in place
