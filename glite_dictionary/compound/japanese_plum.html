Japanese plum

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz plʌm / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn.iːz plʌm / (BrE)
A yellow, olive-sized semitropical fruit with a large central stone and little flesh, used for making jellies.
a semitropical fruit with a large stone

Examples

She made a delicious jam from fresh Japanese plum fruit picked from her garden.
The Japanese plum has a unique flavor that is both sweet and tart, perfect for desserts.
We planted a Japanese plum tree in our backyard last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loquat - an evergreen tree and its edible fruit, sometimes called Japanese plum

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz plʌm / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn.iːz plʌm / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to China and Japan, grown in warm regions and producing small, fuzzy, yellow fruit with a large stone, also called loquat
tree with small yellow fruit; also called loquat

Examples

The Japanese plum thrives in subtropical gardens and is prized for its sweet, tangy fruit.
We planted a Japanese plum in the backyard, and it bloomed beautifully this spring.
Her favorite dessert uses fresh Japanese plums picked straight from the tree.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz plʌm / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz plʌm / (BrE)
A small tree native to China and Japan that produces large yellow to red plums, typically less sweet than European plums.
small Asian tree with large yellow to red plums

Examples

The Japanese plum thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.
We picked several ripe Japanese plums from the tree in the garden.
Compared to European varieties, a Japanese plum often has a tarter taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a sweet, edible fruit with a smooth skin and central stone
  • Degree Higher: European plum - a species of plum native to Europe and western Asia, known for its sweet flavor
