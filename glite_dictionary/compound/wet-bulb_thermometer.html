wet-bulb thermometer

1. NOUN

/ wɛt bʌlb θərˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / wɛt bʌlb θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a thermometer with a bulb covered by a moist cloth, used to measure humidity as part of a psychrometer
thermometer for measuring humidity

Examples

The wet-bulb thermometer is essential for measuring relative humidity in weather stations.
After the wet-bulb thermometer was moistened, the technician started the humidity reading process.
Each psychrometer contains a dry-bulb and a wet-bulb thermometer for accurate measurement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry-bulb thermometer - a thermometer with an uncovered bulb; used with a wet-bulb thermometer in a psychrometer
