flightless bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪtləs bɝd / (AmE), / ˈflaɪtləs bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird that is unable to fly, typically having a flat breastbone without a keel for the attachment of flight muscles, such as an ostrich, emu, or kiwi
bird unable to fly

Examples

The ostrich is the world's largest flightless bird and can run at impressive speeds.
Several species of flightless birds inhabit New Zealand, including the famous kiwi.
Unlike most birds, a flightless bird relies on powerful legs for movement rather than wings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bird - a warm-blooded egg-laying vertebrate distinguished by feathers and forelimbs modified as wings

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪtləs bɝd / (AmE), / ˈflaɪtləs bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird that is unable to fly, typically due to evolutionary adaptations such as reduced wings or increased body size
a bird that cannot fly

Examples

The ostrich is the world's largest flightless bird and runs at impressive speeds.
Many islands are home to unique species of flightless birds that evolved in the absence of predators.
The dodo was a famous flightless bird that became extinct in the 17th century.

Synset Examples

Penguins are flightless birds that thrive in cold climates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bird - a warm-blooded egg-laying vertebrate distinguished by feathers, wings, and a beak

Etymology

From 'flightless' (unable to fly) + 'bird'.
