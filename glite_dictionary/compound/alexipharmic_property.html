alexipharmic property

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛksɪˈfɑrmɪk ˈprɑpərti / (AmE), / əˌlɛksɪˈfɑːmɪk ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
A property that counteracts or neutralizes the effects of poison.
property that counteracts poison
medical

Examples

Certain mushrooms are believed to possess alexipharmic property and have been used in traditional medicine.
Researchers are interested in discovering plants with alexipharmic property to develop new antidotes.
The effectiveness of an alexipharmic property depends on the type of toxin involved.

Synset Examples

The herbal tea was thought to have alexipharmic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidotal property - property of counteracting poison
  • Antonym: toxic property - property of being poisonous

Etymology

From Greek alexipharmakon (antidote), from alexein (to ward off) + pharmakon (poison, drug).
