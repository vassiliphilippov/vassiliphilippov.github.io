pit viper

1. NOUN

/ pɪt ˈvaɪpər / (AmE), / pɪt ˈvaɪpə / (BrE)
A venomous viper found in the Americas, distinguished by a heat-sensitive pit between each eye and nostril, and possessing hollow fangs.
venomous American snake with heat-sensing pits

Examples

The pit viper uses its heat-sensitive pits to locate prey even in total darkness.
A bite from a pit viper can be dangerous without prompt medical attention.
Many species of pit viper inhabit forests and grasslands throughout the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - any of various venomous Old World snakes with long, hollow fangs

Etymology

So named because of the presence of a 'pit' organ used for detecting heat.
