egg yolk

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡ joʊk / (AmE), / ɛɡ jəʊk / (BrE)
the yellow, spherical part inside an egg, surrounded by the white, which nourishes a developing embryo or is used in cooking
yellow part of an egg

Examples

She carefully separated the egg yolk from the white before adding it to the batter.
The recipe requires just one egg yolk for extra richness.
Mix the sugar with the egg yolk until it becomes pale and creamy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: egg white - the clear, viscous part of the egg surrounding the yolk
