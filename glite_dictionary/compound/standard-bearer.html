standard-bearer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændərd ˌbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈstændəd ˌbeərə / (BrE)
a soldier who carries the flag or standard representing a military unit, especially in parades or in battle
soldier who carries a unit's flag

Examples

The standard-bearer marched at the front of the regiment during the ceremony.
In battle, the standard-bearer was a target for enemy archers.
The army honored their fallen standard-bearer with a special memorial.

Etymology

From 'standard' (flag or emblem) + 'bearer' (carrier); used since Middle English for a flag or banner carrier.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstændərd ˌbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈstændəd ˌbeə.rə / (BrE)
An important or leading figure in a movement, organization, or cause, especially one who represents its ideals or direction.
prominent leader or representative

Examples

The senator became the standard-bearer for progressive values in the country.
She emerged as the standard-bearer of the environmental movement after the conference.
Many looked to him as the standard-bearer of the party’s new vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
  • Antonym: follower - a person who supports and is guided by another or by a group

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'standard-bearer', originally denoting someone who carries a military or organizational flag, later used figuratively for leaders upholding group ideals.
