public law

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk lɔ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk lɔː / (BrE)
the area of law that deals with the relationship between government and individuals, as well as matters affecting society as a whole, such as constitutional, administrative, and criminal law
law governing state and public affairs
legal

Examples

Changes to public law can impact the entire population through new regulations and policies.
He specialized in public law, focusing on constitutional issues and government accountability.
Criminal law is a branch of public law that involves prosecutions by the state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: private law - law governing relationships between individuals or organizations, not the state
