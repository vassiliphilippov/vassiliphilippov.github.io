maple-leaved bayur

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪpəl liːvd bəˈjʊr / (AmE), / ˈmeɪpəl liːvd bəˈjʊə / (BrE)
An Indian tree with fragrant white flowers that bloom at night and reddish wood used for planking; often cultivated as an ornamental or shade tree.
Indian ornamental tree with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The maple-leaved bayur is prized for its ornamental beauty in many Indian gardens.
Wood from the maple-leaved bayur is used for making durable planks.
The maple-leaved bayur produces fragrant white flowers that open at night.
