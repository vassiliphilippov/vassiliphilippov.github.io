phantom limb syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.təm lɪm sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm lɪm ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A condition in which a person feels pain or other sensations in a limb that has been amputated.
pain or sensation in an amputated limb
medical

Examples

After his leg was amputated, Tom suffered from intense pain due to phantom limb syndrome.
Doctors explained that phantom limb syndrome is a common experience among amputees.
Physical therapy can help some patients cope with the discomfort of phantom limb syndrome.
