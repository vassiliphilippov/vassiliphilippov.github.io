mountain trail

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn ˌtreɪl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən ˌtreɪl / (BrE)
a path or track that leads through mountainous areas, typically intended for hiking or walking
path through mountainous areas

Examples

We hiked the mountain trail for hours before reaching the summit.
The mountain trail was narrow and surrounded by wildflowers.
A fresh snowfall made the mountain trail challenging to follow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain path - path through mountainous regions
  • Degree Higher: mountain road - road through mountains, usu. for vehicles

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən treɪl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn treɪl / (BrE)
a path or route for walking, hiking, or riding in a mountainous area
path in a mountainous area

Examples

We followed the mountain trail up to the summit early in the morning.
Cyclists are advised to ride carefully on the narrow mountain trail.
After a long hike, we finally reached the end of the mountain trail.

Synset Examples

He likes to ride down the mountain trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiking trail - a path designed for hiking
  • Synonym: mountain path - a narrow route in mountainous terrain
  • Antonym: road - a main route for vehicles, less rugged than a trail
