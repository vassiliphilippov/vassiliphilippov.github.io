centre of immersion

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tər əv ɪˈmɜr.ʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv ɪˈmɜːʃn / (BrE)
(physics) The center of mass of the immersed part of a ship or other floating object.
center of mass of submerged part of a vessel

Examples

The stability of the vessel depends partly on the position of its centre of immersion.
Engineers calculated the exact location of the centre of immersion during the ship's design phase.
If the cargo shifts, the centre of immersion can change, affecting the ship's balance.
