family Hylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli haɪˈlɪdiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli hɪˈlɪdaɪ / (BrE)
a family of amphibians commonly known as tree frogs, characterized by their climbing abilities and adhesive toe pads
the family of tree frogs
medical

Examples

Many species in the family Hylidae are found in tropical rainforests and have remarkable climbing abilities.
Scientists studied the vocalizations of several frogs from the family Hylidae for their research on animal communication.
Unlike most ground-dwelling frogs, members of the family Hylidae possess specialized toe pads for gripping surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree frog - a frog belonging to the family Hylidae, typically living in trees

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hylidae', based on Greek 'hyle' (wood, forest), referencing their typical habitat.
