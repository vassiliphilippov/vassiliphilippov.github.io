NREM sleep

1. NOUN

/ ɛn ɑr i ɛm slip / (AmE), / ɛn ɑːr iː ɛm sliːp / (BrE)
A recurring stage of sleep characterized by the absence of rapid eye movements and dreaming, accounting for about 75% of normal sleep time.
stage of sleep without rapid eye movement
medical

Examples

Most deep restorative processes occur during NREM sleep.
Infants spend more time in NREM sleep compared to adults.
Disturbances in NREM sleep can lead to overall poor sleep quality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: REM sleep - stage of sleep with rapid eye movement and intense dreaming

Etymology

Acronym of 'non-rapid eye movement' sleep.
