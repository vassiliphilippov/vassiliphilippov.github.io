indurated clay

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dʊˈreɪ.tɪd kleɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.djʊˈreɪ.tɪd kleɪ / (BrE)
clay that has become hard, often through natural processes such as drying, heating, or chemical action.
hardened clay

Examples

Geologists often find indurated clay at the base of ancient riverbeds.
The foundation was difficult to dig due to a thick layer of indurated clay.
After centuries of pressure, the soft soil turned into indurated clay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard clay - clay that is solid and not pliable

Etymology

From Latin 'induratus' (hardened) + 'clay'.
