cerotic acid

1. NOUN

/ səˈroʊtɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / səˈrɒtɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a white, solid fatty acid found in waxes, such as beeswax
fatty acid found in waxes
medical

Examples

Cerotic acid is commonly found in beeswax and other plant waxes.
Researchers isolated cerotic acid from the surface of apple skins.
The presence of cerotic acid contributes to the protective nature of natural wax coatings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hexacosanoic acid - another name for cerotic acid, a fatty acid found in waxes

Etymology

From Greek kēros (wax), referring to its presence in wax.
