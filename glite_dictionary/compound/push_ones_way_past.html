to push one's way past

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ wʌnz weɪ pæst / (AmE), / pʊʃ wʌnz weɪ pɑːst / (BrE)
to forcefully make progress through a group of people or an obstacle, often by physically moving others aside
move forward by pushing through a crowd

Examples

We had to push our way past the long line of people waiting outside the store.
The paramedics pushed their way past the reporters to reach the injured.
She pushed her way past the crowd to catch her train in time.

Synset Examples

They had to push their way past the protesters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elbow one's way - to push through a crowd using one's elbows
