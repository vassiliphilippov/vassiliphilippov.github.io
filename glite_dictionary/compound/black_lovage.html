black lovage

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈlʌvɪdʒ / (AmE), / blæk ˈlʌvɪdʒ / (BrE)
A European herb, somewhat resembling celery, widely naturalized along British coasts and often grown as a potherb.
European herb similar to celery

Examples

She added chopped black lovage to the soup for extra flavor.
Black lovage thrives in the sandy soils near the British coastline.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate black lovage as an alternative to celery in cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovage - an aromatic European plant whose leaves and seeds are used in salads and seasoning
