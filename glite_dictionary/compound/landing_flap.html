landing flap

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ flæp / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ flap / (BrE)
a movable hinged surface on the underside of an aircraft wing, lowered to increase lift and reduce speed during landing
movable wing surface for slowing aircraft during landing

Examples

The pilot deployed the landing flap to reduce the speed before touching down on the runway.
A malfunction in the landing flap delayed the flight until repairs were made.
Lowering the landing flap helps create more lift at slower speeds during approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flap - a hinged or flexible surface on a vehicle, especially an aircraft, used for control
