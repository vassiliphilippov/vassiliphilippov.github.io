alligator lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪˌɡeɪtər ˌlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtə ˌlɪzəd / (BrE)
a slender, short-limbed lizard of western North America, distinguished by a fold of skin on each side that allows for body expansion
a slim lizard of western North America

Examples

The alligator lizard escaped quickly through the tall grass.
We spotted an alligator lizard basking on a sunny rock during our hike.
Alligator lizards are recognized by the unique fold of skin along their sides.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, and movable eyelids
