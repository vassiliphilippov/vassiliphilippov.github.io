hound's-tongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊndz ˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊndz ˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
a perennial plant with coarse, tongue-shaped leaves and pale-blue to purple flowers, found in North America
plant with tongue-shaped leaves and blue-purple flowers

Examples

The leaves of the hound's-tongue are used in some traditional remedies.
A field of hound's-tongue bloomed with blue and purple flowers in early summer.
Gardeners often remove hound's-tongue because it can be invasive in some areas.

Etymology

Named for the shape of its leaves, which resemble a dog's tongue.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊndz ˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊndz ˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
a biennial herbaceous plant (Cynoglossum officinale) native to Europe and western Asia, with coarse, tongue-shaped leaves and dark reddish-purple flowers
a Eurasian biennial plant with tongue-shaped leaves

Examples

The leaves of the hound's-tongue plant are said to resemble a dog's tongue.
Farmers often remove hound's-tongue from their pastures because it is toxic to livestock.
Botanists studied the spread of hound's-tongue in the region's wildflower populations.

Etymology

From Old English *hundes tunge*, referring to the resemblance of the leaves to a dog's tongue.
