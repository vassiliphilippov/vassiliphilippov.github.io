under control

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndər kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌʌndə kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
managed, regulated, or restrained so as not to be chaotic, out of hand, or dangerous
managed or restrained; not out of hand

Examples

After a brief panic, the crowd was under control again.
Thanks to the medication, his symptoms are now under control.
The manager assured us that everything was under control despite the power outage.

Synset Examples

Just relax and don’t worry; I've got this under control.
The situation was under control after a quick response.
The fire is now under control, thanks to the firefighters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of control - not managed or restrained; chaotic or unmanageable
