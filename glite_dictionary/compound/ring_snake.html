ring snake

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ sneɪk / (AmE), / rɪŋ sneɪk / (BrE)
A small, nonvenomous North American snake characterized by a yellow or orange ring around its neck.
small North American snake with a neck ring

Examples

A ring snake curled up under the log, its bright neck ring clearly visible.
Many gardeners appreciate the ring snake for helping to control insects and small pests.
The child was fascinated by the harmless ring snake he found in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring-necked snake - Small North American snake with a ring marking around the neck

2. NOUN

/ rɪŋ sneɪk / (AmE), / rɪŋ sneɪk / (BrE)
a harmless European snake (Natrix natrix) with a bright yellow collar, commonly found in England and other parts of Europe.
European snake with yellow collar

Examples

The ring snake is often spotted near ponds and slow-moving rivers in rural England.
Despite its appearance, the ring snake poses no threat to humans and is completely non-venomous.
Naturalists were excited to observe a juvenile ring snake basking on the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass snake - another common name for Natrix natrix
