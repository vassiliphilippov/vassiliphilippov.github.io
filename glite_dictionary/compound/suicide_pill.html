suicide pill

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɪˌsaɪd pɪl / (AmE), / ˈsuːɪˌsaɪd pɪl / (BrE)
A strategy or measure adopted by a company to make itself less attractive to an acquirer, often with serious or self-destructive financial consequences for itself.
self-destructive company takeover defense

Examples

The company considered adopting a suicide pill to prevent a hostile takeover bid.
Some analysts criticized the board's suicide pill strategy as too risky for shareholders.
Activating the suicide pill could severely damage the company's long-term prospects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison pill - defensive tactic used by companies to avoid hostile takeovers

Etymology

Modelled metaphorically on 'poison pill,' reflecting a destructive strategy; based on the concept of a suicide pill as a literal means of self-destruction.
