special session

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a meeting convened in addition to the regular scheduled meetings, typically to address urgent or specific matters
an extra meeting held for specific purposes

Examples

The governor called a special session of the legislature to address the budget crisis.
During summer, the council may hold a special session if urgent decisions are needed.
The board convened a special session to discuss the merger proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: session - a period during which a group meets to conduct business
