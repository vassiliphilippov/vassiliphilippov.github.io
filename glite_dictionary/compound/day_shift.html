day shift

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ʃɪft / (BrE)
The group of workers who work during the daytime period in a workplace, typically between the morning and late afternoon.
workers on the daytime work period

Examples

Many hospitals require nurses to rotate between the day shift and night shift.
She prefers working the day shift because it fits her family's schedule.
The day shift clocks in at 8 a.m. and leaves at 4 p.m. every weekday.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: night shift - workers who work during the night period
  • Antonym: swing shift - workers who work an intermediate or rotating shift

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ʃɪft / (BrE)
the period during the day when employees work, typically from morning to afternoon (such as 8 a.m. to 4 p.m.)
work shift during daytime

Examples

He usually works the day shift at the hospital and spends evenings with his family.
After finishing her day shift, Maria went grocery shopping before heading home.
The factory hires extra workers for the day shift during busy seasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: night shift - the work shift during the night
  • Antonym: swing shift - the work shift between day and night shifts
