dizygotic twin

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.zaɪˈɡɑː.tɪk twɪn / (AmE), / daɪ.zɪˈɡɒ.tɪk twɪn / (BrE)
either of two twins that develop from two separate fertilized eggs and are therefore genetically distinct
twin developed from separate eggs
medical

Examples

She learned that her brother is her dizygotic twin, not an identical twin.
Among the participants, there were both monozygotic and dizygotic twins.
Research on dizygotic twins helps scientists understand the influence of environment on development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monozygotic twin - twin developed from a single fertilized egg

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' meaning 'two' and 'zygon' meaning 'yoke, joining'.
