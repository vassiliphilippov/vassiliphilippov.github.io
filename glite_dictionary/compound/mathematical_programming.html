mathematical programming

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl ˈproʊɡræmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmæθ(ə)ˈmatɪkəl ˈprəʊɡramɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of mathematical methods to select the best outcome from a set of alternatives, often involving optimization under constraints
mathematical method for optimization problems
medical

Examples

Many industries use mathematical programming to allocate resources efficiently and minimize costs.
She specializes in mathematical programming for logistics and transportation management.
Advances in mathematical programming have led to improved solutions for complex scheduling problems.

Synset Examples

High-level controllers such as Model predictive control (MPC) or Real-Time Optimization (RTO) employ mathematical optimization. These algorithms run online and repeatedly determine values for decision variables, such as choke openings in a process plant, by iteratively solving a mathematical optimization problem including constraints and a model of the system to be controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimization - the process of making something as effective or functional as possible

Etymology

Derived from the application of 'programming' in the sense of planning or scheduling, combined with 'mathematical' to indicate quantitative formulation.
