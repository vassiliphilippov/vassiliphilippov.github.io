contempt of cop

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛmpt əv kɑp / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpt ɒv kɒp / (BrE)
disrespectful or defiant behavior toward a police officer, especially when it provokes a punitive response from law enforcement
disrespect toward police
slang

Examples

The protester was arrested for contempt of cop after refusing to follow police instructions.
Making an angry gesture at the officer led to a charge of contempt of cop.
Some critics argue that contempt of cop is used to justify unnecessary arrests.

Synset Examples

He was charged with contempt of cop for arguing.
His sarcastic remarks led to a contempt of cop charge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt of court - offense of being disrespectful or disobedient to a court of law

Etymology

Modeled on the legal phrase 'contempt of court', substituting 'cop' (slang for police officer).
