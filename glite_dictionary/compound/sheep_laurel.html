sheep laurel

1. NOUN

/ ʃip ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ʃiːp ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
A North American dwarf shrub (Kalmia angustifolia) with narrow leaves and small red flowers, poisonous to young livestock.
North American poisonous shrub; Kalmia angustifolia

Examples

The fields were dotted with sheep laurel, its red blossoms standing out against the green foliage.
Farmers warn that sheep laurel can be dangerous to grazing animals if not removed from pastures.
Botanists often compare sheep laurel to mountain laurel due to their similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain laurel - a larger related shrub with broader leaves and pink or white flowers
