Mount Tacoma

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt təˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / maʊnt təˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
an alternative historical name for Mount Rainier, a mountain peak in central Washington State, USA, and the highest peak in the Cascade Range (14,410 feet, 4,392 meters high)
historical name for Mount Rainier

Examples

In older maps, Mount Tacoma is shown instead of Mount Rainier.
Some local activists campaigned to restore the original name, Mount Tacoma.
The snow-capped summit of Mount Tacoma is visible from many parts of Seattle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mount Rainier - mountain peak in central Washington; also known as Tacoma

Etymology

Tacoma derives from an indigenous Lushootseed word for the mountain, also known as Takhoma or Tahoma.
