public prosecutor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈprɑːsɪˌkjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈprɒsɪˌkjuːtə / (BrE)
a government official who prosecutes criminal cases on behalf of the state
government official who prosecutes criminal cases
legal

Examples

The public prosecutor presented compelling evidence during the trial.
After reviewing the case, the public prosecutor decided not to file charges.
In many countries, the public prosecutor is responsible for representing the government in criminal courts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district attorney - a public official who prosecutes cases for a specific district in the US
  • Antonym: defense attorney - a lawyer who represents defendants in criminal cases
