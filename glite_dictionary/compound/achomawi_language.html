Achomawi language

1. NOUN

/ ˌætʃəˈmɑwi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌætʃəˈmɑːwi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Shastan language traditionally spoken by the Achomawi people in northeastern California.
a Native American language of northeastern California

Examples

Linguists are working to document the Achomawi language before it disappears completely.
He grew up listening to elders speak the Achomawi language at tribal gatherings.
Few people today are fluent in the Achomawi language due to cultural changes and assimilation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shastan - a group of languages including Achomawi, once spoken in northern California

Etymology

Named after the Achomawi people, indigenous to northeastern California.
