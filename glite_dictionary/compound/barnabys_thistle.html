Barnaby's thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnəbiˌz ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnəbiˌz ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A European weed with a winged stem and hairy leaves, introduced to the eastern United States.
species of European weed with winged stem

Examples

Barnaby's thistle can often be found growing along the edge of fields in southern Europe.
Gardeners in the eastern United States sometimes struggle to control invasive Barnaby's thistle.
The winged stem and hairy leaves make Barnaby's thistle easy to recognize among other weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a prickly plant in the family Asteraceae
