fair ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr ˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈfeə ˌbɔːl / (BrE)
(baseball) a ball hit by the batter that lands within or passes over the area between the foul lines, and is therefore in play.
ball hit within the foul lines (baseball)

Examples

The umpire called it a fair ball as it landed just inside the baseline.
If you hit a fair ball, you must run to first base.
Her line drive down the third base line was ruled a fair ball by the official.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foul ball - (baseball) a ball hit by the batter that lands outside the foul lines, and is out of play
