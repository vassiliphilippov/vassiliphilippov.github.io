compound interest

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmpaʊnd ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpaʊnd ˈɪntrɪst / (BrE)
Interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods.
interest on principal and accrued interest

Examples

The bank account earns compound interest, so your savings grow faster over time.
If you don’t withdraw the earned interest, compound interest can significantly increase your investment.
She learned how compound interest works in her personal finance class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simple interest - interest calculated only on the principal

Etymology

From Latin 'componere' (to put together) and 'interest' from Latin 'interesse' (to be between, of importance).
