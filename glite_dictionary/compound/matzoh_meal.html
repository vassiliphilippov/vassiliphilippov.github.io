matzoh meal

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːtsoʊ ˌmiːl / (AmE), / ˈmæt.səʊ ˌmiːl / (BrE)
meal made by grinding matzos, traditionally used in Jewish cooking, especially during Passover
ground matzos used in Jewish cooking

Examples

Many Passover recipes call for matzoh meal instead of flour.
To make the dumplings, you will need eggs and matzoh meal.
She coated the fish in matzoh meal before frying it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matzah - unleavened bread traditionally eaten during Passover

Etymology

From Yiddish *matse* (unleavened bread), related to Hebrew *matzah*.
