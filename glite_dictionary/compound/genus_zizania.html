genus Zizania

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs zɪˈzeɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs zɪˈzeɪ.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic grasses commonly known as wild rice, native to North America and East Asia, valued for their edible seeds.
genus of wild rice plants

Examples

The seeds of genus Zizania are harvested and eaten as a delicacy.
Several species in the genus Zizania are important to indigenous North American cultures.
Researchers study genus Zizania to improve wetland conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild rice - common name for edible species of genus Zizania

Etymology

From New Latin 'Zizania', from the Greek 'zizania' (weeds among wheat).
