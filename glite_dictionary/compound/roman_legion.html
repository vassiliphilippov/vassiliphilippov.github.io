Roman legion

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈliː.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈliː.dʒən / (BrE)
A division of 3,000 to 6,000 soldiers, including cavalry, in the army of ancient Rome.
division of soldiers in ancient Roman army

Examples

During the Gallic Wars, a Roman legion played a pivotal role in Caesar's campaigns.
Reenactors dressed as members of a Roman legion marched through the town center.
Each Roman legion was known for its disciplined training and strict hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legion - a division of the Roman army, usually comprising 3,000 to 6,000 soldiers

Etymology

From Latin 'legio', meaning 'levy, conscription, army'.
