lake trout

1. NOUN

/ leɪk traʊt / (AmE), / leɪk traʊt / (BrE)
The flesh of a large trout native to northern lakes, used as food.
fish flesh used as food

Examples

The chef prepared the lake trout with fresh herbs and lemon.
We caught a large lake trout during our fishing trip and grilled it that night.
Smoked lake trout is a delicacy in some northern regions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trout - flesh of any freshwater fish of the genus Salmo and related genera
  • Degree Higher: salmon - flesh of the large fish of the family Salmonidae, highly valued as food

2. NOUN

/ leɪk traʊt / (AmE), / leɪk traʊt / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish of the salmon family found in lakes in Canada and the northern United States, valued as a game fish and for food
a large North American freshwater trout

Examples

We caught a massive lake trout during our fishing trip in Ontario.
The chef prepared a delicious dish with fresh lake trout from the local lake.
Lake trout are prized by anglers for their size and fighting spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common name for several freshwater fish related to salmon
