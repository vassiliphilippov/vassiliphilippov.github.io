wedding band

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛdɪŋ bænd / (AmE), / ˈwɛdɪŋ band / (BrE)
a plain ring, typically made of gold, given during a marriage ceremony to symbolize marital commitment
a ring given at a wedding ceremony

Examples

She glanced at her wedding band and smiled softly, remembering the day they exchanged vows.
After the ceremony, both the bride and groom wore matching wedding bands.
He nervously slipped the wedding band onto her finger during the marriage ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding ring - a ring given or received during a marriage ceremony to symbolize marriage

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛdɪŋ bænd / (AmE), / ˈwɛdɪŋ band / (BrE)
a ring, typically made of precious metal, exchanged by the bride and groom during a wedding ceremony to symbolize their marriage
a ring exchanged during a wedding ceremony

Examples

During the ceremony, they exchanged wedding bands as a symbol of their commitment.
She lost her wedding band at the beach but fortunately someone returned it.
The jeweler offers custom engraving on every wedding band they sell.

Synset Examples

The couple had their names engraven on the wedding bands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding ring - a ring given during a wedding ceremony, often identical in meaning to 'wedding band'
  • Antonym: engagement ring - a ring given as a symbol of an intention to marry, typically prior to the wedding
