Antarctic continent

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɑːrk.tɪk ˈkɑːn.tɪ.nənt / (AmE), / ænˈtɑːk.tɪk ˈkɒn.tɪ.nənt / (BrE)
the extremely cold continent surrounding the South Pole, almost entirely within the Antarctic Circle and covered by a thick ice cap
the icy southernmost continent

Examples

Scientists have set up research stations across the Antarctic continent to study its unique environment.
The Antarctic continent is known for its harsh climate and towering ice sheets.
Due to its extreme conditions, the Antarctic continent has no permanent human population.

Synset Examples

Antarctica is twice the size of Australia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Antarctica - the extremely cold continent that includes the South Pole
