Medieval Greek language

1. NOUN

/ məˈdɑːvəl ɡrik ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪˈdiːvl ɡriːk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The form of the Greek language spoken and written from about 600 to 1200 AD, during the medieval period of Greek history.
Greek language from 600 to 1200 AD

Examples

Scholars often study the Medieval Greek language to understand the Byzantine Empire's literature.
Many religious texts from the era were originally written in Medieval Greek language.
The transition from Ancient Greek to Medieval Greek language shows significant changes in grammar and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Ancient Greek - Greek language from roughly the 9th century BC to 6th century AD
  • Degree Higher: Modern Greek - Greek language from about 1453 to the present

Etymology

From 'medieval' (from Latin 'medium aevum', meaning middle age) and 'Greek language'.
