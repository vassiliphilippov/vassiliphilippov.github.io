seed grain

1. NOUN

/ sid ɡreɪn / (AmE), / siːd ɡreɪn / (BrE)
good quality seeds, such as kernels of corn, that are kept for planting rather than for consumption.
grain or seeds reserved for planting

Examples

Only the best seed grain was selected to ensure a healthy crop for the next season.
Farmers must store seed grain in dry conditions to prevent spoilage.
The agricultural cooperative supplied certified seed grain to local growers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grain - the small hard seeds of food plants such as wheat, rice, or corn
