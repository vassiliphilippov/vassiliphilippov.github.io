Congo red

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡoʊ ˈrɛd / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ ˈrɛd / (BrE)
a red-brown azo dye used as a pH indicator, turning red in basic and blue in acidic solutions
a red-brown dye and pH indicator

Examples

The laboratory technician added Congo red to the sample to determine its pH level.
When exposed to an acidic environment, Congo red changes from red to blue.
Medical researchers use Congo red staining to identify amyloid deposits in tissue samples.

Etymology

Named after the Congo region in Africa, though not related to the place; 'red' refers to the dye's color.
