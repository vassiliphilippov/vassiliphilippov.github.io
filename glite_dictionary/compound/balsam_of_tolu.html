balsam of tolu

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.səm əv təˈlu / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.səm əv təˈluː / (BrE)
an aromatic yellowish-brown resin obtained from the tolu balsam tree, used especially in cough syrups and perfumery
aromatic resin from the tolu tree
medical

Examples

Traditional cough syrups often contained balsam of tolu for its soothing properties.
The perfumer added a drop of balsam of tolu to enrich the fragrance.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of balsam of tolu extracted from South American trees.

Etymology

From Spanish 'bálsamo de tolú', named after Tolú, a region in northern Colombia where the tree is native.
