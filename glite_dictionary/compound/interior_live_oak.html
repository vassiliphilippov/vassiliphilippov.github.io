interior live oak

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɪriər laɪv oʊk / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriə laɪv əʊk / (BrE)
a shrubby evergreen oak tree (Quercus wislizeni) of western North America, chiefly found in foothills away from the coast, and an important component of chaparral ecosystems
a shrubby evergreen oak tree of western North America

Examples

The interior live oak is commonly found in the foothills bordering the Central Valley of California.
Wildfires sometimes benefit the interior live oak, which has adapted to survive in the chaparral.
Unlike the coast live oak, the interior live oak thrives away from the immediate seaside environment.
