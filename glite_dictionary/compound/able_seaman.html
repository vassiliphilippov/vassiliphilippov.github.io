able seaman

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪbəl ˈsiːmən / (AmE), / ˌeɪbəl ˈsiːmən / (BrE)
a sailor in the merchant navy who is skilled in seamanship and holds a qualification equivalent to that of a fully trained deckhand
skilled sailor in the merchant navy

Examples

After several years at sea, he was finally promoted to able seaman.
The shipping company requires all applicants to hold an able seaman certificate.
As an able seaman, she was responsible for operating the lifeboats during emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ordinary seaman - a sailor one rank below able seaman in the merchant navy

Etymology

From able (skilled) + seaman (sailor), used since the 18th century for a qualified naval rank.
