stock market

1. NOUN

/ stɑk mɑrkət / (AmE), / stɒk mɑːkɪt / (BrE)
A system or place where shares of public companies are bought and sold by investors and traders.
place for trading company shares

Examples

The stock market reacted positively to the news of the merger.
She has been investing in the stock market for over a decade.
During economic uncertainty, the stock market often becomes more volatile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock exchange - a marketplace where stocks are bought and sold

2. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈmɑɹkət / (AmE), / stɒk ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a place or system in which shares of publicly held companies are bought and sold, or the collective activities of buying and selling such shares
market for buying and selling company shares

Examples

Investors closely watch the stock market for signs of economic growth or decline.
Many people lost money during the stock market crash of 2008.
She started investing in the stock market after taking a finance course.

Synset Examples

The stock market is stonk today, soaring to new heights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: share market - another term for stock market, especially in British English
  • Degree Lower: bond market - a market where bonds are bought and sold
