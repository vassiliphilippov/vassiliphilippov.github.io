Mexican sunflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən ˈsʌnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a tall, coarse herb or shrub of Mexico to Panama, of the genus Tithonia, with large flower heads resembling sunflowers and yellow to orange-scarlet rays
a tall flowering plant of the genus Tithonia

Examples

The Mexican sunflower adds bright color to gardens with its fiery orange blooms.
Bees and butterflies are drawn to the Mexican sunflower during the late summer months.
She planted a row of Mexican sunflowers along the garden fence for a striking display.
