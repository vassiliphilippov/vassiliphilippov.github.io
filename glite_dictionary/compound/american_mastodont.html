American mastodont

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmæstəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmæstəˌdɒnt / (BrE)
An extinct species of mastodon native to North America, sometimes classified as a mammoth rather than a mastodon.
extinct North American mastodon

Examples

The American mastodont roamed the forests of North America during the late Pleistocene era.
Fossils of the American mastodont have been unearthed in several states, revealing much about its diet and habitat.
Scientists debate whether the American mastodont should be classified separately from mammoths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - an extinct elephant-like mammal with ridged molar teeth
  • Synonym: mammoth - a large extinct elephant, sometimes confused with mastodons

Etymology

From 'American', relating to America, and 'mastodont', from Greek 'mastos' (breast) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the animal's tooth shape.
