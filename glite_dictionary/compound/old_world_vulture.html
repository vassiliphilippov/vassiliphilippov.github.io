Old World vulture

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld ˈvʌltʃər / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
any of several large scavenging birds of prey native to Africa, Europe, and Asia, distinct from New World vultures by taxonomy and characteristics
large scavenging bird of Africa, Europe, and Asia

Examples

The Old World vulture is adapted to soaring over open plains in search of carcasses.
Unlike its American counterpart, the Old World vulture relies mainly on sight to find food.
Several species of Old World vulture are now considered endangered due to habitat loss and poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: New World vulture - scavenging bird found in the Americas, not closely related to Old World vultures
