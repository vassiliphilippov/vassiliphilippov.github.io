continuous creation theory

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪnjuəs kriˈeɪʃən ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjuəs kriˈeɪʃən ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
a theory in cosmology proposing that the universe maintains a constant average density by continually creating matter as galaxies move apart, filling the void left as the universe expands
cosmological theory of ongoing matter creation

Examples

Many physicists once supported the continuous creation theory as an alternative to the Big Bang model.
The continuous creation theory suggests that new matter forms as the universe expands.
Although the continuous creation theory was influential, most scientists now favor the Big Bang theory.

Synset Examples

the steady state theory has been abandoned in favor of the big bang theory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steady state theory - cosmological theory proposing an unchanging universe with ongoing creation of matter
  • Antonym: big bang theory - theory that the universe originated from a singular rapid expansion
