law school

1. NOUN

/ lɔ ˈskuːl / (AmE), / lɔː ˈskuːl / (BrE)
a graduate school where students study to earn a law degree and prepare for a legal career
graduate school for legal studies

Examples

After graduating from college, she decided to apply to law school.
He spent three years at law school before passing the bar exam.
Many top law schools have competitive admission processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medical school - a graduate school for studying medicine
  • Degree Higher: lawyer - a person qualified to practice law
