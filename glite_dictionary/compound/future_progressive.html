future progressive

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjutʃər prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˈfjʊtʃə prəˈɡresɪv / (BrE)
A verb tense used to describe an action that will be ongoing at a particular time in the future.
ongoing future verb tense

Examples

She will be studying abroad this time next year, using the future progressive tense.
When you arrive, I will be waiting for you at the station, expressed in the future progressive.
The sentence 'They will be traveling all summer' uses the future progressive to indicate continuous activity.

Synset Examples

‘I will be running’ is an example of the future progressive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: future continuous - another term for the future progressive tense
  • Degree Lower: present progressive - verb tense showing ongoing action in the present
  • Degree Lower: future simple - verb tense for single actions in the future

Etymology

From 'future' (from Latin futurus, about to be) and 'progressive' (from Latin progressivus, advancing).
