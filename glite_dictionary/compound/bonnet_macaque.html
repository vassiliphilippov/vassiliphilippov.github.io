bonnet macaque

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑː.nɪt məˈkæk / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.ɪt məˈkɑːk / (BrE)
a medium-sized monkey of southern India (Macaca radiata) with a distinctive bonnet-shaped tuft of hair on its head
Indian monkey with a bonnet-like tuft of hair

Examples

The bonnet macaque is commonly seen in temple areas of southern India.
Researchers studied the behavior of the bonnet macaque in its natural habitat.
A group of bonnet macaques quickly adapted to urban environments near Bangalore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaque - any of various medium-sized monkeys of the genus Macaca

Etymology

Named for its distinctive head tuft resembling a bonnet; 'macaque' from French, originally from Portuguese 'macaco'.
