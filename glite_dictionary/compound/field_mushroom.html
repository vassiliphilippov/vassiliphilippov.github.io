field mushroom

1. NOUN

/ fiːld ˈmʌʃ.ruːm / (AmE), / fiːld ˈmʌʃ.rʊm / (BrE)
a common edible mushroom with a white cap, typically found growing in fields and grassy areas, often cultivated for food
edible mushroom found in fields

Examples

We found a cluster of field mushrooms while walking through the meadow.
The field mushroom is often used in soups and salads for its mild flavor.
After a night of rain, many field mushrooms appeared on the lawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - fleshy, spore-bearing fungus
