genus Monotropa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmɑːnəˈtroʊpə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmɒnəˈtrəʊpə / (BrE)
A genus of leafless, fleshy, saprophytic plants, sometimes classified in the family Pyrolaceae, including species such as the Indian pipe.
a genus of leafless, saprophytic plants

Examples

The genus Monotropa includes unusual plants that lack chlorophyll and are often found in forest undergrowth.
Botanists study the genus Monotropa for its unique way of obtaining nutrients from decaying matter.
One notable member of the genus Monotropa is the Indian pipe, known for its ghostly white appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monotropa - A genus of non-photosynthetic plants in the family Ericaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'genus' (type) and 'Monotropa', the genus name from Greek 'monos' (single) + 'tropos' (turn).
