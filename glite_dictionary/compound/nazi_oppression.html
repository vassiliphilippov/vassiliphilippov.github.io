Nazi oppression

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑzi əˌprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɑːzi əˌprɛʃən / (BrE)
the severe and authoritarian control imposed by the Nazi regime in Germany, especially from 1933 to 1945
harsh control by Nazi regime

Examples

The courage of those who resisted Nazi oppression continues to inspire people around the world.
Many families were torn apart by Nazi oppression during World War II.
Historians have documented numerous acts of bravery in the face of Nazi oppression.

Synset Examples

The film depicted the struggles against Nazi oppression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyranny - cruel and oppressive government or rule
  • Synonym: persecution - the act of subjecting someone to hostility and ill-treatment, especially because of religion, race, or political beliefs
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from imprisonment, slavery, or oppression

Etymology

From 'Nazi,' abbreviation of the German 'Nationalsozialist,' and 'oppression,' from Latin 'opprimere' (to press against).
