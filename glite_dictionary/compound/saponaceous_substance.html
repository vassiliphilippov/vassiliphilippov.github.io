saponaceous substance

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈneɪʃəs ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˌsapəˈneɪʃəs ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
a substance or compound that is soap-like in nature or composition
soap-like compound or substance

Examples

Many plants produce a saponaceous substance that foams when mixed with water.
The cleaning agent contains a saponaceous substance extracted from natural sources.
Scientists discovered a saponaceous substance in the bark that helps repel insects.

Synset Examples

The saponaceous substance is found in many plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soap - a substance used for cleaning, made by combining fats or oils with an alkali

Etymology

From Latin 'sapo' (soap) + English suffix '-aceous' (of the nature of).
