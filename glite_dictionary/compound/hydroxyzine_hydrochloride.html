hydroxyzine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑksɪˌzin haɪˌdrɑxləˈraɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksɪziːn haɪˌdrɒksɪləˈraɪd / (BrE)
a drug used as a tranquilizer to treat anxiety and motion sickness; also marketed under the trade names Atarax and Vistaril
a drug for anxiety and motion sickness
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hydroxyzine hydrochloride to help manage her anxiety symptoms.
Before the surgery, he was given hydroxyzine hydrochloride to reduce his nerves and prevent nausea.
Hydroxyzine hydrochloride is often used to treat motion sickness during long flights.

Etymology

From 'hydroxy-' (relating to the hydroxyl group) and 'zine', referencing its chemical structure; 'hydrochloride' denotes the hydrochloric acid salt form.
