water-plantain family

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌplæn.tɪn ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.tə ˌplæn.tɪn ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family of perennial or annual aquatic or marsh plants, including water-plantains, typically found in wet habitats
family of aquatic or marsh plants

Examples

The water-plantain family is common in wetlands throughout North America.
Several species in the water-plantain family are cultivated for use in ponds and water gardens.
Members of the water-plantain family have distinctive broad leaves and thrive in shallow water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alismataceae - the scientific name for the water-plantain family
