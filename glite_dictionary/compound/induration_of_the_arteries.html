induration of the arteries

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndʊˈreɪʃən əv ði ˈɑrtəriz / (AmE), / ˌɪndjʊˈreɪʃən əv ði ˈɑːtəriz / (BrE)
The hardening or thickening of the walls of the arteries, typically due to sclerosis.
hardening of arterial walls
medical

Examples

Long-term hypertension can lead to induration of the arteries and other cardiovascular issues.
The patient's symptoms were attributed to induration of the arteries discovered during his examination.
A high-fat diet may increase the risk of induration of the arteries later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - thickening and hardening of artery walls

Etymology

From Latin 'indurare' meaning 'to harden', and 'artery' from Greek 'artēria'.
