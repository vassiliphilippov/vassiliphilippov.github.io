burst of fire

1. NOUN

/ bɝst əv faɪɚ / (AmE), / bɜːst əv faɪə / (BrE)
a sudden, intense outpouring of flames, energy, or intensity, often occurring unexpectedly
sudden intense outpouring of flames or energy

Examples

The fireplace gave a sudden burst of fire that startled everyone in the room.
Her singing ended with an emotional burst of fire, impressing the entire audience.
With a final burst of fire, the rocket shot into the night sky.

Synset Examples

His speech concluded with a last burst of fire that energized the crowd.

The fireworks ended with a last burst of fire lighting up the sky.
