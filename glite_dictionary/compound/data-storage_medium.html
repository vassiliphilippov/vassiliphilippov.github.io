data-storage medium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtə ˌstɔrɪdʒ ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtə ˌstɔːrɪdʒ ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A physical material or device used to store digital or analog information for later retrieval or use.
device or material for storing information

Examples

Before cloud services, a data-storage medium like the floppy disk was commonly used for backups.
Choosing the right data-storage medium depends on capacity, durability, and speed requirements.
Optical discs are a popular data-storage medium for distributing software and movies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storage device - hardware used to store data
