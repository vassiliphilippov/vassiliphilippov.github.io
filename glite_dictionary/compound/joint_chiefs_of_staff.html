Joint Chiefs of Staff

1. NOUN

/ dʒɔɪnt tʃifs əv stæf / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt tʃiːfs əv stɑːf / (BrE)
the highest-ranking military advisory body to the President of the United States, consisting of the chiefs of the Army, Navy, Air Force, and the Marine Corps commandant
U.S. military advisory body
american

Examples

The Joint Chiefs of Staff met with the President to discuss national security issues.
She received a commendation from the Joint Chiefs of Staff for her outstanding service.
The advice of the Joint Chiefs of Staff is crucial in matters of defense policy.
