Commodore John Barry Bridge

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑː.məˌdɔr dʒɑn ˈbæri brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.məˌdɔː dʒɒn ˈbæri brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a cantilever bridge spanning the Delaware River between Chester, Pennsylvania, and Bridgeport, New Jersey, named after Commodore John Barry.
cantilever bridge over the Delaware River in Pennsylvania

Examples

The Commodore John Barry Bridge offers stunning views of the Delaware River from its central span.
Heavy traffic caused a delay near the Commodore John Barry Bridge during the morning commute.
Construction on the Commodore John Barry Bridge began in the early 1970s and was completed in 1974.

Etymology

Named after Commodore John Barry, a prominent officer in the United States Navy.
