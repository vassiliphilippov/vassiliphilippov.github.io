alluvial fan

1. NOUN

/ əˈlu.vi.əl fæn / (AmE), / əˈljuː.vi.əl fæn / (BrE)
A fan-shaped accumulation of sediment formed where a fast-flowing stream loses velocity and spreads out, typically at the base of mountain ranges.
fan-shaped deposit from flowing stream

Examples

The valley floor was covered by a massive alluvial fan formed over thousands of years.
Sediments deposited by the river created a broad alluvial fan at the mountain's edge.
Geologists studied the alluvial fan to understand past flooding events in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delta - A landform at the mouth of a river where sediment is deposited, forming a roughly triangular area

Etymology

From Latin 'alluvius' (washed against), referring to deposits from flowing water, and 'fan' for its shape.
