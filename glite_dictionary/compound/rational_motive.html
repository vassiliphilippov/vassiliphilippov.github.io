rational motive

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃənl ˈmoʊtɪv / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəl ˈməʊtɪv / (BrE)
a motive or reason for an action that can be justified by logical argument or reasoning
a justifiable, logical motive

Examples

She believed her decision was based on a rational motive rather than emotion.
The jury considered whether the defendant had a rational motive for his actions.
Choosing to save money for the future is often driven by a rational motive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrational motive - a motive not justified by reason or logic
