pronominal phrase

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈnɑmɪnəl freɪz / (AmE), / prəˈnɒmɪnəl freɪz / (BrE)
a phrase that functions in the sentence as a pronoun, taking the place of a noun or noun phrase
phrase acting as a pronoun

Examples

The pronominal phrase 'each of them' refers to all people in the group.
In the sentence, 'one of these', the pronominal phrase acts as the subject.
Teachers often highlight the difference between a simple pronoun and a pronominal phrase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that takes the place of a noun

Etymology

From 'pronominal' (from Latin 'pronomen', meaning 'pronoun') + 'phrase' (from Greek 'phrasis', meaning 'speech').
