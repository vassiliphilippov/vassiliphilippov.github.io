tailless tenrec

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪl.ləs ˈtɛn.rɛk / (AmE), / ˈteɪl.ləs ˈtɛn.rɛk / (BrE)
a species of tenrec native to Madagascar, recognized for its lack of tail and prolific breeding, that feeds mainly on earthworms
a Madagascan mammal that eats earthworms

Examples

The tailless tenrec often forages at night, searching for earthworms in the forest floor.
Researchers observed a tailless tenrec digging vigorously in the moist soil of Madagascar.
Unlike other mammals in its habitat, the tailless tenrec has a rounded shape and no visible tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenrec - a small insectivorous mammal native to Madagascar and Africa
