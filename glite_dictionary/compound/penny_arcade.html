penny arcade

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ɑrˌkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ɑːˌkeɪd / (BrE)
a place with coin-operated machines and games for amusement, especially popular in the early 20th century
amusement venue with coin-operated machines

Examples

The old penny arcade was filled with flashing lights and ringing bells.
He spent all afternoon playing games at the penny arcade by the seaside.
Many cities had a penny arcade where children could enjoy affordable entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcade - a place with a variety of games or amusements, often coin-operated

Etymology

Named for the penny, the common cost per game in early arcades, and 'arcade' meaning a covered passage with shops or amusements.
