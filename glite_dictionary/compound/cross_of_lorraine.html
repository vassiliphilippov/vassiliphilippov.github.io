Cross of Lorraine

1. NOUN

/ krɔs əv ləˈreɪn / (AmE), / krɒs əv ləˈreɪn / (BrE)
a cross consisting of a vertical bar with two horizontal crossbars, the lower one longer than the upper, used as a symbol of Lorraine and in various historical contexts
a cross with two unequal bars, symbolic of Lorraine

Examples

The Cross of Lorraine is prominently featured in the coat of arms of the region.
During World War II, the Free French Forces adopted the Cross of Lorraine as their emblem.
He wore a pendant shaped like the Cross of Lorraine to show his support for the resistance.

Etymology

Named after the region of Lorraine in France, where it is a traditional symbol.
