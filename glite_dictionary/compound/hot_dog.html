hot dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt dɔg / (AmE), / ˈhɒt dɒg / (BrE)
A cooked sausage, typically made of beef or pork, served in a sliced bun and eaten as a sandwich, often with condiments.
frankfurter in a bun

Examples

I bought a hot dog from the street vendor during the baseball game.
She likes her hot dog with mustard and onions.
Kids lined up to get a hot dog at the summer picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankfurter - a type of sausage, often used in hot dogs

2. NOUN

/ hɑt dɔg / (AmE), / hɒt dɒg / (BrE)
a cooked sausage made of finely minced beef or pork, often smoked and typically eaten in a sliced bread roll
type of sausage served in a bread roll

Examples

She grabbed a hot dog from the food cart during her lunch break.
At the baseball game, everyone was eating hot dogs with mustard and relish.
Would you like onions or ketchup on your hot dog?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankfurter - a type of smooth, smoked sausage similar to a hot dog

Etymology

From American English, possibly an allusion to the notion that sausages contained dog meat in the late 19th century.
