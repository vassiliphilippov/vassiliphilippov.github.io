positive adverb

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzətɪv ˈædvɚb / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv ˈædvɜːb / (BrE)
A form of an adverb that expresses a simple quality, without indicating comparison or degree of intensity.
adverb form indicating simple quality

Examples

In the sentence 'She ran fast,' the word 'fast' is used as a positive adverb.
A positive adverb does not compare actions, but simply describes how something is done.
'Quickly' in 'He responded quickly' is an example of a positive adverb.

Synset Examples

An example using a positive-degree adverb that doesn't end in ‘-ly’ would be the adverb ‘fast’ in the sentence, ‘She drove fast’.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: comparative adverb - an adverb indicating a higher degree of comparison
  • Degree Higher: superlative adverb - an adverb indicating the highest degree of comparison
