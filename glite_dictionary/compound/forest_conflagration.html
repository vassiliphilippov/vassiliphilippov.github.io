forest conflagration

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.ɪst ˌkɑn.fləˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪst ˌkɒn.fləˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a large, uncontrollable fire occurring in a forest area
large fire in a forest

Examples

The forest conflagration destroyed thousands of acres before firefighters could contain it.
Smoke from the forest conflagration blanketed nearby towns for days.
New safety measures were implemented after last year's devastating forest conflagration.

Synset Examples

The forest conflagration spread quickly, alarming the residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildfire - an uncontrolled fire in the countryside or wilderness

Etymology

From Latin forestis (forest) and conflagratio (burning up).
