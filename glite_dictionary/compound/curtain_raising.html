curtain raising

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːtən ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtən ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the first public performance of a theatrical production or event; the opening of a show
the first performance of a show

Examples

The theater was buzzing with excitement before the curtain raising of the much-anticipated musical.
His new play had its curtain raising last night and drew a full house.
They planned a special reception to mark the curtain raising of the festival's opening show.

Synset Examples

the opening received good critical reviews

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premiere - the first public performance of a play, film, etc.

Etymology

From 'curtain' (the screen in a theater) and 'raising' (lifting); refers to the rise of the stage curtain at the beginning of a performance.
