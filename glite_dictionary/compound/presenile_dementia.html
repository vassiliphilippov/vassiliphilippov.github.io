presenile dementia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˈsaɪnəl dɪˈmɛnʃə / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈsiːnaɪl dɪˈmɛnʃə / (BrE)
A type of dementia that begins before the age of 65, characterized by a decline in cognitive function.
dementia with onset before age 65
medical

Examples

Early diagnosis of presenile dementia can help improve the quality of life for younger patients.
Her husband began showing signs of presenile dementia when he was only 58 years old.
Doctors differentiate presenile dementia from other cognitive disorders due to its earlier onset.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: senile dementia - dementia with onset in old age, typically after 65

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'senilis' (of old age), combined with Latin 'dementia' (madness, insanity).
