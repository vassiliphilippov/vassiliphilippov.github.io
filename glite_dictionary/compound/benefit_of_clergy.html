benefit of clergy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnɪfɪt əv ˈklɜrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪt əv ˈklɜːdʒi / (BrE)
formal or legal sanction by a religious ceremony, especially marriage; originally, an exemption from ordinary legal jurisdiction granted to clergymen.
religious or legal sanction, esp. marriage
archaic

Examples

They lived together for years without the benefit of clergy and finally decided to marry.
In previous centuries, marriage outside the benefit of clergy was often seen as scandalous.
The phrase benefit of clergy now serves mostly as a historical reference.

Synset Examples

they are living together without benefit of clergy

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'beneficium clericatus' – a privilege granted to clergy; later extended figuratively.
