cautious statement

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔʃəs ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃəs ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
a statement made with careful qualification to avoid risk or error
carefully qualified statement

Examples

She gave a cautious statement to the press, avoiding any strong commitments.
The scientist's cautious statement reflected the need for further research before drawing conclusions.
A cautious statement can help prevent misunderstandings in sensitive situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedge - a word or phrase used to avoid giving a direct answer
  • Antonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
