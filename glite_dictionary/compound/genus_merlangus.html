genus Merlangus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs mərˈlæŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs məˈlæŋɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the cod family, including the common whiting and related species.
a fish genus including the whiting

Examples

The genus Merlangus includes the common whiting, which is found in European coastal waters.
Scientists studied several species within the genus Merlangus to understand their evolutionary relationships.
Commercial fishing regulations often apply to members of the genus Merlangus to ensure the sustainability of the whiting population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiting - a common edible fish of the cod family, Merlangius merlangus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Merlangus', a medieval name for the whiting fish.
