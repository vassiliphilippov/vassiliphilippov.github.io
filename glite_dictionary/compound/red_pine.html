red pine

1. NOUN

/ rɛd paɪn / (AmE), / rɛd paɪn / (BrE)
A pine tree native to eastern North America, having long needles in pairs and reddish bark, known for its valuable timber.
a North American pine tree with reddish bark

Examples

The red pine forests are important habitats for many species of wildlife.
He recognized the red pine by its distinctive reddish bark and long needles.
Many homes in the area were built using sturdy red pine timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scots pine - a Eurasian pine tree with reddish bark

2. NOUN

/ rɛd paɪn / (AmE), / rɛd paɪn / (BrE)
A tall New Zealand timber tree, known for its reddish wood; also called rimu.
New Zealand timber tree (rimu)

Examples

The native forests are home to the majestic red pine, which grows up to 50 meters tall.
Furniture made from red pine is valued for its attractive appearance and durability.
The conservation team studied several mature red pines to monitor their health and growth.
