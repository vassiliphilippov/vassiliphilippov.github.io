hereditary spinal ataxia

1. NOUN

/ həˌrɛdəˌtɛri ˈspaɪnl əˈtæksiə / (AmE), / hɛˌrɛdɪˈtɛəri ˈspaɪnl əˈtæksiə / (BrE)
A progressive disorder involving sclerosis of the posterior and lateral columns of the spinal cord, characterized by muscular weakness and abnormal gait, usually beginning in childhood.
genetic spinal cord disorder causing ataxia
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with hereditary spinal ataxia after observing his abnormal gait and difficulty with coordination.
Families with a history of hereditary spinal ataxia often seek genetic counseling for their children.
Research into hereditary spinal ataxia has led to advances in understanding neurodegenerative diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Friedreich's ataxia - A specific type of hereditary spinal ataxia

Etymology

From Latin hereditarius (hereditary) and Greek ataxiā (lack of order, disorder).
