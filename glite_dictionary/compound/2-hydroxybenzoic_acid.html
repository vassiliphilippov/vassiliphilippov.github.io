2-hydroxybenzoic acid

1. NOUN

/ tu ˌhaɪdroʊksi bɛnˈzoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / tuː ˌhʌɪdrɒksi bɛnˈzəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound used as a fungicide and in the manufacture of aspirin, dyes, and perfumes; also known as salicylic acid
white crystalline chemical compound (salicylic acid)
medical

Examples

2-hydroxybenzoic acid is a key ingredient in many acne treatment products.
The pharmaceutical company synthesizes 2-hydroxybenzoic acid to manufacture aspirin.
Dyes and fragrances often contain 2-hydroxybenzoic acid as a precursor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salicylic acid - white crystalline acid used in medicine and industry

Etymology

From the chemical structure: hydroxy- (hydroxyl group) + benzoic acid (from benzene).
