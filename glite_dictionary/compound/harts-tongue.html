hart's-tongue

1. NOUN

/ hɑrts ˈtʌŋ / (AmE), / hɑːts ˈtʌŋ / (BrE)
A Eurasian fern with simple, lance-shaped fronds, commonly found in shady, moist habitats.
Eurasian fern with lance-shaped fronds

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with hart's-tongue growing among mossy rocks.
Botanists admired the unique appearance of the hart's-tongue fern during their field study.
Gardeners sometimes plant hart's-tongue for its attractive, glossy foliage and tolerance of dense shade.

Etymology

From Middle English; named for its frond's resemblance to a deer's tongue ('hart' meaning male deer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrts ˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːts ˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
A tropical American terrestrial fern with leathery, lance-shaped fronds, sometimes classified in the genus Polybotrya.
tropical American fern with leathery fronds

Examples

The hart's-tongue thrives in shaded, humid environments throughout the tropics.
Gardeners sometimes grow hart's-tongue for its glossy, leathery leaves.
Researchers discovered a new species of hart's-tongue fern in Central America.

Etymology

Named for the shape of the frond, which resembles the tongue of a deer ('hart').
