hatchet man

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃɪt mæn / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪt man / (BrE)
a person employed to carry out difficult or unpleasant tasks, especially those that may ruin reputations or involve dismissals, on behalf of someone in authority
person hired to do unpleasant tasks for a superior

Examples

He gained a reputation as the company's hatchet man after laying off hundreds of workers.
As the new hatchet man, she was tasked with restructuring the entire department.
The politician hired a hatchet man to discredit his opponents during the campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enforcer - a person who imposes obedience or carries out orders, especially in an organization

Etymology

From 'hatchet', a small axe, used metaphorically to suggest someone who 'cuts down' others for an employer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃɪt mæn / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪt man / (BrE)
A man who is hired to kill someone, usually as a professional assassin.
male professional killer or assassin

Examples

The mob boss sent his trusted hatchet man to eliminate the witness.
Detectives believe a hatchet man carried out the carefully planned murder.
He was rumored to work as a hatchet man for various criminal organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hitman - a person hired to kill someone; professional killer or assassin
  • Synonym: assassin - a person who murders someone for political or monetary reasons

Etymology

From 'hatchet', a small axe, and 'man'; originally referred to killers using hatchets in organized crime.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
