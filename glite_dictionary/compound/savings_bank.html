savings bank

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋz ˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋz ˌbaŋk / (BrE)
a financial institution that accepts savings deposits and pays interest, traditionally focused on encouraging personal savings, and now often offering services similar to commercial banks
financial institution for savings deposits

Examples

He decided to open an account at the local savings bank to start building his emergency fund.
Many savings banks now provide online banking and competitive loan rates.
Her grandparents deposited money for her college fund in a savings bank years ago.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: commercial bank - a bank that offers services such as accepting deposits and giving business loans

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋz bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋz baŋk / (BrE)
A small container with a slot in the top, used to keep savings, especially coins, at home.
container for saving coins at home

Examples

My children each have a savings bank shaped like a rocket.
She shook her savings bank to see how many coins were inside.
He deposited his loose change into his savings bank every night.

Synset Examples

the coin bank was empty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piggy bank - a small money box, often shaped like a pig
  • Synonym: money box - a box for saving coins, typically with a slot in the top
