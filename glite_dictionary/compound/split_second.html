split second

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplɪt ˌsɛk.ənd / (AmE), / ˈsplɪt ˌsɛk.ənd / (BrE)
an extremely short moment; an instant
a very brief moment

Examples

She reacted in a split second and avoided the accident.
Everything changed in the split second it took for the power to go out.
He hesitated for a split second, but it was enough for his opponent to score.

Synset Examples

if I had the chance, I'd do it in a flash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - a very short period of time
  • Synonym: instant - an extremely brief period of time
  • Antonym: eternity - an infinite or unending time

Etymology

From 'split' (to divide) and 'second' (unit of time), meaning a second split into smaller parts.
