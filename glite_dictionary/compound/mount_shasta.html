Mount Shasta

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈʃæstə / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈʃɑːstə / (BrE)
a volcanic mountain peak in the Cascade Range in northern California, rising to 14,162 feet (4,317 meters)
volcanic mountain in northern California

Examples

Many climbers are drawn to Mount Shasta for its challenging routes and breathtaking views.
We could see the snow-capped summit of Mount Shasta from miles away as we drove north.
Mount Shasta is an important landmark and a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Etymology

Named after the Shasta people, a Native American tribe in the region.
