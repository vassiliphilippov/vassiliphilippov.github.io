vacuum gage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring pressure below atmospheric pressure (a partial vacuum)
instrument for measuring negative pressure

Examples

The technician checked the vacuum gage to ensure the chamber was properly sealed.
If the vacuum gage registers zero, there may be a leak in the system.
A faulty vacuum gage can lead to incorrect pressure readings in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure gauge - an instrument for measuring pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuus' (empty) and Middle English 'gage' (measuring instrument).
