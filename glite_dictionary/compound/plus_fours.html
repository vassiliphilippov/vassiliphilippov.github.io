plus fours

1. NOUN

/ plʌs fɔrz / (AmE), / plʌs fɔːz / (BrE)
a type of baggy knickerbockers, worn by men, that extend four inches below the knee, traditionally used for sporting activities such as golf.
baggy knickers extending below the knee
british

Examples

He wore a tweed jacket and plus fours to the vintage golf tournament.
Photographs from the 1920s reveal many men sporting plus fours on the golf course.
Although no longer common, plus fours occasionally appear at themed sporting events.

Etymology

From the idea that the trousers are four inches longer than standard knickerbockers ('plus four inches').
