grama grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmə ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of pasture grass native to the plains of South America and western North America, often used for grazing.
a pasture grass of American plains

Examples

The ranchers let their cattle graze freely on the grama grass during the summer months.
Much of the prairie was covered in grama grass and wildflowers.
Drought-tolerant grama grass is an important species for restoring native grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo grass - a similar type of North American prairie grass

Etymology

From Spanish 'grama', meaning 'grass'.
