with a poof

1. X

/ wɪð ə puf / (AmE), / wɪð ə puːf / (BrE)
Used to indicate a sudden and complete disappearance or change, as if by magic.
sudden disappearance or change

Examples

With a poof, all the balloons vanished from the stage.
The cardboard castle collapsed with a poof, much to the children's delight.
He solved the puzzle and, with a poof, the hidden door swung open.

Synset Examples

With a poof, the magician made the rabbit disappear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a flash - very quickly or suddenly
  • Synonym: in an instant - in a very short time, immediately
