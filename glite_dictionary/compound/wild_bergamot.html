wild bergamot

1. NOUN

/ waɪld bərˈɡɑːmət / (AmE), / waɪld ˈbɜːɡəmɒt / (BrE)
a North American aromatic herb of the genus Monarda, with clusters of purple or pinkish flowers, often used in teas or as an ornamental plant
aromatic herb of the genus Monarda

Examples

The leaves of wild bergamot can be used to make a fragrant herbal tea.
Gardeners love planting wild bergamot for its showy flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
Native Americans traditionally used wild bergamot as a medicinal herb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee balm - another common name for Monarda plants

Etymology

From 'wild' (not cultivated) and 'bergamot', a name shared with the bergamot orange due to similar scent.
