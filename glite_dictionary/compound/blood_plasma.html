blood plasma

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈplæzmə / (AmE), / blʌd ˈplɑːzmə / (BrE)
the pale yellowish, liquid component of blood that holds the blood cells in suspension and contains proteins, nutrients, hormones, and waste products
liquid component of blood, holds blood cells
medical

Examples

During the transfusion, the patient received blood plasma to help restore lost fluids and clotting factors.
Scientists study blood plasma to understand how diseases affect different components of the body.
The laboratory separated red blood cells from blood plasma for specialized testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasma - the liquid part of blood or lymph, without the cells
