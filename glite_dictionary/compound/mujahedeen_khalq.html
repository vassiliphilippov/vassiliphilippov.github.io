Mujahedeen Khalq

1. PROPN

/ muːˌdʒɑːhəˈdiːn kælˈk / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑːhəˈdiːn kælˈk / (BrE)
A militant Iranian opposition group, formerly based in Iraq, known for its resistance against the Iranian government.
Iranian opposition group; militant organization

Examples

The Mujahedeen Khalq has been involved in various conflicts with the Iranian government.
Many members of the Mujahedeen Khalq were evacuated from their camp in Iraq in recent years.
The international community has had differing views on the status of the Mujahedeen Khalq.

Etymology

From Arabic 'mujāhidīn' (those who strive, fighters) and Persian 'Khalq' (people).
