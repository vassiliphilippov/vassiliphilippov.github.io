inclusion body myositis

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkluʒən ˈbɑdi ˌmaɪoʊˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən ˈbɒdi ˌmaɪəʊˈsaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A progressive muscle disorder characterized by weakness and inflammation of limb muscles, commonly affecting people over 50, often more in men.
progressive muscle disease in older adults
medical

Examples

His diagnosis of inclusion body myositis explained the persistent muscle weakness in his hands and thighs.
Researchers are studying new treatments for inclusion body myositis to slow its progressive symptoms.
Difficulty swallowing is a common symptom in advanced cases of inclusion body myositis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myositis - inflammation of muscles

Etymology

From 'inclusion body' (referring to abnormal protein deposits in cells) and 'myositis' (from Greek mys 'muscle' + -itis 'inflammation').
