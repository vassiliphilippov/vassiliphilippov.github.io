Egyptian paper rush

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈpeɪpər rʌʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈpeɪpə rʌʃ / (BrE)
a tall sedge native to the Nile valley, historically used for its fiber to make papyrus and other products
tall Nile sedge used for papyrus

Examples

The ancient Egyptians cultivated Egyptian paper rush for making scrolls and other artifacts.
Explorers found remnants of woven mats made from Egyptian paper rush along the Nile.
Today, some craftspeople still use Egyptian paper rush to create traditional boats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papyrus - writing material made from the pith of the Egyptian paper rush
