consonant mutation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnsənənt mjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsənənt mjuˈtʃeɪʃən / (BrE)
a change in a consonant in a word depending on its morphological or syntactic environment, common in some languages
phonological change of consonants in words

Examples

In Welsh, consonant mutation regularly occurs at the beginning of words after certain grammatical triggers.
Some languages, such as Irish, employ consonant mutation to mark grammatical relationships.
Linguists study consonant mutation to understand how language rules affect word forms.

Synset Examples

Welsh is a language with a rich and productive system of initial consonant mutation.

Etymology

From Latin 'consonans' (sounding together) and Latin 'mutatio' (change, alteration).
