imperial gallon

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəl ˈɡælən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəl ˈɡælən / (BrE)
A unit of capacity used in Britain and some other countries, equal to 4 quarts or 4.545 liters, mainly for liquids.
British unit of capacity (4.545 liters)
british

Examples

In the UK, petrol is often sold by the imperial gallon rather than the US gallon.
One imperial gallon of water weighs approximately 10 pounds at its maximum density.
He needed to convert liters to imperial gallons for the British recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallon - standard unit of capacity, varies between US and UK systems
  • Antonym: US gallon - US customary unit of capacity equal to 3.785 liters

Etymology

From 'imperial' referring to the British Empire, and 'gallon,' from Old French *galon*.
