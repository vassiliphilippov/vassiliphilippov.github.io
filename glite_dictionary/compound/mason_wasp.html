mason wasp

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsən wɑsp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsən wɒsp / (BrE)
a solitary wasp that builds nests from hardened mud or clay for its offspring
solitary wasp building mud nests

Examples

A mason wasp collects mud to construct its nest on the wall.
Mason wasps are often seen around gardens during the summer.
The egg-laying habits of the mason wasp help control pest populations.

Etymology

From 'mason' (builder in stone or brick), referring to the wasp's nest-building behavior, and 'wasp'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsən wɑsp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsən wɒsp / (BrE)
a solitary wasp that builds nests from hardened mud for its young
a wasp that makes mud nests

Examples

A mason wasp carefully collects mud to construct a sturdy nest for its eggs.
Gardeners often appreciate mason wasps because they help control pest insects.
The mason wasp sealed each chamber of her nest with a pellet of mud.

Related Terms

Etymology

From 'mason' (a builder in stone or brick) due to nest-building behavior, and 'wasp'.
