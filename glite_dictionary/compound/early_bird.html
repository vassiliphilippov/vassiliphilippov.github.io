early bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrli bɝd / (AmE), / ˈɜːli bɜːd / (BrE)
a person who arrives or gets up early before others do
person who arrives or gets up early

Examples

As an early bird, she always managed to finish her assignments before anyone else started.
The hotel offers a special breakfast deal for early birds who check in before 7 am.
He loved being an early bird and enjoyed the quiet hours of the morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: night owl - person who stays up late at night

Etymology

From the proverb 'the early bird catches the worm', first recorded in the 17th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɝli bɝd / (AmE), / ˈɜːli bɜːd / (BrE)
a person who arrives early or takes the initiative, often in order to gain an advantage
person who arrives early or takes initiative

Examples

My mom is an early bird and always finishes her work before breakfast.
As an early bird, Tom secured the best seats for the concert.
They call her the office early bird because she always arrives before everyone else.

Synset Examples

He believes in being the early bird for opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: latecomer - a person who arrives late
  • Synonym: go-getter - an energetic and enterprising person

Etymology

From the proverb 'the early bird catches the worm,' dating to the 17th century.
