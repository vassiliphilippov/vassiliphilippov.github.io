Greek clover

1. NOUN

/ ɡriːk ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡriːk ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
An annual herb native to southern Europe and eastern Asia, with off-white flowers and aromatic seeds used in medicine and curries.
herb with aromatic seeds used in cooking and medicine

Examples

The seeds of Greek clover are often ground and used in Indian curries.
Herbalists sometimes prescribe Greek clover for its purported health benefits.
We planted Greek clover in the garden to harvest its aromatic seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fenugreek - another common name for Greek clover, used for its seeds in cooking and medicine
