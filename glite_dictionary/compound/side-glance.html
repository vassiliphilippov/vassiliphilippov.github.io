side-glance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌɡlæns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌɡlɑːns / (BrE)
A quick or indirect look to the side, often expressing emotion or intention.
a quick look to the side

Examples

She gave him a sharp side-glance, unsure of his intentions.
With a subtle side-glance, he acknowledged her presence across the room.
The teacher caught the students exchanging a side-glance during the test.

Synset Examples

she shot him an impatient side-glance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance - a brief or hurried look
