thematic vowel

1. NOUN

/ θəˈmætɪk ˈvoʊəl / (AmE), / θɪˈmatɪk ˈvəʊəl / (BrE)
a vowel that appears at the end of a stem and precedes an inflection in certain languages
vowel ending a stem before inflection

Examples

In Latin verb conjugation, the thematic vowel determines the class of the verb.
The absence of a thematic vowel in some forms alters their pronunciation.
Linguists analyze the thematic vowel to study patterns of inflection in Indo-European languages.

Etymology

From Greek 'thematikos' (of a subject or theme) and 'vowel'.
