order Mucorales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər mjuːkɔːˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə mjuːkɔːˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of mainly saprophytic fungi within the class Zygomycetes, including many species that live on decaying organic matter.
taxonomic order of saprophytic fungi
medical

Examples

The order Mucorales includes fungi commonly found as bread molds.
Several members of the order Mucorales can cause infections in humans, especially in immunocompromised patients.
Scientists study the order Mucorales to understand the ecology of decomposing plant material.

Etymology

From New Latin Mucorales, based on the genus Mucor + -ales (order suffix).
