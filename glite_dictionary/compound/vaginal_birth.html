vaginal birth

1. NOUN

/ ˈvædʒɪnəl bɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈvadʒɪnəl bɜːθ / (BrE)
the process of delivering a baby through the vagina, as opposed to by cesarean section
giving birth through the vagina
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a vaginal birth because there were no complications in the pregnancy.
Many women prefer a vaginal birth for its quicker recovery time compared to a cesarean section.
She shared her positive vaginal birth experience at the parenting class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cesarean section - surgical procedure to deliver a baby through incisions in the abdomen and uterus
  • Synonym: childbirth - the process of giving birth to a child

Etymology

From Latin 'vagina' (sheath, scabbard) and 'birth' (Old English 'byrþ').
