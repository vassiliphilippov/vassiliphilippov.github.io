air cushion

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌkʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌkʊʃən / (BrE)
A mechanical device that uses trapped air to absorb shocks and reduce impact from motion.
shock-absorbing device using air

Examples

The car's air cushion reduces the impact when driving over potholes.
Many modern trainers include an air cushion in the sole for extra comfort.
The engineer recommended installing an air cushion to protect the delicate equipment from vibrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock absorber - device that absorbs and dampens shock impulses

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌkʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌkʊʃən / (BrE)
A cushion, typically made of rubber or plastic, that can be filled with air to provide support or comfort.
inflatable cushion for support or comfort

Examples

She sat down on the air cushion to make the hard bench more comfortable.
When camping, an air cushion is useful for resting and keeping warm.
The stadium seats were equipped with a small air cushion for each guest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air mattress - an inflatable mattress filled with air

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌkʊʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌkʊʃ.ən / (BrE)
a layer of trapped air that supports a hovercraft a short distance above the surface of water or ground
layer of trapped air supporting a hovercraft

Examples

The hovercraft glides over water using an air cushion for support.
An air cushion is maintained beneath the craft by powerful fans.
Engineers monitor the air cushion to ensure stability during operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hovercraft - a vehicle that travels above the surface on a cushion of air
