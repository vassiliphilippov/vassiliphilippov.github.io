pure schlock

1. NOUN

/ pjʊr ʃlɑk / (AmE), / pjʊə ʃlɒk / (BrE)
completely inferior or worthless material, especially in art, entertainment, or writing
utterly worthless material
informal

Examples

Most critics agreed that the sequel was pure schlock and not worth watching.
The exhibition was dismissed by reviewers as pure schlock with no originality.
I can't believe I wasted money on that pure schlock; it was terrible from start to finish.

Synset Examples

The movie was pure schlock, lacking any real substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trash - worthless material or entertainment
  • Synonym: junk - of little or no value
  • Antonym: masterpiece - a work of outstanding skill or artistry

Etymology

Schlock is from Yiddish 'shlak' (secondary, refuse).
