long-billed marsh wren

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ bɪld mɑrʃ rɛn / (AmE), / lɒŋ bɪld mɑːʃ rɛn / (BrE)
A small American songbird found in tall reed beds, distinguished by its long bill and preference for marshy habitats.
American marsh-dwelling wren species

Examples

The long-billed marsh wren is often heard singing among the cattails in spring.
We spotted a long-billed marsh wren as we walked along the wetlands boardwalk.
Conservation efforts have helped to protect the habitat of the long-billed marsh wren.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh wren - a North American wren species living in marshes
