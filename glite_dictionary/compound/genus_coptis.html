genus Coptis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkɑp.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkɒp.tɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs with yellow rhizomes and white or yellow flowers, native to temperate regions.
genus of perennial herbs; yellow rhizomes, white/yellow flowers

Examples

The medicinal value of genus Coptis has been recognized in traditional Asian herbal remedies.
Botanists discovered a new species belonging to the genus Coptis in the mountain forests.
Many plants in the genus Coptis are cultivated for their attractive foliage and flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and Neo-Latin 'Coptis', from Greek 'koptein' (to cut), referring to its divided leaves.
