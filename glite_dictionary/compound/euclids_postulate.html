Euclid's postulate

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːklɪdz ˈpɑːstuˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈjuːklɪdz ˈpɒstjʊlət / (BrE)
any of the five foundational axioms proposed by Euclid as the basis for Euclidean geometry
axiom forming basis of Euclidean geometry
medical

Examples

According to Euclid's postulate, through any point not on a given line, there is exactly one parallel to the line.
The fifth Euclid's postulate is also known as the parallel postulate.
Modern mathematicians have explored geometries that do not require Euclid's postulate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiom - a statement accepted as true without proof, especially as a basis in mathematics
  • Synonym: Euclidean geometry - the system of geometry based on Euclid's definitions and postulates

Etymology

Named after Euclid, ancient Greek mathematician, and from Latin 'postulatum' (something assumed without proof).
