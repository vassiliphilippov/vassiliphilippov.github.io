Indian millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a valuable forage grass of dry upland areas and plains found in western North America to northern Mexico
forage grass of North America

Examples

Ranchers often plant Indian millet to provide food for livestock in arid regions.
The tall stalks of Indian millet help prevent soil erosion on open plains.
Researchers are studying Indian millet as a drought-resistant crop alternative.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈmɪlət / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
A type of sorghum grown in dry regions of Asia and North Africa, valued as a cereal crop or forage.
a sorghum cereal grass from dry regions

Examples

Farmers in arid regions often plant Indian millet because it tolerates drought conditions well.
The livestock were fed Indian millet during the dry season when other grasses failed.
Scientists are developing new varieties of Indian millet to improve crop yields in semi-arid areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of grasses cultivated as cereals
