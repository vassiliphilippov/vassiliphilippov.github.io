sand tumor

1. NOUN

/ sænd ˈtuː.mɚ / (AmE), / sænd ˈtjuː.mə / (BrE)
A tumor arising from fibrous tissue of the meninges, choroid plexus, or related brain structures, distinguished by the presence of sandlike particles.
tumor with sandlike particles in brain tissues
medical

Examples

The MRI revealed a sand tumor in the patient’s meninges, prompting immediate consultation with a neurosurgeon.
Doctors noted the characteristic granules during the biopsy of the sand tumor found in her brain.
Researchers are studying how sand tumors develop their distinctive sandlike particles within nervous system tissues.
