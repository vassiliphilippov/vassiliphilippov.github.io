landscape architect

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænd.skeɪp ˌɑr.kəˌtɛkt / (AmE), / ˈlan(d).skeɪp ˈɑː.kɪ.tɛkt / (BrE)
a person who plans and designs outdoor spaces, such as gardens, parks, and landscapes, to be functional and visually attractive
person who designs outdoor spaces and gardens

Examples

The city hired a landscape architect to redesign the public park with new walking paths and native plants.
As a landscape architect, she specializes in creating sustainable gardens for urban environments.
The museum grounds were transformed by the work of an award-winning landscape architect.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gardener - person who tends and cultivates a garden
  • Synonym: architect - person who designs buildings and structures

Etymology

From 'landscape' (originally Dutch 'landschap') + 'architect' (from Greek 'arkhitektōn,' chief builder).
