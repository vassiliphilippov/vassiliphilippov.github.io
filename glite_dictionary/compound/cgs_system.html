cgs system

1. NOUN

/ ˌsi ˌdʒi ˈɛs ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌsiː ˌdʒiː ˈɛs ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system of measurement based on centimeters, grams, and seconds, primarily used in scientific contexts
measurement system using centimeters, grams, and seconds

Examples

The cgs system was widely used in physics before the adoption of SI units.
Many scientific papers from the early 20th century use the cgs system for their measurements.
Today, the International System of Units has mostly replaced the cgs system in most fields.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: SI system - the International System of Units, the modern successor to the cgs system

Etymology

Acronym from 'centimeter-gram-second system', dating to late 19th century scientific usage.
