petty larceny

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛti ˈlɑrsəni / (AmE), / ˈpɛti ˈlɑːsəni / (BrE)
The theft of property whose value is less than a legally specified amount, considered a minor offense compared to grand larceny.
theft of low-value property (minor legal offense)
legal

Examples

He was charged with petty larceny after stealing a watch from the store.
The court dismissed the petty larceny case due to lack of evidence.
Shoplifting small items usually falls under the category of petty larceny in most states.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grand larceny - theft of property exceeding a legally specified value (major theft)
  • Synonym: theft - the act of stealing; general term for taking property illegally

Etymology

From 'petty' (from Old French 'petit', meaning small) and 'larceny' (from Anglo-French 'larcin', theft).
