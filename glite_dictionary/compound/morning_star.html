morning star

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrnɪŋ ˌstɑr / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ ˌstɑː / (BrE)
a planet, usually Venus, visible in the eastern sky shortly before sunrise
Venus as seen before sunrise

Examples

The morning star shone brightly just above the horizon as dawn approached.
For ancient civilizations, the appearance of the morning star was often a signal of important events.
She woke up early to catch a glimpse of the morning star before the sun rose.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evening star - Venus as seen in the evening sky after sunset
