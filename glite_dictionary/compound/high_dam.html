High Dam

1. PROPN

/ haɪ dæm / (AmE), / haɪ dæm / (BrE)
the Aswan High Dam; a massive embankment dam on the Nile River in southern Egypt, built to control flooding, provide irrigation, and generate hydroelectric power
the Aswan High Dam on the Nile in Egypt

Examples

The High Dam has played a crucial role in Egypt's development since its completion.
Tourists often visit the High Dam to learn about its history and impact on the Nile.
Construction of the High Dam created Lake Nasser, one of the largest artificial lakes in the world.
