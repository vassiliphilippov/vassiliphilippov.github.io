McArdle's disease

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑrdlz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / məˈkɑːdlz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a rare inherited disorder characterized by abnormal accumulation of glycogen in skeletal muscle, leading to muscle weakness and cramping
inherited muscle glycogen storage disease
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with McArdle's disease after experiencing severe muscle cramps during exercise.
People with McArdle's disease may need to modify their physical activity to avoid muscle fatigue.
Research into McArdle's disease has led to a better understanding of muscle metabolism disorders.

Etymology

Named after British physician Brian McArdle, who first described the disease in 1951.
