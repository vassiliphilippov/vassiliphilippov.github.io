diesel locomotive

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːzəl loʊˈkoʊmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈdiːzəl ləʊˈkəʊmətɪv / (BrE)
A railway locomotive powered by a diesel engine rather than by steam or electricity.
locomotive powered by a diesel engine

Examples

The old diesel locomotive chugged past the station, pulling a string of freight cars behind it.
Many short-haul trains still rely on a diesel locomotive because some tracks are not electrified.
In the museum, you can see a restored diesel locomotive from the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electric locomotive - locomotive powered by electricity
  • Antonym: steam locomotive - locomotive powered by a steam engine

Etymology

From the name of Rudolf Diesel, inventor of the diesel engine, combined with 'locomotive'.
