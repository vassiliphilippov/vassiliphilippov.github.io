contract bridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.trækt brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trækt brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a card game for four players in two partnerships, where points are scored only for tricks originally bid and won
a type of card game for four players

Examples

After learning the rules of contract bridge, she joined a local club to improve her skills.
Many families enjoy playing contract bridge together during holiday gatherings.
He won the contract bridge tournament after weeks of intense competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridge - a trick-taking card game from which contract bridge developed

Etymology

Named for the 'contract' system introduced in the 1920s, emphasizing bidding contracts for points.
