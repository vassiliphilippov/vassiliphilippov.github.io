codling moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːdlɪŋ mɑːθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒdlɪŋ mɒθ / (BrE)
a small grey moth whose larvae infest and feed inside apples and English walnuts, causing damage to fruit crops
moth whose larvae damage apples and walnuts

Examples

The codling moth is a major pest for apple growers worldwide.
Farmers monitor their orchards carefully to detect any sign of codling moth infestation.
Biological control methods can help manage codling moth populations in commercial fruit production.
