gray lemming

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈlɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈlɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
a small rodent of the Old World lemming species, found in parts of northern Europe and Asia, noted for its gray fur
Old World lemming species

Examples

The gray lemming is well adapted to survive harsh Arctic winters.
Researchers tracked the movements of several gray lemmings across the tundra.
Unlike other rodents, the gray lemming remains active throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - small mouselike rodent found in Arctic regions
