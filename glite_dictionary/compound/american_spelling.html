American spelling

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The standard form of English spelling used in the United States, typically differing from British forms in certain words.
United States form of English spelling
american

Examples

Many children learn American spelling at school, such as writing 'color' instead of 'colour'.
The company uses American spelling in all its official documents.
He noticed the American spelling of 'organize' in the report.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: British spelling - Standard spelling in the United Kingdom, distinct from American spelling in many words
