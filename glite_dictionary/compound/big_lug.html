big lug

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ lʌɡ / (AmE), / bɪɡ lʌɡ / (BrE)
a large, clumsy, but endearing person
a clumsy but lovable person
informal

Examples

Everyone loves Jake; he's just a big lug who always makes us laugh.
Don't be so hard on the big lug; he means well, even if he messes up.
She playfully called her brother a big lug as he stumbled carrying groceries.

Synset Examples

The big lug helped carry all the boxes.
Don’t mind him; he’s just a big lug at heart.
He’s such a big lug, always tripping over his feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oaf - a clumsy or unintelligent person
  • Synonym: lug - a clumsy, awkward person (informal)
