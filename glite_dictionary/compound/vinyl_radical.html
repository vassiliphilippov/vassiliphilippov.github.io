vinyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪnəl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪnɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group (CH2=CH−) derived from ethylene, important in organic synthesis
univalent chemical group from ethylene
medical

Examples

The vinyl radical plays a key role in the formation of polyvinyl chloride.
Researchers studied how the vinyl radical reacts with different compounds.
Generation of a vinyl radical is a critical step in this chemical process.

Etymology

From 'vinyl', derived from Latin 'vinum' (wine), and 'radical', from Latin 'radix' (root).
