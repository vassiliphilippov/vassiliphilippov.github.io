ethical drug

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪkəl ˌdrʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪkəl ˌdrʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that can only be obtained with a prescription from a doctor or dentist
prescription-only drug
medical

Examples

Only a licensed physician can prescribe an ethical drug for this condition.
The pharmacist cannot sell an ethical drug without a valid prescription.
Doctors are careful when prescribing any ethical drug due to potential side effects.

Synset Examples

he told the doctor that he had been taking his prescription regularly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prescription drug - a drug that can only be obtained with a doctor's prescription

Etymology

From the use of 'ethical' to mean 'conforming to professional standards', indicating drugs dispensed according to medical ethics.
