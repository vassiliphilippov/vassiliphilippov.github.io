lymph vessel

1. NOUN

/ lɪmf ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / lɪmf ˈvɛsl̩ / (BrE)
A thin-walled vessel in the lymphatic system that carries lymph fluid and eventually returns it to the bloodstream.
vessel carrying lymph fluid
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a blocked lymph vessel can cause swelling in the affected limb.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the lymph vessel.
Cancer sometimes spreads to other parts of the body through the lymph vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymphatic vessel - another term for lymph vessel
  • Antonym: blood vessel - vessel carrying blood in the circulatory system

Etymology

From Greek 'lympha' (water) and Latin 'vasculum' (small vessel).
