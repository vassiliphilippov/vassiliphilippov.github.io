order Corrodentia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər kəˌroʊˈdɛnʃiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kəˌrəʊˈdɛnʃɪə / (BrE)
a former order of insects that included booklice and bark-lice, now usually classified within Psocoptera
former insect order (booklice, bark-lice)

Examples

The classification of order Corrodentia has changed with advances in insect taxonomy.
Booklice were formerly grouped under order Corrodentia, but now belong to Psocoptera.
Scientists once considered bark-lice as part of the order Corrodentia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Psocoptera - the order of insects now including booklice and bark-lice

Etymology

From Latin 'corrodere' (to gnaw), referring to the gnawing mouthparts of these insects.
