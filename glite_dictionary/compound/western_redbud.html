western redbud

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈrɛdˌbʌd / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈrɛdˌbʌd / (BrE)
A shrub native to the western United States, known for its pink or crimson flowers and tendency to form thickets.
North American flowering shrub

Examples

The western redbud adds vibrant color to gardens in early spring.
Many birds are attracted to the western redbud during its blooming season.
She planted a row of western redbud along the fence for their beautiful flowers and dense growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redbud - a tree or shrub with rosy-pink flowers, especially of the genus Cercis
