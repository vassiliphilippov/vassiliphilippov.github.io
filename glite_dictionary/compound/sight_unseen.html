sight unseen

1. ADV

/ saɪt ʌnˈsin / (AmE), / saɪt ʌnˈsiːn / (BrE)
without having first seen or inspected something; based only on a description or recommendation.
without having seen beforehand

Examples

They decided to buy the car sight unseen, relying on the dealer's reputation.
She agreed to rent the apartment sight unseen, trusting the photos online.
Many collectors purchase rare items sight unseen at auctions.

Synset Examples

They adopted the puppy sight unseen from the shelter.
I bought the house sight unseen, trusting my agent.
Ordering the furniture sight unseen was a gamble.
