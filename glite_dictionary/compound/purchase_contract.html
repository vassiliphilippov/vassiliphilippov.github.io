purchase contract

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.tʃəs ˌkɑn.trækt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.tʃəs ˌkɒn.trækt / (BrE)
a legal agreement that sets out the terms and conditions of a purchase between a buyer and a seller
legal agreement for a purchase

Examples

The company signed a purchase contract with the supplier to secure a steady inventory of products.
Before closing on the house, both parties reviewed the purchase contract carefully.
A detailed purchase contract can help prevent disputes between buyers and sellers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sales contract - contract setting terms of sale between two parties
  • Antonym: lease agreement - contract for renting property
