Aussie salute

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔzi səˈlut / (AmE), / ˈɒzi səˈluːt / (BrE)
A gesture, typically in Australia, of waving a hand near the face to swat away flies.
gesture to wave away flies (Australia)
informal british

Examples

He gave an Aussie salute as soon as the flies started buzzing around his head.
Tourists quickly learn the Aussie salute when visiting the outback in summer.
She couldn't stop laughing after everyone at the barbecue began giving the Aussie salute.

Synset Examples
