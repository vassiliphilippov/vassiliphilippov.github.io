Deep South

1. PROPN

/ dip saʊθ / (AmE), / diːp saʊθ / (BrE)
A region in the southeastern United States, typically including South Carolina, Georgia, Alabama, Mississippi, and Louisiana, noted for its history, culture, and historical association with cotton production and slavery before the Civil War.
southeastern U.S. region with historic ties to slavery and cotton

Examples

Many people consider the Deep South to be the birthplace of several styles of American music.
He grew up in the Deep South, surrounded by the traditions and history of the region.
The summer heat in the Deep South can be almost unbearable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old South - the southern states of the U.S. before the Civil War, especially those associated with a plantation economy
  • Antonym: Midwest - a region of the northern central United States
