intelligence service

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
an organization or unit responsible for collecting and analyzing information, especially about potential threats or enemies, typically for a government
organization gathering and analyzing secret information

Examples

He worked for the British intelligence service during the war.
The intelligence service uncovered a plot against the government.
Many countries have their own intelligence service to protect national security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret service - an organization for intelligence or security activities, especially for protecting leaders
