pressure point

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a place, situation, or aspect that is likely to cause problems or difficulties, especially in a process or system
place where problems may arise

Examples

Negotiations stalled at a pressure point when both sides refused to compromise.
The company's finances have reached a pressure point that demands immediate attention.
The introduction of new technology became a major pressure point for the management team.

Synset Examples

a key pressure point in the controversy was the building permit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashpoint - a place, event, or time at which trouble, such as violence or anger, flares up

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a place on the body where pressure can be applied to an artery to control bleeding at a point further from the heart
point on body to control bleeding
medical

Examples

When treating a severe cut, apply direct pressure over the pressure point to slow the bleeding.
Paramedics are trained to identify each pressure point in case of emergencies.
She pressed firmly on the pressure point in his arm until the bleeding stopped.

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
An area on the body that is particularly sensitive to pressure, often used in massage, acupuncture, or martial arts to produce certain effects.
sensitive area of the body responsive to pressure

Examples

The therapist applied gentle pressure to a pressure point on my shoulder to relieve tension.
Knowing where each pressure point is can help in both healing and self-defense techniques.
During the exam, the doctor pressed a pressure point to check for tenderness.
