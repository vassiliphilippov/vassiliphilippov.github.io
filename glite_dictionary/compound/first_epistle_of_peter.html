First Epistle of Peter

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɜrst ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpitər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ɪˈpɪsl əv ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
the first of two New Testament letters traditionally attributed to Saint Peter the Apostle, addressing early Christian communities
New Testament letter by Saint Peter

Examples

The First Epistle of Peter encourages believers to stand firm in the face of suffering.
Many scholars debate the authorship of the First Epistle of Peter.
Our Bible study group is currently reading the First Epistle of Peter together.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Second Epistle of Peter - the second New Testament letter attributed to Saint Peter

Etymology

Epistle from Latin 'epistula' (letter), from Greek 'epistolē', meaning letter or message. Named for its traditional attribution to Saint Peter.
