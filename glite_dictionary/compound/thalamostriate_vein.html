thalamostriate vein

1. NOUN

/ θəˌlæmoʊˈstraɪeɪt veɪn / (AmE), / θəˌlaməʊˈstraɪeɪt veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the brain that drains blood from the thalamus and corpus striatum
brain vein draining thalamus and corpus striatum
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the thalamostriate vein during the procedure.
Enlargement of the thalamostriate vein can be observed in certain neurological disorders.
Radiologists examined the flow through the thalamostriate vein in the MRI scans.

Etymology

From thalamo-, referring to the thalamus, and striate, referring to the striatum; both are structures in the brain.
