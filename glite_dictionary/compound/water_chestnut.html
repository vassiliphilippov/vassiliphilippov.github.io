water chestnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.tə ˌtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
an aquatic plant of East Asia with edible bulb-shaped tubers, often used in Asian cooking
aquatic plant with edible tubers used in Asian cuisine

Examples

Fresh water chestnut adds a crunchy texture to stir-fry dishes.
She bought a can of water chestnut from the Asian grocery store.
The salad was garnished with slices of water chestnut for extra crispness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.tə ˌtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
an aquatic plant of the genus Trapa with spiny, four-pronged edible nutlike fruits, commonly used as food
aquatic plant with edible nutlike fruits

Examples

The pond was full of water chestnut plants, their floating leaves covering the surface.
She prepared a salad with sliced water chestnut for a crunchy texture.
The spiny fruits of the water chestnut are harvested and eaten in many Asian cuisines.
