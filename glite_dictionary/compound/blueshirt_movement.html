Blueshirt movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌʃɜrt ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈbluːʃɜːt ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a right-wing political movement active in Ireland during the early 20th century, known for its distinctive uniforms and opposition to left-wing groups
right-wing Irish political movement (early 20th cent.)

Examples

The Blueshirt movement played a notable role in Irish politics during the 1930s.
Many members of the Blueshirt movement later joined Fine Gael, a leading Irish political party.
Historians debate the influence of the Blueshirt movement on Ireland's political development.

Synset Examples

The Blueshirt movement had a significant impact on history.

Etymology

From blue shirt (the movement's uniform) + movement, relating to the Irish political association.
