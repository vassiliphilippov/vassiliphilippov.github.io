grey whale

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ weɪl / (AmE), / ɡreɪ weɪl / (BrE)
a medium-sized whale with mottled grey skin, native to the northern Pacific Ocean
Pacific whale species with grey skin

Examples

Whale watchers were lucky to spot a grey whale migrating along the coast.
The grey whale feeds on small crustaceans found on the ocean floor.
In winter, many grey whales travel to warmer waters to breed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale - a very large marine mammal
  • Degree Higher: blue whale - the largest whale species
