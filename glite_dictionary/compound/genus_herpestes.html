genus Herpestes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs hɜrˈpɛstiz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs hɜːˈpɛstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals in the mongoose family, found primarily in Africa and southern Eurasia.
genus of mongooses

Examples

The genus Herpestes includes several species of mongooses native to Africa and Asia.
Scientists studied the behavior patterns within the genus Herpestes for conservation efforts.
The Indian grey mongoose is a member of the genus Herpestes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongoose - small carnivorous mammal, related to the civet

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Herpestes', from Greek 'herpestes' (creeping animal).
