mooring mast

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪŋ mæst / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪŋ mɑːst / (BrE)
a tall structure used to secure an airship when it is not flying
structure for mooring airships

Examples

The crew guided the airship toward the mooring mast for a safe landing.
A historic mooring mast still stands at the old airfield as a relic of the airship era.
Engineers inspected the mooring mast before the arrival of the massive dirigible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooring - the act of securing a vessel with cables or anchors
  • Synonym: mast - a tall vertical structure, often on a ship or as a tower
