common wheat

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑmən ˈwit / (AmE), / ˌkɒmən ˈwiːt / (BrE)
A widely cultivated species of wheat grown in temperate regions, valued for its commercially important grain used in bread and many other foods.
major species of wheat used for bread and foods

Examples

Farmers in the Midwest primarily grow common wheat for use in bread and pasta production.
The yield of common wheat has increased significantly due to advances in agricultural technology.
Most of the world’s flour is milled from common wheat rather than other wheat species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - A cereal plant grown for its grain, from which flour is made.
