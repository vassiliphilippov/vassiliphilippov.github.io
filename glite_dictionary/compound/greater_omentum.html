greater omentum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər oʊˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə əʊˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
a large fold of the peritoneum that hangs down from the stomach and covers the intestines
large peritoneal fold covering the intestines
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the doctor carefully lifted the greater omentum away to access the underlying organs.
The greater omentum serves as a protective layer over the intestines in the abdominal cavity.
In some patients, excess fat can be stored in the greater omentum, contributing to central obesity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lesser omentum - the smaller peritoneal fold between the stomach and liver

Etymology

From Latin 'omentum' meaning 'fatty membrane', with 'greater' distinguishing it from the 'lesser omentum'.
