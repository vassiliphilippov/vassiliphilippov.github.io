genus Sphagnum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsfæɡnəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsfæɡnəm / (BrE)
A large genus of mosses found in temperate bogs, notable for their water-holding leaves and comprising the only genus in the order Sphagnales.
genus of bog mosses (Sphagnum)

Examples

The genus Sphagnum plays a vital role in the formation of peat bogs across the northern hemisphere.
Scientists have identified dozens of species within the genus Sphagnum.
Because of their high water retention, mosses from the genus Sphagnum are often used in horticulture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sphagnum - mosses of the genus Sphagnum

Etymology

From Latin 'sphagnum', from Greek 'sphagnos' (type of moss). 'Genus' is Latin for 'kind' or 'sort'.
