adrenal medulla

1. NOUN

/ əˈdriːnəl məˈdʌlə / (AmE), / əˈdriːnəl mɪˈdʌlə / (BrE)
the inner region of the adrenal gland, which secretes hormones such as epinephrine (adrenaline) and norepinephrine
inner part of the adrenal gland
medical

Examples

The adrenal medulla releases adrenaline in response to stress.
Damage to the adrenal medulla can affect hormone levels in the body.
Researchers studied how the adrenal medulla influences blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adrenal cortex - the outer region of the adrenal gland that produces different hormones; anatomical counterpart

Etymology

From Latin 'ad' (to) + 'renal' (relating to kidney) and 'medulla' (marrow, core).
