High Holy Day

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈhoʊli deɪ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈhəʊli deɪ / (BrE)
Either of the two most important Jewish holy days, Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur, observed with special solemnity.
major Jewish holy day (Rosh Hashanah or Yom Kippur)

Examples

Many Jewish families gather at the synagogue during the High Holy Days.
Observance of each High Holy Day includes special prayers and traditions.
The rabbi gave a powerful sermon at the start of the High Holy Days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rosh Hashanah - the Jewish New Year festival, one of the High Holy Days
  • Synonym: Yom Kippur - the Day of Atonement, one of the High Holy Days
