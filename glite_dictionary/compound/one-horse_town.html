one-horse town

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌn hɔrs taʊn / (AmE), / ˈwʌn hɔːs taʊn / (BrE)
a small, unimportant town with few resources, attractions, or activities
small, unimpressive or unimportant town

Examples

After college, she couldn’t wait to leave her one-horse town behind for the big city.
The band struggled to attract a crowd in the one-horse town they visited on tour.
Despite growing up in a one-horse town, he always dreamed of traveling the world.

Synset Examples

Living in a one-horse town can feel isolating at times.
He grew up in a one-horse town with limited options.
Everyone knows everyone in that one-horse town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backwater - an isolated or little-known place; a place far from progress
  • Antonym: metropolis - a large, important city

Etymology

Derived from the idea of a town so small it needs only one horse; 19th-century American origin.
