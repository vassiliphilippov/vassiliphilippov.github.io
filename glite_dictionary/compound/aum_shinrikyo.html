Aum Shinrikyo

1. PROPN

/ aʊm ʃɪnˈriːkjoʊ / (AmE), / aʊm ʃɪnˈriːkjoʊ / (BrE)
a Japanese doomsday cult and terrorist organization founded in 1987, combining elements of Buddhism and Christianity, notorious for the 1995 Tokyo subway sarin gas attack
Japanese doomsday cult and terrorist group

Examples

The 1995 sarin gas attack in Tokyo was carried out by members of Aum Shinrikyo.
Authorities dismantled much of Aum Shinrikyo's operations after their leader was arrested.
Internationally, Aum Shinrikyo became infamous due to its acts of domestic terrorism in Japan.

Synset Examples

in 1995 Aum members released deadly sarin gas on a Tokyo subway train

Etymology

Japanese, literally 'Aum Supreme Truth'; 'Aum' from the Sanskrit sacred sound om, 'Shinrikyo' meaning 'teaching of the truth'.
