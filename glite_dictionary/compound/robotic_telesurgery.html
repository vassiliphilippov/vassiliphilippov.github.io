robotic telesurgery

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbɑːtɪk ˌtɛləsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɒtɪk ˌtɛlɪˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
Microsurgery performed by a surgeon who operates remotely by controlling robotic instruments.
remote surgery using robotic instruments
medical

Examples

Advancements in robotic telesurgery allow specialists to operate across continents with precision.
The hospital invested in new equipment to expand its robotic telesurgery capabilities.
Training programs for robotic telesurgery are becoming more common in leading medical schools.

Etymology

From 'robotic' (relating to robots) and 'telesurgery' (surgery performed remotely via telecommunications).
