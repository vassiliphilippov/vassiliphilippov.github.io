Davidson's penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪvɪdsənz ˈpɛnstmən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪdsənz ˈpɛnstmən / (BrE)
a mat-forming plant native to western North America, with blue and lavender flowers clustered on short upright stems
mat-forming plant with blue-lavender flowers

Examples

Wildflowers like Davidson's penstemon brighten the rocky slopes each spring.
The garden's collection includes rare species such as Davidson's penstemon.
Hikers spotted clusters of Davidson's penstemon along the trail in full bloom.

Etymology

Named after botanist Anstruther Davidson (1860–1932).
