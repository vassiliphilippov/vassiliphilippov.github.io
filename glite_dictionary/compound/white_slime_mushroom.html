white slime mushroom

1. NOUN

/ waɪt slaɪm ˈmʌʃrʊm / (AmE), / waɪt slaɪm ˈmʌʃrəm / (BrE)
a type of slime mushroom characterized by its white appearance, belonging to the group of slime molds found on decaying wood or plant matter.
a white-colored slime mushroom

Examples

During our hike, we spotted a rare white slime mushroom on a fallen log.
The white slime mushroom thrives in damp and decaying forest environments.
Naturalists often document the appearance of the white slime mushroom during the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slime mold - a broad group of simple, slime-producing organisms formerly classified as fungi
