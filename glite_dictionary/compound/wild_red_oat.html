wild red oat

1. NOUN

/ waɪld rɛd oʊt / (AmE), / waɪld rɛd əʊt / (BrE)
A Mediterranean species of oat believed to be the ancestor of modern cultivated oats.
wild Mediterranean ancestor of cultivated oats

Examples

The wild red oat is commonly found in Mediterranean regions.
Scientists consider the wild red oat to be the primary ancestor of domesticated oats.
Efforts to protect wild red oat populations are important for preserving genetic diversity in crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oat - a cereal plant cultivated for its seed, which is used as food
