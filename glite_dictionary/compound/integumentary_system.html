integumentary system

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈteɡ.jəˌmen.tɛr.i ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛɡ.jʊˈmɛn.təri ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the system consisting of the skin and its related structures, such as hair, nails, and glands, that covers and protects the body
system of skin and related structures
medical

Examples

The integumentary system protects the body from external damage and helps regulate temperature.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure and function of the integumentary system.
Disorders of the integumentary system can include eczema, psoriasis, and skin cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - the thin layer of tissue forming the natural outer covering of the body of a person or animal

Etymology

From Latin 'integumentum' (a covering) + 'systema' (system).
