enigma canon

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪɡmə ˈkænən / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪɡmə ˈkanən / (BrE)
A canon in which the entrances of successive parts were indicated by cryptic symbols and devices, popular in the 15th and 16th centuries.
musical canon with cryptic entrances

Examples

The composer wrote an enigma canon that challenged even the most skilled musicians to decipher its meaning.
Many Renaissance sheet music collections contain at least one enigma canon as a test of wit and skill.
His fascination with puzzles extended to music, where he crafted a remarkable enigma canon for his students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canon - a contrapuntal musical composition with imitative parts

Etymology

From Latin 'enigma' (riddle) and Greek 'kanōn' (rule, measuring stick), reflecting the puzzle-like nature of the musical form.
