carbo loading

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrboʊ ˌloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbəʊ ˌləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of consuming a diet high in starchy foods to increase the amount of stored carbohydrates in the muscles, especially before endurance sports events.
diet to increase muscle carbohydrate reserves

Examples

Many marathon runners rely on carbo loading the night before a race for extra energy.
The team nutritionist recommended carbo loading to maximize the players' endurance.
He researched the best foods for carbo loading before his triathlon.

Synset Examples

carbo loading is used by endurance athletes just before competing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbohydrate - a type of nutrient found in food

Etymology

Shortening of 'carbohydrate loading', from 'carbohydrate' and 'loading' (as in accumulating or filling up).
