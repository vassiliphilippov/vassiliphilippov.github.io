zoonotic disease

1. NOUN

/ zoʊ.əˈnɑ.tɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / zəʊ.əˈnɒt.ɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease that is naturally transmitted from animals to humans.
disease transmissible from animals to humans
medical

Examples

COVID-19 is believed to be a zoonotic disease that originated in bats.
Rabies is a well-known zoonotic disease transmitted through the bite of an infected animal.
Veterinarians take precautions to avoid contracting zoonotic diseases from their animal patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoonosis - a disease that can be transmitted from animals to humans

Etymology

From Greek zōion (animal) and nosos (disease), via 'zoonosis'.
