American licorice

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈmerɪkən ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (BrE)
A North American plant resembling true licorice, with a root possessing similar sweet-flavored properties.
North American plant similar to true licorice

Examples

The root of American licorice is sometimes used as a natural sweetener in herbal teas.
Many native peoples traditionally chewed American licorice for its sweet flavor and medicinal properties.
American licorice thrives in prairies and open woodlands throughout North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - an herbaceous plant with sweet-flavored root, used in confectionery and medicine
