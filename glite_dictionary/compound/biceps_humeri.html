biceps humeri

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪsɛps hjuˈmɪraɪ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪsɛps hjuːˈmɪəriː / (BrE)
A muscle of the upper arm that flexes the elbow and turns the forearm so the palm faces up.
muscle of the upper arm
medical

Examples

The biceps humeri is often exercised through curls to build arm strength.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully examined the patient's biceps humeri for damage.
Strong biceps humeri are important for athletes who rely on arm movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biceps - Muscle at the front of the upper arm

Etymology

From Latin 'biceps' (two-headed) and 'humeri', genitive of 'humerus' (upper arm bone).
