toss-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːs.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtɒs.ʌp / (BrE)
A situation in which the result is uncertain or either of two outcomes seems equally likely.
an uncertain or evenly balanced situation

Examples

It's a toss-up who will win the Best Actor award this year.
The weather is so unpredictable that planning a picnic is always a toss-up.
Whether we get the contract or not is really a toss-up at this point.

Synset Examples

It's a toss-up between the two candidates.
Whether to go out or stay in is a toss-up.
The game was a toss-up until the final minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fifty-fifty - used to mean that the odds for two possible outcomes are equal
  • Antonym: certainty - a situation where the result is definite and known

Etymology

From the literal act of tossing a coin upward to make a decision, dating from the mid-19th century.
