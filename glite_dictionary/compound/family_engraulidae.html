family Engraulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɛnˈɡroʊɪˌlaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɛŋˈɡrɔɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, silver-colored fish commonly known as anchovies, found in marine and coastal waters worldwide.
the anchovy family of fish

Examples

The family Engraulidae includes many commercially important species of anchovies.
Biologists study the migration patterns of fish belonging to the family Engraulidae.
Members of the family Engraulidae are a key part of marine food webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy - a small, oily fish of the family Engraulidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Engraulidae', based on 'Engraulis', the type genus, which comes from Greek 'engraulis' (anchovy).
