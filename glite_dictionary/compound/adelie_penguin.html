Adelie penguin

1. NOUN

/ əˈdiːli ˈpɛŋɡwɪn / (AmE), / əˈdiːli ˈpɛŋɡwɪn / (BrE)
a medium-sized penguin species native to Antarctica, especially found in large colonies along the Adelie Coast
a species of Antarctic penguin

Examples

During their expedition, the researchers observed thousands of Adelie penguins nesting on the ice.
The population of Adelie penguins has remained stable despite harsh Antarctic conditions.
An Adelie penguin can dive deep into the icy waters in search of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - a flightless seabird of the family Spheniscidae found in the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

Named after the Adélie Coast of Antarctica, itself named by the explorer Jules Dumont d'Urville after his wife, Adèle.
