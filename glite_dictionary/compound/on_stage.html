on stage

1. ADV

/ ɑn steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɒn steɪdʒ / (BrE)
appearing or performing in front of an audience as part of a theatrical or musical production
performing in front of an audience

Examples

She felt nervous the first time she was on stage in front of a crowd.
The musicians were on stage for over two hours last night.
He prefers working behind the scenes rather than being on stage.

Synset Examples

He admires the grace of ballet dancers on stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offstage - not on the stage, away from the performance area
