genus Cacalia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈkeɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈkɑː.li.ə / (BrE)
A group of tall, smooth herbs native to the forested mountains of Europe and Asia Minor, formerly including some plants now placed in genus Emilia.
genus of tall herbs (Europe, Asia Minor)

Examples

Several species within the genus Cacalia are found in mountainous areas of southern Europe.
Botanists recently debated the classification of plants formerly assigned to the genus Cacalia.
Some gardeners cultivate members of the genus Cacalia for their ornamental foliage.

Etymology

From Latin 'Cacalia,' the original genus name assigned by Linnaeus, possibly from Greek.
