commonplace book

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmənˌpleɪs bʊk / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənˌpleɪs bʊk / (BrE)
a notebook used to record memorable quotations, ideas, or information for personal reference
notebook for quotations or ideas

Examples

Many writers keep a commonplace book to collect inspiring quotes and thoughts.
Her grandfather's commonplace book is filled with poems and philosophical reflections.
During the Renaissance, scholars often compiled a commonplace book as a valuable intellectual resource.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notebook - a book of blank pages for writing notes in

Etymology

From 'commonplace' (something commonly found) and 'book'; practice dates to Renaissance England.
