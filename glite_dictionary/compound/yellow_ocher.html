yellow ocher

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈoʊkɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a natural earth pigment containing limonite, clay, and silica, used especially for its yellow color in painting and decorative work
natural yellow earth pigment

Examples

The artist mixed yellow ocher with oil to create a warm base for the landscape.
Natural yellow ocher can vary in shade depending on its source and composition.
Many ancient murals were painted using pigments such as yellow ocher and red ocher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow ochre - alternative spelling of yellow ocher
  • Degree Higher: red ocher - earth pigment with a reddish color, contains iron oxide

Etymology

From ocher, via Old French 'ocre', from Latin 'ochra', from Greek 'ōkhra' (pale yellow).
