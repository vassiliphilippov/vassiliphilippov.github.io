family Drosophilidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli droʊˌsɪfəˈlaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli drəʊˌsɪfɪˈlaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small flies commonly known as fruit flies, including species widely used in genetic research.
family of fruit flies

Examples

The family Drosophilidae includes many species that are important in genetic research.
Members of the family Drosophilidae are commonly known as fruit flies.
Biologists often study the family Drosophilidae to understand evolution and genetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit fly - common name for small flies in the Drosophilidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Drosophilidae, based on the type genus Drosophila.
