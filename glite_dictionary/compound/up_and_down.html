up and down

1. ADV

/ ʌp ənd daʊn / (AmE), / ʌp ənd daʊn / (BrE)
alternately upward and downward; in both upward and downward directions
alternately upward and downward

Examples

She glanced up and down the street before crossing.
The elevator went up and down all day.
He looked up and down the ladder to check for any damage.

Synset Examples

he eyed him up and down

2. ADV

/ ʌp ənd daʊn / (AmE), / ʌp ənd daʊn / (BrE)
back and forth or along the length of a path, area, or object, often repeatedly or over its full extent
along the length of something

Examples

The teacher paced up and down the hallway, waiting for news.
She traveled up and down the coast searching for the perfect spot.
The dog ran up and down the stairs all afternoon.

Synset Examples
