convertible bond

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝː.tə.bəl bɑnd / (AmE), / kənˈvɜː.tɪ.bəl bɒnd / (BrE)
a bond that can be exchanged for a predetermined number of shares or other securities under specified conditions
a bond convertible into other securities

Examples

The company issued a convertible bond that could later be exchanged for common stock.
Investors are attracted to a convertible bond because it offers both fixed income and potential equity upside.
She considered purchasing a convertible bond as part of her diversified portfolio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a fixed income investment representing a loan made by an investor to a borrower
