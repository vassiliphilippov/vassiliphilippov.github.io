caiman lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.mən ˌlɪz.ərd / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.mən ˌlɪz.əd / (BrE)
A large, crocodile-like lizard from South America with powerful jaws, known for crushing snails and mussels.
large South American lizard with strong jaws

Examples

The caiman lizard spends much of its time in or near water hunting for prey.
At the zoo, we watched a caiman lizard crush a snail with its powerful jaws.
Caiman lizards are easily recognized by their bright green scales and reddish heads.

Etymology

From "caiman" (type of crocodilian, via Spanish from Carib) + "lizard," referring to its crocodilian appearance.
