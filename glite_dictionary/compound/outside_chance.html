outside chance

1. NOUN

/ ˌaʊtsaɪd ˈtʃæns / (AmE), / ˌaʊtsaɪd ˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
a very small or unlikely possibility of something happening
a slim or unlikely possibility

Examples

He knew there was only an outside chance of passing the final exam without more studying.
The underdog team has an outside chance of making it to the playoffs this year.
There’s an outside chance the weather will clear up before the picnic starts.

Synset Examples

He gave her an outside chance to succeed in the competition.
We have an outside chance of getting tickets to the concert.
There’s only an outside chance of winning the lottery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long shot - an attempt or possibility with little chance of success
  • Antonym: likelihood - the state of being probable; probability
