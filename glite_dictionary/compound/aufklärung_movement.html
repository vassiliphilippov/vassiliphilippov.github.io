Aufklärung movement

1. NOUN

/ aʊfˈklɛrʊŋ ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / aʊfˈklɛərʊŋ ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
An intellectual movement in 18th-century Europe, particularly in Germany, emphasizing reason, individual rights, and critical thinking.
18th-century rationalist intellectual movement

Examples

The Aufklärung movement spread ideas of rationality and criticism throughout German society.
Many prominent philosophers contributed to the development of the Aufklärung movement.
Universities played a crucial role in the Aufklärung movement by fostering free inquiry and debate.

Synset Examples

The Aufklärung movement emphasized reason and individualism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Enlightenment - 18th-century European intellectual movement emphasizing reason and individual rights

Etymology

From German Aufklärung (enlightenment, clarification).
