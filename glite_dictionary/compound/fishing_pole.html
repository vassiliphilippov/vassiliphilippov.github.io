fishing pole

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ poʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ pəʊl / (BrE)
A long, slender rod, typically made of wood, metal, or fiberglass, used to cast and control a fishing line while angling.
rod used for fishing line

Examples

He spent the afternoon by the lake, holding his fishing pole and waiting patiently for a bite.
She bought a new fishing pole as a birthday present for her father.
After repairing his old fishing pole, Tom caught three trout in the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fishing rod - another term for a pole used in fishing to hold the line
