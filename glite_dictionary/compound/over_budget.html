over budget

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvər ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
costing more than the amount officially planned or allocated for a project or activity
exceeding planned financial cost

Examples

The construction was over budget by $2 million at the end of the year.
If the event goes over budget, we may need to reduce expenses elsewhere.
Their film project ran over budget, delaying the release date.

Synset Examples

The project is behind schedule, and another thing, it's over budget.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: under budget - costing less than the planned financial amount
