Lesser Sunda Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˌlɛsər ˈsʌndə ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˌlɛsə ˈsʌndə ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a chain of islands in southeastern Indonesia, east of Java, including Bali, Lombok, Sumbawa, Flores, and Timor
island chain in southeastern Indonesia

Examples

Many tourists visit Bali, which is part of the Lesser Sunda Islands, for its beautiful beaches.
The Lesser Sunda Islands include both volcanic and non-volcanic islands in southeastern Indonesia.
Timor, one of the Lesser Sunda Islands, is shared between Indonesia and Timor-Leste.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Greater Sunda Islands - larger group of islands in Indonesia, west of the Lesser Sunda Islands

Etymology

From 'Sunda', after the larger nearby Sunda Islands, with 'lesser' meaning 'smaller' or 'secondary'.
