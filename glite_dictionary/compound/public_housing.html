public housing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
Housing provided and managed by the government, typically for people with low incomes.
government-provided housing for low-income people

Examples

The city council has invested heavily in public housing over the past decade.
Many families rely on public housing to afford safe and stable homes.
The shortage of public housing has led to long waiting lists in major cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidized housing - housing where the government pays part of the rent or costs
