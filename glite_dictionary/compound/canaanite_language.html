Canaanite language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.nə.naɪt ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.nə.naɪt ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language spoken by the people who lived in Canaan before the Israelite conquest
extinct Semitic language of ancient Canaan

Examples

Scholars study the Canaanite language to better understand the history of the Levant region.
The inscriptions found on ancient tablets were written in the Canaanite language.
Phoenician is a well-known variety of the Canaanite language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrew language - ancient and modern language of the Hebrews

Etymology

Canaanite, from Canaan (ancient region of the Levant), and language; ultimately from Hebrew Kəna‘an.
