family Cyprinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪˈprɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˈprɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater fish that includes carps, tenches, roaches, rudds, and daces.
family of freshwater fish (carps, tenches, etc.)

Examples

The family Cyprinidae contains more than 3,000 species of freshwater fish worldwide.
Common carp and goldfish both belong to the family Cyprinidae.
Scientists study the family Cyprinidae to understand the evolution of diverse freshwater fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish, member of family Cyprinidae
  • Synonym: tench - a freshwater fish, member of family Cyprinidae

Etymology

From Latin Cyprinus (a kind of carp) + -idae (family suffix in zoological classification).
