total rockstar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.t̬əl ˈrɑkˌstɑr / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təl ˈrɒkˌstɑː / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who performs with great skill and impresses others, often used outside of music to praise excellence
an outstanding and impressive performer
informal american

Examples

Everyone agreed that she was a total rockstar after leading the project to success.
You handled those unexpected questions like a total rockstar during the meeting.
After running the marathon, he felt like a total rockstar.

Synset Examples

That dudette is a total rockstar on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star performer - a person who performs exceptionally well
  • Antonym: underachiever - someone who does not perform to their potential

Etymology

From the word 'rockstar', originally referring to a famous rock musician, used figuratively in American English since the late 20th century.
