common amsinckia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən æmˈsɪŋkiə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən æmˈsɪŋkiə / (BrE)
an annual plant of the western United States, known for its coiled spikes of yellow-orange flowers
yellow-flowered annual plant of western US

Examples

The hills were covered in a brilliant display of common amsinckia during the spring blooming season.
Hikers often admire the coiled orange flowers of common amsinckia along trails in California.
After the fire, common amsinckia quickly reestablished itself on the open plains.

Etymology

From genus name Amsinckia, named after German patron Wilhelm Amsinck.
