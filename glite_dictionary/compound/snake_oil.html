snake oil

1. NOUN

/ sneɪk ɔɪl / (AmE), / sneɪk ɔɪl / (BrE)
A substance sold as a medicine with false claims about its effectiveness, especially one that is fraudulent or worthless.
fraudulent or useless medicine

Examples

Many people were fooled into buying snake oil from dubious salesmen in the past.
The new supplement turned out to be just another form of snake oil with no proven benefits.
He warned that miracle cures like this are almost always just snake oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quack medicine - fraudulent or unproven medical remedy

Etymology

Originally referred to oil made from snakes, sold in the 19th-century US as a cure-all; now denotes fraudulent remedies.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪk ˌɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk ˌɔɪl / (BrE)
information or claims intended to deceive or mislead, especially in order to sell something or promote a false idea
deceptive or fraudulent claims

Examples

Many health websites are full of snake oil that promises miraculous results but delivers nothing.
He gave the investors a presentation full of snake oil and empty promises.
Beware of marketing that sounds too good to be true; it's probably just snake oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - deception deliberately practiced to secure unfair or unlawful gain
  • Antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact or reality

Etymology

Originally referred to fraudulent medicinal products sold by traveling salesmen in 19th-century America.
