potassium sodium tartrate

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtæsiəm ˈsoʊdiəm ˈtɑːrˌtreɪt / (AmE), / pəˈtasiəm ˈsəʊdiəm ˈtɑːtreɪt / (BrE)
A double salt of potassium and sodium with tartaric acid, used especially as a mild laxative and in certain medical powders.
a double salt used as a mild laxative
medical

Examples

Potassium sodium tartrate is commonly used in analytical chemistry for preparing certain reagents.
The doctor prescribed potassium sodium tartrate as a gentle laxative for occasional constipation.
Seidlitz powders often contain potassium sodium tartrate as their main active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rochelle salt - another name for potassium sodium tartrate

Etymology

From the chemical names of its constituent elements potassium, sodium, and tartrate (derived from tartaric acid).
