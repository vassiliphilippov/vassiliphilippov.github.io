laser-assisted in situ keratomileusis

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzər əˈsɪstɪd ɪn ˈsaɪtuː ˌkɛrətoʊˈmɪljuːsɪs / (AmE), / ˈleɪzə əˈsɪstɪd ɪn ˈsaɪtjuː ˌkɛrətəʊˈmɪljuːsɪs / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to correct vision by reshaping the cornea with a laser, involving the creation of a thin flap in the corneal tissue
surgery for vision correction using a laser
medical

Examples

Many people choose laser-assisted in situ keratomileusis to reduce their dependence on glasses or contact lenses.
The clinic specializes in laser-assisted in situ keratomileusis for patients with nearsightedness and astigmatism.
After undergoing laser-assisted in situ keratomileusis, Maria experienced improved vision within a day.

Etymology

From 'laser', 'assisted', Latin 'in situ' (in its original place), and Greek 'kerato-' (horn, cornea) plus '-mileusis' (carving).
