ring armor

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / rɪŋ ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
(historical) Flexible armor made of interlinked metal rings, used especially in the Middle Ages for protection in battle.
flexible armor of interlinked rings

Examples

The knight wore ring armor beneath his tunic for added protection.
Archaeologists discovered fragments of ring armor at the medieval burial site.
During the reenactment, he displayed a suit of ring armor to the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain mail - armor made of small metal rings linked together
