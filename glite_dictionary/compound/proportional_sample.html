proportional sample

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpɔrʃənl ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənl ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
A sample taken from a population by dividing it into subgroups (strata) and selecting a random sample from each subgroup in proportion to its size in the overall population.
statistical sampling from proportional strata

Examples

Each group in the study was selected using a proportional sample based on its representation in the overall population.
To ensure fairness, the researchers drew a proportional sample from each age category.
A proportional sample helps reflect the characteristics of the whole population more accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratified sample - a sample in which the population is divided into strata and samples are drawn from each
