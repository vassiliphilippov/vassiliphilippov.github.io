black pepper

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A spice made from whole dried peppercorns with their outer husks, commonly used to season food.
spice made from whole peppercorns

Examples

She added a pinch of black pepper to the soup for extra flavor.
Freshly ground black pepper enhances the taste of any dish.
I prefer using black pepper over white pepper in my recipes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white pepper - spice made from ripe peppercorns with the husk removed
  • Synonym: peppercorn - the dried berry from which black pepper is made

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a climbing vine native to southern India and Sri Lanka, cultivated for its dark red berries (peppercorns) used as a spice
climbing plant producing peppercorns

Examples

Black pepper is cultivated in tropical regions for its aromatic berries.
Farmers in Kerala depend on black pepper as a major cash crop.
The black pepper plant climbs up trees or trellises by means of its aerial roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Piper nigrum - the scientific name of the black pepper plant

Etymology

black (color) + pepper, from Old English 'pipor', from Latin 'piper', from Sanskrit 'pippali'.
