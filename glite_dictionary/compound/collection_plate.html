collection plate

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛkʃən pleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlɛkʃən pleɪt / (BrE)
a shallow dish or tray passed around in a church to collect money offerings from the congregation
dish for church offerings

Examples

The usher moved down the aisle, passing the collection plate to each row.
Coins and bills clinked gently as people contributed to the collection plate.
After the service, the collection plate was brought to the front altar.
