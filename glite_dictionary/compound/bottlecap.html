bottlecap

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtəlˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəlˌkæp / (BrE)
a cap used to seal the opening of a bottle, typically made of metal or plastic
cap that seals a bottle

Examples

He twisted the bottlecap off the soda and took a drink.
The floor was littered with old bottlecaps after the party.
She collects rare bottlecap designs from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cap - a protective cover or top for a container

Etymology

From 'bottle' and 'cap', formed by compounding.
