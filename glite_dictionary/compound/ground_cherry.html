ground cherry

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Physalis, bearing small edible berries enclosed in a papery, bladder-like husk
edible berry plant of genus Physalis

Examples

The ground cherry thrives in warm, sunny gardens and produces sweet, golden fruit.
She added some fresh ground cherries to the salad for a tangy flavor.
Birds often eat the ripe ground cherry fruits before gardeners can harvest them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Physalis - genus of plants known as ground cherries
