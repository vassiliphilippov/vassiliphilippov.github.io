batting average

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætɪŋ ˌævərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbætɪŋ ˌævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a measure of a batter's performance in baseball, calculated as the number of base hits divided by the number of official at-bats
baseball hitting performance statistic

Examples

Ted Williams finished the season with a remarkably high batting average.
A player's batting average often determines how valuable they are to the team.
His batting average dropped after the injury, but he worked hard to recover.

Synset Examples

Ted Williams once had a batting average above .400

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾɪŋ ˌævɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ɪŋ ˌæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
The percentage or proportion of times that an effort, attempt, or activity is successful, by analogy to the baseball statistic.
rate of success in repeated attempts

Examples

The salesperson's batting average improved after they changed their approach.
Our company's batting average for winning proposals has doubled this quarter.
When applying for grants, your batting average is just as important as the total number of applications.

Synset Examples

the salesman's batting average was 7 out of 12

Related Terms

  • Synonym: success rate - the proportion of attempts that are successful

Etymology

Originally from the baseball statistic for hits per at-bat, extended metaphorically to other fields.
