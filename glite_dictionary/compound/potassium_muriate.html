potassium muriate

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈtæsiəm ˈmjʊrieɪt / (AmE), / pəˈtasiəm ˈmjʊərieɪt / (BrE)
a compound of potassium chloride (KCl), often used as a source of potassium in medicine and as a fertilizer; also known as potassium chloride
potassium chloride compound; medicinal/fertilizer use

Examples

The doctor prescribed potassium muriate tablets to correct her electrolyte imbalance.
Farmers often add potassium muriate to the soil to promote healthy plant growth.
He researched the side effects of taking potassium muriate for his potassium deficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium chloride - a chemical compound with the formula KCl, commonly called potassium chloride

Etymology

From 'potassium' (element) and 'muriate', an old term for chloride from Latin 'muria' (brine).
