old warhorse

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈwɔrˌhɔrs / (AmE), / əʊld ˈwɔːˌhɔːs / (BrE)
an experienced and reliable person, especially one who has been involved in something for a long time
experienced and dependable person

Examples

Everyone relied on the old warhorse to manage the project during the crisis.
Though considered an old warhorse, she continued to innovate in her field.
The committee valued the opinion of their old warhorse who had seen it all before.

Synset Examples

The old warhorse still performed well in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person with a lot of experience in a particular field
  • Antonym: rookie - a person who is new to an activity or job

Etymology

Originally a term for a soldier's veteran horse, later used figuratively for people with enduring experience.
