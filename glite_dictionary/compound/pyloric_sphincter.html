pyloric sphincter

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈlɔrɪk ˈsfɪŋktɚ / (AmE), / paɪˈlɒrɪk ˈsfɪŋktə / (BrE)
the ring of muscle at the lower end of the stomach that controls the passage of food into the duodenum
muscle between stomach and duodenum
medical

Examples

The pyloric sphincter regulates the release of partially digested food from the stomach into the small intestine.
A malfunctioning pyloric sphincter can lead to digestive disorders such as pyloric stenosis.
During the operation, the surgeon examined the patient's pyloric sphincter to ensure it was functioning properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphincter - a ring-like muscle that contracts to close an opening
  • Synonym: pylorus - the opening from the stomach into the duodenum

Etymology

From Greek 'pylōrikos' (of the gatekeeper) and Latin 'sphincter' (binder, tightener).
