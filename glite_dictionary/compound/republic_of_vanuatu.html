Republic of Vanuatu

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv vəˈnuːɑːtuː / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv vəˈnɒatuː / (BrE)
A volcanic island country in the South Pacific Ocean, part of Melanesia; independent since 1980.
island country in the South Pacific

Examples

The Republic of Vanuatu gained its independence from joint British and French colonial rule in 1980.
Tourists are drawn to the natural beauty and diverse cultures of the Republic of Vanuatu.
Diplomatic relations between Australia and the Republic of Vanuatu have strengthened in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vanuatu - island country in the South Pacific Ocean

Etymology

Vanuatu from local languages, meaning 'Our Land Forever'; independent from joint British-French rule in 1980.
