noncallable bond

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈkɔl.ə.bəl bɑnd / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɔːl.ə.bəl bɒnd / (BrE)
a bond that cannot be redeemed by the issuer before a specified date, usually maturity
bond that cannot be redeemed before maturity

Examples

The company issued a noncallable bond to assure investors that their funds would be locked in until maturity.
Investors often prefer a noncallable bond because it protects them from early redemption risk.
Unlike a callable bond, a noncallable bond guarantees interest payments for the full term.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: callable bond - a bond that can be redeemed by the issuer before maturity
