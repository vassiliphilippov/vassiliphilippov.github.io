genus Amygdalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.nəs æmˈɪɡ.dəl.əs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.nəs amˈɪɡ.dal.əs / (BrE)
a former botanical genus name for peach and almond trees, now classified under the genus Prunus
former genus for peach and almond trees
medical

Examples

Peach trees were once classified under genus Amygdalus in older botanical texts.
Many botanists used genus Amygdalus before its species were reassigned to Prunus.
The genus Amygdalus included both almonds and peaches according to former plant classifications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: genus Prunus - current genus including peach and almond trees

Etymology

From New Latin 'Amygdalus', from Latin 'amygdalus' (almond tree), from Greek 'amygdalē'.
