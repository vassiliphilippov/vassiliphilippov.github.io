time and a half

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ənd ə hæf / (AmE), / taɪm ənd ə hɑːf / (BrE)
A rate of pay that is one and a half times the regular rate, typically paid for overtime work.
pay rate at 1.5 times normal, for overtime

Examples

Most employees receive time and a half for hours worked beyond forty per week.
The company policy is to pay time and a half for shifts on public holidays.
He was eager to work overtime because he needed the extra money from time and a half.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: double time - a rate of pay that is twice the regular rate, for overtime or holiday work
  • Synonym: overtime - work done beyond regular working hours
