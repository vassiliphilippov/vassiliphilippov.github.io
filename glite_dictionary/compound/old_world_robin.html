Old World robin

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊld ˈwɜrld ˈrɑbɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈwɜːld ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A small Eurasian songbird (Erithacus rubecula) with a reddish breast, commonly found in gardens and woodlands.
Eurasian songbird with red breast

Examples

The Old World robin is often seen hopping around gardens in search of worms.
Her favorite bird is the Old World robin because of its cheerful song and bright breast.
Unlike its American namesake, the Old World robin is much smaller and found throughout Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European robin - Common Eurasian garden bird with orange-red breast
  • Antonym: American robin - A migratory thrush with a red breast, native to North America
