public figure

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌblɪk ˈfɪɡjər / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪk ˈfɪɡə / (BrE)
A person who is well-known or recognized by the public, especially in fields such as politics, entertainment, or business.
well-known or notable person

Examples

As a public figure, she is often photographed at major events.
The judge ruled that the public figure had to prove actual malice in the lawsuit.
Many public figures use social media to connect with their fans.

Synset Examples

they studied all the great names in the history of France
she is an important figure in modern music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment
  • Antonym: private citizen - an ordinary person without public notoriety

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
a person, such as a celebrity or politician, who is widely known by the public and often discussed in the media
a widely known or influential person

Examples

The media tends to demonize certain public figures unfairly.
As a public figure, she is constantly photographed at social events.
It's important for a public figure to act responsibly in the public eye.
