Invincible Armada

1. PROPN

/ ɪnˈvɪnsəbl ɑːrˈmɑːdə / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪnsɪbl ɑːˈmɑːdə / (BrE)
the large fleet of warships sent by Spain against England by order of Philip II in 1588, famously defeated by the English navy
Spanish fleet sent against England in 1588

Examples

The defeat of the Invincible Armada marked a turning point in European history.
Historians often discuss how weather contributed to the destruction of the Invincible Armada in 1588.
England celebrated its naval victory over the Invincible Armada for centuries afterward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish Armada - the Spanish fleet sent against England in 1588

Etymology

From Spanish 'Armada Invencible', meaning 'invincible fleet'.
