Severn River

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛvərn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsɛvən ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in Ontario, Canada, that flows northeast into Hudson Bay.
river in Ontario, Canada, flowing into Hudson Bay

Examples

The Severn River is one of the largest rivers draining into Hudson Bay.
Many Indigenous communities live along the Severn River in northern Ontario.
A canoe expedition down the Severn River offers a glimpse of Canada's wilderness.

Etymology

Named after the River Severn in the UK; Severn may come from Latin 'Sabrina'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɛvərn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsɛvən ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in England and Wales that flows into the Bristol Channel; the longest river in Great Britain
the longest river in Great Britain

Examples

The Severn River is famous for its tidal bore, which attracts many visitors each year.
Flooding along the Severn River caused widespread disruption in nearby towns.
He took a boat trip along the picturesque Severn River during his holiday.

Etymology

From the Welsh 'Hafren' and Latinized as 'Sabrina' in Roman times.
