pull chain

1. NOUN

/ pʊl tʃeɪn / (AmE), / pʊl tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a chain with a handle at the end, used to operate a mechanism when pulled, such as flushing a toilet or turning on a light
chain used to operate a device by pulling

Examples

He reached up to pull the pull chain and turned on the overhead light.
The old-fashioned toilet still had a pull chain to flush it.
She decorated the pull chain with a colorful handle to make it easier to find in the dark.
