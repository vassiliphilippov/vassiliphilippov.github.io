lobed leaf

1. NOUN

/ loʊbd liːf / (AmE), / ləʊbd liːf / (BrE)
a leaf with deeply indented margins, forming distinct rounded or pointed projections
leaf with deeply indented edges

Examples

The oak tree is easily recognized by its lobed leaf shape.
Maples also display a classic lobed leaf with several pointed projections.
Gardeners often select plants with a lobed leaf for visual interest in landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: entire leaf - a leaf with smooth, unbroken margins
  • Antonym: serrated leaf - a leaf with saw-like notched margins
