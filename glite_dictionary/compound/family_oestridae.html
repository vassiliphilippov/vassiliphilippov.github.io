family Oestridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli oʊˈstrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli əʊˈstrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large flies, commonly known as warble flies, whose larvae are internal parasites of mammals.
family of parasitic flies (warble flies)
medical

Examples

The family Oestridae includes several species whose larvae live under the skin of mammals.
Veterinarians often encounter infestations caused by members of the family Oestridae in livestock.
Research on the family Oestridae has helped improve treatments for parasitic fly infections in animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warble fly - a large fly whose larvae form swellings (warbles) on the skin of mammals

Etymology

From New Latin *Oestridae*, the family name based on the genus *Oestrus*, itself from Greek *oistros* (gadfly, frenzy).
