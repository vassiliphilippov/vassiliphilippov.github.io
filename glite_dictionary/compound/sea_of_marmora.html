Sea of Marmora

1. PROPN

/ ˈsi əv mɑrˌmɔrə / (AmE), / ˈsiː əv ˈmɑːmərə / (BrE)
an inland sea in northwestern Turkey, connecting the Black Sea to the Aegean Sea via the Bosporus and the Dardanelles
inland sea in northwestern Turkey

Examples

Many ships pass through the Sea of Marmora each year, linking Europe and Asia.
The Sea of Marmora is renowned for its beautiful coastline and historical significance.
Istanbul lies on the northern shore of the Sea of Marmora, offering stunning waterfront views.

Etymology

From Italian 'Marmara', from Greek 'Μάρμαρα' (Marmara), meaning 'marble', after the island of Marmara noted for its marble.
