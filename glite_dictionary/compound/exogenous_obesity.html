exogenous obesity

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˈzoʊdʒənəs oʊˈbisɪti / (AmE), / ɛɡˈzɒdʒɪnəs əʊˈbiːsɪti / (BrE)
Obesity that is caused primarily by excessive intake of food rather than by genetic or internal factors.
obesity caused by overeating
medical

Examples

A diet high in processed foods can lead to exogenous obesity in both adults and children.
The doctor explained that exogenous obesity differs from obesity caused by metabolic disorders.
Lifestyle changes are essential for preventing exogenous obesity in at-risk populations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endogenous obesity - obesity due to internal factors such as hormonal imbalance

Etymology

From exogenous, from Greek exō ('outside') + -genous ('produced by'), and obesity from Latin obesitas ('fatness').
