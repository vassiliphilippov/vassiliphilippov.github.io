gunboat diplomacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌboʊt dɪˈploʊməsi / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnbəʊt dɪˈpləʊməsi / (BrE)
a foreign policy approach in which a country uses or threatens military force to achieve its objectives, especially in international relations.
use of military threats in diplomacy

Examples

During the late 19th century, European powers often practiced gunboat diplomacy to impose their will on weaker nations.
Critics accused the government of reverting to gunboat diplomacy rather than seeking peaceful solutions.
The threat of naval intervention was a classic example of gunboat diplomacy in action.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: diplomacy - the management of international relations by negotiation
  • Degree Higher: military intervention - the act of a nation using its armed forces in another country

Etymology

Derived from 'gunboat' (a small, armed ship) referring to 19th-century naval interventions, and 'diplomacy', from French 'diplomatie'.
