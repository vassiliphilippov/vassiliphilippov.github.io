finger cymbals

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ ˌsɪmbəlz / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ˌsɪmbəlz / (BrE)
A percussion instrument consisting of a pair of small metal disks worn on the fingers and struck together to produce a tinkling sound, often used in dances such as belly dancing or by Spanish dancers.
small cymbals worn on fingers and struck together

Examples

The dancer played the finger cymbals to match the rhythm of the music.
She bought a pair of finger cymbals for her belly dancing class.
The soft chime of finger cymbals added a sparkling accent to the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castanets - a similar percussion instrument made of wood, clicking together, commonly used by Spanish dancers
