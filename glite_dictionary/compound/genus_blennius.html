genus Blennius

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs blɛˈniː.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs blɛn.ɪ.əs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Blenniidae, comprising certain small marine fishes known as blennies.
type genus of Blenniidae (blennies)
medical

Examples

The genus Blennius includes several species of small marine fish found in coastal waters.
Researchers studied the unique behaviors of the genus Blennius to better understand blenny evolution.
The genus Blennius is characterized by their elongated bodies and comb-like teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blenny - a small, elongated marine fish of the family Blenniidae

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'blennius', meaning blenny.
