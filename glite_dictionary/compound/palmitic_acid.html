palmitic acid

1. NOUN

/ pælˈmɪtɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / pælˈmɪtɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A saturated fatty acid found in palm oil, meat, and dairy products, commonly used in food and industrial applications.
a saturated fatty acid in foods and oils
medical

Examples

Researchers found high levels of palmitic acid in processed foods such as pastries and snacks.
Diets rich in palmitic acid have been linked to increased cholesterol in some studies.
Palm oil contains a significant amount of palmitic acid, making it a common ingredient in many products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stearic acid - a saturated fatty acid found in animal fat and some oils
  • Antonym: oleic acid - a common monounsaturated fatty acid found in natural fats and oils

Etymology

From palm (referring to palm oil) + -itic; originally identified in palm oil.
