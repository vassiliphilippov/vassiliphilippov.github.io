friendly set-to

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛn(d)li sɛt ˈtu / (AmE), / ˈfrɛn(d)li sɛt ˈtəʊ / (BrE)
A mild and good-natured argument or disagreement, usually without hostility.
good-natured or mild argument

Examples

After dinner, the siblings had a friendly set-to about which movie to watch.
Their friendly set-to over football teams made everyone at the table laugh.
We had a friendly set-to at work, but it helped us understand each other better.

Synset Examples

They had a friendly set-to about the best approach.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: argument - a heated exchange of differing views

Etymology

From 'set-to' meaning a bout or dispute, with 'friendly' qualifying its nature.
