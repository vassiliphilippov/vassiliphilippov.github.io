gall midge

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔl mɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɔːl mɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small, delicate fly whose larvae induce abnormal plant growths known as galls
small fly whose larvae cause plant galls

Examples

The oak tree was infested by gall midges that left tiny bumps on its leaves.
Farmers often monitor crops to control gall midge populations and protect their plants.
A scientist collected a sample of the gall midge to study its impact on wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall - an abnormal growth on plants, often caused by insects or mites
