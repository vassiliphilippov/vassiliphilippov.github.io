black bearberry

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈbɛrˌbɛri / (AmE), / blak ˈbɛːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous, creeping shrub found in alpine and circumpolar regions, particularly noted for its bright red foliage in autumn and black or blue-black berries.
arctic shrub with black/blue-black berries

Examples

The black bearberry turns a striking red in the fall across tundra landscapes.
Many birds and mammals feed on the berries produced by the black bearberry.
Its creeping habit allows black bearberry to cover large areas in alpine regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearberry - a low-growing, evergreen shrub with red berries
