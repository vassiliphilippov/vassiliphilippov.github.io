animal black

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməl blæk / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl blæk / (BrE)
A black pigment made from carbonized animal bones, used especially in paints and inks.
black pigment from charred animal bones

Examples

The artist chose animal black as the base for his dark background.
Historically, printer's ink often contained animal black as a main ingredient.
Restorers found traces of animal black pigment in the old painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone black - black pigment made by charring animal bones
