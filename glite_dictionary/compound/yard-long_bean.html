yard-long bean

1. NOUN

/ jɑrd lɔŋ bin / (AmE), / jɑːd lɒŋ biːn / (BrE)
a type of bean with very long, succulent pods, often used as a vegetable in cooking
bean with long pods used as a vegetable

Examples

The stir-fry recipe calls for fresh yard-long beans cut into short sections.
She grew yard-long beans in her garden for their unique flavor and texture.
At the market, vendors sold bundles of crisp yard-long beans alongside other local vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asparagus bean - another name for the yard-long bean
  • Degree Lower: string bean - a green bean with slender edible pods; a similar type of vegetable
