bouncing Bess

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnsɪŋ bɛs / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsɪŋ bɛs / (BrE)
A European plant with pink or white flowers, whose leaves yield a detergent when bruised; also known as soapwort.
plant yielding detergent (soapwort)

Examples

The gardener pointed out a patch of bouncing Bess growing beside the old stone wall.
In the past, people used bouncing Bess to wash delicate fabrics because of its natural soap.
Pink blossoms of bouncing Bess brighten the meadows in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapwort - plant with saponin-rich leaves used for cleaning

Etymology

Refers to the plant soapwort, with 'bouncing Bess' as a traditional English nickname.
