confirmation hearing

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈhɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈhɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
a formal meeting held by the US Senate to consider whether to approve or reject a presidential nominee for a high federal office
Senate meeting to approve/reject a nominee
american legal

Examples

The Senate judiciary committee conducted a confirmation hearing for the Supreme Court nominee.
During her confirmation hearing, the candidate answered difficult questions about her record.
Many journalists covered every detail of the nominee’s confirmation hearing last week.
