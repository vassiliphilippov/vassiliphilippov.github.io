Vincent's angina

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnsənts ænˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈvɪnsənts ænˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease affecting the mouth and throat, characterized by ulceration of the mucous membranes
infectious mouth and throat ulceration
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed the patient with Vincent's angina after observing severe gum ulceration.
Untreated Vincent's angina can lead to serious complications in the mouth and throat.
Symptoms of Vincent's angina include pain, swelling, and bleeding of the affected tissues.

Etymology

Named after French physician Jean Hyacinthe Vincent (1862–1950), who described the disease.
