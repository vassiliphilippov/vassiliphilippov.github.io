Bachelor of Divinity

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr əv dɪˈvɪnəti / (AmE), / ˈbatʃələ əv dɪˈvɪnɪti / (BrE)
A first university degree in theology or religious studies, often awarded to those preparing for Christian ministry.
university degree in theology

Examples

After four years of study, she received her Bachelor of Divinity from the seminary.
Many ministers are required to hold a Bachelor of Divinity before ordination.
He chose to pursue a Bachelor of Divinity to deepen his understanding of Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bachelor of Arts - undergraduate degree in arts subjects
  • Degree Higher: Master of Divinity - graduate degree in theology or religious studies

Etymology

From 'bachelor' (university degree) and 'divinity' (theology, from Latin 'divinitas').
