feudal lordship

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjudl ˈlɔrdʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfjuːd(ə)l ˈlɔːdʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, authority, or domain of a feudal lord, especially during the medieval period
position or domain of a feudal lord

Examples

He inherited the feudal lordship after his father's death.
The decline of feudal lordship changed the structure of medieval society.
Many castles were centers of feudal lordship and administration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiefdom - land or domain held by a feudal lord
  • Degree Lower: vassalage - the state or condition of being a vassal under a lord
