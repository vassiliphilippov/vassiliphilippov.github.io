silver perch

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər pɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a small silvery drumfish (Bairdiella chrysoura) found along the coasts of the United States from New York to Mexico, often mistaken for the white perch.
a small silvery coastal drumfish

Examples

Anglers along the Atlantic coast frequently catch silver perch during the summer months.
The silver perch is often mistaken for the white perch because of its similar coloring.
Coastal waters from New York to Mexico are home to the silver perch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white perch - a North American fish often confused with the silver perch

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ pɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a small, silvery fish found in streams of the eastern United States, valued as both food and game fish
small silvery food and game fish of eastern US streams

Examples

Anglers often seek out the silver perch for its fighting spirit and delicate flavor.
The river is teeming with silver perch, making it a popular fishing spot.
She grilled a fresh silver perch she caught earlier that morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - a type of freshwater game fish
