tartan tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːrtən tæks / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtn̩ tæks / (BrE)
a tax or perceived extra tax, especially one argued to specifically affect Scotland or its people
tax perceived to affect Scotland
british

Examples

Many politicians debated whether the tartan tax was fair to Scottish residents.
Some believe that changes in income tax would introduce a hidden tartan tax in Scotland.
The phrase tartan tax often appears in discussions about Scottish fiscal policy.

Synset Examples

Scottish artisans protested the tartan tax imposed on them.
The tartan tax on imported fabric raised prices significantly.
The government’s tartan tax is a contentious issue.

Etymology

Refers to tartan, a pattern traditionally associated with Scotland, used metaphorically for Scottish interests.
