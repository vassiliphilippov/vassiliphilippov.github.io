anisette de Bordeaux

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪˈsɛt də bɔrˈdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈsɛt də bɔːˈdəʊ / (BrE)
A colorless, sweet, liquorice-flavored liqueur from Bordeaux made from aniseed.
aniseed-flavored liqueur from Bordeaux

Examples

After dinner, she offered her guests a glass of anisette de Bordeaux as a digestif.
The fragrance of anisette de Bordeaux filled the air as the bottle was opened.
He purchased a rare vintage of anisette de Bordeaux during his trip to France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anisette - sweet anise-flavored liqueur

Etymology

From French, literally 'anisette of Bordeaux'; 'anisette' from 'anise', a plant used for flavoring, and 'Bordeaux', a city in France known for its wines and spirits.
