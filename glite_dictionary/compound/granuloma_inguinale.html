granuloma inguinale

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrænjuˈloʊmə ˌɪŋɡwəˈnaɪli / (AmE), / ˌɡranjʊˈləʊmə ˌɪŋɡwɪˈneɪli / (BrE)
a sexually transmitted disease caused by bacteria of the genus Calymmatobacterium, characterized by progressive ulcers and a pimply rash in the genital and groin region
sexually transmitted disease causing genital ulcers
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with granuloma inguinale after presenting with painless ulcers in the groin region.
Public health campaigns aim to reduce the incidence of granuloma inguinale through education and early treatment.
Doctors tested for several sexually transmitted infections, including granuloma inguinale, when the symptoms appeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donovanosis - another name for granuloma inguinale, a sexually transmitted infection

Etymology

From Latin 'granuloma' (a small granular tumor) and 'inguinale' (of the groin).
