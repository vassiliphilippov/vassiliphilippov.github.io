Lyme disease

1. NOUN

/ laɪm dɪˌziz / (AmE), / laɪm dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A bacterial infection transmitted by tick bites, causing rash, fever, and joint swelling; if untreated, it can lead to serious complications.
bacterial infection from tick bites
medical

Examples

Early treatment of Lyme disease can prevent long-term complications.
She developed a bull's-eye rash, a classic symptom of Lyme disease.
Doctors recommend checking for ticks after hiking in areas where Lyme disease is common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick-borne disease - illnesses transmitted by ticks

Etymology

Named after Lyme, Connecticut, where the disease was first identified in the 1970s.
