pseudometric space

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˈmɛtrɪk speɪs / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈmɛtrɪk speɪs / (BrE)
A mathematical space in which a non-negative function, called a pseudometric, measures the distance between points, allowing the distance between distinct points to be zero.
mathematical space allowing zero distance between points

Examples

A pseudometric space allows two different points to have a distance of zero.
In functional analysis, examples of a pseudometric space commonly arise when studying seminorms.
Unlike a metric space, a pseudometric space does not require the distance function to separate all points.

Synset Examples

In the same way as every normed space is a metric space, every seminormed space is a pseudometric space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metric space - a set where a metric defines distance between all pairs of points

Etymology

Formed from pseudo- (false) and metric, reflecting that it generalizes metric spaces by relaxing the requirement that only identical points have zero distance.
