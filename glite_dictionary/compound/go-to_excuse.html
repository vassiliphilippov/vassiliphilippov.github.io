go-to excuse

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ tu ɪkˈskjus / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ tu ɪkˈskjuːs / (BrE)
an excuse that is habitually used or relied upon as the standard or preferred reason for something
preferred or habitual excuse

Examples

Whenever he is late to work, his go-to excuse is traffic on the highway.
Forgetting to set her alarm is her go-to excuse when she misses meetings.
Claiming his phone died has become his go-to excuse for not responding to messages.

Synset Examples

Using an idiot card is his go-to excuse for mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alibi - an explanation for not being somewhere or for one’s actions, often used to avoid blame

Etymology

From the use of 'go-to' meaning 'preferred' or 'relied upon' in American English.
