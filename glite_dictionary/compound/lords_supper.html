Lord's Supper

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrdz ˌsʌpər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdz ˌsʌpə / (BrE)
a Christian sacrament commemorating the Last Supper by consecrating bread and wine
Christian sacrament of bread and wine

Examples

Many churches celebrate the Lord's Supper as a central part of their worship service.
During the Lord's Supper, the congregation partakes of bread and wine in remembrance of Jesus.
The pastor explained the significance of the Lord's Supper to the new members of the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucharist - Christian sacrament commemorating the Last Supper
  • Synonym: Holy Communion - another term for the sacrament involving bread and wine

2. NOUN

/ lɔrdz ˈsʌpər / (AmE), / lɔːdz ˈsʌpə / (BrE)
The meal that Jesus shared with his disciples on the night before his crucifixion, commemorated as a central rite in Christian tradition.
the final meal of Jesus with his disciples

Examples

Many Christian denominations commemorate the Lord's Supper in their worship services.
The painting by Leonardo da Vinci famously depicts the Lord's Supper.
During Holy Week, Christians reflect on the significance of the Lord's Supper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Last Supper - the final meal that Jesus shared with his disciples

Etymology

From 'Lord' referring to Jesus Christ, and 'supper' meaning an evening meal; denotes the last meal Jesus had before his crucifixion.
