hanger-on

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæŋər ˈɑn / (AmE), / ˌhæŋə ˈɒn / (BrE)
a person who persistently follows or associates with others, often to gain advantage or favor, usually in an annoying or unwelcome way
person who persistently follows for advantage

Examples

There were always a few hangers-on at the actor's parties, hoping to meet someone famous.
The successful entrepreneur tried to avoid hangers-on who were only interested in his wealth.
She was annoyed by the constant presence of hangers-on around her new friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - someone who acts obsequiously toward someone important to gain advantage
  • Degree Lower: follower - a supporter or someone who follows another person
  • Degree Higher: parasite - a person who habitually relies on or exploits others

Etymology

Formed from 'hang' + 'on', literally meaning 'one who hangs on' to others.
