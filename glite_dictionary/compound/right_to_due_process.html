right to due process

1. NOUN

/ raɪt tə du ˈproʊ.ses / (AmE), / raɪt tə djuː ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
the legal right, guaranteed by the US Constitution, to fair treatment through the normal judicial system
constitutional right to fair legal procedure
legal american

Examples

Every citizen is entitled to the right to due process under the law.
The court found that his right to due process had been violated during the trial.
Lawyers argued that denying a hearing would infringe the client's right to due process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due process - fair treatment through the judicial system, as a citizen's right
