United Arab Republic

1. PROPN

/ juːˈnaɪt̬ɪd ˈɛrəb rəˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / juːˈnaɪtɪd ˈærəb rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
the official name of a sovereign state formed by a political union between Egypt and Syria from 1958 to 1961, and subsequently used by Egypt alone until 1971
former country: union of Egypt and Syria (1958–1971)

Examples

The United Arab Republic was established through the union of Egypt and Syria in 1958.
After Syria left the United Arab Republic in 1961, Egypt continued using the name until 1971.
Historical documents from the era often refer to Egypt as the United Arab Republic.
