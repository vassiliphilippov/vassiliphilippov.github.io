florist's gloxinia

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔːrɪsts ˌɡlɒkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪsts ˌɡlɒkˈsɪniə / (BrE)
A South American herb (Sinningia speciosa) cultivated as a houseplant for its large leaves and colorful, bell-shaped flowers.
houseplant with large, colored flowers

Examples

The florist's gloxinia brightened the windowsill with its vibrant, bell-shaped blooms.
She decided to grow a florist's gloxinia to add a splash of color to her living room.
When properly cared for, a florist's gloxinia can flower abundantly throughout the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloxinia - plant genus with large, bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Sinningia, and 'gloxinia' after botanist Benjamin Peter Gloxin.
