genus Ephedra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈfɛdrə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈfiːdrə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical evergreen shrubby or creeping plants, native to dry and inhospitable regions, and the sole genus of the family Ephedraceae.
genus of shrubby or creeping desert plants

Examples

The genus Ephedra includes plants that thrive in arid and desert environments.
Medicinal compounds are often extracted from members of the genus Ephedra.
Botanists have long studied the adaptations of the genus Ephedra to dry climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ephedra - A plant belonging to the genus Ephedra, used medicinally

Etymology

From Latin 'Ephedra', the Latinized form of Greek 'ephedra', an ancient name for the plant.
