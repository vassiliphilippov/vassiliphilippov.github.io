Jupiter Optimus Maximus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtɚ ˈɑptɪməs ˈmæksɪməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə ˈɒptɪməs ˈmæksɪməs / (BrE)
the chief epithet of the Roman god Jupiter, emphasizing his supreme power and authority as worshipped across the Roman Empire
epithet for the Roman god Jupiter

Examples

The temple of Jupiter Optimus Maximus was one of the most important religious sites in ancient Rome.
Priests offered sacrifices to Jupiter Optimus Maximus during state ceremonies.
Scholars still debate the rituals associated with Jupiter Optimus Maximus in Roman religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - chief god of the Roman pantheon

Etymology

From Latin: 'Jupiter' (the chief Roman god), 'Optimus' (best), 'Maximus' (greatest).
