frank breech delivery

1. NOUN

/ fræŋk briːtʃ dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / fræŋk briːtʃ dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
a medical situation in which a fetus is positioned so that its buttocks, rather than the head, present first at the mother's pelvic outlet during childbirth
fetal position with buttocks presenting first
medical

Examples

A frank breech delivery can present unique challenges for both the mother and medical team.
During examination, the doctor identified the baby was in a frank breech delivery position.
Planning is essential when a frank breech delivery is detected late in pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breech birth - childbirth in which a baby is delivered feet or buttocks first

Etymology

From 'frank' (full or complete) and 'breech' (buttocks-first fetal position) in medical terminology.
