shaft louse

1. NOUN

/ ʃæft laʊs / (AmE), / ʃɑːft laʊs / (BrE)
a species of louse that lives as a parasite on poultry, often found in the shaft of feathers
louse parasitic on poultry

Examples

The farmer treated his chickens to prevent a shaft louse infestation.
A shaft louse can cause irritation and feather damage in poultry.
Veterinarians recommend checking for shaft louse when birds appear restless or scratch frequently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a small, wingless parasitic insect
