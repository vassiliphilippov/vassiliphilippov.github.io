tomato concentrate

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ ˌkɑnsənˈtreɪt / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪt / (BrE)
A thick, concentrated product made from tomatoes by removing most of the water content, used in cooking to add tomato flavor.
thick, concentrated tomato product

Examples

Stir two tablespoons of tomato concentrate into the sauce for a richer flavor.
Most recipes require tomato concentrate to be cooked down with onions and garlic.
The soup called for a spoonful of tomato concentrate to intensify its color and taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato paste - a very thick, smooth tomato concentrate used in cooking
  • Degree Lower: tomato purée - a less concentrated, smooth tomato sauce
