Lucky Lindy

1. PROPN

/ ˈlʌki ˈlɪndi / (AmE), / ˈlʌki ˈlɪndi / (BrE)
a nickname for Charles Lindbergh, the American aviator who made the first solo nonstop flight across the Atlantic Ocean in 1927
nickname of aviator Charles Lindbergh

Examples

The press dubbed Charles Lindbergh Lucky Lindy after his historic flight.
In the 1920s, Lucky Lindy became a household name in America.
Many young boys dreamed of becoming the next Lucky Lindy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charles Lindbergh - American aviator; made first solo nonstop transatlantic flight

Etymology

Formed from 'lucky' and the shortened form of the surname Lindbergh, referring to Charles Lindbergh's daring and successful transatlantic flight.
