reverse trap

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɝs træp / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs træp / (BrE)
a situation that unexpectedly ensnares or backfires on someone, often causing harm to the original instigator or victim
unexpectedly backfiring situation

Examples

She set a reverse trap for her rival, but it ended up backfiring on her instead.
The detective avoided the reverse trap by thinking two steps ahead of the criminals.
Writing that clause into the contract was a reverse trap for anyone not paying close attention.

Synset Examples

He escaped the reverse trap by changing his approach.
They fell into a reverse trap, making their situation worse.
The new policy created a reverse trap for unwary employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a situation or device designed to catch someone unawares
