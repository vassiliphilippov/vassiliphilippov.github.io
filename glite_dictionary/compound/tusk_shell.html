tusk shell

1. NOUN

/ tʌsk ʃɛl / (AmE), / tʌsk ʃɛl / (BrE)
a marine mollusk with a long, tubular, tapered shell open at both ends, belonging to the class Scaphopoda
marine mollusk with a tapered tubular shell

Examples

The tusk shell burrows into sandy seabeds using its spade-like foot.
Scientists collected several tusk shells for their marine biology study.
The distinctive tubular shape of the tusk shell makes it easy to identify.
