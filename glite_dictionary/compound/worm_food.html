worm food

1. NOUN

/ wɝm fuːd / (AmE), / wɜːm fuːd / (BrE)
Food or material that is spoiled or decomposing and therefore only fit to be eaten by worms; something that is no longer useful or edible.
spoiled food or material fit only for worms
informal

Examples

After sitting in the fridge for weeks, the lettuce turned into worm food.
He threw away the meat because it was basically worm food.
If you forget about those apples, they’ll end up as worm food.

Synset Examples

That old bread is just worm food now.
Don't leave the leftovers out; they’ll become worm food.
