acoustic absorption

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk əbˈzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk əbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a material, structure, or object absorbs sound energy instead of reflecting it when sound waves encounter it.
process of absorbing sound energy

Examples

Materials with high acoustic absorption are essential for reducing noise in concert halls.
The acoustic absorption properties of this foam make it ideal for use in recording studios.
Improved acoustic absorption lowers the levels of reverberation in classrooms.

Synset Examples

The acoustic absorption of the Alpha Cabin itself is very low and the design ensures a high level of sound insulation to keep the background noise level very low in the frequency range of interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound absorption - absorption of sound energy by a material
  • Antonym: sound reflection - process by which sound bounces off a surface rather than being absorbed

Etymology

From 'acoustic', relating to sound, and 'absorption', from Latin absorptio (a swallowing up).
