black buffalo

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈbʌfəloʊ / (AmE), / blæk ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
A large North American freshwater fish (Ictiobus niger) of the sucker family, found mainly in the lower Mississippi River basin.
a North American freshwater fish

Examples

The black buffalo is highly valued by some anglers for its size and strength.
Several black buffalo were caught in the river during the spring spawning season.
Biologists are studying the population of the black buffalo in the Mississippi basin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo fish - a large freshwater fish of the sucker family, native to North America
