atomic structure

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑ.mɪk ˈstrʌk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / əˈtɒ.mɪk ˈstrʌk.tʃə / (BrE)
the arrangement and organization of atoms within a substance
arrangement of atoms in a substance

Examples

Scientists use X-ray crystallography to determine the atomic structure of new materials.
Understanding the atomic structure of an element helps explain its chemical properties.
The atomic structure of graphite gives it unique characteristics compared to diamond.

Synset Examples

The atomic structure of water is fascinating to study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molecular structure - the arrangement of atoms within a molecule

Etymology

From 'atomic', relating to atoms (from Greek atomos, ‘indivisible’), and 'structure', from Latin structura, ‘a fitting together’.
