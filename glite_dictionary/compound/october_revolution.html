October Revolution

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtoʊbɚ ˌrɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ɒkˈtəʊbə ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the overthrow of the Russian Provisional Government by the Bolsheviks led by Lenin in November 1917, marking the start of communist rule in Russia
1917 Bolshevik takeover in Russia

Examples

The October Revolution radically changed the political and social landscape of Russia.
Historians often debate the causes and outcomes of the October Revolution.
The centenary of the October Revolution was marked by various events around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian Revolution - the broader revolution in Russia during 1917
  • Degree Lower: February Revolution - the earlier 1917 revolution that preceded the October Revolution

Etymology

From the date of the uprising, which took place in October according to the Julian calendar then used in Russia.
