Quechua language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the indigenous language of the Quechua people of the Andes, historically spoken by the Incas and still used in parts of South America today
the indigenous language of the Quechua people

Examples

Many Peruvian children still learn Quechua language as their first language at home.
The Quechua language has dozens of dialects spoken across the Andes.
She studied the Quechua language to understand ancient Inca texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - a member of the indigenous people of Peru and surrounding Andes regions

Etymology

From Spanish Quechua, from Quechua *qichwa* (temperate valley).
