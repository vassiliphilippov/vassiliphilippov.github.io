jammy stroke of luck

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmi ˌstroʊk əv ˈlʌk / (AmE), / ˈdʒæmi ˌstrəʊk əv ˈlʌk / (BrE)
an unexpectedly fortunate event, typically occurring by chance rather than through effort or planning (British, informal)
an unexpected, lucky event (British informal)
informal british

Examples

It was a jammy stroke of luck that we caught the last train home just in time.
Her promotion came as a jammy stroke of luck, completely unexpected by everyone in the office.
Winning that holiday was such a jammy stroke of luck for the Johnsons.

Synset Examples

Winning the lottery was a jammy stroke of luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluke - an unlikely chance occurrence, especially a surprising piece of luck
  • Antonym: bad luck - an event causing misfortune or adversity

Etymology

From British slang 'jammy' (lucky or fortunate), likely from 'jam' (slang for luck) in late 19th-century Britain.
