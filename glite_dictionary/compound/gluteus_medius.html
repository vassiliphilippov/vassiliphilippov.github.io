gluteus medius

1. NOUN

/ ɡluːˈtiːəs ˈmiːdiəs / (AmE), / ɡluːˈtiːəs ˈmiːdiəs / (BrE)
the middle of the three muscles in each buttock, situated between the gluteus maximus and gluteus minimus
the middle gluteal muscle of the buttock
medical

Examples

Strengthening the gluteus medius can help stabilize the hips and prevent injuries.
The physical therapist showed me exercises that target the gluteus medius.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the gluteus medius.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gluteus maximus - the largest and outermost gluteal muscle
  • Degree Lower: gluteus minimus - the smallest and innermost gluteal muscle

Etymology

From Latin 'gluteus' (of the buttock) and 'medius' (middle).
