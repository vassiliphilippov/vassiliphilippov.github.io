great hall

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt hɔl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt hɔːl / (BrE)
the main and largest hall in a castle or mansion, traditionally used for dining, gatherings, or entertainment
main hall of a castle or mansion

Examples

The guests assembled in the great hall for the evening banquet.
Torchlight flickered along the stone walls of the great hall as musicians began to play.
Historic castles often feature a magnificent great hall where feasts and ceremonies were once held.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banquet hall - a large room used for formal meals and events
