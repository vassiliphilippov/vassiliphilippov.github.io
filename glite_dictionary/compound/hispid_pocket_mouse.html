hispid pocket mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪspɪd ˈpɑkɪt maʊs / (AmE), / ˈhɪspɪd ˈpɒkɪt maʊs / (BrE)
a species of pocket mouse with large stiff hairs, native to shortgrass prairies in the United States
stiff-haired rodent of US prairies

Examples

The dry habitat is perfect for the hispid pocket mouse to burrow and find seeds.
A hispid pocket mouse can be identified by its stiff, bristly fur and cheek pouches.
Scientists studied the diet of the hispid pocket mouse in the Texas grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket mouse - small rodent with external cheek pouches

Etymology

From Latin 'hispidus' (bristly, hairy) and 'pocket mouse', referring to cheek pouches.
