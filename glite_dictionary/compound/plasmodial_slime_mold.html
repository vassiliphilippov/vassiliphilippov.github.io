plasmodial slime mold

1. NOUN

/ plæzˈmoʊdiəl slaɪm moʊld / (AmE), / plazˈməʊdiəl slaɪm məʊld / (BrE)
a type of slime mold belonging to the class Myxomycetes, characterized by a multinucleate, amoeboid plasmodium stage in its life cycle
type of slime mold (Myxomycetes)
medical

Examples

The plasmodial slime mold moves by streaming its protoplasm across decaying wood.
Scientists studied the unique feeding habits of the plasmodial slime mold under different laboratory conditions.
A plasmodial slime mold can merge into a bright yellow mass during the plasmodium stage.

Etymology

From New Latin 'plasmodium' (multinucleate mass of protoplasm) + 'slime mold'.
