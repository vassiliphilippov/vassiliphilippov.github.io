open-door policy

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.pən ˈdɔr ˌpɑ.lə.si / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.pən ˈdɔː ˌpɒl.ɪ.si / (BrE)
a policy of granting equal trade opportunities to all countries, especially to allow foreign businesses to compete on the same terms as domestic companies
trade policy allowing equal opportunity to all countries

Examples

The country's new open-door policy attracted investors from all over the world.
During the late 19th century, the United States promoted an open-door policy in China.
Some economists argue that an open-door policy increases competition and benefits consumers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: protectionism - policy of restricting imports to protect domestic industries

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊpən ˈdɔr ˌpɑləsi / (AmE), / ˌəʊpən ˈdɔː ˌpɒləsi / (BrE)
a policy of encouraging openness, accessibility, and communication, allowing people to approach those in authority freely
policy allowing open communication and accessibility

Examples

Our company maintains an open-door policy to ensure all employees feel comfortable sharing their concerns.
Many universities have adopted an open-door policy to encourage feedback from students and staff.
Her open-door policy made it easy for team members to approach her with new ideas.

Synset Examples

The school has an open-door policy for parents.
Their open-door policy encourages communication across levels.
The manager implemented an open-door policy for feedback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-door - allowing unrestricted access or movement
