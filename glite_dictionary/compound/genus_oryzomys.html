genus Oryzomys

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ɔːˈraɪzoʊmɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɒˈrɪzəʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents known as rice rats, found mainly in the Americas.
a genus of rice rats

Examples

The genus Oryzomys includes several species of rice rats native to Central and South America.
Many studies have focused on the distribution of the genus Oryzomys in different wetland habitats.
Because they often live in rice fields, members of the genus Oryzomys are sometimes considered agricultural pests.

Etymology

From Greek 'oryza' meaning 'rice' and 'mys' meaning 'mouse', referring to their habitat and appearance.
