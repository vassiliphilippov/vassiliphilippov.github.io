bluestocking society

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌstɑkɪŋ səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌstɒkɪŋ səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
a group or society of educated women who gather to discuss literature and intellectual topics, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries
group of literary-minded women

Examples

The bluestocking society would hold weekly salons to debate poetry and philosophy.
She joined a bluestocking society to meet like-minded women with a passion for books.
Historical records note the influence these bluestocking societies had on literary culture.

Synset Examples

The bluestocking society gathered to discuss literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluestocking - an intellectual or literary woman

Etymology

From 'bluestocking', referring to learned women in 18th-century England, with 'society' denoting a group or club.
