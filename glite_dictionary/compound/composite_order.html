Composite order

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑz.ɪt ˈɔr.dɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒz.ɪt ˈɔː.də / (BrE)
a classical Roman architectural order that combines the decorative acanthus leaves of the Corinthian order with the volute scrolls of the Ionic order
classical Roman architectural style combining Corinthian and Ionic elements

Examples

The Composite order was favored in many grand Roman structures for its elaborate design.
This column capital features characteristics typical of the Composite order.
Architects often used the Composite order to achieve a sense of richness and complexity in their buildings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Corinthian order - classical column style with elaborate acanthus leaves
  • Degree Lower: Ionic order - classical column style with spiral volutes

Etymology

From Latin compositus (put together) and order, referring to its combination of elements from other styles.
