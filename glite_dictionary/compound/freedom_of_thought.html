freedom of thought

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdəm əv θɔt / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm əv θɔːt / (BrE)
the right to hold one's own opinions and beliefs, especially those that may be considered unpopular or controversial
the right to hold one's own beliefs

Examples

The philosopher emphasized the importance of freedom of thought in a democratic society.
Many laws are designed to protect an individual's freedom of thought and expression.
Throughout history, people have fought to preserve freedom of thought against oppression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freedom of speech - the right to express one's opinions publicly
  • Antonym: censorship - suppression or prohibition of speech or writing
