dog days

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡ deɪz / (AmE), / dɒɡ deɪz / (BrE)
The hottest period of the summer, typically between early July and early September; also, a period marked by stagnation or inactivity.
hottest part of summer; period of inactivity

Examples

Many people take vacations during the dog days of August.
Business tends to slow down during the dog days when everyone is on holiday.
The city seemed to sleep through the dog days, waiting for cooler air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer - the warmest season of the year

Etymology

From ancient belief that the star Sirius (the Dog Star) rises with the sun during the hottest summer days.
