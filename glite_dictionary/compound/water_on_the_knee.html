water on the knee

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ɑn ðə ˈni / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ɒn ðə ˈniː / (BrE)
a swelling of the knee caused by excess fluid in the joint, medically known as hydrarthrosis
swelling of the knee from excess joint fluid

Examples

The doctor explained that my water on the knee was due to an injury during soccer practice.
He had to have his water on the knee drained several times over the summer.
Swelling and stiffness are common symptoms when someone has water on the knee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrarthrosis - excess fluid in a joint cavity
