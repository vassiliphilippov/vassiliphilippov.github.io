strong suit

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ suːt / (AmE), / strɒŋ suːt / (BrE)
an attribute, skill, or area at which someone excels; a person's particular strength
person's area of strength or expertise

Examples

Public speaking has never been my strong suit.
Her strong suit is creative problem-solving at work.
While math wasn't his strong suit, he excelled in literature and the arts.

Synset Examples

cooking is his forte

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forte - a person's strong point or area of excellence
  • Antonym: weakness - a lack of strength or skill in a particular area

Etymology

Originally from card games, where a 'strong suit' is the suit in which a player holds the highest cards.

2. NOUN

/ strɔŋ suːt / (AmE), / strɒŋ suːt / (BrE)
A suit in a card game in which a player holds an unusually large number of high cards.
a suit with many high cards (cards)

Examples

He bid hearts because it was his strong suit in the hand.
Clubs turned out to be her strong suit, giving her an advantage in the game.
If your strong suit is spades, consider leading with one in your first trick.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak suit - a suit in a hand with few or no high cards (cards)
