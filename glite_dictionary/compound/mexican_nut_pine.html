Mexican nut pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən nʌt paɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən nʌt paɪn / (BrE)
a small pine tree native to Mexico and southern Texas, typically having two or three needles per cluster, also known as a piñon pine
small pine tree of Mexico and southern Texas

Examples

The Mexican nut pine is valued for its edible seeds, commonly known as pine nuts.
Many animals in southern Texas rely on the Mexican nut pine for food and shelter.
Botanists are studying the ecological role of the Mexican nut pine in arid regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piñon pine - small pine tree known for its edible seeds, related species
