sign of the cross

1. NOUN

/ saɪn əv ðə krɑs / (AmE), / saɪn əv ðə krɒs / (BrE)
a gesture made by moving the right hand to trace the shape of a cross, usually as a profession of faith in Christianity, especially among Catholics
Christian gesture tracing a cross

Examples

Before entering the church, she made the sign of the cross as a mark of reverence.
Some athletes perform the sign of the cross before starting a competition for good luck.
The priest blessed the congregation by making the sign of the cross in the air.
