rattlesnake root

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætlˌsneɪk ruːt / (AmE), / ˈrætlsneɪk ruːt / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant of central and southern Europe with purple florets, belonging to the genus Prenanthes or Hieracium

The herbalist identified the wild plant as rattlesnake root based on its purple florets.
Gardeners value rattlesnake root for its hardy nature and attractive blossoms.
Rattlesnake root grows abundantly in open meadows across central Europe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræɾlˌsneɪk ruːt / (AmE), / ˈratlˌsneɪk ruːt / (BrE)
a North American plant of the genus Nabalus, with long clusters of small flowers, once believed to have medicinal properties
a North American plant of the genus Nabalus

Examples

Native Americans once used the rattlesnake root to treat various ailments.
The rattlesnake root blooms in late summer along forest edges.
Botanists are studying rattlesnake root for its medicinal properties.

Etymology

Named for the belief that its root could treat rattlesnake bites.
