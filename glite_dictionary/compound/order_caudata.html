order Caudata

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər kɔːˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kɔːˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of amphibians that includes salamanders, newts, and related animals characterized by a lizard-like body and a long tail.
order of amphibians (salamanders, newts, etc.)
medical

Examples

Members of the order Caudata are distinguished by their elongated bodies and tails.
The order Caudata includes both terrestrial and aquatic species of salamanders.
Scientists study the order Caudata to learn more about amphibian evolution and diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - any amphibian of the order Caudata, typically having a slender body, moist skin, and a long tail
  • Antonym: order Anura - the order of amphibians comprising frogs and toads, which lack tails as adults

Etymology

From Latin 'cauda' (tail), referring to the tailed nature of the creatures in this order.
