holly-leaved oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑli livd oʊk / (AmE), / ˈhɒli liːvd əʊk / (BrE)
an evergreen oak tree of southern Europe with leaves that resemble holly, known for its hard wood
evergreen oak tree with holly-like leaves

Examples

The holly-leaved oak is often planted in parks for its attractive foliage.
Because of its dense, hard wood, the holly-leaved oak is valued by woodworkers.
The leaves of the holly-leaved oak are spiny and dark green, similar to those of holly trees.
