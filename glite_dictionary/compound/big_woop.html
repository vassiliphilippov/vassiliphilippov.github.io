big woop

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ wup / (AmE), / bɪɡ wuːp / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is unimpressive, unimportant, or not worthy of excitement
expression for unimpressive event
informal

Examples

She told me she got VIP tickets, but I just said, 'big woop.'
You finished your sandwich in one minute—big woop!
He won a small trophy, and everyone was like, 'big woop.'

Synset Examples

Getting an A in class? Big woop, I studied hard.
She shrugged it off, saying, 'Big woop, let's move on.'
To him, it was just a big woop, nothing special.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: so what - expression showing indifference or lack of concern
  • Synonym: who cares - expression indicating lack of interest or importance
