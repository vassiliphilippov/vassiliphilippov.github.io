divey atmosphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.vi ˈætməˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.vi ˈætməsˌfɪə / (BrE)
A relaxed, often shabby or rundown ambiance typical of certain bars or restaurants, generally seen as unpretentious and informal.
casual and shabby ambiance, esp. in bars/restaurants
informal

Examples

The local pub had a divey atmosphere, but it was always packed with friendly regulars.
She preferred the divey atmosphere of small diners to the polished feel of upscale restaurants.
Despite the divey atmosphere, the food and service at the bar exceeded his expectations.

Synset Examples

They loved the divey atmosphere of the local bar.
The restaurant had a divey vibe but great food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive bar - an unpretentious bar, often shabby or run-down

Etymology

From "dive" (informal term for a disreputable bar or club) + -y (adjective forming suffix).
