urinary indican

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrəˌnɛri ɪnˈdeɪkən / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri ɪnˈdeɪkən / (BrE)
a chemical compound (C8H7NO4S) produced in the human liver and excreted in the urine, often used as an indicator in clinical tests
compound found in urine, medical term
medical

Examples

Doctors measured the level of urinary indican to help diagnose the patient's condition.
High concentrations of urinary indican can indicate problems with protein digestion or intestinal flora.
The laboratory test revealed the presence of urinary indican in the sample.

Synset Examples

Urinary indican is produced in liver and excreted with urine.

Etymology

From 'urinary', relating to urine, and 'indican', from the Latin 'indicum' (indigo).
