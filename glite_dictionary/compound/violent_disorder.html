violent disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪə.lənt dɪsˈɔr.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪə.lənt dɪsˈɔː.də / (BrE)
behavior involving outbursts of violence and destruction, often by a group of people, typically in public places
destructive and violent public behavior

Examples

During the protest, several arrests were made for violent disorder after property was damaged.
The police have special units trained to respond to incidents of violent disorder.
The judge warned that anyone convicted of violent disorder would face a lengthy prison sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riot - a violent public disturbance by a crowd
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from disturbance; tranquility
