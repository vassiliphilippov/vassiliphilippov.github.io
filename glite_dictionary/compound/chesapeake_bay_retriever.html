Chesapeake Bay retriever

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɛsəpiːk beɪ rɪˈtrivər / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛsəpiːk beɪ rɪˈtriːvə / (BrE)
a breed of large retriever dog developed in the United States, known for its short, thick, oily coat and used especially for waterfowl hunting
a breed of retriever dog

Examples

The Chesapeake Bay retriever is famous for its endurance in icy waters.
She adopted a Chesapeake Bay retriever because of its loyalty and intelligence.
A Chesapeake Bay retriever requires regular exercise to stay healthy and happy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Labrador retriever - a popular retriever breed known for friendliness and trainability
  • Synonym: Golden retriever - a retriever breed with a long golden coat

Etymology

Named after the Chesapeake Bay region in the United States, where the breed was developed.
