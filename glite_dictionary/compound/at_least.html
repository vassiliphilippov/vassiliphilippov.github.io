at least

1. ADV

/ ət ˈlist / (AmE), / ət ˈliːst / (BrE)
not less than a particular amount, number, or degree; at a minimum.
not less than; minimum amount

Examples

You should drink at least eight glasses of water a day.
The repairs will take at least a week to complete.
She needs at least three references for her job application.

Synset Examples

at least two hours studying the manual
a tumor at least as big as an orange

Related Terms

  • Antonym: at most - not more than a specified amount or degree
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00105032-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ət ˈlist / (AmE), / ət ˈliːst / (BrE)
used to indicate a minimum amount, level, or standard, or to introduce a consolation or clarification when the situation is not ideal
minimum or consolation phrase

Examples

We need at least two more volunteers to start the project.
You should apologize, or at least explain what happened.
The concert was disappointing, but at least the tickets were cheap.

Synset Examples

at least he survived
they felt — at any rate Jim felt — relieved though still wary
the influence of economists — or at any rate of economics — is far-reaching

Related Terms

  • Antonym: at most - at the maximum; not more than
  • Synonym: at any rate - in any case; anyway
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00105348-r
Synset POS: r
