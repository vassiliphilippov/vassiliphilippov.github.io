many a

1. DET

/ ˈmɛni ə / (AmE), / ˈmeni ə / (BrE)
Used before a singular noun to mean 'a large number of' or 'many,' emphasizing each individual in the group.
used to refer to many individual things or people

Examples

Many a student has dreamed of studying abroad.
He has hiked up many a mountain in his lifetime.
Many a time, she wondered if she had made the right decision.

Synset Examples

many a man
many another day will come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: many - a large number of people or things
  • Synonym: numerous - existing in large numbers

2. DET

/ ˈmɛni ə / (AmE), / ˈmeni ə / (BrE)
Used with a singular noun to mean 'each of a large number of' or 'numerous.'
each of many; numerous (with singular noun)
archaic

Examples

Many a student has dreamed of studying abroad.
She has faced many a challenge throughout her life.
Many a time, I've thought about moving to the countryside.

Synset Examples

many a man
many another day will come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: many - a large number of (used with plural nouns)
