board of selectmen

1. NOUN

/ bɔrd əv səˈlɛktmɪn / (AmE), / bɔːd əv səˈlɛktmɪn / (BrE)
a governing body of officials elected to oversee and administer public affairs in a town, especially in New England, USA
elected town governing body in New England

Examples

In many Massachusetts towns, the board of selectmen meets weekly to discuss local issues.
The election for the board of selectmen drew unusually high voter turnout this year.
She served on the board of selectmen for over a decade, helping shape town policy.
