mountain laurel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
a North American evergreen shrub (Kalmia latifolia) with glossy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers, common in wooded or hilly areas
North American evergreen shrub with pink or white flowers

Examples

The mountain laurel is in full bloom along the hiking trail this month.
Gardeners admire mountain laurel for its attractive, glossy foliage and vibrant flowers.
A thicket of mountain laurel provided shelter for the deer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
A tree or shrub of the Pacific coast with aromatic leaves and small cluster-forming flowers, followed by olive-like fruit; its wood is hard and tough.
Pacific coast tree or shrub with aromatic foliage and tough wood

Examples

The wood of the mountain laurel is prized for making tool handles and furniture.
On our hike, we spotted a mountain laurel in full bloom near the stream.
The aromatic leaves of the mountain laurel released a pleasant scent when crushed.
