Lake Tahoe

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈtɑːhoʊ / (AmE), / leɪk ˈtɑːhəʊ / (BrE)
a large freshwater lake located on the border between Nevada and California in the United States, known for its clear water and as a popular resort area
major lake and resort in Nevada-California, US

Examples

Many tourists visit Lake Tahoe during the winter for skiing and snowboarding.
The clear blue water of Lake Tahoe attracts visitors from around the world.
They rented a cabin overlooking Lake Tahoe for their summer vacation.

Etymology

From the Washo word 'dáʔaw', meaning 'the lake'.
