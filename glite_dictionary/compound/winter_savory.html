winter savory

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ ˈsæv.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə ˈsæv.ər.i / (BrE)
an aromatic herb (Satureja montana) with resinous leaves, used as a seasoning in stews, stuffings, and meat dishes
herb used as seasoning for meats

Examples

The chef recommended adding winter savory to the stew for a unique, peppery flavor.
She grew winter savory in her herb garden to use during the colder months.
A handful of chopped winter savory added depth to the meat loaf recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer savory - an aromatic annual herb also used as a seasoning

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntər ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
a perennial subshrub of southern Europe (Satureja montana) with pink or white flowers and leaves used as an herb, having a flavor similar to thyme
herbaceous plant with thyme-like flavor

Examples

She sprinkled chopped winter savory over the roasted vegetables for added flavor.
Winter savory is often used in recipes that call for a peppery, thyme-like taste.
Gardeners value winter savory because it thrives in poor soil and remains green through cold months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer savory - an annual herb related to winter savory, used as a seasoning

Etymology

From 'winter' meaning the cold season, and 'savory', from Old French 'savoure', ultimately from Latin 'satureia', the plant's genus.
