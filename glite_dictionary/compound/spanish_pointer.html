Spanish pointer

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈpɔɪntər / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈpɔɪntə / (BrE)
a breed of strong, slender, short-haired gun dog of Spanish origin, typically white with brown or black patches, known for its ability to scent and point out game
Spanish breed of gun dog; points game

Examples

The Spanish pointer is renowned for its keen sense of smell and athletic build.
During the hunt, the Spanish pointer expertly located and pointed to the hidden quail.
Historically, the Spanish pointer influenced the development of several modern pointing breeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointer - a type of hunting dog that indicates the presence of game by pointing

Etymology

Spanish, referring to its country of origin, and pointer, from its use as a hunting dog that 'points' game.
