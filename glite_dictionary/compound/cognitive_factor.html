cognitive factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːɡnətɪv ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnətɪv ˈfæktə / (BrE)
An element of mental process, such as perception, memory, or reasoning, that influences a person's behavior or performance.
mental influence on behavior or outcome

Examples

Attention is a key cognitive factor in determining how well a person learns new information.
Researchers studied the influence of motivation as a cognitive factor on test performance.
Memory can serve as an important cognitive factor in how we make decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychological factor - an influence originating from the mind or emotions
  • Antonym: environmental factor - an external influence arising from surroundings

Etymology

From Latin cognitivus (pertaining to knowing) and English factor (something that contributes to a result).
