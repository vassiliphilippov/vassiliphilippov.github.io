Roman mile

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən maɪl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən maɪl / (BrE)
an ancient Roman unit of length, approximately equal to 1,620 yards or 1,480 meters
ancient Roman unit of length

Examples

The distance between two towns was measured as three Roman miles.
A Roman mile consisted of 1,000 paces according to ancient standards.
Historians often convert ancient Roman mile measurements into modern units for clarity.

Etymology

From Latin 'mille passus' meaning 'thousand paces'.
