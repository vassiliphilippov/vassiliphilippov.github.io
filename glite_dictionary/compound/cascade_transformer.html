cascade transformer

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈkeɪd trænsˌfɔrmər / (AmE), / kæsˈkeɪd trænsˌfɔːmə / (BrE)
an arrangement of transformers connected in series to produce a high-voltage source
series-connected high-voltage transformer

Examples

The laboratory used a cascade transformer to generate the necessary test voltage.
By connecting three units in a cascade transformer arrangement, the engineers achieved much higher voltages.
The failure of one unit in the cascade transformer disrupted the entire high-voltage supply.

Etymology

From 'cascade' (to arrange in a sequence or series) and 'transformer' (device that transfers electrical energy between circuits).
