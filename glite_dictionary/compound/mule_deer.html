mule deer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːl dɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈmjuːl dɪə / (BrE)
a large deer of western North America with long ears and antlers that are typically forked in two; Odocoileus hemionus.
North American deer with large ears and forked antlers

Examples

We spotted a mule deer grazing near the edge of the forest at dawn.
The distinctive ears of a mule deer help it pick up distant sounds in the wild.
During our hike, we saw several mule deer crossing the mountain trail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white-tailed deer - North American deer species with a white underside to its tail

Etymology

Named for its large, mule-like ears.
