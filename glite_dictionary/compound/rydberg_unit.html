Rydberg unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪd.bɜrɡ ˈjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɪd.bɜːɡ ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit of wave number used in spectroscopy, especially for characterizing the spectral lines of atoms and elements
unit of wave number in spectroscopy

Examples

The energy levels in the hydrogen atom can be expressed in terms of the Rydberg unit.
Spectroscopists use the Rydberg unit to measure wave numbers in atomic spectra.
Calculations in quantum chemistry often reference the Rydberg unit for convenience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave number - the number of waves per unit distance; used in spectroscopy

Etymology

Named after Swedish physicist Johannes Rydberg.
