Toll House cookie

1. NOUN

/ toʊl haʊs ˈkʊki / (AmE), / təʊl haʊs ˈkʊki / (BrE)
A type of cookie containing chocolate chips, originally made famous by the Toll House Inn in Massachusetts.
chocolate chip cookie

Examples

My grandmother always bakes a batch of Toll House cookies for the holidays.
The original recipe for Toll House cookies uses semi-sweet chocolate chips.
She brought homemade Toll House cookies to the school bake sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chocolate chip cookie - cookie containing small chocolate pieces

Etymology

Named after the Toll House Inn in Massachusetts, where the recipe was created in the 1930s.
