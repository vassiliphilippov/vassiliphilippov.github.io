Matthew Walker knot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæθju ˈwɔkər nɑt / (AmE), / ˈmæθju ˈwɔːkə nɒt / (BrE)
a knot tied at the end of a rope to form a stopper, traditionally used in nautical contexts to prevent the end from passing through an opening
nautical stopper knot

Examples

The sailor tied a Matthew Walker knot to prevent the line from slipping through the pulley.
Learning to make a Matthew Walker knot is essential for traditional rigging work.
She admired how neatly the Matthew Walker knot held at the end of the rope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper knot - a knot used to prevent a rope from slipping through a hole or device

Etymology

Named after Matthew Walker, who is credited with inventing the knot in the 18th century.
