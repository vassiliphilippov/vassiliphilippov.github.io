initial stress

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪʃəl strɛs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃəl strɛs / (BrE)
The placement of accent or emphasis on the first syllable of a word.
emphasis on the first syllable of a word

Examples

In English, the word 'table' has initial stress on the first syllable.
Many two-syllable nouns in English exhibit initial stress patterns.
Confusion can arise if the initial stress is not placed correctly in some words.

Synset Examples

The fronting took place more slowly in American English and so Modern American English has forms like elóngate, imprégnate and remónstrate where British English usually has initial stress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: final stress - accent on the last syllable of a word
