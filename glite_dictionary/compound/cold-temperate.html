cold-temperate

1. ADJ

/ koʊldˈtɛmpərət / (AmE), / kəʊldˈtɛmpərət / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the colder parts of temperate regions, especially in reference to climate or waters.
colder part of the temperate zone

Examples

Many marine species thrive only in cold-temperate waters near the poles.
The forest is adapted to the cold-temperate climate with long, frosty winters.
Researchers are studying the impact of climate change on cold-temperate ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: temperate - relating to regions or climates with moderate temperature; not extreme
  • Degree Higher: subtropical - relating to regions or climates just outside the tropical zone, usually warmer than temperate
