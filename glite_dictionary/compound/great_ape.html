great ape

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt eɪp / (AmE), / ɡreɪt eɪp / (BrE)
a large ape of the family Hominidae, such as a gorilla, chimpanzee, orangutan, or bonobo, distinguished from gibbons and smaller apes
a large member of the ape family (e.g., gorilla, chimpanzee)

Examples

The zoo's new exhibit features several species of great ape, including gorillas and orangutans.
Researchers have observed advanced problem-solving skills in the great ape populations studied in the wild.
Unlike monkeys, great apes do not have tails and share closer genetic links with humans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gibbon - a small, slender, long-armed ape of the family Hylobatidae
