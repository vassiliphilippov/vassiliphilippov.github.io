Greek mode

1. NOUN

/ ɡrik moʊd / (AmE), / ɡriːk məʊd / (BrE)
any of the descending diatonic scales used in the music of classical Greece, each with a distinct sequence of intervals.
musical scale from classical Greece

Examples

Ancient composers often based their melodies on the Greek mode known as Dorian.
Music historians study how each Greek mode influenced Western musical traditions.
Understanding the Greek mode system can help performers interpret classical Greek music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mode - a type of musical scale characterized by a specific pattern of intervals
