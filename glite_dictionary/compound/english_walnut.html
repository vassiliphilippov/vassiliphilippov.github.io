English walnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈwɔlnət / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
A nut produced by the Eurasian walnut tree, with a wrinkled two-lobed seed and a hard but relatively thin shell, widely used in cooking and baking.
nut from the Eurasian walnut tree, used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled chopped English walnut on top of the cake for extra crunch.
The recipe calls for one cup of ground English walnut to enhance the flavor.
I keep a jar of English walnut on my kitchen counter for baking and snacking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - any edible nut of the walnut tree

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈwɔlnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
A tree of Eurasian origin (Juglans regia) valued for its large edible nuts and hard, richly grained wood; widely cultivated for both purposes.
tree with edible nuts and valuable wood

Examples

The English walnut is a popular tree in commercial orchards worldwide.
She enjoys the rich flavor of English walnut in baked goods and salads.
Furniture made from English walnut is prized for its beautiful wood grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - the edible nut, or the tree yielding walnuts
  • Degree Lower: black walnut - a North American walnut species with dark wood and smaller nuts
