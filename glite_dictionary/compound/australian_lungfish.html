Australian lungfish

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪljən ˈlʌŋfɪʃ / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪlɪən ˈlʌŋfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, endangered species of freshwater lungfish native to rivers in Queensland, Australia, capable of breathing air as well as using gills
endangered Australian freshwater lungfish

Examples

The Australian lungfish is considered a living fossil because of its ancient lineage.
Scientists monitor the populations of the Australian lungfish to protect it from extinction.
The unique ability of the Australian lungfish to breathe air sets it apart from most other fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungfish - a fish able to breathe air using lungs as well as gills
