to crowd erupted

1. VERB

/ kraʊd ɪˈrʌptɪd / (AmE), / kraʊd ɪˈrʌptɪd / (BrE)
said when a large group of people suddenly and loudly show excitement or emotion, especially in response to an event
when an audience reacts loudly and suddenly

Examples

When the final whistle blew, the crowd erupted in celebration.
As soon as her name was announced, the crowd erupted with applause and cheers.
The crowd erupted as the band took the stage for their encore.

Synset Examples

The crowd erupted when he scored a homer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer - to shout for joy or in praise
  • Antonym: hush fell over the crowd - the sudden quiet of a crowd
