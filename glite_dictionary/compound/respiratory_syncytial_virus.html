respiratory syncytial virus

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔri sɪŋˌsɪʃiəl ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔːri sɪŋˌsɪʃiəl ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus belonging to the paramyxovirus family that infects the respiratory tract, especially in young children, and can cause severe illnesses such as bronchiolitis and pneumonia
virus causing respiratory infections, especially in children
medical

Examples

Children under two are at high risk for severe illness from respiratory syncytial virus.
Doctors recommend extra precautions during respiratory syncytial virus season in daycare centers.
The hospital reported an increase in cases of respiratory syncytial virus-related bronchiolitis this winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: RSV - abbreviation for respiratory syncytial virus

Etymology

From 'respiratory' + 'syncytial' (relating to the formation of syncytia) + 'virus', describing its effect on respiratory cells.
