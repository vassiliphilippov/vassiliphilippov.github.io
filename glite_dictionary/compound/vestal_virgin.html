vestal virgin

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstəl ˈvɜrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɛstəl ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome) a virgin priestess dedicated to the goddess Vesta, responsible for maintaining the sacred fire in her temple.
priestess of Vesta in ancient Rome

Examples

In ancient Rome, a vestal virgin was highly respected and played an important religious role.
The solemn duties of a vestal virgin included keeping Vesta's sacred fire burning.
Violating her vow of chastity was a serious offense for a vestal virgin.

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo Vestalis', meaning 'virgin of Vesta'.
