autosomal recessive defect

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːtoʊˈsoʊməl rɪˈsɛsɪv dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈsəʊməl rɪˈsesɪv dɪˈfekt / (BrE)
a genetic disorder caused by having two copies of a recessive mutant gene located on an autosome
genetic disorder from two recessive autosomal genes
medical

Examples

Cystic fibrosis is an autosomal recessive defect that affects the lungs and digestive system.
Both parents must carry the gene for a child to inherit an autosomal recessive defect.
Sickle cell anemia results from an autosomal recessive defect in the hemoglobin gene.

Etymology

autosomal from 'autosome' (any chromosome not a sex chromosome) + 'recessive' in genetics refers to genes that require two copies to express a trait.
