vestibule of the ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstəˌbjuːl əv ði ɪr / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪbjuːl əv ðiː ɪə / (BrE)
the central cavity of the bony labyrinth in the inner ear, situated between the cochlea and the semicircular canals
central inner ear cavity
medical

Examples

Damage to the vestibule of the ear can affect both balance and hearing.
The doctor explained the function of the vestibule of the ear to the patient during the examination.
Fluid buildup in the vestibule of the ear may cause dizziness or vertigo.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cochlea - spiral-shaped cavity of the inner ear involved in hearing
  • Degree Higher: semicircular canal - one of three looped tubes in the inner ear involved in balance

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance hall or cavity); 'ear' from Old English 'ēare'.
