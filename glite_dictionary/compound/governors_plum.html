governor's plum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvənərz plʌm / (AmE), / ˈɡʌv(ə)nəz plʌm / (BrE)
a small shrubby tree of Madagascar grown in tropical regions as a hedge plant and for its deep red, acidic fruits resembling small plums
tropical shrub with plum-like red fruits

Examples

The governor's plum makes a dense hedge that is ideal for gardens in tropical climates.
Farmers value the governor's plum for both its edible fruit and its ability to form protective barriers.
The tart fruit of the governor's plum can be used to make jams and jellies.
