rifle grenade

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəl ɡrəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˈraɪfl ɡrəˈneɪd / (BrE)
a grenade designed to be fired from a launching device attached to the barrel of a rifle
grenade fired from a rifle

Examples

The soldier loaded a rifle grenade before taking aim at the enemy bunker.
During the exercise, he demonstrated how to safely fire a rifle grenade.
Modern infantry rarely uses the rifle grenade due to the popularity of dedicated launchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grenade - a small bomb thrown by hand or launched to explode after impact or after a set time
