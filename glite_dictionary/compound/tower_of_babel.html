tower of Babel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊɚ əv ˈbeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈtaʊə əv ˈbeɪbəl / (BrE)
A situation characterized by confusion or chaos, especially arising from a mixture of different languages or voices.
confused situation, often with many languages

Examples

As soon as everyone started arguing in different languages, the room became a tower of Babel.
Trying to organize the project across so many departments felt like managing a tower of Babel.
The market was a tower of Babel, with vendors shouting in a dozen tongues.

Synset Examples

Their different languages created a tower of Babel at the event.
The meeting turned into a tower of Babel with everyone speaking at once.
In this international conference, it felt like a tower of Babel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pandemonium - noisy confusion, chaos
  • Synonym: bedlam - a scene of uproar and confusion

Etymology

From the biblical Tower of Babel, where the confusion of languages arose (Genesis 11:1–9).
