corona discharge

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊ.nə dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊ.nə dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an electrical discharge caused by ionization of the surrounding air or gas around a conductor, often appearing as a faint glow
electrical discharge from ionized air/gas

Examples

The transmission line suffered a corona discharge during the thunderstorm, causing a faint blue glow.
Engineers observed corona discharge at the tips of the high-voltage wires.
Improper insulation can lead to corona discharge, reducing the efficiency of electrical equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric discharge - the flow of electric current through air or another substance

Etymology

From Latin 'corona' (crown, halo) referencing the crown-like glow produced around conductors.
