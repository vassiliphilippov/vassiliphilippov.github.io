post-free

1. ADJ

/ poʊstˈfri / (AmE), / pəʊstˈfriː / (BrE)
Sent without charge for postage; with the postage paid by the sender so that the recipient pays nothing.
sent without charge for postage

Examples

The supplier offered to send the catalog post-free to all interested customers.
If you order more than $50 worth of goods, your package will be delivered post-free.
They advertised the books as being available post-free anywhere in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpaid - having postage paid by the sender
  • Synonym: prepaid - paid for in advance, especially postage
  • Antonym: carriage forward - with delivery charges to be paid by recipient

Etymology

Formed from 'post' (mail) and 'free' (without charge).
