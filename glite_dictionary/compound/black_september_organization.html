Black September Organization

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsɛptəmbɚ ˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / blæk ˈsɛptəmbə ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant group that split from the PLO in 1974, known for conducting international terrorist attacks in multiple countries
Palestinian militant group active 1970s

Examples

The Black September Organization was responsible for the 1972 Munich Olympic massacre.
Several countries increased their security measures after attacks by the Black September Organization.
The Black September Organization split from the PLO due to internal disagreements.

Synset Examples

in the 1980s the Fatah-RC was considered the most dangerous and murderous Palestinian terror group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PLO - Palestine Liberation Organization; the larger group from which Black September split

Etymology

Named after the Black September conflict in Jordan in 1970.

2. PROPN

/ blæk səpˈtɛmbɚ ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / blæk səpˈtɛmbə ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant group responsible for several high-profile attacks in the early 1970s, including the killing of 11 Israeli athletes at the 1972 Munich Olympics
Palestinian group known for 1972 Munich Olympics attack

Examples

The Black September Organization was responsible for the massacre at the 1972 Munich Olympics.
After its merger, the Black September Organization ceased to operate independently.
The assassination carried out by the Black September Organization had far-reaching consequences in the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fatah Revolutionary Council - Palestinian militant group into which the Black September Organization was merged
  • Synonym: terrorist organization - group that uses violence and threats to achieve political aims

Etymology

Named after the conflict known as 'Black September' in Jordan in 1970.
