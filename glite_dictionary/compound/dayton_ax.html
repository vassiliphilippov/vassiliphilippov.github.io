Dayton ax

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtən ˌæks / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtən ˌæks / (BrE)
a type of ax with a long handle and a head featuring one cutting edge and one blunt side, commonly used for chopping wood
an ax with a long handle, one cutting edge, and a blunt side

Examples

He used a Dayton ax to split firewood in the backyard.
The Dayton ax is favored by many professional arborists for its balance and durability.
After sharpening his Dayton ax, he was ready to tackle the fallen tree.

Etymology

Named after Dayton, Ohio, where this ax head pattern was popularized.
