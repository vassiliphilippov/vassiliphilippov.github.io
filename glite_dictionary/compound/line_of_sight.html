line of sight

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv saɪt / (AmE), / laɪn əv saɪt / (BrE)
An imaginary straight line extending from the observer's eye to a distant object.
imaginary straight line from observer to object

Examples

The tall building blocked his line of sight to the mountains.
Make sure there is a clear line of sight between the transmitter and the receiver.
The sniper waited patiently, keeping his line of sight fixed on the target.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sightline - an unobstructed line of vision between an observer and an object
