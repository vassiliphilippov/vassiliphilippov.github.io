great fanfare

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈfænˌfɛr / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈfan.feə / (BrE)
A significant display of excitement, publicity, or ceremony surrounding an event or announcement.
much excitement and publicity

Examples

The celebrity arrived at the event with great fanfare, attracting crowds of reporters and fans.
The company launched its new product with great fanfare, but sales failed to meet expectations.
Despite the great fanfare surrounding the announcement, the policy changes were minimal.

Synset Examples

The new beautyship launched with great fanfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanfare - a short, lively sound of trumpets; a lot of public attention or excitement
