liver rot

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɚ ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə ˌrɒt / (BrE)
A disease of the liver, especially in sheep and cattle, caused by infestation with liver flukes and their by-products.
disease of the liver in animals caused by flukes
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the sheep with liver rot after observing signs of jaundice and weight loss.
Farmers vaccinate their cattle to prevent liver rot caused by liver flukes.
Chronic liver rot can significantly reduce livestock productivity and health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liver fluke - a parasitic flatworm that infects the liver, causing disease
