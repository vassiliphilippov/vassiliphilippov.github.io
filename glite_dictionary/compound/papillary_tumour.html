papillary tumour

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæpəˌlɛri ˈtumər / (AmE), / ˈpapɪləɹi ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
A benign tumor consisting of epithelial cells that form rounded or finger-like masses.
benign tumor forming rounded masses
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a papillary tumour after reviewing the biopsy results.
A papillary tumour typically appears as a rounded mass under microscopic examination.
Treatment for a papillary tumour often depends on its size and location.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papillary tumor - benign epithelial tumor forming a rounded mass (US spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'papilla' (nipple) + 'tumour' (swelling).
