cartridge fuse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.trɪdʒ fjuːz / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ fjuːz / (BrE)
A type of fuse encased in a cylindrical tube, used to protect electrical circuits by breaking the flow when overloaded.
fuse encased in a tube

Examples

The electrician replaced the blown cartridge fuse in the power panel.
Many older homes still rely on cartridge fuses for circuit protection.
Before resetting the system, make sure to check each cartridge fuse for damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - a safety device that stops the flow of electric current if it becomes too strong
