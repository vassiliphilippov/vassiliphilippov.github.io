silent butler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlənt ˈbʌtlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt ˈbʌtlə / (BrE)
a small handheld container with a hinged lid, used for collecting crumbs or ashes from tables or fireplaces
small container for crumbs or ashes

Examples

The waiter discreetly used a silent butler to gather crumbs from the table after each course.
My grandmother keeps a vintage silent butler on the mantelpiece to collect ashes from the fireplace.
He inherited his father's silver-plated silent butler, which was still in excellent condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crumb tray - a tray for collecting crumbs from a table

Etymology

From 'silent' (not making noise) + 'butler' (household servant), referring to the tool's discreet, quiet use in tidying up.
