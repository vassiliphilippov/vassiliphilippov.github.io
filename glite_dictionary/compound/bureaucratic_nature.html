bureaucratic nature

1. NOUN

/ bjʊˌroʊkrætɪk ˈneɪtʃər / (AmE), / bjʊəˌrəʊkrætɪk ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
the set of qualities or features typically associated with bureaucracy, such as rigid procedures, excessive regulations, and hierarchical organization
characteristics typical of bureaucracy

Examples

The company's bureaucratic nature made it difficult for decisions to be made quickly.
Critics argue that the bureaucratic nature of government agencies often leads to inefficiency.
Despite its bureaucratic nature, the organization managed to implement several reforms last year.

Synset Examples

Eurocracy often faces criticism for its bureaucratic nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucracy - a system of government or management marked by hierarchical structure and strict rules
