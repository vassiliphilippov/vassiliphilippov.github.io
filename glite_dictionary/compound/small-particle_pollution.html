small-particle pollution

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔl ˈpɑɹtɪkəl pəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːl ˈpɑːtɪkəl pəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
air pollution consisting of fine particles, such as soot, often produced by power plants or diesel engines, which can harm health and the environment.
air pollution from fine particles

Examples

The city has implemented new regulations to limit small-particle pollution from industrial sources.
Health experts warn that small-particle pollution can lead to respiratory problems in children and the elderly.
Reducing small-particle pollution is a top priority for improving urban air quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particulate matter - tiny particles suspended in the air, often a cause of air pollution
