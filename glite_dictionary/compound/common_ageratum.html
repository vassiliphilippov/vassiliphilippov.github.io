common ageratum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən əˈdʒɛrətəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən əˈdʒɛrətəm / (BrE)
a small, tender herb grown as an ornamental for its fluffy, brush-like blue to lavender flowers.
ornamental plant with blue to lavender flowers

Examples

The common ageratum adds a splash of color to many summer gardens.
Gardeners often choose common ageratum for its low height and long-blooming flowers.
Blue beds of common ageratum brightened the flower show this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ageratum - genus of flowering plants with fluffy blue blooms

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ageratum', a genus name derived from Greek 'ageratos' meaning 'ageless', referring to the long-lasting flowers.
