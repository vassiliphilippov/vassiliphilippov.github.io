Department of State

1. PROPN

/ dɪˈpɑrtmənt əv steɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtmənt əv steɪt / (BrE)
The federal department of the United States government responsible for setting and maintaining the country’s foreign policies and conducting international relations.
U.S. government body for foreign affairs
american

Examples

The Department of State issued a travel advisory for tourists visiting the region.
She received an official invitation to visit Washington, D.C. from the Department of State.
Many diplomats are employed by the Department of State to represent the U.S. abroad.
