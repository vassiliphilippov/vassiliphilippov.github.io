western ragweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈræɡˌwid / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈræɡwiːd / (BrE)
a coarse perennial ragweed (Ambrosia psilostachya) with creeping roots, found in dry, barren areas of the southwestern United States and Mexico
a perennial ragweed of the American Southwest

Examples

Western ragweed thrives in dry, rocky soils where few other plants can survive.
Farmers in Texas often battle western ragweed because it competes with their crops for water.
The botanist collected samples of western ragweed to study its resistance to drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragweed - a group of plants known for causing hay fever and allergies
