saturniid moth

1. NOUN

/ səˈtɜːrni.ɪd mɒθ / (AmE), / səˈtʃʊə.ni.ɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
a large, brightly colored moth, often tropical, whose larvae spin silken cocoons; a member of the family Saturniidae.
large, colorful moth of the Saturniidae family

Examples

The saturniid moth is known for its vivid eye-shaped patterns on the wings.
Scientists observed the saturniid moth larvae spinning remarkably strong silken cocoons.
Many saturniid moths are found in tropical forests around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silkmoth - another name for moths of the Saturniidae family

Etymology

From the scientific family name Saturniidae, derived from Saturn, a Roman god, alluding to the eye-like spots on many species.
