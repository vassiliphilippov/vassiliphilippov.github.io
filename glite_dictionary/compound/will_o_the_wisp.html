will o' the wisp

1. NOUN

/ wɪl ə ðə wɪsp / (AmE), / wɪl ə ðə wɪsp / (BrE)
A misleading or elusive thing; something that is impossible or difficult to catch or achieve, like an atmospheric ghost light seen by travelers at night, especially over bogs, which vanishes when approached.
elusive or misleading phenomenon or goal

Examples

For years, investors chased the will o' the wisp of a perfect market strategy.
Her dream of finding buried treasure turned out to be a will o' the wisp.
The peace agreement sometimes seemed like a will o' the wisp, always just out of reach.

Synset Examples

Chasing that will o' the wisp left him feeling lost.
The mysterious light was just a will o' the wisp in the fog.
He followed the will o' the wisp, but it led him astray.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimera - An illusion or fabrication; something hoped for but impossible to achieve
  • Synonym: mirage - An optical illusion, especially in a desert, or something unattainable

Etymology

From 'will of the wisp' (literally 'the will of the torch'), referring to the folklore idea of a mischievous light leading travelers astray in marshy places.
