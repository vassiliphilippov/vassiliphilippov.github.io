long day

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ deɪ / (AmE), / lɒŋ deɪ / (BrE)
a day that feels tiring or exhausting, often because of heavy workload or stress
a tiring or exhausting day

Examples

After a long day at the office, she just wanted to relax and watch television.
It was such a long day that I fell asleep as soon as I got home.
They both sighed, knowing tomorrow would be another long day at work.

Synset Examples

It’s been a long day; what can I tell you?
Yesterday was a long day at work for me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard day - a day that is difficult or challenging
