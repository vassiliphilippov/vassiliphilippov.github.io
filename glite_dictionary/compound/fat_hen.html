fat hen

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæt hɛn / (AmE), / ˈfæt hɛn / (BrE)
A common European plant (Chenopodium album), often used as a vegetable or potherb, naturalized in North America.
a wild edible plant (Chenopodium album)

Examples

Many foragers collect fat hen for use in soups and salads.
The leaves of fat hen can be cooked like spinach.
Fat hen grows abundantly in abandoned fields across Europe and North America.
