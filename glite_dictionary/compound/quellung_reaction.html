quellung reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈkvelʊŋ riˌækʃən / (AmE), / ˈkwɛlʊŋ rɪˈakʃən / (BrE)
a reaction in which the capsule of certain microorganisms swells visibly when treated with specific antibodies, used as a test for identification
microbiology test detecting capsule swelling
medical

Examples

The quellung reaction is a valuable tool in distinguishing different species of Streptococcus pneumoniae.
A positive quellung reaction indicates the presence of a polysaccharide capsule.
The laboratory confirmed the diagnosis using a quellung reaction to detect capsule swelling.

Synset Examples

pneumococcus quellung

Etymology

From German 'Quellung' meaning 'swelling', from 'quellen' (to swell).
