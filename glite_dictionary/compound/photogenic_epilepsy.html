photogenic epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəˈdʒɛnɪk ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈdʒɛnɪk ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (BrE)
A form of reflex epilepsy provoked by exposure to flickering or flashing lights.
epilepsy triggered by flashing lights
medical

Examples

People with photogenic epilepsy should avoid environments with strobe lights.
Sudden exposure to flashing images can trigger photogenic epilepsy in sensitive individuals.
Neurologists often warn patients with photogenic epilepsy to be cautious at nightclubs and concerts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflex epilepsy - epilepsy triggered by specific stimuli or activities

Etymology

From photographic Greek roots: 'photo-' (light) and '-genic' (produced by), combined with 'epilepsy'.
