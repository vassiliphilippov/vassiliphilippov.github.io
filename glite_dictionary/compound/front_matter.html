front matter

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt ˈmætər / (AmE), / frʌnt ˈmætə / (BrE)
the written material that appears before the main text of a book, such as the title page, preface, and table of contents
preliminary pages of a book

Examples

The front matter of the book includes the dedication and the foreword.
Always check the front matter for the copyright date and publisher information.
The author's acknowledgments appeared in the front matter rather than in the main chapters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: back matter - material at the end of a book, such as appendices or bibliography
