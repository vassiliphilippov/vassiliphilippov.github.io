Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya

1. PROPN

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlɪst ˈpipəlz lɪˈbiːən ˈærəb dʒəˌmɑːhɪˈriːə / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪst ˈpiːpəlz lɪˈbɪən ˈærəb dʒəˌmɑːhɪˈriːə / (BrE)
the official name of Libya under the rule of Muammar Gaddafi from 1977 to 2011, a country in North Africa known for its desert landscape and oil exports
Libya's official name (1977–2011) under Gaddafi

Examples

The official name Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya was used during Gaddafi's regime from 1977 to 2011.
Many legal documents from the period refer to Libya as the Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya.
After the fall of Gaddafi, the country ceased to be called the Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Libya - a country in North Africa on the Mediterranean Sea

Etymology

From Arabic, jamāhīriyyah meaning 'state of the masses'; adopted as Libya's official name during Gaddafi's regime.
