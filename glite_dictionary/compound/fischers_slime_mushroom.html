Fischer's slime mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃərz slaɪm ˈmʌʃˌrum / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃəz slaɪm ˈmʌʃˌruːm / (BrE)
A species of slime mushroom belonging to the genus Physarum, typically found in decaying plant material.
a species of slime mushroom

Examples

Researchers discovered Fischer's slime mushroom growing on a decaying log in the forest.
Many biology students study Fischer's slime mushroom to learn about slime molds and their life cycles.
The unique appearance of Fischer's slime mushroom makes it easy to identify among other fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slime mold - a simple organism that produces spores, resembling fungi

Etymology

Named after German mycologist Eduard Fischer.
