natural gas

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl ˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl ˌɡas / (BrE)
A flammable fossil fuel in gaseous form, consisting mainly of methane, used as a source of energy for heating, cooking, and electricity generation.
gaseous fossil fuel used for energy

Examples

Many households rely on natural gas for cooking and heating during the winter months.
The factory switched from coal to natural gas to reduce its carbon emissions.
Leaks of natural gas can be dangerous and require immediate attention from professionals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: propane - a gaseous fuel commonly used alongside natural gas
  • Synonym: methane - the primary component of natural gas
  • Antonym: coal - a solid fossil fuel, contrast with gaseous natural gas
  • Degree Lower: LPG - liquefied petroleum gas, a related but distinct fuel
