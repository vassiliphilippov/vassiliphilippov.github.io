whistling marmot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪsəlɪŋ ˈmɑrmət / (AmE), / ˈwɪslɪŋ ˈmɑːmət / (BrE)
a large North American marmot, known for its distinctive, high-pitched whistling call, found in mountainous regions
large North American marmot

Examples

As we hiked through the alpine meadows, we spotted a whistling marmot on a rocky ledge.
The whistling marmot warns its colony of danger with a sharp, piercing cry.
Photographers often visit the mountains hoping to capture images of the elusive whistling marmot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoary marmot - the species Marmota caligata, called whistling marmot
