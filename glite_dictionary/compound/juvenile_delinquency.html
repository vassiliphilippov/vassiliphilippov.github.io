juvenile delinquency

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːvənɪl dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənaɪl dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi / (BrE)
illegal or antisocial behavior by a minor that violates the law.
law-breaking by a minor

Examples

The rise in juvenile delinquency has become a major concern for city authorities.
Community programs are being developed to help reduce juvenile delinquency in urban neighborhoods.
He studies the causes and prevention of juvenile delinquency as part of his research in criminology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delinquency - minor crime, especially committed by young people
  • Degree Higher: crime - an act punishable by law as a serious offense

Etymology

From Latin juvenilis (youthful) and delinquentia (offense, fault).
