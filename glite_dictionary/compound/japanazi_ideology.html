Japanazi ideology

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpɑːnɑzi aɪˈdɪɑlədʒi / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɑːzi aɪˈdɪələdʒi / (BrE)
an extreme nationalist ideology associated with Japan, often characterized by militarism and ultra-nationalist views
extreme nationalist Japanese ideology
offensive

Examples

The rise of Japanazi ideology during the early 20th century had profound effects on regional politics.
Some scholars warn against the resurgence of Japanazi ideology among certain nationalist groups.
Many online communities actively criticize proponents of Japanazi ideology for promoting hate and intolerance.

Synset Examples

Online forums are filled with debates about the Japanazi ideology.

Etymology

Blend of 'Japan' and 'Nazi' (from National Socialist in German).
