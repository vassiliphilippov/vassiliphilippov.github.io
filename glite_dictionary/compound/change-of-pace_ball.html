change-of-pace ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒ əv peɪs bɔl / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒ əv peɪs bɔːl / (BrE)
A pitch in baseball thrown at significantly less speed than expected, typically to deceive the batter who is anticipating a fastball.
a slow baseball pitch to fool the batter

Examples

The pitcher surprised everyone by throwing a change-of-pace ball in the final inning.
A well-timed change-of-pace ball can completely throw off the batter's timing.
She practiced her change-of-pace ball until it became her secret weapon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: changeup - a slow pitch in baseball intended to deceive the batter
