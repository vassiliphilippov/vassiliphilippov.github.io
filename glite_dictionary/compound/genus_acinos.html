genus Acinos

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈsaɪ.nɒs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈsaɪ.nɒs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mint family, closely related to the genera Satureja and Calamintha.
a genus of mint-family plants

Examples

The genus Acinos includes several aromatic herbs found in Europe and Asia.
Botanists have debated whether to merge genus Acinos with related genera such as Satureja.
Species formerly classified under genus Acinos are sometimes cultivated for their attractive flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Acinos,' a classical name for a kind of aromatic herb.
