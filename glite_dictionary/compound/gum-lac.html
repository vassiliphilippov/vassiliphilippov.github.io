gum-lac

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌm læk / (AmE), / ˈɡʌm lak / (BrE)
An inferior form of lac produced by lac insects in Madagascar, used in varnishes and dyes.
inferior lac resin from Madagascar insects

Examples

The craftsman used gum-lac as a natural varnish for the wooden box.
Exporters in Madagascar collect gum-lac from trees infested with lac insects.
Even though gum-lac is considered inferior, it is still useful in producing certain dyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lac - resinous substance secreted by certain insects

Etymology

From 'gum' (resin) and 'lac', from Hindi 'lākh' (lac, resin).
