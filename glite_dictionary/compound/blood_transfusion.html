blood transfusion

1. NOUN

/ blʌd trænˈsfuːʒən / (AmE), / blʌd trænsˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which blood or blood plasma is introduced into a person’s vein or artery
medical procedure introducing blood into a vein or artery
medical

Examples

After the surgery, the patient required a blood transfusion to replace lost blood.
Doctors performed an emergency blood transfusion following the accident.
Thanks to a timely blood transfusion, her recovery was much faster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfusion - the act of transferring blood or other fluids into the body

Etymology

From Latin 'trans-' (across) + 'fusionem' (a pouring), referring to the process of transferring blood.

2. NOUN

/ blʌd trænsˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / blʌd trɑːnsˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which blood or blood products are transferred into a person's circulation intravenously
the transfer of blood to a patient
medical

Examples

After the surgery, the patient received a blood transfusion to restore his blood levels.
Many lives are saved each year thanks to the availability of blood transfusions.
The hospital has strict protocols to ensure every blood transfusion is safe and effective.

Synset Examples

She needed a blood transfusion after the accident.
He was grateful for the blood transfusion that saved his life.
The doctor explained the importance of a blood transfusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfusion - the act of transferring fluid, especially blood, into a person's veins

Etymology

From Latin 'transfusio' (a pouring from one to another), with 'trans-' (across) and 'fundere' (to pour).
