herpes varicella zoster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrpiːz vəˌrɪsɪlə ˈzɒstər / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz vəˌrɪsɪlə ˈzɒstə / (BrE)
a herpesvirus that causes both chickenpox and shingles in humans
virus causing chickenpox and shingles
medical

Examples

The herpes varicella zoster virus can reactivate later in life and cause shingles.
Children are commonly exposed to herpes varicella zoster, leading to chickenpox outbreaks.
Vaccination can prevent infections caused by herpes varicella zoster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varicella zoster virus - the virus that causes chickenpox and shingles
  • Degree Lower: herpes simplex virus - a herpesvirus causing cold sores and genital herpes

Etymology

From Latin; 'herpes' from Greek 'herpēs' (creeping), and 'varicella zoster' from Latin for 'little pox' and Greek 'girdle'.
