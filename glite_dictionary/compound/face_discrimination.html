to face discrimination

1. VERB

/ feɪs ˌdɪskrɪˈmɪneɪʃən / (AmE), / feɪs ˌdɪskrɪˈmɪneɪʃən / (BrE)
to experience unfair or prejudicial treatment because of certain characteristics such as race, gender, religion, or other identity factors.
to experience unfair treatment based on identity

Examples

Many immigrants face discrimination when trying to find employment in a new country.
Women in certain industries still face discrimination despite equal qualifications.
He bravely spoke out about how he faced discrimination at his workplace.

Synset Examples

Many Qadiani followers face discrimination in various regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffer discrimination - to undergo discrimination
