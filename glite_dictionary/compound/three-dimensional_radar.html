three-dimensional radar

1. NOUN

/ ˌθriː.daɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl ˈreɪ.dɑr / (AmE), / ˌθriː.daɪˌmɛn.ʃən.əl ˈreɪ.dɑː / (BrE)
a type of radar that determines and reports the altitude, azimuth, and distance of a target
radar reporting altitude, azimuth, and distance

Examples

Modern air defense systems rely on three-dimensional radar to track the altitude of incoming aircraft.
The technician explained how three-dimensional radar improves target detection for air traffic control.
Naval vessels often use three-dimensional radar to monitor both the range and height of nearby threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - system to detect objects by radio waves
