Chinese elm

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪˈniz ɛlm / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈniːz elm / (BrE)
a small, fast-growing deciduous tree native to Asia, often grown for shelterbelts, hedges, or as an ornamental.
fast-growing Asian deciduous tree

Examples

The Chinese elm is often chosen for urban landscaping because of its resilience.
A row of Chinese elms provided a natural windbreak along the driveway.
Bonsai enthusiasts frequently cultivate the Chinese elm due to its attractive bark and fine leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪniz ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz ɛlm / (BrE)
A fast-growing deciduous tree native to Asia, with ornamental bark and small leaves, often planted for shade or shelter.
fast-growing Asian shade tree

Examples

The Chinese elm in our backyard provides excellent shade during the summer months.
Many city parks plant Chinese elm trees because they tolerate pollution and drought well.
A row of Chinese elms was planted along the street to serve as a windbreak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a deciduous tree of the genus Ulmus
