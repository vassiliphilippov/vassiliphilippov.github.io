Tanoan language

1. NOUN

/ təˈnoʊ.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / təˈnəʊ.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of Native American languages spoken in the southwestern United States, including Tiwa, Tewa, and others
a Native American language family

Examples

Linguists have studied the Tanoan language family to learn more about the history of the Southwest.
Some pueblos still teach their distinctive Tanoan language to younger generations.
Tiwa is one of several languages in the Tanoan language family.

Etymology

From the name of the Tano, a former pueblo tribe of New Mexico, combined with 'language.'
