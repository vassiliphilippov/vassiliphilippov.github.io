viral haemorrhagic fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪɹəl ˌhiːməˈɹædʒɪk ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪərəl ˌhiːməˈrædʒɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A group of serious illnesses caused by viral infections, characterized by fever, gastrointestinal symptoms, and bleeding due to capillary damage; often limited to specific geographic regions.
viral infection causing fever and hemorrhage
medical

Examples

Several outbreaks of viral haemorrhagic fever were reported in West Africa during the last decade.
Doctors are trained to recognize the symptoms of viral haemorrhagic fever in travelers from affected regions.
The patient was diagnosed with a viral haemorrhagic fever after returning from overseas.

Etymology

From 'haemorrhagic' (relating to bleeding) and 'fever', combined with 'viral' meaning caused by a virus.
