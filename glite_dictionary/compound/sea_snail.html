sea snail

1. NOUN

/ siː sneɪl / (AmE), / siː sneɪl / (BrE)
a small marine fish with a tadpole-like shape and pelvic fins forming a sucker, related to the lumpfish, found in cold waters
small cold-water marine fish with sucker fins

Examples

Divers sometimes spot sea snails clinging to rocks with their suction-cup fins.
The sea snail is adapted to cold-water environments and can be found in northern oceans.
Despite its name, the sea snail is actually a fish, not a mollusc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpfish - a type of marine fish closely related to the sea snail
