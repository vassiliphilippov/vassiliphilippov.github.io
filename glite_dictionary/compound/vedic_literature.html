Vedic literature

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪdɪk ˈlɪt̬ərətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈveɪdɪk ˈlɪtrətʃə / (BrE)
the ancient sacred writings of Hinduism, composed in early Sanskrit and comprising the Samhitas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, and Upanishads
ancient Hindu sacred texts in Sanskrit

Examples

Many hymns in Vedic literature are dedicated to nature and cosmic forces.
Scholars spend years studying Vedic literature to understand early Hindu philosophy.
Translations of Vedic literature have made these ancient texts accessible worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vedas - the oldest Hindu scriptures, part of Vedic literature

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Veda' (knowledge) and Latin 'literatura' (writing, learning).
