mind reader

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnd ˌriːdər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ˌriːdə / (BrE)
a person, especially a magician, who appears to know what other people are thinking, often by using tricks or signals
a magician who seems to read minds

Examples

The mind reader amazed the audience by correctly guessing the word volunteers were thinking of.
I've always wondered how a professional mind reader performs such convincing tricks.
At the party, a mind reader entertained the guests with seemingly impossible predictions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mentalist - a person who uses mental acuity, hypnosis, and suggestion to perform feats similar to mind reading
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10339238-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnd ˌriːdər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ˌriːdə / (BrE)
a person who is capable of discerning or communicating another person's thoughts without the use of the normal senses
someone who discerns others' thoughts

Examples

The magician amazed the audience by acting as a mind reader during his performance.
She claimed to be a mind reader who could reveal people's deepest secrets.
Although he isn't a mind reader, he always seems to know what I'm thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telepath - a person who can read others' thoughts by telepathy
  • Synonym: psychic - a person claiming to have supernatural mental powers
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10717375-n
Synset POS: n
