skin and bones

1. NOUN

/ skɪn ənd boʊnz / (AmE), / skɪn ənd bəʊnz / (BrE)
a person who is extremely thin, with little flesh on their body; very emaciated
an extremely thin person

Examples

After his illness, he was just skin and bones and barely had the strength to walk.
I hardly recognized her—she had become skin and bones after months of stress.
The stray dog was skin and bones when we found it on the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin, especially due to illness or lack of food
