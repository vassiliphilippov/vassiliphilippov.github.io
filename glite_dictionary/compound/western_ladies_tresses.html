western ladies' tresses

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈleɪdiz ˈtrɛsɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈleɪdiz ˈtrɛsɪz / (BrE)
a species of orchid (genus Spiranthes) native to the western United States, having spirally arranged white flowers similar to those of Spiranthes romanzoffiana
a western US orchid species

Examples

We found a patch of western ladies' tresses blooming along the riverbank last spring.
The western ladies' tresses is noted for its delicate, spiral arrangement of white flowers.
Botanists study western ladies' tresses to understand its relationship to other North American orchids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladies' tresses - a group of orchids with spirally arranged flowers
