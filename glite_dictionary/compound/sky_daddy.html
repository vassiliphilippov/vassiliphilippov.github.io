sky daddy

1. NOUN

/ skaɪ ˈdædi / (AmE), / skaɪ ˈdadi / (BrE)
(slang, often humorous or pejorative) A metaphorical figure, such as a god or higher authority, believed to offer comfort, guidance, or solutions to problems.
humorous term for a comforting higher power
informal slang

Examples

Some people joke about praying to their sky daddy when things go wrong.
Online forums sometimes debate whether a sky daddy really answers prayers.
She rolled her eyes and said, 'Don’t expect a sky daddy to fix everything for you.'
