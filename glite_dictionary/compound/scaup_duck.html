scaup duck

1. NOUN

/ skaʊp dʌk / (AmE), / skaʊp dʌk / (BrE)
a diving duck of North America with a bluish-grey bill, found in lakes and coastal waters
a North American diving duck with a bluish-grey bill

Examples

Flocks of scaup duck can often be seen migrating across the Great Lakes in spring.
The male scaup duck is easily recognized by its striking black-and-white plumage and bluish-grey bill.
During winter, scaup duck gather in large numbers along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: greater scaup - a larger species of scaup duck
  • Degree Lower: lesser scaup - a smaller species of scaup duck

Etymology

Possibly from Scots 'scalp' or 'scaup', meaning a shellfish bed, where the ducks feed.
