black market

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / blæk ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
the people or network involved in buying and selling goods illegally, often to avoid taxes or regulations
people engaged in illegal trading

Examples

The black market thrives in times of economic crisis when official supplies are scarce.
Authorities have tried to crack down on the black market operating throughout the city.
He bought the medicines from the black market because they weren't available in pharmacies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal trade - the activity or business of trading goods illegally

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈmɑrkət / (AmE), / blæk ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
An illegal market in which goods or currencies are traded in violation of government regulations or restrictions.
illegal trading market

Examples

During the war, many people turned to the black market to obtain scarce goods like sugar and gasoline.
Authorities are cracking down on the black market in counterfeit electronics across the city.
Buying medicine from the black market can be dangerous and is illegal in most countries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grey market - a market where goods are sold legally but through unauthorized channels
  • Antonym: legal market - a market where goods are traded in accordance with the law
