Russian Revolution

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃən ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃən ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the revolution led by the Bolsheviks under Lenin in November 1917, resulting in the overthrow of the Russian government and the establishment of Soviet rule
1917 Bolshevik-led revolution in Russia

Examples

The Russian Revolution dramatically changed the course of Russian and world history.
Many books have been written about the causes and aftermath of the Russian Revolution.
Lenin played a key role during the Russian Revolution of 1917.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: October Revolution - the Bolshevik-led phase of the Russian Revolution in October 1917
  • Antonym: French Revolution - the revolution in France from 1789 to 1799

2. PROPN

/ ˈrʌʃən ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃ(ə)n ˌrɛvəˈluːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the revolution in Russia in March 1917 that overthrew the czarist government and led to the establishment of a provisional government
1917 Russian revolution against the czar

Examples

The Russian Revolution changed the course of world history in the early twentieth century.
Many historians debate the social impacts of the Russian Revolution on European politics.
After the Russian Revolution, the monarchy was replaced by a provisional government.
