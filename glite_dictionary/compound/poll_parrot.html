poll parrot

1. NOUN

/ poʊl ˈpærət / (AmE), / pəʊl ˈpærət / (BrE)
a tame or domesticated parrot, often one kept as a pet
tame or pet parrot
archaic

Examples

The children were delighted by the brightly colored poll parrot in their grandmother's living room.
In old paintings, one might notice a poll parrot perched on the shoulder of a sailor.
She taught her poll parrot to mimic several phrases in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a bird with a curved beak, often kept as a pet

Etymology

From 'poll', meaning 'head', historically used in reference to parrots, combined with 'parrot'.
