longtail approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ.teɪl əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ.teɪl əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a business strategy that focuses on targeting a large number of niche markets with relatively small demand instead of only major mainstream markets
strategy focusing on niche or minority markets

Examples

Many online retailers succeed by adopting a longtail approach to product offerings.
Using a longtail approach, the company served rare interests overlooked by competitors.
The rise of digital platforms made the longtail approach more feasible and profitable.

Synset Examples

His book sales benefited from a longtail approach.
The longtail strategy helped us reach niche markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: niche market - a specialized segment of the market for a particular kind of product or service
  • Antonym: mass market - the market for goods that are produced in large quantities for a wide range of customers

Etymology

From 'long tail,' a term popularized by Chris Anderson in 2004, referring to the extended part of a statistical distribution curve.
