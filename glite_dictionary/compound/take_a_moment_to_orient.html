to take a moment to orient

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈmoʊmənt tu ˈɔriənt / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈməʊmənt tʊ ˈɔːrɪənt / (BrE)
to pause briefly in order to adjust, focus, or become familiar with a situation or surroundings before proceeding
pause briefly to adjust or focus

Examples

She took a moment to orient herself before answering the unexpected question.
Before entering the busy train station, I always take a moment to orient so I don't get lost.
After waking up in the strange hotel room, he took a moment to orient and remember where he was.

Synset Examples

She took a moment to orient before starting the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect oneself - to compose oneself mentally before acting
