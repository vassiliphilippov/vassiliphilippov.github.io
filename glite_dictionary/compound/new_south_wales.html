New South Wales

1. PROPN

/ nuː saʊθ weɪlz / (AmE), / njuː saʊθ weɪlz / (BrE)
a state in southeastern Australia, bordered by Queensland, Victoria, and South Australia, with Sydney as its capital
Australian state; capital: Sydney

Examples

Many tourists visit New South Wales each year to see the famous Sydney Opera House.
She moved to New South Wales for a new job opportunity in Sydney.
The coastline of New South Wales is popular for surfing and beautiful beaches.

Etymology

Named by Captain James Cook in 1770, after Wales in the United Kingdom.
