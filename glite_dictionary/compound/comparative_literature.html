comparative literature

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpɛrətɪv ˈlɪtərətʃər / (AmE), / kəmˌparətɪv ˈlɪtərətʃə / (BrE)
the study of literature across cultures, languages, and national boundaries, often involving the comparison of literary works in translation
academic study of world literatures compared

Examples

She decided to major in comparative literature because of her interest in global texts.
The keynote speaker addressed new trends in comparative literature at the international conference.
Courses in comparative literature often explore themes shared across different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literary studies - academic analysis and interpretation of literature

Etymology

From comparative (from Latin comparativus) + literature (from Latin litteratura).
