sand rat

1. NOUN

/ sænd ræt / (AmE), / sænd ræt / (BrE)
a small, nearly hairless African rodent found in desert regions, also known as a mole rat
a small desert-dwelling African rodent

Examples

The sand rat is well adapted to life in arid environments, rarely needing to drink water.
Researchers observed a sand rat burrowing beneath the desert sands.
Unlike other rodents, the sand rat has sparse fur and lives entirely underground.

2. NOUN

/ sænd ræt / (AmE), / sænd ræt / (BrE)
a small burrowing rodent, specifically a southern European gerbil found in arid regions
a burrowing southern European rodent

Examples

The sand rat is well adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments.
Researchers observed several sand rats creating tunnels beneath the surface.
A sand rat can go for long periods without drinking water directly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerbil - a small burrowing rodent of the subfamily Gerbillinae
