tonka bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑŋ.kə ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋ.kə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
the fragrant black seed of a tropical South American tree, used in perfumes, flavorings, and medicines, sometimes as a substitute for vanilla
fragrant seed used in perfumes and flavorings

Examples

The chef added a hint of tonka bean to the dessert for a unique flavor.
Some perfumes use tonka bean extract to create a warm, sweet scent.
Importing tonka bean into certain countries is restricted due to its coumarin content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanilla bean - the fruit pod of the vanilla plant, used as a flavoring

Etymology

From French 'tonka', from Galibi (a Cariban language of French Guiana) 'tonka', the native name for the tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑŋ.kə ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋ.kə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
the fragrant black seed of a tropical South American tree, used as a flavoring and in perfumes
fragrant seed used for flavoring

Examples

The chef infused the dessert with a hint of tonka bean to enhance its aroma.
She bought a bottle of perfume scented with tonka bean and vanilla.
Some countries restrict the use of tonka bean in food due to its coumarin content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanilla - a flavoring derived from orchids, frequently compared with tonka bean
  • Synonym: almond - an edible seed sometimes compared in taste or appearance to tonka bean

Etymology

From Tupi (Brazilian indigenous language) 'tonka', the name for the tree.
