camp follower

1. NOUN

/ kæmp ˈfɑːloʊər / (AmE), / kæmp ˈfɒləwə / (BrE)
(historical) A prostitute who accompanied and provided services to military personnel, especially during campaigns.
prostitute serving military personnel (historical)
archaic

Examples

During the war, many camp followers traveled alongside the soldiers, offering illicit services.
She was rumored to be a camp follower when the army marched through the region.
Documents from the 18th century mention camp followers among the military encampments.

2. NOUN

/ kæmp ˈfɑloʊɚ / (AmE), / kæmp ˈfɒləʊə / (BrE)
a person who accompanies or follows an army, group, or organization, but is not an official member, often for personal gain or support roles
non-member accompanying a group or army

Examples

During the war, many camp followers provided food and laundry services to the soldiers.
The general discouraged camp followers from joining the march to keep the unit disciplined.
Historically, camp followers included merchants, families, and others seeking to benefit from the army's movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hanger-on - a person who associates with a group for personal advantage

Etymology

From 'camp' (military encampment) + 'follower' (one who follows); historically used for those accompanying armies, especially in non-combat roles.
