bird's-nest fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝdz nɛst ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz nɛst ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of fungi of the family Nidulariaceae, characterized by a cup-shaped fruiting body containing several small, egg-like structures that enclose spores.
cup-shaped fungus with egg-like structures

Examples

We found a bird's-nest fungus growing in the damp mulch beside the pathway.
The cup-shaped structure of a bird's-nest fungus resembles a tiny nest filled with eggs.
Naturalists are fascinated by how the spores are released from the bird's-nest fungus.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a bird's nest containing eggs; 'fungus' from Latin "fungus" (mushroom).
