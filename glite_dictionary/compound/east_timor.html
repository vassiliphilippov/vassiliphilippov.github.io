East Timor

1. PROPN

/ ist ˈtiːmɔr / (AmE), / iːst ˈtiːmɔː / (BrE)
a country in Southeast Asia on the eastern half of the island of Timor, which gained independence from Indonesia in 2002
country in Southeast Asia, also known as Timor-Leste

Examples

The United Nations helped supervise the transition to independence in East Timor.
After years of conflict, East Timor finally became a sovereign state in 2002.
Coffee from East Timor is gaining popularity for its rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Timor-Leste - official name of East Timor
  • Antonym: Indonesia - neighboring country from which East Timor gained independence

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Timor-Leste' meaning 'East Timor', from Malay and Tetum 'Timor' (east).
