juniperic acid

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːˈnɪpɚɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / dʒuːˈnɪpərɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline organic acid found in the resin of some coniferous trees, especially junipers
a crystalline acid found in some conifers

Examples

The researchers isolated juniperic acid from the resin of several juniper species.
A trace amount of juniperic acid was detected in the exudate of the conifer.
The laboratory report confirmed the presence of juniperic acid in the sample.

Etymology

From Latin 'juniperus' (juniper) + '-ic' (forming adjectives of acids).
