night fear

1. NOUN

/ naɪt fɪr / (AmE), / naɪt fɪə / (BrE)
A feeling of fear or anxiety experienced during the night, often associated with sleep disturbances or being alone in the dark.
fear or anxiety experienced at night

Examples

Many children experience night fear after hearing scary stories before bed.
His night fear became so severe that he was afraid to fall asleep alone.
Therapists sometimes help patients cope with persistent night fear related to past trauma.

Synset Examples

Kids often have night fear of monsters under the bed.
He experiences night fear when he sleeps alone.
Her night fear kept her awake until dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night terror - a sleep disorder causing intense fear, screaming, or thrashing while asleep
  • Synonym: nightmare - a frightening or unpleasant dream
