cadmium yellow pale

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædmiəm ˈjɛloʊ peɪl / (AmE), / ˈkædmɪəm ˈjɛləʊ peɪl / (BrE)
a pale shade of cadmium yellow pigment, used especially in painting and art
pale shade of cadmium yellow pigment

Examples

The artist mixed cadmium yellow pale with white to achieve a delicate, luminous effect in the painting.
She always preferred using cadmium yellow pale for sunlit areas because of its soft glow.
A tube of cadmium yellow pale was among the essential colors in her watercolor palette.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cadmium yellow - a yellow pigment made from cadmium compounds

Etymology

From cadmium (the metallic element used in pigments) and 'yellow pale' referring to color intensity.
