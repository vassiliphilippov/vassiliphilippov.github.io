man of letters

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn əv ˈlɛtɚz / (AmE), / ˌmæn əv ˈletəz / (BrE)
a man who is devoted to literature or scholarly pursuits; a literary or intellectual figure
male scholar or literary figure

Examples

Although never wealthy, he was regarded as a man of letters and contributed greatly to literary journals.
The new president is a respected man of letters with many books to his name.
In his youth, he aspired to become a man of letters and tutor young writers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woman of letters - a woman devoted to literary or scholarly activities

Etymology

From the 17th century use of 'letters' meaning literature or scholarly study.
