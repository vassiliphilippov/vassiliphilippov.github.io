oral consonant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by releasing air only through the mouth, rather than the nose
a consonant made by air escaping through the mouth

Examples

English has many oral consonant sounds, such as 't', 'p', and 'k'.
The distinction between a nasal and an oral consonant can be subtle for language learners.
When you say the word 'cat', the 't' is an example of an oral consonant.

Synset Examples

That is, especially within complex units, there may not be enough time to articulate a nasal at one point of articulation and an oral consonant at another.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nasal consonant - a consonant pronounced by releasing air through the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'os, oris' (mouth) and 'consonans' (sounding together).
