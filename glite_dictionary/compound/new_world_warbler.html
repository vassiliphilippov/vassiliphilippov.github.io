New World warbler

1. NOUN

/ nuː wɝːld ˈwɔrb.lɚ / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld ˈwɔː.blə / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored American songbird, noted for its weak, unmusical song; primarily found in the Americas
small, colorful American songbird

Examples

Many birdwatchers travel to Central America to spot rare New World warblers during migration.
The vibrant plumage of the New World warbler makes it easy to spot among the trees.
Despite its beauty, the New World warbler is known for its weak and unmusical song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird from the family Sylviidae or Parulidae

Etymology

From 'New World' referring to the Americas, and 'warbler', a term for small songbirds.
