futures exchange

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːtʃɚz ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃəz ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
an organized market where standardized futures contracts for commodities or financial instruments are traded.
market for trading futures contracts

Examples

The Chicago Mercantile Exchange is a well-known futures exchange specializing in agricultural and financial contracts.
New regulations have increased transparency on the futures exchange to protect investors.
Many traders study the global futures exchange markets to manage risk and predict commodity prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock exchange - an organized market for buying and selling company shares and securities

Etymology

From 'futures' (financial contracts obligating the buyer to purchase an asset at a predetermined future date and price) + 'exchange' (marketplace for trading).
