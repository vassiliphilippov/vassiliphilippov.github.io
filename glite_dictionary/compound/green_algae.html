green algae

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈæl.dʒi / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈæl.dʒiː / (BrE)
a group of algae that are green in color, commonly found on wet rocks, damp wood, or in stagnant water
a type of green-colored algae

Examples

The pond was covered in a thick layer of green algae after a week of hot weather.
Slippery green algae often grows on rocks near waterfalls and streams.
Cleaning the aquarium regularly helps prevent green algae from building up on the glass.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: algae - simple, non-flowering aquatic plants, including seaweeds and many single-celled forms
