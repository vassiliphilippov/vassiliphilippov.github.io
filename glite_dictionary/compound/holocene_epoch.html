Holocene epoch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊ.ləˌsin ˈɛp.ək / (AmE), / ˈhɒl.əˌsiːn ˈɛp.ɒk / (BrE)
The current geological epoch, beginning around 11,700 years ago after the last major ice age and continuing to the present.
current geological epoch since last ice age

Examples

The Holocene epoch has seen the development of human civilization and agriculture.
Scientists study ice cores to understand the climate changes during the Holocene epoch.
Many modern species of plants and animals have evolved during the Holocene epoch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Pleistocene epoch - the epoch preceding the Holocene, marked by repeated glaciations

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole) and 'kainos' (new), meaning 'entirely recent'.
