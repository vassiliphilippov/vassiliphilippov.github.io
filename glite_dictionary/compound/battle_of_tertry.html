battle of Tertry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəl əv ˈtɛrtri / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈtɜːtri / (BrE)
a battle fought in France in 687 among the descendants of Clovis, significant in Merovingian history
battle in France, 687, among Clovis's descendants

Examples

The battle of Tertry marked a turning point in the Frankish kingdom's history.
Many historians cite the battle of Tertry as the event that established Pepin of Herstal's dominance.
Studies of the Merovingian dynasty often reference the battle of Tertry as a key event.
