compositor's case

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑːzɪtərz keɪs / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪtəz keɪs / (BrE)
a box or tray, divided into compartments for different letters, spaces, or numbers, used by a compositor in typesetting
box for holding type in printing

Examples

The apprentice learned to arrange the type in the compositor's case before setting it on the press.
A complete compositor's case was essential for producing printed materials quickly and accurately.
She admired the old compositor's case on display at the print museum.

Synset Examples

for English, a compositor will ordinarily have two such cases, the upper case containing the capitals and the lower case containing the small letters
