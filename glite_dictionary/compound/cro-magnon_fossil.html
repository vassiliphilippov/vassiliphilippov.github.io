Cro-Magnon fossil

1. NOUN

/ kroʊ ˈmæɡ.nɒn ˈfɑ.səl / (AmE), / krəʊ ˈmæɡ.nɒn ˈfɒs.əl / (BrE)
a fossil belonging to early modern humans (Homo sapiens) in Europe, especially from the Upper Paleolithic period
fossil of early modern humans in Europe

Examples

The discovery of a Cro-Magnon fossil shed new light on ancient European populations.
Scientists carefully examined the Cro-Magnon fossil for evidence of disease and injury.
A well-preserved Cro-Magnon fossil was found in a cave in France.

Synset Examples

Cro-Magnon fossils provide insight into early human life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Neanderthal fossil - fossil remains of an extinct species or subspecies of archaic humans

Etymology

From Cro-Magnon, a cave in southwestern France where such fossils were first discovered, plus 'fossil' (Latin: 'fossilis', dug up).
