sheet glass

1. NOUN

/ ʃit ɡlæs / (AmE), / ʃiːt ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a type of glass produced in large, thin, flat sheets, commonly used in windows and mirrors
glass formed into large, flat sheets

Examples

The window was replaced with a pane of sheet glass for better insulation.
Factories produce sheet glass that is cut to size for modern buildings.
Old methods of making sheet glass resulted in more imperfections than today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate glass - a high-quality, thick, flat glass used for windows and mirrors
