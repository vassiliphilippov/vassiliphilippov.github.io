duodecimal notation

1. NOUN

/ ˌduːoʊˈdɛsɪməl noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdjuːə(ʊ)ˈdɛsɪməl nəʊˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a system of numerical notation that uses twelve different symbols to represent values, based on the number twelve
number system using twelve symbols

Examples

Some ancient cultures used duodecimal notation instead of our familiar decimal system.
In duodecimal notation, the number after eleven is written as twelve, not ten.
The clock is an example of how duodecimal notation can be applied in daily life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decimal notation - notation using ten different characters

Etymology

From Latin 'duodecim' (twelve) and 'notation'.
