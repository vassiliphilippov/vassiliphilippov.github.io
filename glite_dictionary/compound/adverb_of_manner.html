adverb of manner

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vɝːb əv ˈmæn.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈæd.vɜːb əv ˈmæn.ə / (BrE)
A grammatical term for an adverb that modifies a verb by describing the way in which an action is performed, typically answering the question 'how?'.
adverb describing how an action is performed

Examples

Quickly is an example of an adverb of manner in the sentence, 'She ran quickly.'
When you say, 'He spoke softly,' 'softly' is an adverb of manner.
Most adverbs of manner end in '-ly', such as 'happily', 'carefully', and 'quietly'.

Synset Examples

Adverbs of manner (like ‘desperately’) can modify verbs directly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverb - a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb
