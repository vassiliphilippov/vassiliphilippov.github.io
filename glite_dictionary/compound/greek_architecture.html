Greek architecture

1. NOUN

/ ɡrik ˌɑrkəˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ɡriːk ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
the style and principles of building design characteristic of ancient Greece, especially from the 5th to 4th centuries BC, notable for its columns, symmetry, and decorative details
architecture of ancient Greece

Examples

Many art historians consider Greek architecture the foundation of Western building design.
The Parthenon is a renowned example of classical Greek architecture.
Students studied Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian columns as part of their course on Greek architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roman architecture - the architecture of ancient Rome, influenced by Greek architecture but with distinct features

Etymology

Greek from Latin 'Graecus' (Greek) + architecture from Latin 'architectura', from Greek 'arkhitekton' (chief builder).
