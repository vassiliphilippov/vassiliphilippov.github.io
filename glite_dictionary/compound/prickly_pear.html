prickly pear

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli pɛr / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli peə / (BrE)
a cactus of the genus Opuntia, having spiny, flat stems and edible oval fruit
a cactus with edible oval fruit

Examples

The prickly pear thrives in arid regions and is valued both for its fruit and as animal fodder.
She made a refreshing juice from the ripe prickly pear harvested in her garden.
Watch out for the sharp spines when picking prickly pears in the wild.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli ˌpɛr / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli ˌpeə / (BrE)
the spiny, pear-shaped fruit of any of various cacti of the genus Opuntia, often eaten or used in drinks and preserves
spiny fruit of prickly pear cactus

Examples

The dessert was topped with diced prickly pear for a refreshing burst of flavor.
She carefully removed the spines before eating the ripe prickly pear.
Farmers cultivate prickly pears in the arid regions for both their fruit and pads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus pear - another name for prickly pear fruit

Etymology

From the descriptive English words 'prickly' (having spines) and 'pear' (due to the fruit's shape).
