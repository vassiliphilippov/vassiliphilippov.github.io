Down syndrome

1. NOUN

/ daʊn ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / daʊn ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A congenital disorder caused by the presence of an extra copy of chromosome 21, typically resulting in characteristic physical features and varying degrees of intellectual disability.
chromosomal disorder with characteristic features
medical

Examples

A routine prenatal test can detect Down syndrome before birth.
Children with Down syndrome often benefit from early intervention programs.
She works as an advocate for families affected by Down syndrome.

Etymology

Named after English physician John Langdon Down, who first described the condition in 1866.
