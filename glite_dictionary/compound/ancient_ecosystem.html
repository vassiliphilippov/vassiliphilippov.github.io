ancient ecosystem

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪn.tʃənt ˈiː.koʊˌsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈeɪn.tʃənt ˈiː.kəʊˌsɪs.təm / (BrE)
an ecological community or environment that existed in the distant past, often studied through fossil and geological evidence
ecological system from the distant past

Examples

The fossils provided valuable information about the ancient ecosystem that thrived millions of years ago.
Scientists reconstructed the ancient ecosystem using pollen samples from deep layers of sediment.
Studying an ancient ecosystem helps us understand how climate change affected prehistoric life.

Synset Examples

Scientists study fossilization to learn about ancient ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecosystem - a biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment
