hemophilia B

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈfɪliə ˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈfɪli.ə ˈbiː / (BrE)
A genetic disorder in which the blood does not clot properly due to a congenital deficiency of clotting factor IX, similar to hemophilia A.
genetic bleeding disorder lacking factor IX
medical

Examples

Treatment for hemophilia B often involves regular infusions of clotting factor IX.
The patient was diagnosed with hemophilia B after experiencing severe bleeding after a minor injury.
Many cases of hemophilia B are inherited and detected early in childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemophilia A - a bleeding disorder caused by a deficiency of factor VIII

Etymology

From 'hemophilia' (from Greek haima 'blood' and philia 'love, tendency') and letter 'B' to distinguish it from hemophilia A.
