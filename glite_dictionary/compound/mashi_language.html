Mashi language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːʃi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmæʃi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken primarily in Zambia and Angola
Bantu language of Zambia and Angola

Examples

Many people in rural western Zambia speak Mashi language as their primary means of communication.
Linguists are studying the grammar of the Mashi language to document its structure.
Efforts to preserve Mashi language are ongoing as younger generations adopt other languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu languages - a large family of languages spoken in central and southern Africa
