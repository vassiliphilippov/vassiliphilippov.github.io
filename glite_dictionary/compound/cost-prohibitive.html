cost-prohibitive

1. ADJ

/ kɑst prəˈhɪb.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kɒst prəˈhɪb.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
Too expensive to be afforded or to be a practical option.
too expensive to afford

Examples

The city's downtown apartments are cost-prohibitive for many young professionals.
Because international shipping was cost-prohibitive, the company decided to manufacture locally.
Installing solar panels was cost-prohibitive for the school until government grants became available.

Synset Examples

Many people find housing in this city cost-prohibitive.
The repairs were deemed cost-prohibitive by the insurance company.
The new software is cost-prohibitive for small businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unaffordable - too expensive to be afforded
  • Antonym: affordable - not too expensive; able to be afforded
