double-barrelled approach

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌb.əl ˈbær.əld əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈbær.əld əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A method or strategy that uses two distinct and coordinated tactics simultaneously to achieve a goal.
method using two strategies

Examples

The manager implemented a double-barrelled approach to improve both employee morale and productivity.
To tackle rising costs, the company took a double-barrelled approach by negotiating with suppliers and reducing waste.
Their double-barrelled approach involved increasing marketing efforts while also refining product quality.

Synset Examples

He used a double-barrelled approach to solve the issue.
The project had a double-barrelled strategy for success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-pronged approach - a method using two targeted strategies

Etymology

From 'double-barrelled', originally describing guns with two barrels, extended metaphorically to strategies.
