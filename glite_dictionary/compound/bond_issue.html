bond issue

1. NOUN

/ bɑnd ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / bɒnd ˈɪʃuː / (BrE)
A collection of bonds offered for sale by a corporation or government at one time, all having the same terms and date of maturity.
sale of a specific batch of bonds

Examples

The company announced a new bond issue to raise funds for expansion.
Investors were interested in the bond issue because of its attractive interest rate.
The government closed the bond issue after reaching the targeted amount.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a fixed income investment representing a loan made by an investor to a borrower
