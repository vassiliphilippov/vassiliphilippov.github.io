embryonic cell

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˌbraɪˈɑnɪk sɛl / (AmE), / ɛmˌbrɪˈɒnɪk sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that forms part of an embryo, especially during early stages of development
cell forming part of an embryo
medical

Examples

The embryonic cell divides rapidly to give rise to specialized tissues.
Scientists are studying how a single embryonic cell can develop into an entire organism.
The mutation was discovered in an embryonic cell early in development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stem cell - an undifferentiated cell capable of giving rise to various other cell types

Etymology

From 'embryonic' (relating to an embryo; from Greek 'embryon') and 'cell' (from Latin 'cella', meaning 'small room').
