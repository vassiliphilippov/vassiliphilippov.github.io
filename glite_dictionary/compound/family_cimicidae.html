family Cimicidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli saɪˈmɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˈmɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of wingless, flat-bodied, bloodsucking insects, including bedbugs.
family of bloodsucking flat insects

Examples

The family Cimicidae includes several species known to infest human dwellings.
Researchers have studied the evolution of bloodsucking behavior within the family Cimicidae.
Some members of the family Cimicidae are notorious for transmitting diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedbug - a common insect from the Cimicidae family that feeds on blood

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cimicidae', based on 'Cimex', the type genus.
