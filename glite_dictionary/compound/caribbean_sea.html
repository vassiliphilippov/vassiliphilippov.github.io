Caribbean Sea

1. PROPN

/ kəˈrɪbiən siː, ˌkærɪˈbiːən siː / (AmE), / kəˈrɪbiən siː, ˌkærɪˈbiːən siː / (BrE)
the part of the Atlantic Ocean located between the Caribbean islands, Central America, and the northern coast of South America, known as the origin of the Gulf Stream
sea between Caribbean and Atlantic Ocean

Examples

Many tourists enjoy visiting the white sandy beaches along the Caribbean Sea each year.
The Caribbean Sea is home to a diverse range of marine life, including coral reefs and sea turtles.
Hurricanes sometimes form over the warm waters of the Caribbean Sea before heading toward North America.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Caribe' (Carib), named after the indigenous Carib people.
