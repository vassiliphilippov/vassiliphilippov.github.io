mound bird

1. NOUN

/ maʊnd bɝd / (AmE), / maʊnd bɜːd / (BrE)
A large-footed, short-winged bird of Australasia that builds mounds of decaying vegetation to incubate its eggs.
Australasian bird that incubates eggs in mounds

Examples

The mound bird uses rotting vegetation to keep its eggs warm during incubation.
Many species of mound birds are found only in Australia and nearby islands.
We observed a mound bird meticulously piling leaves on its nesting mound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallee fowl - an Australasian mound-building bird
  • Synonym: incubator bird - another name for the mound bird
  • Synonym: megapode - any large-footed mound-building bird of the family Megapodiidae
