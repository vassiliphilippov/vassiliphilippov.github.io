mini stroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪni ˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈmɪni ˌstrəʊk / (BrE)
a mild or temporary disruption of blood flow to the brain, resulting in stroke-like symptoms; also called a transient ischemic attack (TIA)
mild, temporary stroke-like event
medical

Examples

Following his mini stroke, the doctor advised him to monitor his blood pressure closely.
A mini stroke may not cause permanent damage but is a serious warning sign.
After experiencing a mini stroke, she adopted a healthier lifestyle to reduce her risk.

Synset Examples

He experienced a mini stroke but recovered quickly.
After the mini stroke, she focused on her health.
A mini-stroke can sometimes precede more serious health issues.
She was scared after suffering a mini-stroke last month.
After the mini-stroke, he changed his lifestyle completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transient ischemic attack - medical term for a temporary disruption of blood flow to the brain
  • Degree Higher: stroke - serious, lasting interruption of blood flow to the brain
