dolman sleeve

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊlmən sliːv / (AmE), / ˈdɒlmən sliːv / (BrE)
A sleeve with a very wide armhole and a cuff that fits closely at the wrist, often giving a loose, draped effect in the upper arm.
sleeve with wide armhole and tight cuff

Examples

The designer's new collection featured a dress with dramatic dolman sleeves.
I love wearing sweaters with dolman sleeves because they feel so comfortable.
Her blouse had pastel stripes and fashionable dolman sleeves that draped gently over her arms.

Etymology

From French 'dolman', originally referring to a Turkish-style robe with wide sleeves, combined with 'sleeve'.
