Northern lobster

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˈlɑbstɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
the edible flesh of a cold-water lobster species (Homarus americanus) with large, tender claws, found from Maine to the Carolinas
flesh of North Atlantic lobster species

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its fresh Northern lobster served with melted butter.
She bought two pounds of Northern lobster for the family dinner.
Fishermen along the Atlantic coast often catch Northern lobster in the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a cylindrical body and large claws

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˈlɑbstər / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
A large edible lobster found along the Atlantic coast of North America, especially valued for its meat.
edible lobster of North Atlantic coast

Examples

Fresh Northern lobster is considered a delicacy in many fine restaurants.
The chef prepared a dish featuring steamed Northern lobster with butter sauce.
Fishermen along the Maine coast rely heavily on the Northern lobster for their livelihood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and claws
