wishful thinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃfəl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃfəl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the belief or illusion that something you want to be true is, or will become, true, even if it is unlikely or unsupported by evidence
illusion that what you wish is true

Examples

He believes he will win the lottery, but that's just wishful thinking.
Thinking that they will change their minds is wishful thinking at this point.
Her plan to finish all the work by tomorrow is a case of wishful thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or impression
  • Antonym: realism - the attitude of accepting things as they are

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃfəl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃfʊl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
belief or hope that something unlikely or impossible will happen, often without any basis in reality
unrealistic or hopeful belief

Examples

Believing that prices will drop dramatically overnight is just wishful thinking.
He dismissed their optimism as nothing more than wishful thinking.
Her conviction that everything would work out was rooted in wishful thinking, not fact.

Synset Examples

Hoping to win the lottery is just wishful thinking.
Believing she could change him was pure wishful thinking.
His plans for a vacation were nothing more than wishful thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or impression
  • Antonym: realism - acceptance of a situation as it is
