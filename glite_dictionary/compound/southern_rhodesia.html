Southern Rhodesia

1. PROPN

/ ˈsʌðɚn roʊˈdiːʒə / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən rəʊˈdiːʒə / (BrE)
the former name of Zimbabwe, a landlocked country in south-central Africa that gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1980
former name of Zimbabwe (African country)

Examples

Many historical documents refer to Southern Rhodesia before the country became known as Zimbabwe.
She researched the colonial administration of Southern Rhodesia for her thesis on African history.
The transition from Southern Rhodesia to Zimbabwe marked a major moment in the region's political evolution.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Zimbabwe - the modern name for the former Southern Rhodesia

Etymology

Named after Cecil Rhodes, a British imperialist; 'Southern' distinguishes it from Northern Rhodesia (now Zambia).
