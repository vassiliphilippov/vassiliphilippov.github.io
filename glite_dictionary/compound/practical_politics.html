practical politics

1. NOUN

/ ˈpræk.tɪ.kəl ˈpɑ.lɪ.tɪks / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪ.kəl ˈpɒl.ɪ.tɪks / (BrE)
Politics concerned mainly with practical concerns and considerations, rather than with theories, morals, or ideologies.
politics based on practical concerns, not theory

Examples

The mayor was more interested in practical politics than in promoting abstract ideals.
Sometimes practical politics requires compromise and negotiation rather than confrontation.
His experience in practical politics gave him an advantage during the election campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realpolitik - politics or diplomacy based primarily on practical objectives, rather than on ideals
