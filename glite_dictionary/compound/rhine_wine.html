Rhine wine

1. NOUN

/ raɪn waɪn / (AmE), / raɪn waɪn / (BrE)
A white wine produced in the Rhine River valley of Germany; sometimes referred to as 'hock' in British English.
German white wine from the Rhine valley

Examples

The restaurant offers a selection of Rhine wine to pair with seafood dishes.
She bought a bottle of Rhine wine for the dinner party.
Many connoisseurs appreciate the crisp flavors of a good Rhine wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hock - British term for Rhine wine; German white wine

Etymology

Named after the Rhine River in Germany where the wine is produced.
