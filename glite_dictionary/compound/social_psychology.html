social psychology

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl saɪˌkɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of psychology that studies how people's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are influenced by the actual, imagined, or implied presence of others.
study of how people interact in groups

Examples

She decided to pursue a graduate degree in social psychology to better understand group behavior.
Social psychology explores how people's attitudes are shaped by their interactions with others.
The experiment is considered a classic study in social psychology for its insights into conformity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'social' + 'psychology'; 'psychology' from Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) + 'logia' (study).
