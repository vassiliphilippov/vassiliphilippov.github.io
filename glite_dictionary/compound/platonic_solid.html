Platonic solid

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtɑnɪk ˈsɑlɪd / (AmE), / pləˈtɒnɪk ˈsɒlɪd / (BrE)
any of the five convex regular polyhedra, whose faces are congruent regular polygons and whose angles are all equal.
one of five regular, convex polyhedra

Examples

The cube is a type of Platonic solid, along with the tetrahedron and octahedron.
In geometry class, we built models of each Platonic solid from colored paper.
The ancient Greeks believed that each Platonic solid represented one of the elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regular polyhedron - a polyhedron with congruent regular faces and equal angles

Etymology

Named after the philosopher Plato, who wrote about these solids in his works.
