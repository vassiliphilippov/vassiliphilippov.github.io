left atrium

1. NOUN

/ lɛft ˈeɪtriəm / (AmE), / lɛft ˈeɪtrɪəm / (BrE)
the upper left chamber of the heart that receives oxygenated blood from the pulmonary veins and pumps it into the left ventricle
upper left heart chamber
medical

Examples

The left atrium receives oxygen-rich blood from the lungs before passing it to the left ventricle.
Doctors discovered a clot in the patient's left atrium during a routine examination.
An abnormal rhythm was detected originating in the left atrium during the test.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right atrium - upper right chamber of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (entrance hall); in anatomy, used since the 17th century to describe heart chambers.

2. NOUN

/ lɛft ˈeɪ.tri.əm / (AmE), / lɛft ˈeɪ.tri.əm / (BrE)
the left upper chamber of the heart that receives oxygenated blood from the pulmonary veins and pumps it into the left ventricle
left upper chamber of the heart
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the left atrium for any signs of abnormal tissue growth.
Blood enters the left atrium from the lungs before being pumped into the left ventricle.
A clot in the left atrium can increase the risk of a stroke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right atrium - the right upper chamber of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (central hall or court); 'left' indicates position.
