acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene

1. NOUN

/ əˌkrɪl.oʊˌnaɪˌtraɪl bjuːˈtædiːn ˈstaɪriːn / (AmE), / əˌkrɪl.əʊˈnaɪ.traɪl bjuːˈtædiːn ˈstaɪriːn / (BrE)
a tough, lightweight plastic made from polymers of acrylonitrile, butadiene, and styrene, used especially for car bodies and appliance cases
a type of tough plastic (ABS) used in products and appliances

Examples

The dashboard is made from acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene due to its strength and durability.
Modern LEGO bricks are constructed with acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene for a consistent feel and fit.
Many electronic housings use acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene because it resists impact and heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ABS plastic - common abbreviation for acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene

Etymology

Formed from the chemical names: acrylonitrile, butadiene, and styrene; often abbreviated as ABS.
