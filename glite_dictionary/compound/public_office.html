public office

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈɔfɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
a position of authority or responsibility in government, held for the service of the people as a whole
governmental position of authority

Examples

She decided to run for public office to make a difference in her community.
Corruption in public office undermines trust in government institutions.
After leaving public office, he worked as a consultant for nonprofit organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil service - permanent professional branches of a state's administration, excluding military and judicial branches and elected politicians
  • Antonym: private sector - part of the economy run by private individuals or companies
