lacrimal sac

1. NOUN

/ ˈlækrɪməl sæk / (AmE), / ˈlækrɪməl sæk / (BrE)
a small pouch at the inner corner of the eye into which tears drain from the lacrimal ducts before passing into the nasal cavity
tear-draining pouch at eye corner
medical

Examples

Excessive tears may indicate blockage of the lacrimal sac.
During surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the lacrimal sac.
Infections of the lacrimal sac can lead to swelling and discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear duct - a small tube that carries tears from the lacrimal gland to the eye

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrima' (tear) + English 'sac'.
