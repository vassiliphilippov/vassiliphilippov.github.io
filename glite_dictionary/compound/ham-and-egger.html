ham-and-egger

1. NOUN

/ hæm ənd ˈɛɡər / (AmE), / hæm ənd ˈɛɡə / (BrE)
an ordinary, mediocre, or unambitious person
an unremarkable or average person
american informal

Examples

He always saw himself as a ham-and-egger, doing just enough to get by.
The boss wanted someone ambitious, not another ham-and-egger on the team.
Despite his ham-and-egger reputation, he surprised everyone with his creative ideas.

Synset Examples

He's just a ham-and-egger trying to get by.
Don't be a ham-and-egger; aim higher!
That ham-and-egger thinks he can do better than us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average Joe - an ordinary person
  • Synonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or unremarkable

Etymology

Originally American English; from 'ham and eggs,' a simple, common meal, symbolizing ordinariness.
