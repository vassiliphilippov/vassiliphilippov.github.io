back-of-the-envelope

1. ADJ

/ ˌbæk əv ði ɛnˌvəˈloʊp / (AmE), / ˌbæk əv ði ˈɒn.və.ləʊp / (BrE)
Done quickly and informally, without detailed analysis; used for rough calculations or plans.
quick, informal calculation or plan

Examples

We relied on a back-of-the-envelope estimate to decide if the trip was affordable.
His proposal was just a back-of-the-envelope idea, but everyone understood the general concept.
The engineer provided a back-of-the-envelope calculation to show the project's feasibility.

Synset Examples

Her back-of-the-envelope math suggested we would break even by next quarter.
We did a back-of-the-envelope calculation to estimate the project's cost.
He quickly sketched a back-of-the-envelope plan for the meeting.

Etymology

Originates from the idea of making quick calculations on whatever is handy, such as the back of an envelope, in English-speaking contexts since the mid-20th century.
