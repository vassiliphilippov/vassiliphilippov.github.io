Rugel's plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːɡəlz plænˌteɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɡəlz ˈplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
A North American plantain with reddish leafstalks and broad leaves, commonly found in moist habitats.
North American plantain with reddish stems

Examples

We spotted several Rugel's plantain plants along the damp edge of the trail.
The reddish stems helped us identify the Rugel's plantain growing in the meadow.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Rugel's plantain for its attractive foliage.

Etymology

Named after Ferdinand Rugel, a 19th-century German-American botanist.
