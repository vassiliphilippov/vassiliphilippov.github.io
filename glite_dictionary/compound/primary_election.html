primary election

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
an election held before the main election, in which voters select a party's candidates or delegates for the general election
preliminary election to select party candidates

Examples

Voter turnout during the primary election was much higher than expected.
The candidates debated education policy at several primary elections across the state.
Winning the primary election is essential before moving on to the general election.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general election - the main election to choose public officials
