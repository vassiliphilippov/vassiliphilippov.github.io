lienal artery

1. NOUN

/ liˈiːnəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / lɪˈiːnəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that arises from the celiac trunk and supplies blood to the spleen; also known as the splenic artery
artery supplying blood to the spleen
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully located the lienal artery before proceeding with the operation.
Damage to the lienal artery can cause significant bleeding in abdominal injuries.
Blood is supplied to the spleen primarily through the lienal artery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splenic artery - artery that supplies blood to the spleen

Etymology

From Latin 'lienalis' (of the spleen) and 'artery', reflecting its function.
