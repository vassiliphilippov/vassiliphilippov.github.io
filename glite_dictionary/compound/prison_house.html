prison house

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈprɪz(ə)n ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A building or institution where people are kept as punishment for crimes or while awaiting trial; a prison.
correctional institution for confinement or punishment

Examples

The old prison house stood at the edge of town, its windows barred and its walls imposing.
He spent several years in the prison house before his sentence was overturned.
Literature often uses the prison house as a symbol of lost freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a place where people are kept as punishment for a crime or while awaiting trial

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən haʊs / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən haʊs / (BrE)
A place or situation that feels confining or restrictive, resembling a prison.
place or state of seeming confinement

Examples

To him, the small village was a prison house from which he longed to escape.
She described her strict upbringing as a prison house that stifled her creativity.
For many, the rigid routine of the office felt like a prison house, limiting their freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as punishment for a crime
