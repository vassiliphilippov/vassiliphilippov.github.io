distress signal

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɛs ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛs ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
an internationally recognized signal sent from a ship, aircraft, or person to indicate that emergency help is needed
emergency signal indicating need for help

Examples

The crew launched a distress signal when the ship started taking on water.
After the crash, the pilot transmitted a distress signal to request immediate assistance.
Local fishermen responded quickly when they spotted the distress signal from the stranded boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SOS - the Morse code distress signal
  • Synonym: mayday - international spoken distress call
