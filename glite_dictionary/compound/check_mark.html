check mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛk mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛk mɑːk / (BrE)
a written mark (✓) used to indicate that something has been noted, approved, or completed
mark showing completion or approval

Examples

She put a check mark next to each item on the list as she finished it.
Please place a check mark in the box if you agree with the terms and conditions.
The teacher drew a check mark beside the students' correct answers.

Synset Examples

as he called the roll he put a check mark by each student's name

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a mark used to indicate acknowledgment or completion (chiefly British)

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛk ˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛk mɑːk / (BrE)
a symbol (✓) used to indicate correctness, approval, or completion
symbol for correctness or approval

Examples

Please place a check mark next to each correct answer.
The teacher put a check mark at the top of my assignment to show it was complete.
After submitting the online form, a green check mark confirmed my registration.

Synset Examples

The app displayed a blue check mark after syncing.
