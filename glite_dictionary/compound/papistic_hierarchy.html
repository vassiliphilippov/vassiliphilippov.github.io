papistic hierarchy

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpɪstɪk haɪˈrɑːrki / (AmE), / pəˈpɪstɪk haɪˈrɑːki / (BrE)
the leadership and organizational structure of the Roman Catholic Church, especially as seen from a critical or Protestant viewpoint
Catholic Church leadership structure (historical/critical use)

Examples

During the Reformation, many opposed the papistic hierarchy and advocated for new church structures.
Some historians argue that the papistic hierarchy played a crucial role in European politics.
The author's essay criticized the influence of the papistic hierarchy on medieval society.

Synset Examples

The papistic hierarchy was challenged by the reformists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholic Church - the worldwide Christian church under the leadership of the Pope
  • Synonym: papacy - the office or authority of the Pope

Etymology

From 'papistic' (from Medieval Latin papisticus, pertaining to the Pope) and 'hierarchy' (from Greek hierarchia, sacred rule).
