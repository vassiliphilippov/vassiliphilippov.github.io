radioactive waste

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈæktɪv weɪst / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv weɪst / (BrE)
unwanted or leftover material that emits radiation, produced as a by-product of nuclear or radioactive processes
waste emitting harmful radiation

Examples

The power plant must safely store its radioactive waste to prevent environmental contamination.
Disposal of radioactive waste remains a major challenge for the nuclear industry.
Scientists are developing new methods to reduce the amount of radioactive waste produced during energy generation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear waste - waste produced by nuclear reactors or radioactive processes
