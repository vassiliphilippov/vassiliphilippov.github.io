Balkan Wars

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.kən wɔrz / (AmE), / ˈbæl.kən wɔːz / (BrE)
Two wars fought in 1912–1913 over the remaining European territories of the Ottoman Empire, resulting in the loss of almost all Ottoman land in Europe except the area around Constantinople (now Istanbul).
wars in 1912–1913 over Ottoman European territory

Examples

The Balkan Wars dramatically changed the map of southeastern Europe before World War I.
Many historians study the causes and consequences of the Balkan Wars to understand modern Balkan history.
After the Balkan Wars, several new states gained significant territory from the declining Ottoman Empire.

Etymology

From 'Balkan', referring to the Balkan Peninsula, and 'wars', from Old English wyrre (conflict).
