false smut

1. NOUN

/ fɔls smʌt / (AmE), / fɔːls smʌt / (BrE)
a disease of rice in which the grains are covered by a green powder made up of fungal spores (conidia)
disease of rice causing powdery green grains

Examples

Rice crops can suffer from false smut, which affects the quality of the harvest.
Researchers are developing new methods to identify and control false smut in paddy fields.
The appearance of a green powder on rice grains is a key symptom of false smut.
