wide screen

1. NOUN

/ waɪd skriːn / (AmE), / waɪd skriːn / (BrE)
a projection or display screen that is significantly wider than it is high, commonly used for movies and presentations
a projection screen much wider than its height

Examples

The old theater replaced its small projector with a wide screen to give audiences a better view.
Many modern films are produced to fit a wide screen format for a cinematic experience.
The conference room was equipped with a wide screen for presentations and video calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widescreen - format with a width much greater than its height, especially in film and television
