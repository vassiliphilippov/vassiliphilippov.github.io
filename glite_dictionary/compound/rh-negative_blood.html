Rh-negative blood

1. NOUN

/ ɑr eɪ ˈnɛɡətɪv blʌd / (AmE), / ɑː r eɪ ˈnɛɡətɪv blʌd / (BrE)
Blood whose red cells do not have the Rh factor (Rh antigen).
blood lacking the Rh factor
medical

Examples

People with Rh-negative blood can donate to both Rh-negative and Rh-positive recipients.
Before a transfusion, doctors check if the patient has Rh-negative blood to avoid complications.
Pregnant women with Rh-negative blood may need special care if the baby is Rh-positive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Rh-positive blood - blood whose red cells have the Rh factor

Etymology

From 'Rh' for Rhesus, referencing the Rhesus monkey, in which the factor was first discovered.
