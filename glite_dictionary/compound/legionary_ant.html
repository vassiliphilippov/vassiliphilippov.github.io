legionary ant

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdʒəˌnɛri ænt / (AmE), / ˈliːdʒənəri ænt / (BrE)
a tropical, nomadic ant species that preys mainly on other insects, often moving in large swarms
tropical, nomadic insect-hunting ant

Examples

The legionary ant colony moved swiftly through the rainforest, overwhelming any insects in its path.
Researchers study the hunting patterns of the legionary ant to better understand swarm intelligence.
Because of their aggressive foraging, legionary ants are feared by many smaller creatures in the tropics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: army ant - another nomadic predatory ant of similar behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'legionarius' (relating to a legion), referring to their coordinated, army-like behavior.
