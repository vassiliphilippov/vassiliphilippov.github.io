Scotch pancake

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ ˈpænkeɪk / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ˈpænkeɪk / (BrE)
A small, thick pancake made by dropping a spoonful of batter onto a griddle; commonly eaten in the United Kingdom.
small, thick pancake (UK)
british

Examples

For breakfast, she made a stack of Scotch pancakes topped with honey and butter.
Traditional afternoon tea often includes freshly cooked Scotch pancakes with jam.
He learned to make Scotch pancakes while visiting Scotland on holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop scone - British term for a similar small, griddled pancake

Etymology

Named for its association with Scotland; 'Scotch' is an old term for 'Scottish'.
