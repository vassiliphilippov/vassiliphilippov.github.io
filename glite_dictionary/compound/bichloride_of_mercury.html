bichloride of mercury

1. NOUN

/ baɪˌklɔrɪd əv ˈmɜrkjʊri / (AmE), / baɪˌklɔːrɪd əv ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
a poisonous white crystalline compound (HgCl₂) used formerly as a pesticide, antiseptic, and wood preservative
a poisonous compound of mercury used as a disinfectant
medical

Examples

The old laboratory stored bottles of bichloride of mercury for disinfection.
Accidental ingestion of bichloride of mercury can be fatal and requires immediate medical attention.
In the past, bichloride of mercury was commonly used to preserve wood and prevent decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercuric chloride - another name for bichloride of mercury
  • Degree Lower: mercury - the chemical element, symbol Hg, used in compounds like bichloride of mercury

Etymology

From 'bi-' meaning 'two' and 'chloride', referring to two chlorine atoms combined with mercury.
