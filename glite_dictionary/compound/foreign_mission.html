foreign mission

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrən ˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən ˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
A permanent diplomatic office in a foreign country, representing the sending state and headed by a diplomatic official such as a minister or ambassador.
permanent diplomatic office abroad

Examples

The new ambassador took charge of the foreign mission in Paris last month.
There are several staff members working at the foreign mission to assist with visas and diplomatic relations.
Every foreign mission must comply with both international law and local regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embassy - the official residence or offices of an ambassador
  • Synonym: consulate - the place where a consul works and assists citizens abroad
  • Antonym: domestic mission - a similar office within one's own country

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrən ˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən ˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
an organization consisting of missionaries sent to another country to conduct religious work
religious organization in another country

Examples

The church raised funds to support their foreign mission in Africa.
She spent ten years volunteering with a foreign mission in Southeast Asia.
Many foreign missions provide not just religious services, but also education and healthcare in the local community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missionary - a person sent on a religious mission, especially to promote Christianity in a foreign country
  • Antonym: home mission - a religious mission carried out within one's own country
