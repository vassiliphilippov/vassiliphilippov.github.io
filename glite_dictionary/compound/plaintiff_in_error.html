plaintiff in error

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪn.tɪf ɪn ˈɛr.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪn.tɪf ɪn ˈɛr.ə / (BrE)
the party who appeals a decision of a lower court, especially by writ of error
party appealing a court decision
legal

Examples

The plaintiff in error challenged the findings of the lower court.
As plaintiff in error, she submitted a brief to the appeals court.
The judge reviewed the case brought by the plaintiff in error before delivering the ruling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appellant - the party who appeals to a higher court
