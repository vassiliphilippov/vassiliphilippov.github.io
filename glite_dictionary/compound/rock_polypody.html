rock polypody

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈpɑlɪˌpoʊdi / (AmE), / rɒk ˈpɒlɪˌpəʊdi / (BrE)
A chiefly lithophytic or epiphytic fern found in North America and East Asia, typically growing on rocks or trees.
fern growing on rocks or trees

Examples

The shady ledges were covered with rock polypody, thriving among the moss and lichen.
She identified the delicate fronds as rock polypody during the nature walk.
Rock polypody ferns are often found clinging to boulders in cool, moist forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polypody - any fern of the genus Polypodium

Etymology

From 'rock' (referring to its habitat) and 'polypody,' from Latin polypodium, a type of fern.
