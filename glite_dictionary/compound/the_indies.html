the Indies

1. PROPN

/ ði ˈɪndiz / (AmE), / ði ˈɪndiz / (BrE)
a group of islands between North America and South America, including the Greater and Lesser Antilles, often regarded as a popular resort destination
island group in the Caribbean Sea

Examples

Many cruise ships stop at the Indies for their tropical beaches and vibrant culture.
They spent their honeymoon traveling between the beautiful islands of the Indies.
The Caribbean region, often called the Indies, attracts tourists from around the world every year.

Etymology

From Latin 'Indiæ', plural of 'India', influenced by early European explorers' mistaken belief that the Caribbean was part of East Asia (the Indies).
