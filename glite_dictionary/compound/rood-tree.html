rood-tree

1. NOUN

/ rud triː / (AmE), / ruːd triː / (BrE)
A representation of the cross on which Jesus was crucified, especially as depicted in art or literature.
representation of Christ's cross
archaic

Examples

The ancient church still displays a carved rood-tree above its altar.
Medieval poets often wrote about the sacred rood-tree in their verses.
A beautifully painted rood-tree was found during the cathedral's restoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucifix - A representation of a cross with Jesus on it.

Etymology

From Old English 'rōd' (cross) + 'tree', referring to the wooden cross.
