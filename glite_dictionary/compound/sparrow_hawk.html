sparrow hawk

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛroʊ hɑːk / (AmE), / ˈspærəʊ hɔːk / (BrE)
a small North American falcon with brownish upperparts and a barred tail, especially the American kestrel (Falco sparverius)
small North American falcon

Examples

A sparrow hawk perched on the fence, scanning the field for prey.
The sparrow hawk is known for its agile flight and hunting skills.
We spotted a sparrow hawk near the edge of the forest during our hike.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspæroʊ hɑk / (AmE), / ˈspærəʊ hɔːk / (BrE)
A small hawk found in Eurasia and northern Africa, known for preying on small birds and insects.
small Eurasian or North African hawk

Examples

The sparrow hawk swooped down quietly, catching its prey by surprise.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a sparrow hawk during their walks through the woods.
Some farmers encourage the sparrow hawk to keep the population of small rodents in check.
