Flu Manchu

1. NOUN

/ flu mænˈtʃuː / (AmE), / flu mænˈtʃuː / (BrE)
a humorous term for a person who looks ill from the flu, or for a widespread flu outbreak
humorous term for looking ill from the flu
informal

Examples

After a week in bed, he emerged looking like Flu Manchu.
The whole class caught the flu, and parents joked it was a Flu Manchu situation.
Whenever flu season comes, she calls herself Flu Manchu to make her coworkers laugh.

Synset Examples

After catching the flu, he looked like Flu Manchu.
Flu Manchu swept through the office, infecting everyone.
He joked he was feeling like Flu Manchu last winter.

Etymology

A pun combining 'flu' (short for influenza) and the name 'Fu Manchu', a fictional villain created by Sax Rohmer.
