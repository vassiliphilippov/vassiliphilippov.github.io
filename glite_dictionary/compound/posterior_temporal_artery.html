posterior temporal artery

1. NOUN

/ pəˈstɪriɚ tɛmˈpɔrəl ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə tɛmˈpɔːrəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
A branch of the temporal artery that supplies blood to the posterior part of the cortex of the temporal lobe in the brain.
artery supplying back part of temporal lobe
medical

Examples

Damage to the posterior temporal artery can result in localized brain ischemia.
The surgeon carefully isolated the posterior temporal artery during the operation.
A blockage in the posterior temporal artery may impair blood flow to the temporal lobe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: temporal artery - The main artery supplying the temporal region of the head

Etymology

From Latin 'posterior' (coming after), 'temporalis' (of the temples), and 'arteria' (artery).
