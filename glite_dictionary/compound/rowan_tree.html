rowan tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.ən triː / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.ən triː / (BrE)
A Eurasian tree with pinnate leaves, white flowers, and clusters of orange-red berrylike fruits.
Eurasian tree with orange-red fruits

Examples

Every autumn, the rowan tree in our garden produces bright red berries that attract birds.
According to folklore, planting a rowan tree near your home protects against evil spirits.
The hillside was dotted with blooming rowan trees, their fruit shining in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain ash - Another name for the rowan tree, especially in Europe

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'ruadh-an', meaning 'the red one', referring to the color of the berries.
