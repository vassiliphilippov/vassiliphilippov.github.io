black gold

1. NOUN

/ blæk goʊld / (AmE), / blak gəʊld / (BrE)
A term for petroleum (oil), especially when emphasizing its high economic value.
petroleum; valuable natural resource

Examples

The discovery of black gold transformed the small town into a bustling city.
Countries rich in black gold often accumulate great wealth from exports.
The price of black gold fluctuates with global demand and political stability.

Synset Examples

Oil is often referred to as black gold in economics.
Investors are always interested in black gold markets.
The region became wealthy due to its black gold reserves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil - a viscous liquid derived from petroleum, used as fuel
