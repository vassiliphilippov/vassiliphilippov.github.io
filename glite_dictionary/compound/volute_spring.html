volute spring

1. NOUN

/ vəˈluːt ˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / vəˈluːt ˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
a mechanical spring formed in the shape of a spiral or coil, often used to absorb shock or store energy
a spring shaped like a coil

Examples

Many trains use a volute spring under their buffers for shock absorption.
The old machinery relied on a large volute spring to return the lever to its original position.
He replaced the damaged volute spring with a new one designed for heavier loads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coil spring - a spring shaped as a helix or coil

Etymology

From Latin 'voluta' (a spiral scroll), referencing the coil shape.
