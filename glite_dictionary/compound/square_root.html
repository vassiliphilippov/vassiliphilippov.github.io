square root

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr rut / (AmE), / skwɛə ruːt / (BrE)
a number that produces a specified quantity when multiplied by itself
number that multiplies by itself to give another number

Examples

The square root of 49 is 7 because 7 times 7 equals 49.
He struggled to calculate the square root of a large number without a calculator.
Most students first learn about square roots in middle school mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cube root - a number that produces a specified quantity when multiplied by itself twice
