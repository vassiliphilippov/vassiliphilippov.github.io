arolla pine

1. NOUN

/ əˈroʊlə paɪn / (AmE), / əˈrɒlə paɪn / (BrE)
A large European pine tree (Pinus cembra) with five needles per bundle, producing edible cembra nuts and a resinous sap.
European pine tree yielding cembra nuts

Examples

The seeds of the arolla pine are sometimes used as a food delicacy in certain European regions.
We saw a towering arolla pine while hiking in the Alps, its needles clustered in groups of five.
The resin from the arolla pine has been traditionally collected for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss stone pine - another name for the arolla pine (Pinus cembra)

Etymology

From French 'arolle', referring to the Swiss stone pine (Pinus cembra).
