silurid fish

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlʊrɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / sɪˈljʊərɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A member of the Siluridae family, which are Old World freshwater catfishes with smooth skin and a long anal fin merging into an eellike tail.
Old World freshwater catfish (family Siluridae)

Examples

The Mekong giant catfish is a well-known species of silurid fish found in Southeast Asia.
Scientists discovered a new silurid fish species in the river basin last year.
Many silurid fish have an elongated anal fin that blends into the tail, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - A freshwater or marine fish with whiskerlike barbels

Etymology

From Latin 'Silurus', the type genus of the family Siluridae.
