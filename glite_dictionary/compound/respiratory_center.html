respiratory center

1. NOUN

/ rɛˈspɪrətɔri ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrətəri ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
A region in the medulla oblongata and pons of the brainstem that controls the rate and depth of breathing by integrating sensory information about blood oxygen and carbon dioxide levels and directing signals to the respiratory muscles.
part of brainstem controlling breathing
medical

Examples

Damage to the respiratory center can lead to life-threatening breathing problems.
The respiratory center adjusts ventilation in response to high levels of carbon dioxide in the blood.
Medications that depress the respiratory center should be used with caution in patients with respiratory disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medulla oblongata - lower part of the brainstem involved in autonomic functions including breathing
  • Synonym: pons - part of the brainstem involved in respiration control
