occupational disease

1. NOUN

/ əˌkjuːpəˈʃənəl dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃənl dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease or disorder caused by conditions or substances present in the workplace, especially after long-term exposure
disease caused by work conditions
medical

Examples

Prolonged exposure to asbestos in the mines led to a rare occupational disease among the workers.
The company implemented new safety measures to lower the risk of occupational disease in its factories.
Carpal tunnel syndrome is recognized as an occupational disease in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work-related illness - illness linked to work conditions or activities
