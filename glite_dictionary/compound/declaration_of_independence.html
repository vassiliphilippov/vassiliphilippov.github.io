Declaration of Independence

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən əv ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən ɒv ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
the formal statement adopted by the Second Continental Congress on July 4, 1776, proclaiming the independence of the thirteen American colonies from Great Britain
1776 document proclaiming American independence

Examples

Thomas Jefferson was the principal author of the Declaration of Independence.
Each year, Americans celebrate the signing of the Declaration of Independence on July 4th.
The ideas expressed in the Declaration of Independence have inspired movements for freedom around the world.
