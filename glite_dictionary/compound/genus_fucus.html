genus Fucus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfjuːkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfjuːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of cartilaginous brown algae in the family Fucaceae, commonly found in marine environments.
genus of brown marine algae

Examples

The genus Fucus includes several species of brown algae found along rocky shores.
Marine biologists often study the reproductive cycles of the genus Fucus.
Members of the genus Fucus play a vital role in coastal ecosystems as primary producers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Fucaceae - the family containing Fucus and related brown algae

Etymology

From Latin 'Fucus', the name for a type of seaweed.
