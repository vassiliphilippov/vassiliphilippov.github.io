Western culture

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the set of social norms, traditions, values, and practices characteristic of Western Europe and North America, especially in the modern era
modern culture of Western Europe and North America

Examples

The influence of Western culture can be seen in fashion trends around the world.
Many people debate the impact of Western culture on traditional societies.
The spread of Western culture has affected local languages, cuisine, and customs globally.

Synset Examples

when Ghandi was asked what he thought of Western civilization he said he thought it would be a good idea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Eastern culture - the cultural traditions and practices associated with East Asia, South Asia, and the Middle East
