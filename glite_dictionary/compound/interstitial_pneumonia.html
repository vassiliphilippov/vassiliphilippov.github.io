interstitial pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈstɪʃəl pəˈn(j)uːmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪʃəl pəˈnjuːməniə / (BrE)
a chronic lung disease affecting the interstitial tissue (supporting framework) of the lungs, often resulting in inflammation and scarring
chronic disease of lung interstitial tissue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with interstitial pneumonia after complaining of persistent shortness of breath.
Treatment for interstitial pneumonia often involves medication to reduce lung inflammation.
Doctors monitor the progression of interstitial pneumonia with regular chest imaging and lung function tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pneumonia - infection or inflammation of the lungs

Etymology

From Latin *interstitium* (interval, gap) and Greek *pneumōn* (lung).
