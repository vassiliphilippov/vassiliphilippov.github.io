heat flash

1. NOUN

/ hit flæʃ / (AmE), / hiːt flæʃ / (BrE)
a brief, intense burst of heat, especially one produced by an explosion such as an atomic blast
brief, intense burst of heat

Examples

The heat flash from the explosion shattered windows miles away.
Scientists studied the effects of the heat flash on nearby structures.
He suffered burns from the heat flash caused by the detonation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireball - a ball of fire produced by an explosion, especially a nuclear one
