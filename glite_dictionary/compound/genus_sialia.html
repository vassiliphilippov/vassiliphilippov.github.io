genus Sialia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs saɪˈeɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈeɪ.lɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small North American thrushes commonly known as bluebirds.
genus of North American bluebirds

Examples

The genus Sialia includes three species of bluebirds native to North America.
Much research has been conducted on the nesting habits of birds in the genus Sialia.
The striking plumage of members of the genus Sialia makes them popular among bird watchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebird - a small North American songbird with blue plumage

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sialia', genus name introduced in taxonomy.
