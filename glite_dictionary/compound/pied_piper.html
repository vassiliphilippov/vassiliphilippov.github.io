pied piper

1. NOUN

/ paɪd ˈpaɪpər / (AmE), / paɪd ˈpaɪpə / (BrE)
a person who attracts followers or a crowd, especially by means of charm or persuasive appeal, and often leading them to a negative outcome
enticing leader, often to a negative end

Examples

He acted as a pied piper to the youth, leading them into risky ventures.
The charismatic politician became a pied piper for radical change in the country.
Many were drawn to her promises, but she turned out to be a modern pied piper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
  • Antonym: follower - a person who supports and admires a particular person or set of ideas

Etymology

From the legend of the Pied Piper of Hamelin, a folktale from Germany.
