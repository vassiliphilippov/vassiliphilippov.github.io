Pied Piper

1. PROPN

/ paɪd ˈpaɪpər / (AmE), / paɪd ˈpaɪpə / (BrE)
the title character in a German legend and in Robert Browning's poem, who lured rats (and later children) away from the town of Hamelin with his music
legendary character who lured rats (and children) from Hamelin

Examples

The legend of the Pied Piper has been told for generations in Germany and beyond.
Some politicians are referred to as a Pied Piper when they attract many followers.
In literature class, we studied Robert Browning's poem about the Pied Piper of Hamelin.

Etymology

From 'pied' (having two or more colors) and 'piper' (player of a pipe), referencing the character's multicolored clothing and musical instrument.
