lens nucleus

1. NOUN

/ lɛnz ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / lɛnz ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
the central part of the lens in the eye, surrounded by the cortex
central part of the eye's lens
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully removed the lens nucleus during the cataract operation.
A hardening of the lens nucleus can affect vision as people age.
Scientists studied the proteins concentrated in the lens nucleus to understand eye diseases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lens cortex - the outer layer of the lens in the eye
