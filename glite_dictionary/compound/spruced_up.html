spruced up

1. ADJ

/ spruːst ʌp / (AmE), / spruːst ʌp / (BrE)
wearing neat, stylish, or formal clothing; made tidy and attractive in appearance
dressed in neat or formal clothing

Examples

She always feels more confident when she's spruced up for an important meeting.
The old downtown has really been spruced up with new shops and colorful murals.
He spruced up in a sharp suit and tie for the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressed up - wearing formal or fancy clothes
  • Antonym: casual - wearing informal or everyday attire

Etymology

From the verb 'spruce up,' meaning to make oneself neat or smart; 'spruce' possibly from 16th-century English referring to goods from Prussia (Spruce).
