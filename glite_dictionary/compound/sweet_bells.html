sweet bells

1. NOUN

/ swit bɛlz / (AmE), / swiːt bɛlz / (BrE)
A bushy deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, noted for its long racemes of pinkish flowers.
shrub with pinkish flowers (US)

Examples

The sweet bells in her garden bloomed with delicate pink flowers in early summer.
Local nurseries often sell sweet bells because of their attractive appearance and hardiness.
Botanists admire the vibrant racemes produced by sweet bells shrubs each spring.
