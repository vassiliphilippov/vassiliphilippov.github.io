ceiba tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbə triː / (AmE), / ˈsiːbə triː / (BrE)
A large tropical tree with a massive trunk and ridged bark, producing seed pods covered in silky fibers known as kapok.
large tropical tree; source of kapok fiber

Examples

The ceiba tree towers above the jungle, its wide branches supporting a variety of wildlife.
Local artisans use the silky fibers from the ceiba tree to stuff pillows and mattresses.
The villagers believe the ancient ceiba tree is sacred and gather beneath its shade for ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kapok - the silky fiber from the seed pods of the ceiba tree

Etymology

From Spanish 'ceiba', ultimately from Taino or Arawak origins.
