Yorkshire terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɔrkˌʃɪr ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˈjɔːkʃə ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
a very small breed of dog with a long, silky, bluish-grey and tan coat, originally from Yorkshire, England.
small dog breed with long silky coat

Examples

My grandmother has a Yorkshire terrier that loves to sit on her lap.
The Yorkshire terrier won first place in the toy breed division.
Although small, a Yorkshire terrier can be very protective of its owner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of small, energetic dog, originally bred for hunting vermin

Etymology

Named after the county of Yorkshire in northern England, where the breed was developed.
