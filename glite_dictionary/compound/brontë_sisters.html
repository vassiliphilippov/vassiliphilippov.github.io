Brontë sisters

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɑːnti ˈsɪstərz / (AmE), / ˈbrɒnti ˈsɪstəz / (BrE)
three 19th-century English sisters—Charlotte, Emily, and Anne Brontë—who became renowned novelists
19th-century English literary sisters

Examples

Many literary scholars have studied the novels written by the Brontë sisters.
The Brontë sisters lived in Haworth and are admired for their significant contributions to English literature.
A guided tour of the parsonage where the Brontë sisters grew up is available for visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brontë, Charlotte - eldest of the Brontë sisters, author of 'Jane Eyre'
  • Synonym: Brontë, Emily - middle Brontë sister, author of 'Wuthering Heights'
  • Synonym: Brontë, Anne - youngest Brontë sister, author of 'The Tenant of Wildfell Hall'

Etymology

Brontë is the family surname, from Patrick Brontë, their father.
