mountain oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən oʊk / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn əʊk / (BrE)
A tall timber tree with hard, heavy pinkish or light brown wood, found especially in mountainous regions.
a type of tall timber tree

Examples

The dense forest was dominated by towering mountain oak trees.
Carpenters prized the wood from the mountain oak for its strength and durability.
Hikers often pause to rest in the shade of a mountain oak while trekking through the hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a large tree with lobed leaves and acorns
