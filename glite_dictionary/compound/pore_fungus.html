pore fungus

1. NOUN

/ pɔr ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / pɔː ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
a fungus with a spore-bearing surface composed of pores or tubes, typically with a woody fruiting body, especially from the Polyporaceae or Boletaceae families
fungus with pore-like spore surface

Examples

Many trees that die in the forest decompose slowly because of pore fungi.
The shelf-like fruiting bodies of some pore fungi can persist on trunks for years.
Researchers studied a rare species of pore fungus growing on decaying logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polypore - a fungus with pores on its underside, often used synonymously with 'pore fungus'

Etymology

From 'pore' (referring to pore openings) and 'fungus' (from Latin 'fungus', mushroom).
