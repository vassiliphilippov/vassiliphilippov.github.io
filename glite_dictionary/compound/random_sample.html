random sample

1. NOUN

/ ˈrændəm ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈrændəm ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a sample chosen so that every element of the population has an equal chance of being selected
sample chosen at random

Examples

The survey was based on a random sample of 1,000 adults nationwide.
Researchers drew a random sample to ensure their results were unbiased.
Any random sample from a large enough population can provide useful statistical insights.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: representative sample - a sample that accurately reflects the population

2. NOUN

/ ˈrændəm ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈrændəm ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a sample in which every member of the population has an equal probability of being chosen
statistical sample chosen by equal chance

Examples

The researchers selected a random sample of students from the entire school for the survey.
A random sample helps to ensure that the results of the study are not biased.
To minimize selection bias, a random sample of participants was chosen from the general population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: representative sample - a sample that accurately reflects the characteristics of the population
  • Antonym: biased sample - a sample in which certain members of the population are more likely to be included than others

Etymology

Sample from Latin 'exemplum' (example), random from Old French 'randir' (to run).
