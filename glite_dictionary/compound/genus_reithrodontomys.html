genus Reithrodontomys

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌraɪˌθroʊˈdɒn.təˌmɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌraɪˌθrəʊˈdɒn.təˌmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents known as New World harvest mice, native to North and Central America.
genus of New World harvest mice

Examples

The genus Reithrodontomys includes several species of harvest mice found throughout North and Central America.
Biologists have observed that members of the genus Reithrodontomys are primarily nocturnal and have grooved upper incisors.
Many farmers are familiar with the genus Reithrodontomys because these mice often inhabit agricultural fields.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'reithros' (channel) + 'odontos' (tooth) + 'mys' (mouse), referring to the grooved teeth characteristic.
