blank shell

1. NOUN

/ blæŋk ʃɛl / (AmE), / blæŋk ʃɛl / (BrE)
A cartridge for a firearm that contains an explosive charge but no bullet, used for training, signaling, or in stage productions.
cartridge with charge but no bullet

Examples

For safety reasons, the actors used only blank shells during the filming of the action scenes.
The sound of blank shells can still be loud and potentially dangerous at close range.
Soldiers practiced loading and firing rifles with blank shells to simulate real combat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blank cartridge - a cartridge with powder but no bullet, used for simulation or signaling
