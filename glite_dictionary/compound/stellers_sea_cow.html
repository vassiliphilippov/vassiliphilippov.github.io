Steller's sea cow

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlɚz siː kaʊ / (AmE), / ˈsteləz siː kaʊ / (BrE)
an extinct, large marine mammal of the sirenian order, formerly found off the Asiatic coast of the Bering Sea, and first described by Georg Wilhelm Steller in the 18th century
extinct large marine mammal

Examples

The remains of a Steller's sea cow were discovered on a remote island in the Bering Sea.
Steller's detailed observations provide much of what we know about the Steller's sea cow today.
The extinction of the Steller's sea cow is a cautionary tale of overhunting by humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dugong - a living sirenian marine mammal of warm coastal waters
  • Synonym: manatee - a large aquatic mammal, also a member of the order Sirenia

Etymology

Named after German naturalist Georg Wilhelm Steller, who first described the animal in 1741.
