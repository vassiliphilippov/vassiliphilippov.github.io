carbon chauvinist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.bən ˈʃoʊ.və.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bən ˈʃəʊ.vɪ.nɪst / (BrE)
a person who strongly prefers carbon-based energy sources or fossil fuels over renewable energy options
person favoring fossil fuels over renewables

Examples

The senator was labeled a carbon chauvinist after opposing solar and wind energy initiatives.
Critics called the industry lobbyists carbon chauvinists for their persistent defense of oil and coal.
Being a carbon chauvinist is at odds with current climate change policies.

Synset Examples

Being a carbon chauvinist won't help the planet.
Her views on energy made her a carbon chauvinist.
He's such a carbon chauvinist, ignoring renewable options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossil fuel advocate - a person who promotes the use of fossil fuels
  • Antonym: renewables supporter - a person who promotes renewable energy sources

Etymology

Formed from 'carbon' (as in carbon-based fuels) and 'chauvinist' (biased supporter).
