Danish pastry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪnɪʃ ˈpeɪstri / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnɪʃ ˈpeɪstri / (BrE)
A light, sweet, yeast-raised pastry, typically filled with fruit, cheese, or custard.
sweet yeast-raised pastry with filling

Examples

She bought a fresh Danish pastry filled with raspberry jam for breakfast.
The bakery is famous for its cheese Danish pastry and croissants.
Would you like a cup of coffee and a Danish pastry with your afternoon break?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastry - baked dough of flour and fat, sweet or savory

Etymology

Named after the country of Denmark, where this type of pastry originated.
