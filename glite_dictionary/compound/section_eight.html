Section Eight

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkʃən ˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛkʃən ˈeɪt / (BrE)
a discharge from the US Army due to mental unfitness or character traits considered undesirable
US Army discharge for mental unfitness
american

Examples

He received a Section Eight after repeated evidence of psychological issues.
Applying for a Section Eight was considered a way to leave the military involuntarily.
Some veterans faced stigma after being given a Section Eight during their service.

Etymology

Refers to Section VIII of the United States Army Regulation 615-360, which outlined criteria for such discharges.

informal american

Examples

After his erratic behavior, they classified him as a Section Eight and sent him home.
