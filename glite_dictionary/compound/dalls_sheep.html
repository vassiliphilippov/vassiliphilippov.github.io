Dall's sheep

1. NOUN

/ dɑlz ʃip / (AmE), / dɔːlz ʃiːp / (BrE)
a large, white wild sheep native to northwestern Canada and Alaska, known for its distinctive curved horns
wild North American white sheep

Examples

We spotted a Dall's sheep perched high on the rocky cliffs during our hike in Alaska.
The horns of a male Dall's sheep can form impressive curls as they grow older.
Biologists track population trends of Dall's sheep to monitor the health of the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bighorn sheep - a large North American wild sheep with curved horns

Etymology

Named after the American naturalist William Healey Dall (1845–1927).
