judicial proceeding

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl prəˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl prəˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A legal process conducted before a court to determine or enforce rights and obligations.
legal process in a court
legal

Examples

The defendant was summoned to appear at the judicial proceeding next week.
A judicial proceeding often involves the presentation of evidence and legal arguments.
During the judicial proceeding, both parties had the opportunity to present their case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - a formal examination of evidence by a court
  • Synonym: hearing - a formal meeting in a court for legal argument

Etymology

From Latin 'judicialis' (of or belonging to a court of law) + English 'proceeding' (action, process).
