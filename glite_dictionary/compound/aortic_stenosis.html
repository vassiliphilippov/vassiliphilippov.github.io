aortic stenosis

1. NOUN

/ eɪˌɔrtɪk stəˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / eɪˌɔːtɪk stɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a narrowing of the opening of the aortic valve in the heart, restricting blood flow from the left ventricle to the aorta
narrowing of the aortic valve in the heart
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with severe aortic stenosis and required surgery.
Aortic stenosis can cause chest pain, fainting, or shortness of breath during exercise.
Doctors use echocardiography to assess the severity of aortic stenosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitral stenosis - narrowing of the opening of the mitral valve

Etymology

From Greek *aortē* (the aorta) and *stenōsis* (narrowing).
