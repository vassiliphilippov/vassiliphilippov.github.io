red beech

1. NOUN

/ rɛd bitʃ / (AmE), / rɛd biːtʃ / (BrE)
a large tree native to Australasia, known for its reddish wood and used in timber production
Australasian tree with reddish wood

Examples

The red beech is highly valued for its durable timber in New Zealand.
Several red beech trees line the hiking trail in the national park.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of red beech forests in Australasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nothofagus fusca - scientific name of red beech tree

2. NOUN

/ rɛd bitʃ / (AmE), / rɛd biːtʃ / (BrE)
a North American forest tree with light green leaves and edible nuts
North American forest tree with edible nuts

Examples

The red beech is prized for its attractive foliage and delicious nuts.
Many animals depend on the nuts of the red beech for food in the fall.
We hiked through a forest dominated by towering red beeches and maples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - a tree of the genus Fagus, typically with smooth gray bark and small edible nuts
