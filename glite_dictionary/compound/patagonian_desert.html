Patagonian Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˌpæt̬əˈɡoʊniən ˈdɛzərt / (AmE), / ˌpætəˈɡəʊniən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a vast semiarid region in southern Argentina, known as the largest desert in Argentina and the eighth largest in the world
semiarid desert region of southern Argentina

Examples

The Patagonian Desert is famous for its unique wildlife and expansive steppe landscape.
Winds sweeping across the Patagonian Desert shape the region's rugged terrain.
Scientists conduct climate studies in the Patagonian Desert to learn about arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Patagonia - region in southern South America covering parts of Argentina and Chile

Etymology

Patagonian from Patagonia, a region in southern South America; desert from Latin 'desertum' (wilderness).
