base rate

1. NOUN

/ beɪs reɪt / (AmE), / beɪs reɪt / (BrE)
the interest rate set by the Bank of England that serves as a benchmark for lending to other banks and for determining other interest rates in the economy
benchmark interest rate (Bank of England)
british

Examples

The Bank of England decided to raise its base rate for the third time this year.
Mortgage costs are affected by changes in the base rate.
Many loans have interest rates linked to the base rate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest rate - the percentage charged for borrowing money or earned by saving
