Paiute language

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪjuːt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪjuːt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Shoshonean language spoken by the Paiute people of the western United States
language of the Paiute people

Examples

Many elders are working to preserve the Paiute language for future generations.
She took a college course to learn the basics of the Paiute language.
Bilingual signs in the reservation include both English and the Paiute language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shoshone language - the language closely related to Paiute within the Uto-Aztecan family

Etymology

From Paiute (tribal self-designation) + English 'language'; Paiute derives from Spanish Payuchis or native endonym.
