muscle sense

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl sɛns / (AmE), / ˈmʌsəl sɛns / (BrE)
the ability to perceive the position and movement of one's muscles and limbs without visual cues; proprioception
awareness of limb and muscle movement
medical

Examples

Good muscle sense is essential for gymnasts to perform complex routines accurately.
Even with her eyes closed, she relied on her muscle sense to touch her nose.
Physical therapists often test a patient's muscle sense after an injury to assess recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of body parts
