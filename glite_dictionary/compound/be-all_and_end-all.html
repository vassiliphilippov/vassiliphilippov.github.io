be-all and end-all

1. NOUN

/ ˈbi ɔl ənd ˈɛnd ɔl / (AmE), / ˈbiː ɔːl ənd ˈɛnd ɔːl / (BrE)
the most important part or ultimate aim of something; the essential or supreme element
most important or ultimate aim

Examples

For some people, winning is the be-all and end-all of competition.
He treats money as the be-all and end-all of life, neglecting other values.
Academic achievement shouldn't be the be-all and end-all for students.

Synset Examples
