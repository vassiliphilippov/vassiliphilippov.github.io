dish antenna

1. NOUN

/ dɪʃ ænˈtɛnə / (AmE), / dɪʃ ænˈtɛnə / (BrE)
a directional antenna that uses a parabolic dish-shaped reflector to focus radio or microwave signals
directional antenna with a parabolic dish

Examples

The TV signal was received clearly thanks to the dish antenna mounted on the roof.
Many remote research stations rely on a dish antenna for satellite communications.
He adjusted the angle of the dish antenna to improve reception during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parabolic antenna - an alternative term for a dish antenna
  • Antonym: omnidirectional antenna - an antenna that receives signals from all directions
