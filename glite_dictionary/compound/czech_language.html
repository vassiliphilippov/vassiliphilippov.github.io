Czech language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɛk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the West Slavic language spoken mainly in the Czech Republic.
language of the Czech Republic

Examples

He is fluent in both English and Czech language.
They decided to study the Czech language before visiting Prague.
The Czech language uses a Latin-based alphabet with some unique diacritical marks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slovak language - the closely related West Slavic language spoken in Slovakia
  • Synonym: Czech - of or relating to the Czech language or people

Etymology

From Czech 'český jazyk', 'český' meaning 'Czech' and 'jazyk' meaning 'language'.
