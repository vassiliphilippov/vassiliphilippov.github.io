common noun

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən naʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən naʊn / (BrE)
a noun that refers to a general item or concept, rather than a specific name of a person, place, or thing
noun denoting a general class or type

Examples

The word common noun refers to any general item, such as 'city', 'dog', or 'book'.
In the sentence 'The cat sat on the mat,' both 'cat' and 'mat' are common nouns.
Words like 'teacher' and 'river' are examples of common nouns rather than specific names.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proper noun - a noun that names a specific individual, place, or organization
