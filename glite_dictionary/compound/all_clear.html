all clear

1. NOUN

/ ɔl ˈklɪr / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈklɪə / (BrE)
a signal, such as a siren, indicating that danger or an emergency has passed and it is safe again
signal that danger is over

Examples

After the air raid, the siren sounded the all clear so residents could leave their shelters.
We waited inside until the school principal gave the all clear.
The factory resumed operations once the all clear had been announced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alarm - an audible warning signal, especially to indicate danger

2. NOUN

/ ɔl ˈklɪr / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈklɪə / (BrE)
an official signal or message indicating that it is safe to proceed because there is no more danger or obstacles
signal that it is safe to proceed

Examples

After the fire drill, the warden gave the all clear so everyone could return to the building.
We waited in the shelter until we heard the all clear over the loudspeakers.
Don't move until you receive the all clear from the team leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go-ahead - permission to proceed
