vaccinia gangrenosa

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniə ˌɡæŋɡrəˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniə ˌɡæŋɡrɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
a severe and sometimes fatal complication of vaccinia infection, mainly affecting individuals with immune deficiencies, involving progressive tissue death at the site of the original lesion
severe complication of vaccinia with tissue death
medical

Examples

Patients with weakened immune systems are at risk for developing vaccinia gangrenosa after exposure to the vaccine.
The rapid progression of tissue death is a hallmark of vaccinia gangrenosa.
Early recognition of vaccinia gangrenosa is crucial to prevent fatal complications.

Etymology

From Latin 'vaccinia' (cowpox) + 'gangrenosa' (pertaining to gangrene) from Greek 'gangraina'.
