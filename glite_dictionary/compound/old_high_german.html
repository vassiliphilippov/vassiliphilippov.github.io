Old High German

1. NOUN

/ oʊld haɪ ˈdʒɝmən / (AmE), / əʊld haɪ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the form of the High German language spoken until roughly 1200, before the development of Middle High German
early form of High German before 1200

Examples

Scholars often study Old High German texts to understand the evolution of the German language.
The epic poem was originally composed in Old High German during the early Middle Ages.
Comparing Old High German with modern German reveals significant changes in grammar and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Middle High German - the stage of the High German language following Old High German, spoken roughly 1200–1500

Etymology

Old + High German, referring to the earliest documented stage of High German; High German from German Hochdeutsch.

2. NOUN

/ oʊld haɪ ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / əʊld haɪ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the earliest stage of the High German language, spoken and written before about 1200
early form of the High German language

Examples

Scholars often study Old High German texts to understand the development of the German language.
The epic poem "Hildebrandslied" is a notable example written in Old High German.
He specialized in medieval philology, focusing on Old High German grammar and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Middle High German - the German language as spoken and written between 1200 and 1500
  • Degree Higher: Modern High German - the standard literary German language as developed from the 16th century
  • Synonym: Old English - Anglo-Saxon, the ancestor of Modern English

Etymology

Formed from English 'old' + 'High German,' referring to the period and region of the German language.
