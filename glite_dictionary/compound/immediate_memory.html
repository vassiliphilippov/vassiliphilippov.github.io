immediate memory

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmiːdiət ˈmɛməri / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət ˈmɛməri / (BrE)
The ability to recall information or material right after it has been perceived, without delay.
memory for information just perceived

Examples

The test assessed her immediate memory by asking her to repeat a list of words right after hearing them.
Children often have excellent immediate memory, allowing them to recite songs after just a few listens.
Damage to certain brain regions can impair a person's immediate memory without affecting their long-term recall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short-term memory - memory that retains information for a short period, typically seconds to minutes
