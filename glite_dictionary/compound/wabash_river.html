Wabash River

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɑː.bɑʃ ˈrɪv.ər / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.bæʃ ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
A river in the United States that rises in western Ohio and flows southwest across Indiana, joining the Ohio River.
tributary of the Ohio River in the U.S.

Examples

The Wabash River played a crucial role in the early settlement of Indiana.
Flooding along the Wabash River has affected several towns this year.
Many species of fish can be found in the Wabash River and its tributaries.

Etymology

Named after the Miami-Illinois word 'waapaahšiiki', meaning 'pure white', referring to the river's limestone beds.
