marshalling yard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃəlɪŋ jɑrd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəlɪŋ jɑːd / (BrE)
A railway yard where trains are assembled and goods are loaded or unloaded.
rail yard where trains are assembled

Examples

The freight cars were organized in the marshalling yard before being attached to the outbound train.
Workers at the marshalling yard coordinated the assembly of long cargo trains every night.
After arriving, the train was disassembled in the marshalling yard for sorting and unloading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification yard - a railway yard for sorting freight cars

Etymology

From 'marshall' (to arrange in order) + 'yard' (an enclosed area), used since the 19th century in railway contexts.
