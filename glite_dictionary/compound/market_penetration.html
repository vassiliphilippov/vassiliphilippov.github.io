market penetration

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkət ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the extent to which a product or brand is recognized and bought by customers in a particular market
extent of product adoption in a market

Examples

Effective advertising helped the company achieve strong market penetration in its first year.
Low market penetration suggests that many potential customers are still unaware of the product.
Their strategy aims to increase market penetration by offering lower prices and better service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market share - the percentage of an industry's sales that a particular company controls
