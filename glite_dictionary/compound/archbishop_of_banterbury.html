Archbishop of Banterbury

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrtʃˈbɪʃəp əv ˈbæn.təˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃˈbɪʃ.əp əv ˈbæn.tə.b(ə)r.i / (BrE)
a fictitious or humorous title for a person who is being deliberately absurd, nonsensical, or telling unlikely stories
fictitious title for someone being absurd
informal british

Examples

Whenever Tom tells another unbelievable story, we call him the Archbishop of Banterbury.
She crowned herself the Archbishop of Banterbury after telling the most ridiculous joke of the night.
Don't believe everything he says—he's the self-proclaimed Archbishop of Banterbury.

Synset Examples

Nobody believed his tales; he was just the Archbishop of Banterbury.
He claimed to be the Archbishop of Banterbury at the party.
Her ridiculous stories sounded like the Archbishop of Banterbury.

Etymology

A play on 'Archbishop of Canterbury,' replacing 'Canterbury' with 'Banterbury' from 'banter' (playful conversation).
