plum spot

1. NOUN

/ plʌm spɑt / (AmE), / plʌm spɒt / (BrE)
a highly desirable or advantageous location, especially for a particular purpose
desirable or advantageous location

Examples

He managed to reserve a plum spot near the entrance of the festival.
Everyone wanted the plum spot by the window at the new café.
Their office is in a plum spot in the center of the city.

Synset Examples

He found a plum spot for his new restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime location - a location that is particularly good or desirable
  • Antonym: bad spot - a location that is undesirable or disadvantageous

Etymology

From 'plum' meaning something desirable (from the fruit as a metaphor for choice or reward) and 'spot' for place or location.
