first-come-first-serve

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɝst kʌm ˌfɝst sɝv / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst kʌm ˌfɜːst sɜːv / (BrE)
used to describe a system in which people are attended to or receive something in the order in which they arrive, without accepting reservations.
order of arrival; no reservations

Examples

Tickets for the concert are available on a first-come-first-serve basis, so be sure to arrive early.
The seats in the theatre are first-come-first-serve, so customers choose them when they arrive.
All parking spaces at the festival are strictly first-come-first-serve; no reservations are accepted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first-come-first-served - prioritizing people according to the order they arrive

Etymology

Formed by compounding the phrases 'first come' and 'first serve', indicating priority based on order of arrival.
