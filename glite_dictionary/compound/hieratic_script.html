hieratic script

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɪrətɪk skrɪpt / (AmE), / haɪˈɪərətɪk skrɪpt / (BrE)
A cursive form of ancient Egyptian writing, derived from hieroglyphics and chiefly used by priests.
cursive ancient Egyptian writing used by priests

Examples

The hieratic script was mainly used for religious and administrative texts in ancient Egypt.
Scholars deciphered the hieratic script to better understand early Egyptian rituals.
Many papyrus documents are written in hieratic script, reflecting its importance in temple life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hieroglyphics - pictorial writing system of ancient Egypt

Etymology

From Greek 'hieratikos' (priestly), related to 'hiereus' (priest).
