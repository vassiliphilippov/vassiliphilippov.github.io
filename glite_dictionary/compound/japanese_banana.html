Japanese banana

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz bəˈnænə / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz bəˈnænə / (BrE)
an Asiatic banana plant (Musa basjoo) grown especially for its decorative leaves, particularly in Japan
banana plant grown for foliage in Japan

Examples

Gardeners in temperate regions often plant the Japanese banana for its lush, tropical appearance.
The Japanese banana can survive cold winters better than other banana species.
He admired the impressive leaves of the Japanese banana growing by the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - a long curved fruit with yellow skin and soft sweet flesh
