swagger stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈswæɡər stɪk / (AmE), / ˈswæɡə stɪk / (BrE)
a short cane or stick, often covered in leather, carried by army officers as a symbol of authority or for ceremonial purposes
short ceremonial cane carried by army officers

Examples

The colonel carried his swagger stick with him at every inspection.
He tapped the ground with his swagger stick as he addressed the troops.
The museum display included a swagger stick once owned by a famous general.
