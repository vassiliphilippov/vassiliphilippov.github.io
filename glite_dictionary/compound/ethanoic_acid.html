ethanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌθænoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / iːˌθanəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent liquid organic acid, used in manufacturing plastics, pharmaceuticals, and as a main component of vinegar
organic acid used in industry and vinegar
medical

Examples

The laboratory ordered more ethanoic acid for their organic chemistry experiments.
Plastic production often involves the use of ethanoic acid as a precursor.
Vinegar contains ethanoic acid as its main active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetic acid - more common name for ethanoic acid

Etymology

From 'ethan(e)' (the root hydrocarbon) and '-oic acid' (organic acid suffix).
