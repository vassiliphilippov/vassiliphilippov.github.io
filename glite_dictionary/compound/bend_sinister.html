bend sinister

1. NOUN

/ bɛnd ˈsɪnɪstər / (AmE), / bɛnd ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
A diagonal band running from the top right to bottom left of a shield in heraldry, historically sometimes used as a mark of illegitimacy.
heraldic diagonal band (top right to bottom left)

Examples

The family crest features a bend sinister, indicating a historical lineage of illegitimacy.
In heraldry, a bend sinister runs from the shield's upper right to its lower left.
The knight's shield bore a prominent bend sinister across a field of blue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bend - a band or stripe running from top left to bottom right on a shield in heraldry

Etymology

From heraldic use; 'bend' from Old French 'bende', a stripe or band, and 'sinister' from Latin 'sinister', meaning 'left'.
