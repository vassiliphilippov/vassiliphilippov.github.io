theatre director

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiətɚ dəˈrɛktɚ / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə dɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
a person responsible for overseeing and guiding the artistic aspects and staging of a theatre production, including supervising the actors
person in charge of directing a stage production

Examples

The theatre director worked closely with the actors to bring his vision to life on stage.
As a theatre director, she is known for her innovative approach to classical plays.
The success of the musical was largely due to the creativity of its theatre director.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director - person in charge of directing a performance or production
  • Antonym: producer - person who oversees the financial and administrative aspects of a play or show
