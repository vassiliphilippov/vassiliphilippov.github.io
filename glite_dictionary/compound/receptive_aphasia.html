receptive aphasia

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛptɪv əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptɪv əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person can produce fluent but meaningless speech and has severe difficulty understanding spoken or written words.
type of aphasia with impaired comprehension
medical

Examples

Although she spoke in long sentences, her receptive aphasia made understanding conversations nearly impossible.
After his stroke, John developed receptive aphasia and could no longer understand written instructions.
Doctors diagnosed her with receptive aphasia when she repeatedly gave fluent but nonsensical answers to questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wernicke's aphasia - type of receptive aphasia caused by damage to Wernicke's area in the brain
  • Antonym: expressive aphasia - aphasia characterized by difficulty producing speech but relatively preserved comprehension

Etymology

From Latin 'recipere' (to receive) + Greek 'aphasia' (speechlessness).
