florist's chrysanthemum

1. NOUN

/ flɔːrɪsts krɪˈsænθəməm / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪsts krɪˈsanθəməm / (BrE)
a plant of the species Chrysanthemum morifolium, native to China, widely cultivated for its large, colorful flowers used by florists
a cultivated chrysanthemum flower species

Examples

The florist's chrysanthemum is prized for its vibrant blooms and long vase life.
Many gardens feature the florist's chrysanthemum for autumn displays.
New hybrids of the florist's chrysanthemum are constantly being developed by botanists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysanthemum - a flowering plant of the genus Chrysanthemum

Etymology

From Latin 'chrysanthemum', from Ancient Greek 'khrusanthemom' (golden flower); 'florist's' denotes use by florists.
