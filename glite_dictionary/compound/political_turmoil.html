political turmoil

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl ˈtɝːmɔɪl / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈtɜːmɔɪl / (BrE)
a state of confusion, disorder, or instability in the political system of a country, often involving protests, unrest, or changes in government
instability or disorder in political affairs

Examples

The country descended into political turmoil after the disputed election results were announced.
Years of political turmoil have left the economy in a fragile state.
Investors are hesitant to enter regions experiencing political turmoil.

Synset Examples

A suspended congress can lead to political turmoil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: political stability - a situation in which a country’s political system is steady and free from turmoil
