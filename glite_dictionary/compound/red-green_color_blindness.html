red-green color blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˌɡrin ˈkʌlɚ ˌblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˌɡriːn ˈkʌl.ə ˌblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
a form of color blindness in which it is difficult to distinguish between red and green hues
difficulty distinguishing red and green
medical

Examples

People with red-green color blindness may have trouble telling if a traffic light is red or green.
Genetic testing can confirm if someone has red-green color blindness.
Many art teachers provide special resources for students with red-green color blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish between certain colors
  • Antonym: normal color vision - the ability to distinguish all colors typically
