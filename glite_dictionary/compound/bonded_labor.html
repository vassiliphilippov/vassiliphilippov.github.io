bonded labor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndɪd ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɒndɪd ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
A system in which workers are forced to work for an employer to pay off a debt, often under exploitative conditions and sometimes involving their families.
forced labor to pay off a debt

Examples

Many families in rural areas are trapped in bonded labor for generations without hope of escape.
The government introduced stricter laws to combat the practice of bonded labor in factories.
Human rights organizations work to free children from bonded labor in the brick kilns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debt bondage - the condition of being bound to work to pay off a debt
  • Synonym: forced labor - work performed under coercion or threat
