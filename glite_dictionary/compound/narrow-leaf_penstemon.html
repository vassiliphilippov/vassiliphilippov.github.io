narrow-leaf penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊ liːf ˈpɛnstɪmən / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ liːf ˈpɛnstɪmən / (BrE)
a plant of the western United States with small narrow leaves and blue-violet flowers in long open clusters, found from Utah and Colorado to New Mexico and Arizona
western US plant with blue-violet flowers

Examples

The narrow-leaf penstemon is admired for its stunning blue-violet blooms in late spring.
Hikers in Colorado often spot clusters of narrow-leaf penstemon along sunlit trails.
Botanists recorded the presence of narrow-leaf penstemon in both New Mexico and Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penstemon - a genus of plants with showy tubular flowers, commonly known as beardtongues
