trigonometric function

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪɡənəˈmɛtrɪk ˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrɪɡənəˈmɛtrɪk ˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical function of an angle, often defined as the ratio of the lengths of sides in a right triangle, such as sine, cosine, or tangent
function relating angles to triangle side ratios

Examples

The trigonometric function cosine is used to determine the length of the adjacent side in a right triangle.
Graphing a trigonometric function often reveals its periodic nature.
Students learn how each trigonometric function relates angles to ratios of triangle sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sine - a trigonometric function giving the ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse
  • Synonym: cosine - a trigonometric function giving the ratio of adjacent side to hypotenuse
  • Synonym: tangent - a trigonometric function giving the ratio of opposite side to adjacent side

Etymology

From Greek 'trigonon' (triangle) + 'metron' (measure) + English 'function'.
