Joshua tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑʃuə tri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒʃuə tri / (BrE)
A large, branching yucca plant native to the southwestern United States, with spiky leaves and clusters of greenish white flowers.
desert tree-like yucca of the southwestern US

Examples

The Joshua tree can grow up to forty feet high in the Mojave Desert.
Tourists flock to Joshua Tree National Park to see the iconic Joshua tree landscapes.
Scientists study how the Joshua tree adapts to harsh desert conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a genus of perennial shrubs and trees with rosettes of evergreen leaves

Etymology

Named after the biblical figure Joshua, because of the tree's shape, said to resemble Joshua reaching up to the sky in prayer.
