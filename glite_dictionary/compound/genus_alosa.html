genus Alosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈloʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈləʊ.sə / (BrE)
A genus of fish commonly known as shads, found in the herring family Clupeidae.
a genus of shad fish
medical

Examples

The genus Alosa includes several species of shad found in European and North American rivers.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of fish in the genus Alosa.
Many commercial fisheries depend on various members of the genus Alosa for their harvests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shad - a common name for fish in the genus Alosa, a type of herring

Etymology

From Latin 'Alosa', the name given to shad.
