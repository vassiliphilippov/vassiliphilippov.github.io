Volga River

1. PROPN

/ ˈvoʊlɡə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈvɒlɡə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in Europe, flowing through western Russia into the Caspian Sea
longest river in Europe, in Russia

Examples

The Volga River is an important transportation route in Russia.
Many cities, including Volgograd and Kazan, are located along the Volga River.
Tourists often take cruises down the Volga River to admire the scenery.

Etymology

From Russian 'Volga' (Волга), the native name of the river.
