toad rush

1. NOUN

/ toʊd rʌʃ / (AmE), / təʊd rʌʃ / (BrE)
a low-growing annual rush (Juncus bufonius) commonly found in damp, low-lying ground across temperate regions worldwide
a type of small annual rush plant

Examples

The marsh was thick with toad rush and other wetland species.
Farmers often find toad rush growing along the edges of irrigated fields.
Botanists survey toad rush populations to monitor changes in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a grass-like plant of the genus Juncus, typically found in wetlands
