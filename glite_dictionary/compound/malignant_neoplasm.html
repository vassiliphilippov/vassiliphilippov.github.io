malignant neoplasm

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪɡnənt ˈniː.oʊ.plæzəm / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡ.nənt ˈniː.əʊ.plæz.əm / (BrE)
A tumor that is cancerous and tends to invade nearby tissues or spread to other parts of the body.
cancerous tumor
medical

Examples

The biopsy confirmed that the mass was a malignant neoplasm, requiring immediate treatment.
Smoking is a major risk factor for developing a malignant neoplasm in the lungs.
The doctor explained that a malignant neoplasm can metastasize to other organs if not treated early.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benign neoplasm - a non-cancerous tumor that does not invade other tissues
  • Synonym: cancer - a disease caused by uncontrolled division of abnormal cells

Etymology

From Latin *malignus* (evil in nature), and Greek *neoplasma* (new formation).
