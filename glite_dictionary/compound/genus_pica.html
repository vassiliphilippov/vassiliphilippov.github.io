genus Pica

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈpaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈpiː.kə / (BrE)
A genus of birds including the Eurasian magpie and its close relatives, characterized by long tails and black-and-white plumage.
genus of magpies (birds)

Examples

The genus Pica includes several types of true magpies found in Europe and Asia.
Scientists studied the nesting habits of birds in the genus Pica.
Members of the genus Pica are known for their distinctive black and white feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magpie - A long-tailed bird of the crow family known for its black-and-white plumage

Etymology

From Latin 'pica', meaning 'magpie'.
