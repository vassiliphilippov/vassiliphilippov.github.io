family Galbulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli gælˈbjuːlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli galˈbjuːlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical American birds known as jacamars, characterized by slender bodies, long bills, and iridescent plumage.
family of tropical birds (jacamars)

Examples

The family Galbulidae includes several species of jacamars native to Central and South America.
Ornithologists study the family Galbulidae to learn more about tropical bird evolution.
Members of the family Galbulidae are recognized for their striking metallic colors and slender bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacamar - A tropical American bird with a long bill and iridescent plumage

Etymology

From New Latin Galbulidae, from Galbula (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
