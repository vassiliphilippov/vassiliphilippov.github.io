cohune palm

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈhuːn pɑm / (AmE), / kəˈhuːn pɑːm / (BrE)
A tall tropical American palm tree (Attalea cohune) whose large nuts yield valuable oil and a substance used as vegetable ivory.
tropical palm tree yielding oil and vegetable ivory

Examples

The cohune palm is commonly found in the rainforests of Central America.
Artisans carve the nuts of the cohune palm to make decorative buttons and jewelry.
The oil extracted from the cohune palm is used both for cooking and in cosmetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory palm - a palm tree whose nuts are used as a source of vegetable ivory

Etymology

From Spanish 'cohune', of indigenous origin, referring to the palm species Attalea cohune.
