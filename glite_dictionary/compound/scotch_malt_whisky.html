Scotch malt whisky

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ mɔlt ˈwɪski / (AmE), / skɒtʃ mɔːlt ˈwɪski / (BrE)
Whisky distilled in Scotland, especially one made from malted barley in a pot still.
whisky from Scotland made from malted barley

Examples

He poured himself a glass of Scotch malt whisky to enjoy after dinner.
The distillery is renowned for its award-winning Scotch malt whisky.
Collectors often seek out rare bottles of Scotch malt whisky for their unique flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scotch whisky - whisky distilled in Scotland
  • Antonym: blended whisky - whisky made by blending different grained or malt whiskies

Etymology

Scotch, from Scottish (relating to Scotland), plus malt whisky, whisky made from malted barley.
