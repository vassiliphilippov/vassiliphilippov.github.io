reindeer lichen

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌdɪr ˈlaɪkən / (AmE), / ˈreɪndɪə ˈlaɪkən / (BrE)
a greyish, branching lichen found in Arctic and some northern temperate regions, serving as a main food source for reindeer and caribou
a grey, branching Arctic lichen

Examples

Reindeer lichen carpets the tundra in many parts of Scandinavia.
During the harsh winter months, caribou survive by eating reindeer lichen.
Botanists studied the adaptability of reindeer lichen to extreme cold environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lichen - a composite organism formed from algae or cyanobacteria living among filaments of fungi
