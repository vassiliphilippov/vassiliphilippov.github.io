to fly contact

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ˈkɑn.tækt / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈkɒn.tækt / (BrE)
to pilot an aircraft by using visible landmarks or points of reference, rather than relying on instruments
pilot an aircraft using visual landmarks

Examples

During clear weather, pilots often fly contact along familiar routes to ensure safety.
Because of dense fog, the trainee was not allowed to fly contact and used the instruments instead.
He learned to fly contact before being fully trained to fly by instruments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fly instruments - to pilot an aircraft using navigational instruments
