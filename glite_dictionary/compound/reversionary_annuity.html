reversionary annuity

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɝːʒəˌnɛri əˈnuːəti / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃənəri əˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
An annuity paid to a second person if the original beneficiary becomes unable to receive payments, typically after their death or disqualification.
annuity payable to a substitute beneficiary

Examples

After her husband's death, the reversionary annuity provided her with a steady income.
The reversionary annuity will begin only if the primary beneficiary passes away.
He arranged a reversionary annuity to ensure his sister would be cared for if she outlived his wife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annuity - a fixed sum of money paid to someone each year, typically for the rest of their life

Etymology

From 'reversionary' (relating to a reversion, from Law Latin 'reversionarius') and 'annuity' (from Middle English, from Old French 'anuité').
