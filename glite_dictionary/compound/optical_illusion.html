optical illusion

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl ɪˈluʒən / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ɪˈluːʒən / (BrE)
something that deceives the eye by appearing to be other than it is; a visual phenomenon where what is perceived differs from reality.
something that visually deceives the observer

Examples

The spinning wheel appeared to be moving backwards due to an optical illusion.
Artists sometimes use shading to create an optical illusion of depth on a flat surface.
That impossible staircase in the drawing is actually just an optical illusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mirage - an optical effect that makes you see objects that are not really there
  • Antonym: hallucination - a perception of something that does not exist outside the mind

Etymology

From optical (relating to vision, from Greek 'optikos') + illusion (from Latin 'illusio', deception).
