medieval mode

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈdiːvəl moʊd / (AmE), / mɪˈdiːvəl məʊd / (BrE)
any of the musical modes used in Gregorian chant and Western music until about 1600, originating from Greek modes
musical scale in early Western music

Examples

The composition is written entirely in the medieval mode of Dorian.
He studied each medieval mode to better understand Gregorian chant.
Many hymns from the Middle Ages utilize a medieval mode rather than modern scales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gregorian mode - musical mode used in Gregorian chant
  • Degree Higher: Ionian mode - a type of medieval mode, equivalent to the modern major scale
  • Synonym: Greek mode - musical scale system from Ancient Greece

Etymology

From 'medieval' (Middle Ages) + 'mode' (from Latin 'modus', measure, manner).
