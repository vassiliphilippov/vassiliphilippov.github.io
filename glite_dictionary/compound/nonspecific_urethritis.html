nonspecific urethritis

1. NOUN

/ nɑn.spəˈsɪf.ɪk jʊˌriːˈθraɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / nɒn.spəˈsɪf.ɪk jʊəˌriːˈθraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the urethra caused by an unidentified agent, not due to gonorrhea
urethra inflammation of unknown cause
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with nonspecific urethritis after other infections were ruled out.
Symptoms of nonspecific urethritis can include pain during urination and a frequent urge to urinate.
Antibiotics are often prescribed to treat nonspecific urethritis even if the exact organism is unknown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urethritis - inflammation of the urethra

Etymology

From 'non-' (not), 'specific' (from Latin 'specificus'), and 'urethritis' (from Greek 'ourēthra' + '-itis' for inflammation).
