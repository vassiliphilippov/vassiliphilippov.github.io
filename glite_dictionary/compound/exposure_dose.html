exposure dose

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspoʊʒər doʊs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊʒə dəʊs / (BrE)
A measurement of the amount of radiation received, considered in terms of its potential to produce ionization in matter.
measurement of ionizing radiation received
medical

Examples

The technician calculated the exposure dose to ensure the level was safe for patients.
Limiting the exposure dose is essential when working with X-rays in medical settings.
Regulations define maximum exposure dose limits for radiation workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbed dose - amount of radiation energy absorbed per unit mass
