bumper crop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌmpɚ krɑp / (AmE), / ˈbʌmpə krɒp / (BrE)
an unusually large harvest of a particular crop
exceptionally large harvest

Examples

Thanks to favorable weather, the region reported a bumper crop of wheat this season.
Every farmer hoped for a bumper crop after last year's disappointing yields.
The news highlighted the economic impact of the bumper crop on local communities.

Synset Examples

Farmers are celebrating a bumper crop of corn.
The orchard produced a bumper crop of apples.
This year, we had a bumper crop of tomatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvest - the process or period of gathering in crops

Etymology

The word 'bumper' in this sense originates from early use meaning 'something unusually large', combined with 'crop' referring to agricultural yield.
