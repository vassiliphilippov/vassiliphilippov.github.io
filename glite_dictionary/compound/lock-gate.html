lock-gate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑk ˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɒk ˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
a gate at each end of a lock on a canal or river, used to control the water level and allow boats to pass through
gate controlling water in a canal lock

Examples

The old lock-gate was replaced to improve navigation for boats on the canal.
After heavy rain, the lock-gate had to be carefully operated to prevent flooding.
The engineer inspected the lock-gate to ensure it was functioning properly before allowing any vessels to pass.

Etymology

lock (from Old English loc, an enclosure) + gate (from Old English geat, an opening or passage).
