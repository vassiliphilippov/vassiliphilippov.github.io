Japanese chestnut

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈtʃɛsˌnʌt / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
A deciduous tree (Castanea crenata), native to Japan, known for its spreading form and short trunk, cultivated for its edible nuts.
deciduous tree native to Japan

Examples

The Japanese chestnut is commonly grown in orchards for its large edible nuts.
Gardeners value the Japanese chestnut for its resilience and attractive foliage.
Unlike the American species, the Japanese chestnut has a broader canopy and a shorter trunk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - a deciduous tree producing edible brown nuts
