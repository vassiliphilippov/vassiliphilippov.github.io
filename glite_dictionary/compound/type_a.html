type A

1. NOUN

/ taɪp eɪ / (AmE), / taɪp eɪ / (BrE)
the blood group whose red cells carry the A antigen
blood group with A antigen
medical

Examples

Before the surgery, the nurse checked that my blood was type A.
People with type A blood can donate to others with type A or AB blood groups.
He discovered he was type A after a routine health check.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: type B - blood group whose red cells carry the B antigen
  • Degree Lower: type O - blood group with neither A nor B antigens
