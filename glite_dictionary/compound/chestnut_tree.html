chestnut tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt tri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.nət triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree with prickly seed cases, yielding edible nuts and hard wood, with leaves turning yellow-brown in autumn.
deciduous tree with edible nuts

Examples

A large chestnut tree shaded the bench in the park.
In autumn, the leaves of the chestnut tree turn a golden brown.
They gathered chestnut tree nuts to roast by the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak tree - a large deciduous tree with lobed leaves and acorns

Etymology

From Old English *cisten* (chestnut) and *trēow* (tree), ultimately from Latin *castanea*.
