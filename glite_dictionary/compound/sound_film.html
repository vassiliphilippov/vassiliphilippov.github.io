sound film

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd fɪlm / (AmE), / saʊnd fɪlm / (BrE)
a motion-picture film that includes synchronized recorded sound, such as dialogue and sound effects
motion picture with synchronized sound

Examples

The introduction of the sound film revolutionized the movie industry in the late 1920s.
She prefers watching old classics, both silent and sound films.
The documentary explores how the first sound film was produced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: silent film - a motion-picture film without recorded sound
