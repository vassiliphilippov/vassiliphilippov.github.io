genus Carcharodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkɑrkəˈroʊdɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkɑːkəˈrəʊdən / (BrE)
A genus of large, predatory sharks that includes the great white shark.
a genus of predatory sharks

Examples

The genus Carcharodon includes the fearsome great white shark.
Paleontologists have studied fossils from the genus Carcharodon to understand shark evolution.
Members of the genus Carcharodon are known for their large size and powerful jaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great white shark - a large, predatory shark species in this genus

Etymology

From Greek karcharodōn, meaning 'jagged tooth.'
