pure coonery

1. NOUN

/ pjʊr ˈkuːnəri / (AmE), / pjʊə ˈkuːnəri / (BrE)
Offensive behavior or performance characterized by exaggerated, stereotypical caricatures of Black people, often intended to mock or entertain based on racial stereotypes.
offensive, exaggerated racial caricature
offensive

Examples

Many critics denounced the film's portrayal of Black characters as pure coonery.
The performance was condemned for being little more than pure coonery and reinforcing harmful stereotypes.
Historians often point to certain old cartoons as examples of pure coonery in entertainment history.

Synset Examples

His act was pure coonery, making everyone laugh.

Etymology

From 'pure' and 'coonery'; 'coon' is an offensive term for a Black person, rooted in 19th-century American minstrel shows.
