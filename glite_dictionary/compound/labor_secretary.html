Labor Secretary

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ səˈkrɛː.tɛɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə səˈkrɛt.ər.i / (BrE)
the head of the Department of Labor, typically in a government cabinet
head of the Department of Labor

Examples

The Labor Secretary announced new workplace safety regulations yesterday.
After months of searching, the President nominated her as the next Labor Secretary.
Many policies introduced by the Labor Secretary affect millions of workers nationwide.

Synset Examples

the post of Labor Secretary was created in 1913

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Secretary of Labor - another form for the government position heading the Department of Labor

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ səˈkrɛ.tɛri / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə səˈkrɛ.təri / (BrE)
the head of the Department of Labor in the United States government, responsible for labor issues and policies
head of US Department of Labor
american

Examples

The Labor Secretary announced new regulations to protect workers' rights.
She was the first woman to serve as Labor Secretary in the United States.
During the economic crisis, the Labor Secretary played a key role in negotiating with unions.

Synset Examples

the first Labor Secretary was William B. Wilson who was appointed by President Wilson
