Our Lady's mild thistle

1. NOUN

/ aʊr ˈleɪdiz ˈmaɪld ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / aʊə ˈleɪdiz ˈmaɪld ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A tall biennial thistle of the Old World with large, white-blotched leaves and purple flower heads, also known as milk thistle (Silybum marianum), often naturalized in California and South America.
a thistle with blotched leaves; Silybum marianum

Examples

The herbalist recommended Our Lady's mild thistle for liver support and detoxification.
In California, Our Lady's mild thistle has spread widely along roadsides and open fields.
The characteristic white-blotched leaves make Our Lady's mild thistle easy to identify among other thistles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milk thistle - another common name for Silybum marianum

Etymology

The name refers to the Virgin Mary ('Our Lady'), linked in folklore to the plant's white-blotched leaves.
