soft drug

1. NOUN

/ sɔft drʌɡ / (AmE), / sɒft drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that is regarded as relatively mild in its effects and not likely to cause addiction
relatively mild, non-addictive drug

Examples

In some countries, marijuana is classified as a soft drug rather than a hard drug.
Debates about legalization often focus on the distinction between soft drugs and hard drugs.
The doctor explained the difference between a soft drug like cannabis and stronger substances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard drug - a drug that is considered highly addictive and harmful
