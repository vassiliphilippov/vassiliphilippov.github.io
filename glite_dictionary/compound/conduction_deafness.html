conduction deafness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌkʃən ˈdɛfnəs / (AmE), / kənˈdʌkʃən ˈdɛfnɪs / (BrE)
A type of hearing loss caused by problems with the bones or structures of the middle ear, preventing sound from being conducted to the inner ear.
hearing loss due to middle ear problems
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with conduction deafness after examining his middle ear.
People with conduction deafness may benefit from certain types of hearing aids.
Chronic ear infections can sometimes lead to conduction deafness if not properly treated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensorineural deafness - hearing loss due to damage of the inner ear or nerve pathways

Etymology

From Latin 'conductio' (a bringing together) and 'deafness'.
