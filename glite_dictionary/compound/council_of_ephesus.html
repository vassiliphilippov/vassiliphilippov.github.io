Council of Ephesus

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl əv ˈɛf.ə.səs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl ɒv ˈɛf.ɪ.səs / (BrE)
the third ecumenical council of the Christian Church, held in 431, which affirmed Mary as the mother of God and condemned the teachings of Pelagius
third ecumenical council held in 431

Examples

The decision of the Council of Ephesus had a profound impact on Christian theology.
Many doctrines were shaped by the debates at the Council of Ephesus.
Scholars frequently refer to the Council of Ephesus when discussing the early church councils.
