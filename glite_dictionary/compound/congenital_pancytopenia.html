congenital pancytopenia

1. NOUN

/ kənˌdʒɛnɪtl ˌpænsaɪtoʊˈpiːniə / (AmE), / kənˌdʒɛnɪtəl ˌpænsɪtəʊˈpiːnɪə / (BrE)
A rare inherited condition characterized by a deficiency of all types of blood cells and underdevelopment of the bone marrow.
rare inherited anemia with deficient blood cells
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with congenital pancytopenia after several blood tests showed abnormal counts.
Patients with congenital pancytopenia require careful monitoring and specialized treatment for their blood disorders.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families affected by congenital pancytopenia.

Etymology

From Latin 'congenitus' (born together) and Greek 'pan-' (all), 'kytos' (cell), 'penia' (poverty, deficiency).
