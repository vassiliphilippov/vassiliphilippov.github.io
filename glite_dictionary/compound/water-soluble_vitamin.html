water-soluble vitamin

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈsɑljubəl ˈvaɪtəmɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈsɒljʊbəl ˈvɪtəmɪn / (BrE)
a vitamin that dissolves in water and is not stored in the body, requiring regular replenishment from diet
vitamin that dissolves in water
medical

Examples

Vitamin C is a water-soluble vitamin, so your body does not store it for long periods.
Doctors recommend daily intake of water-soluble vitamins to maintain good health.
Unlike fat-soluble vitamins, water-soluble vitamins are excreted in urine if taken in excess.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fat-soluble vitamin - a vitamin soluble in fat, not in water
