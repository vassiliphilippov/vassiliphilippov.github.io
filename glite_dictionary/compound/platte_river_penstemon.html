Platte River penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæt ˈrɪvɚ ˈpɛn.stə.mən / (AmE), / ˈplæt ˈrɪv.ə ˈpɛn.stə.mən / (BrE)
an upright plant native to parts of the western United States, with blue-violet flowers arranged in rings near the stem tips
upright plant with blue-violet flowers, western US

Examples

The Platte River penstemon blooms each spring in the dry hills of Wyoming.
Botanists surveyed the Platte River penstemon populations to monitor their health.
I photographed a Platte River penstemon while hiking near the Idaho-Utah border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penstemon - a genus of mostly North American plants with tubular flowers
