basset hound

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsət haʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪt haʊnd / (BrE)
a breed of dog with short legs, a long body, and long, drooping ears, originally bred for hunting by scent
a dog breed with short legs and long ears

Examples

The basset hound sniffed along the ground, following the scent trail with determination.
Their basset hound has such long ears that they almost touch the floor when he walks.
We adopted a basset hound from the shelter and quickly fell in love with his gentle nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hound - a type of dog bred for hunting

Etymology

From French 'bas' (low) and 'hound', relating to its short stature.
