safflower seed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfˌlaʊ.ər sid / (AmE), / ˈsæf.laʊ.ə siːd / (BrE)
the seed of the safflower plant, used as bird feed, for oil extraction, or as food
seed of the safflower plant

Examples

Many commercial birdseed mixes contain safflower seed because birds like cardinals prefer them.
Cold-pressed safflower seed oil is favored for its light taste and high smoke point.
The nutritionist recommended adding safflower seed to salads for extra crunch and healthy fats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safflower - a thistle-like plant grown for its oil-rich seeds
