false sago

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈseɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈseɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a cycad native to southeastern India with palmlike foliage, not a true sago palm
a cycad plant with palmlike leaves

Examples

The false sago is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
Unlike true sago palms, false sago is actually a cycad and not a palm at all.
Gardeners should note that false sago can be toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sago palm - a tropical plant whose pith is used to produce sago starch
