depth of knowledge

1. NOUN

/ dɛpθ əv ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / dɛpθ əv ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thorough and extensive understanding of a subject or area
thorough understanding of a subject

Examples

His depth of knowledge on ancient history made him a valuable resource for the team.
Employers appreciate candidates who demonstrate true depth of knowledge in their field.
The professor's depth of knowledge was evident in every lecture.

Synset Examples

The depth of her knowledge impressed everyone at the seminar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expertise - expert skill or knowledge in a particular field
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness in general
