western coral snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈkɔrəl sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈkɒrəl sneɪk / (BrE)
a venomous species of coral snake found in Central America and the southwestern United States, known for its distinctive red, yellow, and black banding
a venomous snake of Central America and southwestern US

Examples

The western coral snake has brightly colored bands that warn predators of its venom.
Researchers studied the habitat of the western coral snake in the deserts of Arizona.
If bitten by a western coral snake, seek medical attention immediately due to its potent neurotoxin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - any venomous snake of the family Elapidae marked by bright bands
