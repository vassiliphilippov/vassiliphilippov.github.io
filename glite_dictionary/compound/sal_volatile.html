sal volatile

1. NOUN

/ sæl vəˈlætəl / (AmE), / sæl vəˈleɪtaɪl / (BrE)
A solution of ammonium carbonate in ammonia water and alcohol, formerly used as smelling salts or a stimulant.
ammonia-based solution used as smelling salts

Examples

When someone fainted, the nurse quickly reached for the sal volatile to revive her.
Physicians in the 19th century often administered sal volatile to patients feeling faint.
He carried a small vial of sal volatile in his pocket for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelling salts - aromatic compounds used to arouse consciousness

Etymology

From Latin 'sal' (salt) + 'volatilis' (that can fly, evaporate), referring to its volatile nature.
