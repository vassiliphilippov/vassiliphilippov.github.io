Occam's broom

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑkəmz ˈbruːm / (AmE), / ˈɒkəmz ˈbruːm / (BrE)
A metaphor for deliberately ignoring, omitting, or sweeping away complexities or inconvenient facts to make an issue appear simpler than it actually is.
metaphor for ignoring complexities

Examples

Some scientists warn that using Occam's broom can undermine the integrity of research findings.
The politician was accused of wielding Occam's broom to dismiss inconvenient statistics.
When writing his report, he swept the contradictions under Occam's broom, leaving out essential details.

Synset Examples

Occam's broom can sometimes hide important details.
His explanation was simple, like Occam's broom in action.
They used Occam's broom to sweep away the complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Occam's razor - the principle of preferring the simplest explanation

Etymology

Modeled after 'Occam's razor'; refers to philosopher William of Ockham, using 'broom' to suggest sweeping problems out of sight.
