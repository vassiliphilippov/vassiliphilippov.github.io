style of architecture

1. NOUN

/ staɪl əv ˌɑrkɪˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / staɪl əv ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a distinctive manner or design in which buildings or structures are constructed, defined by characteristic features and principles
distinctive design approach in building

Examples

Gothic is a famous style of architecture known for its pointed arches and flying buttresses.
Many cities boast more than one style of architecture, ranging from classical to modernist buildings.
The museum hosts a special exhibit on the evolution of each style of architecture throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architectural style - a particular characteristic method of building design
