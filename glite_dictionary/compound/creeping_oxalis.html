creeping oxalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ɑkˈsælɪs / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ɒkˈsælɪs / (BrE)
a widely spread, mat-forming weed with many branches, found in many parts of the world; often considered invasive in gardens and lawns.
mat-forming cosmopolitan weed

Examples

Creeping oxalis can quickly cover garden beds if not controlled regularly.
Many homeowners struggle to eradicate creeping oxalis from their lawns.
The tiny yellow flowers of creeping oxalis are often mistaken for those of other weeds.

Etymology

From Latin 'oxalis' (a genus of plants) and English 'creeping' referring to its growth habit.
