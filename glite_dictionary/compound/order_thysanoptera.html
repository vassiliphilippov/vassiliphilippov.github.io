order Thysanoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ θaɪˌsænəpˈtɛrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə θaɪˌsænɒpˈtɛrə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of insects, commonly known as thrips, characterized by fringed wings and minute size.
biological order of thrips insects

Examples

Scientists studied several species in the order Thysanoptera collected from agricultural fields.
The order Thysanoptera includes both plant-feeding and predatory insects.
Damage to crops was attributed to pests belonging to the order Thysanoptera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - minute insects with fringed wings

Etymology

From Greek 'thysan' (fringe) and 'ptera' (wings), referring to the insects’ fringed wings.
