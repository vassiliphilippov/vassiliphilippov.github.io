tricolour television tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌkʌlər ˌtɛləˈvɪʒən tuːb / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌkʌlə ˌtɛlɪˈvɪʒən tjuːb / (BrE)
a picture tube used in color televisions that combines three primary colors to produce the full spectrum of colors on the screen
color TV picture tube using 3 primary colors

Examples

Early color TVs relied on the tricolour television tube to display realistic images.
The tricolour television tube uses red, green, and blue phosphors to produce different colors.
Engineers experimented extensively before perfecting the tricolour television tube for mass production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathode ray tube - an older type of display device using electron beams to create images on a screen
