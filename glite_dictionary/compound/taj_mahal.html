Taj Mahal

1. PROPN

/ tɑdʒ məˈhɑl / (AmE), / tɑːʒ məˈhɑːl / (BrE)
A magnificent marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan between 1632 and 1649 in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, renowned for its stunning architecture and beauty.
famous mausoleum in Agra, India

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit the Taj Mahal every year to admire its intricate architecture.
The Taj Mahal was built as a symbol of eternal love by Shah Jahan.
At sunset, the marble of the Taj Mahal glows with a warm, golden hue.

Etymology

From Persian and Urdu, meaning 'Crown Palace'.
