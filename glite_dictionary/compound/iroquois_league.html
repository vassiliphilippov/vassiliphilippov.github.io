Iroquois League

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪrəkˌwɔɪ liːg / (AmE), / ˈɪrəkˌwɔɪ liːg / (BrE)
a confederation of Native American tribes originally comprising the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca; the Tuscarora joined in 1722, forming the Six Nations
confederation of Iroquois tribes in North America

Examples

The Iroquois League played a crucial role in shaping the political landscape of northeastern North America.
Historians often discuss the structure and governance of the Iroquois League as a model of federalism.
After 1722, the Tuscarora joined the Iroquois League, resulting in it being called the Six Nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Six Nations - another name for the Iroquois League after the Tuscarora joined

Etymology

From the name 'Iroquois,' likely from French, and 'league,' from Old French 'ligue.'
