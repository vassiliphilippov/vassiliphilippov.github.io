field-emission microscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ɪˈmɪʃən ˈmaɪkrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ɪˈmɪʃən ˈmaɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
An electron microscope that uses field emission to observe the surface structure of a solid at high resolution.
electron microscope for surface analysis

Examples

A field-emission microscope can reveal atomic arrangements on a metal surface.
Researchers used a field-emission microscope to study the crystal structure of the sample.
The field-emission microscope provides far greater resolution than traditional optical microscopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electron microscope - microscope that uses electrons instead of light to form an image

Etymology

From 'field emission', referring to the emission of electrons induced by an external electric field, and 'microscope'.
