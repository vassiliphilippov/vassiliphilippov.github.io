devil's flax

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz flæks / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz flæks / (BrE)
a European perennial plant (Linaria vulgaris) with showy yellow and orange flowers, often considered a weed in North America
a European perennial plant with yellow and orange flowers

Examples

The field was dotted with bright clusters of devil's flax swaying in the breeze.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to remove devil's flax because it spreads quickly.
In summer, devil's flax brings a splash of color to roadsides and meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butter-and-eggs - another common name for Linaria vulgaris, the same plant species
