on one hand

1. ADV

/ ɑn wʌn hænd / (AmE), / ɒn wʌn hænd / (BrE)
used to introduce one point of view, aspect, or fact that is to be compared with another
introducing one aspect or point of view

Examples

On one hand, the project could bring economic benefits; on the other, it may harm the environment.
On one hand, he enjoys city life; on the other, he misses the peace of the countryside.
On one hand, you could save money by staying home, but you’d miss out on the experience.

Synset Examples

on the one hand, she is a gifted chemist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on the other hand - used to introduce a contrasting point or aspect
