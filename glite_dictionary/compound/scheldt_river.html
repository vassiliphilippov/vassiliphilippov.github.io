Scheldt River

1. PROPN

/ ʃɛlt ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ʃɛlt ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river that originates in northern France, flows northeast through Belgium, and empties into the North Sea
river in France and Belgium

Examples

The Scheldt River has played a vital role in the history of European trade.
Many cities along the Scheldt River rely on its waters for transportation and industry.
In spring, the banks of the Scheldt River are dotted with wildflowers.

Etymology

From French 'Escaut' and Dutch 'Schelde'.
