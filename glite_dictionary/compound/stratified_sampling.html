stratified sampling

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræt̬.əˌfaɪd ˈsæm.plɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪd ˈsæm.plɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of sampling in which a population is divided into subgroups (strata) and random samples are taken from each stratum
sampling method using subgroups

Examples

Stratified sampling ensures that all major groups within a population are proportionately represented in the study.
The researcher used stratified sampling to select participants from different age groups.
In stratified sampling, each stratum is randomly sampled rather than the population as a whole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: random sampling - method of selecting a sample in which every member has an equal chance

Etymology

From 'stratify', based on Latin 'stratum' (covering or layer), and 'sampling'.
