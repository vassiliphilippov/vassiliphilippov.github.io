free association

1. NOUN

/ fri əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / fri əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a mental process in which one thought spontaneously leads to another, commonly used as a technique in psychoanalysis to reveal unconscious thoughts and feelings
spontaneous linking of ideas or images

Examples

In therapy, patients were encouraged to use free association to explore their unconscious minds.
The poet relied on free association to generate ideas for his latest collection.
During the workshop, free association exercises helped participants unlock their creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stream of consciousness - a literary style representing the continuous flow of thoughts in the mind
