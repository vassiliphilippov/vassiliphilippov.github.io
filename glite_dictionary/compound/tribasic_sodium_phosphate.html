tribasic sodium phosphate

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈbeɪsɪk ˌsoʊdiəm ˈfɑsfeɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈbeɪsɪk ˌsəʊdiəm ˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound, the most basic sodium salt of phosphoric acid (Na3PO4), used as a builder in soaps, detergents, and for water treatment
a sodium salt used in soaps and detergents

Examples

Tribasic sodium phosphate is often added to detergents to soften water and enhance cleaning power.
The laboratory ordered a new supply of tribasic sodium phosphate for its experiments.
Due to environmental concerns, the use of tribasic sodium phosphate in household soaps has been reduced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trisodium phosphate - another name for tribasic sodium phosphate

Etymology

From 'tri-' meaning three, 'basic' referring to the number of sodium ions, and 'phosphate' from French 'phosphate' and Greek 'phosphoros', light-bringing.
