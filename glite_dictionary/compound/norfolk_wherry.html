Norfolk wherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔr.fək ˈ(h)wɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.fək ˈ(h)wɛr.i / (BrE)
a type of large, single-masted sailing barge traditionally used for transporting cargo on rivers and broads in the East Anglia region of England
type of sailing barge from East Anglia, England

Examples

The Norfolk wherry was once a common sight carrying goods across the Broads.
Restorers have carefully rebuilt a 19th-century Norfolk wherry for educational tours.
Sailing enthusiasts gathered to celebrate the annual Norfolk wherry regatta.

Etymology

Named after Norfolk, the English county where it originated, and 'wherry', an old term for a type of boat.
