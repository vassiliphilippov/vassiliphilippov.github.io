mouse ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊs ɪr / (AmE), / ˈmaʊs ɪə / (BrE)
any of several low-growing plants with small leaves resembling a mouse's ear, especially those related to chickweed.
a plant with small, mouse-like leaves

Examples

The garden was carpeted with mouse ear among the grasses and wildflowers.
Mouse ear is easy to identify by its tiny, velvety leaves.
Some people plant mouse ear as a low-growing ground cover.

2. NOUN

/ maʊs ɪr / (AmE), / maʊs ɪə / (BrE)
A small perennial herb with bright blue or white flowers, often referring to plants of the genus Myosotis or similar species.
small perennial herb with blue or white flowers

Examples

Clusters of mouse ear flowers added a delicate blue to the garden border.
The botanist pointed out a rare species of mouse ear growing by the stream.
She chose to plant mouse ear because it stays low and blooms reliably every spring.
