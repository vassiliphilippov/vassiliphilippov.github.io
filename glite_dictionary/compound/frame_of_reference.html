frame of reference

1. NOUN

/ freɪm əv ˈrɛfɚəns / (AmE), / freɪm əv ˈrɛfərəns / (BrE)
a system of coordinates used to specify the position of objects and measure physical phenomena, often relative to an observer or a context
coordinate system for measuring position

Examples

The position of the object can only be determined relative to a frame of reference.
In classical mechanics, a frame of reference is essential for describing motion.
Different observers may choose different frames of reference to analyze the same event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate system - a system for specifying positions with coordinates

2. NOUN

/ freɪm əv ˈrɛf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / freɪm əv ˈref.ərəns / (BrE)
a set of ideas, assumptions, or criteria by which things are judged or understood, providing context for interpreting information or behavior
system of assumptions or context for interpretation

Examples

Cultural background often shapes a person's frame of reference when interpreting new experiences.
In physics, understanding motion requires specifying the frame of reference from which measurements are made.
Her frame of reference made it difficult to appreciate perspectives different from her own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspective - a particular attitude or point of view toward something
  • Synonym: point of view - a particular way of thinking about or considering something
