Grim Reaper

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪm ˈriːpər / (AmE), / ɡrɪm ˈriːpə / (BrE)
A figure representing death, typically depicted as an old man or skeleton carrying a scythe.
personification of death

Examples

Stories often depict the Grim Reaper coming for those whose time has come.
She dressed as the Grim Reaper for the Halloween party, complete with a scythe.
The legend of the Grim Reaper appears in many cultures around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death - the end of the life of a person or organism

Etymology

From 'grim' (meaning forbidding or uninviting) and 'reaper' (one who harvests crops); the phrase evokes death as a harvester of souls.
