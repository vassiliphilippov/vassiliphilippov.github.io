family Pieridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli paɪˈɛrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pɪəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of butterflies that includes cabbage butterflies and sulfur butterflies, characterized by white, yellow, or orange wings often with black spots
a family of butterflies (cabbage and sulfur butterflies)

Examples

The family Pieridae includes the well-known cabbage butterfly found in gardens worldwide.
Many members of the family Pieridae display striking yellow or white coloration on their wings.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of butterflies within the family Pieridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage butterfly - a common butterfly in the Pieridae family
  • Synonym: sulphur butterfly - another common butterfly in the Pieridae family

Etymology

From New Latin, Pieridae, from Pieris (type genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
