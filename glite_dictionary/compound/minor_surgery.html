minor surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ ˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
a surgical procedure that is simple, typically does not require general anesthesia or respiratory assistance, and is usually performed on an outpatient basis
simple surgical procedure
medical

Examples

After a cycling accident, she only needed minor surgery to close the wound.
The clinic specializes in outpatient procedures and minor surgery.
Because the risk was low, he returned to work soon after his minor surgery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major surgery - surgery involving significant risk, often with anesthesia or complex procedures
