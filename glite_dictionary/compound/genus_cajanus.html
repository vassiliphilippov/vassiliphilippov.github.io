genus Cajanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈdʒeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈdʒɑːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of erect, densely branched, shrubby perennial plants native to the Old World tropics, including species like the pigeon pea.
genus of tropical shrubby plants

Examples

The genus Cajanus includes several species valued for their edible seeds.
Agricultural researchers often study the drought resistance of plants in the genus Cajanus.
One of the most important legumes in the tropics belongs to the genus Cajanus.

Etymology

Genus name from Latinized form of Malayalam 'kachang'; scientific binomial nomenclature.
