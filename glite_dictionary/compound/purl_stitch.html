purl stitch

1. NOUN

/ pɜrl stɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɜːl stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a basic knitting stitch in which the needle passes through the front of the stitch from right to left, creating a textured appearance
knitting stitch made by working from the front

Examples

She learned how to make a purl stitch as a beginner in her knitting class.
Alternating rows of purl stitch and knit stitch creates a ribbed texture.
The scarf pattern calls for every second row to be done in purl stitch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knit stitch - the basic knitting stitch made by passing the needle through the back of the stitch

Etymology

From Middle English 'pirlen' (to twist or ripple), related to the twisting action of the stitch.
