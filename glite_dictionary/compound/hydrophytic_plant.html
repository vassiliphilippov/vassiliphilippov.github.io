hydrophytic plant

1. NOUN

/ haɪdrəˈfaɪtɪk plænt / (AmE), / haɪdrəˈfɪtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that grows partly or entirely in water, either rooted in soil or floating freely
a plant that thrives in water or very wet environments

Examples

The pond was filled with hydrophytic plants such as water lilies and reeds.
Some hydrophytic plants are able to survive even in swiftly flowing streams.
When designing a water garden, it is important to choose hydrophytic plants that are suited to your climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aquatic plant - a plant adapted to living in aquatic environments

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro' (water) + 'phyton' (plant).
