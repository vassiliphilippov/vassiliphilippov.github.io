Platonic year

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtɑnɪk jɪr / (AmE), / pləˈtɒnɪk jɪə / (BrE)
the period of about 25,800 years required for one complete cycle of the precession of the equinoxes
cycle of precession of the equinoxes (25,800 years)

Examples

Some ancient astronomers believed that the Platonic year governed the rise and fall of civilizations.
The duration of a Platonic year is about 25,800 years, corresponding to the precession of the equinoxes.
Astronomers study the Platonic year to understand long-term changes in Earth's orientation.

Etymology

From the association of ancient Greek philosopher Plato with the concept of cosmic cycles, combined with 'year'.
