ad hoc

1. ADJ

/ æd ˈhɑk / (AmE), / ad ˈhɒk / (BrE)
formed, arranged, or done for a particular purpose as necessary; improvised or impromptu.
for a particular purpose; improvised

Examples

The board created an ad hoc committee to investigate the sudden issue.
We held an ad hoc meeting to resolve the problem quickly.
Her team developed an ad hoc solution when the system failed.

Synset Examples

an ad hoc committee meeting

Etymology

From Latin 'ad hoc' meaning 'for this'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01802880-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ æd hɑk / (AmE), / ad hɒk / (BrE)
Created or done for a particular purpose as necessary, rather than planned in advance.
for a specific purpose only

Examples

The committee formed an ad hoc group to address the budget shortfall.
We need an ad hoc solution for this unexpected problem.
She made an ad hoc decision without consulting anyone else.

Synset Examples

a coordinated policy instead of ad hoc decisions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvised - done without preparation, created on the spot

Etymology

From Latin ad hoc, meaning 'for this'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01107222-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADV

/ æd ˈhɑk / (AmE), / ad ˈhɒk / (BrE)
created or arranged for a particular purpose as necessary; for a specific case or situation without wider application
for a particular purpose or case

Examples

The company formed an ad hoc committee to address the urgent problem.
Decisions were made ad hoc rather than following established procedures.
They created an ad hoc solution that worked for that unique circumstance.

Synset Examples

they were appointed ad hoc

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent

Etymology

From Latin ad hoc, meaning 'to this'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00252456-r
Synset POS: r
