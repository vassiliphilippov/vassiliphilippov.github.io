slave labour

1. NOUN

/ sleɪv ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / sleɪv ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
work that people are forced to do without pay, often under threat or coercion
work done by forced, unpaid workers

Examples

The factory was shut down after allegations of slave labour were investigated.
International laws prohibit the use of slave labour in all forms of production.
They campaigned for the abolition of slave labour worldwide.

Synset Examples

They protested against products made with slave labour.
Many organizations fight against the use of slave labour globally.
The factory was accused of using slave labour to cut costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forced labor - work that people are compelled to do against their will
