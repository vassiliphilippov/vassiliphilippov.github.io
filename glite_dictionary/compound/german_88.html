German 88

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmən ˌeɪti ˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˌeɪti ˈeɪt / (BrE)
a powerful German anti-aircraft and anti-tank artillery gun used during World War II, known for its effectiveness and impact
German WWII anti-aircraft/artillery gun

Examples

The German 88 was so effective that Allied tanks often avoided its line of fire.
Military historians still study the influence of the German 88 on tank warfare.
He marveled at the engineering precision of the famous German 88.

Synset Examples

The German 88 was feared for its devastating impact.
The German 88 played a crucial role in the war.
He admired the precision of the German 88 artillery.

Etymology

Refers to the 88-millimeter caliber anti-aircraft and anti-tank gun developed by Germany in the 1930s.
