time of it

1. NOUN

/ taɪm əv ɪt / (AmE), / taɪm əv ɪt / (BrE)
a period marked by difficulty or challenge; a hard or trying experience
difficult or trying experience

Examples

He really had a time of it when his computer crashed during the exam.
They're having a time of it adjusting to their new city.
She'll have a time of it juggling work and family if she doesn't get some help.

Synset Examples

He had a time of it getting through all the paperwork.
You’ll have a time of it managing that crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rough time - a period of hardship or difficulty
  • Antonym: easy time - a period of comfort or little difficulty
