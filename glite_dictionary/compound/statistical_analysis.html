statistical analysis

1. NOUN

/ stəˈtɪstɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / stəˈtɪstɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the process of collecting, examining, and interpreting numerical data to discover patterns, trends, or relationships
the examination and interpretation of data

Examples

The research team used statistical analysis to interpret the results of the experiment.
Modern business decisions often rely heavily on statistical analysis to guide their strategies.
Accurate statistical analysis is essential for understanding complex scientific phenomena.

Synset Examples

The epsilon value is crucial for statistical analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: data analysis - the process of systematically applying statistical or logical techniques to describe and evaluate data
  • Degree Higher: statistical inference - the process of drawing conclusions from data subject to random variation

Etymology

From 'statistical', relating to statistics (from New Latin statisticus, German Statistik), and 'analysis', from Greek analysis (a breaking up).
