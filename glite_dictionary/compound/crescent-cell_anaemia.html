crescent-cell anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛsənt sɛl əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈkrɛsənt sɛl əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A hereditary form of anaemia, primarily affecting people of African descent, in which red blood cells become crescent-shaped and break down prematurely.
hereditary anaemia with crescent-shaped red blood cells
medical british

Examples

She was diagnosed with crescent-cell anaemia after experiencing frequent episodes of pain and fatigue.
Crescent-cell anaemia is inherited and often detected in early childhood.
Doctors monitor patients with crescent-cell anaemia for complications such as infections and organ damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle-cell anaemia - hereditary anaemia with sickle-shaped red blood cells; more common term for the same disease

Etymology

From 'crescent', referring to the moon-shaped red blood cells seen in the disease, and 'anaemia', from Greek 'anaimia' (lack of blood).
