Baltic language

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔltɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːltɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a group of Indo-European languages including Lithuanian and Latvian, noted for preserving many archaic features of Proto-Indo-European
a group of Indo-European languages (e.g., Lithuanian, Latvian)

Examples

Lithuanian is considered a particularly archaic Baltic language within the Indo-European family.
Both Latvian and Lithuanian are examples of living Baltic languages.
Scholars study the Baltic languages to reconstruct features of Proto-Indo-European.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic language - a branch of Indo-European languages closely related to Baltic languages
