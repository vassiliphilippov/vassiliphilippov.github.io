corn borer moth

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈbɔr ər mɑθ / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈbɔː rə mɒθ / (BrE)
A moth whose larvae bore into the stems and crowns of corn and other plants, often causing agricultural damage; native to Europe but found in America.
moth whose larvae damage corn plants

Examples

The infestation of corn borer moth larvae caused significant losses in the farmer's crops this season.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of the corn borer moth to develop better pest control methods.
Farmers monitor their fields closely in spring, looking for signs of corn borer moth activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European corn borer - a specific species of corn borer moth (Ostrinia nubilalis)
