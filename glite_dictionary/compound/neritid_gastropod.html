neritid gastropod

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛrɪtɪd ˈɡæstroʊˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈnɛrɪtɪd ˈɡæstrəʊˌpɒd / (BrE)
A marine snail of the family Neritidae, distinguished by having an operculum and a short spiral shell, commonly found in coastal waters.
marine snail with a short spiral shell

Examples

The rocky shoreline was covered with neritid gastropods, their shells glistening in the morning sun.
Marine biologists collected several species of neritid gastropod for their study on coastal biodiversity.
Unlike many other snails, the neritid gastropod closes its shell with a tough, plate-like operculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerite - a type of marine snail in the family Neritidae
  • Degree Higher: gastropod - a large class of mollusks including snails and slugs

Etymology

From New Latin Neritidae (the family name), from Nerita (type genus), from Greek Nerites (a sea deity) + gastropod (from Greek gastēr 'stomach' + pous 'foot').
