covert operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊvərt ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkəʊvɜːt ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a secret action, especially by a government agency, planned so that its sponsorship can be plausibly denied if uncovered
secret government action that can be denied

Examples

The agency launched a covert operation to gather intelligence in the foreign country.
He was recruited to take part in a covert operation during the early years of the conflict.
Many covert operations are designed to ensure plausible deniability for the sponsoring government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clandestine operation - a secret operation intended to conceal its existence or execution
  • Antonym: overt operation - an open and publicly known intelligence or military action

Etymology

From 'covert' (Middle English, from Old French) meaning "hidden" and 'operation.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊvərt ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkəʊvət ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a secret or undercover action, especially by military or intelligence agencies, to achieve a specific goal without being detected
secret military or intelligence action

Examples

The agency planned a covert operation to infiltrate the enemy's network.
She was involved in several covert operations during her military service.
A covert operation was launched to rescue the hostages without alerting the kidnappers.

Synset Examples

They conducted a covert operation to gather intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clandestine operation - an operation carried out in secret, especially for military or intelligence purposes

Etymology

From 'covert' meaning hidden or secret, and 'operation' meaning an organized activity.
