sociological theory

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊsiəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
an explanation or framework that seeks to account for patterns and relationships in human society and social behavior
explanatory framework in sociology

Examples

The conflict perspective is a major sociological theory that examines power struggles in society.
She wrote her dissertation on a new sociological theory concerning urban communities.
Many sociological theories attempt to explain changes in family structure over time.

Synset Examples

An example of a sociological theory is the work of Robert Putnam on the decline of civic engagement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social theory - broader theories about society, not limited to academic sociology

Etymology

From 'sociological' (relating to society) + 'theory' (system of ideas), both ultimately from Latin roots.
