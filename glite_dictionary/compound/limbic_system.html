limbic system

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmbɪk ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbɪk ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a complex system of interconnected neural structures in the brain involved in emotional responses, behavior, and memory
group of brain structures for emotion and memory
medical

Examples

The limbic system plays a vital role in regulating emotions such as fear and pleasure.
Damage to the limbic system can lead to changes in mood and memory function.
Researchers are studying how the limbic system responds to stress and anxiety.

Etymology

From Latin 'limbus' (border, edge), referring to its location at the border of the cortex.
