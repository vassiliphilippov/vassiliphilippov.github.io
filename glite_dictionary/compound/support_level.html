support level

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɔrt ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / səˈpɔːt ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
In the stock market, the price point at which a security is expected to attract enough demand to prevent its price from falling further.
lowest price attracting investors (finance)

Examples

The stock rebounded after hitting its support level at $50 per share.
Traders watch for a support level to identify possible buying opportunities.
Once the support level was breached, the price quickly dropped further.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resistance level - the price at which a security tends to stop rising and may fall

2. NOUN

/ səˈpɔrt ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / səˈpɔːt ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
In finance, a price point at which a falling stock or market is expected to stop declining due to increased demand.
price floor where stock stops falling

Examples

The company's shares rebounded after hitting a support level at $50.
Analysts often look for the support level to predict possible price movements.
Breaking below a key support level can signal further declines in the market.

Synset Examples

The stock price cut below its support level.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resistance level - price point where a rising stock tends to stop climbing
