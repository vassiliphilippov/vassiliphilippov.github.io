moth bean

1. NOUN

/ mɔθ bin / (AmE), / mɒθ biːn / (BrE)
an annual legume (Vigna aconitifolia) native to India, with hairy foliage and small yellow flowers, grown for its edible seeds, forage, and as a soil conditioner
Indian legume grown for food and forage

Examples

The moth bean thrives in hot, dry regions and is valued for its drought resistance.
Farmers plant moth beans to improve soil fertility and provide nutritious fodder for livestock.
The small yellow flowers of the moth bean develop into cylindrical pods containing edible seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - a different but related edible legume species, Vigna radiata
