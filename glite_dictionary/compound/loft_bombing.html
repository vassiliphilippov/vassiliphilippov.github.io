loft bombing

1. NOUN

/ lɑft ˈbɑmɪŋ / (AmE), / lɒft ˈbɒmɪŋ / (BrE)
A bombing technique in which an aircraft approaches a target at low altitude, then sharply pulls up before releasing its bombs, allowing the plane to escape the blast area.
military bombing run involving pull-up before bomb release

Examples

The squadron practiced loft bombing techniques to minimize exposure to enemy air defenses.
During the exercise, they simulated loft bombing runs over the training range.
Modern jets often use loft bombing to take advantage of their speed and agility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dive bombing - a bombing technique where the aircraft dives toward the target
