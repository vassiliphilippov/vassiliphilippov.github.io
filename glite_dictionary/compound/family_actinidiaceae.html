family Actinidiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæktɪˌnɪdiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌæktɪnɪdɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical trees, shrubs, or woody vines, including the kiwifruit and related plants.
a plant family including kiwifruit and woody vines

Examples

The family Actinidiaceae includes economically important fruiting plants such as the kiwifruit.
Botanists have classified several tropical vines within the family Actinidiaceae.
Members of the family Actinidiaceae are commonly found in Southeast Asia and other subtropical regions.

Etymology

From Actinidia (the type genus) + Latin -aceae, a standard suffix for plant family names.
