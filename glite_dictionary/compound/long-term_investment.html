long-term investment

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ tɝm ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / lɒŋ tɜːm ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
An investment that is held for a period longer than one year, often to achieve significant growth or returns over time.
investment held for more than a year

Examples

Buying real estate as a long-term investment is a common strategy for building wealth.
She chose a retirement fund as her primary long-term investment.
Many experts recommend stocks for those seeking a long-term investment option.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-term investment - An investment held for less than one year
