eye-catching

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪ ˌkætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ ˌkatʃɪŋ / (BrE)
immediately attracting attention, especially because of being visually striking or unusual
attracting attention; visually striking

Examples

The designer created an eye-catching logo to help the brand stand out.
She wore an eye-catching red dress to the party, drawing everyone's attention.
The museum's new exhibit features several eye-catching sculptures.

Synset Examples

eye-catching posters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striking - impressively noticeable or unusual; attracting attention
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or striking

2. ADJ

/ ˈaɪˌkætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌkatʃɪŋ / (BrE)
immediately attracting attention or standing out because of being very attractive or unusual
attracting attention; visually striking

Examples

The bold colors of the new packaging made it truly eye-catching on the shelves.
She wore an eye-catching necklace that drew everyone's attention at the party.
The architecture of the building is modern and eye-catching, attracting many tourists.

Synset Examples

The advertisement was designed to be eye-catching and memorable.
The artist's work is always bright and eye-catching.
Her dress was eye-catching, making heads turn.
He dressed eye-catchingly for the gala event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striking - noticeable or impressive in appearance
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or attractive

Etymology

Formed from 'eye' + 'catching' (catch); in use since early 20th century.
