cat cracker

1. NOUN

/ kæt ˈkrækər / (AmE), / kat ˈkrækə / (BrE)
a chemical reactor used in the petroleum industry to convert oils with high boiling points into fuels with lower boiling points using a catalyst
chemical reactor for refining oil

Examples

The refinery recently upgraded its cat cracker to increase gasoline production.
A malfunction in the cat cracker caused a temporary shutdown at the plant.
Oil companies rely on the cat cracker to maximize fuel yields from crude oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalytic cracking - the process of breaking down larger hydrocarbons using a catalyst

Etymology

Shortened form of 'catalytic cracker,' referring to the role of a catalyst in the process.
