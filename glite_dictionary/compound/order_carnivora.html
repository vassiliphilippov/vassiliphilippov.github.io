order Carnivora

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər kɑrˈnɪvərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kɑːˈnɪvərə / (BrE)
A large order of mammals that includes carnivorous and omnivorous animals such as cats, dogs, bears, weasels, raccoons, and their relatives.
order of carnivorous mammals (cats, dogs, bears, etc.)

Examples

The order Carnivora includes animals such as lions, tigers, and bears.
Scientists debated the evolutionary history of the order Carnivora for decades.
Raccoons, though omnivorous, are classified within the order Carnivora.

Etymology

From Latin 'carnivora', meaning 'flesh-eaters', from 'caro' (flesh) + 'vorare' (to devour).
