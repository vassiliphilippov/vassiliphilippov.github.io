riding horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈraɪdɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
a horse that is bred or kept primarily for riding rather than for work or racing
horse kept for riding

Examples

She bought a new riding horse to train for show jumping events.
The stable offers lessons on several well-trained riding horses.
Unlike a workhorse, a riding horse is chosen for its smooth gaits and temperament.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: driving horse - a horse trained or used mainly to pull vehicles
