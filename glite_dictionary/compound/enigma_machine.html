Enigma machine

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪɡmə məˈʃin / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪɡmə məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a cipher device used by Germany during World War II to encode and decode secret messages
cipher device from World War II

Examples

The Enigma machine played a crucial role in the history of cryptography.
Allied codebreakers worked tirelessly to break the codes produced by the Enigma machine.
The Enigma machine is now displayed in museums as a symbol of wartime ingenuity.

Synset Examples

He marveled at the complexity of the Enigma machine.
Historians study the Enigma machine to understand wartime communications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cipher machine - device for encrypting or decrypting messages

Etymology

From German 'Enigma', itself from Latin 'aenigma' (riddle, puzzle).
