genus Zanthoxylum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ˌzænˈθɒksɪləm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌzænˈθɒksɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen trees and shrubs, commonly known as prickly ash, found in tropical and temperate regions.
a genus of trees or shrubs (prickly ash)

Examples

The genus Zanthoxylum includes plants widely used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
Several species in the genus Zanthoxylum are known as Sichuan pepper trees.
The distribution of the genus Zanthoxylum extends across Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly ash - a common name for trees and shrubs in the genus Zanthoxylum

Etymology

From Greek *xanthos* (yellow) and *xylon* (wood), referring to the yellow wood of some species.
