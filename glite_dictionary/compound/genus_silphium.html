genus Silphium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɪlfiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɪlfiəm / (BrE)
a group of tall North American perennial herbs belonging to the genus Silphium, known for their composite flowers and resinous stems
group of tall North American perennial herbs

Examples

The genus Silphium includes several species commonly known as rosinweeds.
Botanists are studying the genus Silphium for its potential ecological benefits.
Many members of the genus Silphium produce large, yellow sunflower-like blooms.

Etymology

From Latin *Silphium*, the name of a plant mentioned in classical texts; used as a scientific genus name.
