genus Anser

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈænsər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈænsə / (BrE)
The genus of typical geese in the Anatidae family, which includes species such as the greylag goose and the pink-footed goose.
genus of typical geese

Examples

Many migratory species in the genus Anser travel thousands of kilometers each year.
The greylag goose is a well-known member of the genus Anser.
Scientists studied the evolutionary relationships within the genus Anser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose - large waterbird of the family Anatidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Anser' meaning 'goose'.
