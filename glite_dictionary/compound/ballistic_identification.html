ballistic identification

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstɪk aɪˌdɛntəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪk aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of determining which gun fired a bullet by analyzing the unique marks left on the bullet and cartridge case
forensic method of matching bullets to guns

Examples

Forensic experts used ballistic identification to link the bullet to the suspect's handgun.
The report included detailed ballistic identification evidence that proved crucial in the trial.
Advances in ballistic identification have made it easier to solve gun-related crimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballistics - the science of projectiles and firearms

Etymology

From 'ballistic', relating to projectiles, and 'identification'.
