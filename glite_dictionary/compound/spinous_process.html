spinous process

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪ.nəs ˈprɑː.ses / (AmE), / ˈspaɪ.nəs ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a bony projection off the back of each vertebra, to which muscles and ligaments are attached
bony projection from a vertebra
medical

Examples

The doctor explained how the spinous process can be felt through the skin along the spine.
Enlargement of the spinous process may indicate certain spinal conditions.
Some animals have an elongated spinous process that supports a dorsal sail.

Synset Examples

Spinous processes are exaggerated in some animals, such as the extinct Dimetrodon and Spinosaurus, where they form a sailback or finback.

Etymology

From Latin 'spina' (thorn, spine) and 'processus' (going forth, projection).
