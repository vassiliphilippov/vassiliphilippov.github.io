perfectly elastic supply

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrfɪktli ɪˈlæstɪk səˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪktli ɪˈlæstɪk səˈplaɪ / (BrE)
a market condition in which any decrease in the price results in the quantity supplied dropping to zero
market condition: supply falls to zero if price drops

Examples

In a market with perfectly elastic supply, producers will not sell at any price below the market rate.
The textbook illustrates perfectly elastic supply with a horizontal supply curve.
If input costs suddenly increase, perfectly elastic supply becomes impossible to maintain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfectly inelastic supply - supply that does not change regardless of price

Etymology

From economic terminology: 'elastic' (from Greek 'elastos', meaning ductile or flexible) and standard English.
