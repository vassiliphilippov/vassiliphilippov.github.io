alpha-adrenergic receptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfə əˌdrɪˈnɜrdʒɪk rɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌælfə æd.rəˌnɜː.dʒɪk rɪˈsɛp.tər / (BrE)
a type of receptor found on nerve cell membranes of the sympathetic nervous system that responds to certain agents by causing effects such as vasoconstriction or contraction of smooth muscles
type of sympathetic nervous system receptor
medical

Examples

The drug selectively binds to the alpha-adrenergic receptor, causing blood vessels to constrict.
Blocking the alpha-adrenergic receptor can help reduce high blood pressure in certain patients.
When stimulated, alpha-adrenergic receptors trigger the contraction of smooth muscles in various tissues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beta-adrenergic receptor - a receptor responding to adrenaline with different effects such as increased heart rate

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha' (the first letter of the Greek alphabet) and 'adrenergic', referring to the effect of adrenaline; 'receptor' from Latin 'receptus' (received).
