coral fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.əl ˈfʌŋ.ɡəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əl ˈfʌŋ.ɡəs / (BrE)
A fungus with branching, coral-like structures, often brightly colored, belonging to the family Clavariaceae.
fungus with coral-like branching

Examples

The forest floor was covered in coral fungus, their shapes twisting like underwater reefs.
Naturalists marveled at the brightly colored coral fungus nestled among the moss.
She photographed a rare species of coral fungus during her hike through the woods.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mushroom - a fleshy, spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus, typically with a stem and cap

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to marine coral; 'fungus' from Latin 'fungus' (mushroom).
