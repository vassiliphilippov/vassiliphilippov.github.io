general practitioner

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnərəl prækˈtɪʃənər / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəl prækˈtɪʃənə / (BrE)
a medical doctor who treats a wide range of illnesses rather than specializing in one field
primary care physician, non-specialist doctor
medical british

Examples

After moving to a new city, I registered with a new general practitioner.
A general practitioner can refer you to a specialist if necessary.
Many patients prefer to see their general practitioner for routine checkups and minor illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family doctor - a doctor who provides general medical care to people of all ages
  • Antonym: specialist - a medical doctor who focuses on a particular area of medicine
