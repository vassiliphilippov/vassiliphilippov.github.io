premature labour

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriməˈtʃʊr ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈtjʊə ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
the onset of labor before the 37th week of pregnancy
labor starting before 37 weeks gestation
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained that premature labour could pose risks to both mother and baby.
Women with certain health conditions may be at higher risk for premature labour.
Modern medicine has improved the survival rates of babies born after premature labour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preterm labor - labor that begins before 37 weeks of pregnancy (U.S. spelling)
