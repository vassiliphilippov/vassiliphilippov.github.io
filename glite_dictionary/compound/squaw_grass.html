squaw grass

1. NOUN

/ skwɑː ɡræs / (AmE), / skwɔː ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a plant native to western North America with woody rhizomes, stiff grasslike leaves, and spikes of creamy white flowers
North American plant with woody rhizomes and white flowers

Examples

The hiker noticed a patch of squaw grass growing near the trail's edge.
Botanists are studying how squaw grass adapts to dry mountain environments.
Clumps of squaw grass were scattered across the rocky hillside.
