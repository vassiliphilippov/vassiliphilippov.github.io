baking soda

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkɪŋ ˌsoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkɪŋ ˌsəʊdə / (BrE)
a white powder consisting of sodium bicarbonate, commonly used in baking, as a cleaning agent, and as an antacid
white powder used in baking and cleaning

Examples

Add one teaspoon of baking soda to the cake batter for a lighter texture.
She cleaned the stained sink by scrubbing it with baking soda and water.
Many people use baking soda as a natural remedy for heartburn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium bicarbonate - the chemical compound with the formula NaHCO3

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkɪŋ ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkɪŋ ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
a white powder (sodium bicarbonate) used as a leavening agent in baking and for cleaning or deodorizing
powder used in baking and cleaning

Examples

If your recipe calls for baking soda, be sure not to confuse it with baking powder.
Many people keep a box of baking soda in the fridge to absorb odors.
You can use baking soda to clean tough stains from kitchen surfaces.

Synset Examples

Using baking soda will help leaven the cake mix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicarbonate of soda - another term for baking soda (especially in UK)

Etymology

Descriptive compound: 'baking' referring to its culinary use, 'soda' from its chemical nature (sodium bicarbonate).
