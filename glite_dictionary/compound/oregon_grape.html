Oregon grape

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪɡən ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ɡreɪp / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to western North America, with spiny pinnate leaves, yellow flowers, and blue-black berries, often grown ornamentally
evergreen shrub with yellow flowers and blue-black berries

Examples

The Oregon grape is popular in gardens for its attractive foliage and bright yellow flowers.
Birds are often attracted to the Oregon grape because of its berries.
She planted an Oregon grape along the walkway to add year-round color to her yard.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ɪ.ɡən ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈɒr.ɪ.ɡən ɡreɪp / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub native to western North America, with holly-like grey-green leaves, yellow flowers, and bluish-black berries
small North American shrub with blue berries

Examples

The Oregon grape blooms early in spring with bright yellow flowers.
Many gardeners use Oregon grape as a hardy ornamental plant in shady areas.
The berries of the Oregon grape are often used to make jellies and jams.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Oregon, where the plant is common, and for its grape-like berries.
