bastion against

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstʃən əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / ˈbæstɪən əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
a place, institution, or thing that acts as a strong defense or safeguard against something unwanted or threatening
strong defense or safeguard against something

Examples

The community center became a bastion against loneliness during the harsh winter months.
Free speech is often seen as a bastion against oppression in democratic societies.
Their savings acted as a bastion against unexpected financial hardships.

Synset Examples

She considered her home a bastion against the outside world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulwark - a defensive wall or means of protection
  • Synonym: fortress - a military stronghold, especially a strongly fortified town

Etymology

From 'bastion' (from French, from Italian 'bastione', related to 'bastire' meaning 'to build') combined with 'against' (from Old English 'ongēan').
