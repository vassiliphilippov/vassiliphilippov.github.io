cosmic ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑz.mɪk reɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.mɪk reɪ / (BrE)
A highly penetrating ionizing radiation from outer space, composed mainly of protons and atomic nuclei, which enters the Earth's atmosphere and produces secondary particles.
high-energy radiation from space

Examples

Scientists study cosmic rays to learn more about the origins of the universe.
Exposure to cosmic rays is a concern for astronauts on long space missions.
A high-altitude balloon detected an increase in cosmic ray activity above the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiation - energy transmitted as rays, waves, or particles

Etymology

From 'cosmic' (relating to the universe) and 'ray' (a beam of energy or particles).
