speech perception

1. NOUN

/ spiːtʃ pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / spiːtʃ pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which spoken language is heard, interpreted, and understood by the listener
the understanding of spoken language

Examples

Research in speech perception helps us understand how people recognize words in noisy environments.
Infants begin to develop speech perception abilities within the first few months of life.
Damage to certain areas of the brain can severely affect speech perception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditory perception - the ability to perceive sounds
  • Antonym: speech production - the process of producing spoken language
