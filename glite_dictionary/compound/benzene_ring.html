benzene ring

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnzin rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛnziːn rɪŋ / (BrE)
a ring-shaped structure consisting of six carbon atoms, each bonded to a hydrogen atom, forming the core of benzene and related compounds
ring-shaped structure in chemistry

Examples

The benzene ring is a fundamental structure in organic chemistry.
Many dyes owe their color to the presence of a benzene ring in their molecules.
Aspirin contains a benzene ring as part of its chemical structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aromatic ring - a ring structure in organic chemistry with delocalized electrons, including benzene

Etymology

From 'benzene', a chemical compound, and 'ring', referring to its cyclic structure.
