Fanconi's anemia

1. NOUN

/ fænˈkoʊniːz əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / fanˈkəʊniz əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder characterized by pancytopenia and underdevelopment of the bone marrow, often leading to increased risk of cancer and physical abnormalities.
rare inherited disorder with bone marrow failure
medical

Examples

The doctors suspected Fanconi's anemia after observing her abnormal blood counts and physical signs.
Genetic counseling was recommended for the family because their child had Fanconi's anemia.
Research into treatments for Fanconi's anemia has led to important advances in bone marrow transplantation.

Etymology

Named after Swiss pediatrician Guido Fanconi (1892–1979), who first described the disease.
