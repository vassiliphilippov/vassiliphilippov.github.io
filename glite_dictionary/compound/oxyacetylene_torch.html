oxyacetylene torch

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːksiəˈsɛtəliːn tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɒksiəˈsiːtəliːn tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
a torch used for welding or cutting metals that burns a mixture of oxygen and acetylene gases to produce a very hot flame
torch used for welding with oxyacetylene

Examples

The welder adjusted the oxyacetylene torch before starting on the metal frame.
Safety goggles are essential when working with an oxyacetylene torch.
Using an oxyacetylene torch, she cut through the steel plate with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowtorch - a tool producing a hot flame for welding or heating

Etymology

From 'oxy-' (relating to oxygen) and 'acetylene', indicating the gases used in the torch.
