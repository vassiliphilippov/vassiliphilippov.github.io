sex hormone

1. NOUN

/ sɛks ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / sɛks ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone that influences the development and growth of sex organs and secondary sexual characteristics
hormone affecting sexual development
medical

Examples

Testosterone is a well-known sex hormone responsible for male characteristics.
Doctors sometimes prescribe sex hormones to treat certain medical conditions.
Changes in sex hormone levels can affect mood and physical development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: androgen - male sex hormone
  • Synonym: estrogen - female sex hormone
