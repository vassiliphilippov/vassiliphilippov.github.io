means grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːnz ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈmiːnz ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall, perennial grass that spreads by creeping rhizomes, used for fodder but considered a serious pest in cultivated land, especially in the southern United States
a tall perennial grass and weed

Examples

Means grass can rapidly overtake agricultural fields if not controlled.
Farmers in the southern United States struggle to manage the spread of means grass.
Some livestock feed includes means grass because of its high nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bermuda grass - a similar grass species often confused with means grass
