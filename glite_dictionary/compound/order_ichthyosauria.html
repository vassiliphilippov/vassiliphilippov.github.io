order Ichthyosauria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌɪkθioʊˈsɔriə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌɪkθiəˈsɔːriə / (BrE)
a group of extinct marine reptiles known as ichthyosaurs, which lived during the Mesozoic era
group of extinct marine reptiles (ichthyosaurs)

Examples

Scientists have found fossils belonging to the order Ichthyosauria in many parts of the world.
The order Ichthyosauria thrived during the Mesozoic era, especially in the Jurassic period.
Members of the order Ichthyosauria were remarkable for their fish-like bodies and long snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichthyosaur - an extinct marine reptile resembling a dolphin

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'sauros' (lizard), reflecting fish-like lizard characteristics.
