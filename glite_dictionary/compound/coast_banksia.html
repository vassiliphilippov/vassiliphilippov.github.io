coast banksia

1. NOUN

/ koʊst ˈbæŋksiə / (AmE), / kəʊst ˈbaŋksɪə / (BrE)
A shrubby Australian tree (Banksia integrifolia) with silky foliage and cylindrical spikes of yellow nectar-rich flowers.
Australian tree with yellow flower spikes

Examples

The coast banksia is a common feature along Australia's eastern shoreline.
Gardeners prize the coast banksia for its attractive flower spikes and ability to attract birds.
We planted a row of coast banksias to create a natural windbreak.
