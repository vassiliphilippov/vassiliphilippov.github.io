Eames chair

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːmz tʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈiːmz tʃɛə / (BrE)
A stylish chair designed by Charles Eames, traditionally made of molded plywood with a seat and back contoured to fit the human body.
iconic molded plywood chair by Charles Eames

Examples

The museum's collection includes an original Eames chair from the 1940s.
She bought a replica Eames chair to add a modern touch to her living room.
Many designers admire the ergonomic shape of the classic Eames chair.

Etymology

Named after Charles Eames (1907–1978), American designer, who created the chair with his wife Ray Eames.
