radioactive dust

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈæktɪv dʌst / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv dʌst / (BrE)
Fine particles containing radioactive material that settle to the ground after a nuclear explosion or accident.
radioactive particles that fall to earth

Examples

After the accident, radioactive dust settled on large areas surrounding the power plant.
Scientists measured the levels of radioactive dust to assess the environmental impact.
People were advised to stay indoors to avoid contact with radioactive dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallout - radioactive particles deposited from the atmosphere after a nuclear explosion
