complementary medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpləˈmɛntəri ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛntəri ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
A medical practice that combines conventional treatments with alternative or non-traditional therapies to support patient care.
combining conventional and alternative medicine

Examples

Many cancer patients turn to complementary medicine to help manage side effects of conventional treatments.
The clinic offers a range of complementary medicine services, including acupuncture and herbal remedies.
Debate continues over the effectiveness of complementary medicine in supporting patient wellness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternative medicine - medical treatments that are not part of standard medical care
