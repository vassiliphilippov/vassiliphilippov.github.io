skinny tie

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪni taɪ / (AmE), / ˈskɪni taɪ / (BrE)
a necktie that is noticeably narrower than standard ties, typically associated with fashion trends from the late 1950s and 1960s
narrow, stylish necktie

Examples

He wore a black skinny tie with his tailored suit for a retro look.
The band members all dressed in matching jackets and skinny ties.
During the 1960s, the skinny tie became a symbol of modern style.

Synset Examples

Skinny ties were first popularized in the late 1950s and early 1960s by British bands such as the Beatles and the Kinks, alongside the subculture that embraced such bands, the mods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necktie - a piece of cloth worn around the neck and tied at the front
