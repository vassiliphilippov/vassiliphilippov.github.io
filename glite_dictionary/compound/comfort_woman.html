comfort woman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərt ˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət ˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman forced into sexual slavery by the Japanese military during World War II, particularly for servicing soldiers in military brothels
woman forced into sexual slavery by Japanese military (WWII)

Examples

The museum exhibit included personal stories from several comfort women who survived World War II.
Historians continue to uncover documents detailing the suffering of comfort women during the occupation.
She became an advocate for human rights after sharing her experiences as a comfort woman.

Synset Examples

she wrote a book about her harsh experiences as a comfort woman

Etymology

Calque of Japanese 慰安婦 (ianfu), meaning 'comfort woman' or 'consolation woman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərt ˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət ˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman forced into sexual slavery by the Japanese military, especially during World War II
woman forced into sexual slavery in wartime

Examples

The story of the comfort woman was finally recognized by the government.
Many survivors of the comfort woman system continue to demand justice.
Historians have documented the suffering endured by each comfort woman during the war.

Synset Examples

She spoke bravely about her experiences as a comfort woman.
The history of comfort women is often overlooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sex slave - a person who is kept as a slave and forced to have sex

Etymology

Literal translation of Japanese 慰安婦 (ianfu), meaning 'comfort woman', used as a euphemism.
