portia tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrʃə tri / (AmE), / ˈpɔːʃɪə triː / (BrE)
A tropical or subtropical tree (Thespesia populnea) found along coasts, cultivated for its ornamental heart-shaped leaves, showy yellow and purple flowers, and valuable reddish wood and seed oil.
ornamental coastal tree; valued for wood and oil

Examples

The portia tree is often planted along shorelines to prevent erosion.
Artisans prize the dense wood of the portia tree for making fine carvings.
The seeds of the portia tree are pressed to produce a valuable oil.
