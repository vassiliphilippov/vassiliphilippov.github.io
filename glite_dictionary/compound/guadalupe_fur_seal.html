Guadalupe fur seal

1. NOUN

/ ɡwɑːdəˌlup fɝ sɪl / (AmE), / ɡwɑːdəˌluːp fə sɪl / (BrE)
a species of fur seal native to the Pacific coast of California and southward to Mexico, noted for its thick fur and once hunted for its pelt
species of fur seal from Pacific coast

Examples

The Guadalupe fur seal is considered a conservation success story after recovering from near extinction.
Scientists monitor the Guadalupe fur seal population to study its migration patterns.
A group of Guadalupe fur seals rested on the rocky shores of Guadalupe Island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur seal - a seal of the genus Arctocephalus or Callorhinus, with a thick coat of fur

Etymology

Named after Guadalupe Island, off the coast of Mexico, the main breeding site of the species.
