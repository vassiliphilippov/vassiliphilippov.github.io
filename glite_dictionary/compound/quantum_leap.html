quantum leap

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑntəm liːp / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm liːp / (BrE)
a sudden and significant advance, increase, or improvement
sudden significant advance or increase

Examples

The new medication marks a quantum leap in the treatment of this disease.
Switching to renewable energy would be a quantum leap for the entire industry.
Her recent work represents a quantum leap in artistic development.

Synset Examples

this may not insure success but it will represent a quantum leap from last summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step change - a significant change, especially an improvement or increase

Etymology

From the scientific term in quantum physics, referring to the abrupt transition of an electron between energy levels.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑntəm lip / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm liːp / (BrE)
a sudden and very significant change or advance, especially in progress or development
sudden, significant advance or change

Examples

The invention of the internet was a quantum leap in communication technology.
Winning the award marked a quantum leap in his artistic career.
This treatment represents a quantum leap in medical science.

Synset Examples

The technology made a quantum leap in just a year.
Her promotion was a quantum leap in her career.
This discovery represents a quantum leap in our understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakthrough - an important discovery or development
  • Antonym: setback - a reversal or hindrance in progress

Etymology

Originally from physics, where 'quantum leap' denotes an abrupt transition between energy states of an atom; figurative sense arose in the 20th century.
