radio-gramophone

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈɡræməˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈɡræməˌfəʊn / (BrE)
an electronic device that combines a radio receiver and a record player in a single unit
combined radio and record player

Examples

My grandparents still have an old radio-gramophone in their living room.
The radio-gramophone provided both music from records and broadcasts in the 1950s.
He restored a vintage radio-gramophone as part of his antique collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiogram - a piece of furniture combining a radio and record player

Etymology

Formed from 'radio' and 'gramophone' to describe a combined device, common in the mid-20th century.
