index fund

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛks fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɛks fʌnd / (BrE)
A mutual fund or exchange-traded fund that aims to replicate the performance of a specific stock or bond market index by investing in its components.
fund tracking a market index

Examples

Many investors prefer to put their money in an index fund for broad market exposure.
The low fees of an index fund make it attractive for long-term investing.
She invested in an S&P 500 index fund to diversify her portfolio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual fund - an investment vehicle pooling money from investors to buy securities

Etymology

From 'index', meaning a statistical measure of change in a securities market, and 'fund', a pool of money for investment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛks fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈɪndɛks fʌnd / (BrE)
a mutual fund or exchange-traded fund designed to follow the performance of a specific stock market index by holding the same securities in the same proportions as the index
fund tracking a stock market index

Examples

Many investors choose an index fund for its low fees and broad market exposure.
The index fund mirrors the performance of the S&P 500 by holding all its component stocks.
She switched her portfolio from active management to a low-cost index fund.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual fund - an investment vehicle that pools money from many investors to purchase securities
  • Synonym: exchange-traded fund - a fund traded on stock exchanges, much like stocks, often tracking an index
