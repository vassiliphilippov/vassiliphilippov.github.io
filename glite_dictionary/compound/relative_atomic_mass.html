relative atomic mass

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv əˈtɑmɪk ˈmæs / (AmE), / ˈrelətɪv əˈtɒmɪk ˈmæs / (BrE)
The mass of an atom of a chemical element, expressed in atomic mass units and compared to one-twelfth the mass of a carbon-12 atom.
mass of atom in atomic mass units
medical

Examples

The relative atomic mass of hydrogen is approximately 1.01.
When calculating the molecular mass, we sum the relative atomic masses of all the atoms present in the molecule.
Carbon-12 is the standard by which all relative atomic masses are measured.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic weight - the average mass of atoms of an element, measured in atomic mass units
  • Antonym: atomic number - the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom
