nagami kumquat

1. NOUN

/ nəˈɡɑːmi ˈkʌmˌkwɑt / (AmE), / nəˈɡɑːmi ˈkʌmkwɒt / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub native to East Asia that bears oval, orange kumquat fruits, commonly grown for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
a shrub producing oval kumquats

Examples

The nagami kumquat is prized both for its tangy fruit and its decorative appeal in gardens.
She planted a nagami kumquat in her backyard to enjoy fresh citrus snacks in the winter.
Unlike other citrus trees, the nagami kumquat tolerates cooler temperatures well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kumquat - a small orange-like fruit or the plant bearing it

Etymology

From Japanese 'nagami' meaning 'long', and English 'kumquat', from Cantonese 'gam gwat' meaning 'golden orange'.
