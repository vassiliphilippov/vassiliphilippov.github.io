mind's ear

1. NOUN

/ maɪndz ɪɚ / (AmE), / maɪndz ɪə / (BrE)
the ability to imagine or recall sounds as if hearing them, especially music or speech, within the mind
ability to imagine sounds mentally

Examples

She practiced the piano until she could replay the piece perfectly in her mind's ear.
Even after the concert ended, the melody lingered in his mind's ear.
Poets often hear the rhythm of their words with their mind's ear before writing them down.

Synset Examples

He played the melody in his mind's ear.
I can hear the song in my mind's ear.
She recited the poem in her mind's ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mind's eye - the ability to imagine or visualize images in the mind

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'mind's eye', modeled on the sensory organ 'ear'.
