Trillium sessile

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪliəm ˈsɛsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪliəm ˈsesɪl / (BrE)
A North American plant species with sessile leaves and red or purple flowers that have a pungent odor, commonly known as toadshade.
species of trillium with sessile leaves and pungent flowers

Examples

The deep red flowers of Trillium sessile appear each spring in shaded woodlands.
Botanists often refer to Trillium sessile as toadshade because of its unique appearance.
The pungent smell of Trillium sessile attracts certain pollinators in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadshade - Common name for Trillium sessile

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Trillium and the Latin 'sessilis' (sitting, without a stalk).
