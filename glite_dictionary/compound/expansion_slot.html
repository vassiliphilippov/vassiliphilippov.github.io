expansion slot

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspænʃən slɑt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspænʃən slɒt / (BrE)
a socket in a computer, especially a microcomputer, designed to accept an additional plug-in circuit board, such as for memory or graphics.
socket for plug-in computer circuit board

Examples

The new graphics card fits into the expansion slot on the motherboard.
Many older computers have only one available expansion slot for upgrades.
Some laptops lack an expansion slot, limiting their ability to add new hardware.

Synset Examples

the PC had three slots for additional memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slot - a long narrow opening for receiving or admitting something
