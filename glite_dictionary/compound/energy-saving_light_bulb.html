energy-saving light bulb

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi ˌseɪvɪŋ ˈlaɪt ˌbʌlb / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi ˌseɪvɪŋ ˈlaɪt ˌbʌlb / (BrE)
A light bulb designed to use less electricity than traditional incandescent bulbs, such as compact fluorescent lamps (CFLs) or LEDs.
electric bulb that uses less energy

Examples

We replaced all the old lamps with energy-saving light bulbs to reduce our electricity bill.
Installing an energy-saving light bulb can significantly lower household energy consumption.
Many supermarkets now stock a wide variety of energy-saving light bulbs for consumers.

Synset Examples

Nowadays, most commonly and widely used bulb in the household is the Compact Fluorescent Light (CFL)
here are three general types of lighting that are widely used in the household nowadays: the (light-emitting diode) LED bulb, (compact fluorescent light) CFL bulb and the incandescent bulb
However, the main problem of CFL is the mercury they contain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CFL - compact fluorescent lamp, a type of energy-saving light bulb
  • Synonym: LED bulb - light-emitting diode bulb, another kind of energy-efficient bulb
  • Antonym: incandescent bulb - traditional light bulb that is less energy-efficient
