Lafora's disease

1. NOUN

/ ləˈfɔr.əz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ləˈfɔː.rəz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare inherited form of epilepsy characterized by muscle spasms, progressive mental deterioration, and the formation of abnormal glycogen deposits in the brain.
rare inherited epilepsy with mental decline
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected Lafora's disease after the patient began experiencing frequent seizures and cognitive decline.
Families affected by Lafora's disease often seek genetic counseling.
Early diagnosis of Lafora's disease is crucial for managing its progressive symptoms.

Etymology

Named after Gonzalo Rodríguez Lafora, Spanish neurologist who first described the disease in 1911.
