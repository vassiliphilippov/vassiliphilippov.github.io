vanadic acid

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnædɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / vəˈnadɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
any of several oxyacids of vanadium, chiefly known through their salts, especially those with the anion VO3−.
a group of oxyacids of vanadium
medical

Examples

The laboratory synthesized vanadic acid to study its chemical reactions.
Certain minerals contain vanadic acid salts, giving them a characteristic color.
Researchers are investigating vanadic acid for its potential use in catalysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanadium pentoxide - an oxide of vanadium, often related to vanadic acids
  • Synonym: vanadate - a salt containing the vanadate ion, derived from vanadic acid

Etymology

From 'vanadic', referring to vanadium, after the element, and 'acid' from Latin 'acidus' (sour).
