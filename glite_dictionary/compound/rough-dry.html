to rough-dry

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌfˌdraɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌdraɪ / (BrE)
to dry (laundry) without smoothing or ironing after washing, usually leaving it slightly rough or crumpled
dry laundry without ironing

Examples

After the power outage, we had to rough-dry all our clothes on the line.
Some people prefer to rough-dry shirts before ironing to save energy.
The instructions say not to rough-dry delicate fabrics, as they may become too wrinkled.

Synset Examples
