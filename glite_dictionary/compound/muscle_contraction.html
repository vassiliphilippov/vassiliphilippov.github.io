muscle contraction

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl kənˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl kənˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
(physiology) a shortening or tensing of a muscle or muscle fiber that results in movement or tension.
shortening or tensing of a muscle
medical

Examples

Frequent muscle contraction during exercise can lead to fatigue.
A cramp is an involuntary muscle contraction that can be quite painful.
Proper hydration helps prevent strong muscle contraction during intense physical activity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxation - the release of tension in a muscle after contraction
