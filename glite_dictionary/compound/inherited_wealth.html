inherited wealth

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd wɛlθ / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd wɛlθ / (BrE)
Wealth received from relatives or ancestors by inheritance, rather than earned through work or investment.
wealth received by inheritance

Examples

She used her inherited wealth to start a charitable foundation.
Much of the city's old architecture was funded by families with significant inherited wealth.
Debate continues over the fairness of inherited wealth in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legacy - money or property left to someone in a will
  • Antonym: earned income - money received from working or investments rather than inheritance
