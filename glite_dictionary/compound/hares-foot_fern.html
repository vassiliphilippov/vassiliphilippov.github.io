hare's-foot fern

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛrz ˈfʊt fɜrn / (AmE), / ˌheəz ˈfʊt fɜːn / (BrE)
a fern of the genus Davallia, characterized by a soft grey, hairy rootstock that resembles a hare's foot
a fern with a hairy rootstock, genus Davallia

Examples

The hare's-foot fern is popular as a houseplant because of its distinctive hairy rhizomes.
Gardeners often grow the hare's-foot fern in hanging baskets to show off its creeping roots.
She identified the plant as a hare's-foot fern by examining its soft, grey rootstock.
