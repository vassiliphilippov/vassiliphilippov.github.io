house snake

1. NOUN

/ haʊs sneɪk / (AmE), / haʊs sneɪk / (BrE)
a nonvenomous snake found chiefly in Africa, typically tan or brown with distinctive markings; some southeastern varieties have red stripes resembling those of coral snakes
a nonvenomous African snake with characteristic markings

Examples

The farmer discovered a house snake in his shed and decided to let it stay to help with rodents.
Although the house snake looks intimidating, it is actually harmless and nonvenomous.
In parts of Africa, people welcome the presence of a house snake in their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king snake - a harmless North American snake species, often with banded coloration
