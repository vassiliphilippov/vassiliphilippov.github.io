vapor density

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪpɚ ˈdɛnsɪti / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə ˈdɛnsɪti / (BrE)
the density of a gas or vapor compared to the density of hydrogen under the same conditions
relative density of a gas to hydrogen

Examples

The vapor density of oxygen is approximately 16 compared to hydrogen.
Knowing the vapor density of a gas can help assess its hazards in the laboratory.
Scientists calculated the vapor density to determine the molecular weight of an unknown substance.
