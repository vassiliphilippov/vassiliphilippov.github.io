historical present

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkəl ˈprɛzənt / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəl ˈprɛzənt / (BrE)
the use of the present tense when describing past events, typically to make the action more vivid or immediate
present tense used for past events

Examples

Writers often use the historical present to create a sense of immediacy in narratives.
In the sentence "She walks into the room and sits down," the historical present is used.
The historical present occurs frequently in spoken storytelling.

Etymology

From 'historical' (from Greek historikos) and 'present' referring to the verb tense.
