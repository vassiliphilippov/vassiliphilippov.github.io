youth subculture

1. NOUN

/ juːθ ˈsʌbˌkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / juːθ ˈsʌbˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
A social group formed by young people, often with distinctive styles, interests, and behaviors that set it apart from the dominant culture, typically influenced by their social class, ethnicity, or chosen music genre.
distinctive social group of young people

Examples

The punk youth subculture of the 1970s challenged traditional norms with its fashion and music.
Sociologists often study how a youth subculture forms around a particular style of music or fashion.
The city became famous for its vibrant youth subculture centered on skateboarding and hip-hop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subculture - a cultural group within a larger culture, often with differing beliefs or interests
