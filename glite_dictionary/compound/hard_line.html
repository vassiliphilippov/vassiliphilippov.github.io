hard line

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd laɪn / (AmE), / hɑːd laɪn / (BrE)
a firm and uncompromising stance or approach, especially in politics or negotiation
firm and uncompromising stance

Examples

The new administration is expected to take a hard line on corruption.
She always adopts a hard line during contract negotiations.
The committee's hard line left little room for compromise.

Synset Examples

the governor took a hard line on drugs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft line - a more flexible or accommodating stance
  • Synonym: tough stance - a strong or strict position on an issue
