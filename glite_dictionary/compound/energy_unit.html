energy unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi ˌjunɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure energy or work, such as the joule or calorie
unit for measuring energy or work

Examples

The joule is a common energy unit in physics.
Calories are a familiar energy unit used on food labels.
Scientists often work with different energy units depending on the context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joule - the SI unit of energy, equal to one newton meter
  • Synonym: calorie - a unit of energy; often used for food energy
