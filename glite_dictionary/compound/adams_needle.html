Adam's needle

1. NOUN

/ ˈædəmz ˈnidl̩ / (AmE), / ˈædəmz ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
A species of yucca plant with long, stiff leaves that have filament-like threads along their edges.
a yucca plant with filamented leaves

Examples

The garden was dominated by a tall Adam's needle in full bloom.
One of the most distinctive features of Adam's needle is its long, pointed leaves with fibrous threads.
Many homeowners plant Adam's needle for its drought tolerance and ornamental flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a plant genus containing species with stiff leaves and tall flower spikes

Etymology

Named for the biblical figure Adam, perhaps referring to the plant's pointed leaves as tools or needles.
