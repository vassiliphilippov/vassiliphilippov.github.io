bottle gentian

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑt̬.əl ˈdʒɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl ˈdʒɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
a gentian plant of eastern North America, noted for its tubular blue or white flowers that remain mostly closed
a North American gentian with tubular closed flowers

Examples

The vibrant blue flowers of the bottle gentian brighten many autumn meadows.
Unlike most other wildflowers, bottle gentian blooms rarely open fully.
We identified several bottle gentians growing along the edge of the woodland trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - a plant of the genus Gentiana, known for its bright blue flowers
