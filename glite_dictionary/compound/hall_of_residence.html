hall of residence

1. NOUN

/ hɔl əv ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / hɔːl əv ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
A building where university or college students live during term time; a dormitory, especially in British English.
university student dormitory (British)
british

Examples

All first-year students are guaranteed a place in a hall of residence.
Living in a hall of residence is a great way to meet new friends at university.
She moved out of her hall of residence after her second year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormitory - a large bedroom for a number of people in a school or institution
