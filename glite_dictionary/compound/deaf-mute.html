deaf-mute

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfˌmjut / (AmE), / ˈdɛːfˌmjuːt / (BrE)
A person who cannot hear or speak, especially as a result of congenital deafness; this term is now considered outdated and offensive.
person unable to hear or speak (offensive, outdated)
offensive archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, many schools for the deaf-mute were established across Europe.
She communicated with her deaf-mute brother using sign language.
The term deaf-mute is now rarely used due to its offensive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deaf - lacking the sense of hearing
  • Synonym: mute - unable to speak

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfˌmjut / (AmE), / ˈdɛfˌmjuːt / (BrE)
A person who is both deaf and unable to speak, typically as a result of congenital or early-onset deafness.
a person who cannot hear or speak

Examples

She learned to communicate with her deaf-mute son using sign language.
In the early 20th century, many schools were established for deaf-mute children.
The author wrote a novel featuring a deaf-mute protagonist overcoming adversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deaf - unable to hear
  • Synonym: mute - unable to speak

Etymology

From 'deaf' (Old English 'dēaf') + 'mute' (Latin 'mutus', silent).
