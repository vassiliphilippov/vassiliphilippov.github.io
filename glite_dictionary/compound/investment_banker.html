investment banker

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈbæŋkər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈbaŋkə / (BrE)
a person who works for a financial institution that helps companies raise capital by underwriting and issuing securities, and provides advisory services on mergers, acquisitions, and other financial matters
a person who facilitates securities underwriting and financial advisory

Examples

After graduating from business school, she became an investment banker at a major firm in New York.
The investment banker advised the company on its upcoming merger to ensure a smooth process.
Many people assume being an investment banker involves long hours and high-pressure deals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: banker - a person who works in or manages a bank
