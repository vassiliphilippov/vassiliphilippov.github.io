Brazilian rosewood

1. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪl.i.ən ˈroʊzˌwʊd / (AmE), / brəˈzɪl.i.ən ˈrəʊz.wʊd / (BrE)
a large Brazilian tree (Dalbergia nigra) valued for its hard, dark-colored wood streaked with black, used especially for furniture and musical instruments
a Brazilian tree valued for its dark wood

Examples

Many luxury guitars are crafted from Brazilian rosewood due to its unique tonal qualities.
Deforestation has severely reduced the population of Brazilian rosewood trees in the wild.
The desk was an antique, made of polished Brazilian rosewood with a deep, reddish-brown hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - a general term for various woods with a rose-like scent and color
