devil's walking stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz ˈwɔkɪŋ ˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz ˈwɔːkɪŋ ˌstɪk / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree or shrub (Aralia spinosa) of eastern North America, with spiny stems and large compound leaves
spiny tree or shrub (Aralia spinosa) of eastern US

Examples

The devil's walking stick in our garden blooms with clusters of white flowers each summer.
Many birds are attracted to the berries of the devil's walking stick in the fall.
Be careful when handling a devil's walking stick, as its stem is covered in sharp spines.
