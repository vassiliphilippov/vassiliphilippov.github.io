Adlerian psychology

1. NOUN

/ ædˈlɪəriən saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / adˈlɪəriən saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A branch of psychology founded by Alfred Adler that emphasizes social integration, a sense of belonging, and the influence of community and lifestyle on personality and behavior.
school of psychology by Alfred Adler

Examples

Many therapists adopt Adlerian psychology to help clients develop a stronger sense of community.
Her approach to counseling is rooted in Adlerian psychology, focusing on social connectedness and personal growth.
Students often study Adlerian psychology to understand the importance of lifestyle and social influences on behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoanalysis - a set of psychological theories and therapeutic techniques founded by Sigmund Freud

Etymology

Named after Alfred Adler (1870–1937), Austrian psychiatrist and founder of this school.
