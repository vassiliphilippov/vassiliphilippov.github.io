blame game

1. NOUN

/ bleɪm ɡeɪm / (AmE), / bleɪm ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a situation in which people or groups try to blame each other for something bad that has happened, rather than accepting responsibility themselves
situation involving mutual accusations

Examples

After the company's failure, a blame game erupted among the executives and employees.
The political blame game following the disaster did nothing to solve the actual problem.
Instead of working together, the committee engaged in a blame game that stalled any real progress.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbleɪm ˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈbleɪm ˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
a situation in which people try to blame each other for something bad that has happened, often to avoid accepting responsibility themselves
situation where blame is exchanged

Examples

After the project's failure, the team quickly started the blame game instead of working together to fix things.
Political leaders often engage in the blame game during times of crisis to avoid accountability.
It's time to stop playing the blame game and look for real solutions.

Synset Examples

The team's failure was just another round of the blame game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scapegoating - the act of blaming an individual or group for something, often unfairly
  • Antonym: accountability - the quality or state of being responsible or answerable
