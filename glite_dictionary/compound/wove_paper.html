wove paper

1. NOUN

/ woʊv ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / wəʊv ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
A type of writing paper with a very faint, uniform mesh pattern, lacking the ribbed texture of laid paper.
paper with a faint mesh pattern

Examples

The publisher selected wove paper for the new edition to create a smooth writing surface.
Collectors often prefer wove paper in antique books because of its durability and appearance.
Unlike laid paper, wove paper does not show prominent lines when held up to the light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: laid paper - writing paper with a ribbed texture from wires in the papermaking mold

Etymology

From past participle of 'weave', referring to the woven wire mesh used in its manufacture.
