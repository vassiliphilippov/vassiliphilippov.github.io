ice cream

1. NOUN

/ aɪs ˈkriːm / (AmE), / aɪs ˈkriːm / (BrE)
A sweet frozen dessert made from cream or milk, sugar, and flavoring, often eaten as a treat.
sweet frozen dessert

Examples

She ordered a bowl of ice cream for dessert.
Nothing beats ice cream on a hot summer day.
They make homemade ice cream in many different flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelato - Italian-style frozen dessert similar to ice cream
  • Degree Lower: sorbet - frozen dessert made from sweetened water and fruit juice or puree

Etymology

From 'ice' (frozen water) and 'cream', first attested in English in the 18th century.

A sweet, frozen dessert made from cream or milk, sugar, and various flavorings, often eaten as a snack or served as a treat.
frozen sweetened dessert

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelato - Italian-style ice cream with a lower fat content

Etymology

From 'ice' (frozen water) and 'cream' (the rich part of milk); first recorded in the 18th century.
