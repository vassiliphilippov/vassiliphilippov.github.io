order Thysanura

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər θaɪsəˈnjʊrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə θɪsəˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
a group of primitive, wingless insects including firebrats, silverfish, and machilids, formerly classified as a single order in entomology.
group of primitive, wingless insects (silverfish, etc.)

Examples

Many kitchens are invaded by order Thysanura insects, such as silverfish and firebrats, especially in humid climates.
Scientists once grouped silverfish and bristletails in order Thysanura, but now they are separated into different orders.
Museum collections sometimes list specimens under order Thysanura, though modern taxonomy has reclassified them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverfish - a small, wingless insect with a silvery body
  • Synonym: firebrat - a small, fast-moving, wingless insect related to the silverfish

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Thysanura,' derived from Greek thysanos (tassel, fringe) and oura (tail), referring to the bristled tails of these insects.
