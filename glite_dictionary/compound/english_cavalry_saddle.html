English cavalry saddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈkæv(ə)lri ˈsæd(ə)l / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈkæv(ə)lri ˈsæd(ə)l / (BrE)
a type of saddle used for cavalry, characterized by a steel cantle and pommel and lacking a horn
saddle used by cavalry, with steel cantle and pommel, no horn

Examples

The museum displayed a rare English cavalry saddle from the 19th century.
He preferred riding with an English cavalry saddle for its sturdy design and comfort.
Unlike the Western style, the English cavalry saddle does not have a horn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle - a seat fastened on the back of a horse for riding
