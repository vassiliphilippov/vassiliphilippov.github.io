lead plant

1. NOUN

/ lid plænt / (AmE), / liːd plɑːnt / (BrE)
a shrub of sandy woodlands and stream banks in the western United States, with pinnate leaves and dull racemose flowers, thought to indicate the presence of lead ore.
a shrub of the western US, indicator of lead ore

Examples

The lead plant is commonly found in the sandy soils along riverbanks in the Midwest.
Some early settlers believed the presence of lead plant signaled nearby mineral deposits.
We saw a cluster of lead plants blooming in the prairie during our hike.
