ammonium chloride

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniəm ˈklɔːrˌaɪd / (AmE), / əˌmɒn.i.əm ˈklɔː.raɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt, often used in dry cells and as a component in chemical processes
white crystalline salt used in batteries

Examples

The electrolyte in some old batteries contains ammonium chloride as a conducting medium.
Pharmaceutical industries sometimes use ammonium chloride as an expectorant.
You can find ammonium chloride listed as an ingredient in certain types of cough medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sal ammoniac - archaic name for ammonium chloride

Etymology

From 'ammonium,' named after the Egyptian god Ammon (ancient source of ammonium salts), and 'chloride', from chlorine.
