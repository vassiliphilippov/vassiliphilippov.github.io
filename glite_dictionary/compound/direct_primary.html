direct primary

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈprʌɪməri / (BrE)
a primary election in which voters directly select the candidates who will run for public office, rather than having candidates chosen by party officials or delegates
primary election where voters choose candidates directly

Examples

The state moved to a direct primary system to give voters more power in selecting candidates.
Many political reforms of the early 20th century included adopting the direct primary for party nominations.
Voters participated enthusiastically in the direct primary to choose their party’s nominee for governor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primary election - an election to select candidates for public office
