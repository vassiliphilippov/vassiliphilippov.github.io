national service

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənəl ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
compulsory service in the armed forces, usually required of citizens during peacetime, often for a set period.
compulsory peacetime military service

Examples

After university, he was required to do national service for two years.
The government considered reintroducing national service to address a shortage of recruits.
His father met many lifelong friends during his national service in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscription - the compulsory enlistment of people in a national service, typically into the armed forces
  • Antonym: voluntary service - service carried out by choice, not required by law
