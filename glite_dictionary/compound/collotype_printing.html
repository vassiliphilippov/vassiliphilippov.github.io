collotype printing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləˌtaɪp ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒləˌtaɪp ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a photomechanical printing process in which a glass plate coated with gelatin carries the image to be reproduced, capable of producing one or more colors
photomechanical printing process using a gelatin-coated plate

Examples

The museum's collection includes rare photographs produced by collotype printing.
Because of its ability to reproduce fine details, collotype printing was popular in art books.
They specialized in collotype printing for color postcards in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collotype - a method of printing using a gelatin-coated plate

Etymology

From 'collotype', combining Greek 'kolla' (glue) and 'type' (impression or form), referring to the gelatin layer used for printing.
