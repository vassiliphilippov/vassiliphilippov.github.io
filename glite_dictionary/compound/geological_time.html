geological time

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.əˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl taɪm / (BrE)
the immense span of time covering the physical formation and development of the Earth, especially before the appearance of humans
the vast duration of Earth's history

Examples

Dinosaurs lived during a period of geological time known as the Mesozoic Era.
The Grand Canyon reveals layers of rock that formed over millions of years of geological time.
Events in geological time often span millions or even billions of years.

Etymology

From 'geological' (relating to geology) + 'time'. 'Geological' derives from Greek 'gē' (earth) and 'logos' (study).
