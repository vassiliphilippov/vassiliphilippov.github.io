white-collar

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪt ˌkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌkɒlə / (BrE)
Relating to employees who perform professional, managerial, or administrative work, typically in an office environment.
involving professional or office work

Examples

He transitioned from a factory job to a white-collar position in the finance department.
Many white-collar workers are able to work remotely from home these days.
The company is downsizing both its white-collar and blue-collar workforce.

Synset Examples

the coal miner's son aspired to a white-collar occupation as a bookkeeper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blue-collar - relating to manual industrial or factory work

Etymology

From the traditional white shirts worn by office workers, contrasted with 'blue-collar' for manual laborers.
