prison chaplain

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən ˈtʃæplɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɪzn ˈtʃæplɪn / (BrE)
a member of the clergy or a religious official who provides religious services and support to inmates in a prison
clergy member serving prisoners

Examples

The prison chaplain organized weekly services for inmates of all faiths.
After his release, he credited the prison chaplain with helping him turn his life around.
The prison chaplain provided comfort to prisoners during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaplain - a member of the clergy attached to an institution, such as a school, hospital, or prison
