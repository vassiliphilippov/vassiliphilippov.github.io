common grape hyacinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ɡreɪp haɪˈæsɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ɡreɪp haɪˈæsɪnθ / (BrE)
a small, prolific plant of the species Muscari botryoides, having clusters of dark blue, bell-shaped flowers resembling grapes, commonly grown as an ornamental in gardens
a plant with blue, grape-like flower clusters

Examples

The common grape hyacinth blooms in early spring, adding a splash of blue to the garden.
Gardeners often plant common grape hyacinth along borders for their striking color and easy care.
A cluster of common grape hyacinth made the flowerbed look lively and inviting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grape hyacinth - any small plant of the genus Muscari with dense clusters of small, usually blue flowers
