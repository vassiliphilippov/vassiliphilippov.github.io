family Cordylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kɔːrˈdɪlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kɔːˈdɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A small family of spiny, ovoviviparous lizards native to Africa, known for their armored scales.
African family of spiny lizards

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the family Cordylidae in South Africa.
Members of the family Cordylidae have evolved tough, spiny scales as a defense against predators.
Many lizards in the family Cordylidae give birth to live young, unlike most reptiles that lay eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cordylus - A genus of spiny-tailed lizards in the family Cordylidae
  • Degree Lower: skink - Any lizard of the family Scincidae, similar in appearance to Cordylidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Cordylidae, based on Greek kordyle (club), referring to shape of the tail in some species.
