clear-cut

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɪr ˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈklɪə ˌkʌt / (BrE)
Definite and easy to perceive, understand, or make a decision about; unambiguous and sharply defined.
definite, easy to understand

Examples

The instructions for the exam were clear-cut and left little room for confusion.
Her stance on the issue was clear-cut, making her arguments easy to follow.
There was a clear-cut winner after the judges' final decision.

Synset Examples

The rules for the competition are clear cut and easy to follow.
The decision was clear cut; we had to move forward.
His explanation left no room for doubt; it was clear cut.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning
  • Synonym: definite - clearly stated or decided; not vague or doubtful

Etymology

From 'clear' (easily perceived) + 'cut' (sharply divided), used figuratively since early 19th century.
