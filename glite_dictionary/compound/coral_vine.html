coral vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɹəl vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒɹəl vaɪn / (BrE)
A prostrate or twining woody vine with small leathery leaves and clusters of red flowers, native to Australia and Tasmania.
Australian woody vine with red flowers

Examples

The coral vine blooms profusely in the Australian spring, adding color to forest edges.
Gardeners admire the vibrant red flowers of the coral vine as it twines along fences.
The small leathery leaves help the coral vine tolerate dry Tasmanian summers.
