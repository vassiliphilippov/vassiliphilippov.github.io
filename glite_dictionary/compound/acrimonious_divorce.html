acrimonious divorce

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈmoʊniəs dɪˈvɔrs / (AmE), / ˌakrɪˈməʊnɪəs dɪˈvɔːs / (BrE)
a divorce in which the separation process is marked by bitterness and hostility between the parties involved
bitter, hostile divorce

Examples

After an acrimonious divorce, they rarely spoke to each other again.
Their acrimonious divorce made headlines in the local newspapers for months.
The lawyer specialized in handling particularly acrimonious divorce cases.

Synset Examples

The acrimonious divorce took years to settle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amicable divorce - a divorce in which the separation is friendly and without hostility
