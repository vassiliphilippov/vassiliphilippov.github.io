Song dynasty

1. NOUN

/ sɔŋ ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / sɒŋ ˈdɪ.nə.sti / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from 960 to 1279, known for significant achievements in art, literature, and philosophy
Chinese imperial dynasty, 960–1279

Examples

The Song dynasty produced remarkable advances in technology and culture that influenced all of East Asia.
During the Song dynasty, landscape painting became one of the most prestigious forms of art in China.
Many classic works of Chinese poetry and philosophy were written during the Song dynasty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Tang dynasty - the Chinese dynasty preceding the Song, 618–907
  • Degree Higher: Yuan dynasty - the Chinese dynasty following the Song, 1271–1368

Etymology

From Mandarin 宋 (Sòng), the Chinese name of the dynasty.
