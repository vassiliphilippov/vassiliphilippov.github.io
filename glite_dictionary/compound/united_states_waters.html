United States waters

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪtɪd ˈsteɪts ˈwɔːt̬ɚz / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtɪd ˈsteɪts ˈwɔːtəz / (BrE)
the area of sea within 12 nautical miles of the coasts of the United States and its territories, over which the United States exercises sovereignty
sea area within 12 miles of U.S. coasts

Examples

Fishing boats must have a special permit to operate in United States waters.
Foreign vessels are subject to inspection when they enter United States waters.
Environmental regulations are strictly enforced in United States waters.

Synset Examples

ships operating in United States waters must adhere to United States laws and regulations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territorial waters - waters extending up to 12 nautical miles from a state's coast, over which it has sovereignty
