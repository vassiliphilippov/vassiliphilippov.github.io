geological dating

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑlədʒɪkəl ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒlədʒɪkəl ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of determining the age of rocks, fossils, or geological formations, typically through chemical or physical analysis
determining the age of rocks or fossils

Examples

Scientists used geological dating to determine that the rocks were more than a billion years old.
Advances in geological dating have improved our understanding of Earth's history.
By applying geological dating techniques, the team was able to estimate the age of the fossil more accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiometric dating - dating geological specimens using radioactive isotopes
