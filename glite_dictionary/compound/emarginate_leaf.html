emarginate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɑrdʒəneɪt liːf / (AmE), / ɪˈmɑːdʒɪneɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf that has a shallow notch at the tip (apex).
leaf with a notched tip

Examples

The botanist pointed out the emarginate leaf on the oak sapling during the field trip.
An emarginate leaf is characterized by a distinct, shallow notch at the tip.
Many species in this genus have at least one emarginate leaf among their foliage.

Etymology

From Latin 'emarginatus' (notched at the edge), describing something with a shallow notch at the margin.
