Empire State

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛmˌpaɪɚ steɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛmpaɪə steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of New York, one of the original thirteen American colonies
nickname for New York State, USA

Examples

Many immigrants arrived in the Empire State seeking new opportunities in the 19th century.
New York earned its nickname, the Empire State, due to its wealth and strategic location.
The Empire State has played a crucial role in American history and commerce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York - a state in the northeastern United States, capital Albany

Etymology

Refers to New York's wealth and resources; popularized in the early 19th century.
