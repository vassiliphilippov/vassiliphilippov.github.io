past simple

1. NOUN

/ pæst ˈsɪmpəl / (AmE), / pɑːst ˈsɪmpəl / (BrE)
the form of a verb used to describe an action or state that occurred before the present time and is no longer continuing
verb tense for completed actions in the past

Examples

When writing a story about yesterday, you should use the past simple tense for events that have finished.
The verb 'walked' is in the past simple form.
Students often confuse the past simple with the present perfect tense.

Synset Examples

Regular verbs form the simple past in ‘-ed’; however there are a few hundred irregular verbs with different forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: past tense - a verb tense expressing actions or states in the past
