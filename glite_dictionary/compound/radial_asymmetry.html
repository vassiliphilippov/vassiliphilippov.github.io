radial asymmetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəl əˈsɪmətri / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəl əˈsɪmətri / (BrE)
The absence of symmetry around a central axis, especially in biological or geometric forms.
lack of symmetry around a central axis

Examples

Some marine sponges exhibit radial asymmetry instead of the more common bilateral symmetry.
In biology, radial asymmetry can affect how an organism interacts with its environment.
The unusual fossil displayed clear radial asymmetry, making classification difficult.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radial symmetry - symmetry around a central axis
  • Antonym: bilateral symmetry - symmetry between two sides of a plane

Etymology

From 'radial' (relating to a radius or rays) and 'asymmetry' (lack of symmetry), both ultimately from Latin origins.
