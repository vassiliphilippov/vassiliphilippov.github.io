existential philosophy

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzɪstɛnʃəl fəˈlɑsəfi / (AmE), / ɛɡˌzɪstɛnʃəl fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
A 20th-century European philosophical movement that emphasizes individual freedom, choice, and responsibility, holding that people define their own existence.
philosophical movement focusing on individual freedom and responsibility

Examples

Jean-Paul Sartre's writings are central to existential philosophy and its focus on personal responsibility.
Many college courses introduce students to existential philosophy through the works of Kierkegaard and Camus.
The question of life's meaning is a major concern in existential philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existentialism - the philosophical theory centered on individual existence, freedom, and choice
  • Degree Lower: nihilism - the rejection of all religious and moral principles, often associated with existential themes

Etymology

From 'existential', relating to existence (from Latin 'existentia'), and 'philosophy', from Greek 'philosophia' (love of wisdom).
