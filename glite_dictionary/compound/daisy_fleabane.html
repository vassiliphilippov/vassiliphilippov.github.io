daisy fleabane

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪzi ˈfliːˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi ˈfliːˌbeɪn / (BrE)
a North American wildflower with small white flowers, often found naturalized in fields and along roadsides
a white-flowered North American wildflower

Examples

The meadow was dotted with daisy fleabane and other wildflowers in early summer.
Gardeners often plant daisy fleabane to attract butterflies and bees.
I spotted a cluster of daisy fleabane growing along the edge of the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleabane - a plant believed to repel fleas, with small daisy-like flowers

Etymology

"Fleabane" comes from the belief that the plant repelled fleas; "daisy" refers to the flower's resemblance to the daisy.
