grain sorghum

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪn ˈsɔrɡəm / (AmE), / ɡreɪn ˈsɔːɡəm / (BrE)
A variety of sorghum (Sorghum bicolor) cultivated chiefly for its edible grain, used as food, animal feed, or in industry.
sorghum cultivated for its grain

Examples

Grain sorghum is well adapted to drought-prone areas where other cereal crops often fail.
Many farmers rotate corn with grain sorghum to improve soil health and diversify their harvest.
The recipe calls for grain sorghum, which can be popped like popcorn or milled into flour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of cereal grasses, including species cultivated for grain and fodder

Etymology

From 'sorghum' (from Italian sorgo, from Latin surgum) and 'grain' (from Latin granum).
