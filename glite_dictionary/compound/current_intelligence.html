current intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.ənt ɪnˈtɛl.ə.dʒəns / (AmE), / ˈkʌr.ənt ɪnˈtɛl.ɪ.dʒəns / (BrE)
Intelligence of all types and forms that is of immediate concern and is usually distributed without significant delay for analysis or interpretation.
intelligence of immediate interest

Examples

The agency relies heavily on current intelligence to inform real-time decisions about national security threats.
In the rapidly changing situation, current intelligence proved more useful than older reports.
Briefings based on current intelligence allowed the military to respond quickly to new developments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finished intelligence - intelligence that has been evaluated and interpreted
