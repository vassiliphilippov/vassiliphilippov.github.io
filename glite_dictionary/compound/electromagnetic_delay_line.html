electromagnetic delay line

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (BrE)
an electrical device that delays signals by using the time it takes for electromagnetic waves to travel along it
device delaying signals using electromagnetic waves

Examples

The engineer used an electromagnetic delay line to synchronize the timing of the signals.
Older televisions sometimes relied on an electromagnetic delay line for color processing.
By adjusting the electromagnetic delay line, the technician achieved the correct signal alignment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay line - a device that delays an electrical signal for a set period
