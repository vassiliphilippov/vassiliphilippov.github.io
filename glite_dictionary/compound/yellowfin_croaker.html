yellowfin croaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌfɪn ˈkroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌfɪn ˈkrəʊkə / (BrE)
a fish of the Pacific coast of North America, noted for its yellow fins and croaking sound
Pacific coastal fish with yellow fins

Examples

The yellowfin croaker is commonly caught by anglers along California beaches.
A yellowfin croaker can be recognized by its golden fins and silvery body.
Many fishermen prize the yellowfin croaker for its tasty white flesh.

Etymology

From the fish's distinctive yellow fins and the croaking noise it produces.
