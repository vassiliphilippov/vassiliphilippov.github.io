dispatch box

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspætʃ bɑks / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ bɒks / (BrE)
an oblong locked container used for carrying official dispatches or other valuables, especially in government or parliamentary settings
official locked box for dispatches or valuables

Examples

The prime minister placed his speech inside the dispatch box before entering the chamber.
Valuable documents were secured overnight in the dispatch box.
The red dispatch box is a symbol of authority in the British Parliament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefcase - a flat, rectangular container carried by hand, used for documents or personal items

Etymology

From 'dispatch' (to send off, from Italian *dispacciare*) + 'box', referring to a container.
