British pound

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ paʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ paʊnd / (BrE)
the official currency unit of the United Kingdom, equal to 100 pence
the main currency of the UK
british

Examples

The exchange rate for the British pound rose sharply against the US dollar.
She exchanged her euros for British pounds before traveling to London.
A cup of coffee in central London might cost around three British pounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound sterling - the formal term for the currency of the UK
  • Antonym: euro - the official currency of countries in the eurozone

Etymology

From Latin 'pondus' meaning 'weight'; 'pound' has long been a unit of weight and value in Britain.
