major tranquilizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒɚ ˈtræŋkwəˌlaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə ˈtræŋkwɪˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a drug used to treat psychotic conditions by producing a calming effect; also known as an antipsychotic
drug for treating psychotic conditions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a major tranquilizer to help manage the patient's schizophrenia.
Do not stop taking your major tranquilizer suddenly without consulting your psychiatrist.
Many older major tranquilizers have been replaced by new medications with fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor tranquilizer - a tranquilizer mainly used to treat anxiety and not psychosis
  • Synonym: antipsychotic - a drug used to manage psychosis
