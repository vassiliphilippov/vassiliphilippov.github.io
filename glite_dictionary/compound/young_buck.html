young buck

1. NOUN

/ jʌŋ bʌk / (AmE), / jʌŋ bʌk / (BrE)
a teenage or young adult man, often noted for energy, enthusiasm, or ambition
teenage or young adult male

Examples

The local basketball team is full of talented young bucks eager to prove themselves.
Every summer, a few young bucks would challenge each other to swim across the river.
As a young buck, he was always the first to volunteer for any adventure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lad - a boy or young man
  • Antonym: old man - an older adult male

Etymology

Originally referring to a young male deer; later, figuratively, to energetic young men.
