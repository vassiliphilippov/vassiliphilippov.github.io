Lyme arthritis

1. NOUN

/ laɪm ɑːrˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / laɪm ɑːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A form of joint inflammation caused by infection with the bacteria that cause Lyme disease, often resulting in swelling, pain, and sometimes fever, typically following a tick bite.
joint inflammation caused by Lyme disease
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with Lyme arthritis after experiencing persistent knee pain and swelling.
Children with Lyme arthritis often respond well to antibiotic treatment if caught early.
The doctor explained that Lyme arthritis can develop months after a tick bite if the infection is untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lyme disease - an infectious disease transmitted by ticks, causing various symptoms including rash and joint pain

Etymology

From 'Lyme' (named after Lyme, Connecticut, where the disease was first identified) + 'arthritis' (inflammation of the joints, from Greek 'arthron' meaning joint).
