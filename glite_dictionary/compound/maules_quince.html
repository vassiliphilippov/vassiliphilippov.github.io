Maule's quince

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːlz ˌkwɪns / (AmE), / ˈmɔːlz ˌkwɪns / (BrE)
A deciduous thorny shrub native to Japan, known for its red blossoms; often grown as an ornamental plant.
Japanese thorny shrub with red flowers

Examples

The flowers of the Maule's quince brighten up gardens in early spring.
She planted a Maule's quince along the edge of the walkway for its vibrant color.
Cut branches of Maule's quince can be used in floral arrangements.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Maule, a 19th-century horticulturist, and quince due to its resemblance to true quince.
