situation comedy

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtʃuˈeɪʃən ˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˌsɪtʃuˈeɪʃən ˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
a humorous television or radio program based on characters in everyday situations, typically with recurring settings and cast
a comedy show based on everyday situations

Examples

The classic situation comedy features a group of friends sharing an apartment and navigating everyday problems.
He wrote for a popular situation comedy that aired during the 1990s.
Many viewers enjoy relaxing in the evening by watching a lighthearted situation comedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sitcom - short form for 'situation comedy', a humorous TV or radio series

Etymology

Formed from 'situation', meaning 'set of circumstances', and 'comedy', from Greek 'kōmōidia' (comic play).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtʃ.uˈeɪ.ʃən ˈkɑː.mə.di / (AmE), / ˌsɪtjʊˈeɪʃən ˈkɒm.ə.di / (BrE)
a humorous television or radio program that is based on situations that could realistically occur in everyday life, typically featuring recurring characters
type of comedy TV or radio series

Examples

The new situation comedy has quickly become a favorite among young viewers.
She dreams of writing her own situation comedy for network television.
Many classic situation comedies from the 1980s are still popular today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sitcom - informal abbreviation for 'situation comedy'

Etymology

From 'situation' and 'comedy'; term popularized in the mid-20th century for TV and radio formats.
