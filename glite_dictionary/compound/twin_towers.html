twin towers

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪn ˈtaʊɚz / (AmE), / ˈtwɪn ˈtaʊəz / (BrE)
the two identical skyscrapers in New York City known as the World Trade Center, which stood from 1973 until their destruction in the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001.
the two World Trade Center skyscrapers in New York City

Examples

The twin towers were once the tallest buildings in New York City.
Many people remember exactly where they were when the twin towers fell in 2001.
Photographs of the New York skyline often featured the twin towers before their destruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World Trade Center - the complex of buildings in New York including the twin towers
