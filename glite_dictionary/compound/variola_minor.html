variola minor

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriˈoʊlə ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˌværiˈəʊlə ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent strain of the virus, resulting in a lower mortality rate than classical smallpox (variola major).
mild form of smallpox
medical

Examples

The outbreak was identified as variola minor, which has a much lower fatality rate than other types of smallpox.
Many survivors of variola minor were left with only mild scars, unlike the severe disfigurement caused by variola major.
Vaccination campaigns were effective in eradicating both variola minor and more virulent forms of smallpox.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: variola major - the more severe and deadly form of smallpox
  • Synonym: smallpox - the infectious disease caused by the variola virus

Etymology

From Latin 'variola' (smallpox) and Latin 'minor' (smaller, lesser).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriˈoʊlə ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˌværɪˈəʊlə ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a less severe form of the smallpox virus characterized by a low fatality rate, usually about 1 percent
milder strain of smallpox virus
medical

Examples

Variola minor caused outbreaks of milder smallpox in the early twentieth century.
Because variola minor resulted in fewer deaths, its presence was sometimes underreported.
The successful eradication of variola minor marked a turning point in global public health.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: variola major - a more severe form of the smallpox virus with a higher fatality rate
  • Synonym: smallpox - an infectious disease caused by the variola virus

Etymology

From Latin 'variola' (smallpox) and 'minor' (smaller, less).
