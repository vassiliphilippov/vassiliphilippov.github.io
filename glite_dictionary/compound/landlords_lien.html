landlord's lien

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌlɔrdz liːn / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)ˌlɔːdz liːn / (BrE)
the legal right of a landlord to seize a tenant's property as security for unpaid rent or damage to the property
landlord's right over tenant's property for unpaid rent
legal

Examples

Under the lease agreement, the landlord's lien allows the landlord to seize the tenant's furniture for unpaid rent.
The tenant was unaware that a landlord's lien could be exercised on his personal belongings.
Many states restrict the use of a landlord's lien to protect tenants from losing essential items.
