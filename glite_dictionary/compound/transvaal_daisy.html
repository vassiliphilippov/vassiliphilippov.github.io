Transvaal daisy

1. NOUN

/ trænzˌvɑl ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / trænzˌvɑːl ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a widely cultivated South African perennial plant, Gerbera jamesonii, with bright orange to flame-colored flower heads
South African perennial flower, Gerbera jamesonii

Examples

The Transvaal daisy is prized for its brilliant color and long-lasting blooms in floral arrangements.
Gardeners often choose the Transvaal daisy because it thrives in sunny locations and is easy to care for.
She planted several Transvaal daisies along the garden path to add a splash of vivid color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerbera - a genus of ornamental plants from which Transvaal daisy is derived

Etymology

Named after the Transvaal region of South Africa and daisy (from Old English 'dægesege').
