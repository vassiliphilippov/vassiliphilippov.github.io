photo op

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊtoʊ ɑp / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊ ɒp / (BrE)
an occasion, often arranged or staged, that is intended to allow people to be photographed, especially for favorable publicity
publicity event for taking photographs

Examples

The mayor visited the school for a photo op with the students.
Critics accused the politician of using disaster relief as a photo op rather than offering real help.
The company arranged a photo op to announce their new community project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photo opportunity - an occasion arranged for taking publicity photographs

Etymology

Shortened from 'photo opportunity,' itself from 'photo(graph)' and 'opportunity.'
