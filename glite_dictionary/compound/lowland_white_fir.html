lowland white fir

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌlænd waɪt fɜr / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlənd waɪt fɜː / (BrE)
a tall fir tree native to the Pacific coast of northwestern America, known for its long curving branches and deep green leaves
species of fir tree (Pacific NW America)

Examples

The lowland white fir thrives in the cool, moist forests along the Pacific coast.
Hikers admired the long, graceful branches of the lowland white fir towering above them.
Conservationists are monitoring the population of lowland white fir in national parks to ensure its survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white fir - a species of fir with silvery bark and flat needles
