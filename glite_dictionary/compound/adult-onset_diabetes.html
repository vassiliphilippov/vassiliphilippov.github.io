adult-onset diabetes

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌlt ˌɑnˌsɛt daɪəˈbitiz / (AmE), / əˈdʌlt ˌɒnˌsɛt daɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
A form of diabetes mellitus developing gradually in adults, typically controlled by diet or oral medication rather than insulin injections.
diabetes type developed in adulthood
medical

Examples

Many people develop adult-onset diabetes due to a combination of genetic factors and lifestyle choices.
After being diagnosed with adult-onset diabetes, she adjusted her diet and began regular exercise.
Obesity is a significant risk factor for adult-onset diabetes in middle-aged adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 2 diabetes - common chronic form of diabetes with adult onset
  • Antonym: juvenile diabetes - form of diabetes usually beginning in childhood
