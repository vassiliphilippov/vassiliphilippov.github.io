hardening of the arteries

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdnɪŋ əv ði ˈɑrtəriːz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdnɪŋ əv ði ˈɑːtəriːz / (BrE)
a condition in which the walls of the arteries become thickened and less elastic, often leading to impaired blood flow; arteriosclerosis
arteries becoming thickened and less elastic

Examples

His doctor warned him that hardening of the arteries could lead to serious heart problems.
Older adults are often at higher risk of hardening of the arteries due to lifestyle and genetics.
Medical researchers are studying the causes and prevention of hardening of the arteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - thickening and hardening of arterial walls
