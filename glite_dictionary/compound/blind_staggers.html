blind staggers

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈstæɡərz / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈstæɡəz / (BrE)
A disease of the central nervous system in horses and cattle, marked by an unsteady, swaying gait and frequent falling.
nervous disease in livestock causing unsteady gait

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with blind staggers after observing its unsteady walk.
Farmers are warned to check their cattle for symptoms of blind staggers, especially after grazing in certain areas.
Left untreated, blind staggers can result in serious injury or death to affected livestock.
