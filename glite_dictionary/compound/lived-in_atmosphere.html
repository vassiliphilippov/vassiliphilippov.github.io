lived-in atmosphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvd ɪn ˈætməˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈlɪvd ɪn ˈat.mə.sfɪə / (BrE)
a comfortable and familiar environment that feels as though it has been occupied and cared for over time
comfortable, familiar environment

Examples

The old cottage had a lived-in atmosphere that made guests feel right at home.
She decorated her office to create a lived-in atmosphere rather than a sterile, formal space.
They loved the hotel's lived-in atmosphere, which made their stay especially relaxing.

Synset Examples

The apartment had a cozy, lived-in atmosphere.
