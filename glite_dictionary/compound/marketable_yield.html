marketable yield

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkətəbəl ˈjiːld / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtəbəl ˈjiːld / (BrE)
the portion of a crop's output that is of sufficient quality and quantity to be sold, usually measured per unit area
saleable crop output per area

Examples

The marketable yield of strawberries was higher than expected this season.
Farmers often focus on increasing their marketable yield rather than total production.
Adverse weather conditions significantly reduced the marketable yield of tomatoes this year.

Synset Examples

The marketable yield of peppers has increased by 5%, as compared to last year.
