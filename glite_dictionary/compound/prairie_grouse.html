prairie grouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ɡraʊs / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ɡraʊs / (BrE)
a brown mottled grouse native to the grasslands of western North America, including species such as the greater prairie chicken and the sharp-tailed grouse
brown mottled grouse of North American grasslands

Examples

During our trip to the Great Plains, we spotted a rare prairie grouse displaying its feathers.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the prairie grouse in the Midwest.
The prairie grouse performs an elaborate mating dance during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - a medium to large game bird with a plump body and feathered legs
