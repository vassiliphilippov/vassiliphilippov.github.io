order Lycoperdales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌlaɪ.kəˈpɜr.deɪlz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌlaɪ.kəˈpɜː.deɪlz / (BrE)
a small order of basidiomycetous fungi characterized by fleshy, often globose fruiting bodies, including puffballs and earthstars
order of fungi including puffballs and earthstars
medical

Examples

The order Lycoperdales includes many species of puffballs found in temperate forests.
Scientists have reclassified some fungi formerly assigned to the order Lycoperdales.
The characteristic fruiting bodies of fungi in the order Lycoperdales are often round and spongy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffball - a type of fungus with a round, spore-filled fruiting body
  • Synonym: earthstar - a fungus with a star-shaped outer layer of the fruiting body

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lycoperdales', based on the genus 'Lycoperdon'.
