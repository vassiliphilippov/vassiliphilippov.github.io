red worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd wɝm / (AmE), / ˈrɛd wɜːm / (BrE)
a small reddish earthworm that lives in soil, helps aerate it, and is often used as fishing bait
a soil-dwelling, reddish earthworm

Examples

Gardeners appreciate the role of red worms in improving soil quality.
On rainy days, red worms can often be seen crawling on the pavement.
He bought a box of red worms to use as bait for his fishing trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a worm that lives in the soil and helps decompose matter
