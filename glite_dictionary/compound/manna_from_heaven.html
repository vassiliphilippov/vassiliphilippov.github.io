manna from heaven

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænə frəm ˈhɛvən / (AmE), / ˈmænə frəm ˈhɛvən / (BrE)
in the Bible, the miraculous food sent by God to the Israelites during their journey in the desert; also used to refer to an unexpected benefit or windfall
miraculous food or unexpected benefit

Examples

The financial aid they received was like manna from heaven for the struggling charity.
After days of hunger, the villagers regarded the food delivery as manna from heaven.
The sudden rainfall was manna from heaven for the drought-stricken region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - an unexpected gain or benefit
  • Synonym: miracle - an extraordinary and welcome event attributed to a divine agency

Etymology

From Hebrew 'mān' (bread or food), as described in the Old Testament Book of Exodus.
