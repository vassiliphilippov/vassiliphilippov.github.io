family Balaenidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bəˌliːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bəˌliːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large baleen whales known as right whales, characterized by their arched jaws and lack of dorsal fins.
family of right whales (baleen whales)

Examples

The family Balaenidae includes all species commonly known as right whales.
Scientists study the migration patterns of whales in the family Balaenidae.
Members of the family Balaenidae are recognized by their distinctive arched mouths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right whale - a large baleen whale of the genus Eubalaena

Etymology

From Latin 'balaena' (whale) with the taxonomic suffix '-idae' for family.
