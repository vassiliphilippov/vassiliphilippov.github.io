twice-baked bread

1. NOUN

/ twaɪs ˈbeɪkt brɛd / (AmE), / twaɪs ˈbeɪkt brɛd / (BrE)
A slice of sweet, leavened bread that is baked a second time until it becomes brown, hard, and crisp.
bread baked twice until crisp

Examples

She served coffee with homemade twice-baked bread as a crunchy treat.
The bakery's signature twice-baked bread is a local favorite for dipping in tea.
After drying in the oven, the twice-baked bread became golden and crisp.
