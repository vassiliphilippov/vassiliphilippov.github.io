lemon rind

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛ.mən ˌraɪnd / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.ən ˌraɪnd / (BrE)
the outer skin of a lemon, used especially as flavoring in cooking and baking
outer skin of a lemon

Examples

She added a teaspoon of lemon rind to the cake batter for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for grated lemon rind to give the sauce a citrusy aroma.
Dried lemon rind can be used to infuse teas and desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon zest - the thin, colored outer layer of a lemon's rind, used as flavoring
