steel engraving

1. NOUN

/ stiːl ɪnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / stiːl ɪnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
an engraving made by incising a design onto a steel plate, from which prints can be made
engraving on a steel plate for printing

Examples

The museum displayed a rare steel engraving of the city's skyline from the 1800s.
He collects antique books that feature detailed steel engravings as illustrations.
The artist spent months perfecting the steel engraving before printing the final image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etching - printmaking technique involving acid to cut into metal plates
  • Antonym: wood engraving - engraving on a block of wood

2. NOUN

/ stil ɪnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / stiːl ɪnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
A print or impression made from an engraved steel plate, often used in illustration and fine art.
print made from an engraved steel plate

Examples

The gallery displayed a rare steel engraving from the Victorian era.
He collected antique books featuring steel engravings of famous landmarks.
The artist explained the differences between a copperplate engraving and a steel engraving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made from an engraved plate

Etymology

Formed from "steel" + "engraving"; popularized in the 19th century when steel plates replaced copper for engraving.
