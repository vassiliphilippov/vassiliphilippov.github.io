Zyklon A

1. NOUN

/ ˈtsaɪk.lɒn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈtsʏklɔn ˈɑː / (BrE)
A pesticide containing methyl cyanoformate as its active ingredient, developed in early 20th-century Germany.
a pesticide with methyl cyanoformate

Examples

The researchers tested Zyklon A for effectiveness against different insect pests in stored grain.
In the 1920s, Zyklon A was one of several pesticides produced by German chemical companies.
Safety protocols were required when handling Zyklon A due to its toxic properties.

Synset Examples

Research at Degesch of Germany led to the development of Zyklon A, a pesticide which released hydrogen cyanide upon exposure to water and heat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Zyklon B - a cyanide-based pesticide used infamously during the Holocaust

Etymology

From German 'Zyklon' (cyclone); the 'A' distinguishes it from 'Zyklon B', a later variant.
