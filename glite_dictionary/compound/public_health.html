public health

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌblɪk ˈhɛlθ / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪk ˈhɛlθ / (BrE)
the health of the population as a whole, especially as monitored, regulated, and promoted by government and institutions
the health of a population as a whole
medical

Examples

The government launched a campaign to raise awareness about public health issues.
Access to clean water is vital for improving public health in developing countries.
He decided to pursue a career in public health after studying epidemiology.

Synset Examples

As a sanitation engineer, she focuses on public health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: community health - the health status and initiatives specific to local communities
  • Antonym: personal health - an individual's own health status and practices
