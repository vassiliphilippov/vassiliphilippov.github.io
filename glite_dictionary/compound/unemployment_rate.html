unemployment rate

1. NOUN

/ ʌnɪmˌplɔɪmənt reɪt / (AmE), / ʌnɪmˌplɔɪmənt reɪt / (BrE)
the percentage of the labor force that is unemployed at a particular time
percentage of unemployed in the workforce

Examples

The government's report showed that the unemployment rate dropped to its lowest level in a decade.
When the economy slows down, the unemployment rate usually rises.
Economists use the unemployment rate to measure the health of the labor market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: employment rate - the percentage of the labor force that is employed
