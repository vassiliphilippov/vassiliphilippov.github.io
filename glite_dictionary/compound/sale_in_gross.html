sale in gross

1. NOUN

/ seɪl ɪn ɡroʊs / (AmE), / seɪl ɪn ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
a sale of a tract of land as a whole, without guarantee of the exact acreage
land sold as a whole without acreage warranty
legal

Examples

The farm was transferred by a sale in gross, so the buyer could not claim for minor acreage discrepancies.
Under a sale in gross, the exact number of acres is not guaranteed to the purchaser.
They agreed to a sale in gross rather than measuring each boundary for accuracy.

Etymology

From legal use of 'gross' meaning whole, undivided; 'in gross' from Law French and Medieval Latin 'in grosso'.
