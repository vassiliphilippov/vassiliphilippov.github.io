sack race

1. NOUN

/ sæk reɪs / (AmE), / sæk reɪs / (BrE)
a children's race in which competitors hop forward with their legs inside a sack, aiming to reach the finish line first
race where competitors hop in sacks

Examples

The school held a sack race during the annual sports day.
Children laughed as they fell over during the sack race at the picnic.
He won the sack race by a small margin, despite stumbling twice.
