lead acetate

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˈæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / lɛd ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a poisonous white crystalline chemical compound, used in dyeing, enameling, and varnishes
a poisonous white chemical compound

Examples

The factory was fined for improper disposal of lead acetate into the local river.
Cotton fibers are sometimes treated with lead acetate during the dyeing process.
Exposure to lead acetate poses serious health risks and requires strict safety measures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead(II) nitrate - a poisonous lead salt used in dyes and matches

Etymology

From 'lead' (the metal) and 'acetate,' derived from Latin 'acetum' (vinegar).
