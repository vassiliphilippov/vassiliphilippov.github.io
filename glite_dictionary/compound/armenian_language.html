Armenian language

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmiːniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɑːˈmiːniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Indo-European language spoken primarily in Armenia and also by communities in surrounding regions.
the main language of Armenia

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Armenian language.
The Armenian language uses its own unique script, distinct from other alphabets.
Many cultural traditions are preserved through the Armenian language spoken at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Armenian - of or relating to Armenia, its people, or the language

Etymology

From Armenian հայերեն (hayeren), referring to the Armenian language.
