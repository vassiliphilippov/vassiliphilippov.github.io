tug of war

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a contest in which two teams pull on opposite ends of a rope, each trying to drag the other across a central line
contest where teams pull on a rope

Examples

Every year, the school organizes a tug of war as part of Sports Day.
A heated tug of war broke out between the rival teams at the festival.
The scouts enjoyed a lively tug of war activity at camp.

Synset Examples

The kids played a tug of war during the picnic.
The tug of war competition was the highlight of the event.
There was a friendly tug of war between the teams.
