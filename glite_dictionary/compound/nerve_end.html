nerve end

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv ɛnd / (AmE), / nɜːv end / (BrE)
the terminal part of a nerve fiber, especially where it does not connect at a synapse, involved in transmitting nerve signals.
terminal part of a nerve fiber

Examples

The scientist studied the nerve end under a microscope to better understand how signals are transmitted.
Damage to a nerve end can affect the sensation in that part of the body.
Each nerve end plays a crucial role in the communication between the nervous system and tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axon terminal - the end of an axon, also called a synaptic bouton
