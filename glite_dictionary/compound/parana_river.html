Parana River

1. PROPN

/ pɑːˈrɑːnə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / pəˈrɑːnə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A major river in South America that flows through Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina, and is a tributary of the Rio de la Plata.
South American river, Rio de la Plata tributary

Examples

The Parana River is crucial for transportation and trade in several South American countries.
Flooding along the Parana River has historically affected cities in Argentina.
The biodiversity of the Parana River attracts many researchers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Spanish 'río Paraná', likely from Guaraní meaning 'relative of the sea'.
