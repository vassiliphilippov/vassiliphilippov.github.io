Holy Eucharist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈjuːkərɪst / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈjuːkərɪst / (BrE)
the Christian sacrament in which bread and wine are consecrated and consumed in remembrance of the Last Supper
Christian sacrament with bread and wine

Examples

Many Christians receive the Holy Eucharist every Sunday during the church service.
At the heart of the Mass is the celebration of the Holy Eucharist.
Children prepare for their First Holy Eucharist as an important milestone in their faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Communion - the Christian ritual of sharing consecrated bread and wine
  • Synonym: Mass - the Roman Catholic celebration of the Eucharist
  • Antonym: baptism - the Christian sacrament of initiation and purification

Etymology

From Greek 'eucharistia' (thanksgiving), adopted into Church Latin as 'eucharistia'.
