heat of sublimation

1. NOUN

/ hit əv səˌblɪˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv ˌsʌblɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quantity of heat absorbed by a unit mass of a substance as it changes directly from solid to vapor without passing through the liquid phase
heat required for solid to gas change

Examples

The heat of sublimation for dry ice is much higher than for water ice.
Scientists measured the heat of sublimation required to turn iodine crystals into vapor.
Accurate calculation of heat of sublimation is essential in chemical thermodynamics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latent heat - heat absorbed or released during a phase change without temperature change
  • Degree Lower: heat of fusion - heat required to change a substance from solid to liquid
  • Degree Higher: heat of vaporization - heat required to change a substance from liquid to gas
