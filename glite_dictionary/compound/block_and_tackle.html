block and tackle

1. NOUN

/ blɑk ənd ˈtækəl / (AmE), / blɒk ənd ˈtækəl / (BrE)
A system of one or more pulleys with ropes or cables, used to lift or move heavy objects.
system of pulleys and ropes for lifting

Examples

The sailors used a block and tackle to hoist the cargo onto the ship.
A block and tackle can make it much easier to lift heavy loads.
He demonstrated how a block and tackle works in the physics classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulley - A wheel on an axle designed to support movement of a cable or belt along its circumference.

2. NOUN

/ blɑk ənd ˈtækəl / (AmE), / blɒk ənd ˈtækəl / (BrE)
a system of ropes and pulleys used for lifting or moving heavy objects
mechanical lifting device of ropes and pulleys

Examples

The workers set up a block and tackle to raise the steel beam safely.
You should use a block and tackle for lifting such a large engine.
He demonstrated how to rig a block and tackle during the workshop.

Synset Examples

The sailors relied on a block and tackle to hoist the sails.
To lift the heavy load, we used a block and tackle.
He learned how to operate the block and tackle system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulley - a wheel on an axle used to support movement and change of direction of a cable or belt

Etymology

From 'block' (pulley or sheave) and 'tackle' (gear or equipment) used together in lifting mechanisms.
