to splutter in disbelief

1. VERB

/ ˈsplʌtɚ ɪn dɪsˈbliːf / (AmE), / ˈsplʌtə ɪn dɪsˈbliːf / (BrE)
to speak in a confused or shocked way because of surprise or disbelief
speak in shock or surprise

Examples

He could only splutter in disbelief when he was accused of cheating.
Many people splutter in disbelief when told the astonishing results of the experiment.
She spluttered in disbelief at the outrageous price of the tickets.

Synset Examples

She would splutter in disbelief at the unexpected news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splutter - make a series of short, explosive, spitting sounds when speaking
