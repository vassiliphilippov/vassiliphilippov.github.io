Illinois language

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪlɪˈnɔɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˈnɔɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Illinois and Miami peoples.
Algonquian language of Illinois and Miami peoples

Examples

Linguists have been working to revive the Illinois language through educational programs.
Historical documents include some prayers written in the Illinois language.
The Miami and the Illinois tribes once communicated using the Illinois language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Miami-Illinois language - a dialect continuum including Illinois and Miami varieties
  • Degree Higher: Algonquian language - a family of Native American languages that includes Illinois

Etymology

Named after the Illinois people; 'Illinois' from French, likely from Algonquian.
