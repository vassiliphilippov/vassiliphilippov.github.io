American black bear

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈblæk ˌbɛɹ / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈblæk ˌbeə / (BrE)
A medium-sized North American bear (Ursus americanus), usually with black or dark brown fur, smaller and less aggressive than the brown bear.
species of North American bear

Examples

The American black bear is commonly found in forests across much of Canada and the United States.
Despite its name, the American black bear can also have brown or cinnamon-colored fur.
We spotted an American black bear searching for berries near the edge of the woods.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brown bear - a large bear species (Ursus arctos), larger and more aggressive than the American black bear
