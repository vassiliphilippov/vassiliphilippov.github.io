biological defense

1. NOUN

/ baɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
measures and procedures taken to protect against attacks or threats involving biological agents, such as bacteria, viruses, or toxins
measures to protect against biological attacks
medical

Examples

The government has invested heavily in biological defense to prepare for potential outbreaks and attacks.
Hospitals play a critical role in the nation's biological defense strategy.
Research into vaccines is an essential component of biological defense programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biodefense - defensive measures against biological threats or agents
