Treasury bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri bɒnd / (BrE)
a government debt security with a maturity of 10 years or more, issued by the U.S. Department of the Treasury
U.S. government security (10+ years)

Examples

Many investors consider Treasury bonds a safe long-term investment.
The U.S. government issues Treasury bonds to help finance its operations.
Interest rates on Treasury bonds can influence mortgage rates and other financial products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government bond - a bond issued by a national government

Etymology

From 'treasury' (referring to the U.S. Treasury) and 'bond' (a financial instrument representing a loan).
