red-backed lemming

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˈlɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˈlɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
a small rodent of the genus Myopus or Lemmus, native to northern Eurasia and North America, characterized by a reddish-brown stripe along its back
lemming with a reddish-brown back

Examples

The red-backed lemming inhabits the tundra regions across northern Europe and Asia.
During harsh winters, the population of red-backed lemmings often fluctuates dramatically.
Predators such as owls regularly hunt the red-backed lemming for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - a small, short-tailed rodent known for population cycles
