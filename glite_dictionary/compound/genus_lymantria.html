genus Lymantria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs lɪˈmæn.tri.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs lɪˈman.trɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Lymantriidae, known for species that are serious pests of trees, such as the gypsy moth.
genus of moths, includes gypsy moth

Examples

The genus Lymantria includes several species known for defoliating forests worldwide.
Entomologists monitor populations of genus Lymantria to prevent outbreaks that might damage local ecosystems.
The gypsy moth, a member of the genus Lymantria, has become an invasive pest in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsy moth - a moth species (Lymantria dispar) that is a well-known pest

Etymology

From New Latin; Lymantria from Greek lymanein (to destroy), referring to its pest species.
