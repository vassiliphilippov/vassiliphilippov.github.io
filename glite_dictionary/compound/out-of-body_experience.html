out-of-body experience

1. NOUN

/ ˌaʊt əv ˈbɑdi ɪkˌspɪriəns / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ˈbɒdi ɪkˌspɪəriəns / (BrE)
an experience in which a person feels as if they are seeing their body from a location outside of it, often described as a dissociative or supernatural event
the sensation of observing oneself from outside the body

Examples

Some people report having an out-of-body experience during moments of extreme stress or trauma.
The documentary featured individuals describing the sensations of their out-of-body experience in vivid detail.
Scientists are studying the neurological mechanisms that might explain the phenomenon of the out-of-body experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: near-death experience - an experience reported by people who have been close to death, often involving a sense of detachment from the body
  • Synonym: dissociation - a psychological experience of feeling detached from one's body or self
