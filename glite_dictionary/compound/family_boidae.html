family Boidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈboʊɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˈbəʊɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of snakes that includes boas and pythons, known for their constricting method of subduing prey.
snake family: boas and pythons

Examples

The family Boidae is characterized by non-venomous snakes that kill their prey by constriction.
Pythons and boas, both in the family Boidae, have similar physical traits but inhabit different continents.
Scientists have long studied the evolution of the family Boidae to understand snake diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boa - large constricting snake found in the Americas
  • Synonym: python - large constricting snake found in Africa, Asia, and Australia

Etymology

From New Latin 'Boidae', from Latin 'boa' (type genus) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
