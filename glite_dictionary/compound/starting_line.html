starting line

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrtɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
a marked line indicating where participants begin a race or game
line marking the start of a race or game

Examples

The runners gathered at the starting line, waiting for the race to begin.
A false start was called, and everyone had to return to the starting line.
Spectators cheered as the cyclists took their positions at the starting line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finish line - line marking the end of a race
