East Anglia

1. PROPN

/ iːst ˈæŋɡliə / (AmE), / iːst ˈæŋɡlɪə / (BrE)
a region in eastern England, historically a kingdom, now comprising Norfolk, Suffolk, and parts of Cambridgeshire and Essex
region of eastern England

Examples

She grew up in East Anglia, surrounded by miles of unspoiled countryside.
The history of East Anglia dates back to the Anglo-Saxon period.
Many bird species migrate to the wetlands of East Anglia each year.

Etymology

From 'east' and Latin 'Anglia' (England), referring to the land settled by the Angles.
