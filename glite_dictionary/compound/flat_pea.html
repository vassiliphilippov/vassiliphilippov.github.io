flat pea

1. NOUN

/ flæt pi / (AmE), / flæt piː / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to Australia and Tasmania, with nearly heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow, pea-like flowers, followed by flat, winged seed pods
Australian shrub with yellow pea-like flowers

Examples

The flat pea thrives in well-drained soils across southern Australia.
Many gardens use the flat pea for its bright yellow blooms and attractive foliage.
After flowering, the flat pea produces distinctive flat seed pods that are easy to spot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - the common edible seed of the legume Pisum sativum

2. NOUN

/ flæt pi / (AmE), / flæt piː / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with purple and pink mottled flowers, sometimes grown for fodder or as green manure.
European perennial plant with mottled flowers

Examples

The farmer decided to plant flat pea as a cover crop to improve the soil.
In the wildflower meadow, the flat pea added a splash of purple and pink color.
Some livestock farmers grow flat pea for use as nutritious fodder for their animals.
