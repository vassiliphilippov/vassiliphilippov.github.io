toxin antitoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪn ˌæntiəˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪn ˌæntiəˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a mixture of a toxin and its corresponding antitoxin used to immunize against a specific disease
mixture for immunization against disease
medical

Examples

The patient received a toxin antitoxin injection to prevent the onset of the disease.
Researchers are developing new forms of toxin antitoxin for improved vaccine safety.
Doctors administer the toxin antitoxin as part of an emergency immunization protocol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccine - a substance used to stimulate the production of antibodies and provide immunity
