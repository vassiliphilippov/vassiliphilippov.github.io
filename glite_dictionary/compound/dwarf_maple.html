dwarf maple

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈmeɪ.pəl / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈmeɪ.pəl / (BrE)
A small species of maple native to northwestern North America, characterized by its short stature.
small maple tree of northwestern North America

Examples

The dwarf maple adds beautiful color to the rocky hillsides each autumn.
Gardeners often choose the dwarf maple for its compact size and brilliant foliage.
In the wild, the dwarf maple grows well in thin, stony soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - a tree or shrub with lobed leaves and winged seeds
