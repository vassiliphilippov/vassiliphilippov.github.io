investors club

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstərz klʌb / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstəz klʌb / (BrE)
a group of individuals who pool their money to invest in securities collectively and share the profits or losses
club for joint small-scale investing

Examples

The members of the investors club meet monthly to discuss potential stocks to purchase.
She joined an investors club to learn more about investing and grow her savings with others.
The investors club voted to sell its shares after reviewing the latest market trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investment club - group where people pool funds to invest together
