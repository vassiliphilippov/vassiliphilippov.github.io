Bible basher

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪbəl ˌbæʃər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪbəl ˌbæʃə / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who aggressively promotes or attempts to impose their religious beliefs on others, especially Christianity
a person who aggressively promotes religion
informal british

Examples

Some people perceive him as a Bible basher because of his outspoken religious opinions.
During the discussion, she tried not to sound like a Bible basher, but her passion was clear.
While he rejects the label, many consider him a Bible basher due to his persistent preaching.

Synset Examples

He always comes off as a Bible basher during debates.
People often label him a Bible basher for his strong beliefs.
She’s not a Bible basher, just passionate about her faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealot - a person who is fanatical and uncompromising in pursuit of their religious ideals
  • Antonym: agnostic - a person who believes that nothing is known or can be known of the existence or nature of God

Etymology

From 'Bible' (the Christian scriptures) and 'basher' (one who attacks or criticizes).
