clary sage

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæri ˌseɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈklæri ˌseɪdʒ / (BrE)
a strong-scented Mediterranean herb with clusters of white, pink, or violet flowers, used for its aromatic oil in flavoring and perfumery
Mediterranean herb used for aromatic oil

Examples

Many essential oils, including clary sage, are prized in aromatherapy for their distinctive scents.
The chef added a touch of clary sage to the sauce for a unique flavor.
Perfumes often use clary sage oil as a key ingredient for its earthy aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - an aromatic plant used as a herb

Etymology

From Latin 'sclarea', referring to the species Salvia sclarea.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɛri ˌseɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkleəri ˌseɪdʒ / (BrE)
An aromatic herb (Salvia sclarea) with large, hairy leaves and bluish or white flowers, often used for flavoring food and in perfumery.
aromatic herb used in cooking and perfumery

Examples

Fresh clary sage leaves add a unique flavor to omelets and fritters.
The chef garnished the lamb dish with chopped clary sage.
Many herbal tea blends include dried clary sage for its aroma and taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - another aromatic herb, Salvia officinalis, used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'clārus' (clear), referring to its use for treating eye problems, and 'sage' (the Salvia genus).
