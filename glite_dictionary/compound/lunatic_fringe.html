lunatic fringe

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːnətɪk frɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈluːnətɪk frɪndʒ / (BrE)
the members of a political or social group who hold extreme or fanatical views, often seen as outside the mainstream
members with extreme or fanatical views

Examples

Some members of the party worry that the lunatic fringe will damage their public image.
The protest attracted a lunatic fringe of activists with radical demands.
While most attendees voiced moderate opinions, the lunatic fringe called for a complete government overhaul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremist - a person with extreme political or religious views
  • Antonym: mainstream - the central or conventional members of a group

Etymology

Coined by US President Theodore Roosevelt in 1913, using 'lunatic' (from Latin lunaticus, 'moonstruck') and 'fringe' (meaning 'outer edge').
