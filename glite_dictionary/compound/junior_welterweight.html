junior welterweight

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːniər ˈwɛltərˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːniə ˈwɛltəˌweɪt / (BrE)
a boxer in a weight division with an upper limit of 140 pounds (63.5 kilograms)
boxer in the 140-pound weight class

Examples

He is training to compete as a junior welterweight in the upcoming championship.
Several famous junior welterweights have gone on to win world titles in higher divisions.
The junior welterweight division often produces fast-paced and exciting boxing matches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: super lightweight - alternative name for the same boxing weight class
