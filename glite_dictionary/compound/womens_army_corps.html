Women's Army Corps

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪmɪnz ˈɑrmi kɔr / (AmE), / ˈwɪmɪnz ˈɑːmi kɔː / (BrE)
a former branch of the United States Army, established during World War II, composed of women soldiers
former women's branch of U.S. Army (WWII)
american

Examples

Many women enlisted in the Women's Army Corps during World War II to serve their country.
The Women's Army Corps played a vital role in support positions throughout the army.
