paddle-wheeler

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædəl ˌwiːlər / (AmE), / ˈpædəl ˌwiːlə / (BrE)
a steam-powered vessel propelled by large, rotating paddle wheels, typically used on rivers or lakes
steam vessel with paddle wheels

Examples

The Mississippi River was once crowded with paddle-wheelers carrying passengers and cargo up and down the waterway.
Tourists can take a trip on a restored paddle-wheeler for a scenic cruise along the lake.
The distinctive rhythm of the paddle-wheeler could be heard long before it appeared around the bend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steamboat - a boat propelled by a steam engine, especially one used on rivers
  • Synonym: steamship - a ship propelled by steam engines
