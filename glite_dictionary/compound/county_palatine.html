county palatine

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊnti pælˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˌkaʊnti pælˈteɪn / (BrE)
a county in England or Ireland where certain feudal lords had royal privileges and powers, historically ruled by a count or earl palatine.
county with special royal privileges
legal british

Examples

The Duchy of Lancaster is an example of a county palatine in English history.
In a county palatine, the earl exercised many of the powers usually reserved for the monarch.
Legal documents from the Middle Ages often refer to ‘the liberties of the county palatine’ and their unique status.

Etymology

From Latin 'palatinus' (of the palace), referring to the authority of rulers with royal powers in certain counties.
