plantar wart

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɑr wɔrt / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tɑː wɔːt / (BrE)
A wart that appears on the sole of the foot, often caused by a viral infection and sometimes painful when walking.
wart on the sole of the foot
medical

Examples

He visited the doctor to have a plantar wart removed from his heel.
Plantar warts can be painful when pressure is applied to the foot.
Walking was difficult because the plantar wart on her foot kept rubbing against her shoe.

Synset Examples

pressure causes plantar warts to develop a painful callus around the soft center

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wart - a small, hard, benign growth on the skin caused by a virus

Etymology

From Latin 'plantaris' (of the sole of the foot) and 'wart'.
