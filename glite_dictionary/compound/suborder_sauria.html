suborder Sauria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌɔrdər ˈsɔriə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːdə ˈsɔːriə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of reptiles that includes the true lizards, such as chameleons and geckos.
taxonomic group of true lizards

Examples

The suborder Sauria includes a wide variety of lizards, from geckos to iguanas.
Many species in the suborder Sauria can regenerate their tails when threatened.
Chameleons, well-known for their adaptive camouflage, are members of the suborder Sauria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - any of various reptiles of the order Squamata, typically with four legs and an external ear opening

Etymology

From Latin 'sauria', from Greek 'sauros' meaning 'lizard'.
