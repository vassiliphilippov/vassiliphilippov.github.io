Yellow River

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
the second-longest river in China, flowing through northern China eastward to the Yellow Sea and known for its yellow silt
major river in northern China

Examples

The Yellow River is often called the cradle of Chinese civilization.
Severe flooding has historically occurred along the Yellow River due to the accumulation of silt.
The silt carried by the Yellow River gives the water its distinctive color.
