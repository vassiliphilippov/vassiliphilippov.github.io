house-to-house

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaʊs tə ˈhaʊs / (AmE), / ˌhaʊs tə ˈhaʊs / (BrE)
involving visiting or covering every house in a street, area, or neighborhood one after another, usually as part of a search, canvassing, or information campaign
visiting every house in turn

Examples

The firefighters conducted a house-to-house search looking for survivors after the storm.
Volunteers are organizing a house-to-house survey to collect information about the neighborhood.
The political party launched a house-to-house canvassing effort before the election.

Synset Examples

a door-to-door campaign
house-to-house coverage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: door-to-door - involving visits made to each house or flat in a given area
