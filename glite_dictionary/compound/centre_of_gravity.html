centre of gravity

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ əv ˈɡræv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə əv ˈɡræv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the point in a body or system where the force of gravity can be considered to act, and where the mass is evenly distributed in all directions
point where gravity acts on a body

Examples

The gymnast struggled to maintain her centre of gravity during the balance beam routine.
Engineers must consider the centre of gravity when designing stable vehicles.
A low centre of gravity helps race cars corner at high speeds without rolling over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center of mass - the point representing the mean position of the matter in a body

Etymology

From Latin 'centrum' (center) and 'gravitas' (weight, heaviness).
