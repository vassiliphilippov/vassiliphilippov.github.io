working class

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ klæs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ klɑːs / (BrE)
The social group consisting of people who do manual or industrial work, especially for hourly wages, rather than professional or managerial work.
social class performing wage-earning manual labor

Examples

The political party promised new policies to support the working class during the recession.
She grew up in a working class neighborhood where most families worked in local factories.
Many sociologists study how globalization has affected the traditional working class in industrial countries.

Synset Examples

there is a shortage of skilled labor in this field

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: middle class - the social group between the upper and working classes, including professionals and businesspeople
  • Degree Higher: upper class - the social group with the highest status in society
