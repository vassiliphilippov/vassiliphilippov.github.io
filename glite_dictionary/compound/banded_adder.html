banded adder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪd ˈædɚ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd ˈædə / (BrE)
a venomous krait, sluggish in movement, with distinctive black and yellow bands on its body
a venomous black and yellow banded krait

Examples

The banded adder is known for its striking black and yellow coloration.
Farmers in the region often spot the banded adder in the grass at dusk.
Despite its venom, the banded adder is generally sluggish and avoids confrontation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krait - a highly venomous Asian snake related to the cobra
