honeydew honey

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌdu ˈhʌni / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪdjuː ˈhʌni / (BrE)
A dark, strongly flavored honey produced by bees from honeydew excreted by plant-sucking insects, typically with a rich aroma and less sweetness than floral honey.
dark, strong honey from plant honeydew

Examples

The beekeeper offered samples of honeydew honey for customers to taste at the market.
Unlike floral honey, honeydew honey is prized for its deep, complex flavor.
Some people prefer honeydew honey on toast because it is less sweet but more aromatic.

Synset Examples

Honeydew honey is popular in some areas, but in other areas, beekeepers have difficulty selling the stronger-flavored product.

Etymology

Named after 'honeydew', the sweet substance secreted by insects like aphids, and 'honey'.
