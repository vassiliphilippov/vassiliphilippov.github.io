genus Secale

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkeɪliː / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkeɪliː / (BrE)
A genus of cereal grasses, widely cultivated for its grain, especially rye.
genus of grasses including rye

Examples

The cereal grain known as rye belongs to the genus Secale.
Botanists classify several wild grasses under the genus Secale.
Bread made from genus Secale is especially popular in Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rye - a cereal grass grown for its grain, especially used for bread and whiskey

Etymology

From Latin 'secale' (rye), used as the scientific name for the genus including rye.
