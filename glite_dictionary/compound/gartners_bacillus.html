Gartner's bacillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːrtnərz bəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːtnəz bəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
a bacterium of the genus Salmonella that causes gastroenteritis in humans
type of Salmonella bacterium
medical

Examples

Gartner's bacillus can be a cause of foodborne illness in humans, resulting in gastrointestinal symptoms.
Strict hygiene is essential to prevent infection by Gartner's bacillus in food processing environments.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of Gartner's bacillus after testing the contaminated samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salmonella - a genus of bacteria causing food poisoning

Etymology

Named after the German physician Hermann Gartner (1850–1925), who studied this bacterium.
