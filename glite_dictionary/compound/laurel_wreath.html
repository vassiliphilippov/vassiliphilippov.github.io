laurel wreath

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəl riːθ / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl riːθ / (BrE)
A circular band made of laurel leaves, traditionally worn on the head as a symbol of victory or honor, especially in ancient Greece and Rome.
circular band of laurel leaves worn as a victory symbol

Examples

The Olympic champion was crowned with a laurel wreath during the closing ceremony.
In ancient Rome, generals who returned victorious received a laurel wreath as a mark of honor.
The statue depicted a figure wearing a laurel wreath to symbolize his achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreath - a circular arrangement of flowers or leaves, worn on the head or used as a decoration

Etymology

From Latin *laurus* (laurel) + Old English *wrēth* (wreath).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔr.əl riːθ / (AmE), / ˈlɒr.əl riːθ / (BrE)
a circular garland made of laurel leaves, traditionally awarded to victors as a symbol of honor or achievement
a crown of laurel leaves given as an honor

Examples

The Olympic victor was crowned with a laurel wreath during the ceremony.
In ancient Rome, a laurel wreath symbolized success and high honor.
The poet wore a laurel wreath to mark his literary achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown - a traditional head ornament given as a sign of victory or honor
  • Synonym: medal - a metal object awarded for achievement

Etymology

From Latin 'laurus' (laurel) + English 'wreath'; used in ancient Greece and Rome as a symbol of victory.
