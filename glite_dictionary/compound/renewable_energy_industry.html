renewable energy industry

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈnuːəbl ˈɛnərdʒi ˈɪndəstri / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəbl ˈɛnədʒi ˈɪndəstri / (BrE)
The group of businesses and organizations engaged in producing, distributing, and selling energy derived from renewable sources such as solar, wind, hydro, and biomass.
industry involved in renewable energy production and sale

Examples

The renewable energy industry has seen rapid growth due to increased government investment in clean technologies.
Several universities now offer specialized degrees for students aiming to join the renewable energy industry.
Job opportunities in the renewable energy industry are expanding as demand for sustainable solutions rises.

Synset Examples

The early 21st century was a very productive time for the renewable energy industry, since many governments set long term renewable energy targets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: energy industry - the collective sector dealing with energy production and distribution
