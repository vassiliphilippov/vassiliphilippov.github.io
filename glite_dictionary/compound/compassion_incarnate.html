compassion incarnate

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpæʃən ɪnˈkɑːrnət / (AmE), / kəmˈpæʃən ɪnˈkɑːnət / (BrE)
The embodiment or personification of compassion in a person, thing, or action.
the embodiment of compassion

Examples

The nurse was compassion incarnate, comforting every patient with genuine care and empathy.
They described their teacher as compassion incarnate, always looking out for those in need.
For many, Mother Teresa represented compassion incarnate in the modern world.

Synset Examples

His kindness felt like compassion incarnate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodiment - a tangible or visible form of an idea, quality, or feeling

Etymology

From Latin 'compassio' (suffering with) and 'incarnatus' (made flesh).
