first clue

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˌkluː / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌkluː / (BrE)
the initial hint, sign, or indication that helps someone realize or discover something
the initial hint or sign

Examples

Her nervous laugh was the first clue that something was wrong.
The first clue in the treasure hunt led us to the old oak tree.
When he admitted he didn't know her name, that was my first clue he was lying.

Synset Examples

When he smiled, I knew; what was your first clue?
She looked guilty; what was your first clue?
I found the missing keys; what was your first clue?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clue - a fact or object that helps to solve a problem or mystery
