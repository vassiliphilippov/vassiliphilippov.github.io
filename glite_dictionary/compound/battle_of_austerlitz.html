Battle of Austerlitz

1. PROPN

/ ˈbætəl əv ˈaʊstərlɪts / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈaʊstəlɪts / (BrE)
A major battle in 1805 during the Napoleonic Wars in which Napoleon's French forces decisively defeated Russian and Austrian armies.
decisive 1805 Napoleonic battle

Examples

The Battle of Austerlitz is often regarded as Napoleon's greatest military achievement.
History students studied the strategies used during the Battle of Austerlitz.
Commemorations are held each year near the site of the Battle of Austerlitz.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Battle of Waterloo - Napoleon's final defeat in 1815

Etymology

Named after Austerlitz (now Slavkov u Brna, Czech Republic), near where the battle took place.
