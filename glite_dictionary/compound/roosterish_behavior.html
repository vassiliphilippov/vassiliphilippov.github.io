roosterish behavior

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːstərɪʃ bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / ˈruːstərɪʃ bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
brash, boastful, or aggressively self-assured behavior, similar to that displayed by a rooster.
brash or boastful behavior

Examples

His roosterish behavior annoyed everyone at the party.
She found his roosterish behavior both amusing and irritating during the meeting.
Their manager's roosterish behavior often overshadowed his genuine accomplishments.

Synset Examples

His roosterish behavior annoyed everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocky - confident in an annoying or arrogant way
  • Antonym: modest behavior - humble or unassuming behavior

Etymology

From 'rooster' (male chicken) and '-ish' (having the qualities of), referring to the strutting or boastful nature of a rooster.
