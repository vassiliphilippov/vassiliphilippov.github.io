Catholic school

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæθəlɪk skuːl / (AmE), / ˈkæθəlɪk skuːl / (BrE)
A school operated by the Catholic Church, typically offering both religious and general education.
school run by the Catholic Church

Examples

Many parents choose a Catholic school for its strong academic and moral environment.
She attended a Catholic school throughout her childhood.
A new Catholic school is opening in the downtown area next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parochial school - a school supported by a religious parish; often Catholic
