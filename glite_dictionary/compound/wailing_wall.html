Wailing Wall

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlɪŋ wɑl / (AmE), / ˈweɪlɪŋ wɔːl / (BrE)
a wall in Jerusalem considered sacred by Jews, believed to be the last remnant of the Second Temple, and used as a site for prayer and lamentation
sacred Jewish prayer site in Jerusalem

Examples

Many people gather at the Wailing Wall in Jerusalem to pray and reflect.
Tourists are often moved by the sight of worshippers at the Wailing Wall.
He placed a written prayer into the cracks of the Wailing Wall.

Etymology

English translation of Hebrew 'HaKotel HaMa'aravi' meaning 'the Western Wall;' the name 'Wailing Wall' refers to Jewish mourners praying at the site.
