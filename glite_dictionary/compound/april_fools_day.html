April Fools' Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪprəl ˈfuːlz ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈeɪprɪl ˈfuːlz deɪ / (BrE)
The first day of April, celebrated in many countries by playing practical jokes and tricks on people.
prank day, 1 April

Examples

Every year, our office organizes harmless pranks for April Fools' Day.
Newspapers sometimes publish fake stories on April Fools' Day to trick their readers.
I nearly believed his wild story until I remembered it was April Fools' Day.

Etymology

From the practice of making fools of people through jokes on the first of April; 'April' (the month) + 'fools' (those tricked) + 'day'.
