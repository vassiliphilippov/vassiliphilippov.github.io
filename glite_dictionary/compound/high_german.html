High German

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / haɪ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the standard form of the German language, developed historically from West Germanic dialects and used as the official language of Germany, Austria, and parts of Switzerland
standard form of the German language

Examples

She studied High German at university before traveling to Berlin.
The book is written in High German, making it accessible throughout Germany.
Many Swiss people speak both Swiss German and High German fluently.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Low German - West Germanic language variety spoken mainly in northern Germany and the Netherlands

Etymology

From German 'Hochdeutsch', meaning 'high German', referring to the dialects of the highland areas.
