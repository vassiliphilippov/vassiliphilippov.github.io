spoken language

1. NOUN

/ ˈspoʊkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspəʊkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
language expressed through speech rather than writing; communication by word of mouth.
language expressed in speech

Examples

Children often acquire their first spoken language by listening to their family and community.
Understanding a new spoken language can be challenging without exposure to native speakers.
The translator was skilled in converting the spoken language into written form.

Synset Examples

his speech was garbled
he uttered harsh language
he recorded the spoken language of the streets

Related Terms

  • Antonym: written language - language expressed in writing rather than speech
