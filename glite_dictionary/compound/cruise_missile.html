cruise missile

1. NOUN

/ kruːz ˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / kruːz ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
a self-propelled, unmanned weapon designed to deliver an explosive payload with high accuracy over long distances
self-propelled unmanned weapon

Examples

The military launched a cruise missile that struck the target with precision.
A cruise missile can travel hundreds of miles before hitting its destination.
Journalists reported that several cruise missiles were used during the attack.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ballistic missile - a missile powered only during the initial phase and following a ballistic trajectory
