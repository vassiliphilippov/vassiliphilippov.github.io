ring-tailed lemur

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋ teɪld ˈliːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ teɪld ˈliːmə / (BrE)
A small lemur native to Madagascar, easily recognized by its long tail with distinctive black and white rings.
lemur with a ringed tail

Examples

The ring-tailed lemur uses its striped tail for balance as it leaps between trees.
We watched a troop of ring-tailed lemurs sunbathe on the rocks at the zoo.
Conservationists are studying the social behavior of the ring-tailed lemur in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a type of primate native to Madagascar
