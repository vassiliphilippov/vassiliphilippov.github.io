brittle maidenhair

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪt̬l ˈmeɪdnˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtl ˈmeɪdnheə / (BrE)
A tropical American fern with broad pinnae, widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
tropical American fern with broad pinnae

Examples

The brittle maidenhair thrived in the greenhouse where humidity was kept high.
Botanists recommend the brittle maidenhair for its attractive, feathery leaves.
Gardens often feature the brittle maidenhair due to its lush, decorative foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a fern with delicate, fan-shaped leaf segments
