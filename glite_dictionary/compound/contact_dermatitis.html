contact dermatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtækt dɚˌmaɪˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tækt ˌdɜː.məˈtaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A skin inflammation resulting from direct contact with a specific allergen or irritant.
skin inflammation from contact with allergen or irritant
medical

Examples

After hiking through the woods, he developed contact dermatitis on his arms from poison ivy.
Wearing latex gloves can sometimes cause contact dermatitis in people with sensitive skin.
The doctor diagnosed her rash as contact dermatitis due to repeated exposure to cleaning chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eczema - a general term for inflamed, irritated skin

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) + -itis (inflammation) and Latin contactus (touch, contact).
