apocalyptic storm

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɑkəˈlɪptɪk stɔrm / (AmE), / əˌpɒkəˈlɪptɪk stɔːm / (BrE)
an extremely severe and catastrophic storm, often evoking the sense of total destruction or the end of the world
catastrophic, world-ending storm

Examples

The city was devastated by an apocalyptic storm that lasted for hours.
As the apocalyptic storm raged outside, families hurried to take cover in shelters.
News reports described the hurricane as an apocalyptic storm unlike anything seen in decades.

Synset Examples

The skies turned dark in an apocalyptic storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempest - a violent windy storm
  • Synonym: hurricane - a violent tropical cyclone
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain falling in very fine drops

Etymology

From 'apocalyptic', from Greek 'apokálypsis' (revelation), suggesting the end of the world, combined with 'storm'.
