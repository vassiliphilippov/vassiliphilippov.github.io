lady beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdi ˌbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a small, round, brightly colored and often spotted beetle that typically feeds on aphids and other insect pests; also known as a ladybug
small, spotted beetle that eats pests

Examples

A lady beetle landed on my hand while I was gardening.
Farmers often introduce lady beetles to control aphid populations in their fields.
Children are fascinated by the bright spots on a lady beetle's back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladybug - another name for a lady beetle, especially in American English
