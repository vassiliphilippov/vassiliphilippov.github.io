independent agency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛpəndənt ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛpəndənt ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
A government organization in the United States established by Congress that operates independently of the executive departments.
US government organization independent of executive departments

Examples

The Federal Communications Commission is an independent agency responsible for regulating interstate communications.
An independent agency was established to oversee consumer product safety in the United States.
Congress often creates an independent agency to address issues beyond the scope of executive departments.
