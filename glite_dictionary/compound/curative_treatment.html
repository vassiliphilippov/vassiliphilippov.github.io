curative treatment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊrətɪv ˈtritmənt / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərətɪv ˈtriːtmənt / (BrE)
a medical therapy or procedure intended to eliminate a disease or heal an illness, rather than just alleviate symptoms
treatment intended to cure disease
medical

Examples

The patient was offered a curative treatment to completely eradicate the infection.
Recent advances in medicine have made curative treatment possible for several types of cancer.
Many people travel abroad to seek curative treatment that is unavailable in their home country.

Synset Examples

They sought out curative treatments for their ailments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: palliative treatment - treatment focused on relieving symptoms rather than curing the disease
  • Synonym: remedy - a medicine or therapy that cures disease or relieves pain

Etymology

From Latin curare (to care for) and treatment (from Old French traiter, to treat).
