left field

1. NOUN

/ lɛft fiːld / (AmE), / lɛft fiːld / (BrE)
the area of the outfield in baseball or softball to the left of home plate when facing the field from the catcher's position
the left area of the outfield in baseball

Examples

He caught the fly ball in left field and threw it back to second base.
A strong hitter sent the ball soaring toward left field for a double.
Their best outfielder played left field all season and rarely made an error.

Synset Examples

the batter flied out to left

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right field - the area of the outfield to the right of home plate as seen from the catcher's position
  • Synonym: center field - the middle area of the outfield in baseball or softball

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛft ˌfild / (AmE), / ˈlɛft ˌfiːld / (BrE)
the area of the outfield in baseball that is to the left as viewed from home plate, or the position of the player who fields there
outfield position in baseball (left side)

Examples

He caught a high fly ball in left field during the second inning.
The coach moved her from center field to left field because of her strong arm.
A line drive went straight over left field and landed in the stands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right field - the outfield position to the right as viewed from home plate

3. NOUN

/ lɛft fiːld / (AmE), / lɛft fiːld / (BrE)
If something is described as 'left field', it is unexpected, unconventional, or surprising, often in an unusual or creative way.
unexpected or unconventional idea or action
informal

Examples

Her idea for the project was really left field, but it ended up being a huge success.
That comment came out of left field and surprised everyone in the room.
Sometimes the best solutions are the most left field ones.

Synset Examples

His response caught me off guard, it was so left field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off the wall - strange or very unconventional
  • Synonym: out of the blue - unexpectedly

Etymology

From baseball, where left field refers to the area of the outfield to the left of the batter, metaphorically extended to mean odd or unexpected.
