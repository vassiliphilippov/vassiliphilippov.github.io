Wrangell-St. Elias National Park

1. PROPN

/ ˈræŋɡəl ˈsaɪnt ˈiːliəs ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑrk / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡəl ˈsaɪnt ˈiːliəs ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑːk / (BrE)
the largest national park in the United States, located in southeastern Alaska, known for its vast wilderness, mountains, and glaciers
largest national park in the US, in Alaska

Examples

Many visitors come to Wrangell-St. Elias National Park to experience its dramatic glaciers and remote landscapes.
Hiking in Wrangell-St. Elias National Park offers breathtaking views of untouched wilderness.
Wildlife such as bears and moose thrive within the boundaries of Wrangell-St. Elias National Park.

Etymology

Named for Mount Wrangell, Baron Ferdinand von Wrangel, and Mount St. Elias.
