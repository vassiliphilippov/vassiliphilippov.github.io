Douglas sea scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌɡləs si skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs si skeɪl / (BrE)
a numerical scale used to measure and describe the height of sea waves, typically from 0 (calm) to 9 (phenomenal)
scale for measuring sea wave heights

Examples

The captain consulted the Douglas sea scale to estimate wave heights during the storm.
According to the Douglas sea scale, the sea state was classified as rough.
Mariners use the Douglas sea scale to report current sea conditions accurately.

Etymology

Named after Captain H.P. Douglas, who introduced the scale in the 1920s.
