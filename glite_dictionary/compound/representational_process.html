representational process

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃənl ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃənəl ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
any basic cognitive process in which something stands for or represents something else, particularly in the mind.
basic mental process of representation

Examples

The ability to imagine a tree when it is not present is a key example of a representational process.
Language relies on the representational process to let words stand for concepts.
In early childhood development, the representational process allows children to use symbols in play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: representation - the act of standing for or symbolizing something
