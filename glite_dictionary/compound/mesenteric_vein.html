mesenteric vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛs.ənˈter.ɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɛs.ənˈter.ɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the intestine and passes between the layers of the mesentery into the portal vein
vein draining blood from the intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully tied off the mesenteric vein to prevent excessive bleeding during the operation.
Blockage in a mesenteric vein can lead to serious complications such as intestinal ischemia.
Anatomy students traced the path of the mesenteric vein as it joined with the portal vein.

Etymology

From 'mesenteric', meaning relating to the mesentery (from Greek 'mesos' meaning 'middle' and 'enteron' meaning 'intestine').
