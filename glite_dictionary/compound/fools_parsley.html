fool's parsley

1. NOUN

/ fʊlz ˈpɑːrsli / (AmE), / fuːlz ˈpɑːsli / (BrE)
A poisonous European plant resembling parsley, now naturalized in America, which can cause nausea and poisoning if eaten.
poisonous parsley-like plant

Examples

The child mistakenly picked fool's parsley from the garden, thinking it was safe to eat.
Fool's parsley looks very similar to edible parsley but is toxic if ingested.
Farmers warn hikers to be cautious of fool's parsley in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: parsley - a herb with crinkly or flat leaves used in cooking
