cross infection

1. NOUN

/ krɔs ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection acquired by a patient in a hospital or healthcare facility from another person or source within that environment
hospital-acquired infection
medical

Examples

Strict hand hygiene helps to prevent cross infection in hospitals.
The patient developed a cross infection after being moved to the intensive care unit.
Hospital staff received training to reduce the risk of cross infection among vulnerable patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nosocomial infection - an infection acquired in a hospital setting

Etymology

From 'cross-' (indicating transfer) + 'infection'.
