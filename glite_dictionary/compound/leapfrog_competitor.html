to leapfrog competitor

1. VERB

/ ˈliːpˌfrɔɡ kəmˈpɛtɪtər / (AmE), / ˈliːpˌfrɒɡ kəmˈpɛtɪtə / (BrE)
to surpass or overtake one’s rivals, especially by taking advantage of sudden progress or innovation
to outpace a competitor by sudden progress

Examples

The new startup managed to leapfrog competitors by launching a revolutionary product.
Their unique approach helped them quickly leapfrog competitors in the crowded market.
By adopting advanced technology, she hopes to leapfrog competitors in her industry.

Synset Examples

The company aims to leapfrog competitors with innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overtake - to move ahead of someone or something in competition
  • Antonym: trail - to fall behind in a competition or race

Etymology

From the verb 'leapfrog', meaning to jump over, combined with 'competitor'
