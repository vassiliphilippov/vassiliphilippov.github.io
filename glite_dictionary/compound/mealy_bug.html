mealy bug

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːli bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈmiːli bʌɡ / (BrE)
A small, scalelike, plant-eating insect that is covered with a powdery, waxy secretion and is often destructive to plants, especially fruit trees.
plant-eating insect pest

Examples

The mealy bug infestation destroyed several rows of grapevines in the vineyard.
Gardeners often look for a white, cottony coating on plants to identify a mealy bug problem.
The farmer treated his citrus trees to prevent mealy bug damage during the growing season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale insect - a group of insects that includes mealy bugs, characterized by protective coatings and plant-feeding habits

Etymology

From 'mealy' (resembling meal or powder) + 'bug', from the insect's powdery appearance.
