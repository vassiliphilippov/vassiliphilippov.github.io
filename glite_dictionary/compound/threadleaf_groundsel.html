threadleaf groundsel

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛdˌlif ˈɡraʊndˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˈθrɛdliːf ˈɡraʊndsl̩ / (BrE)
A bluish-green, bushy plant with narrow leaves, covered in white wool, and bearing branched clusters of yellow flower heads, native to the southwestern United States and toxic to livestock.
a toxic, yellow-flowered southwestern US plant

Examples

Ranchers must keep their cattle away from fields of threadleaf groundsel due to its toxicity.
The distinctive yellow flowers of the threadleaf groundsel make it easy to spot in desert landscapes.
She studied the threadleaf groundsel during her research on poisonous plants of the southwest.

Etymology

From 'threadleaf', referring to the plant's narrow leaves, and 'groundsel', a common name for related plants in the genus Senecio.
