mathematical space

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl speɪs / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl speɪs / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A set of points that satisfies a specific set of postulates or axioms, often forming the basis of study in mathematical fields such as geometry, algebra, or analysis.
set of points defined by mathematical rules
medical

Examples

The concept of a mathematical space is fundamental in many areas of pure and applied mathematics.
A Hilbert space is one example of a mathematical space with additional structure.
Topology concerns the properties of mathematical spaces preserved under continuous transformations.
