gluteal artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːtiəl ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈɡluːtɪəl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
either of the two arteries branching from the internal iliac artery that supply blood to the hip joint and gluteal region
artery supplying the hip and buttock
medical

Examples

The superior gluteal artery is the largest branch of the internal iliac artery.
After the injury, blood flow from the gluteal artery was compromised, requiring surgery.
Knowledge of the gluteal artery's location is important for safe intramuscular injections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'glutaeus' (of the buttocks) and 'arteria' (artery).
