gravitational field

1. NOUN

/ ɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl fiːld / (AmE), / ɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl fiːld / (BrE)
the region of space surrounding a mass in which another mass experiences a force of gravity
region around a mass where gravity acts

Examples

Earth's gravitational field keeps the Moon in orbit around our planet.
Objects dropped from a height accelerate towards the ground due to the gravitational field.
Satellites must counteract the effects of Earth's gravitational field to maintain a stable orbit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic field - region around a magnet or current where magnetic force is exerted
  • Synonym: electric field - region around a charged particle where electric force is exerted

Etymology

From 'gravitational' (related to gravity) + 'field' (area of influence); based on Newtonian physics terminology.
