female circumcision

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːmeɪl ˌsɜːrkəmˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌfiːmeɪl ˌsɜːkəmˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
The removal of part or all of the external female genitalia, especially the clitoris, typically as a cultural or religious practice.
removal of female external genitalia (esp. clitoris)

Examples

The practice of female circumcision is widely condemned by international organizations.
Some cultures still perform female circumcision as part of traditional rituals.
Awareness campaigns aim to end female circumcision worldwide due to its health risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: female genital mutilation - partial or total removal of external female genitalia for non-medical reasons
