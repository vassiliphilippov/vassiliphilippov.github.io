silk screen print

1. NOUN

/ sɪlk skrin prɪnt / (AmE), / sɪlk skriːn prɪnt / (BrE)
A print created using a stencil method in which ink is pressed through a mesh screen onto the printing surface, except where blocked by a stencil.
print made by stencil on a mesh screen

Examples

The gallery is exhibiting a rare silk screen print of Marilyn Monroe by Andy Warhol.
She bought a colorful silk screen print to add to her art collection.
Each silk screen print is signed and numbered by the artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serigraph - A print made by the same screen printing process, especially in fine art

Etymology

From 'silk screen', referring to the original use of silk mesh for the printing screen.
