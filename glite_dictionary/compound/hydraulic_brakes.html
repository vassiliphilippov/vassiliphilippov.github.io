hydraulic brakes

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɔlɪk breɪks / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk breɪks / (BrE)
A type of brake system in which pressure from brake fluid, generated by a pedal-operated piston, is used to apply force to the brake pads or shoes.
brake system using fluid pressure

Examples

Most modern cars are equipped with hydraulic brakes for efficient and reliable stopping power.
A leak in the hydraulic brakes can cause the braking system to fail.
The mechanic explained how hydraulic brakes use fluid to transfer force from the pedal to the wheels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disc brakes - brakes that use calipers to squeeze pairs of pads against a disc

Etymology

From 'hydraulic', relating to liquid movement (Greek 'hydor' meaning water), and 'brake'.
