suede glove

1. NOUN

/ sweɪd ɡlʌv / (AmE), / sweɪd ɡlʌv / (BrE)
A glove made from soft, napped leather, typically from the underside of animal skin such as kidskin or lambskin.
glove made of soft, napped leather

Examples

He wore a stylish suede glove on his left hand for the winter party.
The suede glove was soft and gave her extra grip while driving.
After the rain, her favorite suede glove needed special cleaning to restore its texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leather glove - glove made from leather

Etymology

From French 'gants de Suède' (gloves from Sweden) referring to the leather; 'suede' from the French for Sweden.
