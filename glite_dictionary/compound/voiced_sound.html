voiced sound

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪst saʊnd / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪst saʊnd / (BrE)
a speech sound produced with vibration of the vocal cords
speech sound with vocal cord vibration

Examples

The letter 'b' is pronounced as a voiced sound in English.
Children sometimes confuse the voiced sound /z/ with the voiceless /s/.
In linguistics, a voiced sound is produced when the vocal cords vibrate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voiceless sound - a speech sound produced without vibration of the vocal cords
