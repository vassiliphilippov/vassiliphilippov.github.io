Assam fever

1. NOUN

/ əˌsæm ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / əˌsæm ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A form of visceral leishmaniasis, a serious parasitic disease affecting internal organs, especially the spleen and liver.
parasitic disease affecting internal organs
medical

Examples

The outbreak of Assam fever affected several villages across the region last year.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with Assam fever after a series of laboratory tests.
Preventive measures are essential to control the spread of Assam fever in endemic areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - disease caused by protozoan parasites, spread by sandflies

Etymology

Named after Assam, a region in India, where the disease was first observed.
