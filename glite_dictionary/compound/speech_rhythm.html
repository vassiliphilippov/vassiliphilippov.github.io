speech rhythm

1. NOUN

/ spiːtʃ ˈɹɪð.əm / (AmE), / spiːtʃ ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
the pattern of stressed and unstressed syllables in spoken language, contributing to its natural flow and timing
pattern of stress in spoken language

Examples

Children learning a new language often struggle to master speech rhythm at first.
Poets pay careful attention to speech rhythm to create music in their verse.
She noticed that his speech rhythm was slower than that of native speakers.

Synset Examples

the rhythm of Frost's poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosody - patterns of rhythm and sound used in poetry and language
