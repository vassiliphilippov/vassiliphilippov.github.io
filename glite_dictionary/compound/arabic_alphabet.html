Arabic alphabet

1. NOUN

/ əˈræbɪk ˈælfəˌbɛt / (AmE), / əˈræbɪk ˈæl.fə.bet / (BrE)
the alphabet of 28 letters, derived from Aramaic, used for writing the Arabic language and adapted for other languages such as Urdu
the alphabet used to write Arabic

Examples

The Quran is traditionally written in the Arabic alphabet.
Many street signs in Egypt are displayed in both the Roman and the Arabic alphabet.
Learning the Arabic alphabet is the first step to reading and writing in Arabic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Latin alphabet - the alphabet used for English and many other languages
  • Synonym: Arabic script - the writing system and style used for Arabic and related languages
