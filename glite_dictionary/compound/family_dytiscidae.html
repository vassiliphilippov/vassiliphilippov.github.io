family Dytiscidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli daɪˈtɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli daɪˈtɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic beetles, commonly known as diving beetles, found in freshwater habitats around the world.
family of aquatic diving beetles

Examples

The family Dytiscidae includes more than 4,000 species of swimming beetles worldwide.
Scientists discovered a new member of the family Dytiscidae in the Amazon basin.
Many insects in the family Dytiscidae are important predators in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diving beetle - a common name for beetles of the family Dytiscidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Dytiscidae', from 'Dytiscus', the type genus.
