lithium carbonate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθiəm ˈkɑːrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪθiəm ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white, crystalline powder (Li2CO3) used in the manufacture of glass and ceramics, and as a medication to treat bipolar disorder and certain types of depression
chemical compound used in industry and medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed lithium carbonate to help stabilize his mood swings.
Manufacturers use lithium carbonate in the production of specialty glass and ceramics.
Patients taking lithium carbonate must have their blood levels monitored regularly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lithium - a soft, silvery-white chemical element, symbol Li

Etymology

From lithium, named after its lithium content, and carbonate, derived from its carbonate ion.
