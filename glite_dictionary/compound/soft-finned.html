soft-finned

1. ADJ

/ sɔft fɪnd / (AmE), / sɒft fɪnd / (BrE)
(of fish) having fins composed of soft, flexible rays, rather than hard or spiny ones.
describing fish with soft, flexible fins

Examples

The soft-finned species are often easier to handle due to their flexible fins.
Scientists distinguish between soft-finned and spiny-finned fishes in taxonomy.
Many catfish are soft-finned and lack the hard spines found in other types.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spiny-finned - describing fish with fins that are hard or spiny rather than soft
