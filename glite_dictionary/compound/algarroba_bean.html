algarroba bean

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɡəˈroʊbə bin / (AmE), / ˌalɡəˈrəʊbə biːn / (BrE)
a long pod with edible sweet pulp and small beans, used as animal feed and as a source of a chocolate substitute
long pod with edible pulp, used in feed and as chocolate substitute

Examples

The algarroba bean is often ground into flour as a healthy chocolate alternative.
Farmers feed livestock with algarroba bean pods during the dry season.
Many Mediterranean sweets use the pulp from the algarroba bean for flavor and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob - pod with sweet pulp, used as a chocolate substitute

Etymology

From Spanish 'algarroba' (carob tree or pod), from Arabic 'al-ḥarrūba'.
