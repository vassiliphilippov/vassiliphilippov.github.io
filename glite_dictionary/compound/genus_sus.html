genus Sus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sʌs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sʌs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Suidae, which includes the domestic pig and wild boars
type genus of the pig family (Suidae)

Examples

The genus Sus comprises several species, including the well-known wild boar and domestic pig.
Scientists studied fossils to better understand the evolution of the genus Sus.
Farmers have domesticated animals from the genus Sus for thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sus - the genus that includes pigs and wild boars

Etymology

From Latin 'sus', meaning 'pig'.
