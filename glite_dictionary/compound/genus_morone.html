genus Morone

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs məˈroʊni / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məˈrəʊni / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous fishes found in both fresh and salt water, including several bass species.
a genus of carnivorous fishes

Examples

The genus Morone includes notable game fish such as the striped bass.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of species within the genus Morone.
Anglers often target members of the genus Morone for their fighting ability.

Etymology

From New Latin Morone, used in taxonomy for this group of fishes.
