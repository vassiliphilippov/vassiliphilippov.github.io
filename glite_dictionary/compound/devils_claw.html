devil's claw

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz klɔ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz klɔː / (BrE)
A plant native to the southern United States and Mexico, known for its large pale flowers mottled with purple and a long, curved seed pod resembling a claw.
plant with curved claw-like seed pod

Examples

The unusual seed pod of the devil's claw caught the attention of botanists visiting the desert.
In some cultures, the devil's claw is used for ornamental purposes or in traditional crafts.
After the rains, fields in Texas bloomed with rows of devil's claw flowers.
