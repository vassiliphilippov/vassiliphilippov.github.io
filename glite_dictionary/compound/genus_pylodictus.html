genus Pylodictus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs paɪˈlɒdɪktəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs paɪˈlɒdɪktəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater catfishes commonly known as flathead catfishes, native to North America.
genus of North American flathead catfishes

Examples

The genus Pylodictus includes the well-known flathead catfish found in North American rivers.
Ichthyologists study the genus Pylodictus to understand its ecological impact.
Specimens from the genus Pylodictus are popular among freshwater anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flathead catfish - a large North American freshwater catfish, member of Pylodictus

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'pylē' (gate) + 'diktus' (net), used in zoological naming.
