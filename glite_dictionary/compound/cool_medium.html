cool medium

1. NOUN

/ kul ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / kuːl ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
a form of communication that requires little audience participation or involvement, but offers substantial stimulation; typically applies to media such as television, cartoons, and speech
medium needing little participation

Examples

Television is often described as a cool medium because it requires viewers to fill in much of the content themselves.
Unlike radio, which is considered a 'hot medium,' cartoons are frequently thought of as a cool medium.
McLuhan argued that the cool medium engages the audience in a different way than more information-rich media.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hot medium - a medium that provides high definition and requires less audience participation, e.g. film or radio

Etymology

Coined by Marshall McLuhan in the 1960s from 'cool' (low engagement) and 'medium' (means of communication).
