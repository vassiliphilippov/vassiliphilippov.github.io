Easter egg

1. NOUN

/ ˈistɚ ɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈiːstə ɛɡ / (BrE)
An egg-shaped candy, often made of chocolate and decorated, traditionally given or eaten at Easter.
egg-shaped candy for Easter

Examples

She gave her children a colorful Easter egg filled with jelly beans.
Every spring, shops display an assortment of chocolate Easter eggs for the holiday.
The children searched the garden for hidden Easter eggs on Sunday morning.

2. NOUN

/ ˈistɚ ɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈiːstə ɛɡ / (BrE)
A hard-boiled egg colored and decorated as part of Easter celebrations.
decorated egg for Easter celebrations

Examples

Children ran through the garden searching for hidden Easter eggs.
Each Easter egg was painted a different bright color for the festivities.
The tradition of giving Easter eggs dates back centuries in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egg - an oval or round object laid by a female bird, reptile, fish, or invertebrate, usually containing a developing embryo

Etymology

From 'Easter', the Christian holiday, and 'egg'; the custom comes from ancient fertility symbols and Christian traditions.
