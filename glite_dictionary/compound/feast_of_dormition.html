Feast of Dormition

1. NOUN

/ fiːst əv dɔːrˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / fiːst əv dɔːˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a celebration in the Eastern Orthodox Church commemorating the Virgin Mary's being taken up into heaven at the end of her earthly life, observed on August 15th
Orthodox Christian festival marking Mary's assumption

Examples

The Feast of Dormition is one of the Twelve Great Feasts in the Orthodox Church calendar.
Many churches hold special services to celebrate the Feast of Dormition on August 15th.
Pilgrims gather in Jerusalem each year for the Feast of Dormition to honor the Virgin Mary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Assumption - Roman Catholic feast marking Mary's ascent into heaven

Etymology

From Latin 'dormitio' (falling asleep), referring to the Virgin Mary's death; 'feast' meaning a religious festival.
