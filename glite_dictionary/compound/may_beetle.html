May beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ ˌbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A large, brown, North American beetle that appears in late spring; its larvae feed on grass roots and other plants.
North American leaf-eating beetle found in late spring

Examples

Every spring, May beetles swarm around the porch lights in our neighborhood.
The larvae of the May beetle can cause significant damage to lawns by feeding on the roots.
Children used to collect May beetles in jars during the late spring evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: June bug - A similar large brown beetle, often appearing in June

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ ˌbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A large European beetle, destructive to vegetation as both larva and adult, commonly active in late spring.
a large destructive European beetle

Examples

The May beetle is known for causing significant damage to crops during late spring.
As larvae, May beetles feed on plant roots, making them a threat to agriculture.
We found a May beetle clinging to the leaves in the garden at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockchafer - a common European beetle destructive to vegetation, also called May bug
