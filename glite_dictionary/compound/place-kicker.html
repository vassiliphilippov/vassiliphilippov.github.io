place-kicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪsˌkɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsˌkɪkə / (BrE)
(in American and Canadian football) a player who specializes in kicking the ball from a stationary position, usually during field goal or extra point attempts.
football player who kicks for goal
american

Examples

The team's place-kicker scored three field goals in the game.
After practicing all summer, he finally became the starting place-kicker.
A reliable place-kicker can make a huge difference in close football matches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kicker - a player who kicks the ball in football

Etymology

From 'place kick' (a kick from a stationary position) + 'kicker'.
