wild China tree

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈtʃaɪnə tri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈtʃaɪnə triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States, with pulpy fruit containing saponin.
deciduous tree of southwestern US with pulpy fruit

Examples

The wild China tree is valued for its ability to thrive in arid regions.
Farmers sometimes plant the wild China tree as a windbreak on their land.
The pulpy fruit of the wild China tree contains natural saponins used in traditional cleaning methods.
