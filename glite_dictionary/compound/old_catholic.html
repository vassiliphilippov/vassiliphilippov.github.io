Old Catholic

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / əʊld ˈkæθlɪk / (BrE)
a member of a Christian denomination formed in the 19th century by Catholics, mainly in Germany and Switzerland, who rejected the doctrine of papal infallibility
member of a 19th-century Catholic reform church

Examples

Her grandfather was an Old Catholic who opposed papal infallibility.
The Old Catholic Church spread to several European countries after its founding.
Many Old Catholics maintain traditional liturgical practices distinct from Roman Catholicism.
