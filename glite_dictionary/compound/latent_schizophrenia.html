latent schizophrenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪtənt skɪˌfrɛniə / (AmE), / ˈleɪtənt skɪˌfriːnɪə / (BrE)
a form of schizophrenia in which mild symptoms are present or there is a predisposition to develop the disorder without severe manifestations
form of schizophrenia with mild or hidden symptoms
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist considered the possibility of latent schizophrenia after noticing subtle behavioral changes.
Some researchers argue that latent schizophrenia may progress to more typical forms of the disorder over time.
Early signs of latent schizophrenia can often be difficult to distinguish from normal personality traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizophrenia - a mental disorder characterized by delusions, hallucinations, and disordered thinking

Etymology

From Latin 'latens' (lying hidden) and 'schizophrenia', a psychiatric term coined from Greek 'schizo' (split) and 'phren' (mind).
