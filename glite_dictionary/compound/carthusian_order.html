Carthusian order

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈθuːʃən ˈɔːrdər / (AmE), / kɑːˈθjuːzɪən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic religious order known for its strict asceticism and contemplative life, founded by St. Bruno in 1084
Catholic religious order known for austerity

Examples

The Carthusian order is renowned for its members' commitment to silence and solitude.
She studied the history of the Carthusian order during her religious studies course.
Monks of the Carthusian order dedicate their lives to contemplation and prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Benedictine order - another major Catholic monastic order

Etymology

From Latin 'Cartusia,' the name of the Chartreuse mountains, location of the order's first monastery.
