Great Australian Bight

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ɔːˈstreɪljən baɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɔːˈstreɪlɪən baɪt / (BrE)
a wide, open bay of the Indian Ocean along the southern coast of Australia, known for its rough seas and storms
bay in southern Australia, Indian Ocean

Examples

The coastline of the Great Australian Bight is famous for its dramatic cliffs and marine life.
Sailors often avoid the Great Australian Bight due to its unpredictable and violent storms.
Whale watching is a popular activity along the Great Australian Bight during the migration season.

Etymology

From 'bight,' meaning a bend or curve in a shoreline, and the naming of Australia. 'Bight' is from Old English 'byht' (bend, angle).
