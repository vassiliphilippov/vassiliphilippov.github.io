Gestalt principle of organization

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈstɑlt ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ˌɔrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡəˈʃtɑːlt ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
any of several principles in Gestalt psychology that explain how people tend to group visual elements together into unified wholes or patterns based on factors like similarity, proximity, continuity, and closure.
principle in psychology explaining perceptual grouping

Examples

The Gestalt principle of organization helps explain why we perceive a series of dots as a single line.
Designers often use the Gestalt principles of organization to create visually appealing layouts.
Understanding the Gestalt principle of organization can improve how information is presented in educational materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gestalt - an organized whole perceived as more than the sum of its parts, from Gestalt psychology

Etymology

From German 'Gestalt' (shape, form) and principle (rule, law).
