visceral impact

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsərəl ˈɪmpækt / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl ˈɪmpakt / (BrE)
a strong emotional or instinctive effect, often felt in response to art, events, or experiences
emotional or instinctive effect

Examples

The documentary had a visceral impact that lingered with the audience long after it ended.
Her words carried a visceral impact, moving everyone in the room to tears.
The artist aimed for a visceral impact, hoping to stir deep emotions in viewers.

Synset Examples

The movie's violence left a visceral impact on viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotional impact - the effect something has on one's emotions

Etymology

From Latin visceralis (of the internal organs) + impact (from Latin impactus, pushed against).
