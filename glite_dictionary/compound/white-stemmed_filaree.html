white-stemmed filaree

1. NOUN

/ waɪt stɛmd fəˈlɛri / (AmE), / waɪt stɛmd fɪˈlɑːri / (BrE)
A low annual European herb, naturalized in America, similar to alfilaria, with white stems.
a European herb with white stems, naturalized in America

Examples

The field was dotted with white-stemmed filaree growing among the native grasses.
Researchers found that white-stemmed filaree adapts well to dry environments in western America.
Farmers often consider white-stemmed filaree a common weed in pasture lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alfilaria - a related species of herbaceous plant (Erodium cicutarium)
