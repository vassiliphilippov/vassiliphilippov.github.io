cane reed

1. NOUN

/ keɪn ɹid / (AmE), / keɪn ɹiːd / (BrE)
A tall grass native to the southern United States, often found growing in dense thickets.
tall grass of the southern US

Examples

The wetlands are dominated by thick stands of cane reed near the riverbank.
Local artisans use cane reed to weave baskets and mats.
Biologists are studying the decline of cane reed habitats in the southern states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - a tall, slender-leaved plant of the grass family
