southern lights

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn laɪts / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən laɪts / (BrE)
the aurora seen in the Southern Hemisphere, characterized by natural light displays in the sky near the South Pole
aurora in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

Many travelers dream of seeing the southern lights illuminate the Antarctic sky with vibrant colors.
Scientists study the southern lights to better understand solar activity and its impact on Earth's atmosphere.
Photographers often visit southern Australia or New Zealand to capture images of the southern lights.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northern lights - the aurora of the Northern Hemisphere
