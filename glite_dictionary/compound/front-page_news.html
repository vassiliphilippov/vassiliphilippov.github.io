front-page news

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌnt ˌpeɪdʒ ˈnuz / (AmE), / ˈfrʌnt ˌpeɪdʒ ˈnjuːz / (BrE)
news considered important or sensational enough to be featured prominently on the front page of a newspaper
news prominently featured in a newspaper

Examples

The politician's resignation was front-page news across the entire country.
Winning the championship made the local team front-page news for the first time.
Her heroic actions during the disaster quickly became front-page news.

Synset Examples

The scandal was front-page News of the Screws material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headline news - news of such importance that it is given a prominent headline
