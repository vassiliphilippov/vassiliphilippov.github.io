drive-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪv ɪn / (AmE), / ˈdraɪv ɪn / (BrE)
a place, such as a restaurant or cinema, designed to accommodate customers who remain in their cars while being served or while watching a show
place where service is given to people in cars

Examples

The family watched a movie at the old drive-in last weekend.
We grabbed burgers and milkshakes from a drive-in near the highway.
Many drive-in theaters have closed, but some are making a comeback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive-through - facility where customers receive service without leaving their cars, usually without stopping

Etymology

From 'drive' + 'in,' first used in the 1930s United States to describe roadside businesses for motorists.
