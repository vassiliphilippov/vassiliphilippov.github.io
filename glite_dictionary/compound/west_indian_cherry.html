West Indian cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɛst ˈɪndiən ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˌwest ˈɪndiən ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a tropical American shrub or small tree (Malpighia emarginata) bearing small, edible, acid red fruits that resemble cherries
tropical American shrub with cherry-like fruit

Examples

The juice of the West Indian cherry is high in vitamin C and is often used in health drinks.
Farmers in Brazil cultivate the West Indian cherry for its nutritional benefits and tart flavor.
She added fresh West Indian cherries to the salad for a burst of color and taste.

2. NOUN

/ wɛst ˈɪndiən ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / wɛst ˈɪndiən ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A small, red or yellow, cherry-like fruit of a tropical American shrub (Malpighia glabra), very rich in vitamin C.
tropical fruit high in vitamin C

Examples

The juice of the West Indian cherry is extremely high in vitamin C, making it popular in health supplements.
She planted a West Indian cherry tree in her backyard to enjoy the tart fruit.
Many jams and jellies in the Caribbean are made from the West Indian cherry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acerola - another common name for the West Indian cherry
