snake's head fritillary

1. NOUN

/ ˌsneɪks hɛd frɪˈtɪləri / (AmE), / ˌsneɪks hɛd frɪˈtɪləri / (BrE)
a Eurasian lily (Fritillaria meleagris) with pendant, checkered flowers, usually purple or maroon, widely grown as an ornamental plant
Eurasian lily with checkered flowers

Examples

The snake's head fritillary blooms each spring, displaying its distinctive checkered petals.
Gardeners often plant snake's head fritillaries to add color and interest to damp meadows.
A rare white variety of the snake's head fritillary was discovered growing in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fritillary - any plant of the genus Fritillaria

Etymology

The name refers to the flower's shape and markings resembling a snake's head; 'fritillary' from Latin 'fritillus' (dice box), referencing the checkered pattern.
