American cranberry bush

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈkrænˌbɛri bʊʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈkrænˌbɛri bʊʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous North American shrub or small tree with three-lobed leaves and clusters of red berries, commonly found in moist woods and thickets.
North American shrub with red berries

Examples

The American cranberry bush is popular in gardens for its attractive clusters of red fruit.
Wildlife often feed on the berries of the American cranberry bush in the late summer.
She planted an American cranberry bush along the edge of her backyard pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry bush - shrub with edible red berries
