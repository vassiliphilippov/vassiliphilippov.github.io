fresh seafood

1. NOUN

/ frɛʃ ˈsiːfuːd / (AmE), / frɛʃ ˈsiːfuːd / (BrE)
Seafood that is recently caught and has not been preserved by freezing, salting, or other methods.
recently caught, unpreserved seafood

Examples

Many restaurants along the coast serve fresh seafood caught that same morning.
She prefers buying fresh seafood from the local fish market rather than the supermarket.
During our vacation, we enjoyed platters of fresh seafood every night.

Synset Examples

The whelk stall at the market always has fresh seafood.
The food in Bari is famous for its fresh seafood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frozen seafood - seafood preserved by freezing
