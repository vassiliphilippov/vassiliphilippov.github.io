Babylonian tablet

1. NOUN

/ bəˌboʊˈloʊniən ˈtæblət / (AmE), / bəˌbəʊˈləʊniən ˈtæblət / (BrE)
an ancient clay tablet inscribed with cuneiform writing from the Babylonian civilization, often bearing records, laws, or literary texts
ancient clay artifact from Babylonian civilization

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a Babylonian tablet detailing ancient trade transactions.
The museum's exhibit features a rare Babylonian tablet dating back to 1700 BCE.
Students studied a Babylonian tablet to learn about early astronomical observations.

Synset Examples

The Babylonian tablets revealed insights into early human civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuneiform tablet - clay tablet inscribed with cuneiform script

Etymology

Babylonian, from Babylon (ancient city in Mesopotamia), and tablet (from Latin tabula, 'a flat piece for writing').
