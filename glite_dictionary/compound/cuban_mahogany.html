Cuban mahogany

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bən məˈhɑː.ɡəni / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bən məˈhɒɡ.əni / (BrE)
A species of mahogany tree native to the West Indies, valued for its hard, reddish-brown wood.
mahogany tree from the West Indies

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Cuban mahogany, giving it a rich, deep color.
Overharvesting has made Cuban mahogany increasingly rare in its native habitat.
Collectors prize antique desks made from genuine Cuban mahogany.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown wood from tropical trees
