water lobelia

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər loʊˈbiːliə / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ləʊˈbiːlɪə / (BrE)
An aquatic perennial plant with submerged spongy leaves and blue flowers, native to Europe and North America.
aquatic plant with blue flowers

Examples

We saw clusters of water lobelia blooming near the edge of the lake.
The pond's shallow areas are home to water lobelia and other aquatic plants.
Botanists study water lobelia for its unique submerged leaves and striking blue flowers.

Etymology

From 'lobelia', named after Belgian botanist Matthias de Lobel (1538–1616).
