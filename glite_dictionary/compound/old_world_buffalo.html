Old World buffalo

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld ˈbʌfəloʊ / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
Any of several species of large wild bovines native to Africa and Asia, such as the water buffalo or the Cape buffalo.
large African or Asian wild bovine (e.g., water or Cape buffalo)

Examples

The Old World buffalo includes the familiar water buffalo and the Cape buffalo of Africa.
Compared to the American bison, the Old World buffalo have distinct physical features and habitats.
Researchers study the population of Old World buffalo to better understand their role in local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water buffalo - large domesticated buffalo native to Asia, used as a draft animal
  • Synonym: Cape buffalo - large African wild buffalo (Syncerus caffer)
  • Antonym: American buffalo - common name for bison, native to North America
