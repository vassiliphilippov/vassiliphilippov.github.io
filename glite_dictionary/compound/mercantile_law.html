mercantile law

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜr.kən.taɪl lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.kən.taɪl lɔː / (BrE)
the branch of law governing commercial transactions and the practices of merchants and traders
body of rules for commercial transactions
legal

Examples

Many countries have updated their mercantile law to keep pace with the digital economy.
Disputes between businesses are often settled according to principles of mercantile law.
He specialized in mercantile law during his legal studies at university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercial law - law relating to commerce and trade

Etymology

From 'mercantile' (relating to merchants or trade), itself from Latin 'mercari' (to trade) and 'lex' (law).
