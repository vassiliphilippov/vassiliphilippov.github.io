Maksutov telescope

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːkˈsuːtʌv tɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˌmækˈsuːtɒv tɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
a type of reflecting telescope in which a meniscus lens is used to correct aberrations in the concave mirror, producing sharper images
reflecting telescope with a correcting meniscus lens

Examples

The Maksutov telescope is popular among amateur astronomers due to its compact design and sharp images.
She set up her Maksutov telescope to observe the planets during the clear night.
Many portable observatories use a Maksutov telescope for its ease of alignment and optical quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Schmidt telescope - a type of telescope that also uses a correcting lens to minimize distortions

Etymology

Named after Russian optician Dmitri Maksutov, who invented it in 1941.
