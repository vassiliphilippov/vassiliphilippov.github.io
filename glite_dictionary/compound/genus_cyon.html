genus Cyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsaɪ.ɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A genus of wild canids native to Asia, including the Asiatic wild dog (dhole).
genus of Asian wild dogs (dholes)

Examples

The genus Cyon comprises several species of wild canids found throughout Asia.
Scientists have studied the behavior of the genus Cyon to understand the evolution of pack hunting.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats crucial for survival of animals in the genus Cyon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dhole - Asiatic wild dog belonging to genus Cyon

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Cyon', from Greek 'kyōn' (dog).
