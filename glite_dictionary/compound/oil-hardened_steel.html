oil-hardened steel

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈhɑrdənd stil / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈhɑːd(ə)nd stiːl / (BrE)
Steel that has been quenched in oil to increase its hardness.
steel quenched in oil for hardness

Examples

The manufacturer recommends oil-hardened steel for applications requiring exceptional strength and durability.
After heating, the blade was transformed into oil-hardened steel through a careful quenching process.
Many modern tools make use of oil-hardened steel due to its resistance to wear and deformation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempered steel - steel that has been heat-treated to improve toughness
