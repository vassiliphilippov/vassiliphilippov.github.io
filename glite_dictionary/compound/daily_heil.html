Daily Heil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪli haɪl / (AmE), / ˈdeɪli haɪl / (BrE)
an informal, often humorous or critical, nickname for the British newspaper the Daily Mail, especially when referencing its controversial or sensationalist coverage
parodic nickname for the Daily Mail (UK newspaper)
informal british

Examples

The comedian used the term Daily Heil to mock sensationalist headlines in British tabloids.
Many readers criticize the Daily Heil for its controversial stories and biased reporting.
She shared a meme from the Daily Heil to highlight its tendency toward outrageous news coverage.

Synset Examples

His 'Daily Heil' posts became quite controversial.
She joked about her 'Daily Heil' during lunch.

Etymology

A play on the name "Daily Mail" and the Nazi salute "Heil," used as a satirical criticism of the paper's perceived sensationalism or right-wing stance.
