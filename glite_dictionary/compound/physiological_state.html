physiological state

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkəl steɪt / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkəl steɪt / (BrE)
the condition or functioning status of the body or its internal systems at a particular time
condition of the body's functions

Examples

During exercise, your physiological state changes as your heart rate increases.
The doctor monitored his physiological state throughout the surgery to ensure stability.
Certain drugs can alter the physiological state of the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical condition - the body's state, especially in terms of fitness or health
  • Antonym: pathological state - an abnormal or diseased condition of the body

Etymology

From 'physiological', relating to physiology (from Greek 'phusiologia'), and 'state', from Latin 'status' (condition).
