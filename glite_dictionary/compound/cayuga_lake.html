Cayuga Lake

1. PROPN

/ kaɪˈjuːɡə leɪk / (AmE), / kaɪˈjuːɡə leɪk / (BrE)
a glacial lake in central New York State; the longest of the Finger Lakes region
longest Finger Lake in New York

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit Cayuga Lake each year to enjoy boating and fishing.
The city of Ithaca is located at the southern tip of Cayuga Lake.
Scientists study the unique ecosystem of Cayuga Lake to monitor water quality and wildlife.

Etymology

Named after the Cayuga people, one of the original five nations of the Haudenosaunee (Iroquois Confederacy).
