lunar year

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːnɚ ˌjɪr / (AmE), / ˈluːnə ˌjɪə / (BrE)
a year consisting of 12 lunar months, totaling about 354 days, as used in certain calendars
a year of 12 lunar months

Examples

The Islamic calendar is based on the lunar year, which is shorter than the solar year.
A lunar year is about eleven days shorter than a solar year.
Festivals in some cultures are scheduled according to the lunar year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solar year - a year based on the time the earth takes to orbit the sun

Etymology

From Latin 'luna' (moon) + English 'year'.
