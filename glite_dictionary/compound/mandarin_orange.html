mandarin orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈmændərɪn ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈmændərɪn ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A small citrus tree (Citrus reticulata) native to southeastern Asia, bearing sweet, easily peeled orange fruit.
small sweet citrus fruit and its tree

Examples

She peeled a mandarin orange and shared it with her friends.
The mandarin orange tree in our backyard produces fruit every winter.
Children often enjoy eating mandarin oranges because they are easy to peel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - a variety of mandarin orange with deep orange-red skin

Etymology

From Portuguese 'mandarim', from Malay 'mandarin', after the fruit's association with Chinese mandarins.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmændəˌrɪn ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌmændərɪn ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A small, slightly flattened, reddish-orange citrus fruit with a loose skin, originally from China, known for its sweet flavor and easy-to-peel rind.
a small, sweet, loose-skinned citrus fruit

Examples

She peeled a mandarin orange for her afternoon snack.
The dessert was topped with juicy slices of mandarin orange.
Mandarin oranges are easy for children to open because of their loose skins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - a small citrus fruit similar to the mandarin orange

Etymology

From Portuguese 'mandarim', from Malay 'menteri', via Sanskrit 'mantrin' (counselor); 'mandarin' relates to its Chinese origin.
