to keep a promise

1. VERB

/ kip ə ˈprɑməs / (AmE), / kiːp ə ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
to do what you have promised to do; to fulfill a commitment made to someone
fulfill a commitment or promise

Examples

I always try to keep my promise, even if it is difficult.
She worked hard to keep her promise to her friend.
If you make a commitment, you should keep your promise no matter what.

Synset Examples

I always try to keep my promises.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to break a promise - to fail to do what you have promised
  • Synonym: to fulfill - to carry out or accomplish (something promised or predicted)
