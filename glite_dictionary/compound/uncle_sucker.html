Uncle Sucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəl ˈsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkl ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or tricked, especially used to refer to someone gullible or naïve
a gullible or easily deceived person
informal

Examples

Anyone could see that by investing in that scheme, he played the role of Uncle Sucker.
Don't let them treat you like Uncle Sucker just because you're new here.
She quickly realized she was being set up as Uncle Sucker in their little game.

Synset Examples

He fell for the deal, proving he was Uncle Sucker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked or deceived
  • Synonym: patsy - a person who is easily taken advantage of or victimized
  • Antonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question things
