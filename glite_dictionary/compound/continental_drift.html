continental drift

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.tɪˈnen.t̬əl drɪft / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnen.təl drɪft / (BrE)
the gradual movement of the Earth's continents over geological time, as explained by plate tectonics theory.
the gradual movement of continents

Examples

The theory of continental drift was first proposed by Alfred Wegener in the early 20th century.
Fossil evidence on different continents supports the idea of continental drift.
Students learned about continental drift and its role in shaping the planet's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate tectonics - the theory explaining the structure and motion of Earth's lithosphere
