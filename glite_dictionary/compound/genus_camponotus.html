genus Camponotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kæmˈpoʊnətəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kamˈpɒnətəs / (BrE)
A genus of ants commonly known as carpenter ants, which are known for nesting in wood.
genus of carpenter ants

Examples

The genus Camponotus includes several species of carpenter ants that can damage wooden structures.
Many entomologists study the genus Camponotus to understand ant social behavior.
Members of the genus Camponotus are found in forests and urban areas worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpenter ant - a common name for ants in the genus Camponotus

Etymology

From Latin 'Camponotus', the genus name, from Greek 'kampe' (bend) + 'notos' (back), referring to the shape of the ant.
