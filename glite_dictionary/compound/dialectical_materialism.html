dialectical materialism

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌlɛktɪkəl məˈtɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / daɪˌlɛktɪkəl məˈtɪəriəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the Marxist philosophical approach that interprets political and historical events as the result of conflicts of social forces caused by material needs, based on the idea that material conditions shape ideas and institutions.
Marxist materialist philosophy

Examples

Many scholars have debated the influence of dialectical materialism on twentieth-century political movements.
The philosophy course covered dialectical materialism as a foundation of Marxist thought.
Some critics argue that dialectical materialism oversimplifies the complexity of social change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: historical materialism - Marxist theory emphasizing the role of material conditions in shaping history

Etymology

From 'dialectical' (relating to logical argument) and 'materialism' (the doctrine that matter is the fundamental substance in nature), popularized by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels.
