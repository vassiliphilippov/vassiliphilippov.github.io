scot and lot

1. NOUN

/ skɑt ənd lɑt / (AmE), / skɒt ənd lɒt / (BrE)
historical: the full range of civic duties and taxes formerly required of a citizen, especially in medieval England and Scotland
historical civic obligations and taxes
archaic

Examples

In the old boroughs, only men who paid scot and lot could vote in elections.
The custom of scot and lot placed a range of fiscal duties on townspeople.
He was proud to bear his full share of scot and lot in the community.

Etymology

From Old French *escot* (tax) and English *lot* (share, portion).
