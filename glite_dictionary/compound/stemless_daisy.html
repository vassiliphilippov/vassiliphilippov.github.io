stemless daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛmləs ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈstɛmləs ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A dwarf, nearly stemless herb with a rosette of woolly leaves and large white-rayed flowers, native to central Canada and the western United States.
herb with rosette leaves and white-rayed flowers

Examples

The stemless daisy is often found growing in alpine meadows in the Rockies.
Gardeners appreciate the stemless daisy for its resilience and attractive appearance.
I spotted a patch of stemless daisies during my hike in Arizona.
