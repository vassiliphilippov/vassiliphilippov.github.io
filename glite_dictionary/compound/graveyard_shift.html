graveyard shift

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvjɑrd ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvjɑːd ʃɪft / (BrE)
a work shift that takes place during the night, typically from midnight to 8 a.m.
night work shift (midnight to 8 a.m.)

Examples

He started working the graveyard shift when the company switched to 24-hour operation.
The hospital always needs more nurses willing to take the graveyard shift.
After a week on the graveyard shift, she's still adjusting to sleeping during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night shift - a work shift during nighttime hours

Etymology

From 'graveyard' (cemetery) + 'shift', referring to the quiet, late-night hours resembling the stillness of a graveyard.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvˌjɑrd ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvjɑːd ʃɪft / (BrE)
a work shift that takes place during the night, usually from midnight to early morning
work shift during the night

Examples

During college, I worked the graveyard shift at a local convenience store to earn extra money.
Staff on the graveyard shift often rely on coffee to stay awake through the night.
Security guards assigned to the graveyard shift must remain alert despite the quiet hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night shift - a period of work taking place at night

Etymology

From the association with graveyards, suggesting the quiet and eerie atmosphere of working while most people are asleep.
