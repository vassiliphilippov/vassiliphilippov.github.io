spring heath

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ hiːθ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ hiːθ / (BrE)
A low-growing European shrub that blooms early in spring with bell-shaped red flowers.
dwarf European shrub with early red flowers

Examples

The spring heath is often one of the first plants to flower after winter.
They planted spring heath along the garden border for its early color.
Spring heath thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - A low-growing shrub found in Europe with purple or pink flowers
  • Degree Lower: winter heath - A similar European shrub that blooms in winter
