adrenocorticotropic hormone

1. NOUN

/ əˌdriːnoʊˌkɔːrtɪkoʊˈtrɑːpɪk ˈhɔːrmoʊn / (AmE), / əˌdriːnəʊˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈtrɒpɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the adrenal cortex to secrete steroid hormones
hormone stimulating adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check her adrenocorticotropic hormone levels.
Low adrenocorticotropic hormone production can lead to adrenal insufficiency.
Researchers studied how stress affects secretion of adrenocorticotropic hormone in the participants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ACTH - abbreviation for adrenocorticotropic hormone

Etymology

From adreno- (relating to the adrenal glands) + cortico- (from cortex) + tropic (having an affinity for) + hormone.
