goose down

1. NOUN

/ ɡus daʊn / (AmE), / ɡuːs daʊn / (BrE)
The soft, fine feathers found beneath the outer feathers of a goose, often used as a filling for bedding and clothing due to its lightness and insulating properties.
soft feathers from a goose for bedding or clothing

Examples

The warmest jackets are often filled with goose down to provide excellent insulation.
She bought a luxurious goose down comforter for her bed.
Many winter duvets use goose down for their lightweight warmth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down - soft fine feathers found under the outer feathers of birds
