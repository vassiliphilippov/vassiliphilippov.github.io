slow study

1. NOUN

/ sloʊ ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / sləʊ ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
a person who learns new information or skills more slowly than others
person who learns slowly

Examples

Although he was considered a slow study in school, his perseverance paid off in the long run.
Don't worry if you're a slow study; everyone learns at their own pace.
Sarah knew she was a slow study, so she started preparing for the exam weeks in advance.

Synset Examples

He’s a slow study but eventually understands everything.
Math has always been a slow study for me.
Being a slow study doesn’t mean you won’t succeed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quick study - person who learns quickly
