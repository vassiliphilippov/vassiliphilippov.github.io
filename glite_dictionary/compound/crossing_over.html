crossing over

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsɪŋ ˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes during the prophase of meiosis, resulting in new combinations of genes
exchange of genetic material during meiosis
medical

Examples

Crossing over increases genetic variation among offspring by creating new gene combinations.
During meiosis, the process of crossing over occurs between homologous chromosomes.
Geneticists study crossing over to understand patterns of inheritance.

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' + 'over', referring to the physical crossing of chromosome arms.
