prairie gourd vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ɡɔrd vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ɡɔːd vaɪn / (BrE)
a perennial vine native to dry regions of central and southwestern United States and Mexico, producing small, hard, mottled green inedible fruits
perennial vine with inedible mottled fruits

Examples

The prairie gourd vine is often found sprawling across dry fields in the American Southwest.
Despite its attractive appearance, the fruit of the prairie gourd vine is not edible.
Gardeners sometimes plant prairie gourd vine as ground cover in regions with poor soil.
