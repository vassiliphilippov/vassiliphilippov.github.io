blood sugar

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈʃʊɡɚ / (AmE), / blʌd ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
the concentration of glucose present in the blood, especially as a measure of health
glucose level in the blood
medical

Examples

After eating a meal, your blood sugar levels typically rise for a short period.
People with diabetes must monitor their blood sugar daily to manage their condition.
Sudden drops in blood sugar can cause dizziness and weakness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glucose - a simple sugar which is an important energy source in living organisms
  • Synonym: blood glucose - glucose present in blood, also called blood sugar
