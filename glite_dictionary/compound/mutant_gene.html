mutant gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːtənt dʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtənt dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that has undergone a change (mutation) affecting the typical transmission or expression of a trait
a gene with a mutation affecting traits
medical

Examples

The presence of a mutant gene can increase the risk of certain diseases.
Researchers discovered that the mutant gene was responsible for the abnormal cell growth.
She underwent genetic testing to determine if she carried the mutant gene associated with the disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutated gene - a gene that has undergone mutation
  • Antonym: normal gene - a gene without mutation, typical in the population

Etymology

From 'mutant' (from Latin mutare ‘to change’) + 'gene' (from Greek genos ‘race, offspring’).
