Old World jay

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld dʒeɪ / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld dʒeɪ / (BrE)
a species of jay found in Europe and Asia, particularly referring to the Eurasian jay (Garrulus glandarius)
Eurasian species of jay

Examples

The Old World jay is known for its striking plumage and harsh call.
Many woodlands in Europe are home to the Old World jay.
Unlike some American species, the Old World jay often stores acorns for winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - a bird of the crow family with brightly colored plumage
