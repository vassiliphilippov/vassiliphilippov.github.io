order Salicales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sælɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌsælɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
a botanical order that includes and is coextensive with the family Salicaceae, comprising willows and poplars.
botanical order including Salicaceae

Examples

The order Salicales includes familiar trees such as willows and poplars.
Scientists study the order Salicales to understand the diversity of flowering plants.
Recent classifications confirm that all species in the order Salicales belong solely to the family Salicaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salicaceae - the botanical family including willows and poplars

Etymology

From New Latin Salicales, from Salix (willow) + -ales (order suffix).
