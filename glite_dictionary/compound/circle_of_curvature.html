circle of curvature

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːr.kəl əv ˈkɜːr.və.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəl əv ˈkɜː.vjə.tʃə / (BrE)
the circle that touches a curve at a given point on the concave side and has a radius equal to the radius of curvature at that point
a circle with radius equal to local curve curvature

Examples

At any point on a smooth curve, you can construct a circle of curvature that best approximates the local geometry.
The radius of the circle of curvature changes along the shape of the curve.
In differential geometry, the circle of curvature plays a key role in analyzing curves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osculating circle - the circle that best approximates a curve at a point
