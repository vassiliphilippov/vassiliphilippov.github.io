gambling hell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmbəlɪŋ hɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡæmbəlɪŋ hɛl / (BrE)
A public place where various games of chance are played for money; a gaming house, especially one notorious or illegal.
illicit or notorious gambling house

Examples

The detectives raided the notorious gambling hell on the edge of town.
He lost his entire fortune in a gambling hell frequented by society's outcasts.
In Victorian novels, the gambling hell is often depicted as a place of vice and ruin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casino - a public building where gambling games are played, often legally and in luxurious surroundings
  • Synonym: gambling house - an establishment where gambling takes place

Etymology

From 'gambling' (playing games of chance for money) and 'hell' (used metaphorically for a place of vice or immorality since the 17th century).

/ ˈɡæmblɪŋ hɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡamblɪŋ hɛl / (BrE)

disreputable place for gambling
archaic

Examples

She vowed never to return to that gambling hell again.
