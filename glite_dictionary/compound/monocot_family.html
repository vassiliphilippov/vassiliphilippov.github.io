monocot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌkɑt ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.kɒt ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants whose seeds contain a single cotyledon (embryonic leaf).
family of plants with one seed leaf

Examples

The lily belongs to a monocot family distinguished by a single seed leaf.
Scientists classified the plant into a new monocot family after genetic analysis.
Palm trees are a well-known monocot family found in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dicot family - family of plants with seeds having two cotyledons

Etymology

From 'monocot', short for monocotyledon, from Greek 'monos' (single) and 'kotyledon' (cup-shaped hollow).
