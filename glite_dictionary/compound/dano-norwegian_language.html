Dano-Norwegian language

1. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪnoʊ nɔrˈwiːdʒən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdeɪnəʊ nɔːˈwiːdʒən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a form of Norwegian based on Danish, historically used as one of two official written standards in Norway
historical Norwegian language form based on Danish

Examples

In the 19th century, most Norwegian literature was written in Dano-Norwegian language.
The Dano-Norwegian language served as the basis for the modern Bokmål standard.
Many official documents in Norway used Dano-Norwegian language before the adoption of Bokmål.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bokmål - the modern Norwegian written standard that evolved from Dano-Norwegian
  • Antonym: Nynorsk - the other official written standard of Norwegian, based primarily on Norwegian dialects

Etymology

Blend of 'Dano-', referring to Denmark/Danish, and 'Norwegian'.
