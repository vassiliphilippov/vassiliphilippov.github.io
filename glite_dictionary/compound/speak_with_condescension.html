to speak with condescension

1. VERB

/ spiːk wɪð ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / spiːk wɪð ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
to talk to someone in a way that shows you think you are more important or intelligent than they are
talk down to someone

Examples

The manager tended to speak with condescension when addressing his junior staff.
She realized that to speak with condescension would only make her students resentful.
If you speak with condescension, people are unlikely to respect your opinions.

Synset Examples

He spoke with condescension, alienating the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat someone as if they are less intelligent or important
  • Synonym: to talk down to - to speak to someone as if they are less intelligent
