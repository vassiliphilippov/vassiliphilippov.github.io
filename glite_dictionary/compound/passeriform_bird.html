passeriform bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsərɪˌfɔrm bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈpasərɪfɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird belonging to the order Passeriformes, characterized by perching habits and four toes arranged for gripping; includes most songbirds and small ground birds
bird of the order Passeriformes (perching birds)

Examples

The robin is a common example of a passeriform bird found in many gardens.
Many species of passeriform birds are known for their elaborate courtship songs.
Researchers study the migration patterns of passeriform birds to better understand climate change impacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: songbird - a bird known for its musical song, a typical member of Passeriformes
  • Antonym: nonpasserine - a bird that does not belong to the order Passeriformes

Etymology

From Latin 'Passeriformes', from 'passer' (sparrow) + '-form' (shape, form) indicating resemblance.
