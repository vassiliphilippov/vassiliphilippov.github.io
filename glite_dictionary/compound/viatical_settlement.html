viatical settlement

1. NOUN

/ viˈætɪkəl ˈsɛt̬lmənt / (AmE), / vaɪˈætɪkəl ˈsɛtlmənt / (BrE)
an arrangement in which a terminally ill person sells their life insurance policy to a third party for a lump sum less than the policy's full value
sale of life insurance policy by a terminally ill policyholder

Examples

The company specializes in buying viatical settlements from terminally ill clients.
After being diagnosed with a terminal illness, she decided to seek a viatical settlement to pay her medical bills.
Regulations for viatical settlements vary from state to state in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: life settlement - sale of a life insurance policy by a policyholder for cash (not necessarily terminally ill)

Etymology

From 'viatical' (from Latin 'viaticum', meaning 'provision for a journey'), reflecting the policyholder's final journey.

2. NOUN

/ vaɪˈætɪkəl ˈsɛt̬lmənt / (AmE), / vaɪˈætɪkəl ˈsɛtlmənt / (BrE)
the sale of a life insurance policy by a terminally ill policyholder to a third party for less than its net death benefit but more than its cash surrender value, the buyer then receiving the full benefit upon the policyholder's death
sale of an insurance policy by a terminally ill policyholder
legal

Examples

The company specializes in purchasing viatical settlements from individuals with life-threatening illnesses.
After being diagnosed as terminally ill, he chose to pursue a viatical settlement to cover his medical expenses.
Viatical settlements became more common during the AIDS crisis as patients sought financial security.

Etymology

From Latin 'viaticum' (provision for a journey), reflecting the policyholder's limited time left.
