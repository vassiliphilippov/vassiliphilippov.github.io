flight control

1. NOUN

/ flaɪt kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / flaɪt kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the process of directing and managing the movement of aircraft in flight from ground stations, typically by means of radio or electronic communication with the pilot
ground-based control of aircraft in flight

Examples

The airport's flight control team guided the plane safely through heavy fog.
Advanced flight control systems have greatly improved aviation safety.
Before landing, the pilot maintained constant contact with flight control to receive updated instructions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air traffic control - the activity of directing aircraft on the ground and in the air to ensure their safe operation
