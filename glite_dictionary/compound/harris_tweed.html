Harris Tweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈhærɪs twid / (AmE), / ˈhærɪs twiːd / (BrE)
A type of handwoven tweed cloth made from pure virgin wool in the Outer Hebrides of Scotland.
handwoven Scottish tweed fabric

Examples

He wore a Harris Tweed jacket to the autumn festival.
Authentic Harris Tweed must be handwoven in the Outer Hebrides.
The distinctive pattern of Harris Tweed is recognized around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweed - a rough, woolen fabric, often used for jackets and suits

Etymology

Named after the Isle of Harris in the Outer Hebrides, Scotland, where it is traditionally made.
