genus Neoceratodus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌniːoʊsɛˈrætədəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌniːəʊsɛˈrætədəs / (BrE)
a genus of freshwater lungfish native to Australia, comprising the extant Australian lungfish species
a genus of Australian lungfishes

Examples

The genus Neoceratodus includes the Australian lungfish, an ancient species dating back millions of years.
Researchers studied the genus Neoceratodus to understand the evolution of lungfishes.
The genus Neoceratodus is unique among lungfishes for retaining many primitive features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungfish - freshwater fish with both lungs and gills

Etymology

From Greek neo- (new) + Ceratodus (a related genus name, from Greek keras 'horn' + odous 'tooth').
