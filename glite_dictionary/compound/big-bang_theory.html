big-bang theory

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ bæŋ ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / bɪɡ bæŋ ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the scientific theory that the universe began with a massive explosion from a single, extremely dense and hot point, resulting in its ongoing expansion
theory of the universe's explosive origin

Examples

The big-bang theory is widely accepted among astronomers as the most plausible explanation for the origin of the universe.
According to the big-bang theory, the universe has been expanding since its formation billions of years ago.
Many documentaries explain the big-bang theory in simple terms for those new to cosmology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steady-state theory - the theory that the universe has always existed in its present state and is constantly expanding

Etymology

Coined in the 1940s, with 'Big Bang' referencing the explosive start of the universe.
