Hippo Regius

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɪpoʊ ˈriːdʒiəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊ ˈriːdʒɪəs / (BrE)
An ancient city in Numidia, located in northwestern Africa adjacent to present-day Annaba in northeastern Algeria.
ancient city in North Africa (Numidia)

Examples

Saint Augustine served as bishop of Hippo Regius during the late Roman Empire.
Archaeological remains of Hippo Regius can still be visited in modern Annaba, Algeria.
During antiquity, Hippo Regius was a key center of Christian learning in North Africa.

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'Royal Hippo', from 'Hippo' (the city's name) and 'Regius' (royal).
