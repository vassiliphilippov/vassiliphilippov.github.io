perboric acid

1. NOUN

/ pərˈbɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / pəˈbɒrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline acid derived from boric acid, used mainly in bleaching and as an oxidizing agent
a white crystalline acid used in bleaching

Examples

The laboratory synthesized perboric acid to study its bleaching properties.
Laundry detergents often contain perboric acid as an oxidizing agent.
Researchers measured the reactivity of perboric acid in different chemical solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boric acid - a weak acid of boron used as an antiseptic and insecticide

Etymology

From 'per-' (indicating increased oxygen content) + 'boric acid'.
