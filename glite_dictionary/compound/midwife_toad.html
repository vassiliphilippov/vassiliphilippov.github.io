midwife toad

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.waɪf toʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.waɪf təʊd / (BrE)
A European toad (genus Alytes) whose male carries fertilized eggs wrapped around its hind legs until they hatch.
European toad with unique egg-carrying behavior

Examples

The midwife toad is known for the male's unusual role in carrying eggs on his legs.
Naturalists have long studied the midwife toad because of its fascinating parenting habits.
A midwife toad was spotted in the garden, its hind legs wrapped in strings of eggs.

Etymology

Named for its unique reproductive behavior resembling that of a human midwife.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌwaɪf ˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌwaɪf ˌtəʊd / (BrE)
A small toad of the genus Alytes, native to parts of Europe and known for the male's unique habit of carrying fertilized eggs on its back until they hatch.
European toad species with unique breeding behavior

Examples

The midwife toad is fascinating because the males carry the eggs on their backs until they hatch.
Scientists study the breeding behavior of the midwife toad to understand parental care in amphibians.
Unlike most toads, the midwife toad does not lay its eggs directly in water.

Etymology

Named for the male's role in caring for eggs, analogous to a human midwife.
