pot marjoram

1. NOUN

/ pɑt ˈmɑrdʒərəm / (AmE), / pɒt ˈmɑːdʒərəm / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Origanum onites) with aromatic leaves, used in cooking for its mild flavor.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

Fresh pot marjoram adds a subtle flavor to tomato sauces and soups.
Gardeners often grow pot marjoram in containers on their windowsills.
The chef sprinkled chopped pot marjoram over the roasted vegetables before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - an aromatic herb closely related to pot marjoram

Etymology

From 'pot' (as grown in pots) + 'marjoram', ultimately from Old French 'majorane', from Latin 'majorana'.
