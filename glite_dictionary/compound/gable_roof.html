gable roof

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪbəl ruːf / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪb(ə)l ruːf / (BrE)
A roof with two sloping sides that come together at a ridge, forming a gable at each end.
roof with two slopes and gable ends

Examples

The house features a classic gable roof that gives it a timeless appearance.
Many barns in the countryside have a traditional gable roof design.
Rainwater runs off easily from a gable roof, which helps prevent leaks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hip roof - roof with ends and sides all sloping downwards

Etymology

From 'gable' (from Old French 'gable', fork, roof truss) + 'roof'.
