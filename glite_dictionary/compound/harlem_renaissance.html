Harlem Renaissance

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑrləm ˌrɛnəˈsɑns / (AmE), / ˈhɑːləm ˌrɛnəˈsɑːns / (BrE)
a period in the 1920s in which African-American art, music, and literature experienced a significant revival and gained widespread recognition, centered in the Harlem neighborhood of New York City
African-American cultural movement, 1920s

Examples

Many influential writers emerged during the Harlem Renaissance, including Langston Hughes and Zora Neale Hurston.
The Harlem Renaissance fostered a new sense of black cultural identity in the United States.
Artists of the Harlem Renaissance contributed greatly to American literature, music, and visual arts.

Etymology

Named after Harlem, a neighborhood in New York City, and French 'Renaissance' (rebirth).
