agency security

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənsi sɪˈkjʊrədi / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənsi sɪˈkjʊərəti / (BrE)
A financial security issued by a United States government agency or the Farm Credit System.
security issued by a government agency

Examples

Investors often buy agency security for its perceived safety and reliable returns.
The bank portfolio held a significant portion in US Treasury bonds and agency securities.
A financial advisor explained the benefits of adding agency securities to diversify a portfolio.
