orphan site

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɹfən saɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːfən saɪt / (BrE)
a location contaminated with toxic waste for which no responsible polluter can be identified or held liable for cleanup
toxic waste area with no liable polluter

Examples

The government designated the abandoned factory as an orphan site after no responsible party could be found.
Cleanup of an orphan site often falls to public agencies because the polluter cannot be identified.
There are many orphan sites across the country requiring extensive environmental remediation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Superfund site - a contaminated site in the US identified for cleanup under federal law
