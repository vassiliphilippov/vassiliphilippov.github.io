atomic reactor

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk riˈæktər / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk rɪˈæktə / (BrE)
a device in which controlled nuclear fission is used to generate energy, especially for electricity or research
device for controlled nuclear fission

Examples

The atomic reactor was shut down for maintenance to ensure its safe operation.
Scientists conducted experiments in an atomic reactor to study radiation effects.
A new atomic reactor has been built to supply electricity to the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear reactor - device in which a nuclear chain reaction is controlled to produce energy

Etymology

From atomic (relating to atoms) + reactor (device for initiating and controlling reactions), early 20th century.
