straight poker

1. NOUN

/ streɪt ˈpoʊkər / (AmE), / streɪt ˈpəʊkə / (BrE)
a form of poker in which each player is dealt five cards face down, and bets are made without drawing any further cards
a poker game with no card drawing

Examples

He prefers to play straight poker because he likes the simplicity and speed of the game.
Unlike straight poker, draw poker allows players to improve their hands by replacing cards.
Many old Western movies feature intense games of straight poker in smoky saloons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: draw poker - a form of poker in which players may replace cards by drawing new ones
