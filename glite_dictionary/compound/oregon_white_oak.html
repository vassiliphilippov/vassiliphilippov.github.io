Oregon white oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrəɡən waɪt oʊk / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən waɪt əʊk / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree native to western North America, known for its crooked branches and pale grey bark
a small deciduous tree of western North America

Examples

The Oregon white oak is a key species in the Pacific Northwest's native ecosystems.
We found several ancient Oregon white oaks standing in the open prairie.
Many local birds depend on the acorns produced by the Oregon white oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree or shrub of the genus Quercus, bearing acorns as fruit
