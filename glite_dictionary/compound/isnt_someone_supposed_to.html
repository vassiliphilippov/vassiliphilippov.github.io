isn't someone supposed to

1. X

/ ˈɪzənt ˈsʌmwʌn səˈpoʊzd tu / (AmE), / ˈɪzənt ˈsʌmwʌn səˈpəʊzd tuː / (BrE)
used to express that a person is expected to do something, often indicating surprise or questioning when it has not happened
expressing expectation of an action

Examples

Isn't someone supposed to pick up the guests from the airport tonight?
She's waiting here for hours; isn't someone supposed to help her?
This report is overdue—isn't someone supposed to have submitted it already?

Synset Examples

I can't believe he didn't call, isn't he supposed to?
