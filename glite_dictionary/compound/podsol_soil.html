podsol soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑdˌsoʊl sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɒdsɒl sɔɪl / (BrE)
A type of soil found in temperate to cold, moist climates, characterized by a surface organic mat and a leached, grey subsurface layer, often occurring under coniferous or heath vegetation.
soil type with leached, grey layer

Examples

Podsol soils are commonly found beneath pine forests in northern Europe.
Scientists studied the podsol soil to understand how nutrients move in coniferous ecosystems.
The garden's poor drainage was attributed to the presence of podsol soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: podzol - a soil type with similar characteristics; alternate spelling

Etymology

From Russian 'podzol', meaning 'under-ash', referring to the soil's ash-grey leached layer.
