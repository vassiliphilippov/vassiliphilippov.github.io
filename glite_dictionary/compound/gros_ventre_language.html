Gros Ventre language

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊ vɑːntr̩ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ vɒ̃t ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language spoken by the Gros Ventre (Atsina) people of Montana, United States
Native American Siouan language

Examples

The Gros Ventre language is endangered, with only a handful of fluent speakers remaining today.
Linguists are documenting the Gros Ventre language to help preserve its unique sounds and grammar.
He studied the Gros Ventre language as part of his research on Native American languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - pertaining to the Siouan family of languages

Etymology

From French 'gros ventre' meaning 'big belly', the exonym given to the people by French traders.
