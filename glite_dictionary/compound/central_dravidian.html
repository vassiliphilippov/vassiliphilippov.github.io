Central Dravidian

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəl drəˈvɪdiən / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəl drəˈvɪdiən / (BrE)
a branch of Dravidian languages spoken primarily in central India
a Dravidian language group of central India

Examples

Many tribes in central India communicate using Central Dravidian languages.
Linguists have studied the structure of Central Dravidian to trace the evolution of Indian languages.
Preserving Central Dravidian dialects is important for maintaining regional cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Dravidian - a family of languages spoken mainly in southern India and Sri Lanka
