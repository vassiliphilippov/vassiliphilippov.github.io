microwave spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkˌroʊveɪv spɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊveɪv ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the portion of the electromagnetic spectrum that corresponds to the range of frequencies used by microwaves, typically from about 1 GHz to 300 GHz
part of the electromagnetic spectrum for microwaves

Examples

Satellites use frequencies in the microwave spectrum for reliable long-distance communication.
Scientists study the microwave spectrum to learn about cosmic background radiation.
Wireless networks often operate in the microwave spectrum to transmit data over short distances.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: radio spectrum - range of electromagnetic frequencies used for radio transmission
  • Degree Higher: infrared spectrum - part of the electromagnetic spectrum just above microwaves in frequency
