compound word

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.paʊnd wɝd / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.paʊnd wɜːd / (BrE)
A word made up of two or more existing words or combining forms, often with a meaning that differs from the individual elements.
word formed from two or more words

Examples

The term compound word describes words like toothpaste and watchdog.
English has many compound words that are formed by joining two nouns together.
Children often enjoy making up their own compound words in language games.

Synset Examples

Also common in English is another type of verb-noun (or noun-verb) compound, in which an argument of the verb is incorporated into the verb, which is then usually turned into a gerund, such as breastfeeding, finger-pointing, etc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound - a word made by combining two or more words; broader sense

Etymology

From Latin 'componere' (to put together) and 'word'.
