statute title

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuːt ˈtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈstætjuːt ˈtaɪtəl / (BrE)
the official heading that names a statute or legislative bill, often giving a brief summary of its content
heading naming a law or bill
legal

Examples

The statute title is found at the beginning of the legislative document.
Each statute title covers a specific area of law, such as education or health.
Law students learn to interpret the statute title to understand the scope of a bill.

Synset Examples

Title 8 provided federal help for schools
