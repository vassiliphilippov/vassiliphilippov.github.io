keyhole saw

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiˌhoʊl sɔ / (AmE), / ˈkiːhəʊl sɔː / (BrE)
a handsaw with a long, narrow blade used for cutting small, tight curves or holes in wood or plasterboard
handsaw for cutting small curves or holes

Examples

The carpenter used a keyhole saw to make a precise opening for the light switch.
If you need to cut a small, irregular shape in drywall, a keyhole saw works well.
She reached for her keyhole saw when the jigsaw was too large for the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compass saw - a saw with a narrow, tapering blade for cutting curves
