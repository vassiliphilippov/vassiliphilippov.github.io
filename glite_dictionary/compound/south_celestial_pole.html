South Celestial Pole

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ səˈlɛstʃəl poʊl / (AmE), / saʊθ sɪˈlɛstʃəl pəʊl / (BrE)
the point in the sky directly above the Earth's South Pole, around which the stars appear to rotate for observers in the Southern Hemisphere
point in the sky above Earth's South Pole

Examples

The South Celestial Pole is not marked by a bright star like Polaris in the Northern Hemisphere.
Astronomers in Australia often use the South Celestial Pole to align their telescopes.
Stars seem to circle around the South Celestial Pole when viewed from southern latitudes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North Celestial Pole - the point in the sky above Earth's North Pole
