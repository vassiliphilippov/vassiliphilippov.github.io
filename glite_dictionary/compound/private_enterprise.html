private enterprise

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət ˌɛntɚˌpraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət ˌɛntəˌprʌɪz / (BrE)
an economic system in which businesses and industries are owned and managed by private individuals or organizations rather than by the government, with production and prices largely determined by market forces
system of privately owned businesses

Examples

Many supporters argue that private enterprise drives innovation and economic growth.
Critics claim that unchecked private enterprise can lead to inequality and market failures.
The government introduced reforms to promote private enterprise in key industries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free enterprise - system where private businesses operate with minimal government control
  • Antonym: state-owned enterprise - a business owned and operated by the government
