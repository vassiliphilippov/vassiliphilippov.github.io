safety match

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti ˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ˌmatʃ / (BrE)
a match that can only be ignited by striking it against a specially prepared surface, reducing the risk of accidental ignition
match that lights only on special surfaces

Examples

Always use a safety match to light the stove in the cabin.
The box of safety matches was damp and wouldn't strike properly.
Unlike strike-anywhere matches, a safety match only ignites on the box's prepared strip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - a small stick for producing fire via friction
