European goatsucker

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈɡoʊtˌsʌkər / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈɡəʊtˌsʌkə / (BrE)
a nocturnal bird of Europe and western Asia, also known as the nightjar, with mottled plumage and a distinctive churring call
a European nocturnal bird, the nightjar

Examples

The European goatsucker is more often heard than seen due to its nocturnal habits.
During summer evenings, the churring sound of the European goatsucker can be heard in the countryside.
Researchers set up cameras to observe the feeding behavior of the European goatsucker at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - nocturnal bird with a churring call, also called goatsucker

Etymology

The name 'goatsucker' comes from the ancient belief that the bird sucked milk from goats; 'European' indicates its range.
