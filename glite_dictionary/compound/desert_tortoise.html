desert tortoise

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛz.ɚt ˈtɔr.təs / (AmE), / ˈdez.ət ˈtɔː.təs / (BrE)
a burrowing tortoise found in the arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico, known for its domed shell and slow lifestyle
burrowing tortoise of western US and northern Mexico

Examples

The desert tortoise spends much of its life in underground burrows to escape the extreme heat.
Researchers tracked the movements of several desert tortoises to study their habitat preferences.
Development projects in the Mojave Desert must consider the endangered status of the desert tortoise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortoise - reptile with a domed shell and slow movement
