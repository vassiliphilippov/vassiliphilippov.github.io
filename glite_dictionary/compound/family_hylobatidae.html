family Hylobatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæləˈbeɪtəˌdi / (AmE), / ˌhɪləʊˈbætɪdi / (BrE)
A family of primates known as the lesser apes, including gibbons and siamangs, sometimes considered a subfamily of Pongidae.
family of lesser apes (gibbons and siamangs)

Examples

The family Hylobatidae includes all species of gibbons and siamangs.
Scientists debate whether the family Hylobatidae should be classified separately from the great apes.
Conservation efforts for the family Hylobatidae focus on protecting Southeast Asian forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibbon - smaller apes of the family Hylobatidae, found in Southeast Asia
  • Synonym: siamang - the largest member of the gibbon family, found in Malaysia and Indonesia

Etymology

From New Latin Hylobatidae, based on Hylobates (a genus of gibbons) and the taxonomic suffix -idae for animal families.
