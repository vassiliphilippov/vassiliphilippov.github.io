vice president

1. NOUN

/ vaɪs ˈprɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / vaɪs ˈprɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
An executive officer ranking immediately below a president, who may act as president in their absence or under certain circumstances.
executive officer below the president

Examples

After the president resigned, the vice president assumed office for the remainder of the term.
She was promoted to vice president of marketing after several years as a manager.
The vice president often represents the company at international conferences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: president - the chief executive officer in certain organizations or governments
  • Synonym: deputy - a person designated to act for another; a subordinate

Etymology

From Latin 'vice' (in place of) + 'president.'
