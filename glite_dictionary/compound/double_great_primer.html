double great primer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌɡreɪt ˈpraɪmər / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌɡreɪt ˈprɪmə / (BrE)
a size of printing type equal to 36 points, slightly smaller than meridian.
36-point printing type size

Examples

Early printers often used double great primer for chapter titles and other prominent headings.
The double great primer typeface was uncommon in modern printing but popular during the 19th century.
He ordered a font of double great primer for the special edition cover.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: great primer - a 18-point printing type
  • Degree Higher: meridian - a 44-point printing type size

Etymology

Named for its size relative to 'great primer' in traditional printing type scales.
