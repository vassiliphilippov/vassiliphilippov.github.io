northern woodsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˈwʊdziə / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈwʊdziə / (BrE)
a slender fern native to northern North America, with chestnut-colored stalks and finely divided fronds
a slender fern of northern North America

Examples

The northern woodsia thrives on rocky cliffs in cooler regions across Canada.
During our hike, we identified several northern woodsia ferns growing near the waterfall.
Botanists were excited to find a rare specimen of northern woodsia in the national park.
