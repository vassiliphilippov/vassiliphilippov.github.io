communist economy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmjunɪst ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊnɪst ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
an economic system in which the government controls production, distribution, and prices, typically found in a communist state
state-controlled economic system in communism

Examples

The Soviet Union operated a communist economy for most of the twentieth century.
Transitioning from a communist economy to a market-based system was a complex process.
Many policies in a communist economy are designed to eliminate private ownership of property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planned economy - an economy in which production, investment, prices, and incomes are determined centrally by a government
  • Antonym: market economy - an economic system in which production and prices are determined by unrestricted competition between privately owned businesses
