steam shovel

1. NOUN

/ stiːm ˈʃʌvəl / (AmE), / stiːm ˈʃʌvəl / (BrE)
A large excavating machine powered by steam and used for lifting and moving earth or rock, especially in construction and mining.
excavating machine powered by steam

Examples

The construction crew used a steam shovel to dig the foundation for the new building.
Early gold miners relied on the power of the steam shovel to move massive amounts of soil.
A restored steam shovel stands in the museum as a symbol of industrial progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavator - machine for digging or removing earth

2. NOUN

/ stiːm ˈʃʌvəl / (AmE), / stiːm ˈʃʌvəl / (BrE)
A large excavating machine powered by steam, used for digging and moving earth or other material, especially in construction or mining in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
steam-powered excavating machine

Examples

The construction crew brought a steam shovel to clear the old railway embankment.
In the early 1900s, a steam shovel was essential for large-scale mining operations.
Children watched in awe as the enormous steam shovel scooped up piles of earth at the site.

Synset Examples

The steam shovel dug deep into the earth for the foundation.
They used a steam shovel to clear the construction site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavator - a machine used for digging or moving earth
  • Synonym: power shovel - an electrically or otherwise powered excavating machine

Etymology

Formed from 'steam' (referring to the power source) and 'shovel' (referring to the digging function).
