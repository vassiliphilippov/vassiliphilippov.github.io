molecular biology

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛkjələr baɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / məˈlɛkjʊlə baɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of biology concerned with the structure and activity of biological macromolecules, especially as related to genetics and cell function.
biology branch studying biological macromolecules

Examples

She decided to specialize in molecular biology during her graduate studies.
Advances in molecular biology have revolutionized our understanding of genetic diseases.
The laboratory offers several courses in molecular biology for undergraduate students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biochemistry - the study of chemical processes within and relating to living organisms

Etymology

From 'molecular', relating to molecules, and 'biology', from Greek bios (life) + -logia (study).
