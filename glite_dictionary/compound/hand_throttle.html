hand throttle

1. NOUN

/ hænd ˈθrɑːtəl / (AmE), / hænd ˈθrɒtəl / (BrE)
A lever operated by hand to control the throttle valve of an engine, often used in vehicles or machinery.
hand-operated throttle control lever

Examples

The old tractor's hand throttle had to be adjusted before starting the engine.
He slowly advanced the hand throttle to increase the boat's speed.
Pilots used a hand throttle in early airplanes to control engine power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throttle - a device controlling the flow of fuel or power to an engine
