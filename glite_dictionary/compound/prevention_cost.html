prevention cost

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛnʃən kɑst / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛnʃən kɒst / (BrE)
the cost of activities and efforts aimed at preventing defects in a product or process
cost incurred to prevent defects

Examples

The company increased its prevention cost to improve product reliability and reduce defects.
Investing in prevention cost often results in lower expenses associated with defective products.
Quality planning and staff training are typical components of prevention cost in manufacturing.

Synset Examples

Some example component items of the total prevention costs are: quality planning, quality training and workforce development, and product-design verification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraisal cost - costs associated with measuring and monitoring product quality
  • Antonym: failure cost - costs resulting from defects in products or services
