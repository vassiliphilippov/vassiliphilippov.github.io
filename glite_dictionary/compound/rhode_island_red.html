Rhode Island Red

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊd ˈaɪ.lənd ɹɛd / (AmE), / ˌrəʊd ˈaɪ.lənd red / (BrE)
an American breed of heavy-bodied, brownish-red chicken, valued for both its eggs and meat
American breed of brownish-red chicken

Examples

The Rhode Island Red is a popular choice among backyard poultry keepers.
She keeps a flock of Rhode Island Reds for their reliable egg production.
A prize-winning Rhode Island Red was displayed at the county fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leghorn - Italian breed of chicken, known for egg-laying

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Rhode Island, where the breed originated, and its reddish plumage.
