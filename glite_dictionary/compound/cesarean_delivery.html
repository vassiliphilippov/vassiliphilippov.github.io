cesarean delivery

1. NOUN

/ səˈzɛri.ən dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / sɪˈzɛːrɪən dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
the delivery of a baby by surgical incision through the abdominal wall and uterus
childbirth by surgical incision
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a cesarean delivery due to complications during labor.
After her previous cesarean delivery, she had a fast recovery.
Statistics show an increase in cesarean deliveries over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - a surgical operation for delivering a baby by cutting through the wall of the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin Caesar (name), believed to derive from an early Roman family, associated with the legend of Julius Caesar's birth.
