radio drama

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ ˈdrɑmə / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈdrɑːmə / (BrE)
A play or story performed by actors and broadcast on radio, relying solely on sound without visual elements.
audio play performed on radio

Examples

The BBC has a long tradition of producing high-quality radio drama for its listeners.
She grew up listening to radio drama every Sunday night with her family.
Many classic mystery stories were adapted into radio drama before being made into movies.

Synset Examples

An important turning point in radio drama came when Schenectady, New York's WGY, after a successful tryout on August 3, 1922, began weekly studio broadcasts of full-length stage plays in September 1922, using music, sound effects and a regular troupe of actors, The WGY Players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audio drama - A dramatized performance created specifically for audio platforms, not limited to radio broadcast.

Etymology

Formed from 'radio' (wireless transmission) and 'drama' (play, performance).
