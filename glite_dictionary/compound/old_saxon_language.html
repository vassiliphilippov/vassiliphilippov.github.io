Old Saxon language

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈsæksən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈsæksən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Low German language spoken in northern Germany before 1200, ancestral to modern Low German and closely related to Old English
early form of Low German before 1200

Examples

Scholars study the Old Saxon language to understand the development of early Germanic languages.
The Old Saxon language shares many features with Old English.
Much of our knowledge of the Old Saxon language comes from ancient religious texts like the Heliand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old High German - the early form of High German, spoken south of Old Saxon regions
  • Synonym: Old English - the early medieval English language, closely related to Old Saxon

Etymology

Old Saxon: from the Saxon people, an early Germanic tribe, and 'Old' denoting its historical form.
