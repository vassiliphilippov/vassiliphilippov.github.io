to feel a bit depresh

1. VERB

/ fiːl ə bɪt dəˈprɛʃ / (AmE), / fiːl ə bɪt dɪˈprɛʃ / (BrE)
to experience a slight feeling of depression or sadness; to feel somewhat down or low-spirited
to feel slightly depressed
informal slang

Examples

I feel a bit depresh whenever I hear bad news first thing in the morning.
After failing the exam, she really felt a bit depresh for a few days.
He's been feeling a bit depresh lately, but his friends are trying to cheer him up.

Synset Examples

I've been feeling a bit depresh since the weather turned gloomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feel down - to feel sad or unhappy
  • Degree Higher: depressed - in a state of unhappiness or low spirits

Etymology

From 'feel' + slang shortening 'depresh' from 'depressed'.
