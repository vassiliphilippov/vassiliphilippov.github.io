vena basilica

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə bəˈsɪlɪkə / (AmE), / ˌviːnə bəˈsɪlɪkə / (BrE)
a large vein that drains blood from the back of the hand and forearm and empties into the axillary vein in the upper arm
major vein of the arm
medical

Examples

The nurse located the vena basilica for the intravenous injection.
In anatomy class, we learned how the vena basilica drains blood from the forearm.
An injury to the vena basilica can cause significant bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axillary vein - the large vein into which the vena basilica empties

Etymology

From Latin 'vena basilica', meaning 'royal vein'.
