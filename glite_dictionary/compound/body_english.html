body English

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːdi ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
a motion made by a player as if to influence the direction of a ball or object after it has been propelled
motion to influence ball/object's direction after release
american

Examples

He tried to use body English to guide the bowling ball toward the last pin.
Even after releasing the dart, she leaned with a bit of body English as if to steer it to the target.
Fans laughed as he added dramatic body English to every putt on the golf course.

Etymology

American English, 20th century; patterned after 'body language', with English from 'English' (spin or special movement) in sports slang.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑdi ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
unconscious or deliberate movement of one's body in an attempt to influence the direction or outcome of a moving object, such as a ball or shot, in sports
attempt to influence a ball's direction by body movement

Examples

She tried to give the basketball some body English, leaning as she watched it roll toward the hoop.
Golfers often use body English after a putt, twisting their bodies as if it could steer the ball.
He put a lot of body English into his shot, hoping to curve the ball into the net.

Synset Examples

She leaned to the left, using body English to make the shot.
He relies on body English to control the cue ball.
He used body English to steer the ball into the pocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body language - the conscious and unconscious movements and postures by which attitudes and feelings are communicated

Etymology

Modelled on 'body language' and 'English' meaning side spin in billiards.
