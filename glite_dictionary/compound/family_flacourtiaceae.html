family Flacourtiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fləˌkɔːrtiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli fləˌkɔːtɪɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs formerly recognized in plant taxonomy, now largely included in the family Salicaceae
plant family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The family Flacourtiaceae once included a diverse range of tropical trees and shrubs.
Botanists now typically assign most plants from the family Flacourtiaceae to the Salicaceae family.
She studied the unique fruiting habits of species once classed under the family Flacourtiaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Flacourtia (named after Étienne de Flacourt, French governor), plus standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
