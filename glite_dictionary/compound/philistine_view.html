philistine view

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪˌstiːn vjuː / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪstiːn vjuː / (BrE)
A perspective or attitude that is indifferent or hostile to art and culture; lacking appreciation for fine arts and intellectual pursuits.
anti-art, uncultured perspective

Examples

She dismissed modern painting with her typical philistine view that art should only represent reality.
Many politicians hold a philistine view of cultural funding, seeing the arts as nonessential.
His philistine view on literature prevented him from enjoying classic novels.

Synset Examples

His philistine views on art shocked the critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philistine - a person who is indifferent or hostile to culture and the arts

Etymology

Philistine: from Late Latin Philistinus, from Hebrew Pĕlištī (inhabitant of Philistia), extended figuratively in 19th-century German student slang to mean 'uncultured person.'
