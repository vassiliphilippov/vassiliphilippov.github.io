Australian hare's foot

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən hɛrz fʊt / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən heəz fʊt / (BrE)
A species of hare's-foot fern native to Australia, belonging to the genus Davallia, known for its hairy rhizomes resembling a hare's foot.
Australian fern with hairy rhizomes

Examples

The Australian hare's foot thrives in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight in home gardens.
Botanists admire the distinctive, hairy rhizomes of the Australian hare's foot for their resemblance to a hare's foot.
She decorated her living room with a potted Australian hare's foot to bring some greenery indoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare's-foot fern - any fern with hairy rootstocks of the genus Davallia
