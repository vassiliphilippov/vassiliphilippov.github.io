Seidlitz powder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdlɪts ˌpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdlɪts ˌpaʊdə / (BrE)
a compound of sodium bicarbonate, Rochelle salt, and tartaric acid, formerly used as a mild laxative
effervescent laxative compound
archaic medical

Examples

The patient was advised to take a dose of Seidlitz powder to relieve his constipation.
In the 19th century, Seidlitz powder was a popular household remedy for digestive discomfort.
She remembered her grandmother always keeping Seidlitz powder in the medicine cabinet for upset stomachs.

Etymology

Named after Seidlitz, a spa town in Bohemia (now Sedlec, Czech Republic), noted for its mineral springs.
