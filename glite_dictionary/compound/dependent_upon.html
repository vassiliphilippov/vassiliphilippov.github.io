dependent upon

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt əˈpɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt əˈpɒn / (BrE)
Determined or decided by something that follows or results; contingent on certain conditions or circumstances.
contingent on conditions or circumstances

Examples

The outcome of the case is dependent upon the judge's final decision.
Whether the picnic will happen is dependent upon the weather this weekend.
Her promotion is dependent upon the completion of the ongoing project.

Synset Examples

arms sales contingent on the approval of Congress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contingent on - subject to the condition or happening of something else
  • Antonym: independent of - not affected by or reliant on something else

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt əˈpɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt əˈpɒn / (BrE)
determined or decided by something else; contingent on certain conditions or circumstances
contingent on other conditions

Examples

Success in the program is dependent upon your ability to work hard and stay focused.
The university's funding is dependent upon annual government grants.
Approval of the merger is dependent upon regulatory review.

Synset Examples

arms sales contingent on the approval of Congress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contingent on - subject to chance or dependent on conditions

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang from) and 'upon' (on top of).
