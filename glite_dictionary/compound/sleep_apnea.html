sleep apnea

1. NOUN

/ sliːp ˈæpniə / (AmE), / sliːp ˈapniə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person stops breathing for short periods while asleep, often causing poor sleep and health problems.
pauses in breathing during sleep (medical)
medical

Examples

Many people with sleep apnea are unaware they have the condition until prompted by a partner.
Untreated sleep apnea can increase the risk of heart problems and high blood pressure.
His doctor recommended a sleep study after he complained of fatigue, suspecting sleep apnea.
