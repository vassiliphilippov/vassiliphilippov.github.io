low blow

1. NOUN

/ loʊ bloʊ / (AmE), / ləʊ bləʊ / (BrE)
an unfair or unnecessarily hurtful remark or action, especially one made to hurt someone emotionally
unfair or hurtful remark or action

Examples

His comment about her appearance was a low blow and deeply upset her.
She accused him of delivering a low blow during their argument.
It was considered a low blow to mention his recent job loss.

Synset Examples

Bringing up her past was a real low blow.
He considered the insult a low blow during the debate.
Her comments were seen as a low blow to his confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheap shot - an unfair or unsporting criticism or attack
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

Originally from boxing, where a 'low blow' is a punch below the belt, which is illegal.
