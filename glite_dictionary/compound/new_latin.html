New Latin

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈlæt.ɪn / (AmE), / njuː ˈlat.ɪn / (BrE)
The form of Latin used since the Renaissance, especially for scientific or scholarly purposes and modern nomenclature.
modern form of Latin used since the Renaissance

Examples

Many scientific names of plants and animals are formed in New Latin.
He studied texts written in New Latin to understand historical developments in biology.
New Latin became the international language of science during the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries.

Etymology

From the English 'new' and Latin 'Latinus' (Latin), referring to its post-Renaissance revival and adaptation.

2. NOUN

/ nuː ˈlætɪn / (AmE), / njuː ˈlætɪn / (BrE)
The form of Latin used since the Renaissance, chiefly for scientific, scholarly, and technical purposes, including scientific nomenclature.
Latin used for science and scholarship since the Renaissance

Examples

Many scientific terms, including species names, are derived from New Latin.
During the 18th century, scholars published treatises in New Latin to reach a wider academic audience.
New Latin continues to influence the vocabulary of fields such as botany and medicine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: classical Latin - The form of Latin used during the late Roman Republic and the Roman Empire
  • Degree Lower: Medieval Latin - The form of Latin used in the Middle Ages

Etymology

From English 'new' + 'Latin', referring to its revival and adaptation from classical Latin.
