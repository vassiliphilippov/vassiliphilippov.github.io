to incubate ideas

1. VERB

/ ˈɪŋkjəˌbeɪt aɪˈdiəz / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbeɪt aɪˈdɪəz / (BrE)
to allow ideas to develop and mature gradually over a period before acting on them
develop ideas gradually

Examples

It's often wise to incubate ideas before you make important decisions.
Writers sometimes incubate ideas for months before starting a new novel.
The committee wanted to incubate ideas rather than rush into a solution.

Synset Examples

They decided to incubate their ideas before sharing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brainstorm - think freely to generate ideas

Etymology

Figurative use of 'incubate,' originally from Latin 'incubare' (to lie upon), applied to ideas.
