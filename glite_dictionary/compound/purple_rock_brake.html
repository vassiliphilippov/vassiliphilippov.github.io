purple rock brake

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl rɑk breɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl rɒk breɪk / (BrE)
A small, shallowly creeping North American fern, typically found on cliffs or walls, with dark, glossy leaf stalks.
North American fern with dark glossy stems

Examples

The purple rock brake grows best in rocky crevices along mountain cliffs.
Botanists admire the glossy stems of the purple rock brake when studying native ferns.
We spotted a patch of purple rock brake thriving on the old stone wall.
