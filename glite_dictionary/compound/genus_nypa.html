genus Nypa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈniː.pə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈniː.pə / (BrE)
a monotypic genus of palms, native to Australasia, represented solely by the species Nypa fruticans
a genus of palms (Australasia, single species)

Examples

The genus Nypa is unique among palms for thriving in mangrove habitats.
Researchers studied seed dispersal patterns in the genus Nypa along the riverbanks.
There is only one living species within the genus Nypa, known as the nipa palm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nypa fruticans - the only species in the genus Nypa, a mangrove palm of Southeast Asia and Australasia

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind) + Nypa, from Malay ‘nipah’, referring to the species Nypa fruticans.
