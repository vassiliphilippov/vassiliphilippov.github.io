common axe

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈæks / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈæks / (BrE)
an axe with a long handle and a head that has one cutting edge and one blunt side
standard axe with one cutting and one blunt side

Examples

The lumberjack used a common axe to fell the tree.
A common axe is perfect for splitting logs due to its sturdy design.
The museum displayed a common axe from the nineteenth century among other historical tools.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: double-bitted axe - axe with two symmetrical cutting edges
