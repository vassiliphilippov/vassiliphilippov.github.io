stock company

1. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈkʌmpən.i / (AmE), / stɒk ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a company whose capital is divided into shares that can be bought and sold by shareholders
company with capital divided into shares

Examples

She decided to invest in a stock company to benefit from its potential growth.
A stock company can raise capital by issuing shares to the public.
Many large corporations operate as a stock company to attract investors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint-stock company - a business entity in which shares of the company's stock can be bought and sold by shareholders

2. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / stɒk ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a theatrical company that regularly performs a set repertoire of plays, often with a permanent group of actors
theatrical troupe with a set repertoire

Examples

The old theater hired a stock company to perform a variety of classic plays.
She joined the local stock company and quickly became one of its leading actresses.
During the 1920s, stock companies were a popular way to bring live drama to small towns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repertory company - a company of actors that performs a variety of plays, usually in rotation
