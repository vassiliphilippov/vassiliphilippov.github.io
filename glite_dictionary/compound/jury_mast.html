jury mast

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʊɹi mæst / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊəri mɑːst / (BrE)
a temporary mast fitted to a sailing vessel to replace one that has been lost or broken
temporary replacement mast on a ship

Examples

After the storm destroyed the mainmast, the crew erected a jury mast to continue their voyage.
A jury mast was quickly constructed using spare spars and rigging.
Sailing under a jury mast proved challenging, but they eventually reached the nearest port.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mast - a tall upright post on a ship that supports sails or rigging

Etymology

From 'jury' (temporary or makeshift) and 'mast'. Likely from the nautical use of 'jury' meaning makeshift or substitute.
