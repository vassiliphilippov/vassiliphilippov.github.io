Port Jackson fig

1. NOUN

/ pɔrt ˈdʒæksən fɪɡ / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈdʒæksən fɪɡ / (BrE)
An Australian tree (Ficus rubiginosa) resembling the banyan, often planted for ornament and introduced into South Africa for brushwood.
Australian ornamental tree (Ficus rubiginosa)

Examples

The Port Jackson fig is commonly seen lining the streets of Sydney.
Gardeners often choose the Port Jackson fig for its decorative aerial roots and broad canopy.
In South Africa, the Port Jackson fig is valued as a source of brushwood.

Etymology

Named after Port Jackson, the harbor of Sydney, Australia, where the tree is native.
