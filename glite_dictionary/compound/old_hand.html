old hand

1. NOUN

/ oʊld hænd / (AmE), / əʊld hænd / (BrE)
a person with a lot of experience in a particular job or activity
experienced person; expert

Examples

After working in the company for over thirty years, Janet is considered an old hand at managing difficult clients.
The new recruits relied on the old hand to show them the ropes during their first week.
Being an old hand at carpentry, he quickly fixed the broken shelf without any trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person who has a lot of experience in a particular field
