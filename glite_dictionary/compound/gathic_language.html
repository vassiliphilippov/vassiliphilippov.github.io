Gathic language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæθɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡæθɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an ancient Iranian language in which the Gathas, the most sacred texts of the Zoroastrian faith, were composed
ancient Iranian language of the Zoroastrian Gathas

Examples

The Gathic language is considered the oldest preserved Iranian language.
Scholars study the Gathic language to better understand Zoroastrian holy texts.
His research focused on the grammar of the Gathic language found in ancient manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Avestan - ancient Iranian language of Zoroastrian scripture

Etymology

From Gatha (the sacred Zoroastrian hymns) + English 'language'.
