thick fog

1. NOUN

/ θɪk fɑɡ / (AmE), / θɪk fɒɡ / (BrE)
A dense and heavy fog that significantly reduces visibility.
dense, heavy fog

Examples

After the sun set, a thick fog rolled in and covered the city streets.
Flights were delayed due to the thick fog at the airport this morning.
The harbor was impossible to see through the thick fog that lingered for hours.

Synset Examples

He drove slowly through the thick fog on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dense fog - fog that is thick and hard to see through
  • Degree Lower: light fog - fog that is thinner and less obstructive
