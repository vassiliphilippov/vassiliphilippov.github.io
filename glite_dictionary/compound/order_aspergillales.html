order Aspergillales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌæspərˌdʒɪˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌæspəˌdʒɪˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of fungi characterized by a closed ascocarp (cleistothecium) with asci scattered rather than arranged in a hymenium.
order of fungi with closed ascocarps
medical

Examples

The order Aspergillales includes many species important to medicine and industry.
Researchers are studying the genetics of fungi in the order Aspergillales to better understand their life cycles.
Certain molds classified under the order Aspergillales can produce harmful mycotoxins.

Etymology

From the genus name Aspergillus, combined with scientific order naming conventions (-ales).
