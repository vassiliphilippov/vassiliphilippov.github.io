ego trip

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡoʊ trɪp / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊ trɪp / (BrE)
an action or experience motivated by a desire to boost one's own sense of importance or power
action for self-importance or power
informal

Examples

His speech at the awards ceremony turned into a blatant ego trip.
She accused her boss of taking on the charity project as an ego trip, not out of real concern.
The meeting became an ego trip for the manager, who ignored all suggestions but his own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-aggrandizement - increasing one's own importance or power
  • Antonym: humility - a modest view of one's own importance

Etymology

From 'ego' (Latin, 'I, self') + 'trip' (slang for a self-indulgent experience), popularized in the mid-20th century.
