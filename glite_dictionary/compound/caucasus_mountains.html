Caucasus Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔkəsəs ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkəsəs ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in the region of Caucasia, between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, forming part of the traditional border between Europe and Asia
mountain range between Europe and Asia

Examples

The Caucasus Mountains are renowned for their biodiversity and unique landscapes.
Mount Elbrus, the highest peak in Europe, is located in the Caucasus Mountains.
Political conflicts have occasionally erupted in the regions surrounding the Caucasus Mountains.

Etymology

From Latin 'Caucasus', of uncertain origin, possibly from Scythian or other ancient regional languages.
