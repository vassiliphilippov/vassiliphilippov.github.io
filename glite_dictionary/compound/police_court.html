police court

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis kɔrt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs kɔːt / (BrE)
A lower court that tries minor offenses and can refer serious charges to a higher court for trial.
court for minor criminal cases
legal british

Examples

The accused appeared in the police court to answer charges of petty theft.
Cases of public disorder are often dealt with in the police court.
More serious crimes are sent from the police court to a higher tribunal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magistrates' court - a court with similar functions for minor offenses (mainly in the UK)
  • Degree Higher: crown court - a higher court for serious criminal cases in the UK
