commercial letter of credit

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɝː.ʃəl ˈlɛt̬.ɚ əv ˈkrɛd.ɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmɜː.ʃəl ˈlet.ə əv ˈkrɛd.ɪt / (BrE)
a letter of credit issued to a business entity for commercial transactions, guaranteeing payment for goods or services under specified conditions
a business payment guarantee issued by a bank

Examples

The exporter required a commercial letter of credit before shipping the products overseas.
A commercial letter of credit guarantees that the seller will receive payment once the documents are presented.
To secure the deal, the buyer arranged a commercial letter of credit through their bank.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: letter of credit - a document from a bank guaranteeing payment to a seller
