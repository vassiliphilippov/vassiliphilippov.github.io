genus Plagianthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pleɪˈdʒænθəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs plæˈdʒiːænθəs / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs and trees native to Australia and New Zealand, in the mallow family.
genus of Australian and New Zealand shrubs and trees

Examples

The genus Plagianthus includes several species native to New Zealand.
Researchers studied the adaptation of plants in the genus Plagianthus to their local environment.
A rare tree in the genus Plagianthus was found growing near the coastline.

Etymology

From Greek 'plagios' (oblique, slanting) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to aspects of the plant's flowers.
