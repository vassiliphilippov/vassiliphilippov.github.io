gandy dancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡændi ˌdænsər / (AmE), / ˈɡandi ˌdɑːnsə / (BrE)
a laborer who works on maintaining railroad tracks, especially in the early days of railroads in the United States
railroad track maintenance worker

Examples

My great-grandfather was a gandy dancer who worked on the railroads across the Midwest.
The gandy dancers sang work songs as they synchronized their movements on the tracks.
Before machines took over, teams of gandy dancers did the hard labor of keeping the rails aligned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trackman - railroad worker who maintains the tracks

Etymology

Probably from the Gandy Manufacturing Company, which made track tools, and the rhythmic movement ('dancing') of workers aligning rails.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡændi ˌdænsər / (AmE), / ˈɡandi ˌdɑːnsə / (BrE)
a worker who lays and maintains railroad tracks, especially as part of a crew using manual labor
worker who lays railroad tracks

Examples

A crew of gandy dancers was repairing the railroad ties in the hot sun.
The story recounted his grandfather's days as a gandy dancer in the 1920s.
For decades, gandy dancers played a crucial role in building the nation's railways.

Synset Examples

The gandy dancers worked tirelessly to lay the new tracks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trackman - a worker who lays or repairs railroad tracks

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the Gandy Manufacturing Company (maker of track tools) and the synchronized movements resembling dancing.
