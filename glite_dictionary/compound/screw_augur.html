screw augur

1. NOUN

/ skru ˈɔːɡər / (AmE), / skruː ˈɔːɡə / (BrE)
An orchid of the genus Spiranthes, characterized by tall, erect stems with densely flowered spiraling clusters of creamy white, vanilla-scented flowers, commonly found in damp areas of eastern and central North America.
a North American Spiranthes orchid with spiraling white flower clusters

Examples

Walking through the wetlands, we spotted a screw augur blooming among the reeds.
The screw augur is easily identified by its spiral of delicate white flowers.
Many wildflower enthusiasts hope to see a screw augur in its natural habitat during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spiranthes - a genus of orchids with spirally arranged flowers

Etymology

From the resemblance of the spiral flower arrangement to a screw auger (a tool for boring holes).
