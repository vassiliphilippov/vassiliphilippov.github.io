no point

1. NOUN

/ noʊ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / nəʊ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
no reason or purpose for doing something; no justification or benefit to be gained
no reason or purpose

Examples

There was no point in arguing further; she had already made up her mind.
If the weather stays bad, there's no point in going to the beach.
I realized it was no point trying to fix the broken vase; it was beyond repair.

Synset Examples

There's no point to wait around for a bus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointless - having no purpose; serving no useful function
