target acquisition system

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡɪt əˌkwɪzɪʃən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt əˌkwɪzɪʃ(ə)n ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a shipboard system used to detect, identify, and locate a target with sufficient detail for effective weapon use
system for detecting and identifying targets

Examples

The new target acquisition system enabled the ship to detect incoming threats much faster than before.
Engineers are developing a more precise target acquisition system for use on modern naval vessels.
Accurate data from the target acquisition system is crucial for successful missile deployment.
