rat-licker

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætˌlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈrætˌlɪkə / (BrE)
A person who flatters or ingratiates themselves with others, especially for personal advantage; a sycophant.
sycophant; self-serving flatterer
informal slang

Examples

She realized too late that her new assistant was just another rat-licker hoping to impress the boss.
His colleagues dismissed him as a rat-licker because he always agreed with management, no matter what.
Nobody trusted the rat-licker in meetings, knowing he just wanted a promotion.

Synset Examples

I can't stand that rat-licker always flattering the manager.
He's just a rat-licker trying to climb the corporate ladder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - A person who acts obsequiously toward someone important in order to gain advantage
  • Synonym: toady - A person who behaves obsequiously to someone important
  • Synonym: flatterer - A person who compliments others insincerely for personal gain
  • Antonym: critic - A person who expresses disapproval rather than praise
