genus Tilia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtɪliə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtɪlia / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees with smooth, usually silver-grey bark, including lime trees, lindens, and basswoods, native to North America, Europe, and Asia.
genus of lime trees and lindens

Examples

The genus Tilia includes many species commonly known as lime trees or lindens.
Researchers examined leaves from different trees within the genus Tilia to study their morphology.
The genus Tilia is valued for its ornamental qualities and shade in parks and gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linden - deciduous tree of the genus Tilia
  • Synonym: basswood - common name for North American species of Tilia

Etymology

Modern Latin *Tilia*, from classical Latin (lime tree).
