G agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒi ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
any of a group of non-persistent nerve agents, first synthesized before or during World War II, that act as chemical weapons by disrupting nerve function
type of nerve agent (chemical weapon)
medical

Examples

Sarin is a well-known example of a G agent developed during World War II.
Protective clothing is necessary when handling any G agent due to their extreme toxicity.
The military stored large quantities of G agents in secure facilities until their destruction.

Synset Examples

GB was the only G agent that was fielded by the US as a munition, specifically in rockets, aerial bombs, and artillery shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve agent - a toxic chemical weapon that disrupts the nervous system

Etymology

The letter 'G' from German origin, after Dr. Gerhard Schrader, who developed them.
