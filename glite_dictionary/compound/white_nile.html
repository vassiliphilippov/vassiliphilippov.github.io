White Nile

1. PROPN

/ waɪt ˈnaɪl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈnaɪl / (BrE)
the western main headstream of the Nile River, which flows from Lake Victoria and joins the Blue Nile at Khartoum to form the Nile
western headstream of the Nile River

Examples

The White Nile passes through Uganda and South Sudan before reaching Sudan.
At Khartoum, the White Nile merges with the Blue Nile to form the main Nile River.
Many early explorers aimed to find the source of the White Nile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Blue Nile - the eastern headstream of the Nile River, joining the White Nile at Khartoum
  • Degree Higher: Nile - the major river in northeastern Africa, formed by the White and Blue Niles
