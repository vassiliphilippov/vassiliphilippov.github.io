kinetic energy

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the energy that a body possesses due to its motion
energy possessed due to motion

Examples

The moving car has more kinetic energy than a parked one.
When you throw a ball, its kinetic energy increases as it speeds up.
A roller coaster at the bottom of a hill has maximum kinetic energy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: potential energy - energy stored due to an object's position or state

Etymology

From Greek kīnētikos (of motion) + energy, from Greek energeia (activity, operation).

2. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the energy that an object possesses due to its motion
energy due to motion

Examples

The kinetic energy of the speeding car was converted into heat during braking.
Wind turbines capture the kinetic energy of moving air to generate electricity.
A falling ball gains kinetic energy as it accelerates toward the ground.

Synset Examples

The kinetic energy of the roller coaster thrilled everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: potential energy - energy stored in an object due to its position or condition

Etymology

From Greek 'kinesis' (movement) and 'energy', from Greek 'energeia' (activity).
