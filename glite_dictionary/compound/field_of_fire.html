field of fire

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːld əv ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌfiːld əv ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
the area that can be effectively covered by gunfire from a particular position
area covered by gunfire

Examples

The soldiers carefully selected their position to maximize their field of fire.
Obstacles such as trees and bushes reduced the machine gun's field of fire.
Each bunker was constructed to ensure a wide field of fire over the approach road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line of sight - a straight line along which an observer has unobstructed vision to a target
