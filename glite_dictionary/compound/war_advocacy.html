war advocacy

1. NOUN

/ wɔr ˈædvəkəsi / (AmE), / wɔː ˈædvəkəsi / (BrE)
support or promotion of war as a policy or course of action.
policy of supporting war

Examples

The senator's war advocacy was evident in his speeches supporting military intervention.
Public opinion has often shifted against war advocacy during times of prolonged conflict.
Her article critiqued the government's war advocacy and called for diplomatic solutions instead.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pacifism - the belief in opposition to war or violence as a means of settling disputes
