blue ribbon jury

1. NOUN

/ blu ˈrɪb.ən ˈdʒɝ.i / (AmE), / bluː ˈrɪb.ən ˈdʒʊə.ri / (BrE)
A jury selected for its members' special knowledge or expertise to decide complicated cases.
jury with special expertise for complex cases
legal

Examples

The high-profile corruption trial was decided by a blue ribbon jury with economic expertise.
Because the case involved complex financial instruments, a blue ribbon jury was assembled.
She advocated for a blue ribbon jury to ensure a fair and informed verdict in the scientific dispute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jury - a group chosen to give a verdict in a legal case

Etymology

From 'blue ribbon', denoting distinction or excellence, and 'jury', from Old French 'juree'.
