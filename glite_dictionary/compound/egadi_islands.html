Egadi Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪˈɡɑːdi ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˌɛˈɡɑːdi ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands off the west coast of Sicily in the Mediterranean Sea, known for their historical significance and natural beauty
island group off western Sicily

Examples

The Egadi Islands are a popular destination for travelers seeking quiet beaches and crystal-clear waters.
Ancient naval battles once took place near the Egadi Islands during the First Punic War.
We took a ferry from Trapani to visit the largest of the Egadi Islands, Favignana.

Etymology

From Italian 'Isole Egadi', possibly from Greek 'Aegates' meaning 'islands of goats'.
