Mexican swamp cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən swɑmp ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən swɒmp ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
Wood from the cypresses that grow in the river valleys of the Mexican highlands.
wood of Mexican highland cypresses

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Mexican swamp cypress, prized for its durability and rich color.
They exported Mexican swamp cypress logs for use in boat building.
Artisans prefer Mexican swamp cypress for intricate woodwork due to its fine grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Montezuma cypress - a species of cypress native to Mexico, also known as Taxodium mucronatum

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən swɑmp ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən swɒmp ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
a large cypress tree (Taxodium mucronatum) native to the river valleys of the Mexican highlands, often found growing along streams and lakeshores
cypress tree native to Mexican highlands

Examples

The ancient Mexican swamp cypress in Oaxaca is said to be over two thousand years old.
Botanists visited the river valley to study the rare Mexican swamp cypress species.
Many parks in central Mexico feature the towering Mexican swamp cypress along their lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Montezuma cypress - another name for the Mexican swamp cypress (Taxodium mucronatum)
