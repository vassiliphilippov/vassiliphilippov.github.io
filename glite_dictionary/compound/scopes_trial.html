Scopes trial

1. NOUN

/ skoʊps traɪəl / (AmE), / skəʊps traɪəl / (BrE)
the highly publicized 1925 American court case in which John T. Scopes was tried for teaching evolution in violation of Tennessee law
1925 US trial over teaching evolution in schools

Examples

The Scopes trial marked a turning point in the debate between science and religion in American education.
Books about the Scopes trial explore its impact on the teaching of evolution in schools.
Many historians consider the Scopes trial a pivotal event in the history of American legal and cultural conflict.

Etymology

Named after John T. Scopes, the defendant in the 1925 trial.
