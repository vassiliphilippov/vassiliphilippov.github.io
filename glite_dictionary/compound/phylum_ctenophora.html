phylum Ctenophora

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm tɪˈnɒfərə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm tɪˈnɒfərə / (BrE)
A small phylum of marine animals known as comb jellies or sea acorns, noted for their gelatinous bodies and rows of ciliary plates used for locomotion.
phylum of marine comb jellies

Examples

The phylum Ctenophora includes species commonly known as comb jellies found in oceans worldwide.
Researchers study the transparent bodies characteristic of animals in the phylum Ctenophora.
Unlike jellyfish, members of the phylum Ctenophora move by beating rows of cilia.

Etymology

From Greek 'ktenos' (comb) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to comb-like cilia.
