standard time

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd taɪm / (AmE), / ˈstændəd taɪm / (BrE)
The official time in a particular region, established by law or custom and based on the mean solar time of a standard meridian, used especially in contrast to daylight saving time.
official time zone for a region

Examples

Most countries switch from standard time to daylight saving time in the spring.
The train schedule is based on Eastern standard time, so check your clocks.
When it is noon standard time in New York, it is 9 a.m. in Los Angeles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: daylight saving time - time during which clocks are set ahead of standard time, usually by one hour
