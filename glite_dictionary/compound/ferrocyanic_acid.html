ferrocyanic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛroʊsaɪˈænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊsaɪˈænɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an unstable white acid (H4Fe(CN)6) derived from ferrocyanide salts, not usually found in pure form
a white unstable acid from ferrocyanide salts
medical

Examples

Researchers attempted to isolate ferrocyanic acid, but it decomposed rapidly under normal laboratory conditions.
The synthesis of ferrocyanic acid requires careful handling due to its instability.
Ferrocyanic acid is mainly of academic interest as it is derived from ferrocyanide salts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferrocyanide - a salt containing the complex anion Fe(CN)6^4−

Etymology

From 'ferrocyanide', itself from 'ferro-' (iron) and 'cyanide'.
