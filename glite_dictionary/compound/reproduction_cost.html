reproduction cost

1. NOUN

/ ˌriprəˈdʌkʃən kɔst / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən kɒst / (BrE)
the amount needed to reproduce an exact duplicate of a property or asset, less allowances for factors like depreciation
cost to reproduce an asset or property

Examples

The appraiser calculated the reproduction cost of the building to determine its insurance value.
Unlike replacement cost, reproduction cost estimates the expense to create an exact replica of the original asset.
Changes in materials and labor can significantly affect the reproduction cost of historical properties.
