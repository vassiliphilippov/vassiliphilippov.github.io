offset printing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌsɛt ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a printing technique in which an inked image is transferred from a plate to a rubber blanket and then to the printing surface, typically paper.
a printing method using a rubber blanket transfer

Examples

The magazine was produced using offset printing for higher image quality.
Many newspapers rely on offset printing to release their daily editions.
Advancements in offset printing have made full-color publications more affordable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lithography - printing process using a flat stone or metal plate, precursor to offset printing

Etymology

From 'offset', meaning to transfer, and 'printing'; describes the intermediate transfer process in the technique.
