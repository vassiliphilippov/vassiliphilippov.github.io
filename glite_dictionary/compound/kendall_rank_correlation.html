Kendall rank correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛndəl ræŋk kəˌrleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɛndəl ræŋk kəˌlɪəʃən / (BrE)
A nonparametric statistic used to measure the strength and direction of association between two ranked variables.
statistical measure of association between rankings

Examples

Researchers used the Kendall rank correlation to assess the agreement between the two judges’ rankings.
The Kendall rank correlation is preferred when the data do not meet the assumptions of parametric tests.
She calculated the Kendall rank correlation to determine the relationship between employees’ performance and their seniority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spearman rank correlation - another nonparametric measure of rank correlation

Etymology

Named after British statistician Maurice G. Kendall (1907–1983), who introduced the method.
