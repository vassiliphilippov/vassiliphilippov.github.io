word deafness

1. NOUN

/ wɝːd ˈdɛfnəs / (AmE), / wɜːd ˈdɛfnəs / (BrE)
a condition in which a person has difficulty understanding spoken language, not caused by hearing loss
impaired understanding of spoken language (not hearing loss)
medical

Examples

After his stroke, the patient developed word deafness and could no longer comprehend speech.
Although her hearing was normal, she was diagnosed with word deafness after struggling to follow conversations.
Word deafness can result from damage to specific areas of the brain responsible for language processing.
