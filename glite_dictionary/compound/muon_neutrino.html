muon neutrino

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːɑn nuˈtriːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmjuːɒn njuːˈtriːnəʊ / (BrE)
A subatomic particle that is a type of neutrino associated with the muon, having extremely low mass and no electric charge.
type of neutrino related to muons
medical

Examples

The discovery of the muon neutrino provided crucial evidence for the existence of multiple types of neutrinos.
Scientists detected the presence of a muon neutrino by observing its interaction with other subatomic particles.
Research on the muon neutrino has deepened our understanding of particle physics and the Standard Model.

Synset Examples

In 1962, Leon M. Lederman, Melvin Schwartz and Jack Steinberger showed that more than one type of neutrino exists by first detecting interactions of the muon neutrino (already hypothesised with the name neutretto), which earned them the 1988 Nobel Prize in Physics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electron neutrino - the neutrino associated with the electron
  • Degree Higher: tau neutrino - the neutrino associated with the tau particle
  • Synonym: neutrino - a neutral subatomic particle with very low mass

Etymology

'Muon' from 'mu meson' and 'neutrino' from Italian, diminutive of neutro (neutral).
