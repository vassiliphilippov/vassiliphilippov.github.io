greater pichiciego

1. NOUN

/ ˈgreɪtər ˌpɪtʃiˈdʒiːgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˌpɪtʃɪˈdʒiːgəʊ / (BrE)
A rare burrowing armadillo (Calyptophractus retusus) native to southern South America, known for its pinkish armor and subterranean habits.
a burrowing armadillo of southern South America

Examples

The greater pichiciego spends most of its life underground, making it difficult to observe in the wild.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the greater pichiciego in Argentina and Paraguay.
Unlike its smaller relative, the greater pichiciego is rarely seen due to its secretive habits.

Etymology

From Spanish 'pichiciego', a name used in South America for certain small armadillos.
