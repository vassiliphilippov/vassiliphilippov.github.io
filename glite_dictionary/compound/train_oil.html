train oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪn oʊl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪn ɔɪl / (BrE)
a white to brown oil obtained from the blubber of whales, formerly used for lighting lamps and making soap
oil from whale blubber, used for lighting

Examples

Centuries ago, train oil was a crucial commodity for lighting homes and streets.
The production of train oil declined sharply with the advent of kerosene.
Artisans sometimes used train oil in the manufacture of fine soaps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale oil - oil obtained from whales, used for lighting and lubrication

Etymology

From Dutch 'traan' (tear, drop of oil), referring to oil obtained by rendering blubber.
