lyme grass

1. NOUN

/ laɪm ɡræs / (AmE), / laɪm ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A coarse perennial grass of the genus Elymus, commonly found on sandy coasts and dunes.
a coastal grass of the genus Elymus

Examples

The dunes were stabilized by planting lyme grass along the shore.
Lyme grass thrives in sandy, salty soils where few other plants can survive.
Conservationists use lyme grass to prevent coastal erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand reed - Another grass of sandy areas, also known as Leymus arenarius
