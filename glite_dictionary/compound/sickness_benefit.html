sickness benefit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪknəs ˌbɛnəfɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪknɪs ˌbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
money paid by the government to someone who is unable to work due to illness
government payment for those unable to work due to illness

Examples

After breaking his leg, David applied for sickness benefit while he was unable to work.
Many people are unaware that they may qualify for sickness benefit during long-term illness.
The government recently changed the eligibility criteria for sickness benefit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disability benefit - payment made to those unable to work due to disability
