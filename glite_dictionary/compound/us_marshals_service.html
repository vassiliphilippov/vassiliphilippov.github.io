US Marshals Service

1. PROPN

/ ju ɛs ˈmɑrʃəlz ˌsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / juː ɛs ˈmɑːʃəlz ˌsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
the United States' oldest federal law enforcement agency, responsible for protecting the federal judiciary, transporting federal prisoners, protecting federal witnesses, managing seized assets, and ensuring the operation of the federal judicial system
U.S. federal law enforcement agency
american

Examples

The US Marshals Service played a crucial role in apprehending the escaped convict.
Members of the US Marshals Service are often responsible for transporting high-risk federal prisoners.
The US Marshals Service works closely with other agencies to protect federal judges and witnesses.
