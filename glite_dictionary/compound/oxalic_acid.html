oxalic acid

1. NOUN

/ ɑgˈzælɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɒgˈzælɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a toxic, colorless, crystalline organic acid found in certain plants, often used as a bleach, rust remover, and in chemical analysis
a toxic organic acid used as bleach and rust remover

Examples

The leaves of rhubarb contain high levels of oxalic acid, which can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
Oxalic acid is commonly used to remove rust stains from metal surfaces and household items.
Laboratories may use oxalic acid in various chemical analyses and cleaning procedures.

Etymology

From Latin *oxalis* (sorrel), the plant from which it was first isolated, combined with 'acid'.
