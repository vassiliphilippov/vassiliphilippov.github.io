Persian melon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜːrʒən ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃ(ə)n ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
a variety of muskmelon with a smooth white rind and white or greenish flesh, lacking the typical musky aroma
type of muskmelon with pale, non-musky flesh

Examples

I bought a ripe Persian melon at the farmers' market for dessert.
This salad features slices of Persian melon with feta and mint.
Unlike cantaloupe, a Persian melon has a smoother rind and a milder aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskmelon - a melon with sweet, aromatic flesh, such as cantaloupe

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrʒən ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
A large, oblong green melon with orange flesh, belonging to a variety of winter melon vine, known for its sweet flavor.
a type of large green melon with orange flesh

Examples

She bought a Persian melon from the market to serve at the summer picnic.
The Persian melon is prized for its sweet, fragrant orange flesh.
After slicing the Persian melon, the vibrant color brightened up the fruit platter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - a large, round fruit with sweet flesh and a hard rind, such as watermelon or cantaloupe

Etymology

Persian, indicating origin or association with Persia (modern-day Iran), referring to the region where the melon variety was cultivated.
