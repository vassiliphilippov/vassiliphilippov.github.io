ortolan bunting

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.təl.ən ˈbʌn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔː.təl.ən ˈbʌn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a small brownish songbird (Emberiza hortulana) native to Europe and western Asia, sometimes eaten as a delicacy
a small Old World songbird, delicacy in gastronomy

Examples

The ortolan bunting has been prized for its flavor in French cuisine despite conservation concerns.
Birdwatchers traveled to southern Europe to catch a glimpse of the rare ortolan bunting.
Traditionally, the ortolan bunting was consumed whole, often in a secretive ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a small seed-eating songbird of the family Emberizidae

Etymology

French 'ortolan', from Italian 'ortolano', literally 'gardener' (referring to the bird's habitat); 'bunting' from Old English 'bunting'.
