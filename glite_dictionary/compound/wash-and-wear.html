wash-and-wear

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɑʃ ən ˈwɛr / (AmE), / ˌwɒʃ ən ˈweə / (BrE)
A fabric that is treated so it can be washed and worn without the need for ironing.
fabric that needs no ironing

Examples

Many travelers prefer wash-and-wear clothing to save time on ironing during their trips.
The new shirt is made from a wash-and-wear blend that stays crisp all day.
With wash-and-wear fabrics, doing laundry became much easier for busy families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanent press - fabric treated to resist wrinkles and require little or no ironing
