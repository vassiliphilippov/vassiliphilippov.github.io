Nile crocodile

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪl ˌkrɑː.kəˌdaɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪl ˌkrɒk.əˌdaɪl / (BrE)
a large, aggressive species of crocodile native to freshwater habitats in Africa
large African crocodile species

Examples

The Nile crocodile can reach over five meters in length and is one of Africa's most feared predators.
Researchers observed a group of Nile crocodiles basking on the riverbank in Botswana.
Despite their fearsome reputation, Nile crocodiles play an important role in river ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile with a long snout and sharp teeth

Etymology

Named after the Nile River, where the species is commonly found; 'crocodile' from Greek 'krokodilos'.
