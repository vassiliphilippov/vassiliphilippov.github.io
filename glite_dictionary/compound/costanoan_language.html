Costanoan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊstəˈnoʊ.ən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkɒstəˈnəʊ.ən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the indigenous Penutian language spoken by the Costanoan people of central California
Penutian language of the Costanoan people

Examples

The revival of the Costanoan language is an important cultural project for descendants of the tribe.
Researchers have documented several dialects of the Costanoan language using old recordings and written notes.
Few fluent speakers of the Costanoan language remain today, but linguistic efforts continue.

Etymology

From Spanish 'costano' (coast dweller), referring to the coastal location of the people.
