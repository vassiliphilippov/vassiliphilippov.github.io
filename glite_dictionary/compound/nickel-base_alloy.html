nickel-base alloy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkəl beɪs ˈælɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl beɪs ˈalɔɪ / (BrE)
An alloy in which nickel is the principal element, often used for its resistance to heat and corrosion.
alloy mainly composed of nickel

Examples

The turbine blades are made of a nickel-base alloy to withstand extreme temperatures.
Researchers are developing a new nickel-base alloy for improved corrosion resistance in marine environments.
The properties of each nickel-base alloy determine its suitability for various industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickel - a silvery-white metallic element
  • Synonym: nickel-bronze - an alloy of nickel and bronze
