bride's bonnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪdz ˌbɑnɪt / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdz ˌbɒnɪt / (BrE)
a North American plant bearing one or two white starlike flowers on short, leafless stalks, found from Alaska to California and east to Oregon and Montana
North American plant with starlike white flowers

Examples

We spotted a bride's bonnet growing near the edge of the forest trail.
In spring, the bride's bonnet blooms with delicate white blossoms.
Hikers in Montana often look for the rare bride's bonnet among the wildflowers.
