Orange Order

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒ ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A Protestant fraternal organization founded in Northern Ireland, noted for its political and cultural influence and annual parades celebrating Protestant and unionist identity.
Protestant fraternal group, mainly in Northern Ireland

Examples

The Orange Order organizes annual marches commemorating historical events in Northern Ireland.
Many political discussions in Belfast have referenced the influence of the Orange Order.
Membership in the Orange Order remains significant in some Protestant communities today.

Etymology

Named after William of Orange, the Protestant king celebrated by the Order, founded in 1795.
