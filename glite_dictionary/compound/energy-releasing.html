energy-releasing

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi rɪˈlisɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi rɪˈliːsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a process) involving or resulting in the release of energy, especially in biological or chemical systems.
releasing energy (esp. in biology/chemistry)

Examples

Cellular respiration is an energy-releasing process that provides fuel for cellular functions.
Foods undergo energy-releasing reactions during digestion in the body.
Photosynthesis differs from energy-releasing reactions because it stores rather than releases energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catabolic - of or relating to catabolism, a metabolic process that breaks down molecules and releases energy

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛnərdʒi rɪˌlisɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi rɪˌliːsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a process or reaction) resulting in or accompanied by the release of energy, especially in scientific contexts such as nuclear reactions.
causing energy to be released

Examples

The scientist explained how an energy-releasing reaction powers the sun.
Nuclear fission is a classic example of an energy-releasing process.
The explosion was triggered by a sudden energy-releasing event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exothermic - (of a chemical reaction) accompanied by the release of heat
