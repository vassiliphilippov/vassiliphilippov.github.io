Caddoan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædoʊ.ən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkædəʊ.ən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of Native American languages historically spoken in the Midwest and Southern Plains of the United States by the Caddo people and related groups
Native American language family

Examples

The Caddoan language family includes Caddo, Wichita, and Pawnee among others.
Linguists study the Caddoan language family to understand the culture and migration of Native American tribes.
Many Caddoan language speakers now work to revitalize their ancestral tongues through education initiatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caddo - a member of a Native American people; also their language, a member of the Caddoan family

Etymology

From Caddo, the name of a Native American people, plus '-an'.
