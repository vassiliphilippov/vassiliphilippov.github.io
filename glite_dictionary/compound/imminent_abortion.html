imminent abortion

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmɪnənt əˈbɔɹʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪnənt əˈbɔːʃən / (BrE)
the appearance of symptoms indicating that a miscarriage is likely to occur soon
symptoms signaling impending miscarriage
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited signs of imminent abortion during her second trimester.
Doctors closely monitored her for symptoms of imminent abortion to respond quickly to any complications.
Prompt medical attention is crucial if imminent abortion is suspected in early pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threatened abortion - bleeding in early pregnancy with or without pain, not necessarily leading to miscarriage

Etymology

From Latin imminens (overhanging, impending) + Late Latin abortio (miscarriage).
