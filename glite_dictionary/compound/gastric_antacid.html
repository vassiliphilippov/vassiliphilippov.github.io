gastric antacid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrɪk ænˈtæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk ænˈtasɪd / (BrE)
a substance that neutralizes stomach acid, used to relieve indigestion or heartburn
medicine that neutralizes stomach acid
medical

Examples

After eating spicy food, Sarah took a gastric antacid to calm her upset stomach.
Doctors often recommend a gastric antacid for patients suffering from heartburn.
He always keeps a packet of gastric antacid tablets in his briefcase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antacid - a substance that counteracts or neutralizes acidity

Etymology

From Greek *gastēr* (stomach) and Latin *antacidum* (counteracting acid).
