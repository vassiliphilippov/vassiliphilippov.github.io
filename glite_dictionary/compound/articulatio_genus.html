articulatio genus

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃioʊ ˈdʒiːnəs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃɪəʊ ˈdʒiːnəs / (BrE)
The hinge joint in the human leg that connects the tibia and fibula with the femur and is protected in front by the patella; the knee joint.
the knee joint (anatomy)
medical

Examples

An injury to the articulatio genus can greatly affect a person's ability to walk.
Medical students must learn the structures that support the articulatio genus.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the ligaments of the articulatio genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knee joint - the joint between the thigh and the lower leg

Etymology

From Latin: 'articulatio' (joint) + 'genus' (knee).
