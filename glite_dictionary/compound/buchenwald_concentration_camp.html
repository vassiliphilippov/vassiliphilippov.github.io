Buchenwald concentration camp

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːkənˌwɑld ˌkɑnsənˈtreɪʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˈbuːkənˌvald ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən kamp / (BrE)
a Nazi concentration camp located in central Germany during World War II, primarily used for the imprisonment and murder of Jews and other groups
Nazi concentration camp in central Germany (WWII)

Examples

Many prisoners died at Buchenwald concentration camp due to inhumane conditions.
The liberation of Buchenwald concentration camp was a turning point in revealing Nazi atrocities to the world.
Survivors of Buchenwald concentration camp have shared harrowing testimonies of their experiences.

Etymology

From German 'Buchenwald' meaning 'beech forest'; the camp was named for its location.
