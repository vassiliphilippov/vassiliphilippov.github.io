dickie-seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki ˌsit / (AmE), / ˈdɪki ˌsiːt / (BrE)
a small extra seat that folds out from the back of an old-fashioned two-seater car, typically for a third passenger
fold-out rear seat in vintage cars
british

Examples

The old roadster had a dickie-seat that could accommodate an extra passenger on sunny days.
He restored the vintage car, making sure the dickie-seat was just as comfortable as the front seats.
In the 1930s, children loved to ride in the dickie-seat during family outings.

Etymology

From British English 'dickey', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 19th-century slang for a carriage seat.
