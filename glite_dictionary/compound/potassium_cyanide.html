potassium cyanide

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtæsiəm saɪˈænaɪd / (AmE), / pəˈtæsiəm saɪˈənaɪd / (BrE)
a highly poisonous white crystalline salt (KCN) used in electroplating, photography, and as a chemical reagent
a poisonous white chemical compound (KCN)

Examples

Lab workers handle potassium cyanide with extreme caution due to its high toxicity.
Potassium cyanide is used in some industrial processes, including gold extraction and electroplating.
The symptoms of potassium cyanide poisoning can appear within seconds of exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanide - a chemical compound containing the cyano group, often highly toxic

Etymology

From 'potassium,' a metallic element, and 'cyanide,' from Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue), referring to compounds containing the cyano group.
