sleight of hand

1. NOUN

/ slaɪt əv hænd / (AmE), / slaɪt əv hand / (BrE)
skillful and deceptive use of the hands, especially in performing magic tricks or illusions
manual dexterity for tricks or illusions

Examples

The magician's sleight of hand left the audience utterly amazed.
He stole the watch using a remarkable sleight of hand technique.
Mastering sleight of hand requires patience, practice, and a sharp eye for detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legerdemain - skilful use of one's hands when performing tricks
  • Antonym: clumsiness - lack of skill or dexterity with the hands

Etymology

From Middle English 'sleight' (cunning, skill), from Old Norse 'slœgð' (cleverness).
