lager beer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːɡər bɪr / (AmE), / ˈlɑːɡə bɪə / (BrE)
A type of beer made with bottom-fermenting yeast, usually matured at low temperatures for a crisp, clean taste.
beer made using bottom-fermenting yeast

Examples

He ordered a pint of lager beer to go with his meal.
The brewery specializes in traditional lager beer brewed according to German methods.
Cold lager beer is especially refreshing on a hot summer day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ale - beer made with top-fermenting yeast

Etymology

From German 'Lager' (storage), referring to the beer's traditional maturation process in cool storage.
