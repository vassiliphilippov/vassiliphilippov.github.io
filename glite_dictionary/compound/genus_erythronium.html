genus Erythronium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɛr.ɪˈθroʊ.ni.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɛr.ɪˈθrəʊ.nɪ.əm / (BrE)
a genus of perennial bulbous herbs found in most of northern United States, including species known as dogtooth violet, adder's tongue, trout lily, and fawn lily.
a genus of bulbous perennial herbs

Examples

The genus Erythronium includes several well-known wildflowers, such as the dogtooth violet and trout lily.
Botanists have long studied the genus Erythronium for its unique bulb structure and diverse flower forms.
During our hike, we spotted a rare species belonging to the genus Erythronium blooming along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - common name for various species within genus Erythronium
  • Synonym: adder's tongue - another common name for some species within genus Erythronium
  • Synonym: trout lily - a North American species of Erythronium with mottled leaves

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' meaning 'red', referencing the reddish coloring in some species.
