Queen of England

1. NOUN

/ kwin əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / kwiːn əv ˈɪŋlənd / (BrE)
the female sovereign ruler of England; a queen who reigns as the monarch of England.
female sovereign monarch of England

Examples

Queen Elizabeth II held the title of Queen of England as part of her reign.
During the Tudor period, Mary I became Queen of England after the death of her brother.
Anne Boleyn was crowned Queen of England in 1533.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: King of England - the male sovereign ruler of England
  • Synonym: queen - a female monarch or the wife of a king
