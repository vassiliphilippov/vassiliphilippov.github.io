torpedo-boat destroyer

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˈpidoʊ boʊt dɪˈstrɔɪər / (AmE), / tɔːˈpiːdəʊ bəʊt dɪˈstrɔɪə / (BrE)
an early type of small warship developed to defend against and destroy torpedo boats, serving as the predecessor of the modern destroyer
historical small naval warship, forerunner of destroyer

Examples

The torpedo-boat destroyer played a crucial role in naval battles at the turn of the twentieth century.
Many navies quickly adopted the torpedo-boat destroyer to counter the threat posed by fast enemy torpedo boats.
The museum's oldest ship is a restored torpedo-boat destroyer from the early 1900s.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: destroyer - a fast, maneuverable warship; modern development of the torpedo-boat destroyer

Etymology

From 'torpedo-boat' and 'destroyer', describing the ship's role in combating torpedo boats.
