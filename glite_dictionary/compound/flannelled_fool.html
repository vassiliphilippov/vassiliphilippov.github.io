flannelled fool

1. NOUN

/ ˈflænəld ˈful / (AmE), / ˈflænəld ˈfuːl / (BrE)
a foolish person, often characterized by casual or unthinking behavior, sometimes referring to one dressed in flannel, especially in a traditional or stereotypical British context
foolish person, often in flannel clothing
british

Examples

Don't be a flannelled fool; think before you speak.
He was dismissed as just another flannelled fool at the club.
The media often mock politicians as flannelled fools for their out-of-touch remarks.

Synset Examples

Don't be a flannelled fool; think before you speak.
She called him a flannelled fool for his careless remarks.
He acted like a flannelled fool during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently
  • Synonym: simpleton - a person lacking in common sense; a fool

Etymology

Popularized by Rudyard Kipling in 19th century Britain, referring to amateur cricketers who wore flannel clothing.
