atomic warhead

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ˈwɔrˌhɛd / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈwɔːˌhɛd / (BrE)
the explosive part of a missile or bomb designed to deliver a nuclear (atom) bomb
warhead containing a nuclear bomb

Examples

The missile was fitted with an atomic warhead capable of devastating an entire city.
Intelligence reports indicated that the country was developing an atomic warhead for its new missile system.
During the Cold War, the threat of an atomic warhead attack was a constant concern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear warhead - warhead containing a nuclear (atomic or hydrogen) bomb
  • Antonym: conventional warhead - a non-nuclear explosive warhead
