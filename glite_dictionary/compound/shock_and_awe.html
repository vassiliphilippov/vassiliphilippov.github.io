shock and awe

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑk ənd ɔ / (AmE), / ʃɒk ənd ɔː / (BrE)
a strategy or effect characterized by the use of overwhelming force or spectacular action intended to astonish, intimidate, or impress
use of overwhelming force to impress or intimidate

Examples

The government launched a shock and awe campaign to win public support for the new policy.
The advertising team's shock and awe approach captured the attention of the entire industry.
The general believed a display of shock and awe would force the enemy to surrender quickly.

Synset Examples

His speech was designed to create shock and awe among listeners.
The performance left the audience in shock and awe.
The team's shock and awe tactics surprised their opponents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to defeat or affect strongly; to overpower
  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or overawe, especially in order to make someone do what one wants

Etymology

Originated in U.S. military strategy, especially from the phrase popularized during the Iraq War (2003).
