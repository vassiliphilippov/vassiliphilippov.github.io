optical lens

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl lɛnz / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl lɛnz / (BrE)
a lens made of transparent material that focuses or disperses light, especially in devices such as cameras or microscopes
lens focusing or dispersing light in devices

Examples

The optical lens on this camera allows for sharp, high-quality images even in low light.
Be careful not to scratch the optical lens when cleaning your microscope.
Advancements in optical lens technology have improved smartphone camera performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens - transparent optical device that focuses or disperses light rays

Etymology

From 'optical' (relating to sight or light) + 'lens' (borrowed from Latin 'lens' meaning lentil, due to its shape).
