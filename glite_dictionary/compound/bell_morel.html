bell morel

1. NOUN

/ bɛl məˈrɛl / (AmE), / bɛl məˈrɛl / (BrE)
A type of morel mushroom whose cap is bell-shaped and attached to the stem only at the top.
bell-shaped morel mushroom

Examples

The chef used a bell morel to add a rich, earthy flavor to the dish.
Foragers must distinguish the bell morel from similar-looking but toxic mushrooms.
The distinctive cap shape makes the bell morel easier to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible mushroom with a honeycombed cap
