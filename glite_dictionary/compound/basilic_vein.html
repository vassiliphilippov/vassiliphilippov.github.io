basilic vein

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪl.ɪk veɪn / (AmE), / bəˈsɪl.ɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a superficial vein that drains blood from the back of the hand and forearm, emptying into the axillary vein
vein in the arm draining into the axillary vein
medical

Examples

The nurse located the basilic vein before inserting the intravenous line.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the basilic vein.
Phlebotomists sometimes use the basilic vein for blood draws if other veins are inaccessible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalic vein - a superficial vein of the arm running along the lateral side

Etymology

From Latin 'basilica' (royal, principal), referring to its prominence among veins.
