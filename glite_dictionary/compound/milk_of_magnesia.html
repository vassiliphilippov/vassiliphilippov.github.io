milk of magnesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlk əv mæɡˈniːʒə / (AmE), / ˈmɪlk əv mæɡˈniːziə / (BrE)
a milky white liquid suspension of magnesium hydroxide, used chiefly as a laxative and an antacid
magnesium hydroxide suspension used as laxative and antacid
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended milk of magnesia to relieve her occasional constipation.
Many people use milk of magnesia as an over-the-counter antacid for heartburn.
Always read the instructions before giving milk of magnesia to children.

Etymology

Named for its milky appearance; 'magnesia' from magnesium hydroxide, its primary ingredient.
