law of nature

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
a general principle in science or philosophy that describes consistent and universal relationships observed in the natural world
principle describing natural phenomena

Examples

The law of nature states that energy cannot be created or destroyed.
Many scientists have dedicated their lives to discovering a new law of nature.
Newton's laws are classic examples of a law of nature in physics.

Synset Examples

the laws of thermodynamics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural law - a philosophical theory that certain rights or values are inherent by virtue of human nature
