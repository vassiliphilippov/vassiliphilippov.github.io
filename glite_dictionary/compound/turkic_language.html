Turkic language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrkɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a member of a group of related languages constituting a major subfamily of the Altaic language family, including Turkish, Uzbek, Kazakh, and others
language group including Turkish and related languages

Examples

Uzbek is a Turkic language spoken by millions in Central Asia.
The Turkic language family includes Turkish, Kazakh, and Kyrgyz, among others.
He specialized in the historical development of the Turkic language group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish - the most widely spoken Turkic language, official in Turkey
  • Degree Higher: Altaic language - a hypothesized macro-family including Turkic, Mongolic, and Tungusic languages

Etymology

From Turkic, relating to the Turkic peoples and languages; ultimately from Old Turkic Türk.
