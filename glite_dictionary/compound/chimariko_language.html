Chimariko language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈmɑːrɪkoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈmɑːrɪkəʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language of the Chimariko people, formerly spoken in northern California and classified as part of the Hokan language family
extinct language of the Chimariko people

Examples

Though no native speakers remain, the Chimariko language has been partially documented by linguists.
The Chimariko language was once spoken along the Trinity River in northern California.
He wrote his thesis on the grammar of the Chimariko language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hokan - a proposed family of American Indian languages of California and Mexico

Etymology

Named after the Chimariko people of northern California.
