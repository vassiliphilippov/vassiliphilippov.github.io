emperor moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpərər mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈɛmpərə mɒθ / (BrE)
a large moth native to temperate forests of Eurasia, distinguished by its heavily scaled, transparent wings
large Eurasian moth with transparent wings

Examples

The striking pattern on the wings of the emperor moth makes it easy to identify in the wild.
Each spring, naturalists eagerly await the emergence of the emperor moth from its cocoon.
A photographer managed to capture a close-up image of a resting emperor moth on a tree trunk.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: moth - insect related to the butterfly, generally nocturnal
