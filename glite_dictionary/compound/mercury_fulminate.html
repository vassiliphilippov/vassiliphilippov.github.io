mercury fulminate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrkjəri ˈfʊlmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊri ˈfʊlmɪneɪt / (BrE)
a highly sensitive explosive compound, Hg(CNO)₂, used as a primary detonator in blasting caps and percussion caps
explosive compound used in detonators

Examples

Mercury fulminate is used as a primary explosive in percussion caps for firearms.
The sensitivity of mercury fulminate makes it dangerous to handle without strict safety precautions.
Early blasting caps relied on mercury fulminate to initiate larger explosive charges.

Etymology

From 'mercury' (element) and 'fulminate' (from Latin 'fulminatus', struck by lightning).
