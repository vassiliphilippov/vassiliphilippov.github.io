soured cream

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊərd ˈkɹim / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəd ˈkɹiːm / (BrE)
Cream that has been fermented by lactic acid bacteria to give it a slightly tart flavor and thicker consistency; often used in cooking and as a condiment.
fermented cream with a tart flavor

Examples

She topped her baked potato with a generous spoonful of soured cream.
The recipe calls for soured cream to add richness and tang to the cake.
In the UK, soured cream is often served with chives as a dip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour cream - fermented cream with a tart flavor (common US term)
