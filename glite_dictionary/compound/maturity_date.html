maturity date

1. NOUN

/ məˈtʃʊr.ə.ti deɪt / (AmE), / məˈtʃʊə.rə.ti deɪt / (BrE)
the date on which a financial obligation or instrument, such as a bond or loan, must be repaid in full
date when debt or investment is due for repayment

Examples

The bond's maturity date is set for July 1, 2030, when investors will be repaid.
You must pay the full amount by the maturity date to avoid penalties.
As the maturity date approaches, the value of the investment may fluctuate less.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due date - the date when something, especially a payment, is due
