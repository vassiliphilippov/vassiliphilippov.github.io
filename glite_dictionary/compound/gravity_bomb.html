gravity bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævəti bɑm / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪti bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb released from an aircraft that reaches its target simply by falling under gravity, not by guidance systems
unguided aerial bomb

Examples

The plane released a gravity bomb over the target during the mission.
Many World War II munitions were gravity bombs, lacking any form of guidance system.
Unlike guided munitions, a gravity bomb relies solely on physics to reach its destination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guided bomb - a bomb equipped with a guidance system for precision targeting
