bad debt

1. NOUN

/ bæd dɛt / (AmE), / bæd det / (BrE)
A debt that is unlikely to be recovered or repaid, typically because the borrower is unable or unwilling to pay.
debt unlikely to be repaid

Examples

The company wrote off several bad debts at the end of the financial year.
After months of nonpayment, the account was classified as a bad debt.
Banks try to minimize their exposure to bad debt through careful risk assessment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncollectible - a debt that cannot be collected
