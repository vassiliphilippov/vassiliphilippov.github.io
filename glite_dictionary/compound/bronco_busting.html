bronco busting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːŋkoʊ ˌbʌstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋkəʊ ˌbʌstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of breaking and training a wild horse (bronco) to accept a saddle and rider
the taming of wild broncos for riding

Examples

The rodeo featured exciting bronco busting demonstrations by skilled cowboys.
He spent several years bronco busting on a Texas ranch before becoming a trainer.
Stories of old Western bronco busting adventures are popular in American folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rode breaking - the wider activity of training horses to be ridden

Etymology

From American Spanish 'bronco' (rough, unbroken horse) + English 'busting' (breaking).
