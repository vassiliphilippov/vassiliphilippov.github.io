digital-analog converter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪt̬l ˈænəˌlɔɡ kənˈvɝːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtl ˈænəlɒɡ kənˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
a device that converts digital signals into analogue signals, typically used in audio and video equipment
device converting digital to analogue signals

Examples

The digital-analog converter allows your music player to send audio to standard headphones.
High-quality audio equipment often features a built-in digital-analog converter for improved sound clarity.
Engineers tested the digital-analog converter to ensure accurate signal transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digital-to-analog converter - variant spelling and form of digital-analog converter

Etymology

Formed from 'digital', 'analog', and 'converter', referring to conversion between digital (numeric) and analogue (continuous) signals.
