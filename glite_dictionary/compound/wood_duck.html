wood duck

1. NOUN

/ wʊd dʌk / (AmE), / wʊd dʌk / (BrE)
A brightly colored North American duck, known for nesting in hollow trees, especially in wooded areas near water.
showy North American duck that nests in trees

Examples

We spotted a wood duck swimming quietly among the reeds near the pond.
A male wood duck has striking iridescent feathers and red eyes.
Conservationists work to preserve the habitats favored by the wood duck in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandarin duck - a colorful East Asian duck, similar in appearance to the wood duck
