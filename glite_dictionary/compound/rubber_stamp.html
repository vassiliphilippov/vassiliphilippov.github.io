rubber stamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbər stæmp / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə stæmp / (BrE)
a stamp made of rubber, used to imprint a mark, signature, or design by hand
handheld stamp for imprinting marks

Examples

The secretary quickly pressed the rubber stamp onto the ink pad and stamped the document.
It's easy to make return address labels with a personalized rubber stamp.
Please use the official rubber stamp when approving invoices.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbɚ ˌstæmp / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə ˌstæmp / (BrE)
automatic approval of an action or decision without proper consideration, often merely formal or symbolic
automatic or routine authorization

Examples

The board of directors acted as a rubber stamp for the CEO's decisions.
Many critics claim the committee has become little more than a rubber stamp for government policy.
Without real debate, the council was accused of serving as a rubber stamp for controversial projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formality - something done only for appearance or procedure
  • Synonym: approval - the act of officially agreeing to something
  • Antonym: scrutiny - careful examination or inspection

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌbər stæmp / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə stæmp / (BrE)
to approve or authorize something automatically or without proper consideration, often as a formality
approve automatically, without scrutiny

Examples

The committee refused to simply rubber stamp the proposal without discussion.
Some legislators accuse the council of rubber stamping all of the mayor's decisions.
She won't rubber stamp any policy that she hasn't personally reviewed.

Synset Examples

Don't expect me to rubber stamp your excuses.
He felt like a rubber stamp in the bureaucracy.
The manager won't just rubber stamp every request.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to approve - to officially agree to or accept as satisfactory
  • Synonym: to endorse - to declare one's public approval or support of
  • Antonym: to scrutinize - to examine or inspect closely and thoroughly

Etymology

Refers to actual rubber stamps used to quickly approve documents, metaphorically extended to automatic approval.
