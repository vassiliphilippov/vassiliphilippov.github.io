email address

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl əˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈiːˌmeɪl əˌdrɛs / (BrE)
A unique identifier in the form of a string (such as user@example.com) that allows a person to send and receive messages online via email.
unique identifier for email communication

Examples

Please provide your email address so we can send you the confirmation.
She changed her email address after receiving too much spam.
Each employee is assigned a unique email address upon joining the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email - a system for sending messages electronically

Etymology

Formed from 'email', itself from 'electronic mail', and 'address', meaning a location for communication.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl əˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈiːˌmeɪl əˌdrɛs / (BrE)
A unique identifier used to send and receive electronic mail, typically consisting of a username, the @ symbol, and a domain name.
unique identifier for sending/receiving email

Examples

Please provide your email address so we can send you the confirmation message.
I changed my email address after switching internet providers.
Her work email address is different from her personal one.

Synset Examples

She still has an email address from AOL.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email - electronic message sent over a computer network

Etymology

Formed from 'email' (electronic mail) and 'address' (location or identifier).

WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
