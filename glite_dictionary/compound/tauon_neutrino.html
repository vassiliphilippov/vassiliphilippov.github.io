tauon neutrino

1. NOUN

/ ˌtaʊ.ɒn nuˈtrinoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtaʊ.ɒn njuːˈtriːnəʊ / (BrE)
A type of neutrino associated with the tau lepton, one of the three known neutrinos in particle physics.
neutrino linked to the tau lepton
medical

Examples

The existence of the tauon neutrino was confirmed by experimental evidence in 2000.
In high-energy physics experiments, scientists study properties of the tauon neutrino to understand fundamental forces.
Detection of a tauon neutrino is extremely challenging due to its weak interaction with matter.

Synset Examples

When the third type of lepton, the tau, was discovered in 1975 at the Stanford Linear Accelerator Center, it too was expected to have an associated neutrino (the tau neutrino).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electron neutrino - neutrino associated with the electron
  • Synonym: muon neutrino - neutrino associated with the muon

Etymology

From Greek letter tau (symbol for tau lepton) and 'neutrino' (Italian, diminutive of 'neutro', meaning 'neutral').
