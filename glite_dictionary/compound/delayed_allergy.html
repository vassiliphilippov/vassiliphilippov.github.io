delayed allergy

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈleɪd ˈælɚdʒi / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪd ˈælədʒi / (BrE)
an allergic reaction that becomes noticeable only hours after exposure to the allergen
allergic reaction appearing hours after exposure
medical

Examples

He suffered from a delayed allergy to the medication, developing a rash several hours after taking it.
Some substances can trigger a delayed allergy, making it difficult to identify the cause immediately.
The doctor explained that her symptoms were due to a delayed allergy rather than an immediate reaction.
