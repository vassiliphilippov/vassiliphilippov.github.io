breach of contract

1. NOUN

/ briːtʃ əv ˈkɑntrækt / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
Failure to perform a duty or obligation required by a contract, regarded as a violation of the agreement.
failure to fulfill a contract
legal

Examples

The company was sued for a breach of contract after failing to deliver the goods on time.
A clear breach of contract occurred when he refused to pay for the services.
Employees must be aware of the consequences of breach of contract in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement
