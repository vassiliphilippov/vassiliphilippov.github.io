small-for-gestational-age infant

1. NOUN

/ smɔl fɚ ˌdʒɛstəˈneɪʃənl eɪdʒ ˈɪnfənt / (AmE), / smɔːl fə ˌdʒɛstəˈneɪʃənəl eɪdʒ ˈɪnfənt / (BrE)
a baby whose size and weight are significantly below the average for their gestational age
infant smaller than typical for gestational age
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that their baby was a small-for-gestational-age infant and required special monitoring.
Research shows that small-for-gestational-age infants may face higher risks of certain health problems.
Because she was a small-for-gestational-age infant, she stayed in the neonatal intensive care unit for two weeks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: large-for-gestational-age infant - an infant whose size and weight are significantly above the average for their gestational age
