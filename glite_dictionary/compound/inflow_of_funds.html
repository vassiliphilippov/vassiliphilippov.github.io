inflow of funds

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌfloʊ əv fʌndz / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fləʊ əv fʌndz / (BrE)
the movement of money or financial resources coming into an organization, system, or country
incoming money or resources

Examples

The company relied on a steady inflow of funds from international investors.
A sudden inflow of funds allowed the non-profit organization to expand its services.
Regulatory changes led to a substantial inflow of funds into the local market.

Synset Examples

We saw a significant inflow of funds last quarter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outflow of funds - the movement of money or resources leaving an organization or system
