quick trot

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk trɑt / (AmE), / kwɪk trɒt / (BrE)
A movement that is faster than a walk but slower than a run, usually steady and brisk.
fast-paced steady walk or run

Examples

He broke into a quick trot when he saw the train approaching.
The dog kept a quick trot alongside its owner during their morning exercise.
With a quick trot, she reached the classroom just before the bell rang.

Synset Examples

He gave a quick trot to catch the bus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: run - to move swiftly on foot
  • Degree Lower: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
