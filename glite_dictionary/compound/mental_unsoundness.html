mental unsoundness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntəl ʌnˈsaʊndnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛntəl ʌnˈsaʊndnəs / (BrE)
A state in which a person experiences mental disturbance or disorientation, often implying an inability to think or act normally.
state of mental disturbance or disorientation

Examples

The court questioned his mental unsoundness before allowing him to testify.
After the traumatic event, signs of mental unsoundness became apparent to his family.
Debilitating stress can sometimes lead to temporary mental unsoundness in even the healthiest individuals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind
  • Synonym: mental illness - any disorder that affects mental health
