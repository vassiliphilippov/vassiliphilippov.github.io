ice fog

1. NOUN

/ aɪs fɑɡ / (AmE), / aɪs fɒɡ / (BrE)
A dense winter fog consisting of suspended ice particles, typically occurring at very low temperatures.
dense winter fog of ice particles

Examples

In Alaska, ice fog can make driving extremely hazardous during the coldest months.
The airport was closed due to thick ice fog reducing visibility to near zero.
Researchers observed ice fog forming over the frozen river at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fog - thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the atmosphere
  • Degree Lower: mist - thin fog with reduced visibility
