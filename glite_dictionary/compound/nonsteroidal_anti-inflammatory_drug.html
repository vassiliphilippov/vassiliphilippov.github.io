nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˌstɛɹɔɪdəl ˌænti ɪnˈflæmətɔɹi drʌɡ / (AmE), / nɒnˌstɛəɹɔɪdəl ˌænti ɪnˈflæmətəri drʌɡ / (BrE)
A type of drug that reduces inflammation and pain, does not contain steroids, and is widely used to treat conditions such as arthritis or muscle injuries.
drug reducing inflammation, not a steroid
medical

Examples

Many people take nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs like ibuprofen for headaches and muscle pain.
Consult your doctor before taking a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug if you have stomach problems.
Aspirin is one of the oldest and most widely used nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs.

Synset Examples

NSAIDs inhibit the activity of both Cox-1 and Cox-2 enzymes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NSAID - abbreviation for nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug
  • Antonym: corticosteroid - a class of steroid drugs often used to reduce inflammation, contrasted with NSAIDs

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not), 'steroidal' (relating to steroids), and 'anti-inflammatory' (reducing inflammation). Widely used in medicine since the mid-20th century.
