hereditary pattern

1. NOUN

/ həˈrɛdəˌtɛri ˈpætɚn / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛdɪtəri ˈpætn / (BrE)
(Genetics) the manner in which traits or characteristics are passed from parents to offspring through genes.
pattern of inherited genetic traits
medical

Examples

Certain diseases, such as hemophilia, follow a distinct hereditary pattern.
Research has shown that eye color is determined by a hereditary pattern.
Understanding your family's hereditary pattern may help predict health risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - the process by which genetic information is passed on from parent to child
