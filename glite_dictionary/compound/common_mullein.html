common mullein

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən məˈleɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən mʌˈliːn / (BrE)
a tall, woolly plant with spikes of densely packed yellow flowers, historically used for torches by ancient Greeks and Romans
tall plant with yellow flower spikes

Examples

The common mullein was easy to spot in the field due to its tall yellow flower spikes.
Ancient Romans reportedly used common mullein stalks as torches for ceremonial events.
The leaves of the common mullein are soft and woolly to the touch.
