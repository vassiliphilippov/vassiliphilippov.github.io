daisyleaf grape fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪziˌlif ɡreɪp fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪziˌliːf ɡreɪp fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of grape fern native to North America and Eurasia, characterized by fronds resembling daisy leaves
a grape fern species with daisy-like leaves

Examples

The daisyleaf grape fern is commonly found in moist woodlands across North America.
Botanists were excited to discover a new population of daisyleaf grape fern in the region.
The leaves of the daisyleaf grape fern have a distinctive shape that resembles daisy petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grape fern - any fern of the genus Botrychium, known for grape-like clusters of sporangia
