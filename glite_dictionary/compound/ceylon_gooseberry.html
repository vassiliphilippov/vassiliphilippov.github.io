Ceylon gooseberry

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɑn ˈɡusˌbɛri / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒn ˈɡuːsbəri / (BrE)
a small, shrubby, spiny tree native to Sri Lanka and India, cultivated for its maroon-purple fruit with sweet, gooseberry-like pulp
spiny tree with edible maroon-purple fruit

Examples

The Ceylon gooseberry is prized for its tart, purple fruit, often eaten fresh or made into jam.
She decided to plant a Ceylon gooseberry in her garden for its attractive foliage and nutritious berries.
Locals in Sri Lanka use Ceylon gooseberry fruit to prepare traditional desserts and drinks.

Etymology

Named after Ceylon (now Sri Lanka) and the gooseberry due to similarity in fruit taste.
