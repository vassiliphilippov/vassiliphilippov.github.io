sand lance

1. NOUN

/ sænd ˌlæns / (AmE), / sænd ˌlɑːns / (BrE)
a small, slender, silvery fish found in coastal waters, known for swimming in schools and burrowing into sandy beaches
small silvery fish that burrow into sand

Examples

Schools of sand lance provide an important food source for seabirds and larger fish.
The sand lance quickly burrowed into the sand to avoid predators.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the sand lance along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand eel - another name for the sand lance, a small silvery burrowing fish
