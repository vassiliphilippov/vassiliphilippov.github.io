West African CFA franc

1. NOUN

/ wɛst ˈæfrɪkən si ɛf eɪ ˈfræŋk / (AmE), / wɛst ˈæfrɪkən siː ɛf eɪ ˈfræŋk / (BrE)
the official currency used by eight independent states in West Africa, including Benin, Burkina Faso, Guinea-Bissau, Ivory Coast, Mali, Niger, Senegal, and Togo.
currency of eight West African countries

Examples

Most transactions in Senegal are conducted in West African CFA francs.
The West African CFA franc is pegged to the euro for stability.
Travelers should exchange their money for West African CFA francs before arriving in Mali.

Synset Examples

The Central African CFA franc is of equal value to the West African CFA franc, and is in circulation in several central African states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Central African CFA franc - currency used in several Central African states

Etymology

CFA originally stood for 'Communauté Financière Africaine' (African Financial Community) in French.
