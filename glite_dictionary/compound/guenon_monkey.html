guenon monkey

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈnoʊn ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ɡeˈnɒn ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
A small, slender African monkey with long hind limbs, a long tail, and long hair around its face, belonging to the genus Cercopithecus.
small slender African monkey

Examples

The guenon monkey can often be seen leaping from tree to tree in Central African forests.
Researchers observed a group of guenon monkeys using unique vocalizations to communicate danger.
The distinctive facial hair of the guenon monkey makes it easy to identify among other species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cercopithecus - Genus of African guenon monkeys

Etymology

From French 'guenon', originally referring to a female monkey.
