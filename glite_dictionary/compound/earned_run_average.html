earned run average

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrn(d) ˌrʌn ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɜːnd ˌrʌn ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a statistic measuring a pitcher's effectiveness, calculated as the average number of earned runs allowed per nine innings pitched.
baseball: pitcher's runs allowed per nine innings

Examples

The pitcher's earned run average dropped significantly after several strong performances in July.
With an earned run average of 2.50, she led the league in pitching statistics.
Analysts compared each team's top pitchers based on their earned run average throughout the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ERA - abbreviation for earned run average in baseball
