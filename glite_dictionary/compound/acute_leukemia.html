acute leukemia

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjut ləˈkim.i.ə / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt lɪˈkiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A rapidly progressing form of leukemia characterized by the overproduction of immature white blood cells.
rapidly progressing type of leukemia
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that acute leukemia requires immediate treatment due to its rapid progression.
Children are more likely to develop acute leukemia than adults are.
Advances in medicine have improved survival rates for patients with acute leukemia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronic leukemia - a slowly progressing form of leukemia

Etymology

From Latin 'acutus' (sharp) and Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'haima' (blood).
