urn fungus

1. NOUN

/ ɝn ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ɜːn ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
a fungus with an urn-shaped fruiting body and a nearly black interior, especially members of the genus Urnula
urn-shaped fungus with dark interior

Examples

The urn fungus can often be found growing on decaying wood in spring.
Mycologists identified the specimen as an urn fungus due to its unique dark interior.
The unusual shape of the urn fungus makes it stand out from other fungi.

Etymology

From 'urn', referring to the shape, and 'fungus', from Latin 'fungus' (mushroom).
