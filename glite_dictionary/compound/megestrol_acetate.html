megestrol acetate

1. NOUN

/ məˈɡɛstrəl ˈæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / məˈɡɛstrɒl ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a synthetic progestational drug used in the treatment of certain cancers, including endometrial carcinoma, and sometimes for appetite stimulation
synthetic progestin drug for cancer treatment
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed megestrol acetate to help control the patient's cancer symptoms.
Some patients taking megestrol acetate have reported an improvement in appetite.
Megestrol acetate is often used as part of palliative care for certain types of cancer.
