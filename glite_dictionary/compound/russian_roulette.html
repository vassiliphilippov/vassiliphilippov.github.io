Russian roulette

1. NOUN

/ ˌrʌʃən ruˈlɛt / (AmE), / ˌrʌʃən ruːˈlɛt / (BrE)
A dangerous game in which a person spins the cylinder of a revolver loaded with a single bullet, aims it at their head, and pulls the trigger.
dangerous game of risking death

Examples

He refused to play Russian roulette because he knew how dangerous it was.
The soldiers treated crossing the minefield like a game of Russian roulette.
Investing all your savings in volatile stocks can feel like financial Russian roulette.

Etymology

From the French 'roulette' (little wheel), referencing the spinning cylinder; 'Russian' implies its supposed origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃən ruˈlɛt / (AmE), / ˌrʌʃ.ən ruːˈlɛt / (BrE)
A lethal game of chance in which a person loads a single bullet into a revolver, spins the cylinder, and pulls the trigger while aiming at their own head; also used figuratively for any reckless or extremely risky action or decision.
deadly game of chance; risky undertaking

Examples

He was playing Russian roulette with his health by ignoring the doctor's advice.
The negotiations felt like a game of Russian roulette—one wrong move and everything could collapse.
Investing all your savings in one startup is financial Russian roulette.

Synset Examples

Playing Russian roulette is a dangerous game.
They felt like they were playing Russian roulette with their futures.
He compared their risky investment to Russian roulette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamble - an act of risking money or something valuable on the outcome of an event

Etymology

From Russian, indicating origin, and French roulette (little wheel), referring to the spinning cylinder of the revolver.
