tenpenny nail

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnˌpɛni ˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.pɛni ˌneɪl / (BrE)
A nail that is 3 inches long, especially used in carpentry or construction.
nail 3 inches long

Examples

The carpenter drove a tenpenny nail into the board to secure it.
For this piece of wood, a tenpenny nail should work perfectly.
She reached for a box of tenpenny nails to finish the project.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nail - a small metal spike with a broadened flat head, used for fastening things together
  • Degree Higher: spike - a long, thick nail used for heavy-duty fastening

Etymology

From 'tenpenny', referring to the historical price per hundred nails in old British currency.
