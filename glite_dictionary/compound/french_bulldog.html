French bulldog

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈbʊlˌdɑg / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈbʊldɒg / (BrE)
a small, muscular breed of dog with a smooth coat, a broad, square head, and bat-like ears, originally developed in France
a small, muscular breed of dog

Examples

The couple adopted a French bulldog puppy from the local animal shelter.
Her French bulldog loves to play fetch in the park every morning.
Despite their small size, French bulldogs are known for their strong personalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulldog - a muscular, heavy dog breed with a wrinkled face and short muzzle

Etymology

Named for its country of development, France, and its resemblance to the bulldog breed.
