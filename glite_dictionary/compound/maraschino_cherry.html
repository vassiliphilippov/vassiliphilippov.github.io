maraschino cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærəˈskiːnoʊ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˌmærəˈskiːnəʊ ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a cherry preserved in maraschino liqueur or a similar flavored syrup, often used as a garnish in cocktails and desserts
cherry preserved in maraschino liqueur or syrup

Examples

The bartender placed a maraschino cherry on top of the sundae as a finishing touch.
She loves her cocktails decorated with a bright red maraschino cherry.
After dinner, he enjoyed an old-fashioned garnished with an orange slice and a maraschino cherry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry - small, round stone fruit; the general fruit type

Etymology

From Italian 'maraschino', a liqueur made from Marasca cherries.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrəˈskiːnoʊ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˌmærəˈskiːnəʊ ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A Dalmatian wild cherry tree, Prunus cerasus var. marasca, whose bitter fruit is used to make maraschino liqueur.
Dalmatian cherry tree for liqueur

Examples

The bitter fruit of the maraschino cherry is used by distilleries across Europe.
Growers in Dalmatia still cultivate the original maraschino cherry for traditional liqueur.
The unique flavour of the maraschino cherry sets it apart from other cherry varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maraschino - a cordial or liqueur made from marasca cherries

Etymology

From Italian 'maraschino', from 'marasca' (the cherry variety).
