picture show

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪktʃɚ ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɪktʃə ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a motion picture; a film shown as entertainment in a cinema
a motion picture or film
archaic american

Examples

My grandparents used to go to the picture show every Friday night.
The old theater downtown still hosts a classic picture show once a month.
He bought popcorn before settling in to watch the latest picture show with his friends.

Synset Examples

they went to a movie every Saturday night
the film was shot on location

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movie - a motion picture; a film
  • Synonym: film - a series of moving images; a motion picture

Etymology

Formed from 'picture' and 'show,' referring to the early days of cinema when films were described as moving pictures shows.
