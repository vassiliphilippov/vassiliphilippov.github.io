Marfan's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːr.fænz ˌsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.fænz ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A hereditary disorder characterized by unusually long limbs and fingers, and often associated with eye and cardiovascular problems.
genetic disorder with long limbs and heart issues
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected Marfan's syndrome after noticing the patient's abnormally long limbs and fingers.
People with Marfan's syndrome often require regular monitoring of their heart and blood vessels.
Her diagnosis of Marfan's syndrome helped explain her vision problems and tall, slender build.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ehlers-Danlos syndrome - a genetic connective tissue disorder with flexible joints and stretchy skin

Etymology

Named after French pediatrician Antoine Marfan, who first described the syndrome in 1896.
