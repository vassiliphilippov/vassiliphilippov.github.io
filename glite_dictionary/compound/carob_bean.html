carob bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəb biːn / (AmE), / ˈkærəb biːn / (BrE)
the long, dark brown pod of the carob tree, containing sweet edible pulp and small seeds, used in food and as a chocolate substitute
pod of the carob tree, chocolate substitute

Examples

The carob bean is often ground into a powder to use as a chocolate substitute in baking.
Farmers frequently use carob beans as animal feed because of their high nutritional value.
She added chopped carob bean pods to her homemade granola for a naturally sweet flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob - the evergreen tree or its fruit; main source of carob beans
  • Antonym: cocoa bean - the bean from the cacao tree, used to make chocolate

Etymology

From Arabic 'kharrūb' via Medieval Latin 'carrubia', referring to the carob tree and its pods.
