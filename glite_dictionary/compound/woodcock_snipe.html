woodcock snipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.kɑk snaɪp / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.kɒk snaɪp / (BrE)
an Old World species of snipe, larger and darker than the common snipe
a large, dark Old World snipe species

Examples

The woodcock snipe is often sighted in dense marshlands during the spring migration.
Compared to the common snipe, the woodcock snipe appears noticeably larger and darker.
Birdwatchers traveled far to add the elusive woodcock snipe to their observation lists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird of marshy areas known for its camouflaged plumage and long bill

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.kɑk ˌsnaɪp / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.kɒk ˌsnaɪp / (BrE)
A small, long-billed game bird of the snipe family, valued for sport and eating.
small long-billed game bird

Examples

The hunter was thrilled to spot a woodcock snipe early in the morning.
Local markets often feature woodcock snipe as a delicacy during the hunting season.
We watched a woodcock snipe as it probed the soft ground for insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird with a long straight bill, typically found in marshes and wetlands
  • Synonym: woodcock - a plump long-billed game bird related to snipes, typically found in woodland
