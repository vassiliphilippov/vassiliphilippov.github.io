generative learning

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚətɪv ˈlɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərətɪv ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
a style of learning in which students actively generate meaning and understanding by connecting new information to prior knowledge
active meaning-making in learning

Examples

Many educators believe that generative learning helps students retain information for longer periods.
Workshops often use generative learning to promote critical thinking and understanding.
The new curriculum emphasizes generative learning rather than simple memorization.

Synset Examples

They focused on generative learning approaches in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: active learning - a method of learning that involves engaging with the material, as opposed to passively receiving information
  • Antonym: rote learning - a memorization technique based on repetition, without understanding meaning

Etymology

From 'generative' (producing or creating) + 'learning'.
