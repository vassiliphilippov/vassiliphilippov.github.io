San Juan Hill

1. PROPN

/ sæn ˌwɑn ˈhɪl / (AmE), / san ˌwæn ˈhɪl / (BrE)
a hill in eastern Cuba, near Santiago de Cuba, notable as the site of a major battle during the Spanish-American War in 1898.
historic hill in Cuba, site of 1898 battle

Examples

Theodore Roosevelt gained fame after leading the Rough Riders in the charge up San Juan Hill.
Many history books recount the significance of the battle at San Juan Hill.
Visiting San Juan Hill offers insight into an important event in Cuban and American history.

Synset Examples
