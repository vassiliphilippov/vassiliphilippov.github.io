tree creeper

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈkripər / (AmE), / triː ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
Any of numerous South American and Central American birds with a curved bill and stiffened tail feathers, which climb tree trunks and feed in a manner similar to woodpeckers.
a bird that climbs tree trunks like a woodpecker

Examples

The tree creeper uses its curved bill to search for insects hidden in the bark.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare tree creeper moving quickly up the trunk of an old cedar.
Unlike woodpeckers, the tree creeper relies on its stiff tail feathers for support while climbing.

2. NOUN

/ tri ˈkripɚ / (AmE), / triː ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
Any of various small insect-eating birds of the Northern Hemisphere, which climb up tree trunks using their stiff tail feathers and feet.
small bird that climbs tree trunks

Examples

The brown and white tree creeper spiraled up the oak, searching for insects beneath the bark.
We spotted a tree creeper darting quickly between the trunks during our walk in the woods.
Tree creepers use their stiff tail feathers for support as they hunt along tree bark.
