pearly-shelled mussel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝli ʃɛld ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːli ʃɛld ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
A freshwater or marine mussel that has a shell with a shiny, pearly interior, often used as a source of mother-of-pearl.
mussel with a pearly shell interior

Examples

The river's pearly-shelled mussel population has declined due to pollution.
She admired the delicate luster of the pearly-shelled mussel found on the shore.
Artisans collect pearly-shelled mussels for their valuable mother-of-pearl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mother-of-pearl - iridescent inner layer of mollusk shells
