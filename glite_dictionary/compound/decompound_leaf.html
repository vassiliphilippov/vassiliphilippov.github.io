decompound leaf

1. NOUN

/ diˈkɑmpaʊnd ˌlif / (AmE), / diːˈkɒmpaʊnd ˌliːf / (BrE)
A leaf divided into parts that are themselves compound, forming multiple levels of subdivision.
leaf with parts that are themselves compound

Examples

The fern's decompound leaf structure is admired for its intricate beauty.
Many acacias display a decompound leaf, which helps them conserve water.
In identifying plants, botanists use the presence of a decompound leaf as a key characteristic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: compound leaf - a leaf composed of several distinct leaflets

Etymology

From Latin de- (down, away) + compound, indicating multiple levels of division.
