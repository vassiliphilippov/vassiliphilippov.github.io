civil servant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl ˈsɝːvənt / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl ˈsɜːvənt / (BrE)
a person who works for a government department or agency, typically as part of the permanent professional staff rather than as an elected official
government employee in the civil service

Examples

After years as a civil servant, she became an expert in public policy.
The government is hiring new civil servants for the health department this year.
As a civil servant, his job is to support the work of elected ministers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public servant - a person employed by a government

Etymology

From 'civil' (relating to citizens or government) and 'servant' (person who serves); use dates from 18th century British government.
