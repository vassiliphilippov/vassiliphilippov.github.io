genus Pezophaps

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌpɛz.oʊˈfæps / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌpɛz.əʊˈfæps / (BrE)
A genus of extinct birds, consisting solely of the solitaire, which was native to Rodrigues Island in the Indian Ocean.
genus of extinct bird (solitaire)

Examples

The extinct solitaire was the only member of the genus Pezophaps.
Fossil remains have provided important insights into the evolution of the genus Pezophaps.
Researchers are studying the ecological niche once occupied by the genus Pezophaps on Rodrigues Island.

Etymology

From New Latin Pezophaps, genus name, from Greek pezos (walking) + phaps (pigeon).
