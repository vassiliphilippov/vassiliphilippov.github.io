Holy See

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈsiː / (BrE)
the central governing body of the Roman Catholic Church, presided over by the Pope, and recognized as a sovereign entity; includes the home and administrative offices of the Church in Vatican City
central governing body of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

The Holy See is recognized as an independent state distinct from Vatican City itself.
Diplomatic relations with the Holy See are maintained by many countries worldwide.
The Pope, as head of the Holy See, represents the worldwide Catholic Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vatican City - the independent city-state enclaved within Rome that houses the Holy See

Etymology

From Latin 'Sancta Sedes', meaning 'Holy Seat' or 'Holy Chair', referring to the ecclesiastical seat of the Pope.
