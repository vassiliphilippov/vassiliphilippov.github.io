crosslight effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌlaɪt ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒslaɪt ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
A lighting technique that creates dramatic contrast by illuminating a subject from the side, enhancing depth and texture.
dramatic side-lighting technique

Examples

The crosslight effect added a sense of mystery to the stage scene.
Photographers often use the crosslight effect to highlight textures in portraits.
The sculpture was brought to life by the skilful use of crosslight effect in the gallery.

Synset Examples

The crosslight effect added drama to the photograph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosslighting - the use of side lighting in photography or theatre

Etymology

From 'crosslight', meaning light coming from the side, plus 'effect'.
