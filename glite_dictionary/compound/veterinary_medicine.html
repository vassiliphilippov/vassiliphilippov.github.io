veterinary medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛtərəˌnɛri ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˌvɛtərɪˈnəri ˈmɛdəsɪn / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases and injuries in animals, especially domestic and farm animals.
medical care of animals
medical

Examples

She decided to study veterinary medicine because of her passion for helping animals.
Advances in veterinary medicine have greatly improved the health of farm livestock.
After graduating in veterinary medicine, he opened his own animal clinic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veterinary science - the scientific study of diseases in animals
  • Degree Higher: medicine - the science of diagnosing, treating, and preventing disease in general (humans or animals)

Etymology

From Latin 'veterinarius' (of cattle) and 'medicina' (the art of healing).
