oast house

1. NOUN

/ oʊst haʊs / (AmE), / əʊst haʊs / (BrE)
a building, typically with a conical or pyramidal roof, used for drying hops as part of the brewing process
building for drying hops

Examples

The old oast house in Kent has been converted into a family home.
We visited a traditional oast house to learn how hops were dried for brewing.
Many oast houses have distinctive conical roofs and white cowls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiln - a furnace or oven for baking, burning, or drying something

Etymology

From Middle English 'ost', possibly from Old English 'āst', meaning kiln, combined with 'house'.
