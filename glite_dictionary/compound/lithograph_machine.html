lithograph machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθəˌɡræf məˈʃin / (AmE), / ˈlɪθəˌɡrɑːf məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A machine used to make prints using lithography, in which a flat surface is treated to create an image with ink-absorbing and ink-repelling areas.
machine for printing by lithography

Examples

The museum restored an old lithograph machine to demonstrate traditional printing techniques.
She learned to operate the lithograph machine as part of her art degree.
The company purchased a new lithograph machine to improve print quality and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicator - a device that makes exact copies of documents

Etymology

From 'lithograph' (from Greek 'lithos' meaning 'stone' and 'graphō' meaning 'write') + 'machine'.
