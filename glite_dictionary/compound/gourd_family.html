gourd family

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔrd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ɡɔːd ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of herbaceous vines, including cucumbers, melons, squashes, and pumpkins, scientifically known as Cucurbitaceae
family of vines including cucumbers and squashes

Examples

The gourd family includes popular vegetables like pumpkin, cucumber, and zucchini.
Many members of the gourd family have tendrils that help them climb.
Botanists classify melons as part of the gourd family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cucurbitaceae - the scientific name for the gourd family
  • Synonym: gourd - the fruit or plant of the gourd family

Etymology

gourd: from Old French gourde, from Latin cucurbita; family: referring to the biological classification 'family' (Cucurbitaceae) in taxonomy.
