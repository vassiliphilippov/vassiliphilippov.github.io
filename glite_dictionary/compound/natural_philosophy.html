natural philosophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃɚəl fəˈlɑːsəfi / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
the study of the physical world and the laws governing matter and energy; an early term for the natural sciences, especially physics, before the modern disciplines were distinguished
study of the natural world; early science, especially physics

Examples

In the seventeenth century, many universities offered courses in natural philosophy.
Isaac Newton made groundbreaking contributions to natural philosophy, especially in mechanics and optics.
Her research interests bridge the history of natural philosophy and modern science.

Synset Examples

his favorite subject was physics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physics - the scientific study of matter, energy, and their interactions
  • Degree Higher: philosophy - the study of fundamental nature of knowledge, reality, and existence

Etymology

From Latin 'philosophia naturalis', meaning 'natural philosophy', originally used for scientific study of nature.
