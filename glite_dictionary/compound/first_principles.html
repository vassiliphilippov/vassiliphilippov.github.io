first principles

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˈprɪnsɪpəlz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈprɪnsɪplz / (BrE)
the basic, foundational concepts or fundamental truths at the root of a subject, from which everything else is derived
basic foundational concepts

Examples

When learning physics, it's essential to start from the first principles before tackling more complex topics.
The engineer explained the problem by breaking it down to first principles.
By questioning everything and returning to first principles, she developed a new and innovative approach.

Synset Examples

he mastered only the rudiments of geometry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudiments - basic or introductory elements of a subject
  • Synonym: fundamentals - central or primary rules, principles, or concepts of a subject
  • Synonym: basics - the simplest or most essential facts or principles of a subject
