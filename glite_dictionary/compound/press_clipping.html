press clipping

1. NOUN

/ prɛs ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / prɛs ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
an article or excerpt cut out from a newspaper or magazine, often kept for reference or archival purposes
article cut from a newspaper or magazine

Examples

She kept a press clipping about her son's achievements tucked into her scrapbook.
The journalist showed us a press clipping from the day he started his career.
They compiled a folder full of press clippings to document the company's media coverage.

Synset Examples

he searched through piles of letters and clippings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news clipping - an article cut from a newspaper for reference
