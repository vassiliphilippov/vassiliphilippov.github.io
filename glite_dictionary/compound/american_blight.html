American blight

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən blaɪt / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən blaɪt / (BrE)
a woolly aphid (Eriosoma lanigerum), primarily a bark-feeding pest of apple and other trees, especially affecting aerial parts and roots
a pest of apple and other trees (woolly aphid)

Examples

The orchard suffered significant damage after American blight infested most of the apple trees.
Farmers inspect their trees regularly to prevent the spread of American blight.
Treatment for American blight often involves pruning affected branches and using insecticides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly aphid - a common aphid that infests fruit trees
