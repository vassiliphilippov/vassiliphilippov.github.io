iron deficiency anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn dɪˈfɪʃənsi əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈaɪən dɪˈfɪʃənsi əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A type of anaemia caused by a lack of iron in the body, often due to insufficient intake or chronic blood loss.
anaemia caused by lack of iron
medical british

Examples

Doctors diagnosed her with iron deficiency anaemia after she complained of constant fatigue and weakness.
Pregnant women are at higher risk of developing iron deficiency anaemia and may need supplements.
A diet lacking in red meat and green vegetables can sometimes lead to iron deficiency anaemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaemia - a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • Synonym: iron deficiency anemia - US spelling of iron deficiency anaemia

Etymology

From iron (the mineral) + deficiency (lack) + anaemia (from Greek anaímos, 'without blood').
