common moonseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmuˌnsid / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmuːnsiːd / (BrE)
A woody vine native to eastern North America, with large oval leaves, small white flowers, and purple to blue-black fruits.
woody North American vine with dark fruits

Examples

The common moonseed can often be found growing along forest edges in eastern North America.
He collected samples of the common moonseed to study its distinctive blue-black berries.
Gardeners are warned that the seeds of common moonseed are toxic if ingested.
