oil gland

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ɡlænd / (AmE), / ɔɪl ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland in an animal that secretes oil, especially one in the skin that lubricates and protects
a gland that secretes oil

Examples

The oil gland in a bird's tail helps keep its feathers waterproof.
Humans have oil glands in their skin, which can sometimes become blocked.
Some animals rely on their oil glands to protect against harsh environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sebaceous gland - a small oil-producing gland in the skin
