canyon treefrog

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.jən ˈtriː.frɒɡ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.jən ˈtriː.frɒɡ / (BrE)
a small frog native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically found near water in rocky canyons and cliffs
frog species of southwestern US and northern Mexico

Examples

The canyon treefrog can be found resting on sunlit rocks along desert streams.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of the canyon treefrog in southern Arizona.
You can often hear the distinctive call of the canyon treefrog near canyon pools at night.
