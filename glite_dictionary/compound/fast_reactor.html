fast reactor

1. NOUN

/ fæst ɹiˈæktɚ / (AmE), / fɑːst riˈæktə / (BrE)
a type of nuclear reactor in which nuclear fissions are sustained by fast neutrons due to the absence or minimal use of a moderator
nuclear reactor using fast neutrons

Examples

The new nuclear plant will use a fast reactor for efficient fuel utilization.
Researchers are developing safer designs for modern fast reactors.
Unlike a conventional system, a fast reactor operates without a neutron moderator.
