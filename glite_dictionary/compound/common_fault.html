common fault

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən fɔlt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən fɔːlt / (BrE)
An inclined geological fault in which the hanging wall has moved downward relative to the footwall.
type of inclined geological fault

Examples

Geologists identified a common fault running through the valley, indicating significant tectonic movement.
A common fault is often associated with areas where the crust is being pulled apart.
The diagram illustrates how the hanging wall in a common fault slips downward relative to the footwall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normal fault - an inclined fault where the hanging wall moves downward; synonym for common fault
