parieto-occipital fissure

1. NOUN

/ pəˌraɪ.ətoʊ əkˈsɪp.ɪ.təl ˈfɪʃ.ər / (AmE), / pæˌraɪ.ətəʊ ɒkˈsɪp.ɪ.təl ˈfɪʃ.ə / (BrE)
a deep groove near the back of each cerebral hemisphere separating the parietal and occipital lobes of the brain
groove separating parietal and occipital lobes
medical

Examples

The parieto-occipital fissure can be seen clearly on MRI scans of the brain.
Damage near the parieto-occipital fissure may affect both visual and sensory processing.
Neurosurgeons take care to avoid the parieto-occipital fissure during certain procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulcus - a groove or furrow on the surface of the brain
  • Degree Lower: central sulcus - the sulcus separating frontal and parietal lobes

Etymology

From Latin 'parietalis' (of a wall) and 'occipitalis' (of the back of the head), referring to the lobes separated by the fissure.
