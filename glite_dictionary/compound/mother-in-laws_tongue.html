mother-in-law's tongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðər ɪn lɔz tʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə ɪn lɔːz tʌŋ / (BrE)
A stemless tropical plant (Sansevieria trifasciata) with long, narrow, rigid, upright leaves, frequently grown as a houseplant.
a houseplant with long, stiff leaves

Examples

The mother-in-law's tongue in the corner of the living room hardly needs any water.
Many people choose mother-in-law's tongue because it is easy to care for and purifies the air.
She placed a potted mother-in-law's tongue on her office desk to brighten the space.

Related Terms

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ ɪn lɑz tʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðər ɪn lɔːz tʌŋ / (BrE)
an evergreen plant with long, upright, dark green leaves, often grown as a houseplant and known for its tolerance of neglect; also called 'snake plant.'
an evergreen houseplant with upright leaves

Examples

She placed the mother-in-law's tongue by the window because it thrives in indirect sunlight.
The long, stiff leaves of the mother-in-law's tongue make it a popular choice for modern interiors.
Keep the mother-in-law's tongue away from pets, as its leaves contain toxic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake plant - another common name for mother-in-law's tongue (Sansevieria trifasciata)

Etymology

Named humorously after the supposed sharpness of the 'mother-in-law's tongue,' referring to the leaf's pointed shape; also known as 'snake plant.'
