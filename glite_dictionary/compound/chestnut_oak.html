chestnut oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.nət oʊk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.nət əʊk / (BrE)
A North American oak tree (Quercus montana) with leaves resembling those of the chestnut tree and dark, deeply ridged bark.
a North American oak tree with chestnut-like leaves

Examples

The chestnut oak is known for its deeply ridged bark and large, tooth-edged leaves.
Acorns from the chestnut oak are an important food source for local wildlife.
Along the rocky hillsides, chestnut oak trees thrive where many others cannot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, noted for its strength and hardwood

Etymology

Named for its leaves' resemblance to those of the chestnut tree.
