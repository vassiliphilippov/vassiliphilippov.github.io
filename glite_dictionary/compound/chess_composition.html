chess composition

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛs ˌkɑmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / tʃɛs ˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A puzzle or problem created using chess pieces, presenting a specific task for the solver to achieve, usually following chess rules.
chess puzzle set for solving

Examples

He spent hours studying a difficult chess composition to improve his skills.
The magazine published a monthly chess composition for enthusiasts to solve.
Creating an original chess composition requires both creativity and deep understanding of the game.

Synset Examples

The sequel to Alice's Adventure's in Wonderland was designed to be a playable, albeit whimsical chess problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chess problem - a puzzle using chess pieces with a set objective

Modern chess composition often emphasizes originality and creative solutions.
