genus Ribes

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs ˈraɪbiːz / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs ˈriːbiːz / (BrE)
a genus of flowering shrubs in the family Grossulariaceae, including currants and gooseberries, native to the Northern Hemisphere
genus of shrubs including currants and gooseberries

Examples

The genus Ribes includes both blackcurrants and gooseberries, which are popular in jams and desserts.
Many species within the genus Ribes are valued for their edible fruit.
Botanists studied the distribution of the genus Ribes across cool temperate zones.

Etymology

From Latin *Ribes*, the genus name, possibly from Arabic *ribās* (a type of acidic plant).
