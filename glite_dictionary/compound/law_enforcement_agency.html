law enforcement agency

1. NOUN

/ lɔ ɪnˈfɔrsmənt ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / lɔː ɪnˈfɔːsmənt ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an organization responsible for ensuring that laws are obeyed and for preventing and investigating crimes
organization enforcing the law

Examples

The FBI is the principal law enforcement agency in the United States for federal crimes.
Every law enforcement agency is required to follow strict procedures when collecting evidence.
She has worked with almost every local law enforcement agency in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police force - the civil force responsible for maintaining public order and safety
