Nova Scotia salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊvə ˈskoʊʃə ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˌnəʊvə ˈskəʊʃə ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a type of brine-cured salmon, typically less salty than other cured salmon varieties, sometimes prepared with sugar
mildly brined cured salmon

Examples

We had Nova Scotia salmon on bagels with cream cheese for breakfast this morning.
The deli specializes in Nova Scotia salmon, known for its mild and delicate flavor.
Would you like some Nova Scotia salmon added to your salad?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lox - salmon cured in a brine, typically very salty

Etymology

Named after Nova Scotia, Canada, where this curing style originated.
