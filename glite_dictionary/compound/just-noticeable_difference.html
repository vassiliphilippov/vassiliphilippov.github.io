just-noticeable difference

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʌst ˈnoʊ.tɪ.sə.bəl ˈdɪf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌst ˈnəʊ.tɪ.sə.bəl ˈdɪf.ər.əns / (BrE)
the smallest difference in intensity between two stimuli that a person can detect half the time under controlled conditions
smallest detectable difference between stimuli

Examples

The just-noticeable difference in weight is often determined by repeated testing with very similar objects.
Researchers measured the just-noticeable difference for changes in sound intensity in the experiment.
When the increase in temperature reached the just-noticeable difference, the participant reported feeling warmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difference threshold - the lowest level at which a difference between two stimuli can be detected
