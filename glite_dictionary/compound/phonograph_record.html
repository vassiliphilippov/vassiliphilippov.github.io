phonograph record

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊnəˌɡræf ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəɡrɑːf ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
a flat disc with a continuous groove that stores sound recordings and is played on a phonograph or turntable
disc for storing and playing sound recordings

Examples

My grandfather still has a large collection of phonograph records in his living room.
The phonograph record was once the most popular format for listening to music.
She placed the phonograph record on the turntable and gently lowered the needle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinyl record - disc-shaped audio recording medium made of vinyl plastic
  • Degree Higher: compact disc - digital audio storage medium in disc form
  • Degree Higher: audio cassette - magnetic tape sound recording medium

Etymology

From 'phonograph' (device for reproducing sound from records) + 'record' (from Latin recordari, to remember).
