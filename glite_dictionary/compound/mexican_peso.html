Mexican peso

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən ˈpeɪsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈpeɪsəʊ / (BrE)
the official currency of Mexico, equal to 100 centavos.
currency unit of Mexico

Examples

Tourists usually exchange their dollars for Mexican pesos before arriving in Mexico.
The price of a cup of coffee in Mexico is about twenty Mexican pesos.
The value of the Mexican peso has fluctuated against the euro this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peso - basic unit of currency in several Latin American countries
  • Antonym: US dollar - basic unit of money in the United States

Etymology

From Spanish 'peso', meaning 'weight' or 'coin,' used as a monetary unit.
