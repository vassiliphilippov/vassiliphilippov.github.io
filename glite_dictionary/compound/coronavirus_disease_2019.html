coronavirus disease 2019

1. NOUN

/ kəˌroʊ.nəˌvaɪ.rəs dɪˈziz ˌtwɛnti ˈnaɪntiːn / (AmE), / kəˌrəʊ.nəˌvaɪ.rəs dɪˈziːz ˌtwenti naɪnˈtiːn / (BrE)
A potentially severe viral respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2, first identified in 2019 and responsible for a global pandemic.
respiratory viral disease (COVID-19)
medical

Examples

The spread of coronavirus disease 2019 led to widespread lockdowns around the world.
Researchers have worked rapidly to develop vaccines for coronavirus disease 2019.
Many people experienced remote work for the first time due to coronavirus disease 2019.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: COVID-19 - Abbreviation for coronavirus disease 2019

Etymology

From 'coronavirus', the virus family name, and 'disease 2019' for the year of identification.
