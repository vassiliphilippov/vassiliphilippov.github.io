crowd control

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraʊd kənˌtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈkraʊd kənˌtrəʊl / (BrE)
the activity or measures taken to manage and direct large groups of people, especially to maintain order or ensure safety in public places or events
managing large groups of people

Examples

The police implemented crowd control measures to keep the festival safe.
Proper crowd control is essential during emergency evacuations.
Security staff are trained in crowd control techniques for large sporting events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riot control - the activity of controlling violent crowds during riots
