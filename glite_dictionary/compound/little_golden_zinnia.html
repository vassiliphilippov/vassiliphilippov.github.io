little golden zinnia

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˈɡoʊldən ˈzɪniə / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈɡəʊldən ˈzɪniə / (BrE)
A subshrub native to parts of the southwestern United States and Mexico, with short leafy stems and small, nearly round yellow-orange flower heads.
southwestern US/Mexican flowering subshrub

Examples

The little golden zinnia brightens desert gardens with its yellow-orange blooms.
Botanists discovered several clusters of little golden zinnia along the rocky hillside.
Drought-resistant and vibrant, the little golden zinnia is prized by many landscape designers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: zinnia - a genus of plants with brightly colored, daisy-like flowers
