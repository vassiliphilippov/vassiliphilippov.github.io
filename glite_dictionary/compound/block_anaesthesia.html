block anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ blɑk ənəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / blɒk ˌænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
Anesthesia affecting a specific area of the body by applying an anesthetic to a nerve supplying that area.
regional anesthesia by nerve block
medical british

Examples

The dentist used block anaesthesia to numb the patient's lower jaw before the procedure.
Some surgeries require block anaesthesia instead of general anesthesia for better pain control.
The success of block anaesthesia depends on accurately identifying the target nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve block - an injection to stop pain signals in a nerve
