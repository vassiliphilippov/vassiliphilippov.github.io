at a canter

1. ADV

/ æt ə ˈkænɾɚ / (AmE), / ət ə ˈkæn.tə / (BrE)
At a moderate, easy pace, especially in horse riding, or (figuratively) to achieve something with ease and speed.
at an easy or moderate pace; with ease

Examples

The horse moved elegantly at a canter across the sunny meadow.
Despite its difficulty, she finished the exam at a canter and left the room smiling.
They completed the renovations at a canter, surprising their client with the speed.

Synset Examples

The horse moved gracefully at a canter across the field.
He prefers to ride his horse at a canter for fun.
She completed the course at a canter, impressing the judges.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gallop - fastest pace of a horse
  • Degree Lower: trot - pace of a horse slower than canter

Etymology

From the noun 'canter', a pace of a horse slower than a gallop, from Canterbury (as in 'Canterbury gallop').
