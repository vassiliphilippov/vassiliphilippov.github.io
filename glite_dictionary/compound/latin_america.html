Latin America

1. PROPN

/ ˌlæt.ɪn əˈmɛr.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌlæt.ɪn əˈmɛr.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
the regions of the Americas south of the United States where Romance languages (mainly Spanish and Portuguese) are spoken
regions of the Americas south of the U.S. where Romance languages are spoken

Examples

Many people travel to Latin America to experience its rich cultural heritage and diverse landscapes.
The novel explores political themes in various countries across Latin America.
Several important trade agreements connect the U.S. with Latin America.

Etymology

Latin from the association with the Romance languages, which evolved from Latin; 'America' refers to the continents of North and South America.
