Cabot Cove syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæbət ˈkoʊv ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˌkæbət ˈkəʊv ˌsɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
a humorous term for the occurrence of frequent dramatic or criminal events in a small community, referencing the fictional town of Cabot Cove from the television series 'Murder, She Wrote'
frequent drama or crime in a small place (humorous term)

Examples

Ever since she moved to that quiet village, she joked it was developing Cabot Cove syndrome.
The book's setting suffered from Cabot Cove syndrome, with a new murder mystery every week.
At work, we laughed about our constant troubles as a kind of Cabot Cove syndrome.

Synset Examples

Her life became a mystery, typical of Cabot Cove syndrome.
Everyone in that town seems to have Cabot Cove syndrome.
He joked about having Cabot Cove syndrome after all the drama.

Etymology

From Cabot Cove, the fictional town in 'Murder, She Wrote,' known for an improbably high crime rate.
