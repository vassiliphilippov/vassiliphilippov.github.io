psychological moment

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
the most suitable or opportune time to achieve a desired effect
the most suitable time for an effect

Examples

He waited for the psychological moment to reveal his plan to the group.
The coach spoke to the players at the psychological moment to motivate them before the game.
She chose the psychological moment to ask for a raise, knowing her boss was in a good mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - a very brief period of time
