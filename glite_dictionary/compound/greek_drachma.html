Greek drachma

1. NOUN

/ ɡrik ˈdrækmə / (AmE), / ɡriːk ˈdrækmə / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Greece, replaced by the euro in 2001
former Greek currency unit

Examples

The Greek drachma was replaced by the euro in 2001.
Tourists often exchanged dollars for Greek drachmas before visiting Athens.
Collectors value old Greek drachma coins for their historical significance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - currency replacing the Greek drachma

Etymology

From Greek 'δραχμή' (drachmē), meaning 'a handful' or 'a drachma coin'.
