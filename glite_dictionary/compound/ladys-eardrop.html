lady's-eardrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˈɪrdɹɑp / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˈɪədɹɒp / (BrE)
an erect or climbing shrub native to Brazil, with deep pink to red flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental plant
Brazilian shrub with red or pink flowers

Examples

The lady's-eardrop blooms throughout the summer, adding vibrant color to the garden.
Many gardeners in temperate regions cultivate lady's-eardrop in greenhouses.
The bright red flowers of the lady's-eardrop attract hummingbirds and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuchsia - a genus of flowering plants with showy, pendulous flowers

Etymology

Named for the flower's resemblance to a decorative earring (ear-drop) traditionally worn by women.
