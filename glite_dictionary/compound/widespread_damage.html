widespread damage

1. NOUN

/ ˌwaɪdˌsprɛd ˈdæmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌwaɪdsprɛd ˈdamɪdʒ / (BrE)
extensive destruction or harm affecting a large area or a great number of people or things
extensive harm over large area

Examples

The hurricane left widespread damage throughout the coastal region, uprooting trees and destroying homes.
Wildfires can cause widespread damage to forests, wildlife habitats, and nearby communities.
After the earthquake, emergency crews assessed the widespread damage to roads, bridges, and buildings.

Synset Examples

The massive storm caused widespread damage across the city.
The tornado caused widespread damage across the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - severe and overwhelming damage or destruction
  • Degree Lower: minor damage - damage of small extent or impact
