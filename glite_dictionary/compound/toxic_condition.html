toxic condition

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪk kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪk kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
A physiological state caused by exposure to a poisonous or toxic substance, resulting in harmful effects in the body.
medical state caused by toxins or poisons
medical

Examples

The patient developed a toxic condition after accidentally ingesting the chemical.
Prolonged exposure to heavy metals can result in a dangerous toxic condition.
Doctors acted quickly to treat the toxic condition caused by the snakebite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxicosis - a disease state caused by poisoning or exposure to toxins
