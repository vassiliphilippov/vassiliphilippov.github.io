cuckoo clock

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊku ˌklɑk / (AmE), / ˈkʊkuː ˌklɒk / (BrE)
a clock, typically wall-mounted, that announces the hours with a sound mimicking a cuckoo's call, often with a mechanical bird appearing at each hour
clock that chimes like a cuckoo

Examples

The old wooden cuckoo clock chimed cheerfully every hour from the kitchen wall.
Her grandfather repaired antique cuckoo clocks as a hobby.
At midnight, the little bird popped out of the cuckoo clock and called twelve times.

Etymology

From German 'Kuckucksuhr', combining 'Kuckuck' (cuckoo) and 'Uhr' (clock).
