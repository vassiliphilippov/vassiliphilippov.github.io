optic tract

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑp.tɪk trækt / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tɪk trækt / (BrE)
the part of the visual pathway in the brain that transmits signals from the optic chiasm to the lateral geniculate nucleus, carrying information from the retina.
nerve pathway from retina to brain
medical

Examples

The optic tract carries visual information from the optic chiasm to the brain.
Damage to the optic tract can cause loss of vision in specific visual fields.
Researchers studied how the optic tract transmits signals to the visual cortex.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: optic nerve - the nerve that carries visual information from the eye to the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'optikos' (of seeing) and Latin 'tractus' (track, path).
