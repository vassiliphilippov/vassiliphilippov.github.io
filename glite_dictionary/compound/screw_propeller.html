screw propeller

1. NOUN

/ skruː prəˈpɛlɚ / (AmE), / skruː prəˈpɛlə / (BrE)
a type of propeller with several angled blades that rotates to push against water or air, propelling a vessel or aircraft forward
rotating propeller with angled blades for propulsion

Examples

The steamship was equipped with a screw propeller instead of traditional paddle wheels.
Modern submarines often use a screw propeller for silent and efficient movement through the water.
The invention of the screw propeller revolutionized maritime travel in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propeller - device with rotating blades used for propulsion

Etymology

Combination of 'screw' and 'propeller', likening the spiral action to that of a screw.
