motor region

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtɚ ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
a region of the cerebral cortex responsible for controlling voluntary muscle movements
area of brain controlling movement
medical

Examples

Damage to the motor region can lead to loss of voluntary movement on one side of the body.
Researchers studied the motor region to understand how the brain controls fine motor skills.
The surgeon carefully avoided the motor region during the procedure to prevent impairing the patient's movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motor cortex - the part of the cerebral cortex involved in planning, control, and execution of voluntary movements
