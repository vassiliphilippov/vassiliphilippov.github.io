tragic flaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrædʒɪk flɔ / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪk flɔː / (BrE)
a fault or weakness in the character of the hero in a tragedy, which leads to their downfall
character flaw leading to a hero's downfall

Examples

The protagonist's tragic flaw is his excessive pride, which ultimately leads to his defeat.
Many classic heroes are brought down by a tragic flaw that blinds them to reality.
In Shakespeare's Hamlet, indecision is often seen as the prince's tragic flaw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamartia - a fatal flaw leading to the downfall of a tragic hero
