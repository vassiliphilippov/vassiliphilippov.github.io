sargasso weed

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈɡæsoʊ wiːd / (AmE), / sɑːˈɡæsəʊ wiːd / (BrE)
a type of floating brown seaweed (genus Sargassum) found in oceanic regions, especially in the Sargasso Sea
floating seaweed found in oceans

Examples

The ship became entangled in sargasso weed during the long voyage.
Scientists study how sargasso weed supports diverse marine life in the Atlantic.
Large patches of sargasso weed can be spotted floating across the open sea.

Synset Examples

We found ourselves lost among the sargasso weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sargassum - the genus of brown seaweed called sargasso weed

Etymology

From Portuguese 'sargaço', the name for the seaweed, after the Sargasso Sea.
