whipping post

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpɪŋ ˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈwɪpɪŋ pəʊst / (BrE)
a post to which people convicted of offenses were tied in public to be whipped as a punishment
post for punishing offenders by whipping

Examples

The old prison still has a whipping post in the courtyard as a reminder of its harsh past.
Criminals in colonial America were sometimes punished at the whipping post before a crowd.
The guide explained how the whipping post was once used for public humiliation and corporal punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillory - a wooden framework with holes for the head and hands, used to expose an offender to public scorn
  • Synonym: stocks - a wooden device used for public punishment by confining the ankles or wrists
