alpha and omega

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ənd oʊˈmeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈælfə ənd əʊˈmiːɡə / (BrE)
the most important or essential part of something; the beginning and the end
the essential or most important part

Examples

For many, honesty is the alpha and omega of strong relationships.
Education is the alpha and omega of progress in society.
Respect for rules is the alpha and omega of fair competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something

Etymology

From the first and last letters of the Greek alphabet, symbolizing the beginning and the end.

2. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ənd oʊˈmeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈælfə ənd əʊˈmiːɡə / (BrE)
the first and last; the most important or essential part of something, often signifying completeness or entirety, especially in reference to God's eternity.
the first and last; essential elements

Examples

Her faith in kindness was the alpha and omega of her philosophy.
In many traditions, God is described as the alpha and omega.
Honesty and trustworthiness are the alpha and omega of a good friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginning and end - start and finish, expressing the whole extent of something

Etymology

From the first and last letters of the Greek alphabet, used in the Book of Revelation to signify the comprehensiveness of God.
