roll film

1. NOUN

/ roʊl fɪlm / (AmE), / rəʊl fɪlm / (BrE)
photographic film wound on a spool, typically used in still cameras.
photographic film wound on a spool

Examples

He loaded a fresh roll film into his old camera before heading out to shoot.
Many classic photographers preferred using roll film for its convenience and portability.
After taking the photos, she carefully rewound the exposed roll film for development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sheet film - photographic film supplied in individual sheets
