amphetamine sulphate

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɛtəˌmin ˈsʌl.feɪt / (AmE), / amˌfɛtəˈmiːn ˈsʌlf.eɪt / (BrE)
a sulfate salt of amphetamine used as a central nervous system stimulant, particularly in the treatment of disorders such as ADHD and narcolepsy
sulfate salt of amphetamine, a stimulant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amphetamine sulphate to help manage her narcolepsy symptoms.
Some students misuse amphetamine sulphate in an attempt to enhance concentration during exams.
Amphetamine sulphate is classified as a controlled substance in many countries due to its potential for abuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphetamine - a synthetic stimulant affecting the central nervous system

Etymology

Amphetamine from chemical roots: alpha-methylphenethylamine; sulphate from Latin 'sulphuratum'.
