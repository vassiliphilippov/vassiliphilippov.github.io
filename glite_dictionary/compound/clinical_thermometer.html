clinical thermometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪnɪkəl θərˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a thermometer designed for measuring the temperature of the human body, typically with a scale suited for medical use
body temperature measuring thermometer
medical

Examples

The nurse placed a clinical thermometer under the patient's tongue to check for fever.
Old clinical thermometers often contained mercury, which is now considered hazardous.
It's important to clean a clinical thermometer properly after each use to prevent infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever thermometer - thermometer used to measure body temperature

Etymology

From 'clinical' (relating to medical treatment) and 'thermometer' (device for measuring temperature).
