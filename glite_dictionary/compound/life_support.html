life support

1. NOUN

/ laɪf səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / laɪf səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
medical equipment that keeps a person alive by assisting or replacing vital bodily functions, such as breathing or circulation, when they cannot function independently
medical equipment sustaining life
medical

Examples

The doctors placed her on life support after she stopped breathing on her own.
He was kept alive by life support machines in the intensive care unit.
After several days on life support, the patient showed signs of improvement.

Synset Examples

the patient is on life support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life support system - a system or device for keeping a person alive when they are seriously ill or injured

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf səˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf səˌpɔːt / (BrE)
equipment or systems that keep a person alive in a dangerous or otherwise deadly environment, especially by providing air, water, or other vital needs.
equipment that keeps people alive in deadly environments

Examples

The crew depended on life support during their mission to Mars.
After the accident, the patient was kept alive by life support.
Modern submarines are equipped with advanced life support systems.

Synset Examples

the astronauts relied on their life-support systems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life-support system - system that provides essential support for sustaining life

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf səˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf səˌpɔːt / (BrE)
assistance that is essential for someone's survival or well-being, especially in difficult circumstances; often used figuratively to refer to crucial emotional or practical help.
essential assistance for survival or well-being

Examples

During her illness, her family acted as emotional life support.
After losing his job, his friends became his life support system.
Her mentorship was the life support I needed to rebuild my confidence.

Synset Examples

Her friendship provided me with life support during hard times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - practical or emotional help
  • Synonym: lifeline - something that provides help or protection in a difficult situation
