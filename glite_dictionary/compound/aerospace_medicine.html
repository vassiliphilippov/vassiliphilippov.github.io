aerospace medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛroʊˌspeɪs ˈmɛdəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈeərəʊˌspeɪs ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the study, prevention, and treatment of health problems associated with flight and space travel
medical field for flight and space travel
medical

Examples

He decided to specialize in aerospace medicine after learning about the health challenges faced by astronauts.
Recent advances in aerospace medicine have improved the safety of long-duration space flights.
The symposium will feature experts in aerospace medicine discussing the future of human space exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aviation medicine - the branch of medicine dealing with health problems associated with flying

Etymology

From 'aerospace', combining 'aero-' (air) and 'space', and 'medicine' (from Latin 'medicina', meaning the art of healing).
