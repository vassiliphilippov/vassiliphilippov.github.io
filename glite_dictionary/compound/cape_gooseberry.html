cape gooseberry

1. NOUN

/ keɪp ˈɡuːzbɛri / (AmE), / keɪp ˈɡʊzbəri / (BrE)
a plant of tropical South America (Physalis peruviana) with small, edible, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery husk
edible fruit-bearing South American plant

Examples

In the garden, they planted cape gooseberry to enjoy its sweet, tangy fruits in the summer.
The tart flavor of cape gooseberry makes it perfect for jams and desserts.
After harvesting the cape gooseberry, she carefully removed the delicate husk from each fruit.

Etymology

Named after the Cape of Good Hope, South Africa, where it was cultivated, and 'gooseberry' for its fruit resemblance.
