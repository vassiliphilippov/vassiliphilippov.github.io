moment magnitude scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt ˈmæg.nɪ.tud skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.mənt ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tjuːd skeɪl / (BrE)
a logarithmic scale for measuring the energy released by earthquakes, based on the area of the fault that ruptured; it has largely replaced the Richter scale
earthquake energy measurement scale

Examples

The moment magnitude scale provides a more accurate measurement of large earthquakes than the Richter scale.
Seismologists use the moment magnitude scale to assess the energy released by recent seismic events.
After the quake, officials reported its strength as 7.2 on the moment magnitude scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richter scale - an earlier scale that measured earthquake strength
