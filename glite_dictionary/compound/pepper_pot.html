pepper pot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpər pɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə pɒt / (BrE)
a container with a perforated top used for sprinkling ground pepper
container for sprinkling pepper

Examples

She reached across the table for the pepper pot to season her soup.
The antique silver pepper pot was part of the collection on display.
Please pass me the pepper pot and the salt shaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt shaker - a container for sprinkling salt

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpər pɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə pɒt / (BrE)
a thick soup made with vegetables and tripe, highly seasoned with pepper; often includes dumplings.
a spicy vegetable and tripe soup

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its pepper pot, a spicy soup served with warm bread.
She prepared a traditional pepper pot for the family on a cold winter evening.
Many Caribbean cuisines have their own variation of pepper pot.

/ ˈpɛpɚ pɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə pɒt / (BrE)
a small container with holes in the top, used for sprinkling ground pepper or other spices onto food
container for dispensing ground pepper

Examples

She bought a new pepper pot for her kitchen collection.
After refilling the pepper pot, he set it back on the dining table.
