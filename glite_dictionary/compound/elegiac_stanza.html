elegiac stanza

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛlɪˈdʒaɪ.ək ˈstæn.zə / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈdʒaɪ.ək ˈstæn.zə / (BrE)
A stanza of four lines in iambic pentameter with an ABAB rhyme scheme, often used in elegiac poetry.
quatrain in iambic pentameter with ABAB rhyme

Examples

Many poets in the Romantic era favored the elegiac stanza to express profound loss.
The poem opens with an elegiac stanza reflecting on the beauty of nature.
She experimented with the elegiac stanza, adapting its structure for modern themes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quatrain - a stanza of four lines

Etymology

From Latin 'elegiācus' (of an elegy) and 'stanza' (from Italian, meaning 'room, stopping place').
