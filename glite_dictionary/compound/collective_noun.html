collective noun

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktɪv naʊn / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv naʊn / (BrE)
A noun that is singular in form but refers to a group of people or things as a whole, such as 'team' or 'flock'.
noun for group of people or things

Examples

The word team is a collective noun referring to a group of players.
In English, collective nouns like 'jury' may take singular or plural verbs depending on context.
A flock of sheep is a classic example of a collective noun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mass noun - a noun referring to something uncountable
