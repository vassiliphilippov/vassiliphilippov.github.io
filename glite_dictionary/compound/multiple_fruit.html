multiple fruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltɪpəl frut / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl̩ fruːt / (BrE)
a fruit formed from the fusion of several ovaries from multiple flowers within a common receptacle, as in pineapples and mulberries
fruit formed from fused ovaries of several flowers

Examples

The pineapple is a classic example of a multiple fruit found in tropical regions.
Unlike simple fruits, a multiple fruit develops from the ovaries of several flowers.
Scientists study how individual drupes combine to form a multiple fruit like the mulberry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggregate fruit - fruit formed from the merging of several ovaries from one flower
