Greenland whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnlənd weɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlənd weɪl / (BrE)
a large baleen whale found in Arctic waters, distinguished by its massive bow-shaped head and large mouth; also known as the bowhead whale.
a large Arctic whale (bowhead whale)

Examples

The Greenland whale can live for over 200 years, making it one of the longest-lived mammals.
Many scientists study the migration patterns of the Greenland whale in the Arctic Ocean.
The thick blubber of the Greenland whale helps it survive in freezing polar waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowhead whale - another name for the Greenland whale, an Arctic baleen whale
