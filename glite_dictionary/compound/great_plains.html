Great Plains

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt pleɪnz / (AmE), / ɡreɪt pleɪnz / (BrE)
a large, mostly treeless prairie region of central North America, extending from Canada south through the central United States into Texas
vast grassland region of central North America

Examples

Many bison once roamed freely across the Great Plains before settlers arrived.
Farmers in the Great Plains often face challenges with drought and unpredictable weather.
The Great Plains are famous for their wide-open skies and expansive grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie - large open area of grassland, especially in North America

2. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt pleɪnz / (AmE), / ɡreɪt pleɪnz / (BrE)
a large area of flat, grassy land in central North America, extending from Canada to Texas, known for its prairies and once inhabited mainly by Native Americans
region of flat prairie land in central North America

Examples

Bison once roamed the Great Plains in vast herds before European settlement.
Many settlers traveled across the Great Plains during the westward expansion of the United States.
The climate of the Great Plains is known for having hot summers and cold winters.
