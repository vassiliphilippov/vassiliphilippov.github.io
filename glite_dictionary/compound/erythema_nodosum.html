erythema nodosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.ɪˈθiː.mə noʊˈdoʊ.səm / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˈθiː.mə nəʊˈdəʊ.səm / (BrE)
A skin condition marked by tender red nodules, usually appearing on the shins or legs, often associated with infection or other health problems.
skin disease with tender red leg nodules
medical

Examples

After a viral infection, she developed erythema nodosum with painful lumps on her calves.
The doctor diagnosed erythema nodosum after noticing red, swollen nodules on his legs.
Treatment for erythema nodosum typically involves addressing the underlying cause and reducing inflammation.

Etymology

From Greek 'erythema' (redness) and Latin 'nodosus' (knotty, nodular).
