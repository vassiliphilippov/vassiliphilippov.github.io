musette pipe

1. NOUN

/ mjuˈzɛt ˌpaɪp / (AmE), / mjuˈzɛt ˌpaɪp / (BrE)
a small, simple oboe commonly used in French folk and classical music
small, simple oboe

Examples

The musette pipe is featured in several Baroque compositions.
She learned to play the musette pipe during her studies in France.
The unique sound of the musette pipe adds charm to traditional folk dances.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: oboe - a woodwind instrument with a double reed

Etymology

From French 'musette', a small bagpipe or oboe.
