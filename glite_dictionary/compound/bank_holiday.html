bank holiday

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˈhɑːlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈhɒlɪdeɪ / (BrE)
A public holiday during which banks and most businesses are closed, especially in the United Kingdom.
public holiday when banks are closed
british

Examples

The next bank holiday falls at the end of August, giving many people a long weekend.
Shops and offices were closed on the bank holiday, but some restaurants remained open.
They planned a short trip to the countryside over the May bank holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public holiday - A day when most businesses and schools are closed for a national or religious celebration

Etymology

Refers to days on which banks are legally closed; attested from 1871 in the UK.

2. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˈhɑlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / baŋk ˈhɒlɪdeɪ / (BrE)
a public holiday during which banks and most businesses are closed, especially in the United Kingdom and other parts of the Commonwealth.
official public holiday when banks are closed
british

Examples

We're planning a picnic on the bank holiday in May.
Because of the bank holiday, the shops will have reduced opening hours.
Traffic jams are common at the start of a bank holiday weekend.

Synset Examples

Many people travel during the bank holiday weekend.
The office is closed for the bank holiday on Monday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public holiday - a day when most people do not have to work, for a special reason or event

Etymology

Originally referred to certain days on which banks were closed by law in the UK (Bank Holidays Act 1871).
