corn cockle

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈkɑk.əl / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈkɒk.əl / (BrE)
A European annual plant (Agrostemma githago) with large trumpet-shaped reddish-purple flowers and poisonous seeds, commonly found as a weed in grainfields and along roadsides; now also naturalized in America.
a European weed with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

The bright flowers of the corn cockle stood out among the wheat in the field.
Farmers consider the corn cockle a troublesome weed due to its poisonous seeds.
Roadsides in some parts of America are dotted with blooming corn cockle in summer.

Etymology

From 'corn' (grain) and Middle English 'coccle' (weed), referring to its presence in grainfields.
