Strait of Magellan

1. PROPN

/ streɪt əv məˈɡɛlən / (AmE), / streɪt əv məˈɡælən / (BrE)
A narrow passage of water separating the southern tip of South America (mainland) from Tierra del Fuego and other islands, discovered by Ferdinand Magellan in 1520. It was a key maritime route before the construction of the Panama Canal.
strait between South America and Tierra del Fuego

Examples

Early explorers faced dangerous weather while sailing through the Strait of Magellan.
Before the Panama Canal, ships used the Strait of Magellan to navigate between the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
The treacherous currents and narrow passages make the Strait of Magellan a challenging route even today.

Etymology

Named after Ferdinand Magellan, the Portuguese explorer who navigated it in 1520.
