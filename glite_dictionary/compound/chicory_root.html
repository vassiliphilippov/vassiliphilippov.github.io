chicory root

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkəri ruːt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkəri ruːt / (BrE)
the dried root of the chicory plant, often roasted and used as a coffee substitute or additive
dried root of chicory, used as coffee substitute

Examples

Some people drink chicory root coffee as a caffeine-free alternative.
The baker added powdered chicory root to improve the flavor of the bread.
Roasted chicory root is a common ingredient in New Orleans-style coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - a blue-flowered perennial plant whose roots are used as a coffee substitute

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkəri ruːt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkəri ruːt / (BrE)
the roasted and ground root of the chicory plant, often used as a substitute for or additive to coffee
root of the chicory plant used in food or drink

Examples

Some people prefer chicory root as a caffeine-free alternative to coffee.
The café serves a special blend of coffee with added chicory root for a richer flavor.
After roasting, chicory root develops a deep, earthy taste often used in traditional drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - a blue-flowered plant; its leaves and roots are used as food or drink ingredient
