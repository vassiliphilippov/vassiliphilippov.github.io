little old lady

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l oʊld ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl əʊld ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
an affectionate or mildly humorous term for an elderly woman, often perceived as gentle or harmless
affectionate term for an elderly woman

Examples

The little old lady waved at the children as they passed by her garden.
A little old lady asked me to help her cross the street.
Everyone in the town knows the little old lady who feeds the stray cats.

Synset Examples

The little old lady baked cookies for the neighborhood kids.
