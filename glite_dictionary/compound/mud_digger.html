mud digger

1. NOUN

/ mʌd ˈdɪɡɚ / (AmE), / mʌd ˈdɪɡə / (BrE)
a laborer whose job is to dig ditches or trenches, especially in muddy or wet ground
laborer who digs ditches

Examples

The construction crew hired a new mud digger to help lay the foundation for the building.
As a mud digger, he spent long hours working in wet and difficult conditions.
The job opening called for experienced mud diggers willing to work outdoors in all weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ditcher - a person who digs ditches
