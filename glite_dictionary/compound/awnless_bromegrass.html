awnless bromegrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔnlɪs ˈbroʊmˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɔːnlɪs ˈbrəʊmˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to Europe and temperate Asia, distinguished by its drought resistance, long creeping rhizomes, and the absence or extreme shortness of awns.
a drought-resistant perennial grass, lacking awns

Examples

Awnless bromegrass is often planted to prevent soil erosion in dry climates.
Farmers value awnless bromegrass for its resilience and ability to provide forage in harsh conditions.
Because awnless bromegrass spreads through long rhizomes, it quickly covers open ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bromegrass - a type of grass belonging to the genus Bromus

Etymology

From 'awnless' (without bristle-like appendages) and 'bromegrass' (from genus Bromus, Latinized form of Greek brōmos for oats).
