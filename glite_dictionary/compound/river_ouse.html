River Ouse

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪvər uz / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈuːz / (BrE)
a river in northeastern England that flows southeast to join the River Trent and form the Humber Estuary
river in northeastern England
british

Examples

The River Ouse flooded several nearby villages after days of heavy rain.
Many people enjoy boating and fishing along the River Ouse during the summer months.
York is a historic city that stands on the banks of the River Ouse.

Etymology

From Old English *ūsa*, possibly from a Celtic root meaning 'water' or 'slow-moving river'.
