lead sandwich

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˈsænwɪtʃ / (AmE), / lɛd ˈsænwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A humorous term for an especially heavy or filling sandwich; sometimes used metaphorically to describe a burdensome or difficult situation.
humorous term for a very heavy sandwich
informal

Examples

After that massive lead sandwich, I barely made it back to work.
The deli's signature lead sandwich is famous for its enormous size and weight.
He joked that finishing the report felt like eating a lead sandwich—surprisingly hard to get through.

Synset Examples

For lunch, I had a lead sandwich with extra cheese.
He ordered a lead sandwich filled with smoked turkey.
The kids devoured their lead sandwiches during lunch.
