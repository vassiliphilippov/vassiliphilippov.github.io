wheat germ

1. NOUN

/ wiːt dʒɜrm / (AmE), / wiːt dʒɜːm / (BrE)
the nutritious embryo of the wheat kernel, separated during milling and eaten as a source of vitamins and minerals
embryo of wheat grain, used as food

Examples

She likes to sprinkle wheat germ on her morning cereal for extra nutrition.
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of wheat germ to boost the fiber content.
Many health enthusiasts add wheat germ to smoothies for its vitamins and minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bran - outer layer of cereal grain, separated during milling
  • Degree Lower: endosperm - starchy part of a cereal grain
