Burma padauk

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrmə pəˈdɑʊk / (AmE), / ˈbɜːmə pəˈdaʊk / (BrE)
A tree native to India and Myanmar (Burma) that produces wood similar in appearance and use to mahogany.
tree yielding mahogany-like wood

Examples

The wood of the Burma padauk is often used in high-quality furniture making.
A craftsman admired the reddish-brown hue of the Burma padauk timber.
Many traditional carvings in Southeast Asia are made from Burma padauk wood.
