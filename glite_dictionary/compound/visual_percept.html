visual percept

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl pərˈsɛpt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl pəˈsɛpt / (BrE)
an image or impression formed in the mind as a result of what is seen, especially as studied in psychology or neuroscience
mental image formed by vision

Examples

The artist tried to capture his visual percept of the city skyline on canvas.
Each visual percept is influenced by past experiences and expectations.
After the operation, her visual percept of colors became much sharper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perception - the process of becoming aware of something through the senses

Etymology

From Latin 'visus' (seen) and 'perceptum' (perceived).
