form division

1. NOUN

/ fɔrm dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / fɔːm dɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
a former artificial taxonomic category for plants, used when the true evolutionary relationships were unknown; replaced by 'phylum' in 1993
obsolete botanical taxonomic category
archaic medical

Examples

The mosses were once grouped in a separate form division before genetic research clarified their classification.
Many traditional botanical textbooks refer to form division in their systems of classification.
By the 1990s, the use of form division had been largely replaced by 'phylum' in plant taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: phylum - primary taxonomic rank above class, which replaced form division in botany
