stop bath

1. NOUN

/ stɑp bæθ / (AmE), / stɒp bɑːθ / (BrE)
a weak acid solution used in photographic processing to halt the action of the developer on photographic film or paper
acid bath used in photography to stop development

Examples

After the film has been in the developer, it is quickly placed in the stop bath to prevent overdevelopment.
The stop bath helps to keep the processing time consistent between different rolls of film.
Always wear gloves when preparing a stop bath to avoid skin irritation from the acid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: developer - a chemical solution used to develop photographic film or paper
