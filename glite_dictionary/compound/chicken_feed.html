chicken feed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn fiːd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn fiːd / (BrE)
food, usually in the form of dry mash, specially prepared for feeding chickens and other poultry.
food mixture for poultry

Examples

The farmer poured the chicken feed into the trough for the hens every morning.
Rats can sometimes be attracted to spilled chicken feed around backyard coops.
She bought a large bag of chicken feed to last through the winter.
