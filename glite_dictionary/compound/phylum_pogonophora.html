phylum Pogonophora

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm poʊˌgɒnəˈfɔrə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm pəʊˌɡɒnəˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
a former phylum of marine worms known as beard worms, now generally classified within Siboglinidae
group of marine worms, beard worms

Examples

Scientists used deep-sea submersibles to collect specimens of phylum Pogonophora from hydrothermal vents.
Until recently, phylum Pogonophora was considered separate from annelids in biological classifications.
The unique feeding method of phylum Pogonophora involves absorbing nutrients directly from seawater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beard worm - common term for worms formerly classified as Pogonophora

Etymology

From Greek pōgōn (beard) and -phora (bearing), referring to the worm's bristle-like appendages.
