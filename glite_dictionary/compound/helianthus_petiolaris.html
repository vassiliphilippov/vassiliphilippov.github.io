Helianthus petiolaris

1. NOUN

/ hiˈliːənθəs ˌpiːti.oʊˈlɛərɪs / (AmE), / hiˈliːənθəs ˌpiːti.əˈleərɪs / (BrE)
A species of sunflower with slender, usually branching stems, native to central United States.
a species of sunflower

Examples

Helianthus petiolaris thrives in sandy soils across the central United States.
The prairie was covered with blooming Helianthus petiolaris in early summer.
Botanists distinguish Helianthus petiolaris from other sunflowers by its slender, branching stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with a large yellow flower head, of the genus Helianthus

Etymology

From Greek 'helios' (sun) + 'anthos' (flower) and Latin 'petiolaris' (having a petiole).
