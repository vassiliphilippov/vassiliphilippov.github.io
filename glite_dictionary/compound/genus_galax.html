genus Galax

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪlæks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪlæks / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen herbs native to the southeastern United States, known for their round leaves and small white flowers.
genus of evergreen herbs (SE United States)

Examples

The genus Galax is easily recognized by its shiny, heart-shaped leaves.
Botanists studying the genus Galax often focus on its distinctive flower structure.
Only a single species is widely recognized within the genus Galax, making it unique among related herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Galax - A plant of the genus Galax, especially Galax urceolata.

Etymology

Galax: from Greek 'galaxias' (milky), relating to the star-shaped flowers; 'genus' from Latin genus (kind, type).
