big brother

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
an older brother, especially one regarded with affection or as authoritative
an older brother

Examples

My big brother taught me how to ride a bicycle when I was six.
Whenever I have a problem at school, my big brother gives me advice.
As the big brother, he always looked out for his younger siblings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder brother - an older brother
  • Antonym: little brother - a younger brother
