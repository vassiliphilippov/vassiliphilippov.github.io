old money

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈmʌni / (AmE), / əʊld ˈmʌni / (BrE)
Wealth that has been inherited by members of established upper-class families, especially those who have held it for several generations.
inherited wealth of established families

Examples

She grew up surrounded by old money customs and traditions.
At the gala, the room was filled with both old money and newly rich entrepreneurs.
Some believe that old money families have an air of effortless sophistication.

Synset Examples

he was born with a silver spoon in his mouth
she is the daughter of old money from Massachusetts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new money - Wealth acquired recently, especially by people who have not previously belonged to the upper class

2. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈmʌni / (AmE), / əʊld ˈmʌni / (BrE)
People or families whose wealth has been inherited over several generations, often associated with long-standing social status and traditional values.
inherited wealth over generations

Examples

She always associated the understated style of old money with true sophistication.
Many influential figures in the city come from old money backgrounds.
The charity's donors include several members of old money families.

Synset Examples

She preferred the elegance of old money over new wealth.
Old money often attends exclusive events in the city.
They belonged to old money families, steeped in tradition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new money - people or families who have recently acquired wealth
