seed money

1. NOUN

/ sid ˈmʌni / (AmE), / siːd ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money provided as an initial investment to start a new business or project, especially to cover early expenses before further funding is secured
initial capital for starting a business

Examples

The company raised seed money from angel investors to develop its first prototype.
Without sufficient seed money, many startups fail to get off the ground.
She invested her savings as seed money for her new bakery business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: startup capital - money invested to start a new business
