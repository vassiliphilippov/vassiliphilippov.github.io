noonday shade

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊnˌdeɪ ʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈnəʊndeɪ ʃeɪd / (BrE)
the shadow or cool area provided by something during the middle of the day, especially when the sun is strongest
shade at midday

Examples

The workers rested in the noonday shade to escape the blazing sun.
She sat quietly in the noonday shade, enjoying her lunch and the peacefulness.
Travelers often seek noonday shade beneath large trees during their journey.

Synset Examples

They took a break under the noonday shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midday shade - shade during the middle of the day
