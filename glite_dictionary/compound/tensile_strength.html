tensile strength

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.səl streŋθ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.saɪl streŋθ / (BrE)
the maximum stress that a material can withstand while being stretched or pulled before breaking
maximum stress a material can withstand when stretched
medical

Examples

The steel cables have a high tensile strength, making them suitable for suspension bridges.
It is important to test the tensile strength of materials before using them in construction projects.
The tensile strength of this polymer exceeds that of many traditional plastics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compressive strength - the capacity of a material to withstand loads tending to reduce size

Etymology

From Latin 'tensus' (stretched) and 'strength.'
