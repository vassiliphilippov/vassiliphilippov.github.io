National Library of Medicine

1. PROPN

/ ˌnæʃənəl laɪˈbrɛri əv ˈmɛdɪsən / (AmE), / ˌnæʃənəl laɪˈbrəri əv ˈmɛdɪsən / (BrE)
the United States government library that serves as the world's largest medical library, providing access to biomedical information and research resources
the world's largest medical library in the U.S.

Examples

Researchers often use resources from the National Library of Medicine to access the latest medical studies.
The National Library of Medicine provides online databases, such as PubMed, for medical professionals and the public.
She found valuable historical documents in the National Library of Medicine archives for her graduate thesis.
