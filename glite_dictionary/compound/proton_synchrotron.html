proton synchrotron

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtɑn ˈsɪŋkroʊˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtɒn ˈsɪŋkrəˌtrɒn / (BrE)
a ring-shaped particle accelerator that increases the energy of protons to several billion electron volts
type of proton particle accelerator

Examples

The scientists used a proton synchrotron to accelerate particles for their high-energy experiments.
Europe's largest proton synchrotron has been operational since the 1950s.
Data collected from the proton synchrotron helped advance our understanding of subatomic physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchrotron - ring-shaped particle accelerator for charged particles

Etymology

From 'proton', after Greek 'prōtos' (first), and 'synchrotron', based on 'synchronous' and '-tron' (device).
