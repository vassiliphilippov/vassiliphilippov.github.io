Paris University

1. PROPN

/ ˌpærɪs juˈnɪvərsəti / (AmE), / ˌpærɪs juːˈnɪvəsəti / (BrE)
A historic university in Paris, regarded as the intellectual center of France, especially during the Middle Ages and Renaissance.
historic university in Paris

Examples

The medieval scholars at Paris University played a vital role in shaping Western thought.
She spent a semester abroad studying philosophy at Paris University.
Many Nobel laureates have been affiliated with Paris University throughout its long history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sorbonne - the historic seat and successor institution of the University of Paris

Etymology

From the name of the city Paris and Latin universitas (community, university).
