exoergic reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksoʊˈɜrdʒɪk riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksəʊˈɜːdʒɪk rɪˈækʃən / (BrE)
a nuclear reaction in which energy is released as a result of the process
nuclear reaction that releases energy

Examples

During nuclear fusion, an exoergic reaction results in the release of a large amount of energy.
An exoergic reaction can be identified by observing the increase in temperature during the process.
In this laboratory experiment, students studied both endoergic and exoergic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoergic reaction - nuclear reaction that absorbs energy

Etymology

From Greek exo- (outside) + ergon (work), referencing energy emitted outward.
