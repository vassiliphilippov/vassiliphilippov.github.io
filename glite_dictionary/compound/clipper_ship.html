clipper ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpər ˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈklɪpə ˌʃɪp / (BrE)
a large, fast sailing ship with tall masts and narrow hull, used especially in the mid-19th century for long voyages
fast 19th-century sailing ship

Examples

The clipper ship swiftly crossed the Atlantic despite rough seas.
During the gold rush era, many people traveled on a clipper ship to reach California quickly.
A clipper ship in full sail is a spectacular sight from the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schooner - a sailing ship with two or more masts, typically with the foremast smaller than the mainmast

Etymology

From 'clipper' (fast ship) + 'ship'; 'clip' here means 'to move quickly'.
