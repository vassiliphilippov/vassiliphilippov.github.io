siluriform fish

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlʊrəˌfɔrm fɪʃ / (AmE), / sɪˈljʊərɪfɔːm fɪʃ / (BrE)
any of numerous mostly freshwater fish belonging to the order Siluriformes, usually bottom-dwelling and characterized by whisker-like barbels around the mouth; commonly known as catfish.
freshwater fish of the order Siluriformes; catfish

Examples

The Amazon basin is home to a remarkable diversity of siluriform fish species.
Many siluriform fish have specialized adaptations for living on river bottoms.
Scientists discovered a new species of siluriform fish in the murky waters of the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - common name for fish of the order Siluriformes

Etymology

From modern Latin Siluriformes, from Latin Silurus (a kind of fish) and -form (shape, form).
