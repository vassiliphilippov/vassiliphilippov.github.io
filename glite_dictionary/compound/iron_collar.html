iron collar

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚn ˈkɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
a metal device placed around the neck, used historically as an instrument of execution by strangulation
metal strangulation device for executions

Examples

The executioner secured the iron collar around the prisoner's neck before the sentence was carried out.
In medieval Europe, the iron collar was a symbol of harsh justice and punishment.
The museum exhibit included an authentic iron collar once used in public executions.
