bright shiny object syndrome

1. NOUN

/ braɪt ˈʃaɪni ˈɑbdʒɛkt ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / braɪt ˈʃaɪni ˈɒbdʒɪkt ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a tendency to be easily distracted by new ideas or projects, often abandoning ongoing tasks for something new and attention-grabbing
tendency to be easily distracted by new ideas
informal

Examples

His bright shiny object syndrome made it difficult to finish any of his creative projects.
The team's bright shiny object syndrome caused them to chase every new trend without completing their original goals.
If you suffer from bright shiny object syndrome, you might struggle to stay committed to long-term tasks.

Synset Examples

Her bright shiny object syndrome led to unfinished projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distraction - lack of concentration due to diverted attention
  • Synonym: short attention span - inability to maintain focus for long periods
