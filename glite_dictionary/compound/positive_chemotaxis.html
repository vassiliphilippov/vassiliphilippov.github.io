positive chemotaxis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzɪtɪv ˌkiːmoʊˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪtɪv ˌkiːməʊˈtaksɪs / (BrE)
the movement of a cell or organism toward a higher concentration of a chemical substance
movement toward a chemical stimulus
medical

Examples

Many bacteria display positive chemotaxis when attracted to nutrients in their environment.
The immune response relies on positive chemotaxis to guide white blood cells to sites of infection.
Researchers studied how positive chemotaxis enables sperm cells to locate an egg.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: negative chemotaxis - movement away from a chemical stimulus

Etymology

From Greek 'chemo-' (relating to chemicals) and 'taxis' (arrangement, order, movement).
