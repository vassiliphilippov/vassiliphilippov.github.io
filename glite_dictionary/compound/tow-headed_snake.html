tow-headed snake

1. NOUN

/ toʊˌhɛdɪd ˈsneɪk / (AmE), / təʊˌhɛdɪd ˈsneɪk / (BrE)
A nonvenomous snake of grasslands and woodlands of western North America, resembling rubber with a tail and head of similar shape.
nonvenomous North American snake

Examples

The tow-headed snake is often mistaken for a worm because of its smooth skin and blunt tail.
While hiking, we spotted a tow-headed snake basking in the sun near some tall grass.
Children are sometimes fascinated by the harmless tow-headed snake due to its soft, rubbery appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber boa - A nonvenomous boa found in the western United States and British Columbia, looks and feels like rubber

Etymology

The name refers to its pale, tow-colored (light blonde) head and distinctive appearance.
