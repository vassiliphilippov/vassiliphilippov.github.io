noblesse oblige

1. NOUN

/ noʊˌblɛs oʊˈbliːʒ / (AmE), / nɒˌblɛs əʊˈbliːʒ / (BrE)
the idea that people of high social rank or wealth are morally obliged to act with generosity and honor toward those less privileged
responsibility of privileged to act honorably

Examples

He believed in noblesse oblige and always supported local charities generously.
The tradition of noblesse oblige guided many aristocrats to act with kindness toward their tenants.
Some argue that modern celebrities should practice noblesse oblige by using their influence for good causes.

Etymology

From French, literally 'nobility obliges'.
