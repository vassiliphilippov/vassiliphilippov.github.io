Surinam toad

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʊrɪnæm toʊd / (AmE), / ˈsʊrɪnam təʊd / (BrE)
a South American toad (Pipa pipa) known for incubating its young in pockets on the skin of its back
South American toad; carries young in skin on back

Examples

The Surinam toad is famous for the unusual way it raises its offspring in pockets on its back.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of the Surinam toad in the Amazon rainforest.
A documentary showed baby Surinam toads emerging from their mother's back.

Etymology

Named after Surinam (now Suriname), the South American country where the toad is found, and 'toad'.
