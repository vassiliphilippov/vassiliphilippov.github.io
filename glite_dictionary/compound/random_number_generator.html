random number generator

1. NOUN

/ ˈrændəm ˈnʌmbɚ ˌdʒɛn.ɚˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈræn.dəm ˈnʌm.bə ˌdʒɛn.əˈreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a device or algorithm that produces numbers in a sequence that lack any predictable pattern, typically used in computing, statistics, and games of chance.
device or algorithm for producing random numbers

Examples

A random number generator is essential for running secure online lotteries.
Most video games use a random number generator to produce unexpected events.
The scientist tested the random number generator to ensure its output was truly unpredictable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pseudo-random number generator - an algorithm that generates a sequence of numbers approximating true randomness
  • Degree Higher: true random number generator - a device that generates numbers using a physical random process
