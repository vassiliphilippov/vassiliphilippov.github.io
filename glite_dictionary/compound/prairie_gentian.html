prairie gentian

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A North American wildflower with large, upright, bell-shaped bluish flowers, commonly found in moist prairies and fields.
North American wildflower with bell-shaped bluish flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with blooming prairie gentian during the early summer.
Gardeners prize the prairie gentian for its striking blue and purple flowers.
We photographed a cluster of prairie gentian growing by the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eustoma - the genus to which prairie gentian belongs
