preparation fire

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən faɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən faɪə / (BrE)
artillery or other fire delivered on a target before an assault to weaken or disrupt enemy forces
military fire used before an assault

Examples

The commander ordered preparation fire to soften enemy defenses before advancing.
Heavy preparation fire was used to disrupt the opposing forces' supply lines.
The unit waited patiently as the preparation fire pounded the enemy bunkers.
