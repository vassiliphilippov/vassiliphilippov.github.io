primary winding

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈwaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ˈwʌɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the coil in a transformer or similar device that receives current from a power source and whose changing current induces current in a secondary coil
main coil in a transformer

Examples

The primary winding of the transformer receives the input voltage from the power supply.
If the primary winding is damaged, the transformer will not function correctly.
A changing current in the primary winding induces a voltage in the secondary coil.

Synset Examples

current through the primary coil induces current in the secondary coil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary winding - the coil in a transformer in which current is induced by the primary winding
