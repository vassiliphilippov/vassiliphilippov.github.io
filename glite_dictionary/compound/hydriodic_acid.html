hydriodic acid

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdraɪˈɑdɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / hɪˌdrɪˈɒdɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a solution of hydrogen iodide (HI) in water, typically colorless or yellow, known as a strong acid
a solution of hydrogen iodide in water
medical

Examples

Hydriodic acid is commonly used as a reagent in organic synthesis.
The laboratory stored hydriodic acid in tightly sealed bottles to prevent spoilage.
Due to its reactivity, handling hydriodic acid requires appropriate safety precautions.

Synset Examples

hydriodic acid is a strong acid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrogen iodide - a chemical compound with formula HI, a gas that forms hydriodic acid in water
  • Degree Lower: hydrochloric acid - a strong acid formed by dissolving hydrogen chloride in water

Etymology

From 'hydrogen' and 'iodic', relating to iodine.
