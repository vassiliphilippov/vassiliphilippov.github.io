omnidirectional range

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːmnɪdəˈrɛkʃənəl reɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌɒmnɪdəˈrɛkʃənəl reɪndʒ / (BrE)
a navigation system for aircraft composed of radio beacons that provide precise position and bearing information
a radio navigation system for aircraft

Examples

Many commercial pilots rely on the omnidirectional range system for accurate navigation in poor weather conditions.
The airport upgraded its omnidirectional range equipment to improve aircraft safety and efficiency.
Using the omnidirectional range, the pilot was able to determine the plane's exact position relative to the runway.

Etymology

From omni- (all) + directional range; technical aviation term from the mid-20th century.
