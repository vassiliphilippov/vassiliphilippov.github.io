French window

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A pair of glass doors, usually opening onto a garden or balcony, often set into the exterior wall of a building.
glass doors opening to outside

Examples

They replaced the old back door with elegant French windows that open onto the patio.
Sunlight streamed in through the French windows overlooking the garden.
The living room has large French windows that make the space feel bright and airy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French door - a pair of doors which have glass panes extending for most of their length

Etymology

From 'French' (relating to France) + 'window', reflecting the style's origin in French architecture.
