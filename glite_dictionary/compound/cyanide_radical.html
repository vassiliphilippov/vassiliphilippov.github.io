cyanide radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəˌnaɪd ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪənaɪd ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
the univalent chemical group -CN, consisting of one carbon atom triple-bonded to a nitrogen atom, found in cyanide compounds
the -CN chemical group

Examples

The cyanide radical is highly reactive and can be found in many industrial compounds.
When combined with certain metals, the cyanide radical forms stable salts.
Accidental exposure to a substance containing the cyanide radical can be extremely dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanide - a chemical compound containing the -CN group

Etymology

From 'cyanide' (from Greek 'kyanos' meaning dark blue) and 'radical' (from Late Latin 'radicalis', from 'radix' meaning root).
