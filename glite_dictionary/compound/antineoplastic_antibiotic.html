antineoplastic antibiotic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌnioʊˈplæstɪk ˌæn(t)iˈbaɪɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌniːəʊˈplæstɪk ˌæn(t)iˈbaiətɪk / (BrE)
an antibiotic drug used to treat cancer by inhibiting the growth of cancerous cells, commonly used in chemotherapy
antibiotic used in cancer treatment
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antineoplastic antibiotic as part of her chemotherapy regimen.
Research continues to find new antineoplastic antibiotic drugs with fewer side effects.
Some antineoplastic antibiotics are derived from natural sources like bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemotherapy - treatment of disease, especially cancer, using chemical substances

Etymology

From 'antineoplastic', meaning 'against new growth (tumors)', and 'antibiotic', from Greek 'anti-' (against) + 'bios' (life).
