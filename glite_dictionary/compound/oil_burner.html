oil burner

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈbɜrnər / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
A furnace or heating device that burns oil as fuel to produce heat.
furnace that uses oil as fuel

Examples

We had to replace the oil burner last winter when it stopped working.
The technician came to service the oil burner before the start of the cold season.
Many older homes still rely on an oil burner for heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furnace - an enclosed structure for heating
