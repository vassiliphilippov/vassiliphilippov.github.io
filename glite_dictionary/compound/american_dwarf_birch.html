American dwarf birch

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən dwɔrf bɝtʃ / (AmE), / əˈmer.ɪ.kən dwɔːf bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A small shrub native to cold regions of North America and Greenland, known for its low, spreading growth habit.
cold-region North American shrub

Examples

The American dwarf birch thrives in the colder regions of Alaska and northern Canada.
Researchers are studying the resilience of the American dwarf birch to changing climates in Greenland.
Gardeners often choose the American dwarf birch for landscaping in areas with poor, rocky soils.
