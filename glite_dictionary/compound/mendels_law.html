Mendel's law

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.dəlz lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.dəlz lɔː / (BrE)
Either of the two fundamental principles of heredity identified by Gregor Mendel, describing how traits are inherited from parent to offspring.
genetic principles of heredity by Mendel

Examples

According to Mendel's law, traits are passed from parents to offspring in predictable patterns.
Mendel's law laid the groundwork for modern genetics.
Biology students often learn about Mendel's law in their first genetics course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law of segregation - Mendel's principle stating that allele pairs separate during gamete formation
  • Synonym: law of independent assortment - Mendel's principle that genes for different traits are inherited independently

Etymology

Named after Gregor Mendel (1822–1884), the scientist who formulated these principles.
