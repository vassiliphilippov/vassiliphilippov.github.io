auditory center

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdətɔri ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtəri ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
the region of the brain in the temporal lobe that processes signals from the ear and is responsible for hearing
region of the brain responsible for hearing

Examples

Damage to the auditory center can result in partial or complete loss of hearing.
Researchers are studying how the auditory center processes language and music.
Signals from the cochlea are transmitted to the auditory center via the auditory nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditory cortex - the part of the brain involved in processing sound information
  • Antonym: visual center - the part of the brain involved in processing visual information
