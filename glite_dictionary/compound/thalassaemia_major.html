thalassaemia major

1. NOUN

/ θəˌlæsəˈmiːə ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / θəˌlasəˈmiːə ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
A severe inherited blood disorder in which the body produces little or no hemoglobin, leading to anemia, skeletal abnormalities, and organ enlargement.
severe inherited blood disorder with little or no hemoglobin
medical

Examples

Children diagnosed with thalassaemia major require regular blood transfusions to survive.
The symptoms of thalassaemia major often become apparent within the first two years of life.
Advances in medical care have improved life expectancy for those with thalassaemia major.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thalassaemia - a group of inherited blood disorders affecting hemoglobin production
  • Degree Lower: sickle cell anemia - a hereditary blood disorder characterized by abnormal hemoglobin

Etymology

From Greek 'thalassa' (sea) and '-aemia' (relating to blood), referring to its prevalence around the Mediterranean.
