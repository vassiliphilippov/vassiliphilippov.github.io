low-level radioactive waste

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ ˈlɛvəl ˌreɪdioʊˈæktɪv weɪst / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈlɛvəl ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv weɪst / (BrE)
radioactive waste made up of materials that have been briefly exposed to radiation, such as those from medical tests or hospital procedures
waste briefly exposed to radioactivity
medical

Examples

Hospitals routinely dispose of low-level radioactive waste generated from diagnostic tests.
The plant uses a special facility to store low-level radioactive waste safely.
Regulations require the careful handling of low-level radioactive waste to protect workers and the environment.
