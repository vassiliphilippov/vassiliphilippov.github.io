Bermuda grass

1. NOUN

/ bərˈmjuːdə ɡræs / (AmE), / bəˈmjuːdə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a creeping grass native to southern Europe and widely grown in warm regions for lawns and pastures, especially in the southern United States and India
creeping lawn and pasture grass

Examples

The golf course was covered in lush Bermuda grass that was carefully maintained.
Because of its resilience, many lawns in the South are planted with Bermuda grass.
Farmers often use Bermuda grass as pasture for cows due to its rapid growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cynodon dactylon - scientific name for Bermuda grass
  • Synonym: Kentucky bluegrass - another common grass species used for lawns and pastures

Etymology

Named after Bermuda, where it was first described.
