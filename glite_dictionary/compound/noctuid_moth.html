noctuid moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑk.tju.ɪd mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.tjʊ.ɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
A usually dull-colored, medium-sized, nocturnal moth whose larvae are often destructive agricultural pests; belonging to the family Noctuidae.
medium-sized nocturnal moth, many are crop pests

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control noctuid moth infestations in their crops.
The noctuid moth is mainly active at night and is attracted to light.
Larvae of the noctuid moth can cause significant damage to vegetables and grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutworm - larva of certain noctuid moths, known for crop damage

Etymology

From modern Latin Noctuidae, from Latin 'noctua' (night owl), referring to their nocturnal habits.
