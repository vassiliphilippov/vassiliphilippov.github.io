sin bin

1. NOUN

/ sɪn bɪn / (AmE), / sɪn bɪn / (BrE)
a designated area where a player must temporarily stay as a penalty for an infringement of the rules, typically in sports such as rugby and ice hockey
temporary penalty area in sports

Examples

After committing a foul, the referee sent him straight to the sin bin.
She spent two minutes in the sin bin after an illegal tackle.
Players in the sin bin must wait for the penalty time to expire before returning to the field.

Synset Examples

The player was sent to the sin bin for a foul.
He spent ten minutes in the sin bin for arguing.
She joked about being in the sin bin for her bad behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penalty box - area for players serving penalties in ice hockey and some other sports

Etymology

From 'sin' (meaning wrongdoing) + 'bin' (container), originally British sporting slang.
