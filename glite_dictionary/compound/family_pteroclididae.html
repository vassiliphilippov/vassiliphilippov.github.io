family Pteroclididae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌtɛrəˈklɪdɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌtɛərəˈklɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as sandgrouses, found mainly in dry regions of the Old World.
family of birds: sandgrouses

Examples

The family Pteroclididae is well adapted to life in arid environments.
Birdwatchers often travel to deserts to observe members of the family Pteroclididae.
Unlike pigeons, the family Pteroclididae has specialized feathers for carrying water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandgrouse - any bird of the family Pteroclididae, having stocky bodies and feathered feet

Etymology

From New Latin 'Pteroclididae', based on the type genus 'Pterocles', from Greek 'pteron' (wing) and 'kleis' (key, in allusion to the bird's shape).
