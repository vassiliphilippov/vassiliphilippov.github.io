Special Weapons and Tactics team

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl ˈwɛpənz ənd ˈtæk.tɪks tim / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃ(ə)l ˈwɛp(ə)nz ənd ˈtak.tɪks tiːm / (BrE)
a specially trained group of police officers equipped to handle particularly violent or dangerous situations
police unit trained for high-risk situations
american

Examples

The city called in the Special Weapons and Tactics team to resolve the hostage crisis.
A Special Weapons and Tactics team arrived quickly at the scene after reports of an armed suspect.
Many police departments maintain a Special Weapons and Tactics team for situations requiring advanced training and equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SWAT team - abbreviated form for Special Weapons and Tactics team

Etymology

Acronym SWAT, first used in U.S. police forces in the 1960s; from 'special weapons' and 'tactics'.
