muzzle loader

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌzəl ˌloʊdər / (AmE), / ˈmʌzl̩ ˌləʊdə / (BrE)
An old type of firearm that is loaded from the open end of the barrel (the muzzle), rather than from the breech.
old firearm loaded via muzzle

Examples

The reenactor demonstrated how to fire a muzzle loader at the historical festival.
In the 18th century, soldiers often relied on a muzzle loader in battle.
Hunting with a muzzle loader requires patience and skill due to the slow reloading process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: breechloader - a firearm loaded from the rear end (breech) instead of the muzzle

Etymology

From 'muzzle' (open end of a gun barrel) and 'loader' (one who loads), describing its loading method.
