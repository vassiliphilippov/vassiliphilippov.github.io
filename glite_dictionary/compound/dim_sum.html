dim sum

1. NOUN

/ dɪm ˈsʌm / (AmE), / dɪm ˈsʌm / (BrE)
A style of Chinese cuisine consisting of a variety of small dishes, such as steamed or fried dumplings, served with tea.
Chinese cuisine of small dishes with tea

Examples

We went to a famous restaurant in Chinatown to enjoy traditional dim sum on Sunday morning.
The waiter wheeled over a cart filled with different types of dim sum for us to choose from.
She tried dim sum for the first time and loved the steamed dumplings and buns.

Etymology

From Cantonese 點心 (dim2 sam1), meaning 'touch the heart.'
