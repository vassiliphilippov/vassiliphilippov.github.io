food pyramid

1. NOUN

/ fuːd ˈpɪrəmɪd / (AmE), / fuːd ˈpɪərəmɪd / (BrE)
(ecology) a graphical representation of the hierarchy of food chains in an ecosystem, showing different levels from producers at the base to top predators at the apex.
hierarchy of food chains in an ecosystem

Examples

The food pyramid illustrates how energy is transferred from plants to top predators.
Biologists use the food pyramid to explain the scarcity of large carnivores compared to herbivores.
A disruption at any level of the food pyramid can affect the entire ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trophic level - distinct levels in a food chain, each occupied by organisms with similar feeding roles
  • Synonym: food chain - a sequence of organisms each dependent on the next as a source of food
