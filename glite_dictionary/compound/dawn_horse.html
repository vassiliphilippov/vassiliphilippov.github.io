dawn horse

1. NOUN

/ dɔn hɔrs / (AmE), / dɔːn hɔːs / (BrE)
an extinct, primitive, dog-sized horse with four toes on its front feet, which lived during the Eocene epoch; the earliest known member of the horse family (genus Hyracotherium or Eohippus)
extinct early horse of the Eocene epoch

Examples

The dawn horse roamed the ancient forests over 50 million years ago.
Fossils of the dawn horse reveal its small size and multiple toes.
Scientists study the evolution of the horse from the dawn horse to modern breeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eohippus - the genus of the earliest horse, also called dawn horse

Etymology

Named for its early (dawn) appearance in horse evolution; generic reference to species like Hyracotherium or Eohippus.
