double knit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl nɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl nɪt / (BrE)
a type of knit fabric produced with two sets of needles to create a double thickness, joined by interlocking stitches
thick, interlocked knit fabric

Examples

The jacket is made from a double knit fabric, making it both sturdy and comfortable.
Many vintage dresses from the 1970s were crafted from double knit due to its durability.
I prefer double knit when making structured garments since it holds its shape well.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: jersey - a soft, stretchy knit fabric, usually lighter than double knit
