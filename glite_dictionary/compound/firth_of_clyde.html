Firth of Clyde

1. PROPN

/ fɝːθ əv klaɪd / (AmE), / fəːθ əv klaɪd / (BrE)
an inlet of the sea on the southwestern coast of Scotland, forming part of the western seaboard and opening into the North Channel
inlet of the sea in southwestern Scotland

Examples

The Firth of Clyde is known for its stunning scenery and rich maritime history.
Ferries cross the Firth of Clyde to connect the mainland with the islands.
Many species of marine life can be found in the waters of the Firth of Clyde.

Etymology

Firth from Scots, meaning 'inlet' or 'estuary,' and Clyde being the name of the river; River Clyde from Gaelic 'Cluaidh.'
