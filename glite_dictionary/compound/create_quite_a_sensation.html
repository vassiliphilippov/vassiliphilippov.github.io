to create quite a sensation

1. VERB

/ kriˈeɪt kwaɪt ə sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / kriˈeɪt kwaɪt ə sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
to attract a great deal of public attention or excitement; to cause widespread interest, surprise, or enthusiasm.
attract much attention or excitement

Examples

The artist's new exhibition created quite a sensation in the local art community.
Their sudden engagement created quite a sensation among their friends and family.
The innovative product launch created quite a sensation in the tech world.

Synset Examples

The new restaurant in town is creating quite a sensation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cause a stir - to provoke public interest or excitement
  • Antonym: go unnoticed - to not attract attention or interest
