safety squeeze play

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti skwiz pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti skwiːz pleɪ / (BrE)
a baseball play in which a runner on third base waits for the batter to bunt successfully before attempting to run home, reducing the risk of being put out
baseball bunt play with runner on third base

Examples

The manager called for a safety squeeze play to try to score the tying run.
During the safety squeeze play, the runner waited until the bunt was down before heading home.
Our team executed a perfect safety squeeze play in the ninth inning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suicide squeeze - baseball play where the runner on third starts for home as the pitcher delivers the ball
