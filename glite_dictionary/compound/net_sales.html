net sales

1. NOUN

/ nɛt seɪlz / (AmE), / nɛt seɪlz / (BrE)
the total revenue from sales after subtracting customer discounts, returns, allowances, and freight out
total sales minus discounts and returns

Examples

After deducting returns and allowances, the company's net sales amounted to $950,000 for the quarter.
Analysts often look at net sales rather than gross sales to evaluate a company's true performance.
The annual report showed a significant increase in net sales compared to the previous year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gross sales - total sales before any deductions
