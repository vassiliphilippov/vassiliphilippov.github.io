age norm

1. NOUN

/ eɪdʒ nɔrm / (AmE), / eɪdʒ nɔːm / (BrE)
The average age at which a particular ability, behavior, or performance is expected to appear in a population.
average expected age for a milestone

Examples

The pediatrician explained that walking at twelve months is considered an age norm for toddlers.
Researchers compare children's development to the age norms established by large studies.
Failing to meet a certain age norm may prompt further assessment for developmental delays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milestone - a significant stage or event in development
  • Synonym: norm - a standard or pattern, especially of social behavior
