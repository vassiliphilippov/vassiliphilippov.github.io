lanceolate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænsiəˌleɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈlænsiəˌleɪt liːf / (BrE)
a leaf that is shaped like a lance head, tapering to a point at both ends and widest in the middle
leaf shaped like a lance head

Examples

The willow has a distinctive lanceolate leaf that tapers at both ends.
Botanists often identify plants by the presence of a lanceolate leaf among their foliage.
With its long, narrow, and pointed shape, the lanceolate leaf helps the plant reduce water loss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ovate leaf - a leaf shape that is egg-shaped, broadest near the base
  • Degree Lower: linear leaf - a leaf shape that is very narrow and elongated, with almost parallel sides

Etymology

From Latin 'lanceolatus' (lance-shaped), from 'lancea' (lance).
