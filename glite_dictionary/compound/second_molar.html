second molar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈmoʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈməʊlə / (BrE)
The seventh permanent tooth from the center of the mouth on either side of the upper or lower jaw.
seventh permanent tooth in jaw

Examples

The dentist extracted my upper left second molar due to severe decay.
Children usually get their second molar around the age of twelve.
A filling was necessary for the second molar after the cavity was found.

Synset Examples

The dentist told me there's a cavity in my second molar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: first molar - the sixth permanent tooth from the center of the mouth on either side
  • Degree Higher: third molar - the eighth and last permanent tooth, also called the wisdom tooth
