gentian violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn(t)ʃən ˈvaɪəlɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnʃən ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
a green crystalline compound that turns violet in water, used as a dye, stain, and antiseptic, particularly in medicine and biology
a violet dye and antiseptic used in medicine
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician stained the slide with gentian violet to better observe the bacteria.
Doctors sometimes use gentian violet to treat minor fungal infections of the skin.
The shirt was ruined when a bottle of gentian violet spilled during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crystal violet - alternative name for gentian violet; a synthetic dye and medical antiseptic

Etymology

Named after the color resemblance to gentian flowers and 'violet' for its color; also known as crystal violet.
