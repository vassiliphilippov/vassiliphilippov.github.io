hard-shell crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrd ˌʃɛl kræb / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ˌʃɛl kræb / (BrE)
an edible crab that has not recently molted and therefore has a hard shell
edible crab with a hard shell

Examples

The restaurant specializes in hard-shell crab dishes during the summer season.
Unlike soft-shell varieties, hard-shell crabs require careful cracking to access the meat.
She bought fresh hard-shell crab from the seafood market for tonight's dinner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft-shell crab - an edible crab that has recently molted and has a soft shell
