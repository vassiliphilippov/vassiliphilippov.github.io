multi-seeded

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˈsidɪd / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈsiːdɪd / (BrE)
Containing or having many seeds.
having many seeds

Examples

The farmer planted a multi-seeded variety of tomato to increase his yield.
This bread is especially nutritious because it is made with a multi-seeded flour blend.
You can tell the fruit is multi-seeded by the number of seeds visible when cut open.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seedless - having no seeds

Etymology

Formed from the Latin root 'multi-' (many) and 'seeded' (bearing seeds).
