powdered milk

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdərd mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəd mɪlk / (BrE)
milk from which the water has been removed, leaving a dry powder that can be stored and reconstituted with water
dehydrated milk product

Examples

During the camping trip, we used powdered milk in our coffee instead of fresh milk.
Many recipes for bread call for powdered milk to improve texture and flavor.
Because it doesn’t spoil easily, powdered milk is often kept in emergency food supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry milk - milk that has been dried to a powder
  • Antonym: condensed milk - milk thickened by evaporating much of its water content, usually sweetened
