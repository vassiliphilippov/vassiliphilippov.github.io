posterior cerebral artery

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈstɪriər səˈriːbrəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə səˈriːbrəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that arises from the basilar artery in the brain and divides into three branches, supplying blood to the posterior part of the cerebral hemisphere
a brain artery from the basilar artery
medical

Examples

The posterior cerebral artery supplies blood to the occipital lobe of the brain.
An aneurysm in the posterior cerebral artery can result in visual disturbances.
During surgery, care was taken not to damage the posterior cerebral artery.

Etymology

From Latin 'posterior' (behind, later) and 'cerebralis' (of the brain), referring to its location and the area it supplies.
