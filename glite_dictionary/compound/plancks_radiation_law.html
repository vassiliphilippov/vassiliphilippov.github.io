Planck's radiation law

1. NOUN

/ plæŋks ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən lɔ / (AmE), / plæŋks ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən lɔː / (BrE)
an equation that describes the distribution of energy emitted at different wavelengths by a perfect black body at a given temperature
law describing black body radiation

Examples

The discovery of Planck's radiation law was a milestone in the development of quantum theory.
Scientists use Planck's radiation law to predict the spectrum of stars and other celestial bodies.
Understanding Planck's radiation law helped resolve discrepancies between observed and theoretical black body radiation curves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black body - an idealized physical body that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation

Etymology

Named after German physicist Max Planck (1858–1947).
