pressure pad

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər pæd / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə pæd / (BrE)
a device that detects and responds to weight or pressure, often used to trigger an action such as activating an alarm
device triggered by pressure or weight

Examples

The museum uses a pressure pad to detect if a painting is moved.
Stepping on the pressure pad will automatically open the sliding doors.
The security system relies on a pressure pad hidden under the floor mat.

Synset Examples

The pressure pad activated the alarm when stepped on.
The pressure pad is sensitive enough for quick responses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensor - a device that detects or measures a physical property and records, indicates, or otherwise responds to it
