mercury chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːkjʊri klaɪˈɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊri klɔːˈraɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous white crystalline compound of mercury, used as a pesticide, antiseptic, or wood preservative
poisonous mercury-based chemical compound

Examples

The scientist handled mercury chloride with extreme caution due to its toxic properties.
Historically, mercury chloride was often used as a preservative for wood and as an antiseptic.
You must store mercury chloride in a secure, clearly labeled container to prevent accidental exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrosive sublimate - another name for mercury chloride
  • Synonym: mercury - a dense, silvery d-block element, liquid at room temperature
  • Synonym: mercury(II) chloride - the chemical name for mercury chloride

Etymology

From 'mercury', the element, and 'chloride', from Latin 'chloridum' (chlorine compound).
