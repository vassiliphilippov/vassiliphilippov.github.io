witness protection

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtnəs prəˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs prəˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
an official program that protects individuals who testify as witnesses in criminal cases from threats or harm, often by relocating and providing them with new identities
official program to protect criminal case witnesses
legal

Examples

After testifying against the mob boss, he entered witness protection for his own safety.
The authorities arranged witness protection for several key witnesses in the trial.
She struggled to adjust to her new life under witness protection.

Synset Examples

Witness protection was offered in the interest of justice.
