blackfoot daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækfuːt ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈblækfʊt ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a low-growing, bushy perennial plant of the sunflower family, with white, daisy-like flowers, native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico
a white-flowered desert plant

Examples

The blackfoot daisy thrives in dry, rocky soils where other flowers struggle to grow.
Gardeners appreciate the blackfoot daisy for its long blooming season and drought tolerance.
We spotted a cluster of blackfoot daisies while hiking through the Arizona desert.
