greylag goose

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌlæɡ ɡus / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌlæɡ ɡuːs / (BrE)
a common European wild goose with grey plumage, believed to be the ancestor of many domestic geese breeds
a species of grey wild goose of Europe

Examples

Flocks of greylag geese can often be seen migrating over northern Europe in the autumn.
The greylag goose is considered the ancestor of most domestic goose breeds.
A pair of greylag geese made their nest near the river bank this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck, related to ducks and swans

Etymology

From 'grey' (referring to its colour) and 'lag' (Old Norse 'laggr', meaning 'lagging behind', possibly referencing its late migration).
