federal savings bank

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəl ˈseɪvɪŋz bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl ˈseɪvɪŋz baŋk / (BrE)
A savings bank in the United States that is federally chartered and regulated.
federally chartered US savings bank

Examples

The new branch of the federal savings bank opened last week in our neighborhood.
Depositors at a federal savings bank are protected by federal deposit insurance.
She got her first home loan from a local federal savings bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrift institution - a financial institution that specializes in accepting savings deposits and making mortgage loans
  • Antonym: commercial bank - a bank that offers services to the general public and to companies
