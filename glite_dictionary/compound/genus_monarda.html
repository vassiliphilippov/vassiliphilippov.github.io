genus Monarda

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs moʊˈnɑrdə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məʊˈnɑːdə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic North American herbs in the mint family (Lamiaceae), commonly known as beebalm, horsemint, or wild bergamot.
a genus of aromatic North American herbs

Examples

The genus Monarda includes several plants prized for their showy flowers and aromatic leaves.
Gardeners often cultivate species in the genus Monarda to attract bees and butterflies.
Native Americans historically used members of the genus Monarda for medicinal purposes.

Etymology

From New Latin Monarda, named after Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes (1493–1588).
