buffalo gnat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəloʊ næt / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ nat / (BrE)
A small, blackish, stout-bodied biting fly with aquatic larvae that sucks the blood of birds, humans, and other mammals.
bloodsucking biting fly (insect)

Examples

Hikers are often bothered by buffalo gnats near rivers in the spring.
A swarm of buffalo gnats can make outdoor activities unpleasant.
The veterinarian warned about the dangers that buffalo gnats pose to livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black fly - another common name for these biting flies
