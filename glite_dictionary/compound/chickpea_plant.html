chickpea plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkˌpiː plænt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.piː plɑːnt / (BrE)
An annual Asiatic plant (Cicer arietinum) cultivated for its short pods containing one or two edible seeds known as chickpeas.
plant producing edible chickpeas

Examples

Farmers in India cultivate the chickpea plant for its nutritious seeds.
The chickpea plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.
Researchers are studying the chickpea plant for its drought-resistant qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickpea - the edible seed produced by the chickpea plant

Etymology

From 'chickpea', which is believed to derive from the French 'chiche' (from Latin 'cicer'), and 'plant'.
