isles of Langerhans

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪlz əv ˈlæŋɡɚˌhænz / (AmE), / ˌaɪlz əv ˈlæŋɡəhænz / (BrE)
groups of cells in the pancreas that produce and secrete insulin and other hormones
cell clusters in the pancreas producing hormones
medical

Examples

The isles of Langerhans play a crucial role in regulating blood sugar levels.
Researchers are studying the isles of Langerhans to better understand diabetes.
Damage to the isles of Langerhans can lead to insufficient insulin production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancreas - a large gland behind the stomach, involved in digestion and hormone production

Etymology

Named after German pathologist Paul Langerhans (1847–1888), who discovered these cell clusters.
