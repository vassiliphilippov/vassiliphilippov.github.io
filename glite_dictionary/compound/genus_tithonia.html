genus Tithonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs tɪˈθoʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs tɪˈθəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
A genus of robust flowering herbs native to Mexico and Central America, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower.
a genus of Mexican sunflowers

Examples

The genus Tithonia includes several species popular for their bright orange flowers.
Gardeners often plant members of the genus Tithonia to attract butterflies.
Research into the drought resistance of genus Tithonia has increased interest in these ornamental herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican sunflower - common name for plants of the genus Tithonia

Etymology

From New Latin 'Tithonia', named after Tithonus from Greek mythology.
