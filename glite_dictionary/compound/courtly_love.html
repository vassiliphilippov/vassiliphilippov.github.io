courtly love

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtli lʌv / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtli lʌv / (BrE)
a highly stylized and conventional form of romantic affection developed in the Middle Ages, often characterized by chivalry, secrecy, and unattainable love
medieval code of romantic love

Examples

The ideals of courtly love are reflected in many medieval stories of knights and ladies.
In her literature class, she studied the theme of courtly love in Arthurian legends.
Courtly love often involved secret admiration and poetic expressions rather than direct courtship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalry - the medieval knightly system with its religious, moral, and social code

Etymology

From 'courtly' (relating to a royal court) + 'love'; term originates from medieval European courts.
