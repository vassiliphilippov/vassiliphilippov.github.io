Nineteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪnˌtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt tu ði ˌjuːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts ˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˈnaɪntiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt tuː ði ˌjuːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
an amendment to the U.S. Constitution, adopted in 1920, that guarantees the right to vote cannot be denied or abridged on the basis of sex
U.S. constitutional amendment granting women's suffrage
legal american

Examples

The Nineteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution marked a significant milestone in the American women's rights movement.
Many historians credit the Nineteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution with transforming the political landscape of the country.
Ratified in 1920, the Nineteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution finally granted women the right to vote nationwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
  • Degree Lower: Fifteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution - U.S. constitutional amendment prohibiting denial of vote based on race
