antimalarial drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌænti məˈlɛriəl drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌænti məˈleəriəl drʌɡ / (BrE)
A medicinal drug used to prevent or treat malaria.
drug to prevent or treat malaria
medical

Examples

Many travelers to tropical countries are prescribed antimalarial drug regimens before departure.
The development of resistance to a common antimalarial drug poses serious public health challenges.
Researchers are testing a new antimalarial drug that may be more effective against drug-resistant strains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antimalarial - A substance that prevents or treats malaria

Etymology

From anti- (against) + malaria + -al (adjective-forming suffix), modeled on 'antibacterial'; 'malaria' from Italian 'mala aria' (bad air).
