working papers

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ ˌpeɪpərz / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ ˌpeɪpəz / (BrE)
documents that record the details and steps taken in carrying out a project, especially for reference or review, such as in accounting or auditing.
documents recording project activities

Examples

The accountant kept detailed working papers to support every entry in the final report.
Auditors reviewed last year's working papers before starting the new audit.
All working papers must be filed and stored for future inspection.

Synset Examples

the auditor was required to produce his working papers

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ ˌpeɪpɚz / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ ˌpeɪpəz / (BrE)
a legal document providing information or authorization required for employment, especially for minors or non-citizens in certain countries
legal employment authorization documents
legal

Examples

Many students under 18 must obtain working papers before starting a part-time job.
The company would not hire her without valid working papers.
Immigrants often need to provide working papers when applying for employment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work permit - official document allowing a person to work in a country
