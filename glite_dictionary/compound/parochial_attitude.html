parochial attitude

1. NOUN

/ pəˈroʊkiəl ˈætɪtjud / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊkiəl ˈætɪtjuːd / (BrE)
a narrow-minded or limited perspective, especially one that is focused only on local or small concerns rather than broader issues
narrow or limited outlook

Examples

Her parochial attitude made it difficult for her to appreciate different cultures.
Leaders with a parochial attitude may resist innovative ideas from outside their immediate circle.
The committee criticized the parochial attitude that was holding back organizational growth.

Synset Examples

Their parochial attitudes hindered progress in the community.
She found the town’s parochial mindset stifling.
His parochial views limited his understanding of global issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
  • Synonym: provincial - having a limited or narrow outlook, often considered unsophisticated

Etymology

From 'parochial' meaning relating to a parish (from Late Latin 'parochia') and 'attitude'.
