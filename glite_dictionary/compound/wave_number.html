wave number

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪv ˌnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈweɪv ˌnʌmbə / (BrE)
the number of wavelengths per unit distance, defined as the reciprocal of the wavelength of a wave, commonly used to describe electromagnetic or sound waves
number of wavelengths per unit distance

Examples

The infrared spectrum is often analyzed by measuring the wave number of different absorption peaks.
Scientists use wave number to express the frequency of vibrations in molecular spectroscopy.
The wave number increases as the wavelength decreases.
