great anteater

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈæntiˌɪtər / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈæntiːtə / (BrE)
a large, shaggy-haired, toothless mammal of South America, with a long snout, long tongue, and powerful claws, known for feeding on ants and termites
large toothless South American mammal

Examples

The great anteater uses its long tongue to extract ants from their nests.
A great anteater can eat thousands of insects in a single day.
While hiking in Brazil, we spotted a great anteater moving slowly through the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant anteater - another common name for the great anteater
