closet drama

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑzət ˈdrɑmə / (AmE), / ˈklɒzɪt ˈdrɑːmə / (BrE)
A play written to be read rather than performed on stage.
play meant for reading, not performance

Examples

Mary preferred to write closet drama because she enjoyed exploring characters without worrying about stage directions.
Shelley's 'Prometheus Unbound' is often considered a classic example of closet drama.
Although it was never performed, his closet drama gained attention among literary critics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drama - a play intended for theatrical performance

Etymology

From 'closet' (private room) and 'drama'; describes plays intended for private reading, not public performance.
