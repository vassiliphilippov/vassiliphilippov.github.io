Kickapoo language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkəˌpu ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɪkəpuː ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Kickapoo people, primarily in parts of the United States and Mexico
language of the Kickapoo people

Examples

She is studying the Kickapoo language as part of her research on Native American cultures.
Efforts are being made to preserve and revitalize the Kickapoo language among younger generations.
The Kickapoo language contains many words that reflect the tribe's close relationship with nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - relating to the family of North American indigenous languages including Kickapoo

Etymology

Kickapoo is from the name of the Kickapoo people, an Algonquian-speaking Native American tribe.
