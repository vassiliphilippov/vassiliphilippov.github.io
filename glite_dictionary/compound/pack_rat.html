pack rat

1. NOUN

/ pæk ræt / (AmE), / pæk ræt / (BrE)
A person who collects or hoards unnecessary or discarded items.
person who compulsively collects things

Examples

My grandmother is a real pack rat and keeps old magazines from decades ago.
He became a pack rat, unwilling to throw away anything, no matter how useless it seemed.
Despite her reputation as a pack rat, Lisa finally cleaned out her overflowing attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarder - person who accumulates things compulsively

Etymology

Originally from the North American rodent known for hoarding, adopted figuratively for people in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæk ˌræt / (AmE), / ˈpak ˌrat / (BrE)
a North American rodent of the genus Neotoma, known for its bushy tail and tendency to hoard food and objects
hoarding North American rodent

Examples

The pack rat builds elaborate nests from twigs and household items.
Hiking in the desert, we spotted a pack rat scurrying under a rock.
Pack rats are attracted to shiny objects, sometimes carrying them back to their nests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodrat - another name for a rodent in the genus Neotoma
