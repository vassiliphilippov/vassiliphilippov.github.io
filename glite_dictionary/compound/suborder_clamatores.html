suborder Clamatores

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌɔrdər kləˈmætəriːz / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːdə kləˈmætəriːz / (BrE)
a former or alternative taxonomic group in some bird classification systems, comprising passerine birds with simple vocal organs and limited singing ability; nearly coextensive with the suborder Tyranni
taxonomic group of simple-voiced passerine birds

Examples

The suborder Clamatores includes several families of birds known for their loud calls but simple vocal structures.
Some classification systems recognize suborder Clamatores, distinguished by their limited ability to sing complex songs.
Research into the evolution of passerine birds often references the suborder Clamatores as a distinct lineage.

Etymology

From Latin 'clamator' (shouter, caller) plus the taxonomic suffix '-es'; refers to birds known for loud calls rather than song.
