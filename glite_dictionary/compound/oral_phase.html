oral phase

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl ˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˌfeɪz / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) the first stage of an infant's psychosexual development, in which pleasure is focused on activities involving the mouth, such as sucking and biting.
first psychosexual development stage (psychoanalysis)
medical

Examples

According to Freud, the oral phase is crucial in shaping an infant's future personality.
During the oral phase, infants gain satisfaction primarily from feeding and sucking.
The concept of the oral phase is foundational to psychoanalytic theories of childhood development.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anal phase - the second stage of psychosexual development, focus on anus and bowel control

Etymology

From Latin 'os, oris' (mouth) and Greek 'phasis' (appearance, stage), by way of psychoanalytic terminology.
