developmental learning

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌvɛləpˈmɛntəl ˈlɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˌvɛləpˈmɛntəl ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
learning that naturally occurs as part of a person's cognitive and psychological growth, especially in early life stages
learning as a normal part of development

Examples

Many educators design their curriculum to complement developmental learning during childhood.
Language skills acquired in early years are often attributed to developmental learning.
Researchers study how developmental learning differs from learning later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognitive development - the process of growth in intellectual abilities
  • Antonym: experiential learning - learning through experience, rather than inherent development
