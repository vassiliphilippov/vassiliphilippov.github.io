optic radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪk reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪk reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a bundle of nerve fibers that transmits visual information from the lateral geniculate body to the visual cortex in the brain
visual nerve pathway in the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the optic radiation can result in partial loss of vision.
The MRI scan showed a lesion affecting the patient's optic radiation.
Signals travel along the optic radiation to reach the visual cortex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optic nerve - the nerve that carries visual information from the eye to the brain
  • Degree Higher: visual cortex - part of the brain that processes visual information

Etymology

From optic (relating to vision or the eye) and radiation (spreading out from a central point).
