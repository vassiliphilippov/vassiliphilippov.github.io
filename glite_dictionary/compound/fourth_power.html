fourth power

1. NOUN

/ fɔrθ ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / fɔːθ ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
A number, equation, or function in which the highest variable is raised to the exponent four; an algebraic expression of the fourth degree.
mathematical term: to the fourth exponent

Examples

The volume of a hypersphere is proportional to the fourth power of its radius.
Solving equations involving the fourth power can be more complex than quadratic equations.
He calculated the fourth power of seven to solve the math problem.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cube - a value raised to the third power
  • Degree Lower: square - a value raised to the second power
