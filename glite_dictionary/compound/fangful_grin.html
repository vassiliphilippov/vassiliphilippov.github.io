fangful grin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæŋ.fəl ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ˈfæŋ.fəl ɡrɪn / (BrE)
A smile or grin that reveals one's teeth, often with a menacing or predatory appearance.
smile showing many teeth, often menacing

Examples

The wolf bared its teeth in a fangful grin before attacking.
She greeted him with a fangful grin that sent chills down his spine.
The villain's fangful grin hinted at his cruel intentions.

Synset Examples

He wore a fangful grin, ready to strike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snarl - an angry or menacing grin or growl showing the teeth
  • Degree Lower: smirk - a smile in an irritatingly smug or conceited way
  • Synonym: leer - a suggestive or unpleasant look or grin

Etymology

From 'fang' (sharp tooth) and 'full', forming a compound meaning 'full of fangs/teeth'.
