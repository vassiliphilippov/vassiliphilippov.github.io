corporate welfare

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.pɚ.rət ˈwɛl.fɛr / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pər.ət ˈwɛl.feə / (BrE)
Government financial support or subsidies given to businesses, especially large corporations.
government financial support for businesses

Examples

Critics argue that corporate welfare distorts the free market and unfairly benefits large companies.
The new bill aims to reduce corporate welfare and redirect funds to social programs.
Debates over corporate welfare often center on tax breaks given to major industries.

Synset Examples

The debate on corporate welfare continues in political circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidy - financial assistance granted by a government to a business
