book value

1. NOUN

/ bʊk ˈvælju / (AmE), / bʊk ˈvaljuː / (BrE)
the value of an asset as recorded on a company's balance sheet, calculated as its cost minus accumulated depreciation or amortization
accounting value of an asset after depreciation

Examples

The company's book value increased after the new assets were added to the balance sheet.
Investors often compare the book value of a company to its market value when evaluating stocks.
After years of depreciation, the machinery's book value was significantly lower than its original cost.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: market value - the amount for which something can be sold on a given market

Etymology

From 'book' (referring to accounting books) + 'value'.
