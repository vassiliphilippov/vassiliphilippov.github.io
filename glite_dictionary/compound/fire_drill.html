fire drill

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ drɪl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə drɪl / (BrE)
a practice exercise in which people rehearse the procedures to follow in the event of a fire, including evacuation and safety measures
practice for fire emergency procedures

Examples

The school held a fire drill to make sure students knew how to evacuate safely.
During the fire drill, everyone assembled at the meeting point in the parking lot.
It is important to take each fire drill seriously, even if there is no real danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evacuation drill - a rehearsal for leaving a place quickly in an emergency

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ drɪl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə drɪl / (BrE)
an exercise in which people practice leaving a building safely in case of a fire or other emergency
emergency evacuation practice

Examples

The school held a fire drill to teach students how to evacuate safely.
Everyone calmly exited the building during yesterday’s fire drill.
Regular fire drills help people respond quickly in real emergencies.
