mountain azalea

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən əˈzeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn əˈzeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A creeping, mat-forming evergreen shrub found in high mountain regions of the Northern Hemisphere, grown for its rose-pink flowers.
mat-forming alpine shrub with pink flowers

Examples

The mountain azalea blooms every spring, covering the slopes in a blanket of pink.
Gardeners often choose mountain azalea for rock gardens due to its low, spreading habit.
On our hike, we spotted a wild mountain azalea growing among the alpine rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azalea - flowering shrub of the genus Rhododendron, especially cultivated varieties
