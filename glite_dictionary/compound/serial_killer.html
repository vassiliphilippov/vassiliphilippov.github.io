serial killer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl ˌkɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl ˌkɪlə / (BrE)
A person who murders several people, usually one at a time, with a period of time between each crime, often following a characteristic pattern.
a person who commits multiple murders over time

Examples

The police believed the town was being terrorized by a serial killer who targeted young women.
She wrote a book profiling the psychology of a notorious serial killer.
After months of investigation, authorities finally caught the serial killer responsible for the string of murders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - a person who kills another person with intent
  • Degree Lower: mass murderer - a person who kills multiple people at once or in a short period

Etymology

Formed from 'serial' (from Latin 'series', a sequence) and 'killer'.

a person who murders several people, usually one at a time, over a period of time, often following a characteristic, predictable pattern
person who murders several people over time

Examples

The police finally caught the serial killer after months of investigation.
True crime podcasts often discuss the motives of a serial killer.
The novel centers on a detective tracking down a cunning serial killer.

Synset Examples

The documentary featured a notorious serial killer van.
They discovered clues inside the serial killer van.
The police found the serial killer van abandoned in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass murderer - person who kills many people at one time
  • Synonym: spree killer - person who kills several people in a short period without a cooling-off period
