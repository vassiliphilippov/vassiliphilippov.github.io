ragpicker's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌpɪkərz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌpɪkəz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a form of anthrax infection contracted by inhaling dust that contains Bacillus anthracis, characterized by respiratory symptoms followed by severe cardiovascular collapse
a respiratory form of anthrax infection
medical

Examples

The outbreak of ragpicker's disease in the factory led to several hospitalizations.
Doctors identified the rare symptoms as those typical of ragpicker's disease.
Proper ventilation and sanitation helped prevent new cases of ragpicker's disease among the workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthrax - a serious infectious disease caused by Bacillus anthracis

Etymology

Named from the prevalence of the disease among ragpickers, who were exposed to contaminated materials.
