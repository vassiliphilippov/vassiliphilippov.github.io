black-necked stork

1. NOUN

/ ˌblækˈnɛkt ˌstɔrk / (AmE), / ˌblækˈnɛkt ˌstɔːk / (BrE)
a large Australian stork with mostly white plumage and a distinctive black neck and head
large Australian stork with a black neck

Examples

The black-necked stork is most commonly seen in wetlands across northern Australia.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a black-necked stork near the riverbank during their trip.
Unlike other storks, the black-necked stork has a striking iridescent neck and long red legs.
