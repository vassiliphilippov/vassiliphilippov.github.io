Golden Gate

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈɡeɪt / (BrE)
a strait in western California that connects San Francisco Bay with the Pacific Ocean
strait connecting San Francisco Bay and Pacific Ocean

Examples

Many ships pass through the Golden Gate on their way to the Port of San Francisco.
Fog often spills into the bay over the Golden Gate, creating a dramatic scene.
The Golden Gate is famous for both its natural beauty and its iconic bridge.

Etymology

Named by explorer John C. Frémont in 1846 after the Golden Horn in Istanbul, Turkey.
