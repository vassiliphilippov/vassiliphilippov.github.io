Oxford gray

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksfərd ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒksfəd ɡreɪ / (BrE)
a very dark gray color
very dark gray color

Examples

He chose Oxford gray paint for the walls to create a sophisticated atmosphere.
The suit was tailored from fine Oxford gray fabric, giving it a timeless look.
The artist mixed several shades until she achieved the perfect Oxford gray for the background.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charcoal - a dark gray color
  • Degree Lower: gray - the color between black and white
  • Degree Higher: jet black - an intense, pure black color

Etymology

Named after Oxford cloth, a type of woven fabric originally from Oxford, England; 'gray' indicates the color.
