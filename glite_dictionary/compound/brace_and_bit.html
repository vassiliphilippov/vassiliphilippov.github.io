brace and bit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪs ənd ˈbɪt / (AmE), / ˈbreɪs ənd ˈbɪt / (BrE)
a hand tool for drilling holes, consisting of a turning handle (brace) that holds and rotates a drill bit
a hand-operated drill

Examples

My grandfather showed me how to use a brace and bit to drill holes in the wood.
The old carpenter still kept his brace and bit beside his modern power tools.
Before electric drills, people relied on the brace and bit for woodworking tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - a tool or machine with a rotating cutting tip for making holes

Etymology

From 'brace' (handle for turning) and 'bit' (cutting tool), denoting the two main parts of the tool.
