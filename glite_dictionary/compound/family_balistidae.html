family Balistidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli bəˈlɪstɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli bəˈlɪstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical marine fishes known as triggerfishes, recognized for their oval, flattened bodies and strong dorsal spines.
family of triggerfishes (marine fish)

Examples

The family Balistidae includes some of the most colorful fish found on coral reefs.
Scientists are studying the unique jaw structure of the family Balistidae.
Members of the family Balistidae are known for their defensive dorsal spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triggerfish - a brightly colored tropical marine fish of the family Balistidae

Etymology

From New Latin Balistidae, from genus name Balistes + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
