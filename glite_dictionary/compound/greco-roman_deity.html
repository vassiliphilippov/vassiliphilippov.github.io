Greco-Roman deity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡriːkoʊˈroʊmən ˈdiː.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛkəʊˈrəʊmən ˈdiː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A god or goddess from the classical mythologies of ancient Greece and Rome.
god or goddess from Greek and Roman myths

Examples

Many ancient temples were dedicated to a specific Greco-Roman deity who was believed to protect the city.
The myths surrounding each Greco-Roman deity have influenced Western literature for centuries.
Athena is a well-known Greco-Roman deity associated with wisdom and warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: god - male deity in a polytheistic religion
  • Synonym: goddess - female deity in a polytheistic religion

Etymology

From 'Greco-' (relating to Greece) and 'Roman,' referring to the classical civilizations; 'deity' from Latin 'deitas' (divine nature).
