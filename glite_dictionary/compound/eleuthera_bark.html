eleuthera bark

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːθərə bɑrk / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːθərə bɑːk / (BrE)
the aromatic bark of the cascarilla shrub, used as a tonic and in making incense
aromatic cascarilla bark used as tonic and incense

Examples

The distillery uses eleuthera bark to impart a subtle, spicy aroma to its liqueurs.
Traditional healers often prepare tonics from eleuthera bark to aid digestion and relieve minor ailments.
Incense crafted with eleuthera bark is prized for its distinctive fragrance in ceremonial practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cascarilla bark - the aromatic bark from the Croton eluteria shrub, used medicinally and in perfumery

Etymology

Named after Eleuthera Island in the Bahamas, where the plant is found.
