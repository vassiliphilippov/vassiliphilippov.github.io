electronic superhighway

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˌsuːpərˈhaɪweɪ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˌsuːpəˈhaɪweɪ / (BrE)
a metaphor for the internet and its digital communication networks as a fast and wide-reaching means of transmitting information
metaphor for the internet and digital networks

Examples

The rise of the electronic superhighway has transformed the way we communicate and access information.
Students from across the world connected on the electronic superhighway to collaborate on their group project.
Concerns about privacy persist even as we depend more on the electronic superhighway for daily tasks.

Synset Examples

The internet is our modern electronic superhighway.
We're living in an age of the electronic superhighway.
He surfed the electronic superhighway for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internet - the global system of interconnected computer networks

Etymology

Coined in the 1970s by Nam June Paik, inspired by the idea of highways for information travel.
