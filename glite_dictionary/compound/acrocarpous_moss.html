acrocarpous moss

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈkɑrpəs mɑs / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈkɑːpəs mɒs / (BrE)
a moss species whose main stem ends in the female reproductive organ, so the capsule appears at the apex of the main shoot
moss with capsules at the tip of the main stem

Examples

The botanist identified the specimen as an acrocarpous moss due to its upright growth and terminal capsules.
Most acrocarpous mosses grow in tufts or cushions on rocks and soil.
She compared the acrocarpous moss to a pleurocarpous moss to illustrate their structural differences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleurocarpous moss - a moss with capsules on lateral branches, not at the apex

Etymology

From Greek akron (tip) + karpos (fruit).
