Virginia bluebell

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪn.jə ˈbluːˌbɛl / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i.ə ˈbluːˌbɛl / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to eastern North America, with smooth stems, broad leaves, and clusters of blue bell-shaped flowers, pink when in bud.
North American plant with blue bell-shaped flowers

Examples

In early spring, the Virginia bluebell carpets forest floors with its vibrant blooms.
We planted several Virginia bluebells along the shady border of the garden.
The buds of the Virginia bluebell are pink before they open into blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - a plant with bell-shaped blue flowers, especially Hyacinthoides non-scripta in the UK
