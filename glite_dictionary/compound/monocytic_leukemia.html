monocytic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˈsaɪtɪk luːˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈsaɪtɪk luːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
a type of leukemia characterized by an abnormal increase in monocytes and monoblasts in the blood
leukemia with increased monocytes
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with monocytic leukemia after a series of blood tests.
Treatment options for monocytic leukemia may include chemotherapy and stem cell transplantation.
Researchers are studying the genetic mutations involved in monocytic leukemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - cancer of blood-forming tissues, including bone marrow and lymphatic system

Etymology

From Greek *monos* (single) + *kytos* (cell) and New Latin *leucaemia* (white blood).
