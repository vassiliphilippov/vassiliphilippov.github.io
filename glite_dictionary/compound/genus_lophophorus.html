genus Lophophorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ləˌfɒfəˈrɔːs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lɒˌfɒfəˈrɔːs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored pheasants known as monals, native to the Himalayan region.
genus of Himalayan monals (pheasants)

Examples

The genus Lophophorus includes some of the world's most strikingly colored pheasants.
Researchers observed several species belonging to the genus Lophophorus in the Himalayas.
One famous member of the genus Lophophorus is the Himalayan monal, Nepal's national bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monal - a brightly colored pheasant of the genus Lophophorus

Etymology

From Greek lophos (crest) + phoros (bearing), referring to the crested appearance of these birds.
