genus Halenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs həˈliːniə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs həˈliːniə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the gentian family, native to Eurasia and the Americas, commonly known as spurred gentians.
genus of spurred gentian plants

Examples

The genus Halenia includes several species found in alpine regions.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of the genus Halenia.
The distinctive spurred flowers are characteristic of the genus Halenia.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Halenia', named after Swedish botanist Göran Hahlen.
