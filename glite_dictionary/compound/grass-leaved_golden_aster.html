grass-leaved golden aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræs liːvd ˈɡoʊldən ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːs liːvd ˈɡəʊldən ˈæstə / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the southeastern United States with slender, grass-like leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers; Chrysopsis graminifolia.
a plant; Chrysopsis graminifolia

Examples

In Florida, the grass-leaved golden aster brightens sandy roadsides with its yellow blooms.
Wildlife conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the grass-leaved golden aster.
The slender leaves of the grass-leaved golden aster distinguish it from other asters in its family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden aster - any plant of the genus Chrysopsis with yellow flowers
