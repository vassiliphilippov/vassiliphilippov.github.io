mallow family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæloʊ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmaləʊ ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Malvaceae), including herbs, shrubs, and some trees such as mallows, cotton, and okra
family of plants including mallows, cotton, okra

Examples

Many ornamental plants belong to the mallow family, which is known for its attractive flowers.
Cotton, a member of the mallow family, is one of the world's most important crops.
Okra is an edible plant in the mallow family and is widely used in southern cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malvaceae - the botanical name for the mallow family
