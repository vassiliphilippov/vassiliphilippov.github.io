Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˈrʌʃən ˈsoʊvi.ət ˈfɛdəˌreɪtɪv səˈʃælɪst rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃ(ə)n ˈsəʊvɪət ˈfɛdəˌreɪtɪv səˈʃælɪst rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
the largest and most significant republic of the former Soviet Union, occupying eastern Europe and northern Asia from 1917 to 1991, now known as the Russian Federation
the largest former republic of the USSR

Examples

The Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic played a dominant role in the politics of the USSR.
Lenin declared the formation of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic after the October Revolution.
Many regions that were part of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic are now within modern Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: USSR - the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, a former federation of communist states
  • Degree Higher: Russian Federation - the present-day sovereign state commonly known as Russia
