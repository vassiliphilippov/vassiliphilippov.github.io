spore case

1. NOUN

/ spɔr keɪs / (AmE), / spɔː keɪs / (BrE)
A structure or organ in plants, fungi, or some protists that contains or produces spores.
organ containing or producing spores

Examples

The spore case is located on the underside of the fern leaf.
When mature, the spore case bursts open to release its contents into the air.
Under the microscope, the spore case appeared as a tiny, translucent sac.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sporangium - a structure in which spores are produced

2. NOUN

/ spɔr keɪs / (AmE), / spɔː keɪs / (BrE)
a specialized leaf or branch in certain aquatic ferns that encloses the sori or clusters of sporangia, where spores are produced
structure in ferns enclosing clusters of spores

Examples

The aquatic fern protects its spores within a spore case until they are ready to be released.
Scientists observed the development of the spore case under a microscope.
Each spore case contains many clusters of sporangia vital for the plant's reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sporangium - a structure in which spores are produced
