mineral kingdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl ˈkɪŋdəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl ˈkɪŋdəm / (BrE)
the division of nature comprising all inorganic substances, considered as distinct from the animal and plant kingdoms
division of all inorganic substances

Examples

Geology is the science that studies the mineral kingdom and the processes that shape it.
In ancient classifications, nature was divided into the animal, plant, and mineral kingdom.
Diamonds, as part of the mineral kingdom, are formed deep within the Earth's mantle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animal kingdom - the division of nature comprising all animals
  • Antonym: plant kingdom - the division of nature comprising all plants
