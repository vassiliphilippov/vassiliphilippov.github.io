television tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌvɪʒən tuːb / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌvɪʒən tjuːb / (BrE)
a cathode-ray tube used in a television set to display images by translating electronic signals into visible pictures on a screen
cathode-ray tube in a television

Examples

My old television still works, but the television tube is starting to fade.
Technicians replaced the broken television tube to restore the picture quality.
Many vintage collectors look for sets with the original television tube intact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathode-ray tube - a vacuum tube used to display images in older TVs and monitors
