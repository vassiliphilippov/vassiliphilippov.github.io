first-class honours degree

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈklæs ˈɑnɚz dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈklɑːs ˈɒnəz dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
an undergraduate degree awarded with the highest level of distinction, typically in the UK and some other countries
highest level honours degree (UK)
british

Examples

She was delighted to receive a first-class honours degree in physics from Oxford.
Earning a first-class honours degree requires consistent hard work and dedication throughout your studies.
Many employers value candidates who have achieved a first-class honours degree.
