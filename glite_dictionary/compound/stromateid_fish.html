stromateid fish

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˈmeɪtiɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / strəˈmeɪtɪɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A small marine fish with a short, compressed body and weak or feeble spines, belonging to the family Stromateidae.
small marine fish with compressed body

Examples

The stromateid fish can be found in both temperate and tropical coastal waters.
Marine biologists studied the feeding habits of the local stromateid fish population.
Some stromateid fish species are popular for their mild flavor and are commonly sold as butterfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfish - a common name for several species of stromateid fish

Etymology

From modern Latin Stromateidae, the family name, derived from Greek *strōmat-,* meaning 'bed' or 'spread'.
