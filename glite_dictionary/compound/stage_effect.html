stage effect

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / steɪdʒ ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
an artificial sound, visual, or lighting effect created in a theatrical performance to enhance the atmosphere or action
special effect used in theatre

Examples

The thunderclap was a stage effect that startled the audience during the opening scene.
They hired a technician to manage the stage effects for the musical.
Smoke and flashing lights are common stage effects used to create dramatic moments in plays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: special effect - an illusion or trick in film, television, or stage to simulate imagined events
