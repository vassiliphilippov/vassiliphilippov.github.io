pond bald cypress

1. NOUN

/ pɑnd bɔld ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / pɒnd bɔːld ˈsɪprəs / (BrE)
a cypress tree (Taxodium ascendens) of the southeastern United States, with a swollen base, found in coastal swamps and flood-prone river areas
a cypress tree of southeastern US swamps

Examples

The pond bald cypress thrives in the saturated soils of southeastern wetlands.
Large swathes of pond bald cypress line the edges of the Okefenokee Swamp.
After the flood, the roots of the pond bald cypress were clearly visible above the waterline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - a deciduous cypress tree native to North America

2. NOUN

/ pɑnd bɔld ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / pɒnd bɔːld ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
the wood of a cypress tree (Taxodium ascendens) native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in swamps and river bottoms, notable for its expanded trunk base
wood from the pond bald cypress tree

Examples

The lumber mill specializes in high-quality pond bald cypress used for building projects in humid areas.
Old cabins in the South were sometimes constructed using durable pond bald cypress planks.
Water-resistant pond bald cypress is valued for boat building and dock construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - a North American deciduous coniferous tree (Taxodium distichum) often found in swamps

Etymology

Named from its habitat in ponds and its relation to the bald cypress (Taxodium), with 'bald' referring to the tree's deciduous nature.
