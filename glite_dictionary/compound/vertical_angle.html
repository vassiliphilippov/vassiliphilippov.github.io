vertical angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːtɪkəl ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
either of the two angles opposite each other formed when two straight lines intersect; these angles are equal.
angle opposite another at line intersection

Examples

When two lines cross, each pair of vertical angles is always equal to one another.
The teacher used a diagram to illustrate the concept of vertical angles to the students.
Solving the problem required us to identify all the vertical angles in the shape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adjacent angle - an angle that shares a common side and vertex with another angle
  • Synonym: opposite angle - another term for vertical angle in geometry
