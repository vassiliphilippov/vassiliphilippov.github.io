thermal reactor

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝːməl riˈæktər / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməl rɪˈæktə / (BrE)
a nuclear reactor in which nuclear fission is sustained by neutrons that have been slowed by a moderator to thermal speeds
nuclear reactor using slowed (thermal) neutrons

Examples

A thermal reactor relies on moderators like water or graphite to slow down neutrons for efficient fission.
Most commercial nuclear power plants use a thermal reactor design for electricity generation.
Switching from a thermal reactor to a fast reactor requires major design changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fast reactor - a nuclear reactor using fast neutrons, not moderated to thermal energies

Etymology

Formed from 'thermal' (relating to heat) and 'reactor' (a device for initiating and controlling reactions), referring to the use of thermal (slow) neutrons.
