to launch a satellite

1. VERB

/ lɔntʃ ə ˈsæt̬əˌlaɪt / (AmE), / lɔːntʃ ə ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (BrE)
to send an artificial satellite into space, typically using a rocket
send an artificial satellite into space

Examples

Several countries plan to launch a satellite each year to support technological advancements.
The space agency worked for years before they could successfully launch a satellite.
Private companies are now able to launch a satellite with much lower costs than before.

They launched a new satellite to improve communication.
