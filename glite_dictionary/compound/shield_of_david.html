Shield of David

1. NOUN

/ ʃild əv ˈdeɪvɪd / (AmE), / ʃiːld əv ˈdeɪvɪd / (BrE)
a six-pointed star formed from two overlapping equilateral triangles, used as a symbol of Judaism
symbol of Judaism; six-pointed star

Examples

Many synagogues display the Shield of David on their walls or windows.
The flag of Israel features a prominent blue Shield of David at its center.
He wore a necklace with a small Shield of David as a symbol of his faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Star of David - another name for the same six-pointed star symbolizing Judaism

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Magen David,' meaning 'Shield of David'.
