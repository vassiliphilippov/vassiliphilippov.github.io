holy clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb with pale pink flowers and curved pods, grown as forage and a source of honey, especially in Britain and North America.
a Eurasian perennial herb used as forage

Examples

The field was covered in holy clover, attracting bees with its delicate pink flowers.
Farmers in the region often plant holy clover as a reliable forage crop for their livestock.
The spread of holy clover in calcareous soils has helped increase honey production locally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Securigera varia - Scientific name for holy clover
  • Synonym: crown vetch - Another common name for Securigera varia
