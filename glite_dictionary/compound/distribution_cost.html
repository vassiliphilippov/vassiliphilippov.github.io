distribution cost

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən kɑst / (AmE), / dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən kɒst / (BrE)
an expense incurred by a producer, wholesaler, retailer, or distributor related to delivering goods or services to customers, such as advertising, shipping, and handling costs
costs of getting goods to customers

Examples

The company's distribution cost increased due to higher shipping fees.
Reducing distribution costs can help improve profit margins significantly.
Advertising is often considered a major component of distribution costs in retail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overhead - general business operating expenses not directly linked to production
