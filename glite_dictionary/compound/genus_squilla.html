genus Squilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈskwɪlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈskwɪlə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Squillidae, comprising certain species of marine crustaceans known as mantis shrimps.
type genus of Squillidae (mantis shrimps)

Examples

The genus Squilla includes several species of colorful mantis shrimps found in tropical waters.
Researchers studied the predatory behavior characteristic of the genus Squilla.
The largest member of the genus Squilla can grow up to 40 centimeters long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis shrimp - a marine crustacean of the order Stomatopoda, often noted for its powerful claws

Etymology

From Latin 'squilla', meaning shrimp or prawn.
