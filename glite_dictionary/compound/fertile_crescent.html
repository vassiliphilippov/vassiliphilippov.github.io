Fertile Crescent

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɝːtəl ˈkrɛsənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtaɪl ˈkrɛsənt / (BrE)
an area of fertile land in the Middle East, stretching in a broad arc from the Nile in Egypt to the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, regarded as the cradle of early civilization.
historic region of fertile land in the Middle East

Examples

The Fertile Crescent is often called the birthplace of agriculture and early human civilizations.
Many ancient empires rose and fell in the region known as the Fertile Crescent.
I studied the history of the Fertile Crescent in my world history class.

Etymology

Coined in the early 20th century; 'fertile' from Latin *fertilis* (bearing in abundance) and 'crescent' from Latin *crescere* (to grow), referring to the shape of the area.
