acceptance sampling

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛptəns ˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptəns ˈsɑːmplɪŋ / (BrE)
a statistical method used to determine whether to accept or reject a batch of products by testing a sample for defects
statistical quality control inspection

Examples

The company implemented acceptance sampling to ensure only high-quality products reached the customers.
In acceptance sampling, inspectors examine a sample of items rather than checking every single one.
Effective acceptance sampling reduced costs by minimizing the need for exhaustive quality checks.

Etymology

From 'acceptance' (act of consenting) and 'sampling' (taking a portion for testing), both from Latin roots.
