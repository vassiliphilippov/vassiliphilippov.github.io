testament to history

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəmənt tu ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈtɛstəmənt tʊ ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
something that serves as evidence or proof of historical importance or significance
evidence of historical significance

Examples

The ruins are a testament to history, reminding us of an ancient civilization once thriving here.
Every artifact recovered from the site stands as a testament to history.
The preserved battlefield is a testament to history and the sacrifices made there.

Synset Examples

The ancient ribat stood as a testament to history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monument - a structure commemorating a person or event
  • Synonym: evidence - proof or indication that something is true or exists

Etymology

From 'testament' (from Latin 'testamentum', meaning 'a will, testament') and 'history' (from Greek 'historia', meaning 'inquiry, knowledge acquired by investigation').
