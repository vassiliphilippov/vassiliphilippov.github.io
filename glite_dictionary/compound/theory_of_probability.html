theory of probability

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiəri əv ˌprɑbəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˌprɒbəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics concerned with the analysis of random phenomena and the calculation of probabilities
mathematical study of probabilities

Examples

The theory of probability provides the mathematical foundation for modern statistics and risk analysis.
Students often find the theory of probability challenging but essential for understanding statistical data.
Her research focuses on advanced topics within the theory of probability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probability theory - the branch of mathematics that studies probability

Etymology

From Latin 'theoria' (contemplation, speculation) and 'probabilitas' (probability).
