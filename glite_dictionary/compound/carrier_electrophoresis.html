carrier electrophoresis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæriər ɪˌlɛktroʊfəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæriə ɪˌlɛktrəʊfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
a form of electrophoresis in which substances are separated on a filter paper, used especially for analytical purposes in biochemistry
electrophoresis done on filter paper
medical

Examples

The laboratory used carrier electrophoresis to separate the proteins found in the sample.
Advances in carrier electrophoresis have made it easier to analyze complex biochemical mixtures.
Results indicated that carrier electrophoresis provided clear separation of amino acids on the filter paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrophoresis - the movement of charged particles in a fluid or gel under the influence of an electric field

Etymology

From 'carrier', meaning a medium that conveys something, and 'electrophoresis', from Greek 'elektron' (amber) and 'phorēsis' (being carried).
