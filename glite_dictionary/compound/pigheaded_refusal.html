pigheaded refusal

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪɡˌhɛdɪd rɪˈfjuːzəl / (AmE), / ˌpɪɡˌhɛdɪd rɪˈfjuːzəl / (BrE)
an extremely stubborn or obstinate refusal, especially one that ignores reason or advice
stubborn or obstinate refusal

Examples

Her pigheaded refusal to apologize only made the situation worse.
Despite the team's concerns, his pigheaded refusal to reconsider the plan stalled the project.
Everyone was frustrated by the mayor's pigheaded refusal to listen to alternative solutions.

Synset Examples

His pigheaded refusal to compromise frustrated everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn refusal - an unwillingness to change one's mind or actions
  • Antonym: compromise - an agreement reached by each side making concessions

Etymology

pigheaded: from 'pig' + 'headed', used figuratively since the 18th century to mean stubborn; 'refusal' from Middle English, from Old French 'refuser'.
