prairie orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈɔːrkɪd / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈɔːkɪd / (BrE)
A native North American orchid found in boggy or wet prairies, with racemes of fragrant creamy or greenish-white flowers.
a native wetland orchid of North America

Examples

The rare prairie orchid is protected in several wildlife reserves across the Midwest.
Botanists conducted surveys to monitor the blooming periods of the prairie orchid each spring.
Conservation groups are working to restore wetland habitats suitable for the prairie orchid to thrive.
