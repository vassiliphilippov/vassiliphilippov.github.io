board measure

1. NOUN

/ bɔrd ˈmɛʒər / (AmE), / bɔːd ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
a system of units for measuring the volume of lumber, based on the board foot
system for measuring lumber volume

Examples

The price of the timber was calculated according to the board measure.
Carpenters commonly use board measure when estimating the amount of wood needed for a project.
Lumberyards sell wood by board measure to provide consistent pricing for different sizes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: board foot - a unit of volume for lumber equal to one foot by one foot by one inch
