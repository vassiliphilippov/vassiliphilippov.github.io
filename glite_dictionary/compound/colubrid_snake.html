colubrid snake

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluː.brɪd sneɪk / (AmE), / kɒˈljuː.brɪd sneɪk / (BrE)
a snake belonging to the large family Colubridae, which are mostly non-venomous and found in temperate to tropical regions, inhabiting terrestrial, arboreal, or aquatic environments
mostly harmless snake of the Colubridae family

Examples

The grass snake is a common colubrid snake found across Europe and parts of Asia.
Unlike vipers, most colubrid snakes do not possess potent venom.
She specialized in the study of colubrid snakes and their ecological adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: viper - a member of the Viperidae family, typically venomous snakes
  • Antonym: elapid - a member of the Elapidae family, primarily venomous snakes like cobras and coral snakes

Etymology

From Latin 'coluber' (snake, serpent), the type genus of the family Colubridae.
