tau-plus particle

1. NOUN

/ taʊ plʌs ˈpɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / taʊ plʌs ˈpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a positively charged subatomic particle in the lepton family, similar to the electron but with much greater mass
positively charged heavy lepton (antiparticle)

Examples

Scientists detected a tau-plus particle in the results of the high-energy collision experiment.
The existence of the tau-plus particle was confirmed by observing its distinct decay patterns.
In particle physics, the tau-plus particle is classified as an antilepton due to its positive charge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tau-minus particle - the negatively charged counterpart of the tau-plus particle
  • Synonym: positron - the positively charged antiparticle of the electron

Etymology

From Greek letter 'tau' (τ) and the term 'plus' indicating positive charge.
