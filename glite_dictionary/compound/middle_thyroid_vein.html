middle thyroid vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
a vein on each side of the neck that drains blood from the lateral part of the thyroid gland and empties into the internal jugular vein
vein draining the lateral thyroid into the jugular
medical

Examples

During the thyroidectomy, the surgeon carefully ligated the middle thyroid vein to prevent bleeding.
The radiologist was able to visualize the middle thyroid vein on the ultrasound scan.
Damage to the middle thyroid vein may result in significant blood loss during neck surgery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: internal jugular vein - major vein in the neck that collects blood from the brain and superficial parts of the face and neck
  • Degree Higher: thyroid gland - large gland in the neck producing hormones regulating metabolism
