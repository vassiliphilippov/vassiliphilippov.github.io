family Callitrichaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli kəˌlɪtrɪˈkeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli kəˌlɪtrɪˈkeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of small, aquatic, dicotyledonous herbs, known as the water-starwort family, occurring chiefly in freshwater habitats.
family of aquatic dicot herbs (water-starworts)

Examples

The family Callitrichaceae includes several species adapted to freshwater environments.
Botanists have reclassified some genera previously thought to belong to the family Callitrichaceae.
Members of the family Callitrichaceae are often found in ponds and slow-moving streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Callitriche - A genus of aquatic plants within the family Callitrichaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, from Callitriche (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
