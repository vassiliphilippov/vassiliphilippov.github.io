gun grabber

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌn ˈɡræb.ɚ / (AmE), / ɡʌn ˈɡræb.ə / (BrE)
a person who advocates for stricter gun control laws or is opposed to gun rights, often used disparagingly
person supporting gun control measures
informal american

Examples

Some people accuse the senator of being a gun grabber because of his support for stricter regulations.
The term gun grabber is often used in heated debates over the Second Amendment.
She resented being called a gun grabber simply for supporting background checks.

Synset Examples

The politician was labeled a gun grabber by her opponents.
He believes that being called a gun grabber is unfair.
Critics accused her of being a gun grabber after proposing stricter laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gun control advocate - person supporting laws that restrict firearms
  • Antonym: gun nut - person strongly in favor of gun rights (opposite stance)

Etymology

Formed from 'gun' and slang use of 'grabber' (one who grabs or seeks to take something).
