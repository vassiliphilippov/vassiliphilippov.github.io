isopropyl alcohol

1. NOUN

/ aɪ.səˈproʊ.pɪl ˈæl.kəˌhɑl / (AmE), / aɪ.səˈprəʊ.pɪl ˈæl.kəˌhɒl / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid alcohol used chiefly as a solvent, antiseptic, and in antifreeze; also known as rubbing alcohol
liquid alcohol used as solvent and antiseptic

Examples

You should clean the wound with isopropyl alcohol to prevent infection.
Many hand sanitizers contain isopropyl alcohol as the active ingredient.
The mechanic used isopropyl alcohol to remove grease from the engine parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubbing alcohol - alcohol solution used on skin as antiseptic

Etymology

From 'iso-' (equal) + 'propyl' (a three-carbon alkyl group) + 'alcohol.'
