plant fiber

1. NOUN

/ plænt ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / plɑːnt ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a material consisting of fibers derived from plants, often used in textiles, rope, and other products
fiber material from plants

Examples

Cotton is a common type of plant fiber used in clothing.
The basket was woven from plant fiber collected locally.
Many ropes are made from strong plant fiber like jute or hemp.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animal fiber - fiber derived from animals, such as wool or silk
