scabrous detail

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæbrəs dɪˈteɪl / (AmE), / ˈskæbrəs dɪˈteɪl / (BrE)
shocking or scandalous information, especially sexual or morally offensive details often revealed to the public
shocking or scandalous information

Examples

The journalist promised not to publish any scabrous detail about the politician’s private life.
Some memoirs include every scabrous detail to attract readers' attention.
The documentary avoided the scabrous detail and focused on the victims' recovery instead.

Synset Examples

The article exposed the scabrous details of the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salacious detail - explicitly sexual or lascivious information
  • Antonym: innocuous detail - harmless, unremarkable information

Etymology

From Latin 'scabrosus' meaning 'rough, scabby' + English 'detail'. Used figuratively for indecent or risqué subject matter.
