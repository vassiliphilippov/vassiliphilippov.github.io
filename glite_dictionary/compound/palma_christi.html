palma christi

1. NOUN

/ ˌpælmə ˈkrɪsti / (AmE), / ˌpɑːlmə ˈkrɪsti / (BrE)
a large tropical shrub (Ricinus communis) with palmate leaves and spiny seed capsules, source of castor oil and ricin
tropical shrub; source of castor oil

Examples

The seeds of the palma christi are processed to make castor oil.
Farmers in the region grow palma christi for its valuable oil and medicinal uses.
Care should be taken when handling palma christi seeds, as they contain a potent toxin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castor oil plant - another common name for Ricinus communis

Etymology

From Latin 'palma Christi', meaning 'palm of Christ', referencing the shape of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpælmə ˈkrɪsti / (AmE), / ˌpɑːlmə ˈkrɪsti / (BrE)
a tropical shrub (Ricinus communis) with large palmate leaves, cultivated for its seeds that produce castor oil and ricin
castor oil plant

Examples

The seeds of the palma christi are used in the extraction of castor oil.
Farmers in tropical regions often grow palma christi for its valuable oil-producing seeds.
Poisonous compounds are also present in the seeds of the palma christi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castor oil plant - the plant Ricinus communis, source of castor oil

Etymology

From Latin 'palma Christi' meaning 'palm of Christ', referring to the shape of the leaves.
