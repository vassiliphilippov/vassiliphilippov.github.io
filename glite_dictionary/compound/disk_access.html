disk access

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk ˈæksɛs / (AmE), / dɪsk ˈæksɛs / (BrE)
the process of reading from or writing to a computer disk where information is stored
reading or writing data on a computer disk

Examples

If there are too many disk access operations, the computer may slow down significantly.
The program was optimized to reduce disk access times and improve performance.
When memory is full, the system will use disk access to store additional information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: data access - the act of obtaining data from any storage medium
