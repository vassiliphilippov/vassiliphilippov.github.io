blister blight

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstɚ blaɪt / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə blaɪt / (BrE)
a disease affecting tea plants, marked by blister-like lesions on the leaves, caused by a fungus
fungal disease of tea plants

Examples

The tea crop suffered a severe loss due to a sudden outbreak of blister blight.
Experts recommend early detection to prevent the spread of blister blight in plantations.
Farmers learned new methods to manage and treat blister blight this season.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstər blaɪt / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə ˌblaɪt / (BrE)
A disease of Scotch pines characterized by blister-like lesions on needles and shoots, caused by a fungal infection.
disease of Scotch pines (plant pathology)

Examples

The forester observed several trees affected by blister blight in the plantation.
Preventing blister blight involves regular monitoring and proper sanitation practices.
Blister blight can significantly reduce the growth rate of Scotch pines if left untreated.
