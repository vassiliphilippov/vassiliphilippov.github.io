double acute accent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbl əˈkjut ˈæksənt / (AmE), / ˈdʌbl əˈkjuːt ˈæksənt / (BrE)
a diacritical mark ( ̋ ) consisting of two acute accents, used especially in Hungarian to indicate long rounded vowels
diacritic mark ( ̋ ) used in Hungarian

Examples

The letters ő and ű in Hungarian are written with a double acute accent.
Some fonts do not support the double acute accent, making it difficult to write Hungarian accurately.
He learned how to type the double acute accent on his keyboard for his Hungarian language studies.

Synset Examples

Standard Hungarian has 14 vowels in a symmetrical system: seven short vowels (a, e, i, o, ö, u, ü) and seven long ones, which are written with an acute accent in the case of á, é, í, ó, ú, and with the double acute in the case of ő, ű.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute accent - a single diacritic mark ( ́ )
