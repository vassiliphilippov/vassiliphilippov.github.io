physician-patient privilege

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈzɪʃən ˈpeɪʃənt ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / fɪˈzɪʃən ˈpeɪʃənt ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the legal right of a physician to keep information shared by a patient confidential, unless the patient gives consent to disclose it
right to keep patient information confidential
legal medical

Examples

The court recognized the physician-patient privilege and ruled that the doctor could not be forced to testify against the patient.
Because of physician-patient privilege, the information discussed during your appointment will remain confidential.
Certain laws protect physician-patient privilege, ensuring private medical conversations stay between doctor and patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney-client privilege - the legal right of a client to have communications with their lawyer kept confidential
