soldier-proof

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊldʒɚ pruːf / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒə pruːf / (BrE)
(of an object or material) made to be extremely durable or resistant, especially able to withstand the rough treatment typical in military use.
extremely durable or resistant; able to withstand rough use
informal

Examples

The new phone case is so durable it's practically soldier-proof.
We need soldier-proof gear for this expedition into the rainforest.
The water-resistant watches are advertised as being completely soldier-proof.

Synset Examples

They claim their equipment is soldier-proof against harsh conditions.
The design is soldier-proof, ensuring maximum durability.
These boots are soldier-proof, perfect for rough terrains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolproof - incapable of going wrong or being misused
  • Synonym: to childproof - designed so that children cannot tamper with or access

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'childproof' and 'foolproof', using 'soldier' to indicate high durability requirements.
