corn mayweed

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈmeɪˌwɪd / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈmeɪˌwiːd / (BrE)
a widespread annual European weed with white flowers and finely divided leaves, naturalized and sometimes cultivated in eastern North America; sometimes classified in the genus Matricaria
a European annual weed with white flowers

Examples

Corn mayweed often flourishes in disturbed soils at the edge of crop fields.
The white blooms of corn mayweed can be seen throughout the summer.
Farmers sometimes struggle to control the spread of corn mayweed in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayweed - a type of plant with daisylike flowers, often weedy

2. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈmeɪˌwid / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈmeɪˌwiːd / (BrE)
A European white-flowered weed (Anthemis arvensis), commonly found in fields and naturalized in North America.
white-flowered weed; Anthemis arvensis

Examples

Corn mayweed often grows in wheat fields, competing with crops for nutrients and sunlight.
The white petals of corn mayweed make it easy to spot among other weeds.
Farmers try to control corn mayweed to prevent it from spreading across their land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayweed - any of several plants of the genus Anthemis
