Western world

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn wɜrld / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən wɜːld / (BrE)
the countries of Europe, North America, and South America, collectively regarded as sharing cultural, political, and economic characteristics often associated with the West
countries of Europe and the Americas considered as the West

Examples

The Western world has experienced significant social changes in the last century.
Many technological advancements originated in the Western world before spreading globally.
Debates about values between the Western world and other regions often arise in international forums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the West - refers generally to countries considered culturally or politically Western
  • Antonym: Eastern world - the countries of Asia regarded as a cultural or political group
