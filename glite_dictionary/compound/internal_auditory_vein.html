internal auditory vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːnəl ɔːˈdɪtɔri veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ɔːˈdɪtəri veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the inner ear structures
vein draining the inner ear
medical

Examples

Damage to the internal auditory vein can lead to hearing problems.
The surgeon carefully avoided the internal auditory vein during the procedure.
In some cases, the internal auditory vein may become blocked, affecting balance and hearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - blood vessel carrying blood toward the heart
  • Antonym: internal auditory artery - artery supplying blood to the inner ear
