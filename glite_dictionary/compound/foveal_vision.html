foveal vision

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.vi.əl ˈvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.vi.əl ˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
vision that is mediated by the fovea, providing the sharpest and most detailed central vision.
sharp central vision from the fovea
medical

Examples

Reading is possible because of the high detail in foveal vision.
Eye tests often measure clarity using foveal vision to assess central sharpness.
Damage to the macula can significantly impair foveal vision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peripheral vision - side vision outside the central field

Etymology

From Latin *fovea* (pit, small depression) and English 'vision'.
