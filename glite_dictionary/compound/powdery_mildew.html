powdery mildew

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdəri ˈmɪldu / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəri ˈmɪldjuː / (BrE)
a fungal disease of plants characterized by a white or gray powdery growth on the surfaces of leaves and stems, caused by fungi of the genus Erysiphe and related genera
fungal plant disease with powdery growth

Examples

The roses in the garden developed powdery mildew after several damp and humid days.
Powdery mildew can weaken plants by interfering with photosynthesis.
Gardeners often use special sprays to prevent powdery mildew on vegetables and ornamentals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downy mildew - a distinct but similar plant disease causing fluffy growth, often on the underside of leaves
