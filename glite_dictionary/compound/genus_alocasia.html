genus Alocasia

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊˌkeɪziə / (AmE), / əˈləʊˌkeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Asiatic herbaceous plants, similar to Colocasia, distinguished by a large sterile spadix.
tropical Asiatic plants genus

Examples

The genus Alocasia is known for its striking foliage and unique flowering structure.
Botanists have identified over 70 species within the genus Alocasia.
The leaves of plants in the genus Alocasia are often large and arrow-shaped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colocasia - a related genus of tropical plants, such as taro

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'alos' (a grove) and possibly 'kasis' (appearance).
