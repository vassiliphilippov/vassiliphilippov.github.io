blind gentian

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
a gentian of eastern North America with tubular blue or white flowers that open little or not at all
North American gentian with mostly closed flowers

Examples

We spotted a cluster of blind gentian near the edge of the forest trail.
The blind gentian's flowers stay mostly closed, giving it a unique appearance among wildflowers.
Botanists studied how the blind gentian is pollinated despite its tubular, nearly closed blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle gentian - another common name for blind gentian

2. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A North American wildflower, similar to Gentiana andrewsii, but with larger, bottle-shaped blue or purple flowers.
North American wildflower with large blue or purple flowers

Examples

Every fall, the blind gentian blooms along the edge of the woods near the stream.
She photographed a cluster of vibrant blind gentian in the prairie meadow.
Unlike most flowers, the blind gentian remains closed even when it is ready to pollinate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle gentian - another common name for species similar to blind gentian
  • Degree Lower: Gentiana andrewsii - type species closely related to blind gentian, also called closed gentian
