pointed arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪntɪd ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪd ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch with a pointed apex, especially as characteristic of Gothic architecture
arch with a pointed apex

Examples

The pointed arch is a fundamental element of Gothic cathedral design.
Builders used the pointed arch to support taller and wider structures in medieval Europe.
The church entrance features a beautiful pointed arch decorated with intricate stonework.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: round arch - an arch with a semicircular shape
  • Synonym: Gothic arch - another term for pointed arch; arch with a pointed apex in Gothic style
