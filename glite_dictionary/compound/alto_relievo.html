alto relievo

1. NOUN

/ ˌæltoʊ rəˈliːvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌæltəʊ rɛˈliːvəʊ / (BrE)
A sculptural technique in which figures project at least half of their depth from the background, creating a pronounced three-dimensional effect.
sculptural relief with deep projection

Examples

The ancient frieze was carved in alto relievo, making the figures nearly life-like.
Many Renaissance sculptures are executed in alto relievo to create depth and drama.
The museum’s new exhibit highlights the technique of alto relievo in religious art.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bas-relief - sculptural relief where forms project slightly from the background

Etymology

From Italian 'alto rilievo', meaning 'high relief'.
