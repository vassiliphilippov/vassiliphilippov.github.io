Admiralty Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈædmɪˌrælti ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈædmɪrəlti ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the Bismarck Archipelago, north of Papua New Guinea
group of islands in Bismarck Archipelago

Examples

The Admiralty Islands are known for their diverse marine life and tropical forests.
During World War II, the Admiralty Islands were the site of major military operations.
She researched the unique cultures and traditions found on the Admiralty Islands.

Etymology

Named by Captain Philip Carteret in 1767 after the Admiralty, the authority overseeing the Royal Navy in Britain.
