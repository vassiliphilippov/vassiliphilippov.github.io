assay-mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈæseɪ mɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈæseɪ mɑːk / (BrE)
a mark stamped on an article, especially of precious metal, to certify its origin and authenticity
mark indicating origin and authenticity

Examples

Every gold ring sold in the UK must carry an assay-mark to guarantee its purity.
She checked the silverware for an assay-mark before buying it at the antique shop.
The absence of an assay-mark raised doubts about the necklace's authenticity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallmark - an official mark indicating standard of purity or authenticity, especially on precious metals

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman French 'assai' (trial, test) and 'mark' (sign, stamp).
