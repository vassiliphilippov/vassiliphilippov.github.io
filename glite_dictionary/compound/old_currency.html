old currency

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈkɝn.si / (AmE), / əʊld ˈkʌr.ən.si / (BrE)
a form of money that is no longer used as legal tender
money no longer in use

Examples

The exhibit at the museum featured several types of old currency from the Roman Empire.
Collectors often trade old currency at auctions for significant amounts of money.
Many travelers keep old currency as souvenirs from countries they've visited.

Synset Examples

They paid the fee in maravedi, an old currency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency - money in any form when in actual use as a medium of exchange
