swamp laurel

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
A shrub native to bogs in the northwestern United States, known for its small purple flowers and leaves that are pale and glaucous underneath.
bog shrub with purple flowers and pale leaves

Examples

The swamp laurel thrives in the wet, acidic soil of northern bogs.
Botanists observed clusters of swamp laurel in full bloom during their spring survey.
The leaves of the swamp laurel have a distinctive pale colour on their undersides.

2. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree with small, fragrant white flowers, native to the southeastern United States.
shrubbing tree with fragrant white flowers, SE US

Examples

The garden's border is lined with swamp laurel that blooms each spring.
Wildlife in the area use the dense swamp laurel thickets for shelter.
During our trip to Georgia, we saw a large swamp laurel near the water's edge.
