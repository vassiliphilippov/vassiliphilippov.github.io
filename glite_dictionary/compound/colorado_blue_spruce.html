Colorado blue spruce

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːləˈrædoʊ bluː sprus / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˈrædəʊ bluː spruːs / (BrE)
a tall North American spruce tree (Picea pungens) with blue-green needles and a dense conic crown, often planted as an ornamental.
species of spruce tree with blue-green needles

Examples

The Colorado blue spruce adds striking color to gardens with its silvery-blue needles.
We planted a Colorado blue spruce in the front yard for its attractive shape and hardiness.
Mature Colorado blue spruces can grow over 75 feet tall in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - a type of evergreen tree of the genus Picea

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Colorado, where it is native, and its distinctive blue-green needles.
