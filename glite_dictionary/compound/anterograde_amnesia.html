anterograde amnesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tə.roʊˌɡreɪd æmˈniː.ʒə / (AmE), / ˈæn.tərəʊˌɡreɪd amˈniː.zi.ə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person is unable to form new memories after a trauma or event, even though memories from before the trauma remain intact.
inability to form new memories after trauma
medical

Examples

After the accident, he developed anterograde amnesia and could not remember anything that happened afterwards.
Patients with anterograde amnesia often rely on notes and reminders to cope with daily life.
The film portrays a character suffering from anterograde amnesia in a compelling and realistic way.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retrograde amnesia - loss of memory for events before a trauma

Etymology

From Latin 'ante-' (before) + 'gradus' (step) and Greek 'amnesia' (forgetfulness).
