deviated septum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.vi.eɪ.tɪd ˈsɛp.təm / (AmE), / ˈdiː.vi.eɪ.tɪd ˈsɛp.təm / (BrE)
a condition in which the thin wall between the nostrils is displaced to one side, often causing breathing difficulty
displaced nasal wall; medical condition
medical

Examples

After her doctor diagnosed a deviated septum, she decided to have surgery to improve her breathing.
A deviated septum can cause chronic sinus infections and congestion.
He struggled with sleep apnea for years before learning he had a deviated septum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal septum - the dividing wall between the nostrils

Etymology

From Latin *deviatus* (turned aside) and *septum* (partition).
