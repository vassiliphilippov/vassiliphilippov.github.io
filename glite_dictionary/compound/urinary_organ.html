urinary organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrəˌnɛri ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an organ in the body that removes waste substances from the blood and discharges them in urine
organ removing waste in urine
medical

Examples

The kidneys are the most important urinary organs in the human body.
A disease affecting the urinary organ can lead to serious health problems.
Doctors examined the patient's urinary organs for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney - organ that filters waste from the blood to form urine

Etymology

From Latin 'urinarius' (of urine) + Greek 'organon' (instrument, organ).
