privy council

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪvi ˈkaʊnsl̩ / (AmE), / ˈprɪvi ˈkaʊnsl / (BrE)
A body of advisers appointed to counsel a sovereign or ruler, especially the British monarch.
advisory council to a monarch

Examples

The British Privy Council has played a significant role in constitutional history.
She petitioned the privy council in hopes of overturning the decision.
Several countries established their own version of a privy council to advise their head of state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabinet - A body of high-ranking government officials, typically representing the executive branch

Etymology

From Middle English 'prive counseil', from Old French 'privé' (private) and 'counseil' (council, advice), referring to a council held in private.
