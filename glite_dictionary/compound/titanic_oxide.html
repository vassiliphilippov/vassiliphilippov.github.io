titanic oxide

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈtænɪk ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / tɪˈtanɪk ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, powdery compound (titanium dioxide) used as a pigment for its strong covering ability and durability
white pigment compound (titanium dioxide)

Examples

The paint contains titanic oxide to ensure a brilliant, long-lasting white finish.
Manufacturers often use titanic oxide as a pigment in sunscreens due to its stability.
Extensive research has shown the safety of titanic oxide in food and cosmetic products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titanium dioxide - compound TiO2, principal form of titanic oxide used as a pigment

Etymology

From 'titanic' (relating to titanium) and 'oxide' (compound with oxygen).
