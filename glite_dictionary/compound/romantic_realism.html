romantic realism

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪk ˈriːəlɪzəm / (AmE), / rə(ʊ)ˈmantɪk ˈrɪəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a style of art and literature combining the imaginative and emotional qualities of Romanticism with the depiction of realistic subjects, notably associated with the Hudson River School in 19th-century America
artistic style blending Romanticism and realism

Examples

The Hudson River School is famous for its romantic realism in landscapes that celebrate both nature and accuracy.
Many artists in the 19th century embraced romantic realism to convey the beauty of the American wilderness.
Her novel blends fantasy and romantic realism, reflecting the tensions between imagination and reality.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Romanticism - an artistic movement emphasizing emotion and imagination
  • Degree Higher: realism - the movement or style of representing life as it is without idealization

Etymology

From 'romantic' (pertaining to Romanticism) and 'realism' (depiction of life as it is).
