family Gracilariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ɡrəˌsɪləriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ɡrəˌsɪləriˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small moths whose larvae are leaf miners, belonging to the order Lepidoptera.
family of leaf-mining moths

Examples

The larvae of the family Gracilariidae are known for mining the leaves of various plants.
Researchers studied the distribution of the family Gracilariidae across different ecosystems.
Many species within the family Gracilariidae specialize in feeding on specific host plants.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Gracilariidae, from the genus Gracilaria + -idae (family suffix).
