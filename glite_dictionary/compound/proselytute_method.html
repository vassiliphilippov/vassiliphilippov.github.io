proselytute method

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsəlɪˌtjuːt ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈprɒsəlɪˌtjuːt ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method or practice used to try to convert others to a particular belief, often with persistent or aggressive tactics
method of converting others to a belief

Examples

Her proselytute method of sharing her faith made others uncomfortable in the office.
He used every proselytute method imaginable to win over new followers.
The aggressive proselytute methods employed by the group drew criticism from the public.

Synset Examples

Her proselytute methods annoyed some colleagues at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proselytize - to try to convert someone to one's faith or belief

Etymology

Blend of 'proselyte' (a convert, from Greek 'prosēlytos') and 'prostitute,' implying excessive or inappropriate persuasion.
