crystal mike

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstəl maɪk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl maɪk / (BrE)
A type of microphone in which sound waves cause a piezoelectric crystal to vibrate, generating a varying electrical voltage.
microphone with piezoelectric crystal element

Examples

The engineer connected the crystal mike to the amplifier for the recording session.
A crystal mike is often used in older audio equipment because of its simple design.
He replaced the broken crystal mike with a modern dynamic microphone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microphone - device that converts sound into an electrical signal
