Algonkian language

1. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Algonkian people, belonging to the Algonquian family of indigenous languages in North America
language of the Algonkian people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of the Algonkian language.
Linguists have studied the grammar of the Algonkian language for many years.
The museum exhibit features recordings of the Algonkian language being spoken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian language - a related branch of Native American languages

Etymology

From the name Algonkian (variation of Algonquian), a group of related Native American peoples and languages.
