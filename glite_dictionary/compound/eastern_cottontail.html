eastern cottontail

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstɚn ˈkɑːtənˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈkɒtntɛɪl / (BrE)
A common species of rabbit (Sylvilagus floridanus) native to eastern and central regions of North America, recognized by its fluffy white tail.
a common North American rabbit species

Examples

The eastern cottontail is often seen hopping through suburban backyards at dawn and dusk.
Predators such as hawks and foxes frequently hunt the eastern cottontail in open fields.
Biologists study the eastern cottontail to understand urban wildlife adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottontail - a North American rabbit distinguished by its white cotton-like tail
