negative feedback

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈfiːdbæk / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈfiːdbak / (BrE)
The return of part of the output of a system in a way that reduces or counteracts the original effect, thereby stabilizing the system.
input-counteracting system feedback

Examples

The amplifier uses negative feedback to ensure the output remains stable.
Biological systems often rely on negative feedback to maintain homeostasis.
Engineers designed the circuit with negative feedback to minimize distortion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positive feedback - feedback that increases the effect of the input

Etymology

Formed from 'negative' (opposing) and 'feedback' (return of information), popularized in 20th-century engineering.
