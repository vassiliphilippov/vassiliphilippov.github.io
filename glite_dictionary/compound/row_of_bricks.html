row of bricks

1. NOUN

/ roʊ əv brɪks / (AmE), / rəʊ əv brɪks / (BrE)
a horizontal course of bricks laid side by side, typically in a straight line, in a wall or structure
a horizontal course of bricks

Examples

A single row of bricks forms the base of the garden wall.
The mason laid another row of bricks to increase the wall's height.
Please align each row of bricks carefully for a straight finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: course - a continuous horizontal layer of bricks in a wall
