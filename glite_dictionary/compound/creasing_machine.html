creasing machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːsɪŋ məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈkriːsɪŋ məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
A manual or electric device used to make creases in paper, card, or coverstock, especially in printing and bookbinding.
device for making creases in paper

Examples

The print shop invested in a new creasing machine to improve the quality of their folded brochures.
A creasing machine speeds up the process of preparing hard covers for binding.
After the pages were printed, they were sent through the creasing machine for precise folding.
