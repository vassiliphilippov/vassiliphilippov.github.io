public eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk aɪ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk aɪ / (BrE)
the state of being the focus of widespread public attention
the focus of public attention

Examples

Politicians often find themselves in the public eye, whether they like it or not.
After the scandal, the company was thrust into the public eye for several months.
She values her privacy and tries to stay out of the public eye as much as possible.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed being in the limelight
when Congress investigates it brings the full glare of publicity to the agency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limelight - a position at the center of attention
  • Synonym: spotlight - public attention or notice
