Widal's test

1. NOUN

/ waɪˈdɑlz tɛst / (AmE), / wiˈdɑːlz tɛst / (BrE)
a medical test used to diagnose typhoid fever and other infections caused by Salmonella bacteria, by detecting specific antibodies in a patient's blood.
test for typhoid and Salmonella infections
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a Widal's test to confirm the diagnosis of typhoid fever.
A positive Widal's test indicates the presence of Salmonella antibodies in the blood.
In areas where typhoid is common, the Widal's test is frequently used for screening purposes.

Etymology

Named after French physician Georges-Fernand Widal, who developed the test in 1896.
