person from Porlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsən frəm ˈpɔr.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsən frəm ˈpɔː.lɒk / (BrE)
a person who interrupts or distracts, especially at a critical moment; often used metaphorically
an unwelcome interrupter or distraction

Examples

During my most productive hour, a person from Porlock rang the doorbell and broke my concentration.
Whenever inspiration strikes, it seems a person from Porlock appears to interrupt me.
Writers often dread the arrival of a person from Porlock in the form of an unexpected visitor.

Synset Examples

She joked about being a person from Porlock during her speech.
He was a true person from Porlock, interrupting my thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interloper - a person who becomes involved in a place or situation where they are not wanted
  • Synonym: distraction - something that interrupts or diverts attention

Etymology

From an incident described by Samuel Taylor Coleridge, where a visitor from Porlock interrupted the poet during his writing of 'Kubla Khan.'
