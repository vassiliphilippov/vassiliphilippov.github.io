brachial artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkiəl ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈbræk.i.əl ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
the main artery of the upper arm, continuing from the axillary artery and dividing into the radial and ulnar arteries at the elbow
main artery of the upper arm
medical

Examples

The brachial artery is often used to measure blood pressure with a cuff.
After the accident, surgeons had to repair the torn brachial artery.
The nurse carefully palpated the brachial artery to check for a pulse.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: radial artery - artery in the forearm that is one of the two terminal branches of the brachial artery
  • Degree Lower: ulnar artery - artery in the forearm that is one of the two terminal branches of the brachial artery

Etymology

From Latin 'brachialis' (of the arm) + 'arteria' (artery).
