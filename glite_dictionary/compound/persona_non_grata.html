persona non grata

1. NOUN

/ pərˌsoʊnə nɑn ˈɡrɑːtə / (AmE), / pəˌsəʊnə nɒn ˈɡrɑːtə / (BrE)
A person who is not welcome or unacceptable in a particular place or group, especially for diplomatic reasons.
an unwelcome or unacceptable person

Examples

After the scandal, he became a persona non grata in the political community.
The ambassador was declared persona non grata and expelled from the country.
Ever since her comments, she has been treated as a persona non grata at the club.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persona grata - a person who is welcome or acceptable, especially diplomatically

Etymology

From Latin, literally meaning 'unwelcome person'.

2. NOUN

/ pərˌsoʊnə nɑn ˈɡrɑtə / (AmE), / pəˌsəʊnə nɒn ˈɡrɑːtə / (BrE)
A person, especially a diplomat, who is unacceptable or unwelcome, particularly to the government of the country to which they have been sent.
unwelcome or unacceptable person (esp. diplomatic)
legal

Examples

After his controversial speech, the ambassador was declared persona non grata and expelled from the country.
She became persona non grata in diplomatic circles after leaking confidential documents.
Once the scandal surfaced, the politician was effectively persona non grata in his own party.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persona grata - a welcome or acceptable person, especially as a diplomat

Etymology

From Latin 'persona non grata', meaning 'an unwelcome person'.
