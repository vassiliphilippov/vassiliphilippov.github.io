Lateran Treaty

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.t̬ɚ.ən ˈtriː.ti / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tər.ən ˈtriː.ti / (BrE)
the 1929 agreement between Italy and the Holy See that recognized Vatican City as a sovereign independent state
1929 treaty establishing Vatican City

Examples

The Lateran Treaty established the independence of the Vatican City in 1929.
According to the Lateran Treaty, the Holy See maintains sovereignty over Vatican City.
The Lateran Treaty marked a significant turning point in the relationship between Italy and the Catholic Church.

Etymology

Named after the Lateran Palace in Rome, where the treaty was signed.
