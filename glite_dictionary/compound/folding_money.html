folding money

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊldɪŋ ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈfəʊldɪŋ ˌmʌni / (BrE)
Paper currency issued by a government or central bank, used as a substitute for coins.
paper currency; banknotes
informal

Examples

He reached into his wallet for some folding money to pay the bill.
Do you have any folding money on you, or just coins?
Credit cards are handy, but sometimes you just need a bit of folding money.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coin - a flat, typically round piece of metal used as money
  • Synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money issued by a bank or government
