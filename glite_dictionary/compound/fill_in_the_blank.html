to fill in the blank

1. VERB

/ fɪl ɪn ðə blæŋk / (AmE), / fɪl ɪn ðə blaŋk / (BrE)
to supply the missing word or information in a sentence or statement, often in an exercise or test
supply missing word in a statement

Examples

Please fill in the blank with a suitable noun for each sentence.
On the exam, students had to fill in the blank using the words from the box.
The worksheet requires you to fill in the blank to complete each statement.

Synset Examples

During the quiz, students were told to fill in the blank for each question.
The teacher asked us to fill in the blank with the correct answer.
He struggled to fill in the blank on the test.
