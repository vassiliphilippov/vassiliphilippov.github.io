cyanic acid

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / saɪˈanɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, poisonous, and volatile liquid acid (HCNO) that hydrolyzes easily to ammonia and carbon dioxide
a volatile, poisonous acid (HCNO)
medical

Examples

Laboratory handling of cyanic acid requires strict safety precautions due to its highly poisonous and volatile nature.
When cyanic acid comes into contact with water, it rapidly decomposes into ammonia and carbon dioxide.
Researchers discovered a new reaction pathway involving the intermediate formation of cyanic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isocyanic acid - a structural isomer of cyanic acid, another unstable acid

Etymology

From 'cyanic' + 'acid'; 'cyanic' from Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue), due to the color of some cyanide compounds.
