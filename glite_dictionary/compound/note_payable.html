note payable

1. NOUN

/ noʊt ˈpeɪəbl̩ / (AmE), / nəʊt ˈpeɪəbl̩ / (BrE)
a written promise to pay a specific amount of money at a specified time, usually in the context of business or finance
written promise to pay money at a set time
legal

Examples

The company recorded a note payable on its balance sheet to reflect the money it owes to the bank.
He signed a note payable with a maturity date of six months after the loan was issued.
Both businesses agreed that the note payable would bear interest until the amount was fully paid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promissory note - a written promise to pay a sum of money to a specified person
