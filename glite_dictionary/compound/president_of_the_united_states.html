President of the United States

1. NOUN

/ ˌprezɪˈdɛnt əv ðə ˌjunɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (AmE), / ˌprɛzɪˈdɛnt ɒv ði ˌjuːnɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the elected head of state and chief executive officer of the United States of America
the head of state of the United States

Examples

The President of the United States delivered a speech addressing climate change.
Each new President of the United States brings their own leadership style to the office.
The inauguration of the President of the United States is a major national event.

Synset Examples

a President is elected every four years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime minister - the head of government in parliamentary systems
  • Degree Lower: vice president - the official who is next in rank to a president

2. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt əv ðə ˌjuː.naɪ.tɪd steɪts / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt əv ðə ˌjuːˈnaɪ.tɪd steɪts / (BrE)
the head of state and head of government of the United States, serving as the chief executive officer of the federal government
the chief executive of the US government

Examples

The President of the United States addressed the nation in a televised speech last night.
As President of the United States, she signed several important pieces of legislation.
He dreamed of one day becoming President of the United States and leading the country.

Synset Examples

the President likes to jog every morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: president - an executive officer of a republic, especially the chief executive of a nation
  • Synonym: commander-in-chief - the supreme commander of a nation's military forces
