Primus stove

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪməs stoʊv / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməs stəʊv / (BrE)
a portable stove that burns paraffin (kerosene), commonly used by campers and travelers for cooking outdoors
portable paraffin stove for camping

Examples

We cooked our breakfast on a Primus stove while camping in the mountains.
The hiker always brings a Primus stove for making hot tea during long treks.
Early explorers relied on the Primus stove to prepare meals in harsh conditions.

Etymology

From the trademark Primus, first used in Sweden in the late 19th century.
