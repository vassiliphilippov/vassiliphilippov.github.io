naval weaponry

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl ˈwɛpənri / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˈwɛpənri / (BrE)
Weapons and armaments designed for use by warships or in naval warfare.
weapons for warships

Examples

The country invested heavily in modern naval weaponry to defend its coastline.
Advances in naval weaponry changed the balance of power at sea.
During the war, both sides improved their naval weaponry for better offense and defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaponry - all the weapons possessed by a country or group

Etymology

From 'naval' (relating to ships/navies, from Latin 'navalis') and 'weaponry' (the weapons collectively).
