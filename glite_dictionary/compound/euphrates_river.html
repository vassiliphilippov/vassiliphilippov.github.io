Euphrates River

1. PROPN

/ juˈfreɪtiz ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / juːˈfreɪtiːz ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in southwestern Asia, flowing through Turkey, Syria, and Iraq into the Persian Gulf, and significant in the history of ancient Mesopotamia
major river in southwestern Asia

Examples

The ancient city of Babylon was built along the banks of the Euphrates River.
Much of Iraq's agriculture relies on water from the Euphrates River.
Environmental changes have affected the flow of the Euphrates River in recent decades.

Etymology

From Greek Euphratēs, from Old Persian Ufrātu, from Akkadian Purattu.
