Liberal Democrat Party

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪbərəl ˈdɛməˌkræt ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəl ˈdɛm.ə.kræt ˈpɑː.ti / (BrE)
A centrist political party in the United Kingdom, formed in 1988, supporting social liberalism and reforms to improve the conditions of working people.
centrist political party in the UK
british

Examples

The Liberal Democrat Party proposed new environmental policies during the election campaign.
Many young voters have shown increased support for the Liberal Democrat Party in recent years.
The Liberal Democrat Party was formed from a merger of the Liberal Party and SDP in the late 1980s.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Labour Party - major left-wing political party in the UK
  • Antonym: Conservative Party - major right-wing political party in the UK

Etymology

Name formed from merger of the Liberal Party and the Social Democratic Party in 1988.
