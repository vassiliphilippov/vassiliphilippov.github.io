water of hydration

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər əv haɪˌdreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə əv haɪˌdreɪʃən / (BrE)
water molecules that are chemically bound within a hydrated compound, forming part of its crystal structure
water bound in hydrated compounds

Examples

Copper sulfate pentahydrate contains five molecules of water of hydration per formula unit.
Heating the sample drives off the water of hydration and leaves the anhydrous salt behind.
The blue color of the crystal is due to its water of hydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrate - a compound with water molecules bound to it
