white magic

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmædʒɪk / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmadʒɪk / (BrE)
the practice of magic intended solely for good or beneficial purposes
magic used for good purposes

Examples

She studied white magic to heal and help those in her village.
Some believe that white magic can protect people from harm and misfortune.
The witch claimed her spells were all forms of white magic meant only to do good.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black magic - magic practiced for evil or selfish purposes

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmædʒɪk / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmadʒɪk / (BrE)
magic performed with the intention of bringing about good, healing, or benevolent outcomes, often in contrast to black magic.
beneficial or benevolent magic

Examples

She believed that white magic could protect her family from harm.
Some people turn to white magic as a form of spiritual healing.
The villagers were grateful for the blessings brought by white magic rituals.

Synset Examples

She believed in white magic for healing and protection.
He was fascinated by the concept of white magic in folklore.
They performed a ritual using white magic to bless the home.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black magic - magic intended to cause harm or for evil purposes
