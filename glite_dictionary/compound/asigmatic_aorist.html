asigmatic aorist

1. NOUN

/ əˌsɪɡ.mætɪk ˈeɪ.ə.rɪst / (AmE), / əˌsɪɡ.mætɪk ˈeɪ.ə.rɪst / (BrE)
A form of the aorist tense in Ancient Greek that lacks the sigma (-s-) phoneme in its formation.
type of aorist tense lacking sigma

Examples

The asigmatic aorist is often found in the earliest stages of Ancient Greek.
Many verbs used the asigmatic aorist before the sigmatic forms became more common.
Researchers study the asigmatic aorist to understand historical changes in verb conjugation.

Synset Examples

Over time, the asigmatic aorist became increasingly marked as an archaic language feature and was eventually replaced by the other two aorist formations.

Etymology

From Greek prefix 'a-' (without) + 'sigma' (the Greek letter σ) + 'aorist' (a verb tense).
