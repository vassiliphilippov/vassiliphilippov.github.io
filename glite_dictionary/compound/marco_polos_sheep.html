Marco Polo's sheep

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrkoʊ ˈpoʊloʊz ʃiːp / (AmE), / ˌmɑːkəʊ ˈpəʊləʊz ʃiːp / (BrE)
a large wild sheep native to Central Asia, notable for its massive spiraled horns; sometimes regarded as a variety of argali (Ovis ammon)
wild sheep with large horns found in Central Asia

Examples

The horns of the Marco Polo's sheep can grow to impressive lengths, making them a sought-after trophy.
Researchers in Tajikistan are studying the migration patterns of Marco Polo's sheep to better protect the species.
Marco Polo's sheep inhabit the mountainous regions of the Pamirs, where their large horns help them in fights for dominance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argali - a large wild sheep of Central Asia (Ovis ammon)

Etymology

Named after Marco Polo, the Venetian traveler, who described the animal in the 13th century.
