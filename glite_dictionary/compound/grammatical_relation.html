grammatical relation

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈmætɪkəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmatɪkəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between words or phrases in a sentence that is established by grammatical structure, such as subject, object, or modifier
relationship defined by grammar in a sentence

Examples

The subject and object are common examples of grammatical relations in English sentences.
Understanding grammatical relation helps clarify how words interact within a complex sentence.
Different languages may express the same grammatical relation using different grammatical structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grammatical function - the function performed by a word or phrase in a sentence, such as subject or object
