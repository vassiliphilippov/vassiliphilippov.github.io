charcoal pile

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrˌkoʊl paɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːkəʊl paɪl / (BrE)
a heap of wood, covered with turf or moistened clay, used to make wood charcoal by slow burning
structure for making wood charcoal

Examples

The workers stacked the wood into a charcoal pile and covered it with earth to begin the burning process.
Historical reenactors demonstrated how a traditional charcoal pile was constructed in the forest.
Smoke gently rose from the charcoal pile as the slow transformation of wood to charcoal took place.

Synset Examples

Professional charcoal burners often lived alone in small huts in order to tend their charcoal piles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charcoal kiln - an enclosed oven or structure for producing charcoal
