sculptural relief

1. NOUN

/ skʌlp.tʃɚ.əl rɪˈlif / (AmE), / skʌlp.tʃər.əl rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
a sculptural technique in which shapes or figures are carved from a flat surface so that they project from the background
sculpture with raised shapes or figures

Examples

The museum had an impressive sculptural relief depicting scenes from ancient mythology.
Artists often use sculptural relief to create texture and depth on architectural surfaces.
He spent months working on a sculptural relief for the cathedral's main entrance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bas-relief - sculpture technique where figures are only slightly raised from the background
  • Degree Higher: high relief - sculpture technique where figures project more boldly from the background

Etymology

From sculpture + relief, with 'relief' from French 'relief', meaning "raised work".
