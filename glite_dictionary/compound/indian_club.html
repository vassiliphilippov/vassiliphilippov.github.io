Indian club

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən klʌb / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪən klʌb / (BrE)
a bottle-shaped wooden club used as an exercise implement in fitness training and physical therapy
bottle-shaped exercise club

Examples

He swung the Indian club in wide circles to warm up his shoulders.
Victorian-era gyms often featured exercises with the Indian club.
Physiotherapists sometimes recommend exercises with an Indian club to increase flexibility.

Etymology

Named for their use in traditional physical training in India, adopted in Europe and America in the 19th century.
