prelude to

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛlˌjud tu / (AmE), / ˈprɛljuːd tʊ / (BrE)
an event or action that serves as an introduction or precursor to something more important
introduction to something to come

Examples

The rain was a prelude to the storm, signaling what was about to come.
A sudden drop in temperature can be a prelude to snowfall in the region.
The negotiations were merely a prelude to the much larger agreement signed later.

Synset Examples

The rain was a prelude to the storm.
Her speech served as a prelude to the main event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precursor - a person or thing that comes before another
  • Antonym: epilogue - a section at the end of a book or play that serves as a conclusion

Etymology

From Latin 'praeludium', meaning 'preliminary play', from 'prae-' (before) + 'ludere' (to play).
