cephalic vein

1. NOUN

/ səˈfælɪk veɪn / (AmE), / sɪˈfælɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a large superficial vein of the upper arm that drains into the axillary vein
large vein of the upper arm
medical

Examples

The nurse inserted the IV line into the patient's cephalic vein for blood collection.
During surgery, the cephalic vein is often visible beneath the skin of the upper arm.
Anatomy students traced the path of the cephalic vein from the forearm to the shoulder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basilic vein - another large vein of the arm that runs along the inner side

Etymology

From Latin 'cephalicus' (relating to the head), from Greek 'kephalē' (head); named for its position and prominence.
