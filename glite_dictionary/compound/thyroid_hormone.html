thyroid hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪˌrɔɪd ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd ˈhɔː.məʊn / (BrE)
any of several hormones produced by the thyroid gland, including thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3), that regulate metabolism and other bodily functions
hormone from thyroid gland
medical

Examples

Dr. Smith ordered blood tests to measure her thyroid hormone levels.
An imbalance in thyroid hormone can cause symptoms like fatigue and weight changes.
The production of thyroid hormone is essential for normal growth and development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroxine - main hormone (T4) produced by the thyroid gland
  • Synonym: triiodothyronine - powerful thyroid hormone (T3) affecting metabolism

Etymology

From 'thyroid' (from Greek 'thyreoeidēs', shield-shaped) + 'hormone' (from Greek 'hormōn', setting in motion).
