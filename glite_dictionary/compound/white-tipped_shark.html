white-tipped shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt tɪpt ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt tɪpt ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large deep-water shark with a distinctive white-tipped dorsal fin, found worldwide and considered among the most dangerous sharks
oceanic shark with white-tipped fins

Examples

The white-tipped shark is known for its bold behavior around humans in open water.
Divers observed several white-tipped sharks circling near the reef during their expedition.
The white-tipped shark can be easily identified by the white markings on its dorsal fin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oceanic whitetip shark - a shark species (Carcharhinus longimanus) with white-tipped fins found in deep water
