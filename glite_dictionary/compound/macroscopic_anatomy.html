macroscopic anatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.rəˈskɑː.pɪk əˈnæt.ə.mi / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəˈskɒ.pɪk əˈnæt.ə.mi / (BrE)
The study of the structure of the body and its parts that can be seen without a microscope; also known as gross anatomy.
study of body structures visible to the naked eye
medical

Examples

Students of medicine often begin with macroscopic anatomy before progressing to more detailed microscopic study.
The professor explained that macroscopic anatomy focuses on organs and tissues visible without magnification.
A textbook on macroscopic anatomy typically includes detailed diagrams of the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gross anatomy - the study of body structures visible to the naked eye
  • Antonym: microscopic anatomy - the study of body structures using a microscope

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) and skopein (to look), combined with anatomy from Greek anatomē (dissection).
