brand confusion

1. NOUN

/ brænd kənˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / brænd kənˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
A situation in which consumers mistake one brand for another, often due to similarities in branding, packaging, or advertising.
mistaking one brand for another

Examples

The company's new logo accidentally caused brand confusion with a competitor's product.
Heavy advertising from similar companies can increase the risk of brand confusion among customers.
To avoid brand confusion, each product needs a distinct packaging design.

Synset Examples

Symbol-pushing in advertising often leads to brand confusion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brand recognition - the extent to which consumers can correctly identify a brand
