genus Sanguinaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sæŋɡwɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs saŋɡwɪˈneəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the poppy family, represented by a single species, bloodroot (Sanguinaria canadensis).
genus of flowering plant (bloodroot)

Examples

The genus Sanguinaria is known for its distinctive red sap, especially in the species bloodroot.
Botanists have classified bloodroot as the only member of the genus Sanguinaria.
The genus Sanguinaria is native to eastern North America and blooms in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodroot - a perennial plant of the poppy family with red sap

Etymology

From Latin 'sanguinarius' meaning 'of blood' or 'bloody', referencing the red sap of the bloodroot plant.
