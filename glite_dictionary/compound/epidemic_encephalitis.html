epidemic encephalitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk ɪnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk ɛnˌkɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
a form of encephalitis that occurred in widespread outbreaks between 1915 and 1926, characterized by paralysis of the eye muscles and marked muscular weakness
historical epidemic form of encephalitis
medical

Examples

The outbreak of epidemic encephalitis devastated many communities in the early twentieth century.
Doctors documented thousands of cases of epidemic encephalitis between 1915 and 1926.
Paralysis of eye muscles was one of the main symptoms seen in patients with epidemic encephalitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encephalitis - inflammation of the brain
