fleet admiral

1. NOUN

/ ˈfliːt ˌæd.mɪ.rəl / (AmE), / ˈfliːt ˌæd.mɪ.rəl / (BrE)
an admiral of the highest rank, typically in charge of a country's entire naval fleet
highest naval admiral rank

Examples

He was promoted to fleet admiral during the war for his exceptional leadership.
Only a handful of officers have ever held the title of fleet admiral in the United States Navy.
The fleet admiral is responsible for strategic decisions affecting the entire navy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: admiral - a high-ranking naval officer

Etymology

From 'fleet' (a group of ships) and 'admiral' (a senior naval officer), originally from Arabic 'amīr al-' (commander of the) and Latin 'admirabilis' (admirable).
