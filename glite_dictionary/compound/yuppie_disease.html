yuppie disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌpi diˌziz / (AmE), / ˈjʌpi dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a humorous or critical term for the tendency among young professionals to be excessively materialistic or focused on consumer trends
materialistic attitudes among young professionals

Examples

Many critics argue that yuppie disease is weakening the sense of community among young urbanites.
As soon as he got his first high-paying job, he caught a bad case of yuppie disease.
Social commentators often discuss yuppie disease in the context of modern urban lifestyles.

Synset Examples

She joked that her love for trendy cafes was a sign of yuppie disease.
They called it yuppie disease when he started prioritizing brands over quality.
His obsession with status cars was a classic case of yuppie disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumerism - the protection or promotion of the interests of consumers; the preoccupation with buying and owning goods
  • Synonym: materialism - the tendency to consider material possessions and physical comfort as more important than spiritual values

Etymology

Formed from 'yuppie' (young urban professional) and 'disease' (used figuratively).
