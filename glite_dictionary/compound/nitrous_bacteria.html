nitrous bacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrəs bækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəs bakˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Bacteria found in soil that oxidize ammonia into nitrites, playing a key role in the nitrogen cycle.
soil bacteria converting ammonia to nitrites
medical

Examples

Nitrous bacteria play a crucial role in the first stage of nitrification in the soil.
Without sufficient nitrous bacteria, the process of converting ammonia to nitrite would slow drastically.
Farmers monitor the activity of nitrous bacteria to maintain healthy soil fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrifying bacteria - bacteria that oxidize ammonia to nitrite or nitrite to nitrate

Etymology

From 'nitrous', relating to nitrogen, and 'bacteria', from Greek 'bakterion' (small staff).
