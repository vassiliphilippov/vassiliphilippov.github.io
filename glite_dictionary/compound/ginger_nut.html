ginger nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪndʒər nʌt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒə nʌt / (BrE)
a crisp, typically round biscuit or cookie flavored with ginger, popular in the UK and some Commonwealth countries
crisp ginger-flavored biscuit/cookie
british

Examples

I bought a packet of ginger nuts to have with my tea.
The café served ginger nuts alongside the coffee as a complimentary treat.
He loves the spicy crunch of a traditional ginger nut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger snap - a crisp ginger-flavored biscuit or cookie, especially in North America

Etymology

From 'ginger', the main flavoring ingredient, and 'nut', possibly referencing its shape or texture.
