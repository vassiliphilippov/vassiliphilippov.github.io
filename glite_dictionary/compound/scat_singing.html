scat singing

1. NOUN

/ skæt ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / skæt ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
A style of jazz singing in which the vocalist improvises melodies and rhythms using nonsense syllables instead of words, often imitating the sound of a musical instrument.
jazz singing with improvised syllables

Examples

Ella Fitzgerald was famous for her scat singing during live jazz performances.
The musician broke into a lively session of scat singing, delighting the audience with his improvisation.
In class, we learned the basics of scat singing to develop our jazz vocal skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocal improvisation - creating vocal melodies spontaneously

Etymology

From 'scat', an improvised vocal style in jazz, possibly of imitative origin.
