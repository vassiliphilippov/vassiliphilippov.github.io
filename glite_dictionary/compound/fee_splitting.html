fee splitting

1. NOUN

/ fi ˈsplɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / fiː ˈsplɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice, especially among doctors or lawyers, of paying part of a fee to another for referring a client or patient
payment for referral of clients or patients
legal

Examples

The hospital does not tolerate fee splitting among its medical staff.
Some consider fee splitting to be unethical, especially when it affects patient care.
The law firm was accused of engaging in fee splitting practices to increase referrals.
