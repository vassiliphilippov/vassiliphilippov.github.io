declaratory sentence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɛrəˌtɔri ˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / dɪˈklærətəri ˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
A sentence in the indicative mood that makes a statement or declaration.
sentence that makes a statement

Examples

The teacher asked us to write one declaratory sentence about our hometown.
"The sky is blue" is an example of a declaratory sentence.
In English grammar, a declaratory sentence states a fact or opinion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interrogative sentence - a sentence that asks a question
  • Antonym: imperative sentence - a sentence expressing a command

Etymology

From Latin 'declaratorius' (serving to declare) + 'sentence' from Latin 'sententia' (meaning, opinion).
