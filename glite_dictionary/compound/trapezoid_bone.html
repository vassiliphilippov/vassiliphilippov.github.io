trapezoid bone

1. NOUN

/ trəˈpiː.zɔɪd ˌboʊn / (AmE), / trəˈpiː.zɔɪd ˌbəʊn / (BrE)
the small wrist bone situated between the trapezium and capitate bones in the human hand
a wrist bone between trapezium and capitate
medical

Examples

An X-ray revealed that the trapezoid bone was fractured after the fall.
The trapezoid bone connects with four other bones in the wrist.
Surgeons carefully examined the trapezoid bone during the delicate hand operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpal bone - any of the bones forming the wrist

Etymology

From Latin 'trapezoides' (table-shaped), referring to its shape.
