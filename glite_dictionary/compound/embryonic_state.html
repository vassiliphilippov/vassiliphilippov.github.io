embryonic state

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.briˈɑnɪk steɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈɒnɪk steɪt / (BrE)
An early or undeveloped stage in the development of something.
early or undeveloped stage

Examples

His research was still in its embryonic state when he presented the first results.
The project remains in an embryonic state, with many details yet to be defined.
Great inventions often begin in an embryonic state before being developed into something remarkable.

Synset Examples

His ideas were still in an embryonic state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incipient stage - beginning or initial stage
  • Antonym: mature stage - fully developed stage

Etymology

From embryonic, based on Greek 'embryon' (fetus); 'state' from Latin 'status' (condition).
