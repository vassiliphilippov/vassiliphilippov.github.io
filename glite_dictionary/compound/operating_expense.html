operating expense

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpəˌreɪtɪŋ ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / ˈɒpəreɪtɪŋ ɪkˈspɛns / (BrE)
A regular cost incurred in the day-to-day operation of a business or property, such as utilities, insurance, and property taxes, but not including depreciation, financing, or income taxes.
cost of running a business or property

Examples

The company's operating expenses increased due to higher utility bills and insurance premiums.
We need to reduce our operating expenses to improve profitability.
Rent and maintenance are typical operating expenses for commercial properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operating cost - total expense incurred to run a business, including operating expenses and cost of goods sold
