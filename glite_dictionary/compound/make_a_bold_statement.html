to make a bold statement

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə boʊld ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / meɪk ə bəʊld ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
to express a strong, clear, and confident idea or impression, often in a way that attracts attention
express a strong, confident idea or impression

Examples

Her red dress really made a bold statement at the gala.
Many artists make a bold statement through their work to challenge social norms.
Wearing bright colors in a conservative setting can make a bold statement.

Synset Examples

Her spiky haircut made a bold statement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be very noticeable or remarkable
