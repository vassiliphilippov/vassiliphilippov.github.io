washing soda

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃɪŋ ˌsoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃɪŋ ˌsəʊdə / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound (sodium carbonate) used especially in making soap, cleaning agents, and glass
sodium carbonate used in soaps and cleaning

Examples

Please add a spoonful of washing soda to the laundry to help remove stubborn stains.
Many industrial cleaners contain washing soda because of its powerful grease-cutting properties.
You can find washing soda in the cleaning aisle at most supermarkets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soda ash - another name for sodium carbonate, especially in technical contexts
