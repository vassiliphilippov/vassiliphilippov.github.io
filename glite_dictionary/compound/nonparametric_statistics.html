nonparametric statistics

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˌpærəˈmɛtrɪk stəˈtɪstɪks / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌpærəˈmɛtrɪk stəˈtɪstɪks / (BrE)
the branch of statistics that analyzes data without making assumptions about the form or parameters of the underlying distribution
statistics without distribution assumptions

Examples

Researchers often use nonparametric statistics when data do not meet normality assumptions.
The results were analyzed using nonparametric statistics due to the small sample size.
Many introductory statistics courses now include units on nonparametric statistics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parametric statistics - statistics making assumptions about the form and parameters of distributions

Etymology

From 'nonparametric,' from non- (not) + 'parametric' (pertaining to parameters), and 'statistics' from New Latin 'statisticus.'
