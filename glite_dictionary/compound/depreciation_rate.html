depreciation rate

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən reɪt / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən reɪt / (BrE)
The rate at which the value of an asset decreases over time, often used for tax calculations.
rate of asset value decrease

Examples

The company's accountant explained how the depreciation rate affects annual financial statements.
Different types of assets have different depreciation rates for tax purposes.
A higher depreciation rate reduces the book value of equipment more rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - reduction in value of an asset over time
