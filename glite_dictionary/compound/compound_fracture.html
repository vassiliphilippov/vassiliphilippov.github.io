compound fracture

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpaʊnd ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈpaʊnd ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
A fracture in which a broken bone pierces the skin, often accompanied by an open wound and possible damage to surrounding soft tissue.
broken bone with an open wound
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed a compound fracture that required immediate surgery to prevent infection.
After the accident, the cyclist suffered a compound fracture of the leg, with the bone protruding through the skin.
Treating a compound fracture demands urgent medical attention due to the risk of severe bleeding and contamination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simple fracture - A bone fracture that does not break the skin or damage surrounding tissue
