order Picariae

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər pɪˈkɛəriˌi / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə pɪˈkeərɪˌiː / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order of birds, used in some classifications, nearly equivalent to the order Coraciiformes.
old bird order (taxonomic)

Examples

The order Picariae was proposed in older classifications but is rarely used today.
Some ornithologists considered kingfishers to belong to the order Picariae.
Modern taxonomy has replaced order Picariae with more refined bird groupings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order Coraciiformes - an order of birds including kingfishers and rollers

Etymology

From Latin 'Picariae', the plural of 'picarius' (pertaining to woodpeckers).
