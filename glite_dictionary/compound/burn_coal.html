to burn coal

1. VERB

/ bɝn koʊl / (AmE), / bɜːn kəʊl / (BrE)
to use coal as a fuel by setting it on fire, especially for producing heat or energy
use coal as fuel

Examples

Many power plants still burn coal to generate electricity.
Households used to burn coal in their fireplaces before gas heating became common.
The factory burns coal as its primary source of energy.

Synset Examples

Factories often burn coal to power their machinery.
They decided to burn coal for heating during winter.
The old steam train used to burn coal for fuel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn wood - to use wood as a fuel by burning it
  • Synonym: burn oil - to use oil as a fuel by burning it
