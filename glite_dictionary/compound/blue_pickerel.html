blue pickerel

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈpɪkərəl / (AmE), / bluː ˈpɪkərəl / (BrE)
a variety of walleye or pike that inhabits the Great Lakes, known for its bluish coloration
fish variety in the Great Lakes

Examples

The blue pickerel is often sought by anglers in Lake Erie.
Scientists recently studied the diet of the blue pickerel population.
Local fishermen claim that blue pickerel are harder to catch in midsummer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel - a small North American freshwater pike
