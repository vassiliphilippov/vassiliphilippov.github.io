Lombardy poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑm.bɑr.di ˈpɑ.plɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɒm.bə.di ˈpɒp.lə / (BrE)
a type of poplar tree (Populus nigra 'Italica') known for its tall, narrow, columnar shape and upright branches
a narrow, upright poplar tree

Examples

A row of Lombardy poplars lined the driveway, creating a dramatic, vertical accent in the landscape.
Many people plant the Lombardy poplar for its rapid growth and distinctive shape.
The Lombardy poplar can serve as an effective windbreak on open farmland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a tall, fast-growing tree of the willow family

Etymology

Named after the Lombardy region in northern Italy, where this form of poplar became popular.
