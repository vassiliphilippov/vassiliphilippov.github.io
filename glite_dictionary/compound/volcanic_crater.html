volcanic crater

1. NOUN

/ vɑːlˈkænɪk ˈkreɪtər / (AmE), / vɒlˈkanɪk ˈkreɪtə / (BrE)
a bowl-shaped depression at the summit of a volcano, typically formed by volcanic activity
bowl-shaped depression at a volcano's summit

Examples

The scientists studied the volcanic crater after the recent eruption.
A deep lake has formed inside the ancient volcanic crater.
Tourists hiked to the rim of the volcanic crater to view the spectacular landscape.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: caldera - a large volcanic crater, typically formed by a major eruption

Etymology

From 'volcanic' (relating to volcanoes) and 'crater', from Greek 'krater' (mixing bowl).
