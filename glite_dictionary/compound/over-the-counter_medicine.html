over-the-counter medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚ ðə ˈkaʊntɚ ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈkaʊntə ˈmɛdəsən / (BrE)
A medicine that can be purchased without a doctor's prescription.
medicine sold without a prescription
medical

Examples

Many people rely on over-the-counter medicine to treat common colds and headaches.
You should always read the label before taking any over-the-counter medicine.
He asked the pharmacist for advice on choosing an over-the-counter medicine for his allergies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prescription drug - a medicine that requires a doctor's prescription

Etymology

From 'over the counter', referring to medicines available directly from a pharmacy counter, without prescription.
