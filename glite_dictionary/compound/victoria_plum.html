Victoria plum

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈktɔriə plʌm / (AmE), / vɪˈktɔːriə plʌm / (BrE)
a large, sweet red or yellowish plum variety, commonly eaten as a dessert
a variety of large red plum

Examples

The Victoria plum is prized for its sweet taste and juicy flesh.
We made a delicious tart using fresh Victoria plums from the garden.
Every summer, my grandmother would preserve jars of Victoria plum jam.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: plum - a sweet, fleshy stone fruit

Etymology

Named after Queen Victoria (reigned 1837–1901), under whose reign the variety was first introduced in England.
