credit exposure

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdɪt ɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
The total amount of money that a bank or financial institution has lent and stands to lose if it is not repaid.
amount at risk in lending

Examples

The bank reduced its credit exposure to risky borrowers after the policy change.
Monitoring credit exposure is essential for effective risk management in financial institutions.
International banks carefully track their credit exposure in different countries to prevent major losses.

Synset Examples

The calculated expected maximum exposure value is not to be confused with the maximum credit exposure possible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exposure - the state of being exposed to the possibility of loss
