mountain paca

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈpækə / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈpakə / (BrE)
a large burrowing rodent of the mountainous regions of western South America, known for its spotted fur and edible meat
South American burrowing rodent

Examples

The mountain paca is prized for its meat in many Andean communities.
Researchers tracked a mountain paca through the dense foliage of the cloud forest.
Unlike its lowland relatives, the mountain paca prefers higher, cooler environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paca - large burrowing rodent native to Central and South America
