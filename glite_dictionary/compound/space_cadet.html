space cadet

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪs kəˌdɛt / (AmE), / ˈspeɪs kəˌdɛt / (BrE)
a person who is absent-minded, distracted, or out of touch with reality, often seeming unaware of what is happening around them
distracted or out-of-touch person
informal

Examples

Ever since the meeting started, Jenna has been a total space cadet, staring off into space.
You can't count on Mike to remember details—he's a real space cadet sometimes.
The teacher called on the space cadet in the back row who wasn't paying attention.

Etymology

From 'space' (outer space) and 'cadet' (military trainee), popularized in the late 20th century as slang for someone whose mind seems 'in outer space'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪs kəˌdɛt / (AmE), / ˈspeɪs kəˌdɛt / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is forgetful, absent-minded, or disengaged from their surroundings
absent-minded or forgetful person
informal slang

Examples

My brother is such a space cadet that he left his backpack on the bus twice last week.
Try not to be a space cadet during the exam; pay attention to the questions.
People sometimes think she's a space cadet, but she's just deep in thought.

Synset Examples

He's such a space cadet; he forgot his keys again.
Don't be a space cadet during the meeting!
She's a total space cadet when it comes to directions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatterbrain - a person who is forgetful and disorganized
  • Synonym: absent-minded - tending to forget things because not paying attention
  • Antonym: focused - giving full attention to something

Etymology

Originally from science fiction describing trainees for space travel, later used figuratively for someone whose thoughts seem 'out in space.'
