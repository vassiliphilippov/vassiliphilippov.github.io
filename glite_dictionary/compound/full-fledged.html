full-fledged

1. ADJ

/ fʊl flɛdʒd / (AmE), / fʊl flɛdʒd / (BrE)
Completely developed or having reached the necessary status or qualifications.
fully developed or established

Examples

After years of training, she became a full-fledged doctor.
Their small project has transformed into a full-fledged business over time.
He is now a full-fledged member of the professional association.

Synset Examples

After years of training, she became a full-fledged doctor.
The startup has now grown into a full-fledged company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fully developed - fully grown or complete
  • Antonym: incomplete - not fully developed or lacking essential parts

Etymology

From the phrase 'full' + 'fledged', originally referring to a bird with all its feathers grown for flight.
