to rase the site

1. VERB

/ reɪz ðə saɪt / (AmE), / reɪz ðə saɪt / (BrE)
to demolish or clear a piece of land in preparation for building construction
demolish/prep land for building

Examples

The contractor will rase the site before laying the foundation for the new school.
It took several days to rase the site because of the old concrete.
After they rased the site, construction crews arrived to begin building the shopping center.

Synset Examples

We will rase the site before construction begins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - to pull or knock down a structure
  • Synonym: raise the site - to prepare or clear a site for construction (variant spelling)

Etymology

From 'rase' (an archaic verb meaning to demolish), from Old French *raser* (to scrape, shave), from Latin *rasare* (to scrape), and 'site' (location for building).
