mako shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkoʊ ʃɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkəʊ ʃɑːk / (BrE)
A powerful, fast-swimming mackerel shark found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, known for its pointed snout and agility.
species of fast mackerel shark

Examples

The mako shark is one of the fastest sharks in the ocean, reaching speeds up to 45 miles per hour.
Fishermen off the coast of California often encounter the fierce mako shark.
A mako shark can leap several feet out of the water when hunting prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large predatory fish with a cartilaginous skeleton

Etymology

From Maori 'mako' (shark, shark tooth).
