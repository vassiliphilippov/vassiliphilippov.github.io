family relationship

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli rɪˌleɪʃənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli rɪˌleɪʃənʃɪp / (BrE)
the connection between people related by blood, marriage, or adoption
connection by blood, marriage, or adoption

Examples

Maintaining a strong family relationship is important for a child's emotional health.
The court considered the family relationship between the parties when making its decision.
Adoption creates a legal family relationship even without a biological connection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinship - connection or blood relationship between people
  • Degree Lower: affinity - relationship by marriage, not blood
