finger hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə həʊl / (BrE)
A hole designed for inserting a finger, typically to allow gripping, holding, or controlling an object.
hole for inserting a finger

Examples

She adjusted her grip on the scissors, making sure each finger was in its finger hole.
The bowling ball had three perfectly sized finger holes for a comfortable throw.
He inserted his thumb into the finger hole to help open the jar more easily.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə həʊl / (BrE)
A hole in a woodwind instrument that is covered or uncovered by the player's finger to change the pitch.
hole on a woodwind instrument for pitch control

Examples

The clarinet has several finger holes that must be covered to play different notes.
He learned to cover the finger holes properly to produce a clear melody on his recorder.
Each finger hole on the flute changes the sound when it is opened or closed.
