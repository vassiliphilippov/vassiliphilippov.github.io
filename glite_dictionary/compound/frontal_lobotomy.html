frontal lobotomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌn.t̬əl loʊˈbɑː.t̬ə.mi / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.təl ləʊˈbɒ.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the nerve pathways to and from the frontal lobes of the brain are cut, often resulting in significant changes to cognition and personality
surgical brain procedure affecting frontal lobes
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a frontal lobotomy in the 1940s to treat severe mental illness.
Due to the risks, doctors rarely perform a frontal lobotomy today.
Historical records show that frontal lobotomy often led to drastic changes in personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobotomy - a surgical operation involving incision into the prefrontal lobe of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'frons' (forehead, front) + Greek 'lobos' (lobe) + 'tome' (cutting).
