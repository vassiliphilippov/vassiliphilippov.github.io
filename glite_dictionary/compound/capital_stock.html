capital stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəl stɑk / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtəl stɒk / (BrE)
the total nominal value of the shares that a corporation is authorized to issue, representing ownership in the company.
nominal value of a corporation's issued shares

Examples

The company's capital stock was increased after the new shares were issued.
Investors were interested in the book value of the firm's capital stock.
According to the balance sheet, the capital stock stands at ten million dollars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: share capital - the money a company raises by selling shares

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtl stɑk / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtl stɒk / (BrE)
The total number of shares that a corporation is authorized to issue according to its articles of incorporation.
maximum authorized shares of a corporation
legal

Examples

The company's capital stock was increased to allow for future expansion.
According to the articles of incorporation, the capital stock is set at 10 million shares.
Shareholders voted to amend the capital stock to raise additional funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: share capital - the money raised by a company through issuing shares
