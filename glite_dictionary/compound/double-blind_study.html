double-blind study

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl blaɪnd ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl blaɪnd ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
an experimental method in which neither the participants nor the experimenters know which participants belong to the control group or the test group, to prevent bias
experimental method masking group assignments

Examples

The researchers designed a double-blind study to ensure that neither the doctors nor the patients knew who was receiving the new drug.
A double-blind study minimizes bias by preventing both participants and staff from knowing the treatment assignments.
When evaluating the effectiveness of a vaccine, a double-blind study is often considered the gold standard.

Synset Examples

a double-blind procedure is used to guard against both experimenter bias and placebo effects

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single-blind study - an experimental method in which only the subjects do not know key information
