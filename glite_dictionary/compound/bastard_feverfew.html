bastard feverfew

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstərd ˈfiːvɚˌfjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstəd ˈfiːvəˌfjuː / (BrE)
A tropical American annual weed with small daisy-like white flowers, found as an adventive species in the southern United States.
tropical American annual weed with white flowers

Examples

In the abandoned lot, bastard feverfew quickly took over, covering the ground with white blossoms.
Farmers often struggle to remove bastard feverfew from their fields due to its rapid spread.
The botanist identified the unusual weed as bastard feverfew, noting its small, daisy-like flowers.
