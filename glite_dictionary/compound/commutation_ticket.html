commutation ticket

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən ˈtɪk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən ˈtɪk.ɪt / (BrE)
A ticket that allows multiple journeys or admissions, typically sold at a reduced rate for regular travel or season attendance.
multi-use ticket sold at reduced rate

Examples

He showed his commutation ticket to the conductor before taking his seat.
Many commuters prefer a commutation ticket to save money on daily travel costs.
Her commutation ticket expired at the end of the month, so she had to buy a new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: season ticket - a ticket allowing entry to all events in a season

Etymology

From 'commutation' meaning a substitute or exchange; historically used in railroad contexts for regular travelers.
