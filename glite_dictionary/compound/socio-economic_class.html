socio-economic class

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊsi.oʊ ɪˈkɑnəmɪk klæs / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsiəʊ ɪˈkɒnəmɪk klɑːs / (BrE)
A group of people sharing a similar social, economic, or educational status.
group by economic and social status

Examples

She grew up in a lower socio-economic class and faced many challenges as a result.
Education often plays a significant role in determining one's socio-economic class.
Researchers analyzed how health outcomes vary by socio-economic class across different regions.

Synset Examples

the working class
an emerging professional class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social class - Group of people of similar social status

Etymology

Formed from 'socio-' (from Latin 'socius', companion) and 'economic' (from Greek 'oikonomikos').
