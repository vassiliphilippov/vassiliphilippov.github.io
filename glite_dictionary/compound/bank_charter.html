bank charter

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˈtʃɑrtər / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
an official document granting permission for a bank to operate as a financial institution
official authorization to operate a bank
legal

Examples

The new financial institution received its bank charter after months of regulatory review.
Without a valid bank charter, the company could not legally accept deposits from the public.
Congress debated changes to the rules governing the issuance of a bank charter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charter - an official document granting rights or privileges
