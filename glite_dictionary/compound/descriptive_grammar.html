descriptive grammar

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɪptɪv ˈɡræmər / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv ˈɡramə / (BrE)
a grammar based on the objective analysis and observation of how a language is actually used, rather than on prescribed rules
grammar describing actual language usage

Examples

A descriptive grammar records how native speakers actually use their language, not how they ought to use it.
The linguist wrote a descriptive grammar of the local dialect after years of fieldwork.
Students compared descriptive grammar to prescriptive grammar in their introduction to linguistics course.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prescriptive grammar - grammar based on rules of correct usage
