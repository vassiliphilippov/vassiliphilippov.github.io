liquid oxygen

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd ˈɑksɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈɒksɪdʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
Oxygen in a liquid state, appearing as a pale blue, highly cold, magnetic liquid, commonly used as an oxidizer in rocket propellants and various industrial processes.
oxygen in liquid form, used in science/industry

Examples

During the launch, the rocket was fueled with liquid oxygen and kerosene.
Handling liquid oxygen requires special equipment because of its extremely low temperature.
Hospitals often use liquid oxygen systems to provide a reliable supply for medical needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquid nitrogen - nitrogen in liquid state, used in cryogenics
