urogenital cleft

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuroʊˈdʒɛnətl ˈklɛft / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˈklɛft / (BrE)
the anatomical fissure or groove between the labia majora in female mammals
the fissure between the labia majora
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the urogenital cleft for any signs of inflammation.
During embryonic development, the urogenital cleft forms as the body undergoes differentiation.
The anatomy textbook includes a diagram labeling the urogenital cleft in detail.

Etymology

From 'urogenital' (relating to the urinary and genital organs) + 'cleft' (split or opening).
