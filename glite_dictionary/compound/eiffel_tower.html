Eiffel Tower

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪfəl ˈtaʊər / (AmE), / ˈaɪfəl ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
a famous wrought iron tower in Paris, France, built in 1889 and long known as the world's tallest man-made structure
iconic iron tower in Paris, France

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit the Eiffel Tower every year to enjoy its breathtaking views of Paris.
The Eiffel Tower was originally intended as a temporary structure for the 1889 World's Fair.
At night, the Eiffel Tower sparkles with lights, making the Paris skyline even more magical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Statue of Liberty - iconic statue in New York City, USA

Etymology

Named after engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower.
