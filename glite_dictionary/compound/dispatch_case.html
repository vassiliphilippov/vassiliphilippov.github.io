dispatch case

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspætʃ keɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ keɪs / (BrE)
an oblong, often lockable container used for carrying official dispatches or other valuable documents
lockable case for dispatches/documents

Examples

The diplomat placed the confidential papers securely in his dispatch case before leaving for the meeting.
Antique collectors sometimes search for a classic leather dispatch case from the nineteenth century.
She locked the important legal documents in a sturdy dispatch case for transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefcase - a flat, rectangular case for carrying documents

Etymology

From 'dispatch' (to send off with speed) and 'case' (container).
