cubic inch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪk ɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪk ɪntʃ / (BrE)
a unit of volume equal to that of a cube with sides of one inch, commonly used in the United States
unit of volume (1 inch × 1 inch × 1 inch)

Examples

The engine has a capacity of 350 cubic inches.
One cubic inch is approximately 16.4 cubic centimeters.
The liquid barely filled a single cubic inch of the container.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubic centimeter - a metric unit of volume equal to a cube with 1 cm sides
  • Degree Higher: liter - metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
  • Degree Lower: milliliter - metric unit of volume equal to one-thousandth of a liter
