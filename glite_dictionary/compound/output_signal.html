output signal

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.pʊt ˈsɪɡ.nəl / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.pʊt ˈsɪɡ.nəl / (BrE)
a signal produced and sent out by an electronic or other system as a result of processing input
signal produced by a system

Examples

The amplifier increases the strength of the output signal for better sound quality.
Noise in the output signal can affect the accuracy of electronic measurements.
Engineers closely monitor the output signal to ensure the system is functioning correctly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: input signal - signal entering an electronic system
