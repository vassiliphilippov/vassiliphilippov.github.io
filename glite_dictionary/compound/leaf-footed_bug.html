leaf-footed bug

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːf ˌfʊtɪd bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈliːf ˌfʊtɪd bʌɡ / (BrE)
a large sap-sucking insect, especially of the family Coreidae, with flattened, leaflike expansions on its hind legs
large sap-sucking insect with leaflike legs

Examples

The leaf-footed bug is easily identified by the broad, leaflike structures on its hind legs.
Gardeners often spot the leaf-footed bug feeding on tomatoes and other vegetables.
A leaf-footed bug can sometimes be mistaken for a beetle due to its size and shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coreid bug - sap-sucking bug of the family Coreidae
