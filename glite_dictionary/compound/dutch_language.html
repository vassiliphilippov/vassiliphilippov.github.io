Dutch language

1. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the West Germanic language spoken in the Netherlands, and also in parts of Belgium and Suriname
language spoken in the Netherlands

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Dutch language, which helps in her job.
The Dutch language shares similarities with German and English.
Many books have been translated from the Dutch language into other European languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Flemish - the variety of Dutch spoken in northern Belgium
  • Degree Higher: German language - the West Germanic language of Germany

Etymology

Dutch from Middle Dutch 'duutsch' (of the people), related to 'Deutsch' (German).
