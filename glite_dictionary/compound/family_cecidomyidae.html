family Cecidomyidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli sɛˌsɪdoʊˈmɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli siːˌsɪdəʊˈmɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small flies, known as gall midges, whose larvae typically cause galls on plants.
a family of small flies (gall midges)

Examples

Several species in the family Cecidomyidae are known for causing plant galls in agricultural crops.
The family Cecidomyidae includes many pests that affect ornamental plants and trees.
Entomologists are studying the family Cecidomyidae to understand their role in ecosystem dynamics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall midge - A small fly whose larvae form plant galls

Etymology

From New Latin Cecidomyidae, combining 'Cecidomyia' (the type genus) and '-idae', a zoological family suffix.
