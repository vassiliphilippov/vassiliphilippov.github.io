acidulous flavor

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪdʒuˌləs ˈfleɪvər / (AmE), / əˈsɪdjʊləs ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
A sharp or slightly sour taste.
slightly sour taste

Examples

The acidulous flavor of the vinaigrette added freshness to the salad.
Some apples have an acidulous flavor that makes them ideal for pies.
Chefs often balance an acidulous flavor with a hint of sweetness.

Synset Examples

The acidulous flavor of the dish was unexpected but interesting.
The acidulous taste of the fruit surprised everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour - having an acidic or tart taste
  • Antonym: sweet - having a sugary or pleasant taste

Etymology

From Latin 'acidulus' (slightly sour), diminutive of 'acidus' (sour).
