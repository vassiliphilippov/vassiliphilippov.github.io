Dolomite Alps

1. PROPN

/ ˈdoʊləˌmaɪt ˈælpz / (AmE), / ˈdɒləˌmaɪt ˈælpz / (BrE)
a mountain range in northeastern Italy, forming part of the Alps, notable for its striking dolomitic limestone formations
eastern range of the Alps in Italy

Examples

Many hikers dream of exploring the dramatic peaks of the Dolomite Alps.
The unique rock formations in the Dolomite Alps attract geologists from around the world.
We enjoyed breathtaking views while traveling through the Dolomite Alps during our Italian vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alps - a major mountain system in south-central Europe

Etymology

Named after the mineral dolomite, itself named for French geologist Déodat de Dolomieu.
