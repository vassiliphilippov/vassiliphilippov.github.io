record player

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˌpleɪər / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌpleɪə / (BrE)
A device for playing sound recordings by rotating records and reproducing sound through a stylus and amplifier.
device that plays vinyl records

Examples

She bought a vintage record player to enjoy her father's old vinyl collection.
The sound quality from a record player can be surprisingly rich and warm.
He carefully placed the vinyl on the record player and dropped the needle to start the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turntable - the rotating platform of a record player on which a record sits
