racehorse

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪs.hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈreɪs.hɔːs / (BrE)
A horse that is specifically bred and trained to compete in horse races.
horse bred for racing

Examples

The retired racehorse was adopted by a loving family.
She owns several champion racehorses that have won prestigious races.
Training a young racehorse requires patience and expertise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoroughbred - a breed of horse commonly used in racing
  • Antonym: workhorse - a horse used for labor rather than racing

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈreɪsˌhɔːs / (BrE)
A horse that has been bred and trained to take part in horse races.
horse bred and trained for racing

Examples

The racehorse thundered down the track towards the finish line.
She has always dreamed of owning a champion racehorse.
After retiring from competition, the racehorse was kept as a breeding stallion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoroughbred - a horse of a breed developed for racing, especially one with English ancestry
