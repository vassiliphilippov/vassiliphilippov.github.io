chronological sequence

1. NOUN

/ krəˌnɑːlədʒɪkəl ˈsiːkwəns / (AmE), / krəˌnɒlədʒɪkəl ˈsiːkwəns / (BrE)
a series of events or items arranged in the order in which they occurred in time
events/items in time order

Examples

The historical documents were arranged in a chronological sequence for easier analysis.
She described the accident's events in a clear chronological sequence.
To understand the novel's plot, it's helpful to reconstruct the chronological sequence of events.

Synset Examples

the doctor saw a sequence of patients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a particular order in which related things follow each other

Etymology

From Greek 'khronos' (time) + 'logos' (word, reason), plus Latin 'sequentia' (sequence, a following).
