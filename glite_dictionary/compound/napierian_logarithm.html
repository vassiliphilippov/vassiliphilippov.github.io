Napierian logarithm

1. NOUN

/ nəˈpɪriən ˈlɑːɡəˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / næˈpɪəriən ˈlɒɡərɪðəm / (BrE)
a logarithm to the base e, also known as the natural logarithm
logarithm to the base e

Examples

The Napierian logarithm of a number is used frequently in advanced calculus.
When solving exponential equations, the Napierian logarithm can simplify the calculation.
She explained how to convert a common logarithm into a Napierian logarithm using a mathematical formula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural logarithm - logarithm to the base e

Etymology

Named after John Napier, Scottish mathematician who developed the concept of logarithms.
