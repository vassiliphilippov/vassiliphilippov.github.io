golden yarrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈjæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈjærəʊ / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to western North America, with greyish woolly leaves, branched stems, and golden-yellow flower heads.
North American plant with yellow flower heads

Examples

We spotted a cluster of golden yarrow growing along the hiking trail.
The bright yellow blooms of golden yarrow attract many pollinators in the summer.
Gardeners often plant golden yarrow for its drought resistance and striking flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yarrow - a common Eurasian plant with feathery leaves and clusters of small, usually white flowers
