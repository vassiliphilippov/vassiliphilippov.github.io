high court

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈkɔrt / (AmE), / haɪ ˈkɔːt / (BrE)
the highest court in a country or state, especially in the United States or Commonwealth countries, having the authority to hear the most important cases and appeals.
the highest court in a state or country
legal

Examples

The case was ultimately decided by the high court after several appeals.
She dreams of arguing a case before the state’s high court.
A ruling from the high court set a precedent for all future cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supreme court - the highest federal court in the United States
