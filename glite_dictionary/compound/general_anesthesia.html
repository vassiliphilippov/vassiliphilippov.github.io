general anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚl ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˌænɪsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
A medically induced state of complete unconsciousness caused by anesthetic drugs, typically used for major surgery.
medically induced unconsciousness for surgery
medical

Examples

The patient was placed under general anesthesia before the operation began.
Complications from general anesthesia are rare but can occur during surgery.
Some procedures require only local anesthesia, while others need general anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local anesthesia - loss of sensation in a limited area of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'aisthēsis' (sensation), through Latin and French roots; 'general' in the sense of 'affecting the whole body'.
