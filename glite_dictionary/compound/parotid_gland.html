parotid gland

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑt̬ɪd ɡlænd / (AmE), / pəˈrɒtɪd ɡlænd / (BrE)
the largest salivary gland, located in front of and below each ear, which produces a significant portion of saliva, especially during eating
large salivary gland near the ear
medical

Examples

The parotid gland can sometimes become swollen and painful if infected.
A tumor in the parotid gland may cause facial nerve damage.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid injury to the parotid gland.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: salivary gland - any of the glands in the mouth region that secrete saliva

Etymology

From Greek 'parotis' (gland near the ear): 'para' (beside) + 'ous, otos' (ear).
