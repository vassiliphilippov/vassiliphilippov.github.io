bluestem wheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌstɛm ˈwiːtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbluːstɛm ˈwiːtɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a valuable forage grass native to the western United States, used for grazing and soil conservation
a forage grass of the western U.S.

Examples

Cattle graze on bluestem wheatgrass throughout the summer months.
The rangeland restoration project planted thousands of bluestem wheatgrass seedlings.
Bluestem wheatgrass is well adapted to dry, windy climates in the American West.
