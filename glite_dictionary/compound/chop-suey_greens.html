chop-suey greens

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɑpˈsuːi ɡrinz / (AmE), / ˌtʃɒpˈsuːi ɡriːnz / (BrE)
a leafy vegetable with succulent edible leaves, used especially in Asian cooking
edible leafy vegetable used in Asian cuisine

Examples

She added a handful of chop-suey greens to the stir-fry for extra flavor.
The farmer's market had fresh chop-suey greens perfect for Asian recipes.
You can steam chop-suey greens and serve them as a healthy side dish.

Etymology

From 'chop suey', a Chinese-American dish, referring to the vegetable's use in such dishes.

edible leaves used in Asian cuisine

Examples

Stir-frying chop-suey greens brings out their fresh, slightly peppery flavor.
Many Asian markets sell bundles of chop-suey greens for home cooks.
The chef added a handful of chop-suey greens to the noodle soup for extra aroma.

Etymology

From 'chop suey', a Chinese-American dish; refers to the greens commonly used in such dishes.
