genus Serrasalmus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sɛˈræsælməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɛˈræsælməs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Characidae, commonly known as piranhas, native to South American rivers.
genus of South American piranhas

Examples

The genus Serrasalmus includes some of the most well-known species of piranhas.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the genus Serrasalmus in the Amazon basin.
Members of the genus Serrasalmus are noted for their sharp teeth and aggressive feeding behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piranha - A predatory freshwater fish of South America

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind, type) + Serrasalmus, the type genus name from Latin 'serra' (saw) and 'salmo' (salmon).
