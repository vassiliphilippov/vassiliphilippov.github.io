water horehound

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˈhɔrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈhɔːhaʊnd / (BrE)
an aromatic perennial herb native to the United States, typically found near water and belonging to the mint family
aromatic perennial herb of the U.S.

Examples

Water horehound often grows along the edges of streams and wetlands in the eastern United States.
Native Americans traditionally used water horehound for its medicinal properties.
The leaves of water horehound have a distinct, minty aroma when crushed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horehound - a common name for several plants in the mint family with medicinal uses
