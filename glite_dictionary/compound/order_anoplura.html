order Anoplura

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌænəˈplʊrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌanəˈplʊərə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of insects comprising the sucking lice, which are external parasites of mammals
order of sucking lice (insects)

Examples

The order Anoplura includes many species that parasitize mammals.
Veterinarians study the order Anoplura to understand lice infestations in animals.
Researchers discovered a new species within the order Anoplura last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucking louse - a parasitic insect that feeds on the blood of mammals
  • Antonym: order Mallophaga - order of biting lice, another group of parasitic insects

Etymology

From Greek 'anō' (upwards, above) + 'plura' (sides, ribs), referring to aspects of their anatomy; used in scientific taxonomy.
