black sage

1. NOUN

/ blæk seɪdʒ / (AmE), / blæk seɪdʒ / (BrE)
an aromatic shrub of southern California and Mexico, with woolly leaves and used in traditional remedies; Salvia mellifera.
aromatic Californian shrub (Salvia mellifera)

Examples

The hills were covered with blooming black sage after the winter rains.
Local herbalists often gather black sage for its medicinal properties.
Hikers enjoy the strong scent of black sage along the southern California trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - a plant of the genus Salvia, often used as a herb
