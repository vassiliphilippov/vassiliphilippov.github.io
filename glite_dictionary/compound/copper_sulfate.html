copper sulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpər ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒpə ˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a blue crystalline salt, used as a fungicide, algaecide, and in chemistry, obtained by treating copper oxide with sulfuric acid
blue crystalline chemical compound

Examples

The laboratory ordered a new supply of copper sulfate for its experiments.
Many gardeners use copper sulfate to control fungal diseases on their plants.
When dissolved in water, copper sulfate forms a bright blue solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue vitriol - another name for copper sulfate
