slow march

1. NOUN

/ sloʊ mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / sləʊ mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a walk or procession carried out at a deliberately slow pace, often for ceremonial or formal purposes
procession or walk at a slow pace

Examples

The military band began a slow march across the parade ground.
During the ceremony, the graduates performed a slow march to the stage.
The mourners proceeded in a slow march behind the hearse.

Synset Examples

The protestors began a slow march down Main Street.
They took a slow march to honor the veterans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - an organized group moving forward, especially as part of a ceremony
  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, often with a celebratory or ceremonial purpose
