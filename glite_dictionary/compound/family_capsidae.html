family Capsidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈkæpsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˈkæpsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as leaf bugs, belonging to the order Hemiptera, characterized by their plant-feeding habits and flattened bodies.
a family of plant-feeding leaf bugs

Examples

The family Capsidae includes many small insects commonly known as leaf bugs.
Members of the family Capsidae are frequently found feeding on a variety of plants.
Entomologists study the unique characteristics of the family Capsidae to understand plant-insect interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf bug - common name for insects of the family Capsidae
  • Degree Higher: Hemiptera - the insect order to which Capsidae belongs

Etymology

From modern Latin Capsidae, based on the genus Capsus, and family suffix -idae used in zoological taxonomy.
