leaf-cutting bee

1. NOUN

/ ˈlif ˌkʌtɪŋ bi / (AmE), / ˈliːf ˌkʌtɪŋ biː / (BrE)
a type of bee that cuts rounded pieces from leaves or flowers to build or line its nest
bee that cuts leaves to line its nest

Examples

A leaf-cutting bee was busy collecting leaf pieces for its nest in the garden.
Unlike honey bees, the leaf-cutting bee prefers to use leaves for nest building.
We watched as the leaf-cutting bee carefully cut a circle out of a rose leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee - insect of the family Apidae, known for producing honey and pollinating plants
