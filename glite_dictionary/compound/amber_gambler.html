amber gambler

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.bɚ ˈɡæm.blɚ / (AmE), / ˈæm.bə ˈɡæm.blə / (BrE)
a person who takes dangerous or reckless risks, especially by making risky bets or decisions
person who takes reckless risks
informal british

Examples

He has always been an amber gambler, betting big when the odds are uncertain.
Many drivers become amber gamblers when approaching traffic lights about to turn red.
Her reputation as an amber gambler made others nervous about partnering with her on projects.

Synset Examples

The amber gambler placed his chips with a confident grin.
He was known as the amber gambler, always taking risky bets.
She warned him that being an amber gambler could lead to trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk-taker - a person who takes risks
  • Antonym: cautious person - someone who avoids risks or danger

Etymology

From 'amber' (referring to the amber/yellow light in traffic signals indicating caution) and 'gambler' (person who takes risks).
