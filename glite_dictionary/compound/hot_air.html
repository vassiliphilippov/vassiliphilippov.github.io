hot air

1. NOUN

/ hɑt ɛr / (AmE), / hɒt eə / (BrE)
air that has been heated and tends to rise, especially as used in scientific or everyday contexts.
heated air that rises

Examples

The balloon was lifted by the hot air inside its envelope.
In summer, hot air rising from the pavement can create shimmering effects.
The heater pumped hot air throughout the entire house.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: warm air - air slightly heated above room temperature

2. NOUN

/ hɑt ɛr / (AmE), / hɒt ɛə / (BrE)
empty or insincere talk that has little or no value or substance
empty or insincere talk

Examples

The candidate's promises during the debate turned out to be just hot air.
Everyone saw through his excuses and recognized them as hot air.
Instead of offering solutions, she filled the conversation with hot air.

Synset Examples

He filled the meeting with hot air and no substance.
She dismissed his bombast as mere hot air.
Don't waste my time with hot air excuses.
The politician's speech was just hot air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - language or ideas that are foolish or untrue
  • Antonym: sense - talk or ideas that are reasonable and meaningful
