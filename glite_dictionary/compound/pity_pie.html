pity pie

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪti paɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɪti paɪ / (BrE)
an instance or display of self-indulgent sadness or victimhood, often used humorously
self-indulgent sadness (humorous)
informal

Examples

After the breakup, he sat alone eating a large slice of pity pie.
She refused to let herself have any pity pie after making a mistake at work.
Sometimes it's healthy to acknowledge your feelings, but don't make a habit of serving pity pie.

Synset Examples

He baked a pity pie for himself after losing his job.
She served herself a slice of pity pie last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-pity - excessive or self-indulgent pity for oneself
