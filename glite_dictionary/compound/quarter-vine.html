quarter-vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtər vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə vaɪn / (BrE)
A woody flowering vine native to the southern United States, whose stems show a cross-shaped pattern in transverse section.
woody vine with cross-patterned stem

Examples

The quarter-vine winds its way along the fence, displaying its bright flowers in late spring.
When cut, the stem of a quarter-vine reveals a distinctive cross-shaped pattern.
Gardeners in the South often choose the quarter-vine for its hardy nature and decorative stems.
