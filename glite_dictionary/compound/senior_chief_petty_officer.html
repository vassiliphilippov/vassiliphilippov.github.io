senior chief petty officer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.njɚ tʃiːf ˈpɛt̬.i ˈɑː.fɪ.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiː.njə tʃiːf ˈpet.i ˈɒf.ɪ.sər / (BrE)
a senior noncommissioned officer in the United States Navy or Coast Guard, ranking above chief petty officer and below master chief petty officer
high-ranking noncommissioned officer (Navy/Coast Guard)

Examples

After twenty years of service, he was promoted to senior chief petty officer in the Navy.
The responsibilities of a senior chief petty officer include supervising enlisted personnel and managing training programs.
Maria was proud to see her father retire as a senior chief petty officer after decades of dedication.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chief petty officer - a noncommissioned officer rank one step below senior chief petty officer
  • Degree Higher: master chief petty officer - the highest noncommissioned officer rank in the Navy or Coast Guard
  • Synonym: master sergeant - senior noncommissioned officer rank in the Army, comparable to senior chief petty officer
