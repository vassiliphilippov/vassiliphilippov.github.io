Canadian Maritime Provinces

1. PROPN

/ kəˈneɪdiən ˈmɛrɪˌtaɪm ˈprɑvɪnsɪz / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən ˈmɑːrɪtaɪm ˈprɒvɪnsɪz / (BrE)
the collective name for the Canadian provinces of New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, and Prince Edward Island, located on the Atlantic coast
eastern Canadian coastal provinces (NB, NS, PEI)

Examples

Many tourists visit the Canadian Maritime Provinces to experience their unique coastal culture and scenery.
The Canadian Maritime Provinces are known for their rich seafood traditions and maritime history.
The weather in the Canadian Maritime Provinces tends to be milder than in other parts of Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic Provinces - the Canadian provinces of Newfoundland and Labrador, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, and Prince Edward Island
