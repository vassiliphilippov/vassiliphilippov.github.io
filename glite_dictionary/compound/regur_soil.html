regur soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪɡər sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈreɪɡə sɔɪl / (BrE)
a rich black soil found in India, especially suitable for growing cotton and other crops
rich black Indian soil

Examples

Farmers rely on regur soil for cultivating cotton in central India.
The unique minerals in regur soil make it highly fertile for many crops.
Much of the Deccan plateau is covered with regur soil, also known as black cotton soil.

Etymology

From Marathi 'regur', referring to the black soil of the Deccan region.
