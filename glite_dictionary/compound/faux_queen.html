faux queen

1. NOUN

/ foʊ ˈkwiːn / (AmE), / fəʊ ˈkwiːn / (BrE)
A woman who pretends to be or is regarded as a queen without having a legitimate title or royal status.
woman falsely claiming to be a queen

Examples

She was crowned the faux queen at the costume gala, wearing a dazzling tiara.
Many saw her as a faux queen, more interested in pageantry than leadership.
The tabloids called her a faux queen after she claimed royal descent.

Synset Examples

She was dubbed the faux queen for her pretentious behavior.
Everyone knew her title of faux queen was just for show.
The fashion show featured a faux queen in an extravagant gown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretender - a person who claims or aspires to a title or throne they are not entitled to

Etymology

Faux from French 'faux' (false) + English 'queen'.
