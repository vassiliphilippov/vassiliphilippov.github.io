Ligurian Sea

1. PROPN

/ lɪˈɡjʊriən si / (AmE), / lɪˈɡjʊəriən siː / (BrE)
an arm of the Mediterranean Sea located between the northwest coast of Italy and the island of Corsica
sea between northwest Italy and Corsica

Examples

The Ligurian Sea is known for its stunning blue waters and picturesque coastal towns.
Many cruise ships travel through the Ligurian Sea on their way to ports in Italy and France.
Marine biologists often study dolphins and whales that inhabit the Ligurian Sea.

Etymology

Named after Liguria, a region in northwestern Italy bordering the sea.
