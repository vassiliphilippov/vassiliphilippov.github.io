population control

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the practice of managing or limiting the growth of a population, often through government policies or programs
limitation of population growth

Examples

The government implemented population control measures to address rapid urban growth.
Debates about population control often involve ethical, social, and economic issues.
Some countries have adopted strict population control policies in response to resource shortages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family planning - the practice of controlling the number and spacing of one's children, typically through the use of contraception
