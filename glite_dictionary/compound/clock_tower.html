clock tower

1. NOUN

/ klɑk ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / klɒk ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
a tall structure, usually part of a building, that has a large clock visible on an outside face, typically high above the ground
tower with a large exterior clock

Examples

The old town's clock tower chimes every hour, drawing crowds of tourists.
You can see the clock tower from miles away because it's the tallest building in the city.
The clock tower was restored last year to preserve its historic charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell tower - a tower that contains one or more bells
