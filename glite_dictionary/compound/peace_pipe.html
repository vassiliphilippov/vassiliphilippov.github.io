peace pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːs ˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈpiːs ˌpʌɪp / (BrE)
a highly decorated ceremonial pipe traditionally smoked by some Indigenous peoples of North America on special occasions, often as a token of peace or agreement
Indigenous ceremonial pipe symbolizing peace

Examples

The chiefs gathered and passed the peace pipe to signify the end of hostilities.
During the ceremony, each participant took a turn smoking the peace pipe.
The peace pipe holds great significance in many Native American cultures.
