sugar of lead

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ əv lɛd / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə əv lɛd / (BrE)
a poisonous white crystalline compound (lead acetate) formerly used in dyeing fabrics and in the manufacture of enamels and varnishes
poisonous compound; lead acetate

Examples

Historically, sugar of lead was used in cosmetics despite its toxicity.
The old recipe called for sugar of lead to improve the glaze on pottery.
Symptoms of poisoning developed after exposure to sugar of lead in the dye works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead acetate - another name for sugar of lead, a sweet-tasting, poisonous lead compound

Etymology

Named for its sweet taste and composition as a lead salt; 'sugar' refers to its appearance, 'lead' to its metal content.
