toadeater way

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdˌiːtər weɪ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdiːtə weɪ / (BrE)
A manner of behaving that is sycophantic or excessively obedient, especially to gain favor from someone in power.
sycophantic or obsequious behavior
archaic

Examples

Her toadeater way was obvious to everyone at the office, but it did not earn her any respect.
His toadeater ways irritated those who valued honesty over flattery.
The manager disliked employees with toadeater ways, preferring straightforward communication.

Synset Examples

Her toadeater ways don't impress anyone here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophancy - Obsequious flattery toward someone important
  • Synonym: obsequiousness - Excessive eagerness to please or obey

Etymology

From 'toadeater', an archaic term for a sycophant, referring to performers who claimed to eat toads.
