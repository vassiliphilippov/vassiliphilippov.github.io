tin pest

1. NOUN

/ tɪn pɛst / (AmE), / tɪn pɛst / (BrE)
a physical transformation in which white tin converts into a brittle, powdery grey form at very low temperatures
white tin transforming to grey tin at low temperatures

Examples

At subzero temperatures, tin pest can cause electronic components to fail due to the crumbling of tin solder.
Scientists studied the effects of tin pest on antique organ pipes exposed to severe cold.
The phenomenon of tin pest was first observed in Europe during particularly harsh winters.
