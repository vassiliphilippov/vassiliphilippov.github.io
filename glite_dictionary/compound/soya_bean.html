soya bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪ.ə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪ.ə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A bean from the soybean plant, rich in protein, used as food and animal feed.
protein-rich bean used in food and feed

Examples

Many vegetarians rely on soya bean products as a primary source of protein.
Tofu is made from soya bean curd and is a popular meat substitute.
Farmers often rotate corn and soya bean crops to maintain soil fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - Another spelling of 'soya bean'; North American usage

Etymology

From Japanese *shōyu* (soy sauce) via Dutch *soja*, and English 'bean'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪ.ə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪ.ə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an erect bushy annual plant native to Asia, cultivated for its nutritious oil-rich seeds, which are used for food, animal feed, and soil improvement
plant cultivated for oil-rich seeds; Glycine max

Examples

The farmer rotated his crops and planted soya bean to enrich the soil.
Products like tofu and soy milk are made from soya bean.
The demand for soya bean has increased due to its health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - alternative spelling of soya bean

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu' (soy) and English 'bean'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪ.ə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪ.ə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A legume native to East Asia, cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used as a source of oil, animal feed, and food products.
edible bean used for oil and food

Examples

Many vegetarian foods are made with soya beans as the main ingredient.
The farmer planted soya bean crops to improve the soil quality.
Vegetable oil derived from soya beans is commonly used in cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - alternative spelling of soya bean

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu' (soy sauce) and English 'bean'.
