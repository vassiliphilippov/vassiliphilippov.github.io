alphabetic writing

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfəˈbɛtɪk ˈraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌælfəˈbɛtɪk ˈraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a system of writing that uses an alphabet, where symbols represent individual sounds or letters
writing system using an alphabet

Examples

The Greek script is an early example of alphabetic writing.
The shift from pictographs to alphabetic writing greatly simplified literacy for many cultures.
Modern English uses alphabetic writing rather than logograms or pictographs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabet - a set of letters used in a language's writing system
