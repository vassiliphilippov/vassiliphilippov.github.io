Tutelo language

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈtɛloʊ ˈlæŋgwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tjuːˈtɛləʊ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The Siouan language formerly spoken by the Tutelo people, indigenous to the eastern United States.
extinct Siouan language of the Tutelo people

Examples

The Tutelo language is now considered extinct, with no living native speakers remaining.
Researchers have studied the Tutelo language to reconstruct aspects of Siouan linguistic history.
A few hymns and vocabulary lists survive as records of the Tutelo language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan languages - a family of Native American languages including Tutelo

Etymology

From Tutelo, the name of the indigenous people who spoke the language, of uncertain origin.
