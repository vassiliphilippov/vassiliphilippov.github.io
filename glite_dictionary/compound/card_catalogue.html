card catalogue

1. NOUN

/ kɑrd ˈkætəlɔg / (AmE), / kɑːd ˈkatəlɒg / (BrE)
a library catalogue in which each publication is listed on a separate card, typically arranged systematically in drawers or boxes
library catalogue system using cards

Examples

The library still maintains a card catalogue for older collections not found online.
She searched the card catalogue to locate books on ancient history.
With the advent of computers, many libraries replaced the card catalogue with a digital system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library catalog - a list of all the books and materials in a library

2. NOUN

/ kɑrd ˈkætəlˌɔg / (AmE), / kɑːd ˈkatəlɒg / (BrE)
an alphabetical listing of items, especially books in a library, with each item recorded on a separate card, usually stored in drawers
library index on individual cards

Examples

Before computers, every library used a card catalogue to organize and locate their books.
She spent hours flipping through the card catalogue searching for information about medieval history.
The old wooden card catalogue still stands in the corner as a reminder of past library practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: card index - a system of recording information on index cards, often used as a synonym for card catalogue
  • Antonym: online catalogue - a computerized listing of library holdings, often replacing the card catalogue
