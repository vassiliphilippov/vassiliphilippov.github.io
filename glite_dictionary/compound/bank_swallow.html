bank swallow

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˈswɑloʊ / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
A small migratory swallow (Riparia riparia) of the Northern Hemisphere, which nests in tunnels dug in clay or sand banks.
small migratory bird nesting in banks

Examples

During the spring, large colonies of bank swallows can be seen nesting along the riverbank.
We spotted a bank swallow darting swiftly over the lake in search of insects.
The bank swallow is known for digging horizontal tunnels into sandy cliffs for its nests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand martin - another common name for bank swallow, especially in Europe
