Tappan Zee Bridge

1. PROPN

/ ˌtæpən ˈzi ˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌtæpən ˈziː ˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a cantilever bridge that spanned the Hudson River in New York State, connecting Westchester and Rockland counties
cantilever bridge over the Hudson River in New York

Examples

We drove across the Tappan Zee Bridge to get to Rockland County.
The old Tappan Zee Bridge was replaced by the Governor Mario M. Cuomo Bridge in 2017.
Many commuters relied on the Tappan Zee Bridge as part of their daily route.

Etymology

Named after the Tappan Native American tribe and the Dutch word "zee" (sea).
