Jewish Orthodoxy

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒu.ɪʃ ɔrˈθɑd.ək.si / (AmE), / ˌdʒuː.ɪʃ ɔːˈθɒd.ək.si / (BrE)
Strict adherence to traditional Jewish law and practice as interpreted in the Talmud, typically referring to Orthodox Judaism.
strict observance of traditional Jewish law

Examples

Many families who practice Jewish Orthodoxy strictly observe the Sabbath and dietary laws.
He was raised in Jewish Orthodoxy but later chose a more liberal approach to his faith.
Debates within Jewish Orthodoxy often focus on interpreting religious texts and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orthodox Judaism - branch of Judaism characterized by strict observance of traditional law
  • Antonym: Reform Judaism - branch of Judaism with a liberal interpretation of Jewish law
  • Degree Lower: Conservative Judaism - branch of Judaism between Orthodox and Reform in observance
