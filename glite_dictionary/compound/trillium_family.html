trillium family

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪliəm ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈtrɪliəm ˌfæməli / (BrE)
a small family of perennial herbs having flowers with three petals and three sepals, sometimes classified as a subfamily of the Liliaceae
family of herbs with three-petaled flowers

Examples

The trillium family is known for its unique three-petaled blossoms that bloom in the spring.
Recent studies have debated whether the trillium family should be placed within the Liliaceae.
Many woodland wildflowers, such as those in the trillium family, are sensitive to habitat changes.
