sweet fern

1. NOUN

/ swit fɝn / (AmE), / swiːt fɜːn / (BrE)
A low, shrubby plant with aromatic, deeply divided leaves resembling those of a fern, commonly found in North America and other regions; not a true fern.
aromatic shrub with fern-like leaves

Examples

The hillsides were covered in sweet fern and wildflowers in early summer.
She crushed a sprig of sweet fern to breathe in its spicy scent.
Gardeners appreciate sweet fern for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.

2. NOUN

/ swit fɝn / (AmE), / swiːt fɜːn / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of eastern North America with sweet-scented, fern-like leaves and small white flowers.
North American shrub with fern-like scented leaves

Examples

The woods were filled with the pleasant aroma of sweet fern after the rain.
They collected branches of sweet fern to use as a natural air freshener.
On our hike, we spotted clusters of sweet fern among the undergrowth.
