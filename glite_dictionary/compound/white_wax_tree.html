white wax tree

1. NOUN

/ waɪt wæks tri / (AmE), / waɪt wæks triː / (BrE)
an erect evergreen shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and Japan, with pointed leaves and long upright clusters of flowers, resembling the Japanese privet
evergreen shrub/tree native to East Asia

Examples

The white wax tree is often grown for ornamental purposes in gardens.
Farmers in some regions cultivate the white wax tree for the wax-producing insects it attracts.
The leaves of the white wax tree are glossy and acuminate, adding to its decorative appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ligustrum - a genus of shrubs and trees commonly known as privet
