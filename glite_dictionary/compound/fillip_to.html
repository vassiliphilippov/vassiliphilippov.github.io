fillip to

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪp tu / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp tʊ / (BrE)
something that acts as a stimulus or boost to an activity or process.
stimulus; boost to something

Examples

The new marketing campaign gave a much-needed fillip to the struggling business.
Government investment provided a fillip to local infrastructure projects.
Winning the award was a real fillip to her confidence.

Synset Examples

The new marketing strategy gave a fillip to sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boost - an increase or improvement
  • Synonym: impetus - the force that makes something happen or happen more quickly
  • Antonym: dampener - something that restrains or reduces stimulation

Etymology

From 'fillip', meaning a stimulus or sudden impetus, originally meaning a flick with the finger, likely of imitative origin.
