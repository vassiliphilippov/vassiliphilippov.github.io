main character syndrome

1. NOUN

/ meɪn ˈkɛrɪktɚ ˈsɪndroʊm / (AmE), / meɪn ˈkærɪktə ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a self-centered attitude in which someone believes they are the central figure in most situations, often disregarding others
self-centered belief of being the central figure
informal

Examples

His main character syndrome made it hard for him to listen to anyone else’s advice.
Many teenagers are accused of having main character syndrome when they act as if the world revolves around them.
After some reflection, she realized that her main character syndrome was affecting her relationships.

Synset Examples

His main character syndrome makes him oblivious to others.
People often joke about his main character syndrome.
She realized her main character syndrome was isolating her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egocentrism - focus on oneself and inability to consider others' perspectives
  • Degree Higher: narcissism - excessive self-interest or self-importance

Etymology

From 'main character', the central figure in a story, and 'syndrome', meaning a group of associated characteristics.
