matter-of-course

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæt̬.ɚ əv ˈkɔrs / (AmE), / ˌmæt.ər əv ˈkɔːs / (BrE)
happening as an expected or natural outcome; not surprising or remarkable
expected or natural outcome

Examples

Her acceptance of the award seemed a matter-of-course reaction to her years of hard work.
The promotion was treated as a matter-of-course by everyone in the office.
He handled the crisis with a matter-of-course calm that impressed his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural - expected or not surprising
  • Antonym: unexpected - not expected or unusual

Etymology

From the phrase 'as a matter of course', where 'course' means regular or usual procedure.
