President Wilson

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈwɪlsən / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈwɪlsən / (BrE)
the 28th President of the United States (1856–1924), who led the country during World War I and was instrumental in establishing the League of Nations.
28th President of the United States

Examples

President Wilson addressed Congress about the need to enter World War I.
Many historians credit President Wilson with shaping modern American foreign policy.
The League of Nations was one of President Wilson's major achievements after the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Woodrow Wilson - 28th President of the United States, scholar and statesman
