Petri dish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːtri dɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛtri dɪʃ / (BrE)
A shallow, round, transparent dish with a loose-fitting lid, used for culturing microorganisms such as bacteria.
shallow laboratory dish for culturing microorganisms

Examples

The scientist placed the bacteria sample in a Petri dish to observe its growth.
Mold colonies formed overnight in the Petri dish on the lab bench.
She carefully labeled each Petri dish before starting the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culture dish - a dish used for growing microorganisms

Etymology

Named after German bacteriologist Julius Richard Petri (1852–1921), who invented it.
