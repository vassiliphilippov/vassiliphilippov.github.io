food cycle

1. NOUN

/ fuːd ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / fuːd ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
(ecology) a system in which energy and nutrients are transferred among organisms through a network of interconnected food chains.
network of interrelated food chains (ecology)

Examples

In a healthy ecosystem, the food cycle ensures that nutrients are recycled among plants, animals, and decomposers.
The disruption of just one species can have a significant impact on the entire food cycle.
Students studied the food cycle to understand how energy flows through a forest community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food chain - a sequence showing how each organism is eaten by the next in a community
