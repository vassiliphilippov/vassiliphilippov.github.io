snail mail

1. NOUN

/ sneɪl meɪl / (AmE), / sneɪl meɪl / (BrE)
Mail that is physically delivered by the postal service, as opposed to email or other electronic forms.
traditional postal mail

Examples

Many people still prefer snail mail for sending greeting cards.
The invitation arrived by snail mail a few days after the announcement.
Some organizations only accept applications through snail mail, not by email.

Synset Examples

email is much faster than snail mail

Related Terms

  • Antonym: email - messages sent electronically over the internet

Etymology

Formed by analogy with the slowness of a snail; 'snail' + 'mail', coined in the late 20th century to contrast with email.
