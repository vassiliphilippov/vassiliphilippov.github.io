ice skate

1. NOUN

/ aɪs skeɪt / (AmE), / aɪs skeɪt / (BrE)
a boot with a metal blade attached to the sole, used for skating on ice
boot with a blade for ice skating

Examples

She tied the laces on her ice skate and stepped onto the rink.
I need to get my ice skates sharpened before the competition.
He bought a new pair of ice skates for his daughter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roller skate - boot or shoe with small wheels, used for skating on hard surfaces
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03563584-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ aɪs skeɪt / (AmE), / aɪs skeɪt / (BrE)
to move or glide over ice using ice skates
move on ice wearing skates

Examples

She loves to ice skate at the local rink during winter.
He learned how to ice skate when he was five years old.
We decided to ice skate on the frozen lake over the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skate - to move or glide along a surface on skates

WordNet ID: oewn-01941081-v
Synset POS: v
