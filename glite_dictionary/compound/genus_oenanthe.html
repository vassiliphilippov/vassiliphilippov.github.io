genus Oenanthe

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒinəs oʊˈnænθi / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs əʊˈænθi / (BrE)
A genus of small, insectivorous passerine birds known as wheatears, found mainly in the Old World.
a bird genus including wheatears

Examples

Members of the genus Oenanthe are commonly known as wheatears.
The genus Oenanthe includes several species distributed throughout Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of birds in the genus Oenanthe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheatear - a small songbird of the genus Oenanthe

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek oinanthe, a kind of plant, adapted for the bird genus name.
