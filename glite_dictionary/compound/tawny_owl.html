tawny owl

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔni aʊl / (AmE), / ˈtɔːni aʊl / (BrE)
a medium-sized European owl (Strix aluco) with reddish-brown plumage and a rounded head with black eyes
a reddish-brown European owl

Examples

We saw a tawny owl perched silently on the old oak tree at dusk.
The haunting call of the tawny owl is often heard in European woodlands at night.
The tawny owl hunts mostly small mammals and birds after dark.

Etymology

From 'tawny' meaning 'brownish-yellow', and 'owl'.
