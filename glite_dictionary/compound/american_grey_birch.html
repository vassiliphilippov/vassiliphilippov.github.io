American grey birch

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ɡreɪ bɜrtʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ɡreɪ bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A medium-sized birch tree (Betula populifolia) native to eastern North America, characterized by white or pale grey bark and often found in second-growth forests.
North American birch tree with pale bark

Examples

The American grey birch is easily recognized by its distinctive pale bark.
In some regions, the American grey birch quickly colonizes land after a forest fire.
The woods behind our house are filled with American grey birch and young maples.
