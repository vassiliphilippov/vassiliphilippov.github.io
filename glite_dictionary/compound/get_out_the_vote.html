to get out the vote

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt aʊt ðə voʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt ðə vəʊt / (BrE)
to encourage or organize people to vote, especially in an election campaign
encourage people to vote in an election

Examples

The campaign launched a nationwide effort to get out the vote before election day.
Volunteers went door to door to get out the vote in local neighborhoods.
Social media can be an effective tool to get out the vote among younger voters.

Synset Examples

We need to mobilize everyone to get out the vote.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mobilize - to organize people for action, especially in political contexts
