Haverhill fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪvərˌhɪl ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈheɪvəhɪl ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a form of rat-bite fever found in the United States, characterized by fever, rash, and joint pain, caused by the bacterium Streptobacillus moniliformis
a type of rat-bite fever in the US
medical american

Examples

Several cases of Haverhill fever were reported among children who had contact with pet rats.
The doctor diagnosed him with Haverhill fever after he developed a persistent rash and joint pain.
Public health warnings were issued to prevent outbreaks of Haverhill fever in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat-bite fever - an infectious disease transmitted by rodent bites

Etymology

Named after Haverhill, Massachusetts, where an outbreak occurred in 1926.
