genus Foeniculum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs fɪˈnɪkjʊləm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fɪˈnɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
A small genus of aromatic European herbs with finely divided leaves and yellow flowers, including the fennel.
genus of aromatic European herbs

Examples

The genus Foeniculum contains plants widely used for culinary and medicinal purposes.
Members of the genus Foeniculum are easily recognized by their feathery leaves and aromatic quality.
Botanists study the genus Foeniculum to learn more about the diversity of edible herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic flowering plant species in the genus Foeniculum

Etymology

Foeniculum from Latin 'foeniculum', meaning fennel.
