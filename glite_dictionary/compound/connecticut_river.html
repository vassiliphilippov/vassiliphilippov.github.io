Connecticut River

1. PROPN

/ kəˌnɛtɪkət ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / kəˌnɛtɪkət ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in the northeastern United States, flowing south from northern New Hampshire along the border between New Hampshire and Vermont, through Massachusetts and Connecticut, and emptying into Long Island Sound
major river in the northeastern US

Examples

The Connecticut River is the longest river in New England, stretching over 400 miles.
Many towns along the Connecticut River benefit from its fertile floodplain.
Kayakers enjoy exploring the scenic bends of the Connecticut River in the summer.

Etymology

Named after the state of Connecticut, which derives from the Algonquian word 'quinetucket', meaning 'long tidal river'.
