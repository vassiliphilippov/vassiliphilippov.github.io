lead bank

1. NOUN

/ lid bæŋk / (AmE), / liːd bæŋk / (BrE)
the bank appointed by a group of lending banks to coordinate and manage their collective interests in a loan syndicate
coordinator bank in a loan syndicate

Examples

The lead bank coordinates communication and paperwork among all members of the lending syndicate.
After negotiating the terms, the lead bank presented the deal to the other participating banks.
JP Morgan acted as the lead bank for the large corporate loan arrangement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: syndicate bank - a bank participating in a loan syndicate
