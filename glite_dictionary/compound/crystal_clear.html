crystal clear

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəl ˈklɪr / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl ˈklɪə / (BrE)
(of language or meaning) perfectly clear; easy to understand or free from confusion.
perfectly clear; easily understood

Examples

Her explanation of the process was crystal clear and easy to follow.
The manager's instructions were crystal clear, leaving no room for misunderstanding.
He spoke in a crystal clear voice that everyone could hear.

Synset Examples

writes in a limpid style
lucid directions
a luculent oration
pellucid prose
a crystal clear explanation
a perspicuous argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucid - expressed clearly; easy to understand
  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not clear

Etymology

Figurative use of 'crystal' (clear, transparent mineral) to intensify 'clear'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəl klɪr / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl klɪə / (BrE)
completely transparent and easy to see through; allowing light to pass through with perfect clarity
perfectly transparent; easy to see through

Examples

The water in the mountain stream was crystal clear, revealing every stone below.
On a crystal clear day, you can see all the way to the distant mountains.
Her glass was so crystal clear that it was almost invisible on the table.

Synset Examples

the cold crystalline water of melted snow
crystal clear skies
could see the sand on the bottom of the limpid pool
lucid air
a pellucid brook
transparent crystal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen
  • Synonym: pellucid - transmitting light; able to be seen through with clarity
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent

Etymology

From 'crystal', denoting clarity like that of a natural crystal, and 'clear'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00433489-s
Synset POS: s
