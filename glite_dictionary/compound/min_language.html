Min language

1. NOUN

/ mɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪn ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of several related forms of Chinese spoken primarily in Fujian province and nearby regions, including dialects such as Hokkien and Teochew.
group of Chinese dialects in Fujian region

Examples

Many overseas Chinese communities speak the Min language, especially those with roots in southern Fujian.
The Min language is distinct from Mandarin and Cantonese in both pronunciation and vocabulary.
Researchers have documented the rich variety found within the Min language group, such as Hokkien and Teochew.

Etymology

From Chinese (Mandarin) 闽 (Mǐn), the traditional name for Fujian province.
