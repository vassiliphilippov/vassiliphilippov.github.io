descriptive clause

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɪptɪv klɔz / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv klɑːz / (BrE)
A subordinate clause that adds extra information about a noun phrase without restricting its meaning.
subordinate clause giving extra information

Examples

The sentence includes a descriptive clause that provides more information about the subject.
In 'My brother, who lives in Paris, is coming to visit,' 'who lives in Paris' is a descriptive clause.
You should use commas to set off a descriptive clause from the rest of the sentence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restrictive clause - a clause that limits or restricts the meaning of the noun it modifies
