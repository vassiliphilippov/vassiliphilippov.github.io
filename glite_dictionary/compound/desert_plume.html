desert plume

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛzɚt pluːm / (AmE), / ˈdɛzət pluːm / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the southwestern United States, with thick, plume-like spikes of yellow flowers and leathery blue-green divided leaves; also called Prince's plume.
Southwestern US flowering plant

Examples

The desert plume is well adapted to arid climates, thriving in rocky soils and full sun.
Every spring, the desert plume bursts into bright yellow bloom in the canyons of Arizona.
Gardeners in dry regions often choose desert plume for its drought resistance and ornamental foliage.
