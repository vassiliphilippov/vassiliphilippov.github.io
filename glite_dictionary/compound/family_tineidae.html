family Tineidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli taɪˈniːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɪˈniːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small moths, many species of which are known as clothes moths, whose larvae feed on natural fibers such as wool and can damage textiles.
family of small moths damaging textiles

Examples

The family Tineidae includes several species that are pests in homes and textile industries.
Natural predators help control populations of the family Tineidae in the wild.
Researchers studied the impact of humidity on moths from the family Tineidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothes moth - a type of moth in the family Tineidae
  • Degree Lower: family - a taxonomic rank in biology
  • Degree Higher: order Lepidoptera - the taxonomic order containing moths and butterflies

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Tineidae', based on 'Tinea', the type genus, meaning 'moth'.
