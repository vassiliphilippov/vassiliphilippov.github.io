alimentary canal

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪˈmɛntəri kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈmɛn.tər.i kəˈnæl / (BrE)
the long tube in the body that starts at the mouth and ends at the anus, through which food passes during digestion
the digestive tract from mouth to anus
medical

Examples

Food travels through the alimentary canal during digestion.
The alimentary canal includes the mouth, esophagus, stomach, intestines, and anus.
A blockage in the alimentary canal can cause serious health problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digestive tract - the system of organs for digesting food
  • Synonym: gastrointestinal tract - the stomach and intestine as a functional unit

Etymology

From Latin alimēntum (nourishment) + canalis (channel).
