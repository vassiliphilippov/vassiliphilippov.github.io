James River

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˌrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˌrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in eastern Virginia, USA, that flows eastward into Chesapeake Bay at Hampton Roads
a river in eastern Virginia, USA

Examples

The James River played a significant role during the American Civil War.

Many species of fish can be found in the James River near Richmond.

Etymology

Named after King James I of England.
