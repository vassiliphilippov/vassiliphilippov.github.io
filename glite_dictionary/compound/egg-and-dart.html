egg-and-dart

1. NOUN

/ ɛg ənd dɑrt / (AmE), / ɛg ənd dɑːt / (BrE)
a decorative molding featuring a series of egg-shaped figures alternating with dart-shaped elements, commonly used in classical architecture.
architectural molding with egg and dart shapes

Examples

The ornate cornice was decorated with an intricate egg-and-dart pattern along its edge.
Many ancient Greek temples used egg-and-dart molding to embellish their columns.
A restoration expert replicated the original egg-and-dart design in the historic building's interior.

Etymology

Describes the alternating 'egg' (oval) and 'dart' (arrow-shaped) patterns prominent in classical Greek and Roman molding.
