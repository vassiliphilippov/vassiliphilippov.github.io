slow lane

1. NOUN

/ sloʊ leɪn / (AmE), / sləʊ leɪn / (BrE)
the lane on a road or highway designated for vehicles moving at slower speeds than other traffic
lane for slower-moving vehicles

Examples

Trucks are required to stay in the slow lane on this stretch of highway.
He drove in the slow lane so he could enjoy the scenery at a relaxed pace.
During rush hour, the slow lane was backed up for several miles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fast lane - lane for vehicles moving at faster speeds

2. NOUN

/ sloʊ leɪn / (AmE), / sləʊ leɪn / (BrE)
A way of life characterized by a relaxed, leisurely pace, often with less stress and pressure.
relaxed, leisurely lifestyle

Examples

After years of working long hours, he decided to switch to the slow lane.
Many retirees find happiness in the slow lane, spending more time with family and friends.
Some people thrive in the slow lane, valuing peace and simplicity over constant activity.

Synset Examples

He chose the slow lane, enjoying every moment of his retirement.
I prefer the slow lane in life; it’s less stressful.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fast lane - a way of life characterized by excitement and rapid activity
