Capitol Building

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæpɪtəl ˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtəl ˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
the building in Washington, D.C., where the United States Congress meets; the seat of the U.S. legislative branch
U.S. Congress legislative building in Washington, D.C.
american

Examples

Tourists from all over the world visit the Capitol Building during their stays in Washington, D.C.
Important legislative decisions are debated and voted on inside the Capitol Building.
After the inauguration, the parade traditionally passes by the Capitol Building on its way down Pennsylvania Avenue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: White House - official residence and workplace of the U.S. President
  • Synonym: Congress - the legislative body that meets in the Capitol Building

Etymology

From Latin 'capitoleum', the temple of Jupiter at Rome; 'Capitol' was adopted for the U.S. Congress building, completed in the early 19th century.
