allspice tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌspaɪs tri / (AmE), / ˈɔːlˌspaɪs triː / (BrE)
an aromatic West Indian tree (Pimenta dioica) that produces berries used as the spice allspice
tree producing allspice berries

Examples

The allspice tree is native to the Caribbean and widely grown in Jamaica.
Dried berries from the allspice tree are ground and used in baking and cooking.
Botanists visited the grove to study the aromatic leaves of the allspice tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - the dried, aromatic berry used as a spice

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌspaɪs tri / (AmE), / ˈɔːlˌspaɪs triː / (BrE)
a tropical American tree (Pimenta dioica) with small white flowers and aromatic berries used as a spice
tropical tree with aromatic berries

Examples

The allspice tree is cultivated for its berries, which are dried and used as a spice in cooking.
An allspice tree can reach heights of over ten meters in its native habitat.
The leaves of the allspice tree are also aromatic and sometimes used to flavor local dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - the dried aromatic berries of the allspice tree, used as a spice
