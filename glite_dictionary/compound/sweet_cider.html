sweet cider

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈsaɪdɚ / (AmE), / swiːt ˈsaɪdə / (BrE)
cider made from pressed apples that has not been fermented; non-alcoholic apple juice, especially as traditionally produced.
unfermented apple cider
american

Examples

They served fresh sweet cider at the autumn festival.
Every fall, I look forward to drinking sweet cider with warm donuts.
Unlike hard cider, sweet cider is non-alcoholic and safe for children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard cider - alcoholic beverage made from fermented apple juice
