Begonia rex

1. NOUN

/ bəˈɡoʊniə rɛks / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊniə rɛks / (BrE)
A species of begonia, prized as an ornamental houseplant, with broad, rough-textured leaves patterned in silver, bronze, purple, and red-brown, and inconspicuous flowers.
ornamental plant with patterned leaves

Examples

The striking leaves of the Begonia rex made it the perfect centerpiece for her living room.
Gardeners adore Begonia rex for its dramatic foliage in shades of purple and silver.
He collects rare plants and was thrilled to add a new variety of Begonia rex to his greenhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begonia - a genus of flowering plants with showy leaves or flowers

Etymology

Genus name from French 'bégonia' (after Michel Bégon, French patron of botany); 'rex' is Latin for 'king,' reflecting its status among begonias.
