family Cathartidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kəˌθɑːrˈtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli kəˈθɑːtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large New World vultures that includes condors, turkey vultures, and king vultures.
New World vulture family (Cathartidae)

Examples

The family Cathartidae includes both condors and turkey vultures found across the Americas.
Unlike Old World vultures, members of the family Cathartidae are native to the New World.
Researchers have studied the role of the family Cathartidae in the ecosystem as scavengers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulture - a large bird of prey feeding chiefly on carrion

Etymology

From scientific Latin Cathartidae, based on Greek kathartēs (purifier) and -idae (family suffix).
