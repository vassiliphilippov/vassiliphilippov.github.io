stagnant hypoxia

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡ.nənt haɪˈpɑksi.ə / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.nənt hɪˈpɒksi.ə / (BrE)
A type of hypoxia caused by insufficient oxygen delivery due to slow or impaired blood circulation, as can occur in conditions like congestive heart failure.
lack of oxygen due to poor blood circulation
medical

Examples

Congestive heart failure can cause stagnant hypoxia in peripheral tissues.
The doctor explained that stagnant hypoxia results from inadequate blood flow, not a lack of oxygen in the lungs.
A classic sign of stagnant hypoxia is bluish discoloration in the extremities due to poor circulation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anemic hypoxia - hypoxia due to decreased oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood
  • Degree Lower: hypoxic hypoxia - hypoxia due to low oxygen in the arterial blood
  • Degree Higher: histotoxic hypoxia - hypoxia due to the inability of tissues to utilize oxygen

Etymology

From Latin stagnans, present participle of stagnare (to stagnate), and Greek hypoxia (lack of oxygen).
