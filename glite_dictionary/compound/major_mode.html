major mode

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒɚ moʊd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə məʊd / (BrE)
(in music) a mode whose harmony is based on the major scale, producing a bright or happy sound.
music: harmony based on the major scale

Examples

The piece is written entirely in the major mode, giving it an uplifting character.
Many popular songs use the major mode to create a cheerful atmosphere.
After a somber introduction in minor, the symphony shifts to major mode for its finale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor mode - a mode whose harmony is based on the minor scale
  • Synonym: major scale - a diatonic scale with a specific whole and half step pattern
