Jewish New Year

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuɪʃ nu ˈjɪr / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːɪʃ njuː ˈjɪə / (BrE)
a solemn Jewish festival, also known as Rosh Hashanah, marking the beginning of the Jewish year, observed on the 1st or 1st and 2nd of Tishri with prayers and the blowing of the shofar
Jewish festival marking the new year

Examples

Many families gather to celebrate the Jewish New Year with traditional foods and prayers.
The sound of the shofar is an important symbol of the Jewish New Year.
Each autumn, synagogues are filled with worshippers observing the Jewish New Year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rosh Hashanah - the Jewish New Year festival, celebrated on the first and second days of Tishri
