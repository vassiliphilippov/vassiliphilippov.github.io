Matthew Walker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæθju ˈwɔkər / (AmE), / ˈmæθju ˈwɔːkə / (BrE)
a stopper knot made by intertwining several strands, used especially in ropework to prevent unraveling
a type of stopper knot

Examples

The sailor secured the end of the rope with a Matthew Walker to stop it from fraying.
Learning to tie a Matthew Walker is essential for traditional rigging work.
He demonstrated the correct way to make a Matthew Walker knot during the lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper knot - a knot tied at the end of a rope to prevent it from slipping through a hole or device

Etymology

Named after a sailor, Matthew Walker, who is credited with inventing the knot in the 18th century.
