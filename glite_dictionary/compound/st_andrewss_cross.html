St Andrews's cross

1. NOUN

/ sənt ˈændruːzɪz krɑs / (AmE), / sənt ˈændruːzɪz krɒs / (BrE)
A shrubby plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by yellow to apricot flowers with four petals arranged in a cross shape.
shrubby plant with cross-shaped flowers

Examples

In the wildflower garden, the St Andrews's cross added a splash of yellow to the landscape.
Botanists study the St Andrews's cross for its unique four-petaled blossoms.
We discovered a patch of St Andrews's cross growing along the forest edge in Georgia.

Etymology

Named after Saint Andrew, whose cross is reflected in the plant's four-petal flower arrangement.
