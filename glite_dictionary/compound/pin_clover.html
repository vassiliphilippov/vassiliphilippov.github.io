pin clover

1. NOUN

/ pɪn ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / pɪn ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A European weed with reddish, spreading stems, fernlike leaves, and deep reddish-lavender flowers, naturalized in southwestern United States and Mexico, often grown for forage.
a weed with reddish stems and lavender flowers

Examples

The rancher planted pin clover as a nutritious forage for his livestock.
Pin clover thrives in the arid regions of the southwestern United States.
The slender fruits of pin clover stick upright, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.
