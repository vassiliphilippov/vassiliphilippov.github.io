latissimus dorsi

1. NOUN

/ ləˌtɪsɪməs ˈdɔrsaɪ / (AmE), / ləˌtɪsɪməs ˈdɔːsaɪ / (BrE)
a broad, flat muscle on each side of the middle and lower back that helps with the movement of the shoulder and arm
large back muscle aiding arm movement
medical

Examples

He injured his latissimus dorsi while lifting weights at the gym.
The latissimus dorsi plays a key role in swimming and rowing movements.
Stretching the latissimus dorsi can improve flexibility in the shoulders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapezius - large muscle extending over the back of the neck and shoulders

Etymology

From Latin 'latissimus' (broadest) and 'dorsum' (back).
