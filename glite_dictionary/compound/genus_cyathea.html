genus Cyathea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs saɪˈiːθiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs saɪˈiːθɪə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Cyatheaceae, consisting of large tree ferns native to tropical rainforests and temperate woodlands
genus of tree ferns (Cyatheaceae)

Examples

The genus Cyathea includes many large tree ferns found in tropical rainforests.
Botanists have classified several hundred species within the genus Cyathea.
Some species in the genus Cyathea can grow trunks several meters tall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyathea - a genus of tree ferns in tropical and temperate forests

Etymology

From Greek 'kuathos' (cup), referencing the cup-like shape in some species, and taxonomic Latin form Cyathea.
