non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor

1. NOUN

/ nɑn nuː.kli.oʊ.saɪd rɪˈvɜrs trænˈskrɪp.teɪs ɪnˈhɪb.ɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / nɒn njuː.kli.ə.saɪd rɪˈvɜːs trænˈskrɪp.teɪs ɪnˈhɪb.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used to treat HIV infection, which works by binding directly to reverse transcriptase and blocking the conversion of viral RNA to DNA, often used in combination therapy
class of HIV antiviral drugs
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor as part of her antiretroviral therapy.
Resistance can develop if a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor is not taken consistently.
Doctors often combine a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor with other HIV medications for greater effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: reverse transcriptase inhibitor - drug class inhibiting reverse transcriptase

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not), 'nucleoside', 'reverse transcriptase', and 'inhibitor', combining scientific roots describing mechanism and function.
