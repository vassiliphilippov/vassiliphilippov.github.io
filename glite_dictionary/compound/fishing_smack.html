fishing smack

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ smæk / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ smæk / (BrE)
a type of fishing boat, often fitted with a tank or well to keep the catch alive
a type of fishing boat with a fish well

Examples

The crew spent a week at sea on their fishing smack, returning with a hold full of fresh herring.
In the nineteenth century, many coastal villages depended on the fishing smack for their livelihoods.
The old fishing smack had been restored and now serves as a museum exhibit.

Etymology

From 'smack' meaning a fishing boat, of Dutch origin ('smak', a kind of sailing boat).
