gray snapper

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈsnæpər / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈsnæpə / (BrE)
A predatory marine fish of the western Atlantic (Lutjanus griseus), found in shallow waters off the coast of Florida and valued as a game and food fish.
a marine food fish found off Florida

Examples

The gray snapper is frequently caught by anglers along the Gulf Coast.
We grilled the gray snapper we caught during our fishing trip.
Gray snappers often hide among mangrove roots in shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangrove snapper - another name for the gray snapper, especially used in some regions
