break dance

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪk ˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ˌdɑːns / (BrE)
A style of solo street dance characterized by rapid acrobatic movements, often performed to hip-hop or rap music, with various parts of the body touching the ground.
street dance with acrobatic moves

Examples

The group captivated the crowd with an energetic break dance performance.
He learned to break dance when he was a teenager growing up in New York.
A circle formed around her as she began her impressive break dance moves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakdancing - the practice or action of break dancing

Etymology

From 'break' (as in breakbeat, a section of music) and 'dance.' Popularized in the 1970s in the US.
