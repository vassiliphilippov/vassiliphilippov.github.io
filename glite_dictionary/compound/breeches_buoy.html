breeches buoy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbritʃɪz ˌbuː.i / (AmE), / ˈbriːtʃɪz ˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a life-saving device consisting of a ring buoy fitted with short trousers for support, used to transfer people from ships to shore or between vessels
life-saving ring with support trousers

Examples

The sailors rigged a breeches buoy to rescue crew members from the stranded ship.
During the storm, they used a breeches buoy to bring passengers safely to the shore.
A museum exhibit displays a vintage breeches buoy used in 19th-century sea rescues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life buoy - a ring-shaped flotation device for rescuing people from water
  • Synonym: lifeboat - a boat used for rescuing people at sea

Etymology

From 'breeches' (trousers) and 'buoy',' indicating the ring buoy with supporting trousers.
