pleural cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʊrəl ˌkævəti / (AmE), / ˈplʊərəl ˌkævəti / (BrE)
the space within the thorax between the pleural membranes that surrounds the lungs
space in chest surrounding lungs
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctor carefully examined the pleural cavity for signs of infection.
A buildup of fluid in the pleural cavity can cause difficulty breathing.
The lungs are enclosed within the pleural cavity, which provides a protective environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoracic cavity - the chest cavity that contains the heart and lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'pleura' (side of the body, rib) and 'cavity' (hollow space).
