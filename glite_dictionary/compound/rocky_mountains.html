Rocky Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑki ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˈrɒki ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a major mountain range in western North America, extending from British Columbia in Canada to northern New Mexico in the United States, and forming the continental divide.
major mountain range in western North America

Examples

We drove across the Rocky Mountains during our summer road trip.
The snow-capped peaks of the Rocky Mountains attract hikers and skiers from around the world.
Wildlife such as elk and bears are common in the Rocky Mountains.

Etymology

Named from the descriptive English words 'rocky' and 'mountains', first recorded in early 19th-century exploration accounts.
