minister of religion

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪstər əv rɪˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstə əv rɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
a person who is officially authorized to conduct religious worship and perform religious rites
person authorized to conduct religious worship

Examples

The minister of religion led the congregation in prayer during the Sunday service.
Only a minister of religion is authorized to perform wedding ceremonies in this church.
As a minister of religion, she often visits members of her parish who are unwell.

Synset Examples

clergymen are usually called ministers in Protestant churches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy, especially in Christian churches
  • Synonym: priest - a person, usually in Christian churches, who performs religious ceremonies
