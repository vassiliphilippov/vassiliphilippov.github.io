opossum wood

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑsəm wʊd / (AmE), / əˈpɒsəm wʊd / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree found in the southeastern United States, especially from West Virginia to Florida and Texas.
a tree of the southeastern U.S.

Examples

The leaves of the opossum wood turn golden in autumn.
Many birds nest in the branches of opossum wood trees.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of native opossum wood in Texas.
