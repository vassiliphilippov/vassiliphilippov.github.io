arteria rectalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə rɛkˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə rɛkˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
any of the arteries that supply blood to the rectal region
arteries supplying the rectal area
medical

Examples

The arteria rectalis plays a crucial role in supplying blood to the lower part of the rectum.
Damage to the arteria rectalis can result in impaired blood flow to the rectal tissues.
During the surgery, care was taken to preserve the arteria rectalis to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectal artery - an artery supplying blood to the rectum

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'rectalis' (pertaining to the rectum).
