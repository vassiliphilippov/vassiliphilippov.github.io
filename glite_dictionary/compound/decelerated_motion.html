decelerated motion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛləˌreɪtɪd ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tɪd ˈməʊ.ʃən / (BrE)
Motion in which the velocity decreases continuously over time, especially in the context of physics or mechanics.
motion with continually decreasing velocity

Examples

During braking, a car undergoes decelerated motion until it comes to a stop.
The experiment was designed to measure the force required to maintain decelerated motion in the object.
Astronauts experience decelerated motion as a spacecraft slows down before re-entering the Earth's atmosphere.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accelerated motion - motion with continually increasing velocity

Etymology

From 'decelerate' (from Latin 'de-' + 'celerare', to quicken) + 'motion' (from Latin 'motio', movement).
