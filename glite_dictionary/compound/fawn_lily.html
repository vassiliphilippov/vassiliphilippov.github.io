fawn lily

1. NOUN

/ fɔn ˈlɪli / (AmE), / fɔːn ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A North American wildflower of the genus Erythronium, especially Erythronium californicum, with nodding, creamy white or yellowish flowers and mottled leaves.
wildflower with creamy white or yellowish flowers

Examples

The fawn lily blooms early in spring along the shaded woodland trails.
Many gardeners admire the delicate petals of the fawn lily.
We spotted several fawn lilies growing beside the creek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - Another name for plants of the genus Erythronium, often with similar flowers

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the leaf markings to a young deer's (fawn's) spots.
