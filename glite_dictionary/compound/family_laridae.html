family Laridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ləˈraɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ləˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of seabirds that includes gulls and terns.
family of gulls and terns

Examples

The family Laridae comprises both gulls and terns found in coastal regions around the world.
Research on the family Laridae helps scientists understand the evolution of seabirds.
Bird watchers often focus on members of the family Laridae due to their diverse plumage and behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a seabird of the family Laridae
  • Synonym: tern - a slender seabird related to gulls

Etymology

From New Latin *Laridae*, based on genus *Larus* (gull) and the family suffix *-idae*.
