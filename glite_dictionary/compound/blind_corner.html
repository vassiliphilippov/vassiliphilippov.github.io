blind corner

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈkɔrnər / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
A corner on a road or street that drivers cannot see around, often because the view is blocked by a building, hedge, or other obstacle.
corner of a road with blocked visibility

Examples

Be careful when turning at the blind corner near the school.
A tall hedge created a blind corner at the end of the street, making it dangerous for drivers.
They installed a mirror at the blind corner to improve visibility for cars coming from both directions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind spot - area around a vehicle that the driver cannot see
