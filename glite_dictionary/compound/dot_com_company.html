dot com company

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑt ˈkɑm ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˌdɒt ˈkɒm ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
A company that conducts most of its business via the internet, typically identified by a URL ending in '.com'.
internet-based business using '.com' domain

Examples

The rise of the dot com company transformed global commerce during the late 1990s.
Many investors were eager to buy shares in any promising dot com company.
After the bubble burst, only the most resilient dot com companies survived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: e-commerce company - a company selling products or services online

Etymology

From the phrase '.com', referring to the commercial domain in internet web addresses, and 'company'.
