chip shot

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪp ʃɑt / (AmE), / tʃɪp ʃɒt / (BrE)
In golf, a low and short approach shot played with a quick, controlled swing so that the ball runs along the ground toward the hole.
low running approach shot in golf

Examples

He executed a perfect chip shot that landed just inches from the hole.
The chip shot is an essential skill for golfers to master around the green.
She practiced her chip shot to improve her short game accuracy.

Etymology

From 'chip' (a short, light stroke) + 'shot'. Used in golf since early 20th century.
