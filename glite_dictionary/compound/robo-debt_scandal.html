robo-debt scandal

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊboʊ dɛt ˈskændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbəʊ dɛt ˈskændl / (BrE)
a controversy in Australia over the government's automated system for recovering welfare debts, which led to widespread criticism and legal action due to errors and unfair practices
Australian government welfare debt controversy

Examples

The government apologized to thousands of people affected by the robo-debt scandal.
Media coverage of the robo-debt scandal led to a public inquiry.
The fallout from the robo-debt scandal continues to shape welfare policy debates in Australia.

Synset Examples

The robo-debt scandal led to widespread public protests.
Many families were affected by the errors in the robo-debt system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandal - an action or event causing public outrage or censure

Etymology

Robo-debt from 'robotic' (automated) and 'debt'; refers to an Australian program in the 2010s-2020s.
