temporal role

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl roʊl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl rəʊl / (BrE)
a linguistic term referring to the function of a noun phrase that indicates the time at which a state or an action occurs in a sentence
linguistic function marking time in sentences

Examples

In the sentence 'He arrived yesterday', 'yesterday' serves as the temporal role.
Linguists analyze the temporal role to understand when actions in a text occur.
The word 'morning' in 'They left in the morning' fulfills a temporal role in the sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semantic role - the role that a noun phrase plays in the meaning of a sentence

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of time) and English 'role'.
