tree sparrow

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / tri ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
a small Eurasian sparrow (Passer montanus) with chestnut crown and white cheeks, smaller than the house sparrow
small Eurasian sparrow species

Examples

The tree sparrow is easily identified by the black spot on its white cheek.
Researchers observed a decline in tree sparrow populations throughout Europe.
We spotted a flock of tree sparrows feeding on the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: house sparrow - a common sparrow species closely related to the tree sparrow

2. NOUN

/ tri ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / tri ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
a small finch-like bird, particularly the Eurasian species (Passer montanus), found in woodland and farmland, and in North America also refers to Spizelloides arborea, common in winter in the northern United States
a type of sparrow found in woodland areas

Examples

Every winter, flocks of tree sparrows gather near the northern woods to search for food.
We spotted a tree sparrow perched on the garden fence alongside a robin.
The tree sparrow is often confused with the closely related house sparrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparrow - small, plump, brown and grey bird of the family Passeridae
