dwarf chestnut

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
A shrubby chestnut tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its small edible nuts.
small chestnut tree with edible nuts

Examples

The dwarf chestnut thrives in sandy soils throughout the southeastern United States.
Wildlife often feed on the small nuts produced by the dwarf chestnut.
We planted a dwarf chestnut in the backyard for its ornamental value and edible nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinquapin - alternative name for dwarf chestnut (Castanea pumila)
