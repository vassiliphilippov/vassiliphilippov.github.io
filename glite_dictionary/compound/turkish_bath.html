Turkish bath

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.kɪʃ bæθ / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.kɪʃ bɑːθ / (BrE)
A steam room, often followed by a shower and massage, modeled on the traditional Middle Eastern public bathing ritual.
Middle Eastern-style steam bath facility

Examples

After a long flight, she relaxed in the hotel’s Turkish bath to refresh herself.
The old city still features an ornate Turkish bath built during the Ottoman era.
He enjoys going to a Turkish bath for the steam and traditional massage treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauna - A Finnish-style hot steam bath

Etymology

Refers to the Turkish public bath tradition, from Turkish 'hamam', popularized in Western Europe in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːkɪʃ bæθ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪʃ bɑːθ / (BrE)
A type of steam bath in which you sweat in a heated steam room before usually receiving a massage and a cold shower, traditionally associated with Middle Eastern bathing customs.
type of steam bath with massage and cold shower

Examples

Many tourists enjoy visiting a Turkish bath to relax after a long day of sightseeing.
The hotel offers a traditional Turkish bath experience, complete with a steam room and massage.
After spending an hour in the Turkish bath, I felt completely rejuvenated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauna - A Finnish steam bath in which hot air and steam are used to induce sweating

Etymology

From the national adjective 'Turkish' and 'bath,' referring to the Middle Eastern origin of the practice.
