coast boykinia

1. NOUN

/ koʊst bɔɪˈkɪniə / (AmE), / kəʊst bɔɪˈkɪniə / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to western North America, with leaves mainly at the base and clusters of small white flowers
a plant with basal leaves and white flower clusters

Examples

The moist woodland was carpeted with coast boykinia in full bloom.
Hikers in California often spot coast boykinia along shaded stream banks.
Botanists are studying the distribution of coast boykinia across different elevations.

Etymology

From 'Boykinia', the genus name honoring American botanist Samuel Boykin, combined with 'coast', referring to its coastal habitat.
