order Cydippea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sɪˈdɪpiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɪˈdɪpiə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of ctenophores characterized by having two long, feather-like (pinnate) tentacles.
order of ctenophores with long pinnate tentacles

Examples

Scientists have discovered several new species within the order Cydippea in the deep Atlantic Ocean.
Members of the order Cydippea are distinguished by their two prominent tentacles.
Research on the order Cydippea has helped illuminate the evolution of ctenophores.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Cydippe and the taxonomic rank 'order'.
