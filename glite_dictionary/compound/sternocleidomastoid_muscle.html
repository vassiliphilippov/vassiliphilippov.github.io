sternocleidomastoid muscle

1. NOUN

/ stɜr.noʊ.klaɪ.doʊˌmæs.tɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / stɜː.nəʊ.klaɪ.dəʊˌmæs.tɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
a thick muscle on each side of the neck that runs from the sternum and clavicle to the mastoid process, turning the head and flexing the neck
muscle in the neck involved in head movement
medical

Examples

A strained sternocleidomastoid muscle can make turning your head very painful.
The doctor palpated the sternocleidomastoid muscle to check for any tenderness.
Tension in the sternocleidomastoid muscle is sometimes linked to headaches.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek roots: 'sterno-' (sternum), 'cleido-' (clavicle), and 'mastoid' (mastoid process of the skull).
