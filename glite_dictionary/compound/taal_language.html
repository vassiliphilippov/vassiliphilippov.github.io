Taal language

1. NOUN

/ tɑl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tɑːl ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an official language of South Africa, closely related to Dutch and Flemish; also known as Afrikaans
official language of South Africa, related to Dutch

Examples

Many South Africans grew up speaking Taal language at home and school.
Linguists study the Taal language to understand its evolution from Dutch.
The Taal language was officially recognized alongside English in South Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afrikaans - a West Germanic language spoken in South Africa and Namibia

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'taal' meaning 'language'; 'taal' derives from Dutch 'taal'.
