footprint evidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌprɪnt ˈɛvədəns / (AmE), / ˈfʊtprɪnt ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
evidence consisting of or based on footprints, especially at a crime scene or site of investigation.
evidence based on footprints

Examples

The prosecution relied on footprint evidence to place the suspect at the crime scene.
Investigators discovered footprint evidence leading away from the broken window.
The jury found the footprint evidence to be highly persuasive in reaching their verdict.

Synset Examples

there was footprint evidence that he had been at the scene of the crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forensic evidence - evidence used in legal proceedings, often collected during investigations
