streptococcus tonsillitis

1. NOUN

/ strɛp.təˈkɑk.əs tɑn.səˌlaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / strɛp.təˈkɒk.əs tɒn.sɪˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A bacterial infection of the tonsils and pharynx caused by streptococcus bacteria, typically resulting in sore throat and inflammation.
bacterial tonsil infection
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed Emily with streptococcus tonsillitis after examining her sore throat and swollen tonsils.
Antibiotics are often prescribed to treat streptococcus tonsillitis and reduce the risk of complications.
Children are particularly susceptible to streptococcus tonsillitis during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strep throat - streptococcal infection of the throat

Etymology

From New Latin *Streptococcus* (bacterial genus) and *tonsillitis* (inflammation of the tonsils), from Latin *tonsilla* (tonsil) + -itis (inflammation).
