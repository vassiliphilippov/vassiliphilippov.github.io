running stitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a simple sewing stitch in which the needle passes in and out of the fabric at regular intervals
basic sewing stitch made by passing in and out of fabric

Examples

Begin with a running stitch along the edge of the fabric to join the two pieces together.
Learning how to sew a running stitch is essential for any beginner.
Use a running stitch to create a simple seam on lightweight materials.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: backstitch - a sewing stitch that doubles back on the previous stitch
  • Degree Lower: basting stitch - a long, loose temporary stitch in sewing
