redwood family

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌwʊd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈrɛdwʊd ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a group of coniferous trees, traditionally considered a distinct botanical family (Taxodiaceae), now often included within the Cupressaceae (cypress family)
group of coniferous trees (Taxodiaceae)

Examples

The redwood family includes some of the largest and oldest trees in the world.
Recent studies suggest that the redwood family should be classified within the cypress family.
Botanists have debated whether the redwood family is truly separate from other conifers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress family - family of coniferous trees and shrubs, Cupressaceae
