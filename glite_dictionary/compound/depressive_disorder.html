depressive disorder

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈprɛsɪv dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsɪv dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A mental health condition marked by persistent feelings of sadness and a loss of interest, severe enough to require clinical attention.
a severe clinical depression
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a depressive disorder after months of persistent sadness and fatigue.
Many people with a depressive disorder benefit from therapy and medication.
He struggled to manage daily life due to his depressive disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depression - persistent feeling of sadness and lack of interest

Etymology

From 'depressive', derived from Latin 'deprimere' (to press down), and 'disorder'.
