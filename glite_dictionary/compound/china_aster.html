China aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnə ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
A cultivated plant (Callistephus chinensis) native to China, valued for its large, brightly colored flowers often grown for cutting.
ornamental plant with bright flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of the China aster made it a favorite choice for summer bouquets.
Gardeners often plant China asters in borders for a splash of color throughout the season.
She clipped a pink China aster and added it to the arrangement on her dining table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - Any plant of the genus Aster, related plants with star-shaped flowers

Etymology

Named after China, where the species originated, and 'aster' from Greek 'astēr' (star), referring to the star-shaped flowers.
