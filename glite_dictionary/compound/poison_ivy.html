poison ivy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən ˈaɪvi / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən ˈaɪvi / (BrE)
a climbing or trailing plant common in eastern and central North America, with three-part leaves and white berries, that produces an oil causing skin irritation and rash when touched
a rash-causing North American plant

Examples

Be careful when hiking, as poison ivy can cause a painful rash if you touch it.
Many children learn to recognize poison ivy by its group of three leaflets.
After gardening near the woods, she discovered a poison ivy rash on her arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison oak - a related North American plant causing similar skin irritation

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən ˌaɪvi / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən ˌaɪvi / (BrE)
A form of skin inflammation caused by contact with the poison ivy plant, typically resulting in an itchy rash.
skin rash from poison ivy plant

Examples

She was absent from work because her poison ivy had become severely inflamed.
After hiking through the woods, he realized he had developed poison ivy on his arms and legs.
The doctor prescribed a cream to soothe her poison ivy reaction.

Synset Examples

my poison ivy is drying up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact dermatitis - skin inflammation caused by direct contact with a substance
