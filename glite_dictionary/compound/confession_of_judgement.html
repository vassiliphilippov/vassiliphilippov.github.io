confession of judgement

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən əv ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən əv ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
a judgment entered by a court based on a debtor's written admission of liability, without a trial or regular legal proceedings
court judgment entered by debtor's admission
legal

Examples

The lender required the borrower to sign a confession of judgement before approving the loan.
By signing a confession of judgement, the debtor allowed the court to enter judgment without a trial.
A confession of judgement can leave borrowers vulnerable to immediate collection actions.
