chicken pox

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkən ˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn ˌpɒks / (BrE)
A highly contagious viral disease, especially affecting children, that causes an itchy skin rash with red spots and blisters.
contagious viral disease with skin rash
medical

Examples

My youngest son caught chicken pox just before his birthday party.
Before vaccines were available, almost every child had chicken pox at some point.
You should stay home from school if you have chicken pox until all the blisters have scabbed over.

Synset Examples

The doctor confirmed it was indeed chicken pox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varicella - another term for chicken pox; the medical/technical term

Etymology

The term may stem from the blisters' resemblance to peck marks from chickens.
