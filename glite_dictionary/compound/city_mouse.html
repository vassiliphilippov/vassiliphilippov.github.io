city mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪti maʊs / (AmE), / ˈsɪti maʊs / (BrE)
a person who prefers living in a city or feels uncomfortable in rural environments
person who prefers city life

Examples

Although she grew up in a small town, she quickly became a true city mouse after moving to New York.
As a city mouse, Mark struggled to adjust to the quiet of farm life.
My cousin is definitely a city mouse; she can't stand being without public transport or coffee shops.

Synset Examples

As a city mouse, he loved the vibrant nightlife.
The city mouse found life in the countryside dull.
In the city, he felt like a country mouse out of place.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: country mouse - a person who prefers rural life or feels out of place in urban settings

Etymology

Based on the character in the fable 'The Town Mouse and the Country Mouse', using 'city' as a modern equivalent of 'town'.
