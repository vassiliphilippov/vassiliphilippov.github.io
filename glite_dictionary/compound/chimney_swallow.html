chimney swallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪmni ˌswɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪmni ˌswɒləʊ / (BrE)
An American swift (Chaetura pelagica) that often nests in unused chimneys and similar structures.
a North American bird that nests in chimneys

Examples

A chimney swallow darted above the rooftops searching for insects.
In the spring, chimney swallows return to nest in old brick chimneys.
During the renovation, workers found a nest belonging to a chimney swallow inside the flue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimney swift - North American swift that nests in chimneys and hollow trees

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪmni ˈswɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪmni ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
a common swallow of North America and Europe that typically nests in barns or chimneys; also known as a barn swallow
common swallow nesting in barns or chimneys

Examples

The chimney swallow swooped low over the fields, catching insects in midair.
Farmers often welcome the arrival of the chimney swallow each spring.
A pair of chimney swallows built their nest in the old barn rafters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn swallow - a common swallow found worldwide, known for nesting in barns and other human structures
