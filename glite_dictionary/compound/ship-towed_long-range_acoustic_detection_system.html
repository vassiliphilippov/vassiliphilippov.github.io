ship-towed long-range acoustic detection system

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪp toʊd lɔŋ reɪndʒ əˈkuːstɪk dɪˈtɛkʃən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ʃɪp təʊd lɒŋ reɪndʒ əˈkjuːstɪk dɪˈtɛkʃən ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a shipboard sonar system designed for long-range detection, which is towed behind a vessel to identify underwater objects or threats
naval sonar system towed behind a ship

Examples

The navy upgraded their fleet with a new ship-towed long-range acoustic detection system for enhanced submarine tracking.
During the exercise, the crew deployed the ship-towed long-range acoustic detection system to monitor underwater activity.
Maintaining the ship-towed long-range acoustic detection system is crucial for effective maritime security operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towed array sonar - a type of sonar system towed behind a vessel to detect underwater sounds
