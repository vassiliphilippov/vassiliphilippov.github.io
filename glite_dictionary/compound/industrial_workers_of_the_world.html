Industrial Workers of the World

1. PROPN

/ ɪnˌdʌstriəl ˈwɜrkərz əv ðə ˈwɜrld / (AmE), / ɪnˌdʌstri.əl ˈwɜːkəz əv ðə ˈwɜːld / (BrE)
a radical international labor union founded in 1905 in Chicago, known for advocating workers' rights and the abolition of capitalism; also called 'the Wobblies'
historic American labor union

Examples

The Industrial Workers of the World played a crucial role in early twentieth-century labor movements.
Membership in the Industrial Workers of the World declined significantly after World War I.
Many songs written by the Industrial Workers of the World inspired workers across the United States.
