repertory company

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛpərˌtɔri ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈrɛpətəri ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a theatrical company that regularly performs a selection of plays from a repertoire, typically rotating them over a season
theatrical company with rotating plays

Examples

The repertory company staged three different classics during the summer festival.
She joined a well-known repertory company after graduating from drama school.
A repertory company allows actors to play a variety of roles in different plays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theater company - a group of actors and others who perform theatrical productions together

Etymology

From 'repertory' (from Latin 'repertorium', inventory) + 'company'.
