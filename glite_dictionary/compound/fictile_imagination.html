fictile imagination

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪk.taɪl ɪˌmæ.dʒəˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɪk.taɪl ɪˌmadʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The creative power of shaping and molding stories or ideas as if working with a flexible material.
creative ability to shape narratives

Examples

Her fictile imagination brought vivid worlds and characters into her stories.
The artist's fictile imagination transformed everyday occurrences into unforgettable tales.
Critics praised the novelist for her fictile imagination and her ability to craft compelling narratives.

Synset Examples

Her fictile imagination brought the story to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images not present to the senses

Etymology

From Latin 'fictilis' (molded, made of clay), related to 'imaginatio' (imagination).
