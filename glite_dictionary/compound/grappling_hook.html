grappling hook

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræp.lɪŋ hʊk / (AmE), / ˈɡræp.lɪŋ hʊk / (BrE)
A tool with multiple hooks, typically attached to a rope, used for grasping or holding objects, especially in climbing or retrieval.
hooked tool used with rope to grasp objects

Examples

The pirate threw a grappling hook onto the enemy ship to board it.
Using a sturdy grappling hook, the climbers secured their rope to the top of the cliff.
The rescue team relied on a grappling hook to retrieve the lost backpack from the ravine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapple - to seize or hold firmly, especially with a tool or hands

Etymology

From 'grapple' (to seize or grip) + 'hook', with usage dating to the 17th century.
