order Charales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər kəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kəˈrɑːliːz / (BrE)
An order of macroscopic algae found in fresh and brackish water, characterized by having a distinct axis; includes the stoneworts.
order of freshwater algae; stoneworts

Examples

The order Charales includes many stonewort species found in freshwater lakes.
Scientists study the order Charales to understand the evolution of green algae.
The unique structure of algae in the order Charales makes them important in aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonewort - a type of aquatic algae belonging to the order Charales

Etymology

From New Latin 'Charales', based on the type genus 'Chara'.
