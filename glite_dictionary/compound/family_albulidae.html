family Albulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ælˌbjuːlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ælˌbjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as bonefishes, found in warm seas and valued as game fish.
family of marine bonefishes

Examples

The family Albulidae includes several species prized by anglers for their fighting ability.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of the family Albulidae in the Bahamas.
The unique jaw structure of fish in the family Albulidae helps them feed on benthic invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonefish - a silvery game fish found in warm coastal waters

Etymology

From New Latin Albulidae, from 'Albula' (type genus) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
