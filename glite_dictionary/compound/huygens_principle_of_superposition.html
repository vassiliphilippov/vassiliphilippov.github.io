Huygens' principle of superposition

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪgənz ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ˌsuːpərpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈhɔɪgənz ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ˌsuːpəzɪˈʃən / (BrE)
the principle that the displacement at any point due to the superposition of wave systems is equal to the sum of the displacements of the individual waves at that point
principle stating wave displacements add together

Examples

According to the Huygens' principle of superposition, the total displacement at any point is the sum of the displacements caused by each wave.
The Huygens' principle of superposition is fundamental for analyzing complex wave patterns in physics.
Engineers rely on the Huygens' principle of superposition to model the behavior of light and sound waves.

Synset Examples

the principle of superposition is the basis of the wave theory of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principle of superposition - the general law stating that resultant displacement is the sum of individual displacements

Etymology

Named after Dutch physicist Christiaan Huygens (1629–1695); 'superposition' from Latin superponere (to place upon).
