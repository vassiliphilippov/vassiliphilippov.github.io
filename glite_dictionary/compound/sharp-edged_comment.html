sharp-edged comment

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrp ɛdʒd ˈkɑːmɛnt / (AmE), / ʃɑːp ɛdʒd ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
a remark or statement that is critical, harsh, or intended to hurt someone’s feelings
critical or cutting remark

Examples

He made a sharp-edged comment that embarrassed his colleague during the meeting.
Her sharp-edged comment stung more than she probably intended.
Tired of his sharp-edged comments, the team asked for a more positive approach.

Synset Examples

Her sharp-edged comments left everyone stunned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting remark - a comment that is intended to hurt or criticize someone deeply
  • Synonym: sarcastic comment - a remark using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
