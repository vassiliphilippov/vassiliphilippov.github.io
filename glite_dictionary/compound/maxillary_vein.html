maxillary vein

1. NOUN

/ mækˈsɪləri veɪn / (AmE), / mækˈsɪləri veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located in the face that continues from the pterygoid plexus and joins the superficial temporal vein to form the retromandibular vein
vein in the face forming part of the facial venous system
medical

Examples

The maxillary vein plays a significant role in draining blood from the deep structures of the face.
An obstruction in the maxillary vein can cause swelling and discomfort in the jaw and cheek area.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the maxillary vein.

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla' (jawbone) + 'vein'.
