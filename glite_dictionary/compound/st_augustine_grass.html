St. Augustine grass

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstɪn ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstɪn ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a low, mat-forming grass native to the southern United States and tropical America, commonly grown as a lawn grass
a common lawn grass of southern US and tropics

Examples

St. Augustine grass is often chosen for its durability in warm climates.
Our backyard was newly sodded with St. Augustine grass last spring.
Unlike Bermuda grass, St. Augustine grass thrives in partial shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawn grass - type of grass grown for lawns

Etymology

Named after St. Augustine, a city in Florida, USA, where the grass was widely cultivated.
