seshy mood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛʃi mud / (AmE), / ˈsɛʃi mʊːd / (BrE)
A relaxed, casual atmosphere or feeling, often associated with social gatherings where people are unwinding together.
relaxed, casual atmosphere or feeling
informal slang

Examples

After a long week, everyone was in a seshy mood at the barbecue.
The living room had a seshy mood, with soft music and dim lighting.
Whenever those friends get together, the seshy mood makes everyone feel at ease.

Synset Examples

Everyone was in a seshy mood after the event.
He always brings a seshy vibe to the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chill - relaxed, laid-back atmosphere
  • Synonym: party mood - atmosphere of celebration or festivity
  • Antonym: tense - state of heightened stress or anxiety

Etymology

From "sesh" (short for "session") plus the suffix "-y", creating an adjective, combined with "mood".
