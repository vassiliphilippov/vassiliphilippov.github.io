scut work

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌt wɝk / (AmE), / ˈskʌt wɜːk / (BrE)
work consisting of menial, tedious, or unpleasant tasks often regarded as trivial or unrewarding
menial or tedious work

Examples

During his internship, he was assigned most of the scut work no one else wanted to do.
She was tired of the endless scut work required to keep the clinic running smoothly.
New staff often handle the scut work before being given more responsibility.

Synset Examples

the hospital hired him to do scut work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudgery - hard, menial, or dull work
  • Synonym: grunt work - routine, physical, or menial labor

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; 'scut' may derive from a term for the short tail of a rabbit, possibly implying something small or insignificant.
