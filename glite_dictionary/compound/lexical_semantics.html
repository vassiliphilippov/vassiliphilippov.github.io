lexical semantics

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛksɪkəl səˈmæntɪks / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəl sɪˈmantɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics that studies the meanings of words and the relationships between them
study of word meanings and relations

Examples

The field of lexical semantics examines how words convey different shades of meaning.
Modern linguistics has made significant advances in lexical semantics over the past century.
A course in lexical semantics can help you better understand nuance in language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semantics - the study of meaning in language

Etymology

From 'lexical' (relating to words) and 'semantics' (from Greek sēmantikos, meaning 'significant').
