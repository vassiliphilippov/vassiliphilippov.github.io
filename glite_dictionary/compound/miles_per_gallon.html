miles per gallon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlz pər ɡælən / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlz pə ˈɡælən / (BrE)
a measure of how far a vehicle can travel using one gallon of fuel, indicating fuel efficiency
fuel efficiency measurement (distance per gallon)

Examples

The new hybrid car boasts 50 miles per gallon on the highway.
Most drivers want vehicles with higher miles per gallon to save on fuel costs.
Environmental standards require reporting the miles per gallon for all new cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilometers per liter - a metric measure of fuel efficiency (distance traveled per liter of fuel)
