idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪdioʊˈpæθɪk θrʌmˌboʊˌsaɪtəˈpinɪk pərˈpjʊrə / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk θrʌmˌbɒsaɪtəˈpiːnɪk pɜːˈpjʊərə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the onset of purpura caused by a reduction in circulating blood platelets of unknown origin.
blood disorder causing purpura from low platelets
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura after noticing excessive bruising and low platelet counts.
Children with idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura often recover without treatment, but adults may require medication.
Researchers are still studying the possible causes of idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura to improve treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purpura - purple spots on the skin from bleeding under the surface

Etymology

From Greek 'idios' (own, private), 'pathos' (suffering), 'thrombocyte' (blood platelet), and 'purpura' (purple spots).
