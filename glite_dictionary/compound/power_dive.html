power dive

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər daɪv / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə daɪv / (BrE)
A dive performed by an aircraft, in which the descent is accelerated by both gravity and the thrust of the engine.
steep, engine-accelerated aircraft dive

Examples

The pilot executed a power dive to demonstrate the aircraft's capabilities at the airshow.
During the emergency, the fighter went into a steep power dive to evade enemy fire.
A controlled power dive can increase an aircraft's speed rapidly, but it requires considerable skill.

Related Terms
