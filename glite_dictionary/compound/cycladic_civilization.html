Cycladic civilization

1. PROPN

/ saɪˈklædɪk ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈklædɪk ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the Bronze Age civilization that flourished on the Cyclades islands in the southern Aegean Sea from about 3000 to 1100 BC
ancient Bronze Age civilization of the Cyclades

Examples

Archaeologists have discovered finely crafted figurines from the Cycladic civilization on several islands.
The Cycladic civilization is known for its distinctive marble sculptures and pottery.
Many artifacts from the Cycladic civilization are exhibited in museums around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minoan civilization - Bronze Age civilization on Crete, related to Cycladic period
  • Synonym: Mycenaean civilization - Late Bronze Age civilization on mainland Greece, overlapping with Cycladic period

Etymology

Cycladic, from Cyclades (Greek Κυκλάδες) islands in the Aegean Sea; civilization from Latin 'civilis'.
