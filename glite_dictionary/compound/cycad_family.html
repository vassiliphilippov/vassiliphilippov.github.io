cycad family

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkæd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪkad ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of ancient, palm-like plants whose fertilization occurs through spermatozoids, related to ferns
a family of ancient, palm-like plants

Examples

The cycad family includes several species that have existed since the age of dinosaurs.
Members of the cycad family are often mistaken for palms due to their similar appearance.
Fossil records show that plants from the cycad family were abundant in prehistoric forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cycadaceae - the botanical family of cycads
  • Synonym: cycad - a plant belonging to the cycad family

Etymology

From New Latin Cycadaceae, based on Cycas (the genus) and the suffix -aceae for plant families.
