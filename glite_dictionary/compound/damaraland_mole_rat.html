Damaraland mole rat

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɑrəˌlænd moʊl ræt / (AmE), / dəˈmɑːrəlænd məʊl ræt / (BrE)
A colonial burrowing rodent of western Africa, similar to the naked mole rat.
colonial burrowing rodent of western Africa

Examples

Researchers studied a group of Damaraland mole rats to learn more about their social behavior.
The Damaraland mole rat lives in underground colonies that can number up to 40 individuals.
Unlike other rodents, the Damaraland mole rat shares many traits with the naked mole rat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked mole rat - a nearly hairless, burrowing rodent species closely related to the Damaraland mole rat

Etymology

Named after Damaraland, a region in Namibia, southern Africa.
