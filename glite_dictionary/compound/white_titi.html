white titi

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈtiːti / (AmE), / waɪt ˈtiːti / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and Brazil, noted for its slender clusters of white flowers and orange or crimson foliage.
a flowering shrub or small tree

Examples

The white titi blooms in spring, covering its branches with elegant clusters of white flowers.
Gardeners often choose white titi for its striking foliage that turns orange and crimson in autumn.
Wildlife are attracted to the nectar-rich blossoms of the white titi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titi - a shrub or small tree, especially Cyrilla racemiflora, with racemes of white flowers
