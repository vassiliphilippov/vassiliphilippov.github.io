carpe diem

1. X

/ ˌkɑr.peɪ ˈdi.em / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.peɪ ˈdiː.em / (BrE)
an expression used to urge someone to make the most of the present moment without worrying about the future
seize the day; make the most of now

Examples

For her birthday, she decided to carpe diem and booked a spontaneous trip to Paris.
Whenever Michael is faced with a choice, he remembers to carpe diem and never looks back.
His favorite motto is carpe diem, reminding him to embrace every opportunity.

Synset Examples

He always says to carpe the diem, not to wait.
It's time to carpe the diem and enjoy life.
She reminded me to carpe the diem every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize the day - to take full advantage of opportunities in the present moment

Etymology

From Latin 'carpe diem' meaning 'seize the day,' from the Roman poet Horace.
