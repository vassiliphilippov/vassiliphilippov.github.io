Mason jar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsən dʒɑr / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsən dʒɑː / (BrE)
A glass jar with a screw-top lid that forms an air-tight seal, commonly used for home canning and preserving foods.
glass canning jar with screw lid

Examples

She filled the Mason jar with homemade strawberry jam.
Pickles are often stored in a Mason jar to keep them fresh.
After sterilizing the Mason jars, she carefully poured in the hot tomatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canning jar - a glass jar used for preserving food by canning
  • Synonym: Kilner jar - a type of glass jar for preserving food, typically used in the UK

Etymology

Named after John L. Mason (1832–1902), who invented the jar in 1858.
