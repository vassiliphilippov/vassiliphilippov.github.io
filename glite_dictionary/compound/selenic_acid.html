selenic acid

1. NOUN

/ səˈlinɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈliːnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a strong, colorless inorganic acid (H₂SeO₄) similar to sulfuric acid, used mainly as an oxidizing agent in chemical reactions
a strong inorganic acid (H₂SeO₄)
medical

Examples

Laboratory tests revealed that selenic acid can oxidize many metals quickly.
Exposure to selenic acid should be carefully controlled due to its strong oxidizing properties.
Researchers compared the efficacy of selenic acid with sulfuric acid in catalyzing the reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfuric acid - a strong acid (H₂SO₄) analogous to selenic acid
  • Degree Lower: selenous acid - a weaker acid (H₂SeO₃) with selenium in a lower oxidation state

Etymology

From selenium + -ic, indicating a higher oxidation state of selenium.
