field garlic

1. NOUN

/ fiːld ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / fiːld ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
a wild species of garlic with a strong flavor, native to Europe and Asia, often found in fields and grassy places
wild species of garlic plant

Examples

She foraged for field garlic in the meadows to add a tangy flavor to her dishes.
Field garlic can quickly spread in gardens if not controlled.
The pungent smell of field garlic is noticeable during the spring months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild garlic - another wild onion plant with a similar pungent taste
