research rocket

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɝːtʃ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːtʃ ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a rocket that is launched to conduct experiments or tests, often in research and development settings
rocket used for scientific tests

Examples

The team launched a research rocket to collect atmospheric data above the Arctic Circle.
Several research rockets are fired each year to test new propulsion systems.
A research rocket can help scientists better understand the effects of microgravity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sounding rocket - rocket used to carry instruments for scientific experiments at high altitudes
