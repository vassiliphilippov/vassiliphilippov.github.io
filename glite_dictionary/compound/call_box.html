call box

1. NOUN

/ kɔl bɑks / (AmE), / kɔːl bɒks / (BrE)
a numbered compartment in a post office where mail is kept for collection by the addressee
box for mail pickup at a post office

Examples

He checked his call box every morning for new letters from overseas.
Only authorized staff are allowed access to each call box in the post office.
When I moved to a new city, I rented a call box for my mail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post office box - locked box in a post office for mail collection
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03996568-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kɔl bɑks / (AmE), / kɔːl bɒks / (BrE)
a public booth equipped with a telephone for making calls, typically found on streets or highways
public telephone booth

Examples

The driver rushed to the nearest call box to report the accident.
Many old call boxes have been removed due to the popularity of mobile phones.
The police officer directed her to use the call box by the main gate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone booth - a small kiosk containing a public telephone
  • Synonym: telephone kiosk - a public booth for telephone calls, especially in British English
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04408815-n
Synset POS: n
