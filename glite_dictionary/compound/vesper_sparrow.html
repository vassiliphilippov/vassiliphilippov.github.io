vesper sparrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛspər ˌspɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɛspə ˌspærəʊ / (BrE)
a North American finch (Pooecetes gramineus) noted for its melodious evening song
a North American finch with an evening song

Examples

A vesper sparrow perched on the fence, filling the meadow with its sweet evening song.
Birdwatchers often look for the vesper sparrow at dusk, when it is most active.
The distinctive markings on the vesper sparrow make it easy to identify among other grassland birds.

Etymology

From Latin 'vesper' (evening), referring to its evening song.
