pine-tar rag

1. NOUN

/ paɪn tɑɹ ræɡ / (AmE), / paɪn tɑː ræɡ / (BrE)
a cloth soaked with pine tar, used on the handle of a baseball bat to improve a batter's grip
rag soaked with pine tar, for gripping bat
american

Examples

The batter reached for the pine-tar rag before stepping up to the plate.
Coaches keep a pine-tar rag in the dugout for players to use during games.
The umpire inspected the pine-tar rag to ensure it met the regulations.
