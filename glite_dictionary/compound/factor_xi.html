factor XI

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɚ ɛl ˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ɛl ˈaɪ / (BrE)
a blood coagulation factor, the deficiency of which leads to a tendency to bleed excessively
blood clotting protein (factor 11)
medical

Examples

A deficiency of factor XI can cause excessive bleeding after surgery.
Patients with inherited factor XI deficiency may experience mild to moderate hemophilia.
Doctors tested his blood for factor XI levels to determine the cause of his symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: factor IX - a different coagulation factor, also known as Christmas factor
  • Degree Higher: factor XII - another coagulation factor, also called Hageman factor

Etymology

Factor XI uses Roman numerals and is named according to the order of discovery in the coagulation cascade.
