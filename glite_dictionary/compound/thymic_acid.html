thymic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌθʌɪmɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline compound used in perfumes, in preserving biological specimens, in embalming, or as a medical fungicide and antiseptic
crystalline compound used in preservation and medicine
medical

Examples

The laboratory uses thymic acid to preserve delicate tissue samples for later analysis.
Adding thymic acid to the perfume formula enhanced its longevity.
The antiseptic properties of thymic acid make it valuable in certain medical preparations.

Etymology

From 'thymic,' relating to thyme (the plant), as it is derived from thymol, plus 'acid'.
