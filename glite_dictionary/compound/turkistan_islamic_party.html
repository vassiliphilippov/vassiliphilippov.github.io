Turkistan Islamic Party

1. PROPN

/ tɜrˈkɪstæn aɪˈslæmɪk ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / tɜːˈkɪstæn ɪˈslæmɪk ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
an Islamist militant group founded in 1996 that seeks to establish an Islamic state in Central Asia and opposes the secular government of Uzbekistan.
Islamist militant group in Central Asia

Examples

The Turkistan Islamic Party claimed responsibility for several attacks in Central Asia.
Security forces increased patrols after learning about the activities of the Turkistan Islamic Party.
Reports indicate that the Turkistan Islamic Party operates along drug trafficking routes from Afghanistan.
