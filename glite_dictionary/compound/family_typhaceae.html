family Typhaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli taɪˈfeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli tɪˈfeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of perennial marsh plants with creeping rootstocks and long, linear leaves, commonly found in wetlands.
family of marsh plants (Typhaceae)

Examples

The family Typhaceae includes plants such as cattails, which are common in marshy habitats.
Many wetland restoration projects feature species from the family Typhaceae due to their ecological value.
The long, ribbon-like leaves are a key identifying feature of plants in the family Typhaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattail - common name for plants in the genus Typha, member of Typhaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from the genus name Typha plus the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
