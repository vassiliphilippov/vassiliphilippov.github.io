pen-pusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnˌpʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɛnˌpʊʃə / (BrE)
a person employed to do routine paperwork or administrative tasks, often regarded as uninteresting or lacking in creativity
person doing routine office work
informal

Examples

After his promotion, he worried he would become just another pen-pusher in the corporate machine.
She resented being seen as a pen-pusher when she actually managed important financial reports.
He left his job as a pen-pusher to pursue a more creative career in graphic design.

Synset Examples

Being a pen-pusher isn't her dream career.
He felt like a pen-pusher in his monotonous office job.
She called him a pen-pusher for his lack of creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucrat - an official in a government department, usually concerned with procedural correctness
  • Synonym: paper-shuffler - person doing a lot of paperwork, often with little productive outcome

Etymology

From 'pen' (writing instrument) + 'pusher' (person who pushes or moves something), originally referring to office workers who used pens for paperwork.
