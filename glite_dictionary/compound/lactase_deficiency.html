lactase deficiency

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.teɪs dɪˈfɪʃ.ən.si / (AmE), / ˈlæk.teɪs dɪˈfɪʃ.ən.si / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the body has an insufficient amount of the enzyme lactase, resulting in an inability to digest lactose found in milk and dairy products.
inability to digest lactose due to lack of lactase
medical

Examples

People with lactase deficiency often experience bloating and cramps after consuming dairy products.
Doctors can diagnose lactase deficiency using a hydrogen breath test.
Managing lactase deficiency usually involves avoiding foods and drinks that contain lactose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactose intolerance - inability to digest lactose due to lactase deficiency

Etymology

From 'lactase', an enzyme name formed from Latin 'lac' (milk) and '-ase' (enzyme suffix), and 'deficiency'.
