United Mexican States

1. PROPN

/ juˈnaɪtɪd ˈmɛksɪkən steɪts / (AmE), / juˈnaɪtɪd ˈmɛksɪkən steɪts / (BrE)
the official name of Mexico, a republic in southern North America that gained independence from Spain in 1810
official name for the country of Mexico

Examples

The constitution of the United Mexican States was adopted in 1917 and remains in effect today.
International treaties sometimes refer to Mexico by its official name, the United Mexican States.
Customs forms at the border may list 'Country of Citizenship' as the United Mexican States instead of Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexico - country in southern North America, officially called the United Mexican States

Etymology

Translation of the Spanish 'Estados Unidos Mexicanos', the official name of Mexico.
