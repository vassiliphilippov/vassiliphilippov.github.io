ambulant plague

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmbjələnt pleɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈambjʊlənt pleɪɡ / (BrE)
A mild form of bubonic plague, in which the infected person is able to move about and does not display severe symptoms.
mild form of bubonic plague
medical

Examples

Because ambulant plague presents with only mild symptoms, it is often mistaken for a routine illness.
The spread of ambulant plague went undetected for weeks due to its subtler presentation.
Doctors were relieved that the outbreak was limited to cases of ambulant plague rather than the more deadly form.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bubonic plague - a severe, often deadly infectious disease caused by Yersinia pestis

Etymology

From Latin 'ambulans' (walking) and 'plague'.
