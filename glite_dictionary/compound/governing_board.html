governing board

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnɪŋ bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənɪŋ bɔːd / (BrE)
A group of people officially responsible for directing and managing the affairs of an organization or institution.
group managing an institution

Examples

The university's governing board met last week to discuss the new policy changes.
A new member was appointed to the hospital's governing board after the previous chair retired.
Non-profit organizations often rely on a governing board to ensure accountability and proper management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: board of directors - a group of people elected to represent shareholders and oversee the activities of a company
