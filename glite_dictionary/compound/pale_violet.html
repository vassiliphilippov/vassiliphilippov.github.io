pale violet

1. NOUN

/ peɪl ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / peɪl ˈvʌɪələt / (BrE)
A leafy-stemmed violet native to eastern North America, having large white or creamy flowers faintly marked with purple.
leafy North American violet with pale flowers

Examples

The pale violet is often found in moist woodlands across the eastern United States.
We photographed a beautiful cluster of pale violets blooming by the stream.
Gardeners value the pale violet for its delicate, cream-colored flowers with purple veins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple, blue, or white flowers
