mugho pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːgoʊ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈmjuːgəʊ paɪn / (BrE)
a low, shrubby pine tree native to central Europe, with short, bright green needles grouped in pairs
a low shrubby pine tree native to Europe

Examples

The mugho pine is often used in landscaping because of its compact shape.
Gardeners value the mugho pine for its hardiness and attractive green needles.
I planted a mugho pine at the edge of my rock garden last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain pine - an alternative name for the mugho pine

Etymology

From Italian dialect 'mughus', referring to dwarf pine species.
