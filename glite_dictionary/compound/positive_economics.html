positive economics

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzətɪv ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪks / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of economics focused on describing, explaining, and predicting economic phenomena without making value judgments or policy prescriptions
branch of economics describing and explaining phenomena

Examples

Positive economics seeks to explain how markets function without recommending particular economic policies.
The distinction between positive economics and normative economics is fundamental to understanding economic thought.
Many researchers focus on positive economics to ensure their analysis remains objective and fact-based.

Synset Examples

There is a large part of the economics of education that is explicitly about the development of a positive economics of the demand for schooling that has no policy implications, even if NAP were true.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normative economics - the branch of economics that expresses value judgments and prescribes policies

Etymology

From 'positive' (factual) and 'economics', distinguishing descriptive analysis from normative concerns.
