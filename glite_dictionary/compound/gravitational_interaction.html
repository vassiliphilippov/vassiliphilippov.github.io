gravitational interaction

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˌveɪʃənəl ˌɪntəˈrækʃən / (AmE), / ɡravɪˌteɪʃənəl ˌɪntərˈakʃən / (BrE)
the weak fundamental force that acts between objects because of their mass; responsible for the attraction of bodies toward each other
force of attraction between masses

Examples

The gravitational interaction between the Earth and the Moon causes the tides.
Physicists are studying how the gravitational interaction might be mediated by particles called gravitons.
On a cosmic scale, the gravitational interaction organizes galaxies and keeps planetary systems together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - the force that attracts bodies toward the center of the earth or toward each other
  • Antonym: electromagnetic interaction - a fundamental force responsible for electric and magnetic effects
  • Antonym: strong interaction - the strongest fundamental force, acting between subatomic particles
  • Antonym: weak interaction - the weak force responsible for radioactive decay

Etymology

From 'gravitational', relating to gravity (Latin gravitas, 'weight'), and 'interaction', from Latin inter- 'between' + actio 'action'.
