Libyan Islamic Group

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪbiən ɪsˈlɑmɪk ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈlɪbiən ɪsˈlæmɪk ɡruːp / (BrE)
a Libyan militant Islamist organization formed in 1995, known for its opposition to the Libyan government and alignment with al-Qaeda
Libyan militant Islamist organization (1995–)

Examples

The Libyan Islamic Group was formed in the 1990s as an opposition to Gaddafi's regime.
Authorities blamed the Libyan Islamic Group for several attacks in Libya during the late 1990s.
Members of the Libyan Islamic Group reportedly had ties to other extremist organizations in the region.
