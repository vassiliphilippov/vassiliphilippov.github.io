radioactive dating

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdi.oʊˈæktɪv ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of determining the age of an object by measuring the amount of radioactive material it contains, commonly used with isotopes such as carbon-14
dating method using radioactive isotopes

Examples

Scientists use radioactive dating to estimate the age of ancient fossils and rocks.
The museum explained how radioactive dating was used to verify the artifacts' origins.
By analyzing the isotopes present, researchers refined their radioactive dating techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiocarbon dating - a form of radioactive dating using carbon-14
