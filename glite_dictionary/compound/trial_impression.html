trial impression

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪəl ɪmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl ɪmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
an impression made during the printing process to check for errors before producing the final print
test print to check for errors

Examples

The printer made a trial impression to ensure the type was set correctly.
After reviewing the trial impression, they found several spelling mistakes that needed correction.
It is standard practice to produce a trial impression before the main print run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - a printed copy made for checking before the final version
