Virgin Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪn ˌaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn ˌaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the northeastern West Indies, east of Puerto Rico, divided between the United States and Britain
group of Caribbean islands divided between US and UK

Examples

Many tourists visit the Virgin Islands each year for their beautiful beaches and clear waters.
The British Virgin Islands and the US Virgin Islands each have distinct governments.
Hurricane Irma caused severe damage to the Virgin Islands in 2017.

Etymology

Named by Christopher Columbus after the legendary St. Ursula and her 11,000 virgins.
