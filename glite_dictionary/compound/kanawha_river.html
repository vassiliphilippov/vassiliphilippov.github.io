Kanawha River

1. PROPN

/ kəˈnɔː ə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / kəˈnɔː ə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in West Virginia, USA, that is a tributary of the Ohio River
tributary of the Ohio River in West Virginia

Examples

The Kanawha River flows through the heart of West Virginia and supports local industries.
A scenic paddle down the Kanawha River reveals beautiful mountain landscapes.
Flooding along the Kanawha River in 2016 caused significant damage to several communities.

Etymology

From the name of the Native American Kanawha people, after whom the river is named.
