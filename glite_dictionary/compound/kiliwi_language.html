Kiliwi language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːliwɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkiːliwə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Kiliwa people of northern Baja California, Mexico.
language of the Kiliwa people of northern Baja California

Examples

The Kiliwi language is considered endangered, with only a few speakers remaining today.
Researchers have published a dictionary to help preserve the Kiliwi language.
Cultural festivals often include storytelling in the Kiliwi language to keep traditions alive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman language - a family of indigenous languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

Named after the Kiliwa people, indigenous to northern Baja California.
