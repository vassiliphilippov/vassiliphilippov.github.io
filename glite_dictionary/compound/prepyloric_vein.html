prepyloric vein

1. NOUN

/ priˈpaɪlɔrɪk veɪn / (AmE), / priˈpaɪlɒrɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located near the pylorus of the stomach, serving as a tributary to the right gastric vein
vein near the pylorus of the stomach
medical

Examples

Surgeons often identify the prepyloric vein during procedures involving the lower stomach.
The prepyloric vein is an important landmark for gastric surgery.
Bleeding from the prepyloric vein can complicate certain types of abdominal operations.

Etymology

From Latin 'pre-' (before) and 'pylorus' (gatekeeper, the opening from the stomach into the duodenum).
