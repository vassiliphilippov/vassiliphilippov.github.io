Fedayeen Saddam

1. NOUN

/ fɛdəˌjin sæˈdam / (AmE), / fɛdəˌjiːn sæˈdam / (BrE)
a paramilitary organization formed in Iraq in 1995 by young soldiers to support Saddam Hussein and suppress domestic opposition
Saddam Hussein's paramilitary organization (Iraq, 1995–2003)

Examples

The Fedayeen Saddam played a key role in maintaining support for Saddam Hussein's regime.
Reports indicated that the Fedayeen Saddam engaged in violent repression of political opponents.
After the fall of Baghdad, members of the Fedayeen Saddam went into hiding or fled the country.

Etymology

From Arabic 'fedayeen' (meaning 'those who sacrifice themselves') and 'Saddam' (referring to Saddam Hussein).
