return on invested capital

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɝn ɑn ɪnˈvɛstɪd ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːn ɒn ɪnˈvɛstɪd ˈkæpɪtəl / (BrE)
a measure of how efficiently a company generates profit from its total capital, expressed as a percentage, usually calculated by dividing earnings before interest and taxes by invested capital
profitability ratio measuring profit on capital invested

Examples

The company's return on invested capital improved significantly after restructuring its operations.
Analysts often compare return on invested capital across firms to assess managerial effectiveness.
A high return on invested capital may indicate that a business is using its resources efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return on equity - measure of profitability relating net income to shareholders' equity
  • Synonym: return on assets - indicator of how profitable a company is relative to its total assets
