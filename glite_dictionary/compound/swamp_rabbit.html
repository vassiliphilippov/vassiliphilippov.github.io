swamp rabbit

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a large cottontail rabbit native to the swamps and lowland areas of the southeastern United States
a cottontail rabbit of southeastern U.S. swamps

Examples

We spotted a swamp rabbit darting across the marshy ground.
The swamp rabbit is known for its strong swimming abilities.
Many birds and mammals, including the swamp rabbit, thrive in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottontail rabbit - a rabbit with a white fluffy tail, native to North America

2. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a large cottontail rabbit native to marshy and swampy areas of the southern United States, especially from North Carolina to Florida
a rabbit found in southern U.S. swamps

Examples

We spotted a swamp rabbit darting through the reeds near the lake's edge.
The swamp rabbit is well adapted to living in wet, marshy environments.
Unlike other rabbits, the swamp rabbit is an excellent swimmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottontail - a type of North American rabbit with a characteristic fluffy white tail
