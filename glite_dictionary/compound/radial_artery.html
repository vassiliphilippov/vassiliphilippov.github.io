radial artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəl ˈɑrti / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl ˈɑːti / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the brachial artery below the elbow, running down the forearm to the wrist and palm
artery of the forearm and hand
medical

Examples

The pulse is often measured at the radial artery on the wrist.
During the procedure, the surgeon inserted a catheter into the radial artery.
She felt a sharp pain along her radial artery after the injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulnar artery - artery that runs along the inner side of the forearm parallel to the radial artery

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (ray, spoke of a wheel) and 'arteria' (artery).
