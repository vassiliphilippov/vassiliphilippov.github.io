field chamomile

1. NOUN

/ fiːld ˈkæməˌmaɪl / (AmE), / fiːld ˈkaməˌmaɪl / (BrE)
A European white-flowered plant of the aster family, often considered a weed, that has become naturalized in North America.
white-flowered European weed naturalized in North America

Examples

The meadow was dotted with patches of field chamomile, their white blooms bright in the morning sun.
Farmers often struggle to control field chamomile as it spreads quickly across cultivated land.
Botanists identified the weed as field chamomile, a species introduced from Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamomile - A daisy-like plant known for its medicinal and aromatic uses
