order Cilioflagellata

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sɪliˌoʊflædʒəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɪlɪˌəʊflædʒɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order of single-celled organisms, variably classified in protozoans or algae, now often placed in the phylum Pyrrophyta
taxonomic order of single-celled organisms

Examples

The order Cilioflagellata includes several species once classified as protozoans.
Research on the order Cilioflagellata has led to a better understanding of plankton diversity.
Some scientists now place members of the order Cilioflagellata in the phylum Pyrrophyta.

Etymology

From Latin 'cilio-' (relating to cilia) and 'flagellata' (bearing flagella), referring to the organisms' motile structures.
