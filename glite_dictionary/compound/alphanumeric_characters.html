alphanumeric characters

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.fə.nʊˈmɛr.ɪk ˈkɛr.ɪk.tərz / (AmE), / ˌal.fə.njuːˈmɛr.ɪk ˈkær.ɪk.təz / (BrE)
characters consisting of both letters and numbers, and sometimes including punctuation marks
letters, numbers, and sometimes punctuation marks

Examples

Passwords must contain at least eight alphanumeric characters for security purposes.
The database will only accept entries made up of alphanumeric characters and not special symbols.
When creating a username, avoid using spaces and stick to alphanumeric characters only.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alphabetic characters - characters consisting only of letters
  • Degree Lower: numeric characters - characters consisting only of numbers

Etymology

Formed from 'alpha-' (from Greek alphabet) and 'numeric', meaning relating to letters and numbers.
