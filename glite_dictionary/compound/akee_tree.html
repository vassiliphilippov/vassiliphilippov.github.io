akee tree

1. NOUN

/ əˈkiː triː / (AmE), / əˈkiː triː / (BrE)
A tropical tree (Blighia sapida) native to West Africa, cultivated for its fragrant flowers and distinctive, edible but potentially poisonous fruit, especially in the Caribbean.
tropical tree with edible, colorful fruit

Examples

The akee tree thrives in Jamaica’s warm climate and is known for its striking red fruit.
We planted an akee tree in our garden to enjoy both its shade and colorful blossoms.
Before eating the fruit of the akee tree, it is important to make sure it is fully ripe.

Etymology

From West African languages (Yoruba 'àkìí'); introduced to the Caribbean by Captain William Bligh in the late 18th century.
