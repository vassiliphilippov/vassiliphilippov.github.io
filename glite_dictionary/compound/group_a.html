group A

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp eɪ / (AmE), / ɡruːp eɪ / (BrE)
the blood group in the ABO system whose red cells carry the A antigen
blood group with A antigen
medical

Examples

The patient was found to have group A blood and needed a compatible donor.
Donors with group A blood can safely give to patients with type AB blood.
Blood banks often have shortages of group A during donation drives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood group - classification of blood based on antigens on red blood cells
  • Degree Lower: group B - the blood group whose red cells carry the B antigen
  • Degree Higher: group AB - the blood group whose red cells carry both the A and B antigens
  • Degree Lower: group O - the blood group whose red cells carry neither A nor B antigens
