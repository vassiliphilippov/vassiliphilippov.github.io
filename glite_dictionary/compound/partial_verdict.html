partial verdict

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrʃəl ˈvər.dɪkt / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.ʃəl ˈvɜː.dɪkt / (BrE)
(Law) a decision in which the defendant is found guilty on some charges but acquitted on others within the same trial.
legal ruling: guilty on some charges, not others
legal

Examples

The jury returned a partial verdict, finding the defendant guilty on two counts but not guilty on the others.
In complex trials, a partial verdict can be delivered before the jury decides on the remaining charges.
The judge accepted the partial verdict and scheduled further proceedings for the unresolved charges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verdict - the decision of a jury in a legal case

Etymology

From Latin 'pars' (part) and Old French 'verdict', from 'ver' (true) + 'dit' (saying).
