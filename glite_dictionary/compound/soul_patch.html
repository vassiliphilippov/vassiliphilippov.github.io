soul patch

1. NOUN

/ soʊl pætʃ / (AmE), / səʊl pætʃ / (BrE)
a small patch of facial hair just below the lower lip and above the chin
small patch of hair below lower lip

Examples

He grew a soul patch to give his look a bit of retro flair.
Many jazz musicians in the 1960s were known for their signature soul patch.
She teased him about shaving off his soul patch before the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goatee - a small, pointed beard on the chin

Etymology

Probably from the idea of a distinctive spot ('patch') associated with 'soul' musicians in the 1950s and 1960s.
