baptismal font

1. NOUN

/ bæpˈtɪzməl fɑnt / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪzməl fɒnt / (BrE)
a large, usually decorative bowl or basin, often in a church, that holds water used for baptisms
bowl for baptismal water

Examples

The baby was christened at the baptismal font in the village church.
The ornate marble baptismal font dates back to the 14th century.
The priest sprinkled water from the baptismal font onto the infant’s head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: font - a large vessel for holding baptismal water

Etymology

From 'baptismal' (relating to baptism) and Latin 'fons, fontis' (spring, fountain).
