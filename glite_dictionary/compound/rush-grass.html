rush-grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a grass with wiry stems and sheathed flowering panicles, belonging to various species
a grass with wiry stems and sheathed panicles

Examples

The field was dotted with patches of rush-grass that thrived in the wet soil.
She bent down to study the wiry stems of the rush-grass by the stream's edge.
Some species of rush-grass are often mistaken for true rushes due to their appearance.

Etymology

Named for its similarity in appearance to rushes and its grass-like nature.
