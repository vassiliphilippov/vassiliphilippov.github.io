apple of Peru

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəl əv pəˈruː / (AmE), / ˈæpəl əv pəˈruː / (BrE)
a tall, annual tropical weed with strong-smelling leaves, large white or violet trumpet-shaped flowers, and prickly fruits, known to be highly poisonous
a poisonous tropical weed with trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

Children should never touch the apple of Peru because all parts of the plant are toxic.
The garden was invaded by apple of Peru, which quickly outgrew the other flowers.
She recognized the apple of Peru by its distinctive trumpet-shaped blossoms and prickly seed pods.

Etymology

Named after Peru, its presumed region of origin, and 'apple' for its fruit's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpəl əv pəˈruː / (AmE), / ˈæpəl əv pəˈruː / (BrE)
a coarse herb native to South America, grown for its blue-and-white flowers and bladderlike fruit containing a dry berry
South American flowering herb with bladderlike fruit

Examples

The apple of Peru is often grown in gardens for its ornamental flowers and unusual fruit.
Some gardeners find the apple of Peru invasive due to its rapid spreading.
The bladderlike pods of the apple of Peru enclose a dry berry that rattles when shaken.
