sense of abjection

1. NOUN

/ sɛns əv æbˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / sɛns əv æbˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of extreme disappointment, humiliation, or shame
a feeling of deep shame or humiliation

Examples

After being publicly criticized, she was overwhelmed by a sense of abjection.
The team's defeat left them with a lingering sense of abjection all week.
He could not shake the sense of abjection that followed his mistake.

Synset Examples

He felt a sense of abjection after losing the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abjection - a state of humiliation or degradation
  • Synonym: humiliation - a feeling of being humiliated or disgraced
  • Antonym: pride - a feeling of self-respect or personal worth

Etymology

From Latin 'abjectio' (casting away, rejection), with 'abjection' meaning a low or degraded state.
