method of fluxions

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.əd əv ˈflʌk.ʃənz / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.əd əv ˈflʌk.ʃənz / (BrE)
an early name for differential calculus, concerning the study of how functions change as their variables change, by using derivatives and differentials
historical term for differential calculus

Examples

Newton introduced the method of fluxions as a means to analyze changing quantities.
In historical mathematics, the method of fluxions corresponds to modern differential calculus.
Many textbooks once explained calculus using the method of fluxions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: differential calculus - the branch of calculus dealing with derivatives and differentials
  • Antonym: integral calculus - the branch of calculus dealing with integrals

Etymology

Coined by Isaac Newton in the late 17th century; 'fluxion' derives from Latin 'fluxus' (flow).
