mechanical system

1. NOUN

/ məˈkænɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A set of interconnected parts that interact according to the laws of mechanics, often to perform a specific function.
group of interacting mechanical parts

Examples

The engineer designed a mechanical system to automate the assembly line.
Regular maintenance is crucial for any mechanical system to prevent breakdowns.
A car's transmission is a complex mechanical system that requires careful calibration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanism - a system of parts working together in a machine
