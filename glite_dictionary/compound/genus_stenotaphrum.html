genus Stenotaphrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌstɛn.oʊˈtæf.rəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌstɛn.əʊˈtæf.rəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical grasses, some species of which are commonly used as lawn grasses, such as St. Augustine grass.
a genus of lawn grasses

Examples

Many warm-climate lawns are planted with genus Stenotaphrum species like St. Augustine grass.
Botanists classify St. Augustine grass as a member of the genus Stenotaphrum.
The genus Stenotaphrum includes several grasses valued for their durability in landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Augustine grass - A species of grass (Stenotaphrum secundatum) commonly used for lawns.

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Stenotaphrum', the botanical genus name.
