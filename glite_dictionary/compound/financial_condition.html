financial condition

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of a person’s or organization’s finances, especially relating to available assets and liabilities at a given time
state of finances

Examples

After reviewing the company’s financial condition, investors decided to proceed cautiously.
The report painted a bleak picture of the country’s financial condition after the recession.
They hired an accountant to assess their personal financial condition before applying for a loan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financial status - current situation or level of one's finances
  • Antonym: insolvency - the inability to pay debts; financial distress
