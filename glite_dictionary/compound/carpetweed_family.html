carpetweed family

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrpɪtˌwid ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪtwiːd ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of succulent herbs or small shrubs, primarily native to South Africa, New Zealand, and North America, including carpetweeds and fig marigolds (Aizoaceae)
a family of succulent herbs and small shrubs (Aizoaceae)

Examples

Many species in the carpetweed family are known for their drought tolerance.
Scientists have classified mesembryanthemums under the carpetweed family.
The carpetweed family includes both ornamental and weedy plant varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aizoaceae - the scientific name for the carpetweed family
