mountain watercress

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn ˈwɔtərˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən ˈwɔːtəkres / (BrE)
a mat-forming perennial plant found in cold springs of the eastern United States, known for its small, edible leaves
a U.S. aquatic plant with edible leaves

Examples

Streams in the Appalachian region are often lined with mountain watercress.
People sometimes use mountain watercress as a garnish in salads.
Mountain watercress thrives best in the cool, clear waters of elevated springs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - an aquatic plant with pungent edible leaves
