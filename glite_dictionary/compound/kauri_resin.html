kauri resin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊri ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəri ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a pale, hard resin obtained from kauri trees of New Zealand, often found as a fossil and used in making varnishes and linoleum
resin from New Zealand kauri trees

Examples

The museum showcased an ancient piece of kauri resin excavated from a New Zealand forest.
Artisans in the 19th century prized kauri resin for its use in high-quality varnishes.
Chunks of fossilized kauri resin are sometimes collected by beachcombers along the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum - a resinous substance of plant origin

Etymology

From Māori 'kauri' (the tree Agathis australis) + English 'resin'.
