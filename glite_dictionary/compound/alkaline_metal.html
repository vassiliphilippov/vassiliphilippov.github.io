alkaline metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌlaɪn ˈmɛt.əl / (AmE), / ˈal.kəˌlaɪn ˈmɛt.əl / (BrE)
any of the soft, highly reactive monovalent metals in Group I of the periodic table, including lithium, sodium, potassium, rubidium, cesium, and francium
group I reactive metal (chemistry)

Examples

Sodium is an alkaline metal frequently used in chemical reactions.
All alkaline metals readily lose one electron to form positive ions.
Francium is the rarest naturally occurring alkaline metal on Earth.

Synset Examples

the hydroxides of the alkali metals are strongly alkaline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkali metal - group I elements; alternative term more commonly used

Etymology

Formed from 'alkaline' (referring to basic properties) and 'metal' (elemental substance).
