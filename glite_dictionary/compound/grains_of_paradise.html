grains of paradise

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪnz əv ˈpærəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / ɡreɪnz əv ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
a West African plant (Aframomum melegueta) whose pungent peppery seeds are used as a spice
West African plant with peppery seeds

Examples

In medieval Europe, grains of paradise were used as a substitute for black pepper.
The chef added a pinch of grains of paradise to the stew for a unique, spicy flavor.
Today, grains of paradise are popular in craft gin recipes for their aromatic qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melegueta pepper - the pungent seeds of Aframomum melegueta, used as a spice

Etymology

From 'grain' plus 'paradise', referring to its exotic, valuable seeds imported to Europe since the Middle Ages.
