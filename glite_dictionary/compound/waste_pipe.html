waste pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪst ˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈweɪst ˌpʌɪp / (BrE)
a pipe that carries liquid waste away, especially from a sink, bath, or appliance
pipe for carrying liquid waste

Examples

The waste pipe beneath the kitchen sink became blocked and needed to be replaced.
After installing the new dishwasher, a plumber fitted a waste pipe to carry away the water.
Make sure the waste pipe is securely attached so there are no leaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drainpipe - pipe that carries waste water or rainwater away
  • Antonym: supply pipe - pipe that brings water into a building or appliance
