Japanese carpet grass

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈkɑrpɪt ɡræs / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈkɑːpɪt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a type of lawn grass native to East Asia, commonly used in tropical and subtropical lawns for ground cover
tropical lawn grass species

Examples

Japanese carpet grass thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used for lawns in Southeast Asia.
The gardener recommended planting Japanese carpet grass because it requires little maintenance.
In some southern U.S. states, Japanese carpet grass is a popular option for low-growing ground cover.
