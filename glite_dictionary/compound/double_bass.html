double bass

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌb.əl ˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈbeɪs / (BrE)
the largest and lowest-pitched instrument of the violin family, typically with four strings and played with a bow or by plucking
largest, lowest-pitched violin family instrument

Examples

The orchestra's double bass added a deep, rich tone to the performance.
She carefully tuned her double bass before the concert began.
Many jazz musicians enjoy improvising on the double bass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrabass - another term for the double bass
  • Degree Higher: cello - smaller, higher-pitched member of the violin family
