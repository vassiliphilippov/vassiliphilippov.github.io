electronic balance

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
a device for measuring weight or mass that uses electronic components to generate a readout proportional to the displacement of its pan
electronic device for measuring weight or mass

Examples

The laboratory technician weighed the chemical samples using the electronic balance.
Before starting the experiment, make sure the electronic balance is properly calibrated.
Many pharmacies rely on an electronic balance to measure small quantities of medicine accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance - device for determining weight or mass by comparison
