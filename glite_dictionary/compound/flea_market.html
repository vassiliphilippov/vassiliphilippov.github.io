flea market

1. NOUN

/ ˈfliː ˌmɑːrkət / (AmE), / ˈfliː ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
an outdoor market where people sell inexpensive or secondhand goods, often from stalls or booths
open-air market for secondhand goods

Examples

We found a rare vintage record at the flea market last weekend.
Every Sunday, the flea market is bustling with people looking for bargains.
She loves browsing old books and antiques at the flea market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrift shop - a shop selling secondhand goods at low prices

Etymology

From the French 'marché aux puces' (market of fleas), referring to potential fleas in old goods.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfli ˌmɑrkət / (AmE), / ˈfli ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
an outdoor market where people sell secondhand goods, antiques, or inexpensive items
market for secondhand goods

Examples

Every Saturday, she visits the flea market to hunt for hidden treasures.
They bought a vintage lamp at a flea market near town last weekend.
You can often negotiate prices when shopping at a flea market.

Synset Examples

I found a rare postage stamp at the flea market.
She loves to sell bargains at flea markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrift shop - a shop that sells used goods, typically to raise funds for a charity

Etymology

Originally from French marché aux puces (market of fleas), referring to the idea that old items might have fleas.
