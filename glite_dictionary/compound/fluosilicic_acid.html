fluosilicic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌfluː.oʊ.sɪˈlɪs.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌfluː.əʊ.sɪˈlɪs.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
an unstable, poisonous, and corrosive acid (H2SiF6), mainly known through its salts and used in water fluoridation and electroplating
a poisonous, corrosive acid used in industry
medical

Examples

Fluosilicic acid is most commonly used in the fluoridation of public water supplies.
Laboratory staff must handle fluosilicic acid with great caution due to its highly corrosive nature.
When fluosilicic acid reacts with moisture, it releases toxic fumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrofluoric acid - a highly corrosive acid containing hydrogen and fluorine

Etymology

From fluosilicate + acid, indicating its composition of fluorine and silicon.
