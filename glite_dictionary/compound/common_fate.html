common fate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən feɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən feɪt / (BrE)
a principle in Gestalt psychology stating that elements moving or behaving similarly are perceived as belonging together as a group
Gestalt principle: grouping by shared movement

Examples

The Gestalt principle of common fate explains why we perceive a flock of birds flying together as a single group.
In visual perception, common fate causes us to group lines moving in the same direction.
Animations often use common fate to make certain objects stand out as cohesive units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proximity - Gestalt principle: grouping by closeness
  • Synonym: similarity - Gestalt principle: grouping by resemblance
