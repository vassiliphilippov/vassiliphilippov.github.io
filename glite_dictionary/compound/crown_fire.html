crown fire

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
A forest fire that spreads rapidly through the tops (crowns) of trees, often ahead of the ground fire.
forest fire spreading through tree tops

Examples

The intense heat caused a crown fire that raced through the treetops, threatening nearby communities.
Firefighters worked tirelessly to control the crown fire before it could leap across the river.
Studying the effects of a crown fire helps scientists understand how forests recover after severe wildfires.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ground fire - a wildfire that burns the organic material beneath the surface litter of the forest floor
