family Gerridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌdʒɛrɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌdʒɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as water striders, which are able to walk on the surface of water.
family of water strider insects

Examples

The family Gerridae consists of insects commonly found skating across ponds and streams.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Gerridae in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the family Gerridae have specialized legs that allow them to move effortlessly on water surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water strider - an insect able to walk on water; member of family Gerridae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gerridae', based on type genus 'Gerris', named using Greek 'gerron' (reed, raft).
