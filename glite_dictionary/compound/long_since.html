long since

1. ADV

/ lɔŋ sɪns / (AmE), / lɒŋ sɪns / (BrE)
a long time ago; at a distant or comparatively distant time in the past
a long time ago

Examples

He has long since moved away from the city where he grew up.
The tradition has long since disappeared in this region.
She realized she had long since forgotten the details of that day.

Synset Examples

2. ADV

/ lɔŋ sɪns / (AmE), / lɒŋ sɪns / (BrE)
a long time ago; at or before a time in the past that is now distant
a long time ago; long before now

Examples

They had long since accepted the outcome and moved on with their lives.
Her worries about the exam had long since faded by the time the results arrived.
The factory has long since closed and now stands abandoned.

Synset Examples

They had long since moved on from that issue.
It has long since become clear that change is necessary.
He had long since forgotten about the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long ago - a long time ago; in the distant past
