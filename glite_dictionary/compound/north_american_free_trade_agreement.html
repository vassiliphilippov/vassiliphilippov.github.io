North American Free Trade Agreement

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ əˈmɛrɪkən friː treɪd əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / nɔːθ əˈmɛrɪkən friː treɪd əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
an agreement established in 1994 to promote free trade between the United States, Canada, and Mexico
trade agreement between the US, Canada, and Mexico

Examples

The North American Free Trade Agreement significantly increased trade among the three participating countries.
Debate over the North American Free Trade Agreement was prominent in political discussions during the 1990s.
After the North American Free Trade Agreement took effect, cross-border business investments grew rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NAFTA - abbreviation for North American Free Trade Agreement
