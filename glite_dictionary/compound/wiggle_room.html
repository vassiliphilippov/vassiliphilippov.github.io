wiggle room

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡəl ruːm / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡl ruːm / (BrE)
flexibility or freedom to make changes or adjustments to plans, interpretations, or agreements
flexibility or leeway for adjustment
informal

Examples

The new contract doesn't give us much wiggle room if problems arise.
She negotiated for extra wiggle room in the project timeline.
Without some wiggle room, even a small error could cause delays.

Synset Examples

the request left some wiggle room for future restructuring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leeway - extra space or freedom to move or change plans
  • Synonym: latitude - freedom to act or decide
