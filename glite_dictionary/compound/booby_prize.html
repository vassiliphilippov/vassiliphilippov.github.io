booby prize

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːbi ˌpraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbuːbi ˌprʌɪz / (BrE)
a prize given as a joke or consolation to the person who finishes last in a contest or competition
joke prize for last place

Examples

He finished last in the race and received the booby prize, a plastic trophy.
At the charity auction, the booby prize turned out to be a humorous rubber chicken.
Everyone laughed when the booby prize was revealed at the end of the trivia night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consolation prize - prize awarded to a non-winner to recognize participation

Etymology

From 'booby' (fool) + 'prize'; 'booby' from Spanish 'bobo' (stupid, fool).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuːbi praɪz / (AmE), / ˈbuːbi prʌɪz / (BrE)
a humorous or mock award given to someone who finishes last or performs poorly in a competition, often as a joke or consolation
joke award for poor performance

Examples

Getting a booby prize is better than going home empty-handed.
She laughed when she received the booby prize at the contest.
After finishing last in the race, he proudly accepted his booby prize.

Synset Examples

Getting a booby prize is better than going home empty-handed.
He won the booby prize for his terrible performance.
She laughed when she received the booby prize at the contest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consolation prize - a prize given to a participant who does not win but is acknowledged for their effort

Etymology

From 'booby' (meaning a fool or simpleton, possibly from Spanish 'bobo') + 'prize'.
