blue fig

1. NOUN

/ bluː fɪɡ / (AmE), / bluː fɪɡ / (BrE)
An Australian tree with hard white timber, glossy green leaves, white flowers, and small glossy blue fruits containing a single seed.
Australian tree with blue fruit

Examples

The blue fig is valued for its attractive blue fruit and strong timber.
Several blue fig trees were planted along the nature reserve trail.
Birds are attracted to the blue fig when its bright fruit ripens in summer.

2. NOUN

/ blu fɪɡ / (AmE), / bluː fɪɡ / (BrE)
the edible fruit of the Brisbane quandong tree (Elaeocarpus grandis), native to Australia and resembling a fig in shape
fruit of the Brisbane quandong tree

Examples

The blue fig is often used in jams and desserts in Queensland.
I tasted a fresh blue fig during my trip to Australia.
Some birds feed on the blue fig as a natural source of food.
