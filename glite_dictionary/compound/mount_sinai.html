Mount Sinai

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈsaɪnaɪ / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈsaɪnaɪ / (BrE)
a mountain in the southern Sinai Peninsula, traditionally regarded as the place where Moses received the Ten Commandments.
mountain in Sinai; site of biblical significance

Examples

Pilgrims from around the world travel to Mount Sinai to experience its religious significance.
The story of Moses receiving the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai is central to Judeo-Christian tradition.
Hiking Mount Sinai at sunrise offers breathtaking views of the Sinai Peninsula.
