posterior naris

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈstɪriər ˈnɛrɪs / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪərɪə ˈnɑːrɪs / (BrE)
either of the two openings at the back of the nasal cavity that lead to the nasopharynx
opening at back of nasal cavity
medical

Examples

A blockage in the posterior naris can lead to breathing difficulties in newborns.
The endoscope passed through the posterior naris to examine the nasopharynx.
Inflammation of the posterior naris may cause nasal obstruction.

Etymology

From Latin 'posterior' (behind, later) and 'naris' (nostril, of the nose).
