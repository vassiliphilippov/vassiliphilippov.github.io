Canada porcupine

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnædə ˈpɔrkjupaɪn / (AmE), / kəˈnædə ˈpɔːkjʊpaɪn / (BrE)
a large porcupine of northeastern North America, having barbed spines hidden in its coarse fur, known for gnawing wood for salt and grease
a porcupine species of northeastern North America

Examples

The Canada porcupine is known for its barbed quills, which it uses to defend itself from predators.
We spotted a Canada porcupine gnawing on the wooden porch in search of salt.
Unlike other rodents, the Canada porcupine often climbs trees to feed on bark and leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North American porcupine - another common name for the Canada porcupine
  • Degree Lower: porcupine - large rodent with spiny coat
