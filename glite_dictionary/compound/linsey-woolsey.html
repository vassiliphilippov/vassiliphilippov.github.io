linsey-woolsey

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪnzi ˈwʊlzi / (AmE), / ˌlɪnzi ˈwʊlzi / (BrE)
A coarse, durable fabric woven from a combination of linen and wool, traditionally used for clothing and household textiles.
coarse linen-and-wool fabric

Examples

She wore a linsey-woolsey skirt that had belonged to her grandmother.
During the colonial period, linsey-woolsey was valued for its warmth and resilience.
The museum displayed several garments made of linsey-woolsey to illustrate 18th-century fashion.

Synset Examples

He wore a linsey-woolsey coat during the chilly evening.
Her linsey-woolsey dress was both durable and stylish.
The costume was made of linsey-woolsey fabric for warmth.

Etymology

From Middle English 'lincy-wolcy', combining 'linsey' (from 'linen') and 'woolsey' (from 'wool').
