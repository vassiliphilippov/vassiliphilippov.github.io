ex-president

1. NOUN

/ ɛks ˈprɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ɛks ˈprɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
a person who previously held the office of president
former president

Examples

The ex-president was invited to speak at the international conference.
Security for the ex-president remains a high priority for the government.
Many ex-presidents write memoirs after leaving office.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: president - the elected head of a republic or organization
  • Antonym: incumbent - current holder of an office or position

Etymology

From the prefix 'ex-' (Latin ex-, meaning 'out of, former') + 'president'.
