incomplete abortion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəmˈplit əˈbɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈpliːt əˈbɔːʃən / (BrE)
a termination of pregnancy in which not all the products of conception are expelled from the uterus
termination of pregnancy with retained tissue
medical

Examples

After the miscarriage, an ultrasound revealed an incomplete abortion that required further medical attention.
Doctors monitor patients closely to ensure an incomplete abortion does not lead to infection or other complications.
Treatment for an incomplete abortion may involve medication or a surgical procedure to remove remaining tissue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: complete abortion - termination of pregnancy with expulsion of all products of conception
