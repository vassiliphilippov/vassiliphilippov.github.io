second growth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˌɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
an area of trees that has regrown after the original forest was destroyed by fire or logging
regrowth of forest after original was destroyed

Examples

After the wildfire, a thick second growth of pine slowly covered the hills.
Much of the region consists of second growth forests following decades of logging.
Ecologists study how second growth differs from original old growth forests in terms of biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: old growth - original, undisturbed mature forest
