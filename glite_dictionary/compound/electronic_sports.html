electronic sports

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk spɔrts / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk spɔːts / (BrE)
Competitive tournaments or activities in which players compete using video games, often involving organized teams, audiences, and prize money.
competitive video gaming

Examples

Professional gamers often compete in electronic sports tournaments for large cash prizes and international recognition.
Universities are increasingly offering scholarships to talented students in electronic sports programs.
The rise of electronic sports has transformed gaming into a mainstream spectator activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esports - shortened form of 'electronic sports', referring to the same activity
