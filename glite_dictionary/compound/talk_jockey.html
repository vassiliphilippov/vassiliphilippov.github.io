talk jockey

1. NOUN

/ tɑk ˈdʒɑki / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈdʒɒki / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who dominates conversations or speaks excessively, often preventing others from contributing
person who dominates discussions
informal

Examples

In every meeting, there's always a talk jockey who can't let anyone else get a word in.
Nobody likes being around a talk jockey who monopolizes every conversation.
She realized she was acting like a talk jockey and made an effort to listen more.

Synset Examples

He acts like a talk jockey, always dominating conversations.
Being a talk jockey can be exhausting for others.
The talk jockey of the group never lets others speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, often incessantly
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens rather than talks a lot

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'disc jockey' (DJ); 'jockey' meaning someone who manipulates or controls.
