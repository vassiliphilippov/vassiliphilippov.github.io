geriatric millennial

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛriˈætrɪk məˈlɛniəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛriˈætrɪk mɪˈlɛniəl / (BrE)
a millennial born in the late 1970s to early 1980s, often characterized by feeling somewhat out of touch with newer trends and technology
an older millennial born in the late 1970s to early 1980s
informal

Examples

As a geriatric millennial, he remembers life before the internet was everywhere.
Many geriatric millennials find themselves acting as a bridge between Gen X and younger millennials.
The article humorously described what it means to be a geriatric millennial in today's tech-savvy world.

Synset Examples

He identifies as a geriatric millennial, loving vintage tech.
As a geriatric millennial, she feels out of touch with trends.
Being a geriatric millennial means grappling with student debt.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: millennial - a person born between the early 1980s and mid-1990s to early 2000s

Etymology

From 'geriatric' (relating to old age) and 'millennial' (person born roughly between 1981 and 1996), often used humorously.
