neonatal mortality

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊˈneɪt̬l̩ mɔːrˈtæləti / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈneɪtəl mɔːˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the rate of death among newborns within the first 28 days of life
death rate in first 28 days of life
medical

Examples

Improvements in healthcare have lowered neonatal mortality rates in many developing countries.
Access to skilled birth attendants is crucial for reducing neonatal mortality.
The government launched a program to address high neonatal mortality in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infant mortality - death rate among children under one year of age
  • Degree Higher: child mortality - death rate among children under five years of age

Etymology

From 'neonatal' (from Greek 'neos' meaning new and Latin 'natus' meaning born) + 'mortality' (from Latin 'mortalitas', from 'mortalis' meaning mortal).
