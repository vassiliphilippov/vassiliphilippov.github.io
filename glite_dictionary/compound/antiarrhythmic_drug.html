antiarrhythmic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌænti.əˈrɪð.mi.ɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌænti.əˈrɪð.mɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug used to prevent or treat abnormal heart rhythms (arrhythmias)
drug treating abnormal heart rhythms
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed an antiarrhythmic drug after experiencing episodes of atrial fibrillation.
Doctors closely monitor patients taking antiarrhythmic drugs for potential side effects.
Some antiarrhythmic drugs can interact with other medications and require careful management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiarrhythmic - a substance used to prevent or cure irregular heart rhythms

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'arrhythmic' (irregular rhythm) + 'drug'.
