Atlantic herring

1. NOUN

/ əˈtlæn.tɪk ˈhɛɹ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ətˌlæntɪk ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
a species of herring found in large shoals in the northern Atlantic Ocean, important as a food fish
a food fish of the northern Atlantic

Examples

The Atlantic herring migrates in huge schools across the northern oceans each year.
Fishermen rely on the Atlantic herring for both local markets and export.
Smoked Atlantic herring is considered a delicacy in many coastal cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a silvery fish of temperate northern seas, eaten as food
