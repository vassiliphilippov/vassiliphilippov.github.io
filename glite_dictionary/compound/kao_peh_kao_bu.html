kao peh kao bu

1. NOUN

/ kaʊ pɛ kaʊ bu / (AmE), / kaʊ pɛ kaʊ bu / (BrE)
(in Singapore English) A person who complains incessantly or is overly dramatic, often in a critical manner.
incessant or dramatic complainer (Singapore English)
informal

Examples

Don't be such a kao peh kao bu whenever things don't go your way.
Her constant kao peh kao bu behavior made everyone avoid her.
The manager warned the staff not to act like a kao peh kao bu over minor issues.

Synset Examples

Stop being a kao peh kao bu and just enjoy life.
Her kao peh kao bu attitude annoys everyone.
He always complains, a real kao peh kao bu.

Etymology

From Hokkien, literally 'cry father cry mother', used in Singaporean colloquial English to describe someone complaining loudly or excessively.
