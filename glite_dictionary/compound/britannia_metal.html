Britannia metal

1. NOUN

/ brɪˈtæniə ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / brɪˈtænɪə ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a silvery-white alloy, similar to pewter, composed mainly of tin with antimony and copper, used for making tableware and decorative objects
silvery-white tin alloy for tableware

Examples

The teapot is made of Britannia metal, which has a lustrous finish.
During the 19th century, Britannia metal became popular for affordable household items.
Collectors often seek out antique cutlery crafted from Britannia metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pewter - a malleable metal alloy, traditionally composed of tin and lead

Etymology

Named after 'Britannia', the national personification of Britain, reflecting its British origin.
