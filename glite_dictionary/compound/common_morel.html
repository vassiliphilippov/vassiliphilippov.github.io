common morel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən məˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən məˈrɛl / (BrE)
an edible mushroom with a honeycombed cap, usually yellow or brown, highly valued as a delicacy
edible mushroom with honeycombed cap

Examples

Foraged common morel mushrooms are prized by chefs for their rich flavor.
The texture of a common morel is both delicate and slightly chewy when cooked.
He found several common morels growing under the old elm trees in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - a type of edible mushroom with a distinctive honeycomb appearance
