juniper berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːnəpər ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnɪpə ˈbɛri / (BrE)
the small, berrylike cone produced by the juniper tree, commonly used as a spice and in flavoring gin
small, berrylike cone of the juniper tree

Examples

Traditional gin gets its distinctive flavor from juniper berries.
The chef sprinkled crushed juniper berries into the sauce for a unique aroma.
Dried juniper berries are often used to season game meat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: berry - a small, juicy fruit without a stone

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːnɪpɚ ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnɪpə ˌbɛri / (BrE)
The small, usually bluish, berrylike cone of the juniper plant, often used as a spice and for flavoring gin.
edible berrylike cone of the juniper plant

Examples

The chef used crushed juniper berries to add a unique flavor to the dish.
Many gins are distilled with juniper berries as a key botanical ingredient.
Wild juniper berries can be foraged and used in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - a shrub or small tree of the genus Juniperus
