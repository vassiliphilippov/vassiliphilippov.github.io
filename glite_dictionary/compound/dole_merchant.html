dole merchant

1. NOUN

/ doʊl ˈmɜːrtʃənt / (AmE), / dəʊl ˈmɜːtʃənt / (BrE)
a person who profits from or exploits government welfare assistance, typically viewed negatively
someone profiting from welfare aid
informal british

Examples

The opposition leader described the company as a dole merchant exploiting government handouts.
Critics argue that some landlords have become dole merchants by relying on tenants' welfare payments.
The article accused her of being a dole merchant amidst cuts to social programs.

Synset Examples

In the debate, he labeled her a dole merchant for her policies.
The politician criticized the dole merchant for exploiting the system.
She accused him of being a dole merchant who profits from others' misfortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welfare cheat - a person who obtains welfare benefits fraudulently or dishonestly
  • Degree Lower: beneficiary - a person who receives benefits, especially from government welfare

Etymology

From 'dole' (British informal for welfare payments) + 'merchant' (a person who deals in something).
