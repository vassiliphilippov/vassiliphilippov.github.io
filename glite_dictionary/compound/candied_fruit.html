candied fruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændid fruːt / (AmE), / ˈkændid fruːt / (BrE)
Fruit that has been cooked in sugar syrup and coated with sugar crystals, used as a sweet treat or ingredient in baking.
fruit preserved with sugar

Examples

She used chopped candied fruit to decorate the top of the cake.
A traditional fruitcake often contains a mix of raisins and candied fruit.
You can find packages of candied fruit in specialty baking stores around the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glacé fruit - fruit coated in sugar syrup, similar to candied fruit
  • Antonym: dried fruit - fruit preserved by drying, not using sugar syrup
