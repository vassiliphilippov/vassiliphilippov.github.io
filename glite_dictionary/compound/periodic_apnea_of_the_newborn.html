periodic apnea of the newborn

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈɹiː.ə.dɪk ˈæp.ni.ə əv ðə ˈnuːˌbɔɹn / (AmE), / pɪˈɹɪː.ə.dɪk ˈæp.nɪ.ə əv ðə ˈnjuːˌbɔːn / (BrE)
A medical condition in newborns characterized by irregular breathing, with intervals of rapid breathing followed by temporary cessation of breathing; thought to be linked to sudden infant death syndrome.
irregular breathing condition in newborns
medical

Examples

Doctors closely monitor infants with periodic apnea of the newborn for any signs of distress.
Parents are often taught how to respond if periodic apnea of the newborn occurs at home.
Research has explored the relationship between periodic apnea of the newborn and sudden infant death syndrome.
