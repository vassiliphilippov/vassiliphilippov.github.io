provitamin A

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.vaɪˌtæm.ɪn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.vaɪˌtæm.ɪn ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A substance, especially beta-carotene, found in plants that the body can convert into vitamin A.
precursor of vitamin A in plants
medical

Examples

Carrots are a rich source of provitamin A, which the body converts into vitamin A.
The nutritionist recommended foods high in provitamin A to improve eye health.
Spinach contains significant amounts of provitamin A in the form of beta-carotene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta-carotene - a strongly colored red-orange pigment abundant in plants and fruit; the most common provitamin A
  • Degree Higher: vitamin A - a fat-soluble vitamin important for vision and immune function

Etymology

Formed from 'pro-' (before) and 'vitamin A,' indicating a precursor to vitamin A.
