white squire

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈskwaɪər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈskaɪə / (BrE)
An investor or company that acquires a minority stake in a target company to help it avoid a hostile takeover, without gaining control.
minority investor defending against takeovers

Examples

When faced with a hostile bid, the board sought the help of a white squire to secure its independence.
The white squire purchased just enough shares to block the takeover attempt.
Several potential white squires were approached to support the beleaguered company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white knight - an investor who rescues a company from a hostile takeover by acquiring a controlling interest

Etymology

Modelled after the term 'white knight'; 'squire' here implies a lesser role than a 'knight'.
