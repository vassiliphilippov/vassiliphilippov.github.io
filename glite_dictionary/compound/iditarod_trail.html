Iditarod Trail

1. PROPN

/ ˌaɪ.dɪˌtɑː.rɑd ˈtreɪl / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.dɪˌtɑː.rɒd ˈtreɪl / (BrE)
a historic trail in Alaska, extending about 1,100 miles from Anchorage to Nome, famous as the route of the annual Iditarod dogsled race
historic trail in Alaska; route of the Iditarod race

Examples

Every March, mushers and their dog teams race along the Iditarod Trail through the harsh Alaskan wilderness.
The Iditarod Trail was originally used by Native Alaskans and gold prospectors.
Tourists can hike certain sections of the historic Iditarod Trail during the summer months.

Etymology

Iditarod from a Deg Hit’an (Alaska Athabascan) place name; became famous through the sled dog race.
