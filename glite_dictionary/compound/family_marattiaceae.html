family Marattiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli mərˌætiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli məˌrætɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical ferns with very large fronds, belonging to the order Marattiales
family of large, tropical ferns

Examples

The family Marattiaceae includes some of the largest and most ancient fern species known to science.
Botanists have discovered new species within the family Marattiaceae in remote tropical forests.
Many members of the family Marattiaceae have distinctive, thick stalks supporting their massive fronds.

Etymology

From the genus name Marattia (named after Italian botanist Giovanni Francesco Maratti) + New Latin suffix -aceae denoting plant families.
