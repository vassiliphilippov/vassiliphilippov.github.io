harpy eagle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrpi ˌiɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpi ˌiːɡl / (BrE)
a large black-and-white crested eagle native to the tropical forests of Central and South America
large crested eagle of tropical America

Examples

The harpy eagle is known for its striking feathered crest and powerful talons.
Researchers spotted a nesting harpy eagle high in the rain forest canopy.
Few birds in the Amazon are as impressive as the harpy eagle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagle - large bird of prey with a hooked beak and broad wings

Etymology

From 'harpy', a mythological creature, due to its fierce appearance, and 'eagle'.
