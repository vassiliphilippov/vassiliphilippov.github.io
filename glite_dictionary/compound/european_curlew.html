European curlew

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈkɜrlju / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpi.ən ˈkɜːljuː / (BrE)
A large wading bird (Numenius arquata) native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its long curved bill and distinctive call.
large Eurasian wading bird with curved bill

Examples

We watched a European curlew probing the mudflats for food at dawn.
The unique, haunting call of the European curlew echoed across the marshes.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the European curlew in several countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curlew - a wading bird with a long down-curved bill
