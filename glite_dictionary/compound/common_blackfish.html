common blackfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈblækˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈblækˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, dark-colored whale found off the Atlantic coast of the United States, with the largest male often acting as the school leader
a small, dark Atlantic whale

Examples

Many tourists hope to spot a common blackfish during their boat tours along the coast.
The pod of common blackfish moved swiftly through the waves, led by a large male.
Scientists are studying the social behavior of the common blackfish in the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot whale - large, dark-colored oceanic dolphin, also known as blackfish
