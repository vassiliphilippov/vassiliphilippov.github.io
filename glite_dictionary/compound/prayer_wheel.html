prayer wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr ˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈpreə ˌwiːl / (BrE)
a cylindrical device, often inscribed or wrapped with prayers, that is spun on a spindle as a form of devotional practice, especially in Tibetan Buddhism
rotating Tibetan Buddhist devotional cylinder

Examples

Pilgrims spun the prayer wheel as they walked around the temple, reciting mantras under their breath.
The intricate designs on the prayer wheel reflected centuries of Tibetan craftsmanship and spiritual tradition.
She bought a small prayer wheel at the monastery to take home as a symbol of peace.
