new criticism

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈkrɪtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / njuː ˈkrɪtɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
a method of literary criticism that focuses on close reading and analysis of the text itself, independent of external context or authorial intent
literary criticism focusing on close reading of text

Examples

The professor encouraged his students to apply new criticism when analyzing poetry.
Many scholars believe that new criticism brought greater rigor to the study of literature.
Unlike historical approaches, new criticism concentrates solely on the text's language and form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close reading - careful analysis of a literary text's language and structure

Etymology

Formed in the early 20th century from 'new' + 'criticism', referring to a departure from earlier critical methods.
