summer savoury

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ ˈsævəri / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə ˈsævəri / (BrE)
an aromatic herb (Satureja hortensis) with delicately flavored leaves, often used to season food
a herb used for seasoning food

Examples

She sprinkled chopped summer savoury over the roasted vegetables for added flavor.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of dried summer savoury to enhance the taste of the soup.
In her herb garden, she grows summer savoury next to the basil and thyme.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: winter savoury - a related perennial herb used for seasoning

Etymology

From the combination of 'summer' and 'savoury', distinguishing it from 'winter savoury'. 'Savoury' from Old French savoure, from Latin satureia (the plant).
