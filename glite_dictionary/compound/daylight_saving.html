daylight saving

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌlaɪt ˈseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪlaɪt ˈseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of advancing clocks by one hour during warmer months to extend evening daylight
setting clocks forward to extend evening daylight

Examples

Many countries observe daylight saving to make better use of daylight during summer months.
Clocks should be set forward an hour for daylight saving this weekend.
Some regions have chosen not to implement daylight saving due to minimal seasonal changes in sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daylight saving time - the period when clocks are set forward by one hour
