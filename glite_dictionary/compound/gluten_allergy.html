gluten allergy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːtən ˈælɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɡluːtən ˈælədʒi / (BrE)
A condition in which the immune system reacts negatively to gluten, often causing gastrointestinal symptoms and other adverse effects.
allergic reaction to gluten
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with a gluten allergy, she switched to a gluten-free diet.
Many restaurants now offer options for those with a gluten allergy.
He carries a medical card explaining his gluten allergy when traveling abroad.

Synset Examples

He takes medication for his gluten allergy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celiac disease - an autoimmune disorder triggered by gluten ingestion
  • Degree Lower: gluten intolerance - a sensitivity to gluten without an immune response
