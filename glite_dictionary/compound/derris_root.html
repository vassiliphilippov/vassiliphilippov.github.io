derris root

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrɪs ruːt / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪs ruːt / (BrE)
the root of a woody vine from the genus Derris, used as a source of the natural insecticide rotenone
root of derris plant; source of rotenone

Examples

The powdered derris root was traditionally used to stun fish in Southeast Asia.
Farmers sometimes use derris root as a natural alternative to synthetic pesticides.
Botanists are studying the chemical compounds found in derris root.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotenone - a natural insecticide derived from certain plants

Etymology

From the scientific name Derris, a genus of plants in Southeast Asia.
