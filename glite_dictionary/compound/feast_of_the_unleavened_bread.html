Feast of the Unleavened Bread

1. PROPN

/ ˈfiːst əv ði ʌnˈlɛvənd ˈbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈfiːst əv ði ʌnˈlɛvənd ˈbrɛd / (BrE)
A Jewish festival, also known as Passover, observed for eight days from the 15th of Nissan, commemorating the Exodus of the Israelites from Egypt.
Jewish festival commemorating the Exodus

Examples

During the Feast of the Unleavened Bread, Jewish families eat matzah to remember their ancestors' journey out of Egypt.
Many traditions and rituals are observed throughout the Feast of the Unleavened Bread period.
The Feast of the Unleavened Bread is rich with symbolic foods and ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passover - A Jewish holiday commemorating the Exodus, also known as Pesach
