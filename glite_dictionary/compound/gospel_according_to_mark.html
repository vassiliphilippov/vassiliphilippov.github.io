Gospel According to Mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑspəl əˈkɔrdɪŋ tu mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl əˈkɔːdɪŋ tuː mɑːk / (BrE)
the second book of the New Testament and the shortest of the four canonical Gospels, recounting the life and teachings of Jesus Christ as recorded by Mark.
second book of the New Testament; shortest Gospel

Examples

The Gospel According to Mark provides a concise account of the life and ministry of Jesus.
Biblical scholars often compare the Gospel According to Mark with the other synoptic Gospels.
In church, the priest read a passage from the Gospel According to Mark during the service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel According to Matthew - the first book of the New Testament; a Gospel account of Jesus's life
  • Synonym: Gospel According to Luke - the third book of the New Testament; a Gospel account of Jesus's life
  • Synonym: Gospel According to John - the fourth book of the New Testament; a Gospel account of Jesus's life

Etymology

From Greek 'euangelion kata Markon', meaning 'good news according to Mark'.
