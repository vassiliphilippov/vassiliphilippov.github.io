order Isospondyli

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr dər ˌaɪ soʊˈspɒn dɪlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔː də ˌaɪ səʊˈspɒn dɪlaɪ / (BrE)
a former order of primitive teleost fishes with soft fins, including salmon, trout, herring, shad, sardines, anchovies, whitefish, smelts, and tarpon
former order of primitive teleost fishes
medical

Examples

The order Isospondyli includes species such as salmon, herring, and tarpon.
Research on the order Isospondyli has advanced our understanding of fish evolution.
Many commercially important fish, like sardines and anchovies, belong to the order Isospondyli.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: teleost - a large group of modern bony fishes

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) and 'spondylos' (vertebra), referring to their vertebral structure.
