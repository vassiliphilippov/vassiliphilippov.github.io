hand ax

1. NOUN

/ hænd ˌæks / (AmE), / hænd ˌæks / (BrE)
A prehistoric stone tool with a cutting edge, typically held in the hand and used for chopping.
prehistoric stone chopping tool

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a hand ax at the ancient settlement site.
The hand ax is considered one of the earliest tools created by humans.
He explained how early humans used a hand ax to process animal carcasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaver - a large, broad-bladed knife or prehistoric stone tool for chopping
