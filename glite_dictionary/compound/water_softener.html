water softener

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌsɔfənɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌsɒfənə / (BrE)
a substance or device that reduces the hardness of water by removing or replacing minerals such as calcium and magnesium
substance/device that softens hard water

Examples

We installed a water softener to prevent mineral buildup in our pipes.
The water softener needs to be refilled with salt every month.
Using a water softener can improve the effectiveness of soaps and detergents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard water - water with high mineral content
  • Synonym: soft water - water with low mineral content
