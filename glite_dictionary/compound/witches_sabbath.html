witches' Sabbath

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃɪz ˈsæbəθ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪz ˈsæbət / (BrE)
A supposed midnight gathering of witches to practice witchcraft and sorcery, often believed in medieval Europe to involve demonic rituals.
gathering of witches for sorcery

Examples

Many medieval accounts describe the witches' Sabbath as a terrifying nocturnal celebration.
In literature, the witches' Sabbath is often portrayed as a scene of chaos and supernatural power.
The villagers believed that a witches' Sabbath had taken place deep in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magical skills or abilities
  • Degree Lower: witch-hunt - an attempt to find and punish people whose opinions are unpopular and who are said to behave like witches

Etymology

From French 'sabbat', derived from Latin 'sabbatum' (Sabbath), alluding to subversive or heretical gatherings contrary to the Christian Sabbath.
