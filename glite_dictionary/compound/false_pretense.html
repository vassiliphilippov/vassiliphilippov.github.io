false pretense

1. NOUN

/ fɔls prɪˈtɛns / (AmE), / fɔːls prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
(Law) The crime of deliberately misrepresenting a fact with the intention to deceive and thereby obtain property or money.
crime of deception for gain
legal

Examples

The suspect was charged with false pretense after convincing the victim to transfer money based on a lie.
Obtaining goods by false pretense is considered a criminal offense in many jurisdictions.
He used false pretense to gain access to confidential company information.
