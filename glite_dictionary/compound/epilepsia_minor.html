epilepsia minor

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɛp.si.ə ˈmaɪ.nɚ / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɛp.si.ə ˈmaɪ.nə / (BrE)
A type of seizure of short duration, characterized by brief unconsciousness and local muscle spasms or twitching.
brief seizure with unconsciousness and minor spasms
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with epilepsia minor after observing the brief seizure episode.
Patients experiencing epilepsia minor often have momentary lapses in awareness without convulsions.
Research into epilepsia minor has improved treatment options for those with absence seizures.

Synset Examples

the girl was frightened by her first petit mal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petit mal - A mild form of epilepsy with brief loss of consciousness

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'minor epilepsy', with 'epilepsia' from Greek epilēpsia (seizure, epilepsy) and 'minor' meaning 'lesser'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsiə ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsiə ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
A type of epilepsy characterized by brief, sudden episodes of impaired consciousness and sometimes other mild abnormalities.
form of mild epilepsy with brief impaired consciousness
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her condition as epilepsia minor after observing several brief episodes of unconsciousness.
Children with epilepsia minor may experience spells of staring and unresponsiveness in class.
Treatment for epilepsia minor usually involves medication to control the mild seizures.

Synset Examples

she has been suffering from petit mal since childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petit mal - A mild form of epilepsy with brief lapses in consciousness

Etymology

From Latin: 'epilepsia' meaning epilepsy and 'minor' meaning lesser or smaller.
