chukka boot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʌkə buːt / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkə buːt / (BrE)
A type of ankle-length boot with two or three pairs of eyelets for lacing, often made of suede or leather.
ankle-high laced boot, often suede

Examples

He wore a stylish pair of chukka boots to the office party.
Brown suede chukka boots can be paired with both jeans and chinos.
She bought new chukka boots for the upcoming autumn season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert boot - a casual ankle-high leather boot with a crepe sole, modeled after boots worn in the desert

Etymology

Probably from the word 'chukka' in polo, meaning a period of play.
