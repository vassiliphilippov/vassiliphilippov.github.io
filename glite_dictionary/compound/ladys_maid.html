lady's maid

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz meɪd / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪz meɪd / (BrE)
a female servant whose job is to help a woman with her personal needs, especially dressing, grooming, and other tasks
a woman’s personal attendant

Examples

The duchess’s lady's maid assisted her in selecting a gown for the evening.
In Victorian novels, a lady's maid often played a crucial role in her mistress’s daily life.
Her lady's maid discreetly handled all of her wardrobe and grooming needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valet - a man's personal attendant who helps with clothes and appearance
