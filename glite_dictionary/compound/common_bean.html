common bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən bin / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən biːn / (BrE)
an annual climbing or bushy plant (Phaseolus vulgaris) grown for its edible seeds or pods, widely cultivated as a food crop
edible bean plant (Phaseolus vulgaris)

Examples

Many farmers grow common bean as a staple crop because it is high in protein.
The common bean can be found in a variety of colors and shapes, including black, red, and white.
After harvesting, the common beans are dried and stored for use throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney bean - a common variety of common bean with kidney-shaped seeds
  • Antonym: scarlet runner bean - a different bean species grown for its seeds and flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A bean plant of the species Phaseolus vulgaris, grown for its edible seeds or pods, eaten fresh or dried.
edible bean (Phaseolus vulgaris)

Examples

The farmer harvested rows of common beans to sell at the local market.
Many traditional dishes use common bean varieties as a main ingredient.
You can eat common beans either fresh in salads or dried in soups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney bean - a type of common bean with a kidney shape, typically red or white
  • Synonym: string bean - a variety of common bean with long, slender pods
