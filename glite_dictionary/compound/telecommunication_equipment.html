telecommunication equipment

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləkəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (BrE)
equipment or devices used for the transmission of information over a distance, such as telephones, radios, or fiber optic cables.
devices enabling long-distance communication

Examples

Many businesses invest in advanced telecommunication equipment to improve connectivity across multiple locations.
The factory specializes in manufacturing telecommunication equipment for cellular networks worldwide.
Upgrading outdated telecommunication equipment is vital for ensuring reliable communication services.

Etymology

From 'telecommunication' (formed from Greek 'tele' meaning 'far' and Latin 'communicare' meaning 'to share') + 'equipment'.
