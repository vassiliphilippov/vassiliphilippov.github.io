body language

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑdi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process of communicating nonverbally through conscious or unconscious gestures, posture, and facial expressions
nonverbal communication using body movements

Examples

Her body language showed she was not interested in the conversation.
You can tell a lot about someone’s mood from their body language.
In interviews, positive body language can help make a good impression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonverbal communication - communication without the use of spoken language
