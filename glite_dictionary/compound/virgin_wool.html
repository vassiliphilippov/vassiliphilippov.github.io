virgin wool

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪn wʊl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn wʊl / (BrE)
wool that has not been previously processed, woven, or used, typically taken from a sheep's first shearing or directly from fleece.
wool not previously processed or used

Examples

Many high-quality suits are made from virgin wool for superior softness and durability.
The label stated that the cardigan was made from 100% virgin wool.
Purchasing virgin wool products supports sustainable and ethical farming practices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recycled wool - wool made from previously used or processed wool

Etymology

From 'virgin' (unspoiled, unused) and 'wool', referring to wool in its first, unused state.
