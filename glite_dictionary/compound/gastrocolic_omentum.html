gastrocolic omentum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˈkoʊlɪk oʊˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˈkəʊlɪk əʊˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
a part of the peritoneum that connects the stomach to the colon and covers the intestines
peritoneum connecting stomach and colon
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully lifted the gastrocolic omentum to access the pancreas.
Inflammation of the gastrocolic omentum can lead to abdominal pain and complications.
During the anatomy lecture, the professor highlighted the structure of the gastrocolic omentum.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastēr' (stomach) + 'kolon' (colon) + Latin 'omentum' (fatty membrane).
