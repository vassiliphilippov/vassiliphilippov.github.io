incense cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsɛns ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sens ˈsiː.də / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree of western North America with aromatic wood, reddish-brown bark, and foliage resembling that of a cypress
tall evergreen tree of western North America

Examples

The incense cedar is well known for its pleasant scent and durable wood.
Many California parks are home to the majestic incense cedar.
The reddish bark of the incense cedar makes it easy to identify in the wild.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsɛns ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌsɛns ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
an evergreen tree of southwestern South America, New Zealand, or New Caledonia, with glossy leaves and aromatic wood.
aromatic evergreen tree

Examples

The furniture was crafted from incense cedar because of its pleasing scent and appearance.
Walking through the forest, we smelled the distinctive aroma of incense cedar in the air.
The glossy, evergreen leaves of the incense cedar make it a popular ornamental tree.
