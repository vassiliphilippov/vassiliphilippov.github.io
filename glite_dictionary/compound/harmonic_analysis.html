harmonic analysis

1. NOUN

/ hɑːrˈmɑː.nɪk əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒn.ɪk əˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the mathematical study or process of breaking down a periodic function or signal into a sum of simple sinusoidal components, such as sines and cosines
mathematical decomposition into sine waves

Examples

Harmonic analysis is essential for identifying the frequency components present in a sound wave.
The mathematician specialized in harmonic analysis and its applications to signal processing.
Many engineering problems can be solved by applying harmonic analysis methods to periodic data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fourier analysis - mathematical technique for analyzing periodic functions using sines and cosines

Etymology

From harmony (in the sense of musical harmony) and analysis; reflects the decomposition of signals into frequency components.
