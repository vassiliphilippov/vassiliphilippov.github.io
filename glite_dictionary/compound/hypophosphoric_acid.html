hypophosphoric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊfɑːsˈfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəfɒsˈfɒrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline tetrabasic acid, H4P2O6, used in chemical analysis and as a reducing agent
a crystalline tetrabasic acid (chemical)
medical

Examples

Laboratory technicians prepared hypophosphoric acid for the experiment.
The reduction properties of hypophosphoric acid are useful in certain chemical reactions.
Hypophosphoric acid contains two phosphorus atoms per molecule.

Etymology

From hypo- (under, less) + phosphoric (from phosphorus) + acid.
