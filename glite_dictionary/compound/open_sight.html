open sight

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən saɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən saɪt / (BrE)
a rear gunsight with an open notch rather than a peephole or telescopic lens, typically used on rifles or handguns for aiming
gunsight with an open notch

Examples

The old rifle was equipped with an open sight, making it easy to aim quickly.
Many beginners learn to shoot using an open sight before moving to advanced optics.
He adjusted the open sight to improve his accuracy at the shooting range.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peep sight - gunsight with a small hole for aiming
  • Antonym: telescopic sight - sight with a telescopic lens for long-range aiming on firearms
