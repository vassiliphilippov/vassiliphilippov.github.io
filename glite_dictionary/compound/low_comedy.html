low comedy

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
Comedy that relies on slapstick, physical humor, and crude jokes rather than wit or sophisticated dialogue.
slapstick or crude humor genre

Examples

The film relies heavily on low comedy, with plenty of pratfalls and pie-throwing gags.
Some critics dismissed the play as mere low comedy, lacking subtlety or intellectual appeal.
Although often popular with audiences, low comedy is sometimes undervalued by scholars.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high comedy - comedy characterized by witty dialogue and sophisticated humor
