performative verb

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɔrmətɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːmətɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
A verb that names and enacts the speech act it performs, such as 'promise', 'apologize', or 'declare'.
a verb enacting a speech act

Examples

A classic example of a performative verb is 'promise' in 'I promise to help you.'
The professor explained how using a performative verb can both describe and execute an action in language.
'I apologize' is only a true apology if 'apologize' functions as a performative verb in the statement.

Synset Examples

It is for this reason that ‘apologize’ is called a performative verb, defined as a verb denoting linguistic action that can both describe a speech act and express it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech act - an utterance that performs an action, not just conveying information

Etymology

From 'performative' (from 'perform') + 'verb'; 'performative' coined in linguistic theory by J. L. Austin.
