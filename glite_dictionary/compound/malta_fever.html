Malta fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔl.tə ˈfi.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːl.tə ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by brucella bacteria, transmitted to humans from animals or animal products, and marked by fever, headache, and other flu-like symptoms
infectious disease from brucella bacteria
medical

Examples

The laboratory confirmed that the patient was suffering from Malta fever after testing the blood sample.
Historically, Malta fever posed a serious risk to farmers who dealt with livestock.
Pasteurization of milk greatly reduced the occurrence of Malta fever in the general population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brucellosis - infectious disease caused by brucella bacteria

Etymology

Named after Malta, where the disease was first extensively studied in the 19th century.
