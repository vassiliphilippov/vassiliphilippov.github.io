Abstract Expressionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbstrækt ɪkˈsprɛʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæbstrakt ɪkˈsprɛʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
a style of painting originating in New York in the 1940s, characterized by spontaneous, abstract forms and expressive brushwork, representing the first major American art movement to develop independently of European artists.
a 1940s American expressive abstract art movement

Examples

Many critics argue that Abstract Expressionism marked a turning point in the history of modern art.
Jackson Pollock is one of the most famous figures in Abstract Expressionism.
Her exhibition features several large canvases painted in the style of Abstract Expressionism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Action painting - a style of painting closely associated with Abstract Expressionism, featuring dynamic, spontaneous application of paint

Etymology

From 'abstract' (not representing external reality) and 'expressionism' (art that expresses emotional experience rather than physical reality).
