chaparral broom

1. NOUN

/ təˌpærəl ˈbruːm / (AmE), / ʃəˌpærəl ˈbruːm / (BrE)
a widely spreading evergreen shrub of the southwestern United States, with clusters of small flower heads in a leafy panicle
evergreen shrub native to southwestern US

Examples

The chaparral broom thrives in dry, rocky soils throughout Arizona and New Mexico.
Local gardeners often use chaparral broom as a drought-tolerant landscaping plant.
In spring, the chaparral broom produces clusters of small white flowers.
