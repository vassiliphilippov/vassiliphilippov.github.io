rise of papism

1. NOUN

/ raɪz ʌv ˈpeɪpɪzəm / (AmE), / raɪz ɒv ˈpeɪpɪzəm / (BrE)
an increase in the influence or authority of the Pope or the Catholic Church in political affairs
increased Catholic Church influence in politics

Examples

The historian discussed the rise of papism during the early modern period.
Some politicians feared the rise of papism might threaten national autonomy.
Debates about the rise of papism shaped religious policies in nineteenth-century Europe.

Synset Examples

The article criticized the rise of papism in modern politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papism - support for or allegiance to the Pope or the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From 'rise' (increase) + 'papism' (from French 'papisme', referring to support for the Pope).
