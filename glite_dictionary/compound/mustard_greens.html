mustard greens

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstərd ɡrinz / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd ɡriːnz / (BrE)
the leaves of certain varieties of mustard plant, eaten as a vegetable, typically cooked or steamed
edible leaves of the mustard plant

Examples

Southern cuisine often features mustard greens simmered with smoked meats for extra flavor.
She picked fresh mustard greens from her garden to add to the stir-fry.
The sharp, peppery taste of mustard greens sets them apart from other leafy vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collard greens - leaves of collard plants, eaten as a vegetable
  • Synonym: spinach - edible green leafy vegetable

Etymology

From 'mustard', the plant of the genus Brassica or Sinapis, and 'greens', edible leafy parts of plants.
