cheap shot

1. NOUN

/ tʃip ʃɑt / (AmE), / tʃiːp ʃɒt / (BrE)
an act of unfair or unsportsmanlike aggression, especially one that is illegal or unnecessarily violent
unfair or unsportsmanlike aggressive act

Examples

The referee gave him a penalty for the cheap shot during the final minutes of the game.
Reporters criticized the politician for taking a cheap shot at his opponent during the debate.
Fans were outraged when the player delivered a cheap shot after the whistle had blown.

Synset Examples

he called a penalty on them when the lineman took a cheap shot at the quarterback

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foul - an act that breaks the rules in sports
  • Antonym: sportsmanship - fair and generous behavior in sport

2. NOUN

/ tʃip ʃɑt / (AmE), / tʃiːp ʃɒt / (BrE)
an unfair or excessively critical remark, often directed at someone unable to respond or defend themselves
unfair or excessively critical remark

Examples

Her comment about his appearance was clearly a cheap shot during the debate.
The comedian took a cheap shot at the politician, making the audience uncomfortable.
He apologized afterward, admitting that his insult was nothing more than a cheap shot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: low blow - a remark or action that is unnecessarily hurtful or unfair
