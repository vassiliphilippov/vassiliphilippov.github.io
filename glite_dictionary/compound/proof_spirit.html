proof spirit

1. NOUN

/ pruːf ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / pruːf ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
A mixture containing 50 percent alcohol by volume at 60 degrees Fahrenheit, used as a standard for measuring alcohol strength.
mixture with 50% alcohol by volume

Examples

High-quality whiskey is often measured against the standard of proof spirit.
In the nineteenth century, proof spirit was commonly used in laboratory experiments.
The customs officer tested the liquor to see if it matched the strength of proof spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcohol by volume - the percentage of alcohol in a mixture, commonly used standard

Etymology

From 'proof', an old term for alcohol strength, and 'spirit', meaning distilled liquor.
