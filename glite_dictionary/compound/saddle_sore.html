saddle sore

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædəl sɔr / (AmE), / ˈsædəl sɔː / (BrE)
A sore or irritation, especially one caused by chafing from a saddle on a horseback rider.
sore caused by chafing from a saddle

Examples

After a long ride, she developed a painful saddle sore on her thigh.
Wearing padded shorts can help prevent saddle sores when riding for extended periods.
He treated his saddle sore with ointment and took a break from riding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chafing - skin irritation caused by friction

2. NOUN

/ ˈsædəl sɔr / (AmE), / ˈsædəl sɔː / (BrE)
A sore or lesion on a horse's back caused by an ill-fitting or badly adjusted saddle.
sore on a horse from saddle

Examples

The horse developed a saddle sore after a long day of riding with poorly adjusted tack.
Regular cleaning and proper fitting of equipment can help prevent saddle sores in horses.
The veterinarian treated the saddle sore with a medicated ointment to promote healing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall - a sore on the skin caused by rubbing or pressure, often on horses
