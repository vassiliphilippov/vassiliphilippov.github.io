wet work

1. NOUN

/ wɛt wɝːk / (AmE), / wɛt wɜːk / (BrE)
covert operations that involve killing or violence, especially assassination, typically carried out by a government or criminal organization.
covert operation involving killing or violence

Examples

During his career, he never spoke about the wet work he conducted for the agency.
The novel details the wet work required to eliminate political enemies.
They hired a specialist known for his efficiency in wet work.

Synset Examples

He was involved in wet work during his time in covert ops.
She knew that wet work was part of the job description.
The police suspect that the gang is involved in wet work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassination - the act of killing someone for political or religious reasons

Etymology

Originally Russian criminal slang, literally ‘wet’ (referring to being stained with blood), later adopted into English intelligence jargon.
