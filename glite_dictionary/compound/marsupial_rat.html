marsupial rat

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈsuːpiəl ræt / (AmE), / mɑːˈsuːpɪəl ræt / (BrE)
A small, sharp-nosed insectivorous marsupial that superficially resembles a mouse or rat.
small marsupial resembling a rat

Examples

The Australian marsupial rat feeds mainly on insects in the undergrowth.
Unlike true rats, the marsupial rat carries its young in a pouch.
Biologists studied the nocturnal behavior of the marsupial rat in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse - small rodent of the genus Mus
  • Synonym: rat - medium-sized rodent with a pointed snout

Etymology

Formed from 'marsupial' (from Latin 'marsupium', meaning pouch) and 'rat'.
