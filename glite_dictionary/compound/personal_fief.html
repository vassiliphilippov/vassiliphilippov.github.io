personal fief

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsənl ˈfiːf / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənl ˈfiːf / (BrE)
an area, organization, or sphere controlled as if it were one's private property, especially by someone in a position of authority
organization or area run as private domain

Examples

The new manager transformed the team into his own personal fief, making all decisions himself.
She ruled the office as a personal fief and brooked no opposition to her authority.
After years in charge, the governor treated the state budget like his personal fief.

Synset Examples

The boss ran the department as if it were his personal fief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fief - a territory or domain in feudal society, held on condition of feudal service

Etymology

From 'fief' (Middle English, from Old French 'fief', a feudal estate); 'personal' indicating individual ownership or control.
