yellow jack

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ dʒæk / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ dʒæk / (BrE)
A yellow flag hoisted on a ship to indicate quarantine, signifying the presence of disease aboard.
yellow quarantine flag on a ship

Examples

The ship raised a yellow jack as it approached the harbor, warning others of illness on board.
According to protocol, any vessel flying a yellow jack must remain isolated until cleared by authorities.
The sight of the yellow jack was enough to make nearby sailors nervous about a possible outbreak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow flag - the flag signifying quarantine on ships

Etymology

From 'yellow' (color of the flag) + 'jack' (nautical: flag or signal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ dʒæk / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ dʒæk / (BrE)
a species of fish (Caranx bartholomaei) found in the western Atlantic and Gulf of Mexico, recognized by its yellowish coloring
a fish species of the western Atlantic

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed a large yellow jack he had caught off the Florida coast.
Grilled yellow jack is a popular dish in many Caribbean restaurants.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of yellow jacks in the Gulf of Mexico.

3. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˌdʒæk / (BrE)
another name for yellow fever, a serious infectious disease caused by a flavivirus transmitted by mosquitoes, leading to fever, jaundice, and hemorrhage
yellow fever; mosquito-borne viral disease
medical

Examples

Some ports flew the yellow jack to signal an outbreak of disease on board.
The doctor suspected the patient had yellow jack after observing symptoms of jaundice and fever.
In the 19th century, outbreaks of yellow jack caused widespread fear and quarantines in southern cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow fever - a viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, causing fever and jaundice
