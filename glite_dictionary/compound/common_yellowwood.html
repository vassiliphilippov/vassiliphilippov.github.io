common yellowwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈjɛloʊˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈjɛləʊˌwʊd / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen tree native to South Africa, known for its yellowish wood and ornamental use.
South African tree with yellowish wood

Examples

The common yellowwood is prized for its attractive, dense foliage in South African gardens.
Furniture makers often use timber from the common yellowwood for its durability and color.
Conservation projects protect the common yellowwood, which is a symbol of South African heritage.
