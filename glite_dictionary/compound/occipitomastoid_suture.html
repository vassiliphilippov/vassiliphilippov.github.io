occipitomastoid suture

1. NOUN

/ əkˌsɪpɪtoʊˈmæstɔɪd ˈsuːtʃər / (AmE), / ɒkˌsɪpɪtəʊˈmæstɔɪd ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
the suture between the occipital and temporal bones of the skull, continuing from the lambdoid suture
anatomical suture between occipital and temporal bones
medical

Examples

The occipitomastoid suture is easily identified during a cranial dissection.
Damage near the occipitomastoid suture can affect structures at the base of the skull.
On X-ray, the occipitomastoid suture appears as a faint line joining the occipital and temporal bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lambdoid suture - the suture between the occipital and parietal bones of the skull

Etymology

From Latin 'occiput' (back of the head) + 'mastoid' (breast-shaped) + 'suture' (seam).
