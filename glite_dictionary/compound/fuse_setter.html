fuse setter

1. NOUN

/ fjuz ˈsɛtɚ / (AmE), / fjuːz ˈsɛtə / (BrE)
a device used for setting the time on mechanical fuzes of artillery projectiles
device that sets projectile fuze timing

Examples

The technician used a fuse setter to prepare each shell before loading it into the cannon.
Modern artillery crews rely on automatic fuse setters to ensure precise timing for detonation.
During training, soldiers learned how to operate the manual fuse setter correctly.

Synset Examples

Naval and anti-aircraft artillery started using analogue computers before World War 2, and these were connected to the guns to automatically aim them; they also had automatic fuze setters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - a safety device or component that ignites or sets off an explosive charge
  • Synonym: fuze - a device that initiates the explosion of a munition
