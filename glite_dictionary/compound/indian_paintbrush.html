Indian paintbrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈpeɪntˌbrʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈpeɪntˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
An erect perennial plant native to eastern and southern United States, known for its showy orange flowers.
flowering plant with orange blossoms

Examples

The Indian paintbrush brightened the meadow with its vibrant orange blossoms.
We spotted several Indian paintbrushes growing along the hiking trail last spring.
Gardeners appreciate the Indian paintbrush for its striking color and easy maintenance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈpeɪntˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈpeɪnt.brʌʃ / (BrE)
A wild plant of the genus Castilleja, having dense spikes of brightly colored, bract-covered flowers, often red or orange.
wild plant with brightly colored bracts

Examples

The meadow was covered in blooming Indian paintbrush and other wildflowers.
We spotted a bright red Indian paintbrush during our hike in the mountains.
Artists are often inspired by the vivid colors of the Indian paintbrush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Castilleja - genus of flowering plants known as Indian paintbrush
