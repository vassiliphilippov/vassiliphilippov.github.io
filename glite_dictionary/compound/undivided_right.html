undivided right

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈvaɪ.dɪd ˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈvaɪ.dɪd ˈraɪt / (BrE)
a legal interest in property whereby each co-owner has an equal right to possess and enjoy the entire property, rather than a physically divided share
legal right to enjoy whole jointly-owned property
legal

Examples

Each co-owner held an undivided right to the entire piece of land.
The concept of undivided right prevents any one tenant from claiming a specific portion of the property.
Under joint tenancy, all parties share an undivided right to use and enjoy the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint tenancy - co-ownership of property where parties have undivided rights
  • Synonym: tenancy in common - co-ownership with undivided rights but potentially unequal shares
