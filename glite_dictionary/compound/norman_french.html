Norman French

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔːrmən ˌfrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmən ˌfrɛntʃ / (BrE)
the variety of Old French spoken by the Normans in medieval times, especially influential in England after the Norman Conquest
medieval Norman dialect of Old French

Examples

Much of English legal vocabulary originates from Norman French introduced after the Norman Conquest.
The documents were written in Norman French, making them difficult for modern readers to interpret.
Linguists study Norman French to understand its influence on the English language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old French - the Romance language spoken in northern France from the 9th to the 13th century

Etymology

From Old French *normant* (Norman) + *françois* (French).
