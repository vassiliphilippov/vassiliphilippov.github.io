genus Heterocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhɛt.ə.roʊˈsiː.fəl.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhɛt.ərəˈsiː.fəl.əs / (BrE)
A genus of burrowing rodents, best known for the naked mole-rat, native to parts of East Africa.
genus of burrowing African rodents

Examples

The genus Heterocephalus is famous for including the unusual naked mole-rat.
Scientists study the genus Heterocephalus to learn about subterranean mammal adaptations.
Within the genus Heterocephalus, the naked mole-rat displays remarkable resistance to cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked mole-rat - A nearly hairless, burrowing rodent found in East Africa, notable member of genus Heterocephalus

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + kephalē (head), referring to 'different-headed'.
