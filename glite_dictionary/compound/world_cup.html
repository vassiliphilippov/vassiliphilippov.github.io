World Cup

1. NOUN

/ wɝld kʌp / (AmE), / wɜːld kʌp / (BrE)
an international soccer tournament held every four years, in which national teams compete to determine the world champion
international soccer tournament held every four years

Examples

Brazil has won the World Cup five times, making it the most successful national team in history.
Millions of fans watched the World Cup final live on television.
Hosting the World Cup can bring significant economic and cultural benefits to a country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: FIFA World Cup - the official name of the international men's soccer tournament
  • Degree Lower: European Championship - continental soccer tournament for European national teams
