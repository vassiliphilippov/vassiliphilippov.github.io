combat boot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmˌbæt buːt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.bæt buːt / (BrE)
a sturdy, lace-up boot that extends halfway up to the knee, typically worn by soldiers and often used as practical footwear in civilian life
military-style lace-up boot

Examples

He laced up his combat boot tightly before heading out on the hike.
The soldier's combat boot was heavy but provided excellent ankle support.
In recent years, the combat boot has become a popular fashion item.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: boot - a type of footwear covering the foot and ankle and sometimes the lower leg

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.bæt buːt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.bæt buːt / (BrE)
A sturdy, laced leather boot originally designed for soldiers, now also worn as a fashion item, especially by members of certain subcultures.
rugged, lace-up leather boot

Examples

He wore black combat boots with his jeans to complete the look.
Many soldiers are required to wear combat boots as part of their uniform.
Her collection includes several pairs of brightly colored combat boots.

Synset Examples

My daughter bought herself twenty eyelet combat boots and now she has to wake up thirty minutes earlier if she wants to lace them up before leaving for school.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: boot - a type of footwear that covers the ankle and sometimes the lower leg

Etymology

From 'combat' (meaning 'fight, battle') and 'boot'; originally referring to military footwear.
