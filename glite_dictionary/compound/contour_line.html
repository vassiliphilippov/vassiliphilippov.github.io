contour line

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.tʊr laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tjʊə laɪn / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map that connects points of equal elevation above sea level.
line on map for equal elevation

Examples

The hikers followed the contour line on the map to find an easy walking path up the hill.
Steep terrain is indicated by contour lines being close together.
Learning to read contour lines is essential for navigation in mountainous areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isoline - a line connecting points of equal value, especially on maps

Etymology

From French 'contour' (outline, from 'contenir' to contain) + 'line'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtʊr laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tjʊə laɪn / (BrE)
A line on a map that connects points of equal elevation above sea level, used to represent terrain features.
line on a map showing elevation

Examples

The contour lines on the map show the height of the land above sea level.
By following the contour line, hikers can maintain a constant elevation along the trail.
Closely spaced contour lines indicate steep terrain on a topographic map.

Synset Examples

The contour lines on the map indicate elevation changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isopleth - a line on a map connecting points of equal value
  • Synonym: contour - the outline representing the shape of something

Etymology

From French 'contour' (outline) and English 'line'.
