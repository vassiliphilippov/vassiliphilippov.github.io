palmy time

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːlmi taɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlmi taɪm / (BrE)
A period of prosperity, success, or flourishing; a time when things are going particularly well.
period of prosperity or success

Examples

He often reminisces about the palmy times of his youth when life seemed simple and joyful.
The company's palmy time ended with the economic recession of the late 2000s.
During the palmy time of the Renaissance, art and science flourished in Europe.

Synset Examples

He described his childhood as a palmy time full of adventure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heyday - a period of greatest success, power, or popularity
  • Antonym: decline - a period of deterioration or waning prosperity

Etymology

From 'palmy' (prosperous, flourishing), derived from palm as a symbol of victory or triumph.
