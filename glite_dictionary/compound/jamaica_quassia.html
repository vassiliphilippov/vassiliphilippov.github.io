Jamaica quassia

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈmeɪkə ˈkwɑːs.i.ə / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪkə ˈkwæs.i.ə / (BrE)
A West Indian tree (Picrasma excelsa) whose wood yields Jamaica quassia, used as a bitter tonic and insecticide.
West Indian tree yielding quassia drug

Examples

The wood of Jamaica quassia is often used to make a traditional herbal tonic.
Researchers are studying extracts from Jamaica quassia for their potential medicinal properties.
Bitter infusions from the bark of Jamaica quassia have been used as natural insecticides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quassia - a bitter medicinal extract from several tropical trees

Etymology

Named after Jamaica, where the tree is native, and 'quassia' from the genus Quassia, after Graman Quassi.

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈmeɪ.kə kwəˈsiː.ə / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪ.kə kwæsˈiː.ə / (BrE)
an extract obtained from the wood of Picrasma excelsa, used as a bitter flavoring and in herbal medicine, similar to the extract from Quassia amara
bitter extract from Picrasma excelsa used medicinally

Examples

Jamaican herbalists often use Jamaica quassia as a natural remedy for digestive problems.
The bitter taste in the tonic comes from Jamaica quassia extract.
Some traditional medicines incorporate Jamaica quassia for its alleged healing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quassia - bitter extract from the wood of several tropical trees

Etymology

Named after Jamaica, where the tree Picrasma excelsa is native, and quassia, from the Surinamese plant Quassia amara.
