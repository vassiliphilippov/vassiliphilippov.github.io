phthalic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈθælɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈθalɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline acid used in the manufacture of dyes, perfumes, and other chemicals
acid used in dyes and perfumes
medical

Examples

Many industrial processes require phthalic acid as a precursor for creating plasticizers.
Pure phthalic acid is a white, crystalline solid frequently used in the manufacture of synthetic dyes.
The laboratory ordered a new supply of phthalic acid for its ongoing research.

Etymology

From phthal- (from naphthalene) + -ic, indicating relation to naphthalene compounds.
