side-blotched lizard

1. NOUN

/ saɪd blɑtʃt ˈlɪzɚd / (AmE), / saɪd blɒtʃt ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
a small, spotted lizard (Uta stansburiana) native to arid regions of the western United States, known for a dark blotch on each side behind the front limbs
species of small lizard native to western US deserts

Examples

The side-blotched lizard is often seen basking on sunlit rocks in the Mojave Desert.
Researchers studied the mating strategies of the side-blotched lizard over several years.
Unlike many other reptiles, the side-blotched lizard is active during the heat of the day.
