disco music

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪskoʊ ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɪskəʊ ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
a style of popular dance music with a steady bass beat, prominent in the late 1970s, intended mainly for dancing at discotheques
1970s dance music genre

Examples

The club was filled with the lively sounds of disco music all night long.
Many classic disco music hits from the 1970s are still popular today.
She dressed in vintage clothing and danced to disco music at the retro party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funk - a style of danceable rhythmic music, especially popular in the 1970s
  • Degree Higher: house music - a genre of electronic dance music that evolved from disco
  • Degree Higher: pop music - popular music, especially commercially recorded music

Etymology

From 'disco', short for discotheque (French for nightclub), combined with 'music'.
