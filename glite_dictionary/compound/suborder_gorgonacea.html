suborder Gorgonacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈɔrdər ˌɡɔrɡəˈneɪʃiə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːdə ˌɡɔːɡəˈneɪsɪə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of soft corals, including red corals and sea fans, characterized by their branching, tree-like colonies.
taxonomic suborder of red corals and sea fans

Examples

The suborder Gorgonacea contains many spectacular sea fans found in tropical oceans.
Researchers studied the biodiversity within the suborder Gorgonacea to understand reef ecosystems better.
Red corals from the suborder Gorgonacea are prized for their beauty and ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Alcyonacea - Order of corals that includes soft corals and sea fans

Etymology

From Greek 'Gorgo-' (from Gorgons, mythological figures) and Latin taxonomic suffix '-acea'.
