spurge laurel

1. NOUN

/ spɝdʒ ˈlɔr.əl / (AmE), / spɜːdʒ ˈlɒr.əl / (BrE)
a bushy Eurasian shrub (Daphne laureola) with glossy, leathery oblong leaves and yellow-green flowers, often found in woodlands
Eurasian shrub with glossy leaves and yellow-green flowers

Examples

The woodland was thick with spurge laurel, its glossy leaves gleaming in the shade.
She planted a spurge laurel in her garden to add some evergreen color year-round.
Due to its toxicity, spurge laurel should be handled with care, especially around pets.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to both spurge (Euphorbia spp.) and laurel (Laurus nobilis); genus name from Latin *Daphne*, ancient name for laurel.
