genus Adansonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs æd.ænˈsoʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ad.ænˈsəʊ.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical trees known as baobabs, notable for their thick trunks and edible fruit, found in Africa, Madagascar, and Australia.
genus of tropical baobab trees

Examples

The genus Adansonia includes all eight recognized species of baobab trees.
Researchers are studying the ecological role of genus Adansonia in sub-Saharan Africa.
Fruits from the genus Adansonia are rich in vitamin C and are used in local diets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baobab - Tropical tree with a thick trunk and edible fruit

Etymology

Named after French botanist Michel Adanson.
