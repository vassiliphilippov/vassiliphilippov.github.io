common water snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈwɔtər sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈwɔːtə sneɪk / (BrE)
A nonvenomous snake of the western United States, usually found near water, sometimes classified in the genus Nerodia.
western US nonvenomous water snake

Examples

We saw a common water snake basking on a log by the riverbank.
The common water snake rarely strays far from a water source in its habitat.
Many people mistake the harmless common water snake for a venomous species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nerodia - Genus of nonvenomous water snakes in North America
