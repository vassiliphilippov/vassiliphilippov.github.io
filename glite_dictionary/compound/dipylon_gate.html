Dipylon gate

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɪpɪˌlɑn ɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪpɪˌlɒn ɡeɪt / (BrE)
the main entrance gate to ancient Athens on its western side, notable as the site near which a distinctive style of pottery was discovered
main western gate of ancient Athens

Examples

The famous Dipylon gate marked the principal entrance to ancient Athens from the west.
Many archaeologists have uncovered important pottery fragments near the Dipylon gate.
The style of vases found at the Dipylon gate is distinctive of the Geometric period.

Etymology

From Greek 'Dipylon' (double gate), referring to its dual gateways.
