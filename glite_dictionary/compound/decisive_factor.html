decisive factor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪv ˈfæktər / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪv ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a point, fact, or circumstance that conclusively determines the outcome of a situation or decision
something that settles a decision or outcome

Examples

Her skill was the decisive factor in winning the competition.
Price proved to be the decisive factor for most customers when choosing a product.
Weather conditions became a decisive factor in delaying the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determining factor - a fact or circumstance that shapes or influences a result
  • Degree Lower: contributing factor - a circumstance that adds to the result but doesn't alone decide it
