antigenic determinant

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈdʒɛn.ɪk dɪˈtɜr.mɪ.nənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdʒen.ɪk dɪˈtɜː.mɪ.nənt / (BrE)
the specific part of an antigen molecule that an antibody binds to
part of antigen that antibodies attach to
medical

Examples

The antibody specifically recognizes the antigenic determinant on the surface of the virus.
Researchers mapped each antigenic determinant to better understand immune responses.
Changes in the antigenic determinant can allow pathogens to evade detection by the host's immune system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epitope - the part of an antigen molecule recognized by the immune system

Etymology

From 'antigen' and 'determinant'; 'antigen' from Greek 'anti-' (against) + 'genein' (to produce).
