ketone body

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːtoʊn ˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈkiːtəʊn ˌbɒdi / (BrE)
any of a group of three compounds (acetoacetic acid, beta-hydroxybutyric acid, and acetone) produced in the liver as intermediate products during the breakdown of fats, especially when carbohydrate intake is low or in certain metabolic conditions
compound produced in fat metabolism
medical

Examples

During periods of fasting, the liver produces more ketone bodies to supply energy to the brain.
High levels of ketone bodies in the blood can be a sign of uncontrolled diabetes.
A ketogenic diet aims to increase ketone body production by restricting carbohydrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetone - a colorless, volatile, flammable liquid ketone
  • Synonym: acetoacetic acid - a ketone body formed during fat metabolism
  • Synonym: beta-hydroxybutyric acid - one of the three ketone bodies

Etymology

From 'ketone', a chemical compound, and 'body', referring to a distinct chemical entity.
