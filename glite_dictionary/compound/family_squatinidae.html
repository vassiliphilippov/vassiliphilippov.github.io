family Squatinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli skwɒˈtɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli skwæˈtɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of bottom-dwelling, ray-like sharks known as angel sharks, found mainly in temperate and tropical seas
a family of bottom-dwelling sharks

Examples

The family Squatinidae includes species commonly known as angel sharks.
Recent studies on the family Squatinidae have revealed new insights into their unique evolutionary adaptations.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect members of the family Squatinidae from overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angel shark - a common name for species in Squatinidae

Etymology

From New Latin Squatinidae, from Squatina (type genus), which is from Latin squatina (a kind of shark).
