field crop

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌkrɑp / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌkrɒp / (BrE)
a crop, such as grains, cotton, or hay, grown primarily for agricultural purposes other than fruits or vegetables
an agricultural crop other than fruits or vegetables

Examples

Cotton is a common field crop in southern regions of the United States.
After rotating his field crops each year, the farmer noticed improved soil quality.
Wheat and barley are typical examples of field crops grown in Europe.

Synset Examples

cotton, hay, and grain are field crops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain - the seeds of cereals used as food, especially wheat, corn, or rice
  • Antonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed
