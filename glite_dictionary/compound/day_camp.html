day camp

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ kæmp / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ kæmp / (BrE)
A camp for children that offers supervised activities and care during the daytime hours, but does not provide overnight accommodation.
camp for children during the daytime

Examples

My daughter attended a day camp last summer and loved all the crafts and games.
The local day camp runs from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. every weekday in July.
Unlike sleep-away camps, a day camp allows children to return home each evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer camp - a camp offering recreational and educational activities during the summer holidays
  • Antonym: overnight camp - a camp where children stay overnight, typically for several days or weeks
