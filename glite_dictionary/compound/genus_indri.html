genus Indri

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɪn.dri / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɪn.dri / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Indriidae, comprising certain large lemurs native to Madagascar.
type genus of Indriidae (lemur family)

Examples

The genus Indri includes some of the largest living lemurs in Madagascar.
Scientists have studied the vocalizations of the genus Indri to learn more about primate communication.
All members of the genus Indri are critically endangered due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indriidae - the biological family of large lemurs to which Indri belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (type, kind) and French 'Indri', the local Malagasy name for the animal.
