Shiga bacillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiɡə bəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / ˈʃiːɡə bəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
a bacterium of the genus Shigella, particularly Shigella dysenteriae, that causes bacillary dysentery in humans
bacterium causing dysentery
medical

Examples

The discovery of the Shiga bacillus revolutionized our understanding of infectious dysentery.
Doctors identified the infection as being caused by the Shiga bacillus after laboratory testing.
Proper sanitation can help prevent outbreaks of diseases caused by the Shiga bacillus.

Etymology

Named after Japanese bacteriologist Kiyoshi Shiga, who discovered the bacterium in 1897.
