to jiak kentang

1. VERB

/ dʒiːɑk kɛnˈtɑŋ / (AmE), / dʒiːæk kɛnˈtæŋ / (BrE)
(slang) To feel overly full or stuffed after a meal, especially from overeating.
feel overly full or stuffed after eating
slang

Examples

After finishing his third plate of food, he said he was jiak kentang.
Be careful not to jiak kentang during the buffet or you'll regret it later.
My aunt always tells us not to jiak kentang before dessert arrives.

Synset Examples

She always complains about feeling jiak kentang after dinner.
Eating too much makes me feel jiak kentang.
After the meal, he felt a bit jiak kentang.

Etymology

From Hokkien 'jiak' (to eat) and Malay 'kentang' (potato). Common in Southeast Asian English.
