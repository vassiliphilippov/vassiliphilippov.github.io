fipple flute

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪpəl fluːt / (AmE), / ˈfɪpəl fluːt / (BrE)
A tubular wind instrument played by blowing into a fipple mouthpiece, usually with 8 finger holes.
type of wind instrument with a fipple mouthpiece

Examples

The recorder is a well-known type of fipple flute used in schools.
She collects rare fipple flutes from around the world for her music studio.
Because of its simple design, the fipple flute is often recommended for beginners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recorder - a woodwind musical instrument with a fipple mouthpiece
  • Degree Higher: flute - a woodwind instrument blown across a hole, not using a fipple

Etymology

From 'fipple' (the mouthpiece part) and 'flute'.
