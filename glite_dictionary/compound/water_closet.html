water closet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌklɑsət / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌklɒzɪt / (BrE)
a toilet, especially as referred to in British English and in older usage
British term for a toilet
british

Examples

Please use the water closet down the hall if you need to freshen up.
The sign in the old hotel pointed to the nearest water closet.
He asked the conductor where the water closet could be found on the train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toilet - a fixture for defecation and urination

Etymology

From 19th-century English, combining 'water' (referring to water supply or flush) and 'closet' (private room).
