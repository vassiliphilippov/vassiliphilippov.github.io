principle of liquid displacement

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ˈlɪkwɪd dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ˈlɪkwɪd dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the scientific principle in hydrostatics stating that the volume of a body immersed in a liquid is equal to the volume of the liquid displaced by the body
scientific law in hydrostatics about fluid displacement

Examples

The principle of liquid displacement is fundamental to understanding why objects float or sink.
When designing submarines, engineers rely on the principle of liquid displacement to control buoyancy.
She demonstrated the principle of liquid displacement by submerging a stone and measuring the water overflow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archimedes' principle - physical law stating an object immersed in a fluid experiences a buoyant force equal to the weight of displaced fluid
