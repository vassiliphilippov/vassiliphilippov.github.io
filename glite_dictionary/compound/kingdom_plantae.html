kingdom Plantae

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋ.dəm plænˈteɪ.i / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋ.dəm ˈplæn.teɪ / (BrE)
the taxonomic kingdom in biology that comprises all living and extinct plants
the plant kingdom in taxonomy

Examples

Modern taxonomists place flowering plants as part of the kingdom Plantae.
The kingdom Plantae includes mosses, ferns, conifers, and flowering plants.
Algae were once considered part of the kingdom Plantae, but are now classified separately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - a living organism of the kind exemplified by trees, shrubs, grasses, ferns, and mosses
  • Antonym: kingdom Animalia - the animal kingdom in biological classification

Etymology

From Latin 'planta' (plant); 'kingdom' from Old English 'cyningdom' (realm of a king).
