Synoptic Gospels

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɑptɪk ˈɡɑspəlz / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒptɪk ˈɡɒspəlz / (BrE)
the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, and Luke in the New Testament, which describe events in Christ's life from a similar perspective
the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, and Luke

Examples

In biblical studies, the Synoptic Gospels are often compared to highlight similarities and differences in their narratives.
The parable appears in all three Synoptic Gospels, but not in the Gospel of John.
Scholars analyze the Synoptic Gospels to understand the sources behind each account of Jesus's life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel - the record of Christ's life and teaching in the first four books of the New Testament

Etymology

From Greek 'synoptikos' (seeing together); used since the 18th century to describe the similar view of these Gospels.
