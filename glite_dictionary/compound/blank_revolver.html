blank revolver

1. NOUN

/ blæŋk rɪˈvɑlvər / (AmE), / blæŋk rɪˈvɒlvə / (BrE)
a revolver designed to make the sound of gunfire without firing bullets, typically used for training or theatrical purposes
revolver firing blanks, not bullets

Examples

The director used a blank revolver to simulate gunfire during the play.
Police officers sometimes train with a blank revolver for safety reasons.
He was startled by the loud noise of the blank revolver at the start of the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starter pistol - a gun that fires blanks to signal the start of a race
