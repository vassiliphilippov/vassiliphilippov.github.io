thermodynamics of equilibrium

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜrmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪks əv ˌiːkwəˈlɪbrɪəm / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪks əv ˌiːkwɪˈlɪbrɪəm / (BrE)
the branch of thermodynamics concerned with the study and characterization of systems in thermal equilibrium
study of thermodynamics in equilibrium states

Examples

Much of classical physics relies on the thermodynamics of equilibrium to describe stable systems.
Advanced students learn how the thermodynamics of equilibrium differs from that of rapidly changing processes.
The thermodynamics of equilibrium provides the foundation for understanding phase transitions in materials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-equilibrium thermodynamics - branch of thermodynamics focused on systems not in equilibrium

Etymology

thermodynamics: from Greek thermē (heat) + dynamis (power); equilibrium: from Latin aequilibrium (equal balance)
