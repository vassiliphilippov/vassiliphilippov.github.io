hand clapping

1. NOUN

/ ˈhænd ˌklæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhænd ˌklæpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of striking the palms of the hands together repeatedly to show approval or admiration
applause by clapping hands

Examples

The crowd responded with enthusiastic hand clapping after the performance.
Her speech was met by loud hand clapping from the audience.
The children's hand clapping echoed through the hall as they cheered their friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applause - the noise made by a group of people clapping their hands to show approval
  • Antonym: booing - the act of making loud noises to show disapproval, often opposite of applause
