Lighthouse of Alexandria

1. PROPN

/ ˈlaɪthaʊs əv ˌæləɡˈzændriə / (AmE), / ˈlaɪthaʊs əv ˌælɪɡˈzændriə / (BrE)
an ancient lighthouse built on the island of Pharos at Alexandria around 285 BC, regarded as one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World
ancient lighthouse, one of the Seven Wonders

Examples

The Lighthouse of Alexandria guided sailors safely into the ancient port for centuries.
Historians consider the Lighthouse of Alexandria one of the greatest architectural achievements of its time.
Many legends surround the enduring light that once shone from the Lighthouse of Alexandria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pharos - another name for the Lighthouse of Alexandria
