Norman Conquest of England

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɔrmən kənˈkwɛst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmən kənˈkwɛst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
the invasion and occupation of England by the Normans in 1066, following their victory at the Battle of Hastings
the 1066 Norman invasion of England

Examples

The Norman Conquest of England dramatically changed the country's language and culture.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of the Norman Conquest of England on English society.
The Norman Conquest of England began after King Harold was defeated at the Battle of Hastings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Hastings - the decisive battle in 1066 leading to Norman conquest
