thysanuran insect

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈsæn.jʊ.rən ˈɪn.sɛkt / (AmE), / θaɪˈsæn.jʊ.rən ˈɪn.sɛkt / (BrE)
a primitive wingless insect belonging to the order Thysanura, commonly called a bristletail, characterized by a long, slender body and three long tail-like appendages
primitive wingless insect; bristletail

Examples

A thysanuran insect can often be found hidden under rocks and leaf litter in damp environments.
Scientists studied the molting process of the thysanuran insect to better understand primitive insect evolution.
The thysanuran insect is easily recognized by its three long, bristly tails extending from the abdomen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristletail - a primitive wingless insect with bristle-like tails

Etymology

From Greek 'thysanos' (tassel, fringe) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the bristle-like tail appendages.
