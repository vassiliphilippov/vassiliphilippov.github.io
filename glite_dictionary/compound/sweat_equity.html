sweat equity

1. NOUN

/ swɛt ˈɛkwəti / (AmE), / swɛt ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the increased value or ownership interest in a property or business earned by a person through their own labor, rather than by investing money
ownership value earned through labor

Examples

After months of painting and repairing the house themselves, they built up considerable sweat equity.
Many startups reward early employees with sweat equity in place of high salaries.
The renovation added value to the home, thanks to the family’s sweat equity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equity - the value of ownership in a property or business

Etymology

From 'sweat' (hard work) and 'equity' (ownership value), originally in the context of property and later business.

2. NOUN

/ swɛt ˈɛkwɪti / (AmE), / swɛt ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the value added to a project or business through a person's own effort and labor rather than financial investment
value from personal effort in a project

Examples

She gained a significant share in the company through sweat equity rather than cash investment.
By doing most of the repairs himself, Daniel built up a lot of sweat equity in the property.
Many startups reward founders with sweat equity for their hard work and commitment.

Synset Examples

He put in sweat equity to renovate the old house.
Investors love businesses that show sweat equity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capital - money or assets invested in a business

Etymology

From 'sweat' (physical effort) and 'equity' (ownership value), first used in the mid-20th century in business contexts.
