King John

1. PROPN

/ kɪŋ dʒɑn / (AmE), / kɪŋ dʒɒn / (BrE)
the King of England from 1199 to 1216, known for signing the Magna Carta in 1215 after conflict with the barons.
King of England (1199–1216); signer of Magna Carta

Examples

King John was forced by the barons to seal the Magna Carta in 1215.
The reign of King John was marked by the loss of English territories in France.
Many historians regard King John as one of the most controversial monarchs in English history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: King Richard I - King John’s brother and predecessor as King of England
  • Synonym: Magna Carta - a charter signed by King John in 1215, limiting royal power
