sash window

1. NOUN

/ sæʃ ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / sæʃ ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
a window made of one or more movable panels, or sashes, that typically slide vertically to open and close
window with sliding sashes

Examples

The old house still has its original sash windows with wooden frames.
We plan to restore the Victorian sash windows to improve ventilation and aesthetics.
She opened the sash window to let a breeze into the room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: casement window - a window that is attached to its frame by one or more hinges

Etymology

From 'sash' (window frame), itself from French 'châssis' (frame).
