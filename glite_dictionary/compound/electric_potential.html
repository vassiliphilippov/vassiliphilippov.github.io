electric potential

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
the amount of electric energy per unit charge at a point in a circuit, measured in volts
amount of electric energy per unit charge
medical

Examples

The electric potential across the capacitor was measured at 12 volts.
A difference in electric potential drives the flow of current in a circuit.
The concept of electric potential is fundamental to understanding electromagnetism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltage - electric potential difference, measured in volts

Etymology

From 'electric', relating to electricity, and 'potential', from Latin 'potentia' (power).
