sheep's sorrel

1. NOUN

/ ʃips ˈsɔːrəl / (AmE), / ʃiːps ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a small plant with arrow-shaped, pleasantly acid-tasting leaves, commonly found in dry places
small plant with acid-tasting leaves

Examples

We found patches of sheep's sorrel growing along the dry hillside.
The tangy flavor of sheep's sorrel makes it a tasty addition to salads.
Gardeners often remove sheep's sorrel as it can spread quickly in sandy soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - a plant with acidic-tasting leaves, often used in salads
