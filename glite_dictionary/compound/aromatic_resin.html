aromatic resin

1. NOUN

/ əˈɹoʊˌmætɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / əˈɹəʊˌmætɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
A natural, fragrant substance exuded by certain trees and plants, used in perfumery, incense, and traditional medicine.
fragrant tree sap or plant exudate

Examples

The ancient Egyptians used aromatic resin in their burial rituals and ceremonies.
Many perfumes are created using extracts from aromatic resin.
Frankincense is a well-known type of aromatic resin harvested from the Boswellia tree.

Synset Examples

The balsam tree is known for its aromatic resin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance from some plants or trees
