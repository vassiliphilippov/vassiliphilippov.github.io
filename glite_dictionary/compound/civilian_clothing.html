civilian clothing

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈvɪliən ˈkloʊðɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪliən ˈkləʊðɪŋ / (BrE)
ordinary clothes worn by people who are not members of the military, police, or other uniformed groups
ordinary clothes, not uniforms

Examples

The soldiers changed into civilian clothing before leaving the base.
After years in uniform, she preferred the comfort of civilian clothing.
During undercover operations, detectives wear civilian clothing to blend in.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniform - distinctive clothing worn by members of an organization
