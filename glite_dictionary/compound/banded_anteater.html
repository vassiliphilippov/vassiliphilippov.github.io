banded anteater

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪd ˈæntiˌitər / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd ˈæntiːtə / (BrE)
a small Australian marsupial with a long snout and strong claws, known for feeding on termites and nearly extinct
Australian marsupial, termite eater

Examples

The banded anteater uses its long tongue to extract termites from narrow tunnels.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of the endangered banded anteater.
Unlike true anteaters, the banded anteater is a marsupial native to Western Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numbat - a small termite-eating Australian marsupial
