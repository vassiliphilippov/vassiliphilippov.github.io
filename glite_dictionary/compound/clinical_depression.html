clinical depression

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪnɪkəl dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
a medically diagnosed mental disorder characterized by persistent and severe feelings of sadness and loss of interest, often requiring professional treatment
severe, diagnosable depression requiring treatment
medical

Examples

After months of struggling to get out of bed, Sarah was diagnosed with clinical depression.
Effective treatment for clinical depression often includes a combination of medication and therapy.
He spoke openly about his experience living with clinical depression to raise awareness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: depression - persistent feeling of sadness or loss of interest
  • Synonym: clinical anxiety - medically diagnosable anxiety disorder

Etymology

From 'clinical', relating to the observation and treatment of patients, and 'depression' (from Latin 'deprimere', to press down).
