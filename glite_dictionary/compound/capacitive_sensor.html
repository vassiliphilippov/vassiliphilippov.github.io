capacitive sensor

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpæs.ɪ.tɪv ˈsɛn.sɚ / (AmE), / kəˈpæs.ɪ.tɪv ˈsɛn.sə / (BrE)
A device that detects the presence or properties of objects by measuring changes in capacitance, usually to sense objects that are conductive or have a different dielectric constant than air.
device detecting objects via capacitance

Examples

The smartphone screen responds to touch using a capacitive sensor that detects the electrical properties of your skin.
A capacitive sensor is often used in industrial machinery for non-contact object detection.
They installed a capacitive sensor in the faucet to enable hands-free operation.

Synset Examples

Size and spacing of the capacitive sensor are both very important to the sensor's performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proximity sensor - a sensor that detects the presence of objects without physical contact
  • Degree Lower: inductive sensor - a sensor that detects metal objects using electromagnetic induction

Etymology

From 'capacitive', relating to capacitance (from Latin 'capacitas' meaning 'capacity'), and 'sensor', from Latin 'sentire' meaning 'to feel'.
