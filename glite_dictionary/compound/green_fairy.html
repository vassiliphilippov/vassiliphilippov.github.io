green fairy

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈfeəri / (BrE)
A nickname for absinthe, referring to the strong, green-colored alcoholic spirit.
nickname for absinthe (alcoholic drink)

Examples

Legend says that the green fairy inspired many famous artists and writers in 19th-century France.
Bartenders in Prague offer traditional rituals for serving the green fairy to tourists.
He ordered a glass of the green fairy, intrigued by its mysterious reputation.

Synset Examples

She enjoyed absinthe, often referring to it as the green fairy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absinthe - green anise-flavored alcoholic spirit

Etymology

The term refers to the green color of absinthe and the effects the drink was thought to have.
