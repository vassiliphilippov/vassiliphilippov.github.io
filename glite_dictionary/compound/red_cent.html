red cent

1. NOUN

/ ɹɛd sɛnt / (AmE), / ɹɛd sent / (BrE)
a very small amount of money or value; anything considered insignificant or worthless
something of very little value

Examples

I wouldn't pay a red cent for that old chair.
He hasn't contributed a red cent to the project.
Her opinion isn't worth a red cent to me.

Synset Examples

his promise is not worth a damn
not worth one red cent
not worth shucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plug nickel - a coin or thing of no value or worth
  • Synonym: damn - something of no value (in 'not worth a damn')
  • Antonym: valuable - of great worth or value

Etymology

Originally referred to the copper 1-cent coin of the United States, which had a reddish color.

2. NOUN

/ rɛd sɛnt / (AmE), / rɛd sɛnt / (BrE)
(informal) A very small amount of money; used especially in negative statements to mean no money at all.
a very small amount of money
informal american

Examples

After paying his bills, he didn't have a red cent left to his name.
I wouldn't lend a red cent to someone so irresponsible.
They refused to spend a red cent renovating the old building.

Synset Examples

She wouldn't give him a red cent after the betrayal.
I wouldn't spend a red cent on that overpriced item.

Etymology

From the color of the copper one-cent coin in the United States, which would appear reddish.
