Great Wen

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt wɛn / (AmE), / ɡreɪt wɛn / (BrE)
a disparaging historical nickname for London, especially in reference to its rapid urban growth and associated problems
nickname for London highlighting urban issues
archaic british

Examples

Many writers of the 19th century denounced the Great Wen for its squalor and overcrowding.
After moving to the countryside, he refused to return to the Great Wen.
The relentless expansion of the Great Wen was a concern among social critics of the era.

Synset Examples

He criticized the Great Wen for its congestion and pollution.
London is often referred to as the Great Wen in literature.
The Great Wen symbolizes the sprawling nature of urban growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: London - the capital city of the United Kingdom

Etymology

Coined in early 19th century by William Cobbett; 'wen' is an old word for a benign swelling or cyst, used metaphorically for London's swelling size.
