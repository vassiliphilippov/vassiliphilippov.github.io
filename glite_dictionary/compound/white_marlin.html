white marlin

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmɑrlɪn / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmɑːlɪn / (BrE)
a species of marlin found in the western Atlantic Ocean, known for its elongated body and distinctive white underbelly, growing up to about 180 pounds
a species of marlin in the western Atlantic

Examples

The fisherman was thrilled to catch a white marlin during his trip to the Caribbean.
A white marlin is prized both for its fighting spirit and its rarity among sports anglers.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of the white marlin to help protect its population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - a large game fish with a long body and a spear-like snout
