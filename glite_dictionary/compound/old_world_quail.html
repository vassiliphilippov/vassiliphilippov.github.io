Old World quail

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld kweɪl / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld kweɪl / (BrE)
a small game bird with a rounded body and short tail, native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, distinct from New World quail
small game bird of Europe, Asia, and Africa

Examples

The Old World quail is commonly found in grasslands across Europe and Asia.
Unlike the partridge, the Old World quail has a more rounded body and shorter tail.
Many hunters in Africa prize the Old World quail for its elusive nature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: New World quail - a small game bird native to the Americas, unrelated to the Old World quail
