pecan tree

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkɑn tri / (AmE), / pɪˈkæn tri / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to the southern United States and Mexico, cultivated for its edible nuts.
tree grown for edible pecan nuts

Examples

The old pecan tree in our backyard produces hundreds of nuts every autumn.
Farmers in Georgia often cultivate pecan trees for commercial nut production.
A squirrel darted up the pecan tree, searching for the ripest nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecan - the edible nut produced by the pecan tree

Etymology

From Algonquian *pakani* (a nut).
