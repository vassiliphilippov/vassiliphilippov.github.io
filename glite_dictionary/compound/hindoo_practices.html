Hindoo practices

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.duː ˈpræk.tɪs.ɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.duː ˈpræk.tɪs.ɪz / (BrE)
the customs and religious or cultural beliefs associated with Hinduism, especially as referred to in older texts using the archaic spelling 'Hindoo'
beliefs and customs of Hinduism (archaic spelling)
archaic

Examples

Many travelers in the 19th century documented their observations of Hindoo practices in India.
The debate addressed how colonial policies affected traditional Hindoo practices.
She researched ancient texts to better understand the origins of various Hindoo practices.

Synset Examples

He follows Hindoo practices as part of his heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindu practices - the customs and religious or cultural beliefs associated with Hinduism (modern spelling)

Etymology

‘Hindoo’ is an archaic variation of ‘Hindu,’ from Persian ‘Hindū’, referring to people of India, particularly followers of Hinduism.
