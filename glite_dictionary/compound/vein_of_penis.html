vein of penis

1. NOUN

/ veɪn əv ˈpiːnɪs / (AmE), / veɪn əv ˈpiːnɪs / (BrE)
a vein that transports blood away from the penis
vein serving the penis (anatomy)
medical

Examples

During surgery, the vein of penis was carefully preserved to maintain proper blood flow.
The doctor explained that injury to a vein of penis can cause excessive bleeding.
Ultrasound imaging revealed that the vein of penis was enlarged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dorsal vein of penis - the main vein running along the top of the penis
  • Antonym: artery of penis - artery supplying blood to the penis
