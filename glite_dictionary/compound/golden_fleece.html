Golden Fleece

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊl.dən ˈfliːs / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl.dən ˈfliːs / (BrE)
in Greek mythology, the fleece of gold sought by Jason and the Argonauts, kept by the king of Colchis and guarded by a dragon
mythical golden fleece sought by Jason

Examples

The quest for the Golden Fleece is a classic tale from ancient Greek mythology.
Jason assembled the Argonauts to retrieve the Golden Fleece from Colchis.
Stories of the Golden Fleece have inspired countless literary and artistic works.

Etymology

From Greek mythology; literal translation of Greek 'Chrysómallon Déras' (golden fleece).
