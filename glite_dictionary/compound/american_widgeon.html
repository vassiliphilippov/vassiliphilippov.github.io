American widgeon

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwɪdʒən / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwɪdʒən / (BrE)
a North American species of widgeon duck, the male of which is distinguished by a white crown
North American duck with white-crowned male

Examples

We spotted an American widgeon paddling quietly near the edge of the lake.
During migration, large flocks of American widgeons stop at wetland reserves across the Midwest.
The distinctive white crown makes the male American widgeon easy to identify among other ducks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widgeon - a type of dabbling duck
