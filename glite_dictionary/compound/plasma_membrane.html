plasma membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæz.mə məˈmɝː.breɪn / (AmE), / ˈplæz.mə məˈmeɪn.breɪn / (BrE)
the thin, flexible barrier surrounding the cytoplasm of a cell, composed mainly of a double layer of lipids and proteins, which regulates the movement of substances in and out of the cell
cell's outer membrane

Examples

The plasma membrane helps maintain the cell's internal environment by regulating the entry and exit of substances.
Proteins embedded in the plasma membrane are essential for cell signaling and transport.
The scientist used a microscope to study the structure of the plasma membrane in plant cells.

Synset Examples

all cells have a cell membrane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell membrane - the membrane surrounding the cytoplasm of a cell

Etymology

From 'plasma' (biological fluid) + 'membrane' (from Latin membrana, skin).
