Goldie's wood fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldiz wʊd fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldiz wʊd fɜːn / (BrE)
a large North American fern with a blackish, glossy stipe, commonly found in moist woodland areas
a North American fern species

Examples

The shaded hillside was covered with Goldie's wood fern and other native plants.
Botanists are particularly interested in Goldie's wood fern due to its robust size and distinctive stipe.
We spotted a cluster of Goldie's wood ferns along the forest trail.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist John Goldie (1793–1886), who first described the species.
