fluorescent screen

1. NOUN

/ fluˌɔːˈrɛsənt skrin / (AmE), / flʊˌɒrˈɛsənt skriːn / (BrE)
a transparent screen coated with a phosphor that emits visible light when exposed to X-rays or cathode rays
screen emitting visible light under X-rays or cathode rays

Examples

The technician placed a fluorescent screen behind the object to detect the X-rays passing through it.
Medical imaging often relies on a fluorescent screen to visualize internal body structures in real time.
He was fascinated by how the fluorescent screen lit up when exposed to cathode rays in the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphor - a substance that emits light when exposed to radiation
  • Synonym: scintillation screen - a screen used to detect radiation by producing flashes of light

Etymology

From 'fluorescent', from Latin 'fluor', and 'screen'.
