black magic

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk ˈmædʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌblæk ˈmadʒɪk / (BrE)
the use of supernatural powers or magic for evil and selfish purposes, often intended to cause harm
magic used for evil or harmful purposes

Examples

Stories about black magic have fascinated people throughout history.
He claimed that his misfortune was the result of someone practicing black magic against him.
Many cultures have rituals intended to protect people from the harmful effects of black magic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magic, especially involving the supposed invocation of evil spirits
  • Antonym: white magic - magic used for good or benevolent purposes
