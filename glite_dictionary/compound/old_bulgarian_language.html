Old Bulgarian language

1. NOUN

/ oʊld bʌlˈɡɛriən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊld bʌlˈɡɛəriən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the earliest Slavic literary language, into which the Bible was translated in the 9th century, and used by Slavic peoples in the Middle Ages
early Slavic literary language, 9th century

Examples

The Old Bulgarian language played a key role in the spread of Christianity among Slavic peoples.
Linguists study texts written in Old Bulgarian language to better understand early Slavic cultures.
The Bible was first translated into the Old Bulgarian language in the ninth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old Church Slavonic - the first Slavic literary language, also known as Old Bulgarian

Etymology

Called 'Old Bulgarian' due to its close relation to the dialects in the Bulgarian region; known as Old Church Slavonic in linguistics.
