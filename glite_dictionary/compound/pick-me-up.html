pick-me-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk mi ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpɪk mi ˌʌp / (BrE)
A drink or substance taken to restore one's energy or spirits, especially an alcoholic beverage.
energetic or restorative drink

Examples

After a long flight, he needed a strong pick-me-up before his meeting.
A hot coffee in the morning is my favorite pick-me-up.
She offered him a quick pick-me-up to help him feel better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonic - A medicinal substance or drink that gives a feeling of vigor or well-being.
  • Synonym: restorative - Something that has the ability to restore health, strength, or well-being.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.mi.ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.mi.ˌʌp / (BrE)
a drink or other substance that makes you feel more lively, refreshed, or energetic, especially when you are tired or feeling low
something that invigorates or refreshes

Examples

After a long day at work, a cup of coffee is the perfect pick-me-up.
She reached for an energy drink as a quick pick-me-up before her meeting.
A short walk in the fresh air was just the pick-me-up he needed.

Synset Examples

she needed the pickup that coffee always gave her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
  • Synonym: tonic - a medicinal substance taken to give a feeling of vigor or well-being
  • Antonym: sedative - a drug or agent that calms or relaxes

Etymology

From the phrase 'pick me up', meaning to make someone feel better or more energetic.
