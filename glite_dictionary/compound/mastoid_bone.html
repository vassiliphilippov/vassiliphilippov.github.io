mastoid bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɔɪd boʊn / (AmE), / ˈmæstɔɪd bəʊn / (BrE)
the bony protrusion located behind the ear at the base of the skull, forming part of the temporal bone
bony part behind the ear

Examples

The surgeon made an incision behind the ear to access the mastoid bone.
Infections of the mastoid bone can lead to serious complications if left untreated.
She felt pain and swelling near her mastoid bone after the ear infection worsened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporal bone - the bone on the side of the skull to which the mastoid bone is attached

Etymology

From Greek 'mastoides' (breast-shaped) and Latin 'os' (bone).
