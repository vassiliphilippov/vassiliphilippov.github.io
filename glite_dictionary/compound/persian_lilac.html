Persian lilac

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrʒən ˈlɪlæk / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ˈlɪlæk / (BrE)
A tree native to northern India and China, with clusters of purple flowers and small, inedible yellow fruits; grown as a shade tree in the southern United States.
ornamental tree with purple flowers and yellow fruits

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful Persian lilac in full bloom near the entrance.
Birds flocked to the Persian lilac, despite its fruits being inedible to humans.
Many southern homes plant Persian lilac trees for their decorative flowers and ample shade.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrʒən ˈlaɪlæk / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ˈlɪlæk / (BrE)
a small, densely branching shrub native to Asia, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant lilac flowers
Asiatic shrub with lilac flowers

Examples

The Persian lilac blooms each spring, filling the garden with a sweet, floral scent.
Many gardeners choose Persian lilac for its attractive appearance and fragrant flowers.
She planted a Persian lilac by the walkway to add color and fragrance to her yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilac - a shrub with fragrant purple or white flowers
