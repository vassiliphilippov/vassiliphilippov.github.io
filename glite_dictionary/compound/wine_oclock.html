wine o'clock

1. NOUN

/ waɪn əˈklɑk / (AmE), / waɪn əˈklɒk / (BrE)
An informal time designated for relaxing and drinking wine, often at the end of the day or to mark the start of leisure time.
an informal time to drink wine
informal

Examples

After a stressful afternoon, it was finally wine o'clock and she poured herself a glass.
They joked that wine o'clock started earlier on Fridays.
At wine o'clock, friends gathered on the patio to enjoy a drink together.

Synset Examples

I always look forward to wine o'clock after a long day.
She declared it wine o'clock as soon as she got home.
It's finally wine o'clock; let's celebrate the weekend!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beer o'clock - an informal time to drink beer

Etymology

Formed humorously on the pattern of 'five o'clock,' using 'wine' plus 'o'clock' to indicate time for wine.
