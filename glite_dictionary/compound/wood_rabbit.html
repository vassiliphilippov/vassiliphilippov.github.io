wood rabbit

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈræb.ɪt / (AmE), / wʊd ˈræb.ɪt / (BrE)
a small North American rabbit with greyish or brownish fur and a tail with a white underside, serving as a host for certain tick species
small rabbit native to North America

Examples

The wood rabbit is commonly found in forests and woodland edges across North America.
Many predators in the region rely on the wood rabbit as a primary food source.
Researchers discovered that the wood rabbit often carries ticks associated with Lyme disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton-tail rabbit - common name for several species of North American rabbits closely related to the wood rabbit
