Ringer solution

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋər səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋə səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a sterile aqueous solution of sodium, potassium, and calcium chlorides that is isotonic to animal tissues, used medically to correct dehydration and as a medium in physiological experiments
isotonic saline for medical/experimental use
medical

Examples

The tissue samples were immersed in Ringer solution to keep them viable during the experiment.
After severe dehydration, the patient was given an intravenous drip of Ringer solution.
Researchers often use Ringer solution as a medium for studying isolated organs in vitro.

Etymology

Named after the British physiologist Sydney Ringer (1836–1910).
