moon shot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːn ˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈmuːn ˌʃɒt / (BrE)
the launching of a spacecraft intended to travel to the moon
launch of spacecraft to the moon

Examples

The Apollo 11 moon shot marked a historic achievement in space exploration.
Millions watched the live broadcast of the moon shot with anticipation and excitement.
Planning a moon shot required years of preparation and cutting-edge technology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmun ʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈmuːn ʃɒt / (BrE)
an extremely ambitious or innovative project or goal, often one that is difficult or unlikely to succeed
extremely ambitious project or goal

Examples

The company's plan to develop a flying car is a true moon shot in the tech industry.
Opening a school on Mars might seem like a moon shot, but some scientists are seriously considering it.
His proposal was dismissed as a moon shot, but it inspired creative thinking among the team.

Synset Examples

He proposed a moon shot idea to revolutionize renewable energy.
Launching a satellite to Mars is a real moon shot.
Their project is a moon shot, aiming for groundbreaking results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long shot - an attempt or guess with little chance of success

Etymology

From the U.S. space program's 'moon shot'—the Apollo missions aiming to land on the moon in the 1960s.
