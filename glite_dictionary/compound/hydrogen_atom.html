hydrogen atom

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən ˈætəm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ˈætəm / (BrE)
An atom consisting of a single proton and a single electron; the simplest type of atom, representing the element hydrogen.
an atom of hydrogen

Examples

The hydrogen atom is the simplest atom in the universe, often used as a model in quantum mechanics.
When a hydrogen atom loses its electron, it becomes a proton.
The emission spectrum of the hydrogen atom reveals important information about atomic structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: helium atom - an atom of helium, containing two protons, two neutrons, and two electrons
