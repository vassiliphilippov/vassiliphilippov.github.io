death wish

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ wɪʃ / (AmE), / dɛθ wɪʃ / (BrE)
an unconscious or unexpressed desire to die, often discussed in psychoanalysis and psychology
unconscious urge to die (psychoanalytic term)

Examples

The patient seemed to possess a death wish, engaging repeatedly in life-threatening behavior.
Some psychoanalysts attribute self-destructive tendencies to the unconscious presence of a death wish.
He denied having a death wish, though his actions suggested otherwise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thanatos - the death drive; the instinct towards death and self-destruction in psychoanalytic theory
  • Antonym: life instinct - the drive towards survival, propagation, and other life-affirming instincts; opposite of death wish in psychoanalysis
