Big Board

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ bɔrd / (AmE), / bɪɡ bɔːd / (BrE)
a nickname for the New York Stock Exchange, located at 11 Wall Street in New York City
nickname for New York Stock Exchange

Examples

Many investors watch the Big Board for signs of economic trends.
The Big Board experienced significant gains after the announcement.
He got a job as a trader on the Big Board right after college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York Stock Exchange - major stock exchange in New York City

2. NOUN

/ bɪɡ bɔrd / (AmE), / bɪɡ bɔːd / (BrE)
a large electronic or physical display board showing current stock prices, especially at a stock exchange
large stock price display at an exchange

Examples

Many traders watched the Big Board anxiously as the market fluctuated throughout the day.
The Big Board at the New York Stock Exchange is an iconic symbol of Wall Street.
Tourists often take photos of the Big Board when they visit the stock exchange.

Synset Examples

Investors gathered around the Big Board to check updates.
She studied the trends displayed on the Big Board.
The stock prices were flashing on the Big Board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticker tape - a device that prints stock prices as they are updated, formerly used in trading floors
