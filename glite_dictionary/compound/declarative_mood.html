declarative mood

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɛr.ə.tɪv mud / (AmE), / dɪˈklær.ət.ɪv mjʊːd / (BrE)
the grammatical mood used for making factual statements or declarations, usually not marked by special verb forms
grammatical mood for factual statements

Examples

Most English sentences are written in the declarative mood to convey information or facts.
"The sky is blue" is an example of a sentence in the declarative mood.
Teachers often ask students to identify whether a sentence is in the declarative mood or another form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperative mood - grammatical mood expressing commands
  • Antonym: interrogative mood - grammatical mood expressing questions
  • Synonym: indicative mood - another term for declarative mood, common in traditional grammar

Etymology

From Latin 'declarativus' (serving to declare) and 'mood' in the grammatical sense.
