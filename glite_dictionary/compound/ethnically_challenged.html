ethnically challenged

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛθnɪkli tʃəˈlændʒd / (AmE), / ˈɛθnɪkli tʃəˈlændʒd / (BrE)
humorous term referring to a person who lacks awareness of their ethnic identity or faces difficulties related to ethnicity or culture
lacking sense of ethnic identity (humorous)
informal

Examples

He described himself as ethnically challenged because he knew little about his cultural heritage.
At the international potluck, I felt ethnically challenged when I couldn't contribute a traditional dish.
Some people use the term ethnically challenged in a humorous way to admit their lack of cultural knowledge.

Synset Examples

He jokingly referred to himself as ethnically challenged in the diverse group.
She felt ethnically challenged during the cultural festival.
Some people might consider being ethnically challenged a privilege.
