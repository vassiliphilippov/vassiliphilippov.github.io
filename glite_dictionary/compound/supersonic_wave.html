supersonic wave

1. NOUN

/ suː.pɚˈsɑː.nɪk weɪv / (AmE), / suː.pəˈsɒ.nɪk weɪv / (BrE)
A wave that travels at a speed faster than the speed of sound in a given medium.
wave moving faster than sound

Examples

The supersonic wave from the explosion shattered glass windows a mile away.
Researchers studied the effects of a supersonic wave passing through different materials.
You could hear a distinct crack as the supersonic wave traveled through the tunnel.

Synset Examples

The supersonic waves created a stunning visual display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock wave - a sharp change of pressure in a narrow region traveling through a medium, often moving faster than sound

Etymology

From 'supersonic' (from Latin 'super' meaning 'above' + 'sonus' meaning 'sound') + 'wave'.
