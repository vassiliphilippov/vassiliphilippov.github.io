delusions of grandeur

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈluʒənz əv ˈɡræn.dʒɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈluː.ʒənz əv ˈɡrɑːn.djə / (BrE)
false or exaggerated beliefs that one is more important, powerful, or influential than is actually the case, often found in certain mental health conditions
false beliefs of great importance or power
medical

Examples

His delusions of grandeur made him believe he was destined to rule the world.
Patients suffering from delusions of grandeur often claim to have special powers or identities.
The therapist addressed her client’s delusions of grandeur during their weekly sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megalomania - obsession with the exercise of power, especially in the domination of others

Etymology

From French 'délire de grandeur', used in psychiatric literature.
