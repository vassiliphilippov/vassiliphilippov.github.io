order Palmales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər pælˈmeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə pælˈmeɪliːz / (BrE)
a botanical order that consists of the palm family (Palmae), including all species of palms
botanical order including palm trees

Examples

Within the order Palmales, there are hundreds of palm species found worldwide.
Recent studies have revised the classification of the order Palmales based on genetic evidence.
The coconut palm is one of the most recognized members of the order Palmales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Palmae - the palm family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Palmales', formed from 'Palmae' (the palm family) + '-ales' (Latin suffix for botanical orders).
