oh dark hundred

1. NOUN

/ oʊ dɑrk ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / əʊ dɑːk ˈhʌndrɪd / (BrE)
a humorous phrase used to refer to a very early, unspecified hour of the morning, especially before dawn
very early morning hour (humorous)
informal

Examples

We woke up at oh dark hundred to start our long road trip.
The team met at oh dark hundred to begin their training exercise.
She often jogs at oh dark hundred when the streets are empty.

Synset Examples

The alarm rang at oh dark hundred, time to get up!
We left the house at oh dark hundred to avoid traffic.
I woke up at oh dark hundred to catch my flight.

Etymology

Humorous alteration of military time expressions such as 'oh six hundred' (0600 hours), replacing the numeral with 'dark' to signify before dawn.
