risk of exposure

1. NOUN

/ rɪsk əv ɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / rɪsk əv ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the probability or likelihood of coming into contact with a harmful or infectious agent
chance of encountering a harmful agent
medical

Examples

People working in hospitals have a higher risk of exposure to infectious diseases.
Proper safety equipment can greatly reduce your risk of exposure to hazardous chemicals.
When traveling during an outbreak, you should consider your risk of exposure before making plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - the possibility of something bad happening
  • Synonym: exposure - the condition of being exposed to something harmful
