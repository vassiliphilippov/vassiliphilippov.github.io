Housatonic River

1. PROPN

/ ˌhuːsəˈtɑːnɪk ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌhuːsəˈtɒnɪk ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the northeastern United States, originating in western Massachusetts and flowing south through Connecticut into Long Island Sound
river in Massachusetts and Connecticut, USA

Examples

The Housatonic River is known for its scenic beauty and popular fishing spots in Connecticut.
Heavy rainfall caused the Housatonic River to overflow its banks last spring.
Local communities along the Housatonic River work together to protect its natural habitats.

Etymology

From an Algonquian language, possibly Mohican, meaning 'place beyond the mountain'.
