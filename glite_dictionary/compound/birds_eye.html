bird's eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdz ˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz ˌaɪ / (BrE)
a plant of the Old World, typically with axillary racemes of blue-and-white flowers, also known as speedwell
a plant with blue-and-white flowers

Examples

In the spring, the meadow was dotted with bright bird's eye flowers.
She planted a patch of bird's eye in her cottage garden for their cheerful colors.
The delicate blue-and-white petals of the bird's eye attracted many pollinators.
