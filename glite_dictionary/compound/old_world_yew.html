Old World yew

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɝːld juː / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld juː / (BrE)
a species of yew tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its longevity and slow growth
European species of yew tree

Examples

The ancient Old World yew in the churchyard is believed to be over a thousand years old.
Botanists studied the growth rings of a mature Old World yew to estimate its age.
Many traditional English gardens feature at least one Old World yew as a decorative evergreen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - an evergreen tree or shrub with red berries, of the genus Taxus
