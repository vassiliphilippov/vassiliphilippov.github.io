delta hepatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛltə hɛˈpaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɛltə hɛˈpatɪs / (BrE)
A severe form of hepatitis caused by infection with the hepatitis D virus, often occurring as a coinfection with hepatitis B.
severe hepatitis caused by hepatitis D virus
medical

Examples

Patients with delta hepatitis often experience more serious liver damage than those with other forms of hepatitis.
The risk of chronic liver disease increases when delta hepatitis occurs alongside hepatitis B infection.
Vaccination against hepatitis B can help prevent delta hepatitis in high-risk populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatitis D - liver inflammation caused by the hepatitis D virus
  • Degree Lower: hepatitis B - liver inflammation caused by the hepatitis B virus

Etymology

From Greek letter 'delta', denoting 'D', the type of hepatitis virus.
