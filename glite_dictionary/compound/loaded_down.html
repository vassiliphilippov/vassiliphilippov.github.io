loaded down

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊdəd daʊn / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪd daʊn / (BrE)
carrying or supporting a heavy physical weight or burden
carrying a heavy load

Examples

She was loaded down with groceries and could barely walk to her car.
The donkey is loaded down with supplies for the long journey.
After shopping, my backpack was loaded down and felt twice as heavy as before.

Synset Examples

tree limbs burdened with ice
a heavy-laden cart
loaded down with packages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdened - carrying a heavy load or burden
  • Synonym: overloaded - carrying or bearing more than can be easily supported

2. ADJ

/ ˌloʊdɪd ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌləʊdɪd ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
having to carry or endure a heavy burden, especially of work, responsibility, or worries
weighed down with burdens or responsibilities

Examples

She felt loaded down with work as deadlines piled up.
He was loaded down with packages when he returned from shopping.
The manager looked loaded down by the pressure of running the company.

Synset Examples

bowed down with troubles
found himself loaded down with responsibilities
overburdened social workers
weighed down with cares

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weighed down - burdened or oppressed by something heavy or worrisome
  • Synonym: overburdened - given more than can be easily managed or carried
