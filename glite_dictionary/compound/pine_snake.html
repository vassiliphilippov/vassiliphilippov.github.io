pine snake

1. NOUN

/ paɪn sneɪk / (AmE), / paɪn sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, nonvenomous North American snake chiefly found in pine forests of the eastern and southeastern United States, belonging to the genus Pituophis
a large North American snake of pine forests

Examples

We spotted a pine snake basking in the sun along the edge of the pine woods.
Pine snakes help control rodent populations in their native habitats.
Because their habitats are shrinking, the pine snake is now considered a threatened species in some states.
