concrete jungle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋ.krit ˈdʒʌŋ.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kriːt ˈdʒʌŋ.ɡl̩ / (BrE)
an area of a city with many large modern buildings, perceived as harsh, dangerous, or unpleasant
an urban area with many buildings, seen as harsh or unsafe

Examples

After moving to the concrete jungle of Manhattan, she missed the quiet of her hometown.
The film highlights the struggles of survival in the concrete jungle.
Many people dream of escaping the concrete jungle for a simpler life in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urban jungle - a city area viewed as chaotic or dangerous

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.krit ˈdʒʌŋ.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kriːt ˈdʒʌŋ.ɡl̩ / (BrE)
a city or urban area with many buildings and little greenery, often perceived as harsh or unwelcoming
dense urban area with many buildings

Examples

Moving from the countryside to the concrete jungle was a big adjustment for her.
The novel describes life in the concrete jungle of New York City.
Many people seek parks as an escape from the concrete jungle surrounding them.

Synset Examples
