to haul in a big catch

1. VERB

/ hɔl ɪn ə bɪɡ kætʃ / (AmE), / hɔːl ɪn ə bɪɡ kætʃ / (BrE)
to catch and pull in a large amount of fish, typically as part of a fishing activity
catch a large amount of fish

Examples

The fishermen hoped to haul in a big catch before the storm hit.
After hours of waiting, she finally managed to haul in a big catch.
They celebrated on the docks after hauling in a big catch this morning.

Synset Examples

She managed to haul in a big catch today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land a big catch - to successfully catch a large fish or a large amount of fish
