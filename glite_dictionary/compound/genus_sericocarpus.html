genus Sericocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌsɛr.ɪ.koʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌsɛr.ɪ.kəʊˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A small genus of herbaceous plants in the eastern United States, commonly known as white-topped asters.
genus of white-topped asters

Examples

The genus Sericocarpus includes several species native to the eastern United States.
Botanists are interested in the distinctive white flowers of the genus Sericocarpus.
You can find members of the genus Sericocarpus blooming in open woodland areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the genus Aster, related in taxonomy

Etymology

From Greek 'serikos' (silky) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the silken fruit covering.
