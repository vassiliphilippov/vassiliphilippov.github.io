blue pikeperch

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈpaɪkˌpɜrtʃ / (AmE), / bluː ˈpaɪkˌpɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a variety of pikeperch (Sander vitreus glaucus) that inhabits the Great Lakes, known for its bluish coloration and once an important commercial fish
fish variety from Great Lakes

Examples

The blue pikeperch was once abundant in the Great Lakes but is now considered extinct.
Fishermen used to rely on the blue pikeperch as a staple catch during the 20th century.
The distinctive coloration of the blue pikeperch made it easily recognizable among other lake fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walleye - North American freshwater fish related to the pikeperch
