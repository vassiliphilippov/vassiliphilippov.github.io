Tupac Katari Guerrilla Army

1. PROPN

/ ˈtuːpɑːk kɑːˈtɑːri ɡəˈrɪlə ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈtuːpak kaːˈtaːri ɡəˈrɪlə ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
a Bolivian armed group, considered terrorist by authorities, active from the remnants of insurgents trained by Che Guevara, known for attacking infrastructure and government targets
Bolivian insurgent armed group

Examples

The Bolivian government increased security after the Tupac Katari Guerrilla Army claimed responsibility for several attacks.
Reports indicate that the Tupac Katari Guerrilla Army targeted oil pipelines in remote areas.
Scholars have studied the impact of the Tupac Katari Guerrilla Army on Bolivian politics and security.

Etymology

Named after Tupac Katari, an 18th-century Aymara leader who led an indigenous uprising against Spanish rule in Bolivia.
