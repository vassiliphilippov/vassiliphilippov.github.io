Eye of Ra

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ əv ˈrɑ / (AmE), / ˌaɪ əv ˈrɑː / (BrE)
In ancient Egyptian mythology, a goddess personifying the sun's destructive power, often depicted as a lioness and regarded as an extension of the sun god Ra.
Egyptian mythological goddess, Ra's destructive aspect

Examples

The Eye of Ra was invoked for protection and vengeance in ancient ceremonies.
Artists often depict the Eye of Ra as a fierce lioness or blazing sun.
Legends describe the Eye of Ra as both a protector and destroyer among the gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ra - the ancient Egyptian sun god

Etymology

From Egyptian mythology; 'Eye' refers to an extension or aspect of the god Ra.
