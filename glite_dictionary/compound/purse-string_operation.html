purse-string operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːs strɪŋ ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːs strɪŋ ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a suture is placed to close the cervix in a pregnant woman, typically to prevent premature opening and loss of pregnancy
suture procedure to close cervix in pregnancy
medical

Examples

The obstetrician recommended a purse-string operation to help maintain the pregnancy.
After undergoing a purse-string operation, she was advised to limit physical activity.
The purse-string operation is often performed when the cervix is unable to stay closed during pregnancy.

Etymology

From the resemblance of the circular suture technique to the tightening of a purse string.
