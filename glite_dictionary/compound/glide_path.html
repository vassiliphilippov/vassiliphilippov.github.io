glide path

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪd ˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈɡlaɪd ˌpɑːθ / (BrE)
the trajectory that an aircraft follows during its final approach to landing, typically guided by landing systems
trajectory followed by aircraft while landing

Examples

The pilot aligned the aircraft with the glide path for a smooth landing.
Any deviation from the glide path could result in an unsafe landing.
Air traffic control provided guidance to help the jet maintain its glide path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach path - the path an aircraft takes toward a landing runway

2. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪd pæθ / (AmE), / ɡlaɪd pɑːθ / (BrE)
a planned and smooth route to achieving a particular goal or completing a project
smooth route to a goal

Examples

With a clear glide path, the company expects to complete the merger by the end of the year.
The manager set a glide path that would allow the team to meet every milestone on time.
Investors are looking for a predictable glide path towards profitability.

Synset Examples

We’re on a glide path to meet our goals.
The project has a clear glide path to completion.
They established a glide path for the initiative's rollout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trajectory - the path followed by a moving object or process
  • Synonym: roadmap - a plan or strategy intended to achieve a particular goal

Etymology

Originally from aviation, referring to the path an aircraft follows when descending to land; now used figuratively.
