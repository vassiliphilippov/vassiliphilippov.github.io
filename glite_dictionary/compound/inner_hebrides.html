Inner Hebrides

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪnɚ ˈhɛbrɪˌdiz / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈhɛbrɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
a group of islands located off the western coast of Scotland, between the Outer Hebrides and the mainland
group of Scottish islands west of mainland

Examples

The Inner Hebrides are famous for their rugged scenery and rich wildlife.
Many tourists visit the Inner Hebrides to experience traditional Scottish culture.
She spent her summer hiking across several islands in the Inner Hebrides.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Outer Hebrides - group of islands off the northwest coast of mainland Scotland, farther from the mainland than the Inner Hebrides

Etymology

From Latin 'Hebrides', likely from the Greek 'Ἐβοῦδαι' (Eboudai), with 'inner' indicating proximity to the Scottish mainland.
