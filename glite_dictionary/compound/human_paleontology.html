human paleontology

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən ˌpeɪliənˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ˌpalɪɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of human fossils, focusing on the evolution and development of humans from prehistoric times
scientific study of human fossils
medical

Examples

Advancements in human paleontology have greatly expanded our understanding of prehistoric humans.
She chose to specialize in human paleontology because of her fascination with ancient human remains.
Important discoveries in human paleontology are often highlighted in scientific journals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of fossils

Etymology

From 'paleontology' (French paléontologie), from Greek palaios (ancient) + ont- (being) + -logia (study), with 'human' specifying the focus on Homo sapiens and related species.
