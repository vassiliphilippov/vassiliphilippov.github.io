petit larceny

1. NOUN

/ pɛˈti lɑrˈsɛni / (AmE), / ˈpeti lɑːˈsɛni / (BrE)
The crime of stealing property of low monetary value, usually below a set legal threshold.
theft of property of small value
legal

Examples

The defendant was charged with petit larceny after stealing several inexpensive items from the shop.
Under state law, petit larceny is considered a misdemeanor and carries a lesser penalty than grand larceny.
She was found guilty of petit larceny when she took a wallet containing only a small amount of cash.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grand larceny - larceny of property worth more than the legal threshold

Etymology

From Anglo-French *petit* (small) and Old French *larsin* (theft).
