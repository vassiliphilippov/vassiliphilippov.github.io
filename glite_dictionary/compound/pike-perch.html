pike-perch

1. NOUN

/ paɪkˌpɜrtʃ / (AmE), / paɪkˌpɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A predatory freshwater fish of the perch family, having an elongated body and resembling a pike, especially the zander or walleye.
predatory freshwater fish (perch family)

Examples

The fisherman was thrilled to catch a large pike-perch in the river.
Some lakes are known for their abundant populations of pike-perch.
Grilled pike-perch is considered a delicacy in many European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zander - a common European species of pike-perch
  • Synonym: walleye - a North American species of pike-perch

Etymology

From 'pike' (a type of predatory fish) and 'perch' (family Percidae), referring to its resemblance to a pike and its classification as a perch.
