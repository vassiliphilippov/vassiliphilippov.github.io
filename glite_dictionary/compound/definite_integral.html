definite integral

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfɪnət ˈɪntəˌgrəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪnɪt ˈɪntɪgrəl / (BrE)
the integral of a function calculated over a specific interval, producing a numerical value
integral evaluated over a particular interval

Examples

The area under the curve can be found by calculating the definite integral between the given bounds.
Her homework included several problems requiring the evaluation of a definite integral.
In calculus, mastering how to compute a definite integral is an essential skill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indefinite integral - an integral without specified limits, representing a family of functions
  • Synonym: integral - the general concept of integration in mathematics

Etymology

From 'definite' (clearly defined) and 'integral', from Latin integralis (whole).
