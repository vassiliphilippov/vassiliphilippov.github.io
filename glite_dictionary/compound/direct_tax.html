direct tax

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt tæks, daɪˈrɛkt tæks / (AmE), / dəˈrɛkt tæks, daɪˈrɛkt tæks / (BrE)
A tax that is paid directly by an individual or organization on whom it is imposed, rather than being passed on to others.
tax paid directly by the taxpayer

Examples

The government relies heavily on direct tax revenue such as income tax.
Corporate profits are typically subject to direct tax in most countries.
A direct tax is paid directly by individuals or businesses to the government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indirect tax - a tax collected by an intermediary, such as a retailer, from the person who bears the ultimate economic burden of the tax
