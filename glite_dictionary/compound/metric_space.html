metric space

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrɪk speɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪk speɪs / (BrE)
In mathematics, a set of points in which a distance (called a metric) is defined between every pair of points, satisfying specific properties such as non-negativity, symmetry, and the triangle inequality.
mathematical set with a defined distance function

Examples

The concept of a metric space is fundamental in modern analysis and topology.
Every Euclidean space is a metric space because it provides a natural way to measure distance.
In a metric space, the triangle inequality ensures the shortest distance between two points is a straight line.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: topological space - a set with a topology; generalizes metric spaces

Etymology

From 'metric' (meaning 'of measurement', from Greek metron) and 'space' (in the sense of a mathematical set).
