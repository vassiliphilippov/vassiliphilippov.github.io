grey willow

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A Eurasian shrub or small tree of the willow family, with pale, downy twigs, commonly found in wetlands and riverbanks.
a Eurasian shrubby willow with pale twigs

Examples

The grey willow is widely found along the banks of rivers throughout Europe.
Botanists identified the shrub as a mature grey willow due to its distinctive silvery twigs.
Conservationists are working to protect local wetlands where the grey willow naturally thrives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub with narrow leaves and flexible branches, especially of the genus Salix
