International Maritime Organization

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪntərˈnæʃənl ˈmɛrɪˌtaɪm ˌoʊrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl ˈmærɪˌtaɪm ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a United Nations specialized agency responsible for regulating international shipping and maritime safety
UN agency for international shipping

Examples

The International Maritime Organization sets safety standards for ships around the world.
Many countries are members of the International Maritime Organization to ensure maritime laws are consistent.
The International Maritime Organization was established to promote safe and efficient shipping.
