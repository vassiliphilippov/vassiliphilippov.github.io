Lower California

1. PROPN

/ ˈloʊ.ɚ ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔːr.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔː.ni.ə / (BrE)
a mountainous peninsula in northwestern Mexico, also known as the Baja California peninsula
peninsula in northwestern Mexico

Examples

Many tourists visit Lower California for its scenic beaches and mild climate.
The rugged terrain of Lower California is popular among hikers and adventure seekers.
In the 19th century, explorers mapped much of Lower California and its mountainous landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baja California - the more common Spanish name for the Lower California peninsula

Etymology

From English 'lower' (southern in this context) and 'California', reflecting its location south of Alta (Upper) California.
