police blotter

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˈblɑtɚ / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˈblɒtə / (BrE)
an official daily written record of arrests and other events at a police station
daily police record of events

Examples

The local newspaper prints excerpts from the police blotter every week.
After the incident, the arrest was promptly entered into the police blotter.
Reporters often check the police blotter for news of recent crimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logbook - a book in which daily events or operations are recorded

Etymology

From 'police' + 'blotter', referring to the book used for making daily entries.
