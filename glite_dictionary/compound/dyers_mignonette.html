dyer's mignonette

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪɚz mɪn.jəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəz mɪn.jəˈnɛt / (BrE)
A European plant (Reseda luteola), cultivated for its yellow dye and naturalized in North America.
plant used for yellow dye

Examples

The stems of dyer's mignonette were traditionally harvested for dye-making.
Gardeners sometimes grow dyer's mignonette for its historical significance as well as its bright flowers.
Dyer's mignonette has become naturalized in many parts of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - another name for Reseda luteola, a plant used as a yellow dye
  • Degree Higher: mignonette - a group of fragrant flowering plants in the genus Reseda

Etymology

The word 'mignonette' is from French, diminutive of 'mignon' (dainty, darling).
