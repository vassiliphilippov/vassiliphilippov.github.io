judicial review

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl rɪˈvju / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl rɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
The power of a court to examine the actions of government officials or agencies, or the decisions of lower courts, and determine whether they are lawful or constitutional.
court power to review actions or decisions
legal

Examples

The Supreme Court has the authority of judicial review to rule on the constitutionality of laws.
Through judicial review, the court overturned the government's decision as unlawful.
Many legal scholars debate the proper scope of judicial review in a democracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeal - a request to a court for a review of a lower court's decision

Etymology

From Latin 'judicialis' (of courts/judges) and 'review' (to see again). The term became prominent in Anglo-American law in the 19th century.
