glyceryl ester

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsəriːl ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsəriːl ˈɛstə / (BrE)
an ester formed from glycerol and an acid, commonly found in fats and oils
an ester of glycerol
medical

Examples

Many vegetable oils are composed primarily of glyceryl esters derived from fatty acids.
The breakdown of glyceryl esters in the body releases energy for metabolic processes.
Chemists analyze glyceryl esters to understand their role in nutrition and biochemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triglyceride - a type of glyceryl ester consisting of one glycerol and three fatty acids

Etymology

From 'glyceryl', derived from glycerol, and 'ester', from German 'Essigäther' (acetic ether).
