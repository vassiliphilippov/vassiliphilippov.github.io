fair catch

1. NOUN

/ fɛr ˈkætʃ / (AmE), / feə ˈkatʃ / (BrE)
(In American football) a catch made by a player who has signaled in advance that they will not attempt to advance the ball after catching a punt, and therefore may not be tackled.
signal catch in American football
american

Examples

The player called for a fair catch to prevent being tackled.
After signaling for a fair catch, he safely received the ball without interference.
It's important to know when a fair catch is the smartest option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punt - a kick where the ball is dropped and kicked before it touches the ground (in football)
