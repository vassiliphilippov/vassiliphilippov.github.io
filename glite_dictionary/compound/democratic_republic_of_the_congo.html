Democratic Republic of the Congo

1. PROPN

/ dəˈmɑːkrətɪk rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ðə ˈkɑːŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒkrətɪk rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ðə ˈkɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
A country in central Africa that gained independence from Belgium in 1960, known for its vast natural resources and river systems.
country in central Africa

Examples

The Democratic Republic of the Congo is the second largest country in Africa by area.
Many rare minerals are mined in the Democratic Republic of the Congo.
The Congo River flows through the heart of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Republic of the Congo - A neighboring country to the west, also on the Congo River

Etymology

The name reflects its political system (a republic) and its location along the Congo River; formerly known as Zaire.
