perfoliate leaf

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfoʊliˌeɪt lif / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːlɪeɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf in which the base completely surrounds the stem, so that the stem appears to pass through the leaf.
leaf whose base encircles the stem

Examples

The stem of the plant passes through the center of each perfoliate leaf.
Some species of honeysuckle are known for their distinctive perfoliate leaves.
The botanist pointed out a rare perfoliate leaf structure on the wildflower.

Etymology

From Latin 'perfoliatus', meaning 'surrounded by leaves'.
