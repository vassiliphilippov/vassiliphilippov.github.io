bar examination

1. NOUN

/ bɑr ˌɛɡ.zəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / bɑː ˌɛɡ.zəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an official examination that a person must pass to qualify to practice law in a particular jurisdiction
official legal qualifying exam
legal

Examples

After years of preparation, Maria finally passed the bar examination and became a licensed attorney.
Most law graduates spend months studying intensively for the bar examination.
Without passing the bar examination, you cannot officially practice as a lawyer in this state.

Synset Examples

applicants may qualify to take the New York bar examination by graduating from an approved law school
he passed the bar exam on his third try

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar exam - short form of 'bar examination', a legal qualifying test

Etymology

From 'bar' (the legal profession) and 'examination', referring to the test for legal qualification.
