family Mimosaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli mɪˌmoʊsəˈsiːi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli mɪˌməʊsəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of spiny, woody plants (especially shrubs or small trees) whose leaves often respond to touch, commonly grouped within the family Leguminosae
group of touch-sensitive spiny plants

Examples

The family Mimosaceae includes plants whose leaves fold when touched, such as the sensitive plant.
Botanists have debated whether to classify certain acacias within the family Mimosaceae or the Leguminosae.
Many ornamental shrubs belonging to the family Mimosaceae are grown for their unique leaf movements.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Leguminosae - the family of plants that includes legumes, peas, and beans

Etymology

From genus Mimosa (from Latin mimus, 'mimic, actor'), with the botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
