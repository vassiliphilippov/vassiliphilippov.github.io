comprehensive school

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmprɪˈhɛnsɪv skuːl / (AmE), / ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv skuːl / (BrE)
A large state secondary school in Britain or Canada that takes children of all abilities, without selection by academic achievement or aptitude.
secondary school for all abilities (Britain/Canada)
british

Examples

After primary education, most students in England attend a comprehensive school in their local area.
The local comprehensive school offers a wide range of subjects to cater for students of all abilities.
Debates about education often compare selective schools with the comprehensive school system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grammar school - selective British secondary school for academic students
