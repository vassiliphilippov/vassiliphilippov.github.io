shipping pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpɪŋ nəˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpɪŋ njʊˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a fatal bacterial infection in cattle and sheep, marked by high fever and pneumonia, often contracted when animals are stressed or transported
deadly infection in cattle and sheep from shipping stress
medical

Examples

If calves are not properly cared for during transport, they may develop shipping pneumonia.
Several cases of shipping pneumonia were reported after the long journey to the new farm.
Proper ventilation in trailers can help prevent shipping pneumonia in livestock.
