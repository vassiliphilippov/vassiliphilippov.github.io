horse-and-buggy

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɔrs ən ˈbʌɡi / (AmE), / ˌhɔːs ən ˈbʌɡi / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the period before the widespread use of automobiles, especially in reference to outdated methods or old-fashioned ways of life.
old-fashioned; before automobiles

Examples

His ideas about education are rather horse-and-buggy for the twenty-first century.
The town still has some horse-and-buggy charm with its preserved historical buildings.
Some people refer to letter writing as a horse-and-buggy form of communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquated - old-fashioned; outdated
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
