talking-to

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔkɪŋ tu / (AmE), / ˈtɔːkɪŋ tʊ / (BrE)
an act of reprimanding or scolding someone, usually by speaking to them firmly about their behavior
a scolding or reprimand

Examples

After arriving late again, Mark got a talking-to from his supervisor.
The coach gave the team a stern talking-to at halftime.
If you keep breaking the rules, you're going to get a serious talking-to sooner or later.

Synset Examples

He gave her a talking-to about being late.
After the meeting, she received a talking-to from her boss.
The teacher's talking-to made the students reflect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scolding - an angry or stern reprimand
  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval
  • Antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration
