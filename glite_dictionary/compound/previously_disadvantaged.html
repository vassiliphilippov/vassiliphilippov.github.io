previously disadvantaged

1. ADJ

/ ˈpriːviəsli dɪsˈædvəntɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈpriːviəsli dɪsˈædvɒntɪdʒd / (BrE)
having experienced social or economic disadvantage in the past, especially as a result of systemic discrimination or inequality
having faced past disadvantage

Examples

The university has special programs for previously disadvantaged applicants from rural areas.
Efforts to support previously disadvantaged communities are crucial for achieving social equality.
Many scholarships now reserve funds specifically for previously disadvantaged students.

Synset Examples

The scholarship is aimed at previously disadvantaged students.
They focus on helping previously disadvantaged groups thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underprivileged - lacking basic advantages due to poverty or social status
