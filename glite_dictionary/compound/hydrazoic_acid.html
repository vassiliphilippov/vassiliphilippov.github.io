hydrazoic acid

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdræzoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˌdræzəʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, explosive, volatile, and highly poisonous liquid with a pungent odor, used in chemical synthesis and as a reagent
volatile, explosive, poisonous chemical compound
medical

Examples

Hydrazoic acid is extremely dangerous and must be handled with specialized safety equipment in the laboratory.
Because of its explosive nature, hydrazoic acid is rarely used outside of controlled conditions.
The chemist prepared a small amount of hydrazoic acid for use in a specific chemical reaction.

Etymology

Derived from hydrazoic, based on 'hydra-' (related to hydrogen) and '-az-' (from azote, an old name for nitrogen).
