school ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːl ˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈskuːl ˌʃɪp / (BrE)
a ship used for training students in seamanship and navigation, typically preparing them for a career as sailors or naval officers
ship used for training sailors

Examples

The navy assigned several cadets to the school ship for practical seamanship training.
During her time on the school ship, she learned navigation and first aid.
Many maritime academies operate a school ship as part of their curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: training ship - a ship used for teaching seamanship, especially to young sailors
