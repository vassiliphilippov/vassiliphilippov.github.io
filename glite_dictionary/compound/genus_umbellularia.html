genus Umbellularia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ʌmˌbɛljʊˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ʌmˌbɛljʊˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic evergreen trees native to the Pacific coast of North America, including the California bay laurel.
genus of aromatic Pacific coast evergreens

Examples

The genus Umbellularia includes only one living species, commonly called the California bay laurel.
Botanists have studied the aromatic leaves characteristic of the genus Umbellularia.
The forests along the Pacific coast contain members of the genus Umbellularia among other evergreen trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Umbellularia californica - the only species in the genus, known as California bay laurel

Etymology

From Latin 'umbella' (umbrella, parasol) and '-aria' (pertaining to), referring to the umbrella-like shape of the flower clusters.
