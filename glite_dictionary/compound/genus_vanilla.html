genus Vanilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs vəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs vəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical climbing orchids found in the Old and New Worlds, notable for species that produce vanilla pods.
genus of tropical climbing orchids

Examples

The genus Vanilla includes species that are the natural source of vanilla flavoring.
Researchers are studying the pollination process of the genus Vanilla in South America.
Many species within the genus Vanilla produce aromatic pods prized for use in cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - any plant of the orchid family

Etymology

From Latin 'vanilla', meaning 'little pod', and 'genus', meaning 'kind' or 'type'.
