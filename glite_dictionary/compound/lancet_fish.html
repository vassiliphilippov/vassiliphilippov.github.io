lancet fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæn.sət ˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlan.sɪt ˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, elongate, scaleless oceanic fish with sharp teeth and a long, sail-like dorsal fin
large oceanic fish with a sail-like dorsal fin

Examples

The lancet fish is rarely seen near shore, as it prefers the open ocean.
A sudden appearance of a lancet fish surprised the scientists during their deep-sea expedition.
The lancet fish uses its sharp teeth to catch squid and smaller fish.

Etymology

From 'lancet', referencing the fish's sharp, lance-like appearance, and 'fish'.
