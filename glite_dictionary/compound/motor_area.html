motor area

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtər ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə ˈeəriə / (BrE)
a region of the cerebral cortex responsible for the control of voluntary muscle movements
region of cortex controlling movements
medical

Examples

Damage to the motor area can lead to partial or complete loss of movement.
During surgery, doctors carefully avoided the motor area to prevent affecting the patient's mobility.
Neuroscientists are studying how the motor area coordinates complex hand movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motor cortex - region of the cerebral cortex involved in planning, control, and execution of voluntary movements
