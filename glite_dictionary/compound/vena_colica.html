vena colica

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːnə kəˈlaɪkə / (AmE), / ˌviːnə kɒˈliːkə / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the large intestine
vein draining the large intestine
medical

Examples

The vena colica plays an essential role in draining blood from the colon.
In the surgery, the doctor carefully identified the vena colica before proceeding.
Blockage of the vena colica can lead to severe complications in blood flow to the large intestine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesenteric vein - a vein that drains blood from the intestine to the portal vein

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'colica' (related to the colon).
