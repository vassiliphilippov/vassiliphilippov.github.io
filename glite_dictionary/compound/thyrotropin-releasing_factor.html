thyrotropin-releasing factor

1. NOUN

/ θaɪroʊˌtroʊpɪn rɪˈlising ˈfæktər / (AmE), / θaɪrəˌtrəʊpɪn rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of thyroid-stimulating hormone from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone controlling thyroid-stimulating hormone release
medical

Examples

Research shows that thyrotropin-releasing factor is crucial in regulating thyroid hormone levels.
The scientist explained how the hypothalamus secretes thyrotropin-releasing factor to signal the pituitary gland.
Disorders affecting the secretion of thyrotropin-releasing factor can lead to thyroid dysfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyrotropin-releasing hormone - another term for thyrotropin-releasing factor
  • Degree Lower: thyroid-stimulating hormone - hormone released from the anterior pituitary, stimulated by TRF

Etymology

From 'thyrotropin' (a hormone affecting the thyroid) and 'releasing factor' (a substance that triggers hormone secretion).
