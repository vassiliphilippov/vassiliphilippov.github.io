true intentions

1. NOUN

/ tru ɪnˈtɛnʃənz / (AmE), / truː ɪnˈtɛnʃənz / (BrE)
a person's real motives or purposes, especially those hidden or not immediately obvious
someone's real motives or purposes

Examples

The politician's true intentions were eventually revealed through his actions.
It's often difficult to tell what someone's true intentions are in a business negotiation.
She wondered if he had any true intentions beyond friendship.

Synset Examples

It's hard to apprehend his true intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulterior motive - a secret reason for doing something
