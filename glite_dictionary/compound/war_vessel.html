war vessel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr ˌvɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌvɛsəl / (BrE)
a ship owned or operated by a government and designed or equipped for warfare
government warship

Examples

The navy deployed its largest war vessel to patrol the disputed waters.
During the ceremony, several decommissioned war vessels were opened for public tours.
The treaty placed restrictions on the size and armament of each country's war vessel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warship - a ship equipped for warfare
  • Antonym: merchant vessel - a ship used for commercial purposes
