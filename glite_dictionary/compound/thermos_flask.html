thermos flask

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝːmoʊs flæsk / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməs flɑːsk / (BrE)
A container with double walls and a vacuum between them, used for keeping liquids hot or cold for long periods.
vacuum container for hot or cold drinks

Examples

She poured hot coffee from the thermos flask during the picnic.
He always carries a thermos flask filled with ice water on hikes.
The tea stayed warm in the thermos flask all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum flask - container with a vacuum for thermal insulation
  • Degree Lower: flask - a bottle, usually narrow-necked, used to hold liquids

Etymology

Thermos from Greek thermē (heat); 'Thermos' was originally a trademarked brand name.
