sheet pile

1. NOUN

/ ʃiːt paɪl / (AmE), / ʃiːt paɪl / (BrE)
a long, flat pile made of metal, concrete, or wood, driven vertically into the ground alongside others to retain soil or prevent water seepage
long pile for retaining soil or water

Examples

The construction crew installed sheet piles along the riverbank to prevent erosion.
Steel sheet piles are often used to support excavation sites in urban areas.
To stop water from seeping into the foundation, a row of sheet piles was driven deep into the ground.
