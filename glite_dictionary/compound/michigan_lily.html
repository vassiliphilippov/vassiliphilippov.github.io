Michigan lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃɪɡən ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃɪɡən ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a North American lily with tall stems and orange-red, recurved flowers marked with deep crimson spots, native to central North America
North American lily with orange-red, spotted flowers

Examples

We spotted a Michigan lily blooming by the edge of the meadow in early summer.
The vibrant orange-red petals of the Michigan lily stood out among the green grasses.
Gardeners value the Michigan lily for its striking recurved flowers and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant of the genus Lilium, with large prominent flowers
