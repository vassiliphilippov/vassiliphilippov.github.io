damask violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæməsk ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈdaməsk ˈvʌɪəlɪt / (BrE)
a tall cultivated herb with fragrant flowers, especially in the evening, native to Europe and Asia and widely naturalized elsewhere; also called hesperis or dame's violet
fragrant evening-flowering herbaceous plant

Examples

The damask violet releases a stronger fragrance as dusk falls.
Damask violet is often used in cottage gardens for its evening scent.
We planted damask violets along the walkway to enjoy their flowers in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dame's violet - another common name for Hesperis matronalis

Etymology

'Damask' refers originally to the Syrian city of Damascus, once associated with textile patterns; 'violet' describes the flower's color.
