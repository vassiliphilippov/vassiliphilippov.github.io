family Osmeridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɑːzˌmɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɒzˌmɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, silvery fish known as smelts, found in cold northern waters.
family of smelt fishes

Examples

Researchers have discovered several new species within the family Osmeridae in the Arctic region.
The lifecycle of the family Osmeridae is affected by changes in water temperature and salinity.
Smelts, belonging to the family Osmeridae, are often an important part of commercial fisheries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - small, silvery fish of cold northern waters

Etymology

From scientific Latin Osmeridae, based on genus name Osmerus.
