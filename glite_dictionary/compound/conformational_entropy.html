conformational entropy

1. NOUN

/ kənˌfɔrˈmeɪʃənəl ˈɛntɹəpi / (AmE), / kənˌfɔːˈmeɪʃənəl ˈɛntrəpi / (BrE)
The entropy associated with the number of possible spatial arrangements (conformations) of a molecule, calculated from the probability that each state could occur by chance.
entropy due to possible conformations of a molecule

Examples

The conformational entropy of the protein increases as it unfolds at higher temperatures.
Chemists calculate conformational entropy to understand how flexible a molecule can be in solution.
Changes in conformational entropy may influence the binding of molecules in a biological system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entropy - measure of disorder or randomness in a system

Etymology

From 'conformation' (Latin conformatio, shaping) + 'entropy' (from Greek entropia, transformation).
