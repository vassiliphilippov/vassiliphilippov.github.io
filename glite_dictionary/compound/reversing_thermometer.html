reversing thermometer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɜrsɪŋ θɚˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsɪŋ θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a thermometer designed to register the temperature of deep waters, often used in oceanography or hydrography
thermometer for deep-water temperature measurement

Examples

The oceanographer lowered the reversing thermometer to measure the temperature several hundred meters below the surface.
A reversing thermometer must be carefully handled to ensure accurate readings in deep-sea environments.
Research teams use a reversing thermometer when collecting oceanographic data during expeditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermometer - an instrument for measuring temperature
