Republic of North Macedonia

1. PROPN

/ rəˈpʌblɪk əv nɔrθ ˌmæsəˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv nɔːθ ˌmæsəˈdəʊniə / (BrE)
A landlocked country in southeastern Europe, located on the Balkan Peninsula, which gained independence from Yugoslavia in 1991.
country in southeastern Europe, formerly part of Yugoslavia

Examples

The Republic of North Macedonia became a member of the United Nations in 1993.
Tourism in the Republic of North Macedonia has grown rapidly in recent years.
Skopje is the capital city of the Republic of North Macedonia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Macedonia - region and historical area in southeastern Europe

Etymology

From Greek Makedonía, the historical region of Macedonia; modern usage refers to the state founded in 1991.
