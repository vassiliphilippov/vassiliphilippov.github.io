Croton bug

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊtən bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊtən bʌɡ / (BrE)
A small, light-brown cockroach (Blattella germanica) originally brought to the United States from Europe; a common household pest.
small household cockroach pest

Examples

The kitchen was infested with Croton bugs that scattered whenever the lights were turned on.
Many city dwellers have encountered a Croton bug hiding in their apartments.
Exterminators were called to deal with the persistent problem of Croton bugs in the old building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a nocturnal insect pest with a flat, oval body

Etymology

Named after Croton waterworks in New York City, where the pest became notorious in the 19th century.
