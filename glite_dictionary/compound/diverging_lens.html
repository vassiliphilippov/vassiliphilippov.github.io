diverging lens

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɜrdʒɪŋ lɛnz / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒɪŋ lɛnz / (BrE)
a lens that causes parallel rays of light to spread out after passing through it
lens that spreads out light rays

Examples

When light passes through a diverging lens, the rays spread out instead of coming together.
A diverging lens is commonly used in eyeglasses for people who are nearsighted.
The technician explained how a diverging lens alters the path of incoming light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concave lens - a lens that is thinner at the center than at the edges and causes light rays to diverge
  • Antonym: converging lens - a lens that brings parallel rays of light together to a focus

Etymology

From 'diverge' (to move apart) + 'lens', a term in optics.
