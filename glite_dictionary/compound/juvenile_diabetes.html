juvenile diabetes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl ˌdaɪəˈbiːtiz / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvɪnaɪl ˌdaɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
a form of diabetes mellitus that develops in children or young adults, requiring lifelong insulin treatment
type 1 diabetes with childhood onset
medical

Examples

After her son was diagnosed with juvenile diabetes, she learned how to manage his insulin injections.
Children with juvenile diabetes must monitor their blood sugar levels closely every day.
The hospital provides special education for families coping with juvenile diabetes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 1 diabetes - insulin-dependent diabetes, usually beginning in childhood
  • Antonym: type 2 diabetes - a type of diabetes that develops mainly in adults, often associated with overweight
