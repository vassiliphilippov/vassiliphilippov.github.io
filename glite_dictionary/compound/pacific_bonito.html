Pacific bonito

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk bəˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk bəˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
a species of bonito (Sarda lineolata) found along the Pacific coast of the Americas, known for its dark, oily flesh often used in canning
a species of bonito fish from the Pacific coast

Examples

Local fishermen often catch Pacific bonito during the summer months.
The dark flesh of Pacific bonito is prized for canning and export.
I learned to recognize Pacific bonito by the distinctive stripes along its back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonito - a type of medium-sized predatory fish related to tuna
