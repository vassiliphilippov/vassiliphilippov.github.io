Proterozoic aeon

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌtɛrəˈzoʊɪk ˈiː.ɒn / (AmE), / prəʊˌtɛrəʊˈzəʊɪk ˈiː.ən / (BrE)
The geological eon that occurred from about 2,500 to 544 million years ago, marked by the emergence of bacteria, fungi, and primitive multicellular organisms.
geological eon from 2,500 to 544 million years ago

Examples

The fossil record from the Proterozoic aeon provides insight into the earliest multicellular life.
Geologists often study rocks dating back to the Proterozoic aeon to understand Earth's early atmosphere.
During the Proterozoic aeon, simple organisms began to evolve into more complex forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Precambrian - the vast span of time before the Cambrian period, including the Proterozoic
  • Degree Higher: Phanerozoic eon - the eon following the Proterozoic, marked by abundant animal life
  • Degree Lower: Archean eon - the eon before the Proterozoic, marked by the formation of the Earth's crust and earliest life

Etymology

From Greek proteros (earlier) + zōē (life) + eon (age, eternity).
