family Nymphalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli nɪmˈfæləˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli nɪmˈfælədiː / (BrE)
a large family of butterflies known for their vibrant colors and diverse patterns, including some of the most well-known species worldwide
large family of colorful butterflies

Examples

The family Nymphalidae includes well-known butterflies like monarchs and admirals.
Many species in the family Nymphalidae exhibit striking and colorful wing patterns.
Researchers study the family Nymphalidae to learn more about butterfly evolution and diversity.

Etymology

From New Latin *Nymphalidae*, based on the type genus *Nymphalis*.
