neural network

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʊrəl ˈnɛtwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl ˈnɛtwɜːk / (BrE)
a system of interconnected neurons or groups of nerve cells that work together to perform a specific function in the body
system of interconnected nerve cells

Examples

The human brain contains many complex neural networks responsible for movement, sensation, and thought.
Damage to a neural network in the spinal cord can result in loss of sensation or paralysis.
Scientists study how different neural networks cooperate to control muscle coordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve network - complex network of nerve fibers or cells

Etymology

From Latin 'neuralis' (relating to nerves) + English 'network'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʊrəl ˈnɛtˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl ˈnɛtwɜːk / (BrE)
A computer system inspired by the structure of the human brain, consisting of interconnected processors that can learn and make decisions.
computer system modeled on the brain

Examples

A neural network can identify patterns in large sets of data far more efficiently than traditional algorithms.
Scientists use a neural network to recognize handwritten characters on scanned documents.
Modern voice assistants rely on complex neural networks to understand and respond to speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial intelligence - the theory and development of computer systems able to perform tasks that require human intelligence

Etymology

From 'neural' (relating to nerves or the nervous system) and 'network' (a system of interconnected elements).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06738216-n
Synset POS: n
