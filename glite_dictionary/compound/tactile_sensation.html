tactile sensation

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæktaɪl sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈtæktaɪl sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the physical feeling resulting from pressure or contact detected by the skin's sense receptors
touch sensation detected by the skin

Examples

The rough fabric produced an unpleasant tactile sensation on her hands.
Children use their sense of tactile sensation to explore new objects.
Loss of tactile sensation in the fingertips can affect daily activities.

Synset Examples

she likes the touch of silk on her skin
the surface had a greasy feeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - sense that perceives physical contact or pressure

Etymology

From Latin tactilis (tangible, of touch) and sensation (from Latin sensatio, perception).
