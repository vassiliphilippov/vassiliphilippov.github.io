false dragon head

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈdræɡən hɛd / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈdræɡən hɛd / (BrE)
A North American plant of the mint family, having a terminal spike of two-lipped pink or white flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental.
North American flowering plant; ornamental

Examples

The garden's false dragon head bloomed in vibrant shades of pink throughout the summer.
She planted false dragon head along the fence to attract bees and butterflies.
Botanists often refer to the false dragon head by its other name, obedient plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedient plant - another common name for the same species (Physostegia virginiana)
