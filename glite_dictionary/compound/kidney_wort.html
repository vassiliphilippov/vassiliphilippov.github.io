kidney wort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdni wɜrt / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni wɜːt / (BrE)
A widely spreading evergreen shrub of the southwestern United States with flower heads in a leafy panicle.
evergreen shrub of southwestern US

Examples

The gardens featured several native plants, including kidney wort thriving in the rocky soil.
Botanists identified the shrub as kidney wort due to its characteristic flower heads.
Hikers were surprised to find kidney wort blooming along the canyon trail.

Etymology

From 'kidney' (referring to kidney shape) + 'wort' (Old English 'wyrt', meaning 'plant' or 'herb').
