preconceived idea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd aɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd aɪˈdɪə / (BrE)
an opinion or belief formed before having enough evidence or knowledge to support it
an opinion formed without sufficient evidence

Examples

It's dangerous to make decisions based on preconceived ideas rather than facts.
She tried to approach the situation without any preconceived ideas or judgments.
We must challenge our preconceived ideas if we want to truly understand other cultures.

Synset Examples

he did not even try to confirm his preconceptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preconception - an idea or opinion formed before having enough information
  • Synonym: bias - an inclination or prejudice for or against something

Etymology

From 'preconceived' (past participle of 'preconceive', from Latin prae- before + concipere to conceive) and 'idea' (from Greek 'ἰδέα', form, notion).
