common carotid artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən kəˈrɑt̬ɪd ˈɑrˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən kəˈrɒtɪd ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
either of the two major arteries running upward in the neck that divide into the external and internal carotid arteries, supplying blood to the head and neck.
major neck artery supplying head and neck
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully located the common carotid artery before beginning the operation.
A blockage in the common carotid artery can reduce blood flow to the brain significantly.
Ultrasound imaging showed narrowing of the patient's left common carotid artery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: internal carotid artery - main artery supplying blood to the brain
  • Degree Higher: external carotid artery - main artery supplying blood to the face and neck

Etymology

From Latin 'carotis' (from Greek 'karōtis', stupor, relating to effects on brain if blocked), and 'arteria' (artery).
