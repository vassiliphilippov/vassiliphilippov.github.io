flick-knife

1. NOUN

/ flɪk naɪf / (AmE), / flɪk naɪf / (BrE)
A pocketknife with a blade that springs open by pressing a button.
spring-loaded pocketknife
british

Examples

The police confiscated a flick-knife from one of the suspects.
Carrying a flick-knife is illegal in many countries.
He pressed the button and the flick-knife snapped open in his hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switchblade - A pocketknife with a spring-loaded blade that opens automatically

Etymology

From 'flick' (meaning to move quickly) + 'knife'; commonly used in British English for 'switchblade'.
