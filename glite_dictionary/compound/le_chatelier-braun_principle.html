Le Chatelier-Braun principle

1. NOUN

/ lə ʃæˈtɛljeɪ brɑːn ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / lə ʃaˈtɛljeɪ brɔːn ˈprɪnsɪpl̩ / (BrE)
the principle stating that if a change is made to a system in equilibrium, the system will adjust to counteract that change and reach a new equilibrium
principle of equilibrium adjustment in chemistry

Examples

According to the Le Chatelier-Braun principle, increasing the temperature shifts the reaction equilibrium to favor the products.
When pressure is applied to a system at equilibrium, the Le Chatelier-Braun principle predicts how the system responds.
Students were asked to explain the Le Chatelier-Braun principle using examples from chemical industry processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Le Chatelier's principle - alternative name for the same chemical equilibrium principle

Etymology

Named after French chemist Henri Le Chatelier and German scientist Karl Ferdinand Braun.
