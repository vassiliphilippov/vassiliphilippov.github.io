Persian Gulf

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɜrʒən ɡʌlf / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ɡʌlf / (BrE)
a shallow extension of the Arabian Sea bordered by Iran and several countries of the Arabian Peninsula, notable for its vast oil reserves and strategic significance
shallow sea between Iran and Arabian Peninsula

Examples

Oil tankers frequently travel through the Persian Gulf to transport crude oil to global markets.
The geopolitical tensions in the Persian Gulf can affect international energy prices.
Marine life in the Persian Gulf has adapted to its high temperatures and salinity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabian Gulf - alternative name for the Persian Gulf, commonly used in some Arab countries

Etymology

From Persian, named for the Persian (Iranian) region bordering the gulf.
