Lutheran principles

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːθɚrən ˈprɪnsəpəlz / (AmE), / ˈluːθərən ˈprɪnsɪpəlz / (BrE)
the beliefs and doctrines characteristic of Lutheranism, especially as taught by Martin Luther and his followers
beliefs characteristic of Lutheranism

Examples

Her upbringing was deeply rooted in Lutheran principles and traditions.
The community organized discussions about how Lutheran principles guided ethical decision-making.
Some schools incorporate Lutheran principles into their educational philosophy.

Synset Examples

The Lutherist principles influence their family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lutheranism - branch of Protestant Christianity based on Martin Luther's teachings
  • Antonym: Calvinist principles - beliefs associated with Calvinism

Etymology

From German *lutherisch* (Lutheran), after Martin Luther (1483–1546), founder of Lutheranism, and Latin *principium* (beginning, basis).
