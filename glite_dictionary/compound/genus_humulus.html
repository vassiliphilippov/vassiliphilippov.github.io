genus Humulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs hjuːˈmjuːləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hjuːˈmjuːləs / (BrE)
A genus of hardy perennial climbing plants, including hops, native to Europe, North America, and Asia.
genus of vines including hops

Examples

The genus Humulus contains several species important to the brewing industry.
Scientists have studied the medicinal properties of plants within the genus Humulus.
Among the vines of the genus Humulus, Humulus lupulus is the best known variety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - the plant or its flower used in brewing

Etymology

From Latin 'humulus' meaning hops, used in botanical nomenclature.
