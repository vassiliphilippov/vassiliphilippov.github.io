inhalation anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌheɪˈleɪʃən ˌænəsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ɪnˌheɪˈleɪʃən ˌænɪsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
a form of general anesthesia induced by administering anesthetic gases or vapors through inhalation
general anesthesia by inhaled gases or vapors
medical

Examples

The patient received inhalation anesthesia before undergoing surgery.
Modern operating rooms are equipped for administering inhalation anesthesia safely and efficiently.
Due to allergies, the doctor chose inhalation anesthesia rather than an intravenous method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhalation anesthetic - an anesthetic administered by inhalation
  • Antonym: intravenous anesthesia - anesthesia produced by administering drugs into a vein
