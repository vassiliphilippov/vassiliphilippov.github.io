genus Cephalotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌsɛfəˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌsɛfəˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous plants with one species, the Australian pitcher plant, native to southwest Australia.
carnivorous plant genus (Australian pitcher plant)

Examples

Botanists classify the genus Cephalotus as monotypic because it contains only one species.
The genus Cephalotus is famous for its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that trap insects.
In the wild, the genus Cephalotus can be found growing in boggy areas of southwestern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cephalotus follicularis - the only species within the genus Cephalotus; Australian pitcher plant

Etymology

From Greek 'kephalē' (head), referring to the shape of the plant's pitcher.
