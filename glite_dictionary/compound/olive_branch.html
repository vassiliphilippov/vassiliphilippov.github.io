olive branch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɪv bræntʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪv brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
an offer or gesture made to end a disagreement or bring about peace between parties
gesture of peace or reconciliation

Examples

After months of silence, she finally extended an olive branch to her old friend.
The government offered an olive branch to the opposition in hopes of avoiding further conflict.
By apologizing publicly, he intended his words as an olive branch to mend their relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peace offering - something given to try to end a conflict or disagreement
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior or attitude

Etymology

From the tradition of using the olive branch as a symbol of peace, originating in Ancient Greece and the Mediterranean region.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑlɪv bræntʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪv brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
a gesture or symbol indicating a desire for peace or reconciliation
gesture of peace or reconciliation

Examples

After their argument, he decided to offer an olive branch and apologize.
The two companies extended an olive branch to each other during the negotiations.
She hoped her small gift would be seen as an olive branch, ending the tension between them.

Synset Examples

Offering an olive branch can help mend broken relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peace offering - something given to make peace or settle a dispute
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior

Etymology

From the ancient custom of using an olive branch as a symbol of peace, especially in ancient Greece and Rome.
