long shot

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
A person or thing regarded as unlikely to succeed or win, especially in a competition or contest.
unlikely contender or winner

Examples

Despite being a long shot, the horse pulled ahead in the final stretch and won the race.
He entered the tournament as a long shot, but surprised everyone by reaching the finals.
Many investors considered the tech startup a long shot in the crowded market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning

Etymology

Originally from shooting terminology, referring to a shot taken from a great distance and thus unlikely to succeed.

2. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
an attempt or venture that is unlikely to succeed but offers the possibility of great rewards
unlikely venture with possible big reward

Examples

It might seem like a long shot, but winning the competition is still possible.
Investing in that startup is a long shot, but the potential returns are huge.
Applying for the job felt like a long shot, yet she decided it was worth a try.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outside chance - a small chance that something will happen
  • Antonym: sure thing - something that is certain to happen or succeed
