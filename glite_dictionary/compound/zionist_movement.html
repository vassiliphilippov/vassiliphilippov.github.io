Zionist movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.ə.nɪst ˈmuːv.mənt / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ə.nɪst ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
A movement that began in the late 19th century among Jews worldwide with the aim of establishing a Jewish homeland in Palestine.
movement to establish a Jewish homeland

Examples

The Zionist movement gained momentum in Europe in the years leading up to World War I.
Many historians have examined the impact of the Zionist movement on Middle Eastern politics.
The foundation of Israel in 1948 was the culmination of decades of Zionist movement activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zionism - the movement for the development and protection of a Jewish nation in what is now Israel
  • Antonym: anti-Zionism - opposition to the Zionist movement or to Israel

Etymology

From 'Zionist' (rooted in Zion, a biblical term for Jerusalem) and 'movement'.
