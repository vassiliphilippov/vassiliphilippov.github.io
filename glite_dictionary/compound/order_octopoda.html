order Octopoda

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ɑkˈtɑpədə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌɒktəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a taxonomic group of cephalopod mollusks that includes octopuses and paper nautiluses
taxonomic group: octopuses

Examples

The order Octopoda comprises both the familiar octopus and the paper nautilus.
Scientists study the order Octopoda to better understand cephalopod intelligence.
Members of the order Octopoda are known for their ability to change color and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied, eight-armed cephalopod mollusk

Etymology

From Greek 'okto' (eight) + 'pous' (foot), referring to the eight-limbed nature of its members; used in modern scientific Latin.
