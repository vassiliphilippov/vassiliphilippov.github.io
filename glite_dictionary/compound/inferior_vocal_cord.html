inferior vocal cord

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriər ˈvoʊkəl kɔrd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˈvəʊkəl kɔːd / (BrE)
either of the two lower vocal folds in the larynx that come together to form the glottis and produce vocal tone when air passes between them
lower vocal fold in the larynx
medical

Examples

Damage to the inferior vocal cord can affect a person's voice quality.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully avoided the inferior vocal cord.
The inferior vocal cord plays a crucial role in speech and singing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior vocal cord - either of the two upper vocal folds in the larynx, not involved in sound production
