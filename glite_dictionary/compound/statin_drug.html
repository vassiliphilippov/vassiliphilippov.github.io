statin drug

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætn drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈstatɪn drʌɡ / (BrE)
a type of medicine that lowers cholesterol levels in the blood by inhibiting the enzyme HMG-CoA reductase.
cholesterol-lowering medicine
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed a statin drug to help control my high cholesterol.
Some people experience muscle pain as a side effect of taking a statin drug.
Long-term use of a statin drug can significantly reduce the risk of heart attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statin - drug that inhibits cholesterol synthesis in the liver
  • Synonym: cholesterol-lowering drug - drug used to reduce blood cholesterol levels

Etymology

From 'statin', a class name for this type of drug, derived from chemical nomenclature; 'drug' from Middle English 'drogge'.
