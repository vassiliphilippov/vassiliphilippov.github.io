universal gas constant

1. NOUN

/ juˌnɪvɚsəl ˈɡæs ˌkɑnstənt / (AmE), / juːˌnɪvəsəl ˈɡæs ˌkɒnstənt / (BrE)
the physical constant denoted by R, appearing in the equation of state for ideal gases, equal to approximately 8.3143 joules per kelvin per mole
physical constant in gas equation (R)

Examples

The value of the universal gas constant is essential for calculations in thermodynamics.
Scientists use the universal gas constant when applying the ideal gas law to various substances.
You can find the universal gas constant in most chemistry reference tables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas constant - physical constant in the ideal gas law; also known as R
