acidophilus milk

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪˈdɑːfɪləs mɪlk / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈdɒfɪləs mɪlk / (BrE)
milk fermented with Lactobacillus acidophilus bacteria, often consumed to promote digestive health or treat gastrointestinal disorders
milk fermented with Lactobacillus acidophilus
medical

Examples

Many people drink acidophilus milk to improve their digestion.
Doctors sometimes recommend acidophilus milk as a natural remedy for mild intestinal discomfort.
You can find acidophilus milk in most health food stores among other probiotic products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yogurt - fermented milk product made with different bacteria

Etymology

From New Latin 'acidophilus' (acid-loving), describing the bacteria, combined with 'milk'.
