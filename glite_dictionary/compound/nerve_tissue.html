nerve tissue

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈtɪsjuː / (BrE)
Tissue consisting of neurons and supporting cells, responsible for transmitting nerve impulses throughout the body.
tissue made of neurons

Examples

Damage to the nerve tissue in his spine caused paralysis in his legs.
Scientists are studying how nerve tissue regenerates after injury.
In the human body, nerve tissue forms the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous tissue - tissue composed of neurons and glia
  • Antonym: muscle tissue - tissue that makes muscles and produces movement
