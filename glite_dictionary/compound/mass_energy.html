mass energy

1. NOUN

/ mæs ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / mæs ˈɛnəˌdʒi / (BrE)
(Physics) The energy that a body possesses by virtue of its mass, especially as described by the equation E = mc² in relativity theory.
energy equivalent of mass (relativity)

Examples

According to Einstein's famous equation, mass energy can be converted into other forms of energy.
The concept of mass energy revolutionized modern physics in the twentieth century.
Nuclear reactions demonstrate the transformation of mass energy into heat and light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rest energy - the energy equivalent to the rest mass of a body (E = mc²)
