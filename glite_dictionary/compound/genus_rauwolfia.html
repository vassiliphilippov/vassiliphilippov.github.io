genus Rauwolfia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs raʊˈwʊlfiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs raʊˈwʊlfiə / (BrE)
A pantropical genus of somewhat poisonous shrubs and small trees, some species of which are used in medicine.
genus of poisonous pantropical shrubs and trees

Examples

Several drugs are extracted from the roots of genus Rauwolfia species, particularly in traditional medicine.
Researchers are studying the chemical compounds found in genus Rauwolfia for their therapeutic potential.
Members of the genus Rauwolfia can be found in tropical regions worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rauwolfia serpentina - a species of Rauwolfia used medicinally

Etymology

Named after the German physician Leonhard Rauwolf (1535–1596).
