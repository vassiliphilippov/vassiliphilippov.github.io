Queen's tobacco-pipe

1. NOUN

/ kwiːnz təˈbækoʊ paɪp / (AmE), / kwiːnz təˈbækəʊ paɪp / (BrE)
A type of collectible clay smoking pipe, traditionally associated with a long stem and sometimes linked to 17th or 18th-century England.
type of collectible clay pipe

Examples

The antique shop displayed a Queen's tobacco-pipe among its rare collectibles.
His prized possession was an 18th-century Queen's tobacco-pipe with intricate designs.
Museum visitors admired the delicate craftsmanship of the Queen's tobacco-pipe in the exhibition.
