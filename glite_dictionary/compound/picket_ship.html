picket ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkɪt ˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt ˌʃɪp / (BrE)
A ship stationed as a guard or lookout to warn of enemy approach or movement.
ship stationed for guard or lookout duty

Examples

The navy deployed a picket ship to monitor the enemy’s movements.
A picket ship alerted the fleet to the approaching aircraft.
During the exercise, the picket ship maintained constant radio contact with the command center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patrol boat - a small naval vessel used for patrol duties

Etymology

From 'picket' (a soldier or small unit placed on guard duty), patterned after military usage.
