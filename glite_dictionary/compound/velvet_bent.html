velvet bent

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət bɛnt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt bɛnt / (BrE)
a common grass (Agrostis canina) with slender stems and narrow leaves, often used for lawns and golf courses
a slender, narrow-leaved grass species

Examples

The golf course is famous for its velvet bent greens that provide a smooth putting surface.
Some gardeners prefer planting velvet bent due to its fine texture and adaptability.
The velvet bent thrives in cool, moist conditions and is commonly found in northern Europe.
