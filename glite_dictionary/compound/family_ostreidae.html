family Ostreidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌɑːstriˈiːdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌɒstrɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of bivalve mollusks known as oysters, including many edible species found in marine habitats.
family of oysters (bivalve mollusks)
medical

Examples

The family Ostreidae includes species commonly consumed as delicacies around the world.
Marine biologists study the family Ostreidae to better understand oyster reefs and their ecosystems.
Members of the family Ostreidae are found in both temperate and tropical coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - edible bivalve mollusk with irregular shell

Etymology

From Latin 'ostrea' (oyster) + taxonomic suffix 'idae' indicating a family.
