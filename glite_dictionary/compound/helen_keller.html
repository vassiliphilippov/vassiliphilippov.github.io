Helen Keller

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛlən ˈkɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛlən ˈkɛlə / (BrE)
(1880–1968) American lecturer, author, and activist who was blind and deaf from an early age and became an influential advocate for people with disabilities.
American activist who was blind and deaf

Examples

Helen Keller overcame severe disabilities to become an inspiration to millions around the world.
The story of Helen Keller and her teacher Anne Sullivan is taught in many schools.
Many organizations for the blind honor Helen Keller's legacy.
