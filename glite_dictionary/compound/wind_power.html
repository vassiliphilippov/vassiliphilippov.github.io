wind power

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / wɪnd ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
power derived from the wind, especially for generating electricity using windmills or turbines.
electricity or energy generated by wind

Examples

Many countries are investing heavily in wind power to reduce their reliance on fossil fuels.
The use of wind power has surged as technology for wind turbines becomes more efficient and affordable.
Some regions generate all of their electricity from renewable sources like wind power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solar power - power obtained by harnessing energy from the sun's rays
  • Antonym: hydroelectric power - power generated by harnessing energy from moving water
