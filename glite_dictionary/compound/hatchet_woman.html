hatchet woman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃɪt ˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪt ˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is employed to carry out unpleasant or ruthless tasks on behalf of someone in authority
woman carrying out unpleasant tasks for a superior

Examples

The CEO brought in a hatchet woman to restructure the failing department.
As a feared hatchet woman, she handled layoffs and tough negotiations without flinching.
Rumors spread quickly when the company hired a new hatchet woman known for drastic cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatchet man - a man employed to do unpleasant or ruthless tasks for a superior

Etymology

Modeled after 'hatchet man', from 'hatchet' (a small axe), implying ruthlessness.
