prescription medicine

1. NOUN

/ prəˈskrɪpʃən ˈmɛdəsɪn / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
a drug that can only be obtained with a written order from a qualified medical practitioner
drug available only by doctor's order

Examples

Always follow your doctor's instructions when taking prescription medicine.
Some people misuse prescription medicine by taking more than the recommended dose.
Certain prescription medicine requires strict monitoring by a healthcare professional.

Synset Examples

he told the doctor that he had been taking his prescription regularly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: over-the-counter - medicine that can be bought without a prescription
  • Synonym: prescription drug - another term for prescription medicine
