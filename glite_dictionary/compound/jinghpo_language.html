Jinghpo language

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋ.poʊ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋ.pəʊ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Kachinic language spoken by the Jinghpo people, primarily in Myanmar and parts of China and India
language of the Jinghpo people

Examples

My friend is fluent in the Jinghpo language because her family is originally from Kachin State.
Linguists have studied the Jinghpo language to document its unique grammatical structures.
The Jinghpo language uses a variety of tones that can change the meaning of words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kachin language - another name for the Jinghpo language

Etymology

From Jinghpo, the self-designation of the ethnic group and their language.
