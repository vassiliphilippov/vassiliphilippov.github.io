hyperkinetic syndrome

1. NOUN

/ haɪpɚkɪˈnɛtɪk ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / hʌɪpəkɪˈnɛtɪk ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a medical condition, mainly affecting boys, characterized by excessive activity, behavioral problems, and learning difficulties
a behavioral disorder in children
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with hyperkinetic syndrome after noting persistent hyperactivity and impulsiveness.
Educational interventions can be helpful for students who struggle with hyperkinetic syndrome.
Researchers are studying the causes of hyperkinetic syndrome to find better treatments.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over) + 'kinetikos' (pertaining to motion) and Modern Latin 'syndroma' (syndrome).
