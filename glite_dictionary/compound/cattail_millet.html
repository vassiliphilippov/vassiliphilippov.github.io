cattail millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌteɪl ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈkættaɪl ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a tall grass with thick, cylindrical spikes, cultivated in Africa and Asia for its grain and in the United States mainly for forage
tall grass with grain, cultivated in Africa/Asia

Examples

Farmers in parts of India rely on cattail millet as a dependable source of food and fodder.
The cylindrical spikes of cattail millet are easily recognizable during harvest season.
Some traditional African beers are made using the grain of cattail millet.
