American elder

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈɛl.dɚ / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈɛl.də / (BrE)
a species of elder shrub (Sambucus canadensis) native to central and eastern North America, known for its clusters of purple-black berries used in wines and jellies
North American shrub with purple-black berries

Examples

The American elder produces berries in late summer that are often used for making jam.
You can identify the American elder by its large clusters of tiny white flowers and deeply serrated leaves.
My grandmother used American elder berries to make her famous homemade wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder - shrub or small tree with clusters of white flowers and small berries
