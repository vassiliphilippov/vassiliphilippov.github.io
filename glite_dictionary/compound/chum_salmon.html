chum salmon

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌm ˈsæmən / (AmE), / tʃʌm ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a large species of Pacific salmon (Oncorhynchus keta) with small spots on its back, important as a food fish
a species of Pacific salmon, food fish

Examples

During their autumn migration, thousands of chum salmon return to spawn in Alaskan rivers.
The fishermen hauled in a huge catch of chum salmon before dawn.
Chum salmon is sometimes marketed under the names keta or dog salmon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sockeye salmon - another species of Pacific salmon
  • Degree Lower: pink salmon - a smaller species of Pacific salmon
  • Degree Higher: chinook salmon - the largest species of Pacific salmon
