the fair sex

1. NOUN

/ ðə fɛr sɛks / (AmE), / ðə feə sɛks / (BrE)
women regarded collectively, especially in a chivalrous or idealizing context; an old-fashioned or literary term for women.
an old-fashioned term for women

Examples

In Victorian novels, gentlemen were expected to protect the fair sex at all costs.
Discussions about rights for the fair sex have evolved greatly over the years.
He was known for his charm and kindness towards the fair sex.

Synset Examples

He always treats the fair sex with respect.
Many leaders advocate for the rights of the fair sex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: women - adult human females

Etymology

Refers to the idea of women as possessing beauty ('fair'), popular since the 17th century.
