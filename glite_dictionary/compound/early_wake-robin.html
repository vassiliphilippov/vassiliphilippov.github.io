early wake-robin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrli ˈweɪk ˌrɑbɪn / (AmE), / ˈɜːli ˈweɪk ˌrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A low perennial plant with white flowers, belonging to the trillium family, found in the southeastern United States.
white-flowered trillium of the southeastern U.S.

Examples

The woods were dotted with blooming early wake-robin after the spring rains.
She found a rare early wake-robin growing along the forest trail in Georgia.
Botanists study the early wake-robin for its unique flowering period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of spring-flowering perennial plants

Etymology

Named for its early blooming period and its resemblance to the common name 'wake-robin' for trillium species.
