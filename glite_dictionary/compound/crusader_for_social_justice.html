crusader for social justice

1. NOUN

/ kɹuˈseɪdɚ fəɹ ˈsoʊʃəl ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / kruˈseɪdə fə ˈsəʊʃəl ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
a person who actively campaigns or fights for social equality and fairness
person who advocates for social equality

Examples

As a crusader for social justice, she devoted her life to fighting discrimination.
The mayor has often been described as a crusader for social justice in the city.
Many young people see themselves as crusaders for social justice, participating in protests and advocacy work.

Synset Examples

He was a crusader for social justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activist - a person who campaigns for social or political change
  • Antonym: opponent of social justice - someone who opposes or resists social equality and fairness

Etymology

From 'crusader' (one who fights for a cause), influenced by the historical Crusades, and 'social justice' (fair and equal treatment in society).
