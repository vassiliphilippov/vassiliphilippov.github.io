myrtle bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtəl bɝd / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtl bɜːd / (BrE)
a North American warbler, also known as the yellow-rumped warbler, similar to Audubon's warbler
North American warbler; yellow-rumped warbler

Examples

The myrtle bird can often be seen flitting among the wax myrtle bushes.
Last spring, we spotted a myrtle bird during our birdwatching trip in the park.
Unlike Audubon's warbler, the myrtle bird has a distinct white throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-rumped warbler - a common North American species of warbler, Setophaga coronata
  • Degree Higher: Audubon's warbler - a western form of the yellow-rumped warbler, Setophaga auduboni

Etymology

Named for its association with the wax myrtle plant, whose berries it often eats.
