marsh cress

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ krɛs / (AmE), / mɑːʃ krɛs / (BrE)
an annual or biennial plant growing in damp areas, sometimes eaten in salads or used as a potherb; considered a weed in some regions
a plant found in damp areas, sometimes edible

Examples

The flooded ditch was full of marsh cress growing alongside the reeds.
Some gardeners remove marsh cress to keep it from spreading among native plants.
She picked fresh marsh cress to add a peppery flavor to her salad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - a plant with small edible leaves used in salads
