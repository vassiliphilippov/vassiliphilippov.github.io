horse brier

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
a very prickly woody vine of the eastern United States with tough, round stems, shiny leathery leaves, small greenish flowers, and clusters of inedible shiny black berries
prickly North American woody vine

Examples

The dense thicket of horse brier made it difficult to walk through the woods.
Clusters of black berries dangled from the horse brier vines along the trail.
Gardeners often avoid planting horse brier because of its tough stems and sharp thorns.
