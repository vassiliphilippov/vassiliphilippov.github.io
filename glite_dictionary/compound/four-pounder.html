four-pounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌpaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌpaʊndə / (BrE)
a cannon or artillery gun designed to fire a projectile weighing four pounds
type of artillery gun for four-pound shot

Examples

The army positioned a four-pounder at the edge of the field to support the infantry.
During the battle, the four-pounder proved effective against lightly armored targets.
Historians discovered a well-preserved four-pounder among the ruins of the old fort.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: six-pounder - an artillery gun that throws a shot weighing six pounds
  • Degree Lower: two-pounder - an artillery gun that throws a shot weighing two pounds

Etymology

From 'four' (the number) + 'pounder', denoting a weapon firing a shot of specified weight.
