veal scallopini

1. NOUN

/ vil ˌskæləˈpini / (AmE), / viːl ˌskæl.əˈpiː.ni / (BrE)
A dish consisting of thinly sliced, sautéed veal cutlets, often served with a sauce.
thinly sautéed veal cutlets

Examples

He ordered the veal scallopini with lemon and capers at the Italian restaurant.
The chef recommends serving veal scallopini with a side of fresh pasta.
My grandmother taught me how to make delicious veal scallopini for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schnitzel - a breaded and fried meat cutlet, usually veal or pork
  • Synonym: piccata - Italian dish of thinly sliced meat sautéed and served in a lemon-butter sauce

Etymology

From Italian 'scaloppine', diminutive of 'scaloppa' (thin slice), and 'veal', meaning young beef.
