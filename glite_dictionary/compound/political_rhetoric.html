political rhetoric

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈrɛtərɪk / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈrɛtərɪk / (BrE)
language intended to persuade or influence others, especially in a political context
persuasive language in politics

Examples

The candidate's political rhetoric focused heavily on reform and change.
Many voters were unmoved by the empty promises in his political rhetoric.
Critics accused the party of using divisive political rhetoric to sway the public.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his cant as mere political rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetoric - the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing
