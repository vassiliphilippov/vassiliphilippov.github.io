quantitative relation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑːn.t̬əˌteɪ.t̬ɪv rɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒn.tɪ.tə.tɪv rɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between quantities or measurable magnitudes, especially as expressed in mathematical or scientific terms
relationship between measurable quantities
medical

Examples

Scientists study the quantitative relation between the amount of fertilizer used and crop yield.
The law expresses a precise quantitative relation between pressure and volume in gases.
In his research, he discovered a new quantitative relation among the variables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportionality - the property of being proportional; a specific type of quantitative relation
  • Antonym: qualitative relation - a relationship based on qualities rather than measurable quantities

Etymology

From Latin 'quantitas' (quantity) and Latin 'relatio' (a bringing back, relation).
