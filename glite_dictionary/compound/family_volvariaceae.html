family Volvariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli voʊvɛrɪˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli vɒlˌvɛəriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi within the order Agaricales, characterized by specific morphological features, including a volva at the base of the fruiting body.
family of fungi in Agaricales

Examples

The family Volvariaceae contains several species with distinctive volvas at the base of the mushroom cap.
Mycology students studied members of the family Volvariaceae to understand fungal classification.
This mushroom was identified as belonging to the family Volvariaceae by its characteristic structure.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Volvaria + -aceae (family suffix).
