purple-hooded orchis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˌhʊdɪd ˈɔːrkɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˌhʊdɪd ˈɔːkɪs / (BrE)
A North American orchid with a spike of violet-purple flowers mixed with white, where sepals and petals form a hood.
North American orchid with hooded purple flowers

Examples

The purple-hooded orchis blooms in early spring across parts of eastern North America.
Botanists identified a rare purple-hooded orchis growing near the riverbank.
Many gardeners admire the unique coloration of the purple-hooded orchis in their native plant collections.
