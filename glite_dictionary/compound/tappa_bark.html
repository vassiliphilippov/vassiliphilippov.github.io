tappa bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑpə bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtæpə bɑːk / (BrE)
the thin fibrous bark of the paper mulberry or Pipturus albidus, used especially in the making of tapa cloth in Polynesia and parts of Asia
fibrous bark used for tapa cloth

Examples

Traditional Polynesian crafts often use tappa bark as the main raw material for making cloth.
The process of pounding tappa bark transforms it into a soft, flexible fabric.
Collectors value clothing and artwork made from tappa bark for their historical significance.

Etymology

From Polynesian and Oceanic languages (compare Samoan ‘tapa’); refers to bark used in tapa cloth making.
