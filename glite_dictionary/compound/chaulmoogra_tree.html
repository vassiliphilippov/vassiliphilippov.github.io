chaulmoogra tree

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔlˈmuːɡrə triː / (AmE), / tʃɔːlˈmuːɡrə triː / (BrE)
An East Indian tree whose oily seeds are used to produce chaulmoogra oil, formerly utilized in treating leprosy.
East Indian tree used for medicinal oil

Examples

The chaulmoogra tree is famous for producing oil once used to treat leprosy.
Early medical treatments for leprosy often relied on extracts from the chaulmoogra tree.
Researchers studied the seeds of the chaulmoogra tree in hopes of developing new medicines.

Etymology

From Hindi *chaulmugra*, from Sanskrit *karanja* (tree species), adopted into English during colonial usage for medicinal purposes.
