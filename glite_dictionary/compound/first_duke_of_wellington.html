First Duke of Wellington

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɜrst ˈduːk əv ˌwɛlɪŋˈtən / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈdjuːk əv ˌwɛlɪŋˈtən / (BrE)
the title of Arthur Wellesley (1769–1852), a British general who defeated Napoleon at Waterloo and later served as Prime Minister
British general and statesman (Arthur Wellesley)

Examples

The First Duke of Wellington played a crucial role in defeating Napoleon at the Battle of Waterloo.
Many biographies have been written about the First Duke of Wellington and his influence on European history.
As Prime Minister, the First Duke of Wellington faced several important political challenges.

Related Terms
