white marriage

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmærɪdʒ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmarɪdʒ / (BrE)
A marriage in which the partners do not engage in sexual relations or romantic involvement.
marriage without sexual or romantic relations

Examples

The couple decided on a white marriage to maintain their independence while living together.
Rumors spread about the royal couple's white marriage, though neither ever confirmed it.
For financial convenience, they entered into a white marriage despite lacking a romantic relationship.

Synset Examples

She entered a white marriage to avoid societal pressures.
They chose a white marriage for practical reasons.
Their white marriage allowed them to share expenses.

Etymology

From the use of 'white' to denote purity or non-consummation; the phrase has been used in European contexts since at least the late 19th century.
