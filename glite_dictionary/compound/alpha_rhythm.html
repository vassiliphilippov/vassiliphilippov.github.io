alpha rhythm

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ˌrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˌrɪð.əm / (BrE)
a pattern of brainwave activity with a frequency of 8–12 hertz, typically seen in an awake but relaxed person
a type of brainwave (8–12 Hz) seen when relaxed
medical

Examples

During meditation, the alpha rhythm becomes more prominent in the brain's electrical activity.
Doctors observed the patient's alpha rhythm while she rested quietly with her eyes closed.
A normal alpha rhythm generally indicates that the person is awake but in a relaxed state.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beta rhythm - brainwave pattern of higher frequency (13–30 Hz), associated with active thinking
  • Degree Lower: theta rhythm - slower brainwave pattern (4–8 Hz), seen in light sleep or drowsiness

Etymology

From Greek alpha (the first letter of the Greek alphabet) + rhythm, reflecting its identification as the first type of brain rhythm described.
