American frogbit

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈfrɑgˌbɪt / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈfrɒgˌbɪt / (BrE)
An aquatic plant native to North America, with roundish heart-shaped or kidney-shaped floating leaves, typically found rooted in muddy bottoms of ponds and ditches.
North American aquatic plant with floating leaves

Examples

Many ponds in the region are covered with American frogbit during the summer.
The leaves of American frogbit float on the surface, providing shelter for small aquatic animals.
He studied how American frogbit adapts to varying water depths.
