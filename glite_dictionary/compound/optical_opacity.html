optical opacity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl oʊˈpæsɪti / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl əʊˈpæsɪti / (BrE)
The degree to which a material does not allow light to pass through; the property of being opaque to light.
lack of transparency to light

Examples

The optical opacity of the fog made it impossible to see the road ahead.
Scientists study the optical opacity of different materials for use in protective eyewear.
Increasing the optical opacity of the solution prevented any light from passing through.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of allowing light to pass through
  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque

Etymology

From 'optic' (relating to sight or light) and 'opacity' (from Latin 'opacitas', meaning shadiness or darkness).
