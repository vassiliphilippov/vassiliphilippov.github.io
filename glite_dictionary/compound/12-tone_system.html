12-tone system

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɛlv toʊn ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈtwɛlv təʊn ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a method of musical composition using all twelve semitones of the chromatic scale in a fixed order (tone row), typically associated with serial music and developed by Arnold Schoenberg.
method of musical composition using all twelve tones

Examples

Schoenberg's development of the 12-tone system marked a turning point in modern music.
Composers like Berg and Webern also experimented with the 12-tone system in their works.
The 12-tone system requires the use of all twelve notes before repeating any.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serialism - a compositional technique based on a series of values, such as pitches or rhythms

Etymology

Formed from '12-tone' (referring to the twelve semitones in the chromatic scale) plus 'system'. Popularized in the early 20th century by Arnold Schoenberg.
