magnetic moment

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnɛtɪk ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / mæɡˌnɛtɪk ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
A measure of the strength and orientation of a magnet or dipole's ability to exert torque in a magnetic field.
measure of magnetic strength and orientation

Examples

The magnetic moment of an electron determines how it interacts with a magnetic field.
Scientists calculated the magnetic moment of the sample to better understand its properties.
A bar magnet's magnetic moment points from its south to north pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - the physical phenomenon produced by moving electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces
