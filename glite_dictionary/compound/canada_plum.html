Canada plum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænədə plʌm / (AmE), / ˈkanədə plʌm / (BrE)
A small tree native to northeastern North America, producing oblong orange-red fruit.
small North American tree with orange-red fruit

Examples

The Canada plum thrives in moist soils across the northeastern United States and Canada.
In spring, the Canada plum displays clusters of white flowers before it bears fruit.
Local farmers cultivate Canada plum trees for their tart, flavorful plums used in preserves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American plum - a related plum species native to North America
