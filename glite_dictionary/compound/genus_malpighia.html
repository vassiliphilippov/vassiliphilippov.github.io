genus Malpighia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs mælˈpɪɡ.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs malˈpɪɡ.ɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs and trees in the family Malpighiaceae, native to the Americas, which includes the Barbados cherry.
a genus of tropical shrubs/trees (Malpighiaceae)

Examples

The genus Malpighia includes several species valued for their edible fruit.
Botanists have studied the genus Malpighia for clues about tropical plant evolution.
Acerola, a rich source of vitamin C, belongs to the genus Malpighia.

Etymology

From Latin Malpighia, named after Marcello Malpighi (1628–1694), Italian biologist.
