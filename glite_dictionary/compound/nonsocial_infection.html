nonsocial infection

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈsoʊʃəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / nɒnˈsəʊʃəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection acquired at a hospital or other healthcare facility, not present or incubating prior to admission
infection acquired in a healthcare facility
medical

Examples

Proper hand hygiene can reduce the risk of nonsocial infection during hospital stays.
Several patients developed a nonsocial infection while recovering from surgery.
Protocols are in place to limit nonsocial infections in intensive care units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nosocomial infection - an infection contracted in a hospital

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'social' + 'infection', denoting an infection not contracted through social contact but rather in institutional settings.
