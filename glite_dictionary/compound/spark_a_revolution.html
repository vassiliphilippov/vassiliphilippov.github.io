to spark a revolution

1. VERB

/ spɑrk ə ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / spɑːk ə ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
To cause or initiate a major change or uprising, often suddenly or decisively.
cause a major change or uprising

Examples

Her bold speech sparked a revolution that changed the country forever.
Social media has been known to spark a revolution in how people communicate and organize.
Historians argue that economic hardship often sparks a revolution among dissatisfied populations.

Synset Examples

The philosophe's ideas sparked a revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - to set in motion, to initiate; start something burning
  • Synonym: start a rebellion - to begin an organized opposition or uprising
