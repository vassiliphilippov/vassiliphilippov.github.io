oxidation state

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːksɪˈdeɪʃən steɪt / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdeɪʃən steɪt / (BrE)
The degree of oxidation of an atom, ion, or molecule, represented by the number of electrons it has gained, lost, or shared when forming chemical bonds.
measure of electron loss or gain in chemistry
medical

Examples

The oxidation state of iron in Fe2O3 is +3.
Chlorine can have more than one oxidation state depending on the compound.
When balancing redox reactions, it is important to know the oxidation state of each element.

Synset Examples

the oxidation number of hydrogen is +1 and of oxygen is -2

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxidation number - alternative term for oxidation state in chemistry

Etymology

From 'oxidation' (Latin 'oxygenium' + '-ation') and 'state', meaning condition or status.
