red scare

1. NOUN

/ rɛd skɛr / (AmE), / rɛd skeə / (BrE)
a period of widespread fear and anxiety about the rise of communism, especially in the United States during the early and mid-20th century
period of widespread fear of communism

Examples

The red scare led to widespread suspicion and the blacklisting of many individuals in Hollywood.
During the first red scare, the government cracked down on suspected radicals and anarchists.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of the red scare on American society and politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: McCarthyism - the practice of making accusations of subversion or treason without proper regard for evidence
  • Synonym: witch hunt - a campaign against a particular group of people, typically based on unsubstantiated charges

Etymology

From 'red' (symbolizing communism) and 'scare' (sudden fear); used from the early 20th century.
