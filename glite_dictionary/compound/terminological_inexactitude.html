terminological inexactitude

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɝː.mə.nəˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl ˌɪn.ɪɡˈzæk.tɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˌtɜː.mɪ.nəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl ˌɪn.ɪɡˈzæk.tɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the use of language that is intentionally or unintentionally imprecise or misleading
imprecise or misleading language

Examples

The politician resorted to terminological inexactitude to avoid answering the journalist's question directly.
Accusations of terminological inexactitude undermined the credibility of the company's public statements.
The lawyer pointed out several instances of terminological inexactitude in the opposing counsel's argument.

Synset Examples

His speech was full of terminological inexactitude, confusing everyone.
The report was criticized for its terminological inexactitude on the subject.
We must avoid terminological inexactitude in our legal documents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive
  • Synonym: euphemism - a mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt

Etymology

Originates from a phrase famously used by British politician Winston Churchill as a euphemism for lying. Based on "terminology" (the study of terms) and "inexactitude" (lack of precision).
