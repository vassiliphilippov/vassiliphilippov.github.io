typhus fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.fəs ˈfiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.fəs ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease caused by rickettsiae, transmitted by lice or fleas, and characterized by high fever, headache, and a skin rash
infectious disease with fever and rash
medical

Examples

The outbreak of typhus fever devastated the refugee camp, causing widespread panic.
Doctors worked tirelessly to control the spread of typhus fever after reports of new cases in the city.
Historical records describe typhus fever as a major cause of death during wars and famines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typhoid fever - a different infectious disease with fever, caused by Salmonella typhi

Etymology

From Greek typhos (smoke, stupor), describing the delirium caused by the disease.
