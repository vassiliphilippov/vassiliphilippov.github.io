mouth-to-mouth resuscitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaʊθ tə ˈmaʊθ rɪˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaʊθ tə ˈmaʊθ rɪˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a first aid procedure in which a person breathes air into the lungs of someone who has stopped breathing, in an attempt to revive them
emergency first aid breathing technique
medical

Examples

A lifeguard performed mouth-to-mouth resuscitation on the boy until he started breathing again.
In emergencies, knowing how to give mouth-to-mouth resuscitation can save lives.
The nurse taught the students the basics of mouth-to-mouth resuscitation during the first aid class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiopulmonary resuscitation - an emergency procedure combining chest compressions with artificial ventilation

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaʊθ tə ˈmaʊθ rəˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaʊθ tə ˈmaʊθ rɪˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a first aid method of artificial respiration in which one person breathes air into the lungs of another through direct mouth-to-mouth contact
method of artificial respiration
medical

Examples

The lifeguard immediately began mouth-to-mouth resuscitation when he saw the swimmer wasn't breathing.
Knowing how to perform mouth-to-mouth resuscitation can save lives in emergency situations.
Paramedics arrived quickly and continued mouth-to-mouth resuscitation until the patient started breathing again.

Synset Examples

He performed mouth-to-mouth resuscitation to save her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial respiration - a technique for forcing air into and out of the lungs of someone not breathing
