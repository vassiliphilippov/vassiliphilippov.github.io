radiocarbon dating

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˌkɑrbən ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪəʊˌkɑːbən ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a method for determining the age of an object containing organic material by measuring the amount of carbon-14 it contains
method of dating organic materials by carbon-14 content

Examples

Scientists used radiocarbon dating to estimate the age of the ancient manuscript.
The bones were analyzed using radiocarbon dating techniques to determine when the animal lived.
Radiocarbon dating has revolutionized archaeology by providing accurate dates for organic remains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon dating - dating by determining amount of carbon-14

Etymology

Radiocarbon from 'radio-' (radioactivity) and 'carbon,' dating from the 1940s.
