cast iron

1. NOUN

/ kæst ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / kɑːst ˈaɪən / (BrE)
A hard, brittle alloy of iron containing a high proportion of carbon, used for casting into shapes but unsuitable for forging or bending.
brittle iron alloy used in casting

Examples

The antique cookware was made entirely of cast iron and weighed several pounds.
The engineer recommended cast iron pipes for the new sewage system due to their durability.
Despite its strength, cast iron is too brittle for certain kinds of structural work.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: steel - an alloy of iron with less carbon, less brittle than cast iron
  • Degree Lower: wrought iron - iron with very low carbon content, more malleable than cast iron

Etymology

From 'cast' (to shape by pouring molten material into a mold) + 'iron'.
