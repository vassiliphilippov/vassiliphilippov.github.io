bowler hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlər hæt / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlə hæt / (BrE)
a hard, round felt hat with a narrow brim, traditionally worn by men
round, hard felt hat with a narrow brim

Examples

He placed his bowler hat carefully on the coat rack.
The detective was instantly recognizable by his bowler hat and umbrella.
In Victorian England, the bowler hat symbolized a respectable, middle-class man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derby - another name for bowler hat, mainly US

Etymology

Named after English hatmakers Thomas and William Bowler, who designed it in 1849.
