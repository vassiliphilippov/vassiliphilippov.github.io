order Rhynchocephalia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌrɪŋkoʊˌsɛˈfeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌrɪŋkəʊˌsɛˈfeɪlɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of reptiles that includes modern tuataras and various extinct forms from the Middle Triassic period
order of reptiles including tuataras and extinct relatives

Examples

The order Rhynchocephalia is known today only by the surviving tuatara species of New Zealand.
Fossils belonging to the order Rhynchocephalia provide valuable insights into the evolution of reptiles.
Scientists believe that the order Rhynchocephalia originated during the middle Triassic period.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhynchos' (snout) and 'kephalē' (head), referring to the beaked head shape.
