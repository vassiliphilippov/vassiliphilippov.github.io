anticipatory retaliation

1. NOUN

/ ænˌtɪsəˌpeɪtəri ˌrɛtəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ænˌtɪsɪˌpeɪtəri ˌrɛtəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
Action taken in advance to counter or deter a perceived threat, especially before an actual attack occurs.
preemptive retaliatory action

Examples

The military justified their anticipatory retaliation by citing credible intelligence of an imminent attack.
Engaging in anticipatory retaliation can sometimes prevent larger conflicts from erupting.
Critics argue that anticipatory retaliation risks punishing parties before any real harm is done.

Synset Examples

Anticipatory retaliation can escalate conflicts quickly.
His anticipatory retaliation surprised everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retaliation - action taken in return for an injury or offense
  • Synonym: preemptive strike - a first strike made to prevent an expected attack
