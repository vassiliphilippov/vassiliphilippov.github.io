vitamin K

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtəmɪn keɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəmɪn keɪ / (BrE)
a fat-soluble vitamin essential for blood clotting and bone health
fat-soluble vitamin essential for blood clotting
medical

Examples

Green leafy vegetables are a good source of vitamin K in the diet.
The doctor prescribed a vitamin K supplement to help improve blood clotting.
Newborns are often given a vitamin K injection to prevent bleeding disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin D - a fat-soluble vitamin that promotes calcium absorption and bone health
  • Synonym: vitamin C - a water-soluble vitamin essential for growth and immune function

Etymology

From 'vitamin' (from Latin 'vita' meaning 'life') and 'K', from German 'Koagulationsvitamin', due to its role in coagulation.
