azygous vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.zɪ.ɡəs veɪn / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.zɪ.ɡəs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein running up the right side of the thoracic vertebral column that drains blood from the thoracic and abdominal walls into the superior vena cava
a vein draining the thoracic wall into the superior vena cava
medical

Examples

The azygous vein arches over the right lung before entering the superior vena cava.
On the CT scan, an enlarged azygous vein was observed in the mediastinum.
Surgeons must be cautious not to damage the azygous vein during procedures on the posterior thoracic wall.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'zygon' (yoke), meaning 'unpaired'.
