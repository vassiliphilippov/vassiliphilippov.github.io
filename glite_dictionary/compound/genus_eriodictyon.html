genus Eriodictyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɛri.əˈdɪktioʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɛri.əˈdɪktɪən / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen shrubs native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for their medicinal uses.
a genus of evergreen shrubs from southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

Several species in the genus Eriodictyon have been used in traditional medicine.
The genus Eriodictyon includes plants commonly known as yerba santa.
Botanists are studying the ecological role of the genus Eriodictyon in arid environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'genus' (kind) and Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'diktyon' (net), referring to the net-like veins of the leaves.
