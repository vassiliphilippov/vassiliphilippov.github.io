Master of the Rolls

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.tɚ əv ðə roʊlz / (AmE), / ˌmɑː.stər əv ðə rəʊlz / (BrE)
the senior judge in charge of the civil division of the Court of Appeal in England and Wales
senior judge in the English Court of Appeal
legal british

Examples

The Master of the Rolls issued a landmark ruling in the case.
After his appointment as Master of the Rolls, he was widely respected in the judiciary.
The legal community awaited the Master of the Rolls's decision with great interest.

Synset Examples

As Master of the Rolls, he ensured justice was served.
The Master of the Rolls is a crucial figure in law.

Etymology

From the historical responsibility for keeping court rolls (records); 'rolls' refers to official registers kept on rolls of parchment.
