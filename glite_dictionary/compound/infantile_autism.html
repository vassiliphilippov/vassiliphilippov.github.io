infantile autism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnfənˌtaɪl ɔˈtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɪnfəntaɪl ɔːˈtɪzəm / (BrE)
a rare and serious childhood disorder characterized by withdrawal, lack of social interaction, and significant language deficits
childhood disorder marked by social and language deficits
medical

Examples

Infantile autism is often diagnosed before a child turns three years old.
The parents sought treatment after noticing signs of infantile autism in their child.
Many medical professionals now refer to infantile autism as part of the autism spectrum disorder.

Synset Examples

there is considerable dispute among specialists concerning infantile autism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autism - a developmental disorder marked by problems with social interaction and communication

Etymology

From Latin 'infantilis' (of an infant) and Greek 'autismos' (self-ism, withdrawal into self).
