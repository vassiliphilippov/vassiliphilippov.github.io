mascarene grass

1. NOUN

/ mæs.kəˈrin ɡræs / (AmE), / mas.kəˈriːn ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A creeping perennial grass native to Asia, often used as a drought-resistant lawn grass, especially in the southern United States.
creeping Asian drought-resistant lawn grass

Examples

Some homeowners in hot climates prefer mascarene grass for its ability to stay green during droughts.
The landscaper recommended planting mascarene grass because it spreads quickly and needs little watering.
New lawns of mascarene grass require less maintenance compared to traditional turf varieties.

Etymology

Named after the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.
