to plug and chug

1. VERB

/ plʌɡ ənd tʃʌɡ / (AmE), / plʌɡ ənd tʃʌɡ / (BrE)
(informal) To solve a problem by directly substituting values into a formula and carrying out straightforward calculations, often without deep understanding.
solve by direct substitution into formula
informal

Examples

Sometimes math problems are easy if you just plug and chug the numbers into the equation.
Rather than analyzing the problem, he decided to plug and chug to get the answer quickly.
The instructor reminded us not to only plug and chug but also to understand the underlying concepts.

Synset Examples

It's all about plug and chug in this formula.
In math, you just plug and chug the numbers.
She prefers to plug and chug when solving equations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calculate - to determine mathematically, often by computation
