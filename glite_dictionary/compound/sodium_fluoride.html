sodium fluoride

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈflɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline salt (NaF) used especially in the fluoridation of water supplies and in dental products to prevent tooth decay
compound used to prevent tooth decay
medical

Examples

Most toothpastes contain sodium fluoride to help prevent cavities.
The city council voted to add sodium fluoride to the public water supply.
Excessive consumption of sodium fluoride can be harmful, so recommended levels are strictly regulated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluoride - a compound of fluorine, especially used in dental care

Etymology

Formed from 'sodium' (from Medieval Latin 'sodium') and 'fluoride' (from Latin 'fluor', meaning 'flow').
