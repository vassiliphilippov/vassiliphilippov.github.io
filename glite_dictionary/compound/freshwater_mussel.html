freshwater mussel

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃˌwɔtər ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃwɔːtə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a bivalve mollusk that inhabits rivers, lakes, and streams, especially in North America
bivalve mollusk found in freshwater

Examples

The freshwater mussel population has declined due to pollution and habitat loss.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of the freshwater mussel in the Mississippi River.
Some species of freshwater mussel are considered endangered and need protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - type of shellfish with a hinged shell
