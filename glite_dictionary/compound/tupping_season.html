tupping season

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌpɪŋ ˌsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈtʌpɪŋ ˌsiːzn̩ / (BrE)
The period during which sheep are bred, typically in the autumn.
sheep breeding season (autumn)

Examples

The farmers prepared their flock carefully for the tupping season in late September.
It is important to manage nutrition before and during the tupping season to ensure successful breeding.
During tupping season, rams are introduced to the ewes to start the breeding process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeding season - the period when animals mate and reproduce

Etymology

From 'tupping' (mating of sheep; from 'tup', a ram) + 'season'.
