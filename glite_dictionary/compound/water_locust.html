water locust

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˈloʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈləʊkəst / (BrE)
A tree of the southern United States swamps and bottomlands, known for its short oval pods and dark, heavy wood.
tree of wet U.S. regions with heavy wood

Examples

The dense wood of the water locust is valued for construction in some regions.
Water locust trees thrive in the swamps and floodplains of the southern United States.
He pointed out a water locust by its distinctive short, oval seed pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey locust - a North American tree with clusters of fragrant flowers and long, flat pods
