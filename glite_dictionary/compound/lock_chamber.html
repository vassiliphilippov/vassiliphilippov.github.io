lock chamber

1. NOUN

/ lɑk ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / lɒk ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
an enclosed section of a canal with gates at each end, used to raise or lower boats by adjusting the water level
section of canal for raising/lowering boats

Examples

The boat waited in the lock chamber as the water level began to rise.
Each lock chamber can accommodate vessels up to 100 meters long.
After closing the gates, the crew pumped water into the lock chamber to lift the barge.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lock (canal) - a device in a canal for raising and lowering boats
