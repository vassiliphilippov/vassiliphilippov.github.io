biochemical mechanism

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈkɛm.ɪ.kəl ˈmɛk.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈkɛm.ɪ.kəl ˈmɛk.əni.zəm / (BrE)
a chemical process or sequence of events that occurs in living organisms and is fundamental to life
chemical process in living organisms

Examples

Researchers are studying the biochemical mechanism responsible for cell division.
Understanding the biochemical mechanism of photosynthesis has advanced agricultural science.
Some diseases are caused by disruptions in a key biochemical mechanism within the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biochemical process - a process involving chemical reactions in living organisms

Etymology

From 'biochemical' (relating to chemistry of living organisms) and 'mechanism' (from Greek mēkhanē, device).
