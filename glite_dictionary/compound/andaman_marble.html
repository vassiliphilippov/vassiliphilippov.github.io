Andaman marble

1. NOUN

/ ˈændəmən ˈmɑr.bəl / (AmE), / ˈændəmən ˈmɑː.bəl / (BrE)
a large tree native to Asia, especially the Andaman Islands, with hard wood marked in a marbled or zebra-like pattern.
large Asian tree with marbled wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from rare Andaman marble, prized for its distinctive swirling patterns.
A plantation of Andaman marble trees was established to supply the timber industry.
Experts identified the log as Andaman marble due to the unique markings on its wood.
