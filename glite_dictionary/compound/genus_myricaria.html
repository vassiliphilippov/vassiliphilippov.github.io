genus Myricaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌmaɪ.rɪˈkɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmɪ.rɪˈkeə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous shrubs or subshrubs native to southern Europe, Siberia, and China, known for their tolerance of chalky soil.
a genus of resilient deciduous shrubs

Examples

The genus Myricaria thrives along riverbanks in chalky soils across Eurasia.
Several ornamental plants belong to the genus Myricaria, prized for their feathery foliage.
Recent studies have focused on the ecological adaptation of the genus Myricaria to arid environments.

Etymology

From Latin 'myrica', meaning tamarisk, and New Latin scientific naming conventions for genera.
