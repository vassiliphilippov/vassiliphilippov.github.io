Iron Chancellor

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn ˈtʃænsələr / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˈtʃɑːnsələ / (BrE)
a nickname for Otto von Bismarck, the German statesman who unified Germany in the late 19th century
nickname for Otto von Bismarck, German unifier

Examples

Otto von Bismarck is often remembered as the Iron Chancellor who shaped modern Germany.
The Iron Chancellor used his political skills to unite the German states in the 19th century.
Historians frequently refer to Bismarck as the Iron Chancellor due to his uncompromising leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Otto von Bismarck - German statesman (1815–1898), unified Germany

Etymology

Refers to Otto von Bismarck's reputation for strength and resolve; iron symbolizes firmness, and chancellor was his political title.
