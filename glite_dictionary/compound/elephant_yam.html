elephant yam

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfənt jæm / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfənt jam / (BrE)
a large, putrid-smelling aroid plant native to southeastern Asia, especially the Philippines, cultivated for its edible tuber
a tropical plant with edible tuber

Examples

The roots of the elephant yam are harvested and eaten as a delicacy in many Asian countries.
Farmers in India often cultivate elephant yam for its high nutritional value and commercial potential.
Despite its strong odor, elephant yam is popular in traditional Filipino dishes.
