come-all-ye

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌm ɔl ˈji / (AmE), / ˌkʌm ɔːl ˈjiː / (BrE)
an event or gathering that is open to everyone; an invitation for all to join
open event or gathering for everyone

Examples

The community center will host a come-all-ye this Saturday evening to welcome new residents.
Their annual picnic is always a come-all-ye, with everyone encouraged to bring food and friends.
At the festival, the come-all-ye spirit made everyone feel included and welcome.

Synset Examples

The festival was a come-all-ye event for the community.
They organized a come-all-ye gathering to celebrate the holiday.
The club's come-all-ye meetings attract many new members.

Etymology

From the traditional phrase 'come all ye', often used in Irish and Scottish folk songs to invite everyone to participate.
