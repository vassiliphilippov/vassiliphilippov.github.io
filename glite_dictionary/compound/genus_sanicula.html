genus Sanicula

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs səˈnɪk.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs səˈnɪk.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly American perennial herbs in the parsley family, known as sanicle.
genus of American herbs (sanicle)

Examples

Many woodland plants belong to the genus Sanicula.
The genus Sanicula includes several species with medicinal uses.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the genus Sanicula in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanicle - a common name for herbs of the genus Sanicula

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanicula,' the type genus name.
