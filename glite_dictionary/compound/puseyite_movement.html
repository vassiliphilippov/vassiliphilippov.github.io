Puseyite movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːziˌaɪt ˈmuːv.mənt / (AmE), / ˈpjuːziˌaɪt ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
A 19th-century movement within the Anglican Church, advocating revival of Catholic traditions and practices.
19th-century Anglican revival movement

Examples

The Puseyite movement sought to reintroduce older rituals into Anglican worship.
Many Victorian-era theologians were influenced by the Puseyite movement.
The reforms proposed by the Puseyite movement sparked much debate within the Church of England.

Synset Examples

The Puseyite movement influenced many church reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oxford Movement - A similar movement advocating Anglican Church reform

Etymology

Named after Edward Bouverie Pusey, one of the movement's leaders.
