sounding board

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A thin board in a musical instrument that vibrates to reinforce and amplify its sound.
board in instrument amplifying sound

Examples

The guitar's sounding board is essential for producing a rich, resonant tone.
Luthiers carefully select wood for the sounding board to enhance the instrument's quality.
Damage to the piano's sounding board can significantly affect its acoustic properties.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a person whose opinions or reactions are used to test or develop ideas before taking further action
person for testing ideas or reactions

Examples

Whenever I have a new business idea, I use my brother as a sounding board.
It's helpful to have a sounding board when making difficult decisions.
She acted as a sounding board for his proposals before he presented them to the team.

Synset Examples

I would use newspapermen as a sounding board for such policies

Etymology

Originally from the literal sense of a board placed behind a speaker to reflect sound, later used figuratively.
