substantia nigra

1. NOUN

/ səbˈstænʃiə ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / sʌbˌstan(t)siə ˈnɪɡrə / (BrE)
a darkly pigmented layer of grey matter in the midbrain that is involved in the regulation of movement and is affected in Parkinson's disease and other neurological disorders
dark grey matter region in the midbrain
medical

Examples

Damage to the substantia nigra is a hallmark feature of Parkinson's disease.
Researchers are studying how the substantia nigra impacts motor control and coordination.
Loss of dopamine-producing neurons in the substantia nigra leads to movement disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midbrain - the central part of the brainstem

Etymology

From Latin 'substantia' (substance) and 'nigra' (black), referring to its dark coloration.
