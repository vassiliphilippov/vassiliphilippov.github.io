lined snake

1. NOUN

/ laɪnd sneɪk / (AmE), / laɪnd sneɪk / (BrE)
A small, secretive snake (Tropidoclonion lineatum) found in the central United States, known for its striped appearance and preference for habitats such as city dumps, parks, prairies, and open woods.
a small, striped North American snake

Examples

We saw a lined snake slithering near the edge of the park after a rainstorm.
The lined snake is often overlooked because of its small size and secretive habits.
Researchers discovered that the lined snake primarily feeds on earthworms in grassy areas.
