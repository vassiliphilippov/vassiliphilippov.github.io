sac fungus

1. NOUN

/ sæk ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / sæk ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
any fungus of the phylum Ascomycota, characterized by producing spores in a sac-like structure called an ascus.
fungus producing spores in a sac (ascus)

Examples

Penicillium, a common sac fungus, is widely used in the production of antibiotics.
The yeast used in baking and brewing is a type of sac fungus.
Morels are a prized edible sac fungus found in many forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascomycete - fungus of the phylum Ascomycota (ascomycetous fungus)

Etymology

From 'sac', referring to the ascus, and 'fungus', from Latin 'fungus' (mushroom).
