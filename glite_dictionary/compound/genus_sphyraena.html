genus Sphyraena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sfɪˈriːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sfɪˈriːnə / (BrE)
The genus of fish comprising the barracudas, and the sole genus in the family Sphyraenidae.
genus of barracudas

Examples

The genus Sphyraena includes several species of large, predatory marine fish known as barracudas.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Sphyraena in the Indo-Pacific region.
All members of the genus Sphyraena are characterized by their elongated bodies and fearsome teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracuda - a large predatory tropical marine fish

Etymology

From Latin 'Sphyraena', used as a genus name in scientific classification.
