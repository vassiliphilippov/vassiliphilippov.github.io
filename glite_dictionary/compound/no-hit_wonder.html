no-hit wonder

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊ hɪt ˈwʌndər / (AmE), / ˌnəʊ hɪt ˈwʌndə / (BrE)
a person, group, or work that fails to achieve popularity or any notable success, particularly in the music industry
unsuccessful person or work, esp. in music
informal

Examples

After three failed singles, the band was dismissed as a no-hit wonder by critics.
Her book received little attention and she soon gained a reputation as a no-hit wonder.
It's hard to recover in show business once you've been labeled a no-hit wonder.

Synset Examples

She was labeled a no-hit wonder after multiple flops.
His last album turned out to be a no-hit wonder.
That band is just a no-hit wonder, unfortunately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one-hit wonder - person or group known for only one popular success

Etymology

Modeled after 'one-hit wonder', combining 'no hit' (no popular success) with 'wonder'.
