vigil light

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdʒəl laɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪdʒɪl laɪt / (BrE)
A candle, often placed before an image or altar, lit by worshippers in a church as an act of devotion or prayer.
candle lit in church as devotion

Examples

She knelt before the altar and lit a vigil light in memory of her grandmother.
Dozens of vigil lights flickered quietly in the darkened chapel during the midnight service.
He placed a fresh vigil light next to the statue of the saint and whispered a silent prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: votive candle - a candle lit as an offering or in fulfillment of a vow

Etymology

From 'vigil', meaning a period of keeping awake for devotional purposes, combined with 'light' for the candle.
