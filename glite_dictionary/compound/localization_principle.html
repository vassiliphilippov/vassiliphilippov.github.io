localization principle

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ.kə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprɪn.sə.pəl / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl / (BrE)
The principle that specific functions are associated with particular, localized areas in an organ or part of the body, especially in the brain.
principle of specific functions having distinct locations

Examples

The localization principle suggests that different brain regions are responsible for distinct cognitive functions.
Researchers have used the localization principle to map language abilities to specific parts of the brain.
Early studies of the localization principle shaped our understanding of neural organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: localization - the act of restricting to a particular place or position
