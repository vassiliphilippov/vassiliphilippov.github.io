Old Ironsides

1. PROPN

/ oʊld ˈaɪrnˌsaɪdz / (AmE), / əʊld ˈaɪənˌsaɪdz / (BrE)
the nickname for the USS Constitution, a historic 44-gun United States frigate famed for victories in the War of 1812; now preserved as a museum ship in Boston
historic United States Navy frigate
american

Examples

The crew of Old Ironsides celebrated each victory with a rousing cheer.
Tourists visit Boston to walk the decks of Old Ironsides and learn about its storied past.
During the War of 1812, Old Ironsides became a symbol of American naval strength.

Etymology

Nickname given early in the ship's career due to the resilience of her wooden hull in battle; 'iron sides' implies strong, impenetrable sides.
