Bow Street Runner

1. NOUN

/ boʊ strit ˈrʌnər / (AmE), / bəʊ striːt ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
a member of the first organized police force in London, active from the mid-18th to early 19th centuries
historical London police officer
archaic

Examples

The novel features a Bow Street Runner investigating a string of robberies in 18th-century London.
Dressed as a Bow Street Runner, he explained the role to visiting students at the museum.
Before the Metropolitan Police, Bow Street Runners patrolled London's streets and tracked down criminals.

Synset Examples

He dressed as a Bow Street Runner for the historical play.
Historically, the Bow Street Runners were London's first police force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police officer - a person whose job is to enforce laws and keep public order
  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes

Etymology

Named after Bow Street in London, where the force was based.
