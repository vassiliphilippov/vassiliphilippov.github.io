genetic map

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk mæp / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛtɪk mæp / (BrE)
A diagram showing the relative positions of genes on a chromosome.
diagram of gene locations on a chromosome
medical

Examples

Scientists used a genetic map to identify the location of certain inherited traits.
The creation of a genetic map helped researchers understand the structure of the fruit fly genome.
Modern medicine relies on the genetic map to detect genes related to diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromosome map - a diagram showing the relative positions of genes on a chromosome

Etymology

From 'genetic' (relating to genes) and 'map' (diagram of location or relationship).
