Tyrrhenian Sea

1. PROPN

/ tɪˈriːniən ˈsiː / (AmE), / tɪˈriːniən ˈsiː / (BrE)
the part of the Mediterranean Sea located between the western coast of Italy and the islands of Corsica, Sardinia, and Sicily
sea between Italy, Corsica, Sardinia, and Sicily

Examples

Many cruise ships travel through the Tyrrhenian Sea on their way to the Italian coast.
The Tyrrhenian Sea is known for its deep blue waters and scenic islands.
Scientists are studying marine life unique to the Tyrrhenian Sea.

Etymology

From Latin 'Mare Tyrrhenum', named after the Tyrrhenians, an ancient people of the region.
