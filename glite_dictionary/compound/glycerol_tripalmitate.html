glycerol tripalmitate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsəˌrɔl traɪˈpælmɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərəʊl traɪˈpælmɪteɪt / (BrE)
A triglyceride in which all three fatty acid groups are composed of palmitic acid.
triglyceride of palmitic acid
medical

Examples

Glycerol tripalmitate is commonly found in animal fats and some plant oils.
Chemists analyzed the properties of glycerol tripalmitate under different temperatures.
The breakdown of glycerol tripalmitate yields glycerol and palmitic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tristearin - a triglyceride formed from stearic acid
  • Synonym: palmitic acid - a common saturated fatty acid found in fats and waxes

Etymology

From 'glycerol', a simple polyol compound, and 'tripalmitate', indicating three palmitic acid esters.
