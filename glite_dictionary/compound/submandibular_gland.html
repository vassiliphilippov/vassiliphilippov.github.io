submandibular gland

1. NOUN

/ sʌb.mænˈdɪbjələr ˈɡlænd / (AmE), / sʌb.mænˈdɪb.jʊ.lə ˈɡlænd / (BrE)
either of a pair of salivary glands located beneath the lower jaw, responsible for producing a significant portion of saliva, especially during the night
salivary gland under the lower jaw
medical

Examples

The submandibular gland can sometimes become swollen due to infection.
Surgery may be required to remove a stone from the submandibular gland.
The doctor checked the submandibular gland during the oral examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parotid gland - major salivary gland located near the ear
  • Synonym: sublingual gland - salivary gland beneath the tongue

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) and 'mandibula' (jaw).
