shaft encoder

1. NOUN

/ ʃæft ɪnˈkoʊdər / (AmE), / ʃɑːft ɪnˈkəʊdə / (BrE)
an electro-mechanical device that converts the angular position or motion of a shaft or axle into an analog or digital code
device for measuring shaft position or motion

Examples

The shaft encoder provided real-time position feedback to the robotic arm.
If the shaft encoder fails, the system may lose track of the motor’s position.
Engineers installed a shaft encoder to monitor the rotation of the conveyor belt.

Synset Examples

Rotary encoders are used in many applications that require precise shaft unlimited rotation — including industrial controls, robotics, special purpose photographic lenses, computer input devices (such as optomechanical mice and trackballs), controlled stress rheometers, and rotating radar platforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotary encoder - a type of encoder for rotary motion or position
