genus Lilium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɪli.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɪlɪ.əm / (BrE)
The type genus of the lily family (Liliaceae), comprising the true lilies, widely cultivated for their large, showy flowers.
genus of true lilies (Liliaceae)

Examples

The genus Lilium includes species with large, fragrant flowers found in temperate regions.
Gardeners often plant varieties from the genus Lilium for their spectacular blooms.
Several hybrids within the genus Lilium have become popular ornamental plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - common name for any plant of the genus Lilium

Etymology

From Latin 'lilium' (lily), botanical Latin name for the genus.
