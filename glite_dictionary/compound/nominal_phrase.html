nominal phrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl freɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl freɪz / (BrE)
A group of words with a noun (or equivalent) as its main word, which can function as the subject or object in a sentence.
phrase acting as subject or object

Examples

In the sentence 'The tall man in the hat walked by,' 'The tall man in the hat' is a nominal phrase.
A nominal phrase can be a single word like 'Sarah' or a group of words like 'my favorite book.'
Teachers often ask students to identify the nominal phrase in each sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noun phrase - a phrase with a noun as its head, functioning as subject or object

Etymology

From Latin 'nominalis' (relating to a name or noun).
