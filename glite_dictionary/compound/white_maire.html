white maire

1. NOUN

/ waɪt maɪr / (AmE), / waɪt meə / (BrE)
A small tree native to New Zealand, known for its red pulpy one-seeded fruit.
New Zealand tree with red fruit

Examples

The threatened white maire is an important component of certain New Zealand forests.
We found several white maire trees bearing bright red fruit along the trail.
Restoration projects have included planting white maire to support native biodiversity.
