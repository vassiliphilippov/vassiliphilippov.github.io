vorticose vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔrtɪˌkoʊs veɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɔːtɪkəʊs veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins formed by branches from the back surface of the eye and the ciliary body, which drain into the ophthalmic veins
vein of the eye draining into ophthalmic veins
medical

Examples

The doctor identified a dilated vorticose vein during the fundus examination.
Vorticose veins play a crucial role in the venous drainage of the eyeball.
Bleeding from a vorticose vein can sometimes occur during ocular surgery.

Etymology

From Latin 'vorticose', meaning whorled, and 'vein'.
