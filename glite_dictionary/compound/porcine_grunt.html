porcine grunt

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːrˌsaɪn ɡrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.saɪn ɡrʌnt / (BrE)
a low, guttural sound resembling the noise made by a pig
pig-like grunting sound

Examples

The old man let out a porcine grunt as he lifted the heavy box.
Her porcine grunt could be heard across the dining hall, drawing amused glances from others.
With a porcine grunt, the boy expressed his annoyance without saying a word.

Synset Examples

He made a porcine grunt as he enjoyed his meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - a low, guttural sound, often made by animals or people

Etymology

From Latin 'porcinus' (of a pig) and 'grunt' (imitative sound word).
