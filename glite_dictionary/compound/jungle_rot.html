jungle rot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl rɑt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl rɒt / (BrE)
A skin infection or ulceration caused by prolonged exposure to damp, unsanitary conditions, typically in tropical climates, leading to sores and tissue breakdown.
skin infection from tropical wet conditions
informal medical

Examples

The soldiers' feet became infected with jungle rot after weeks in the wetlands.
If left untreated, jungle rot can cause severe ulcers and discomfort.
Doctors treated cases of jungle rot among hikers returning from the rainforest.
