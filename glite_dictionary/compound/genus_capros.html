genus Capros

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkæproʊs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkæprɒs / (BrE)
A genus of small, laterally compressed fish in the family Caproidae, commonly known as boarfish.
genus of boarfish (fish)

Examples

The genus Capros includes several species of boarfish found in the eastern Atlantic Ocean.
Scientists recently studied the distribution of the genus Capros in Mediterranean waters.
Members of the genus Capros are easily recognized by their deep, compressed bodies and distinctive snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boarfish - a small fish of the family Caproidae, also called Capros

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (type, kind) and 'Capros', from Greek 'kapros' (boar), referencing the boarfish's snout.
