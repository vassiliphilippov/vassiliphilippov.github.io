Kamchatka Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ kæmˈtʃætkə pəˈnɪnsjə / (AmE), / kæmˈtʃætkə pɪˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
a large peninsula in eastern Russia, located between the Bering Sea and the Sea of Okhotsk, known for its volcanic activity and natural beauty
peninsula in eastern Russia, between Bering Sea and Sea of Okhotsk

Examples

The Kamchatka Peninsula is famous for its numerous volcanoes and geothermal phenomena.
Several rare species of animals can be found on the Kamchatka Peninsula.
Expeditions to the Kamchatka Peninsula attract scientists and adventure travelers from around the world.

Etymology

From Russian Камчатка (Kamchatka), the name of the region.
