strange quark

1. NOUN

/ streɪndʒ kwɑrk / (AmE), / streɪndʒ kwɔːk / (BrE)
A type of elementary particle with an electric charge of -1/3, a mass about 988 times that of an electron, and a strangeness of -1.
elementary particle in physics

Examples

The strange quark is an essential component in the structure of certain subatomic particles called kaons.
Physicists study the interactions of the strange quark to better understand the strong nuclear force.
The discovery of the strange quark was a significant milestone in the development of the Standard Model of particle physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quark - a fundamental constituent of matter in particle physics

Etymology

Formed by combining 'strange' (referring to its property of strangeness) and 'quark' (from James Joyce's Finnegans Wake, adopted in physics).
