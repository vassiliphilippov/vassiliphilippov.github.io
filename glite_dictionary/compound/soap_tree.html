soap tree

1. NOUN

/ soʊp triː / (AmE), / səʊp triː / (BrE)
A tall, tree-like yucca native to the southwestern United States, often with fibrous leaves and a large flower cluster.
yucca tree from southwestern US

Examples

The soap tree is well adapted to arid environments in the American Southwest.
Native peoples traditionally used parts of the soap tree for making soap and fiber.
A cluster of creamy white flowers blooms atop the soap tree each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a genus of perennial shrubs and trees with rosettes of evergreen leaves
