second string

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
a group of players used as substitutes, who replace or relieve the starting members of a team
sports: substitute players or team

Examples

After the starters were injured, the coach had to rely on the second string for the rest of the game.
The team's second string proved surprisingly effective when faced with tough competition.
During preseason matches, coaches often give the second string more playing time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: starter - player who begins the game
  • Synonym: substitute - player who replaces another during the game

Etymology

From the use of 'second string' in archery, referring to a spare string kept in reserve; later extended to describe backups or substitutes.
