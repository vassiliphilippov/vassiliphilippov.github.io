head honcho

1. NOUN

/ hɛd ˈhɑntʃoʊ / (AmE), / hɛd ˈhɒntʃəʊ / (BrE)
the person in charge; an important or influential leader, especially in an organization or group
person in charge or leader
informal american

Examples

After the restructuring, Mark became the head honcho of the company.
Everyone answers directly to the head honcho on major projects.
She worked hard for years before being promoted to head honcho of her division.

Synset Examples

he thinks he's a big shot
she's a big deal in local politics
the Qaeda commander is a very big fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big shot - an important or influential person
  • Synonym: top dog - the most important person in a group
  • Antonym: underling - a person lower in status or rank

Etymology

American English; 'honcho' from Japanese 'hancho' (group leader), popularized among U.S. troops during the Korean War.
