cowl muscle

1. NOUN

/ kaʊl ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / kaʊl ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
either of two flat triangular muscles of the shoulder and upper back responsible for moving the shoulders and arms
muscle in shoulder and upper back
medical

Examples

The cowl muscle helps to stabilize your shoulder during arm movements.
Injury to the cowl muscle can limit mobility and cause pain in the upper back.
Physical therapy may strengthen the cowl muscle after a shoulder injury.
