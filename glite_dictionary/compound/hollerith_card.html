Hollerith card

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːlərɪθ kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈhɒlərɪθ kɑːd / (BrE)
a card used to record data in the form of punched holes, once widely used in early computing and data processing
punched card for data recording

Examples

The census data was processed using thousands of Hollerith cards.
Early computer programmers used Hollerith cards to input and store information.
Stacks of Hollerith cards were once a common sight in academic and government offices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punch card - a card with holes punched for data storage or processing

Etymology

Named after Herman Hollerith, the American inventor who developed the punched card system.
