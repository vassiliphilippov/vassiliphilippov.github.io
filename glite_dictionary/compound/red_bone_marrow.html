red bone marrow

1. NOUN

/ rɛd boʊn ˈmɛroʊ / (AmE), / rɛd bəʊn ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
the soft, spongy tissue found in certain bones, responsible for producing red blood cells, particularly abundant in children and some adult bones
blood cell-forming bone tissue
medical

Examples

In healthy children, red bone marrow is present in most of their long bones.
The doctor explained that red bone marrow is essential for producing new blood cells.
Diseases like leukemia can affect the red bone marrow and disrupt normal blood cell formation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yellow bone marrow - bone marrow with a higher fat content, mainly involved in fat storage
  • Synonym: bone marrow - the soft tissue inside bones that produces blood cells
