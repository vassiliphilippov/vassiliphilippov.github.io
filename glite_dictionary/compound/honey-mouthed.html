honey-mouthed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌni maʊθt / (AmE), / ˈhʌni maʊθt / (BrE)
(of a person or speech) speaking in a charming or flattering way, often insincerely or with the intent to deceive.
charming or flattering, often insincerely

Examples

The honey-mouthed politician charmed the audience, but many doubted his sincerity.
Beware of honey-mouthed promises that sound too good to be true.
Her honey-mouthed remarks made everyone feel welcome, though some questioned her true intentions.

Synset Examples

His honey-mouthed compliments won her over instantly.
The honey-mouthed salesman convinced me to buy it.
She had a honey-mouthed way of speaking that charmed everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet-talking - skilled at using charming or flattering speech
  • Synonym: deceitful - deliberately misleading or dishonest
  • Antonym: blunt - direct and straightforward, sometimes to the point of rudeness

Etymology

From 'honey' (the sweet substance) and 'mouthed,' referencing sweetness in speech.
