cutaneous anthrax

1. NOUN

/ kuˈteɪniəs ˈænˌθræks / (AmE), / kjuːˈteɪnɪəs ˈænθræks / (BrE)
a form of anthrax infection characterized by skin lesions that progress from a papule to a vesicle and ulcer, often associated with severe systemic symptoms and potentially fatal if untreated
skin form of anthrax infection
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with cutaneous anthrax after noticing the distinctive skin ulcer.
Prompt treatment is essential when cutaneous anthrax is suspected to reduce the risk of fatal complications.
Exposure to infected animals can lead to cutaneous anthrax, especially in agricultural settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthrax - an infectious disease caused by Bacillus anthracis

Etymology

From Latin 'cutaneus' (of the skin) and 'anthrax' (Greek for 'coal', referring to the black eschar formed).
