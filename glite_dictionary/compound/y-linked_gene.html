Y-linked gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪ lɪŋkt dʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈwaɪ lɪŋkt dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene located on the Y chromosome that is inherited only through the male line
gene located on Y chromosome
medical

Examples

Inheritance of a Y-linked gene can only occur from father to son.
Scientists are studying a Y-linked gene that may influence male fertility.
The discovery of a new Y-linked gene helped explain a pattern of inheritance in the family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: X-linked gene - a gene located on the X chromosome
