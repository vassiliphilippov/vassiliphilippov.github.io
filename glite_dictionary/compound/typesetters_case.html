typesetter's case

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪpˌsɛtərz keɪs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpsɛtəz keɪs / (BrE)
a shallow tray divided into compartments for holding the individual pieces of type used by a compositor in printing
compartmented tray for holding type in printing

Examples

The old print shop still displayed a typesetter's case filled with lead letters.
Each typesetter's case was carefully organized to speed up the compositor's work.
He explained that the capital letters were stored in a separate typesetter's case.

Synset Examples

for English, a compositor will ordinarily have two such cases, the upper case containing the capitals and the lower case containing the small letters
