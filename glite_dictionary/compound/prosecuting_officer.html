prosecuting officer

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑsɪˌkjutɪŋ ˈɔfəsər / (AmE), / ˈprɒsɪˌkjuːtɪŋ ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a government official responsible for conducting criminal prosecutions on behalf of the state or public authority
official who prosecutes on behalf of the state
legal

Examples

The prosecuting officer presented strong evidence against the defendant during the trial.
As a prosecuting officer, her role is to ensure that justice is served in criminal cases.
The judge requested clarification from the prosecuting officer regarding the timeline of events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosecutor - a lawyer who conducts the case against a defendant in a criminal court
