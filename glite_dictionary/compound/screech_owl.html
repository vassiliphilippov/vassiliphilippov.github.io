screech owl

1. NOUN

/ skritʃ aʊl / (AmE), / skriːtʃ aʊl / (BrE)
a small North American owl with hornlike tufts of feathers, known for its call that sounds like a quavering whistle
small North American owl with a distinctive call

Examples

We heard a screech owl calling in the woods last night.
A screech owl may nest in tree cavities close to suburban areas.
The piercing call of the screech owl startled the campers.

2. NOUN

/ skritʃ aʊl / (AmE), / skriːtʃ aʊl / (BrE)
a small owl with a screeching cry, found especially in North and Central America
small owl with a screeching cry

Examples

A screech owl called out loudly in the darkness behind our cabin.
The distinctive cry of the screech owl echoed through the woods at night.
Biologists studied the nesting habits of the local screech owls for several months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - nocturnal bird of prey with a flat face and large eyes
