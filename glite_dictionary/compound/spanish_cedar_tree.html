Spanish cedar tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈsiːdər triː / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ ˈsiːdə triː / (BrE)
a tropical American tree (Cedrela odorata) known for its fragrant wood, widely used particularly for making cigar boxes
tropical American tree with fragrant wood

Examples

The Spanish cedar tree is prized for its aromatic wood, which is perfect for making cigar boxes.
Many plantations in Central America cultivate the Spanish cedar tree for commercial timber.
The lightweight wood from the Spanish cedar tree resists pests and humidity exceptionally well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - an evergreen coniferous tree with fragrant wood
