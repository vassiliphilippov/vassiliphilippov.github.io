genus Apodemus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæpəˈdiːməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌapəˈdiːməs / (BrE)
A genus of field mice native to Europe and Asia, commonly known as Old World field mice.
genus of Old World field mice

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Apodemus in Central Asia.
The genus Apodemus includes several species of mice commonly found in European woodlands.
Members of the genus Apodemus are often studied for their ecological role in grassland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field mouse - small wild mouse commonly found in fields; member of Apodemus or related genera

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Apodemus*, the name of the genus, from Greek *apodēmos* (away from home, abroad).
