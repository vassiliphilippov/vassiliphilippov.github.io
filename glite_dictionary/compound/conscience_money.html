conscience money

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnʃəns ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈkɒnʃəns ˌmʌni / (BrE)
money paid voluntarily to make amends for past dishonesty, especially to relieve feelings of guilt.
voluntary payment to atone for dishonesty

Examples

The anonymous conscience money was sent to the tax office to cover past unpaid taxes.
She left an envelope of conscience money at the charity’s door after years of guilt.
Some institutions keep records of conscience money received from people trying to set things right.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnʃəns ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈkɒnʃəns ˌmʌni / (BrE)
money given to relieve guilt or to make amends for a wrongdoing, especially when the recipient was previously wronged by the giver
money given to relieve guilt

Examples

The company secretly paid conscience money to the victims of the fraud.
Feeling remorseful, she sent conscience money anonymously to the charity she once deceived.
He regarded the donation as conscience money after realizing the harm he caused.

Synset Examples

The company offered conscience money after the scandal.
He returned the money as conscience money for his mistakes.
She donated to charity as a form of conscience money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restitution - the act of restoring or compensating for loss or harm
