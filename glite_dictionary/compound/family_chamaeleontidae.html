family Chamaeleontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli kæˌmiːliˈɒntɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kamɪˌliːɒnˈtɪːdiː / (BrE)
A taxonomic family of reptiles comprising the Old World chameleons, sometimes classified as a superfamily within Sauria.
taxonomic family: Old World chameleons

Examples

The family Chamaeleontidae includes many species that can rapidly change their skin color.
Scientists have been studying the evolutionary history of the family Chamaeleontidae for decades.
Most members of the family Chamaeleontidae are native to Africa and Madagascar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chameleon - A lizard known for changing its skin color; member of Chamaeleontidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Chamaeleontidae, from the genus name Chamaeleo (chameleon).
