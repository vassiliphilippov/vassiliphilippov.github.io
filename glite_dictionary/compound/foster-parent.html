foster-parent

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑstər ˌpɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˌpeərənt / (BrE)
a person who takes care of and raises a child temporarily as a parent, but is not the child's legal or biological parent and has not adopted the child
temporary guardian of a non-biological child

Examples

She became a foster-parent to two siblings after their mother was hospitalized.
Every foster-parent receives special training before welcoming children into their home.
As a foster-parent, he provided a stable environment for children in need.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: adoptive parent - a person who legally adopts a child
  • Antonym: biological parent - a child's genetic parent

Etymology

From Old English 'fostrian' (to nourish, rear) and 'parent' from Latin 'parens' (father or mother).
