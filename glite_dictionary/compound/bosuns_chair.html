bosun's chair

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊsənz t͡ʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈbəʊsənz t͡ʃɛː / (BrE)
a device consisting of a board or seat attached to ropes, used to suspend a person while working high above, especially on a ship's mast or over its side
seat suspended by rope for working aloft on ships

Examples

The sailor sat in the bosun's chair while repairing the rigging high above the deck.
For safety, always inspect the bosun's chair before using it to work over the side of the ship.
Modern climbers sometimes use a bosun's chair for building maintenance or window cleaning on skyscrapers.

Etymology

From 'bosun', a shortened form of 'boatswain', and 'chair', meaning a seat.
