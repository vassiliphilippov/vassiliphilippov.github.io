monkey-bread tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki brɛd triː / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki brɛd triː / (BrE)
An African tree (baobab) with an exceptionally thick trunk and fruit containing edible pulp known as monkey bread.
African tree with edible fruit pulp

Examples

The monkey-bread tree grows in the savannas of Africa and is known for its massive trunk.
Local villagers collect the fruit of the monkey-bread tree to use its nutritious pulp in food and drinks.
During our safari, we stopped to rest under a towering monkey-bread tree for some shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baobab - African tree with thick trunk and edible fruit

Etymology

Refers to the edible fruit pulp called 'monkey bread'; commonly a name for the baobab tree.
