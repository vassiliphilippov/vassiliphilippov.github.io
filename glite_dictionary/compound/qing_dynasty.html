Qing dynasty

1. PROPN

/ tʃɪŋ ˈdaɪ.næ.sti / (AmE), / tʃɪŋ ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the last imperial dynasty of China, ruling from 1644 to 1912, during which the country was governed by the Manchu people
the last imperial dynasty of China (1644–1912)

Examples

The Qing dynasty was marked by significant territorial expansion and population growth in China.
During the Qing dynasty, the country faced both internal rebellions and external pressures from foreign powers.
The fall of the Qing dynasty in 1912 ended over two thousand years of imperial rule.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ming dynasty - the Chinese dynasty preceding the Qing (1368–1644)
  • Synonym: dynasty - a sequence of rulers from the same family or group

Etymology

From Mandarin 清朝 (*Qīng cháo*), with ‘Qing’ meaning ‘clear’ or ‘pure’.
