striped gentian

1. NOUN

/ straɪpt ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / straɪpt ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A perennial marsh gentian of eastern North America, characterized by blue-violet striped flowers.
marsh gentian of eastern North America

Examples

In late summer, the striped gentian adds a splash of blue to the wet meadows.
Hikers often pause to admire the unusual flowers of the striped gentian along wooded trails.
The conservation team is working to protect habitats vital for the striped gentian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - a plant of the genus Gentiana, noted for showy flowers
