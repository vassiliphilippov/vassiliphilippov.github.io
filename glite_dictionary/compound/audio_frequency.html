audio frequency

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːdioʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiəʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
a frequency of sound waves that can be heard by the human ear, typically between 20 and 20,000 hertz
sound frequency audible to humans

Examples

The engineer measured the audio frequency of the signals coming from the speakers.
Musicians often adjust equipment to enhance specific audio frequencies for better sound quality.
Dogs can hear higher than normal audio frequency ranges compared to humans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ultrasonic - frequencies higher than the range of human hearing
  • Degree Lower: infrasonic - frequencies lower than the range of human hearing

Etymology

From 'audio' (relating to hearing or sound) and 'frequency' (number of occurrences per unit time).
