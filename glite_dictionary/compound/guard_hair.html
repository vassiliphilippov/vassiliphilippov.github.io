guard hair

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑrd hɛr / (AmE), / ɡɑːd heə / (BrE)
one of the coarse, outer hairs of the fur of some mammals that protects the softer underfur beneath
outer coarse hair of mammal fur

Examples

The guard hair on the arctic fox protects it from harsh winds and cold temperatures.
You can distinguish the softer undercoat from the longer, thicker guard hair in most dogs.
She brushed the cat's guard hair to remove dirt and keep the coat healthy.
