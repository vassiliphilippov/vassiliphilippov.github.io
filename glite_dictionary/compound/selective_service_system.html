Selective Service System

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛktɪv ˈsɝːvɪs ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv ˈsɜːvɪs ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The independent federal agency in the United States responsible for administering the compulsory military draft.
US federal agency administering the military draft
american

Examples

All eligible men were required to register with the Selective Service System during the war.
The Selective Service System has not conducted a draft since 1973, but registration is still mandatory.
Many young Americans are unfamiliar with the purpose of the Selective Service System.
