choice feminism

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔɪs ˈfɛmɪnɪzəm / (AmE), / tʃɔɪs ˈfɛmɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
a strand of feminism that emphasizes the importance of individual choice and personal empowerment for women, regardless of how those choices are viewed by others
feminist view stressing individual choice

Examples

Many young women today identify with choice feminism in their approach to empowerment.
Critics argue that choice feminism can sometimes overlook broader structural inequalities.
The author's essay discusses how choice feminism influences contemporary debates about gender roles.

Synset Examples

She believes in choice feminism for women's empowerment.
Many debate the implications of choice feminism today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feminism - the belief in social, economic, and political equality for women
