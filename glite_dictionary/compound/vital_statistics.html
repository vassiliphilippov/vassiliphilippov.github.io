vital statistics

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪt̮l stəˈtɪstɪks / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.təl stəˈtɪs.tɪks / (BrE)
official data relating to births, deaths, marriages, and health, typically collected by government agencies.
official records of births, deaths, marriages, and health

Examples

The government publishes annual vital statistics to help monitor public health trends.
Researchers analyze vital statistics to assess changes in birth and mortality rates over time.
Accurate vital statistics are essential for planning healthcare resources in any country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demographics - statistical data about populations

Etymology

From 'vital' meaning 'pertaining to life', and 'statistics', from New Latin statisticum collegium (council of state).
