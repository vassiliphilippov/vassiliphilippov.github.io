pinto bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn.toʊ ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.təʊ ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A variety of mottled or spotted bean, typically used dried in cooking, especially common in the southwestern United States and Mexican cuisine.
mottled dried bean used in cooking

Examples

I added a cup of pinto beans to the chili for extra flavor and texture.
Pinto beans are commonly used in burritos and refried beans in Mexican dishes.
The recipe calls for dried pinto beans, which should be soaked overnight before cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney bean - a type of bean shaped like a kidney, often red

Etymology

From Spanish 'pinto' meaning 'painted' or 'spotted', referring to the appearance of the bean.
