genus Aspis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈæs.pɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈæs.pɪs / (BrE)
A taxonomic genus of venomous snakes known as horned vipers, native to parts of Europe and Africa.
group of horned viper snakes

Examples

The genus Aspis includes several species of horned vipers found in the Mediterranean regions.
Biologists study the venom of snakes in the genus Aspis for potential medical uses.
The sand viper is one representative of the genus Aspis.

Etymology

From Latin 'aspis' (viper or snake).
