full admiral

1. NOUN

/ fʊl ˈædmɪrəl / (AmE), / fʊl ˈædmɪrəl / (BrE)
A naval officer of the highest rank below fleet admiral and above vice admiral, typically commanding a fleet or naval forces.
highest standard naval admiral rank

Examples

He was promoted to full admiral after years of dedicated service.
As a full admiral, she commanded the entire Pacific fleet.
During the ceremony, the president congratulated the new full admiral on his appointment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vice admiral - a naval officer of rank below admiral
  • Degree Higher: fleet admiral - a naval officer of the highest possible commissioned rank
