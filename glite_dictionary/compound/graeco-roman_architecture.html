Graeco-Roman architecture

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪkoʊˈroʊmən ˌɑːrkɪˈtɛktʃər / (AmE), / ˌɡriːkəʊˈrəʊmən ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
A style of architecture influenced by the ancient Greeks and Romans, characterized by classical elements such as columns, arches, and symmetry.
architecture influenced by Greeks and Romans

Examples

Many government buildings in Washington, D.C. showcase Graeco-Roman architecture with imposing columns and domes.
The museum’s facade was designed in the style of Graeco-Roman architecture to evoke a sense of antiquity.
Students studied Graeco-Roman architecture to understand the origins of many Western structural designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical architecture - architectural style rooted in principles of ancient Greece and Rome

Etymology

From Greek 'Graikos' (Greek) and Latin 'Romanus' (Roman), referring to the combined influence of Greek and Roman culture.
