amyloid protein plaque

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.i.lɔɪd ˈproʊ.tin plæk / (AmE), / ˈæm.ɪ.lɔɪd ˈprəʊ.tiːn plɑːk / (BrE)
an abnormal deposit of amyloid protein in nervous tissue, often found in the brains of people with Alzheimer's disease
abnormal brain deposit linked to Alzheimer's
medical

Examples

The accumulation of amyloid protein plaque in the brain is a hallmark of Alzheimer's disease.
Researchers are studying how amyloid protein plaques develop and disrupt normal neural function.
Early detection of amyloid protein plaque could help in the diagnosis of neurodegenerative conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amyloid plaque - abnormal deposit of amyloid protein in tissues, especially the brain

Etymology

From 'amyloid', from Greek 'amylon' (starch) and '-oid' (like); 'plaque' from French 'plaque' (plate, patch).
