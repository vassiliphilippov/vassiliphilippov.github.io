Saharaui Arab Democratic Republic

1. PROPN

/ səˌhɑːrwi ˌɛːræb dəˌmɒkrætɪk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / səˌhɑːraʊi ˌærəb dɪˌmɒkrætɪk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
A partially recognized state in the Western Sahara region of the Maghreb, claiming sovereignty over Western Sahara.
partially recognized state in Western Sahara

Examples

The Saharaui Arab Democratic Republic has sought international recognition since its declaration of independence in 1976.
Several African countries recognize the Saharaui Arab Democratic Republic as the legitimate government of Western Sahara.
Diplomatic relations with the Saharaui Arab Democratic Republic are a subject of international debate.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Saharaui' (relating to the Sahara) and Arabic roots referring to Arab identity.
