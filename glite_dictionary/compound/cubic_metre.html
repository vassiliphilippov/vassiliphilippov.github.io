cubic metre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪk ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪk ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to the volume of a cube with sides of one metre; equivalent to 1,000 litres
metric unit of volume (1,000 litres)

Examples

The swimming pool holds almost two thousand cubic metres of water.
Each shipment contained approximately five cubic metres of sand.
A cubic metre of wood weighs much less than a cubic metre of steel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liter - metric unit of capacity equal to one cubic decimeter

Etymology

From 'cubic' (Latin cubus, 'cube') + 'metre', from French mètre, from Greek metron ('measure').
