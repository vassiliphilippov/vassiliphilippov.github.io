bottle cork

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑt̬l kɔrk / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl kɔːk / (BrE)
a plug made of cork or similar material used to seal the mouth of a bottle, especially a wine bottle
plug sealing a bottle, often wine

Examples

He struggled to remove the bottle cork with the old corkscrew.
After opening the wine, she placed the bottle cork back to keep it fresh.
A broken bottle cork can be difficult to extract from the neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cork - material from the bark of the cork oak, or any stopper made from it
