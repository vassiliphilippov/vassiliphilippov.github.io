ray of light

1. NOUN

/ reɪ əv laɪt / (AmE), / reɪ əv laɪt / (BrE)
a narrow beam or column of light, especially one that is visible as it shines through darkness or from a light source.
narrow beam or column of light

Examples

A single ray of light shone through the clouds, illuminating the field below.
She followed the ray of light that entered the dark room through a crack in the door.
The lighthouse sent a ray of light sweeping across the stormy sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam of light - a wide or narrow stream of light from a source
