corrugated fastener

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəˌɡeɪtɪd ˈfæstənər / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əˌɡeɪ.tɪd ˈfɑːs.tən.ə / (BrE)
a small strip of corrugated steel with sharp points on one side, used to join pieces of wood in rough carpentry
metal tool for joining wood

Examples

The carpenter hammered a corrugated fastener into the joint to hold the boards together.
Using a corrugated fastener is a quick way to reinforce a basic wooden frame.
Several corrugated fasteners were required to secure the pieces before the glue dried.
