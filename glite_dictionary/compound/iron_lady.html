Iron Lady

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrn ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
(sometimes the Iron Lady) A nickname for Margaret Thatcher, the first female Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, noted for her tough and uncompromising political style.
nickname for Margaret Thatcher, UK Prime Minister
british

Examples

Margaret Thatcher was often called the Iron Lady due to her strict leadership style.
The media dubbed her the Iron Lady after a forceful speech about the Soviet Union.
As the Iron Lady, she was both admired and criticized for her resolute policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Margaret Thatcher - British Prime Minister from 1979 to 1990, known as the Iron Lady

Etymology

Nickname reportedly coined by a Soviet journalist in 1976, referencing her tough stance against the Soviet Union.
