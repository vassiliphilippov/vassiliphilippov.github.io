chant notation

1. NOUN

/ tʃænt noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / tʃɑːnt nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a system of musical notation, traditionally using neumes, for transcribing Gregorian chant and other plainchant melodies
musical notation for Gregorian chant

Examples

Medieval manuscripts display chant notation written in elegant neumes on four-line staves.
Learning to read chant notation is essential for singing traditional Gregorian melodies correctly.
The choir director explained the history of chant notation before rehearsing the piece.

Synset Examples

This is a description of the traditional Gregorian Chant notation, so that anyone will be able to read the notation and sing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neume - a basic element of Western and Eastern systems of musical notation prior to the invention of five-line staff notation
  • Synonym: plainchant - a body of chants used in the liturgies of the Western Church
