Gothic arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːθɪk ɑrt͡ʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk ɑːt͡ʃ / (BrE)
a pointed arch, characteristic of Gothic architecture, with a joint at the apex instead of a keystone
pointed arch in Gothic architecture

Examples

The cathedral’s windows are framed by elegant Gothic arches that soar towards the ceiling.
Medieval builders favored the Gothic arch for its strength and aesthetic appeal.
You can easily recognize a Gothic arch by its pointed shape at the top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointed arch - an arch with a pointed apex

Etymology

From 'Gothic', referring to the architectural style that flourished in Europe in the late medieval period, and 'arch'.
