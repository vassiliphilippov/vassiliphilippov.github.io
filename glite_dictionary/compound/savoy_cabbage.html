savoy cabbage

1. NOUN

/ səˈvɔɪ ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / səˈvɔɪ ˈkabɪdʒ / (BrE)
A variety of cabbage with a round head and crinkly, tender leaves.
variety of cabbage with crinkly leaves

Examples

I added chopped savoy cabbage to the stew for extra flavor and texture.
The market had a fresh display of savoy cabbage alongside other leafy greens.
She prefers savoy cabbage because its leaves are less tough than those of regular cabbage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a leafy vegetable, usually with a dense round head

Etymology

From French 'chou de Savoie', referring to the Savoy region of France where it was cultivated.

2. NOUN

/ səˈvɔɪ ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / səˈvɔɪ ˈkabɪdʒ / (BrE)
A variety of cabbage with a compact head of wrinkled, crinkled leaves, typically used as a vegetable.
cabbage with crinkled leaves

Examples

The recipe calls for fresh savoy cabbage to give the soup a rich texture.
She prefers savoy cabbage over regular cabbage because of its delicate, crinkly leaves.
Sautéing savoy cabbage with garlic and olive oil makes a delicious side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a leafy vegetable forming a dense green or purple head

Etymology

From French 'chou de Savoie', meaning 'cabbage of Savoy', a region in France.
