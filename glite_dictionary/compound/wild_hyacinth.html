wild hyacinth

1. NOUN

/ waɪld haɪˈæsɪnθ / (AmE), / waɪld hʌɪˈasɪnθ / (BrE)
a perennial flowering plant, sometimes classified in the genus Scilla, with blue or purple flowers, growing wild in woodlands and meadows; also refers to similar species in related genera
a woodland plant with blue-purple flowers

Examples

The woods were carpeted with blooming wild hyacinth in early spring.
She photographed a cluster of wild hyacinth during her hike through the meadow.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the native wild hyacinth.

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈhaɪ.əˌsɪnθ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈhaɪ.ə.sɪnθ / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the lily family (Camassia species), native to eastern and central North America, bearing clusters of star-shaped blue or purple flowers
a North American perennial plant with blue flowers

Examples

The woods were carpeted with blooming wild hyacinth in early spring.
Botanists studied the pollinators visiting the wild hyacinth flowers.
Many gardeners appreciate the delicate clusters of the wild hyacinth in natural landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - a North American plant with edible bulbs, related to wild hyacinth
