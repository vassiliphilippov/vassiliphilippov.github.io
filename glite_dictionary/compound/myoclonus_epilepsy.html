myoclonus epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˈkloʊnəs ɪˈpɪlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəʊˈkləʊnəs ɪˈpɪlɪpsi / (BrE)
A form of epilepsy characterized by involuntary muscle jerks (myoclonus), progressive mental decline, and typically of genetic origin.
form of epilepsy with muscle jerks and mental decline
medical

Examples

Patients with myoclonus epilepsy often experience brief, shock-like muscle spasms and cognitive decline.
The doctor explained that myoclonus epilepsy can be inherited and may worsen over time.
Early intervention is important in managing the symptoms of myoclonus epilepsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilepsy - a neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent episodes of seizures
  • Synonym: myoclonus - sudden, involuntary muscle jerks

Etymology

From Greek 'myo-' (muscle) + 'clonus' (violent, confused motion) and Greek 'epilēpsia' (seizure, epilepsy).
