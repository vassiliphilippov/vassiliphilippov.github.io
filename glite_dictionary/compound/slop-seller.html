slop-seller

1. NOUN

/ slɑpˌsɛlɚ / (AmE), / slɒpˌsɛlə / (BrE)
A person who deals in cheap ready-made clothing, especially in historical contexts.
dealer in cheap ready-made clothing
archaic

Examples

In 19th-century London, the slop-seller catered to sailors in need of inexpensive garments.
The slop-seller's shop was filled with piles of rough, affordable clothing for laborers.
He earned his living as a slop-seller, peddling garments to the working poor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slops - cheap ready-made clothing, especially for sailors

Etymology

From 'slop' meaning cheap ready-made clothing and 'seller' (one who sells).
