master cylinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚ ˌsɪlɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə ˌsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
a device in a vehicle's braking system that contains brake fluid and is operated by a piston to generate hydraulic pressure for braking
hydraulic brake system component

Examples

When the master cylinder fails, you may notice a spongy brake pedal or reduced braking power.
He replaced the old master cylinder to fix the car's faulty braking system.
Checking the fluid level in the master cylinder is essential for regular vehicle maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brake drum - the part of a drum brake against which the brake shoes press
  • Synonym: brake caliper - component of disk brakes that squeezes the brake pads against the disc
