sailing ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ ʃɪp / (BrE)
a large ship propelled by sails and typically having more than one mast
ship powered by sails

Examples

The museum features a beautifully restored sailing ship from the nineteenth century.
During the Age of Exploration, sailing ships were essential for long sea voyages.
The crew worked together to raise the sails on the sailing ship as the wind picked up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailboat - a smaller boat propelled by sails
  • Antonym: steamship - ship powered by steam engines
