serum albumin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪrəm ælˈbjumɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəm ælˈbjuːmɪn / (BrE)
a type of albumin protein found in blood serum, important for maintaining the osmotic pressure of the blood
albumin protein in blood serum
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check the serum albumin levels.
Low serum albumin can indicate liver or kidney disease.
Researchers isolated serum albumin to study its properties in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albumin - a group of simple proteins that are soluble in water

Etymology

From Latin 'serum' (whey) and 'albumin', from Latin 'albus' (white).
