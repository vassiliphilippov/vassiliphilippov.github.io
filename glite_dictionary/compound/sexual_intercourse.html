sexual intercourse

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkʃuəl ˌɪntərkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈsɛksjʊəl ˈɪntəkɔːs / (BrE)
the physical activity in which two people, typically a male and a female, have sex, especially involving penetration of the vagina by the penis
physical act of sex between individuals

Examples

The couple discussed the importance of protection before engaging in sexual intercourse.
Medical professionals often answer questions related to sexual intercourse and reproductive health.
Laws regarding consent are essential in matters involving sexual intercourse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copulation - the sexual union of two individuals, especially for reproduction
  • Antonym: sexual abstinence - the practice of refraining from sexual intercourse

Etymology

From Latin 'sexus' (sex) and Late Latin 'intercursus' (a running between, intercourse).
