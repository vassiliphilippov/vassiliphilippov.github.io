family Lymantriidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli lɪˌmænˈtraɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli lɪˌmanˈtrɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of moths, known as tussock moths, whose caterpillars are often hairy and can be significant pests of trees.
a family of hairy tussock moths

Examples

The family Lymantriidae includes many species whose caterpillars can defoliate forests.
Scientists are studying the global spread of the family Lymantriidae and its ecological impact.
Several members of the family Lymantriidae are considered serious pests in agriculture and forestry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lymantria - the type genus of the family Lymantriidae
  • Synonym: tussock moth - common name for moths in the family Lymantriidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Lymantriidae', based on 'Lymantria', the type genus, from Greek 'lymantria' meaning 'destroyer'.
