fresh water

1. NOUN

/ frɛʃ ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / frɛʃ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that is not salty, typically found in rivers, lakes, and streams, as opposed to seawater
water that is not salty

Examples

The pond is filled with fresh water from a nearby spring.
Many fish species require fresh water habitats to survive.
We need to conserve fresh water resources for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salt water - water containing a high concentration of salt, especially sea water
