hog cranberry

1. NOUN

/ hɑɡ ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / hɒɡ ˈkrænˌbəri / (BrE)
An evergreen, mat-forming shrub native to North America and northern Eurasia, with small white flowers and red berries; its leaves turn red in autumn.
evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

The hog cranberry carpets the forest floor with its glossy green leaves and red berries.
In autumn, the leaves of the hog cranberry turn a brilliant shade of crimson.
Wildlife often feed on the berries of the hog cranberry throughout the colder months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lingonberry - evergreen shrub with edible red berries, native to boreal forests
  • Synonym: cranberry - edible red berry from plants of the genus Vaccinium

Etymology

From 'hog' and 'cranberry', possibly referring to its berries resembling cranberries and perhaps eaten by hogs; pattern follows plant-naming conventions.
