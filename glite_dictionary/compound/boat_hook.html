boat hook

1. NOUN

/ boʊt hʊk / (AmE), / bəʊt hʊk / (BrE)
a long pole with a hook at one end, used for pushing or pulling boats or for catching things in the water.
pole with hook for pushing/pulling boats

Examples

The sailor reached out with the boat hook to catch the drifting rope.
Using a boat hook, she pulled the dinghy closer to the dock.
He used the boat hook to retrieve a floating life jacket from the water.
