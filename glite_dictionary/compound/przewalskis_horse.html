Przewalski's horse

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈvɑlzkiːz hɔɹs / (AmE), / ʃəˈvalskiːz hɔːs / (BrE)
a wild, endangered horse native to central Asia, resembling an ass and considered the last true wild horse species
wild horse species native to central Asia

Examples

The Przewalski's horse is critically endangered and survives only in small protected populations.
Unlike domestic horses, the Przewalski's horse has a stockier build and erect mane.
Scientists are reintroducing Przewalski's horse to its native grasslands in Mongolia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild horse - any undomesticated horse species

Etymology

Named after Russian explorer Nikolai Przhevalsky (1839–1888), who described the animal to western science.
