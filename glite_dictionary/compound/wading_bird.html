wading bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪdɪŋ bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈweɪdɪŋ bɜːd / (BrE)
any of various long-legged birds that wade in shallow water to catch food, such as herons, storks, and ibises.
long-legged bird that wades in water

Examples

The marsh was full of wading birds searching for fish and insects.
Many wading birds have long, thin legs and bills adapted for catching prey in the water.
I spotted a rare wading bird near the edge of the lake this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorebird - bird commonly found along shores and mudflats
