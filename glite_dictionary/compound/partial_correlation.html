partial correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrʃəl ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical measure of the relationship between two variables after controlling for the effect of one or more other variables
correlation between two variables controlling for others

Examples

The researchers used partial correlation to analyze the relationship between income and education while controlling for age.
A partial correlation can reveal whether two variables are still related after accounting for a third factor.
Understanding partial correlation is essential in multivariate statistical analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation - the relationship between two or more things that change together

Etymology

From 'partial' (meaning incomplete or limited) and 'correlation' (from Latin 'correlatio', a bringing together).
