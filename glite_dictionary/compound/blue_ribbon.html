blue ribbon

1. NOUN

/ blu ˈrɪbən / (AmE), / blu ˈrɪbən / (BrE)
An honor or award given to recognize the highest achievement or excellence, often symbolized by a blue ribbon.
award for highest achievement

Examples

She proudly displayed the blue ribbon she won at the science fair.
The pie was so delicious, it received a blue ribbon at the county competition.
Winning a blue ribbon is considered a great honor among the participants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first prize - top award in a contest
  • Antonym: consolation prize - an award given to a non-winner

Etymology

From the use of blue-colored ribbons as symbols of highest distinction at fairs and competitions.

2. NOUN

/ blu ˈrɪbən / (AmE), / blu ˈrɪbən / (BrE)
An award given to indicate excellence or first place, or a symbol of high quality.
award for excellence; symbol of high quality

Examples

She was thrilled to receive the blue ribbon for her painting at the fair.
Winning a blue ribbon meant his science project was the best in the school.
The bakery is known for its blue ribbon pies, famous across the county.

Synset Examples

She won a blue ribbon for her award-winning pie.
He was proud to receive a blue ribbon for his project.
The blue ribbon signifies excellence in competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first prize - award given for first place in a competition
  • Synonym: gold medal - an award for first place, especially in sports

Etymology

From the blue ribbons awarded at competitions, especially in the United States, originally inspired by the blue ribbon of the Order of the Garter in the UK.
