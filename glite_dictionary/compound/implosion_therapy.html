implosion therapy

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈploʊʒən ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɪmˈpləʊʒən ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a form of behavior therapy in which a patient is exposed to intense and overwhelming stimuli related to their fears, with the aim of reducing anxiety through repeated exposure.
behavior therapy involving intense exposure to fears
medical

Examples

Some psychologists recommend implosion therapy for clients suffering from severe phobias.
During implosion therapy, the patient is asked to confront their most intense fears in a controlled environment.
The effectiveness of implosion therapy is still debated among mental health professionals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flooding - behavioral therapy technique exposing a person to their fear at full intensity

Etymology

From 'implosion' (inward bursting) and 'therapy' (treatment of disease); term coined in clinical psychology in the mid-20th century.
