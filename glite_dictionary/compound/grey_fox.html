grey fox

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ fɑks / (AmE), / ɡreɪ fɒks / (BrE)
a species of fox (Urocyon cinereoargenteus) with dark grey fur, native to Central America and the southern United States
a North American species of fox

Examples

The grey fox is known for its ability to climb trees unlike most other foxes.
Researchers spotted a family of grey foxes near the edge of the forest.
The grey fox has a coat that helps it blend into rocky habitats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: red fox - a widespread species of fox with reddish fur (Vulpes vulpes)
