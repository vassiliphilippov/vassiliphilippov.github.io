mere fodder

1. NOUN

/ mɪr ˈfɑdər / (AmE), / mɪə ˈfɒdə / (BrE)
used to describe someone or something regarded only as material to be used for a specific, often unimportant, purpose
only material for a purpose

Examples

The celebrity scandal was mere fodder for tabloid newspapers.
Her ideas were treated as mere fodder for the company's marketing campaign.
The politician's mistakes became mere fodder for his critics in the press.

Synset Examples

His jokes were mere fodder for the writers' new sitcom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannon fodder - people regarded as expendable resources, especially in war
  • Synonym: raw material - basic material used in a process

Etymology

From 'mere' (meaning 'only') and 'fodder' (originally referring to food for livestock, from Old English 'foddor').
