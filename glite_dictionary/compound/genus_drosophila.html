genus Drosophila

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs drəˈsɑː.fə.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs drɒˈsɒf.ɪ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of small fruit flies in the family Drosophilidae, many species of which are used extensively in genetic research.
a fruit fly genus in Drosophilidae

Examples

The genus Drosophila contains many species used as model organisms in genetics.
Research on the genus Drosophila has contributed greatly to our understanding of inheritance.
Biologists often compare different species within the genus Drosophila to study evolutionary traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Drosophila - any species of the fruit fly genus Drosophila

Etymology

From New Latin, Drosophila; Greek 'drosos' (dew) + 'philos' (loving), referring to its fondness for moist environments.
