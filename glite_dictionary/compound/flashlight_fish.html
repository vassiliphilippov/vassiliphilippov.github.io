flashlight fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌlaɪt fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌlaɪt fɪʃ / (BrE)
A tropical fish with a luminous organ beneath its eye, found in the warm waters of the western Pacific and Puerto Rico.
bioluminescent tropical fish

Examples

The flashlight fish uses its luminous organ to attract prey in the darkness of the ocean.
While diving at night, we spotted a school of flashlight fish glowing beneath the reef.
Aquariums often feature the flashlight fish for its unique bioluminescent display.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌlaɪt fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflæʃlʌɪt fɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, deep-sea fish with a light-producing organ beneath each eye, found in tropical waters
deep-sea fish with light organs under eyes

Examples

The flashlight fish uses its glowing organs to attract prey in the pitch-black ocean depths.
Marine biologists recently captured a rare flashlight fish off the coast of Indonesia.
At the aquarium, visitors were fascinated by the gentle blinking of the flashlight fish in the darkened tank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanternfish - deep-sea fish with photophores for light emission

Etymology

From 'flashlight', referring to the fish's ability to emit light, and 'fish'.
