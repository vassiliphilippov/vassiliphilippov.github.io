lamellicorn beetle

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɛlɪˌkɔrn ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ləˈmɛlɪkɔːn ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A beetle with antennae ending in hard, plate-like segments.
beetle with plate-like antennae segments

Examples

The lamellicorn beetle can be identified by its unique, plate-like antennae.
Many species of scarab are classified as lamellicorn beetles due to their characteristic antennae.
Entomologists studied the evolutionary advantages of the lamellicorn beetle’s antenna structure.

Etymology

From Latin 'lamella' (thin plate) + 'cornu' (horn), referring to the structure of the antennae.
