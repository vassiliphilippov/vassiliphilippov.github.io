plexus caroticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs kəˈrɑːtɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs kəˈrɒtɪkəs / (BrE)
a network of nerves surrounding the internal carotid artery
network of nerves around carotid artery
medical

Examples

The surgeon took care to avoid damaging the plexus caroticus during the procedure.
Stimulation of the plexus caroticus can result in changes to heart rate and blood pressure.
The anatomy text provided a detailed diagram of the plexus caroticus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotid plexus - another term for plexus caroticus, network of nerves around carotid artery

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network) and 'caroticus' (of the carotid).
