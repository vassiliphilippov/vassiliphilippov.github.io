Nova style salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.və staɪl ˈsæm.ən / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.və staɪl ˈsæm.ən / (BrE)
Salmon that has been brine-cured, typically with less salt than other cured salmons, and sometimes sugar is used in the curing process.
mildly brined cured salmon

Examples

She ordered a bagel with Nova style salmon for breakfast.
The Nova style salmon was tender and delicately flavored, unlike the saltier lox.
Many delis serve Nova style salmon as an option for sandwiches and platters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lox - brined salmon, often saltier

Etymology

From the abbreviation of Nova Scotia, indicating the origin of the curing style.
