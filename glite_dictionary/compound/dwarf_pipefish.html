dwarf pipefish

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈpaɪpfɪʃ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈpaɪpfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small species of pipefish, about 4 inches long, found off the coast of Florida
a small pipefish found off Florida

Examples

The dwarf pipefish is known for its slender, elongated body and lives in shallow coastal waters.
Researchers observed several dwarf pipefish swimming near the seagrass beds off the Florida Gulf Coast.
Due to their size, dwarf pipefish can be difficult to spot among the aquatic plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipefish - a slender, elongated fish related to seahorses
