Federal Reserve Board

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdɚəl rɪˈzɝv bɔrd / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜːv bɔːd / (BrE)
the seven-member governing body of the Federal Reserve System, responsible for setting monetary policy in the United States
seven-member board overseeing the US Federal Reserve System

Examples

The Federal Reserve Board announced a change in interest rates to curb inflation.
Janet Yellen chaired the Federal Reserve Board before becoming Secretary of the Treasury.
The decisions made by the Federal Reserve Board have a significant impact on the economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Federal Reserve System - central banking system of the United States
