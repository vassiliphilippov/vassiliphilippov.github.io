anise camphor

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪs ˈkæmfər / (AmE), / ˈænɪs ˈkæmfə / (BrE)
an aromatic organic compound, also known as anethole, widely used as a flavoring substance in food and beverages
aromatic flavoring compound (anethole)

Examples

The distinctive flavor of licorice comes from anise camphor present in the extract.
Many traditional liqueurs include anise camphor for its sweet aroma and taste.
Researchers analyzed the chemical properties of anise camphor to improve food flavorings.

Synset Examples

Anethole is an aromatic, unsaturated ether related to lignols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anethole - the main component of anise camphor, used as a flavoring agent

Etymology

From 'anise', the plant source, and 'camphor', referring to its camphor-like odor; the compound is also known as anethole, from Greek 'anēthon' (dill, anise).
