Wintun language

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntuːn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɪntuːn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an indigenous Copehan language spoken by the Wintun people of northern California
indigenous language of the Wintun people

Examples

The Wintun language is an important part of the cultural heritage of northern California tribes.
There are ongoing efforts to preserve and revitalize the Wintun language among younger generations.
Linguists have studied the Wintun language to better understand its structure and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wintun - a member of a group of Native American people of northern California

Etymology

From Wintun, the name of the indigenous people, plus 'language'.
