alluvial flat

1. NOUN

/ əˈluviəl flæt / (AmE), / əˈluːviəl flat / (BrE)
A level area of land formed by repeated deposits of alluvial (water-borne) sediment, typically by rivers or streams.
flat land formed by river deposits

Examples

The river changed course, leaving behind a fertile alluvial flat ideal for farming.
Many villages in the region are built on alluvial flats susceptible to seasonal flooding.
The alluvial flat was covered in silt after the spring floods receded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floodplain - flat land area adjacent to a river, formed mainly by river deposition

Etymology

From Latin 'alluvius' (to wash against) and 'flat' (level surface).
