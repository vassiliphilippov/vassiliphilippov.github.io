doomsday event

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːmz.deɪ ɪˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˈduːmz.deɪ ɪˈvɛnt / (BrE)
a catastrophic or apocalyptic occurrence, often believed to have catastrophic consequences for humanity or the world
catastrophic or apocalyptic occurrence

Examples

Many people worry that climate change could trigger a doomsday event in the near future.
The movie imagines a doomsday event that forces humanity to flee the planet.
Scientists warn that certain asteroid impacts could qualify as a doomsday event.

Synset Examples

The scientists predicted a doomsday event could happen in our lifetime.
He prepared for the doomsday event by stocking up on supplies.
Many movies depict a doomsday event that changes the world forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apocalypse - the complete final destruction of the world
  • Synonym: catastrophe - an event causing great and usually sudden damage

Etymology

From 'doomsday' (Old English dōmes dæg, 'day of judgment') + 'event'.
