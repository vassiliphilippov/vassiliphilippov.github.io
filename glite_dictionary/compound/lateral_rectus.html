lateral rectus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl ˈrɛk.təs / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl ˈrɛk.təs / (BrE)
the muscle on the outer side of the eyeball that moves the eye outward, away from the nose
muscle that moves eye outward
medical

Examples

The lateral rectus is responsible for moving the eye away from the midline of the face.
Damage to the lateral rectus muscle can cause the eye to turn inward, a condition known as esotropia.
The optometrist tested the function of her lateral rectus during the eye examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: medial rectus - the eye muscle responsible for moving the eyeball inward, toward the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of the side) and 'rectus' (straight).
