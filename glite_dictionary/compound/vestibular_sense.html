vestibular sense

1. NOUN

/ vɛˈstɪbjələr sɛns / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪbjʊlə sɛns / (BrE)
the sense that enables perception of balance, movement, and spatial orientation, arising from structures in the inner ear
sense of balance and spatial orientation
medical

Examples

After the roller coaster ride, Julia's vestibular sense was temporarily confused, making her feel dizzy.
The vestibular sense helps us maintain balance while walking or running on uneven ground.
Damage to the inner ear can affect a person's vestibular sense, resulting in difficulties with coordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equilibrioception - the ability to sense balance and spatial orientation

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance hall) referring to an area of the inner ear.
