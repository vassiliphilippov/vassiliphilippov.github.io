alkali grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kə.laɪ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪ ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a plant native to western North America with grasslike leaves and greenish-white flowers, often found in saline or alkaline soils
a salt-tolerant North American grass

Examples

Alfalfa is often planted alongside alkali grass in fields with saline soil.
The alkali grass thrives in areas where few other plants can survive.
Researchers are studying the resilience of alkali grass to better understand soil adaptation.
