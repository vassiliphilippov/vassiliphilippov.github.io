elapid snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛl ə pɪd sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛl ə pɪd sneɪk / (BrE)
any of a family of venomous snakes found in warm regions around the world, characterized by fixed hollow fangs used to deliver venom.
a type of venomous snake found in warm regions

Examples

The elapid snake family includes well-known members like cobras, mambas, and coral snakes.
Scientists are studying the venom of the elapid snake for potential medical uses.
An elapid snake can deliver a dangerous bite with its fixed fangs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra - a large, venomous elapid snake known for its hood
  • Antonym: viper - a venomous snake of the family Viperidae, not an elapid

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Elapidae', the family name, from Greek 'élapē' (kind of snake).
