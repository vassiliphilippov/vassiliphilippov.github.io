Colorado Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːləˌreɪdoʊ ˈdezərt / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˌreɪdəʊ ˈdezət / (BrE)
an arid region in southeastern California, noted for its hot, dry climate and unique desert flora and fauna
arid region in southeastern California

Examples

The Colorado Desert is home to a variety of cacti and wildlife adapted to its harsh climate.
During spring, wildflowers blossom across the Colorado Desert, attracting visitors from around the world.
Scientists study the unique ecosystem of the Colorado Desert to understand how plants survive extreme conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sonoran Desert - a large desert region in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, of which the Colorado Desert is a part
