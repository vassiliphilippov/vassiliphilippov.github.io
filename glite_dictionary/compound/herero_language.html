Herero language

1. NOUN

/ həˈrɛroʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / həˈrɛərəʊ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Bantu language spoken by the Herero people in Namibia, Botswana, and Angola.
Bantu language of the Herero people

Examples

The Herero language is an important part of Namibia's cultural heritage.
She is fluent in both English and Herero language.
Many oral traditions are preserved through storytelling in the Herero language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ovambo language - A Bantu language spoken mainly in Namibia

Etymology

From the name of the Herero people, the speakers of this language.
