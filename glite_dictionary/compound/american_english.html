American English

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ / (AmE), / əˌmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ / (BrE)
the English language as spoken and written in the United States, with its own distinctive vocabulary, pronunciation, and usage
English as used in the United States

Examples

Many students prefer to learn American English because they plan to study in the United States.
The spelling of 'color' in American English differs from 'colour' in British English.
Television shows often reflect the vocabulary and accents of American English.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: British English - the English language as used in Great Britain
