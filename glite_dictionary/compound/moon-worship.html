moon-worship

1. NOUN

/ ˈmun ˌwɝːʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmuːn ˌwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
the religious practice of worshiping the moon as a deity or spiritual force
worship of the moon as a deity

Examples

Some ancient cultures practiced moon-worship as part of their religious traditions.
Archaeological evidence suggests that moon-worship was common in early Mesopotamia.
The ritual of moon-worship involved offerings made to the lunar deity every full moon.
