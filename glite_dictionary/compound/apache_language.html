Apache language

1. NOUN

/ əˈpætʃi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈpætʃi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Apache people, belonging to the Athabaskan family of Native American languages
language of the Apache people

Examples

She spent years studying the Apache language to help preserve its cultural heritage.
Many young people are now learning the Apache language in community classes.
The Apache language uses sounds and grammar that are very different from English.

Etymology

Apache from Zuni apachu (enemy), referring to the people; 'language' from Latin lingua.
