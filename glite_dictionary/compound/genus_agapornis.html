genus Agapornis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs æɡəˈpɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs aɡəˈpɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small parrots in the family Psittacidae, commonly known as lovebirds, native to Africa.
genus of small African parrots (lovebirds)

Examples

The genus Agapornis includes nine species, all commonly referred to as lovebirds.
Scientists have studied the social behavior of birds in the genus Agapornis for decades.
African rainforests are the natural habitat of much of the genus Agapornis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovebird - a small, colorful African parrot often kept as a pet

Etymology

From Greek 'agapē' (love) and 'ornis' (bird).
