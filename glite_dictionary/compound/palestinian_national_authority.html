Palestinian National Authority

1. PROPN

/ ˌpælɪˈstiːniən ˈnæʃənl ɔːˈθɔrəti / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈstiːniən ˈnæʃnəl ɔːˈθɒrɪti / (BrE)
the governing body established to administer parts of the West Bank and Gaza Strip, with limited authority under agreements between Israel and the Palestine Liberation Organization
governing body for parts of West Bank and Gaza Strip

Examples

The Palestinian National Authority was established in 1994 following the Oslo Accords.
Many international organizations recognize the Palestinian National Authority as the representative of the Palestinian people.
Talks between Israel and the Palestinian National Authority aim to address longstanding issues in the region.
