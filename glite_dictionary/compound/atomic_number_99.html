atomic number 99

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ˈnʌmbɚ ˈnaɪnti naɪn / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈnʌmbə ˈnaɪnti naɪn / (BrE)
the chemical element einsteinium, a radioactive transuranic element with the atomic number 99, produced by bombarding plutonium with neutrons
chemical element einsteinium (element 99)

Examples

Scientists discovered atomic number 99 in the debris of the first hydrogen bomb explosion.
Atomic number 99 is named einsteinium after the renowned physicist Albert Einstein.
The production of atomic number 99 requires sophisticated laboratory techniques involving neutron bombardment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: einsteinium - a synthetic chemical element with symbol Es and atomic number 99
