training school

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪnɪŋ skuːl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnɪŋ skuːl / (BrE)
A correctional institution designed for the detention, discipline, and rehabilitation of young or first-time offenders.
juvenile correctional institution

Examples

After his arrest, he was sent to a training school for rehabilitation.
The state established its first training school for juvenile offenders in 1902.
Many former residents of the training school went on to lead successful lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform school - a school for the correctional training of young offenders
  • Synonym: juvenile detention center - facility where young offenders are held in detention

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪnɪŋ skuːl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnɪŋ skuːl / (BrE)
a school that provides practical education and instruction in vocational or technical skills.
school for vocational or technical skills

Examples

After high school, she attended a training school to become a certified electrician.
Many training schools offer programs in culinary arts, automotive repair, and information technology.
The city's new training school equips students with practical skills needed in today’s job market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocational school - a school giving instruction in skilled trades
