railroad vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌroʊd vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈreɪlrəʊd vaɪn / (BrE)
a creeping perennial plant with thick, sprawling stems and large, bright flowers, commonly found on coastal sand dunes in the southeastern United States, especially from Florida to Texas
creeping coastal plant in southeast US

Examples

Railroad vine helps stabilize sand dunes along the Gulf Coast with its extensive root system.
The bright purple flowers of the railroad vine are a striking sight on the beach in summer.
Conservationists are planting railroad vine to protect fragile coastal habitats from erosion.
