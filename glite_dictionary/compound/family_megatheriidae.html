family Megatheriidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli məˌɡæθəˈraɪəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli mɛˌɡæθəˈrɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, extinct ground sloths that lived from the Oligocene to the Holocene epoch, native to South America
family of extinct ground sloths

Examples

The family Megatheriidae included some of the largest land mammals of South America.
Fossils from the family Megatheriidae provide insight into prehistoric ecosystems.
Scientists study the family Megatheriidae to better understand extinction events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Megatherium - the type genus of family Megatheriidae, a giant ground sloth
  • Degree Lower: sloth - slow-moving mammals of Central and South America

Etymology

From New Latin Megatheriidae, from Megatherium (a genus of giant ground sloths) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
