empty promise

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpti ˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛmpti ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
a promise made without any intention of being kept or fulfilled
a promise not intended to be kept

Examples

After several empty promises from management, the workers lost trust in the company.
Voters are frustrated with politicians who make empty promises during elections.
He apologized again, but she was tired of his empty promises and wanted action.

Synset Examples

People are tired of the empty promises from polluticians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broken promise - a promise that has not been kept
