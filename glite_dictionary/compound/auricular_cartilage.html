auricular cartilage

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈɪkjələr ˈkɑːrtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjələ ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the cartilage forming the structure of the outer ear
cartilage in the outer ear
medical

Examples

Injury to the auricular cartilage can lead to deformation of the ear's shape.
The doctor explained that the auricular cartilage supports the outer ear structure.
Some piercings go through the auricular cartilage, which may take longer to heal.

Synset Examples

The auricular cartilage helps shape our outer ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartilage - firm, flexible connective tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' (outer ear) and 'cartilago' (cartilage).
