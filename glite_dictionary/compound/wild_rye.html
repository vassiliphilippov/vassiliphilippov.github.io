wild rye

1. NOUN

/ waɪld raɪ / (AmE), / waɪld raɪ / (BrE)
any of several perennial grasses of the genus Elymus, typically found in North America and Eurasia, often used for forage or soil conservation.
a grass of the genus Elymus

Examples

The wild rye growing along the riverbank provided shelter for small animals.
Many restoration projects use wild rye because it stabilizes soil effectively.
The field was dotted with patches of wild rye swaying gently in the wind.
