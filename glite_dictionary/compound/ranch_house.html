ranch house

1. NOUN

/ ˈræntʃ ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈrɑːntʃ ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A single-story house with a low-pitched roof, typically having a long, close-to-the-ground profile.
single-story house with a low roof

Examples

They decided to buy a ranch house because they preferred living on one floor.
The ranch house had a spacious backyard and large windows.
After retiring, she moved into a cozy ranch house in the suburbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungalow - a low house with a broad front porch, typically single-story

Etymology

From American English 'ranch', relating to ranch-style buildings found on ranches, plus 'house'.
