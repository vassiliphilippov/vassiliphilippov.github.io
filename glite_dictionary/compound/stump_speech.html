stump speech

1. NOUN

/ stʌmp spiːtʃ / (AmE), / stʌmp spiːtʃ / (BrE)
a speech repeatedly delivered by a politician during a campaign, typically outlining their main policies and goals
campaign speech repeated by a politician

Examples

The candidate delivered her stump speech to an enthusiastic crowd in the town square.
Reporters noted that his stump speech rarely changed from city to city.
Voters grew tired of hearing the same stump speech at every rally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign speech - a speech made by a candidate during a political campaign

Etymology

From 'stump', referring to early politicians standing on tree stumps to address crowds.
