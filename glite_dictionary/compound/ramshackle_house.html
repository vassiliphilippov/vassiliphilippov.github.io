ramshackle house

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmˌʃækəl haʊs / (AmE), / ˈræmʃakl haʊs / (BrE)
A house that is in poor condition, badly maintained, or likely to collapse.
dilapidated or poorly maintained house

Examples

They decided to buy the ramshackle house and restore it to its former glory.
A fierce storm tore part of the roof off the ramshackle house at the end of the street.
The children imagined the ramshackle house was haunted as they dared each other to enter.

Synset Examples

He lived in a ramshackly house that needed serious repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilapidated house - a house in disrepair or falling apart
  • Antonym: mansion - a large, impressive, and well-maintained house

Etymology

Ramshackle from English, originally an alteration of 'ramshackled', from 'ransack'.
