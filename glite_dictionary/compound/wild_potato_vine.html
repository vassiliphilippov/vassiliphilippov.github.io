wild potato vine

1. NOUN

/ waɪld pəˈteɪtoʊ vaɪn / (AmE), / waɪld pəˈteɪtəʊ vaɪn / (BrE)
A tropical American trailing or climbing perennial plant, known for its large, starchy root; sometimes considered an ancestor of the sweet potato.
tropical plant with large starchy root

Examples

The wild potato vine sprawled across the garden, its leaves providing ground cover.
Botanists studied the wild potato vine to learn more about the origins of the sweet potato.
Farmers in the region sometimes harvest the massive root of the wild potato vine for food.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sweet potato - a starchy, sweet-tasting root vegetable
