Master of Science

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɚ əv ˈsaɪ.əns / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stər əv ˈsaɪ.əns / (BrE)
A postgraduate academic degree awarded for study in the field of science or related disciplines.
postgraduate degree in science

Examples

She received her Master of Science in biology from a prestigious university.
To qualify for the position, applicants must hold a Master of Science in engineering or a related field.
After completing his Master of Science, he decided to pursue a doctorate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Bachelor of Science - undergraduate degree in science
  • Degree Higher: Doctor of Philosophy - highest academic degree, often following a Master's

Etymology

From Latin 'magister scientiae', meaning 'master of science'; adopted as an international academic designation.
