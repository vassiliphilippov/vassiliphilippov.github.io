red line

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a painted red line at the center of an ice hockey rink that divides it into two halves
center line in ice hockey rink

Examples

The puck sailed just past the red line before the referee blew his whistle.
Crossing the red line is necessary for certain types of passes in ice hockey.
The teams lined up on either side of the red line at the start of the game.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a limit or boundary in negotiations or discussions that must not be crossed without causing a breakdown or serious consequences
non-negotiable limit in negotiations

Examples

Negotiators were warned that crossing the red line could end the talks entirely.
Our company has a red line: we will not compromise our core values.
He made it clear that extending the deadline was a red line for his team.

Synset Examples

The negotiators established a red line for the agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line in the sand - a point beyond which one will not go; an established limit
