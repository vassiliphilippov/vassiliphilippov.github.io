barley-sugar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrli ˌʃuɡər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːli ˌʃuːɡə / (BrE)
a brittle, transparent, sweet candy made by melting and cooling cane sugar, often twisted into sticks
a twisted, transparent candy made from cane sugar

Examples

She offered the children a stick of barley-sugar as a treat.
The old sweet shop still sold jars of traditional barley-sugar.
He remembered the taste of barley-sugar from his childhood visits to his grandmother's house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candy - sweet food made with sugar or syrup
  • Degree Lower: toffee - soft chewy or hard sweet made with sugar and butter

Etymology

From 'barley' and 'sugar'; originally made with barley water and sugar.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrli ˌʃuɡər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːli ˌʃuːɡə / (BrE)
A hard, often twisted, translucent candy originally made with barley water and sugar, now typically made from sugar and flavouring.
type of hard twisted candy

Examples

As a child, my grandmother always kept a jar of barley-sugar on her kitchen shelf.
The old-fashioned barley-sugar sticks were displayed in a glass jar at the sweet shop.
She offered me a piece of barley-sugar during our afternoon tea.

Synset Examples

I bought some barley-sugar at the fair last weekend.
She reminisced about her childhood treats of barley-sugar.
The candy shop sells delicious barley-sugar sweets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candy - sweet food made from sugar or syrup
  • Synonym: toffee - a chewy sweet made from sugar and butter
  • Synonym: lozenge - a small, sweet, flavored tablet, often medicated

Etymology

Named from its original ingredients, barley and sugar; attested from the 17th century.
