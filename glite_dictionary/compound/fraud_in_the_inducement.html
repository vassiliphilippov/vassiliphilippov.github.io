fraud in the inducement

1. NOUN

/ frɔd ɪn ði ɪnˈdusmənt / (AmE), / frɔːd ɪn ði ɪnˈdjuːsmənt / (BrE)
A type of fraud in which one party uses deception to persuade another to enter into an agreement or contract, thereby invalidating the contract.
fraud used to induce agreement or contract
legal

Examples

The court found that fraud in the inducement had occurred, rendering the contract void.
Her lawyer alleged fraud in the inducement because she was misled about the terms of the agreement.
If you can prove fraud in the inducement, you may be able to rescind the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception for personal gain
