Master of Arts

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɚ əv ˈɑrts / (AmE), / ˌmɑːstə əv ˈɑːts / (BrE)
A master's degree awarded for study in the arts, humanities, or social sciences.
postgraduate degree in arts or humanities

Examples

She earned her Master of Arts in English literature from a prestigious university.
After completing his Master of Arts, he started teaching at the college.
Applicants must hold a Master of Arts or equivalent to be considered for the program.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Master of Science - master's degree in science or technology subjects

Etymology

From Latin 'magister artium', meaning 'master of the arts'.
