varietal wine

1. NOUN

/ vəˈraɪ.ɪ.t̬əl waɪn / (AmE), / vəˈraɪ.ɪ.təl waɪn / (BrE)
A wine made mainly from one type of grape and labeled with the name of that grape.
wine made from a single grape variety

Examples

Chardonnay is a popular varietal wine enjoyed around the world.
The label indicates that this varietal wine is made from Pinot Noir grapes.
Many consumers prefer varietal wines because of their distinctive grape-specific flavors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blended wine - a wine made from more than one grape variety

Etymology

From 'varietal', meaning relating to a variety, especially of grape, and 'wine'.
