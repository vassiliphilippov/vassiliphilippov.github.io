beta globulin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtə ˈɡloʊbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a type of globulin protein in blood plasma that transports iron and other substances
blood plasma protein that carries iron
medical

Examples

Beta globulin is essential for the transport of iron in the bloodstream.
The patient showed an abnormal level of beta globulin during the blood test.
Researchers are studying the role of beta globulin in various blood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alpha globulin - a different type of globulin in blood plasma
  • Degree Higher: gamma globulin - another major type of plasma globulin, important in immunity

Etymology

From Greek beta (second letter of the Greek alphabet) + globulin, referring to its migration pattern in electrophoresis.
