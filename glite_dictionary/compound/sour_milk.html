sour milk

1. NOUN

/ saʊɚ mɪlk / (AmE), / saʊə mɪlk / (BrE)
milk that has become sour, typically as a result of fermentation or spoilage, causing it to have a tangy taste and thicker texture
milk that has turned sour or gone bad

Examples

She accidentally poured sour milk into her coffee and grimaced at the taste.
Many traditional recipes use sour milk to add moisture and a tangy flavor to baked goods.
The sour milk smelled unpleasant, so he threw the entire carton away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoiled milk - milk that has become unsafe or unpleasant to consume due to bacterial growth
