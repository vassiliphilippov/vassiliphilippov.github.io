family Nostocaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmili nɒˌstoʊˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli nɒˌstɒˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of blue-green algae (cyanobacteria) found in freshwater and soil, known for forming colonies or filaments.
family of blue-green algae

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the family Nostocaceae in a remote mountain pond.
Many members of the family Nostocaceae can fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting their ecosystems.
The distinctive gelatinous colonies are characteristic of the family Nostocaceae.

Etymology

From Nostoc (the type genus name, of uncertain origin) + Latin -aceae (a suffix forming family names in botany).
