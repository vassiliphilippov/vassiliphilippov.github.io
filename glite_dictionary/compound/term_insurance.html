term insurance

1. NOUN

/ tɝm ɪnˈʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / tɜːm ɪnˈʃʊə.rəns / (BrE)
A type of insurance that provides coverage at a fixed rate of payments for a limited period of time, with no cash value at expiration.
life insurance with a fixed term and no cash value

Examples

Many young families choose term insurance because it offers higher coverage at a lower cost.
When his policy expired, John decided to renew his term insurance for another decade.
Unlike permanent policies, term insurance does not build any savings or investment component.

Synset Examples

term insurance is most often associated with life insurance policies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whole life insurance - insurance that provides coverage for the insured's entire life and includes a cash value
