concave polyhedron

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈkeɪv pəˈliːˌhiːdrən / (AmE), / kɒnˈkeɪv pɒlɪˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A polyhedron in which at least one of its plane sections forms a concave polygon.
polyhedron with concave sections

Examples

A concave polyhedron can have indentations or recesses, unlike its convex counterpart.
It is challenging to calculate the volume of a concave polyhedron due to its inward-facing angles.
Students examined models of both a concave polyhedron and a convex one for comparison.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convex polyhedron - a polyhedron where all plane sections are convex polygons
