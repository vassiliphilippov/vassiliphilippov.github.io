poke bonnet

1. NOUN

/ poʊk ˈbɑːnɪt / (AmE), / pəʊk ˈbɒnɪt / (BrE)
A woman's hat with a projecting brim that is tied under the chin, popular in the 19th century.
women's hat with a projecting brim, tied under the chin
archaic

Examples

In the period drama, every lady wore a poke bonnet as part of her costume.
The museum displayed a beautiful silk poke bonnet from the Victorian era.
She tied the ribbon of her poke bonnet securely beneath her chin before stepping outside.

Etymology

From 'poke' meaning a bag, and 'bonnet' from French 'bonnet' (cap). The poke refers to the projecting shape of the brim.
