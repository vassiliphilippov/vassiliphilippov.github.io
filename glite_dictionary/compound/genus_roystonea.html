genus Roystonea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌrɔɪˈstəniə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌrɔɪˈstiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of West Indian palm trees with feather-like leaves, also known as royal palms.
genus of West Indian royal palms

Examples

The genus Roystonea is renowned for its tall and elegant royal palms.
Botanists have studied the genus Roystonea extensively across the Caribbean islands.
Several iconic avenues in Havana are lined with palms belonging to the genus Roystonea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal palm - a palm tree of the genus Roystonea

Etymology

Named for the American general Roy Stone, who collected plants in the Caribbean.
