Battle of Britain

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæɾəl əv ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈbrɪtən / (BrE)
the prolonged aerial campaign in 1940 during which the German Luftwaffe bombed British cities and fought the Royal Air Force in World War II
World War II aerial campaign over Britain

Examples

The Battle of Britain marked a turning point in World War II history.
Many stories of heroism emerged from the pilots who fought in the Battle of Britain.
The outcome of the Battle of Britain helped prevent a German invasion of the United Kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Blitz - the sustained bombing campaign of British cities by Germany during World War II
