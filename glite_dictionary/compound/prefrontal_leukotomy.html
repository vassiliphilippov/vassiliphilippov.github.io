prefrontal leukotomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˈfrʌntəl luːˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈfrʌntəl luːˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure that involves severing nerve pathways in the frontal lobe of the brain, formerly used to treat certain mental illnesses but often resulting in significant cognitive and personality changes
surgical brain procedure for mental illness
medical

Examples

The controversial practice of prefrontal leukotomy was widely abandoned after the development of psychiatric medications.
He suffered lasting personality changes following a prefrontal leukotomy in the 1950s.
Medical historians discuss prefrontal leukotomy as an example of early neurosurgical treatment for mental illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobotomy - a surgical operation cutting into the prefrontal lobe of the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'tome' (cutting), originally coined in the early 20th century as a medical term.
