leaf-nosed snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːf ˌnoʊzd sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈliːf ˌnəʊzd sneɪk / (BrE)
a pale, blotched snake with a blunt snout found in southwestern North America, belonging to various related species
blunt-snouted snake of southwestern North America

Examples

The leaf-nosed snake is often spotted beneath rocks in arid regions of Arizona.
Unlike rattlesnakes, the leaf-nosed snake is non-venomous and rarely poses a danger to humans.
Researchers tracked the diet of the leaf-nosed snake to better understand its role in the desert ecosystem.
