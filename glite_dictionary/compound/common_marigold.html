common marigold

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.ən ˈmær.iˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən ˈmær.ɪˌɡəʊld / (BrE)
a species of annual plant native to Europe, with bright yellow or orange flowers, widely grown as an ornamental and used in herbal remedies; also known as calendula
a yellow or orange European annual flower (calendula)

Examples

The common marigold adds a splash of vibrant color to gardens throughout the summer.
Herbalists often use common marigold petals in natural skin treatments.
We planted a row of common marigolds along the walkway to deter pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calendula - another name for the common marigold (Calendula officinalis)
