New Zealand spinach

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈziːlənd ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (AmE), / njuː ˈziːlənd ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (BrE)
a sprawling Australasian plant (Tetragonia tetragonioides) cultivated for its edible, succulent leaves and young shoots, often used as a spinach substitute
a succulent-leaved edible Australasian plant

Examples

New Zealand spinach is a popular substitute for true spinach in summer gardens because it tolerates heat well.
She sautéed the fresh New Zealand spinach leaves with garlic and olive oil for dinner.
Gardeners appreciate how easily New Zealand spinach grows in sandy soils near the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinach - an edible plant with dark green leaves, commonly used as a vegetable
