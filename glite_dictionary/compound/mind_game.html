mind game

1. NOUN

/ maɪnd ɡeɪm / (AmE), / maɪnd ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a game or activity designed to challenge or exercise the intellect or mental skills
intellectual or brain-challenging game

Examples

Chess is a classic mind game that requires strategic thinking and planning.
He enjoys solving crosswords and other mind games in his free time.
Sudoku is a popular mind game that helps keep your brain sharp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzle - a game or problem that tests ingenuity or knowledge
  • Synonym: brain teaser - a puzzle requiring thought to solve
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00461040-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnd ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a deliberate psychological tactic or manipulation intended to confuse, intimidate, or gain an advantage over someone, especially in a competitive context
psychological manipulation for advantage

Examples

During the chess match, he tried to throw his opponent off balance with subtle mind games.
Some managers use mind games to motivate their employees or keep them alert.
She quickly recognized the mind game he was playing and refused to react.

Synset Examples

football players try to play mind games with the opposition
the jeweler's mind game is to convince lovers that the size of a gemstone reflects the depth of their feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychological warfare - the use of psychological tactics to undermine an opponent's morale
  • Synonym: bluff - an attempt to deceive or intimidate an opponent by pretending to have a stronger position
