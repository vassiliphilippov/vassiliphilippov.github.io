magnetic inclination

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˌɪnkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The angle that a magnetic needle or the Earth's magnetic field makes with the horizontal plane at a specific location.
angle between magnetic field and horizon

Examples

The survey team measured the magnetic inclination at several points across the field.
Changes in magnetic inclination can indicate variations in the Earth's magnetic field over time.
Compasses give different readings depending on the local magnetic inclination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic dip - the angle made with the horizontal by the Earth's magnetic field lines
