Pacific sardine

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk sɑrˈdin / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk sɑːˈdiːn / (BrE)
a small species of pilchard (Sardinops sagax) commonly found off the Pacific coast of North America, often used as food or bait
a small fish found off the North American Pacific coast

Examples

Commercial fishing for Pacific sardine declined sharply after the 1940s due to overfishing.
The Pacific sardine is an important food source for many larger marine animals.
After being caught, the Pacific sardine is often canned and exported worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, edible, oily fish; general term for similar species
