high dudgeon

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈdʌdʒən / (AmE), / haɪ ˈdʌdʒən / (BrE)
A feeling of intense indignation or anger, especially as expressed in a proud or self-righteous way; now chiefly used in the phrase 'in high dudgeon.'
intense indignation or anger

Examples

She left the meeting in high dudgeon after being criticized by her colleagues.
The actor stormed off the stage in high dudgeon when the director rejected his ideas.
Whenever things don't go his way, he tends to respond in high dudgeon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indignation - anger or annoyance provoked by what is perceived as unfair treatment
  • Antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction

Etymology

'Dudgeon' is of uncertain origin, first recorded in the 16th century in the sense of a feeling of offense or resentment.

2. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈdʌdʒən / (AmE), / haɪ ˈdʌdʒən / (BrE)
A state of great anger or indignation, often expressed in a formal or exaggerated way.
great anger or indignation

Examples

After the meeting, she left the office in high dudgeon, refusing to speak to anyone.
He responded to the criticism with high dudgeon, insisting he had done nothing wrong.
Several employees resigned in high dudgeon over the proposed company changes.

Synset Examples

He was in high dudgeon, upset over the unfair treatment.
She stormed out in high dudgeon after the argument.
The critics wrote their reviews in high dudgeon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indignation - anger or annoyance provoked by unfair treatment
  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility

Etymology

Probably from the obsolete word 'dudgeon' meaning anger or resentment, origin uncertain; possibly related to 16th-century English.
