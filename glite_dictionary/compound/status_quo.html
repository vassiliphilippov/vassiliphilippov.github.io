status quo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtəs ˈkwoʊ, ˈstæ- / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtəs ˈkwəʊ, ˈstæ- / (BrE)
the existing state of affairs, especially regarding social, political, or economic situations
existing state of affairs

Examples

The new policy is designed to disrupt the status quo in the education system.
Many people resist changes that threaten the status quo.
She challenged the status quo by proposing radical new ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: status - the social or professional position of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin, meaning ‘the state in which’.
