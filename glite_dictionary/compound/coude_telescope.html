coude telescope

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈdeɪ ˈtɛləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / kuːˈdeɪ ˈtɛlɪskəʊp / (BrE)
a reflecting telescope designed so that light is directed to a fixed focus point, often for use with a plate holder or spectrograph
type of reflecting telescope with fixed focus

Examples

The observatory installed a coude telescope to enable high-resolution spectrographic studies.
Unlike other designs, a coude telescope directs the light path to a stationary laboratory setup.
Researchers used the coude telescope for long-exposure observations due to its stable focal point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflecting telescope - a telescope that uses a mirror to collect and focus light

Etymology

From French 'coudé' meaning 'elbowed', describing the angled path of light within the telescope.
