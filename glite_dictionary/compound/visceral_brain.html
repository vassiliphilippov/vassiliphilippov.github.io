visceral brain

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsərəl breɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl breɪn / (BrE)
a set of neural structures in the brain involved in emotional behavior and processing
part of the brain controlling emotions
medical

Examples

Damage to the visceral brain can significantly affect a person's emotional responses.
The visceral brain is critical for processing feelings like fear and pleasure.
Scientists study the visceral brain to better understand the neurological basis of emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limbic system - a group of brain structures associated with emotion and memory

Etymology

From Latin 'viscera' (internal organs) in reference to the connection between emotions and internal bodily responses.
