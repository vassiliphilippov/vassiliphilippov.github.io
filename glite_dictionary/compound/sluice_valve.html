sluice valve

1. NOUN

/ sluːs vælv / (AmE), / sluːs vɑːlv / (BrE)
a valve or gate for regulating the flow of water through a sluice
valve controlling water in a sluice

Examples

The engineer inspected the sluice valve to ensure it was not leaking.
Flood control depends on the careful operation of each sluice valve along the canal.
Before repairs began, all sluice valves needed to be closed to stop the water flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gate valve - a valve that opens by lifting a barrier out of the path of the fluid

Etymology

From 'sluice,' meaning a channel for water, and 'valve,' from Latin 'valva' (leaf of a door).
