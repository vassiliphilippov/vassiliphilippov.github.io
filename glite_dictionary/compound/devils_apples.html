devil's apples

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz ˈæpəlz / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz ˈæpəlz / (BrE)
a Mediterranean plant with purple flowers, yellow fruits, and a forked root, once believed to have magical properties
a plant with purple flowers and yellow fruits

Examples

The legend claimed that devil's apples could cure mysterious illnesses.
Ancient herbalists often sought devil's apples for their supposed magical roots.
We found a field of devil's apples blooming near the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrake - a plant with a forked root resembling a human figure, historically thought to have magical powers

Etymology

Refers to plant roots associated in folklore with the devil; 'apple' as in 'fruit', not as the literal apple tree.
