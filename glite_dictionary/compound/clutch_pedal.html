clutch pedal

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌtʃ ˌpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈklʌtʃ ˌpɛdəl / (BrE)
A pedal in a vehicle that is pressed to engage or disengage the engine from the transmission, allowing gears to be changed.
pedal for engaging/disengaging engine and transmission

Examples

I pressed the clutch pedal before shifting to second gear.
The clutch pedal in her old car was much harder to push than in newer models.
Remember to fully depress the clutch pedal when starting the engine.

Synset Examples

he smoothly released the clutch with one foot and stepped on the gas with the other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brake pedal - pedal used to slow or stop a vehicle
  • Synonym: accelerator pedal - pedal used to increase a vehicle’s speed
