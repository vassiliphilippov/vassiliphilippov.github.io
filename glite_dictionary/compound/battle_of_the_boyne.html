Battle of the Boyne

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ðə ˈbɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ðə ˈbɔɪn / (BrE)
a decisive battle fought in Ireland in 1690, where the forces of William III defeated those of the deposed King James II, ending attempts to restore Catholicism in England by the Stuarts
1690 battle in Ireland between William III and James II

Examples

The anniversary of the Battle of the Boyne is still celebrated by some in Northern Ireland.
Historians often discuss the political consequences of the Battle of the Boyne for Irish society.
In 1690, William III's victory at the Battle of the Boyne marked a turning point in British and Irish history.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ðə bɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ðə bɔɪn / (BrE)
a battle fought in Ireland in 1690 in which William III defeated James II, marking a pivotal event in Irish and British history
historic battle in Ireland, 1690

Examples

The Battle of the Boyne was a turning point in Irish history.
Many annual parades in Northern Ireland commemorate the Battle of the Boyne.
Historians often debate the consequences of the Battle of the Boyne for religious and political power in Britain.

Etymology

Named after the River Boyne in eastern Ireland, near where the battle took place.
