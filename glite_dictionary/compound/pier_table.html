pier table

1. NOUN

/ pɪr ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / pɪə ˈteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
A low, narrow table designed to be set below a pier glass against a wall, often used in hallways or entryways.
narrow table placed under a pier glass

Examples

The antique pier table was positioned gracefully beneath the gilded mirror in the entry hall.
She placed a vase of fresh flowers on the elegant pier table to brighten the room.
The pier table and matching pier glass created a sophisticated focal point in the living area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: console table - a narrow table fixed to a wall, often similar to a pier table

Etymology

From 'pier glass' (a tall mirror placed between windows), itself from French 'pierre' (pillar, pier).
