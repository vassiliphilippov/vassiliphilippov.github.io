brown lacewing

1. NOUN

/ braʊn ˈleɪsˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / braʊn ˈleɪsˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
a small dark-colored insect of the family Hemerobiidae, known for its delicate wings and predatory larvae that feed on aphids and other pests
a small, dark-colored lacewing insect

Examples

The brown lacewing is a beneficial insect often found in gardens, where it helps control aphid populations.
I spotted a brown lacewing resting on the window last night.
Farmers encourage the presence of brown lacewings as a natural method of pest management.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: green lacewing - a light green insect of the family Chrysopidae
