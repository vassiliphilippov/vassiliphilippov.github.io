human botfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən ˈbɑtˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ˈbɒtˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a large tropical American fly whose larvae are parasitic on humans and other mammals
parasitic tropical American fly
medical

Examples

The human botfly is notorious for laying its eggs on the skin of mammals.
Doctors removed a human botfly larva from the patient's arm after their return from the rainforest.
Travelers to parts of Central and South America are warned about the risks of human botfly infestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a fly whose larvae are parasitic on mammals
