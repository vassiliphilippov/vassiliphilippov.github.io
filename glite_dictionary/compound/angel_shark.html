angel shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪndʒəl ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈeɪndʒəl ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a type of shark with a broad, flat body and winglike pectoral fins, resembling a ray but swimming like other sharks
a broad, flat-bodied shark species

Examples

The angel shark is often mistaken for a ray because of its flat body shape.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered angel shark populations in European waters.
The distinctive appearance of the angel shark helps it to blend into sandy ocean floors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - a large fish with a cartilaginous skeleton, known for its predatory habits
