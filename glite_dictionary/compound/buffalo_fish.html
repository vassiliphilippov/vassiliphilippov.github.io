buffalo fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəˌloʊ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ fɪʃ / (BrE)
any of several large freshwater fish of the genus Ictiobus, native to the Mississippi valley in North America, known for their sucker-like mouths.
large freshwater sucker fish of North America

Examples

The buffalo fish is commonly found in the rivers and lakes of the Mississippi valley.
Fishermen prize the buffalo fish for its size and fighting spirit.
Many Native American tribes historically relied on the buffalo fish as a food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucker - a freshwater fish with a mouth adapted for sucking, family Catostomidae
