unsung hero

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsʌŋ ˈhiːroʊ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌŋ ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
a person whose good deeds or significant contributions are not publicly recognized or celebrated
someone who does good without recognition

Examples

Although her name was never mentioned in the credits, she remained the unsung hero of the project.
Teachers are often the unsung heroes working tirelessly for their students' success.
Many healthcare workers became unsung heroes during the pandemic, quietly risking their lives for others.

Synset Examples

She’s the unsung hero of the charity, always working behind the scenes.
He was the unsung hero who saved the day without seeking praise.
The janitor is the unsung hero of our school, keeping it clean.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities

Etymology

Formed from 'unsung' (not celebrated in song or praise) and 'hero'; 'unsung' from Old English and 'hero' from Greek hērōs.
