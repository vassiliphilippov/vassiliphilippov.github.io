puritan lifestyle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊr.ɪ.tən ˈlaɪf.staɪl / (AmE), / ˈpjʊə.rɪ.tən ˈlaɪf.staɪl / (BrE)
A way of living characterized by strict moral values, self-discipline, and avoidance of indulgence or luxury.
life of strict moral values

Examples

He chose to adopt a puritan lifestyle after years of excess and indulgence.
Many early settlers in New England led a puritan lifestyle, emphasizing hard work and modesty.
She found it difficult to maintain a puritan lifestyle in the middle of a bustling city.

Synset Examples

Living a puritan lifestyle, she avoids all forms of indulgence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of pleasure
  • Antonym: hedonism - pursuit of pleasure and self-indulgence

Etymology

From 'Puritan', a 16th–17th century English Protestant group advocating strict moral and religious discipline, and 'lifestyle'.
