propenoic acid

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌpiːnoʊˈɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / prəʊˌpiːnəʊˈɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an unsaturated liquid carboxylic acid, also known as acrylic acid, used in the production of acrylic resins and plastics
unsaturated carboxylic acid used in making acrylics

Examples

Propenoic acid is widely used in the synthesis of polymers for paints and adhesives.
The chemical plant increased its capacity to produce propenoic acid for the plastics industry.
Exposure to propenoic acid should be minimized due to its irritating properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic acid - alternative common name for propenoic acid

Etymology

From 'propene' (the hydrocarbon) + '-oic acid' (indicating carboxylic acid).
