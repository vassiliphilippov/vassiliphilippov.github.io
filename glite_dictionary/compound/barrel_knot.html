barrel knot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəl nɑt / (AmE), / ˈbærəl nɒt / (BrE)
a knot used especially by anglers to join two fishing lines by wrapping the ends around each other two or three times
knot for joining fishing lines

Examples

The barrel knot is often used by fly fishermen to connect different weights of leader material.
To repair the broken line, she tied a secure barrel knot to join the two ends.
He practiced making a barrel knot until he could tie it quickly and efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood knot - a similar knot used for joining two pieces of fishing line
