family Characidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kəˈræsɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kəˈræsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of tropical freshwater fishes found in Africa, South America, and Central America, including tetras and related species.
family of tropical freshwater fishes

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep family Characidae members, such as tetras, because of their bright colors and active behavior.
Species within the family Characidae are native to rivers and lakes across South America.
Biologists are studying the evolutionary relationships among fishes in the family Characidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetra - small tropical freshwater fish from the family Characidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Characidae', based on Greek 'kharax' (a sharp stake or point), used in naming the type genus Charax.
