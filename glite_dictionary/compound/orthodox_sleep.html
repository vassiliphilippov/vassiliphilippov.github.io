orthodox sleep

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrθəˌdɑks sliːp / (AmE), / ˈɔːθədɒks sliːp / (BrE)
A recurring stage of sleep during which rapid eye movements and dreaming do not occur, accounting for about 75% of normal sleep time.
stage of sleep without rapid eye movements or dreams
medical

Examples

Most deep restorative processes occur during periods of orthodox sleep.
The researchers measured the proportion of orthodox sleep in each subject's sleep cycle.
Children often spend more time in orthodox sleep than adults do.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: REM sleep - stage of sleep with rapid eye movements and dreaming
  • Synonym: non-REM sleep - sleep stage lacking rapid eye movement or dreams; another name for orthodox sleep
