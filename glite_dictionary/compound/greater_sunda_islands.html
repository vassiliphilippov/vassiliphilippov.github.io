Greater Sunda Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈsʌndə ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈsʌndə ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of large islands in the Malay Archipelago of Southeast Asia, comprising Borneo, Sumatra, Java, and Sulawesi (Celebes)
group of large islands in Southeast Asia

Examples

The Greater Sunda Islands are known for their extraordinary biodiversity and rich cultural heritage.
Borneo, Sumatra, Java, and Sulawesi make up the Greater Sunda Islands of Indonesia.
Many species found in the Greater Sunda Islands are not found anywhere else in the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Lesser Sunda Islands - chain of smaller islands east of the Greater Sunda Islands
