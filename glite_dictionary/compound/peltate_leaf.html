peltate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛl.teɪt lif / (AmE), / ˈpɛl.teɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf that is shaped like a shield, with the petiole attached to the lower surface rather than the edge, as in nasturtium leaves.
shield-shaped leaf with central petiole

Examples

The nasturtium is easily identified by its round, peltate leaf with the stem attached near the center.
A botanist described the rare plant as having a distinctive peltate leaf arrangement.
Many aquatic plants feature a broad, peltate leaf that floats on the surface of the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmate leaf - leaf with lobes radiating from a single point

Etymology

From Latin 'pelta' (small shield), referring to the shield-like shape.
