ant lion

1. NOUN

/ ˈænt ˌlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈant ˌlʌɪ.ən / (BrE)
the larval stage of several insects (family Myrmeleontidae), known for preying on ants and other small insects by trapping them in sandy pits.
larval insect that preys on ants

Examples

The ant lion builds a conical pit in the sand to catch its prey.
Children watched an ant lion larva patiently waiting at the bottom of its trap.
Naturalists studied how the ant lion uses its jaws to capture ants.

Etymology

Named for its predatory behavior towards ants, likened to a 'lion' among ants.
