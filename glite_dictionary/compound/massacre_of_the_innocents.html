Massacre of the Innocents

1. PROPN

/ ˈmæsəkɚ əv ði ˈɪnəsənts / (AmE), / ˈmɑːsəkə əv ði ˈɪnəsənts / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the event in which King Herod ordered the execution of all male infants in Bethlehem, often depicted in art and literature as a symbol of tragic and unjust slaughter.
biblical event: Herod's massacre of infants

Examples

Many Renaissance paintings portray the Massacre of the Innocents with dramatic emotion and vivid detail.
The story of the Massacre of the Innocents has inspired countless artists across the centuries.
Historians debate the historical basis of the Massacre of the Innocents described in the Gospel of Matthew.
