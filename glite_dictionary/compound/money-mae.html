Money-Mae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni meɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌni meɪ / (BrE)
A person, typically a woman, who is especially savvy with money matters or skilled at finding good bargains.
person skilled in finances or bargain-hunting
informal

Examples

After helping her friends save money on vacation, they started calling her Money-Mae.
At every sale, Money-Mae manages to walk away with the best deals.
Whenever a big purchase comes up, we always ask Money-Mae for her advice.

Synset Examples

She became the Money-Mae of her family after her promotion.
As the Money-Mae of the group, she always finds the best deals.
Everyone turns to her for financial advice; she's Money-Mae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain hunter - a person who looks for the best prices or deals
  • Synonym: penny pincher - a person who is careful with spending and seeks to save money

Etymology

Modelled after 'Fannie Mae,' adopting 'Mae' as a playful moniker for a female skilled with money.
