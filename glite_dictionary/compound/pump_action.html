pump action

1. NOUN

/ pʌmp ˈækʃən / (AmE), / pʌmp ˈak.ʃən / (BrE)
A mechanism in certain rifles or shotguns in which a sliding lever is moved back and forth to eject a spent cartridge, cock the firearm, and load a new round.
firearms mechanism using sliding lever action

Examples

Many hunters prefer a pump action shotgun for its reliability and speed.
He practiced cycling the pump action smoothly before heading out to the range.
The officer used a pump action rifle during the training exercise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bolt action - action mechanism operated by manually manipulating a bolt
  • Antonym: lever action - firearm action operated by a lever located around the trigger area
