striate vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪ.eɪt veɪn / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.eɪt veɪn / (BrE)
any of the veins that drain the striate body and are tributaries to the basal vein in the brain.
vein draining the striate body in the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the striate vein can lead to complications in cerebral blood flow.
The neurosurgeon pointed out the location of the striate vein during the procedure.
Understanding the function of the striate vein is crucial in certain neurological studies.

Etymology

From Latin 'striatus' meaning striped, referring to the striate body in the brain.
