lynx-eyed

1. ADJ

/ lɪŋks aɪd / (AmE), / lɪŋks aɪd / (BrE)
Having very keen or sharp eyesight; able to notice even the smallest details.
having very keen eyesight

Examples

The lynx-eyed detective noticed the small scratch on the window frame.
Her lynx-eyed editor caught every tiny factual error in the manuscript.
Only a lynx-eyed observer would have spotted the rare bird in the dense foliage.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted as a cat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-eyed - having very keen vision
  • Synonym: eagle-eyed - having very keen or penetrating vision

Etymology

Refers to the lynx, a wild cat noted since ancient times for its keen eyesight, used figuratively in English since the early 17th century.
