cringe culture

1. NOUN

/ krɪndʒ ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / krɪndʒ ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
A social atmosphere in which people are criticized or mocked for behavior deemed embarrassing, resulting in widespread secondhand embarrassment and self-censorship.
social environment focused on embarrassment or mockery

Examples

Many teenagers feel pressured by cringe culture when posting online.
The rise of cringe culture has made some people more cautious about sharing their interests.
Some argue that cringe culture stifles creativity and individual expression.

Synset Examples

Cringe culture makes people afraid to express themselves.
Many artists thrive despite the pressures of cringe culture.
She avoids social media because of the cringe culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel culture - The practice of withdrawing support for public figures after they have done or said something considered objectionable

Etymology

From 'cringe' (feel embarrassment or disgust) and 'culture' (shared attitudes or practices).
