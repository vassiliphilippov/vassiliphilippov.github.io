type IV allergic reaction

1. NOUN

/ taɪp fɔr əˈlɜrdʒɪk riˈækʃən / (AmE), / taɪp fɔː əˈlɜːdʒɪk riˈækʃən / (BrE)
An allergic reaction that develops hours after exposure to an allergen, typically involving immune cell-mediated responses rather than antibodies.
delayed immune allergic reaction
medical

Examples

The rash she developed was due to a type IV allergic reaction to nickel in her jewelry.
Doctors diagnosed his skin inflammation as a type IV allergic reaction after patch testing.
A type IV allergic reaction typically emerges 24 to 72 hours after contact with the allergen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delayed hypersensitivity - an immune response that appears hours after exposure to an antigen
