rose moss

1. NOUN

/ roʊz mɑs / (AmE), / rəʊz mɒs / (BrE)
A low-growing flowering plant (Portulaca grandiflora) widely cultivated for its moss-like foliage and numerous brightly colored blossoms.
ornamental flowering plant with moss-like foliage

Examples

The vibrant blooms of rose moss add color to the garden all summer long.
She planted rose moss in the sunny border for its drought tolerance and easy care.
Many gardeners choose rose moss because its flowers open in bright sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portulaca - a genus of flowering plants of which rose moss is a species
  • Synonym: portulaca - another common name for rose moss
