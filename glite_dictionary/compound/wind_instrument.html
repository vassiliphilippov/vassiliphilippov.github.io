wind instrument

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (AmE), / wɪnd ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (BrE)
a musical instrument in which sound is produced by the movement of air through an enclosed column, typically by blowing or with bellows
musical instrument sounded by air

Examples

The clarinet is a popular wind instrument in orchestras and bands.
She learned to play several wind instruments, including the flute and saxophone.
A good wind instrument player must have excellent breath control.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brass instrument - a wind instrument made of brass or similar metal
  • Degree Lower: woodwind instrument - a wind instrument traditionally made of wood

Etymology

Formed from 'wind' (moving air) and 'instrument'.
