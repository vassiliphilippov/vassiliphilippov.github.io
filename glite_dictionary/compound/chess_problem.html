chess problem

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛs ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / tʃɛs ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
A puzzle involving chess pieces arranged on a board, requiring the solver to achieve a specified objective, such as checkmating the opponent in a certain number of moves.
a puzzle using chess pieces and rules

Examples

She spent her afternoon trying to solve a particularly challenging chess problem.
The magazine features a new chess problem each week for readers to enjoy.
Many grandmasters enjoy creating their own chess problems as a test of creativity.

Synset Examples

The sequel to Alice's Adventure's in Wonderland was designed to be a playable, albeit whimsical chess problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzle - a game, toy, or problem that tests ingenuity or knowledge
  • Synonym: chess - a board game for two players using special pieces
