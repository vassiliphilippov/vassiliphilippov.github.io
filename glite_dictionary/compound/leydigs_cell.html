Leydig's cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪdɪɡz sɛl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪdɪɡz sɛl / (BrE)
a cell found in the testes that produces and secretes the hormone testosterone
testicular cell that secretes testosterone
medical

Examples

Leydig's cell dysfunction can lead to hormonal imbalances and fertility issues in adult males.
The researcher observed a high concentration of Leydig's cells in the tissue sample.
Testosterone is primarily produced by Leydig's cell in the testes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testosterone - the primary male sex hormone produced by Leydig's cells

Etymology

Named after German anatomist Franz Leydig (1821–1908), who first described the cell.
