goat god

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊt ɡɑd / (AmE), / ɡəʊt ɡɒd / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a god of fields and woods, shepherds and flocks, depicted as having the legs, horns, and ears of a goat; identified with Pan or the Roman gods Sylvanus and Faunus.
mythical deity with goat-like features

Examples

According to the ancient myth, the goat god played music on his pipes, inspiring fear and frenzy.
A statue of the goat god stood at the edge of the forest, covered in moss and vines.
Roman mythology often blended the goat god with similar rustic deities like Faunus and Sylvanus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pan - Greek god of the wild, flocks, and shepherds, with goat features
  • Synonym: faun - mythical woodland creature with goat features, Roman mythology
