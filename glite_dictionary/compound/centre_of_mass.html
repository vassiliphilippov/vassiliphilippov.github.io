centre of mass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ əv mæs / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə əv mæs / (BrE)
The point in a body or system where the total mass may be considered to be concentrated for calculations involving motion and balance.
mean position of mass in a body

Examples

The centre of mass of the object was located using a simple balancing experiment.
If you support the board at its centre of mass, it will stay level.
In physics problems, we often calculate the trajectory of the centre of mass rather than tracking every particle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center of gravity - the point in a body where the force of gravity can be considered to act
