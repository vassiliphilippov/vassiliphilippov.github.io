atheocratic policy

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪθi.oʊˈkrætɪk ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˌeɪθi.əˈkrætɪk ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A government policy or principle that is based on non-theistic or non-religious foundations, explicitly excluding religious doctrine.
policy based on non-theistic principles

Examples

The government's new atheocratic policy advocates for education reform without reference to any religious traditions.
Some citizens oppose an atheocratic policy, fearing it denies moral values rooted in faith.
Debates over atheocratic policy have become more common in secular societies.

Synset Examples

Debates on atheocratic policies are frequent in parliament.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theocratic policy - a policy based on religious or theistic principles
  • Synonym: secular policy - policy not influenced by religious considerations

Etymology

Formed from 'atheocratic' (from Greek 'a-' meaning 'not' + 'theos' meaning 'god' + '-cratic' relating to rule) and 'policy'.
