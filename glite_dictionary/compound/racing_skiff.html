racing skiff

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsɪŋ skɪf / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪŋ skɪf / (BrE)
a narrow, lightweight boat designed for a single rower, used in competitive rowing
narrow boat for single rower in races

Examples

The athlete trained daily in his racing skiff to prepare for the upcoming regatta.
Expert rowers appreciate the stability and speed of a modern racing skiff.
Transporting a racing skiff requires care due to its long, slender design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - a long, narrow racing boat used in rowing

Etymology

From 'skiff', meaning a small boat, with 'racing' denoting use in competition.
