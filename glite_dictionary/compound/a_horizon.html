A horizon

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
the uppermost layer of soil, typically rich in organic material and humus, found just below the surface
top layer of soil profile with humus

Examples

The A horizon in this forest soil is dark and rich with decomposed leaves.
Farmers often monitor the A horizon to assess soil fertility and potential crop yields.
Erosion can cause the A horizon to become thinner, reducing soil productivity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: B horizon - the soil layer beneath the A horizon, often containing minerals leached from above
  • Degree Higher: O horizon - the soil layer above the A horizon, mostly consisting of organic matter like decomposed leaves

Etymology

Named using the letter 'A' to denote its position in a standard soil profile classification.
