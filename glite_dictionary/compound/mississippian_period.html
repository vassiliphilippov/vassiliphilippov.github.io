Mississippian period

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpiən ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpiən ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
A subdivision of the Carboniferous Period, from about 345 to 310 million years ago, marked by expanded land areas and the appearance of primitive ammonites and winged insects.
geological period within the Carboniferous

Examples

The Mississippian period saw the first appearance of large, complex forests on land.
Many limestone deposits in North America date back to the Mississippian period.
Scientists study fossils from the Mississippian period to understand early marine life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pennsylvanian period - the later subdivision of the Carboniferous Period

Etymology

Named after the Mississippi River Valley in the United States, where rocks from this period are well exposed.
