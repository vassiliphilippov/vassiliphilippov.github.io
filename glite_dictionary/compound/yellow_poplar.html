yellow poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈpɑplɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
the light, easily worked wood of the tulip tree, often used for furniture and veneer
wood from the tulip tree

Examples

The craftsman selected yellow poplar for its smooth finish and durability.
Most of the cabinets in the house are made of yellow poplar, giving the rooms a light, airy feel.
Because yellow poplar is easy to work with, it is a favorite among woodworkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip tree - a North American tree (Liriodendron tulipifera) whose wood is called yellow poplar

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈpɑplɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
A tall North American deciduous tree with large, tulip-shaped greenish-yellow flowers, valued for its soft white wood used in cabinet work; also known as tulip tree.
North American tree with tulip-like flowers; timber source

Examples

The yellow poplar is prized by woodworkers for its fine, even-grained timber.
In spring, the yellow poplar is covered in greenish-yellow tulip-shaped flowers.
Our cabin was built from locally sourced yellow poplar logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip tree - another name for yellow poplar, a North American deciduous tree
