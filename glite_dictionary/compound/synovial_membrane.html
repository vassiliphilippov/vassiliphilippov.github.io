synovial membrane

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnoʊ.vi.əl ˈmɛm.breɪn / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊ.vi.əl ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
A thin membrane lining the capsule of a synovial joint, which secretes synovial fluid for lubrication.
membrane lining a joint capsule
medical

Examples

The synovial membrane produces the fluid that helps lubricate the knee joint.
If the synovial membrane becomes inflamed, it can lead to joint pain and stiffness.
Arthritis often affects the synovial membrane, causing swelling and discomfort in the affected area.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: synovial fluid - the lubricating fluid secreted by synovial membranes in joints

Etymology

From 'synovial', relating to synovia (joint fluid), from Latin 'synovia', and 'membrane', from Latin 'membrana' (skin, parchment).
