genus Sinapis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˈneɪpɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˈneɪpɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of Old World flowering herbs in the family Brassicaceae, usually including the white mustard.
genus of Old World herbs (mustard family)

Examples

The genus Sinapis includes several species used as sources of mustard.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within the genus Sinapis.
Some taxonomists consider the genus Sinapis a separate group from Brassica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus Brassica - genus of plants including cabbages, broccoli, and related species

Etymology

From Latin 'Sinapis', from Greek 'sinapi' meaning 'mustard'.
