stump-water

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmp ˌwɑtɚ / (AmE), / ˈstʌmp ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water that is mixed with debris or seepage from tree stumps, often cloudy and usually considered unsafe to drink.
water mixed with debris from tree stumps

Examples

After the flood, the fields were flooded with stump-water that smelled of decaying wood.
Thirsty, he considered drinking the stump-water but decided it was too risky.
The color of the stump-water made it clear it was not suitable for cooking.

Synset Examples

The river was muddy, filled with stump-water from the rain.
The fishermen complained about the stump-water affecting their catch.
Avoid drinking stump-water; it's not safe.
