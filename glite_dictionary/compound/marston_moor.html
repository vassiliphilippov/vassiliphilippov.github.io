Marston Moor

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrstən mʊr / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstən mɔː / (BrE)
an area of former moorland in northern England, site of a major battle during the English Civil War in 1644
historical moor in northern England

Examples

The Battle of Marston Moor was a turning point in the English Civil War.
Many historians visit Marston Moor to study the remains of the old battlefield.
A monument now stands on Marston Moor to commemorate those who fought there.
