pseudoskeptic approach

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˈskɛptɪk əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈskɛptɪk əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A manner or method of argument that appears skeptical but lacks true, critical inquiry or open-mindedness.
false skeptical attitude or method

Examples

Many debates are derailed by participants who use a pseudoskeptic approach rather than honest questioning.
Adopting a pseudoskeptic approach can hinder progress, as it focuses on dismissal over investigation.
The scientist cautioned against the pseudoskeptic approach that rejects new evidence without fair examination.

Synset Examples

His pseudoskeptic approach frustrates genuine scientific discourse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: skepticism - an attitude of doubting the truth or value of something
  • Degree Higher: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas

Etymology

Formed from 'pseudo-' (false) and 'skeptic', from Greek 'skeptikos' (thoughtful, inquiring).
