desert iguana

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛzɚt ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈdɛzət ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (BrE)
a small, long-tailed lizard native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico
lizard native to southwestern US and northwestern Mexico

Examples

The desert iguana is often seen basking on rocks during the hottest part of the day.
Researchers observed a desert iguana burrowing into the sandy soil to escape predators.
During our hike, we spotted several desert iguanas near the cacti.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iguana - a large herbivorous lizard native to Central and South America and the Caribbean
