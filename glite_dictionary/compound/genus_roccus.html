genus Roccus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈrɑkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈrɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Serranidae, including the common striped bass.
genus of marine fish (striped bass)

Examples

The genus Roccus includes several species popular with anglers along the Atlantic coast.
Taxonomic changes have occurred with the genus Roccus over the years.
Some field guides still refer to the striped bass as belonging to the genus Roccus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped bass - a common marine fish also known as Roccus saxatilis

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Roccus', former genus name for striped bass.
