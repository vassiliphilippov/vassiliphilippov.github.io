base pair

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪs ˌpɛr / (AmE), / ˈbeɪs ˌpeə / (BrE)
a pair of complementary nitrogenous bases in a DNA or RNA molecule, joined by hydrogen bonds, such as adenine–thymine or guanine–cytosine in DNA
paired nitrogenous bases in DNA or RNA

Examples

The DNA double helix is stabilized by millions of base pairs linking the two strands.
A mutation can occur when a base pair is incorrectly inserted during DNA replication.
RNA contains the base pairs adenine–uracil and guanine–cytosine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleotide - molecule consisting of a nitrogenous base linked to a sugar and phosphate; building block of nucleic acids
