golden age

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
a period of outstanding achievement, prosperity, or excellence in a particular field or activity
period of greatest achievement or prosperity

Examples

Many historians consider the Renaissance to be the golden age of art and literature.
The invention of the steam engine marked the golden age of industrial progress.
Fans often reminisce about the golden age of radio, when broadcasts captivated audiences nationwide.

Synset Examples

it was the golden age of cinema

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heyday - period of greatest success or popularity
  • Antonym: dark age - period of decline or stagnation

Etymology

From classical tradition, referring to an early period of human existence viewed as idyllic and prosperous, influenced by works such as Ovid's 'Metamorphoses' (Latin: aurea aetas).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊl.dən eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl.dən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
a period in history, literature, or a field marked by great peace, prosperity, or outstanding achievement
period of great prosperity or achievement

Examples

Many consider the 1950s to be the golden age of American television.
The city experienced a golden age of art and architecture during the Renaissance.
Historians debate when the empire’s golden age truly began and ended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renaissance - period of revival and renewed interest in something
  • Antonym: dark age - a period of decline or decay

Etymology

From Greek 'Chrýseon génos' (golden race); the term 'golden age' has been used in English since the 16th century to describe an idealized past.
