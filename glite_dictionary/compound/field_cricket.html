field cricket

1. NOUN

/ fiːld ˈkrɪkɪt / (AmE), / fiːld ˈkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
a common black or dark brown cricket found in fields in North America, known for its chirping and sometimes damaging crops or entering buildings
a type of black or brown cricket insect

Examples

The distinctive chirping of the field cricket can be heard on summer evenings.
Farmers often try to protect their crops from damage by the field cricket.
A field cricket darted across the kitchen floor, startling everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket - an insect known for its chirping sound
