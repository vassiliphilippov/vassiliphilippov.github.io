Peasants' Revolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛzənts rɪˈvoʊlt / (AmE), / ˈpɛzənts rɪˈvəʊlt / (BrE)
a widespread uprising in England in 1381 by peasants and lower classes against taxation and social injustice, ultimately suppressed by King Richard II
1381 English peasants' uprising

Examples

The Peasants' Revolt of 1381 was a turning point in English history.
Many textbooks discuss the causes and consequences of the Peasants' Revolt.
In the aftermath of the Peasants' Revolt, feudal laws in England were gradually reformed.
