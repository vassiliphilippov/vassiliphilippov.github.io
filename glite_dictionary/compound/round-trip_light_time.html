round-trip light time

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd trɪp ˈlaɪt ˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd trɪp ˈlaɪt ˌtaɪm / (BrE)
the time required for a signal to travel from Earth to a spacecraft or celestial body and back to the starting point, measured using the speed of light
signal travel time to and from a space object

Examples

The round-trip light time to Mars can be over 24 minutes depending on the planets' positions.
Scientists calculate the round-trip light time before sending commands to distant spacecraft.
Because of the round-trip light time, communication with the probe is delayed by several minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light-time - the time taken by light to travel a certain distance
