genus Curcuma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kɜrˈkuː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈkjuː.mə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical perennial herbs of the ginger family, native to Asia, including species such as turmeric
genus of tropical Asian herbs (ginger family)

Examples

The genus Curcuma includes several plants with important culinary and medicinal uses.
Turmeric, a member of the genus Curcuma, is widely used as a spice in many cuisines.
Researchers are studying different species within the genus Curcuma for their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turmeric - a spice and plant (Curcuma longa) from this genus

Etymology

From Latin 'Curcuma', from Arabic 'kurkum' (saffron or turmeric).
