cluster of differentiation 8

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌstər əv ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃiˈeɪʃən eɪt / (AmE), / ˈklʌstə əv ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃɪˈeɪʃən eɪt / (BrE)
a membrane glycoprotein (commonly abbreviated as CD8) found mainly on the surface of cytotoxic T cells, important for immune response
a cell surface glycoprotein (CD8) on cytotoxic T cells
medical

Examples

The presence of cluster of differentiation 8 on T cells is crucial for targeting infected cells.
Researchers measured levels of cluster of differentiation 8 in blood samples to assess immune function.
Most cytotoxic T lymphocytes display the cluster of differentiation 8 glycoprotein on their surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CD8 - abbreviation for cluster of differentiation 8

Etymology

Formed from 'cluster of differentiation', a classification system for cell surface molecules, plus the identifying number 8.
