wild sweet potato vine

1. NOUN

/ waɪld swiːt pəˈteɪtoʊ vaɪn / (AmE), / waɪld swiːt pəˈteɪtəʊ vaɪn / (BrE)
a tropical American climbing or trailing perennial plant with a large starchy root, sometimes considered an ancestor of the cultivated sweet potato
tropical vine with large starchy root

Examples

The wild sweet potato vine grows rapidly in tropical climates and can cover large areas.
Botanists studied the wild sweet potato vine to learn more about the origins of the modern sweet potato.
Its enormous root makes the wild sweet potato vine an important source of food for some communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet potato - a starchy, sweet-tasting root vegetable from the same genus
