Seidlitz powders

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdlɪts ˌpaʊdərz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdlɪts ˌpaʊdəz / (BrE)
a type of effervescent medicinal powder containing sodium bicarbonate, Rochelle salt, and tartaric acid, formerly used as a laxative
effervescent medicinal laxative powder
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Seidlitz powders as a gentle laxative for his patient.
In Victorian times, Seidlitz powders were a popular remedy for digestive complaints.
She mixed the Seidlitz powders with water, watching them fizz before drinking.

Etymology

Named after Seidlitz, a village in Bohemia (now in the Czech Republic), known for its mineral springs.
