genus Ficus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfiːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees, shrubs, or climbers, including the common fig and related species.
genus of fig trees and related plants

Examples

The genus Ficus includes hundreds of species found throughout the tropics.
Several houseplants belong to the genus Ficus, such as the popular rubber plant.
Many animals rely on the fruit produced by trees of the genus Ficus for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig tree - tree or shrub bearing edible figs

Etymology

From Latin 'ficus' (fig).
