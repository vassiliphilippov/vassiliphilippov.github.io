common teasel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈtizəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈtiːzəl / (BrE)
a prickly plant native to Europe and Asia, with lilac flowers and dried flower heads used in raising a nap on woolen cloth
a prickly plant with lilac flowers

Examples

The fields were sprinkled with common teasel, attracting bees and butterflies.
Traditional weavers used dried common teasel heads to finish woolen fabrics.
After blooming, the lilac flowers of common teasel turn into spiky, cone-shaped seed heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - a prickly plant whose dried heads are used in textile work
