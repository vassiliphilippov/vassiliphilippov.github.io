lip service

1. NOUN

/ lɪp ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / lɪp ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
Support or agreement that is insincerely expressed but not backed up by actions.
insincere expression of support or agreement

Examples

The company paid lip service to environmental issues while continuing to pollute the river.
Politicians often give lip service to healthcare reform without passing meaningful laws.
We need more than just lip service; we need real change in this organization.

Synset Examples

She offered lip service but ignored the problem.
We need action, not just lip service to change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty promise - a promise made without intention of keeping it
  • Synonym: tokenism - the practice of making only a perfunctory or symbolic effort
  • Antonym: action - the process of doing something to achieve a result

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'lip' (mouth) and 'service' (act of serving), referring to verbal expression without real commitment.
