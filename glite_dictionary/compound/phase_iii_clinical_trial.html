phase III clinical trial

1. NOUN

/ feɪz θriː klɪnɪkəl traɪəl / (AmE), / feɪz θriː klɪnɪkəl traɪəl / (BrE)
a large-scale clinical trial conducted to confirm the effectiveness and monitor the side effects of a treatment or drug that has shown promising results in earlier phases, often required before formal regulatory approval
large-scale confirmatory drug trial
medical

Examples

The new cancer drug is currently being tested in a phase III clinical trial involving thousands of patients.
A successful phase III clinical trial is often the last step before a drug is approved by regulatory agencies.
After passing the phase III clinical trial, the company submitted the treatment for FDA review.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phase I clinical trial - small early safety trial of a drug or treatment
  • Degree Lower: phase II clinical trial - medium-sized trial to test efficacy and safety before phase III
  • Degree Higher: phase IV clinical trial - post-approval trial for additional safety data

Etymology

From 'phase' (stage of development) and 'clinical trial' (medical experiment), with 'III' denoting the third major stage in drug testing.
