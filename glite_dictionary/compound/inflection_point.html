inflection point

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɛkʃən pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃən pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a moment or stage at which significant change occurs; a critical turning point in a process, situation, or organization
critical turning point; significant moment of change

Examples

The launch of their new product marked an inflection point in the company's history.
Many analysts believe we are approaching an inflection point in renewable energy adoption.
Her decision to change careers proved to be a major inflection point in her life.

Synset Examples

The merger became the inflection point for our company.
This new policy marks an inflection point in our strategy.
Reaching this milestone was an inflection point for growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a time at which a decisive change in a situation occurs

Etymology

From Latin 'inflectere' (to bend), originally used in mathematics to describe a point of curve change.
