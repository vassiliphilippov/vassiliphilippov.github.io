Japanese lawn grass

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz lɔn ɡræs / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz lɔːn ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass native to East Asia, commonly used for lawns, especially in China, Japan, and the United States.
East Asian grass used for lawns

Examples

The gardener recommended planting Japanese lawn grass because of its resilience to foot traffic.
Many golf courses in warm climates use Japanese lawn grass for its fine texture and dense growth.
After reseeding with Japanese lawn grass, their yard looked lush and green throughout the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoysia japonica - The botanical name for Japanese lawn grass
  • Antonym: Bermuda grass - A different grass species used for lawns
