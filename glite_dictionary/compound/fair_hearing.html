fair hearing

1. NOUN

/ fɛr ˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / feə ˈhɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
a legal proceeding in which someone is given an impartial opportunity to present their case before a decision is made, especially when immediate judicial recourse is unavailable
impartial legal proceeding; chance to present one's case
legal

Examples

Everyone has the right to a fair hearing before decisions affecting their rights are made.
The defendant argued that she had not received a fair hearing in the deportation case.
A fair hearing is essential to protect individuals from unjust government actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due process - the legal requirement that the state respect all legal rights owed to a person
