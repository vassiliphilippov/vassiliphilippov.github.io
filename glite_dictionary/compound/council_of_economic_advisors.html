Council of Economic Advisors

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊnsəl əv iˈkɑːnəmɪk ædˈvaɪzɚz / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnsəl əv iːˈkɒnəmɪk ədˈvaɪzəz / (BrE)
a group of economists who advise the President of the United States on economic policy
US presidential economic advisory group
american

Examples

The Council of Economic Advisors issued a report on the current state of the job market.
The President frequently consults the Council of Economic Advisors before making major policy decisions.
Congress requested input from the Council of Economic Advisors regarding potential tax reforms.
