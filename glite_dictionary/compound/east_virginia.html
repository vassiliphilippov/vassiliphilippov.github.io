East Virginia

1. PROPN

/ ist vərˈdʒɪn.jə / (AmE), / iːst vəˈdʒɪn.jə / (BrE)
the region in the eastern part of the U.S. state of Virginia, often contrasted with West Virginia or western areas of Virginia.
region in eastern Virginia, USA

Examples

Many early settlers traveled through East Virginia before moving further south.
The climate in East Virginia is influenced by its proximity to the Atlantic Ocean.
Folk songs from East Virginia are an important part of Appalachian music traditions.

Synset Examples

The history of East Virginia is fascinating.
He spent his summer in East Virginia visiting family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West Virginia - a state formed from the western part of Virginia
