race problem

1. NOUN

/ reɪs ˈprɑːbləm / (AmE), / reɪs ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
A social or political issue arising from conflict or tension between different racial groups in the same area.
issue caused by conflict between races

Examples

The city faced a significant race problem during the early 1960s civil rights movement.
Government policies were enacted in an attempt to address the longstanding race problem.
Community leaders held discussions aimed at finding solutions to the local race problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racial conflict - sustained struggle or tension between different racial groups
  • Antonym: racial harmony - state of peaceful coexistence between races
