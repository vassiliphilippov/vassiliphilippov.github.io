commercial instrument

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɝː.ʃəl ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɜː.ʃəl ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (BrE)
a legal document used in the conduct of commerce, such as a bill of exchange, promissory note, or check
legal document for commercial transactions
legal

Examples

The lawyer explained that a promissory note is a type of commercial instrument used in lending agreements.
Banks often deal with various commercial instruments in the course of daily business.
A bill of exchange is a common commercial instrument utilized in international trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiable instrument - a written order or promise to pay a specific sum of money that can be transferred to another party
