powder room

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.dɚ ˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.də ˌruːm / (BrE)
a restroom for women, especially in a public or semi-public building
women's restroom (public place)

Examples

Excuse me, could you tell me where the powder room is?
She stepped into the powder room to freshen her makeup before returning to the party.
The hotel lobby has a beautifully decorated powder room for guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restroom - a room containing toilets and sinks for public use
  • Synonym: toilet - a room equipped with a toilet and often a washbasin

Etymology

From 'powder' (face powder) + 'room'; originally denoting a room where women could reapply makeup.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdɚ ruːm / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə ruːm / (BrE)
a restroom for women in a public place, or a small bathroom for guests in a home
women's restroom or guest bathroom

Examples

She excused herself to visit the powder room before the meal was served.
The powder room at the theater was crowded during intermission.
Guests were welcomed to use the downstairs powder room during the party.

Synset Examples

The powder room was busy during the intermission.
The restaurant's powder room was elegantly decorated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restroom - public toilet facility
  • Synonym: bathroom - room containing a toilet (and often a sink/shower)

Etymology

Refers to a room where women could 'powder' their noses; 'powder' meaning cosmetic face powder.
