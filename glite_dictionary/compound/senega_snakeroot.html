senega snakeroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪɡə ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪɡə ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
An eastern North American plant (Polygala senega) with clusters of small white flowers and roots formerly used in medicine.
North American plant with medicinal roots

Examples

Early settlers used senega snakeroot to treat coughs and respiratory illnesses.
The dried root of senega snakeroot was once a popular ingredient in herbal remedies.
You can identify senega snakeroot by its cluster of white flowers and tall, slender stems.

Etymology

From 'Seneca', a North American indigenous people, who used the plant medicinally, and 'snakeroot' referring to plants believed to treat snakebite.
