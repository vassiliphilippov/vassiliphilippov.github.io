family Marantaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌmærənˈteɪsi.iː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌmærænˈteɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of tropical perennial herbs with usually starchy rhizomes, including various genera such as Maranta.
a family of tropical perennial herbs

Examples

Many species in the family Marantaceae are prized as ornamental houseplants.
Members of the family Marantaceae typically grow in tropical regions and have starchy underground rhizomes.
The family Marantaceae includes plants known for their unique leaf motions in response to light.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Marantaceae', named after Bartolomeo Maranta, Italian botanist (1500–1571).
