Indian lettuce

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Montia with edible, pleasant-tasting leaves, often eaten as a salad green or cooked vegetable
edible plant in the genus Montia

Examples

Native Americans traditionally harvested Indian lettuce as a nutritious wild green in spring.
The leaves of Indian lettuce are mildly flavored and can be added to salads or sandwiches.
Gardeners often grow Indian lettuce for its tender, edible foliage in cool seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miner’s lettuce - another common name for Montia perfoliata, a related edible plant
