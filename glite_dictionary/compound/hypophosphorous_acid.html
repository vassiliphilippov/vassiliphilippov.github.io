hypophosphorous acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊ.fɑsˈfɔːr.əs ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pə.fɒsˈfɒr.əs ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
A clear or yellow monobasic acid (H3PO2) used as a reducing agent, especially in chemical analysis and industry.
a monobasic acid (H3PO2); chemical compound
medical

Examples

The laboratory ordered more hypophosphorous acid for the synthesis of specialized chemicals.
Exposure to hypophosphorous acid should be minimized because it can be hazardous to health.
One use of hypophosphorous acid is in the electroless plating of metals.

Etymology

From hypo- (less) and phosphorous acid, indicating a form with fewer oxygen atoms than phosphorous acid.
