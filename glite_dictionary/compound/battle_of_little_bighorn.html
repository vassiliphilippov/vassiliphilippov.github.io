Battle of Little Bighorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˌlɪt̬l baɪˈɡhɔːrn / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌlɪtl baɪˈɡhɔːn / (BrE)
a battle in 1876 near the Little Bighorn River in Montana, where U.S. cavalry led by General Custer was defeated by Sioux and Cheyenne warriors
famous 1876 battle between U.S. Army and Native Americans

Examples

The Battle of Little Bighorn remains one of the most studied military engagements in American history.
Custer's defeat at the Battle of Little Bighorn shocked the nation at the time.
Books and films have explored the causes and consequences of the Battle of Little Bighorn.
