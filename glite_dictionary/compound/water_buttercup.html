water buttercup

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˈbʌtərˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈbʌtəkʌp / (BrE)
A plant found in ponds and slow streams, with submerged and floating leaves and white flowers, native to Europe and North America.
aquatic plant with white flowers

Examples

In the shallow pond, clusters of water buttercup bloomed among the reeds.
The white flowers of the water buttercup stood out against the green, floating leaves.
Many aquatic insects use the water buttercup as shelter during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - a type of bright yellow wildflower, genus Ranunculus
