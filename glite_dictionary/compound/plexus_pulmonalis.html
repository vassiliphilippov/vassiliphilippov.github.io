plexus pulmonalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs pʌlˈmoʊnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs pʌlˈməʊnəlɪs / (BrE)
An autonomic nerve plexus located in each lung, forming part of the nervous system of the respiratory tract.
autonomic nerve plexus in the lung
medical

Examples

The plexus pulmonalis plays a crucial role in regulating bronchial secretions and airway constriction.
Damage to the plexus pulmonalis can affect lung function and respiratory health.
Both sympathetic and parasympathetic fibers are present in the plexus pulmonalis of the lung.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulmonary plexus - another term for plexus pulmonalis; the network of autonomic nerves in the lung

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network) and 'pulmonalis' (of the lung).
