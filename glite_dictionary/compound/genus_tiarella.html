genus Tiarella

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tiˈæɹələ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tiˈarələ / (BrE)
A small genus of North American perennial herbs with mostly basal leaves and slender racemes of delicate white flowers.
genus of North American herbs with white flowers

Examples

The genus Tiarella includes several species known for their attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Tiarella in the Appalachian Mountains.
Gardeners often choose plants from the genus Tiarella to provide ground cover in shaded areas.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Tiarella', diminutive of Latin 'tiara' (a kind of headdress), referring to the shape of the flower cluster.
