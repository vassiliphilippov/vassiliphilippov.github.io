good word

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd wɝd / (AmE), / ɡʊd wɜːd / (BrE)
a statement of praise or recommendation about someone or something; a commendation
statement of praise or recommendation

Examples

She put in a good word for me when I applied for the job.
If you see the manager, could you say a good word on my behalf?
I always try to have a good word for anyone who works hard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recommendation - an act of endorsing or supporting someone or something
  • Antonym: criticism - expression of disapproval or negative judgment
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɡʊd wɝd / (AmE), / ɡʊd wɜːd / (BrE)
a message, report, or information that brings positive news; good news
message or report of good news

Examples

We finally received the good word that our proposal was accepted.
Has the doctor given you any good word about your recovery?
He brought the good word from the capital that the war was over.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bad news - news that is unpleasant or unfavorable
