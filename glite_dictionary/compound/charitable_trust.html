charitable trust

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃærətəbl ˌtrʌst / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːrɪtəbl ˌtrʌst / (BrE)
A trust established to hold and manage assets for purposes such as charity, religion, education, or science, rather than for private profit.
legal trust set up for charity or public benefit
legal

Examples

The university received a large donation from a charitable trust to fund new scholarships.
Many museums rely on income from their charitable trust to support exhibitions and programs.
A charitable trust must be organized and operated exclusively for public benefit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust - an arrangement where property is held by one party for another's benefit
