cover crop

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvər krɑp / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə krɒp / (BrE)
a crop grown primarily to protect and improve the soil, especially to prevent erosion and add organic matter when plowed under
crop grown to protect and enrich soil

Examples

Farmers often plant cover crops in the off-season to enrich the soil.
Planting a cover crop such as clover can help prevent soil erosion during winter months.
After harvesting corn, the field was seeded with a cover crop to restore nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green manure - plants grown and then plowed under to improve soil fertility
