macedoine of vegetables

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsɪˈdwæn əv ˈvɛdʒtəblz / (AmE), / ˌmæsɪˈdwɑːn əv ˈvɛdʒtəblz / (BrE)
a mixture of small, diced vegetables, often served as a salad or garnish
mixture of diced vegetables

Examples

The chef served a delicious macedoine of vegetables alongside the roasted chicken.
She added a macedoine of vegetables to her picnic basket for a healthy snack.
A classic French menu often includes a starter of macedoine of vegetables in vinaigrette.

Synset Examples

She prepared a colorful macedoine of vegetables for dinner.
I love the crunch of a well-made macedoine salad.

Etymology

From French macédoine, originally referring to a mixture of diverse peoples, later applied to mixed fruit or vegetables.
