bad decision

1. NOUN

/ bæd dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / bad dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
A choice or judgment that is considered poor, unwise, or likely to have negative consequences.
an unwise or poor choice or judgment

Examples

Missing the deadline was a bad decision that affected the entire project.
He realized quitting his job had been a bad decision in hindsight.
Choosing to drive in the storm turned out to be a bad decision.

Synset Examples

It was a bad decision to skip the event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: good decision - a choice or judgment that is considered wise or beneficial
  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong or misguided
