pyroelectric material

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊɪˌlɛktrɪk məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəʊɪˌlɛktrɪk məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
A substance that produces an electric charge or voltage when its temperature changes, especially when heated or cooled.
material generating voltage by temperature change
medical

Examples

Tourmaline is a naturally occurring pyroelectric material used in scientific instruments.
Engineers developed a new pyroelectric material for heat sensors in infrared cameras.
The pyroelectric material generated a small voltage when exposed to rapid temperature changes.

Synset Examples

Although artificial pyroelectric materials have been engineered, the effect was first discovered in minerals such as tourmaline.

Etymology

From 'pyro-' (Greek for fire) and 'electric', referring to electricity produced by heat.
