blue sage

1. NOUN

/ bluː seɪdʒ / (AmE), / bluː seɪdʒ / (BrE)
an aromatic shrub of western North America, with grayish leaves and blue flowers, found in arid regions
aromatic shrub of western North America

Examples

A row of blue sage grew along the edge of the garden, filling the air with a pleasant scent.
The dry hills were dotted with blue sage and cactus.
Gardeners often choose blue sage because it attracts pollinators and is drought-tolerant.
