Parallel Lives

1. PROPN

/ ˌpæɹəˌlɛl ˈlaɪvz / (AmE), / ˌparəˌlɛl ˈlʌɪvz / (BrE)
a collection of biographies of notable Greeks and Romans written by Plutarch, often used as a source in literature and history
work of biographies by Plutarch

Examples

Many of Shakespeare's Roman plays drew inspiration from Parallel Lives by Plutarch.
Historians still consult Parallel Lives for insights into classical figures.
My professor recommended reading Parallel Lives to better understand ancient leadership.

Etymology

From the English words 'parallel' (from Greek 'parallēlos') and 'lives', reflecting the paired biographies format.
