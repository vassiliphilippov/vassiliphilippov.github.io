bell pepper

1. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / bɛl ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a large, bell-shaped sweet pepper that is typically green, red, yellow, orange, or sometimes black, eaten raw or cooked as a vegetable
a sweet, bell-shaped pepper

Examples

She diced a bell pepper and added it to the salad.
The recipe calls for both red and green bell peppers.
A fresh bell pepper can add a sweet crunch to any dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsicum - another term for bell pepper, especially outside North America
  • Antonym: chili pepper - a hot, spicy variety of pepper

2. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / bɛl ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A variety of sweet, mild pepper with thick, bell-shaped fruit, commonly eaten raw or cooked.
sweet, mild, bell-shaped pepper

Examples

She sliced a bell pepper to add color and crunch to the salad.
Red bell peppers are sweeter than green ones and are often used in stir-fries.
Growing your own bell peppers in the garden can be very rewarding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsicum - the genus of plants to which the bell pepper belongs
  • Antonym: chili pepper - a hot-tasting pepper from the same genus as bell pepper
