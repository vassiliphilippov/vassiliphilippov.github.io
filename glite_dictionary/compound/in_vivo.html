in vivo

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈviːvoʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈviːvəʊ / (BrE)
(of a process or experiment) taking place in a living organism.
within a living organism (scientific/medical)
medical

Examples

The researchers conducted the drug test in vivo to observe its effects on living tissue.
Unlike studies performed in vitro, experiments carried out in vivo reveal how substances behave in real biological environments.
Many discoveries in pharmacology have come from comparing results of in vivo and in vitro experiments.

Synset Examples

studies conducted in vivo

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in vitro - outside a living organism, in an artificial environment (e.g., a test tube)

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'within the living'.

medical

Examples

Researchers observed protein production in vivo rather than in a petri dish.
The procedure allows for in vivo imaging of brain activity.
