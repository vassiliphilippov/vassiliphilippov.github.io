triphosphoric acid

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌfɑsˈfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / traɪˌfɒsˈfɒrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a chemical acid formed as a partial anhydride of three phosphoric acid molecules, mainly known through its salts and esters
a polyphosphoric acid compound
medical

Examples

The laboratory synthesized triphosphoric acid to study its properties under high temperatures.
Many industrial cleaners contain salts derived from triphosphoric acid for water softening.
Researchers are investigating the potential uses of triphosphoric acid esters in biochemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphoric acid - a common mineral acid containing phosphorus

Etymology

From tri- (three) and phosphoric acid, indicating three phosphoric acid units.
