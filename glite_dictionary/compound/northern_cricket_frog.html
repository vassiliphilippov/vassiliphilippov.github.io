northern cricket frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɹðɚn ˈkrɪkɪt frɑg / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈkrɪkɪt frɒg / (BrE)
a small species of cricket frog native to the eastern and central United States, known for its jumping ability and distinctive call
a frog species of eastern and central US

Examples

The northern cricket frog is often found near ponds and streams in the Midwest.
Scientists are studying the population decline of the northern cricket frog due to habitat loss.
At dusk, the high-pitched calls of northern cricket frogs can be heard throughout the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket frog - a frog of the genus Acris, known for its cricket-like chirping
