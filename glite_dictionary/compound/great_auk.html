great auk

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ɔk / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɔːk / (BrE)
a large, flightless seabird (Pinguinus impennis) that was native to rocky islands off the northern Atlantic coasts and is now extinct
extinct large flightless seabird

Examples

The great auk was hunted to extinction in the 19th century.
Museum displays of the great auk illustrate the impact of human activities on wildlife.
Sailors once mistook the great auk for a small penguin due to its appearance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: auk - a bird of the family Alcidae, related sea birds of the Northern Hemisphere
