primary subtractive color for light

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri səbˈtræktɪv ˈkʌlɚ fər laɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɪməri səbˈtraktɪv ˈkʌlə fə laɪt / (BrE)
any of the three colors—magenta, cyan, and yellow—that, when subtracted from white light, yield the primary colors for light in color mixing
one of the key colors (magenta, cyan, yellow) used in subtractive color mixing

Examples

In color printing, magenta is a primary subtractive color for light and is essential for creating a wide range of hues.
When studying the science of optics, students learn about the primary subtractive colors for light and how they interact.
Yellow, as a primary subtractive color for light, plays a crucial role in the reproduction of full-color images.

Synset Examples

the primary subtractive colors for light are magenta, cyan, and yellow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: additive primary color - one of the primary colors (red, green, blue) used in additive color mixing

Etymology

Formed from 'primary' (from Latin 'primarius', principal), 'subtractive', and 'color'; a term used in color science and printing.
