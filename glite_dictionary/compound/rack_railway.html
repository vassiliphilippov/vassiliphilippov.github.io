rack railway

1. NOUN

/ ræk ˈreɪlˌwɛɪ / (AmE), / ræk ˈreɪl.weɪ / (BrE)
a railway for steep gradients in which a cogwheel on the locomotive engages with a toothed central rail to provide traction
railway with cogwheel system for steep slopes

Examples

The mountain's summit is accessible by a rack railway that winds up the steep slopes.
Engineers chose to construct a rack railway to overcome the challenging incline.
Tourists marveled at the unique cog mechanism of the rack railway as it climbed higher and higher.

Etymology

From 'rack', referring to the toothed rail, and 'railway'.
