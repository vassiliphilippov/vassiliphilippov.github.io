non-market economy

1. NOUN

/ nɑn ˈmɑrkət ɪˈkɑnəmi / (AmE), / nɒn ˈmɑːkɪt ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
An economy in which government controls resources, production, and prices rather than market forces of supply and demand.
state-controlled economy

Examples

China was considered a non-market economy by many Western countries for decades.
Many argue that a non-market economy allows more government oversight of resource allocation.
The transition from a non-market economy to a market economy can be difficult and complex.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: market economy - an economy where decisions are driven by supply and demand with little government intervention
