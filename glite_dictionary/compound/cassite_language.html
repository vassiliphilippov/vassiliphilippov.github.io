Cassite language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.aɪt ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæs.aɪt ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct ancient language spoken by the Kassite people in Mesopotamia during the second millennium BCE.
ancient language of the Kassites

Examples

Scholars continue to study the Cassite language to better understand Kassite culture and administration.
Only a few inscriptions in the Cassite language have survived from ancient Mesopotamia.
The Cassite language remains only partially deciphered due to limited written records.

Etymology

Cassite from Kassite, the name of the ancient people who spoke the language.
