World Series

1. NOUN

/ wɜrld ˈsɪriz / (AmE), / wɜːld ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
the annual championship series of Major League Baseball in North America, played between the champions of the American League and the National League
Major League Baseball championship series
american

Examples

Every October, millions of fans tune in to watch the World Series games.
Winning the World Series is the dream of every professional baseball player in the United States.
The New York Yankees have won the World Series more times than any other team.

Synset Examples

we watched the World Series on TV

Etymology

First held in 1903; 'World' reflects the early scope and prestige of the event.
