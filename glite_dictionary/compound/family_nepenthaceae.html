family Nepenthaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛˌpɛnˈθeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌnɛˌpɛnˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical pitcher plants, coextensive with the genus Nepenthes, known for their distinctive insect-trapping pitfall traps.
family of tropical pitcher plants (Nepenthes)

Examples

The family Nepenthaceae is renowned for its carnivorous species distributed mainly in Southeast Asia.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Nepenthaceae during their expedition.
All members of the family Nepenthaceae produce unique pitcher-shaped leaves for trapping insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nepenthes - genus of tropical pitcher plants

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Nepenthes (the type genus) and family suffix -aceae.
