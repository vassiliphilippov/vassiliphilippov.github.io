strawberry tomato

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri təˈmeɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstrɒbəri təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a stout, hairy annual plant of eastern North America, bearing sweet yellow fruits; also known as Physalis pruinosa
hairy annual plant with sweet yellow fruit

Examples

The strawberry tomato thrives in well-drained soils and produces fruits in midsummer.
She planted strawberry tomatoes in her garden for their sweet, tangy flavor.
Many gardeners grow strawberry tomato for its unusual appearance and delicious yellow fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - another common name for plants of the genus Physalis, with small edible fruit enclosed in papery husks

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri təˈmeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstrɒbəri təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A North American annual plant with round, edible, fleshy yellow berries enclosed in a papery husk, cultivated for decorative and edible purposes.
annual plant with yellow berries in a husk

Examples

The strawberry tomato is often grown in gardens for its attractive yellow berries.
Children enjoy picking the edible fruit of the strawberry tomato in late summer.
After removing the husk, the strawberry tomato can be eaten raw or used in jams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - plant with small edible berries inside a papery husk
