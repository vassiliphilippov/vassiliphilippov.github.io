Crow Nation

1. PROPN

/ ˈkroʊ ˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ ˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A Native American people of the Siouan language family who traditionally lived in eastern Montana and surrounding areas.
Native American people of Montana

Examples

The Crow Nation has preserved many traditional customs and ceremonies.
Members of the Crow Nation participate in an annual tribal gathering in Montana.
She is proud of her ancestry as a descendant of the Crow Nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - A group of Native American peoples of the Great Plains; closely related linguistic family

Etymology

From the tribe's name for themselves, Apsáalooke, meaning 'children of the large-beaked bird,' interpreted as 'Crow' by early translators.
