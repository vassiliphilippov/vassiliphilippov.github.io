family Pennatulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pɛˌnætjuːˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli pɛnˌætjʊˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine colonial cnidarians known as sea pens, characterized by their feather-like appearance and found in soft-bottomed ocean habitats
a family of sea pen cnidarians

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species in the family Pennatulidae off the coast of Australia.
The colorful members of the family Pennatulidae are often called sea pens because of their shape.
Marine biologists study the diversity of the family Pennatulidae to understand ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea pen - a colonial marine cnidarian resembling a quill pen

Etymology

From New Latin 'Pennatulidae', formed from 'Pennatula' (a genus of sea pens) and '-idae' (a zoological family suffix).
