bee beetle

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈbiː.təl / (AmE), / biː ˈbiː.tl̩ / (BrE)
a European beetle that infests beehives, often damaging honeycombs and disturbing bees
European beetle that infests beehives

Examples

The bee beetle is considered a pest due to its affinity for beehives.
Beekeepers in Europe take measures to protect their hives from the bee beetle.
The presence of a bee beetle can cause significant harm to honey production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetle - an insect of the order Coleoptera with hard forewings
