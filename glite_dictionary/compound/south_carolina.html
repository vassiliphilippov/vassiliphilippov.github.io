South Carolina

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ ˌkærəˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / saʊθ ˌkarəˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
A state in the southeastern United States, originally one of the British colonies that formed the United States; its capital is Columbia.
southeastern U.S. state; former British colony

Examples

The Civil War began when South Carolina seceded from the Union in 1860.
She spent her summer vacation exploring historic cities in South Carolina.
Rice and indigo were important crops in colonial South Carolina.

Etymology

Named after King Charles I of England; 'Carolina' from the Latin form of 'Charles'.

2. PROPN

/ saʊθ kærəˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / saʊθ kærəˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
A state in the southeastern United States, part of the Deep South, and one of the original 13 colonies.
a state in the southeastern United States

Examples

My grandparents retired to South Carolina for the mild winters and beautiful coastline.
The Civil War began when Confederate forces fired on Fort Sumter in South Carolina.
She grew up in South Carolina and still visits her hometown every summer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North Carolina - a neighboring state to the north, also one of the original colonies
  • Synonym: Georgia - a neighboring state to the south and west of South Carolina

Etymology

Named after King Charles I of England; 'Carolina' derives from the Latin 'Carolus' for Charles.
