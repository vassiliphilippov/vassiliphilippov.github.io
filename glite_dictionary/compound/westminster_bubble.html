Westminster bubble

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstmɪnstər ˈbʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɛsmɪnstə ˈbʌbəl / (BrE)
A situation in British politics where politicians, journalists, and insiders become isolated from the general public, often resulting in a narrow or distorted perspective.
isolated political environment in British politics
british

Examples

Many journalists accused the government of becoming trapped in the Westminster bubble and ignoring public opinion.
After months spent inside the Westminster bubble, she struggled to relate to her constituents’ concerns.
Critics argue that political leaders in the Westminster bubble often overlook issues facing ordinary people.

Synset Examples

He criticized her for being in a Westminster bubble.
Living in the Westminster bubble can distort their perspective.
Politicians often get trapped in the Westminster bubble.

Etymology

From 'Westminster', the seat of the UK Parliament, combined with 'bubble' to suggest isolation or detachment.
