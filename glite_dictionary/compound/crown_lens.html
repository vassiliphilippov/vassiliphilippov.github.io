crown lens

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn lɛnz / (AmE), / kraʊn lɛnz / (BrE)
A lens made from optical crown glass, known for its low dispersion and clarity, used in optical instruments.
lens made of crown glass

Examples

The microscope uses a crown lens to achieve clear and accurate images.
Optical designers often pair a crown lens with a flint lens to correct chromatic aberration.
He replaced the damaged element with a high-quality crown lens to improve the camera's performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flint lens - a lens made of optical flint glass

Etymology

So named because the glass 'crown glass' was historically produced in a crown-like disk; 'lens' from Latin 'lens' (lentil).
