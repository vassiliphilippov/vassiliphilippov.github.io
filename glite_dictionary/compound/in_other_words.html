in other words

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈʌðɚ wɝːdz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈʌðə wɜːdz / (BrE)
used to introduce a different way of expressing something, often to clarify or explain it more clearly
used to clarify or restate

Examples

The instructions were unclear; in other words, nobody knew what to do.
You must pay the full amount in advance—in other words, no installments are allowed.
She stopped responding to calls; in other words, she wanted to end the conversation.

Synset Examples

The project is delayed; in other words, we need more time.
She was unhappy; in other words, she was upset.
He didn't pass; in other words, he failed the exam.
