buck-and-wing

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌk ən ˈwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌbʌk ən ˈwɪŋ / (BrE)
a solo tap dance characterized by fast, sharp taps and movements, traditionally performed in minstrel shows and early vaudeville.
a lively solo tap dance style

Examples

The dancer impressed the audience with his energetic buck-and-wing routine.
She learned the buck-and-wing while studying early American dance forms.
A traditional buck-and-wing often features complex footwork and lively rhythms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap dance - a dance characterized by rhythmical tapping of the feet

Etymology

From American English; 'buck' from a slang term for a Black man and 'wing' from the wing-like arm movements. Originates in 19th-century minstrel shows.
