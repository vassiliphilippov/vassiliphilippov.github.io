American germander

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən dʒərˈmændər / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən dʒɜːˈmændə / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to North America, with serrated leaves and cream to purple flowers arranged in spikelike racemes.
North American flowering subshrub

Examples

In the wildflower garden, the American germander bloomed abundantly throughout the summer.
Bees are often attracted to the pale purple flowers of the American germander.
The botanist identified the plant by its serrate leaves as American germander.
