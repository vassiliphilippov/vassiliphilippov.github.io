Battle of Plataea

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv pləˈtiː.ə / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv pləˈtiː.ə / (BrE)
a decisive defeat of the Persian army by the Greek city-states at Plataea in 479 BC, marking the end of the Persian invasion of Greece
decisive Greek victory over Persia in 479 BC

Examples

The Battle of Plataea marked the end of the Persian Wars in ancient Greece.
Historians often cite the Battle of Plataea as a turning point in Western history.
During the Battle of Plataea, Greek forces united to achieve a decisive victory.

Etymology

From the place name Plataea (Greek Plataia), where the battle occurred.
