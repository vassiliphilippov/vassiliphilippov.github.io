prairie smoke

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri smoʊk / (AmE), / ˈpreəri sməʊk / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant (Geum triflorum) with hairy basal pinnate leaves, purple flowers, and plume-tipped fruits.
a North American perennial plant with plume-tipped fruits

Examples

The wildflowers in the meadow included prairie smoke, its feathery plumes swaying in the breeze.
Each spring, prairie smoke adds a touch of purple and silver to the grasslands.
Gardeners value prairie smoke for both its unique flowers and its drought resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geum triflorum - Botanical name for prairie smoke
