press gallery

1. NOUN

/ prɛs ˈɡæləri / (AmE), / prɛs ˈɡaləri / (BrE)
an area, often a balcony, reserved for reporters in a legislative or governmental hall, providing vantage for journalistic observation
area for reporters in legislative halls

Examples

The senator addressed the members of the press gallery after the parliamentary session.
Photographers in the press gallery captured every moment of the debate.
Admission to the press gallery is usually restricted to accredited journalists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallery - a raised area or balcony in a theater, church, or other public building
  • Synonym: press box - an area designated for journalists at sporting or other large public events
