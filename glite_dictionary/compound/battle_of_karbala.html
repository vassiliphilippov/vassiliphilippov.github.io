Battle of Karbala

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv kɑːrˈbɑː.lə / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv kɑːˈbɑː.lə / (BrE)
a battle fought in 680 CE near Karbala (in present-day Iraq), in which Husayn ibn Ali, the grandson of Muhammad, and his followers were killed by the forces of the Umayyad caliphate
historic 680 CE battle near Karbala, Iraq

Examples

Every year, millions of people commemorate the Battle of Karbala during the Islamic month of Muharram.
The historical significance of the Battle of Karbala has shaped the religious practices of Shi'a Muslims worldwide.
Scholars often discuss the aftermath of the Battle of Karbala in the context of Islamic history.

Synset Examples

the Battle of Kerbala is the basis for the Ta'ziyehs that are performed annually

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Badr - a significant early battle in Islamic history
  • Synonym: Battle of Uhud - another early battle involving early Muslims and Meccans
