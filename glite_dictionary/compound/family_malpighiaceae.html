family Malpighiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌmælˌfɪdʒiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌmalpɪdʒɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical shrubs and trees, including flowering plants found mainly in the Americas.
tropical plant family (shrubs or trees)

Examples

Several species within the family Malpighiaceae are known for their medicinal properties.
The family Malpighiaceae contains over 75 genera and is native mostly to tropical regions.
Botanists have discovered new flowering plants in the family Malpighiaceae in South America.

Etymology

Named after Marcello Malpighi (1628–1694), Italian physician and biologist, with the Latin botanical suffix -aceae indicating a plant family.
