sand phlox

1. NOUN

/ sænd flɑks / (AmE), / sænd flɒks / (BrE)
A low, mat-forming herbaceous plant found in rocky areas of the United States, known for its small, colorful flowers.
North American mat-forming wildflower

Examples

The sand phlox brightened the rocky hillside with its delicate pink blossoms.
Gardeners often use sand phlox as ground cover in sunny spots.
We spotted several patches of sand phlox during our hike in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlox - a genus of flowering plants known for clusters of colorful blooms
