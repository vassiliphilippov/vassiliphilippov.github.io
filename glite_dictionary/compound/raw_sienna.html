raw sienna

1. NOUN

/ rɔ siˈɛnə / (AmE), / rɔ siˈɛnə / (BrE)
a natural yellowish-brown earth pigment used in paint, made from untreated sienna clay
yellowish-brown earth pigment

Examples

The artist used raw sienna to create warm tones in his landscape painting.
Many watercolor palettes include a pan of raw sienna for natural-looking shadows.
She mixed raw sienna with other pigments to achieve the desired golden hue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: burnt sienna - reddish-brown pigment made by heating raw sienna

Etymology

Sienna from Italian 'terra di Siena', meaning 'earth of Siena', a region in Italy known for this pigment.

2. NOUN

/ rɔ siˈɛnə / (AmE), / rɔː siˈɛnə / (BrE)
a yellowish-brown to dark tan pigment or color, originally made from natural earth containing iron oxide and manganese oxide
yellowish-brown pigment or color

Examples

The artist chose raw sienna for the warm undertones in her landscape painting.
He mixed raw sienna with white to create a lighter shade for the walls.
The set designer requested fabrics in raw sienna to match the vintage look of the furniture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: burnt sienna - a reddish-brown pigment made by heating raw sienna

Etymology

From Italian 'terra di Siena' (earth of Siena), referring to the earth from the Siena region in Italy used to make the pigment; 'raw' distinguishes it from 'burnt sienna', which is heated.
