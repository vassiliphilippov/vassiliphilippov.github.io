air pocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌpɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌpɒkɪt / (BrE)
a localized area of low atmospheric pressure or descending air that causes an aircraft to lose altitude suddenly
a patch of low-pressure air causing sudden aircraft drop

Examples

The plane dropped unexpectedly when it hit an air pocket over the mountains.
Passengers gripped their seats tightly as the jet encountered a sudden air pocket.
Pilots are trained to handle situations when an air pocket causes brief turbulence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulence - irregular motion of the air affecting aircraft
