disk pack

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk pæk / (AmE), / dɪsk pæk / (BrE)
A removable set of magnetic disks, stacked together and used as a unit in a disk storage device.
removable set of magnetic disks for storage

Examples

The technician carefully loaded the disk pack into the mainframe's storage unit.
Each disk pack can store several megabytes of data, which was significant for its time.
He labeled the disk pack to avoid confusion with others in the computer lab.
