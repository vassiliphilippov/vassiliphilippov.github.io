Perejil Island

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛrəˈhil ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəˈhiːl ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
a small, uninhabited Mediterranean islet near the coast of Morocco, claimed by both Morocco and Spain
small disputed Mediterranean islet near Morocco

Examples

The sovereignty of Perejil Island has been a source of dispute between Morocco and Spain.
In 2002, a brief conflict arose over control of Perejil Island.
Few people have ever set foot on Perejil Island due to its uninhabited and remote nature.

Etymology

Spanish 'Isla de Perejil', meaning 'Parsley Island'.
