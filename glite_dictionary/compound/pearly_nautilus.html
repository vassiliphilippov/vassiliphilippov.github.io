pearly nautilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːli ˈnɔːtɪləs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːli ˈnɔːtɪləs / (BrE)
a cephalopod mollusc native to the Indian and Pacific Oceans, known for its spiral shell with pale pearly partitions
spiraled cephalopod mollusc with pearly shell

Examples

The pearly nautilus is often admired for its beautifully spiraled shell.
Researchers studied the habitat of the pearly nautilus in the Pacific Ocean.
Ancient jewelry sometimes contains pieces of pearly nautilus shell due to its unique luster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautilus - marine mollusc with a spiral shell
