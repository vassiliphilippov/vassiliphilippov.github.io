Hadrian's Wall

1. PROPN

/ ˈheɪdriənz wɔl / (AmE), / ˈheɪdrɪənz wɔːl / (BrE)
a defensive wall built by the Romans under Emperor Hadrian in the 2nd century AD, marking the northern limit of Roman Britain
ancient Roman wall in northern England

Examples

We saw the remains of Hadrian's Wall during our holiday in northern England.
Hadrian's Wall once marked the edge of the Roman Empire in Britain.
Many hikers enjoy walking along the path that follows Hadrian's Wall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Antonine Wall - a later Roman defensive wall in Scotland, north of Hadrian's Wall

Etymology

Named after the Roman emperor Hadrian (Latin Hadrianus) who ordered its construction.
