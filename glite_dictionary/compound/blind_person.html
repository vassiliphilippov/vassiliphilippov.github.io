blind person

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈpɜrsən / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a person who has little or no ability to see; someone with severe visual impairment
a person with severe visual impairment

Examples

The library offers books in braille for every blind person who requests them.
Guide dogs are specially trained to help a blind person navigate safely.
As a blind person, she relies on assistive technology to use her computer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sighted person - a person who can see; opposite of 'blind person'
