accentual system

1. NOUN

/ ækˈsɛn.tʃu.əl ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ækˈsɛn.tjʊ.əl ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the pattern or rules of accentuation used in a particular language, especially regarding where stress falls in words or phrases
system of word accent patterns in a language

Examples

The English accentual system places primary stress on certain syllables within words.
Linguists compare the accentual system of different languages to study stress patterns.
Changes in the accentual system can affect how words are pronounced over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosodic system - the overall system of rhythm and intonation in speech

Etymology

From 'accentual', relating to accent or stress, and 'system', from Greek 'systēma' (organized whole).
