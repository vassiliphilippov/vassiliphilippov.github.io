cow oak

1. NOUN

/ kaʊ oʊk / (AmE), / kaʊ əʊk / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous tree of the eastern United States, valued for its durable wood used in timber, basketry, and chair seats.
deciduous hardwood tree of eastern U.S.

Examples

The cow oak is prized for its strong, flexible wood used in making baskets.
Several mature cow oaks stood at the edge of the forest, providing ample shade.
The craftsman carefully wove chair seats from the split wood of the cow oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp chestnut oak - Another name for cow oak (Quercus michauxii)
