honey bucket

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni ˌbʌkət / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ˌbʌkɪt / (BrE)
a portable toilet, often a bucket or drum, used for sanitation in camps, festivals, or remote areas without plumbing
portable toilet, especially in remote areas

Examples

The camp provided a honey bucket for use during the expedition.
After the concert, the honey buckets were emptied and cleaned by the staff.
Many remote cabins rely on a honey bucket instead of indoor plumbing.

Synset Examples

The camp had a honey bucket for sanitation.
They set up a honey bucket at the festival.
Using a honey bucket can be convenient in remote areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portable toilet - a moveable toilet used in areas without plumbing
  • Synonym: outhouse - a small outdoor building containing a simple toilet

Etymology

From 'honey' plus 'bucket', originally a euphemism for a bucket toilet's contents.
