family Belemnitidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bəˌlɛmˈnɪtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bɛˌlɛmˈnɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of extinct cephalopod mollusks from the Mesozoic era, known for their bullet-shaped internal shells.
extinct family of Mesozoic cephalopods

Examples

The family Belemnitidae thrived during the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods before becoming extinct.
Fossils from the family Belemnitidae are often recognized by their bullet-shaped guards.
Paleontologists study members of the family Belemnitidae to learn more about ancient marine ecosystems.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Belemnitidae, based on the genus Belemnites, from Greek 'belemnon' (dart or javelin), referring to their shape.
