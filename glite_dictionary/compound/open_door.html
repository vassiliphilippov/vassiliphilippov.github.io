open door

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊpən ˈdɔr / (AmE), / ˌəʊpən ˈdɔː / (BrE)
a policy that allows all countries equal opportunities to trade with a particular nation, especially used with reference to China in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
policy allowing equal trading rights

Examples

The diplomat advocated an open door policy to encourage international trade.
The open door in China helped Western nations gain access to its vast markets.
Many historians study the impact of the open door on global economic relations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed door - a policy or situation that restricts trade or excludes foreign influence

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən ˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˌdɔː / (BrE)
A policy or state of allowing unrestricted or easy access; freedom for people to enter or communicate openly.
freedom of access; unrestricted entry

Examples

The new manager adopted an open door policy to encourage feedback from staff.
Our school has an open door to parents who wish to discuss their children's progress.
By keeping an open door, the organization fosters trust and transparency among employees.

Synset Examples

he maintained an open door for all employees

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed door - a situation or policy that restricts access
