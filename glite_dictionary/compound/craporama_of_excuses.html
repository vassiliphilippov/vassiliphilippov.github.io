craporama of excuses

1. NOUN

/ ˌkræpoʊˈrɑmə əv ɪkˈskjusɪz / (AmE), / ˌkrapəˈrɑːmə əv ɪkˈskjuːsɪz / (BrE)
a large and unimpressive collection of poor excuses
many poor excuses
informal slang

Examples

She came up with a craporama of excuses for missing the meeting.
The student's craporama of excuses only made the teacher more suspicious.
After hearing his craporama of excuses, no one believed his story.

Synset Examples

His craporama of excuses didn't convince anyone.

Etymology

Coined from 'crap' (slang for rubbish) combined with the mock-suffix '-orama' (meaning 'display'), likely modeled on words like 'panorama'.
