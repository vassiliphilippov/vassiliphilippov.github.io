double blind

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl blaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl blaɪnd / (BrE)
A test or experiment in which neither the participants nor the experimenters know who is receiving a particular treatment, used to prevent bias.
a bias-reducing experimental procedure

Examples

The clinical trial was conducted as a double blind to ensure unbiased results.
Researchers used a double blind methodology to test the efficacy of the new drug.
A double blind study reduces the influence of both experimenter and participant expectations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single blind - an experiment in which only the subject does not know whether they are receiving the treatment
