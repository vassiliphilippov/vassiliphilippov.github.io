Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪnɪˈsoʊtə ˌmʌltiˈfeɪzɪk pərˌsɒnˈælɪti ɪnˈvɛntɔri / (AmE), / ˌmɪnɪˈsəʊtə ˌmʌltɪˈfeɪzɪk pəˌsɒnˈælɪti ɪnˈvɛntəri / (BrE)
a standardized psychological test consisting of a large set of questions used to assess personality traits and psychological disorders
psychological personality assessment test
medical

Examples

The Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory is frequently used by psychologists to assess mental health.
After completing the Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory, the patient discussed the results with her therapist.
Many clinical studies rely on data from the Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory to diagnose psychological disorders.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Minnesota, where it was developed in the 1940s; multiphasic indicates multiple traits measured.
