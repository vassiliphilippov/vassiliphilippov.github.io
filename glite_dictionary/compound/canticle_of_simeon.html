Canticle of Simeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tɪ.kəl əv ˈsɪm.i.ən / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɪ.kəl əv ˈsɪm.i.ən / (BrE)
a prayer or hymn spoken by Simeon in the biblical book of Luke (2:29-32), often used in Christian liturgy
biblical prayer of Simeon (Luke 2:29-32)

Examples

The choir ended the service by singing the Canticle of Simeon as the congregation listened in silence.
Many churches recite the Canticle of Simeon during evening prayers or at funerals.
The Canticle of Simeon expresses gratitude and peaceful acceptance at the end of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nunc Dimittis - the Latin name for the Canticle of Simeon, beginning with 'Now you dismiss'

Etymology

Canticle from Latin 'canticulum' (little song), Simeon from Hebrew 'Shim'on'.
