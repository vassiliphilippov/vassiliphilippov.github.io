Cape Cod

1. PROPN

/ keɪp kɑd / (AmE), / keɪp kɒd / (BrE)
a peninsula in southeastern Massachusetts, extending into the Atlantic Ocean and popular as a vacation and resort area
Massachusetts peninsula and resort area

Examples

Every summer, thousands of tourists flock to Cape Cod for its beaches and charming towns.
We rented a cottage on Cape Cod for our family vacation this year.
Artists have long been inspired by the light and scenery of Cape Cod.

Etymology

Named after the fish species (cod) found in its waters by early English explorers in the early 17th century.
