tree-frog

1. NOUN

/ tri frɔg / (AmE), / triː frɒg / (BrE)
Any of several arboreal frogs, especially those of the Old World, characterized by adhesive pads or suckers on their toes for climbing.
an arboreal frog with toe pads

Examples

The tree-frog uses its sticky pads to cling to leaves high in the rainforest canopy.
We spotted a bright green tree-frog resting on a bamboo stalk during our hike.
Unlike many aquatic frogs, the tree-frog is adapted to life among the branches.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true frog - any frog belonging to the family Ranidae, typically lacking adhesive pads
  • Synonym: arboreal - living in trees

2. NOUN

/ triː frɑːɡ / (AmE), / triː frɒɡ / (BrE)
A small frog that lives in trees, typically having adhesive pads on its toes for climbing.
arboreal frog with adhesive toe pads

Examples

The bright green tree-frog leapt from branch to branch high above the ground.
At night, the croaking of tree-frogs echoed through the rainforest.
Biologists observed a rare species of tree-frog perched on a tropical leaf.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: frog - a tailless amphibian of the order Anura
