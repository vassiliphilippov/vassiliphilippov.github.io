lateen-rig

1. NOUN

/ ləˈtin rɪɡ / (AmE), / ləˈtiːn rɪɡ / (BrE)
a sail rig in which a triangular sail is set on a long yard mounted at an angle on the mast, sloping down toward the stern
sailing rig with a triangular sail

Examples

The traditional dhow is famous for its lateen-rig, which gives it excellent maneuverability.
Sailors admire the simplicity and efficiency of the lateen-rig on small boats.
The new yacht was fitted with a lateen-rig for better performance in light winds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: square-rig - sail rig where sails are set square to the keel line

Etymology

From French 'latine', perhaps from Italian 'latina' (meaning 'Latin'), referring to the Mediterranean origins of the rig.
