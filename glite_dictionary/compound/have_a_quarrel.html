to have a quarrel

1. VERB

/ hæv ə ˈkwɔrəl / (AmE), / hæv ə ˈkwɒrəl / (BrE)
to argue or fall out with someone, usually in a way that causes bad feelings
argue or fall out with someone

Examples

My parents often have a quarrel about where to spend the holidays.
It is not unusual for siblings to have a quarrel over small things.
They had a quarrel last night, but made up this morning.

Synset Examples

They had a quarrel over who would do the dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - an angry argument, especially between people who know each other well
  • Synonym: to argue - to speak angrily to someone, telling them that you disagree with them
