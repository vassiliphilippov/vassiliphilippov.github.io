turnip greens

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.nɪp ɡrinz / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪp ɡriːnz / (BrE)
the tender, edible leaves of young turnip plants, often cooked as a vegetable
edible leaves of young turnips

Examples

She sautéed the turnip greens with garlic and onions for dinner.
In the South, turnip greens are a classic side dish served with cornbread.
Fresh turnip greens are packed with vitamins and minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collard greens - edible leaves of a type of cabbage, often used similarly to turnip greens
  • Synonym: mustard greens - edible leaves from mustard plants, similar in use to turnip greens
