pure hopium

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊr hoʊˌpiːəm / (AmE), / ˈpjʊə həʊˌpiːəm / (BrE)
a state of completely unrealistic optimism or hope, often in the face of contrary evidence
completely unrealistic optimism or hope
informal slang

Examples

Even when the project was failing, the team was running on pure hopium.
Believing the stock would triple overnight was just pure hopium, not sound investing.
His speech was filled with pure hopium rather than practical solutions.

Synset Examples

His constant optimism felt like pure hopium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopium - unrealistic or irrational hope, especially in finance or politics

Etymology

Blend of 'hope' and 'opium', formed in internet culture to describe hope as an addictive delusion; 'pure' intensifies the sense.
