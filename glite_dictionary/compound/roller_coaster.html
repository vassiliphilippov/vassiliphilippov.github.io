roller coaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlər ˌkoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlə ˌkəʊstə / (BrE)
an elevated railway at an amusement park featuring sharp curves and steep inclines, designed for entertainment
amusement park ride with steep tracks

Examples

The children screamed with excitement as the roller coaster plunged down the steep track.
I've never been on a roller coaster that goes upside down before.
Waiting in line for the new roller coaster took almost an hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ride - an amusement park attraction

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlər ˌkoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlə ˌkəʊstə / (BrE)
a situation or experience marked by sudden or extreme changes, especially of mood, fortune, or condition
a situation with abrupt changes

Examples

The stock market has been a roller coaster ride lately, with prices rising and falling rapidly.
Our relationship felt like an emotional roller coaster with all its sudden highs and lows.
His career took him on a roller coaster of successes and setbacks over the years.

3. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlər ˌkoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlə ˌkəʊstə / (BrE)
a structure at an amusement park consisting of a track with steep inclines and sharp curves, along which cars travel at high speeds for entertainment
amusement park ride with steep drops

Examples

The new roller coaster at the park has loops that make riders scream with excitement.
She closed her eyes tightly as the roller coaster climbed to its highest peak.
Many children dream of riding their first roller coaster at a big amusement park.

Synset Examples

Freaking gosh, that roller coaster was intense!

Etymology

From 'roller', referring to rollers or wheels, and 'coaster', meaning something that coasts or glides; originally describing the rolling cars on the track.
