science by press conference

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəns baɪ prɛs kənˈfɛrəns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪəns baɪ prɛs kənˈfɛrəns / (BrE)
The practice of announcing scientific findings to the public or media before they are published in peer-reviewed journals.
announcing science to media before journals

Examples

Some researchers criticize science by press conference for bypassing traditional peer review.
The announcement of the discovery was an example of science by press conference, rather than formal publication.
He warned against the dangers of relying on science by press conference instead of established scientific methods.

Synset Examples

They prefer science by press conference over traditional publications.
