copper beech

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑpər ˌbiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒpə ˌbiːtʃ / (BrE)
a variety of European beech tree (Fagus sylvatica purpurea) with shiny purple or copper-colored leaves, often grown as an ornamental plant
European beech tree with copper-colored leaves

Examples

The ancient copper beech in the park is a local landmark, famous for its rich, dark foliage.
In spring, the copper beech displays striking coppery-red leaves that turn deeper purple as summer approaches.
They planted a row of copper beeches along the driveway to add vibrant color to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - a deciduous tree with smooth gray bark and edible nuts
