Clovis culture

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊvɪs ˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊvɪs ˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
A prehistoric Paleo-American culture in Central and North America, noted for characteristic fluted stone projectile points.
prehistoric North American culture

Examples

The discovery of a Clovis culture site provided valuable insights into early human migration in North America.
Archaeologists unearthed dozens of fluted spear points linked to the Clovis culture.
Many experts consider the Clovis culture to be the earliest widespread human presence in the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Folsom culture - a later Paleo-American culture with distinct stone points

Etymology

Named after Clovis, New Mexico, where artifacts were first discovered.
