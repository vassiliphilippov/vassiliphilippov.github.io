flux density unit

1. NOUN

/ flʌks ˈdɛnsəti ˌjuːnɪt / (AmE), / flʌks ˈdɛnsəti ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the amount of flux passing through a unit area, especially in physics and engineering
unit measuring flux per unit area

Examples

The tesla is the standard flux density unit in the International System of Units.
When measuring electromagnetic fields, scientists often refer to the flux density unit for precision.
Magnetic sensors are calibrated using an appropriate flux density unit to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tesla - SI unit of magnetic flux density

Etymology

From 'flux' (Latin 'fluxus', flow), 'density', and 'unit'.
