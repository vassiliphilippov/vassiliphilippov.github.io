corroborating evidence

1. NOUN

/ kəˌrɑbəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈɛvədəns / (AmE), / kəˌrɒbəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
evidence that supports or confirms evidence already presented, especially in a legal proceeding.
evidence supporting existing proof (legal)
legal

Examples

The prosecutor presented corroborating evidence to strengthen the witness's statement.
Without corroborating evidence, the defendant could not be convicted beyond a reasonable doubt.
Police searched for corroborating evidence to verify the claims made during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: evidence - facts or information that help to prove something, especially in a court of law
