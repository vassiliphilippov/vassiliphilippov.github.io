congenital defect

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a physical or functional abnormality that is present at birth
abnormality present at birth
medical

Examples

The child was born with a rare congenital defect affecting his heart.
Early diagnosis of a congenital defect can improve the chances of successful treatment.
Doctors discussed the possible causes of her congenital defect with the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth defect - an abnormality present at birth

Etymology

From Latin 'congenitus' (born together) + English 'defect'.
