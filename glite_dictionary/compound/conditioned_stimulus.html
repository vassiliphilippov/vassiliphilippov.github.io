conditioned stimulus

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənd ˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənd ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
A stimulus that, after association with an unconditioned stimulus, triggers a conditioned response in classical conditioning.
stimulus that provokes a learned response

Examples

The bell became a conditioned stimulus after several pairings with food in Pavlov's experiment.
Fear of dogs can develop when a neutral event becomes a conditioned stimulus for anxiety.
A light flash was used as the conditioned stimulus in the laboratory study.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconditioned stimulus - a stimulus that naturally and automatically triggers a response

Etymology

From 'conditioned', the past participle of 'condition', and Latin 'stimulus' (goad, spur).
