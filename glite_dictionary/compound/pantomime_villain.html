pantomime villain

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.təˌmaɪm ˈvɪl.ən / (AmE), / ˈpæn.tə.maɪm ˈvɪl.ən / (BrE)
A character, especially in theater or stories, who is portrayed as clearly and exaggeratedly evil or bad, often eliciting boos from the audience.
exaggeratedly evil character in stories or theater
british

Examples

The actor delighted in playing the pantomime villain, drawing boos from the audience at every appearance.
Every good fairytale has its hero and its pantomime villain to stir up drama.
He embraced his role as the pantomime villain, hamming up every evil laugh and sneer.

Synset Examples

He played the role of the pantomime villain perfectly.
Everyone loves a good pantomime villain in stories.
The crowd booed the pantomime villain off the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villain - character who is evil or wicked in a story

Etymology

From 'pantomime,' a theatrical entertainment, and 'villain,' originally from Old French 'vilain' (villager, bad person).
