dark chocolate

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk ˈtʃɑk.lət / (AmE), / dɑːk ˈtʃɒk.lət / (BrE)
a type of chocolate with a high percentage of cocoa solids, little or no milk, and less sugar than milk chocolate
high-cocoa chocolate with little or no milk

Examples

She bought a bar of dark chocolate to use in her baking recipe.
Many people prefer dark chocolate because of its rich flavor and health benefits.
The dessert was topped with shavings of dark chocolate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: milk chocolate - chocolate made with milk powder or condensed milk
  • Synonym: bittersweet chocolate - dark chocolate with a higher cocoa content and less sugar
