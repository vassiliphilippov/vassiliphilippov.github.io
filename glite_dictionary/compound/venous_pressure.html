venous pressure

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnəs ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by the circulating blood on the walls of the veins
pressure of blood in the veins
medical

Examples

The doctor measured the patient's venous pressure to assess his heart function.
Elevated venous pressure can be a sign of heart failure or other cardiovascular issues.
Central venous pressure monitoring is important in critical care medicine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arterial pressure - the pressure of blood in the arteries

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and Old French 'pressier' (to press).
