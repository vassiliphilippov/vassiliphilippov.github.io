timbale case

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmˌbeɪl keɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɪmˌbɑːl keɪs / (BrE)
a small, crisp pastry shell used to hold creamy mixtures of minced foods, often as an appetizer or hors d'oeuvre
small pastry shell for fillings

Examples

Each timbale case was filled with a rich mushroom and chicken mixture.
The chef prepared the appetizer by baking the timbale cases until golden brown.
Serve the seafood salad in a delicate timbale case for a touch of elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timbale - dish of minced food baked in a pastry shell or mold

Etymology

timbale from French, originally meaning 'kettledrum,' referring to the shape; case from Old French 'cas' (box, container).
