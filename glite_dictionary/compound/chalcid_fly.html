chalcid fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsɪd flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkælsɪd flaɪ / (BrE)
a tiny wasp-like insect whose larvae are parasitic on the eggs and larvae of other insects, many of which are considered beneficial for controlling pest species
tiny parasitic insect; beneficial against pests

Examples

A farmer introduced chalcid flies to help control the population of destructive crop pests.
The larvae of the chalcid fly develop inside the eggs of other insects.
Entomologists often study chalcid flies for their role in natural pest management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitoid wasp - a wasp whose larvae live as parasites that kill their hosts

Etymology

From Greek khalkos (copper), referring to the metallic coloring of some species.
