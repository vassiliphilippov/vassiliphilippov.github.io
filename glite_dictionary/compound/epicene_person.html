epicene person

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈsin ˈpɜrsn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsiːn ˈpɜːsn / (BrE)
a person who has both male and female sexual characteristics and organs, making clear assignment of male or female at birth impossible
person with both male and female sexual characteristics
medical

Examples

The doctors determined that the newborn was an epicene person, requiring further genetic testing.
An epicene person may face complex social and medical challenges throughout life.
The study examined the experiences of epicene persons in different cultures.

Etymology

From Greek epikoinos (common to both), used in English for ambiguous gender characteristics.
