Chechen language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛtʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛtʃən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language of the Chechen people, belonging to the Northeast Caucasian language family and spoken mainly in Chechnya and neighboring regions
language spoken by the Chechen people

Examples

Many students in Grozny study the Chechen language alongside Russian.
Linguists consider the Chechen language one of the largest Northeast Caucasian languages.
He wrote his thesis on the grammar of the Chechen language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chechen - a native or inhabitant of Chechnya or the related language
