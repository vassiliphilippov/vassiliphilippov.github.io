myopic view

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑːpɪk vjuː / (AmE), / maɪˈɒpɪk vjuː / (BrE)
a perspective that is narrow or lacking in foresight; a way of seeing things that fails to consider the broader context or future consequences
narrow or short-sighted perspective

Examples

He criticized the committee for taking a myopic view of the issue and ignoring long-term effects.
If we adopt a myopic view, we may miss valuable opportunities for growth.
Her myopic view of the market led to poor investment decisions.

Synset Examples

Being myopic in business can lead to failure.
His myopic view on life limits his opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrow view - an understanding limited in scope; lacking breadth.
  • Antonym: broad view - a perspective that is comprehensive and takes many factors into account.

Etymology

From 'myopic', meaning short-sighted, which derives from Greek 'muōps' (closing the eyes).
