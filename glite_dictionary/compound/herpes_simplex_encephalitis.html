herpes simplex encephalitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːpiːz ˈsɪmˌplɛks ɪnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz ˈsɪmplɛks ɛnˌkɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A form of acute encephalitis caused by infection with herpes simplex virus type 1, typically affecting the temporal and frontal lobes of the brain.
viral brain inflammation caused by herpes simplex
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with herpes simplex encephalitis after presenting with seizures and confusion.
Early treatment of herpes simplex encephalitis with antiviral medication can greatly improve outcomes.
MRI scans showed inflammation in the temporal lobes, confirming the suspicion of herpes simplex encephalitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encephalitis - inflammation of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'herpes' (a spreading eruption) and Greek 'enképhalos' (brain) + '-itis' (inflammation).
