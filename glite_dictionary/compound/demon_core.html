demon core

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.mən kɔr / (AmE), / ˈdiː.mən kɔː / (BrE)
a subcritical mass of plutonium used in early nuclear tests by the United States, known for causing fatal criticality accidents in the 1940s
plutonium core used in nuclear tests, 1940s

Examples

The demon core was involved in two fatal accidents at Los Alamos Laboratory.
Many safety protocols were developed after incidents with the demon core.
Physicists studied the demon core extensively to understand nuclear chain reactions.

Synset Examples

Each mishap with the demon core taught valuable lessons.

Etymology

From 'demon' + 'core'; so called due to its association with deadly criticality accidents.
