mother-of-pearl cloud

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌðər əv ˈpɜrl klaʊd / (AmE), / ˌmʌðə ɒv ˈpɜːl klaʊd / (BrE)
A high-altitude, iridescent cloud that appears luminous and is visible when the sun is just below the horizon.
luminous high-altitude iridescent cloud

Examples

A rare mother-of-pearl cloud lit up the northern skies with shimmering colors just after sunset.
Scientists say mother-of-pearl clouds often form in extremely cold stratospheric conditions.
She captured a photograph of a mother-of-pearl cloud showing brilliant shades of pink and green above the horizon.

Etymology

The name comes from the resemblance of the cloud's colors to mother-of-pearl (nacre).
