worm gear

1. NOUN

/ wɜrm ɡɪr / (AmE), / wɜːm ɡɪə / (BrE)
a gear system consisting of a shaft with a screw thread (the worm) that meshes with a toothed wheel (the worm wheel), used to change the direction of rotary motion's axis
gear with a screw-threaded shaft meshing with a wheel

Examples

The worm gear allows precise control of the mechanism's movement.
By using a worm gear, the engineer could change the axis of rotation.
Many heavy-duty lifts employ a worm gear to increase torque and reduce speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm - the threaded shaft in a worm gear system
  • Synonym: worm wheel - the toothed wheel in a worm gear system
