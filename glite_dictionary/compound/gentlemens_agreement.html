gentlemen's agreement

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn.tl̩.mənz əˈɡri.mənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.tl.mənz əˈɡriː.mənt / (BrE)
An informal agreement based on trust and honor between parties and not enforceable by law.
informal, honor-based agreement

Examples

There was only a gentlemen's agreement between the partners, so nothing was signed.
The two companies relied on a gentlemen's agreement rather than a formal contract.
They broke the gentlemen's agreement, causing distrust among the board members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contract - a legally binding agreement

Etymology

From 'gentleman' (a man of good social position) + 'agreement'; refers to traditional notions of trust among gentlemen.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛntəlmɪnz əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntlmɪnz əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
An informal and unwritten agreement or understanding, based on trust and honor rather than legal obligation.
informal agreement based on trust

Examples

The two companies entered into a gentlemen's agreement to avoid direct competition in the same regions.
Without any written contract, they relied on a gentlemen's agreement to settle the terms of the deal.
Although not legally binding, the gentlemen's agreement was honored by all involved parties.

Synset Examples

The gentlemen's agreement was made in good faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties

Etymology

From 'gentlemen' (suggesting the honor of those involved) + 'agreement'. Popularized in late 19th century English.
