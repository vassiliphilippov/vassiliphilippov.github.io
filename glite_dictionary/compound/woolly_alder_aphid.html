woolly alder aphid

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊli ˈɔldɚ ˈeɪfɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈɔːldə ˈeɪfɪd / (BrE)
a species of aphid with a waxy, wool-like covering that feeds on alder trees
an aphid that infests alder trees

Examples

The woolly alder aphid often covers the branches of alder trees with a white, cottony mass.
Gardeners noticed an increase in woolly alder aphid populations last summer.
A colony of woolly alder aphid can severely weaken young trees if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small sap-sucking insect
