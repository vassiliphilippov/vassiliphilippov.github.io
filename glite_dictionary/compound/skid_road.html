skid road

1. NOUN

/ skɪd roʊd / (AmE), / skɪd rəʊd / (BrE)
a rough road made by placing logs side by side, used for dragging freshly cut timber in logging operations
road of logs for hauling timber

Examples

The workers used a skid road to haul the giant tree trunks out of the forest.
Early logging operations often relied on a skid road for moving timber.
Without a skid road, transporting the felled logs would be much more difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corduroy road - a road made of logs laid side by side, especially over swampy ground

Etymology

From 'skid' (log used to slide objects) and 'road'; used in North American logging.

2. NOUN

/ skɪd roʊd / (AmE), / skɪd rəʊd / (BrE)
a district in a town frequented by loggers, often associated with rough and disreputable establishments
district frequented by loggers

Examples

He spent his early years working in the bars along skid road.
The town's skid road was bustling with activity during the logging season.
Rumors about the dangers of skid road kept many locals away after dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skid row - a run-down urban area frequented by vagrants and the poor

Etymology

Originally referred to roads made for skidding logs in logging towns of the US and Canada; later came to mean the surrounding district.
