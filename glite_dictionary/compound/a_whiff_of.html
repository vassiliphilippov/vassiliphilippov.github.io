a whiff of

1. NOUN

/ ə wɪf ʌv / (AmE), / ə wɪf ɒv / (BrE)
a brief or slight smell of something, especially when noticed in passing
a brief smell of something

Examples

As I walked by the bakery, I caught a whiff of fresh bread.
The air carried a whiff of perfume as she passed.
He opened the container and immediately got a whiff of sour milk.

Synset Examples

He got a whiff of something burning in the kitchen.

Related Terms
