climbing hydrangea

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ haɪˈdreɪndʒiə / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ hʌɪˈdrændʒɪə / (BrE)
a deciduous climbing plant with aerial roots and large, flat clusters of white flowers, often grown on walls or fences
deciduous climbing plant with aerial roots and flat flower clusters

Examples

The climbing hydrangea covered the old stone wall with fragrant white blossoms.
Gardeners appreciate the climbing hydrangea for its ability to thrive in shady areas.
She trained the climbing hydrangea to grow up the side of her garden shed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ haɪˈdreɪndʒiə / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ hʌɪˈdrændʒɪə / (BrE)
A climbing shrub with aerial roots, opposite leaves, and clusters of small white flowers, native to East Asia and commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
ornamental climbing shrub with white flowers

Examples

The climbing hydrangea added a stunning display of white blossoms to the stone wall.
Gardeners appreciate the climbing hydrangea for its ability to cover fences and trellises.
Although slow to establish, a mature climbing hydrangea can transform any shaded garden corner.
