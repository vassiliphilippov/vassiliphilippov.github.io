Greenland caribou

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnlænd ˈkærɪbuː / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlənd ˈkærɪbuː / (BrE)
A large Arctic deer found in Greenland, with both sexes bearing large antlers; closely related to the reindeer of Eurasia and known as caribou in North America.
Arctic deer native to Greenland

Examples

The Greenland caribou has adapted to survive the extreme Arctic climate.
Researchers are tracking the migration patterns of the Greenland caribou across the tundra.
Local communities depend on the Greenland caribou for traditional hunting and cultural practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caribou - North American term for reindeer
  • Synonym: reindeer - Eurasian term for the same species
