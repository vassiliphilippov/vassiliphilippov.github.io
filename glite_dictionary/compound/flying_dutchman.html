Flying Dutchman

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈdʌtʃmən / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈdʌtʃmən / (BrE)
a legendary ghost captain doomed to sail the seas forever as punishment, often associated with a phantom ship sighted at sea
legendary ghost captain of a phantom ship

Examples

Many sailors told stories of seeing the Flying Dutchman during their long ocean voyages.
Legends say the Flying Dutchman can never make port and is cursed to roam the seas for eternity.
Writers have drawn inspiration from the myth of the Flying Dutchman in books and operas.

Etymology

From Dutch folklore; 'Dutchman' referring to a man from the Netherlands and 'flying' depicting the ship's restless wandering.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈdʌtʃmən / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈdʌtʃmən / (BrE)
a legendary ghost ship said to appear during storms near the Cape of Good Hope, believed to be an omen of bad luck
legendary ghost ship

Examples

Many sailors claim to have seen the Flying Dutchman when thick fog rolls in at sea.
The legend of the Flying Dutchman has inspired countless novels and operas.
Superstitious mariners believe that sighting the Flying Dutchman foretells disaster.

Etymology

From the legend of a Dutch captain who was doomed to sail the seas forever; 'Dutchman' meaning a man from the Netherlands.
