maiden voyage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdn ˈvɔɪɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdn ˈvɔɪɪdʒ / (BrE)
the first journey made by a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle after it has been built or launched
first journey of a new vehicle

Examples

The cruise ship's maiden voyage attracted passengers from around the world eager to experience it first.
Many superstitions surround the maiden voyage of a new vessel.
The Titanic tragically sank during its maiden voyage in 1912.

Synset Examples

in 1912 the ocean liner Titanic sank on its maiden voyage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaugural - marking the beginning of an institution, activity, or period of office
  • Antonym: final voyage - the last journey made by a ship or vehicle

Etymology

From 'maiden' meaning 'first' or 'new', and 'voyage' from Old French 'voiage', from Latin 'viaticum' (journey, provisions for a journey).
