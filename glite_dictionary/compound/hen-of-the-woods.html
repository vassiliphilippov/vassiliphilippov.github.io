hen-of-the-woods

1. NOUN

/ hɛn əv ðə wʊdz / (AmE), / hɛn əv ðə wʊdz / (BrE)
a large, greyish-brown edible fungus with overlapping caps resembling a hen, found at the base of trees
edible fungus with overlapping caps

Examples

We found a massive hen-of-the-woods growing at the base of an old oak tree.
The chef sautéed slices of hen-of-the-woods for the dinner special.
Foragers prize the hen-of-the-woods for its flavor and meaty texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maitake - another name for hen-of-the-woods, often used in Japanese cuisine

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a fluffed-up hen resting in the woods.
