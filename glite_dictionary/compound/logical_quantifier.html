logical quantifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈkwɑntɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈkwɒntɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a word or symbol in logic or mathematics, such as 'some', 'all', or 'no', that specifies the quantity of elements to which a proposition applies
word or symbol indicating quantity in logic

Examples

The universal logical quantifier is often denoted by the symbol ∀ in mathematics.
In formal proofs, correct use of a logical quantifier is essential for clarity and rigor.
'Some' and 'all' are examples of words that function as a logical quantifier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantifier - word or phrase that indicates quantity
