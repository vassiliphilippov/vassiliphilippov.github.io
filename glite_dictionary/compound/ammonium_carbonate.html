ammonium carbonate

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniəm ˈkɑːrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈməʊniəm ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound (NH4)2CO3 used in smelling salts, baking powder, and in the manufacture of ammonium compounds.
a white crystalline chemical compound

Examples

Many bakers once used ammonium carbonate as a leavening agent in cookies and crackers.
The strong smell of ammonium carbonate can revive someone who has fainted.
Chemists often handle ammonium carbonate with care due to its tendency to release ammonia gas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelling salts - a mixture of ammonium carbonate and perfume used as a stimulant
  • Degree Lower: ammonium bicarbonate - a related compound used in baking and smelling salts

Etymology

Formed from 'ammonium' (from ammonia) and 'carbonate' (from Latin 'carbonas' meaning charcoal, coal).
