Union soldier

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːniən ˈsoʊldʒər / (AmE), / ˈjuːniən ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
a member of the Union Army during the American Civil War (1861–1865)
Northern Civil War soldier (U.S.)

Examples

The Union soldier stood guard at the entrance to the camp throughout the night.
Many Union soldiers wrote letters home describing the hardships they faced.
After the battle, the Union soldier tended to his wounded comrades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Confederate soldier - a member of the Confederate Army during the American Civil War
