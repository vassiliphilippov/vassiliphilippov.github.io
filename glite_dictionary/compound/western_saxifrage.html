western saxifrage

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈsæksɪˌfrædʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈsæksɪfrɪdʒ / (BrE)
A saxifrage plant with loose clusters of white flowers on hairy stems, growing from a basal rosette of leaves, native to moist slopes of western North America.
a plant with white flowers native to western North America

Examples

The western saxifrage blooms in early spring on moist mountain slopes.
We spotted several western saxifrage plants during our hike in the Rockies.
Gardeners in the Pacific Northwest sometimes cultivate western saxifrage for its delicate flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxifrage - any plant of the genus Saxifraga, known for growing in rocky places

Etymology

From 'western' (directional adjective) + 'saxifrage', from Latin saxifraga, meaning 'stone-breaker'.
