rising tide

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪzɪŋ taɪd / (AmE), / ˈraɪzɪŋ taɪd / (BrE)
the period during which the sea level is increasing as water flows in, between low tide and the following high tide
period of incoming tide

Examples

The boats were able to enter the harbor with the rising tide.
Fishermen set out early to take advantage of the rising tide.
The rocky shore disappeared beneath the rising tide as the afternoon progressed.

Synset Examples

a tide in the affairs of men which, taken at the flood, leads on to fortune

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ebb tide - the period when the sea level falls between high tide and the following low tide
