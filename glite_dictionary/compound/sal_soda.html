sal soda

1. NOUN

/ sæl ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / sæl ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
sodium carbonate, a white, water-soluble salt used in making soap, glass, and paper
sodium carbonate; a chemical compound

Examples

Add a spoonful of sal soda to soften the laundry water.
Many glass manufacturers rely on sal soda as a key ingredient.
In the past, sal soda was commonly used in paper production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washing soda - another name for sodium carbonate, used in cleaning
  • Degree Lower: baking soda - sodium bicarbonate, a related chemical compound for baking and cleaning

Etymology

From Latin 'sal' (salt) and English 'soda', the latter from Italian 'soda' (soda ash).
