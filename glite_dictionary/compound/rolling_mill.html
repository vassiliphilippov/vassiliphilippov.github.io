rolling mill

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.lɪŋ mɪl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.lɪŋ mɪl / (BrE)
A factory or machine for rolling metal into sheets, bars, or other shapes by passing it through pairs of heavy rollers.
machine for rolling metal sheets or bars

Examples

The new rolling mill increased the plant’s production of steel sheets significantly.
He works at a rolling mill where they shape hot metal into long bars.
Modern rolling mills use computer controls to ensure precise thickness and quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steel mill - factory for producing steel
  • Synonym: foundry - workshop or factory for casting metal

Etymology

From 'roll' (to turn over and over) + 'mill' (factory for processing materials); used since the 18th century in metalworking.
