fair-trade agreement

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr treɪd əˌɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˈfeə treɪd əˌɡriːmənt / (BrE)
an agreement between the manufacturer of a trademarked product and its retail distributors to sell the product at or above a set minimum price, often illegal under U.S. law
agreement on minimum retail prices
legal

Examples

During the 20th century, fair-trade agreements were controversial and often challenged in court.
A fair-trade agreement prohibited stores from selling the product at a discount below the manufacturer’s set price.
Legal reforms eventually outlawed most forms of the fair-trade agreement in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: price-fixing - an agreement among competitors to raise, fix, or otherwise maintain the price at which their goods or services are sold
