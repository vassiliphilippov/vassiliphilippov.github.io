purple amaranth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈæm.əˌrænθ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈæm.ərænθ / (BrE)
A tall, showy annual plant native to tropical America, with hairy stems, long spikes of usually red flowers, deep purple-flushed leaves, and seeds sometimes used as a cereal.
tropical annual plant with purple leaves and edible seeds

Examples

The purple amaranth stands out in gardens due to its vibrant leaves and tall, striking flowers.
Many cultures use purple amaranth seeds as a nutritious grain in traditional dishes.
She grew purple amaranth in her backyard for both its ornamental beauty and edible seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - any plant of the genus Amaranthus, often with brightly colored flowers and edible seeds
