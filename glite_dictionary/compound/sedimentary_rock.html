sedimentary rock

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛdəˈmɛntəri rɑk / (AmE), / ˌsɛdɪˈmentəri rɒk / (BrE)
Rock formed by the accumulation and consolidation of mineral and organic particles, typically deposited by water, wind, or ice.
rock formed from deposited sediments

Examples

The canyon walls display distinct layers of sedimentary rock formed over millions of years.
Fossils are commonly found embedded in sedimentary rock.
Limestone is a type of sedimentary rock created from accumulated shell and coral fragments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: igneous rock - rock formed by the solidification of molten magma
  • Antonym: metamorphic rock - rock formed by the alteration of existing rocks due to heat and pressure

Etymology

From Latin sedimentum (settling, sediment) and rock.
