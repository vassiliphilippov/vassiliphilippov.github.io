classical scholar

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl ˈskɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl ˈskɒlə / (BrE)
a person who studies ancient Greek and Latin language, literature, and culture
scholar of ancient Greek and Latin

Examples

She is a renowned classical scholar at the university, specializing in ancient Greek tragedies.
The classical scholar published a new translation of Cicero's speeches.
As a classical scholar, he spent years decoding Latin manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicist - a specialist in the study of ancient Greek and Latin classics

Etymology

From 'classical' (relating to ancient Greek and Roman culture) and 'scholar' (from Old French escoler, from Latin scholarius).
