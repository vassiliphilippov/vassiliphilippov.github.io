sheep polypore

1. NOUN

/ ʃip ˈpɑliˌpɔr / (AmE), / ʃiːp ˈpɒliˌpɔː / (BrE)
an edible fungus with a whitish, often circular cap, white pore surface, small pores, and a central stalk, found under conifers
an edible whitish fungus found under conifers

Examples

The sheep polypore is sometimes collected by mushroom enthusiasts despite its bland taste.
We discovered a cluster of sheep polypore growing beneath the pine trees during our hike.
Although sheep polypore is edible, many foragers prefer mushrooms with more flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polypore - a group of bracket fungi with pores on the underside of the fruiting body

Etymology

Named for its pale, wool-like appearance, resembling sheep, and for being a type of polypore fungus.
