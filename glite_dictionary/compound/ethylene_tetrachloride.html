ethylene tetrachloride

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθiˌlin ˌtɛtrəˈklɔraɪd / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪliːn ˌtɛtrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a chlorinated hydrocarbon compound formerly used as an anthelmintic agent against hookworm and other nematodes
chlorinated compound, former anthelmintic
medical

Examples

Ethylene tetrachloride was once administered to treat human hookworm infections before safer alternatives became available.
Workers were exposed to ethylene tetrachloride fumes during the cleaning process at the factory.
Research on ethylene tetrachloride toxicity led to stricter regulations on its use in medicine and industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon tetrachloride - a chlorinated hydrocarbon solvent, once used in cleaning and fire extinguishers

Etymology

Ethylene from Greek 'aithēr' (upper air) and tetrachloride from 'tetra-' (four) + 'chloride' (chlorine compound).
