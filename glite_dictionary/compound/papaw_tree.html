papaw tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæˌpɔː ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈpɑːˌpɔː ˌtriː / (BrE)
A small tree native to the eastern United States, noted for its oblong leaves and large, edible, fleshy fruit.
small North American tree with edible fruit

Examples

The ripe fruit of the papaw tree is often eaten raw or baked into desserts.
She planted a papaw tree in the backyard for its unique, creamy fruit.
Many wildlife species feed on the fruit produced by the papaw tree in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - Another spelling for the fruit and tree (Asimina triloba) of eastern North America
  • Antonym: papaya - Tropical tree and fruit (Carica papaya), often called pawpaw in some regions

Etymology

A variant of 'pawpaw', possibly from Spanish 'papaya', referring specifically to the North American tree Asimina triloba.
