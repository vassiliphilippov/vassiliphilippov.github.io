genus Brachycome

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbræk.ɪˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbræk.ɪˌkəʊm / (BrE)
A genus of mostly Australian herbs in the family Asteraceae, characterized by basal or alternate leaves and loosely corymbose flower heads.
genus of Australian flowering herbs

Examples

Several species within the genus Brachycome are popular as ornamental garden plants.
Botanists recently discovered a new member of the genus Brachycome in Western Australia.
The genus Brachycome is notable for its daisy-like flower heads and adaptability to dry climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brachycome - a genus of herbs in the daisy family, native to Australia and nearby regions

Etymology

From Ancient Greek brakhýs (short) and kóme (hair), referring to the short pappus hairs on the seeds.
