aquatic mammal

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwɑːtɪk ˈmæməl / (AmE), / əˈkwætɪk ˈmaməl / (BrE)
a mammal that lives in water, such as whales, dolphins, seals, or manatees
mammal that lives in water

Examples

Whales are one of the largest aquatic mammals found in the oceans.
Researchers studied various aquatic mammals to learn about their adaptation to underwater environments.
Seals, as aquatic mammals, are able to remain submerged for long periods while hunting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine mammal - a mammal that lives in or near the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'aquaticus' (relating to water) and 'mammal' from Latin 'mamma' (breast).
