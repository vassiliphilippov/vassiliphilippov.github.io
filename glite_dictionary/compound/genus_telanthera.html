genus Telanthera

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs tɛˈlænθərə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs tɛˈlanθərə / (BrE)
a former botanical genus now included in the genus Alternanthera; used in historical classifications for certain flowering plants
former genus of flowering plants

Examples

Many species once classified under genus Telanthera are now placed in the genus Alternanthera.
The name genus Telanthera is often encountered in older botanical texts.
As taxonomic understanding changed, genus Telanthera became obsolete and is no longer in current use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alternanthera - genus of flowering plants in the amaranth family, currently including former Telanthera species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'genus' (Latin: kind, category) and 'Telanthera', a former taxonomic name of Greek origin.
