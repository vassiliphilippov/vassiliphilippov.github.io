chief operating officer

1. NOUN

/ tʃif ˌɑpəˈreɪtɪŋ ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / tʃiːf ˌɒpəˈreɪtɪŋ ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
the executive responsible for managing the day-to-day operations of a company, typically reporting directly to the chief executive officer or the board of directors.
senior executive overseeing company operations

Examples

After ten years with the company, she was promoted to chief operating officer.
The chief operating officer outlined the new strategy at the annual staff meeting.
As chief operating officer, he oversees both manufacturing and customer service divisions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: CEO - the highest-ranking executive in a company
  • Degree Lower: operations manager - manager overseeing specific operational processes
