variorum edition

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriˈɔrəm əˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛəriˈɔːrəm ɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
an edition of a written work that includes notes, variants, or comments by different editors or scholars
edition with notes or variants by various scholars

Examples

The latest variorum edition of Shakespeare's plays highlights differences among early manuscripts.
Scholars rely on the variorum edition to study how critical interpretations have evolved.
A variorum edition often includes extensive footnotes and commentary from multiple editors.

Etymology

From Latin 'variorum' (of various persons); first used in reference to editions of classical texts collated by several scholars.
