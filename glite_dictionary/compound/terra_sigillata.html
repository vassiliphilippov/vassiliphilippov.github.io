terra sigillata

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˌsɪdʒɪˈlɑtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə sɪˌdʒɪˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A type of fine, glossy red earthenware pottery made from clay, especially that from the Aegean island of Lemnos, widely produced in the Roman Empire.
fine red Roman earthenware pottery

Examples

The museum's collection features several fine examples of terra sigillata from the first century.
Archaeologists uncovered fragments of terra sigillata at the Roman villa site.
Collectors prize terra sigillata for its distinctive red gloss and historical value.

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'sealed earth', from 'terra' (earth) and 'sigillata' (stamped or sealed).
