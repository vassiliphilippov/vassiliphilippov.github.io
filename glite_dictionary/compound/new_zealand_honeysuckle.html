New Zealand honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈziːlənd ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / njuː ˈziːlənd ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (BrE)
a slender, elegant tree native to New Zealand, bearing racemes of red flowers and producing valuable mottled red timber
New Zealand tree with red flowers and timber

Examples

The New Zealand honeysuckle is valued for its striking red blooms and attractive wood.
Gardeners plant the New Zealand honeysuckle for its ornamental qualities and adaptability.
Furniture makers prize New Zealand honeysuckle timber for its unique color and grain.
