blanc fixe

1. NOUN

/ blɑŋk ˈfiks / (AmE), / blɒ̃ fiːks / (BrE)
A white, insoluble, radiopaque powder (barium sulfate) used as a pigment, especially in paints and paper.
white insoluble pigment (barium sulfate)

Examples

The artist added blanc fixe to her oil paints to achieve a brighter white.
Manufacturers often use blanc fixe as a filler in glossy paper products.
Modern laboratory procedures require pure blanc fixe for certain chemical analyses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium sulfate - white insoluble powder used in pigments and medical imaging

Etymology

From French 'blanc fixe', literally 'permanent white'.
