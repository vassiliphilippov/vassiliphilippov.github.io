International System

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntəˈnæʃənl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃnəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the internationally agreed system of metric units of measurement, known as the SI, used in science and industry
system of metric units (SI)

Examples

Most scientific journals require measurements to be reported using the International System of units.
The International System ensures consistency in measurements across countries and disciplines.
Students need to learn the basic units of the International System for physics and chemistry.

Synset Examples

Today the United States is the only country in the world not totally committed to the Systeme International d'Unites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SI - abbreviation for Système International d'Unités, the International System of Units
  • Antonym: imperial system - traditional British system of weights and measures

Etymology

From International (between nations) and System; specifically referring to the French 'Système International d'Unités'.
