water hammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌhæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌhamə / (BrE)
a banging or knocking noise in pipes caused by a sudden change in the flow of water or steam, often resulting from quickly closed valves
banging noise in pipes caused by fluid flow changes

Examples

The plumber explained that the loud water hammer was due to a faulty valve shutting off too quickly.
Installing air chambers can help prevent water hammer in your home's plumbing system.
We heard a sudden water hammer every time the washing machine finished filling.
