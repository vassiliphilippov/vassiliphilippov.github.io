lights-out

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪts.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪts.aʊt / (BrE)
a scheduled time when lights are to be turned off, signaling bedtime, especially in military, institutional, or group living environments
scheduled bedtime in institutions

Examples

The soldiers were expected to be in their bunks by lights-out every night.
After lights-out, the dormitory was completely silent.
The camp counselors announced lights-out at 10 p.m. sharp.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtsˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtsˌaʊt / (BrE)
an unexpected loss of electric light or power, often causing darkness in a place
an unexpected power outage

Examples

There was a sudden lights-out at the office just as the meeting started.
The children were frightened by the unexpected lights-out during the thunderstorm.
After the lights-out, emergency lamps flickered on in the hallway.

Synset Examples

The sudden lights-out during the movie startled everyone.
It was a lights-out situation when the storm hit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackout - a period when lights or power go out
