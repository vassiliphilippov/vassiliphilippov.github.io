thermal equilibrium

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜr.məl iˌkwɪl.ɪˈbri.əm / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.məl iˌkwɪl.ɪˈbriː.əm / (BrE)
A state in which all parts of a system have the same temperature and no heat flows between them.
state where temperatures are equal throughout a system

Examples

After several minutes, the coffee and the room reached thermal equilibrium.
The principle of thermal equilibrium is fundamental to thermodynamics.
Once in thermal equilibrium, no further heat exchange occurs between the objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equilibrium - a state of balance or stability

Etymology

From Greek thermē (heat) and Latin aequilibrium (level balance).
