penicillin F

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn ɛf / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn ɛf / (BrE)
a naturally occurring form of penicillin, the first isolated in Great Britain, used as an antibiotic
a type of penicillin antibiotic
medical

Examples

Researchers in the 1940s studied penicillin F after its isolation from Penicillium notatum.
Unlike penicillin F, other penicillins have since become more commonly used in medicine.
The discovery of penicillin F marked a major milestone in the development of antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: penicillin G - a widely used natural penicillin antibiotic
  • Degree Higher: penicillin V - an orally active form of penicillin

Etymology

Penicillin from Latin 'penicillus' (paintbrush) + F denoting variant; first identified in Great Britain.
