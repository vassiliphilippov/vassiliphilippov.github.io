producer price index

1. NOUN

/ prəˈdusər praɪs ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / prəˈdjuːsə praɪs ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
An official index that measures changes in the average prices received by domestic producers for their output at the wholesale or producer level.
index of changes in wholesale prices

Examples

The producer price index rose sharply last month, indicating increasing costs for manufacturers.
Economists use the producer price index to monitor inflation at the wholesale level.
A significant change in the producer price index can eventually impact consumer prices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consumer price index - an index measuring changes in the prices paid by consumers
