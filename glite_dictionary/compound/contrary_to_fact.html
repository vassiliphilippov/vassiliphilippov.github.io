contrary to fact

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnˌtrɛri tuː fækt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə.ri tʊ ˈfækt / (BrE)
describing a statement, condition, or hypothesis that is imagined or contrary to what is actually true; often used in grammar or argument to discuss unreal or hypothetical situations.
imagined or unreal; contrary to actual fact

Examples

His argument was based on a contrary to fact hypothesis that never actually occurred.
If you speak in the contrary to fact conditional, you use 'were' instead of 'was.'
She created a contrary to fact scenario to illustrate her point.
