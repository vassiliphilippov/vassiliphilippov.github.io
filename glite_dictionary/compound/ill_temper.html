ill temper

1. NOUN

/ ɪl ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ɪl ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
a persistent state of irritability or anger
persistent angry or irritable mood

Examples

His ill temper made it difficult for anyone to approach him at work.
Even small annoyances could easily spark her ill temper.
Despite his reputation for ill temper, he tried to remain civil at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad temper - an angry or irritable state of mind
  • Antonym: good temper - a cheerful and good-natured mood or disposition
