genus Amoeba

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈmiː.bə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈmiː.bə / (BrE)
A genus of protozoan organisms found in moist soil and aquatic environments, known for their shapeless, moving bodies.
a protozoan genus; shapeless aquatic organisms

Examples

The genus Amoeba is widely studied in schools as a classic example of a single-celled organism.
Members of the genus Amoeba move by extending their pseudopodia.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Amoeba in a freshwater lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amoeba - single-celled organism of the genus Amoeba

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'amoibē' meaning 'change' or 'exchange', referring to the organism's changing shape.
