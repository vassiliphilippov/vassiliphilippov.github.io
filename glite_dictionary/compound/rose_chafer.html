rose chafer

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz ˌtʃeɪfər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌtʃɑːfə / (BrE)
a common North American beetle whose larvae feed on roots and adults on leaves and flowers of plants, especially rose bushes, apple trees, or grape vines
North American beetle pest of plants

Examples

Gardeners often battle the rose chafer because it damages flowers and foliage.
The larvae of the rose chafer can destroy the roots of young plants.
After noticing many holes in my rose petals, I discovered a rose chafer feeding among the blooms.

Etymology

From 'rose' (referring to its favored plant) + 'chafer', Old English *ceafor* (beetle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz ˌtʃeɪfər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌtʃɑːfə / (BrE)
a common European beetle with metallic green coloring; its larvae feed on plant roots and adults feed on the leaves and flowers of plants such as roses
a green European beetle that feeds on plants

Examples

Gardeners often find the rose chafer feasting on their prized flowers during the spring and summer months.
The larvae of the rose chafer can cause significant damage to the roots of grass and young plants.
After inspecting the roses, she discovered several rose chafers nestled among the petals and leaves.
