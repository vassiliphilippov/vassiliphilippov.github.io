House of Commons

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaʊs əv ˈkɑːmənz / (AmE), / ˌhaʊs əv ˈkɒmənz / (BrE)
the elected lower chamber of the British Parliament, responsible for legislation and representing the public
lower house of the British Parliament
british

Examples

Debates in the House of Commons often draw media attention across the country.
The Prime Minister answered questions during a session in the House of Commons.
A bill must be approved by both the House of Commons and the House of Lords to become law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Lords - the upper house of the British Parliament

Etymology

From 'house' (assembly) + 'commons' (the common people), used in this parliamentary sense since the 14th century.
