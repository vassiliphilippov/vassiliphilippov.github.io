toothache tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːθˌeɪk triː / (AmE), / ˈtuːθˌeɪk triː / (BrE)
a small deciduous aromatic shrub or tree with spiny branches and yellowish flowers, native to eastern North America, traditionally used for relieving toothache
aromatic North American shrub or tree used for toothaches

Examples

The bark of the toothache tree was once chewed to ease tooth pain.
A toothache tree can be found growing in the woodlands of the southeastern United States.
Native Americans used parts of the toothache tree for medicinal purposes.

Etymology

Named for its historical use in treating toothaches; from English 'toothache' + 'tree'.
