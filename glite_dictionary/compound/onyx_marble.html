onyx marble

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnɪks ˈmɑrbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒnɪks ˈmɑːbəl / (BrE)
a hard, compact variety of calcite used as a decorative stone, often resembling onyx in appearance
a decorative variety of calcite, similar to onyx

Examples

The bathroom countertop was crafted from beautiful onyx marble.
Interior designers often choose onyx marble for its unique, translucent appearance.
The sculptor preferred onyx marble for delicate decorative pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onyx - a semi-precious stone with parallel bands, often black and white
  • Synonym: calcite - a common mineral made of calcium carbonate

Etymology

Blend of 'onyx', from Greek 'ὄνυξ' (onyx, fingernail, claw), and 'marble', from Latin 'marmor'.
