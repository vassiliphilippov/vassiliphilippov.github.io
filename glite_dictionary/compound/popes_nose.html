pope's nose

1. NOUN

/ poʊps noʊz / (AmE), / pəʊps nəʊz / (BrE)
the fatty tail part of a cooked fowl, especially a chicken or turkey, considered a delicacy by some
the tail of a cooked fowl

Examples

She always claimed the pope's nose was the tastiest part of the roast chicken.
At Thanksgiving, my uncle made sure to save the pope's nose for himself.
Some people avoid eating the pope's nose, but others consider it a treat.

2. NOUN

/ poʊps ˈnoʊz / (AmE), / pəʊps ˈnəʊz / (BrE)
the fatty, fleshy tail end of a bird, especially a turkey, often considered a delicacy by some
the tail end of a bird, especially a turkey

Examples

Every Thanksgiving, my grandmother insists on eating the pope's nose herself.
He was surprised to learn that some people consider the pope's nose a delicacy.
As the turkey was carved, the pope's nose was saved for the youngest guest.

Synset Examples

Some people love the pope's nose while others do not.
The chef served the turkey with the pope's nose intact.
He carved the pope's nose off for a snack.

Etymology

Probably from a humorous or ironic reference to the shape of a bishop’s or pope’s hat; first recorded in early 18th-century English.
