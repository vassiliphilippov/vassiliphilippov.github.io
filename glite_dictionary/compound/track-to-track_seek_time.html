track-to-track seek time

1. NOUN

/ træk tə træk sik taɪm / (AmE), / træk tə træk siːk taɪm / (BrE)
(computing) the time required for a read/write head in a storage device to move to an adjacent data track.
time to move read/write head to adjacent track

Examples

The track-to-track seek time of this hard drive is just 2 milliseconds, making it very efficient.
Engineers often measure the track-to-track seek time to assess a drive's responsiveness for rapid data access.
Improvements in track-to-track seek time have helped increase overall system performance in data centers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seek time - average time for a read/write head to reach a specific data track
