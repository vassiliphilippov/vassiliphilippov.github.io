cocoa bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊ biːn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəʊ biːn / (BrE)
The dried and fully fermented seed of the cacao tree, used to make cocoa and chocolate.
seed of the cacao tree used for chocolate

Examples

The cocoa bean is the essential ingredient in chocolate production.
Farmers in West Africa harvest and dry the cocoa beans before exporting them.
She visited a plantation to learn how cocoa beans are processed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacao bean - another term for cocoa bean
  • Degree Higher: chocolate - food made from roasted and ground cacao seeds

Etymology

From Spanish 'cocoa', a variant of 'cacao', from Nahuatl 'cacahuatl'.
