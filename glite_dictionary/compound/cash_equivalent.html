cash equivalent

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
a highly liquid asset that can be readily converted into a known amount of cash and typically has a maturity of three months or less
highly liquid asset convertible to cash

Examples

The company keeps a large portion of its funds in cash equivalents to ensure liquidity.
Short-term treasury bills are commonly considered cash equivalents on a balance sheet.
Businesses often invest surplus money in cash equivalents for better flexibility and security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquid asset - asset readily convertible to cash
  • Degree Lower: cash - money in coins or notes, as distinct from checks or credit
