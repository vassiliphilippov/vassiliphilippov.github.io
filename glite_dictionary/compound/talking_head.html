talking head

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔkɪŋ hɛd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːkɪŋ hɛd / (BrE)
a person, especially on television, who talks directly to the camera with only their head and upper body visible on the screen, often giving opinions or commentary.
person who speaks directly to camera on TV

Examples

The news program featured several talking heads discussing the day's political developments.
Many viewers are tired of endless talking heads offering their opinions on cable news.
As a talking head, she quickly became known for her sharp political analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commentator - person who provides opinions or commentary, especially on TV or radio

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔkɪŋ ˈhɛd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːkɪŋ ˈhɛd / (BrE)
a person, especially in television or video, who appears onscreen speaking directly to the audience, often delivering commentary or opinions
person onscreen speaking commentary

Examples

The debate featured several talking heads giving their opinions on the event.
She was hired as a talking head for the panel discussion on education reform.
Many viewers find the constant stream of talking heads on cable news to be repetitive.

Synset Examples

I can't stand that talking head on the news.
The documentary features a talking head explaining the history.
She felt like a talking head during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commentator - someone who provides spoken commentary, especially on television or radio
