part to whole relation

1. NOUN

/ pɑrt tu (w)hoʊl rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / pɑːt tʊ ˈhəʊl rɪˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the semantic relation in which one element is a part of a larger whole
relation between a part and its whole

Examples

Understanding the part to whole relation helps explain how terms like 'wheel' and 'car' are connected.
In linguistic theory, a part to whole relation describes how smaller elements contribute to the structure of a larger unit.
The part to whole relation is key in analyzing the organization of objects and concepts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meronymy - the semantic relation between a part and a whole
