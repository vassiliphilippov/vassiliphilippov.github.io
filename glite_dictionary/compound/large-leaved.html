large-leaved

1. ADJ

/ lɑrdʒ livd / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ liːvd / (BrE)
(of a plant) having relatively large leaves compared to others of its kind.
having relatively large leaves

Examples

The large-leaved philodendron makes a bold statement in any living room.
Gardeners often choose large-leaved hostas for shady borders.
This region is known for its large-leaved maples, which provide ample shade.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small-leaved - having relatively small leaves
