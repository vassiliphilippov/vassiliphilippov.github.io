rank-order correlation coefficient

1. NOUN

/ ræŋk ˈoʊrdər kəˌrleɪʃən koʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ræŋk ˈɔːdə kəˌrleɪʃən kəʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a statistical measure used to assess the relationship between the ranks of scores on two variables
statistical measure of ranks correlation

Examples

The rank-order correlation coefficient is often used in psychology to compare test rankings.
Researchers calculated the rank-order correlation coefficient to examine the relationship between the two sets of rankings.
A high rank-order correlation coefficient suggests a strong association between the two variables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spearman's rank correlation coefficient - a widely used type of rank-order correlation coefficient
