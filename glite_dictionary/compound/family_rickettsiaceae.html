family Rickettsiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli rɪˌkɛtsiˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli rɪˌkɛtsiˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of microorganisms resembling bacteria, inhabiting arthropod tissues, but capable of causing disease in vertebrates
a family of bacteria-like microorganisms
medical

Examples

The family Rickettsiaceae includes organisms that are known to cause typhus and Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
Researchers have found new species within the family Rickettsiaceae infecting ticks.
Understanding the family Rickettsiaceae is crucial for developing better treatments for rickettsial diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rickettsia - a genus of bacteria-like microorganisms within the family Rickettsiaceae

Etymology

From genus name Rickettsia, named after Howard Taylor Ricketts + Latin family suffix -aceae.
