family Staphylinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌstæfɪˈlɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌstæfɪlɪˈnɪːdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small, slender, and active beetles commonly known as rove beetles, characterized by very short wing covers.
family of beetles; rove beetles
medical

Examples

The family Staphylinidae includes over 60,000 species of rove beetles worldwide.
Entomologists often collect specimens from the family Staphylinidae when studying decaying organic matter.
Several members of the family Staphylinidae play important roles in natural pest control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rove beetle - common name for beetles in the family Staphylinidae

Etymology

From New Latin, Staphylinidae, from the genus Staphylinus, ultimately from Greek staphylē (bunch of grapes), referring to the beetle’s shape.
