Alpine woodsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn ˈwʊdziə / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn ˈwʊdzɪə / (BrE)
A slender fern of northern North America, characterized by shiny chestnut-colored stalks and bipinnate fronds with usually distinct marginal spore clusters.
species of slender North American fern

Examples

We found a rare patch of Alpine woodsia growing among the rocks on the mountain slope.
The Alpine woodsia thrives in cool, shaded crevices of northern forests.
Botanists were excited to discover Alpine woodsia in this region for the first time.
