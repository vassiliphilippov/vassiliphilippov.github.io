false saffron

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈsæfrən / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈsæfrən / (BrE)
a thistlelike Eurasian plant with red or orange flower heads and seeds that produce a valuable oil, often used as a substitute for saffron
plant with saffron-like flowers; yields oil

Examples

The oil extracted from false saffron is widely used in cooking and cosmetics.
Many gardeners plant false saffron for its vibrant, colorful blooms and hardiness.
In some recipes, false saffron is used as an affordable alternative to true saffron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safflower - a plant whose flowers and seeds are used for coloring and oil
  • Antonym: saffron - a spice from the dried stigma of the crocus flower
