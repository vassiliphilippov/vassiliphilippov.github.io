lumbar pain

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌm.bɑr peɪn / (AmE), / ˈlʌm.bɑː peɪn / (BrE)
Pain affecting the lumbar region or lower back, often resulting from muscle strain, arthritis, or injury.
pain in the lower back
medical

Examples

She visited the clinic because her lumbar pain had become unbearable over the weekend.
Prolonged sitting can often lead to lumbar pain in office workers.
The doctor suggested exercises to relieve his recurring lumbar pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backache - pain in the back

Etymology

From Latin 'lumbalis' (pertaining to the loins) and 'pain'.
