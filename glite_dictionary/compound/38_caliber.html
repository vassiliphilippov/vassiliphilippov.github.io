.38 caliber

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɜrtiˈeɪt ˈkæləbɚ / (AmE), / ˌθɜːtiˈeɪt ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
of or relating to the bore of a gun, or its ammunition, that measures thirty-eight hundredths of an inch in diameter
gun bore/round size (0.38 inch)

Examples

She bought a .38 caliber revolver for personal protection.
The detective found a .38 caliber shell casing at the crime scene.
This handgun uses .38 caliber ammunition, which is popular for self-defense.

Synset Examples

a .38 caliber shell

Etymology

From the decimal measurement of the bore diameter (0.38 inch) plus 'caliber', from French calibre.
