Outer Mongolia

1. PROPN

/ ˈaʊtər mɒnˈɡoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə mɒŋˈɡəʊliə / (BrE)
the region comprising the present-day country of Mongolia, distinguished from Inner Mongolia (an autonomous region of China); formerly a term for the area when it was a part of the Chinese empire.
historical term for present-day Mongolia

Examples

During the Qing dynasty, Outer Mongolia was under Chinese administration, but it declared independence in 1911.
Many travelers cross the Gobi Desert when journeying from Outer Mongolia into China.
The history of Outer Mongolia is shaped by its unique nomadic culture and strategic location.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Inner Mongolia - autonomous region of China bordering Mongolia

Etymology

Formed by combining 'outer' (meaning 'further from the center') with 'Mongolia'; used in the 19th–20th centuries to distinguish the region from Inner Mongolia.

2. PROPN

/ ˈaʊtər mɒŋˈɡoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə mɒŋˈɡəʊliə / (BrE)
the region of Mongolia located outside of Inner Mongolia, historically referring to the area that is now the independent state of Mongolia
region referring to modern Mongolia

Examples

She plans to spend the summer traveling across Outer Mongolia to learn about its nomadic heritage.
The border between Outer Mongolia and Russia has been important throughout history.
Researchers conducted ecological surveys in the remote grasslands of Outer Mongolia.

Synset Examples

Outer Mongolia has a rich nomadic culture and traditions.
She dreams of exploring Outer Mongolia on horseback.
The vast steppes of Outer Mongolia are breathtaking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Inner Mongolia - autonomous region of China adjacent to Mongolia

Etymology

Named in contrast to 'Inner Mongolia'; 'outer' from English, 'Mongolia' from Russian 'Mongoliya', ultimately from Mongol ethnonym.
