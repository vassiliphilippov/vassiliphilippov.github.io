structural formula

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌk.tʃɚ.əl ˈfɔr.mjə.lə / (AmE), / ˈstrʌk.tʃər.əl ˈfɔː.mjʊ.lə / (BrE)
a chemical formula that shows the arrangement of atoms within a molecule, rather than just the number of each type of atom
shows atom arrangement in a molecule
medical

Examples

The structural formula of ethanol shows the arrangement of hydrogen, carbon, and oxygen atoms.
Chemists often draw a structural formula to explain how atoms bond in a compound.
Unlike a molecular formula, a structural formula provides detailed information about a molecule's structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: molecular formula - chemical formula showing only the number and type of atoms in a molecule

Etymology

Formed from 'structure' and 'formula', standard English roots in scientific use.
