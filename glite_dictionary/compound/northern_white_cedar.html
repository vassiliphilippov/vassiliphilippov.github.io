northern white cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn waɪt ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən waɪt ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree of eastern North America with tiny, scalelike leaves on flattened branchlets, commonly used as an ornamental or hedge plant
a North American evergreen tree

Examples

The northern white cedar is often used for privacy hedges in suburban yards.
Many wildlife species rely on the northern white cedar for shelter during the winter.
The lumber from northern white cedar is valued for its resistance to decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborvitae - another name for northern white cedar
