genus Chinchilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (BrE)
the genus of rodents that includes the common chinchilla and closely related species native to South America
rodent genus including the chinchilla

Examples

The genus Chinchilla is known for its members' exceptionally soft fur.
Scientists are studying two species within the genus Chinchilla to understand their evolutionary history.
The genus Chinchilla is native to the Andes mountains of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinchilla - small South American rodent valued for its soft fur

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and modern Latin 'Chinchilla' after a Peruvian word.
