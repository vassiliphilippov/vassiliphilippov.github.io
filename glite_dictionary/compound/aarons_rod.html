Aaron's rod

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrənz rɑd / (AmE), / ˈɛːrənz rɒd / (BrE)
a tall, woolly plant (verbascum thapsus) with dense yellow flower spikes, formerly used for making torches in ancient Greece and Rome
a tall woolly plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The tall spikes of Aaron's rod could be seen growing along the roadside.
In ancient ceremonies, people used dried Aaron's rod as torches.
Gardeners often plant Aaron's rod for its decorative yellow flowers and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mullein - a tall woolly-leafed plant with yellow flowers, Verbascum thapsus

Etymology

Alludes to the biblical rod of Aaron, from the Old Testament; later applied to various tall-stemmed plants resembling a rod.
