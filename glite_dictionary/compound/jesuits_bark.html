Jesuit's bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛzjuɪts bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzjʊɪts bɑːk / (BrE)
the medicinal bark of cinchona trees, used as a source of quinine and quinidine, traditionally used to treat malaria
medicinal bark of cinchona trees; quinine source

Examples

Early explorers brought Jesuit's bark back to Europe as a remedy for malaria.
The use of Jesuit's bark revolutionized malaria treatment in the 17th century.
Doctors once prescribed Jesuit's bark to patients suffering from fever in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinchona bark - the bark from cinchona trees used for medicine
  • Synonym: quinine - a medicinal compound derived from cinchona bark

Etymology

Named because Jesuit missionaries introduced cinchona bark to Europe in the 17th century.
