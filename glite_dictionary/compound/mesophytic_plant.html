mesophytic plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsəˈfaɪtɪk plænt / (AmE), / ˌmiːsəˈfaɪtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
A land plant that grows in environments with a moderate or average supply of water, as opposed to very dry or very wet conditions.
plant thriving in moderate moisture

Examples

The oak tree is a common example of a mesophytic plant found in temperate forests.
Most crops we cultivate are mesophytic plants that require moderate watering for optimal growth.
Unlike cacti, which are xerophytes, a mesophytic plant cannot survive in arid deserts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: xerophyte - plant adapted to dry environments
  • Antonym: hydrophyte - plant adapted to aquatic or very wet environments

Etymology

From Greek mesos (middle) + phuton (plant), referring to plants of intermediate moisture conditions.
