winter's bark tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntɚz bɑrk tri / (AmE), / ˈwɪntəz bɑːk triː / (BrE)
a South American evergreen tree (Drimys winteri) that yields winter's bark and a light, soft wood similar to basswood
South American evergreen tree yielding winter's bark

Examples

The winter's bark tree was once used by sailors to treat scurvy due to its high vitamin C content.
Botanists are fascinated by the fragrant leaves and medicinal bark of the winter's bark tree.
Several winter's bark trees grow in the botanical garden's South American section.

Etymology

Named after English explorer John Winter, who used the bark to prevent scurvy.
