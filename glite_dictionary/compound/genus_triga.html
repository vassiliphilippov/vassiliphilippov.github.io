genus Triga

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtraɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
The type genus of the Triglidae family, which includes certain gurnards or sea robins.
type genus of Triglidae (gurnards)

Examples

The genus Triga includes several species of marine fish known for their armored heads.
Ichthyologists study the genus Triga to understand the evolution of gurnards.
One distinctive feature of fish in the genus Triga is their large pectoral fins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Triglidae - the fish family containing gurnards and sea robins

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and the modern New Latin genus name 'Triga.'
