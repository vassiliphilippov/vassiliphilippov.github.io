low blueberry

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈbluːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A low-growing deciduous shrub of northeastern North America, bearing sweet dark blue edible berries in compact clusters.
small blueberry bush of northeastern North America

Examples

Many wild low blueberries can be found growing in the forests of Maine.
Harvesting low blueberry plants is a summer tradition for some local families.
The sweet taste of low blueberries makes them popular for making jams and pies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - deciduous shrub bearing blue edible berries
