prefrontal lobotomy

1. NOUN

/ priˈfrʌntəl loʊˈbɑtəmi / (AmE), / priˈfrʌntəl ləˈbɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the severing of nerve connections to and from the prefrontal cortex of the brain, formerly used to treat certain mental disorders, often resulting in significant changes in personality and cognition
surgical procedure severing frontal brain connections
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist recommended a prefrontal lobotomy for severe cases that did not respond to other treatments.
Many patients who underwent a prefrontal lobotomy in the 1940s experienced profound personality changes.
The film depicted the tragic consequences of a prefrontal lobotomy performed on the protagonist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobotomy - surgical operation cutting into the brain's frontal lobes

Etymology

From 'prefrontal' (in front of the frontal lobe) and Greek 'lobos' (lobe) + 'tome' (cutting).
