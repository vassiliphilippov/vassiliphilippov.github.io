Lowlands of Scotland

1. PROPN

/ ˈloʊˌlændz əv ˈskɑt.lənd / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlændz əv ˈskɒt.lənd / (BrE)
the southern and central region of Scotland distinguished by its relatively low-lying land and lack of mountains, in contrast to the Highlands
region of Scotland with low-lying land

Examples

Much of the population of the Lowlands of Scotland is concentrated in the cities of Glasgow and Edinburgh.
The cultural traditions of the Lowlands of Scotland differ from those in the Highlands.
Farmers in the Lowlands of Scotland benefit from the region’s fertile soil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Highlands of Scotland - mountainous region in the north of Scotland
