junior featherweight

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːniər ˈfɛðərˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːniə ˈfɛðəweɪt / (BrE)
A weight category in boxing and some other combat sports for competitors who weigh no more than 122 pounds (55.34 kg).
boxing weight category (≤122 lbs)

Examples

He moved up to junior featherweight after winning several titles in a lower division.
The junior featherweight champion successfully defended her title last night.
Many aspiring boxers compete in the junior featherweight division before moving up in weight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: featherweight - boxing weight class up to 126 pounds
