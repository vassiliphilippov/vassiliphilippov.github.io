hexanedioic acid

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˌsænɪˈdioʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / hɛkˌsænɪˈdiːəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a dicarboxylic acid (C6H10O4) used mainly in the manufacture of nylon and other polyamides
a chemical used to make nylon

Examples

Hexanedioic acid is a key raw material in the production of nylon 6,6 fibers.
Industries often store hexanedioic acid in large quantities for polymer manufacturing.
The laboratory synthesized pure hexanedioic acid using an oxidation reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adipic acid - common name for hexanedioic acid, a dicarboxylic acid used in nylon production

Etymology

From hexane + dioic, indicating a six-carbon chain with two carboxylic acid groups. Commonly known as adipic acid.
