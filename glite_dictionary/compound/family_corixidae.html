family Corixidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli kɔːˈrɪksɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli kɒˈrɪksɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic insects known as water boatmen, found in freshwater habitats worldwide.
a family of aquatic insects (water boatmen)

Examples

Scientists observed a large population of family Corixidae in the shallow pond.
The family Corixidae includes many species that are important in freshwater ecosystems.
Students studied the characteristics of family Corixidae for their entomology project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water boatman - common name for insects of the family Corixidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Corixidae,' derived from the genus name 'Corixa', itself from Greek 'koryx' (leather bottle, sack), plus '-idae', a standard suffix for animal family names.
