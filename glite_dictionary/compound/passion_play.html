Passion play

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃən pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpæʃən pleɪ / (BrE)
a play that dramatizes the events of the Passion of Christ, especially his trial, suffering, and death
play depicting the Passion of Christ

Examples

Many tourists travel to Oberammergau to see the town's famous Passion play.
The Passion play has been performed annually in the village for centuries.
Schoolchildren participated in a modern adaptation of the Passion play during Easter celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery play - a medieval play based on biblical stories

Etymology

From Latin 'passio' meaning 'suffering', referring to the suffering of Christ.
