family Ephippidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɛˈfɪpɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɛˈfɪpɪdiː / (BrE)
A small family of marine fishes known as spadefishes, found in warm and temperate seas.
family of marine spadefishes

Examples

The family Ephippidae includes about eight species of spadefishes.
Researchers observed several members of the family Ephippidae near the reef.
The distinctive shape of fishes in the family Ephippidae makes them easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefish - a common name for members of the family Ephippidae

Etymology

From Greek *ephippos* (saddle) referencing the saddle-like shape of the fish.
