manipulative tactic

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪpjəˌleɪtɪv ˈtæktɪk / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjələtɪv ˈtæktɪk / (BrE)
a strategy designed to control or influence others, often in a deceptive or unfair way
strategy to control or influence others

Examples

She exposed their manipulative tactic of spreading false information to win support.
The company's manipulative tactics led to complaints from customers.
By recognizing the manipulative tactic, employees were able to resist pressure from management.

Synset Examples

Many left the trans cult after realizing its manipulative tactics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploy - a cunning plan or action to turn a situation to one's own advantage
  • Synonym: ruse - a trick or strategy intended to deceive someone
