en deshabille

1. ADJ

/ ɑ̃ deɪzæˈbiːl / (AmE), / ɑ̃ deɪˈzæbiːl / (BrE)
partly dressed in a loose or careless manner, typically in casual or informal attire within the home
partly dressed or in a loose state of dress

Examples

She greeted her guests en deshabille, wrapped only in a silk robe.
He was caught en deshabille when the unexpected visitor arrived early in the morning.
The artist painted his muse en deshabille, capturing her casual elegance.

Etymology

From French 'en déshabillé', meaning 'undressed' or 'in a state of undress'.
