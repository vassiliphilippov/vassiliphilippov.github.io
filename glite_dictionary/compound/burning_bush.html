burning bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrnɪŋ ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub, especially of the genus Euonymus, having purple capsules that enclose bright scarlet seeds
a shrub with purple fruit capsules and scarlet seeds

Examples

The garden features a beautiful burning bush that displays crimson seeds every autumn.
Last fall, the burning bush in our yard turned a brilliant shade of red.
Botanists often study the burning bush because of its unique seed capsules.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːnɪŋ bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ bʊʃ / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Dictamnus albus) with white flowers that give off a flammable vapor in hot weather.
plant with flammable white flowers

Examples

The burning bush in our garden released a strange scent during the hottest part of summer.
On a warm afternoon, the vapor from the burning bush can actually ignite if exposed to a flame.
Botanists studied the burning bush to understand its unusual ability to emit flammable gases.
