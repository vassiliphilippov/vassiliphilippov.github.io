systematic desensitisation

1. NOUN

/ sɪstəˌmætɪk dɪˌsɛnsətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪstəˌmætɪk dɪˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a method in behavior therapy for reducing phobias or anxiety by gradually exposing a person to the feared object or situation under relaxed conditions until the fear is diminished
behavior therapy technique to reduce phobias/anxiety
medical

Examples

The psychologist recommended systematic desensitisation to help her overcome her intense fear of dogs.
Through systematic desensitisation, patients gradually learn to confront their anxieties in a controlled environment.
Researchers found systematic desensitisation to be effective in treating phobias such as fear of flying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exposure therapy - a psychological treatment involving exposure to feared situations
  • Synonym: flooding - a behavioral therapy exposing patients directly to feared situations

Etymology

From systematic (Greek systematikos, 'orderly') + desensitisation, from Latin de- + sensus ('feeling').
