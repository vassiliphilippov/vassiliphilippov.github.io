hairy finger grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛri ˈfɪŋɡər ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈheəri ˈfɪŋɡə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a species of grass native to Europe, grown for hay and often found as a naturalized weed in the United States
a European forage grass species

Examples

Farmers often harvest hairy finger grass to use as feed for livestock.
Hairy finger grass can quickly spread in pastures, making it a common weed in some U.S. states.
The botanist identified the unfamiliar plant as hairy finger grass during her field survey.
