polygonal chain

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪgənl tʃeɪn / (AmE), / pəˈlɪɡənəl tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a connected sequence of straight line segments in geometry, where each segment joins two points and consecutive segments share an endpoint
connected sequence of line segments (geometry)

Examples

To visualize the boundary, imagine drawing a polygonal chain that connects all the corner points.
The algorithm constructs a polygonal chain to approximate the curve.
You can represent the shape as a polygonal chain composed of several segments.

Synset Examples

The upper convex hull is a polygonal chain that connects the leftmost point in P to the rightmost one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygon - a plane figure with at least three straight sides and angles

Etymology

From 'polygon', from Greek 'polugōnos' (many-angled) + 'chain'.
