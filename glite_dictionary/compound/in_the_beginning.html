in the beginning

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə bəˈɡɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪn ði bɪˈɡɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
at or during the initial stage of a period, process, or activity; at first
at the initial stage; at first

Examples

In the beginning, I found the new city overwhelming, but now I feel at home here.
He struggled in the beginning of the semester but improved over time.
In the beginning, their business had only three employees.

Synset Examples

why didn't you tell me in the first place?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at first - from the beginning, initially
  • Antonym: in the end - at or near the final stage; finally

2. ADV

/ ɪn ðə bɪˈɡɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə bɪˈɡɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
at the start or origin of something; in the early stages.
at the start; at first

Examples

In the beginning, there were only two people working at the company.
Many people found the new subject difficult in the beginning, but improved over time.
In the beginning, the universe was empty and dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at first - in or at the beginning
  • Antonym: in the end - eventually, after everything has been considered
