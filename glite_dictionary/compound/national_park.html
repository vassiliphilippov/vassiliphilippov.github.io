national park

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənəl pɑrk / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl pɑːk / (BrE)
an area of land protected by a national government to conserve its natural environment and make it accessible to the public
protected area of land by a national government

Examples

Yellowstone was established as the first national park in the United States in 1872.
We spent our vacation hiking and camping in a stunning national park last summer.
Efforts to preserve wildlife habitats often result in the creation of a new national park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nature reserve - area of land managed mainly for wildlife conservation
  • Synonym: state park - a park managed by a state government
