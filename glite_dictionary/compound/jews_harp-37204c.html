Jew's harp

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːz hɑːrp / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːz hɑːp / (BrE)
a small lyre-shaped musical instrument held between the teeth and played by plucking a flexible metal tongue while varying the mouth's shape to create different tones
small plucked musical instrument held in the mouth

Examples

He played a lively tune on his Jew's harp at the folk festival.
The unique sound of the Jew's harp came from its vibrating metal tongue.
She bought a vintage Jew's harp at the market during her travels in Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaw harp - alternative name for the Jew's harp

Etymology

The origin is uncertain; possibly from English folk usage rather than any connection to Jewish culture.
