crepe marocain

1. NOUN

/ kreɪp ˌmærəˈkeɪn / (AmE), / kreɪp ˌmærɒˈkɛɪn / (BrE)
A heavy dress crepe fabric with a distinctively crinkled surface, similar to Canton crepe, used especially for women's dresses.
heavy crinkled dress fabric

Examples

She chose a deep blue crepe marocain for her wedding dress, appreciating its elegant texture.
The store specializes in vintage gowns made from crepe marocain and other luxurious fabrics.
A tailor recommended crepe marocain because it drapes beautifully and resists wrinkling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canton crepe - a lightweight, crinkled silk fabric

Etymology

From French, literally ‘Moroccan crepe’.
