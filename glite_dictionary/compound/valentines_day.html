Valentine's Day

1. NOUN

/ ˌvælənˈtaɪnz ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌvalənˈtʌɪnz ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
A holiday celebrated on February 14 in which people express affection for each other, often by giving cards, gifts, or flowers.
February 14 holiday for expressing affection

Examples

On Valentine's Day, many couples enjoy a romantic dinner together.
Children often exchange cards with classmates to celebrate Valentine's Day.
He bought her a bouquet of roses for Valentine's Day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valentine - a card or gift sent on Valentine's Day

Etymology

Named after Saint Valentine, a Christian martyr; celebration traced back to Roman and medieval traditions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvælənˌtaɪnz deɪ / (AmE), / ˈvaləntaɪnz deɪ / (BrE)
A holiday celebrated on February 14th, when people express love and affection for others, often by exchanging cards, gifts, or tokens.
holiday for expressing love on February 14th

Examples

On Valentine's Day, couples often exchange cards and flowers to show their affection.
She received a box of chocolates from her friend on Valentine's Day.
Many restaurants offer special menus to celebrate Valentine's Day.

Etymology

Named after St. Valentine, an early Christian saint, associated with romantic love since the Middle Ages.
