infernal region

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɝːnəl ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəl ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
(In Christianity) the abode of Satan and evil forces; a place of eternal punishment for sinners, commonly known as Hell.
Hell; place of eternal punishment

Examples

Many ancient texts vividly describe the infernal region as a place of eternal suffering.
In the painting, tormented souls appear to writhe in the infernal region below the heavens.
The preacher warned the congregation about the consequences of a life that leads to the infernal region.

Synset Examples

Hurl'd headlong … To bottomless perdition, there to dwell
a demon from the depths of the pit
Hell is paved with good intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hell - the underworld; place of eternal punishment in Christianity

Etymology

From Latin infernalis (of the lower regions) and regionem (region).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɜrnəl ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəl ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
(literary) the world of the dead or a place of torment; the underworld in religious or mythological belief systems.
the world of the dead; the underworld

Examples

Ancient myths often describe the infernal region as a place of eternal punishment for wrongdoers.
The poet's vision of the infernal region was filled with fire, darkness, and lost souls.
Many cultures have stories about heroes who dare to journey into the infernal region to rescue the dead.

Synset Examples

No one goes to Hades with all his immense wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underworld - the mythical abode of the dead
  • Antonym: heaven - the abode of God and the angels; the opposite of hell or the infernal region

Etymology

From Latin infernus (of the lower regions), ultimately from inferus (low, below).
