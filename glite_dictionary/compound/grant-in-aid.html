grant-in-aid

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænt ɪn eɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnt ɪn eɪd / (BrE)
an amount of money given to a person, school, or organization for a particular purpose, especially for educational projects
financial support for a project, often educational

Examples

The university received a grant-in-aid to support its new science program.
Many students rely on a grant-in-aid to help pay for their studies.
The government awarded a large grant-in-aid to improve rural schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money given by an organization for a particular purpose

Etymology

Formed from 'grant' (from Old French 'graanter', to promise) + 'in aid', meaning for assistance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡrænt ɪn ˈeɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡrɑːnt ɪn ˈeɪd / (BrE)
a sum of money provided by a central government to a local government, institution, or organization for a specific purpose
central government financial grant to local government

Examples

The city received a grant-in-aid to improve its public transportation system.
Several schools rely on grants-in-aid from the state for funding their programs.
Without a grant-in-aid, the museum would have struggled to stay open this year.

Etymology

Formed from 'grant' and 'aid'; commonly used in government finance since the 19th century.
