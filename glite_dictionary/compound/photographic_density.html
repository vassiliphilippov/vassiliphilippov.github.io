photographic density

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊ.təˈɡræf.ɪk ˈdɛn.sə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.təˈɡræf.ɪk ˈden.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
a measure of how much a photographic material or substance reduces the transmission of light or other electromagnetic radiation through it
degree light is blocked in a photographic material

Examples

The photographic density of the film determines how much detail is captured in darker areas.
Accurate measurement of photographic density is essential in quality control for printing processes.
A higher photographic density indicates that less light is transmitted through the photographic plate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque, not allowing light to pass through
  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of allowing light to pass through; clear enough to be seen through
