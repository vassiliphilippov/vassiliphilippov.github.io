double time

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbl taɪm / (AmE), / ˈdʌbl taɪm / (BrE)
Payment at twice the standard wage rate, typically for working overtime or on holidays.
payment at twice the usual wage

Examples

The employees received double time for working on Christmas Day.
If you come in on Sunday, you'll be paid double time.
She agreed to the extra shift knowing she would earn double time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtime - time worked beyond normal working hours
  • Degree Lower: time and a half - payment at one and a half times the normal rate

2. ADV

/ ˈdʌbəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l taɪm / (BrE)
at a speed that is twice as fast as normal; very quickly
at twice the normal speed

Examples

The soldiers were ordered to march in double time to reach the camp before sunset.
When the deadline approached, the team worked at double time to finish the project.
Let's rehearse the piece again, but this time play it in double time to pick up the energy.

Synset Examples

now let's play the piece again double-quick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double-quick - at very fast speed; quickly
  • Antonym: half time - at half the usual speed (especially in music)

Etymology

From 'double' (twice as much) + 'time', used originally in music and military marching contexts.
