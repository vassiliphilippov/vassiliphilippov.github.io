dumdum bullet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmˌdʌm ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʌmˌdʌm ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
A soft-nosed small-arms bullet designed to expand on impact, causing a large, gaping wound.
expanding soft-nosed bullet

Examples

The use of dumdum bullets has been banned by many international conventions.
He identified the ammunition as a dumdum bullet due to its soft, exposed lead tip.
Doctors struggled to treat the injuries caused by a single dumdum bullet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollow-point bullet - a bullet with a hollowed-out shape in the tip, designed to expand upon impact

Etymology

Named after Dum Dum, a town in India where such bullets were first made.
