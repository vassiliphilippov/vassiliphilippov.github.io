Yule log

1. NOUN

/ juːl lɑg / (AmE), / juːl lɒg / (BrE)
A large log traditionally burned in a fireplace at Christmas as part of old custom, especially in Europe.
large log burned at Christmas

Examples

On Christmas Eve, the family gathered around the fireplace to watch the Yule log burn.
The tradition of the Yule log dates back centuries in many European countries.
She decorated the hearth with holly and placed a Yule log ready for the festivities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bûche de Noël - a cake shaped like a Yule log, served at Christmas

Etymology

From 'Yule' (Old English 'geol', a Christmas festival) + 'log'.
