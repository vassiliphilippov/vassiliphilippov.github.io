Appalachian Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˌæpəˈleɪtʃən ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈleɪtʃi.ən ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in eastern North America, extending from southeastern Canada to northern Alabama in the United States, historically forming a barrier to early westward expansion.
mountain range in eastern North America

Examples

The Appalachian Mountains are known for their rich biodiversity and beautiful fall foliage.
Early American settlers found it difficult to cross the Appalachian Mountains during westward migration.
Many popular hiking trails, including the Appalachian Trail, run along the Appalachian Mountains.

Etymology

Named after the Apalachee, a Native American people of northern Florida.
