million-dollar question

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪljən ˈdɑlər ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪljən ˈdɒlə ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
an important or decisive question whose answer is highly sought after or significant
a crucial or key question

Examples

The million-dollar question is whether the company can turn a profit next year.
Her presentation started with the million-dollar question everyone wanted answered.
Finding a solution to climate change is the million-dollar question for scientists worldwide.

Synset Examples

What’s the million-dollar question in this debate?
The million-dollar question is whether it will work.
Everyone is asking the million-dollar question about the future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big question - an important or critical question
  • Antonym: trivial question - a question of little importance

Etymology

From the phrase 'million dollars', signifying something of great value; patterned after 'the $64,000 question', a popular American TV phrase.
