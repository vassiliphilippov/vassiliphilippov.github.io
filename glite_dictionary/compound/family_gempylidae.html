family Gempylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒɛmˈfɪlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dʒɛmˈfɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated, predatory marine fish known as snake mackerels, found in deep ocean waters.
family of snake mackerels (fish)

Examples

The family Gempylidae includes several predatory fish found in deep waters around the world.
Biologists studied the unique teeth structures of fish in the family Gempylidae.
Members of the family Gempylidae are often called snake mackerels due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake mackerel - elongated, predatory deep-water fish of the family Gempylidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Gempylidae*, from type genus *Gempylus* and suffix *-idae* (denoting zoological families).
