common lilac

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈlaɪlək / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈlaɪlək / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub, native to Europe and widely grown as an ornamental in gardens, known for its heart-shaped leaves and large clusters of highly fragrant lilac or white flowers.
a fragrant garden shrub with lilac or white flowers

Examples

The common lilac bushes in our yard fill the air with a sweet scent every spring.
Gardeners often choose the common lilac for its beautiful clusters of purple flowers.
A single stem of common lilac brightened the entire room with its fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Syringa vulgaris - the scientific name for the common lilac
  • Degree Lower: lilac - a shrub with pale purple flowers; general term for related plants
