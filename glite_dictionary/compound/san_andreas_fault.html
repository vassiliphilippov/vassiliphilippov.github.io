San Andreas Fault

1. PROPN

/ ˌsæn ænˈdreɪəs fɔlt / (AmE), / ˌsæn ænˈdreɪəs fɔːlt / (BrE)
a major geological fault in California, running from San Diego to San Francisco, and known for causing significant earthquakes
major geological fault in California

Examples

The San Andreas Fault is responsible for many of California's most powerful earthquakes.
Seismologists monitor the San Andreas Fault to study tectonic activity and earthquake risks.
Driving along the San Andreas Fault, you can sometimes see dramatic evidence of past seismic shifts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fault - a crack in the Earth's crust along which movement has occurred

Etymology

Named after the San Andreas Lake, which was itself named in Spanish after Saint Andrew (San Andreas in Spanish).
