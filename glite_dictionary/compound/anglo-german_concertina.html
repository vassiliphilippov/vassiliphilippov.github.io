Anglo-German concertina

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊ ˌdʒɜrmən kənˈsɝː.tɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊ ˌdʒɜː.mən kənˈsɜː.tɪ.nə / (BrE)
a type of concertina with bellows and buttons on both ends, known for its distinctive construction and square or hexagonal shape
concertina with buttons on both ends

Examples

He bought an Anglo-German concertina to play traditional Irish folk music.
The Anglo-German concertina features distinctly separate button layouts on each end.
Many collectors seek rare Anglo-German concertinas from the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concertina - a small musical instrument with bellows and buttons producing notes

Etymology

Named for its origins in both England and Germany, combining features from both traditions.
