bobby pin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbi pɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒbi pɪn / (BrE)
A small, flat wire hairpin with prongs pressed tightly together, used to hold hair in place.
flat hairpin for holding hair in place

Examples

She used a bobby pin to hold back a stray lock of hair.
After searching her purse, Maria finally found a bobby pin to secure her bun.
In the 1920s, women often styled their short hair with a bobby pin.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a bobby pin a ‘grip’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairpin - a device for holding hair in place

Etymology

From 'bobbed' hair, a style popular in the 1920s, with 'pin' referring to its function.
