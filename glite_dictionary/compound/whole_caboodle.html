whole caboodle

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl kəˈbudl̩ / (AmE), / həʊl kəˈbuːdl̩ / (BrE)
the whole group of things or people; everything involved
the whole lot; everything
informal

Examples

After cleaning out the attic, we donated the whole caboodle to charity.
He sold his business, house, car—the whole caboodle—before moving abroad.
If you want to upgrade, you might as well get the whole caboodle at once.

Synset Examples

we saw the whole shebang
a hotdog with the works
we took on the whole caboodle
for $10 you get the full treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole shebang - the whole thing; everything
  • Synonym: the works - everything included; all available items
  • Antonym: part - a portion or piece rather than the whole

Etymology

'Caboodle' is said to be an alteration of 'boodle,' from Dutch 'boedel' (property, possessions).
