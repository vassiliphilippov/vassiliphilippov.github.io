at odds

1. ADV

/ ət ˈɑdz / (AmE), / ət ˈɒdz / (BrE)
in conflict or disagreement with someone or something
in disagreement; in conflict

Examples

The manager found herself at odds with her team over the new strategy.
Their explanations are at odds with the evidence presented in court.
The new policy is at odds with the company's traditional values.

Synset Examples

His actions were at odds with his words.
They were at odds over the best way to proceed.
The siblings are often at odds about money.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in agreement - having the same opinion; not in conflict
  • Synonym: at loggerheads - in strong disagreement or dispute
