physical therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
The treatment of disease, injury, or deformity using physical methods such as exercise, massage, and other modalities, rather than by drugs or surgery.
treatment using exercise, massage, etc.
medical

Examples

After his knee surgery, Dan needed several months of physical therapy to regain strength and flexibility.
The doctor recommended physical therapy instead of medication for her chronic back pain.
Many patients benefit from physical therapy following joint replacement procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physiotherapy - British term for physical therapy; treatment with physical methods

Etymology

From 'physical' (relating to the body) and 'therapy' (from Greek therapeia, healing).
