space age

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪs eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪs eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period in history that began with the first human activities in outer space, typically considered to start in 1957 and continuing to the present day.
era beginning with first space travel (1957–present)

Examples

The invention of the satellite marked the dawn of the space age.
Many technologies we use today were developed during the space age.
Her fascination with the space age led her to study astronomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic age - the period following the first use of atomic energy (from 1945 onwards)
