horizontal integration

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntl̩ ˌɪntəˌɡreɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntəl ˌɪntɪˈɡreɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
The process of a company increasing its production of goods or services at the same level of the supply chain by acquiring or merging with similar companies.
acquiring firms at same production level

Examples

The tech giant expanded its market share through horizontal integration by acquiring several competing startups.
Horizontal integration allows companies to reduce competition and consolidate resources.
A classic example of horizontal integration is when one airline buys another operating in the same region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vertical integration - combining firms at different stages of production

Etymology

From 'horizontal' (parallel to the horizon) and 'integration' (combining parts into a whole); business usage from early 20th century.
