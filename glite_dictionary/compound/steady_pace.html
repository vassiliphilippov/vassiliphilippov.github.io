steady pace

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛdi peɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi peɪs / (BrE)
a consistent speed or rate of movement without significant changes or interruptions
consistent, unchanging speed

Examples

He trained himself to run at a steady pace for the entire marathon.
The hikers walked at a steady pace to conserve their energy.
Progress on the project continued at a steady pace despite the minor setbacks.

Synset Examples

He maintained a steady pace throughout the race.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brisk pace - a rather quick or energetic speed
  • Degree Lower: slow pace - a movement or progress at a lower speed
