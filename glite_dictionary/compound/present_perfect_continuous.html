present perfect continuous

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt ˈpɝːfəkt kənˈtɪnjuːəs / (AmE), / ˈprɛznt ˈpɜːfɪkt kənˈtɪnjʊəs / (BrE)
A verb tense formed with 'has been' or 'have been' plus the present participle, used to describe actions that began in the past and are still continuing or have only just stopped.
verb tense for ongoing past-to-present actions

Examples

He has been working here for three years.
They have been waiting for the bus since morning.
She has been studying all night for the exam.

Synset Examples

The present perfect progressive tense usually conveys the meaning of recently or lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present perfect progressive - alternative name for the same verb tense

Etymology

From Latin 'praesens' (present), 'perfectus' (completed), and 'continuus' (unbroken).
