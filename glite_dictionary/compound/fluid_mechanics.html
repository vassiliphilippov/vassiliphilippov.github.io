fluid mechanics

1. NOUN

/ fluː.ɪd məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / ˈfluː.ɪd mɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics concerned with the motion and forces of liquids and gases
branch of physics studying liquids and gases

Examples

He is studying fluid mechanics as part of his engineering degree.
The textbook covers the fundamental principles of fluid mechanics.
Researchers in fluid mechanics analyze how water and air move under various conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrodynamics - study of liquids in motion
  • Synonym: aerodynamics - study of gases in motion, especially air

Etymology

From Latin 'fluidus' (flowing) and Greek 'mechanikē' (the art of contriving, mechanical science).
