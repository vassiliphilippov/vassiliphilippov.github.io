agglutinating activity

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡluːtəˌneɪtɪŋ ækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / əˌɡluːtəˌneɪtɪŋ ækˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the process by which small suspended particles join together to form larger clumps, often leading to precipitation from solution
process of clumping suspended particles

Examples

The antibody showed strong agglutinating activity against the bacteria in the sample.
Researchers measured the agglutinating activity to detect the presence of the virus.
Without sufficient agglutinating activity, the suspended particles remained evenly dispersed in the solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agglutination - the process of clumping together, especially in a biological or chemical context

Etymology

Agglutinating from Latin 'agglutinare' (to glue together); 'activity' from Latin 'activitas'.
