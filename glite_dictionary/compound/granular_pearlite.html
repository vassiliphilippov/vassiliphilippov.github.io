granular pearlite

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡræn.jə.lɚ ˈpɪrˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɡran.jʊ.lə ˈpɜː.laɪt / (BrE)
a microstructural form of steel or iron in which cementite appears in globules rather than in layers, typically resulting from very slow cooling
microstructure in slowly cooled steel

Examples

The slow cooling process caused granular pearlite to form within the steel's structure.
Microscopic examination revealed areas of granular pearlite interspersed with other phases.
Engineers noted that granular pearlite affects the hardness and toughness of iron alloys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearlite - a microstructural constituent of steel and cast iron, consisting of alternating layers of ferrite and cementite

Etymology

From the word 'granular' (grain-like) and 'pearlite,' a microstructural component of steel.
