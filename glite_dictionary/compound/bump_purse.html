to bump purse

1. VERB

/ bʌmp pɜrs / (AmE), / bʌmp pɜːs / (BrE)
to share or split expenses, especially by jointly paying for something; sometimes used metaphorically for romantic or social engagement through financial sharing
share or split expenses together

Examples

We usually bump purse when we grab coffee together after work.
Whenever they go traveling, the friends bump purse to cover accommodation costs.
After dinner, the couple decided to bump purse instead of letting one person pay.

Synset Examples
