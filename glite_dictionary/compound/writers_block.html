writer's block

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtərz blɑk / (AmE), / ˈraɪtəz blɒk / (BrE)
A temporary psychological condition in which a writer is unable to produce new work or experiences a creative slowdown.
inability to write due to psychological block

Examples

After finishing her first novel, she suffered from writer's block for months.
Many authors struggle with writer's block at some point in their careers.
He feared that his recent writer's block would delay his new book.

Synset Examples

he had writer's block: the words wouldn't come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - obstruction or hindrance, especially mental

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtərz ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈraɪtəz ˌblɒk / (BrE)
A temporary inability to produce written work, often due to a lack of inspiration or creative ideas.
inability to write due to lack of inspiration

Examples

Many novelists experience writer's block at some point in their careers.
She tried several techniques to overcome her writer's block, but nothing seemed to help.
After months of writer's block, he finally finished his manuscript.

Synset Examples

The poetastress struggled with writer's block today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - an obstruction or hindrance
