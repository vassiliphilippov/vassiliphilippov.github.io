Third Crusade

1. PROPN

/ θɝd kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / θɜːd kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
the Crusade from 1189 to 1192 led by Richard I of England and the king of France against Muslim forces in the Holy Land, ultimately unsuccessful due to internal conflict and strong opposition
military campaign (1189–1192) by European leaders in the Holy Land

Examples

The Third Crusade attempted to recapture Jerusalem after its fall to Saladin in 1187.
Despite initial successes, the Third Crusade ultimately failed to achieve its main objectives.
Many famous leaders, including Richard the Lionheart, took part in the Third Crusade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crusade - a series of medieval military expeditions launched by Europeans
