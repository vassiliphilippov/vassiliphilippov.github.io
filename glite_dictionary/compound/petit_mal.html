petit mal

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛti ˈmæl / (AmE), / ˌpɛti ˈmæl / (BrE)
A type of brief epileptic seizure characterized by a sudden, short lapse in consciousness, sometimes accompanied by minor muscle twitching.
brief epileptic seizure with loss of awareness
medical

Examples

During her petit mal, she stared blankly for a few seconds before continuing her sentence.
The doctor explained that a petit mal is less dramatic than other types of seizures.
After experiencing a petit mal, he often felt confused but quickly recovered.

Synset Examples

the girl was frightened by her first petit mal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absence seizure - Another term for brief lapses in consciousness, often used instead of 'petit mal' in modern medicine
  • Degree Higher: grand mal - A more severe epileptic seizure with convulsions and loss of consciousness

Etymology

From French 'petit mal', meaning 'little illness' or 'minor sickness'.
