Beaufort scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.fərt skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.fət skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale for measuring wind force, ranging from 0 (calm) to 12 (hurricane), used internationally
scale measuring wind force (0–12)

Examples

The weather report indicated a wind speed of force 7 on the Beaufort scale.
Sailors often consult the Beaufort scale to assess sea conditions before departure.
According to the Beaufort scale, a gentle breeze corresponds to force 3.

Etymology

Named after Sir Francis Beaufort, British naval officer who devised the scale in 1805.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊfərt skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊfət skeɪl / (BrE)
a system for estimating wind strength, ranging from 0 (calm) to 12 (hurricane), based on observed conditions at sea or on land
scale measuring wind force

Examples

According to the Beaufort scale, a wind speed of 20 knots is classified as a fresh breeze.
The storm reached a level 10 on the Beaufort scale, causing severe damage along the coast.
Meteorologists frequently refer to the Beaufort scale when describing wind conditions in weather reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind scale - scale for measuring wind force

Etymology

Named after Sir Francis Beaufort (1774–1857), British admiral and naval hydrographer, who devised the scale.
