blacktail deer

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌteɪl dɪr / (AmE), / ˈblækteɪl dɪə / (BrE)
A subspecies of mule deer found in western North America, known for its distinctive black tail.
a mule deer with a black tail, western North America

Examples

We spotted a blacktail deer grazing at the edge of the forest during our hike.
The distinctive dark coloring on the blacktail deer's tail makes it easy to identify.
Many hunters value the blacktail deer for its elusive nature and agility in dense woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mule deer - a large deer native to western North America with mule-like ears
