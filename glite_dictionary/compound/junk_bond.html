junk bond

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋk bɑnd / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk bɒnd / (BrE)
a bond with a low credit rating (BB or lower), carrying a high risk of default but offering higher yields; often issued by companies with questionable creditworthiness
high-risk, high-yield bond with low credit rating

Examples

Many investors are wary of investing in junk bonds due to their higher risk of default.
During the 1980s, junk bonds played a major role in financing corporate takeovers.
The company issued junk bonds to raise capital despite its poor credit rating.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: investment-grade bond - a bond with a high credit rating, considered safer
