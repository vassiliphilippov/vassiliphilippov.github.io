river bottom

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvər ˌbɑt̬əm / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˌbɒtəm / (BrE)
the ground at the lowest part of a river channel, especially where the river flows or once flowed
ground at the bottom of a river channel

Examples

The children found smooth stones on the river bottom during their hike.
Years of erosion had exposed ancient fossils in the dry river bottom.
The water was so clear that you could see fish swimming along the river bottom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riverbed - the bed or channel in which a river flows
