intermediate temporal artery

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmiːdiət ˈtɛmpərəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət ˈtɛmpərəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery in the temporal region of the brain that supplies blood to the middle part of the temporal lobe's cerebral cortex
artery serving the middle temporal cortex
medical

Examples

Damage to the intermediate temporal artery can affect memory functions in the brain.
The surgeon carefully avoided the intermediate temporal artery during the procedure.
Studies have mapped the blood flow of the intermediate temporal artery in patients with strokes.

Etymology

From 'intermediate' (from Latin intermedius, lying between), 'temporal' (from Latin temporalis, of the temples), and 'artery' (from Greek artēria).
