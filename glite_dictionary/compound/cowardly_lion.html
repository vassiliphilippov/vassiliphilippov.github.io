cowardly lion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊ.ɚd.li ˈlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.əd.li ˈlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A person who is timid or lacks courage, especially one expected to be brave.
timid or fearful person

Examples

After freezing during the crisis, Paul felt like a cowardly lion instead of a leader.
The coach called him a cowardly lion for not taking the critical shot.
She admitted she had been a cowardly lion to avoid telling the truth.

Synset Examples

He felt like a cowardly lion when he avoided confrontation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - A person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: hero - A person admired for courage

Etymology

Alludes to the Cowardly Lion, a character from L. Frank Baum's 'The Wonderful Wizard of Oz' (1900).
