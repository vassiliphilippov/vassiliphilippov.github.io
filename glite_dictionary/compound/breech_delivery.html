breech delivery

1. NOUN

/ briːtʃ dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / briːtʃ dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
the birth of a baby in which the feet or buttocks emerge before the head
childbirth with feet or buttocks first
medical

Examples

The doctor prepared for a breech delivery after noticing the baby's position on the ultrasound.
A breech delivery can increase the risks for both the mother and the baby.
Because of complications, an emergency cesarean section was performed during the breech delivery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cephalic delivery - delivery in which the head appears first

Etymology

From 'breech', meaning 'buttocks', and 'delivery', from Latin 'deliberare' (to set free).
