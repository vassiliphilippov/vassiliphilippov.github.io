calathian violet

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæð.i.ən ˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (AmE), / kəˈlɑː.θi.ən ˈvaɪ.əlɪt / (BrE)
A perennial Eurasian gentian with sky-blue, funnel-shaped flowers, typically found in damp, open heaths.
a Eurasian gentian with sky-blue flowers

Examples

The calathian violet blooms in profusion across the moorland every spring.
Botanists were delighted to discover several rare calathian violet plants during the field survey.
She pressed a calathian violet between the pages of her notebook as a keepsake from the trip.
