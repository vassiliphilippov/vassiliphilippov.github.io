intestinal artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈɑrˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a branch of the superior mesenteric artery that supplies blood to the ileum of the intestine
branch artery supplying the ileum
medical

Examples

During surgery, the intestinal artery was carefully identified to prevent accidental damage.
A blockage in the intestinal artery can severely impact blood flow to the ileum.
The intestinal artery branches off from the superior mesenteric artery.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
any of several arteries, branches of the superior mesenteric artery, that supply blood to the jejunum and other parts of the small intestine.
artery supplying the small intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the intestinal artery during the operation.
Blockage in an intestinal artery can lead to serious complications such as intestinal ischemia.
Anatomy students must learn the location of each main intestinal artery for their exams.

Etymology

From 'intestinal', relating to the intestine, and 'artery', from Greek artēria (windpipe, later blood vessel).
