dryland berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪˌlænd ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌlænd ˈbɛri / (BrE)
A low, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States that bears dark blue sweet berries.
shrub with dark blue sweet berries

Examples

The dryland berry is prized for its sweet, dark blue fruit in the summer.
We found a patch of dryland berries while hiking in the woods.
Wildlife often feed on the ripe dryland berry shrubs throughout the region.
