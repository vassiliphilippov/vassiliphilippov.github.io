first sergeant

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˌsɑrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
a noncommissioned officer in the Army above the rank of staff sergeant and below master sergeant, typically responsible for administrative duties within a unit
Army rank above staff sergeant

Examples

The first sergeant called the roll and gave out assignments for the day.
After years of service, she was promoted to first sergeant in her battalion.
The first sergeant is often seen as the backbone of a company in the Army.
