poor law

1. NOUN

/ pʊr lɔ / (AmE), / pɔː lɔː / (BrE)
a law providing relief or support to poor people, especially referring to historical laws in Britain governing assistance to the needy
law providing support for the poor
legal british

Examples

The English poor law system shaped social welfare policies for centuries.
Reform of the poor law in the 19th century aimed to improve conditions for the destitute.
Debate over the effectiveness of the poor law was common among Victorian politicians.
