carabao English

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrəˌbaʊ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkærəbəʊ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
A form of English characterized by nonstandard grammar, strong accent, or local vernacular, especially as spoken in the Philippines.
nonstandard English with Filipino accent

Examples

Some teachers find it challenging to communicate with students who only speak carabao English.
Her carabao English made native speakers smile with understanding.
He apologized for his carabao English before starting his speech.

Synset Examples

His carabao English made it hard to understand him.
Learning carabao English can be challenging for new students.
She laughed at his carabao English during the presentation.

Etymology

From 'carabao', a type of water buffalo in the Philippines, combined with 'English', implying a rough or nonstandard version of the language.
