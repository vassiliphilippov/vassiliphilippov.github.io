Neckar River

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛkɑr ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈnɛkaː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southwestern Germany, rising in the Black Forest and flowing north into the Rhine River
river in southwestern Germany

Examples

The Neckar River passes through several picturesque towns, including Heidelberg and Tübingen.
Many vineyards line the banks of the Neckar River, helping to produce some of Germany’s finest wines.
Tourists often enjoy taking boat cruises along the scenic Neckar River in the summer.

Etymology

Named after the Neckar, from Old High German 'Neckar', possibly of Celtic origin.
