barn swallow

1. NOUN

/ bɑrn ˈswɑloʊ / (AmE), / bɑːn ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
a common species of swallow (Hirundo rustica) found in North America and Europe, often nesting in barns and other structures
common species of swallow nesting in barns

Examples

A pair of barn swallows built their nest in the rafters of the old stable.
The barn swallow is easily recognized by its long, forked tail and agile flight.
Every spring, barn swallows return to the same farmhouse to raise their young.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: swallow - a small migratory songbird in the family Hirundinidae
