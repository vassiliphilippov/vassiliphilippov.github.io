order Cydippidea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sɪˌdɪpɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɪˌdɪpɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
An order of ctenophores characterized by having two long, feather-like tentacles.
order of ctenophores with long, feathered tentacles

Examples

The order Cydippidea includes ctenophores with characteristic long, feather-like tentacles.
Scientists discovered a new species within the order Cydippidea during their deep-sea expedition.
Members of the order Cydippidea are often studied for their unique feeding mechanisms.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'ordo' (order) and 'Cydippe', the type genus.
