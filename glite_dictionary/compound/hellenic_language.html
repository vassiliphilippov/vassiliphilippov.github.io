Hellenic language

1. NOUN

/ həˈlɛnɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɛˈlɛnɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the branch of the Indo-European language family that includes ancient and modern Greek
branch of languages including Greek

Examples

The Hellenic language group includes both ancient and modern forms of Greek.
Scholars often study changes within the Hellenic language over several centuries.
The Hellenic language is an important subject in the study of Indo-European linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - the main language included in the Hellenic language branch

Etymology

From Greek Hellēnikós (Greek, Hellenic), relating to Hellas (Greece).
