common osier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈoʊziɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈəʊziə / (BrE)
a species of willow (Salix viminalis) with long, flexible twigs used especially in basket-making
species of willow used in basketry

Examples

The baskets were expertly woven from common osier gathered near the riverbanks.
He studied traditional crafts and learned how to harvest and prepare common osier for weaving.
Many English wetlands are home to the common osier, prized for its flexible branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osier - willow with pliant branches used in weaving
  • Synonym: willow - deciduous tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Old French *osier* (willow twig), of uncertain origin.
