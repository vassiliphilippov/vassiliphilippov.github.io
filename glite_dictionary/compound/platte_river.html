Platte River

1. PROPN

/ ˌplæt ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌplæt ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in Nebraska, United States, flowing eastwards to join the Missouri River
river in Nebraska, U.S.

Examples

The Platte River played a crucial role in westward migration during the 19th century.
Migratory birds stop along the Platte River each spring, attracting many wildlife enthusiasts.
Flooding along the Platte River caused several communities to evacuate last year.

Etymology

From French 'platte' meaning 'flat', describing the river's broad, shallow character.
