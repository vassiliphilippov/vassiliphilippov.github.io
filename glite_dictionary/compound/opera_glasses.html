opera glasses

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑpərə ˌɡlæsɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒpərə ˌɡlɑːsɪz / (BrE)
A small pair of binoculars designed for use at theatrical performances to get a clearer view of the stage.
small binoculars for theater use

Examples

She pulled out her opera glasses to get a better look at the performers on stage.
He bought a vintage pair of opera glasses at the antique store.
From their seats in the balcony, the couple shared a set of opera glasses during the play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binoculars - an optical device with two lenses for viewing distant objects with both eyes

Etymology

From 'opera' (theatrical performance) + 'glasses' (binoculars), indicating their popular use at operas.
