cleft lip

1. NOUN

/ klɛft lɪp / (AmE), / klɛft lɪp / (BrE)
a congenital split or opening in the upper lip, present from birth
birth defect: split in upper lip
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed an operation to repair the baby's cleft lip.
Children born with a cleft lip often require ongoing medical care.
Support groups help parents understand the challenges of raising a child with a cleft lip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleft palate - a congenital split in the roof of the mouth

Etymology

From 'cleft' meaning a split or fissure, and 'lip'.
