family Uranoscopidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrəˌnɑːˈskɑːpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌjʊə.rə.nɒˈskɒ.pɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as stargazers, characterized by upward-facing eyes and mouths, found in temperate and tropical seas.
a family of marine stargazer fishes

Examples

The family Uranoscopidae includes marine fishes commonly known as stargazers.
Researchers studied feeding habits within the family Uranoscopidae for a recent marine biology paper.
Members of the family Uranoscopidae can deliver electric shocks to their prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stargazer - common name for fishes of the family Uranoscopidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Uranoscopidae', from Greek 'ouranos' (sky) + 'skopein' (to look), referencing their upward-gazing eyes.
