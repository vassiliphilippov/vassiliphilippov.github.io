War Department

1. PROPN

/ wɔr dɪˈpɑːrtmənt / (AmE), / wɔː dɪˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
the former executive department of the United States government responsible for military affairs, established in 1789 and merged into the Department of Defense in 1947
former U.S. government military department

Examples

The War Department played a crucial role during the Civil War.
In 1947, the War Department was reorganized into the Department of Defense.
Many historical documents can be traced to the War Department.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Department of Defense - the U.S. federal department formed by combining the War and Navy Departments in 1947
