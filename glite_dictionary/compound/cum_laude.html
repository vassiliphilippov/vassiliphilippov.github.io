cum laude

1. ADV

/ kʊm ˈlaʊdeɪ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈlaʊdi / (BrE)
(of an academic degree or distinction) with honor; used to indicate that a degree was obtained with academic distinction.
with academic honor or distinction

Examples

She graduated cum laude from the university, earning recognition for her hard work and dedication.
Many employers are impressed by candidates who received a cum laude distinction on their diploma.
He proudly wore his cum laude honor cords during the commencement ceremony.

Synset Examples

a cum laude graduate

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: magna cum laude - with great academic honor or distinction
  • Degree Higher: summa cum laude - with highest academic honor or distinction

Etymology

From Latin 'cum laude', meaning 'with praise'.

2. ADV

/ kʌm ˈlaʊdeɪ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈlaʊdi / (BrE)
With distinction; used to indicate that a degree was earned with academic honors, typically the lowest in a hierarchy of honors.
with academic distinction (lowest honors)

Examples

She graduated cum laude from one of the most prestigious universities in the country.
His thesis was so impressive that he was awarded his degree cum laude.
Only a small percentage of students achieve the honor of finishing cum laude.

Synset Examples

he graduated cum laude

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: magna cum laude - with great distinction (second-highest academic honors)
  • Degree Higher: summa cum laude - with highest distinction (top academic honors)

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'with praise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292590-r
Synset POS: r
