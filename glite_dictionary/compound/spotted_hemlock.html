spotted hemlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑt̬ɪd ˈhɛmlɑk / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
a tall biennial water hemlock of northeastern North America with purple-spotted stems and clusters of extremely poisonous tuberous roots resembling small sweet potatoes
a highly poisonous North American water hemlock

Examples

The spotted hemlock grows in wet meadows and marshes throughout the northeastern United States.
Farmers are warned to keep livestock away from spotted hemlock because of its toxic roots.
The purple-spotted stems help botanists identify spotted hemlock from other similar-looking plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hemlock - a group of extremely poisonous plants in the genus Cicuta
