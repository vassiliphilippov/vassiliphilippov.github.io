red wolf

1. NOUN

/ ɹɛd wʊlf / (AmE), / ɹɛd wʊlf / (BrE)
a rare, reddish-grey wolf native to the southeastern United States, known for its slender build and reddish fur
a rare North American wolf species

Examples

The red wolf is one of the most endangered canids in the world.
Wildlife biologists are working to reintroduce the red wolf to its native habitats.
We saw a documentary about the history and conservation of the red wolf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray wolf - a large wolf species found across North America and Eurasia
