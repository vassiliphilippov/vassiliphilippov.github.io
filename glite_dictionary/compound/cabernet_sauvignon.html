Cabernet Sauvignon

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæbərneɪ soʊˈviːnjən / (AmE), / ˌkæbɜːneɪ səʊˈviːnjɒ̃ / (BrE)
A variety of red wine, originally from the Bordeaux region, made from a particular black grape; also, the grape used to produce this wine.
type of red wine or grape variety

Examples

She ordered a glass of Cabernet Sauvignon to pair with her steak.
Many wine lovers consider Cabernet Sauvignon to be a classic choice for red wine.
The vineyard is famous for its rich, full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Merlot - another major variety of red wine grape
  • Antonym: Chardonnay - popular variety of white wine and grape

Etymology

From French, named after the grape varieties 'Cabernet' and 'Sauvignon'.
