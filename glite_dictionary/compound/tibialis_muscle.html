tibialis muscle

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈbaɪəlɪs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / tɪˈbeɪlɪs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
either of two muscles in the leg, originating from the tibia, involved in the movement of the foot
muscle in the leg involved in foot movement
medical

Examples

The tibialis muscle plays a crucial role in stabilizing the ankle during walking.
After his injury, the doctor examined his tibialis muscle for signs of strain.
Strengthening the tibialis muscle can help prevent shin splints in runners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tibialis anterior - muscle at the front of the lower leg, responsible for dorsiflexing the foot
  • Synonym: tibialis posterior - muscle at the back of the lower leg, responsible for inverting and plantarflexing the foot

Etymology

From Latin 'tibialis' (of the tibia), from 'tibia' (shin bone).
