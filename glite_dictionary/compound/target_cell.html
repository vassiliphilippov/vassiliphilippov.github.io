target cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡɪt sɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt sɛl / (BrE)
an abnormal red blood cell with a dark ring surrounding a dark center, seen in certain anemias
abnormal red blood cell with dark ring; anemia
medical

Examples

A blood smear revealed numerous target cells indicative of anemia.
The presence of target cells can help diagnose certain types of thalassemia.
In sickle cell disease, target cells may be seen alongside sickled erythrocytes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡət sɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that has specific receptors allowing it to respond to a particular antigen, hormone, drug, or other agent, or that is the focus of activity by a virus, immune cell, or nerve fiber
cell affected by a specific agent
medical

Examples

Insulin binds to target cells in muscle and fat tissue to regulate glucose uptake.
The virus enters its target cells by attaching to specific surface receptors.
Antibodies are designed to identify and bind to their target cell in the immune response.
