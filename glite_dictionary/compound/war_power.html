war power

1. NOUN

/ wɔr ˈpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / wɔː ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a special authority exercised by a government, especially the executive branch, during wartime that goes beyond normal peacetime powers
special government powers during war
legal

Examples

The president invoked war power provisions to respond rapidly to the international conflict.
Congress debated the limits of executive war power in light of the new military campaign.
During wartime, some civil liberties may be restricted under war power authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency powers - special powers granted to government during crises
