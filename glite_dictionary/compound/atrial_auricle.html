atrial auricle

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˈɔr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˈɔː.rɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a small, ear-shaped pouch projecting from the upper part of each atrium of the heart
ear-shaped projection of the atrium (heart)
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the atrial auricle for signs of abnormal growth.
Blood clots can sometimes form in the left atrial auricle of patients with atrial fibrillation.
Each heart has an atrial auricle that helps increase the capacity of the atrium.

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (entrance hall) and 'auricula' (little ear).
