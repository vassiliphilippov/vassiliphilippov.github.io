never-was

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛvɚˌwʌz / (AmE), / ˈnɛvəˌwɒz / (BrE)
a person or thing that has never achieved success or recognition
person or thing that never achieved success

Examples

Many critics consider him a never-was despite his early promise.
At the end of the show, he was dismissed as just another never-was.
That failing product quickly became a never-was in the tech world.

Synset Examples

He’s a never-was in the music industry.
That idea was a never-was and quickly forgotten.
She used to be famous, but now she’s a never-was.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: has-been - a person or thing formerly famous but now out of date or forgotten
