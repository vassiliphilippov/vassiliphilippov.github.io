order Myxobacteriales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ maɪksoʊˌbæktɪriˈeɪliz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌmɪksəʊˌbæktɪriˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of higher bacteria known for their complex life cycles and ability to form fruiting bodies
a bacterial taxonomic order

Examples

The order Myxobacteriales includes bacteria that move by gliding and are studied for their unique social behaviors.
Biologists have discovered new species in the order Myxobacteriales found in soil samples.
Research into the order Myxobacteriales has advanced our understanding of multicellular bacterial development.

Etymology

From Greek 'myxa' (slime) + 'bacterium' (rod) + Latin order ending '-ales'.
