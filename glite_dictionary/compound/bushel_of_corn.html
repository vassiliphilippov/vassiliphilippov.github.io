bushel of corn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃəl əv kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃəl əv kɔːn / (BrE)
a unit for measuring an amount of corn, equal to 8 gallons (about 36.4 liters) by dry measure in the US
unit of measure for corn

Examples

The farmer stored a bushel of corn in his barn for the winter.
Prices have increased for a bushel of corn this year due to drought.
She won a prize for guessing the exact weight of a bushel of corn at the fair.

Synset Examples

He bought a bushel of corn from the farmer's market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bushel - a unit of dry measure used in the US and UK
  • Degree Lower: peck - a quarter of a bushel, another measure for dry goods

Etymology

From 'bushel,' a measure from Old French 'boissel,' and 'corn', meaning grain.
