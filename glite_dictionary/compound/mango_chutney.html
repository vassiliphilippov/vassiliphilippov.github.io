mango chutney

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡoʊ ˈtʃʌtni / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəʊ ˈtʃʌtni / (BrE)
a sweet and spicy condiment made from mangoes, often including spices, vinegar, and sugar, typically served with Indian or South Asian dishes
sweet and spicy mango condiment

Examples

She served the grilled chicken with a generous spoonful of mango chutney on the side.
The recipe called for mango chutney to add sweetness and spice to the curry dish.
He spread mango chutney on his cheese sandwich for an exotic twist.

Synset Examples

I added mango chutney to my sandwich.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chutney - a spiced condiment of Indian origin, made with fruits or vegetables

Etymology

From Hindi 'chatni' (to lick) and 'mango'; 'chutney' entered English via Indian cuisine during British colonial rule.
