moth orchid

1. NOUN

/ mɔθ ˈɔrkɪd / (AmE), / mɒθ ˈɔːkɪd / (BrE)
An orchid of the genus Phalaenopsis, known for its large, flat flowers and broad, glossy leaves, often grown as a houseplant.
a type of orchid, genus Phalaenopsis

Examples

The moth orchid is a popular choice for home decorators because of its elegant flowers and easy care.
She kept a blooming moth orchid on her kitchen windowsill all year round.
Many gardeners admire the delicate beauty of the moth orchid during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phalaenopsis - genus of moth orchids
  • Degree Higher: orchid - plant of the family Orchidaceae

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its flowers to moths and from modern Latin Phalaenopsis (the genus name), from Greek phalaina 'moth' and -opsis 'appearance'.
