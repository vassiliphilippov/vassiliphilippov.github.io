great lobelia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪt loʊˈbiliə / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ləʊˈbiːlɪə / (BrE)
A tall, leafy perennial herb native to eastern North America, known for its dense spikes of blue flowers.
tall North American herb with blue flowers

Examples

The great lobelia flourishes in wet meadows and along stream banks throughout the summer.
Gardeners prize the great lobelia for its vibrant blue flowers and towering height.
Wildlife conservationists often plant great lobelia to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobelia - any plant of the genus Lobelia, often with blue or violet flowers
