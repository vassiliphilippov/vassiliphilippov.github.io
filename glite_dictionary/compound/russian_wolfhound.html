Russian wolfhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃən ˈwʊlfˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃ(ə)n ˈwʊlfˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
A tall, fast-moving breed of dog, also known as the Borzoi, originally bred in Russia for hunting wolves.
a tall, fast hound dog breed from Russia

Examples

The Russian wolfhound is known for its graceful appearance and athletic ability.
As a puppy, the Russian wolfhound was already taller than most other dogs in the park.
She chose a Russian wolfhound because of its gentle temperament and striking looks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Borzoi - another name for the Russian wolfhound

Etymology

From its origin in Russia and its historical use in wolf hunting; also known as 'Borzoi' from Russian 'borzaya' (swift).
