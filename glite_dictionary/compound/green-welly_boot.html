green-welly boot

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈwɛli buːt / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈwɛli bʊt / (BrE)
a waterproof rubber boot, typically green, worn in wet or muddy conditions and associated with country activities, especially in Britain
green waterproof rubber boot for wet conditions
british

Examples

Many people wore their green-welly boots to the countryside fair after the rain.
She bought new green-welly boots for her hiking trip in the Lake District.
His green-welly boots were covered in mud after walking the dog through the fields.

Synset Examples

Everyone admired her stylish green-welly boots at the event.
She wore her green-welly boots to the muddy festival.
His green-welly shoes were perfect for the countryside hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wellington boot - tall waterproof rubber boot for wet conditions

Etymology

From 'green' (color) + 'welly', a shortening of 'Wellington boot', named after the Duke of Wellington.
