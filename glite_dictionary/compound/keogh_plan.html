Keogh plan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiː.oʊ plæn / (AmE), / ˈkiː.əʊ plæn / (BrE)
A tax-deferred retirement savings plan available to self-employed people and employees of unincorporated businesses in the United States.
US retirement savings plan for self-employed
american

Examples

Many self-employed individuals choose a Keogh plan to save for retirement.
The company set up a Keogh plan to help partners defer their taxes.
Consult your accountant to see if a Keogh plan is the right option for you.

Etymology

Named after U.S. Representative Eugene Keogh, who sponsored the bill creating the plan in 1962.
