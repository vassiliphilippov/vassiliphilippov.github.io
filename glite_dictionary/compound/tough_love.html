tough love

1. NOUN

/ tʌf lʌv / (AmE), / tʌf lʌv / (BrE)
a stern but caring approach to discipline or support, intended to help someone improve their behavior or situation
firm but caring discipline or support

Examples

The teacher used tough love to help her students stay focused and motivated.
Sometimes, parents need to show tough love to guide their children through difficult times.
His coach's tough love approach helped him reach his full potential.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, tough love is necessary for growth.
She believes in tough love to help her children succeed.
The coach knows how to dish out tough love to his players.
His approach was tough love, not meant to hurt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discipline - training to act in accordance with rules; firm control or regulation
  • Antonym: leniency - softness or permissiveness in discipline

Etymology

Coined in the 20th century from 'tough' (firm, strict) and 'love' (affection, care), referring to the combination of strictness with genuine concern.
