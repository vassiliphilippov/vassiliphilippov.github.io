new territory

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈtɛrətɔri / (AmE), / njuː ˈtɛrɪtəri / (BrE)
an area that has been recently acquired, discovered, or explored, often unfamiliar or undeveloped
recently acquired or explored area

Examples

Scientists are eager to investigate the new territory they just mapped in the rainforest.
The company is expanding into new territory with its latest product line.
Moving to a foreign country was new territory for her, both exciting and intimidating.

Synset Examples

The settler built a home in the new territory.
