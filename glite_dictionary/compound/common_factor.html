common factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a number that divides two or more other numbers exactly, without leaving a remainder
number dividing two or more numbers exactly

Examples

The number 3 is a common factor of both 6 and 9.
To simplify the fraction, find the greatest common factor of the numerator and denominator.
Twelve has several common factors with eighteen, including 1, 2, 3, and 6.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: greatest common divisor - the largest common factor of two or more numbers
  • Synonym: factor - a number that divides another number exactly
