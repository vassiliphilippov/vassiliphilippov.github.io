contract under seal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtrækt ˌʌndər ˈsiːl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trækt ˌʌndə ˈsiːl / (BrE)
a formal contract signed and authenticated by the affixing of a seal, traditionally wax, giving it special legal status
formal contract authenticated by a seal
legal

Examples

In some jurisdictions, a contract under seal does not require consideration to be enforceable.
The purchase agreement was made as a contract under seal, giving it stronger legal force.
Historically, a contract under seal indicated the solemn intent of the parties involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deed - a legal document signed and delivered, especially one regarding ownership of property
