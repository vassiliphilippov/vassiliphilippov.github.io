agent bank

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənt ˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt ˌbaŋk / (BrE)
a bank appointed by a group of lenders to manage a syndicated loan and act on their behalf
bank managing syndicated loan

Examples

The agent bank coordinates communication between the borrower and the lenders in a syndicated loan.
When problems arose, the agent bank stepped in to resolve disputes among the participating banks.
Each member of the group relied on the agent bank to distribute payments correctly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: syndicate bank - a bank that is part of a lending syndicate without serving as the lead agent

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənt bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt baŋk / (BrE)
a bank that acts on behalf of another, typically foreign, bank in financial transactions or operations
bank acting for another bank

Examples

The foreign lender appointed an agent bank to facilitate transactions in the new market.
As an agent bank, it managed the loan payments and communicated with the syndicate.
Each agent bank is responsible for ensuring compliance with international banking regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correspondent bank - a bank that provides services on behalf of another bank, especially in another country
