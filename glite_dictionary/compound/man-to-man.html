man-to-man

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæn.təˈmæn / (AmE), / ˌmæn.təˈmæn / (BrE)
(of a system of play, especially in sport) in which each defensive player is responsible for guarding one specific opponent.
system of defense where each player guards an opponent

Examples

The coach preferred a man-to-man defense over a zone strategy for this game.
Our team switched from man-to-man coverage to zone in the second half.
Many players find man-to-man marking more challenging than playing in a zone defense.

Synset Examples

one-on-one defense

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zone defense - a system where each defender guards an area rather than a specific person

2. ADV

/ ˌmæn tə ˈmæn / (AmE), / ˌmæn tə ˈmæn / (BrE)
in a straightforward, honest, or frank manner, especially in private between two people
in a direct and honest manner

Examples

Let's discuss this man-to-man and sort out our differences.
He spoke man-to-man with his son about responsibility.
They wanted a man-to-man conversation to clear the air.

Synset Examples

we must talk man-to-man

Etymology

Originally from sports, where 'man-to-man' refers to a style of defense; later extended to direct, person-to-person interaction.
