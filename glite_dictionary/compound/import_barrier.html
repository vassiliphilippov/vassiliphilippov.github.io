import barrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪm.pɔrt ˈbæɹ.i.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pɔːt ˈbæɹ.i.ə / (BrE)
a regulation or policy that restricts the import of goods from other countries
restriction on imported goods

Examples

The government introduced an import barrier to protect domestic industries from foreign competition.
Many economists argue that removing every import barrier would benefit consumers.
An import barrier such as a quota can limit the quantity of goods entering a country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade barrier - a government-imposed restraint on the flow of international goods or services
