white-telephone farce

1. NOUN

/ waɪt tɛləˌfoʊn fɑrs / (AmE), / waɪt tɛlɪˌfəʊn fɑːs / (BrE)
a genre of light, sophisticated comedy, especially in Italian film of the 1930s and 1940s, featuring upper-class settings and decor, often characterized by the presence of white telephones as a symbol of luxury
light comedy genre in Italian film/theater

Examples

The festival features a screening of a rare white-telephone farce from pre-war Italy.
He specializes in directing white-telephone farces that evoke the glamour of a bygone era.
Critics praised the film as a successful revival of the white-telephone farce genre.

Synset Examples

The movie was a classic white-telephone farce from the 70s.

Etymology

Refers to the white telephones used as a symbol of affluence in Italian films of the era.
