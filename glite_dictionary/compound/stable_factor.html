stable factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪbəl ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəl ˈfaktə / (BrE)
a coagulation factor produced in the kidney under the influence of vitamin K, also known as Factor VII, important in blood clotting
blood coagulation factor (Factor VII)
medical

Examples

A deficiency of stable factor can result in serious bleeding disorders.
Doctors measured the level of stable factor during the blood coagulation test.
Vitamin K is necessary for the synthesis of stable factor in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Factor VII - another name for stable factor, a blood coagulation protein
  • Degree Lower: labile factor - another blood coagulation factor, also known as Factor V
