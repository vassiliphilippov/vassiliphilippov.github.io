far cry

1. NOUN

/ fɑr kraɪ / (AmE), / fɑː kraɪ / (BrE)
a very great difference from something; a situation that is significantly different from another
a great difference

Examples

This new version is a far cry from the original design.
Her calm reaction was a far cry from her usual anger.
The city now is a far cry from what it was twenty years ago.

Synset Examples

it was a far cry from what he had expected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worlds apart - completely different or opposite
  • Antonym: similar - having a resemblance or likeness
