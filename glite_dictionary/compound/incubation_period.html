incubation period

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪŋ.kjuˈbeɪ.ʃən ˌpɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˌɪŋ.kjuːˈbeɪ.ʃən ˌpɪə.ri.əd / (BrE)
the time between being infected with a disease and the appearance of symptoms
time between infection and symptoms
medical

Examples

The incubation period for the flu is typically one to four days after exposure.
Understanding the incubation period helps doctors determine when a person became infected.
Shortening the incubation period can make it more difficult to control outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latent period - period between infection and infectiousness

Etymology

From Latin incubatio (brooding, hatching) and periodus (cycle, period).
