founding father

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndɪŋ ˈfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndɪŋ ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
a man who establishes an important institution, organization, or nation, especially one regarded as a key originator or leader
an important male founder of an institution or nation

Examples

Many people consider Benjamin Franklin a founding father of the United States.
As a founding father of the university, his influence is still felt today.
She wrote a biography of the founding father of that charitable organization.

Synset Examples

George Washington is the father of his country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: founder - a person who establishes an institution or organization
