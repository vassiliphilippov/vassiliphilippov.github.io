clash of the ash

1. NOUN

/ klæʃ əv ði æʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ əv ði æʃ / (BrE)
a hurling match or a contest, especially referring to the sound of hurleys (traditionally made of ash wood) striking together; often used to describe hurling games in Ireland
hurling match (especially Irish context)

Examples

The annual final is always a much-anticipated clash of the ash in the county.
Fans gathered in the stadium for an exciting clash of the ash between fierce rivals.
Growing up in Ireland, he dreamed of playing in a clash of the ash at Croke Park.

Synset Examples

The play depicted a clash of the ash between two rival clans.
The documentary highlighted the clash of the ash in the community.
We witnessed a clash of the ash during the festival.

Etymology

Refers to hurleys (sticks used in hurling) traditionally made of ash wood, and the sound when they strike together.
