daisy wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪzi ˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi ˌwiːl / (BrE)
A wheel-shaped printing element with characters arranged around its edge, used in some typewriters and printers to produce text on paper.
wheel-shaped printer element

Examples

The daisy wheel in the old typewriter needed to be replaced after years of use.
Early word processors often used daisy wheel technology to achieve higher print quality.
He was fascinated by the intricate design of the daisy wheel in his first office printer.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a daisy flower, with characters radiating from the center like petals.
