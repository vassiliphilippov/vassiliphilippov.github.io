great cerebral vein

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt səˈribɹəl veɪn / (AmE), / ɡreɪt səˈriː.brəl veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein in the brain formed by the union of the two internal cerebral veins, which drains into the straight sinus
large vein in the brain
medical

Examples

A blockage in the great cerebral vein can lead to serious neurological complications.
During the brain surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the great cerebral vein.
The great cerebral vein is sometimes referred to by its Latin name, vein of Galen.
