Quonset hut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.sət hʌt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.sɪt hʌt / (BrE)
A prefabricated building with a semicircular cross section made of corrugated metal, originally developed for military use.
semicircular prefabricated metal building

Examples

During the war, soldiers were housed in Quonset huts on the base.
The company stored equipment in an old Quonset hut behind the warehouse.
After renovation, the Quonset hut was transformed into a trendy office space.

Etymology

From Quonset Point, Rhode Island, USA, where such huts were first manufactured during World War II.
