Venus's flytrap

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnəs ɪz ˈflaɪˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs ɪz ˈflaɪˌtræp / (BrE)
a small carnivorous plant native to the Carolinas, with hinged leaves that snap shut to trap insects for food
a carnivorous plant with snapping leaves

Examples

A Venus's flytrap can close its leaves in less than a second after sensing an insect.
He bought a Venus's flytrap from the garden center for his classroom science project.
The unique mechanism of the Venus's flytrap fascinates botanists and students alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher plant - a different type of carnivorous plant that traps insects in a pitfall trap

Etymology

Named after Venus, the Roman goddess of love, and its characteristic trapping action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈviːnəsɪz ˈflaɪˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈviːnəsɪz ˈflaɪˌtræp / (BrE)
a carnivorous plant native to the coastal plains of the Carolinas, with hinged leaf blades that snap shut to trap insects.
carnivorous plant with trapping leaves

Examples

The Venus's flytrap closes its leaves rapidly when an insect touches its sensitive hairs.
Many gardeners try to grow a Venus's flytrap as a unique houseplant.
In its natural habitat, the Venus's flytrap thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aldrovanda - an aquatic carnivorous plant with trapping leaves

Etymology

Named after the Roman goddess Venus, symbolizing attraction, and 'flytrap' referring to its method of catching prey.
