sex characteristic

1. NOUN

/ sɛks ˌkærəktəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / sɛks ˌkarɪktəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
a physical or psychological trait that is strongly associated with being male or female rather than the other sex
trait associated with one sex
medical

Examples

Puberty leads to the development of various sex characteristics such as breast growth and facial hair.
Doctors may examine internal and external sex characteristics when determining a patient's biological sex.
Psychological sex characteristics, like gender identity, can differ greatly from individual to individual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secondary sex characteristic - physical traits that develop during puberty distinguishing the sexes
