worm snake

1. NOUN

/ wɜrm sneɪk / (AmE), / wɜːm sneɪk / (BrE)
A small, slender, wormlike snake of the eastern United States, typically reddish or brown, often found under stones or logs.
small wormlike snake of eastern US

Examples

We lifted a rock and discovered a worm snake wriggling beneath it.
Because of its appearance, the worm snake is often mistaken for an earthworm by gardeners.
The worm snake spends most of its life burrowing in loose soil and leaf litter.

2. NOUN

/ wɜrm sneɪk / (AmE), / wɜːm sneɪk / (BrE)
A small, slender burrowing snake resembling a worm, typically found in warm regions and having vestigial eyes.
small burrowing snake with vestigial eyes

Examples

The worm snake is often mistaken for an earthworm because of its size and shape.
We found a worm snake hiding under a rock in the garden.
Unlike most snakes, the worm snake spends most of its life burrowing underground.
