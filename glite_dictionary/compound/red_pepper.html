red pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
a type of hot, usually long and thin red chili pepper, either fresh or dried, used to flavor food
hot red chili pepper used in food

Examples

She added some chopped red pepper to give the soup a spicy kick.
The market had stalls piled high with dried red peppers of every size.
Avoid touching your eyes after handling red pepper, as it can cause irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - a hot-tasting red or green fruit of a variety of capsicum, used as a pungent spice in cooking
  • Antonym: bell pepper - a mild red, yellow, or green pepper used as a vegetable

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A pungent powder made from the dried, ground pods and seeds of Capsicum peppers, used as a spice in cooking.
pungent ground Capsicum spice

Examples

She sprinkled a pinch of red pepper onto her pizza for extra heat.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of ground red pepper to enhance the flavor.
I prefer using red pepper instead of black pepper in spicy dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cayenne pepper - hot red powder made from cayenne chilies
  • Antonym: black pepper - pungent spice made from Piper nigrum berries
