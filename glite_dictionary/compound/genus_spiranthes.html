genus Spiranthes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs spaɪˈrænθiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs spaɪˈranθiːz / (BrE)
A large, widespread genus of terrestrial orchids characterized by white flowers.
genus of white-flowered terrestrial orchids

Examples

Several species within the genus Spiranthes are commonly known as ladies' tresses.
Botanists recently discovered a new orchid belonging to the genus Spiranthes in the region.
The unique spiral arrangement of flowers is characteristic of the genus Spiranthes.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'spira' (coil) and Greek 'anthos' (flower), referencing the spiral arrangement of the flowers.
