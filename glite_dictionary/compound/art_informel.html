Art Informel

1. NOUN

/ ɑrt ɛn.fɔrˈmɛl / (AmE), / ɑːt ɛ̃.fɔːˈmɛl / (BrE)
A style of painting originating in France in the 1950s, characterized by irregular, spontaneous applications of color, and comparable to abstract expressionism.
1950s French abstract painting style

Examples

Several notable painters in postwar Paris became leading figures in Art Informel.
The exhibition features extraordinary works emblematic of the Art Informel movement.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of Art Informel on contemporary European art.

Synset Examples

Around 1945 a number of European and American painters established a crucial phase in modern art known broadly as Art Informel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract expressionism - A style of painting developed in the United States in the 1940s and 1950s characterized by gestural brush-strokes or mark-making.

Etymology

From French, literally 'informal art'; 'art' + 'informel' (informal).
