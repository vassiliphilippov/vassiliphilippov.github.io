buffer storage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfɚ ˈstɔɹɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
(computer science) a section of RAM used for temporarily holding data while it is being transferred between computer components or devices.
temporary storage area in RAM (computing)

Examples

The printer's buffer storage helped prevent data loss during the large document transfer.
If the buffer storage fills up, new data may be delayed until space becomes available.
Improving the buffer storage can enhance the system's data transfer efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffer - memory area for temporary data storage (computing)
