compost pile

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmpoʊst paɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpɒst paɪl / (BrE)
a heap of decaying organic material, such as vegetation and manure, used to produce compost for fertilizing soil
heap of organic material for composting

Examples

The gardener turned the compost pile regularly to help it decompose more quickly.
Kitchen scraps and yard waste were added to the compost pile in the backyard.
A well-maintained compost pile can significantly reduce household waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compost heap - a heap of organic material for composting; synonymous with 'compost pile'

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.poʊst paɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pɒst paɪl / (BrE)
a heap of decomposing organic matter, such as leaves and food scraps, used to make compost
heap of decomposing organic matter

Examples

She turned the compost pile to aerate it and speed up decomposition.
A well-maintained compost pile produces rich soil for the garden.
The compost pile began to steam on the cold morning.

Synset Examples

He found white maggots in the compost pile.
