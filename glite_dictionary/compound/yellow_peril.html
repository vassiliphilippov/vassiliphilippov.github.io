yellow peril

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈpɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈpɛrəl / (BrE)
a dated and offensive term referring to a supposed threat posed to Western civilization by East Asian peoples or nations
racist term for threat from Asians
offensive archaic

Examples

Media coverage in the early 20th century often invoked the yellow peril as a reason for restrictive immigration laws.
The notion of the yellow peril fueled widespread discrimination against Asian communities in Western countries.
Historians now recognize that the yellow peril stereotype was rooted in fear and racial prejudice.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈpɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈpɛrəl / (BrE)
an outdated and racist ideology and term referring to the fear or perceived threat of East Asian peoples to the West, especially relating to immigration and cultural influence
racist ideology fearing Asian immigration
offensive

Examples

The idea of the yellow peril was widely discussed in newspapers during the early 20th century.
Some scholars analyze how yellow peril shaped Western perceptions of Asian countries.
The politician's comments were criticized as echoing yellow peril stereotypes.

Synset Examples

The term yellow peril reflects historical racial fears.
He studied the impact of yellow peril on immigration policies.
Yellow peril rhetoric has been largely debunked today.

Etymology

Phrase originating in late 19th-century English, reflecting Western anxieties about East Asian immigration.
