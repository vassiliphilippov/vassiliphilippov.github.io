zygomatic bone

1. NOUN

/ zaɪɡəˈmætɪk boʊn / (AmE), / zaɪɡəˈmætɪk bəʊn / (BrE)
the bone forming the prominence of the cheek and part of the orbit beneath the eye
bone forming the cheek prominence
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully realigned the zygomatic bone after the accident.
Fracture of the zygomatic bone can cause alterations in facial appearance.
The prominent zygomatic bone gives her face a striking structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheekbone - the bone forming the prominence of the cheek
  • Degree Lower: maxilla - upper jaw bone in vertebrates

Etymology

From Greek zygōma (yoke, bone of the cheek) + -ic, referring to its position and shape.
