terrestrial dynamical time

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɛstriəl daɪˈnæmɪkəl taɪm / (AmE), / təˈrɛstrɪəl daɪˈnæmɪkəl taɪm / (BrE)
(astronomy) a measure of time based on Earth's orbital motion, representing mean solar time corrected for variations in Earth's movements.
astronomical time scale based on Earth's motion

Examples

Modern astronomers use terrestrial dynamical time to calculate precise planetary positions.
The introduction of terrestrial dynamical time improved the accuracy of astronomical observations.
Terrestrial dynamical time was eventually replaced by terrestrial time in astronomical calculations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial time - modern astronomical time standard based on Earth's orbit
