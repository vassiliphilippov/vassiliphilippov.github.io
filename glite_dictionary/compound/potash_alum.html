potash alum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tæʃ ˈæl.əm / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.tæʃ ˈæl.əm / (BrE)
a white crystalline substance, the potassium double sulfate of aluminum, used in dyeing, water purification, and as an astringent
potassium aluminum sulfate compound

Examples

The chemist demonstrated the properties of potash alum by dissolving it in water.
In traditional textile dyeing, potash alum is often used as a mordant.
Adding potash alum to the water helped clarify the solution during purification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alum - a class of double sulfates of aluminum used for various purposes

Etymology

From 'potash,' referring to potassium, and 'alum,' from Latin 'alumen' (bitter salt).
