Rhodesian ridgeback

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈdiːʒən ˈrɪdʒˌbæk / (AmE), / rəʊˈdiːʒən ˈrɪdʒˌbæk / (BrE)
a powerful, short-haired breed of African hunting dog known for the distinctive ridge of hair that grows along its back in the opposite direction to the rest of the coat
breed of African hunting dog with a back ridge

Examples

The Rhodesian ridgeback is known for its loyalty and distinctive ridged spine.
A Rhodesian ridgeback can be an excellent companion for active families.
The hunter relied on his Rhodesian ridgeback to help track game in the African bush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridgeback - breed of dog with a ridge of hair along the back

Etymology

Named after Rhodesia (now Zimbabwe), where the breed was developed, and for the ridged hair on its back.
