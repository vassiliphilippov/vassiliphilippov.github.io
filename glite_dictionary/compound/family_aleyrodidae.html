family Aleyrodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæliəˈrɒdəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˌæliəˈrəʊdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, sap-sucking insects known as whiteflies, often pests of various plants.
taxonomic family of whiteflies (insects)

Examples

The greenhouse tomato plants were infested with family Aleyrodidae, damaging their growth.
Many insecticides are formulated specifically to target pests from family Aleyrodidae.
Entomologists study family Aleyrodidae to better understand agricultural pest control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefly - A small, white-winged sap-sucking insect, pest to many plants

Etymology

From Aleyrodidae, the scientific family name, derived from Greek 'aleyron' (flour), referring to whiteflies' powdery wings.
