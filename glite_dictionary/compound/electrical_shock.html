electrical shock

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ʃɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ʃɒk / (BrE)
a sudden physical reaction caused by electric current passing through the body, usually resulting in pain or muscle contraction
injury from electric current

Examples

He suffered an electrical shock while repairing the appliance.
The warning signs indicate a risk of electrical shock if touched.
Proper insulation can prevent accidental electrical shock in the workplace.

Synset Examples

subjects received a small electric shock when they made the wrong response
electricians get accustomed to occasional shocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric shock - a sudden jolt caused by electricity passing through the body; variant term
