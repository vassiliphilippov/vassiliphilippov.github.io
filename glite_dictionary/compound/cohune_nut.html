cohune nut

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈhuːn nʌt / (AmE), / kəˈhuːn nʌt / (BrE)
the large nut of the cohune palm, with a hard white shell similar to ivory nuts, used for oil and sometimes as a substitute for ivory
nut of the cohune palm tree

Examples

The oil extracted from the cohune nut is used in cooking and soap making.
Local artisans carved buttons from the hard shell of the cohune nut.
The cohune nut is prized for both its edible kernel and durable shell.
