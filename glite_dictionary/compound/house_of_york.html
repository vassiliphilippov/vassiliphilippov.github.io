House of York

1. PROPN

/ haʊs əv jɔrk / (AmE), / haʊs əv jɔːk / (BrE)
the English royal house, a branch of the Plantagenet dynasty, that ruled England from 1461 to 1485, whose emblem was a white rose
English royal house (1461–1485, white rose emblem)

Examples

The House of York played a major role in the Wars of the Roses.
Richard III was the last king from the House of York.
The emblem of the House of York was a white rose.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Plantagenet - an English royal dynasty preceding the House of York
  • Antonym: House of Lancaster - rival English royal house during the Wars of the Roses
