Chemical Weapons Convention

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈwɛpənz kənˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈwɛpənz kənˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
an international treaty that prohibits the development, production, acquisition, stockpiling, transfer, and use of chemical weapons
international treaty banning chemical weapons

Examples

Many countries signed the Chemical Weapons Convention to prevent the spread of dangerous toxins.
The Chemical Weapons Convention came into force in 1997, marking a milestone in disarmament history.
Compliance with the Chemical Weapons Convention is monitored by the Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical weapon - a weapon that uses chemicals designed to inflict harm or death
