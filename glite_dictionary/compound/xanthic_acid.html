xanthic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈzænθɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈzanθɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
Any of a class of unstable organic acids containing sulfur, used chiefly in organic chemistry.
unstable sulfur-containing organic acid
medical

Examples

The laboratory was equipped to handle unstable substances like xanthic acid.
Researchers studied the properties of xanthic acid to understand its chemical behavior.
Certain reactions involve xanthic acid as an intermediate compound.

Etymology

From Greek 'xanthos' (yellow), referring to the yellow color of some derivatives.
