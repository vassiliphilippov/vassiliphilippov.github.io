accounts receivable

1. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊnts rɪˈsivəbəl / (AmE), / əˈkaʊnts rɪˈsiːvəbəl / (BrE)
money owed to a business by its customers for goods or services that have been delivered but not yet paid for
money owed to a business by customers

Examples

Our company's accounts receivable increased significantly after we extended credit to several new clients.
The finance team prepares an accounts receivable report at the end of each month.
Managing accounts receivable efficiently helps maintain healthy cash flow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accounts payable - money a company owes to its suppliers

Etymology

From 'account', meaning a record of financial transactions, and 'receivable', meaning able to be received.
