tuba root

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːbə ruːt / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbə ruːt / (BrE)
A woody vine with bright green leaves and clusters of rose-tinted white flowers; its swollen roots contain rotenone, used as a fish poison and insecticide.
plant root with rotenone, used as poison

Examples

The villagers used tuba root to stun fish in the river for easier catching.
Scientists extract rotenone from tuba root as a natural insecticide.
You should handle tuba root carefully because it contains toxic compounds.

Etymology

From Tagalog and other Philippine languages 'tuba' meaning 'juice' or 'sap.'
