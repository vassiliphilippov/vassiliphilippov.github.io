knuckle joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌkəl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈnʌkəl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A type of mechanical joint that allows movement in only one plane, typically used to connect two rods or bars.
mechanical joint allowing single-plane movement

Examples

The knuckle joint in the device allows the lever to move up and down efficiently.
Engineers chose a knuckle joint to connect the two shafts because of its flexibility in one direction.
A knuckle joint must be properly lubricated to prevent wear from repeated motion.

Etymology

From 'knuckle', denoting a protruding joint, + 'joint', from Latin 'junctio' (joining).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʌkəl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈnʌkəl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a joint of the finger, especially any of those visible when the fist is closed
joint of the finger (when fist is closed)

Examples

He cracked his knuckle joint absentmindedly while waiting for the meeting to start.
The boxer suffered a minor injury to his right knuckle joint after the match.
Arthritis can sometimes cause swelling and pain in the knuckle joints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knuckle - the prominent joint of a finger, especially where it joins the hand

Etymology

From Middle English 'knokel' (knuckle) and 'joint' (from Old French 'jointe', from Latin 'iunctio')
