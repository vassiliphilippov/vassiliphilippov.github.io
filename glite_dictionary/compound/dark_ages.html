Dark Ages

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk ˈeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / dɑːk ˈeɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
the period of European history between the decline of the Roman Empire and the beginning of the Renaissance, often characterized as a time of cultural and economic deterioration
historical period after the fall of Rome and before the Renaissance

Examples

Many historians now reconsider the idea that the Dark Ages lacked cultural progress.
During the Dark Ages, much classical knowledge was preserved by monasteries.
The invention of the printing press marked the transition out of the Dark Ages in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Middle Ages - the period of European history between the fall of Rome and the Renaissance

Etymology

From the notion of a 'dark' period following the classical era, first used in the 14th century and popularized in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ dɑrk ˈeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / dɑːk ˈeɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
the period in European history from the fall of the Roman Empire to the beginning of the Middle Ages, traditionally considered as marked by cultural and economic decline
early medieval period of cultural decline

Examples

Many historians believe the printing press ended the Dark Ages in Europe.
During the Dark Ages, scientific and literary progress slowed significantly.
Some scholars argue that the term Dark Ages is misleading and outdated.

Synset Examples

The invention of the printing press ended the Dark Ages.
Many historians refer to the 500s as the Dark Ages.
Art and culture suffered during the Dark Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Middle Ages - the period of European history from the 5th to the late 15th century

Etymology

From 'dark' (suggesting lack of enlightenment) and 'ages' (period of time).
