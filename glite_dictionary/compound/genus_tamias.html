genus Tamias

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈteɪ.mi.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈteɪ.mi.as / (BrE)
A genus of chipmunks native to eastern North America, comprising several species.
genus of eastern North American chipmunks

Examples

The genus Tamias is known for its members' distinctive cheek pouches used to transport food.
Taxonomists have debated the classification within the genus Tamias for many years.
Only one species of genus Tamias is commonly found in the eastern forests of the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - small striped rodent of North America

Etymology

From Latin 'Tamias', used as a generic name in zoological classification.
