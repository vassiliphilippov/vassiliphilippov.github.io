prop root

1. NOUN

/ prɑp ruːt / (AmE), / prɒp ruːt / (BrE)
a root that grows from a stem above the ground and provides support, as found in plants like mangroves and corn
a supporting root growing from a stem

Examples

Mangrove trees are well known for their dense network of prop roots that stabilize them in muddy soil.
The corn plant developed several prop roots to help anchor it during strong winds.
Without its prop roots, the tall tree would be much more likely to topple in a storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerial root - a root that grows above the ground, not necessarily for support
