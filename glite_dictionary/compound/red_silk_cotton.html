red silk cotton

1. NOUN

/ rɛd sɪlk ˈkɑtn̩ / (AmE), / rɛd sɪlk ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
a fiber obtained from the red silk-cotton tree of eastern India, regarded as inferior to kapok
fiber from the red silk-cotton tree

Examples

The stuffing inside the pillow was made of red silk cotton.
Because red silk cotton is less resilient than kapok, it is rarely used for life jackets.
Villagers harvested red silk cotton annually from the native trees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kapok - a silky fiber from a tropical tree, used for stuffing
