clog dance

1. NOUN

/ klɑɡ dæns / (AmE), / klɒɡ dɑːns / (BrE)
A traditional dance performed while wearing shoes with wooden soles, featuring heavy stamping steps.
traditional dance with wooden-soled shoes

Examples

The group performed a clog dance at the local folk festival.
She learned the intricate steps of the clog dance from her grandmother.
During the parade, the sound of wooden shoes echoed through the streets as the clog dance began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap dance - A dance performed in shoes fitted with metal plates, making a tapping sound
  • Antonym: ballet - A highly technical dance form with its own vocabulary

Etymology

From 'clog', meaning a type of wooden-soled shoe, and 'dance'.
