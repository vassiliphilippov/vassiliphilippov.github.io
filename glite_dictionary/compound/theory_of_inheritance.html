theory of inheritance

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiəri əv ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (BrE)
a scientific theory explaining how traits and characteristics are passed from one generation to the next, especially in living organisms
biological theory about genetic inheritance

Examples

Mendel's work laid the foundation for the modern theory of inheritance.
The theory of inheritance has evolved significantly since the discovery of DNA.
Debates over the theory of inheritance were common among scientists in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heredity - the passing on of traits from parents to offspring
  • Synonym: genetics - the scientific study of heredity and genes
