locust pod

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəst pɑd / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəst pɒd / (BrE)
the long seed pod of a locust tree, containing small beans and sweet edible pulp, used as animal feed or a chocolate substitute
long, sweet pod from the locust tree

Examples

The farmer collected locust pods to feed the goats during the winter.
Some health food stores sell locust pod flour as a cocoa alternative.
Children enjoyed chewing the sweet pulp from the locust pod after school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob pod - pod from the carob tree, used similarly as a chocolate substitute
