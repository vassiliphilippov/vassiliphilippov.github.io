corozo palm

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊzoʊ pɑm / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊzəʊ pɑːm / (BrE)
any of several tropical American palm trees that bear corozo nuts, often used for buttons and other products
tropical American palm with edible nuts

Examples

The corozo palm is widely harvested for its nuts, which are used to make eco-friendly buttons.
Many local crafts in Colombia are made from the fruit of the corozo palm.
The corozo palm thrives in the humid tropics of Central and South America.

Etymology

From Spanish 'corozo', referring to the fruit of the palm.
