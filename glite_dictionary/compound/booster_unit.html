booster unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːstər ˌjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈbuːstə ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
the first stage of a multistage rocket, designed to provide the initial thrust for launch.
first stage of a multistage rocket

Examples

The booster unit separated after liftoff as planned, allowing the main rocket to continue into orbit.
Engineers checked the booster unit for any signs of damage after the test flight.
Each booster unit is designed to burn for just a few minutes before detaching from the main stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booster - a rocket engine or device providing additional thrust at launch
