mutual affection

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːtʃuəl əˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəl əˈfɛkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a feeling of fondness or sympathy that is shared equally by two people or groups
shared fondness or sympathy between people

Examples

Their mutual affection grew stronger as they spent more time together.
The story depicts the mutual affection between a teacher and her students.
Years of collaboration led to a deep mutual affection and respect among the scientists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
