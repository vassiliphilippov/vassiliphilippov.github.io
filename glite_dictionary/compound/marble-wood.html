marble-wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrbəl wʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːb(ə)l wʊd / (BrE)
A large Asiatic tree (Diospyros malabarica) known for its hard wood with a marbled, zebra-like appearance.
Asiatic tree with marbled hard wood

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from rare marble-wood, giving it a unique striped pattern.
In some regions, marble-wood is prized for use in decorative furniture and flooring.
Experts can identify marble-wood by the distinctive markings in its grain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrbəl ˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəl ˌwʊd / (BrE)
a hard wood characterized by a marbled or mottled appearance, often used in fine woodworking and furniture making
hard wood with marbled appearance

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from rare marble-wood prized for its striking patterns.
Collectors often seek out antique desks made with marble-wood due to their durability and beauty.
The table's marble-wood finish gave it a luxurious and unique look.

Etymology

Compound of 'marble' (referring to its marbled appearance) and 'wood'.
