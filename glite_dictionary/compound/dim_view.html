dim view

1. NOUN

/ dɪm vju / (AmE), / dɪm vjuː / (BrE)
an unfavorable or disapproving opinion or attitude toward someone or something
disapproving opinion or attitude

Examples

The principal took a dim view of the students' prank and promised consequences.
Historically, society has had a dim view of such unconventional lifestyles.
Many critics have a dim view of the film, citing its lack of originality.

Synset Examples

She takes a dim view of his constant lateness.
The manager takes a dim view of unprofessional behavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to approve - to think that someone or something is good or acceptable
