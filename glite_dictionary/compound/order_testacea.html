order Testacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər tɛˈseɪʃiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə tɛˈseɪsiə / (BrE)
A former taxonomic group of single-celled amoeboid protozoans that produce shells, especially studied within the Rhizopoda.
group of shelled amoeboid protozoans

Examples

The order Testacea includes amoeboid organisms that build protective shells.
Scientists once classified many freshwater protozoa under the order Testacea.
Research on the order Testacea has helped illuminate the evolution of modern amoebae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Testacea - main type genus or members of order Testacea
  • Degree Higher: Rhizopoda - larger group to which these shelled protozoans were once assigned

Etymology

From Latin testa (shell) + scientific classification.
