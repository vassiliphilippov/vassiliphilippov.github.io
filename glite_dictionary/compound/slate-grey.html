slate-grey

1. ADJ

/ sleɪt ɡreɪ / (AmE), / sleɪt ɡreɪ / (BrE)
of a gray color resembling that of slate; a dark, bluish-grey tint
dark bluish-grey color

Examples

Heavy clouds gave the morning sky a slate-grey hue.
She chose a slate-grey suit for the formal event.
The artist used slate-grey to paint the shadows on the stone wall.

Synset Examples

the slaty sky of dawn

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: charcoal - a dark grayish-black color
  • Degree Lower: ash-grey - a pale grey color
  • Degree Higher: navy blue - a very dark shade of blue
  • Synonym: steel-grey - a medium to dark shade of grey with a blue tint

Etymology

From 'slate', the fine-grained gray rock, and 'grey', denoting the color.
