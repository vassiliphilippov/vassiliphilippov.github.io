conjugated protein

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.dʒəˌɡeɪ.tɪd ˈproʊ.tiːn / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒʊˌɡeɪ.tɪd ˈprəʊ.tiːn / (BrE)
a protein that consists of amino acids combined with other chemical groups, such as carbohydrates, lipids, or metals
protein combined with other substances
medical

Examples

Hemoglobin is a classic example of a conjugated protein because it contains an iron-containing group.
The laboratory is studying the function of various conjugated proteins in metabolism.
Deficiency in certain conjugated proteins can lead to metabolic disorders.

Etymology

From Latin 'conjugare' (to join together) + 'protein', reflecting the combination of protein and another substance.
