myristic acid

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈrɪstɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪstɪk ˌasɪd / (BrE)
a saturated fatty acid found naturally in animal and vegetable fats, especially nutmeg oil
a saturated fatty acid in fats and oils
medical

Examples

Coconut oil is a rich source of myristic acid, along with other fatty acids.
Some cosmetics use myristic acid for its moisturizing properties.
Researchers analyzed the content of myristic acid in various edible oils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmitic acid - another common saturated fatty acid found in fats and oils

Etymology

From Latin 'Myristica', the genus name for the nutmeg tree, where it was first discovered.
