atomic energy

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the energy released by nuclear reactions, especially fission or fusion of atomic nuclei
energy released by nuclear reactions

Examples

The development of atomic energy revolutionized the production of electricity.
Concerns over the safety of atomic energy plants have led to stricter regulations.
Scientists debated the peaceful uses and dangers of atomic energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear energy - energy released during nuclear fission or fusion

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
energy released as a result of nuclear reactions, especially by the splitting or fusion of atomic nuclei
energy from nuclear reactions

Examples

Interest in atomic energy surged after the discovery of nuclear fission in the 20th century.
Many countries invest in research to make atomic energy safer and more efficient.
Proponents argue that atomic energy can reduce our reliance on fossil fuels.

Synset Examples

Her atomic energy kept the team motivated through long hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear energy - energy produced from nuclear reactions, such as fission or fusion

Etymology

atomic: from Greek atomos (indivisible) + energy: from Greek energeia (activity, operation).
