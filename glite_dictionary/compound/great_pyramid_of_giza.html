Great Pyramid of Giza

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ˈpɪɹəˌmɪd əv ˈɡizə / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈpɪrəmɪd əv ˈɡiːzə / (BrE)
the largest of the pyramids in Egypt, built as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu (Cheops) around 2600 BC, notable for its massive size and historical importance
ancient Egyptian royal tomb; largest pyramid at Giza

Examples

The Great Pyramid of Giza remains one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Many tourists visit the Great Pyramid of Giza to marvel at its architectural splendor.
Archaeologists have studied the Great Pyramid of Giza for clues about ancient Egyptian civilization.

  • Degree Lower: pyramid - a monumental structure with a square or triangular base and sloping sides
