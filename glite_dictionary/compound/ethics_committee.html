ethics committee

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪks kəˌmɪti / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪks kəˌmɪti / (BrE)
a group of people officially appointed to consider, evaluate, or advise on ethical matters, especially in organizations such as hospitals or research institutions
group appointed to consider ethical issues

Examples

The hospital's ethics committee reviewed the controversial case before making a recommendation.
She served on the university ethics committee to help resolve conflicts of interest in research.
All clinical trials must be approved by an ethics committee to ensure patient rights are protected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics board - a group that reviews ethical aspects of research proposals
