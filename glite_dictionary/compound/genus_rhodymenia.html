genus Rhodymenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌroʊ.dɪˈmiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌrəʊ.dɪˈmiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Rhodymeniaceae, consisting of various species of red algae found mainly in marine environments.
type genus of red algae family Rhodymeniaceae

Examples

Many species within the genus Rhodymenia are important for their ecological role in marine environments.
Researchers have studied the reproductive cycles of the genus Rhodymenia to better understand red algae diversity.
The genus Rhodymenia is known for its distinctive rose-red coloration and branching thallus.

Etymology

Rhodymenia from Greek rhodon (rose) + mene (moon), referring to the appearance of the algae.
