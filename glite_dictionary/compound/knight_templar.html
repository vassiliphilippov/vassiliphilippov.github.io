Knight Templar

1. NOUN

/ naɪt tɛmˈplɑr / (AmE), / naɪt ˈtɛmplə / (BrE)
a member of a medieval Christian religious military order founded in 1118 to protect pilgrims and the Holy Sepulcher in Jerusalem
member of medieval Christian military order

Examples

The Knight Templar rode across Europe to aid Christian pilgrims on their journey to Jerusalem.
Many legends surround the Knight Templar and their mysterious treasures.
As part of the crusades, a Knight Templar would take holy vows and defend sacred sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Templar - member of the Knights Templar; alternate name

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ‘Templarius’, referring to their base at the Temple of Solomon in Jerusalem.

2. NOUN

/ naɪt ˈtɛmplɚ / (AmE), / naɪt ˈtɛmplə / (BrE)
a member of a Masonic order in the United States inspired by the medieval Knights Templar
member of a US Masonic order

Examples

He became a Knight Templar after years of dedication to his local Masonic lodge.
The annual meeting of the Knights Templar attracted members from across the state.
Wearing the distinctive regalia, each Knight Templar took part in the ceremonial procession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freemason - member of the international fraternal order known as Freemasonry
  • Degree Lower: Knights Templar - member of the original medieval Catholic military order

Etymology

From the name of the medieval order 'Knights Templar,' adopted by Masonic groups in the 18th century.
