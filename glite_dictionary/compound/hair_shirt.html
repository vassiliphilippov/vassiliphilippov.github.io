hair shirt

1. NOUN

/ hɛr ʃɝːt / (AmE), / heə ʃɜːt / (BrE)
a coarse garment made from animal hair, worn next to the skin as a form of religious penance or self-discipline
coarse garment for penance

Examples

The monk donned a hair shirt as an act of devotion during Lent.
In medieval times, wearing a hair shirt was believed to purify the soul.
She read about saints who endured discomfort by wearing a hair shirt beneath their robes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sackcloth - coarse cloth used as a symbol of mourning or repentance

Etymology

From the practice in medieval Christianity of wearing garments made of rough animal hair for penance.
