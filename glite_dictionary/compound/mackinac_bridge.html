Mackinac Bridge

1. PROPN

/ ˈmækɪnɔː ˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmækɪnɔː ˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a long suspension bridge spanning the Straits of Mackinac, connecting the Upper and Lower Peninsulas of Michigan, USA
suspension bridge in Michigan, USA

Examples

Every summer, thousands of tourists visit the Mackinac Bridge to admire its stunning views.
Heavy winds sometimes cause the Mackinac Bridge to close temporarily for safety reasons.
The Mackinac Bridge connects Michigan’s Upper and Lower Peninsulas across the Straits of Mackinac.

Etymology

From Mackinac, the name of the strait and island, derived from French adaptation of Odawa word 'Michilimackinac'.
