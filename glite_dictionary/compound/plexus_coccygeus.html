plexus coccygeus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs kɒkˈsɪdʒiəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs kɒkˈsɪdʒiəs / (BrE)
a small network of nerves formed by the conjunction of the fifth sacral and coccygeal nerves near the coccyx
network of nerves near the coccyx
medical

Examples

The plexus coccygeus is located near the tip of the spine, close to the coccyx.
Injury to the plexus coccygeus can result in pain or discomfort in the lower back region.
In anatomical studies, the plexus coccygeus is often examined for its role in supplying nerves to the pelvic area.

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network) and 'coccygeus' (of the coccyx, the tailbone).
