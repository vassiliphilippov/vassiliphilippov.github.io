jerk water

1. NOUN

/ dʒɝk ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / dʒɜːk ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
1. Humorous term for unappealing or poor-quality water. 2. A spicy sauce, typically used in cooking, especially for marinating or flavoring meats.
poor-quality water; spicy sauce for cooking

Examples

The tap in the old building only dispensed jerk water that tasted terrible.
He slathered his chicken in jerk water before roasting it over the fire.
No one wanted to drink the jerk water after seeing its cloudy appearance.

Synset Examples

This drink tastes like jerk water mixed with soda.
He poured jerk water all over his food for flavor.
I can't believe you actually drank that jerk water.
The chef used jerk-water to marinate the chicken.
His dish was drenched in jerk-water, making it inedible.

Etymology

'Jerk' in the culinary sense comes from the Spanish 'charqui' (dried meat); used in Caribbean 'jerk' seasoning.
