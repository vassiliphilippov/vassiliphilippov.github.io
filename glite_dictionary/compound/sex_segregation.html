sex segregation

1. NOUN

/ sɛks ˌsɛɡ.rəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / sɛks ˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the practice or system of separating people according to their sex, especially the seclusion of women in some Hindu or Muslim traditions
separation of people by sex; especially women's seclusion

Examples

Under traditional rules of sex segregation, women were rarely seen in public spaces.
The community practiced sex segregation as a means of preserving cultural values.
Legal reforms have challenged the longstanding custom of sex segregation in education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gender segregation - the separation of people based on gender in society or institutions
