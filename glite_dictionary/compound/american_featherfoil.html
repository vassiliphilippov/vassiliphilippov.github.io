American featherfoil

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈfɛðərˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈfɛðəˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
A species of featherfoil native to the eastern United States, characterized by submerged, spongy, inflated flower stalks and white flowers.
aquatic plant with spongy flower stalks

Examples

The pond is home to the rare American featherfoil, easily identified by its spongy stems.
Botanists surveyed the wetland for evidence of American featherfoil during the growing season.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining populations of American featherfoil in its natural habitat.
