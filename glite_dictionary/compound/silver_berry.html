silver berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous shrubby tree of Europe and western Asia with grey leaves and small yellow fruits covered in silvery scales; sometimes spiny.
deciduous shrubby tree with silvery fruits

Examples

The silver berry thrives in dry, sandy soils across parts of Europe and Asia.
In spring, the silver berry produces delicate yellow fruits with a distinctive sheen.
Gardeners value the silver berry for its ornamental foliage and hardy nature.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər ˌberi / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to North America, noted for its silvery leaves and fruits; also known as 'wolf-willow'.
North American shrub with silvery leaves and fruits

Examples

The silver berry thrives in dry, open habitats across the central United States.
Birds are attracted to the silver berry bush because of its distinctive fruit.
The gardener planted a row of silver berry shrubs along the fence for their silvery foliage.
