Japanese lacquer tree

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈlækər tri / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈlækə triː / (BrE)
A small tree native to East Asia that produces a toxic sap used to make lacquer.
East Asian tree producing lacquer sap

Examples

The Japanese lacquer tree is cultivated for its valuable resin used in traditional crafts.
Because the sap of the Japanese lacquer tree is toxic, it must be handled with care.
Artisans have used the Japanese lacquer tree for centuries to create exquisite lacquerware.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquer tree - tree yielding sap used to make lacquer
