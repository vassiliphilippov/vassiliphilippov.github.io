genus Cichorium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkɔːr.i.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkɒr.i.əm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, including chicory and endive.
genus of plants including chicory and endive

Examples

The leaves of several species in the genus Cichorium are used in salads.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of the genus Cichorium for potential health benefits.
Chicory and endive, both members of the genus Cichorium, are popular in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - a blue-flowered plant whose root is used as a coffee substitute and salad leaf
  • Synonym: endive - leafy vegetable plant related to chicory

Etymology

From Latin 'Cichorium', the plant name; 'genus' from Latin meaning 'kind' or 'type'.
