genus Plesianthropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs plis.iˈænθ.rə.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pliːziˈænθ.rə.pəs / (BrE)
a former genus name for certain early hominids, now classified under Australopithecus
historical name for an early hominid genus

Examples

The fossil was once classified as belonging to genus Plesianthropus before being reassigned to Australopithecus.
In older scientific literature, you may encounter references to genus Plesianthropus.
Debates about human ancestry sometimes mention genus Plesianthropus as a historical classification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus - the genus to which these hominids are now assigned

Etymology

From Greek 'plesios' (near) and 'anthrōpos' (man); scientific Latin formation.
