deep-lying fear

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːpˌlaɪɪŋ ˈfɪr / (AmE), / ˈdiːpˌlaɪɪŋ ˈfɪə / (BrE)
a fundamental or hidden fear that influences one's feelings or behavior, often without being immediately obvious
hidden or fundamental fear

Examples

Therapy helped her confront her deep-lying fears from childhood.
Many people have deep-lying fears that affect their everyday decisions.
The novel explores the deep-lying fear of abandonment.

Synset Examples

He discovered some deep-lying fears about public speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: superficial fear - a fear that is shallow or not deeply rooted
