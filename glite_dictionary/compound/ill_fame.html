ill fame

1. NOUN

/ ɪl feɪm / (AmE), / ɪl feɪm / (BrE)
the state of being widely known for something bad or dishonorable
notoriety; bad reputation

Examples

The scandal brought ill fame to the entire company.
He lived the rest of his life burdened by ill fame.
The small town suffered from ill fame after the crime was reported in the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notoriety - the state of being famous or well known for some bad quality or deed
  • Synonym: infamy - the state of being well known for some bad quality or deed
  • Antonym: good reputation - the state of being widely respected or admired
