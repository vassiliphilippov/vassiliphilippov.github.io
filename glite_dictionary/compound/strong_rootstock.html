strong rootstock

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ ˈruːtstɑk / (AmE), / strɒŋ ˈruːtstɒk / (BrE)
a healthy and vigorous base plant onto which another plant is grafted, chosen for its strength and disease resistance
base plant for grafting, vigorous and healthy

Examples

The gardener always selects strong rootstock to ensure healthy grafted trees.
Using strong rootstock can increase disease resistance in the new plants.
Experts recommend planting with strong rootstock for better growth and yield.

Synset Examples

The gardener used strong rootstock for the new plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rootstock - a plant onto which another plant is grafted
