broadcast medium

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæst ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːst ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A means or channel of transmitting information to a wide audience via telecommunications, such as radio or television.
a channel for mass telecommunications, e.g. TV or radio

Examples

Television is a popular broadcast medium for delivering news and entertainment to millions of people.
Radio was the dominant broadcast medium before the advent of the internet.
The internet is rapidly becoming the preferred broadcast medium for live events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medium - a means of doing something or communicating information
  • Synonym: broadcasting - transmitting information to a widespread audience
