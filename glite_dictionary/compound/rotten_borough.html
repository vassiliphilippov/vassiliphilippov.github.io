rotten borough

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑtən ˈbɝːoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɒtən ˈbʌrə / (BrE)
(historical) An English parliamentary constituency that had very few voters and could be easily controlled or influenced, especially before the Reform Act of 1832.
historical constituency with few electors
british

Examples

Before the Reform Act, many MPs were elected from rotten boroughs with only a handful of voters.
The historian discussed how rotten boroughs distorted representation in Parliament.
Gatton was infamous as a rotten borough, sending members to Parliament despite its tiny population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket borough - A parliamentary borough or constituency in England controlled by one person or family

Etymology

From 'rotten' (decayed, corrupt) + 'borough' (an English town with privileges); term used before the 1832 Reform Act.
