purple sanicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈsænɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈsænɪkl / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the northwestern United States and British Columbia, known for its yellow, red, or purple flower clusters.
a plant with yellow, red, or purple flowers native to NW North America

Examples

We discovered a purple sanicle blooming near the edge of the forest trail.
The purple sanicle thrives in the moist woodlands of British Columbia.
Botanists often survey populations of purple sanicle to monitor ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanicle - a perennial herb of the genus Sanicula
