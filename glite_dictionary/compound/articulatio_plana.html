articulatio plana

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃioʊ ˈpleɪnə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃɪəʊ ˈplɑːnə / (BrE)
A type of synovial joint that allows only gliding movements between the articulating bones, such as those found in the carpal bones of the wrist.
joint allowing gliding movements
medical

Examples

In the wrist, the carpal bones are connected by articulatio plana, enabling smooth gliding during movement.
The superior and inferior articular surfaces of vertebrae form an articulatio plana type of joint.
Damage to an articulatio plana can restrict the subtle movements essential for hand flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plane joint - a synovial joint that allows only gliding motions (synonym)

Etymology

From Latin 'articulatio' (joint) and 'plana' (flat).
