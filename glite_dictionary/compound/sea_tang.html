sea tang

1. NOUN

/ si tæŋ / (AmE), / si tæŋ / (BrE)
a coarse type of seaweed found along seashores, especially large or leathery varieties
a kind of coarse seaweed

Examples

At low tide, children collected sea tang from the rocks to use in their garden.
The slippery sea tang clung to our feet as we waded through the shallow water.
After the storm, heaps of sea tang lined the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - any plant-like marine alga, especially larger ones used as food or fertilizer
