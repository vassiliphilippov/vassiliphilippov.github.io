cathode-ray oscilloscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæθoʊd reɪ əˈsɪləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈkæθəʊd reɪ əˈsɪləsˌkəʊp / (BrE)
an electronic instrument that displays visual representations of varying electrical signals using a cathode-ray tube
instrument displaying electrical signals visually

Examples

The cathode-ray oscilloscope displayed the voltage waveform clearly on its screen.
In the electronics lab, students learned to use a cathode-ray oscilloscope to analyze signal patterns.
Before digital devices became common, engineers relied on a cathode-ray oscilloscope for many measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscilloscope - an instrument for viewing oscillations in electrical signals

Etymology

From 'cathode-ray' (referring to the cathode-ray tube) and 'oscilloscope' (from Latin 'oscillare', to swing).
