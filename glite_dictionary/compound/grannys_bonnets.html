granny's bonnets

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræniz ˈbɑːnəts / (AmE), / ˈɡræniz ˈbɒnɪts / (BrE)
a common European columbine (Aquilegia vulgaris) with variously colored, short-spurred flowers, naturalized in North America
a European columbine flower

Examples

The garden was filled with clusters of granny's bonnets in full bloom.
She planted granny's bonnets along the path for a splash of color in spring.
Collectors especially love the unique shapes of granny's bonnets for floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - a plant of the genus Aquilegia with distinctive spurred flowers

Etymology

Named for the flower's resemblance to the traditional bonnets once worn by elderly women ('granny').
