preclinical phase

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˈklɪnɪkəl feɪz / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈklɪnɪkəl feɪz / (BrE)
the stage of medical research in which a new drug or device is tested in the laboratory and on animals before clinical trials in humans
stage of pre-human medical research
medical

Examples

The new medication showed promising results during its preclinical phase on animal models.
Before starting trials on humans, researchers must complete the preclinical phase of drug development.
Investment was secured after the preclinical phase demonstrated the device’s safety in laboratory tests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clinical trial - test of a drug or treatment on human volunteers

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'clinical' (relating to the observation and treatment of actual patients) + 'phase'.
