Chichewa language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈtʃeɪwə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈtʃeɪwə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Bantu language spoken by the Chewa people of east central Africa, chiefly in Malawi, Zambia, and Mozambique
a Bantu language of east central Africa

Examples

He learned to speak Chichewa language while living in Malawi for several years.
The official documents were translated into the Chichewa language for local residents.
Many students are eager to study the Chichewa language and understand the culture of the Chewa people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nyanja - another name for the Chichewa language

Etymology

From Chichewa, the name of the language in the native tongue; 'Chewa' refers to the ethnic group.
