Blue Nile

1. PROPN

/ bluː ˈnaɪl / (AmE), / bluː ˈnaɪl / (BrE)
a major tributary of the Nile River, originating in Ethiopia and joining the White Nile at Khartoum, Sudan to form the Nile.
tributary of the Nile River

Examples

The Blue Nile flows from Lake Tana in Ethiopia before merging with the White Nile.
Many settlements have developed along the banks of the Blue Nile.
The annual flooding of the Blue Nile was crucial to ancient Egyptian agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: White Nile - the other main tributary forming the Nile at Khartoum
