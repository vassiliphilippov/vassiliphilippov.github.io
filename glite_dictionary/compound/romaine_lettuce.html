romaine lettuce

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmeɪn ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪn ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with long, crisp, dark-green leaves and a firm rib, often used in salads.
lettuce with long, crisp leaves

Examples

She prepared a fresh salad using crisp romaine lettuce and cherry tomatoes.
Many Caesar salads are made with romaine lettuce because of its crunchy texture.
I bought a head of romaine lettuce at the farmer's market yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cos lettuce - another name for romaine lettuce

Etymology

From French 'romaine' meaning 'Roman', referring to its supposed origin in Rome.

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈmeɪn ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪn ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with long, crisp, dark-green leaves forming a loosely packed, elongated head.
type of lettuce with long, crisp leaves

Examples

She topped her salad with fresh romaine lettuce and cherry tomatoes.
Many classic Caesar salads are made only with romaine lettuce.
The grocery store was out of romaine lettuce after the recent recall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cos lettuce - another name for romaine lettuce

Etymology

From French 'romaine', meaning 'Roman', possibly referring to its cultivation in the Mediterranean region.
