bit bucket

1. NOUN

/ bɪt ˈbʌkət / (AmE), / bɪt ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
a notional place where lost or discarded data is said to go, especially in computing
imaginary place for lost data (computing)

Examples

After the power outage, my entire project went straight to the bit bucket.
Any errors in the transmission will send the data into the bit bucket.
He joked that all his unsaved changes are now in the bit bucket.

Synset Examples

He realized his files were sent to the bit bucket.
His computer crashed, and everything vanished into the great bit bucket in the sky.
All my unsaved work ended up in the bit bucket.
Don't forget to back up; the bit bucket awaits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black hole - an informal term for a place where things disappear without trace

Etymology

From 'bit', the basic unit of computer data, plus 'bucket', suggesting a receptacle for disposal.
