bath foam

1. NOUN

/ bæθ foʊm / (AmE), / bɑːθ fəʊm / (BrE)
a substance in the form of crystals, powder, or liquid that creates bubbles, scents, and softens the water when added to a bath
substance for foaming and scenting bathwater

Examples

I poured some bath foam into the tub and watched the bubbles rise.
The lavender-scented bath foam made the bathroom smell wonderful.
She prefers using bath foam to traditional bar soap when she takes a long soak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble bath - a product that creates bubbles in bathwater
