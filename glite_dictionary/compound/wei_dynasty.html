Wei dynasty

1. NOUN

/ weɪ ˈdaɪnəsti / (AmE), / weɪ ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
any of several imperial dynasties of China, most notably the Cao Wei (220–265) and Northern Wei (386–534), which ruled parts of northern China during different periods.
imperial Chinese dynasties, 220–265 and 386–534

Examples

Historians study the art and government of the Wei dynasty to understand early Chinese civilization.
During the Wei dynasty, major advancements were made in architecture and Buddhism.
The Wei dynasty played a crucial role in shaping the politics of northern China.

Etymology

From Chinese 魏 (Wèi), the name of the ruling house and region.
