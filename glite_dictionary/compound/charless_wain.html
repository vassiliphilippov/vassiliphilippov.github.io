Charles's Wain

1. NOUN

/ ˈʧɑrlzɪz weɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlzɪz weɪn / (BrE)
an old name for the group of seven bright stars forming the Big Dipper in the constellation Ursa Major
old name for the Big Dipper in Ursa Major
archaic

Examples

In the clear night sky, Charles's Wain was easily visible above the horizon.
The poet described the journey of Charles's Wain across the heavens.
In old English folklore, Charles's Wain guided travelers at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Big Dipper - the group of seven stars in Ursa Major forming a dipper shape
  • Degree Higher: Ursa Major - the constellation containing the Big Dipper

Etymology

From Old English 'Carles wæn', meaning 'Charles's wagon,' referring to the shape's resemblance to a wagon or cart.
