shore pine

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔr paɪn / (AmE), / ʃɔː paɪn / (BrE)
a shrubby pine tree (Pinus contorta) native to the coastal northwestern United States, with red to yellow-brown bark fissured into small squares and two-needle clusters
a coastal North American pine tree

Examples

The twisted branches of the shore pine are a common sight along the Oregon coast.
Native shrubs and shore pine help stabilize the sandy dunes near the shore.
She planted a shore pine in her coastal garden for its resilience to salty air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lodgepole pine - a closely related pine species found inland, with straighter growth
