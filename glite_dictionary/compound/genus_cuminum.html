genus Cuminum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kjuːˈmɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kjuːˈmɪnəm / (BrE)
A small genus of aromatic plants in the family Apiaceae, including the species Cuminum cyminum (cumin).
genus of aromatic plants (cumin)

Examples

The genus Cuminum contains only a few species, the best known being cumin.
Botanists have classified cumin under the genus Cuminum in the family Apiaceae.
Studying the genus Cuminum helps researchers understand the properties of cumin seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumin - spice made from the dried seed of Cuminum cyminum

Etymology

From Latin 'Cuminum', from Greek 'kuminon' (cumin).
