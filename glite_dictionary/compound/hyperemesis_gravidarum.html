hyperemesis gravidarum

1. NOUN

/ haɪpərˌɛməsɪs ˌɡrævɪˈdɛrəm / (AmE), / haɪpəˌriːməsɪs ˌɡravɪˈdɑːrəm / (BrE)
A severe form of nausea and vomiting during pregnancy that can lead to dehydration and complications if untreated.
severe nausea/vomiting in pregnancy
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hyperemesis gravidarum after experiencing persistent vomiting for several weeks.
Women with hyperemesis gravidarum may require hospitalization to manage severe dehydration.
Treatments for hyperemesis gravidarum include intravenous fluids and specialized medical care.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: morning sickness - mild nausea and vomiting during early pregnancy

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (excessive), emesis (vomiting), and Latin gravidarum (of pregnant women).
