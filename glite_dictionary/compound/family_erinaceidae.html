family Erinaceidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɛrɪˈneɪsiˌaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɛrɪˈneɪsiːˌaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, spiny mammals known as true hedgehogs, native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.
the hedgehog family (mammals)

Examples

Members of the family Erinaceidae are characterized by their distinctive spines and insectivorous diet.
The family Erinaceidae includes several genera of hedgehogs found in different parts of the world.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary relationships within the family Erinaceidae to understand their adaptation to various habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedgehog - a small, spiny mammal in the family Erinaceidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Erinaceidae', based on 'Erinaceus', the Latin name for the hedgehog genus.
