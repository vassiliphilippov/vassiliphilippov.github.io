sedge family

1. NOUN

/ sɛdʒ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / sɛdʒ ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a large family of grass-like flowering plants, Cyperaceae, including bulrushes, chufas, cotton grasses, papyrus, and umbrella plants
the plant family Cyperaceae

Examples

Many wetland ecosystems are dominated by members of the sedge family.
The sedge family includes important species such as papyrus and cotton grass.
Bulrushes, which belong to the sedge family, are commonly found near lakes and rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyperaceae - the botanical name for the sedge family
