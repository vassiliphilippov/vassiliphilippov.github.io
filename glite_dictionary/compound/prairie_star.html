prairie star

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛəri stɑr / (AmE), / ˈpreəri stɑː / (BrE)
a plant with mainly basal leaves and slender open racemes of white or pale pink flowers, native to prairies and open forests of northwestern United States, British Columbia, and Alberta
a plant with pale pink or white flowers from North America

Examples

The delicate prairie star bloomed along the edge of the grassland trail this spring.
Scientists observed several prairie stars thriving in the restored ecosystem.
She carefully photographed a cluster of prairie star flowers in the national park.
