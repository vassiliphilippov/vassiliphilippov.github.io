Eastern Turki language

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn ˈtɜːrki ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈtɜːki ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Turkic literary language of medieval Central Asia, associated with the region historically ruled by a son of Genghis Khan.
Turkic literary language of medieval Central Asia

Examples

Several important historical texts were written in Eastern Turki language during the medieval period.
Linguists have studied the Eastern Turki language to better understand Turkic language evolution.
The Eastern Turki language influenced later literary traditions in Central Asia.

Etymology

From 'Eastern' (direction) + 'Turki' (relating to Turkic peoples/languages), referencing regions in Central Asia.
