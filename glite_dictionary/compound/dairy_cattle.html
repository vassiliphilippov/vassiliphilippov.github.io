dairy cattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛri ˌkæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈdeəri ˌkætl̩ / (BrE)
Cattle bred or kept primarily for the production of milk rather than meat.
cattle raised for milk production

Examples

Many farmers depend on dairy cattle to supply fresh milk and other dairy products.
The veterinary conference focused on new health challenges facing dairy cattle.
Improved breeding techniques have increased the productivity of dairy cattle worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beef cattle - cattle bred primarily for meat production
