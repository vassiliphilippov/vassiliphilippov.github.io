close-set

1. ADJ

/ ˈkloʊsˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsˌsɛt / (BrE)
Placed or positioned closely together, especially referring to parts of the body such as eyes or teeth.
placed or positioned closely together

Examples

She has close-set eyes that give her a very distinctive look.
The dentist noted that his close-set teeth made cleaning difficult.
The painting features a figure with close-set eyebrows, adding intensity to the expression.

Synset Examples

close-set eyes
close-set teeth
her eyes are close set

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wide-set - placed or positioned with a greater than usual distance between them

2. ADJ

/ ˈkloʊsˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsˌsɛt / (BrE)
Placed or positioned very near to one another, especially referring to features such as eyes or teeth.
very near together

Examples

Her close-set eyes gave her a distinctive appearance.
The actor had close-set teeth, which made his smile unique.
Some people inherit close-set facial features from their parents.

Synset Examples

close-set eyes
close-set teeth
her eyes are close set

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wide-set - features that are placed far apart
