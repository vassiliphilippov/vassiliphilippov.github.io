swamp poplar

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈpɑplɚ / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
A North American poplar tree with large, rounded, scalloped leaves and brownish bark and wood, commonly found in wetland areas.
North American poplar tree species

Examples

The wetland was dominated by towering swamp poplar trees along the riverbanks.
Scientists studied the leaves of the swamp poplar to better understand its adaptation to marshy soils.
After the flood, several swamp poplar saplings sprouted near the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a tall, fast-growing tree of the willow family
