given name

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪvən ˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvən ˌneɪm / (BrE)
a person's first name, as distinct from their surname or family name
a person's first name

Examples

On official documents, she always uses her full given name and surname.
Parents often spend months choosing a given name for their child.
His given name is David, but everyone calls him Dave.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surname - family name, inherited and used after the given name
  • Synonym: first name - another term for 'given name'

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪv.ən neɪm / (AmE), / ˈɡɪv.ən neɪm / (BrE)
a person's first name, as assigned at birth or baptism, as distinguished from a surname or family name
a person's first name

Examples

On the application, please enter your given name followed by your surname.
Her given name is Elizabeth, but she usually goes by Liz.
It is customary in some cultures to place the family name before the given name.

Synset Examples

On the form, please write your given name and surname.
His given name is Michael, but everyone calls him Mike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first name - the name given to someone at birth, as opposed to the surname
