family Myacidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli maɪˈæsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli maɪˈæsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of soft-shell clams, marine bivalve mollusks found in sandy or muddy substrates.
a family of soft-shell clams

Examples

The family Myacidae includes several commercially important species of clams.
Scientists study the family Myacidae to understand the adaptation of mollusks to soft substrates.
Many members of the family Myacidae are harvested for human consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft-shell clam - a type of edible clam with a thin, brittle shell

Etymology

From New Latin Myacidae, based on the type genus Mya.
