Lautaro Popular Rebel Forces

1. NOUN

/ laʊˈtɑːroʊ ˈpɑːpjələr ˈrɛbəl ˈfɔːrsɪz / (AmE), / laʊˈtɑːrəʊ ˈpɒpjʊlə ˈrɛbəl ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
a Chilean armed group formed in the 1980s that used violent means to oppose the military government
Chilean insurgent group, 1980s

Examples

The government increased military patrols in areas controlled by the Lautaro Popular Rebel Forces.
Several leaders of the Lautaro Popular Rebel Forces were arrested during the crackdown.
The activities of the Lautaro Popular Rebel Forces drew international attention in the late 1980s.

Etymology

Named after Lautaro, a 16th-century indigenous Mapuche leader who resisted Spanish conquest.
