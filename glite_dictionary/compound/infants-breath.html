infant's-breath

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnfənts ˌbrɛθ / (AmE), / ˈɪnfənts ˌbrɛθ / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb (Gypsophila paniculata) notable for its large clusters of small white flowers, often used in floral arrangements; naturalized in North America.
a plant with small white flowers, used in floristry

Examples

She chose infant's-breath to complement the roses in her bridal bouquet.
Large fields of infant's-breath bloom in the summer, creating a sea of white blossoms.
Florists often use infant's-breath as a delicate filler in arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby's-breath - Common name for the same plant species, Gypsophila paniculata
