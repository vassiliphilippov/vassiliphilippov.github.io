depository financial institution

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑzɪˌtɔri fəˈnænʃəl ˌɪnstɪˈtuʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt(ə)ri fəˈnænʃəl ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
a financial organization that accepts deposits from the public and uses the funds for lending and investment activities
bank that accepts deposits and lends money

Examples

The government regulates every depository financial institution to ensure consumer protection.
A depository financial institution can include commercial banks and savings institutions.
Opening an account at a depository financial institution allows customers to safely store their money.

Synset Examples

he cashed a check at the bank
