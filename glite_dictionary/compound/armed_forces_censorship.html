armed forces censorship

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrmd ˈfɔːrsɪz ˌsɛnsɚˈʃɪp / (AmE), / ɑːmd ˈfɔːsɪz ˌsensəˈʃɪp / (BrE)
the examination and suppression of personal communications by military authorities, especially messages sent to or from members of the armed forces
military censorship of personal communications

Examples

During wartime, armed forces censorship strictly monitored soldiers' letters to prevent sensitive information from being leaked.
Some historians study the impact of armed forces censorship on morale and family communication during conflicts.
The implementation of armed forces censorship often led to frustration among service members and their loved ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military censorship - the censorship of information by military authorities
  • Antonym: civil censorship - censorship applied by civil authorities, as opposed to military
