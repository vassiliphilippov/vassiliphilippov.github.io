inferior thyroid vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˈθaɪˌrɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located on each side of the neck that drains blood from the thyroid gland and empties into the brachiocephalic (innominate) vein
vein draining the thyroid gland
medical

Examples

The inferior thyroid vein drains blood from the lower region of the thyroid gland into the brachiocephalic vein.
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully preserved the inferior thyroid vein to prevent excessive bleeding.
An enlarged inferior thyroid vein may be seen in some thyroid disorders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: superior thyroid vein - vein that drains the upper part of the thyroid gland

Etymology

From Latin 'vena thyroidea inferior' (lower thyroid vein).
