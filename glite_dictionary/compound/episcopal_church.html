Episcopal Church

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌpɪskəpəl tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ɪˌpɪskəpl tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The autonomous branch of the Anglican Communion in Scotland.
Scottish branch of the Anglican Communion

Examples

The Episcopal Church in Scotland holds services in cathedrals across the country.
She joined the Episcopal Church after moving to Edinburgh.
The history of the Episcopal Church dates back to the seventeenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anglican Communion - The global association of churches in communion with the Church of England

Etymology

From episcopal, relating to bishops (from Late Latin episcopalis), and church (from Old English cirice).

2. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪskəpəl tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪskəpəl tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The official Anglican church in the United States, which is in full communion with the Church of England and the Archbishop of Canterbury.
Anglican church in the U.S.

Examples

She was baptized at the Episcopal Church in her hometown.
The Episcopal Church supports a variety of outreach programs in the community.
My grandparents attend an Episcopal Church every Sunday.

Etymology

From 'episcopal', relating to bishops, from Greek 'episkopos' (overseer), and 'church', from Old English 'cirice'.
