quick assets

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk ˈæsɛts / (AmE), / kwɪk ˈæsɛts / (BrE)
assets in the form of cash or that can be rapidly converted into cash, such as short-term investments and receivables
assets quickly convertible to cash

Examples

The company's quick assets allow it to cover immediate expenses without delay.
Investors often examine quick assets to determine a firm's short-term financial health.
Unlike inventory, quick assets can be turned into cash within days.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: current assets - assets expected to be converted into cash within one year
