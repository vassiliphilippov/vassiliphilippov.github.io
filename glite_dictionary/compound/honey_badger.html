honey badger

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni ˌbædʒər / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ˌbadʒə / (BrE)
a nocturnal carnivorous mammal of Africa and southern Asia, known for its strength, ferocity, and tendency to raid beehives; also called the ratel.
nocturnal carnivorous mammal (ratel)

Examples

The honey badger is renowned for its fearless attitude and ability to fend off much larger predators.
We watched a documentary that showed a honey badger breaking into a beehive for honey.
Despite its size, the honey badger is one of the most ferocious animals in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratel - alternative name for the honey badger
