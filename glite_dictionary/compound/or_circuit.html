OR circuit

1. NOUN

/ ɔr ˈsɜr.kɪt / (AmE), / ɔː ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
An electronic circuit or logic gate that produces a positive output when any of its inputs receive a positive signal.
logic gate producing output if any input fires

Examples

The technician tested the OR circuit to ensure it responded to all possible input combinations.
In digital electronics, an OR circuit is used to combine multiple signals into a single logic output.
The malfunctioning OR circuit caused the system to give incorrect results when only one input was active.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: AND circuit - a logic gate that produces output only if all inputs fire
  • Synonym: OR gate - a logic gate with similar function to an OR circuit

Etymology

OR from the logical operation 'or', meaning either input; circuit from Latin 'circuitus', meaning a going around.
