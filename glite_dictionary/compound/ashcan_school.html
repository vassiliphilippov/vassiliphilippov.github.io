Ashcan School

1. PROPN

/ ˈæʃˌkæn skuːl / (AmE), / ˈæʃˌkan skuːl / (BrE)
A group of American painters founded in 1907, known for their realistic portrayals of gritty aspects of urban life.
American realist artists group (1907)

Examples

The Ashcan School artists captured the harsh realities of early 20th-century New York.
Many credit the Ashcan School with laying the groundwork for American modernism.
Works from the Ashcan School often depict crowded streets and working-class neighborhoods.

Etymology

Named for their focus on scenes of ordinary urban life, often depicting garbage cans (ashcans) and city streets.
