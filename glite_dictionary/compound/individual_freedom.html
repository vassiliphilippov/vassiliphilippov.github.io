individual freedom

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəl ˈfriːdəm / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəl ˈfriːdəm / (BrE)
the right or ability of a person to act and think as they choose, without undue restraint from authorities or others
the right to act and think freely

Examples

Many people fought for individual freedom during the revolution.
The law is designed to protect individual freedom in society.
Debates about individual freedom often arise in discussions of government power.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that statism stifles individual freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberty - the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions
  • Synonym: personal autonomy - the ability to make one's own choices independently
  • Antonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control; the opposite of freedom
