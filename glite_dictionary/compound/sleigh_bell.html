sleigh bell

1. NOUN

/ sleɪ bɛl / (AmE), / sleɪ bɛl / (BrE)
a small metal bell, often attached to a sleigh or the harness of a horse pulling a sleigh, that jingles when moved
small bell used on sleighs or harnesses

Examples

The horses wore sleigh bells that jingled merrily as they trotted through the snow.
She kept a set of sleigh bells by the fireplace as a holiday decoration.
You could hear the distant sound of sleigh bells on a cold winter night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jingle bell - a small, hollow, spherical bell that makes a jingling sound

Etymology

From 'sleigh', from Dutch 'slee' (sled), and 'bell', from Old English 'belle'.
