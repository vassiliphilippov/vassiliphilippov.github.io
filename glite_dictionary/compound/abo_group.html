ABO group

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.biːˈoʊ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.biːˈəʊ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a system for classifying human blood based on the presence or absence of A and B antigens, dividing blood into four groups: A, B, AB, and O
human blood type classification system
medical

Examples

Patients must be matched by their ABO group before a blood transfusion.
The doctor explained the significance of each ABO group to the medical students.
Blood banks organize their inventories according to ABO group and Rh factor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood group - any of the various types into which human blood is classified

Etymology

From the initial letters A, B, O, representing the blood groups in the classification system, first described by Austrian immunologist Karl Landsteiner in the early 20th century.
