auditory nerve

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɔdətɛri nɝv / (AmE), / ɔːˈdɪtəri nɜːv / (BrE)
the nerve that transmits sensory information from the inner ear to the brain, enabling hearing and balance
nerve carrying signals from the ear to the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the auditory nerve can result in hearing loss or dizziness.
The auditory nerve carries electrical signals from the cochlea to the brain for sound interpretation.
Doctors examined the patient's auditory nerve after he reported a sudden loss of balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cochlear nerve - the branch of the auditory nerve that carries hearing information
  • Synonym: vestibular nerve - the branch of the auditory nerve that carries balance information

Etymology

From Latin 'audire' (to hear) + 'nerve', relating to its function in hearing.
