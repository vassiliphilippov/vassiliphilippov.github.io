pitch accent

1. NOUN

/ pɪtʃ ˈæksɛnt / (AmE), / pɪtʃ ˈæksənt / (BrE)
an accent or emphasis given to a syllable or word in speech by varying its pitch rather than its loudness
accent given by pitch variation

Examples

Japanese is known for having a pitch accent system rather than a stress accent system.
Linguists study how pitch accent distinguishes meaning in tonal languages.
In Swedish, the pitch accent can change the meaning of a word completely.

Etymology

Formed from 'pitch' (related to sound frequency) and 'accent' (stress or emphasis).
