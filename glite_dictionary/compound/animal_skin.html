animal skin

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməl skɪn / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl skɪn / (BrE)
the outer layer of tissue covering an animal's body, often used after removal for leather or fur
outer tissue layer of an animal

Examples

Leather is typically made from treated animal skin.
Some traditional drums use animal skin stretched over a wooden frame for the drumhead.
The market sold belts, bags, and jackets made from various types of animal skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - the skin of a large animal, especially after tanning
  • Synonym: pelt - the skin of an animal, especially with its fur, wool, or hair still on it
