family Casuaridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kæˌzjuːəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kazjuːəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, flightless, ostrich-like birds, including the cassowaries.
family of large flightless birds (cassowaries)

Examples

The family Casuaridae includes three species of cassowaries found in Australia and New Guinea.
Scientists study the evolutionary history of the family Casuaridae to understand flightlessness in birds.
Members of the family Casuaridae are known for their distinctive helmet-like casques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassowary - a large flightless bird native to New Guinea and Australia

Etymology

From New Latin Casuaridae, from genus Casuarius (the cassowary).
