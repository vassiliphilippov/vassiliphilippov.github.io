port of call

1. NOUN

/ pɔrt əv kɔl / (AmE), / pɔːt əv kɔːl / (BrE)
a port where a ship stops during a voyage, except its home port
intermediate stop for a ship

Examples

The ship's first port of call after leaving New York was Lisbon.
We visited several ports of call on our Mediterranean cruise.
Singapore is a popular port of call for many cargo vessels.
