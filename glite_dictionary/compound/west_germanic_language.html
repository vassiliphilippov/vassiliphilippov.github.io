West Germanic language

1. NOUN

/ wɛst dʒɚˈmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / wɛst dʒɜːˈmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a branch of the Germanic language family that includes English, German, and Dutch
branch of the Germanic language family

Examples

English is a West Germanic language spoken by millions of people worldwide.
Dutch, along with German, is classified as a West Germanic language.
The study of a West Germanic language can provide insights into the development of modern English.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Germanic language - a branch of the Indo-European language family
  • Synonym: English - the West Germanic language spoken in England and elsewhere
  • Synonym: German - the West Germanic language spoken in Germany
