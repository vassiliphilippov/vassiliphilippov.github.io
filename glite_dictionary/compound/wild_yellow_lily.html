wild yellow lily

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈjɛloʊ ˈlɪli / (AmE), / waɪld ˈjɛləʊ ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a common lily native to the eastern United States, having nodding yellow or reddish flowers spotted with brown
a lily with yellow or reddish spotted flowers

Examples

The wild yellow lily blooms in woodlands during early spring, adding bursts of color to the forest floor.
We spotted several wild yellow lily plants along the hiking trail, their flowers nodding in the breeze.
Gardeners often admire the striking appearance of the wild yellow lily for natural landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout lily - a common name for Erythronium americanum, a related species of wildflower
