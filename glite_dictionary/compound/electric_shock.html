electric shock

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ʃɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ʃɒk / (BrE)
A sudden injury or trauma caused by electrical current passing through the body, often leading to burns, heart rhythm disturbances, or unconsciousness.
injury from electrical current

Examples

The worker received a severe electric shock after coming into contact with exposed wiring.
It's important to turn off the mains power to avoid the risk of an electric shock.
She was treated for burns that resulted from an electric shock during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrocution - death or severe injury by electric shock

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ʃɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ʃɒk / (BrE)
a sudden discharge of electricity through a person's body, especially as a form of punishment or torture.
sudden jolt of electricity, esp. as punishment

Examples

The prisoner cried out when an electric shock was delivered as punishment.
The use of electric shock in interrogations is condemned by human rights organizations.
He suffered an electric shock when he accidentally touched the exposed wire.

3. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktrɪk ʃɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ʃɒk / (BrE)
A sudden physical reaction caused by the passage of electric current through the body, typically resulting in pain, muscle spasms, or injury.
injury from electricity passing through the body

Examples

He suffered an electric shock while repairing the toaster, but fortunately it was not serious.
Some toys carry warnings about the risk of electric shock if misused near water.
The technician wore rubber gloves to avoid getting an electric shock while working with live wires.

Synset Examples

subjects received a small electric shock when they made the wrong response
electricians get accustomed to occasional shocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - a sudden, disturbing physical or emotional impact
  • Degree Higher: electrocution - death or severe injury caused by electric shock
