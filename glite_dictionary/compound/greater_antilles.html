Greater Antilles

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ænˈtɪliz / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ænˈtɪliːz / (BrE)
a group of large islands in the western part of the West Indies, including Cuba, Jamaica, Hispaniola, and Puerto Rico
group of major Caribbean islands

Examples

Many cruise ships stop at the Greater Antilles to explore Cuba and Puerto Rico.
The Greater Antilles are home to several unique species of plants and animals.
Christopher Columbus first landed in the Greater Antilles during his voyages to the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Lesser Antilles - a group of smaller islands in the eastern West Indies

Etymology

From 'greater' (larger) and 'Antilles', after the medieval name for the West Indies.
