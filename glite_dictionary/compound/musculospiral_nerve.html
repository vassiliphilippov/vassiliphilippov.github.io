musculospiral nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌs.kjə.loʊˈspaɪ.rəl nɜrv / (AmE), / ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləʊˈspaɪ.rəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the largest branch of the brachial plexus, running down the humerus and dividing near the elbow to supply the skin on the back of the hand and the extensor muscles
major nerve of the arm (radial nerve)
medical

Examples

The musculospiral nerve is susceptible to injury in fractures of the humerus.
Damage to the musculospiral nerve can cause weakness in the wrist and fingers.
The surgeon traced the musculospiral nerve along the upper arm to locate the site of compression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radial nerve - another term for the musculospiral nerve

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) and 'spiralis' (spiral), referring to the nerve's winding course around the humerus.
