operant conditioning

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.pɚ.rənt kənˈdɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ər.ənt kənˈdɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a learning process in which the likelihood of a behavior is increased or decreased through reinforcement or punishment that follows the behavior
learning by reinforcement or punishment

Examples

The teacher used operant conditioning to encourage good behavior in her students.
Animal trainers often rely on operant conditioning techniques to teach tricks.
Through operant conditioning, the rats learned to press the lever to receive food.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: classical conditioning - learning process by association between stimuli

Etymology

From 'operant' (acting or producing effects) and 'conditioning' (process of training or accustoming).
