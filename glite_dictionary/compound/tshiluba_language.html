Tshiluba language

1. NOUN

/ tʃiˈluːbə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈluːbə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken in southeastern Democratic Republic of the Congo
Bantu language of southeastern Congo

Examples

Many people in southeastern Congo speak Tshiluba language as their mother tongue.
She studied the Tshiluba language to better communicate with local communities.
The Tshiluba language has a rich oral tradition and diverse dialects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu language - a member of the Bantu family of languages

Etymology

From local name Tshiluba, a native term for the language.
