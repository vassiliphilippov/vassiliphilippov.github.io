speed trap

1. NOUN

/ spiːd træp / (AmE), / spiːd træp / (BrE)
a section of road where police hide to catch drivers who are exceeding the speed limit, typically using radar or cameras
hidden police checkpoint to catch speeding drivers

Examples

The police set up a speed trap at the bottom of the hill to catch speeding motorists.
I slowed down when I saw a speed trap sign ahead on the highway.
She received a ticket last month after unknowingly driving through a speed trap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed camera - a camera used to detect speeding vehicles
