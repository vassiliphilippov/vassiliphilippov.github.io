Austin Friar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːstɪn ˈfraɪər / (AmE), / ˈɔːstɪn ˈfraɪə / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic religious order of hermits following the Rule of Saint Augustine
member of the order of Saint Augustine

Examples

The church was originally founded by Austin Friars in the thirteenth century.
Several Austin Friars played a significant role in the city's religious life.
The monastery belonged to the order of the Austin Friars until the dissolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Augustinian - member of a religious order following the rule of Saint Augustine

Etymology

Austin is a contraction of Augustine, after Saint Augustine of Hippo.
