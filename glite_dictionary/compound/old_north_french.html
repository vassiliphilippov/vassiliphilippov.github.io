Old North French

1. NOUN

/ oʊld nɔrθ frɛntʃ / (AmE), / əʊld nɔːθ frentʃ / (BrE)
the medieval Norman dialect of Old French, spoken in northern France during the Middle Ages.
medieval Norman dialect of Old French

Examples

Scholars study Old North French to better understand medieval literature from Normandy.
The influence of Old North French can still be found in some English words today.
Documents written in Old North French reveal much about medieval society in northern France.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Old French - the Romance language spoken in northern France from roughly the 9th to 14th centuries

Etymology

Compound of 'old', 'north', and 'French'; refers to the Norman language of northern France in the Middle Ages.
