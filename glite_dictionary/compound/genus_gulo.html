genus Gulo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɡuː.loʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɡuː.ləʊ / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous mammals in the family Mustelidae, which includes the wolverine.
carnivorous mammal genus (wolverine)

Examples

The genus Gulo is represented today by its only living species, the wolverine.
Researchers debated the evolutionary history of genus Gulo based on fossil evidence.
Members of the genus Gulo are known for their strength and aggressive hunting behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolverine - a large, fierce member of the genus Gulo

Etymology

From Latin *gulo* (glutton), used in zoological taxonomy.
