Confederate States

1. PROPN

/ kənˌfɛdərət ˈsteɪts / (AmE), / kənˌfɛdərət ˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the collective name for the southern states of the United States that seceded to form their own government (the Confederacy) from 1861 to 1865.
southern states that seceded in 1861 (U.S. Civil War)
american

Examples

The Confederate States declared their secession from the Union in 1861, leading to the outbreak of the Civil War.
Many leaders of the Confederate States were later barred from holding public office after the war.
Textbooks often discuss the economic differences between the Union and the Confederate States.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union - the northern states opposing the Confederacy during the U.S. Civil War
  • Synonym: Confederacy - the government formed by the Confederate States during the American Civil War

Etymology

From 'confederate' (from Latin 'confoederatus', united by a league) and 'states'.
