Acroclinium roseum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.roʊˈklɪn.i.əm ˈroʊ.zi.əm / (AmE), / ˌak.rəʊˈklɪn.i.əm ˈrəʊ.zɪ.əm / (BrE)
A flowering plant native to southwestern Australia, known for its bright pink, daisylike, papery flowers, often grown for drying.
flower with pink papery daisylike blooms

Examples

The dried Acroclinium roseum added a splash of color to the winter bouquet.
Gardeners often plant Acroclinium roseum for its long-lasting pink flowers.
Acroclinium roseum thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is prized for floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink everlasting - another common name for Acroclinium roseum, highlighting its persistent flower heads

Etymology

From genus name Acroclinium (from Greek 'akron' meaning top, and 'klinein' meaning to bend) and Latin 'roseum' (rose-colored).
