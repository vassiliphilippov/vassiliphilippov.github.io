dry wall

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌwɔːl / (BrE)
a wall built by fitting stones together without the use of mortar
a wall of stones without mortar

Examples

Many old fields are bordered by dry wall fences that have stood for centuries.
The farmer repaired the damaged dry wall after the winter storms.
She took a photograph of the ancient dry wall snaking through the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry stone wall - a wall built from stones without mortar (British term)
