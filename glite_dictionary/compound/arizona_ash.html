Arizona ash

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪzoʊnə æʃ / (AmE), / əˈrɪzənə æʃ / (BrE)
a small, shrubby ash tree (Fraxinus velutina) native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico
species of ash tree native to the US Southwest

Examples

The leaves of the Arizona ash turn yellow before falling in autumn.
Landscapers often choose Arizona ash for its rapid growth and drought tolerance.
A mature Arizona ash can provide ample shade in desert gardens.

Etymology

Named after the US state of Arizona, indicating its native range.
