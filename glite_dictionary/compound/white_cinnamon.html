white cinnamon

1. NOUN

/ ˌwaɪt ˈsɪn.ə.mən / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˈsɪn.ə.mən / (BrE)
the highly aromatic inner bark of the Canella winterana tree, used as a condiment and tonic
aromatic bark used as spice and tonic

Examples

In traditional Caribbean medicine, white cinnamon is valued for its beneficial properties.
The chef added a pinch of white cinnamon to the dessert for a delicate, spicy flavor.
You can find white cinnamon sold in specialty spice shops and herbalist stores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - the aromatic bark used as a spice from Cinnamomum species
