rearguard action

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːrˌɡɑrd ˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɪəɡɑːd ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a defensive action, especially one intended to protect a retreating force or delay the progress of an opponent
defensive action to protect a retreat or delay opponent

Examples

The general ordered a rearguard action to slow the enemy's advance while the main army withdrew.
Despite knowing their efforts would fail, the activists mounted a rearguard action against the proposed law.
Her attempts to modernize the department seemed like a rearguard action against inevitable change.

Synset Examples

They fought a rearguard action to protect their allies.
The troops engaged in rearguard action to cover the retreat.
The team performed a rearguard action during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing forces or pulling back

Etymology

From military use of 'rearguard' (the troops protecting the rear of a formation) + 'action'.
