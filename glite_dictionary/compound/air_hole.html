air hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈeə həʊl / (BrE)
a region of low atmospheric pressure or descending air that causes an aircraft to lose height suddenly
sudden low-pressure region for planes

Examples

The plane shuddered as it passed through an air hole over the mountains.
Turbulence was severe, and we dropped suddenly when we hit an air hole.
Pilots are trained to respond quickly when encountering an unexpected air hole during flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downdraft - a downward current of air
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11443142-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈeə həʊl / (BrE)
a hole made to allow the passage of air, especially for ventilation or breathing purposes
hole allowing air to pass through

Examples

Please make sure there is at least one air hole in the lid so the animal can breathe.
The bread rises evenly because the dough has small air holes throughout.
He covered the air hole with tape to keep out the cold draft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilation hole - a hole made to provide ventilation
