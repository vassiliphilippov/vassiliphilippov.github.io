big kahuna

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ kəˈhuːnə / (AmE), / bɪɡ kəˈhuːnə / (BrE)
an important or influential person, especially the most powerful or prominent individual in a particular group or field
a highly important or influential person
informal american

Examples

After the merger, Sarah quickly became the big kahuna in the company.
Everyone wanted to impress the big kahuna at the award ceremony.
If you want that promotion, you'll have to talk to the big kahuna first.

Synset Examples

He’s the big kahuna at the tech conference.
They finally met the big kahuna of the industry.
Being the big kahuna means making tough decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top dog - person who is in charge or the most important
  • Synonym: big cheese - an important or influential person
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'kahuna' (priest, expert) with 'big' as an English intensifier; popularized in 20th-century American English.
