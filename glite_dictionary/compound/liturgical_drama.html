liturgical drama

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈtɝdʒɪkəl ˈdrɑmə / (AmE), / lɪˈtɜːdʒɪkəl ˈdrɑːmə / (BrE)
a type of medieval play based on biblical stories, performed in churches on holy days, usually in Latin and often chanted
medieval church play based on the Bible

Examples

The liturgical drama of the Elx Mystery Play (Misteri d'Elx) traces its origins to the Middle Ages.
Scholars study liturgical drama to understand the evolution of Western theater and religious ceremony.
Many liturgical dramas were performed entirely in Latin for church audiences on feast days.

Synset Examples

The liturgical drama of the Elx Mystery Play (Misteri d'Elx) has its origins in the 13th or 15th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morality play - an allegorical medieval play teaching moral lessons
  • Synonym: mystery play - a medieval play representing biblical stories

Etymology

From Latin 'liturgicus' (of public worship) and Greek 'drama' (action or play).
