blazing star

1. NOUN

/ ˈbleɪzɪŋ stɑr / (AmE), / ˈbleɪzɪŋ stɑː / (BrE)
A biennial plant native to the southwestern United States, having white stems, toothed leaves, and large pale yellow flowers that open in the early morning.
biennial plant with large pale yellow flowers

Examples

The blazing star blooms in the early hours before most other flowers begin to open.
Gardeners in the Southwest often cultivate the blazing star for its striking yellow flowers.
We spotted a field of blazing stars during our morning hike through the desert.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbleɪzɪŋ stɑːr / (AmE), / ˈbleɪzɪŋ stɑː / (BrE)
Any of various North American plants of the genus Liatris, known for racemes or panicles of small, brightly colored flower heads.
North American wildflower (genus Liatris)

Examples

Every summer, the blazing star blooms in vibrant purple across the prairie.
We spotted a cluster of blazing star while hiking in Texas.
