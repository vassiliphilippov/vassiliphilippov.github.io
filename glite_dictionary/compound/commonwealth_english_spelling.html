Commonwealth English spelling

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmənˌwɛlθ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɒmənˌwɛlθ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the standard system of English spelling used in the United Kingdom, Ireland, and most Commonwealth countries, as distinguished from American spelling
spelling standard in UK and Commonwealth countries

Examples

The word "colour" is an example of Commonwealth English spelling as opposed to "color" in American English.
Most textbooks in Australia follow Commonwealth English spelling for consistency with British usage.
Teachers often explain the differences between American and Commonwealth English spelling to their students.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: American English spelling - the standard spelling system used in the United States
