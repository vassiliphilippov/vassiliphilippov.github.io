Fast of Av

1. NOUN

/ fæst əv ɑv / (AmE), / fɑːst ɒv ɑːv / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a major fast day commemorating the destruction of the temples in Jerusalem, observed on the ninth day of the month of Av.
Jewish fast day marking the destruction of the temples

Examples

Many Jews observe the Fast of Av as a day of mourning and prayer.
On the Fast of Av, it is customary to refrain from eating and drinking for 25 hours.
The synagogue was somber during the reading of Lamentations on the Fast of Av.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Tisha B'Av* meaning 'ninth of Av', with 'Av' being the fifth month of the Hebrew calendar.
