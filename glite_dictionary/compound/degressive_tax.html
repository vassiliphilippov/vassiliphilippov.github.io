degressive tax

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡrɛsɪv tæks / (AmE), / dɪˈɡrɛsɪv tæks / (BrE)
a tax in which the rate decreases as the amount subject to taxation increases
tax with decreasing rate as base increases

Examples

The government proposed a degressive tax system to lessen the burden on high-volume producers.
Some economists criticize the degressive tax for favoring those with higher incomes.
A degressive tax can be used to encourage investment in certain industries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive tax - tax in which rate increases as base increases
  • Synonym: regressive tax - tax with decreasing effective rate as income rises

Etymology

From 'degressive', modeled on 'regressive' and 'progressive', with 'tax'. 'Degressive' comes from French 'dégressif', from Latin 'de-' (down) + 'gradi' (to step).
