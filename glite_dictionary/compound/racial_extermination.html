racial extermination

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪʃəl ɪkˌstɝːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəl ɪkˌstɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the systematic killing of an entire racial or cultural group, often as part of a deliberate policy or campaign
systematic killing of a racial group

Examples

The documentary detailed the horrors of racial extermination during the war.
Many survivors still bear the trauma of witnessing racial extermination in their communities.
International law now prosecutes those responsible for acts of racial extermination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genocide - the deliberate killing of a large group of people, especially those of a particular nation or ethnic group
