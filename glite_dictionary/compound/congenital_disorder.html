congenital disorder

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A medical condition or abnormality that is present from birth, often resulting from genetic or environmental factors during development.
medical condition present at birth
medical

Examples

Early detection of a congenital disorder can help improve a child's chances for healthy development.
Genetic counseling may reduce the risk of certain congenital disorders in future pregnancies.
The hospital specializes in the treatment of rare congenital disorders found in newborns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth defect - a physical or biochemical abnormality present at birth

Etymology

From Latin congenitus (born together, innate) and disorder (lack of order or normal function).
