optical prism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
a transparent, typically triangular, piece of glass or quartz used to refract or disperse light
device to refract or disperse light

Examples

When white light passes through an optical prism, it is separated into a spectrum of colors.
The experiment required a high-quality optical prism to accurately manipulate the laser beam.
He observed the inverted image formed by the optical prism during the demonstration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prism - a transparent object, often triangular, used to refract light

Etymology

From 'optical' (relating to sight or light) + 'prism', from Greek 'prisma' (something sawed or shaped).
