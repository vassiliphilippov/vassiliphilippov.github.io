ground rent

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd rɛnt / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd rɛnt / (BrE)
an amount paid periodically by a tenant or leaseholder for the right to occupy and improve a piece of land, typically where the land is owned by someone else
payment for right to occupy land

Examples

The tenant must pay ground rent annually to the owner of the land.
Many leaseholders are concerned about rising ground rent charges on their properties.
The contract specified a nominal ground rent in addition to regular maintenance fees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leasehold - a property held under a lease
  • Antonym: freehold - permanent and absolute tenure of land or property with freedom to dispose of it at will
