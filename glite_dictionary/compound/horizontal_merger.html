horizontal merger

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntl ˈmɝːdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntl ˈmɜːdʒə / (BrE)
a merger between two companies operating in the same business sector, often as competitors at the same stage of production
merger of companies in the same sector

Examples

The government blocked the proposed horizontal merger between the two telecommunications giants.
A horizontal merger can eliminate competition and increase market share for the new company.
Economists often debate the effects of a horizontal merger on consumer prices and industry innovation.

Synset Examples

The example of horizontal merger would be if a health care system buys another health care system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vertical merger - a merger between companies at different stages of production or distribution
