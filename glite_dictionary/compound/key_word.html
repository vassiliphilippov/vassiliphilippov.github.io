key word

1. NOUN

/ ki wɜrd / (AmE), / kiː wɜːd / (BrE)
a word chosen to serve as the essential pattern or sequence for decoding an encrypted message
word used to decode a cipher

Examples

A new key word must be selected to decrypt this year's top-secret communication.
The spy remembered his key word and entered it into the cipher machine.
Without the correct key word, the encoded message could not be translated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keyword - a significant word for indexing or searching
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06504311-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ki wɝːd / (AmE), / kiː wɜːd / (BrE)
a word of particular importance used to identify or categorize the content of a text, especially for indexing or searching
important word used for indexing or searching

Examples

To improve your search results, use a specific key word instead of general terms.
The librarian suggested adding more key words to the database entry.
When writing an abstract, include at least one important key word from your topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keyword - alternative compound, sometimes written as one word
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06303887-n
Synset POS: n
