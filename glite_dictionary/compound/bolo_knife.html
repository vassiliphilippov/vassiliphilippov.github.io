bolo knife

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊloʊ naɪf / (AmE), / ˈbəʊləʊ naɪf / (BrE)
A long, heavy knife with a single-edged blade, traditionally used in the Philippines, typically for agricultural or combat purposes.
long, heavy Philippine knife

Examples

Farmers in the Philippines often use a bolo knife to clear thick brush from fields.
The soldier demonstrated how to sharpen a bolo knife before their jungle training exercise.
He admired the craftsmanship of the ornate bolo knife displayed at the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machete - a broad, heavy knife used as a tool or weapon

Etymology

From Tagalog 'bolo' (large knife).
