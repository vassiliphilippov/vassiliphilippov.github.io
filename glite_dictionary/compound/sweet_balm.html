sweet balm

1. NOUN

/ swit bɑːm / (AmE), / swiːt bɑːm / (BrE)
a bushy perennial herb of the mint family, with small white or yellowish flowers and fragrant, lemon-scented leaves, often grown in gardens; another name for lemon balm (Melissa officinalis)
a fragrant perennial herb; lemon balm

Examples

I planted sweet balm in my herb garden to attract bees and butterflies.
The leaves of sweet balm make a soothing herbal tea.
Sweet balm escaped from gardens and now grows wild in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon balm - a perennial herb with lemon-scented leaves, also known as Melissa officinalis
