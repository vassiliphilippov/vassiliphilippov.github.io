circuit board

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kɪt bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kɪt bɔːd / (BrE)
A flat piece of insulating material on which electronic components are mounted and interconnected, used to build electronic circuits, especially in computers and other devices.
board for mounting electronic circuits

Examples

The graphics card is a circuit board that enhances the computer's visual performance.
Technicians repaired the circuit board to restore the device's functionality.
Every modern smartphone contains a highly complex circuit board inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: printed circuit board - a board used in electronics to mount and connect components using conductive pathways
