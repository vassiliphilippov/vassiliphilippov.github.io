popular front

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələr frʌnt / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊlə frʌnt / (BrE)
A left-wing political coalition consisting of different parties, formed to oppose a common adversary, especially fascist movements.
left-wing political coalition

Examples

During the 1930s, the popular front in France united several leftist groups against rising fascism.
The party decided to join a popular front to increase its chances in the upcoming election.
Historians often debate the effectiveness of the popular front strategy in resisting authoritarian regimes.
