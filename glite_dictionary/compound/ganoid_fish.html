ganoid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæ.nɔɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡan.ɔɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
a primitive type of fish having thick, bony scales with a shiny covering, found in certain ancient and living species
fish with thick, shiny bony scales

Examples

The sturgeon is a well-known example of a ganoid fish with its hard, gleaming scales.
Fossils of ganoid fish reveal much about the evolution of vertebrate species.
Scientists study the scales of ganoid fish to understand their protective adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: teleost - a more advanced group of bony fishes
  • Synonym: gar - a type of living ganoid fish

Etymology

From Greek *ganos* (brightness, sheen) and *-oid* (like), referring to the shiny, enamel-like scales.
