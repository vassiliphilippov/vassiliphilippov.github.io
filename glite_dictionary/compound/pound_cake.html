pound cake

1. NOUN

/ paʊnd keɪk / (AmE), / paʊnd keɪk / (BrE)
a rich, dense cake traditionally made with a pound each of butter, sugar, eggs, and flour
rich, dense cake made with equal parts butter, sugar, eggs, and flour

Examples

She baked a classic pound cake for the family picnic, following her grandmother's recipe.
A slice of pound cake tastes delicious with fresh berries and whipped cream.
The bakery is known for its moist and buttery pound cake.

Etymology

Named for the original recipe, which used a pound of each main ingredient.
