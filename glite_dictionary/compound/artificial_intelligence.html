artificial intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl ɪnˌtɛl.əˈdʒɛns / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl ɪnˌtɛl.ɪˈdʒɛns / (BrE)
The study and creation of computer systems that can perform tasks normally requiring human intelligence, such as learning and problem-solving.
computer systems simulating human intelligence

Examples

Many industries are adopting artificial intelligence to improve efficiency and make better decisions.
Researchers believe that artificial intelligence will transform the way we live and work in the future.
He is pursuing a career in artificial intelligence and hopes to develop smarter robots.

Synset Examples

workers in AI hope to imitate or duplicate intelligence in computers and robots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machine learning - a subset of artificial intelligence focused on self-learning computer systems
  • Antonym: natural intelligence - the intelligence displayed by humans and animals

Etymology

From Latin 'artificialis' (artificial) and Latin 'intelligentia' (understanding, intellect).
