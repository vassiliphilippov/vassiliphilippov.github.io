family Cracticidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kræktɪˌdaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kræktɪˌdaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Australian birds, including butcherbirds, currawongs, and related species, formerly classified with the shrikes (Laniidae).
Australian bird family (butcherbirds, currawongs, etc.)

Examples

The family Cracticidae comprises several species of Australian butcherbirds and currawongs.
Taxonomists once included the family Cracticidae within the shrike family, Laniidae.
Members of the family Cracticidae are known for their complex vocalizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcherbird - A type of bird in the family Cracticidae
  • Antonym: family Laniidae - The shrike family, formerly considered to include Cracticidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cracticidae, based on Cracticus (genus name), ultimately from Greek kraktikos (noisy or shrill).
