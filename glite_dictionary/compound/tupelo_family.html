tupelo family

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːpəˌloʊ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈtjuːpɪˌləʊ ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family of flowering trees in the order Myrtales, including the sour gum trees (genus Nyssa).
plant family including sour gum trees

Examples

The tupelo family is known for its water-resistant wood, which is especially valued in carving.
Botanists classify sour gum trees within the tupelo family due to their similar floral structures.
The wetland habitat is home to a variety of species from the tupelo family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nyssaceae - The scientific name for the tupelo family
