Congress of Industrial Organizations

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːŋɡrəs əv ˌɪndəˈstrɪəl ˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs əv ˌɪndəˈstrɪəl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃənz / (BrE)
a federation of North American industrial unions, founded in 1935, that merged with the American Federation of Labor in 1955
historic US labor union federation

Examples

The Congress of Industrial Organizations played a major role in organizing workers in the 1930s.
After the merger in 1955, the Congress of Industrial Organizations ceased to exist as a separate entity.
Leaders of the Congress of Industrial Organizations advocated for workers' rights and fair wages.
