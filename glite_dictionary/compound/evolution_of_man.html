evolution of man

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛvəˈluːʃən əv mæn / (AmE), / ˌiːvəˈluːʃən əv mæn / (BrE)
the gradual development of humans from earlier forms of life over millions of years
human evolutionary development

Examples

The museum exhibit traces the evolution of man from early primates to modern Homo sapiens.
Many textbooks include diagrams illustrating the evolution of man through fossil records.
Debates about the evolution of man have shaped both science and philosophy.

Synset Examples

They studied the evolution of man through prehistory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human evolution - the scientific study of how humans evolved
  • Antonym: creationism - the belief that humans were created as described in the Bible

Etymology

From Latin 'evolutio' (unrolling, development) and Old English 'mann' (person, human being).
