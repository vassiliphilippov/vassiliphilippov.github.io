black swan

1. NOUN

/ blæk swɑn / (AmE), / blak swɒn / (BrE)
a large Australian swan with black feathers and a red bill
large Australian swan with black plumage

Examples

During their trip to Australia, they spotted a rare black swan gliding across the lake.
The black swan is famous for its striking plumage and graceful movements.
John took several photographs of a black swan swimming peacefully in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swan - a large waterbird with a long neck and white or black plumage
  • Degree Lower: mute swan - common white European swan

2. NOUN

/ blæk swɑn / (AmE), / blæk swɒn / (BrE)
an unpredictable or unforeseen event, typically one with extreme consequences
unpredictable, high-impact event

Examples

The financial crisis of 2008 is often cited as a black swan that caught economists and policymakers by surprise.
A pandemic was the kind of black swan event many governments failed to prepare for.
His sudden success was a black swan that none of his competitors had anticipated.

Synset Examples

Her unexpected resignation was a black swan for the team.
They never anticipated such a black swan in their planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outlier - something outside the norm; rare or exceptional case
  • Antonym: normal event - an expected or routine occurrence

Etymology

Popularized by Nassim Nicholas Taleb in his 2007 book 'The Black Swan'; originally from the European belief that all swans were white until black swans were discovered in Australia.
