desensitisation procedure

1. NOUN

/ diːˌsɛnsəˌtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒər / (AmE), / diːˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a technique in behavior therapy for treating phobias or anxiety disorders, where a person is gradually exposed to a feared object or situation under relaxed conditions until their anxiety decreases or disappears
gradual exposure therapy for anxiety or phobias

Examples

The therapist recommended a desensitisation procedure to help him overcome his fear of spiders.
Many patients benefit from a desensitisation procedure when dealing with severe anxiety disorders.
A successful desensitisation procedure can enable individuals to face situations that once triggered intense fear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic desensitisation - a structured form of desensitisation procedure used in behavior therapy
  • Synonym: exposure therapy - a method of treating anxiety by exposing patients to fears

Etymology

From 'desensitisation' (from Latin de- 'away from' + 'sensitise', based on 'sensus' meaning 'sense') and 'procedure' (from Latin 'procedere', meaning 'to go forward').
