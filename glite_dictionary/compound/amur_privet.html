Amur privet

1. NOUN

/ əˈmjʊɹ ˈprɪvət / (AmE), / əˈmjʊə ˈprɪvɪt / (BrE)
an eastern Asian shrub (Ligustrum amurense) often grown as an ornamental for its dense, persistent foliage
eastern Asian ornamental shrub

Examples

The Amur privet is popular for creating low-maintenance hedges in urban gardens.
Gardeners value the Amur privet for its ability to retain leaves through winter.
She planted Amur privet along the driveway for a thick, evergreen screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privet - a shrub of the genus Ligustrum often used for hedges

Etymology

Amur refers to the Amur River region in Asia; 'privet' from Old French 'privet'.
