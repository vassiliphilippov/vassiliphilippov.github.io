five-fingered maidenhair fern

1. NOUN

/ faɪv ˈfɪŋɡɚd ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr fɜrn / (AmE), / faɪv ˈfɪŋɡəd ˈmeɪd(ə)nˌheə fɜːn / (BrE)
A hardy North American fern with palmate, recurved branches and lustrous dark reddish stems.
North American fern with palmate branches and reddish stems

Examples

The five-fingered maidenhair fern thrives in the shaded woodlands of North America.
Gardeners often admire the delicate structure of the five-fingered maidenhair fern.
After planting a five-fingered maidenhair fern, be sure to keep the soil consistently moist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - Any fern of the genus Adiantum, known for delicate fronds
