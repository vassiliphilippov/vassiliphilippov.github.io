jerboa rat

1. NOUN

/ dʒərˈboʊ.ə ræt / (AmE), / dʒəˈbɔː.ə ræt / (BrE)
a large Australian rodent with long hind legs adapted for leaping
Australian jumping rodent

Examples

The jerboa rat is known for its impressive leaping ability in the Australian outback.
Researchers observed the jerboa rat adapting quickly to changes in its environment.
Many predators find it difficult to catch a jerboa rat because of its powerful hind legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat - a small rodent, typically with a long tail

Etymology

From 'jerboa' (a jumping rodent of North Africa and Asia) and 'rat', named for its leaping resemblance.
