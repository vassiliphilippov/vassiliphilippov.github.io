light sleep

1. NOUN

/ laɪt sliːp / (AmE), / laɪt sliːp / (BrE)
a short or shallow sleep in which a person is easily awakened and does not reach deep sleep stages
a brief, easily disturbed sleep

Examples

He fell into a light sleep while waiting for his flight at the airport.
During light sleep, people may wake up easily from minor noise or movement.
She only managed a light sleep before her alarm went off in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - a short period of sleep, especially during the day
  • Antonym: deep sleep - a stage of sleep marked by slow brain waves and physical rest, difficult to wake from
