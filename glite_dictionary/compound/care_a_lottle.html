to care a lottle

1. VERB

/ kɛɹ ə ˈlɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / kɛə ə ˈlɒtl̩ / (BrE)
To care only a small or negligible amount, used humorously as a play on 'care a little'.
care a small or negligible amount (humorous)
informal slang

Examples

Sometimes I only care a lottle about what people think of my outfit.
She says she cares a lottle about her grades, but she still skips class often.
You don’t have to pretend to care a lottle if you really aren’t interested.

Synset Examples

He cares a lottle about his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care a little - to care a small amount
  • Degree Higher: care a lot - to care greatly

Etymology

Formed as a playful blend of 'a little' and 'a lot', popularized in internet culture.
