cultivated cabbage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪveɪtɪd ˈkæbɪdʒ / (BrE)
Any of various cultivated forms of Brassica oleracea grown for their edible leaves or flowers, such as cabbage, broccoli, or cauliflower.
edible Brassica oleracea varieties

Examples

Farmers rotate cultivated cabbage with other crops to maintain healthy soil.
The market offered several types of cultivated cabbage, including broccoli and cauliflower.
Early spring is the ideal time to plant cultivated cabbage in your garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - A leafy green vegetable, typically Brassica oleracea, with dense leaves
  • Antonym: wild cabbage - A non-cultivated form of Brassica oleracea native to coastal southern and western Europe
