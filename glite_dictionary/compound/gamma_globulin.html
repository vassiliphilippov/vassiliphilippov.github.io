gamma globulin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmə ˈɡlɑbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡamə ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a class of plasma proteins, chiefly immunoglobulins, that play a major role in the body's immune responses
class of immune-response plasma proteins
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an injection of gamma globulin to help boost her immunity.
After traveling to the region, he received a gamma globulin shot for hepatitis protection.
Low levels of gamma globulin can make someone more susceptible to infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - any of a class of proteins functioning as antibodies in the immune response

Etymology

From Greek 'gamma' (the third letter of the Greek alphabet) + Latin 'globulus' (small sphere), indicating its shape and electrophoretic mobility.
