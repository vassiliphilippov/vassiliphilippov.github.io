calamine lotion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləˌmaɪn ˈloʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈkæləmɪn ˈləʊʃən / (BrE)
a liquid preparation containing calamine, used to relieve itching or mild skin irritations
medicated lotion for relieving skin irritation
medical

Examples

Apply a thin layer of calamine lotion to soothe the chickenpox rash.
He used calamine lotion to relieve the itch from insect bites.
The doctor recommended calamine lotion for her mild sunburn.

Etymology

From 'calamine', which comes from French 'calamine', from Medieval Latin 'calamina' (zinc ore), and 'lotion', from Latin 'lotio' (washing).
