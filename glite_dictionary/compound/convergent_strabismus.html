convergent strabismus

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝːdʒənt strəˈbɪzməs / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒənt strəˈbɪzməs / (BrE)
A type of strabismus in which one or both eyes turn inward toward the nose.
strabismus with inward-turning eyes
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with convergent strabismus and began treatment with corrective lenses.
Children with convergent strabismus may experience difficulties with depth perception if left untreated.
A specialist explained that convergent strabismus can sometimes require surgical intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esotropia - a form of strabismus where one or both eyes turn inward
  • Antonym: divergent strabismus - strabismus in which one or both eyes turn outward away from the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'convergere' (to approach, come together) and Greek 'strabismos' (a squinting).
