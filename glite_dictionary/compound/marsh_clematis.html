marsh clematis

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈklɛm.ə.tɪs / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈklɛm.ə.tɪs / (BrE)
a climbing plant native to the southern United States, characterized by bluish-purple flowers
climbing plant with bluish-purple flowers

Examples

The marsh clematis blooms beautifully in wetland areas during the spring.
Gardeners in the southern United States often cultivate marsh clematis for its attractive flowers.
The bluish-purple blossoms of marsh clematis attract bees and butterflies alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clematis - any climbing plant of the genus Clematis
