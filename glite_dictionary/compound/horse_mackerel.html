horse mackerel

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrs ˌmækərl / (AmE), / ˌhɔːs ˈmæk.ər.əl / (BrE)
a large, elongated food fish of the Atlantic waters of Europe, commonly valued for its meat
a European Atlantic food fish

Examples

Fishermen caught several horse mackerel during their trip off the coast of Spain.
The restaurant specializes in grilled horse mackerel served with lemon and herbs.
Scientists noted a decline in the local horse mackerel population over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack mackerel - a related species of food fish found in other regions
  • Degree Higher: mackerel - a general term for a group of fast-swimming marine fish

2. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈmækɚəl / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈmækərəl / (BrE)
a fish of various species, especially in the jack family, found in the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans and used as food, especially in California.
a fish used as food, esp. in California

Examples

Many anglers in California hope to catch horse mackerel during the summer fishing season.
The restaurant served a delicious grilled horse mackerel with lemon and herbs.
Biologists are studying the migration patterns of the horse mackerel along the Pacific coast.
