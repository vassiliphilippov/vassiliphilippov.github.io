Zion National Park

1. PROPN

/ ˈzaɪ.ən ˈnæʃ.ən.əl pɑrk / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ən ˈnæʃ.ən.əl pɑːk / (BrE)
a national park in southwestern Utah, United States, known for its dramatic canyons, sandstone cliffs, and scenic gorges carved by mountain rivers
national park in Utah, USA with canyons and cliffs

Examples

Zion National Park attracts millions of visitors each year who come to hike its trails and admire the stunning scenery.
We spent our summer vacation exploring the canyons of Zion National Park in Utah.
The towering sandstone cliffs are one of the most famous features of Zion National Park.

Etymology

Named after Zion Canyon, which was named by Mormon settlers after the biblical Zion.
