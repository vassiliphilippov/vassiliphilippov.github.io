mooring tower

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪŋ ˌtaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪŋ ˌtaʊə / (BrE)
a tall structure used for securing airships to the ground when not in flight
structure for anchoring airships

Examples

The Zeppelin docked at the mooring tower outside the city limits.
Engineers inspected the mooring tower for signs of wear after a storm.
During the golden age of airships, every major airport had a mooring tower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooring mast - a tall pole or structure used to anchor airships
