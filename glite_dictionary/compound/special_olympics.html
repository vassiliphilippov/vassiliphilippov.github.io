Special Olympics

1. PROPN

/ ˈspɛʃəl əˈlɪmpɪks / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl əˈlɪmpɪks / (BrE)
An international sports organization that holds athletic competitions, similar to the Olympic Games, for people with intellectual or physical disabilities.
international sports competitions for people with disabilities

Examples

Many athletes train hard all year to compete in the Special Olympics.
Her volunteer work with the Special Olympics inspired others in the community to get involved.
The city hosted the Special Olympics for the first time last summer.

Etymology

Formed from 'special' (referring to people with disabilities) and 'Olympics' (international athletic games, from Greek Olympia).
