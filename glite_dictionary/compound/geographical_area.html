geographical area

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˌɑːɡrəˈfɪkl ˈɛriə / (AmE), / dʒiˌɒɡrəˈfɪkl ˈeəriə / (BrE)
A region of the Earth's surface distinguished or defined by specific boundaries or characteristics.
region with defined geographic boundaries

Examples

This geographical area is known for its unique plant species and diverse wildlife.
Each geographical area has its own weather patterns and climate conditions.
The study focused on the economic development of rural geographical areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: region - an area, especially part of a country or the world, having definable characteristics
  • Synonym: zone - an area or stretch of land having a particular characteristic, purpose, or use

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' (earth) and 'graphia' (writing or description), plus 'area' from Latin 'area' (open space).
