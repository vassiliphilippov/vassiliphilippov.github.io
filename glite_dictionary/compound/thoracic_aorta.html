thoracic aorta

1. NOUN

/ θɔˈræsɪk eɪˈɔɹtə / (AmE), / θɔːˈræsɪk eɪˈɔːtə / (BrE)
the part of the aorta that passes through the chest (thorax), supplying blood to the chest wall and organs within the thorax
section of the aorta in the chest
medical

Examples

A tear in the thoracic aorta can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
The surgeon examined the thoracic aorta before proceeding with the bypass procedure.
Plaque build-up in the thoracic aorta may restrict blood flow to vital organs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: abdominal aorta - section of the aorta below the thoracic region, supplying blood to the abdomen
  • Degree Lower: aortic arch - curved portion of the aorta between the ascending and descending aorta

Etymology

From Greek 'thōrax' (breastplate, chest) and Latin 'aorta' (main artery).
