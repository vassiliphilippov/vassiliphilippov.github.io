water chinquapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər tʃɪŋˌkwɑːpɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə tʃɪŋˌkwɒpɪn / (BrE)
A water lily native to eastern North America, known for its pale yellow flowers and edible, globular nutlike seeds.
North American water lily with edible seeds

Examples

The pond was covered with water chinquapin, its pale yellow flowers standing out against the green leaves.
Native Americans historically used the seeds of the water chinquapin as a food source.
We spotted several water chinquapins during our canoe trip through the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large floating leaves and showy flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ tʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə tʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (BrE)
the edible nutlike seed of the American lotus, noted for its flavor similar to a chinquapin
edible seed of the American lotus

Examples

The water chinquapin was once an important food for Native Americans in some regions.
Many wildfowl feed on water chinquapin seeds growing in shallow ponds and lakes.
Some chefs use water chinquapin as a unique addition to their dishes due to its distinctive taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lotus seed - edible seeds of a lotus plant
  • Degree Lower: chinquapin - a type of edible nut or tree in the beech family

Etymology

Formed from 'water' + 'chinquapin', referring to the aquatic plant and the resemblance in flavor.
