long hour

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ˈaʊɚ / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈaʊə / (BrE)
an extended period of time spent working or engaged in an activity, often longer than usual or expected
an extended work period

Examples

After the merger, the whole team put in long hours to meet project deadlines.
She spent a long hour waiting at the airport for her delayed flight.
As a nurse, he regularly works long hours during the holiday season.

Synset Examples

He worked a long hour to meet the deadline.
She spent a long hour on the phone with customer service.
As a city banker, he worked long hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtime - work done in addition to regular working hours
  • Antonym: short hour - a shorter than usual working period
