scrofulous condition

1. NOUN

/ ˈskroʊ.fjələs kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈskrɒf.jʊ.ləs kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by swollen or enlarged lymph nodes, especially in the neck, often as a result of tuberculosis infection
disease with swollen lymph nodes
medical

Examples

The physician identified a scrofulous condition in the patient's neck during the examination.
Children in the 19th century were sometimes afflicted with a scrofulous condition related to tuberculosis.
A scrofulous condition may require prolonged treatment and careful monitoring by healthcare professionals.

Synset Examples

The doctor noted the scrofulous condition of the patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrofula - tuberculous swelling of the lymph nodes, especially of the neck
  • Synonym: tuberculosis - infectious disease affecting the lungs and sometimes other parts of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'scrofula' (lymphatic gland disease), diminutive of 'scrofa' (breeding sow), due to the appearance of the glands.
