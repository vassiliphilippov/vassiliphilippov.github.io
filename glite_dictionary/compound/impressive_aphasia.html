impressive aphasia

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɛsɪv əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛsɪv əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A type of aphasia characterized by fluent but meaningless speech and a severe impairment in understanding spoken or written words.
fluent, meaningless speech with poor comprehension (aphasia)
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with impressive aphasia after noting his fluent but nonsensical speech.
People with impressive aphasia often cannot understand what is being said to them, even if they speak easily themselves.
Unlike expressive aphasia, impressive aphasia severely impairs comprehension while speech remains fluent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptive aphasia - Aphasia in which understanding language is impaired, also known as Wernicke's aphasia
  • Antonym: expressive aphasia - Aphasia characterized by difficulty producing language, also called Broca's aphasia

Etymology

From Latin 'impressivus' (able to effect) and Greek 'aphatos' (speechless).
