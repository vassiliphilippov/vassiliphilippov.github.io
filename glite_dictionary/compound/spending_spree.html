spending spree

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛndɪŋ spri / (AmE), / ˈspɛndɪŋ spriː / (BrE)
a short period of extravagant or excessive spending, often in a carefree manner
brief period of excessive spending

Examples

After receiving her bonus, Jane went on a spending spree at the mall.
The company was criticized for its spending spree on luxury offices.
A sudden spending spree can quickly drain your savings if you're not careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shopping spree - a brief period of buying many things, especially for pleasure

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛndɪŋ spri / (AmE), / ˈspɛndɪŋ spriː / (BrE)
A short period during which someone spends a lot of money, often in an unrestrained or impulsive way.
period of excessive spending

Examples

They went on a spending spree after winning the lottery and bought several new cars.
Following the holiday season, Sarah regretted her spending spree when she saw her credit card bill.
The company's annual bonus led to a spending spree among employees at the electronics store.

Synset Examples

After his spending spree, he joked about needing a cashectomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splurge - an act of spending money freely or extravagantly
