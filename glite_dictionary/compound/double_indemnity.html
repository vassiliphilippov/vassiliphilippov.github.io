double indemnity

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ɪnˈdɛm.nə.ti / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ɪnˈdem.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
a clause in an insurance policy that pays twice the stated amount in the event of accidental death
insurance clause paying twice in case of accidental death
legal

Examples

His life insurance policy included a double indemnity provision in case of accidental death.
Because of the double indemnity clause, her beneficiaries received twice the usual payout.
The lawyer explained how a double indemnity claim could be filed after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indemnity - security or protection against financial loss
