visual defect

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒu.əl dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
an impairment or abnormality in the ability to see, affecting the sense of sight
impairment of the sense of sight
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a visual defect that required corrective lenses.
If left untreated, a minor visual defect can worsen over time.
Some children are born with a visual defect that affects their ability to read and write.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vision impairment - decreased ability to see to a degree that causes problems not fixable by usual means
  • Degree Higher: blindness - the condition of lacking visual perception
