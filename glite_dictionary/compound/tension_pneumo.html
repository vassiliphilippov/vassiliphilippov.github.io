tension pneumo

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnʃən ˈnuːmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɛnʃən ˈnjuːməʊ / (BrE)
A medical emergency in which air becomes trapped in the chest cavity under pressure, potentially collapsing the lung and compressing the heart and other vital structures.
collapsed lung with trapped air (medical emergency)
medical

Examples

A tension pneumo requires immediate intervention to prevent cardiac arrest.
After the accident, doctors quickly identified a tension pneumo using a chest X-ray.
If left untreated, a tension pneumo can lead to rapid clinical deterioration.

Synset Examples

They rushed him to surgery for the tension pneumo.
The doctor diagnosed him with tension pneumo after the accident.
Tension pneumo can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tension pneumothorax - full term for tension pneumo
  • Degree Lower: pneumothorax - air in the chest cavity (less severe)

Etymology

Shortened form of 'tension pneumothorax' from Greek pneuma (air, breath) and thorax (chest).
