genus Lycosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs laɪˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lɪˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Lycosidae, comprising wolf spiders found in various regions worldwide.
type genus of wolf spiders

Examples

The genus Lycosa includes many species commonly referred to as wolf spiders.
Scientists have classified several large, agile hunting spiders under the genus Lycosa.
Members of the genus Lycosa are known for their keen eyesight and fast movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf spider - a member of the family Lycosidae; hunting spider known as wolf spider

Etymology

From Latin 'lycosa' (a kind of spider), from Greek 'lykos' (wolf), referring to 'wolf spider'.
