black catechu

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈkæt̬ʃu / (AmE), / blak ˈkætʃuː / (BrE)
an extract from the heartwood of Acacia catechu, used for dyeing, tanning, preserving fishnets and sails, and formerly in medicine.
extract from Acacia catechu used in dyeing and tanning

Examples

The fishermen treated their nets with black catechu to protect them from decay.
Textile manufacturers often used black catechu as a natural dye for fabrics.
In traditional medicine, black catechu was once used as an astringent.

Etymology

From Malay 'kachu' or Hindi 'kattha', referring to the extract from the Acacia catechu tree.
