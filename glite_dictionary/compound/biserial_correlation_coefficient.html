biserial correlation coefficient

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈsɪriəl kəˌrleɪʃən koʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (AmE), / baɪˈsɪəriəl kəˌlɛɪʃən kəʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a statistical measure used to determine the relationship between a continuous variable and a dichotomous variable
statistical measure involving continuous and dichotomous variables
medical

Examples

The biserial correlation coefficient is often used in psychological research when one variable is binary.
To validate the results, the researcher calculated the biserial correlation coefficient between test scores and gender.
A high biserial correlation coefficient indicates a strong association between the continuous and dichotomous variables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation coefficient - statistical measure expressing the degree of relationship between two variables

Etymology

From 'bi-' meaning two, and 'serial'; formed within statistical terminology.
