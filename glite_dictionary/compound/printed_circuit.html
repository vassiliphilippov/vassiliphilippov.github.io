printed circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntɪd ˈsɝːkət / (AmE), / ˈprɪntɪd ˈsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
an electronic circuit in which conductive paths are printed or etched on an insulating board, allowing components to be connected without traditional wiring
electronic circuit on a board with printed connections

Examples

The technician carefully examined the printed circuit for signs of damage.
Modern electronic devices rely on the efficiency of the printed circuit to connect their components.
They repaired the broken trace on the printed circuit board to fix the malfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: printed circuit board - board on which a printed circuit is mounted
