family Sphingidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sfɪŋˈɡɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli sfɪŋˈɡɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of moths known as hawkmoths, characterized by their swift flight and ability to hover, commonly found worldwide.
taxonomic family of hawkmoths

Examples

The family Sphingidae includes more than 1,400 species of hawkmoths worldwide.
Many members of the family Sphingidae are known for their ability to hover while feeding on nectar.
Entomologists study the family Sphingidae to better understand moth pollination behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkmoth - a moth in the family Sphingidae, noted for rapid flight

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sphingidae', based on 'Sphinx', the type genus, and standard taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
