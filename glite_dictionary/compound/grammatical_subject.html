grammatical subject

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈmætɪkəl ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmatɪkəl ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
The noun, noun phrase, or pronoun in a sentence that typically indicates what the sentence is about and performs the action or is described.
the main noun or pronoun in a sentence

Examples

In the sentence 'Birds sing at dawn,' 'Birds' is the grammatical subject.
Even in the passive voice, the grammatical subject may be acted upon rather than performing the action.
To find the grammatical subject, look for the noun or pronoun that the verb agrees with.

Synset Examples

In the sentence ‘the bear was killed by the hunter’, the topicalized goal of the action (‘the bear’) is the grammatical subject of the passive sentence and is acted upon by the agent (‘the hunter’), which is the logical, but not the grammatical, subject of the passive sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subject - the main noun or pronoun doing or being something in a sentence

Etymology

From Latin 'grammaticalis' (relating to grammar) and 'subjectum' (something placed underneath, subject).
