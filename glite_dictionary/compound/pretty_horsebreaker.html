pretty horsebreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛti ˈhɔrsˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈprɛti ˈhɔːsbɹeɪkə / (BrE)
a woman skilled in training and breaking horses, especially as noted in 19th-century Britain
woman skilled in training horses
archaic british

Examples

In Victorian England, a pretty horsebreaker could achieve both fame and notoriety.
Many admired the confidence and skill of the pretty horsebreaker as she rode through the countryside.
The novel describes her transformation from a timid girl to a renowned pretty horsebreaker.

Synset Examples

She was known as a pretty horsebreaker among the locals.

Etymology

Compound of 'pretty' (attractive) and 'horsebreaker' (one who trains horses), associated with Victorian England.
