greater yellowlegs

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈjɛloʊˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈjɛləʊˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
a large North American shorebird (Tringa melanoleuca) with long yellow legs and a slender bill, found in marshes and wetlands.
large North American marsh bird

Examples

The greater yellowlegs is often seen wading through shallow marshes searching for food.
Birdwatchers spotted a flock of greater yellowlegs during their migration along the Atlantic coast.
Unlike the lesser yellowlegs, the greater yellowlegs has a noticeably longer bill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lesser yellowlegs - a smaller species of yellowlegs shorebird (Tringa flavipes)
