New Zealand cotton

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈziːlənd ˈkɑtn̩ / (AmE), / njuː ˈziːlənd ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
a fiber obtained from the bast of New Zealand ribbonwood trees, resembling true cotton fiber
fiber from New Zealand ribbonwood trees

Examples

The fabric was made using New Zealand cotton instead of traditional cotton.
Some textile manufacturers value New Zealand cotton for its silky texture.
Researchers are testing the durability of New Zealand cotton in various applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft, white fiber from the cotton plant
