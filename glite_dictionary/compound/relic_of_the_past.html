relic of the past

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪk əv ðə pæst / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪk əv ðə pɑːst / (BrE)
an object, custom, or idea that remains from a much earlier time, often considered outdated or obsolete
an object or idea from an earlier era

Examples

That typewriter is a relic of the past in today's digital world.
The tradition is charming but now mostly a relic of the past.
Her grandmother's recipes are treasured as precious relics of the past.

Synset Examples

The old icehouse was a relic from the past.
The razee was a relic of the past, now unusable.
The old scythe hung in the barn, a relic of the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relic - an object surviving from an earlier time, typically with historical interest
