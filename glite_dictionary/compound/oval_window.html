oval window

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vəl ˈwɪn.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəl ˈwɪn.dəʊ / (BrE)
a membrane-covered opening in the inner wall of the middle ear to which the base of the stapes is attached, transmitting sound vibrations to the inner ear
membrane opening in the middle ear
medical

Examples

The vibration from the stapes is transferred through the oval window to the fluid-filled cochlea.
Damage to the oval window can result in hearing loss and balance problems.
Doctors examined the patient's oval window during the ear surgery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: round window - another membrane-covered opening in the middle ear
