returning officer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɝnɪŋ ˈɑfəsɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːnɪŋ ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
the official responsible for overseeing an election in a particular electoral district and certifying the results
official who oversees and certifies election results

Examples

The returning officer announced the final vote counts late in the evening.
Each constituency has a returning officer to supervise the electoral process.
After the ballots were tallied, the returning officer declared the winning candidate.

Etymology

From 'return' (as in to send back results) + 'officer'; used in British and Commonwealth election contexts.
