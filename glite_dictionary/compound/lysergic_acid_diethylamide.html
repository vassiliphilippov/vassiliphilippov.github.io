lysergic acid diethylamide

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌsɜrdʒɪk ˈæsɪd daɪˌɛθɪlˈæmaɪd / (AmE), / laɪˌsɜːdʒɪk ˈæsɪd daɪˌiːθɪlˈæmaɪd / (BrE)
a powerful synthetic hallucinogenic drug, often abbreviated as LSD, derived from lysergic acid
a hallucinogenic drug (LSD)

Examples

The effects of lysergic acid diethylamide can last for up to 12 hours.
He was researching the impact of lysergic acid diethylamide on brain activity.
In the 1960s, lysergic acid diethylamide was associated with countercultural movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LSD - common abbreviation for lysergic acid diethylamide, a hallucinogenic drug
  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a drug that causes hallucinations

Etymology

From lysergic acid (a compound derived from ergot fungus) and diethylamide (a chemical suffix denoting its amide structure).
