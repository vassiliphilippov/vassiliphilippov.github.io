black grama

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈɡræmə / (AmE), / blæk ˈɡræmə / (BrE)
A perennial pasture grass native to the western United States and northern Mexico, valued for grazing livestock.
pasture grass of western North America

Examples

Cattle in the Southwest often graze on black grama during the dry season.
Healthy stands of black grama are important for erosion control in arid grasslands.
Researchers measured the recovery of black grama after a wildfire in New Mexico.
