oxidation-reduction indicator

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkˌsɪˈdeɪʃən rɪˈdʌkʃən ˌɪndɪˈkeɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdeɪʃən rɪˈdʌkʃən ˌɪndɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a chemical indicator that undergoes a reversible color change depending on the oxidation or reduction state of the solution
chemical indicator for redox states

Examples

A common oxidation-reduction indicator in titrations is methylene blue, which changes color depending on the solution's redox state.
The chemist added an oxidation-reduction indicator to the reaction to monitor its progress visually.
Using an oxidation-reduction indicator helps determine the endpoint in redox titrations by a noticeable color shift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redox indicator - an indicator that marks the presence of oxidation-reduction reactions

Etymology

Composed of 'oxidation' and 'reduction', from Latin roots, and 'indicator', from Latin indicare (to indicate).
