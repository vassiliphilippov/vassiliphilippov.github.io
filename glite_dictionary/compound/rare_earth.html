rare earth

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛr ˈɝːθ / (AmE), / ˌreə ˈɜːθ / (BrE)
any of a set of metallic chemical elements in the lanthanide series, with atomic numbers 57 through 71, often used in electronics and industry.
metallic element in the lanthanide series

Examples

The magnets in smartphones often contain rare earth elements for greater efficiency.
China produces a significant share of the world's rare earth metals needed for high-tech industries.
Scientists are searching for new ways to recycle rare earth materials from used electronics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanthanide - any element from lanthanum to lutetium in the periodic table
  • Degree Higher: transition metal - any element in groups 3 through 12 of the periodic table

Etymology

Refers to the historical difficulty in obtaining these elements in pure form.
