redundancy check

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʌndənsi tʃɛk / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌndənsi tʃɛk / (BrE)
A method or system used to detect errors in the transmission or storage of data, often by adding redundant information.
system for detecting data errors

Examples

Many computers use a redundancy check to identify errors during data transmission.
The engineer implemented a redundancy check to increase the reliability of the system.
Without a proper redundancy check, corrupted data might go undetected.
