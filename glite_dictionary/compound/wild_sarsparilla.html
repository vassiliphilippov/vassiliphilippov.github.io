wild sarsparilla

1. NOUN

/ waɪld sɑːrspəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / waɪld sɑːspəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
a perennial North American herb (Aralia nudicaulis) with aromatic roots used as a substitute for sarsaparilla
North American herb with aromatic roots

Examples

The aromatic root of wild sarsparilla was once used by Indigenous peoples in traditional medicine.
In the spring, wild sarsparilla spreads quickly across forest floors in central and eastern North America.
Some old recipes for homemade beverages called for wild sarsparilla instead of sarsaparilla root.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarsaparilla - a tropical American plant whose roots are used for flavoring drinks

Etymology

From 'wild' meaning growing in a natural state, and 'sarsaparilla', from Spanish 'zarzaparrilla' (a trailing plant).
