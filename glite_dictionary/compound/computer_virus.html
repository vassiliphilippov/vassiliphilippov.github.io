computer virus

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpjuːt̬ɚ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtə ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
A malicious software program designed to reproduce itself and often cause damage by altering or destroying data or programs on a computer.
malicious self-replicating computer program

Examples

A computer virus can infect a system through an email attachment if you're not careful.
Outdated antivirus software failed to catch the latest computer virus.
He lost all his important files after a computer virus corrupted his hard drive.

Synset Examples

a true virus cannot spread to another computer without human assistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malware - software intended to damage or disable computers
  • Degree Higher: worm - self-replicating computer program that spreads without attaching to files
  • Synonym: trojan horse - malicious software disguised as legitimate software

Etymology

Formed by analogy with biological viruses; 'virus' from Latin meaning 'poison'.
