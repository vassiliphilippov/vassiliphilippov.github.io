genus Lagopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlæɡəpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlæɡəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the grouse family, known as ptarmigans, found in arctic and subarctic regions.
genus of ptarmigans (birds)

Examples

The genus Lagopus includes several species of ptarmigan adapted to cold climates.
Researchers studied the plumage of birds in the genus Lagopus to understand their camouflage.
All species within the genus Lagopus are well-camouflaged for snowy environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ptarmigan - A northern bird in the grouse family, belonging to genus Lagopus

Etymology

From New Latin Lagopus, from Ancient Greek lagōpous 'hare-foot', referring to feathered feet.
