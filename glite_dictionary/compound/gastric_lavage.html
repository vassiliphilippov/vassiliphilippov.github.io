gastric lavage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrɪk ləˈvɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk ləˈvɑːʒ / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which the stomach is washed out with water or a saline solution to remove ingested substances, such as poisons or blood
medical stomach-washing procedure
medical

Examples

The patient was rushed to the emergency room for a gastric lavage after ingesting a toxic substance.
Gastric lavage is often performed when someone has overdosed on certain medications.
In rare cases, a gastric lavage may be required to clear the stomach of blood following severe bleeding.

Synset Examples

when the doctor ordered a gastric lavage the hospital pumped out my stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomach pump - device or procedure for removing substances from the stomach

Etymology

From French 'lavage' (washing) and 'gastric' from Greek 'gaster' (stomach).
