Bedlington terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdlɪŋtən ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˈbɛdlɪŋtən ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
A small breed of dog, originally from England, with a curly, pale coat and a distinctive lamb-like appearance.
lamb-like breed of small terrier dog

Examples

The Bedlington terrier is well known for its gentle temperament and unique lamb-like look.
At the dog show, her Bedlington terrier attracted a lot of attention for its unusual coat.
John has been training his Bedlington terrier for agility competitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of small energetic dog originally bred for hunting

Etymology

Named after the town of Bedlington in Northumberland, England, where the breed was first developed.
