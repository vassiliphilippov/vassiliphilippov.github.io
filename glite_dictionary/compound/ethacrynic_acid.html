ethacrynic acid

1. NOUN

/ ɛθəˌkrɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌiːθəˌkrɪnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a diuretic drug used to treat edema, especially in patients allergic to sulfa drugs
a diuretic drug for treating edema
medical

Examples

Patients who are allergic to sulfa drugs may be prescribed ethacrynic acid as a diuretic alternative.
The doctor adjusted the dosage of ethacrynic acid to treat the patient's persistent edema.
Prolonged use of ethacrynic acid can sometimes cause hearing problems as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diuretic - a substance that promotes the production of urine
