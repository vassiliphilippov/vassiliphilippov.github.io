hatchet job

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃɪt dʒɑb / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪt dʒɒb / (BrE)
a malicious and unfair criticism, report, or attack intended to damage someone's reputation or misrepresent their actions
malicious attack on someone's reputation

Examples

The article was nothing more than a hatchet job on the senator's record.
After reading the review, the director accused the critic of doing a hatchet job on her film.
He was the victim of a hatchet job that unfairly portrayed him as dishonest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: character assassination - the act of deliberately attempting to destroy someone's reputation
  • Synonym: smear campaign - a deliberate effort to damage or call into question someone's reputation
  • Antonym: commendation - the act of praising or approving someone or something

Etymology

Originally from the phrase 'bury the hatchet' (meaning to make peace), but reinterpreted to refer to an attack, as with a hatchet.
