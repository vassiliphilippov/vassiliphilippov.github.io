epic poetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪk ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
A genre of poetry that celebrates the deeds and adventures of heroic figures, often in a grand, elevated style.
poetry about heroic deeds and adventures

Examples

Homer's Iliad and Odyssey are classic examples of epic poetry.
Many cultures have their own traditions of epic poetry, recounting the exploits of national heroes.
She studied the structure and themes of epic poetry during her literature course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic - a long narrative poem about heroic deeds

Etymology

From Latin 'epicus', from Greek 'epikos' (relating to song or word), and 'poetry' from Greek 'poietēs' (maker, poet).
