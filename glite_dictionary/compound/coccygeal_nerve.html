coccygeal nerve

1. NOUN

/ kɑkˈsɪdʒiəl nɜrv / (AmE), / kɒkˈsɪdʒiəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the lowest pair of spinal nerves, located near the coccyx at the base of the spinal column
lowest pair of spinal nerves
medical

Examples

The coccygeal nerve innervates the area around the tailbone.
Damage to the coccygeal nerve is rare but may affect pelvic functions.
Doctors located inflammation near the patient's coccygeal nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal nerve - any of the pairs of nerves that emerge from the spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin 'coccyx' (tailbone) and '-geal' (relating to), indicating a connection to the coccyx.
