enabling act

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈneɪblɪŋ ækt / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪblɪŋ ækt / (BrE)
a provision in a law that gives official authorities the power to implement or enforce the law
law provision granting implementation powers
legal

Examples

The constitution requires an enabling act for the implementation of new regulations.
Without an enabling act, government agencies lack the authority to enforce certain policies.
Parliament passed an enabling act to empower the ministry to introduce health reforms.

Etymology

From the verb 'enable' and 'act' meaning legal statute or provision.
