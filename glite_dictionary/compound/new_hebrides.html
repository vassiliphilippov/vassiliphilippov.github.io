New Hebrides

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈhɛbrɪˌdiz / (AmE), / njuː ˈhɛbrɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
the former name of an island group in the southwest Pacific, now known as Vanuatu, which gained independence in 1980.
former name of Vanuatu, island group in the Pacific

Examples

The islands formerly known as the New Hebrides gained independence and are now called Vanuatu.
He studied colonial history in the New Hebrides during the period of British and French rule.
Many languages are spoken on the New Hebrides, making it a culturally diverse region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vanuatu - island country in the South Pacific, formerly known as New Hebrides

Etymology

From New (adopted to distinguish from the Hebrides in Scotland) and Hebrides (a group of islands off Scotland); name given by European explorers.
