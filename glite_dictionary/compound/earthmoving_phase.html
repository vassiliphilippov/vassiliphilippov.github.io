earthmoving phase

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθˌmuːvɪŋ feɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌmuːvɪŋ feɪz / (BrE)
the stage of a construction project in which earth or soil is excavated, moved, or shaped to prepare the site for further building work
construction stage involving soil excavation

Examples

The project faced delays during the earthmoving phase due to unexpected underground obstructions.
After the earthmoving phase is finished, the foundation work can begin.
Environmental assessments are often required before starting the earthmoving phase of large developments.

Synset Examples

They hired experts for the earthmoving phase of the site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavation - the process of digging or removing earth
