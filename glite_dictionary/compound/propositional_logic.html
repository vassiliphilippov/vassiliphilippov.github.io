propositional logic

1. NOUN

/ prəˌpɑːzɪʃənl ˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / prəˌpɒzɪʃənl ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
a branch of symbolic logic that studies propositions and their logical relationships, using connectives such as 'and', 'or', and 'not'
branch of symbolic logic about propositions

Examples

Propositional logic is often the first subject taught in introductory logic courses.
Understanding propositional logic helps students develop strong analytical reasoning skills.
The proof was constructed using only the laws of propositional logic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: predicate logic - branch of logic that deals with predicates and quantifiers beyond propositional logic
  • Degree Higher: symbolic logic - the broader field using symbols to represent logical forms and relationships

Etymology

From 'proposition' (statement) and 'logic' (reasoning), both from Latin roots.
