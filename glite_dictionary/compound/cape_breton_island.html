Cape Breton Island

1. PROPN

/ keɪp ˈbrɛtən ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / keɪp ˈbrɛtən ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
an island forming the northeastern part of Nova Scotia, Canada, known for its scenic landscapes and cultural heritage.
island in northeastern Nova Scotia, Canada

Examples

Many tourists visit Cape Breton Island each year to enjoy its natural beauty and Celtic culture.
The Cabot Trail, a famous scenic drive, is located on Cape Breton Island.
My grandparents emigrated from Scotland and settled on Cape Breton Island in the early 1900s.

Etymology

Named after the French region of Brittany ('Bretagne').
