Paleo-Amerind culture

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊ əˈmɛrɪnd ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊ əˈmɛrɪnd ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the prehistoric culture associated with the earliest human inhabitants of North and South America
prehistoric culture of early Americans

Examples

Many archaeologists study the Paleo-Amerind culture to understand how the earliest peoples adapted to new environments.
Artifacts discovered in the region have been traced back to the Paleo-Amerind culture period.
The spread of human populations across the Americas is closely tied to developments in Paleo-Amerind culture.

Etymology

Paleo-, from Greek 'palaios' (ancient), and Amerind, blend of 'American' and 'Indian'.
