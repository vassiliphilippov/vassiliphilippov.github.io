rooter skunk

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtər skʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈruːtə skʌŋk / (BrE)
a large skunk with a naked muzzle and a white back and tail, found in southwestern North America and Mexico
large skunk native to southwestern North America and Mexico

Examples

The rooter skunk is easily recognized by its long snout and striking white stripe.
Farmers in the region often spot rooter skunks searching for food at night.
Researchers tracked the movements of a rooter skunk to study its foraging behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hog-nosed skunk - a skunk species characterized by its hog-like snout, also called the rooter skunk
