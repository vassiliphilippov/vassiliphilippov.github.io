to bridle enthusiasm

1. VERB

/ ˈbraɪdəl ɪnˈθuziˌæzəm / (AmE), / ˈbraɪd(ə)l ɪnˈθjuːziˌæzəm / (BrE)
to restrain or control one's excitement or eagerness, especially in situations where excessive enthusiasm is inappropriate
restrain one's excitement or eagerness

Examples

You should bridle enthusiasm when giving constructive feedback to avoid overwhelming your team.
During the negotiations, he tried to bridle enthusiasm and remain objective.
She realized she must bridle enthusiasm to appear more professional in meetings.

Synset Examples

She learned to bridle her enthusiasm when presenting ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to hold back or keep under control
  • Synonym: curb enthusiasm - to control or limit excitement or eagerness

Etymology

From 'bridle', meaning to restrain (originally a headgear for a horse), combined with 'enthusiasm'.
