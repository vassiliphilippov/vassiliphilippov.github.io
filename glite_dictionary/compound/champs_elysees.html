Champs Elysees

1. PROPN

/ ʃæ̃z eɪˈliːzeɪ / (AmE), / ʃɒ̃z eɪˈliːzeɪ / (BrE)
A broad and famous avenue in Paris, renowned for its elegant shops, cafes, and as a major tourist attraction.
famous avenue in Paris

Examples

Many tourists stroll down the Champs Elysees each year to admire its luxury shops and historic landmarks.
The parade marched proudly along the Champs Elysees during Bastille Day celebrations.
Cafés on the Champs Elysees offer a wonderful view of Parisian life.

Etymology

From French 'Champs-Élysées', meaning 'Elysian Fields'.
