photoelectric emission

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk ɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk ɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the release of electrons from a material when it absorbs electromagnetic radiation of sufficiently high energy
release of electrons due to light or radiation
medical

Examples

When ultraviolet light strikes a metal surface, photoelectric emission can result in the release of electrons.
Scientists measured the rate of photoelectric emission to understand the energy of incident photons.
The phenomenon of photoelectric emission supports the particle theory of light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photoelectric effect - the emission of electrons from a material when exposed to light

Etymology

From 'photo-' (relating to light), 'electric,' and 'emission.'
