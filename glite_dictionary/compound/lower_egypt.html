Lower Egypt

1. PROPN

/ ˈloʊ.ɚ ˈiː.dʒɪpt / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə ˈiː.dʒɪpt / (BrE)
the northern region of ancient Egypt, comprising the Nile delta area and forming one of the two primary ancient administrative divisions.
northern region of ancient Egypt, Nile delta

Examples

During the Old Kingdom, Lower Egypt played a crucial role in trade with Mediterranean regions.
The Pharaohs of Lower Egypt wore the red crown as a symbol of their rule.
The fertile lands of Lower Egypt enabled the growth of powerful ancient cities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Upper Egypt - the southern region of ancient Egypt, the other major administrative district
