nose count

1. NOUN

/ noʊz kaʊnt / (AmE), / nəʊz kaʊnt / (BrE)
an act of counting the number of people present, especially for a census or vote
counting people present

Examples

The manager took a quick nose count before starting the meeting.
After a nose count, it was clear that the majority supported the proposal.
The club does a nose count at every event to track participation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headcount - the number of people present

Etymology

Based on the use of 'nose' as synecdoche for a person and 'count' meaning tally.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊz ˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈnəʊz ˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
an informal count or survey of people present, used to determine attendance or support
informal count of people present
informal

Examples

At the start of the meeting, the organizer did a quick nose count to see who was there.
A nose count showed that more than half of the students were absent that day.
Before the vote, the committee performed a nose count to estimate the level of support.

Synset Examples

The nose count revealed a surprising turnout.
During the event, a quick nose count was necessary.
They did a nose count to see how many attended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head count - the number of people present; similar informal attendance tally

Etymology

From the use of 'nose' to represent a person in informal enumeration.
