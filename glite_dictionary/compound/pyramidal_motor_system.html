pyramidal motor system

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈræmɪdəl ˈmoʊtɚ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / pɪˈræmɪdəl ˈməʊtə ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of motor nerve fibers that originate in the cerebral cortex and descend through the brainstem to the spinal cord, controlling voluntary muscle movement
system of nerves controlling voluntary movement
medical

Examples

Damage to the pyramidal motor system can result in muscle weakness and loss of voluntary movement.
The pyramidal motor system is primarily responsible for initiating voluntary movements in the body.
Neurologists often examine the pyramidal motor system when patients show signs of abnormal motor function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrapyramidal system - network of brain structures that modulate involuntary motor activity

Etymology

From 'pyramidal', referring to the pyramids of the medulla in the brainstem, and 'motor system'.
