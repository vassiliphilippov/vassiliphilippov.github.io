blue pike

1. NOUN

/ bluː paɪk / (AmE), / bluː paɪk / (BrE)
a variety of walleye fish, once found in the Great Lakes, known for its bluish coloration
a fish species from the Great Lakes

Examples

The blue pike was once abundant in Lake Erie but is now considered extinct.
Fishermen recalled catching blue pike during the early 20th century.
The distinctive coloring helped the blue pike stand out among other fish in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walleye - a freshwater fish related to the blue pike
