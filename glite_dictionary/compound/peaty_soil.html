peaty soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiti sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈpiːti sɔɪl / (BrE)
Soil that contains a high proportion of peat, making it rich in organic material and beneficial for plant growth.
soil rich in peat; organic and fertile

Examples

The roses thrived in the peaty soil of her backyard garden.
Farmers prefer peaty soil for growing certain vegetables due to its moisture retention.
A layer of peaty soil helped retain nutrients and encouraged healthy root development.

Synset Examples

Her garden was enhanced by a peaty soil that promoted growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loamy soil - soil composed of sand, silt, and clay, suitable for plant growth
  • Antonym: clay soil - soil with a high clay content, often dense and heavy

Etymology

From 'peat' (early Middle English, from Anglo-Norman French 'pete') + 'soil'.
