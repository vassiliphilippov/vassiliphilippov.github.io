Hottentot fig

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtənˌtɑt fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɒtɪntɒt fɪɡ / (BrE)
A low-growing South African succulent plant (Carpobrotus edulis) with fleshy leaves and a capsular fruit containing edible pulp.
South African succulent plant with edible fruit

Examples

The Hottentot fig spreads rapidly, making it popular for stabilizing dunes along the coast.
We tasted the sweet pulp from the fruit of the Hottentot fig while hiking in South Africa.
Many gardens use Hottentot fig as a groundcover for its drought-resistant qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carpobrotus edulis - scientific name for Hottentot fig

Etymology

Hottentot, an outdated term for the Khoikhoi people, + fig, referring to the fruit produced by the plant.
