wild celery

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈsɛləri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈsɛləri / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with long, narrow leaves, found in Europe, Asia, and Australia; often referring to various species of the genus Vallisneria
a type of aquatic plant

Examples

The pond was covered in wild celery, providing shelter for small fish.
Ducks often feed on wild celery found in shallow lakes.
Scientists study the growth patterns of wild celery to monitor aquatic ecosystem health.

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈsɛl.ə.ri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈsɛl.ə.ri / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant of Europe and temperate Asia, closely related to cultivated celery, with aromatic leaves and stalks
a European and Asian herb related to celery

Examples

Many chefs use wild celery to add a unique flavor to their dishes.
The banks of the river were lined with tall wild celery plants swaying in the breeze.
In some regions, wild celery is gathered in the spring for traditional recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - a cultivated plant with edible crisp stalks
