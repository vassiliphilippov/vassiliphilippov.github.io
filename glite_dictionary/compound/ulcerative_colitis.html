ulcerative colitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlsɚətɪv kəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌʌlsərətɪv kəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A chronic inflammatory disease of the colon and rectum, causing episodes of abdominal pain, fever, and severe diarrhea.
chronic inflammatory colon disease
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with ulcerative colitis after months of unexplained abdominal pain and diarrhea.
Many people with ulcerative colitis manage their symptoms with medication and dietary changes.
Researchers are studying the underlying causes of ulcerative colitis to develop more effective treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crohn's disease - another type of chronic inflammatory bowel disease
  • Degree Lower: irritable bowel syndrome - a less severe, non-inflammatory bowel disorder

Etymology

From Latin 'ulcerativus' (of an ulcer) and Greek 'kolon' (colon, large intestine) + '-itis' (inflammation).
