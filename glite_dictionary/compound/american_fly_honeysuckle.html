American fly honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən flaɪ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən flaɪ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (BrE)
an erect deciduous shrub native to North America, with yellow-white flowers, belonging to the honeysuckle family
North American shrub with yellow-white flowers

Examples

The American fly honeysuckle blooms in early spring, brightening shady woodland edges with its pale yellow flowers.
Gardeners often plant American fly honeysuckle for its ornamental value and wildlife appeal.
Many birds feed on the berries produced by American fly honeysuckle in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a group of twining shrubs with sweet-smelling flowers
