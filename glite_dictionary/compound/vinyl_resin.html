vinyl resin

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.nəl ˈrɛz.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.nɪl ˈrɛz.ɪn / (BrE)
a thermoplastic material produced by polymerizing compounds containing the vinyl group, used in plastics and coatings
thermoplastic polymer derived from vinyl compounds

Examples

The company specializes in producing high-quality vinyl resin for use in construction materials.
Coatings made from vinyl resin are resistant to water and many chemicals.
Manufacturers often choose vinyl resin because it offers flexibility and durability for plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyvinyl chloride - a common type of vinyl resin used in pipes and cable insulation

Etymology

From 'vinyl', relating to the ethenyl group (C2H3), and 'resin', from Latin 'resina' (tree resin).
