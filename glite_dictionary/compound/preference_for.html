preference for

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛf(ə)rəns fɔr / (AmE), / ˈprɛf(ə)rəns fə / (BrE)
a liking for or tendency to choose one thing over another; the act of favoring one option, person, or thing above others
the state of favoring one thing over another

Examples

She expressed a strong preference for organic food at the restaurant.
The company has shown a preference for hiring candidates with foreign language skills.
Do you have any preference for where we sit in the theatre?

Synset Examples

She has a preference for one's particular style of music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over others
  • Antonym: aversion to - a strong dislike or disinclination toward something
