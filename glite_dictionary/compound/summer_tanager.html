summer tanager

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ tæˈnædʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə tæˈnædʒə / (BrE)
a North American songbird (Piranga rubra) found in the middle and southern United States, with males being deep rose-red and females mostly yellow
a North American songbird species

Examples

We spotted a summer tanager perched in the flowering dogwood tree.
The male summer tanager is easily recognized by its vibrant red plumage.
Birdwatchers travel to the southern states each spring to see migrating summer tanagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanager - a type of small, colorful songbird
