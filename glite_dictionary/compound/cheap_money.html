cheap money

1. NOUN

/ tʃip ˈmʌni / (AmE), / tʃiːp ˈmʌni / (BrE)
credit or loans available at low rates of interest, making borrowing less expensive
credit available at low interest rates

Examples

During periods of cheap money, companies often invest more in expansion and development.
The housing market boomed when cheap money flooded the economy.
Central banks sometimes pursue policies that create cheap money to stimulate growth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dear money - credit available at high rates of interest
