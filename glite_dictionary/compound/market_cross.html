market cross

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkət krɑs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt krɒs / (BrE)
a cross-shaped stone monument set up in the marketplace of a town, traditionally serving as a meeting place for public business and proclamations
cross-shaped monument in a town marketplace
british

Examples

The medieval town still has its original market cross at the center of the square.
Tourists stopped to admire the old market cross and take photographs.
Announcements used to be read out loud beneath the market cross every week.

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'market' and 'cross', referring to a cross erected in a marketplace.
