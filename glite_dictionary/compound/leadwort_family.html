leadwort family

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdˌwɔrt ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈliːdwɜːt ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family of perennial herbs, shrubs, and vines (Plumbaginaceae), found worldwide and especially common in saltwater habitats
family of salt-tolerant herbs and shrubs (Plumbaginaceae)

Examples

Several species in the leadwort family thrive in coastal marshes and salt flats.
The leadwort family includes both ornamental and wild-growing plants.
Botanists classify sea lavender as part of the leadwort family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plumbaginaceae - the botanical family containing leadworts and related plants

Etymology

From 'leadwort', the common name for plants in the genus Plumbago, and 'family' in the botanical sense.
