common bile duct

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən baɪl dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən baɪl dʌkt / (BrE)
the duct in the body formed by the union of the hepatic and cystic ducts, which carries bile from the liver and gallbladder to the duodenum
duct carrying bile to the duodenum
medical

Examples

The surgeon checked the common bile duct for any signs of blockage during the procedure.
Gallstones can sometimes become lodged in the common bile duct, causing severe abdominal pain.
An ultrasound revealed a dilation in the patient's common bile duct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatic duct - duct that carries bile from the liver
  • Synonym: cystic duct - duct that carries bile from the gallbladder
