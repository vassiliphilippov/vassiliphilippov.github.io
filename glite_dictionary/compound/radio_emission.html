radio emission

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ ɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
An electromagnetic wave within the radio frequency range, typically with a wavelength between 0.5 cm and 30,000 meters.
electromagnetic wave in radio frequency range

Examples

Astronomers detected a strong radio emission coming from the distant galaxy.
The radio emission from the planet helps scientists study its atmosphere.
Background radio emission can interfere with sensitive radio telescopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio wave - an electromagnetic wave used for radio communication

Etymology

From 'radio' (relating to radio waves) + 'emission' (the act of sending out).
