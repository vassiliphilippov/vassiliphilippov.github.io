Malagasy Republic

1. PROPN

/ məˌlæɡəsi rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / məˌlæɡəsi rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
the official name of Madagascar from 1958 to 1975, a republic on the island of Madagascar that gained independence from France in 1960
former name of Madagascar (1958–1975)

Examples

The Malagasy Republic gained independence from France in 1960, beginning a new era for the island nation.
Between 1958 and 1975, Madagascar was officially known as the Malagasy Republic.
The constitution of the Malagasy Republic established French and Malagasy as official languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Madagascar - an island country in the Indian Ocean, current official name of the state formerly called Malagasy Republic

Etymology

From 'Malagasy', the demonym for the people and language of Madagascar, and 'Republic', from Latin 'res publica' (public matter).
