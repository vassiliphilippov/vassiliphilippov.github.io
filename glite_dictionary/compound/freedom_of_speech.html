freedom of speech

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdəm əv spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm əv spiːtʃ / (BrE)
the right to express opinions and ideas without fear of government censorship or punishment
right to express opinions freely

Examples

Many activists rallied to defend freedom of speech after the law was proposed.
Journalists believe that freedom of speech is essential for a healthy democracy.
Debates about freedom of speech often arise on university campuses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of speech or writing
