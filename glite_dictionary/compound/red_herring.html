red herring

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈhɛr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈhɛr.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a herring that has been dried and smoked, giving it a reddish color
dried and smoked reddish herring

Examples

The fisherman brought home a red herring to eat for dinner.
Smoked foods like red herring were common in England for centuries.
The distinctive smell of red herring filled the old market hall.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɹɛd ˈhɛɹɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɹɛd ˈhɛɹɪŋ / (BrE)
a preliminary version of a prospectus filed with regulators, distinguished by a disclaimer that its details may change before the final version is issued
preliminary financial prospectus
legal american

Examples

The company filed a red herring with the SEC before its official IPO.
Investors reviewed the red herring to assess the company's potential before the release of the final prospectus.
Regulations require all changes between the red herring and the final prospectus be clearly disclosed.

Synset Examples

because some portions of the cover page are printed in red ink a preliminary prospectus is sometimes called a red herring

Etymology

Refers to red ink on early versions of financial prospectuses, marking them as incomplete.
