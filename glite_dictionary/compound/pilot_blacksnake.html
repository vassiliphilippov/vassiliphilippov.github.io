pilot blacksnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ˈblækˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ˈblækˌsneɪk / (BrE)
A large, harmless, shiny black non-venomous snake found in North America, also known as the black rat snake.
large harmless black North American snake

Examples

The pilot blacksnake is known for its glossy black scales and docile nature.
Farmers often welcome a pilot blacksnake in their barns to help control rodents.
A pilot blacksnake can grow up to eight feet long, making it one of the longest snakes in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black rat snake - large harmless North American black snake, alternate name for pilot blacksnake

Etymology

From 'pilot' (one who guides) + 'blacksnake', with 'pilot' possibly referring to the snake's reputation for leading other snakes, though this is folklore.
