tannic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌtænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌtænɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a complex, plant-derived organic substance from the group of tannins, used especially in tanning leather and sometimes in medicine
a compound used in tanning and medicine

Examples

Leather is often treated with tannic acid to increase its durability.
The laboratory ordered a fresh supply of tannic acid for the next round of experiments.
Some herbal remedies contain tannic acid as an astringent ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tannin - a group of naturally occurring polyphenols found in plants

Etymology

From 'tannin' (French tannin, from 'tan'—oak bark used in tanning) + 'acid'.
