saddle hackle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædəl ˈhækəl / (AmE), / ˈsædl ˈhækəl / (BrE)
a long, narrow feather from the back (saddle area) of a domestic fowl, often used for fly-tying and decorative purposes
long narrow feather from a fowl's back

Examples

Fly-tiers often seek high-quality saddle hackle for tying realistic dry flies.
The rooster's saddle hackle feathers were particularly glossy and long.
He collected several saddle hackle from his chickens to use in his next craft project.
