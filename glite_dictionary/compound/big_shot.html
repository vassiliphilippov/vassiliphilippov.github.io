big shot

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ʃɑt / (AmE), / bɪɡ ʃɒt / (BrE)
an important or influential person, especially one who has power or authority in a particular field or organization
an important or influential person
informal

Examples

After the merger, he became a big shot in the company.
Everyone wanted to talk to the big shot at the party.
She acted like a big shot after her latest promotion.

Synset Examples

Don't let the big shot attitude go to your head.
He thinks he's a big shot in the office.
She finally got to meet a big shot producer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: VIP - a person of very important status or influence
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence
