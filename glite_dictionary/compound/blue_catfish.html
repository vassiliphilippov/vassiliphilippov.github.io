blue catfish

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈkætfɪʃ / (AmE), / bluː ˈkatfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large North American freshwater catfish, especially found in the Mississippi Valley, known for its bluish coloring and significant size.
large North American freshwater catfish

Examples

The blue catfish can grow to impressive sizes in the wild.
Fishermen often try to catch blue catfish in the deep waters of the Mississippi River.
The blue catfish is prized for both its size and tasty flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channel catfish - another North American freshwater catfish species
