ethanal trimer

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːθəˌnæl ˈtraɪmər / (AmE), / ˌiːθəˌnæl ˈtrɪmə / (BrE)
A colorless liquid that is a cyclic trimer of acetaldehyde, used as a sedative and a solvent.
cyclic trimer of acetaldehyde; sedative and solvent

Examples

The laboratory uses ethanal trimer as a common solvent for various reactions.
Because of its sedative properties, ethanal trimer was once prescribed in medicine.
Researchers studied the behavior of ethanal trimer under different temperature conditions.

Etymology

From 'ethanal' (systematic name for acetaldehyde) and 'trimer' (molecule formed from three identical units).
