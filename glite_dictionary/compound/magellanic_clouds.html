Magellanic Clouds

1. NOUN

/ məˌdʒɛˈlænɪk klaʊdz / (AmE), / məˌdʒɛˈlænɪk klaʊdz / (BrE)
either of two irregular dwarf galaxies, the Large Magellanic Cloud and the Small Magellanic Cloud, that orbit the Milky Way and are visible near the South Celestial Pole
two dwarf galaxies orbiting the Milky Way

Examples

The Magellanic Clouds are easily visible with the naked eye from the Southern Hemisphere.
Astronomers have discovered several star clusters within the Magellanic Clouds.
Many southern sky watchers are fascinated by the brightness of the Magellanic Clouds on clear nights.

Etymology

Named after Ferdinand Magellan, whose 16th-century expedition first recorded them in detail during circumnavigation.
