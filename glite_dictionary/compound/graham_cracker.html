graham cracker

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræm ˌkrækər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.əm ˌkræk.ər / (BrE)
A semisweet, crisp, flat cracker made from whole-wheat flour, commonly used for snacks or desserts.
sweet whole-wheat cracker
american

Examples

He topped his ice cream with crumbled graham cracker pieces for extra crunch.
The recipe calls for a graham cracker crust to complement the cheesecake filling.
At the campfire, we made s'mores by sandwiching chocolate and marshmallows between graham crackers.

Etymology

Named after Sylvester Graham, a 19th-century dietary reformer who advocated the use of whole-wheat flour.
