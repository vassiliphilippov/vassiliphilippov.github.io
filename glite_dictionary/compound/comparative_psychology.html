comparative psychology

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpærətɪv saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / kəmˌpærətɪv saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology that studies the behavior of animals, often in comparison with human behavior
branch of psychology studying animal behavior

Examples

She is conducting research in comparative psychology to better understand how animals learn.
Many discoveries in comparative psychology have contributed to our knowledge of cognition.
Students interested in animal behavior often pursue courses in comparative psychology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal psychology - study of the mental processes and behavior of animals
  • Degree Lower: behavioral psychology - branch of psychology focused on observable behavior

Etymology

From comparative, meaning 'relating to comparison', and psychology, from Greek psykhe (soul, mind) + -logia (study).
