Divina Commedia

1. PROPN

/ diˌviːnə kämˈmeːdia / (AmE), / diˌviːnə kɒmˈmeːdɪə / (BrE)
the narrative epic poem by Dante Alighieri, regarded as one of the greatest works of world literature, describing the poet's journey through Hell, Purgatory, and Paradise.
Dante's epic poem describing a journey through the afterlife

Examples

Dante's Divina Commedia has influenced countless writers and artists over the centuries.
The Divina Commedia is often studied for its allegorical depiction of the afterlife.
Many scholars analyze the structure and symbolism found in the Divina Commedia.

Etymology

Italian for 'Divine Comedy'; title given by Giovanni Boccaccio, from Latin 'divinus' (divine) and 'comoedia' (comedy).
