northern casque-headed frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɹðɚn ˈkæskˌhɛdɪd frɑg / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈkɑːskˌhɛdɪd frɒg / (BrE)
A terrestrial, burrowing, nocturnal frog found in grassy terrain and scrub forests of the southwestern United States, noted for its very hard upper surface of the head.
burrowing frog of the US southwest

Examples

The northern casque-headed frog is rarely seen during the day, as it spends most of its time underground.
Researchers discovered a northern casque-headed frog during their survey of the New Mexico grasslands.
The distinctive armored head of the northern casque-headed frog helps protect it from predators.
