fern seed

1. NOUN

/ fɝn sid / (AmE), / fɜːn siːd / (BrE)
The minute asexual reproductive spore of ferns, once mistakenly believed to be a true seed and traditionally thought to grant invisibility to its possessor.
asexual spore of ferns; folk invisibility charm

Examples

In folklore, fern seed was said to make a person invisible if carried.
Some botanists once believed that fern seed produced new ferns, but it is actually a spore.
The tiny, dust-like fern seed fascinated those who sought its magical properties.
