Ancient Greek language

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪnʃənt ˈɡrik ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌeɪnʃənt ˈɡriːk ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the form of the Greek language used from the 9th century BCE to about the 6th century CE, prior to the Roman Empire
the Greek language before the Roman Empire

Examples

Many classical texts were originally written in Ancient Greek language and later translated into Latin.
She studied the Ancient Greek language to better understand historical philosophy.
The Ancient Greek language has contributed many words to modern English vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Greek language - the language of the Greeks in general

Etymology

Greek 'Hellēnikḗ glôssa', adopted via Latin 'Graecus'.
