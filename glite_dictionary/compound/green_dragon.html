green dragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːn ˌdræɡ.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn ˌdræɡ.ən / (BrE)
A European arum plant (Arum maculatum) resembling the cuckoopint, known for its green spathe and spadix.
European arum plant resembling cuckoopint

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with green dragon, its vibrant green spathes standing tall among the leaves.
Gardeners often mistake the green dragon for the closely related cuckoopint.
He was fascinated by the unusual flowers of the green dragon growing near the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoopint - Another common name for Arum maculatum, a European arum plant

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrin ˌdræɡən / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn ˌdræɡən / (BrE)
an early spring-flowering plant of eastern North America, similar to jack-in-the-pulpit, with digitate leaves and a slender greenish yellow spathe and elongated spadix
North American spring-flowering plant

Examples

The green dragon blooms in the shady woodlands each spring.
Many gardeners appreciate the unique appearance of the green dragon in their native plant beds.
We spotted a patch of green dragons near the riverbank while hiking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack-in-the-pulpit - a related North American plant with a hooded spathe and club-shaped spadix
