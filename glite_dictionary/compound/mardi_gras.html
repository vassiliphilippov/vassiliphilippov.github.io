Mardi Gras

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːr.di ˈɡrɑː / (AmE), / ˌmɑː.di ˈɡrɑː / (BrE)
a festival held on Shrove Tuesday, especially celebrated in New Orleans, involving parades, music, and masquerade festivities before the start of Lent
carnival festival before Lent

Examples

Every year, thousands of people travel to New Orleans to experience Mardi Gras.
Colorful floats and elaborate costumes are a hallmark of Mardi Gras parades.
She wore a vibrant mask and beads to the Mardi Gras celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carnival - a festival period before Lent celebrated with parades and festivities

Etymology

From French ‘Mardi Gras’, meaning ‘Fat Tuesday’.

2. PROPN

/ ˌmɑrdi ˈɡrɑ / (AmE), / ˌmɑːdi ˈɡrɑː / (BrE)
The last day before Lent, celebrated in many countries with festivals, parades, and parties, especially in New Orleans.
the last day before Lent; festival

Examples

Every year, thousands of tourists visit New Orleans to celebrate Mardi Gras.
Colorful parades and elaborate masks are traditions associated with Mardi Gras.
The city streets were filled with music and dancing during Mardi Gras.

Etymology

From French, literally 'Fat Tuesday', marking the last day of feasting before Lent.
