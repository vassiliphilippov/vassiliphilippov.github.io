regular octahedron

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjələr ɑk.təˈhiː.dɹɑn / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjələ ˌɒk.təˈhiː.drən / (BrE)
A three-dimensional solid figure with eight faces, each of which is an equilateral triangle and all faces and angles are congruent.
polyhedron with eight equilateral triangle faces

Examples

In geometry class, we constructed a regular octahedron out of paper to understand its symmetry.
The diamond crystal lattice can be visualized as a network of regular octahedra connected at their vertices.
A regular octahedron is one of the five Platonic solids, with all faces being identical equilateral triangles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: octahedron - polyhedron with eight faces
  • Degree Lower: regular tetrahedron - polyhedron with four equilateral triangle faces
  • Degree Higher: cube - regular polyhedron with six square faces

Etymology

From Latin 'octahedron' (eight faces), with 'regular' indicating all faces and angles are equal; rooted in Greek 'oktaedron' (eight-sided).
