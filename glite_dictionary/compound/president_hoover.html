President Hoover

1. PROPN

/ ˌprɛzɪdənt ˈhuːvɚ / (AmE), / ˌprɛzɪdənt ˈhuːvə / (BrE)
the 31st President of the United States (1929–1933), under whose administration the stock market crashed and the Great Depression began
31st U.S. President (1929–1933)

Examples

President Hoover faced immense challenges during the onset of the Great Depression.
Historians often debate the policies implemented by President Hoover during his term.
The election of 1932 resulted in President Hoover losing to Franklin Roosevelt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Herbert Hoover - 31st President of the United States (1874–1964)
  • Antonym: Franklin Roosevelt - 32nd President of the United States, succeeded Hoover
