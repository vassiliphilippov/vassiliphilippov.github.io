family Hyaenidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli haɪˈɛnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli haɪˈiːnɪdiː / (BrE)
the biological family comprising hyenas, medium-sized carnivorous mammals found in Africa and Asia
taxonomic family of hyenas

Examples

Researchers discovered a new fossil belonging to the family Hyaenidae in the region.
All modern hyenas are members of the family Hyaenidae in the order Carnivora.
The family Hyaenidae is characterized by powerful jaws and a unique social structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyena - a carnivorous mammal of the family Hyaenidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hyaenidae', based on 'Hyaena' (the genus) and '-idae' indicating a zoological family.
