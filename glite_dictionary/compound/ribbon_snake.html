ribbon snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪbən ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɪbən ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
A slender, yellow-striped North American garter snake, often found near water.
a slender yellow-striped North American garter snake

Examples

We saw a ribbon snake sliding quickly along the edge of the pond.
The biologist explained how the ribbon snake differs from other garter snakes.
A ribbon snake is harmless to humans and helps control insect populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garter snake - a common name for snakes of the genus Thamnophis, to which ribbon snakes belong
