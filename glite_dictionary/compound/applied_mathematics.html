applied mathematics

1. NOUN

/ əˈplaɪd ˌmæθəˈmætɪks / (AmE), / əˈplaɪd ˌmæθəˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics concerned with mathematical methods that are used in practical fields such as science, engineering, business, and industry
mathematics for practical applications

Examples

After years of studying applied mathematics, Emily joined a team designing pollution control systems.
Many advances in computer science rely on concepts from applied mathematics.
He specializes in applied mathematics, using mathematical models to solve real-world engineering problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure mathematics - mathematics concerned with abstract concepts not intended for practical use
