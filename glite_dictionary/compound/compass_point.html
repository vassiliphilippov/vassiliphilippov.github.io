compass point

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.pəs pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.pəs pɔɪnt / (BrE)
any of the thirty-two directions marked on the card of a compass, such as north, northeast, or southwest, used for navigation and orientation.
one of the 32 directions on a compass

Examples

The sailors made sure their course followed the compass point indicated on the chart.
Each compass point represents a specific direction to help in navigation.
He adjusted his heading a few degrees to the east-northeast compass point.

Synset Examples

he checked the point on his compass

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cardinal point - one of the four main compass directions: north, south, east, or west
