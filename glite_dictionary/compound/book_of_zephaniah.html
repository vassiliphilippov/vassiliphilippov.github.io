Book of Zephaniah

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˌzɛfəˈnaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˌzɛfəˈnaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Zephaniah, mainly concerning the coming judgment of God upon Judah
Old Testament prophetic book

Examples

Many scholars have studied the themes of judgment and redemption found in the Book of Zephaniah.
The Book of Zephaniah is often read during Advent as it speaks about hope amid judgment.
She referenced the Book of Zephaniah in her sermon to illustrate the importance of repentance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zephaniah - Hebrew prophet of the seventh century BC
  • Degree Lower: Book of Habakkuk - an Old Testament book of prophecy
  • Degree Higher: Book of Haggai - an Old Testament book of prophecy following Zephaniah

Etymology

Zephaniah, from Hebrew *Ṣəphanyāh*, meaning 'Yahweh has hidden'.
