international nautical mile

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.tɚˈnæʃ.ən.əl ˈnɔː.tɪ.kəl maɪl / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈnæʃ.ən.əl ˈnɔː.tɪ.kəl maɪl / (BrE)
a unit of length used in navigation, equal to exactly 1,852 meters, based on one minute of arc of latitude
unit of length (navigation), 1,852 meters

Examples

The ship covered 500 international nautical miles during its journey across the Atlantic.
A flight between the two cities is approximately 800 international nautical miles in distance.
Maritime charts use the international nautical mile as the standard unit for measuring distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautical mile - a unit of length used in navigation, about 1,852 meters
  • Antonym: mile - a unit of length equal to 1,609.344 meters

Etymology

From 'nautical mile', standardized as the 'international nautical mile' in 1929 by an international agreement.
