hydraulic Keynesianism

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːlɪk ˌkeɪnziˈænɪzəm / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk ˌkeɪnziˈænɪzəm / (BrE)
an economic theory emphasizing the use of fiscal policy and government intervention to regulate demand, viewing the economy as a system that can be managed through such controls
Keynesian economic theory stressing fiscal policy and control

Examples

Many governments adopted hydraulic Keynesianism as a response to economic recessions in the mid-20th century.
The limitations of hydraulic Keynesianism became apparent during periods of stagflation.
Some policymakers argue that hydraulic Keynesianism offers predictable results through careful fiscal interventions.

Synset Examples

Critics argue hydraulic Keynesianism doesn't address long-term growth.
Many economists support hydraulic Keynesianism for boosting the economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Keynesianism - economic theory advocating government intervention to stabilize the economy

Etymology

From 'hydraulic,' likening the economy to a system managed by controls and valves, and 'Keynesianism,' after economist John Maynard Keynes.
