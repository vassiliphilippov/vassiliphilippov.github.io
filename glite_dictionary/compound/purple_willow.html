purple willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A Eurasian willow tree (Salix purpurea) with reddish or purple twigs and bark containing tannin.
a willow tree with purple twigs

Examples

The basket was woven from the slender branches of purple willow.
Gardeners often plant purple willow along riverbanks to prevent soil erosion.
The bark of the purple willow is traditionally used for tanning leather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osier - a willow tree with long, flexible branches used for basketry
