horse trading

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
hard bargaining or negotiation in which both sides make significant concessions to reach a deal
shrewd, hard negotiation with mutual concessions

Examples

Intense horse trading continued into the night before the bill finally passed.
After days of horse trading, the two companies agreed on a merger.
Political decisions often involve horse trading behind closed doors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargaining - negotiation to reach an agreement
  • Synonym: negotiation - discussion to reach an agreement

Etymology

From the practice of bargaining for horses, known for requiring skillful negotiation and compromise.

2. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of exchanging horses, typically involving skillful and shrewd bargaining
exchange of horses with bargaining

Examples

The fair was known for its lively horse trading among local farmers.
His grandfather once made a living through honest horse trading in the countryside.
Old stories of horse trading reveal the importance of good negotiation skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barter - exchange of goods or services without money
