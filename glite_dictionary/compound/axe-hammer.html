axe-hammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈæks ˌhæmər / (AmE), / ˈæks ˌhamə / (BrE)
A weapon or tool with an axe blade on one side and a hammer on the opposite side, typically used in battle or for specific tasks.
weapon with both axe and hammer heads

Examples

The medieval soldier wielded an axe-hammer during the battle, making use of both sides in close combat.
Archaeologists uncovered an ancient axe-hammer at the excavation site.
The axe-hammer is displayed in the museum alongside other historic weapons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: war hammer - weapon with a hammer head used in combat
