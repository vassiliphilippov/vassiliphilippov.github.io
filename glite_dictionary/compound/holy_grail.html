Holy Grail

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈɡreɪl / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈɡreɪl / (BrE)
The cup or chalice said to have been used by Jesus Christ at the Last Supper and later sought by knights in legend as a sacred or miraculous object.
legendary cup of the Last Supper

Examples

Many adventurers have searched for the Holy Grail throughout history and literature.
For medieval knights, finding the Holy Grail was the ultimate quest.
Some consider scientific immortality to be the modern equivalent of the Holy Grail.

Etymology

From Old French *graal* (cup, vessel), from Medieval Latin *gradalis* (cup, dish).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈɡreɪl / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈɡreɪl / (BrE)
something that is eagerly sought after or pursued as the ultimate goal or ideal achievement.
the ultimate or most desired goal

Examples

For many scientists, discovering a cure for cancer is the Holy Grail of medical research.
A sustainable, clean source of energy is often described as the Holy Grail for environmentalists.
Perfect work-life balance is the Holy Grail that so many professionals seek.

Synset Examples

Finding a job you love is the Holy Grail for many.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - an aim or desired result
  • Synonym: prize - something highly valued or sought after

Etymology

From the legendary cup said to be used by Jesus at the Last Supper, sought by knights in medieval legend.
