double-propeller plane

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl prəˈpɛl.ɚ pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl prəˈpel.ə pleɪn / (BrE)
an aircraft equipped with an engine that drives two propellers rotating in opposite directions to enhance stability
aircraft with two counter-rotating propellers

Examples

The engineer explained how a double-propeller plane maintains better stability during flight.
They watched a rare double-propeller plane take off from the small airfield.
Collectors often seek out models of vintage double-propeller planes for their historical value.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: propeller plane - an aircraft powered by one or more propellers
