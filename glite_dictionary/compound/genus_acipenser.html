genus Acipenser

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæsɪˈpɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæsɪˈpɛnsə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Acipenseridae, comprising sturgeon species.
the sturgeon genus of fishes

Examples

The genus Acipenser includes several species important for their roe, known as caviar.
Some of the largest freshwater fish belong to the genus Acipenser.
The biologist specialized in the study of genus Acipenser for conservation purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturgeon - large, primitive fish known for roe (caviar)

Etymology

From Latin *Acipenser* (sturgeon).
