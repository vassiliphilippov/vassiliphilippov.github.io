cell wall

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl wɑl / (AmE), / ˈsɛl wɔːl / (BrE)
a rigid layer that surrounds the cell membrane of plants, fungi, and certain bacteria, providing structural support and protection
rigid outer layer of plant and some microbial cells
medical

Examples

The cell wall gives plant cells their characteristic rectangular shape.
Some antibiotics target the cell wall of bacteria to kill or inhibit them.
Fungi also have a cell wall that differs in composition from that of plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cell membrane - the semipermeable membrane surrounding the cytoplasm of a cell
