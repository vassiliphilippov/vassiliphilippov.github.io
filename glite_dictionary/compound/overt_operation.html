overt operation

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈvɝt ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əʊˈvɜːt ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an intelligence or military activity carried out openly without concealment or secrecy
openly conducted intelligence activity

Examples

The agency launched an overt operation to gather information during the conference.
Journalists were allowed to cover the overt operation as it unfolded.
Unlike covert missions, an overt operation is conducted under full public scrutiny.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covert operation - an intelligence or military activity carried out secretly or with concealment
