Bachelor of Music

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr əv ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələ əv ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A bachelor's degree awarded for studies in music, typically after a three- or four-year course at a university or college.
bachelor's degree in music

Examples

She earned her Bachelor of Music at a prestigious conservatory.
After completing his Bachelor of Music, he went on to pursue a master's degree.
The university offers a comprehensive Bachelor of Music program in performance and composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bachelor's degree - an undergraduate academic degree awarded by colleges and universities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'baccalaureus musicae', combining 'bachelor' (university degree) and 'music'.
