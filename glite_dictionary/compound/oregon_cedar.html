Oregon cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪɡən ˈsiːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a large timber tree of western North America, growing up to 200 feet tall, with a trunk diameter of up to 12 feet
large timber tree native to western North America

Examples

The forest was thick with towering Oregon cedars reaching toward the sky.
Many homes in the Pacific Northwest use Oregon cedar for durable outdoor furniture.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of the ancient Oregon cedar in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Port Orford cedar - another common name for the same tree, Chamaecyparis lawsoniana
  • Degree Higher: cedar - general name for several coniferous trees with aromatic wood
