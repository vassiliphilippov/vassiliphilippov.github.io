anseriform bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɚ.ɪ.fɔːrm bɝːd / (AmE), / ˈan.sər.ɪ.fɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
A bird belonging to the order Anseriformes, typically web-footed and adapted for swimming, such as ducks, geese, and swans.
web-footed swimming bird (Anseriformes)

Examples

The mallard is one of the most widespread anseriform birds in the world.
Biologists are studying the migration patterns of several anseriform birds in the wetland region.
Most anseriform birds have broad, flat bills adapted for dabbling in the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a common type of anseriform bird, known for swimming and quacking
  • Synonym: goose - a large waterfowl and member of Anseriformes
  • Synonym: swan - a large, graceful water bird of the order Anseriformes

Etymology

From Latin ‘anser’ (goose) + ‘-iform’ (having the form of).
