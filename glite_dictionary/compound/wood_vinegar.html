wood vinegar

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈvɪɡəɹ / (AmE), / wʊd ˈvɪɡə / (BrE)
a reddish-brown liquid obtained from the distillation of wood, containing acetic acid, methanol, acetone, wood oils, and tars, used in various chemical processes
liquid from wood distillation, contains acetic acid

Examples

Traditional charcoal kilns often produce wood vinegar as a by-product, collected for agricultural use.
The chemist explained how wood vinegar can act as a natural pesticide in organic farming.
During the distillation process, wood vinegar is separated from tar and purified for industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyroligneous acid - alternative name for wood vinegar
