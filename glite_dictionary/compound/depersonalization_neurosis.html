depersonalization neurosis

1. NOUN

/ diˌpɝːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən nuˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / diˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən njʊˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A psychological disorder characterized by persistent or recurrent feelings of detachment from one's self or a sense of unreality, often accompanied by emotional numbing and a loss of personal identity.
psychological disorder with sense of unreality
medical

Examples

Some patients with depersonalization neurosis describe feeling as if they are observing themselves from outside their body.
Treatment of depersonalization neurosis often includes psychotherapy and, in some cases, medication.
Her experience of depersonalization neurosis made everyday interactions feel distant and unreal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depersonalization disorder - a disorder featuring persistent feelings of detachment from oneself

Etymology

From 'depersonalization' (de- + personal + -ization), and 'neurosis,' from Greek 'neuron' (nerve).
