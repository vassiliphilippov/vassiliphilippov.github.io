sweet sorghum

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈsɔɹɡəm / (AmE), / swiːt ˈsɔːɡəm / (BrE)
A type of sorghum grown mainly for its sweet juice, which is used to make syrup.
sorghum cultivated for syrup

Examples

The farmers harvested sweet sorghum to produce a thick, amber syrup for local markets.
Many traditional Southern recipes use sweet sorghum syrup as a sweetener.
Research efforts are under way to improve the yield of sweet sorghum varieties for biofuel production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - tall grass cultivated for its grain and as a source of syrup
