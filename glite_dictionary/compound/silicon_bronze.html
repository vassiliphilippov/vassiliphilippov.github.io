silicon bronze

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪkən ˈbrɑnz / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkən ˈbrɒnz / (BrE)
an alloy of bronze containing 2-3% silicon, known for its resistance to corrosion and often used in marine and industrial applications
a corrosion-resistant bronze alloy with silicon

Examples

The shipbuilder chose silicon bronze bolts for their superior resistance to seawater corrosion.
Sculptures made from silicon bronze develop a distinctive patina over time.
Welders often prefer silicon bronze for joining copper and brass materials due to its durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronze - an alloy of copper and tin, sometimes with other elements
