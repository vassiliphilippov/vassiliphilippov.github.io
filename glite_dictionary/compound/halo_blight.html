halo blight

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪloʊ ˌblaɪt / (AmE), / ˈheɪləʊ ˌblaɪt / (BrE)
a bacterial disease of bean plants that causes water-soaked spots surrounded by yellow halos on leaves
disease of bean plants

Examples

The farmer was concerned when he noticed halo blight spreading across his bean crop.
Proper sanitation can help prevent halo blight from affecting susceptible plants.
Symptoms of halo blight include distinctive yellow rings around lesions on the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪloʊ blaɪt / (AmE), / ˈheɪləʊ blaɪt / (BrE)
a disease causing yellow or light-colored halos around necrotic spots on the leaves of oats and other grasses, typically caused by bacterial infection
plant disease causing halo spots on leaves

Examples

Many farmers lost their oat crops due to a sudden outbreak of halo blight last summer.
The characteristic yellow rings around leaf spots are a symptom of halo blight.
Proper crop rotation can help reduce the incidence of halo blight in fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blight - a plant disease causing damage or death to leaves, stems, or entire plants
