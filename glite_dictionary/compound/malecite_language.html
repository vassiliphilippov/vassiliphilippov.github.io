Malecite language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæləˌsaɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmalɪˌsiːt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language historically spoken by the Malecite and Passamaquoddy peoples of northeastern North America
Algonquian language of Malecite and Passamaquoddy peoples

Examples

Few fluent speakers of the Malecite language remain today, making its preservation urgent.
She is conducting research on the grammar of the Malecite language.
The Malecite language shares similarities with other Algonquian languages in the region.

Etymology

From Maliseet (indigenous name Wolastoqiyik), the name of the people, plus 'language'.
