order Ophioglossales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrdɚ oʊˌfaɪəˌɡlɑːˈseɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə ˌəʊfiəˌɡlɒˈseɪliːz / (BrE)
A botanical taxonomic order comprising the family Ophioglossaceae, which includes the adder's-tongue ferns and their relatives.
taxonomic order of adder's-tongue ferns

Examples

The order Ophioglossales includes unique ferns such as adder's-tongues and grape ferns.
Botanists have long debated the evolutionary placement of the order Ophioglossales.
Recent studies have revealed new species within the order Ophioglossales in tropical regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Ophioglossum' (the genus name, from Greek 'ophis' meaning 'snake' and 'glossa' meaning 'tongue') with the standard botanical order suffix '-ales'.
