gadoid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡædɔɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡadɔɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish belonging to the family Gadidae, including cod, haddock, and related species, typically having soft fins.
fish of the family Gadidae

Examples

The Atlantic cod is perhaps the most well-known gadoid fish harvested commercially.
Many gadoid fishes are important both ecologically and economically in the North Atlantic Ocean.
Scientists study the migratory patterns of gadoid fish to understand changes in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish of the Gadidae family, valued as food
  • Synonym: haddock - a species of gadoid fish commonly eaten as food

Etymology

From modern Latin Gadidae (family name) and Greek eidos (form, likeness).
