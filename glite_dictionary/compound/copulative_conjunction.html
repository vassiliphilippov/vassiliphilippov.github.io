copulative conjunction

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑpjələtɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒpjʊlətɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a conjunction used to connect words, phrases, or clauses that express addition, typically represented by 'and' in English
a conjunction expressing addition, like 'and'

Examples

The word copulative conjunction refers to words like 'and' that join similar sentence elements together.
In the sentence 'She bought apples and oranges,' 'and' acts as a copulative conjunction.
When combining two independent clauses, a copulative conjunction such as 'and' can be used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinating conjunction - a conjunction that connects words, phrases, or clauses of equal syntactic importance

Etymology

From Latin 'copulativus', meaning 'joining together', and 'conjunction', from Latin 'coniunctio'.
