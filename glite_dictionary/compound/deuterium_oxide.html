deuterium oxide

1. NOUN

/ duːˈtɪriəm ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / djuːˈtɪəriəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a form of water in which most or all of the hydrogen atoms are replaced with deuterium, used especially as a moderator in nuclear reactors
heavy water; water with deuterium
medical

Examples

The nuclear reactor uses deuterium oxide as a neutron moderator.
Scientists measured the properties of deuterium oxide in the laboratory.
Deuterium oxide is sometimes called heavy water due to its higher density.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy water - another term for deuterium oxide
  • Degree Lower: water - the common liquid made of hydrogen and oxygen

Etymology

Formed from 'deuterium', the isotope of hydrogen, and 'oxide', meaning a compound with oxygen.
