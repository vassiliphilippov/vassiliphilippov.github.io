yellow bile

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ baɪl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ baɪl / (BrE)
(historical) one of the four bodily humors, believed to be secreted by the liver and to cause irritability and anger.
historical bodily humor linked to anger
archaic

Examples

Ancient physicians believed an excess of yellow bile was responsible for a person's quick temper.
The theory of yellow bile played a significant role in medieval medical diagnoses.
References to yellow bile can be found in classical literature discussing the balance of humors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choler - another name for yellow bile, linked to anger in ancient humoral theory
