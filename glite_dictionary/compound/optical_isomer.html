optical isomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl ˈaɪsəmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ˈaɪsəmə / (BrE)
either of two chemical compounds (stereoisomers) that are mirror images of each other but cannot be superimposed, often differing in the direction they rotate plane-polarized light
one of two non-superimposable mirror-image molecules
medical

Examples

Lactic acid has two optical isomers that differ in how they rotate polarized light.
The separation of optical isomers is crucial in pharmaceutical manufacturing.
Some optical isomers of drugs can have very different biological effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enantiomer - each of a pair of optical isomers that are mirror images
  • Antonym: diastereomer - a stereoisomer that is not a mirror image of another

Etymology

From 'optical' (relating to sight or light) and 'isomer' (from Greek 'isos' meaning equal and 'meros' meaning part).
