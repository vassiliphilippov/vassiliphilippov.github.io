tidewater stream

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdˌwɔtər strim / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdwɔːtə striːm / (BrE)
a stream whose flow and water levels are significantly influenced by incoming tides from a nearby sea or estuary, often extending far upstream
stream influenced by tidal effects

Examples

Many fish migrate up the tidewater stream to breed during the spring season.
The tidewater stream rose noticeably with each incoming tide, flooding the marshlands.
Researchers measured the salinity levels in the tidewater stream to understand the extent of saltwater intrusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estuary - the tidal mouth of a river, where freshwater mixes with seawater
