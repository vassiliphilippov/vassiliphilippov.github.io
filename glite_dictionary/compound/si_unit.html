SI unit

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs ˈaɪ ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˈaɪ ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement belonging to the International System of Units (SI), the standardized system used in science and industry worldwide
unit in the International System of Units (SI)

Examples

Length is measured in SI units such as meters and kilometers.
Physicists prefer to use SI units to ensure consistency in their experiments.
The SI unit for temperature is the kelvin, not the degree Celsius.

Synset Examples

Today the United States is the only country in the world not totally committed to the Systeme International d'Unites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metric unit - a unit of measurement based on the metric system

Etymology

Abbreviation of French ‘Système International (d'Unités)’ (International System of Units).
