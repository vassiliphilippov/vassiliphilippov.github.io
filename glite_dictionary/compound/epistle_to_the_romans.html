Epistle to the Romans

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl tə ðə ˈroʊmənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tə ðə ˈrəʊmənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament in the Bible, presenting the doctrines of Saint Paul in a letter addressed to the Christians in Rome
a New Testament book by Saint Paul

Examples

Many theologians have studied the Epistle to the Romans for its influence on Christian doctrine.
He quoted from the Epistle to the Romans during his sermon on faith and grace.
The Epistle to the Romans addresses issues faced by the early Christian community in Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romans - Alternative title for the Epistle to the Romans
  • Synonym: Epistle to the Corinthians - another Pauline letter in the New Testament

Etymology

Epistle from Greek 'epistolē' (letter), referring to Paul's letter to the Romans.
