Khoikhoin language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪ.kɔɪn ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪ.kɔɪn ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the Khoisan languages spoken by the Khoikhoin, a pastoral people of Namibia and South Africa
Khoisan languages of the Khoikhoin people

Examples

Some elders in the village still speak the Khoikhoin language fluently.
Researchers are working to document the Khoikhoin language before it disappears.
The museum's exhibit featured recordings of the Khoikhoin language alongside cultural artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Khoisan language - languages of the indigenous peoples of southern Africa characterized by click sounds

Etymology

Khoikhoin, meaning 'real people' in the Khoikhoin language; 'language' from Latin lingua (tongue, speech).
