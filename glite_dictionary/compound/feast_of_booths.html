Feast of Booths

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːst əv buðz / (AmE), / ˈfiːst əv buːðz / (BrE)
a major Jewish festival, also known as Sukkot, observed beginning on the 15th of Tishri, commemorating the shelter of the Israelites during their 40 years in the wilderness
major Jewish festival (Sukkot)

Examples

Many families build a temporary shelter to celebrate the Feast of Booths.
The Feast of Booths is a time of joy and thanksgiving in the Jewish tradition.
Schools in some areas close during the Feast of Booths to allow Jewish students to observe the holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sukkot - Jewish festival also called Feast of Booths
