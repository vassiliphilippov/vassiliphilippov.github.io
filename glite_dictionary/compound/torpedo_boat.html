torpedo boat

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˈpidoʊ boʊt / (AmE), / tɔːˈpiːdəʊ bəʊt / (BrE)
a small, fast warship designed for launching torpedo attacks, especially in coastal waters
small high-speed warship for torpedo attacks

Examples

The navy deployed a torpedo boat to patrol the coastline near the harbor.
During the battle, the torpedo boat quickly launched its attack before speeding away.
Early in the twentieth century, the torpedo boat became a key part of many coastal defenses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: destroyer - a fast warship larger than a torpedo boat, used to escort and protect other vessels

Etymology

From 'torpedo' (aquatic mine or self-propelled explosive device) and 'boat', combining to describe a vessel specialized for torpedo attack.
