Hindu calendar

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.du ˈkæl.ən.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.djuː ˈkæl.ən.də / (BrE)
a lunisolar calendar system used for determining religious festivals and observances in Hinduism, with an extra month added approximately every three years to align lunar and solar cycles
lunisolar calendar used in Hinduism

Examples

Many Indian festivals are scheduled according to the Hindu calendar instead of the Gregorian calendar.
The Hindu calendar occasionally adds a leap month to keep the lunar months aligned with the solar year.
Astrologers often consult the Hindu calendar to determine auspicious dates for weddings and ceremonies.
