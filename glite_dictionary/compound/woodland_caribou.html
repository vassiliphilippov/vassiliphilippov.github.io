woodland caribou

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.lənd ˈkær.ɪˌbu / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.lənd ˈkær.ɪˌbuː / (BrE)
a large subspecies of caribou (Rangifer tarandus caribou) native to the coniferous forests of southern Canada
subspecies of caribou in Canadian forests

Examples

The woodland caribou is now considered a threatened species in many regions of Canada.
We spotted a small herd of woodland caribou moving quietly through the forest.
Efforts to protect the habitat of the woodland caribou have increased in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caribou - a large North American reindeer
