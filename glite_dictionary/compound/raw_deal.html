raw deal

1. NOUN

/ rɔ ˈdiːl / (AmE), / rɔː ˈdiːl / (BrE)
unfair or harsh treatment; a situation in which someone is treated badly or is given unfair conditions.
unfair treatment
informal

Examples

She got a raw deal when she was blamed for the mistake.
Many workers feel they are getting a raw deal under the new policy.
After years of loyal service, being dismissed like that was truly a raw deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfairness - lack of fairness or justice
  • Antonym: justice - fair and just treatment or behavior

2. NOUN

/ rɔ dɪl / (AmE), / rɔː dɪəl / (BrE)
an unfair or harsh situation or treatment; something that is not just or equitable
unfair or harsh treatment

Examples

After working so hard, he thought he got a raw deal when he was passed over for promotion.
Many customers felt they received a raw deal when the company changed its return policy without notice.
She always speaks up if she thinks someone is getting a raw deal in negotiations.

Synset Examples

He felt he got a raw deal on the car.
That was a raw deal for the workers involved.
She believed she was given a raw deal at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfair - not just, inequitable, or unjust treatment or situation
  • Synonym: rough deal - tough or unfair treatment, similar in meaning
  • Antonym: fair shake - equitable or just treatment
