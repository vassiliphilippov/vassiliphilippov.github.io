stop number

1. NOUN

/ stɑp ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / stɒp ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
The ratio of the focal length to the diameter of a camera lens system, used to indicate the lens aperture.
ratio indicating lens aperture (photography)

Examples

The photographer adjusted the stop number to control the amount of light entering the camera.
A lower stop number means a wider aperture and more exposure.
Many beginners confuse the stop number with shutter speed when learning about camera settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: f-number - another term for the stop number in photography
