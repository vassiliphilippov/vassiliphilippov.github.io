Federal Reserve

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
the central bank of the United States, responsible for regulating the nation's monetary policy and financial system
U.S. central banking system

Examples

The Federal Reserve announced a new interest rate hike to curb inflation.
Many economists closely watch decisions made by the Federal Reserve.
The chairman of the Federal Reserve addressed concerns about the nation's economic outlook.

Synset Examples

the Fed seeks to control the United States economy by raising and lowering short-term interest rates and the money supply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central bank - a bank that manages a state's currency, money supply, and interest rates
