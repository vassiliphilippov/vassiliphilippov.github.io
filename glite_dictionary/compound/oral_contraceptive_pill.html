oral contraceptive pill

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl kənˌtræsɛptɪv pɪl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl kənˌtræsɛptɪv pɪl / (BrE)
a pill taken by mouth containing hormones (estrogen and progestin) to prevent ovulation and thus avoid pregnancy
birth control pill; hormonal contraceptive
medical

Examples

Women are advised to take the oral contraceptive pill at the same time each day for maximum effectiveness.
Her doctor prescribed the oral contraceptive pill to regulate her menstrual cycle as well as prevent pregnancy.
Many teenagers are now choosing the oral contraceptive pill as their primary method of birth control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth control pill - pill taken to prevent pregnancy, containing hormones
  • Synonym: contraceptive - device or drug used to prevent pregnancy
