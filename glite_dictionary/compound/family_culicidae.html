family Culicidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kjuːˈlɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfaməli kjuːˈlɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects comprising all species of mosquitoes.
the mosquito family (insects)

Examples

The family Culicidae includes over 3,500 different species of mosquitoes worldwide.
Researchers study the family Culicidae to understand how mosquitoes transmit diseases like malaria.
Insects of the family Culicidae are widespread in tropical and subtropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - A small flying insect that bites and feeds on blood

Etymology

From Latin 'Culicidae', derived from 'culex' meaning 'gnat' or 'mosquito'.
