anarcho-Bolshevism

1. NOUN

/ ænˌɑrkoʊ boʊlˈʃɛvɪzəm / (AmE), / anˌɑːkəʊ bɒlˈʃɛvɪzəm / (BrE)
A political ideology that seeks to combine principles of anarchism and Bolshevism.
ideology combining anarchism and Bolshevism

Examples

Some historians debate whether anarcho-Bolshevism was ever a cohesive political movement.
The pamphlet outlined the principles of anarcho-Bolshevism during the revolutionary period.
Her research explores the tensions within early anarcho-Bolshevism as it developed among left-wing groups.

Synset Examples

His beliefs were rooted in anarcho-Bolshevism and anti-capitalism.
She studied anarcho-Bolshevism and its historical impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchism - political philosophy advocating stateless societies
  • Synonym: Bolshevism - Russian revolutionary socialist ideology

Etymology

Formed from 'anarcho-' (from 'anarchism') and 'Bolshevism'.
