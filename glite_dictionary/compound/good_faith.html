good faith

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd feɪθ / (AmE), / ɡʊd feɪθ / (BrE)
honest or sincere intention, without any attempt to deceive or defraud
honest intention; sincerity

Examples

The negotiations were conducted in good faith by both parties.
She signed the contract in good faith, unaware of any hidden clauses.
The company acted in good faith to resolve the customer's complaint.

Synset Examples

he acted in good faith
doubt was expressed as to the good faith of the immigrants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bad faith - intentions that are dishonest or meant to deceive
