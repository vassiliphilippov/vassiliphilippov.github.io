telephone set

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn sɛt / (AmE), / ˈtelɪfəʊn set / (BrE)
A device that transmits and receives sound electronically, allowing people to speak to each other over a distance.
device for telephone communication

Examples

She reached for the telephone set to make an urgent call.
The company upgraded every office with a modern telephone set.
He accidentally dropped the telephone set, causing it to stop working.

Synset Examples

I talked to him on the telephone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephone - system or device for transmitting speech at a distance

Etymology

From 'telephone' (from Greek tele- 'far' + phōnē 'voice') + 'set' (collection of equipment).
