bergamot mint

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜːrɡəˌmɑt mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəmət mɪnt / (BrE)
a perennial aromatic herb native to eastern North America, known for its showy clusters of tubular flowers and mint-scented leaves
aromatic herb with showy flowers, native to eastern North America

Examples

The garden was filled with bergamot mint blooming in shades of red and lavender.
Many herbal teas contain bergamot mint for its refreshing, citrus-floral aroma.
Pollinators like bees and butterflies are attracted to bergamot mint during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee balm - common name for several species of Monarda, including bergamot mint

Etymology

Named after 'bergamot' for its similar fragrance, combined with 'mint' due to its botanical family.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːɡəˌmɑt mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəmɒt mɪnt / (BrE)
a type of mint plant whose leaves have a fragrance similar to that of the bergamot orange
mint plant with a bergamot-like scent

Examples

The gardener recommended planting bergamot mint to attract bees and butterflies.
She added a few leaves of bergamot mint to her tea for a citrusy flavor.
Dried bergamot mint is sometimes used in potpourri because of its pleasant aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - an aromatic plant with a cool, refreshing flavor, commonly used in cooking

Etymology

Bergamot from Italian 'bergamotta', the name of the citrus fruit; mint from Old English 'minte'.
