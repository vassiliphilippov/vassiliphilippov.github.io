family Molidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈmoʊlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈmɒlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, ocean-dwelling sunfishes known for their distinct flattened bodies and small mouths
a family of ocean sunfishes

Examples

Members of the family Molidae are among the heaviest known bony fishes in the world.
Researchers discovered a rare species within the family Molidae during their Pacific expedition.
The unique shape of fishes in the family Molidae makes them easily identifiable to marine biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocean sunfish - a large, flattened marine fish, also known as Mola

Etymology

From New Latin 'Molidae', from 'Mola', the type genus, meaning 'millstone' in Latin, referencing the shape of the fish.
