bay scallop

1. NOUN

/ beɪ ˈskæləp / (AmE), / beɪ ˈskæləp / (BrE)
A small edible scallop that inhabits shallow waters and mud flats along the Atlantic coast of North America.
small scallop found on Atlantic coast of North America

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious appetizer using fresh bay scallop ceviche.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the declining populations of the bay scallop.
Fishermen along the New England coast often harvest bay scallop during the fall season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - a type of edible marine bivalve mollusk with a fan-shaped shell
