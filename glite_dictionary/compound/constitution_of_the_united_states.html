Constitution of the United States

1. PROPN

/ kənˌstɪtuˈʃən əv ðə ˌjuːnɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (AmE), / kənˌstɪtjuˈʃən əv ðə ˌjuːnɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the foundational legal document outlining the framework, principles, and organization of the government of the United States, written in 1787 and ratified by the original thirteen states
foundational legal document of the U.S. government

Examples

The Constitution of the United States established the structure and powers of the federal government.
Many legal debates refer directly to the Constitution of the United States for guidance and authority.
The Bill of Rights was added as amendments to the original Constitution of the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitution - a set of fundamental principles by which a state is governed
