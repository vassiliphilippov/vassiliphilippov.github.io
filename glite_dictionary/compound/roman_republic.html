Roman Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊ.mən rɪˈpʌb.lɪk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən rɪˈpʌb.lɪk / (BrE)
the ancient Roman state that existed from 509 BC to 27 BC, characterized by government through an elected Senate and marked by social and political upheaval ending with the rise of Augustus.
ancient Roman state before the empire

Examples

Many of the political institutions of the Roman Republic influenced modern democracies.
Historians often debate the reasons for the fall of the Roman Republic.
Julius Caesar played a pivotal role in the transition from the Roman Republic to the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Roman Empire - the state that succeeded the Roman Republic, beginning with Augustus

Etymology

From Latin 'Roma' (Rome) and 'res publica' (public affair, state).
