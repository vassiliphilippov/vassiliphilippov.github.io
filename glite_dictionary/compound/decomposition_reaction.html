decomposition reaction

1. NOUN

/ diˌkɑmpəˈzɪʃən riˈækʃən / (AmE), / diˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which a single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances
chemical reaction splitting one compound into simpler substances
medical

Examples

A decomposition reaction occurs when hydrogen peroxide breaks down into water and oxygen gas.
During the experiment, we observed a decomposition reaction when the compound was heated.
Many cleaning products rely on a decomposition reaction to release active ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: combination reaction - a chemical reaction where two or more substances combine to form a single product

Etymology

From Latin decompositio (separation) + reaction, referring to a response involving decomposition.
