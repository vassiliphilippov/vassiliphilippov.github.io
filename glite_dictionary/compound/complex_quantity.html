complex quantity

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛks ˈkwɑːntəti / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛks ˈkwɒntɪti / (BrE)
(mathematics) a number of the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers and i is the square root of −1.
number of the form a + bi

Examples

In electrical engineering, impedance is often represented as a complex quantity.
The solution to the equation involves finding a complex quantity with both real and imaginary parts.
Each point on the Argand diagram represents a unique complex quantity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex number - a number of the form a + bi; same as complex quantity
  • Antonym: real number - a value representing a quantity along a continuous line, without an imaginary part
