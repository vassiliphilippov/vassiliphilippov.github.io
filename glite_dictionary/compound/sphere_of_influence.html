sphere of influence

1. NOUN

/ sfɪr əv ɪnˈfluəns / (AmE), / sfɪə r əv ɪnˈfluəns / (BrE)
A country or region in which another country has significant cultural, economic, military, or political influence, though it does not have formal authority over it.
area under another country's strong influence

Examples

During the Cold War, Eastern Europe was considered the Soviet Union's sphere of influence.
China has been extending its sphere of influence in Africa through investment and diplomacy.
The country's leaders were determined to resist being drawn into any foreign sphere of influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphere - an area of activity, interest, or expertise
  • Synonym: dominance - state of having control or influence over others

Etymology

From 'sphere', meaning an area or domain, and 'influence', from Latin *influere* (to flow in); in use since the 19th century in international relations.

2. NOUN

/ sfɪr əv ɪnˈfluəns / (AmE), / sfɪə ɒv ɪnˈfluəns / (BrE)
an area in which a person, organization, or country has power to affect events or decisions, often beyond its formal authority
area of power or influence

Examples

International companies often compete for a larger sphere of influence in emerging markets.
Her innovative ideas quickly expanded her sphere of influence within the organization.
After years of work, his sphere of influence now extends to government policy.

Synset Examples

She operates in a different sphere of influence than her colleagues.
The company's sphere of influence extends globally.
He has a vast sphere of influence in the tech industry.
Her charisma helps expand her sphere of influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area or field over which someone has control or influence
  • Synonym: control - the power to influence or direct people's behavior or the course of events

Etymology

Sphere from Greek 'sphaira' (globe, ball); influence from Latin 'influere' (to flow in).
