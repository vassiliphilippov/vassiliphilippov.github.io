final period

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪnəl ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the last segment or division of play in a game, especially in sports such as hockey or basketball.
last segment of play in a game

Examples

The team made a remarkable comeback in the final period to win the championship.
Fans were on the edge of their seats throughout the final period of the hockey game.
Both coaches emphasized the importance of defense going into the final period.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: overtime - extra period played when a game is tied at the end of regular periods
  • Degree Lower: first period - the initial segment of play in a game divided into periods
