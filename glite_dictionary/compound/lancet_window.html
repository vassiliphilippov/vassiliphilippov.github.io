lancet window

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæn.sɪt ˌwɪn.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːn.sɪt ˌwɪn.dəʊ / (BrE)
a tall, narrow window with a pointed arch at its top, typical of Gothic architecture and usually lacking decorative tracery
tall, narrow, pointed-arch window

Examples

The church was illuminated by sunlight streaming through the stained-glass lancet window behind the altar.
Many cathedrals feature a tall lancet window as part of their original Gothic design.
The restoration team carefully replaced the broken panes in the ancient lancet window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose window - large circular stained-glass window, typically in Gothic churches

Etymology

From 'lancet', referring to the window's resemblance to a lance or spear; 'lancet' from Old French 'lancette', diminutive of 'lance'.
