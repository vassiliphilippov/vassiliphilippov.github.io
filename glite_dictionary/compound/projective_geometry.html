projective geometry

1. NOUN

/ prəˈdʒɛktɪv dʒiˈɑmɪtri / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛktɪv dʒiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
a branch of geometry that studies properties which remain unchanged under projection, such as the alignment of points and the intersection of lines
branch of geometry on projection invariants

Examples

Many advanced courses in mathematics include projective geometry as a separate topic.
The principles of projective geometry are used in fields such as computer graphics and art.
Desargues’ theorem is a fundamental result in projective geometry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometry - mathematics dealing with shapes, sizes, and properties of space

Etymology

From 'projective' (relating to projection) + 'geometry'.
