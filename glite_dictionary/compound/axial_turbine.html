axial turbine

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksiəl tɝːˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈæksiəl tɜːˈbiːn / (BrE)
A type of turbine in which the working fluid flows parallel to the axis of rotation, commonly used in jet engines and power plants.
turbine with fluid flow along the axis

Examples

The axial turbine design is essential for high-performance jet engines.
Repairs were needed after a blade broke off the axial turbine.
Engineers chose an axial turbine for its efficiency at high flow rates.

Synset Examples

Whereas for an axial turbine the rotor is ‘impacted’ by the air flow, for a radial turbine, the flow is smoothly orientated at 90 degrees by the compressor towards the combustion chamber and driving the turbine in the same way water drives a watermill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radial turbine - a turbine in which the working fluid flows perpendicular to the shaft
