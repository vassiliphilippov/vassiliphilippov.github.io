to poke one's head

1. VERB

/ poʊk wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / pəʊk wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to stretch or push one's head forward or through an opening, usually to look or to get attention
to stretch or put one's head forward

Examples

He poked his head around the corner to see if anyone was coming.
She poked her head through the window to say hello.
The dog poked its head out of the car window, enjoying the breeze.

Synset Examples

He poked his head through the sunroof to get a better view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick one's head - to put one's head forward or out through an opening
  • Antonym: withdraw one's head - to pull one's head back from an opening or position
