amaryllis family

1. NOUN

/ əˌmærɪlɪs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / əˌmærɪlɪs ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Amaryllidaceae) including snowdrops, daffodils, and narcissus, sometimes considered a subfamily of the lily family (Liliaceae)
family of flowering plants including daffodils and snowdrops

Examples

The amaryllis family includes many popular garden flowers such as the daffodil and snowdrop.
Some botanists classify the amaryllis family as a subfamily of the lily family.
Bulbs from the amaryllis family are often planted in the autumn for spring blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily family - family of plants including lilies (Liliaceae)

Etymology

Amaryllis from Greek Amaryllis, a shepherdess in Virgil's pastorals, and family from Latin familia.
