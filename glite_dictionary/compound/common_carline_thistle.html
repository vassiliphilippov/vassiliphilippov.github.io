common carline thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈkɑrlaɪn ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkɑːlaɪn ˈθɪsl̩ / (BrE)
A Eurasian thistle (Carlina vulgaris) that grows in sand dunes and dry chalky soils, with spiny leaves and a distinctive yellowish flower head.
spiny Eurasian thistle species

Examples

The common carline thistle is often found in sandy, well-drained soils across parts of Europe.
Gardeners sometimes plant common carline thistle to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
On our hike, we spotted a common carline thistle thriving among the chalky hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a plant with prickly leaves and stems, often with purple flowers
  • Synonym: Carlina vulgaris - the scientific name for the common carline thistle

Etymology

From Latin 'Carlina' (the genus name), sometimes said to be named after Emperor Charlemagne (Carolus).
