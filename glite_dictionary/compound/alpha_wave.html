alpha wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə weɪv / (AmE), / ˈalfə weɪv / (BrE)
a type of brainwave observed in an awake but relaxed person, with a frequency of 8–12 Hz, as seen in an electroencephalogram
type of relaxed brainwave (8–12 Hz)
medical

Examples

The scientist measured the subject's alpha wave activity during meditation.
Listening to soothing music is known to increase alpha wave production in the brain.
When you close your eyes and relax, your brain often produces more alpha waves.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beta wave - a type of brainwave with a higher frequency, associated with alertness

Etymology

From the Greek letter alpha (α), used to designate the first in a series; applied to this brainwave type based on classification order in early EEG research.
