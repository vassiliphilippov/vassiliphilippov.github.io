right fielder

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ˌfiːldər / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌfiːldə / (BrE)
the player who occupies the position of right field in baseball or softball, responsible for fielding balls hit to the right outfield
player in right field position (baseball/softball)

Examples

After his injury, the coach moved him to right fielder to reduce the strain on his arm.
The right fielder made a spectacular catch near the foul line.
Many teams rely on their right fielder to have a strong throwing arm for plays at third base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center fielder - the player who occupies the position of center field in baseball or softball
  • Synonym: left fielder - the player who occupies the position of left field in baseball or softball
