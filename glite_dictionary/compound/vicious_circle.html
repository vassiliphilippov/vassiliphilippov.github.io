vicious circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃəs ˈsɝːkəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃəs ˈsɜːkl̩ / (BrE)
A situation in which the solution to a problem creates circumstances that make the original problem worse or harder to solve, especially by circular reasoning.
self-perpetuating problematic situation

Examples

The country's economic troubles created a vicious circle of rising unemployment and falling demand.
Trying to lose sleep to reduce stress is a vicious circle that only increases anxiety in the long run.
The debate descended into a vicious circle, with each side repeating their original assumptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circular reasoning - an argument in which the conclusion is assumed in the premises
  • Antonym: virtuous circle - a beneficial cycle in which positive effects reinforce themselves

Etymology

From Latin 'vitiosus' (full of faults) and Latin 'circulus' (circle).
