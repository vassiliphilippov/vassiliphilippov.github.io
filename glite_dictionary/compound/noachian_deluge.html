Noachian deluge

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈeɪkiən ˈdɛlˌjudʒ / (AmE), / nəʊˈeɪkɪən ˈdɛljuːdʒ / (BrE)
(Biblical) the great flood described in the Book of Genesis, said to have occurred in the time of Noah as divine punishment for human wickedness.
the Biblical flood in Noah's time

Examples

The story of the Noachian deluge is found in both biblical and ancient Near Eastern literature.
Many ancient myths parallel the Noachian deluge, though details of the flood often differ.
The Noachian deluge has inspired countless works of art and literature through the centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Flood - another term for the deluge in the time of Noah

Etymology

From Noachian, relating to Noah (from Hebrew ‘Noach’), combined with 'deluge', from Latin 'diluvium' (flood).
