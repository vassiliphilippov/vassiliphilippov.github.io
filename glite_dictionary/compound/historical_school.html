historical school

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkəl skuːl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəl skuːl / (BrE)
A school of 19th-century German economists and legal philosophers who explained modern economic and legal systems in evolutionary or historical terms.
19th-century German economic and legal school

Examples

The ideas of the historical school greatly influenced later developments in social science.
Many scholars debate the merits of the historical school compared to classical economic theories.
The historical school emphasized the importance of cultural context when analyzing economic systems.
