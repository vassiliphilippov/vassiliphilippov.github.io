celestial point

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛstʃəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstɪəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a point on the celestial sphere used in astronomy to define positions or directions in the sky
astronomical point on celestial sphere

Examples

The vernal equinox is a significant celestial point used in astronomy to mark the beginning of spring.
Navigators once relied on measuring angles between stars and a celestial point to determine their position at sea.
Each celestial point is defined by its coordinates on the celestial sphere.

Etymology

From Latin 'caelestis' (heavenly) + English 'point'.
