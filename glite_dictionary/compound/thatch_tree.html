thatch tree

1. NOUN

/ θætʃ tri / (AmE), / θætʃ triː / (BrE)
A small palm native to southern Florida and the West Indies, resembling the silvertop palmetto.
a small palm of southern Florida and the West Indies

Examples

The thatch tree is often used in landscaping because of its resilience in tropical climates.
Many traditional roofs in the Caribbean are made from the fronds of the thatch tree.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of the thatch tree in southern Florida.
