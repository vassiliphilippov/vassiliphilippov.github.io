water back

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər bæk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə bæk / (BrE)
A water heater consisting of a tank or pipes installed at the back of a fireplace or in the firebox of a stove, used to heat water for domestic use.
tank/pipes for heating water in a fireplace or stove

Examples

The old farmhouse still had a water back behind the kitchen stove.
Before electric heaters, many homes relied on a water back to provide hot water.
The plumber replaced the rusted water back in the fireplace with a modern unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boiler - a device for heating water
