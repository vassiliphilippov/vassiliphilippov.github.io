inferior conjunction

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
an alignment in which the Earth and a planet are on the same side of the Sun, appearing in a straight line
astronomical alignment; planet and Earth on same side of Sun

Examples

During inferior conjunction, Mercury passes directly between the Earth and the Sun.
Venus is closest to Earth when it is at inferior conjunction.
Astronomers observe planetary transits during inferior conjunction events.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior conjunction - alignment of the Earth and a planet on opposite sides of the Sun

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctio' (joining together); 'inferior' from Latin 'inferior' (lower, below).
