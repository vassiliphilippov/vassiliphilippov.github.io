American Labor Party

1. PROPN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈleɪ.bɚ ˈpɑːr.ti / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈleɪ.bə ˈpɑː.ti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, established in 1936 in New York by labor unions and liberals who broke away from the Democratic Party
former US political party (1930s-1950s)

Examples

The American Labor Party played a significant role in New York politics during the 1930s and 1940s.
Many progressive candidates received support from the American Labor Party in various local elections.
After the decline of the American Labor Party, its members often returned to the Democratic fold.
