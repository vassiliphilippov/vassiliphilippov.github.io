Asiatic cholera

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪʒiˈætɪk ˈkoʊlərə / (AmE), / ˌeɪzɪˈatɪk ˈkɒlərə / (BrE)
an acute and often severe intestinal infection caused by ingesting contaminated water or food, characterized by profuse diarrhea and dehydration
severe intestinal infection from contaminated water or food
medical

Examples

In the 19th century, outbreaks of Asiatic cholera devastated cities across Europe.
The government implemented strict sanitation reforms to prevent the spread of Asiatic cholera.
Doctors treated Asiatic cholera with oral rehydration solutions to combat dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholera - an infectious and often fatal bacterial disease of the small intestine

Etymology

From 'Asiatic' (relating to Asia) and 'cholera', the disease was first widely recognized during epidemics in Asia in the 19th century.
