diving bell

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪvɪŋ bɛl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪvɪŋ bɛl / (BrE)
a strong chamber with an open bottom, supplied with compressed air, used to transport people or equipment underwater for work or exploration
underwater chamber for diving work

Examples

The workers descended to the ocean floor inside a diving bell.
Early salvage operations relied on the use of a diving bell.
A modern diving bell can withstand significant underwater pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a vessel designed for underwater operations
