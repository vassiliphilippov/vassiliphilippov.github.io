surface chemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːfɪs ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry concerned with chemical and physical processes occurring at the surfaces or interfaces between different phases, especially between a liquid and a gas
branch of chemistry studying interfaces

Examples

Surface chemistry plays a crucial role in the development of new catalysts for industrial processes.
The study of surface chemistry helps explain how detergents clean oily surfaces.
Advances in surface chemistry have led to innovations in nanotechnology and materials science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical chemistry - branch of chemistry dealing with physical properties and processes
