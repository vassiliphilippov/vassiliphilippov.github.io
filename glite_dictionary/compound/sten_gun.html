Sten gun

1. NOUN

/ stɛn ˌgʌn / (AmE), / stɛn ˌɡʌn / (BrE)
A lightweight submachine gun, widely used by British forces during World War II.
British WWII submachine gun
british

Examples

The soldiers advanced cautiously, each carrying a Sten gun for protection.
During the war museum tour, we saw an original Sten gun on display.
He recounted stories of using his Sten gun during the fierce battles of World War II.

Etymology

Named after its designers Shepherd and Turpin, and ENfield where it was first produced.
