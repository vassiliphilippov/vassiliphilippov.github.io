lipid-lowering medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪpɪd ˌloʊərɪŋ ˈmɛdəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪpɪd ˌləʊərɪŋ ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
a medicine that reduces blood cholesterol levels by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase, especially used to lower lipid levels in the bloodstream
drug that reduces cholesterol levels
medical

Examples

Patients with high cholesterol are often prescribed a lipid-lowering medicine to manage their condition.
My doctor recommended a lipid-lowering medicine after my recent blood test results showed elevated cholesterol levels.
Statins are a commonly used type of lipid-lowering medicine in cardiovascular disease prevention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statin - a class of drugs that reduce cholesterol by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase
