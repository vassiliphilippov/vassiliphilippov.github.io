academic gown

1. NOUN

/ əˈkædəmɪk ɡaʊn / (AmE), / əˈkadəmɪk ɡaʊn / (BrE)
a long, loose robe worn on formal academic occasions, especially by university staff and graduates
formal robe for academics or graduates

Examples

Graduates marched across the stage in their academic gowns to receive their diplomas.
The professor donned his academic gown before the commencement ceremony began.
In many universities, an academic gown is worn by faculty members during formal events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robe - a long, loose outer garment; general term for a similar garment
  • Synonym: cap and gown - the traditional academic dress worn for graduation ceremonies
