field marshal

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
the highest rank of officer in some armies, usually above general
highest army officer rank

Examples

The president promoted him to field marshal after his victories in the war.
Few countries still appoint field marshals in modern times.
As a field marshal, she commanded the entire armed forces.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: general - an officer of high rank in the army, but usually one rank below field marshal

Etymology

From field (open land, battlefield) + marshal (from Old French mareschal, meaning 'high officer'); originally a senior commander on the battlefield.
