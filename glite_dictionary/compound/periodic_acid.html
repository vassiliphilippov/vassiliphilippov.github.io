periodic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪriˈɑdɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriˈɒdɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline acid of iodine containing oxygen, used as an oxidizing agent in chemistry
an oxidizing acid of iodine
medical

Examples

Periodic acid is commonly used to oxidize sugars in laboratory analyses.
The reaction involves adding periodic acid to the solution as an oxidizing agent.
Researchers studied the effects of periodic acid on various organic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: iodic acid - an acid of iodine containing oxygen with lower oxidation state

Etymology

From 'periodic' (chemistry term referring to per-iodic, relating to iodine's oxidation states) + 'acid'.
