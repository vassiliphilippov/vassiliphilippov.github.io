deflationary spiral

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfleɪʃəˌnɛri ˈspaɪrəl / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃənəri ˈspaɪərəl / (BrE)
A situation in which falling prices and wages accelerate, causing further decreases and increasing the value of currency, often leading to a worsening economic downturn.
worsening cycle of falling prices and wages

Examples

During the recession, the country risked entering a deflationary spiral as prices and wages kept dropping rapidly.
Economists warned that a deflationary spiral could lead to prolonged economic stagnation.
Central banks often take action to prevent a deflationary spiral from taking hold in the economy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflationary spiral - A cycle of rising prices and wages that intensifies itself
  • Degree Lower: deflation - A general decline in prices across an economy
