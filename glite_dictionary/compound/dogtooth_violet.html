dogtooth violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌtuθ ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌtuːθ ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
A perennial woodland plant with spring flowers, widely cultivated, of the genus Erythronium.
a perennial woodland spring-flowering plant

Examples

The dogtooth violet blooms early in the spring, carpeting the forest floor with its delicate flowers.
Several varieties of dogtooth violet are popular in ornamental gardens for their attractive foliage and blossoms.
She recognized the broad leaves of a dogtooth violet growing by the woodland stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erythronium - botanical genus including the dogtooth violet

Etymology

From the resemblance of its bulb to a dog's tooth; 'violet' from the flower's color.
