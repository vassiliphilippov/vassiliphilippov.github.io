genus Clangula

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs klæŋˈɡjuːlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs klæŋˈɡjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of ducks in the family Anatidae, which includes the long-tailed duck.
a genus of ducks

Examples

The genus Clangula comprises a single living species, the long-tailed duck.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of birds in the genus Clangula.
The genus Clangula is known primarily for its unique adaptation to cold water environments.

Etymology

From Latin 'clangula', a diminutive of 'clangere' (to resound), used as the name for this duck genus.
