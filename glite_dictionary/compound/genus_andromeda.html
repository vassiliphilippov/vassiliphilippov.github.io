genus Andromeda

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ænˈdrɑːmədə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ænˈdrɒmɪdə / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing shrubs native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere, belonging to the heath family (Ericaceae)
a genus of northern low-growing shrubs

Examples

The genus Andromeda contains attractive evergreen shrubs often found in boggy soils.
Gardeners appreciate species in the genus Andromeda for their delicate pink flowers.
Botanists studied the distribution of the genus Andromeda throughout northern latitudes.

Etymology

New Latin, named after Andromeda, a figure in Greek mythology, for the plant's perceived delicate beauty.
