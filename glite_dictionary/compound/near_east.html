Near East

1. PROPN

/ nɪr ist / (AmE), / nɪə iːst / (BrE)
a historical term for the region around the eastern Mediterranean, typically including Southwest Asia and Egypt, notable as the birthplace of several ancient civilizations and major religions
historical region around eastern Mediterranean

Examples

He has traveled extensively throughout the Near East as part of his research on ancient cultures.
Many pivotal events in world history took place in the Near East.
Archaeologists uncovered new artifacts from the Near East dating back thousands of years.

Synset Examples

the Middle East is the cradle of Western civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Middle East - region in Western Asia and Northeast Africa, often used interchangeably with Near East in modern usage

Etymology

From 'near', meaning close, and 'east', indicating the direction relative to Europe; used since the late 19th century to distinguish it from the 'Far East'.
