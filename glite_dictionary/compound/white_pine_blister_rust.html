white pine blister rust

1. NOUN

/ waɪt paɪn ˈblɪstər rʌst / (AmE), / waɪt paɪn ˈblɪstə rʌst / (BrE)
a serious disease of pines, especially white pines, caused by rust fungi of the genus Cronartium, characterized by blisters and damage to bark and sapwood
disease of pines caused by rust fungi

Examples

The forestry team discovered white pine blister rust affecting several trees in the national park.
Proper management practices can help prevent the spread of white pine blister rust in young forests.
Scientists are researching ways to make white pines more resistant to white pine blister rust.
