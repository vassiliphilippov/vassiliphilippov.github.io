Sixth Crusade

1. NOUN

/ sɪksθ kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / sɪksθ kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
the Crusade from 1228 to 1229 led by Holy Roman Emperor Frederick II, who secured control of Jerusalem through negotiation rather than battle
Crusade (1228–1229) led by Frederick II

Examples

Frederick II embarked on the Sixth Crusade after being excommunicated by the Pope.
The Sixth Crusade was notable for achieving its goals primarily through diplomacy.
Unlike previous campaigns, the Sixth Crusade restored Jerusalem to Christian rule without major battles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Fifth Crusade - the Crusade preceding the Sixth Crusade, from 1217 to 1221
  • Degree Higher: Seventh Crusade - the Crusade following the Sixth Crusade, launched in 1248
