polyunsaturated fatty acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˌʌnˌsætʃəˈreɪtɪd ˈfæti ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌʌnˌsætʃəˈreɪtɪd ˈfæti ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an unsaturated fatty acid with two or more double or triple bonds in its carbon chain, commonly found in fish and certain vegetable oils
type of fatty acid with multiple double bonds
medical

Examples

Many health experts recommend increasing your intake of polyunsaturated fatty acids for better heart health.
Salmon is a good source of polyunsaturated fatty acids, which can help reduce cholesterol levels.
Corn and soybean oils are rich in polyunsaturated fatty acids.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monounsaturated fatty acid - a fatty acid with only one double bond in its carbon chain

Etymology

From 'poly-' meaning 'many', 'unsaturated' (not containing the maximum number of hydrogen atoms), and 'fatty acid'.
