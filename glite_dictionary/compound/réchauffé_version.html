réchauffé version

1. NOUN

/ reɪˈʃoʊfeɪ ˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / reɪˈʃɒfeɪ ˈvɜːʒən / (BrE)
a re-used or recycled version of something, especially lacking originality or freshness
recycled or reused version

Examples

The director's new film felt like a réchauffé version of his earlier work.
Many critics dismissed the novel as a réchauffé version of classic detective stories.
Audiences are quick to spot a réchauffé version of last year's trend.

Synset Examples

Her story felt like a réchauffé version of last year's news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recycled version - an edition or instance reused from previous material
  • Antonym: original version - the first or earliest version of something

Etymology

From French 'réchauffé' (reheated), used in English for something served up again.
