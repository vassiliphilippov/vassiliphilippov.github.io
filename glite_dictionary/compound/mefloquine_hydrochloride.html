mefloquine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛf.ləˌkwiːn haɪˌdrɒk.sɪˈlɔːr.aɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɛf.ləˌkwiːn hʌɪˌdrɒksɪˈlɔːraɪd / (BrE)
an antimalarial drug used to treat and prevent malaria, especially in cases resistant to chloroquine; may cause neuropsychiatric side effects in some individuals
antimalarial drug for chloroquine-resistant cases
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed mefloquine hydrochloride for her trip to a malaria-endemic region.
Some patients experience vivid dreams or anxiety after taking mefloquine hydrochloride.
Resistance to chloroquine has increased the use of mefloquine hydrochloride in travel medicine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chloroquine - a medication to prevent and treat malaria
  • Synonym: quinine - an antimalarial drug derived from cinchona bark
