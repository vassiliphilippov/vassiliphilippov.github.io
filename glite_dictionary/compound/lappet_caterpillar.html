lappet caterpillar

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪt ˈkæt̬ɚˌpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪt ˈkætəpɪlə / (BrE)
the larval stage of a lappet moth, typically distinguished by its tufts or lappet-like protuberances
larva of a lappet moth

Examples

The lappet caterpillar is easily recognized by the flaps of skin along its sides.
Gardeners should be aware of the damage lappet caterpillars can cause to fruit trees.
As it grows, the lappet caterpillar sheds its skin several times before becoming a moth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lappet moth - a moth of the family Lasiocampidae, known for its lobed skin flaps (lappets) on the caterpillar
