chest tone

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛst toʊn / (AmE), / tʃɛst təʊn / (BrE)
the lower range or register of the human voice, especially as used in speaking or singing, characterized by resonance in the chest area
lower vocal register in singing or speech

Examples

The singer used a rich chest tone to bring warmth to the low notes.
Proper breath support enhances your chest tone when performing.
Actors often develop their chest tone to improve projection and depth in their voice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: head voice - upper vocal register produced with resonance in the head
  • Synonym: chest register - vocal register associated with chest resonance
