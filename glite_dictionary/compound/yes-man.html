yes-man

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛs mæn / (AmE), / ˈjɛs man / (BrE)
a person who always agrees with people in authority, especially to gain their approval; someone who never opposes or questions their superiors
someone who always agrees with authority

Examples

He's just a yes-man who never dares to voice his own opinion.
The manager surrounded herself with yes-men to avoid criticism of her decisions.
In politics, a yes-man is rarely respected by colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - someone who acts obsequiously toward someone important
  • Synonym: toady - a person who behaves obsequiously to someone important
  • Antonym: contrarian - a person who opposes or rejects popular opinion

Etymology

Formed from 'yes' (expression of assent) and 'man' (person). Popularized in early 20th-century English.
