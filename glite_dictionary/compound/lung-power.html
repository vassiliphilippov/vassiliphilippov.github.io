lung-power

1. NOUN

/ lʌŋ ˈpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / lʌŋ ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the ability to speak or sing loudly and strongly, especially for an extended period
ability to speak or sing loudly

Examples

The opera singer's remarkable lung-power impressed everyone in the audience.
Coaches often say that athletes need good lung-power for shouting instructions during a game.
Her lung-power made her the ideal choice for the role of town crier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voice - sound produced in a person's larynx and uttered through the mouth

Etymology

Formed from 'lung' (referring to the organ used in breathing) and 'power,' indicating strength or capacity.
