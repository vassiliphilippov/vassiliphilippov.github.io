tussock bellflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌsək ˈbɛlˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈtʌsək ˈbɛlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A European perennial plant of the bellflower family, growing in clumps with spreading stems and blue or white bell-shaped flowers.
European perennial bellflower plant

Examples

The tussock bellflower brightens gardens with its clusters of blue blossoms every summer.
She planted tussock bellflowers along the border to add a splash of color.
In the wild, tussock bellflowers often grow together in dense clumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - any plant of the genus Campanula, known for their bell-shaped flowers
