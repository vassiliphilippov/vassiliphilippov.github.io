little yus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtəl ˈjuːs / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈjuːs / (BrE)
a letter representing a nasalized front vowel in the early Cyrillic and Glagolitic alphabets, used in Common Slavonic
Slavic alphabet letter for nasal vowel

Examples

The little yus was used to represent a nasalized vowel in Old Church Slavonic manuscripts.
Scholars study the usage of little yus to understand historical Slavic phonology.
The letter little yus no longer appears in modern Cyrillic alphabets.

Synset Examples

The names of the letters do not imply capitalization, as both little and big yus exist in majuscule and minuscule variants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big yus - related Slavic alphabet letter for a back nasal vowel

Etymology

From English 'little' + Old Church Slavonic 'jusŭ' (used for the vowel letter).
