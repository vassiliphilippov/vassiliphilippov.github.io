Carolina allspice

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæɹəˈlaɪnə ˈɔlˌspaɪs / (AmE), / ˌkarəˈlʌɪnə ˈɔːlˌspʌɪs / (BrE)
a hardy shrub native to the southeastern United States, noted for its clove-scented wood and fragrant reddish-brown flowers
a fragrant shrub of the southeastern U.S.

Examples

The Carolina allspice fills the spring air with a rich, spicy fragrance from its blossoms.
Gardeners admire the Carolina allspice for its attractive, aromatic foliage and flowers.
A mature Carolina allspice shrub can become a highlight in a shade garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetshrub - another common name for Carolina allspice
  • Synonym: Calycanthus floridus - the scientific name for Carolina allspice
