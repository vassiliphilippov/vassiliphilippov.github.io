battle axe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l ˌæks / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl ˌæks / (BrE)
A large, heavy axe used as a weapon in combat; informally, an aggressive, domineering woman.
large combat axe; aggressive woman (informal)
informal

Examples

The museum displayed a medieval battle axe along with other ancient weapons.
His grandmother was a real battle axe, always scolding the neighbors for their behavior.
In the fantasy novel, the hero wielded a massive battle axe in the final fight.

Synset Examples

The old lady was known as the neighborhood's battle axe.
She used her battle axe to chop firewood.
He swung his battle axe with fierce determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axe - a tool with a heavy bladed head mounted across a handle
  • Synonym: shrew - a bad-tempered or aggressively assertive woman

Etymology

From 'battle' and 'axe'; weapon sense from Middle English period; figurative use (woman) coins from early 20th century.
