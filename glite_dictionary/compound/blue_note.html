blue note

1. NOUN

/ bluː noʊt / (AmE), / bluː nəʊt / (BrE)
A musical note, typically the third or seventh degree of a scale, that is slightly lowered in pitch for expressive effect in jazz or blues music.
note slightly flattened in jazz/blues

Examples

In jazz improvisation, the blue note gives the melody its soulful character.
The guitarist used a blue note to add emotional depth to his solo.
Singers often slide into the blue note to express sadness or longing in blues music.
