chorale prelude

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈrɑl ˈprɛljuːd / (AmE), / kɔːˈrɑːl ˈprɛljuːd / (BrE)
A short organ composition based on a chorale melody, typically elaborated with variations.
organ piece based on a chorale

Examples

The organist played a beautiful chorale prelude before the church service began.
Bach composed many chorale preludes that are still performed today.
A chorale prelude often serves as an introduction to congregational singing.

Etymology

From German 'Choralvorspiel,' with 'chorale' denoting a hymn tune and 'prelude' from Latin 'praeludium.'
