supporting tower

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɔrtɪŋ ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / səˈpɔːtɪŋ ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
a tower constructed to bear the weight of or steady another structure, such as a bridge or power line
tower that provides structural support

Examples

The main cables of the suspension bridge rest on a supporting tower at each end of the span.
Workers inspected the supporting tower to ensure it could hold the new transmission lines safely.
A supporting tower is essential for stabilizing tall radio antennas in windy conditions.
