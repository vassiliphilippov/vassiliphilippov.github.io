Swiss Confederation

1. PROPN

/ swɪs kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / swɪs kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the official name of Switzerland, a landlocked federal republic in central Europe
official name of Switzerland

Examples

The Swiss Confederation was established in 1848 as a federal state.
The Swiss Confederation is known for its policy of neutrality.
Delegates from various cantons attended the founding of the Swiss Confederation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Switzerland - the modern country in central Europe

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Confoederatio Helvetica', referring to the union of Swiss cantons.
