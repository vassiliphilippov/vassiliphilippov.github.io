the p-word

1. NOUN

/ ði ˈpiː ˌwɝːd / (AmE), / ði ˈpiː ˌwɜːd / (BrE)
A euphemism used to refer indirectly to a word beginning with 'p' that is considered sensitive, taboo, or offensive.
euphemism for a sensitive 'p' word

Examples

The politician deliberately avoided saying the p-word on national television.
Teachers are cautious about mentioning the p-word in front of students.
Some people find even the use of the p-word offensive in public discussions.

Synset Examples

He avoided using the p-word during the meeting.
The p-word often sparks heated debates.
Discussing the p-word can be controversial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the n-word - euphemism for a racial slur starting with 'n'
  • Synonym: the f-word - euphemism for an offensive or taboo word starting with 'f'

Etymology

Modelled after similar euphemisms like 'the n-word', formed from the initial letter plus 'word'.
