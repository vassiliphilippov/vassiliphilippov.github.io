draught beer

1. NOUN

/ dræft bɪr / (AmE), / drɑːft bɪə / (BrE)
beer served from a keg or cask rather than from a bottle or can
beer served from a keg or cask

Examples

He always prefers to order draught beer instead of bottled beer at the pub.
The bartender offered a wide selection of draught beer on tap.
Some people believe that draught beer has a fresher taste than canned beer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bottled beer - beer sold in bottles
  • Synonym: keg beer - beer served from a pressurized keg

Etymology

'Draught' from Old English 'dragan' (to draw or pull), referring to beer drawn from a cask.
