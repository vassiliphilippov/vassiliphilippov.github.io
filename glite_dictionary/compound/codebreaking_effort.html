codebreaking effort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊdˌbreɪkɪŋ ˈɛfərt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdˌbreɪkɪŋ ˈefət / (BrE)
an operation or organized activity focused on deciphering coded or encrypted messages, especially in a military or intelligence context
activity to decipher coded messages

Examples

The Bletchley Park team's codebreaking effort played a pivotal role in shortening World War II.
A successful codebreaking effort can reveal important enemy communications and strategies.
Modern cybersecurity increasingly relies on both strong encryption and the ability to resist any codebreaking effort.

Synset Examples

The Enigma machine was crucial in WWII codebreaking efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptanalysis - the analysis of codes and ciphers to break them
  • Degree Higher: intelligence operation - a covert activity to gather secret information, often by a government
