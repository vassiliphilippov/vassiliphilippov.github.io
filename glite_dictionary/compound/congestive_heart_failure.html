congestive heart failure

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛstɪv hɑrt ˈfeɪljər / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstɪv hɑːt ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
a condition in which the heart is unable to pump blood efficiently, causing a buildup of fluid in the tissues
heart condition with fluid buildup
medical

Examples

Her grandfather was admitted to the hospital with congestive heart failure and required special treatment.
People with congestive heart failure often experience swelling in their legs and shortness of breath.
The doctor explained that lifestyle changes could help manage congestive heart failure symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart failure - the inability of the heart to pump blood effectively
