poor man's cocaine

1. NOUN

/ pʊr mænz koʊˈkeɪn / (AmE), / pɔː mænz kəʊˈkeɪn / (BrE)
A cheap or easily available substitute for cocaine, often used to refer to stimulants like caffeine.
inexpensive substitute for cocaine; usually caffeine
informal

Examples

Many people call energy drinks the poor man's cocaine because of their stimulating effects.
After pulling an all-nighter, she reached for poor man's cocaine to stay awake during her shift.
He joked that strong coffee was his daily dose of poor man's cocaine.
