water hemlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈhɛmlɑk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
a tall, extremely poisonous perennial herb of the parsley family, found in wet places in Eurasia and North America
highly poisonous marsh plant

Examples

The leaves and roots of water hemlock are highly toxic to both humans and animals.
After mistakenly ingesting water hemlock, the hikers required immediate medical attention.
Farmers warn their children to avoid picking wild plants like water hemlock near streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - the plant family including several poisonous species
