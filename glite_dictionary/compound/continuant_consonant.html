continuant consonant

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.ənt ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.ənt ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by narrowing but not fully closing the vocal tract, allowing the airstream to pass continuously
consonant sound allowing continuous airflow

Examples

Fricatives like continuant consonant sounds are common in European languages.
The English 'v' is classified as a continuant consonant because air flows through the mouth without complete obstruction.
Students learned to distinguish a continuant consonant from a stop during their linguistics lesson.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stop consonant - a consonant produced by blocking the airflow completely

Etymology

From 'continuant', from Latin 'continuare' (to make continuous), and 'consonant' from Latin 'consonans'.
