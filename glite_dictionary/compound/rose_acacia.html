rose acacia

1. NOUN

/ roʊz əˈkeɪʃə / (AmE), / rəʊz əˈkeɪsɪə / (BrE)
A large shrub or small tree native to eastern North America, with bristly stems and large clusters of pink flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental.
ornamental shrub or tree with pink blossoms

Examples

The rose acacia in our garden blooms brilliantly every spring.
Horticulturists often recommend rose acacia for adding color to landscapes.
We planted a rose acacia near the entrance to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Robinia hispida - The scientific name for rose acacia
