spearnose bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrˌnoʊz bæt / (AmE), / ˈspɪəˌnəʊz bæt / (BrE)
A New World bat with a pointed nose leaf, found from the southern United States to Paraguay.
New World bat with a pointed nose leaf

Examples

The spearnose bat is known for its distinctive nose shape and echolocation abilities.
Researchers discovered a small colony of spearnose bats roosting in a cave in Texas.
Unlike other bat species, the spearnose bat prefers lowland forests near rivers.
