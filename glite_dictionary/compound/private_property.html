private property

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
Movable possessions or personal property, as opposed to real estate or land.
movable personal property (not real estate)

Examples

The law protects the rights of individuals to own private property such as vehicles and jewelry.
Customs officials searched the bags, treating them as private property belonging to the travelers.
The distinction between private property and land holdings is important in both taxation and inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real property - land and buildings as opposed to movable possessions

Etymology

Formed from 'private' (from Latin privatus, 'individual, personal') and 'property' (from Latin proprietas, 'ownership').
