chronic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɑːnɪk ləˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌkrɒnɪk lɪˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A form of leukemia that progresses slowly over time, often developing gradually and lasting for months or years.
slowly progressing form of leukemia
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with chronic leukemia after noticing an abnormal increase in white blood cells.
Treatment options for chronic leukemia have improved significantly over the past decade.
Unlike acute forms, chronic leukemia can remain asymptomatic for years before showing noticeable symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acute leukemia - a rapidly progressing form of leukemia

Etymology

From Greek *khronikos* (long-lasting) and *leukaimia* (white blood), referring to a persistent disease of the blood.
