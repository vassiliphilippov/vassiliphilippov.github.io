colour wash

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər wɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə wɒʃ / (BrE)
A thin wash of whitewash or water-based paint tinted with a colored pigment, used to give a subtle, semi-transparent color to a surface.
thin, tinted wash of paint for subtle color
british

Examples

The artist applied a gentle colour wash over the canvas to create a soft background.
Victorian homes often feature walls decorated with a pastel colour wash.
A blue colour wash can give a calming effect to any room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitewash - a solution of lime and water, used for whitening walls
  • Synonym: wash - a thin layer of diluted paint
