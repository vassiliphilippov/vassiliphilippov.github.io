northern oriole

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðɚn ɔˈriː.oʊl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
a medium-sized, brightly colored New World oriole, formerly considered a single species, now split into the Baltimore oriole and Bullock's oriole.
a species of New World oriole bird

Examples

The northern oriole is known for its vivid orange and black plumage.
In spring, birdwatchers hope to spot a northern oriole in the park.
Song recordings helped scientists distinguish between the Baltimore and Bullock's forms of the northern oriole.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Baltimore oriole - a species of oriole found in eastern North America
  • Degree Lower: Bullock's oriole - a species of oriole found in western North America
