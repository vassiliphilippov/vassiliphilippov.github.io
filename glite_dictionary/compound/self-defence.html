self-defence

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / sɛlf dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
the act of protecting oneself from physical harm, especially by using force as a response to an attack
the act of protecting oneself from harm

Examples

She claimed she acted in self-defence when she hit her attacker.
Learning self-defence can boost your confidence in dangerous situations.
The suspect said the shooting was justified as an act of self-defence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-protection - the act of keeping oneself safe from harm

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'defence', originally from Old French 'defense', from Latin 'defensio'.
