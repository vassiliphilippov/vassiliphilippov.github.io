pix chest

1. NOUN

/ pɪks tʃɛst / (AmE), / pɪks tʃɛst / (BrE)
a chest in which newly minted coins are stored pending official examination (assay)
chest for coins awaiting assay

Examples

The newly minted coins were carefully placed in the pix chest for safekeeping until the assayers arrived.
Each pix chest was sealed and stored securely as part of the official minting process.
Historians studied the contents of an old pix chest found in the Royal Mint archives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyx - the box or chest used in the trial of the pyx

Etymology

From 'pix', which comes from Latin 'pyxis' (box), used especially for holding samples for the trial of the pyx, a historic procedure in the UK Mint.
