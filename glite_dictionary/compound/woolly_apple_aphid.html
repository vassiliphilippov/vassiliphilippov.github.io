woolly apple aphid

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊli ˈæpəl ˈæfɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈapəl ˈafɪd / (BrE)
a small, sap-sucking insect that infests apple trees, characterized by a white, wool-like filament covering its body, and commonly causing damage to bark, roots, and branches.
sap-sucking insect pest of apple trees

Examples

The orchard suffered significant damage due to an infestation of woolly apple aphid.
Gardeners recommend monitoring trees regularly to detect the presence of woolly apple aphid colonies early.
A sticky, white substance on the bark is often a sign of woolly apple aphid activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small sap-sucking insect of the superfamily Aphidoidea
