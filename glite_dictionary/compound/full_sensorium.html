full sensorium

1. NOUN

/ fʊl sɛnˈsɔriəm / (AmE), / fʊl sɛnˈsɔːriəm / (BrE)
an intense experience that engages all the senses at once
multi-sensory intense experience

Examples

The multimedia exhibition was a full sensorium, immersing visitors in visuals, sound, and tactile displays.
When she entered the bustling market, the aromas, colors, and noises created a full sensorium effect.
The chef's new dish is designed to offer diners a full sensorium of taste, smell, and texture.

Synset Examples

The concert was a full sensorium of sights and sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensory overload - a state of being overwhelmed by intense sensory input

Etymology

From Latin *sensorium* (seat of sensation or sense organ).
