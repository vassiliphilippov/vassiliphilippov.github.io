Blackburnian warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˌblækˈbɜːrniən ˈwɔːrblɚ / (AmE), / ˌblækˈbɜːniən ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
A North American wood warbler with striking black-and-white plumage and a bright orange-and-black head and throat.
a North American wood warbler species

Examples

We spotted a Blackburnian warbler perched high in the spruce tree.
The bright orange throat of the Blackburnian warbler makes it easy to identify during migration season.
Many birdwatchers travel north in spring just to catch a glimpse of the Blackburnian warbler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood warbler - a group of small songbirds found in woodland, mostly in North America

Etymology

Named after Anna Blackburne, an English naturalist.
