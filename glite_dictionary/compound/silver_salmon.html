silver salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a small salmon, found along the northern Pacific coasts and the Great Lakes, noted for its silvery appearance
a small silvery Pacific salmon

Examples

Fishermen hope to catch silver salmon as they migrate upstream each fall.
The silver salmon is highly prized by both commercial and sport fishers.
Researchers studied the life cycle of the silver salmon in the Great Lakes region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coho salmon - a small salmon species native to the North Pacific (Oncorhynchus kisutch)

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈsæmən / (BrE)
the pinkish, fatty flesh of a small salmon species (the coho salmon) found in the Pacific and Great Lakes, used as food
a type of salmon flesh used as food

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious meal featuring silver salmon with lemon and herbs.
Anglers are often excited to catch silver salmon during the fall spawning season.
You can find fresh silver salmon in local fish markets along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coho salmon - another name for silver salmon, a species of salmon
  • Degree Higher: sockeye salmon - species of salmon with red flesh

Etymology

Named for the silvery color of the fish's body; refers to the coho salmon (Oncorhynchus kisutch).
