family Lycosidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli laɪˈkɑsɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli laɪˈkəʊsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of spiders commonly known as wolf spiders, characterized by their robust bodies and excellent vision.
the wolf spider family of spiders

Examples

The family Lycosidae contains hundreds of species commonly referred to as wolf spiders.
Members of the family Lycosidae are known for their excellent eyesight and hunting skills.
Biologists studied several genera within the family Lycosidae to understand their behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf spider - a common name for members of the family Lycosidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Lycosidae', based on Greek 'lykos' (wolf), referring to the spiders' hunting habits.
