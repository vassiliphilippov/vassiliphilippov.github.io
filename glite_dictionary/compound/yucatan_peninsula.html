Yucatan Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː.kəˈtɑːn pəˈnɪn.sə.lə / (AmE), / ˌjuː.kəˈtæn pəˈnɪn.sɪ.lə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in southeastern Mexico, separating the Gulf of Mexico from the Caribbean Sea
peninsula in southeastern Mexico

Examples

Many tourists visit the Yucatan Peninsula to see the ancient Mayan ruins of Chichen Itza.
The Yucatan Peninsula is known for its unique wildlife and beautiful beaches along the Caribbean coast.
Hurricanes occasionally strike the Yucatan Peninsula during the Atlantic storm season.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Yucatán', the name given to the region by early Spanish explorers.
