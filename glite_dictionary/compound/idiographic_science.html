idiographic science

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪdioʊˈɡræfɪk ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈɡrafɪk ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
an approach to knowledge that emphasizes the study and understanding of unique, individual, and subjective phenomena, often through detailed case analysis rather than general laws
study focusing on unique, individual phenomena

Examples

Experience sampling methods are vital tools in modern idiographic science to understand individual personality processes.
Clinical psychology frequently employs idiographic science to explore unique patterns in patients’ experiences.
Unlike nomothetic approaches, idiographic science prioritizes in-depth study of single cases or events.

Synset Examples

Experience sampling methods are essential tools for building a modern idiographic approach to understanding personality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nomothetic science - an approach focused on discovering general laws or principles across cases

Etymology

From Greek idios (own, private) and -graphic (writing, describing); used in academic discourse since the late 19th century.
