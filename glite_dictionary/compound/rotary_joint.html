rotary joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a freely moving joint in which movement is restricted to rotation around a single axis, such as the joint between the radius and ulna in the forearm
joint allowing rotational movement
medical

Examples

The elbow contains a rotary joint that allows the arm to twist and turn objects.
In anatomy class, we studied how the radius and ulna form a rotary joint in the forearm.
Robotic arms often mimic the movement of a rotary joint to achieve greater flexibility.

Synset Examples

the articulation of the radius and ulna in the arm is a pivot joint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pivot joint - a joint that allows only rotary movement
