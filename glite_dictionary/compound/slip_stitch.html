slip stitch

1. NOUN

/ slɪp stɪtʃ / (AmE), / slɪp stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a loose stitch catching only one or two threads of fabric, typically designed to be invisible from the right side
invisible sewing or knitting stitch

Examples

She used a slip stitch to close the seam, making her handiwork almost invisible.
To finish the edge neatly, crochet a row of slip stitches along the border.
Mastering the slip stitch is crucial for achieving a professional look in sewing projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladder stitch - another sewing stitch used for closing seams invisibly
  • Antonym: backstitch - a strong stitch used in sewing seams
