merchant ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝː.tʃənt ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.tʃənt ʃɪp / (BrE)
a ship used for transporting cargo or commercial goods
ship for carrying cargo

Examples

The port was full of merchant ships unloading their goods from abroad.
During the war, several merchant ships were escorted by naval vessels for protection.
He worked as a radio operator on a merchant ship crossing the Atlantic.

Synset Examples

they did much of their overseas trade in foreign bottoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cargo ship - a ship designed to carry goods rather than passengers
  • Antonym: warship - a ship equipped for warfare

Etymology

From 'merchant', Middle English, from Old French 'marcheant', and 'ship', Old English 'scip'.
