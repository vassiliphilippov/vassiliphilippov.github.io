direct current

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈkɝːrənt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
An electric current that flows steadily in one direction only, as opposed to alternating current.
steady one-direction electric current

Examples

Batteries supply direct current to electronic devices.
Unlike alternating current, direct current flows in only one direction.
Most portable electronics run on direct current from their batteries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alternating current - an electric current that reverses direction periodically

Etymology

From 'direct' (straight) + 'current' (flow of electricity).
