catch phrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃ ˌfreɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatʃ ˌfreɪz / (BrE)
A well-known phrase or expression, often associated with a particular person, group, or media, that is frequently repeated and easily recognized.
a well-known, often repeated phrase or expression

Examples

The comedian became famous for his signature catch phrase on the TV show.
Every politician seems to have a catch phrase to sum up their campaign message.
That old movie is full of memorable catch phrases people still quote today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a short memorable phrase used in advertising or promotion
  • Synonym: catchword - a word or phrase that is popular at a particular time or in a particular context
