snowy egret

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊ.i ˈiː.ɡrət / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊ.i ˈiː.ɡrət / (BrE)
a small white heron (Egretta thula) of the Americas, with slender black legs and bright yellow feet, noted for its delicate plumes
small white heron native to the Americas

Examples

We watched a snowy egret gracefully hunt for fish along the riverbank.
A snowy egret stood out against the mudflats with its brilliant white feathers.
Many photographers try to capture the unique movements of the snowy egret during nesting season.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: egret - a group of herons with white plumage and ornamental feathers
  • Degree Higher: great egret - a larger white heron species (Ardea alba)

Etymology

From 'snowy' (referring to its white color) + 'egret', from Old French aigrette, a diminutive of 'aigron' meaning 'heron'.
