checkered adder

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkɚd ˈædɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəd ˈadə / (BrE)
A nonvenomous tan and brown king snake, often marked with an arrow-shaped spot on the back of its head and, in southeastern regions, red stripes resembling those of coral snakes.
a nonvenomous king snake with tan, brown, and sometimes red stripes

Examples

The checkered adder is commonly mistaken for the venomous coral snake due to its red stripes.
Researchers observed a checkered adder basking in the sun near the forest edge.
Unlike many snakes in the region, the checkered adder is harmless to humans.
