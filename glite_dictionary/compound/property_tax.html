property tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːpərti tæks / (AmE), / ˈprɒpəti tæks / (BrE)
A tax levied by a local government on real estate, based on the assessed value of the property.
tax on real estate value

Examples

The city increased the property tax to fund new schools and parks.
Homeowners received their annual property tax bill in the mail last week.
Rising property tax rates can make it more expensive to own a house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land tax - a tax specifically on land rather than buildings or improvements
  • Antonym: income tax - a tax on earnings from work or investments
