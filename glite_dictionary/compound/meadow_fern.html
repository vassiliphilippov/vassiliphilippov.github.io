meadow fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of fern native to northeastern North America, typically found in meadows and open woods.
fern species of northeastern North America

Examples

The meadow fern flourishes in the damp fields of the Adirondack region.
We spotted several meadow ferns growing along the hiking trail.
Botanists often study the meadow fern to learn more about native plant species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fern - a plant with feathery or leafy fronds and no flowers, reproducing by spores
