gamma ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmə ˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæmə ˌreɪ / (BrE)
A type of electromagnetic radiation, emitted during radioactive decay, with extremely short wavelength and high energy.
high-energy electromagnetic radiation

Examples

The scientist used a detector to measure gamma rays emitted from the radioactive sample.
Exposure to high levels of gamma rays can be harmful to living tissue.
Astronomers study bursts of gamma rays from distant galaxies to learn about the universe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: X-ray - a form of electromagnetic radiation with a longer wavelength than gamma rays

Etymology

From the Greek letter gamma (Γ), used to denote this type of radiation, combined with 'ray'.
