common dandelion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈdændəˌlaɪən / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈdændɪˌlaɪən / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant (Taraxacum officinale), widely naturalized as a weed in North America, with yellow flowers and deeply toothed leaves, often used in salads and wine-making.
a widespread yellow-flowered weed; edible plant

Examples

The common dandelion is often considered a weed but is edible and nutritious.
She picked a bunch of common dandelion leaves to add to her salad.
Many gardeners struggle to remove common dandelion from their lawns each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - a plant with yellow flowers and toothed leaves, Taraxacum officinale
