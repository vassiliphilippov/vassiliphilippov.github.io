velvet ant

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət ænt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt ænt / (BrE)
A solitary wasp of the family Mutillidae, covered in brightly colored, velvety hair, with wingless females.
solitary wasp with velvety hair; females wingless

Examples

The velvet ant is famous for its painful sting, earning it the nickname 'cow killer.'
Despite its name, the velvet ant is actually a type of wasp rather than a true ant.
We spotted a brightly colored velvet ant crawling across the path during our hike.

Etymology

Named for its velvety hair and ant-like appearance, though it is not a true ant.
