comb-footed spider

1. NOUN

/ koʊm ˈfʊtɪd ˈspaɪdər / (AmE), / kəʊm ˈfʊtɪd ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
a spider belonging to the family Theridiidae, distinguished by a row of comb-like bristles on each hind foot
a spider with comb-like bristles on hind feet

Examples

The comb-footed spider can often be found spinning irregular webs in dark corners.
Scientists identified the specimen as a comb-footed spider due to the bristles on its hind legs.
Some species of comb-footed spider are known for their ability to catch prey much larger than themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Theridiidae - the scientific family of comb-footed spiders
