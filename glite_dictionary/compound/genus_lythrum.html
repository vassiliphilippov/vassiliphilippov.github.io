genus Lythrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlaɪθrəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlɪθrəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous flowering plants in the loosestrife family, known for their pink or purple flowers.
a genus of flowering plants (loosestrife)

Examples

Gardeners often plant genus Lythrum species for their striking flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
Some wetlands are dominated by the invasive purple loosestrife, a member of the genus Lythrum.
The botanical characteristics of the genus Lythrum are important for accurate plant identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosestrife - a common name for plants of the Lythrum genus and related genera

Etymology

From Latin lythrum, meaning loosestrife.
