simple fracture

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
A fracture in which a bone is broken but does not pierce the skin or damage surrounding tissue extensively.
bone break not piercing skin
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed a simple fracture in her left arm.
He was relieved that his injury was only a simple fracture and not more serious.
Treating a simple fracture usually involves immobilizing the broken bone with a cast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compound fracture - a fracture in which the bone pierces the skin
