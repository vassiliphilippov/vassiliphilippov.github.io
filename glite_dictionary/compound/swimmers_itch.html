swimmer's itch

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪmərz ɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈswɪməz ɪtʃ / (BrE)
an itchy rash caused by an allergic reaction to certain microscopic parasites that burrow into the skin, typically after swimming in infested water
itchy rash from waterborne parasites
medical

Examples

After swimming in the lake, James developed a severe case of swimmer's itch on his legs.
Public health officials posted warnings to prevent swimmer's itch outbreaks at local beaches.
Children are especially susceptible to swimmer's itch when playing in shallow, warm water.
