ash heap

1. NOUN

/ ˈæʃ ˌhip / (AmE), / ˈæʃ ˌhiːp / (BrE)
a pile or mound of ashes, especially those remaining after something has been burned
a pile of ashes from burning

Examples

After the fire was put out, only an ash heap remained where the shed had stood.
The children played near the old ash heap behind the factory.
He tossed the burnt wood onto the growing ash heap in the corner of the yard.

Synset Examples

The workers cleared the ash-heap after the construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash pile - a pile of ashes, especially after burning material
