cold cream

1. NOUN

/ koʊld kriːm / (AmE), / kəʊld kriːm / (BrE)
a thick, soft cream used to clean and moisturize the skin, especially on the face
moisturizing cosmetic cream for skin

Examples

She used cold cream every night to remove her makeup before bed.
Applying cold cream helped to keep her skin soft and smooth during winter.
Many people prefer cold cream as a gentle cleanser for sensitive skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moisturizer - a substance used to prevent dryness in the skin
  • Synonym: lotion - a liquid preparation applied to the skin

Etymology

From the cooling sensation it produces on application, with 'cream' from Latin 'cremor' (thick juice or broth).

2. NOUN

/ koʊld kriːm / (AmE), / kəʊld kriːm / (BrE)
A cosmetic cream, typically used for softening and cleansing the skin.
cosmetic cream for skin cleansing/softening

Examples

She used cold cream every night to remove her makeup and keep her skin soft.
My grandmother swears by cold cream as part of her nightly beauty routine.
Many people find that cold cream helps prevent dryness during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moisturizer - a cosmetic used to keep skin moist
  • Synonym: lotion - liquid preparation for cosmetic or medicinal use

Etymology

From the French 'cérat de Galien', later known in English as 'cold cream' due to the cool feeling on the skin when applied.
