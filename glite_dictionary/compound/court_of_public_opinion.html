court of public opinion

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt əv ˈpʌblɪk əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / kɔːt əv ˈpʌblɪk əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
the judgement or perception of the general public regarding a person or issue, often outside of legal proceedings
the public's collective judgment

Examples

Public figures are often tried in the court of public opinion before any legal process begins.
The company's reputation suffered greatly after the court of public opinion turned against it.
Despite being acquitted in a court of law, she was still condemned in the court of public opinion.

Synset Examples

The scandal quickly became a matter of the court of public opinion.
Celebrities often face judgment in the court of public opinion.
He was found guilty in the court of public opinion before the trial.
