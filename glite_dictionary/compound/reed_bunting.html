reed bunting

1. NOUN

/ rid ˈbʌntɪŋ / (AmE), / riːd ˈbʌntɪŋ / (BrE)
a small European songbird of marshy areas, with a streaked brown back and a black head in the male
European songbird found in marshes

Examples

The reed bunting is often spotted among the reeds near the riverbanks in spring.
Male reed buntings can be recognized by their striking black heads during the breeding season.
Conservationists are working to restore habitats suitable for the endangered reed bunting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a group of small seed-eating birds with conical bills
