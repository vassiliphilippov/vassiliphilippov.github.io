apex of the sun's way

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪpɛks əv ðə sʌnz weɪ / (AmE), / ˈeɪpɛks əv ðə sʌnz weɪ / (BrE)
the point on the celestial sphere toward which the sun and the entire solar system appear to move relative to the fixed stars
astronomy: solar system's apparent direction of movement

Examples

Some astronomers calculate the apex of the sun's way to determine our galaxy's motion.
Studies of the apex of the sun's way help map the solar system's trajectory through space.
The direction known as the apex of the sun's way is important for understanding stellar movement.

Etymology

apex from Latin apex (summit, tip); see also 'way' referring to the sun's apparent path.
