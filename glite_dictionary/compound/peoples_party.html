People's Party

1. PROPN

/ ˈpipəlz ˌpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈpiːpəlz ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, founded in 1891 to promote currency expansion and state control of railroads; also known as the Populist Party
US political party (1891–1908), Populist Party

Examples

The People's Party advocated reforms to help farmers and industrial workers in the late 19th century.
In 1892, the People's Party nominated James B. Weaver for president of the United States.
The influence of the People's Party can still be seen in some modern economic policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Populist Party - another name for the People's Party, US political party (1891–1908)
