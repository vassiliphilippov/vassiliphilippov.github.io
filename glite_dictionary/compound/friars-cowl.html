friar's-cowl

1. NOUN

/ ˈfraɪərz ˌkaʊl / (AmE), / ˈfraɪəz ˌkaʊl / (BrE)
A tuberous perennial plant with a cowl-shaped maroon or violet-black spathe, native to the Mediterranean, Canary Islands, and Azores.
Mediterranean plant with cowl-shaped spathe

Examples

The unusual flowers of the friar's-cowl add a gothic charm to Mediterranean gardens.
Botanists have documented the friar's-cowl in the wilds of the Canary Islands.
With its dark, hooded spathe, the friar's-cowl is easily recognized among local flora.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its sheath-like flower (spathe) to a friar's cowl (hood).
