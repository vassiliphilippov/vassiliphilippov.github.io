deep-rooted papism

1. NOUN

/ ˌdipˈruːtɪd ˈpeɪpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdiːpˈruːtɪd ˈpeɪpɪzəm / (BrE)
a strong and firmly established belief in the authority of the Pope
firm belief in papal authority

Examples

Her family has a history of deep-rooted papism that shaped their religious traditions.
Despite the Reformation, some communities maintained deep-rooted papism for centuries.
His speeches often reveal a deep-rooted papism underlying his arguments for church authority.

Synset Examples

His views reflect a deep-rooted papism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papism - belief in or devotion to the Pope's authority

Etymology

From 'deep-rooted' (thoroughly established) + 'papism' (belief in or devotion to the Pope).
