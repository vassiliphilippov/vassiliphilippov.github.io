Wall Street

1. PROPN

/ wɔl strit / (AmE), / wɔːl striːt / (BrE)
A street in lower Manhattan, New York City, famous as the location of the New York Stock Exchange; often used to refer to the U.S. financial industry as a whole.
center of U.S. finance; NY stock exchange street

Examples

Many people blame Wall Street for the financial crisis of 2008.
She works for a major firm on Wall Street.
Legislation was passed to place more regulations on Wall Street after several scandals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - management of large amounts of money and investments

2. NOUN

/ wɔl strit / (AmE), / wɔːl striːt / (BrE)
used to refer to the financial markets and securities industry of the United States, or the people and companies involved in them, especially in New York City.
the US financial markets sector

Examples

Many investors keep a close eye on Wall Street to track the latest economic trends.
The government announced new regulations to prevent future excesses in Wall Street.
She found a challenging job with a major Wall Street investment firm after graduating from college.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Main Street - typical American small businesses and everyday investors, contrasted with Wall Street

Etymology

Named after a street in Lower Manhattan, New York City, which is home to the New York Stock Exchange and other financial institutions.

3. NOUN

/ wɔl strit / (AmE), / wɔːl striːt / (BrE)
A street in the financial district of lower Manhattan, New York City, known as the center of the American financial industry and used as a symbol for the financial markets and institutions of the United States.
center of American finance; street in New York City

Examples

Many banks and investment firms are headquartered on Wall Street.
The economic crisis led to widespread protests against Wall Street.
She works for a major Wall Street law firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - the management of large amounts of money

Etymology

Named after an actual wall built by Dutch colonists in the 17th century in New Amsterdam (now New York City).
