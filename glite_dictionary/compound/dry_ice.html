dry ice

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ aɪs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ aɪs / (BrE)
solid carbon dioxide, which sublimates at −78.5°C and is used mainly as a refrigerant
solid form of carbon dioxide

Examples

The shipment was packed with dry ice to keep the samples frozen.
Always wear gloves when handling dry ice to avoid frostbite.
As dry ice sublimates, it produces a mysterious fog effect for stage shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon dioxide - a colorless, odorless gas formed by respiration and combustion
