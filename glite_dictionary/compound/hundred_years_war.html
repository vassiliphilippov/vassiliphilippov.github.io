Hundred Years' War

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌndrəd jɪrz wɔr / (AmE), / ˈhʌndrəd jɪəz wɔː / (BrE)
a series of wars fought intermittently between England and France from 1337 to 1453, primarily over claims to the French throne
wars between England and France (1337–1453)

Examples

The Hundred Years' War significantly shaped the course of European history and national identity.
Joan of Arc emerged as a key figure during the later stages of the Hundred Years' War.
The Hundred Years' War began in 1337 and concluded in 1453, spanning more than a century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thirty Years' War - a European war fought primarily in Central Europe (1618–1648)
