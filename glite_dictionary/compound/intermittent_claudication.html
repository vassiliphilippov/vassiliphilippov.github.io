intermittent claudication

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmɪtənt ˌklɔːdəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪt.ənt ˌklɔː.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
pain and cramping in the leg muscles caused by inadequate blood supply, typically brought on by walking and relieved by rest
painful leg cramps from poor blood supply
medical

Examples

The patient developed intermittent claudication while walking to the store, which improved when he rested.
Symptoms of intermittent claudication are often an early warning of peripheral artery disease.
His doctor diagnosed the pain in his calf as intermittent claudication after a vascular examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claudication - limping or lameness, often from pain or obstruction of blood flow

Etymology

From Latin *claudicatio* (limping), with 'intermittent' reflecting the episodic nature of symptoms.
