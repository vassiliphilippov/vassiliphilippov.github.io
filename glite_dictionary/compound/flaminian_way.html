Flaminian Way

1. PROPN

/ fləˈmɪniən weɪ / (AmE), / fləˈmɪniən weɪ / (BrE)
an ancient Roman road in Italy, constructed in 220 BC by Gaius Flaminius, running north from Rome to Cisalpine Gaul
ancient Roman road from Rome northward

Examples

Tourists often walk along parts of the Flaminian Way to experience Roman history.
The Flaminian Way was vital for military and trade routes in ancient Italy.
Remnants of the Flaminian Way can still be found north of Rome today.

Etymology

Named after Gaius Flaminius, Roman consul who commissioned the road.
