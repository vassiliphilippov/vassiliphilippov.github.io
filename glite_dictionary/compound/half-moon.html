half-moon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæf ˌmun / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌmuːn / (BrE)
the crescent-shaped pale area at the base of a human fingernail, also called the lunula
crescent area at base of fingernail

Examples

She admired the pale half-moon at the base of her nails.
A healthy half-moon is a sign of good circulation.
He noticed the half-moon on his index finger looked smaller than usual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunula - the pale, crescent-shaped area at the base of a fingernail

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæf ˌmun / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌmuːn / (BrE)
the phase of the Moon when half its visible surface is illuminated, occurring at the first or last quarter
Moon phase with half its face illuminated

Examples

The half-moon hung low in the evening sky, casting a gentle glow over the landscape.
During a half-moon, it is often easier to observe lunar craters with a telescope.
We planned our night hike to coincide with the half-moon for better visibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarter moon - the phase of the moon when it appears as a half-circle
  • Degree Higher: full moon - the phase of the moon when it is fully illuminated
  • Degree Lower: new moon - the phase of the moon when it is not visible from Earth
