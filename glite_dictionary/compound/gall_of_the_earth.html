gall of the earth

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔl əv ðə ɝθ / (AmE), / ɡɔl əv ði ɜːθ / (BrE)
A perennial herb found in the southern and eastern United States, with drooping clusters of pinkish flowers and thick leaves shaped like a lion's foot, sometimes classified in the genus Prenanthes.
herbaceous plant with pink flowers, U.S. native

Examples

The field behind our house was dotted with gall of the earth blooming in early summer.
Botanists debated whether to classify gall of the earth under Prenanthes or a different genus.
I mistook the broad leaves of gall of the earth for a dandelion at first glance.
