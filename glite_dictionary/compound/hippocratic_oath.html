Hippocratic oath

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈkrætɪk oʊθ / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkrætɪk əʊθ / (BrE)
a solemn oath taken by doctors pledging to observe specific medical ethics and principles, historically attributed to Hippocrates
ethical oath taken by doctors
medical

Examples

Before beginning his residency, he took the Hippocratic oath at a formal ceremony.
The Hippocratic oath reminds physicians to prioritize patient welfare above all else.
Some modern medical schools have modified the Hippocratic oath to reflect contemporary values.

Etymology

Named after Hippocrates, an ancient Greek physician, considered the father of medicine.
