cupid's dart

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːpɪdz dɑrt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpɪdz dɑːt / (BrE)
a south European perennial plant (Catananche caerulea) with flat blue flowers and dark centers, often grown as an ornamental
south European blue-flowered plant

Examples

She planted cupid's dart in her garden for its striking blue flowers.
The delicate blossoms of cupid's dart bloom throughout the summer.
Many florists use cupid's dart in fresh arrangements because of its unique appearance.

Etymology

The name refers to the flower's association with Cupid, the Roman god of love, and its long, slender flower stalks resembling darts.
