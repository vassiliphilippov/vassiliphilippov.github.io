Boolean algebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːliən ælˌdʒɛbrə / (AmE), / ˈbuːliən ˈæl.dʒɪ.brə / (BrE)
A branch of algebra in which the values of variables are restricted to true and false, used in logic, set theory, and computing.
algebra of logical values; true/false logic

Examples

Boolean algebra is fundamental to the design of modern digital circuits.
In their computer science course, students learned how Boolean algebra can be applied to programming.
George Boole's work gave rise to Boolean algebra, which underpins much of today's computing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolic logic - Logic in which symbols represent propositions
  • Synonym: set theory - Branch of mathematics dealing with sets

Etymology

Named after George Boole (1815–1864), English mathematician and logician.
