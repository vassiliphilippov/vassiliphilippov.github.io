storm center

1. NOUN

/ stɔrm ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
the central area in a storm where the barometric pressure is lowest, often associated with the most severe weather conditions
central region of lowest pressure in a storm

Examples

The hurricane's storm center passed directly over the island, bringing intense winds and rain.
Meteorologists closely tracked the movement of the storm center to predict the path of the cyclone.
Heavy rainfall was recorded near the storm center, but conditions improved as the system moved away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye - calm, clear area at the center of a tropical cyclone

2. NOUN

/ stɔrm ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
the central point or focal area of trouble, controversy, or disturbance
center of trouble or disturbance

Examples

The disputed policy quickly became the storm center of public debate.
During the crisis, she found herself at the storm center of political controversy.
The scandal made the company headquarters the storm center of media attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epicenter - the central point of something, typically a difficult or unpleasant situation
  • Synonym: eye of the storm - the calm region at the center of a storm or metaphorically, the central, often quiet point of trouble

3. NOUN

/ stɔrm ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / stɔːm ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
the area of lowest atmospheric pressure at the core of a storm, or a designated location for coordinating storm response efforts
the core area of a storm or coordination center

Examples

Meteorologists issued warnings as the storm center moved closer to the coast.
Aid workers gathered at the storm center to organize relief efforts.
The path of the storm center was projected to pass over several major cities.

Synset Examples
