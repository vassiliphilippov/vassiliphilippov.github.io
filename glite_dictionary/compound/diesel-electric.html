diesel-electric

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiː.zəl ɪˈlɛk.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌdiː.zəl ɪˈlɛk.trɪk / (BrE)
a locomotive powered by electric motors that receive current produced by an onboard diesel engine
locomotive powered by diesel-generated electricity

Examples

The diesel-electric hauled heavy freight trains across the mountain pass.
Many modern passenger trains use a diesel-electric for reliable service.
Engineers praised the efficiency of the new diesel-electric designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diesel locomotive - locomotive powered directly or indirectly by a diesel engine
  • Antonym: electric locomotive - locomotive powered by electricity from an external source

Etymology

Diesel from Rudolf Diesel (inventor of the diesel engine) + electric.
