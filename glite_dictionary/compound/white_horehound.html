white horehound

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈhɔr.haʊnd / (AmE), / waɪt ˈhɔː.haʊnd / (BrE)
A European aromatic herb (Marrubium vulgare) with hairy leaves and clusters of small white flowers, whose leaves produce a bitter extract used medicinally and as flavoring.
European aromatic herb with white flowers

Examples

The herbalist recommended white horehound tea to help soothe my cough.
Fields of white horehound bloom in the summer, attracting bees to their tiny flowers.
Traditional candies are sometimes flavored with extracts from white horehound leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horehound - a group of aromatic herbs of the genus Marrubium

Etymology

Horehound from Old English 'hārhūne'; 'white' refers to the pale, downy appearance of the plant.
