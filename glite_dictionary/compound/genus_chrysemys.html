genus Chrysemys

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kraɪˈsiː.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kraɪˈsiː.mɪs / (BrE)
A genus of turtles, commonly known as painted turtles, found mainly in North America.
genus of painted turtles

Examples

The genus Chrysemys includes the widely distributed painted turtles of North America.
Researchers discovered a new fossil belonging to the genus Chrysemys in the ancient riverbed.
Young naturalists studied the habits of turtles in the genus Chrysemys for their project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysemys picta - a species of painted turtle within the genus Chrysemys

Etymology

From Greek 'chryseos' (golden) and 'emys' (freshwater turtle), taxonomically named.
