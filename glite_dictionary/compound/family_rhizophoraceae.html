family Rhizophoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɪzəˌfɔrəˌsiː / (AmE), / ˌrʌɪzəˌfɒrəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical trees and shrubs, often forming dense coastal jungles, especially mangroves
family of tropical coastal trees and shrubs (mangroves)

Examples

The family Rhizophoraceae includes many species essential for stabilizing tropical shorelines.
Researchers are studying the resilience of plants in the family Rhizophoraceae to climate change.
Many mangrove forests are dominated by members of the family Rhizophoraceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangrove - tropical coastal tree or shrub adapted to saline conditions

Etymology

From New Latin 'Rhizophoraceae', based on the type genus 'Rhizophora' (from Greek 'rhiza' meaning root and 'phora' meaning bearing).
