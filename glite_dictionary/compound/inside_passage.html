Inside Passage

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪn.saɪd ˈpæs.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd ˈpæs.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a long coastal waterway stretching from Seattle, Washington, to Skagway, Alaska, protected by islands and mainland, and known for marine travel and scenic views
protected coastal route in Alaska and British Columbia

Examples

Many cruise ships travel through the Inside Passage for its breathtaking scenery and wildlife.
The Inside Passage offers a sheltered route for boats along the coasts of British Columbia and Alaska.
During their journey, they explored several small towns along the Inside Passage.
