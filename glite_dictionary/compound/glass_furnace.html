glass furnace

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs ˈfɝːnɪs / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs ˈfɜːnɪs / (BrE)
a furnace designed for melting and processing large quantities of glass, especially in industrial glassmaking.
industrial furnace for melting glass

Examples

The glass furnace reached temperatures high enough to melt the raw materials into liquid glass.
Engineers monitored the glass furnace closely to ensure the consistency of the molten glass.
Shutting down a glass furnace is a complex process that can take several days.

Synset Examples

A typical glass furnace holds hundreds of tonnes of molten glass, and so it is simply not practical to shut it down every night, or in fact in any period short of a month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furnace - an enclosed structure for heating materials to high temperatures
