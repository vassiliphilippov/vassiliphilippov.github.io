cotton candy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑt̬n ˌkændi / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn ˌkændi / (BrE)
A light, fluffy candy made by spinning boiled sugar into thin threads, often served on a stick.
spun sugar candy
american

Examples

We bought some cotton candy at the fair and shared it between us.
The children were fascinated by the bright pink cotton candy spinning in the machine.
She hadn't tasted cotton candy since she was a child visiting amusement parks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candyfloss - British English term for spun sugar confection
