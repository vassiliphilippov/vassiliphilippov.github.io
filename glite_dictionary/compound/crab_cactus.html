crab cactus

1. NOUN

/ kræb ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / krab ˈkaktəs / (BrE)
A South American cactus with jointed stems, often grown as a houseplant for its typically red flowers; sometimes classified as genus Schlumbergera.
South American houseplant cactus with red flowers

Examples

The crab cactus is prized for its vibrant red flowers during the holiday season.
My grandmother keeps a large crab cactus in her living room window.
With proper care, a crab cactus can bloom spectacularly year after year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas cactus - A popular houseplant cactus, usually blooming in winter, also from genus Schlumbergera
