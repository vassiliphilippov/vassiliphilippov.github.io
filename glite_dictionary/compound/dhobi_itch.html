dhobi itch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊbi ˌɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊbi ˌɪtʃ / (BrE)
A fungal infection that affects moist areas of the body, especially the inner thighs and groin.
fungal infection of moist body parts
medical

Examples

Many athletes develop dhobi itch due to excessive sweating.
The doctor prescribed an antifungal cream to treat his dhobi itch.
Wearing loose clothing can help prevent dhobi itch in hot and humid climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinea cruris - medical term for fungal infection of the groin

Etymology

From Hindi 'dhobī' (washerman), referencing people frequently affected due to moist environments.
