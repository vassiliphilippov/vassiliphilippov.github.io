long pepper

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a slender tropical climbing plant (Piper longum) native to the eastern Himalayas, cultivated for its aromatic fruit used as a spice similar to black pepper
tropical plant used as a spice

Examples

Long pepper was once highly valued as a spice in ancient Mediterranean cultures.
The chef used ground long pepper to add a unique flavor to the dish.
You can sometimes find dried long pepper at specialty spice markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black pepper - common spice from Piper nigrum
  • Degree Higher: pepper - spicy fruit or vegetable used as seasoning

2. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a flowering vine (Piper longum) that produces long, slender, and very hot pepper fruits, used as a spice
spicy fruit of the Piper longum plant

Examples

The recipe called for a pinch of ground long pepper to add more heat.
Historically, long pepper was highly valued in ancient Roman cuisine.
He grew long pepper in his garden to experiment with exotic spices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black pepper - a different spice from Piper nigrum
