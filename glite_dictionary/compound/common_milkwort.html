common milkwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmɪlkˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmɪlkˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a small European perennial plant (Polygala vulgaris) with many branches bearing racemes of blue, pink, or white flowers, once believed to promote lactation
European perennial plant with colorful racemes

Examples

The common milkwort is often found in meadows and grassy slopes across Europe.
She identified the small blue flower as common milkwort during her botany fieldwork.
Historically, common milkwort was used in folk medicine to encourage milk production in nursing mothers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkwort - any plant of the genus Polygala
