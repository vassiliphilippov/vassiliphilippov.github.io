yellow hawkweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈhɔːkˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈhɔːkwiːd / (BrE)
A European species of hawkweed with yellow flowers, introduced into northeastern United States, where it is considered a troublesome weed in some areas.
a species of hawkweed with yellow flowers

Examples

The field was covered in yellow hawkweed during the late summer months.
Gardeners worry that yellow hawkweed may take over native plant areas if left unchecked.
Efforts to control yellow hawkweed populations have increased in some northeastern states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkweed - any of numerous plants of the genus Hieracium, often with yellow or orange flowers
