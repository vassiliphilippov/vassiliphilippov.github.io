string bass

1. NOUN

/ strɪŋ beɪs / (AmE), / strɪŋ beɪs / (BrE)
the largest and lowest-pitched instrument of the violin family, also known as the double bass, typically used in orchestras and jazz bands
large, low-pitched violin-family instrument

Examples

He played the string bass in the school orchestra.
The jazz trio featured a piano, drums, and a string bass.
She bought a vintage string bass for her music collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double bass - another term for string bass; large, low-pitched string instrument
