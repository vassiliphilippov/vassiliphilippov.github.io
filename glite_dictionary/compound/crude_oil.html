crude oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːd ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkruːd ɔɪl / (BrE)
A naturally occurring, unrefined petroleum product composed mainly of hydrocarbons, extracted from the ground and used as a raw material in the production of fuels and chemicals.
unrefined petroleum as extracted from the ground

Examples

The price of crude oil rose sharply after the hurricane disrupted supply lines.
Large reserves of crude oil have been discovered off the coast of Brazil.
Before it can be used as fuel, crude oil must be refined to remove impurities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petroleum - liquid mixture of hydrocarbons, synonymous with crude oil
  • Antonym: refined oil - petroleum that has been processed to remove impurities
