phylum Bryozoa

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm braɪˈoʊzoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪləm braɪˈəʊzəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A group of aquatic invertebrate animals, known as bryozoans, that live in colonies; found in marine and freshwater habitats.
a group of colonial aquatic invertebrates

Examples

The phylum Bryozoa includes species that build intricate colonies resembling underwater gardens.
Researchers found new fossil evidence of phylum Bryozoa in the ancient reef deposits.
Students studied the feeding mechanisms unique to phylum Bryozoa in their marine biology class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bryozoan - an individual animal belonging to the phylum Bryozoa
  • Antonym: phylum Cnidaria - a different phylum of aquatic invertebrates such as jellyfish and coral

Etymology

From Greek 'bryon' (moss) and 'zoon' (animal), referring to their moss-like appearance.
