chrome yellow

1. NOUN

/ kroʊm ˈjɛloʊ / (AmE), / krəʊm ˈjɛləʊ / (BrE)
a bright yellow pigment consisting chiefly of normal lead chromate, often mixed with other lead compounds
a yellow pigment made from lead chromate

Examples

The artist chose chrome yellow for the sunflowers to achieve a bright, vibrant look.
Early school buses in the United States were painted chrome yellow for visibility.
Restorers used chrome yellow to match the original pigment on the historic building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead chromate - a yellow chemical compound used as a pigment
  • Degree Lower: cadmium yellow - a yellow pigment made from cadmium sulfide

Etymology

From 'chrome', referring to chromium, and 'yellow', its color; named after its chemical composition.
