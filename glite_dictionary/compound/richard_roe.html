Richard Roe

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd roʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd rəʊ / (BrE)
a name used in legal proceedings to refer to an unknown or fictitious male party, typically the defendant
fictitious male name in legal cases
legal

Examples

The court document listed Richard Roe as the defendant since the real identity was still unknown.
During the hearing, Richard Roe was named pending the actual party's identification.
In many legal cases, Richard Roe is used alongside John Doe for unidentified individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Doe - fictitious name for an unknown or anonymous person, especially male, used in legal proceedings
  • Antonym: Jane Doe - fictitious name for an unknown or anonymous female person in legal proceedings
