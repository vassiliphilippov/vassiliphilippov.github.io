southern spadefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərn ˈspeɪdˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈspeɪdfʊt / (BrE)
a species of spadefoot toad (Scaphiopus couchii) found in the southwestern United States
a toad species native to southwestern US

Examples

The southern spadefoot is well adapted to life in arid regions of the southwestern United States.
During the rainy season, southern spadefoots emerge from underground to breed in temporary pools.
A southern spadefoot can be identified by the spade-shaped projections on its hind feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefoot - a toad with a spade-like projection on each hind foot used for digging
