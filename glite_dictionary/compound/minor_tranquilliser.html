minor tranquilliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ trænˈkwɪlʌɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə trænˈkwɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a type of drug used to relieve anxiety and reduce tension or irritability, typically less potent than major tranquillisers
anxiety-relieving drug; mild tranquilliser
medical british

Examples

The doctor prescribed a minor tranquilliser to help manage her anxiety before surgery.
He became dependent on a minor tranquilliser after months of stressful work shifts.
Although effective, prolonged use of minor tranquilliser medications can sometimes lead to addiction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major tranquilliser - a stronger tranquilliser used mainly to treat psychosis
