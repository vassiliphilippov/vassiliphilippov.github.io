B-scan ultrasonography

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiː skæn ˌʌltrəˌsɑnəˈɡræfi / (AmE), / ˈbiː skæn ˌʌltrəˌsɒnəˈɡræfi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique using ultrasound to view and assess structures at the back of the eye
ultrasound imaging of the eye's back
medical

Examples

The doctor used B-scan ultrasonography to detect a retinal detachment in the patient's left eye.
B-scan ultrasonography is especially useful when cataracts prevent a clear view of the retina.
After the trauma, B-scan ultrasonography revealed swelling in the posterior segment of the eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultrasonography - imaging with high-frequency sound waves

Etymology

B-scan refers to 'brightness scan', from ultrasound imaging terminology; 'ultrasonography' from Latin 'ultra' (beyond) and Greek 'sonos' (sound) + '-graphy' (recording).
