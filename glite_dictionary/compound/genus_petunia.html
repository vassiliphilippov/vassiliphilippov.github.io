genus Petunia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pəˈtjuː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pɪˈtjuː.ni.ə / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial herbs and shrubs native to tropical South America, widely cultivated for their colorful and fragrant flowers.
genus of flowering plants from South America

Examples

The genus Petunia includes many species prized by horticulturists for their vibrant blooms.
Researchers are studying the genus Petunia to understand floral scent evolution.
Several hybrids within the genus Petunia are popular ornamental plants around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petunia - colorful flowering plant often grown in gardens

Etymology

‘Petunia’ from French, from obsolete Portuguese ‘petum’, from Tupi ‘petyn’ (tobacco); the genus is related to tobacco plants.
