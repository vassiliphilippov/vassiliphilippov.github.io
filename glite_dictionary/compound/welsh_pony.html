Welsh pony

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
a breed of small, hardy ponies originally from Wales, known for their intelligence and good temperament
breed of small Welsh pony

Examples

The Welsh pony is a favorite among children for riding lessons owing to its gentle nature.
Her family has bred Welsh ponies on their farm for generations.
At the county fair, a striking Welsh pony won first prize in the show ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pony - a small horse, especially one of a small breed
