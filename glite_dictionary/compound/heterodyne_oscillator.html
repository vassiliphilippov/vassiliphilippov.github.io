heterodyne oscillator

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛtərəˌdaɪn ˌɑsɪˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərədaɪn ˌɒsɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic oscillator that produces a signal which, when combined with an incoming radio signal, creates sum and difference frequencies (heterodynes)
electronic oscillator producing heterodyne signals

Examples

A heterodyne oscillator is an essential component in a superheterodyne radio receiver.
The engineer adjusted the heterodyne oscillator to improve the radio's frequency response.
By tuning the heterodyne oscillator, the technician could select different radio channels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillator - a device for generating oscillating signals, especially electrical current

Etymology

From Greek heteros (other, different) + dynamis (power) and oscillator, referring to the effect of mixing signals.
