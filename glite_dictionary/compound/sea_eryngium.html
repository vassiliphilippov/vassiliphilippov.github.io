sea eryngium

1. NOUN

/ si ɛˈrɪndʒiəm / (AmE), / si ɪˈrɪndʒɪəm / (BrE)
A European evergreen plant of the genus Eryngium, with twisted spiny leaves, formerly used for its roots as an aphrodisiac.
European evergreen plant with spiny leaves

Examples

The sea eryngium grows wild along many coastal sand dunes.
In the past, the roots of sea eryngium were thought to have medicinal properties.
Botanists studied the unique adaptation of sea eryngium to salty environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea holly - another common name for Eryngium maritimum, a coastal plant with spiny leaves

Etymology

From 'sea' (referring to its coastal habitat) and 'Eryngium', the genus name.
