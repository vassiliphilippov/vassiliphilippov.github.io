cliff swallow

1. NOUN

/ klɪf ˈswɑloʊ / (AmE), / klɪf ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
A North American swallow that nests in colonies and builds bottle-shaped mud nests on cliffs and walls.
North American swallow building mud nests on cliffs

Examples

A large colony of cliff swallows returned to the canyon every spring.
We watched a cliff swallow gather mud for its nest along the riverbank.
The cliff swallow is easily recognized by its square tail and pale forehead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small migratory songbird with pointed wings and long tail
