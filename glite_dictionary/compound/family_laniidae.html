family Laniidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ləˈnaɪɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ləˈnaɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds known as shrikes, characterized by their hooked beaks and predatory habits.
family of shrike birds

Examples

The family Laniidae includes several species of shrikes found across Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Researchers studied the hunting behaviors of birds in the family Laniidae.
The hooked bill is a distinctive feature of the family Laniidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a bird of the family Laniidae, known for impaling prey

Etymology

From Latin 'Lanius' (butcher), the type genus, plus the taxonomic suffix '-idae' for families.
