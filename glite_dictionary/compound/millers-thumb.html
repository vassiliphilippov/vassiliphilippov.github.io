miller's-thumb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlərz ˌθʌm / (AmE), / ˈmɪləz ˌθʌm / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish (sculpin) found in European and North American streams, with a broad, flattened head
small freshwater fish with broad head

Examples

The miller's-thumb can often be found hiding among stones in fast-flowing streams.
Anglers sometimes catch a miller's-thumb when fishing for trout.
The distinctive head of the miller's-thumb makes it easy to identify in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

Named for the supposed resemblance between the fish's broad, flat head and the thumb of a miller, flattened by work at the mill.
