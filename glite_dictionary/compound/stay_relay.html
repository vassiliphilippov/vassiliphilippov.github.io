stay relay

1. NOUN

/ steɪ rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / steɪ rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
an electrical relay that keeps its contact position indefinitely without power applied to its coil
relay holding position without power

Examples

The stay relay holds its position even when the power supply is removed.
In the circuit, a stay relay is used to maintain the state of the device after activation.
Technicians prefer stay relays in low-power applications to reduce energy consumption.

Synset Examples

A latching relay allows remote control of building lighting without the hum that may be produced from a continuously (AC) energized coil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latching relay - a relay that remains in its last state without continuous power
