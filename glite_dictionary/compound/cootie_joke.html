cootie joke

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuː.ti ˌdʒoʊk / (AmE), / ˈkuː.ti ˌdʒəʊk / (BrE)
a childish joke or remark that involves imaginary germs or dirtiness, often used among children
childish joke about imaginary germs

Examples

Even in fifth grade, some kids still told cootie jokes at recess.
He tried to make everyone laugh with another silly cootie joke.
The teacher asked the class to stop with the cootie jokes and focus on their work.

Synset Examples

He was too old for cootie jokes, but still laughed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cootie - imaginary germ thought to be spread by contact, especially among children

Etymology

"Cootie" is a North American slang term for an imaginary germ, possibly from World War I slang.
