phase III

1. NOUN

/ feɪz ˌθriː / (AmE), / feɪz ˌθriː / (BrE)
The third stage of clinical trials for a treatment or drug, involving large-scale testing to confirm its effectiveness and monitor side effects before FDA approval.
final large-scale clinical trial stage
medical

Examples

After passing phase III, the new medication was submitted for FDA approval.
The cancer treatment showed promising results during its phase III trials.
Several drugs failed in phase III due to unexpected side effects.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phase I - first stage in clinical trials; initial testing of a new drug in humans
  • Degree Lower: phase II - second stage of clinical trials, testing effectiveness and safety in larger groups
  • Degree Higher: phase IV - post-marketing studies after drug approval

Etymology

From 'phase' meaning a stage in a process plus Roman numeral III indicating the third stage.
