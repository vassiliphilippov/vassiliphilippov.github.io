Jordan almond

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔrdən ˈɑlmənd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdən ˈɑːlmənd / (BrE)
an almond candy coated with a hard, often colored and flavored layer of sugar
sugar-coated almond confection

Examples

Guests were given small bags of Jordan almonds as wedding favors.
She placed a bowl of colorful Jordan almonds on the dessert table.
The Jordan almond is a popular treat during Easter celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragée - a sugar-coated nut or candy, similar confectionery

Etymology

Possibly from the French 'jardin' (“garden”), referring to a cultivated almond, or connected with the Jordan River region.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔrdn ˈɑlmənd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdən ˈɑːlmənd / (BrE)
a large, smooth almond, often sugar-coated and used as a confectionery, especially at weddings and celebrations
sugar-coated almond confection

Examples

At the wedding, each guest received five Jordan almonds in a delicate organza bag.
She bought a box of pastel-colored Jordan almonds for the Easter celebration.
The tradition of giving Jordan almonds at celebrations symbolizes health, wealth, happiness, and long life.

Etymology

From French 'amande de la Jordaine', possibly named after the Levant (region around the Jordan River) where such almonds were believed to originate.
