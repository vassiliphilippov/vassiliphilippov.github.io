three-legged mare

1. NOUN

/ ˈθriˌlɛɡɪd mɛr / (AmE), / ˈθriːˌlɛɡɪd mɛə / (BrE)
A horse with three legs, often used metaphorically to refer to something that is handicapped, imperfect, or difficult.
a horse with three legs; used metaphorically

Examples

Despite the odds, he chose to ride the three-legged mare to prove his determination.
Some saw the project as a three-legged mare, unlikely to ever succeed.
Even with a three-legged mare, she managed to reach her destination.

Synset Examples

She advised him to ride the three-legged mare and keep going.
