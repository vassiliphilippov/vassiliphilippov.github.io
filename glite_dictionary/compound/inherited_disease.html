inherited disease

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease or disorder that is passed from parents to offspring through genes
genetically transmitted disease
medical

Examples

Cystic fibrosis is an inherited disease caused by a faulty gene.
Doctors can use genetic tests to identify the risk of inherited diseases.
Tay-Sachs is a rare inherited disease that primarily affects children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic disorder - a disorder caused by abnormalities in genes or chromosomes
