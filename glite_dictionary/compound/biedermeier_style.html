Biedermeier style

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːdərˌmaɪ.ər staɪl / (AmE), / ˈbiːdəˌmaɪə staɪl / (BrE)
A style of art, furniture, and interior design popular among the European middle class, especially in Germany and Austria, during the early 19th century, representing a transition between Neoclassicism and Romanticism.
early 19th-century European artistic style

Examples

The living room was decorated in the Biedermeier style, featuring elegant yet simple furniture.
Museum visitors admired the paintings and furnishings typical of the Biedermeier style.
Her collection of antiques included several chairs crafted in the Biedermeier style from early 19th-century Austria.

Synset Examples

In general, the Biedermeier style offered visual evidence of the conflict of ideas between Classicism and Romanticism that continued during the first half of the 19th century.

Etymology

German, from 'Biedermeier', the name of a fictional, conventional middle-class citizen.
