absolute threshold

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səˌlut ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.luːt ˈθrɛʃ.həʊld / (BrE)
the smallest level of stimulus intensity that can be detected by a person or organism
minimum detectable level of stimulation

Examples

The absolute threshold for tasting sugar varies from one person to another.
A faint sound below the absolute threshold will not be heard by the human ear.
Researchers measure the absolute threshold for detecting light in controlled laboratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshold - the point or level at which something begins or changes
