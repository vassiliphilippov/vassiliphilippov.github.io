sea god

1. NOUN

/ si ɡɑd / (AmE), / siː ɡɒd / (BrE)
a deity believed to personify or govern the sea, often depicted in mythology as controlling the waters
mythological deity of the sea

Examples

In Greek mythology, Poseidon was revered as a powerful sea god who could cause storms and earthquakes.
She read a legend about a sea god who protected fishermen from harm during their voyages.
Ancient sailors would offer sacrifices to the sea god before embarking on long journeys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poseidon - Greek god of the sea
  • Synonym: Neptune - Roman god of the sea
  • Synonym: sea goddess - female deity of the sea
