copper sulphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpər ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒpə ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a bright blue crystalline chemical compound, used chiefly as a fungicide and in electroplating, obtained by the action of sulfuric acid on copper oxide
a blue chemical compound used as a fungicide

Examples

The laboratory technician dissolved the copper sulphate crystals in water.
Gardeners use copper sulphate to control fungal diseases in crops.
Accidentally ingesting copper sulphate can be highly toxic to humans and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper sulfate - alternative spelling of copper sulphate (chiefly US)
