plaster bandage

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstər ˈbændɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə ˈbændɪdʒ / (BrE)
A bandage made of gauze and plaster of Paris, used to form a rigid covering that immobilizes broken bones during healing.
rigid bandage used to immobilize broken bones
medical

Examples

The doctor applied a plaster bandage to immobilize the patient's broken arm.
After his leg was set, a plaster bandage was wrapped carefully around it.
Old-fashioned plaster bandages are now often replaced by lighter synthetic materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast - a hard protective shell for immobilizing broken bones
