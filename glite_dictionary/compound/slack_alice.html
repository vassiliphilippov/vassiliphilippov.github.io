slack Alice

1. NOUN

/ slæk ˈælɪs / (AmE), / slæk ˈælɪs / (BrE)
a person who is habitually lazy, careless, or irresponsible, often especially about promptness or completing tasks
lazy or irresponsible person (colloquial)
informal british

Examples

Don’t be a slack Alice or you might lose your job.
My friends call me slack Alice because I’m always late to everything.
After several warnings, the manager fired the slack Alice of the team.

Synset Examples

She joked about being a slack Alice in her studies.
Don’t be a slack Alice; get your work done on time.
He's known as slack Alice for always missing deadlines.
