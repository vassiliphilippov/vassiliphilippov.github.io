to lobotomize creativity

1. VERB

/ loʊˈbɑtəˌmaɪz kriˈeɪt̬ɪvɪti / (AmE), / ləˈbɒtəmaɪz kriːˈeɪtɪvɪti / (BrE)
to severely diminish or stifle one's creative abilities, often by imposing restrictions or through disempowering experiences
suppress creative abilities

Examples

Some managers unintentionally lobotomize creativity by micromanaging every detail of their team's work.
Rigid corporate cultures can lobotomize creativity in talented employees.
She was determined not to let negativity lobotomize creativity in her art.

Synset Examples

I refuse to let this experience lobotomize my creativity.
He feared that the strict guidelines would lobotomize the project.
Don't let the rules lobotomize your original ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stifle creativity - to restrain or suppress creativity
  • Antonym: encourage creativity - to support or foster creativity

Etymology

Lobotomize from Greek 'lobos' (lobe) and 'tomia' (cutting), originally referencing a medical procedure; here used metaphorically with 'creativity'.
