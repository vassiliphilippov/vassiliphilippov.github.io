choroidal artery

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɔɪ.dl ˈɑːr.tɚ.i / (AmE), / kəˈrɔɪ.dl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the choroid plexus in the brain
artery supplying the choroid plexus
medical

Examples

During the surgery, care was taken not to damage the choroidal artery.
The choroidal artery plays a crucial role in supplying nutrients to brain tissues.
Blockage of a choroidal artery can result in neurological complications.

Etymology

From 'choroid', relating to the choroid plexus, and 'artery', from Greek 'artēria'.
