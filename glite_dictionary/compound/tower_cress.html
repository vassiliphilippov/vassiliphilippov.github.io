tower cress

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊ.ɚ krɛs / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə kɹɛs / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant with small white flowers, also known as Arabis, that grows upright and is widely distributed in Europe.
an erect Eurasian plant with white flowers

Examples

The wild tower cress was blooming at the edge of the forest.
Gardeners often cultivate tower cress for its delicate white flowers.
Hikers in Europe frequently spot tower cress growing along country trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabis - genus of flowering plants including tower cress

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊ.ɚ krɛs / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə krɛs / (BrE)
a European plant of the cabbage family with stiff, upright stems, sometimes classified in the genus Turritis
a European plant with stiff upright stems

Examples

We found a patch of tower cress growing beside the old stone wall.
The tall, slender stems of tower cress make it easy to recognize in the wild.
Botanists sometimes refer to tower cress as Turritis glabra, highlighting its stiff, upright growth.

Related Terms
