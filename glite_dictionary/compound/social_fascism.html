social fascism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl ˈfæʃɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈfæʃɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an ideology or practice combining elements of socialism with authoritarian or repressive social control, or used pejoratively to describe left-wing policies seen as restrictive or undemocratic
authoritarian socialism or repressive social control

Examples

Some political opponents accused the ruling party of social fascism due to its restrictive policies.
The newspaper's editorial warned against creeping social fascism in the regulation of public opinion.
She argued that mandatory surveillance measures amounted to a soft form of social fascism in society.

Synset Examples

The movement was criticized for its tendencies toward social fascism.
Some believe that censorship reflects a form of social fascism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascism - an authoritarian and nationalistic right-wing system of government and social organization
  • Synonym: authoritarianism - the enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • Synonym: socialism - a political and economic theory of social organization that advocates collective ownership and control

Etymology

Formed from 'social' and 'fascism'; used by communists in the early 20th century to describe authoritarian socialist movements.
