Votyak language

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtiˌæk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɒtjak ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Votyak people, also known as Udmurt
Finno-Ugric language of the Votyak/Udmurt people

Examples

She studied the Votyak language as part of her research into Finno-Ugric cultures.
The Votyak language has unique phonetic characteristics that distinguish it from neighboring languages.
Efforts are being made to preserve and revitalize the Votyak language among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Udmurt - the more common name for the people and their language

Etymology

Votyak is an older Russian exonym for the Udmurt people; language belongs to the Finno-Ugric subgroup.
