analytic geometry

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæl.ɪ.tɪk dʒiˈɑː.mə.tri / (AmE), / əˈnæl.ɪ.tɪk dʒiˈɒm.ə.tri / (BrE)
The branch of mathematics that uses algebraic techniques, particularly in coordinate systems, to study geometric shapes and properties.
the use of algebra to study geometry

Examples

In analytic geometry, equations can describe circles, parabolas, and other shapes precisely.
He preferred analytic geometry to classical Euclidean proofs because of its algebraic methods.
The foundations of analytic geometry were laid by René Descartes in the 17th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate geometry - geometry using coordinates; synonym for analytic geometry

Etymology

From analysis (mathematical method) and geometry; popularized in the 17th century by René Descartes.
