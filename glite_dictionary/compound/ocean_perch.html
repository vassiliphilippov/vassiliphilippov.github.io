ocean perch

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊʃən pɜːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a North Atlantic rockfish with reddish body and considered as a food fish
a North Atlantic rockfish

Examples

Fishermen caught a dozen ocean perch on their trip yesterday.
Grilled ocean perch is a popular dish in coastal restaurants.
The ocean perch thrives in the cold waters of the North Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockfish - a general term for fish with firm, spiny fins, including the ocean perch

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊʃən pɝtʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a large edible fish of the North Atlantic, found off the coasts of America and Europe, valued for its firm white flesh
large edible North Atlantic fish

Examples

Fresh ocean perch is often served grilled or baked in seafood restaurants along the coast.
The fisherman proudly displayed his catch of ocean perch on the dock.
Many chefs recommend ocean perch for its mild flavor and firm texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosefish - another name for the ocean perch, especially Sebastes marinus
  • Synonym: redfish - a common alternative name for ocean perch, especially Sebastes marinus
