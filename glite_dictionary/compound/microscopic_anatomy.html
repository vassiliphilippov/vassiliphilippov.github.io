microscopic anatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈskɑpɪk əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəˈskɒpɪk əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
The branch of anatomy that studies the microscopic structure of tissues and organs, typically using a microscope.
study of tissues and organs at the microscopic level
medical

Examples

Students in medical school often take a course in microscopic anatomy to learn about tissue structure.
Advancements in technology have greatly enhanced the study of microscopic anatomy.
The laboratory is equipped for both gross and microscopic anatomy research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: histology - the study of the microscopic structure of tissues

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'skopein' (to look) and 'anatomē' (dissection).
