inside information

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪd ˌɪn.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd ˌɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
confidential information that is not available to the general public, often giving an advantage
confidential, non-public information

Examples

The journalist had inside information about the merger before it was announced.
He was accused of trading stocks using inside information.
She gave us inside information on the company's future plans.

Synset Examples

after the trial he gave us the real details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - information kept hidden from others
  • Antonym: public knowledge - information known or available to everyone
