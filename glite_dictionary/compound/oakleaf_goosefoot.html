oakleaf goosefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkˌliːf ˈɡuːsfʊt / (AmE), / ˈəʊkliːf ˈɡuːsfʊt / (BrE)
An annual European plant (Chenopodium glaucum) with spikes of greenish flowers and leaves that are white and hairy on the underside; common as a weed in North America.
European plant with hairy-leaved spikes

Examples

The field was overrun with oakleaf goosefoot after the heavy rains.
Gardeners often mistake oakleaf goosefoot for other types of weeds in their flower beds.
Many birds are attracted to the seeds of oakleaf goosefoot in late summer.
