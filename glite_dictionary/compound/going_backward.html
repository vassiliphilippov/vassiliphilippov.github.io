going backward

1. X

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ ˈbækˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ ˈbækwəd / (BrE)
Returning to a previous state, condition, or way of doing things, often seen as a reversal of progress.
reverting to a previous state or condition

Examples

It felt like the company was going backward instead of making progress.
If we remove these changes, we’d just be going backward.
Her recovery has stalled, and now she seems to be going backward in her health.

Synset Examples

Going backward won't solve our problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regress - move backward or revert to an earlier state
  • Antonym: advance - move forward or progress
