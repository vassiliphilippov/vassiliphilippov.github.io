manifest destiny

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪfɛst ˈdɛstəni / (AmE), / ˌmænɪfɛst ˈdɛstɪni / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine in 19th-century America that the expansion of the United States across the continent was both justified and inevitable, often considered to be sanctioned by God
19th-century American expansionist doctrine

Examples

Many Americans in the 1800s believed in manifest destiny and supported westward expansion.
The idea of manifest destiny was used to justify the displacement of Native Americans.
Historians debate the moral consequences of manifest destiny in American history.

Etymology

Coined in 1845 by journalist John L. O'Sullivan; from 'manifest' (clear, obvious) and 'destiny.'
