drive line

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪv ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdraɪv ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a mechanism that transmits power from the engine to the driving wheels of a motor vehicle
mechanism transmitting engine power to wheels

Examples

The mechanic said the drive line needed repairs after the accident.
Modern electric vehicles have a much simpler drive line than traditional cars.
A faulty drive line can cause vibrations and noise while driving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drivetrain - the group of components that deliver power to the driving wheels
