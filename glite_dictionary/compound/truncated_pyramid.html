truncated pyramid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌŋˌkeɪtɪd ˈpɪrəˌmɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋ.keɪ.tɪd ˈpɪrə.mɪd / (BrE)
A solid figure formed by cutting a pyramid with a plane parallel to its base and removing the top portion, resulting in a frustum.
frustum of a pyramid; geometric solid

Examples

The monument was designed with a truncated pyramid as its base.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a truncated pyramid.
The sculpture resembled a truncated pyramid standing on a square platform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frustum - the portion of a solid (normally a cone or pyramid) that lies between two parallel planes cutting it

Etymology

From Latin truncare (to cut off) + Greek pyramis (pyramid).
