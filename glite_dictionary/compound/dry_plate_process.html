dry plate process

1. NOUN

/ draɪ pleɪt ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / draɪ pleɪt ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a photographic method, now historical, using a glass plate coated with a light-sensitive gelatin emulsion that is exposed and developed to produce an image
historical photographic method with glass plates

Examples

Photographers in the late 19th century often relied on the dry plate process to capture images quickly and efficiently.
The advent of the dry plate process revolutionized photography by eliminating the need to coat plates immediately before exposure.
He studied the dry plate process as part of his research into early photographic techniques.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wet plate process - an earlier photographic method using a wet collodion-coated glass plate
