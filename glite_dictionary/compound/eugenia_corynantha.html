Eugenia corynantha

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdʒiːniə kɔːrɪˈnænθə / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːniə ˌkɒrɪˈnænθə / (BrE)
An Australian tree of the myrtle family that bears sour red fruit.
Australian tree with sour red fruit

Examples

The Eugenia corynantha is known for its distinctively sour red fruits.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of Eugenia corynantha in Australian rainforests.
Many birds feed on the fruit of the Eugenia corynantha tree during the summer months.

Etymology

From the genus name Eugenia, named after Prince Eugene of Savoy, and the species epithet from Greek 'koryne' (club) and 'anthos' (flower).
