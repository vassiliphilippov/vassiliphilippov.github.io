Sun King

1. NOUN

/ sʌn kɪŋ / (AmE), / sʌn kɪŋ / (BrE)
a title for Louis XIV of France (1638–1715), whose long reign was distinguished by expansion of French power and the splendor of his court at Versailles
Louis XIV of France; French monarch 1638–1715

Examples

Louis XIV, known as the Sun King, centralized power in France like no other European monarch.
Historians often cite the Sun King as a symbol of absolute monarchy.
The Sun King's lavish parties at Versailles became legendary throughout Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Louis XIV - King of France from 1643 to 1715, known as the Sun King

Etymology

From the French 'le Roi Soleil,' an epithet used to describe Louis XIV's symbolic association with the sun
