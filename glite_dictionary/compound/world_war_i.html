World War I

1. PROPN

/ wɜrld wɔr wʌn / (AmE), / wɜːld wɔː wʌn / (BrE)
the war fought from 1914 to 1918 between the Allies and the Central Powers, involving most of the world's great powers at the time
the First World War, 1914–18

Examples

Many historians consider World War I a turning point in modern history.
The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand was the immediate cause of World War I.
My great-grandfather fought in the trenches during World War I.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: World War II - the global war fought 1939–45, following World War I
  • Synonym: the Great War - another name for World War I, especially before World War II occurred

Etymology

Named as the first global conflict of its kind; 'war' from Old English 'wearr', 'world' from Old English 'weorold'.
