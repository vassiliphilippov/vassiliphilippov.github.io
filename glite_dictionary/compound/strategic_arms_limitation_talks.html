Strategic Arms Limitation Talks

1. PROPN

/ strəˈtiːdʒɪk ɑrmz ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən tɔks / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk ɑːmz ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən tɔːks / (BrE)
A series of negotiations between the United States and the Soviet Union, beginning in 1969, aimed at limiting the two countries' nuclear weapon stockpiles.
US–Soviet nuclear arms negotiations (1969–1979)

Examples

The first Strategic Arms Limitation Talks led to a significant reduction in nuclear weapons deployment.
Historians debate the effectiveness of the Strategic Arms Limitation Talks during the Cold War era.
Delegates from both nations met in Helsinki for the Strategic Arms Limitation Talks in 1969.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SALT - Acronym for Strategic Arms Limitation Talks, referring to the same set of negotiations
