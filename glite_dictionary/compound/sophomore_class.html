sophomore class

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑfˌmoʊr klæs / (AmE), / ˈsɒf.mɔː klɑːs / (BrE)
the group of students in the second year of a four-year college or high school
second-year group in US school or college
american

Examples

The sophomore class is organizing a charity fundraiser this semester.
Enrollment in the sophomore class increased substantially compared to last year.
Many students feel more confident by the time they reach the sophomore class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: freshman class - the group of first-year students in a four-year college or high school
  • Degree Higher: junior class - the group of third-year students in a four-year college or high school
  • Degree Higher: senior class - the group of fourth-year students in a four-year college or high school

Etymology

From 'sophomore' (second-year student), which is from Greek 'sophos' (wise) + 'moros' (foolish), referring to a person in their second year; 'class' from Latin 'classis' (a division).
