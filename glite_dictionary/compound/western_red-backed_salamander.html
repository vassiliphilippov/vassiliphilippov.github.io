western red-backed salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˈsæləˌmændər / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
a small salamander native to the Pacific coast of North America, characterized by its reddish dorsal stripe
a salamander species of the Pacific coast

Examples

The western red-backed salamander is commonly found in moist forested areas along the Pacific coast.
Scientists studied the western red-backed salamander to understand its unique adaptations to its environment.
Children often spot a western red-backed salamander hiding under rocks and fallen logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - an amphibian with a slender body and tail
