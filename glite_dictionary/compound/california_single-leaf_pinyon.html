California single-leaf pinyon

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈfɔrnjə ˈsɪŋɡəl liːf ˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈfɔːniə ˈsɪŋɡl̩ liːf ˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
a small pine tree native to California, closely related to the Rocky Mountain pinyon but with single needles per fascicle
a small pine tree native to California

Examples

The California single-leaf pinyon is native to the dry mountains of eastern California.
Scientists are studying the drought resistance of the California single-leaf pinyon.
The cone of the California single-leaf pinyon contains edible seeds prized by local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rocky Mountain pinyon - a similar species of pinyon pine with two needles per fascicle
