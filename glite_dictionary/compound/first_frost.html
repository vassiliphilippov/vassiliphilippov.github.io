first frost

1. NOUN

/ fɝst frɑst / (AmE), / fɜːst frɒst / (BrE)
the first occurrence of frost in a particular season, marking the onset of cold weather
season's initial occurrence of frost

Examples

Gardeners often check the weather forecast in October for the first frost.
Many plants wither after the first frost of the year.
The farmers hurried to harvest their crops before the first frost arrived.

Synset Examples

The first frost arrived, covering everything in white.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frost - a deposit of small white ice crystals formed on the ground or other surfaces when the temperature falls below freezing
