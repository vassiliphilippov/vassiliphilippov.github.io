French letter

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
an old-fashioned euphemism for a condom; a thin sheath used during sexual intercourse as a contraceptive or to prevent disease
old euphemism for condom
archaic

Examples

He found an unopened French letter in the pocket of an old coat.
In Victorian times, people would sometimes use the term French letter instead of condom.
The teacher explained that the term French letter is considered outdated and rarely used today.

Synset Examples

The kids giggled when they found a French letter in the attic.
He sent her a French letter to express his feelings.
She received a French letter during the summer vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condom - a sheath-shaped contraceptive device

Etymology

Refers to the association of 'French' with things considered risqué in English (1800s); 'letter' used as a euphemism.
