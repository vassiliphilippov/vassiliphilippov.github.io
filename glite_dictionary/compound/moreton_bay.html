Moreton Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɔrtn̩ beɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtən beɪ / (BrE)
a large bay on the eastern coast of Australia, an inlet of the Tasman Sea, lying to the east of Brisbane
bay east of Brisbane, Australia

Examples

Many tourists enjoy swimming and sailing in Moreton Bay during the summer months.
The islands in Moreton Bay are home to diverse wildlife and stunning natural scenery.
Fishing in Moreton Bay has been a tradition for local communities for generations.

Etymology

Named by Captain James Cook in 1770 after James Douglas, 14th Earl of Morton.
