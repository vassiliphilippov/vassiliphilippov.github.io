buck sergeant

1. NOUN

/ bʌk ˈsɑrdʒənt / (AmE), / bʌk ˈsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
a sergeant of the lowest rank in the military, especially in the U.S. Army or Marine Corps
lowest-ranking sergeant (military)
informal american

Examples

He was promoted from buck sergeant to staff sergeant within a year.
During basic training, the buck sergeant was tough but fair with new recruits.
My uncle served as a buck sergeant in the army during the Vietnam War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sergeant - a non-commissioned military officer of middle rank

Etymology

The term 'buck' is an American slang modifier reinforcing the basic rank; 'sergeant' is from Middle French 'sergent'.
