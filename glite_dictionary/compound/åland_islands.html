Åland Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑlɑnd ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈɔːlænd ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
an archipelago of approximately 6,000 islands in the Gulf of Bothnia, under Finnish control, situated between Sweden and Finland
archipelago under Finnish control in the Baltic Sea

Examples

The Åland Islands have their own flag and a degree of political autonomy within Finland.
Ferries between Sweden and Finland often stop at the Åland Islands for passengers to disembark.
Many tourists visit the Åland Islands in summer for cycling and sailing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baltic Sea - a sea of Northern Europe, bordered by countries including Finland and Sweden
  • Synonym: Finland - the country that governs the Åland Islands

Etymology

From Swedish 'Åland', possibly from Old Norse 'Ahvaland' (land of water).
