family Hemerobiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli hɛməˌroʊbiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli hɛməˌrəʊbɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Neuroptera, commonly known as brown lacewings.
family of brown lacewings (insects)

Examples

The family Hemerobiidae includes many species of brown lacewings found in forests worldwide.
Researchers studied the family Hemerobiidae to understand their role in controlling pest populations.
Members of the family Hemerobiidae are distinguished by their finely veined wings and soft bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacewing - insect with delicate, lace-like wings, family Chrysopidae or Hemerobiidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin, named after the genus Hemerobius.
