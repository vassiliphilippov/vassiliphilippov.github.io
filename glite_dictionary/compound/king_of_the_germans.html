King of the Germans

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ əv ðə ˈdʒɜrmənz / (AmE), / kɪŋ əv ðə ˈdʒɜːmənz / (BrE)
the sovereign ruler of the German people, especially used as a royal title in medieval and early modern Europe before the establishment of the Holy Roman Emperor.
historical royal title for the German sovereign

Examples

The title of King of the Germans was adopted by rulers before their coronation as Holy Roman Emperor.
Frederick II was elected as King of the Germans in 1212 by the German princes.
The King of the Germans held considerable influence over the affairs of Central Europe during the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Holy Roman Emperor - the sovereign ruler of the Holy Roman Empire

Etymology

From Middle English king + of + the + Germans, describing the ruler of the German kingdoms in the medieval Holy Roman Empire.
