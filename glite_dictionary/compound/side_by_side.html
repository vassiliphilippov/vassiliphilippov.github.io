side by side

1. ADJ

/ saɪd baɪ saɪd / (AmE), / saɪd baɪ saɪd / (BrE)
closely related or associated with each other; existing together or in proximity.
closely associated; existing together

Examples

The two companies worked side by side on the ambitious project.
For many years, the different cultures existed side by side in the same city.
Modern technology and ancient traditions often continue side by side in Japan.

Synset Examples

a city in which communism and democracy had to live side by side

Related Terms

  • Synonym: together - in or into one place, group, or mass; in company
  • Antonym: apart - separated by distance or time, not together

2. ADJ

/ saɪd baɪ saɪd / (AmE), / saɪd baɪ saɪd / (BrE)
next to each other, with no space in between; immediately adjoining or touching
next to each other, adjoining

Examples

The two houses were built side by side on the same street.
We walked side by side along the riverside path, enjoying the view.
The statues stood side by side in the gallery, attracting everyone's attention.
