order Scorpionida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər skɔrˌpiːˈoʊnɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə skɔːˌpɪˈɒnɪdə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order within the class Arachnida, comprising the true scorpions.
order of true scorpions (taxonomy)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the order Scorpionida in the deserts of North Africa.
The order Scorpionida is distinguished by its members' segmented tails and venomous stingers.
Taxonomists debated the classification of certain fossil specimens as part of the order Scorpionida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorpion - arachnid with pincers and a poisonous sting

Etymology

From Latin 'scorpio' (scorpion) and Neo-Latin taxonomic suffix '-ida'.
