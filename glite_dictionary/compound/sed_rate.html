sed rate

1. NOUN

/ sɛd ˌreɪt / (AmE), / sɛd ˌreɪt / (BrE)
the rate at which red blood cells settle in a sample of blood, measured under standardized conditions; often used to detect inflammation in the body
rate at which blood cells settle; test for inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a sed rate test to check for inflammation.
A high sed rate can indicate an underlying health condition.
Her sed rate was elevated, suggesting an infection or autoimmune disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythrocyte sedimentation rate - medical term for the rate at which red blood cells settle; another name for sed rate

Etymology

Shortened form of 'sedimentation rate'.

2. NOUN

/ sɛd reɪt / (AmE), / sɛd reɪt / (BrE)
A blood test measuring how quickly red blood cells settle at the bottom of a test tube, used to detect inflammation in the body.
blood test for inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor checked my sed rate to look for signs of inflammation.
An elevated sed rate often indicates the presence of an inflammatory condition.
She was relieved when her sed rate returned to normal after treatment.

Synset Examples

Monitoring the sed rate can help diagnose autoimmune diseases.
The doctor ordered a sed rate test to check for inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythrocyte sedimentation rate - the full name for sed rate; the rate at which red blood cells settle in a period of one hour

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'sedimentation rate'.
