panoramic sight

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænəˈræmɪk saɪt / (AmE), / ˌpænəˈræmɪk saɪt / (BrE)
a type of telescopic gunsight for artillery that can be rotated horizontally in a full circle to allow precise aiming in any direction
gunsight for artillery that rotates 360 degrees

Examples

The artillery crew adjusted the panoramic sight to align with the target coordinates.
Modern howitzers are often equipped with a panoramic sight for greater flexibility in aiming.
After cleaning the panoramic sight, the team prepared for another round of firing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunsight - an optical device for aiming firearms or artillery

Etymology

From 'panoramic' (relating to a wide view) and 'sight' (device for aiming), referring to the full-circle rotation capability.
