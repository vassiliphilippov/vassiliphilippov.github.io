magnetic declination

1. NOUN

/ məɡˈnɛtɪk ˌdɛkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the angle between magnetic north and true north at a particular location, used in navigation and map reading
angle between magnetic north and true north

Examples

The magnetic declination at this location is about 5 degrees east of true north.
Navigators must correct for magnetic declination when charting their course.
Changes in magnetic declination over time can affect compass readings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic variation - the difference between magnetic north and true north; another term for magnetic declination
  • Degree Higher: magnetic inclination - the angle made by the Earth's magnetic field with the horizontal plane at a given location

Etymology

From 'magnetic' (relating to magnetism) and 'declination' from Latin 'declinatio' (a bending away).
