rat kangaroo

1. NOUN

/ ræt kæŋˈɡuːroʊ / (AmE), / ræt kæŋˈɡuːrəʊ / (BrE)
Any of several small, rabbit-sized, ratlike marsupials native to Australia, belonging to the family Potoroidae.
small Australian marsupial resembling a rat or rabbit

Examples

The rat kangaroo is most active at night, foraging for roots and fungi.
Scientists are studying the habitat loss of the eastern rat kangaroo in Australia.
Unlike larger kangaroos, the rat kangaroo moves in quick, short hops through the underbrush.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kangaroo - a large plant-eating Australian marsupial
  • Synonym: potoroo - a small Australian marsupial of the genus Potorous
