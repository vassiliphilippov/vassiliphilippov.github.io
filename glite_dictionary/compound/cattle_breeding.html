cattle breeding

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætl ˌbriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkætl ˌbriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of producing and raising cattle by selective mating and management
the breeding and raising of cattle

Examples

Modern farms rely on advanced techniques for cattle breeding to improve milk production.
The university offers specialized courses in cattle breeding for aspiring livestock managers.
Selective cattle breeding has led to the development of more productive and disease-resistant herds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal breeding - the practice of selectively mating animals to produce desired traits
