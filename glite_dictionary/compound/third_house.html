third house

1. NOUN

/ θɝd haʊs / (AmE), / θɜːd haʊs / (BrE)
(US, politics) An interest group that seeks to influence legislators or government officials, especially through lobbying.
lobbying interest group
american

Examples

Many critics believe that the third house holds too much power over political decisions.
The bill was heavily pushed by members of the third house representing large corporations.
Some lawmakers argue that the third house threatens democratic accountability by operating behind the scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobby - to try to influence legislators or officials on a particular issue
  • Synonym: pressure group - organized group seeking to influence public policy

Etymology

Refers to an unofficial 'third' legislative body, beyond the official two houses, made up of lobbyists.
