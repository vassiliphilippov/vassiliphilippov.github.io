white hope

1. NOUN

/ waɪt hoʊp / (AmE), / waɪt həʊp / (BrE)
a person or thing considered likely to bring success to a particular field or activity
person or thing expected to succeed

Examples

The new player is considered the white hope of the struggling football team.
Many investors see this technology as the white hope for renewable energy.
The ambitious young lawyer became the firm's white hope after winning several high-profile cases.

Synset Examples

this company is the great white hope of the nuclear industry's waste management policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great hope - a person or thing expected to achieve great success
  • Synonym: dark horse - a candidate or competitor about whom little is known but who unexpectedly achieves success

Etymology

Originally used in early 20th-century boxing to denote a white boxer expected to defeat Black champions; now used more generally.
