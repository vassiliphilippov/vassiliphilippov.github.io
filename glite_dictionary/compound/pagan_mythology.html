pagan mythology

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡən mɪˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡən mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the body of myths, legends, and traditional stories belonging to ancient pagan religions, especially those before the advent of Christianity
myths and legends of pagan religions

Examples

Many ancient rituals are rooted in pagan mythology and have survived through cultural traditions.
The architecture of the temple was inspired by symbols from pagan mythology.
Scholars often compare Norse and Greek pagan mythology to understand common mythic themes.

Synset Examples

The novel explored themes of pagan mythology and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mythology - traditional stories belonging to a culture

Etymology

From Latin 'paganus' (country-dweller, heathen) and 'mythologia' (mythology).
