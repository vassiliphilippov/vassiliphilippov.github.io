radical cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈrædɪkəl sɛl / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl sɛl / (BrE)
a small, secretive group of extremists or terrorists who act independently as part of a larger network
small group of extremists or terrorists

Examples

The government cracked down on a suspected radical cell operating in the region.
Intelligence agencies monitor communication between members of a radical cell to prevent potential attacks.
It is difficult to infiltrate a radical cell due to their tight security and secrecy.

Synset Examples

to insure operational security the members of adjacent terrorist cells usually don't know each other or the identity of their leadership

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorist cell - a small group of terrorists operating together
