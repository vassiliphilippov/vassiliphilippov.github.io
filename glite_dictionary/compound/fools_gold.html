fool's gold

1. NOUN

/ fulz ɡoʊld / (AmE), / fuːlz ɡəʊld / (BrE)
a pale yellow mineral, iron pyrite, that is often mistaken for gold
iron pyrite; mineral resembling gold

Examples

Many miners were disappointed to discover that the shiny rocks were only fool's gold.
He mistook the glittering mineral for real gold, but it was just fool's gold.
Fool's gold can easily deceive people who are not familiar with minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrite - a mineral consisting of iron disulfide, often called fool's gold

2. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlz ˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfuːlz ˌɡəʊld / (BrE)
A mineral that looks like gold but is actually worthless, especially iron pyrite; also, anything that appears valuable but is actually worthless.
a mineral or thing falsely appearing valuable

Examples

Many inexperienced miners were disappointed to find only fool's gold in their pans.
The promise of quick riches from the lottery is often just fool's gold.
She mistook admiration for love, not realizing it was only fool's gold.

Synset Examples

Don't be fooled by appearances; that's just fool's gold.
That shiny rock turned out to be fool's gold.
Investing in that scheme was like chasing fool's gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrite - a yellow mineral also known as 'fool's gold'
  • Antonym: gold - precious yellow metal used as money and ornament

Etymology

From the deceptive resemblance of iron pyrite to real gold, fooling gold seekers.
