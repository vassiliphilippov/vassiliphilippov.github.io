temporal adverbial

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpɚəl ædˈvɝbiəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ədˈvɜːbɪəl / (BrE)
an adverbial phrase or word that indicates when an action occurs
adverbial indicating time

Examples

In the sentence 'She left yesterday,' the word yesterday is a temporal adverbial.
Teachers often ask students to identify the temporal adverbial in a sentence.
Adding a temporal adverbial helps clarify when the event takes place.

Synset Examples

An adverb phrase that answers the question ‘when?’ is called a temporal adverbial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverbial - a word or phrase acting as an adverb

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of time) and 'adverbialis' (relating to an adverb).
