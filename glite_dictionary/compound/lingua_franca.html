lingua franca

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪŋɡwə ˈfræŋkə / (AmE), / ˌlɪŋɡwə ˈfraŋkə / (BrE)
A language used as a common means of communication between people whose native languages are different.
bridge language between groups

Examples

English has become the lingua franca of international business and science.
Swahili serves as a lingua franca in East Africa, connecting speakers of different native languages.
In the 17th century, French was the lingua franca of European diplomacy.

Synset Examples

Koine is a dialect of ancient Greek that was the lingua franca of the empire of Alexander the Great and was widely spoken throughout the eastern Mediterranean area in Roman times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pidgin - A simplified language that develops as a means of communication between speakers of different native languages
  • Synonym: creole - A stable, natural language resulting from the mixing of parent languages

Etymology

From Italian 'lingua franca' (Frankish tongue), originally referring to a trade pidgin used in the Mediterranean.
