Bachelor of Theology

1. NOUN

/ ˌbætʃələr əv θiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌbætʃələ əv θɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A first university degree awarded for studies in theology.
undergraduate theology degree

Examples

After four years of study, she earned her Bachelor of Theology from the seminary.
A Bachelor of Theology is often required for those planning to become ordained ministers.
He decided to pursue a Bachelor of Theology to deepen his understanding of religious texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bachelor of Arts - a bachelor's degree awarded for an arts or humanities subject
  • Degree Higher: Master of Theology - a postgraduate degree in theology
