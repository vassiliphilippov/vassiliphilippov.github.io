to work for a living

1. VERB

/ wɜrk fəɹ ə lɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / wɜːk fə ə lɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
to earn money by having a job or employment; to support oneself financially through regular work
earn a living through employment

Examples

Many people must work for a living to provide for their families and meet their needs.
She didn't inherit wealth; she had to work for a living just like everyone else.
After retiring, he joked that he no longer had to work for a living every day.

Synset Examples

In our office, we say, don't call me sir, I work for a living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a living - to earn money to support oneself
  • Synonym: to earn a living - to gain enough money for basic needs by working
