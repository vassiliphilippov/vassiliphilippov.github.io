Stamp Act

1. PROPN

/ stæmp ækt / (AmE), / stæmp ækt / (BrE)
a law passed by the British Parliament in 1756 imposing a tax in the form of an official stamp on newspapers and legal or commercial documents in the American colonies, leading to widespread protest and its eventual repeal in 1766
a 1756 British tax law affecting American colonies

Examples

The Stamp Act of 1756 sparked intense opposition among the American colonists.
Widespread protests against the Stamp Act contributed to the revolutionary movement in the colonies.
The repeal of the Stamp Act marked a significant victory for colonial resistance.
