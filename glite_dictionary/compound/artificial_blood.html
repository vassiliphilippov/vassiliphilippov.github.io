artificial blood

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl blʌd / (AmE), / ɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl blʌd / (BrE)
a liquid designed to transport oxygen in the body, used as a temporary substitute for human blood
synthetic liquid used as a blood substitute
medical

Examples

Researchers hope that artificial blood could one day help save trauma patients in emergencies.
The hospital participated in a clinical trial testing the new artificial blood product.
Because of the shortage of donors, scientists are developing artificial blood as an alternative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood substitute - a substance used to replace blood for transfusions
