monkey bridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a high and narrow platform, typically situated above a ship's deck or within an engine or boiler room, used for observation or access
high narrow platform on ship or in engine room

Examples

The engineer inspected the engines from the monkey bridge above the boiler room.
Crew members used the monkey bridge to observe the deck during docking procedures.
Safety rails were installed along the monkey bridge to prevent accidents while crossing it.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small, often open deck above the pilot house on a ship, serving as the highest navigational bridge
the highest navigational deck on a ship

Examples

The captain stood on the monkey bridge to get a clear view of the harbor.
During storms, only experienced officers were allowed on the monkey bridge.
From the monkey bridge, the crew scanned the horizon for approaching vessels.
