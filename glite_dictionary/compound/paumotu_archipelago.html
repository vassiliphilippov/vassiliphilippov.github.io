Paumotu Archipelago

1. PROPN

/ ˌpaʊˈmoʊtu ɑːrˈkɪpəˌleɪgoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpaʊˈməʊtuː ˌɑːkɪˈpeləɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group of about 80 coral islands in French Polynesia, also known as the Tuamotu Archipelago
coral island group in French Polynesia

Examples

The Paumotu Archipelago is known for its stunning coral reefs and clear turquoise waters.
Many divers travel to the Paumotu Archipelago to experience its abundant marine life.
Researchers are studying climate change effects on the Paumotu Archipelago and its delicate ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tuamotu Archipelago - another name for the Paumotu Archipelago

Etymology

From Paumotu, an indigenous name for the islands, also called Tuamotu; 'archipelago' from Italian 'arcipelago'.
