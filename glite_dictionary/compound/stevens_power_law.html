Stevens' power law

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːvənz ˈpaʊ.ɚ lɔ / (AmE), / ˈstiːvənz ˈpaʊ.ə lɔː / (BrE)
a principle in psychophysics stating that the perceived intensity of a stimulus grows as a power function of the actual stimulus intensity
principle on perceived stimulus intensity

Examples

Stevens' experiments supported Stevens' power law rather than the older logarithmic model.
In research on pain perception, Stevens' power law often provides a better fit than alternative models.
The psychology student explained Stevens' power law during her presentation on sensory experiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Weber-Fechner law - related law in psychophysics about sensation and stimulus

Etymology

Named after American psychologist S. S. Stevens (1906–1973).
