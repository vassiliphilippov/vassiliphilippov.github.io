aurora australis

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈɹoʊ.ɹə ɔˈstreɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɔː.rə ɔːˈstreɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
An aurora occurring in the Southern Hemisphere, also known as the southern lights, where the sky is illuminated by natural light displays near the South Pole.
southern hemisphere natural light display

Examples

Scientists travel to Antarctica to study the aurora australis and gather data on the Earth's magnetosphere.
Tourists hope for clear nights to witness the spectacular aurora australis over the southern polar skies.
Photographers captured incredible images of the aurora australis lighting up the horizon in Tasmania.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aurora borealis - the aurora of the Northern Hemisphere (northern lights)

Etymology

From Latin 'aurora' (dawn) and 'australis' (southern).
