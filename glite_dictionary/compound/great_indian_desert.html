Great Indian Desert

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈɪndiən ˈdezərt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈɪndiən ˈdezət / (BrE)
a large desert region located east of the Indus River in northwestern India and southeastern Pakistan, also known as the Thar Desert
desert region in India and Pakistan

Examples

The Great Indian Desert receives very little rainfall each year, making it one of the driest regions in India.
Several unique animal species have adapted to the harsh conditions of the Great Indian Desert.
The colorful festivals of Rajasthan often reflect the vibrant culture of the Great Indian Desert region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thar Desert - another name for the Great Indian Desert
  • Degree Higher: Sahara Desert - a major desert in North Africa
