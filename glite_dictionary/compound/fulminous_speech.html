fulminous speech

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlmɪnəs spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfʊlmɪnəs spiːtʃ / (BrE)
a speech or style of writing that is intense, powerful, and captivating to the audience or readers
intense, powerful speech or writing

Examples

The politician delivered a fulminous speech that left the audience in awe.
Her fulminous speech at the rally inspired many people to take action.
Critics praised the author's fulminous speech for its intensity and rhetoric.

Synset Examples

He gave a fulminous speech that energized the audience.
The writer's fulminous style captivated her readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassioned speech - speech delivered with intense emotion and passion

Etymology

From Latin 'fulminare' (to strike with lightning), via 'fulminous' meaning 'like lightning'.
