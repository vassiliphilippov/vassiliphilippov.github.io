hairy willowherb

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛri ˈwɪloʊˌhɜrb / (AmE), / ˈheəri ˈwɪləʊhɜːb / (BrE)
a plant of Europe and Asia with purplish-red flowers and hairy stems and leaves; introduced into North America
plant with purplish-red flowers and hairy stems

Examples

The hairy willowherb thrives in moist soils alongside rivers and streams.
We found a patch of hairy willowherb blooming in the meadow during late summer.
Gardeners often remove hairy willowherb because it can spread quickly and become invasive.
