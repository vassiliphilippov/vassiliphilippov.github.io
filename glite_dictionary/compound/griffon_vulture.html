griffon vulture

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪf.ən ˌvʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪf.ən ˌvʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
a large vulture found in southern Europe and northern Africa, with pale body plumage and black wings
large pale vulture of southern Europe and northern Africa

Examples

A flock of griffon vultures soared over the rocky cliffs in search of carrion.
The conservation area provides a habitat for the endangered griffon vulture.
We spotted several griffon vultures circling in the warm afternoon air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulture - a large bird of prey feeding chiefly on carrion

Etymology

From French 'griffon', referring to a legendary creature; used to describe the bird’s appearance.
