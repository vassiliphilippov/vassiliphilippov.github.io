flag of truce

1. NOUN

/ flæɡ əv truːs / (AmE), / flæɡ əv truːs / (BrE)
A white flag displayed to indicate a request for a ceasefire, surrender, or to negotiate during a conflict.
white flag signaling truce or surrender

Examples

The soldiers approached the enemy lines, carrying a flag of truce to signal their desire to negotiate.
Under the protection of a flag of truce, the diplomats crossed the battlefield safely.
Raising a flag of truce, the general hoped to end the fighting without further bloodshed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white flag - flag signaling surrender or truce, universally recognized
