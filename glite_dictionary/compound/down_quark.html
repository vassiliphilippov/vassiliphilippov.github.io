down quark

1. NOUN

/ daʊn kwɑrk / (AmE), / daʊn kwɑːk / (BrE)
a stable subatomic particle of the quark family, with an electric charge of -1/3 and a mass about 607 times that of an electron
a type of quark (subatomic particle)

Examples

The proton is composed of two up quarks and one down quark.
A neutron consists of one up quark and two down quarks.
Physicists study the role of the down quark in nuclear reactions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: up quark - a quark with an electric charge of +2/3, partner of the down quark
  • Synonym: quark - a fundamental constituent of matter in physics
