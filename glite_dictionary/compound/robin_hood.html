Robin Hood

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɑːbɪn ˈhʊd / (AmE), / ˌrɒbɪn ˈhʊd / (BrE)
a legendary English outlaw of the 12th century, famous for robbing the rich to help the poor
legendary English outlaw and folk hero

Examples

Many people admire Robin Hood for his sense of justice and his efforts to help the poor.
The tales of Robin Hood have inspired numerous books, films, and television shows.
Some people refer to activists who fight inequality as modern-day Robin Hoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outlaw - person who has broken the law and lives outside its protection
  • Synonym: folk hero - a person admired for brave or noble acts in traditional stories

Etymology

From Middle English Robin (a diminutive of Robert) and Hood (referring to his typical headwear); the legendary figure appeared in English ballads from the 13th century.
