stearic acid

1. NOUN

/ stiˈɛrɪk ˈæsɪd, ˈstɪrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / stɪəˈrɪk ˈæsɪd, stiˈærɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a saturated fatty acid that is waxy and solid at room temperature, found widely in animal and vegetable fats
a saturated fatty acid in fats and oils

Examples

Candle manufacturers often use stearic acid to harden their products.
Stearic acid is commonly derived from animal fats for use in cosmetics and soaps.
Laboratory analysis revealed the presence of stearic acid in the food sample.

Etymology

From Greek 'stear' (fat, tallow) + '-ic', relating to acids.
