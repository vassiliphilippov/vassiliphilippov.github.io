genus Buckleya

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈbʌkliə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈbʌkliə / (BrE)
A small genus of parasitic shrubs native to Asia and America, belonging to the family Santalaceae.
a genus of parasitic shrubs

Examples

The genus Buckleya consists of parasitic shrubs found in both Asia and North America.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Buckleya in the mountains of China.
Members of the genus Buckleya attach their roots to those of other plants to obtain nutrients.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Botsford Buckley, American botanist.
