xanthoma disseminatum

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθoʊmə ˌdɪsəˌmɪnəˈdætəm / (AmE), / zanˈθəʊmə ˌdɪsəˌmɪnəˈdætəm / (BrE)
A rare, chronic disorder in adults marked by the development of orange or brownish papules on many areas of the body.
rare chronic disorder with widespread papules
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed the patient with xanthoma disseminatum after observing numerous yellowish papules on their skin.
Treatment options for xanthoma disseminatum are limited due to its rarity and chronic nature.
Physicians often find xanthoma disseminatum challenging to treat because it affects multiple surfaces of the body.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + -oma (tumor or growth); disseminatum from Latin 'disseminatus' meaning scattered.
