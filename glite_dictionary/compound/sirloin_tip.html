sirloin tip

1. NOUN

/ sɝːˈlɔɪn tɪp / (AmE), / sɜːˈlɔɪn tɪp / (BrE)
a cut of beef taken from the upper end of the sirloin, known for being lean and slightly less tender than other sirloin cuts
lean beef cut from upper sirloin

Examples

The chef prepared the sirloin tip roast with herbs and garlic for a special dinner.
At the butcher's, I asked for two pounds of sirloin tip for stir-fry.
Although sirloin tip is leaner, it can be very flavorful when cooked properly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sirloin - a cut of beef from the back, between the ribs and the rump

Etymology

From 'sirloin', from Middle French 'sur longe' (above the loin) + 'tip' (end or point).
