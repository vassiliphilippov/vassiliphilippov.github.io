spark of interest

1. NOUN

/ spɑrk əv ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / spɑːk əv ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
a feeling or moment of initial curiosity or enthusiasm about something
initial curiosity or enthusiasm

Examples

The teacher's enthusiastic explanation created a spark of interest in the students.
Her new hobby began with just a spark of interest but soon grew into a passion.
Reading about space exploration gave him a spark of interest in science.

Synset Examples

Their conversation ignited a spark of interest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: interest - a feeling of wanting to know or learn about something
