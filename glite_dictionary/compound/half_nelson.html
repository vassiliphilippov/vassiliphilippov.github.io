half nelson

1. NOUN

/ hæf ˈnɛlsən / (AmE), / hɑːf ˈnɛlsən / (BrE)
a wrestling hold in which one arm is passed under the opponent's arm and pressure is applied to the back of the neck
wrestling hold with one arm under opponent's arm

Examples

The coach demonstrated a half nelson to show how quickly you can pin an opponent.
She escaped from his half nelson and reversed the position.
During the match, he used a half nelson to gain the upper hand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: full nelson - a wrestling hold where both arms are passed under the opponent's and hands are joined behind the neck

Etymology

From the surname Nelson, possibly after Horatio Nelson, used for several wrestling holds; 'half' indicates use of one arm.
