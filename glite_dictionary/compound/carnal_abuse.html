carnal abuse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrnəl əˈbjus / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnəl əˈbjuːs / (BrE)
(law) Sexual intercourse with a person who has not reached the age of consent, even if the minor participates willingly.
sexual intercourse with someone below age of consent
legal

Examples

The court found him guilty of carnal abuse of a minor.
New laws were introduced to address the issue of carnal abuse.
The term carnal abuse appears frequently in legal documents concerning child protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statutory rape - sexual activity with a person below the legal age of consent
  • Degree Higher: rape - non-consensual sexual intercourse

Etymology

From Latin 'carnalis' (fleshly), and 'abuse' meaning misuse or improper treatment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrnəl əˈbjus / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnəl əˈbjuːs / (BrE)
the act of engaging in sexual contact by an adult with a child, particularly without involving sexual intercourse; often a legal term relating to child sexual abuse
sexual contact by adult with a child (legal term)
legal

Examples

The court found the defendant guilty of carnal abuse of a minor.
Legislation has increased penalties for those convicted of carnal abuse.
The report revealed multiple cases of carnal abuse in the institution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: child sexual abuse - sexual abuse involving a child

Etymology

From Latin 'carnalis' meaning 'fleshly' plus English 'abuse'.
