avoirdupois weight

1. NOUN

/ ˌævɚdjuˈpɔɪz weɪt / (AmE), / ˌavə.djuːˈpɔɪz weɪt / (BrE)
a system of weights based on the 16-ounce pound, commonly used in English-speaking countries for everyday goods
system of weights using 16-ounce pounds

Examples

In the United States, most groceries are sold according to the avoirdupois weight system.
The avoirdupois weight system is different from the troy weight system used for precious metals.
He learned to convert between metric units and avoirdupois weight during his science class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: troy weight - a system of weights used mainly for precious metals and gemstones

Etymology

From Middle French avoir de pois (goods of weight), referring to merchandise sold by weight.
