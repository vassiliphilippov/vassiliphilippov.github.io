untenanted property

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtɛnəntɪd ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛnəntɪd ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
A building, apartment, or other property that currently has no tenants occupying it.
property without tenants

Examples

The landlord decided to renovate the untenanted property before seeking new renters.
Investors are often attracted to untenanted property because of the potential for higher returns after renovation.
Local authorities are considering new taxes on long-term untenanted property.

Synset Examples

They finally sold the untenanted property last month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacant - (of property) not occupied; empty
  • Antonym: tenanted property - property that is occupied by tenants

Etymology

From 'untenanted', meaning not occupied by tenants, + 'property'. 'Tenant' from Old French 'tenant', present participle of 'tenir' (to hold).
