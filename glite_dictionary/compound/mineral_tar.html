mineral tar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl tɑr / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl tɑː / (BrE)
a thick, black, sticky substance derived from minerals, intermediate in composition between petroleum and asphalt
thick black tar between petroleum and asphalt

Examples

The pipe was coated with mineral tar to prevent corrosion.
During the industrial era, mineral tar was widely used for waterproofing roofs.
If mineral tar is overheated, it can release harmful fumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tar - a dark, thick, sticky liquid obtained from wood or coal, used for waterproofing
  • Degree Higher: asphalt - a black, sticky substance used for road surfaces
  • Degree Lower: petroleum - a naturally occurring liquid found beneath the earth's surface, used to produce fuel
