cubital nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bɪ.tl̩ nɝːv / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪ.tl̩ nɜːv / (BrE)
a nerve running along the inner side of the arm, passing near the elbow, and supplying muscles and skin on the medial side of the hand
nerve along inner arm near elbow
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided the cubital nerve during the procedure to prevent nerve damage.
Compression of the cubital nerve can result in numbness and weakness in the hand.
An injury to the cubital nerve near the elbow may require medical evaluation and treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulnar nerve - major nerve of the arm running near the elbow

Etymology

From Latin 'cubitum' meaning 'elbow', referring to its location near the elbow.
