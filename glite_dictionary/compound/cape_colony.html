Cape Colony

1. PROPN

/ keɪp ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / keɪp ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
a former province in southern South Africa, originally settled by the Dutch in 1652, ceded to Great Britain in 1814, and divided into three provinces in 1994.
former province of South Africa

Examples

The British government assumed control of the Cape Colony in the early 19th century.
Many settlers in the Cape Colony were of Dutch descent, known as Afrikaners.
In 1994, the Cape Colony was divided into the Western Cape, Eastern Cape, and Northern Cape provinces.

Etymology

Named after the Cape of Good Hope, which was the principal region of the colony; 'colony' from Latin 'colonia' (settlement).
