Ord kangaroo rat

1. NOUN

/ ɔrd kəŋˈɡuː roʊ ræt / (AmE), / ɔːd kæŋˈɡuː rəʊ ræt / (BrE)
a species of kangaroo rat (Dipodomys ordii) widely distributed in the plains and mountain areas of the central and western United States.
species of kangaroo rat native to central/western US

Examples

The Ord kangaroo rat can be found throughout much of the Great Plains region.
Researchers observed the burrowing habits of the Ord kangaroo rat in several habitats.
A Ord kangaroo rat can survive long periods without drinking water due to its efficient kidneys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - small jumping rodent native to North America

Etymology

Named after George Ord, American zoologist.
