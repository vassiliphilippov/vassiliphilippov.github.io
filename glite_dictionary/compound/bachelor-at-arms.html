bachelor-at-arms

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr æt ˈɑrmz / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələ æt ˈɑːmz / (BrE)
A knight of the lowest order, entitled to display only a pennon.
a knight of the lowest order

Examples

In the tournament, only a bachelor-at-arms was permitted to bear a pennon on his lance.
During the ceremony, he was dubbed a bachelor-at-arms, marking the beginning of his knighthood.
Medieval chronicles often list the achievements of each bachelor-at-arms alongside those of higher-ranking knights.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: knight - a man awarded a non-hereditary title for service or valor

Etymology

From medieval French 'bacheler' (knight bachelor) + 'at arms' (prepared for battle).
