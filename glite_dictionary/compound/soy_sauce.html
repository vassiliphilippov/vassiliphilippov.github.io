soy sauce

1. NOUN

/ sɔɪ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔɪ sɔːs / (BrE)
a dark, salty liquid made from fermented soybeans and used especially in Asian cooking as a condiment or seasoning
salty liquid condiment from fermented soybeans

Examples

Could you please pass the soy sauce for my sushi?
Many recipes call for a tablespoon of soy sauce to enhance the flavor.
She accidentally spilled soy sauce on the white tablecloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamari - a type of Japanese soy sauce
  • Synonym: Worcestershire sauce - a fermented liquid condiment, sometimes used similarly

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu' (soy sauce), via Chinese 'jiàngyóu', reflecting the Asian origins of the condiment.
