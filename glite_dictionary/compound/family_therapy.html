family therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌθɛrəpi / (BrE)
A type of psychotherapy in which members of a family are treated together to improve communication and resolve conflicts.
psychological treatment for families as a group

Examples

The couple decided to try family therapy to address long-standing issues with their children.
Many mental health professionals recommend family therapy for families experiencing communication problems.
After several months of family therapy, their relationships had noticeably improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counseling - professional guidance in resolving personal or psychological problems
  • Antonym: individual therapy - psychological treatment focused on a single person
