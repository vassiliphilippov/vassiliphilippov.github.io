visual space

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl speɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒjʊəl speɪs / (BrE)
the area or extent of space as perceived visually, as opposed to physical or conceptual space
perceived spatial field

Examples

It can be difficult to judge depth accurately in a two-dimensional visual space.
Artists often manipulate visual space to create a sense of realism in their paintings.
The experiment measured how we perceive objects at different distances in visual space.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physical space - the actual, measurable space in the physical world
