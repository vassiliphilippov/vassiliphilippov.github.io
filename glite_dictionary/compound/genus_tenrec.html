genus Tenrec

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtɛnˌrɛk / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtɛn.rɛk / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Tenrecidae, comprising certain mammals known as tenrecs.
type genus of Tenrecidae (tenrecs)

Examples

The genus Tenrec is native to Madagascar and includes a variety of small mammals.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Tenrec during their expedition.
Members of the genus Tenrec exhibit a remarkable diversity in form and habitat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tenrecidae - family of mammals including tenrecs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Tenrec', from Malagasy 'tandraka'.
