grape juice

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪp d͡ʒus / (AmE), / ɡreɪp d͡ʒuːs / (BrE)
the juice obtained from grapes, typically consumed as a beverage or used as an ingredient in foods and drinks
juice made from grapes

Examples

Would you like a glass of grape juice with your breakfast?
She used fresh grape juice to make homemade jelly.
The children enjoyed drinking chilled grape juice on a hot afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juice - liquid from fruits or vegetables

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪp ˌdʒus / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪp ˌdʒuːs / (BrE)
A sweet, non-alcoholic drink made by extracting the liquid from grapes.
drink made from grapes

Examples

She poured a glass of grape juice for each guest at breakfast.
Children often prefer grape juice over soft drinks at parties.
The recipe calls for a cup of fresh grape juice to add natural sweetness.

Synset Examples

They served grape juice during the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fruit juice - juice made by pressing or otherwise extracting liquid from fruits
