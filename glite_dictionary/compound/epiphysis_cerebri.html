epiphysis cerebri

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌpɪfəsɪs səˈriːbraɪ / (AmE), / ɪˌpɪfɪsɪs səˈriːbrɪ / (BrE)
a small endocrine gland located in the brain, beneath the back part of the corpus callosum, that secretes the hormone melatonin
a brain gland that secretes melatonin
medical

Examples

The epiphysis cerebri plays a crucial role in regulating sleep cycles through secretion of melatonin.
Scientists have studied the epiphysis cerebri to better understand its influence on circadian rhythms.
Damage to the epiphysis cerebri can disrupt an individual's hormonal balance and sleep patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineal gland - another name for epiphysis cerebri; tiny endocrine gland in the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'epiphysis' (growth, outgrowth) and Latin 'cerebri' (of the brain).
