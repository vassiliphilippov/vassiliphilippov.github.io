Yazoo River

1. PROPN

/ jæˈzu ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / jæˈzuː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in western Mississippi, United States, that flows southwest to join the Mississippi River above Vicksburg
river in western Mississippi, U.S.

Examples

The Yazoo River is known for its important role in Mississippi's agricultural history.
They went fishing along the banks of the Yazoo River during their summer vacation.
Heavy rains caused the Yazoo River to overflow its banks last year.

Etymology

Named after the Yazoo people, an Indigenous tribe historically living near its banks.
