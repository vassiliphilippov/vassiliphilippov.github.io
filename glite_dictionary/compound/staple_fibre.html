staple fibre

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪpəl ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪpəl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a natural fibre, such as cotton, wool, hemp, or flax, that is of short length and can be twisted together to form yarn
short natural fibre for spinning yarn

Examples

Raw cotton is a common staple fibre used in the textile industry.
The quality of wool depends largely on the length of its staple fibre.
Different plants produce staple fibres with varying properties for making yarns.

Synset Examples

staple fibers vary widely in length

Related Terms

  • Antonym: filament fibre - a fibre of indefinite or extreme length, such as silk

Etymology

Staple from Old English 'stapol' (pillar, support); 'fibre' from Latin 'fibra' (fiber, thread).
