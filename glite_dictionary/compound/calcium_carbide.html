calcium carbide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsiəm ˈkɑrˌbaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm ˈkɑːbaɪd / (BrE)
A gray chemical compound (CaC₂) used mainly for producing acetylene gas and in various industrial processes.
chemical compound used to make acetylene

Examples

When calcium carbide reacts with water, it produces acetylene gas.
Miners once used calcium carbide lamps for illumination underground.
Improper storage of calcium carbide can be dangerous due to its reactivity with moisture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetylene - a colorless gas used as a fuel and chemical building block

Etymology

From 'calcium', a chemical element, and 'carbide', from French 'carbure', relating to carbon.
