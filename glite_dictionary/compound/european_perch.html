European perch

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən pɜrtʃ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a freshwater fish of the perch family (Perca fluviatilis), native to Europe and northern Asia, known for its greenish body and distinctive red fins
a European species of perch (fish)

Examples

Anglers often prize the European perch for its fighting spirit and striking colors.
The lake is home to several species, including the European perch and pike.
Researchers studied the diet of the European perch in different river systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - a freshwater fish of the family Percidae
