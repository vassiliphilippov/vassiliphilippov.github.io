field of regard

1. NOUN

/ fiːld əv rəˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / fiːld əv rɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
the entire area of the physical environment visible to a stationary eye at one time
visual area seen by a stable eye

Examples

The pilot's field of regard was limited by the aircraft's small cockpit window.
New virtual reality headsets allow for a larger field of regard than traditional displays.
Researchers measured each participant's field of regard during the visual experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field of view - the observable area a person can see at any given moment
