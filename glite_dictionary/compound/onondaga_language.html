Onondaga language

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑnənˈdɑːɡə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɒnənˈdɑːɡə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language traditionally spoken by the Onondaga people, indigenous to central New York State
an Iroquoian language of the Onondaga people

Examples

The Onondaga language is taught in some schools to help preserve the tribe's cultural heritage.
Few fluent speakers of the Onondaga language remain, making revitalization efforts increasingly important.
Linguists are documenting the Onondaga language to ensure its sounds and grammar are not lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - a family of North American indigenous languages

Etymology

From Onondaga, the name of the people and their principal village; Onondaga is from the Iroquoian term meaning 'people of the hills.'
