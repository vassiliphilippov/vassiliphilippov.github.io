genus Scorpaena

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnəs skɔːrˈpeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌskɔːpəˈiːnə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Scorpaenidae, comprising the scorpionfishes.
type genus of scorpionfishes

Examples

The genus Scorpaena includes some of the most venomous fish in the ocean.
Biologists identified the specimen as belonging to the genus Scorpaena.
The distinctive spines of the genus Scorpaena serve as a defense mechanism against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scorpaenidae - the family of scorpionfishes

Etymology

From Latin 'scorpaena', a type of fish, from Greek 'skorpaina'.
