kitul tree

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈtʊl tri / (AmE), / kɪˈtuːl triː / (BrE)
a species of fishtail palm (Caryota urens), native to India and Southeast Asia, whose sap is used to produce jaggery and whose trunk provides sago
a palm tree yielding jaggery and sago

Examples

The kitul tree is tapped for its sweet sap, which villagers turn into jaggery.
Farmers harvest sago from the trunk of the kitul tree during the dry season.
In certain parts of Sri Lanka, the kitul tree plays an important role in traditional agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fishtail palm - a type of palm tree with leaves that resemble the tail of a fish
  • Synonym: jaggery - unrefined sugar made from the sap of certain palms and sugarcane
