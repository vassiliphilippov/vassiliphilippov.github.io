time exposure

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / taɪm ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
an exposure of a photographic film or sensor for a relatively long period, typically more than half a second, to capture more light and detail
long exposure in photography

Examples

The stunning night photograph was achieved by using a time exposure of several seconds.
A time exposure can help reveal star trails in the night sky.
She experimented with time exposure photography to create creative light patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long exposure - an exposure made for a long duration to capture more light in photography

2. NOUN

/ taɪm ɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / taɪm ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
a photograph taken with a long exposure time, allowing more light to reach the film or sensor and capturing motion or low-light details
photograph with long exposure time

Examples

He used a time exposure to capture the star trails across the night sky.
A time exposure can reveal the movement of cars as glowing lines on the road.
The photographer created a beautiful time exposure of the waterfall, making the water look silky and smooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long exposure - photograph taken with a prolonged shutter speed
