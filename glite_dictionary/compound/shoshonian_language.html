Shoshonian language

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈʃoʊniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʃəˈʃəʊniən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A subfamily of the Uto-Aztecan languages spoken primarily in the southwestern United States.
Uto-Aztecan language subfamily in the US Southwest

Examples

The Shoshonian language group includes languages such as Shoshoni, Comanche, and Paiute.
Linguists have studied the Shoshonian language to better understand the migration patterns of native peoples.
Many Shoshonian languages are recognized for their similarities in vocabulary and grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uto-Aztecan - A large family of North American Indian languages

Etymology

From Shoshoni (a native people and their language) + -an, formed on the pattern of language family names.
