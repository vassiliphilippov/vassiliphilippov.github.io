lance corporal

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæns ˌkɔrpərəl / (AmE), / ˈlɑːns ˌkɔːpərəl / (BrE)
a military rank in the army or marine corps, above private first class and below corporal
military rank above private first class

Examples

After two years of service, he was promoted to lance corporal in the Marine Corps.
The lance corporal received a commendation for his bravery during the mission.
As a lance corporal, she was assigned more responsibility than a private.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: corporal - a military rank above lance corporal
  • Degree Lower: private first class - a military rank below lance corporal

Etymology

'Lance' derives from the Old French 'lance', and 'corporal' from Italian 'caporale', originally referring to a leader of a small group of soldiers.
