Equatorial Guinean franc

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkweɪtɔːriəl ɡiˈniən fræŋk / (AmE), / ɪˌkweɪtəriəl ɡɪˈniːən fræŋk / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in the Republic of Equatorial Guinea, used before the adoption of the CFA franc.
former currency of Equatorial Guinea

Examples

The Equatorial Guinean franc was replaced by the CFA franc in 1985.
Historians found old banknotes of the Equatorial Guinean franc in the archives.
Prices were once listed in Equatorial Guinean francs throughout the country.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: CFA franc - currency currently used in Equatorial Guinea and several West African countries
