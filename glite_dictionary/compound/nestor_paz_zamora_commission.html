Nestor Paz Zamora Commission

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛstɔr ˈpɑs zəˈmɔrə kəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɛstɔː pæz zəˈmɔːrə kəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a left-wing militant organization in Bolivia that serves as an umbrella for various small indigenous subversive groups, reviving Marxist-Leninist ideologies originally established by Che Guevara in the 1960s.
Bolivian militant Marxist-Leninist organization

Examples

The Bolivian government increased security after the Nestor Paz Zamora Commission claimed responsibility for the attacks.
Members of the Nestor Paz Zamora Commission were arrested during a raid in La Paz.
International agencies have monitored the activities of the Nestor Paz Zamora Commission due to its history of violence.

Etymology

Named after Néstor Paz Zamora, a Bolivian revolutionary figure.
