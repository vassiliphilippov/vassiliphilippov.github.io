snare drum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɛr drʌm / (AmE), / ˈsnɛə drʌm / (BrE)
a small cylindrical drum with two heads, one of which has snares stretched across it, producing a sharp, distinctive sound in orchestras and bands
small drum with snares across lower head

Examples

The marching band’s rhythm depended on the steady beat of the snare drum.
She learned to play the snare drum in her school’s percussion ensemble.
A sharp crack from the snare drum kicked off the concert’s opening number.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bass drum - large drum with a deep tone, used in bands and orchestras

2. NOUN

/ snɛr drʌm / (AmE), / snɛə drʌm / (BrE)
A small drum with metal wires stretched across the bottom head, producing a sharp, crisp sound, used especially in orchestras and marching bands.
drum with wires for a sharp sound

Examples

The bandleader signaled the snare drum to start the marching rhythm.
She practiced rudiments on her snare drum for hours every day.
During the concert, the snare drum added a dramatic effect to the music.

Synset Examples

The musician used a snare drum to keep the rhythm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bass drum - a large drum with a low pitch
