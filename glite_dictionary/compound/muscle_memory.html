muscle memory

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl ˌmɛməri / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl ˌmeməri / (BrE)
the ability to reproduce a particular movement or motor task automatically, acquired through repetition or practice rather than conscious thought
automatic recall of learned movements

Examples

After years of playing piano, the complex finger movements became pure muscle memory.
Typing quickly is often a result of muscle memory built up over time.
Even after a long break, her muscle memory helped her ride the bicycle effortlessly again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motor skill - a learned sequence of movements that combine to produce a smooth, efficient action
