genus Ceratopogon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌsɛrətoʊˈpoʊɡən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌsɛrətəˈpəʊɡən / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Ceratopogonidae, comprising biting midges known for their small size and bloodsucking habits.
type genus of Ceratopogonidae (biting midges)
medical

Examples

The genus Ceratopogon includes many species of bloodsucking midges found worldwide.
Entomologists often study the genus Ceratopogon due to its impact on human and animal health.
Some members of the genus Ceratopogon are known vectors for various animal diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ceratopogonidae - family of biting midges to which genus Ceratopogon belongs

Etymology

From Greek *keras* (horn) + *pōgōn* (beard), referring to the appearance of the insects.
