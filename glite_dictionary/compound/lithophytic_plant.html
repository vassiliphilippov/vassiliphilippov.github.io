lithophytic plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪθəˈfɪtɪk plænt / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəˈfɪtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that grows on rocks or stony soil and derives nourishment mainly from the atmosphere rather than the soil.
plant that grows on rocks or stony soil

Examples

Certain orchids are lithophytic plants that thrive on rocky cliffs and derive moisture from the air.
Special adaptations allow lithophytic plants to survive where soil is sparse or absent.
Botanists study lithophytic plants to understand how life can colonize barren stone surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphyte - a plant that grows on another plant but is not parasitic

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) and phyton (plant).
