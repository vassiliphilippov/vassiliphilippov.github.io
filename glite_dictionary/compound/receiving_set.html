receiving set

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsivɪŋ sɛt / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvɪŋ sɛt / (BrE)
an electronic device that receives, demodulates, and amplifies transmitted radio signals, typically referring to a radio or television receiver
electronic receiver for radio or television signals

Examples

The antique receiving set in the museum still works after all these years.
Early radio enthusiasts built their own receiving set to listen to broadcasts.
In the 1920s, owning a receiving set was considered a luxury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receiver - a device that receives radio, television, or other signals

Etymology

From 'receive' (from Latin recīpere) + 'set' (in the sense of a piece of equipment); historical technical term.
