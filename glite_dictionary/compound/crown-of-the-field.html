crown-of-the-field

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraʊn əv ðə ˈfiːld / (AmE), / ˌkraʊn əv ðə ˈfiːld / (BrE)
A European annual plant with large trumpet-shaped reddish-purple flowers and poisonous seeds, commonly found in grainfields and along roadsides; also naturalized in America.
European annual with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of the crown-of-the-field stood out against the golden wheat.
Farmers consider crown-of-the-field a troublesome weed because of its rapid spread.
Children are warned not to touch the seeds of the crown-of-the-field, as they are poisonous.
