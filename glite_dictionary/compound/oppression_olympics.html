Oppression Olympics

1. NOUN

/ əˈprɛʃən əˈlɪmpɪks / (AmE), / əˈprɛʃ(ə)n əˈlɪmpɪks / (BrE)
an informal term for competition among individuals or groups to establish who has experienced greater levels of oppression or victimization
competition over who has suffered more oppression
informal

Examples

Many activists warn against engaging in the Oppression Olympics within social justice movements.
Some panel discussions deteriorated into Oppression Olympics, rather than finding common ground.
She criticized the tendency to play the Oppression Olympics when discussing societal inequalities.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that the Oppression Olympics distract from genuine issues.
The debate turned into an Oppression Olympics discussion.
We shouldn't engage in Oppression Olympics; everyone’s struggles matter.

Etymology

Modeled after 'Olympics,' referencing the international sporting competition, combined with 'oppression' to denote rivaling claims of victimhood.
