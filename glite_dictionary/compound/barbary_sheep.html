Barbary sheep

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbəri ʃiːp / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəri ʃiːp / (BrE)
a wild sheep of northern Africa, with a reddish-brown coat and curved horns, found especially in rocky mountains and deserts
wild sheep of northern Africa

Examples

The Barbary sheep is well adapted to arid mountain environments in the Sahara.
Zookeepers recently welcomed a baby Barbary sheep into their wildlife preserve.
Because of its agility, the Barbary sheep can easily climb steep, rocky slopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aoudad - another name for the Barbary sheep, especially its scientific name (Ammotragus lervia)

Etymology

From Barbary, the historical name for the North African coastal region.
