galvanic pile

1. NOUN

/ ɡælˈvænɪk paɪl / (AmE), / ɡælˈvanɪk pʌɪl / (BrE)
an early battery consisting of a series of voltaic cells stacked in sequence, devised by Alessandro Volta
early electric battery invented by Volta

Examples

The galvanic pile was a crucial invention that helped pave the way for modern batteries.
Scientists in the 19th century experimented with the galvanic pile to better understand electricity.
Alessandro Volta is known for inventing the galvanic pile, the first true battery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltaic pile - early form of battery made of alternating discs of metals and electrolytes

Etymology

From Italian physicist Luigi Galvani (whose work inspired Volta) and 'pile', referring to the stack of discs.
