left atrioventricular valve

1. NOUN

/ lɛft ˌeɪtrioʊvɛnˈtrɪkjələr vælv / (AmE), / lɛft ˌeɪtrɪəʊvɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə vælv / (BrE)
the valve with two cusps located between the left atrium and left ventricle of the heart, also known as the mitral valve
heart valve between left atrium and ventricle
medical

Examples

The left atrioventricular valve ensures that blood flows from the left atrium to the left ventricle without backflow.
During heart surgery, doctors inspected the left atrioventricular valve for signs of damage.
Rheumatic fever can cause narrowing of the left atrioventricular valve, affecting proper heart function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitral valve - the valve between the left atrium and left ventricle; another name for left atrioventricular valve
  • Antonym: right atrioventricular valve - the valve between the right atrium and right ventricle (tricuspid valve)

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'atrium' (chamber), 'ventriculus' (small belly/chamber), and 'valva' (valve).
