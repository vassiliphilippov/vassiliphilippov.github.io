regression equation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡrɛʃən ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛʃən ɪˈkweɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
an equation showing the mathematical relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables, used to predict probable values of the dependent variable
statistical equation predicting variable values

Examples

The scientist used a regression equation to estimate future population growth.
You can derive the regression equation from the given dataset to predict the outcome variable.
A simple linear regression equation often takes the form y = a + bx.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression analysis - a statistical method for examining relationships between variables

Etymology

From regression (from Latin 'regressio', going back) + equation (from Latin 'aequatio', making equal).
