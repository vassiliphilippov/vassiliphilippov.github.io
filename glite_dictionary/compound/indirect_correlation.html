indirect correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dəˈrɛkt ˌkɔːr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛkt ˌkɒr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between two variables in which one increases as the other decreases, indicated by a negative correlation coefficient
negative statistical relationship

Examples

There was an indirect correlation between job satisfaction and stress levels among employees.
Researchers found an indirect correlation between smoking rates and academic performance in adolescents.
The data revealed an indirect correlation between air pollution and respiratory health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inverse correlation - a statistical relationship where an increase in one variable results in a decrease in another
  • Antonym: direct correlation - a relationship where two variables increase or decrease together

Etymology

From Latin 'correlatio' (relationship); 'indirect' from Latin 'indirectus' (not straight).
