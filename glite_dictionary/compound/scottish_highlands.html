Scottish Highlands

1. PROPN

/ ˈskɑːtɪʃ ˈhaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈhaɪləndz / (BrE)
a mountainous region in northern Scotland, noted for its rugged landscape, traditional dress such as kilts and tartan, and historical clan system
mountainous region of northern Scotland

Examples

The Scottish Highlands are known for their breathtaking scenery and dramatic mountain ranges.
Many tourists visit the Scottish Highlands to explore its lochs and ancient castles.
Traditional music and festivals are an important part of life in the Scottish Highlands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Lowlands - the southern, lower-lying part of Scotland
