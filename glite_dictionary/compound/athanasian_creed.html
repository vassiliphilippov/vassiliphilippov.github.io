Athanasian Creed

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈneɪʒən krid / (AmE), / ˌaθəˈneɪzɪən kriːd / (BrE)
a statement of Christian faith, traditionally attributed to Athanasius, outlining key doctrines of the Trinity and the incarnation
Christian statement of faith on Trinity and Incarnation

Examples

Many churches recite the Athanasian Creed during their liturgical services on special occasions.
The Athanasian Creed emphasizes the equality of the three persons of the Trinity.
Scholars have debated the origins and authorship of the Athanasian Creed for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicene Creed - another major Christian statement of faith
  • Synonym: Apostles' Creed - earlier statement of Christian belief

Etymology

Named after Athanasius of Alexandria, although not authored by him.
