American lobster

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈlɑbstɚ / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
The edible flesh of a large cold-water lobster with big tender claws, found from Maine to the Carolinas.
edible cold-water lobster flesh

Examples

Fresh American lobster is often considered a delicacy in upscale restaurants.
The chef prepared a bisque using sweet, tender American lobster meat.
We ordered steamed American lobster for our special anniversary dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - marine crustacean with a hard shell and large claws

Examples
