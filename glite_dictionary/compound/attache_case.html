attache case

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæʃ eɪ keɪs / (AmE), / əˈtæʃ eɪ keɪs / (BrE)
a flat, rectangular briefcase, typically with a hard frame, used for carrying documents
shallow rectangular briefcase

Examples

He placed the confidential folder in his attache case before leaving the office.
The lawyer arrived in court carrying an elegant attache case.
She bought a new attache case for her first day at the law firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefcase - a case with a handle for carrying papers or documents

Etymology

From French attaché (person assigned to an embassy) and English case, reflecting its use by diplomats.
