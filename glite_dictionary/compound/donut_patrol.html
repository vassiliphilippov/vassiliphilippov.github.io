donut patrol

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊnət pəˌtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊnət pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
A humorous or informal term for a group tasked with overseeing something trivial, especially used to refer jokingly to police officers on duty.
humorous term for trivial supervision or police on duty
informal

Examples

After midnight, the security guards referred to themselves as the donut patrol to lighten the mood.
He laughed when his friends called the neighborhood watch the donut patrol.
The officers didn't mind being nicknamed the donut patrol during their overnight shift.

Synset Examples

He joked about joining the donut patrol for his new job.
The officers were on donut patrol, keeping the streets safe.
They formed a donut patrol to check local shops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat cop - a police officer who patrols a regular route
  • Synonym: mall cop - a security guard at a shopping mall, informal

Etymology

Combines 'donut', a ring-shaped pastry, with 'patrol', from French 'patrouiller'. The police association is based on a stereotype in American culture.
