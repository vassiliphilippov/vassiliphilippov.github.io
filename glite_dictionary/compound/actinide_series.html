actinide series

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪˌnaɪd ˈsɪriz / (AmE), / ˈæktɪnʌɪd ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
(chemistry) a group of 15 radioactive elements in the periodic table from actinium to lawrencium, with increasing atomic numbers.
set of 15 radioactive chemical elements
medical

Examples

Scientists study the actinide series to understand the behavior of radioactive materials.
Uranium and plutonium are both members of the actinide series.
Exploring the actinide series is important for advancements in nuclear energy research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanthanide series - a group of 15 elements from lanthanum to lutetium in the periodic table

Etymology

From 'actinium', the first element in the series, and the suffix '-ide'.
