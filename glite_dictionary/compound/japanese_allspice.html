Japanese allspice

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈɔlspaɪs / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈɔːlspaɪs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to Japan, cultivated for its aromatic yellow flowers.
fragrant Japanese flowering shrub

Examples

The Japanese allspice blooms early in spring, filling the garden with a sweet fragrance.
Botanists admire the unique aroma of Japanese allspice during the winter months.
She decided to plant a Japanese allspice near the entrance for its beautiful yellow flowers.
