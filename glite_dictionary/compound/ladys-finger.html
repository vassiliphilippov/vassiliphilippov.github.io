lady's-finger

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˌfɪŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˌfɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A tall annual plant (Abelmoschus esculentus), cultivated for its long, slender green pods used in soups and stews; also known as okra.
tropical plant grown for edible green pods (okra)

Examples

Lady's-finger is a key ingredient in traditional gumbo recipes from the southern United States.
We planted several lady's-fingers in our vegetable garden this spring.
She prefers lady's-finger fried with spices as a side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okra - the edible green seed pod of Abelmoschus esculentus
