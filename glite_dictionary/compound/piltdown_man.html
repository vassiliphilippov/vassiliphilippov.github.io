Piltdown man

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪltdaʊn mæn / (AmE), / ˈpɪltdaʊn man / (BrE)
an early human fossil discovered in England and once considered a missing link in human evolution, later shown to be a scientific hoax
faked early human fossil discovery

Examples

For decades, scientists believed the Piltdown man represented a crucial evolutionary link.
The exposure of the Piltdown man as a forgery shocked the scientific community.
Exhibits on the Piltdown man illustrate the impact of scientific fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoax - a deception, especially a trick played for a joke or to make someone look foolish

Etymology

Named after Piltdown, Sussex, England, where the discovery was made in 1912.
