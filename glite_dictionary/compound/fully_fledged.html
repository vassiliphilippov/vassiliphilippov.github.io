fully fledged

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʊli flɛdʒd / (AmE), / ˈfʊli flɛdʒd / (BrE)
Having gained full status, rights, or maturity; completely developed or established.
completely developed; having full status

Examples

Her promotion made her a fully fledged member of the management team.
It takes several years of training to become a fully fledged surgeon.
The startup has grown into a fully fledged company with hundreds of employees.

Synset Examples

a full-fledged lawyer
by the age of seventeen I was a full-fledged atheist
sees itself as a fully fledged rival party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full-fledged - Having attained full development or status
  • Antonym: novice - A person who is new or inexperienced

Etymology

From 'fledge' meaning (of a bird) to acquire feathers for flight; 'fully fledged' extended figuratively to mean fully developed.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʊli flɛdʒd / (AmE), / ˈfʊli ˈflɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a bird) having developed all the feathers necessary for flight; fully developed or mature.
having full adult development or plumage

Examples

The chick became fully fledged in just a few weeks and was soon ready to leave the nest.
After months of care, the injured sparrow is now fully fledged and able to fly on its own.
Most eagles do not leave the nest until they are fully fledged and strong enough to soar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fledged - (of a bird) having feathers necessary for flight
