Cenozoic era

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːnəˈzoʊɪk ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˌsiːnəˈzəʊɪk ˈɪərə / (BrE)
the most recent geological era, spanning from about 66 million years ago to the present, characterized by the development and dominance of mammals and birds
recent geological era

Examples

The extinction of the dinosaurs marked the beginning of the Cenozoic era.
During the Cenozoic era, mammals became the dominant land animals.
Fossils from the Cenozoic era provide important clues about the evolution of modern species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Mesozoic era - the geological era before the Cenozoic era
  • Degree Lower: Paleozoic era - an earlier geological era, preceding the Mesozoic

Etymology

From Greek *kainos* (new) + *zoion* (animal).
