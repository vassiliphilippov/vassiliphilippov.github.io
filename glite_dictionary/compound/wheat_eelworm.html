wheat eelworm

1. NOUN

/ wiːt ˈiːlˌwɜrm / (AmE), / wiːt ˈiːlˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a small roundworm (nematode) that parasitizes wheat plants, often causing disease and crop loss
parasitic roundworm affecting wheat

Examples

The presence of wheat eelworm in the soil can significantly reduce crop yields.
Farmers monitor their fields regularly for signs of wheat eelworm infestation.
Controlling wheat eelworm is important to prevent damage to wheat plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nematode - a type of roundworm
