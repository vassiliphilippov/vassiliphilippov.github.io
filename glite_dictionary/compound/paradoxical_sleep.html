paradoxical sleep

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈdɑksɪkəl sliːp / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈdɒksɪkəl sliːp / (BrE)
a phase of sleep characterized by rapid eye movements and vivid dreaming, during which brain activity resembles that of wakefulness
phase of sleep with rapid eye movements and dreaming
medical

Examples

Children often experience longer periods of paradoxical sleep than adults do.
Researchers measured brain activity during paradoxical sleep to study dreaming.
Loss of paradoxical sleep may affect memory and emotional regulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: REM sleep - phase of sleep with rapid eye movement and dreaming

Etymology

From 'paradoxical' (seemingly self-contradictory), describing the contrast between active brain waves and sleeping body.
