dry mop

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ mɑp / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ mɒp / (BrE)
a mop with a dry, soft head, used for dusting or cleaning floors without water
a dry mop for dusting floors

Examples

She used a dry mop to quickly remove the dust from the hallway floor.
The janitor reached for the dry mop instead of the wet one.
Every classroom is cleaned daily with a dry mop to keep it free of dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mop - a cleaning tool with an absorbent head for washing floors
