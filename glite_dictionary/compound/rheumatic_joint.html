rheumatic joint

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmætɪk dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ruːˈmatɪk dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A joint that is inflamed or affected by rheumatism, often causing pain and stiffness.
joint affected by rheumatism
medical

Examples

The doctor examined his rheumatic joint for signs of inflammation and swelling.
She experiences severe pain in every rheumatic joint during cold weather.
Physical therapy brought some relief to her most affected rheumatic joint.

Synset Examples

His rheumaticky joints made it hard to move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritic joint - joint affected by arthritis
  • Antonym: healthy joint - joint without disease or inflammation

Etymology

From 'rheumatic' (related to rheumatism) + 'joint'. 'Rheumatic' from Greek rheuma (a flow/stream), via Latin and French.
