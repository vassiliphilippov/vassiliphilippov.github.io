frigate bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪɡət bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈfrɪɡət bɜːd / (BrE)
A large tropical seabird with long wings, a forked tail, and a long hooked bill, known for soaring flight and often stealing food from other birds.
tropical seabird with long wings and forked tail

Examples

The frigate bird is often seen gliding effortlessly above tropical coastlines.
A male frigate bird inflates its bright red throat pouch during mating season.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of the frigate bird across the Pacific Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificent frigatebird - A specific species of frigate bird (Fregata magnificens)
  • Degree Higher: seabird - General group of birds that live by the sea

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a fast warship known as a 'frigate' due to its agility and speed.
