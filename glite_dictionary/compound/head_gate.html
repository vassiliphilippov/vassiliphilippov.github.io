head gate

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛd ˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛd ˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
A gate at the upstream end of a lock or canal, used to control the flow of water.
flow-control gate at canal or lock entrance

Examples

The engineers opened the head gate to increase the flow of water into the canal.
Debris near the head gate was blocking the water, requiring immediate removal.
Careful adjustment of the head gate ensured the reservoir maintained appropriate water levels.

Etymology

From 'head' meaning the upper part or start (here of water) and 'gate' as a barrier or valve.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛd ˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛd ˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
a movable barrier or valve at the inlet of a channel or sluice, used to control the flow of water
water control gate at channel inlet

Examples

The dam operator raised the head gate to release more water downstream.
Maintenance crews inspected the head gate to ensure it was functioning properly.
Farmers adjusted the head gate to regulate irrigation water flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice gate - a gate that controls water flow in a sluice

Etymology

From 'head' (top, source) + 'gate' (barrier), indicating a gate at the source or upstream part of a water channel.
