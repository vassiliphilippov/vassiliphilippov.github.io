literary period

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌreri ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
A span of time in literature marked by distinct cultural, societal, or artistic characteristics and trends, often associated with related groups of authors.
distinct era in literature

Examples

The Romantic literary period was characterized by a focus on emotion and individualism.
Scholars often debate where one literary period ends and another begins.
Many students struggle to distinguish the features of each literary period in their essays.

Synset Examples

Trying to fix a definitive set of dates to a literary period is never straightforward, and our attempts to do so are often somewhat arbitrary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literary movement - Group of authors sharing common styles or goals within a period
