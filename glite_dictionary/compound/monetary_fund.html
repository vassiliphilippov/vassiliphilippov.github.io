monetary fund

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌnəˌtɛri fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪtəri fʌnd / (BrE)
an amount of money reserved or set aside for a specific purpose, especially in finance or government
a reserve of money for a purpose

Examples

The organization established a monetary fund to support research into renewable energy.
After the disaster, a monetary fund was created to aid those affected.
Each department contributes a portion of its budget to the central monetary fund.
