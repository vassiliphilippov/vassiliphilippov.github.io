purple-fringed orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.pəl ˌfrɪndʒd ˈɔɹ.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ˌfrɪndʒd ˈɔː.kɪd / (BrE)
A North American orchid with clusters of fragrant purple fringed flowers, found in wet meadows and marshes.
North American orchid with purple fringed flowers

Examples

We spotted a rare purple-fringed orchid growing near the edge of the marsh.
The purple-fringed orchid blooms in mid-summer and attracts many butterflies.
She photographed the delicate petals of the purple-fringed orchid during her hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - A plant family with brightly colored and fragrant flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.pəl ˈfrɪndʒd ˈɔɹ.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ˈfrɪndʒd ˈɔː.kɪd / (BrE)
A North American wild orchid with large, pale purple fringed flowers.
North American orchid with fringed purple flowers

Examples

The purple-fringed orchid blooms in damp meadows from early summer to midsummer.
Hikers admired the delicate beauty of the purple-fringed orchid along the forest trail.
Conservation groups are working to protect habitats of the purple-fringed orchid in the northeastern United States.
