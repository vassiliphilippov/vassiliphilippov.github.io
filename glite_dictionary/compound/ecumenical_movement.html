ecumenical movement

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌkjuːməˈnɪkəl ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / iːˌkjuːməˈnɪkəl ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
A movement promoting cooperation and unity among Christian churches, aiming ultimately for universal Christian unity.
movement for Christian church unity

Examples

The ecumenical movement seeks to bring together different Christian denominations in a spirit of cooperation.
Many church leaders participated in conferences organized by the ecumenical movement to foster unity.
The ecumenical movement gained momentum after World War II, emphasizing dialogue and mutual respect among churches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenism - the principle or aim of promoting unity among the world's Christian churches

Etymology

From Greek oikoumenikos (of the inhabited world) + movement.
