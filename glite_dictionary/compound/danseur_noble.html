danseur noble

1. NOUN

/ dɑ̃ˈsɝː ˈnoʊbl̩ / (AmE), / dɑ̃ˈsɜː ˈnəʊbl / (BrE)
a male ballet dancer who performs leading roles, typically as the partner of a ballerina, characterized by dignity and elegance
male ballet dancer in leading role

Examples

The danseur noble captivated the audience with his elegant movements and strong stage presence.
As a danseur noble, he often performed majestic roles in classical ballets.
The company held auditions to find a talented danseur noble to star in their new production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballerina - female principal ballet dancer

Etymology

From French, literally 'noble dancer'.
