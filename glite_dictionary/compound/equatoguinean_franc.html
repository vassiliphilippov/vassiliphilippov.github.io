Equatoguinean franc

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.wə.toʊˈɡɪn.i.ən fræŋk / (AmE), / ˌiː.kwə.təʊˈɡɪn.i.ən fræŋk / (BrE)
the former basic monetary unit of Equatorial Guinea, replaced by the Central African CFA franc in 1985.
former currency of Equatorial Guinea

Examples

In 1979, the Equatoguinean franc was the official currency of the country.
The value of the Equatoguinean franc remained stable until it was replaced by the CFA franc.
Many old banknotes featuring the Equatoguinean franc are now collectors' items.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Central African CFA franc - currency that replaced the Equatoguinean franc in Equatorial Guinea
