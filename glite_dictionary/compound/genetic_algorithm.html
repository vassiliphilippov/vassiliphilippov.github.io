genetic algorithm

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈælɡəˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈælɡərɪðəm / (BrE)
(Computing) An optimization and search technique that uses methods inspired by the process of natural selection to find approximate solutions to complex problems.
optimization technique inspired by natural selection

Examples

The team used a genetic algorithm to optimize the design of the new engine.
Training schedules were automatically generated using a genetic algorithm that evaluated thousands of combinations.
By applying a genetic algorithm, they discovered an efficient solution that conventional methods failed to find.

Synset Examples

Also, in searching a large state-space, multi-modal state-space, or n-dimensional surface, a genetic algorithm may offer significant benefits over more typical search of optimization techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolutionary algorithm - an algorithm based on mechanisms of biological evolution
  • Synonym: heuristic - problem-solving approach using practical methods not guaranteed to be optimal

Etymology

From 'genetic', relating to heredity or genes, and 'algorithm', a set of rules for solving a problem. Named for its analogy with biological evolution.
