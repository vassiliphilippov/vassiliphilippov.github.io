Davis Cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪvɪs ˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪs ˌkʌp / (BrE)
an annual international team competition in men's tennis, for which the winning team receives a large silver cup
annual international men's tennis team competition

Examples

Every year, countries around the world compete in the Davis Cup to be crowned tennis champions.
He was proud to represent his country in the Davis Cup final last November.
Winning the Davis Cup remains one of the greatest achievements in men's team tennis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Fed Cup - annual international women's team tennis competition

Etymology

Named after Dwight F. Davis, who donated the original trophy in 1900.
