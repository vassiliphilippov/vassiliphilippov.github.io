genus Erwinia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɜrˈwɪn.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɜːˈwɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped, gram-negative bacteria that are primarily plant pathogens, many of which affect woody plants.
genus of plant pathogenic bacteria
medical

Examples

The genus Erwinia includes several species that cause diseases in fruit trees.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Erwinia that affects potatoes.
Proper identification of bacteria from the genus Erwinia is important for managing crop diseases.

Etymology

Named after American plant pathologist Erwin Frink Smith.
