summer crookneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ ˈkrʊknɛk / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə ˈkrʊknɛk / (BrE)
A type of yellow summer squash with a thin, curved neck and slightly warty skin, commonly used in cooking.
yellow squash with curved neck and warty skin

Examples

The farmer brought fresh summer crookneck squash to the market every week.
I sautéed sliced summer crookneck with onions and herbs for a simple side dish.
She grows summer crookneck in her garden because it's tender and flavorful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer squash - a group of squashes harvested while immature and eaten as a vegetable

Etymology

From 'summer' (the season in which it grows) and 'crookneck' referring to its curved neck shape.
