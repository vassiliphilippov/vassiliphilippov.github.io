twee style

1. NOUN

/ twi staɪl / (AmE), / twiː staɪl / (BrE)
A style that is excessively quaint, sweet, or sentimental, often to the point of being perceived as affected or superficial.
overly sweet or sentimental style

Examples

The café’s twee style features pastel colors, floral prints, and vintage teacups.
Critics dismissed the film’s twee style as insincere and affected, but audiences found it endearing.
She decorated her room in a twee style that felt whimsical and nostalgic.

Synset Examples

He finds her twee style charming and unique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or affected by emotion rather than reason
  • Synonym: kitschy - having poor taste, excessively sentimental, or gaudy style

Etymology

From 'twee', an alteration of 'sweet', plus 'style'. 'Twee' gained popularity in British English in the early 20th century.
