order Casuariiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒuˌɛəriːəˌfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈkæzjʊərɪˌfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of large, flightless birds that includes cassowaries and emus, native to Australia and New Guinea.
taxonomic order of cassowaries and emus

Examples

The order Casuariiformes consists of large, flightless birds found primarily in Australia and New Guinea.
Scientists have been studying the evolution of the order Casuariiformes to understand the loss of flight in birds.
Both cassowaries and emus are members of the order Casuariiformes, sharing similar anatomical features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassowary - large, flightless bird native to New Guinea and Australia
  • Synonym: emu - large, flightless Australian bird

Etymology

From New Latin Casuariiformes, from Casuarius (the genus name for cassowaries) + -iformes (order suffix).
