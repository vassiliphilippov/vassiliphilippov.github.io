styloid process

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪ.lɔɪd ˈprɑː.ses / (AmE), / ˈstaɪ.lɔɪd ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a slender, pointed projection of bone extending from the base of the temporal bone in the skull
bony projection from the temporal bone
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the styloid process during the procedure.
X-rays revealed a fracture near the styloid process of his skull.
The styloid process serves as an attachment point for several muscles in the neck.

Etymology

From Greek 'stylos' (pillar) and Latin 'processus' (going forth, projection).
