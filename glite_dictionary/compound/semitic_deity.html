Semitic deity

1. NOUN

/ səˈmɪt̬ɪk ˈdiːəti / (AmE), / sɪˈmɪtɪk ˈdiːəti / (BrE)
A god or goddess worshipped by the ancient Semitic peoples, such as the Babylonians, Assyrians, Hebrews, and Phoenicians.
deity worshipped by ancient Semites

Examples

The ancient Hebrews worshipped Yahweh as their principal Semitic deity.
Ba'al was a prominent Semitic deity associated with fertility and storms.
Artifacts depicting the Semitic deity Ishtar have been discovered at several archaeological sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: god - a superhuman being worshipped as having power over nature or human fortunes
  • Synonym: goddess - a female deity

Etymology

Semitic: from Semite, referring to a group of ancient peoples of the Middle East; deity: from Latin deitas (divinity).
