nitrogen mustard

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtɹədʒən ˈmʌstɚd / (AmE), / ˈnʌɪtrɪdʒən ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a toxic chemical compound structurally related to mustard gas, used especially in the treatment of cancer as a chemotherapy agent
toxic compound used in cancer chemotherapy
medical

Examples

Nitrogen mustard is used as a chemotherapy agent in the treatment of certain types of cancer.
Researchers are studying new uses for nitrogen mustard in the fight against Hodgkin's lymphoma.
Because of its toxicity, nitrogen mustard must be handled with extreme care in medical settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard gas - a toxic chemical warfare agent causing blisters

Etymology

So named because it contains nitrogen and is chemically related to mustard gas.
