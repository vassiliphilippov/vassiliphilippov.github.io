bush shrike

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ʃraɪk / (AmE), / bʊʃ ʃraɪk / (BrE)
A medium-sized, often colorful African bird of the family Malaconotidae, similar to shrikes, known for their melodious calls and bush-dwelling habits.
African bird related to shrikes

Examples

The rare bush shrike is known for its distinctive, whistling song.
We spotted a colorful bush shrike perched among the dense foliage.
Many bush shrikes are difficult to observe due to their secretive habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a predatory songbird with a hooked bill, in the family Laniidae
