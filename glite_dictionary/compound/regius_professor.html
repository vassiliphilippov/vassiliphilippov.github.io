Regius professor

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒiəs prəˈfɛsər / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒiəs prəˈfɛsə / (BrE)
the holder of a professorship at a British university created by royal patronage
holder of a royal professorship at a British university
british

Examples

She was appointed as the new Regius professor of mathematics at Oxford.
The university celebrated the 400th anniversary of its first Regius professor.
Many Regius professors have made significant contributions to their fields.

Etymology

From Latin 'regius' (royal) and 'professor' (teacher).
