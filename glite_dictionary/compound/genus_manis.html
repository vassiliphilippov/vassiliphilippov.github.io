genus Manis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmeɪ.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmæ.nɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Manidae, comprising all species of pangolins.
type genus of pangolins (Manidae)

Examples

All pangolins belong to the genus Manis, which is the only genus in the family Manidae.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Manis in Southeast Asia.
The genus Manis is characterized by its distinctive overlapping scales made of keratin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pangolin - any mammal of the family Manidae, scaly anteaters

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Manis,' the given name for this genus.
