resting potential

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstɪŋ pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪŋ pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
the electrical potential difference across the membrane of a nerve cell when it is not transmitting a nerve impulse
membrane potential of inactive nerve cell
medical

Examples

The resting potential of a neuron is essential for the generation of nerve impulses.
Damage to the cell membrane can disturb the resting potential and affect nerve function.
Scientists measure the resting potential to study how neurons communicate in the brain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: action potential - the change in electrical potential associated with the passage of an impulse along a nerve cell
