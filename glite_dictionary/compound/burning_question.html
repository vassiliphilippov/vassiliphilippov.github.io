burning question

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝː.nɪŋ ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪŋ ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
an urgent or important question that needs to be answered quickly
urgent or important question

Examples

The burning question on everyone's lips was whether the company would announce layoffs.
After the meeting, our burning question was still left unanswered.
Climate change remains a burning question for scientists and policymakers alike.

Synset Examples

His burning question about the project sparked a lively debate.
The burning question on everyone’s mind is who will win.
The burning question is whether we can meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressing issue - an urgent matter that needs immediate attention
  • Antonym: trivial question - a question of little importance

Etymology

Metaphorically uses 'burning' for intensity or urgency, combined with 'question'.
