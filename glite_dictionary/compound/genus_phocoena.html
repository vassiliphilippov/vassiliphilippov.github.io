genus Phocoena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs foʊˈsiːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fəˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small toothed whales, commonly known as porpoises, found in coastal waters of the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of porpoises

Examples

The genus Phocoena includes species such as the harbor porpoise and the vaquita.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within the genus Phocoena to improve conservation efforts.
All members of the genus Phocoena share certain skull and tooth characteristics distinguishing them from other porpoises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porpoise - a small marine mammal related to the dolphin

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name based on Greek phōkaina (porpoise).
