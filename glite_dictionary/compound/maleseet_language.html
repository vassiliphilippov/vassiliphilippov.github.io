Maleseet language

1. NOUN

/ məˈliːsiːt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məˈliːsiːt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Malecite and Passamaquoddy peoples in northeastern North America
Algonquian language of the Malecite and Passamaquoddy

Examples

The Maleseet language is still spoken by some members of the community today.
Linguists have documented many traditional stories in the Maleseet language.
Efforts are underway to preserve and teach the Maleseet language to younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maliseet - a member of an Algonquian-speaking indigenous people

Etymology

From Maliseet (self-designation: Wolastoqey), the name of the indigenous people who speak the language.
