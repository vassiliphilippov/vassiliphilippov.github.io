melodic phrase

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɑdɪk freɪz / (AmE), / məˈlɒdɪk freɪz / (BrE)
A sequence of musical notes that form a distinctive and recognizable unit within a melody.
distinctive sequence of musical notes

Examples

The violin played a beautiful melodic phrase that echoed through the hall.
She recognized the familiar melodic phrase from her favorite symphony.
The composer repeated the melodic phrase to give the piece a sense of unity.

Synset Examples

she was humming an air from Beethoven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melody - a sequence of single notes that is musically satisfying
