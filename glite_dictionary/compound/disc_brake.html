disc brake

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk breɪk / (AmE), / dɪsk breɪk / (BrE)
A type of brake in which friction is applied to both sides of a spinning disc by brake pads, usually operated hydraulically.
brake using pads on both sides of a disc

Examples

Modern cars often use disc brakes on the front wheels for more effective stopping power.
The mechanic replaced the worn disc brake pads with new ones.
Cyclists prefer bikes equipped with disc brakes for their superior performance in wet weather.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drum brake - brake that uses a drum and shoes to create friction
