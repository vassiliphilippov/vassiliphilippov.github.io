slop jar

1. NOUN

/ slɑp dʒɑr / (AmE), / slɒp dʒɑː / (BrE)
a large container, typically with a lid, used to collect waste water from a washbasin or chamber pot, especially before indoor plumbing became common
large container for waste water

Examples

In the old farmhouse, a slop jar stood discreetly behind a curtain in the bedroom.
She emptied the slop jar every morning as part of her household chores.
Before modern plumbing, every guest room had its own slop jar for convenience during the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamber pot - a bowl kept in a bedroom and used as a toilet, especially in the past
