partial abortion

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrʃəl əˈbɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl əˈbɔːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the termination of a pregnancy in which not all of the products of conception are expelled from the uterus
incomplete expulsion in abortion
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed a partial abortion and recommended further treatment to prevent infection.
Sometimes, a partial abortion requires surgical intervention to remove retained tissue.
After experiencing complications, she was treated for a partial abortion at the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomplete abortion - an abortion in which parts of the products of conception remain in the uterus
