bearing metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrɪŋ ˌmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈbeərɪŋ ˌmɛtəl / (BrE)
an alloy, often based on lead or tin, specifically used for making machine bearings due to its low friction and durability
alloy for machine bearings

Examples

The mechanic replaced the worn bearing metal to restore smooth operation to the engine.
Modern turbines require high-quality bearing metal for reliable, long-term performance.
If the bearing metal fails, severe damage to machinery can occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babbitt metal - a soft alloy used as bearing material
