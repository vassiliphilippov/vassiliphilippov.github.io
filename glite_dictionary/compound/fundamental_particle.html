fundamental particle

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˈpɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmentl ˈpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a subatomic particle that is not known to be made up of smaller components, regarded as a basic constituent of all matter
basic constituent of matter in physics

Examples

The discovery of the Higgs boson was a milestone in identifying a new fundamental particle.
Physicists debate whether the electron is truly a fundamental particle or composed of smaller elements.
String theory proposes that each fundamental particle is a different vibration of a tiny string.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elementary particle - a subatomic particle not known to have substructure
