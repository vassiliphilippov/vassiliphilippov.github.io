safety bicycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti ˌbaɪsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ˌbaɪsɪkl / (BrE)
a type of bicycle with two wheels of equal size, and pedals connected to the rear wheel via a chain and gear mechanism, designed to be safer than earlier bicycle forms
bicycle with equal wheels and chain drive

Examples

The introduction of the safety bicycle revolutionized personal transportation in the late nineteenth century.
Unlike the penny-farthing, the safety bicycle provided a more stable and comfortable ride.
Collectors often seek out antique safety bicycles for their historical significance and unique design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: penny-farthing - a type of early bicycle with a large front wheel and much smaller rear wheel
  • Synonym: bicycle - a vehicle with two wheels powered by pedals

Etymology

Named for its safer design compared to the earlier 'ordinary' or 'penny-farthing' bicycle; 'safety' highlights the innovation.
