jobsworth attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑbzˌwɜrθ ˈætɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒbzwəːθ ˈatɪtjuːd / (BrE)
An inflexible insistence on following rules and regulations, often at the expense of common sense or practicality.
inflexible rule-following behavior
british

Examples

Her jobsworth attitude made dealing with customer service almost impossible.
The manager's jobsworth attitude slowed down the entire project.
Despite the emergency, his jobsworth attitude prevented him from making an exception.

Synset Examples

Her jobsworth attitude made the process frustrating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucratic - excessively concerned with official procedure
  • Antonym: flexible - willing to change or compromise

Etymology

From 'jobsworth' (British), meaning 'a person who sticks rigidly to rules' (literally, a job is worth more than common sense), combined with 'attitude'.
