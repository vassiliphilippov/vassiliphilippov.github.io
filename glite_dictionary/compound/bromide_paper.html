bromide paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.maɪd ˈpeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.maɪd ˈpeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a type of photographic paper coated with silver bromide emulsions, producing neutral black or blue-black image tones when developed
photographic paper with silver bromide emulsion

Examples

Many classic prints from the 20th century were made using bromide paper for its rich tonal quality.
Photographers often choose bromide paper to achieve a neutral black image tone in their darkroom.
The gallery displayed several vintage photographs developed on bromide paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photographic paper - paper coated with a light-sensitive chemical for producing photographic prints

Etymology

From 'bromide', referring to the silver bromide compound used in the emulsion.
