order Piperales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər paɪˈpɛreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə paɪˈpɪəreɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of flowering plants that includes families such as Piperaceae (the pepper family), Saururaceae, and Chloranthaceae.
an order of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

The order Piperales includes the well-known black pepper plant, Piper nigrum.
Botanists study the distinctive floral structures found in the order Piperales.
Many species in the order Piperales are native to tropical and subtropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: order Laurales - a related order of flowering plants
  • Degree Lower: order Arecales - a different order of monocotyledonous flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin 'piper' (pepper) plus the suffix '-ales' used in botanical taxonomy for orders.
