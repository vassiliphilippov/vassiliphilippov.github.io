absorption spectrum

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔːrpʃən ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
A spectrum showing dark lines or bands where specific wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation have been absorbed by a medium through which the radiation has passed.
spectrum showing absorbed wavelengths

Examples

The scientist analyzed the absorption spectrum to determine which elements were present in the gas sample.
Each chemical element produces a unique absorption spectrum when light passes through it.
The experiment revealed new features in the absorption spectrum of the material at low temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emission spectrum - spectrum of light emitted by a substance

Etymology

From 'absorption' (act of absorbing) and 'spectrum' (band of colors or wavelengths).
