obedient plant

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈbiːdiənt plænt / (AmE), / əʊˈbiːdiənt plɑːnt / (BrE)
a North American perennial plant (Physostegia virginiana) with spikes of two-lipped pink or white flowers, grown in gardens for its attractive blooms
North American plant with pink or white spikes

Examples

The obedient plant brightened up the garden with its striking pink blossoms.
Gardeners appreciate the obedient plant because its flowers can be positioned in different directions.
I planted a row of obedient plants along the fence to add color in late summer.

Etymology

The common name refers to the plant's stems, which can be bent to stay in a chosen position (‘obedient’).
