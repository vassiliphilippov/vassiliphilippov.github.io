spurious wing

1. NOUN

/ ˈspjʊriəs wɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspjʊəriəs wɪŋ / (BrE)
a small tuft of stiff feathers attached to the first digit (thumb) of a bird's wing, helping with flight control
tuft of feathers on a bird's wing digit

Examples

The spurious wing helps birds make precise adjustments during slow flight or landing.
Some species have a very prominent spurious wing that can be easily observed up close.
The alula, also known as the spurious wing, is crucial for preventing stalls in flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alula - the small projection on the front edge of a bird's wing, also called the 'bastard wing'
