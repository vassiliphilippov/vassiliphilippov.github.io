banker's bill

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkərz bɪl / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkəz bɪl / (BrE)
a piece of paper money, especially one issued by a central bank
paper money from a central bank

Examples

The collector proudly displayed a rare banker's bill from the early twentieth century.
He received payment in the form of a banker's bill rather than coins.
The museum's exhibit included several banker's bills from different countries.

Synset Examples

he peeled off five one-thousand-zloty notes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money issued by a bank, legal tender
  • Antonym: coin - metal money as opposed to paper money
