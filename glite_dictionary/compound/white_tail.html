white tail

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt teɪl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt teɪl / (BrE)
A common North American deer (Odocoileus virginianus) whose tail has a white underside, often called the white-tailed deer.
North American deer with white tail underside

Examples

A young white tail crossed the road in front of our car early this morning.
Hunters often look for the distinctive white tail as the deer bounds away.
The white tail population has increased in many suburban areas in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-tailed deer - another term for the white tail, Odocoileus virginianus
