clerid beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrɪd ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a beetle of the family Cleridae, typically predatory on other insects and often brightly colored or metallic
a predatory beetle of the family Cleridae

Examples

A clerid beetle quickly hunted down the smaller insect on the tree bark.
Scientists released a colony of clerid beetles to control the pest population in the forest.
The metallic blue sheen of the clerid beetle made it easy to spot among the leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetle - an insect with a hard shell-like wing case

Etymology

From the family name Cleridae, derived from Greek klēridios (a kind of insect).
