pleuropneumonialike organism

1. NOUN

/ ˌplʊroʊnuˌmoʊniəlaɪk ˈɔrɡənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌplʊərəʊnjuːˌməʊniaʊlaɪk ˈɔːɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
a type of mycoplasma bacterium, resistant to antibiotics, that causes a form of pneumonia in humans
antibiotic-resistant bacterium causing pneumonia
medical

Examples

Some forms of pneumonia are caused by pleuropneumonialike organisms that can be difficult to treat with standard antibiotics.
Researchers are studying how pleuropneumonialike organisms resist commonly used medications.
The patient was diagnosed with a rare infection due to a pleuropneumonialike organism.

Etymology

From 'pleuropneumonia' (a disease affecting the lungs) and 'like', referencing resemblance to organisms causing pleuropneumonia in cattle.
