Japanese sumac

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / dʒəˈpəniːz ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
A small Asian tree that yields a toxic sap used to make lacquer.
Asian tree used for lacquer

Examples

The sap of the Japanese sumac is traditionally used to produce high-quality lacquerware.
Many artisans value the Japanese sumac for its unique resin.
Contact with the Japanese sumac can cause skin reactions due to its toxic exudate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquer tree - tree from which lacquer is obtained
