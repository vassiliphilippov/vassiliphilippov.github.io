Black Hand

1. NOUN

/ blæk hænd / (AmE), / blæk hænd / (BrE)
a secret criminal or terrorist society active in the United States in the early 20th century, infamous for extortion and violent crimes within Italian-American communities
criminal society in early 20th-century America

Examples

The police worked hard to dismantle the Black Hand and restore peace to the neighborhood.
Many immigrants lived in fear of the Black Hand because of its notorious extortion demands.
Reports of the Black Hand's criminal activities filled the newspapers in the early 1900s.

Etymology

From Italian 'La Mano Nera' (the Black Hand), referring to the society's use of threatening notes bearing a black hand symbol.
