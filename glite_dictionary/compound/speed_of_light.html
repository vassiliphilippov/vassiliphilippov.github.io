speed of light

1. NOUN

/ spid əv laɪt / (AmE), / spiːd əv laɪt / (BrE)
the constant speed at which light travels in a vacuum, exactly 299,792,458 meters per second
the speed at which light travels in a vacuum

Examples

Nothing in the universe can travel faster than the speed of light.
The theory of relativity is based on the constancy of the speed of light.
Scientists have measured the speed of light with incredible precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light speed - the speed at which light travels; used interchangeably

2. NOUN

/ spiːd əv laɪt / (AmE), / spiːd əv laɪt / (BrE)
the maximum speed at which light travels in a vacuum, often used figuratively to mean extremely fast movement or progress
the fastest possible speed (literal and figurative)

Examples

Thanks to technology, information can now be shared at the speed of light.
When the news broke, rumors spread at the speed of light.
He finished the assignment at the speed of light, impressing his teacher.

Synset Examples

Information travels at the speed of light these days.
The train moved at the speed of light, or so it felt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light speed - alternate term for speed of light
