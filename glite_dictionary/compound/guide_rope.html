guide rope

1. NOUN

/ ɡaɪd roʊp / (AmE), / ɡaɪd rəʊp / (BrE)
A rope attached to a load, especially when lifted by a crane, used to control or guide the movement of the load.
rope for steering crane loads

Examples

The workers used a guide rope to prevent the steel beam from spinning as it was lifted.
Always hold the guide rope firmly when directing the load into position.
A snapped guide rope can lead to dangerous swinging of the suspended cargo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guy rope - a rope used for stabilizing a tent, mast, or other structure
