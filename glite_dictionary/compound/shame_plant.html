shame plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪm ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪm ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a tropical plant (Mimosa pudica) with sensitive leaves that fold and droop when touched or at night, native to tropical America and Australia
tropical plant with sensitive, folding leaves

Examples

The shame plant quickly closed its leaves when I touched it during our trip to the rainforest.
Many gardens grow the shame plant for its fascinating reaction to movement.
Children are often amazed by how the shame plant responds to a gentle tap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mimosa pudica - the botanical name of the shame plant
