reconstructive surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.tɪv ˈsɝː.dʒə.ri / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.tɪv ˈsɜː.dʒəri / (BrE)
Surgery that restores the form and function of body tissues, often after injury, disease, or for cosmetic reasons.
surgery to repair or restore tissues
medical

Examples

After the accident, she required reconstructive surgery to repair damage to her face.
Advances in reconstructive surgery have helped countless burn victims regain their appearance and function.
Some patients choose reconstructive surgery following the removal of a tumor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plastic surgery - branch of surgery focused on repairing and reconstructing tissue
  • Degree Lower: cosmetic surgery - surgery to improve appearance
