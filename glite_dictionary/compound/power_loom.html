power loom

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər luːm / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə luːm / (BrE)
a machine for weaving cloth in which the weaving process is operated by mechanical power rather than by hand
mechanically operated weaving machine

Examples

The introduction of the power loom revolutionized the textile industry in the nineteenth century.
A single worker could oversee several power looms operating at once.
Factory tours demonstrated how fabric was woven quickly using a power loom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loom - a device for weaving thread or yarn into textiles
