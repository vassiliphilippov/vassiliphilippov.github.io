genus Aramus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʤiːnəs əˈræməs / (AmE), / ˈʤiːnəs əˈræməs / (BrE)
A genus of large brown long-billed wading birds found in warm swampy regions of the Western Hemisphere, including the limpkin.
genus of wading birds, includes the limpkin

Examples

The genus Aramus is represented by a single living species, the limpkin.
Researchers studied the habitat of birds within the genus Aramus in Florida's wetlands.
The distinctive call of birds from the genus Aramus can be heard in swampy regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpkin - a large brown wading bird of the genus Aramus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Aramus', the scientific name for this bird genus.
