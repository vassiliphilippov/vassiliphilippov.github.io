charnel house

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrnəl haʊs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnəl haʊs / (BrE)
A building or vault where the bodies or bones of the dead are stored.
building for storing corpses or bones

Examples

The monastery included a charnel house where monks' bones were kept after burial.
Historians examined the ancient charnel house to learn about funeral customs.
A sense of unease filled the air as they entered the abandoned charnel house.

Etymology

From Old French charnel (from Latin carnālis, from caro ‘flesh’) + house.
