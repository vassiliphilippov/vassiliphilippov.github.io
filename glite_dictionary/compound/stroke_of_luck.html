stroke of luck

1. NOUN

/ stroʊk əv lʌk / (AmE), / strəʊk əv lʌk / (BrE)
an unexpected event or circumstance that brings good fortune
unexpected good fortune

Examples

Finding the perfect apartment on my first day of searching was a real stroke of luck.
They scored the winning goal by a stroke of luck in the final minute of the match.
Missing the train turned out to be a stroke of luck, because I ran into an old friend at the station.

Synset Examples

Winning the lottery was a stroke of luck for him.
He claimed his new invention was a stroke of luck.
It was a stroke of luck that I found my lost wallet.
She had a stroke of luck when she got the job offer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - a sudden, unexpected gain or piece of good fortune
  • Antonym: bad luck - an instance of misfortune or adversity
