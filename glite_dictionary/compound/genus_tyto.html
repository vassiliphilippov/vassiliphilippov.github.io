genus Tyto

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtaɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtɪtəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of owls in the family Tytonidae, commonly known as barn owls, characterized by a heart-shaped facial disc.
genus of barn owls (Tytonidae)

Examples

The distinctive barn owl, a member of the genus Tyto, is easily recognized by its pale face.
Scientists have conducted genetic studies on several species within the genus Tyto.
Many species in the genus Tyto are found on islands around the world.

Etymology

From New Latin Tyto, the genus name, from Greek tytō (an owl).
