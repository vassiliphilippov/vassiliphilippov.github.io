wax light

1. NOUN

/ wæks laɪt / (AmE), / wæks laɪt / (BrE)
A stick of wax with a wick in the center, used for lighting; a candle.
stick of wax with a wick; candle
archaic

Examples

She placed a wax light on the table, its gentle glow filling the room.
In the old chapel, only a single wax light illuminated the altar.
He lit a wax light to guide his way through the darkened hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candle - a stick or block of wax with a wick for burning

Etymology

wax from Old English 'weax' (wax) + light (source of illumination).
