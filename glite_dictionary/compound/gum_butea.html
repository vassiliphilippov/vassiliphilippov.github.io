gum butea

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm buːˈteɪ.ə / (AmE), / ɡʌm bjuːˈtɪə / (BrE)
a dried resinous juice obtained from the dhak tree (Butea monosperma), used especially as an astringent
resin from the dhak tree, used as an astringent

Examples

The herbalist recommended gum butea for its soothing properties on inflamed skin.
In traditional medicine, gum butea has been prized for its astringent effects.
They collected gum butea from the dhak trees to prepare a topical ointment.

Etymology

From 'gum' (resin) and the genus name Butea, from the scientific name Butea monosperma.
