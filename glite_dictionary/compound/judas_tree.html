Judas tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːdəs triː / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəs triː / (BrE)
A small tree of the eastern Mediterranean, Cercis siliquastrum, with masses of purplish-red flowers that grow directly from the trunk and branches before the leaves appear; widely cultivated for its decorative value, and its wood is used for veneers.
Mediterranean tree with pink-red flowers

Examples

The Judas tree bursts into vivid bloom every spring before its leaves emerge.
Many gardens in southern Europe feature the Judas tree for its striking flowers.
According to legend, Judas Iscariot hanged himself from a Judas tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redbud - North American trees of the Cercis genus with similar flowers

Etymology

Named after Judas Iscariot, who, according to legend, hanged himself from this type of tree.
