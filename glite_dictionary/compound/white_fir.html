white fir

1. NOUN

/ waɪt fɜr / (AmE), / waɪt fɜː / (BrE)
a medium to tall fir tree of western North America, with a conical crown and tiered branches, whose crushed leaves have an orange scent
a North American fir tree species

Examples

The white fir is commonly found in the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.
She recognized the tree by the scent of its crushed needles and identified it as a white fir.
Many birds nest in the branches of the white fir in the national forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam fir - a North American fir tree with aromatic foliage; another fir species
  • Synonym: Douglas fir - a North American evergreen tree, not a true fir but often associated

2. NOUN

/ waɪt fɝː / (AmE), / waɪt fɜː / (BrE)
a medium to tall evergreen tree (Abies concolor) of central and western North America, with soft wood and a narrow, upright crown
a North American fir tree (Abies concolor)

Examples

The forest was dominated by white fir and ponderosa pine.
We planted a young white fir in our backyard to provide shade.
The lumber from white fir is often used for construction and furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - an evergreen tree of the genus Abies
