trimmer arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmər ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmə ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch constructed between trimmers in a floor to support the weight of a hearth or fireplace opening
arch supporting a hearth between floor trimmers

Examples

The old house featured a trimmer arch beneath the fireplace to ensure its stability.
Before installing the new hearth, builders reinforced the floor with a trimmer arch.
A mason explained how the trimmer arch distributes the fireplace's weight across the joists.

Etymology

From 'trimmer' (a beam in the floor supporting the ends of joists around an opening) + 'arch'.
