genus Sargassum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sɑrˈɡæsəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɑːˈɡasəm / (BrE)
A genus of brown seaweed found in tropical and temperate oceans, especially known for floating species that form large masses like the Sargasso Sea.
a genus of brown seaweed

Examples

Several species within the genus Sargassum create large floating mats in the Sargasso Sea.
Researchers are studying the ecological impact of the genus Sargassum on coastal communities.
The genus Sargassum includes both free-floating and attached species of brown algae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sargassum - the brown seaweed of the genus Sargassum

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, origin) and 'Sargassum', the New Latin genus name, from Portuguese 'sargaço' (seaweed).
