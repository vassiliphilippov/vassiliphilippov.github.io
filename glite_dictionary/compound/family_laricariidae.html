family Laricariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌlərɪˌkɛəriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌlarɪˌkɛːrɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of armored catfish native to freshwater habitats in Central and South America, known for their bony plates and sucker mouths.
a family of armored catfish

Examples

The family Laricariidae includes many popular aquarium species known for cleaning algae.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of the family Laricariidae in the Amazon Basin.
Members of the family Laricariidae have evolved distinctive sucker mouths for clinging to rocks in fast-flowing streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored catfish - common name for species of family Laricariidae

Etymology

From New Latin Laricariidae, named after the type genus Loricaria, from Latin 'lorica' (breastplate).
