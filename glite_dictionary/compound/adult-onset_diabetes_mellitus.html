adult-onset diabetes mellitus

1. NOUN

/ əˌdʌlt ˌɑnˌsɛt daɪəˌbitiz məˈlaɪtəs / (AmE), / əˌdʌlt ˌɒnˌsɛt daɪəˌbiːtiːz məˈlaɪtəs / (BrE)
A mild form of diabetes mellitus that develops gradually in adults, typically managed with diet and oral medication rather than insulin injections.
type of diabetes developing in adults
medical

Examples

Managing adult-onset diabetes mellitus often requires significant changes in daily eating habits.
The doctor explained that adult-onset diabetes mellitus can be controlled with oral medication in many cases.
Obesity increases the risk of developing adult-onset diabetes mellitus later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 2 diabetes - chronic condition affecting glucose metabolism, typically developing in adulthood
  • Antonym: juvenile diabetes - type 1 diabetes mellitus, usually developing in children or adolescents

Etymology

From Latin 'diabetes' (passing through) and modern medical terminology; 'adult-onset' refers to occurrence in adulthood.
