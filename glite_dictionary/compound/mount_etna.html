Mount Etna

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈɛtnə / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈɛtnə / (BrE)
a large active volcano on the east coast of Sicily, Italy, and the highest volcano in Europe
Europe's highest active volcano in Sicily

Examples

Mount Etna is one of the most active volcanoes in the world, attracting scientists and tourists alike.
The eruption of Mount Etna in 2002 caused widespread ash clouds across eastern Sicily.
Many villages at the foot of Mount Etna are shaped by the volcano's frequent activity.

Etymology

From Latin 'Aetna', originally from Greek 'Aitna', name of the mountain.
