electric power

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trɪk ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
the rate at which electrical energy is transferred by an electric circuit, equal to the product of voltage and current
rate of energy transfer in an electric circuit

Examples

The factory required a constant supply of electric power to operate its machinery.
She studied how electric power is generated and distributed across the national grid.
A surge in electric power can damage sensitive electronic devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watt - unit of power in the International System of Units
