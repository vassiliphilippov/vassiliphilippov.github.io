family Orycteropodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli ɔːrɪktəˌrɒpəˌdaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɒrɪktəˌrɒpɒˈdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing, insectivorous mammals represented by the aardvark, native to Africa.
aardvark family of mammals

Examples

The family Orycteropodidae includes only one living species, the aardvark.
Researchers studied the evolution of burrowing behaviors in the family Orycteropodidae.
The unique dentition of the family Orycteropodidae distinguishes it from other mammalian families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardvark - a nocturnal African mammal with a tubular snout, the only living member of this family

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Orycteropus' (genus name for the aardvark) + '-idae' (standard zoological suffix for animal families).
