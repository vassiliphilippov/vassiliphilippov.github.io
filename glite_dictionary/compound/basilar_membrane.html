basilar membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsələr ˈmɛmbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈbæzɪlə ˈmembreɪn / (BrE)
a thin, stiff membrane in the cochlea of the inner ear that supports the organ of Corti and plays a key role in hearing by transmitting sound vibrations
membrane in the cochlea supporting the organ of Corti
medical

Examples

Damage to the basilar membrane can lead to permanent hearing loss.
The vibrations from sound waves travel along the basilar membrane in the cochlea.
Researchers studied how the basilar membrane responds to different frequencies of sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cochlea - spiral-shaped cavity forming a division of the inner ear
  • Synonym: organ of Corti - structure in the cochlea containing hair cells for detecting sound

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base) and modern Latin 'membrana' (membrane).
