bad hat

1. NOUN

/ bæd hæt / (AmE), / bæd hæt / (BrE)
a person who deliberately causes trouble or engages in dishonest behavior
troublemaker or dishonest person

Examples

They warned me to avoid him, saying he was a real bad hat around town.
In the novel, the villain is described as a notorious bad hat who brings chaos wherever he goes.
The school has no tolerance for bad hats intent on disrupting classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troublemaker - a person who habitually causes trouble
  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or dishonest person

Etymology

Possibly from 19th-century slang, where 'hat' referred metonymically to a person.
