dung beetle

1. NOUN

/ dʌŋ ˈbit̬əl / (AmE), / dʌŋ ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a beetle that feeds on dung and rolls it into balls, often laying its eggs inside the dung balls
beetle that feeds on and rolls dung

Examples

A dung beetle can roll a ball of dung many times its own weight.
Ancient Egyptians considered the dung beetle to be a symbol of rebirth and transformation.
We watched as a dung beetle expertly maneuvered its ball of dung across the dirt path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarab - a beetle, especially one associated with dung, from the Scarabaeidae family
