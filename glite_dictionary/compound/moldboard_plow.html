moldboard plow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldˌbɔrd plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldbɔːd plaʊ / (BrE)
a type of plow with a curved metal blade (moldboard) that turns over soil to prepare it for planting
plow with a curved blade for turning soil

Examples

The farmer used a moldboard plow to prepare the field for spring planting.
Early American settlers relied on the moldboard plow to break tough prairie soils.
By replacing the old plow with a modern moldboard plow, efficiency on the farm improved dramatically.
