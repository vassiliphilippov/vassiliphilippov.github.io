family Roridulaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli roʊˌrɪdjuːˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli rɒˌrɪdjʊˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
A small family of carnivorous flowering plants native to South Africa, sometimes classified within the family Droseraceae.
plant family of carnivorous South African plants

Examples

The genus Roridula is the only member of the family Roridulaceae.
Botanists debate whether to include the family Roridulaceae in Droseraceae.
Plants in the family Roridulaceae are known for their sticky, insect-trapping leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Droseraceae - the sundew family of carnivorous plants

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from the genus name Roridula + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
