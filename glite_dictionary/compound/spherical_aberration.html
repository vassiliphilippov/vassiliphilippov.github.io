spherical aberration

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈrɪkəl əˈbɛrˌeɪʃən / (AmE), / sfɪˈrɪk.jʊ.lər əˈbɛr.eɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an optical aberration in which light rays passing through the edges of a spherical lens or mirror are focused at different points, resulting in a blurred or distorted image
optical distortion in lenses or mirrors

Examples

Optical telescopes are often affected by spherical aberration, leading to blurry images of distant stars.
The manufacturer designed the lens to minimize spherical aberration and improve image clarity.
Correcting spherical aberration is essential for producing high-quality microscope lenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromatic aberration - an optical aberration causing color fringes due to different wavelengths focusing at different points

Etymology

From 'spherical' (having the shape of a sphere) + 'aberration' (deviation from the norm), used in optics since the 19th century.
