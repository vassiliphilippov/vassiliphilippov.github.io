giant chinkapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (BrE)
a small ornamental evergreen tree native to the Pacific Coast, with glossy yellow-green leaves that are yellow on the underside, and edible nuts
Pacific Coast evergreen tree with edible nuts

Examples

The giant chinkapin is valued for its beautiful foliage and edible nuts.
They planted a giant chinkapin in the garden to attract wildlife.
Hikers along the Pacific Coast often spot the giant chinkapin growing in the wild.
