self-deletion function

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf dɪˈliʃən ˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf dɪˈliːʃən ˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A feature, especially in digital systems, that deletes files, messages, or data automatically after a set condition is met.
automatic content-removal feature (technology)

Examples

Our chat app uses a self-deletion function to erase messages after 24 hours.
After enabling the self-deletion function, her documents were securely removed from the system.
Some email services offer a self-deletion function for sensitive correspondence.

Synset Examples

She triggered the self-deletion function by mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auto-delete - feature that automatically deletes content without user intervention
  • Antonym: manual deletion - deleting content through user action rather than automatically
