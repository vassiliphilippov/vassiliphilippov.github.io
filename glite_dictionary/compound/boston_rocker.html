Boston rocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑstən ˈrɑkər / (AmE), / ˈbɒstən ˈrɒkə / (BrE)
a type of rocking chair with a high spindle back and a decorative top panel, often associated with 19th-century American design
rocking chair with high spindle back and decorative top

Examples

Her grandmother's antique Boston rocker sat by the living room window.
He restored an old Boston rocker he found at a flea market.
The museum displayed a beautiful 19th-century Boston rocker among other period furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocking chair - a chair mounted on rockers for rocking back and forth

Etymology

Named after the city of Boston, where this style became popular in the early 19th century.
