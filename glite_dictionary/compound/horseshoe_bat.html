horseshoe bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌʃu bæ t / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsʃuː bæt / (BrE)
A bat of the family Rhinolophidae, characterized by a horseshoe-shaped fold of skin on the nose.
bat with horseshoe-shaped nose skin

Examples

The horseshoe bat navigates the night using its sophisticated echolocation abilities.
Scientists have discovered a new species of horseshoe bat in the remote cave system.
A horseshoe bat can be identified by the distinctive leaf-shaped structure on its nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal, flying mammal

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌʃu bæt / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsʃuː bæt / (BrE)
Any of several bats of the family Hipposideridae found in Africa, the Philippines, or Australia, characterized by a horseshoe-shaped structure on the nose.
bat with a horseshoe-shaped nasal leaf

Examples

The horseshoe bat is easily identified by the unique shape of its nose.
Scientists have studied horseshoe bats to understand their use of sonar for navigation.
A colony of horseshoe bats was discovered deep within the cave system.
