alkali bee

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kə.laɪ ˌbiː / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪ ˌbiː / (BrE)
a solitary bee native to the western United States, valued for its role in pollinating alfalfa crops
a bee species that pollinates alfalfa

Examples

The alkali bee is often used by farmers to improve alfalfa seed production.
Because the alkali bee prefers salty soils, it thrives in certain agricultural regions of the western U.S.
Unlike honeybees, the alkali bee is a solitary pollinator that nests underground.
