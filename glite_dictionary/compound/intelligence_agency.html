intelligence agency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an organization responsible for collecting and analyzing information about foreign governments or potential threats, typically for national security purposes
organization that gathers secret information

Examples

The CIA is a prominent intelligence agency based in the United States.
She worked for an intelligence agency gathering information on cyber threats.
Several countries cooperated by sharing reports from their intelligence agencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy agency - an organization engaged in espionage
  • Synonym: secret service - a government agency engaging in espionage or security
