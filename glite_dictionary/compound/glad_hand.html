glad hand

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæd hænd / (AmE), / ɡlæd hænd / (BrE)
A warm or enthusiastic welcome or greeting, sometimes given insincerely for effect or advantage.
an enthusiastic (sometimes insincere) welcome or greeting

Examples

The politician gave everyone a glad hand as he walked through the crowd.
She greeted her rival with a glad hand, though her smile was forced.
Many guests received the CEO's glad hand, but few believed his warmth was genuine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welcome - the act of greeting someone in a friendly way
  • Antonym: snub - to ignore or treat someone coldly
