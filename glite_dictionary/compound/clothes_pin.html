clothes pin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊðz ˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈkləʊðz ˌpɪn / (BrE)
a small clip, typically made of wood or plastic, used to attach clothes to a clothesline while drying
fastener for attaching clothes to a clothesline

Examples

She used a clothes pin to keep her shirt from blowing away in the wind.
There is a basket full of clothes pins by the laundry line.
After washing the sheets, he hung them up with a clothes pin on each corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peg - a British term meaning a device for securing clothes to a line
