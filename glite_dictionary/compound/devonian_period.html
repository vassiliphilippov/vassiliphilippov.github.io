Devonian period

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvoʊniən ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊniən ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the geological period from about 419 to 359 million years ago, marked by the dominance of fishes and the first appearance of amphibians and ammonites
geological period known for fish dominance

Examples

The Devonian period was a time of significant evolutionary developments in marine life.
During the Devonian period, early amphibians began to transition from water to land.
Paleontologists have discovered well-preserved fossils from the Devonian period in several parts of the world.

Etymology

From Latin 'Devonia' (Devon), named after Devon, England, where rocks from this period were first studied.
