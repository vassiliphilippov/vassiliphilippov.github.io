genus Porphyrula

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpɔːrfəˈruːlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpɔːfɪˈruːlə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family, including the American purple gallinules.
biological genus of purple gallinules

Examples

The genus Porphyrula comprises species known for their vibrant purple and green plumage.
Researchers studied the habitat of birds within the genus Porphyrula across Central and South America.
The American purple gallinule, a member of the genus Porphyrula, is admired for its striking colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Porphyrula - the genus including purple gallinules

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Porphyrula', diminutive of Greek 'porphyra' meaning 'purple', referring to the bird's plumage.
