genus Mergus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmɜːrɡəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmɜːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish-eating ducks, commonly known as mergansers, characterized by slender, serrated bills.
a genus of fish-eating ducks (mergansers)

Examples

The genus Mergus includes several species of diving ducks known for their narrow, serrated bills.
Researchers observed a decline in the population of birds belonging to the genus Mergus in the region.
The red-breasted merganser is one of the most widely distributed members of the genus Mergus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merganser - a fish-eating duck with a slender, serrated bill

Etymology

From Latin *mergus* (diver, water bird).
