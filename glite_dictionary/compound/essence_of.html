essence of

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛsəns ʌv / (AmE), / ˈɛsəns ɒv / (BrE)
the most important or fundamental quality or nature of something; what makes something what it is
most important quality of something

Examples

The chef's new dish truly captured the essence of traditional French cuisine.
The film manages to convey the essence of childhood innocence.
This book explores the essence of leadership in a modern context.

Synset Examples

The artist captured the essence of the nymph of the pavement in her mural.
The artwork reflects the essence of the kingdom of Pisces.
Her line drawing captured the essence of the landscape beautifully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something
  • Synonym: substance - the essential nature or most important quality of something

Etymology

From Latin 'essentia' (being, essence) and English 'of.'
