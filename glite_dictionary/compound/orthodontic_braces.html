orthodontic braces

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrθoʊˈdɑntɪk ˈbreɪsɪz / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈdɒntɪk ˈbreɪsɪz / (BrE)
a dental appliance made of wires and brackets used to correct irregularities in the alignment of teeth
dental appliance for aligning teeth
medical

Examples

The dentist recommended orthodontic braces to help straighten her teeth.
Many teenagers wear orthodontic braces to improve their smiles.
After two years, his orthodontic braces were finally removed, revealing perfectly aligned teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braces - dental device for correcting teeth alignment

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) and 'odous' (tooth), combined with 'braces' (supports).
