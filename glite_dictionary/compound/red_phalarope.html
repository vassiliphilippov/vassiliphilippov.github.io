red phalarope

1. NOUN

/ rɛd fəˈlɛroʊp / (AmE), / rɛd fəˈlærəʊp / (BrE)
A small migratory wading bird (Phalaropus fulicarius) found in northern oceans and lakes, known for its striking red breeding plumage.
a migratory wading bird of northern oceans

Examples

A flock of red phalaropes was spotted feeding near the Arctic coastline.
The red phalarope undergoes a remarkable plumage change during the breeding season.
Birdwatchers travel to northern lakes in summer hoping to see the elusive red phalarope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phalarope - a small shorebird of the family Phalaropodidae

Etymology

From 'red' (referring to breeding plumage) and 'phalarope', from Greek phalaris (coot) + pous (foot).
