assault gun

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɔlt ɡʌn / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt ɡʌn / (BrE)
an armored vehicle equipped with a large gun, mounted on a tank chassis without a turret, used to support infantry and as an antitank weapon
armored vehicle with a large mounted gun

Examples

The army deployed an assault gun to provide heavy support for the advancing infantry.
During the battle, the assault gun targeted enemy tanks from a concealed position.
Many World War II movies feature scenes with assault guns supporting soldiers in urban combat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-propelled gun - armored vehicle with a large gun, designed for mobile fire support

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɔlt ɡʌn / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt ɡʌn / (BrE)
a type of automatic or semiautomatic rifle with a large magazine, designed primarily for military use
military automatic or semiautomatic rifle

Examples

The soldier was equipped with an assault gun and extra ammunition for the mission.
Strict regulations govern the civilian ownership of an assault gun in many countries.
During the demonstration, the museum displayed a restored World War II assault gun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault rifle - a lightweight automatic rifle designed for military use

Etymology

From 'assault' (attack) + 'gun'; popularized in 20th-century military terminology.
