temporal cortex

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl ˈkɔrtɛks / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex located inside the temples, involved in auditory processing and memory
region of the brain near the temples
medical

Examples

Scientists are studying the temporal cortex to better understand how we perceive language.
Damage to the temporal cortex can affect memory and auditory processing.
The left temporal cortex plays a major role in understanding spoken words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parietal cortex - part of the cerebral cortex at the upper back area of the brain
  • Synonym: frontal cortex - the anterior part of the cerebral cortex, associated with reasoning and movement
  • Synonym: occipital cortex - the rearmost part of the cerebral cortex, associated with visual processing

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of the temples) and 'cortex' (bark, rind, later used for brain's outer layer).
