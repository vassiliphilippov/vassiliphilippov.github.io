icterus neonatorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪktərəs ˌnioʊˌneɪəˈtɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈɪktərəs ˌniːəʊˌnɛəˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
A yellowish discoloration of the skin and eyes in newborn infants, usually resolving on its own after a few days.
jaundice in newborn infants
medical

Examples

The pediatrician assured us that icterus neonatorum typically fades within a week after birth.
Phototherapy is sometimes used if icterus neonatorum becomes pronounced or prolonged.
Because icterus neonatorum is common, parents are often advised simply to monitor their baby's color and feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaundice - yellowish discoloration of the skin or eyes due to bilirubin; general term

Etymology

From Latin 'icterus' (jaundice) and 'neonatorum' (of the newborn).
