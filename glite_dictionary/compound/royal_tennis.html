royal tennis

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl ˈtɛn.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl ˈtɛn.ɪs / (BrE)
an ancient game, regarded as the precursor of modern lawn tennis, played in an enclosed four-walled court with rules distinct from lawn tennis.
ancient precursor of lawn tennis played indoors

Examples

He became fascinated with royal tennis after reading about its royal history.
Unlike lawn tennis, royal tennis is played indoors on a court with complex walls and slopes.
Many historic English estates still have royal tennis courts in use today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: real tennis - the traditional indoor racket sport from which lawn tennis developed

Etymology

From the association of the sport with royal courts in France and England.
