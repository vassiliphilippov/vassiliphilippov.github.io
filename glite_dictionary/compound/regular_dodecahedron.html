regular dodecahedron

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛgjələr doʊˌdɛkəˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˈrɛgjʊlə dəʊˌdɛkəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
a dodecahedron whose twelve faces are congruent regular pentagons
dodecahedron with regular pentagonal faces

Examples

The regular dodecahedron is one of the five Platonic solids.
Children constructed a model of a regular dodecahedron during the geometry lesson.
A regular dodecahedron can be inscribed in a sphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dodecahedron - polyhedron with twelve faces
  • Degree Higher: regular icosahedron - polyhedron with twenty regular triangular faces

Etymology

From Greek 'dōdeka-' (twelve) and '-hedron' (face or base).
