invasion of Iwo

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveɪʒən əv ˈiːwoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪʒən əv ˈiːwəʊ / (BrE)
the military operation in February and March 1945 during which American forces landed on and captured the island of Iwo Jima from Japanese defenders in World War II
World War II battle on Iwo Jima island

Examples

The invasion of Iwo marked a significant turning point in the Pacific theater of World War II.
During the invasion of Iwo, both American and Japanese forces suffered heavy casualties.
Many historians study the strategies used in the invasion of Iwo to understand amphibious warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Iwo Jima - the battle for control of Iwo Jima in 1945
