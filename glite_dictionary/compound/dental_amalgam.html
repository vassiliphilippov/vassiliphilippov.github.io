dental amalgam

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛntəl əˈmælɡəm / (AmE), / ˈdɛntəl əˈmalɡəm / (BrE)
an alloy of mercury with another metal, typically silver, used by dentists to fill cavities in teeth
alloy used to fill teeth cavities
medical

Examples

The dentist used dental amalgam to fill the cavity in her molar.
Research has examined the safety of dental amalgam in dental treatments.
Decades ago, most fillings were made from dental amalgam, but alternatives are now available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amalgam - an alloy of mercury with another metal

Etymology

amalgam: from Middle French *amalgamer*, from Medieval Latin *amalgama* (an alloy of mercury).
