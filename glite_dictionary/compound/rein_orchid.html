rein orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪn ˌɔr.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈreɪn ˌɔː.kɪd / (BrE)
Any of several American wildflowers in the genus Platanthera, characterized by a kidney-shaped lip on the flower.
American wildflower with kidney-shaped lip

Examples

We spotted a rare rein orchid blooming near the creek during our hike.
The rein orchid is known for its delicate greenish-white flowers and sweet fragrance.
Several species of rein orchid can be found throughout North America.

Etymology

So called from the long, slender spur of the flower resembling a rein.
