tallow oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæloʊ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtæləʊ ɔɪl / (BrE)
an animal oil obtained by pressing tallow, used as a lubricant or in industrial applications
oil pressed from tallow, used industrially

Examples

Tallow oil is commonly used as a lubricant in heavy machinery due to its high viscosity.
The factory switched to tallow oil for its cooling systems because of its cost-effectiveness.
Some soap manufacturers use tallow oil as a primary ingredient in their products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tallow - hard animal fat used for making candles, soap, and lubricants

Etymology

From "tallow" (solid animal fat) and "oil".
