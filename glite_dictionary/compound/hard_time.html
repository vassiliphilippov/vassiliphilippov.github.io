hard time

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd taɪm / (AmE), / hɑːd taɪm / (BrE)
a period of difficulty or trouble that requires effort to overcome
period of difficulty

Examples

She had a hard time adjusting to her new job, but eventually things improved.
We're going through a hard time financially, but we're confident it will get better.
He gave me a hard time about missing the meeting, but I explained my situation.

Synset Examples

we had a hard time getting here
analysts predicted rough sledding for handset makers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficult time - a period involving hardship or struggle
  • Antonym: easy time - a period with no significant difficulty
