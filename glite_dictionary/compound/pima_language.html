Pima language

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːmə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpiːmə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Pima people in parts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico
language of the Pima people

Examples

Few people today are fluent in the Pima language, although efforts to revitalize it continue.
A dictionary of the Pima language was published to help preserve the tribe's cultural heritage.
She learned the Pima language to communicate with elders in the community.

Etymology

Pima, name of the people, from O'odham "pi 'añi mac" (‘I don't know’), which was misunderstood by Spanish explorers as their ethnic name.
