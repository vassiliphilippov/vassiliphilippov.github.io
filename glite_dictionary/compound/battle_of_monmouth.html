Battle of Monmouth

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəl əv ˈmɑnməθ / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈmɒnməθ / (BrE)
a significant battle fought in New Jersey during the American Revolution in 1778, which ended with a British withdrawal
American Revolutionary War battle (1778) in New Jersey

Examples

The Battle of Monmouth was notable for its extreme heat and the endurance of both armies.
Many historians consider the Battle of Monmouth a turning point in the Revolutionary War.
Washington's leadership during the Battle of Monmouth enhanced his reputation among his troops.
