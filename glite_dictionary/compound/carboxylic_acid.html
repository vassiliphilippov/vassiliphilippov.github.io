carboxylic acid

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˌbɑkˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / kɑːˌbɒkˈsɪlɪk ˈasid / (BrE)
an organic compound containing one or more carboxyl (–COOH) groups, which makes it acidic
organic acid with carboxyl group
medical

Examples

Acetic acid is the simplest example of a carboxylic acid found in everyday life.
The presence of a carboxylic acid group significantly affects the properties of the molecule.
Chemists often study reactions involving carboxylic acids in organic synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fatty acid - a type of carboxylic acid with a long aliphatic chain
  • Synonym: acetic acid - a common example of a carboxylic acid, found in vinegar

Etymology

From 'carboxyl' (the carboxyl group, –COOH) and 'acid'.
