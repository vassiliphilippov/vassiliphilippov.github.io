rule of morphology

1. NOUN

/ ruːl əv mɔrˈfɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ruːl əv mɔːˈfɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a linguistic rule that governs the formation and structure of words in a language
linguistic rule for word formation
medical

Examples

The plural '-s' in English is a common rule of morphology that adds 's' or 'es' to form plurals.
Teachers often explain the rule of morphology that governs the transformation of verbs into their past tense forms.
Understanding the rule of morphology helps linguists trace the evolution of words over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphological rule - a rule describing how word forms change or are constructed

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form) + -logia (study); morphology meaning study of forms or word structure.
