Douay Bible

1. PROPN

/ ˌduː.eɪ ˈbaɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˌduː.eɪ ˈbaɪ.bəl / (BrE)
An English translation of the Latin Vulgate Bible prepared by Roman Catholic scholars at Douay and Rheims in the late 16th and early 17th centuries.
English translation of the Vulgate by Catholic scholars

Examples

Many English-speaking Catholics relied on the Douay Bible before other Catholic translations became available.
The Douay Bible differs from the King James Bible in several passages.
He used a copy of the Douay Bible for his theological studies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: King James Bible - An English translation of the Bible commissioned by King James I of England

Etymology

Named after Douay (now Douai), a town in northern France where the translation was produced.
