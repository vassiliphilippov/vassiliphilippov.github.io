genus Crex

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkrɛks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkrɛks / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family, Rallidae, best known for the corncrake (Crex crex).
bird genus including the corncrake

Examples

The genus Crex is notable for its single living species, the corncrake.
Recent surveys revealed that the genus Crex once included more species in prehistoric times.
Birdwatchers hope to spot members of the genus Crex during migration seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crex crex - the corncrake, the most common species in genus Crex

Etymology

From New Latin Crex, the genus name, itself from Ancient Greek krex (a kind of bird, corncrake).
