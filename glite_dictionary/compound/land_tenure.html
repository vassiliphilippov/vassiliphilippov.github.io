land tenure

1. NOUN

/ lænd ˈtɛnjɚ / (AmE), / lænd ˈtɛnjə / (BrE)
the legal right or system by which land is owned or occupied
system of owning or occupying land
legal

Examples

The system of land tenure varied greatly between feudal England and colonial America.
Unclear land tenure often leads to disputes over ownership and usage rights.
Changes in land tenure have affected agricultural productivity across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freehold - permanent and absolute tenure of land
  • Antonym: leasehold - land held under a lease

Etymology

From 'land' (Old English *land*) and 'tenure' (from Old French, from Latin *tenere*, to hold).
