common camas

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈkæməs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkæməs / (BrE)
a wildflower native to western North America, having a large edible bulb and racemes of blue star-shaped flowers.
wildflower with edible bulb from western North America

Examples

Native peoples used to harvest common camas bulbs as an important food source.
In late spring, the meadows are covered in the blue blooms of common camas.
Scientists are studying the ecology of common camas in restored prairies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - a general term for plants of the Camassia genus with edible bulbs

Etymology

From Nez Perce *qém’es* or related Native American languages, adopted into English through explorers and botanists in North America.
