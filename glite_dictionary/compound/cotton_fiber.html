cotton fiber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtn ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
the soft, silky fibers obtained from the cotton plant, used as the raw material for making cotton textiles
soft fibers from cotton plants

Examples

After harvesting, the cotton fiber is separated from the seeds at the gin.
High-quality sheets are made from long-staple cotton fiber.
Scientists are developing new ways to dye cotton fiber without polluting water sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - the soft, white, fibrous substance surrounding the seeds of the cotton plant
  • Antonym: linen - cloth made from the fibers of the flax plant
