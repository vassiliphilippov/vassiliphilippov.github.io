steam whistle

1. NOUN

/ stiːm ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / stiːm ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
a whistle that produces sound using steam, commonly fitted to steam-powered engines or boilers as a signal device.
whistle operated by steam as a signal

Examples

The loud steam whistle signaled the end of the factory shift.
Children covered their ears when the train's steam whistle blew as it entered the station.
The old tugboat's steam whistle echoed across the harbor at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistle - device that makes a high-pitched sound
