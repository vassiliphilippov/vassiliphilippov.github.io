Old Gooseberry

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈɡusˌbɛri / (AmE), / əʊld ˈɡuːsbəri / (BrE)
A person who meddles or interferes in others' affairs; also, a traditional card game.
a meddler; a type of card game

Examples

During the meeting, Tom couldn't resist playing Old Gooseberry and offering unsolicited advice.
After dinner, the family gathered to enjoy a round of Old Gooseberry before bed.
She accused her neighbor of being an Old Gooseberry for constantly asking personal questions.

Synset Examples

She has a knack for playing Old Gooseberry in arguments.
He loves to play Old Gooseberry when things get tense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busybody - a person who meddles in others' affairs
  • Synonym: meddler - someone who interferes in other people's business

Etymology

Possibly a humorous or playful extension of 'gooseberry', with uncertain origin.
