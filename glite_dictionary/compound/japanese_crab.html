Japanese crab

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz kræb / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz kræb / (BrE)
The meat of a crab species found in Japan, often canned and used as food.
crabmeat from Japan, often canned

Examples

The chef added fresh Japanese crab to the sushi rolls for an authentic flavor.
Canned Japanese crab is popular in gourmet food stores worldwide.
She prepared a salad using shredded Japanese crab and a light dressing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabmeat - the edible flesh of a crab
