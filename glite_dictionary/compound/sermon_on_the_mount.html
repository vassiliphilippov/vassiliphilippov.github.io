Sermon on the Mount

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːmən ɑn ðə maʊnt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːmən ɒn ðə maʊnt / (BrE)
the discourse delivered by Jesus Christ, containing key teachings, as recorded in the Gospels of Matthew (chapters 5–7) and Luke (6:20–49) in the New Testament
famous discourse of Jesus in the Bible

Examples

Many Christians study the Sermon on the Mount to better understand the moral teachings of Jesus.
The Sermon on the Mount includes the famous Beatitudes, which emphasize humility and compassion.
Scholars often reference the Sermon on the Mount when discussing the ethical framework of Christianity.
