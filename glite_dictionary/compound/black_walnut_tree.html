black walnut tree

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈwɔlnət tri / (AmE), / blæk ˈwɔːlnʌt triː / (BrE)
A North American walnut tree (Juglans nigra) with hard, dark wood and an edible nut.
North American walnut tree with edible nut

Examples

The black walnut tree is prized for both its valuable wood and its delicious nuts.
A large black walnut tree shaded the farmhouse in the summer.
Gardeners sometimes avoid planting a black walnut tree because its roots can inhibit the growth of other plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut tree - A tree of the genus Juglans, producing edible nuts
