slipper spurge

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpər ˌspɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈslɪpə ˌspɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a tropical American shrubby succulent plant resembling a cactus, having foot-shaped bracts; any plant of the genus Pedilanthus
a succulent plant with foot-shaped bracts

Examples

The slipper spurge thrives in warm, arid climates and is prized for its unusual bracts.
Gardeners often mistake the slipper spurge for a cactus due to its succulent stems.
A mature slipper spurge can add a unique decorative touch to a tropical garden.

Etymology

From 'slipper', referencing the foot-shaped (slipper-like) bracts, and 'spurge', a common name for plants of the family Euphorbiaceae.
