orange pekoe

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrɪndʒ ˈpiːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪndʒ ˈpiːkəʊ / (BrE)
A high-quality grade of black tea made from the young leaves and buds of the tea plant, grown especially in India, Sri Lanka, and Java.
superior grade of black tea

Examples

He prefers to start his morning with a strong cup of orange pekoe.
The hotel served afternoon tea featuring orange pekoe and a selection of pastries.
I bought a box of premium orange pekoe from a specialty tea shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black tea - Tea that has been fully oxidized, producing a dark color and robust flavor

Etymology

'Pekoe' from Chinese 'bái hóu' (white hair), referring to the fine white hairs on young tea leaves; 'orange' possibly from the Dutch royal House of Orange-Nassau, used as a mark of quality.
