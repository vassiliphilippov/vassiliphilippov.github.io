Enewetak Atoll

1. PROPN

/ ɛˈneɪwiˌtɑk əˈtɑl / (AmE), / ɛˈniːwɪtæk əˈtɒl / (BrE)
an atoll in the Marshall Islands, known as the site of a major World War II battle and later as a location for U.S. atomic bomb testing
atoll in the Marshall Islands, WWII and atomic test site

Examples

During the Cold War, the United States conducted nuclear tests at Enewetak Atoll.
The Battle of Enewetak Atoll was a significant campaign in the Pacific during World War II.
Scientists still study the long-term environmental impact of nuclear testing on Enewetak Atoll.

Etymology

From Enewetak, the local Marshallese name of the atoll.
