parlour car

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑɹlər kɑɹ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə kɑː / (BrE)
a railroad passenger car designed for daytime travel, offering individual chairs and requiring an extra fare for more comfort than standard seating.
comfort-focused railroad passenger car

Examples

Passengers paid extra to ride in the parlour car and enjoy its spacious seats.
The parlour car offered larger windows and more comfort than the regular coaches.
If you want to work on the train, reserving a parlour car seat is often worth the additional fee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pullman car - luxurious railroad sleeping or parlor car

Etymology

From 'parlour', meaning a sitting room, reflecting the car's comfortable seating.
