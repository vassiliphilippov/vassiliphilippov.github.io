genus Pithecia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pɪˈθiː.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pɪˈθiː.sɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of South American monkeys known as sakis, characterized by long, bushy tails and shaggy hair.
genus of saki monkeys

Examples

The genus Pithecia includes several species of saki monkeys found in the Amazon basin.
Researchers are studying the social behavior of primates in the genus Pithecia.
Distinctive facial markings are a feature of monkeys in the genus Pithecia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saki - A type of South American monkey belonging to the genus Pithecia

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Pithecia,' the taxonomic name for the genus of saki monkeys.
