blind curve

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd kɝv / (AmE), / blaɪnd kɜːv / (BrE)
A curve or bend in a road that obscures the driver's view of what lies ahead.
road bend with obstructed view ahead

Examples

The accident occurred when the car skidded around a blind curve on the mountain road.
Drivers should slow down when approaching a blind curve to avoid collisions.
You often see warning signs posted before a blind curve on rural highways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind corner - a corner on a road that cannot be seen around
  • Degree Higher: sharp curve - a curve in the road with a tight bend
