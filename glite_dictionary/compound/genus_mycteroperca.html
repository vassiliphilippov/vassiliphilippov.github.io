genus Mycteroperca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmaɪktəroʊˈpɜːrkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmɪktərəˈpɜːkə / (BrE)
A genus of medium to large marine fishes in the grouper family, known for their stout bodies and importance in commercial and recreational fishing.
a genus of groupers (fish)

Examples

The genus Mycteroperca includes several species important to commercial fisheries.
Marine biologists are studying the behavior of fish in the genus Mycteroperca.
One well-known member of the genus Mycteroperca is the gag grouper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouper - a large marine fish of the family Serranidae, which includes groupers

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Mycteroperca' formed from Greek 'mykter' (nose) and 'perke' (perch).
