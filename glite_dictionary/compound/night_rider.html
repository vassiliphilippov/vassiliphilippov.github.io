night rider

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt ˌraɪdər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt ˌraɪdə / (BrE)
a member of a secret, usually mounted, group in the southern United States after the Civil War, who engaged in acts of intimidation and revenge, often through violence or threats
member of secret vigilante group in post–Civil War U.S. South

Examples

A group of night riders terrorized local farmers who refused to cooperate.
During the early twentieth century, night riders threatened tobacco growers in the region.
The legacy of the night rider still casts a shadow over that rural community.
