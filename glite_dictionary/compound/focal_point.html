focal point

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊkəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the thing or person that receives the most attention or interest; the center of activity or focus
center of attention or interest

Examples

The new library quickly became the focal point of the community.
Economic development is the focal point of the government's new policy.
Her artwork served as the focal point of the entire exhibition.

Synset Examples

the focus of activity shifted to molecular biology
he had no direction in his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the center of interest or activity

Etymology

From 'focus' (from Latin focus, hearth) and 'point' (from Old French point, from Latin punctum, prick, dot).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊkəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
The point at which rays of light or other radiation meet after being focused, or from which they appear to diverge.
point where light rays converge or diverge

Examples

The lens brings the rays of light to a focal point on the screen.
In a convex mirror, the focal point is located behind the mirror's surface.
The scientist adjusted the apparatus so that the focal point was exactly at the sensor's position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the point at which rays of light meet after reflection or refraction

Etymology

From Latin focus (hearth, fireplace) + point; used in optics to denote the convergence of rays.
