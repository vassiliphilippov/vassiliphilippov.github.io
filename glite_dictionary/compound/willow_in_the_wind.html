willow in the wind

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊ ɪn ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ ɪn ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
a person who is graceful, supple, or flexible, especially in movement or character
person who is graceful and flexible

Examples

Her yoga teacher, a true willow in the wind, demonstrated remarkable balance and poise.
He moved through the crowded room like a willow in the wind, avoiding every obstacle with ease.
On stage, she became a willow in the wind, captivating the audience with her fluid, graceful dance.

Synset Examples

The dancer moved gracefully, a willow in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graceful - having elegance or smoothness of movement
  • Synonym: supple - bending and moving easily and gracefully
  • Antonym: rigid - stiff, not flexible

Etymology

From 'willow,' a tree known for its slender, flexible branches, used metaphorically with 'wind.'
