magnetostrictive speaker

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡnət̬oʊˈstrɪktɪv ˈspiːkər / (AmE), / ˌmæɡnətəʊˈstrɪktɪv ˈspiːkə / (BrE)
a type of loudspeaker in which the magnet and coil assembly remains stationary, using magnetostriction to produce sound; commonly used in sonar and specialized audio applications
loudspeaker using magnetostrictive technology

Examples

The company unveiled a new magnetostrictive speaker for underwater communication devices.
Unlike traditional designs, a magnetostrictive speaker can operate more efficiently in harsh environments.
Researchers tested the durability of the magnetostrictive speaker under extreme temperature variations.

Synset Examples

Magnetostrictive speaker drivers have some special advantages: they can provide greater force (with smaller excursions) than other technologies; low excursion can avoid distortions from large excursion as in other designs; the magnetizing coil is stationary and therefore more easily cooled; they are robust because delicate suspensions and voice coils are not required.

Etymology

From magnetostrictive (relating to change in shape of a material when magnetized) + speaker.
