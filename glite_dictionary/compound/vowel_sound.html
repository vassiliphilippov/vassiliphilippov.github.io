vowel sound

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaʊəl saʊnd / (AmE), / ˈvaʊəl saʊnd / (BrE)
A speech sound produced with the vocal tract open, typically forming the nucleus of a syllable.
speech sound with open vocal tract

Examples

The English language has many different vowel sounds that can be difficult for learners to master.
She practiced each vowel sound carefully to improve her pronunciation.
In linguistics, a vowel sound is defined by the shape and position of the tongue and lips.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonant sound - a speech sound produced with a partial or complete closure of the vocal tract
