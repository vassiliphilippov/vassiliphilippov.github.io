in vitro

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ˈviːtroʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈviːtrəʊ / (BrE)
(of a biological process) occurring or taking place outside a living organism, typically in an artificial environment such as a test tube or culture dish.
performed outside a living organism
medical

Examples

The scientists studied the effects of the drug in vitro before moving on to animal trials.
Many genetic experiments are first performed in vitro to assess safety.
The development of in vitro fertilization has helped many people conceive children.

Synset Examples

in vitro fertilization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in vivo - in a living organism; opposite of in vitro

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'in glass', originally referring to experiments conducted in glass containers.
