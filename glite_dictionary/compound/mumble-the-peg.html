mumble-the-peg

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌm.bəl ðə pɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈmʌm.bəl ðə pɛɡ / (BrE)
a traditional children's game in which players throw or flip a jackknife in specific ways so that it sticks in the ground
children's game involving throwing a knife

Examples

As children, we often played mumble-the-peg in the backyard during summer evenings.
The rules of mumble-the-peg require skill and careful knife-throwing.
He showed his grandson how to play mumble-the-peg with an old pocketknife.

Etymology

From the phrase 'mumble the peg,' referring to the act of removing a peg from the ground, possibly while mumbling, as part of the game's penalty.
