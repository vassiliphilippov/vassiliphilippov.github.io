spinal anesthetic

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnl ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnl ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
An anesthetic that is injected into the spinal canal to induce loss of sensation, typically in the lower body, during surgery or medical procedures.
anesthetic injected into the spinal canal
medical

Examples

The surgeon administered a spinal anesthetic before beginning the procedure.
Many women opt for a spinal anesthetic during childbirth to reduce pain.
Because of her medical condition, a spinal anesthetic was considered safer than general anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidural - a type of anesthesia injected into the epidural space around the spinal cord
