chamfer bit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæmfər bɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːmfə bɪt / (BrE)
A cutting tool attachment for a drill or router, used to create a beveled edge on wood, metal, or other materials.
tool for creating a beveled edge

Examples

To add a decorative edge to the shelf, he used a chamfer bit on his router.
When replacing the old flooring, she beveled the board edges with a chamfer bit.
A chamfer bit is ideal for smoothing sharp corners on wooden furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bevel bit - a bit for making angled cuts or edges

Etymology

From 'chamfer' (from French *chanfrein*, beveled edge) and 'bit' (tool for drilling or cutting).
