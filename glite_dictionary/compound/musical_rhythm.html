musical rhythm

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuzɪkəl ˈrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
the regular pattern or unit of beats in a piece of music, forming its underlying timing structure
basic rhythmic unit in music

Examples

The musical rhythm of the piece made everyone want to dance.
She practiced every day to master the musical rhythm required for the song.
The conductor emphasized the importance of strong musical rhythm throughout the orchestra.

Synset Examples

the piece has a fast rhythm
the conductor set the beat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat - the basic unit of time in music
