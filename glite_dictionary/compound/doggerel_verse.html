doggerel verse

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑɡərəl vɝs / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡərəl vɜːs / (BrE)
comic verse with irregular rhythm and rough, often trivial or trivializing content, typically regarded as of little literary value.
comic verse of irregular measure

Examples

The poet often amused his friends with witty doggerel verse at parties.
Children sometimes write doggerel verse about their teachers for fun.
Despite its playful tone, the doggerel verse contained a clever hidden message.

Synset Examples

he had heard some silly doggerel that kept running through his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doggerel - comic verse composed in irregular rhythm

Etymology

From Middle English 'dogerel', of uncertain origin, possibly derived from 'dog'.
