Quechuan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwɑn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language or group of closely related languages of the Quechua people, originally spoken by the Incas and still used in parts of South America.
language of the Quechua people; language of the Incas

Examples

The Quechuan language was once the official tongue of the Inca Empire.
Many communities in Peru still speak Quechuan language as their first language.
Linguists have studied the grammar and vocabulary of the Quechuan language extensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - the people inhabiting the Peruvian Andes and their descendants

Etymology

From Quechua *qichwa* (temperate valley, the people and their language), via Spanish.
