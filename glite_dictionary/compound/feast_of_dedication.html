Feast of Dedication

1. NOUN

/ fiːst əv ˌdɛdəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / fiːst əv ˌdɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an eight-day Jewish festival, also known as Hanukkah, commemorating the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC
Jewish festival, also called Hanukkah

Examples

During the Feast of Dedication, families gather to light the menorah each night.
The Feast of Dedication is celebrated with traditional foods and games.
Many people know the Feast of Dedication by its Hebrew name, Hanukkah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanukkah - an eight-day Jewish festival commemorating the rededication of the Temple; also called Festival of Lights
