flatbed press

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌbɛd ˌprɛs / (AmE), / ˈflætbɛd ˌprɛs / (BrE)
a type of printing press in which the type or image is arranged on a flat surface and the paper is pressed onto it by a rolling cylinder
printing press with flat type bed

Examples

The museum displayed an antique flatbed press from the early 20th century.
Many small printing shops still use a flatbed press for specialty projects.
The invention of the rotary press eventually replaced the flatbed press in large-scale newspaper printing.
