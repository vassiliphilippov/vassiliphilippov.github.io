crab apple

1. NOUN

/ kræb ˈæpəl / (AmE), / kræb ˈæpəl / (BrE)
a small, sour apple, typically not eaten raw but used for making preserves or jelly
small sour apple used in preserves

Examples

The jelly made from crab apples has a distinctive tart flavor.
A mature crab apple tree adds beauty to many gardens in the spring.
Although crab apples are too sour to eat raw, they are excellent for baking.

Synset Examples

crabapples make a tangy jelly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - the round fruit with red or green skin and crisp flesh

Etymology

From crab (possibly from Old Norse *skrabbaputr*, a kind of wild apple) + apple.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræb ˌæp.əl / (AmE), / ˈkræb ˌæp.əl / (BrE)
A small, sour apple from any of several species of wild or cultivated apple trees, often used for making preserves or grown for ornamental purposes.
small, sour apple or apple tree

Examples

The tree in our backyard produces crab apples every autumn, which we use to make jelly.
Many gardeners plant crab apple trees for their beautiful spring blossoms.
She collected a basket of crab apples to use in a tart, despite their tart flavor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: apple - the round fruit of a tree of the rose family, typically with thin green or red skin and crisp flesh

3. NOUN

/ kræb ˈæpəl / (AmE), / kræb ˈapəl / (BrE)
a small, sour wild apple or the tree that bears such fruit, especially of species related to the cultivated apple
a small, sour wild apple or its tree

Examples

The old orchard is filled with crab apples that are too sour to eat raw.
She made a batch of jelly using freshly picked crab apples.
In the autumn, the branches of the crab apple are heavy with small, colorful fruit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: apple - the round fruit of a tree of the rose family, which typically has thin green or red skin and crisp flesh

Etymology

From 'crab', of uncertain origin, denoting the wild apple, and 'apple'.
