celestial orbit

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛstʃəl ˈɔːrbɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstɪəl ˈɔːbɪt / (BrE)
the path, usually elliptical, that a celestial body follows as it revolves around another, such as a planet around a star or a moon around a planet.
the path of a body in space around another

Examples

The Earth's celestial orbit around the Sun determines the length of a year.
Scientists studied the celestial orbit of the newly discovered comet.
Satellites must be launched into a stable celestial orbit to function properly.

Synset Examples

he plotted the orbit of the moon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orbit - the curved path of a celestial object or spacecraft around a star, planet, or moon

Etymology

From Latin 'caelestis' (heavenly) and Latin 'orbita' (track, path).
