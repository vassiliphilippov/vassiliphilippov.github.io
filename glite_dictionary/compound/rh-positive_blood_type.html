Rh-positive blood type

1. NOUN

/ ɑr eɪtʃ ˈpɑzətɪv ˈblʌd taɪp / (AmE), / ɑː r eɪtʃ ˈpɒzətɪv ˈblʌd taɪp / (BrE)
A blood group in which the red blood cells carry the Rh factor (Rh antigen), present in approximately 85% of people.
blood type with Rh antigen
medical

Examples

All donors with Rh-positive blood type are compatible with recipients who are Rh positive.
It is especially important to know if a pregnant woman has an Rh-positive blood type.
About 85% of the population has Rh-positive blood type, making it the most common.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Rh-negative blood type - blood type whose red cells lack the Rh factor

Etymology

Rh from rhesus, after the rhesus monkey in which the factor was first discovered.
