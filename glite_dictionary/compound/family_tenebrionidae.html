family Tenebrionidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɛˌniːbrioʊˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɛˌniːbrɪəʊˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of beetles that includes darkling beetles and mealworms, known for their hard bodies and survival in dry environments
a family of beetles (darkling beetles, mealworms)

Examples

The family Tenebrionidae includes many species used in scientific research and as food for pets.
Members of the family Tenebrionidae are commonly found in arid environments across the world.
Mealworms, the larvae of beetles in the family Tenebrionidae, are popular for feeding reptiles and birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darkling beetle - a common name for beetles in the family Tenebrionidae
  • Synonym: mealworm - larval stage of beetles in the Tenebrionidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Tenebrio' (a kind of beetle) + scientific family suffix '-idae'.
