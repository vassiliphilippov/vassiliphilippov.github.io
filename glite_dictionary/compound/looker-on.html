looker-on

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊkər ɑn / (AmE), / ˈlʊkər ɒn / (BrE)
A person who watches an event, situation, or activity without taking part; a spectator or observer.
a spectator or observer

Examples

At the parade, I was just a looker-on, enjoying the festivities from the sidewalk.
The police asked every looker-on if they had seen anything suspicious during the accident.
Although tempted to join, she remained a silent looker-on throughout the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectator - a person who watches an event, especially a sports event, without taking part
  • Antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something
