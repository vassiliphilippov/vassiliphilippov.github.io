water system

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔdɚ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
an arrangement or facility for collecting, treating, and distributing water to a community or area
facility supplying water to an area

Examples

The city upgraded its water system to provide cleaner drinking water to residents.
A break in the water system left the entire neighborhood without running water for hours.
Many rural areas still lack a reliable water system for their communities.

Synset Examples

the town debated the purification of the water supply
first you have to cut off the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water supply - the available amount or system of water for a region or building

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a river together with all of its tributaries; a network of interconnected streams and rivers forming a drainage basin.
network of a river and its tributaries

Examples

The Mississippi River water system covers a vast region of North America.
Scientists study the local water system to understand how pollutants travel downstream.
The Amazon water system supports countless species through its extensive network of tributaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drainage basin - the area of land where precipitation collects and drains off into a common outlet, such as a river
