light heavyweight

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (AmE), / laɪt ˈhɛviweɪt / (BrE)
A competitor in a weight division in combat sports, such as boxing or mixed martial arts, heavier than a middleweight but lighter than a heavyweight.
weight division in combat sports

Examples

He won the light heavyweight championship after an intense fifteen-round fight.
Several light heavyweights competed in tonight's main event.
She moved up from middleweight to light heavyweight to challenge for the title.

Related Terms
