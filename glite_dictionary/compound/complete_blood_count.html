complete blood count

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplit blʌd kaʊnt / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːt blʌd kaʊnt / (BrE)
a medical test that measures the numbers of red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets in a sample of blood
medical test measuring blood cell counts
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a complete blood count to check for signs of infection.
A complete blood count can help diagnose anemia and other blood disorders.
Before the surgery, her physician requested a complete blood count for safety.
