creeping windmill grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈwɪndˌmɪl ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈwɪndˌmɪl ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial Australian grass with long spikes arranged like the vanes of a windmill.
Australian perennial grass with long, windmill-like spikes

Examples

The field was covered in creeping windmill grass, swaying gently in the breeze.
Farmers often plant creeping windmill grass to help control soil erosion in Australia.
Researchers studied how creeping windmill grass adapted to changes in rainfall patterns.
