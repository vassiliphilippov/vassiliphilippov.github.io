sweet rocket

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈrɑkɪt / (AmE), / swiːt ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
A tall herbaceous plant (Hesperis matronalis), cultivated for its fragrant, usually purple or white flowers that are especially scented at night; widely naturalized in Europe, Asia, and North America.
fragrant flowering plant, Hesperis matronalis

Examples

The garden was filled with sweet rocket, its perfume drifting through the air at dusk.
She planted sweet rocket along the fence to attract bees and butterflies.
Clusters of white and purple sweet rocket brightened the old cottage garden.

Etymology

From the sweet scent of its flowers combined with 'rocket', a common name for plants of the Brassicaceae family.
