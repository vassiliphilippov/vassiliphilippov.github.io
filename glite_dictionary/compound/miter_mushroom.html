miter mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪtər ˈmʌʃruːm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə ˈmʌʃrəm / (BrE)
A type of helvella mushroom with a saddle-shaped fertile cap, creamy color, and a stalk that is fluted and pitted.
a creamy-colored, saddle-shaped helvella mushroom

Examples

The miter mushroom is easily identified by its creamy color and saddle-shaped cap.
Foragers are careful to distinguish the miter mushroom from similar species in the forest.
The stalk of a miter mushroom is often fluted and pitted, making it quite distinctive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helvella - a genus of fungi with irregularly shaped caps and fluted stems

Etymology

From the shape of the cap resembling a bishop's miter (head-dress).
