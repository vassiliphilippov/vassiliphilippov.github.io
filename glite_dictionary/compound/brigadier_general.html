brigadier general

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɪɡəˌdɪr ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌbrɪɡəˌdɪə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
a military officer of high rank, ranking below major general and above colonel
military rank below major general

Examples

The brigadier general addressed the troops before the operation began.
After years of service, she was promoted to brigadier general in the army.
The brigadier general coordinated with other officers to ensure the success of the mission.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: major general - a general officer ranking above a brigadier general
  • Degree Lower: colonel - a military rank below brigadier general

Etymology

From French 'brigadier général', originally denoting the commander of a brigade.
