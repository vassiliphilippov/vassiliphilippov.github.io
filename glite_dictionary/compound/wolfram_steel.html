wolfram steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.frəm stiːl / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.frəm stiːl / (BrE)
a very hard, heat-resistant alloy steel containing tungsten
a hard, tungsten-containing steel alloy

Examples

Toolmakers often use wolfram steel for its extreme durability and resistance to heat.
The introduction of wolfram steel revolutionized the manufacturing of cutting instruments in the early 20th century.
Compared to ordinary alloys, wolfram steel can maintain its toughness at much higher temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tungsten steel - steel alloyed with tungsten for strength and hardness

Etymology

From 'wolfram', an alternative name for tungsten (from German Wolfram), combined with 'steel'.
