Lolo-Burmese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊloʊ ˈbɜrmiz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌləʊləʊ ˈbɜːmiːz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of the Tibeto-Burman language family spoken in northern Myanmar (Burma) and Yunnan, China.
Tibeto-Burman language branch in Myanmar and Yunnan

Examples

Linguists have studied the Lolo-Burmese language to understand migration patterns in Southeast Asia.
Many ethnic groups in Yunnan speak dialects of the Lolo-Burmese language.
The classification of the Lolo-Burmese language family is still debated among scholars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tibeto-Burman language - A large language family of East and Southeast Asia, including Burmese, Tibetan, and others

Etymology

The name comes from the principal ethnic groups and regions where the languages are spoken: 'Lolo' (an older term for Yi people) and 'Burmese'.
