deep cervical vein

1. NOUN

/ diːp ˈsɝvɪkəl veɪn / (AmE), / diːp ˈsɜːvɪkəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located in the neck that accompanies the deep cervical artery, responsible for draining blood from muscles and tissues near the cervical spine
a vein in the deep part of the neck
medical

Examples

The deep cervical vein drains blood from the muscles at the back of the neck.
Injury to the deep cervical vein can cause significant bleeding during neck surgery.
Detailed images showed a blockage in the patient's deep cervical vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deep cervical artery - an artery of the same name, located in the neck

Etymology

From Latin 'cervix' (neck), with 'deep' indicating its anatomical position.
