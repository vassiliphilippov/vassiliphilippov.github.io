Venn diagram

1. NOUN

/ vɛn ˈdaɪ.əˌɡræm / (AmE), / vɛn ˈdaɪ.əɡram / (BrE)
a diagram using overlapping circles to show the relationships between different sets, especially in mathematics or logic
diagram showing relationships between sets

Examples

The math teacher drew a Venn diagram on the board to explain the concept of union and intersection.
We used a Venn diagram to compare the similarities and differences between mammals and birds.
Her presentation included a colorful Venn diagram illustrating the overlap among project teams.

Etymology

Named after John Venn (1834–1923), English logician, who introduced the diagram.

2. NOUN

/ vɛn ˈdaɪəˌgræm / (AmE), / vɛn ˈdaɪəɡræm / (BrE)
a diagram consisting of overlapping circles that shows the logical relationships and common elements between different sets
diagram using circles to show set relationships

Examples

The teacher used a Venn diagram to illustrate the similarities and differences between mammals and reptiles.
You can use a Venn diagram to compare the features of two stories.
The data is best represented visually in a Venn diagram showing the intersection of the two groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euler diagram - a diagram similar to a Venn diagram, but only depicting actual relationships between sets

Etymology

Named after British logician John Venn (1834–1923), who introduced the concept.
