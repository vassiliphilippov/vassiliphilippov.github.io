Komodo dragon

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊdoʊ ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / kəˈməʊdəʊ ˈdræɡən / (BrE)
a very large species of monitor lizard native to certain Indonesian islands, known as the world's largest lizard
large Indonesian monitor lizard

Examples

The Komodo dragon can grow up to ten feet in length and is a top predator in its habitat.
During our trip to Indonesia, we saw a Komodo dragon basking in the sun.
Scientists study the saliva of the Komodo dragon for its unique properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor lizard - any large lizard of the Varanidae family

Etymology

From Komodo, the island in Indonesia where the lizard is found, and 'dragon' for its large, reptilian appearance.
