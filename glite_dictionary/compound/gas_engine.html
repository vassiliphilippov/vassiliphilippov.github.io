gas engine

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈɛndʒɪn / (AmE), / ɡas ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
An internal-combustion engine that operates with natural gas as fuel instead of gasoline vapor.
internal-combustion engine using natural gas

Examples

The factory replaced its old diesel units with a modern gas engine to reduce emissions.
A gas engine can provide reliable power in remote locations where natural gas is available.
Many public transport buses are now fitted with a gas engine to meet environmental standards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gasoline engine - internal-combustion engine using gasoline vapor as fuel
