Union Army

1. NOUN

/ ˈjunjən ˈɑrmi / (AmE), / ˈjuːnjən ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
the army of the northern states of the United States during the American Civil War (1861–1865)
Northern army in the American Civil War

Examples

The Union Army fought to preserve the United States during the Civil War.
General Grant led the Union Army to several important victories.
Many immigrants served in the Union Army, contributing to its diverse ranks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Confederate Army - the army of the southern states during the American Civil War
