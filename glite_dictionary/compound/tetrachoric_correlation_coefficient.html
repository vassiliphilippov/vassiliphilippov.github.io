tetrachoric correlation coefficient

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈkɔrɪk ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən kəˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈkɒrɪk ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən kəˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a mathematical estimate of the correlation between two variables, each measured as a dichotomy but assumed to be normally distributed, used in statistics to approximate the Pearson correlation for underlying continuous data
statistical correlation estimate for dichotomous variables
medical

Examples

The tetrachoric correlation coefficient is often used to estimate the relationship between two binary test items.
Researchers calculated the tetrachoric correlation coefficient to assess the association between passing two different exams.
A high tetrachoric correlation coefficient suggests a strong underlying connection between the dichotomous traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pearson correlation coefficient - statistical measure of linear correlation for continuous variables
  • Degree Lower: point biserial correlation coefficient - correlation coefficient for one dichotomous and one continuous variable

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) and khōros (place); describes correlation in a 2×2 table (four cells).
