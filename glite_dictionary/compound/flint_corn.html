flint corn

1. NOUN

/ flɪnt kɔrn / (AmE), / flɪnt kɔːn / (BrE)
A type of maize with kernels that have a hard outer layer enclosing a soft endosperm, often used for decoration or as food.
type of corn with hard-shelled kernels

Examples

Native Americans traditionally grew flint corn for both food and decoration.
The hard outer layer of flint corn makes it less susceptible to pests.
During autumn, some people use colorful flint corn cobs as festive decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dent corn - a type of field corn with kernels that have a dent on the top
