melanin-challenged

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛlənɪn ˈtʃælɪndʒd / (AmE), / ˈmɛlənɪn ˈtʃælɪndʒd / (BrE)
(humorous) Having little or no melanin in the skin; having light or pale skin.
humorous term for light-skinned person
informal slang

Examples

She laughed and called herself melanin-challenged after getting sunburned at the beach.
Some people use the phrase melanin-challenged as a lighthearted way to refer to fair skin.
He often jokes about being melanin-challenged during the summer because he can't tan.

Synset Examples

The term melanin-challenged is often used lightheartedly.
He jokingly referred to himself as melanin-challenged.
She embraced her melanin-challenged skin with confidence.

Etymology

Patterned after terms like 'vertically challenged', using 'melanin' (pigment in the skin) + 'challenged' (playfully implying lacking).
