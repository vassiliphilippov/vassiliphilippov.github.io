vaudeville theater

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔd.vɪl ˈθi.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɔːd.vɪl ˈθɪə.tə / (BrE)
A theater in which vaudeville performances are staged, typically featuring a variety of comic, musical, and specialty acts.
theater for vaudeville performances

Examples

The old vaudeville theater downtown has been renovated into a concert hall.
Many famous comedians got their start performing on the vaudeville theater stage.
The history museum included a replica of a 1920s vaudeville theater in its exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: music hall - a theater featuring musical entertainment, especially in Britain

Etymology

From 'vaudeville', a form of variety entertainment, and 'theater'. 'Vaudeville' derives from French.
