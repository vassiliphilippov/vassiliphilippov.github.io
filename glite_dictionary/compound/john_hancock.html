John Hancock

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑn ˈhæn.kɑk / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈhæn.kɒk / (BrE)
a person's signature, especially used informally to request someone to sign their name
a person's signature (informal, especially in US)
informal american

Examples

Just put your John Hancock at the bottom of the page.
The delivery man asked for my John Hancock before handing over the package.
She scribbled her John Hancock on the credit card receipt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signature - a person's name written in their own handwriting

Etymology

From John Hancock (1737–1793), first signer of the US Declaration of Independence, known for his large, stylish signature.
