dusky-coloured

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌski ˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈdʌski ˌkʌləd / (BrE)
having a dark or slightly shadowy color; somewhat dark in hue
having a somewhat dark color

Examples

The bird's feathers are a striking dusky-coloured shade that blends with the twilight.
She preferred dusky-coloured fabrics for a more elegant, understated look.
The grapes they picked were smaller and dusky-coloured, rich with flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark-coloured - having a dark color

Etymology

From 'dusky' (darkish, shadowy) + 'coloured' (having color, from Latin 'coloratus').
