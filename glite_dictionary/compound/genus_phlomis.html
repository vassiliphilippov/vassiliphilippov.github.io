genus Phlomis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfloʊmɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfləʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World aromatic herbs, subshrubs, or shrubs, often with woolly leaves, in the mint family.
a genus of aromatic Old World plants

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the distinctive appearance of genus Phlomis in Mediterranean gardens.
The genus Phlomis includes a variety of species known for their woolly, aromatic foliage.
Botanists recently discovered a new species belonging to the genus Phlomis in Turkey.

Etymology

From Latin 'Phlomis', the classical name for a plant, and 'genus' meaning kind or group.
