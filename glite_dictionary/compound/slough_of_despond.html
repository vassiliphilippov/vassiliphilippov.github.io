slough of despond

1. NOUN

/ slaʊ əv dɪ'spɑnd / (AmE), / slaʊ əv dɪ'spɒnd / (BrE)
a state of extreme despair or depression
state of deep depression
archaic

Examples

After so many personal setbacks, he felt trapped in a slough of despond and saw no way out.
The novel's protagonist spends much of the story wandering through a slough of despond.
Her sudden unemployment plunged her into a temporary slough of despond, but she recovered with time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despair - the complete loss or absence of hope

Etymology

From John Bunyan's 17th-century allegory 'The Pilgrim's Progress', where it refers to a deep bog symbolizing spiritual despair.
