legislative council

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛdʒɪˈsleɪtɪv ˈkaʊnsl̩ / (AmE), / ˌlɛdʒɪˈsleɪtɪv ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
a law-making body, typically one that is unicameral, often found in certain states or countries
unicameral law-making body

Examples

The state established a legislative council to oversee its new constitution.
Members of the legislative council met to discuss education reform.
The bill must be approved by the legislative council before becoming law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parliament - the supreme legislative body in some countries
