false alumroot

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈælumˌrut / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈæləmruːt / (BrE)
a plant of western North America with clumps of mainly basal leaves and cream or pale pink fringed flowers in several long racemes
a North American plant with pale pink to cream flowers

Examples

We spotted a patch of false alumroot growing beside the trail in the mountains.
The delicate flowers of the false alumroot attract many pollinators each spring.
Gardeners sometimes choose false alumroot for its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alumroot - a related plant in the Heuchera genus, often with similar leaves and flowers
