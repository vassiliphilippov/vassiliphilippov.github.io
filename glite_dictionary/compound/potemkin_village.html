Potemkin village

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈtɛm.kɪn ˈvɪl.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / pəˈtɛm.kɪn ˈvɪl.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
an impressive facade or display that hides an undesirable fact or an unimpressive reality; something that appears substantial or impressive but is in fact insubstantial or deceptive
something deceptively impressive or insubstantial

Examples

The new administrative building was nothing more than a Potemkin village, concealing a poorly run operation.
Critics argue that the city's reforms are a Potemkin village designed to impress voters before the election.
Beneath its high-tech image, the company turned out to be a Potemkin village with outdated systems and processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facade - an outward appearance that is maintained to conceal a less pleasant reality
  • Synonym: sham - something that is not what it is purported to be

Etymology

From Russian historical legend about Grigory Potemkin, who allegedly built fake villages to impress Empress Catherine II during her visit to Crimea in 1787.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈtɛm.kɪn ˈvɪl.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / pəˈtɛm.kɪn ˈvɪl.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
an impressive or elaborate facade intended to mask undesirable facts or create a false appearance
deceptive facade; false front

Examples

The new town square was nothing more than a Potemkin village designed for tourists.
What appeared to be reform was simply a Potemkin village to appease critics.
Analysts dismissed the company's profits as a Potemkin village hiding underlying losses.

Synset Examples

They built a Potemkin village to impress the inspectors.
His lavish office was just a Potemkin village for show.
The new development looked like a Potemkin village, hiding real issues.
His claims were a Potemkin village, all for show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facade - false or deceptive outward appearance
  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true

Etymology

From Russian Потёмкинская деревня (Potyómkinskaya derevnya), after Grigory Potemkin, who allegedly built mock villages to impress Catherine the Great.
