Air Corps

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛr kɔr / (AmE), / ˈeə kɔː / (BrE)
the former airborne division of the United States Army, responsible for military aviation before the establishment of the Air Force
former US Army aviation branch
american

Examples

The Air Corps played a crucial role during World War II before the Air Force became a separate branch.
My grandfather served in the Air Corps as a pilot in the 1940s.
After its reorganization, the Air Corps was succeeded by the United States Air Force.
