drug cocktail

1. NOUN

/ drʌɡ ˈkɑk.teɪl / (AmE), / drʌɡ ˈkɒk.teɪl / (BrE)
a combination of different drugs taken together, especially as a treatment for diseases such as HIV/AIDS
combination of drugs, esp. for HIV/AIDS treatment
medical

Examples

Doctors often prescribe a drug cocktail to help control the progression of HIV in patients.
The effectiveness of the drug cocktail has improved life expectancy for people living with AIDS.
Researchers are developing a new drug cocktail that may work even better against resistant strains of the virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combination therapy - the use of more than one medication or treatment together
