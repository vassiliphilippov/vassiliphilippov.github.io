wild lupine

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈluːpɪn / (AmE), / waɪld ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
a perennial plant (Lupinus perennis) native to eastern and central North America, with palmate leaves and showy racemes of blue flowers
a North American flowering plant

Examples

The wild lupine blooms in brilliant blue clusters every spring in the eastern woodlands.
Conservationists are restoring wild lupine habitats to support the endangered Karner blue butterfly.
A patch of wild lupine can add color and attract pollinators to your native garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - a plant of the genus Lupinus, known for palmately divided leaves and pea-like flowers
