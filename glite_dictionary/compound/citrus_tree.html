citrus tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs triː / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs triː / (BrE)
A tropical or subtropical evergreen tree of the genus Citrus, cultivated for its juicy, edible fruits such as oranges, lemons, and limes.
tree of the Citrus genus, grown for edible fruits

Examples

The citrus tree in our backyard produces dozens of oranges each year.
Farmers planted rows of citrus trees to increase their lemon harvest.
A healthy citrus tree needs plenty of sunlight and regular watering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus - a genus of flowering trees and shrubs producing citrus fruits

Etymology

From Latin *citrus* (citron tree) and English *tree*.
