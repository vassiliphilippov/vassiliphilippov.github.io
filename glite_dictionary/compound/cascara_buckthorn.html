cascara buckthorn

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈkɑːrə ˈbʌkθɑːn / (AmE), / kæsˈkɑːrə ˈbʌkθɒn / (BrE)
a shrubby tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for producing the laxative cascara sagrada
tree yielding cascara sagrada (laxative)

Examples

The cascara buckthorn is commonly found along the coastal forests of Oregon and California.
Many herbal remedies contain extracts from the bark of cascara buckthorn.
Indigenous peoples used cascara buckthorn as a natural treatment for constipation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cascara sagrada - the dried bark of the cascara buckthorn, used as a laxative

Etymology

From Spanish 'cáscara' (bark) and 'buckthorn', translation of the plant's scientific name.
