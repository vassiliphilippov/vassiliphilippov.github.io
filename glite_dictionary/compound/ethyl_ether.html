ethyl ether

1. NOUN

/ ˈiθɪl ˈiθɚ / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl ˈiːθə / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid formerly used as an inhalation anesthetic and as a solvent
volatile flammable liquid (formerly anesthetic)
medical

Examples

Before safer agents were developed, ethyl ether was commonly used to induce surgical anesthesia.
The laboratory strictly controls the storage of ethyl ether due to its high flammability.
Early experiments in anesthesia often relied on ethyl ether for its effectiveness and availability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diethyl ether - another name for ethyl ether

Etymology

From 'ethyl' (from ethane) and 'ether' (from Greek 'aithēr', meaning 'upper air')
