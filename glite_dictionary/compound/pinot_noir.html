Pinot noir

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːnoʊ ˈnwɑr / (AmE), / ˌpiːnəʊ ˈnwɑː / (BrE)
A dry red wine made from a variety of black grape originally from Burgundy, France, now grown in many wine-producing regions.
a type of dry red wine or its grape variety

Examples

She ordered a glass of Pinot noir to enjoy with her steak.
The vineyard is famous for its award-winning Pinot noir.
Many collectors appreciate the delicate flavors of aged Pinot noir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Merlot - a variety of red wine grape and the wine made from it
  • Antonym: Chardonnay - a variety of white wine grape and the wine made from it

Etymology

French, literally 'black pine', referring to the grape's pinecone-shaped clusters and dark color.

2. NOUN

/ piˈnoʊ ˈnwɑr / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəʊ ˈnwɑː / (BrE)
a variety of red grape, originally from the Burgundy region of France, used for making red wine; also, a red wine made from this grape
red wine grape and wine variety

Examples

The sommelier recommended a Pinot noir to complement our dinner.
She prefers Pinot noir for its delicate flavor and lighter body.
California is known for producing excellent Pinot noir wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Merlot - a type of red wine and grape variety
  • Antonym: Chardonnay - a type of white wine and grape variety

Etymology

From French 'Pinot' (from 'pin', meaning 'pine', referring to the pinecone shape of the grape clusters) and 'noir' (black).
