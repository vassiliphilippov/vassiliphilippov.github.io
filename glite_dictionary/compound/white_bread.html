white bread

1. NOUN

/ waɪt brɛd / (AmE), / waɪt brɛd / (BrE)
Bread made from wheat flour that has been refined and usually bleached, resulting in a light color and soft texture.
bread made from refined wheat flour

Examples

She prefers white bread for her sandwiches instead of wholemeal varieties.
Many doctors recommend reducing white bread intake due to its lower fiber content.
The bakery sells fresh white bread every morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholemeal bread - bread made from whole wheat flour
  • Antonym: brown bread - bread that is dark in color, typically made from whole grain or rye flour
