Oracle of Apollo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrəkəl əv əˈpɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒrəkəl əv əˈpæləʊ / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the shrine at Delphi where a priestess delivered Apollo's prophecies, often in obscure or ambiguous language.
Delphic shrine delivering Apollo's prophecies

Examples

Pilgrims traveled from distant lands to seek guidance from the Oracle of Apollo at Delphi.
The ambiguous riddles given by the Oracle of Apollo have puzzled historians for centuries.
In ancient times, kings would consult the Oracle of Apollo before making important decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Delphic oracle - the priestess of Apollo at Delphi who delivered prophecies
  • Degree Lower: oracle - a person or place regarded as a source of wise counsel or prophecy

Etymology

Oracle: from Latin oraculum (divine announcement); Apollo: from Greek Apollōn, the god's name.
