subordinate clause

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɔːrdɪnət klɔːz / (AmE), / səˈbɔːdɪnət klɑːz / (BrE)
a clause in a complex sentence that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence and functions as a noun, adjective, or adverb within the sentence
dependent clause in a sentence

Examples

Although he was tired, the subordinate clause could not stand alone as a complete sentence.
"Because she studied hard" is a subordinate clause that explains why she did well.
A subordinate clause often starts with words like "because," "although," or "when."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependent clause - a clause that cannot stand alone as a sentence and depends on the main clause
  • Antonym: main clause - a clause that can stand alone as a complete sentence
