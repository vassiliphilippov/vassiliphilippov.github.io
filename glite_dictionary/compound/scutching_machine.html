scutching machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌtʃɪŋ məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈskʌtʃɪŋ məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
A machine used to separate the fibers of cotton, silk, or flax from their woody parts during processing.
machine for separating textile fibers

Examples

The new scutching machine increased the efficiency of processing flax at the textile mill.
After harvesting, the cotton was run through a scutching machine to clean the fibers.
A properly adjusted scutching machine helps preserve the length and quality of linen fibers.

Synset Examples

A modern scutching machine can process up to 500 kilograms (1,100 lb) of flax every hour, and produces about 70 kilograms (150 lb) of flax fibers and 30 kilograms (66 lb) of tow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scutch - to separate fibers by beating or scraping

Etymology

From 'scutch', meaning to separate fibers, ultimately from Middle English 'scutchen' (to beat flax).
