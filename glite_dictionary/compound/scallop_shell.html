scallop shell

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæləp ʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈskæləp ʃɛl / (BrE)
the fan-shaped shell of a scallop, often featuring radiating ribs and associated with symbols or decoration
fan-shaped shell of a scallop

Examples

A scallop shell was found on the beach, gleaming in the sun.
The pilgrims wore a scallop shell as a symbol during their journey.
She used a scallop shell to scoop water from the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - the hard outer covering of certain animals
