Comanche language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmæn.tʃi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈman.tʃi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language belonging to the Comanche people, part of the Shoshonean branch of the Uto-Aztecan language family, traditionally spoken by the Comanche.
language of the Comanche people

Examples

There are ongoing efforts to revitalize the Comanche language among younger generations.
Few people today are fluent speakers of the Comanche language.
She studied the Comanche language as part of her research on Native American cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shoshonean - a branch of the Uto-Aztecan language family to which Comanche belongs

Etymology

Comanche from the Ute term *kimantsi* (“enemy”); language refers to the group’s own tongue.
