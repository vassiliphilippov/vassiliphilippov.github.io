dystopian society

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈtoʊpiən səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / dɪsˈtəʊpɪən səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
an imagined community or society that is undesirable or frightening, typically characterized by oppression, control, and lack of personal freedoms
an imaginary oppressive and nightmarish society

Examples

Many novels depict a dystopian society where individual freedoms are severely restricted.
The film's setting was a futuristic dystopian society controlled by technology and surveillance.
Critics often compare real-world policies to those found in a dystopian society as a warning.

Synset Examples

The film portrayed a dystopian society resembling communazism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: utopian society - an imagined ideal or perfect society

Etymology

From 'dystopia' (from Greek 'dys-' bad + 'topos' place) and 'society'.
