puella publica

1. NOUN

/ pwɛl.ə ˈpuː.bli.kə / (AmE), / pwɛl.ə ˈpjuː.blɪ.kə / (BrE)
A historical term for a female entertainer or prostitute in ancient Rome, also referred to as a 'public woman'.
public woman; female entertainer or prostitute in ancient Rome

Examples

Many ancient texts refer to the puella publica as an accepted part of Roman society.
Her research included a chapter about the life of a puella publica in the first century BCE.
The status of a puella publica differed greatly from that of other Roman women.

Synset Examples

In ancient Rome, a puella publica had various roles.
The term puella publica describes a public woman in history.
She studied the concept of puella publica in her thesis.

Etymology

Latin, literally 'public girl' or 'public woman'.
