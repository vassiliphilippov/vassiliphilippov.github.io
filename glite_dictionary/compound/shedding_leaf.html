shedding leaf

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɛdɪŋ liːf / (AmE), / ˈʃɛdɪŋ liːf / (BrE)
(of a plant) losing leaves as part of a regular seasonal cycle.
plant that loses leaves seasonally

Examples

The maple is a classic example of a shedding leaf tree found in temperate regions.
Gardeners need to clean up fallen leaves from shedding leaf plants before winter arrives.
Unlike evergreens, shedding leaf trees provide a burst of color before dropping their foliage.

Synset Examples

Maple trees are deciduous, shedding leaves every fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deciduous - (of a tree or shrub) shedding its leaves annually
