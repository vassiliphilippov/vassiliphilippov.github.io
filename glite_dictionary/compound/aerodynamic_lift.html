aerodynamic lift

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊdaɪˈnæmɪk lɪft / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk lɪft / (BrE)
the upward force on an airfoil or wing, generated by the motion of air, that counters the force of gravity
upward force generated by airflow over a wing

Examples

The airplane relies on aerodynamic lift to stay aloft during flight.
Engineers analyzed how changes in wing design affected aerodynamic lift.
Birds generate aerodynamic lift with each downward stroke of their wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - upward force that opposes weight in flight

Etymology

From 'aerodynamic', from Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'dynamis' (power) and 'lift', referring to upward force.
