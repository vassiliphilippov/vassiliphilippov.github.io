refugee camp

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛfjudʒi kæmp / (AmE), / ˌrɛfjʊˈdʒiː kamp / (BrE)
A place where people who have been forced to leave their country because of war, political oppression, or religious persecution are given temporary shelter and assistance.
shelter for displaced people

Examples

Thousands of families were forced to live in a refugee camp after fleeing the conflict.
The international aid organization delivered food and supplies to the overcrowded refugee camp.
Children in the refugee camp received basic education provided by volunteers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - a place with temporary shelter, such as tents or huts, especially for soldiers or travelers
