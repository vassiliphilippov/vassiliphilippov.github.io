twin-aisle airplane

1. NOUN

/ twɪn ˈaɪl ˈɛrˌpleɪn / (AmE), / twɪn ˈaɪl ˈeəˌpleɪn / (BrE)
a large commercial airplane with two aisles separating three rows of seats
a commercial airplane with two aisles

Examples

Most international flights use twin-aisle airplanes to accommodate more passengers on long journeys.
Passengers find it easier to move around on a twin-aisle airplane compared to a single-aisle design.
Boeing and Airbus both manufacture popular twin-aisle airplanes for the global market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-aisle airplane - a commercial airplane with one aisle
