Panchen Lama

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.tʃən ˈlɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈpæn.tʃən ˈlɑː.mə / (BrE)
the second highest spiritual leader in Tibetan Buddhism, ranking immediately after the Dalai Lama
second highest lama in Tibetan Buddhism

Examples

The Panchen Lama plays a vital role in Tibetan Buddhist religious practices.
Controversy surrounds the identification of the current Panchen Lama.
Pilgrims traveled great distances to receive blessings from the Panchen Lama.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Dalai Lama - the highest spiritual leader in Tibetan Buddhism

Etymology

From Tibetan pan-chen ('great scholar') and Sanskrit lama ('master, teacher').
