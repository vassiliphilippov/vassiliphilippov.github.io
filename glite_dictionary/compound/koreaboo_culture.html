Koreaboo culture

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːriəˌbu ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːriəˌbu ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
A cultural phenomenon characterized by excessive or obsessive admiration of South Korean popular culture by non-Koreans, often accompanied by attempts to imitate Korean language, behavior, or style.
excessive admiration of Korean culture
informal slang

Examples

The rise of Koreaboo culture has led to greater interest in Korean language classes worldwide.
Some criticize Koreaboo culture for promoting stereotypes rather than genuine understanding.
Her fascination with Korean dramas and music quickly evolved into participation in Koreaboo culture.

Synset Examples

The Koreaboo culture fascinates many outside of Korea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeaboo - a non-Japanese person obsessed with Japanese culture, especially anime and manga
  • Degree Lower: K-pop - a genre of popular music originating from South Korea

Etymology

Blend of 'Korea' and '-boo' (from 'weeaboo', a slang term for people obsessed with Japanese culture), with 'culture'.
