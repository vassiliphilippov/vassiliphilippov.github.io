sold out

1. ADJ

/ soʊld aʊt / (AmE), / səʊld aʊt / (BrE)
(1) No longer having tickets or items available for sale; fully booked. (2) Having betrayed one's principles or values, especially for personal gain or profit.
fully booked; or betrayed principles for profit

Examples

The tickets for the performance were sold out by noon.
Many critics accused the artist of having sold out to commercial interests.
I arrived late and found the restaurant was completely sold out of its famous pastries.

Synset Examples

Many fans felt he sold out when he changed genres.
I was disappointed to find the event sold-out.
The concert was completely sold-out within hours.
All tickets for the show were sold-out quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booked up - completely reserved or filled up, especially of accommodation or tickets
  • Synonym: sold out (betrayed) - betrayed principles for personal profit; informal usage of 'sold out'
  • Antonym: available - able to be obtained or bought (antonym to 'sold out' in ticketing sense)
