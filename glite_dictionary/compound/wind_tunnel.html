wind tunnel

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd ˈtʌnəl / (AmE), / wɪnd ˈtʌnəl / (BrE)
a large structure in which air is moved at controlled speeds to test the effects of airflow on objects such as aircraft or cars
structure for testing effects of moving air

Examples

Engineers use a wind tunnel to test new airplane designs before full production begins.
The car was placed inside a wind tunnel to measure its aerodynamic efficiency.
Research on the effects of wind on bridges often involves scale models in a wind tunnel.
