young blood

1. NOUN

/ jʌŋ blʌd / (AmE), / jʌŋ blʌd / (BrE)
A younger member or group of members of an organization or society, considered as a source of fresh ideas or renewed energy.
new energetic member(s) of a group

Examples

The company hired some young blood to bring fresh ideas into the team.
We need young blood if the club is going to survive for another decade.
Bringing in young blood revitalized the committee and encouraged new initiatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: new blood - a new member introduced to revitalize a group
