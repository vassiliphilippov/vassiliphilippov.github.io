diatonic scale

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈtɑ.nɪk skeɪl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈtɒ.nɪk skeɪl / (BrE)
a musical scale with seven distinct pitches per octave, arranged in a specific pattern of whole and half steps; in standard Western music, it forms the basis of major and minor scales
musical scale with specific interval pattern

Examples

The melody moves up a diatonic scale, creating a familiar and natural sound.
Many folk songs are based on the diatonic scale rather than chromatic scales.
Learning to play the diatonic scale is a fundamental skill for any aspiring pianist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chromatic scale - a musical scale with twelve pitches, each separated by a semitone

Etymology

From Greek 'diatonikos' (stretching to the octave).
