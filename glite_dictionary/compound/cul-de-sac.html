cul-de-sac

1. NOUN

/ kʌl də ˈsæk / (AmE), / kʌl də ˈsæk / (BrE)
a street or passage closed at one end, usually with only one way in or out
a street closed at one end

Examples

Their new house is located at the end of a quiet cul-de-sac.
Children rode their bicycles safely around the cul-de-sac.
We had to turn around because the road ended in a cul-de-sac.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead end - a street with no exit at one end

Etymology

From French 'cul-de-sac', literally 'bottom of a sack'.
