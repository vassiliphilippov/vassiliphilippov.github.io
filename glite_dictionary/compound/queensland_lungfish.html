Queensland lungfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwiːnzˌlænd ˈlʌŋˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkwiːnz.lənd ˈlʌŋ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A rare, endangered species of lungfish native to rivers in Queensland, Australia, known for its ancient lineage and ability to breathe air.
endangered Australian lungfish species

Examples

The Queensland lungfish is a living fossil, virtually unchanged for millions of years.
Researchers are working to conserve the Queensland lungfish due to its endangered status.
Unlike most fish, the Queensland lungfish can breathe air using a single lung.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungfish - a freshwater fish with both gills and lungs
