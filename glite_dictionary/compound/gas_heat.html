gas heat

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs hiːt / (AmE), / ɡæs hiːt / (BrE)
A heating system that uses natural gas as its source of energy.
heating system using natural gas

Examples

The old apartment building relied on gas heat to keep residents warm during the winter.
After switching to gas heat, their monthly bills went down considerably.
Many homeowners prefer gas heat for its efficiency and cost savings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electric heat - a heating system that uses electricity as its source
  • Antonym: oil heat - a heating system that burns oil for heating
