hoary puccoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔɹi pəˈkun / (AmE), / ˈhɔːri pəˈkuːn / (BrE)
A perennial North American plant with greyish hairy leaves, known for producing a red or yellow pigment.
North American plant with hairy leaves

Examples

The hoary puccoon blooms in spring, brightening prairies with its colorful flowers.
Early settlers used the hoary puccoon root to produce natural dye.
Conservationists are working to restore hoary puccoon habitats across the Midwest.

Etymology

From 'hoary', meaning greyish white (referring to the hairy leaves), and 'puccoon', from Virginia Algonquian *poughkone* (a plant that yields a pigment).
