tangible goods

1. NOUN

/ ˈtændʒəbəl ɡʊdz / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪbəl ɡʊdz / (BrE)
items or products that have a physical form and can be touched or handled
physical items; goods that can be touched

Examples

Retailers must keep accurate inventory records of all their tangible goods.
Unlike online services, tangible goods require storage and shipping to customers.
Buying tangible goods often offers the benefit of physical ownership.

Synset Examples

A physical good may cost more than an intangible good, but is easier to return or track.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intangible goods - products that do not have a physical presence, such as digital items or services

Etymology

tangible from Latin tangibilis (that may be touched) + goods, Middle English godes (possessions).
