male fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪl fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈmeɪl fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of fern native to North America and Europe, whose rhizomes and stalks produce an oleoresin formerly used to expel tapeworms
fern species, source of tapeworm remedy

Examples

The rhizomes of the male fern were once widely harvested for medicinal purposes.
Botanists studied the male fern to better understand its ability to expel parasitic worms.
You can still find the male fern growing wild in many shaded woodlands across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dryopteris filix-mas - the scientific name for the male fern

Etymology

Named for its supposed 'masculine' characteristics, contrasting with the 'female fern'.
