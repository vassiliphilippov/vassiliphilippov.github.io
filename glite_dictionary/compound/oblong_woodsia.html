oblong woodsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːblɔŋ ˈwʊdsiə / (AmE), / ˈɒblɒŋ ˈwʊdsiə / (BrE)
a common fern of northern temperate regions, typically growing on rocks, with rusty-brown stalks and lance-shaped, pinnate fronds
a northern temperate rock-dwelling fern

Examples

The oblong woodsia thrives in rocky outcrops throughout the northern forests.
We identified several oblong woodsia ferns growing near the mountain trail.
Botanists often study oblong woodsia for its ability to survive in harsh, rocky environments.
