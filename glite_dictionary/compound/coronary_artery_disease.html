coronary artery disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.əˌner.i ˈɑr.t̬ə.ri dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əˌnɛr.i ˈɑː.tə.ri dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A condition in which the arteries that supply blood to the heart muscle become hardened and narrowed, often due to buildup of cholesterol and other materials.
disease of the heart's arteries
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with coronary artery disease, he changed his diet and began regular exercise.
Smoking is a major risk factor for developing coronary artery disease.
Many heart attacks are caused by coronary artery disease blocking blood flow to heart tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - hardening and thickening of artery walls
  • Synonym: atherosclerosis - buildup of fats, cholesterol, and other substances in artery walls

Etymology

From Latin 'coronarius' (relating to a crown, referring to the arteries encircling the heart) + 'artery' + 'disease'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəˌnɛri ˈɑrtɚi dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri ˈɑːtəri dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A condition in which fatty deposits build up in the walls of the coronary arteries, causing them to narrow and restricting blood flow to the heart.
disease caused by narrowing of coronary arteries
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with coronary artery disease after complaining of chest pain and shortness of breath.
Smoking and a sedentary lifestyle can significantly increase the risk of developing coronary artery disease.
Treatments for coronary artery disease may include lifestyle changes, medication, or surgery such as angioplasty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - hardening or thickening of the arteries
  • Synonym: atherosclerosis - a form of arteriosclerosis due to fatty deposits in the arteries

Etymology

From Latin 'coronarius' (pertaining to a crown or encircling, referring to the arteries encircling the heart) and 'arteria' (artery) + 'disease'.
