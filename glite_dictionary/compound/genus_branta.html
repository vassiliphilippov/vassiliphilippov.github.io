genus Branta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈbræntə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈbrantə / (BrE)
A genus of wild geese that includes several species native to the Northern Hemisphere, such as the Canada goose and the barnacle goose.
a genus of wild geese

Examples

The genus Branta includes species like the Canada goose and the barnacle goose.
Research on migratory patterns often focuses on geese in the genus Branta.
The distinctive black head is a key characteristic of birds in the genus Branta.

Etymology

From Latin 'Branta', the scientific name for this goose genus.
