peace process

1. NOUN

/ pis ˈproʊ.sɛs / (AmE), / piːs ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a series of diplomatic actions or negotiations intended to end conflict and promote lasting peace between opposing parties, especially in regions affected by war or violence
series of actions to achieve peace

Examples

The government announced a new peace process in hopes of ending the decades-long civil war.
International organizations are often called on to facilitate a stalled peace process.
Many citizens remain skeptical about the sincerity of their leaders in pursuing the peace process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiation - discussion aimed at reaching an agreement
  • Synonym: conflict resolution - the process of resolving a dispute or disagreement
  • Antonym: war - armed conflict between groups or countries
