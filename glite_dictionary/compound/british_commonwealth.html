British Commonwealth

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈkɑmənˌwɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈkɒmənwɛlθ / (BrE)
an association of the United Kingdom and its former colonies that are now independent sovereign states, united by historical ties and allegiance to the British Crown
association of UK and former colonies

Examples

The British Commonwealth played a significant role in shaping modern international relations.
Several countries gained independence but chose to remain in the British Commonwealth.
Queen Elizabeth II has long served as the symbolic head of the British Commonwealth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Commonwealth of Nations - modern successor to the British Commonwealth; current name of the voluntary association

Etymology

Formed from 'British' (of or relating to Britain) and 'Commonwealth' (from Middle English 'commun welthe', common good).
