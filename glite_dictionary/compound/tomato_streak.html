tomato streak

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ strik / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ striːk / (BrE)
A disease affecting various plants such as tomatoes, potatoes, and peas, caused by a mixed infection of potato and tomato mosaic viruses, resulting in streaks or mottling on the plants.
disease caused by mixed mosaic virus infection in plants

Examples

Agricultural scientists are studying how tomato streak affects crop yields in different climates.
Some tomato plants showed signs of tomato streak after a prolonged period of rain.
Proper crop rotation can help prevent the spread of tomato streak among susceptible plants.
