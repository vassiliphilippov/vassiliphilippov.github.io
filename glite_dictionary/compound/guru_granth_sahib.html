Guru Granth Sahib

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡuːru ˌɡrɑnθ səˈhiːb / (AmE), / ˈɡʊəruː ˌɡrʌnθ səˈhiːb / (BrE)
the principal sacred scripture of Sikhism, consisting of hymns, poetry, and the teachings of the first five Sikh gurus and other saints
sacred scripture of Sikhism

Examples

Sikhs treat the Guru Granth Sahib with great reverence in their places of worship.
The Guru Granth Sahib was compiled by Guru Arjan, the fifth Sikh guru, in 1604.
During important ceremonies, passages from the Guru Granth Sahib are read aloud to the congregation.

Etymology

From Punjabi, meaning 'respected teacher's exposition'; 'Guru' (teacher), 'Granth' (book), 'Sahib' (honorific).
