functionally illiterate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənəli ɪˈlɪtərət / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃnəli ɪˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
Having reading and writing skills that are inadequate for managing daily living and employment tasks that require literacy beyond a basic level.
unable to read/write sufficiently for daily needs

Examples

Even though he finished high school, he is functionally illiterate and struggles to fill out job applications.
Many adults are functionally illiterate, making it difficult for them to read instructions or manage finances.
The literacy program aims to help those who are functionally illiterate improve their basic reading and writing skills.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: illiterate - unable to read or write at all

Etymology

functionally from 'function' (Latin functio, -onis, performance) + illiterate (from Latin illitteratus, unlearned).
