Ch'in dynasty

1. PROPN

/ tʃɪn ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / tʃɪn ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the Chinese dynasty (246 BC–206 BC) that established the first centralized imperial government and began construction of much of the Great Wall
Chinese dynasty, 246–206 BC

Examples

The unification of China under the Ch'in dynasty marked a turning point in the country's history.
The Ch'in dynasty is famous for beginning work on the Great Wall and standardizing Chinese script.
Many scholars study artifacts from the Ch'in dynasty to better understand early imperial China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Qin dynasty - the same Chinese dynasty as rendered with Pinyin spelling

Etymology

From Wade-Giles romanization of Mandarin 'Qín', the ruling house's name.
