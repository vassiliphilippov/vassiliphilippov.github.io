savings bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋz bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋz bɒnd / (BrE)
a government bond that cannot be bought or sold after its original purchase, typically used by individuals to save money and earn interest
government bond for saving, non-negotiable

Examples

Many people invest in savings bonds to prepare for their children's education expenses.
The savings bond matured after ten years, paying back more than its original value.
She received a savings bond as a birthday gift from her grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government bond - a bond issued by a government to raise funds
