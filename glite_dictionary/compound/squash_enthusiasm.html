to squash enthusiasm

1. VERB

/ skwɑʃ ɪnˈθuziˌæzəm / (AmE), / skwɒʃ ɪnˈθjuːziˌæzəm / (BrE)
to deliberately suppress or diminish someone's excitement or eagerness
deliberately suppress excitement

Examples

Her manager's harsh comments seemed to squash enthusiasm among the whole team.
I didn't mean to squash enthusiasm, but I had to point out the project risks.
The teacher never wants to squash enthusiasm in her students for asking questions.

Synset Examples

He tried to squash her enthusiasm, but she persisted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dampen enthusiasm - to make someone less excited or enthusiastic
