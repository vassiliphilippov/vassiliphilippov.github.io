Sinai Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪnaɪ ˈdɛzərt / (AmE), / ˈsɪnaɪ ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a large desert region on the Sinai Peninsula in northeastern Egypt, lying between the Mediterranean Sea to the north and the Red Sea to the south
desert region in northeastern Egypt

Examples

Many biblical stories are believed to have taken place in the Sinai Desert.
The Sinai Desert is known for its dramatic landscapes and harsh climate.
Bedouin tribes have traveled across the Sinai Desert for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sinai Peninsula - the peninsula in northeastern Egypt between the Mediterranean and Red Seas
  • Degree Higher: Sahara Desert - the world's largest hot desert in North Africa
  • Degree Lower: Negev Desert - a desert region in southern Israel

Etymology

Sinai from Hebrew 'Sīnai', referring to the biblical Mount Sinai.
