New English Bible

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / njuː ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈbaɪbəl / (BrE)
a modern English translation of the Bible and Apocrypha, first published in the mid-20th century
modern English Bible translation

Examples

Many churches in the UK use the New English Bible for their readings and study groups.
The New English Bible aims to provide a clear and modern version of scripture for contemporary readers.
He compared passages in the New English Bible with those in older translations to better understand the meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bible - the sacred scriptures of Christianity consisting of the Old and New Testaments
