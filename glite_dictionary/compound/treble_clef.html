treble clef

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛbəl klɛf / (AmE), / ˈtrɛbəl klɛf / (BrE)
the symbol at the beginning of a staff in written music indicating that the notes are to be read with the G above middle C on the second line
musical clef symbol for high pitches

Examples

The violin part is written in the treble clef.
She learned to identify the treble clef when reading sheet music.
Guitarists usually read music written in the treble clef.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bass clef - clef used for lower-pitched notes, placing F below middle C on the fourth line

Etymology

From Old French 'treble' (high-pitched) and Middle English 'clef' (key, symbol), from French, from Latin 'clavis' (key).
