piece of eight

1. NOUN

/ ˌpis əv ˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpiːs əv ˈeɪt / (BrE)
an old Spanish silver coin valued at eight reales, widely used in international trade from the 16th to 19th centuries
old Spanish silver coin (8 reales)
archaic

Examples

The pirate captain emptied a chest filled with pieces of eight onto the table.
In colonial America, a piece of eight was often accepted as legal currency.
He found an old piece of eight while digging on the beach.

Etymology

From Spanish 'peso de a ocho' (piece of eight [reales]).
