binary digit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌnɛri ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪnəri ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
either of the two symbols, 0 or 1, used in binary number notation, forming the basic unit of information in computing.
either 0 or 1 in binary notation

Examples

In digital electronics, a binary digit can have only two possible values: 0 or 1.
Each binary digit in a sequence represents a power of two.
The term 'bit' is an abbreviation of binary digit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - the basic unit of information in computing (short for binary digit)

Etymology

From 'binary' (from Latin 'binarius', meaning 'containing two') and 'digit' (from Latin 'digitus', meaning 'finger, toe').
