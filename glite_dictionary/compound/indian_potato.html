Indian potato

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən pəˈtoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən pəˈtəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
A North American climbing plant (Apios americana) with fragrant flowers and edible tubers, traditionally used as food by Native Americans.
North American vine with edible tubers

Examples

Early settlers learned to eat Indian potato from Native Americans.
The Indian potato produces clusters of small, nutritious tubers underground.
Many foragers seek out Indian potato during the late summer months.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant native to central and eastern North America, with edible tuberous roots
North American plant with edible tubers

Examples

Early settlers learned to cook the Indian potato roots as a source of nourishment.
The tubers of the Indian potato are edible and were gathered by Native Americans for centuries.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Indian potato for its attractive flowers and nutritious roots.
