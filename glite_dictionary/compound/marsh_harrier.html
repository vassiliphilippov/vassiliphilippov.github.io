marsh harrier

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈhɛriər / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈhæriə / (BrE)
a large, slender bird of prey (Circus aeruginosus and related species) native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, found mainly in marshy or wetland areas
a bird of prey found in wetlands

Examples

We spotted a marsh harrier gliding silently over the reeds at dawn.
The wetlands are an important breeding ground for the endangered marsh harrier.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of marsh harriers in the region.

Etymology

From 'marsh' (wetland) + 'harrier' (a bird of prey in the genus Circus).
