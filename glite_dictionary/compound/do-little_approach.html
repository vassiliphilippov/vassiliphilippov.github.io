do-little approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈduː ˌlɪtəl əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈduː ˌlɪtəl əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a policy or way of acting that involves making minimal effort or intervention
policy of minimal effort or involvement

Examples

The CEO's do-little approach let problems escalate until they were out of control.
Politicians criticized the mayor for taking a do-little approach to public safety.
Instead of facing the issue, he adopted a do-little approach and hoped it would resolve itself.

Synset Examples

She prefers a do-little approach to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hands-off approach - an approach involving little or no direct intervention
  • Antonym: active approach - an approach involving active involvement or intervention
