out-and-out

1. ADJ

/ aʊt ənd aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt ənd aʊt / (BrE)
used to emphasize that someone or something is a complete or total example of a particular quality, often something negative.
complete; total; utter (esp. negative)
informal

Examples

The accusation was an out-and-out lie, with no basis in reality.
Her latest novel is an out-and-out success among critics and readers alike.
It was out-and-out chaos in the stadium after the final whistle.

Related Terms
