library fine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪbrɛri faɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪbrəri faɪn / (BrE)
a sum of money charged by a library for returning borrowed books or other materials after the due date
money charged for overdue library books

Examples

If you return your book late, you may have to pay a library fine.
She accumulated a hefty library fine by forgetting to bring back her textbooks on time.
The school waived all library fines during the holiday period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fine - a sum of money exacted as a penalty by a court of law or other authority
