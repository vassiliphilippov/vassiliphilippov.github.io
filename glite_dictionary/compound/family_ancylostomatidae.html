family Ancylostomatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæŋsɪloʊstəˈmeɪtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˌænˌsɪlɒstəˈmeɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic nematode worms known as hookworms, which infect the intestines of mammals, including humans.
family of parasitic hookworms
medical

Examples

The family Ancylostomatidae includes several species that cause significant health problems in humans and animals.
Medical researchers study how the family Ancylostomatidae infects intestinal tracts and spreads disease.
Veterinary textbooks discuss the life cycle of the family Ancylostomatidae when covering internal parasites in dogs and cats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hookworm - a parasitic nematode worm that normally inhabits the intestines of mammals

Etymology

From modern Latin Ancylostomatidae, based on Greek ankylos (crooked) and stoma (mouth).
