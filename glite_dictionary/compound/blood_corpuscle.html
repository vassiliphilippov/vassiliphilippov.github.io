blood corpuscle

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈkɔːrpəsəl / (AmE), / blʌd ˈkɔːpəsl / (BrE)
any of the cells found in blood, such as red blood cells (erythrocytes), white blood cells (leukocytes), and sometimes platelets.
cell found in blood
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the blood corpuscle appeared misshapen, indicating a possible disorder.
Platelets, often considered a type of blood corpuscle, are essential for normal clotting.
The doctor explained that a reduction in red blood corpuscle count could lead to anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood cell - a cell normally found in blood, including red and white cells and platelets

Etymology

From Latin 'corpusculum' (a little body), diminutive of 'corpus' (body).
