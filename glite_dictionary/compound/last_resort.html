last resort

1. NOUN

/ læst rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / lɑːst rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
a final course of action to be taken only when all else has failed
final option in desperate situations

Examples

If negotiations fail, military action may be considered a last resort.
She saw borrowing money from her parents as a last resort.
Calling the police should be a last resort, not your first reaction.

Synset Examples

‘pis aller’ is French for ‘worst going’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last ditch - a final attempt or measure, especially when everything else has failed

2. NOUN

/ læst rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / lɑːst rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
the final course of action or option available when all others have failed
final possible option

Examples

Negotiation was their last resort before declaring a strike.
If all else fails, surgery will be used as a last resort.
Borrowing money from friends should only be a last resort.

Synset Examples

To drown the miller was a last resort for the desperate farmers.
He considered it a last resort to ask for help.
They saw the new strategy as a last resort.
Calling her was my last resort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last ditch - final attempt before failure
  • Antonym: first resort - the initial or preferred option
