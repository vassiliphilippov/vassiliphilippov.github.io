Winter Olympic Games

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪntɚ əˈlɪmpɪk ɡeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə əˈlɪmpɪk ɡeɪmz / (BrE)
a major international sporting event held every four years for competitions in winter sports such as skiing, skating, and ice hockey
international winter sports competition

Examples

The Winter Olympic Games will be hosted in Italy in 2026.
Athletes from around the world compete in the Winter Olympic Games every four years.
Snowboarding was first introduced to the Winter Olympic Games in 1998.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Summer Olympic Games - an international multi-sport event held every four years for summer sports

Etymology

From 'Olympic Games', named after Olympia in Greece, with 'winter' specifying the season for these events (first held in 1924).
