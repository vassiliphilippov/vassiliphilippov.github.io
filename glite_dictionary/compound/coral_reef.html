coral reef

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəl ˈrif / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl ˈriːf / (BrE)
A ridge of rock in the sea formed by the growth and accumulation of coral, often creating reefs of limestone.
a marine ridge formed from coral

Examples

The Great Barrier coral reef is visible from space and is home to thousands of marine species.
Overfishing and pollution are major threats to the health of a coral reef.
Snorkelers can admire the bright fish and colorful corals living in the shallow waters above the coral reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reef - a ridge of rock or sand just above or below the surface of the sea
