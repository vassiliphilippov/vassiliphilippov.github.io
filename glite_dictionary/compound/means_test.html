means test

1. NOUN

/ ˈminz tɛst / (AmE), / ˈmiːnz tɛst / (BrE)
an official examination of someone’s financial resources to determine eligibility for financial aid or welfare benefits
assessment of finances for aid eligibility

Examples

Applicants must undergo a means test to qualify for social assistance.
The government uses a means test to determine who is eligible for public housing.
After failing the means test, she was denied further financial support.
