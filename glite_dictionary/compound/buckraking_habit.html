buckraking habit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌreɪkɪŋ ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌreɪkɪŋ ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
the practice or pattern of making money, especially by public officials or professionals, through methods considered unethical or improper
unethical money-making practice

Examples

The senator's buckraking habit was exposed by investigative journalists.
Many citizens are frustrated by the buckraking habit seen among public officials.
His buckraking habit eventually led to his resignation from office.

Synset Examples

His buckraking habits eventually led to his downfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckraking - making money by exploiting one's position, especially unethically
  • Synonym: graft - dishonest or illegal use of a person’s position to get money or advantages

Etymology

From American slang 'buckraking' (making easy money) and 'habit' (a regular practice).
