lieutenant colonel

1. NOUN

/ luːˈtɛnənt ˈkɝːnəl / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt ˈkɜːnəl / (BrE)
a commissioned officer in the armed forces, ranking above major and below colonel, especially in the army, air force, or marines
military rank above major, below colonel

Examples

The battalion was led by a lieutenant colonel with twenty years of experience.
After his promotion, she addressed him as lieutenant colonel during the ceremony.
As a lieutenant colonel, he was responsible for several hundred soldiers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: colonel - a commissioned officer above lieutenant colonel
  • Degree Lower: major - a commissioned officer below lieutenant colonel

Etymology

From French 'lieutenant' (deputy) and 'colonel' (commander of a column).
