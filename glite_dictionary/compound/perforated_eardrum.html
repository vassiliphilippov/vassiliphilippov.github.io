perforated eardrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrfəˌreɪtɪd ˈɪrˌdrʌm / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfəreɪtɪd ˈɪədrʌm / (BrE)
an eardrum with a hole or tear in it, often causing hearing loss and increased risk of ear infections
eardrum with a hole or tear
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a perforated eardrum could cause temporary hearing loss.
She was diagnosed with a perforated eardrum after experiencing severe ear pain.
Swimming with a perforated eardrum increases the risk of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruptured eardrum - an eardrum that has burst or split open
