pilomotor reflex

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪloʊˈmoʊtər ˈriːflɛks / (AmE), / ˌpɪləʊˈməʊtə ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
the reflex causing the erection of hairs on the skin in response to cold, emotional stress, or skin irritation
reflex that raises hairs on the skin
medical

Examples

The pilomotor reflex can be triggered by a sudden chill or a strong emotional response.
When frightened, many animals display a visible pilomotor reflex as their fur stands on end.
Skin irritation may also activate the pilomotor reflex, leading to noticeable goosebumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosebumps - the raised bumps on the skin caused by the pilomotor reflex

Etymology

From Latin 'pīlus' (hair) + 'motor' (mover) + 'reflex' (from Latin 'reflexus', turned back). Commonly used in physiology and medicine.
