fever pitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːvər pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːvə pɪtʃ / (BrE)
a state of intense excitement or agitation
intense excitement

Examples

The excitement in the stadium reached fever pitch as the final goal was scored.
Rumors about the new product launch sent anticipation to fever pitch in the tech community.
As the trial neared its verdict, tension in the courtroom was at fever pitch.

Synset Examples

the crowd was at fever pitch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frenzy - a state of wild excitement or uncontrolled activity
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness or lack of strong emotion

Etymology

Originated as a musical term; from the idea of fever as high temperature and pitch as a degree of intensity.
