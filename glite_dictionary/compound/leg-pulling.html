leg-pulling

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛɡ ˌpʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡ ˌpʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of jokingly trying to make someone believe something that is not true
joking act of deceiving someone

Examples

His constant leg-pulling made it hard to know when he was being serious.
She didn't realize it was just leg-pulling until everyone started laughing.
After all that leg-pulling, I finally understood the joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasing - making fun of or provoking someone in a playful way
  • Synonym: practical joke - a trick played on someone in order to make them look foolish or amuse others

Etymology

From the literal phrase 'to pull someone's leg', meaning to tease or joke by deception, first recorded in the late 19th century.
