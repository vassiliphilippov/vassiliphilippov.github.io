calendar day

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.ən.dɚ deɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ən.də deɪ / (BrE)
a period of twenty-four hours beginning at midnight, as used in calendars or for legal and official purposes
a day from midnight to midnight

Examples

The contract must be signed within seven calendar days of receipt.
All orders placed before noon will be processed the same calendar day.
Interest on the loan is calculated based on the actual number of calendar days in the month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: business day - a day when business is typically conducted, usually excluding weekends and holidays

Etymology

From 'calendar' (from Latin 'calendarium', account book) + 'day'.
