mythical place

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪθɪkəl pleɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪkəl pleɪs / (BrE)
A place that exists only in imagination or legend, often described in fictional, mythological, or religious texts.
imaginary or legendary place

Examples

Many stories speak of the mythical place of Atlantis, lost beneath the sea.
For centuries, explorers searched for El Dorado, a mythical place of immense wealth.
He longed to visit a mythical place where all his dreams could come true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect place or state
  • Antonym: real place - an actual location that exists in the real world

Etymology

From Greek mȳthos (myth) + Latin locus (place).
