superfamily Tyrannidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌfæm.ə.li taɪˌræ.nɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌfæm.ɪ.li tɪˌran.ɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A large group of New World tyrant flycatchers, most numerous in Central and South America, with species also found in the United States and Canada.
taxonomic group of tyrant flycatchers

Examples

The superfamily Tyrannidae contains over 400 species of birds.
Researchers studied migration patterns within the superfamily Tyrannidae to understand their adaptation to changing climates.
Some of the most colorful members of the superfamily Tyrannidae can be found in the rainforests of Brazil.

Etymology

From taxonomic Latin: 'superfamily' denoting a higher taxonomic grouping, and 'Tyrannidae', from Latin 'tyrannus' (tyrant) + '-idae' (family suffix).
