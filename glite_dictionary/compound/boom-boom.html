boom-boom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbum ˌbum / (AmE), / ˈbuːm ˌbuːm / (BrE)
a loud, deep, explosive sound, often used to represent the noise of an explosion, fireworks, or a gunshot
loud explosive sound

Examples

The fireworks went off with a loud boom-boom that echoed down the street.
Suddenly, we heard a boom-boom from the construction site next door.
The play ended dramatically with a boom-boom that startled the entire audience.

Synset Examples

The fireworks went off with a loud boom-boom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise, like an explosion or gunshot
  • Synonym: crash - a loud, sudden noise as of something breaking
