Monell metal

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɛl ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / məˈnɛl ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
an alloy consisting primarily of nickel and copper, often with small amounts of iron, manganese, or aluminum, used for its strength and corrosion resistance
nickel-copper alloy, corrosion-resistant

Examples

The propeller was made from Monell metal to ensure durability in saltwater.
Because of its high resistance to corrosion, Monell metal is often used in chemical processing equipment.
The engineer recommended using Monell metal for the heat exchanger due to its strength.

Etymology

Named after Ambrose Monell, an American industrialist.
