milkweed butterfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlkwid ˌbʌtɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlkwiːd ˌbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
a large American butterfly, typically with deep orange wings and black and white markings, whose larvae feed on milkweed plants; includes the monarch butterfly
large orange American butterfly; larvae eat milkweed

Examples

Every autumn, thousands of milkweed butterflies migrate south across North America.
The bright orange wings of the milkweed butterfly help warn predators of its toxicity.
Children often observe the transformation from caterpillar to milkweed butterfly in school science projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarch butterfly - famous migratory American butterfly with orange and black wings, Danaus plexippus
