gold mine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊld ˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊld ˌmaɪn / (BrE)
A place where gold is extracted from the earth by mining.
mine where gold is extracted

Examples

The discovery of a new gold mine revitalized the entire region, bringing jobs and economic growth.
They visited an abandoned gold mine during their vacation in Australia.
The company's profits soared after acquiring a productive gold mine in Nevada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an excavation in the earth for extracting minerals

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld maɪn / (AmE), / ɡəʊld maɪn / (BrE)
a place, source, or thing that produces or contains a large amount of something valuable or desirable
source of plentiful valuable or desired things

Examples

The internet is a gold mine of information for researchers.
Their attic turned out to be a gold mine of antique furniture.
This old cookbook is a gold mine for anyone who loves baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasure trove - a place or source containing an abundant amount of something valuable

Etymology

From literal 'gold mine', meaning a place where gold is mined, used figuratively for abundant valuable sources since the 19th century.
