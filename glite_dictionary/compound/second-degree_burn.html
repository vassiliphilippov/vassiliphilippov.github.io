second-degree burn

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd dɪˌɡri bɝn / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd dɪˌɡri bɜːn / (BrE)
A burn in which the skin forms blisters and there is superficial destruction of the dermis.
burn causing blisters and dermis damage
medical

Examples

The child suffered a second-degree burn after accidentally touching the hot oven door.
Treatment for a second-degree burn usually involves cooling the area and covering it with a clean bandage.
Because he had a second-degree burn, his skin developed painful blisters and redness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: first-degree burn - a mild burn affecting only the outer layer of skin (epidermis)
  • Degree Higher: third-degree burn - a severe burn involving destruction of the entire thickness of the skin
