wheat scab

1. NOUN

/ wiːt skæb / (AmE), / wiːt skab / (BrE)
a disease affecting the heads of wheat plants, caused by certain fungi and resulting in damage to the grain and reduced yield
disease of wheat heads

Examples

Farmers worry about wheat scab reducing this year's harvest.
Researchers are developing resistant varieties to combat wheat scab.
Early detection of wheat scab is crucial to prevent the spread of the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scab - a plant disease causing crusty spots or lesions
