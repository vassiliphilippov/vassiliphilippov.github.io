Areca palm

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɛkə pɑm / (AmE), / əˈriːkə pɑːm / (BrE)
A palm native to southeastern Asia that bears betel nuts, which are scarlet or orange single-seeded fruits with a fibrous husk.
southeast Asian palm producing betel nuts

Examples

The Areca palm is widely cultivated in tropical regions for its ornamental value.
Farmers harvest the fruit of the Areca palm to produce betel nuts.
The leaves of the Areca palm are sometimes used for making baskets and mats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betel nut - the seed of the Areca palm, often chewed in Asia

Etymology

From genus name Areca, likely derived from Malayalam 'adakka', referring to the betel nut.
