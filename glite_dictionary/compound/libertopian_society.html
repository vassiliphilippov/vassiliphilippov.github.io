libertopian society

1. NOUN

/ lɪbərˈtoʊpiən səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / lɪbəˈtəʊpiən səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
A hypothetical society organized according to libertarian principles, emphasizing individual freedom, minimal government, and free markets.
society based on libertarian ideals

Examples

Many novels depict a libertopian society where government intervention is minimal and property rights are absolute.
The founders aimed to create a libertopian society with maximum personal autonomy and voluntary cooperation.
Some critics argue that a true libertopian society is unrealistic in practice despite its philosophical appeal.

Synset Examples

They sought a libertopian society built on freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: libertarianism - a political philosophy seeking to maximize individual liberty
  • Degree Higher: utopia - an imagined perfect society

Etymology

Modelled on 'libertarian' and 'utopian'.
