savings bank trust

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋz bæŋk trʌst / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋz baŋk trʌst / (BrE)
A savings account created by an individual who acts as trustee for a named beneficiary, retaining control during their lifetime, with the remaining funds payable to the beneficiary after the trustee's death.
savings account held in trust for a beneficiary
legal

Examples

She set up a savings bank trust so her grandson would inherit the money after her death.
Many people use a savings bank trust to ensure their assets are passed on directly to beneficiaries.
The terms of the savings bank trust allowed her to manage the funds during her lifetime.
