peace sign

1. NOUN

/ ˈpis saɪn / (AmE), / ˈpiːs saɪn / (BrE)
a symbol consisting of three lines within a circle, representing peace, especially associated with anti-war movements since the mid-20th century.
symbol for peace (three lines in a circle)

Examples

She wore a silver necklace featuring a peace sign pendant.
The protesters carried banners and painted the peace sign on their faces.
A faded peace sign was spray-painted on the old brick wall downtown.

Synset Examples

In the 1950s the ‘peace sign’ as it is known today, was designed as the logo for the British Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament and adopted by anti-war and counterculture activists in the United States and elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dove - a bird commonly used as a symbol of peace
  • Synonym: olive branch - an object representing peace or the offer of peace

Etymology

Created in 1958 by Gerald Holtom for the British Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament.
