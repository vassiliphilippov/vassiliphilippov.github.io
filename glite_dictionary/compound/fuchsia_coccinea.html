Fuchsia coccinea

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːʃə kɒkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʃə kɒkˈsɪniə / (BrE)
an erect or climbing shrub native to Brazil, with deep pink to red flowers, belonging to the genus Fuchsia
Brazilian shrub with red flowers

Examples

The Fuchsia coccinea thrives best in moist, shaded areas of Brazilian forests.
Botanists admire the striking deep red blossoms of Fuchsia coccinea.
Many gardeners cultivate Fuchsia coccinea for its vivid, decorative flowers.

Etymology

Fuchsia from Latinized name of German botanist Leonhart Fuchs; coccinea from Latin 'coccineus' meaning 'scarlet'.
