family Teredinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɛˌraɪdəˈnaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɛrɪˌdɪnɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks, commonly known as shipworms, that bore into and damage wood submerged in seawater.
family of wood-boring shipworms

Examples

Many species within the family Teredinidae are notorious for damaging wooden vessels and piers.
Marine biologists discovered a new member of the family Teredinidae off the coast of Indonesia.
Shipbuilders developed new treatments to protect wood from organisms such as the family Teredinidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - a marine bivalve mollusk that bores into submerged wood

Etymology

From New Latin Teredinidae, based on Teredo (type genus), from Greek terēdōn (woodworm).
