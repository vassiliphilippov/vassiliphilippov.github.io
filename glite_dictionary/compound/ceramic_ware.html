ceramic ware

1. NOUN

/ səˈræmɪk wer / (AmE), / səˈræmɪk weə / (BrE)
articles such as plates, cups, or bowls that are made from ceramic materials, typically by firing clay or similar substances.
articles made from ceramic material

Examples

The museum displayed ancient ceramic ware from China and Persia.
She collects vintage ceramic ware such as teapots and serving bowls.
Both practical and decorative, ceramic ware is popular in many households.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pottery - vessels and objects made from fired clay
  • Synonym: porcelain - fine white ceramic material

Etymology

From Greek 'keramikos' (of pottery), via Latin 'ceramicus'.
