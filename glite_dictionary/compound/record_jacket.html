record jacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
a protective outer cover, usually made of cardboard, for a phonograph (vinyl) record
outer cover for a vinyl record

Examples

She carefully slid the vinyl back into its record jacket to protect it from dust.
The album's record jacket featured bold, colorful artwork that caught everyone's attention.
Collectors often seek out original record jackets because of their unique designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record sleeve - the paper or plastic inner sleeve protecting a vinyl record
