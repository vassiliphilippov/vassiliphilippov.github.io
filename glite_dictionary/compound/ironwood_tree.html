ironwood tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌwʊd triː / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌwʊd triː / (BrE)
a small, slow-growing deciduous tree of northern Iran, known for its hard wood and low domed shape
hardwood tree of northern Iran

Examples

The ironwood tree thrives in the forests of northern Iran despite the harsh climate.
Local craftsmen use timber from the ironwood tree to create durable furniture.
The botanical garden recently acquired a rare ironwood tree for its collection.

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌwʊd ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌwʊd ˌtriː / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree of eastern North America, known for its very hard wood, scientifically called hop hornbeam (Ostrya virginiana).
North American tree with very hard wood

Examples

The ironwood tree is valued for its extremely durable wood, often used for tool handles and mallets.
We spotted an ironwood tree growing at the edge of the forest during our hike.
Carpenters admire the ironwood tree because its timber is exceptionally tough and resistant to wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop hornbeam - a tree with hard wood, native to North America and Eurasia
