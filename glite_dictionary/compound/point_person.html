point person

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪnt ˌpɜːrsn̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt ˌpɜːs(ə)n / (BrE)
A soldier who moves at the front of a patrol, responsible for detecting danger or leading the group.
soldier at the front of a patrol

Examples

The point person signaled for the patrol to stop after spotting movement ahead.
Being the point person takes courage, as you are the first to encounter any danger.
During the mission, the point person maintained constant communication with the rest of the squad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scout - A soldier sent ahead to gather information or detect danger
