know-it-all

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ ɪt ɔl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ ɪt ɔːl / (BrE)
a person who acts as if they know everything and is unwilling to accept advice or new information from others
person who acts as if they know everything

Examples

Nobody likes working with a know-it-all who can't accept anyone else's ideas.
She can come across as a know-it-all, but she's actually quite open-minded.
Don't be a know-it-all; sometimes it's important to listen to others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smarty-pants - a person who behaves as if they know everything
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest estimate of one's own importance

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ ɪt ɔl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ ɪt ɔːl / (BrE)
a person who behaves as if they know everything and are eager to share their opinions, often in an annoying way
person who acts as if they know everything

Examples

Nobody likes working with a know-it-all who never listens to other ideas.
Ever since he got that promotion, he's become a real know-it-all.
She tried not to sound like a know-it-all during the meeting.

Synset Examples

He's such a know-it-all that people avoid him at parties.
She acts like a know-it-all, always correcting everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smart aleck - a person who is irritating because they behave as if they know everything
  • Antonym: humble - someone modest about their knowledge or abilities
