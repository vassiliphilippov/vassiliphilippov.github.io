immunologic response

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmjunəˈlɑdʒɪk rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˈlɒdʒɪk rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a bodily defense reaction in which the immune system recognizes and responds to an invading antigen by producing antibodies or activating immune cells specifically against that antigen
body's immune reaction to an antigen
medical

Examples

Vaccines work by stimulating an immunologic response against specific diseases.
The severity of symptoms can depend on the strength of the body's immunologic response.
Researchers studied the immunologic response to the new virus in several patient groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immune response - the reaction of the immune system to foreign substances

Etymology

From 'immunologic', relating to immunity (from Latin 'immunitas', exemption), and 'response'.
