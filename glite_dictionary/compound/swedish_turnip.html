Swedish turnip

1. NOUN

/ ˈswidɪʃ ˈtɚnɪp / (AmE), / ˈswiːdɪʃ ˈtɜːnɪp / (BrE)
A cruciferous plant (Brassica napus Napobrassica) with a large, bulbous, edible yellow root, commonly used as a vegetable; also known as rutabaga or swede.
edible yellow-rooted vegetable plant

Examples

We used Swedish turnip in the stew for a sweet, earthy flavor.
Farmers harvested a large crop of Swedish turnip this year.
In Britain, Swedish turnip is often served mashed with butter as a side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rutabaga - another name for Swedish turnip, especially in North America
  • Synonym: swede - British English name for Swedish turnip

2. NOUN

/ ˈswidɪʃ ˈtɜrnɪp / (AmE), / ˈswiːdɪʃ ˈtɜːnɪp / (BrE)
the large yellow root of a rutabaga plant, eaten as a vegetable
the edible yellow root of a rutabaga

Examples

The recipe calls for a diced Swedish turnip to add a sweet flavor to the soup.
Farmers harvested hundreds of Swedish turnips before the first frost hit.
Have you ever tried roasting Swedish turnip with olive oil and herbs?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rutabaga - a large, yellow-fleshed root vegetable also called "Swede" or "Swedish turnip"
  • Degree Lower: turnip - a different, usually white-fleshed root vegetable
