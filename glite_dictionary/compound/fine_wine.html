fine wine

1. NOUN

/ faɪn waɪn / (AmE), / faɪn waɪn / (BrE)
Wine of the highest quality, often valued for its superior taste and craftsmanship.
high-quality wine

Examples

He keeps a small cellar stocked with only the fine wine from top vineyards.
At the reception, guests enjoyed some fine wine paired with gourmet cheese.
Collectors often invest in fine wine as its value can increase over time.

Synset Examples

The toff ordered the finest wine for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: table wine - ordinary wine usually served with meals
  • Degree Lower: cheap wine - inexpensive, low-quality wine
