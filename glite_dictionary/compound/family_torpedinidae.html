family Torpedinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɔːrˌpɛdɪˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli tɔːˌpɛdɪˈniːdiː / (BrE)
A family of electric rays in the order Torpediniformes, known for their ability to produce electric shocks.
family of electric rays

Examples

The family Torpedinidae includes several species capable of generating electric shocks.
Scientists discovered a new member of the family Torpedinidae off the Mediterranean coast.
The unique defense mechanism of the family Torpedinidae has fascinated marine biologists for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Torpedo - a genus of electric rays

Etymology

From New Latin 'Torpedinidae', based on the genus name 'Torpedo'.
