treacherous water

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛtʃərəs ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃərəs ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
A body of water that is especially dangerous or unpredictable, often due to strong currents, hidden obstacles, or severe weather.
dangerous or unpredictable water conditions

Examples

Many sailors have lost their lives to the treacherous waters off this coast.
Divers are often warned about the treacherous water near the submerged rocks.
Despite the treacherous water, the crew managed to reach the island safely.

Synset Examples

The sea rover navigated through treacherous waters with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous waters - sea or river conditions that are unsafe
