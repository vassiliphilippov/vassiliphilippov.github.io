end point

1. NOUN

/ ˈendˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈendˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
the place or moment at which something ends or is completed
place or moment where something ends

Examples

The end point of the trail is marked by a large wooden sign.
After months of negotiation, the two companies finally reached an end point.
The scientist recorded the end point of the chemical reaction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts
  • Synonym: termination - the action of bringing something to an end or the state of being ended

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd ˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɛnd ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
the final stage or result of a process, activity, or period of development
final stage or result in a process

Examples

The experiment continued until it reached its end point after six hours.
Every journey has an end point, but the experiences along the way matter most.
The team discussed what the end point of the project should look like.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - an aim or desired result
  • Antonym: starting point - the place or situation where something begins
