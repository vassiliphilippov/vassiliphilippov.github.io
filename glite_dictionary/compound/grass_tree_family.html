grass tree family

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs tri ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs tri ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, Xanthorrhoeaceae, that includes genera such as Xanthorrhoea, commonly known as grass trees, and related subfamilies like Asphodeloideae and Hemerocallidoideae
a family of flowering plants (Xanthorrhoeaceae)

Examples

The grass tree family is notable for species with long, slender leaves and tall flowering spikes.
Botanists debate whether the grass tree family should be classified separately from Liliaceae.
Several Australian native plants belong to the grass tree family, especially those found in dry, sandy areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xanthorrhoeaceae - the botanical family commonly known as the grass tree family
