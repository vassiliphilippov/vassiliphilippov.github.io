Scythian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪθ.i.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɪθ.i.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iranian language spoken by the ancient Scythians in Eurasia, now extinct
extinct Iranian language of the Scythians

Examples

The Scythian language is no longer spoken but has been studied through ancient texts.
Researchers have found evidence of the Scythian language in inscriptions across the Eurasian steppes.
Her thesis focused on the grammar and vocabulary of the Scythian language.

Etymology

Scythian from Greek 'Skythēs' (Σκύθης), referring to the Scythians, with 'language' denoting speech or tongue.
