to hugger-mugger

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌɡər ˌmʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈhʌɡə ˌmʌɡə / (BrE)
To act in a stealthy or secretive way.
act stealthily or secretly (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The thief tried to hugger-mugger about the alley, avoiding the lamps' glow.
They hugger-muggered their plans, telling no one about the intended escape.
The conspirators were hugger-muggering in the shadows, wary of being overheard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak - to move, give, or take in a quiet, stealthy way
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make known what was secret or hidden

Etymology

Probably from reduplication with initial consonant change, origin uncertain; first recorded in the 16th century.
