fetal movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtəl ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəl ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
the motion of a fetus within the uterus, typically first noticed by the 16th week of pregnancy
the motion of a fetus within the uterus
medical

Examples

Many pregnant women eagerly await the first fetal movement as a sign of their baby's growth.
The doctor asked if she had noticed any fetal movement during the past week.
Decreased fetal movement can sometimes indicate the need for further medical evaluation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quickening - the first noticeable movement of the fetus in pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin 'fetus' (offspring) and Middle English 'movement', from Old French 'movement'.
