rock maple

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / rɒk ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
a maple tree of eastern and central North America, especially the sugar maple, with hard, close-grained wood used for furniture and flooring; its sap is the main source of maple syrup and sugar
hardwood maple tree, source of maple syrup

Examples

The countertops were made of beautiful rock maple that gleamed in the sunlight.
Vermont is famous for its rock maple forests and delicious maple syrup.
Many traditional baseball bats are crafted from sturdy rock maple wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar maple - a species of maple (Acer saccharum) known for its sap used in making maple syrup
