backbreaking work

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌbreɪkɪŋ wɜrk / (AmE), / ˈbækˌbreɪkɪŋ wɜːk / (BrE)
extremely strenuous or physically demanding labor, often exhausting and requiring great effort
extremely strenuous labor

Examples

After hours of backbreaking work in the field, the workers finally took a much-needed break.
Moving all their belongings up three flights of stairs was truly backbreaking work.
They were exhausted from the backbreaking work required to repair the storm damage.

Synset Examples

She had to do backbreaking labor to support her family.
The backbreaking work left us exhausted by evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard labor - work that is physically very demanding
  • Antonym: light work - work that is not physically demanding
