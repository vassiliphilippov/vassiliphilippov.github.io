great gross

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ɡrɔs / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɡrɒs / (BrE)
A quantity equal to twelve gross, or 1,728 items.
twelve gross (1,728) items

Examples

The factory produced a great gross of screws this month for export.
He ordered a great gross of pencils to supply the entire school.
A great gross is the same as twelve gross, or 1,728 individual units.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gross - A unit equal to 144 items (twelve dozen)

Etymology

From 'gross' (144 items), from Old French 'gros'. 'Great gross' by analogy to 'gross' and 'dozen'.
