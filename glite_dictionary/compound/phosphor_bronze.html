phosphor bronze

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑs.fɚ ˌbroʊnz / (AmE), / ˈfɒs.fə ˌbrəʊnz / (BrE)
a corrosion-resistant alloy of copper and tin containing a small amount of phosphorus, used especially for bearings, gears, and springs
a corrosion-resistant copper-tin-phosphorus alloy

Examples

The bearings were made of phosphor bronze to prevent corrosion.
Many gears in marine engines use phosphor bronze for their durability.
He replaced the old bushings with new phosphor bronze ones to improve performance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bronze - an alloy of copper and tin

Etymology

From 'phosphor' (short for phosphorus) and 'bronze' (from Italian bronzo).
