biomedical science

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛd.ɪ.kəl ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈmɛd.ɪ.kəl ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
The application of principles and methods from the natural sciences to medicine, particularly in research and healthcare.
application of natural sciences to medicine

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in biomedical science after her undergraduate studies.
Recent advances in biomedical science have improved treatments for many diseases.
The university offers a master's program in biomedical science focusing on molecular biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life science - scientific study of living organisms

Etymology

From 'bio-' (life) + 'medical' (relating to medicine) + 'science' (systematic knowledge).
