steamer clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmər klæm / (AmE), / ˈstiːmə klæm / (BrE)
A type of soft-shell clam, especially one that is commonly steamed and eaten in its shell.
a soft-shell clam served steamed

Examples

The restaurant specializes in fresh steamer clams with melted butter.
We ordered a bucket of steamer clams as an appetizer at the beachside shack.
After gathering steamer clams, we cooked them over an open fire for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft-shell clam - an edible clam with a thin shell, often called a steamer clam

2. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmər klæm / (AmE), / ˈstiːmə klam / (BrE)
an edible clam with a thin, oval-shaped shell, found in coastal regions of the United States and Europe, often cooked by steaming
an edible thin-shelled clam

Examples

The seafood restaurant is famous for its fresh steamer clams served with melted butter.
We collected a bucket of steamer clams along the muddy shoreline at low tide.
Steaming is the traditional way to prepare steamer clams in New England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft-shell clam - an edible clam with a thin shell, also called 'steamer' in the US
