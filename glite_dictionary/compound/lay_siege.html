to lay siege

1. VERB

/ leɪ siːdʒ / (AmE), / leɪ siːdʒ / (BrE)
to surround a place and try to capture it, or to attack persistently over a period of time
attack or surround persistently

Examples

The invading army laid siege to the fortress, cutting off all supplies for months.
Reporters laid siege to the celebrity's hotel after news broke of the scandal.
During the war, several cities were laid siege as opposing forces struggled for control.

Synset Examples

The army laid siege to the city for weeks.
The fans laid siege to the concert venue early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to besiege - to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force its surrender

Etymology

From Old French *sege* (seat, blockade), from Latin *sedere* (to sit); 'lay' as in 'to set upon'.
