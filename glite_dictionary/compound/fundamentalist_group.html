fundamentalist group

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪst ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪst ɡruːp / (BrE)
an organization or movement that adheres strictly to the basic principles of a particular religion, often resisting change or modern influences
group with strict religious principles

Examples

The fundamentalist group organized a protest against the new policy changes.
Several members of the fundamentalist group spoke at the community meeting.
The fundamentalist group rejected modern interpretations of their faith.

Synset Examples

The fundamentalist group opposed any form of change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sect - a subgroup within a religion with distinct beliefs
  • Antonym: mainstream group - a group with widely accepted or moderate religious views
