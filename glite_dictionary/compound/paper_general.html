paper general

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ ˈdʒɛnɚəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
a person who gives orders or holds a position of authority but lacks real power or influence
person with nominal authority but no real power

Examples

Many saw the new manager as just a paper general with no real influence over decisions.
Despite his impressive title, he was little more than a paper general in the company.
She refused to be reduced to a paper general, demanding the authority her position deserved.

Synset Examples

As the paper general, he distributed tasks to the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figurehead - person who holds a position of authority but has no real power
  • Synonym: paper tiger - something or someone that appears threatening but is ineffectual

Etymology

Modeled after the expression 'paper tiger', using 'paper' to signify something insubstantial or only appearing strong.
