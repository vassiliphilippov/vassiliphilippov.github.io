sea lavender

1. NOUN

/ si ˈlævəndɚ / (AmE), / si ˈlævəndə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Limonium, typically found in temperate salt marshes, bearing spikes of white or mauve flowers
a salt-marsh plant of the genus Limonium

Examples

Clusters of sea lavender brightened the edges of the tidal flats with delicate purple hues.
Botanists study the tolerance of sea lavender to salty environments in marsh restoration projects.
After the storm, much of the sea lavender along the coast was washed away.
