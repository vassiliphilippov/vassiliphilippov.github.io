Lesser Antilles

1. PROPN

/ ˌlɛsər ænˈtɪliz / (AmE), / ˌlɛsə ænˈtɪliːz / (BrE)
a chain of islands in the southeastern West Indies, forming the eastern boundary of the Caribbean Sea
chain of Caribbean islands

Examples

Many tourists visit the Lesser Antilles for their beautiful beaches and vibrant culture.
The Lesser Antilles form a natural border between the Atlantic Ocean and the Caribbean Sea.
Hurricanes frequently pass through the Lesser Antilles during the storm season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Greater Antilles - the larger group of islands in the Caribbean, including Cuba, Jamaica, Hispaniola, and Puerto Rico

Etymology

From 'lesser' (smaller in size) + 'Antilles,' originating from medieval Latin 'Antillia,' a semi-legendary island in the Atlantic.
