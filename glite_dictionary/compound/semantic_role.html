semantic role

1. NOUN

/ səˈmæntɪk roʊl / (AmE), / sɪˈmantɪk rəʊl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The underlying relationship a constituent (such as a noun phrase) has with the main verb in a clause, such as agent, patient, or experiencer.
underlying linguistic relationship with a verb

Examples

Linguists analyze the semantic role of each noun phrase in a sentence to better understand sentence structure.
In the sentence 'The dog bit the man,' 'the dog' has the agent semantic role, while 'the man' has the patient role.
Understanding semantic roles helps language learners grasp how meaning is constructed in complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thematic role - another term for a semantic role in linguistics

Etymology

From 'semantic', relating to meaning (from Greek sēmantikos), and 'role', from French rôle (part played by an actor).
