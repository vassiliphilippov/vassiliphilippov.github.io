white telephone

1. NOUN

/ waɪt tɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / waɪt ˈtɛlɪfəʊn / (BrE)
a telephone, typically white in color, that is used to symbolize or designate a direct, dedicated communication line, especially in official or privileged contexts
dedicated, often symbolic, telephone line

Examples

A white telephone on the president's desk connects him directly to his top advisers.
Only authorized staff were allowed to use the white telephone in the control room.
In classic films, the white telephone became a symbol of wealth and exclusivity.

Synset Examples

He picked up the white telephone to call her.
