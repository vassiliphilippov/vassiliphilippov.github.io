able-bodied sailor

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪbəlˈbɑdid ˈseɪlər / (AmE), / ˌeɪblˈbɒdid ˈseɪlə / (BrE)
a sailor in the merchant navy who is fully trained and qualified for all regular duties onboard
fully trained sailor (merchant navy)

Examples

After several years of experience, he was promoted to able-bodied sailor on the merchant vessel.
The ship needed three more able-bodied sailors before it could leave port.
An able-bodied sailor must be skilled in navigation, maintenance, and emergency procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaman - an enlisted sailor in the navy; sometimes used more generally for sailors
  • Degree Lower: ordinary seaman - a junior rank below able-bodied sailor in the merchant navy
