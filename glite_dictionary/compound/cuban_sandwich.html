Cuban sandwich

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bən ˈsæn.wɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bən ˈsæn.wɪdʒ / (BrE)
a sandwich consisting of a crusty roll filled with ham, roast pork, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard, typically pressed and served hot
a pressed sandwich with pork, ham, cheese, and pickles

Examples

I ordered a Cuban sandwich for lunch because I love the combination of ham and pickles.
Their café is famous for their authentic Cuban sandwich with perfectly pressed bread.
She tried making a homemade Cuban sandwich after watching a cooking show on television.

Etymology

Named for its origins in the Cuban immigrant communities of Florida in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
