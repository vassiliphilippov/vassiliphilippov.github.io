litmus test

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtməs tɛst / (AmE), / ˈlɪtməs tɛst / (BrE)
A coloring material obtained from lichens that turns red in acid solutions and blue in alkaline solutions, used as a rough indicator of acidity or alkalinity.
chemical indicator of acidity or alkalinity

Examples

In the laboratory, a litmus test is often used to determine if a solution is acidic or basic.
She dipped the litmus test strip into the liquid and watched it turn bright red.
The students learned how to use a litmus test as part of their chemistry experiment.

Etymology

From 'litmus', from Middle English, from Old Norse 'litmosi' (dye moss), referring to its lichen origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtməs tɛst / (AmE), / ˈlɪtməs tɛst / (BrE)
a single decisive factor used to judge or measure the presence, quality, or acceptability of something, especially in forming an opinion or making a decision
a decisive indicator or criterion

Examples

The politician's stance on healthcare has become a litmus test for many voters.
Honesty is the main litmus test I use when hiring new employees.
Her approval of the proposal was seen as a litmus test for the project's future success.

Synset Examples

her litmus test for good breeding is whether you split infinitives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yardstick - a standard by which something is judged or measured
  • Synonym: acid test - a conclusive test or decisive proof
  • Synonym: deal breaker - an issue or factor that is decisive in making a decision

Etymology

Originally from the use of litmus paper in chemistry to test acidity or alkalinity.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtməs tɛst / (AmE), / ˈlɪtməs tɛst / (BrE)
a question, situation, or event used as a decisive indicator of whether something meets a particular standard or quality
decisive indicator or criterion

Examples

The upcoming election will be a real litmus test for the new policy.
How the public responds to the campaign is a key litmus test for its success.
His reaction to criticism was seen as a litmus test of his leadership style.

Synset Examples

The project's success would be a litmus test for future funding.
The performance review served as a litmus test for promotions.
Her reaction was a litmus test of our friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid test - a definitive or most important test to judge quality or truth

Etymology

Originally from the use of litmus paper to test acidity; adopted figuratively in the 20th century.
