mugo pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːgoʊ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈmjuːɡəʊ paɪn / (BrE)
a low, shrubby pine native to central Europe, with short, bright green needles in pairs; widely used as an ornamental plant
low shrubby pine of central Europe

Examples

The mugo pine is often planted in rock gardens due to its compact growth habit.
We chose a mugo pine for the front yard because it is hardy and needs little maintenance.
Bonsai enthusiasts frequently use the mugo pine for training miniature trees.

Etymology

From Italian 'mugo', referring to the European mountain pine (Pinus mugo).
