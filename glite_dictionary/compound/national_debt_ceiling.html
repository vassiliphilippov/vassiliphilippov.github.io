national debt ceiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənl dɛt ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl dɛt ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an official limit set by a government, especially the US Congress, on the amount of money the government may borrow; this limit is periodically raised by legislators
official government borrowing limit

Examples

The U.S. Congress held an emergency session to discuss raising the national debt ceiling.
Debates about the national debt ceiling often dominate political discourse during times of economic crisis.
If the national debt ceiling is not increased, the government may be unable to meet its financial obligations.
