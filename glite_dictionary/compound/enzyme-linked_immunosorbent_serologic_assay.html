enzyme-linked immunosorbent serologic assay

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.zaɪm ˈlɪŋkt ɪˌmjun.oʊˈsɔr.bənt səˈrɑl.ɪk əˈseɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛn.zaɪm ˈlɪŋkt ɪˌmjuː.nəʊˈsɔː.bənt səˈrɒ.lɪk əˈseɪ / (BrE)
a laboratory test that uses an enzymatic reaction to detect the presence of specific substances such as antibodies, antigens, viruses, or bacteria in a sample
laboratory test for detecting substances by enzymatic reaction
medical

Examples

The enzyme-linked immunosorbent serologic assay is routinely used to screen blood samples for HIV antibodies.
Doctors ordered an enzyme-linked immunosorbent serologic assay to confirm the presence of the bacterial infection.
Researchers relied on the enzyme-linked immunosorbent serologic assay for accurate detection of the virus in patients' sera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ELISA - abbr. of enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay, a laboratory test for detecting substances using an enzyme reaction

Etymology

A compound term formed from 'enzyme-linked' and 'immunosorbent assay'; commonly abbreviated as ELISA.
