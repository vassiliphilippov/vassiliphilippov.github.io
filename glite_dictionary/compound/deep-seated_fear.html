deep-seated fear

1. NOUN

/ dip ˈsitɪd fɪr / (AmE), / diːp ˈsiːtɪd fɪə / (BrE)
a fear that has existed for a long time and is firmly established
long-standing and strong fear

Examples

He struggled with a deep-seated fear of failure throughout his career.
Her deep-seated fear of dogs made visiting friends with pets difficult.
Many people have a deep-seated fear of public speaking.

Synset Examples

She had a deep-seated fear of heights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of or aversion to something
  • Degree Lower: anxiety - a feeling of worry or nervousness, often about something that could happen

Etymology

From 'deep-seated,' meaning firmly established, originating in the 18th century.
