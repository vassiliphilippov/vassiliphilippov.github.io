interparietal suture

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərpəˈraɪətl ˈsutʃər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəpəˈraɪətl ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
the immovable joint that connects the two parietal bones of the skull
skull joint linking parietal bones
medical

Examples

During the autopsy, the coroner noted that the interparietal suture was intact.
Cranial development studies often focus on the closure patterns of the interparietal suture.
In rare cases, premature fusion of the interparietal suture can lead to skull deformities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagittal suture - the suture connecting the two parietal bones along the midline of the skull

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) and 'parietalis' (of the wall), referring to the parietal bones.
