Nahuatl language

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑː.wɑːtəl læŋˌɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.wɑːtəl ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Nahua people of central Mexico and neighboring regions.
Uto-Aztecan language of the Nahua people

Examples

Many ancient manuscripts were written in Nahuatl language during the Aztec Empire.
The Nahuatl language is still spoken by over a million people in Mexico today.
Linguists study the Nahuatl language to learn more about indigenous cultures and histories.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *nāhuatl* (speech, language).
