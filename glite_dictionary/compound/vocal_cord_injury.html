vocal cord injury

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊ.kəl kɔrd ˈɪn.dʒə.ri / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kəl kɔːd ˈɪn.dʒə.ri / (BrE)
damage to the vocal cords that affects the ability to speak or produce sound
damage to the vocal cords
medical

Examples

The patient suffered a vocal cord injury after the car accident and needed speech therapy.
Prolonged shouting during the performance caused a vocal cord injury that required medical attention.
A vocal cord injury may result in hoarseness or loss of voice until it heals.

Synset Examples

A true vocal cord injury can affect speaking.
