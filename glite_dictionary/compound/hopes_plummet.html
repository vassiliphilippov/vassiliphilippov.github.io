to hopes plummet

1. VERB

/ hoʊps ˈplʌmət / (AmE), / həʊps ˈplʌmɪt / (BrE)
to experience a sudden and significant loss of optimism or expectation for a positive outcome.
to lose optimism suddenly

Examples

When she heard the disappointing news, her hopes plummeted instantly.
Investors' hopes plummeted after the company announced its losses.
As the score worsened, the team's hopes plummeted in the final quarter.

Synset Examples

He watched his hopes plummet when he failed the test.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hopes rise - to feel a sudden increase in optimism
