agony column

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡəni ˌkɑləm / (AmE), / ˈæɡəni ˌkɒləm / (BrE)
a section of a newspaper or magazine where readers' personal problems are published along with advice from an expert or columnist
newspaper column for personal problems and advice
british

Examples

She wrote to the agony column hoping for guidance on her relationship troubles.
For years, readers have shared their secrets and worries in the agony column.
The agony column offered thoughtful advice to a concerned mother in yesterday's paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advice column - a newspaper or magazine column that answers readers' requests for advice

Etymology

From the noun 'agony' (intense mental suffering) + 'column' (a regular newspaper feature); chiefly used in British English.
