shunt wound DC motor

1. NOUN

/ ʃʌnt waʊnd di si ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ʃʌnt waʊnd diː siː ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
a type of direct current (DC) motor in which the field windings and armature are connected in parallel, providing good speed regulation under varying loads
DC motor with parallel field and armature windings

Examples

The shunt wound DC motor is favored in applications where constant speed is required despite changes in load.
Industrial equipment often uses a shunt wound DC motor for its reliable speed regulation.
Unlike a series DC motor, a shunt wound DC motor maintains a more consistent speed under variable loads.

Synset Examples

A shunt DC motor has good speed regulation even as the load varies, but does not have the starting torque of a series DC motor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: series wound DC motor - a DC motor whose field and armature windings are connected in series
  • Degree Higher: compound wound DC motor - a DC motor with both series and shunt field windings

Etymology

From 'shunt', meaning parallel electrical connection, combined with 'wound' (past participle of 'wind'), and DC motor (direct current motor).
