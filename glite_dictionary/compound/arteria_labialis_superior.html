arteria labialis superior

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə læˈbeɪliɪs suːˈpɪəriɚ / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪərɪə læˈbeɪlɪɪs suːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
A branch of the facial artery that supplies blood to the upper lip.
artery supplying the upper lip
medical

Examples

During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the arteria labialis superior.
The arteria labialis superior branches off from the facial artery to supply the upper lip.
Injury to the arteria labialis superior can lead to significant bleeding in the region.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery), 'labialis' (of the lip), 'superior' (upper).
