inside job

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪd dʒɑb / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd dʒɒb / (BrE)
a crime or dishonest act committed with the help of someone within the organization or group affected
crime helped by insider

Examples

The police suspected that the robbery was an inside job because the alarms were disabled.
It quickly became clear that the data leak was an inside job involving a trusted employee.
Rumors about the museum theft being an inside job spread throughout the local community.

Synset Examples

the police decided that the crime was an inside job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayal - act of betraying trust or confidence
  • Synonym: heist - robbery, especially one involving a group or elaborate plan

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪd ˌdʒɑb / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd ˌdʒɒb / (BrE)
a crime committed with the help of someone working within the organization where it occurs
crime committed by an insider

Examples

The security breach turned out to be an inside job involving a disgruntled employee.
Police suspected the robbery was an inside job because there were no signs of forced entry.
After months of investigation, they confirmed it was indeed an inside job.

Synset Examples

The theft was clearly an inside job.
They suspected that it was an inside job all along.
The investigation revealed it was an inside job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heist - an act of stealing; a robbery
  • Synonym: burglary - illegal entry to commit a crime, especially theft
