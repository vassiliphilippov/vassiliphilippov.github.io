ground roller

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌroʊlər / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌrəʊlə / (BrE)
A bird native to Madagascar from the family Brachypteraciidae, known for its terrestrial and crepuscular habits, feeding mainly on insects and worms.
terrestrial Madagascan bird (Brachypteraciidae)

Examples

Several species of ground roller are endemic to the forests of Madagascar.
The ground roller hunts insects and worms on the forest floor at dusk.
Researchers observed a nesting pair of ground rollers near the riverbank.
