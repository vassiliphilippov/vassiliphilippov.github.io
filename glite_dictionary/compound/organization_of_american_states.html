Organization of American States

1. PROPN

/ ˌɔːɹɡənəˈzeɪʃən əv əˈmɛɹɪkən steɪts / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən əv əˈmɛrɪkən steɪts / (BrE)
an international organization of most countries in the Americas, established in 1948 to promote cooperation in military, economic, social, and cultural matters
international organization of American countries

Examples

The Organization of American States met to discuss human rights issues in Latin America.
Membership in the Organization of American States is seen as important for regional diplomacy.
The Organization of American States was instrumental in mediating the dispute between member countries.
