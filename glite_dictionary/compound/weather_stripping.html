weather stripping

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðɚ ˌstrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə ˌstrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A narrow strip of material fixed around doors or windows to seal joints and prevent the entry of cold air, water, or dust.
sealant strip for doors/windows

Examples

He installed weather stripping around the door to keep the cold air out.
Because the window had no weather stripping, a draft came into the room every winter.
Proper weather stripping can help reduce energy bills by preventing air leaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - material used to block or close an opening
