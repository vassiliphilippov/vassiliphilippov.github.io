ecclesiastical benefice

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl ˈbɛnəfɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːzɪˈæstɪkəl ˈbɛnɪfɪs / (BrE)
An endowed church office that provides income to its holder.
endowed church office with income

Examples

He was appointed to an ecclesiastical benefice in the rural parish.
The income from the ecclesiastical benefice supported the church’s activities.
The council discussed how the ecclesiastical benefice should be managed after the bishop's retirement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefice - an endowed church office giving income to its holder

Etymology

From Latin 'beneficium' (favor, benefit) and 'ecclesiasticus' (of the church).
