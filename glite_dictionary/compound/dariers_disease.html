Darier's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæri.eɪz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈdæri.eɪz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare hereditary skin disorder characterized by dark, crusted patches that may contain pus.
rare hereditary skin disorder
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed her with Darier's disease after examining the persistent, crusted skin patches.
Recent advances in genetics have helped scientists understand the hereditary basis of Darier's disease.
Living with Darier's disease often requires ongoing skin care and regular visits to a specialist.

Etymology

Named after French dermatologist Ferdinand-Jean Darier, who first described the disease in 1889.
