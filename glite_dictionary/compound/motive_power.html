motive power

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.tɪv ˌpaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.tɪv ˌpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the energy or force that provides motion to a machine, vehicle, or system
energy or force causing motion

Examples

Steam engines once provided motive power for trains across the country.
The electric motor supplies the motive power to the conveyor belt in the factory.
During the discussion, she focused on the need for alternative sources of motive power in modern transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driving force - the force that puts something into motion
  • Synonym: motive - reason or force behind an action or movement
  • Antonym: inertia - resistance of any physical object to a change in its state of motion

Etymology

From Latin 'motivus' (moving) + English 'power'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtɪv ˈpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈməʊtɪv ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the energy or force that causes motion or drives machinery
energy or force driving machinery

Examples

The electric motor supplies the motive power for the conveyor belt.
Steam was once the main source of motive power in factories.
Wind turbines convert wind energy into motive power to generate electricity.

Synset Examples

Sunlight is the motive power behind photosynthesis.
The new engine provides the motive power for the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - to provide the force that moves or powers something

Etymology

From Latin 'motivus' (that moves) and 'power'.
