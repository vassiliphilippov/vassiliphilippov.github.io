queen consort

1. NOUN

/ kwin ˈkɑnsɔrt / (AmE), / kwiːn ˈkɒnsɔːt / (BrE)
the wife of a reigning king; a queen by virtue of being the king's spouse, rather than reigning in her own right
wife of a reigning king

Examples

Queen Victoria was not a queen consort, but a queen regnant who ruled in her own right.
During his reign, King George VI's wife, Elizabeth, held the title of queen consort.
The role and influence of a queen consort can vary greatly depending on the traditions of the monarchy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: queen regnant - a queen who reigns in her own right
  • Synonym: king - a male monarch or reigning ruler of a kingdom

Etymology

From 'queen' (Old English 'cwēn', woman, wife, queen) and 'consort' (from Latin 'consors', partner, spouse).
