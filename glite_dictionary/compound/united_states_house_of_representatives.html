United States House of Representatives

1. PROPN

/ juˈnaɪtɪd steɪts haʊs əv rɛprɪˌzɛntətɪvz / (AmE), / juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts haʊs əv rɛprɪˌzɛntətɪvz / (BrE)
the lower legislative chamber of the United States Congress, consisting of elected representatives from each state, responsible for creating and passing federal laws
lower legislative chamber of US Congress
american

Examples

A bill must pass both the Senate and the United States House of Representatives before it becomes law.
Elections for the United States House of Representatives are held every two years.
She was recently elected to the United States House of Representatives from her home state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: United States Senate - the upper legislative chamber of the United States Congress
