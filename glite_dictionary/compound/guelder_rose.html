guelder rose

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛl.dɚ roʊz / (AmE), / ˈɡɛl.də rəʊz / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to Europe and Asia, with clusters of white flowers and bright red berries, often grown as an ornamental plant.
a shrub with white flowers and red berries

Examples

The guelder rose in our garden is full of white blossoms this year.
In autumn, the guelder rose produces striking clusters of red berries that attract birds.
Gardeners often plant guelder rose for its ornamental value and vibrant fall colors.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Gelderse roos', referring to the Gelderland region of the Netherlands.
