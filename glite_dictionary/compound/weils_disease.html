Weil's disease

1. NOUN

/ waɪlz dɪˈziz / (AmE), / waɪlz dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a severe, sometimes fatal form of leptospirosis in humans, characterized by jaundice, fever, and kidney damage, caused by infection with Leptospira bacteria
severe form of leptospirosis in humans
medical

Examples

After hiking in a tropical area, he developed symptoms consistent with Weil's disease.
Doctors suspected Weil's disease when the patient presented with jaundice and kidney failure.
Public health officials warned about the risk of contracting Weil's disease after flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leptospirosis - a bacterial disease affecting humans and animals

Etymology

Named after Adolf Weil, the German physician who first described the disease in 1886.
