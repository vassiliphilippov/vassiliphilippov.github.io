fungus gnat

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋɡəs næt / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋɡəs nat / (BrE)
A small, dark gregarious fly whose larvae feed on fungi and are often destructive to mushrooms and young plants.
small fly destructive to fungi and seedlings

Examples

The gardener noticed fungus gnats swarming around the damp soil of her potted plants.
Mushroom growers often struggle to control fungus gnats in their greenhouses.
Infestations of fungus gnats can cause serious damage to seedlings and young roots.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋɡəs næt / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋɡəs nat / (BrE)
a small, mosquito-like insect whose larvae feed on fungi or decaying organic matter
small mosquito-like insect; larvae feed on fungi

Examples

Fungus gnat infestations are common in houseplants with overly damp soil.
The larvae of the fungus gnat can damage the roots of young seedlings.
Sticky traps are often used to capture adult fungus gnats indoors.
