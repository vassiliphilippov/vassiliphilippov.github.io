short leash

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt liʃ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt liːʃ / (BrE)
close or strict control or supervision over someone or something, often with limited freedom
close or strict control

Examples

Since his mistake, the manager has kept him on a short leash at work.
Parents sometimes put their teenagers on a short leash to keep them out of trouble.
After escaping once, the dog was kept on a short leash during walks.

Synset Examples

The dog was kept on a short leash during the walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tight rein - strict control or supervision
