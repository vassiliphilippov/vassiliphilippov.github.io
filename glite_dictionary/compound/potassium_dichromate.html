potassium dichromate

1. NOUN

/ pəˌtæsiəm daɪˈkrəʊmeɪt / (AmE), / pəˌtæsiəm dɪˈkrəʊmət / (BrE)
an orange-red crystalline salt used in making dyes, in photography, and as an oxidizing agent
orange-red chemical compound, salt of potassium and dichromate

Examples

Potassium dichromate is widely used as an oxidizing agent in laboratory experiments.
When handling potassium dichromate, always use gloves due to its toxic nature.
Old photographic processes relied on potassium dichromate for developing images.

Etymology

From potassium, the metal element, and dichromate, from di- (two) + chromate (from Greek 'chrōma', color).
