genus Carica

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈraɪkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈraɪkə / (BrE)
The type genus of the Caricaceae family, consisting of tropical American trees including the papaya.
genus of tropical American trees including papaya

Examples

The genus Carica includes the well-known papaya tree found in tropical regions.
Botanists classified the new species within the genus Carica based on its fruit structure.
Several tropical American trees belong to the genus Carica, recognized for their edible fruits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Caricaceae - the plant family that includes the genus Carica
  • Synonym: papaya - the common name for the most well-known species in the genus Carica

Etymology

From Latin 'Carica', a genus name, and Greek 'karike', a kind of fig.
