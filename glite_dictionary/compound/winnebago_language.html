Winnebago language

1. NOUN

/ wɪnəˈbeɪɡoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / wɪnəˈbeɪɡəʊ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language traditionally spoken by the Winnebago (Ho-Chunk) people of North America
Siouan language of the Winnebago people

Examples

Linguists have documented endangered features of the Winnebago language spoken in Wisconsin.
He is learning the Winnebago language to reconnect with his cultural heritage.
A bilingual dictionary was published for the Winnebago language and English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ho-Chunk language - another common name for the language of the Ho-Chunk (Winnebago) people

Etymology

Winnebago from Algonquian *Wiinibiigoo*; the language is part of the Siouan language family.
