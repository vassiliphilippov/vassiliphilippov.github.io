oil oligarch

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈɑlɪˌɡɑrk / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈɒlɪˌɡɑːk / (BrE)
a very wealthy and powerful person who controls or has an influential stake in the oil industry, typically within a specific country or region
wealthy, powerful person in the oil industry

Examples

The oil oligarch invested millions in foreign real estate ventures.
As an oil oligarch, his opinions could sway national energy policies.
A new generation of oil oligarchs is emerging as the industry evolves.

Synset Examples

An oil oligarch's fortune can shape entire industries.
The oil oligarch's wealth was evident in his luxurious lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oligarch - a very rich business leader with a great deal of political influence

Etymology

From 'oil', referring to petroleum, and 'oligarch', from Greek 'oligarkhēs' meaning 'member of an oligarchy'.
