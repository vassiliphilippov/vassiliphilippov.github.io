patent log

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætənt lɒg / (AmE), / ˈpeɪt(ə)nt lɒg / (BrE)
a cigar-shaped instrument with rotary fins used to measure the speed of a ship through water
instrument to measure a ship's speed

Examples

The captain checked the patent log to determine the ship's velocity over the past hour.
A well-maintained patent log provides reliable speed measurements for navigation.
Sailors preferred the patent log over older methods because it was more accurate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: log - instrument for measuring a ship's speed and distance travelled

Etymology

From 'patent' (protected by a patent) and 'log' (device for measuring a ship's speed).
