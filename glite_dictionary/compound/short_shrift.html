short shrift

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ʃrɪft / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ʃrɪft / (BrE)
quick and unsympathetic treatment; a brief period of consideration before dismissal or rejection.
quick and unsympathetic dismissal

Examples

The manager gave my proposal short shrift and moved on to the next topic.
Some politicians give short shrift to environmental concerns in their speeches.
When I complained about the noise, the landlord gave me short shrift.

Synset Examples

they made short shrift of my request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush-off - an abrupt or dismissive rejection
  • Synonym: dismissal - the act of sending someone away or rejecting something

Etymology

From the phrase 'short shrift', originally meaning a brief time given to a condemned prisoner for confession before execution; 'shrift' from Old English 'scrift' (confession).

2. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ʃrɪft / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ʃrɪft / (BrE)
little attention or consideration given to something or someone
little attention or consideration

Examples

Because the issue was considered unimportant, it was given short shrift in the report.
Many customer complaints receive short shrift from the company.
He gave his colleague's suggestions short shrift during the planning session.

Synset Examples

The project received short shrift due to budget cuts.
Don't give me short shrift; I deserve better.
He gave her short shrift during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - lack of proper attention or care
  • Antonym: attention - careful consideration or notice

Etymology

Originally referred to the brief time allowed for a condemned person to confess before execution (from Middle English 'shrift', confession or absolution).
