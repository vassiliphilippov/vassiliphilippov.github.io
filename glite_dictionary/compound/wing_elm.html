wing elm

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋ ɛlm / (AmE), / wɪŋ ɛlm / (BrE)
A North American species of elm tree (Ulmus alata) characterized by twigs and young branches with conspicuous corky projections.
North American elm tree with corky wing-like twigs

Examples

The wing elm is easily recognized by its distinctive corky branches.
Several wing elms were planted along the park trail for shade and beauty.
Birds often perch on the rough, corky twigs of the wing elm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - any tree of the genus Ulmus, characterized by serrated leaves and often tall stature

Etymology

Named for the wing-like corky ridges on its branches; 'elm' from Old English 'elm'.
