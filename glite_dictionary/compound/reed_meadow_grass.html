reed meadow grass

1. NOUN

/ rid ˈmɛdoʊ ɡræs / (AmE), / riːd ˈmɛdəʊ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A pasture grass that grows in moist places throughout North America, commonly found in wetlands.
pasture grass of moist North American areas

Examples

The thick stand of reed meadow grass made the wetlands a haven for waterfowl.
Farmers often rely on reed meadow grass to provide summer grazing for their cattle in low-lying fields.
Careful management of reed meadow grass helps maintain the biodiversity of North American marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed canary grass - a similar grass species found in wet meadows
