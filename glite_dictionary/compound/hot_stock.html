hot stock

1. NOUN

/ hɑt stɑk / (AmE), / hɒt stɒk / (BrE)
A newly issued stock that is in such high demand that investors are eager to buy it quickly.
popular new stock issue

Examples

The company's IPO quickly became a hot stock as investors rushed to buy shares.
Analysts warned that purchasing a hot stock could be risky due to potential volatility.
She made a large profit by investing early in a hot stock that soared on its first trading day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blue chip - A well-established and financially sound company stock with a history of reliable performance.
  • Antonym: penny stock - A stock with a very low market price, often highly speculative and risky.
