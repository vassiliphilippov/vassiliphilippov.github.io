overhand pitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌhænd pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌhænd pɪtʃ / (BrE)
a baseball pitch in which the pitcher throws the ball with the hand moving above the shoulder
baseball pitch thrown above the shoulder

Examples

The pitcher delivered an overhand pitch with incredible speed.
Many fastballs are thrown using an overhand pitch to generate maximum velocity.
She practices her overhand pitch to improve her accuracy and power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sidearm pitch - a baseball pitch thrown with the arm moving roughly parallel to the ground
  • Antonym: underhand pitch - a baseball pitch in which the hand moves below the waist
