occipital lobe

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɪpɪt̬l̩ loʊb / (AmE), / ɒkˈsɪpɪtəl ləʊb / (BrE)
the rearmost lobe of each cerebral hemisphere of the brain, responsible mainly for visual processing
part of brain at back of head
medical

Examples

Damage to the occipital lobe can result in visual disturbances or even blindness.
The brain's occipital lobe is primarily responsible for processing visual information.
During brain surgery, doctors must carefully avoid injuring the occipital lobe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: parietal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex near the upper back of the brain
  • Degree Lower: temporal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex on the sides of the brain
  • Degree Higher: frontal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex at the front of the brain

Etymology

From Latin occiput (back of the head) and lobe (Greek lobos - lobe of the ear or brain).
