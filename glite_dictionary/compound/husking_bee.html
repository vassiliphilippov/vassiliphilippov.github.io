husking bee

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌskɪŋ bi / (AmE), / ˈhʌskɪŋ biː / (BrE)
a social gathering, especially in rural communities, where people come together to husk corn, often accompanied by festivities
corn husking social event

Examples

Every autumn, the community held a husking bee to prepare corn for the winter.
She met her future husband at a lively husking bee organized by their neighbors.
The old photographs showed families enjoying a husking bee in the barn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn raising - a social event where a community comes together to build a barn

Etymology

From 'husk' (to remove the outer covering) and 'bee' (dialectal: a gathering for communal work), both of English origin.
