Sue, Grabbit and Runne

1. NOUN

/ suː ˈɡræbɪt ənd ˈrʌn / (AmE), / suː ˈɡræbɪt ənd ˈrʌn / (BrE)
a humorous phrase referring to someone who is opportunistic or acts hastily for personal gain, often used to describe dubious or unscrupulous businesses, especially fictional law firms
humorous term for opportunists or dubious firms

Examples

When the shady company closed overnight, people called it a classic Sue, Grabbit and Runne operation.
Don't trust every online advertisement—some are run by Sue, Grabbit and Runne types looking for quick profit.
He laughed and said his business was no Sue, Grabbit and Runne, assuring us of its integrity.

Synset Examples

Her attitude was all Sue, Grabbit and Runne during the sale.
He joked about being a Sue, Grabbit and Runne with his quick decisions.

Etymology

A mock law firm name formed from verbs 'sue', 'grabbit' (grab it), and 'run', referencing opportunistic or unscrupulous behavior.
