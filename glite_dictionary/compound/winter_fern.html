winter fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tər fɝn / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə fɜːn / (BrE)
a large, branching biennial herb with fernlike leaves and white flowers, native to Eurasia and Africa but also found in North America; all parts are extremely poisonous
poisonous biennial herb with fernlike leaves

Examples

Many hikers avoid touching the winter fern because of its toxic properties.
The winter fern thrives in damp woodlands across Eurasia and parts of North America.
Despite its attractive appearance, every part of the winter fern is dangerous if ingested.
