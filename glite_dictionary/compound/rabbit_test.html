rabbit test

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt tɛst / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt tɛst / (BrE)
an early pregnancy test in which a woman's urine is injected into a female rabbit and the animal's ovaries are examined for changes indicating pregnancy
historical pregnancy test using a rabbit
medical

Examples

In the 1940s, doctors sometimes used the rabbit test to determine pregnancy.
The rabbit test has been replaced by more humane and accurate methods today.
She read with fascination about the history of the rabbit test in medical diagnostics.

Etymology

Named for the use of rabbits in the test, developed in the 1920s.
