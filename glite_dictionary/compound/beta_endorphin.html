beta endorphin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.tə ɛnˈdɔr.fɪn / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə ɛnˈdɔː.fɪn / (BrE)
an endorphin produced by the pituitary gland that acts as a natural painkiller in the body
endogenous pain-relieving hormone
medical

Examples

The pituitary gland releases beta endorphin during prolonged exercise, helping to reduce the sensation of pain.
Some studies suggest that laughter increases the production of beta endorphin in the brain.
Patients with higher levels of beta endorphin often report experiencing less discomfort after surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorphin - endogenous peptide hormone that relieves pain

Etymology

From 'beta', the second letter of the Greek alphabet, and 'endorphin', from 'endogenous' and 'morphine'.
