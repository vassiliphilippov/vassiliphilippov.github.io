Belgian Congo

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛldʒən ˈkɑŋgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɛldʒən ˈkɒŋgəʊ / (BrE)
the name of a former Belgian colony in central Africa, which became the Democratic Republic of the Congo after gaining independence in 1960
former Belgian colony in central Africa

Examples

The Belgian Congo gained its independence in 1960, becoming a new nation.
Many resources were exploited during the colonial period in the Belgian Congo.
The history of the Belgian Congo remains a subject of much research and debate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Democratic Republic of the Congo - country in central Africa, formerly known as Belgian Congo
  • Degree Lower: Congo Free State - the name of the territory before it became Belgian Congo

Etymology

Named after Belgium, the colonial power, and the Congo region.
