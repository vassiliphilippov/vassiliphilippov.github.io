light ballast

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ˈbæl.æst / (AmE), / laɪt ˈbæl.ɑːst / (BrE)
An electrical device used to start and regulate the current in fluorescent and discharge lamps.
device for regulating fluorescent/discharge lamps

Examples

The electrician replaced the faulty light ballast to stop the fluorescent light from flickering.
Some older offices still use magnetic light ballasts instead of newer electronic ones.
A malfunctioning light ballast can cause humming noises in the ceiling fixtures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballast - a device that stabilizes current in electrical circuits, especially in lamps

Etymology

From 'light' (referring to lamps) and 'ballast' (originally meaning stabilizing material), the latter from Middle Dutch 'ballast'.
