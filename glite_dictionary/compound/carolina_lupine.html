Carolina lupine

1. NOUN

/ kɛrəˈlaɪnə ˈluːpaɪn / (AmE), / kærəˈlaɪnə ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
A perennial plant (Thermopsis villosa) native to the eastern United States, with tall spires of yellow flowers similar to true lupines.
eastern US plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The Carolina lupine brightened the garden with its vibrant yellow blooms.
Gardeners often plant Carolina lupine for its showy, lupine-like flower spikes.
Unlike true lupines, Carolina lupine prefers slightly acidic soils and tolerates heat well.
