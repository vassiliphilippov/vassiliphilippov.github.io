Uruguay potato vine

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˌɡwaɪ pəˈteɪtoʊ vaɪn / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˌɡwaɪ pəˈteɪtəʊ vaɪn / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to South America, known for its vine growth habit and edible tubers.
a South American vine plant with edible tubers

Examples

The Uruguay potato vine thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.
Gardeners prize the Uruguay potato vine for its attractive blossoms and edible tubers.
Several varieties of Uruguay potato vine are grown for both ornamental and culinary uses.
