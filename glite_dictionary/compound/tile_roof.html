tile roof

1. NOUN

/ taɪl ruːf / (AmE), / taɪl ruːf / (BrE)
A roof constructed using fired clay tiles set in overlapping rows.
roof made of clay tiles

Examples

The old church still has its original tile roof, which has lasted for centuries.
After the storm, some of the tile roof needed to be repaired.
Many Mediterranean houses are known for their distinctive red tile roof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shingle roof - a roof made of overlapping shingles (tiles or slates); similar style, different materials
