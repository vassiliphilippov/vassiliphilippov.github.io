direct correlation

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˌkɔːrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between two variables in which they increase or decrease together, indicated by a positive correlation coefficient between 0 and +1
positive relationship between variables

Examples

A direct correlation exists between hours studied and exam scores in this class.
The scientists found a direct correlation between exercise and improved mental health.
There is often a direct correlation between job satisfaction and productivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inverse correlation - a relationship where one variable increases as the other decreases
