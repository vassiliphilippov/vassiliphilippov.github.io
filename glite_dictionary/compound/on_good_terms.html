on good terms

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ɡʊd tɝmz / (AmE), / ɒn ɡʊd tɜːmz / (BrE)
having a friendly or harmonious relationship with someone
having a friendly relationship

Examples

After the argument, it took some time for them to be on good terms again.
She always tries to stay on good terms with her colleagues at work.
Despite their differences, the two companies remained on good terms.

Synset Examples

They are on good terms after resolving their issues.
It's important to be on good terms with your boss.
I hope to stay on good terms with my neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on bad terms - having a hostile or unfriendly relationship
