Port Orford cedar

1. NOUN

/ pɔrt ˈɔrfərd ˈsidɚ / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈɔːfəd ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
The wood obtained from the Port Orford cedar tree, valued for its durability, fragrance, and light weight, often used in woodworking and specialty applications.
wood from the Port Orford cedar tree

Examples

The musical instrument maker chose Port Orford cedar for its unique tonal qualities.
Due to its rot resistance, Port Orford cedar is popular for use in boat building.
They lined the closets with Port Orford cedar to keep insects away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chamaecyparis lawsoniana - the Port Orford cedar tree

Etymology

Named after Port Orford, a city in Oregon, USA, where the tree is native.

2. NOUN

/ pɔrt ˈɔrfərd ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈɔːfəd ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a large coniferous tree native to western North America, valued for its timber and ornamental use
large North American timber tree

Examples

The Port Orford cedar is prized for its strong, decay-resistant wood used in boatbuilding.
Many gardens in the region feature the distinctive blue-green foliage of Port Orford cedar.
Diseases have threatened wild populations of Port Orford cedar along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - any of several coniferous trees with fragrant wood

Etymology

Named after Port Orford, Oregon, where the tree was first scientifically described; 'cedar' from Latin 'cedrus'.
