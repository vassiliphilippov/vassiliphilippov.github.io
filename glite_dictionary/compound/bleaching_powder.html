bleaching powder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːtʃɪŋ ˌpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈbliːtʃɪŋ ˌpaʊdə / (BrE)
a white powder consisting mainly of calcium hypochlorite, used chiefly for bleaching fabrics and as a disinfectant
chemical bleaching and disinfecting powder

Examples

The hospital used bleaching powder to disinfect the contaminated areas.
You can remove tough stains on white clothes by soaking them in water with bleaching powder.
Proper handling of bleaching powder is important, as the chemical can irritate the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorinated lime - another term for bleaching powder
