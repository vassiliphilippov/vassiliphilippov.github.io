pernyi moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrni ˌmɑθ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːni ˌmɒθ / (BrE)
A large Chinese moth (Antheraea pernyi) that produces a brownish silk, used in sericulture.
Chinese moth producing brownish silk

Examples

The pernyi moth is widely farmed in China for its valuable silk.
Silk produced by the pernyi moth is coarser and darker than that of the silkworm.
Biologists studied the life cycle of the pernyi moth to improve silk production methods.

Etymology

Named after Jean Perrin, French entomologist, with the species name 'pernyi' in his honor.
