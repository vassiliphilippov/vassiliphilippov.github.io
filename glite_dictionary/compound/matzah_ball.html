matzah ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑtsə bɔl / (AmE), / ˈmatsə bɔːl / (BrE)
A dumpling made from matzo meal, eggs, and fat, traditionally served in Jewish cuisine, especially in soup.
Jewish dumpling made from matzo meal

Examples

My grandmother always makes delicious matzah balls for Passover dinner.
The soup was served with one large matzah ball floating in the bowl.
Have you ever tried chicken soup with matzah balls during the holidays?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumpling - small mass of dough cooked by boiling or steaming, sometimes filled

Etymology

From Yiddish 'matse' (unleavened bread) and English 'ball'.
