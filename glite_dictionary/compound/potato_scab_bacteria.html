potato scab bacteria

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ skæb bækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ skæb bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
bacteria responsible for causing potato scab, a disease in potatoes marked by brown, corky patches on the tubers
bacteria causing disease in potatoes

Examples

Crop rotation can help prevent the spread of potato scab bacteria in the soil.
The presence of potato scab bacteria led to a significant decrease in the farm's potato yield.
Researchers are developing resistant varieties to reduce losses caused by potato scab bacteria.
