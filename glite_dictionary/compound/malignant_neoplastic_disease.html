malignant neoplastic disease

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪɡ.nənt niː.oʊˈplæstɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡ.nənt niː.əʊˈplæstɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease characterized by a malignant tumor or growth that arises from uncontrolled and abnormal cell division, potentially spreading to other body parts via the lymphatic system or bloodstream.
malignant tumor or cancer
medical

Examples

Early detection is crucial in treating malignant neoplastic disease effectively.
She was diagnosed with a rare form of malignant neoplastic disease last year.
Recent advances in medicine have improved survival rates for those with malignant neoplastic disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - a disease caused by uncontrolled division of abnormal cells in a part of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'malignus' (evil in nature) and Greek 'neoplasma' (new formation) plus 'disease'.
