saddle seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædəl sit / (AmE), / ˈsædəl siːt / (BrE)
A type of chair seat that is slightly concave and sometimes has a raised ridge in the center, resembling the seat of a saddle.
a concave, saddle-like chair seat

Examples

The old wooden stool featured a saddle seat that provided extra comfort during long hours of work.
Many craftsmen prefer a saddle seat because it distributes weight more evenly across the body.
She admired the elegant saddle seat carved into the dining room chairs.

Synset Examples

the saddle seat fitted his buttocks nicely

Etymology

From 'saddle' (the seat for a rider on a horse) and 'seat,' referring to the shape's resemblance to a horse saddle.
