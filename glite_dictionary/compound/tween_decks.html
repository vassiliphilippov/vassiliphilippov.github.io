'tween decks

1. ADV

/ twiːn dɛks / (AmE), / twiːn dɛks / (BrE)
in or into the space between the decks of a ship.
in the space between ship's decks

Examples

The sailors slept 'tween decks during the long voyage.
Cargo was stored 'tween decks to maximize space on the ship.
Passengers often found the air stuffy when living 'tween decks for weeks at sea.

Etymology

Shortened form of 'between decks', chiefly used in maritime contexts.
