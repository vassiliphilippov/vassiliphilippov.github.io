potassium acid carbonate

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtæsiəm ˈæsɪd ˈkɑrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / pəˈtasiəm ˈasɪd ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a crystalline salt (KHCO3) used in baking powder as a leavening agent and as an antacid to relieve indigestion
crystalline salt used in baking and medicine
medical

Examples

Many bakers use potassium acid carbonate as a leavening agent in recipes requiring low-sodium alternatives.
Doctors sometimes recommend potassium acid carbonate to neutralize stomach acid and relieve indigestion.
Because it reacts with acids to release carbon dioxide, potassium acid carbonate is often found in baking powder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium bicarbonate - alternative name for potassium acid carbonate, KHCO3

Etymology

From 'potassium' (element name) + 'acid carbonate' describing its chemical composition.
