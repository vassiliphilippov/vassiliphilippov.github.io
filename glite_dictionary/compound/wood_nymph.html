wood nymph

1. NOUN

/ wʊd nɪmf / (AmE), / wʊd nɪmf / (BrE)
a mythological female spirit or minor deity associated with forests or woods, especially in Greek and Roman mythology
mythological female spirit of the woods

Examples

The ancient Greeks believed that every forest had a wood nymph watching over it.
In the old tale, a wood nymph enchanted travelers who wandered into her grove.
Artists often depicted a wood nymph as a graceful woman surrounded by trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dryad - a tree or wood nymph in Greek mythology
