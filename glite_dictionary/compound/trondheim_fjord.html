Trondheim Fjord

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɑnˌhaɪm ˈfjɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtrɒnˌhaɪm ˈfjɔːd / (BrE)
a long, narrow inlet of the Norwegian Sea in central Norway, known for its scenic beauty and historical significance
fjord in central Norway

Examples

The stunning landscapes around the Trondheim Fjord attract visitors from all over the world.
Ancient settlements have been discovered along the shores of the Trondheim Fjord.
Sailing on the Trondheim Fjord offers breathtaking views of Norway's natural beauty.

Etymology

Named after the city of Trondheim; 'fjord' from Norwegian, meaning a narrow sea inlet.
