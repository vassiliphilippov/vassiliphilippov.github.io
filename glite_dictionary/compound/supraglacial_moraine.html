supraglacial moraine

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːprəˈɡleɪʃəl məˈreɪn / (AmE), / ˌsuːprəˈɡleɪsɪəl məˈreɪn / (BrE)
A mound or ridge of debris that accumulates on the surface of a glacier, formed from material falling onto or carried by the glacier.
debris on glacier surface

Examples

The mountaineers crossed a supraglacial moraine on their way up the glacier.
Scientists found ancient plant material buried within the supraglacial moraine.
Spring meltwater often exposes new debris on the supraglacial moraine.

Synset Examples

The debris can accumulate due to ice flow toward the surface in the ablation zone, melting of surface ice or from debris that falls onto the glacier from valley sidewalls, forming superglacial moraines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lateral moraine - a type of moraine formed along the sides of a glacier
  • Antonym: subglacial moraine - moraine formed beneath a glacier

Etymology

From Latin 'supra' (above) + 'glacies' (ice), referring to its position on top of glacier ice.
