Kanarese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˈriːz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈriːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken mainly in the Indian state of Karnataka
a Dravidian language of southern India

Examples

The Kanarese language has a rich literary history dating back over a thousand years.
Many people in southern India speak the Kanarese language as their mother tongue.
Linguists classify the Kanarese language as part of the Dravidian family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kannada - the standardized name for the same Dravidian language

Etymology

Kanarese is an older English variant name for Kannada, from the region name 'Canara' (now Karnataka), itself a colonial corruption of 'Karunadu' (Kannada for 'elevated land').
