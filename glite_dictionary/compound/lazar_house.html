lazar house

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæzər ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈlæzə ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a hospital for people with infectious diseases, especially leprosy, in historical contexts
historical hospital for leprosy and infectious diseases
archaic

Examples

In medieval Europe, a lazar house was often located on the outskirts of town.
They restored the old lazar house as a historical monument to early medicine.
Outcasts suffering from leprosy were sent to the local lazar house for care.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'lazaretum', ultimately from the biblical Lazarus, associated with leprosy
