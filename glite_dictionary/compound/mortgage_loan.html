mortgage loan

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.gɪdʒ loʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.ɡɪdʒ ləʊn / (BrE)
a loan secured by real estate, typically used to purchase property, and repaid over a specified period
loan secured by real estate

Examples

They applied for a mortgage loan to purchase their first home.
Because of his low credit score, his mortgage loan was approved at a higher interest rate.
After paying off their mortgage loan, the family owned their house outright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortgage - legal agreement by which property is used as security for a loan
  • Synonym: home loan - loan used to buy a house, secured by real estate

Etymology

From Old French 'mort gage' (dead pledge) for 'mortgage' and Middle English 'lonen' (to lend) for 'loan'.
