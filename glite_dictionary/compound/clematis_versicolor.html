Clematis versicolor

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmeɪtɪs vɜr.sɪˌkʌl.ɚ / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs ˌvɜː.sɪˈkʌl.ə / (BrE)
a woody vine native to the southern United States, noted for its purple or blue flowers with leathery, recurved sepals
woody vine with purple or blue flowers

Examples

The Clematis versicolor brightened the garden with its vibrant blue flowers.
Gardeners in the South often plant Clematis versicolor for its showy appearance.
We identified the vine as Clematis versicolor by its distinctive leathery sepals.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Clematis' meaning a climbing plant, and 'versicolor' meaning 'of various colors'.
