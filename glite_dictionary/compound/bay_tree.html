bay tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ ˌtriː / (BrE)
A small Mediterranean evergreen tree (Laurus nobilis) with aromatic glossy leaves used for flavoring food, and historically used by the ancient Greeks to crown victors.
Mediterranean tree with aromatic leaves used in cooking

Examples

The chef added a fresh bay tree leaf to the simmering stew for extra flavor.
In ancient Greece, champions were crowned with wreaths made from the bay tree.
She planted a bay tree in her garden to use its aromatic leaves in her cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laurel - another name for the bay tree or similar trees
  • Degree Higher: olive - another Mediterranean tree with culinary uses
