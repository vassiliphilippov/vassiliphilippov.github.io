criminal congress

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəl ˈkɑŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəl ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
sexual intercourse between individuals that is forbidden or illegal under the law, such as incest or adultery in certain jurisdictions.
illegal sexual intercourse
legal

Examples

The court found him guilty of criminal congress with a minor, leading to a lengthy prison sentence.
Laws regarding criminal congress differ between countries and are often subject to debate and reform.
Historical records often mention cases of criminal congress that shocked communities and prompted legal changes.

Etymology

From Latin 'congressus' (meeting) and 'criminalis' (relating to a crime).
