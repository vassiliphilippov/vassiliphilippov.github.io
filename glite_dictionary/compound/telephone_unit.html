telephone unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪfəʊn ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the quantity of telephone service consumed, often for billing purposes
unit for measuring telephone use

Examples

The cost of international calls is calculated based on the number of telephone units used.
Each telephone unit is equivalent to one minute of talk time.
The phone company charges you a fixed rate per telephone unit.
