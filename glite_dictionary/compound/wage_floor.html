wage floor

1. NOUN

/ weɪdʒ flɔr / (AmE), / weɪdʒ flɔː / (BrE)
The lowest level at which wages are legally permitted to be paid.
minimum legal wage level

Examples

The government introduced a wage floor to ensure workers receive a basic income.
Many economists debate the effects of raising the wage floor on employment.
If the wage floor increases, some small businesses may struggle to afford higher salaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minimum wage - the lowest wage permitted by law or by a special agreement
