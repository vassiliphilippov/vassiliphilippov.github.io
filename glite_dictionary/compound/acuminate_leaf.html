acuminate leaf

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuː.mə.nət liːf / (AmE), / əˈkjuː.mɪ.nət liːf / (BrE)
A leaf that gradually tapers to a long, slender point at its tip.
leaf narrowing to a slender point

Examples

The plant is easily recognized by its acuminate leaf that ends in a delicate point.
Botanists identified the species by examining the shape of its acuminate leaf.
The acuminate leaf structure helps some rainforest trees shed water quickly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: acute leaf - a leaf with a sharp but less tapered point

Etymology

From Latin 'acuminatus' (sharpened, pointed).
