access road

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksɛs roʊd / (AmE), / ˈæksɛs rəʊd / (BrE)
A short road that provides a way to enter or leave a main road, especially an expressway or highway.
short road giving entry to a main road or expressway

Examples

To reach the main highway, take the access road on your right.
A new access road was constructed to improve traffic flow to the hospital.
In heavy traffic, the access road can sometimes be faster than the expressway itself.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call an access road a ‘slip road’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip road - a road that allows vehicles to leave or enter a motorway (chiefly British)
