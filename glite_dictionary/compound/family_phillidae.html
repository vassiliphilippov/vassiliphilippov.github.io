family Phillidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈfɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈfɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects, commonly known as leaf insects, noted for their broad, flat bodies and camouflage that resembles leaves.
a family of leaf insects

Examples

The family Phillidae contains remarkable insects that mimic leaves for camouflage.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Phillidae in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the family Phillidae are prized in insect collections because of their unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf insect - insect that resembles a leaf as camouflage, member of family Phillidae

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' meaning 'leaf', referring to their leaf-like appearance.
