gold alloy

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld ˈælɔɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈælɔɪ / (BrE)
a mixture of gold with one or more other metals, often made to improve strength or change color, commonly used in jewelry and industry
mixture of gold and other metals

Examples

Most wedding rings are made from a gold alloy to make them more durable.
The jeweler recommended a gold alloy with a higher percentage of copper for a rosy color.
Dental crowns are often crafted from a special gold alloy for strength and longevity.

Synset Examples

Gold alloy is often used in jewelry making.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy - a mixture of metals
  • Antonym: pure gold - gold with no added metals
