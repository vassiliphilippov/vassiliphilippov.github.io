Helladic civilization

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈlædɪk sɪˌvɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / hɛˈlædɪk sɪˌvɪləˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the main Bronze Age culture of mainland Greece, which flourished from about 2500 to 1100 BC
Bronze Age Greek mainland culture

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable artifacts from the Helladic civilization in southern Greece.
The Helladic civilization is known for its early use of bronze and advances in architecture.
Scholars often compare the Helladic civilization with the Minoan civilization of Crete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minoan civilization - the Bronze Age culture of Crete
  • Synonym: Mycenaean civilization - later phase of Helladic culture in mainland Greece

Etymology

From Greek 'Hellas' (the Greek name for Greece) plus 'ic'.
