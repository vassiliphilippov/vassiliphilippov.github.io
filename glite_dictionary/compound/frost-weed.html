frost-weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑst wid / (AmE), / ˈfrɒst wiːd / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb native to the eastern United States, known for clusters of white flowers and sometimes producing frost-like exudations at its base.
a tall perennial herb with white flowers

Examples

In autumn, frost-weed displays beautiful white blooms across the meadow.
Gardeners appreciate frost-weed for its ability to attract pollinators.
After the first frost, you can often see delicate ice formations at the base of the frost-weed.
