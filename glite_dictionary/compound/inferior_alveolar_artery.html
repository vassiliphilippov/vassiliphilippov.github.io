inferior alveolar artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriər ælˈvioʊlər ˈɑːrti / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ælˈviːələ ˈɑːti / (BrE)
an artery that runs through the mandibular canal supplying blood to the lower teeth
artery supplying blood to lower teeth
medical

Examples

The inferior alveolar artery is often examined during dental surgeries.
Damage to the inferior alveolar artery can lead to significant bleeding in oral procedures.
The dentist explained how the inferior alveolar artery supplies blood to the lower jaw.

Etymology

From Latin 'alveolaris' (of a cavity) and 'arteria' (artery).
