Great Lakes

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt leɪks / (AmE), / ɡreɪt leɪks / (BrE)
a group of five large, interconnected freshwater lakes in central North America—Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, and Ontario
five major lakes in central North America

Examples

The Great Lakes contain about 21% of the world's surface fresh water.
Shipping routes across the Great Lakes are vital for transporting goods between the United States and Canada.
Environmentalists are working to protect the unique ecosystems of the Great Lakes.
