multiengine plane

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˈɛn.dʒɪn pleɪn / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈɛn.dʒɪn pleɪn / (BrE)
An airplane that has two or more engines.
airplane with two or more engines

Examples

The pilot trained on a multiengine plane to prepare for commercial flights.
It requires extra certification to fly a multiengine plane safely.
Damage to one engine on a multiengine plane doesn't necessarily mean disaster.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-engine plane - an airplane with only one engine

Etymology

From 'multi-' (many) + 'engine' + 'plane' (short for 'airplane').
