Schwann cell

1. NOUN

/ ʃwɑn sɛl / (AmE), / ʃwæn sɛl / (BrE)
a cell in the peripheral nervous system that surrounds nerve fibers and forms the myelin sheath
cell forming myelin in peripheral nerves
medical

Examples

The Schwann cell wraps around the nerve axon to insulate it and speed up signal transmission.
Damage to a Schwann cell can lead to complications in nerve function.
Researchers are studying how Schwann cells regenerate after injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oligodendrocyte - cell forming myelin sheath in the central nervous system

Etymology

Named after Theodor Schwann (1810–1882), German physiologist.
