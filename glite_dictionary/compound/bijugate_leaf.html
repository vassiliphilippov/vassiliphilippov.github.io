bijugate leaf

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈdʒuː.ɡət lif / (AmE), / baɪˈdʒuː.ɡeɪt liːf / (BrE)
A pinnate leaf with two pairs of leaflets.
pinnate leaf with two pairs of leaflets

Examples

The botanist examined the bijugate leaf to count its pairs of leaflets.
This species is characterized by its bijugate leaf arrangement, making identification easier.
You can distinguish a bijugate leaf by the presence of two opposite pairs of leaflets on the stalk.

Etymology

From Latin prefix bi- (two) and Latin jugatus (yoked, paired).
