old saying

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈseɪɪŋ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈseɪɪŋ / (BrE)
a traditional expression or proverb that is widely known and repeated
traditional proverb or expression

Examples

There's an old saying that a stitch in time saves nine.
She often quotes the old saying about honesty being the best policy.
The old saying 'better late than never' is still relevant today.

Synset Examples

The old saying about an elephant trap warns you to be cautious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proverb - a short, well-known saying stating a general truth or piece of advice
  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a principle or truth
