gossip and hearsay

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːsɪp ənd ˈhɪrseɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪp ənd ˈhɪəseɪ / (BrE)
unverified information or rumors, often passed from person to person without confirmation of their truth
unverified rumors or information

Examples

The article was based purely on gossip and hearsay, with no real evidence to support its claims.
You shouldn't make important decisions on the basis of gossip and hearsay.
Her reputation suffered from all the gossip and hearsay circulating in the small town.

Synset Examples

She doesn’t lay store by gossip and hearsay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumor - a currently circulating story or report of uncertain or doubtful truth
  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about other people, typically involving details that are not confirmed as being true
  • Antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true
