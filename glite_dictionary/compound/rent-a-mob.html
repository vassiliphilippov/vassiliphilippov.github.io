rent-a-mob

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnt ə mɑb / (AmE), / ˈrɛnt ə mɒb / (BrE)
A group of people hired to attend demonstrations, protests, or events, often to give a false impression of popular support or opposition.
hired group for protests or demonstrations
informal

Examples

The politician was accused of using a rent-a-mob to boost attendance at his rally.
Media reports suggested the angry crowd was nothing more than a rent-a-mob.
Organizers denied that the demonstration had involved a rent-a-mob to sway public opinion.

Synset Examples

They hired a rent-a-mob to show support for the cause.
The protest looked organized, but it was just a rent-a-mob.
The event was lively, thanks to a well-managed rent-a-mob.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: protester - person who publicly demonstrates strong objection to something

Etymology

From 'rent-a-' (as in 'rent-a-car') plus 'mob', indicating a hired crowd.
