ferricyanic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛrɪsaɪˈænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɛrɪsaɪˈanɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a brown, unstable acid formed by the action of acids on ferricyanides, chiefly known in solution and not isolated in pure form
a brown, unstable acid from ferricyanide

Examples

Ferricyanic acid is typically recognized by its brown color in aqueous solutions.
The chemist attempted the preparation of ferricyanic acid but found it highly unstable.
Researchers studied the reaction of ferricyanic acid with different metal ions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ferrocyanic acid - a related acid formed from ferrocyanide

Etymology

From 'ferri-' (iron) and 'cyanic' (containing cyanogen); denotes a compound of iron and cyanogen.
