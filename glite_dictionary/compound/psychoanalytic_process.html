psychoanalytic process

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊəˌnælɪtɪk ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊəˌnælɪtɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
the mental or therapeutic processes postulated by psychoanalytic theory, especially those occurring during psychoanalysis
process in psychoanalytic theory
medical

Examples

The psychoanalytic process often involves exploring unconscious thoughts and childhood experiences.
An effective psychoanalytic process requires a strong therapeutic relationship between analyst and patient.
The concept of transference is central to understanding the psychoanalytic process.

Etymology

From 'psychoanalytic', relating to psychoanalysis (from Greek 'psyche' meaning mind or soul, and 'analysis'), and 'process' (from Latin 'processus').
