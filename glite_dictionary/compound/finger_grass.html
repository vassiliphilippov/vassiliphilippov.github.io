finger grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡər ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any grass of the genus Chloris, typically found in short grasslands, especially on waste ground or poor soils
grass of the genus Chloris

Examples

Finger grass can quickly spread across abandoned fields and vacant lots.
Many farmers consider finger grass to be a persistent weed in their crops.
The soft blades of finger grass are favored by grazing animals in tropical regions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass with creeping stems that root easily, often considered a lawn weed.
a creeping lawn weed grass

Examples

Finger grass can quickly spread across an entire lawn if not controlled early.
Gardeners often struggle to remove finger grass from flower beds and vegetable patches.
Regular mowing may help prevent finger grass from taking over your yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabgrass - a common grass weed similar in habit and appearance
