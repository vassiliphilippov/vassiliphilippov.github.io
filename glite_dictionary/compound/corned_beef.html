corned beef

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn(d) bif / (AmE), / kɔːnd biːf / (BrE)
Beef that has been cured or pickled in a seasoned brine.
beef preserved in brine

Examples

We made delicious sandwiches with slices of corned beef and mustard.
The traditional dish for St. Patrick's Day is corned beef and cabbage.
She prefers her corned beef served warm with potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastrami - seasoned, smoked, and cured meat, typically beef

Etymology

From 'corn', an old word for grains of salt, meaning beef preserved with coarse salt.

2. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈbif / (AmE), / kɔːnd ˈbiːf / (BrE)
Beef that has been cured or pickled in brine, often used in sandwiches or served with cabbage.
beef cured in brine

Examples

He made a delicious sandwich with corned beef and mustard on rye bread.
Many people enjoy eating corned beef and cabbage on St. Patrick's Day.
The deli sells both pastrami and corned beef by the pound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastrami - highly seasoned smoked beef
  • Degree Lower: roast beef - beef cooked by roasting

Etymology

From 'corn', an old English term for grains of salt, and 'beef' (from Old French 'boef', from Latin 'bos').
