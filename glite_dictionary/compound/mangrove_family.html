mangrove family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋˌgroʊv ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡrəʊv ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A group of trees and shrubs, primarily of the family Rhizophoraceae, that typically grow in dense thickets along tropical and subtropical seacoasts.
group of coastal mangrove trees/shrubs

Examples

Species in the mangrove family are well adapted to saline coastal environments.
The mangrove family includes many trees that protect shorelines from erosion.
Ecologists are researching the unique root systems found in the mangrove family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangrove - a tropical tree that grows in coastal swamps or tidal areas
