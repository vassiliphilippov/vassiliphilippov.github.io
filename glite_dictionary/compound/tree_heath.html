tree heath

1. NOUN

/ tri hɛθ / (AmE), / triː hiːθ / (BrE)
A gaunt, evergreen shrubby tree native to Tasmania, with slender tapering leaves 3 to 5 feet long.
Tasmanian evergreen shrubby tree

Examples

The tree heath is known for its long, slender leaves and hardy nature.
Botanists are studying the unique ecology of the tree heath in Tasmania.
Gardeners admired the elegant silhouette of the mature tree heath in the landscape.

2. NOUN

/ tri hiθ / (AmE), / triː hiːθ / (BrE)
an evergreen Mediterranean shrub (Erica arborea) with fragrant white flowers in clusters and hard woody roots used for making tobacco pipes
evergreen Mediterranean shrub (Erica arborea)

Examples

The roots of tree heath are highly prized for making quality tobacco pipes.
In spring, the tree heath displays clusters of fragrant white flowers along its branches.
Gardeners in Mediterranean climates often plant tree heath for its evergreen foliage and attractive blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - any of various low-growing shrubs, often found on heaths or moorlands
