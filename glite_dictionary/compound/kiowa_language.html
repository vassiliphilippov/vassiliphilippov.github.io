Kiowa language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.oʊ.wə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.əwə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Tanoan language traditionally spoken by the Kiowa people of the southern Plains in the United States
the language of the Kiowa people

Examples

She is studying the Kiowa language as part of her research into Native American cultures.
Efforts are being made to revitalize the Kiowa language among younger generations.
The dictionary contains detailed entries for words in the Kiowa language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tanoan - a language family that includes Kiowa and several Pueblo languages

Etymology

From Kiowa, the name of the indigenous people, combined with 'language'.
