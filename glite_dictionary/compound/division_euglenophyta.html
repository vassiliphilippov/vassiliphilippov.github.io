division Euglenophyta

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən juːˌɡliːnoʊˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən juːˌɡliːnəʊˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
a group of free-swimming flagellate algae classified as a division in the plant kingdom, comprising mostly aquatic organisms found in fresh water.
a division of flagellate algae

Examples

Scientists studied the structure of division Euglenophyta under the microscope.
Members of division Euglenophyta exhibit both plant-like and animal-like characteristics.
Freshwater samples revealed a high abundance of organisms from division Euglenophyta.

Etymology

From New Latin Euglenophyta, from 'Euglena' (the type genus) and Greek 'phyton' (plant).
