family Rhincodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli rɪŋˌkoʊdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli rɪŋˌkɒdəntaɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of small-toothed sharks that comprises only a single species, the whale shark.
shark family including the whale shark
medical

Examples

The family Rhincodontidae includes only one living species, the whale shark.
Researchers study genetic markers specific to the family Rhincodontidae to learn more about its evolutionary history.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting members of the family Rhincodontidae due to their vulnerable status.

Etymology

From New Latin Rhincodontidae, formed from the type genus Rhincodon.
