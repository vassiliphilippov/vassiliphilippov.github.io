a long time

1. NOUN

/ ə lɔŋ taɪm / (AmE), / ə lɒŋ taɪm / (BrE)
a considerable or extended period of time
an extended period; a while

Examples

It took a long time to finish the project, but the results were worth it.
We haven't seen each other for a long time, so we have a lot to catch up on.
Learning a new language can take a long time, but persistence pays off.

Synset Examples

It’s been a long time since we last met.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: a while - a short or unspecified period of time
