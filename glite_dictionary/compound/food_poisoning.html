food poisoning

1. NOUN

/ fuːd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / fuːd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
illness caused by eating food that is contaminated with harmful bacteria, viruses, or toxins
illness from contaminated food

Examples

Several people contracted food poisoning after eating at the restaurant.
Symptoms of food poisoning include nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.
To prevent food poisoning, always refrigerate leftovers promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastroenteritis - inflammation of the stomach and intestines, often caused by infection
