Book of Nehemiah

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˌniːəˈmaɪə / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˌniːəˈmaɪə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament describing how Nehemiah led the rebuilding of Jerusalem after the Babylonian Captivity
Old Testament book about Nehemiah rebuilding Jerusalem

Examples

Many scholars study the Book of Nehemiah to understand the history of post-exilic Jerusalem.
The Book of Nehemiah records details about the rebuilding of Jerusalem's walls.
During Bible study, we discussed leadership lessons from the Book of Nehemiah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ezra - the Old Testament book preceding Nehemiah, also about the return from Babylonian exile

Etymology

Nehemiah from Hebrew Nəḥemyāh, meaning 'comforted by Yahweh'.
