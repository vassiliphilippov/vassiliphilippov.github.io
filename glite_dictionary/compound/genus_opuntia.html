genus Opuntia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs oʊˈpʌn.ti.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əʊˈpʌn.tɪ.ə / (BrE)
a large genus of cactuses native to the Americas, commonly known as prickly pears, with flat, spiny pads and edible fruit.
genus of American cactuses (prickly pears)

Examples

The genus Opuntia includes many species commonly referred to as prickly pears.
Members of the genus Opuntia are well adapted to arid environments.
The edible fruit from cacti of the genus Opuntia is often used in jams and drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly pear - a common name for species in the genus Opuntia

Etymology

Opuntia: from Latin, named after the ancient Greek city of Opus, where a similar plant was found.
