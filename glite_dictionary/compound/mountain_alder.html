mountain alder

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn ˈældɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
A tree native to the western United States, typically found along stream banks and moist slopes, known for its smooth bark and oval leaves.
tree native to western United States

Examples

We saw a beautiful mountain alder growing by the riverbank.
The leaves of the mountain alder turn yellow in autumn.
Several mountain alders were planted to prevent soil erosion on the slope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a type of tree or shrub in the genus Alnus

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈældɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈældə / (BrE)
a small shrubby maple native to eastern North America, known for its scarlet foliage in autumn
shrubby maple of eastern North America

Examples

The mountain alder turns a brilliant red in the fall, attracting photographers and nature lovers.
We spotted a cluster of mountain alder near the edge of the woods during our hike.
The leaves of the mountain alder are especially vivid compared to other maples in the region.
