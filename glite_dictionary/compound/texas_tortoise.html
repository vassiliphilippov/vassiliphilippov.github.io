Texas tortoise

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈtɔːrtəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈtɔːtəs / (BrE)
a species of tortoise native to southern Texas and northern Mexico, closely related to the desert tortoise
species of tortoise native to Texas and Mexico

Examples

The Texas tortoise is listed as a threatened species in the United States.
We spotted a Texas tortoise slowly crossing the dusty road near the ranch.
Biologists monitor the habitat of the Texas tortoise to aid in its conservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert tortoise - species of tortoise native to the Mojave and Sonoran deserts
