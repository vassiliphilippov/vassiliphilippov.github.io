Sacramento sturgeon

1. NOUN

/ sækrəˈmɛntoʊ ˈstɜrdʒən / (AmE), / sakrəˈmɛntəʊ ˈstɜːdʒən / (BrE)
a large food and game fish found in marine and fresh waters along the northwestern coast of North America, particularly in the Sacramento River.
game and food fish of northwest North America

Examples

The Sacramento sturgeon is highly prized by anglers for its size and strength.
Many restaurants on the West Coast serve dishes made from Sacramento sturgeon.
Efforts to conserve the Sacramento sturgeon have increased due to declining populations in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white sturgeon - a large North American sturgeon species

Etymology

Sacramento from the Sacramento River in California; sturgeon from Old French esturgeon, from Latin acipenser.
