tooth decay

1. NOUN

/ tuθ dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / tuːθ dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
The gradual destruction of a tooth's hard surface caused by bacterial activity, often resulting in cavities.
dental disease; decomposition of tooth tissue
medical

Examples

Regular brushing and flossing can help prevent tooth decay in both children and adults.
If left untreated, tooth decay can lead to painful infections or tooth loss.
The dentist found evidence of tooth decay during my annual check-up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - a hole in a tooth caused by decay
  • Synonym: caries - technical term for tooth decay
