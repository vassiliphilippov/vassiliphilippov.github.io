darkling beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrklɪŋ ˌbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈdɑːklɪŋ ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a black or dark-colored, hard-bodied beetle, typically slow-moving, whose larvae often feed on decaying plant material or stored grain
a black or dark-colored hard-bodied beetle

Examples

The farmer discovered several darkling beetles in the grain storage bin.
A darkling beetle crawled slowly over the damp forest floor.
Some people raise darkling beetles as a source of protein-rich food for pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealworm - larval form of the darkling beetle
  • Synonym: weevil - various beetles with elongated snouts, related to darkling beetle

Etymology

From 'darkling' (meaning 'in the dark' or 'dark-colored'), referring to the beetle's typical coloration, and 'beetle' (from Old English 'bitela').
