pernicious anaemia

1. NOUN

/ pərˈnɪʃəs əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / pəˈnɪʃəs əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A chronic and progressive form of anaemia in adults, caused by the body's inability to absorb vitamin B12 due to a lack of intrinsic factor in the stomach.
chronic anaemia from lack of vitamin B12 absorption
medical british

Examples

Because pernicious anaemia affects the absorption of vitamin B12, patients often require lifelong treatment.
The doctor suspected pernicious anaemia after noting the patient's fatigue and neurological symptoms.
Early diagnosis of pernicious anaemia can prevent serious complications such as nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megaloblastic anaemia - a type of anaemia characterized by large red blood cells, often caused by vitamin B12 deficiency
  • Antonym: iron deficiency anaemia - anaemia caused by insufficient iron, a different common type of anaemia

Etymology

From Latin 'perniciosus' (destructive) and Modern Latin 'anaemia' (from Greek 'anaimia', bloodlessness).
