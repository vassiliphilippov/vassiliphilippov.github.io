group AB

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp ˌeɪˈbiː / (AmE), / ɡruːp ˌeɪˈbiː / (BrE)
the blood group whose red blood cells carry both A and B antigens
blood group with both A and B antigens
medical

Examples

Because he is group AB, he can receive blood from any ABO blood type.
The doctor noted that her group AB status made her a universal plasma donor.
People with group AB blood are relatively rare in many populations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: group O - the blood group whose red cells carry neither A nor B antigens
  • Degree Lower: group A - the blood group whose red cells carry only the A antigen
  • Degree Lower: group B - the blood group whose red cells carry only the B antigen
