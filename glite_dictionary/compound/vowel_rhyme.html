vowel rhyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaʊəl raɪm / (AmE), / ˈvaʊəl raɪm / (BrE)
The repetition of similar vowel sounds in the stressed syllables of successive words, especially as a poetic device.
repetition of similar vowel sounds

Examples

Many poets use vowel rhyme to create musicality in their verses.
The subtle vowel rhyme in the second stanza draws the reader's attention.
She analyzed the poem for its use of consonance and vowel rhyme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assonance - repetition of vowel sounds in nearby words
