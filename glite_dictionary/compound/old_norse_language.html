Old Norse language

1. NOUN

/ oʊld nɔrs ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊld nɔːs ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the extinct North Germanic language spoken in Scandinavia and Iceland from about 700 to 1350 AD
extinct medieval Scandinavian language

Examples

Many medieval sagas were originally written in Old Norse language.
The influence of the Old Norse language can be seen in many modern Scandinavian place names.
He studied runic inscriptions to learn more about the Old Norse language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old Icelandic - the western variety of the Old Norse language, spoken in medieval Iceland
  • Degree Lower: Old English - the early form of the English language, used in England before 1150
  • Degree Higher: Modern Icelandic - the modern language descended from Old Norse, spoken in Iceland today

Etymology

Old Norse from Old Norse 'norrœna', referring to the northern people and their language.
