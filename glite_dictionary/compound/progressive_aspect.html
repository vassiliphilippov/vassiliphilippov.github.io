progressive aspect

1. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˈæspɛkt / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˈæspɛkt / (BrE)
the grammatical aspect of a verb that expresses an action or state as ongoing or continuing in time
verb aspect showing ongoing action

Examples

In English, the progressive aspect is formed using a form of 'be' plus the present participle.
The sentence 'She is reading' illustrates the progressive aspect in action.
Not all languages use the progressive aspect to indicate ongoing events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuous aspect - aspect expressing ongoing action or state; often synonymous with progressive aspect

Etymology

From Latin 'progressivus' (advancing), from 'progredi' (to go forward) plus 'aspect' from Latin 'aspectus' (appearance).
