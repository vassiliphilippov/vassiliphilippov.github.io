Australian nettle tree

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˈnɛt̬l ˌtri / (AmE), / əˈstreɪliən ˈnɛtəl ˌtriː / (BrE)
any of several tall Australian trees of the genus Laportea, known for their nettle-like leaves and sometimes stinging hairs
tall Australian tree of genus Laportea

Examples

The leaves of the Australian nettle tree can cause irritation when touched.
Many gardeners avoid planting the Australian nettle tree because of its stinging hairs.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of the Australian nettle tree in subtropical climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle tree - a tree with leaves or features resembling those of nettles
