sulfur dioxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌl.fɚ daɪˈɑks.aɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə daɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, toxic gas (SO₂) with a pungent odor, produced naturally by volcanoes and used in industry; a major contributor to air pollution and acid rain
toxic gas, cause of acid rain
medical

Examples

Sulfur dioxide is released during the combustion of fossil fuels such as coal and oil.
Exposure to high levels of sulfur dioxide can cause respiratory problems.
The eruption spewed ash and sulfur dioxide into the atmosphere, affecting air quality for weeks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sulfur trioxide - an oxide of sulfur (SO₃); forms sulfuric acid with water
  • Synonym: sulfur - a yellow nonmetallic element

Etymology

From sulfur, from Latin 'sulfur', and dioxide, from Greek 'di-' (two) + 'oxys' (sharp, acid).
