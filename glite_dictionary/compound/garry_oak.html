Garry oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæri oʊk / (AmE), / ˈɡæri əʊk / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree of western North America, with crooked branches and pale grey bark, scientifically known as Quercus garryana
tree native to western North America

Examples

The Garry oak is an important part of the ecosystem in the Pacific Northwest.
We hiked through a forest dominated by ancient Garry oaks and wildflowers.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Garry oak habitats from development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oregon white oak - another common name for Garry oak, a tree native to northwestern North America

Etymology

Named after Nicholas Garry, a 19th-century official of the Hudson's Bay Company.
