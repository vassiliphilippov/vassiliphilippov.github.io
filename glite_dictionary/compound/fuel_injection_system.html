fuel injection system

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːəl ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈfjʊə(ə)l ɪnˈdʒɛkʃ(ə)n ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a mechanical system that injects atomized fuel directly into the cylinders of an internal-combustion engine, eliminating the need for a carburetor
mechanical system for delivering fuel in engines

Examples

A modern car's fuel injection system improves both efficiency and emissions compared to older designs.
Engine trouble was traced to a fault in the fuel injection system.
Many high-performance vehicles rely on a direct fuel injection system for precise fuel delivery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carburetor - a device in an internal-combustion engine that mixes air with a fine spray of liquid fuel
