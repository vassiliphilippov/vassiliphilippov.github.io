Central Powers

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛntrəl ˈpaʊərz / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈpaʊəz / (BrE)
the alliance of Germany, Austria-Hungary, and their partner nations that opposed the Allies in World War I (1914–1918)
WWI alliance led by Germany and Austria-Hungary

Examples

During World War I, the Central Powers were primarily composed of Germany and Austria-Hungary.
The defeat of the Central Powers led to major changes in the political map of Europe.
Historians often debate the roles played by each member of the Central Powers during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Allies - the coalition of nations opposing the Central Powers in World War I
