vesicular stomatitis

1. NOUN

/ vəˈsɪkjələr stəˈmætɪtɪs / (AmE), / vɪˈsɪkjʊlə stəʊˈmatɪtɪs / (BrE)
a viral disease affecting horses, cattle, swine, and sometimes humans, characterized by the formation of vesicles in the mouth and on the skin
viral disease forming mouth vesicles in livestock
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the outbreak as vesicular stomatitis after examining lesions in the herd.
Precautions were taken to prevent the spread of vesicular stomatitis among the cattle at the ranch.
In rare cases, humans can contract vesicular stomatitis through direct contact with infected animals.

Etymology

From Latin 'vesicula' (small bladder or vesicle) and Greek 'stomatitis' (inflammation of the mouth).
