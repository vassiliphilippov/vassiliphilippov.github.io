floral cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔrəl kʌp / (AmE), / ˈflɔːrəl kʌp / (BrE)
a cuplike or tubular part of a flower, typically found in some plant families, that bears the sepals, stamens, and often the calyx
cuplike/tubular flower structure bearing sepals and stamens

Examples

The floral cup supports both the sepals and petals in many members of the rose family.
Botanists examined the structure of the floral cup to identify the plant species.
In some plants, nectar accumulates at the base of the floral cup.
