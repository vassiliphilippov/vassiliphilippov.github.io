Fourth Lateran Council

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrθ ˌlæt̬.ə.rən ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθ ˌlæt.ər.ən ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
an ecumenical council of the Roman Catholic Church held in 1215, notable for condemning Albigensianism, issuing reform decrees, and defining key doctrines such as transubstantiation
a major Roman Catholic council held in 1215

Examples

The Fourth Lateran Council marked a turning point in church reform during the medieval period.
One of the doctrines defined at the Fourth Lateran Council was transubstantiation.
The decisions made by the Fourth Lateran Council had lasting effects on Christian theology and ecclesiastical law.

Etymology

Lateran refers to the Lateran Palace in Rome, where the council was held; 'Fourth' signifies its sequence among such councils.
