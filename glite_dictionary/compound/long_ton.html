long ton

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ tʌn / (AmE), / lɒŋ tɒn / (BrE)
a unit of weight in the British imperial system, equal to 2,240 pounds (approximately 1,016 kilograms)
British unit of weight (2,240 pounds)
british

Examples

The ship was capable of carrying over 10,000 long tons of cargo.
Steel production was measured in long tons before Britain adopted the metric system.
One long ton is equivalent to 2,240 pounds, making it heavier than the short ton.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short ton - U.S. customary unit of weight equal to 2,000 pounds
