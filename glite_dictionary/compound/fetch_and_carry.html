to fetch and carry

1. VERB

/ fɛtʃ ənd ˈkæri / (AmE), / fɛtʃ ənd ˈkæri / (BrE)
to perform menial tasks or run errands, especially for someone else, often in a subordinate role
do menial tasks or errands for someone

Examples

During the event, he was expected to fetch and carry for the guests.
She grew tired of fetch and carry jobs around the office.
He refused to fetch and carry any longer and asked for more responsibilities.

Synset Examples

She felt like a fetch and carry servant during the event.
He was always the one to fetch and carry for the group.
They relied on him to fetch and carry all day.
She was tired of the fetch-and-carry routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wait on - to serve someone; to act as a servant
  • Synonym: serve - to act as a servant or helper to someone
