Catalina cherry

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtæ.lɪ.nə ˈtʃɛr.i / (AmE), / kæ.təˈliː.nə ˈtʃɛr.i / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree (Prunus ilicifolia) native to Catalina Island, California, known for its dark red edible fruit
evergreen shrub/tree native to Catalina Island

Examples

The Catalina cherry produces small, dark fruit that attract many birds on the island.
Gardeners appreciate the Catalina cherry for its drought resistance and glossy leaves.
Researchers studied the population of Catalina cherry on Santa Catalina Island to monitor its conservation status.

Etymology

Named after Santa Catalina Island, off the coast of southern California.
