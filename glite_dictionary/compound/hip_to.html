hip to

1. ADJ

/ hɪp tu / (AmE), / hɪp tuː / (BrE)
informed about or aware of the latest trends, ideas, or developments
informed about latest trends
informal american

Examples

These days, you have to be hip to social media trends to succeed in advertising.
She tries to stay hip to the latest developments in technology.
He isn't hip to what's popular among younger people anymore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: with it - knowledgeable about current trends or ideas
  • Antonym: out of touch - not aware of recent developments or trends

Etymology

Originated in American slang, with 'hip' meaning 'fashionable or aware' since the early 20th century.
