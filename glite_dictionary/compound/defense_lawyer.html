defense lawyer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛns ˌlɔjər / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns ˌlɔːjə / (BrE)
A lawyer who represents the accused party (defendant) in a court of law.
lawyer for the accused in court
legal

Examples

The defense lawyer argued that there was not enough evidence to convict his client.
Her defense lawyer cross-examined the key witness during the trial.
A skilled defense lawyer can often negotiate a plea deal for the accused.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosecutor - a lawyer who represents the state in criminal cases
  • Synonym: defense attorney - lawyer representing the defendant (synonym)
