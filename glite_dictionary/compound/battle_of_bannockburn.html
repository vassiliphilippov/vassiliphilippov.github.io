Battle of Bannockburn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænəkˌbɝn ˈbætəl / (AmE), / ˈbænəkˌbɜːn ˈbætəl / (BrE)
a decisive battle in 1314 near Stirling, Scotland, in which Scottish forces under Robert the Bruce defeated the English, securing Scottish independence
decisive 1314 battle securing Scottish independence

Examples

The Battle of Bannockburn marked a turning point in Scotland's struggle for independence.
Historians still discuss the tactics used at the Battle of Bannockburn.
Thousands gathered to commemorate the anniversary of the Battle of Bannockburn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Hastings - a famous battle in 1066 that changed English history
  • Synonym: Robert the Bruce - Scottish king who led at the Battle of Bannockburn

Etymology

Named after Bannockburn, a village near Stirling, Scotland, and the term 'battle'.
