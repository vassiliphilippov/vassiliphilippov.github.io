white whale

1. NOUN

/ waɪt weɪl / (AmE), / waɪt weɪl / (BrE)
a small northern whale, especially the beluga, that is white when adult and found in Arctic and subarctic waters
small Arctic whale; beluga

Examples

The white whale is easily recognized by its distinctive white color and rounded forehead.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of the white whale in northern waters.
A group of white whales surfaced near our boat, enchanting everyone on board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beluga - a small white whale of the Arctic and subarctic regions
