water line

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line marked on the hull of a ship indicating the level to which the water rises when the vessel is afloat on an even keel
mark showing ship's water level

Examples

The water line on the old ship showed obvious signs of rust and algae.
Before each voyage, the crew checked the water line to ensure the vessel was not overloaded.
After a week at sea, a clear water line appeared along the ship's hull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plimsoll line - the marking on a ship's hull indicating safe loading limit

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə laɪn / (BrE)
a line that marks the level reached by a body of water, especially on a wall, boat, or other object
mark indicating water level

Examples

The water line on the basement wall showed how high the floodwaters had risen.
After the river receded, a muddy water line was visible along the trees.
The old boathouse still displays the water line from last year's storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-water mark - the highest point reached by a body of water
  • Synonym: watermark - a mark indicating the former level of water
