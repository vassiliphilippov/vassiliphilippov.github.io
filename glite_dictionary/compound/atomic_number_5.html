atomic number 5

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɑmɪk ˈnʌmbɚ faɪv / (AmE), / əˌtɒmɪk ˈnʌmbə faɪv / (BrE)
the chemical element boron, which has the atomic number 5; a trivalent metalloid found in hard, black crystals or as a yellow or brown powder.
boron as identified by atomic number

Examples

Scientists refer to atomic number 5 when discussing the properties of boron.
Pure atomic number 5 is rarely found in nature but is widely used in industry.
The periodic table lists boron under the heading atomic number 5.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boron - a trivalent metalloid element, symbol B, atomic number 5
