gallic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline organic acid obtained from tannin and used in ink, dyes, and pharmaceutical preparations
colorless crystalline organic acid from tannin

Examples

Researchers extracted gallic acid from oak galls for use in traditional ink making.
The antioxidant properties of gallic acid are studied in pharmaceutical research.
Tannins in tea are partly responsible for its gallic acid content.

Etymology

From French *galique* 'from gall' (oak gall), referring to its source.
