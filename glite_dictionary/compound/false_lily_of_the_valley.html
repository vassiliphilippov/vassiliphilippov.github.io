false lily of the valley

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈlɪli əv ðə ˈvæli / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈlɪli əv ðə ˈvæli / (BrE)
a small plant with white flowers native to western Europe to Japan, resembling the true lily of the valley but belonging to a different genus
a plant with white flowers, resembles lily of the valley

Examples

The false lily of the valley thrives in moist forest environments.
Gardeners often confuse false lily of the valley with its more fragrant namesake.
We saw patches of false lily of the valley blooming along the trail in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily of the valley - a fragrant flowering plant (Convallaria majalis) with bell-shaped white flowers

2. NOUN

/ fɔls ˌlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (AmE), / fɔːls ˌlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (BrE)
A small herbaceous plant of northern North America, with two leaves and spikes of small, fragrant white flowers, resembling the true lily of the valley.
northern North American herb with white flowers

Examples

We spotted a cluster of false lily of the valley growing along the woodland trail.
Unlike its namesake, the false lily of the valley thrives in cooler climates across Canada.
The delicate white blossoms of the false lily of the valley give the forest floor a subtle fragrance in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily of the valley - a European woodland plant with sweetly scented white bell-shaped flowers
