inguinal hernia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡwənl ˈhɝːniə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡwɪnəl ˈhɜːniə / (BrE)
a hernia in which a loop of intestine protrudes into the inguinal canal, most commonly occurring in males
hernia where intestine enters inguinal canal
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with an inguinal hernia and scheduled for surgery.
Heavy lifting can increase the risk of developing an inguinal hernia.
During the examination, the doctor found evidence of an inguinal hernia on the right side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hernia - protrusion of an organ through the structure that contains it

Etymology

From Latin 'inguinalis' (of the groin) and 'hernia' (rupture).
