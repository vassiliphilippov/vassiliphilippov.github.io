goose bump

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡus ˌbʌmp / (AmE), / ˈɡuːs ˌbʌmp / (BrE)
A small, raised area on the skin that appears when you are cold, frightened, or experiencing strong emotions, caused by the contraction of tiny muscles at the base of hair follicles.
small raised area on skin caused by cold or emotion

Examples

The scary movie gave me goose bumps all over my arms.
She felt goose bumps as she stepped into the chilly night air.
Hearing his voice after so many years sent goose bumps down her spine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gooseflesh - the rough, slightly bumpy appearance of skin when cold or frightened

Etymology

So named because the skin appears similar to that of a plucked goose.
