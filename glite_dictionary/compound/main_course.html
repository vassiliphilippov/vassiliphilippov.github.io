main course

1. NOUN

/ meɪn ˈkɔrs / (AmE), / meɪn ˈkɔːs / (BrE)
(Nautical, historical) The principal square sail set on the mainmast of a sailing ship.
principal square sail on the mainmast

Examples

The ship unfurled its main course as the wind picked up.
Sailors worked together to lower the heavy main course before the approaching storm.
With the main course set, the vessel gained impressive speed across the open sea.

2. NOUN

/ meɪn kɔrs / (AmE), / meɪn kɔːs / (BrE)
the principal and most substantial dish served in a meal, usually following the starter and before dessert
principal dish of a meal

Examples

For dinner, I ordered the steak as my main course.
The main course will be served after the soup.
She spent hours preparing the main course for her guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrée - the main course of a meal, especially in America
