hinge joint

1. NOUN

/ hɪndʒ dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / hɪndʒ dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a freely moving joint in the body that allows movement primarily in one plane, like the motion of a door hinge
a joint allowing movement in one plane
medical

Examples

The elbow is a classic example of a hinge joint in the human body.
Unlike ball-and-socket joints, a hinge joint permits movement in only one direction.
In anatomy class, we learned that the knee functions as a hinge joint.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ball-and-socket joint - a joint that allows movement in multiple directions
  • Degree Higher: joint - the point where two bones are connected

2. NOUN

/ hɪndʒ dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / hɪndʒ dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A joint that allows movement in only one plane, like the opening and closing of a door; commonly found in the human body, such as the elbow or knee.
joint allowing movement in one plane

Examples

The human elbow is a classic example of a hinge joint that allows the arm to bend and straighten.
The knee functions as a hinge joint, enabling movement similar to the opening and closing of a door.
Unlike a ball-and-socket joint, a hinge joint permits motion in only one direction.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: joint - a place where two bones are connected
