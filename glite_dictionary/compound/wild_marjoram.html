wild marjoram

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈmɑrdʒəˌræm / (AmE), / waɪld ˈmɑːdʒərəm / (BrE)
an aromatic perennial plant (Origanum vulgare) native to Eurasia, commonly known as oregano and used as a culinary herb
aromatic Eurasian perennial herb (oregano)

Examples

The chef garnished the pizza with fresh wild marjoram leaves for added flavor.
Wild marjoram grows abundantly along the hillsides in southern Europe.
Many herbal teas include wild marjoram because of its pleasant aroma and taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - the dried leaves of the wild marjoram plant, used as a spice
