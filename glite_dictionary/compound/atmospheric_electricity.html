atmospheric electricity

1. NOUN

/ ˌætməsˈfɛrɪk ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌatməsˈfɛrɪk ɪlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
the study or occurrence of electrical phenomena, such as lightning and static charges, in the Earth's atmosphere
electrical phenomena in the atmosphere

Examples

Scientists study atmospheric electricity to understand how lightning and other atmospheric phenomena occur.
The presence of atmospheric electricity can sometimes be detected with special instruments during thunderstorms.
She gave a lecture on the history of atmospheric electricity and its effects on weather patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightning - a flash of light in the sky caused by electricity during a storm
