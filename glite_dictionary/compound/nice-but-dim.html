nice-but-dim

1. ADJ

/ naɪs bət dɪm / (AmE), / naɪs bət dɪm / (BrE)
(of a person) pleasant and friendly but lacking in intelligence or common sense.
pleasant but unintelligent person
informal british

Examples

Everyone found him nice-but-dim, always eager to help but rarely getting things right.
My nice-but-dim brother volunteered again, even though he never remembers the instructions.
She played the classic nice-but-dim character in the sitcom, making the audience laugh with her well-meaning mistakes.

Synset Examples

She’s a nice-but-dim coworker, but we love her.
He's nice-but-dim, always forgetting the simplest things.
He’s nice-but-dim, which makes meetings interesting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dim - unintelligent or slow to understand
  • Synonym: thick - (British, informal) stupid or slow-witted
  • Degree Higher: charming - very pleasant or attractive
