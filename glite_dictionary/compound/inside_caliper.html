inside caliper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪd ˈkæl.ɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd ˈkæl.ɪ.pə / (BrE)
a caliper designed for measuring the internal dimensions of a cavity or hole, with points on its legs that curve outward
caliper for measuring internal dimensions

Examples

The machinist used an inside caliper to check the diameter of the pipe.
You need an inside caliper when measuring the width of a groove or slot.
An inside caliper is an essential tool in both woodworking and metalworking shops.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outside caliper - a caliper for measuring external dimensions
