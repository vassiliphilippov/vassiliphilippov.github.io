Secretary of Labor

1. NOUN

/ səˈkrɛtəɹi əv ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / sɪˈkrɛtəri əv ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
the official who serves as the head of the Department of Labor in the United States government
head of the US Department of Labor
american

Examples

After the president resigned, the Secretary of Labor was appointed as acting head of state.
The Secretary of Labor announced new policies to support workers’ rights last week.
Many labor unions consult with the Secretary of Labor to discuss wage regulations.

Synset Examples

the post of Labor Secretary was created in 1913

  • Synonym: Labor Secretary - alternative phrasing for Secretary of Labor

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkrəˌtɛri əv ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɛkrəˌtəri əv ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
the head of the United States Department of Labor; a member of the president's cabinet responsible for labor issues and policies
head of the U.S. Department of Labor
american

Examples

The president appointed a new Secretary of Labor to address worker rights and employment policies.
As Secretary of Labor, she introduced several important reforms to workplace safety regulations.
When the Secretary of Labor testified before Congress, he emphasized the need for higher minimum wages.
