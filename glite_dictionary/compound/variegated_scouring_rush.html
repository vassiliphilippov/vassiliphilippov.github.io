variegated scouring rush

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriˌeɪɡeɪtɪd ˈskaʊərɪŋ rʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɛəriˌeɪɡeɪtɪd ˈskaʊərɪŋ rʌʃ / (BrE)
a plant species (Equisetum variegatum) found in northern North America, Greenland, and northern and central Europe, characterized by jointed, hollow stems with prominent ridges and often used historically for scouring.
species of horsetail plant

Examples

The variegated scouring rush thrives in damp meadows and along streambanks across northern Europe.
Botanists admire the striped stems of the variegated scouring rush when conducting field studies.
Historically, settlers used the abrasive stems of variegated scouring rush to clean pots and pans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum with hollow, jointed stems
