mineral fertiliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl ˈfəːtɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
an inorganic fertilizer made from minerals, used to supply essential nutrients to plants
inorganic fertilizer from minerals

Examples

Many farmers prefer to use mineral fertiliser for rapid crop growth.
The use of mineral fertiliser can improve soil nutrient levels efficiently.
Some gardeners combine organic and mineral fertiliser to achieve the best results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic fertiliser - fertilizer derived from natural organic sources such as compost or manure
