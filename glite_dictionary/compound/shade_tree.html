shade tree

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪd triː / (AmE), / ʃeɪd triː / (BrE)
A tree planted or valued chiefly for providing shade from sunlight.
tree grown mainly for shade

Examples

We planted a shade tree in the backyard to make summer afternoons more comfortable.
A large shade tree can lower the temperature around your house significantly.
Maples are often chosen as shade trees for parks and streets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ornamental tree - tree grown primarily for decorative purposes
  • Degree Higher: canopy tree - a large tree whose branches form a canopy overhead
