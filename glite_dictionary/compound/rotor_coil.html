rotor coil

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊtər kɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtə kɔɪl / (BrE)
the rotating armature or winding in an electric motor or generator that carries current and interacts with the magnetic field to produce motion or induce voltage
rotating coil in a motor or generator

Examples

The rotor coil must be properly insulated to prevent short circuits in the generator.
Engineers checked the resistance of each rotor coil before assembling the electric motor.
A malfunctioning rotor coil can cause the entire machine to run inefficiently.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stator coil - the stationary coil in a motor or generator
