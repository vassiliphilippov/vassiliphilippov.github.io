family Nepidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli nɪˈpaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli nɛˈpiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic insects, commonly known as water scorpions, characterized by their elongated bodies and breathing tubes
family of aquatic insects (water scorpions)

Examples

Members of the family Nepidae are known for their long, slender bodies and raptorial forelegs.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Nepidae in Southeast Asian wetlands.
The family Nepidae is commonly referred to as water scorpions due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water scorpion - aquatic insect of family Nepidae

Etymology

From modern Latin *Nepidae*, from the genus name *Nepa*.
