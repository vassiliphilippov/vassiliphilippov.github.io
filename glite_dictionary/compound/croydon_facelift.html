Croydon facelift

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔɪdən ˈfeɪslɪft / (AmE), / ˈkrɔɪdən ˈfeɪslɪft / (BrE)
A term used, often humorously or critically, to refer to urban regeneration efforts or cosmetic improvements, especially when considered superficial.
urban cosmetic improvements; superficial regeneration
british

Examples

The city council promised a Croydon facelift to revitalize the old market district.
Some critics argue that the Croydon facelift only hides deeper social issues in the area.
After the Croydon facelift, property values in the neighborhood began to rise.

Synset Examples

The neighborhood received a Croydon facelift last year.
Residents were thrilled with the Croydon facelift improvements.
After the Croydon facelift, the area looked vibrant.

Etymology

From Croydon, a borough of London, combined with 'facelift' (a cosmetic surgery for the face), indicating superficial or cosmetic refurbishment.
