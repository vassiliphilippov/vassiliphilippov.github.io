solvency margin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlvənsi ˈmɑːrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɒlvənsi ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
the amount by which an insurance company's assets exceed its liabilities, calculated according to regulations, indicating financial stability.
excess of assets over liabilities (insurance)

Examples

The insurer maintained a strong solvency margin throughout the fiscal year.
Regulators require companies to report their solvency margin annually.
A low solvency margin can jeopardize an insurance firm's operating license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvency - ability to meet long-term financial obligations
