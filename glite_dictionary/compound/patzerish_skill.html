patzerish skill

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætsərɪʃ skɪl / (AmE), / ˈpatzərɪʃ skɪl / (BrE)
A level of skill that is notably poor or clumsy, especially in playing games such as chess.
clumsy or poor skill in games

Examples

He put on a brave face, but his patzerish skill was obvious to everyone at the chess club.
Her patzerish skill made the board game more entertaining for the other players.
Though he took the competition seriously, his patzerish skill meant he lost most of his matches.

Synset Examples

Despite his patzerish skills, he enjoyed the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incompetence - lack of ability or skill

Etymology

Formed from 'patzer', a slang term in chess for an unskilled player, from German, with '-ish' suffix indicating qualities of a patzer.
