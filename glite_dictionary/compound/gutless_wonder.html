gutless wonder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌtləs ˈwʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtləs ˈwʌndə / (BrE)
a person who lacks courage or determination; a coward
cowardly person
informal

Examples

He was called a gutless wonder for running away from the fight.
Many people considered the manager a gutless wonder because she never made tough decisions.
Don’t be a gutless wonder—speak up for yourself.

Synset Examples

He refused to stand up for his beliefs, just a gutless wonder.
She thought he was a gutless wonder for not defending her.
Critics called him a gutless wonder for avoiding the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage or is easily frightened
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage; not afraid

Etymology

From 'gutless' (lacking courage) + 'wonder' (used ironically to describe someone notable for something undesirable).
