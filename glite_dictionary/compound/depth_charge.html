depth charge

1. NOUN

/ dɛpθ tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɛpθ tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a type of bomb designed to explode at a specific depth underwater, used especially to destroy submarines
underwater bomb for destroying submarines

Examples

The destroyer launched a depth charge to target the enemy submarine.
During World War II, depth charges were a crucial weapon against underwater threats.
The echo sounder detected an enemy vessel, so the crew prepared to drop a depth charge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torpedo - a self-propelled underwater missile
  • Degree Lower: mine - an explosive device placed in water or on land
