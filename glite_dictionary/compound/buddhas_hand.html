Buddha's hand

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊdəz hænd / (AmE), / ˈbʊdəz hand / (BrE)
A large, lemon-like citrus fruit with finger-like segments, usually lacking pulp or juice.
citrus fruit with finger-like segments

Examples

The Buddha's hand is often used to perfume rooms because of its strong citrus fragrance.
Chefs sometimes use thin slices of the Buddha's hand as a garnish for desserts or salads.
You can find Buddha's hand in specialty markets, especially during the winter season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citron - a type of citrus fruit closely related to Buddha's hand

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to representations of the Buddha's hand in Asian art.
