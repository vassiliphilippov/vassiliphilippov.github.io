personal pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsənl ˈproʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənl ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
A pronoun that is used to refer to a specific person or thing, distinguishing between first, second, and third person.
pronoun referring to person (I, you, he, she, etc.)

Examples

In English, personal pronouns include words like 'I', 'you', 'he', and 'she'.
Children learn to use personal pronouns at an early age.
When writing formally, it is important to use personal pronouns correctly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that replaces a noun

Etymology

From 'personal' (from Latin personalis, from persona 'person') + 'pronoun' (from Latin pronomen, from pro- 'for' + nomen 'name').
