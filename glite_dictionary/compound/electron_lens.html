electron lens

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛk.trɑn lɛnz / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn lɛnz / (BrE)
An electronic device that uses electric or magnetic fields to focus a beam of electrons, typically used in electron microscopes and particle accelerators.
device focusing electron beams

Examples

The electron lens is crucial for focusing the electron beam in a transmission electron microscope.
Scientists adjusted the electron lens to obtain a clearer image of the specimen.
Modern electron microscopes rely on an advanced electron lens system for high-resolution imaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens - a piece of glass or other transparent material used to focus light rays
