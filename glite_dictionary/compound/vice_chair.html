vice chair

1. NOUN

/ vaɪs tʃɛr / (AmE), / vaɪs tʃɛː / (BrE)
a person who ranks immediately below the chairperson and may serve in their place if necessary
person ranking just below a chairperson

Examples

She was elected vice chair of the committee at last week's meeting.
If the chair is absent, the vice chair will preside over the session.
The vice chair presented the annual report on behalf of the board.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chairperson - the presiding officer of a meeting, committee, etc.

Etymology

From Latin vice (in place of) + English chair (short for chairperson).
