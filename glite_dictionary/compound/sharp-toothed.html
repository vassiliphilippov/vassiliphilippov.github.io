sharp-toothed

1. ADJ

/ ʃɑrptuθt / (AmE), / ʃɑːptuːθt / (BrE)
having sharp teeth, especially used to describe animals with pointed or dangerous teeth
having sharp teeth

Examples

The sharp-toothed piranha is feared by swimmers and fishermen alike.
We spotted a sharp-toothed creature lurking beneath the waves.
Ancient myths often warn of sharp-toothed monsters guarding hidden treasures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fang-toothed - having long, pointed teeth; used similarly of animals

2. ADJ

/ ʃɑrp tuθt / (AmE), / ʃɑːp tuːθt / (BrE)
having teeth that are pointed or able to cut easily, often used to describe animals that prey on others
having pointed, cutting teeth

Examples

A sharp-toothed piranha lurked beneath the surface of the river.
The dinosaur museum displayed a skeleton of a sharp-toothed predator.
Be careful when swimming here, as the reef is home to several sharp-toothed species.

Synset Examples

The sharp-toothed creature prowled the dark forest.
That animal has sharp-toothed fangs for hunting.
Watch out for the sharp-toothed fish in the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fang - a long, pointed tooth, especially in animals that prey on others
