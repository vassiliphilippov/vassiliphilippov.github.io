evil eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈivəl aɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːvəl aɪ / (BrE)
a look believed to have the power to cause harm, misfortune, or injury to others, often due to envy or malice
look believed to cause harm or bad luck

Examples

She gave him the evil eye after he ruined the surprise party.
Some people believe wearing a charm can protect you from the evil eye.
The villagers feared the evil eye and took precautions against jealous neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a spell or invocation believed to bring harm or bad luck

Etymology

From Old English 'yfel' (evil) + 'eye', the superstition traces back to ancient Greek and Roman beliefs.
