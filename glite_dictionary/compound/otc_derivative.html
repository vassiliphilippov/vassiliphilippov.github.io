OTC derivative

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ ti ˈsi dəˌrɪvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊ tiː ˈsiː dɪˈrɪvətɪv / (BrE)
a financial contract whose terms are privately negotiated and traded directly between two parties, rather than through an exchange
privately traded financial contract

Examples

Many financial institutions prefer to use OTC derivatives for greater flexibility in contract terms.
The risks associated with OTC derivatives can be higher due to the lack of standardization and regulation.
He managed a portfolio that included both exchange-traded and OTC derivatives.

Synset Examples

The OTC derivative market is the largest market for derivatives, and is largely unregulated with respect to disclosure of information between the parties, since the OTC market is made up of banks and other highly sophisticated parties, such as hedge funds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exchange-traded derivative - a derivative traded via an organized exchange

Etymology

OTC is an abbreviation of 'over the counter', indicating transactions made outside of formal exchanges.
