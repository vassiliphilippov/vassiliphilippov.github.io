fair play

1. NOUN

/ fɛr pleɪ / (AmE), / feə pleɪ / (BrE)
impartial and just behavior, especially in sports or competition, in accordance with rules and ethics
impartial behavior in sports or competition

Examples

The spirit of fair play is essential in any athletic competition.
Coaches urge players to follow the principles of fair play at all times.
Without fair play, the outcome of the game would not be respected.

Synset Examples

The referee emphasized fair play throughout the match.
She believes in fair play and honesty in competition.
It’s important to practice fair play in sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sportsmanship - respectful and ethical behavior in sports
