leg of lamb

1. NOUN

/ lɛɡ əv læm / (AmE), / lɛɡ əv læm / (BrE)
the hind leg of a lamb, typically prepared and cooked as a roast
cut of lamb for roasting

Examples

We're serving a roast leg of lamb for Easter dinner this year.
She bought a fresh leg of lamb from the butcher for the family gathering.
The recipe called for marinating the leg of lamb overnight for maximum flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rack of lamb - a cut of lamb comprising the rib section, often roasted
