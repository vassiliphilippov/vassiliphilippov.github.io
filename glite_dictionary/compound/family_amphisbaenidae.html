family Amphisbaenidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli æmˌfɪzbiəˈniːdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli amˌfɪzbiˈniːdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing, legless lizards known as worm lizards, characterized by their elongated bodies and underground lifestyle.
a family of worm lizards

Examples

The family Amphisbaenidae includes several species of burrowing, legless reptiles found in tropical regions.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Amphisbaenidae in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the family Amphisbaenidae are often called worm lizards because of their appearance and lifestyle.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Amphisbaenidae, based on Amphisbaena (genus name), from Greek amphisbainein (to go both ways, i.e., move forwards and backwards), referring to the genus' reputed movement.
