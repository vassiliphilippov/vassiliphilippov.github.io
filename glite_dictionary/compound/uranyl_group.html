uranyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrˌænɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.rə.nɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a bivalent chemical group with the formula UO₂, often found forming salts with acids
a chemical group: UO₂ in compounds
medical

Examples

The yellow color of the crystals is due to the presence of the uranyl group.
Many compounds containing the uranyl group are used in analytical chemistry.
The reactivity of the uranyl group allows it to form various salts with acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uranyl - the divalent UO₂²⁺ ion or group containing uranium and oxygen

Etymology

From 'uranium' + '-yl', indicating a chemical group containing uranium.
