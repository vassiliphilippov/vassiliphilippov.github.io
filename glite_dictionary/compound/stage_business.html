stage business

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / steɪdʒ ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
small actions or gestures performed by an actor on stage, not part of the main action, to enhance the character or scene's realism
actions by an actor to add realism or effect

Examples

The actor's clever stage business with the letter drew laughter from the audience.
She silently engaged in stage business by dusting a bookshelf while the main dialogue continued.
His stage business with the hat added depth to his character's nervousness.

Synset Examples

his business with the cane was hilarious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage direction - an instruction in the script of a play, indicating movement or action of performers
