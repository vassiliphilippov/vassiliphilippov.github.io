barbigerous nature

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈbɪdʒərəs ˈneɪtʃər / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɪdʒərəs ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
the state or quality of having bristles or stiff hairs
having bristles or stiff hairs

Examples

The barbigerous nature of the plant made it difficult to handle without gloves.
Biologists noted the barbigerous nature of several newly discovered species.
The caterpillar's barbigerous nature was a defense mechanism against predators.

Synset Examples

The barbigerous nature of the plant deterred many herbivores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hirsute - covered with hair
  • Antonym: glabrous - lacking hair or bristles

Etymology

From Latin 'barbiger' (bearded) + English 'nature'.
