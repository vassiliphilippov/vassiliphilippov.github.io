mountain gorilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tən gəˈrɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn ɡəˈrɪl.ə / (BrE)
a large, rare subspecies of gorilla living in the mountainous forests of central Africa, especially the Virunga Mountains and Bwindi Impenetrable Forest.
subspecies of gorilla found in African mountains

Examples

Researchers spent months observing the mountain gorilla in its natural habitat.
The mountain gorilla population has declined due to poaching and habitat loss.
Tourists travel to Rwanda to catch a glimpse of the rare mountain gorilla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorilla - the largest great ape native to Africa
