milk sugar

1. NOUN

/ mɪlk ˈʃʊɡər / (AmE), / mɪlk ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
a sugar found in milk, consisting of one glucose molecule linked to one galactose molecule; also known as lactose
a sugar present in milk (lactose)

Examples

Certain people are unable to digest milk sugar due to lactose intolerance.
The process of cheese-making involves the breakdown of milk sugar by bacteria.
Milk sugar gives milk its slightly sweet taste.

Synset Examples

cow's milk contains about 4.7% lactose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactose - the disaccharide sugar found in milk
