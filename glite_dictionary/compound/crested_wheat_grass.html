crested wheat grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛstɪd ˈwiːt ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛstɪd ˈwiːt ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A Eurasian grass species (Agropyron cristatum) grown in North America, especially in the Great Plains, for forage and erosion control.
Eurasian grass used for forage and erosion control

Examples

Crested wheat grass is widely planted on the prairies to prevent soil erosion.
Farmers often rely on crested wheat grass as a reliable forage crop for their livestock.
The field was reseeded with crested wheat grass after the drought to restore vegetation.
