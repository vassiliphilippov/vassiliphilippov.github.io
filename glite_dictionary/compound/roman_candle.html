Roman candle

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈkæn.dəl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈkæn.dl̩ / (BrE)
a cylindrical firework that emits a series of colored balls of fire one after another
a type of cylindrical firework

Examples

The children watched in awe as a Roman candle shot bursts of colored light into the night sky.
He bought a Roman candle for the Fourth of July celebration.
It's important to handle a Roman candle carefully, as it can be dangerous if misused.

Etymology

Named after the city of Rome, where similar fireworks were popularized.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈkæn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈkæn.dl̩ / (BrE)
a type of firework that propels one or more exploding shells or colorful sparks into the air, typically in succession.
type of firework

Examples

During the celebration, he lit a Roman candle and cheered as it shot sparks high into the night.
For safety, always keep your distance when lighting a Roman candle.
The city’s fireworks show ended with dozens of Roman candles illuminating the sky.

Synset Examples

He lit a Roman-candle and watched it shoot colorful sparks into the sky.
The fireworks display featured a stunning Roman-candle effect.

Etymology

Named after Italy (Rome), where fireworks of this type were popularized; 'candle' refers to its candle-like shape.
