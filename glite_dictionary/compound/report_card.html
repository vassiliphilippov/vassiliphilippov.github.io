report card

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɔrt ˌkɑrd / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːt ˌkɑːd / (BrE)
A written document showing a student's academic performance and conduct, typically issued by a school at regular intervals.
school record of academic performance

Examples

At the end of each term, students receive a report card from their teachers.
Her report card reflected a marked improvement in mathematics and science this semester.
Parents are often required to sign and return their child's report card to the school.

Synset Examples

his father signed his report card

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grade report - written summary of a student's grades

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɔrt kɑrd / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːt kɑːd / (BrE)
an official document that shows a student's academic grades or performance over a specific period, typically issued by a school
record of a student's academic performance

Examples

Sara was delighted when her report card arrived with straight A's.
At the end of each term, every student receives a report card from the school.
Parents usually review their child's report card to discuss progress and set goals.

Synset Examples

Her report card showed a C in history this semester.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grade report - a statement listing the grades received by a student
