for show

1. X

/ fɔɹ ʃoʊ / (AmE), / fɔː ʃəʊ / (BrE)
intended to impress others but not serving a practical purpose; superficial display.
as a superficial display

Examples

His generosity was all for show and didn't extend to genuine help.
The company installed new lights for show, but the factory remained inefficient.
She bought an expensive vase for show, never intending to use it.

Synset Examples

He only wears suits for show, not for comfort.
The decorations were just for show, not functional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy - making a conspicuous display to attract attention
  • Synonym: for effect - done to create an impression, not sincerely or practically
  • Antonym: practical - intended for actual use rather than appearance
