Gambier Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡæmbiər ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈɡæmbɪə ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the south-central Pacific Ocean, forming part of French Polynesia
islands in French Polynesia, Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Gambier Islands are renowned for their unique culture and historical churches.
Researchers visited the Gambier Islands to study their diverse marine life.
Tourists often travel to the Gambier Islands seeking tranquil beaches and unspoiled scenery.

Etymology

Named after British Admiral James Gambier.
