sago fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪɡoʊ fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈseɪɡəʊ fɜːn / (BrE)
a showy tree fern of New Zealand and Australia, distinguished by a crown of pinnately divided fronds with whitish undersides
tree fern of New Zealand and Australia

Examples

The lush garden featured a magnificent sago fern as its centrepiece.
Botanists studied the unique characteristics of the sago fern native to Australia.
The sago fern thrives in well-drained soil with indirect sunlight.

Etymology

From 'sago', referring to its resemblance to the sago palm, combined with 'fern'.
