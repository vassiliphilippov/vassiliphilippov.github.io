judgment of dismissal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒmənt əv dɪsˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt əv dɪsˈmɪsəl / (BrE)
a court decision that disposes of a case without proceeding to trial
court ruling ending a case before trial
legal

Examples

The judge entered a judgment of dismissal after finding insufficient evidence to proceed.
Due to procedural errors, a judgment of dismissal was granted in favor of the defendant.
Plaintiff's attorney appealed the judgment of dismissal to a higher court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismissal - the act of removing a case from court consideration
