uranyl nitrate

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈreɪnɪl ˈnaɪtreɪt / (AmE), / jʊˈreɪnɪl ˈnaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a yellow crystalline salt, UO2(NO3)2, obtained by reacting uranyl compounds or uranium salts with nitric acid, used in nuclear processing and analytical chemistry
a yellow salt of uranium and nitric acid
medical

Examples

The laboratory stored several bottles of uranyl nitrate for use in analytical experiments.
Uranyl nitrate is highly soluble in water but must be handled with great care due to its radioactivity.
The production of yellowcake sometimes involves the intermediate step of forming uranyl nitrate crystals.

Etymology

From 'uranyl', referring to the uranyl ion (UO2^2+), and 'nitrate', referring to the NO3^- group; both from Latin roots for uranium and nitrum (sodium carbonate).
