Supreme Court of the United States

1. PROPN

/ səˈpriːm kɔrt əv ði juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts / (AmE), / sjuːˈpriːm kɔːt ɒv ði juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts / (BrE)
the highest federal court in the United States, with ultimate appellate authority and jurisdiction over all other U.S. courts
highest U.S. federal court

Examples

The Supreme Court of the United States ruled on the landmark case in 1954.
Decisions made by the Supreme Court of the United States are binding across the country.
The President nominated a new justice to the Supreme Court of the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Supreme Court - the highest court in a country
