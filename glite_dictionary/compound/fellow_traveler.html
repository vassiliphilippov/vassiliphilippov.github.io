fellow traveler

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ ˈtrævələr / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ ˈtrævələ / (BrE)
a person who travels together with another; a companion on a journey.
companion on a journey

Examples

On the long train ride, I enjoyed talking with my fellow traveler about our destinations.
Each fellow traveler brought a different perspective to the hiking group.
She quickly became friends with her fellow traveler during their tour of Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion - a person who accompanies someone, especially on a trip

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ ˈtrævəlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ ˈtrævələ / (BrE)
a person who sympathizes with the aims of a political group, especially Communists, without formally joining or being a member
person sympathetic to a political cause (esp. Communism)

Examples

During the Cold War, many artists were labeled as fellow travelers because of their leftist sympathies.
He was accused of being a fellow traveler although he never joined the party.
The senator warned against fellow travelers spreading subversive ideas in universities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathizer - a person who agrees with or supports a sentiment or ideology
