family Ambystomatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbɪstoʊˈmætɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌæmbɪstəʊˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of salamanders found in North America, commonly known as mole salamanders.
a family of North American salamanders

Examples

Scientists have discovered a new species within the family Ambystomatidae in northern Mexico.
Members of the family Ambystomatidae are often referred to as mole salamanders due to their burrowing habits.
The family Ambystomatidae includes several species that exhibit remarkable regenerative abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole salamander - common name for members of the Ambystomatidae family

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Ambystomatidae, based on the type genus Ambystoma, from Greek amblys (blunt) + stoma (mouth).
