macaroni philosopher

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækəˈroʊni fəˈlɑsəfər / (AmE), / ˌmækəˈrəʊni fəˈlɒsəfə / (BrE)
A person who indulges in theorizing or speculation without taking practical action, often regarded as impractical or unrealistic.
an impractical theorist; a dreamer

Examples

Don't be a macaroni philosopher; the world needs people who take real steps.
He was criticized as a macaroni philosopher whose ideas never left the drawing board.
Her friends called her a macaroni philosopher because she talked more than she did.

Synset Examples

Don't be a macaroni philosopher; take action instead.
He’s just a macaroni philosopher, dreaming instead of acting.
Being a macaroni philosopher won't pay the bills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreamer - A person who is impractical or unrealistic
  • Antonym: pragmatist - A person who is practical and focuses on action

Etymology

From 'macaroni', a term for a foppish person in 18th-century Britain, combined with 'philosopher'.
