alpine goldenrod

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn ˈɡoʊldənˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn ˈɡəʊldənˌrɒd / (BrE)
A species of goldenrod with bristly hairs on the edges of its leaf stalks, found in mountainous regions of western North America.
mountainous North American goldenrod plant

Examples

The alpine goldenrod thrives in the high-altitude meadows of the Rocky Mountains.
Botanists can identify alpine goldenrod by the bristly hairs on its leaf stalks.
Hikers often spot alpine goldenrod during late summer along mountain trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - a genus of chiefly North American plants with clusters of small yellow flowers
