hanging chad

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɪŋ tʃæd / (AmE), / ˈhæŋɪŋ tʃad / (BrE)
a small piece of paper on a ballot that is not fully detached after being punched, remaining attached by one corner
partially detached ballot piece

Examples

Several ballots were disqualified because they had a hanging chad that made the voter's intention unclear.
The controversy over the hanging chad changed the way many states design their voting systems.
He inspected his ballot carefully to make sure there was no hanging chad left after voting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chad - a small piece of waste paper produced when a hole is punched in paper

Etymology

From 'hang' + 'chad', the latter meaning a small scrap; popularized during the 2000 U.S. presidential election recount.
