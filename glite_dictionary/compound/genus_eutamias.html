genus Eutamias

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs juːˈteɪmi.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs juːˈteɪmɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of chipmunks found in western America and Asia.
genus of chipmunks in America and Asia

Examples

The genus Eutamias is distinguished by specific stripe patterns on its members' backs.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Eutamias in the mountains of western China.
Many chipmunks classified under the genus Eutamias inhabit rocky slopes and forested areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - A small ground squirrel with striped back, native to North America and Asia

Etymology

From New Latin 'Eutamias,' a genus name used in zoological classification; 'genus' from Latin meaning 'kind' or 'type.'
