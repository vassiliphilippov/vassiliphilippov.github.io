white chip

1. NOUN

/ waɪt tʃɪp / (AmE), / waɪt tʃɪp / (BrE)
A gambling token, usually a poker chip, colored white and representing a specific low monetary value.
poker chip of low value (white)

Examples

He placed a white chip on the table to call the small blind.
She cashed in all her white chips at the cashier's desk.
Most home poker sets use the white chip as the lowest denomination.

Synset Examples

She cashed in her white chip for a drink.
He won a white chip in the poker tournament.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: red chip - Poker chip of medium value, usually colored red
  • Degree Higher: blue chip - Poker chip of high value, usually colored blue
