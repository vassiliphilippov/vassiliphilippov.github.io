founders' share

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndərz ʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndəz ʃeə / (BrE)
a type of share in a company given to the founders, often carrying special privileges such as enhanced voting rights or larger dividends
special shares given to company founders

Examples

The company's founders' shares carry extra voting rights on decisions affecting company direction.
At the initial public offering, the co-founder retained significant control through his founders' shares.
The founders' share structure helped the original owners maintain influence after outside investment.
