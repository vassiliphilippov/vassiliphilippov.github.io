Japanese hop

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænəs hɑp / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs hɒp / (BrE)
a climbing ornamental vine native to eastern Asia, grown for its attractive, variegated leaves
ornamental Asian climbing vine

Examples

The Japanese hop is valued in gardens for its decorative foliage and fast growth.
Many gardeners plant Japanese hop along fences to create a living screen during summer.
Researchers are studying whether Japanese hop can become invasive in non-native environments.
