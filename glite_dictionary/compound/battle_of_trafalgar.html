Battle of Trafalgar

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv trəˈfælɡər / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv trəˈfælɡə / (BrE)
a major naval battle fought in 1805 off the southwest coast of Spain, in which British forces under Admiral Nelson defeated the combined French and Spanish fleets during the Napoleonic Wars
1805 naval battle; British victory over French and Spanish

Examples

The Battle of Trafalgar changed the course of European naval history.
Admiral Nelson became a national hero after his victory at the Battle of Trafalgar.
Many historians regard the Battle of Trafalgar as the Royal Navy’s greatest triumph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Waterloo - the 1815 battle in which Napoleon was defeated by the British and Prussians

Etymology

From Trafalgar, the cape off the coast of Spain near which the battle was fought; the name derives from Arabic 'Taraf al-Ghar' meaning 'Cape of the Cave'.
