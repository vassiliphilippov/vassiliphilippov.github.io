pill in the pocket

1. NOUN

/ pɪl ɪn ðə ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / pɪl ɪn ðə ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
a method of keeping a medication, such as for heart rhythm problems, readily available to take immediately when symptoms occur, rather than on a fixed schedule
readily accessible medication for urgent use
medical

Examples

Dr. Smith recommended a pill in the pocket approach for her occasional arrhythmia episodes.
Many patients with atrial fibrillation carry a pill in the pocket for sudden symptoms.
He travels with a pill in the pocket so he can manage his heart condition quickly if necessary.

Synset Examples

He always keeps a pill in the pocket for emergencies.
He made sure there was a pill in the pocket before leaving.
