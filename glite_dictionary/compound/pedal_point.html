pedal point

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a sustained note, usually in the bass, over which harmonies change, commonly used in music to create tension and resolution
sustained bass note in music

Examples

The composer used a pedal point in the bass to build suspense during the piece.
After several measures, the pedal point was finally resolved with a dramatic chord change.
A low pedal point held by the organ created a sense of underlying stability in the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drone - a note or tone sustained throughout a piece of music

Etymology

From 'pedal' (relating to a foot-operated mechanism, as on organs) and 'point' (note), referring to organ bass notes held by foot pedals.
