imaginary part

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmædʒɪˌnɛri pɑrt / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪnəri pɑːt / (BrE)
The component of a complex number that is a real number multiplied by the square root of minus one (i).
component of a complex number multiplied by i

Examples

The imaginary part of the number 5 + 3i is 3.
In electrical engineering, the imaginary part helps describe alternating current.
When adding complex numbers, you add their real parts and their imaginary parts separately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real part - the component of a complex number not multiplied by i

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmædʒɪˌnɛri pɑrt / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪnəri pɑːt / (BrE)
the component of a complex number that is a multiple of the square root of -1, usually denoted as the coefficient of i
mathematical part of a complex number

Examples

The imaginary part of 3 + 4i is 4.
When solving the equation, make sure to separate the real and imaginary part.
The imaginary part becomes zero when the complex number is purely real.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real part - the component of a complex number not involving i

Etymology

Formed from 'imaginary' and 'part', referring to the component of a number involving the imaginary unit i.
