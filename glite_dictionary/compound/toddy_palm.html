toddy palm

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑdi pɑlm / (AmE), / ˈtɒdi pɑːm / (BrE)
a tall palm tree native to South and Southeast Asia, whose sap is used to make toddy or jaggery, and whose pith is used for sago
Asian palm yielding sap for toddy and jaggery

Examples

The sap collected from the toddy palm is often fermented to make a popular local drink.
Farmers harvest the sweet juice from the toddy palm at dawn each morning.
Jaggery made from the toddy palm is a common ingredient in traditional desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmyra palm - another palm tree used to make toddy and jaggery

Etymology

From 'toddy' (a drink made from palm sap) + 'palm', indicating the tree's use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑdi ˌpɑm / (AmE), / ˈtɒdi ˌpɑːm / (BrE)
a tall palm tree native to Africa, India, and Southeast Asia, valued for its hard wood, sweet sap used to make palm wine and sugar, and leaves used for thatching and weaving
palm tree yielding sap for wine and sugar

Examples

The villagers climbed the toddy palm at dawn to collect fresh sap for the day's wine.
Leaves from the toddy palm are woven into mats and used for roofing in rural areas.
Sugar made from toddy palm sap is prized in many traditional Asian desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm wine - an alcoholic drink made from the fermented sap of palm trees

Etymology

From 'toddy', likely from Hindi 'ṭaṛī', meaning palm wine, and 'palm'.
