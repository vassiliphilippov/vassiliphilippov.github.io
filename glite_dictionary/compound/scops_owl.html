scops owl

1. NOUN

/ skɑps aʊl / (AmE), / skɒps aʊl / (BrE)
A small owl with prominent ear tufts and a distinctive whistling call, found in Eurasia and Africa.
small owl with ear tufts and a whistling call

Examples

We spotted a scops owl perched silently on a branch at dusk.
The call of the scops owl can be heard throughout the Mediterranean night.
Despite its small size, the scops owl is an efficient nocturnal hunter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - nocturnal bird of prey with a large head and eyes

Etymology

From modern Latin genus name 'Scops', itself from Greek 'skōps' meaning 'owl'.
