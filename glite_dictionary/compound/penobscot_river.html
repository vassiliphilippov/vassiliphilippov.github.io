Penobscot River

1. PROPN

/ pəˈnɑbˌskɑt ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / pəˈnɒbˌskɒt ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in central Maine, U.S., flowing southeast into Penobscot Bay on the Atlantic Ocean
river in central Maine, U.S.

Examples

The Penobscot River supports a diverse ecosystem, including Atlantic salmon and bald eagles.
Several towns in Maine are located along the Penobscot River.
The Penobscot River was once vital to the region's lumber industry.

Etymology

Named after the Penobscot people, an Indigenous group native to the region.
