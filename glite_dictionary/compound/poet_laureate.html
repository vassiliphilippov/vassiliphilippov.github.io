poet laureate

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊət lɔˈreɪət / (AmE), / ˌpəʊɪt lɒˈrɪət / (BrE)
A poet who is regarded as holding an honorary position, often for a group, region, or nation, whether officially appointed or not.
honorary poet for a group or region

Examples

Maya Angelou was often called the poet laureate of the African American community.
He became the unofficial poet laureate of his neighborhood, reciting verses at every local event.
In their hearts, she will always be the poet laureate of our small town.

Synset Examples

she is the poet laureate of all lyricists
he is the poet laureate of Arkansas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laureate - a person who is honored with an award for outstanding creative or intellectual achievement

Etymology

From Latin 'laureatus' (crowned with laurel), laurel wreaths being a symbol of honor since ancient times.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊət lɔˈrɪət / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɪt lɒˈrɪət / (BrE)
the official poet appointed by the monarch or government, especially in Great Britain, to compose poems for special occasions and national events
officially appointed national poet
british

Examples

She was named poet laureate of the United Kingdom in 2009.
The poet laureate wrote a moving ode for the Queen's birthday.
As poet laureate, he was expected to celebrate national milestones through his poetry.

Synset Examples

the poet laureate is expected to provide poems for great national occasions

Etymology

From Latin 'laureatus' (crowned with laurel), reflecting ancient practice of honoring poets with laurel wreaths.
