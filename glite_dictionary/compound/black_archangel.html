black archangel

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈɑːrtʃˌeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / blæk ˈɑːtʃˌeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
An ill-smelling European herb with wrinkled leaves and whorls of dark purple flowers, known for its strong scent.
European herb with dark purple flowers

Examples

We found a patch of black archangel growing in the shade near the old stone wall.
The leaves of the black archangel have a distinctive wrinkled texture and an unpleasant smell.
Gardeners sometimes avoid planting black archangel because of its strong scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamium galeobdolon - Botanical name for black archangel
