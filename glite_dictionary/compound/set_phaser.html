to set phaser

1. VERB

/ sɛt ˈfeɪzər / (AmE), / sɛt ˈfeɪzə / (BrE)
to adjust a phaser weapon to a specific setting in preparation for use, especially in science fiction contexts
adjust a phaser weapon for use

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to set phasers to stun before boarding the ship.
During the emergency drill, she remembered to set phasers to a low power setting.
He quickly set phaser to maximum just as the enemy ship appeared on the viewscreen.

Synset Examples

In the simulation, we had to set phasers to evade the enemy.
Before the battle, they set phasers to maximum power.
The captain ordered the crew to set phasers to stun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arm - to prepare or ready a weapon for use

Etymology

From the science fiction series Star Trek; 'phaser' derives from 'phased energy rectification.'
