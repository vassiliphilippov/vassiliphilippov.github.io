social ostracism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.ʃəl ˈɑstrəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ʃəl ˈɒstrəˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the exclusion of an individual or group from social interactions or society, typically as a form of punishment or rejection
exclusion from social interactions

Examples

After revealing her secret, she suffered years of social ostracism from her community.
Some students fear social ostracism if they do not conform to group norms.
The threat of social ostracism can be a powerful tool for enforcing social rules.

Synset Examples

Her refusal to join us led to social ostracism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostracism - the act of excluding someone from a society or group

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ostrakismos (exile by popular vote), via ostracism, combined with social.
