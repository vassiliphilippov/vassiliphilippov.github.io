Fast of Gedaliah

1. NOUN

/ fæst əv ɡəˈdɑːliə / (AmE), / fɑːst əv ɡɛˈdeɪlɪə / (BrE)
(Judaism) A minor Jewish fast observed on Tishri 3, commemorating the assassination of Gedaliah, the governor of Judah.
Jewish fast day commemorating Gedaliah's assassination

Examples

Many observant Jews mark the Fast of Gedaliah by abstaining from food and drink from dawn until dusk.
The Fast of Gedaliah is one of several minor fasts in the Jewish calendar.
On the Fast of Gedaliah, special prayers are recited to remember the tragedy that befell Judah.
