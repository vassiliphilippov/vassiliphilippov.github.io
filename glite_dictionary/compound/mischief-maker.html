mischief-maker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstʃɪf ˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmɪstʃɪf ˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately causes trouble or discord, often in a playful or disruptive way
someone who stirs up trouble

Examples

The teacher warned the class not to listen to the mischief-maker sitting at the back of the room.
Even as a child, he was known to be a bit of a mischief-maker, always playing pranks on his friends.
Every office has at least one mischief-maker who stirs up rumors just for fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troublemaker - a person who habitually causes difficulty or problems
