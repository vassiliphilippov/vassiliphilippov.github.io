ecce homo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.eɪ ˈhoʊ.moʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛk.eɪ ˈhəʊ.məʊ / (BrE)
a painting or sculpture representing Jesus Christ wearing a crown of thorns, typically depicting the moment he was presented to the crowd before the Crucifixion.
artwork of Jesus crowned with thorns

Examples

The museum's collection features a rare ecce homo painted in the seventeenth century.
She wrote her thesis on the symbolism in ecce homo sculptures across Europe.
The damaged ecce homo fresco in Spain became famous after an attempted restoration.

Etymology

Latin for 'behold the man', reportedly spoken by Pontius Pilate when presenting Jesus to the crowd.
