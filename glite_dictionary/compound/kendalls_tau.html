Kendall's tau

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛndəlz taʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɛnd(ə)lz taʊ / (BrE)
a nonparametric statistical measure used to assess the degree of agreement or association between two rankings
statistical measure of ranking agreement

Examples

The researcher chose Kendall's tau to analyze the relationship between the two judges' scores.
Because the data were ordinal, Kendall's tau was preferred over Pearson's correlation coefficient.
In the paper, they reported a high Kendall's tau value, indicating strong agreement between rankings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spearman's rank correlation - another nonparametric measure of correlation between rankings

Etymology

Named after British statistician Maurice G. Kendall, who introduced the method in 1938.
