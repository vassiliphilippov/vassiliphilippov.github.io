Port Jackson pine

1. NOUN

/ pɔrt ˈdʒæksən paɪn / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈdʒæksən paɪn / (BrE)
an Australian evergreen tree (Callitris rhomboidea) of the cypress pine family, characterized by small, globular cones
Australian cypress pine tree

Examples

In the dry forests of eastern Australia, the Port Jackson pine is a common sight.
The wood from the Port Jackson pine is valued for its durability in construction.
Botanists studied the globular cones produced by the Port Jackson pine during their fieldwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress pine - a coniferous tree belonging to the genus Callitris

Etymology

Named after Port Jackson, the natural harbour of Sydney, Australia.
