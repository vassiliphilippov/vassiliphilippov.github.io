pitch and toss

1. NOUN

/ pɪtʃ ənd tɔs / (AmE), / pɪtʃ ənd tɒs / (BrE)
a game of chance in which players throw coins toward a mark or target, with the closest winning; often played casually for small stakes
game throwing coins at a mark

Examples

On summer evenings, the boys would play pitch and toss near the old railway tracks.
He lost all his spare coins in a heated game of pitch and toss.
Some local festivals feature traditional pitch and toss competitions for children.

Synset Examples

It's just a simple game of pitch and toss for fun.
We played pitch and toss with coins at the park.
