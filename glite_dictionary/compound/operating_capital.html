operating capital

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑpəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈɒpəreɪtɪŋ ˈkæpɪtəl / (BrE)
The money or assets a business uses in its day-to-day operations, separate from funds for financial transactions or long-term investments.
funds used for daily business operations

Examples

The company raised additional operating capital to keep its factories running efficiently during the slow season.
Insufficient operating capital can lead to cash flow problems for small businesses.
She managed the firm's operating capital carefully to ensure daily expenses were always covered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working capital - funds available to cover a company's short-term liabilities and operations
