May apple

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ ˌæpl / (BrE)
The edible but bland fruit of the mayapple plant, often yellow when ripe.
edible fruit of mayapple plant

Examples

The children searched the woods for ripe May apples during their hike.
While May apples are safe to eat when fully ripe, the unripe fruit and other parts of the plant are toxic.
Jelly made from May apple has a mild, slightly tart flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayapple - North American plant bearing a single white flower and edible yellow fruit

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ ˌæpəl / (BrE)
A North American herb (Podophyllum peltatum) with umbrella-like leaves, a poisonous root, and edible but bland yellow fruit.
a North American plant with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The May apple blooms in shaded woodlands each spring, producing its distinctive white flowers.
Although the fruit of the May apple is edible, the rest of the plant is highly toxic.
We found several May apples growing beside the hiking trail, their leaves forming green umbrellas.
