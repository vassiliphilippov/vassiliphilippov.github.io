sublingual gland

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈlɪŋɡwəl ɡlænd / (AmE), / sʌbˈlɪŋɡwəl ɡlænd / (BrE)
a small salivary gland located beneath the tongue on either side of the mouth, producing mucin, the viscous component of saliva
salivary gland under the tongue
medical

Examples

The dentist checked her sublingual gland for any signs of infection.
Swelling of the sublingual gland can cause discomfort under the tongue.
Saliva produced by the sublingual gland helps keep the mouth moist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salivary gland - any of several glands that secrete saliva into the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'lingua' (tongue).
