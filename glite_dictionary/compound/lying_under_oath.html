lying under oath

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪɪŋ ˌʌndɚ oʊθ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪɪŋ ˌʌndə əʊθ / (BrE)
the criminal offense of deliberately making false statements while sworn to tell the truth, especially in a court of law
criminal offense of lying while sworn to tell the truth
legal

Examples

The witness was found guilty of lying under oath during the trial.
Lying under oath can result in severe legal penalties, including imprisonment.
He lost his job because he was caught lying under oath in a civil case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perjury - the offense of willfully telling an untruth in a court after having taken an oath or affirmation
