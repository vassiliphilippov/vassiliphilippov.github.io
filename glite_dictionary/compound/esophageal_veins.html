esophageal veins

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌsɑfəˈdʒiəl veɪnz / (AmE), / ɪˌsɒfəˈdʒiːəl veɪnz / (BrE)
the small veins that drain blood from the esophagus into the brachiocephalic vein or the azygos veins
veins draining blood from the esophagus
medical

Examples

The esophageal veins can become enlarged in patients with liver disease.
Surgeons carefully avoided damaging the esophageal veins during the procedure.
Rupture of the esophageal veins may lead to severe bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esophagus - the muscular tube connecting the throat with the stomach
  • Synonym: vein - blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart

Etymology

From 'esophageal' (relating to the esophagus), from Greek *oisophagos* (esophagus), and 'vein', from Latin *vena* (vein).
