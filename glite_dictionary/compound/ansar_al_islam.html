Ansar al Islam

1. PROPN

/ ænˈsɑr æl ɪsˈlɑm / (AmE), / ænˈsɑː æl ɪsˈlɑːm / (BrE)
a militant Islamist group once active in the Kurdish region of Iraq, opposed to secular government and associated with terrorist activities
militant Islamist group in Iraqi Kurdistan

Examples

Ansar al Islam claimed responsibility for the attack, intensifying concerns in the region.
Several leaders of Ansar al Islam were reportedly linked to other extremist groups in the Middle East.
The international community continues to monitor Ansar al Islam due to its history of violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: al-Qaeda - global militant Islamist organization
  • Synonym: Taliban - Islamic fundamentalist group in Afghanistan and Pakistan

Etymology

From Arabic 'Anṣār al-Islām' meaning 'supporters of Islam'.
