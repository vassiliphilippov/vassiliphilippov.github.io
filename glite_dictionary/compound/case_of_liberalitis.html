case of liberalitis

1. NOUN

/ keɪs əv lɪbərˈaɪtɪs / (AmE), / keɪs əv lɪbərəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
a humorous or critical term for an exaggerated or misguided concern for liberal ideas or policies
exaggerated concern for liberal ideas (humorous/critical)
informal

Examples

Some commentators accused the mayor of having a case of liberalitis when he proposed the new regulations.
Whenever social issues come up, he seems to develop a sudden case of liberalitis.
Critics dismissed the reforms as nothing more than a classic case of liberalitis.

Synset Examples

Many dismissed his views as just a case of liberalitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalism - political philosophy based on liberty and equality

Etymology

Modeled partly on medical terminology, combining 'liberal' with the mock-disease suffix '-itis'.
