net estate

1. NOUN

/ nɛt ɪˈsteɪt / (AmE), / nɛt ɪˈsteɪt / (BrE)
the value of a person's estate remaining after deducting all debts, funeral, and administrative expenses from the total assets, used for distribution and taxation purposes
estate value after deductions
legal

Examples

After all debts and expenses were settled, the net estate was divided among the heirs.
Federal and state taxes are calculated based on the net estate rather than the gross estate.
The lawyer explained that only the net estate would be distributed to the beneficiaries.
