hand glass

1. NOUN

/ hænd ɡlæs / (AmE), / hænd ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a small mirror with a handle, designed to be held in the hand for personal use
small mirror with a handle

Examples

She checked her makeup in a hand glass before entering the meeting.
The antique hand glass had an ornate silver handle.
He handed her a hand glass so she could see the back of her hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mirror - a reflective surface, typically of glass, for viewing oneself

2. NOUN

/ hænd ɡlæs / (AmE), / hænd ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A small magnifying lens with a handle, used to produce an enlarged image of an object.
a handheld magnifying lens

Examples

She used a hand glass to examine the tiny print on the label.
The jeweler inspected the gemstone closely through his hand glass.
With a hand glass, even the smallest details of the painting became visible.

Synset Examples

the magnifying glass was invented by Roger Bacon in 1250
