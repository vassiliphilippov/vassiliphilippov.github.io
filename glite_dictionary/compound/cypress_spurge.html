cypress spurge

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprəs spɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪprəs spɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the spurge family with narrow leaves resembling those of a cypress, native to Europe and often considered a weed in North America
a perennial plant resembling cypress; a weed

Examples

Cypress spurge can quickly spread throughout lawns and gardens, making it difficult to control.
The narrow, needle-like leaves of cypress spurge easily distinguish it from other weeds.
Many gardeners try to remove cypress spurge because it can be toxic to pets if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euphorbia - the genus of flowering plants to which cypress spurge belongs
