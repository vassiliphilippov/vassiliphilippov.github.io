virgin forest

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪn ˈfɔrɪst / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn ˈfɒrɪst / (BrE)
A forest or woodland in a mature state, largely untouched and uninfluenced by human activity.
untouched, mature natural woodland

Examples

Scientists are studying the biodiversity of the virgin forest deep within the Amazon.
A virgin forest offers a glimpse of nature before widespread human settlement.
Logging companies are not permitted to harvest timber from a virgin forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old-growth forest - a forest that has attained great age without significant disturbance
