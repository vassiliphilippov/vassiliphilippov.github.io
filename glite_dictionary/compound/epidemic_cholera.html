epidemic cholera

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈdɛmɪk ˈkoʊlɚə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk ˈkɒlərə / (BrE)
A severe, acute intestinal infection caused by consuming water or food contaminated with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae, typically resulting in outbreaks affecting many people.
acute intestinal infection causing outbreaks
medical

Examples

The spread of epidemic cholera in the 19th century led to major public health reforms.
Access to clean water can significantly reduce cases of epidemic cholera in affected regions.
During the outbreak, hundreds fell ill with epidemic cholera after drinking contaminated water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholera - an infectious disease causing severe watery diarrhea

Etymology

From Greek 'epidemia' (prevalent among people) + Neo-Latin 'cholera' (bile, illness characterized by vomiting and diarrhea).
