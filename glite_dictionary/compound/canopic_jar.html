canopic jar

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnoʊpɪk dʒɑr / (AmE), / kəˈnɒpɪk dʒɑː / (BrE)
A lidded jar used in ancient Egypt to hold the internal organs of a mummified body during the embalming process.
ancient Egyptian jar for mummified organs

Examples

The archaeologist carefully lifted the canopic jar from the burial chamber.
Each canopic jar was dedicated to protecting a different organ of the deceased.
Museum visitors were fascinated by the beautifully decorated canopic jar on display.

Etymology

From Canopus, a city in ancient Egypt, where such jars were first discovered, plus 'jar'.
