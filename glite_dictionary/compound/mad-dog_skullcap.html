mad-dog skullcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæd dɔɡ ˈskʌlˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈmad dɒɡ ˈskʌl.kæp / (BrE)
a North American herb (Scutellaria lateriflora) of the mint family that was formerly used as a remedy for epilepsy and other nervous disorders
North American medicinal herb

Examples

The herbalist recommended mad-dog skullcap as a traditional remedy for anxiety.
In the 19th century, mad-dog skullcap was widely used to treat nervous disorders.
Researchers are studying the chemical compounds found in mad-dog skullcap for potential medicinal benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skullcap - a plant of the genus Scutellaria, especially as a medicinal herb

Etymology

The name refers to the plant's historical use in treating 'mad dog' (rabid dog) bites and its helmet-shaped flowers ('skullcap').
