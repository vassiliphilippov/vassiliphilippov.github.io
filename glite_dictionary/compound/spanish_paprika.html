Spanish paprika

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ pəˈpriːkə / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ pəˈpriːkə / (BrE)
a mild, sweet or smoky seasoning powder made from Spanish varieties of pimiento peppers, used in cooking
seasoning made from Spanish pimiento peppers

Examples

Traditional paella is often seasoned with Spanish paprika to give it a rich, smoky flavor.
The chef sprinkled some Spanish paprika over the roasted vegetables for extra color and taste.
Many recipes call for Spanish paprika instead of the regular kind because of its distinct mildness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paprika - a powder made from ground, dried pimiento peppers
