letters patent

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtərz ˈpætənt / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəz ˈpatənt / (BrE)
An official written and sealed document from a monarch or government granting a right, title, monopoly, or privilege to an individual or organization.
official document granting a right or privilege
legal

Examples

The inventor was awarded letters patent for his innovative machine.
Ownership of the land was confirmed through the king's letters patent.
Without valid letters patent, no one can claim the exclusive right to manufacture that product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patent - exclusive right granted for an invention

Etymology

From Medieval Latin litterae patentes, meaning 'open letters', referring to documents meant to be accessible to the public.
