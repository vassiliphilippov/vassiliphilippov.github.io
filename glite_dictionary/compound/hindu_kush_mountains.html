Hindu Kush Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɪndu ˈkʊʃ ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˌhɪnduː ˈkʊʃ ˈmaʊntənz / (BrE)
a mountain range in Central and South Asia, extending west of the Himalayas, mainly across Afghanistan and Pakistan
mountain range west of the Himalayas

Examples

Many explorers have attempted to cross the Hindu Kush Mountains despite their treacherous terrain.
Snowfall in the Hindu Kush Mountains often blocks remote villages for months each year.
The Hindu Kush Mountains are known for their rich history and striking natural beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Himalayas - the highest mountain range bordering the Hindu Kush to the east

Etymology

From Persian ‘Hindu Kūh’ meaning ‘Hindu Mountains’.
