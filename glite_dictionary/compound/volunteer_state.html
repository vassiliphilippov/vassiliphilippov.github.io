Volunteer State

1. PROPN

/ ˌvɑːlənˈtɪr steɪt / (AmE), / ˌvɒlənˈtɪə steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the U.S. state of Tennessee, especially in reference to its residents' reputation for volunteering for military service
nickname for Tennessee, US state

Examples

She moved to the Volunteer State to begin her studies at Vanderbilt University.
The Volunteer State is famous for its country music and barbecue traditions.
Many tourists visit the Volunteer State each year to explore its natural parks and historic sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tennessee - a state in the southeastern United States

Etymology

Refers to Tennessee's tradition of volunteer soldiers, especially noted during the War of 1812.
