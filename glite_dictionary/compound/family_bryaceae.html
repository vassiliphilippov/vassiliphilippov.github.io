family Bryaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli braɪˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli braɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of acrocarpous mosses, including many common moss species found in temperate regions
a family of acrocarpous mosses

Examples

The family Bryaceae includes several widely distributed species of moss.
Many members of the family Bryaceae thrive in moist, shaded environments.
Researchers are studying the reproductive habits of mosses in the family Bryaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin Bryaceae, based on the type genus Bryum and Latin -aceae (family ending).
