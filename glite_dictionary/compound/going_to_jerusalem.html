going to Jerusalem

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ tə dʒəˈruːsələm / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ tə dʒəˈruːsələm / (BrE)
a children's game in which players walk around a circle of chairs while music plays and must find a seat when the music stops; each round, one chair is removed and one player is eliminated until only one remains
children's game like musical chairs

Examples

At the birthday party, the children played going to Jerusalem and laughed as they rushed for seats.
The teacher set up a row of chairs for a round of going to Jerusalem in the gym.
Every time the music stopped in going to Jerusalem, someone was left standing and had to cheer for the others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musical chairs - a children's game where players scramble for seats when music stops
