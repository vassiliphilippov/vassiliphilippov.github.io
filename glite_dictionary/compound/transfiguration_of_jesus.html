Transfiguration of Jesus

1. NOUN

/ trænsˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃən əv ˈdʒiːzəs / (AmE), / trænsˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃən əv ˈdʒiːzəs / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the event in which Jesus was visibly transformed and shone with radiant light on a mountain, witnessed by three of his disciples.
Christian event: Jesus's radiant transformation

Examples

The Transfiguration of Jesus is described as a moment of divine revelation to Peter, James, and John.
Many artists throughout history have painted scenes depicting the Transfiguration of Jesus on Mount Tabor.
In Christian theology, the Transfiguration of Jesus represents the manifestation of his divine glory.
