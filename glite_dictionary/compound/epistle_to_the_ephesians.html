Epistle to the Ephesians

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl tə ði ɪˈfiːʒənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tə ðiː ɪˈfiːzɪənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament, an epistle attributed to Saint Paul addressed to the Christians in Ephesus, discussing the divine plan and unity in Christ
a New Testament book (Paul's letter to the Ephesians)

Examples

The Epistle to the Ephesians emphasizes unity and reconciliation in Christ.
Many scholars have debated the authorship of the Epistle to the Ephesians.
Our study group read passages from the Epistle to the Ephesians each week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epistle to the Galatians - another New Testament letter attributed to Saint Paul
  • Synonym: Epistle to the Romans - New Testament epistle written by Paul to the Romans

Etymology

Epistle: from Latin 'epistola', from Greek 'epistolē' (letter); Ephesians: from Ephesus, an ancient city in Asia Minor.
