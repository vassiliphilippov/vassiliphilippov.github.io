shy bladder

1. NOUN

/ ʃaɪ ˈblædɚ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ ˈblædə / (BrE)
difficulty or inability to urinate when other people are present, especially in public restrooms
difficulty urinating in public places
informal

Examples

Her shy bladder made travel stressful because she avoided public restrooms.
Many people with a shy bladder find it easier to use a single-stall bathroom.
He admitted that his shy bladder prevented him from participating in certain social events.

Synset Examples

Her shy bladder made it hard to use crowded bathrooms.
He often struggled with a shy bladder in public restrooms.
He joked about his shy bladder during the camping trip.
