clam chowder

1. NOUN

/ klæm ˈtʃaʊdɚ / (AmE), / klæm ˈtʃaʊdə / (BrE)
a thick soup made with clams, potatoes, onions, and usually milk or tomatoes
a soup made with clams and vegetables

Examples

He ordered a bowl of clam chowder to start his meal.
The restaurant is famous for its creamy New England clam chowder.
On cold days, she loves making homemade clam chowder for her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chowder - a thick soup, usually made with milk or cream and vegetables

Etymology

From 'clam', a type of shellfish, and 'chowder', from French 'chaudière' (cauldron).
