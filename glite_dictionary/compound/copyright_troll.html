copyright troll

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpiˌraɪt troʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɒpiraɪt trəʊl / (BrE)
a person or organization that aggressively enforces copyright claims, typically for financial gain and often without producing original content themselves
person or group exploiting copyright claims for profit

Examples

The copyright troll filed dozens of lawsuits against independent artists.
Many companies have been targeted by a copyright troll demanding settlements.
Startups often worry about receiving letters from a copyright troll.

Synset Examples

Many small businesses fear copyright trolls targeting their content.
She warned her friends about the risks of copyright trolls.
The copyright troll sent us a threatening letter about our website.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patent troll - a person or company that enforces patent rights to collect licensing fees, often aggressively

Etymology

From 'copyright' (legal right over creation) + 'troll', slang for someone who provokes or exploits online.
