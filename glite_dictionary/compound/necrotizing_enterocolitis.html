necrotizing enterocolitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛk.rəˌtaɪ.zɪŋ ˌɛn.tə.roʊ.kəˈlaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛk.rəˌtɪ.zɪŋ ˌɛn.tə.rəʊ.kəˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A serious inflammatory disease of the intestines, primarily affecting premature infants, which can result in the death of intestinal tissue.
acute intestinal disease in premature infants
medical

Examples

The doctors quickly recognized signs of necrotizing enterocolitis in the premature newborn.
Early diagnosis of necrotizing enterocolitis can greatly improve the chances of recovery.
Many hospitals have protocols for managing infants at risk of necrotizing enterocolitis.

Etymology

From Greek 'nekros' (dead body) + 'entero' (intestine) + 'colitis' (inflammation of the colon).
