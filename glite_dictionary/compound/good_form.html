good form

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd fɔrm / (AmE), / ɡʊd fɔːm / (BrE)
behavior that is considered proper or acceptable according to social conventions at a particular time
proper or acceptable social behavior

Examples

Apologizing sincerely after making a mistake is considered good form in most cultures.
It was not good form for him to check his phone during the dinner.
She always makes sure to write thank-you notes, thinking it's simply good form.

Synset Examples

it is not good form to brag about winning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bad form - behavior that is considered improper or unacceptable by social conventions
