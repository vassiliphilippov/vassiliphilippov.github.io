beggars' velvet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡərz ˈvɛlvɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəz ˈvɛlvɪt / (BrE)
a type of fabric, typically cheap or threadbare, associated with poverty or a shabby appearance
cheap or shabby fabric linked to poverty

Examples

The artist's latest piece used beggars' velvet to convey themes of hardship and resilience.
After years of wear, the chair was covered with beggars' velvet instead of fine upholstery.
Victorian writers sometimes described tenements filled with the drapes of beggars' velvet.
