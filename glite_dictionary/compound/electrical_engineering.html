electrical engineering

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl ˌɛn(d)ʒɪˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl ˌɛn(d)ʒɪˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism, including power generation, distribution, and the design of electrical equipment
branch of engineering using electricity

Examples

She decided to study electrical engineering at one of the top universities.
Advancements in electrical engineering have transformed the way we use and generate power.
Many companies seek candidates with a background in electrical engineering for their research and development teams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electronics - the branch of physics and technology concerned with the design of circuits using transistors and microchips

Etymology

From 'electric' + 'engineering', both from Latin and French roots.
