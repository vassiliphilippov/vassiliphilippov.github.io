brother-in-law

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðər ɪn lɔ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðə ɪn lɔː / (BrE)
the brother of one's spouse or the husband of one's sibling
male relative by marriage

Examples

After the wedding, James became my brother-in-law and quickly fit into the family.
Her brother-in-law always brings delicious desserts to family gatherings.
I asked my brother-in-law to help me fix the kitchen sink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sister-in-law - female relative by marriage

Etymology

Derived from 'brother' + 'in law' indicating relationship established through marriage rather than blood.
