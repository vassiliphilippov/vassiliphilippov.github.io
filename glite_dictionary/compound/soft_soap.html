soft soap

1. NOUN

/ sɔft soʊp / (AmE), / sɒft səʊp / (BrE)
a type of soft or liquid soap made from vegetable oils, sometimes used to treat certain skin conditions
soft or liquid soap from vegetable oils

Examples

The doctor recommended using soft soap to cleanse sensitive skin areas.
Patients with eczema may benefit from washing with soft soap instead of harsher cleansers.
Soft soap is often preferred in dermatological practice due to its gentle formulation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard soap - a soap made with sodium hydroxide, typically solid

2. NOUN

/ sɔft soʊp / (AmE), / sɒft səʊp / (BrE)
exaggerated praise or insincere flattery used to persuade or gain favor; persuasive talk intended to influence someone
insincere flattery to gain favor

Examples

He tried to win their approval with plenty of soft soap but failed to convince them.
Don't fall for her soft soap; she's only being nice because she wants something.
The politician's speech was filled with soft soap, but his promises rang hollow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive and insincere praise given to further one's own interests
  • Synonym: blarney - flattering or ingratiating talk; deceptive or misleading praise
  • Antonym: straight talk - honest and direct speech, without flattery

Etymology

Originally referred to actual soft soap, then figuratively to smooth, slippery talk (19th c.).
