sour gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊɚ ˌɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə ˌɡʌm / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of eastern North America (Nyssa sylvatica), with columnar shape, horizontal limbs, small late-emerging leaves, and vivid fall color.
deciduous tree (Nyssa sylvatica) of eastern North America

Examples

The sour gum is prized for its brilliant red foliage in autumn.
Birds feast on the small blue berries produced by the sour gum tree.
We planted a sour gum in our backyard to add fall color to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black gum - another common name for Nyssa sylvatica
  • Synonym: tupelo - common name for several trees in the Nyssa genus
