proprietary drug

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpraɪəˌtɛri drʌɡ / (AmE), / prəˈprʌɪət(ə)ri drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that is marketed under a trademarked brand name and is protected by a patent, meaning only the company holding the patent can produce and sell it.
branded, patented drug
medical

Examples

The pharmaceutical company developed a new proprietary drug that is effective against malaria.
Once the patent expires, other manufacturers can produce generic versions of a proprietary drug.
Doctors may prescribe either a proprietary drug or a generic equivalent depending on availability and cost.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generic drug - a drug that is equivalent to a proprietary drug but is not protected by a patent or sold under a brand name

Etymology

From 'proprietary' (from Latin proprietarius, 'owner') + 'drug'.
