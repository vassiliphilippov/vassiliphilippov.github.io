strawberry bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈstrɒˌbəri bʊʃ / (BrE)
A hardy shrub native to the southeastern United States, with clove-scented wood and fragrant red-brown flowers.
shrub with fragrant wood and flowers

Examples

The strawberry bush thrives in well-drained, shaded areas of southern woodlands.
Gardeners often plant strawberry bushes for their attractive foliage and unique flowers.
The scent of the strawberry bush's wood is reminiscent of cloves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈstrɒˌbəri bʊʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its green leaves and crimson, pod-like fruits containing seeds.
North American shrub with crimson pods

Examples

The strawberry bush in my backyard produces bright red pods each autumn.
Many gardeners plant strawberry bushes for their ornamental value and colorful fruit.
Deer are often attracted to the leaves of the strawberry bush.
