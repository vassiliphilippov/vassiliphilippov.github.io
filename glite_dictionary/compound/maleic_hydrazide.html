maleic hydrazide

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪɪk haɪˌdræzaɪd / (AmE), / məˈliːɪk hɪˌdræzaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound used as a herbicide and plant growth regulator
a herbicide and plant growth regulator
medical

Examples

The use of maleic hydrazide is strictly regulated to ensure food safety.
Researchers found that maleic hydrazide effectively controls unwanted sprouting in potatoes.
Residual maleic hydrazide in crops must be monitored to protect consumers' health.

Synset Examples

Maleic hydrazide's residue in food can cause liver damage.

Etymology

From 'maleic' (relating to maleic acid, from Latin 'malum' meaning apple) and 'hydrazide' (from 'hydrazine').
