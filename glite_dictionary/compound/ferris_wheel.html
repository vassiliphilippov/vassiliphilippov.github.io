Ferris wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrɪs wil / (AmE), / ˈfɛrɪs wiːl / (BrE)
a large vertical revolving wheel with passenger cars suspended on its outer edge, typically found at amusement parks or fairs
vertical rotating ride at amusement parks

Examples

The city fair isn’t complete without a Ferris wheel lighting up the night sky.
They waited in line for almost an hour to ride the Ferris wheel together.
From the top of the Ferris wheel, you could see the entire amusement park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observation wheel - large Ferris wheel built mainly for viewing landscapes/cities

Etymology

Named after George W. Ferris, who designed the original for the 1893 World's Fair in Chicago.
