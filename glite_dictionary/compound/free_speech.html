free speech

1. NOUN

/ fri spiːtʃ / (AmE), / friː spiːtʃ / (BrE)
the right to express one's opinions publicly without government interference or censorship
the right to express opinions freely

Examples

The university is committed to protecting free speech on campus.
Debates about free speech often arise during times of political unrest.
He argued that free speech is essential in a democratic society.

Synset Examples

Many believe regressive leftism stifles free speech.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of speech or writing
  • Synonym: freedom of expression - the broader right to express opinions and ideas
