Rheims-Douay Bible

1. PROPN

/ rimz duˈeɪ ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / riːmz ˈduːeɪ ˈbaɪbəl / (BrE)
an English translation of the Vulgate made by Roman Catholic scholars in the late 16th and early 17th centuries
English Catholic translation of the Vulgate

Examples

The Rheims-Douay Bible remains an important text for English-speaking Catholics.
Many of the phrases from the Rheims-Douay Bible are still quoted in religious literature.
She compared passages from the Rheims-Douay Bible to those in other translations.

Etymology

Named after the French cities of Rheims and Douay, where the translation was produced.
