tin-pot idea

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪn pɑt aɪˈdiə / (AmE), / ˈtɪn pɒt aɪˈdɪə / (BrE)
an idea or plan that is trivial, insignificant, or unimportant; lacking in value or seriousness
trivial or insignificant idea

Examples

She dismissed his tin-pot idea without a second thought.
The committee ignored yet another tin-pot idea from the intern.
No one paid attention to his tin-pot idea during the brainstorming session.

Synset Examples

His tin-pot ideas were quickly dismissed in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial idea - an idea of little importance or seriousness
  • Antonym: grand idea - an idea that is impressive or ambitious

Etymology

From 'tin-pot', originally meaning something cheap and unimportant, likening to an object made of tin, used figuratively for triviality.
