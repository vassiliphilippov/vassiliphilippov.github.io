rum-blossom

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌm ˌblɑsəm / (AmE), / ˈrʌm ˌblɒsəm / (BrE)
an enlargement and reddening of the nose with visible blood vessels, often associated with excessive alcohol consumption
nose disfigurement from alcohol use
archaic

Examples

His nose had developed a noticeable rum-blossom after years of drinking.
The old sailor was known for his red rum-blossom and cheerful songs.
Medical texts once attributed rum-blossom to excessive consumption of spirits.

Etymology

From 'rum' (a type of alcoholic drink) and 'blossom,' referring to the redness resembling a flower.
