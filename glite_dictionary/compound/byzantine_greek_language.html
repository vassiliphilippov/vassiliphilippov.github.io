Byzantine Greek language

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈzæntaɪn ɡrik ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈzæntaɪn ɡriːk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the form of the Greek language used from about 600 to 1200 AD during the Byzantine Empire.
Greek language form, c. 600–1200 AD

Examples

Scholars studied texts written in Byzantine Greek language to understand the culture of the empire.
The Byzantine Greek language evolved from Ancient Greek and influenced the development of Modern Greek.
Many historical documents from the Byzantine Empire are preserved in Byzantine Greek language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Medieval Greek - Greek language form c. AD 600–1453
  • Degree Lower: Ancient Greek - Greek language used before the Byzantine period
  • Degree Higher: Modern Greek - the current form of the Greek language

Etymology

Byzantine from Byzantium, the ancient name for Constantinople (now Istanbul).
