Peruvian mastic tree

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruː.vi.ən ˈmæs.tɪk triː / (AmE), / pəˈruː.vi.ən ˈmæs.tɪk triː / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to Peru, having a broad rounded crown, slender drooping branches, clusters of greenish flowers, and rose-pink fruits
a small evergreen tree native to Peru

Examples

The Peruvian mastic tree thrives in warm, subtropical climates and is admired for its ornamental value.
Clusters of rose-pink fruit hang from the branches of the Peruvian mastic tree each autumn.
Gardeners often plant the Peruvian mastic tree for its attractive foliage and flowers.
