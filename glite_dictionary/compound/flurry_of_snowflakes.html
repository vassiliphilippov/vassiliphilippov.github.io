flurry of snowflakes

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɝi əv ˈsnoʊˌfleɪks / (AmE), / ˈflʌri əv ˈsnəʊfleɪks / (BrE)
a brief, light fall of snow, often occurring suddenly
brief, light snowfall

Examples

The children ran outside to play in the flurry of snowflakes that began suddenly.
During our walk, a flurry of snowflakes dusted the trees and rooftops.
A gentle flurry of snowflakes made the city look magical for a few minutes.

Synset Examples

A flurry of snowflakes covered the ground quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow flurry - a brief or sudden snowfall
