spaghetti squash

1. NOUN

/ spəˈɡɛti skwɑʃ / (AmE), / spəˈɡɛti skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A medium-sized, oval squash whose flesh separates into long, spaghetti-like strands when cooked, often used as a substitute for pasta.
type of squash with stringy flesh

Examples

You can bake a spaghetti squash and use the strands as a low-carb alternative to pasta.
She roasted the spaghetti squash and topped it with tomato sauce and cheese.
Have you ever tried making spaghetti squash with garlic and olive oil?

2. NOUN

/ spəˈɡɛti skwɑʃ / (AmE), / spəˈɡɛti skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a type of squash with oval, yellowish fruit whose cooked flesh separates into strands resembling spaghetti
a squash with flesh like spaghetti strands

Examples

She prepared a delicious meal using roasted spaghetti squash instead of pasta.
The flesh of spaghetti squash can be shredded into noodle-like strands after cooking.
Many people enjoy spaghetti squash as a low-carb alternative to traditional spaghetti.
