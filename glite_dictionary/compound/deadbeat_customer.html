deadbeat customer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌbit ˈkʌstəmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɛdbiːt ˈkʌstəmə / (BrE)
a customer who fails to pay their debts or is consistently unreliable in making payments
customer who fails to pay or is unreliable

Examples

The shop owner is careful to avoid doing business with deadbeat customers.
After several warnings, we had to cut off the account of a deadbeat customer.
Companies lose money every year because of deadbeat customers who fail to settle their bills.

Synset Examples

I can't stand dealing with deadbeat customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadbeat - a person who avoids paying debts

Etymology

Formed from 'deadbeat' (slang for someone who avoids paying debts) + 'customer'.
