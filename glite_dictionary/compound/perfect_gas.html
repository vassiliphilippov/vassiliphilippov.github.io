perfect gas

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrfɪkt ɡæs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪkt ɡas / (BrE)
an idealized gas whose molecules are considered to have negligible size and no intermolecular forces
idealized gas with no intermolecular forces

Examples

In theory, a perfect gas follows the gas laws under all temperature and pressure conditions.
The concept of a perfect gas is useful for understanding thermodynamic processes.
Real gases behave like a perfect gas only at low pressures and high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideal gas - a gas that exactly obeys the ideal gas law under all conditions
