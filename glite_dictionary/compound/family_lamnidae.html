family Lamnidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli læmˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli læmˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of oceanic sharks, known as mackerel sharks, including species such as the great white shark and mako sharks.
family of mackerel sharks

Examples

The family Lamnidae includes the powerful great white shark, known for its size and predatory skills.
Marine biologists study the behavior of different species within the family Lamnidae.
Mako sharks, part of the family Lamnidae, are among the fastest swimming sharks in the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel shark - a common name for members of the family Lamnidae

Etymology

From New Latin Lamnidae, based on Greek lamna (a kind of shark).
