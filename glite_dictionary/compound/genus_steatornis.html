genus Steatornis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs stiːəˈtɔːrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs stiːəˈtɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds representing the only genus in the family Steatornithidae, known for the oilbird species.
genus of oilbirds; only genus in Steatornithidae

Examples

The genus Steatornis includes only the oilbird, a rare nocturnal species of South America.
Researchers have studied the unique echolocation abilities found within the genus Steatornis.
Conservationists are concerned about habitat loss affecting birds in the genus Steatornis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilbird - A nocturnal bird of South America, only species in genus Steatornis

Etymology

From Latin 'steatornis' (fat bird), used in scientific taxonomy.
