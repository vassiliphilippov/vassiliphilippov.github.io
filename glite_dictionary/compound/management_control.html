management control

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪdʒmənt kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒmənt kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
internal processes or procedures used by managers to direct, monitor, and evaluate an organization's activities and performance
internal controls performed by managers

Examples

Effective management control ensures that the company's objectives are met efficiently and ethically.
The lack of management control contributed to the company's financial mismanagement.
Implementing new management controls helped reduce operational risks in the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal control - a process designed to ensure operational effectiveness and efficiency
