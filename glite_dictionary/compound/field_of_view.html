field of view

1. NOUN

/ fiːld əv vjuː / (AmE), / fiːld əv vjuː / (BrE)
the extent of the observable area that can be seen at any given moment, especially through an optical instrument
extent of visible area seen at once

Examples

The camera's field of view is wide enough to capture the entire room.
Adjusting the telescope will change your field of view and reveal more stars.
Pilots rely on a clear field of view for safe navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field - the area visible through an optical instrument; part of field of view
