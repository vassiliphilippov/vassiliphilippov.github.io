hemerobiid fly

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛm.əˈroʊ.bi.ɪd flaɪ / (AmE), / ˌhiː.məˈrəʊ.bɪ.ɪd flaɪ / (BrE)
A small, dark-colored lacewing fly belonging to the family Hemerobiidae, known for its delicate wings and predatory larvae.
a type of small dark-colored lacewing fly

Examples

The hemerobiid fly is often found in gardens where it preys on aphids.
A hemerobiid fly can be recognized by its brown, delicate wings and soft body.
Scientists study the hemerobiid fly for its beneficial role in controlling pest insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacewing - insect with delicate netted wings, family Chrysopidae or Hemerobiidae
  • Degree Lower: green lacewing - a common type of lacewing, family Chrysopidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Hemerobiidae, the family name, which comes from Greek hēmerobios (living for a day).
