to seek closure

1. VERB

/ siːk ˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / siːk ˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
to try to achieve a sense of emotional resolution, especially after a painful or troubling experience
try to find emotional resolution

Examples

After the breakup, Maria tried to seek closure by writing a letter to her ex-partner.
Many victims attend court hearings to seek closure after a crime has been committed against them.
He traveled back to his hometown in order to seek closure for events from his childhood.

Synset Examples

He sought closure by confronting his past mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find closure - to achieve emotional resolution
  • Synonym: to move on - to progress emotionally after a difficult experience
