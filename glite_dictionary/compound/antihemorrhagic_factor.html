antihemorrhagic factor

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.hɪˈmɔːr.ə.dʒɪk ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.hɪˈmɒr.ə.dʒɪk ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
a fat-soluble vitamin that is essential for the clotting of blood; also known as vitamin K
vitamin aiding blood clotting
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a supplement containing antihemorrhagic factor to help improve his blood clotting ability.
Certain leafy vegetables are rich in antihemorrhagic factor, making them important for patients with bleeding disorders.
A deficiency in antihemorrhagic factor can lead to serious problems with bleeding and wound healing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin K - a vitamin essential for blood clotting

Etymology

From 'anti-' meaning 'against', 'hemorrhagic' meaning 'causing bleeding', and 'factor' signifying a substance or component.
