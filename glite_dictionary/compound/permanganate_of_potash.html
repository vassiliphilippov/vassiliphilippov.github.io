permanganate of potash

1. NOUN

/ pərˈmæŋ.ɡəˌneɪt əv ˈpoʊ.tæʃ / (AmE), / pəˈmæŋ.ɡə.neɪt əv ˈpɒ.tæʃ / (BrE)
a poisonous chemical salt, also known as potassium permanganate, that forms dark purple crystals and is used as an oxidizing agent, bleach, disinfectant, and antiseptic
a chemical salt used as an oxidizing agent and disinfectant

Examples

Laboratory technicians added permanganate of potash to the solution to initiate the oxidation reaction.
In the past, permanganate of potash was commonly used to disinfect wounds and purify water.
The vivid color of permanganate of potash makes it easily recognizable in chemical demonstrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium permanganate - the chemical name for permanganate of potash

Etymology

'Potash' refers to potassium compounds; 'permanganate' denotes the ion MnO₄⁻; the name reflects its potassium and manganese content.
