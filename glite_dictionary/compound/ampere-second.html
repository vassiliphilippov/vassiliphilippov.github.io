ampere-second

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmˌpɪr ˈsekənd / (AmE), / ˈæmpɪə ˈsekənd / (BrE)
A unit of electric charge equal to the charge transferred by a current of one ampere in one second, equivalent to one coulomb.
unit of electric charge; coulomb

Examples

The battery can deliver 500 ampere-seconds of charge before needing to be recharged.
One ampere-second is equivalent to one coulomb of electric charge.
The experiment measured the flow of electricity in ampere-seconds to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coulomb - the SI unit of electric charge, equivalent to one ampere-second

Etymology

From ampere (named after André-Marie Ampère) + second (time unit).
