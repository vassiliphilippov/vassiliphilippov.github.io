enlisted person

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈlɪstɪd ˈpɜrsən / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɪstɪd ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a member of the armed forces who holds a rank below that of a commissioned officer
non-officer member of military

Examples

Every enlisted person receives basic training before taking on further responsibilities.
The benefits offered to an enlisted person differ from those of officers.
She decided to become an enlisted person after finishing high school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: officer - a member of the armed forces holding a position of authority, especially a commissioned officer

Etymology

From 'enlist' (to enroll, especially in the armed forces) + 'person'.
