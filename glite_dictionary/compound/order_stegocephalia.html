order Stegocephalia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrdər ˌstiːɡoʊsəˈfeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌstiːɡəʊsəˈfiːlɪə / (BrE)
in former classifications, a division of class Amphibia comprising all pre-Jurassic and some later extinct large salamandriform amphibians.
extinct group of primitive amphibians

Examples

Fossils of order Stegocephalia provide insight into the early evolution of amphibians.
In the nineteenth century, paleontologists frequently classified prehistoric amphibians under order Stegocephalia.
The bony skulls characteristic of order Stegocephalia helped protect these ancient creatures from predators.

Etymology

From Greek stego- (roof) + kephalē (head), referring to the bony plates on the skull.
