herpes labialis

1. NOUN

/ hɚˌpiz læˈbaɪ.əlɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɜː.piːz læˈbeɪ.li.əs / (BrE)
A common viral infection of the lips and mouth characterized by painful blisters, caused by herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1).
cold sores on the lips or mouth
medical

Examples

She frequently develops herpes labialis during periods of stress.
Antiviral creams can help reduce the severity of herpes labialis outbreaks.
Doctors recommend avoiding direct contact to prevent the spread of herpes labialis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold sore - a small, painful blister on the lip caused by the herpes simplex virus

Etymology

From Latin 'herpes' (creeping) and 'labialis' (of the lips).
