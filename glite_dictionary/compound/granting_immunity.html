granting immunity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræntɪŋ ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːntɪŋ ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
the official act of exempting someone, especially from prosecution or legal action
official act of exempting someone (esp. legally)
legal

Examples

The court's granting immunity to the witness ensured her testimony would be candid.
The investigation depended on the granting immunity to key participants.
His lawyer negotiated a granting immunity deal with the authorities.

Synset Examples

he was granted immunity from prosecution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunity - protection or exemption from something, especially an obligation or penalty
  • Antonym: prosecution - the institution and conducting of legal proceedings against someone
