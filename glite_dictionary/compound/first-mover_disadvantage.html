first-mover disadvantage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˌmuːvɚ dɪsədˈvæntɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌmuːvə dɪsədˈvɑːntɪdʒ / (BrE)
the disadvantages or risks experienced by a company or individual who is the first to enter a particular market, such as unproven demand, high costs, or paving the way for competitors
drawbacks faced by being first in a market

Examples

Entering the market early exposed the company to a first-mover disadvantage as competitors quickly improved on its ideas.
Despite being innovative, the startup suffered a first-mover disadvantage due to high initial development costs.
The team debated whether the first-mover disadvantage would affect their product launch strategy.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, the first-mover disadvantage outweighs the advantages.
The first-mover disadvantage can hit new companies hard.
They faced a first-mover disadvantage in the tech market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first-mover advantage - the benefits gained by being the first to enter a market
