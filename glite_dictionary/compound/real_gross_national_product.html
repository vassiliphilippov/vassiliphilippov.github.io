real gross national product

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəl ɡroʊs ˌnæʃənəl ˈprɑːdʌkt / (AmE), / ˌrɪəl ɡrəʊs ˌnæʃn(ə)l ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
Gross national product that has been adjusted for the effects of inflation, reflecting the value of goods and services produced in constant prices.
GNP adjusted for inflation

Examples

Economists use real gross national product to compare economic output across different years.
The real gross national product rose by 2.5% despite rising inflation.
Tracking real gross national product gives a clearer picture of a country's economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gross national product - the total value of goods and services produced by a country in one year
