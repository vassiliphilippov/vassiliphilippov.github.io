slippery slope

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪp.ə.ri sloʊp / (AmE), / ˈslɪp.əri sləʊp / (BrE)
a situation or course of action likely to lead to increasingly negative or undesirable consequences
situation leading to worsening consequences

Examples

Many argue that allowing one exception creates a slippery slope that erodes the original rule.
Introducing censorship could set us on a slippery slope toward restricting free speech.
The lawyer warned that accepting this claim would lead down a slippery slope.

Synset Examples

This decision could start a slippery slope of problems.
Be careful; that choice is a slippery slope.
Ignoring small issues can lead to a slippery slope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downward spiral - a process of continuously worsening circumstances
