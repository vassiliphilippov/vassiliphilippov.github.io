scarlet tanager

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːrlət təˈnændʒər / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt ˈtænɪdʒə / (BrE)
A North American songbird, the male of which is bright red with black wings and tail, belonging to the cardinal family.
American songbird; red male with black wings and tail

Examples

The scarlet tanager is often spotted in deciduous forests during spring and summer.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to see a male scarlet tanager with its vivid red plumage.
Unlike the female, the male scarlet tanager is easily recognized by its bright coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanager - A type of colorful songbird found in the Americas

Etymology

From the color 'scarlet' + 'tanager', from Spanish 'tángara', via Tupi (an indigenous Brazilian language).
