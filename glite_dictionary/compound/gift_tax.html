gift tax

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪft tæks / (AmE), / ɡɪft tæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed on the transfer of property from one person to another without compensation, typically during the giver's lifetime
tax on gifts of property or money
legal

Examples

If you exceed the annual exclusion, you may owe gift tax on the excess amount.
The couple consulted a financial advisor to minimize their potential gift tax liability.
Certain charitable donations are exempt from gift tax under the law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inheritance tax - tax levied on property received from someone who has died
