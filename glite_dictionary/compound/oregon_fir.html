Oregon fir

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪɡən fɪr / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən fɜː / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to northwestern North America, known for its short needles and egg-shaped cones; another name for the Douglas fir.
tall fir tree of northwestern North America

Examples

The Oregon fir is an important source of timber in the Pacific Northwest.
Many old growth forests in Oregon are dominated by towering Oregon fir trees.
The needles of the Oregon fir are short and flat, making them easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - a tall evergreen conifer native to western North America
