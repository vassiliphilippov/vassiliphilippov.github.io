family Pectinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pɛkˈtɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pɛkˈtɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks commonly known as scallops, characterized by their fan-shaped shells and ability to swim by clapping their shells together.
the biological family of scallops

Examples

The family Pectinidae includes a wide variety of scallop species found throughout the world's oceans.
Researchers studied the unique swimming abilities of bivalves in the family Pectinidae.
Seafood enthusiasts often refer to the edible shellfish in the family Pectinidae simply as 'scallops'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - a marine bivalve mollusk with a fan-shaped shell

Etymology

From Latin 'pecten' meaning 'comb', referring to the shape of the shells; 'idae' is a standard suffix for zoological families.
