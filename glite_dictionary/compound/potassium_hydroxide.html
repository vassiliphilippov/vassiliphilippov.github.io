potassium hydroxide

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtæsiəm haɪˈdrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / pəˈtæsiəm haɪˈdrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white caustic solid potassium compound (KOH) used in industry and agriculture, especially as a strong alkali and cleaning agent
a strong alkali compound (KOH)

Examples

The laboratory stored potassium hydroxide in a tightly sealed container to prevent moisture absorption.
Many industrial cleaners contain potassium hydroxide due to its effectiveness at breaking down organic matter.
Farmers sometimes use potassium hydroxide to adjust the pH level of their soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium hydroxide - a strong alkali compound (NaOH) similar to potassium hydroxide

Etymology

potassium from Modern Latin 'potassium' (from 'potash'), hydroxide from 'hydro-' (water) and 'oxide'
