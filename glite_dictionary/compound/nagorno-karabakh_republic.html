Nagorno-Karabakh Republic

1. PROPN

/ nəˌɡɔrnoʊ kəˈrɑbɑk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / nəˌɡɔːnəʊ kəˈrɑːbæk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
an unrecognized de-facto state in the South Caucasus, self-declared in the territory of Nagorno-Karabakh.
unrecognized state in South Caucasus

Examples

The Nagorno-Karabakh Republic declared independence in 1991, though it is not recognized by most countries.
Peace negotiations regarding the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic have involved Armenia, Azerbaijan, and international mediators.
The capital of the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic is Stepanakert, which serves as its administrative center.

Etymology

Nagorno-Karabakh from Russian (Nagorno, 'mountainous') and Turkic-Persian (Karabakh, 'black garden'); 'Republic' from Latin 'res publica'.
