Javel water

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒævəl ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒævəl ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
a solution of sodium hypochlorite used as a disinfectant or bleaching agent
sodium hypochlorite solution

Examples

Laboratory technicians prepared a diluted solution of Javel water for the disinfection process.
The janitor recommended using Javel water to clean stubborn stains on the tiles.
Handle Javel water with care, as it can be harmful to your skin and eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical used to whiten or disinfect

Etymology

From French 'eau de Javel' (water from Javel, a district in Paris where it was first produced).
