Quaker gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪkɚ ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈkweɪkə ɡʌn / (BrE)
A dummy cannon or piece of artillery, often made of wood, used to deceive an enemy into overestimating the presence of real weapons.
dummy cannon or artillery piece

Examples

During the siege, the defenders positioned a Quaker gun on the wall to fool the enemy scouts.
Civil War armies sometimes relied on Quaker guns when they lacked enough real artillery.
The old log painted black served as a Quaker gun to deter any would-be attackers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dummy - something made to look real but is not genuine
  • Synonym: decoy - something used to mislead or lure

Etymology

From 'Quaker' (member of the Religious Society of Friends, known for pacifism) + 'gun'; refers to pacifist deception in warfare.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪkər ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈkweɪkə ɡʌn / (BrE)
A fake cannon or non-functional weapon used to deceive or bluff an enemy.
fake cannon or weapon used as a bluff

Examples

During the battle, the defenders set up a Quaker gun to make their fort look more heavily armed.
The enemy scouts were fooled by the Quaker gun placed on the hillside.
Museum visitors were surprised to learn that the old cannon on display was actually a Quaker gun.

Synset Examples

They built a Quaker gun to bluff their enemies during the siege.
The old cannon was just a Quaker gun, not functional at all.
The soldiers realized the threat was a mere Quaker gun.

Etymology

From 'Quaker' (member of the Religious Society of Friends, known for pacifism) + 'gun', referring to a weapon that is harmless, as Quakers are stereotypically non-violent.
