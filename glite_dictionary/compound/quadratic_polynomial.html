quadratic polynomial

1. NOUN

/ kwɑˈdrætɪk pəˈlɪn.iˌoʊ.mi.əl / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrætɪk pəˈlɪn.iˌəʊ.mi.əl / (BrE)
a polynomial of the second degree, typically in the form ax² + bx + c, where a, b, and c are constants and a ≠ 0
polynomial of degree two

Examples

The equation x² + 5x + 6 is a classic example of a quadratic polynomial.
To find the roots of a quadratic polynomial, you can use the quadratic formula.
Many natural phenomena can be modeled using a quadratic polynomial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadratic - an algebraic expression or equation of the second degree
  • Degree Higher: cubic polynomial - a polynomial of the third degree
  • Degree Lower: linear polynomial - a polynomial of the first degree

Etymology

From Latin quadratus (squared) and Greek polynomos (having many terms).
