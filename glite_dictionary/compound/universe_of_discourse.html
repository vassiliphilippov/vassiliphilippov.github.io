universe of discourse

1. NOUN

/ ˈjunɪˌvɝs əv dɪsˈkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪˌvɜːs əv dɪsˈkɔːs / (BrE)
the set of all entities under consideration in a particular discussion, theory, or logical analysis
set of things considered in a discussion

Examples

In mathematical logic, the universe of discourse defines which elements are relevant for a given problem.
Each theory may have a unique universe of discourse determined by its assumptions and scope.
When interpreting a statement, it's critical to clarify the universe of discourse involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - set of possible values or elements involved in a particular function or discussion

Etymology

From 'universe' (entirety, whole) + 'of discourse' (subject of discussion); used in logic and mathematics since the 19th century.
