Sinhalese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪn(h)əˈliːz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌsɪn(h)əˈliːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the main language of Sri Lanka, belonging to the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European language family, spoken by the Sinhalese people.
the main language of Sri Lanka

Examples

The constitution of Sri Lanka recognizes both Sinhalese language and Tamil as official languages.
Students at the university can choose to study the Sinhalese language as part of their curriculum.
She is fluent in both English and the Sinhalese language, which she learned from her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sinhala - alternative name for the Sinhalese language

Etymology

Sinhalese from Sanskrit 'Siṃhala' (Sri Lanka) + English 'language'.
