genus Coccus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Coccidae, a group of scale insects.
type genus of Coccidae (scale insects)

Examples

The genus Coccus includes several species of scale insects harmful to plants.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Coccus in South America.
Effective pest control targets members of the genus Coccus to protect crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coccidae - a family of scale insects

Etymology

From Latin 'coccus', meaning 'berry' (referring to the shape of the insect).
