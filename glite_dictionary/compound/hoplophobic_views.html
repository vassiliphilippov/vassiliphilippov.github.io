hoplophobic views

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑpləˈfoʊbɪk vjuːz / (AmE), / ˌhɒpləˈfəʊbɪk vjuːz / (BrE)
Strong fear or aversion to firearms reflected in a person's beliefs or attitudes.
fearful or anti-gun attitudes

Examples

The politician's hoplophobic views were evident in her proposed stricter firearm regulations.
Some commentators accused the media of promoting hoplophobic views during the debate.
His hoplophobic views often put him at odds with gun rights advocates.

Synset Examples

Her hoplophobic views made her very vocal at the rally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gun control - advocacy of restrictions on firearms

Etymology

From 'hoplophobia', coined in the 20th century from Greek 'hoplon' (weapon) + '-phobia' (fear).
