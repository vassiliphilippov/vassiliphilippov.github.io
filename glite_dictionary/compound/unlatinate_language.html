unlatinate language

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈlætɪnət ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʌnˈlatɪnət ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A style of language that deliberately avoids words of Latin origin in favor of simpler, more native or Germanic ones.
language avoiding Latin-derived words

Examples

In his writing, he chose to use unlatinate language to make the text accessible to all readers.
Teachers sometimes prefer unlatinate language when explaining complex topics to young students.
The document was praised for its clear and direct unlatinate language.

Synset Examples

Using unlatinate language helped the audience understand better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain English - clear, simple English avoiding jargon or complexity

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'Latinate' (from Latin) + 'language'.
