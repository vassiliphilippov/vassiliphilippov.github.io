suborder Heteroptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌɔrdər ˌhɛtəˈrɑptərə / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɔːdə ˌhɛtəˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
a large suborder of insects within the order Hemiptera, comprising the true bugs, characterized by forewings that are hardened at the base and membranous at the tips
suborder of insects; true bugs

Examples

Many agricultural pests belong to the suborder Heteroptera due to their piercing and sucking mouthparts.
Representatives of the suborder Heteroptera can be found in diverse aquatic and terrestrial habitats.
The water strider is a well-known insect within the suborder Heteroptera.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hemiptera - order of insects including true bugs, aphids, and cicadas
  • Synonym: bug - informal term for a member of Heteroptera, an insect with sucking mouthparts

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + pteron (wing), referring to their distinctive forewings.
