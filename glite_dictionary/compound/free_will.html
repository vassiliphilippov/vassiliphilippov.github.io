free will

1. NOUN

/ fri wɪl / (AmE), / friː wɪl / (BrE)
the ability to make choices independently, not determined by external forces or fate
ability to choose independently

Examples

Many philosophers have debated whether humans truly possess free will or if our actions are predetermined.
Religious traditions often discuss the concept of free will in relation to moral responsibility.
He believes that free will is essential for a person to be held accountable for their choices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: determinism - the doctrine that all events, including human action, are ultimately determined by causes external to the will
