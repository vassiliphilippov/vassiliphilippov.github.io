red-backed mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˌmaʊs / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˌmaʊs / (BrE)
a small rodent of mountainous regions in Eurasia and America, usually referring to several species of red-backed voles
a vole of mountainous areas, Eurasia/America

Examples

Scientists discovered a new population of red-backed mice living in the alpine meadows.
The red-backed mouse is adapted to survive cold mountain environments.
Some predators rely heavily on the red-backed mouse as a food source in Eurasian forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-backed vole - a small rodent of the genus Clethrionomys or Myodes
