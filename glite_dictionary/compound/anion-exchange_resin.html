anion-exchange resin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.aɪ.ɑn ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈrɛz.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌæn.aɪ.ɒn ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈrɛz.ɪn / (BrE)
A synthetic resin that contains basic functional groups and exchanges anions (negatively charged ions) with anions in a surrounding solution, commonly used in water purification and chemical separation processes.
resin that exchanges anions with a solution

Examples

The laboratory uses an anion-exchange resin to remove unwanted impurities from the water supply.
A strongly basic anion-exchange resin can efficiently adsorb chloride and nitrate ions.
Replacing the anion-exchange resin ensures the purification system remains effective.

Synset Examples

An anion-exchange resin may be either strongly or weakly basic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cation-exchange resin - a resin that exchanges cations (positively charged ions) with those in solution

Etymology

From 'anion' (negatively charged ion) + 'exchange resin' (synthetic polymer used for ion exchange).
