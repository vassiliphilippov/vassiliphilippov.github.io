genus Arctostaphylos

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrktəˈstæfɪˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˌɑːktəˈstæfɪˌlɒs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family, commonly known as manzanita or bearberry, native to western North America.
a genus of shrubs including manzanita and bearberry

Examples

The genus Arctostaphylos includes many species known for their distinctive red bark and evergreen leaves.
Botanists are currently studying the distribution of the genus Arctostaphylos in California's chaparral regions.
Some garden enthusiasts plant members of the genus Arctostaphylos as ornamentals due to their attractive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manzanita - common name for several species of Arctostaphylos
  • Synonym: bearberry - common name for certain species of Arctostaphylos and related genera

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + staphyle (grape), referring to the fruits eaten by bears.
