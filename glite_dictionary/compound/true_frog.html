true frog

1. NOUN

/ tru frɔg / (AmE), / truː frɒg / (BrE)
A frog of the family Ranidae, typically insectivorous, semiaquatic, with smooth moist skin, long hind legs, and webbed feet.
frog of the family Ranidae

Examples

The common European true frog is often found in ponds and streams.
Unlike toads, true frogs have smooth, moist skin and long jumping legs.
Many species of true frog are excellent swimmers due to their webbed feet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: frog - a tailless amphibian with a short squat body, moist skin, and very long hind legs
  • Antonym: toad - amphibian similar to a frog, but typically with drier skin and more terrestrial habits
