ankylosing spondylitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋ.kəˌloʊ.sɪŋ ˌspɑn.dəˈlaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌæŋ.kɪˌləʊ.sɪŋ ˌspɒn.dɪˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A chronic inflammatory disease that primarily affects the spine and can lead to the fusion of vertebrae, resulting in reduced flexibility and mobility.
chronic inflammatory spinal disease
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with ankylosing spondylitis after months of persistent back pain and stiffness.
Physical therapy can help people living with ankylosing spondylitis maintain mobility in their spine.
She learned that ankylosing spondylitis often affects young men but can occur in women as well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spondylitis - inflammation of the vertebrae

Etymology

From Greek ankylos (crooked) and spondylos (vertebra), with -itis denoting inflammation.
