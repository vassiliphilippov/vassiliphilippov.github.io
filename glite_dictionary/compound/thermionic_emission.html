thermionic emission

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜrmaɪˈɑnɪk ɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːmɪˈɒnɪk ɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the release of electrons from a heated material, especially a metal
release of electrons from heated material

Examples

The efficiency of vacuum tubes depends on thermionic emission from their cathodes.
Researchers measured the rate of thermionic emission as the filament temperature increased.
Certain metals show significant thermionic emission when heated to high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photoelectric effect - the emission of electrons when light shines on a material

Etymology

From 'therm-' (relating to heat) + 'ion' + '-ic'; 'emission' from Latin 'emissio' (sending out).
