surety bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊrəti bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈʃʊərɪti bɒnd / (BrE)
a bond provided by a third party guaranteeing to pay the recipient for any loss if the terms of a contract are not fulfilled
contract guarantee by third party
legal

Examples

The contractor was required to provide a surety bond before starting work on the project.
Without a surety bond, the client refused to sign the construction agreement.
Many public works contracts mandate a surety bond to protect against nonperformance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance bond - a bond guaranteeing completion of work or supply of goods

Etymology

From 'surety' (Middle English, from Old French 'seurté') + 'bond' (Middle English).
