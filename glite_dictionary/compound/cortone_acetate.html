Cortone Acetate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrˌtoʊn ˈæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəʊn ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a corticosteroid hormone used as an anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressant, formerly marketed under this trade name
a corticosteroid drug, trade name
medical

Examples

After the diagnosis, the doctor started her on Cortone Acetate to reduce inflammation.
Some patients with adrenal insufficiency once received Cortone Acetate as part of their therapy.
Over time, Cortone Acetate was replaced by more effective corticosteroids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cortisone - a corticosteroid hormone closely related to Cortone Acetate
  • Degree Higher: hydrocortisone - a corticosteroid hormone to which cortisone is converted in the body

Etymology

Cortone is a trade name for cortisone; acetate denotes it is in acetate (salt) form.
