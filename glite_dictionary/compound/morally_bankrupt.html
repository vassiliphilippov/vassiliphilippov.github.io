morally bankrupt

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrəli ˈbæŋkrʌpt / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəli ˈbæŋkrʌpt / (BrE)
completely lacking in moral principles or ethical standards
lacking moral principles

Examples

The lawyer was known for his morally bankrupt behavior, ignoring justice in pursuit of profit.
Many felt the regime was morally bankrupt, caring little for human rights or fairness.
Her actions during the crisis were so morally bankrupt that even her colleagues were shocked.

Synset Examples

His morally bankrupt decisions cost him everything.
She called the politician morally bankrupt for his lies.
The company was deemed morally bankrupt after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - willing to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain
  • Antonym: upright - behaving in a morally correct way
