four-act play

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ˈækt pleɪ / (AmE), / fɔː ˈækt pleɪ / (BrE)
A play that is divided into four distinct sections or acts, often used in early twentieth-century theatre.
a play in four acts

Examples

The director chose to stage a classic four-act play for the theatre festival.
Many of Chekhov's works are structured as a four-act play with a clear progression of action.
Her thesis analyzed the dramatic tension in a four-act play compared to a three-act format.

Synset Examples

The technical feat which Ibsen here achieves of carrying through without a single break the whole action of a four-act play has been much commented on and admired.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three-act play - a play divided into three acts
  • Degree Higher: five-act play - a play divided into five acts
