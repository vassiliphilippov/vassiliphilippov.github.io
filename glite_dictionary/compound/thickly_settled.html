thickly settled

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɪkli ˈsɛtld̩ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkli ˈsɛtld / (BrE)
(of an area) having a large number of people living close together; densely populated.
densely populated area

Examples

The eastern part of the city is thickly settled and always bustling with activity.
Their house is located just outside the thickly settled downtown district.
Many villages in that region became thickly settled during the industrial revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: densely populated - having many people living in a small area
  • Antonym: sparsely populated - having few people living in a large area
