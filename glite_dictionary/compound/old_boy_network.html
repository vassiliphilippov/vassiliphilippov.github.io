old boy network

1. NOUN

/ oʊld bɔɪ ˈnɛt.wɝk / (AmE), / əʊld bɔɪ ˈnɛt.wɜːk / (BrE)
an informal system in which men with similar backgrounds, especially from elite schools, use their social connections to help each other professionally, often excluding outsiders
informal professional support network based on social ties

Examples

Breaking into the old boy network can be challenging for those without established connections.
Many critics argue that the old boy network perpetuates inequality in the workplace.
The company was accused of relying on an old boy network when filling top positions.

Synset Examples

professional women have developed an old boy network of their own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people
  • Antonym: meritocracy - a social system based on ability and achievement rather than background

Etymology

Originating in the early 20th century, from the term 'old boy' for a former pupil of a British public school.

2. NOUN

/ oʊld bɔɪ ˈnɛtwɝk / (AmE), / əʊld bɔɪ ˈnɛtwɜːk / (BrE)
an informal system in which men who attended the same school or belong to the same social group use their influence to help each other, especially in business or politics
system of favoritism among connected men

Examples

Many senior positions in the firm are filled through the old boy network rather than open competition.
He complained that the old boy network made it impossible for outsiders to get ahead.
Despite efforts to promote equality, the old boy network still holds considerable power in the industry.

Synset Examples

The old boy network still influences hiring decisions.
He got the job through the old boy network.
Critics argue the old boy network favors the same faces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cronyism - the practice of favoring friends or associates, especially in political appointments
  • Synonym: nepotism - favoring relatives, especially by giving them jobs

Etymology

From British public school slang 'old boy' meaning a former pupil, combined with 'network.'
