pancreatic fibrosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.kriˈæt.ɪk faɪˈbroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌpaŋ.krɪˈæt.ɪk faɪˈbrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterised by the development of fibrous tissue in the pancreas, often resulting in impaired pancreatic function.
fibrous tissue formation in the pancreas
medical

Examples

Chronic pancreatic fibrosis can lead to digestive problems over time.
Researchers are studying genetic factors that increase the risk of pancreatic fibrosis.
A biopsy confirmed the patient was suffering from pancreatic fibrosis.

Etymology

From Greek 'pancreas' (all flesh) and Latin 'fibrosis' (pertaining to fibers).
