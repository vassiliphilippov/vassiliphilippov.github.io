genus Jatropha

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs dʒəˈtroʊfə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs dʒəˈtrəʊfə / (BrE)
A genus of mainly tropical American plants in the family Euphorbiaceae, including several species important for medicinal uses and biofuel production.
tropical plant genus in Euphorbiaceae

Examples

The genus Jatropha includes plants that thrive in tropical climates and are valued for their medicinal properties.
Research into the oil-producing seeds of the genus Jatropha has increased due to interest in biofuels.
Members of the genus Jatropha can be found in diverse habitats across the Americas.

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind, type) and New Latin Jatropha, from Greek iatros (physician) and trophe (nourishment), referring to medicinal uses.
