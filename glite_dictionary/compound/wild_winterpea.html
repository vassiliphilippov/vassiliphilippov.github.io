wild winterpea

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈwɪntər ˌpiː / (AmE), / waɪld ˈwɪntə ˌpiː / (BrE)
A weak-stemmed winter annual plant native to the Mediterranean, long established in the southern United States and cultivated as a cover and pasture crop.
Mediterranean winter annual plant used as cover crop

Examples

Farmers in the region plant wild winterpea to enrich the soil during the off-season.
Grazing animals often feed on wild winterpea when it is used as a pasture crop.
The wild winterpea is valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion.
