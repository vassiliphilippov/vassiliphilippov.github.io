tonic water

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑnɪk ˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
A carbonated beverage flavored with lime or lemon and containing quinine, often used as a mixer in alcoholic drinks.
bitter carbonated beverage with quinine

Examples

She ordered a gin and tonic water at the bar.
The bitterness of tonic water comes from the quinine it contains.
Some people prefer to drink tonic water on its own with a slice of lime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soda water - carbonated water, often used as a mixer
  • Synonym: quinine - bitter compound used in tonic water
