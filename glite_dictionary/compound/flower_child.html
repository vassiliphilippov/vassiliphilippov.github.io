flower child

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a young person, especially in the 1960s or 1970s, who rejects conventional values and advocates peace, love, and simple, communal living
hippie or countercultural youth

Examples

My aunt was a true flower child during the late 1960s, traveling to San Francisco to join the peace movement.
The documentary features interviews with several former flower children about their quest for a better world.
He described himself as a flower child, rejecting materialism in favor of simple living and harmony with nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hippie - a member of a youth subculture in the 1960s, advocating peace and love
  • Antonym: conformist - a person who conforms to accepted behavior or established practices

Etymology

Popularized during the 1960s in the United States as a term for hippies, who often wore flowers as symbols of peace and love.
