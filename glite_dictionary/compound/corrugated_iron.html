corrugated iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəˌɡeɪtɪd ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəɡeɪtɪd ˈaɪən / (BrE)
sheet iron or steel, usually galvanized, pressed into a wavy pattern of parallel ridges and hollows, used especially for roofing and siding
galvanized sheet iron with a wavy pattern

Examples

The roof was made of corrugated iron to provide extra strength.
Old sheds often have rusted sheets of corrugated iron on their walls.
They patched the fence with a piece of corrugated iron after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrugated steel - galvanized steel sheet with parallel ridges and hollows
