Lothian Region

1. PROPN

/ ˈloʊ.ði.ən ˈriː.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ði.ən ˈriː.dʒən / (BrE)
a former administrative district in southeast central Scotland, including the area around Edinburgh, on the south side of the Firth of Forth.
former region in southeast Scotland
british

Examples

Many tourists visit the Lothian Region each year to explore Edinburgh and its surroundings.
The Lothian Region was established as a local government area in 1975 before being abolished in 1996.
She grew up in a small town in the Lothian Region near the coast.

Etymology

From the name Lothian, possibly from a British Celtic origin, referring to a historical region in southern Scotland.
