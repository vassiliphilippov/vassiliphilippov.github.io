heptadecanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛptəˌdɛkəˈnoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛptəˌdækəˈnəʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A colorless crystalline synthetic fatty acid with 17 carbon atoms, used in chemical and biological research.
synthetic 17-carbon fatty acid
medical

Examples

Heptadecanoic acid is rarely found in nature but is important in metabolic studies.
The lab ordered a purified sample of heptadecanoic acid for their lipid research.
Analyzing the fatty acid profile, they detected trace amounts of heptadecanoic acid in the specimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: margaric acid - another name for heptadecanoic acid
  • Degree Lower: fatty acid - a carboxylic acid with a long aliphatic chain

Etymology

From 'hepta-' (Greek for seven), 'deca-' (Greek for ten), and '-oic acid' (from organic chemistry nomenclature).
