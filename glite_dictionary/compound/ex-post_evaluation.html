ex-post evaluation

1. NOUN

/ ɛks ˌpoʊst ˌɛvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛks ˌpəʊst ˌiːvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the assessment of an initiative or project after it has been completed, used to measure outcomes and effectiveness
assessment after completion of an initiative

Examples

The organization conducted an ex-post evaluation to determine the true impact of the development project.
An ex-post evaluation revealed that the intended goals of the program had not been fully achieved.
Effective ex-post evaluation helps policymakers learn from past initiatives and improve future ones.

Synset Examples

This ex post evaluation of exceptional access under the 2010 stand-by arrangement on Greece was prepared by a staff team of the International Monetary Fund.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ex-ante evaluation - assessment conducted before an initiative begins

Etymology

From Latin ex post (after the event) + evaluation.
