schizophrenic psychosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪtsəˈfrɛnɪk saɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəˈfrɛnɪk saɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a psychotic disorder characterized by distortions of reality, disorganized thought and language, and withdrawal from social interaction, occurring in schizophrenia
type of psychosis in schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The patient was hospitalized after experiencing a severe episode of schizophrenic psychosis.
Early intervention can help manage the symptoms of schizophrenic psychosis more effectively.
Her research focuses on the cognitive effects of schizophrenic psychosis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: psychosis - a severe mental disorder characterized by a disconnection from reality
  • Synonym: schizophrenia - a severe mental disorder characterized by distortions in thinking, perception, emotions, language, sense of self, and behavior

Etymology

From Greek 'schizein' (to split) + 'phren' (mind) and Greek 'psychosis' (disordered mind).
