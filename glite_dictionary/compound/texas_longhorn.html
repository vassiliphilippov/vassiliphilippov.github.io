Texas longhorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈlɔːŋhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈlɒŋhɔːn / (BrE)
A breed of long-horned beef cattle originating in the southwestern United States, known for its characteristic long horns.
a breed of long-horned cattle

Examples

The Texas longhorn is famous for its impressive, wide horns that can span up to seven feet.
Early settlers relied on the hardy Texas longhorn for both meat and leather.
We visited a ranch and saw a herd of Texas longhorns grazing in the fields.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Texas and the cattle's distinctively long horns.
