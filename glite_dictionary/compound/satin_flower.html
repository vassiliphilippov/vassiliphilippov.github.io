satin flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæt̬.ɪn ˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ɪn ˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A southeastern European plant grown for its fragrant purplish flowers and round, flat, silvery-white seedpods often used for decoration.
ornamental plant with silvery seedpods

Examples

The satin flower makes a striking addition to any bouquet with its bright purple blooms.
Many people dry the satin flower's seedpods to use in decorative vases.
In spring, the satin flower fills the garden with color and a pleasant scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - An ornamental plant grown for its silvery seedpods, also called 'satin flower'.
