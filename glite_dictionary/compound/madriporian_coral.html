madriporian coral

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædrəˈpɔriən ˈkɔrəl / (AmE), / ˌmædrɪˈpɔːrɪən ˈkɒrəl / (BrE)
A type of coral with a calcareous skeleton, whose colonies are fundamental in forming coral reefs and islands.
reef-building coral with calcareous skeleton

Examples

Madriporian corals are essential in the construction of some of the world's largest coral reefs.
The scientist studied the anatomy of madriporian coral specimens collected from the Caribbean Sea.
Pollution and rising temperatures pose a significant threat to madriporian coral populations globally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stony coral - hard corals that build reefs

Etymology

From Italian 'madrepora' (mother-pore), referring to the coral's porous appearance.
