mononuclear phagocyte system

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.noʊˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈfædʒ.əˌsaɪt ˌsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəʊˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈfædʒ.əˌsaɪt ˌsɪs.təm / (BrE)
A system in the body made up of widely distributed macrophages, both free and fixed, that are derived from bone marrow and play a role in immune defense and removal of cellular debris.
biological system of macrophages
medical

Examples

The mononuclear phagocyte system helps clear pathogens and debris from tissues throughout the body.
Research into the mononuclear phagocyte system has improved understanding of immune responses in various diseases.
Liver Kupffer cells are a major component of the mononuclear phagocyte system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticuloendothelial system - older term for the mononuclear phagocyte system
  • Synonym: macrophage - a type of phagocyte central to the mononuclear phagocyte system

Etymology

From 'mono-' (single) + 'nuclear' + 'phagocyte' (cell that engulfs particles) + 'system'.
