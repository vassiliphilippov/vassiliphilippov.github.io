family Cotingidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli koʊˈtɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kəʊˈtɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds in the order Passeriformes that includes cotingas and umbrella birds, native to Central and South America.
a family of birds including cotingas and umbrella birds

Examples

The family Cotingidae comprises a diverse group of birds found throughout Central and South America.
Researchers have recently discovered a new species within the family Cotingidae.
Many members of the family Cotingidae are known for their elaborate courtship displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotinga - a brightly colored South American bird of the cotinga family
  • Synonym: umbrella bird - a tropical American bird with a large crest, in the cotinga family

Etymology

From New Latin Cotingidae, from Cotinga (type genus) + -idae (denoting family).
