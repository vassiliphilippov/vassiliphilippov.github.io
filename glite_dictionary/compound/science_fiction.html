science fiction

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəns ˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪəns ˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
A literary genre that deals with imaginative concepts such as advanced science and technology, space exploration, time travel, and their impact on society.
fictional genre involving advanced science or technology

Examples

She has always enjoyed reading science fiction novels about distant planets and alien civilizations.
Many blockbuster movies are based on science fiction stories featuring futuristic technology.
The author is renowned for blending social issues and science fiction themes in her work.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: speculative fiction - broad genre including science fiction, fantasy, and other imaginative literature
  • Degree Higher: fantasy - genre of fiction involving magical or supernatural elements
  • Synonym: science fantasy - genre combining science fiction and fantasy elements

Etymology

Formed from 'science' and 'fiction'; recorded in English since the early 20th century.
