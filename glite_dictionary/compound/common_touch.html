common touch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən tʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən tʌtʃ / (BrE)
the ability to relate easily and naturally to ordinary people, especially as a quality admired in public figures
ability to relate to ordinary people

Examples

The mayor's common touch made her extremely popular with local residents.
Despite his wealth, the actor has never lost his common touch.
Her common touch allows her to connect with people from all walks of life.

Synset Examples

The leader's common touch helped bridge the gap.
His common touch makes him relatable to everyone.
She has a common touch that endears her to fans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share the feelings of others
