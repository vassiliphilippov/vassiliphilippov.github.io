French telephone

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ tɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / frɛntʃ tɛlɪˌfəʊn / (BrE)
a telephone set with both the mouthpiece and earpiece mounted on a single handle, as opposed to separate pieces
telephone with combined mouthpiece and earpiece

Examples

The museum displays a French telephone from the 1920s in its communication technology exhibit.
She answered the ringing French telephone that sat elegantly on the desk.
Collectors often seek out the distinctive design of a French telephone for their vintage collections.

Etymology

Named after its origin or popularization in France in the early 20th century.
