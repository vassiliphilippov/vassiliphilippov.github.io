order Rajiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌreɪ.dʒɪˈfɔr.miːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌreɪ.dʒɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of cartilaginous fish with flattened bodies, including rays, skates, guitarfishes, and sawfishes.
taxonomic order of flattened fish (rays, skates, etc.)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the order Rajiformes in the coastal waters.
All rays and skates belong to the order Rajiformes, which distinguishes them from sharks.
The unique features of the order Rajiformes make them fascinating subjects for marine biologists.

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Rajiformes', from 'Raja' (ray) + '-formes' (having the form of).
