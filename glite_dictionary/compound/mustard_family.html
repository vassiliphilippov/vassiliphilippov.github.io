mustard family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstərd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including mustards, cabbages, broccoli, turnips, and cresses, characterized by four-petaled flowers; also known as Brassicaceae
plant family including mustards and cabbages

Examples

Broccoli is a member of the mustard family, along with cabbage and kale.
The mustard family includes many important agricultural crops.
Gardeners often plant several vegetables from the mustard family in their plots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassicaceae - scientific name for the mustard family
