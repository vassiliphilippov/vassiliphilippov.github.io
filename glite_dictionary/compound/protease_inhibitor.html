protease inhibitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.tiˌeɪs ɪnˈhɪb.ɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.ti.eɪs ɪnˈhɪb.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
a drug that blocks the activity of a protease enzyme, especially used as an antiviral treatment for HIV infection
drug that blocks protease enzymes, esp. for HIV
medical

Examples

Doctors often prescribe protease inhibitors as part of combination therapy for HIV patients.
The development of protease inhibitors was a major breakthrough in the fight against AIDS.
Some protease inhibitor drugs can have side effects that need to be carefully managed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiretroviral - drug used to treat retroviral infections, especially HIV
  • Synonym: enzyme inhibitor - a substance that slows or stops the activity of an enzyme

Etymology

Formed from 'protease' (an enzyme that breaks down proteins) and 'inhibitor' (something that restrains or slows activity).
