milk sickness

1. NOUN

/ mɪlk ˈsɪknəs / (AmE), / mɪlk ˈsɪknəs / (BrE)
A disease, chiefly affecting humans and animals, caused by drinking milk or eating dairy products from cows that have ingested certain toxic plants, especially white snakeroot.
disease from drinking toxic cow's milk
medical

Examples

During the 19th century, many settlers in the Midwest died from milk sickness.
Scientists eventually discovered that milk sickness was caused by toxins in cows that ate white snakeroot.
Medical journals contain accounts of outbreaks of milk sickness among early American pioneers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlk ˌsɪk.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪlk ˌsɪk.nəs / (BrE)
A disease affecting livestock, especially cattle, caused by poisoning from eating certain kinds of snakeroot, with potential transmission to humans via contaminated milk.
disease from snakeroot poisoning in cattle
medical

Examples

Early settlers in the Midwest frequently lost cattle to milk sickness.
People who drank contaminated milk sometimes developed milk sickness themselves.
Prevention of milk sickness involved removing white snakeroot from pastures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremetol poisoning - poisoning resulting from ingestion of tremetol, the toxin in snakeroot

Etymology

From the words 'milk' and 'sickness'; the name refers to sickness caused by drinking milk from affected animals.
