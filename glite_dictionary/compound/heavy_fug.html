heavy fug

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi fʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi fʌɡ / (BrE)
A thick, stuffy, and often smoky or oppressive atmosphere, especially in a room or enclosed space.
thick, stuffy atmosphere in a room

Examples

After hours of cooking, a heavy fug clung to the kitchen and made it hard to breathe.
The old library had a heavy fug of dust and musty books lingering in the air.
He opened the window to clear the heavy fug left after the guests departed.

Synset Examples

After the party, the room was filled with a heavy fug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miasma - an unpleasant or unhealthy atmosphere
  • Synonym: haze - a slight obscuration of the lower atmosphere, typically caused by fine suspended particles
  • Antonym: fresh air - clean, outdoor air not affected by smoke or stuffiness

Etymology

"Fug" is of uncertain origin, possibly from Scottish or dialectal English, denoting close, stuffy air; "heavy" describes the intensity of the atmosphere.
