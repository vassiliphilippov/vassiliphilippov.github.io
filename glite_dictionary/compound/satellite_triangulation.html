satellite triangulation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætəlˌaɪt traɪˌæŋgjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsætəlaɪt traɪˌæŋgjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a technique in satellite geodesy where the position and shape of the Earth are determined by measuring angles to artificial satellites from multiple ground stations
geodetic positioning using satellites

Examples

Modern geodesists rely on satellite triangulation to achieve precise measurements of Earth's surface.
The invention of satellite triangulation greatly improved the accuracy of global mapping.
National mapping agencies use satellite triangulation when integrating regional geodetic networks.

Synset Examples

Efforts are now under way to extend and tie together existing continental networks by satellite triangulation so as to facilitate the adjustment of all major geodetic surveys into a single world datum and determine the size and shape of the Earth spheroid with much greater accuracy than heretofore obtained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangulation - measuring angles from known points to determine locations

Etymology

From 'satellite' (an artificial body orbiting Earth) and 'triangulation' (measurement of angles for determining positions).
