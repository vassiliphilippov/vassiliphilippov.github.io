gum benjamin

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ˈbɛn.dʒə.mɪn / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈbɛn.dʒɪ.mɪn / (BrE)
A fragrant gum resin obtained from certain trees, used especially in perfumes and for treating skin irritation.
fragrant gum resin, used for skin and perfumes

Examples

The apothecary recommended gum benjamin as a soothing remedy for her irritated skin.
A faint scent of gum benjamin lingered in the old wooden chest.
He mixed gum benjamin with other resins to create a fragrant incense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoin - A balsamic resin obtained from trees of the genus Styrax

Etymology

From Benjamin, an old term for benzoin, ultimately from Arabic lubān jāwī (frankincense from Java).
