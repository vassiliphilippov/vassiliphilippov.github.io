active electrolocation

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪv ɪˌlɛktroʊloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv ɪˌlɛktrəʊləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which an animal detects objects and its environment by generating an electric field and sensing changes in that field with electroreceptor organs.
animal sensing by generating electric fields

Examples

Many species of fish use active electrolocation to navigate murky waters where vision is limited.
Researchers study active electrolocation to better understand how electric fish perceive their surroundings.
During active electrolocation, the animal emits a weak electric field and senses objects that distort it.

Synset Examples

In a process called active electrolocation, animals are able to detect and analyse objects in their environment, which allows them to perceive a detailed electrical picture of their surroundings even in complete darkness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passive electrolocation - detecting electric fields from external sources, not self-generated

Etymology

From 'active' and 'electrolocation', the latter combining 'electro-' (electricity) and 'location' (act of locating).
