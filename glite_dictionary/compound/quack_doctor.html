quack doctor

1. NOUN

/ kwæk ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / kwæk ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
A person who fraudulently claims to have medical skills or qualifications and practices medicine without proper credentials.
fraudulent or unqualified medical practitioner

Examples

The police arrested the quack doctor for endangering patients with fake treatments.
Many people were fooled by the quack doctor's confident manner and false medical claims.
Advertisements for miracle cures often lead people to unscrupulous quack doctors.

Synset Examples

The quack doctor was eventually exposed for his scams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charlatan - a person falsely claiming to have special knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: doctor - a qualified practitioner of medicine

Etymology

From 'quack' (fraudulent medical practitioner), itself from Dutch 'quacksalver' (someone who cures with salves), and 'doctor'.
