mathematical logic

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl ˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
the branch of logic that studies formal systems, statements, and proofs using mathematical methods
branch of logic using mathematics

Examples

She specialized in mathematical logic during her doctoral studies at the university.
The foundations of computer science rely heavily on mathematical logic.
Many paradoxes in philosophy are explored using tools from mathematical logic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formal logic - logic concerning the form rather than the content of statements
  • Degree Higher: mathematics - the abstract science of number, quantity, and space
  • Degree Lower: logic - reasoning conducted or assessed according to strict principles of validity

Etymology

From 'mathematical', relating to mathematics, and 'logic', from Greek 'logikē' (art of reasoning).
