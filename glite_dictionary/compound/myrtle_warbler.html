myrtle warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrt̬l ˈwɔrb(ə)lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtl ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
a North American songbird, a subspecies of the yellow-rumped warbler, with distinctive white throat and yellow patches on the head, rump, and sides
North American warbler bird

Examples

The myrtle warbler is easy to identify by the bright yellow patches on its sides and rump.
During migration, flocks of myrtle warblers can be seen feeding in open woodlands.
I spotted a myrtle warbler in the garden yesterday, singing in the early morning light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Audubon's warbler - a closely related or formerly separate subspecies of yellow-rumped warbler
