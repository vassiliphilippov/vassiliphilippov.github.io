inferior vena cava

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriər ˌviːnə ˈkeɪvə / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˌviːnə ˈkeɪvə / (BrE)
the large vein that carries blood from the lower body and abdomen to the right atrium of the heart
major vein carrying blood to the heart from the lower body
medical

Examples

A blockage in the inferior vena cava can cause swelling in the legs.
The inferior vena cava returns deoxygenated blood to the heart from the lower half of the body.
Surgeons carefully examined the patient's inferior vena cava for signs of obstruction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior vena cava - the large vein that carries blood from the upper body to the right atrium of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'vena cava' (hollow vein), 'inferior' meaning lower.
