coal black

1. NOUN

/ koʊl blæk / (AmE), / kəʊl blæk / (BrE)
A very dark, pure black, like the color of coal.
a very dark, pure black color

Examples

She had long, flowing hair that was coal black and shiny.
The night sky was so coal black that not a star could be seen.
His eyes were described as coal black in the novel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet black - an intense, deep black color

Etymology

From 'coal' (the combustible black rock) and 'black'; used to emphasize the deep blackness resembling coal.
