to herald the arrival

1. VERB

/ ˈhɛr.əld ði əˈraɪ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.əld ði əˈraɪ.vəl / (BrE)
to announce or signal the beginning or coming of something new or significant
announce the beginning of something

Examples

The blooming of cherry blossoms often heralds the arrival of spring in Japan.
Dark clouds and a sudden drop in temperature heralded the arrival of the thunderstorm.
In many cultures, the appearance of the first snowflake heralds the arrival of winter.

Synset Examples

He will herald the arrival of spring with a festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to announce - to make known publicly
  • Synonym: to proclaim - to declare or announce officially or publicly

Etymology

From 'herald', meaning 'to announce', with historical use referring to formal messengers.
