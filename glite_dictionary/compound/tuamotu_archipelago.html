Tuamotu Archipelago

1. PROPN

/ tuˈɑːmoʊtu ˌɑːrkəˈpɛləˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / tuːˈæmətuː ˌɑːkɪˈpeləɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group of about 80 coral islands in French Polynesia, known for their extensive atolls and clear lagoons
group of coral islands in French Polynesia

Examples

The Tuamotu Archipelago is famous for its breathtaking coral reefs and marine life.
Researchers traveled to the Tuamotu Archipelago to study rare species of tropical fish.
Tourists are often drawn to the remote beauty of the Tuamotu Archipelago in French Polynesia.

Etymology

From Tuamotuan (Polynesian language) Tuamotu (literally ‘distant islands’) + Italian archipelago.
