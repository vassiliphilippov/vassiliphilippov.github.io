plain flour

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn flaʊɚ / (AmE), / pleɪn flaʊə / (BrE)
flour that does not contain a raising agent, commonly used in baking and cooking
flour without a raising agent
british

Examples

Most British recipes for cakes use plain flour and add baking powder separately.
If you only have plain flour, you can't substitute it for self-raising flour without adding a raising agent.
She prefers to use plain flour for making sauces and gravies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: self-raising flour - flour containing a raising agent, used for baking
