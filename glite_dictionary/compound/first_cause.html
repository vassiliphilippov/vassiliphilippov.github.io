first cause

1. NOUN

/ fɝst kɔz / (AmE), / fɜːst kɔːz / (BrE)
in philosophy and theology, an agent that is the ultimate origin of everything and is itself uncaused, often identified with God
the ultimate uncaused cause; origin of all things

Examples

Many theologians argue that God is the first cause of the universe.
The debate about the first cause has occupied philosophers for centuries.
Some people seek a first cause to explain the existence of the cosmos.

Synset Examples

God is the first cause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime mover - the initial source of motion or change (Aristotelian philosophy)
