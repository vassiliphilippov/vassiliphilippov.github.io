Alexander Archipelago

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændər ˌɑrkɪˈpɛləˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændə ˌɑːkɪˈpeləɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group of islands off the southeastern coast of Alaska, forming part of the Alaska Panhandle
island group off southeastern Alaska

Examples

Cruising through the Alexander Archipelago, we saw dozens of seabirds and remote fishing villages.
The Alexander Archipelago is home to a rich diversity of wildlife, including bears and eagles.
Many of the islands in the Alexander Archipelago are accessible only by boat or seaplane.

Etymology

Named after Alexander Baranov, a Russian governor of Alaska; from Greek 'arkhipelagos' (chief sea, later group of islands).
