money dealer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni ˌdiːlər / (AmE), / ˈmʌni ˌdiːlə / (BrE)
a person who receives, invests, or pays out money, usually in a professional or commercial capacity
person handling financial transactions

Examples

The money dealer made significant investments on behalf of his clients.
As a money dealer, she was responsible for overseeing large cash transactions.
The government regulates the activities of every licensed money dealer in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financier - a person concerned with the management of large amounts of money
  • Synonym: banker - an officer or owner of a bank
