prison camp

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən kæmp / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən kæmp / (BrE)
a camp where prisoners, often employed in government projects or forced labor, are detained under guard
a camp for detaining and employing prisoners

Examples

During the war, he was held in a prison camp for several years.
The government used the prison camp inmates to construct roads and railways.
Many letters describing life in the prison camp survived in family archives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - a place where large numbers of people are detained or confined under armed guard
  • Synonym: labor camp - a camp where prisoners or detainees are forced to work

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən kæmp / (AmE), / ˈprɪzn̩ kæmp / (BrE)
a camp where prisoners of war are confined, usually under guard
camp for prisoners of war

Examples

During the war, many soldiers were held in a prison camp far from home.
After the escape, security at the prison camp was tightened considerably.
The museum features artifacts recovered from a World War II prison camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - a place where large numbers of political prisoners or persecuted minorities are deliberately imprisoned
