giant armadillo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ɑːrməˈdɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ɑːməˈdɪləʊ / (BrE)
a very large species of armadillo native to South America, growing up to about three feet long, not including the tail
large South American armadillo species

Examples

The giant armadillo is the largest living species of armadillo, found mostly in South American rainforests.
Biologists studied the burrows created by the giant armadillo to understand its role in the ecosystem.
Unlike smaller armadillos, the giant armadillo eats larger insects and even small vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armadillo - burrowing mammal with a leathery armor shell
