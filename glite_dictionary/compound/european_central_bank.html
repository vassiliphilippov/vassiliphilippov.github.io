European Central Bank

1. PROPN

/ juˈroʊpiən ˈsɛntrəl bæŋk / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈsɛntrəl bæŋk / (BrE)
the official central bank responsible for monetary policy within the eurozone, comprising member states of the European Union that use the euro as their currency
central bank for the eurozone

Examples

The European Central Bank announced new measures to help stabilize the eurozone economy.
Decisions made by the European Central Bank have significant effects on world financial markets.
The European Central Bank sets interest rates for countries that use the euro.

Synset Examples

The European Central Bank is Europe's equivalent of the Federal Reserve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Federal Reserve - central bank of the United States
