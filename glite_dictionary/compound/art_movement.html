art movement

1. NOUN

/ ɑrt ˈmuvmənt / (AmE), / ɑːt ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
A group of artists who share similar ideas, philosophies, or goals, often working in a distinct style or focused on a common set of principles.
group of artists sharing common principles

Examples

The Impressionist art movement revolutionized painting in the late nineteenth century.
She specializes in researching the Surrealist art movement and its major figures.
Modern art museums often organize exhibitions around a specific art movement or style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: school - a group of artists with a common style or philosophy
