second person

1. NOUN

/ ˈsekənd ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˈsekənd ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
the grammatical category used to refer to the person or people being addressed, typically represented by pronouns such as 'you' and corresponding verb forms
grammar: form for 'you' or addressed person

Examples

In English, the pronoun second person is represented by 'you'.
Verbs in the second person often change their endings in languages like Spanish.
The teacher explained the difference between second person and third person narration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first person - grammatical form referring to the speaker (e.g., 'I', 'we')
  • Antonym: third person - grammatical form referring to others besides the speaker and addressee (e.g., 'he', 'she', 'they')

Etymology

Formed from 'second' (from Latin 'secundus', meaning 'following', 'next after first') and 'person' (from Latin 'persona'). Used in grammar since the 16th century to distinguish between speaker, addressee, and others.
