maypole dance

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪˌpoʊl dæns / (AmE), / ˈmeɪpəʊl dɑːns / (BrE)
a traditional English folk dance performed around a tall pole decorated with flowers and ribbons, especially on May Day
traditional folk dance around a maypole

Examples

Every spring, children participate in the maypole dance at the village green.
The maypole dance involves weaving colorful ribbons in patterns as dancers move around the pole.
Spectators gathered to watch the lively maypole dance during the festival.

Synset Examples

The maypole dance is a cherished tradition in our town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maypole - a tall pole decorated for May Day festivities

Etymology

From 'maypole' (a tall pole used in May Day celebrations) + 'dance'.
