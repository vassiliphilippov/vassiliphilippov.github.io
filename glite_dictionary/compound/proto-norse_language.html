Proto-Norse language

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˈnɔrs ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈnɔːs ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the earliest attested stage of the North Germanic languages, spoken in Scandinavia until about 700 AD
early North Germanic language of Scandinavia

Examples

Scholars study the Proto-Norse language to understand the origins of Scandinavian dialects.
Runic inscriptions are some of the few surviving records of the Proto-Norse language.
Before the Viking Age, the people of Scandinavia spoke the Proto-Norse language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Old Norse - the North Germanic language spoken after Proto-Norse, from about 700–1350 AD
  • Degree Lower: Proto-Germanic - the reconstructed ancestor of all Germanic languages

Etymology

Composed of 'proto-', meaning 'first' or 'early', and 'Norse', from Old Norse 'norrœnn'.
