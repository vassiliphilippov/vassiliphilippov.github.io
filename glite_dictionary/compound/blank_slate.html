blank slate

1. NOUN

/ blæŋk sleɪt / (AmE), / blæŋk sleɪt / (BrE)
a situation in which something begins with no previous history or influences, allowing a fresh start
fresh start with no prior history

Examples

After years in the same job, he longed for a blank slate where he could pursue his own dreams.
The new coach promised to treat every player as a blank slate regardless of their past.
When the company was restructured, employees felt like they were starting with a blank slate.

Synset Examples

Moving to a new city gave him a blank slate.
After the incident, he felt like a blank slate.
She wanted a fresh start, a complete blank slate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fresh start - an opportunity to begin again without prejudice; new beginning
