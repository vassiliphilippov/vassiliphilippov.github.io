Frisian Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɪʒiən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈfrɪzɪən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a chain of islands in the North Sea off the coasts of the Netherlands, Germany, and Denmark, extending from the IJsselmeer to Jutland
island chain in the North Sea, Europe

Examples

Many tourists enjoy birdwatching on the Frisian Islands during the spring migration.
Strong winds are common along the Frisian Islands throughout the year.
The Frisian Islands are known for their unique coastal landscapes and traditional villages.

Etymology

Named after the Frisians, a people native to the region.
