China jute

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnə dʒut / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə dʒuːt / (BrE)
a tall annual herb or subshrub of tropical Asia, with velvety leaves and yellow flowers, grown for its strong fiber; also naturalized in southeastern Europe and the United States
Asian plant yielding strong fiber

Examples

The production of China jute has significantly increased in recent years due to its economic value.
Textiles made from China jute are known for their strength and durability.
Farmers in southeastern Europe have started cultivating China jute for its valuable fiber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jute - a long, soft, shiny vegetable fiber spun into coarse threads
