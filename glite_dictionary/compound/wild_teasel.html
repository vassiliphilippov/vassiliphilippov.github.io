wild teasel

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈtiːzəl / (AmE), / waɪld ˈtiːzəl / (BrE)
A tall prickly plant of the teasel family, native to Europe and Asia, with white to pinkish flowers, naturalized in parts of North America.
prickly European plant with pale flowers

Examples

The dried heads of the wild teasel are often used in flower arrangements.
We spotted a wild teasel growing by the side of the country road.
Invasive in some areas, wild teasel can outcompete native plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - a plant with prickly stems and flower heads, genus Dipsacus
