strobe light

1. NOUN

/ ˈstroʊb ˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstrəʊb ˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a device that emits regular, brief flashes of light, often used to make moving objects appear stationary or for special effects
device emitting flashing light

Examples

The club used a strobe light to enhance the atmosphere on the dance floor.
When the strobe light flashes, the spinning fan appears to stop moving.
Safety drills used a strobe light to alert workers of an emergency evacuation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroboscope - an instrument that makes a cyclically moving object appear stationary

Etymology

From Greek 'strobos' (whirling) and English 'light.'
