Cantonese dialect

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.təˈniz ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ˌkæn.təˈniːz ˈdaɪ.ə.lɛkt / (BrE)
the variety of Chinese spoken in Guangdong (Canton), Hong Kong, and among overseas Chinese communities
variety of Chinese spoken in Guangdong and Hong Kong

Examples

Many residents of Hong Kong speak Cantonese dialect as their first language.
She is fluent in both Mandarin and Cantonese dialect, making her an excellent interpreter.
Films in the Cantonese dialect are popular throughout Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mandarin dialect - the official and most widely spoken dialect of Chinese
  • Degree Higher: Chinese language - the group of languages that make up the Sinitic branch of the Sino-Tibetan family

Etymology

From Canton, the former English name for Guangzhou, combined with -ese (denoting language), and dialect.
