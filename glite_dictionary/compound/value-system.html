value-system

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæljuː ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈvæljuː ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A set of principles or standards by which a person or group determines what is right and wrong.
set of ethical principles or standards

Examples

Different cultures often have distinct value-systems that influence their customs and laws.
His value-system was shaped by his parents and early education.
The company promotes a value-system based on honesty and respect.

Synset Examples

the Puritan ethic
a person with old-fashioned values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethic - a set of moral principles
  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
