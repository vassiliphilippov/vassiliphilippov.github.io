Sunni Muslim

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʊni ˈmʊzlɪm / (AmE), / ˈsʊni ˈmʊzlɪm / (BrE)
A member of the largest branch of Islam, which recognizes the first four caliphs as Muhammad's rightful successors.
member of Sunni branch of Islam

Examples

The majority of Muslims worldwide are considered Sunni Muslims and follow established traditions.
As a Sunni Muslim, he observes the rituals and holidays common in his community.
The differences between a Sunni Muslim and a Shia Muslim stem from early Islamic history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shia Muslim - member of the branch of Islam that regards Ali as Muhammad's rightful successor

Etymology

From Arabic 'sunnī' (one who follows the Sunnah) and 'Muslim.'
