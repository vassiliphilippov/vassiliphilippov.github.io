word structure

1. NOUN

/ wɝːd ˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / wɜːd ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the way in which the sounds or elements of a word are organized or arranged
organization of sounds or elements in a word

Examples

The word structure in English differs from that of Japanese.
Linguists study word structure to understand how sounds combine in different languages.
Children often make errors in word structure when learning to speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonotactics - rules governing allowable sound arrangements in a language
