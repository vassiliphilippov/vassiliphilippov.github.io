phylum Rotifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm roʊˈtɪfərə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪləm rəʊˈtɪfərə / (BrE)
A phylum of microscopic aquatic animals known as rotifers, characterized by a wheel-like ciliated structure at their front end.
phylum of microscopic aquatic animals (rotifers)
medical

Examples

The phylum Rotifera includes more than 2,000 described species of rotifers.
Researchers discovered a new species within the phylum Rotifera in a freshwater pond.
Members of the phylum Rotifera are important for studying aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotifer - a microscopic aquatic animal belonging to the phylum Rotifera

Etymology

From Latin 'rota' (wheel) and '-fera' (bearing), referring to the wheel-like cilia.
