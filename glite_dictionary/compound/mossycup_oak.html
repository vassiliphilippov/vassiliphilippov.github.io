mossycup oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑsiˌkʌp oʊk / (AmE), / ˈmɒsiˌkʌp əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large species of deciduous oak native to central and eastern North America, characterized by ovoid acorns set in large, fringed cups and tough, close-grained wood
North American oak tree species with fringed acorn cups

Examples

The massive mossycup oak in the park provides plenty of shade during the summer.
Carpenters prized the durable wood of the mossycup oak for making furniture and flooring.
He identified the tree by its large, fringed acorn cups, a sure sign it was a mossycup oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur oak - another common name for mossycup oak, Quercus macrocarpa

Etymology

From 'mossy' (referring to the fringed, moss-like cup of the acorn) + 'cup' + 'oak'.
