Guinea corn

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni kɔːn / (BrE)
A type of sorghum (Sorghum bicolor) grown in dry regions of Asia and North Africa for its grain, used as food and fodder.
a grain-producing sorghum plant

Examples

Farmers in northern Nigeria often plant Guinea corn because it thrives in dry climates.
The harvest of Guinea corn was especially good this year, providing food for the whole village.
She ground the Guinea corn into flour to make traditional flatbreads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of cereal grasses with edible grain

Etymology

Likely named for Guinea, a region of West Africa, where it was widely cultivated.
