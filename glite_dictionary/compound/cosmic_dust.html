cosmic dust

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑzmɪk dʌst / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɪk dʌst / (BrE)
tiny solid particles and gases found dispersed throughout interstellar space
tiny particles in space

Examples

Scientists study cosmic dust to understand the origins of planets and stars.
Telescopes can detect cosmic dust by observing how it blocks or scatters starlight.
Meteor showers are sometimes caused by streams of cosmic dust entering Earth's atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interstellar dust - dust located in the space between stars
