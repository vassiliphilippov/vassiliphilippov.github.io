vector-borne transmission

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛktɚ bɔrn trænzˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɛktə bɔːn trænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the indirect spread of an infectious agent to a person through the bite or contact of a vector, such as a mosquito or tick
disease spread by vectors like mosquitoes
medical

Examples

Malaria is a classic example of a disease spread through vector-borne transmission.
Controlling mosquitoes helps prevent vector-borne transmission of several dangerous viruses.
Public health officials focus on reducing vector-borne transmission during outbreaks of diseases like Zika and dengue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct transmission - the spread of an infectious agent directly from person to person
