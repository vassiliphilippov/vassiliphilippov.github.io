vegetation unit

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛdʒɪˈteɪʃən ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˌvɛdʒɪˈteɪʃən ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A distinct area or patch of land characterized by a specific group of plant species, differentiable from surrounding areas by its vegetation.
distinct area defined by its plant community

Examples

The researchers mapped each vegetation unit to assess the health of the ecosystem.
A wetland vegetation unit often supports different wildlife compared to a forest unit nearby.
Fire management strategies vary depending on the type of vegetation unit present in an area.
