Apalachicola River

1. PROPN

/ əˌpælətʃɪˈkoʊlə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / əˌpælətʃɪˈkəʊlə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in northwestern Florida, formed by the junction of the Chattahoochee and Flint Rivers at the Florida border
river in northwestern Florida

Examples

The Apalachicola River provides a vital habitat for many rare and endangered species.
We went kayaking on the Apalachicola River during our Florida vacation.
The Apalachicola River is known for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty.

Etymology

From the name of the Apalachicola people, indigenous to the region.
