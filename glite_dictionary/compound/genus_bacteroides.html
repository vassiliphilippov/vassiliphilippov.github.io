genus Bacteroides

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs bæk.təˈrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bæk.təˈrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Bacteroidaceae, consisting of Gram-negative, rod-shaped, anaerobic bacteria that do not produce endospores or pigments and are commonly found in the gut of humans and animals.
genus of gut bacteria in Bacteroidaceae
medical

Examples

The genus Bacteroides plays a crucial role in the human digestive system.
Recent studies have explored the diversity within the genus Bacteroides found in animal guts.
Antibiotics can disrupt the balance of the genus Bacteroides in the microbiome.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bacteroidaceae - the family to which Bacteroides belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and Neo-Latin 'Bacteroides', from Greek 'baktron' (rod) and '-eides' (resembling), referring to rod-shaped cells.
