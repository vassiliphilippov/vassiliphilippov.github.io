basic cognitive process

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪk ˈkɑɡnətɪv ˈprɑsɛs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪk ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a fundamental mental operation involved in acquiring and retaining knowledge, such as perception, memory, or attention
fundamental mental process for knowledge

Examples

Attention is a basic cognitive process that allows us to focus on relevant information.
Perception is considered a basic cognitive process essential for making sense of the world.
Researchers study how memory, a basic cognitive process, is formed and retrieved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognition - mental action or process of acquiring knowledge and understanding
  • Degree Higher: higher cognitive process - advanced mental operation such as reasoning or problem solving
