dunny man

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌni mæn / (AmE), / ˈdʌni mæn / (BrE)
a person whose job is to empty or clean outdoor toilets, especially in areas without sewerage; formerly common in Australia
person who empties outdoor toilets
british

Examples

The dunny man arrived early every Friday to empty the old backyard toilet.
When the sewer was finally connected, the dunny man's visits stopped.
As a child, she was terrified of the dunny man and his noisy truck.

Synset Examples

Everyone knows the dunny man by name around here.
The dunny man left a funny note on the door.
The dunny man came to empty the toilet this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night soil collector - person who collects and removes human waste from non-flush toilets

Etymology

From Australian English 'dunny' (toilet) + 'man'. 'Dunny' from British dialect 'dunnekin' or 'dannikin' (privy).
