photochemical exchange

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈkɛmɪkəl ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈkɛmɪkəl ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
an exchange resulting from the chemical action of light or radiant energy
exchange caused by light-induced chemical process

Examples

In this experiment, the photochemical exchange between carbon dioxide and oxygen is carefully measured.
The ozone layer is affected by photochemical exchange reactions in the upper atmosphere.
Researchers studied a novel photochemical exchange that occurred under ultraviolet light exposure.

Etymology

From 'photo-' meaning light and 'chemical', relating to chemistry.
