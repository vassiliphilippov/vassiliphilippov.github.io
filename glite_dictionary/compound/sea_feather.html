sea feather

1. NOUN

/ si ˈfɛðər / (AmE), / siː ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
a type of coral that forms feathery, plume-like colonies, often found in warm ocean waters
featherlike colonial coral

Examples

Divers admired the vibrant sea feather colonies swaying gently on the coral reef.
Some species of sea feather provide shelter for small fish and marine invertebrates.
The museum had a preserved sea feather on display, showing its intricate structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorgonian - a soft coral forming branching or fan-shaped colonies
