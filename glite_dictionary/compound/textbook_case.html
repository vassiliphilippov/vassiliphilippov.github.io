textbook case

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkstˌbʊk keɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstˌbʊk keɪs / (BrE)
An instance or occurrence that perfectly exemplifies a particular situation, condition, or rule, often used as a standard example.
an ideal or perfect example

Examples

The police report was described as a textbook case of fraud.
Doctors say her symptoms were a textbook case of the illness.
This is a textbook case of how not to handle customer complaints.

Synset Examples

It turned out to be a textbook case in the trial.
The detective called it a textbook case of crime.
Her recovery was a textbook case of resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archetype - a very typical example of a certain person or thing
  • Antonym: exception - a case that does not follow the rule or pattern

Etymology

From 'textbook', meaning a standard book of instruction, and 'case', meaning an instance or example.
