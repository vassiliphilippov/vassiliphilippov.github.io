deep-rooted tradition

1. NOUN

/ diːp ˈruːtɪd trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / diːp ˈruːtɪd trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a tradition that is firmly established and resistant to change, often passed down through generations
firmly established tradition

Examples

In many rural areas, deep-rooted traditions still influence community decisions and celebrations.
The festival reflects the deep-rooted traditions of the region's indigenous people.
Changing such deep-rooted traditions often requires generations of effort and education.

Synset Examples

They had deep-rooted traditions that shaped their culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tradition - a custom or belief passed down within a group or society
