Yankee corn

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæŋki kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈjaŋki kɔːn / (BrE)
a type of corn with kernels that have a hard outer layer surrounding a softer inner part (the endosperm)
corn with hard kernels and soft endosperm

Examples

Farmers in the Northeast often grew Yankee corn because of its resistance to cold weather.
The tough kernels of Yankee corn make it ideal for grinding into cornmeal.
This museum exhibit displays several varieties, including Yankee corn and sweet corn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flint corn - type of corn with hard outer kernel layer
