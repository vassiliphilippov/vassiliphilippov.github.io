quick squizz

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk skwɪz / (AmE), / kwɪk skwɪz / (BrE)
A brief or hurried look; a glance, especially used informally.
a brief glance; quick look
informal british

Examples

She took a quick squizz at the letter before putting it in her bag.
He invited us to have a quick squizz around his new apartment.
Give the contract a quick squizz and let me know if you spot any errors.

Synset Examples

She gave me a quick squizz at the new dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance - a brief or hurried look
  • Synonym: peer - to look closely or with difficulty

Etymology

From "quick" (rapid) and "squizz," Australian/British slang from 20th century, possibly from "quiz" meaning to peer or look.
