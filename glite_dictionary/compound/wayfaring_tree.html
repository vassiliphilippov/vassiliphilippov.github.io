wayfaring tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪˌfeəriŋ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈweɪˌfeərɪŋ ˌtriː / (BrE)
a vigorous deciduous European shrub (Viburnum lantana) often found along waysides, with clusters of white flowers and red berries that turn black
European shrub with white flowers and berries

Examples

The wayfaring tree blooms in late spring, producing attractive clusters of white flowers.
Birds are drawn to the berries of the wayfaring tree as they ripen from red to black.
She researched the medicinal uses of the wayfaring tree for a local gardening project.

Etymology

Named for its frequent presence along roadsides (ways); 'wayfaring' from 'way' (road) and 'fare' (to travel).
