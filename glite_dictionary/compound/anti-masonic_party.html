Anti-Masonic Party

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti məˈsɑː.nɪk ˈpɑːr.ti / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti məˈsɒ.nɪk ˈpɑː.ti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, founded in 1825 to oppose Freemasonry in public affairs
US political party opposing Freemasonry

Examples

The Anti-Masonic Party was the first third party in the political history of the United States.
Many members of the Anti-Masonic Party later joined the Whig Party.
The influence of the Anti-Masonic Party declined after the 1830s.
