curb market

1. NOUN

/ kɝb ˈmɑrkət / (AmE), / kɜːb ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a market for trading securities that are not listed on a formal stock exchange, originally conducted on the street outside the exchange
market for unlisted securities

Examples

Many investors turned to the curb market when their shares were not accepted by the official exchange.
The curb market originally began on Broad Street, just outside the New York Stock Exchange.
After being delisted, the company’s stock continued to trade on the curb market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: over-the-counter market - market for securities not listed on a formal exchange

Etymology

From the practice of stockbrokers conducting trades on the curb, or edge, of the street outside the stock exchange in New York.

2. NOUN

/ kɝb ˈmɑrkət / (AmE), / kɜːb ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
an informal outdoor market, often held on sidewalks or curbs, where vendors sell goods directly to the public
informal outdoor street market

Examples

Local farmers sell their produce at the curb market every weekend.
She enjoys browsing handmade crafts at the city curb market.
The curb market offers fresh vegetables and flowers at affordable prices.

Synset Examples

He found some great deals at the curb market this morning.
Every Saturday, the curb market attracts many local shoppers.
The curb market was bustling with vendors selling fresh produce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: street market - a regular market set up outdoors on streets

Etymology

From 'curb' (the edge of a sidewalk) and 'market' (a place for buying and selling goods).
