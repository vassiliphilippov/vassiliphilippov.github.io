thought-reader

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔt ˌridər / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt ˌriːdə / (BrE)
A magician who appears to read people's thoughts, often by using subtle signals or tricks.
magician who appears to read thoughts

Examples

The thought-reader amazed the audience by revealing private details about volunteers.
Many people were skeptical, suspecting that the thought-reader had assistance from someone in the crowd.
After the show, the thought-reader refused to reveal how he performed his tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mentalist - a performer who claims to have psychic powers, especially related to mind-reading or prediction

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɔtˌridər / (AmE), / ˈθɔːtˌriːdə / (BrE)
a person who claims to have the ability to communicate or discern others' thoughts directly, especially by telepathy
someone who claims to read thoughts directly

Examples

The magician claimed to be a thought-reader who could guess what audience members were thinking.
With her mysterious skills, she was once regarded as a true thought-reader.
Skeptics often challenge anyone who presents themselves as a thought-reader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mind reader - a person who claims the ability to know other people's thoughts
  • Synonym: telepath - a person with the supposed ability to read or communicate thoughts mentally
