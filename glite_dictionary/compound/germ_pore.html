germ pore

1. NOUN

/ dʒɜrm pɔr / (AmE), / dʒɜːm pɔː / (BrE)
a small opening in the outer wall of a spore or pollen grain through which the germ tube or pollen tube emerges during germination
pore in spore/pollen wall for germination

Examples

Each pollen grain contains a germ pore that allows the pollen tube to grow out during fertilization.
The number and position of germ pores on pollen grains can help identify plant species.
A breakdown in the germ pore may hinder the successful germination of the spore.
