cirrus cloud

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪr.əs klaʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪr.əs klaʊd / (BrE)
A thin, wispy cloud composed of fine ice crystals, typically found at high altitudes.
a thin, wispy high-altitude cloud

Examples

On clear days, you can often spot cirrus clouds streaking across the high sky.
Pilots watch for cirrus clouds as a sign of changing weather.
The delicate wisps of cirrus clouds caught the colors of the setting sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cumulus cloud - a puffy, white low-level cloud
  • Antonym: stratus cloud - a low, gray, uniform cloud layer

Etymology

From Latin 'cirrus' meaning 'curl' or 'fringe', referring to the cloud's appearance.
