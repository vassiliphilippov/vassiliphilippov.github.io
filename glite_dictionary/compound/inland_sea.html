Inland Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪn.lænd si / (AmE), / ˈɪn.lənd siː / (BrE)
an extensive arm of the Pacific Ocean in southern Japan, bordered by the islands of Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu and connected to the Sea of Japan by a narrow channel
large sea in southern Japan

Examples

The Inland Sea is renowned for its scenic beauty and mild climate.
Hiroshima, one of Japan's major ports, is located on the coast of the Inland Sea.
Many tourists take ferries to explore the islands scattered throughout the Inland Sea.
