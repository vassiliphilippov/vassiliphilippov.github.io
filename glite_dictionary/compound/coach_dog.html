coach dog

1. NOUN

/ koʊtʃ dɑɡ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ dɒɡ / (BrE)
a large dog breed with a smooth white coat marked with black or brown spots, also known as the Dalmatian
a breed of dog with black or brown spots (Dalmatian)

Examples

The coach dog was originally bred to run alongside horse-drawn carriages.
Her family adopted a coach dog from the animal shelter last summer.
The distinctive spots make the coach dog easy to recognize at dog shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dalmatian - a breed of dog with a white coat and black or brown spots

Etymology

Named for its historical use as a companion to horse-drawn coaches; associated with Dalmatia, a region in Croatia.
