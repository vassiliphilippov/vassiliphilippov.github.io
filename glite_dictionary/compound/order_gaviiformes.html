order Gaviiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ɡəˌvaɪ.ɪˈfɔr.miːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ɡəˌvaɪ.ɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of large aquatic birds, including loons and some extinct related forms.
taxonomic order of aquatic birds (loons)

Examples

The order Gaviiformes is best known for including the common loon.
Scientists study fossils to learn about extinct members of the order Gaviiformes.
Members of the order Gaviiformes are expert divers that inhabit northern lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loon - large aquatic bird of the northern hemisphere

Etymology

From modern Latin Gaviiformes, based on genus Gavia (the loon) + -formes (having the form of).
