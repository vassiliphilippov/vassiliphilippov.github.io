golden polypody

1. NOUN

/ ˈgoʊl.dən pəˈlɪ.pə.di / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl.dən pəˈlɪ.pə.di / (BrE)
A mat-forming fern with creeping rootstocks and large, deeply divided fronds, found in North America, Europe, Africa, and eastern Asia.
a type of mat-forming fern

Examples

The golden polypody is commonly found clinging to rocky outcrops in temperate forests.
Gardeners appreciate the golden polypody for its attractive, deeply lobed fronds.
During their hike, they spotted a patch of golden polypody flourishing along the stream bank.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊl.dən pəˈlɪp.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl.dən pəˈlɪp.ə.di / (BrE)
a tropical American fern with brown scaly rhizomes and large, deeply lobed, bluish-green fronds, sometimes classified in the genus Polypodium
a tropical American fern species

Examples

The golden polypody is prized by gardeners for its striking foliage.
A mature golden polypody can bring a tropical feel to indoor spaces.
Botanists often debate whether the golden polypody belongs in the genus Polypodium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with fronds
