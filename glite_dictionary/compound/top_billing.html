top billing

1. NOUN

/ tɑp ˈbɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / tɒp ˈbɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the most prominent position given to a performer's name in advertisements or credits, especially on a theatrical poster
most prominent placement of a performer's name

Examples

The famous actor was upset when he did not receive top billing for the movie.
Her name appeared in top billing on all the promotional materials.
He worked hard for years to finally achieve top billing on Broadway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star billing - the status of being advertised as a star performer

2. NOUN

/ tɑp ˈbɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / tɒp ˈbɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the most prominent position or highest recognition among the performers in a show, film, or similar production, usually listed first in credits
the most prominent performer position

Examples

She was thrilled to receive top billing for her performance in the play.
The actor finally achieved top billing after years of supporting roles.
In most major films, only one or two stars are given top billing.

Synset Examples

The new movie featured him in top billing.
Her conviction was evident in every speech.
Only the best actors get top billing in Hollywood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star billing - being listed as a star performer in credits

Etymology

From 'top' (highest position) + 'billing' (listing of performers); originates in show business terminology.
