uniocular dichromat

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːniˈɑkjələr daɪˈkroʊmæt / (AmE), / ˌjuːniˈɒkjʊlər daɪˈkrəʊmæt / (BrE)
a person who has normal color vision in one eye and dichromacy in the other, a rare condition valuable for color vision research
person with different color vision in each eye
medical

Examples

A uniocular dichromat provides unique insights for experiments studying human color perception.
The study recruited a rare uniocular dichromat to compare visual responses between each eye.
Because a uniocular dichromat has different color processing in each eye, their experiences are invaluable to researchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dichromat - a person or animal with only two functional types of color receptors

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'ocular' (eye) and Greek 'di-' (two) + 'chroma' (color).
