telegraph post

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌɡræf poʊst / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf pəʊst / (BrE)
a tall upright pole used to support telegraph or telephone wires above the ground
pole supporting telegraph wires

Examples

The old telegraph post in front of the house was covered in ivy.
Many rural roads still have wooden telegraph posts lining their edges.
A bird perched on the top of the telegraph post, scanning the fields below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utility pole - a pole supporting electrical or communication cables
  • Degree Lower: lamp post - a post supporting a street lamp

Etymology

From 'telegraph', referring to the electrical communication system, and 'post', meaning a long, upright support.
