Epistle to Philemon

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl tə faɪˈliːmən / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tə faɪˈliːmən / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament consisting of an epistle from Saint Paul to Philemon, appealing for the forgiveness of a runaway slave
book of the New Testament; letter from Paul to Philemon

Examples

The Epistle to Philemon is one of the shortest books in the New Testament.
Many scholars analyze the Epistle to Philemon for its message on forgiveness and reconciliation.
During Bible study, we discussed themes from the Epistle to Philemon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible
  • Synonym: Pauline epistles - epistles in the New Testament attributed to Saint Paul

Etymology

Epistle from Greek *epistolē* (letter), Philemon from Greek *Philēmōn*.
