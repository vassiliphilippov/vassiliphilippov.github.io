communications protocol

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃənz ˈproʊtəˌkɑl / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃənz ˈprəʊtəkɒl / (BrE)
a set of rules governing the format and transmission of data between devices in a computer network
rules for data transmission in computer networks
medical

Examples

The internet relies on a standardized communications protocol to ensure data is sent and received accurately.
Developers must adhere to the established communications protocol when designing new network software.
Without a consistent communications protocol, two computers may not be able to exchange information correctly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protocol - an official procedure or system of rules

Etymology

From 'communication' (Latin communicare, to share) and 'protocol' (from French protocole, official record).
