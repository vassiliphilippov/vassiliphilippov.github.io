skeleton key

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛlətən ki / (AmE), / ˈskɛlətən kiː / (BrE)
a key designed to open many different locks by having most of its bit filed away
key that opens many locks

Examples

The thief used a skeleton key to quietly unlock the old wooden door.
She found a rusty skeleton key hidden behind a loose brick in the cellar.
A skeleton key can sometimes open several antique locks of similar make.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master key - a key designed to open a set of several locks
