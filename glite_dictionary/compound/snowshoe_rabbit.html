snowshoe rabbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌʃu ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌʃuː ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a large-footed North American hare that turns white in winter for camouflage; also called the snowshoe hare
large-footed North American hare; white in winter

Examples

The snowshoe rabbit is known for its large hind feet, which help it move across deep snow.
In winter, the fur of the snowshoe rabbit changes to white to blend with its snowy surroundings.
Lynx prey primarily on the snowshoe rabbit in the boreal forests of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowshoe hare - another common name for the same animal
  • Degree Lower: rabbit - small burrowing mammal of the family Leporidae
  • Synonym: hare - fast-running, long-eared mammal related to rabbits

Etymology

Named for its large feet that resemble snowshoes; also known as the snowshoe hare (Lepus americanus).
