arroz con pollo

1. NOUN

/ əˌroʊs kɔn ˈpoʊjoʊ / (AmE), / əˌrɒs kɒn ˈpɒljəʊ / (BrE)
A Spanish or Latin American dish of rice and chicken cooked together and highly seasoned, often with saffron.
Spanish rice and chicken dish

Examples

She ordered arroz con pollo at the restaurant, savoring the blend of aromatic spices.
The chef demonstrated how to prepare a traditional arroz con pollo using saffron and fresh vegetables.
My grandmother's recipe for arroz con pollo has been passed down through generations.

Etymology

From Spanish 'arroz' (rice) + 'con' (with) + 'pollo' (chicken).
