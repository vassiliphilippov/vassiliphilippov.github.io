star anise

1. NOUN

/ stɑr əˈnis / (AmE), / stɑː əˈniːs / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to Japan and Taiwan, whose star-shaped fruit is used as a spice; the flowers are not fragrant.
small tree; source of star-shaped spice

Examples

The dried fruit of the star anise is often used in Asian cooking.
A star anise tree can be identified by its glossy leaves and star-shaped fruit.
Despite its name, star anise blossoms have little to no fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Illicium verum - the spice-producing Chinese star anise plant

3. NOUN

/ stɑr əˈnis / (AmE), / stɑː əˈniːs / (BrE)
A star-shaped spice with a flavor similar to anise, used in Asian cooking and traditional medicine.
star-shaped spice used in Asian cuisine

Examples

Many Chinese recipes call for star anise to add a distinctive licorice-like flavor.
She bought a packet of star anise at the spice market to use in her soups.
Some herbal remedies use star anise for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anise - aromatic Mediterranean plant and its seeds, used as a spice

Etymology

From the resemblance of the spice to a star and the similarity in flavor to anise; from French 'anis étoilé'.
