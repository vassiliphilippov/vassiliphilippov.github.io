Hualpai language

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːlpaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlpaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Hualapai people of northwestern Arizona, United States.
language of the Hualapai people

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Hualpai language, preserving her cultural heritage.
The Hualpai language is being taught in schools to revitalize its use among younger generations.
Linguists are documenting the grammar and vocabulary of the Hualpai language for future study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico

Etymology

From Hualapai, the name of the indigenous people of northwestern Arizona.
