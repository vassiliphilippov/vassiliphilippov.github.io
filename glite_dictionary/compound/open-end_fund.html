open-end fund

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən ɛnd fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən end fʌnd / (BrE)
a type of investment company that continuously issues and redeems shares at their net asset value, allowing investors to buy or sell shares directly from the fund.
investment company issuing and redeeming shares

Examples

Many investors prefer open-end funds because they can redeem their shares at any time.
The net asset value of an open-end fund is calculated daily based on its holdings.
Unlike closed-end funds, open-end funds issue new shares as demand increases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual fund - an investment fund that pools money from many investors to purchase securities
