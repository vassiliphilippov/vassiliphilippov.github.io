English violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
A European species of violet, typically with purple to white flowers, widely grown and naturalized; commonly known as sweet violet.
a European violet species with purple or white flowers

Examples

The English violet produces fragrant flowers in early spring.
Gardeners often plant English violet for its lovely scent and ground cover.
She picked a bouquet of English violet from the woodland edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet violet - European violet with fragrant purple or white flowers
