Lane's Prince Albert

1. NOUN

/ leɪnz ˌprɪns ˈæl.bɚt / (AmE), / leɪnz ˌprɪns ˈæl.bət / (BrE)
a British variety of apple known for its use in cooking, characterized by its firm flesh and tart flavor
variety of cooking apple
british

Examples

The tart flavor of Lane's Prince Albert makes it perfect for apple pies.
Many traditional British recipes call for Lane's Prince Albert apples due to their firmness.
She planted a Lane's Prince Albert tree in her garden to enjoy home-grown cooking apples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bramley - another well-known British cooking apple

Etymology

Named after its English originator Thomas Squire Lane in the mid-19th century and after Prince Albert, husband of Queen Victoria.
