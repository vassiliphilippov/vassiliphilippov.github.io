purple locoweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈloʊ.kəˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ˈləʊ.kəˌwiːd / (BrE)
a tufted plant of the southwestern United States, known for its purple, pink, or white flowers, belonging to the genus Oxytropis and sometimes causing locoism in livestock
a plant with purple, pink, or white flowers found in the southwestern US

Examples

Many ranchers worry about their cattle grazing on purple locoweed during the spring.
The bright blossoms of purple locoweed add color to the otherwise arid landscape.
Scientists studied the toxic effects of purple locoweed on local livestock herds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locoweed - a general term for various poisonous plants of the genus Astragalus and Oxytropis
