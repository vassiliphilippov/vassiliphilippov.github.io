sugar beet

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ ˌbit / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ˌbiːt / (BrE)
a variety of beet with a large, sweet, white root grown commercially for the extraction of sugar
beet grown for sugar production

Examples

The farmer planted acres of sugar beet to supply the local refinery.
Europe produces much of its sugar from sugar beet rather than sugar cane.
After harvesting the sugar beet, the roots are processed to extract sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - a plant with a thick root, typically used as food or for sugar

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɡɚ biːt / (AmE), / ˈsuːɡə biːt / (BrE)
A white-rooted variety of beet cultivated for its high sugar content, used as a source of commercial sugar.
plant grown for sugar extraction

Examples

Farmers in the region grow sugar beet as a major cash crop.
The factory processes thousands of tons of sugar beet each year.
Sugar beet requires rich soil and plenty of water to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - a root vegetable, Beta vulgaris, with several cultivated varieties
