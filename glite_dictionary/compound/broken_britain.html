Broken Britain

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkən ˈbrɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən ˈbrɪtn̩ / (BrE)
a phrase describing the perceived state of serious social and economic problems in the United Kingdom, especially relating to issues such as crime, poverty, and moral decline
perceived state of UK social and economic problems
british

Examples

Many newspapers have run stories about the rise of crime in Broken Britain.
The opposition leader blamed government policies for the problems facing Broken Britain.
Public debates frequently reference Broken Britain when discussing youth unemployment and social unrest.

Synset Examples

The report highlighted issues contributing to Broken Britain.
Politicians often discuss solutions for Broken Britain.
Many debate the causes of a so-called Broken Britain.

Etymology

Phrase popularised in British media and politics in the late 2000s.
