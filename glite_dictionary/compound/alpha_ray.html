alpha ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˌreɪ / (BrE)
a stream of alpha particles emitted during the radioactive decay of certain substances
stream of alpha particles from radioactive decay

Examples

The radioactive element released an alpha ray detected by the laboratory equipment.
Exposure to an alpha ray can be dangerous if the source is ingested or inhaled.
Early experiments in nuclear physics often studied the properties of the alpha ray.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beta ray - stream of beta particles emitted in radioactive decay
  • Degree Higher: gamma ray - electromagnetic radiation of high frequency emitted in radioactive decay

Etymology

From 'alpha', the first letter of the Greek alphabet, referring to the type of radiation, and 'ray', meaning a beam or stream.
