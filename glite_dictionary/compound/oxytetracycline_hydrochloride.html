oxytetracycline hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksitɛtrəˌsaɪklin haɪˌdrɑkləˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒksitɛtrəˌsaɪkliːn haɪˌdrɒkləˈraɪd / (BrE)
a yellow, crystalline antibiotic derived from a soil actinomycete, used to treat various bacterial and rickettsial infections
a crystalline antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed oxytetracycline hydrochloride for his respiratory tract infection.
Veterinarians often use oxytetracycline hydrochloride to treat infections in livestock.
The crystalline form of oxytetracycline hydrochloride is easily dissolved for intravenous administration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxytetracycline - an antibiotic of the tetracycline group
  • Degree Lower: tetracycline - a group of broad-spectrum antibiotics

Etymology

Oxytetracycline: from 'oxy-' (containing oxygen) + 'tetracycline' (from Greek 'tetra-' four + 'kyklos' circle), describing its four-ring structure; hydrochloride indicates the hydrochloride salt form.
