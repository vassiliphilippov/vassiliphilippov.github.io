information science

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈsaɪ.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈsaɪ.əns / (BrE)
The study of how information is collected, organized, stored, retrieved, and used, especially through technology.
study of managing and using information

Examples

She is pursuing a master's degree in information science to learn about data management.
Advances in information science have transformed how we search for and process knowledge.
The conference brought together experts in information science from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library science - the study of library organization, management, and information resources
