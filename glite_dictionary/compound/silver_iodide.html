silver iodide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈaɪəˌdaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈaɪədaɪd / (BrE)
a yellowish inorganic compound used in photography, cloud seeding, and medical applications
compound used in photography and cloud seeding
medical

Examples

Scientists released silver iodide into the clouds in hopes of producing rain.
Photographic films once made extensive use of silver iodide for capturing images.
The meteorologist explained how silver iodide helps stimulate precipitation during dry spells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium iodide - a compound used as a source of iodine in medicine and chemistry

Etymology

Formed from 'silver' and 'iodide' (the ion of iodine); used since the 19th century in scientific contexts.
