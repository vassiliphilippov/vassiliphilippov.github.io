coax cable

1. NOUN

/ koʊ.æks ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / kəʊ.æks ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
A type of electrical cable used to transmit high-frequency signals, consisting of a central conductor, insulating layer, metallic shield, and outer insulating layer.
high-frequency electrical transmission cable

Examples

The television in the living room is connected to the antenna with a coax cable.
To get a clearer signal, try replacing the old wire with a new coax cable.
Many internet modems still use a coax cable to connect to the wall outlet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coaxial cable - A transmission line for high-frequency signals consisting of an inner conductor and an outer shield

Etymology

Short for 'coaxial cable', from 'coaxial' meaning sharing a common axis, describing its structure.
