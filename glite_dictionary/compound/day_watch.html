day watch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ wɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ wɒtʃ / (BrE)
a group of workers assigned to work during the daytime, typically covering the shift from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m.
workers on a daytime shift

Examples

The day watch reported several unusual incidents during their shift.
She was assigned to the day watch for the next two weeks.
After the day watch ended, the evening crew took over.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: night watch - workers assigned to work during the nighttime
  • Antonym: night shift - group of workers who work during the night hours
