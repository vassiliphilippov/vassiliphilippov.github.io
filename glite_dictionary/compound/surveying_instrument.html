surveying instrument

1. NOUN

/ sərˈveɪɪŋ ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / sɜːˈveɪɪŋ ˈɪnstrəmənt / (BrE)
an instrument used by surveyors to measure distances, angles, and elevations for mapping or land assessment
tool for measuring distances and angles in surveying

Examples

A surveying instrument is essential for accurately mapping out construction sites.
Modern surveying instruments use laser technology for greater precision.
Before starting, the team calibrated each surveying instrument to ensure reliable results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theodolite - a precision surveying instrument for measuring angles
  • Synonym: transit - a type of surveying instrument for measuring horizontal and vertical angles
