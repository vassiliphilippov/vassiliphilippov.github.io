countersink bit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌsɪŋk bɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsɪŋk bɪt / (BrE)
A drill bit designed to enlarge the upper part of a hole so that the head of a screw or bolt can sit flush with or below the surface.
tool for enlarging a hole's upper part

Examples

He used a countersink bit to create space for the screw heads to sit flush with the wood.
Before attaching the hinge, drill each hole with a countersink bit to prevent splintering.
The carpenter replaced his old countersink bit after noticing it was dulling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countersink - conical hole cut to allow a screw or bolt head to sit flush

Etymology

From 'countersink' (to make a conical hole) + 'bit' (cutting tool attachment).
