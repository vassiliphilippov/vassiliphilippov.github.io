to broach someone's concerns

1. VERB

/ broʊtʃ ˈsʌmwʌnz kənˈsɝnz / (AmE), / brəʊtʃ ˈsʌmwənz kənˈsɜːnz / (BrE)
to introduce or raise one's worries or issues for discussion with others
bring up worries for discussion

Examples

She was unsure how to broach her concerns with the rest of the team.
It's important to broach someone's concerns early in a project to avoid misunderstandings.
After thinking it over, he finally broached his concerns to his supervisor.

Synset Examples

He decided to broach his concerns after lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise concerns - to express worries or issues for discussion
  • Degree Higher: address concerns - to deal with or give attention to worries or issues
