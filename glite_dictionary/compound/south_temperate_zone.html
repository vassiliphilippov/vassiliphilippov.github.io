South Temperate Zone

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ ˈtɛmpərət zoʊn / (AmE), / saʊθ ˈtɛmpərət zəʊn / (BrE)
The temperate zone of the Earth located between the Antarctic Circle and the Tropic of Capricorn in the Southern Hemisphere.
temperate region south of Tropic of Capricorn

Examples

The South Temperate Zone experiences mild summers and cool winters due to its location between major latitudinal lines.
Many species of penguins inhabit the South Temperate Zone, adapting to the moderate climate.
Australia's southern coastline lies within the South Temperate Zone, resulting in varied seasonal weather.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North Temperate Zone - the temperate zone between the Arctic Circle and the Tropic of Cancer
