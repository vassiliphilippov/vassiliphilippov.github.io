bluggy day

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌɡi deɪ / (AmE), / ˈblʌɡi deɪ / (BrE)
A dull, dreary day characterized by wet and muddy weather.
dull, wet, and muddy day

Examples

It's a bluggy day, perfect for curling up with a book.
After a week of bluggy days, the sun finally came out.
Farmers often struggle to work outside during a bluggy day.

Synset Examples

It's a bluggy day, perfect for staying indoors.
He complained about the bluggy weather ruining his plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreary day - a dull, bleak, and lifeless day
  • Antonym: sunny day - a day with plenty of sunshine
