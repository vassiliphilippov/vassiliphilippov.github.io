split-half correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsplɪt ˈhæf ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsplɪt ˈhɑːf ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical measure of reliability calculated by correlating scores on two halves of a test
test reliability correlation method

Examples

The researcher used a split-half correlation to estimate the test's internal consistency.
A high split-half correlation suggests that the exam is reliably measuring the same construct.
When the split-half correlation is low, the test may not be consistent across its items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliability - the degree to which a test yields consistent results
