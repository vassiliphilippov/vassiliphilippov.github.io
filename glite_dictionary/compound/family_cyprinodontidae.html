family Cyprinodontidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌprɪnoʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌprɪnədəʊnˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of small soft-finned fishes, including killifishes, flagfishes, swordtails, and guppies
family of small soft-finned fishes

Examples

The family Cyprinodontidae includes many species commonly kept in home aquariums.
Members of the family Cyprinodontidae are found in freshwater and brackish habitats worldwide.
Research on the family Cyprinodontidae has provided insights into fish adaptation and evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin Cyprinodontidae, based on Cyprinodon (a genus name) + -idae (family suffix).
