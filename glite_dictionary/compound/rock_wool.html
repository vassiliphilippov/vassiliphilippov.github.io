rock wool

1. NOUN

/ rɑk wʊl / (AmE), / rɒk wʊl / (BrE)
A light, fibrous material made from molten rock, used especially as an insulator in buildings and industry.
light fibrous insulating material

Examples

The attic was lined with rock wool to keep the house warm in winter.
Workers wore masks while installing rock wool insulation in the new building.
Unlike fiberglass, rock wool can withstand higher temperatures, making it ideal for fireproofing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mineral wool - a general term for fibrous insulating materials made from minerals or rocks
