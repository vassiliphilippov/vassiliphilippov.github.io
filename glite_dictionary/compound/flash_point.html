flash point

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈflæʃ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the lowest temperature at which the vapor of a combustible liquid can be ignited in air
minimum ignition temperature of a liquid's vapor

Examples

The flash point of gasoline is much lower than that of diesel fuel.
Always keep materials with a low flash point away from open flames.
Before handling chemicals, check their flash point to ensure proper storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition temperature - the minimum temperature at which a substance catches fire

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈflæʃ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the moment at which a situation is ready to erupt into violence, conflict, or crisis; a critical or volatile stage
critical point before eruption or crisis

Examples

Tensions in the city reached a flash point after weeks of protests.
The debate escalated quickly, nearing its flash point when tempers flared.
This neighborhood has long been considered a flash point for political unrest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boiling point - the stage at which someone loses control of their emotions; an extreme limit

Etymology

Originally from chemistry, where it denotes the temperature at which a substance ignites; figurative use dates from the late 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈflæʃ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a critical moment at which a situation suddenly becomes more serious or intense
critical moment of escalation or change

Examples

The city reached a flash point after weeks of mounting tension between communities.
The controversial law became the flash point for nationwide demonstrations.
Negotiations broke down, and the situation hit a flash point overnight.

Synset Examples

The incident was the flash point for protests.
Tensions hit a flash point after the announcement.
The debate reached a flash point last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a moment when a decisive change occurs
  • Synonym: breaking point - the moment at which someone or something gives way under strain

Etymology

Originally a technical term for the temperature at which a substance ignites; later used figuratively in English.
