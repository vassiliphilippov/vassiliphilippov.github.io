lambent light

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmbənt laɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæmbənt laɪt / (BrE)
A softly glowing or gently flickering light, often with a delicate or wavering quality.
softly glowing or flickering light

Examples

The lambent light from the fireplace gave the room a warm, inviting glow.
Moonbeams cast a lambent light on the quiet garden.
He watched the lambent light reflect off the gentle ripples of the lake.

Synset Examples

The lambent light danced across the water's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow - a steady radiance of light or heat
  • Synonym: gleam - a brief or faint appearance of light

Etymology

From Latin 'lambere' (to lick), referring to light that seems to 'lick' a surface.
