Mojave aster

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi ˈæstər / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi ˈastə / (BrE)
A wild aster with greyish leafy stems and narrow pale lavender or violet flowers, native to rocky desert slopes from California to Arizona and Utah.
desert wildflower of western US

Examples

On our hike, we spotted a beautiful Mojave aster blooming among the desert rocks.
The delicate petals of the Mojave aster stand out against the arid landscape.
Gardeners value the Mojave aster for its resilience to drought and striking purple flowers.
