flame cell

1. NOUN

/ fleɪm sɛl / (AmE), / fleɪm sɛl / (BrE)
a specialized excretory cell in flatworms that functions to remove waste materials
excretory cell in flatworms

Examples

The planarian's flame cells help regulate water balance in its body.
Scientists studied the function of the flame cell in waste removal.
Flatworms rely on flame cells as a primitive excretory system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protonephridium - a network of tubules with ciliated cells for waste removal in invertebrates
