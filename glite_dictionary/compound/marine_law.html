marine law

1. NOUN

/ məˈrin lɔ / (AmE), / məˈriːn lɔː / (BrE)
The branch of law that governs activities and disputes related to territorial and international waters, shipping, and ocean resources.
law relating to the sea and shipping
legal

Examples

The firm specializes in marine law, representing clients involved in international shipping disputes.
She developed an interest in marine law after studying the regulations that protect ocean fisheries.
New graduates in marine law are in high demand due to the complexities of international trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maritime law - the branch of law dealing with shipping and navigation on seas and oceans
