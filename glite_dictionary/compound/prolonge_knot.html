prolonge knot

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈlɔndʒ nɒt / (AmE), / prəˈlɒndʒ nɒt / (BrE)
A knot tied in a rope for attaching and dragging a gun carriage.
knot used to drag a gun carriage

Examples

The artillery crew quickly tied a prolonge knot to secure the cannon for transport.
Learning to tie a prolonge knot was essential for 19th-century gunners.
Without a proper prolonge knot, the rope might slip while dragging the heavy carriage.

Etymology

From French 'prolonge', meaning an extended rope; military terminology from artillery usage.
