Carolingian dynasty

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈlɪndʒiən ˈdaɪnəsti / (AmE), / ˌkarəˈlɪndʒiən ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
the Frankish royal family founded by Charlemagne's father that ruled much of Western Europe from 751 to 987
Frankish royal family ruling 751–987

Examples

The Carolingian dynasty played a crucial role in shaping medieval Europe.
Scholars often debate the legacy of the Carolingian dynasty in arts and administration.
After the fall of the Carolingian dynasty, France entered a period of significant political change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Merovingian dynasty - Frankish dynasty preceding the Carolingians
  • Antonym: Capetian dynasty - French royal dynasty succeeding the Carolingians

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Carolus (Charles), referencing Charlemagne, plus 'dynasty'.
