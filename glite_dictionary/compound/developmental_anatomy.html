developmental anatomy

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌvɛləpˈmɛntl əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / dɪˌvɛləpˈmɛntl əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
the branch of anatomy concerned with the structural changes in an organism from fertilization to maturity
study of organismal structural changes from fertilization to maturity
medical

Examples

She specializes in developmental anatomy, focusing on the growth of organs before birth.
The textbook on developmental anatomy helped me understand how humans form from a single cell.
Advances in developmental anatomy have improved our understanding of congenital disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embryology - the branch of biology studying embryos and their development

Etymology

From 'development' (growth, evolution) + 'anatomy' (study of structure), both from Greek/Latin roots.
