mountain viscacha

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən vɪˈskɑːtʃə / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn vɪˈskɑːtʃə / (BrE)
a rodent native to the mountainous regions of Chile and Peru, sometimes bred in captivity
South American mountain rodent

Examples

The mountain viscacha is well adapted to the rocky slopes of the Andes.
Researchers studied the diet of mountain viscachas living at high altitude.
Some zoos have begun breeding mountain viscachas to help conserve the species.

Etymology

From Spanish 'vizcacha', originally from Quechua 'wisk’acha'.
