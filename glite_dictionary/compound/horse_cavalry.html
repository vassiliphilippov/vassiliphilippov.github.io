horse cavalry

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈkævəlri / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈkævəlri / (BrE)
soldiers who are trained to fight while mounted on horses
mounted troops; soldiers on horseback

Examples

The horse cavalry played a crucial role in the battle by flanking the enemy's infantry.
Until the early twentieth century, many armies maintained large units of horse cavalry.
After the invention of tanks, traditional horse cavalry became largely obsolete in modern warfare.

  • Synonym: cavalry - troops trained to fight on horseback

2. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈkævəlri / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈkævəlri / (BrE)
an army unit consisting of soldiers mounted on horseback, used especially before the widespread use of vehicles
military unit of mounted soldiers

Examples

During the nineteenth century, horse cavalry played a decisive role in many battles.
The general ordered the horse cavalry to flank the enemy's position at dawn.
Modern armies rarely use horse cavalry, having replaced them with armored vehicles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavalry - soldiers who fight on horseback
  • Antonym: infantry - soldiers who fight on foot
