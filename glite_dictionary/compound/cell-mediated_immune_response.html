cell-mediated immune response

1. NOUN

/ sɛl ˌmiːdiˌeɪtɪd ɪˈmjun rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / sɛl ˌmiːdɪˌeɪtɪd ɪˈmjuːn rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
an immune response mainly involving T cells, rather than antibodies, which acts chiefly against viral or fungal infections or transplanted tissues
immune response involving T cells
medical

Examples

The cell-mediated immune response is essential for combating viral infections that evade antibodies.
Transplanted organs can be rejected due to the body's cell-mediated immune response.
Certain diseases impair the cell-mediated immune response, making patients more susceptible to infections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: humoral immune response - an immune response involving antibodies in body fluids
