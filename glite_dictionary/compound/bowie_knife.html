Bowie knife

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.i ˌnaɪf / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.i ˌnaɪf / (BrE)
a large, heavy hunting knife with a single-edged blade, typically with a crossguard
large single-edged hunting knife

Examples

The collector displayed his prized Bowie knife with its distinctive curved blade.
While camping, he used a Bowie knife to cut firewood and prepare food.
The legend of the Bowie knife began at the Alamo, where it was famously wielded in battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunting knife - a knife used primarily for preparing game

Etymology

Named after James Bowie (1796–1836), an American pioneer and soldier.
