staccato beat

1. NOUN

/ stəˈkɑtoʊ bit / (AmE), / stəˈkɑːtəʊ biːt / (BrE)
A musical rhythm marked by short, sharply separated sounds or notes.
rhythm with short, detached sounds

Examples

The drums played a staccato beat that echoed through the hall.
She tapped her fingers on the table in a staccato beat while waiting.
The composer's new piece features a distinct staccato beat throughout the melody.

Synset Examples

The music had a staccato beat that made everyone dance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legato - a smooth, connected musical style

Etymology

Italian 'staccato' meaning 'detached'.
