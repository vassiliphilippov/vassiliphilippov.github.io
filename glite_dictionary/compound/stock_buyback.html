stock buyback

1. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈbaɪˌbæk / (AmE), / stɒk ˈbaɪˌbæk / (BrE)
an act by which a corporation repurchases its own outstanding shares, often to increase share value or reduce the likelihood of a takeover
repurchase of a company's own stock

Examples

The company announced a stock buyback program to return value to its shareholders.
After the stock buyback, the share price rose significantly.
Some investors view a stock buyback as a sign of management's confidence in the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: share repurchase - the act of a company buying back its own shares
