building block

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪldɪŋ blɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɪldɪŋ blɒk / (BrE)
a basic component or element from which something is constructed or developed, especially in a natural or scientific context
basic component or element

Examples

Proteins are formed from smaller building blocks called amino acids.
DNA is made up of four chemical building blocks arranged in different sequences.
A solid education is often described as a building block for future success.

Synset Examples

units of nucleic acids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪl.dɪŋ blɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.dɪŋ blɒk / (BrE)
a block, usually made of stone, concrete, or a similar material, that is used in the construction of buildings
block used in construction

Examples

The children used a building block to stack up a tall tower.
Each building block in the wall was carefully put in place.
Modern homes are often constructed from prefabricated building blocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brick - rectangular block of clay used in building

/ ˈbɪldɪŋ ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɪldɪŋ ˌblɒk / (BrE)

fundamental component or basis

Examples

Integrity is regarded as a key building block of strong leadership.
Effective communication acts as a vital building block in developing a positive workplace culture.
