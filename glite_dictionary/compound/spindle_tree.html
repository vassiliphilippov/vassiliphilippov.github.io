spindle tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪndl̩ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈspɪndl ˌtriː / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Euonymus, with showy, usually reddish berries, often grown for ornament or found in woodland
woody shrub or tree of genus Euonymus

Examples

The spindle tree produces vibrant pink and orange berries in the autumn.
Gardeners often plant the spindle tree for its attractive foliage and seeds.
A wild spindle tree was growing along the edge of the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euonymus - the genus of shrubs and small trees known as spindle trees

Etymology

Named for its traditional use; the hard wood was used to make spindles for spinning wool.
