mute swan

1. NOUN

/ mjut swɑn / (AmE), / mjuːt swɒn / (BrE)
A large Eurasian swan (Cygnus olor), known for being less vocal than other swans and often domesticated.
a large Eurasian swan species, less vocal

Examples

The mute swan glided gracefully across the pond, barely making a sound.
Lake Windermere is home to several families of mute swans year-round.
A pair of mute swans built their nest near the riverbank last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swan - a large long-necked waterbird
