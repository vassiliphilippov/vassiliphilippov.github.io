blow gas

1. NOUN

/ bloʊ ɡæs / (AmE), / bləʊ ɡas / (BrE)
the gas emitted from a generator during a blow period, typically in industrial or engineering settings
gas released from a generator during blowing

Examples

The engineer measured the temperature of the blow gas during the maintenance check.
Excess blow gas must be vented safely to prevent accidents in the plant.
Analysis of the blow gas can reveal important information about the generator's efficiency.
