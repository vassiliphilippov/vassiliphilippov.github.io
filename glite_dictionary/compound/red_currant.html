red currant

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈkɜr.ənt / (AmE), / rɛd ˈkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
a small, sour, bright red berry produced by a shrub of the genus Ribes, commonly used in jams, jellies, and desserts
a small tart red berry used in cooking

Examples

The recipe called for a cup of fresh red currants to add tartness to the sauce.
She spread red currant jelly on her morning toast for a burst of flavor.
Baskets of red currants were on display at the farmers' market in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black currant - a similar berry that is black in color and also used in cooking

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / rɛd ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a small, round, red edible berry that grows on a shrub native to Europe, or the plant itself, often cultivated for its fruit
a small edible red berry or the plant it grows on

Examples

The tart flavor of red currant makes it ideal for jams and jellies.
She decorated the dessert with a handful of fresh red currant.
Many gardeners grow red currant bushes for their attractive berries and foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black currant - a similar berry, but black and with a different flavor profile
  • Synonym: gooseberry - a related shrub with green or purple edible berries
  • Synonym: white currant - a variety of currant with white berries
