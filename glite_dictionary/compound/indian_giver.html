Indian giver

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˌɡɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˌɡɪvə / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person who gives a gift and later asks for it back.
offensive term for someone who asks for a gift back
offensive

Examples

He called me an Indian giver when I asked for the borrowed book back.
The phrase Indian giver is often considered hurtful and outdated.
Don't be an Indian giver; a gift should not come with strings attached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promise breaker - a person who does not keep their promises

Etymology

From a stereotype attached to interactions between Native Americans and European settlers; now widely recognized as offensive and inaccurate.
