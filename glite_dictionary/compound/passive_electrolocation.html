passive electrolocation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv ɪˌlɛktroʊloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv ɪˌlɛktrəʊləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The ability of certain fish and aquatic amphibians to detect and locate external electric fields in their environment using specialized electroreceptors.
detection of external electric fields by aquatic animals

Examples

Weakly electric fish use passive electrolocation to sense the presence of other animals in the water.
Some amphibians rely on passive electrolocation because they cannot generate electric fields themselves.
The study investigated how environmental noise affects passive electrolocation in catfish.

Synset Examples

Weakly electric fish use their electrosensory systems for electrocommunication, active electrolocation and low-frequency passive electrolocation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active electrolocation - generation and detection of self-produced electric fields

Etymology

Formed from 'passive' and 'electrolocation,' the latter from 'electro-' (relating to electricity) and 'location.'
