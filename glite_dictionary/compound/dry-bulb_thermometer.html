dry-bulb thermometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ bʌlb θərˈmɑːmɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ bʌlb θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A thermometer with an unmoistened bulb, used to measure the temperature of the air.
thermometer measuring air temperature

Examples

The technician checked the room temperature using a dry-bulb thermometer placed near the vent.
In weather stations, both a dry-bulb thermometer and a wet-bulb thermometer are used for humidity readings.
A dry-bulb thermometer only measures the air temperature, without considering moisture in the air.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wet-bulb thermometer - A thermometer with a moistened bulb used alongside a dry-bulb to measure humidity
