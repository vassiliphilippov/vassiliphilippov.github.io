methylene group

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.əˌliːn ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈmiːθɪliːn ɡruːp / (BrE)
a bivalent group in organic chemistry with the formula –CH₂–, derived from methane, and linking two atoms or groups in a molecule
bivalent group –CH₂– in chemistry
medical

Examples

The methylene group plays a crucial role in the compound's reactivity.
In this reaction, a hydrogen atom is replaced by a methylene group.
Chemists often refer to the methylene group when discussing structural formulas in organic molecules.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: methyl group - the monovalent group –CH₃
  • Synonym: methylene - the group –CH₂– or the compound CH₂

Etymology

From Greek 'methy' (wine) + 'hyle' (wood, material), underlying the word 'methane', plus 'group'.
