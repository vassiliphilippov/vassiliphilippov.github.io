business school

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪznəs skuːl / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪs skuːl / (BrE)
a school or part of a university that offers courses in business, management, and related subjects, often leading to graduate degrees such as an MBA
school offering business and management degrees

Examples

She graduated from a prestigious business school in New York.
Many executives choose to return to business school to improve their management skills.
Getting into a top business school requires a strong academic and professional background.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MBA - Master of Business Administration, a graduate degree offered by business schools
