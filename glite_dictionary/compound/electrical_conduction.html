electrical conduction

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl kənˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl kənˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the passage of an electric current through a conductor, such as a metal wire or electrolyte
passage of electricity through a conductor

Examples

Copper is commonly used in wires because of its excellent electrical conduction properties.
The experiment measured how temperature affects electrical conduction in various materials.
Saltwater allows electrical conduction, making it unsafe to use electronic devices near it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conductivity - the ability of a material to conduct electricity
