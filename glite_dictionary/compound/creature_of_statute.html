creature of statute

1. NOUN

/ ˈkritʃɚ əv ˈstætʃuːt / (AmE), / ˈkriːtʃə əv ˈstætjuːt / (BrE)
an entity, such as a corporation or body, that exists because it has been created or defined by law
entity created by law
legal

Examples

A corporation is a creature of statute and can act only as permitted by law.
Many government agencies are established as creatures of statute.
The non-profit organization is a creature of statute, with its powers detailed in the relevant act.

Synset Examples
