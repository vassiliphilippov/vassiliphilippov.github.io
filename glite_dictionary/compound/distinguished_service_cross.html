Distinguished Service Cross

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt ˈsɜrvɪs krɔs / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt ˈsɜːvɪs krɒs / (BrE)
A United States Army decoration awarded for extraordinary heroism in combat against an armed enemy.
US Army decoration for extraordinary heroism

Examples

He was awarded the Distinguished Service Cross for his actions during the battle.
Only a handful of soldiers have ever received both the Medal of Honor and the Distinguished Service Cross.
Her grandfather’s Distinguished Service Cross is proudly displayed in the family’s living room.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Medal of Honor - highest US military decoration for valor in action
  • Degree Lower: Silver Star - third-highest military combat decoration for valor in the US armed forces
