modulus of rigidity

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.djʊ.ləs əv rɪˈdɪd.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.jʊ.ləs əv rɪˈdɪd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the measure of a material's ability to resist deformation under a shearing force; the ratio of shear stress to shear strain
measure of resistance to shearing (physics)

Examples

The modulus of rigidity for steel is much greater than for rubber.
Engineers must consider the modulus of rigidity when designing bridges.
An increase in temperature can decrease the modulus of rigidity of certain materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shear modulus - another term for modulus of rigidity, commonly used in physics
  • Degree Lower: Young's modulus - measure of a material's elastic properties under tensile stress (stretching)

Etymology

From Latin modulus (small measure) and rigidus (stiff, hard), used in physics to denote an elasticity constant.
