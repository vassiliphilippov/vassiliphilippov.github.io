sharecrop farmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛrˌkrɑp ˈfɑrmər / (AmE), / ˈʃeəkrɒp ˈfɑːmə / (BrE)
A farmer, especially a tenant, who cultivates land owned by someone else in return for a share of the crops produced.
farmer who works land for a share of crops

Examples

After the Civil War, many former slaves became sharecrop farmers in the southern United States.
As a sharecrop farmer, he struggled to earn enough to support his family.
The community consisted mostly of sharecrop farmers and their families during the 1930s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharecropper - person who farms rented land for a share of the crop

Etymology

From 'sharecrop' (from 'share' and 'crop') + 'farmer'; refers to the agricultural system of sharecropping.
