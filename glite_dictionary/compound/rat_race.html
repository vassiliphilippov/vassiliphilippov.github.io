rat race

1. NOUN

/ ˈræt ˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˈræt ˌreɪs / (BrE)
A way of life in which people are caught up in a fiercely competitive struggle for wealth or power, leading to an exhausting routine with little time for relaxation.
exhausting, competitive routine or lifestyle

Examples

After twenty years in the rat race, he finally decided to move to the countryside.
Many people dream of escaping the rat race and finding a slower pace of life.
She was tired of the daily rat race and longed for more meaningful work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grind - hard, monotonous routine of work
  • Antonym: escape the rat race - to leave an exhausting, competitive routine

Etymology

Alludes to the frenzied, aimless competition of laboratory rats racing through mazes, first attested in the mid-20th century.
