common ax

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən æks / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən æks / (BrE)
an axe with a long handle and a head that has one cutting edge and one blunt side, typically used for chopping wood
axe with one cutting edge and one blunt side

Examples

The lumberjack grabbed his common ax to cut down the old pine tree.
A common ax is designed for versatility, making it a staple in many tool sheds.
He swung the common ax with practiced skill, splitting the log cleanly in half.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axe - a tool with a heavy bladed head mounted across a handle for chopping
