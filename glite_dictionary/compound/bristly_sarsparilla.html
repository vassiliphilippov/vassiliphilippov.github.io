bristly sarsparilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪsli sɑːrspəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsli sɑːspəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A bristly herb native to eastern and central North America, known for its black fruit and medicinal bark.
North American herb with black fruit and medicinal bark

Examples

The bristly sarsparilla is often found growing along forest edges in the eastern United States.
Many indigenous peoples used the roots of bristly sarsparilla in traditional medicine.
In the summer, the bristly sarsparilla produces small clusters of black berries.
