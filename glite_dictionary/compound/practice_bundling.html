to practice bundling

1. VERB

/ ˈpræktɪs ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪs ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (BrE)
To sleep fully clothed in the same bed with one's betrothed, as part of a historical courtship custom.
historical courtship custom involving shared bed
archaic

Examples

In colonial America, some couples would practice bundling as part of their courtship ritual.
Historians have noted that families sometimes encouraged their children to practice bundling for propriety's sake.
She was surprised to learn that her ancestors once practiced bundling before marriage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bundling - the practice of sleeping clothed with a potential spouse

Etymology

From 'practice' + 'bundling', referring to the custom; 'bundling' comes from Dutch 'bondel' (bundle).
