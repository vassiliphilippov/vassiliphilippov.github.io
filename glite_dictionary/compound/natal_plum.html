natal plum

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.t̬l plʌm / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.təl plʌm / (BrE)
a large, closely branched shrub native to South Africa, with forked bright green spines, shiny leaves, and edible fruit
South African shrub with edible fruit

Examples

The natal plum is often grown as an ornamental hedge in warm climates.
She tasted the sweet, red fruit of the natal plum for the first time on her trip to South Africa.
The natal plum produces fragrant white flowers before setting its characteristic fruit.

Etymology

Named for the Natal region of South Africa, combined with 'plum' due to the fruit's resemblance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.tl plʌm / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.təl plʌm / (BrE)
a South African evergreen shrub (Carissa macrocarpa) with forked spines and edible, plumlike red fruit, often used as a hedge plant
South African shrub with plumlike fruit

Examples

The natal plum is often chosen for its dense, spiny foliage as a protective hedge in gardens.
Many gardeners appreciate the sweet flavor of the natal plum fruit when it ripens deep red.
A row of natal plum shrubs lines the walkway, attracting birds with their colorful fruits.

Etymology

Named after the Natal region in South Africa, where the plant originates, and 'plum' for its fruit's appearance.

3. NOUN

/ ˈneɪt̬l plʌm / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.təl plʌm / (BrE)
An edible, scarlet, plumlike fruit of a South African shrub, often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
edible, scarlet fruit of a South African shrub

Examples

The natal plum has a sweet, cranberry-like flavor when fully ripe.
They planted a natal plum hedge along the garden path for both its fruit and flowers.
Birds are attracted to the bright red natal plum growing on the shrub outside our window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carissa - the South African shrub that bears the natal plum

Etymology

From Natal, a region in South Africa, where the plant is native, and 'plum' for its resemblance to the fruit.
