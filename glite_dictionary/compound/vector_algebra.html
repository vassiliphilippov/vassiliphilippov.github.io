vector algebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛktər ˈælʤəbrə / (AmE), / ˈvɛktə ˈælʤəbrə / (BrE)
the branch of algebra concerned with the study of vectors and vector spaces, including their operations and properties
algebra of vectors and vector spaces

Examples

In physics, vector algebra is essential for describing motion and forces.
The fundamentals of vector algebra are taught in introductory mathematics courses.
Understanding vector algebra helps engineers solve problems in multiple dimensions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linear algebra - the branch of mathematics concerning vector spaces and linear mappings

Etymology

From Latin 'vector' (carrier) and Arabic 'al-jabr' (reunion of broken parts, the origin of 'algebra').
