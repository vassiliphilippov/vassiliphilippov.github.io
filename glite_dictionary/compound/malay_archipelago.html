Malay Archipelago

1. PROPN

/ məˈleɪ ˌɑːrtʃɪˈpɛləˌgoʊ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ ˌɑːtʃɪˈpɛlɪgəʊ / (BrE)
a group of islands in Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, lying between the Asian mainland and Australia
group of islands in Southeast Asia, between Asia and Australia

Examples

The Malay Archipelago contains thousands of islands with diverse cultures and languages.
Charles Darwin studied the wildlife of the Malay Archipelago during his voyage.
The Malay Archipelago stretches from Sumatra to New Guinea.

Etymology

Malay refers to the Malay people; archipelago from Greek 'arkhipelagos' (chief sea/islands).

2. PROPN

/ məˈleɪ ɑːrtʃɪˌpɛləˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ ˌɑːtʃɪˈpɛlɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
the group of islands situated between mainland Southeast Asia and Australia, including Indonesia, the Philippines, and surrounding areas
region of islands between Asia and Australia

Examples

Alfred Russel Wallace wrote extensively about the Malay Archipelago and its unique wildlife.
The Malay Archipelago is home to thousands of islands and diverse cultures.
Earthquakes and volcanic activity are common in the Malay Archipelago due to its location on the Pacific Ring of Fire.

Etymology

From Malay (referring to the Malay ethnic group) and archipelago (from Italian, ultimately from Greek 'arkhipelagos', meaning 'chief sea').
