iron horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.ɚn hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ən hɔːs / (BrE)
an early term for a locomotive, especially in the nineteenth century
early term for locomotive
archaic

Examples

Writers of the Old West often marveled at the arrival of the iron horse in frontier towns.
The museum displays an original iron horse from the golden age of railroads.
As the iron horse roared down the track, crowds gathered to witness the marvel of steam power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotive - a powered railway vehicle used for pulling trains

Etymology

Refers to locomotives of the 19th century, from 'iron' (metal) and 'horse' (as steam engines replaced horse-drawn vehicles).
