salty tooth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔlti tuθ / (AmE), / ˈsɒlti tuːθ / (BrE)
A craving or strong preference for salty foods or snacks.
craving for salty foods

Examples

She always gives in to her salty tooth by eating popcorn at the movies.
Late at night, his salty tooth makes him reach for a bag of pretzels.
People with a salty tooth tend to choose chips over cookies.

Synset Examples

After dinner, he satisfied his salty tooth with chips.
Her salty tooth led her to the snack aisle.
I have a salty tooth and love pretzels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet tooth - a craving for sweet foods

Etymology

Modeled on the idiom 'sweet tooth,' substituting 'salty' for those who prefer salty flavors.
