colt's tooth

1. NOUN

/ koʊlts tuθ / (AmE), / kəʊlts tuːθ / (BrE)
the first set of teeth that a young horse (colt) has, sometimes believed to be a rare and lucky find
first set of a young horse's teeth

Examples

The stable hand carefully examined the colt's tooth he found in the straw.
In rural folklore, a colt's tooth is often kept as a good luck charm.
The veterinarian explained that a colt's tooth is usually shed as the horse matures.

Synset Examples

He lost a colt's tooth during the playful scuffle.
Finding a colt's tooth is rare and considered lucky.
