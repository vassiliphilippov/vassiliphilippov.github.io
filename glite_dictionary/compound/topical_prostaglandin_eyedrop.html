topical prostaglandin eyedrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑpɪkəl ˌproʊstəˌɡlændɪn ˈaɪˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪkəl ˌprɒstəˌɡlændɪn ˈaɪˌdrɒp / (BrE)
an eyedrop medication containing prostaglandins, applied to the eye's surface to increase the outflow of aqueous humor and treat glaucoma
eyedrop medication for glaucoma treatment
medical

Examples

My ophthalmologist prescribed a topical prostaglandin eyedrop to help manage my intraocular pressure.
Using a topical prostaglandin eyedrop every night has significantly improved her glaucoma.
He experienced mild irritation after starting a topical prostaglandin eyedrop for his eye condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta-blocker eyedrop - eyedrop medication using beta-blockers for glaucoma

Etymology

From 'topical' (from Greek 'topikos', local) + 'prostaglandin' (biochemical compounds, originally isolated from the prostate gland) + 'eyedrop'.
