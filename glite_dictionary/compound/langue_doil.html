langue d'oil

1. NOUN

/ lɑ̃ɡ də ˈwiːl / (AmE), / lɑ̃ɡ də ˈwiːl / (BrE)
a group of medieval dialects of French spoken in central and northern France, which later developed into standard French
medieval French dialects of northern France

Examples

The langue d'oil was prevalent in northern France during the Middle Ages.
Many modern French words originate from the langue d'oil dialects.
The distinction between langue d'oil and langue d'oc regions is important in French linguistic history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: langue d'oc - medieval dialects of French spoken in southern France

Etymology

From French, literally 'language of yes', from Old French 'oil' (yes), distinguishing it from 'langue d'oc'.
