notational system

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈteɪʃənəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / nəʊˈteɪʃənəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system of symbols or signs used for representing concepts, quantities, or other specific information, especially in technical or academic fields.
system of symbols for representing information

Examples

The scientific community adopted a new notational system to standardize data representation across disciplines.
Musical scores are written using a universal notational system that musicians around the world understand.
Mathematicians created a specialized notational system to simplify the study of complex equations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notation - A set of written symbols used to represent something
