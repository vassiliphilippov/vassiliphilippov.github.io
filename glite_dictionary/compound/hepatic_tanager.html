hepatic tanager

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈpætɪk ˈtænədʒər / (AmE), / hɪˈpætɪk ˈtanɪdʒə / (BrE)
a North American songbird (Piranga flava) found in the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its reddish plumage
songbird of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

We spotted a hepatic tanager perched on a pine branch during our hike.
The male hepatic tanager is noted for its striking reddish coloration.
Birdwatchers travel to Arizona to catch a glimpse of the rare hepatic tanager.

Etymology

From 'hepatic' meaning 'liver-colored' and Spanish 'tanagra' (tanager).
