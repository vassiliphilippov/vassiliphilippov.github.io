civic center

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvɪk ˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪk ˌsɛntə / (BrE)
A building or complex used as a focal point for social, cultural, or community activities within a town or city.
community building for social and cultural events

Examples

The city is building a new civic center downtown to host concerts and public gatherings.
Our local civic center offers classes, art exhibitions, and community meetings.
The ceremony was held at the civic center and attended by hundreds of residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: community center - a place where members of a community gather for activities

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvɪk ˌsɛntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪk ˌsɛntə / (BrE)
the central part of a city, often where public buildings and offices are located
central district of a city

Examples

The new museum is located right in the civic center of the city.
Downtown shops surround the historic civic center, making it a lively place.
Many public events are held at the civic center each year.
