revolving credit

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɑlvɪŋ ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlvɪŋ ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
A credit arrangement that allows a consumer to borrow repeatedly up to a specified limit, with the balance able to be paid down or drawn on at any time.
flexible credit line up to a set limit

Examples

She used her revolving credit to make several large purchases and paid off the balance over time.
Many credit cards offer revolving credit so customers can borrow as needed up to a set limit.
Business owners often rely on revolving credit to manage cash flow fluctuations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit line - an arrangement allowing borrowing up to a certain limit
