medical dressing

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪkəl ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
a piece of sterile cloth or similar material used to cover and protect a wound or sore
material for covering a wound

Examples

After cleaning the cut, the nurse applied a fresh medical dressing to prevent infection.
It is important to change the medical dressing regularly to promote healing.
The doctor removed the old medical dressing to examine the wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandage - a strip of material used to bind a wound or protect an injured part of the body
