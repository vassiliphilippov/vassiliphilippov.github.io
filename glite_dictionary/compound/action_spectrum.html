action spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.ʃən ˈspɛk.trəm / (AmE), / ˈak.ʃən ˈspɛk.trəm / (BrE)
A graph or chart showing the effectiveness of electromagnetic radiation in producing a photochemical reaction at different wavelengths.
graph of photochemical efficiency by wavelength

Examples

The action spectrum of photosynthesis shows the wavelengths most effective for plant growth.
Researchers plotted the action spectrum to determine which light wavelengths trigger the photochemical reaction.
Comparing the action spectrum with the absorption spectrum helps to identify the pigments involved in the process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption spectrum - spectrum showing how much light is absorbed at each wavelength

Etymology

Formed from 'action' (result or effect) and 'spectrum' (range of wavelengths); common in scientific terminology.
