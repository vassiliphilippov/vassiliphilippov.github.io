family Scarabaeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli skəˌræbiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli skəˌræbiˈeɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of beetles commonly known as scarab or dung beetles, many of which feed on dung, decaying matter, or plant material
scarab or dung beetle family

Examples

The family Scarabaeidae includes thousands of beetle species found worldwide.
Many members of the family Scarabaeidae play an essential role in decomposing animal waste.
Researchers discovered a new species in the family Scarabaeidae while surveying the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarabaeus - a type of scarab beetle
  • Synonym: dung beetle - a beetle that feeds on dung

Etymology

From New Latin 'Scarabaeidae', based on Latin 'scarabaeus' (scarab beetle).
