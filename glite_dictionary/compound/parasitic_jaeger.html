parasitic jaeger

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk ˈjeɪɡər / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk ˈjɛːɡə / (BrE)
a medium-sized, Arctic-breeding seabird of the skua family, known for chasing other birds to steal their food
a type of Arctic seabird, also called Arctic skua

Examples

The parasitic jaeger often harasses gulls and terns to force them to drop food.
During migration, several parasitic jaegers were spotted along the coast.
A parasitic jaeger can be identified by its sharp flight and long central tail feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skua - a large seabird known for aggressive behavior
  • Synonym: Arctic skua - another common name for the parasitic jaeger
  • Degree Higher: great skua - a larger related seabird

Etymology

From 'parasitic', referring to its food-stealing behavior, and 'jaeger', from German 'Jäger' (hunter).
