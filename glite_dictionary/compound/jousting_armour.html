jousting armour

1. NOUN

/ ˈʤaʊstɪŋ ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / ˈdʒaʊstɪŋ ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A heavy suit of plate armor specifically designed for jousting, not for free combat, typically produced in the late 15th to 16th century.
armor for jousting tournaments

Examples

The jousting armour could weigh as much as 50 kilograms, making it difficult to move quickly.
In the museum, we saw an elaborate set of jousting armour used by a 16th-century knight.
Unlike battle armor, jousting armour was built to absorb the impact of a lance during tournaments.

Synset Examples

The jousting armour could weigh as much as 50 kg (100 pounds).

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: plate armor - armor made from large metal plates; general category including jousting armour

Etymology

From Middle English 'joust' (from Old French 'joster', to tilt, compete in a tournament) + 'armour' (from Old French 'armure', from Latin 'armatura').
