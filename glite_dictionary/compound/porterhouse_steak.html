porterhouse steak

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtərˌhaʊs steɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəˌhaʊs steɪk / (BrE)
A large steak cut from the thick end of the short loin, containing a T-shaped bone and a large piece of tenderloin.
large steak cut with bone and tenderloin

Examples

He ordered a porterhouse steak cooked medium rare at the restaurant.
The porterhouse steak is known for its generous portion and rich flavor.
At the barbecue, they grilled a massive porterhouse steak for everyone to share.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: T-bone steak - steak cut from the short loin containing a T-shaped bone, but with less tenderloin than a porterhouse

Etymology

Named after porterhouses (inns or taverns) in the United States where this cut of steak was served in the 19th century.
