Qatari dirham

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈtɑːri ˈdɪrˌhæm / (AmE), / kəˈtɑːri ˈdɪəˌræm / (BrE)
a monetary unit of Qatar, equal to one hundredth of a Qatari riyal
monetary unit of Qatar

Examples

The price of the coffee was ten Qatari dirhams at the café.
Exchange rates showed that one Qatari dirham was worth less than one US cent.
He received his change in Qatari dirhams after purchasing a souvenir.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Qatari riyal - the main currency of Qatar

Etymology

Dirham from Arabic 'dirham', itself from Greek 'drachme'; Qatari from Qatar, the country.
