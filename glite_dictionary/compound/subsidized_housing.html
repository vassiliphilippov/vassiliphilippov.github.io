subsidized housing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd ˈhaʊ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd ˈhaʊ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Housing in which the cost is partly paid by a government or other organization to make it more affordable for residents.
government- or organization-supported housing

Examples

Many low-income families rely on subsidized housing to find a safe place to live.
The city expanded its subsidized housing program to address the homelessness crisis.
He applied for subsidized housing after losing his job.

Synset Examples

Residents in the company town enjoyed subsidized housing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public housing - government-provided housing for low-income residents
  • Synonym: affordable housing - housing priced to be accessible to people with low or moderate incomes
