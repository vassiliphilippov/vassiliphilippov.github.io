communication trench

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmjunəˈkeɪʃən trɛntʃ / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən trɛntʃ / (BrE)
a trench built to provide protected passage between the rear and front lines in a defensive military position
protected military trench for movement

Examples

The soldiers used the communication trench to move safely between their base and the front line.
During the war, every communication trench was carefully camouflaged to avoid detection by the enemy.
Heavy rain caused the communication trench to flood, making movement difficult for the troops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trench - a long, narrow ditch used in military operations
