to tie someone's shoes

1. VERB

/ taɪ sʌmwʌnz ʃuːz / (AmE), / taɪ sʌmwənz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to fasten the laces of someone's shoes so they are secure
fasten the laces of shoes

Examples

Could you help me tie my shoes?
Children often need assistance to tie their shoes when they start school.
He stopped in the middle of the path to tie his shoes.

Synset Examples

He managed to tie his shoes one-handed.
Don't forget to tie your shoes before running.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untie someone's shoes - to unfasten the laces of someone's shoes
