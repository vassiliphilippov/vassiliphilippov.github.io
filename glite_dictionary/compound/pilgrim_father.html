Pilgrim Father

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɡrəm ˈfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɡrɪm ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
one of the English settlers who sailed to America on the Mayflower in 1620 and founded the Plymouth Colony in New England.
early English settler in Plymouth Colony, 1620

Examples

The Pilgrim Fathers are remembered for their historic journey on the Mayflower.
Many Americans celebrate Thanksgiving in honor of the Pilgrim Fathers' first successful harvest.
The descendants of the Pilgrim Fathers still live in New England today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pilgrim - a person who journeys to a sacred place for religious reasons

Etymology

From 'pilgrim' (a person who journeys for religious reasons) and 'father' (founder or ancestor), referring to the founding settlers of Plymouth Colony.
