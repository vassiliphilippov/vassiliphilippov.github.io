red pimpernel

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈpɪmpəɹˌnɛl / (AmE), / rɛd ˈpɪmpənɛl / (BrE)
a low-growing annual herb (Anagallis arvensis) with scarlet, white, or purple flowers that close before rainy weather
annual herb with scarlet or purple flowers

Examples

The red pimpernel is known for its delicate scarlet flowers that close when rain is approaching.
Gardeners often plant red pimpernel to add a splash of color to their flower beds.
The petals of the red pimpernel fold up tightly on cloudy or rainy days.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blue pimpernel - a closely related plant with blue flowers

Etymology

Pimpernel from Middle English ‘pympernelle’, from Old French ‘pimpernelle’, from Medieval Latin ‘piperinella’.
