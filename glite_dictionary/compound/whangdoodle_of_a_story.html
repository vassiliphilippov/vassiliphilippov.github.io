whangdoodle of a story

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæŋˌduːdəl əv ə ˈstɔːri / (AmE), / ˈwæŋˌduːdəl əv ə ˈstɒri / (BrE)
an exaggerated or fanciful story, often told for entertainment rather than factual accuracy
an exaggerated or fanciful story

Examples

Jake spun a whangdoodle of a story about catching a fish that was bigger than his boat.
She loves inventing a whangdoodle of a story to amuse her little brother before bedtime.
The politician delivered a whangdoodle of a story at the campaign rally, much to everyone's delight.

Synset Examples

He told a whangdoodle of a story to entertain the kids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tall tale - an exaggerated story, often unreliable
  • Synonym: fib - a small or trivial lie

Etymology

From 'whangdoodle', a fanciful or whimsical creature invented in American English in the 19th century, combined with 'story'.
