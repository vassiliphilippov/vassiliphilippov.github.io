liver disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvər dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a disease that affects the liver, impairing its function or causing damage
disease affecting the liver
medical

Examples

Many alcoholics are at risk of developing liver disease if they do not change their habits.
Chronic hepatitis can lead to serious liver disease over time.
The doctor explained that early detection is crucial in treating liver disease.
