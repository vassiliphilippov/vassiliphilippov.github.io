tout ensemble

1. ADV

/ tu ɑ̃ˈsɑ̃bl / (AmE), / tu ɑ̃ˈsɑ̃bl / (BrE)
with everything taken into account; all things considered.
all things considered

Examples

The decision, considered tout ensemble, appears to be a wise one.
He liked the house tout ensemble, even if some details were lacking.
The artist's use of color and shape creates a pleasing tout ensemble.

Synset Examples

altogether, I'm sorry it happened
all in all, it's not so bad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altogether - with everything considered; entirely
  • Synonym: all in all - taking everything into account

Etymology

From French, literally 'all together.'
