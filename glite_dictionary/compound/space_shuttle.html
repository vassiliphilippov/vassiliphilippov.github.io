space shuttle

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪs ˌʃʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪs ˌʃʌt.əl / (BrE)
a reusable spacecraft with wings, designed to transport people and cargo between Earth and space, capable of controlled descent through Earth's atmosphere
reusable spacecraft for Earth-space transport

Examples

The space shuttle completed its mission and landed safely at Kennedy Space Center.
Many astronauts have traveled to the International Space Station aboard the space shuttle.
The space shuttle program marked a significant milestone in human space exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spacecraft - a vehicle designed for travel or operation in outer space
