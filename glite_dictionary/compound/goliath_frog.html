goliath frog

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlaɪəθ frɑɡ / (AmE), / ɡəˈlaɪəθ frɒɡ / (BrE)
the largest living frog species, native to central Africa, reaching lengths of up to a foot and weighing as much as 10 pounds
largest living frog species of Africa

Examples

Scientists traveled to Cameroon to study the endangered goliath frog and its habitat.
The goliath frog can grow much larger than any other amphibian currently living on Earth.
Many zoos showcase the goliath frog to highlight biodiversity and conservation issues.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: frog - a tailless amphibian with a short squat body

Etymology

Named after the biblical giant Goliath, for its immense size.
