Cantabrian Mountains

1. PROPN

/ kænˈtæbriən ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / kanˈtabrɪən ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a range of mountains in northern Spain, running parallel to the coast of the Bay of Biscay
mountain range in northern Spain

Examples

The Cantabrian Mountains are home to diverse wildlife, including the endangered Cantabrian brown bear.
Snow often caps the peaks of the Cantabrian Mountains well into late spring.
Hiking in the Cantabrian Mountains offers breathtaking views of Spain's northern coastline.

Etymology

From the region of Cantabria in northern Spain; 'Cantabrian' ultimately from Latin 'Cantabri', the name of an ancient people inhabiting the area.
