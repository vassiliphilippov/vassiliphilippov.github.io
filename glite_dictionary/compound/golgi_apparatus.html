Golgi apparatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldʒi əˌpærətəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɒldʒi əˈparətəs / (BrE)
an organelle in the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells involved in the modification, sorting, and packaging of proteins and lipids for secretion or delivery to other organelles
organelle for packaging cell products

Examples

Proteins are packaged and modified in the Golgi apparatus before being sent to their destination.
The scientist observed a well-developed Golgi apparatus in the glandular cells under the microscope.
Disruption of the Golgi apparatus can affect the secretion of enzymes in certain cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Golgi body - another term for the Golgi apparatus

Etymology

Named after Italian scientist Camillo Golgi, who discovered it in 1898.
