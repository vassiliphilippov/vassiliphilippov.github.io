Aksa Martyrs Brigades

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːksə ˈmɑːrtərz brɪˈɡeɪdz / (AmE), / ˈæksə ˈmɑːtəz brɪˈɡeɪdz / (BrE)
a militant Palestinian group, an offshoot of al-Fatah, active mainly in the West Bank and known for carrying out attacks in Israel, especially around 2002.
Palestinian militant group, Fatah offshoot

Examples

The Israeli government announced security measures in response to attacks by the Aksa Martyrs Brigades in 2002.
Members of the Aksa Martyrs Brigades claimed responsibility for several bombings during the conflict.
News outlets reported that the Aksa Martyrs Brigades had increased their activity in the West Bank.

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Aqsa', referring to the Al-Aqsa Mosque in Jerusalem, combined with 'martyrs' and 'brigades'.
