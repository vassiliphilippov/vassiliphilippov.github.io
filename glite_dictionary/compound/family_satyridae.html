family Satyridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli səˈtaɪɹɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sæˈtɪəriːdiː / (BrE)
a family of butterflies commonly found near woodland edges, widely distributed and recognized for their earthy coloration
a family of woodland-edge butterflies

Examples

The family Satyridae includes many species adapted to shaded woodland environments.
Researchers observed several members of the family Satyridae at the forest's edge.
Many butterflies in the family Satyridae have brown or muted wing patterns for camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satyr butterfly - common name for butterflies of the family Satyridae

Etymology

From modern Latin Satyridae, the family name, based on Satyrus, a genus within the group.
