American language

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the English language as spoken and written in the United States, distinguished by its own vocabulary, pronunciation, and usage.
English as used in the United States
american

Examples

Many immigrants quickly learn to speak American language to adapt to their new home.
Her accent showed she was fluent in American language rather than British English.
He taught a course on the history and development of American language.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: British English - the English language as used in the United Kingdom
