Newton's first law of motion

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtənz ˈfɝst lɔ əv ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtənz ˈfɜːst lɔː əv ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
The law stating that an object remains at rest or in uniform motion in a straight line unless acted upon by an external force.
law of inertia in physics

Examples

According to Newton's first law of motion, a stationary object will remain stationary unless acted upon by a force.
The principle described in Newton's first law of motion is often referred to as the law of inertia.
When the car suddenly stopped, his body continued moving forward because of Newton's first law of motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law of inertia - another name for Newton's first law stating objects remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a force
  • Degree Higher: Newton's second law of motion - states that force equals mass times acceleration (F=ma)
  • Synonym: Newton's third law of motion - states that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction

Etymology

Named after English physicist Sir Isaac Newton (1642–1727).
