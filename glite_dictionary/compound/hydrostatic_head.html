hydrostatic head

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈstætɪk hɛd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈstætɪk hɛd / (BrE)
the pressure at a specific point in a liquid, expressed as the vertical height of a column of the liquid required to produce that pressure
pressure in liquid shown as vertical column height

Examples

The dam was designed to withstand the hydrostatic head generated by the deepest part of the reservoir.
A greater hydrostatic head increases the pressure exerted at the bottom of the tank.
Engineers calculated the hydrostatic head to ensure the pipeline would not burst under pressure.

Etymology

From 'hydrostatic', relating to fluids at rest (Greek 'hydōr' water + 'statikos' causing to stand), and 'head', meaning height or pressure of a liquid column.
