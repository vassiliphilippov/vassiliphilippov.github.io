compass saw

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.pəs sɔ / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.pəs sɔː / (BrE)
a handsaw with a long, narrow, tapering blade designed for cutting curved or irregular lines in wood or other materials
narrow-bladed saw for cutting curves

Examples

The carpenter used a compass saw to cut a curved shape in the plywood.
A compass saw is ideal for making tight, intricate cuts where other saws cannot reach.
When making circular holes in furniture panels, a compass saw offers great control and precision.
