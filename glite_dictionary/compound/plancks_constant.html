Planck's constant

1. NOUN

/ plæŋks ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / plæŋks ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a fundamental physical constant that relates the energy of a photon to its frequency, approximately equal to 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ joule-seconds
constant relating photon energy to frequency

Examples

The value of Planck's constant is crucial in calculations involving quantum mechanics.
Scientists use Planck's constant to determine the energy of subatomic particles.
Without Planck's constant, the relationship between energy and frequency in photons could not be accurately defined.

Etymology

Named after German physicist Max Planck (1858–1947), who introduced it in 1900.
