in fee

1. ADP

/ ɪn fi / (AmE), / ɪn fiː / (BrE)
(law) With absolute ownership of property, especially land, free from limitations or conditions; held in fee simple.
absolute ownership of property (law)
legal

Examples

Under the new will, she received the estate in fee rather than for life.
He inherited the house in fee, giving him full rights to sell or bequeath it.
The land was granted to the nobleman in fee by the king.

Synset Examples

She received her property in fee from her parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fee simple - absolute ownership of land, not limited by time or conditions

Etymology

From Old French 'fe', from Medieval Latin 'feodum' (feudal estate).
