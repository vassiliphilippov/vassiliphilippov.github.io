genus Cyperus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs saɪˈpɪr.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈpɪər.əs / (BrE)
the type genus of the sedge family (Cyperaceae), comprising grasslike, rhizomatous herbs found worldwide except in very cold regions
type genus of sedge family, grasslike herbs

Examples

The well-known papyrus plant belongs to the genus Cyperus.
Many species in the genus Cyperus thrive in tropical wetlands around the world.
Botanists recently discovered a new specimen in the genus Cyperus during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyperaceae - the sedge family of plants, to which genus Cyperus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'Cyperus', used as the genus name in botanical classification.
