Prince Albert yew

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪns ˈælbɚt juː / (AmE), / ˈprɪns ˈælbət juː / (BrE)
A small yew tree with attractive foliage and partially weeping branches, cultivated as an ornamental plant, native to the mountains of southern Chile.
ornamental yew tree from Chile

Examples

The Prince Albert yew is prized for its graceful, weeping branches in landscape gardens.
Gardeners often choose the Prince Albert yew for shady locations due to its lush foliage.
During our trip to Chile, we saw the rare Prince Albert yew in its mountain habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - any of several evergreen trees or shrubs of the genus Taxus
