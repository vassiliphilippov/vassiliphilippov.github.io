to look peakish

1. VERB

/ lʊk ˈpiːkɪʃ / (AmE), / lʊk ˈpiːkɪʃ / (BrE)
to appear unwell or exhausted, especially with a pale or sickly appearance
appear unwell or exhausted

Examples

After a week of difficult exams, he really did look peakish.
You look peakish; maybe you should lie down for a while.
Whenever she's nervous, she tends to look peakish and pale.

Synset Examples

She looked peakish after staying up all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look ill - to appear to be unwell
  • Antonym: look healthy - to appear healthy or well

Etymology

From 'look' (to seem) + 'peakish', an adjective meaning sickly or pale, from 'peaky'.
