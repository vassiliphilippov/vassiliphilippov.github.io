suborder Nematocera

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər ˌnɛməˈtɒsərə / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə ˌnɪːməˈtɒsərə / (BrE)
A suborder of insects within the order Diptera, including mosquitoes, gnats, crane flies, and similar slender-bodied flies, characterized by long antennae and delicate wings.
suborder of slender, long-antennaed flies

Examples

The suborder Nematocera includes mosquitoes, midges, and crane flies.
Researchers studied the larval forms typical of the suborder Nematocera.
Most members of the suborder Nematocera have delicate bodies and long, threadlike antennae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Diptera - the order of insects containing true flies

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Nematocera*, from Greek *nēma* (thread) and *keras* (horn), referring to their threadlike antennae.
