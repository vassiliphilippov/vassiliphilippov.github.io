gestational age

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛˈsteɪʃənəl eɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒɛˈsteɪʃənəl eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the age of an embryo or fetus, measured from the time of fertilization, used to describe the progress of a pregnancy
the age of an embryo or fetus from fertilization
medical

Examples

The doctor estimated the baby's gestational age at 20 weeks based on the ultrasound scan.
Accurate determination of gestational age is important for monitoring fetal development and planning delivery.
She gave birth prematurely at a gestational age of just 32 weeks.

Etymology

From 'gestation' (from Latin gestatio, meaning carrying), and 'age', referring to the time period since fertilization.
