Republic of Moldova

1. PROPN

/ rɪˌpʌblɪk əv moʊlˈdoʊvə / (AmE), / rɪˌpʌblɪk əv mɒlˈdəʊvə / (BrE)
A landlocked country in eastern Europe, formerly part of the Soviet Union, which became independent in 1991.
country in eastern Europe

Examples

The Republic of Moldova declared its independence from the Soviet Union in 1991.
Wine production is an important industry in the Republic of Moldova.
Many tourists are drawn to the rich cultural heritage of the Republic of Moldova.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Moldova - Country in eastern Europe (short form of Republic of Moldova)
  • Degree Lower: Transnistria - Breakaway region in eastern Moldova

Etymology

From Latin 'republica' (state) and the name 'Moldova,' referring to the region along the Moldova River.
