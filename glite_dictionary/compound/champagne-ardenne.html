Champagne-Ardenne

1. PROPN

/ ʃæmˈpeɪn ɑrˈdɛn / (AmE), / ʃæmˈpæn ɑːˈdɛn / (BrE)
a former administrative region in northeastern France, known for its production of Champagne and its historical significance
former region of northeastern France

Examples

The vineyards of Champagne-Ardenne are famous worldwide for producing sparkling wine.
During our trip to France, we spent several days exploring Champagne-Ardenne.
Historically, Champagne-Ardenne has played a significant role in French culture and economy.

Etymology

From French Champagne (wine region) + Ardenne (from the Ardennes forest).
