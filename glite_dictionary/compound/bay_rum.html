bay rum

1. NOUN

/ beɪ ˈrʌm / (AmE), / beɪ ˈrʌm / (BrE)
an aromatic liquid, typically used as an aftershave or cologne, originally made by distilling the leaves of the West Indian bay tree with rum
aromatic liquid used as cologne or aftershave

Examples

He splashed bay rum on his face after shaving, enjoying its distinctive spicy scent.
The old barber shop was filled with the fragrance of bay rum and talc.
Many traditional aftershaves contain bay rum as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aftershave - a lotion or liquid applied to the skin after shaving
  • Synonym: cologne - a perfumed liquid for men's use

Etymology

From 'bay', referring to the bay tree, and 'rum', the alcoholic spirit; originally from the West Indies.
