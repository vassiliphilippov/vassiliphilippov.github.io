epizootic disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪzuˈɑtɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪzəʊˈɒtɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease affecting a large number of animals in a particular population, similar to an epidemic in humans
disease outbreak among animals
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of epizootic disease can have devastating effects on livestock industries.
The veterinarian monitored the herd for signs of epizootic disease following reports of illness in neighboring farms.
Control measures must be implemented quickly to contain an epizootic disease among wildlife populations.

Synset Examples

Scientists study epizootic diseases to prevent outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidemic - a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in humans

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + zoon (animal) + -otic, modeled after 'epidemic'.
