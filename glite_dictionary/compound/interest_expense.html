interest expense

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntrəst ɪkˌspɛns / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəst ɪkˌspɛns / (BrE)
the amount of interest paid on borrowed money, typically recorded as a cost in accounting or financial statements
cost paid for borrowing money

Examples

The company's interest expense increased after it took on new loans.
Reducing interest expense can significantly improve a firm's profitability.
They analyzed quarterly reports to track changes in interest expense over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interest income - money earned from lending money or from deposits
