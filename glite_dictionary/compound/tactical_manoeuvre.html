tactical manoeuvre

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tɪ.kəl məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / ˈtæktɪkəl məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
an action or movement carefully planned to achieve a particular tactical advantage, especially in military or strategic situations
action for tactical advantage

Examples

The general ordered a tactical manoeuvre to outflank the enemy forces during the battle.
Implementing a tactical manoeuvre at the last minute helped the team secure the win.
Her quick tactical manoeuvre ensured she was in the best position to negotiate the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim

Etymology

From tactic (Greek taktikē, art of arrangement) + manoeuvre (French manœuvre, manual labor or movement).
