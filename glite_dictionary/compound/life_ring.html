life ring

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ˌrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ˌrɪŋ / (BrE)
a life preserver shaped like a ring, usually made of buoyant material, used for saving people from drowning
ring-shaped life preserver

Examples

Every boat is required by law to carry at least one life ring for emergencies.
The child clung tightly to the life ring after falling into the pool.
A crew member threw a life ring overboard to rescue the struggling swimmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifebuoy - a buoyant ring thrown to help someone keep afloat in water
  • Synonym: life jacket - a sleeveless jacket filled with buoyant material
