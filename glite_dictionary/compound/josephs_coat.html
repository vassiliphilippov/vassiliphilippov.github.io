Joseph's coat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒoʊzəfs koʊt / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪfs kəʊt / (BrE)
a perennial ornamental plant of southeastern Asia noted for its large, brightly colored or variegated leaves and spikes of blue-violet flowers, sometimes placed in the genus Solenostemon
ornamental plant with brightly colored leaves

Examples

The garden was full of Joseph's coat plants, their leaves displaying vivid shades of red, yellow, and green.
She chose Joseph's coat to add a splash of color to her flowerbeds.
In warmer climates, Joseph's coat can be grown outdoors year-round as a decorative foliage plant.

Etymology

Named for the biblical Joseph, whose 'coat of many colors' inspired the name due to the plant's multicolored leaves.
