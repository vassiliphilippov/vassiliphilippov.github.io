Peloponnesian War

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛləpəˈniʒən wɔr / (AmE), / ˌpɛləpəˈniːzɪən wɔː / (BrE)
a war fought from 431 to 404 BC between Athens and its allies and the Peloponnesian League led by Sparta, resulting in the defeat of Athens
ancient Greek war (431-404 BC) between Athens and Sparta

Examples

Many historians study the Peloponnesian War to understand the decline of classical Athens.
Thucydides wrote a famous account of the Peloponnesian War.
The Peloponnesian War significantly changed the balance of power among Greek city-states.

Etymology

From Peloponnesus, the peninsula in southern Greece, and war.
