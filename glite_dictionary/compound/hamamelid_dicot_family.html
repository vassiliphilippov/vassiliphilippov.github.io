hamamelid dicot family

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmiːlɪd ˈdaɪˌkɑt ˈfæmli / (AmE), / ˌhæməˈmiːlɪd ˈdaɪkɒt ˈfæmli / (BrE)
A family of mostly woody dicotyledonous flowering plants, often with unisexual flowers commonly borne in catkins.
family of woody dicotyledonous plants
medical

Examples

The hamamelid dicot family includes many ornamental shrubs commonly found in gardens.
Some botanists study the hamamelid dicot family to understand the evolution of woody plants.
Witch hazels are perhaps the most well-known members of the hamamelid dicot family.

Etymology

From the genus Hamamelis (witch hazel) and 'dicot', referring to plants with two embryonic seed leaves.
