Hoya carnosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɔɪə kɑrˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌhɔɪə kɑːˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of succulent, climbing plant native to southern Asia, known for its umbels of pink and white star-shaped flowers.
a species of climbing plant with star-shaped flowers

Examples

The Hoya carnosa in her living room bloomed with clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers last spring.
Because of its thick leaves and beautiful flowers, Hoya carnosa is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
He misted his Hoya carnosa weekly to maintain the humidity it needs to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hoya - genus of tropical climbing plants

Etymology

From genus name Hoya, named after Thomas Hoy, an English botanist; species name 'carnosa' from Latin meaning 'fleshy'.
