Bank of England

1. PROPN

/ bæŋk əv ɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / bæŋk əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
the central bank of the United Kingdom, responsible for issuing banknotes and setting monetary policy
the UK's central bank

Examples

The Bank of England raised interest rates to curb inflation.
Many economists closely monitor decisions made by the Bank of England.
The Bank of England was founded in 1694.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central bank - a national bank that provides financial and banking services for its country's government and commercial banking system
