European catfish

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈkatˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, elongated catfish native to central and eastern Europe, known for its considerable size
large catfish species of Europe

Examples

The European catfish can grow to an impressive length of over two meters.
Fishermen in the Danube often hope to catch a European catfish due to its size.
The diet of the European catfish includes fish, amphibians, and sometimes birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wels - another name for the European catfish
