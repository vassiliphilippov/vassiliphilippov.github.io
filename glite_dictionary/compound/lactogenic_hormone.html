lactogenic hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæk.toʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk ˈhɔr.moʊn / (AmE), / ˌlæk.təʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk ˈhɔː.məʊn / (BrE)
a hormone, such as prolactin, secreted by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the growth of mammary glands and initiates milk production after childbirth in females
hormone that stimulates milk production
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the lactogenic hormone is crucial for initiating milk secretion after childbirth.
During pregnancy, levels of lactogenic hormone rise to prepare the mammary glands for lactation.
Scientists are studying the effects of the lactogenic hormone on breastfeeding success rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolactin - hormone that stimulates milk production in mammals

Etymology

From Latin 'lac' (milk) and Greek 'genes' (producing), with 'hormone' from Greek 'hormōn' (to set in motion).
