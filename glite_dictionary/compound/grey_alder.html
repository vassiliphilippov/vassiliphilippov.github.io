grey alder

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈældɚ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈældə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to Europe, with smooth grey bark, typically found in moist soils and sometimes introduced to North America.
European deciduous tree with smooth grey bark

Examples

The grey alder is often planted to stabilize riverbanks because of its strong roots.
We spotted a stand of grey alder along the edge of the woodland.
Leaves from the grey alder decompose quickly, enriching the soil beneath it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a tree or shrub of the birch family
