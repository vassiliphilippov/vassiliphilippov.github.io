Hooker's onion

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkərz ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / ˈhʊkəz ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
a North American wild onion species (Allium acuminatum) with a strong onion smell and an umbel of pink flowers on a leafless stalk, found from British Columbia to California, Arizona, Wyoming, and Colorado
a wild North American onion plant

Examples

While hiking in the Rockies, we saw clusters of Hooker's onion growing along the trail.
The pink flowers of Hooker's onion make it easy to spot in early summer.
Botanists often study Hooker's onion to learn more about native wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild onion - any onion species growing wild rather than cultivated

Etymology

Named after botanist William Jackson Hooker (1785–1865).
