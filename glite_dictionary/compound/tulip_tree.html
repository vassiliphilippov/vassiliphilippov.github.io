tulip tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːlɪp ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈtjuːlɪp ˌtriː / (BrE)
a tall North American deciduous tree (Liriodendron tulipifera) with large tulip-shaped greenish-yellow flowers and soft white wood used for cabinet making
North American tree with tulip-shaped flowers

Examples

The tulip tree in our backyard produces stunning yellow and green blossoms every spring.
Cabinet makers prize the wood from the tulip tree for its smooth texture and light color.
A towering tulip tree shaded the front of the old farmhouse.

Etymology

Named for its tulip-shaped flowers; 'tulip' from French 'tulipe', relating to the shape of the flowers.
