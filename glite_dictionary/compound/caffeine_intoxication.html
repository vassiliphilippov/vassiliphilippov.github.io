caffeine intoxication

1. NOUN

/ kæˈfiːn ɪnˌtɑksɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / kæˈfiːn ɪnˌtɒksɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical condition caused by consuming excessive amounts of caffeine, resulting in symptoms such as restlessness, insomnia, and rapid heartbeat
condition from excessive caffeine intake
medical

Examples

Drinking too much coffee in a short period can result in caffeine intoxication with unpleasant side effects.
The emergency room admitted a patient suffering from caffeine intoxication after he consumed several energy drinks.
Doctors warned that excessive energy drink consumption could increase the risk of caffeine intoxication.
