cheek muscle

1. NOUN

/ tʃik ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / tʃiːk ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a muscle of the face that flattens the cheek and draws back the angle of the mouth, such as the buccinator muscle
muscle that flattens the cheek
medical

Examples

The cheek muscle plays a key role in helping you blow air out of your mouth.
An injury to the cheek muscle can make it difficult to chew food properly.
During facial surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the cheek muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buccinator - the main muscle that flattens the cheek
