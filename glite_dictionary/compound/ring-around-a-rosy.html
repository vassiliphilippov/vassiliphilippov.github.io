ring-around-a-rosy

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪŋ əˈraʊnd ə ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˌrɪŋ əˈraʊnd ə ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
a traditional children's game in which players dance in a circle and squat or fall down at a certain signal, often while singing the rhyme of the same name
children's circle game and nursery rhyme

Examples

The children played ring-around-a-rosy during recess and laughed as they all fell down.
At the party, even the shyest kids joined in a round of ring-around-a-rosy.
She taught her little brother how to play ring-around-a-rosy in the backyard.
