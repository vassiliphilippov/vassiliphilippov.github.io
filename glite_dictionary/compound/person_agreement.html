person agreement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsən əˌɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsən əˌɡriːmənt / (BrE)
(in grammar) the correspondence of grammatical person, such as first, second, or third person, between elements in a sentence, typically between pronouns and verbs.
agreement of person between pronoun and verb

Examples

In English, person agreement requires that 'I' is followed by 'am' and not 'is'.
Errors in person agreement are common among learners of foreign languages.
Spanish verbs have more complex person agreement than English verbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subject-verb agreement - correspondence between subject and verb in number and person
