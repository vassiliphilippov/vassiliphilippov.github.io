tulip poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːlɪp ˌpɑplɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːlɪp ˌpɒplə / (BrE)
A tall North American deciduous tree with large tulip-shaped greenish-yellow flowers and conelike fruit, valued for its soft white wood used especially in cabinetmaking.
North American tree with tulip-shaped flowers and useful wood

Examples

The wood of the tulip poplar is prized for its fine grain and workability.
In spring, the tulip poplar produces striking greenish-yellow flowers high in its branches.
A row of tulip poplar trees provides welcome shade along the park path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liriodendron tulipifera - The botanical name for the tulip poplar
  • Synonym: yellow poplar - Alternative common name for the tulip poplar

Etymology

Named for the shape of its flowers, which resemble tulips, and its relation to poplars.
