pariah dog

1. NOUN

/ pəˈraɪ.ə dɔg / (AmE), / pəˈraɪ.ə dɒg / (BrE)
A semi-wild, ownerless dog, typically of mixed breed, found roaming around villages in Asia, especially India.
semi-wild mongrel dog of Asia, esp. India

Examples

Pariah dogs can often be seen scavenging in Indian villages.
The local veterinary team set up a clinic to vaccinate pariah dogs against rabies.
Some animal lovers have begun adopting pariah dogs as pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stray dog - an ownerless dog found wandering in public places

Etymology

From Tamil 'paraiyan', denoting an outcast, originally applied to a social group in southern India.
