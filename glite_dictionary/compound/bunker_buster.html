bunker buster

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋkɚ ˌbʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkə ˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a powerful bomb designed to penetrate hardened underground targets before exploding, typically used against bunkers or fortified facilities
bomb for penetrating underground targets

Examples

The military deployed a bunker buster to destroy the enemy's underground command center.
A bunker buster is capable of penetrating several meters of reinforced concrete before detonation.
Engineers are developing new bunker buster bombs with enhanced precision and deeper penetration capabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - an explosive weapon that detonates to cause destruction

Etymology

Formed from 'bunker' (fortified underground shelter) and 'buster' (thing that breaks or destroys, from 'bust').
