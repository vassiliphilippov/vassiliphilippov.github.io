barking spider

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrkɪŋ ˈspaɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːkɪŋ ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
a humorous or fictional creature said to make barking noises, often used as a playful explanation for an unexpected sound, such as flatulence
humorous or fictional noisy creature
informal slang

Examples

When the room fell silent, someone joked that a barking spider had passed by.
He blamed the strange noise on a barking spider, making everyone laugh.
Kids love to pretend they've seen a barking spider in the backyard.

Synset Examples

We spotted a barking spider in the garden today.
They say a barking spider means rain is coming.
I heard a barking spider outside my window last night.
