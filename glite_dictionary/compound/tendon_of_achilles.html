tendon of Achilles

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɪliz ˈtɛndən / (AmE), / əˈkɪliːz ˈtɛndən / (BrE)
the large tendon at the back of the ankle that connects the calf muscles to the heel bone
the large tendon at the back of the ankle
medical

Examples

Overuse can lead to inflammation of the tendon of Achilles, especially in athletes.
She tore her tendon of Achilles during the marathon and had to undergo surgery.
Proper stretching helps prevent injuries to the tendon of Achilles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Achilles tendon - alternative name for tendon of Achilles

Etymology

Named after Achilles, a hero of Greek mythology whose only vulnerable spot was his heel.
