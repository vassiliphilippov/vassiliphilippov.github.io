basket oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæskət oʊk / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt əʊk / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous tree found in the eastern United States, valued for its durable wood, which is used for timber and traditionally split and woven into baskets or chair seats.
oak tree used for basketry and timber

Examples

Furniture makers often choose basket oak for its strength and attractive grain.
The local craftsman demonstrated how to weave a sturdy basket using basket oak strips.
In many forests of the eastern United States, basket oak trees are a common sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, typically with lobed leaves and acorns
