pit-house

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɪtˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A shelter partly or fully dug into the ground, often with a roof built over the excavation, traditionally used for human habitation or for sheltering animals.
house partly dug into the ground

Examples

Some indigenous peoples constructed pit-houses that provided insulation against harsh winters.
Archaeologists discovered several ancient pit-houses along the riverbank.
The villagers used a pit-house as communal shelter during the coldest months.

Synset Examples

Dugouts are one of the most ancient types of human housing known to archeologists, and the same methods have evolved into modern ‘earth shelter’ technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dugout - a shelter dug out from the earth, often synonymous with pit-house

Etymology

Formed from 'pit' (a hole in the ground) + 'house' (dwelling).
