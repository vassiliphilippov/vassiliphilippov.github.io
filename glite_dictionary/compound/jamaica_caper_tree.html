Jamaica caper tree

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈmeɪ.kə ˈkeɪ.pər tri / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪ.kə ˈkeɪ.pə triː / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to southern Florida and the West Indies, known for its glossy leaves and fragrant flowers.
tropical shrub native to Florida and West Indies

Examples

The Jamaica caper tree is often planted as an ornamental shrub in warm climates.
Birds are attracted to the fragrant blossoms of the Jamaica caper tree.
The leaves of the Jamaica caper tree are glossy and leathery, making it a popular choice for gardens.
