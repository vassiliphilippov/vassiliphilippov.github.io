cue stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː ˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈkjuː ˌstɪk / (BrE)
a long, tapering rod used to strike the cue ball in pool or billiards
rod used to strike the cue ball in billiards or pool

Examples

He chalked his cue stick before taking the next shot.
A high-quality cue stick can make a real difference in a player's performance.
Please bring your own cue stick to the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cue - implement used to strike balls in cue sports

Etymology

From 'cue', possibly from French 'queue' (tail), referring to the tapered end, and 'stick'.
