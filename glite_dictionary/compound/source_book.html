source book

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrs ˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈsɔːs ˌbʊk / (BrE)
A book containing a collection of original or historically important documents, often used for study or reference.
book of original or important documents

Examples

The professor assigned a source book filled with primary texts from the Enlightenment period.
Historians often rely on a source book to access original government documents.
The anthology serves as a valuable source book for students studying ancient civilizations.
