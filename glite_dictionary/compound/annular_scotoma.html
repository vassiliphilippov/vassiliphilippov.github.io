annular scotoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jələr skoʊˈtoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈæn.jʊ.lə ˈskəʊ.tə.mə / (BrE)
a circular area of impaired or lost vision that surrounds the center of the visual field
ring-shaped visual field defect
medical

Examples

The patient reported an annular scotoma that made it difficult to see objects straight ahead.
Tests revealed an annular scotoma characteristic of certain types of glaucoma.
An annular scotoma can sometimes go unnoticed until vision loss becomes significant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scotoma - an area of partial or complete loss of vision surrounded by a field of normal vision

Etymology

From Latin 'annularis' (ring-shaped) and Greek 'skotos' (darkness).
