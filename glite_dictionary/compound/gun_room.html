gun room

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌn ruːm / (AmE), / ɡʌn ruːm / (BrE)
the quarters for midshipmen and junior officers on a British warship, traditionally also used for storing firearms
junior officers’ quarters on a British warship
british

Examples

During his first voyage, the young officer was assigned to the gun room with the other midshipmen.
The gun room was not only their sleeping quarters but also a place for study and recreation.
Historians often visit restored ships to see how life in the gun room was for junior officers.

Etymology

From 'gun' + 'room'; historically related to the room's original function for storing firearms.
