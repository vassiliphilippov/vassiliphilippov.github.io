experimental psychology

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛr.əˈmɛn.tl saɪˌkɑː.ləˈdʒi / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛr.ɪˈmɛn.tl saɪˌkɒ.ləˈdʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology concerned with using experimental methods to investigate psychological processes and behavior
branch of psychology using experiments

Examples

She chose to specialize in experimental psychology during her graduate studies.
Many groundbreaking discoveries in experimental psychology have shaped how we understand memory and learning.
The university offers several courses in experimental psychology focusing on research design and methodology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behavior
