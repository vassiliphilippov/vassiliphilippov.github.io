Federal Party

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a major political party in the United States in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, founded by Alexander Hamilton, that advocated a strong central government
early American political party

Examples

The Federal Party supported the ratification of the United States Constitution.
Alexander Hamilton was a leading figure in the Federal Party.
The decline of the Federal Party led to the rise of new political factions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Democratic-Republican Party - another major US political party in the same era
  • Synonym: Whig Party - successor political party to the Federalists in part

Etymology

Named from 'federal', describing support for a federation of states, and 'party'.
