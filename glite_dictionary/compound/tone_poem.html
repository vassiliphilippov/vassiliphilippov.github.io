tone poem

1. NOUN

/ toʊn ˈpoʊ.əm / (AmE), / təʊn ˈpəʊ.əm / (BrE)
an orchestral piece that evokes a story, scene, or idea, often inspired by literature or folk tales
orchestral piece inspired by a story or idea

Examples

Richard Strauss’s tone poem ‘Don Juan’ is a staple of the orchestral repertoire.
The conductor’s favorite piece was a tone poem based on an old Norse legend.
Debussy’s ‘La Mer’ is sometimes described as a tone poem for orchestra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symphonic poem - an orchestral composition that illustrates or evokes the content of a poem, short story, novel, painting, landscape, or other source
