wrack and ruin

1. NOUN

/ ræk ənd ˈruː.ɪn / (AmE), / ræk ənd ˈruː.ɪn / (BrE)
a condition of complete destruction, decay, or collapse; total ruin
state of total destruction or decay

Examples

After years of neglect, the garden fell into wrack and ruin.
The abandoned theater was left to wrack and ruin when funding ran out.
His business was in wrack and ruin following the economic downturn.

Synset Examples

His finances were in wrack and ruin after the failed business.
The once thriving park is now in wrack and ruin.
The old house fell into wrack and ruin after years of neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - the process or state of total destruction or decay
  • Synonym: decay - the process of gradual destruction or decline

Etymology

From 'wrack' (destruction or ruin) and 'ruin'; the phrase dates to at least the 16th century, often in the form 'rack and ruin'.
