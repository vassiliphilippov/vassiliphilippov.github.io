Barbary ape

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.bə.ri eɪp / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bəri eɪp / (BrE)
a tailless macaque native to rocky cliffs and forests of northwestern Africa and Gibraltar, also known as the Barbary macaque
tailless macaque of Africa and Gibraltar

Examples

The famous Barbary ape is a popular attraction for visitors in Gibraltar.
Scientists study the social behavior of the Barbary ape in its natural habitat.
Unlike most monkeys, the Barbary ape has no tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbary macaque - another common name for the species Macaca sylvanus

Etymology

Barbary, referring to the Barbary Coast region of North Africa, plus 'ape'.
