deckle edge

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəl ɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəl ɛdʒ / (BrE)
a rough, uncut edge of paper, originally produced when using a deckle in handmade papermaking, or deliberately imitated in machine-made paper
rough, uncut edge of paper

Examples

The first edition of the novel was printed on paper with a deckle edge, giving it a distinctive look.
Collectors often seek books that retain their original deckle edge for historical value.
Some luxury stationery uses a deckle edge to evoke the feel of handmade paper.

Etymology

From 'deckle', a frame used in papermaking, and 'edge'.
