lethargic encephalitis

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθɑrdʒɪk ˌɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈθɑːdʒɪk ˌɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A form of encephalitis that caused a widespread epidemic between 1915 and 1926, characterized by paralysis of the extrinsic eye muscles and profound muscular weakness.
epidemic encephalitis with muscle paralysis (1915–1926)
medical

Examples

The mysterious outbreak of lethargic encephalitis in the early twentieth century deeply concerned doctors worldwide.
Researchers still investigate the long-term effects of lethargic encephalitis patients from the 1920s.
Symptoms of lethargic encephalitis often included severe lethargy and difficulties controlling eye movement.

Etymology

From Greek 'lethargos' (drowsy) and 'encephalitis' (inflammation of the brain).
