lemon law

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən lɔ / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən lɔː / (BrE)
a law providing consumers with a remedy for vehicles or other products that repeatedly fail to meet standards of quality and performance
consumer protection law for defective products
american legal

Examples

Many states have enacted a lemon law to protect buyers from defective automobiles.
After his new truck broke down twice in a month, he considered filing a claim under the lemon law.
It's important to read about the lemon law before making a major vehicle purchase.

Synset Examples

He filed a claim under the lemon law for his faulty car.
The lemon law protects buyers from defective vehicles.
She learned about the lemon law after buying her first car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer protection - regulations to safeguard buyers of goods and services

Etymology

From American slang ‘lemon’ for a defective car; first used in US legal contexts in the late 20th century.
