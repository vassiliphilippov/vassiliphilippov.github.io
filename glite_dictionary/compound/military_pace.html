military pace

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri peɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri peɪs / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to a single step in military marching, typically 30 inches for quick time or 36 inches for double time
standard marching step length in military

Examples

The length of a military pace is standardized to ensure uniform marching among soldiers.
During the drill, each cadet was measured against the official military pace.
Instructions specified advancing five military paces from the starting line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pace - a single step taken when walking or running
