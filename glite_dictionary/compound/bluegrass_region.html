Bluegrass Region

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌɡræs ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌɡrɑːs ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
an area in central Kentucky, USA, known for its fertile bluegrass pastures and thoroughbred horse farms
region in central Kentucky, USA

Examples

The Bluegrass Region is famous for its rolling hills and picturesque horse farms.
Many tourists visit Kentucky to experience the beauty of the Bluegrass Region.
Thoroughbred breeding has made the Bluegrass Region renowned across the world.

Etymology

Named for the bluegrass (Poa pratensis) that grows abundantly in the region.
