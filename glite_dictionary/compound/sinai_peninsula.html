Sinai Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪ.naɪ pəˈnɪn.sjə.lə / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.naɪ pɪˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
A peninsula in northeastern Egypt, situated at the northern end of the Red Sea, forming a land bridge between Africa and Asia.
peninsula in northeastern Egypt

Examples

The Sinai Peninsula has been a strategic crossroads between Africa and Asia for centuries.
Many rare species of plants and animals are found only in the Sinai Peninsula.
Peace talks often reference the boundaries of the Sinai Peninsula due to its historical significance.

Etymology

Named after Mount Sinai, a prominent mountain in the region; Sinai from Hebrew 'Sinai' (mount of God).
