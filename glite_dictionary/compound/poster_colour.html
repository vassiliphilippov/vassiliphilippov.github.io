poster colour

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstər ˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə ˌkʌlə / (BrE)
A type of paint made from pigments mixed with water-soluble glutinous materials, often used for posters and illustrations.
opaque water-based paint for art
british

Examples

Children often use poster colour to create vibrant school projects.
The artist prefers poster colour because of its bright and opaque finish.
You should let the poster colour dry completely before applying a second layer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempera - A painting medium where pigments are mixed with a water-soluble binder such as egg yolk
  • Synonym: gouache - A method of painting using opaque pigments ground in water and thickened with glue or size
  • Degree Lower: watercolour - A painting method using pigments suspended in water
