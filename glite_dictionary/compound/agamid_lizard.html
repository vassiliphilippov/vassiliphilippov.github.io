agamid lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡəmɪd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈæɡəmɪd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A lizard belonging to the family Agamidae, typically found in Africa, Asia, and Australia, known for their crest-like spines and often colorful appearance.
lizard of the family Agamidae

Examples

The agamid lizard basked on the sun-warmed rock, displaying its brightly colored throat.
Researchers discovered a new species of agamid lizard in the forests of Southeast Asia.
Many agamid lizards possess the ability to change color to blend in with their surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agamid - a lizard of the family Agamidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Agamidae, the scientific family name, from Greek 'agamis' (unmarried, used for a type of lizard).
