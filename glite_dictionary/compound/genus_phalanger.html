genus Phalanger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs fəˈlæŋdʒər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fəˈlæŋdʒə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Phalangeridae, comprising cuscuses, marsupials native to Australia, New Guinea, and nearby islands.
genus of marsupials called cuscuses

Examples

The genus Phalanger includes several species of cuscuses found in Australasia.
Researchers discovered a new member of the genus Phalanger in remote forests.
Conservation efforts focus on the endangered species within the genus Phalanger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuscus - a marsupial native to Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From New Latin Phalanger, from Greek phalángion (spider's web, referring to a kind of web-fingered animal).
