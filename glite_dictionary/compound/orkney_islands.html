Orkney Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔrkni ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈɔːkni ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of about 70 islands off the northeastern coast of Scotland, in the North Atlantic and North Sea; noted for their archaeological sites and natural beauty
archipelago off northeastern Scotland

Examples

My grandparents visited the Orkney Islands last summer and loved the stunning coastal scenery.
Excavations on the Orkney Islands have revealed Neolithic settlements thousands of years old.
The unique wildlife of the Orkney Islands attracts nature enthusiasts from around the world.

Etymology

From Old Norse "Orkneyjar," meaning 'islands of the orcs' (sea calves or seals).
