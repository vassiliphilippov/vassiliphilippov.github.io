UN agency

1. NOUN

/ juː ˈɛn ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / juː ˈɛn ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an organization that is part of the United Nations responsible for carrying out specific functions or missions
organization within the United Nations

Examples

The World Health Organization is a UN agency focused on global public health.
Several UN agencies provided aid after the natural disaster struck the region.
UNICEF is a UN agency dedicated to helping children in need worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United Nations - an international organization founded in 1945 to promote peace, security, and cooperation

Etymology

UN is an abbreviation of United Nations, founded in 1945 (agency from Medieval Latin agentia, 'doing, operation').
