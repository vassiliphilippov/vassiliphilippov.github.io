tactical maneuver

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tɪ.kəl məˈnuː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tɪ.kəl məˈnjuː.və / (BrE)
an action or movement made to achieve a specific advantage in a tactical situation, often in military or strategic contexts
action for tactical advantage

Examples

The general ordered a tactical maneuver to flank the enemy forces at dawn.
In chess, a well-planned tactical maneuver can turn the tide of a game.
The coach praised the team's quick tactical maneuver that led to the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term goal
  • Antonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake, especially in a tactical context
