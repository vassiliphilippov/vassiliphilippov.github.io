polemical style

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɛmɪkəl staɪl / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪkəl staɪl / (BrE)
a manner of writing or speaking that is controversial, argumentative, or strongly critical, often intended to provoke debate
argumentative or controversial manner of expression

Examples

The critic's polemical style made his reviews both engaging and provocative.
She is famous for her polemical style when debating social issues.
The polemical style of the essay led to widespread discussion among scholars.

Synset Examples

The author's polemical style often alienates readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polemical - relating to strongly critical or argumentative writing or speech

Etymology

From 'polemical' (from Greek *polemikos*, meaning 'warlike, hostile') + 'style'.
