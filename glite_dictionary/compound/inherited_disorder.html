inherited disorder

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd dɪsˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a disease or condition that is passed genetically from parents to offspring
a genetically transmitted disease or condition
medical

Examples

Cystic fibrosis is an inherited disorder that affects the lungs and digestive system.
Doctors can use genetic testing to identify the risk of certain inherited disorders.
Hemophilia is a well-known example of an inherited disorder affecting blood clotting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic disorder - a disorder caused by abnormalities in genes or chromosomes
