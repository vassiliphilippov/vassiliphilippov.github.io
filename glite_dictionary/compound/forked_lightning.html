forked lightning

1. NOUN

/ fɔrkt ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːkt ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of lightning that travels in a zigzag path with branches or divisions resembling a fork.
zigzag, branching lightning flash

Examples

A brilliant flash of forked lightning illuminated the night sky during the storm.
We watched as forked lightning danced across the horizon, followed by a clap of thunder.
Photographers often try to capture forked lightning because of its dramatic appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sheet lightning - lightning that illuminates a whole cloud, without a visible bolt
