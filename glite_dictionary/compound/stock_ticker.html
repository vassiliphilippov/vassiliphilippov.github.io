stock ticker

1. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈtɪkər / (AmE), / stɒk ˈtɪkə / (BrE)
A telegraphic machine that automatically prints stock prices and financial information on a continuous strip of paper, known as ticker tape.
machine for printing stock prices on tape

Examples

The stock ticker relayed the latest share prices directly to the trading floor.
In the early twentieth century, every brokerage office had a stock ticker in a prominent location.
Collectors sometimes display antique stock tickers as reminders of financial history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticker tape - narrow strip of paper used to record information from a ticker

Etymology

From 'stock' (as in stock market) and 'ticker', so called because of the machine's ticking sound when in operation.
