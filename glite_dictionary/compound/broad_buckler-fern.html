broad buckler-fern

1. NOUN

/ brɔd ˈbʌk.lɚ fɝn / (AmE), / brɔːd ˈbʌk.lə fɜːn / (BrE)
A European fern species with triangular fronds, commonly found in woodlands and known for its broad, shield-shaped leaves.
a species of European shield fern

Examples

The broad buckler-fern thrives in the moist, shaded woodlands across much of Europe.
Gardeners often plant the broad buckler-fern for its attractive, lacy fronds.
During our hike, we spotted a cluster of broad buckler-ferns growing beside the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield fern - a type of fern with shield-shaped fronds
