genus Eleusine

1. NOUN

/ ˈʤiː.nəs ɪˈluːsɪni / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɛˈluːsɪni / (BrE)
A genus of annual and perennial grasses native to savannas and upland grasslands, including some species cultivated as cereals.
genus of annual and perennial grasses

Examples

The species genus Eleusine is widespread in tropical and subtropical regions.
Researchers studied the distribution of genus Eleusine in African savannas.
Certain grains from the genus Eleusine are important in traditional agriculture.

Etymology

From Latin 'Eleusine', the genus name given in botanical taxonomy.
