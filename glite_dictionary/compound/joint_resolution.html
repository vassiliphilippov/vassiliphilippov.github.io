joint resolution

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔɪnt ˌrɛzəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪnt ˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a legislative measure passed by both houses of a legislative body, which becomes law when approved by the executive or passed over the executive's veto
measure passed by both houses of a legislature
legal

Examples

The Senate passed a joint resolution to authorize military intervention.
Congress approved a joint resolution to amend the constitution.
After lengthy debate, the joint resolution was signed into law by the President.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill - a draft of a proposed law presented to a legislature for enactment
  • Degree Lower: concurrent resolution - a resolution adopted by both houses that does not have the force of law
