round-bottom

1. ADJ

/ ˈraʊnd ˌbɑtəm / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd ˌbɒtəm / (BrE)
Having a rounded bottom, typically used to describe containers or objects whose base is curved rather than flat.
having a rounded or curved bottom

Examples

The chemist carefully heated the solution in a round-bottom flask.
For certain experiments, a round-bottom vessel is preferred for even heating.
She placed the round-bottom glassware on the stand before beginning the reaction.

Synset Examples

round-bottom flasks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flat-bottom - having a flat or level base
