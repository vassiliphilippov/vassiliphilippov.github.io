elaterid beetle

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪtərɪd ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪtərɪd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
Any of various beetles of the family Elateridae, commonly known as click beetles, found in many regions worldwide.
beetle of the family Elateridae (click beetle)

Examples

The elaterid beetle is easily recognizable by the clicking sound it makes when it flips itself upright.
Entomologists studied several species of elaterid beetles collected from the forest floor.
Farmers are concerned about the damage elaterid beetles and their larvae can cause to crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: click beetle - a beetle of the family Elateridae, known for its clicking mechanism

Etymology

From New Latin Elateridae, the scientific name for this beetle family.
