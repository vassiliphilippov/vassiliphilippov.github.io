artificial insemination

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.t̬əˈfɪʃ.əl ɪnˌsem.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl ɪnˌsem.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing semen into a female's uterus or oviduct by artificial means, rather than by sexual intercourse, to achieve pregnancy
introduction of semen without intercourse
medical

Examples

The couple chose artificial insemination as a way to conceive after years of infertility.
Some breeders use artificial insemination to introduce desirable traits into animal populations.
Advances in artificial insemination have helped many people start families who otherwise could not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in vitro fertilization - fertilization of an egg outside the body

Etymology

From Latin 'artificialis' (artificial) and 'inseminatio' (act of sowing seed).
