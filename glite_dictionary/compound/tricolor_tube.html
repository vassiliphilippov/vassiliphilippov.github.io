tricolor tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌkʌlər tuːb / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌkʌlə tjuːb / (BrE)
a type of color television picture tube that uses three primary color elements to produce the full spectrum of colors
color TV tube combining three primary colors

Examples

The technician replaced the old tricolor tube to restore vibrant colors on the television.
Early color televisions relied on a tricolor tube to display images in red, green, and blue.
A malfunction in the tricolor tube can cause distorted colors on the screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathode-ray tube - a vacuum tube used to display images in televisions and monitors
