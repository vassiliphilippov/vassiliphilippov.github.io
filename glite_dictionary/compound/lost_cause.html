lost cause

1. NOUN

/ lɔst kɔz / (AmE), / lɒst kɔːz / (BrE)
a person or thing that cannot succeed or be changed because hope for success has been lost or is futile
a hopeless or futile case or effort

Examples

She knew trying to convince him to change his mind was a lost cause.
After several failed attempts, the project began to look like a lost cause.
Don't waste your time; that old car is a lost cause now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopeless case - a person or thing for which there is no hope of success
  • Synonym: vain attempt - an effort that is unsuccessful and futile

2. NOUN

/ lɑst kɔz / (AmE), / lɒst kɔːz / (BrE)
a person, thing, or effort that has no chance of success or improvement
hopeless effort or situation

Examples

After several failed attempts, negotiating with them felt like a lost cause.
She refused to give up, even when everyone said it was a lost cause.
Restoring the old, damaged painting turned out to be a lost cause.

Synset Examples

Trying to change his mind is a lost cause.
He felt like a lost cause after failing again.
She realized the project was a lost cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopeless case - a person or situation regarded as impossible to improve
