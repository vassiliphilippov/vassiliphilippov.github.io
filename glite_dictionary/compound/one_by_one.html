one by one

1. ADV

/ wʌn baɪ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn baɪ wʌn / (BrE)
Used to indicate that things are done or happen individually and in succession, rather than all at once.
individually, in succession

Examples

The students entered the classroom one by one as the bell rang.
She handed out the gifts one by one to each guest.
The lights went out one by one until the room was completely dark.

Synset Examples

she sold the plates by the piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one at a time - happening individually, not simultaneously

2. ADV

/ wʌn baɪ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn baɪ wʌn / (BrE)
separately and in succession; individually, with each item or person dealt with in turn
individually, in sequence

Examples

The students entered the classroom one by one as the bell rang.
She checked the documents one by one to make sure nothing was missing.
As the candles were lit one by one, the room grew brighter.

3. ADV

/ wʌn baɪ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn baɪ wʌn / (BrE)
Used to describe things happening in succession, with each item or person following the previous one, rather than all at once.
in succession, one after another

Examples

The guests approached the host one by one to express their thanks.
The teacher called on the students one by one to answer the question.
As the clock struck midnight, the candles went out one by one.

Synset Examples

the prisoners came out one by one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one after another - in sequence, following one by one
Source: WordNet

4. ADV

/ wʌn baɪ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn baɪ wʌn / (BrE)
in succession, with each person or thing dealt with individually and in order
in succession, one at a time

Examples

The students entered the classroom one by one to collect their results.
As the power returned, the lights came on one by one throughout the house.
The kittens followed their mother one by one into the garden.

Synset Examples

He would plod through the assignments one by one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individually - separately, one at a time
