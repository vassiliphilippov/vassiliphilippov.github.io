Lennon specs

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.ən spɛks / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.ən spɛks / (BrE)
Eyeglasses with round frames, especially those resembling the style worn by John Lennon.
round-framed eyeglasses style, after John Lennon

Examples

He wore his Lennon specs to the retro-themed party, completing his 1960s look.
Many vintage shops now carry Lennon specs due to their enduring popularity.
She recognized him instantly by the round Lennon specs perched on his nose.

Synset Examples

Windsors are very chic this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Windsor glasses - eyeglasses with round wire frames; similar style made famous in early 20th century
  • Antonym: horn-rimmed glasses - eyeglasses with thick frames made of horn or plastic; contrasting style

Etymology

Named after John Lennon (1940–1980), member of The Beatles, known for wearing distinctive round eyeglasses, and specs (short for spectacles).
