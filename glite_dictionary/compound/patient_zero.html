patient zero

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪʃənt ˈzɪəɹoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpeɪʃənt ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the first identified person infected in an outbreak of a disease, from whom the infection may have spread to others
first identified case in a disease outbreak

Examples

The search for patient zero helped researchers understand how the virus spread.
After months of investigation, authorities finally identified patient zero in the epidemic.
Tracking down patient zero can be essential for preventing further infections.

Synset Examples

They believe they have found patient zero in the investigation.
Identifying patient zero was crucial in controlling the disease.
The team traced the outbreak back to patient zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: index case - the first documented patient in an epidemiological investigation

Etymology

Modelled on medical 'patient' + 'zero' (indicating the starting point of an index). Popularized during investigations into the early AIDS epidemic.
