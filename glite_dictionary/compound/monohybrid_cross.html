monohybrid cross

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊnoʊˈhaɪbrɪd krɒs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈhaɪbrɪd krɒs / (BrE)
a genetic cross between two individuals focusing on a single trait with two alleles, as in Mendel's experiments with peas
genetic cross involving one trait

Examples

In genetics class, we studied a monohybrid cross to understand inheritance patterns involving one trait.
Mendel’s famous experiments on peas used the monohybrid cross to reveal basic laws of heredity.
The results from a monohybrid cross helped explain the concept of dominant and recessive alleles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dihybrid cross - a genetic cross focusing on two traits

Etymology

From 'mono-' meaning 'one' and 'hybrid cross', used in genetics.
