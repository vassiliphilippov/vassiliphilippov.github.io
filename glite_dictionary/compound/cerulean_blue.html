cerulean blue

1. NOUN

/ səˈruːliən bluː / (AmE), / sɪˈruːlɪən bluː / (BrE)
a light greenish-blue pigment made from oxides of cobalt and tin, often used in painting
a light greenish-blue pigment

Examples

The artist chose cerulean blue for the sky in her landscape painting.
A splash of cerulean blue brightened the otherwise muted color palette.
He mixed cerulean blue with white to achieve a softer shade for the seascape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - a bright blue color like a clear sky

Etymology

From Latin 'caeruleus' meaning 'sky-blue'.
