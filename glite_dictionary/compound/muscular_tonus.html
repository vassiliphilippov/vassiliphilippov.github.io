muscular tonus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjələr ˈtoʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə ˈtəʊ.nəs / (BrE)
the normal state of slight, continuous contraction maintained by muscles, even when at rest
normal muscle tension or tone
medical

Examples

Physical therapists assess muscular tonus to diagnose neuromuscular conditions.
Improved muscular tonus helps maintain posture and joint stability.
Loss of muscular tonus can be a sign of neurological disease.

Synset Examples

exercise improves muscle tone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscle tone - the normal level of firmness or tension in muscles
  • Degree Lower: hypotonia - reduced muscle tonicity
  • Degree Higher: hypertonia - increased muscle tonicity

Etymology

From Latin 'tonus' (tone, tension), from Greek 'tonos' (stretching, tension).
