family Opisthognathidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ɒˌpɪsθoʊɡˈnæθɪdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ɒˌpɪsθɒɡˈnæθɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small marine fishes known as jawfishes, characterized by their large mouths and burrowing habits.
a family of marine jawfishes

Examples

The family Opisthognathidae includes species that are popular among aquarium enthusiasts for their unique behaviors.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Opisthognathidae off the coast of Indonesia.
Members of the family Opisthognathidae are known for constructing elaborate burrows in sandy sea bottoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jawfish - a small marine fish of the Opisthognathidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'opistho-' (behind) and 'gnathos' (jaw), forming the taxonomic name Opisthognathidae.
