Scottish deerhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈdɪrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈdɪəhɑʊnd / (BrE)
a very large and tall breed of rough-coated dog developed in Scotland for hunting deer; known as the royal dog of Scotland
breed of large hunting dog from Scotland

Examples

The Scottish deerhound is admired for its graceful stride and gentle temperament.
For centuries, nobles in Scotland prized the Scottish deerhound for its prowess in hunting deer.
Their Scottish deerhound quickly became a favorite among visitors due to its striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish wolfhound - a tall breed of dog originally bred for hunting wolves in Ireland

Etymology

Scottish, from Scotland + deerhound (a hound for hunting deer)
