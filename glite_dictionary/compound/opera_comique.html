opera comique

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpərə koʊˈmik / (AmE), / ˌɒpərə kɒˈmik / (BrE)
a genre of opera, originating in France, characterized by spoken dialogue and usually a happy ending
French opera with spoken dialogue and happy ending

Examples

The company's next production is a classic opera comique featuring sparkling French melodies.
She specializes in singing leading roles in opera comique because of her strong acting skills.
Unlike grand opera, an opera comique includes spoken dialogue between musical numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opera buffa - an Italian comic opera, typically with lively and humorous themes
  • Antonym: opera seria - serious or tragic Italian opera from the 18th century

Etymology

French, literally 'comic opera'.
