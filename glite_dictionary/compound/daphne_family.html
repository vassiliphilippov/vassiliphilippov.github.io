daphne family

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæfni ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈdæfni ˌfæməli / (BrE)
a family of trees, shrubs, and herbs (Thymelaeaceae) with tough bark, found especially in Australia and tropical Africa
family of tough-barked trees, shrubs, and herbs

Examples

The daphne family includes many ornamental shrubs valued for their fragrant flowers.
Certain members of the daphne family are known for their tough, flexible bark, which is used in papermaking.
Some plants in the daphne family are found in tropical regions of Africa and Australia.

Etymology

Named after the genus Daphne, from Greek 'daphne' (laurel).
