Oxford grey

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑks.fərd ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒks.fəd ɡreɪ / (BrE)
a very dark shade of grey, often associated with traditional British academic or formal attire
a very dark grey color

Examples

He wore an Oxford grey suit to the graduation ceremony.
The living room was painted in a deep Oxford grey to give it a sophisticated feel.
She preferred Oxford grey for her winter coat, finding it both elegant and practical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charcoal - a dark grey color
  • Degree Lower: pewter - a medium-dark grey color

Etymology

Named after the city of Oxford, England, where the color is traditionally used in academic robes and attire.
