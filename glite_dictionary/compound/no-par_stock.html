no-par stock

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊ ˈpɑr stɑk / (AmE), / ˌnəʊ ˈpɑː stɒk / (BrE)
Stock that has no par value specified in the corporate charter or on the stock certificate.
stock with no stated par value

Examples

The company decided to issue no-par stock to allow greater flexibility in pricing.
Investors should understand the legal implications of purchasing no-par stock.
Unlike traditional shares, no-par stock does not state a minimum selling price.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: par value - the nominal or face value of a bond, share, or coupon
