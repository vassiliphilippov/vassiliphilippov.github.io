broadcast scheduling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæst ˈskɛdʒuˌlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːst ˈʃedjuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of planning and arranging the sequence and timing of programs on broadcast media such as television, radio, or online channels
planning broadcast program schedules

Examples

The team in charge of broadcast scheduling worked late to finalize the fall lineup.
Changes in broadcast scheduling can significantly impact a show's ratings.
Advances in technology have made broadcast scheduling more flexible and data-driven.

Synset Examples

In Broadcast programming, counterprogramming is the practice of offering television programs to attract an audience from another television station airing a major event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: programming - the arrangement or scheduling of broadcast content
