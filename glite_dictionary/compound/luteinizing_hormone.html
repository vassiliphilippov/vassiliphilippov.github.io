luteinizing hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtiˌnaɪzɪŋ ˈhɔːrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈluːtɪnʌɪzɪŋ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
A hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates ovulation in females and the production of androgens in males.
gonadotropic hormone stimulating ovulation or androgen production
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check her luteinizing hormone levels.
An increase in luteinizing hormone triggers ovulation during the menstrual cycle.
Low luteinizing hormone can cause problems with fertility in both men and women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follicle-stimulating hormone - A hormone that stimulates the growth of ovarian follicles in females; involved in reproductive processes

Etymology

From 'luteinize' (make or become yellow, refering to the corpus luteum) + 'hormone'; 'luteinize' from Latin 'luteus' (yellow).
