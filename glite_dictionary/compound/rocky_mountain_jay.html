Rocky Mountain jay

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑki ˈmaʊntən dʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɒki ˈmaʊntɪn dʒeɪ / (BrE)
A Canada jay (Perisoreus canadensis) with a white head, found widely from Montana to Arizona in the Rocky Mountains.
Canada jay of the Rocky Mountains

Examples

We spotted a Rocky Mountain jay while hiking near the tree line.
The Rocky Mountain jay is known for its boldness around campsites.
Several Rocky Mountain jays gathered near the picnic area hoping for scraps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada jay - a North American bird species, Perisoreus canadensis
