desert mariposa tulip

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɝt ˌmærɪˈpoʊsə ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːt ˌmærɪˈpəʊsə ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A mariposa lily with clusters of bell-shaped vermilion, orange, or yellow flowers on short stems, found in southern California, Arizona, and Mexico.
flowering plant; southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The desert mariposa tulip blooms in vibrant colors during the spring in the Mojave Desert.
Botanists studied the adaptation of the desert mariposa tulip to arid environments.
The garden display featured several rare desert mariposa tulips collected from southern California.
