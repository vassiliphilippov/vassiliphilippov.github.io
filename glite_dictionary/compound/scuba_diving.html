scuba diving

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːbə ˌdaɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskuːbə ˌdaɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of swimming and exploring underwater while using a self-contained underwater breathing apparatus (scuba).
underwater swimming with scuba equipment

Examples

We took a course to learn scuba diving before our trip to the Caribbean.
Many marine biologists practice scuba diving to study coral reefs up close.
My dream vacation includes lots of scuba diving in tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: snorkeling - swimming at the surface with a snorkel but not using scuba gear
  • Degree Lower: skin diving - underwater swimming without scuba, often just holding breath

Etymology

Acronym from 'Self-Contained Underwater Breathing Apparatus'.
