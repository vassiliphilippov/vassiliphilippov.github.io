Ringer's solution

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋərz səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋəz səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
an aqueous solution containing sodium chloride, potassium chloride, and calcium chloride, isotonic with animal tissues, used to correct dehydration and as a medium in physiological experiments
isotonic saline solution for medical use
medical

Examples

The laboratory prepared Ringer's solution to maintain the tissue sample's viability during the experiment.
Patients suffering from dehydration may be given Ringer's solution intravenously to restore fluid balance.
The contractile properties of the muscle were studied while it was bathed in Ringer's solution.

Etymology

Named after Sydney Ringer, British physiologist (1836–1910), who developed the solution.
