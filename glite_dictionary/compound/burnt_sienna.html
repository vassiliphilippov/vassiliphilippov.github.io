burnt sienna

1. NOUN

/ bɝːnt siˈɛnə / (AmE), / bɜːnt siˈɛnə / (BrE)
a reddish-brown pigment or color, originally made by heating natural sienna earth
a reddish-brown pigment or color

Examples

The artist mixed burnt sienna with white to create a warm, earthy tone.
Her living room walls were painted in burnt sienna to give the space a cozy feel.
He prefers to use burnt sienna in his landscape paintings for its rich color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sienna - a yellow-brown natural earth pigment

Etymology

From 'burnt' (past participle of 'burn') and 'sienna', from Italian 'terra di Siena' (earth from Siena, a city in Italy).

2. NOUN

/ bɝnt siˈɛnə / (AmE), / bɜːnt siˈɛnə / (BrE)
a reddish-brown color produced by heating raw sienna pigment, commonly used in painting and art
a reddish-brown color used in art

Examples

She painted the autumn leaves in a warm shade of burnt sienna.
The artist mixed burnt sienna with white to achieve a softer hue.
He chose burnt sienna for the walls to create a cozy atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sienna - a yellow-brown earth pigment used as a colorant

Etymology

From Italian 'terra di Siena' (earth of Siena), named after Siena, Italy; 'burnt' refers to the heating process changing the color.
