family Geometridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒiːˌɑːmɛˈtrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌdʒiːəʊˈmɛtrɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of moths whose larvae are known as measuring worms or inchworms, characterized by their slender bodies and looping gait.
a family of moths (measuring worms/inchworms)

Examples

The larvae of the family Geometridae are often called inchworms because of their distinctive movement.
Many species in the family Geometridae play important roles in their ecosystems as herbivores.
The family Geometridae includes thousands of moth species found around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inchworm - larval stage of Geometridae, known for looping movement
  • Degree Lower: caterpillar - larval form of a moth or butterfly

Etymology

From New Latin Geometridae, from genus Geometra, from Greek geometres (earth measurer).
