genus Chamaea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Paridae, containing the wrentit (Chamaea fasciata).
genus of birds including the wrentit

Examples

The genus Chamaea is best known for its only living species, the wrentit.
Researchers have studied the evolutionary history of the genus Chamaea for decades.
Unlike other Paridae, the genus Chamaea contains just a single extant species.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and taxonomic name 'Chamaea', derived from Ancient Greek 'khamaí' (on the ground).
