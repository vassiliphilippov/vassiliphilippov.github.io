Christmas Eve

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪsməs ˈiv / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsməs ˈiːv / (BrE)
The day before Christmas, celebrated on December 24th in many countries, often marked by festivities and anticipation of Christmas Day.
the day before Christmas

Examples

On Christmas Eve, families often gather for a special dinner and exchange gifts.
Many children find it difficult to sleep on Christmas Eve because they're excited for Christmas morning.
Church services are held on Christmas Eve in many communities around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Christmas - the annual Christian festival on December 25th celebrating Christ's birth

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsməs iv / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsməs iːv / (BrE)
The evening or day before Christmas Day, celebrated on December 24th, often marked by festive preparations and traditions.
the evening before Christmas Day

Examples

We exchanged gifts with our family on Christmas Eve before attending midnight Mass.
Children often hang their stockings on Christmas Eve in hopes of a visit from Santa Claus.
The streets were filled with festive lights and music on Christmas Eve.

Synset Examples

Kids eagerly awaited Santy to arrive on Christmas Eve.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Christmas Day - December 25th, day celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From 'Christmas' (the festival commemorating Christ's birth) and 'eve' (evening before an event).
