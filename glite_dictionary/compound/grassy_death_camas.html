grassy death camas

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræs.i dɛθ ˈkæm.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡræs.i dɛθ ˈkæm.əs / (BrE)
a poisonous plant native to western North America and Mexico, known for its grass-like leaves and toxicity to grazing animals
poisonous western North American plant

Examples

Ranchers must be careful, as grassy death camas is extremely toxic to livestock.
The fields in early summer were dotted with the pale flowers of grassy death camas.
Veterinarians warn that ingestion of grassy death camas can be fatal for cattle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death camas - any of several highly poisonous plants of the genus Zigadenus
