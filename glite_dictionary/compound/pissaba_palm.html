pissaba palm

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsɑːbə pɑːm / (AmE), / pɪˈsɑːbə pɑːm / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree whose strong leaf fibers are used to make ropes, mats, and brushes.
Brazilian palm yielding useful fibers

Examples

The fibers of the pissaba palm are commonly used in the manufacture of durable rope.
Local artisans weave baskets from the tough leaves of the pissaba palm.
Exporters rely on the pissaba palm for its strong, versatile fibers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piassava palm - alternative spelling of pissaba palm
