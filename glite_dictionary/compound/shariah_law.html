shariah law

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈriː.ə lɔː / (AmE), / ʃəˈriː.ə lɔː / (BrE)
The religious and legal code governing the moral and legal aspects of life in Islam, derived from the Koran and the teachings and example of Muhammad.
Islamic religious and legal code

Examples

Some countries implement shariah law as the foundation of their legal systems.
The debate over whether shariah law should influence civil law is ongoing in several nations.
Legal scholars study shariah law to understand its impact on family and criminal cases.

Synset Examples

sharia is only applicable to Muslims
under Islamic law there is no separation of church and state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islamic law - Religious law based on the teachings of Islam

Etymology

From Arabic 'šarīʿa' (path, way), referring to Islamic law.
