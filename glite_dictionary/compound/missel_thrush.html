missel thrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəl θrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsəl θrʌʃ / (BrE)
a large European songbird of the thrush family, known for feeding on mistletoe berries, with spotted underparts and a loud, fluting song
a large European thrush

Examples

The missel thrush was singing loudly at the edge of the forest.
Farmers often see missel thrushes feeding on mistletoe berries in winter.
A pair of missel thrushes nested high in the old oak tree.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: song thrush - a smaller European thrush with spotted breast
  • Synonym: fieldfare - a European thrush species closely related to the missel thrush

Etymology

From Middle English 'missel' (mistletoe) + 'thrush'.
