half-life

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌlaɪf / (BrE)
the time required for a quantity to decrease to half its initial value, especially the time taken for half the atoms in a radioactive substance to decay
time for amount to decrease by half

Examples

The half-life of carbon-14 is about 5,730 years.
Some medications have a short half-life, requiring more frequent dosing.
Scientists measured the half-life of the new isotope accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - decrease in quantity, especially of radioactive substances
  • Synonym: mean life - the average lifetime of a radioactive particle before decay

Etymology

Formed from 'half' and 'life' to denote the period for half of something to diminish.
