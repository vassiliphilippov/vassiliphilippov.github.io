acute gastritis

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjut ɡæsˈtraɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ɡæsˈtraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A sudden inflammation of the stomach lining, often caused by ingesting irritants such as alcohol or certain medications.
sudden stomach lining inflammation
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with acute gastritis after experiencing severe stomach pain and nausea.
Overuse of painkillers can sometimes lead to acute gastritis.
Doctors prescribed a special diet to help manage his acute gastritis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronic gastritis - long-term inflammation of the stomach lining

Etymology

From Latin 'gastritis' (inflammation of the stomach) and 'acutus' (sharp, sudden).
