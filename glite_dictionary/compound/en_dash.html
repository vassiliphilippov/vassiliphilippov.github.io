en dash

1. NOUN

/ ɛn dæʃ / (AmE), / ɛn dæʃ / (BrE)
A dash used in printing and writing, typically the width of an 'n', that is used to indicate ranges or connections between numbers, dates, or words.
punctuation dash of medium length

Examples

The dates 1990–2000 should be connected by an en dash, not a hyphen.
You can insert an en dash in Microsoft Word by pressing Ctrl+minus on the numeric keypad.
In formal writing, an en dash is preferred for indicating a range between pages, such as pages 15–20.

Synset Examples

The en dash is commonly used to indicate a closed range of values — a range with clearly defined and finite upper and lower boundaries — roughly signifying what might otherwise be communicated by the word ‘through’.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: em dash - a dash that is one 'em' wide (the width of the letter 'M')
  • Degree Lower: hyphen - a short horizontal mark used to join words or break them at the end of a line

Etymology

From the width of the letter 'N' in printing, contrasting with 'em dash,' which is the width of 'M'.
