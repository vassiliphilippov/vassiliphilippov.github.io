long-term

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ tɜrm / (AmE), / lɒŋ tɜːm / (BrE)
lasting, relating to, or affecting a relatively long period of time.
lasting for a long period

Examples

She decided to make a long-term investment in real estate.
The company is focusing on long-term growth rather than immediate profits.
A long-term illness can have significant financial and emotional consequences.

Synset Examples

the long-run significance of the elections
the long-term reconstruction of countries damaged by the war
a long-term investment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-term - lasting or relating to a short period of time
  • Synonym: long-run - relating to or lasting over a long period
