ecological warfare

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkɑːlədʒɪkəl ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / iːˌkɒlədʒɪkəl ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
Deliberate acts of violence or sabotage carried out to achieve environmental or ecological political aims.
violence to achieve environmental aims

Examples

The activists resorted to ecological warfare by sabotaging construction equipment.
Some governments have condemned ecological warfare as a form of extremism.
Debates continue over whether ecological warfare helps or harms the environmental movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmental terrorism - use of violence to protect the environment; alternative political term
