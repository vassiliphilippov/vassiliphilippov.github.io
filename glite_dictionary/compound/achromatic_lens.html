achromatic lens

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈmætɪk lɛnz / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈmatɪk lɛnz / (BrE)
A lens made of two or more elements designed to reduce or eliminate chromatic aberration, producing images with minimal color distortion.
lens that minimizes color distortion

Examples

The microscope was fitted with an achromatic lens to ensure clear, sharp images.
Using an achromatic lens eliminates the color fringing seen in cheaper cameras.
Engineers developed a new achromatic lens for high-precision optical instruments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chromatic aberration - failure of a lens to focus all colors to the same point

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'chrōma' (color) and Latin 'lens' (lentil; glass).
