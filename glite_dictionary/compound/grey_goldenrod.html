grey goldenrod

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈɡoʊl.dənˌrɑd / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈɡəʊl.dənˌrɒd / (BrE)
a North American plant (Solidago nemoralis) with yellow flowers, sometimes used in dyeing
a plant with yellow flowers, used in dyeing

Examples

The fields were full of grey goldenrod in late summer, their yellow blooms brightening the landscape.
Early settlers used grey goldenrod as a natural source of dye for fabrics.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of grey goldenrod across the eastern United States.
