airborne transmission

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌbɔrn trænzˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈeəbɔːn trænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the spread of an infectious agent through the air as an aerosol, typically entering through the respiratory tract
spread of infection through the air
medical

Examples

Many diseases that cause pandemics rely on airborne transmission from person to person.
Improvements in ventilation can reduce the risk of airborne transmission in crowded settings.
Researchers studied how airborne transmission contributed to the rapid spread of the virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: droplet transmission - spread of infection through contact with respiratory droplets
