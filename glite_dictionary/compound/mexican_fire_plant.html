Mexican fire plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən ˈfaɪɚ plænt / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən ˈfaɪə plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant with bright red and green foliage, native to Mexico and the United States, commonly known as the poinsettia
a plant, also called poinsettia

Examples

Many people use a Mexican fire plant as a festive decoration during the holiday season.
The bright red leaves of the Mexican fire plant add color to winter gardens.
Botanists often refer to the Mexican fire plant by its scientific name, Euphorbia pulcherrima.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poinsettia - a popular plant with red and green leaves used as a Christmas decoration
