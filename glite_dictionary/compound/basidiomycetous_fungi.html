basidiomycetous fungi

1. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdioʊˈmaɪsiːtəs ˈfʌŋɡaɪ / (AmE), / bəˌsɪdɪəʊˈmaɪsiːtəs ˈfʌŋɡiː / (BrE)
Fungi belonging to the subdivision Basidiomycota, typically producing spores on basidia.
fungi of the Basidiomycota subdivision
medical

Examples

Many common mushrooms are classified as basidiomycetous fungi in scientific taxonomy.
Some basidiomycetous fungi play a vital role in decomposing wood in forests.
Rusts and smuts are parasitic basidiomycetous fungi affecting various crops.

Etymology

From modern Latin Basidiomycetes, based on Greek basidion (little pedestal) + mykēs (fungus).
