vice crime

1. NOUN

/ vaɪs kraɪm / (AmE), / vaɪs kraɪm / (BrE)
A crime involving immoral or depraved conduct, such as prostitution or illegal gambling, that is prohibited by law.
crime involving immoral conduct (e.g. prostitution, gambling)

Examples

Local authorities have been cracking down on vice crimes such as illegal gambling and prostitution.
The city established a special task force to address vice crimes in nightlife districts.
New laws targeting vice crimes have sparked debates about personal freedom versus public morality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victimless crime - a crime that does not directly harm another individual, often applied to vice crimes

Etymology

From 'vice' (immoral conduct) and 'crime' (an act forbidden by law).
