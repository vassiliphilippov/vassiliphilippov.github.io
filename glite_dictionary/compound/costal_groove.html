costal groove

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.stəl ɡruv / (AmE), / ˈkɒs.təl ɡruːv / (BrE)
a groove along the inner surface of a rib where nerves and blood vessels are located
groove on the inside of a rib
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the costal groove protects important nerves and blood vessels.
An injury to the costal groove can result in serious complications.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the costal groove on human ribs.

Etymology

From Latin 'costa' (rib) + English 'groove'.
