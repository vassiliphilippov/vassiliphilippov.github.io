drilling bit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪlɪŋ bɪt / (AmE), / ˈdrɪlɪŋ bɪt / (BrE)
a tool or device attached to the end of a drill, specifically designed for cutting or boring through rock, earth, or other materials when drilling for oil
tool used in drilling for oil

Examples

The drilling bit wore down quickly while drilling through the hard rock layer.
Engineers inspected the drilling bit before lowering it into the oil well.
A new type of drilling bit can increase efficiency in deep-sea drilling operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill bit - the cutting part of a drill used for making holes
