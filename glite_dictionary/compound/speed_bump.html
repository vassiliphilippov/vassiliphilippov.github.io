speed bump

1. NOUN

/ spiːd bʌmp / (AmE), / spiːd bʌmp / (BrE)
A raised band across a road, designed to slow down traffic.
raised road strip to slow traffic

Examples

Drivers must slow down when they approach a speed bump in residential areas.
The city installed several speed bumps to reduce accidents near the school.
She didn't notice the speed bump and spilled her coffee in the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleeping policeman - British term for a speed bump

2. NOUN

/ spid bʌmp / (AmE), / spiːd bʌmp / (BrE)
A problem or obstacle that slows progress or causes difficulty.
obstacle that slows progress

Examples

This new regulation is just another speed bump on our path to success.
Every project encounters a speed bump or two before reaching completion.
Losing their main investor was a major speed bump for the startup.

Synset Examples

Navigating that speed bump in the road was tricky.
Watch out for that Newfoundland speed bump; it's a doozy!
The project faced a speed bump due to budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - a thing that blocks one's way or hinders progress
  • Synonym: setback - a reversal or check in progress

Etymology

Modelled after the literal raised area on roads called a 'speed bump', used metaphorically since the late 20th century.
