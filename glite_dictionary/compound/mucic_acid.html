mucic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline acid (C6H10O8) obtained by oxidizing galactose or milk sugar, used in chemical analysis and research
a crystalline acid found in milk sugar
medical

Examples

Researchers isolated mucic acid from the oxidation of galactose in the laboratory.
The chemical properties of mucic acid make it useful in analytical chemistry.
Crystals of mucic acid were observed under the microscope during the experiment.

Etymology

From Latin 'mucus' (slime), due to its presence in milk mucilage.
