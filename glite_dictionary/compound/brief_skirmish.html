brief skirmish

1. NOUN

/ briːf ˈskɝː.mɪʃ / (AmE), / briːf ˈskɜː.mɪʃ / (BrE)
a short and minor fight or conflict, often occurring suddenly and not escalating into a large-scale battle
short, minor conflict or fight

Examples

The two opposing politicians engaged in a brief skirmish during the televised debate.
After a brief skirmish with the guards, the protestors were escorted from the building.
The animals had a brief skirmish before going their separate ways.

Synset Examples

The two teams had a brief skirmish during practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skirmish - a minor fight or battle
