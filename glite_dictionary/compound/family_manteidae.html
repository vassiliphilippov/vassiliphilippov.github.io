family Manteidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli mænˈtiːɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli mænˈtiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as mantises, characterized by their elongated bodies and prominent forelegs adapted for grasping prey.
family of insects: mantises

Examples

The family Manteidae includes over 2,400 species of praying mantises.
Biologists study the unique hunting techniques of insects in the family Manteidae.
Many gardeners appreciate the family Manteidae for their role in controlling pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - large predatory insect with long forelegs

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Manteidae', from the genus name 'Mantis'.
