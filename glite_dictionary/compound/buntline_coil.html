buntline coil

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌntˌlaɪn kɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈbʌntˌlaɪn kɔɪl / (BrE)
A basic method for coiling and storing rope, usually to prevent tangling, especially on ships.
method of coiling rope to prevent tangling

Examples

The sailor demonstrated how to neatly arrange the rope using a buntline coil.
After washing the deck, she put the hose away in a buntline coil to keep it tidy.
Properly making a buntline coil helps ensure ropes are ready for immediate use.

Synset Examples

Tie your rope in a gasket coil to prevent it from tangling.

Etymology

From 'buntline', a nautical term referring to ropes used for gathering up the bottom of a sail, and 'coil', meaning to wind into a series of loops.
