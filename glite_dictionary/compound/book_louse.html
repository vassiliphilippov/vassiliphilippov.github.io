book louse

1. NOUN

/ bʊk laʊs / (AmE), / bʊk laʊs / (BrE)
a minute, wingless insect that feeds on mold in books and papers, sometimes damaging them
minute insect damaging books and papers

Examples

A book louse can often be found in old libraries or storage rooms filled with paper.
The preservation team discovered a book louse infestation damaging rare manuscripts.
Despite its name, the book louse does not bite humans and is mostly harmless except to paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psocid - a member of the Psocidae family of insects, to which the book louse belongs
