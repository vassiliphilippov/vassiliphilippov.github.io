petroleum geology

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtroʊliəm dʒiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊliəm dʒiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of geology that studies the origin, occurrence, movement, accumulation, and exploration of oil and natural gas resources
study of oil and gas geology
medical

Examples

After studying petroleum geology, she started working for an international oil company.
Advances in petroleum geology have led to more effective methods for exploring oil reserves.
The university offers a master's program specializing in petroleum geology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: geology - the science that deals with the earth's physical structure and substance
  • Degree Lower: economic geology - the study of materials which can be used for economic and/or industrial purposes

Etymology

From 'petroleum' (from Latin 'petra' meaning rock and 'oleum' meaning oil) and 'geology' (from Greek 'gē' meaning earth and 'logia' meaning study).
