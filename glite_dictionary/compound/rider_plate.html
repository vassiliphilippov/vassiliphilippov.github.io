rider plate

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdər pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈraɪdə pleɪt / (BrE)
a horizontal beam or plate attached to the top of a ship's vertical keel or to the keelson, providing additional structural strength
horizontal beam above a ship's keel

Examples

The engineer inspected the rider plate for any signs of corrosion before the vessel departed.
To reinforce the hull, a rider plate was welded onto the existing keel structure.
The shipbuilder explained how the rider plate distributes the stress along the keelson.
