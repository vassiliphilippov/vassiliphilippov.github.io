Western diamondback

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈdaɪəməndˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈdaɪəməndˌbæk / (BrE)
a large and venomous rattlesnake (Crotalus atrox) native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for the distinctive diamond-shaped patterns on its back
large venomous rattlesnake of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

Hikers in Arizona are warned to watch out for the Western diamondback along desert trails.
The Western diamondback can deliver a venomous bite if threatened.
Researchers tracked several Western diamondbacks to study their habitat preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - any of several venomous American snakes with a segmented rattle at the end of the tail
