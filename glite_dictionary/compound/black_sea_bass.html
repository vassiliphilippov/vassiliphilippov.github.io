black sea bass

1. NOUN

/ blæk si bæs / (AmE), / blæk siː bæs / (BrE)
a bluish-black striped species of sea bass found along the Atlantic coast of the United States, valued as a food and game fish
a species of sea bass with dark stripes

Examples

Fishermen were excited to catch a large black sea bass near the shore.
The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh black sea bass.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of the black sea bass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bass - any of various marine fish of the family Serranidae
