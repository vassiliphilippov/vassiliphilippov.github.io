brush-footed butterfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌʃ ˌfʊtɪd ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃ ˌfʊtɪd ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
A medium to large butterfly of the family Nymphalidae, often brightly colored, with reduced forelegs that are not used for walking.
butterfly with reduced forelegs (Nymphalidae)

Examples

The brush-footed butterfly is easily recognized by its bright wings and small front legs.
Many garden visitors were delighted to spot a brush-footed butterfly among the flowers.
Children observed the unique forelegs of the brush-footed butterfly during their nature walk.

Etymology

Named for their brush-like, reduced forelegs; 'brush-footed' refers to this anatomical feature.
