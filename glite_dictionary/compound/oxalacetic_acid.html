oxalacetic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksələˈsɛtɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒksələˈsɛtɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline organic acid formed by the oxidation of maleic acid, important in the metabolism of fats and carbohydrates
acid formed in fat and carbohydrate metabolism
medical

Examples

During the citric acid cycle, oxalacetic acid plays a key role in energy production.
A deficiency in oxalacetic acid can disrupt normal cellular metabolism.
Scientists measured the concentration of oxalacetic acid in muscle tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxaloacetate - the ion or salt form of oxalacetic acid

Etymology

From 'oxal-' (from oxalic acid) + 'acetic' (referring to acetic acid), indicating its chemical structure.
