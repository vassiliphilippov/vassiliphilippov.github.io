capitate bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪˌteɪt boʊn / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪteɪt bəʊn / (BrE)
the largest of the carpal bones in the wrist, located in the center, with a rounded head that articulates with the third metacarpal bone
central wrist bone with a rounded head
medical

Examples

The capitate bone is centrally located in the wrist and is the largest of the carpal bones.
A fracture of the capitate bone can cause pain and limited motion in the hand.
During the examination, the doctor identified swelling near the capitate bone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpal bone - one of the bones of the wrist
  • Degree Lower: scaphoid bone - one of the carpal bones, located near the base of the thumb
  • Degree Higher: hamate bone - a carpal bone on the outside edge of the wrist

Etymology

From Latin 'capitatus' meaning 'having a head', from 'caput' (head).
