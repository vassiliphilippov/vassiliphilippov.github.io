Stone Age

1. NOUN

/ stoʊn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / stəʊn eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the earliest known period of human culture, marked by the use of stone tools and weapons
earliest period of human culture

Examples

The discovery of ancient cave paintings dates back to the Stone Age.
Early humans in the Stone Age made tools from flint and other stones.
Anthropologists study Stone Age artifacts to learn about prehistoric societies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bronze Age - period following the Stone Age, marked by use of bronze tools
  • Degree Higher: Iron Age - period following the Bronze Age, characterized by iron use

Etymology

Formed from 'stone' + 'age', referring to the period when stone was primarily used for tools.

2. NOUN

/ stoʊn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / stəʊn eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the earliest known period of human history, marked by the use of stone tools and a primitive way of life
prehistoric era of stone tools

Examples

Archaeologists found Stone Age artifacts in the cave in northern Europe.
The documentary explores daily life during the Stone Age.
Sometimes, new technology makes older methods feel like they belong in the Stone Age.

Synset Examples

They used tools that resembled those from the Stone Age.
His communication skills felt like they were from the Stone Age.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bronze Age - period following the Stone Age, characterized by the use of bronze tools and weapons
  • Degree Higher: Iron Age - period after the Bronze Age, marked by iron tools and weapons

Etymology

From 'stone' + 'age', referring to the era when stone was the primary material for tools and weapons.
