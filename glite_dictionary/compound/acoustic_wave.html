acoustic wave

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk weɪv / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk weɪv / (BrE)
A wave that transmits sound, typically through a medium such as air, water, or solids, by means of mechanical vibrations.
wave that transmits sound

Examples

An acoustic wave travels through air as a series of compressions and rarefactions.
Scientists measured the speed of the acoustic wave as it passed through different materials.
The device generates an acoustic wave to detect objects underwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound wave - A wave of compression and rarefaction that transmits sound in a medium

Etymology

From acoustic, relating to sound, and wave, from Old English 'wæf', meaning moving disturbance.
