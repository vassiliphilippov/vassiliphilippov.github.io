cafe curtain

1. NOUN

/ kæˈfeɪ ˈkɜːr.t̬ən / (AmE), / kæˈfeɪ ˈkɜː.tən / (BrE)
A short, typically sheer curtain hung on a rod partway up a window, often used in kitchens or cafes to allow light in while maintaining privacy.
short sheer curtain hung partway up a window

Examples

She hung a new cafe curtain in the kitchen to let in sunlight while keeping the room private.
The quaint little diner had red-checkered cafe curtains covering its windows.
After redecorating, they chose sheer white cafe curtains for the breakfast nook.

Etymology

From French 'café' (coffee house) + English 'curtain'; named for its common use in European cafes.
