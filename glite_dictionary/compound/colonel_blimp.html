Colonel Blimp

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɝːnəl ˈblɪmp / (AmE), / ˌkɜːnəl ˈblɪmp / (BrE)
an elderly, pompous, and reactionary ultranationalist; a person with outdated, ultra-conservative opinions, especially in politics, named after a British cartoon character.
pompous, ultranationalist elderly person
british

Examples

The newspaper described the politician as a modern-day Colonel Blimp, out of touch with contemporary values.
Whenever there's a debate on immigration, a few predictable Colonel Blimps inevitably voice their opinions.
He dismissed the proposal with all the bluster of a true Colonel Blimp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactionary - a person who opposes progress or liberalization

Etymology

Named after Colonel Blimp, a cartoon character created by Sir David Low in the 1930s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.nəl blɪmp / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.nəl blɪmp / (BrE)
a pompous, reactionary person; originally, a cartoon character created by Sir David Low representing conservative and out-of-touch attitudes in Britain.
pompous, reactionary person; cartoon by David Low

Examples

Many newspapers described the politician as a typical Colonel Blimp for his outdated views on society.
In debates, he often sounded like a modern-day Colonel Blimp, refusing to accept any change.
The play satirizes British tradition through the character of Colonel Blimp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactionary - a person opposing political or social progress or reform

Etymology

From a cartoon character created by Sir David Low in the 1930s.
