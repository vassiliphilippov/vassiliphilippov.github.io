sack coat

1. NOUN

/ sæk koʊt / (AmE), / sak kəʊt / (BrE)
a man's hip-length coat with a straight back, typically forming the jacket of a suit
man's hip-length suit jacket

Examples

He wore a classic sack coat with matching trousers to the formal dinner.
The sack coat became popular in the late nineteenth century for its comfort and style.
This vintage shop has several genuine wool sack coats on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacket - a short coat, usually extending to the hips
  • Antonym: tailcoat - a man's formal coat with a long tail at the back

Etymology

From 'sack' (possibly from French 'sacque') and 'coat', describing the loose, sack-like fit of the garment.
