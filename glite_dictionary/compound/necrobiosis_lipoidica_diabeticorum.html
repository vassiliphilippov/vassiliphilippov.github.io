necrobiosis lipoidica diabeticorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛk.roʊ.baɪˈoʊ.sɪs lɪˌpɔɪˈdɪk.ə ˌdaɪəˌbitɪˈkɔːr.əm / (AmE), / ˌnɛk.rəʊ.baɪˈəʊ.sɪs lɪˌpɔɪˈdɪk.ɪ ˌdaɪəˌbiː.tɪˈkɒr.əm / (BrE)
a rare skin condition characterized by thin, shiny, yellowish patches, often on the legs, commonly associated with diabetes mellitus
skin disease related to diabetes
medical

Examples

Necrobiosis lipoidica diabeticorum affects the shins of some people with long-term diabetes.
Doctors often diagnose necrobiosis lipoidica diabeticorum after noticing distinctive patches on a patient’s lower legs.
The treatment for necrobiosis lipoidica diabeticorum can be challenging and may require ongoing medical care.

Etymology

From Latin and Greek roots: 'necrobiosis' (Greek nekros, dead + bios, life), 'lipoidica' (Greek lipos, fat), 'diabeticorum' (Latin, of diabetics).
