chernozemic soil

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɜr.noʊˈzɛm.ɪk sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˌtʃɜː.nəʊˈzɛm.ɪk sɔɪl / (BrE)
A fertile, black-colored loamy soil rich in humus, found especially in temperate grasslands such as those of Russia and Ukraine.
fertile black loam found in grasslands

Examples

Extensive areas of chernozemic soil in Ukraine support high yields of wheat and barley.
The farm's productivity was attributed to the thick layer of chernozemic soil beneath the surface.
Researchers studied the unique properties of chernozemic soil to understand its agricultural value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chernozem - a fertile black soil rich in humus, typical of cool to temperate semiarid regions

Etymology

From Russian 'chernozem' (чернозём), meaning 'black earth'.
