Magellanic Cloud

1. NOUN

/ məˌdʒɛˈlænɪk klaʊd / (AmE), / məˌdʒɛˈlanɪk klaʊd / (BrE)
either of two irregular dwarf galaxies visible from the Southern Hemisphere, orbiting the Milky Way: the Large Magellanic Cloud and the Small Magellanic Cloud.
two dwarf galaxies near the Milky Way

Examples

The Magellanic Cloud is easily visible to the naked eye from the Southern Hemisphere on clear nights.
Astronomers study the Magellanic Cloud to learn about the formation of dwarf galaxies.
During his voyage, Magellan recorded the Magellanic Cloud, which now bears his name.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Large Magellanic Cloud - the larger of the two Magellanic Clouds, irregular dwarf galaxy
  • Degree Lower: Small Magellanic Cloud - the smaller of the two Magellanic Clouds, irregular dwarf galaxy

Etymology

Named after the explorer Ferdinand Magellan, whose expedition recorded them on his circumnavigation (1519–1522).
