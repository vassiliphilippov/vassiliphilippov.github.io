act of Congress

1. NOUN

/ ækt əv ˈkɑŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ækt əv ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
a law formally passed by the United States Congress
law passed by the U.S. Congress

Examples

It usually takes an act of Congress to change federal regulations.
The Civil Rights Act is a famous act of Congress that shaped U.S. history.
Receiving a commemorative medal often requires an act of Congress.

Synset Examples

Passing the new law required an act of Congress.
That policy change was an unexpected act of Congress.
The funding approval was finally an act of Congress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
