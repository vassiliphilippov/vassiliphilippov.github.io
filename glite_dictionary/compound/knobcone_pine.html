knobcone pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑbˌkoʊn paɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɒbkəʊn paɪn / (BrE)
a medium-sized pine tree native to the Pacific coast of the United States, distinguished by cones with a prominent knob on each scale
a pine tree of western North America with knobby cones

Examples

The knobcone pine thrives in areas frequently disturbed by fires.
Botanists noted the unique cones of the knobcone pine during their survey.
The forest restoration plan includes planting several knobcone pine seedlings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - a coniferous tree of the genus Pinus
