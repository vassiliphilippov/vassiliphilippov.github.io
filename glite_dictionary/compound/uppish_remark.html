uppish remark

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɪʃ rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈʌpɪʃ rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
An arrogant or presumptuous comment; a remark that displays a sense of superiority or self-importance.
arrogant or presumptuous comment

Examples

He couldn't help making an uppish remark during the meeting, which irritated his colleagues.
Whenever she feels threatened, she resorts to an uppish remark to assert herself.
The teacher warned that any uppish remark would not be tolerated in class.

Synset Examples

His uppish attitude annoyed everyone at the party.
She found his uppish remarks quite off-putting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snide remark - a derogatory or mocking comment
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From 'uppish', meaning showing arrogance or self-importance, + 'remark'.
