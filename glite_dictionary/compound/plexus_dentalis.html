plexus dentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs dɛnˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs dɛnˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
a network of nerves supplying the teeth and their surrounding structures
network of nerves for the teeth
medical

Examples

Damage to the plexus dentalis can result in loss of sensation in the teeth and gums.
The dentist explained that the intense pain might be due to inflammation of the plexus dentalis.
Local anesthesia is administered near the plexus dentalis to numb the entire dental arch during surgery.

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network) and 'dentalis' (of the teeth).
