court of chancery

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt əv ˈtʃænsəri / (AmE), / kɔːt əv ˈtʃɑːnsəri / (BrE)
a court with jurisdiction over cases of equity rather than law, especially formerly in England and in some U.S. states
historical equity court
legal british

Examples

The court of chancery was often called upon to resolve disputes that common law courts could not address.
She filed her petition in the court of chancery seeking an injunction against her former business partner.
Historically, the court of chancery handled cases involving trusts, estates, and guardianships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equity court - a court that decides cases on the principles of equity

Etymology

From 'chancery', ultimately from Latin cancellaria (chancellorship, office of a chancellor).
