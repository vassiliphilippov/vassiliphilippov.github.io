basic price

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.sɪk ˌpraɪs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.sɪk ˌpraɪs / (BrE)
The price of a good or service before adding any sales tax or value-added tax (VAT), and including any subsidies received by the seller.
price before tax, includes subsidies

Examples

The basic price of the product does not include any sales tax or VAT.
Farmers received a higher basic price this year due to government subsidies.
Before calculating the final bill, the accountant first notes the basic price of each service.

Synset Examples

Even though the basic price was established a decade ago, some of our business partners want to raise it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market price - price at which goods are bought and sold in a market
  • Degree Higher: list price - the price of an item before any discounts
