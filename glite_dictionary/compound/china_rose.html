China rose

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnə roʊz / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə rəʊz / (BrE)
a species of rose native to China, known for its role as an ancestor of many cultivated garden roses
species of rose native to China

Examples

The China rose is prized for its continuous blooming throughout the year.
Many modern garden roses have genetic links to the China rose.
She planted a vibrant China rose in the center of her flower bed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnə roʊz / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə rəʊz / (BrE)
a large, showy shrub or small tree native to Asia, with large single or double red or deep-red flowers, especially Hibiscus rosa-sinensis
a shrub with large red flowers, Hibiscus rosa-sinensis

Examples

The China rose bloomed every morning in their garden, adding brilliant color to the pathway.
She planted a China rose in a large pot near the kitchen window.
Botanists admire the hardy nature and beauty of the China rose in tropical climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibiscus - a genus of flowering plants with large, showy blossoms

Etymology

Named for its origin in China and its rose-like flowers.
