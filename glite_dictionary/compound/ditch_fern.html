ditch fern

1. NOUN

/ dɪtʃ fɝn / (AmE), / dɪtʃ fɜːn / (BrE)
a large, deeply rooted fern found worldwide, with upright, bipinnate, compound, tufted fronds
a large, tufted fern species

Examples

The ditch fern thrives in damp, open areas near streams and ditches.
She recognized the distinctive fronds of the ditch fern along the woodland path.
Gardeners often plant ditch ferns to add a lush, green backdrop to shaded gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracken - a type of large, coarse fern of worldwide distribution
