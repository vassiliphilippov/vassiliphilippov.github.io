homeotic gene

1. NOUN

/ hoʊmiˈɑːtɪk dʒin / (AmE), / həʊmiˈɒtɪk dʒiːn / (BrE)
any of a group of genes that control the early development of body structures and organization in an embryo.
gene controlling embryonic development
medical

Examples

Mutations in a homeotic gene can result in misplaced body parts during development.
Scientists study the function of each homeotic gene to understand genetic regulation in fruit flies.
The discovery of the homeotic gene revolutionized the field of developmental biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hox gene - a specific type of homeotic gene important in body plan development

Etymology

From 'homeotic', relating to homeosis, and 'gene'; 'homeosis' from Greek 'homoios' (similar).
