two-furrow plough

1. NOUN

/ tuː ˈfɝːroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / tuː ˈfʌrəʊ plaʊ / (BrE)
a plough equipped with two moldboards, allowing it to cut two furrows simultaneously
plough with two moldboards

Examples

The farmer used a two-furrow plough to quickly prepare the large field for planting.
With a two-furrow plough, a team of horses could double the efficiency of tillage compared to a single-furrow model.
The introduction of the two-furrow plough revolutionized crop production on heavy soils.

Synset Examples

Heavy volcanic loam soils, such as are found in New Zealand, require the use of four heavy draught horses to pull a double-furrow plough.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single-furrow plough - a plough with one moldboard
  • Degree Higher: multi-furrow plough - a plough with more than two moldboards
