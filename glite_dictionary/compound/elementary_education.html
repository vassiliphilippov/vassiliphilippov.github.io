elementary education

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈmɛn.tə.ri ˌɛdʒ.əˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛl.ɪˈmen.tər.i ˌed.jʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The first stage of formal education, teaching basic subjects such as reading, writing, and arithmetic to young children, typically in primary or grade school.
basic education for young children

Examples

All children have the right to receive elementary education free of charge.
The country reformed its elementary education system to improve literacy rates.
She decided to pursue a career in elementary education after graduating from college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primary education - The first stage of formal education for children, especially in the UK and other countries
