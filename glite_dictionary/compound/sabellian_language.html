Sabellian language

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɛliən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / səˈbɛliən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Osco-Umbrian language of ancient Italy, known primarily from a small number of inscriptions
extinct ancient Italian language

Examples

Scholars have studied the Sabellian language using fragments found on ancient stone tablets.
The Sabellian language provides valuable insight into the linguistic history of pre-Roman Italy.
Although few inscriptions remain, the Sabellian language has been partially reconstructed by linguists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oscan - an extinct Osco-Umbrian language related to Sabellian

Etymology

From Latin 'Sabellus', meaning 'of the Sabellians,' an ancient Italian people.
