durative aspect

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊrətɪv ˈæspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərətɪv ˈæspekt / (BrE)
a grammatical aspect of verbs that expresses the ongoing or continuous duration of an action or state
grammatical aspect expressing duration

Examples

Some languages use verb endings to indicate the durative aspect of an action.
The durative aspect emphasizes actions that are not seen as completed or instantaneous.
Scholars have debated whether English truly has a durative aspect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progressive aspect - aspect that expresses ongoing actions
  • Antonym: perfective aspect - aspect that expresses a completed action

Etymology

From Latin 'durare' (to last), and 'aspectus' (appearance, look).
