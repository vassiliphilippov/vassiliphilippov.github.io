home front

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm frʌnt / (AmE), / həʊm frʌnt / (BrE)
the people who stay in a country and work to support its military during a war, as well as their activities and conditions
civilian activities and life during wartime

Examples

During World War II, the home front was vital to supporting troops overseas.
Her grandmother worked in a munitions factory on the home front while her grandfather served as a soldier.
Government propaganda encouraged people on the home front to contribute to the war effort by saving resources.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: battlefront - the region or area where fighting occurs during war
