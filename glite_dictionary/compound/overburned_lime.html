overburned lime

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˌbɜrnd ˈlaɪm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌbɜːnd ˈlaɪm / (BrE)
Lime that has been heated to such a high temperature that it loses its reactivity, resulting in poor or no bond strength.
lime heated beyond optimal, loses reactivity

Examples

The use of overburned lime can result in weak mortar that crumbles easily.
If overburned lime is present in a mixture, it can compromise the durability of concrete.
Heating limestone for too long may produce overburned lime that is unsuitable for construction.

Synset Examples

Overburned lime will not hold up to to freeze-thaw weather cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quicklime - calcium oxide produced by heating limestone
  • Antonym: hydrated lime - lime that has been combined with water
