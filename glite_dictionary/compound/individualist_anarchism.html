individualist anarchism

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.ə.lɪst ˈæn.ɚˌkɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.ə.lɪst ˈæn.əˌkɪz.əm / (BrE)
a tradition within anarchism that emphasizes the importance of the individual and their autonomy over external authorities such as society, traditions, or ideological systems
anarchist ideology focused on individual autonomy

Examples

Many philosophers who support individualist anarchism argue that personal freedom is the highest social ideal.
The principles of individualist anarchism often clash with those of collectivist movements.
She wrote a compelling essay on the history and influence of individualist anarchism in Europe.

Synset Examples

Traditionally, individualist anarchism has considered itself part of the fold of left-anarchism (though not social anarchism), a broader movement that opposes both capitalism and the state, which it sees as dual forces of oppression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchism - political philosophy advocating self-governed societies
