metaphosphoric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəfəˈsfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəfəˈsfɒrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a glassy, solid acid often used as a dehydrating agent, with the chemical formula (HPO₃)n
a glassy, solid acid (HPO₃)n
medical

Examples

For some laboratory procedures, metaphosphoric acid is added as a strong dehydrating agent.
The precipitation of proteins can be achieved by using metaphosphoric acid in the sample preparation.
Unlike orthophosphoric acid, metaphosphoric acid is commonly found in a glassy solid state.

Etymology

From meta- (meaning 'beyond' or 'change') and 'phosphoric acid', indicating a modified form of phosphoric acid.
