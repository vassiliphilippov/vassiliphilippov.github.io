to navel-gaze

1. VERB

/ ˈneɪvəl ˌɡeɪz / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˌɡeɪz / (BrE)
to spend too much time thinking about oneself or one's own problems without taking action or considering others
excessively introspect or self-reflect
informal

Examples

Sometimes it's easier to navel-gaze than to confront real problems head-on.
The team was accused of navel-gazing instead of delivering results for their clients.
If you always navel-gaze, you'll miss opportunities for growth and change.

Synset Examples

She tends to navel-gaze instead of taking action.
Navel-gazing won’t solve our problems.
Stop navel-gazing and focus on the task at hand.
His navel-gazey attitude annoyed everyone in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to introspect - to examine one's own thoughts or feelings
  • Synonym: self-reflect - to think about one's own character or actions
  • Antonym: act - to do something rather than just think

Etymology

From the phrase 'navel-gazing,' originally meaning to gaze at one's navel as a symbol of self-absorption.
