destruction fire

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrʌkʃən faɪɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌkʃən faɪə / (BrE)
fire directed solely at destroying material objects, typically in a military context.
fire aimed at destroying objects

Examples

The artillery unit was ordered to lay down destruction fire on the enemy's ammunition depot.
Using destruction fire, the engineers eliminated obstacles that blocked the advance.
Command authorized destruction fire only after all civilians had evacuated the area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suppressive fire - fire intended to hinder or prevent enemy action rather than destroy
