musical octave

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪkəl ˈɑːktɪv / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl ˈɒktɪv / (BrE)
A musical interval spanning eight notes, in which the higher note has twice the frequency of the lower note.
interval of eight notes in music

Examples

Her vocal range covers more than one musical octave effortlessly.
Many Western scales are organized so that notes repeat at each musical octave.
A musical octave is fundamental to the structure of modern music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octave - an interval of eight notes in music

Etymology

Octave from Latin 'octava' (eighth).
