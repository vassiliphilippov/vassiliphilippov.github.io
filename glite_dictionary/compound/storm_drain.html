storm drain

1. NOUN

/ stɔrm dreɪn / (AmE), / stɔːm dreɪn / (BrE)
an underground pipe or channel system designed to carry away excess rainwater or surface water from streets and other paved areas
drainage system for excess rainwater

Examples

Heavy rain caused the storm drain to overflow, flooding the nearby street.
Leaves can block a storm drain, preventing water from flowing through it properly.
City workers inspected the storm drains before the predicted thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewer - an underground conduit for carrying off waste water and sewage
