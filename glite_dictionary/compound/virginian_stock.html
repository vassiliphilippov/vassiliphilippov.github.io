Virginian stock

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniən stɑk / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniən stɒk / (BrE)
an annual flowering plant (Malcolmia maritima) cultivated for its fragrant loose clusters of white, pink, red, or lilac flowers, native to the Mediterranean region
fragrant annual garden plant

Examples

She planted Virginian stock along the edges of the flower bed for a splash of color.
The Virginian stock filled the garden with its sweet scent all summer long.
Gardeners value Virginian stock for its quick growth and vibrant blossoms.

Etymology

Probably so named for an assumed or mistaken connection to Virginia or the New World, with 'stock' meaning a flowering plant.
