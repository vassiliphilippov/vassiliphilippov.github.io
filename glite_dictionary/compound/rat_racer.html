rat racer

1. NOUN

/ ˈræt ˌreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈræt ˌreɪsə / (BrE)
a person who competes aggressively for success, often using unethical methods
person who competes aggressively for success

Examples

In his quest for promotion, Tom became a true rat racer, stepping on others to get ahead.
Many rat racers are willing to sacrifice their integrity just for a bigger bonus.
She refused to become a rat racer, choosing work-life balance over ruthless ambition.

Synset Examples

He's such a rat racer, always cutting corners to win.
The rat racer in the group never plays fair.
Everyone knows he's a rat racer, always looking for shortcuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat race - relentless competitive struggle for success

Etymology

From "rat race," a metaphor for relentless competitive struggle, and "racer."
