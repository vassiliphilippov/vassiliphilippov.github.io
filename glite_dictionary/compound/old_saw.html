old saw

1. NOUN

/ oʊld sɔ / (AmE), / əʊld sɔː / (BrE)
a traditional saying or proverb that has been repeated often and expresses a common truth or piece of advice
well-known saying or proverb

Examples

He dismissed her advice as just an old saw that didn't really apply anymore.
The teacher reminded us of the old saw about patience being a virtue.
After the meeting, he quoted the old saw that success comes to those who wait.

Synset Examples

You know the old saw, 'A penny saved is a penny earned.'
She always references the old saw about honesty being the best policy.
It's an old saw, but practice makes perfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proverb - a short, well-known saying stating a general truth or piece of advice

Etymology

From 'saw' meaning 'saying' or 'maxim', a usage dating back to Old English *sagu* (speech, saying).
