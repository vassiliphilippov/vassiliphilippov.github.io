auction bridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔkʃən brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɔːkʃən brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a form of bridge in which excess tricks over the contract count toward the game score, now largely replaced by contract bridge
an early variant of the card game bridge

Examples

The club president reminisced about playing auction bridge before contract bridge became popular.
At the turn of the century, auction bridge was the preferred pastime among card enthusiasts.
She learned the rules of auction bridge from her grandmother, who played regularly decades ago.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: contract bridge - modern standard form of the card game bridge
  • Synonym: bridge - card game using a standard deck and played by four players in two partnerships

Etymology

From 'auction', referring to the bidding system, and 'bridge', the card game.
