osseous tissue

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑsiəs ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪəs ˈtɪʃuː / (BrE)
the hard, calcified connective tissue that forms the structural elements of bones in vertebrates
calcified tissue forming bones
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that osseous tissue regeneration would be essential for the patient's recovery.
Osteoporosis is a disease that weakens the osseous tissue in the bones, making them fragile.
Scientists study osseous tissue to understand bone growth and repair in mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone - the rigid organ that forms part of the skeleton

Etymology

From Latin 'osseus' (bony), from 'os, ossis' (bone), plus 'tissue'.
