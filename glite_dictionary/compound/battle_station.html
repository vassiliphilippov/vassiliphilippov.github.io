battle station

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾl ˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbætəl ˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a designated position or location for military personnel to occupy during combat or when preparing for military action, especially on a ship or in a fortified location
military position assigned for combat

Examples

All sailors must report to their battle station immediately when the alarm sounds.
The crew trained regularly so that everyone knew exactly what to do at their battle station.
During the surprise attack, he rushed straight to his assigned battle station.

Synset Examples

Each soldier knew their role at the battle station.
He reported to his battle station at dawn.
The crew took their positions at the battle station.
