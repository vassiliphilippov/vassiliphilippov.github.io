Old World mistletoe

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld ˈmɪslˌtoʊ / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈmɪslˌtəʊ / (BrE)
A European parasitic shrub with greenish branching stems, leathery leaves, and clusters of waxy white berries, traditionally associated with Christmas.
European shrub with white berries, Christmas tradition

Examples

People often hang Old World mistletoe in their homes as part of Christmas celebrations.
The berries of Old World mistletoe are toxic if ingested.
In some cultures, Old World mistletoe symbolizes peace and goodwill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - a parasitic shrub with white berries from several species
