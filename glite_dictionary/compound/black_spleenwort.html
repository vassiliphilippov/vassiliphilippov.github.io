black spleenwort

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsplinˌwɜrt / (AmE), / blæk ˈspliːnwɜːt / (BrE)
A spleenwort fern native to Europe, Africa, and Asia, with pinnate fronds and known for its astringent properties.
a spleenwort fern of Europe, Africa, and Asia

Examples

The black spleenwort is often found growing on rocky hillsides throughout southern Europe.
Some herbalists use dried black spleenwort for its mild astringent properties.
During our hike, we identified several rare plants, including the distinctive black spleenwort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asplenium adiantum-nigrum - the scientific name for black spleenwort
  • Degree Lower: spleenwort - any fern of the genus Asplenium
