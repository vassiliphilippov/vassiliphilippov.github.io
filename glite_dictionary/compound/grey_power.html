grey power

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the political and social influence exerted by older adults, particularly as a voting bloc or advocacy group
political influence of older adults

Examples

Many politicians now seek the support of grey power during election campaigns.
Grey power has become increasingly important as the population ages.
The rise of grey power is changing public policy on healthcare and pensions.

Synset Examples

The movement for senior rights is often called grey power.
Grey power advocates for the elderly in society.
She believes in the influence of grey power on politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senior power - the influence wielded by senior citizens, especially in politics

Etymology

Formed from "grey" (referring to grey hair associated with age) + "power".
