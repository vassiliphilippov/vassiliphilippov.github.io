genus Phrynosoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌfrɪ.nəˈsoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌfrɪ.nəʊˈsəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A genus of lizards known as horned lizards, native to North and Central America and distinguished by spiny scales on their bodies and heads.
genus of horned lizards

Examples

The genus Phrynosoma comprises several species of horned lizards found throughout arid regions.
Biologists have long studied the defensive behaviors unique to the genus Phrynosoma.
Members of the genus Phrynosoma are often called 'horned toads' due to their toad-like shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned lizard - A lizard with horn-like spines; common name for members of genus Phrynosoma

Etymology

Phrynosoma from Greek 'phrynos' (toad) and 'soma' (body), referring to the lizard's toad-like appearance.
