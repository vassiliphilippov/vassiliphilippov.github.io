sundew family

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdu ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.djuː ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family of carnivorous plants, including sundews and their relatives
family of carnivorous plants

Examples

The sundew family comprises several species known for their sticky, insect-trapping leaves.
Many members of the sundew family thrive in nutrient-poor, boggy soils.
Botanists study the unique adaptations of the sundew family to understand carnivorous plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Droseraceae - the scientific name for the sundew family
