compound protein

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.paʊnd ˈproʊ.tin / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.paʊnd ˈprəʊ.tiːn / (BrE)
a protein composed of amino acids combined with other chemical substances, such as carbohydrates or metals
protein combined with other substances

Examples

Hemoglobin is a classic example of a compound protein containing iron.
Many enzymes are compound proteins that require additional non-protein groups to function.
Laboratory analysis showed that the mysterious sample was a compound protein rather than a simple one.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simple protein - a protein made only of amino acids and no other chemical group

Etymology

From 'compound', meaning something made by combining two or more elements, and 'protein', from Greek 'proteios' (of first importance).
