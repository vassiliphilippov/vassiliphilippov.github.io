pearl-clutcher

1. NOUN

/ pɝl ˈklʌtʃɚ / (AmE), / pɜːl ˈklʌtʃə / (BrE)
A person who is excessively prim, easily shocked, or prone to exaggerated displays of moral outrage or propriety.
someone overly prim or easily shocked
informal

Examples

Stop being such a pearl-clutcher and try to see things from a different perspective.
The debate was interrupted by a pearl-clutcher who objected to the frank discussion.
Online forums often attract at least one pearl-clutcher whenever controversial topics come up.

Synset Examples

Stop being such a pearl-clutcher and relax.
His pearl-clutcher attitude annoys everyone at work.
She’s a pearl-clutcher when it comes to politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity
  • Antonym: progressive - a person open to new ideas and less easily shocked; the opposite social attitude

Etymology

From the expression 'to clutch one's pearls,' representing an exaggerated gesture of shock or disapproval.
