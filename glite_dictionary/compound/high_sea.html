high sea

1. NOUN

/ haɪ si / (AmE), / haɪ siː / (BrE)
the part of the sea that is not under the jurisdiction of any country; the open sea beyond territorial waters
open sea beyond national jurisdiction

Examples

Pirates used to roam the high sea far from the reach of any nation’s law.
International agreements often govern activities on the high sea to protect the environment.
A ship may fish freely on the high sea, but not within another country's territorial waters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: territorial waters - waters under the jurisdiction of a state
