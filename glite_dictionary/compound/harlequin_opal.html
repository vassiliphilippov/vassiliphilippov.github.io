harlequin opal

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrlɪkwɪn ˈoʊpəl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːlɪkwɪn ˈəʊpəl / (BrE)
a type of opal gemstone characterized by a reddish base and small, brilliant patches of color resembling a harlequin's pattern
opal with reddish base and brilliant color patches

Examples

The jeweler displayed a rare harlequin opal that gleamed with every color of the rainbow.
She inherited a necklace set with a beautiful harlequin opal from her grandmother.
Collectors value the harlequin opal for its unique patchwork of vivid hues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opal - a gemstone consisting of hydrated silica with a play of color

Etymology

Derived from 'harlequin' (a comic character in Italian commedia dell'arte known for patchwork costume) and 'opal' (gemstone).
