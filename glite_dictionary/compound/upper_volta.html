Upper Volta

1. PROPN

/ ˈʌpɚ ˈvoʊltə / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˈvəʊltə / (BrE)
the former name of Burkina Faso, a landlocked country in western Africa, used before 1984
former name of Burkina Faso (West Africa)

Examples

In 1960, Upper Volta gained its independence from France.
The country now known as Burkina Faso was called Upper Volta until 1984.
Many historical documents refer to Upper Volta during the colonial period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Burkina Faso - current name of the same West African country

Etymology

Named after its location above the Volta River; 'Volta' from the Portuguese for 'turn', referencing the shape of the river.
