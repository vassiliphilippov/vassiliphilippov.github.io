clue stick

1. NOUN

/ klu stɪk / (AmE), / kluː stɪk / (BrE)
(slang) A humorous imaginary object used to describe giving someone a strong hint or a piece of obvious advice to help them understand something they have missed.
imaginary object for giving strong hints
informal slang

Examples

The manager gave him a clue stick when he still didn't get the point.
Sometimes it feels like you need a clue stick to understand the new software.
She wished someone would just hit her with the clue stick about the surprise party.

Synset Examples

I needed a clue stick to understand his hints.
