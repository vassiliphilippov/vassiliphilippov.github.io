Second Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ðə kəˈrɪnθiənz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔːl ði əˈpɒsl tuː ðə kəˈrɪnθiənz / (BrE)
the second letter in the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint Paul, addressed to the church at Corinth
book of the New Testament (Paul's second letter to the Corinthians)

Examples

Many theologians study the Second Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians for its teachings on reconciliation.
He quoted a passage from the Second Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians during his sermon.
The Second Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians is often abbreviated as '2 Corinthians' in modern translations.
