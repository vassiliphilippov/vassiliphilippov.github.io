shortfin mako

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌfɪn ˈmeɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtfɪn ˈmeɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a swift, active species of bluish shark (Isurus oxyrinchus) found in warm waters worldwide, known as an important game fish
fast bluish shark species found worldwide

Examples

The shortfin mako is considered one of the fastest sharks in the ocean.
Fishermen prize the shortfin mako for its strength and speed.
Researchers tagged several shortfin makos to study their long-distance migrations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: longfin mako - a related shark species with longer pectoral fins
  • Synonym: great white shark - a large predatory shark species

Etymology

From 'shortfin' (referring to its fin size) and 'mako', from Māori 'makō' (shark).
