Battle of Fontenoy

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˌfɒntəˈnwɑɪ / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌfɒntəˈnwɑː / (BrE)
a battle fought in 1745 near Tournai, in which the French army under Marshal Saxe defeated the English and their allies under the Duke of Cumberland during the War of the Austrian Succession
battle in 1745; French victory over English and allies

Examples

The Battle of Fontenoy marked a turning point in the War of the Austrian Succession.
Many historians consider the tactics used at the Battle of Fontenoy innovative for their time.
The Battle of Fontenoy is often remembered for the French army's decisive victory over their enemies.
