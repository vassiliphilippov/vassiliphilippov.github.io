magnolia family

1. NOUN

/ mægˈnoʊliə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊliə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants including the genera Magnolia, Liriodendron, and Manglietia.
family of flowering plants (Magnoliaceae)

Examples

Many species in the magnolia family are prized for their large, fragrant blossoms.
The tulip tree belongs to the magnolia family and is native to North America.
Researchers are studying the evolution of the magnolia family using genetic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Magnoliaceae - The botanical family of magnolias and related plants

Etymology

From the genus name 'Magnolia', named after French botanist Pierre Magnol, combined with 'family' in the botanical sense.
