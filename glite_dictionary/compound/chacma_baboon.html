chacma baboon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃækmə bəˈbun / (AmE), / ˈtʃakmə bəˈbuːn / (BrE)
a large, greyish baboon native to southern and eastern Africa, known for its dog-like muzzle and social behavior
large African baboon species

Examples

The chacma baboon is often seen around campsites in South Africa's national parks.
Researchers observed a troop of chacma baboons crossing the savannah at dawn.
Unlike other primates, the chacma baboon has a remarkably long canine tooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baboon - a large monkey with a dog-like face and large canines

Etymology

From Khoikhoi *chacma*, likely describing the animal.
