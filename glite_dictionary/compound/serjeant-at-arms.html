serjeant-at-arms

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrdʒənt æt ˈɑrmz / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdʒənt æt ˈɑːmz / (BrE)
an officer of a legislative body or court responsible for maintaining order and carrying out official commands
officer maintaining order in legislature or court

Examples

The serjeant-at-arms escorted the disruptive protester from the chamber.
It is the duty of the serjeant-at-arms to uphold the authority of the Speaker.
The parliament recognized the serjeant-at-arms for his years of dedicated service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sergeant-at-arms - officer maintaining order in a legislative or deliberative body; modern spelling variant

Etymology

From Middle English 'sergeant' (servant, officer) + 'at arms', indicating a role with ceremonial weapons; historic spelling 'serjeant' often retained for this office.
