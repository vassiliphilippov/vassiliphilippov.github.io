Yangtze Kiang

1. PROPN

/ ˈjæŋtsi kiˈɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈjaŋtsi kiˈaŋ / (BrE)
the longest river in Asia, flowing eastward from Tibet to the East China Sea near Shanghai, China
the longest river in Asia

Examples

The Yangtze Kiang has played a vital role in China's history and economy.
Many cities along the Yangtze Kiang rely on its waters for transportation and irrigation.
The Three Gorges Dam was constructed across the Yangtze Kiang to control flooding and generate electricity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yangtze River - the main river in China, synonymous with Yangtze Kiang

Etymology

From Chinese 'Yángzǐ Jiāng', literally 'Yangzi River'.
