Hitchiti language

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈtʃɪti ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɪˈtʃɪti ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Muskogean language formerly spoken by the Hitchiti people, chiefly in what is now Georgia, USA
a Muskogean Native American language

Examples

The Hitchiti language was once spoken throughout parts of Georgia and Florida.
Linguists have studied the Hitchiti language to learn more about Muskogean grammar.
Few people today can speak or understand the Hitchiti language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogean - a family of Native American languages spoken in the southeastern United States

Etymology

From Hitchiti, the self-designation of the Hitchiti people.
