threadbare excuse

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛdˌbɛr ɪkˈskjus / (AmE), / ˈθrɛdˌbeə ɪksˈkjuːs / (BrE)
an unconvincing or feeble justification, often used repeatedly and no longer believable
unconvincing or weak excuse

Examples

He gave a threadbare excuse for being late, which the teacher did not accept.
After years of threadbare excuses, his boss finally lost patience with him.
The mayor's threadbare excuse failed to satisfy the public's demand for answers.

Synset Examples

Her threadbare excuses convinced no one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretext - a false reason given to hide the real one
  • Antonym: plausible excuse - an excuse that seems reasonable or likely

Etymology

Figurative use of 'threadbare' (worn thin) to describe overused or ineffective excuses.
