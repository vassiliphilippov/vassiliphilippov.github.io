thrust fault

1. NOUN

/ θrʌst fɔlt / (AmE), / θrʌst fɔːlt / (BrE)
a geological fault where rocks on the upper side have moved upward over the lower side due to compressive forces
geological fault formed by compression

Examples

The Himalayas were formed in part by the movement along a thrust fault.
Geologists identified a major thrust fault running through the mountain range.
During the earthquake, a thrust fault caused the land to buckle and rise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal fault - a fault where the hanging wall moves downward relative to the footwall
  • Synonym: reverse fault - a fault in which the upper block moves up relative to the lower block, similar to a thrust fault but usually at a steeper angle
