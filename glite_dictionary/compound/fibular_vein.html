fibular vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪbjələr veɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪbjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
a vein accompanying the peroneal arteries, running up the back of the leg from the heel to join the posterior tibial veins
vein in the lower leg near the fibula
medical

Examples

Deep injury to the leg may damage the fibular vein and cause significant bleeding.
During surgery, the fibular vein is carefully preserved to maintain proper circulation.
Ultrasound can help identify a clot in the fibular vein of the lower leg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peroneal vein - another name for the fibular vein
  • Degree Lower: anterior tibial vein - vein in the lower leg running alongside the anterior tibial artery
  • Degree Higher: posterior tibial vein - vein in the lower leg into which the fibular vein drains

Etymology

From Latin 'fibula' (the outer bone of the lower leg) + vein.
