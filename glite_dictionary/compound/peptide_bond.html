peptide bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛp.taɪd bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.tʌɪd bɒnd / (BrE)
a covalent chemical bond formed between the carboxyl group of one amino acid and the amino group of another, linking them in a peptide or protein chain
linkage between amino acids in proteins

Examples

During protein synthesis, a peptide bond forms between adjacent amino acids.
Enzymes can catalyze the breaking of a peptide bond in a protein molecule.
The stability of the peptide bond is crucial for the overall structure of proteins.

Etymology

From 'peptide' (from Greek 'peptos', digested) + 'bond' (joining).
