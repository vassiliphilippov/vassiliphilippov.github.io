Harper's Ferry

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑrpərz ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpəz ˈfɛri / (BrE)
A small town in northeastern West Virginia, famous as the site of abolitionist John Brown's raid on the federal arsenal in 1859.
historic town in West Virginia, USA
american

Examples

Many tourists visit Harper's Ferry to learn about its role in American history.
The raid on Harper's Ferry is often taught in high school history classes.
Scenic hiking trails surround Harper's Ferry, making it popular with outdoor enthusiasts.

Etymology

Named after Robert Harper, who established a ferry service there in the mid-18th century.
