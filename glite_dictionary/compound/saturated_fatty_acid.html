saturated fatty acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætʃəˌreɪtəd ˈfæti ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈsatʃərɛɪtɪd ˈfæti ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a fatty acid that has no double bonds between the carbon atoms in its hydrocarbon chain, and thus cannot absorb any more hydrogen atoms; chiefly found in animal fats
type of fatty acid with no double bonds
medical

Examples

Diets high in saturated fatty acid have been linked to increased cholesterol levels.
Butter contains a significant amount of saturated fatty acid compared to vegetable oils.
Reading food labels can help you monitor your saturated fatty acid intake for better heart health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsaturated fatty acid - a fatty acid having one or more double bonds
