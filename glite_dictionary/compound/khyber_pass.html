Khyber Pass

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaɪbɚ pæs / (AmE), / ˈkaɪbə pɑːs / (BrE)
a mountain pass of great strategic and commercial importance in the Hindu Kush, on the border between Pakistan and Afghanistan, long used as a route for trade and invasions
strategic mountain pass between Pakistan and Afghanistan

Examples

The Khyber Pass has been a vital trade route connecting Central Asia and South Asia for centuries.
Many armies have crossed the Khyber Pass during their invasions of the Indian subcontinent.
Tourists marvel at the rugged beauty of the Khyber Pass and its historical significance.

Etymology

From Pashto 'Khyber', traditional name of the region, and English 'pass'.
