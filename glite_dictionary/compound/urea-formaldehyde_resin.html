urea-formaldehyde resin

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌriə fɔrˈmældəˌhaɪd ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / jʊˌriːə fɔːˈmældɪhaɪd ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a hard, clear, thermosetting synthetic resin made from urea and formaldehyde, used in electrical fittings, adhesives, and surface finishes
synthetic resin used in adhesives and fittings

Examples

The switch covers in the old house were made of urea-formaldehyde resin.
Many wood adhesives contain urea-formaldehyde resin for its strong bonding properties.
Furniture manufacturers often use urea-formaldehyde resin as a finish for its durability and gloss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melamine-formaldehyde resin - a similar thermosetting resin made from melamine and formaldehyde

Etymology

From urea and formaldehyde, the two main chemical components, referring to the process of their polymerization.
