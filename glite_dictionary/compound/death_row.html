death row

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ roʊ / (AmE), / dɛθ rəʊ / (BrE)
the part of a prison where prisoners who have been sentenced to death are kept while awaiting execution
section of prison for inmates awaiting execution

Examples

After twenty years on death row, the prisoner was finally exonerated by new evidence.
Journalists were granted rare access to interview inmates living on death row.
Many debates about the justice system focus on the ethics of death row.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the carrying out of a death sentence
