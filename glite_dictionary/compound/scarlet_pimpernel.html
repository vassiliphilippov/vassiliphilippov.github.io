scarlet pimpernel

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑr.lət ˈpɪm.pərˌnɛl / (AmE), / ˈskɑː.lɪt ˈpɪm.pə.nɛl / (BrE)
a small annual herbaceous plant with scarlet, white, or purple flowers that typically close at the approach of rainy weather, native to Europe and widely naturalized elsewhere
herb with small scarlet, white, or purple flowers

Examples

The scarlet pimpernel is often found growing in open fields and gardens across Europe.
She identified the flower as a scarlet pimpernel because of its vivid red petals.
After the rain, the scarlet pimpernel blossoms remained tightly closed.

Etymology

Scarlet from Middle English; pimpernel from Old French *pimprenelle*, from late Latin *piperinella*.
