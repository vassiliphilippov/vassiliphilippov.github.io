family Vireonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪrioʊˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌvɪəriəʊˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small American insectivorous songbirds, including the vireos.
family of American songbirds (vireos)

Examples

The family Vireonidae is known for its small, greenish songbirds known as vireos.
Many species within the family Vireonidae inhabit North and South America.
Researchers recently discovered a new member of the family Vireonidae in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Vireonidae, based on the type genus Vireo.
