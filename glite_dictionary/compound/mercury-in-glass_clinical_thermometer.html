mercury-in-glass clinical thermometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɜːrkjəri ɪn ɡlæs ˈklɪnɪkəl θərˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌmɜːkjʊəri ɪn ɡlɑːs ˈklɪnɪkəl θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a thermometer containing mercury in a glass tube, designed to measure human body temperature, usually covering a range around normal body temperature
clinical thermometer using mercury in glass
medical

Examples

The nurse used a mercury-in-glass clinical thermometer to check the patient's fever.
Many hospitals have replaced the mercury-in-glass clinical thermometer with digital alternatives for safety reasons.
After shaking down the mercury-in-glass clinical thermometer, she placed it under the patient's tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinical thermometer - thermometer for measuring human body temperature

Etymology

From 'mercury' (the element used as liquid metal inside the glass tube) and 'thermometer' (from Greek 'thermo-' meaning 'heat' and 'metron' meaning 'measure').
