somnambulatory behavior

1. NOUN

/ sɑmˈnæmbjələˌtɔri bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / sɒmˈnæmbjələˌtɔːri bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
behavior involving walking or performing other actions while asleep, often without conscious awareness
actions performed while sleepwalking
medical

Examples

His somnambulatory behavior included wandering around the house at night without remembering it in the morning.
Doctors observed several cases of somnambulatory behavior among the sleep clinic patients.
The safety of children with somnambulatory behavior requires extra precautions around stairs and doors.

Synset Examples

Her somnambulatory behavior worried her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepwalking - walking or carrying out activities while asleep

Etymology

From Latin 'somnus' (sleep) + 'ambulare' (to walk), via the medical term 'somnambulism.'
