Fifth Lateran Council

1. PROPN

/ fɪfθ ˈlætərən ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / fɪfθ ˈlatərən ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
the ecumenical council of the Roman Catholic Church held from 1512 to 1517 in Rome, which enacted disciplinary reforms and discussed plans for a crusade against Turkey.
Roman Catholic Church council (1512–1517)

Examples

The Fifth Lateran Council issued important decrees on church reform in the early 16th century.
Historians often debate the long-term impact of the Fifth Lateran Council on the Catholic Reformation.
One major topic at the Fifth Lateran Council was the organization of a crusade against the Ottoman Turks.

Etymology

From Lateran (the papal basilica and palace in Rome) plus 'fifth' (indicating its chronological order among the Lateran councils).
