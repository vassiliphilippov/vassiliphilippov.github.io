higher law

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪər lɔ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə lɔː / (BrE)
a principle or ethical rule regarded as overriding or superior to the laws enacted by society or the state
a principle above enacted law

Examples

Many activists argued that a higher law demanded civil disobedience against unjust legislation.
Throughout history, some people have claimed loyalty to a higher law than that of their government.
The concept of higher law has often played a crucial role in social reform movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural law - a body of unchanging moral principles regarded as a basis for all human conduct
  • Antonym: statutory law - written laws enacted by a legislative body
