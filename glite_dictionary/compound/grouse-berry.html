grouse-berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊs ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊs ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A shrub native to northwestern North America, bearing red berries.
shrub with red berries of NW North America

Examples

The hikers stopped to pick grouse-berries along the trail.
Wildlife, such as birds and bears, often feed on the grouse-berry shrub’s fruit.
She read a field guide to identify the grouse-berry growing near the river.
