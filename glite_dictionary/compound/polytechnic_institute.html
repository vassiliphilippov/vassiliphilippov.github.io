polytechnic institute

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈtɛknɪk ˈɪnstɪˌtuːt / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈtɛknɪk ˈɪnstɪˌtjuːt / (BrE)
A college or school that provides instruction in a wide range of technical and applied sciences and industrial arts.
technical and applied sciences college

Examples

After graduating from a polytechnic institute, she started a career in engineering.
The city council decided to invest in a new polytechnic institute to boost local industries.
Admissions to the polytechnic institute are highly competitive due to its strong reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technical college - a college offering courses in technical and vocational subjects
  • Degree Higher: university - an institution of higher education and research

Etymology

From Greek 'poly' (many) + 'technē' (art, skill), and Latin 'institutum' (establishment).
