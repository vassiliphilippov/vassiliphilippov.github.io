internment camp

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrn.mənt kæmp / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːn.mənt kamp / (BrE)
a place where people, such as prisoners of war or civilians during conflict, are detained under armed guard, especially for political or military reasons
camp for detaining prisoners or civilians

Examples

During World War II, many Japanese Americans were sent to internment camps across the United States.
The government faced international criticism for establishing an internment camp for political dissidents.
He wrote a memoir about his childhood experiences in an internment camp during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - a place where large numbers of people are detained under harsh conditions

Etymology

From 'internment' (from French *interner*, to confine) + 'camp' (from Latin *campus*, field).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrn.mənt kæmp / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːn.mənt kæmp / (BrE)
A detention facility where people are confined, usually without trial, for political or military reasons, especially during wartime.
facility for detaining people for political or military reasons

Examples

During World War II, thousands of Japanese Americans were forced into internment camps.
The museum's main exhibit highlights the daily life of children in an internment camp.
He wrote a memoir detailing his experiences as a survivor of an internment camp.

Synset Examples

The family was sent to an internment camp during the war.
Survivors of the internment camp shared their stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - prison camp where large numbers of people are detained, often under harsh conditions
  • Synonym: prison camp - place where prisoners, especially of war, are kept

Etymology

From 'internment' (from French 'interner', to confine) and 'camp' (from Latin 'campus', field).
