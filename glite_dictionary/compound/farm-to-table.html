farm-to-table

1. ADJ

/ fɑrm tə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / fɑːm tə ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
Relating to a system or movement that promotes the direct sourcing and consumption of food from local farms, minimizing intermediaries.
locally sourced food movement

Examples

Many restaurants now feature farm-to-table menus to support local agriculture and reduce environmental impact.
She prefers farm-to-table produce because it is fresher and more sustainable.
The farm-to-table movement has changed the way people think about sourcing their food.

Synset Examples

To appreciate farm-to-table, you should go the entire animal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locally sourced - food that originates from nearby producers
  • Antonym: factory-farmed - produced using large-scale industrial farming methods
