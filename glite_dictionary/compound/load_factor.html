load factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊd ˌfæktər / (AmE), / ˈləʊd ˌfæktə / (BrE)
(Aeronautics) The ratio of an external load applied to an aircraft to the actual weight of the aircraft, typically measured in g-forces.
ratio of external load to aircraft weight (aeronautics)

Examples

During turbulence, the load factor can increase significantly, putting extra stress on the aircraft's structure.
The aircraft is designed to withstand a load factor of up to 6g during sharp maneuvers.
Pilots monitor the load factor to ensure they remain within safe operational limits while flying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: g-force - unit of measurement for acceleration felt as weight
