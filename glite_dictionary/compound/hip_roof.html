hip roof

1. NOUN

/ hɪp ruːf / (AmE), / hɪp ruːf / (BrE)
A roof with all sides sloping downward to the walls, usually with a fairly gentle slope.
roof with sloping sides and ends

Examples

The house features a hip roof that adds to its classic design.
Builders recommend a hip roof for better wind resistance in stormy areas.
The architect drew plans for a bungalow with a low hip roof.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gable roof - roof with two sloping sides that form a gable at each end
