standing wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɪŋ weɪv / (AmE), / ˈstændɪŋ weɪv / (BrE)
A wave in which certain points, called nodes, remain fixed while others between them vibrate with maximum amplitude, producing a stationary pattern.
stationary wave with fixed nodes

Examples

The pattern of a standing wave can be observed on a vibrating guitar string.
Microwave ovens create standing waves that heat food unevenly in some spots.
In physics class, we studied how standing waves are formed in closed tubes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: traveling wave - a wave in which the amplitude varies from point to point over time
