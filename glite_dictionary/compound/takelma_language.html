Takelma language

1. NOUN

/ təˈkɛlmə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / təˈkɛlmə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Native American language of the Penutian family formerly spoken by the Takelma people of southwestern Oregon
extinct Native American language of Oregon

Examples

Linguists have studied the Takelma language to better understand its unique structure and history.
Many words from the Takelma language have been preserved in academic works despite the language's extinction.
The last native speaker of the Takelma language passed away in the 20th century.

Etymology

From Takelma, the name of the indigenous people and their self-designation, plus 'language'.
