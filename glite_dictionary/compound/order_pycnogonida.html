order Pycnogonida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌpɪknoʊˈɡɒnɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌpɪknəˈɡɒnɪdə / (BrE)
a group of marine arthropods, commonly known as sea spiders, characterized by long legs and a small body.
taxonomic order of sea spiders
medical

Examples

The order Pycnogonida includes more than 1,300 described species found throughout the world's oceans.
Scientists study the anatomy of order Pycnogonida to better understand arthropod evolution.
Despite their spider-like appearance, members of the order Pycnogonida are only distantly related to true spiders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea spider - marine arthropod of order Pycnogonida

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Pycnogonum' (meaning 'thick knee') + '-ida' (denoting an order).
