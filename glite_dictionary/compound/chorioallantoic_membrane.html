chorioallantoic membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrioʊəˌlænˈtoʊɪk ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˌkɔːriəʊəˌlænˈtəʊɪk ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
a highly vascular fetal membrane formed by the fusion of the chorion and the adjacent wall of the allantois, found in bird and reptile embryos
vascular fetal membrane in embryos
medical

Examples

The chorioallantoic membrane is often used in scientific research to study blood vessel growth.
Damage to the chorioallantoic membrane can affect gas exchange in reptile embryos.
Eggs of many bird species develop a chorioallantoic membrane to support the growing embryo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnion - the innermost membrane that encloses the embryo of a mammal, bird, or reptile
  • Synonym: chorion - the outermost fetal membrane in reptiles, birds, and mammals
  • Synonym: allantois - a fetal membrane involved in respiration and excretion, part of the chorioallantoic membrane

Etymology

From Greek 'chorion' (membrane enclosing the embryo) and 'allantois' (sausage-shaped), referring to the two fused components.
