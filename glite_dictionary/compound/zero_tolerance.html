zero tolerance

1. NOUN

/ ˌziɹoʊ ˈtɑlɚəns / (AmE), / ˌzɪəɹəʊ ˈtɒl(ə)ɹəns / (BrE)
a policy of enforcing rules or laws strictly without making any exceptions for minor infractions
strict policy with no exceptions

Examples

The school has a zero tolerance policy on bullying to protect all students.
Many cities have adopted zero tolerance laws to combat crime in troubled neighborhoods.
The company implemented zero tolerance for workplace harassment after several incidents were reported.

Synset Examples

he urged zero tolerance for priests who abuse children sexually

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant
