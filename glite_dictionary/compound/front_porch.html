front porch

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt pɔrtʃ / (AmE), / frʌnt pɔːtʃ / (BrE)
a covered structure attached to the front entrance of a house or building, typically used as a sitting area or for shelter from the weather
covered entrance area at the front of a house

Examples

We sat on the front porch to enjoy the warm summer evening.
She decorated her front porch with plants and lanterns.
He waved goodbye from the front porch as they drove away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porch - a covered shelter projecting in front of the entrance of a building
