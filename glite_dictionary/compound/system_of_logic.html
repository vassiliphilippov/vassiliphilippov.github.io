system of logic

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəm əv ˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəm əv ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
a structured set of principles and rules used to analyze, deduce, and reason arguments or proofs
set of logical principles for reasoning

Examples

He studied Aristotle's system of logic in his philosophy class.
The mathematician developed a new system of logic to solve complex problems.
Every system of logic has its own set of axioms and rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logic - the science of reasoning, proof, and principles of deduction
