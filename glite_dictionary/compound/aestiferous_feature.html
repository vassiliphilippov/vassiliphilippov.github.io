aestiferous feature

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːstəˈfɛɹəs ˈfiːtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌiːstəˈfɛːrəs ˈfiːtʃə / (BrE)
A feature or element that is aesthetically pleasing or attractive to the eye.
a visually pleasing element

Examples

The modern art museum was filled with aestiferous features that delighted visitors.
Landscape designers often incorporate aestiferous features to enhance the visual appeal of a park.
The restoration project highlighted several aestiferous features that had been hidden for decades.

Synset Examples

Aestiferous features defined the architecture of the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornament - a decorative object, especially one that adds beauty
  • Antonym: blemish - an imperfection that spoils appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'aesthetica' (perception of beauty) and English 'feature'.
