yellow loosestrife

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈluːsstraɪf / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈluːsstraɪf / (BrE)
a perennial plant with yellow flowers, commonly found in Europe and Asia, often regarded as a weed
a yellow-flowered plant, often a weed

Examples

Yellow loosestrife grows abundantly along the riverbanks every summer.
Many gardeners remove yellow loosestrife because it can spread quickly and crowd out other plants.
In some regions, yellow loosestrife is valued for its bright, cheerful flowers.

Etymology

From 'yellow' describing the flower color and 'loosestrife,' from Old English *lōstrīfe*, applied to various plants in the genus Lysimachia.
