parietal gyrus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈraɪ.ɪ.tl ˈɡaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / pəˈraɪ.ɪ.təl ˈɡaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
any of the ridges (convolutions) on the surface of the parietal lobe in the brain's cerebrum
convolution on the parietal lobe of the brain
medical

Examples

Research shows that the parietal gyrus plays a significant role in spatial perception and movement planning.
Damage to the parietal gyrus can lead to difficulties in sensory processing and coordination.
Scientists studied the activity in the right parietal gyrus during mathematical problem-solving tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parietal lobe - the division of the cerebrum at the upper back of the skull
  • Synonym: gyrus - a ridge on the surface of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'parietalis' (of a wall) and Greek 'gyrus' (circle, ring).
