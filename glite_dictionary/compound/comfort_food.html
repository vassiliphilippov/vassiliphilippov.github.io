comfort food

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərt fuːd / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət fuːd / (BrE)
Food that is simply prepared and gives a feeling of wellbeing, often associated with childhood or home cooking, typically high in sugar or carbohydrates.
food giving a sense of wellbeing, often nostalgic

Examples

After a long, stressful day, she turned to comfort food like macaroni and cheese to feel better.
Many people crave comfort food when they're homesick or feeling down.
Chicken soup is a classic example of comfort food for cold winter evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soul food - traditional food associated with African American cuisine, especially in the southern US

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.fɚt fuːd / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.fət fuːd / (BrE)
Food that is eaten to provide a sense of emotional comfort or nostalgia, often associated with home or childhood.
food that brings emotional comfort

Examples

When I'm feeling down, a plate of comfort food like macaroni and cheese helps me feel better.
Her favorite comfort food is homemade apple pie, which reminds her of her childhood.
On rainy days, many people crave comfort food like stew or mashed potatoes.

Synset Examples

Grandma's chicken soup is my comfort food.
Grandma's lamb pie is the best comfort food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soul food - traditional African-American cuisine in the southern US, often associated with comfort and home-cooked meals
  • Degree Lower: junk food - food that is high in calories but low in nutritional value, often eaten for comfort or convenience
