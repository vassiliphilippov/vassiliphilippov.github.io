gigundous mistake

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈɡʌn.dəs mɪˈsteɪk / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɡʌn.dəs mɪsˈteɪk / (BrE)
an extremely large or significant error or blunder
an extremely large mistake
informal

Examples

Forgetting the anniversary dinner was a gigundous mistake that he regretted for months.
Calling the wrong client was a gigundous mistake in the middle of the negotiations.
Purchasing the faulty equipment turned out to be a gigundous mistake for the company.

Synset Examples

He made a gigundous mistake by forgetting her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge mistake - a very large or serious mistake
  • Antonym: minor mistake - a small or insignificant mistake

Etymology

From the informal term 'gigundous,' a blend of 'gigantic' and 'humongous.'
