family Haliotidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌhæliˈoʊtɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌhæliˈəʊtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod mollusks commonly known as abalones, characterized by their ear-shaped shells.
the biological family of abalones

Examples

The family Haliotidae includes several species prized for their decorative shells and edible flesh.
Scientists are studying the distribution of family Haliotidae in coastal waters worldwide.
Many conservation efforts now focus on protecting members of the family Haliotidae from overfishing and environmental threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abalone - a type of edible sea snail with a flattened, ear-shaped shell

Etymology

From New Latin Haliotidae, based on Greek haliotis ('sea ear') and the taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
