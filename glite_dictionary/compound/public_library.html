public library

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈlaɪˌbrɛri / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈlaɪ.brəri / (BrE)
A library supported by public funds and open for use by the general public, providing books, resources, and services at no direct cost to users.
library open to the public

Examples

The city built a new public library to serve its growing population.
She volunteers at the local public library every weekend.
You can borrow up to ten books at a time from the public library.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library - a place where books and other materials are kept for people to use or borrow
