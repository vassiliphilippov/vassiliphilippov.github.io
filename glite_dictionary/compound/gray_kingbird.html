gray kingbird

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈkɪŋˌbɝd / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈkɪŋˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a large North and South American bird of the tyrant flycatcher family, breeding in the southeastern US and wintering in tropical America, similar to but larger than the eastern kingbird
a large flycatcher bird of the Americas

Examples

We spotted a gray kingbird perched on a wire near the beach.
The gray kingbird migrates from the southeastern United States to the Caribbean each winter.
Unlike the eastern kingbird, the gray kingbird has a slightly larger body and a heavier bill.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eastern kingbird - a smaller related kingbird species found in North America
