bush poppy

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, often grown for its fragrant golden yellow flowers
evergreen shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The bush poppy is prized in gardens for its vibrant golden blossoms.
We saw a cluster of bush poppy shrubs along the hiking trail in southern California.
Gardeners recommend planting bush poppy in well-drained soil to encourage healthy growth.
