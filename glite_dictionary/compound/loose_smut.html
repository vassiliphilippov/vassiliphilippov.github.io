loose smut

1. NOUN

/ luːs smʌt / (AmE), / luːs smʌt / (BrE)
a disease of cereal grains, especially wheat and barley, in which the entire head becomes a powdery mass of fungal spores
fungal disease of cereal grains

Examples

Many wheat fields were devastated by loose smut infections this season.
The presence of loose smut can drastically reduce grain yields if left untreated.
Farmers use resistant varieties to protect crops from loose smut.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covered smut - a different smut disease where the grains are enclosed by spore masses

2. NOUN

/ luːs smʌt / (AmE), / luːs smʌt / (BrE)
a fungal disease of grains, especially caused by Ustilago species, in which the flowering head is replaced by a powdery mass of spores
a grain disease caused by a smut fungus

Examples

Wheat farmers must monitor their crops for signs of loose smut during the growing season.
Loose smut can dramatically reduce yields if left untreated in barley fields.
The agricultural extension office provides guidelines for preventing loose smut infestations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covered smut - a smut disease of grains where spore masses are held within membranes
