Papanicolaou smear

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæpəˈnɪkəˌlaʊ smɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌpæpənɪˈkælaʊ smɪə / (BrE)
a test involving the collection of cells from the cervix and uterus to examine for abnormal or pre-cancerous changes
test for abnormal cervical cells
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a Papanicolaou smear during her annual check-up.
A Papanicolaou smear can help detect cervical cancer at an early stage.
It is important for women to have regular Papanicolaou smears as part of routine health care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pap smear - common term for the Papanicolaou smear test

Etymology

Named after Greek-American physician George N. Papanicolaou, who developed the test.
