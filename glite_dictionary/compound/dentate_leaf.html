dentate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛn.teɪt lif / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.teɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf having a margin with tooth-like projections pointing outward.
leaf with a toothed margin
medical

Examples

The oak tree in the garden has dentate leaves with prominent pointed teeth along the edges.
Identifying plants by their dentate leaves can be helpful in botany class.
Some herbs, such as nettle, are recognized by their sharply dentate leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated leaf - a leaf with a saw-toothed edge; teeth pointing toward the apex

Etymology

From Latin 'dentatus', meaning 'toothed'.
