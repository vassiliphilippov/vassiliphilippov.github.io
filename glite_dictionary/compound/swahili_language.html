Swahili language

1. NOUN

/ swɑːˈhiːli ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / swɑːˈhiːli ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Bantu language widely spoken in East Africa, serving as the official language of Kenya and Tanzania and used as a lingua franca in the region
official Bantu language of East Africa

Examples

Many people in Tanzania and Kenya speak Swahili language as their mother tongue.
He decided to study the Swahili language before traveling to East Africa.
The Swahili language serves as a bridge for communication among different ethnic groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kiswahili - native name for Swahili language

Etymology

From Arabic 'sawāḥil', meaning 'coasts', referring to the East African coast.
