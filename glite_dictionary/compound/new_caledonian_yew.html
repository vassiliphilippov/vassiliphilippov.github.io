New Caledonian yew

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˌkælɪˈdoʊniən juː / (AmE), / njuː ˌkælɪˈdəʊniən juː / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree (yew) native to New Caledonia, also cultivated in eastern Australia, New Zealand, and Hawaii
yew tree native to New Caledonia

Examples

The New Caledonian yew is highly valued for its timber in the Pacific region.
Many gardens in New Zealand feature the New Caledonian yew for its decorative foliage.
Botanists were excited to discover a mature New Caledonian yew growing outside its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - type of evergreen tree with red berries and needle-like leaves
