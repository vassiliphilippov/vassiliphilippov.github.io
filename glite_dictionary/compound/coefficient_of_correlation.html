coefficient of correlation

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv ˌkɒrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical measure indicating the extent to which two variables change together, ranging from -1 (perfect negative correlation) to +1 (perfect positive correlation)
statistical measure of variable association
medical

Examples

The coefficient of correlation between income and education in the study was found to be 0.78.
A high coefficient of correlation suggests a strong relationship between the two measurements.
If the coefficient of correlation is close to zero, there is little or no linear relationship between the variables.

Synset Examples

what is the correlation between those two variables?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation - a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things

Etymology

From Latin co- (together), efficiō (to make), and correlate, referring to a measure that shows how quantities vary together.
