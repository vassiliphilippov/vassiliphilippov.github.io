old school

1. NOUN

/ oʊld skuːl / (AmE), / əʊld skuːl / (BrE)
People who support or follow traditional ideas, practices, or styles, often in contrast to newer trends.
group favoring traditional ideas

Examples

Many chefs from the old school insist on making pasta by hand.
He considers himself part of the old school when it comes to journalism.
The old school tends to resist adopting new technologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - person who prefers traditional values and is cautious about change
  • Antonym: progressive - person favoring or implementing new ideas and liberal reform
