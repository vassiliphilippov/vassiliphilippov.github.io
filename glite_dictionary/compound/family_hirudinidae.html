family Hirudinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli haɪˌruːdɪˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli haɪˌruːdɪˈniːdiː / (BrE)
a family of leeches in the class Hirudinea, which includes the medicinal leech and related species.
family of leeches (Hirudinea)

Examples

The family Hirudinidae includes the well-known medicinal leech used in clinical therapies.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the family Hirudinidae in freshwater habitats.
Members of the family Hirudinidae are characterised by their ability to secrete anticoagulant substances.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hirudinidae', based on 'Hirudo' (genus of leeches) and '-idae' (denoting animal family).
