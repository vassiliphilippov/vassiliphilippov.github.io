September 29

1. NOUN

/ sɛpˈtɛmbɚ ˈtwɛnti ˈnaɪn / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛmbə ˈtwɛnti ˈnaɪn / (BrE)
In England, Wales, and Ireland, the day known as Michaelmas, marking a quarter day and honoring the archangel Michael.
a quarter day known as Michaelmas
british

Examples

Universities traditionally begin their first term around September 29, also known as Michaelmas.
Rent payments and other contracts were often due on September 29 in historical Britain.
The church held a special service on September 29 to celebrate the feast of St Michael.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Michaelmas - the feast of St Michael and All Angels, observed on September 29
