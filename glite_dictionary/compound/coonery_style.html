coonery style

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːnəri staɪl / (AmE), / ˈkuːnəri staɪl / (BrE)
A style of performance or behavior characterized by the perpetuation of offensive racial stereotypes, especially those relating to Black people.
performance style using racial stereotypes
offensive

Examples

Many critics denounced the film for its coonery style and blatant stereotypes.
The actor eventually apologized for adopting a coonery style in his comedy routines.
Some social commentators challenge entertainment that still uses a coonery style for humor.

Synset Examples

The comedian was known for his coonery style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minstrelsy - the performance tradition involving exaggerated, stereotyped portrayals of Black people

Etymology

From 'coon', a racial slur for Black people, and '-ery' denoting behavior or practices.
