type metal

1. NOUN

/ taɪp ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / taɪp ˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
an alloy of lead, tin, and antimony used in making printing type for letterpress printing.
alloy used for printing type

Examples

Early printers relied on type metal for casting durable letters.
The composition of type metal ensures sharp printing impressions and easy casting.
Some old printing presses still contain blocks made from original type metal.

Etymology

From 'type' (printing letter) and 'metal', referring to its use in casting movable type.
