genus Casuarius

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kæˈsuːəri.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kæˈsjuːəri.əs / (BrE)
The sole genus of the family Casuaridae, comprising the large, flightless birds known as cassowaries.
genus of cassowaries (birds)

Examples

The genus Casuarius includes all living species of cassowaries found in Australia and New Guinea.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of birds within the genus Casuarius.
The southern cassowary, a member of the genus Casuarius, is known for its distinctive casque.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassowary - large, flightless bird native to Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From Latin 'Casuarius', the scientific name for cassowaries, given by taxonomists.
