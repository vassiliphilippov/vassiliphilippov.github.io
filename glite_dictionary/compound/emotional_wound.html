emotional wound

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənəl wund / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənəl wuːnd / (BrE)
A deep psychological pain or trauma resulting from distressing experiences or events.
psychological pain or trauma

Examples

It took her years of therapy to finally overcome her emotional wound.
Writing about his experiences helped him process the emotional wound left by losing his job.
Even after the conflict ended, many people carried deep emotional wounds for decades.

Synset Examples

Time can help heal emotional wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychological trauma - a deeply distressing or disturbing mental experience
  • Antonym: physical wound - an injury to the body, such as a cut or break in the skin
