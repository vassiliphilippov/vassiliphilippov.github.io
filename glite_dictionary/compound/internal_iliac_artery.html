internal iliac artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl ˈɪli.æk ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː.nəl ˈɪl.i.æk ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
the inner branch of the common iliac artery that supplies blood to the pelvic organs and gluteal region
artery supplying blood to the pelvis and buttocks
medical

Examples

During surgery, care was taken to avoid injuring the internal iliac artery.
The internal iliac artery branches to supply blood to multiple pelvic organs.
A blockage in the internal iliac artery can lead to significant blood flow issues in the pelvis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external iliac artery - the outer branch of the common iliac artery that supplies blood to the lower limb

Etymology

From Latin *iliacus* (of the flanks/groin) and *arteria* (artery); 'internal' denotes its position relative to 'external iliac artery'.
