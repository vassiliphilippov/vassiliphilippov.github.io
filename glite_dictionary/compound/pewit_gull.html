pewit gull

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːɪt ɡʌl / (AmE), / ˈpjuːɪt ɡʌl / (BrE)
a small European gull with a black head, especially during the breeding season
small black-headed European gull

Examples

In early spring, the pewit gull returns to its breeding grounds along marshy lakes.
We spotted a flock of pewit gulls calling noisily near the estuary.
The pewit gull is easily identified by its dark hood and slender build.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-headed gull - small European gull with a distinctive black (summer) or dark head

Etymology

Pewit refers to its call, which resembles 'pee-wit'; gull from Old English 'gull'.
