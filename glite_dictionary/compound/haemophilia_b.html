haemophilia B

1. NOUN

/ hiːməˈfɪliə bi / (AmE), / hiːməˈfɪlɪə biː / (BrE)
A rare hereditary clotting disorder caused by a deficiency of clotting factor IX, resulting in prolonged bleeding.
rare hereditary clotting disorder
medical

Examples

The boy was diagnosed with haemophilia B after experiencing severe bruising and unexplained bleeding.
Treatment for haemophilia B often involves regular infusions of clotting factor IX.
Carriers of the haemophilia B gene may not show symptoms but can pass the disorder to their children.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: haemophilia A - a hereditary clotting disorder caused by deficiency of factor VIII
  • Synonym: hemophilia B - US spelling of haemophilia B

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and '-philia' (love, tendency), with the letter B denoting the specific type distinguished from haemophilia A.
