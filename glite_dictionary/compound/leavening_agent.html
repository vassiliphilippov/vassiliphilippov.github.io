leavening agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvənɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈlɛvənɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A substance added to dough or batter to make it rise and become lighter and softer, such as yeast, baking powder, or baking soda.
substance that makes dough rise

Examples

Yeast is a traditional leavening agent used in making bread and pizza dough.
Baking powder serves as a convenient leavening agent for cookies and cakes.
Without a proper leavening agent, your baked goods may turn out dense and heavy.

Synset Examples

I know I can use chemical leaveners, but I think that using yeast as a leavening agent is more healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaven - substance used to cause dough to rise
  • Synonym: yeast - fungus used as a leavening agent in baking
  • Synonym: baking powder - chemical leavening agent used in baking

Etymology

From 'leaven', from Old French 'levain', from Latin 'levamen' (alleviation, leavening).
