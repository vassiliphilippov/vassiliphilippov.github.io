lever tumbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːvər ˈtʌmblər / (AmE), / ˈliːvə ˈtʌmblə / (BrE)
a flat metal component inside a lever lock which must be lifted to the correct position to allow the lock to open
flat metal part in a lever lock

Examples

The locksmith explained how each lever tumbler must be aligned for the key to turn the lock.
Old safes often use a multiple lever tumbler mechanism to provide added security.
When the lever tumbler sticks, the lock may not open even with the correct key.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tumbler - a moving part in a lock that must be set to permit the bolt to move
