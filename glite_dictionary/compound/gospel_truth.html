gospel truth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑspəl truθ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl truːθ / (BrE)
a statement or belief that is completely true and should not be doubted
absolute truth; unquestionable fact

Examples

Don't take every rumor as the gospel truth before checking the facts.
She accepted his explanation as the gospel truth and refused to believe anything else.
It would be unwise to treat everything you read online as the gospel truth.

Synset Examples

his word was gospel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truth - the quality or state of being true
  • Antonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive

Etymology

From 'gospel' meaning 'the teaching or revelation of Christ', implying ultimate truth.
