Balmoral Castle

1. PROPN

/ bælˈmɔrəl ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / bælˈmɒrəl ˈkɑːsl / (BrE)
a large castle in northeastern Scotland that serves as a private residence of the British sovereign
royal residence in Scotland
british

Examples

The Queen frequently spends her summer holidays at Balmoral Castle.
Tourists admire the stunning scenery surrounding Balmoral Castle in Scotland.
Balmoral Castle has been a private royal residence since the reign of Queen Victoria.

Etymology

Named after the estate of Balmoral in Aberdeenshire, Scotland; 'Balmoral' is thought to derive from Scottish Gaelic 'Baile Mòr Allt', meaning 'big house of the river'.
