coaster brake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstər breɪk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstə breɪk / (BrE)
a type of bicycle brake that is activated by pedaling backward, typically integrated into the rear hub
bicycle brake engaged by pedaling backward

Examples

The old bike was equipped with a coaster brake instead of the more modern hand brakes.
Children often prefer bikes with a coaster brake because they're easier to use.
Remember to pedal backward gently to engage the coaster brake when you want to stop.
