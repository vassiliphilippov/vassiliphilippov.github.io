ground snake

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd sneɪk / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd sneɪk / (BrE)
a small, shy, brightly-ringed snake found in arid or semiarid areas of western North America
small ringed snake of western North America

Examples

A ground snake is often mistaken for a coral snake due to its bright rings.
The biologist carefully picked up the ground snake, noting its calm demeanor.
Ground snakes are rarely seen during the daytime as they prefer to hide under rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - a legless, carnivorous reptile
