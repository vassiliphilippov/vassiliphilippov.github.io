family Ericaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɛrɪˈkeɪsiː, ɛrɪˈkeɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɛrɪˈkeɪsiː, ɛrɪˈkeɪsiaɪ / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, known as the heather family, which includes heathers, rhododendrons, and blueberries
the heather plant family

Examples

There are over 100 genera included in the family Ericaceae.
Blueberries and rhododendrons both belong to the family Ericaceae.
Botanists are researching the evolution of species within the family Ericaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - common name for plants of the genus Erica and related species

Etymology

From New Latin Ericaceae, based on the genus Erica (heather).
