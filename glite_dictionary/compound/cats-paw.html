cat's-paw

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætsˌpɔ / (AmE), / ˈkætsˌpɔː / (BrE)
A type of hitch made in the middle of a rope, forming two loops (eyes) for attaching tackle.
a rope hitch with two loops for tackle

Examples

The sailor tied a cat's-paw in the rope before hooking it to the block.
To lift the heavy crate, they used a cat's-paw to attach the pulley system.
It's important to know how to make a cat's-paw when rigging sailing equipment.
