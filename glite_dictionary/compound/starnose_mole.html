starnose mole

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːrˌnoʊs moʊl / (AmE), / ˈstɑːnəʊs məʊl / (BrE)
an amphibious mole native to eastern North America with twenty-two pink, fleshy tentacles around its nose
amphibious North American mole with nose tentacles

Examples

The starnose mole uses its unusual nose tentacles to detect prey underwater.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the starnose mole during spring.
A starnose mole can be found in wetlands across eastern Canada and the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - a small, burrowing mammal with velvety fur and strong forelimbs
