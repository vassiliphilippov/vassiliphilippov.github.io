judgment in personam

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɪn pərˈsoʊ.nəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɪn pəˈsəʊ.nəm / (BrE)
a court judgment directed against a specific individual or entity, typically requiring payment of money damages
legal judgment against a person or entity
legal

Examples

The court issued a judgment in personam requiring the defendant to pay damages to the plaintiff.
A judgment in personam is enforceable against the individual wherever they may be found.
Unlike a judgment affecting property, a judgment in personam creates a personal obligation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: judgment in rem - a judgment affecting the interests of all persons in a particular property

Etymology

From Latin 'in personam', meaning 'against the person'.
