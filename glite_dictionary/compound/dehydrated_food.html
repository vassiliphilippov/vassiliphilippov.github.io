dehydrated food

1. NOUN

/ diˈhaɪdreɪtəd fud / (AmE), / diˈhaɪdreɪtɪd fuːd / (BrE)
Food that has been preserved by having most of its water content removed through dehydration.
food preserved by dehydration

Examples

Many campers rely on dehydrated food because it is lightweight and easy to carry.
Dehydrated food can retain much of its nutritional value if processed correctly.
The astronauts' menu included several types of dehydrated food for their mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dried food - food preserved by drying
  • Antonym: fresh food - food not preserved or processed
