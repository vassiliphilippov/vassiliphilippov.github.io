field mustard

1. NOUN

/ fild ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / fiːld ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a weedy Eurasian plant (Brassica rapa) commonly found in fields and often regarded as a pest, with yellow flowers and edible leaves
a weedy Eurasian plant; Brassica rapa

Examples

The field mustard spread rapidly through the farmer's wheat crop, reducing its yield.
Researchers study field mustard to understand how weeds adapt to agricultural environments.
She recognized field mustard by its bright yellow flowers scattered across the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard plant - any of several plants of the genus Brassica with pungent seeds or leaves
