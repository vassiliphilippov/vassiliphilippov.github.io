pressure sore

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər sɔr / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə sɔː / (BrE)
A sore area of the skin that develops when constant pressure restricts blood flow, often seen in bedridden patients.
ulcer caused by prolonged pressure on skin
medical

Examples

The nurse carefully checked the patient's back for any signs of a pressure sore.
Regular repositioning can help prevent the development of a pressure sore in immobile patients.
She had a painful pressure sore on her heel after being bedridden for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedsore - an ulcer formed on the skin due to prolonged pressure
