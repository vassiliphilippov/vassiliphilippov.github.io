trans man

1. NOUN

/ trænz mæn / (AmE), / tranz mæn / (BrE)
A person who was assigned female at birth but identifies and lives as a man, typically after transitioning.
a person assigned female at birth who identifies as male

Examples

As a trans man, he often shares his story to support others.
Many healthcare providers are working to better understand the needs of trans men.
The author, a well-known trans man, advocates for transgender rights.

Synset Examples

Being a trans man has shaped his perspective on gender.
He proudly identifies as a trans man in his community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trans woman - a person assigned male at birth who identifies as female

Etymology

From 'trans-', meaning 'across' or 'beyond', and 'man'. The prefix traces back to Latin 'trans'.
