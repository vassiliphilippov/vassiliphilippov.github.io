morris dancing

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrɪs ˌdænsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒrɪs ˌdɑːnsɪŋ / (BrE)
A traditional English folk dance performed by groups of dancers in costume, usually accompanied by music.
traditional English folk dance
british

Examples

Every May, the local group performs morris dancing in the village square.
He became interested in morris dancing after watching a festival showcase.
Many British towns have morris dancing teams that rehearse throughout the year.

Etymology

From the word 'Morris', an alteration of 'Moorish', indicating supposed origins or inspiration from Moorish (North African) dancers.
