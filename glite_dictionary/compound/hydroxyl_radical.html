hydroxyl radical

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑksɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
A highly reactive chemical species consisting of an oxygen and a hydrogen atom (•OH), often found in chemical compounds such as bases, some acids, and alcohols.
reactive chemical group -OH
medical

Examples

The hydroxyl radical is one of the most reactive chemical species in atmospheric chemistry.
Many reactions involving the hydroxyl radical play a crucial role in biological processes.
Scientists detected the presence of the hydroxyl radical in the reaction by using advanced spectroscopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroxide - a compound containing the -OH group as the anion

Etymology

From 'hydroxyl' (hydro- + Greek oxys 'sharp', referring to oxygen) and 'radical' (from Late Latin radicalis, from radix 'root').
