genus Eryngium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈrɪn.dʒi.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈrɪn.dʒɪ.əm / (BrE)
a large genus of decorative plants with thistle-like flower heads, found worldwide and often known as sea hollies
genus of decorative thistle-like plants (sea hollies)

Examples

Many gardeners choose genus Eryngium for its striking blue flower heads and distinctive look.
Species in the genus Eryngium are commonly called sea hollies and are popular in floral arrangements.
The genus Eryngium is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Etymology

From Latin 'Eryngium,' related to Greek 'ērungion' (sea holly).
