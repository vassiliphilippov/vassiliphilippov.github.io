no sugarcoating

1. NOUN

/ noʊ ˈʃʊɡɚˌkoʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈʃʊɡəkəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
no attempt to make something seem more pleasant, acceptable, or less harsh than it really is
not making something seem better than it is

Examples

Let's be honest with our feedback—no sugarcoating this time.
The doctor told me the truth about my condition, with no sugarcoating.
In his report, the journalist delivered the facts with no sugarcoating.

Synset Examples

He was always frank about his opinions, no sugarcoating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sugarcoat - to make something seem more pleasant than it is
  • Degree Higher: blunt - uncompromisingly forthright

Etymology

From 'sugarcoat', meaning to make something seem more attractive or palatable, combined with 'no' for negative sense.
