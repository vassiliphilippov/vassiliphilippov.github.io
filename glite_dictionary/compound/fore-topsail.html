fore-topsail

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈtɑpˌseɪl / (AmE), / fɔːˈtɒpseɪl / (BrE)
The topsail attached to the foremast of a sailing ship.
topsail located on a foremast

Examples

The crew quickly hoisted the fore-topsail to catch the morning breeze.
After reducing the fore-topsail, the ship became easier to handle in the strong wind.
The captain ordered the damaged fore-topsail to be replaced before the next voyage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topsail - a sail set above the lowest sail on a mast

Etymology

From 'foremast' (the forward mast) and 'topsail' (the sail above the lowest sail), signifying position and function.
