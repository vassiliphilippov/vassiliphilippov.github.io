combat zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmˌbæt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbæt zəʊn / (BrE)
an area where military forces are actively engaged in fighting during a conflict or war
area of active military fighting

Examples

The soldiers spent several months stationed in the combat zone.
Civilians were evacuated from the combat zone for their safety.
Medical teams provided urgent care to wounded soldiers in the combat zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: war zone - an area where war is taking place
  • Antonym: peace zone - an area not affected by war or conflict

/ ˈkɑːmˌbæt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbæt zəʊn / (BrE)
a city district noted for vice, crime, and disorder
city district with high crime and vice

Examples

Journalists were warned not to enter the combat zone after dark without police escorts.
The city council debated how to rehabilitate the old combat zone downtown.
Many residents avoid the combat zone because of frequent reports of violence and drug activity.

Related Terms
