Nuclear Regulatory Commission

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˌrɛɡ.jəˈleɪ.tɔːr.i kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.ə ˌrɛɡ.jʊˈleɪ.tər.i kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a United States federal agency established in 1974 to license and oversee nuclear power plants and ensure the safe use of nuclear materials
US federal agency overseeing nuclear safety

Examples

The Nuclear Regulatory Commission is responsible for ensuring that nuclear power plants comply with federal safety standards.
After the incident, the Nuclear Regulatory Commission conducted a thorough investigation into the reactor's safety protocols.
Engineers must follow guidelines set by the Nuclear Regulatory Commission when designing new nuclear facilities.
