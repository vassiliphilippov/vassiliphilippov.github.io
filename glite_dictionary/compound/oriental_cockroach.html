oriental cockroach

1. NOUN

/ ɔːr.iˈɛn.t̬əl ˈkɑːk.roʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɒr.iˈɛn.təl ˈkɒk.rəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a species of dark brown cockroach (Blatta orientalis) originally from Asia, now found worldwide
a species of dark brown cockroach

Examples

We discovered an oriental cockroach in the kitchen late last night.
The oriental cockroach prefers damp and dark environments, making basements a common habitat.
Pest control companies often get calls about the resilient oriental cockroach during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a flattened, brownish insect of the order Blattodea

Etymology

From Latin 'orientalis' meaning 'eastern', referring to its Asian origin.
