lightweight aerated concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtˌweɪt ˈɛr.eɪ.tɪd kənˈkriːt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌweɪt ˈeə.rɪ.eɪ.tɪd kənˈkriːt / (BrE)
A type of aerated concrete that incorporates an air-entraining agent or lightweight aggregates, such as expanded clay, cork granules, or vermiculite, to make the material lighter than traditional concrete.
type of lighter, aerated concrete

Examples

The new building was constructed using lightweight aerated concrete for improved insulation and reduced weight.
Engineers chose lightweight aerated concrete to minimize the load on the structure's foundation.
Because of its thermal properties, lightweight aerated concrete is popular in energy-efficient construction projects.

Synset Examples

Do not confuse cellular concrete with aerated autoclaved concrete, which is manufactured off-site using an entirely different method.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: aerated concrete - concrete containing tiny air bubbles, making it lighter and more insulating
  • Synonym: autoclaved aerated concrete - precast, lightweight aerated concrete cured in an autoclave
