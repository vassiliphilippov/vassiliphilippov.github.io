highbush cranberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbʊʃ ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbʊʃ ˈkrænˌbɛri / (BrE)
a deciduous North American shrub or small tree with three-lobed leaves and bright red berries, often grown for ornamental purposes and edible fruit
North American shrub with red berries

Examples

The highbush cranberry produces clusters of vibrant red berries in late summer.
Many gardeners plant highbush cranberry as an attractive ornamental shrub for their landscapes.
Wildlife such as birds are attracted to the fruit of the highbush cranberry.
