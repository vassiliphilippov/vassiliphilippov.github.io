southern arrow wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn ˈæroʊ wʊd / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈærəʊ wʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its blue-black berries and flexible wood once used for making arrows.
North American shrub with flexible wood and berries

Examples

The southern arrow wood produces berries that attract birds in late summer.
Indigenous peoples once crafted arrow shafts from southern arrow wood because of its strength and flexibility.
We planted southern arrow wood along the property line for its dense foliage and beautiful blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowwood - Shrubs of the Viburnum genus known for their use in arrow-making
