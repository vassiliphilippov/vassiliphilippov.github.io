Welsh parsley

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ ˈpɑr.sli / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈpɑː.sli / (BrE)
A type of parsley, also known as Alexanders, used as a culinary herb for its aromatic leaves and stems.
an edible herb used in cooking

Examples

She garnished the roast chicken with freshly chopped Welsh parsley.
A handful of Welsh parsley can brighten up any salad.
Gardeners in Britain often grow Welsh parsley for its flavor and hardiness.

Synset Examples

Welsh parsley adds a unique flavor to the soup.
She picked some Welsh parsley from the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alexanders - another name for Welsh parsley, a cultivated edible plant
  • Degree Lower: parsley - common garden herb used as a garnish or seasoning

Etymology

Welsh, referring to Wales or historical use in Britain, and parsley, from Old French 'persil', from Latin 'petroselinum'.
