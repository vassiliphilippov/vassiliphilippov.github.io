closed door

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd dɔr / (AmE), / kləʊzd dɔː / (BrE)
referring to private matters or situations kept hidden from public view
private or hidden matter

Examples

The negotiations happened behind a closed door, away from the media's attention.
She suspected that important decisions were being made inside the closed door of the boardroom.
Many conflicts within the family remained a closed door to outsiders.

Synset Examples

The secrets lie behind the closed doors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open door - a policy or situation of accessibility or transparency
