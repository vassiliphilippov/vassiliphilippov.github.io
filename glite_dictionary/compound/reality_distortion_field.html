reality distortion field

1. NOUN

/ riˈæləti dɪˈstɔrʃən fiːld / (AmE), / riˈalɪti dɪˈstɔːʃən fiːld / (BrE)
a situation or effect in which perception is skewed, often by someone's charisma or influence, causing others to believe in an implausible or overly optimistic vision
skewed perception due to charisma or influence

Examples

The CEO is so convincing that his explanations create a reality distortion field around the company's poor performance.
Within the startup's reality distortion field, impossible deadlines actually seem achievable.
His passionate speeches cast a reality distortion field, making the risks appear trivial to investors.

Synset Examples

The team's enthusiasm seemed like a reality distortion field.
His charisma creates a reality distortion field.
Working with him feels like being in a reality distortion field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a false conception or belief
  • Synonym: delusion - a belief maintained despite being contradicted by reality

Etymology

Popularized in the 1980s by Apple employees to describe Steve Jobs's persuasive charisma.
