postfix notation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊst.fɪks noʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈpəʊst.fɪks nəʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A way of writing mathematical expressions without parentheses, in which each operator comes after its operands.
operator-after-operand notation (math)

Examples

In postfix notation, the expression '3 4 +' means 3 plus 4.
Many calculators use postfix notation because it eliminates the need for parentheses.
Learning to convert between infix and postfix notation is important in computer science.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prefix notation - Operator-before-operand notation in math
  • Antonym: infix notation - Operator-between-operands notation in math

Etymology

From 'postfix', meaning 'to fix after', and 'notation', referring to a system of symbols; usage in mathematics dates to the mid-20th century.
