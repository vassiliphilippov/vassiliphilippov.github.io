double-bitted ax

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌbɪtɪd æks / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌbɪtɪd æks / (BrE)
an ax with a blade that has cutting edges on both sides of the head
axe with two cutting edges

Examples

The lumberjack preferred using a double-bitted ax for chopping and splitting wood.
A double-bitted ax allows the user to alternate between two sharp edges during extended work.
For safety, always check both blades of a double-bitted ax before use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-bitted ax - an ax with only one cutting edge
