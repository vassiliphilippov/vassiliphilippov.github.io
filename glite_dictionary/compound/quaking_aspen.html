quaking aspen

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪkɪŋ ˈæspən / (AmE), / ˈkweɪkɪŋ ˈaspən / (BrE)
A species of aspen tree (Populus tremula) with a broad, much-branched crown, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.
species of aspen tree (Populus tremula)

Examples

The leaves of the quaking aspen flutter in the slightest breeze, creating a shimmering effect.
A grove of quaking aspens turns brilliant yellow in the autumn.
Many wildlife species depend on quaking aspen stands for food and shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspen - a type of deciduous tree with trembling leaves

Etymology

Named for its trembling leaves; 'aspen' from Old English æspe.
