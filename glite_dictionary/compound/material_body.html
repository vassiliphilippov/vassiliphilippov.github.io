material body

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɪriəl ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəl ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
the physical body of a human being, as contrasted with the mind or spirit
physical human body

Examples

The philosopher argued that the material body is separate from the soul.
Modern medicine focuses chiefly on the treatment of the material body.
Some religious texts draw a distinction between the material body and the spirit.

Synset Examples

Leonardo studied the human body
he has a strong physique
the spirit is willing but the flesh is weak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical body - the physical structure of a person or organism
  • Synonym: flesh - the soft substance of a human or animal body, as opposed to the spirit
  • Antonym: spirit - the non-physical part of a person, contrasted with the body

Etymology

From Latin 'materialis' (of matter) + English 'body'; used to specify the physical rather than the spiritual aspect.
