escrow funds

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛs.kroʊ fʌndz / (AmE), / ˈɛs.krəʊ fʌndz / (BrE)
Money or assets held by a third party on behalf of two other parties until the fulfillment of certain conditions or obligations.
money held by a third party until conditions are met
legal

Examples

The buyer placed the payment into escrow funds to ensure the terms of the agreement were met.
At closing, the escrow funds were released to the seller after all conditions had been satisfied.
The company keeps all customer deposits as escrow funds until the project is completed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escrow - an arrangement where a third party holds money or property until conditions are fulfilled

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'escrowe' (a scrap or scroll; a deed delivered to a third person until a condition is met), combined with 'funds' (money available for a purpose).
