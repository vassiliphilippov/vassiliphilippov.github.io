creole-fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriː.oʊl fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkriː.əʊl fɪʃ / (BrE)
A deep-sea fish found in the tropical Atlantic, known for its bright coloration and elongated body.
deep-sea fish of tropical Atlantic

Examples

Divers spotted a bright creole-fish darting among the coral reefs.
The creole-fish is prized for its vivid purple and yellow hues in marine aquariums.
Studying the migration patterns of the creole-fish helps scientists understand Atlantic ocean currents.
