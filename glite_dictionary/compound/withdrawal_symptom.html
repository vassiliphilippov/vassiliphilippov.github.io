withdrawal symptom

1. NOUN

/ wɪðˈdrɔəl ˈsɪmptəm / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔː.əl ˈsɪmp.təm / (BrE)
a physical or psychological condition that occurs after stopping or reducing the intake of an addictive substance
effect experienced after stopping an addictive drug
medical

Examples

After quitting smoking, he experienced several withdrawal symptoms, such as headaches and irritability.
Insomnia is a common withdrawal symptom when stopping certain medications suddenly.
Doctors monitored her closely for any severe withdrawal symptoms following her alcohol detoxification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdrawal - the process of ceasing to take an addictive drug
