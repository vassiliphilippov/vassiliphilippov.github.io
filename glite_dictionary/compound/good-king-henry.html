Good-King-Henry

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd kɪŋ ˈhɛnri / (AmE), / ɡʊd kɪŋ ˈhɛnri / (BrE)
A European plant (Chenopodium bonus-henricus) with edible leaves, often grown as a potherb and sometimes found naturalized in North America.
a European edible-leaved plant

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate Good-King-Henry for its nutritious leaves and shoots.
In the spring, wild Good-King-Henry can sometimes be found growing along country roadsides.
Historically, Good-King-Henry was a popular potherb in rural European diets.

Etymology

Named after King Henry IV of France, reputedly a benefactor of the poor, though the exact origin is unclear.
