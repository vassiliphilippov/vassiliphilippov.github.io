spider monkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdər ˌmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə ˌmʌŋki / (BrE)
an arboreal monkey of Central and South America, with long slender limbs and a long prehensile tail used for grasping and swinging through trees.
arboreal monkey of Central/South America

Examples

The spider monkey uses its long tail to swing gracefully between tree branches.
During our trip to the rainforest, we spotted a family of spider monkeys feeding on fruit.
Unlike other primates, the spider monkey can hang by its tail while eating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howler monkey - a large New World monkey known for its loud howls
