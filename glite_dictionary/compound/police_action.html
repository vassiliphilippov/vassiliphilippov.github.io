police action

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a limited military operation, carried out without a formal declaration of war, intended to address violations of international peace and order
limited military operation without declaration of war

Examples

The government justified its intervention as a police action to restore peace in the region.
Many historians debate whether the conflict should be called a war or a police action.
United Nations forces were sent in during the police action to enforce international law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: war - armed conflict between countries or groups
