American hackberry

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈhækˌbɛri / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈhækbəri / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree native to the southern United States, known for its small, deep purple berries and use as a shade tree
large North American deciduous shade tree

Examples

The American hackberry is often planted along city streets because it is highly tolerant of pollution.
Birds are attracted to the American hackberry because of its abundance of small purple berries.
The leaves of the American hackberry turn yellow in the fall, adding color to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackberry - a tree of the genus Celtis, known for its berries and rough bark
