bald cypress

1. NOUN

/ bɔld ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / bɔːld ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
A large deciduous coniferous tree of the southeastern United States, growing in swamps and wetlands, noted for its flared trunk base and durable wood.
tree of southeastern U.S. swamps

Examples

The swamp was filled with ancient bald cypress trees rising from the still water.
Furniture made from bald cypress is valued for its resistance to decay.
We paddled our canoe between the wide trunks of bald cypress in the bayou.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - an evergreen coniferous tree with scale-like leaves

3. NOUN

/ bɔld ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / bɔːld ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
A large deciduous conifer tree of the genus Taxodium, native to wetlands in the southeastern United States.
deciduous conifer tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The wetland was filled with ancient bald cypress trees draped in Spanish moss.
A mature bald cypress can live for hundreds of years near swamps or rivers.
Many photographers are drawn to the striking silhouette of the bald cypress in the misty bayou.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any coniferous tree of the family Cupressaceae

Etymology

From 'bald' (lacking leaves) and 'cypress', after the habit of shedding leaves in winter.

The wood of a deciduous coniferous tree of the genus Taxodium, especially native to North America, valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
durable wood from Taxodium trees

Examples
