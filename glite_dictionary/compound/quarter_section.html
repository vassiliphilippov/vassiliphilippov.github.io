quarter section

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtər ˌsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə ˌsɛkʃən / (BrE)
A unit of land area equal to one-quarter of a section, typically 160 acres, measuring half a mile on each side.
land unit of 160 acres

Examples

The farm covers one quarter section west of the main road.
He inherited a quarter section from his grandfather in Nebraska.
Each quarter section was marked by stone cairns at its boundaries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: section - a land area of 640 acres in the Public Land Survey System

Etymology

Formed from 'quarter' (one-fourth) and 'section,' from the Public Land Survey System (U.S.).
