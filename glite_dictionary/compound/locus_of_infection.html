locus of infection

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəs əv ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəs əv ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the specific site in the body where an infection begins or is primarily located
site in body where infection starts
medical

Examples

The doctors worked to identify the locus of infection before recommending a treatment plan.
In this patient, the lungs were found to be the locus of infection.
Antibiotics are chosen based on the likely locus of infection and the causative organism.

Etymology

From Latin 'locus' meaning 'place', used in medical terminology for the site of a process.
