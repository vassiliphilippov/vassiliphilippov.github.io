winding-clothes

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪndɪŋ ˌkloʊðz / (AmE), / ˈwaɪndɪŋ ˌkləʊðz / (BrE)
A cloth or garment used to wrap a corpse for burial.
burial cloth for wrapping a corpse
archaic

Examples

The body was wrapped in winding-clothes before being placed in the tomb.
In old novels, burials often mention winding-clothes as part of the ceremony.
Fragments of ancient winding-clothes were discovered in the crypt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shroud - a cloth used to wrap a dead body for burial

Etymology

From 'winding' (the act of wrapping) and 'clothes.'
