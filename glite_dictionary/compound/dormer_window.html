dormer window

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrmɚ ˌwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmə ˌwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A window that is set vertically in a projection built out from a sloping roof.
window projecting from a sloping roof

Examples

The attic was brightly lit, thanks to a large dormer window overlooking the garden.
From the street, the dormer window gave the old cottage its distinctive charm.
We installed a dormer window to create more space and light in the upstairs bedroom.

Etymology

From Middle French *dormeor* (sleeping room), referring to rooms with such windows.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrmər ˌwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmə ˌwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
a window that projects vertically from a sloping roof, usually set in a small gabled structure
vertical window projecting from a sloping roof

Examples

The attic was brightened by a large dormer window overlooking the garden.
They installed a dormer window to create more headroom in the bedroom.
Through the dormer window, she could see the rooftops of neighboring houses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormer - a roofed structure, often containing a window, that projects vertically beyond the plane of a pitched roof

Etymology

From Middle French dormeor (sleeping room), referring to rooms with such windows.
