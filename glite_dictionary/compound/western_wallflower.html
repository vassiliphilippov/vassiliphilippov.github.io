western wallflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈwɔlˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈwɔːlflaʊə / (BrE)
A biennial or short-lived perennial plant of western North America, with orange-yellow flowers, sometimes placed in the genus Cheiranthus.
prairie plant with orange-yellow flowers (North America)

Examples

We found a cluster of western wallflowers growing along the edge of the prairie trail.
The bright orange blooms of the western wallflower are a common sight in the spring.
Botanists sometimes classify the western wallflower in the genus Cheiranthus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - a plant of the genus Erysimum with fragrant yellow or orange flowers
