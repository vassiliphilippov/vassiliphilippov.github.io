deer fly fever

1. NOUN

/ dɪr flaɪ ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / dɪə flaɪ ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a highly infectious disease of rodents, especially rabbits and squirrels, that can be transmitted to humans by ticks, flies, or direct contact with infected animals
infectious rodent disease transmissible to humans
medical

Examples

Hunters are sometimes exposed to deer fly fever when handling wild rabbits in endemic areas.
Symptoms of deer fly fever in humans may include fever, chills, and swollen glands.
Protective clothing can help prevent the transmission of deer fly fever from infected animals or insects.
