adult respiratory distress syndrome

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌlt rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔri dɪˈstrɛs sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / əˈdʌlt rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔːri dɪˈstrɛs sɪnˈdrəʊm / (BrE)
A severe medical condition in which the lungs are inflamed, stiff, and unable to exchange oxygen properly, often caused by injury or exposure to harmful chemicals.
severe lung condition causing breathing difficulty
medical

Examples

The patient developed adult respiratory distress syndrome after inhaling toxic fumes in the factory.
Doctors worked quickly to treat his adult respiratory distress syndrome with mechanical ventilation and oxygen therapy.
Exposure to certain chemical vapors can lead to adult respiratory distress syndrome in otherwise healthy individuals.
