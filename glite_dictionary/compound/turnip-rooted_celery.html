turnip-rooted celery

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.nɪp ˈruː.tɪd ˈsɛl.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪp ˈruː.tɪd ˈsɛl.əri / (BrE)
A type of celery grown for its large, round, edible, aromatic root, also called celeriac.
root vegetable, celeriac

Examples

She added diced turnip-rooted celery to the stew for extra flavor.
Turnip-rooted celery is often used in soups, salads, and purees.
Farmers harvested a large crop of turnip-rooted celery this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celeriac - the same plant, turnip-rooted celery
