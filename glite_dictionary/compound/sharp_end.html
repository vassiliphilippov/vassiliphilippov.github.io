sharp end

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrp ɛnd / (AmE), / ʃɑːp end / (BrE)
the pointed or dangerous part of an object, or the most risky or challenging part of a situation
pointed/dangerous part; most challenging aspect

Examples

Always hold the knife by the handle, not the sharp end.
As a nurse, she often found herself at the sharp end of emergency situations.
Be cautious when handling broken glass, as the sharp end can cause injury.

Synset Examples

He accidentally grabbed the sharp end of the knife.
Be careful with the sharp end of that tool.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blunt end - the rounded or less dangerous part of an object
