estate of the realm

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈsteɪt əv ðə ɹeɪlm / (AmE), / ɪˈsteɪt əv ðə rɛlm / (BrE)
A major social class or order with specific political rights, regarded collectively as part of the body politic, especially in historical contexts such as in the United Kingdom.
major political social class (historical)

Examples

During the Middle Ages, the nobility formed a prominent estate of the realm alongside the clergy and the commoners.
Each estate of the realm historically had specific rights and responsibilities within the government structure.
The concept of estate of the realm is key to understanding the parliamentary system of early modern Europe.
