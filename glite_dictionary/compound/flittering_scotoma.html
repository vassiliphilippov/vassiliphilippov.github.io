flittering scotoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪtərɪŋ skoʊˈtoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈflɪtərɪŋ skəʊˈtəʊmə / (BrE)
A localized area of reduced vision bordered by flickering or shimmering colored lights, often indicating the onset of a migraine.
disturbed vision with shimmering lights (migraine)
medical

Examples

During her migraine attacks, she often experienced a flittering scotoma just before the headache began.
A flittering scotoma may last for several minutes and cause temporary impairment of vision.
Doctors asked if he saw a flittering scotoma before his other migraine symptoms appeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scintillating scotoma - a visual aura with sparkling or flashing lights, often preceding migraine

Etymology

From 'flitter' (to move lightly or rapidly) and Greek skotoma (darkness, dimness of vision).
