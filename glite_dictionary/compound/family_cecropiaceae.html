family Cecropiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli ˌsiːkrəpaɪˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌsiːkrəʊpaɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
A small family of tropical flowering plants, sometimes classified within the family Moraceae, comprising mainly the genus Cecropia.
tropical plant family; sometimes part of Moraceae
medical

Examples

Several species within the family Cecropiaceae are known for their rapid growth in disturbed tropical forests.
The family Cecropiaceae has sometimes been grouped with Moraceae due to morphological similarities.
Researchers study the ecological role of family Cecropiaceae in supporting various animal species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: family Moraceae - The mulberry or fig family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin *Cecropia*, the type genus, named after Cecrops, a mythical king of Athens, plus the botanical suffix *-aceae* (family).
