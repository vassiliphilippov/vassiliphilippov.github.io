fire marshal

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
an official in charge of a fire department, or responsible for investigating fires and enforcing fire safety regulations
head or official of a fire department

Examples

The fire marshal inspected the building for safety compliance after the renovations were completed.
During the ceremony, the city's fire marshal was awarded for his years of dedicated service.
The fire marshal determined that the blaze had been caused by faulty wiring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire chief - the person in command of a fire department

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
an official responsible for preventing fires, investigating their causes, and enforcing fire safety regulations
official in charge of fire prevention and investigation

Examples

The fire marshal inspected the building to ensure all safety codes were met.
If you have questions about fire drills, ask the fire marshal for guidance.
After the warehouse fire, the fire marshal determined that faulty wiring was the cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire inspector - an official who inspects buildings for fire hazards

Etymology

From fire + marshal, with 'marshal' from Old French 'mareschal' (stable keeper, officer).
