Jordan curve

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔrdn ˌkɝv / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdn ˌkɜːv / (BrE)
a simple closed curve in a plane that does not cross itself
simple closed non-intersecting curve

Examples

In topology, a Jordan curve divides the plane into an inside and an outside region.
Every simple closed polygonal path is an example of a Jordan curve.
The professor illustrated the concept of a Jordan curve on the whiteboard.

Etymology

Named after French mathematician Camille Jordan (1838–1922) who studied its properties.
