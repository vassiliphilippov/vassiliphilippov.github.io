First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Thessalonians

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɜrst ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ðə ˌθɛsəˈloʊniənz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ɪˈpɪsl əv pɔːl ði əˈpɒsl tuː ðə ˌθesəˈləʊniənz / (BrE)
the first letter written by Saint Paul to the Christians in Thessalonica, included as a book of the New Testament
a New Testament book; Paul's first letter to the Thessalonians

Examples

Many scholars study the First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Thessalonians to understand early Christian communities.
During Sunday school, we read passages from the First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Thessalonians.
The First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Thessalonians is often cited in discussions about Christian hope and perseverance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Second Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Thessalonians - Paul's second letter to the Thessalonians in the New Testament

Etymology

Epistle from Greek 'epistolē' (letter); Thessalonians refers to people of Thessalonica, an ancient Greek city.
