femoral pulse

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛmərəl pʌls / (AmE), / ˈfɛmərəl pʌls / (BrE)
the pulse that can be felt over the femoral artery in the groin region
pulse felt in the groin (femoral artery)
medical

Examples

The doctor checked the femoral pulse to assess blood flow to the leg.
A weak or absent femoral pulse can indicate a blockage in the artery.
Palpating the femoral pulse is a routine part of a vascular examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radial pulse - pulse felt at the wrist (radial artery)
  • Synonym: carotid pulse - pulse felt on the neck (carotid artery)

Etymology

From Latin *femoralis* (of the thigh), referring to the femur bone.
