superior labial artery

1. NOUN

/ suˈpɪriɚ ˈleɪbiəl ˈɑːrˌtəri / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˈleɪbɪəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery branching from the facial artery that supplies blood to the upper lip
artery supplying the upper lip
medical

Examples

The superior labial artery can be seen during certain maxillofacial surgeries.
Damage to the superior labial artery may cause significant bleeding in the upper lip region.
The superior labial artery supplies oxygenated blood to the tissues of the upper lip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inferior labial artery - artery supplying the lower lip

Etymology

From Latin 'superior' (higher), 'labium' (lip), and 'arteria' (artery).
