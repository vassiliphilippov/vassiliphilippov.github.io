to need a break

1. VERB

/ nid ə breɪk / (AmE), / niːd ə breɪk / (BrE)
to require time off or rest, typically due to tiredness or stress
require time off or rest

Examples

After working all morning, I really need a break to recharge.
She told her boss that she needed a break from the constant pressure.
Sometimes you just need a break to clear your mind and relax.

Synset Examples

I just need a break, you knowwhaddamean?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a break - to pause from activity for rest
