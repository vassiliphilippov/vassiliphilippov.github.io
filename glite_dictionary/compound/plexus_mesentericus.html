plexus mesentericus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs mɛzənˈtɛrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs mɛzɛnˈtɛrɪkəs / (BrE)
a network of autonomic nerves in the mesentery that supplies the intestines
autonomic nerve network in the mesentery
medical

Examples

The plexus mesentericus plays a critical role in regulating intestinal function.
During surgery, damage to the plexus mesentericus can result in significant complications.
Researchers studied the plexus mesentericus to better understand nerve supply to the gut.

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network, braid) and 'mesentericus' (of the mesentery).
