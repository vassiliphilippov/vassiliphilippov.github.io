Boott's goldenrod

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊts ˈgoʊldənˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈbʊts ˈgəʊldənˌrɒd / (BrE)
A variety of goldenrod (Solidago arguta var. bootii) native to North America, characterized by its yellow flower clusters.
a North American variety of goldenrod

Examples

Boott's goldenrod is found in open woodlands and along forest edges in the eastern United States.
Botanists studied the blooming pattern of Boott's goldenrod compared to other goldenrod varieties.
The conservation team monitored several patches of Boott's goldenrod in the wildlife reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - any plant of the genus Solidago, known for its yellow flowers

Etymology

Named after Francis Boott, British-American physician and botanist, combined with 'goldenrod' for the plant's common name.
