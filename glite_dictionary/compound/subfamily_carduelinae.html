subfamily Carduelinae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæməli kɑrˌduːəˈlaɪni / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæməli kɑːdjuːəˈliːniː / (BrE)
A taxonomic subgroup within the finch family, comprising various seed-eating birds such as goldfinches and siskins.
a subgroup of finches (birds)

Examples

The subfamily Carduelinae includes well-known birds such as greenfinches and goldfinches.
Ornithologists often debate the precise species that belong to the subfamily Carduelinae.
Many members of the subfamily Carduelinae are recognizable by their strong, conical beaks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: finch - a small seed-eating songbird of the family Fringillidae

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Carduelinae' from Latin 'carduelis' (goldfinch), with taxonomic suffix -inae for subfamilies.
