West Indian snowberry

1. NOUN

/ wɛst ˈɪndiən ˈsnoʊˌbɛri / (AmE), / wɛst ˈɪndɪən ˈsnəʊˌbɛri / (BrE)
an evergreen climbing shrub native to southern Florida and the West Indies, valued for its clusters of fragrant white or creamy flowers and round, white, succulent berries
a climbing shrub with white berries

Examples

The West Indian snowberry thrives in warm, humid climates and is often seen climbing along garden fences.
Gardeners appreciate the fragrant blossoms and decorative berries of the West Indian snowberry.
Birds are known to feast on the juicy fruits of the West Indian snowberry during the late summer months.
