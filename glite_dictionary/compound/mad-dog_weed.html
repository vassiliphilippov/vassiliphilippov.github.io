mad-dog weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæd dɔg wid / (AmE), / ˈmad dɒg wiːd / (BrE)
an American mint plant that produces a resinous substance, formerly used as an antispasmodic
American mint plant; medicinal use
medical american

Examples

Early settlers used mad-dog weed as a remedy for muscle spasms.
The leaves of mad-dog weed contain a resin that was valued in herbal medicine.
Botanists studied mad-dog weed to understand its historical medical significance.
