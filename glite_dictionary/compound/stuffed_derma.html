stuffed derma

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌft ˈdɝːmə / (AmE), / ˈstʌft ˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
A traditional Jewish dish made of roasted fowl intestines filled with a seasoned mixture of matzo meal and suet, typically served as part of festive meals.
Jewish dish of stuffed fowl intestines

Examples

At the Seder dinner, my grandmother always prepares her famous stuffed derma.
The traditional recipe for stuffed derma calls for a filling of matzo meal and chopped onions.
He enjoyed the hearty flavor of stuffed derma during the family gathering.

Etymology

From Yiddish 'derma' meaning 'intestine'; ultimately from German 'Darm' (intestine).
