webworm moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛbˌwɝm mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈwɛbˌwɜːm mɒθ / (BrE)
A moth of several species whose larvae spin a web and live within it, often feeding on leaves inside the web.
moth whose larvae spin and live in webs

Examples

The leaves of the trees were covered with webs made by webworm moths in late summer.
The webworm moth is known for the distinctive webbing its caterpillars create on branches.
Farmers often battle infestations of webworm moths to protect their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, typically nocturnal
