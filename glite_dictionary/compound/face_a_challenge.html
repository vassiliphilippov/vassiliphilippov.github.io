to face a challenge

1. VERB

/ feɪs ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / feɪs ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
to deal with or confront a difficult situation or obstacle
confront a difficulty

Examples

Whenever we face a challenge at work, our team pulls together to find a solution.
As a young athlete, she had to face many challenges before reaching the top.
It's important to remain optimistic when you face a challenge in life.

Synset Examples

The settler faced many challenges in the wilderness.
In the story, Job faced many trials yet remained steadfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - succeed in dealing with (a problem or difficulty)
  • Antonym: to avoid - keep away from or not confront a challenge
