rainbow trout

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌboʊ traʊt / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbəʊ traʊt / (BrE)
A species of trout with a reddish stripe along its sides, native to the rivers and coastal waters of western North America and often used in sport fishing and aquaculture.
a species of trout with a reddish stripe

Examples

The river is well known for its population of rainbow trout.
She caught a large rainbow trout during their fishing trip in Alaska.
Many chefs prize rainbow trout for its delicate flavor and firm texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common freshwater fish of the salmon family

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌboʊ traʊt / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbəʊ traʊt / (BrE)
the flesh of a Pacific trout (Oncorhynchus mykiss) that migrates between salt and fresh water, used as food
flesh of a migratory Pacific trout used as food

Examples

Grilled rainbow trout is a favorite dish at many seafood restaurants.
She bought fresh rainbow trout from the fish market for dinner.
The chef recommended seasoning the rainbow trout with lemon and herbs before baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common name for several species of freshwater fish
  • Synonym: salmon - anadromous fish related to trout, valued for its flesh

Etymology

Named for its rainbow-colored markings; 'trout' likely from Old English *truht* and Middle English *troute*.
