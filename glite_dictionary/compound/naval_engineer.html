naval engineer

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl ɛn.dʒəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a naval officer responsible for the operation and maintenance of a ship's engines and related mechanical systems
naval officer in charge of ship's engines

Examples

The naval engineer oversaw repairs to the ship's main engine after the storm.
Before departure, the naval engineer conducted a thorough inspection of all machinery.
She trained as a naval engineer and now serves on an aircraft carrier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine engineer - an engineer who designs and maintains mechanical systems of ships

Etymology

From 'naval' (relating to ships/navy) and 'engineer' (from Latin 'ingeniator', one who contrives or devises).
