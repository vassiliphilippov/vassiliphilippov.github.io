order Anostraca

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər æˈnɒstrəkə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə æˈnɒstrəkə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of small aquatic crustaceans, including fairy shrimps and brine shrimps, characterized by the absence of a carapace.
taxonomic order of fairy and brine shrimps

Examples

The order Anostraca includes species commonly known as fairy shrimps, found in freshwater ponds.
Research on the order Anostraca has provided valuable insight into the adaptation of crustaceans to extreme environments.
Brine shrimps are a notable member of the order Anostraca, often used as food in aquaculture.

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'ostrakon' (shell), referring to their lack of a carapace.
