temporal order

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
the arrangement or sequence of events according to when they occur in time
sequence of events in time

Examples

The story was told in temporal order, making it easy to follow the events as they happened.
Understanding the temporal order of experiments is crucial in scientific research.
Confusion arose because the events were not presented in their correct temporal order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronology - the arrangement of events or dates in the order of their occurrence
  • Antonym: random order - an arrangement with no particular sequence or pattern

Etymology

From Latin tempus (time) and Latin ordo (order, arrangement).
