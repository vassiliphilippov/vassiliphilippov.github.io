common valerian

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən vəˈlɛriən / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən vəˈlɛːrɪən / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant with fragrant flowers, whose rhizomes are used in traditional medicine.
a medicinal perennial plant

Examples

Many herbalists suggest using common valerian as a natural sleep aid.
The garden was full of common valerian, attracting butterflies with its fragrant blooms.
He bought dried common valerian roots to prepare a calming tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valerian - plant genus including common valerian

Etymology

From Latin 'valeriana', the name of the plant genus.
