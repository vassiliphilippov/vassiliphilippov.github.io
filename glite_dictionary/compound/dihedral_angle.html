dihedral angle

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈhiː.dɹəl ˈæŋ.ɡəl / (AmE), / daɪˈhiː.dɹəl ˈæŋ.ɡəl / (BrE)
the angle formed between two intersecting planes
angle between two planes

Examples

In chemistry, the dihedral angle between two bonds affects the molecule's properties.
The dihedral angle of an airplane wing helps determine its stability in flight.
Geometric problems often require calculation of the dihedral angle between two surfaces.

Synset Examples

Plane A which contains an isosceles right triangle forms a dihedral angle of 60 degrees with another plane B.

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and 'hedra' (face or seat); referring to the angle between two plane faces.
