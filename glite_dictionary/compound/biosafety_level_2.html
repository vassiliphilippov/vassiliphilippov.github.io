biosafety level 2

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl tu / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl tuː / (BrE)
A set of laboratory practices and containment measures required when working with agents that pose moderate hazards to humans and the environment, where such agents can cause disease but are not readily transmissible.
moderate lab safety level for hazardous agents
medical

Examples

The laboratory is classified as biosafety level 2 because it handles potentially hazardous bacteria.
All researchers working in biosafety level 2 facilities must wear protective clothing and follow strict protocols.
The virus required biosafety level 2 precautions due to its moderate risk to human health.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: biosafety level 1 - lowest level of lab safety; minimal risk to humans
  • Degree Higher: biosafety level 3 - higher level of lab safety for more dangerous agents
