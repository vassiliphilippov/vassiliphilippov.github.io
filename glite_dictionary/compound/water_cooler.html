water cooler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌkuːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌkuːlə / (BrE)
a device that cools and dispenses drinking water, especially in offices or public spaces
device for dispensing cooled drinking water

Examples

The new office has a water cooler in the break room for employees.
I filled my bottle at the water cooler before heading into the meeting.
People often gather around the water cooler to have informal conversations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drinking fountain - a public apparatus supplying drinking water, typically in a stream from a spout

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌkulɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌkuːlə / (BrE)
a place in an office where employees gather informally to talk, often used to refer to casual conversation and gossip in the workplace
place for workplace chatter

Examples

The manager knew most rumors started at the water cooler rather than in official meetings.
Many employees enjoy catching up with each other at the water cooler during breaks.
Discussions at the water cooler can sometimes lead to new ideas and collaborations.

Synset Examples

The latest office gossip spread quickly by the water cooler.
She often found herself chatting with colleagues by the water cooler.
We gathered around the water cooler to discuss weekend plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break room - a room where employees can relax and have informal conversations
