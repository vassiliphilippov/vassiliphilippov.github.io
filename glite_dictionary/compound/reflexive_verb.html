reflexive verb

1. NOUN

/ rəˈflɛksɪv vɝb / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
A verb whose subject and object refer to the same person or thing, indicating the agent performs the action on itself.
grammatical term; verb acting on itself

Examples

In the sentence 'She dressed herself', 'dressed' is a reflexive verb.
In some languages, every reflexive verb uses a special pronoun.
'He prides himself on his work'—here 'prides' is used as a reflexive verb.

Synset Examples

the sentence ‘he washed’ has a reflexive verb
‘perjure’ is a reflexive verb because you cannot perjure anyone but yourself

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transitive verb - a verb that requires a direct object
  • Antonym: intransitive verb - a verb that does not require a direct object

Etymology

From Latin reflexivus (bending back) + verb.

2. NOUN

/ rəˈflɛksɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
a verb whose subject and object refer to the same person or thing, typically used with a reflexive pronoun
verb where subject and object are the same

Examples

Wash is a reflexive verb in the sentence 'She washed herself.'
In Spanish, many actions are expressed using a reflexive verb.
He explained that 'enjoy oneself' is considered a reflexive verb in English grammar.

Synset Examples

In grammar, a reflexive verb refers to the subject itself.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transitive verb - a verb that requires a direct object
  • Antonym: intransitive verb - a verb that does not take a direct object

Etymology

From Latin 'reflexivus' (bending back), referring to reflection.
