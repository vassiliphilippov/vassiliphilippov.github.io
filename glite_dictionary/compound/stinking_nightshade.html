stinking nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous plant of the Old World with a strong unpleasant smell, sticky hairy leaves, and yellow-brown flowers, known for producing hyoscyamine and scopolamine
a poisonous smelly herbaceous plant

Examples

The herbalist warned us that stinking nightshade is highly toxic and should not be touched.
During the hike, we spotted stinking nightshade growing near the old stone wall.
Ancient physicians sometimes used extracts from stinking nightshade for medicinal purposes, despite its dangers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: henbane - a synonym; another common name for stinking nightshade
