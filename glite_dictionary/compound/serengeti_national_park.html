Serengeti National Park

1. PROPN

/ sɛrənˈɡɛti ˈnæʃənəl ˈpɑrk / (AmE), / sɛrənˈɡɛti ˈnaʃənəl ˈpɑːk / (BrE)
a large national park in northern Tanzania, famous for its vast savanna, rich wildlife, and annual animal migrations
national park in northern Tanzania

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit the Serengeti National Park every year to witness the great wildebeest migration.
Lions, elephants, and zebras are just a few of the animals that inhabit Serengeti National Park.
Conservationists consider Serengeti National Park one of the most important wildlife reserves in Africa.

Etymology

From Serengeti, a Maasai word meaning 'endless plains', and national park.
