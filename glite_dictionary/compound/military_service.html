military service

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪləˌtɛri ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
A system of land tenure in which a person held land in exchange for serving in the lord's army, especially in feudal society.
land tenure by serving in a lord's army

Examples

Under the feudal system, knights were often granted land in return for military service.
The obligation of military service shaped both the army and the social hierarchy in medieval England.
Failure to provide military service could result in the loss of one’s land during the Middle Ages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˈsɜːrvɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
a branch of a nation's armed forces, such as the army, navy, or air force
a branch of the armed forces

Examples

He served in the military service for ten years before retiring.
The country has three main branches of military service: the army, navy, and air force.
Enlistment in a military service is often a requirement for national defense.

Related Terms
