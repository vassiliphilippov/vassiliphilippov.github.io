Australian crawl

1. NOUN

/ əˌstreɪljən ˈkrɔl / (AmE), / əˌstreɪliən ˈkrɔːl / (BrE)
A swimming stroke in which the arms move alternately overhead and the legs perform a flutter kick, commonly used in freestyle swimming.
swimming stroke with alternating arms and flutter kick

Examples

He won the race by mastering the Australian crawl technique.
Many swimmers prefer the Australian crawl because it is fast and efficient.
During practice, she focused on improving her Australian crawl stroke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freestyle - a swimming race or technique where any stroke may be used, typically the Australian crawl

Etymology

Named after Australian swimmer Richard Cavill, who popularized the stroke in the early 20th century.
