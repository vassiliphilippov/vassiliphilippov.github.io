percoid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.kɔɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.kɔɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
any of numerous spiny-finned fishes of the order Perciformes, such as perches, basses, and related species
a fish of the order Perciformes

Examples

The scientist studied several species of percoid fish found in the river.
Many percoid fish are popular among anglers for their fighting abilities and taste.
Some percoid fish can adapt to a wide range of freshwater and marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perciform - belonging to the order Perciformes

Etymology

From New Latin Percidae (the perch family) and Greek eidos (form, shape).
