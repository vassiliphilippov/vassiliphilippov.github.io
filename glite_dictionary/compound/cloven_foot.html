cloven foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊ.vən fʊt / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.vən fʊt / (BrE)
a split hoof, popularly believed to be a sign of the Devil or Satan
mark or hoof of Satan in legend

Examples

Some old paintings depict the Devil with a cloven foot to emphasize his evil nature.
Rumors spread through the village that the mysterious visitor had a cloven foot hidden under his cloak.
In medieval folklore, a cloven foot was often seen as undeniable proof of someone being in league with the Devil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoof - the hard foot of certain animals, especially horses, cows, and goats

Etymology

From Old English *clofon* (split) + *foot*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊvən fʊt / (AmE), / ˈkləʊvən fʊt / (BrE)
A hoof that is split into two distinct parts at its tip, as found in ruminants and pigs.
hoof split into two at the end

Examples

Many farm animals, such as goats and sheep, have a cloven foot that helps them walk on rocky terrain.
A pig's distinctive cloven foot makes tracks that can be easily recognized in soft soil.
According to some folklore, the devil is said to have a cloven foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoof - hard foot covering in animals such as horses and cattle

Etymology

From Old English 'clofon' (split, cleft) and 'foot.'
