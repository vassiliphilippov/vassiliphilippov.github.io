polyvinyl acetate

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈvaɪnəl ˈæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈvaɪnɪl ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a synthetic resin produced by the polymerization of vinyl acetate, commonly used as an ingredient in paints, adhesives, and other products
synthetic resin used in paints and adhesives

Examples

The glue is made from polyvinyl acetate, making it safe and non-toxic for children.
Many artists prefer paints that use polyvinyl acetate as a binder because of its durability.
Carpenters often use polyvinyl acetate adhesive for woodworking due to its strong bond.

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many) + 'vinyl acetate' (its chemical monomer).
