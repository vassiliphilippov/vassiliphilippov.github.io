Secretary General

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkrəˌtɛri dʒəˈnɛrəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛkrɪˌteəri dʒəˈnɛrəl / (BrE)
the chief administrative officer of an international organization, especially the United Nations
chief officer of an international organization

Examples

The Secretary General addressed the assembly about the new peace initiative.
Ban Ki-moon served as Secretary General of the United Nations from 2007 to 2016.
The role of the Secretary General involves mediating international disputes and leading discussions.
