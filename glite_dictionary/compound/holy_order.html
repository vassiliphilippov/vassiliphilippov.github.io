holy order

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
(usually plural) The rank or office held by a Christian clergyman in the hierarchy of the church; a sacramental act conferring this status.
rank or office of Christian clergy

Examples

After years of study, he finally entered the holy orders and became a priest.
Debates about the role of women in holy orders have continued for decades.
Receiving holy orders is considered one of the sacraments in many Christian denominations.

Synset Examples

theologians still disagree over whether ‘bishop’ should or should not be a separate Order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordination - the act of conferring holy orders

Etymology

From Latin 'ordo' (order, rank), used in Christian context for clerical office.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
the sacrament by which someone is ordained as a member of the clergy, especially in Christian churches
sacrament of ordination

Examples

After years of study, he finally received holy order and became a priest.
She is considering entering holy order after her university education.
The bishop administered holy order during the special ceremony at the cathedral.

  • Synonym: ordination - the act of conferring holy orders
