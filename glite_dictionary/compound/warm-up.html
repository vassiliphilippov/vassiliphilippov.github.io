warm-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrm ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
A series of mild exercises or activities performed before a more strenuous activity to prepare the body or voice.
preparatory mild exercises or activities

Examples

Be sure to do a warm-up before lifting heavy weights to avoid injury.
The choir began with a short warm-up to relax their vocal cords.
Her warm-up routine takes about fifteen minutes before every swim practice.

Synset Examples

the singers have to warm up
the marathon runner did not warm up and hurt himself

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cool-down - light activity after exercise to help the body recover

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'to warm up', referring to raising temperature or activity level before exertion.
