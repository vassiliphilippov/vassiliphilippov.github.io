dwarf oak

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf oʊk / (AmE), / dwɔːf əʊk / (BrE)
A small, deciduous shrubby oak of northeastern and central United States, known for its sweet edible nuts and dense thickets.
a small shrubby oak tree of North America

Examples

The dense thickets of dwarf oak provide shelter for numerous small animals.
A variety of wildlife feeds on the nuts produced by the dwarf oak.
Hikers admired the hardy dwarf oak growing along the rocky hillsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree or shrub of the genus Quercus, bearing acorns as fruit
