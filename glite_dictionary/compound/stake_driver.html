stake driver

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪk ˌdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪk ˌdraɪvə / (BrE)
A North American bittern (Botaurus lentiginosus), a wading bird of the heron family, known for its booming call and somewhat smaller size than the European bittern.
a North American bittern bird

Examples

We spotted a stake driver hiding among the reeds in the marsh.
The loud, booming call of the stake driver is easy to recognize at dawn or dusk.
Many birdwatchers visit wetlands in hopes of observing the elusive stake driver in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American bittern - a North American bittern (Botaurus lentiginosus)
  • Degree Higher: European bittern - a Eurasian bittern species (Botaurus stellaris)

Etymology

So named from its call, which resembles the sound of a stake being driven into the ground.
