plumb bob

1. NOUN

/ plʌm bɑb / (AmE), / plʌm bɒb / (BrE)
a heavy, pointed weight, usually made of metal, attached to a plumb line to establish a vertical reference line.
metal weight for a plumb line

Examples

The carpenter used a plumb bob to ensure the wall was perfectly vertical.
She dropped the plumb bob from the ceiling to mark a straight line on the floor.
Before starting construction, a plumb bob was essential for making accurate measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumb line - a cord with a plumb bob used to determine verticality

Etymology

From Middle English 'plumb' (lead, a weight) and 'bob' (a short, weighted object).
