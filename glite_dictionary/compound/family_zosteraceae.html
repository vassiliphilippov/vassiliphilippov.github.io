family Zosteraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌzoʊstəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌzɒstəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic flowering plants, also known as eelgrass family, sometimes classified as equivalent to Potamogetonaceae.
a family of aquatic flowering plants

Examples

The family Zosteraceae includes marine eelgrass species found in temperate coastal waters.
Researchers study the distribution of family Zosteraceae to understand changes in marine ecosystems.
Some botanists merge family Zosteraceae with Potamogetonaceae based on genetic similarities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Potamogetonaceae - a family of aquatic flowering plants sometimes considered equivalent to Zosteraceae

Etymology

From genus name Zostera, from Greek zōstēr (belt, girdle), plus family suffix -aceae.
