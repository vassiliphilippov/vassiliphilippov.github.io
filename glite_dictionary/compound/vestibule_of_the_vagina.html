vestibule of the vagina

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstəˌbjuːl əv ðə vəˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪbjuːl əv ðə vəˈdʒaɪnə / (BrE)
the space between the labia minora that contains the opening of the urethra and vaginal orifice
space between labia minora in female anatomy
medical

Examples

A small cyst developed in the vestibule of the vagina, requiring medical attention.
During the examination, the doctor inspected the vestibule of the vagina for signs of infection.
The glands located in the vestibule of the vagina play a crucial role in lubrication.

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance hall) and 'vagina' (sheath, scabbard).
