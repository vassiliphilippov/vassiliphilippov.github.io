family Spirulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli spɪˈruːlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli spɪˈruːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mollusks coextensive with the genus Spirula, sometimes classified within the order Belemnoidea in older taxonomies.
family of mollusks including Spirula

Examples

The family Spirulidae includes only one living genus, Spirula, known for its coiled internal shell.
Some older classifications placed the family Spirulidae within the order Belemnoidea.
The unique shell of the family Spirulidae makes it important for studies on cephalopod evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spirula - a genus of deep-sea mollusks with internal coiled shells

Etymology

Spirulidae from New Latin, based on the genus Spirula + -idae (denoting a family in zoological taxonomy).
