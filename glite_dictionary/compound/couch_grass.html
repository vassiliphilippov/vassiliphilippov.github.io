couch grass

1. NOUN

/ kaʊtʃ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European grass with creeping rhizomes, often considered an invasive weed and naturalized in North America.
European grass with creeping rhizomes, weed

Examples

Couch grass can quickly take over garden beds due to its rapid spreading roots.
Many gardeners struggle to remove couch grass from their lawns as it is very persistent.
The invasive nature of couch grass makes it a challenging weed to control in agriculture.
