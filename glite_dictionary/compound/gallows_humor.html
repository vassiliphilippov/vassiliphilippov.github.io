gallows humor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæloʊz ˈhjuːmər / (AmE), / ˈɡaləʊz ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
humor that treats serious, tragic, or taboo subjects in a light or mocking way, often to cope with distress or adversity
dark humor about serious or tragic topics

Examples

The nurse relied on gallows humor to get through difficult shifts at the hospital.
They exchanged gallows humor while waiting for the verdict, easing their tension.
Some soldiers cope with danger by using gallows humor among themselves.

Synset Examples

His gallows humor helped the group cope with stress.
They shared gallows humor to lighten the grim situation.
She often uses gallows humor in tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark humor - humor that makes light of unpleasant or serious subjects

Etymology

From 'gallows' (a frame for execution by hanging) combined with 'humor', referring to jokes made in the face of death or suffering.
