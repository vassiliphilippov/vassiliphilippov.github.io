creeping St John's wort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːpɪŋ sənt ˈdʒɑnz wɜrt / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ sənt ˈdʒɒnz wɜːt / (BrE)
a creeping evergreen shrub with bright yellow star-shaped summer flowers, often grown as ground cover
creeping evergreen shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The creeping St John's wort quickly spread to cover the bare patch in our garden.
Gardeners often choose creeping St John's wort for its low maintenance and attractive flowers.
In the summer, the creeping St John's wort produces a carpet of bright yellow blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St John's wort - a group of plants in the genus Hypericum, many with yellow flowers
