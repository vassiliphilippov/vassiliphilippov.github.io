Revised Standard Version

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈvaɪzd ˈstændɚd vɚˈʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪzd ˈstændəd vəˈʒɪən / (BrE)
a major English translation of the Bible, first published in the mid-20th century as a revision of the American Standard Version.
English Bible translation (mid-20th century)

Examples

Many Protestant churches use the Revised Standard Version for scripture readings during services.
The Revised Standard Version aimed to provide a clearer and more accurate translation than its predecessors.
She owns several translations of the Bible, including the Revised Standard Version.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: American Standard Version - an earlier English translation of the Bible (1901)
  • Degree Lower: King James Version - the classic English translation of the Bible (1611)
  • Degree Higher: New Revised Standard Version - a later revision of the Revised Standard Version (1989)
