Curie point

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊri pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəri pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the temperature at which a ferromagnetic material loses its ferromagnetism and becomes paramagnetic
temperature where ferromagnetism disappears

Examples

Above the Curie point, the material behaves as a paramagnet rather than a ferromagnet.
Nickel's Curie point is around 358°C, at which it loses its magnetic properties.
When heated past the Curie point, iron stops exhibiting permanent magnetism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Curie temperature - alternative name for the Curie point

Etymology

Named after French physicist Pierre Curie, who discovered the phenomenon.
