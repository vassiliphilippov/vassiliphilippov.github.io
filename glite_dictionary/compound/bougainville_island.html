Bougainville Island

1. PROPN

/ ˌboʊɡənˈvɪl ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / ˌbəʊɡənˈvɪl ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
the largest island of the Solomon Islands archipelago, part of Papua New Guinea
largest island of the Solomon Islands, in Papua New Guinea

Examples

The intense fighting on Bougainville Island during World War II left lasting scars on the region.
After years of unrest, Bougainville Island held a referendum on independence from Papua New Guinea.
The biodiversity of Bougainville Island attracts researchers and nature enthusiasts from around the world.

Etymology

Named after French explorer Louis Antoine de Bougainville.
