Seychelles islands

1. PROPN

/ seɪˈʃɛlz ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / seɪˈʃɛlz ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of about 90 islands in the western Indian Ocean, north of Madagascar, constituting the country of Seychelles
island group and country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

The Seychelles islands are renowned for their pristine beaches and unique wildlife.
Many travelers dream of visiting the Seychelles islands for their natural beauty and tranquility.
The climate of the Seychelles islands is tropical, making it a popular year-round destination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Madagascar - large island nation to the south of the Seychelles islands

Etymology

From French 'Seychelles,' named after Jean Moreau de Séchelles, a French statesman.
