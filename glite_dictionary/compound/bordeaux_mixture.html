Bordeaux mixture

1. NOUN

/ bɔrˈdoʊ ˌmɪkstʃər / (AmE), / bɔːˈdəʊ ˌmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
a mixture of copper sulfate and quicklime used as a fungicide, especially in vineyards and gardens
fungicidal mixture of copper sulfate and quicklime

Examples

Many grape growers still use Bordeaux mixture to protect their vines from fungal diseases.
The gardener applied Bordeaux mixture to the tomato plants as a preventive treatment.
Bordeaux mixture is valued for its effectiveness against mildew and other plant diseases.

Etymology

Named after Bordeaux, a region in France where it was first developed for use in vineyards.
