solid food

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑlɪd fuːd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd fuːd / (BrE)
food that is not liquid, typically chewed and swallowed, as opposed to drink; used as a source of nourishment.
non-liquid food nourishment

Examples

After surgery, he was advised to avoid solid food for a few days.
Babies are usually introduced to solid food at around six months old.
She prefers solid food over soups and smoothies.

Synset Examples

food and drink

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquid food - food in liquid form, such as soup or broth
