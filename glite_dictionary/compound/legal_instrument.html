legal instrument

1. NOUN

/ ˈliɡəl ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
A formal written document that establishes a contractual relationship or grants a legal right.
formal legal document
legal

Examples

The company signed a legal instrument to formalize the agreement.
A property transfer requires a valid legal instrument to be recorded.
Without a legal instrument, the contract has no binding effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deed - a legal document, especially one regarding ownership of property
  • Synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement between parties, often in written form
  • Synonym: will - a legal document expressing wishes about property after death
