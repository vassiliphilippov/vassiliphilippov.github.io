Pearl Harbor

1. PROPN

/ pɝl ˈhɑrbɚ / (AmE), / pɜːl ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
a harbor in Hawaii, to the west of Honolulu, famous as the site of a United States naval base attacked by Japan on December 7, 1941
US naval base in Hawaii attacked in 1941

Examples

Tourists visit Pearl Harbor every year to learn about its role in World War II history.
The surprise attack on Pearl Harbor led the United States to enter World War II.
Many memorials and museums at Pearl Harbor honor those who lost their lives during the attack.
