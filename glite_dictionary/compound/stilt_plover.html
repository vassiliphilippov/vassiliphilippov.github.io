stilt plover

1. NOUN

/ stɪlt ˈploʊvər / (AmE), / stɪlt ˈpləʊvə / (BrE)
a long-legged, three-toed black-and-white wading bird found in inland ponds, marshes, or brackish lagoons
long-legged marsh wading bird

Examples

We spotted a stilt plover feeding quietly along the edge of the pond.
The black-and-white markings of the stilt plover make it easy to identify in the marsh.
During migration season, several stilt plovers can be seen resting in brackish lagoons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilt - a long-legged wading bird of the genus Himantopus
