gaviiform seabird

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈvaɪ.ɪ.fɔrm ˈsiːˌbɝd / (AmE), / ɡəˈvaɪ.ɪ.fɔːm ˈsiːˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A seabird belonging to the order Gaviiformes, which includes loons or divers commonly found in northern regions.
seabird of the order Gaviiformes

Examples

Scientists observed a gaviiform seabird nesting along the remote northern lakeshore.
During migration, several species of gaviiform seabird travel long distances between their breeding and wintering grounds.
The haunting calls of the gaviiform seabird are often heard echoing over northern lakes in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loon - A common name for birds in the family Gaviidae, part of Gaviiformes

Etymology

From modern Latin Gaviiformes, the order name, from Latin gavia (sea mew, gull).
