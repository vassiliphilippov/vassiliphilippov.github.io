untethered from

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛðɚd frəm / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛðəd frəm / (BrE)
Not connected or secured to something; free from attachment or restraint.
not attached or connected to something

Examples

After years of strict routines, she finally felt untethered from her obligations.
The spacecraft drifted untethered from its docking station in orbit.
Many artists create their best work when they feel untethered from expectations.

Synset Examples

The balloon floated away, untethered from its string.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tethered to - secured or fastened to something
  • Synonym: detached from - separated or disconnected from something

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'tether' (to tie or secure) + '-ed' (past participle), with 'from' indicating separation.
