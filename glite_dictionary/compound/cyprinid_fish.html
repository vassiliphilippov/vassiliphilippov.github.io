cyprinid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪprɪnɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪprɪnɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
a soft-finned freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae, typically having toothless jaws and cycloid scales
freshwater fish of the Cyprinidae family

Examples

The carp is a well-known type of cyprinid fish found in many freshwater lakes.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep cyprinid fish for their hardiness and variety.
Researchers studied the mating patterns of a rare cyprinid fish in the river ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish, typically a member of the Cyprinidae family
  • Synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish, a common member of the Cyprinidae family

Etymology

From Greek kyprinos (carp) + -id (belonging to family).
