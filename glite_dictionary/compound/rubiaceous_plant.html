rubiaceous plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːbiˈeɪʃəs ˈplænt / (AmE), / ˌruːbɪˈeɪʃəs ˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
any plant belonging to the botanical family Rubiaceae, which includes trees, shrubs, or vines such as coffee and gardenia
plant of the Rubiaceae family

Examples

The coffee tree is a well-known example of a rubiaceous plant.
Many rubiaceous plants are valued for their ornamental flowers and foliage.
Some species of rubiaceous plant are used in traditional medicine across the tropics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rubiaceae - the botanical family comprising rubiaceous plants

Etymology

From Latin 'Rubia' (madder), the type genus of the family Rubiaceae, plus '-aceous' (relating to).
