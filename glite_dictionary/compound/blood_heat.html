blood heat

1. NOUN

/ blʌd hit / (AmE), / blʌd hiːt / (BrE)
the normal temperature of the human body, around 98.6°F (37°C), often referenced in medical or literary contexts
normal body temperature

Examples

The medicine should be given at blood heat to ensure comfort for the patient.
The water was warmed to blood heat before adding the yeast.
Her skin was cool, not at blood heat as it should have been.
