cellular slime mold

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljələr slaɪm moʊld / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə slaɪm məʊld / (BrE)
a type of slime mold that remains cellular and nucleate throughout its life cycle, differing from true slime molds by not forming a continuous multinucleate mass
a type of slime mold remaining cellular

Examples

Cellular slime mold colonies are often studied to understand how single cells can work together as a multicellular organism.
The unique life cycle of the cellular slime mold has attracted the interest of evolutionary biologists.
Unlike other slime molds, a cellular slime mold retains its individual cell structure even during aggregation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: slime mold - a broad group of fungus-like organisms, including both cellular and acellular types
