family Batidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌbætɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌbatɪˈdeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of plants, coextensive with the genus Batis, known as saltworts and typically found in coastal or saline environments.
a plant family including saltworts (Batis)

Examples

The coastal region is home to several halophytes, including members of the family Batidaceae.
Researchers have studied the unique adaptations of the family Batidaceae to saline environments.
The family Batidaceae includes species that thrive in salt marshes and mangrove ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Batis - a genus of salt-tolerant plants within Batidaceae

Etymology

From New Latin Batidaceae, based on the genus name Batis and the family suffix -aceae.
