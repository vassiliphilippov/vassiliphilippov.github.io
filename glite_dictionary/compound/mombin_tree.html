mombin tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːm.bɪn triː / (AmE), / ˈmɒm.bɪn triː / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub or small tree, especially of the genus Spondias, that produces purplish fruit.
tropical American shrub or small tree

Examples

The mombin tree thrives in the humid climate of Central America.
Local markets often sell the tart fruit of the mombin tree.
Some traditional medicines use extracts from the bark of the mombin tree.

Etymology

From Tupi *mõbi* via Portuguese *mombim*.
