alizarin yellow

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzərɪn ˈjɛloʊ / (AmE), / əˈlɪzərɪn ˈjɛləʊ / (BrE)
any of several yellow dyes used in chemistry and industry; not chemically related to alizarin but named for similar application methods
a class of yellow dyes used in industry

Examples

The textile was dyed a bright shade using alizarin yellow for a vibrant finish.
Some artists prefer alizarin yellow for its lightfast properties in watercolor painting.
Chemists synthesized a new compound similar in application to alizarin yellow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alizarin - a red dye originally extracted from madder root

Etymology

From 'alizarin', a red dye from Arabic *al-ʕaṣarah* (juice), and 'yellow' for its color.
