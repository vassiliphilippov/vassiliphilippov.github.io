menstrual cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnstruəl ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛnstrʊəl ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
the monthly cycle in women and other female mammals during which the lining of the uterus thickens in preparation for possible pregnancy and is shed if pregnancy does not occur
monthly reproductive cycle in women
medical

Examples

The average menstrual cycle lasts about 28 days but can vary widely between individuals.
Healthcare professionals recommend tracking your menstrual cycle for better understanding of reproductive health.
Irregularities in the menstrual cycle can sometimes indicate underlying health conditions.

Synset Examples

the average menstrual cycle is 28 days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - the monthly menstruation in women
  • Degree Lower: ovulation - release of an egg from the ovary during the menstrual cycle

Etymology

From Latin 'menstruus' (monthly) and Greek 'kyklos' (circle, cycle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnstruəl ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛnstrʊəl ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
the regular monthly sequence of hormonal and physiological changes in women that enables reproduction
monthly cycle of female reproductive changes
medical

Examples

Many women keep a diary to track their menstrual cycle throughout the year.
Irregularities in the menstrual cycle can be a sign of health issues.
The menstrual cycle is influenced by various hormones such as estrogen and progesterone.

Synset Examples

She experienced severe pain during her menstrual cycle.
Many women track their menstrual cycles for health reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - the monthly discharge of blood from the uterus

Etymology

From Latin 'menstruus' (monthly, from 'mensis' meaning month) and Greek 'kyklos' (circle, cycle).
