Ubykh language

1. NOUN

/ uˈbɪk læŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / juːˈbɪk læŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Northwest Caucasian language formerly spoken in Turkey by the Ubykh people
extinct Northwest Caucasian language

Examples

Linguists have studied the Ubykh language for its unusually large number of consonants.
After the death of its last native speaker in 1992, the Ubykh language became extinct.
Few written records of the Ubykh language exist today, making its preservation challenging.

Etymology

Named after the Ubykh people of the Caucasus region.
