Book of Esther

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˈɛstə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament that tells the story of Esther, a Jewish woman who became queen of Persia and saved her people from destruction
biblical book about Esther and her people's salvation

Examples

Many scholars study the Book of Esther for its themes of faith and courage.
The festival of Purim commemorates the events recorded in the Book of Esther.
She found inspiration in the story of bravery told in the Book of Esther.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esther - Jewish heroine and queen of Persia in the Book of Esther
