cork tree

1. NOUN

/ kɔrk tri / (AmE), / kɔːk triː / (BrE)
a prickly Australian tree of the coral tree family, known for its soft, spongy wood
prickly Australian tree with spongy wood

Examples

The cork tree is valued for its unusually soft, spongy wood in some traditional crafts.
Botanists studied the cork tree to understand its adaptation to the Australian climate.
A row of cork trees lines the entrance to the nature reserve.

2. NOUN

/ kɔrk tri / (AmE), / kɔːk triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree with thick, corky bark, found in parts of Asia and other regions, sometimes cultivated for its bark or appearance.
tree with thick corky bark

Examples

The cork tree in our garden turns a brilliant yellow each autumn.
Commercial use of the cork tree bark is common in certain Asian countries.
He admired the deeply fissured bark of the old cork tree near the temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phellodendron - genus of cork trees native to eastern Asia
