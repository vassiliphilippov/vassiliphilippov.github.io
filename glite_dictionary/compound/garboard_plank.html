garboard plank

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːrˌbɔrd ˌplæŋk / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbəd ˌplaŋk / (BrE)
the first plank laid next to the keel on either side of a wooden ship's hull
the plank nearest the keel on a wooden ship

Examples

During repairs, the garboard plank had to be completely replaced due to extensive rot.
The shipbuilder carefully fitted the garboard plank alongside the keel.
If the garboard plank is damaged, water may easily seep into the hull.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: keel - the central structural base of a ship or boat

Etymology

From Dutch *garboord* (garboard, bottommost plank) + English *plank*.
