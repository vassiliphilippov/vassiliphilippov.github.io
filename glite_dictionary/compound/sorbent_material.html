sorbent material

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrbənt məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɔːbənt məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a material that takes up another substance by absorption or adsorption
material that absorbs or adsorbs substances

Examples

Activated carbon is a commonly used sorbent material in water filtration systems.
Researchers tested several sorbent materials to remove oil from contaminated soil.
Selecting the right sorbent material can improve the efficiency of chemical spill cleanups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbent - material that takes in substances through absorption
  • Synonym: adsorbent - material that takes in substances through adsorption

Etymology

From the Latin 'sorbere' (to suck in), connected to 'sorbent' meaning 'having the property of absorbing or adsorbing'.
