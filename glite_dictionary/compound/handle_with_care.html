to handle with care

1. VERB

/ ˈhændl wɪð ˈkɛr / (AmE), / ˈhændl wɪð ˈkeə / (BrE)
to treat something or someone gently and cautiously to avoid damage or harm
to treat gently and cautiously

Examples

Fragile packages should always be handled with care to prevent breakage.
You need to handle with care when dealing with sensitive electronic equipment.
She reminded her son to handle with care when feeding the baby rabbits.

Synset Examples

Our groomer always handles the cats with care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat gently - to act toward with gentle or careful behavior
  • Antonym: to mishandle - to treat roughly or improperly
