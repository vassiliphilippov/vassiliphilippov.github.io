Danish krone

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.nɪʃ ˈkroʊ.nə / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.nɪʃ ˈkrəʊ.nə / (BrE)
the official unit of currency in Denmark, equal to 100 øre
currency unit of Denmark

Examples

The price of the ticket is 50 Danish kroner, which is about eight U.S. dollars.
She exchanged her euros for Danish kroner before visiting Copenhagen.
The Danish krone has remained stable against major currencies in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krone - unit of currency in Denmark and Norway

Etymology

From Danish 'krone' meaning 'crown.'
