hairy wood mint

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛri wʊd mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈheəri wʊd mɪnt / (BrE)
a North American perennial herb (Blephilia hirsuta) with square stems and clusters of light blue, pale purple, or white flowers spotted with purple
a North American herb with spotted flowers

Examples

The hairy wood mint thrives in moist woodland environments across eastern North America.
Botanists admire the delicate blooms of hairy wood mint for their subtle purple spots.
Gardeners appreciate hairy wood mint for attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
