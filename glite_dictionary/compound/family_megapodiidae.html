family Megapodiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌmɛɡəˌpoʊdiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌmɛɡəˌpəʊdiˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Australasian birds known as megapodes, characterized by their large feet and unique nesting habits involving mounds of decomposing vegetation.
a family of birds (megapodes)

Examples

The family Megapodiidae includes several species unique to the Australasian region.
Scientists have studied the unusual nesting behavior of birds in the family Megapodiidae.
Members of the family Megapodiidae are known for burying their eggs in warm mounds to incubate them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megapode - a bird of the family Megapodiidae, known for mound-building nesting

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Megapodiidae', from Greek 'mega-' (large) and 'pous' (foot).
