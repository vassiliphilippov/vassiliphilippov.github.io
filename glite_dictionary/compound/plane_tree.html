plane tree

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn triː / (AmE), / pleɪn triː / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of the genus Platanus, with thin, pale, peeling bark, lobed leaves, and spherical fruit clusters
species of Platanus tree with peeling bark

Examples

The plane tree in the square provided welcome shade during the hot summer months.
London is famous for its avenues lined with plane trees that withstand urban pollution well.
Every autumn, the bark of the old plane tree peels off in large, paper-thin patches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycamore - a common name often used for the plane tree in North America

Etymology

From Old French 'plane', from Latin 'platanus', from Greek 'platanos' (plane tree).
