iron putty

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn ˈpʌti / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˈpʌti / (BrE)
a cement-like substance made by mixing ferric oxide and boiled linseed oil, used for its acid-resistant properties
acid-resistant compound of ferric oxide and linseed oil

Examples

The mechanic sealed the crack in the engine block with iron putty for a quick repair.
Because of its resistance to acids, iron putty is often used in chemical processing equipment.
He applied a layer of iron putty to the pipe to prevent further leakage.
