Aesop's fables

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːsɑps ˈfeɪbəlz / (AmE), / ˈiːsɒps ˈfeɪbəlz / (BrE)
a collection of moral stories attributed to the ancient Greek storyteller Aesop, featuring animals and conveying ethical lessons
famous collection of moral stories by Aesop

Examples

Many of Aesop's fables teach moral lessons through simple but memorable animal characters.
As a child, I loved reading Aesop's fables and learning the morals at the end of each story.
Teachers often use Aesop's fables to spark discussions about right and wrong in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fable - a short story conveying a moral lesson, often with animals as characters

Etymology

Named after Aesop, an ancient Greek storyteller believed to have lived in the 6th century BCE.
