out-of-bounds

1. ADJ

/ ˌaʊt əv ˈbaʊndz / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ˈbaʊndz / (BrE)
(in sports or other contexts) outside the designated boundaries or limits, especially where play is not allowed.
outside designated boundaries or limits

Examples

The referee ruled the ball out-of-bounds after it crossed the sideline.
Spectators are not allowed in the out-of-bounds areas of the ski resort.
Parking in an out-of-bounds zone may result in a fine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in-bounds - inside the designated area or limits
  • Synonym: foul - against the rules, especially in sports

2. ADJ

/ ˌaʊt əv ˈbaʊndz / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ˈbaʊndz / (BrE)
Not allowed to be entered or used by a particular group; forbidden or off-limits.
forbidden area or access

Examples

Access to the laboratory is strictly out-of-bounds for students without supervision.
The swimming pool is out-of-bounds after 10 p.m. for safety reasons.
Certain government documents are out-of-bounds to the public for security purposes.

Synset Examples

that area is off-limits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off-limits - not to be entered or used; forbidden

Etymology

From the phrase 'out of bounds', originally from sporting contexts meaning outside the boundaries of play.
