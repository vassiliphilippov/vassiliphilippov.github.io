verde antique

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛər.deɪ ænˈtik / (AmE), / ˌvɛə.deɪ ænˈtiːk / (BrE)
a type of dark green marble with white or light-colored veins, often used in decorative architecture and sculpture
dark green decorative marble

Examples

The floors of the grand foyer were inlaid with verde antique imported from Italy.
We chose verde antique for the fireplace surround to add a touch of elegance to the room.
Sculptors often prefer verde antique for its striking color and fine grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serpentine - a group of greenish minerals often used decoratively, similar in appearance to verde antique

Etymology

From Italian 'verde antico' meaning 'ancient green', referring to its color and classical use.
