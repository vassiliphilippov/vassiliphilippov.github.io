Japanese bittersweet

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs ˈbɪtərˌswit / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈbɪtəˌswiːt / (BrE)
an ornamental climbing vine native to Asia, with orange-yellow fruit and a scarlet covering around the seeds, naturalized in North America
ornamental Asian vine with orange-yellow fruit

Examples

The Japanese bittersweet has become an invasive species in some American forests.
Gardeners prize Japanese bittersweet for its colorful autumn berries.
We found a tangle of Japanese bittersweet growing along the old fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - a related climbing plant with yellowish capsules and orange arils
