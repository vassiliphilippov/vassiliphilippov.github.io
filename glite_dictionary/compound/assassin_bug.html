assassin bug

1. NOUN

/ əˈsæs.ɪn bʌɡ / (AmE), / əˈsæs.ɪn bʌɡ / (BrE)
a predatory true bug, usually long-legged, that feeds on other insects and, in some species, can suck blood from mammals
predatory true bug feeding on insects or mammals

Examples

The assassin bug is known for its sharp beak, which it uses to pierce its prey.
Some species of assassin bug can deliver a painful bite if handled carelessly.
Gardeners often welcome the presence of assassin bugs, as they help control pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduviid bug - another name for an assassin bug, especially in taxonomy

Etymology

Named for their predatory ambush hunting, likened to an assassin's behavior.
