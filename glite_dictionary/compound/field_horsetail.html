field horsetail

1. NOUN

/ ˈfild ˌhɔrˌstɛɪl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌhɔːsteɪl / (BrE)
a perennial plant (Equisetum arvense) common in Eurasia, Greenland, and North America, recognized for its slender, hollow stems and bushy branches, often found in fields and damp ground
a common perennial plant with slender, jointed stems

Examples

We found several field horsetail plants growing along the edge of the meadow.
The spread of field horsetail can be difficult to control in agricultural fields.
In spring, young shoots of field horsetail emerge before the green stems appear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - any plant of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a horse's tail; 'horsetail' from Old English 'hors' (horse) + 'tægel' (tail).
