Southern Ocean

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərn ˈoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈəʊʃən / (BrE)
the ocean surrounding Antarctica, extending from the coast of Antarctica to 60° south latitude, recognized as the fifth and newest world ocean
the ocean around Antarctica

Examples

The Southern Ocean is home to unique marine species that can’t be found anywhere else in the world.
Oceanographers are still learning about the currents and weather patterns of the Southern Ocean.
Tourists sometimes cruise the Southern Ocean to catch a glimpse of Antarctica’s icy landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Antarctica - the continent at the South Pole, encircled by the Southern Ocean

Etymology

Named for its position in the southern hemisphere, surrounding Antarctica.
