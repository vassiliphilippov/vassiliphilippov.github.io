tail end

1. NOUN

/ teɪl ɛnd / (AmE), / teɪl end / (BrE)
the last part or final section of something, especially a period of time or an event
last part or final section

Examples

We only caught the tail end of the movie because we arrived late.
Sales usually slow down at the tail end of the year.
She missed most of the meeting and just joined for the tail end.

Synset Examples

the fag end of this crisis-ridden century
the tail of the storm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beginning - the start or first part of something
  • Synonym: end - the final point or conclusion of something

2. NOUN

/ teɪl ɛnd / (AmE), / teɪl ɛnd / (BrE)
a part or projection that resembles the tail of an animal, especially at the rear of something
rear projection; part resembling a tail

Examples

A comet's tail end is visible even to the naked eye.
The parade continued until only the tail end of it could be seen in the distance.
At the tail end of the aircraft, you can see a distinctive fin-like structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rear - the back part of something
  • Antonym: front - the forward part of something
