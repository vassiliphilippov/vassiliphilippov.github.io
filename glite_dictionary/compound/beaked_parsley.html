beaked parsley

1. NOUN

/ ˈbikt ˈpɑr.sli / (AmE), / ˈbiːkt ˈpɑː.sli / (BrE)
an aromatic annual herb of the Old World, cultivated for its finely divided and often curly leaves, used especially in soups and salads
aromatic herb with curly leaves used in cooking

Examples

Fresh beaked parsley adds a distinctive aroma to many Mediterranean dishes.
The chef sprinkled chopped beaked parsley over the soup before serving.
You can grow beaked parsley in your garden for its flavorful leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - an aromatic herb whose leaves are used as a garnish or seasoning
