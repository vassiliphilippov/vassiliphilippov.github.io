red saunders

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈsɔːndɚz / (AmE), / rɛd ˈsɔːndəz / (BrE)
a tree native to India and Southeast Asia, valued for its hard, fragrant wood used in cabinetwork and its dark red heartwood employed as a dyewood
tree with fragrant timber and red dyewood

Examples

The rare red saunders is increasingly protected due to its valuable wood.
Artisans often seek red saunders for its rich color and fragrance in fine furniture.
Extracts from red saunders have been used as a natural dye for centuries.
