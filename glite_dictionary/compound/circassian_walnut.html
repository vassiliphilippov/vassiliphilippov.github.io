Circassian walnut

1. NOUN

/ sərˈkæsiən ˈwɔlnʌt / (AmE), / səˈkæsiən ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
a Eurasian walnut tree valued for its large edible nut and hard, attractively patterned wood, widely cultivated for both purposes
Eurasian walnut tree with prized nut and wood

Examples

Furniture makers prize Circassian walnut for its beautiful grain and durability.
The large edible nuts of the Circassian walnut are harvested in the autumn.
Many parks in Europe feature Circassian walnut trees lining the walkways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - general term for any tree or nut of the genus Juglans
  • Degree Lower: black walnut - a North American walnut species with dark wood

Etymology

Circassian refers to Circassia, a region in the northwestern Caucasus, indicating the tree's geographic origin.
