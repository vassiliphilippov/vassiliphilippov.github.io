dead-man's-fingers

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛdˌmænzˈfɪŋɡɚz / (AmE), / ˌdɛdˌmænzˈfɪŋɡəz / (BrE)
a fungus of the genus Xylaria, whose dark, finger-like fruiting bodies emerge from decaying wood
fungus with finger-like fruiting bodies (Xylaria)

Examples

We spotted a cluster of dead-man's-fingers growing from a fallen tree in the forest.
The bizarre appearance of dead-man's-fingers makes them easily identifiable among woodland fungi.
A naturalist explained that dead-man's-fingers are actually the fruiting bodies of the fungus Xylaria polymorpha.
