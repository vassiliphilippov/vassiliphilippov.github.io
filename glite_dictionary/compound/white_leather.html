white leather

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈlɛðər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈlɛðə / (BrE)
leather that has been tanned or treated with alum and/or salt, typically resulting in a pale or white appearance
leather treated with alum and salt

Examples

The gloves were made from soft white leather prized for its flexibility.
Restorers used white leather to repair the bindings of the ancient manuscript.
She wore boots crafted from white leather, which complemented her elegant dress.
