crested wheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛstɪd ˈwiːtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛstɪd ˈwiːtɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A Eurasian grass species widely cultivated in North America for forage and erosion control.
grass used for forage and erosion control

Examples

Farmers often seed crested wheatgrass to restore overgrazed rangeland.
Crested wheatgrass quickly establishes itself and helps prevent soil erosion in the Great Plains.
Livestock feed on crested wheatgrass during the early summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheatgrass - any of several grasses of the genus Agropyron or related species
