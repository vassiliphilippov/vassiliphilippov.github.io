police state

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis steɪt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs steɪt / (BrE)
A country in which the government exercises strict and repressive control over the people, typically by using the police or secret police.
oppressive government ruled by police control

Examples

Critics warn that the government’s new surveillance laws risk turning the country into a police state.
Many citizens lived in fear under the police state that restricted freedom of expression.
Writers often use dystopian fiction to explore life in a police state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalitarian state - a government that seeks to control all aspects of life
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlis steɪt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs steɪt / (BrE)
A government that maintains strict control and surveillance over its citizens, often restricting personal freedoms.
government with strict control over citizens

Examples

Many activists warned that the new laws could turn the country into a police state.
Under a police state, dissent is often met with harsh punishment and censorship.
Citizens living in a police state may find their privacy and freedoms severely limited.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that surveillance creates a police state.
Living in a police state limits personal freedoms.
They fled the country to escape a police state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalitarianism - system of government that is centralized and dictatorial
  • Antonym: democracy - system of government by the whole population or eligible members
