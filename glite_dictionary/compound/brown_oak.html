brown oak

1. NOUN

/ braʊn oʊk / (AmE), / braʊn əʊk / (BrE)
a large tree native to Australasia, valued for its timber
large Australasian tree

Examples

The furniture was crafted from the wood of the brown oak, giving it a unique color.
You can spot a brown oak by its distinctive broad leaves in the Australian forest.
Many wildlife species depend on the brown oak for food and shelter.
