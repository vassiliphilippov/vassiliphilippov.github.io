fifth column

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪfθ ˈkɑːləm / (AmE), / ˌfɪfθ ˈkɒləm / (BrE)
A group of people within a country or organization who secretly support its enemies and may engage in espionage or sabotage.
subversive group aiding an enemy

Examples

Rumors spread that a fifth column was operating inside the city, passing information to the enemy.
The government launched an investigation to root out the fifth column threatening national security.
During wartime, fear of a fifth column can lead to stricter security measures and suspicion among citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays their country or cause
  • Antonym: ally - a person or group that cooperates with or helps another; usually an antonym in this context

Etymology

From the Spanish 'quinta columna,' reportedly used during the Spanish Civil War (1936–39) to describe supporters inside enemy-held Madrid.
