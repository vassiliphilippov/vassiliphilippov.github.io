pot marigold

1. NOUN

/ pɑt ˈmærɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / pɒt ˈmærɪˌɡəʊld / (BrE)
a common European annual plant with bright yellow or orange flowers, often grown in gardens; Calendula officinalis
a European annual flowering plant

Examples

The pot marigold is often used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory properties.
Bright orange pot marigold flowers added a cheerful touch to the garden border.
She grows pot marigolds every summer and uses the petals to decorate salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calendula - another name for the pot marigold; Calendula officinalis
  • Degree Higher: marigold - a wider group of flowering plants in the daisy family

Etymology

Named for its historical use in cooking pots; 'marigold' from 'Mary's gold', referencing the Virgin Mary.
