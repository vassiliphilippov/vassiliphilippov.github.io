Menominee whitefish

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˈmini ˈwaɪtfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌmiːnəˈmiːni ˈwaɪtfɪʃ / (BrE)
a species of whitefish with a bronze back found in northern North America and Siberia
a whitefish species with a bronze back

Examples

The Menominee whitefish is often caught in the colder waters of northern lakes.
Biologists study the migration patterns of the Menominee whitefish across North America and Siberia.
Fishermen admire the bronze back of the Menominee whitefish during the annual fishing season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - any of several freshwater fishes with white meat

Etymology

Named after the Menominee people, native to the region around Lake Michigan.
