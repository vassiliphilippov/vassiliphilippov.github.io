lower criticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.ər krɪˈtɪs.ɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə krɪˈtɪs.ɪzəm / (BrE)
The scholarly study and comparison of existing manuscript copies of the Scriptures to establish the original text.
study of biblical manuscripts to establish original text

Examples

Scholars engaging in lower criticism analyze variations in ancient Bible manuscripts.
He published several articles on lower criticism and its impact on modern Bible translations.
The history of lower criticism reflects changing views on the reliability of textual evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: textual criticism - The scholarly study of manuscripts to determine the original wording of a text
  • Antonym: higher criticism - The study of biblical texts with focus on literary, historical, and source analysis

Etymology

From the distinction in biblical scholarship between 'lower criticism' (textual criticism) and 'higher criticism' (literary/historical analysis); 'criticism' from Greek *kritikos* (able to judge).
