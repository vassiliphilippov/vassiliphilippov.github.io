downy birch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊni bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊni bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A European birch tree with dull white to pale brown bark and slightly drooping branches covered in fine hairs.
a European birch tree with hairy branches

Examples

The downy birch is commonly found in wet woodlands and marshy areas of northern Europe.
Unlike the silver birch, the downy birch has hairy shoots and duller bark.
The leaves of the downy birch are softer and less serrated compared to other birch species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: silver birch - a related but distinct birch species (Betula pendula)
