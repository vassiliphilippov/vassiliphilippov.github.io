chronological age

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɑː.nəˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkrɒn.əˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the age of a person or thing measured in complete years and months from birth or creation.
age measured in years and months

Examples

Although his chronological age was 50, he appeared much younger.
School entry is usually determined by a child's chronological age.
A person's chronological age may not match their physical or mental development.

Synset Examples

his chronological age was 71 years

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biological age - a measure of how well or poorly your body is functioning relative to your actual calendar age

Etymology

From Greek 'khronos' (time) + 'logos' (word, account), combined with 'age' from Old French 'aage, eage', based on Latin 'aetas'.
