Baltic state

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔltɪk steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːltɪk steɪt / (BrE)
any of the three countries in Northern Europe on the eastern coast of the Baltic Sea—Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania.
one of Estonia, Latvia, or Lithuania

Examples

Lithuania is one Baltic state that has seen significant economic growth since joining the European Union.
The history of each Baltic state is unique, though they share common cultural ties.
Many tourists visit a Baltic state to experience its medieval cities and beautiful coastlines.

Synset Examples

The Baltic states have rich cultural heritage.
