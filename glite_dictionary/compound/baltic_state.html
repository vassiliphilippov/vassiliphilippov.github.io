Baltic State

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔl.tɪk steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.tɪk steɪt / (BrE)
any of the three European countries bordering the eastern coast of the Baltic Sea: Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania.
one of three countries on the eastern Baltic Sea

Examples

Lithuania is considered one of the Baltic States alongside Latvia and Estonia.
The history of each Baltic State is marked by periods of occupation and eventual independence.
Many tourists are discovering the unique culture of every Baltic State on their travels.

Etymology

From 'Baltic' (referring to the Baltic Sea) and 'state' (country).
