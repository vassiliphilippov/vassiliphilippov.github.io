silver-leaved nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər liːvd ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə liːvd ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
a weedy plant with silvery leaves, violet, blue, or white flowers, and round berries, native to central United States and South America, often used to curdle milk
a weedy plant with silvery leaves and colorful flowers

Examples

The fields were overrun with silver-leaved nightshade after the summer rains.
Many ranchers dislike silver-leaved nightshade because it spreads quickly among crops.
Some traditional cheese makers use berries from silver-leaved nightshade to curdle milk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightshade - a plant of the genus Solanum, often with poisonous parts
