Lake Baikal

1. PROPN

/ leɪk baɪˈkɑl / (AmE), / leɪk baɪˈkɑːl / (BrE)
a large lake in southern Siberia, Russia, known as the world's deepest and oldest freshwater lake
deepest freshwater lake in Siberia, Russia

Examples

Scientists have studied the unique ecosystem of Lake Baikal for decades.
Lake Baikal contains about 20% of the world's unfrozen freshwater supply.
The clear waters of Lake Baikal attract tourists and researchers from around the globe.

Etymology

From Russian 'Байкал' (Baykal), of uncertain origin; possibly from Turkic.
