end organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd ˌɔːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɛnd ˌɔːɡən / (BrE)
a specialized structure at the peripheral end of certain motor or sensory nerve fibers, often serving as a receptor or effector in the nervous system
specialized nerve ending structure
medical

Examples

Damage to the end organ can result in loss of sensation or muscle function.
Each tactile receptor in the skin is an end organ that responds to pressure.
The neurologist explained that the disease affected the end organs at the nerve terminals.
