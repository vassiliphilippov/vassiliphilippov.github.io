hydrogen peroxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrədʒən pəˈrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrɪdʒən pəˈrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, viscous liquid with strong oxidizing properties, used as a bleaching agent, disinfectant, and in high concentrations as a rocket fuel oxidizer
oxidizing liquid used as bleach and disinfectant
medical

Examples

The dentist used hydrogen peroxide to clean the wound before the procedure.
She poured a small amount of hydrogen peroxide onto the stain to bleach it.
Rocket engineers use highly concentrated hydrogen peroxide as an oxidizer in propulsion systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a substance used to whiten or disinfect

Etymology

Formed from 'hydrogen' and 'peroxide' (from French peroxyde).
