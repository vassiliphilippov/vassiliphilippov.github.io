Wilton carpet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪltən ˈkɑːrpɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪltən ˈkɑːpɪt / (BrE)
A type of woven carpet made on a Jacquard loom, with loops that are cut to create a dense, velvety pile.
type of velvety woven carpet

Examples

The living room was fitted with a luxurious Wilton carpet in a deep blue shade.
You can recognize a Wilton carpet by its close, velvety pile and distinct weaving method.
Hotels often choose Wilton carpets for their durability and elegant appearance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Brussels carpet - a similar carpet with uncut loops

Etymology

Named after Wilton, a town in Wiltshire, England, where this style of carpet was first made.
