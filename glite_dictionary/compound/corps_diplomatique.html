corps diplomatique

1. NOUN

/ kɔr ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (AmE), / kɔː ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (BrE)
The collective body of foreign diplomats accredited to a particular country or government.
body of foreign diplomats

Examples

The annual reception for the corps diplomatique was attended by ambassadors from more than fifty countries.
Members of the corps diplomatique enjoy certain privileges under international law.
A new ambassador was welcomed into the corps diplomatique at a formal ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diplomatic service - government department managing international relations and diplomats

Etymology

From French, meaning 'diplomatic corps'.
