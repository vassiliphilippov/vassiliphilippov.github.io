family Gasterophilidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌɡæstərəˈfɪlᵻˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˌɡæstərəˈfɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of botflies whose larvae parasitize horses and other animals; known as horse botflies.
taxonomic family of horse botflies

Examples

Veterinarians study the family Gasterophilidae to better understand horse health issues caused by these botflies.
Infestation by the family Gasterophilidae can lead to digestive problems in horses.
Research on the family Gasterophilidae has advanced our knowledge of parasitic insect life cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - fly whose larvae are internal parasites of animals

Etymology

From New Latin: Gasterophilidae, based on Gasterophilus (type genus) from Greek gastēr (stomach) + philos (loving).
