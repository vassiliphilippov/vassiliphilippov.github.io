fringe-toed lizard

1. NOUN

/ frɪndʒ toʊd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / frɪndʒ təʊd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A lizard with long, pointed scales around its toes, found in the deserts of the United States and Mexico.
desert lizard with fringed toes

Examples

The fringe-toed lizard is specially adapted to run across loose desert sand.
Scientists observed the unique movement patterns of the fringe-toed lizard in the Mojave Desert.
Unlike other species, the fringe-toed lizard has long scales on its toes that help with burrowing.
