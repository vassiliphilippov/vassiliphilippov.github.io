sound pollution

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd pəˈluʃən / (AmE), / saʊnd pəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
unwanted or harmful noise in the environment that may disturb people or cause adverse effects
unwanted or harmful environmental noise

Examples

Many cities have put regulations in place to reduce sound pollution in residential areas.
Constant sound pollution from traffic can disrupt people's sleep and affect their health.
The construction site was a major source of sound pollution for the nearby neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noise pollution - unwanted or harmful sound in the environment
