pearl sago

1. NOUN

/ pɜrl ˈseɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / pɜːl ˈseɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
sago that has been processed into small, round, pearl-like grains, commonly used in cooking and desserts
sago in small, round grains

Examples

The pudding recipe calls for pearl sago as the main ingredient.
You need to soak the pearl sago in water before boiling it.
In some Southeast Asian desserts, pearl sago creates a unique, chewy texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sago - a starchy substance obtained from palm stems, used in food
