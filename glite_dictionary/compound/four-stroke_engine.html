four-stroke engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr ˌstroʊk ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɔː ˌstrəʊk ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
An internal combustion engine in which a cycle of four distinct strokes—intake, compression, power, and exhaust—completes a single operating cycle.
type of internal combustion engine with four strokes

Examples

Many motorcycles use a four-stroke engine for improved fuel efficiency and lower emissions.
The mechanic explained how the four-stroke engine differs from its two-stroke counterpart.
Early cars often had a four-stroke engine designed for durability and reliability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: two-stroke engine - internal combustion engine that completes a power cycle in two strokes
