brown lemming

1. NOUN

/ braʊn ˈlɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / braʊn ˈlɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
A small, burrowing rodent native to northwestern Canada and Alaska, known for its brown fur and short tail.
small rodent of northwestern Canada and Alaska

Examples

The brown lemming is an important food source for many Arctic predators.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of the brown lemming during the summer.
Climate change may impact the population of the brown lemming in Alaska.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - small, burrowing rodent
