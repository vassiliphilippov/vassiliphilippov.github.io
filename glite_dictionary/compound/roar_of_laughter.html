roar of laughter

1. NOUN

/ rɔr əv ˈlæftɚ / (AmE), / rɔːr əv ˈlɑːftə / (BrE)
a very loud and unrestrained burst of laughter
very loud burst of laughter

Examples

The audience erupted into a roar of laughter at the comedian's witty remark.
His silly dance moves provoked a roar of laughter from everyone at the party.
At the end of the meeting, someone made a clever joke that evoked a roar of laughter.

Synset Examples

He let out a roar of laughter at the joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guffaw - a loud and boisterous laugh
  • Degree Lower: giggle - a light, silly, or nervous laugh
