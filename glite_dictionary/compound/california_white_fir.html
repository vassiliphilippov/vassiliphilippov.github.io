California white fir

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈfɔrniə waɪt fɜr / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈfɔːniə waɪt fɜː / (BrE)
a medium to tall fir tree native to central and western United States, noted for its narrow upright crown and soft wood
fir tree native to U.S. West with soft wood

Examples

The California white fir is commonly found in the mountains of the western United States.
Lumber from the California white fir is valued for its softness and workability.
We identified several California white fir trees during our hike in the Sierra Nevada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white fir - a species of fir tree with pale bark and needles
