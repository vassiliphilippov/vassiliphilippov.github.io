X-linked gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛks lɪŋkt dʒin / (AmE), / ˈɛks lɪŋkt dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that is located on the X chromosome, often associated with traits or disorders inherited through the X chromosome
gene located on the X chromosome
medical

Examples

Certain disorders, like hemophilia, are caused by mutations in an X-linked gene.
Because males have only one X chromosome, the effects of an X-linked gene are often more pronounced in them.
Researchers identified a new X-linked gene responsible for the rare condition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autosomal gene - gene located on a non-sex chromosome (autosome)

Etymology

From 'X-linked' (referring to the X chromosome) and 'gene.'
