airplane propeller

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌpleɪn prəˈpɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈeəˌpleɪn prəˈpelə / (BrE)
a rotating device with blades attached to an aircraft engine that pushes against air to generate thrust
rotating device on an aircraft for thrust

Examples

The mechanic inspected the airplane propeller for any signs of damage before the flight.
Birds can sometimes strike the airplane propeller, causing safety concerns for pilots.
The vintage biplane featured a wooden airplane propeller at its nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propeller - device with rotating blades for propulsion
