unicorn root

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.nɪ.kɔrn ruːt / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.kɔːn ruːt / (BrE)
any of several perennial plants of the genus Aletris, having grasslike leaves and bitter roots, traditionally reputed to cure colic.
herbaceous plant of the genus Aletris

Examples

Traditional healers have long used unicorn root to treat digestive issues.
The bitter extract of unicorn root was once popular in folk medicine.
Botanists studied several species of unicorn root found in North America.

Etymology

Named for the shape of the root, which is said to resemble a unicorn's horn.
