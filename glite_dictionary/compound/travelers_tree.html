traveler's tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrævələrz ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈtræv(ə)ləz ˌtriː / (BrE)
a tall tropical plant (Ravenala madagascariensis) with fan-shaped leaves, edible nuts, and leafstalks that provide drinkable sap, reputedly used by travelers as an emergency water source
tropical plant with fan-shaped leaves, also called traveler's palm

Examples

The traveler's tree is a striking feature of Madagascar’s landscape, easily recognized by its large, fan-shaped leaves.
Explorers relied on the traveler's tree for water when no fresh sources were available.
A mature traveler's tree can reach impressive heights, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler's palm - another common name for the traveler's tree

Etymology

Named for its usefulness to travelers, as its leafstalks hold drinkable water; not a true tree or palm.
