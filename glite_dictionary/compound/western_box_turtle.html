Western box turtle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn bɑks ˈtɝt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən bɒks ˈtɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
a species of box turtle primarily found in the prairies of the western United States and northern Mexico
species of prairie box turtle

Examples

The Western box turtle is often seen basking in the sun on prairie grasslands.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the Western box turtle.
Young children spotted a Western box turtle crossing the dirt road near their farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box turtle - a turtle of the genus Terrapene, known for its domed shell and ability to close itself within it
