foster care

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑstər kɛr / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə keə / (BrE)
supervised care for children, especially those who are neglected or delinquent, provided in an institution or by a substitute family when their natural parents are unable to care for them
supervised care for children away from home

Examples

After his parents were unable to care for him, he was placed in foster care.
Many children in foster care eventually find permanent homes through adoption.
The government is working to improve the foster care system and support foster families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adoption - the legal process of taking another person's child into one's family
  • Synonym: orphanage - an institution for children whose parents are dead or unable to care for them
