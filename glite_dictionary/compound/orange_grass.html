orange grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəndʒ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
an annual, wiry-stemmed North American weed with very small, scalelike leaves and small yellow flowers
a North American weed with scalelike leaves and yellow flowers

Examples

In the meadow, patches of orange grass grew among taller wildflowers.
Botanists are studying orange grass due to its unique adaptation to dry soils.
Some gardeners consider orange grass a stubborn weed that invades lawns rapidly.
