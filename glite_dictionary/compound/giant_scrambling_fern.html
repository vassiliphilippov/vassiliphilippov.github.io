giant scrambling fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈskræmblɪŋ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈskræmblɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
A large climbing or spreading fern native to the Pacific region and China, capable of forming patches up to 18 feet high.
large spreading fern native to Pacific and China

Examples

The giant scrambling fern quickly overtook the garden wall, creating a dense, green curtain.
Researchers found several giant scrambling ferns growing along the forest edge in Fiji.
Landscapers recommend pruning the giant scrambling fern regularly to keep it under control.
