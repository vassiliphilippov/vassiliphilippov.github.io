Salvation Army

1. PROPN

/ ˌsælˌveɪʃən ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˌsælˌveɪʃən ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
a Christian charitable organization known for evangelism and providing social services to the poor and homeless
international Christian charity organization

Examples

The Salvation Army organizes annual drives to collect clothing for those in need.
After losing his job, John received invaluable support from the Salvation Army.
Volunteers at the Salvation Army distribute meals to the homeless every evening.

Etymology

Founded in London in 1865 by William Booth as the East London Christian Mission, renamed Salvation Army in 1878.
