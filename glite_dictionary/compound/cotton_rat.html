cotton rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtən ræt / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən ræt / (BrE)
a burrowing rodent with long hair, native to southern North America and Central America, known for being destructive to crops
long-haired burrowing rodent of the Americas

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control cotton rat populations in their fields.
The cotton rat builds intricate burrows that can damage crop roots.
Researchers studied disease transmission among cotton rats in the wild.
