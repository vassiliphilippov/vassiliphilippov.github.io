Lake Ontario

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ɑnˈtɛr.i.oʊ / (AmE), / leɪk ɒnˈtɛə.ri.əʊ / (BrE)
the smallest and easternmost of the Great Lakes of North America, located between Ontario, Canada, and New York, USA
the smallest Great Lake in North America

Examples

The city of Toronto is located on the northern shore of Lake Ontario.
Many ships travel through the St. Lawrence River from Lake Ontario to the Atlantic Ocean.
In the winter, heavy snowfall often occurs near Lake Ontario due to lake-effect snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Lakes - a group of five large lakes in North America

Etymology

From the Iroquoian word 'Ontarí'io', meaning 'lake of shining waters'.
