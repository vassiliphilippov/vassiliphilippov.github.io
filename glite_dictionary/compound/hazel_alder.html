hazel alder

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzəl ˈɔldər / (AmE), / ˈheɪz(ə)l ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub or small tree of eastern North America, with smooth bark and rounded leaves, commonly found near streams and wetlands
shrub or small tree of eastern North America

Examples

We found a cluster of hazel alder growing along the riverbank.
The hazel alder provides important habitat for wildlife in wetland areas.
In early spring, the hazel alder produces catkins before its leaves appear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a type of tree or shrub in the birch family
