dihydric alcohol

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈhaɪdrɪk ˈælkəˌhɔl / (AmE), / daɪˈhɪdrɪk ˈalkəhɒl / (BrE)
A chemical compound containing two hydroxyl groups (-OH) in each molecule; also known as a glycol.
alcohol with two hydroxyl groups

Examples

Ethylene glycol is a common example of a dihydric alcohol used in antifreeze solutions.
The presence of two -OH groups makes a dihydric alcohol more reactive than a monohydric alcohol.
Scientists studied the properties of the new dihydric alcohol in various chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycol - an alcohol with two hydroxyl groups, synonym for dihydric alcohol

Etymology

From 'di-' meaning two, 'hydric' relating to hydrogen, and 'alcohol' from Arabic 'al-kuḥl'.
