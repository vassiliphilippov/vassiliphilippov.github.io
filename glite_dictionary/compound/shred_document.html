to shred document

1. VERB

/ ʃrɛd ˈdɑːkjumənt / (AmE), / ʃrɛd ˈdɒkjumənt / (BrE)
to cut a document into small pieces, typically to destroy it and protect sensitive information
destroy a document by cutting it into pieces

Examples

Before discarding any confidential files, make sure to shred document to prevent identity theft.
Many offices have policies requiring employees to shred document containing sensitive client information.
She quickly used the machine to shred document after the meeting had ended.

Synset Examples

I need to shred these documents before throwing them away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to cause something to no longer exist
  • Synonym: to dispose of - to get rid of something
