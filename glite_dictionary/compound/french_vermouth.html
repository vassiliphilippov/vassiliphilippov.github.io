French vermouth

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ vərˈmuθ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ vəˈmuːθ / (BrE)
a dry, pale amber variety of vermouth originally produced in France, commonly used in cocktails
a dry, pale vermouth from France

Examples

He added a splash of French vermouth to his martini.
Classic recipes often call for French vermouth rather than the sweeter Italian type.
The dry flavor of French vermouth balances the bold notes in many cocktails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermouth - a fortified wine flavored with various botanicals
  • Antonym: Italian vermouth - a sweet, red variety of vermouth from Italy
