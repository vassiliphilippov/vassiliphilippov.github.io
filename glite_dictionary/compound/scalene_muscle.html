scalene muscle

1. NOUN

/ skəˈliːn ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / skəˈliːn ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
any of several muscles in the side of the neck, extending from the cervical vertebrae to the first or second ribs and involved in moving the neck and assisting in breathing
muscle in the neck aiding movement and respiration
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the pain was due to tightness in the scalene muscle.
Inhalation is assisted by the contraction of the scalene muscles during deep breathing.
Physical therapists often treat the scalene muscle to relieve neck stiffness.

Etymology

From Latin 'scalenus', from Greek 'skalenos' (uneven, irregular), referring to the muscles' shape.
