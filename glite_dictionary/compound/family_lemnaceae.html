family Lemnaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli lɛmˈneɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli lɛmˈniːsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of small free-floating aquatic plants, commonly known as duckweeds, characterized by their simple thalloid structure and lack of true stems or leaves.
family of free-floating aquatic plants

Examples

The laboratory was conducting research on the nutrient uptake of family Lemnaceae in polluted water.
Many wetlands support an abundance of family Lemnaceae species, providing a food source for waterfowl.
Ecologists monitor the rapid reproduction rates of family Lemnaceae to assess freshwater ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - Common name for plants in the family Lemnaceae

Etymology

From New Latin Lemnaceae, based on genus Lemna, from Greek lemná (duckweed).
