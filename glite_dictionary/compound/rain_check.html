rain check

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪn ˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈreɪn ˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
A promise that an offer, invitation, or opportunity that is declined now will be made again in the future.
promise to renew an offer later

Examples

I'm busy tonight, but can I take a rain check on that dinner invitation?
The store manager offered a rain check since the item was out of stock.
She gave him a rain check when she couldn't make it to lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - the act of delaying or deferring something

Etymology

Originally from the practice of giving a ticket for future use when baseball games were postponed due to rain in late 19th-century America.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪn ˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈreɪn tʃɛk / (BrE)
a ticket or voucher given to spectators at an outdoor event, entitling them to admission at a later date if the event was postponed or cancelled due to rain
ticket for postponed event due to rain

Examples

Due to heavy showers, the baseball game was cancelled and everyone received a rain check.
If the concert is rained out, just hold on to your rain check for the rescheduled date.
When the match was postponed, ticket holders were issued a rain check for entry to the future event.
