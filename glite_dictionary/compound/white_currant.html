white currant

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a variety of currant plant bearing small, translucent white berries, often grown in gardens for their sweet flavor
type of currant with white berries

Examples

The dessert was garnished with a handful of white currants for a fresh, tangy touch.
She planted white currants alongside red and black varieties in her garden.
White currant jam is less common than red, but has a delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red currant - a species of currant with bright red berries
  • Synonym: black currant - a species of currant with dark purple to black berries

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a small, white, translucent edible berry produced by certain cultivars of the red currant (Ribes rubrum)
a type of small white edible berry

Examples

She made a delicious tart with fresh white currants from the garden.
The market was selling both red and white currants in small baskets.
Unlike red currants, white currants have a milder, sweeter flavor ideal for desserts.

Synset Examples

White currants are rarely specified in savoury cooking recipes compared with their red counterparts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: red currant - a small red edible berry from Ribes rubrum
