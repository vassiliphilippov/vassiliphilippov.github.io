acne vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈækni vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈækni vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A common skin condition characterized by pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads, typically affecting adolescents and young adults.
common type of acne
medical

Examples

Many teenagers experience acne vulgaris during puberty.
A dermatologist confirmed her diagnosis as acne vulgaris.
Proper skin care can help manage the symptoms of acne vulgaris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acne - a skin condition causing pimples and spots

Etymology

From Latin 'acne' (skin eruption) and 'vulgaris' (common, ordinary).
