fermentation alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɚˌmɛnˈteɪʃən ˈælkəˌhɑl / (AmE), / ˌfəːmɛnˈteɪʃən ˈalkəhɒl / (BrE)
alcohol (ethanol) produced by fermentation, especially as the intoxicating component in alcoholic beverages and with industrial uses
alcohol produced by fermentation

Examples

The production of fermentation alcohol is essential for making wine and beer.
Many cleaning solutions contain fermentation alcohol as a disinfectant.
Researchers are studying fermentation alcohol as a renewable alternative fuel source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethanol - the chemical compound, especially as used in beverages and fuel
  • Synonym: alcohol - the intoxicating agent in liquors; ethanol

Etymology

From Latin fermentatio (fermentation) + Arabic al-kuḥl (alcohol).
