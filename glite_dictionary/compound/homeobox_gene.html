homeobox gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmioʊˌbɑks dʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmiəʊˌbɒks dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene containing a homeobox DNA sequence, involved in regulating development and segmentation in embryos
gene involved in embryonic development
medical

Examples

A mutation in a homeobox gene can cause dramatic changes in an organism's body plan.
Scientists study the homeobox gene to better understand embryonic segmentation.
Disruptions in the homeobox gene family may lead to developmental disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeotic gene - a gene that controls the development of anatomical structures

Etymology

From 'homeobox', which combines 'homeo-' (Greek, similar) and 'box' (DNA sequence), and 'gene'.
