eucalyptus gum

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkəlɪptəs ɡʌm / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs ɡʌm / (BrE)
a reddish-brown dried gummy substance exuded from the bark of certain eucalyptus trees, especially Eucalyptus camaldulensis, used in various products and industries
reddish-brown gum from eucalyptus trees

Examples

The company uses eucalyptus gum as a natural thickener in their food products.
Large blobs of eucalyptus gum can often be seen hardening on the trunks after the bark is cut.
Some traditional medicines incorporate eucalyptus gum for its supposed healing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum arabic - pale gummy exudate from acacia trees used in similar applications
