dita bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːtə bɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈdiːtə bɑːk / (BrE)
an evergreen tree of eastern Asia and the Philippines, noted for its large leathery leaves and compact clusters of small green-white flowers; its bark was formerly used in medicine.
evergreen tree of Asia, bark used medicinally

Examples

Traditional healers once valued dita bark for its medicinal properties.
The dita bark tree produces clusters of delicate green-white flowers each spring.
Researchers are studying the compounds found in dita bark for potential pharmaceutical uses.
