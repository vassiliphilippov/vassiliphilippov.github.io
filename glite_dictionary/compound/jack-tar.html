Jack-tar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæk tɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk tɑː / (BrE)
A sailor, especially one in the British navy; a seaman.
a sailor, especially in the British navy
archaic

Examples

The old novel tells the tale of a Jack-tar who sailed the world in search of adventure.
As a young Jack-tar, he spent years braving storms on the open sea.
Many Jack-tars would gather in the harbor tavern after a long voyage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor - a person who works on a ship as part of the crew
  • Antonym: landsman - person inexperienced with the sea or ships

Etymology

From 'Jack' (generic name for a man) and 'tar', referring to the tar used to waterproof sailors' clothing and gear.
