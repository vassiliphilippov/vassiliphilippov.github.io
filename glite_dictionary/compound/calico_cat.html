calico cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪkoʊ kæt / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ kæt / (BrE)
A domestic cat with a coat that has patches of black, white, and orange or cream-colored fur.
cat with black, white, and orange patches

Examples

The little girl named her calico cat Muffin because of its sweet personality.
A calico cat is often considered lucky in some cultures.
Our neighbor's calico cat has the brightest orange patches on its fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortoiseshell cat - cat with mainly black and orange mixed coloring

Etymology

From 'calico,' referring to the printed cotton fabric, likened to the patchwork appearance of the cat's fur.
