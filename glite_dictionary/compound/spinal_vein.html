spinal vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the spinal cord
vein draining the spinal cord
medical

Examples

The spinal vein plays a critical role in draining blood from the spinal cord.
Damage to a spinal vein can lead to serious neurological complications.
The surgeon carefully avoided the major spinal veins during the operation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spinal artery - an artery supplying blood to the spinal cord
