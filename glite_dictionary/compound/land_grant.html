land grant

1. NOUN

/ lænd ɡrænt / (AmE), / land ɡrɑːnt / (BrE)
an official allocation of public land, especially by a government to an institution, organization, or individual, such as for building railways or establishing colleges
official allocation of public land

Examples

The university was established on a land grant from the government in the 19th century.
Many railroads in the United States were built with the help of federal land grants.
Settlers benefited when the king issued a land grant to encourage development of the new territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money or other aid given by an organization, especially by a government
