quality control

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑləti kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒləti kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the process of maintaining standards in manufactured products by testing a sample of output against the specification
process ensuring products meet quality standards

Examples

The company has implemented strict quality control measures to ensure customer satisfaction.
Without proper quality control, defective products could reach the market and harm the brand's reputation.
Regular inspections are an important part of the quality control process in manufacturing plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quality assurance - the maintenance of a desired level of quality in a service or product, especially by means of attention to every stage of the process of delivery or production
