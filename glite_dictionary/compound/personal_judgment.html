personal judgment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsənl ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənl ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
(Law) A court decision that requires an individual or organization to pay money damages to another party.
legal judgment requiring payment of damages
legal

Examples

The court issued a personal judgment against the company for breach of contract.
If you lose the lawsuit, you might face a personal judgment and be required to pay damages.
A personal judgment can affect your credit and may result in wage garnishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - an official decision made by a court
