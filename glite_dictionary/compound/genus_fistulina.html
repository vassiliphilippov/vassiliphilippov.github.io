genus Fistulina

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs fɪs.tjʊˈliː.nə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs fɪs.tjʊˈliː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by crowded, individually separated pores.
genus of fungi with separate pores

Examples

The genus Fistulina includes the well-known beefsteak fungus found on oak trees.
Mycologists studied the unique pore structure of fungi in the genus Fistulina.
Several edible species within the genus Fistulina are known for their meat-like texture.

Etymology

From Latin 'Fistulina', referring to the type species; 'genus' meaning Biological group.
