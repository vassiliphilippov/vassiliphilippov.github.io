automatic pilot

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmæt̬ɪk ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
a device that automatically controls the steering of a ship, aircraft, or spacecraft to maintain a set course
device that steers vehicles automatically

Examples

The plane was able to cruise comfortably on automatic pilot during most of the flight.
Most modern ships are equipped with an automatic pilot for ease of navigation.
After takeoff, the captain switched to automatic pilot to keep the aircraft steady.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopilot - system for automatically steering a vehicle

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑtəˈmætɪk ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
A state in which a person performs actions without conscious thought or self-awareness, as if functioning mechanically.
state of acting without self-awareness

Examples

Most mornings, I prepare breakfast on automatic pilot without really thinking about it.
During the long meeting, he answered questions on automatic pilot, barely paying attention.
After years in the job, she sometimes feels like she's working on automatic pilot.

Synset Examples

she went about her chores on automatic pilot
too much of the writing seems to have been done on automatic pilot
she talked and he dozed and my mind went on autopilot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopilot - a cognitive state of acting without self-awareness

Etymology

Originally from aviation, where 'automatic pilot' refers to a device that steers a ship or aircraft without constant human control; extended metaphorically to human behavior.
