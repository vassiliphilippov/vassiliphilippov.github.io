transurethral resection of the prostate

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræn.sjʊˈriː.θrəl rɪˈsɛk.ʃən əv ðə ˈprɑː.steɪt / (AmE), / ˌtranz.jʊˈriː.θrəl rɪˈsek.ʃən əv ðə ˈprɒs.teɪt / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which tissue from the prostate gland is removed through the urethra, often to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia
surgical removal of prostate tissue via the urethra
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a transurethral resection of the prostate to relieve his urinary symptoms.
Many men with enlarged prostate undergo a transurethral resection of the prostate as part of their treatment.
After his transurethral resection of the prostate, he experienced a significant improvement in urinary flow.

Etymology

From 'transurethral' (through the urethra), 'resection' (surgical removal), and 'prostate' (from Greek 'prostatēs').
