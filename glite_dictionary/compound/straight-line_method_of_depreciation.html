straight-line method of depreciation

1. NOUN

/ streɪt laɪn ˈmɛθəd əv dɪˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / streɪt laɪn ˈmɛθəd əv dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(accounting) a method of calculating depreciation in which an equal amount of an asset's cost is expensed each year over the asset's useful life.
equal annual depreciation method (accounting)
legal

Examples

Many companies use the straight-line method of depreciation to allocate the cost of their equipment over time.
Under the straight-line method of depreciation, the annual expense remains constant throughout the asset's useful life.
The accountant recommended adopting the straight-line method of depreciation for the new software system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - reduction in the value of an asset over time
