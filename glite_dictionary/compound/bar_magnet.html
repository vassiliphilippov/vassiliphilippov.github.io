bar magnet

1. NOUN

/ bɑr ˈmægnət / (AmE), / bɑː ˈmægnət / (BrE)
a straight, rectangular magnet with north and south magnetic poles at either end
a rectangular magnet with poles at each end

Examples

The science teacher demonstrated how a bar magnet attracts iron filings on the tabletop.
If you place a bar magnet near a compass, the needle will move due to the magnetic field.
Most children's science kits include a bar magnet for simple experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnet - an object that produces a magnetic field
  • Degree Higher: electromagnet - a magnet created by electric current in a coil
