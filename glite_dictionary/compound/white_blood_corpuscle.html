white blood corpuscle

1. NOUN

/ waɪt blʌd ˈkɔrpəsəl / (AmE), / waɪt blʌd ˈkɒːpəsəl / (BrE)
a type of blood cell that helps protect the body by engulfing and digesting bacteria, fungi, and other harmful organisms
immune-system blood cell
medical

Examples

During the infection, your white blood corpuscle count will likely increase.
A white blood corpuscle helps destroy bacteria that enter the body.
Doctors examined her white blood corpuscle levels for signs of disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white blood cell - another term for white blood corpuscle; immune-system cell in blood
  • Antonym: red blood cell - the oxygen-carrying blood cell; not part of immune response
