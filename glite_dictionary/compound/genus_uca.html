genus Uca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈjuːkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈjuːkə / (BrE)
A genus of crabs known as fiddler crabs, distinguished by the extreme size difference between the claws of the males.
genus of fiddler crabs

Examples

The genus Uca includes many species commonly known as fiddler crabs.
Researchers are studying the mating dances of crabs within the genus Uca.
Many coastal wetlands are inhabited by various members of the genus Uca.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiddler crab - small crab with an enlarged claw in males

Etymology

From Modern Latin Uca, the genus name assigned in zoological classification.
