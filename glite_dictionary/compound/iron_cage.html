iron cage

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn keɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən keɪdʒ / (BrE)
a situation or structure in which someone is trapped with no possibility of escape, often used metaphorically to describe oppressive social or bureaucratic systems
inescapable trap or confinement, often metaphorical

Examples

The bureaucracy can feel like an iron cage, restricting creativity and freedom.
She realized she was living in an iron cage of expectations imposed by her family.
Many critics describe modern society as an iron cage that limits individual choice.
