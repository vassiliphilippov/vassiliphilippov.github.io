bladder worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædər wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈblædə wɜːm / (BrE)
the encysted saclike larval stage of certain tapeworms, typically found in the tissues of intermediate hosts
larval stage of some tapeworms
medical

Examples

The veterinarian explained that the cyst in the cow’s muscle was caused by a bladder worm.
Humans can become infected with bladder worms by ingesting undercooked pork containing the larvae.
The life cycle of a tapeworm includes a stage where it exists as a bladder worm in an intermediate host.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapeworm - parasitic flatworm of the class Cestoda

Etymology

From 'bladder', reflecting the saclike appearance, and 'worm' for its parasitic nature.
