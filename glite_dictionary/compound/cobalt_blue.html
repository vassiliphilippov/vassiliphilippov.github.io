cobalt blue

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌbɔlt bluː / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌbɒlt bluː / (BrE)
a deep, vivid shade of blue with a slight greenish tint, originally made using cobalt salts
deep, greenish-blue color

Examples

The artist used cobalt blue to give depth to the sky in her painting.
He wore a striking cobalt blue shirt to the party.
Ceramic tiles glazed in cobalt blue decorated the courtyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - bright blue color like the sky
  • Degree Lower: navy blue - very dark shade of blue
  • Synonym: cerulean - sky-blue color

Etymology

From cobalt, the element used to create the pigment, and blue, referring to the color.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌbɔlt blu / (AmE), / ˈkəʊbɔːlt bluː / (BrE)
a deep, greenish-blue pigment made from cobalt oxide and alumina, also referring to the color resembling this pigment
deep greenish-blue pigment or color

Examples

The artist mixed cobalt blue to achieve the perfect tone in her landscape painting.
I love the way the walls look after being painted a vibrant cobalt blue.
Ancient Chinese pottery often features a distinct cobalt blue glaze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerulean - a deep sky-blue color

Etymology

Named for its main component, cobalt (from German Kobalt, meaning 'goblin', due to mining difficulties), plus 'blue' for the color.
