pickerel frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkərəl frɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈpɪkərəl frɒɡ / (BrE)
A small brown frog (Lithobates palustris) with distinctive square spots, found in meadows and grassy areas in eastern North America.
a spotted frog native to eastern North America

Examples

We spotted a pickerel frog near the edge of the pond during our hike.
The pickerel frog is recognized by the dark rectangular spots on its back.
Unlike other frogs, the pickerel frog secretes a mild toxin as a defense against predators.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance in pattern to the pickerel fish.
