Romaic language

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmeɪɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an older term for the modern Greek vernacular, spoken from the end of the medieval period to the present; now usually called Modern Greek
historical term for modern Greek vernacular

Examples

The scholar devoted his life to the study of the Romaic language and its literature.
During the Ottoman era, the Romaic language was the spoken tongue among Greeks.
Many older texts refer to modern Greek as the Romaic language rather than simply Modern Greek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Modern Greek - the language currently spoken in Greece and Cyprus

Etymology

From Medieval Greek Rhōmaïkós (Roman, i.e. Byzantine) referring to the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) heritage.
