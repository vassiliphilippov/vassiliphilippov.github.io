the Great Calamity

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˌɡreɪt kəˈlæmɪti / (AmE), / ðə ˌɡreɪt kəˈlæmɪti / (BrE)
the period of severe famine in Ireland between 1846 and 1851, caused by potato blight, during which around a million people died and many emigrated
the Irish Potato Famine (1846–1851)

Examples

Historians continue to study the impact of the Great Calamity on Irish society and culture.
During the Great Calamity, entire villages were depopulated as people fled or starved.
Many families trace their American roots back to ancestors who left Ireland during the Great Calamity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Great Hunger - another name for the Irish Potato Famine
