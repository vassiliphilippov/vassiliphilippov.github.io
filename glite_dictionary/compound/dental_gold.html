dental gold

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛntəl ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈdɛntəl ɡəʊld / (BrE)
a gold alloy specifically used in dentistry, often for fillings, crowns, and other dental restorations
gold alloy used in dentistry

Examples

The dentist recommended a crown made of dental gold for its durability and biocompatibility.
She inherited several ounces of dental gold from her late father, who had collected old dental work.
Because dental gold resists corrosion, it has long been a popular choice for fillings and bridges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy - a metal made by combining two or more metallic elements
