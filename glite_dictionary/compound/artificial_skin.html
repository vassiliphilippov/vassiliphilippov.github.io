artificial skin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəl skɪn / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈfɪʃl̩ skɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic two-layered material used to replace or cover damaged human skin, especially in the treatment of burns
synthetic skin substitute, used in burn treatment
medical

Examples

The doctors used artificial skin to help heal the patient's severe burns.
Advancements in artificial skin technology have improved outcomes for burn victims.
Researchers are developing new types of artificial skin that better mimic natural tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin graft - transplanted skin used to cover a wound or burn
