variola minor virus

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛəriˈoʊlə ˈmaɪnər ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌvɑːrɪˈəʊlə ˈmɪnə ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
A less severe type of smallpox virus with a fatality rate of about 1 percent, also known as Alastrim.
milder form of smallpox virus
medical

Examples

The variola minor virus caused outbreaks of a milder form of smallpox in South America and southern Africa.
Researchers studied the variola minor virus to understand why its fatality rate was lower than that of variola major.
After the eradication of smallpox, the variola minor virus no longer occurs naturally in the population.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: variola major virus - the more severe type of smallpox virus, with a higher fatality rate

Etymology

From New Latin 'variola' (smallpox) + minor (Latin for 'smaller' or 'lesser').
