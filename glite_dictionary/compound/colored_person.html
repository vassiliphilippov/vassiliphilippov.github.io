colored person

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚd ˌpɚsən / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd ˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
(offensive, dated, US) A term formerly used in the United States to refer to a Black person; now considered offensive and outdated.
dated, offensive US term for a Black person
offensive american

Examples

The organization once restricted membership to colored persons only, reflecting the attitudes of its time.
Historical documents often use the term colored person, which is now recognized as offensive.
In the early 20th century, facilities were often designated as 'for colored persons only.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black person - a person belonging to a racial group having dark skin, especially of African ancestry
