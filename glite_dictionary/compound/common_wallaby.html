common wallaby

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən wəˈlæbi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən wəˈlæbi / (BrE)
a species of small wallaby, typically reaching a height of about 30 inches, native to Australia and surrounding regions
a small species of wallaby

Examples

The common wallaby is often seen grazing in open grasslands at dawn and dusk.
Our guide pointed out a common wallaby hiding among the eucalyptus trees.
Unlike kangaroos, the common wallaby is much smaller and more elusive in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - a small- or medium-sized marsupial native to Australia
