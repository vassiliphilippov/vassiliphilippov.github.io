privy purse

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪvi pɜrs / (AmE), / ˈprɪvi pɜːs / (BrE)
an allowance of money provided for a monarch's personal expenses, separate from official or state funds
monarch's personal allowance
british

Examples

The British sovereign once received a privy purse for personal expenditures beyond official duties.
Controversy arose over the reduction of the privy purse after changes in royal finances.
India abolished the privy purse for former princes after gaining independence.

Etymology

From 'privy', meaning private, and 'purse', meaning a sum of money; originally referring to the British sovereign's personal finances.
