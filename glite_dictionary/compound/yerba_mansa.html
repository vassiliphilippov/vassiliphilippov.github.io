yerba mansa

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛrbə ˈmɑːnsə / (AmE), / ˌjɜːbə ˈmɑːnsə / (BrE)
a stoloniferous herb native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its pungent rootstock and small spicate flowers surrounded by white bracts
herbaceous plant of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

In traditional medicine, extracts from the yerba mansa root are used to treat various ailments.
The wetlands of New Mexico are home to abundant stands of yerba mansa.
Botanists studied the adaptation mechanisms of yerba mansa in arid environments.

Etymology

From Spanish 'yerba mansa', meaning 'calm herb' or 'mild herb'.
