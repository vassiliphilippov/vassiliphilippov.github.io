ague grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡju ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡju ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A North American herb (colicroot) with a scurfy or grainy perianth and white flowers, native to the southeastern United States.
herb native to southeastern U.S.

Examples

The herbalist recommended ague grass for its traditional medicinal properties.
You can often find ague grass growing wild in the southeastern United States.
Botanists studied the scurfy perianth of ague grass to understand its classification.

Etymology

From 'ague' (an old term for fever and shivering) and 'grass,' referring to its former medicinal use as a remedy for fevers.
