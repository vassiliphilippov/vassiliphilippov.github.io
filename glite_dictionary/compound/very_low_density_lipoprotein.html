very low density lipoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛri ˌloʊ ˈdɛnsəti ˌlaɪpoʊˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˌvɛri ˌləʊ ˈdɛnsəti ˌlɪpəʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a large type of lipoprotein in the blood rich in triglycerides, which transports fats to tissues and is a precursor to LDL cholesterol
a type of blood lipoprotein rich in triglycerides
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a high very low density lipoprotein level increases the risk of heart disease.
During the test, the patient's very low density lipoprotein concentration was found to be above normal.
Researchers study how very low density lipoprotein particles contribute to the buildup of arterial plaque.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: low density lipoprotein - a type of lipoprotein responsible for transporting cholesterol in the blood, sometimes called 'bad cholesterol'

Etymology

Formed from the phrase 'very low density' describing its relative lipid-protein ratio and 'lipoprotein' (from 'lipo-' fat + 'protein').
