public record

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
an official document or piece of information that a governmental body is required to keep and make accessible to the public
official document open to public scrutiny
legal

Examples

The land ownership information became a public record after the city council's decision.
Court proceedings are typically documented and preserved as part of the public record.
Journalists often request access to public records to investigate matters of public interest.

Synset Examples

Although public records are records of public business, they are not necessarily available without restriction, although Freedom of Information legislation (FOI) that has been gradually introduced in many jurisdictions since the 1960s has made access easier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: private record - a record not accessible to the general public
  • Synonym: official record - an officially maintained document or file
