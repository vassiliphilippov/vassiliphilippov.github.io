crab grass

1. NOUN

/ kræb ɡræs / (AmE), / kræb ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass with creeping stems that root easily, commonly considered a weed in lawns and gardens.
a weed grass with creeping stems

Examples

Crab grass spreads quickly during the summer, taking over many lawns if not controlled.
He spent the afternoon pulling up crab grass from the edges of the garden path.
A thick layer of mulch can help prevent crab grass from growing in the flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weed - an unwanted plant, especially in a lawn or garden
