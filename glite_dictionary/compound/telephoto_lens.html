telephoto lens

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləˈfoʊtoʊ lɛnz / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈfəʊtəʊ lɛnz / (BrE)
a camera lens with a long focal length that magnifies distant objects, making them appear closer than they are
camera lens for magnifying distant objects

Examples

Photographers often use a telephoto lens to capture wildlife shots from a distance.
He attached a telephoto lens to his camera to photograph the athletes on the field.
A telephoto lens allows you to fill the frame with faraway subjects without moving closer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wide-angle lens - a camera lens with a short focal length, capturing a wider field of view
  • Synonym: zoom lens - a camera lens with an adjustable focal length

Etymology

From 'tele-' (meaning 'far') and 'photo' (from Greek for 'light'), describing a lens for photographing distant subjects.
