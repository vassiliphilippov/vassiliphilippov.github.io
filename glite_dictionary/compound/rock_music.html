rock music

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈmjuzɪk / (AmE), / rɒk ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A genre of popular music that originated in the 1950s, characterized by a strong rhythm and often centered on the electric guitar, drawing influences from rhythm and blues and country music.
genre of popular music from the 1950s

Examples

Many classic bands from the 1970s helped define the sound of rock music.
She grew up listening mostly to rock music and still prefers it today.
This festival includes a variety of genres, but the main focus is on rock music.

Synset Examples

rock is a generic term for the range of styles that evolved out of rock'n'roll.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock and roll - early style of popular music with a strong rhythm; precursor to rock music
  • Degree Lower: pop music - genre of popular music with wide appeal, beginning in the mid-20th century
  • Degree Higher: heavy metal - a loud, aggressive subgenre of rock music

Etymology

From 'rock and roll,' a style that emerged in the 1950s in the United States.
