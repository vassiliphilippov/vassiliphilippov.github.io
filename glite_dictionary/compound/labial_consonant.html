labial consonant

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əl ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bɪ.əl ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound in which the lips are used to produce the articulation, such as 'p', 'b', and 'm'
consonant articulated with the lips

Examples

In English, the sounds represented by labial consonants include 'p', 'b', and 'm'.
Children often learn to pronounce labial consonants early because they are easy to articulate.
The linguistics professor explained how labial consonant sounds differ from other consonants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bilabial consonant - a consonant articulated with both lips
  • Antonym: dental consonant - a consonant articulated with the tongue against the teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip) and 'consonant-' (sounding together).
