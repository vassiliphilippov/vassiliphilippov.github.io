tempest-swept

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpɪstˌswɛpt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpɪstˌswɛpt / (BrE)
Battered or struck repeatedly by storms or by severe adversities.
pounded by storms or adversity

Examples

The lighthouse stood firm on the tempest-swept cliffs, a beacon to passing ships.
After years of war, the nation remained tempest-swept and weary, longing for peace.
He wrote a novel set on a tempest-swept island, where the protagonists faced both nature and their own fears.

Etymology

From 'tempest' (violent storm) + past participle of 'sweep'.
