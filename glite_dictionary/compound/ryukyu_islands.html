Ryukyu Islands

1. PROPN

/ riˈjuːkju ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / rɪˈjuːkjuː ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a chain of islands in the western Pacific Ocean, southwest of mainland Japan, formerly administered by the United States and returned to Japan in 1972
island chain southwest of Japan

Examples

The Ryukyu Islands are known for their subtropical climate and unique culture.
After World War II, the Ryukyu Islands were under U.S. administration until 1972.
Okinawa is the largest of the Ryukyu Islands and a major tourist destination.

Etymology

From Japanese 琉球 (Ryūkyū), the traditional name of the islands.
