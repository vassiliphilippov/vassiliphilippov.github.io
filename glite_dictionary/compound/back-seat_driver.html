back-seat driver

1. NOUN

/ ˌbækˌsiːt ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˌbækˌsiːt ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
a person who gives unwanted advice or tries to control situations, especially while someone else is performing a task
person who gives unwanted advice

Examples

My mother is a notorious back-seat driver who always has something to say during our trips.
I know you're trying to help, but please don't be a back-seat driver.
He doesn't drive himself, but he's the worst back-seat driver I've ever met.

Synset Examples

As a back-seat driver, she couldn't help but comment on his choices.
I appreciate advice, but I don't need a back-seat driver.
He's such a back-seat driver, always telling me how to drive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meddler - a person who interferes in others' affairs without invitation
  • Synonym: interloper - a person who becomes involved in a situation where they are not wanted

Etymology

From the literal position of a passenger in the back seat giving instructions to the driver; first attested in early 20th-century American English.
