principle of parsimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ˈpɑrsəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ˈpɑːsɪməni / (BrE)
the idea that, among competing explanations, the simplest one with the fewest assumptions should be preferred
the idea that simpler explanations are better

Examples

Scientists often rely on the principle of parsimony when formulating new theories.
According to the principle of parsimony, unnecessary complexity should be avoided in scientific models.
The principle of parsimony helped her choose the most straightforward explanation for the phenomenon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Occam's razor - the problem-solving principle that the simplest solution is most likely correct
