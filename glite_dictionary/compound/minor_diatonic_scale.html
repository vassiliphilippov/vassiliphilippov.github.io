minor diatonic scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ daɪ.əˈtɑnɪk skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə daɪ.əˈtɒnɪk skeɪl / (BrE)
A diatonic scale in which the intervals between the 2nd and 3rd, and the 5th and 6th notes are semitones, giving it a minor tonality.
a type of minor diatonic scale in music

Examples

The composer based the melody on a minor diatonic scale for a more melancholy effect.
You can hear the use of a minor diatonic scale in many classical pieces.
Learning to play the minor diatonic scale helps musicians understand minor keys in Western music.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major diatonic scale - a diatonic scale with major interval pattern
  • Synonym: minor scale - a musical scale with a minor third interval

Etymology

From Latin minor (smaller) + Greek diatonikos (through tones), describing the scale's interval structure.
