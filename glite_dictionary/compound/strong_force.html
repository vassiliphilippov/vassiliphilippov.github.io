strong force

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ fɔrs / (AmE), / strɒŋ fɔːs / (BrE)
the fundamental force of nature that binds protons and neutrons together in the atomic nucleus, mediated by particles called gluons
fundamental force binding atomic nuclei

Examples

The strong force is responsible for holding the nuclei of atoms together despite the repulsion between protons.
In particle physics, the strong force is mediated by gluons exchanged between quarks.
Without the strong force, all atomic nuclei would fall apart and matter as we know it would not exist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak force - the fundamental force responsible for radioactive decay
