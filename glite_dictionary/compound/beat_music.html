beat music

1. NOUN

/ bit ˈmjuː.zɪk / (AmE), / biːt ˈmjuː.zɪk / (BrE)
a style of pop and rock music that originated in the United Kingdom in the early 1960s, characterized by strong rhythms and simple melodies
1960s British pop/rock music genre

Examples

Many early bands from Liverpool played beat music before exploring more experimental styles.
The Beatles are often credited with popularizing beat music across the world.
By the late 1960s, beat music had evolved into new genres like psychedelic rock.

Synset Examples

Beat music declined in popularity in the UK around 1967 with the emergence of various Psychedelic Rock and Progressive Rock styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: British Invasion - the wave of British pop and rock bands that became popular in the US in the 1960s
  • Degree Lower: rock and roll - a popular music genre from the 1950s and 1960s, influencing beat music
  • Degree Higher: psychedelic rock - a style of rock music that followed and expanded on beat music in the late 1960s

Etymology

From 'beat' (referring to strong rhythm) and 'music'; associated with the 1960s British 'beat' scene.
