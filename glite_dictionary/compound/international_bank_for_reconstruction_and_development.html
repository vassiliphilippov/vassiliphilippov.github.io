International Bank for Reconstruction and Development

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəl bæŋk fɚ rɪˌkɑnstrəkˈʃən ənd dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl bæŋk fə rɪˌkɒnstrʌkˈʃən ənd dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
A United Nations agency established to provide loans and financial assistance for the development of member countries, especially developing nations.
UN agency providing loans for development

Examples

The International Bank for Reconstruction and Development approved a loan to fund new infrastructure projects in the region.
Many developing countries receive financial support from the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development to aid their economic growth.
The International Bank for Reconstruction and Development is often referred to as part of the World Bank Group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World Bank - International financial institution that provides loans and grants to the governments of low- and middle-income countries
