Ozark sundrops

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊˌzɑrk ˈsʌnˌdrɑps / (AmE), / ˈəʊzɑːk ˈsʌndrɒps / (BrE)
A species of evening-opening primrose native to the south central United States, known for its bright yellow flowers.
evening-opening primrose of south central US

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of Ozark sundrops light up the garden every summer.
Ozark sundrops thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
On their hike through Missouri, they spotted a cluster of Ozark sundrops near the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a plant of the genus Oenothera, known for its bright flowers

Etymology

Named after the Ozark region in the United States and 'sundrop', a common name for primroses with bright yellow flowers.
