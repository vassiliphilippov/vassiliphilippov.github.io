sound reflection

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd rɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / saʊnd rɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the return or repetition of a sound when sound waves bounce off a surface
bouncing of sound waves; echo

Examples

The sound reflection from the wall allowed them to hear their voices twice.
Engineers must consider sound reflection when designing concert halls.
Caves often produce a noticeable sound reflection, making even small noises seem louder.

Synset Examples

she could hear echoes of her own footsteps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - a sound or series of sounds caused by the reflection of sound waves from a surface back to the listener
