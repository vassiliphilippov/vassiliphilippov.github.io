common polypody

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən pəˈlɪpəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən pəˈlɪpədi / (BrE)
a species of fern (Polypodium vulgare) with creeping rootstocks and deeply lobed fronds, found in North America, Europe, Africa, and East Asia
a species of fern with lobed fronds

Examples

The common polypody thrives on mossy rocks and tree trunks in shaded woodlands.
Botanists studied the medicinal uses of common polypody in traditional herbal remedies.
In the garden, common polypody can create an attractive ground cover in cool, damp areas.
