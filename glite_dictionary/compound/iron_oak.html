iron oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn oʊk / (AmE), / ˈaɪən əʊk / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen oak tree of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, with oblong, leathery, often spiny-edged leaves.
medium-sized evergreen oak tree of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The dense wood of the iron oak makes it valuable for furniture making.
Iron oak trees can withstand the hot, arid conditions of the Southwest.
We spotted an iron oak growing along the rocky hillside during our hike.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn oʊk / (AmE), / ˈaɪən əʊk / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree of eastern and central United States with tough, moisture-resistant wood, often used for fence posts
a hardy deciduous American tree

Examples

The sturdy fence was built from iron oak posts that would last for decades.
Iron oak is prized for its tough, moisture-resistant wood, especially in rural construction.
In the forests of the Midwest, iron oak stands out for its dark green leaves and resilience.
