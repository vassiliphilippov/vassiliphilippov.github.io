Canadian pondweed

1. NOUN

/ kəˈneɪdiən ˈpɑn(d)ˌwid / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən ˈpɒn(d)wiːd / (BrE)
a freshwater aquatic plant of North America, often found growing in lakes and ponds and widely naturalized in Europe
North American aquatic plant

Examples

Canadian pondweed is commonly used in aquariums for its oxygenating properties.
The lake was covered in thick mats of Canadian pondweed, making boating difficult.
After being introduced, Canadian pondweed spread rapidly through many European waterways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterweed - a general term for aquatic plants of the family Hydrocharitaceae
