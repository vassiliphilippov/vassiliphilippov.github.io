plantain lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɪn ˌlɪl.i / (AmE), / ˈplan.tɪn ˌlɪl.i / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the genus Hosta, known for its broad, ribbed leaves and clusters of white, blue, or lilac flowers, often grown as ground cover in gardens.
ornamental perennial plant (Hosta genus)

Examples

She planted several plantain lilies along the shaded border of her garden.
The broad leaves of the plantain lily create a lush, green ground cover beneath the trees.
Gardeners prize the plantain lily for its attractive foliage and delicate summer blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hosta - a genus of shade-tolerant foliage plants, commonly known as plantain lilies
