envy of

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnvi ʌv / (AmE), / ˈɛnvi ɒv / (BrE)
a person or thing that is so admirable or desirable as to arouse envy in others
someone or something greatly admired or envied

Examples

His powerful new car quickly became the envy of the entire neighborhood.
The team's success is the envy of their rivals in the league.
Her stylish wardrobe is the envy of all her friends.

Synset Examples

Her colorful lunchbox was the envy of her classmates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jealousy - a feeling of envy towards someone else's achievements or possessions
