Amaryllis belladonna

1. NOUN

/ əˌmær.əˈlɪs ˌbɛl.əˈdɑn.ə / (AmE), / əˌmær.ɪˈlɪs ˌbɛl.əˈdæn.ə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to South Africa, often cultivated for its fragrant white or rose-colored flowers.
flowering plant with fragrant blooms

Examples

The Amaryllis belladonna blooms beautifully in late summer, brightening gardens with its rose-colored flowers.
Botanists recommend planting Amaryllis belladonna bulbs in well-drained soil for best results.
She gave her friend a bouquet made entirely of Amaryllis belladonna blossoms.

Etymology

From Latin: 'amaryllis' (a pastoral name) and 'belladonna' (beautiful lady).
