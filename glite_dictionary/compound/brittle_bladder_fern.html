brittle bladder fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtəl ˈblædər fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtəl ˈblædə fɜːn / (BrE)
A delicate fern native to North America and Europe, having thin divided fronds and brittle stems.
a delicate fern with brittle stems

Examples

The brittle bladder fern thrives in shaded, rocky crevices across North America and Europe.
Because of its fragile stems, handling the brittle bladder fern requires extra care in the garden.
Botanists studied the unique adaptation of the brittle bladder fern to dry, calcareous soils.
