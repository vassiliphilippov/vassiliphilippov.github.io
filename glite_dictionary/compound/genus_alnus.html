genus Alnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈælnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈal.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees and shrubs known as alders, commonly found in temperate climates and characterized by toothed leaves and catkins.
genus of trees/shrubs called alders

Examples

The genus Alnus includes several species commonly found along riverbanks and wetlands.
Botanists studied the reproductive structures unique to the genus Alnus.
Members of the genus Alnus are important for their nitrogen-fixing abilities in the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a tree or shrub of the genus Alnus

Etymology

From Latin 'alnus' (alder).
