black larch

1. NOUN

/ blæk lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / blæk lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a medium-sized North American larch tree (Larix laricina), native to Canada and the northern United States, with a broad conic crown and rust-brown scaly bark
a North American larch tree species

Examples

The black larch is known for its distinctive rust-brown bark and soft needles.
During autumn, the needles of the black larch turn a brilliant yellow before they fall.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the habitat of the black larch in Alaska.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarack - another name for the same tree species (Larix laricina)
