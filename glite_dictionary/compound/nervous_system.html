nervous system

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɝː.vəs ˌsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈnɜː.vəs ˌsɪs.təm / (BrE)
The network of nerve cells and fibers that transmits nerve impulses between parts of the body, coordinating sensation and control.
body's network of nerve cells and fibers

Examples

The human nervous system is responsible for processing information from the environment.
Certain diseases can damage the nervous system and affect movement or sensation.
The study focused on how the nervous system adapts to injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brain - the organ of soft nervous tissue inside the skull of vertebrates, functioning as the coordinating center of sensation and thought
  • Synonym: spinal cord - the cylindrical bundle of nerve fibers and associated tissue forming the central nervous system, running down the spine
