single-furrow plough

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈfɜːroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˈfʌrə plʌʊ / (BrE)
a plough designed with a single blade for turning over the soil, typically operated by one animal or person, having one wheel at the front and handles for steering at the rear
plough with one blade and wheel

Examples

The farmer used a single-furrow plough to prepare the field for planting.
A single-furrow plough is ideal for small plots and lighter soils.
Traditionally, a single-furrow plough would be pulled by one horse to turn the earth.

Synset Examples

A single draught horse can normally pull a single-furrow plough in clean light soil, but in heavier soils two horses are needed, one walking on the land and one in the furrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plough - an implement for cutting, lifting, turning over, and partly pulverizing soil
