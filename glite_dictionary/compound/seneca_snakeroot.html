Seneca snakeroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪkə ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪkə ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
An eastern North American plant with a terminal cluster of small white flowers and roots used in traditional medicine.
plant with medicinal roots and white flowers

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used Seneca snakeroot to treat respiratory ailments and snakebites.
In spring, Seneca snakeroot blooms with delicate white flowers atop slender stems.
The herbalist showed me a dried sample of Seneca snakeroot and explained its historical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polygala senega - botanical name for Seneca snakeroot

Etymology

Named after the Seneca people of North America, who used its roots for medicinal purposes, combined with 'snakeroot' for its use in treating snakebites.
