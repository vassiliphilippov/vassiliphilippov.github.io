Ladrone Islands

1. PROPN

/ ləˈdroʊn ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ləˈdrəʊn ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a former name for a chain of coral and volcanic islands in Micronesia, including Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands, located between New Guinea and Japan.
former name for Micronesian island chain including Guam and the Northern Marianas

Examples

The explorer Ferdinand Magellan visited the Ladrone Islands during his voyage in 1521.
The name Ladrone Islands was later replaced by the Mariana Islands to honor Queen Mariana of Spain.
Historians studied old maps that marked Guam and its neighbors as the Ladrone Islands.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Islas de los Ladrones' meaning 'Islands of Thieves'.
