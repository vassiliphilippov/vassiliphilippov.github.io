National Security Council

1. PROPN

/ ˌnæʃənəl sɪˈkjʊrəti ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / ˌnæʃnəl sɪˈkjʊərəti ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
a committee within the executive branch of a government that advises the head of state on national security, foreign policy, and military matters, and supervises intelligence agencies such as the CIA
governmental advisory committee on security and foreign affairs

Examples

The National Security Council met to discuss the country's response to the cyberattack.
She was appointed as an advisor to the National Security Council last year.
Key decisions on foreign policy often pass through the National Security Council.
