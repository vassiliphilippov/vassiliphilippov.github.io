fish-worship

1. NOUN

/ fɪʃ ˈwɜɹʃɪp / (AmE), / fɪʃ ˈwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
The practice of worshipping fish as deities or sacred beings, often as part of religious or cultural traditions.
the worship of fish as deities

Examples

Ancient communities along the Nile were known for their practice of fish-worship.
Historically, fish-worship was prevalent in several Asian cultures.
The temple is famous for rituals related to fish-worship that attract many visitors.
