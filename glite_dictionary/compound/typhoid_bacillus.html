typhoid bacillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪˌfɔɪd bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.fɔɪd bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
a bacterium (Salmonella typhi) that causes typhoid fever in humans
bacterium causing typhoid fever
medical

Examples

The typhoid bacillus can spread through contaminated food and water, leading to outbreaks in areas with poor sanitation.
Scientists were able to identify the typhoid bacillus under the microscope and confirm the diagnosis.
Proper hygiene and vaccination are effective in preventing infection by the typhoid bacillus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salmonella - genus of bacteria including typhoid bacillus

Etymology

From 'typhoid' (relating to typhus-like fever) and Latin 'bacillus' (small rod), due to the shape of the bacterium.
