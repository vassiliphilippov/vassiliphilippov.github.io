false azalea

1. NOUN

/ fɔls əˈzeɪliə / (AmE), / fɔːls əˈzeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A straggling shrub native to northwestern North America, with bluish-tinged leaves and clusters of small bell-shaped flowers.
Northwestern North American shrub with bluish foliage

Examples

The false azalea blooms in spring, its bell-shaped flowers attracting local pollinators.
Botanists surveyed a valley where false azalea grew among fir and hemlock trees.
Gardeners appreciate the distinctive blue-green leaves of false azalea in their woodland borders.
