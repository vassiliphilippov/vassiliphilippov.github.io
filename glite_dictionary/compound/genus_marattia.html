genus Marattia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs məˈrætiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məˈrætiə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the family Marattiaceae, characterized by sporangia fused together in two rows.
genus of ferns (Marattiaceae) with fused sporangia

Examples

The genus Marattia includes some of the largest fern species.
Botanists studied the unique sporangial structure of the genus Marattia.
Fossils of the genus Marattia have been found in several parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marattiaceae - family of ferns including genus Marattia

Etymology

From Latin 'Marattia', named after Italian botanist Giovanni Francesco Maratti.
