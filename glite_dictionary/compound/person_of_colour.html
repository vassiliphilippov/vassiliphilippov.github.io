person of colour

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜːrsn əv ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsn əv ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A person who is not white or of European descent, especially in societies where whiteness is the majority or norm.
non-white person; not of European descent

Examples

The company has launched new initiatives to support persons of colour in the workplace.
She is the first person of colour to serve as mayor in the city's history.
Many museums are working to better represent people of colour in their collections and staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: person of color - Alternative (primarily American) spelling

Etymology

From 'person' + 'of colour', with 'of colour' recorded in this sense from the late 18th century in North America.
