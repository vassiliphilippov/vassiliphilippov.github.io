Virginia pine

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə paɪn / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniə paɪn / (BrE)
a small, shrubby pine tree native to the eastern United States, with twisted branches and short needles in pairs
a pine tree species of eastern U.S.

Examples

The Virginia pine thrives in poor clay soils where few other trees can survive.
We planted several Virginia pines along the edge of our property for a natural windbreak.
The twisted branches of the Virginia pine make it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub pine - another name for a small or shrubby pine tree

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Virginia, where it is common.
