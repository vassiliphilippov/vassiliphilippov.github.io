big dipper

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a roller coaster in an amusement park, typically featuring sharp curves and steep inclines
amusement park roller coaster
british

Examples

We screamed as the big dipper raced down another steep drop.
The amusement park built a new big dipper that opened last summer.
Riding the big dipper was the highlight of their day at the fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roller coaster - an amusement ride with steep slopes and sharp turns

Etymology

From the resemblance to the up-and-down movement associated with the constellation 'Big Dipper.'
