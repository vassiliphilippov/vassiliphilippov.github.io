wool grease

1. NOUN

/ wʊl ɡris / (AmE), / wʊl ɡriːs / (BrE)
A yellow, viscous animal oil extracted from raw wool, consisting mainly of fatty acids and esters, often used in ointments and cosmetics.
oil extracted from wool, used in ointments

Examples

The ointment contains purified wool grease to help soften skin.
Lanolin is derived from wool grease obtained during processing of sheep's wool.
Some traditional soaps are made with wool grease as a moisturizing ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanolin - a refined form of wool grease used in cosmetics and ointments
