vena metacarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪ.nə ˌmɛ.təˈkɑr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌviː.nə ˌmɛ.təˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
Any of the dorsal and palmar veins located in the hand.
veins of the hand
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the vena metacarpus during the procedure.
In anatomy class, students must be able to locate the vena metacarpus on a diagram of the hand.
Damage to the vena metacarpus can lead to swelling and impaired circulation in the hand.

Etymology

From Latin vena (vein) and metacarpus (the part of the hand between the wrist and fingers).
