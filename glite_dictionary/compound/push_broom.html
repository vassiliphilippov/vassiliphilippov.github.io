push broom

1. NOUN

/ pʊʃ bruːm / (AmE), / pʊʃ bruːm / (BrE)
A wide broom with a long handle that is designed to be pushed ahead to clean floors or large areas.
wide floor broom pushed ahead

Examples

The janitor used a push broom to sweep the dust from the hallway.
After the parade, volunteers brought out push brooms to clean up the streets.
Our garage gets dirty quickly, so I keep a push broom handy for easy sweeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a cleaning tool with a long handle and bristles, for sweeping
