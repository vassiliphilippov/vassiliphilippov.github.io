Sacramento River

1. PROPN

/ ˌsækrəˈmɛntoʊ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəʊ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in northern California that rises near Mount Shasta and flows south to the San Francisco Bay
major river in northern California
american

Examples

Sacramento River is the longest river entirely within California, stretching nearly 400 miles.
The city of Sacramento sits at the confluence of the American River and the Sacramento River.
Salmon migrate each year up the Sacramento River to their spawning grounds.

Etymology

Named after the Spanish "Río del Sacramento," referring to the Blessed Sacrament.
