Danish language

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.nɪʃ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.nɪʃ ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the official Scandinavian language spoken mainly in Denmark
official language of Denmark

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Danish language.
The Danish language uses the Latin alphabet with three additional letters.
Many historical documents are preserved in the Danish language in Denmark's archives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norwegian language - the official language of Norway, closely related to Danish
  • Synonym: Swedish language - the official language of Sweden, another closely related Scandinavian language

Etymology

From Danish 'dansk' (Danish) + English 'language', with roots in Old Norse.
