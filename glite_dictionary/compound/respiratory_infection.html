respiratory infection

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛspɪˌrɛtɔri ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrət(ə)ri ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection affecting any part of the respiratory tract, such as the nose, throat, airways, or lungs
an infection of the respiratory tract
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a respiratory infection after she developed a persistent cough and fever.
Many common colds are actually caused by mild respiratory infections.
Wearing a mask can help prevent the spread of a respiratory infection in public spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respiratory tract - the passageways that carry air to and from the lungs
  • Degree Lower: upper respiratory infection - infection in the upper part of the respiratory tract (nose, throat)
  • Degree Higher: lower respiratory infection - infection in the lower part of the respiratory tract (lungs, bronchi)

Etymology

respiratory from Latin respirare (to breathe), infection from Latin infectio (a staining, infection)
