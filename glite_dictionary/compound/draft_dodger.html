draft dodger

1. NOUN

/ dræft ˈdɑdʒɚ / (AmE), / drɑːft ˈdɒdʒə / (BrE)
a person who illegally avoids being drafted into military service
one who illegally avoids military draft

Examples

Many Americans became draft dodgers by fleeing to Canada during the Vietnam War.
The government cracked down on draft dodgers during times of national conflict.
He was labeled a draft dodger after refusing to report for military service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscientious objector - someone who refuses military service for moral or religious reasons
