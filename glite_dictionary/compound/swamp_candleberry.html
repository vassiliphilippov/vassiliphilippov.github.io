swamp candleberry

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈkændlˌbɛri / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈkændlˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous, aromatic shrub native to eastern North America, known for its grey-green, wax-coated berries.
aromatic shrub with wax-coated berries

Examples

The woods along the river were filled with swamp candleberry, its fragrant scent easily noticeable in the air.
She collected wax-coated berries from the swamp candleberry to make homemade candles.
During the summer, the swamp candleberry bushes attract many songbirds seeking food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bayberry - a shrub with aromatic leaves and waxy berries, often used for candles
