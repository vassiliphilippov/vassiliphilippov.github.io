tangelo tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtændʒəˌloʊ tri / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪləʊ triː / (BrE)
A tree that is a hybrid between a grapefruit and a mandarin orange, grown especially for its fruit called tangelos.
tree hybrid of grapefruit and mandarin orange

Examples

The farmer planted a row of tangelo trees along the southern edge of his orchard.
A healthy tangelo tree can yield dozens of sweet fruits each season.
Researchers are developing new varieties of tangelo trees resistant to citrus diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangelo - the fruit produced by the tangelo tree, a hybrid of tangerine and pomelo/grapefruit

Etymology

Blend of 'tangerine' and 'pomelo', with 'tree' appended.
