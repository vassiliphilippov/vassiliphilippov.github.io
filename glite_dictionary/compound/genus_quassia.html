genus Quassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kwəˈsiː.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kwɒˈsiː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees and shrubs with pinnate leaves and large scarlet flowers, some of whose bark is used medicinally.
genus of tropical trees with medicinal bark

Examples

The bark of some trees in the genus Quassia is valued for its medicinal properties.
Researchers are studying the potential anti-malarial compounds found in the genus Quassia.
Many species in the genus Quassia produce striking scarlet flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Quassia,' named after Graman Quassi, an 18th-century Surinamese botanist and healer.
