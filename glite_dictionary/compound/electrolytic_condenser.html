electrolytic condenser

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈlɪtɪk kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəˈlɪtɪk kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a fixed capacitor in which two electrodes are separated by an electrolyte, commonly used in electronic circuits
fixed capacitor with electrodes and electrolyte

Examples

The circuit diagram shows an electrolytic condenser as the main component for energy storage.
Early radios often used an electrolytic condenser to filter out electrical noise.
Ensure that the polarity is correct when installing an electrolytic condenser in a power supply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrolytic capacitor - a capacitor that uses an electrolyte; modern term for 'electrolytic condenser'

Etymology

From 'electrolytic' (relating to electrolysis) and 'condenser', an early term for 'capacitor' in electronics.
