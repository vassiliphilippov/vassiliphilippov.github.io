carbonic acid gas

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈbɑnɪk ˈæsɪd ɡæs / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒnɪk ˈæsɪd ɡas / (BrE)
an older term for carbon dioxide; a heavy, odorless, colorless gas formed during respiration and the decomposition of organic substances, and absorbed by plants during photosynthesis
older name for carbon dioxide (CO₂)

Examples

The scientist demonstrated that carbonic acid gas is exhaled during human respiration.
In early experiments, Joseph Priestley studied the properties of carbonic acid gas.
Plants remove carbonic acid gas from the atmosphere during photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon dioxide - a colorless, odorless gas (CO₂) produced by burning carbon and organic compounds

Etymology

Refers to the gas formed by or associated with carbonic acid; historically used before 'carbon dioxide' became standard.
