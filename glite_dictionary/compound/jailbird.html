jailbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪl.bɝd / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪl.bɜːd / (BrE)
a person who has been in jail repeatedly, especially a habitual criminal
habitual criminal, frequent prisoner

Examples

After years in and out of prison, he became known as a notorious jailbird in the community.
The old jailbird tried to keep his head down after his latest release.
Stories about jailbirds always fascinated crime reporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convict - person found guilty of a criminal offense
  • Synonym: criminal - person who commits crimes

Etymology

Formed from 'jail' + 'bird' (early 17th c.), likening a prisoner to a caged bird.
