incandescent lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt læmp / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt læmp / (BrE)
an electric light in which a wire filament, usually made of tungsten, emits light when heated by an electric current inside a glass enclosure
electric lamp with glowing filament

Examples

The old desk was illuminated by an incandescent lamp that gave off a warm, yellowish light.
Replacing an incandescent lamp with an LED bulb can reduce energy consumption significantly.
Because of its inefficiency, the incandescent lamp has been largely phased out in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light bulb - a device that produces light from electricity, commonly used in homes; can refer to various types, including incandescent

Etymology

From Latin incandescere (to glow) and lampas (lamp).
