theory of evolution

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪri əv ˌiːvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˌiːvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the scientific explanation that living organisms have developed and diversified from earlier forms during the history of the earth, particularly through the process of natural selection
scientific explanation of species development

Examples

Charles Darwin is often credited with formulating the theory of evolution by natural selection.
Many biologists rely on the theory of evolution to explain the diversity of life on Earth.
The theory of evolution has been supported by evidence from genetics, paleontology, and comparative anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolution - gradual development of living things over time
