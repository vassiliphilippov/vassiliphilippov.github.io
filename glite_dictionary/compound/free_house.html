free house

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriː ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈfriː ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A public house that is not owned or controlled by a particular brewery and is free to sell products from any supplier.
pub not owned by a brewery
british

Examples

He prefers to drink at a free house because of the wider choice of beers.
The new owners turned the pub into a free house last year.
Many free houses offer local craft ales alongside traditional favorites.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tied house - a pub owned or controlled by a brewery and obliged to sell its products
