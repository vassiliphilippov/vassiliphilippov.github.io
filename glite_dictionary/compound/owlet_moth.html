owlet moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊlɪt mʌθ / (AmE), / ˈaʊlɪt mɒθ / (BrE)
A usually dull-colored, medium-sized, nocturnal moth of the family Noctuidae, whose larvae are often agricultural pests.
medium-sized nocturnal moth; Noctuidae family

Examples

Many farmers are concerned about owlet moth larvae damaging their crops during the growing season.
The owlet moth is mostly active at night and is attracted to outdoor lights.
Biologists often collect data on owlet moth populations to monitor environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noctuid - member of the Noctuidae family of moths
  • Degree Higher: moth - insect related to butterflies, usually nocturnal

Etymology

From 'owlet', diminutive of 'owl', due to some species having wing markings resembling owl eyes.
