thyme-leaved sandwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪm liːvd ˈsændˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm liːvd ˈsændˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a small sprawling Eurasian annual plant of the pink family, naturalized throughout North America, with leaves resembling those of thyme
sprawling Eurasian annual plant

Examples

The thyme-leaved sandwort thrives in rocky outcrops and sandy soils across much of North America.
We identified several thyme-leaved sandwort plants during our botanical survey last spring.
Because of its sprawling growth habit, thyme-leaved sandwort often forms low mats in gardens and meadows.
