herb of grace

1. NOUN

/ hɜrb əv ɡreɪs / (AmE), / hɜːb əv ɡreɪs / (BrE)
A European perennial herb (Ruta graveolens) with strong-scented, grey-green, bitter-tasting leaves, sometimes causing skin irritation.
a strong-scented European perennial herb

Examples

The gardener pointed to the herb of grace growing near the old stone wall.
Some traditional recipes call for a pinch of herb of grace for its bitterness.
She wore a sprig of herb of grace as a symbol of remembrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rue - A strong-scented perennial herb, Ruta graveolens

Etymology

Named as 'herb of grace' from its historical association with religious or medicinal use; commonly refers to rue (Ruta graveolens).
