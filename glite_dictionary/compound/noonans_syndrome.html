Noonan's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːnənz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈnuːnənz ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a genetic disorder characterized by short stature, distinctive facial features such as low-set ears, and often reduced fertility; affects both males and females
genetic disorder with facial and growth features
medical

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the boy had Noonan's syndrome after observing his distinctive facial features.
Some children with Noonan's syndrome may require special care due to their heart problems.
Genetic counseling is often recommended for families affected by Noonan's syndrome.

Etymology

Named after American pediatrician Jacqueline Noonan, who first described the condition.
