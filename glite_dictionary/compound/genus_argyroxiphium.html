genus Argyroxiphium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrdʒi.oʊˈzɪfiˌəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːdʒi.ɒkˈsɪfi.əm / (BrE)
A small genus of Hawaiian shrubs known for their spreading and rosette-forming growth habits, commonly called 'silverswords.'
genus of Hawaiian rosette-forming shrubs

Examples

The genus Argyroxiphium is famous for its rare 'silversword' plants native to Hawaii.
Researchers studied the unique adaptations of the genus Argyroxiphium to harsh volcanic environments.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting species within the genus Argyroxiphium from extinction.

Etymology

From Greek 'argyros' (silver) and 'xiphium' (sword), referring to the silvery sword-shaped leaves.
