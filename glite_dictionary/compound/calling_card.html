calling card

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlɪŋ ˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlɪŋ ˌkɑːd / (BrE)
a prepaid card that is used in place of cash to pay for telephone calls, especially from public phones or long-distance.
prepaid card for making telephone calls

Examples

He bought a calling card at the store before making an international call.
Many travelers use a calling card to save money on long-distance charges.
You can't make this call without entering the number from your calling card.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlɪŋ kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlɪŋ kɑːd / (BrE)
a small card bearing a person's name, left as a greeting or proof of visit, especially in social or formal contexts
greeting card left to indicate a visit

Examples

In Victorian times, a gentleman would always leave his calling card at a friend's home if they were not in.
She found a calling card on her doorstep, a sign that someone had visited.
After visiting her neighbor, she left a calling card to let them know she had stopped by.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlɪŋ kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlɪŋ kɑːd / (BrE)
Something that serves as a distinctive means of identification or introduction, especially one that is characteristic of a person or organization.
means of identification or introduction

Examples

Her sharp wit became her calling card in the world of stand-up comedy.
The detective left his unique calling card at every crime scene.
Their innovative design has become the company's calling card in the market.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trademark - a distinctive characteristic or feature identifying someone or something
  • Synonym: hallmark - a distinguishing characteristic or feature

Etymology

Originally referred to a small card with one's name, left as a visitor; now also used figuratively.
