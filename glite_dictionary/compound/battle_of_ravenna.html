Battle of Ravenna

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæɾl̩ əv rəˈvɛnə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv rəˈvɛnə / (BrE)
A major battle fought in 1512 between French forces and an alliance of Spaniards, Swiss, and Venetians during the Italian Wars.
1512 battle in Italian Wars

Examples

The Battle of Ravenna marked a turning point in the Italian Wars.
Historians often analyze tactics used in the Battle of Ravenna to study early modern warfare.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the bloody Battle of Ravenna in 1512.

Etymology

From the city of Ravenna in Italy, where the battle took place.
