souari nut

1. NOUN

/ suˈɑːri nʌt / (AmE), / suˈɑːri nʌt / (BrE)
a large edible nut-like seed from a South American tree, mainly used for food or oil
large edible seed from South America

Examples

The souari nut is prized for its rich flavor and nutritional value in many South American cuisines.
She bought several souari nuts at the market to use in traditional desserts.
Oil extracted from the souari nut is often used in gourmet cooking.

Etymology

From French 'souari', from the name of the Guiana tree producing the nut.

2. NOUN

/ suˈɑri nʌt / (AmE), / suˈɑːri nʌt / (BrE)
a large South American evergreen tree with trifoliate leaves, producing drupes with edible nutlike seeds used as food and for cooking oil
South American tree with edible seeds

Examples

The souari nut is often pressed to extract flavorful cooking oil.
Many dishes in Brazil use roasted souari nuts as a key ingredient.
The souari nut tree thrives in the humid forests of northern South America.

Etymology

From French 'souari', from a native South American (Cariban) word.
