theory of weak interaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪəri əv ˈwiːk ˌɪntərˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˈwiːk ˌɪntərˈækʃən / (BrE)
the scientific theory that explains the weak force, one of the four fundamental forces of nature responsible for processes like beta decay in atomic nuclei
physics theory about the weak force

Examples

The theory of weak interaction is essential for understanding certain types of radioactive decay.
Physicists developed the theory of weak interaction to explain phenomena not covered by the electromagnetic or strong forces.
Fermi was one of the pioneers in formulating the theory of weak interaction.

Synset Examples

Fermi's ‘theory of β rays’ or, as it came to be called, the theory of weak interaction, was decisively advanced by two experimental discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroweak theory - the theory unifying weak and electromagnetic interactions
