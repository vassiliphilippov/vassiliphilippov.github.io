striate body

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪ.eɪt ˈbɑːdi / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.eɪt ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a mass of white and grey matter in the brain, located in front of the thalamus in each cerebral hemisphere, consisting of the caudate nucleus and lenticular nucleus
striped mass of brain tissue (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The striate body plays a crucial role in regulating voluntary movement.
Damage to the striate body can lead to disorders such as Parkinson's disease.
Research on the striate body has advanced our understanding of motor control in the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basal ganglia - group of nuclei in the brain associated with movement regulation

Etymology

From Latin 'striatus' (striped) and 'body' (referring to its striped appearance).
