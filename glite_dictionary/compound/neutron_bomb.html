neutron bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.trɑn bɑm / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.trɒn bɒm / (BrE)
a type of nuclear weapon that produces a large amount of lethal neutron radiation but causes relatively little blast damage to structures
nuclear weapon emitting lethal neutron radiation

Examples

The military discussed deploying a neutron bomb to maximize radiation effects while minimizing structural destruction.
Some activists protested the development of the neutron bomb because of its potential for mass casualties.
Unlike traditional nuclear weapons, a neutron bomb intentionally releases a higher proportion of deadly radiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic bomb - a bomb that derives its destructive power from nuclear reactions
  • Degree Higher: hydrogen bomb - a bomb more powerful than an atomic bomb, using fusion of hydrogen isotopes

Etymology

From 'neutron', a subatomic particle, and 'bomb'; term coined in the mid-20th century.
