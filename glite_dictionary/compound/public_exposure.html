public exposure

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of making something widely known or subject to public discussion and debate
making something widely known

Examples

The scandal received public exposure after journalists published the leaked documents.
Without public exposure, the issue might never have been addressed by lawmakers.
The artist's work gained significant public exposure after being displayed at the national gallery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publicity - the state of being public or widely known
  • Antonym: secrecy - the state of being kept secret or hidden
