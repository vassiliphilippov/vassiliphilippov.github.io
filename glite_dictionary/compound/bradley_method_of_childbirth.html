Bradley method of childbirth

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrædli ˌmɛθəd əv ˈtʃaɪldbɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈbrædli ˌmɛθəd əv ˈtʃaɪldbɜːθ / (BrE)
a method of preparing for natural childbirth through education, exercises, nutrition, breathing techniques, and relaxation, with emphasis on the father's involvement
natural childbirth preparation method
medical

Examples

Many couples choose the Bradley method of childbirth to have a more natural labor experience.
Prenatal classes in the Bradley method of childbirth focus on relaxation and the father's supportive role.
The Bradley method of childbirth emphasizes nutrition and breathing techniques during pregnancy and delivery.

Etymology

Named after Dr. Robert A. Bradley, who developed the method in the 1940s.
