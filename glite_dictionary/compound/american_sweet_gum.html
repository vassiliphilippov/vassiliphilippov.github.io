American sweet gum

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛrɪkən swit ɡʌm / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪkən swiːt ɡʌm / (BrE)
a North American deciduous tree (Liquidambar styraciflua) with star-shaped leaves, prickly spherical fruit clusters, and fragrant resinous sap
North American tree with prickly fruit and fragrant sap

Examples

The American sweet gum is known for its striking star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant colors in autumn.
Sticky sap from the American sweet gum was once used as a chewing gum substitute by Native Americans.
Landscapers often plant American sweet gum trees in parks for their ornamental value and shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet gum - deciduous tree of the genus Liquidambar with fragrant resin
