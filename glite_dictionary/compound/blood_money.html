blood money

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈmʌni / (AmE), / blʌd ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money paid as compensation to the family of someone who has been killed, especially in cultures or legal systems where such payments are customary.
compensation for a killing

Examples

The family refused to accept blood money for the loss of their son.
In some legal systems, blood money may be offered as an alternative to criminal prosecution.
The tradition of paying blood money has existed in various cultures throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - payment for loss or suffering

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌd ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈblʌd ˌmʌni / (BrE)
money paid as a reward for information leading to the capture or conviction of a murderer.
reward for information about a murderer

Examples

The police offered blood money to anyone with information about the suspect's whereabouts.
Rumors spread that someone had claimed the blood money after turning in the fugitive.
He refused to accept blood money, feeling it was wrong to profit from another's death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bounty - a sum paid for capturing or killing a person

Etymology

Originally referred to compensation paid to the family of a murder victim; 'blood' refers to the crime, 'money' to the payment.

3. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈmʌni / (AmE), / blʌd ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money paid to a hired killer for committing a murder
money paid to a hired murderer

Examples

The assassin was offered a suitcase full of blood money for the job.
He refused to take blood money for the contract killing, despite the large sum.
She was haunted by the knowledge that her wealth was built on blood money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - an act of murder carried out for payment
  • Degree Lower: bribe - money paid to influence someone's actions, especially illegally
  • Degree Higher: bounty - money offered as a reward for bringing someone in, often dead or alive

Etymology

From the literal association of 'blood' with killing, and 'money' as payment; usage attested since the 16th century.
