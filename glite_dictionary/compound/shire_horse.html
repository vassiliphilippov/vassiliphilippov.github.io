shire horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪɚ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪə hɔːs / (BrE)
A large, strong British breed of draft horse, known for its size and power, traditionally used for heavy work such as pulling carts and plows.
large British draft horse breed
british

Examples

The shire horse pulled the wagon effortlessly across the muddy field.
At the agricultural fair, children marveled at the size of the shire horse.
Breeders take pride in maintaining the pure bloodlines of the shire horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft horse - a large horse bred for heavy work

Etymology

From 'Shire', a traditional term for a county in England, where the breed originated.
