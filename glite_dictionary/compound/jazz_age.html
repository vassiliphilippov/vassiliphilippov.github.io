Jazz Age

1. NOUN

/ dʒæz eɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒæz eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period of the 1920s in the United States, marked by prosperity, youthful energy, and the popularity of jazz music; often associated with social change and the works of F. Scott Fitzgerald
the 1920s era of prosperity and jazz in the US

Examples

F. Scott Fitzgerald's novels capture the spirit of the Jazz Age perfectly.
The Jazz Age was a time of dramatic social and cultural change in America.
Many parties during the Jazz Age were filled with lively music and dancing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roaring Twenties - another term for the 1920s, especially in the US and Europe

Etymology

Named for the popularity of jazz music during this period.
