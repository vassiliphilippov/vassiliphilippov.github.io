English elm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɛlm / (BrE)
A broad, spreading species of elm (Ulmus procera) native to Europe, commonly found throughout Europe and widely planted elsewhere.
a species of elm tree native to Europe

Examples

The English elm is known for its tall, graceful shape and dense foliage.
Many historic parks in Britain are lined with English elms dating back centuries.
Diseases such as Dutch elm disease have greatly reduced the number of English elms across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a deciduous tree with serrated leaves and winged seeds
