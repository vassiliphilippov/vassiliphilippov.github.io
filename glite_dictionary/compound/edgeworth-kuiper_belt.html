Edgeworth-Kuiper belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdʒˌwɜrθ ˈkaɪpər bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒwəθ ˈkaɪpə bɛlt / (BrE)
A disk-shaped region of small celestial bodies beyond the orbit of Neptune, believed to be the source of many comets.
region of minor planets beyond Neptune

Examples

The Edgeworth-Kuiper belt is home to many icy minor planets orbiting our Sun.
Astronomers believe Pluto is one of the largest objects in the Edgeworth-Kuiper belt.
The study of the Edgeworth-Kuiper belt has helped scientists understand the formation of the solar system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asteroid belt - the region between Mars and Jupiter containing many small rocky bodies

Etymology

Named after astronomers Kenneth Edgeworth and Gerard Kuiper, who theorized its existence.
