black medick

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk ˌmɛd.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈblæk ˌmɛd.ɪk / (BrE)
a low-growing European plant with small yellow flowers and curved black seed pods, commonly naturalized in North America
low prostrate European herb with yellow flowers

Examples

A patch of black medick was thriving along the edge of the footpath.
You can recognize black medick by its tiny yellow flowers and distinctive black seed pods.
Farmers often find black medick spreading in pastures and lawns throughout spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop clover - a related plant with small yellow flowers

Etymology

From the black color of its seed pods and Medieval Latin *medica* (a kind of clover).
