garden sorrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial herb (Rumex acetosa) with slightly acidic, arrow-shaped leaves, cultivated for use in salads and spring greens
edible leafy herb with acidic taste

Examples

She added fresh garden sorrel to the salad for a tangy flavor.
In spring, garden sorrel is one of the first herbs to appear in the garden.
The chef used chopped garden sorrel as a garnish for the fish dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - an edible herb with acidic-tasting leaves

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a low perennial plant (Rumex acetosa) with small, silvery-green leaves that are ovate to hastate, used as a culinary herb or salad green
a leafy perennial herb used in salads

Examples

She added fresh garden sorrel to the salad for a tangy flavor.
The leaves of garden sorrel are often used in French cuisine.
We planted garden sorrel in the herb patch next to the parsley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - a plant with sour-tasting, edible leaves
