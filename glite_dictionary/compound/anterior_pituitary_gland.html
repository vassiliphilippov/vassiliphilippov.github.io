anterior pituitary gland

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪriɚ pɪˈtjuːəˌtɛri ɡlænd / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə pɪˈtʃuːɪtəri ɡlænd / (BrE)
the front part of the pituitary gland, responsible for producing several important hormones
front lobe of pituitary gland
medical

Examples

The anterior pituitary gland secretes hormones that regulate growth and metabolism.
Tumors in the anterior pituitary gland can disrupt hormone balance in the body.
Doctors tested her anterior pituitary gland function to diagnose her endocrine disorder.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posterior pituitary gland - the back lobe of the pituitary gland

Etymology

From Latin *anterior* (before, in front) and *pituitarius* (related to phlegm), referring to the gland's position and function.
