silicon oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪlɪˌkɑn ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɪlɪkən ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white or colorless, vitreous, insoluble solid (SiO₂) occurring widely in the earth's crust in various forms, such as quartz and cristobalite
a chemical compound (SiO₂); quartz, sand, etc.

Examples

The majority of beach sand is composed of silicon oxide in the form of quartz grains.
Laboratory glassware is often made from fused silicon oxide due to its high melting point.
Engineers studied how impurities in silicon oxide could affect the performance of electronic devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz - a hard, crystalline mineral consisting of silicon dioxide (SiO₂)
  • Synonym: silica - another name for silicon dioxide, especially in mineral form

Etymology

From 'silicon' (element name) and 'oxide' (compound with oxygen), following scientific naming conventions.
