Linear B

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪniər ˈbi / (AmE), / ˌlɪniə ˈbiː / (BrE)
an ancient syllabic script used in Greece, chiefly for writing Mycenaean Greek, during the 13th century B.C.
ancient Greek syllabic script

Examples

The discovery of Linear B tablets revolutionized our understanding of Mycenaean Greece.
Michael Ventris is famous for deciphering the Linear B script in the 1950s.
Many records in Linear B were found at the palace of Knossos.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Linear A - an undeciphered ancient script of Crete, predecessor of Linear B

Etymology

Named after the linear form of the script and distinguished from the related Linear A; 'B' denotes its sequence of discovery.
