Hansen's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈhænsənz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈhansənz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A chronic infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium leprae, marked by skin lesions, nerve damage, and wasting of body parts; also known as leprosy.
leprosy; chronic infectious disease
medical

Examples

Early diagnosis and treatment of Hansen's disease can prevent permanent disability.
Stigma surrounding Hansen's disease has decreased due to better public understanding.
The clinic specializes in treating patients with Hansen's disease from rural communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leprosy - An infectious disease causing skin lesions and nerve damage; another name for Hansen's disease.

Etymology

Named after Gerhard Armauer Hansen, Norwegian physician who identified the causative bacterium in 1873.
