Tyne River

1. PROPN

/ taɪn ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / taɪn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in northern England that flows eastward to the North Sea, passing through Newcastle upon Tyne
river in northern England

Examples

The Tyne River is famous for its iconic bridges in Newcastle.
Several rowing clubs host annual events on the Tyne River.
Artists often paint the scenic banks of the Tyne River at sunset.

Etymology

From Old English *Tīn*, of Celtic origin, possibly meaning 'river' or 'stream'.
