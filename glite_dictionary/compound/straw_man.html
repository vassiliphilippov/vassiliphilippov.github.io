straw man

1. NOUN

/ strɔː mæn / (AmE), / strɔː mæn / (BrE)
an object in the shape of a human figure, typically made of straw and old clothes, placed in fields to scare away birds from crops
figure made of straw to scare birds

Examples

The farmer built a straw man to protect his cornfield from crows.
Several straw men stood in the field, swaying gently in the wind.
As a child, she was afraid of the old straw man near the vegetable patch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarecrow - an object in the shape of a person, set up to scare birds from crops

2. NOUN

/ strɔː mæn / (AmE), / strɔː mæn / (BrE)
A man who is used as a front or cover for illegal, fraudulent, or questionable activities.
man used as a front for illicit activities

Examples

He was just a straw man for the criminal operation, with no real power.
The company used a straw man to hide their involvement in the fraudulent scheme.
Investigators discovered that the land was registered under a straw man to conceal the true owner's identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front man - a person who represents an organization or activity but is not the real decision-maker

Etymology

Originally from the literal use of 'straw man' as a figure made of straw, extended metaphorically to mean someone lacking real power or substance.

3. NOUN

/ strɔ mæn / (AmE), / strɔː mæn / (BrE)
an argument or position that is misrepresented in order to be easily criticized or disproved
misrepresented argument for easy refutation

Examples

The politician attacked a straw man instead of addressing her opponent's actual points.
By creating a straw man, he avoided dealing with the real issue in the debate.
Critics accused her of knocking down a straw man argument rather than responding seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red herring - a misleading clue or argument that diverts attention
  • Synonym: argumentum ad hominem - an argument attacking the person rather than the position

Etymology

From the image of a man made of straw, which can be easily knocked down; used since the 19th century in figurative argument.

4. NOUN

/ strɔː mæn / (AmE), / strɔː mæn / (BrE)
An argument or position that is misrepresented in order to be easily attacked or refuted, thereby diverting attention from the real issue.
misrepresentation of an argument to attack it

Examples

The politician set up a straw man by exaggerating the proposal before tearing it down.
Debaters often attack a straw man instead of addressing their opponent's actual position.
It's unfair to misrepresent my argument as a straw man just to dismiss it.

Synset Examples

He argued against a straw man to avoid the real issue.
Don't create a straw man to distract from our goals.
Her opponent used a straw man argument during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red herring - something that misleads or distracts from the relevant issue

Etymology

Originally from the literal 'straw man' (a figure made of straw used as a dummy); the figurative sense developed in the 20th century to describe a rhetorical technique.
