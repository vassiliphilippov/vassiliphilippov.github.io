autocratic leadership style

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈkrætɪk ˈlidərˌʃɪp staɪl / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪk ˈliːdəʃɪp staɪl / (BrE)
a leadership approach in which one person holds absolute authority and makes decisions without input from others
leadership style with one-person authority

Examples

The manager's autocratic leadership style often discouraged employees from voicing their opinions.
Due to his autocratic leadership style, important decisions were made without consulting the team.
Over time, the company shifted away from an autocratic leadership style to encourage more collaboration.

Synset Examples

His autocratic leadership style alienated the team.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: democratic leadership style - a leadership style that involves group participation in decisions
  • Antonym: laissez-faire leadership style - a leadership style with minimal leader intervention

Etymology

From 'autocratic' (Greek 'autokratēs' meaning 'ruling by oneself') + 'leadership style'.
