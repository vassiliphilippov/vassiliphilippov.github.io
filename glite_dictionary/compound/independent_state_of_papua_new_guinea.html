Independent State of Papua New Guinea

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪndəˈpɛndənt steɪt əv ˌpæpjuː nə ɡɪˈniː / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt steɪt əv ˌpæpjʊ ə njuː ˈɡiːni / (BrE)
the official name of Papua New Guinea, a parliamentary democracy occupying the eastern half of the island of New Guinea in the southwest Pacific Ocean, which became an independent state within the Commonwealth of Nations in 1975
official name of Papua New Guinea

Examples

The Prime Minister of the Independent State of Papua New Guinea addressed the United Nations General Assembly.
Since 1975, the Independent State of Papua New Guinea has maintained its status as a member of the Commonwealth.
Trade agreements between Australia and the Independent State of Papua New Guinea have strengthened economic relations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Papua New Guinea - a country in the southwestern Pacific occupying the eastern half of New Guinea
