Jemaah Islamiyah

1. PROPN

/ dʒəˈmɑ ɪsˌlɑːmiˈjɑː / (AmE), / dʒəˈmɑː ɪsˌlɑːmiˈjɑː / (BrE)
A secretive Southeast Asian militant Islamist group formed in 1993, known for its involvement in terrorist activities, particularly in Indonesia, the Philippines, Singapore, and Malaysia.
Southeast Asian militant Islamist group

Examples

The government increased security measures after the Jemaah Islamiyah attacks in Bali.
Several countries in Southeast Asia collaborated to dismantle Jemaah Islamiyah networks.
Reports suggest that Jemaah Islamiyah remains active despite recent setbacks.

Etymology

From Indonesian and Malay, literally meaning 'Islamic Group'.
