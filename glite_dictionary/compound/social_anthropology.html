social anthropology

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.ʃəl ænˌθrɑː.pəˈlɑː.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ʃəl ænˌθrɒ.pəˈlɒ.dʒi / (BrE)
The branch of anthropology that studies human societies, institutions, and cultures, especially as they relate to social relationships and structures.
study of human societies and cultures

Examples

She decided to specialize in social anthropology after taking an introductory course at university.
Social anthropology provides insights into how different societies organize their communities and rituals.
The research in social anthropology often involves long-term fieldwork among various groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultural anthropology - the branch of anthropology focusing on cultural variation among humans
  • Antonym: physical anthropology - the branch of anthropology concerned with biological and physical development of humans

Etymology

From Latin 'sociālis' (of society) and 'anthropologia' (study of humanity).
