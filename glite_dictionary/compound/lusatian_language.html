Lusatian language

1. NOUN

/ luːˈseɪʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / luːˈseɪʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a West Slavic language spoken by the Sorbian people in southeastern Germany, particularly in Lusatia
West Slavic language of Lusatia, Germany

Examples

The Lusatian language is taught at local schools in the region to preserve cultural heritage.
She is fluent in both German and Lusatian language due to her Sorbian background.
Efforts to revive the Lusatian language have increased in recent years among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sorbian - any of two closely related Slavic languages spoken in Lusatia

Etymology

From Latin Lusatia (the region in Germany) + English 'language'.
