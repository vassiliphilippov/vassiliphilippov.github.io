alizarine red

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzəriːn rɛd / (AmE), / əˈlɪzəriːn rɛd / (BrE)
a bright orange-red color produced by dyeing with alizarine, often used in textiles
a bright orange-red dye color

Examples

The vibrant curtains were dyed a deep alizarine red that added warmth to the room.
She preferred alizarine red over other shades for her cotton dresses.
The artist chose alizarine red to capture the intensity of the sunset in his painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alizarin - orange-red dye originally made from madder root

Etymology

From 'alizarine', which itself is from Arabic 'al ʿaṣārah' (juice or extract), referring to the dye, plus 'red'.
