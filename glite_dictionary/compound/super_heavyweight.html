super heavyweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpɚ ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈsuːpə ˈhɛviweɪt / (BrE)
A weight division in combat sports for competitors who exceed the upper weight limit of the heavyweight class, typically the heaviest category.
heaviest division in combat sports

Examples

He competed as a super heavyweight at the Olympic Games.
The super heavyweight title bout drew a large audience.
She became the national super heavyweight champion after a stunning victory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: heavyweight - the weight class below super heavyweight in combat sports

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpər ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˌsuːpə ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (BrE)
an amateur boxer who weighs more than 201 pounds (91 kg), competing in the heaviest weight class
amateur boxer over 201 pounds

Examples

He competed as a super heavyweight in the national boxing championship.
The super heavyweight division is known for its powerful competitors.
Olympic boxing includes a super heavyweight category for the largest fighters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: heavyweight - a boxer in the weight class below super heavyweight
