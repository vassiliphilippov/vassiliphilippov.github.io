chain store

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪn stɔr / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn stɔː / (BrE)
a retail store that is one of a group of stores owned and operated by the same company, usually selling the same merchandise
a store in a retail chain

Examples

The town's new chain store offers the same products as its other locations nationwide.
Small retailers sometimes struggle to compete with a large chain store in the area.
She prefers shopping at local markets instead of a chain store.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independent store - a store not part of a retail chain, managed independently
  • Synonym: franchise - a store operating under the brand of a larger company but owned by an individual or business

Etymology

From 'chain' (a connected series) + 'store' (retail shop); term arose in early 20th-century American retail.
