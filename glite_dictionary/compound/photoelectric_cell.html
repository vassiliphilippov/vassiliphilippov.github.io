photoelectric cell

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk sɛl / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk sɛl / (BrE)
A device that detects and measures light or other electromagnetic radiation by converting it into electrical energy.
device detecting/measuring light with electricity

Examples

The security system uses a photoelectric cell to detect any movement in the hallway.
A photoelectric cell is often found in automatic lighting systems that respond to changes in daylight.
Scientists measured the intensity of the laser beam with a sensitive photoelectric cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocell - a device that generates an electrical signal in response to light

Etymology

From 'photo-' meaning light and 'electric' from electricity, indicating its function to convert light into electrical energy.
