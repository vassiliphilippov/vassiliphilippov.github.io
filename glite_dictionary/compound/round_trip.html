round trip

1. NOUN

/ raʊnd trɪp / (AmE), / raʊnd trɪp / (BrE)
a journey to a place and back again to the starting point
a journey to a place and back

Examples

She booked a round trip ticket to Paris for her vacation.
A round trip from New York to Boston takes about eight hours by car.
The special offer includes meals and a round trip airfare.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one-way - a journey to a place without returning

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd ˌtrɪp / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd ˌtrɪp / (BrE)
a journey from one place to a destination and back again, usually returning to the starting point
a journey to a destination and back

Examples

He bought a round trip ticket to San Francisco for the conference.
The bus schedule shows the times for the round trip to the lake and back.
A round trip to Madrid costs less if you book in advance.

Synset Examples

We booked a round trip to New York for the weekend.
The flight offers a convenient round trip for business travelers.
She enjoyed a scenic round trip through the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one-way ticket - a ticket that allows travel to a destination but not back
  • Synonym: return ticket - a British English synonym for a round trip ticket
