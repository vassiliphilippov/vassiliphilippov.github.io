genus Acokanthera

1. NOUN

/ əˌkoʊˈkænθərə / (AmE), / əˌkɒˈkænθərə / (BrE)
A small genus of trees and shrubs native to Arabia and Africa, known for containing strongly toxic cardiac glycosides.
toxic plant genus of Arabia and Africa

Examples

The genus Acokanthera includes several species that have been used in the making of arrow poisons.
Botanists study the genus Acokanthera for its unique toxic compounds.
Many of the shrubs in the genus Acokanthera are found in East African woodlands.

Etymology

From Acokanthera, the name of the type genus, formed from Greek roots 'ako-' (point) and 'kanthos' (thorn).
