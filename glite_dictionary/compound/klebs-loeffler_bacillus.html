Klebs-Loeffler bacillus

1. NOUN

/ kleɪbz ˈlɛflər bəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / kleɪbz ˈlɜːflə bəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium (Corynebacterium diphtheriae) that causes diphtheria
bacterium that causes diphtheria
medical

Examples

The discovery of the Klebs-Loeffler bacillus marked a turning point in understanding diphtheria.
Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent infection by the Klebs-Loeffler bacillus.
Doctors identified the presence of Klebs-Loeffler bacillus in the patient's throat sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Corynebacterium diphtheriae - scientific name for Klebs-Loeffler bacillus
  • Synonym: diphtheria - serious infectious disease caused by Klebs-Loeffler bacillus

Etymology

Named after German bacteriologists Edwin Klebs and Friedrich Loeffler, who discovered the bacillus.
