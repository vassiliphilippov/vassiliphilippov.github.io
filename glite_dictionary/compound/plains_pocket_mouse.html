plains pocket mouse

1. NOUN

/ pleɪnz ˈpɑkɪt maʊs / (AmE), / pleɪnz ˈpɒkɪt maʊs / (BrE)
a small rodent native to the open grassland regions of the central United States, known for its external cheek pouches used to carry food
small rodent of U.S. plains with cheek pouches

Examples

The plains pocket mouse stores seeds in its fur-lined cheek pouches.
Researchers trapped a plains pocket mouse to study its unique feeding habits.
Habitat loss has threatened the population of the plains pocket mouse in some states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket mouse - any small rodent of the family Heteromyidae with external cheek pouches
