martello tower

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈtɛloʊ ˈtaʊər / (AmE), / mɑːˈtɛləʊ ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
A small, circular stone fort built for coastal defense, especially in the 19th century.
circular coastal defense fort

Examples

The restored martello tower now operates as a local museum.
During the Napoleonic Wars, many martello towers were constructed along the coast.
You can see an old martello tower overlooking the bay near the village.

Etymology

From Italian 'Torre di Mortella', a tower at Mortella Point, Corsica, which inspired British design after its successful defense in 1794.
