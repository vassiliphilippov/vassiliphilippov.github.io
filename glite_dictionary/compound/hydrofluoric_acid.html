hydrofluoric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈflʊrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈflʊərɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a highly corrosive, poisonous, and weak acid formed by dissolving hydrogen fluoride in water, widely used in industry and chemistry
a poisonous, corrosive acid used in industry
medical

Examples

Hydrofluoric acid can etch glass and must be handled with extreme caution in laboratories.
Workers are required to wear protective gear when dealing with hydrofluoric acid.
If hydrofluoric acid comes into contact with skin, it can cause serious injury.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hydrochloric acid - a strong corrosive acid formed by dissolving hydrogen chloride in water

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to hydrogen) + fluoric (relating to fluorine), referring to its chemical constituents.
