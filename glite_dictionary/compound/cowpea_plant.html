cowpea plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌpi ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊpiː ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a sprawling annual plant, native to the Old World, cultivated for its edible seeds and as forage, especially in the southern United States
type of cultivated legume plant

Examples

The cowpea plant thrives in hot, dry climates and requires minimal water to grow.
Farmers often cultivate the cowpea plant to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
After harvesting, the cowpea plant residue is sometimes used as green manure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowpea - the edible seed or legume produced by the cowpea plant

Etymology

From 'cowpea' (the edible legume, from its use as cattle fodder) + ‘plant’.
