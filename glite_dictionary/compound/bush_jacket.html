bush jacket

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈdʒækət / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈdʒækɪt / (BrE)
A loose-fitting jacket resembling a shirt, typically with four patch pockets and a belt, originally designed for use in hot climates.
loose jacket with pockets and belt

Examples

He wore a bush jacket and khaki trousers while exploring the savannah.
The bush jacket became popular among safari-goers for its practicality.
Several stylish versions of the classic bush jacket are available in modern stores.

Etymology

From 'bush' (wilderness, from Dutch 'bosch') and 'jacket', reflecting origin as outdoor wear for colonial Africa and Asia.
