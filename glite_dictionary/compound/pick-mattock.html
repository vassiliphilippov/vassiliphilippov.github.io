pick-mattock

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˈmætək / (AmE), / pɪkˈmætək / (BrE)
A hand tool that combines a pick and an adze, used for digging and breaking up hard soil or rocky ground.
a tool combining a pick and adze

Examples

The gardener used a pick-mattock to break up the stony soil in the backyard.
A sturdy pick-mattock is essential for digging trenches in rocky terrain.
She packed her pick-mattock before setting out to restore the ancient pathway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mattock - a hand tool with a chisel or axe blade for digging or chopping
  • Synonym: pickaxe - a hand tool with a pointed end and a chisel end, used for breaking hard ground

Etymology

From 'pick', referring to a pointed tool, and 'mattock', an Old English term for a digging tool.
