Baader Meinhof Gang

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːdər ˈmaɪnhɒf ɡæŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːdə ˈmaɪnhɒf ɡæŋ / (BrE)
A radical left-wing revolutionary terrorist group that operated in Germany from 1968 to 1977, known for acts of violence and political activism.
German left-wing terrorist group (1968–1977)

Examples

The Baader Meinhof Gang carried out a series of high-profile attacks across Germany in the 1970s.
A documentary examines the rise and fall of the Baader Meinhof Gang and its impact on German society.
The Baader Meinhof Gang was also known as the Red Army Faction.

Etymology

Named after founders Andreas Baader and Ulrike Meinhof.
