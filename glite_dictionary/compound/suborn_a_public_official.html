to suborn a public official

1. VERB

/ səˈbɔrn ə pəˈblɪk əˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / səˈbɔːn ə ˈpʌblɪk əˈfɪʃl / (BrE)
to bribe or induce a public official to commit an unlawful or dishonest act
bribe or induce a public official unlawfully
legal

Examples

The defense attorney was convicted because he tried to suborn a public official during the investigation.
Anyone who attempts to suborn a public official can face serious legal consequences.
He was found guilty of conspiracy to suborn a public official in the procurement process.

Synset Examples

He was accused of attempting to suborn a public official.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - to dishonestly persuade someone to act in one's favor, usually with money

Etymology

From Latin 'subornare' (to equip secretly), from 'sub-' (under) + 'ornare' (to furnish).
