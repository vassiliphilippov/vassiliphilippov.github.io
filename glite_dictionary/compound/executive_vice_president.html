executive vice president

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv vaɪs ˌprɛzɪˈdɛnt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv vaɪs ˌprɛzɪˈdɛnt / (BrE)
a vice president who has executive authority and is often responsible for major divisions or functions within an organization
high-ranking vice president with executive authority

Examples

The company appointed an executive vice president to oversee its expansion into new markets.
She was promoted to executive vice president after successfully leading the marketing division.
As executive vice president, he reports directly to the CEO and manages several key departments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vice president - an official ranking immediately below the president
