nasal bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.zəl boʊn / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.zəl bəʊn / (BrE)
either of a pair of elongated, rectangular bones forming the bridge of the nose in humans and some animals
bone forming the bridge of the nose
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully repositioned the nasal bone after the accident.
A fracture in the nasal bone is a common injury during contact sports.
The bridge of your nose is formed by the paired nasal bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal - relating to the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'nasalis' (of the nose) and 'bone'.
