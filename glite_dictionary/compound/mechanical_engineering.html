mechanical engineering

1. NOUN

/ məˌkænɪkəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪˌkænɪkəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the design, construction, and operation of machines and mechanical systems
branch of engineering dealing with machines

Examples

She decided to study mechanical engineering because she enjoys designing machines and solving technical problems.
Advances in mechanical engineering have made modern transportation more efficient and reliable.
The university offers a highly regarded program in mechanical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil engineering - engineering discipline focusing on construction of infrastructure, such as roads and bridges
  • Synonym: electrical engineering - engineering discipline concerned with electrical systems and devices
  • Synonym: chemical engineering - engineering field applying chemistry to industry

Etymology

From mechanical (relating to machines) + engineering (the discipline of designing and building), first used in the early 19th century.
