swamp dewberry

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈduːˌbɛri / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈdjuːˌbɛri / (BrE)
a bristly trailing plant of eastern North America, Rubus hispidus, bearing small black berries similar to blackberries
bristly berry plant of eastern North America

Examples

The swamp dewberry is often found in wet, boggy areas of the eastern United States.
Birds are known to feed on the fruit of the swamp dewberry during late summer.
The bristly stems of the swamp dewberry make it easy to distinguish from other plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dewberry - a trailing bramble plant bearing edible berries
