mystery novel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstəri ˈnɑvəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪstəri ˈnɒvəl / (BrE)
a novel in which a crime or puzzle forms the central plot, and the reader is challenged to solve the mystery before it is revealed
novel focused on solving a mystery or crime

Examples

Agatha Christie is one of the most famous authors of the mystery novel genre.
I stayed up all night reading a gripping mystery novel that kept me guessing until the last page.
The book club chose a classic mystery novel for their next discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective novel - a novel featuring a detective who solves crimes
  • Degree Lower: thriller - a novel with exciting and suspenseful plot, often involving danger or crime
