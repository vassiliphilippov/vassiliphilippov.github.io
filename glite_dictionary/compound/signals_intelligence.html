signals intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnəlz ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəlz ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
Intelligence information collected by intercepting and analyzing signals, such as communications, electronic transmissions, or telemetry.
collection of intelligence from intercepted signals

Examples

Military agencies rely heavily on signals intelligence to monitor potential threats.
Advancements in technology have greatly enhanced the capabilities of signals intelligence operations.
The report revealed that signals intelligence played a key role in discovering the plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communications intelligence - intelligence gathered from intercepted communications
  • Synonym: electronics intelligence - intelligence gathered from electronic signals

Etymology

Formed from 'signals' and 'intelligence'; 'intelligence' in the sense of information gathering, common in military use since the 20th century.
