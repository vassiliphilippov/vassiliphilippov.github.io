pilot burner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ˌbɝːnər / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ˌbɜːnə / (BrE)
a small auxiliary gas burner that provides a continuous flame to ignite a larger gas burner when needed
small gas burner for igniting a main burner

Examples

The pilot burner must always be lit for the main oven to function properly.
If the pilot burner goes out, you should follow safety procedures before relighting it.
Technicians checked the old stove's pilot burner for blockages during maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot light - a small gas flame kept burning to relight a main burner automatically
