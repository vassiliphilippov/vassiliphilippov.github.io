boojum tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːdʒəm triː / (AmE), / ˈbuːdʒəm triː / (BrE)
A tall, columnar succulent tree native to northwestern Mexico and parts of southwestern California, known for its honey-scented creamy yellow flowers.
succulent tree of Mexico and California

Examples

The boojum tree is easily recognized by its tall, spiny trunk and sparse branches.
During our trip to Baja California, we saw several boojum trees blooming in the desert.
Botanists are fascinated by the unusual structure of the boojum tree.

Etymology

Coined in the 20th century, likely referencing the 'boojum' from Lewis Carroll's poem 'The Hunting of the Snark'.
