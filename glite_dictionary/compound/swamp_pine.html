swamp pine

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp paɪn / (AmE), / swɒmp paɪn / (BrE)
a type of pine tree that thrives in or tolerates wet, marshy soil, such as the loblolly or longleaf pine
pine tree found in wetlands

Examples

The dense stand of swamp pine provided shelter for a variety of wetland animals.
Many conservation efforts focus on preserving natural habitats like the swamp pine forests of the Southeast.
The timber industry often relies on species such as swamp pine for pulpwood and construction lumber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loblolly pine - a pine native to the southeastern U.S. that grows in wet areas
  • Synonym: longleaf pine - a pine tree native to the southeastern United States, often found in moist or sandy soils
