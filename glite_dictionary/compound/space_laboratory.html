space laboratory

1. NOUN

/ speɪs ˈlæbrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / speɪs ˈlæbrətəri / (BrE)
a manned artificial satellite placed in a fixed orbit, designed for conducting scientific research in space
manned artificial satellite for research

Examples

Scientists aboard the space laboratory conducted experiments in zero gravity.
The launch of the new space laboratory marked a milestone in international cooperation.
Living conditions in a space laboratory require careful planning and innovative technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: space station - a large artificial satellite for people to live and work in space
