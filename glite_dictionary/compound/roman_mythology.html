Roman mythology

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən mɪˈθɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən mɪˈθɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The body of traditional stories, beliefs, and legends of the ancient Romans, featuring gods, heroes, and rituals that shaped Roman culture and religion.
mythology of ancient Rome

Examples

Many schoolchildren learn about Jupiter and Mars from Roman mythology.
Roman mythology includes myths about the founding of Rome by Romulus and Remus.
The influence of Roman mythology can be seen in European literature and art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek mythology - the mythology and legendary stories of the ancient Greeks

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən mɪˈθɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən mɪˈθɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the body of myths and stories originating from ancient Rome, often involving gods, heroes, and the creation of the world
mythology of ancient Rome

Examples

Many of the gods in Roman mythology were borrowed from Greek traditions and given new names.
Students often study Roman mythology to learn about ancient religious beliefs and cultural values.
The tale of Romulus and Remus is a famous story in Roman mythology.

Synset Examples

They discussed Roman mythology during their history class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek mythology - the collection of myths from ancient Greece

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman) and Greek 'mythologia' (mythology).
