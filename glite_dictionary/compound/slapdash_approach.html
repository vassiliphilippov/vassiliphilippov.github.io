slapdash approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæpˌdæʃ əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈslæpˌdæʃ əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a method or way of doing something that is careless and done too quickly, without enough attention to detail
careless or hasty method

Examples

The manager criticized the team's slapdash approach to solving the problem.
Rushing through reports with a slapdash approach often results in errors.
Her slapdash approach to organizing the event caused a lot of confusion.

Synset Examples

His slapdash approach led to many mistakes.
They finished the project in a slapdash manner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method - a particular procedure for accomplishing something
  • Antonym: careful approach - a method characterized by thoroughness and attention to detail

Etymology

From 'slapdash' (carelessly done), first recorded in English in the 17th century; approach from French 'approcher', to come near.
