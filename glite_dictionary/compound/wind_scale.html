wind scale

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd skeɪl / (AmE), / wɪnd skeɪl / (BrE)
an international numerical scale for measuring wind force, generally ranging from 0 (calm) to 12 (hurricane), used especially in meteorology.
scale measuring wind force

Examples

The weather report mentioned that the storm had reached level 10 on the wind scale.
Meteorologists use the wind scale to classify the strength of different storms.
Sailors often consult the wind scale before setting out to sea.
