bib-and-tucker

1. NOUN

/ bɪb ən ˈtʌkər / (AmE), / bɪb ənd ˈtʌkə / (BrE)
a person's best or most attractive clothes, typically worn for a special occasion
one's best or finest clothes
archaic

Examples

She always wore her best bib-and-tucker to the town dance.
For the wedding, he arrived in his finest bib-and-tucker.
In her new bib-and-tucker, she felt ready to impress everyone.

Synset Examples

she wore her best bib-and-tucker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: best clothes - one's finest clothes worn on special occasions

Etymology

Originating in the 18th century, from 'bib' (a piece of cloth worn over the chest) and 'tucker' (a lace or cloth garment worn around the neck and bosom by women).
