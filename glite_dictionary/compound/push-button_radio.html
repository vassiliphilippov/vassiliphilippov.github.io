push-button radio

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊʃ ˌbʌtən ˈreɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈpʊʃ ˌbʌtən ˈreɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
a radio receiver that is tuned by pressing preset buttons rather than turning a dial
radio with preset tuning buttons

Examples

The vintage car still has its original push-button radio, which works perfectly.
He preferred using a push-button radio for the convenience of quickly changing stations.
In the 1960s, the push-button radio became a popular feature in many households.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio - device that receives radio broadcasts
