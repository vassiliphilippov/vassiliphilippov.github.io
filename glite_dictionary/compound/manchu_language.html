Manchu language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.tʃu ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tʃuː ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Tungusic language historically spoken by the Manchu people of northeastern China.
Tungusic language of the Manchu

Examples

Few people today still speak the Manchu language fluently.
The Manchu language was once the official court language during the Qing dynasty.
Linguists are working to preserve texts written in the Manchu language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tungusic language - a family of languages of eastern Siberia and Manchuria, including Manchu

Etymology

From Manchu "Manju" and English "language".
