finish line

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ laɪn / (BrE)
a line marking the end point of a race or competition, indicating where competitors must cross to finish
line marking the end of a race

Examples

She sprinted towards the finish line with determination and crossed it in first place.
As the runners neared the finish line, the crowd erupted in cheers.
The horse stumbled just meters before reaching the finish line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: starting line - line marking where a race begins
  • Synonym: tape - strip stretched across the finish line in some races
