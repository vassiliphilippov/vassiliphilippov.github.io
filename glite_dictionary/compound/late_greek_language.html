Late Greek language

1. NOUN

/ leɪt ˈɡrik ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / leɪt ˈɡriːk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the stage of the Greek language spoken and written from the 3rd to the 8th centuries AD
Greek language from 3rd to 8th centuries

Examples

Scholars often study texts written in Late Greek language to understand the development of Byzantine literature.
The Late Greek language marks a transitional period between Ancient and Medieval Greek.
Several important theological works were composed in the Late Greek language during the early Christian era.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Ancient Greek - Greek language from the 9th century BC to the 6th century AD
  • Degree Higher: Medieval Greek - Greek language from about the 9th to the 15th century

Etymology

From English 'late' + 'Greek', indicating the later form of the Greek language; 'Greek' from Latin 'Graecus'.
