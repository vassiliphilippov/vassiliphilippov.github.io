Thompson Seedless

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑmpsən ˈsiːdləs / (AmE), / ˈtɒmpsən ˈsiːdləs / (BrE)
A variety of seedless green grape, commonly grown in California and widely used as a table grape and for raisins.
seedless green table grape variety

Examples

The Thompson Seedless is prized for its sweet flavor and lack of seeds.
Farmers in California grow large quantities of Thompson Seedless grapes each year.
Many raisins found in supermarkets are made from Thompson Seedless grapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sultana - seedless grape variety often used for raisins
  • Antonym: Concord grape - American grape variety with seeds and dark purple skin

Etymology

Named after William Thompson, who introduced the variety to California in the late 19th century.
