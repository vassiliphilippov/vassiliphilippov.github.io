to push into overdrive

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ˈɪntu ˈoʊvərˌdraɪv / (AmE), / pʊʃ ˈɪntuː ˈəʊvəˌdraɪv / (BrE)
to begin working or functioning with greatly increased effort, speed, or intensity
begin to work with greatly increased intensity

Examples

With the deadline approaching, the team pushed into overdrive to complete the assignment.
When the alarm sounded, the staff pushed into overdrive to handle the emergency.
In the final minutes of the game, the players pushed into overdrive and scored two goals.

Synset Examples

She pushed herself into overdrive to finish the project.
During the holidays, our sales team went into overdrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go into overdrive - to begin working with increased energy or intensity

Etymology

From 'overdrive,' an automotive term meaning a gear that allows high speed with less effort, figuratively extended to mean increased intensity.
