British Imperial System

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɪtɪʃ ɪmˈpɪriəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌbrɪtɪʃ ɪmˈpɪəriəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of weights and measures formerly used in Britain and some other countries, based on units such as the foot, pound, and pint
system of weights and measures used in Britain
british

Examples

The British Imperial System was officially replaced by the metric system in the United Kingdom in the late 20th century.
Some recipes converted from the British Imperial System require careful calculation to use metric units correctly.
The British Imperial System uses measurements such as inches, feet, and gallons.
