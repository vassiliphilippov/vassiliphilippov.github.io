ailanthus silkworm

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈlænθəs ˈsɪlkwɜːrm / (AmE), / aɪˈlanθəs ˈsɪlkwɜːm / (BrE)
the large green larva of the cynthia moth, known for feeding on ailanthus trees and producing silk
larva of the cynthia moth

Examples

The ailanthus silkworm feeds exclusively on the leaves of the tree of heaven.
In Asia, the ailanthus silkworm is sometimes cultivated for silk production.
The bright green color of the ailanthus silkworm makes it easier to spot among ailanthus leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cynthia moth - a large moth species also known as Samia cynthia
  • Degree Higher: silkworm - larva of moths used in silk production

Etymology

From 'ailanthus', the genus name of its favored host tree, and 'silkworm' for its silk-producing nature.
