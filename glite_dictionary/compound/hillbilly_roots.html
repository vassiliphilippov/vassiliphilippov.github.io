hillbilly roots

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪlˌbɪli ruːts / (AmE), / ˈhɪlˌbɪli ruːts / (BrE)
ancestry or heritage from rural, especially southern, regions of the United States
rural or southern U.S. heritage
informal american

Examples

She often talked about her hillbilly roots when recalling family traditions.
Despite living in the city, he never forgot his hillbilly roots.
The music festival celebrated the hillbilly roots of the region.

Synset Examples

She embraced her hillbilly roots with pride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redneck roots - ancestry from rural, working-class southern regions

Etymology

From 'hillbilly' (early 20th century American English, meaning rural person) + 'roots' (ancestry, origin).
