cyrilla family

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɪl.ə ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪl.ə ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a group of shrubs and trees with leathery leaves and small white flowers in racemes, including the genera Cyrilla and Cliftonia
group of shrubs/trees with leathery leaves, genus Cyrilla

Examples

The cyrilla family includes shrubs found in the southeastern United States and the Caribbean.
Botanists studied the leathery leaves characteristic of plants in the cyrilla family.
The marshland was dominated by species from the cyrilla family, especially Cyrilla racemiflora.
