audio-lingual acquisition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔdioʊ lɪŋˈɡwɑl əˌkwɪzɪˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiəʊ lɪŋˈɡwæl əˌkwɪzɪˈʃən / (BrE)
a method of language learning that emphasizes intensive listening and speaking practice to develop proficiency
language learning through listening and speaking
medical

Examples

Many language classes use audio-lingual acquisition techniques to improve students' speaking skills.
The audio-lingual acquisition approach requires repeated drills and listening exercises.
Researchers have debated the effectiveness of audio-lingual acquisition compared to other language learning methods.

Etymology

From 'audio-' relating to hearing, and 'lingual' from Latin 'lingua' (tongue, language), combined with 'acquisition'.
