sound bite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊnd ˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd ˌbaɪt / (BrE)
a short extract from a recorded statement, especially one made by a politician or official, broadcast on radio or television
short extract from a speech or interview

Examples

The politician's sound bite was featured on the evening news.
Journalists often look for a memorable sound bite during press conferences.
The campaign team prepared several sound bites for the candidate to use in interviews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clip - short segment of audio or video
  • Synonym: quote - a repetition of someone else's statement or thoughts

Etymology

Formed from 'sound' + 'bite', originally in broadcasting (late 20th century) to signify a 'bite-sized' audio clip.
