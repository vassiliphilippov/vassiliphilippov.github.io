histamine blocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪstəˌmiːn ˈblɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈhɪstəmiːn ˈblɒkə / (BrE)
a medication that reduces stomach acid by blocking the action of histamine on H2 receptors in the stomach lining, used to treat conditions like ulcers and gastroesophageal reflux
medicine reducing gastric acid via H2 receptor blockade
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a histamine blocker to help reduce the patient's stomach acid.
Many people with ulcers benefit from taking a histamine blocker as part of their treatment.
If you suffer from chronic heartburn, your physician might recommend a histamine blocker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: H2 blocker - a synonym for histamine blocker (blocking H2 receptors)
