Venetian red

1. NOUN

/ vəˈniːʃən rɛd / (AmE), / vɪˈniːʃən rɛd / (BrE)
a deep, warm shade of red with a hint of brown, traditionally made from natural earth pigments
a deep, earthy red color

Examples

The artist chose Venetian red for the background to add warmth to the painting.
A wall painted in Venetian red creates a cozy and inviting atmosphere in the room.
The fabric was dyed a beautiful shade of Venetian red, reminiscent of classical Renaissance art.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red - a primary color at the end of the visible spectrum
  • Degree Higher: crimson - a rich deep red color inclining to purple

Etymology

Named after Venice, where this pigment was originally manufactured; 'Venetian' referring to Venice.
