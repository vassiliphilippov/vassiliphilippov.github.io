posse of friends

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːsi əv frɛndz / (AmE), / ˈpɒsi əv frɛndz / (BrE)
an informal group of friends who regularly spend time together
informal group of friends
informal

Examples

She always brings her posse of friends whenever she goes out to eat.
The festival was full of teenagers hanging out in their posse of friends.
After class, he met up with his posse of friends to play basketball.

Synset Examples

She rolled up to the party with her posse of friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group of friends - a collection of people who are friends
  • Synonym: posse - a group of friends or associates (informal)

Etymology

From 'posse', originally meaning 'a body of men summoned by a sheriff', later adopted in American slang to mean a group of friends or associates.
