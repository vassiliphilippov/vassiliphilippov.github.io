Battle of Shiloh

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈʃaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈʃaɪlɒ / (BrE)
a major battle of the American Civil War fought in 1862 in southwestern Tennessee, marked by heavy casualties and the withdrawal of Confederate forces.
Civil War battle in Tennessee, 1862

Examples

The Battle of Shiloh resulted in heavy losses on both sides during the Civil War.
Historians often debate the strategic significance of the Battle of Shiloh.
Many soldiers described the chaos and noise of the Battle of Shiloh in their letters home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Gettysburg - major Civil War battle fought in Pennsylvania in 1863

Etymology

Named after a nearby church called Shiloh; 'Shiloh' from Hebrew, meaning 'place of peace'.
