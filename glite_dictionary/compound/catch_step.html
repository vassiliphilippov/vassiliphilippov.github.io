to catch step

1. VERB

/ kætʃ stɛp / (AmE), / katʃ stɛp / (BrE)
to match the pace or rhythm of another person, especially while walking or moving together
match another's pace or rhythm

Examples

He tried to catch step with his friend as they climbed the stairs together.
The children quickly caught step with the marching band in the parade.
In the dark hallway, she slowed down to catch step with her guide.

Synset Examples

Please catch step with me while we walk.
She had to catch step to keep up with him.
Let's catch step as we head to the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep pace - to go at the same speed as someone or something
