supraorbital torus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːprəˈɔrbɪtl ˈtɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌsuːprəˈɔːbɪtəl ˈtɔːrəs / (BrE)
a prominent bony ridge located above the eye sockets on the frontal bone, often found in early human ancestors and some modern humans
bony ridge above eye sockets
medical

Examples

The fossil skull featured a pronounced supraorbital torus characteristic of early Homo species.
Some modern human populations retain a modest supraorbital torus above the eyes.
Researchers measured the thickness of the supraorbital torus to compare evolutionary traits.

Etymology

From Latin 'supraorbitalis' (above the orbit) and 'torus' (bulge or swelling).
