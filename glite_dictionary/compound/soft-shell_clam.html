soft-shell clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑft ʃɛl klæm / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ʃɛl klæm / (BrE)
an edible clam with a thin, brittle, oval-shaped shell, found in coastal regions of North America and Europe, often used as food
an edible clam with a thin shell

Examples

Steamed soft-shell clams are a popular summer treat along the New England coast.
He raked the mudflats at low tide to dig for soft-shell clams.
The restaurant served fresh soft-shell clam chowder garnished with parsley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a type of bivalve mollusk with a shell, commonly eaten as seafood
