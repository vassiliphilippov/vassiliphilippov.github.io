stamp tax

1. NOUN

/ stæmp tæks / (AmE), / stæmp tæks / (BrE)
a tax collected by requiring an official stamp to be purchased and affixed to certain documents or publications
tax requiring purchase of a stamp for documents
legal

Examples

During the colonial period, the British government imposed a stamp tax on all legal documents.
The introduction of a stamp tax led to widespread protests among merchants and lawyers.
To finalize the transaction, you must pay the stamp tax and attach the stamped certificate to the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp duty - a tax paid on certain documents, often used interchangeably with 'stamp tax'
