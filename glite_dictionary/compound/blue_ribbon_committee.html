blue ribbon committee

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈrɪb.ən kəˈmɪ.t̬i / (AmE), / bluː ˈrɪb.ən kəˈmɪ.ti / (BrE)
an independent and distinguished group of experts, often appointed to investigate or report on an important governmental issue
distinguished investigatory committee

Examples

A blue ribbon committee was formed to examine the city's financial problems and recommend solutions.
The government announced the creation of a blue ribbon committee to study healthcare reforms.
After months of investigation, the blue ribbon committee released its findings in a detailed report.

Etymology

'Blue ribbon' has long symbolized high quality or distinction; applied to committees since the late 19th century in the U.S.
