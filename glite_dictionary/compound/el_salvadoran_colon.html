El Salvadoran colon

1. NOUN

/ ɛl sælˌvɑːrdɔːn koʊˈlɒn / (AmE), / ɛl sælˌvɑːdərən kəˈlɒn / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in El Salvador, equal to 100 centavos, used prior to adoption of the US dollar.
former currency unit of El Salvador

Examples

Before 2001, prices in El Salvador were listed in El Salvadoran colons rather than US dollars.
The value of the El Salvadoran colon fluctuated significantly during the late 20th century.
Tourists today can no longer use the El Salvadoran colon in El Salvador, as the country now uses the US dollar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centavo - a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the colon

Etymology

Named after Christopher Columbus (Cristóbal Colón in Spanish), reflecting Spanish usage.
