ipso facto

1. ADV

/ ˌɪpsoʊ ˈfæktoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪpsəʊ ˈfæktəʊ / (BrE)
by that very fact; as a direct consequence of the fact mentioned
by that very fact

Examples

He was the only qualified candidate and ipso facto became the leader of the project.
Any act of theft is ipso facto a violation of the law.
If the contract is invalid, then ipso facto, so are its terms.

Synset Examples

ipso facto, her innocence was established

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'by the fact itself.'
