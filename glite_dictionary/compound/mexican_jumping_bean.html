Mexican jumping bean

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ ˌbin / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ ˌbiːn / (BrE)
the seed of certain Mexican shrubs containing a moth larva, which moves and makes the seed twitch or jump
seed that jumps due to insect larva

Examples

As a child, I was fascinated by the Mexican jumping bean I bought at the fair.
The movement of a Mexican jumping bean is caused by a tiny moth larva inside.
Scientists studied the peculiar behavior of the Mexican jumping bean to learn more about insect activity.
