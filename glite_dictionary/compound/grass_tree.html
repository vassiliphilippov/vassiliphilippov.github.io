grass tree

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs tri / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs triː / (BrE)
A gaunt evergreen shrubby tree native to Tasmania, characterized by slender, tapering leaves that are 3 to 5 feet long.
Tasmanian shrubby evergreen tree with long tapering leaves

Examples

A grass tree can be identified by its tall, narrow leaves and shrubby appearance.
The grass tree thrives in the wild landscapes of Tasmania.
Botanists studied several mature grass trees in the region to understand their growth patterns.

2. NOUN

/ ɡræs tri / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs triː / (BrE)
Any of several Australian evergreen perennial plants with short, thick woody stems, topped with a tuft of grasslike leaves and producing acaroid resins, especially of the genus Xanthorrhoea.
Australian perennial plant with grasslike tufted leaves

Examples

The Australian landscape was dotted with distinctive grass trees rising above the scrub.
Early settlers used the resin from grass trees as an adhesive and sealant.
Fire helps the grass tree to flower and regenerate in its natural habitat.

3. NOUN

/ ɡræs tri / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs triː / (BrE)
An elegant tree native to New Zealand, having either a single or branching trunk, each with terminal clusters of long, narrow leaves and large panicles of fragrant white, yellow, or red flowers.
New Zealand tree with long leaves and fragrant flowers

Examples

We admired the grass tree with its striking foliage during our walk in the New Zealand forest.
The grass tree produces beautiful clusters of fragrant flowers in the summer season.
Botanists study the grass tree to understand its unique adaptation to the New Zealand environment.
