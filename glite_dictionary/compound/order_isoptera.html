order Isoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər aɪˈsɒptərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə aɪˈsɒptərə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of social insects, including termites, characterized by living in colonies; sometimes placed in the subclass Exopterygota
taxonomic order of termites

Examples

Members of the order Isoptera are commonly known as termites and are famous for their colony-building behavior.
The order Isoptera plays a crucial role in recycling decaying wood and plant material in many ecosystems.
Research on the order Isoptera has contributed to our understanding of eusociality among insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - a social insect belonging to the order Isoptera

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + pteron (wing), referring to equal-sized wings of the insects.
