shotgun shack

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑtˌɡʌn ʃæk / (AmE), / ˈʃɒtɡʌn ʃæk / (BrE)
a narrow, single-story house, typically with rooms arranged one behind another and no hallway, common in the southern United States
narrow, single-story house with rooms in a row
american

Examples

The band practiced every weekend in their old shotgun shack downtown.
Growing up in a shotgun shack, she learned to appreciate simple living.
They restored a historic shotgun shack and turned it into a café.

Synset Examples

They lived in a small shotgun shack by the beach.
He grew up in a shotgun shack in the city.
The shotgun shack was charming despite its size.

Etymology

From 'shotgun' + 'shack', likened to the fact that a shotgun could be fired through the front door and out the back without hitting walls.
