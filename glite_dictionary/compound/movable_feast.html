movable feast

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvəbəl fiːst / (AmE), / ˈmuːvəb(ə)l fiːst / (BrE)
A religious holiday or festival whose date changes from year to year.
religious holiday with changing date

Examples

Easter is the most well-known movable feast in the Christian calendar.
Unlike Christmas, which is celebrated on December 25th, Pentecost is a movable feast.
Many cultures have at least one important movable feast each year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixed feast - a religious holiday that falls on the same date every year
