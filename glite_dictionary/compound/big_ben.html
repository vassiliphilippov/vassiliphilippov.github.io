Big Ben

1. PROPN

/ bɪɡ bɛn / (AmE), / bɪɡ bɛn / (BrE)
The great bell and commonly the clock in the clock tower of the Houses of Parliament in London, often used to refer to the tower itself.
famous clock and bell in London

Examples

The chimes of Big Ben are broadcast to mark the new year in the UK.
On our trip to London, we took many photos of Big Ben.
Maintenance work on Big Ben has temporarily silenced its famous hourly strikes.

Etymology

Named after Sir Benjamin Hall, the commissioner of works when the bell was installed, or possibly heavyweight boxer Benjamin Caunt.
