soft Muzak

1. NOUN

/ sɔft ˈmjuːzæk / (AmE), / sɒft ˈmjuːzak / (BrE)
light, instrumental background music, typically played in public spaces to create a calm atmosphere
light instrumental background music

Examples

The hotel lobby was filled with soft Muzak that made waiting more pleasant.
She found it hard to concentrate with soft Muzak playing quietly in the office.
In the restaurant, the soft Muzak blended seamlessly into the background chatter.

Synset Examples

The elevator played soft Muzak to ease the tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: background music - music meant to be heard unobtrusively in the background

Etymology

Muzak is a trademark name originating in the early 20th century for piped background music.
