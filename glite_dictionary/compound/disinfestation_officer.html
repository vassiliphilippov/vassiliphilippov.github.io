disinfestation officer

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˌfɛˈsteɪʃən ˈɑfɪsər / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˌfɛˈsteɪʃən ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to destroy or remove vermin or pests, usually by using chemicals or other methods
person employed to eliminate pests

Examples

The city hired a disinfestation officer to control the rat population in the sewers.
After discovering termites in the old building, a disinfestation officer was called in immediately.
As a disinfestation officer, her daily tasks included inspecting premises and applying pest control solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exterminator - a person whose job is to kill unwanted insects or animals

Etymology

From 'disinfestation' (from Latin dis- + infestare, to attack) and 'officer'.
