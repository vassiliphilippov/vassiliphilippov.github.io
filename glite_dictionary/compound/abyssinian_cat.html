Abyssinian cat

1. NOUN

/ əˌbɪsˈɪniən kæt / (AmE), / əˌbɪsˈɪniən kat / (BrE)
a slender short-haired breed of domestic cat of African origin, noted for its brownish fur with a reddish undercoat
breed of cat with reddish-brown fur

Examples

The Abyssinian cat is admired for its playful and affectionate nature.
Her family adopted an Abyssinian cat with strikingly beautiful fur.
Many breeders consider the Abyssinian cat one of the oldest existing feline breeds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: domestic cat - a small domesticated carnivorous mammal that is kept as a pet or for catching mice

Etymology

Named after Abyssinia (now Ethiopia), where the breed was believed to have originated.
