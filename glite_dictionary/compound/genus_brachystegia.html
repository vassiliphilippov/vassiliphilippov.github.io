genus Brachystegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs ˌbrækɪˈstiːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs ˌbrækɪˈstiːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical African timber trees known for pale golden heartwood uniformly striped with dark brown or black.
African genus of timber trees

Examples

The genus Brachystegia is a key component of miombo woodland ecosystems in Africa.
Wood from trees of the genus Brachystegia is valued for its distinctive striped appearance.
Botanists are studying the distribution of the genus Brachystegia across the continent.

Etymology

From Greek 'brachys' (short) and 'stegē' (cover), referring to features of the flower.
