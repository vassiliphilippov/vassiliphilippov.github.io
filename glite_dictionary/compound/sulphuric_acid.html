sulphuric acid

1. NOUN

/ sʌlˈfjʊrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sʌlˈfjʊərɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a strong, highly corrosive acid (H₂SO₄) used in industry and laboratory processes
a strong, corrosive acid (H₂SO₄)

Examples

Concentrated sulphuric acid can cause severe burns if it comes into contact with the skin.
Battery acid in cars is actually diluted sulphuric acid.
The laboratory keeps sulphuric acid stored in a special container due to its corrosive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrochloric acid - a strong, corrosive acid (HCl) used in industry and laboratory processes
  • Degree Lower: acetic acid - an organic acid in vinegar

Etymology

From sulphur (element name) + -ic (forming adjectives) + acid.
