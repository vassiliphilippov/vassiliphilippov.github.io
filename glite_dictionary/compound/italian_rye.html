Italian rye

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæljən raɪ / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪən raɪ / (BrE)
A European grass (Lolium multiflorum) widely used for hay, turf, and as green manure, especially in agriculture.
a European grass used for hay and turf

Examples

Farmers often plant Italian rye to improve soil fertility between crop cycles.
The lush green lawn was seeded with Italian rye for a quick and durable cover.
High-quality hay is frequently made from Italian rye due to its rich nutritional content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ryegrass - a grass of the genus Lolium used as pasture or forage
