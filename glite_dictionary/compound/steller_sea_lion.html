Steller sea lion

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlər si ˈlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈstɛlə si ˈlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
a large species of sea lion native to the northern Pacific Ocean, known for being the largest of all sea lions
largest sea lion of the northern Pacific

Examples

The Steller sea lion is listed as near threatened due to declines in its population.
Researchers studied a colony of Steller sea lions off the Alaskan coast.
A mature male Steller sea lion can weigh over 1,000 kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sea lion - a large marine mammal related to the seal

Etymology

Named after Georg Wilhelm Steller, German naturalist who first described the species.
