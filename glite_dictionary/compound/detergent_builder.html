detergent builder

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɜrdʒənt ˈbɪldər / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːdʒənt ˈbɪldə / (BrE)
a chemical substance added to soaps or detergents to enhance their cleaning effectiveness, often by softening water or removing impurities
substance enhancing detergent cleaning power

Examples

Many modern laundry soaps use a detergent builder to improve cleaning performance in hard water.
The addition of a detergent builder can help break down minerals that inhibit soap’s action.
Phosphates were once common as a detergent builder before environmental concerns led to alternatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: builder - a substance that increases the effectiveness of a cleaning agent
