Gulf States

1. PROPN

/ ɡʌlf steɪts / (AmE), / ɡʌlf steɪts / (BrE)
the countries in southwestern Asia that border the Persian Gulf, typically including Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, the United Arab Emirates, and Oman
countries bordering the Persian Gulf

Examples

Economic cooperation among the Gulf States has increased in recent years.
Tourists are attracted to the modern cities developed in many Gulf States.
The Gulf States are known for their significant oil reserves.

Synset POS: n

2. PROPN

/ ɡʌlf steɪts / (AmE), / ɡʌlf steɪts / (BrE)
the group of U.S. states that border the Gulf of Mexico, specifically Alabama, Florida, Louisiana, Mississippi, and Texas
U.S. states bordering the Gulf of Mexico

Examples

Hurricane Katrina had a devastating impact on several of the Gulf States.
Tourism is a major industry in the Gulf States due to their warm coastal climate.
The Gulf States have diverse cultures influenced by both the American South and the Gulf of Mexico.
