Irish monetary unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɹɪʃ ˈmʌnəˌtɛri ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ˈaɪɹɪʃ ˈmʌnɪtəri ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a former unit of currency used in Ireland before the adoption of the euro
a former currency unit of Ireland

Examples

Before 2002, the Irish monetary unit was the punt.
Prices were displayed in both euros and the old Irish monetary unit during the transition period.
He exchanged his dollars for the Irish monetary unit at the currency office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punt - the principal Irish currency unit before the euro
