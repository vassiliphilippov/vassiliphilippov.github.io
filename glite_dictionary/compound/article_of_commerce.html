article of commerce

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtɪkəl əv ˈkɑːmɚs / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪkəl əv ˈkɒməs / (BrE)
an item offered for sale or trade, especially as part of commercial activity
item offered for sale

Examples

The import regulations apply to any article of commerce entering the country.
Pharmaceutical drugs are considered an article of commerce subject to federal law.
The court ruled that the product was an article of commerce under the statute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commodity - a raw material or primary agricultural product that can be bought and sold
