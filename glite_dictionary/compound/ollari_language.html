Ollari language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɑːri ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒləri ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken in south central India, particularly in Odisha, Koraput district, and the Pottangi and Nandapur blocks
language of south central India (Dravidian)

Examples

The Ollari language is considered vulnerable due to the small number of native speakers.
Researchers are working to document the grammar and vocabulary of the Ollari language.
She grew up speaking the Ollari language at home with her family in Odisha.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dravidian language - a family of languages spoken mainly in southern India and Sri Lanka
