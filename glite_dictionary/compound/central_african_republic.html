Central African Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛntrəl ˈæfrɪkən rəˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəl ˈæfrɪkən rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
A landlocked country in central Africa, formerly under French colonial rule, which became independent in 1960.
country in central Africa

Examples

The Central African Republic shares borders with several other African nations, including Chad and Sudan.
After gaining independence in 1960, the Central African Republic continued to face economic and political challenges.
Humanitarian organizations often provide aid in the Central African Republic due to ongoing conflicts.
