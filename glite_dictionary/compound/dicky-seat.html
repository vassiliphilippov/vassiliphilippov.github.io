dicky-seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki siːt / (AmE), / ˈdɪki siːt / (BrE)
a small additional seat that folds out at the back of an old-fashioned two-seater car, used as an extra seat
extra folding seat at back of old car
british

Examples

The old Bentley still had its original dicky-seat hidden in the back.
She loved riding in the dicky-seat during Sunday outings as a child.
The mechanic showed us how the dicky-seat folded out from the rear of the car.

Etymology

British English, from 'dickey' (an exposed seat at the back of a vehicle), likely from earlier sense (servant's seat), 19th century.
