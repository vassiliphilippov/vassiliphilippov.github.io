genus Ledum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈliː.dəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɛd.ʌm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs native to north temperate regions, formerly classified in the heath family.
genus of evergreen shrubs

Examples

The genus Ledum includes several species commonly known as Labrador tea.
Botanists once classified these northern shrubs in the genus Ledum before reassigning them to Rhododendron.
Characteristics of the genus Ledum are leathery leaves and white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhododendron - genus of shrubs or small trees, some formerly classified as Ledum

Etymology

From Latin 'Ledum', from Greek 'lēdon' (wild rosemary).
