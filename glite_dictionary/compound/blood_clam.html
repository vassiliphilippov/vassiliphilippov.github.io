blood clam

1. NOUN

/ blʌd klæm / (AmE), / blʌd klam / (BrE)
a species of edible bivalve mollusc with red-colored blood, commonly found in coastal mudflats of Asia and known for its hemoglobin-rich blood
edible clam with red blood

Examples

The blood clam is prized in some Asian cuisines for its unique flavor and texture.
Researchers studied the blood clam to understand how its red blood helps it survive in low-oxygen environments.
Eating raw blood clam can sometimes carry health risks due to its habitat.
