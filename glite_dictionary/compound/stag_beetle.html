stag beetle

1. NOUN

/ stæɡ ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / stæɡ ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A large beetle of the family Lucanidae, the male of which has large, branched mandibles resembling a stag's antlers.
large beetle with antler-like jaws

Examples

The male stag beetle uses its impressive jaws to fight other males during mating season.
Children often marvel at the size and antler-like mandibles of a stag beetle in the garden.
Conservation programs aim to protect the habitat of the endangered stag beetle across Europe.

Etymology

Named for the male beetle's mandibles, which resemble a stag's antlers.
