gum elemi

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ɛˈlɛmi / (AmE), / ɡʌm ɛˈlɛmi / (BrE)
a fragrant resin obtained from certain trees in the Burseraceae family, used in incense, varnishes, and perfumes
fragrant resin from Burseraceae trees

Examples

The artist used gum elemi to give a glossy finish to his oil paintings.
Traditional incense blends often include gum elemi for its fresh, citrusy fragrance.
Harvesters collect gum elemi by making incisions in the bark of Burseraceae trees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: resin - a sticky substance secreted by some plants

Etymology

From French 'élémie', probably from Arabic 'al-lāmī', the name of the tree producing the resin.
