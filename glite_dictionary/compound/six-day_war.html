Six-Day War

1. NOUN

/ sɪks deɪ wɔr / (AmE), / sɪks deɪ wɔː / (BrE)
a brief war fought in June 1967 between Israel and neighboring Arab states, resulting in a significant shift in Middle Eastern power dynamics
1967 Arab-Israeli war

Examples

The Six-Day War dramatically altered the balance of power in the Middle East.
Many historians study the causes and consequences of the Six-Day War.
During the Six-Day War, Israel captured significant territories from neighboring countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yom Kippur War - the 1973 conflict between Israel and a coalition of Arab states
