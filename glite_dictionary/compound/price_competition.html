price competition

1. NOUN

/ praɪs kəmˌpɛt̬ɪˈʃən / (AmE), / praɪs kəmˌpɛtɪˈʃən / (BrE)
competition between businesses in which they lower their prices to attract customers
competition by lowering prices

Examples

The store engaged in price competition by offering deep discounts during the holiday season.
Intense price competition in the airline industry often leads to lower fares for travelers.
Some companies avoid price competition by focusing on superior customer service instead.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-price competition - competition based on factors other than price, such as quality or service
