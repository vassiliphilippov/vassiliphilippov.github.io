breach of the peace

1. NOUN

/ briːtʃ əv ðə piːs / (AmE), / briːtʃ ɒv ðə piːs / (BrE)
an act that disturbs public order or causes a disturbance or disruption to public tranquility, often regarded as a minor offense in law
act disturbing public order
legal

Examples

The man was arrested for committing a breach of the peace during the demonstration.
Police warned that any breach of the peace would not be tolerated at the festival.
After a noisy dispute with neighbors, he was charged with breach of the peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - an act of interrupting public order or peace

Etymology

From 'breach' (Old French *breche*, an opening or break) + 'peace' (Latin *pax*). Legal use dates to Middle English.
