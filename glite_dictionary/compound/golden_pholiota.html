golden pholiota

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən foʊˈliːətə / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən fəˈlɪətə / (BrE)
a yellow-gilled fungus (Pholiota aurivella) found from Alaska south along the North American coast, known for its attractive golden color
a yellow-gilled woodland fungus

Examples

We found a cluster of golden pholiota growing on a decaying log during our hike.
The cap of the golden pholiota shines brightly when the sunlight hits it.
Mushroom enthusiasts are often excited to spot the distinctive golden pholiota in coastal forests.

Etymology

From genus name Pholiota, from Greek 'pholis' (scale), referring to its scaly cap; 'golden' for its color.
