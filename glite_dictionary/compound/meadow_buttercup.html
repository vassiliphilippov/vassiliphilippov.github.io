meadow buttercup

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ ˈbʌtərkʌp / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈbʌtəkʌp / (BrE)
a perennial European wildflower (Ranunculus acris) with bright yellow spring flowers, commonly found in meadows and widely naturalized in North America
yellow-flowered wild plant of meadows

Examples

The field was dotted with meadow buttercup, creating a sea of yellow blooms in spring.
Children picked meadow buttercup flowers during their walk through the countryside.
The meadow buttercup thrives in open, grassy areas with plenty of sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - any of various plants of the genus Ranunculus with yellow flowers
