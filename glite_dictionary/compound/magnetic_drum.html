magnetic drum

1. NOUN

/ məgˈnɛɾɪk drʌm / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk drʌm / (BrE)
a cylindrical device coated with magnetic material, used for storing computer data and programs, especially in early computers
early computer data storage device

Examples

The original computer used a magnetic drum to store its programs and data.
Engineers had to carefully synchronize memory access with the rotation of the magnetic drum.
Today, a magnetic drum is mostly a historical artifact, replaced by faster storage devices.
