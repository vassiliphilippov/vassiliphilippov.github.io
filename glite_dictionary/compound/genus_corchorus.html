genus Corchorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kɔːrˈkɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kɔːˈkɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical herbs or subshrubs, widely distributed especially in Asia, including jute plants
a genus of tropical herbs, including jute plants

Examples

Several species within the genus Corchorus are cultivated for their valuable fiber known as jute.
Botanists have identified new herbs belonging to the genus Corchorus in Southeast Asia.
The leaves of the genus Corchorus are used in traditional cuisine in some African and Asian countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jute - a strong fiber obtained from plants of the genus Corchorus, used for making rope and cloth

Etymology

From New Latin, Corchorus is the classical Greek name for a wild plant.
