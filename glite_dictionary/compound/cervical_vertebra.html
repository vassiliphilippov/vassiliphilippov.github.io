cervical vertebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrvɪkəl ˈvɜːrtɪbrə / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪkəl ˈvɜːtɪbrə / (BrE)
any one of the seven bones that form the neck region of the human spine
one of the neck vertebrae in the spine
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the injury was to a cervical vertebra near the top of the spine.
An X-ray showed a fracture in the third cervical vertebra.
The flexibility of your neck depends largely on the cervical vertebrae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thoracic vertebra - one of the twelve vertebrae in the upper and mid-back region of the spine
  • Degree Higher: lumbar vertebra - one of the five vertebrae in the lower back region of the spine

Etymology

From Latin 'cervix' (neck) and 'vertebra' (joint, bone of the spine).
