valence electron

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪləns ɪˈlɛktrɑn / (AmE), / ˈveɪləns ɪˈlɛktrɒn / (BrE)
an electron in the outermost shell of an atom that can participate in forming chemical bonds with other atoms
electron in atom's outer shell for bonding
medical

Examples

The number of valence electrons determines an element's chemical reactivity.
Oxygen has six valence electrons in its outer shell.
Sodium donates its single valence electron to chlorine to form table salt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electron - a subatomic particle with negative charge
  • Antonym: core electron - an electron not in the outer shell of an atom

Etymology

From 'valence', relating to an atom's ability to combine with others, and 'electron'. 'Valence' from Latin 'valentia' (strength, capacity).
