rock rose

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌroʊz / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌrəʊz / (BrE)
any of various small flowering plants of the genus Helianthemum, bearing rose-like yellow, white, or reddish flowers and typically growing in rocky places.
flowering plant of the genus Helianthemum

Examples

A cluster of rock rose blooms brightened the edge of the garden path.
The rock rose thrives in dry, rocky soils with plenty of sunlight.
Gardeners often choose rock rose for its colorful, long-lasting blossoms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌroʊz / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌrəʊz / (BrE)
any of several small evergreen shrubs of the genus Cistus, native to southern Europe and North Africa, grown for their showy flowers and aromatic foliage
a shrub with showy flowers and aromatic leaves

Examples

The rock rose thrives in dry, sunny gardens where few other plants will survive.
She admired the delicate pink blooms of the rock rose along the hillside trail.
Gardeners appreciate the aromatic foliage of the rock rose in Mediterranean-style landscapes.
