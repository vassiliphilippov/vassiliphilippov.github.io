orphans' asylum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːr.fənz əˈsaɪ.ləm / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fəns əˈsaɪ.ləm / (BrE)
a public institution that provides care and housing for orphans, especially children without parents
institution for the care of orphans

Examples

Many children found a new home at the orphans' asylum after losing their parents.
The city raised funds to support the local orphans' asylum during the harsh winter.
In the 19th century, it was common for abandoned children to be placed in an orphans' asylum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphanage - an institution that takes care of orphans

Etymology

From 'orphan' and 'asylum', the latter from Greek 'asylon' meaning 'refuge'.
