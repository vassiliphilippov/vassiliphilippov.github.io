to spot promote

1. VERB

/ spɑt prəˈmoʊt / (AmE), / spɒt prəˈməʊt / (BrE)
to promote someone immediately or on the spot, usually bypassing normal procedures or steps
promote immediately, bypassing procedure

Examples

The manager decided to spot promote her after witnessing her excellent leadership during the crisis.
He was spot promoted to team leader following his outstanding performance in the project presentation.
Sometimes, exceptional employees are spot promoted when urgent vacancies need to be filled quickly.

Synset Examples

Supreme Bishop Digby had been spot-promoted to Archangel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to raise someone to a higher rank or position
