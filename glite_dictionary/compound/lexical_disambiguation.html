lexical disambiguation

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛksɪkəl dɪsˌæmbɪɡjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəl dɪsˌæmbɪɡjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of determining which meaning of a word with multiple possible senses is intended in a given context
identifying intended meaning of a word

Examples

Lexical disambiguation is crucial in machine translation to ensure words are interpreted correctly.
The software uses advanced algorithms for lexical disambiguation to handle ambiguous language.
Researchers have proposed new models to improve lexical disambiguation in natural language processing systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: word sense disambiguation - the process of determining which sense of a word is used in a context, especially by computers

Etymology

lexical from Latin 'lexicalis' (of words); disambiguation from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'ambiguus' (doubtful) + -ation (process).
