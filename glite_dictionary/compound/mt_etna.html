Mt Etna

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈɛtnə / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈɛtnə / (BrE)
a large, historically active volcano on the east coast of Sicily, Italy; the highest volcano in Europe
volcano in Sicily, Italy

Examples

Scientists regularly monitor Mt Etna for signs of volcanic activity.
Many tourists visit Mt Etna to hike its slopes and enjoy the views.
The eruption of Mt Etna has shaped the landscape of eastern Sicily for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vesuvius - a famous volcano near Naples, Italy
  • Synonym: volcano - an opening in Earth's crust that allows lava, ash, and gases to escape

Etymology

From Latin 'Aetna', from Ancient Greek 'Αἴτνα' (Aítnā), name of the volcano.
