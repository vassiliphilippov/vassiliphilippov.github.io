Petersburg Campaign

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːtɚzˌbɝːɡ kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəzˌbɜːɡ kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
the final military campaign of the American Civil War (1864–65), in which Union forces under Grant besieged and eventually defeated Confederate forces under Lee at Petersburg, Virginia
final campaign of the American Civil War (1864–65)

Examples

The Petersburg Campaign marked a turning point in the American Civil War.
Many historians study the tactics used during the Petersburg Campaign.
The long siege of the Petersburg Campaign led to the eventual fall of Richmond.
