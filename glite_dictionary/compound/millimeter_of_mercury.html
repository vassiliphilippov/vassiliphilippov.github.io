millimeter of mercury

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪləˌmitər əv ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌmiːtə əv ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to 0.001316 atmosphere, commonly used in measuring blood pressure and in scientific contexts
unit of pressure (0.001316 atm)
medical

Examples

The patient's blood pressure was recorded as 120 over 80 millimeters of mercury.
Atmospheric pressure at sea level is approximately 760 millimeters of mercury.
The old barometer measures pressure in millimeters of mercury, not in pascals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mmHg - abbreviation for millimeter of mercury, unit of pressure
  • Degree Higher: atmosphere - unit of pressure equal to the pressure of Earth's atmosphere at sea level
  • Synonym: pascal - the SI unit of pressure, equal to one newton per square meter

Etymology

Named for the use of a millimeter of mercury in barometers, following research by Evangelista Torricelli.
