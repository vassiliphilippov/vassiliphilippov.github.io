hard up

1. ADJ

/ hɑrd ˈʌp / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈʌp / (BrE)
having very little money; in a difficult financial situation, especially temporarily
having little money; financially struggling
informal

Examples

After the unexpected repairs, they were hard up for cash.
Many students find themselves hard up at the end of the semester.
Being hard up, he had to skip his vacation plans this year.

Synset Examples

After losing his job, he was really hard up.
Being hard up, they started cooking at home more.
She felt hard up for cash before payday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broke - having no money
  • Antonym: flush - having plenty of money
