national income

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənəl ˈɪnˌkʌm / (AmE), / ˈnæʃnəl ˈɪŋkʌm / (BrE)
the total value of all income earned within a nation, including wages, profits, interest, rents, and pension payments, during a specified period (usually one year)
total income earned in a nation in a period

Examples

The government's report showed that national income had increased steadily over the past five years.
A drop in national income can signal economic trouble for a country.
Economists use national income statistics to compare living standards between nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gross domestic product - the total value of goods and services produced within a country in a year
  • Degree Lower: personal income - individuals' total earnings from wages, investments, etc.
  • Synonym: gross national product - the total value of goods and services produced by a country's residents, including those abroad
