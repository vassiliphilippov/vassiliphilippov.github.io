white-bellied swallow

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈbɛlid ˈswɑloʊ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈbɛlɪd ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
a bluish-green and white North American swallow, known for nesting in tree cavities; also called tree swallow
North American swallow species

Examples

The white-bellied swallow is commonly seen near lakes and rivers during the summer months.
Researchers observed a white-bellied swallow building a nest in an old tree cavity.
A flock of white-bellied swallows darted across the open field catching insects in mid-air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree swallow - North American swallow also known as white-bellied swallow (Tachycineta bicolor)
