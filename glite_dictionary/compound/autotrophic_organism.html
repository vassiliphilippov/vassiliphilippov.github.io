autotrophic organism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˈtroʊfɪk ˈɔːɡənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈtrɒfɪk ˈɔːɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
An organism that can produce its own food from inorganic substances, typically through photosynthesis or chemosynthesis.
organism that produces its own food

Examples

Green plants are classic examples of autotrophic organisms.
Unlike animals, autotrophic organisms can survive without consuming other living things.
Algae are also considered autotrophic organisms because they synthesize their own nourishment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterotrophic organism - organism that cannot make its own food and must consume others

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'trophē' (nourishment).
