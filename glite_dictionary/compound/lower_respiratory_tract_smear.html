lower respiratory tract smear

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊər rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔri trækt smɪr / (AmE), / ˈləʊə rɪˌspɪrəˌtəri trɑːkt smɪə / (BrE)
a sample collected from the lower respiratory tract, prepared on a microscope slide for cytological examination, especially to diagnose lung diseases
sample for lung disease diagnosis
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a lower respiratory tract smear to check for signs of infection in the lungs.
A lower respiratory tract smear can help detect cancerous cells in patients with persistent cough.
Pathologists examined the lower respiratory tract smear for evidence of tuberculosis.
