water wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːtər wiːl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə wiːl / (BrE)
A large wheel driven by the flow of water, used to power machinery or generate energy, especially before the use of electricity.
wheel driven by water to generate power

Examples

The old mill still uses a water wheel to grind wheat into flour.
Engineers examined the ancient water wheel to study early methods of harnessing energy.
A large water wheel was once the main source of power for the textile factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbine - machine for producing continuous power by a wheel or rotor

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtəˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌwiːl / (BrE)
A large wheel with buckets or compartments on its rim, used to raise water from a stream or pond, typically for irrigation or powering machinery.
wheel for raising or moving water

Examples

The old mill still has a functioning water wheel that once powered its machinery.
Farmers in ancient times used a water wheel to irrigate their fields.
The museum displayed a model of a water wheel used for lifting water from the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noria - a type of water wheel for lifting water

Etymology

From Middle English 'water' + 'wheel', denoting a wheel turned by or used to move water.
