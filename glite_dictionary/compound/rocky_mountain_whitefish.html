Rocky Mountain whitefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑki ˈmaʊntən ˈwaɪtfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɒki ˈmaʊntɪn ˈwaɪtfɪʃ / (BrE)
A species of freshwater whitefish (Prosopium williamsoni) found in the rivers and lakes of the western United States and Canada.
freshwater whitefish species of western North America

Examples

Anglers often try to catch Rocky Mountain whitefish in the cool streams of Montana.
The population of Rocky Mountain whitefish has declined in some areas due to environmental changes.
Scientists study the habitat preferences of the Rocky Mountain whitefish to help conserve the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - a family of freshwater fish with pale flesh
