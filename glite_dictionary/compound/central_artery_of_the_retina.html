central artery of the retina

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəl ˈɑːrtəri əv ðə rɪˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈɑːtəri əv ðə rɪˈtaɪnə / (BrE)
a branch of the ophthalmic artery that enters the eye alongside the optic nerve and supplies blood to the retina
artery supplying blood to the retina
medical

Examples

Blockage of the central artery of the retina can lead to sudden vision loss.
The ophthalmologist examined the flow in the central artery of the retina.
A healthy central artery of the retina is crucial for maintaining proper vision.
