Battle of the Marne

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv ðə ˈmɑrn / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ðə ˈmɑːn / (BrE)
a major battle during World War I in northwestern France, where Allied forces defeated the German army in 1918
World War I battle in France, 1918

Examples

The Battle of the Marne marked a turning point in World War I.
Many historians study the tactics used during the Battle of the Marne.
Commemorations of the Battle of the Marne are held annually in France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Verdun - a major World War I battle fought in 1916 between French and German forces
  • Synonym: Battle of the Somme - a large-scale World War I battle in 1916 on the Western Front
