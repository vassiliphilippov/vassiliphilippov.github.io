housing start

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊzɪŋ stɑrt / (AmE), / ˈhaʊzɪŋ stɑːt / (BrE)
the beginning of construction on a new house or residential building, often used as an economic indicator
start of construction on a new house

Examples

The government reported an increase in housing starts this quarter, suggesting a strengthening economy.
Rising interest rates often cause housing starts to decline as borrowing becomes more expensive.
Economists monitor housing starts as a leading indicator of economic performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: building start - the beginning of construction on any building
