Christ plant

1. NOUN

/ kraɪst plænt / (AmE), / kraɪst plɑːnt / (BrE)
A spiny shrub native to Madagascar, with long, woody stems, few leaves, and scarlet bracts, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
spiny ornamental shrub with scarlet bracts

Examples

She pruned the branches of her Christ plant to encourage more blooms.
The Christ plant is popular in gardens for its resilience to dry conditions.
Its vivid red bracts make the Christ plant a striking addition to any greenhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown of thorns - another common name for Euphorbia milii

Etymology

Named for its thorny stems, said to resemble the crown of thorns worn by Christ.
