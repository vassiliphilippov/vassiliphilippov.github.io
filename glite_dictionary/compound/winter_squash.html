winter squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a type of squash with a thick rind and yellow to orange flesh, harvested in the fall and suitable for long storage
squash with thick rind, stores well

Examples

We roasted the winter squash with herbs and served it as a side dish.
Proper storage allows winter squash to be enjoyed well into the winter months.
Delicata and butternut are both popular varieties of winter squash.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: summer squash - squash with tender skin and flesh, harvested in the summer

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntər skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A type of squash, such as butternut or acorn, that has a hard rind and matures in late summer or autumn, typically stored for use during the winter.
a hard-rinded squash harvested in autumn

Examples

I roasted a winter squash with herbs and olive oil for dinner last night.
Many recipes for soup and stew call for winter squash as a main ingredient.
Farmers harvest winter squash in the fall so it can be enjoyed throughout the colder months.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: summer squash - a soft-skinned type of squash harvested when immature
