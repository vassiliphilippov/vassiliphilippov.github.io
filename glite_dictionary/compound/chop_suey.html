chop suey

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑp ˈsuː.i / (AmE), / tʃɒp ˈsuː.i / (BrE)
A Chinese-style dish consisting of meat or fish stir-fried with vegetables and seasoned with soy sauce, typically served with rice; originally created in the United States.
Chinese-style stir-fry dish with meat and vegetables

Examples

We ordered chop suey and fried rice at the local Chinese restaurant.
Her favorite comfort food is homemade chop suey packed with vegetables.
The menu features classic American-Chinese dishes like chop suey and egg foo young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir-fry - Dish of ingredients quickly fried in a small amount of oil over high heat

Etymology

From Chinese Pidgin English, possibly from Cantonese tsaap sui (杂碎), meaning 'miscellaneous leftovers.'
