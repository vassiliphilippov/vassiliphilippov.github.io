double agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a spy who pretends to spy for one country or organization while actually spying for another, usually for two opposing sides
spy who works for two opposing sides

Examples

He was later revealed to be a double agent passing secrets to the enemy.
The novel's main character is a double agent caught between two intelligence agencies.
During the Cold War, several double agents played key roles in major espionage operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - a person employed to collect secret information
