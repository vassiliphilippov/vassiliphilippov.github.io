fluid drive

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪd ˌdraɪv / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪd ˌdraɪv / (BrE)
a device used in automotive transmissions that couples the engine to the drive shaft using a fluid to transmit power smoothly
type of automotive power coupling

Examples

The vintage car was equipped with fluid drive for a smoother ride.
One advantage of fluid drive is the reduced wear on gears.
Early 1950s Chryslers were famous for their innovative fluid drive systems.
