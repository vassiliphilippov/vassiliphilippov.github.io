perishingly cold

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛrɪʃɪŋli koʊld / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪʃɪŋli kəʊld / (BrE)
(of weather or conditions) extremely cold; unbearably cold.
extremely cold (of weather)

Examples

We waited for the bus in the perishingly cold wind for nearly an hour.
She forgot her gloves, so her hands were perishingly cold during the walk.
Camping was difficult because the nights became perishingly cold after sunset.

Synset Examples

It was perishingly cold outside last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freezing - very cold; at or below the freezing point
  • Degree Lower: chilly - uncomfortably or unpleasantly cold
  • Synonym: frigid - extremely cold in temperature

Etymology

Formed from 'perishingly' (from 'perish', from Old French 'periss-', Latin 'perire' meaning 'to die') plus 'cold'.
