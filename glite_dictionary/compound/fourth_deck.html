fourth deck

1. NOUN

/ fɔrθ dɛk / (AmE), / fɔːθ dɛk / (BrE)
the fourth and lowest deck on a large ship, often used for storage or crew accommodations
lowest deck on a large ship

Examples

The cargo was stored deep in the fourth deck to keep the upper decks clear.
Crew members rarely visited the fourth deck unless there was an inspection.
During the storm, water seeped into the fourth deck, causing concern among the sailors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: main deck - the principal deck of a ship
