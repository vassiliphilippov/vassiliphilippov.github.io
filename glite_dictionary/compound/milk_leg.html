milk leg

1. NOUN

/ mɪlk lɛg / (AmE), / mɪlk lɛg / (BrE)
a painful swelling of the leg caused by thrombosis of the femoral vein, typically occurring in women after childbirth
painful postpartum leg swelling (thrombosis)
medical archaic

Examples

After giving birth, she was diagnosed with milk leg due to severe pain and swelling.
In the past, milk leg was a common but dangerous postpartum complication.
The patient sought treatment for milk leg, a condition now rare thanks to modern medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlegmasia alba dolens - medical term for milk leg (painful swelling of the leg due to vein thrombosis)

Etymology

Refers to the pale, milk-like appearance of the swollen leg; first recorded in the 18th century.
