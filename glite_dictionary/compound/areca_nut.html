areca nut

1. NOUN

/ əˈriːkə nʌt / (AmE), / ˈærɪkə nʌt / (BrE)
the seed of the betel palm, commonly chewed with betel leaves and lime as a stimulant and digestive aid in South and Southeast Asia
seed of the betel palm chewed as a stimulant

Examples

In many parts of India, people chew areca nut with betel leaves as part of traditional ceremonies.
The doctor warned about the health risks associated with frequent areca nut consumption.
She bought a packet of dried areca nuts from the local market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betel nut - alternative term for areca nut

Etymology

From Areca, the genus name for the betel palm, which comes from Malayalam 'adakka', meaning areca nut.
