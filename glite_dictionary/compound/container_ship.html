container ship

1. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪnɚ ʃɪp / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnə ʃɪp / (BrE)
a large cargo vessel designed to carry standardized shipping containers
large vessel for containerized cargo

Examples

The container ship docked at the port after a three-week journey across the Pacific Ocean.
A giant container ship can transport thousands of containers at once, making it essential for global trade.
Due to heavy fog, the container ship was delayed in entering the harbor.

Synset Examples

the weight of the documentation of all the consignments on board a contemporary container ship can exceed 90 pounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cargo ship - a ship designed to carry goods rather than passengers
