nonlinear correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnˈlɪniɚ ˌkɔːrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈlɪniə ˌkɒrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between two variables in which the rate of change is not constant, so the pattern does not form a straight line when graphed
statistical relationship with varying rates of change

Examples

The researchers found a nonlinear correlation between exercise duration and weight loss in the study group.
In many biological systems, variables often show a nonlinear correlation rather than a simple linear one.
A scatter plot helped the team visualize the nonlinear correlation present in their data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linear correlation - a relationship in which the rate of change between variables is constant

Etymology

Formed from 'nonlinear' (not linear) and 'correlation' (from Latin 'correlatio', meaning relation together).
