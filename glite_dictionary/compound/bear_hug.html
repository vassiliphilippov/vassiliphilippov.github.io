bear hug

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛr ˌhʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈbeə ˌhʌɡ / (BrE)
A strong embrace in which one locks their arms tightly around another, especially as a wrestling hold.
wrestling hold with arms locked tightly

Examples

The wrestler trapped his opponent in a bear hug and squeezed tightly.
She gave her friend a bear hug after not seeing her for years.
His bear hug was so strong it nearly took my breath away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hug - an act of holding someone tightly in one's arms

2. NOUN

/ bɛr hʌɡ / (AmE), / beə hʌɡ / (BrE)
an offer to buy a company at a price so attractive that the company's directors are almost forced to accept it, typically due to shareholder expectations
attractive takeover bid (business)

Examples

The company received a bear hug from a larger competitor, making rejection almost impossible for the board.
Shareholders were thrilled when the bear hug was announced, as it promised a high return on their investments.
Despite the generous bear hug, the directors hesitated before finally recommending acceptance to the shareholders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takeover bid - an offer to purchase a controlling interest in a company

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'bear hug,' suggesting a forceful but enticing embrace.
