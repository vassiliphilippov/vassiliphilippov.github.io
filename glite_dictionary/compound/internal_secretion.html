internal secretion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl sɪˈkriʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnl sɪˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
a substance produced by an endocrine gland and released directly into the bloodstream to act on specific tissues
secretion from an endocrine gland into the blood
medical

Examples

The adrenal glands are responsible for the internal secretion of important hormones like adrenaline.
Damage to the thyroid affects its internal secretion, which can lead to health issues.
Insulin is an example of an internal secretion that regulates blood sugar levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone - a regulatory substance produced in an organism and transported in tissue fluids

Etymology

From Latin 'internus' (inward) and 'secretio' (separation, secretion).
