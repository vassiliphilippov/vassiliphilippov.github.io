erectile organ

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɛk.taɪl ˈɔːr.ɡən / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛk.taɪl ˈɔː.ɡən / (BrE)
an organ in the body that contains erectile tissue and becomes enlarged and firm due to blood flow, such as the penis or clitoris
organ containing erectile tissue
medical

Examples

The penis is the primary erectile organ in male mammals.
Researchers studied the structure of the clitoris as an erectile organ in female mammals.
Most erectile organs function by filling with blood and becoming rigid during arousal.

Etymology

From Latin 'erectus' (upright) and Greek 'organon' (instrument, organ).
