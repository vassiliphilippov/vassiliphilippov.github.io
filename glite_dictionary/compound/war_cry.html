war cry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr ˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌkraɪ / (BrE)
a loud shout or phrase used to encourage or rally soldiers, especially before battle
yell to rally troops in battle

Examples

The general's fierce war cry inspired his troops before the final charge.
As the battle began, their war cry echoed across the valley.
Each tribe had its own distinctive war cry to rally the warriors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle cry - a yell or phrase shouted in battle for encouragement

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr ˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌkraɪ / (BrE)
a phrase or slogan used to inspire or rally people to support a cause, movement, or group
slogan used to rally support

Examples

The candidate adopted 'Change Now!' as her war cry during the election campaign.
Freedom was the war cry of the movement's supporters.
Their team's war cry echoed through the stadium, energizing the fans.

Synset Examples

a cry to arms
our watchword will be ‘democracy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a short, memorable phrase used in advertising or publicity

Etymology

Originally referred to the shouted call used by warriors in battle; 'war' from Old English 'werre', and 'cry' from Old French 'crier' (to cry out).
