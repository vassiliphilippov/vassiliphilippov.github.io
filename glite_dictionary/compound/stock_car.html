stock car

1. NOUN

/ stɑk kɑr / (AmE), / stɒk kɑː / (BrE)
a car kept in a dealer's inventory for general sale, as opposed to a custom-ordered vehicle
car held by a dealer for regular sale

Examples

The dealership has several stock cars available for immediate purchase.
He chose a stock car from the lot instead of placing a special order.
Most buyers prefer to test drive a stock car before making a decision.

Etymology

Derived from 'stock' (inventory) and 'car'.

2. NOUN

/ stɑk kɑr / (AmE), / stɒk kɑː / (BrE)
A racing car with the basic frame and structure of a commercially available automobile, often modified for speed and safety in competitions.
racing car based on a standard production model

Examples

The stock car sped past the finish line, thrilling the cheering crowd.
He spent years restoring an old Chevy into a competitive stock car.
Many American motorsport events feature stock cars rather than open-wheel racers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racing car - a car designed and built for racing
