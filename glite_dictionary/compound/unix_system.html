UNIX system

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɪks ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪks ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A powerful, multiuser operating system known for its stability and influence on many modern systems, originally developed in the 1970s.
multiuser computer operating system

Examples

Many servers around the world run a UNIX system because of its stability and reliability.
After taking the course, she felt confident navigating the UNIX system using the command line.
The UNIX system has influenced the design of many modern operating systems, including Linux and macOS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Linux - an operating system modeled on UNIX

Etymology

From 'Uniplexed Information and Computing Service'; coined as a pun on 'Multics' in 1969 at Bell Labs.
