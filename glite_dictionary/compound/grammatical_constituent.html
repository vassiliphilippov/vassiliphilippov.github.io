grammatical constituent

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈmætɪkəl kənˈstɪtʃuənt / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmætɪkəl kənˈstɪtjʊənt / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or clause that forms a part of a larger grammatical structure within a sentence
part of a grammatical structure

Examples

The subject is a grammatical constituent that typically comes before the verb.
A noun phrase can function as a grammatical constituent within a sentence.
Understanding each grammatical constituent helps in analyzing sentence structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrase - a group of words functioning as a unit in a sentence
  • Synonym: clause - a group of words with a subject and verb, forming part of a sentence

Etymology

constituent from Latin 'constituens', present participle of 'constituere' (to set up, establish); grammatical from Greek 'grammatikos'.
