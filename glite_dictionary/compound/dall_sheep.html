Dall sheep

1. NOUN

/ dɔl ʃip / (AmE), / dɔːl ʃiːp / (BrE)
a large, white wild sheep native to northwestern Canada and Alaska, known for its curved horns
large white wild sheep of northwestern Canada and Alaska

Examples

We spotted a group of Dall sheep on the rocky cliffs during our hike in Alaska.
The Dall sheep is easily recognized by its thick white coat and impressive curled horns.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the Dall sheep population in northern Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bighorn sheep - a related wild sheep species of North America
  • Degree Lower: mountain goat - a related climber found in similar northern terrain

Etymology

Named after William Healey Dall, American naturalist and explorer (1845–1927).
