Tang dynasty

1. PROPN

/ tæŋ ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / tæŋ ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from 618 to 907, known for its cultural, political, and economic achievements
imperial Chinese dynasty (618–907)

Examples

The Tang dynasty is often considered a golden age of Chinese civilization.
Many poets from the Tang dynasty are still celebrated today for their literary achievements.
The influence of the Tang dynasty extended far beyond the borders of China.

Etymology

From Chinese 唐 (Táng), the ruling family name, combined with 'dynasty' from Greek via Latin.
