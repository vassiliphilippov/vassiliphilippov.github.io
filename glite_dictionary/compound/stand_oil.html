stand oil

1. NOUN

/ stænd ɔɪl / (AmE), / stænd ɔɪl / (BrE)
a thick, slow-drying oil made by heating linseed, tung, or soya oil to high temperatures, used especially as a paint medium
thick, heat-treated drying oil used in paints

Examples

Artists often mix stand oil into their paints to create a smoother, glossier finish.
The manufacturer recommends using stand oil for improved paint durability and flexibility.
Stand oil is less yellowing than raw linseed oil, making it popular among conservators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linseed oil - an oil extracted from flax seeds, used as a drying oil in paint
  • Degree Higher: drying oil - an oil that hardens to a tough, solid film after a period of exposure to air
