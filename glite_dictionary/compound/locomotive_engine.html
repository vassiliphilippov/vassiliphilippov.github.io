locomotive engine

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ.kəˈmoʊ.tɪv ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kəˈməʊ.tɪv ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A self-propelled railway vehicle that supplies the motive power for a train.
railway vehicle that pulls trains

Examples

The museum restored an old locomotive engine to working condition.
A powerful locomotive engine hauled the freight across the country.
Steam rose from the locomotive engine as it prepared to depart the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotive - self-propelled railway vehicle for pulling trains

Etymology

From Latin 'loco' (from a place) and Medieval Latin 'motivus' (causing motion), combined with 'engine'.
