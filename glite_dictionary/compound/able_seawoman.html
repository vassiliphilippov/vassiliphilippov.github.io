able seawoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪbəl ˈsiːˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈeɪbl ˈsiːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female sailor in the merchant marine who is trained in special skills and qualified for particular duties.
female trained merchant marine sailor

Examples

She worked her way up the ranks to become an able seawoman on the cargo ship.
The crew included an experienced able seawoman who specialized in navigation.
An able seawoman must complete rigorous training before serving on international vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: able seaman - a sailor in the merchant marine trained in special skills

Etymology

Modelled on 'able seaman', using 'seawoman' as the female equivalent.
