water-cooled reactor

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ kuːld riˈæktɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə kuːld rɪˈæktə / (BrE)
A nuclear reactor in which water is used as the primary coolant to remove heat from the reactor core.
nuclear reactor using water as coolant

Examples

The new water-cooled reactor design improves both efficiency and safety measures.
Japan relies heavily on water-cooled reactors for its nuclear energy production.
Engineers are exploring ways to reduce water consumption in water-cooled reactor systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear reactor - a device used to initiate and control a sustained nuclear chain reaction
