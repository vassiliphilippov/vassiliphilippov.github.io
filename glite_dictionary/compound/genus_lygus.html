genus Lygus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs ˈlaɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs ˈlaɪɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of plant-sucking bugs in the family Miridae, known for damaging crops such as alfalfa and cotton.
a genus of plant-sucking bugs

Examples

Many farmers struggle with infestations of genus Lygus in their cotton fields.
The genus Lygus includes several economically important pest species.
Scientists studied the effects of pesticides on the genus Lygus population.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Lygus', the formal genus name assigned in scientific classification.
