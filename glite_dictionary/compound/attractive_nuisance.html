attractive nuisance

1. NOUN

/ əˈtræktɪv ˈnusəns / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪv ˈnjuːsəns / (BrE)
a potentially dangerous object or condition on a property that is likely to attract children and may cause them harm
hazard on property that may attract children
legal

Examples

The abandoned well was deemed an attractive nuisance because neighborhood children often played nearby.
Owners are required by law to secure any attractive nuisance on their property, such as unused machinery.
The colorful, unfenced trampoline was considered an attractive nuisance by local authorities.

Synset Examples

their swimming pool is an attractive nuisance; they should fence it in

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nuisance - something causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Degree Higher: hazard - a danger or risk
