River Thames

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɪvɚ ˈtɛmz / (AmE), / ˌrɪvə ˈtɛmz / (BrE)
the longest river in England, flowing eastward through London to the North Sea
the main river flowing through London

Examples

Boats of all shapes and sizes sailed along the River Thames during the festival.
The historic bridges crossing the River Thames are popular landmarks in London.
Pollution in the River Thames has decreased significantly over the last few decades.

Etymology

From ancient Celtic *Tamesas*, meaning 'dark', later Latinized as *Tamesis* and used in English as Thames.
