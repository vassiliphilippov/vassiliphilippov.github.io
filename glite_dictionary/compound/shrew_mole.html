shrew mole

1. NOUN

/ ʃruː moʊl / (AmE), / ʃruː məʊl / (BrE)
a small, slender mole with a long snout and tail, resembling a shrew but belonging to the mole family
a slender mole with a long snout and tail

Examples

The shrew mole is difficult to spot because it spends most of its life underground.
Unlike true shrews, the shrew mole has powerful digging forelimbs for burrowing.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of the Japanese shrew mole in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - a small burrowing mammal with velvety fur, a pointed snout, and very small eyes
