Harding grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdɪŋ ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdɪŋ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to Australia and South Africa, often cultivated in North America as a forage grass for livestock.
perennial forage grass species

Examples

The farmer planted Harding grass in the pasture to provide nutritious feed for his cattle.
Harding grass is drought-resistant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it ideal for arid regions.
Researchers are studying the impact of Harding grass on native plant biodiversity in California.
