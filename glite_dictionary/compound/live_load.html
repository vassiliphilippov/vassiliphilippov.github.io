live load

1. NOUN

/ laɪv loʊd / (AmE), / laɪv ləʊd / (BrE)
A load on a structure, such as a bridge or building, that is variable or movable, such as people, vehicles, or furniture.
variable structural load (e.g., traffic, people)

Examples

Engineers calculate the live load on a bridge to ensure it can safely carry moving traffic.
The live load in an office building increases during working hours as more people are present.
Designers must account for both the dead load and the live load when planning a new structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dead load - the permanent, stationary load on a structure from its own weight
