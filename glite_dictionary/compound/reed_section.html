reed section

1. NOUN

/ rid ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / riːd ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the group of musicians in a band or orchestra who play reed instruments, such as clarinets and saxophones
musicians playing reed instruments in a group

Examples

The reed section played a beautiful harmony during the concert.
We need another clarinet player for the reed section in the school orchestra.
The jazz band's reed section includes saxophones, clarinets, and a bassoon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brass section - the group of musicians playing brass instruments in a band or orchestra
  • Antonym: string section - the group of musicians playing string instruments in a band or orchestra
  • Synonym: woodwind section - the section of an orchestra composed of woodwind instruments
