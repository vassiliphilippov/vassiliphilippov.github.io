hush puppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌʃ ˌpʌpi / (AmE), / ˈhʌʃ ˌpʌpi / (BrE)
A small ball of seasoned cornmeal batter that is deep-fried, typically served as a side dish in Southern American cuisine.
deep-fried cornmeal ball, southern US dish
american

Examples

We ordered a basket of hush puppies to share with our fried catfish.
The restaurant is famous for its crispy hush puppy appetizers.
She made hush puppies from scratch for the family picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornbread - a type of bread made from cornmeal, popular in the Southern US

Etymology

Originally from Southern United States; possibly from the practice of tossing fried cornmeal bits to dogs to keep them quiet ('hush the puppies').
