positive pole

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːzətɪv poʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv pəʊl / (BrE)
the terminal of a battery or electrical device that has a positive electric charge compared to the other terminal
the positively charged terminal of a battery

Examples

Always connect the red wire to the positive pole of the battery to avoid short circuits.
The positive pole is often marked with a plus sign for easy identification.
Make sure the device's positive pole matches the battery's terminal before turning it on.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: negative pole - the terminal of a battery with a negative electric charge

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːzətɪv poʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv pəʊl / (BrE)
the end of a magnet that points toward the north when the magnet is freely suspended; also called the north pole of a magnet.
north-seeking pole of a magnet

Examples

When suspended by a string, the positive pole of the magnet always points northward.
Scientists placed a compass near the positive pole to observe the direction of magnetic force.
The positive pole is attracted to the Earth's magnetic south pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north pole - the end of a magnet that points toward the Earth's north
  • Antonym: negative pole - the end of a magnet that points toward the south
