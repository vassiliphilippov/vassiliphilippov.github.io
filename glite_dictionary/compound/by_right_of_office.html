by right of office

1. ADV

/ baɪ raɪt əv ˈɑːfɪs / (AmE), / baɪ raɪt əv ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
because of the powers or privileges granted by one's official position or role; by virtue of holding an office.
by virtue of one's official position

Examples

The dean became a member of the board by right of office.
He chairs the committee by right of office, not by election.
As mayor, she is involved in the council by right of office.

Synset Examples

the president sat on the committee ex officio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ex officio - by virtue of one's office or official position
