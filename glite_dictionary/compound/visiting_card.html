visiting card

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪzɪtɪŋ kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪtɪŋ kɑːd / (BrE)
a small card bearing a person's name and sometimes address, left as a token of a visit or to announce one's presence
small card left to show a visit

Examples

She left her visiting card at the reception desk when no one was home.
In Victorian times, exchanging visiting cards was an important social custom.
He handed the butler his visiting card to announce his arrival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business card - a card bearing professional contact information

Etymology

From the practice of leaving cards during social visits in the 18th–19th centuries.
