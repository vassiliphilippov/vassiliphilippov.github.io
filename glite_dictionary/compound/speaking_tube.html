speaking tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiːkɪŋ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈspiːkɪŋ tjuːb / (BrE)
a tube designed to carry the sound of a person's voice from one room to another, often used before electronic communication devices
tube for carrying voice sound between rooms

Examples

The captain shouted orders through the speaking tube to the engine room below.
Before intercoms, offices sometimes relied on a speaking tube for communication between floors.
She leaned in close to the speaking tube to make sure her message would be heard clearly.
