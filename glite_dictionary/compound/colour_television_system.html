colour television system

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər ˌtɛləˌvɪʒən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˌtɛlɪˌvɪʒən ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system for broadcasting and receiving television signals that display images in colour rather than black and white.
system for broadcasting TV in colour

Examples

The introduction of the colour television system revolutionized the way people experienced home entertainment.
Engineers are working to improve the reliability of the colour television system in rural areas.
It was not until the 1970s that the colour television system became widely available in households.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black-and-white television - a television that displays images in shades of grey
