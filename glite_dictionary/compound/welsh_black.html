Welsh Black

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ blæk / (AmE), / wɛlʃ blak / (BrE)
a breed of hardy, dual-purpose cattle, black in color, developed in Wales for both beef and milk production
breed of Welsh black cattle

Examples

The Welsh Black is renowned for its adaptability to harsh climates.
Many farmers in Wales raise Welsh Black cattle for both milk and beef production.
A herd of Welsh Black grazed peacefully in the hillside pasture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hereford - a British breed of beef cattle known for its red body and white face
  • Synonym: Jersey - a breed of dairy cattle from Jersey, noted for high butterfat milk

Etymology

Named after Wales, where the breed originated, and its characteristic black color.
