decision-making

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɪʒ.ən ˌmeɪ.kɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪʒ.ən ˌmeɪ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making choices or reaching conclusions, especially after considering various alternatives
the process of making decisions

Examples

Good decision-making is critical for effective leadership in any organization.
He realized that stress was affecting his decision-making abilities.
The policy aims to involve more people in the decision-making process.

Synset Examples

His inertia kept him from making important decisions.
Divitis can lead to unnecessary complications in decision-making.
The softheaded decision-making caused a lot of confusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - the ability to make considered decisions or come to sensible conclusions

Etymology

From 'decision' + 'making', first attested in English in the mid-20th century.
