court order

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / kɔːt ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an official directive issued by a court of law requiring a person to do or refrain from doing a specific act
official directive from a court
legal

Examples

The company complied with the court order and released the necessary documents to the investigators.
Failure to obey a court order can result in fines or imprisonment.
A restraining order is a type of court order designed to protect individuals from harm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injunction - an order from a court preventing someone from doing something
  • Synonym: subpoena - a writ ordering a person to attend a court
