record cover

1. NOUN

/ ˈrekərd ˌkʌvər / (AmE), / ˈrekɔːd ˌkʌvə / (BrE)
a protective sleeve, usually made of cardboard or paper, designed to hold and protect a phonograph record
protective sleeve for a phonograph record

Examples

He carefully slipped the vinyl back into its record cover to keep it from getting scratched.
The record cover featured iconic artwork that made the album instantly recognizable.
Collectors often pay a premium for albums with the original record cover in good condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: album cover - the cover or sleeve of a music album, often featuring artwork
