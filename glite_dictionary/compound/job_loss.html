job loss

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑb lɔs / (AmE), / dʒɒb lɒs / (BrE)
the situation in which someone loses their job, especially as a result of economic factors or organizational changes
the state of losing one's job

Examples

The company announced further job loss due to the economic downturn.
Automation in the factory led to significant job loss over the past year.
Policies were implemented to help workers affected by job loss.

Synset Examples

Luddism reflects concerns about job loss from machines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unemployment - the state of not having a job
