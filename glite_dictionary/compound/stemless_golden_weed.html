stemless golden weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛm.ləs ˈɡoʊl.dən wiːd / (AmE), / ˈstɛm.ləs ˈɡəʊl.dən wiːd / (BrE)
A dark green, upright herb native to the northwestern United States and southwestern Canada, with stiff leaves in dense tufts and yellow flower heads; sometimes placed in the genus Haplopappus.
herbaceous plant of NW US and SW Canada

Examples

The stemless golden weed flourishes in well-drained soils across the northern prairies.
Wildlife often depend on the stemless golden weed for cover during the early summer.
Botanists debated whether to classify the stemless golden weed under Haplopappus or a distinct genus.
