arterial blood

1. NOUN

/ ɑɹˈtɪɹ.i.əl blʌd / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪə.ri.əl blʌd / (BrE)
blood that is found in arteries, typically rich in oxygen except in the pulmonary artery
blood present in arteries
medical

Examples

Measurement of arterial blood gases is essential in monitoring critically ill patients.
Arterial blood is usually bright red due to its high oxygen content.
Doctors collected a sample of arterial blood for analysis during the procedure.

Synset Examples

except for the pulmonary artery the arterial blood is rich in oxygen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: venous blood - blood found in veins, typically low in oxygen

Etymology

From 'arterial' (relating to arteries) + 'blood'. 'Arterial' from Latin 'arteria' (artery).
