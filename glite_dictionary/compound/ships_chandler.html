ship's chandler

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪps ˈtʃændlər / (AmE), / ʃɪps ˈtʃændlə / (BrE)
a dealer who supplies ships with equipment, such as sails, ropes, and other provisions needed for sailing
dealer in supplies for ships

Examples

The ship's chandler delivered the ropes and canvas needed for the long voyage.
Before setting sail, the captain stopped by the ship's chandler to restock provisions.
For centuries, every port town had at least one ship's chandler supplying mariners with essential equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chandler - a dealer in supplies and equipment, especially for ships

Etymology

From chandler, meaning 'dealer in provisions', itself from Old French *chandelier* (candlemaker), reflecting the merchant's original trade, extended to nautical supplies.
