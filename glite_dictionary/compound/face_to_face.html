face to face

1. ADV

/ ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
with people or things positioned directly opposite each other; directly facing one another
directly facing each other

Examples

The two chairs were placed face to face for the interview.
Their offices were face to face across the hallway, making it easy to talk.
At the negotiation table, the leaders sat face to face to discuss the agreement.

Synset Examples

the two photographs lay face-to-face on the table
lived all their lives in houses face-to-face across the street
they sat opposite at the table

2. ADV

/ ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
with people close together so that they are looking directly at each other; involving direct personal interaction or confrontation.
in direct personal contact; directly confronting

Examples

After months of online meetings, the team finally met face to face in the office.
The teacher prefers to resolve conflicts face to face rather than through email.
Standing face to face with her childhood hero, she could hardly believe it.

Synset Examples

the boy and the policeman suddenly came face-to-face at the corner
they spoke face to face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in person - in the presence of each other, as opposed to remotely
  • Antonym: remotely - from a distance, without direct physical contact
