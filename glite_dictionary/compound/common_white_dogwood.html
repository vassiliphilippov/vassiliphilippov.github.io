common white dogwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən waɪt ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən waɪt ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous tree admired for its large white or pink bracts, vivid autumn foliage, and clusters of red berries.
deciduous tree with white or pink bracts

Examples

The common white dogwood brightens gardens in spring with its showy flowers.
In autumn, the common white dogwood displays vibrant red and purple foliage.
The red berries of the common white dogwood attract birds in late fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - a tree or shrub of the genus Cornus, known for its showy bracts and berries
