Jupiter Fulminator

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtər ˌfʊlmɪˈneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə ˌfʌlmɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
an epithet of Jupiter, representing him as the bringer or hurler of lightning in Roman mythology
epithet of Jupiter as lightning-bringer

Examples

The ancient Romans prayed to Jupiter Fulminator during thunderstorms to protect their crops.
A temple was dedicated to Jupiter Fulminator following a miraculous deliverance by lightning.
The poet described the wrath of Jupiter Fulminator in vivid, electrifying terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - chief god of the Roman pantheon

Etymology

From Latin Iuppiter Fulminator, 'Jupiter the Hurler of Lightning'.
