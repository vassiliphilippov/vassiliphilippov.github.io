fetal distress

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːt̬l dɪˈstrɛs / (AmE), / ˈfiːtl dɪˈstres / (BrE)
A medical condition during pregnancy in which a fetus shows signs of abnormal heart rhythm or insufficient oxygen, indicating potential health risk.
abnormal fetal condition before birth
medical

Examples

The doctors monitored for fetal distress throughout labor to ensure the baby remained healthy.
Signs of fetal distress may prompt an emergency delivery to protect the baby's well-being.
Abnormal heart rate patterns are common indicators of fetal distress during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: asphyxia - lack of oxygen causing unconsciousness or death; linked as a possible consequence or cause in fetal cases
