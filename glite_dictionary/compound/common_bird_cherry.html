common bird cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən bɝd ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən bɜːd ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A small European cherry tree (Prunus padus) resembling the American chokecherry, known for its clusters of fragrant white flowers and small black fruit.
European cherry tree (Prunus padus) with white flowers

Examples

The common bird cherry is often planted in parks for its ornamental flowers and shade.
In spring, the common bird cherry attracts many pollinators with its fragrant white blossoms.
Berries from the common bird cherry are a favorite food for local birds in rural Europe.

Related Terms
