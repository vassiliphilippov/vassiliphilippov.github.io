Morgan le Fay

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɔrɡən lə ˈfeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡən lə ˈfeɪ / (BrE)
In Arthurian legend, a powerful enchantress who was the half-sister and often the adversary of King Arthur.
enchantress in Arthurian legend, Arthur's half-sister

Examples

Many tales depict Morgan le Fay using her magic to oppose King Arthur and his knights.
The mysterious character of Morgan le Fay appears in several adaptations of Arthurian legend.
Scholars continue to debate whether Morgan le Fay was truly wicked or simply misunderstood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: King Arthur - legendary king of Britain in Arthurian legends

Etymology

From Old French 'Morgain le Fay', where 'le Fay' derives from 'la fée', meaning 'the fairy'.
