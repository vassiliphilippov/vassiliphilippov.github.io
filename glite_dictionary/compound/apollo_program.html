Apollo program

1. NOUN

/ əˈpoʊloʊ ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / əˈpɒləʊ ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
the series of US space missions in the 1960s and 70s designed to land humans on the Moon and bring them safely back to Earth
US lunar spaceflight program (1960s–70s)

Examples

The Apollo program achieved the first successful Moon landing in 1969.
Many technological advances stemmed from research done for the Apollo program.
The Apollo program inspired a generation to pursue careers in science and engineering.

Synset Examples

the first lunar landing was achieved by the Apollo program on July 20, 1969

Etymology

Named after Apollo, the Greek god; chosen by NASA in 1960 for its connotations of aspiration and achievement.
