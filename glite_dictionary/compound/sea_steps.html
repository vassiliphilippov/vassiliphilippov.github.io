sea steps

1. NOUN

/ si stɛps / (AmE), / si stɛps / (BrE)
a ladder or steps lowered over the side of a ship to allow people to come aboard from the sea or a boat
ladder lowered from a ship's side

Examples

The sailor tossed down the sea steps to help the visitors come aboard.
For safety, always secure the sea steps before allowing passengers to descend.
He waited at the bottom of the sea steps until the small boat pulled alongside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangway - a passage or ramp by which people board or leave a ship
