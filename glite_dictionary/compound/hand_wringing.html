hand wringing

1. NOUN

/ hænd ˈrɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / hænd ˈrɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
Excessive worrying or nervous agitation about a situation, often expressed by repeatedly twisting or clasping one's hands.
excessive worrying, esp. shown by clasping hands

Examples

The constant hand wringing did nothing to address the root of the problem.
Media coverage often leads to public hand wringing after major disasters.
Her hand wringing was obvious as she waited for the results.

Synset Examples

Instead of hand wringing, we need to take action.
Stop the hand wringing and focus on solutions.
His hand wringing showed how anxious he was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or unease
  • Antonym: composure - the state of being calm and in control
