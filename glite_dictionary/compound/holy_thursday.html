Holy Thursday

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈθɜrzdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈθɜːzdeɪ / (BrE)
The Thursday before Easter, observed by Christians to commemorate the Last Supper of Jesus with his disciples.
Thursday before Easter; commemorates the Last Supper

Examples

Many churches hold evening services on Holy Thursday to remember the Last Supper.
On Holy Thursday, the priest washed the feet of twelve parishioners as part of the ceremony.
Schools in some countries close for Holy Thursday to allow participation in religious observances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maundy Thursday - the Thursday before Easter, commemorating the Last Supper

Etymology

From 'holy' (sacred) and 'Thursday'; in Christian tradition, also called Maundy Thursday.
