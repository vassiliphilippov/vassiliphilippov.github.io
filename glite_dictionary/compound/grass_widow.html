grass widow

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs ˈwɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs ˈwɪdəʊ / (BrE)
a woman who is divorced or separated from her husband, especially one whose husband is away for extended periods
woman divorced or separated from her husband
archaic

Examples

The villagers gossiped endlessly about the new grass widow who had moved into the old cottage.
During the war, many officers' wives became temporary grass widows while their husbands served overseas.
She disliked being labeled a grass widow after her separation.

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from the idea of a woman left to 'lie on the grass,' i.e., not in the marital home. Used since the 16th century.
