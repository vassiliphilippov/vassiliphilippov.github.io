Dachau concentration camp

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɑːxaʊ ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˈdaːkaʊ ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (BrE)
A Nazi concentration camp near Munich in southern Germany, established in 1933, used primarily for imprisoning Jews and other persecuted groups during the Holocaust.
Nazi concentration camp near Munich

Examples

The liberation of Dachau concentration camp by Allied forces was a turning point in World War II.
Many survivors of Dachau concentration camp have shared their harrowing stories.
A memorial now stands on the site of the former Dachau concentration camp to honor the victims.

Etymology

From Dachau, the name of the nearby German town, from Old High German 'daha' (clay) + 'ouwa' (island, water meadow).
