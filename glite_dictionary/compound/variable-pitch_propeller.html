variable-pitch propeller

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriəbl ˈpɪtʃ prəˈpɛlər / (AmE), / ˌveəriəbl ˈpɪtʃ prəˈpɛlə / (BrE)
A propeller whose blade angle can be adjusted to optimize performance under different flight conditions.
adjustable-angle aircraft propeller

Examples

The new aircraft uses a variable-pitch propeller for improved efficiency at different speeds.
Pilots adjust the variable-pitch propeller during flight to optimize engine performance.
Older planes often used fixed-pitch propellers, but most modern ones have a variable-pitch propeller.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixed-pitch propeller - an aircraft propeller with non-adjustable blades
