smoking gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkɪŋ ˈɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkɪŋ ˈɡʌn / (BrE)
Indisputable or conclusive evidence, especially of a crime or wrongdoing.
conclusive evidence of wrongdoing

Examples

The leaked emails became the smoking gun that implicated the officials in the scandal.
Investigators searched for a smoking gun to tie the suspect directly to the crime.
Her confession was the smoking gun the jury needed to reach a verdict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - evidence that establishes a fact or the truth of a statement
  • Degree Lower: circumstantial evidence - evidence that relies on inference rather than direct proof

Etymology

Originally from the image of a recently fired gun as evidence of guilt.
