present perfect progressive

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt ˈpɜrfɪkt prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɛznt ˈpɜːfɪkt prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
a verb tense formed with 'has been' or 'have been' plus the present participle, expressing an ongoing action that began in the past and continues to the present
English verb tense: ongoing action from past to present

Examples

She has been working here for five years, using the present perfect progressive tense to show ongoing action.
Teachers often ask students to practice forming the present perfect progressive in English class.
The present perfect progressive is used to describe actions that started in the past and are still happening now.

Synset Examples

The present perfect progressive tense usually conveys the meaning of recently or lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present perfect continuous - alternate name for the same tense
