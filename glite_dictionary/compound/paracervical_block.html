paracervical block

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.əˈsɜr.vɪ.kəl blɑk / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈsɜː.vɪ.kəl blɒk / (BrE)
a type of regional anesthesia produced by injecting a local anesthetic on each side of the cervix, commonly used during labor and childbirth
regional anesthesia for childbirth
medical

Examples

The doctor performed a paracervical block to ease the patient's labor pain.
A paracervical block can effectively provide regional anesthesia during certain gynecological procedures.
Some patients may experience temporary side effects after a paracervical block.

Etymology

From 'para-' (beside) and 'cervical' (relating to the cervix).
