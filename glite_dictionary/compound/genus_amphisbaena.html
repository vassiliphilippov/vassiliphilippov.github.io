genus Amphisbaena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæmfɪzˈbiːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌamfɪzˈbiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of legless, burrowing reptiles in the family Amphisbaenidae, commonly known as worm lizards.
genus of worm lizards

Examples

The genus Amphisbaena is known for its elongated, limbless body and burrowing habits.
Several unique species belong to the genus Amphisbaena, each adapted to a subterranean lifestyle.
Scientists continue to discover new lizard species within the genus Amphisbaena in South America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Amphisbaenidae - family of worm lizards including the genus Amphisbaena

Etymology

From Latin 'amphisbaena', originally from Greek 'amphisbaí̄na', a mythological serpent; scientific taxonomic usage.
