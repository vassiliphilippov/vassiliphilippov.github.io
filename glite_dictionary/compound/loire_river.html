Loire River

1. PROPN

/ lwɑːr ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlwaː ˌrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in France, rising in the Massif Central and flowing north and west to the Atlantic Ocean
longest river in France

Examples

The Loire River valley is famous for its beautiful castles and vineyards.
Many tourists enjoy cycling along the banks of the Loire River during the summer.
The Loire River often floods in the spring due to melting snow from the Massif Central.

Etymology

From French 'Loire', from Latin 'Liger', the ancient name of the river.
