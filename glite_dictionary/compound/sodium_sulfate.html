sodium sulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a white, bitter-tasting crystalline salt used in the manufacture of glass, paper, dyes, and pharmaceuticals
a salt used in industry and manufacturing

Examples

Sodium sulfate is commonly used in the production of detergents and paper.
Large quantities of sodium sulfate are required for the glass-making industry.
You should handle sodium sulfate with care, as it can be irritating to the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium carbonate - a white crystalline salt used in making glass and paper
  • Synonym: sodium chloride - common salt, used in food and industry

Etymology

From sodium, a metallic element, and sulfate, from French 'sulfate', from Latin 'sulfur'.
