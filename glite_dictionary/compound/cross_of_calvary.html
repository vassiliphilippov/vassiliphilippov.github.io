cross of Calvary

1. NOUN

/ krɔs əv ˈkælvəri / (AmE), / krɒs əv ˈkælvəri / (BrE)
A representation of a Latin cross standing on three steps, symbolizing the hill of Calvary where Jesus was crucified.
a Latin cross on three steps (Christian symbol)

Examples

The altar was adorned with a golden cross of Calvary at its center.
In Christian art, the cross of Calvary is depicted as standing on three steps symbolizing faith, hope, and charity.
The church features a stained glass window illustrating the cross of Calvary above the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin cross - a cross with a longer descending arm; principal symbol of Christianity

Etymology

From the name Calvary, the site outside Jerusalem where Jesus was crucified, from Latin 'Calvaria' (skull).
