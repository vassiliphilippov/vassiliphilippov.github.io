reflecting telescope

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛktɪŋ ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛktɪŋ ˈtɛlɪskəʊp / (BrE)
an optical telescope that uses a large concave mirror to collect and focus light, producing an image that can be magnified by an eyepiece
telescope using a mirror to focus light

Examples

Isaac Newton invented the reflecting telescope in 1668, changing the course of astronomy.
The observatory houses a large reflecting telescope used for deep-sky observations.
Unlike a refractor, a reflecting telescope uses mirrors rather than lenses to gather light.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refracting telescope - optical telescope using lenses to focus light
