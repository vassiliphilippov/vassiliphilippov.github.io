wet lung

1. NOUN

/ wɛt lʌŋ / (AmE), / wɛt lʌŋ / (BrE)
A serious form of acute lung injury marked by severe inflammation and fluid accumulation in the lungs, leading to coughing, difficulty in breathing, and poor oxygen exchange, often caused by exposure to irritants such as chemical vapors.
acute lung injury with fluid and inflammation
medical

Examples

Exposure to chemical fumes in the factory caused him to develop wet lung within a few hours.
Doctors rushed to treat the patient, fearing that her severe coughing was a symptom of wet lung.
Inhalation of corrosive vapors can lead to wet lung, which often requires intensive medical care.
