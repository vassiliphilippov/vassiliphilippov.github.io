Doctor of Philosophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktər əv fəˈlɑsəfi / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
an advanced academic degree awarded for original research, typically after several years of postgraduate study
advanced research degree (PhD)

Examples

After years of research and a successful thesis defense, she received her Doctor of Philosophy.
Earning a Doctor of Philosophy requires making an original contribution to knowledge in your field.
Many university professors hold a Doctor of Philosophy in their area of expertise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PhD - abbreviation for Doctor of Philosophy

Etymology

From Latin 'doctor philosophiae', meaning 'teacher of philosophy'.
