mother-in-law plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ ɪn lɔː ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə ɪn lɔː ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
An evergreen ornamental plant with large, dark green leaves, known for containing a toxin that can cause swelling of the tongue and throat if ingested.
an ornamental evergreen plant with toxic leaves

Examples

She placed a mother-in-law plant in the corner to liven up the living room.
The leaves of the mother-in-law plant are beautiful but can be harmful if eaten.
Gardeners often choose the mother-in-law plant for its resilience and striking foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumb cane - another name for the same plant; Dieffenbachia
