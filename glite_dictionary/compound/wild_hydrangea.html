wild hydrangea

1. NOUN

/ waɪld haɪˈdreɪndʒə / (AmE), / waɪld hɪˈdrændʒɪə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, known for its clusters of creamy white flowers.
North American shrub with white flowers

Examples

The wild hydrangea thrives in the shaded woodlands of the Appalachian region.
Gardeners often plant wild hydrangea to attract pollinators with its clusters of white blooms.
In late spring, the wild hydrangea displays its beautiful creamy flowers along forest edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrangea - A genus of flowering plants with showy flower heads, often grown as ornamentals
