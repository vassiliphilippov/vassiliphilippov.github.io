mere pabulum

1. NOUN

/ mɪr ˈpæbjələm / (AmE), / mɪə ˈpæbjʊləm / (BrE)
unsubstantial, bland, or trivial material, especially in writing or speech; content lacking in intellectual or emotional substance
bland or trivial material

Examples

The speech was dismissed as mere pabulum by most critics.
She found the movie to be mere pabulum, with nothing original or stimulating.
Many talk shows today offer mere pabulum rather than real discussion.

Synset Examples

He criticized the article for being mere pabulum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pabulum - bland or insipid intellectual material

Etymology

Pabulum: from Latin 'pabulum' (food, fodder); 'mere': from Latin 'merus' (pure, unmixed).
