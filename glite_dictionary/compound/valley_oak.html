valley oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæli oʊk / (AmE), / ˈvæli əʊk / (BrE)
a tall, graceful, deciduous oak tree native to California, with leathery leaves and slender pointed acorns
a type of oak tree native to California

Examples

The valley oak can be seen across the Californian countryside, providing important habitat for wildlife.
In autumn, the leaves of the valley oak turn golden and fall to the ground.
Botanists studied a centuries-old valley oak that towers above the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree or shrub of the genus Quercus
