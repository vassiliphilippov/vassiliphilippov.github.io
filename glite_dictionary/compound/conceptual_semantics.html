conceptual semantics

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛptʃuəl səˈmæntɪks / (AmE), / kənˈsɛptjʊəl sɪˈmæntɪks / (BrE)
the branch of semantics that examines the mental and cognitive structures underlying meaning in language
cognitive branch of semantics

Examples

The theory of conceptual semantics seeks to explain how we mentally organize word meaning.
Many linguists use conceptual semantics to analyze the relationship between language and thought.
Her research in conceptual semantics explores how people structure and process meaning cognitively.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: semantics - the study of meaning in language

Etymology

From 'conceptual' (relating to concepts) and 'semantics' (from Greek sēmantikos, meaning 'significant')
