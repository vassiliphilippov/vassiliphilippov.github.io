pesticide poisoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛstɪˌsaɪd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪsʌɪd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
a harmful or toxic condition caused by ingesting, inhaling, or coming into contact with a pesticide
harm caused by pesticide exposure
medical

Examples

Farm workers are at higher risk of pesticide poisoning due to frequent exposure to chemicals.
Symptoms of pesticide poisoning can include headache, nausea, and difficulty breathing.
The hospital treated several cases of pesticide poisoning after the incident at the factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisoning - the condition of being harmed by poison
