chestnut-bark disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt bɑrk dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.nʌt bɑːk dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A disease affecting American chestnut trees, caused by a fungus, resulting in cankers on the bark and often leading to the tree's death.
disease of American chestnut trees

Examples

The spread of chestnut-bark disease devastated forests in the eastern United States in the early 20th century.
Scientists are working on ways to breed resistance to chestnut-bark disease in American chestnuts.
Many old trees succumbed to chestnut-bark disease before preventive treatments were developed.
