cosmic microwave background radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑzmɪk ˈmaɪkroʊˌweɪv ˈbækˌɡraʊnd reɪdiˌeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɪk ˈmaɪkrəʊˌweɪv ˈbækˌɡraʊnd reɪdiˌeɪʃən / (BrE)
the faint microwave radiation left over from the early stages of the universe (the Big Bang), now filling the universe with an almost uniform temperature of about 2.7 kelvin
remnant microwave radiation from the early universe

Examples

Astronomers study the cosmic microwave background radiation to learn about the origins of the universe.
The cosmic microwave background radiation provides strong evidence for the Big Bang theory.
Tiny fluctuations in the cosmic microwave background radiation have helped scientists map the universe's early structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: background radiation - general term for detectable radiation left from early space events
