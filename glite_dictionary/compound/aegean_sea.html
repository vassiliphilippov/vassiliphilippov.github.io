Aegean Sea

1. PROPN

/ əˈdʒiən si / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒiːən siː / (BrE)
the part of the Mediterranean Sea located between Greece and Turkey, historically significant as a major trade route for ancient civilizations
sea between Greece and Turkey

Examples

The Aegean Sea is famous for its clear blue waters and many beautiful islands.
Ancient Greek ships once sailed the Aegean Sea, connecting Athens and Crete.
Many tourists visit the Aegean Sea each summer for sailing, swimming, and exploring historic sites.

Etymology

From Latin 'Aegaeus' (from Greek 'Aigaíos'), relating to Aegae, an ancient city; possibly from the myth of King Aegeus.
