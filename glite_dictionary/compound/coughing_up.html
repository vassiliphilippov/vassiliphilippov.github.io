coughing up

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑfɪŋ ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkɒfɪŋ ˌʌp / (BrE)
the act of expelling food, phlegm, or other material from the throat or lungs by coughing
expelling material by coughing

Examples

Heavy smokers may experience frequent coughing up of phlegm in the morning.
After accidentally swallowing a crumb, he began coughing up small pieces at the dinner table.
Doctors are concerned when patients report coughing up blood, as it can indicate serious conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectoration - the act of ejecting phlegm or mucus from the throat or lungs
