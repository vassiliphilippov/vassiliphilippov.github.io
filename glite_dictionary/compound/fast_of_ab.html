Fast of Ab

1. NOUN

/ fæst ʌv ɑːv / (AmE), / fɑːst əv ɑːv / (BrE)
a major Jewish fast day commemorating the destruction of the temples in Jerusalem
Jewish fast day for temple destruction

Examples

Many people observe the Fast of Ab by refraining from eating and drinking for 25 hours.
The Fast of Ab commemorates both the first and second destructions of the Jewish Temple.
During the Fast of Ab, synagogues hold special mourning services and prayers.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Tisha B'Av' (the ninth of Av), Av being a month in the Hebrew calendar.
