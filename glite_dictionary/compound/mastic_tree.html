mastic tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɪk tri / (AmE), / ˈmastɪk triː / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region, cultivated for its aromatic resin used in food, medicine, and industry
Mediterranean evergreen shrub with aromatic resin

Examples

The mastic tree thrives in dry, rocky soils of southern Europe and North Africa.
Chewing gum was once made from resin harvested from the mastic tree.
The aromatic sap from the mastic tree is prized for its unique flavor in Mediterranean cuisine.

Etymology

From Latin mastiche, from Greek mastikhē (resin of the mastic tree).
