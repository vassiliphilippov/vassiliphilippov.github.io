Bosworth Field

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑzwərθ ˈfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbɒzwəθ ˈfiːld / (BrE)
the site of the final battle of the Wars of the Roses in 1485, where Richard III was defeated and Henry Tudor became Henry VII.
site of the decisive 1485 battle ending the Wars of the Roses

Examples

The Battle of Bosworth Field marked the end of the Plantagenet dynasty.
Many historians visit Bosworth Field to study where Richard III met his fate.
The outcome of Bosworth Field changed the course of English history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Hastings - decisive 1066 battle that changed English history

Etymology

Named after the nearby village of Market Bosworth in Leicestershire, England.
