jumping gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ dʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ dʒiːn / (BrE)
a segment of DNA that can move to different positions within the genome of a cell, especially found in bacteria; a transposable genetic element
DNA segment able to move within a genome

Examples

The discovery of the jumping gene revolutionized our understanding of genetic mutation.
Some scientists study how a jumping gene can disrupt normal cell function and lead to disease.
Barbara McClintock won a Nobel Prize for her research on the jumping gene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transposon - a DNA sequence that can change its position within a genome

Etymology

Named for its ability to 'jump' to different genome locations; coined in 1950 by geneticist Barbara McClintock.
