gas embolism

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈɛm.boʊˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈɛm.bəʊˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which gas bubbles enter the bloodstream, often causing pain and potentially serious complications due to rapid changes in pressure.
painful condition from gas bubbles in the blood
medical

Examples

Divers are at risk of a gas embolism if they ascend too quickly from deep water.
The patient suffered a gas embolism following an accident involving compressed air.
Symptoms of a gas embolism can include severe joint pain and difficulty breathing.

Etymology

From 'gas' (from Greek 'khaos', chaos) and 'embolism' from Greek 'embolos', meaning 'plug' or 'stopper'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈɛm.bə.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈɛm.bə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
a blockage of a blood vessel by an air or gas bubble, which can occur accidentally during medical procedures or as a complication from scuba diving
blockage of a vessel by gas bubble
medical

Examples

A gas embolism can be life-threatening if not treated promptly by medical professionals.
Divers are trained to surface slowly to reduce the risk of developing a gas embolism.
The patient suffered a gas embolism during a routine surgical procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air embolism - blockage of a blood vessel by an air bubble

Etymology

From 'gas' (from Greek 'khaos', empty space/air) and medical term 'embolism' (from Greek 'embolos', plug).
