green snake

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin sneɪk / (AmE), / ɡriːn sneɪk / (BrE)
either of two North American, mainly insect-eating snakes that are green in color
a North American green-colored insectivorous snake

Examples

We spotted a green snake slithering through the tall grass near the riverbank.
The green snake feeds mainly on insects and spiders found in its habitat.
Children were fascinated by the bright color of the green snake at the nature center.

2. NOUN

/ ɡrin sneɪk / (AmE), / ɡriːn sneɪk / (BrE)
Any of various nonvenomous colubrid snakes of Africa that are typically green in color.
nonvenomous African green colubrid snake

Examples

The green snake is often found in the grasslands of sub-Saharan Africa.
Researchers observed a green snake basking on a tree branch near the river.
Many bird species prey on the eggs of the green snake in the wild.
