creeping thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A European thistle (Cirsium arvense) with creeping roots, naturalized in North America and considered a troublesome weed.
a European weed with creeping roots

Examples

Creeping thistle is an invasive species that can quickly take over gardens and fields.
Farmers dread the rapid spread of creeping thistle due to its persistent roots.
Efforts to control creeping thistle require regular monitoring and removal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada thistle - Another common name for Cirsium arvense
