string quartette

1. NOUN

/ strɪŋ kwɔrˈtɛt / (AmE), / strɪŋ kwɔːˈtɛt / (BrE)
an instrumental group consisting of two violins, a viola, and a cello, or a musical composition written for such a group
group of two violins, a viola, and a cello

Examples

The string quartette played Mozart's music beautifully at the recital.
He composed a new string quartette for the orchestra's upcoming concert.
A typical string quartette includes two violinists, a violist, and a cellist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: string quartet - group or composition for two violins, viola, and cello

Etymology

From 'string' (referring to stringed instruments) + Italian 'quartetto' (group of four).
