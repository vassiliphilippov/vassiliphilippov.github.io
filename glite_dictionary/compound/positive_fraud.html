positive fraud

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzətɪv frɔd / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv frɔːd / (BrE)
Deliberate deceit involving concealment or false representation with the intention to injure another, recognized as an actionable offense in law.
deliberate legal deceit for harm
legal

Examples

The company was found liable for positive fraud after intentionally hiding crucial information from investors.
Under the law, positive fraud requires proof that the defendant acted with the intent to deceive.
Making a false statement with the purpose of misleading a buyer constitutes positive fraud.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
