medial geniculate body

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiəl dʒəˌnɪkjʊlət ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəl dʒɪˌnɪkjʊlət ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a part of the thalamus that acts as the final processing center in the auditory pathway before information reaches the auditory cortex
part of the thalamus for auditory processing
medical

Examples

The medial geniculate body is essential for relaying auditory signals to the brain's cortex.
Damage to the medial geniculate body can result in impaired auditory perception.
Researchers studied activity changes in the medial geniculate body after exposure to loud noise.

Etymology

From Latin 'medialis' (middle), 'geniculum' (little knee, describing the shape); anatomical term.
