spectacled caiman

1. NOUN

/ spɛk.tə.kəld ˈkeɪ.mən / (AmE), / spɛk.tə.kl̩d ˈkeɪ.mən / (BrE)
a species of caiman with bony ridges above the eyes, native to regions from southern Mexico to Argentina
a caiman species with bony eye ridges

Examples

The spectacled caiman is known for the distinct bony ridge between its eyes.
We saw a spectacled caiman basking on the riverbank during our trip to Costa Rica.
Because of its adaptability, the spectacled caiman thrives in a wide variety of freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caiman - a semiaquatic reptile similar to an alligator, found in Central and South America

Etymology

From 'spectacled' (resembling eyeglasses) + 'caiman', from Spanish 'caimán'.
