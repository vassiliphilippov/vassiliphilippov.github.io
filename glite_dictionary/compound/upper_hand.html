upper hand

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpər hænd / (AmE), / ˈʌpə hænd / (BrE)
a position of advantage, power, or control over someone or something
position of advantage; control

Examples

After months of negotiations, the company finally gained the upper hand in the deal.
She was determined not to let her opponent take the upper hand in the argument.
The rebels held the upper hand until reinforcements arrived for the government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position
  • Synonym: edge - an advantageous margin or position in a competition
  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable circumstance or condition that reduces the chances of success
