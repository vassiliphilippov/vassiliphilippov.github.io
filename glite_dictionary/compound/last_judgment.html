Last Judgment

1. NOUN

/ læst ˈʤʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / lɑːst ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the final day at the end of the world when God judges all humans, rewarding the good and punishing the wicked.
the final judgment of all souls (Christianity)

Examples

The painting depicts the moment of the Last Judgment with angels and souls awaiting their fate.
Many believers reflect on the Last Judgment as a time of ultimate accountability.
The sermon focused on preparing one's soul for the coming of the Last Judgment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment day - the day of God's final judgment of humanity
  • Degree Lower: apocalypse - the complete final destruction of the world, as described in the Book of Revelation

Etymology

From Middle English, translating Latin 'judicium ultimum', found in Christian theological texts.
