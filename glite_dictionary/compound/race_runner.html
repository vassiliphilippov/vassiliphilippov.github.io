race runner

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪs ˌrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈreɪs ˌrʌnə / (BrE)
a very swift lizard found in eastern and central parts of the United States, known for its speed
a fast lizard of the US

Examples

The race runner darted across the dusty trail before I could snap a photo.
In the wild, a race runner relies on its speed to evade predators.
Many herpetologists study the behavior of the race runner in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From 'race' (to move at speed) + 'runner' (one who runs), referring to its rapid movement.
