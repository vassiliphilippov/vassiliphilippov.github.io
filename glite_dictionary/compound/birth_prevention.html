birth prevention

1. NOUN

/ bɝːθ prɪˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / bɜːθ prɪˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
the act or practice of limiting the number of children born, especially by means of contraception or family planning
practice of limiting the number of children born

Examples

Many public health campaigns promote birth prevention to reduce unintended pregnancies.
Access to modern methods of birth prevention can empower individuals and families.
Debates over birth prevention methods are common in discussions of reproductive rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraception - the use of methods or devices to prevent pregnancy
  • Synonym: family planning - the practice of controlling the number and timing of children in a family
