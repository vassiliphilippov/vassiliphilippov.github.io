don't-know

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊntˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊntˌnəʊ / (BrE)
a person who responds 'I don't know' to a question, especially in a public opinion poll or survey
person who answers 'I don't know' in a poll

Examples

The survey results included 20% don't-knows, which made interpretation difficult.
Pollsters often analyze how many don't-knows there are among undecided voters.
In the latest poll, don't-know responses increased compared to last month.

Synset Examples

70% in favor, 13% opposed and 17% don't-knows
