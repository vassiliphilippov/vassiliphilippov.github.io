order Coleoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrdər koʊliˈɒptərə / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə kəʊliˈɒptərə / (BrE)
A large order of insects comprising the beetles, characterized by hard forewings that cover the flying wings.
the insect order of beetles

Examples

Scientists estimate that the order Coleoptera contains more species than any other order of animals.
The order Coleoptera includes both ladybugs and scarab beetles.
Entomologists study the order Coleoptera to better understand insect diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetle - an insect of the order Coleoptera

Etymology

From Greek 'koleos' (sheath) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to beetles' hardened forewings.
