carnal knowledge

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.nəl ˈnɑː.lɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.nəl ˈnɒ.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
sexual intercourse, especially as a legal or formal term; typically refers to penetration as an element in certain crimes or contracts
sexual intercourse (legal/formal term)
legal archaic

Examples

The court found that the accused had engaged in carnal knowledge with the complainant.
In many older legal texts, the term carnal knowledge is used to refer to sexual relations.
The statute defined the crime as the unlawful carnal knowledge of a minor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexual intercourse - physical union of sexual organs, especially involving penetration

Etymology

From Latin carnalis (of the flesh) + knowledge, used in legal and biblical contexts to denote sexual relations.
