suborder Sciuromorpha

1. NOUN

/ sjuːˌɔːrəˈmɔːfə / (AmE), / sjuːˌɔːrəˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of rodents including squirrels, marmots, gophers, beavers, and related species.
rodent suborder: squirrels, marmots, etc.

Examples

The suborder Sciuromorpha encompasses a wide variety of rodents, including squirrels and their relatives.
Scientists debate the exact classification within the suborder Sciuromorpha based on recent genetic findings.
Members of the suborder Sciuromorpha are found on every continent except Australia and Antarctica.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Sciuromys' (squirrel genus) and Greek 'morphē' (form, shape).
