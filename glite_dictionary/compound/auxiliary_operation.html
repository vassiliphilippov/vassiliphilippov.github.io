auxiliary operation

1. NOUN

/ ɔɡˈzɪljəri ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈzɪljəri ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an operation carried out by external or off-line equipment not managed by the central processing unit
operation by off-line equipment in computing

Examples

The backup process is considered an auxiliary operation because it is performed by external storage devices.
Many auxiliary operations are automated to prevent interruption to the main system.
Off-line printing is a typical example of an auxiliary operation in older computer setups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main operation - the primary process performed by the central processing unit
