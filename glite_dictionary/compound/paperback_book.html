paperback book

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.pɚˌbæk bʊk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.pəˌbæk bʊk / (BrE)
a book bound in a flexible paper cover rather than a rigid one
book with a paper cover

Examples

I bought a paperback book to read on the plane because it was lighter than a hardcover.
She prefers collecting paperback books as they are generally more affordable and easier to carry.
The novel is available both as a paperback book and as an e-book.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardcover book - a book with a rigid protective cover
  • Synonym: paperback - a book with flexible paper covers (sometimes used as a noun)

Etymology

From 'paperback', itself from 'paper' and 'back', originally denoting the flexible cover, and 'book'.
