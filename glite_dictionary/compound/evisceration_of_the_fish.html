evisceration of the fish

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌvɪs.əˈreɪ.ʃən əv ðə fɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪˌvɪs.əˈreɪ.ʃən əv ðə fɪʃ / (BrE)
the removal of the internal organs from a fish, typically as part of cleaning or preparing it for cooking or study
removal of internal organs from a fish

Examples

The evisceration of the fish is a necessary step before cooking it.
After catching the trout, he performed a quick evisceration of the fish at the riverbank.
Proper evisceration of the fish ensures better flavor and texture when preparing seafood dishes.

Synset Examples

The evisceration of the fish was done expertly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gutting - removing the internal organs of a fish or animal

Etymology

From Latin 'eviscerare' (to disembowel), from 'e-' (out) + 'viscera' (internal organs).
