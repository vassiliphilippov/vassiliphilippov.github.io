fair trade

1. NOUN

/ fɛr treɪd / (AmE), / feə treɪd / (BrE)
a trading partnership based on dialogue, transparency, and respect, that seeks greater equity in international trade by offering better trading conditions to, and securing the rights of, marginalized producers and workers
ethical system of commerce ensuring fair payment and conditions

Examples

Many coffee brands now carry the fair trade label to support farmers in developing countries.
She chooses to buy fair trade chocolate because it ensures ethical labor practices.
The company is committed to sourcing only fair trade products for its supply chain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethical trade - trade that emphasizes ethical and socially responsible practices
