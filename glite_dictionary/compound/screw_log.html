screw log

1. NOUN

/ skru lɑg / (AmE), / skruː lɒg / (BrE)
a cigar-shaped device fitted with rotary fins, formerly used to measure the speed of a ship in water
nautical instrument for measuring ship speed

Examples

The crew lowered the screw log into the water to check their speed.
Before GPS, ships relied on instruments like the screw log for navigation.
The old sailing vessel still had a screw log stowed among its navigation tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patent log - a type of mechanical ship log, often using a rotating element
