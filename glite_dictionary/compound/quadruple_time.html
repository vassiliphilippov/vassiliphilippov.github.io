quadruple time

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdrupəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˈkwɒdrʊpl taɪm / (BrE)
a musical time signature or rhythm in which each measure contains four beats.
musical meter with four beats per bar

Examples

The waltz is typically in triple time, while the march is in quadruple time.
Many modern pop songs are written in quadruple time for easy dancing.
The conductor emphasized the strong first beat of each bar in quadruple time.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duple time - musical meter with two beats per bar
  • Degree Lower: triple time - musical meter with three beats per bar
  • Synonym: common time - a type of quadruple time, usually notated as 4/4

Etymology

From Latin 'quadruplus' (fourfold) and 'time' as in musical meter.
