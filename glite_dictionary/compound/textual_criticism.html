textual criticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkstʃuəl krɪˈtɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstjʊəl krɪˈtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the scholarly study of comparing texts and related materials to determine their authenticity or original form
study of verifying texts' authenticity

Examples

The historian devoted years to textual criticism to authenticate the ancient manuscripts.
Students of biblical studies often practice textual criticism to analyze scriptural variations.
Advances in technology have changed the methods of textual criticism in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the analysis and judgment of the merits and faults of a literary or artistic work
  • Synonym: philology - the study of language in written historical sources

Etymology

From Latin 'textus' (woven, text) and Greek 'kritikē' (critique).
