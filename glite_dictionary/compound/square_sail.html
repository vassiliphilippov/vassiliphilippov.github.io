square sail

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr seɪl / (AmE), / skwɛː seɪl / (BrE)
a four-sided sail set on a horizontal yard suspended from the middle of a mast, used mainly on old sailing ships
four-sided sail on a horizontal yard

Examples

The ship was equipped with a large square sail to take advantage of the prevailing winds.
During the Age of Sail, vessels often relied on the efficiency of the square sail for ocean crossings.
As we approached the harbor, the crew lowered the square sail to prepare for docking.
