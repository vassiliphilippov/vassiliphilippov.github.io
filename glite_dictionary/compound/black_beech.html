black beech

1. NOUN

/ blæk bitʃ / (AmE), / blæk biːtʃ / (BrE)
A large forest tree native to New Zealand, known for its dark bark and dense wood; Nothofagus solandri.
New Zealand forest tree (Nothofagus solandri)

Examples

The black beech is commonly found in the mountain forests of New Zealand's South Island.
Carpenters value black beech wood for its strength and durability.
Several birds nest in the branches of the black beech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - a type of deciduous tree with smooth gray bark and oval leaves
