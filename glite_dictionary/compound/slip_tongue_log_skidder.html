slip tongue log skidder

1. NOUN

/ slɪp tʌŋ loɡ ˈskɪdər / (AmE), / slɪp tʌŋ lɒɡ ˈskɪdə / (BrE)
A type of skidder used in logging, with dangling tongs between the wheels to lift one end of a log off the ground, especially in the 19th and early 20th centuries.
logging device with tongs to lift logs

Examples

The old lumber camp displayed a slip tongue log skidder used for moving massive logs through the forest.
During the tour, the guide explained how the slip tongue log skidder helped reduce the strain on horses hauling timber.
Historians restored a slip tongue log skidder to demonstrate 19th-century logging techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skidder - a vehicle used for pulling logs from a forest
