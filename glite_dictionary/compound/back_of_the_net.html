back of the net

1. NOUN

/ bæk əv ðə nɛt / (AmE), / bæk ɒv ðə nɛt / (BrE)
the part of a goal into which the ball enters, especially when a goal is scored in sports such as football or soccer
part of a goal where ball enters (sports)

Examples

The crowd erupted when the ball finally found the back of the net.
His powerful strike went straight into the back of the net.
After several missed chances, she was thrilled to see her shot hit the back of the net.

Synset Examples

She scored, and the crowd cheered as it went in the back of the net.
That shot hit the back of the net perfectly.
The striker celebrated as the ball hit the back of the net.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - the area or structure players try to score in, especially in soccer or hockey
