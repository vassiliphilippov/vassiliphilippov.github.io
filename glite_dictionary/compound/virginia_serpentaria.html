Virginia serpentaria

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪn.jə ˌsɜr.pənˈtɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i.ə ˌsɜː.pənˈteə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A species of birthwort (Aristolochia serpentaria) native to the woodlands of the eastern United States, known for its slender, curved roots.
a North American woodland birthwort

Examples

The Virginia serpentaria thrives in shady woodlands across the eastern United States.
Herbalists once used Virginia serpentaria root in traditional remedies, though it is rarely used today.
Botanists led a field trip to study the Virginia serpentaria and its unique flowering structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birthwort - a group of plants in the genus Aristolochia

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Virginia and from Latin 'serpentaria', meaning 'of a serpent', from 'serpens' (serpent), referring to the root's resemblance to a snake.
