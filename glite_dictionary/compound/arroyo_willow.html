arroyo willow

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɔɪ.oʊ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / əˈrɔɪ.əʊ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a shrubby willow native to the western United States, commonly found near streams and in moist areas
shrubby willow of western US

Examples

The arroyo willow thrives along riverbanks and provides shelter for local wildlife.
In California, restoration projects often include planting arroyo willow to prevent soil erosion.
The narrow leaves of the arroyo willow distinguish it from other willow species.

Etymology

From Spanish 'arroyo' (stream) + English 'willow' (the tree).
