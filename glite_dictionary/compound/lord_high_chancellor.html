Lord High Chancellor

1. NOUN

/ lɔrd haɪ ˈtʃænsələr / (AmE), / lɔːd haɪ ˈtʃɑːnsələ / (BrE)
the highest-ranking officer of the Crown in the United Kingdom, acting as the head of the judiciary and presiding over the House of Lords
highest judicial officer in the UK
british

Examples

The Lord High Chancellor played a crucial role in the country's judicial reforms during the nineteenth century.
As Lord High Chancellor, he was responsible for overseeing the legal system of England and Wales.
Queen Victoria appointed a new Lord High Chancellor following the sudden resignation of his predecessor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lord Chancellor - chief judicial officer and presiding officer of the House of Lords

Etymology

Title derives from Old French 'chancelier' and 'lord' meaning a nobleman, with 'high' denoting supreme rank.
