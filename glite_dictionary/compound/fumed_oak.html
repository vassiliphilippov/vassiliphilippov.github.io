fumed oak

1. NOUN

/ fjuːmd oʊk / (AmE), / fjuːmd əʊk / (BrE)
Oak wood treated with ammonia fumes to give it a weathered or darkened appearance, often used in furniture and cabinetry.
oak wood darkened by ammonia fuming

Examples

The craftsman selected fumed oak for the new dining table to provide a rich, vintage look.
This antique cabinet is made from fumed oak, giving it a unique character and patina.
Many interior designers choose fumed oak for luxury kitchens and offices.
