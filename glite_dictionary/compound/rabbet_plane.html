rabbet plane

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt pleɪn / (BrE)
a woodworking hand tool used for cutting rabbets (grooves or recesses) along the edge of a piece of wood
hand tool for cutting wood rabbets

Examples

The craftsman used a rabbet plane to cut a neat groove along the cabinet door.
Make sure to adjust the blade on your rabbet plane before starting the cut.
Older woodworkers often prefer a traditional rabbet plane for joinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbet - a groove or recess cut in wood along the edge or surface

Etymology

From 'rabbet', meaning a groove in wood, and 'plane', a tool for shaping wood.
