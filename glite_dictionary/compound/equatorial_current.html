equatorial current

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkwætəriəl ˈkɝːrənt / (AmE), / ɪˌkwætəˈrɪəl ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
any of the ocean currents that flow westward along the equator, driven mainly by trade winds.
ocean current flowing west at the equator

Examples

The Atlantic equatorial current carries warm water westward across the ocean.
Researchers studied the effects of the equatorial current on regional weather patterns.
Ships traveling along the equatorial current can take advantage of its steady flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: current - a body of water or air moving in a definite direction
