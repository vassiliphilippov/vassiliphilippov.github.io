Superfund program

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌfʌnd ˈproʊ.ɡræm / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌfʌnd ˈprəʊ.ɡræm / (BrE)
A United States federal government program, administered by the Environmental Protection Agency, that locates, investigates, and cleans up the nation's worst sites contaminated with hazardous waste.
US program for hazardous waste site cleanup
american

Examples

The Superfund program was created to ensure that hazardous waste sites are cleaned up safely and efficiently.
Local communities have pushed for their polluted sites to be added to the Superfund program.
Through the Superfund program, the EPA holds polluters accountable for the cost of environmental restoration.

Synset Examples

some have intimated that the Superfund's money may have turned into a political slush fund

Etymology

Named after the trust fund established for cleanup, established by the Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act (CERCLA) of 1980.
