genus Squalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈskweɪləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈskweɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of small, spiny dogfish sharks found in oceans worldwide
genus of spiny dogfish sharks

Examples

The genus Squalus includes several species of dogfish sharks commonly found in temperate and tropical waters.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the genus Squalus to understand shark diversity.
Many fisheries around the world catch members of the genus Squalus for their meat and liver oil.

Etymology

From Latin squalus (a kind of sea fish or shark).
