personal identity

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɝːsənl aɪˈdɛntəti / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsənl aɪˈdɛntɪti / (BrE)
the characteristics and qualities that make a person distinct from others and are regarded as continuing over time
distinct and continuous individuality

Examples

The experience changed his personal identity and how he viewed himself.
Questions about personal identity are common in philosophy and psychology.
Joining the organization made her feel she was losing her personal identity.

Synset Examples

you can lose your identity when you join the army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identity - the fact of being who or what a person or thing is

Etymology

From Latin 'persona' (person) and 'identitas' (sameness, identity).
