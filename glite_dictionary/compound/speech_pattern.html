speech pattern

1. NOUN

/ spiːtʃ ˈpætɚn / (AmE), / spiːtʃ ˈpætən / (BrE)
a distinctive and characteristic manner of speaking, especially as determined by accent, rhythm, or intonation
distinctive manner of speaking

Examples

Her southern speech pattern made her stand out when she moved to New York.
The therapist analyzed the child's speech pattern to assess linguistic development.
His unique speech pattern was easily recognized by his students.

Synset Examples

he couldn't suppress his contemptuous accent
she had a very clear speech pattern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accent - a particular way of pronouncing words characteristic to a region or group
