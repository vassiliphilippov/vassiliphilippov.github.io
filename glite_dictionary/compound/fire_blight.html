fire blight

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ blaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə blaɪt / (BrE)
a serious bacterial disease that causes blackening and wilting of the leaves, branches, and blossoms of apple and pear trees
bacterial disease of apple and pear trees

Examples

The orchard lost several trees this year due to fire blight.
Experts recommend pruning infected branches to control the spread of fire blight.
Pear trees are especially vulnerable to fire blight during warm and wet spring weather.
