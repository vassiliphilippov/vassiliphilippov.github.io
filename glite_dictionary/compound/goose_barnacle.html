goose barnacle

1. NOUN

/ ɡuːs ˈbɑrnəkəl / (AmE), / ɡuːs ˈbɑːnəkəl / (BrE)
a stalked barnacle that attaches itself to ship hulls, driftwood, or floating objects in the sea
a stalked marine barnacle

Examples

The hull of the ship was covered in hundreds of goose barnacles after months at sea.
Divers inspected the driftwood, which was encrusted with goose barnacles thriving in the intertidal zone.
A cluster of goose barnacles dangled from the floating log washed up on the beach.

Etymology

So called because their long stalks were once fancifully believed to resemble the necks of geese.
