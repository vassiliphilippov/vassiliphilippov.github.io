too early

1. ADV

/ tu ˈɝːli / (AmE), / tuː ˈɜːli / (BrE)
at a time before the expected, appropriate, or desired time
before the expected or proper time

Examples

We arrived too early for the concert, so the doors were still closed.
It's too early to make a decision about his future.
She felt it was too early in the semester to start stressing about exams.

Synset Examples

The technology feels like it could be a hundred years too early.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: too late - after the expected, appropriate, or desired time
