the pox

1. NOUN

/ ðə pɑks / (AmE), / ðə pɒks / (BrE)
a contagious skin disease, especially smallpox or syphilis, used historically for such illnesses
contagious skin disease (historical term)
archaic

Examples

He feared that the pox would infect the whole village.
During the 18th century, outbreaks of the pox were a serious health threat.
Many historical documents mention the pox as a devastating disease.

Synset Examples

He was worried the pox would spread among the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - an acute, highly contagious viral disease characterized by fever and pustules
  • Synonym: syphilis - a chronic, contagious sexually transmitted infection historically called 'the pox'

Etymology

From English 'pox', plural of 'pock', meaning pustule or blister.
