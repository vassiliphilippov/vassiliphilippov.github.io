to get some coffee

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt sʌm ˈkɑfi / (AmE), / ɡɛt səm ˈkɒfi / (BrE)
to obtain or buy coffee, often implying drinking it, especially in a social or casual context
to buy or obtain coffee

Examples

Let’s get some coffee before the meeting starts.
She asked if I wanted to get some coffee after work.
We decided to get some coffee and catch up on old times.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a coffee - to drink a cup of coffee, especially socially
