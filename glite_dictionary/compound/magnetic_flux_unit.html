magnetic flux unit

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk flʌks ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / mæɡˈnetɪk flʌks ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the quantity of magnetic flux, representing the strength of a magnetic field over a given area
unit of magnetic flux measurement

Examples

The weber is the standard magnetic flux unit in the International System of Units.
To calculate the total magnetic flux unit, measurements of field strength and area are required.
Scientists compare different magnetic flux units when studying variations in electromagnetic fields.
