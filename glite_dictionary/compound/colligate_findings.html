to colligate findings

1. VERB

/ kəˈlɪɡeɪt ˈfaɪndɪŋz / (AmE), / kɒˈlɪɡeɪt ˈfaɪndɪŋz / (BrE)
to gather and organize research results into a coherent whole.
gather and organize research results

Examples

The research team aims to colligate findings from previous experiments for a comprehensive review.
It is essential to colligate findings before drawing any significant conclusions.
She spent several weeks trying to colligate findings from surveys and interviews.

Synset Examples

The scientist will colligate findings from multiple studies.
We need to colligate the data before analyzing the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collate - to collect and combine information
  • Degree Higher: analyze findings - to examine research results in detail

Etymology

From Latin 'colligatus', past participle of 'colligare' (to bind together), and 'findings' meaning research results.
