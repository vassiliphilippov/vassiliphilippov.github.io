power plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A building or group of buildings where electricity is generated for distribution to consumers.
an electricity-generating facility

Examples

The city relies on a coal-fired power plant for most of its electricity.
Engineers inspected the power plant after the thunderstorm caused a brief outage.
Plans to build a solar power plant were discussed at the town meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power station - a facility where electricity is generated
