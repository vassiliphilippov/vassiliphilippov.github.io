family Kasuwonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli kəˌsuːwoʊˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli kəˌsuːwəˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of oceanic bonitos, considered a separate taxonomic family in some classifications of fish.
a fish family of oceanic bonitos

Examples

The family Kasuwonidae is recognized by some ichthyologists as distinct from related families.
Members of the family Kasuwonidae are commonly called oceanic bonitos.
Recent studies have debated the classification of the family Kasuwonidae among scombrid fishes.

Etymology

Kasuwonidae is formed from the generic name 'Kasuwonus' and the taxonomic family suffix '-idae', following scientific naming conventions.
