epilepsia major

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsiə ˈmeɪdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsiə ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
epilepsy characterized by attacks involving loss of consciousness, tonic muscle spasms, and generalized jerking (formerly called 'grand mal')
form of epilepsy with loss of consciousness and spasms
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with epilepsia major after observing a series of severe seizures.
During the nineteenth century, epilepsia major was a common term for major convulsive episodes.
Modern neurology now refers to epilepsia major as 'grand mal' or tonic-clonic seizures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand mal - form of epilepsy with severe convulsions

Etymology

From Latin 'epilepsia' (epilepsy) and 'major' (greater).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsiə ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsiə ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
a type of epileptic seizure in which the patient loses consciousness and experiences convulsions affecting the whole body
type of epileptic seizure with convulsions
medical

Examples

The patient suffered an epilepsia major at work and was rushed to the hospital.
Doctors once used the term epilepsia major to describe severe generalized seizures.
Symptoms of epilepsia major include a sudden loss of consciousness and violent muscle contractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand mal - another term for an epileptic seizure involving loss of consciousness and convulsions
  • Synonym: tonic-clonic seizure - medical term for seizures with loss of consciousness and convulsions

Etymology

From Latin, with 'epilepsia' meaning epilepsy and 'major' meaning greater.
