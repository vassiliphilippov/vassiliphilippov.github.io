louis d'or

1. NOUN

/ luːˈiː dɔːr / (AmE), / luːˈiː dɔː / (BrE)
a former French gold coin, issued from the 17th to the 18th centuries, bearing the image of King Louis
former French gold coin

Examples

The museum displayed a rare louis d'or minted during the reign of Louis XIV.
He inherited a collection of louis d'ors from his grandfather.
The louis d'or was highly valued for its gold content in 18th-century France.

Etymology

French, literally 'Louis of gold', named after French kings called Louis.
