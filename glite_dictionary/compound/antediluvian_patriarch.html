antediluvian patriarch

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.t̬ɪ.dɪˈlu.vi.ən ˈpeɪ.tri.ɑrk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.dɪˈljuː.vi.ən ˈpeɪ.tri.ɑːk / (BrE)
any of the early biblical patriarchs who lived before the flood described in the story of Noah.
early biblical patriarch before the Flood

Examples

Methuselah is often considered one of the most prominent antediluvian patriarchs in biblical tradition.
Genealogies in Genesis list several antediluvian patriarchs who reportedly lived for centuries.
The story of the antediluvian patriarchs is central to early biblical history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriarch - a biblical ancestor of the human race or of the Hebrew people
  • Synonym: antediluvian - of or relating to the period before the biblical Flood
  • Degree Higher: Noah - the biblical patriarch who built the ark before the Flood

Etymology

From Latin 'ante-' (before) + 'diluvium' (flood) + Greek 'patriarchēs' (chief or head of a family).
