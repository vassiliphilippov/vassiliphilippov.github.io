nimbus cloud

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪmbəs klaʊd / (AmE), / ˈnɪmbəs klaʊd / (BrE)
A type of thick, dark grey cloud that produces rain.
rain-producing dark cloud

Examples

A nimbus cloud loomed above the city, promising a heavy downpour.
The weather report warned that nimbus clouds were forming along the coast.
Dark nimbus clouds blocked out the afternoon sun as rain began to fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumulonimbus - A towering, dense cloud associated with thunderstorms and heavy rain
  • Degree Lower: stratus - A low-level cloud forming a continuous horizontal gray sheet

Etymology

From Latin nimbus (cloud, rainstorm).
