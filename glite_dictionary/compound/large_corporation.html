large corporation

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ ˌkɔr.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ˌkɔː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a business organization that is very big in size and has significant influence or operations, often internationally
very big and influential company

Examples

Many graduates aspire to join a large corporation for better career opportunities.
A large corporation often has resources to invest in research and development.
Regulations can significantly impact how a large corporation conducts its business internationally.

Synset Examples

She works at a large corporation with global operations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small business - a privately owned company with relatively low revenue and size
  • Synonym: company - a business organization that sells goods or services
