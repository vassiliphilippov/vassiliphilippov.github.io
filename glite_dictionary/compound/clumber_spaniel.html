Clumber spaniel

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌmbɚ spæniəl / (AmE), / ˈklʌmbə ˈspænɪəl / (BrE)
a breed of spaniel known for its large size, heavy build, and long, silky coat, originally bred in England for hunting
a breed of heavy, long-haired spaniel

Examples

The Clumber spaniel is known for its gentle temperament and loyalty.
At the dog show, her Clumber spaniel won best in breed.
They chose a Clumber spaniel because of its suitability for family life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spaniel - a type of dog breed used for hunting and retrieving

Etymology

Named after Clumber Park in Nottinghamshire, England, where the breed was developed.
