luminous flux

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːmɪnəs flʌks / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəs flʌks / (BrE)
the rate at which light energy is emitted from a source, measured in lumens
rate of flow of light energy

Examples

The luminous flux of an LED bulb is higher than that of an incandescent bulb of the same power.
Scientists measure the luminous flux to determine how effective a light source is for illumination.
The lamp's luminous flux was sufficient to light up the entire room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumen - the SI unit of luminous flux

Etymology

From Latin 'lumen' (light) and 'fluxus' (flow).
