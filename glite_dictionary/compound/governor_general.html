governor general

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡʌvərnər ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌɡʌvənə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the official appointed as the representative of the monarch in a Commonwealth country, often performing ceremonial or constitutional duties
the monarch's representative in a Commonwealth nation

Examples

The governor general presided over the ceremony, representing the monarch in Canada.
After her appointment as governor general, she carried out a number of constitutional duties.
New laws often require the signature of the governor general before coming into effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viceroy - the governor of a country or province who rules as the representative of a sovereign
  • Degree Lower: governor - an official who governs a state, province, or other area

Etymology

From English 'governor' + 'general', patterned after French 'gouverneur général'.
