bone oil

1. NOUN

/ boʊn ɔɪl / (AmE), / bəʊn ɔɪl / (BrE)
a yellowish oily liquid obtained by distilling animal bones, used as a lubricant and in leather manufacturing
oily liquid from bones, lubricant

Examples

The tanneries in the old town used bone oil for treating leather hides.
Engineers once relied on bone oil as a lubricant for heavy machinery.
The strong smell of bone oil filled the factory during the distillation process.

2. NOUN

/ boʊn ɔɪl / (AmE), / bəʊn ɔɪl / (BrE)
a dark, foul-smelling oil obtained by heating bones in the absence of air, used mainly in sheep dips and to denature alcohol
oil derived from bones, used industrially

Examples

The factory processed animal remains to produce bone oil for industrial applications.
Historically, bone oil was a common ingredient in sheep dips to control parasites.
Because of its strong odor, proper ventilation is essential when working with bone oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal oil - oil derived from animal tissues
