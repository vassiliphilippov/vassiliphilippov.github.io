ring armour

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / rɪŋ ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
Flexible protective armor made from interlinked metal rings, used especially during the Middle Ages.
flexible armor of interlinked metal rings

Examples

The museum's medieval exhibit features a rare suit of ring armour from the 13th century.
Knights often wore ring armour beneath their tunics for added protection in battle.
The flexibility of ring armour allowed warriors greater freedom of movement than plate armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain mail - armor made of small metal rings linked together in a pattern
