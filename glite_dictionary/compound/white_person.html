White person

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈpɝː.sən / (AmE), / waɪt ˈpɜː.sən / (BrE)
A person of European descent with a light-skinned or pale complexion, especially as categorized by race or ethnicity.
person of European descent; pale/light skin

Examples

The census data showed an increase in the number of White persons living in the area.
She identified as a White person when asked about her ethnicity.
Many White people immigrated to the country during the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Black person - a person belonging to a racial group with dark skin, often of African descent

Etymology

From 'white' (Old English 'hwīt', pale in color) + 'person' (Latin 'persona').
