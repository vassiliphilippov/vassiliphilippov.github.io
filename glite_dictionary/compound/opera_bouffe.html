opera bouffe

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpərə ˈbuf / (AmE), / ˌɒpərə ˈbuːf / (BrE)
a comic opera with a happy ending, often featuring spoken dialogue and lighthearted, satirical themes
comic opera with spoken dialogue

Examples

The local theater is performing an opera bouffe famous for its witty satire and playful melodies.
She prefers the lively atmosphere of opera bouffe to the seriousness of grand opera.
His favorite composer specialized in opera bouffe, delighting audiences with humor and charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operetta - a short opera, usually light or comic in theme

Etymology

From French 'opéra bouffe', meaning 'comic opera'.
