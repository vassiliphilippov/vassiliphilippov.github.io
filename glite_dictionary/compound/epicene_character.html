epicene character

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪsiːn ˈkærəktɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪsiːn ˈkærəktə / (BrE)
A character in literature, drama, or other media who exhibits both male and female physical or psychological traits, or whose gender is ambiguous.
character with both male and female traits

Examples

The novel's epicene character fascinated readers with their fluid identity.
In classical theater, an epicene character might be portrayed to challenge conventional gender roles.
Critics praised the director's bold interpretation of the opera's epicene character.

Synset Examples

The epicene character in the story challenged gender norms.

Etymology

From Greek 'epikoinos' (common to all), via Latin 'epicoenus', describing shared characteristics.
