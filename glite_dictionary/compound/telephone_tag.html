telephone tag

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn tæɡ / (AmE), / ˈtelɪfəʊn tæɡ / (BrE)
a situation in which two or more people repeatedly try to call each other by telephone but fail to connect and end up leaving messages instead
repeated missed phone connections

Examples

We spent the morning playing telephone tag and never actually managed to talk.
After days of telephone tag, the manager finally answered my call.
It's frustrating when important details get delayed by endless rounds of telephone tag.

Synset Examples

After a few rounds of telephone tag, they finally spoke.
They ended up in telephone tag instead of a meeting.
We played telephone tag all week trying to connect.

Etymology

From 'telephone' + 'tag', likening the back-and-forth of missed calls to the children's game of tag.
