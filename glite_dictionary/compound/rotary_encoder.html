rotary encoder

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri ɪnˈkoʊdər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri ɪnˈkəʊdə / (BrE)
an electro-mechanical device that converts the angular position or motion of a rotating shaft or axle to an analog or digital code
device for measuring shaft rotation

Examples

The robot's precise arm movements are made possible by a rotary encoder attached to each joint.
A rotary encoder can help provide accurate feedback on shaft position in industrial machinery.
Engineers replaced the faulty sensor with a new rotary encoder to restore full motion control.

Synset Examples

Rotary encoders are used in many applications that require precise shaft unlimited rotation — including industrial controls, robotics, special purpose photographic lenses, computer input devices (such as optomechanical mice and trackballs), controlled stress rheometers, and rotating radar platforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encoder - a device that converts information into code

Etymology

From 'rotary' (relating to rotation) and 'encoder' (a device that converts information into code).
