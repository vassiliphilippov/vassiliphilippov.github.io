external maxillary artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɝːnəl ˈmæksələrɪ ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈmæksələri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that originates from the external carotid artery and supplies blood to the neck and face
artery supplying neck and face
medical

Examples

The external maxillary artery plays a crucial role in supplying blood to the face and neck.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the external maxillary artery.
Swelling in the region may compress the external maxillary artery, reducing blood flow to facial tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial artery - alternative name for the external maxillary artery, main artery supplying blood to the face
