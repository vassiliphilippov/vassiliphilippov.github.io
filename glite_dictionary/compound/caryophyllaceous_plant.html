caryophyllaceous plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛri.oʊfəˈleɪʃəs ˈplænt / (AmE), / ˌkæri.əfəˈleɪʃəs ˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes species such as carnations and pinks
plant of the Caryophyllaceae family

Examples

The garden features a variety of caryophyllaceous plants such as pinks and carnations.
Many caryophyllaceous plants have distinctive opposite leaves and swollen nodes.
She specializes in the study of caryophyllaceous plants and their unique floral structures.

Etymology

From modern Latin Caryophyllaceae (the botanical family name), from Greek karyophyllon (clove).
