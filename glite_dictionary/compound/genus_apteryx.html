genus Apteryx

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs æpˈtɪr.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs æpˈtɪər.ɪks / (BrE)
The genus of flightless birds known as kiwis, native to New Zealand and belonging to the family Apterygidae.
genus of flightless birds (kiwis)

Examples

The genus Apteryx includes all living species of kiwi birds.
Scientists are studying the genetics of the genus Apteryx to understand its unique evolution.
Unlike most birds, members of the genus Apteryx have tiny vestigial wings and no ability to fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwi - flightless bird native to New Zealand

Etymology

From Greek apterux, meaning 'without wing' (a- 'without' + pteryx 'wing').
