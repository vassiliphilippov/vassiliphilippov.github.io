buckwheat tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌwiːt triː / (AmE), / ˈbʌkwiːt triː / (BrE)
A tree native to low-lying coastal areas of the southeastern United States, known for its glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers.
tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The buckwheat tree is often planted in parks in the southeastern United States for its beautiful white blossoms.
Wildlife in the region depend on the buckwheat tree for shelter and food.
The glossy leaves of the buckwheat tree sparkle in the sunlight after a rainstorm.
