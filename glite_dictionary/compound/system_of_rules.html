system of rules

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəm əv ruːlz / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəm əv ruːlz / (BrE)
a set of principles or methods, often complex and organized, that govern behavior or direct how something operates
organized set of governing principles

Examples

Every country has its own system of rules that citizens are expected to follow.
The school established a new system of rules to improve student behavior.
Learning a language often involves understanding its system of rules for grammar and usage.

Synset Examples

they have to operate under a system they oppose
that language has a complex system for indicating gender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code - a set of rules or principles, especially for conduct
  • Synonym: regulation - a rule or directive made and maintained by an authority
  • Antonym: anarchy - absence of government and absolute freedom of the individual; lack of rules
