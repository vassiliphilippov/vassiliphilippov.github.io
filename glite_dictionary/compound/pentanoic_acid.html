pentanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ pɛnˌtænoʊˈɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / pɛnˌtanəʊˈɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a clear, oily liquid carboxylic acid (C5H10O2) used in making perfumes, pharmaceuticals, and plasticizers
a carboxylic acid used in perfumes and drugs
medical

Examples

Pentanoic acid is often used by chemists to manufacture certain fragrance compounds.
Exposure to pentanoic acid can cause irritation if not handled with care in the laboratory.
Some drugs contain derivatives of pentanoic acid to enhance their effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valeric acid - another name for pentanoic acid

Etymology

From 'penta-' (Greek for five) + '-anoic acid' (from organic chemistry nomenclature).
