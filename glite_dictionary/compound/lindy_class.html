Lindy class

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪndi klæs / (AmE), / ˈlɪndi klɑːs / (BrE)
a class or course for learning the Lindy Hop dance, a lively form of swing dance originated in the United States
class for learning Lindy Hop dance

Examples

She signed up for a Lindy class at the local community center.
After taking just one Lindy class, he was hooked on swing dancing.
Many dance studios offer a weekly Lindy class for all skill levels.

Synset Examples

They took a Lindy class to learn the classic swing dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lindy Hop - the dance style taught in a Lindy class; a lively swing dance

Etymology

Lindy from Lindy Hop, a swing dance named after aviator Charles Lindbergh ("Lucky Lindy"); class from Latin 'classis'.
