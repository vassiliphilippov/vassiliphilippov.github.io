Semitic-speaking

1. ADJ

/ səˈmɪtɪk ˌspiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪˈmɪtɪk ˌspiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
able to speak or communicate in a Semitic language.
able to speak a Semitic language

Examples

Many Semitic-speaking communities can be found throughout the Middle East and North Africa.
The museum's exhibition featured artifacts from ancient Semitic-speaking peoples.
She is Semitic-speaking, fluent in both Arabic and Hebrew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Semitic - relating to a family of languages including Hebrew, Arabic, and Aramaic
  • Antonym: Indo-European-speaking - able to speak an Indo-European language

Etymology

From 'Semitic', referring to a family of languages, and 'speaking'.
