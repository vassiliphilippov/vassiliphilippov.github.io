caper family

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪpər ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpə ˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, Capparaceae, that includes the caper and related genera such as Capparis and Cleome.
family of flowering plants (Capparaceae)

Examples

The caper family includes a variety of shrubs and small trees found in warm climates.
The Mediterranean caper bush is a well-known member of the caper family.
Botanists recently reclassified certain species within the caper family based on genetic evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Capparaceae - scientific name for the caper family of plants

Etymology

Named after the type genus Capparis (caper) + family suffix.
