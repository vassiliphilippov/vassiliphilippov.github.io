writ of error

1. NOUN

/ ɹɪt əv ˈɛɹɚ / (AmE), / ɹɪt əv ˈɛɹə / (BrE)
an official order from an appellate court requiring a lower court to provide the record of a trial for review of legal errors
order for review of trial records by a higher court
legal

Examples

The defense filed a writ of error to challenge the verdict in the appellate court.
A writ of error was granted, compelling the lower court to submit the trial records for review.
Historically, a writ of error allowed cases to be re-examined for mistakes in legal procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeal - a legal challenge to a court's decision in a higher court

Etymology

From Middle English 'writ' (written order) and 'error' (mistake), used in legal contexts since the 13th century.
