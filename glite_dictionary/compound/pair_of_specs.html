pair of specs

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛɚ əv ˈspɛks / (AmE), / ˈpeə əv ˈspɛks / (BrE)
An informal term for eyeglasses; a set of lenses held in a frame used to correct or assist vision.
informal term for eyeglasses
informal

Examples

She realized she couldn't read the small print without her pair of specs.
After breaking his old pair of specs, he went to the optician for a new one.
It's amazing how much clearer the world seems through a new pair of specs.

Synset Examples

He lost his pair of specs while working in the garden.
A new pair of specs helped her read much better.
She bought a stylish pair of specs online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glasses - eyeglasses; lenses set in a frame, used to correct or assist vision
  • Synonym: spectacles - formal term for eyeglasses

Etymology

Shortened from 'spectacles' (late Middle English), itself from Latin 'spectaculum' (a show).
