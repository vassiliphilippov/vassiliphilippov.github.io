polygenic disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈdʒɛnɪk dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdʒɛnɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
An inherited disease that is caused by the combined effect of multiple genes, rather than a single gene.
inherited disease caused by multiple genes
medical

Examples

Type 2 diabetes is a common example of a polygenic disease influenced by multiple genetic and environmental factors.
Many heart conditions are considered polygenic diseases because several genes contribute to an individual's risk.
Researchers study polygenic disease patterns to better understand complex hereditary disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monogenic disease - an inherited disease caused by a single gene mutation

Etymology

From 'polygenic' (from Greek 'polys' meaning 'many' and 'genes') + 'disease'.
