complementary angles

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑmpləˈmɛn.tə.ri ˈæŋ.ɡəlz / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛn.təri ˈæŋ.ɡlz / (BrE)
Two angles whose measures add up to exactly 90 degrees.
two angles adding to 90 degrees

Examples

If two angles are complementary angles, their measures add up to 90 degrees.
The teacher asked the students to find pairs of complementary angles in the diagram.
An angle of 30 degrees and an angle of 60 degrees are complementary angles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supplementary angles - two angles whose measures add up to 180 degrees

Etymology

From Latin 'complementum' (that which fills up or completes) and 'angulus' (angle).
