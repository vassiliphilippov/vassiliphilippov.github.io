family Betulaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛtʃəˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌbɛtjʊˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of monoecious trees and shrubs including birches, alders, hornbeams, hazels, and hop-hornbeams
a botanical family of birch and related trees

Examples

The family Betulaceae includes well-known trees such as birches and alders.
Some gardeners specialize in cultivating members of the family Betulaceae for their ornamental value.
Pollination within the family Betulaceae typically occurs by wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - tree of the genus Betula, a member of Betulaceae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Betulaceae, based on the type genus Betula (birch) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
