dowager's hump

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊədʒɚz hʌmp / (AmE), / ˈdaʊədʒəz hʌmp / (BrE)
an abnormal outward curvature of the upper spine, resulting in a hunched appearance, especially in elderly women, often due to osteoporosis
spinal curvature caused by osteoporosis
medical

Examples

The patient developed a noticeable dowager's hump after years of untreated osteoporosis.
Physical therapy can help manage the discomfort associated with a dowager's hump.
Elderly women are at higher risk for developing a dowager's hump as they age.

Etymology

From 'dowager' (a dignified elderly woman, especially a widow) and 'hump', referencing the stereotypical posture in older women.
