powerful rhetoric

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ.fəl rɛˈtɒr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə.fəl rɪˈtɒr.ɪk / (BrE)
language that is especially persuasive and impactful, often used to influence people or convey strong emotions
persuasive and impactful language

Examples

The candidate's powerful rhetoric swayed many undecided voters during the debate.
She relied on powerful rhetoric to inspire her team before the big match.
Historians often cite Martin Luther King Jr.'s powerful rhetoric as a key factor in the civil rights movement.

Synset Examples

Her speech was full of powerful rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eloquence - the ability to use language in a persuasive and moving way
  • Antonym: bland rhetoric - language that lacks impact or persuasiveness
