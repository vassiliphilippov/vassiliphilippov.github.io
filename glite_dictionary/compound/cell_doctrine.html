cell doctrine

1. NOUN

/ sɛl ˈdɑktrɪn / (AmE), / sɛl ˈdɒktrɪn / (BrE)
The scientific theory in biology which states that cells are the basic structural and functional units of all living organisms.
theory that cells are the basis of life

Examples

The cell doctrine revolutionized biology by establishing the cell as the basic unit of all living organisms.
According to the cell doctrine, every new cell arises from a pre-existing cell.
The introduction of the cell doctrine in the 19th century marked a turning point in medical science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell theory - scientific theory that all living things are composed of cells

Etymology

Formed from 'cell' (from Latin 'cella', storeroom or chamber) and 'doctrine' (from Latin 'doctrina', teaching).
