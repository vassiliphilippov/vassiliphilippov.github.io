duck-billed dinosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌk ˌbɪld ˈdaɪ.nəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈdʌk ˌbɪld ˈdaɪ.nə.sɔː / (BrE)
any of a group of large plant-eating dinosaurs of the Cretaceous period, having a long, flat, duck-like bill; especially referring to hadrosaurids.
plant-eating dinosaur with a duck-like bill

Examples

The fossilized skull of a duck-billed dinosaur was discovered in the Canadian badlands.
Many paleontologists believe the duck-billed dinosaur used its broad beak to eat aquatic plants.
Children at the museum were fascinated by the huge skeleton of a duck-billed dinosaur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hadrosaur - a type of duck-billed dinosaur
