heel tip

1. NOUN

/ hil tɪp / (AmE), / hiːl tɪp / (BrE)
The lower end of a shoe or boot heel, usually made of plastic or metal, that makes contact with the ground and can often be replaced when worn.
bottom piece of a shoe heel

Examples

The cobbler replaced the worn-out heel tip on my favorite boots.
After months of daily use, her shoe's heel tip finally came loose.
It's important to check the heel tip regularly to prevent damage to the entire heel.

Synset Examples

The bottom of most heels usually has a plastic or metal heel tip that wears away with use and can be easily replaced.
