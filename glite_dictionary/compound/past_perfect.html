past perfect

1. NOUN

/ pæst ˈpɜrfɪkt / (AmE), / pɑːst ˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
a verb tense used to refer to an action that was completed before another past action or time
a verb tense denoting completed past action

Examples

He explained that he had left his keys at home, using the past perfect tense.
In English grammar, the past perfect is formed with 'had' plus the past participle.
Students often confuse the past perfect with the simple past when writing narratives.

Synset Examples

‘I had finished’ is an example of the past perfect

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: present perfect - a verb tense describing completed actions relevant to the present

Etymology

From 'past' (referring to previous time) and Latin 'perfectus' (completed, finished).
