genus Entelea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɛnˈtiː.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɛnˈtiː.li.ə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs native to New Zealand, known for their large leaves and distinctive flowers.
genus of evergreen New Zealand shrubs

Examples

The genus Entelea includes plants commonly found in the forests of New Zealand.
Scientists identified a new species belonging to the genus Entelea after extensive field research.
Leaves from the genus Entelea are notable for their impressive size and unique texture.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Entelea', used as botanical nomenclature for this genus.
