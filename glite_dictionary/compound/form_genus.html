form genus

1. NOUN

/ fɔrm ˈdʒɛnəs / (AmE), / fɔːm ˈdʒɛnəs / (BrE)
In biology, an artificial taxonomic category used for organisms whose true relationships are unclear, often providing the first part of a species' scientific name.
artificial genus for obscure relationships (biology)

Examples

In paleontology, a form genus is often assigned to fossils that cannot be classified more precisely.
The fungus was placed in a form genus because its relationships to other fungi are not well understood.
Because of incomplete data, many prehistoric plants are grouped under a temporary form genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus - a principal taxonomic category ranking above species and below family

Etymology

From Latin 'forma' (shape, form) and 'genus' (kind, type).

2. NOUN

/ fɔrm ˈdʒiː.nəs / (AmE), / fɔːm ˈdʒiː.nəs / (BrE)
an artificial taxonomic category created based on similarities in form, used especially for fossil organisms whose true relationships are unclear
artificial taxonomic group based on form

Examples

Many fossil plants are classified into a form genus due to limited information from their remains.
Paleontologists use the term form genus when describing organisms with uncertain evolutionary relationships.
The form genus concept helps group fossils that share similar structures but may not be closely related.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus - biological classification ranking above species, below family

Etymology

From Latin 'forma' (shape) + Latin 'genus' (kind, type).
