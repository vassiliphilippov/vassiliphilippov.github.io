soft palate

1. NOUN

/ sɔft ˈpælɪt / (AmE), / sɒft ˈpælət / (BrE)
the muscular, flexible part toward the back of the roof of the mouth that closes off the nasal passages during swallowing or speaking
muscular part of roof of mouth
medical

Examples

The surgeon needed to examine the soft palate before proceeding with the operation.
When you swallow, the soft palate rises to block food from entering your nose.
A cleft soft palate can cause difficulties with speech and feeding in infants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard palate - the bony front part of the roof of the mouth
