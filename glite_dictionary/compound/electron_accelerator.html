electron accelerator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛk.trɑn əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a scientific device that accelerates electrons to high speeds, often for experimental or medical purposes
device that accelerates electrons

Examples

The laboratory just installed a new electron accelerator to enhance its research capabilities.
Physicists use an electron accelerator to study subatomic particles and fundamental forces.
Medical treatments for cancer sometimes employ an electron accelerator for radiation therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particle accelerator - device that accelerates charged particles

Etymology

From 'electron,' named by G. Johnstone Stoney (1891), and 'accelerator,' from Latin accelerare (to hasten).
