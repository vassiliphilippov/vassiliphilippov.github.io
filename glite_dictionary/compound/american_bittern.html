American bittern

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈbɪtɚn / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈbɪtən / (BrE)
a North American species of bittern, a stocky wading bird, slightly smaller than its European relative, with a streaked brown and buff plumage (Botaurus lentiginosus)
a North American species of bittern (bird)

Examples

The American bittern is known for its booming call that echoes across marshes in spring.
Birdwatchers patiently waited to spot an elusive American bittern hiding among the reeds.
Although American bitterns are secretive, their unique camouflage makes them fascinating to observe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittern - a wading bird with brownish plumage of the heron family
