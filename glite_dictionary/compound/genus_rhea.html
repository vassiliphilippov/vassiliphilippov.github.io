genus Rhea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈriːə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈriːə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Rheidae, comprising large, tall, flightless South American birds similar to but smaller than ostriches.
type genus of Rheidae, tall flightless South American birds

Examples

The genus Rhea is native to the grasslands and open woodlands of South America.
Scientists studied the unique adaptations of birds in the genus Rhea for their environment.
Unlike the ostrich, members of the genus Rhea have three toes on each foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhea - a large, flightless bird native to South America, similar to but smaller than the ostrich
  • Degree Higher: ostrich - a very large African flightless bird

Etymology

From Latin 'rhea', from the genus name given by Carl Linnaeus.
