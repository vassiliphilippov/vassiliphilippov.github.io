Irish National Liberation Army

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ ˌnæʃnəl ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈænmi / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ ˌnaʃnəl ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈænmi / (BrE)
a radical paramilitary organization seeking the removal of British forces from Northern Ireland and the unification of Ireland
Irish republican paramilitary group

Examples

The British government regarded the Irish National Liberation Army as a terrorist organization during the Troubles.
After years of violent activities, the Irish National Liberation Army declared a ceasefire in 1998.
Scholars have written extensively about the history of the Irish National Liberation Army and its impact on Northern Irish politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Provisional Irish Republican Army - Irish republican paramilitary group active during the Troubles
  • Antonym: Ulster Defence Association - Loyalist paramilitary group in Northern Ireland
