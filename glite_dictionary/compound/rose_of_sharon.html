rose of Sharon

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz əv ˈʃɛrən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz əv ˈʃærən / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Asia, with large bell-shaped flowers in shades of rose, purple, or white, widely grown as an ornamental plant.
ornamental shrub/tree with showy flowers

Examples

Our garden is brightened each summer by the vibrant rose of Sharon blossoms.
The rose of Sharon thrives in a variety of soil types and climates.
She planted a rose of Sharon near the fence to create a colorful hedge.

Etymology

Biblical phrase 'rose of Sharon' (Song of Solomon), later applied to various flowering plants; now mostly to Hibiscus syriacus.
