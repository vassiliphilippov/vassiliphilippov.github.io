local bashaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəl bəˈʃɑː / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəl bəˈʃɑː / (BrE)
a local authority figure or ruler, especially one with autocratic power
local autocratic ruler; authority figure
archaic

Examples

The local bashaw was respected and even feared by all the villagers.
During the occupation, the new local bashaw enforced his own set of rules.
Some communities resented the control of their local bashaw and eventually rebelled.

Synset Examples

The local bashaw was known for his strict laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasha - a high-ranking Turkish officer or governor; original sense of 'bashaw'
  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler
  • Antonym: subject - a person under the rule of a sovereign

Etymology

From 'bashaw', an Anglicized form of 'pasha', a title for high officials in the Ottoman Empire, combined with 'local'.
