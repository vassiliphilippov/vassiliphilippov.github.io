sciaenid fish

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈiːnɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / saɪˈiːnɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish belonging to the family Sciaenidae, known for their carnivorous diet and large air bladder used to produce sound; includes species such as drums and croakers.
fish of the Sciaenidae family, sound-producing

Examples

Many commercial fisheries target sciaenid fish for their firm, white flesh and distinctive flavors.
The croaker is a well-known sciaenid fish that emits sound by vibrating its swim bladder.
Researchers studied the vocalization patterns of several sciaenid fish species in the estuary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum - a type of sciaenid fish known for producing a drumming sound
  • Synonym: croaker - a common name for several species of sciaenid fish

Etymology

From New Latin Sciaenidae, from genus Sciaena (a kind of sea fish), from Greek skiaina.
