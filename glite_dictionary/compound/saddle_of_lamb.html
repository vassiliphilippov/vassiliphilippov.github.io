saddle of lamb

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædəl əv læm / (AmE), / ˈsædəl əv læm / (BrE)
a cut of lamb consisting of the backbone and both loins, often roasted as a single joint
a cut of lamb including backbone and loins

Examples

The chef prepared a saddle of lamb stuffed with herbs and garlic for the main course.
For the holiday feast, they roasted a whole saddle of lamb to serve a large group of guests.
At the butcher’s, she asked for a fresh saddle of lamb to try a new recipe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rack of lamb - a cut of lamb including the rib section
