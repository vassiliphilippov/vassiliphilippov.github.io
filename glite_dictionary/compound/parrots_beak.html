parrot's beak

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəts biːk / (AmE), / ˈpærəts biːk / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub of New Zealand, known for its clawlike or beaklike scarlet to white flowers
New Zealand shrub with beaklike flowers

Examples

The parrot's beak produces striking scarlet flowers during the spring season.
Many gardens in New Zealand feature the parrot's beak for its ornamental beauty.
Researchers studied the unique shape of the parrot's beak flowers to understand pollinator attraction.
