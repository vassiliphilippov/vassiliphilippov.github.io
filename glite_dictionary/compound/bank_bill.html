bank bill

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk bɪl / (AmE), / baŋk bɪl / (BrE)
a piece of paper money, especially one issued by a central bank, used as legal tender for financial transactions
paper money issued by a bank

Examples

She deposited the bank bill into her account after receiving it from a client.
He paid for his lunch with a crisp fifty-dollar bank bill.
The old bank bill was rare and valuable to collectors.

Synset Examples

he peeled off five one-thousand-zloty notes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money issued by a bank, used as legal tender
  • Antonym: coin - a piece of metal used as money

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial institution) + 'bill' (written statement of money owed).
