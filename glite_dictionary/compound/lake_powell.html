Lake Powell

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈpaʊəl / (AmE), / leɪk ˈpaʊəl / (BrE)
a large reservoir on the Colorado River, located in southern Utah and northern Arizona, known as the second largest in the United States.
major US reservoir on the Colorado River

Examples

Every summer, families flock to Lake Powell for boating and camping trips.
The creation of Lake Powell was the result of building the Glen Canyon Dam.
Drought has significantly lowered water levels at Lake Powell in recent years.

Etymology

Named after John Wesley Powell, American explorer of the Colorado River.
