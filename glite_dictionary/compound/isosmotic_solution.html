isosmotic solution

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsɑzˈmɑtɪk səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪsɒzˈmɒtɪk səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a solution with the same osmotic pressure as another solution, especially as blood or bodily fluids
solution with equal osmotic pressure
medical

Examples

The intravenous fluid administered was an isosmotic solution to prevent cellular swelling.
Using an isosmotic solution helps maintain the balance of fluids in medical treatments.
Blood plasma is naturally an isosmotic solution relative to red blood cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyperosmotic solution - solution with higher osmotic pressure than another
  • Antonym: hypoosmotic solution - solution with lower osmotic pressure than another

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'osmos' (pushing) + 'solution'.
