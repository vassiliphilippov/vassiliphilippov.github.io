bunya bunya tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌnjə ˈbʌnjə tri / (AmE), / ˈbʌnjə ˈbʌnjə triː / (BrE)
An Australian coniferous tree (Araucaria bidwillii) with large edible seeds similar in taste to roasted chestnuts, valued by Aboriginal Australians.
Australian conifer with edible seeds

Examples

The bunya bunya tree produces large cones that contain edible seeds enjoyed by local wildlife.
Aboriginal communities have traditionally gathered the seeds of the bunya bunya tree for food.
Botanists traveled to Queensland to study the ancient bunya bunya tree and its ecological significance.

Etymology

From the Aboriginal Gubbi Gubbi language 'banya'.
