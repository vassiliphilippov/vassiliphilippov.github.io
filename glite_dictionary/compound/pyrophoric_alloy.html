pyrophoric alloy

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈfɔrɪk əˈlɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌpaɪrəˈfɒrɪk əˈlɔɪ / (BrE)
an alloy that emits sparks when struck or scratched with steel, commonly used in lighter flints
alloy emitting sparks when struck

Examples

The pyrophoric alloy inside the lighter creates sparks when the wheel is spun.
Workers handle pyrophoric alloys with care due to their ability to ignite easily.
A new type of pyrophoric alloy was developed for use in emergency fire-starting kits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flint - a hard stone used to emit sparks, especially for starting fires

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr-' (fire) and '-phoros' (bearing), with 'alloy' from Old French 'aloi'.
