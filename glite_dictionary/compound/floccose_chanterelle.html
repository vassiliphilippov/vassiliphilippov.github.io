floccose chanterelle

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑːkoʊs ˌʃæn.təˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˈflɒk.əʊs ˌʃæn.təˈrel / (BrE)
a mildly poisonous mushroom with a fruiting body shaped like a hollow trumpet, belonging to the genus Cantharellus
a mildly poisonous trumpet-shaped mushroom

Examples

The floccose chanterelle can be mistaken for edible varieties, so caution is advised when foraging.
Experts warn against eating the floccose chanterelle due to its mild toxicity.
The forest floor was dotted with clusters of floccose chanterelles after the autumn rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanterelle - an edible, trumpet-shaped mushroom

Etymology

From Latin floccosus (tufted, woolly) and French chanterelle (type of mushroom).
