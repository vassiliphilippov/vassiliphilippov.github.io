subfamily Petauristidae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæməli pɛˌtɔːrɪˈstaɪdiː / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæmɪli piːˌtɔːrɪˈstiːdiː / (BrE)
A subfamily of flying squirrels found in the Old World, comprising species known as Old World flying squirrels.
taxonomic group of Old World flying squirrels

Examples

Members of the subfamily Petauristidae are known for their remarkable gliding abilities in forest canopies.
Researchers discovered a new species within the subfamily Petauristidae in Southeast Asia.
The subfamily Petauristidae is distinguished from New World flying squirrels by anatomical differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying squirrel - a type of squirrel capable of gliding using a membrane between the limbs

Etymology

From New Latin Petauristidae, based on Petaurista (a genus of flying squirrel) and Latin -idae (taxonomic suffix for animal families).
