military reserve

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri rɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
A part of the armed forces composed of personnel who are not on active duty but can be called up for service in emergencies.
armed forces not on active duty

Examples

After the hurricane, members of the military reserve were called in to assist with relief operations.
He spent ten years in the military reserve after completing his active duty service.
Many countries maintain a military reserve to ensure they can respond quickly to emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve forces - armed forces that can be called up in emergencies
  • Antonym: active duty - full-time service in the armed forces
