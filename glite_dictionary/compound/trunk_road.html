trunk road

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋk roʊd / (AmE), / trʌŋk rəʊd / (BrE)
A major road designed for long-distance traffic, especially one forming part of a national or regional network.
major road for long-distance traffic
british

Examples

The lorry overturned on the trunk road, causing major traffic delays.
Many villages are connected to the city by a trunk road that runs through the countryside.
Plans are underway to upgrade the old trunk road to improve transportation efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highway - a main road, especially one connecting major towns or cities
  • Antonym: minor road - a less important or secondary road

Etymology

From 'trunk' (main stem or central part) and 'road'; 'trunk road' referring to a principal route.
