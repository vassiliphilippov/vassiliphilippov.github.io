House of Windsor

1. PROPN

/ ˌhaʊs əv ˈwɪnzər / (AmE), / ˌhaʊs əv ˈwɪnzə / (BrE)
the reigning royal family of the United Kingdom and other Commonwealth realms since 1917
British royal family since 1917
british

Examples

The House of Windsor has seen several historic changes during its reign.
Queen Elizabeth II was a prominent member of the House of Windsor.
Many key royal events have been celebrated by the House of Windsor over the past century.

Etymology

The name 'Windsor' was adopted in 1917, replacing 'House of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha', due to anti-German sentiment during World War I.
