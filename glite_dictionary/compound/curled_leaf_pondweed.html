curled leaf pondweed

1. NOUN

/ kɝld lif ˈpɑndˌwid / (AmE), / kɜːld liːf ˈpɒndˌwiːd / (BrE)
a European aquatic plant (Potamogeton crispus), now naturalized in the eastern United States and California, noted for its curly, ribbon-like submerged leaves
European aquatic plant with curly leaves

Examples

The shallow lake was covered in thick patches of curled leaf pondweed during the summer.
Gardeners often struggle to control the spread of curled leaf pondweed in ornamental ponds.
Scientists are studying how curled leaf pondweed affects native aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pondweed - an aquatic plant of the genus Potamogeton
