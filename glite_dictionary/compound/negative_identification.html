negative identification

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡət̬ɪv ˌaɪˌdɛntəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˌaɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of identifying someone by establishing that they are not among a group of individuals already recognized by a system; often used to prevent a person from assuming multiple identities
method of excluding someone from a known group based on identity

Examples

The airport security system uses negative identification to ensure that only authorized staff enter restricted areas.
Banks may adopt negative identification to prevent users from creating multiple fraudulent accounts.
Unlike traditional methods, negative identification focuses on excluding individuals from the list of known identities.

Synset Examples

a system for negative identification can prevent the use of multiple identities by a single person
