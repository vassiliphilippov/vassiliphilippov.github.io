pep rally

1. NOUN

/ pɛp ˈrӕli / (AmE), / pɛp ˈrӕli / (BrE)
an event, typically held before a sports game, where students or supporters gather to encourage enthusiasm and support for their team
event to boost team spirit before a game
american

Examples

The school organized a pep rally to get everyone excited for the big football game.
Cheerleaders led the crowd in chants during the pep rally Friday afternoon.
Before the championship, the team attended a pep rally full of music and cheers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rally - a mass meeting of people for a common purpose

Etymology

Formed from 'pep' (short for 'pep up', meaning energy or vigor) and 'rally' (a gathering for a common purpose); originated in American English in the early 20th century.
