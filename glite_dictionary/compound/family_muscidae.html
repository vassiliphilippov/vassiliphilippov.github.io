family Muscidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli mjuːˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli mjuːˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of two-winged flies that includes the housefly and related species.
family of flies including houseflies

Examples

The family Muscidae includes many species found around human habitation.
Scientists study the family Muscidae to understand disease transmission.
Members of the family Muscidae are known for their rapid breeding cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housefly - a common fly species found in homes

Etymology

From New Latin 'Muscidae', based on Latin 'musca' (fly).
