bile duct

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪl dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪl dʌkt / (BrE)
a tube that carries bile from the liver and gallbladder to the duodenum.
tube carrying bile to the intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a stone that was blocking the bile duct.
Damage to the bile duct can cause serious digestive problems.
An infection in the bile duct may require immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatic duct - the duct that carries bile from the liver
  • Synonym: cystic duct - the duct that carries bile from the gallbladder
