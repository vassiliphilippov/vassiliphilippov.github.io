degree centigrade

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡri ˈsɛntɪɡreɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈɡri ˈsentiɡreɪd / (BrE)
a unit for measuring temperature on the centigrade (Celsius) scale, equal to one one-hundredth of the interval between the freezing and boiling points of water.
a unit of temperature on the Celsius scale

Examples

The temperature is expected to drop to five degrees centigrade tonight.
Most scientists now use degrees centigrade to report their temperature results.
Water freezes at zero degrees centigrade and boils at one hundred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degree Celsius - a unit for measuring temperature on the Celsius scale

Etymology

From Latin 'centum' (hundred) and 'gradus' (step, degree); the term 'centigrade' was replaced by 'Celsius' but still understood.
