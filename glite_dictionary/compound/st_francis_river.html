St. Francis River

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈfrænsɪs ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈfrɑːnsɪs ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the central United States, rising in Missouri and flowing southeast through Arkansas as a tributary of the Mississippi River
river in Missouri and Arkansas, USA

Examples

The St. Francis River often floods during the spring rains in Arkansas.
We went fishing by the banks of the St. Francis River last weekend.
The St. Francis River plays a vital role in local agriculture due to its water resources.

Etymology

Named after Saint Francis of Assisi.
