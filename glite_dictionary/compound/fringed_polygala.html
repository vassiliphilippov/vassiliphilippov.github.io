fringed polygala

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪndʒd pəˌlɪɡələ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪndʒd pəˌlɪɡələ / (BrE)
A perennial milkwort of eastern North America with wintergreen-like leaves and typically rosy-purple flowers featuring winged sepals.
North American perennial milkwort with rosy-purple flowers

Examples

The woods were dotted with clusters of fringed polygala in full bloom.
We identified the fringed polygala by its rosy-purple flowers and wintergreen-like leaves.
Hikers paused to admire several patches of fringed polygala along the trail.

Etymology

From polygala, a genus name from Greek 'poly' (many) and 'gala' (milk), in reference to believed milk-increasing properties.
