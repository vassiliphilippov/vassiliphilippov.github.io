Eastern Standard Time

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstɚn ˈstændɚd taɪm / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈstændəd taɪm / (BrE)
the standard time of the eastern part of the United States, five hours behind Greenwich Mean Time (GMT-5)
time zone for eastern US (GMT-5)
american

Examples

New York City observes Eastern Standard Time during the winter months.
The conference call is scheduled for 3:00 p.m. Eastern Standard Time.
When clocks are set back in November, we return to Eastern Standard Time.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Eastern Daylight Time - the daylight saving time for the eastern US
