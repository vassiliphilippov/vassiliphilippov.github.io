Mackem accent

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækəm ˈæksɛnt / (AmE), / ˈmækəm ˈæksənt / (BrE)
the distinctive way of speaking English characteristic of people from Sunderland and the surrounding area
accent of people from Sunderland
british

Examples

Some people find the Mackem accent difficult to understand at first.
She spoke with a strong Mackem accent, instantly revealing her Sunderland roots.
The local radio station often features presenters with a Mackem accent.

Synset Examples

The Mackem accent is distinctive and charming.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Geordie accent - the accent of people from Newcastle and Tyneside

Etymology

'Mackem' derives from a local Sunderland term meaning 'make them,' used to refer to locals; 'accent' from Latin 'accentus' (tone, signal).
