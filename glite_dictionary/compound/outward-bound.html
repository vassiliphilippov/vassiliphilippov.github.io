outward-bound

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtwɚd ˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtwəd ˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a ship, train, etc.) going away from a particular place, especially a port or city, on the first stage of its journey.
going away from a place; departing

Examples

The port was bustling with outward-bound ships ready to sail at dawn.
As the outward-bound train pulled away, she waved goodbye to her family.
Customs officers inspected all outward-bound cargo leaving the country.

Synset Examples

the departing train
an outward journey
outward-bound ships

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homeward-bound - heading toward home or returning

Etymology

From 'outward' (away from the center) + 'bound' (destined or heading).
