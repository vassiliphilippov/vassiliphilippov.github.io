base runner

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪs ˌrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪs ˌrʌnə / (BrE)
a player on the batting team in baseball who is on base or running in an attempt to reach a base
baseball player running or occupying a base

Examples

The base runner tried to steal second, but the catcher threw him out.
There were two base runners on when the batter hit a home run.
A skilled base runner knows when to take chances on the field.
