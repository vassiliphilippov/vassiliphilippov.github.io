nasotracheal tube

1. NOUN

/ ˌneɪzoʊˈtreɪkiəl tuːb / (AmE), / ˌneɪzəʊˈtreɪkɪəl tjuːb / (BrE)
A medical tube inserted into the trachea through the nose and pharynx to deliver oxygen or maintain an airway.
medical tube for airway via nose
medical

Examples

The doctor decided to use a nasotracheal tube for the procedure due to the patient's facial injuries.
Proper placement of the nasotracheal tube was confirmed by monitoring the patient's breathing sounds.
During surgery, the anesthesiologist carefully inserted the nasotracheal tube to secure the airway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endotracheal tube - a tube inserted into the trachea through the mouth or nose to maintain an open airway
  • Degree Lower: orotracheal tube - a tube inserted into the trachea through the mouth

Etymology

From 'naso-' (relating to the nose) + 'tracheal' (relating to the trachea, from Greek 'tracheia') + 'tube'.
