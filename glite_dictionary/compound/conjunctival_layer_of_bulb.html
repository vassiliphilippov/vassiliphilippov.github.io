conjunctival layer of bulb

1. NOUN

/ kənˌdʒʌŋkˈtaɪvəl leɪər əv bʌlb / (AmE), / kənˌdʒʌŋkˈtʌɪvəl leɪə əv bʌlb / (BrE)
the part of the conjunctiva that covers the front of the sclera and the surface epithelium of the cornea in the eye
anatomical layer of the conjunctiva in the eye
medical

Examples

The conjunctival layer of bulb plays a crucial role in protecting the surface of the eye.
Inflammation of the conjunctival layer of bulb can cause discomfort and redness.
Surgeons must take care not to damage the conjunctival layer of bulb during procedures involving the anterior eye.
