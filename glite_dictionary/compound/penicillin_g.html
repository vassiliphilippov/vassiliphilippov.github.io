penicillin G

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn ˈdʒi / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn ˈdʒiː / (BrE)
a naturally occurring form of penicillin used as the principal component in many commercial antibiotics, effective against a broad range of bacteria
a major form of penicillin antibiotic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed penicillin G to treat the patient's bacterial infection.
Many hospitals stock penicillin G for emergency treatment of severe infections.
Allergic reactions can sometimes occur in patients receiving penicillin G.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penicillin - a group of antibiotics derived from Penicillium molds

Etymology

The 'G' stands for 'Gold', indicating its status as the standard or reference penicillin type.
