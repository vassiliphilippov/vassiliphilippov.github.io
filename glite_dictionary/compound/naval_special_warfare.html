Naval Special Warfare

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl ˈspɛʃəl ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˈspɛʃəl ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
the branch of a navy responsible for conducting unconventional and counter-guerrilla warfare, including special operations
naval special operations branch

Examples

The Naval Special Warfare community includes the elite SEAL teams renowned for their rigorous training.
He served for ten years in Naval Special Warfare, participating in numerous covert operations.
The government announced increased funding for Naval Special Warfare to bolster counter-terrorism capabilities.
