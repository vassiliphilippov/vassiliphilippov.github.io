silkworm moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlkˌwɜrm mɔθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌwɜːm mɒθ / (BrE)
A moderate-sized moth native to Asia, whose larvae produce silk and feed on mulberry leaves, especially Bombyx mori.
moth species producing silk

Examples

The silkworm moth is cultivated extensively for its ability to produce valuable silk threads.
After completing its pupal stage, the silkworm moth emerges and lays eggs for the next generation.
Farmers carefully tend to the silkworm moths to ensure high-quality silk production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silkworm - the larva of the silkworm moth that produces silk

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlkˌwɝm mɔθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌwɜːm mɒθ / (BrE)
a moth of the family Saturniidae, especially those whose larvae produce silk, such as the Bombyx mori
moth whose larvae produce silk

Examples

The silkworm moth is famous for its role in the production of natural silk.
Many species of silkworm moth belong to the family Saturniidae.
Scientists study the silkworm moth to better understand sericulture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bombyx mori - domesticated silkworm moth species
