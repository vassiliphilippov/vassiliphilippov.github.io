bush clover

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A shrubby or herbaceous plant of the genus Lespedeza, used for forage, soil improvement, and hay, especially in the southern United States.
a plant of the genus Lespedeza, used in forage and hay

Examples

Farmers in the region often plant bush clover to help restore soil fertility.
The fields were covered with wild bush clover, attracting many pollinators in the summer.
She identified the low-growing bush clover as a valuable forage crop for cattle.
