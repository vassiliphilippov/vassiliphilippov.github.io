Lehigh River

1. PROPN

/ ˈliːhaɪ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈliːhaɪ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in eastern Pennsylvania, USA, flowing southeast into the Delaware River
river in eastern Pennsylvania, USA

Examples

The Lehigh River is popular for whitewater rafting and fishing during the summer months.
Several towns along the Lehigh River developed because of the coal and steel industries.
The autumn foliage along the Lehigh River attracts many photographers and tourists every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Delaware River - major river on the Atlantic coast of the United States

Etymology

Named after the Lehigh Valley and Lehigh County; 'Lehigh' is derived from the Lenape word 'Lechauwekink', meaning 'where there are forks'.
