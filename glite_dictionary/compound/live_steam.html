live steam

1. NOUN

/ laɪv stim / (AmE), / laɪv stiːm / (BrE)
steam at full pressure that comes directly from a boiler, used especially in engines or industrial equipment.
steam at full boiler pressure

Examples

Engineers must carefully manage live steam to avoid dangerous pressure build-ups.
The live steam was released through a safety valve to prevent an explosion.
Hobbyists often build scale locomotive models powered by live steam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steam - water vapor produced by boiling or evaporation
