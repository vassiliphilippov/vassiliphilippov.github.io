to dance the night away

1. VERB

/ dæns ðə naɪt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / dɑːns ðə naɪt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to spend a long period of time dancing, especially during the entire night or until very late
spend the night dancing

Examples

They decided to dance the night away at the wedding reception.
We put on our best clothes and danced the night away at the annual gala.
Couples filled the floor, ready to dance the night away under the stars.

Synset Examples

She danced the night away at a club in Copacabana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - to enjoy oneself at a lively gathering, often dancing
  • Antonym: sleep the night away - to sleep for most or all of the night
