gas gangrene

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈɡæŋˌɡriːn / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈɡæŋɡriːn / (BrE)
A severe and often fatal bacterial infection of tissue, mainly caused by Clostridium bacteria, producing gas and toxins that destroy tissue.
severe bacterial infection with tissue death
medical

Examples

The doctors diagnosed the rapidly spreading infection as gas gangrene, requiring immediate surgery.
If left untreated, gas gangrene can lead to shock and death within hours.
Following the traumatic injury, signs of gas gangrene developed around the wound site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangrene - death of body tissue due to loss of blood supply or bacterial infection
