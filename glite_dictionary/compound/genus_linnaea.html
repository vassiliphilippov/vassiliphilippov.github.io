genus Linnaea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs lɪˈniːə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lɪˈniːə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the honeysuckle family, with the sole species twinflower (Linnaea borealis).
plant genus; includes twinflower

Examples

The genus Linnaea contains only a single species known as the twinflower.
Botanists have long debated the classification of genus Linnaea within the honeysuckle family.
Carl Linnaeus named the genus Linnaea after himself in recognition of its delicate beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twinflower - a small, creeping, evergreen shrub, Linnaea borealis, native to northern forests

Etymology

Named after Carl Linnaeus, Swedish botanist.
