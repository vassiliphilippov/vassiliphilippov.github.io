prairie lotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
A North American annual plant with red or rose-colored flowers, commonly found on prairies.
North American plant with rose-colored flowers

Examples

While hiking across the plain, Maria spotted a cluster of beautiful prairie lotus in bloom.
The prairie lotus thrives in open grassland environments with plenty of sunlight.
Gardeners value the prairie lotus for its vibrant rose-colored flowers that attract pollinators.
