blennioid fish

1. NOUN

/ blɛˈniːɔɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / blɪˈniːɔɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
any of a group of elongated, mostly scaleless marine fishes with large pectoral fins and reduced pelvic fins, typically found in shallow coastal waters.
type of elongated marine fish

Examples

The rocky coastline provides an excellent habitat for various types of blennioid fish.
Researchers observed that the blennioid fish displays unusual territorial behavior.
Many aquarium enthusiasts appreciate the unique appearance of blennioid fish.

Etymology

From New Latin Blennioidea, the taxonomic group name, ultimately from Greek blennos (mucus, slime).
