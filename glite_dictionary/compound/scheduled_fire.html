scheduled fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛdʒuəld ˈfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈʃedjuːld ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
artillery or gunfire planned to occur at a preset time as part of a military operation
prearranged military fire at set time

Examples

The commander ordered scheduled fire to begin at 0600 hours sharp.
Due to poor visibility, the unit relied on scheduled fire rather than on-call support.
Careful coordination is required to avoid friendly casualties during scheduled fire missions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planned fire - fire arranged in advance during military operations
  • Antonym: on-call fire - fire delivered when requested, not at a set time
