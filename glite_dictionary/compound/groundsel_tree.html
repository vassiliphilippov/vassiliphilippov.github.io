groundsel tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd.səl ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd.səl ˌtriː / (BrE)
A shrub native to salt marshes of eastern and south-central North America and the West Indies, with fruit surrounded by white, plumelike hairy tufts.
North American salt marsh shrub

Examples

The groundsel tree thrives in coastal salt marshes, where it helps prevent erosion.
In the autumn, the groundsel tree is easily recognized by its fluffy white seeds.
Local wildlife often uses the groundsel tree for shelter and nesting material.
