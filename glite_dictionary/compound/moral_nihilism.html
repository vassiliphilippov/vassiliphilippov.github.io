moral nihilism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl ˈnaɪɪlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl ˈnaɪɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
The meta-ethical theory that nothing is intrinsically moral or immoral, and that moral values have no objective foundation.
denial of objective moral values

Examples

Some philosophers argue that moral nihilism challenges the very basis of ethical systems.
His advocacy of moral nihilism proved controversial in academic circles.
The novel explores themes of moral nihilism, where characters reject traditional values.

Synset Examples

The moral nihilism of celebrity culture is played out on reality television shows, most of which encourage a dark voyeurism into other people's humiliation, pain, weakness, and betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moral realism - the view that moral facts exist objectively
  • Degree Lower: moral relativism - the belief that moral judgments are true or false only relative to some standpoint

Etymology

From Latin moralis (moral) + nihil (nothing), with nihilism meaning denial of meaningful aspects of life.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl ˈnaɪ.əˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒr.əl ˈnaɪ.ɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical doctrine that denies the existence of inherent moral values or objective moral truths
the belief that moral values are unfounded

Examples

Many philosophers debate whether moral nihilism is a sustainable position in ethics.
Critics of moral nihilism argue that it erodes the foundations of law and society.
Her essay explored the consequences of adopting moral nihilism in modern culture.

Synset Examples

Moral nihilism can undermine societal values.
His belief in moral nihilism shocked his friends.
She argued that moral nihilism leads to chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moral relativism - the idea that moral judgments are true or false only relative to a particular framework
  • Antonym: moral absolutism - the belief that there are objective universal moral standards

Etymology

From Latin 'moralis' (moral) and 'nihil' (nothing), combined with '-ism'.
