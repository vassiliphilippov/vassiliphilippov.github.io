amabilis fir

1. NOUN

/ əˈmæbɪlɪs fɜr / (AmE), / əˈmæbɪlɪs fəː / (BrE)
a medium to tall fir tree native to western North America, with a conical crown, horizontal branches, and leaves that emit an orange scent when crushed
fir tree of western North America

Examples

The amabilis fir is often used as a Christmas tree in regions where it is native.
When you crush the needles of the amabilis fir, you can smell a hint of orange.
The forest was dominated by towering amabilis fir and other evergreen species.

Etymology

From Latin 'amabilis' meaning 'lovely' or 'pleasing', used in the scientific name Abies amabilis.
