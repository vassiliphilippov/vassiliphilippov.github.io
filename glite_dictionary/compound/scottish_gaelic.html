Scottish Gaelic

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːtɪʃ ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈskɑːtɪʃ ˈɡælɪk / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈɡælɪk / (BrE)
the Celtic language of Scotland, distinct from Irish Gaelic and spoken especially in the Highlands and islands.
the Gaelic language spoken in Scotland

Examples

She is learning Scottish Gaelic to connect with her heritage.
Several road signs in the Highlands are written in both English and Scottish Gaelic.
The traditional music of Scotland often features lyrics in Scottish Gaelic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Irish Gaelic - the Gaelic language spoken in Ireland

Etymology

From Scottish + Gaelic, referring to the distinct Gaelic language of Scotland.
