pyloric valve

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈlɔːrɪk vælv / (AmE), / paɪˈlɒrɪk vælv / (BrE)
the ring of muscle at the lower end of the stomach that controls the passage of food into the duodenum
muscle sphincter between stomach and duodenum
medical

Examples

The pyloric valve prevents food from passing from the stomach to the intestines too quickly.
During surgery, the doctor examined the pyloric valve for signs of blockage or abnormality.
Disorders of the pyloric valve can lead to problems with digestion in infants and adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pylorus - the region of the stomach that connects to the duodenum

Etymology

From Greek 'pylōrikos' (of a gatekeeper), from 'pylōros' (gatekeeper).
