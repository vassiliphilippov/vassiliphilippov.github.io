Minamata Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˌminəˈmɑːtə beɪ / (AmE), / ˌmiːnəˈmɑːtə beɪ / (BrE)
a bay on the west coast of Kyushu, Japan, known for severe mercury poisoning among residents due to industrial pollution in the 1950s
bay in Kyushu, Japan, site of mercury poisoning incident

Examples

The tragic mercury poisoning in Minamata Bay drew international attention to industrial pollution.
Many families in the area suffered serious health effects from eating fish caught in Minamata Bay.
Environmental reforms in Japan were partially inspired by the disaster in Minamata Bay.

Etymology

From the Japanese place name Minamata (水俣), combining 'water' (水) and 'sultry place' (俣).
