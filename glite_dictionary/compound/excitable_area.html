excitable area

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsaɪ.tə.bəl ˈɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪ.tə.bəl ˈeə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a region of the brain's cortex that influences motor movements by being able to respond to stimulation
region of cortex affecting motor function

Examples

In neurological studies, the excitable area was observed to react immediately to electrical stimulation.
Damage to an excitable area of the brain can lead to loss of muscle control.
Researchers mapped the excitable area responsible for coordinating fine motor movements in the hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motor cortex - area of the cerebral cortex involved in planning, control, and execution of voluntary movements
