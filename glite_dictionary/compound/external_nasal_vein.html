external nasal vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəl ˈneɪzəl veɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈneɪzəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the external nose, emptying into the angular or facial vein
vein draining blood from the external nose
medical

Examples

The external nasal vein collects blood from the surface of the nose and carries it toward the facial vein.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the external nasal vein.
Swelling of the external nasal vein can occasionally occur after nasal injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial vein - major vein of the face into which the external nasal vein may drain
