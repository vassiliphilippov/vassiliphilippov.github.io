family Ploceidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ploʊˈsiːɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pləʊˈsiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds known as weaverbirds, found mainly in Africa and noted for their elaborate woven nests.
bird family: weaverbirds

Examples

The family Ploceidae includes numerous species of weaverbirds noted for their intricate nest-building abilities.
Birdwatchers in Africa often hope to spot members of the family Ploceidae near wetlands and grasslands.
The bright plumage and complex nests distinguish the family Ploceidae from other small bird families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaverbird - any bird of the family Ploceidae, known for weaving elaborate nests

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Ploceidae', from the genus name 'Ploceus', ultimately from Greek 'plokeus' (weaver).
