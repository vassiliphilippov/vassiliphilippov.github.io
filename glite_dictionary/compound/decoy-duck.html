decoy-duck

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːˌkɔɪ dʌk / (AmE), / ˈdiːkɔɪ dʌk / (BrE)
An artificial duck, often made of wood or plastic, used by hunters to attract real ducks.
fake duck used to attract real ducks

Examples

The hunter carefully placed a decoy-duck on the lake early in the morning.
She bought a set of decoy-ducks to improve her chances during duck season.
A wooden decoy-duck can sometimes fool even the wariest birds.

Synset Examples

He set up a decoy-duck in the pond for the hunt.
A decoy-duck can make all the difference in a successful hunt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoy - an object or person used to lure someone or something
