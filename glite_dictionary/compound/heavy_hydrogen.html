heavy hydrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈhaɪdrədʒən / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈhaɪdrədʒən / (BrE)
an isotope of hydrogen, also called deuterium, which contains one neutron in its nucleus instead of none.
isotope of hydrogen with one neutron (deuterium)

Examples

Heavy hydrogen is used in nuclear reactors as a moderator because of its unique properties.
The scientist explained the difference between ordinary hydrogen and heavy hydrogen to the class.
Water containing heavy hydrogen is known as heavy water and is important in some scientific applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deuterium - isotope of hydrogen with one neutron
