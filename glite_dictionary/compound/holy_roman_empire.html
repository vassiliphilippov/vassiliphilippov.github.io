Holy Roman Empire

1. PROPN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈroʊmən ˈɛmpaɪər / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈrəʊmən ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
a historical empire in central Europe, established in 962 and lasting until 1806, comprising numerous territories under an emperor, notable for its political and religious significance during the Middle Ages and early modern period
historical European empire (962–1806)

Examples

The Holy Roman Empire played a major role in shaping European history for nearly a thousand years.
Napoleon's conquest led to the dissolution of the Holy Roman Empire in 1806.
Historians often debate the political structure and influence of the Holy Roman Empire.
