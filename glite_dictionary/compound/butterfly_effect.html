butterfly effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪ ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəflaɪ ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
the idea that a small change or event in one system can cause significant and unpredictable consequences elsewhere, especially in complex systems
small action leading to large, unpredictable effects

Examples

The butterfly effect suggests that one small decision can ultimately lead to major outcomes half a world away.
Many scientists reference the butterfly effect when discussing the unpredictable nature of the weather.
Her research examines how the butterfly effect operates within complex ecological systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripple effect - a spreading, gradually increasing effect or influence resulting from a single action

Etymology

Coined in the 1970s from the suggestion that a butterfly flapping its wings could influence weather patterns, illustrating concepts in chaos theory.
