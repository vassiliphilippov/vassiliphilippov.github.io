windowpane oyster

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndoʊˌpeɪn ˈɔɪstər / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊˌpeɪn ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
a marine bivalve mollusk found chiefly in the coastal waters of the Philippines, known for its large, thin, translucent shell
marine bivalve with translucent shell

Examples

The shells of the windowpane oyster are often used to make decorative lanterns and windows.
Fishermen in coastal villages harvest the windowpane oyster for both food and its valuable shells.
The windowpane oyster is prized for its thin, translucent shell known as capiz.
