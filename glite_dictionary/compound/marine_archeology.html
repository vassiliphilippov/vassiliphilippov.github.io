marine archeology

1. NOUN

/ məˈrin ɑrkiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / məˈriːn ˌɑːkɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of human history and prehistory through the investigation of underwater sites, artifacts, and remains
study of underwater archeological sites

Examples

Many fascinating discoveries have been made through marine archeology in the Mediterranean Sea.
She decided to pursue a career in marine archeology after studying shipwrecks.
Marine archeology requires special diving skills and equipment for underwater research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archeology - the study of human history through material remains

Etymology

From Latin 'marinus' (of the sea) and Greek 'arkhaiologia' (study of ancient things).
