prodeath policy

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈdɛθ ˈpɑlɪsi / (AmE), / prəʊˈdɛθ ˈpɒlɪsi / (BrE)
A policy that supports or advocates the use of the death penalty.
policy supporting the death penalty

Examples

The new administration has proposed a prodeath policy that is dividing the country.
Activists protested fiercely against the government's prodeath policy during the rally.
Legislators debated whether a prodeath policy truly serves as a crime deterrent.

Synset Examples

The prodeath policies sparked a heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antideath penalty policy - a policy opposing the use of the death penalty

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'pro-' meaning 'in favor of', 'death', and 'policy'.
