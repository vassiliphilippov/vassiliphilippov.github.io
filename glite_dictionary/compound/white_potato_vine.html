white potato vine

1. NOUN

/ waɪt pəˈteɪtoʊ vaɪn / (AmE), / waɪt pəˈteɪtəʊ vaɪn / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to South America, widely cultivated for its edible starchy tubers, with underground stems (stolons) and poisonous vines
plant with edible tubers and poisonous vines

Examples

The white potato vine produces the common potatoes found in grocery stores.
Many gardeners grow white potato vine for its nutritious tubers, but take care because the vines are toxic.
The white potato vine thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato - the starchy tuber of Solanum tuberosum
