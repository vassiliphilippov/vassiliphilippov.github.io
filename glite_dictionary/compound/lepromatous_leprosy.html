lepromatous leprosy

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛprəˈmeɪtəs ˈlɛprəsi / (AmE), / ˌlɛprəˈmeɪtəs ˈlɛprəsi / (BrE)
a severe form of leprosy characterized by widespread skin lesions and involvement of multiple body systems
serious form of leprosy with widespread lesions
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with lepromatous leprosy after doctors observed diffuse skin lesions and nerve involvement.
Unlike tuberculoid leprosy, lepromatous leprosy can cause disfigurement and affect internal organs.
Treatment for lepromatous leprosy requires a combination of antibiotics over an extended period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tuberculoid leprosy - a milder form of leprosy with limited skin lesions

Etymology

From Greek 'lepros' (scaly) and Latin 'leprosus' (affected by leprosy), 'lepromatous' meaning relating to nodular leprosy.
