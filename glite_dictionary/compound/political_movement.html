political movement

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a group of people working together to promote or achieve a specific political goal or cause
group seeking political change

Examples

The civil rights political movement transformed American society in the 1960s.
Many young people are joining the environmental political movement to push for policy reforms.
A successful political movement often requires strong leadership and widespread public support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social movement - organized effort by a group to promote or resist social change
