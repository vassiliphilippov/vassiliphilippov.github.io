life assurance

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf əˌʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf əˌʃɔːrəns / (BrE)
An insurance policy that pays a sum of money to designated beneficiaries when the insured person dies.
insurance paying on death of insured

Examples

Many people choose life assurance to provide financial security for their families.
The terms of life assurance policies can differ from those of regular life insurance.
In the UK, life assurance is often used as a form of long-term financial planning.

Synset Examples

in England they call life insurance ‘life assurance’
