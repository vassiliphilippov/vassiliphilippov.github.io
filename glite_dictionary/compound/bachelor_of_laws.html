Bachelor of Laws

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr əv lɔz / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələ əv lɔːz / (BrE)
a degree in law, typically awarded after completing undergraduate studies in law.
undergraduate law degree

Examples

She received her Bachelor of Laws from a prestigious university in London.
After completing his Bachelor of Laws, he pursued a master's degree in international law.
Many law firms require a Bachelor of Laws as the minimum qualification for entry-level positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LL.B. - abbreviation for Bachelor of Laws

Etymology

From Latin baccalaureus legum, with 'legum' being the genitive plural of 'lex', meaning 'law'.
