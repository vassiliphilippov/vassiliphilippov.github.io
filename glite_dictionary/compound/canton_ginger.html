Canton ginger

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tɑn ˈdʒɪn.dʒər / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tən ˈdʒɪn.dʒə / (BrE)
A tropical Asian plant widely cultivated for its pungent root, used as a source of gingerroot and powdered ginger.
tropical Asian plant, source of gingerroot

Examples

The chef added freshly grated Canton ginger to the sauce for a zesty kick.
Traditional herbalists often use Canton ginger in natural remedies for digestion.
At the market, I bought a piece of Canton ginger to use in my stir-fry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - a spice made from the pungent root of the ginger plant

Etymology

Canton refers to the historical Anglicized name for Guangzhou, China, an early exporter of ginger.
