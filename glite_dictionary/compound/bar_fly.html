bar fly

1. NOUN

/ bɑɹ flaɪ / (AmE), / bɑː flaɪ / (BrE)
a person who spends a lot of time drinking at bars, often regarded as a habitual or frequent patron
habitual bar-goer or drinker
slang

Examples

He became known as a bar fly after showing up at the local tavern almost every night.
The film featured a few colorful bar flies exchanging stories at the counter.
She didn't want people to think she was just another bar fly hanging around for free drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regular - a person who frequently visits a particular place, especially a bar or restaurant
