Scots Gaelic

1. NOUN

/ skɑts ˈɡeɪlɪk / (AmE), / skɒts ˈɡaːlɪk / (BrE)
the variety of the Gaelic language spoken in Scotland, distinct from Irish or Manx Gaelic
the Gaelic language of Scotland

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Scots Gaelic.
Efforts are being made to preserve and promote Scots Gaelic in schools.
Many place names in the Highlands are derived from Scots Gaelic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish Gaelic - the Gaelic language spoken in Ireland
  • Synonym: Manx - the Gaelic language spoken on the Isle of Man

Etymology

From Scots, referring to Scotland, and Gaelic, from Irish and Scottish Gaelic Gàidhlig.
