serpent-worship

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːpənt ˌwɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɜːpənt ˌwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
the religious practice of venerating or worshipping snakes, often as deities or sacred beings
the worship of snakes

Examples

Ancient cultures in India and Africa practiced serpent-worship as part of their religious beliefs.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts related to serpent-worship at the excavation site.
The mythologies of several civilizations feature gods associated with serpent-worship.
