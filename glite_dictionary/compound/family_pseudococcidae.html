family Pseudococcidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌsjuːdoʊˈkɒksaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌsjuːdəʊˈkɒksaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, soft-bodied insects known as mealybugs, many of which are pests on plants.
a family of insects; mealybugs

Examples

The family Pseudococcidae includes a wide variety of mealybug species that feed on plant sap.
Scientists studied specimens from the family Pseudococcidae to understand their impact on crops.
Most pests in the family Pseudococcidae can be controlled using natural predators or insecticidal soap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealybug - Common name for insects in the family Pseudococcidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Pseudococcidae', combining 'pseudo-' (false) and 'coccus' (berry; used for scale insects).
