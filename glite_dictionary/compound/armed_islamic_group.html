Armed Islamic Group

1. PROPN

/ ɑːrmd ɪˈslɑːmɪk ɡruːp / (AmE), / ɑːmd ɪˈslæmɪk ɡruːp / (BrE)
a militant organization of Islamic extremists founded in Algeria in 1992, known for violent activities aimed at replacing the secular government with an Islamic state
Algerian militant Islamic organization (est. 1992)

Examples

The Armed Islamic Group was widely reported to be responsible for several bombings in the 1990s.
Membership in the Armed Islamic Group was outlawed by the Algerian government.
International agencies have condemned the violent acts carried out by the Armed Islamic Group.
