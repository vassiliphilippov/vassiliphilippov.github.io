India-rubber fig

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndiə ˈrʌbər fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˌɪndiə ˈrʌbə fɪɡ / (BrE)
A large tropical Asian tree (Ficus elastica) often grown as a houseplant, and formerly a source of natural rubber (Assam rubber).
tropical fig tree; common houseplant

Examples

The India-rubber fig makes a striking addition to any living room with its glossy leaves.
In the nineteenth century, the India-rubber fig was widely harvested for its latex.
Even when grown indoors, the India-rubber fig can reach an impressive height if properly cared for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber plant - another name for India-rubber fig (Ficus elastica)

Etymology

Refers to India, region of origin, and rubber, as it was once a rubber source.
