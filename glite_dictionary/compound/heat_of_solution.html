heat of solution

1. NOUN

/ hit əv səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the amount of heat evolved or absorbed when one mole of a substance is dissolved in a large volume of solvent
heat evolved or absorbed when a substance dissolves

Examples

The heat of solution for sodium chloride is slightly endothermic.
When ammonium nitrate dissolves in water, its heat of solution is strongly negative, causing the mixture to cool.
Chemists often measure the heat of solution to understand solubility and reaction energetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat of reaction - the heat evolved or absorbed during a chemical reaction

Etymology

Formed from "heat" and "solution" based on scientific usage in thermodynamics.
