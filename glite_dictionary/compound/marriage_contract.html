marriage contract

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærɪdʒ ˈkɑntrækt / (AmE), / ˈmærɪdʒ ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
a legal agreement made by two people before they marry, outlining the terms and conditions of their marriage, especially regarding property and financial matters
legal agreement before marriage
legal

Examples

They signed a marriage contract to clarify how their assets would be divided in case of divorce.
The lawyer explained every clause of the marriage contract before the couple agreed to it.
In some cultures, a marriage contract is considered as important as the wedding ceremony itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prenuptial agreement - a contract made between two people before they marry, concerning the ownership of their respective assets
