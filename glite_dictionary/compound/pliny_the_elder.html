Pliny the Elder

1. PROPN

/ ˈplɪni ði ˈɛldər / (AmE), / ˈplɪni ði ˈɛldə / (BrE)
(23–79 AD) A Roman author and naturalist, best known for his encyclopedic work 'Natural History'; he died while observing the eruption of Mount Vesuvius.
Roman author and naturalist (23–79 AD)

Examples

Pliny the Elder wrote extensively on topics ranging from natural history to mineralogy.
The death of Pliny the Elder during the eruption of Vesuvius is well-documented by his nephew.
Historians often reference Pliny the Elder for insights into life in Ancient Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pliny the Younger - Roman lawyer and author, nephew of Pliny the Elder
