credit point

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdɪt pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure academic work completed by a student, typically awarded for passing a course, seminar, or module
unit of academic workload or credit

Examples

Each course is worth three credit points toward your degree.
He needs to earn 120 credit points to graduate from the program.
European universities often use the ECTS credit point system to measure student workload.

Synset Examples

One academic year corresponds to 60 ECTS credits that are equivalent to 1500-1800 hours of study in all countries respective of standard or qualification type and is used to facilitate transfer and progression throughout the Union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit - recognition for meeting academic requirements
