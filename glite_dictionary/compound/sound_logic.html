sound logic

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd ˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
reasoning or arguments that are valid, well-founded, and based on solid principles
valid or well-founded reasoning

Examples

His proposal was accepted because it was supported by sound logic and evidence.
Without sound logic, even the most passionate speech is unconvincing.
The professor emphasized the importance of using sound logic in constructing philosophical arguments.

Synset Examples

The argument lacked any sound logic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fallacy - mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound argument
