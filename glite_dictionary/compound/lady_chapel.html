lady chapel

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdi ˌtʃæpəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi ˌtʃæpəl / (BrE)
a small chapel within a church, dedicated to the Virgin Mary
chapel in a church for the Virgin Mary

Examples

The cathedral's lady chapel features stunning stained glass windows dedicated to the Virgin Mary.
During morning prayers, the congregation gathered quietly in the lady chapel.
A wedding was held in the historic lady chapel at the back of the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chapel - a small place of worship

Etymology

From 'Lady' (referring to the Virgin Mary) and 'chapel'; 'Lady' in this context is a reverential reference to Mary, mother of Jesus.
