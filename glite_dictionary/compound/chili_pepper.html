chili pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A small, usually red or green, sharply spicy fruit used to add heat and flavor to food.
hot, spicy fruit used as seasoning

Examples

The chef uses fresh chili pepper to give the soup a fiery kick.
She accidentally touched her eyes after slicing a chili pepper.
Adding a chili pepper to the salsa made it much spicier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot pepper - another term for spicy peppers like chili pepper
  • Antonym: bell pepper - a mild, sweet type of pepper, not spicy

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chīlli* (chili), via Spanish *chile*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A small, hot-tasting red or green pod of a variety of capsicum, used in sauces, relishes, or as a spice; the plant that bears these pods.
very hot pepper plant or its fruit

Examples

He added a fresh chili pepper to the curry for extra heat.
You can grow chili peppers in your backyard if you provide enough sunlight.
The market offers both red and green chili peppers all year round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsicum - the genus of flowering plants including chili and bell peppers
  • Antonym: bell pepper - a mild, sweet variety of capsicum with thick flesh, not hot

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chīlli* (chili) and English 'pepper.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli ˌpepər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli ˌpepə / (BrE)
A small, hot-tasting pod of a variety of capsicum, used in cooking to add a spicy flavor.
hot, spicy pepper used in cooking

Examples

He chopped a chili pepper into the salsa to make it extra spicy.
I can't eat food with too much chili pepper because it burns my mouth.
The bright red chili pepper stood out among the green vegetables.

Synset Examples

The chili pepper was a great addition to the dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsicum - Genus of plants that includes chili pepper
  • Antonym: bell pepper - A mild, sweet variety of capsicum, not hot

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chīlli* (pepper) via Spanish *chile*; 'pepper' added in English for European reference.
