face lift

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪs lɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs lɪft / (BrE)
A cosmetic surgical procedure in which facial skin is tightened and excess tissue is removed to reduce signs of aging.
cosmetic surgery to tighten facial skin

Examples

She considered getting a face lift to look younger for her high school reunion.
After her face lift, she felt more confident about her appearance.
Some celebrities are open about having had a face lift to maintain their looks.

Synset Examples

some actresses have more than one face lift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhytidectomy - the medical term for surgical removal of wrinkles (face lift)

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪs ˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs ˌlɪft / (BrE)
A renovation or cosmetic improvement to the outward appearance of something, especially a building, without major structural changes.
cosmetic renovation or improvement

Examples

The old library got a much-needed face lift last year, with a fresh coat of paint and new windows.
After ten years in business, the café underwent a complete face lift to attract more customers.
The city has given the downtown area a face lift to boost tourism.

Synset Examples

give your home a facelift
more than a facelift, the new model marks a fundamental change of direction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovation - the process of improving a building or structure
  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its former condition

Etymology

From the surgical term 'face-lift', meaning a cosmetic procedure for the face; extended figuratively to buildings and objects.
