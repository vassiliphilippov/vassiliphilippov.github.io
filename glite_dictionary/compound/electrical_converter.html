electrical converter

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl kənˈvɝːtər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl kənˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
A device that changes alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) or vice versa.
device for changing AC to DC or vice versa

Examples

The electrical converter allowed her to use her laptop while traveling in a country with different voltage standards.
You need an electrical converter if your appliance is designed for a different type of current.
Many modern electrical converters can both step up and step down voltage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectifier - device that converts AC to DC
  • Synonym: inverter - device that converts DC to AC
