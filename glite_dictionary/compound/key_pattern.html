key pattern

1. NOUN

/ ki ˈpætɚn / (AmE), / ki ˈpætn̩ / (BrE)
an ornamental design featuring a repeated arrangement of vertical and horizontal lines, often used in architectural decoration or relief work.
ornamental design of repeated lines

Examples

The frieze was decorated with a classic key pattern that gave the room a Greek feel.
You can find a key pattern along the edges of ancient vases and architecture.
A silver key pattern framed the front door, adding an elegant geometric touch.

Synset Examples

there was a simple fret at the top of the walls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fret - an ornamental design of repeated geometric patterns, usually in relief
  • Synonym: meander - a decorative border constructed from a continuous line, shaped into a repeated motif
