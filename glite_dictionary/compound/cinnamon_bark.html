cinnamon bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmən bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmən bɑːk / (BrE)
the aromatic inner bark of certain trees, especially of the genus Cinnamomum, used as a spice in cooking and baking
aromatic bark used as a spice

Examples

The recipe calls for two sticks of cinnamon bark and several cloves.
She added a piece of cinnamon bark to the simmering pot of mulled wine.
Ground cinnamon bark is commonly used in both sweet and savory dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - the spice made from dried aromatic bark, often ground

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmən bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmən bɑːk / (BrE)
the aromatic outer layer of certain cinnamon trees, especially Saigon cinnamon, used as a spice and sometimes medicinally
aromatic spice from cinnamon trees

Examples

Traditional Chinese medicine sometimes includes cinnamon bark as a warming ingredient in herbal remedies.
A stick of cinnamon bark adds a warm, sweet flavor to mulled wine.
Some herbal teas feature cinnamon bark for its aromatic and carminative properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - a spice made from the dried inner bark of certain trees of the genus Cinnamomum
