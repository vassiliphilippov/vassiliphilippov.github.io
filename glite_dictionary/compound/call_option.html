call option

1. NOUN

/ kɑl ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
a financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to purchase an asset at a specified price within a specified period.
right to buy an asset at a set price

Examples

The investor purchased a call option on shares of the tech company.
A call option gives the buyer the right to buy stock at a specific price.
Traders often use a call option to speculate on rising market prices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: put option - an option to sell an asset at a set price

Etymology

From 'call', meaning to demand or request, and 'option', from Latin 'optio' (choice). Used in financial contexts since the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ kɔl ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
A financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to purchase a specific asset at a predetermined price within a specified time period.
financial contract granting buying rights

Examples

She decided to invest in a call option to benefit from a potential rise in the company's share price.
A call option gives the investor the right to purchase stock at a fixed price.
The trader profited by exercising his call option just before the market rallied.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: put option - a financial contract that gives the holder the right to sell an asset at a set price
