pleurisy root

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʊrɪzi ruːt / (AmE), / ˈplʊərɪzi ruːt / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant (Asclepias tuberosa) with showy orange flowers, formerly used in traditional medicine.
a North American perennial plant with orange flowers

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used pleurisy root as a medicinal herb.
The garden was brightened by clusters of pleurisy root in full bloom.
Many butterflies are attracted to pleurisy root due to its vibrant orange flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly weed - another common name for Asclepias tuberosa, known for attracting butterflies

Etymology

Named for its historical use in treating pleurisy; 'root' refers to the plant's tuberous root.
