American barberry

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən bɑːrˌbɛri / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbɑːbəri / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its leaves that turn scarlet in autumn, yellow racemes of flowers, and shiny red ellipsoid berries.
deciduous shrub with scarlet autumn leaves and red berries

Examples

The bright red berries of the American barberry attract many birds in autumn.
Gardeners often choose American barberry for its colorful fall foliage.
The American barberry thrives in well-drained soils across eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese barberry - a related species of barberry native to Japan
