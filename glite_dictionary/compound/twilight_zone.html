twilight zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪ.laɪt zəʊn / (BrE)
A situation or area that is ambiguous, undefined, or intermediate between two distinct states, categories, or conditions.
ambiguous or indeterminate area

Examples

The new law falls into a twilight zone between established regulations and recent technological changes.
Her emotions were in a twilight zone, neither happiness nor sadness, just a confusing in-between.
Science fiction often explores the twilight zone between reality and imagination.

Synset Examples

but there is still a twilight zone, the tantalizing occurrences that are probably noise but might possibly be a signal
in the twilight zone between humor and vulgarity
in that no man's land between negotiation and aggression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray area - an ill-defined situation or concept that is not clearly one thing or another
  • Synonym: no man's land - an area of uncertainty, especially one not clearly belonging to any category

Etymology

Originally from the scientific term for the dimly lit region between the ocean's surface and deep sea; popularized in the figurative sense by the American television show 'The Twilight Zone' (1959–64).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪ.laɪt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪ.laɪt zəʊn / (BrE)
the layer of the ocean between the surface and the deep ocean, at depths where only faint light reaches
ocean layer with faint light

Examples

Many deep-sea creatures in the twilight zone have adapted to the low levels of sunlight.
Some fish migrate daily from the twilight zone to feed near the surface at night.
Scientists are studying the twilight zone to understand how it affects the global carbon cycle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sunlit zone - uppermost oceanic layer with full sunlight penetration
  • Degree Lower: midnight zone - ocean layer where no sunlight penetrates

Etymology

From 'twilight' (faint light after sunset) + 'zone'; metaphorically describing the dimly lit ocean layer.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt zəʊn / (BrE)
a situation or place that is strange, mysterious, or difficult to understand, often one that feels surreal or confusing
surreal or confusing situation

Examples

After the announcement, the office entered a twilight zone of rumors and uncertainty.
The city at dawn felt like a twilight zone, empty and strangely silent.
When the rules changed without warning, we found ourselves in a twilight zone of confusion.

Synset Examples

This situation feels like we're in the twilight zone.
Their conversation took a turn into the twilight zone.
He joked about entering the twilight zone with his weird dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limbo - an intermediate or uncertain state
  • Antonym: normality - a state of being normal or usual

Etymology

Popularized by the American television series 'The Twilight Zone' (1959–1964), where it referred to a mysterious or unsettling experience.
