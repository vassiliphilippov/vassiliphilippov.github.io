hunger march

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡər mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a protest march by unemployed people, typically demanding work or relief from poverty
protest march by the unemployed

Examples

During the Great Depression, thousands joined the hunger march to demand jobs and food.
News reports covered a hunger march that ended at the steps of Parliament.
The hunger march was organized by activists seeking attention to mass unemployment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest march - a group of people walking together to publicly demonstrate strong objection to something
