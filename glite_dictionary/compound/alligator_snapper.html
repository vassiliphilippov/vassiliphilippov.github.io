alligator snapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪˌɡeɪtər ˈsnæpər / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtə ˈsnæpə / (BrE)
a large species of snapping turtle with three ridges on its shell, native to the southeastern United States
large snapping turtle of southeastern US

Examples

The alligator snapper is one of the largest freshwater turtles in North America.
Wildlife biologists tagged several alligator snappers for a conservation study.
Be careful when handling an alligator snapper, as it has a powerful bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapping turtle - large freshwater turtle known for its strong jaw and aggressive nature
