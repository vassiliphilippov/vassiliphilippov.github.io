sounding line

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
a weighted line used in nautical contexts to measure the depth of water
weighted line to measure water depth

Examples

The sailor dropped the sounding line overboard to check the depth near the shore.
Before sonar was invented, every ship carried a sounding line for safe navigation.
He marked knots on the sounding line to record different depths accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumb line - line with a weight, used for vertical reference or depth measurement

Etymology

From 'sounding' (nautical term for measuring depth) + 'line', referring to the cord or rope used.
