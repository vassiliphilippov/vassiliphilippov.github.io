kangaroo jerboa

1. NOUN

/ kæŋˈɡuːɹoʊ dʒərˈboʊ.ə / (AmE), / kæŋˈɡuːrəʊ dʒɜːˈbəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A small Australian marsupial of the genus Bettongia, also known as the brush-tailed rat kangaroo, with a long tail and hopping gait.
small Australian marsupial (Bettongia)

Examples

The kangaroo jerboa is known for its agile jumps and nocturnal habits.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats critical for the kangaroo jerboa.
Unlike true jerboas, the kangaroo jerboa is actually a marsupial native to Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bettong - any of several small brown jumping marsupials of Australia in the genus Bettongia or related genera

Etymology

From 'kangaroo', the name for various Australian marsupials, and 'jerboa', a type of jumping rodent, referring to its appearance and movement.
