acute brain disorder

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːt breɪn dɪsˌɔrdər / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt breɪn dɪsˌɔːdə / (BrE)
a sudden and reversible disturbance of brain function, often causing confusion or disorientation, typically resulting from injury, drugs, or infection
sudden, reversible brain disturbance
medical

Examples

The sudden onset of confusion led doctors to suspect an acute brain disorder.
Many acute brain disorders can improve with prompt treatment of the underlying cause.
Delirium is a common type of acute brain disorder in hospitalized elderly patients.

Etymology

From 'acute' (from Latin acutus 'sharp') + 'brain disorder'.
