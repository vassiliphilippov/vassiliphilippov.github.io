A battery

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ ˌbætəri / (AmE), / ˈeɪ ˌbatəri / (BrE)
a battery used to supply electric current for heating the filaments of a vacuum tube
battery for vacuum tube filaments

Examples

In early radios, an A battery was necessary to heat the tube filaments.
The technician replaced the A battery to restore power to the vacuum tube heater.
Collectors often seek original A battery packs for vintage radio equipment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: B battery - battery providing plate voltage in a vacuum tube
  • Degree Higher: C battery - battery providing grid bias in a vacuum tube
