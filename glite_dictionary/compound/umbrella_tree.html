umbrella tree

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbrɛl.ə tri / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛl.ə triː / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia and northern New Guinea, with palmately compound leaves, often grown as an ornamental plant
Australian small tree with compound leaves

Examples

The umbrella tree in their garden grew rapidly and provided deep shade each summer.
Many homes feature an umbrella tree indoors because it is easy to care for and attractive.
Native to Australia, the umbrella tree adapts well to a variety of climate conditions.

2. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbɹɛl.ə ˌtriː / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɹɛl.ə ˌtriː / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of eastern North America, known for its creamy white flowers and large leaves arranged like umbrellas at the ends of its branches.
deciduous North American tree with umbrella-shaped leaves

Examples

The umbrella tree provides excellent shade in the summer with its broad leaves.
In spring, the umbrella tree is covered with clusters of white flowers.
We planted an umbrella tree near the patio for its beauty and cooling effect.
