sovok vibe

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈvɒk vaɪb / (AmE), / səʊˈvɒk vaɪb / (BrE)
A retro, outdated, or utilitarian atmosphere or style reminiscent of the Soviet era, often characterized by austerity and functional design.
atmosphere reminiscent of the Soviet era

Examples

The cafe’s decor had such a sovok vibe that it felt like stepping into a time capsule from the 1980s.
She joked that the office’s sovok vibe made every meeting feel like a scene from an old Soviet film.
Despite the modern renovations, there was still a sovok vibe in the building’s harsh lighting and utilitarian furniture.

Synset Examples

The sovok vibe of the building felt stuck in time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soviet-style - characteristic of the Soviet Union's aesthetics or approaches

Etymology

From Russian 'sovok' (slang for Soviet person or Soviet-style), combined with English 'vibe' (atmosphere).
