cucumber tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːkʌmbɚ triː / (AmE), / ˈkjuːkʌmbə triː / (BrE)
an American deciduous magnolia (Magnolia acuminata) with large leaves and greenish-yellow flowers, bearing fruit shaped like a small cucumber
American magnolia tree with cucumber-like fruit

Examples

The cucumber tree is cultivated for its attractive foliage and unusual fruit.
In the wild, the cucumber tree can grow up to thirty meters tall.
Gardeners planted a cucumber tree at the edge of the park for shade and ornamental value.

Etymology

Named for its fruit, which resembles a small cucumber; 'cucumber' from Old French 'cocombre,' Latin 'cucumis', and 'tree'.
