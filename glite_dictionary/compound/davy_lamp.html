Davy lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.vi læmp / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.vi læmp / (BrE)
a safety oil lamp with a wire gauze shield, designed for use in mines to prevent ignition of flammable gases such as methane
safety lamp for miners

Examples

The miners relied on the Davy lamp to safely illuminate the coal seams.
A Davy lamp was a key invention that reduced accidents in underground mining.
He proudly displayed his grandfather's old Davy lamp in the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety lamp - a lamp designed to prevent ignition of flammable gases

Etymology

Named after Sir Humphry Davy, British chemist and inventor (1778–1829), who invented the lamp in 1815.
