military control

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the control of a country or area by the armed forces of a foreign power
control of a region by foreign military

Examples

The country remained under military control for several years following the invasion.
During the transitional period, all major decisions were made under military control.
The international community condemned the regime's use of military control to suppress dissent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupation - the control and possession of a territory by a foreign military power
