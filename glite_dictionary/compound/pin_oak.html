pin oak

1. NOUN

/ pɪn oʊk / (AmE), / pɪn əʊk / (BrE)
a large deciduous oak tree (Quercus palustris) of eastern and central North America, with deeply lobed leaves and slender branches, often found in wet soils
a North American oak tree species

Examples

The pin oak is popular in landscaping because of its graceful shape and fast growth.
A row of pin oaks lines the street in front of the school, providing beautiful autumn color.
Due to its preference for moist soil, the pin oak thrives in low-lying areas and floodplains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red oak - a type of oak with red-tinged wood, related oak group

Etymology

So called for its slender, pin-like branchlets.

2. NOUN

/ pɪn oʊk / (AmE), / pɪn əʊk / (BrE)
A fast-growing North American oak tree (Quercus palustris) with deeply lobed leaves that turn bright red in autumn, commonly found in damp soil.
deciduous oak tree of North America

Examples

The pin oak in our front yard turns a brilliant red every autumn.
Many city parks plant pin oaks because they grow quickly and provide ample shade.
A pin oak thrives best in low-lying areas with moist soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree in the genus Quercus, known for its hard wood and lobed leaves

Etymology

Named for its slender, pin-like twigs.
