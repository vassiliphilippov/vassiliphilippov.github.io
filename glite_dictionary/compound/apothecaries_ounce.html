apothecaries' ounce

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑθəˌkɛriz aʊns / (AmE), / əˈpɒθəkəriz aʊns / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in the apothecaries' system, equal to 480 grains or one twelfth of a troy pound.
historical apothecary weight unit
archaic medical

Examples

The recipe called for one apothecaries' ounce of powdered rhubarb root.
In the old medical texts, dosages were often listed in apothecaries' ounces and grains.
Pharmacists no longer use the apothecaries' ounce as a standard measure, but it appears in historical documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ounce - a unit of weight equal to one sixteenth of a pound avoirdupois or one twelfth of a troy or apothecaries' pound
  • Degree Higher: apothecaries' pound - unit of weight in the apothecary system, equal to 5760 grains

Etymology

From 'apothecary' (a historical term for pharmacist) and 'ounce', from Latin 'uncia' (twelfth part).
