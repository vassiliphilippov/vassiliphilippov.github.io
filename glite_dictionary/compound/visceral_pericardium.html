visceral pericardium

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsərəl ˌpɛrɪˈkɑrdiəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl ˌpɛrɪˈkɑːdiəm / (BrE)
the inner layer of the pericardium that closely envelops the heart
inner layer of the heart's pericardium
medical

Examples

The visceral pericardium directly adheres to the surface of the heart.
During the dissection, the visceral pericardium was carefully separated from the underlying tissue.
Inflammation of the visceral pericardium can cause pericarditis and chest pain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parietal pericardium - the outer layer of the pericardium

Etymology

From Latin 'visceralis' (of the internal organs) and Greek 'perikardion' (around the heart).
