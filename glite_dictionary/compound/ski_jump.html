ski jump

1. NOUN

/ ski dʒʌmp / (AmE), / ski dʒʌmp / (BrE)
a steep ramp constructed for skiers to glide down and launch themselves into the air during competitions or practice
ramp for ski jumping

Examples

The athletes soared off the ski jump and landed gracefully on the slope below.
The construction of a new ski jump has attracted international competitions to the area.
He trained for months before attempting the largest ski jump on the course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ski jumping - the sport of jumping from a ski jump
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04236682-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ski dʒʌmp / (AmE), / ski dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to leap or perform a jump while skiing, especially as part of the sport of ski jumping.
to jump while skiing

Examples

Before he could compete professionally, he had to learn how to ski jump safely.
Dozens of athletes ski jump every winter at the local resort.
She ski jumped for the first time and found the experience exhilarating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump - to move suddenly and quickly in a specified way; leap through the air
