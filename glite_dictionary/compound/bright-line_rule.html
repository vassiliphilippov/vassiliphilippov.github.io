bright-line rule

1. NOUN

/ braɪt laɪn ruːl / (AmE), / braɪt laɪn ruːl / (BrE)
a clearly defined and unambiguous rule or standard that provides clear guidance in decision-making, especially in legal or professional contexts
clear, unambiguous rule or standard
legal

Examples

The new law introduced a bright-line rule to eliminate ambiguity in tax compliance.
To avoid confusion, management adopted a bright-line rule regarding overtime approval.
Courts often prefer a bright-line rule over subjective standards in contentious cases.

Synset Examples

He established a bright line rule for his employees.
Establishing a bright-line rule helped clarify our responsibilities.
The policy outlines a bright-line rule for reporting expenses.
Following a bright-line rule simplifies decision-making processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rule of thumb - a general guideline based on experience rather than a strict rule

Etymology

Formed from 'bright line' (a clearly visible distinction) and 'rule.' Originated in legal jargon, 20th century.
