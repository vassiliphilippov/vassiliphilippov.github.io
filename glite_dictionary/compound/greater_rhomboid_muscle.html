greater rhomboid muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈrɑːmbɔɪd ˈmʌsl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈrɒmbɔɪd ˈmʌsl / (BrE)
a muscle in the upper back that draws the scapula toward the spine, located beneath the minor rhomboid muscle
muscle in upper back near scapula
medical

Examples

The greater rhomboid muscle helps retract the scapula toward the spine during certain movements.
An injury to the greater rhomboid muscle can cause significant upper back pain and discomfort.
Strengthening the greater rhomboid muscle improves posture and supports healthy shoulder mechanics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhomboid muscle - muscles in the upper back that connect the scapula to the spinal column
  • Degree Lower: lesser rhomboid muscle - the smaller of the rhomboid muscles, located above the greater rhomboid muscle

Etymology

From Latin 'rhomboideus' (rhomboid-shaped) and Greek 'rhomboeides'.
