primary censorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈsɛnsɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpraɪməri ˈsɛnsəʃɪp / (BrE)
censorship carried out by military unit personnel on the personal communications of members within the unit
military censorship of unit members' communications

Examples

During the war, primary censorship ensured soldiers' letters did not disclose sensitive information.
All personal mail was subject to primary censorship by the designated officers in the unit.
His letter home was delayed due to primary censorship procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - suppression or prohibition of speech or writing
