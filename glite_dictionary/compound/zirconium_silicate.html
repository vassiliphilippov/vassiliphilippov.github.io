zirconium silicate

1. NOUN

/ zɚˈkoʊniəm ˈsɪlɪkeɪt / (AmE), / zɜːˈkəʊniəm ˈsɪlɪkeɪt / (BrE)
a common mineral found in small crystals, used mainly as a source of zirconium and as a gemstone when transparent
a mineral, main source of zirconium

Examples

Opaque zirconium silicate is valued for its heat resistance in industrial processes.
Jewelry makers prize transparent zirconium silicate for its brilliance and clarity.
Most commercial zirconium is extracted from mineral sands rich in zirconium silicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zircon - the mineral form of zirconium silicate, used as a gemstone

Etymology

From zirconium, named after the mineral zircon (from Arabic 'zarqūn' meaning 'cinnabar' or 'vermilion'), and silicate, from Latin 'silica' (flint).
