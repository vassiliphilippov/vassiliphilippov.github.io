soot black

1. NOUN

/ sut blæk / (AmE), / sʊt blæk / (BrE)
a very dark, deep black color resembling the appearance of soot
an intense, deep black color

Examples

The car was painted a striking soot black that gleamed in the sunlight.
He chose curtains in soot black to match the minimalist décor of his apartment.
The artist mixed his paints to create the perfect shade of soot black for the shadowy figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet black - an intensely black color

Etymology

From 'soot' (the black powdery substance produced by burning) + 'black.'
