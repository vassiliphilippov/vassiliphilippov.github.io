Cooper Union

1. PROPN

/ ˈkuːpər ˈjuːniən / (AmE), / ˈkuːpə ˈjuːniən / (BrE)
A private college in New York City, founded in 1859 by Peter Cooper, known for offering education in the arts, architecture, and engineering.
private college in New York City

Examples

After graduating from Cooper Union, she began her career as an architect in Manhattan.
The art exhibition featured works by several Cooper Union alumni.
He was thrilled to be accepted to Cooper Union due to its strong engineering program.

Etymology

Named after Peter Cooper (1791–1883), its founder.
