boric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈbɒrɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a white or colorless, slightly acidic compound, soluble in water and ethanol, used as an antiseptic, preservative, and in manufacturing glass, paper, and adhesives
a mildly acidic chemical compound

Examples

The pharmacist recommended boric acid as an effective antiseptic for minor cuts.
Certain insecticides contain boric acid as an active ingredient to eliminate pests.
Boric acid crystals are used in laboratories for various chemical tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borax - a related boron compound used in cleaning and as a flux

Etymology

From 'boric', relating to boron, and 'acid'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a weak acid often used as an antiseptic, insecticide, or in the manufacture of glass and ceramics, with the chemical formula H₃BO₃
a weak acid containing boron and oxygen

Examples

Doctors sometimes use boric acid as an antiseptic for minor cuts and burns.
Powdered boric acid is effective against some household insects.
The laboratory prepared a solution of boric acid for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borate - a salt or ester of boric acid

Etymology

From boron, the element name, and 'acid' from Latin 'acidus' (sour).
