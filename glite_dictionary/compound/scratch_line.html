scratch line

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrætʃ ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃ ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line marked to indicate the starting point of a race or game
starting line in a race or game

Examples

The runners lined up behind the scratch line, waiting for the signal to begin.
In old boxing matches, a scratch line was drawn on the floor to mark where fighters started each round.
Make sure you stand with your toes just behind the scratch line before the race starts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starting line - the line marking the start of a race

Etymology

From 'scratch' (to make a mark on a surface) and 'line'; originally in sports where a mark was scratched into the ground to serve as a starting point.
