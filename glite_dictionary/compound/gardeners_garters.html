gardener's garters

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdnərz ˈɡɑrtərz / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdnəz ˈɡɑːtəz / (BrE)
a perennial grass of marshy meadows and ditches with broad leaves, found in Europe and North America
a broad-leaved marsh grass

Examples

The marsh was full of gardener's garters, their broad striped leaves shimmering in the breeze.
In the botanical garden, gardener's garters grew alongside other wetland plants.
She planted gardener's garters near the pond to help control erosion.

Etymology

From the plant's resemblance to the striped garters once worn by gardeners to hold up their stockings.
