rock lobster

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈlɑb.stɚ / (AmE), / rɒk ˈlɒb.stə / (BrE)
a warm-water lobster without large claws, especially those from Australia, South Africa, Florida, and California, typically sold as frozen tails
clawless warm-water lobster species

Examples

The chef prepared grilled rock lobster tails with a side of lemon butter.
You can often find frozen rock lobster from Australia in many supermarkets.
Unlike the Maine lobster, the rock lobster does not have large claws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny lobster - a common name for warm-water lobsters without large claws

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌlɑb.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌlɒb.stə / (BrE)
a large edible marine crustacean with a spiny shell and no large claws, found in warm seas, also known as spiny lobster
large edible spiny crustacean without large pincers

Examples

Many seaside restaurants serve freshly caught rock lobster as a delicacy.
The diver spotted a large rock lobster hiding among the coral reefs.
Unlike the true lobster, the rock lobster has no large pincers on its front legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny lobster - a large marine crustacean with a spiny shell and no large claws
  • Degree Higher: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a strong shell and prominent claws
