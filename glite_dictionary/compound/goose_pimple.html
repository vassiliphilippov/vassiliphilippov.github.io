goose pimple

1. NOUN

/ ɡus ˈpɪmpəl / (AmE), / ɡuːs ˈpɪmpəl / (BrE)
A small raised bump on the skin, caused by cold, fear, or strong emotion, resulting from tiny muscles contracting at the base of hair follicles.
small bump on skin from cold or fear

Examples

A cold wind made goose pimples break out along her arms.
He felt goose pimples rise on his neck as the horror story reached its climax.
Whenever she gets nervous, goose pimples appear on her skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose bump - A small raised area on the skin due to cold or emotion
  • Synonym: gooseflesh - Condition of skin with small bumps due to cold or fear

Etymology

Formed from 'goose', referencing the bumpy skin of a plucked goose, and 'pimple'.
