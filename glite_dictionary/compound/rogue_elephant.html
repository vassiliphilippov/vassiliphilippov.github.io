rogue elephant

1. NOUN

/ roʊɡ ˈɛləfənt / (AmE), / rəʊɡ ˈɛlɪfənt / (BrE)
An elephant that has become aggressive and separated from its herd, often causing destruction or behaving unpredictably.
an aggressive, solitary elephant

Examples

The villagers lived in fear of the rogue elephant that had trampled through their crops.
A rogue elephant can be extremely dangerous when it is cut off from its group.
Wildlife officials tracked the rogue elephant across the reserve to prevent further damage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: elephant - a large mammal with a trunk and tusks
