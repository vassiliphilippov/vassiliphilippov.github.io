Islamic Party of Turkestan

1. PROPN

/ ɪzˈlɑːmɪk ˈpɑːrti əv tʊrkɛˈstɑːn / (AmE), / ɪzˈlɑːmɪk ˈpɑːti ɒv tɜːkɪˈstɑːn / (BrE)
a militant Islamist group founded in 1996 that opposes Uzbekistan's secular government and seeks to establish an Islamic state in Central Asia; known for militant activities and links to drug trafficking.
militant Islamist group in Central Asia

Examples

The government has accused the Islamic Party of Turkestan of carrying out attacks in the region.
International observers are concerned about the influence of the Islamic Party of Turkestan in Central Asian conflicts.
Security forces intercepted members of the Islamic Party of Turkestan attempting to cross the border.
