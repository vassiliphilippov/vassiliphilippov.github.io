metric ton

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrɪk tʌn / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪk tɒn / (BrE)
a unit of mass equal to 1,000 kilograms
unit of mass equal to 1,000 kg

Examples

The shipment weighed nearly two metric tons when it arrived at the port.
A metric ton of coal produces about two tons of carbon dioxide when burned.
Last year, the farm harvested over ten metric tons of wheat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ton - a unit of weight equal to 2,000 pounds (US) or 2,240 pounds (UK)

Etymology

From metric (relating to the metric system) and ton (a standard unit of weight).
