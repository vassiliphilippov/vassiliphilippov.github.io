red trillium

1. NOUN

/ rɛd trɪˈlɪliəm / (AmE), / rɛd trɪˈlɪlɪəm / (BrE)
a perennial plant of eastern North America, with malodorous pink to purple flowers and an astringent root formerly used in folk medicine
woodland plant with pink-purple flower

Examples

We found a beautiful patch of red trillium growing beside the forest stream.
The root of the red trillium was once used by indigenous peoples as a traditional remedy.
In spring, the red trillium blooms with a striking, deep purple flower in shady woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of spring-flowering perennial herbs with three-petaled flowers

2. NOUN

/ rɛd trɪˈliːəm / (AmE), / rɛd trɪˈlɪəm / (BrE)
A woodland wildflower of northeastern North America, with sessile leaves and red or purple, foul-smelling flowers.
North American wildflower with red or purple flowers

Examples

We spotted a cluster of red trillium blooming near the forest path in early spring.
Gardeners admire the unique beauty of red trillium even though its scent is unpleasant.
The red trillium is often found in the damp woodlands of the northeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of spring-blooming perennial plants native to temperate North America and Asia
