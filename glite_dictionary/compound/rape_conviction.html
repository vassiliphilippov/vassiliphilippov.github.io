rape conviction

1. NOUN

/ reɪp kənˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / reɪp kənˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
a legal determination that someone is guilty of the crime of rape
legal finding of guilt for rape

Examples

The defendant received a ten-year sentence following his rape conviction last month.
Her testimony was crucial in securing the rape conviction of the accused.
New legislation imposes stricter penalties for anyone with a prior rape conviction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they were charged
