Scotch pine

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ paɪn / (AmE), / skɒtʃ paɪn / (BrE)
a medium to large pine tree native to northern Europe and Asia, with two needles per cluster and reddish-brown, flaky bark
type of pine tree from Europe and Asia

Examples

The Scotch pine is commonly planted as a Christmas tree due to its sturdy branches.
We spotted a grove of Scotch pine on our hike through the mountains.
The reddish bark of the Scotch pine peels off in distinctive flakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scots pine - another name for Scotch pine

Etymology

From 'Scotch', an old term for 'Scottish', referring to its prevalence in Scotland, and 'pine' from Latin 'pinus'.
