blood clotting

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈklɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / blʌd ˈklɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which liquid blood changes into a semi-solid mass (a blood clot) to stop bleeding
the process of forming a blood clot
medical

Examples

Adequate vitamin K is essential for normal blood clotting in the human body.
A disorder affecting blood clotting can lead to excessive bleeding or dangerous clots.
The patient was given medication to prevent abnormal blood clotting after surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulation - the process of a liquid becoming thick or solid, especially blood
