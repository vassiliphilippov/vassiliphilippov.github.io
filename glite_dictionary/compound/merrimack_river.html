Merrimack River

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛrɪˌmæk ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪˌmæk ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the northeastern United States, rising in central New Hampshire, flowing through New Hampshire and Massachusetts, and emptying into the Atlantic Ocean.
river in New Hampshire and Massachusetts, USA

Examples

The Merrimack River is known for its picturesque views and historic significance in the Northeast.
Many communities along the Merrimack River rely on it as a source of water and recreation.
The cities of Manchester and Lowell were built on the banks of the Merrimack River.

Etymology

From the Native American (Abenaki) word thought to mean 'swift water place'.
