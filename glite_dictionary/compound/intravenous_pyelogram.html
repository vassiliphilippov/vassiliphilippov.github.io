intravenous pyelogram

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈvinəs paɪˈɛləˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈviː.nəs paɪˈɛl.ə.ɡræm / (BrE)
an X-ray examination of the kidneys and ureters after the injection of a radiopaque dye into a vein
X-ray test of kidneys and ureters
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an intravenous pyelogram to investigate the cause of the patient's kidney pain.
An intravenous pyelogram can reveal blockages or abnormalities in the urinary tract.
Before performing the intravenous pyelogram, the technician explained the procedure to the patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IVP - abbreviation for intravenous pyelogram

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein), 'pyelo-' (of the pelvis of the kidney), and Greek 'gramma' (something written).
