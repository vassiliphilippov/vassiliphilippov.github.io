tartar emetic

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrtər ɪˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtə ɪˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless salt (antimony potassium tartrate) formerly used as a mordant and in medicine as an emetic
poisonous salt used as a mordant and emetic
medical

Examples

The chemist explained the toxic effects of tartar emetic if ingested.
In the 19th century, tartar emetic was commonly used to induce vomiting.
Accidental exposure to tartar emetic can result in severe poisoning.

Etymology

From 'tartar' (potassium bitartrate) and 'emetic', indicating its origin and use as a substance that induces vomiting.
