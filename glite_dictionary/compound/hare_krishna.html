Hare Krishna

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːreɪ ˈkrɪʃnə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːreɪ ˈkrɪʃnə / (BrE)
a chant consisting of repeated invocations to the Hindu god Krishna, used especially in devotional practices of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness
Hindu devotional chant to Krishna

Examples

The devotees recited the Hare Krishna for hours during the festival.
Every morning, she begins her meditation with the Hare Krishna.
The sound of the Hare Krishna filled the temple hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantra - a sacred utterance, sound, or phrase repeated in meditation

Etymology

From Sanskrit, where 'Hare' is a vocative form addressing the divine energy of God, and 'Krishna' refers to the Hindu deity Krishna.
