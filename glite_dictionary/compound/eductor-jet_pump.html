eductor-jet pump

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʌktər dʒɛt pʌmp / (AmE), / ɪˈdʌktə dʒɛt pʌmp / (BrE)
A pump that uses the Venturi effect to move fluids or create suction by using a jet of fluid, often steam or water, to draw another fluid into the flow.
pump using jet effect to move fluids

Examples

The engineer installed an eductor-jet pump to remove water from the flooded basement.
An eductor-jet pump operates without moving mechanical parts, making it reliable for challenging environments.
We use an eductor-jet pump in the laboratory setup to achieve the necessary vacuum.

Synset Examples

Depending on the specific application, an injector can take the form of an eductor-jet pump, a water eductor, a vacuum ejector, a steam-jet ejector, or an aspirator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injector - device that uses a jet of fluid to move another fluid
  • Synonym: ejector - device that expels fluid via suction created by a jet
  • Synonym: aspirator - device that creates suction to move fluids or gases

Etymology

From 'eductor', from Latin 'educere' (to lead out), and 'jet pump', referring to movement by a jet stream.
