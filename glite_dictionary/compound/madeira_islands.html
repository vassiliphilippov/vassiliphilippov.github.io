Madeira Islands

1. PROPN

/ məˈdɛrə ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / məˈdɪərə ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the Atlantic Ocean, west of Morocco, that form an autonomous region of Portugal
autonomous Portuguese islands in the Atlantic Ocean

Examples

The Madeira Islands attract thousands of tourists every year due to their mild climate and beautiful landscapes.
Wine exports from the Madeira Islands have been famous for centuries.
Hiking enthusiasts often visit the Madeira Islands to explore their dramatic volcanic terrain.

Etymology

Portuguese madeira (wood), named for the main island's forests; Madeira discovered by Portuguese explorers in the 15th century.
