wood hyacinth

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (AmE), / wʊd ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial flowering plant with blue, pink, or white bell-shaped flowers, typically growing in woodlands; sometimes classified in the genus Scilla.
a woodland flowering plant with blue, pink, or white bells

Examples

The wood hyacinth carpeted the forest floor with its vivid blue blossoms in spring.
Gardeners often plant wood hyacinth for its colorful flowers and ability to thrive in shade.
In Britain, the wood hyacinth is a familiar sight in ancient woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - a common name for the same or similar plant, especially in Britain
  • Degree Higher: hyacinth - a related or similar-looking bulbous flowering plant
