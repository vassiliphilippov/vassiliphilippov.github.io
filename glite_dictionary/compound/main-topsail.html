main-topsail

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪn ˌtɑpˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪn ˌtɒp.seɪl / (BrE)
A sail set above the mainsail on the mainmast of a sailing ship.
sail above mainsail on mainmast

Examples

The crew hurried to raise the main-topsail as the wind picked up.
During the storm, the captain ordered the main-topsail to be taken down for safety.
With the main-topsail unfurled, the ship gained speed on the open sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topsail - a sail set above another sail on a mast

Etymology

From 'main' (mainmast) and 'topsail' (sail above the mainsail), used in nautical contexts.
