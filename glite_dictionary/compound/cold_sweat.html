cold sweat

1. NOUN

/ koʊld swɛt / (AmE), / kəʊld swɛt / (BrE)
A state in which a person sweats while feeling cold, typically due to fear, shock, or illness.
perspiring while feeling cold, often from fear

Examples

She woke up in a cold sweat after the nightmare.
The news left him standing in a cold sweat, unable to move.
Public speaking used to bring on a cold sweat for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspiration - process of sweating
  • Degree Higher: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger, pain, or harm
  • Degree Higher: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
