Weddell Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɛdəl si / (AmE), / ˈwɛdəl siː / (BrE)
an area of the Southern Ocean, east of the Antarctic Peninsula, forming part of the Antarctic waters
sea in Antarctica, east of the Antarctic Peninsula

Examples

The Weddell Sea is known for its vast, floating ice shelves.
Researchers discovered new marine species in the Weddell Sea region.
Strong winds can cause rapid changes in sea ice conditions in the Weddell Sea.

Etymology

Named after British explorer James Weddell (1787–1834).
