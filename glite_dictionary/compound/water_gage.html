water gage

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an instrument for indicating the level of water in a tank, boiler, reservoir, or similar container
instrument for measuring water level

Examples

The engineer checked the water gage to ensure the boiler was filled to the correct level.
Heavy rainfall caused the river's water gage to rise dramatically overnight.
A faulty water gage can lead to inaccurate measurements and potential equipment damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water gauge - instrument used for measuring the water level; common modern spelling
