keratosis blennorrhagica

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs blɛnˌɔrəˈdʒaɪkə / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs blɛnˌɒrəˈdʒaɪkə / (BrE)
A skin disease marked by scaly rashes on the palms and soles, typically associated with Reiter's syndrome.
scaly rash linked to Reiter's syndrome
medical

Examples

The presence of keratosis blennorrhagica is an important clue in the diagnosis of Reiter's syndrome.
Patients with keratosis blennorrhagica often develop scaly lesions on the soles of their feet.
A dermatologist identified the characteristic keratosis blennorrhagica rash during a routine examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Reiter's syndrome - A medical condition causing joint inflammation, urethritis, and conjunctivitis

Etymology

From Greek 'keratosis' (horny growth) and 'blennorrhagia' (mucous discharge).
