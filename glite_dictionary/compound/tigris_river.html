Tigris River

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪɡrɪs ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡrɪs ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in southwestern Asia, flowing through Turkey and Iraq and joining the Euphrates to form the Shatt al-Arab
major river in southwestern Asia

Examples

The ancient city of Nineveh was located on the banks of the Tigris River.
Many civilizations flourished between the Tigris River and the Euphrates.
Farmers in Iraq rely on the Tigris River for irrigation.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Tigris, from Old Persian Tigra, meaning 'fast' or 'arrow-like.'
