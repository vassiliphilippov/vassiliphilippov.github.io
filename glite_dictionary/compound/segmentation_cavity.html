segmentation cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən ˈkæv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən ˈkæv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a fluid-filled space formed within the blastula during early embryonic development
fluid-filled cavity in a blastula
medical

Examples

The segmentation cavity increases in size as the embryo develops into the blastula stage.
Researchers observed the formation of a segmentation cavity in the early stages of frog embryogenesis.
The fluid within the segmentation cavity plays a crucial role in cell differentiation.

Etymology

From 'segmentation' (division into segments) + 'cavity' (hollow space); used in embryology.
