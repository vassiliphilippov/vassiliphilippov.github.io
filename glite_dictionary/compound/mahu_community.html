mahu community

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːhuː kəˌmjunɪti / (AmE), / ˈmɑːhuː kəˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
A cultural group in Hawaii comprised of individuals who identify with the mahu tradition, embodying both masculine and feminine characteristics and holding a unique place in Hawaiian culture.
cultural group in Hawaii

Examples

The mahu community celebrated their heritage with a parade.
Many members of the mahu community work to educate others about Hawaiian traditions.
The contributions of the mahu community are recognized throughout the islands.

Synset Examples

The mahu community celebrated their heritage with a parade.

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'māhū', meaning a person of a third gender or non-binary identity, and English 'community'.
