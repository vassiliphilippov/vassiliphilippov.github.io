dilation and curettage

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈleɪʃən ənd kjʊˈrɛtɪdʒ / (AmE), / daɪˈleɪʃən ənd kjʊəˈrɛtɪdʒ / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the cervix is widened and the lining of the uterus is scraped with a curette, often to obtain tissue samples, stop bleeding, remove tumors, or manage pregnancy-related issues
surgical widening of the cervix and scraping of the uterus
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a dilation and curettage might be necessary after a miscarriage.
A dilation and curettage was performed to remove abnormal tissue from the uterus.
Recovery from a dilation and curettage procedure is usually quick and uncomplicated.

Etymology

From Latin 'dilatio' (widening) and French 'curette' (scraper), from 'curer' (to clean).
