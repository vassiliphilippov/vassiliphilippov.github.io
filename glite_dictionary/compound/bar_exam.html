bar exam

1. NOUN

/ bɑr ɪɡˈzæm / (AmE), / bɑː ɪɡˈzæm / (BrE)
an examination that tests whether a person is qualified to practice law in a particular jurisdiction
law qualification examination
legal

Examples

After months of studying, she finally took the bar exam in California.
He celebrated with his friends after passing the bar exam on his first attempt.
Many law graduates experience stress while preparing for the bar exam.

Synset Examples

applicants may qualify to take the New York bar examination by graduating from an approved law school
he passed the bar exam on his third try

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar examination - formal version of bar exam, same meaning

Etymology

From 'bar', referring to the legal profession, and 'exam', short for examination.
