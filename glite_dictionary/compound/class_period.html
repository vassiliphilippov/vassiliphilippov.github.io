class period

1. NOUN

/ klæs ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / klɑːs ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
a regularly scheduled segment of time during which a particular subject is taught as part of a course of study
scheduled lesson time in school

Examples

My favorite class period is mathematics, which takes place right after lunch.
The teacher announced that there would be a quiz during tomorrow's class period.
Students must remain seated until the class period ends and the bell rings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesson - a period of learning or teaching
  • Antonym: recess - break period between classes at school
