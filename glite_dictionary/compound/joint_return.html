joint return

1. NOUN

/ dʒɔɪnt rɪˈtɜrn / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt rɪˈtɜːn / (BrE)
a tax return filed by a married couple together, reporting their combined income and deductions
tax return filed by a couple
legal american

Examples

They decided to file a joint return to take advantage of certain tax benefits.
When preparing their joint return, the couple gathered all relevant financial documents.
If you are married, a joint return may result in a lower tax bill than filing separately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individual return - a tax return filed by a single taxpayer
