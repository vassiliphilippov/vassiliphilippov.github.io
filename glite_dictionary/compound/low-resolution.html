low-resolution

1. ADJ

/ ˌloʊˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
(of computer screens or images) producing or displaying images that are not sharply defined, with limited detail or clarity.
producing images with limited detail

Examples

The website loaded only low-resolution images, making it difficult to see the details.
Many older video games feature low-resolution graphics compared to modern titles.
When the file was compressed, it became pixelated and low-resolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-resolution - producing images that are sharply defined, with a lot of detail
