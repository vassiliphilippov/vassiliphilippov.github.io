terraced house

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.əst ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.əst ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house that is part of a row of identical or similar houses joined together side by side by shared walls, forming a continuous terrace.
house in a row of joined homes
british

Examples

They bought a terraced house on a quiet street in London.
Her childhood home was a small terraced house overlooking the park.
The row of terraced houses has stood for over a century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: row house - a house forming part of a row of similar houses joined by their side walls, chiefly used in American English

Etymology

From 'terrace' meaning a row of houses, from Old French *terrasse* (platform), and 'house'.
