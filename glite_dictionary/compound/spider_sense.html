spider sense

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdɚ sɛns / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə sɛns / (BrE)
a heightened intuition or instinctive feeling that alerts someone to impending danger or trouble, especially without clear evidence
instinctive sense of impending danger
informal

Examples

As I walked down the dark alley, my spider sense told me something was wrong.
Her spider sense always kicked in when a deal seemed too good to be true.
The moment he entered the meeting, his spider sense started tingling about the negotiations.

Synset Examples

My spider sense is tingling; I feel watched.
My spider sense is tingling; something's off here.
I walked into the room, and my spider sense is tingling.
She trusted her Spidey sense when meeting new people.
His Spidey sense kicked in during the tense situation.
His Spidey sense told him something was wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively, without conscious reasoning
  • Synonym: sixth sense - a supposed intuitive faculty giving awareness not explicable in terms of normal perception

Etymology

Originates from the comic book character Spider-Man, whose 'spider sense' warned him of danger.
