low-level flight

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ ˈlɛvəl ˈflaɪt / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈlɛvəl ˈflaɪt / (BrE)
the action of flying an aircraft at a very low altitude above the ground
flight at very low altitude

Examples

The pilot demonstrated exceptional skill during low-level flight over the desert.
Military jets often use low-level flight to avoid radar detection.
Birds are sometimes startled by low-level flight of helicopters.
