Fusarium wilt

1. NOUN

/ fuˈzɛəriəm wɪlt / (AmE), / fjʊˈzɛəriəm wɪlt / (BrE)
A plant disease characterized by the wilting and yellowing of leaves, caused by pathogenic fungi of the genus Fusarium.
a plant disease caused by Fusarium fungi
medical

Examples

Banana plants are especially vulnerable to Fusarium wilt, which can devastate entire crops.
The spread of Fusarium wilt has become a major concern among tomato growers worldwide.
Farmers implement strict hygiene measures to prevent Fusarium wilt from infecting their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilt disease - any plant disease causing wilting of leaves or stems

Etymology

From the genus name Fusarium (Latin, 'spindle-shaped'), named for the fungus causing the disease, and 'wilt' meaning to become limp.
