white milkweed

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmɪlkwid / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmɪlkwiːd / (BrE)
A tall herb native to the southwestern United States, known for its leafless white waxy stems and whitish star-shaped flowers.
a tall herb with white stems and starlike flowers

Examples

The white milkweed grows abundantly in the deserts of Arizona.
Researchers studied the properties of white milkweed in its natural habitat.
The leafless stems of white milkweed make it easy to identify among other wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - any of various plants of the genus Asclepias, known for a milky juice
