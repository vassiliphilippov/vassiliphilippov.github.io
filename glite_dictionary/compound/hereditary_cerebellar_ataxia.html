hereditary cerebellar ataxia

1. NOUN

/ həˌrɛdəˌtɛri səbɛˈlɛlər əˈtæksiə / (AmE), / hɛˈrɛdɪtəri sɛˈbɛlɪlə əˈtæksiə / (BrE)
A neurological disorder, usually beginning in late childhood or early adulthood, marked by problems with balance and coordination, unsteady gait, speech difficulties, and involuntary eye movements, caused by inherited defects affecting the cerebellum.
inherited disorder affecting balance and coordination
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed her with hereditary cerebellar ataxia after noting her gait and family history.
People with hereditary cerebellar ataxia often develop speech and coordination problems as symptoms progress.
Genetic counseling can be important for families affected by hereditary cerebellar ataxia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ataxia - lack of muscle coordination

Etymology

From 'hereditary' (inherited) and 'cerebellar ataxia' (loss of coordination due to cerebellum disease).
