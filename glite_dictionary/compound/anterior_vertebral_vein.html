anterior vertebral vein

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪriər vərˈtibrəl veɪn / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə vəˈtiːbrəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that accompanies the ascending cervical artery and drains into the vertebral vein, located at the front of the cervical vertebrae.
vein near cervical vertebrae, drains into vertebral vein
medical

Examples

The anterior vertebral vein plays a crucial role in draining blood from the cervical spinal cord.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the anterior vertebral vein.
Enlargement of the anterior vertebral vein can indicate circulatory issues in the neck region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vertebral vein - vein that drains the cervical spinal cord and posterior part of the neck

Etymology

From Latin: anterior (in front) + vertebra (joint of the spine) + vein.
