order Rickettsiales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər rɪˌkɛtsiˈeɪlz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə rɪˌkɛtsiˈeɪlz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of pleomorphic, Gram-negative microorganisms, including many intracellular bacteria pathogenic to humans and animals
order of Gram-negative microorganisms
medical

Examples

The order Rickettsiales comprises several important pathogens that cause diseases in humans and animals.
Research on the order Rickettsiales has contributed greatly to our understanding of intracellular bacteria.
Many species in the order Rickettsiales are transmitted by arthropod vectors such as ticks and lice.

Etymology

Name based on Howard Taylor Ricketts, American pathologist, plus the standard taxonomic suffix -ales.
