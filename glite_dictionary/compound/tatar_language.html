Tatar language

1. NOUN

/ təˈtɑr ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / təˈtɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Tatar people, mainly living between the Volga and Ural Mountains in Russia and surrounding areas
Turkic language spoken by Tatar people

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Tatar language.
The Tatar language uses a variant of the Cyrillic alphabet in modern Russia.
Many schools in Tatarstan teach children in the Tatar language as well as Russian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - a family of languages including Tatar, Turkish, Uzbek, and others

Etymology

From Tatar, an ethnic group name, ultimately from Persian تاتار (Tātār).
