Colored People Time

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚd ˈpipəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd ˈpiːpl taɪm / (BrE)
(offensive) A humorous or stereotypical term referring to the perception that Black people are habitually late or have a relaxed attitude toward punctuality, sometimes used within the Black community, but considered offensive.
(offensive) stereotype about Black people and tardiness
offensive

Examples

The meeting started late, and everyone joked it must be Colored People Time.
She apologized for arriving late, saying she was running on Colored People Time.
References to Colored People Time are now widely criticized as perpetuating harmful stereotypes.

Synset Examples

The party started an hour late, typical Colored People Time.
They arrived late, joking about Colored People Time.
She laughed, saying it's just Colored People Time at work.
They embraced Colored People's Time, arriving after the scheduled start.
He was late, attributing it to Colored People's Time.
Colored People's Time is often a lighthearted excuse for tardiness.

Etymology

From 'colored people' (now offensive) + 'time'; originated in the early 20th century US as a racist stereotype.
