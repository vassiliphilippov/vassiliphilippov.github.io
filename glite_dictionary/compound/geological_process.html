geological process

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈprɑsɛs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a natural process that changes or shapes the Earth's geological features, such as erosion, sedimentation, or volcanism
natural process altering Earth's features

Examples

Erosion is a geological process that has shaped the landscape over millions of years.
Plate tectonics is another important geological process responsible for mountain formation.
Understanding every geological process is vital for predicting natural disasters such as earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erosion - the process of the gradual destruction of land or rock, often by wind or water
  • Synonym: weathering - the breakdown of rocks and minerals due to atmospheric conditions

Etymology

From 'geological', relating to geology (from Greek 'gē' meaning 'earth'), and 'process', from Latin 'processus' (advance, progression).
