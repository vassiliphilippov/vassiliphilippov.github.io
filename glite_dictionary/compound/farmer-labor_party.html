Farmer-Labor Party

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɑrmər ˈleɪbɚ ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmə ˈleɪbə ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a former minor political party in the United States during the early 20th century, primarily representing the interests of farmers and laborers
US minor party (early 20th century)

Examples

The Farmer-Labor Party played a significant role in Minnesota politics during the 1920s.
Many progressive reforms were initially championed by the Farmer-Labor Party.
Historians frequently mention the influence of the Farmer-Labor Party on later political movements.
