Hudson River School

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌdsən ˈrɪvər skuːl / (AmE), / ˈhʌdsən ˈrɪvə skuːl / (BrE)
a group of 19th-century American landscape painters known for depicting the Hudson River valley and surrounding regions
19th-century American landscape art movement

Examples

The Hudson River School artists captured the natural beauty of the American landscape in their paintings.
Many art historians consider the Hudson River School to be the foundation of American landscape painting.
Paintings from the Hudson River School often feature dramatic skies and detailed wilderness scenes.

Etymology

Named after the Hudson River in New York, where many of the artists were inspired to paint.
