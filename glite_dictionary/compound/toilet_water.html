toilet water

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪlət ˌwɑtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlət ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
a pleasantly scented liquid, lighter than cologne, used on the skin as a fragrance
light perfumed liquid for the skin

Examples

She dabbed a little toilet water behind her ears before leaving for the party.
The shop offered a wide selection of toilet waters with floral or citrus notes.
Many people prefer toilet water for a light daytime fragrance instead of something stronger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eau de toilette - perfumed water with a light scent, for use on the body
  • Degree Higher: cologne - a scented liquid for the skin, typically stronger than toilet water

Etymology

From French 'eau de toilette', referring to scented water used for personal grooming.
