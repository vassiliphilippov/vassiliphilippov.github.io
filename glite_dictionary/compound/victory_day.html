Victory Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪktəri deɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɪktəri deɪ / (BrE)
A day set aside to commemorate a significant victory, especially in a military conflict, often celebrated as a public holiday in various countries.
commemorative day marking a victory

Examples

Many countries celebrate Victory Day to mark the end of World War II.
The city hosts a grand parade every Victory Day in remembrance of fallen soldiers.
Schools are closed on Victory Day as it is a national holiday.
