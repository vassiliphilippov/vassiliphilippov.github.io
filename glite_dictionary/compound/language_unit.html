language unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a distinct element or segment of language, such as a word, morpheme, or phrase, into which linguistic messages can be analyzed
distinct element of language

Examples

A word is considered a basic language unit in most linguistic studies.
Phonemes and morphemes are both types of language units analyzed by linguists.
Understanding each language unit helps in decoding and interpreting complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: morpheme - the smallest meaningful language unit
  • Degree Higher: phrase - a unit of language larger than a word
