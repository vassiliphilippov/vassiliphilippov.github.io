trench warfare

1. NOUN

/ trɛntʃ ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / trɛntʃ ˈwɔː.feə / (BrE)
A method of fighting in which opposing armies attack, counterattack, and defend from relatively permanent systems of trenches dug into the ground facing each other.
combat fought from opposing trenches

Examples

World War I is often remembered for the brutal trench warfare that left millions dead.
The movie accurately depicts the harsh realities of trench warfare.
After months of trench warfare, both sides began to suffer from low morale and heavy losses.

Synset Examples

instead of the war ending quickly, it became bogged down in trench warfare

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guerrilla warfare - a form of irregular warfare using small, mobile groups and surprise attacks

Etymology

Combination of 'trench' (from Old French 'tranchée', meaning 'a ditch, entrenchment') and 'warfare'. Gained particular prominence in World War I.

2. NOUN

/ trɛntʃ ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / trɛntʃ ˈwɔːfɛə / (BrE)
A situation or struggle, especially a prolonged one, in which neither side can defeat the other or make much progress, often used figuratively for difficult and deadlocked competition.
prolonged, deadlocked struggle or contest

Examples

After months of trench warfare between the two companies, neither could claim victory in the market.
The negotiations became a form of trench warfare, with each side stubbornly defending its position.
Political debates often descend into trench warfare, with both parties refusing to compromise.

Synset Examples

the hope that his superior campaigning skills would make a difference evaporated in the realization that electioneering had become a form of trench warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation in which further action is blocked; deadlock
  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made
  • Antonym: breakthrough - an important and dramatic advance; an end to a prolonged stalemate

Etymology

Originally from military terminology describing the style of combat in trenches during World War I.
