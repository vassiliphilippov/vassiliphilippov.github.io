well-heeled

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈhild / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈhiːld / (BrE)
having plenty of money; wealthy or affluent
wealthy; affluent

Examples

Many well-heeled patrons attended the art auction to support the museum.
Despite their well-heeled background, the siblings remained humble and charitable.
The new restaurant quickly became popular among the city's well-heeled residents.

Synset Examples

They are well-heeled and can afford luxury vacations.
The charity gala attracted a well-heeled crowd.
He comes from a well-heeled family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live comfortably

Etymology

Originally American English (mid-19th century), referring metaphorically to having good shoes, hence being prosperous.
