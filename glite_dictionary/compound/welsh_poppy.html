Welsh poppy

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
a poppy native to western Europe, especially Wales, with showy pale yellow flowers, often cultivated in gardens
a yellow-flowered poppy native to Wales

Examples

The Welsh poppy thrives in shady gardens and is prized for its delicate yellow flowers.
Many gardeners choose to plant the Welsh poppy for a splash of color in early summer.
She photographed a group of Welsh poppies blooming along the stone wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poppy - a flowering plant of the Papaveraceae family, often with showy flowers
