blood spavin

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈspævɪn / (AmE), / blʌd ˈspævɪn / (BrE)
A swelling on a horse's hock caused by enlargement of the veins, often associated with distension.
swelling of a horse's hock caused by vein distension
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with a blood spavin after noticing the swelling near the hock.
Show horses with blood spavin are often not allowed to compete due to lameness issues.
A blood spavin may not always cause pain, but it can indicate underlying joint problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spavin - a swelling of a horse's hock joint due to disease
