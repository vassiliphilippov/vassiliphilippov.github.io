Book of Obadiah

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv oʊˈbeɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv əʊˈbeɪdɪə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Obadiah; the shortest book in the Christian Bible.
Old Testament book of prophecies

Examples

The Book of Obadiah is known for its brief yet powerful message against Edom.
She cited a passage from the Book of Obadiah during the seminar on Old Testament prophets.
Many scholars have studied the Book of Obadiah to understand its historical context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Obadiah - the prophet whose words are recorded in the Book of Obadiah

Etymology

Obadiah is a Hebrew name meaning 'servant of the Lord'; the book is named after its attributed author, Obadiah.
