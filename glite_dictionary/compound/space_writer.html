space writer

1. NOUN

/ speɪs ˈraɪtər / (AmE), / speɪs ˈraɪtə / (BrE)
a writer, especially in journalism, who is paid according to the amount of printed space their copy occupies
writer paid by space used

Examples

As a space writer, he always tried to submit longer articles to maximize his earnings.
Many newspapers once relied on space writers before switching to fixed salaries.
The space writer quickly learned to avoid unnecessary words to make the most of each inch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staff writer - a member of the permanent staff of a newspaper or magazine who writes articles
