genus Kogia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkoʊdʒiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkəʊɡiə / (BrE)
A genus of small toothed whales in the family Kogiidae, which includes the pygmy and dwarf sperm whales.
genus of pygmy sperm whales

Examples

Scientists studying marine mammals often focus on the unique characteristics of the genus Kogia.
The genus Kogia is distinguished from the larger sperm whale genus, Physeter.
Both species in the genus Kogia are rarely seen due to their elusive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy sperm whale - a small whale species (Kogia breviceps) in the family Kogiidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Kogia,' the genus name given to these whales.
