American pasqueflower

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈpæskˌflaʊər / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈpæskˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a short, hairy perennial plant with early spring blue-violet or lilac flowers, native to North America and Siberia
hairy perennial plant with blue-violet flowers

Examples

The American pasqueflower blooms early in the spring, adding vibrant color to the prairie.
We spotted several American pasqueflowers during our hike across the grasslands.
Gardeners admire the American pasqueflower for its delicate, hairy stems and striking flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasqueflower - herbaceous plant with silky, hairy leaves and purple flowers, blooming in spring

Etymology

'Pasqueflower' from Old French 'pasque' (Easter), referring to its blooming time.
