financial organisation

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl ˌɔːrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
An institution, either public or private, that collects funds from the public or other institutions and invests them in financial assets.
institution that invests collected funds in financial assets

Examples

The central bank plays a vital role as a financial organisation in the country's economy.
Many people trust their savings to a reputable financial organisation.
Choosing the right financial organisation can greatly impact your investment returns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financial institution - an organization that manages money, such as banks or investment firms
