myeloblastic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.ə.loʊˌblæstɪk luˈkiː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.ə.ləˌblæstɪk luːˈkiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of cancer affecting blood-forming tissues, characterized by an abnormal increase of myeloblasts in the bloodstream.
cancer with excess myeloblasts in blood
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that myeloblastic leukemia requires immediate and aggressive treatment.
Recent medical advances have improved survival rates for children diagnosed with myeloblastic leukemia.
Her research focuses on new therapies for patients suffering from myeloblastic leukemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - cancer of blood-forming tissues resulting in high numbers of abnormal white blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'myelos' (marrow) + 'blastos' (germ, sprout) + 'leukemia' (condition of white blood).
