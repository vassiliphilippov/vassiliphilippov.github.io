metaplastic anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈplæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈplastɪk əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A form of pernicious anemia in which the different formed elements of the blood undergo abnormal changes.
anemia with abnormal blood cell changes
medical british

Examples

The diagnosis of metaplastic anaemia was confirmed by laboratory tests showing abnormal blood cell morphology.
Patients with metaplastic anaemia often require vitamin B12 injections as part of their treatment.
Research into the causes of metaplastic anaemia has advanced our understanding of blood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pernicious anemia - A severe form of anemia caused by the body's inability to absorb vitamin B12

Etymology

From Greek 'meta-' (change) + 'plastic' (relating to formation) + 'anaemia' (lack of blood).
