diametrical opposition

1. NOUN

/ daɪəˈmɛtrɪkəl ˌɑpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈmɛtrɪkəl ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a complete or direct opposition, especially as if situated at opposite ends of a diameter
complete or direct opposition; on opposite extremes

Examples

The two theories exist in diametrical opposition to each other, making compromise difficult.
Political views in the country often fall into diametrical opposition, leaving little room for middle ground.
Their personalities stood in diametrical opposition, with one being extremely cautious and the other highly adventurous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antithesis - a direct opposite or contrast
  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else

Etymology

From Greek 'diametros' (diameter) and Latin 'oppositio' (opposition).
