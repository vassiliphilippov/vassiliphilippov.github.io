ground fir

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd faɪr / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd fɜː / (BrE)
A common North American club moss (Lycopodium obscurum), also known as ground pine or princess pine, with evergreen, fir-like foliage.
a North American club moss, Lycopodium obscurum

Examples

The forest floor was covered with ground fir resembling miniature evergreens.
Many people mistakenly identify ground fir as a young pine tree due to its appearance.
During the holidays, ground fir is sometimes used in decorative wreaths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground pine - another common name for Lycopodium obscurum
  • Synonym: princess pine - another common name for Lycopodium obscurum
