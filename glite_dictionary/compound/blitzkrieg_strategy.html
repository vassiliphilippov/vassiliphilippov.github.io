blitzkrieg strategy

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪtsˌkriɡ ˈstræt̬ədʒi / (AmE), / ˈblɪtskriːɡ ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
a fast and intense campaign or effort, often intended to achieve rapid results
fast and intense campaign or effort

Examples

The marketing team adopted a blitzkrieg strategy to capture market share quickly.
The company used a blitzkrieg strategy to outpace competitors during the holiday season.
Their blitzkrieg strategy left little time for rivals to respond to the new product launch.

Synset Examples

They executed a blitzkrieg strategy for the product launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blitzkrieg - a sudden and overwhelming military attack
  • Synonym: campaign - an organized course of action to achieve a goal

Etymology

From German 'Blitzkrieg' (lightning war), originally a WWII military term, combined with 'strategy'.
