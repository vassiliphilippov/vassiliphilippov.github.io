reed pipe

1. NOUN

/ ɹid paɪp / (AmE), / ɹiːd paɪp / (BrE)
an organ pipe that produces sound by means of a vibrating reed, typically used in pipe organs to create distinctive tones
organ pipe with a vibrating reed

Examples

The organist selected a reed pipe to add a bright, brassy sound to the piece.
Many church organs feature at least one reed pipe in their arrangement of stops.
Dust often accumulates inside the reed pipe, affecting the clarity of its tone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flue pipe - an organ pipe that produces sound by air flowing through, without a vibrating reed
