gut buster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌt ˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌt ˌbʌstə / (BrE)
A very large and filling meal or food item, especially one that can be difficult to finish due to its size.
very large and filling meal
informal

Examples

That burger was a real gut buster; I was full after just half of it.
The restaurant is famous for its breakfast gut buster that includes six eggs and a pound of bacon.
He ordered a gut buster sandwich that barely fit on the plate.
