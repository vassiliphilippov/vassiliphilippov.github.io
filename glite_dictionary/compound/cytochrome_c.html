cytochrome c

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtoʊˌkroʊm ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪtəˌkrəʊm ˈsiː / (BrE)
a small, highly abundant protein found in the mitochondria of cells, playing a key role in the transfer of energy during cellular respiration
mitochondrial protein involved in energy transfer
medical

Examples

Cytochrome c plays a crucial role in the process of cellular respiration.
Scientists measured the release of cytochrome c to assess cell health.
The structure of cytochrome c has been studied in many species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytochrome oxidase - an enzyme in the electron transport chain of mitochondria

Etymology

From cytochrome (from Greek 'kytos' meaning 'cell' and 'chroma' meaning 'color') and the letter 'c' used to distinguish this cytochrome type.
