civil day

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl deɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl deɪ / (BrE)
A day measured from midnight to midnight, as recognized in most civil and legal systems.
day measured midnight to midnight

Examples

The contract specifies the deadline as the end of the civil day on June 30.
Astronomers distinguish between the civil day and the sidereal day for precise measurements.
A civil day always begins at midnight and ends at the following midnight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: solar day - The period it takes for the sun to return to the same position in the sky, typically about 24 hours
  • Antonym: sidereal day - The time taken for Earth to rotate once relative to the stars, slightly shorter than a civil day
