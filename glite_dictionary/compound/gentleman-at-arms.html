gentleman-at-arms

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛntlmən æt ˈɑrmz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntlmən ət ˈɑːmz / (BrE)
one of a group of forty ceremonial guards who attend the British sovereign on state occasions
ceremonial guard for British sovereign
british

Examples

At the coronation, a gentleman-at-arms stood solemnly by the throne.
The gentlemen-at-arms are easily recognized by their ornate uniforms.
It is a great honor to be appointed as a gentleman-at-arms.

Etymology

From 'gentleman', meaning a man of good birth, and 'at arms', referring to being armed or serving as a soldier.
