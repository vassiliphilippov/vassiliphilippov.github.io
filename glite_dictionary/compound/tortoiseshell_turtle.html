tortoiseshell turtle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtəsˌʃɛl ˈtɜrt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtəsˌʃɛl ˈtɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
a tropical sea turtle with a hawk-like beak, valued for its striking shell and as a source of food
tropical sea turtle with a distinctive shell

Examples

The tortoiseshell turtle is critically endangered due to demand for its beautiful shell.
Divers often hope to spot a tortoiseshell turtle among the coral reefs.
Exporting tortoiseshell turtle shell has been banned to help protect the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawksbill turtle - a species of sea turtle known for its beak-like jaw and ornate shell
