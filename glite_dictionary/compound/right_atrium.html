right atrium

1. NOUN

/ raɪt ˈeɪtriəm / (AmE), / raɪt ˈeɪtrɪəm / (BrE)
the right upper chamber of the heart that receives deoxygenated blood from the body through the venae cavae and coronary sinus
right upper heart chamber
medical

Examples

The right atrium collects deoxygenated blood before passing it to the right ventricle.
Doctors detected an abnormal rhythm originating in the patient's right atrium.
During the procedure, a catheter was inserted into the right atrium for diagnostic testing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left atrium - the left upper chamber of the heart
  • Synonym: atrium - an upper chamber of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (central hall), used in anatomical context since the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌraɪt ˈeɪ.tri.əm / (AmE), / ˌraɪt ˈeɪ.tri.əm / (BrE)
the right upper chamber of the heart that receives deoxygenated blood from the body through the venae cavae and coronary sinus
right upper chamber of the heart
medical

Examples

The right atrium collects blood returning from the body before passing it to the right ventricle.
Doctors examined the patient's right atrium for signs of abnormal heart rhythms.
Heart defects may sometimes affect the structure of the right atrium.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left atrium - left upper chamber of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (central hall or court of a Roman house).
