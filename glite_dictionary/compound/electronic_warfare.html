electronic warfare

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
military action that uses electromagnetic energy to detect, exploit, disrupt, or prevent hostile use of the electromagnetic spectrum
military use of electromagnetic tactics

Examples

Recent advances in electronic warfare have significantly changed modern military strategy.
The military invested in new technologies to improve its electronic warfare capabilities.
Training in electronic warfare is essential for defense forces in technologically advanced conflicts.
