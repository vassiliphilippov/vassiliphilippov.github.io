pine family

1. NOUN

/ paɪn ˈfæməli / (AmE), / paɪn ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A large family of coniferous trees and shrubs, known scientifically as Pinaceae, including pines, firs, spruces, and their relatives.
coniferous tree family (Pinaceae)

Examples

Many species in the pine family are important sources of timber and paper products.
The pine family includes familiar trees like spruces, firs, and larches.
Several members of the pine family are adapted to survive in cold, mountainous regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinaceae - the botanical family of coniferous trees including pines and firs
