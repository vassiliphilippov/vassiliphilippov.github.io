Heliogabalian festival

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːli.oʊˈɡæbəliən ˈfɛstɪvəl / (AmE), / ˌhiːli.əˈɡæbəliən ˈfestɪvəl / (BrE)
a festival associated with or inspired by the practices of the Roman Emperor Heliogabalus, often characterized by extravagance and opulence
festival linked to Emperor Heliogabalus

Examples

The city museum recreated a Heliogabalian festival to showcase the lavish customs of ancient Rome.
During the Heliogabalian festival, guests feasted and celebrated with elaborate performances.
Scholars debate whether the original Heliogabalian festival was a religious or primarily secular event.

Synset Examples

Heliogabalian festivals are colorful and lively.

Etymology

From Heliogabalus, Roman emperor (c. 203–222 AD), combined with 'festival'.
