stuffed tomato

1. NOUN

/ stʌft təˈmeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / stʌft təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A tomato that has been hollowed out, filled with a savory mixture such as meat, rice, or vegetables, and baked briefly.
tomato filled with mixture and baked

Examples

He prepared a delicious stuffed tomato filled with herbed rice and cheese for dinner.
The restaurant's menu featured a classic Mediterranean stuffed tomato as an appetizer.
She brought a tray of stuffed tomatoes to the potluck, and they were quickly devoured.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuffed pepper - pepper filled with seasoned mixture and baked

2. NOUN

/ stʌft təˈmeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / stʌft təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A tomato hollowed out and filled with a mixture of salad ingredients, usually served cold as an appetizer or side dish.
tomato filled with salad mixture

Examples

She prepared a delicious stuffed tomato for the picnic by filling it with tuna salad.
The buffet included stuffed tomatoes as a colorful and refreshing option.
A stuffed tomato makes a light and healthy lunch during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuffed pepper - a pepper filled with a mixture, typically of vegetables, rice, or meat
