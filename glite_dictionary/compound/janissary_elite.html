janissary elite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒænɪˌsɛri ɪˈlit / (AmE), / ˈdʒænɪsəri ɪˈliːt / (BrE)
an elite military group of soldiers serving in the Ottoman Empire, known for their loyalty, discipline, and influence in state affairs
elite Ottoman military group

Examples

The janissary elite were highly trained and often wielded great power in the Ottoman court.
Historians debate the origins of the janissary elite and their recruitment methods.
Without the janissary elite, the Ottoman military would have struggled to maintain its dominance.

Synset Examples

The janissary elite played a crucial role in the empire's expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Janissary - member of the Ottoman imperial guard

Etymology

From Turkish 'yeniçeri' (new soldier) + English 'elite' (the best part).
