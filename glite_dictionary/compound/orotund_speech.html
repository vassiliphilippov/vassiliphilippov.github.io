orotund speech

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɹəˌtʌnd spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒɹətʌnd spiːtʃ / (BrE)
speech that is rich, full, and resonant in sound, often perceived as impressive or pompous.
rich, resonant speech style

Examples

The actor's orotund speech commanded the attention of everyone in the theater.
Her orotund speech was perfect for the grand occasion, echoing through the room with authority.
Despite the orotund speech, some listeners felt it sounded overly theatrical and artificial.

Synset Examples

She delivered an orotund speech that captivated everyone.
The orotund tones of the singer resonated beautifully.
His orotund voice filled the entire hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orotund - (of a voice or speech) full, round, and imposing
  • Synonym: sonorous speech - speech with a deep, rich, and full sound
  • Antonym: thin speech - speech that is weak or lacking fullness

Etymology

From Latin 'orotundus' meaning 'round-mouthed, clear' (from 'os', mouth, and 'rotundus', round).
