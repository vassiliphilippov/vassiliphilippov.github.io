hickory pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪkəri paɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɪkəri paɪn / (BrE)
a small two-needled pine tree of the eastern United States (Appalachians), characterized by dark brown flaking bark and thorn-tipped cone scales
a small pine tree of the Appalachian region

Examples

The hickory pine is often found on the rocky slopes of the Appalachian Mountains.
Some birds and squirrels feed on the seeds of the hickory pine cones.
Foresters have noted the distinctive bark patterns of the hickory pine.
