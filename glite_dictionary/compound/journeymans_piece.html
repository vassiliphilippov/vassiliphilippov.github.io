journeyman's piece

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːniˌmænz pis / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːniˌmanz piːs / (BrE)
a skilled piece of work produced by a journeyman to demonstrate competence in a trade, typically as part of qualifying as a master
work showing a journeyman's skill in a trade

Examples

His journeyman's piece displayed exceptional attention to detail and craftsmanship.
Apprentices were often judged by the quality of their journeyman's piece before becoming full members of the guild.
She carved a wooden chest as her journeyman's piece, impressing the examination committee.

Synset Examples

His journeyman's piece displayed exceptional craftsmanship.

The guild praised her journeyman's piece as outstanding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: masterpiece - a work produced to qualify for the rank of master in a guild

Etymology

From 'journeyman' (from Old French 'jornee', day's work) + 'piece' (object, work), reflecting a tradition in skilled trades.
