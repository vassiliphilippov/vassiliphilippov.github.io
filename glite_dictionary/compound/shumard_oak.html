Shumard oak

1. NOUN

/ ʃuːˈmɑrd oʊk / (AmE), / ʃuːˈmɑːd əʊk / (BrE)
A large deciduous oak tree of the southern and eastern United States, noted for its seven- to nine-lobed elliptical leaves, large acorns, and coarse-grained wood.
large North American oak tree

Examples

The Shumard oak is valued for its sturdy wood and tolerance to a variety of soils.
In autumn, the leaves of the Shumard oak turn brilliant shades of red and orange.
Many parks in the southeastern United States have planted Shumard oak trees for shade and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red oak - a group of oak trees with reddish wood, including the Shumard oak
  • Antonym: white oak - another group of oak trees, generally paler and not as coarse-grained as the Shumard oak

Etymology

Named after Benjamin Franklin Shumard, American geologist and paleontologist (1820–1869).
