vascular system

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæskjələr ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈvæskjʊlə ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the network of vessels and tissues in animals or plants that circulates fluids such as blood, lymph, or sap throughout the body
system circulating fluids in bodies
medical

Examples

The human vascular system plays a vital role in delivering oxygen and nutrients to cells.
Damage to the vascular system can lead to serious health complications such as strokes or heart attacks.
Plants also have a vascular system to transport water and nutrients from the roots to the leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circulatory system - the system that circulates blood and lymph through the body
  • Degree Lower: lymphatic system - system of vessels and nodes conveying lymph

Etymology

From Latin 'vasculum' (small vessel) and 'systema' (system).
