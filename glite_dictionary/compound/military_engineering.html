military engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˌɛnʤəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ˌɛnʤɪˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the art and science of designing and constructing military works, and maintaining military lines of transport and communications.
design and construction of military works

Examples

He specialized in military engineering during his career in the armed forces.
Advancements in military engineering have transformed battlefield logistics.
The course covers topics such as fortification and military engineering.

Synset Examples

Modern military engineering can be divided into three main tasks or fields: combat engineering, strategic support, and ancillary support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineering - the application of science and math to solve problems, especially in design and construction

Etymology

From Latin 'militarius' (pertaining to soldiers) and 'engineering,' from Latin 'ingeniarius' (engineer).
