Old English sheepdog

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈʃipdɔɡ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈʃiːpdɒɡ / (BrE)
a large breed of sheepdog with a dense, shaggy blue-grey and white coat and a typically short tail, originally bred in England for herding sheep
breed of large, shaggy herding dog

Examples

The Old English sheepdog is known for its distinctive shaggy coat and gentle temperament.
As a puppy, the Old English sheepdog needs plenty of socialization to become a well-adjusted adult.
Many families choose the Old English sheepdog for its loyalty and playful nature.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sheepdog - a dog trained to herd sheep

Etymology

Named for its origins in England and its historic role as a sheepdog; 'Old English' refers to its traditional British heritage.
