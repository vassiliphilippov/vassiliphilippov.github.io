Sonora gum

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɔrə ɡʌm / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrə ɡʌm / (BrE)
an acidulous gum resin obtained from the creosote bush (Larrea tridentata), often found in arid regions of North America
gum resin from the creosote bush

Examples

The Sonora gum exuded from the creosote bush has traditional medicinal uses among indigenous peoples.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of Sonora gum to assess its potential in pharmaceutical products.
Collectors in the desert harvest Sonora gum for its unique properties and amber hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum - a viscous secretion from plants, often used in adhesives or food

Etymology

Named after the Sonoran Desert and 'gum', meaning plant resin.
