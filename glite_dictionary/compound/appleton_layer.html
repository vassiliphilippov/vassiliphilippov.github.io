Appleton layer

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəltən ˌleɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈæpəltən ˌleɪə / (BrE)
the uppermost region of the ionosphere, containing the highest concentration of free electrons and important for long-range radio transmission
the highest region of the ionosphere

Examples

Radio operators rely on the Appleton layer to communicate across vast distances.
During the day, the density of electrons in the Appleton layer increases significantly.
The discovery of the Appleton layer revolutionized long-distance radio transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ionosphere - the part of the Earth's upper atmosphere ionized by solar radiation

Etymology

Named after Sir Edward Appleton, British physicist who studied the ionosphere.
