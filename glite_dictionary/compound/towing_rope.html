towing rope

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊɪŋ ˌroʊp / (AmE), / ˈtəʊɪŋ ˌrəʊp / (BrE)
a strong rope used for towing a vessel or vehicle, especially in nautical contexts
rope used for towing boats or vehicles

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to secure the towing rope before the rescue operation began.
Without a sturdy towing rope, the smaller boat wouldn't have managed to pull the disabled yacht.
They checked the towing rope for any frayed strands before connecting it to the tow truck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towline - a rope or cable used in towing
