table d'hote

1. ADJ

/ ˌteɪbəl doʊt / (AmE), / ˌtæbəl ˈdəʊt / (BrE)
(of a meal or menu) offering a complete course or set of courses with limited choices at a fixed price.
complete meal at fixed price

Examples

The restaurant offers a table d'hote menu every evening, including three courses and coffee.
We chose the table d'hote option, which was more affordable than ordering à la carte.
Most of the guests prefer the table d'hote because it simplifies choosing from the menu.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: à la carte - referring to individual menu items priced separately

Etymology

From French 'table d’hôte' (host’s table), originally referring to the host’s table at an inn where guests would eat together.
