tracing routine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsɪŋ ruˈtin / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsɪŋ ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a routine in a computer program that records a chronological sequence of its execution, often for debugging or analysis purposes
routine that records program execution

Examples

The tracing routine captured each function call, making it easier for developers to identify bugs.
During testing, the tracing routine generated a detailed log of the program's execution order.
Many programmers activate a tracing routine when they suspect there are hidden logic errors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debugging routine - a program routine used to assist in identifying and fixing errors
