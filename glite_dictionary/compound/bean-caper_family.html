bean-caper family

1. NOUN

/ ˈbin ˌkeɪpər ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈbiːn ˌkeɪpə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of small trees, shrubs, and herbs (Zygophyllaceae) found in warm, arid, and saline regions, often resinous and sometimes poisonous
family of small often resinous plants (Zygophyllaceae)

Examples

The bean-caper family includes several plants that thrive in desert environments.
Scientists are studying the medicinal properties of some shrubs in the bean-caper family.
Guaiacum and Larrea are well-known genera within the bean-caper family.
