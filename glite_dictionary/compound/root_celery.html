root celery

1. NOUN

/ ruːt ˈsɛləri / (AmE), / ruːt ˈsɛləri / (BrE)
a variety of celery grown for its large, round, edible root rather than its stalks or leaves; also called celeriac.
variety of celery with edible root

Examples

I like to use root celery in soups because it adds a delicious, earthy flavor.
The chef peeled and diced root celery for the vegetable stew.
You can mash root celery as a healthy alternative to potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celeriac - another name for root celery, edible root vegetable
  • Synonym: celery - the common vegetable grown for edible stalks
