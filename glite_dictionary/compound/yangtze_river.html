Yangtze River

1. PROPN

/ ˈjæŋtsi ˌrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈjæŋtsi ˌrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in Asia, flowing eastward from Tibet through China into the East China Sea near Shanghai
longest river of Asia, in China

Examples

Many species unique to the Yangtze River are threatened by pollution and development.
The Yangtze River plays a vital role in China's history, culture, and economy.
They took a cruise along the Yangtze River to admire its breathtaking scenery.

Etymology

From Chinese 長江 (Cháng Jiāng), meaning 'Long River'; 'Yangtze' originates from a local name for a stretch near Yangzhou.
