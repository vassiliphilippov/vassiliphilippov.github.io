open-hearth process

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊpənˈhɑrθ ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌəʊpənˈhɑːθ ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a method for producing steel by melting pig iron and scrap in an open-hearth furnace
steelmaking process using an open-hearth furnace

Examples

The open-hearth process revolutionized steel production in the late nineteenth century.
Many steel mills abandoned the open-hearth process in favor of newer technologies.
The open-hearth process allowed for greater control over the composition of the final steel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Bessemer process - an earlier method of steelmaking using a blast of air
