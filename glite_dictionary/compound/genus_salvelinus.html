genus Salvelinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sælˈvɛlɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs salˈvɛlɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Salmonidae, including the brook trout and other chars.
genus of freshwater fish including chars

Examples

The genus Salvelinus includes several species of cold-water fish, such as the brook trout and Arctic char.
Scientists studied how climate change affects populations within the genus Salvelinus.
Members of the genus Salvelinus are known for their beautiful coloration and preference for clear, cold streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: char - a fish of the genus Salvelinus
  • Synonym: trout - a common name for various freshwater fish, including some in Salvelinus

Etymology

From Latin 'Salvelinus', the genus name, from Old French 'salvelin' (a kind of trout).
