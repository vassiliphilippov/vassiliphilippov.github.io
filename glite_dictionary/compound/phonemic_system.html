phonemic system

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈnimɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / fəˈniːmɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The set of distinct phonemes recognized in a particular language, forming its basic sound system.
set of phonemes in a language
medical

Examples

Every language organizes its sounds differently, using a unique phonemic system.
Children learn the phonemic system of their native language at an early age.
Linguists compare the phonemic system of various languages to understand their similarities and differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of the sound system of a language

Etymology

From 'phonemic' (relating to phonemes) and 'system' (organized set).
