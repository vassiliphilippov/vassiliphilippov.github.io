trust busting

1. NOUN

/ trʌst ˈbʌstɪŋ / (AmE), / trʌst ˈbʌstɪŋ / (BrE)
government actions aimed at breaking up corporate trusts and monopolies, especially under U.S. antitrust laws
government efforts to break monopolies
legal american

Examples

The president made trust busting a key part of his reform agenda.
Early twentieth-century trust busting changed the landscape of American business.
Many historians credit federal trust busting with encouraging competition and protecting consumers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antitrust - prohibiting or regulating monopolies to promote competition
  • Antonym: monopoly - exclusive control by one company over a market or product

Etymology

Formed from 'trust' (large business organization) and 'busting' (breaking up), especially associated with U.S. policies in the early 20th century.
