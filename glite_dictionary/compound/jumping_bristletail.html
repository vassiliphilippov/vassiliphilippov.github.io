jumping bristletail

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ ˈbrɪsəlˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ ˈbrɪs(ə)lˌteɪl / (BrE)
A small, wingless insect found in dark, damp environments such as under dead logs, known for making sudden leaps when disturbed.
small wingless leaping insect

Examples

When disturbed, the jumping bristletail darts away in a surprising leap.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of the jumping bristletail under decaying logs.
A jumping bristletail is often mistaken for a silverfish, but it can jump remarkable distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristletail - a primitive, wingless insect with bristle-like tails
