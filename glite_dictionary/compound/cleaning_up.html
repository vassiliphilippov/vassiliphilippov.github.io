cleaning up

1. NOUN

/ ˌkliːnɪŋ ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌkliːnɪŋ ˈʌp / (BrE)
the act or process of making a place clean by removing dirt, rubbish, or mess
the act of making something clean

Examples

After the party, the cleaning up took almost two hours.
Volunteers did most of the cleaning up after the community event.
The children agreed to help with the cleaning up before they went outside.

Synset Examples

Cleaning up the detoilet was a huge task.
Cleaning up the filth took longer than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean-up - the process of making something clean, often used as a noun
