Yukon Time

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːkɑn taɪm / (AmE), / ˈjuːkɒn taɪm / (BrE)
the standard time in the time zone encompassing the 135th meridian west, formerly used in parts of Alaska and the Yukon territory, equivalent to UTC−9 hours.
time zone, UTC−9 hours (Alaska/Yukon)

Examples

Yukon Time is one hour behind Pacific Standard Time.
The region switched from Yukon Time to Pacific Time in the 1980s.
Television broadcasts were scheduled according to Yukon Time until the change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alaska Time - the standard time zone for Alaska (UTC−9)

Etymology

Named after the Yukon territory in northwestern Canada.
