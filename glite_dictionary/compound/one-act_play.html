one-act play

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌn ˌækt ˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈwʌn ˌækt ˈpleɪ / (BrE)
a short play presented in a single act, without division into multiple acts
short play in a single act

Examples

The theater festival featured a new one-act play written by a local author.
He auditioned for the lead in a one-act play about family secrets.
Many schools choose to perform a one-act play for their annual drama competition.

Synset Examples

The origin of the one-act play may be traced to the very beginning of drama: in ancient Greece, Cyclops, a satyr play by Euripides, is an early example.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play - a dramatic work for the stage
