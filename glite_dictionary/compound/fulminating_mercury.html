fulminating mercury

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʊlməˌneɪtɪŋ ˈmɜrkjʊri / (AmE), / ˌfʊlmɪˌneɪtɪŋ ˈmɜːkjʊri / (BrE)
a highly explosive chemical compound, mercury(II) fulminate, which detonates if struck or heated, used in detonators and percussion caps
explosive compound used in detonators

Examples

The scientist handled fulminating mercury with extreme caution due to its sensitivity to shock.
Old-fashioned ammunition primers often used fulminating mercury as the primary explosive.
If fulminating mercury dries out completely, even a slight tap can trigger an explosion.

Etymology

From 'fulminate', meaning to explode violently, from Latin 'fulminare' (to strike with lightning), and 'mercury', the element.
