Aegadean Isles

1. PROPN

/ ˌiːɡəˈdiːən aɪlz / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəˈdiːən aɪlz / (BrE)
a group of islands off the west coast of Sicily in the Mediterranean Sea, also known as the Egadi Islands
group of islands near Sicily in the Mediterranean

Examples

The Aegadean Isles are known for their crystal-clear waters and rich marine life.
During our summer vacation, we visited the Aegadean Isles and explored ancient ruins.
The Battle of the Aegadean Isles was a decisive victory in Roman history.

Etymology

From Latin 'Aegates', the Latin name for the islands.

2. PROPN

/ ˌiːɡəˈdiːən aɪlz / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəˈdiːən aɪlz / (BrE)
islands west of Sicily, now called the Egadi Islands, known as the site of a decisive Roman naval victory over Carthage in 241 BC that ended the First Punic War
historical name for the Egadi Islands, Italy

Examples

The final battle of the First Punic War was fought near the Aegadean Isles in 241 BC.
Many historians visit the Aegadean Isles to study the ancient naval artifacts found there.
The Aegadean Isles are now known for their beautiful coastline as well as their rich history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egadi Islands - group of islands off the west coast of Sicily, Italy

Etymology

From Latin 'Aegates', the Roman name for the islands.
