rallying point

1. NOUN

/ ˈræliɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a person, idea, or event that serves to unite people, especially those previously divided or scattered
something that unites people

Examples

The old leader became a rallying point for those seeking change.
Their shared goal served as a rallying point in the negotiations.
The monument acts as a rallying point for the local community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focal point - central or most important part or place
  • Antonym: dividing line - a point or factor that separates or divides groups
