genus Delphinium

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.nəs dɛlˈfɪni.əm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.nəs dɛlˈfɪnɪəm / (BrE)
A large genus of mainly perennial, upright, branching herbs from northern temperate regions, some of which are poisonous.
genus of flowering herbs, some poisonous

Examples

The genus Delphinium includes several species known for their showy flowers.
Some plants in the genus Delphinium are toxic if ingested by livestock.
Botanists often study the genus Delphinium to understand its diversity and medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin 'Delphinium', the name of the type genus, from Greek 'delphinion' (larkspur).
