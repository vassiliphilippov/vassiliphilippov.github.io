pay-phone

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ ˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ ˌfəʊn / (BrE)
A public telephone operated by inserting coins or a payment card.
public coin-operated telephone

Examples

She called her friend from a pay-phone because she’d left her mobile at home.
In emergencies, pay-phones can be a lifesaver when your cellphone fails.
The city council is considering removing the last pay-phone booths downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephone - a device for transmitting voices over a distance using wire or radio
