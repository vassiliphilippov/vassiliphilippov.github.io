common maidenhair

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmeɪd(ə)nhɛː / (BrE)
a delicate fern with slender, shining black leaf stalks, found in many parts of the world
a type of delicate fern

Examples

The common maidenhair thrives in humid forest environments, where moisture is abundant.
Botanists often admire the delicate structure of the common maidenhair when conducting fieldwork.
I planted a common maidenhair in my terrarium because of its graceful appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a fern of the genus Adiantum, with delicate leaves and black stems
