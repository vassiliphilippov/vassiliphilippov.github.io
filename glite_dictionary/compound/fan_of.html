fan of

1. NOUN

/ fæn ʌv / (AmE), / fan ɒv / (BrE)
a person who admires or supports a particular person, thing, or activity
someone who admires or supports something

Examples

I'm a huge fan of science fiction movies and never miss any new releases.
She became a fan of the band after hearing their first album.
Many people are fan of Italian cuisine because of its rich flavors and fresh ingredients.

Synset Examples

They are a fan of hiking and explore new trails every weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiast - a person who is very interested in a particular activity or subject
  • Synonym: admirer - someone who admires a person or thing
  • Antonym: critic - someone who expresses disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

"Fan" is an abbreviation of "fanatic," from Latin *fanaticus* (inspired by a deity).
