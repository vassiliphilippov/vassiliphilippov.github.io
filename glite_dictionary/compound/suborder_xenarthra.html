suborder Xenarthra

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈɔrdər ziˈnɑːrθrə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːdə ziːˈnɑːθrə / (BrE)
A suborder of placental mammals that includes armadillos, anteaters, and sloths, native to the Americas.
group of mammals: armadillos, anteaters, sloths

Examples

The suborder Xenarthra comprises mammals such as armadillos, anteaters, and sloths found in Central and South America.
Research on the suborder Xenarthra has revealed fascinating evolutionary adaptations among its members.
Many conservation efforts focus on species within the suborder Xenarthra due to their ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Edentata - old order including anteaters, sloths, and armadillos

Etymology

From New Latin Xenarthra, meaning 'strange joints,' referring to unique vertebral features of these mammals.
