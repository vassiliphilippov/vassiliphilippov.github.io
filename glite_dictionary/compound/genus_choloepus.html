genus Choloepus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈloʊ.ə.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈləʊ.ə.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of sloths in the family Megalonychidae, commonly known as the two-toed sloths.
genus of two-toed sloths

Examples

The genus Choloepus is distinguished from other sloths by having only two toes on their forelimbs.
Researchers have studied the evolution of the genus Choloepus to better understand sloth adaptation.
All living members of the genus Choloepus inhabit the forests of Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-toed sloth - a sloth of the genus Choloepus with two toes on its forelimbs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Choloepus', from Greek elements meaning 'lame foot'.
