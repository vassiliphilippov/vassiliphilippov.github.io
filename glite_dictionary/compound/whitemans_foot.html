whiteman's foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtmənz fʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtmənz fʊt / (BrE)
A common European perennial weed (Plantago major), naturalized in many parts of the world and often found in lawns and roadsides.
a perennial weed, Plantago major

Examples

The yard was covered with whiteman's foot after a wet spring.
She pulled up several whiteman's foot plants from the garden.
Early settlers called Plantago major whiteman's foot because it spread along their paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - the plant Plantago major, also known as white man's foot

Etymology

The name references the belief that the plant spread following the movement of European colonists, i.e. "white men."
