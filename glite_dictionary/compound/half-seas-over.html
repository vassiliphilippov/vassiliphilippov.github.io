half-seas-over

1. ADJ

/ ˌhæfˌsizˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˌsiːzˈəʊvə / (BrE)
British, informal, old-fashioned: drunk; intoxicated.
drunk; intoxicated (old-fashioned, British)
informal british archaic

Examples

After four pints, he was quite half-seas-over and started singing loudly in the pub.
She stumbled through the door, clearly half-seas-over from the party.
In Victorian novels, a gentleman found half-seas-over was rarely welcome at formal gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunk - intoxicated with alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol

Etymology

Probably from nautical slang, originally referring to being 'halfway across the sea', later transferred to a state of inebriation.
