genus Cercocebus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs sərˌkoʊˈsiːbəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɛˌkəʊˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys known as mangabeys, native to Africa.
a genus of African mangabey monkeys

Examples

The genus Cercocebus includes several species of mangabeys found in African forests.
Scientists have studied the behavior of monkeys in the genus Cercocebus to understand primate social structures.
Unlike some other primates, the genus Cercocebus is distinguished by their strong jaws and cheek pouches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangabey - a medium-sized monkey of West Africa with cheek pouches for food storage

Etymology

From New Latin: ‘Cercocebus’, from Greek kerkos (tail) + kēbos (monkey).
