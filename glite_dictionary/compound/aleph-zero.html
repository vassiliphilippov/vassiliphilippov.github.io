aleph-zero

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɛf ˈzɪəroʊ / (AmE), / ˈælɛf ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the cardinal number representing the size of the set of natural numbers; the smallest infinite quantity in set theory, denoted ℵ₀.
the smallest infinite cardinal number

Examples

Set theory defines aleph-zero as the cardinality of all natural numbers.
The concept of aleph-zero is essential to understanding infinite sets in mathematics.
No real-world collection can be said to have aleph-zero members, since it describes an abstract infinity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countable infinity - the concept of an infinite set that can be put into one-to-one correspondence with the natural numbers
  • Degree Higher: aleph-one - the next higher cardinal number after aleph-zero

Etymology

From Hebrew 'aleph' (first letter of the Hebrew alphabet) plus the numeral 'zero'; introduced in mathematical context by Georg Cantor.
