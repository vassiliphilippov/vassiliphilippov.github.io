shellbark hickory

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlˌbɑrk ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlbɑːk ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A North American hickory tree (Carya laciniosa) with loose, grey, shaggy bark and edible nuts.
North American hickory tree with shaggy bark

Examples

The shellbark hickory produces some of the largest edible nuts among native hickories.
We identified a shellbark hickory by its distinctive shaggy bark in the forest.
Carpenters valued the tough wood of the shellbark hickory for making durable tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - A related North American hickory tree with shaggy bark

Etymology

From 'shellbark' describing its bark and 'hickory', an Algonquian-derived name for the genus.
