Pierian spring

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈɪriən sprɪŋ / (AmE), / paɪˈɪəriən sprɪŋ / (BrE)
A source of knowledge or inspiration, especially relating to artistic or intellectual creativity.
source of knowledge or inspiration

Examples

In his search for creativity, the poet turned to the Pierian spring for inspiration.
The teacher hoped her lessons would become a Pierian spring for her students.
Many writers have tried to drink from the Pierian spring to ignite their imagination.

Synset Examples

He believes that education is the Pierian spring of wisdom.
They sought inspiration from the Pierian spring of art.
Her writings draw from the Pierian spring of knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fount of knowledge - a source of great knowledge
  • Synonym: well of inspiration - a source of inspiration

Etymology

From Pieria, a region in ancient Macedonia associated with the Muses in Greek mythology, considered sources of inspiration.
