seborrheic eczema

1. NOUN

/ sɛbəˌriɪk ˈɛk.sɪmə / (AmE), / sɛbəˌriːɪk ˈɛk.sɪmə / (BrE)
A chronic skin condition characterized by red, inflamed skin with greasy scales, typically occurring on the scalp, eyelids, or other areas rich in oil glands.
chronic skin condition with greasy scales
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed her with seborrheic eczema after noticing the greasy, scaly patches on her scalp.
Managing seborrheic eczema often requires medicated shampoos and creams to reduce inflammation and flaking.
Babies can also develop seborrheic eczema, commonly known as cradle cap, on their heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seborrheic dermatitis - a similar term for the same or closely related skin condition

Etymology

From Greek 'sebum' (fat, tallow) and 'eczein' (to boil out); related to the production of oil by skin glands.
