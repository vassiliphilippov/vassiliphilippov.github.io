African hemp

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.rɪ.kən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈæf.rɪ.kən hemp / (BrE)
A large shrub native to South Africa with many hairy branches, large hairy leaves, and clusters of conspicuous white flowers.
South African shrub with hairy leaves and white flowers

Examples

The African hemp is often grown in gardens for its ornamental white flowers.
You can recognize African hemp by its large, conspicuously hairy leaves and branches.
In some regions, African hemp is used as a natural hedge due to its dense growth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæfrɪkən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈæfrɪkən hɛmp / (BrE)
A strong, fibrous plant of the genus Sansevieria native to South Africa, used historically for making bowstrings and other fibers.
fibrous South African plant (Sansevieria)

Examples

The fibers of African hemp were traditionally used to make strong ropes and twine.
Gardeners often grow African hemp as an ornamental plant for its striking leaves.
Researchers are studying the potential uses of African hemp in sustainable materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sansevieria - a genus of tropical African plants known for their fibrous leaves
