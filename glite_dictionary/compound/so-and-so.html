so-and-so

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ ənd ˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ ənd ˌsəʊ / (BrE)
used to refer to a particular person whose name is not known, remembered, or mentioned; an unspecified individual.
unspecified or unnamed person

Examples

I bumped into so-and-so at the coffee shop, but couldn't remember his name.
She called and said that so-and-so from accounting needed those documents by noon.
Whenever you forget a name, you can just say so-and-so instead.

Synset Examples

I saw so-and-so at the event last night.
I can’t remember the name of so-and-so from the meeting.
Did you hear what so-and-so did over the weekend?
