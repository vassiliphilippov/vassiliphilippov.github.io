strategic military decision

1. NOUN

/ strəˈtiːdʒɪk ˌmɪlɪˈtɛri dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk ˌmɪlɪˈtəri dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a planned choice or judgment made to gain military advantage or achieve specific goals in military operations
plan or judgment for military advantage

Examples

The general's strategic military decision changed the course of the campaign.
Making a strategic military decision requires careful consideration of risks and potential gains.
History students analyzed each strategic military decision made during the conflict.

Synset Examples

Tojo was known for his strategic military decisions.
