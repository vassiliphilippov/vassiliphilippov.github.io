general delivery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚəl dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
a service at a post office where mail is held for people to collect in person, rather than being delivered to their address
post office mail-holding service
american

Examples

I had my letters sent to general delivery while I was traveling across the country.
If you don’t have a permanent address, you can collect your mail at general delivery.
He went to the post office every week to check the general delivery window.
