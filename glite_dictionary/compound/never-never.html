never-never

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛvɚ ˈnɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɛvə ˈnɛvə / (BrE)
an arrangement for paying for something in regular installments over a period of time, rather than paying the full amount immediately
installment payment plan
british informal

Examples

They couldn't afford to buy the television outright, so they got it on the never-never.
Many people fell into debt after purchasing appliances on the never-never.
My father warned me against buying anything on the never-never.

Synset Examples

we bought a car on the never-never

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment plan - a system for paying for goods in regular parts over time

Etymology

Originated in British slang; possibly from the idea of the debt never being fully paid off ('never, never' being a playful repetition for emphasis).
