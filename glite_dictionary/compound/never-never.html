Never-Never

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛvər ˈnɛvər / (AmE), / ˈnɛvə ˈnɛvə / (BrE)
a remote and sparsely inhabited area of the Australian outback, often regarded as wild or inhospitable
remote Australian outback region
british

Examples

Few people have ever ventured into the Never-Never, where the land stretches for hundreds of miles without a sign of civilization.
After months in the crowded city, she longed for the silence and space of the Never-Never.
Explorers faced countless dangers crossing the harsh and unforgiving Never-Never in the early days of settlement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outback - the remote and usually uninhabited inland regions of Australia

Etymology

From Australian English, first recorded in the late 19th century; origin uncertain, possibly from the idea of a place one would 'never, never' go or return from.
