feral person

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrəl ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˈfɪərəl ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a person who has lived isolated from human contact from a very young age and is not socialized according to human norms
person lacking socialization

Examples

The story of the feral person found living in the woods fascinated researchers and the public alike.
Having grown up alone in the wild, he exhibited many behaviors typical of a feral person.
The psychologist explained that a feral person might never fully adapt to normal human society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild child - a child who has lived isolated from human contact and lacks socialization
  • Antonym: civilized person - a person who conforms to the social norms and expectations of society

Etymology

From Latin 'fera' (wild animal), with 'feral' meaning wild or undomesticated; used in English to describe people lacking socialization.
