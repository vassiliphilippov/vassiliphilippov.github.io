steam boiler

1. NOUN

/ stiːm ˈbɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / stiːm ˈbɔɪlə / (BrE)
a sealed container in which water is heated until it becomes steam, especially for providing power in engines or heating systems
sealed vessel generating steam from water

Examples

The old factory still uses a steam boiler to power its machinery.
Routine maintenance is essential to ensure the steam boiler operates safely and efficiently.
The explosion was caused by a build-up of pressure in the steam boiler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boiler - vessel for heating fluids, typically to generate steam
  • Synonym: steam generator - device that produces steam
