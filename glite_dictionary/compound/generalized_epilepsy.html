generalized epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəˌlaɪzd ɛˈpɪləpsi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪzd ɪˈpɪləsi / (BrE)
A type of epilepsy characterized by seizures that involve loss of consciousness and tonic spasms of the muscles, followed by generalized jerking of the body.
type of epilepsy with widespread seizures
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with generalized epilepsy after she experienced several severe seizures.
People living with generalized epilepsy may have seizures that affect both sides of the brain.
His treatment plan was adjusted to better control his generalized epilepsy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: focal epilepsy - a type of epilepsy characterized by seizures originating in a specific region of the brain
