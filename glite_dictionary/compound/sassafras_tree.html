sassafras tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæsəˌfræs triː / (AmE), / ˈsasəˌfras triː / (BrE)
a North American deciduous tree with aromatic leaves and bark, used as a source of sassafras oil
North American aromatic tree

Examples

The bark of the sassafras tree has long been used to make tea and traditional medicines.
We spotted a sassafras tree in the woods, easily recognized by its distinctive leaves.
The aromatic oil extracted from the sassafras tree was once common in root beer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sassafras - the plant genus or its aromatic substance

Etymology

From Spanish 'sasafrás', from French 'sassafras', of unknown origin.
