in the wake of

1. ADP

/ ɪn ðə weɪk ʌv / (AmE), / ɪn ðə weɪk ɒv / (BrE)
occurring as a result of or following closely after (an event or situation)
following as a result of something

Examples

There was increased security in the wake of the recent attacks.
Several new policies were introduced in the wake of the scandal.
People remained cautious in the wake of last year's financial crisis.

Synset Examples

In the wake of Gloriagate, public trust plummeted.
In the wake of his departure, a deafening silence lingered.
She started a new project in the wake of the crisis.
In the wake of the storm, we assessed the damage.
Many changes happened in the wake of the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: following - coming after in order or time

Etymology

From the nautical term 'wake', meaning the trail left in water by a moving ship.
