heavy flak

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi flæk / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi flæk / (BrE)
intense criticism or strong public backlash directed at a person or action.
strong criticism; intense backlash

Examples

The minister came under heavy flak after the budget cuts were announced.
She faced heavy flak from the media for her outspoken remarks.
The company's new policy has drawn heavy flak from environmental groups.

Synset Examples

The politician received heavy flak for his controversial comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flak - criticism or hostile reaction
  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'flak', originally referring to anti-aircraft fire (from German 'Fliegerabwehrkanone'), to mean verbal attack.
