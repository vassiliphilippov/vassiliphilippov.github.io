compass plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmpəs plænt / (AmE), / ˈkʌmpəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A North American annual plant with red or rose-colored flowers, known for its distinctive orientation to the sun.
North American plant with colorful flowers

Examples

The compass plant is easily recognized by its tall stalk and vivid flowers.
Gardeners often plant the compass plant for its hardiness and unique leaf alignment.
Because it thrives in prairie settings, the compass plant is a favorite in restoration projects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmpəs plænt / (AmE), / ˈkʌmpəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant, especially of the genus Silphium, whose leaves align in a north-south direction, traditionally believed to indicate the cardinal points
plant with cardinal point-aligned leaves

Examples

The compass plant is native to the American prairies and can be used to find direction.
Pioneers believed that the leaves of the compass plant would always point north and south.
He studied the orientation of the compass plant to understand how plants interact with sunlight.
