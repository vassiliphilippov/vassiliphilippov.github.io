Caley pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪli piː / (AmE), / ˈkeɪli piː / (BrE)
A weak-stemmed, winter annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, grown in southern United States as a cover or pasture crop.
Mediterranean winter annual crop plant

Examples

The Caley pea is often planted to improve soil quality in the off-season.
Farmers use Caley pea as forage for livestock in southern regions.
Planting Caley peas helps prevent soil erosion during the winter months.
