Labrador tea

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæbrəˌdɔr ˈti / (AmE), / ˌlæbrəˌdɔː ˈtiː / (BrE)
an evergreen North American shrub with white or creamy bell-shaped flowers and dark green hairy leaves, sometimes used to make tea
North American shrub used for tea

Examples

Early settlers brewed Labrador tea from the leaves during harsh winters.
The Labrador tea plant has hairy evergreen leaves and clusters of small white flowers.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Labrador tea in traditional remedies.
