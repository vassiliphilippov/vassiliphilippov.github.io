modern therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdərn ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˈmɒdn̩ ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the current methods and practices used in psychological treatment, especially as distinguished from older or traditional approaches
contemporary psychological treatment methods

Examples

Recent advances in neuroscience have greatly influenced modern therapy techniques.
Many practitioners of modern therapy incorporate mindfulness into their sessions.
Despite new approaches, some principles of modern therapy are rooted in traditional theories.

Synset Examples

Sigmund's work is still relevant in modern therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychotherapy - the treatment of mental disorder by psychological means
