good humour

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ˈhjumər / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
a cheerful and pleasant mood or temperament; a state of being in high spirits and easy to get along with
cheerful and pleasant mood

Examples

Despite the delays, she maintained her good humour throughout the evening.
His good humour made everyone at the party feel comfortable and relaxed.
A sense of good humour helps people cope with difficult situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: bad humour - a state of being irritable or annoyed
