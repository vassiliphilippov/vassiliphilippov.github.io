barking frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrkɪŋ frɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːkɪŋ frɒɡ / (BrE)
a species of frog native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, noted for its call that resembles a dog's bark
type of frog with a bark-like call

Examples

The barking frog is often heard at night during the rainy season in Arizona.
Researchers recorded the unique sound of a barking frog for their wildlife study.
Unlike most frogs, the barking frog's call can be mistaken for a small dog.
