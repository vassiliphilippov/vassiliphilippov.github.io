Corn Belt

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn bɛlt / (AmE), / kɔːn bɛlt / (BrE)
an area of the Midwestern United States where corn is the predominant crop, especially known for high agricultural productivity
region in the Midwest U.S. where corn is widely grown
american

Examples

Farmers in the Corn Belt depend heavily on weather patterns for their annual harvest.
Iowa is at the heart of the Corn Belt, producing millions of tons of corn each year.
Economic changes in the Corn Belt can impact food prices across the country.
