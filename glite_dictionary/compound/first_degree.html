first degree

1. NOUN

/ fɜrst dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / fɜːst dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
the degree of one, especially referring to a polynomial or equation whose highest variable exponent is one
degree of one (mathematics)

Examples

A first degree equation is known as a linear equation.
The simplest polynomials are those of first degree.
All variables in this system are of first degree.

Synset Examples

all of the terms in a linear equation are of the first degree

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: second degree - degree of two, especially for quadratic equations
  • Synonym: linear - in mathematics, of or relating to the first degree
