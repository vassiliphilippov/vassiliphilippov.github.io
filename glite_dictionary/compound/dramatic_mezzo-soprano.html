dramatic mezzo-soprano

1. NOUN

/ drəˈmæt̬ɪk ˌmet.soʊ səˈprɑː.noʊ / (AmE), / drəˈmæt.ɪk ˌmet.səʊ səˈprɑː.nəʊ / (BrE)
A type of mezzo-soprano voice with a strong, rich tone capable of singing over an orchestra, typically ranging from the F below middle C to the G two octaves above.
operatic mezzo-soprano voice type

Examples

The role requires a dramatic mezzo-soprano with a powerful voice and commanding stage presence.
Many Verdi operas feature parts written for a dramatic mezzo-soprano, demanding both strength and subtlety.
She trained for years to develop into a true dramatic mezzo-soprano capable of performing challenging roles.

Synset Examples

The dramatic mezzo-soprano can sing over an orchestra and chorus with ease and was often used in the 19th century opera, to portray older women, mothers, witches and evil characters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mezzo-soprano - a female singing voice with a range between soprano and contralto
  • Synonym: dramatic soprano - a powerful soprano voice suited for dramatic roles

Etymology

Mezzo-soprano from Italian 'mezzo' (middle) + 'soprano' (high); dramatic from Greek 'dramatikos' (pertaining to drama).
