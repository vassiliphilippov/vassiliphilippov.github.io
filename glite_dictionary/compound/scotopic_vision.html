scotopic vision

1. NOUN

/ skoʊˈtɑpɪk ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / skəʊˈtɒpɪk ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the ability to see in very low light conditions, such as at night or in dim illumination.
ability to see in low light
medical

Examples

Owls possess highly developed scotopic vision, allowing them to hunt efficiently at night.
When entering a dark room, it takes time for your scotopic vision to fully adapt.
Astronomers rely on their scotopic vision when working with faint stars and distant galaxies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: photopic vision - vision under well-lit conditions, primarily using cone cells

Etymology

From Greek 'skotos' (darkness) and '-opia' (vision).
