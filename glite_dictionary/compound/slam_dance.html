slam dance

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæm ˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈslæm ˌdɑːns / (BrE)
a style of dancing to punk rock music in which participants deliberately bump or slam into one another
punk rock dance style involving slamming into others

Examples

During the concert, the fans started to slam dance in front of the stage.
She learned how to slam dance at her first punk rock show.
Some venues prohibit slam dancing because of safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mosh - a similar form of dance involving energetic and rough movement

Etymology

Formed from 'slam' (to strike forcefully) + 'dance'.

2. VERB

/ ˈslæm ˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈslæm ˌdɑːns / (BrE)
to dance in a style involving energetic and often violent movements, typically by slamming into other dancers, especially associated with punk rock or hardcore music.
dance by slamming into others (punk style)

Examples

At the concert, dozens of fans began to slam dance near the front of the stage.
He used to slam dance at hardcore punk shows in his youth.
The crowd cleared a circle on the floor so everyone could slam dance safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mosh - to dance in a similar aggressive style, especially at rock concerts

Etymology

From 'slam' (to strike forcefully) + 'dance'. Originated in the punk subculture of the late 1970s and 1980s.
