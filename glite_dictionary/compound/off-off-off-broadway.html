off-off-off-Broadway

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔf ˌɔf ˌɔf ˈbrɔdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒf ˌɒf ˌɒf ˈbrɔːdˌweɪ / (BrE)
Describing a theater production that is highly experimental or independent, performed in a venue far removed from the mainstream New York theater scene, and even smaller or less commercial than typical off-off-Broadway productions.
very experimental, independent NYC theater

Examples

Several artists got their start acting in off-off-off-Broadway plays before moving to bigger stages.
The director's new off-off-off-Broadway experiment pushed the boundaries of live theater.
She wrote and produced an off-off-off-Broadway musical in an old warehouse downtown.

Synset Examples

The festival featured innovative off-off-off-Broadway productions.
She dreams of starring in an off-off-off-Broadway show.
They staged an off-off-off-Broadway play in a tiny basement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: off-Broadway - theatrical productions in smaller NYC venues than Broadway
  • Degree Lower: off-off-Broadway - very small or experimental NYC theatrical venues

Etymology

Formed by repeating 'off-' to indicate increasing distance from mainstream Broadway, referencing New York theater districts.
