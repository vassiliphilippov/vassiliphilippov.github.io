mosquito hawk

1. NOUN

/ məˈskitoʊ hɔk / (AmE), / məˈskiːtəʊ hɔːk / (BrE)
A large, slender-bodied insect with iridescent wings, known for preying on mosquitoes and similar insects; commonly refers to a dragonfly.
a predatory insect, usually a dragonfly

Examples

The pond was full of mosquito hawks darting above the water at dusk.
Gardeners welcome mosquito hawks because they help control the mosquito population.
I watched a mosquito hawk catch its prey in midair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - a predatory flying insect of the order Odonata

2. NOUN

/ məˈskiːtoʊ hɔk / (AmE), / məˈskiːtəʊ hɔːk / (BrE)
A mainly nocturnal North American bird, also known as the nighthawk, which feeds on insects.
a nocturnal insect-eating bird (nighthawk)
informal american

Examples

Many people believe that the mosquito hawk helps control pest populations by eating insects at night.
We spotted a mosquito hawk swooping for bugs in the dusky evening sky.
Farmers appreciate the presence of the mosquito hawk during warm summer nights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nighthawk - a North American nocturnal insect-eating bird
