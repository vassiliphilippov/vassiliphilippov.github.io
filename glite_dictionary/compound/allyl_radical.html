allyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈælaɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈælɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
the univalent, unsaturated organic radical C3H5, derived from propylene
univalent organic radical C3H5

Examples

The allyl radical is frequently encountered in organic synthesis reactions.
Free allyl radical intermediates play a crucial role in the mechanism of this process.
The stability of the allyl radical is greater than that of most alkyl radicals due to resonance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allyl group - the group C3H5 as a substituent in organic compounds

Etymology

From 'allyl', relating to the group derived from allyl alcohol, plus 'radical', a term in chemistry for a group of atoms.
