golf widow

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑlf ˈwɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ɡɒlf ˈwɪdəʊ / (BrE)
a woman whose husband is often absent because he spends a lot of time playing golf
woman neglected due to husband's golfing

Examples

After her husband joined the country club, she became a classic golf widow.
Many golf widows joke about taking up new hobbies while their partners are on the course.
Being a golf widow can be lonely during tournament season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass widow - a woman whose husband is away from her, especially for extended periods

Etymology

Modeled after 'grass widow'; combines 'golf' and 'widow' to express the sense of social abandonment.
