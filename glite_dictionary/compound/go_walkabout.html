to go walkabout

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈwɔːkəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈwɔːkəbaʊt / (BrE)
to travel or roam freely, often for the sake of exploration or self-discovery, typically without a fixed destination
to wander or travel for exploration or self-discovery
informal

Examples

After losing his job, Tom decided to go walkabout through South America in search of new experiences.
Sometimes, when stressed, she likes to go walkabout in the nearby forest to clear her mind.
They plan to go walkabout this summer and see where the road takes them.

Synset Examples

After finishing college, he decided to go walkabout in Australia.
Every summer, she likes to go walkabout in the countryside.
He plans to go walkabout for a few months to find himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to move about without a fixed course or destination
  • Synonym: to explore - to travel through an area to learn about it

Etymology

From Australian Aboriginal English 'walkabout', meaning a period of wandering in the bush; the phrase evolved in general English.
