gopher snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊfər ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊfə ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a large nonvenomous North American snake (Pituophis catenifer), often found in burrows, with dark markings on a lighter body, native to southern North America and Mexico
a large nonvenomous North American snake

Examples

The gopher snake is often mistaken for a rattlesnake because of its similar markings.
A gopher snake slithered into the burrow looking for rodents to eat.
Farmers appreciate the presence of gopher snakes because they help control pest populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullsnake - another large nonvenomous snake closely related to the gopher snake

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊfər sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊfə sneɪk / (BrE)
A large, nonvenomous snake of western North America, especially known for inhabiting rodent burrows.
large nonvenomous western North American snake

Examples

The gopher snake is often mistaken for a rattlesnake because of its similar appearance and defensive behavior.
We spotted a gopher snake sunning itself near the edge of the field.
Gopher snakes help control rodent populations by invading their burrows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull snake - a large subspecies of gopher snake found in central United States

Etymology

Named for its tendency to enter gopher and other rodent burrows.
