bittersweet chocolate

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪt̬ɚˈswit ˈtʃɑk.lət / (AmE), / ˌbɪtəˈswiːt ˈtʃɒk.lət / (BrE)
A type of dark chocolate made from chocolate liquor with added cocoa butter, containing small amounts of sugar and usually some vanilla and lecithin.
type of dark chocolate with little sugar

Examples

She prefers bittersweet chocolate in her desserts because it is less sweet than milk chocolate.
The recipe calls for four ounces of bittersweet chocolate to be melted and mixed into the batter.
He bought a bar of bittersweet chocolate to snack on during his afternoon break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark chocolate - chocolate containing a high percentage of cocoa solids and little or no milk
