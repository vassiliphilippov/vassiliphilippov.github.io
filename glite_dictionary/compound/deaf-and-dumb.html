deaf-and-dumb

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛf ən ˈdʌm / (AmE), / ˌdɛf ən ˈdʌm / (BrE)
(offensive, dated) Lacking the ability to hear and speak; unable to hear or speak.
unable to hear or speak (dated, offensive)
offensive archaic

Examples

In the past, children who were deaf-and-dumb often faced significant social barriers.
The novel featured a character described as deaf-and-dumb, reflecting the language of the time.
It is now considered offensive to refer to someone as deaf-and-dumb; use 'deaf' or 'mute' instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deaf - unable to hear
  • Synonym: mute - unable to speak
