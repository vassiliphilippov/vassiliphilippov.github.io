germ cell

1. NOUN

/ dʒɝm sɛl / (AmE), / dʒɜːm sɛl / (BrE)
a reproductive cell (such as a sperm or egg) that transmits genetic information to offspring
reproductive cell (sperm or egg)
medical

Examples

Sperm and eggs are both types of germ cells that carry genetic material to the next generation.
A mutation in a germ cell can be passed on to children, unlike one in a somatic cell.
The process of fertilization involves the union of two germ cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: somatic cell - any cell of the body except reproductive cells
