short selling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrt ˌsɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt ˌsɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the sale of securities or commodities that the seller does not own, with the intention of buying them back later at a lower price to make a profit
selling securities or commodities one does not own

Examples

Many investors practice short selling to profit when they anticipate a stock’s price will drop.
The financial regulator imposed a ban on short selling during periods of high market volatility.
He lost a significant amount of money due to unsuccessful short selling strategies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: buying long - purchasing securities expecting their value to rise

Etymology

From 'short' (inadequate, lacking) + 'selling'; financial usage since the 19th century.
