Aegadian Islands

1. PROPN

/ iːˈɡeɪdiən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / iːˈɡeɪdiən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands west of Sicily, known today as the Egadi Islands, famous as the site of a decisive Roman naval victory over Carthage in 241 BC
island group west of Sicily, also Egadi Islands

Examples

The Aegadian Islands are a popular destination for tourists seeking natural beauty and history.
The decisive battle that ended the First Punic War occurred near the Aegadian Islands.
Researchers are studying shipwrecks around the Aegadian Islands to learn more about ancient naval warfare.

Etymology

From Latin 'Aegates Insulae', the ancient name of the islands.

2. PROPN

/ iːˈɡeɪdiən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / iːˈɡeɪdiən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of small islands off the west coast of Sicily in the Mediterranean Sea
group of islands near Sicily

Examples

The Aegadian Islands are known for their crystal-clear waters and rich marine life.
We took a ferry from Trapani to visit the Aegadian Islands during our vacation.
Archaeologists have found ancient shipwrecks near the Aegadian Islands.

Etymology

From Italian 'Isole Egadi', from Latin 'Aegates Insulae'.
