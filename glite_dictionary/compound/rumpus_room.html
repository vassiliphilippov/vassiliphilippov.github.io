rumpus room

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmpəs ruːm / (AmE), / ˈrʌmpəs ruːm / (BrE)
a room in a house set aside for recreation and noisy activities, especially children's play or parties
recreation room for noisy activities

Examples

The children spent the afternoon playing games in the rumpus room.
We turned the basement into a rumpus room for family parties and movie nights.
After dinner, the guests moved to the rumpus room to relax and play table tennis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playroom - a room set aside for children's activities and play

Etymology

From 'rumpus' (commotion, noise), origin uncertain, + 'room'. Usage dates to mid-20th century.
