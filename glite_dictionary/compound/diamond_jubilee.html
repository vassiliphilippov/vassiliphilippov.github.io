diamond jubilee

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəmənd dʒuːˈbɪli / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd dʒuːˈbiːliː / (BrE)
the 60th anniversary of an important event, especially the reign or foundation of a monarch or institution, or a celebration of this anniversary
60th anniversary celebration

Examples

The city held a grand parade to mark its diamond jubilee since its founding in 1863.
Queen Elizabeth II celebrated her diamond jubilee with events across the United Kingdom.
The university's diamond jubilee was commemorated with a series of lectures and exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: golden jubilee - 50th anniversary celebration
  • Degree Higher: platinum jubilee - 70th anniversary celebration

Etymology

Formed from 'diamond' as a symbol of durability and 'jubilee' (from Latin 'jubilaeus', relating to celebration).
