exchange premium

1. NOUN

/ ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈpriːmiəm / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈpriːmiəm / (BrE)
a fee charged for converting one currency into another, often reflecting the cost above the standard exchange rate
fee for exchanging currencies

Examples

Travelers often pay an exchange premium when converting cash at airports.
The exchange premium can significantly increase the cost of international transactions.
Banks may charge an exchange premium in addition to the basic exchange rate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commission - a fee for a service, often in finance or business
