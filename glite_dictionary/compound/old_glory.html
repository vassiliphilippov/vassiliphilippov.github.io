Old Glory

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈɡlɔri / (AmE), / əʊld ˈɡlɔːri / (BrE)
a nickname for the national flag of the United States of America
nickname for the U.S. flag

Examples

Hundreds of people gathered to watch Old Glory being raised at the ceremony.
He always displays Old Glory on national holidays.
The sight of Old Glory flying high fills her with pride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stars and Stripes - another nickname for the U.S. flag

Etymology

Coined by American sea captain William Driver in 1831 for his flag; later adopted as a popular nickname.
