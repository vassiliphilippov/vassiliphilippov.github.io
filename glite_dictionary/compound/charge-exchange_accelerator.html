charge-exchange accelerator

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrdʒ ɪksˈʧeɪndʒ əkˌsɛləˈreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːdʒ ɪksˈʧeɪndʒ əkˌsɛləˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a machine that accelerates ions, allowing high-energy ions to escape from plasma following a charge exchange process
device for accelerating ions via charge exchange

Examples

The laboratory installed a new charge-exchange accelerator to improve their plasma research capabilities.
High-energy ions are collected using a charge-exchange accelerator during fusion experiments.
Scientists discussed the design challenges of the charge-exchange accelerator at the physics conference.
