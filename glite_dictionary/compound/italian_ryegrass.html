Italian ryegrass

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæliən ˈraɪˌɡræs / (AmE), / ɪˈtæliən ˈraɪɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European grass (Lolium multiflorum) widely cultivated for hay, turf, and as green manure, especially in the United States
a grass used for hay, turf, and green manure

Examples

Italian ryegrass is often planted in pastures for its high yield and quick growth.
The farmer chose Italian ryegrass for green manure to improve the soil's fertility.
Many lawns in the region are seeded with Italian ryegrass due to its lush green appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ryegrass - a type of grass of the genus Lolium used for lawns and fodder
