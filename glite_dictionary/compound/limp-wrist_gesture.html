limp-wrist gesture

1. NOUN

/ lɪmp rɪst ˈdʒɛstʃər / (AmE), / lɪmp rɪst ˈdʒɛstʃə / (BrE)
A gesture made by letting one's wrist hang loosely, often used to convey sarcasm, mockery, or affectation.
gesture with a loosely hanging wrist

Examples

She made a limp-wrist gesture to signal her sarcastic disapproval.
The comedian was criticized for using the limp-wrist gesture in his act.
His exaggerated limp-wrist gesture drew laughter from the audience, though some found it offensive.

Synset Examples

The limp-wrist gesture was meant to convey sarcasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mocking gesture - a gesture intended to mock
