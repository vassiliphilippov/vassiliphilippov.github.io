skin cell

1. NOUN

/ skɪn sɛl / (AmE), / skɪn sɛl / (BrE)
any of the various cells that form the skin, such as keratinocytes, melanocytes, or Langerhans cells
a cell that makes up the skin

Examples

The outer layer of your body is constantly shedding skin cells as part of the renewal process.
Researchers grew new skin cells in the lab for use in grafting onto burn victims.
Daily exfoliation helps remove dead skin cells and keeps your complexion clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidermal cell - a cell forming part of the outer layer of the skin
  • Degree Lower: keratinocyte - the main type of cell found in the epidermis
