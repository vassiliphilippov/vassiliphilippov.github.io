radioactive material

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˌoʊˈæktɪv məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
A substance that contains atoms which emit radiation as a result of radioactive decay.
substance that emits radiation

Examples

Highly radioactive material must be handled with extreme caution to prevent exposure.
The power plant stores its radioactive material in heavily shielded containers.
Environmental agencies monitor sites for illegal dumping of radioactive material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radioisotope - an isotope of an element that emits radiation
  • Degree Higher: radiation - energy emitted from radioactive substances
