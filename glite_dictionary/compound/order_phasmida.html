order Phasmida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər fæsˈmɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə fæsˈmɪdə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of insects including stick insects and leaf insects, sometimes regarded as a suborder of Orthoptera.
taxonomic order of stick and leaf insects

Examples

Scientists classify stick insects under the order Phasmida due to their unique body shapes.
Many leaf insects, which resemble leaves for camouflage, are included in the order Phasmida.
Some entomologists debate whether the order Phasmida should be a suborder of Orthoptera.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Orthoptera - order including grasshoppers, crickets, and related insects
  • Synonym: stick insect - insect belonging to the order Phasmida, known for their stick-like appearance

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Phasma' (genus name) and '-ida' (order suffix).
