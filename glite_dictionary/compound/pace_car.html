pace car

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪs kɑr / (AmE), / ˈpeɪs kɑː / (BrE)
A high-performance vehicle that leads a field of racing cars around the track before the start of a race, then leaves the course.
car that leads racers before start

Examples

The pace car led the drivers around the track for one lap before the race began.
When the pace car pulled off, the racers accelerated to top speed.
If there is an accident on the track, a pace car may be deployed to control the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety car - car used to control speed during accidents or hazards in racing
