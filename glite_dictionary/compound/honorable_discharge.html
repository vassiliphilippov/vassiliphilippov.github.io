honorable discharge

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑnərəbəl dɪsˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbəl dɪsˌtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an official release from the armed forces, given when a service member has completed their duties with a commendable record
military release with commendable record
legal

Examples

After serving for four years, he was granted an honorable discharge from the army.
She proudly displayed her honorable discharge certificate on the wall.
Receiving an honorable discharge can help veterans access certain benefits and opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dishonorable discharge - a release from military service for serious misconduct
  • Degree Lower: general discharge - a military discharge for satisfactory but not exemplary service
