selling point

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an aspect or feature of a product or service that makes it appealing or attractive to prospective buyers
feature making something attractive to buyers

Examples

The spacious backyard is a major selling point for this house.
Energy efficiency is an important selling point for modern appliances.
One key selling point of the restaurant is its organic menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantage - a beneficial feature or circumstance
  • Antonym: drawback - a feature that makes something less attractive

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an aspect or feature of a product or service that makes it particularly attractive to buyers or customers
feature that attracts buyers

Examples

The new smartphone's camera quality was its biggest selling point.
Location is often a key selling point when buying a house.
One unique selling point of their program is the lifetime support they offer.

Synset Examples

The main selling point of the car is its fuel efficiency.
The warranty is a strong selling point for many customers.
Her selling point was the unique design of her artwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawcard - an attractive feature that draws people in
  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable aspect that deters buyers
