Dutch Guiana

1. PROPN

/ dʌtʃ ɡiˈænə / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ɡiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
the former name for Suriname, a country in northeastern South America that gained independence from the Netherlands in 1975
former name for Suriname

Examples

The colony of Dutch Guiana was under Dutch control until the mid-20th century.
Many immigrants came to Dutch Guiana to work on plantations during the colonial era.
In 1975, Dutch Guiana became the independent nation of Suriname.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Suriname - country in northeastern South America, formerly known as Dutch Guiana

Etymology

From 'Dutch' (of the Netherlands) and 'Guiana,' the regional name given to parts of northern South America.
