Br'er Rabbit

1. NOUN

/ brɛr ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / brɛː ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a fictional rabbit who appears as a clever trickster in folk tales collected by Joel Chandler Harris, originally attributed to oral African-American storytelling traditions
fictional clever trickster rabbit in folklore

Examples

As a child, I loved reading about the adventures of Br'er Rabbit.
The stories of Br'er Rabbit often feature him outsmarting his enemies with clever tricks.
Many American folktales include animals like Br'er Rabbit as main characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uncle Remus - the fictional narrator of collected folklore tales featuring Br'er Rabbit

Etymology

'Br'er' is a dialectal contraction of 'brother'; character popularized in the Uncle Remus stories by Joel Chandler Harris (1880), drawing on African and Cherokee folklore.
