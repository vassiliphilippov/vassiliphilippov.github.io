potassium carbonate

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtæsiəm ˈkɑrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / pəˈtæsiəm ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white, water-soluble salt (K₂CO₃) that is basic in solution, used in the manufacture of glass, soap, and cleaning agents
a basic white salt used in glass and cleaning agents

Examples

The laboratory technician added potassium carbonate to the mixture to increase its alkalinity.
Manufacturers use potassium carbonate in the production of specialty glass.
If you are making soap from scratch, you might need potassium carbonate as an ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium carbonate - a similar white salt (Na₂CO₃), used in cleaning and glassmaking

Etymology

From 'potassium', after the element, and 'carbonate', referring to compounds containing carbon and oxygen.
