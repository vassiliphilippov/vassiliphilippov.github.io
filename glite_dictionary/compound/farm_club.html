farm club

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrm ˌklʌb / (AmE), / ˈfɑːm ˌklʌb / (BrE)
a minor-league sports team that is owned or controlled by a major-league team, especially in baseball, to develop players for the higher league
minor-league team owned by a major-league team
american

Examples

The young pitcher spent two years playing for the team's farm club before making his major league debut.
Many future stars hone their skills with a farm club affiliated with a big league team.
The organization announced the promotion of several players from its farm club after a successful season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minor league - a league below the major leagues in professional sports
  • Antonym: major league - the highest professional league in a sport

Etymology

From 'farm' (as in a place where something is cultivated) and 'club' (sports association); refers to cultivating talent for the major leagues.
