typical jerboa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪpɪkəl dʒərˈboʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈtɪpɪkəl dʒɜːˈbəʊ.ə / (BrE)
a small, nocturnal, jumping rodent with long hind legs, found in arid regions of Asia and northern Africa
small jumping rodent of Asia and northern Africa

Examples

The typical jerboa uses its long hind legs to leap across the desert sand.
A typical jerboa can cover surprising distances in a single bound.
Naturalists observed a typical jerboa foraging for seeds beneath the moonlit sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerboa - small hopping desert rodent

Etymology

From French 'jerboas', from Arabic 'yarbū‘'.
