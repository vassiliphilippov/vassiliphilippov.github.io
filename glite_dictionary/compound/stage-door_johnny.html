stage-door Johnny

1. NOUN

/ ˌsteɪdʒ dɔr ˈdʒɑni / (AmE), / ˌsteɪdʒ dɔː ˈdʒɒni / (BrE)
a man who waits outside a theater stage door to meet or get autographs from performers, usually as an enthusiastic fan
enthusiastic male theater fan waiting at stage door

Examples

After every performance, a stage-door Johnny would present flowers to the lead actress.
The theater's security recognized the familiar stage-door Johnnies outside after the show.
Back in the 1920s, being a stage-door Johnny was a popular way to meet famous performers.

Synset Examples

Every show had a stage-door Johnny waiting for autographs.
She laughed when she saw a stage-door Johnny among the crowd.
The stage-door Johnny was eager to meet his favorite actors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fan - an enthusiastic admirer or supporter, especially of an entertainer

Etymology

From 'stage door', the rear or side entrance to a theater, and 'Johnny', a generic term for a man; popularized in late 19th and early 20th century theater culture.
