conversion reaction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝːʒən riˈækʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːʃən riˈækʃən / (BrE)
A mental disorder in which psychological conflict is unconsciously converted into physical symptoms such as paralysis or loss of sensation, without a medical cause.
mental disorder involving physical symptoms with no medical cause
medical

Examples

After her traumatic experience, she developed a conversion reaction and lost sensation in her legs.
Doctors ruled out any physical causes for his paralysis, concluding it was a conversion reaction.
The patient’s sudden blindness was diagnosed as a conversion reaction resulting from acute emotional stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversion disorder - A psychological condition involving physical symptoms without a medical cause, also known as functional neurological symptom disorder

Etymology

From 'conversion' (from Latin convertō, to turn around) and 'reaction' (from Latin reactio, a doing again).
