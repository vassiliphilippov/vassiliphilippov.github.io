cancer cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænsɚ sɛl / (AmE), / ˈkænsə sɛl / (BrE)
a cell in the body that has become abnormal and divides uncontrollably, forming the basis of a malignant tumor
cell involved in malignant tumor
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the cancer cell had multiplied rapidly in her body.
Scientists are researching new ways to target the cancer cell without harming healthy tissues.
A single cancer cell can eventually lead to the formation of a dangerous tumor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumor cell - cell that forms part of a tumor
