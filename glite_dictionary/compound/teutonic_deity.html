Teutonic deity

1. NOUN

/ tuˈtɑnɪk ˈdiːɪti / (AmE), / tjuːˈtɒnɪk ˈdiːɪti / (BrE)
A god or goddess worshipped by the ancient Teutons (ancient Germanic peoples).
god or goddess in ancient Teutonic mythology

Examples

Odin is one of the best-known Teutonic deities in Norse mythology.
Legends of the Teutonic deities have influenced many modern fantasy stories.
Ancient rituals were performed to honor various Teutonic deities during seasonal festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Germanic god - a deity belonging to ancient Germanic mythology

Etymology

Teutonic from Latin Teutonicus (pertaining to the Teutons), deity from Latin deitas (divine nature).
