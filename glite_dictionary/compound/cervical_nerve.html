cervical nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.vɪ.kəl nɝːv / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪ.kəl nɜːv / (BrE)
any of eight pairs of spinal nerves that originate in the cervical region of the spinal cord
nerve from the cervical spinal region
medical

Examples

Injury to a cervical nerve can lead to weakness or numbness in the arms.
The doctor explained how each cervical nerve controls different muscles in the neck and upper limbs.
Physical therapy helped restore movement after damage to a cervical nerve.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: spinal nerve - any nerve that emerges from the spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin 'cervix' (neck) + nerve.
