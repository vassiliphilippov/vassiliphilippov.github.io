Rochelle salt

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈʃɛl sɑlt / (AmE), / rəʊˈʃɛl sɒlt / (BrE)
A white crystalline double salt (potassium sodium tartrate) used in medicine as a mild laxative and in various industrial applications.
potassium sodium tartrate, a medicinal/industrial salt

Examples

The chemist demonstrated how to crystallize Rochelle salt in the laboratory.
Rochelle salt was once a key ingredient in early piezoelectric devices.
Some over-the-counter laxatives previously contained Rochelle salt as an active component.

Etymology

Named after the French town of La Rochelle, where it was first prepared.
