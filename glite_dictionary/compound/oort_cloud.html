Oort cloud

1. NOUN

/ ɔrt klaʊd / (AmE), / ɔːt klaʊd / (BrE)
A hypothetical vast spherical shell of icy objects that surrounds the solar system far beyond Pluto, from which some comets are believed to originate.
hypothetical distant shell of icy objects around the solar system

Examples

Astronomers speculate that long-period comets come from the Oort cloud.
The existence of the Oort cloud is supported by observations of distant comets entering the solar system.
Some scientists believe that the Voyager spacecraft may one day reach the Oort cloud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kuiper belt - a region of the solar system beyond Neptune, containing many small icy bodies

Etymology

Named after Dutch astronomer Jan Oort, who proposed its existence in 1950.
