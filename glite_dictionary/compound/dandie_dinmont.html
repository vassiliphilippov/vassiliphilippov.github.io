Dandie Dinmont

1. NOUN

/ ˈdændi ˈdɪnmɒnt / (AmE), / ˈdændi ˈdɪnmɒnt / (BrE)
a breed of small terrier with a long, wiry coat and drooping ears, known for its gentle and affectionate nature
a small terrier breed with wiry coat

Examples

The Dandie Dinmont is distinguished by its long body and characteristic topknot of hair.
Her family has bred Dandie Dinmont terriers for generations.
A Dandie Dinmont often makes a loyal and affectionate companion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a breed of dog originally bred for hunting and digging out vermin

Etymology

Named after a character, Dandie Dinmont, in Sir Walter Scott's novel 'Guy Mannering' (1815), who owned dogs of this type.
