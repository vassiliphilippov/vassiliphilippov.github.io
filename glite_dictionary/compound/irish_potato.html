Irish potato

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a common variety of potato, especially the white, edible tuber widely cultivated as a staple food in Ireland and elsewhere
a kind of edible tuber, staple food

Examples

The Irish potato grew well in the cool, damp climate of Ireland.
A shortage of Irish potatoes led to the historic famine in the 19th century.
She planted rows of Irish potato in her garden every spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato - a starchy tuber of the plant Solanum tuberosum
  • Antonym: sweet potato - an edible root tuber, botanically unrelated to the potato

Etymology

Compound of 'Irish' and 'potato', referring to potatoes as a staple crop in Ireland.
