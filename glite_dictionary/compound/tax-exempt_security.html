tax-exempt security

1. NOUN

/ tæks ɪɡˈzɛmpt sɪˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / tæks ɪɡˈzɛmpt sɪˈkjʊərəti / (BrE)
A security, such as a bond or note, whose income is not subject to taxation by the government.
security whose income is not taxed

Examples

Municipal bonds are a common type of tax-exempt security popular with investors seeking to reduce their tax burden.
The interest earned on this tax-exempt security is not included in your taxable income.
Many retirees invest in tax-exempt securities to maximize their income after taxes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: taxable security - a security subject to taxation
