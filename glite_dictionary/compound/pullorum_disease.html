pullorum disease

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɔrəm dɪˌziz / (AmE), / pʊˈlɔːrəm dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a contagious bacterial disease affecting young chickens, caused by Salmonella pullorum and characterized by white diarrhea and high mortality
bacterial disease of young chickens
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the flock with pullorum disease after several chicks showed signs of white diarrhea.
Good biosecurity measures can help prevent pullorum disease in poultry farms.
Many young chicks died last year because of an outbreak of pullorum disease.

Etymology

From New Latin pullorum, genitive plural of Latin pullus (young animal), referring to chicks.
