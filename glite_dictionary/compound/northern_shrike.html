northern shrike

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˈʃraɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈʃraɪk / (BrE)
A predatory songbird (Lanius borealis) found in northern North America, known for impaling its prey on thorns.
predatory songbird of northern North America

Examples

The northern shrike is often spotted perched high on telephone wires in winter.
A northern shrike will sometimes impale insects or even small mammals on sharp branches.
Birdwatchers are excited whenever a northern shrike is seen during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a family of predatory songbirds known for impaling prey
