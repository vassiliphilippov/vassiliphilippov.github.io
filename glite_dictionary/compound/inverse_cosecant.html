inverse cosecant

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɜrs koʊˈsiːkənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːs kəʊˈsiːkənt / (BrE)
The angle whose cosecant is equal to a given number; the inverse function of the cosecant in trigonometry.
trigonometric function, inverse of cosecant

Examples

The inverse cosecant of a number can be found using a scientific calculator.
When solving trigonometric equations, you may need to use the inverse cosecant to determine the angle.
She explained how the inverse cosecant function is related to right triangles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arccosecant - the inverse function of cosecant; another term for inverse cosecant
  • Antonym: cosecant - the reciprocal of the sine function in trigonometry

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'inversus' (turned upside down, reversed) and 'cosecant', itself from 'co-' (complement) and 'secant'.
