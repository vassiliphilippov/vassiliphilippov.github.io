common barley

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈbɑrli / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbɑːli / (BrE)
a species of barley widely cultivated for its grain, used in breakfast foods, animal feed, and the production of malt beverages
a widely grown barley species

Examples

Common barley is a key ingredient in many breakfast cereals and snack bars.
Many farmers grow common barley as a feed crop for their livestock.
Malted common barley is essential in the production of several popular beers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a cereal plant producing grains used for food and brewing
