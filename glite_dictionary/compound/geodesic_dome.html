geodesic dome

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪk doʊm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪk dəʊm / (BrE)
A lightweight, spherical structure made of interlocking polygons, designed to distribute stress evenly across its surface.
spherical structure of interlocking polygons

Examples

The science museum was housed inside a striking geodesic dome designed by a renowned architect.
Many eco-friendly communities use geodesic domes for their energy efficiency and unique appearance.
Buckminster Fuller believed the geodesic dome could solve housing shortages around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dome - a rounded vault forming the roof of a building or structure

Etymology

From 'geodesic', relating to the shortest path between points on a curved surface, combined with 'dome'. The concept was popularized by architect R. Buckminster Fuller.
