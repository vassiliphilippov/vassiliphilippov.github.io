posttraumatic stress disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊsttrəˈmætɪk ˈstrɛs dɪsˌɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌpəʊsttrəˈmatɪk ˈstrɛs dɪsˌɔːdə / (BrE)
A mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event, causing persistent symptoms such as flashbacks, avoidance, and heightened anxiety.
anxiety disorder caused by trauma
medical

Examples

Many veterans develop posttraumatic stress disorder after returning from combat.
Therapists have effective strategies to help those living with posttraumatic stress disorder.
The book describes her personal struggle with posttraumatic stress disorder following a car accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PTSD - abbreviation for posttraumatic stress disorder

Etymology

From post- (after) + traumatic (relating to trauma) + stress + disorder.
