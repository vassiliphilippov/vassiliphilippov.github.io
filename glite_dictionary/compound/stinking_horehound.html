stinking horehound

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈhɔrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈhɔːˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
an ill-smelling European herb with wrinkled leaves and whorls of dark purple flowers, commonly known for its pungent odor
ill-smelling European herb

Examples

The stinking horehound is often found growing along rocky hillsides in southern Europe.
Many gardeners avoid planting stinking horehound due to its strong, unpleasant scent.
The herbalist explained the difference between common horehound and stinking horehound to her students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horehound - a plant of the mint family with wrinkled leaves, used in medicine and candy

Etymology

From 'stinking' (emitting a strong, unpleasant smell) + 'horehound,' from Old English 'hārhūne', a type of plant.
