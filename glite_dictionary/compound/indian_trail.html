Indian trail

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən treɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən treɪl / (BrE)
a path through the wilderness originally formed and used by Indigenous peoples of North America
Native American wilderness path

Examples

The settlers followed the old Indian trail through the dense forest.
He discovered an ancient Indian trail while hiking in the mountains.
Many modern roads were originally based on Indian trails laid down centuries ago.
