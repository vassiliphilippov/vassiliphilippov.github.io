great crested grebe

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈkrɛstɪd ɡrib / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈkrɛstɪd ɡriːb / (BrE)
a large species of grebe found in Eurasia and parts of Africa, with distinctive black ear tufts and ornate head plumes
large grebe with head tufts

Examples

The great crested grebe is known for its elaborate mating dance on the water.
We spotted a pair of great crested grebes nesting by the lake.
During spring, the plumage of the great crested grebe becomes especially striking.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grebe - a diving waterbird with lobed toes
