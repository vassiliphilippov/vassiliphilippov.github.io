betel pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtəl ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈbiːtəl ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
An Asian climbing plant whose leaves are chewed with betel nut by people in Southeast Asia.
Asian plant chewed with betel nut

Examples

The leaves of the betel pepper are commonly chewed with areca nut in many Asian countries.
She wrapped a small piece of lime in a betel pepper leaf before offering it to her guest.
Cultivation of betel pepper requires warm, humid conditions found in the tropics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betel nut - Seed of the betel palm, chewed with betel pepper leaves

Etymology

From Portuguese 'betel', from Malayalam 'vettila' (betel leaf).
