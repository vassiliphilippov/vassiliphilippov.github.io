grip-and-grin

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪp ənd ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪp ən ɡrɪn / (BrE)
A social event or occasion, especially one involving brief greetings and staged photographs of people shaking hands and smiling.
staged photo opportunity with handshakes and smiles

Examples

Many politicians attend grip-and-grin sessions during their campaigns to appear personable.
The awards ceremony ended with the usual grip-and-grin photos on stage.
I find most grip-and-grin events exhausting, but they're important for networking.

Synset Examples

He hates the grip-and-grin events but attends for networking.
The grip-and-grin photo op was awkward but necessary.
She perfected her smile for the grip-and-grin session.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photo op - an arranged opportunity to take photographs, especially of politicians or celebrities

Etymology

Formed from 'grip' (shake hands) and 'grin' (smile), describing typical actions in staged event photos.
