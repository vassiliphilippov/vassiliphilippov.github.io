Taymyr Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ taɪˈmɪr pəˈnɪn.sə.lə / (AmE), / taɪˈmɪə pəˈnɪn.sɪ.lə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in northern Siberia, Russia, extending into the Arctic Ocean between the Yenisei and Khatanga rivers
peninsula in northern Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Taymyr Peninsula is home to some of the northernmost settlements in the world.
Expeditions to the Taymyr Peninsula have contributed to our understanding of Arctic ecology.
Much of the Taymyr Peninsula remains uninhabited due to its harsh climate.

Etymology

From Russian Таймырский полуостров (Taymyrsky poluostrov); Taymyr from the Taymyra River.
