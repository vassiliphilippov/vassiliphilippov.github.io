family Actinomycetaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæktɪnoʊˌmaɪsiːˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌæktɪnəʊˌmaɪsiːˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of filamentous, anaerobic bacteria, some species of which are notable for causing diseases in humans and animals.
group of filamentous anaerobic bacteria
medical

Examples

The family Actinomycetaceae includes bacteria known for forming branching filaments resembling fungal hyphae.
Some pathogens in the family Actinomycetaceae can cause actinomycosis in both humans and animals.
Researchers are studying the family Actinomycetaceae for its role in breaking down organic material in soil.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Actinomyces' (a genus name) + '-aceae' (a standard suffix for bacterial families).
