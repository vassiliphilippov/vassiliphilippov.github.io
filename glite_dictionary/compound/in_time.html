in time

1. ADV

/ ɪn taɪm / (AmE), / ɪn taɪm / (BrE)
before the expected or final moment; early enough to avoid being late or missing something
early enough; not late

Examples

She arrived just in time to catch the last train.
If you leave now, you’ll make it in time for the meeting.
The ambulance got there in time and the patient survived.

Synset Examples

we made it to the party in time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on time - punctually, at the scheduled or exact time
  • Antonym: late - after the expected or scheduled time

2. ADV

/ ɪn taɪm / (AmE), / ɪn taɪm / (BrE)
eventually; after some time has passed or at some future date, often implying an unspecified period before something occurs
eventually; after some time

Examples

With practice, you will get better at painting in time.
Some friendships may fade, but others grow stronger in time.
You may not understand now, but in time it will all make sense.

Synset Examples

he longed for the flowers that were yet to show themselves
sooner or later you will have to face the facts
in time they came to accept the harsh reality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eventually - after a period of time; finally
  • Antonym: immediately - without any delay; at once
