tree line

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtriːˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the imaginary line on a mountain or in polar regions above which trees do not grow due to harsh environmental conditions
upper limit of tree growth

Examples

Few plants can survive above the tree line, where the landscape is dominated by rocks and mosses.
The hikers paused for lunch just below the tree line to enjoy the last bit of shade.
Climate change has caused the tree line in some regions to move to higher elevations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timberline - the upper limit of tree growth in mountains or cold regions
