pole horse

1. NOUN

/ poʊl hɔrs / (AmE), / pəʊl hɔːs / (BrE)
the horse assigned the innermost starting position next to the inside rail in a harness race
harness racing horse with innermost position

Examples

Because he drew the pole horse position, his chances of a fast start were much higher.
Trainers were excited when their horse was listed as the pole horse for the big race.
The announcer noted that the pole horse had a clear advantage on the tight track.

2. NOUN

/ poʊl hɔrs / (AmE), / pəʊl hɔːs / (BrE)
A draft horse harnessed beside the shaft or pole of a vehicle, typically as part of a team pulling a wagon or carriage.
draft horse next to the vehicle's pole

Examples

The farmer hitched a strong pole horse to the wagon for the day's work.
During the parade, the pole horse maintained a steady pace alongside the others.
He explained how to harness the pole horse properly for safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheel horse - a horse harnessed nearest the wheels of a wagon or carriage
