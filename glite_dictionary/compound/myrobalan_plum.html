myrobalan plum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪrəˈbælən plʌm / (AmE), / ˌmɪərəˈbælən plʌm / (BrE)
a small Asiatic tree (Prunus cerasifera) bearing edible red or yellow fruit, often grown for ornament or fruit
Asian tree with edible fruit

Examples

The myrobalan plum is commonly planted as an ornamental tree in city gardens.
She made a delicious jam from the ripe myrobalan plum harvested from her backyard.
Some herbal medicines use extracts from the leaves and fruit of the myrobalan plum.

Etymology

From Greek 'myrobalanos', meaning 'fragrant nut' or 'date', via Latin.
