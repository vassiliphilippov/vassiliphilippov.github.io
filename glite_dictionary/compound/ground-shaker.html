ground-shaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌʃeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌʃeɪkə / (BrE)
a huge herbivorous dinosaur of the Cretaceous period, found in western North America, known for its massive size
large Cretaceous North American dinosaur

Examples

The fossils of the ground-shaker indicate it was one of the largest land animals to have ever lived.
Scientists believe the ground-shaker used its massive tail for defense against predators.
A complete skeleton of a ground-shaker was recently discovered in Montana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - a group of reptiles from the Mesozoic era, often large and extinct
