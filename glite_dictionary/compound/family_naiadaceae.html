family Naiadaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪ.əˈdeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.əˈdeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
A monotypic family of aquatic plants characterized by narrow leaves and small flowers, formerly recognized in botanical classification.
family of aquatic aquatic plants (botany)

Examples

The aquarium contained rare species from the family Naiadaceae, identifiable by their slender leaves.
Botanists often debate the classification of aquatic flora, including the family Naiadaceae.
Members of the family Naiadaceae are commonly found in slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Naiad - genus of aquatic plants in Naiadaceae

Etymology

From New Latin Naiadaceae, based on Naiad (a genus in the family) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant families).
