claymore mine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪ.mɔr maɪn / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.mɔː maɪn / (BrE)
a type of antipersonnel land mine that explodes in a directed blast towards approaching enemies
directional antipersonnel land mine

Examples

The soldier carefully set up a claymore mine at the perimeter of the camp.
During the patrol, they discovered a claymore mine concealed along the trail.
Instructions for deploying a claymore mine are part of standard military training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land mine - an explosive device placed on or just under the ground

Etymology

Named after the 'claymore', a large Scottish sword, due to the mine's shape and intended destructive effect.
