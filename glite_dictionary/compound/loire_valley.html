Loire Valley

1. PROPN

/ lwɑːr ˈvæli / (AmE), / lwɑː ˈvæli / (BrE)
a region in central France along the Loire River, famous for its historic châteaux and wine production
historic French region known for wine and châteaux

Examples

Many tourists visit the Loire Valley to admire its stunning castles and gardens.
The Loire Valley is renowned for producing some of the world's finest white wines.
They spent their summer traveling through the Loire Valley, sampling local wines and cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bordeaux - a famous French wine-producing region

Etymology

French Loire, from Latin Liger (the river's name), combined with 'valley'.
