fulminic acid

1. NOUN

/ fʊlˈmɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / fʊlˈmɪnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
An unstable acid (HCNO) that exists primarily in the form of explosive salts and esters, isomeric with cyanic acid.
unstable, explosive acid (HCNO)

Examples

Because of its instability, fulminic acid is rarely handled in its pure form.
Researchers study the explosive properties of fulminic acid and its salts.
Fulminic acid is isomeric with cyanic acid, which has different chemical properties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cyanic acid - An isomeric acid with formula HOCN

Etymology

From Latin fulmen (lightning), referring to the explosive nature; 'fulminic' related to 'fulminate'.
