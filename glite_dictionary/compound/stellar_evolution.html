stellar evolution

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlər ˌɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈstelə ˌiːvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a star forms, changes, and eventually dies over time
the lifecycle of a star

Examples

Many textbooks include diagrams to illustrate the key stages of stellar evolution.
Understanding stellar evolution helps astronomers explain the origin of elements in the universe.
Recent discoveries have changed our view of how black holes form during stellar evolution.

Synset Examples

Astronomers study supernovae to understand stellar evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star formation - the process by which dense regions in molecular clouds collapse to form stars

Etymology

From Latin 'stella' (star) and 'evolutio' (unrolling, development).
