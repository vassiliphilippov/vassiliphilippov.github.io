over-the-shoulder bombing

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚ ðə ˈʃoʊldɚ ˈbɑmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈʃəʊldə ˈbɒmɪŋ / (BrE)
A military bombing technique in which a bomb is released after passing vertical flight, causing it to be thrown backward toward the target.
bombing technique tossing bomb backward

Examples

The pilots were trained in over-the-shoulder bombing to avoid flying directly over the target.
During the mission, the aircraft performed an over-the-shoulder bombing maneuver to strike the heavily defended site.
Experts demonstrated over-the-shoulder bombing at the air show, impressing the crowd with its precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loft bombing - A bombing technique where a bomb is released during a climb so it follows a lofted trajectory to the target.
