family Lythraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli laɪˈθreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli lɪˈθreɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, including herbs, shrubs, and small trees, typically bearing pink or purple flowers.
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The family Lythraceae includes the crape myrtle, which is popular in gardens.
Several wetland species belong to the family Lythraceae, known for their showy flowers.
Botanists classified the new plant as part of the family Lythraceae due to its floral structure.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lythrum', the type genus, and Neo-Latin '-aceae' (denoting plant families).
