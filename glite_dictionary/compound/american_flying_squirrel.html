American flying squirrel

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈskwɝːəl / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
Either of two species of small nocturnal squirrels native to North America that are able to glide using a membrane of skin between their limbs.
small gliding squirrel of North America

Examples

At night, the American flying squirrel glides from tree to tree in search of food.
Researchers observed a family of American flying squirrels nesting in the old oak forest.
The American flying squirrel uses its skin membrane to travel long distances between trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying squirrel - a type of squirrel capable of gliding
