Missouri primrose

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈzʊri ˈpraɪmˌroʊz / (AmE), / mɪˈzʊəri ˈprʌɪmˌrəʊz / (BrE)
a North American evening-blooming primrose with large yellow flowers, native to south central United States.
evening-blooming primrose of south-central US

Examples

The garden is full of Missouri primrose, which opens its blossoms in the evening.
We planted a row of Missouri primrose along the backyard fence for their vibrant yellow flowers.
Native bees are often attracted to the Missouri primrose in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening primrose - a North American plant with pale yellow flowers that open in the evening
