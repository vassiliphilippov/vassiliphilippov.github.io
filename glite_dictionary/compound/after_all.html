after all

1. ADV

/ ˌæftɚ ˈɔl / (AmE), / ˌɑːftə ˈɔːl / (BrE)
used to emphasize a reason or point that should be taken into consideration, especially when it may affect understanding or decisions
used to emphasize a reason or consideration

Examples

We should help him; after all, he's done so much for us in the past.
She decided not to go to the party; after all, she wasn't feeling well.
You shouldn't be surprised if he disagrees—after all, he warned us about these issues.

Synset Examples

after all, she is your boss, so invite her
he is, after all, our president

2. ADV

/ ˌæftər ˈɔl / (AmE), / ˌɑːftə ˈɔːl / (BrE)
used to indicate that something happened or is true despite earlier doubts or expectations to the contrary
despite expectations; in the end

Examples

She decided to go to the concert after all, even though she was tired.
It seemed like it would rain, but the sun came out after all.
He didn't want to join us at first, but he showed up after all.

Synset Examples

came to the party after all
it didn't rain after all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimately - finally; in the end
  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; however
