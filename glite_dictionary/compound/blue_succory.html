blue succory

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈsʌkəri / (AmE), / bluː ˈsʌkəri / (BrE)
A South European plant (Cichorium intybus) with flat blue ray flowers and dark centers, also known as chicory.
South European plant with blue flowers

Examples

The field was dotted with the bright blue flowers of blue succory.
Farmers often grow blue succory along roadsides for its hardy, attractive blooms.
Blue succory is sometimes used as a coffee substitute after roasting its roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - A plant with bright blue flowers, used as a salad or coffee substitute

Etymology

From 'blue' (color) and 'succory', an old variant of 'succory', from Latin 'succorium' for chicory.
