elixir of life

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪksər əv laɪf / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪksə əv laɪf / (BrE)
a mythical or hypothetical substance thought to grant immortality or maintain youth and life indefinitely, especially in alchemical tradition
mythical substance granting immortality

Examples

Legends tell of an elixir of life that would allow the one who drinks it to live forever.
Throughout the Middle Ages, alchemists searched tirelessly for the elixir of life.
The story's villain is obsessed with finding the elixir of life to become immortal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosopher's stone - legendary alchemical substance believed to enable transmutation of metals or confer immortality

Etymology

Elixir from Arabic *al-iksīr* (the philosopher’s stone), combined with 'of life' to indicate its purported effect.
