Richter scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪktər skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɪktə skeɪl / (BrE)
a logarithmic scale formerly used to measure the magnitude of earthquakes, based on the amplitude of seismic waves recorded by seismographs
scale for measuring earthquake magnitude

Examples

The earthquake registered 6.7 on the Richter scale, causing widespread damage across the region.
No significant tremors above 3 were detected on the Richter scale after the initial quake.
The seismologist explained how the Richter scale works to measure earthquake strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnitude - the great size or importance of something; in this context, the energy released by an earthquake

Etymology

Named after Charles F. Richter (1900–1985), American seismologist, who developed the scale in 1935.
