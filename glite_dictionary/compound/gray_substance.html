gray substance

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
the greyish tissue in the nervous system, containing nerve cell bodies and unmyelinated fibers, found especially in the cerebral cortex
greyish nervous tissue in the brain or spinal cord
medical

Examples

The cerebral cortex is primarily composed of gray substance that processes information.
Damage to the gray substance in the spinal cord can lead to loss of motor function.
Scientists studied the gray substance to better understand brain activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray matter - the darker tissue of the brain and spinal cord, consisting mainly of nerve cell bodies and branching dendrites
