propyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.pəl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.pɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a monovalent organic group with the formula C3H7–, derived from propane by removing one hydrogen atom
organic group from propane, C3H7–
medical

Examples

The attachment of a propyl group can significantly alter the properties of a molecule.
Researchers identified a compound containing a propyl group bonded to the main chain.
In this reaction, the hydrogen atom is replaced by a propyl group.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ethyl group - a monovalent organic group C2H5– derived from ethane
  • Degree Higher: butyl group - a monovalent organic group C4H9– derived from butane

Etymology

From 'propane' (a three-carbon alkane) and 'yl', a suffix used in organic chemistry for groups derived from parent compounds.
