pectic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɛktɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a complex acid formed from pectin, present in ripe fruits and some vegetables
acid found in ripe fruits and vegetables
medical

Examples

During the ripening process, much of the fruit's pectin is converted into pectic acid.
The presence of pectic acid can affect the texture and quality of jam.
Researchers studied how pectic acid breaks down in different vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectin - a polysaccharide present in plant cell walls, used as a gelling agent

Etymology

From 'pectic' (relating to pectin, from Greek pēktos 'congealed') + 'acid'.
