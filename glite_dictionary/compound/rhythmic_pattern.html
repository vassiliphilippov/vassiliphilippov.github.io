rhythmic pattern

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪð.mɪk ˈpæt.ɚn / (AmE), / ˈrɪð.mɪk ˈpæt.ən / (BrE)
(Prosody) A regular arrangement of stressed and unstressed syllables in poetry or verse, forming the structure of its rhythm.
regular arrangement of rhythm in verse

Examples

The poem's rhythmic pattern gives it a musical quality that enhances its emotional impact.
Different cultures often employ unique rhythmic patterns in their traditional poetry.
By varying the rhythmic pattern, the poet kept the reader engaged throughout the collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - the basic rhythmic structure of a verse or lines in verse
