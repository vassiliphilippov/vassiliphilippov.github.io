digitalis glycoside

1. NOUN

/ dɪdʒɪˈtælɪs ˈɡlaɪkəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / dɪdʒɪˈtælɪs ˈɡlɪkəsiːd / (BrE)
any of a group of compounds derived from the foxglove plant, used as powerful stimulants for the heart in medical treatment
compound from foxglove used as a heart stimulant
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a digitalis glycoside to help regulate her heartbeat.
Overdosing on digitalis glycoside can lead to serious cardiac side effects.
Researchers study the effects of digitalis glycoside in patients with heart failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digoxin - a specific digitalis glycoside used in medicine
  • Synonym: cardiac stimulant - a drug that increases the activity of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'digitalis' (foxglove) and Greek 'glykys' (sweet) + 'eidos' (form).
