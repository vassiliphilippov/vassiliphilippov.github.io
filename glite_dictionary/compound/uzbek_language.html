Uzbek language

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌzbɛk ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌzbɛk ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Uzbek people, primarily in Uzbekistan and surrounding regions
Turkic language of Uzbekistan

Examples

She is fluent in Uzbek language and often works as a translator in Central Asia.
The Uzbek language uses the Latin alphabet in official documents today.
Learning the Uzbek language opened new opportunities for him to travel and work abroad.

Etymology

From Uzbek, the name of the ethnic group, ultimately from the personal name Ozbeg, combined with 'language'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌzbɛk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʊzbɛk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Uzbek people, primarily in Uzbekistan and surrounding regions.
Turkic language of the Uzbek people

Examples

The official language of Uzbekistan is Uzbek language, which uses a modified Latin script.
He is fluent in both Russian and Uzbek language.
Many schools in Central Asia teach classes in Uzbek language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish language - the Turkic language spoken in Turkey and Cyprus

Etymology

From Uzbek, the name of the people, plus 'language'.
