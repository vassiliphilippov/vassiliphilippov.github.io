superlative adjective

1. NOUN

/ suːˈpərlətɪv ˈædʒɪktɪv / (AmE), / suːˈpɜːlətɪv ˈædʒɪktɪv / (BrE)
an adjective expressing the highest degree of a quality, used for comparing three or more things
adjective expressing the highest degree of quality

Examples

In the sentence 'She is the smartest student,' 'smartest' is a superlative adjective.
Writers often use superlative adjectives to make their opinions more persuasive.
The grammar exercise asked students to change the regular adjective into a superlative adjective.

Synset Examples

They always use superlative adjectives in their reviews.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: comparative adjective - an adjective expressing a higher degree than another but not the highest

Etymology

From Latin superlativus (exaggerative), from superlatus (carried over, raised high), and adjective from Latin adjectivum.
