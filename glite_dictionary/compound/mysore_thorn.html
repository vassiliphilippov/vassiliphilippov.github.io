Mysore thorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪsɔr ˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪsɔː ˌθɔːn / (BrE)
a spreading thorny shrub native to tropical Asia, known for its large upright clusters of yellow flowers marked with red
thorny shrub of tropical Asia with yellow and red flowers

Examples

The Mysore thorn is often planted as a hedge because of its dense, spiny branches.
Gardeners admire the Mysore thorn for its striking clusters of yellow flowers with a red mark.
In some regions, the Mysore thorn is considered an invasive species due to its rapid spread.

Etymology

Named after Mysore, a region in southern India, where the plant is native.
