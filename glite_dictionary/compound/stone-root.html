stone-root

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊn ˌrut / (AmE), / ˈstəʊn ˌruːt / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant (Collinsonia canadensis) with strong-scented, serrate leaves and yellowish flowers, native to the eastern United States.
North American perennial herb (Collinsonia canadensis)

Examples

The leaves of the stone-root have a distinctive scent when crushed.
Herbalists sometimes use stone-root in traditional remedies for its reputed medicinal properties.
In the forest understory, stone-root blooms with clusters of yellowish flowers during late summer.
