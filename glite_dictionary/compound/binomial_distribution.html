binomial distribution

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈnoʊmiəl dɪstrɪˈbjuʃən / (AmE), / bɪˈnəʊmiəl dɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
A probability distribution that describes the number of successes in a fixed number of independent trials, each with the same probability of success.
probability distribution in statistics

Examples

In statistics, the binomial distribution models the probability of a given number of heads in coin tosses.
She used a binomial distribution to calculate the likelihood of getting four correct answers by guessing.
The binomial distribution assumes each trial is independent and the probability of success remains constant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normal distribution - a continuous probability distribution that is symmetrical and bell-shaped

Etymology

From Latin 'binomium' (having two names) and 'distribution'.
