genus Colaptes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs koʊˈlæptiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈlæptiːz / (BrE)
A genus of woodpeckers in the family Picidae, known as flickers.
a genus of woodpeckers (flickers)

Examples

The genus Colaptes includes several species of flickers found across the Americas.
Researchers observed notable differences between members of the genus Colaptes and related woodpeckers.
The northern flicker, classified within the genus Colaptes, is common in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flicker - a type of woodpecker found in the Americas, species within the genus Colaptes

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind, type) and Colaptes, the genus name from Greek kolaptēs (pecker, striker).
