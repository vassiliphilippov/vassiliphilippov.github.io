red time

1. NOUN

/ rɛd taɪm / (AmE), / rɛd taɪm / (BrE)
A period of critical urgency, high pressure, or financial loss, especially when immediate action is necessary.
critical or urgent period

Examples

With the product launch so close, the team knew they were in red time.
The company entered red time when quarterly profits dropped sharply.
Traders become especially cautious during red time in volatile markets.

Synset Examples

We are entering red time as deadlines approach.
It's red time for the stock market right now.
During red time, everyone is on high alert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected situation requiring immediate action
