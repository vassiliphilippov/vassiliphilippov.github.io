primary tooth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri tuθ / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri tuːθ / (BrE)
A tooth in young children that is part of the first set of temporary teeth and is eventually replaced by a permanent tooth.
first set of temporary teeth in children
medical

Examples

By age six, most children will begin to lose their primary teeth as permanent ones emerge.
Dentists recommend regular check-ups even when a child only has primary teeth.
Decay in a primary tooth can affect the health of developing permanent teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milk tooth - a temporary tooth in young mammals, synonym for primary tooth
  • Antonym: permanent tooth - an adult tooth that replaces a primary tooth
