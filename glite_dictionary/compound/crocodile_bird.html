crocodile bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑːkəˌdaɪl bɝːd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒkədaɪl bɜːd / (BrE)
an African bird (the Egyptian plover) that feeds on parasites found on crocodiles
African bird eating parasites on crocodiles

Examples

The crocodile bird is often seen picking insects off basking reptiles on the riverbank.
Some believe the crocodile bird helps crocodiles by cleaning their teeth and mouths.
Tourists were fascinated to see a crocodile bird fearlessly approaching a resting crocodile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian plover - an African bird species known for its association with crocodiles
