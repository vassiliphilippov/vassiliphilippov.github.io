mistletoe cactus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪsəltəʊ ˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A tropical epiphytic cactus of the genus Rhipsalis, with slender stems and small white or pinkish fruit, often found growing on trees.
a tropical epiphytic cactus (Rhipsalis)

Examples

The mistletoe cactus needs a warm, humid environment to thrive indoors.
She admired the delicate white berries of the mistletoe cactus in the tropical greenhouse.
Unlike most cacti, the mistletoe cactus prefers shade and can even grow in rainforests.

Etymology

From 'mistletoe', due to the plant's hanging habit, and 'cactus', from Greek 'kaktos'.
