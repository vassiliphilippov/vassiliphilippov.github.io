calcium light

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsiəm laɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm laɪt / (BrE)
a powerful light produced by directing a flame at a cylinder of lime, used especially in theaters before the introduction of electric lighting
historical intense stage light using lime

Examples

The actors were clearly visible under the calcium light during the performance.
In the 19th century, theaters relied on calcium light to illuminate the stage.
A bright calcium light was focused on the star, making her the center of attention.

Etymology

Named for the use of lime (calcium oxide) as the light-emitting material.
