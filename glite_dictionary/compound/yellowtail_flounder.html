yellowtail flounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌteɪl ˈflaʊndɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌteɪl ˈflaʊndə / (BrE)
a North American flounder (Limanda ferruginea) distinguished by its yellowish tail, commonly found in Atlantic coastal waters
a flounder with a yellowish tail

Examples

Fishermen caught a large yellowtail flounder off the coast of Nova Scotia.
The yellowtail flounder is prized for its mild flavor and delicate texture.
Overfishing has caused a decline in the yellowtail flounder population in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish found in coastal waters
