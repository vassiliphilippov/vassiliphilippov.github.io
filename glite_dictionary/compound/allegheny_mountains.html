Allegheny Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˌæləˈɡeɪni ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˌæləˈɡeɪni ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range forming the western part of the Appalachian Mountains, extending from northern Pennsylvania to southwestern Virginia in the United States
mountain range in eastern United States

Examples

The Allegheny Mountains are known for their scenic beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities.
A thick fog settled over the Allegheny Mountains early in the morning, obscuring the peaks from view.
Many hikers travel through the Allegheny Mountains to enjoy the region's diverse wildlife and lush forests.

Etymology

From Lenape (Delaware) word *welhik hane* meaning 'best flowing river'; 'Allegheny' was first applied to the river and later to the mountains.
