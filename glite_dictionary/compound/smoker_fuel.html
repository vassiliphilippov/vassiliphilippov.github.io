smoker fuel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊ.kɚ ˌfjuːl / (AmE), / ˈsməʊ.kə ˌfjʊːl / (BrE)
a natural, uncontaminated material used as fuel in bee smokers to produce smoke for calming bees
fuel used in bee smokers

Examples

The beekeeper carefully selected smoker fuel to avoid introducing chemicals into the hive.
Hessian and pine needles are common types of smoker fuel among beekeepers.
Using natural smoker fuel helps protect the health of the bee colony.

Synset Examples

Smoker fuels include hessian, burlap, pine needles, corrugated cardboard, paper egg cartons, and rotten or punky wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - a substance burned to produce energy or heat
