genus Caragana

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkæ.rəˈɡɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkæ.rəˈɡɑː.nə / (BrE)
A large genus of Asiatic deciduous shrubs or small trees in the pea family, known for their yellow flowers and often used as ornamental or shelterbelt plants.
genus of Asiatic shrubs or small trees

Examples

The genus Caragana includes many species used in windbreaks and ornamental gardens.
Some members of the genus Caragana are known for their drought resistance.
Scientists are studying the genus Caragana for its ability to improve soil quality in arid regions.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Russian 'karagana', a local name for these plants.
