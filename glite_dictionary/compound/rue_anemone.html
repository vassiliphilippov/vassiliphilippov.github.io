rue anemone

1. NOUN

/ ˈru æˈnɛməni / (AmE), / ˈruː əˈnɛməni / (BrE)
a woodland flower native to eastern North America, with delicate, cup-shaped white or pinkish flowers resembling those of anemone
woodland flower with delicate anemone-like blooms

Examples

The woods were filled with rue anemone and other spring wildflowers.
She bent down to pick a delicate rue anemone from the forest floor.
Botanists admire the fragile beauty of the rue anemone in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemone - a genus of flowering plants with similar cup-shaped blooms

Etymology

From rue (the herb, for similarity in appearance) + anemone (a type of flowering plant, from Greek anemōnē).
