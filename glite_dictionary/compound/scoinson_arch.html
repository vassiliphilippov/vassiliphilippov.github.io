scoinson arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔɪnsən ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɔɪnsən ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch that supports or reinforces part of a wall, especially within an architectural structure.
arch supporting part of a wall

Examples

The mason carefully restored the scoinson arch above the old window.
A scoinson arch is often used in the design of Gothic cathedrals to support thick walls.
During renovation, they discovered a hidden scoinson arch within the stone structure.
