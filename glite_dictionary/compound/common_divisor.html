common divisor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən dɪˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən dɪˈvaɪzə / (BrE)
a number that divides two or more integers exactly, leaving no remainder
number that divides two or more integers

Examples

12 is a common divisor of both 36 and 60.
To find a common divisor, list the factors of each number and look for shared ones.
Six is the greatest common divisor of 18 and 24.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common factor - a number that is a factor of two or more numbers
  • Antonym: common multiple - a number that is a multiple of two or more numbers
