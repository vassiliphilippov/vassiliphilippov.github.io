colic artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlɪk ˈɑːɹtɛɹi / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
any of several arteries that supply blood to the colon.
artery supplying blood to the colon
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the colic artery before proceeding with the procedure.
Damage to a colic artery can lead to reduced blood flow in the colon.
Radiologists observed narrowing of a colic artery on the scan.

Etymology

From Greek *kōlikos* (relating to the colon) and Latin *arteria* (artery).
