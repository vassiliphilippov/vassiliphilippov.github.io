black saltwort

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsɔltwɝt / (AmE), / blæk ˈsɔːltwɜːt / (BrE)
a small, fleshy coastal herb of North America, found along seashores and in brackish marshes, with pink or white flowers
North American coastal herb

Examples

Black saltwort is often found thriving in tidal marshlands along the Atlantic coast.
The succulent stems of black saltwort help it survive the salty environment.
Botanists are studying how black saltwort adapts to changing shoreline conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltwort - a plant of the genus Salsola or related genera, growing in saline or alkaline soils
