Napier's bones

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪpiɚz boʊnz / (AmE), / ˈneɪpiəz bəʊnz / (BrE)
A set of numbered rods invented by John Napier in the 17th century, formerly used to facilitate multiplication and division.
historical calculating device of numbered rods

Examples

Before calculators, Napier's bones were popular among merchants for handling large multiplications.
The museum displayed an original set of Napier's bones next to other early calculating tools.
Teachers sometimes use replicas of Napier's bones to explain historical methods of arithmetic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abacus - a frame with beads for calculating, used before modern calculators

Etymology

Named after John Napier (1550–1617), the Scottish mathematician who invented them.
