abducens nerve

1. NOUN

/ æbˈduːsɛnz nɝːv / (AmE), / æbˈdjuːsɛnz nɜːv / (BrE)
the sixth cranial nerve, responsible for controlling the lateral movement of the eye by innervating the lateral rectus muscle
sixth cranial nerve controlling eye movement
medical

Examples

Damage to the abducens nerve can cause double vision and difficulty moving the eye outward.
The ophthalmologist tested the function of the abducens nerve during the eye examination.
An injury to the abducens nerve may result in an inability to look to the side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranial nerve - any of the twelve major nerves that emerge directly from the brain

Etymology

From Latin abducens (drawing away), referring to the muscle's function of moving the eye outward.
