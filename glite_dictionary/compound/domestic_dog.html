domestic dog

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmestɪk dɔɡ / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪk dɒɡ / (BrE)
a domesticated mammal (Canis familiaris), descended from the wolf, kept by humans as a pet or working animal; occurs in many breeds
a domesticated mammal, companion animal

Examples

The domestic dog was the first animal to be domesticated by humans.
Each breed of domestic dog has its own unique characteristics and temperament.
Scientists study the ancestry of the domestic dog to learn how it evolved from the wolf.

Synset Examples

the dog barked all night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - a domesticated carnivorous mammal, Canis familiaris

Etymology

From Latin 'domesticus' (belonging to the house) and Old English 'docga' (dog).
