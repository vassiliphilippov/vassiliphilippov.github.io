spate of burglaries

1. NOUN

/ speɪt əv ˈbɜrɡləriz / (AmE), / speɪt əv ˈbɜːɡləriz / (BrE)
a sudden series or large number of burglaries occurring within a short period
sudden surge in burglary incidents

Examples

The neighborhood was shaken by a spate of burglaries over the summer months.
Police have increased patrols after a recent spate of burglaries in the area.
Following the spate of burglaries, residents have installed new security systems.

Synset Examples

There was a spate of burglaries in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime wave - a sudden and notable increase in crime in an area
  • Synonym: burglary - the act of illegally entering a building to commit a crime, especially theft

Etymology

From 'spate', meaning a sudden flood or large number, origin uncertain but possibly from Old French 'espat' (flood).
