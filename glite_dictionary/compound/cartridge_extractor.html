cartridge extractor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.trɪdʒ ɪkˌstræk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ ɪkˌstræk.tə / (BrE)
a mechanism in a firearm that removes the empty cartridge case from the chamber after firing
firearm mechanism for removing spent cartridge case

Examples

The cartridge extractor failed, causing the empty shell to remain stuck in the chamber.
Modern rifles are equipped with a durable cartridge extractor for reliable operation.
Gunsmiths often inspect the cartridge extractor when troubleshooting ejection problems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ejector - a device in a firearm that expels the empty cartridge case from the firearm
