pin bone

1. NOUN

/ pɪn boʊn / (AmE), / pɪn bəʊn / (BrE)
a part of the sirloin cut of beef, located between the flat bone and the porterhouse
section of sirloin between flat bone and porterhouse

Examples

The chef carefully sliced the pin bone section to ensure every steak was perfectly tender.
Traditionally, the pin bone steak is known for its unique combination of flavor and tenderness.
Learning to identify the pin bone helps butchers prepare quality sirloin cuts.
