Four Hundred

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / fɔː ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
the group considered to be the most socially exclusive set of people in a particular city, especially historically in New York
exclusive social elite group (esp. in New York)

Examples

The city's Four Hundred attended the lavish charity ball at the mansion.
In the Gilded Age, being accepted by the Four Hundred was a mark of true social status.
She dreamed of one day joining the ranks of the Four Hundred and attending their exclusive events.

Etymology

Said to originate from the number of people who could fit into Mrs. Astor's ballroom in 19th-century New York.
