stock-still

1. ADJ

/ stɑkˈstɪl / (AmE), / stɒkˈstɪl / (BrE)
completely still; not moving at all.
completely motionless

Examples

She stood stock-still when she heard the unexpected sound behind her.
The deer remained stock-still in the clearing, hoping not to be seen.
He sat stock-still throughout the movie, completely absorbed in the story.

Synset Examples

frozen with horror
they stood rooted in astonishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionless - not moving; still
  • Synonym: frozen - still and unable to move, often due to fear or shock

Etymology

From 'stock' meaning tree trunk or stem (i.e. fixed, unmoving), combined with 'still.'

2. ADV

/ stɑk ˈstɪl / (AmE), / stɒk ˈstɪl / (BrE)
completely still; not moving at all
completely still, motionless

Examples

The child stood stock-still when she heard the loud noise outside.
During the thunderstorm, the dog remained stock-still under the table.
You need to stay stock-still while I take the photograph.

Synset Examples

She was stock-still as the surprise unfolded.
He remained stock-still, afraid to make a sound.
The deer stood stock-still, watching us closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionless - not moving; still
  • Synonym: immobile - not moving; stationary
  • Antonym: restless - unable to remain still

Etymology

From 'stock' meaning tree trunk or stem (suggesting rigidity) + 'still'.
