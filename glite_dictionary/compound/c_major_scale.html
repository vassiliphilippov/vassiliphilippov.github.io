C major scale

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ skeɪl / (AmE), / siː ˈmeɪ.dʒə skeɪl / (BrE)
the major scale in music that consists of the notes C, D, E, F, G, A, and B, and has no sharps or flats
major scale with no sharps or flats

Examples

The C major scale is often the first scale beginners learn on the piano.
Many simple melodies are composed in the C major scale because it avoids accidentals.
To practice your finger technique, try playing the C major scale ascending and descending.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: G major scale - major scale based on G, with one sharp
  • Degree Lower: F major scale - major scale based on F, with one flat

Etymology

Named for the tonic note C and the major scale structure in Western music.
