tufted titmouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌftɪd ˈtɪtˌmaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtʌftɪd ˈtɪtˌmaʊs / (BrE)
a small songbird with a gray crest found in the eastern and midwestern United States, noted for its distinctive tufted head
small crested North American songbird

Examples

A tufted titmouse perched on the feeder outside my kitchen window, searching for sunflower seeds.
While hiking in the forest, we spotted a tufted titmouse singing from a pine branch.
The distinctive grey crest helps birdwatchers quickly identify the tufted titmouse among other woodland birds.

Etymology

From 'tufted' (having a tuft) and 'titmouse', from Middle English 'titmose', where 'mose' means small bird.
