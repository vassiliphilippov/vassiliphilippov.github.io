gravity's pull

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævətiˌz pʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪtiz pʊl / (BrE)
the force by which gravity attracts objects toward the center of a mass, especially toward the Earth
the force of gravity

Examples

The gravity's pull on the Moon is weaker than on Earth.
She struggled to walk uphill, battling gravity's pull with each step.
Engineers must always account for gravity's pull when designing tall buildings.

Synset Examples

Astronauts experience reduced effects of gravity's pull.
He felt gravity's pull as he climbed the hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravitational force - the force that attracts any objects with mass
