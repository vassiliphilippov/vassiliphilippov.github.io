fraught with

1. ADJ

/ frɔt wɪð / (AmE), / frɔːt wɪð / (BrE)
filled with or full of something unpleasant or undesirable; characterized by a particular negative quality or element.
filled with something undesirable

Examples

The negotiations were fraught with difficulty from the very start.
Her journey across the mountains was fraught with danger and uncertainty.
Starting a new business can be fraught with unexpected challenges.

Synset Examples

Their relationship was fraught with misunderstandings.
Traveling during the pandemic is fraught with risks.
The meeting was fraught with tension and uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full of - containing as much as possible; filled with
  • Antonym: devoid of - completely lacking; empty of

Etymology

From Middle English 'fraught', past participle of 'fraughten' (to load), from Middle Dutch 'vraghen' (to load a ship).
