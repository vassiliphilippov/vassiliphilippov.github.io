execution of instrument

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən əv ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən əv ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
(law) the act of completing a legal document, such as a contract or deed, by signing (and sometimes sealing and delivering) it so that it becomes legally binding and enforceable.
completion of a legal document by signing
legal

Examples

The execution of instrument was delayed pending the arrival of all necessary signatories.
Proper execution of instrument ensures the contract is legally binding and enforceable in court.
The lawyer reviewed the document before proceeding with its execution of instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the act of carrying out or putting something into effect
