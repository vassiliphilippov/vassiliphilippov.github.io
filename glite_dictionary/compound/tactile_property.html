tactile property

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.taɪl ˈprɑː.pɚ.ti / (AmE), / ˈtæk.taɪl ˈprɒ.pə.ti / (BrE)
a characteristic of an object that can be perceived by touching it, such as texture, hardness, or temperature
property sensed by touch

Examples

The tactile property of silk makes it pleasant to wear against the skin.
Scientists measured the tactile property of the fabric to ensure comfort for patients.
She selected the blanket based on its soft tactile property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: texture - the feel or appearance of a surface, such as smoothness or roughness

Etymology

From Latin tactile (from tangere, to touch) and property (from Latin proprietatem, ownership).
