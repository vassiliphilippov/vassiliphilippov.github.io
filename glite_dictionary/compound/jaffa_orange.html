Jaffa orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæfə ɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæfə ɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a sweet, almost seedless variety of orange originally grown in Israel, noted for its thick skin and juiciness
sweet, nearly seedless orange of Israel

Examples

The Jaffa orange is prized for its sweet flavor and lack of seeds.
Exports of Jaffa oranges helped build Israel's agricultural industry.
I love starting my mornings with fresh Jaffa orange juice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a round, sweet citrus fruit with a tough bright reddish-yellow rind

Etymology

Named after the port city of Jaffa (Yafo) in Israel, where it was first exported.
