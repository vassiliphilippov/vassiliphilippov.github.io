family Cervidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sərˈvɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli səːˈvɪdiː / (BrE)
the biological family that includes deer, such as reindeer, moose, muntjacs, elk, and roe deer
the deer family (taxonomic group)

Examples

The family Cervidae encompasses all true deer species, including moose and reindeer.
Biologists study the antler growth in various members of the family Cervidae.
Muntjacs, which belong to the family Cervidae, are native to parts of Asia.

Etymology

From Latin 'cervus' (deer), with 'Cervidae' as the modern taxonomic family name.
