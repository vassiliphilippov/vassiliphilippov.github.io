offer price

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfər ˌpraɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒfə ˌpraɪs / (BrE)
The price at which a seller, especially a broker, is willing to sell a particular security or asset.
the price at which something is offered for sale

Examples

The offer price for the company's shares was set at $15 per share.
Investors noted that the offer price was higher than the previous day's closing price.
You should compare the offer price and the bid price before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bid price - the price at which a buyer is willing to purchase a security
