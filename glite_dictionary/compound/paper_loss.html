paper loss

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ lɔs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə lɒs / (BrE)
A loss that exists on paper, resulting from a decline in the market value of an investment, but has not been realized by selling the asset.
unrealized loss in investment value

Examples

Although the shares fell sharply, her paper loss disappeared when the market rebounded.
The company's paper loss this quarter does not affect its cash flow until assets are sold.
He isn't concerned about a paper loss because he intends to hold the investment long term.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paper profit - an unrealized profit on an investment; gain not yet realized

Etymology

From 'paper' (referring to records or statements) and 'loss', indicating a loss only reflected on paper until realized.
