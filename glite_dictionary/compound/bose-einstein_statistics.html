Bose-Einstein statistics

1. NOUN

/ boʊs ˈaɪnstaɪn stəˈtɪstɪks / (AmE), / bəʊz ˈaɪnstaɪn stəˈtɪstɪks / (BrE)
A statistical law describing the distribution of identical particles with integer spin, whose wave function remains unchanged when two particles are swapped, and to which the Pauli exclusion principle does not apply.
statistical law for certain quantum particles

Examples

The behavior of photons in a laser can be described using Bose-Einstein statistics.
Unlike electrons, bosons such as helium-4 atoms obey Bose-Einstein statistics.
Bose-Einstein statistics predicts the phenomenon of Bose-Einstein condensation at very low temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Fermi-Dirac statistics - statistical law for particles obeying the Pauli exclusion principle (fermions)

Etymology

Named after Indian physicist Satyendra Nath Bose and German-American physicist Albert Einstein.
