Arizona wild cotton

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪzoʊnə waɪld ˈkɑtən / (AmE), / əˌrɪzəʊnə waɪld ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
a shrub native to southern Arizona and Mexico, known for its cotton-like seed capsules
a shrub of southern Arizona and Mexico

Examples

The Arizona wild cotton produces soft seed fibers that resemble commercial cotton.
Botanists studied how Arizona wild cotton adapts to arid conditions in the Sonoran Desert.
The natural habitat of Arizona wild cotton is threatened by land development and climate change.
