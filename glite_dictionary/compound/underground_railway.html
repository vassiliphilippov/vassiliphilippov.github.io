Underground Railway

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌɡraʊnd ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌɡraʊnd ˈreɪlweɪ / (BrE)
a secret network organized to help enslaved people escape to free states or Canada, chiefly in the United States before the Civil War
secret network aiding escaped slaves (US history)

Examples

The Underground Railway assisted thousands of enslaved individuals to gain their freedom before the Civil War.
Harriet Tubman was a famous conductor on the Underground Railway.
Many abolitionists risked their lives to participate in the Underground Railway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Underground Railroad - another term for the Underground Railway, primarily American spelling

Etymology

Named after literal railways, reflecting the system's clandestine routes and terminology.
