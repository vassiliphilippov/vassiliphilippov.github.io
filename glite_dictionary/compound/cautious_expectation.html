cautious expectation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔʃəs ˌɛkspɛkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃəs ˌɛkspɛkˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of hope or anticipation tempered by carefulness or hesitation about a possible outcome
guarded hope or anticipation

Examples

Many investors viewed the new government policy with cautious expectation rather than outright enthusiasm.
The scientists waited for the test results in an atmosphere of cautious expectation.
He watched the developments unfold with a sense of cautious expectation.

Synset Examples

She approached the interview with cautious expectation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future
  • Antonym: pessimism - tendency to expect the worst or see the worst aspect of things
