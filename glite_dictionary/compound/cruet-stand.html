cruet-stand

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːɪt stænd / (AmE), / ˈkruːɪt stænd / (BrE)
A small frame or holder designed to hold cruets containing condiments such as oil, vinegar, salt, or pepper, typically found on dining tables.
stand for cruets with condiments

Examples

The waiter brought a cruet-stand so we could season our salad as we liked.
She polished the silver cruet-stand before setting it on the dining table.
At the restaurant, each table was furnished with a cruet-stand containing oil, vinegar, and pepper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruet - small container for liquids like oil or vinegar

Etymology

From 'cruet' (a small container for liquids) and 'stand' (support or holder).
