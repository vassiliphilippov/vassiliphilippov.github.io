Virginia chain fern

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə tʃeɪn fɜrn / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniə tʃeɪn fɜːn / (BrE)
A North American fern species (Woodwardia virginica) found in wetlands and moist woodlands.
North American wetland fern species

Examples

The Virginia chain fern thrives in the marshlands along the eastern coast of North America.
Botanists often study the Virginia chain fern for its unique spore patterns.
I spotted several Virginia chain ferns growing near the edge of the wetland trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain fern - common name for ferns in the genus Woodwardia
