Ellis Island

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛlɪs ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪs ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
an island in New York Bay that served as the main immigration station for the United States from 1892 to 1954
former main US immigration station in New York

Examples

Many immigrants first set foot in America at Ellis Island in the early 20th century.
The history of Ellis Island is closely tied to American immigration.
We visited the museum located on Ellis Island during our trip to New York.

Synset Examples

some twelve millions immigrants passed through Ellis Island
