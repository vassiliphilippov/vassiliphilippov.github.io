Sunyavadi approach

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːnjəˈvɑːdi əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsuːnjəˈvɑːdi əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A philosophical method or perspective emphasizing the concept of emptiness (shunyata) and the attainment of inner peace, especially in Buddhist thought.
philosophy emphasizing emptiness and peace

Examples

The Sunyavadi approach encourages practitioners to let go of attachments and embrace emptiness.
Her teachings incorporate the Sunyavadi approach to help students find inner tranquility.
Some scholars debate the influence of the Sunyavadi approach on modern mindfulness practices.

Synset Examples

The Sunyavadi approach emphasizes inner peace.
He follows a Sunyavadi philosophy in his teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Madhyamaka - Buddhist school centered on the philosophy of emptiness

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'śūnyavāda' (doctrine of emptiness): śūnya (empty) + vāda (doctrine).
