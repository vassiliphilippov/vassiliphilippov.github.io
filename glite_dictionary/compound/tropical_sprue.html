tropical sprue

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːpɪkəl spru / (AmE), / ˈtrɒpɪkəl spruː / (BrE)
A chronic disease, most common in tropical regions, characterized by impaired absorption of nutrients in the small intestine, leading to diarrhea, weight loss, and malnutrition.
chronic tropical intestinal disorder
medical

Examples

Doctors in the region suspected the patient suffered from tropical sprue due to her persistent symptoms.
Proper diagnosis and dietary management can significantly improve outcomes for those with tropical sprue.
Researchers are still studying the exact cause of tropical sprue, which remains unclear despite decades of investigation.

Etymology

From 'tropical', relating to the tropics, and 'sprue', possibly of Dutch origin meaning 'mouth disease'.
