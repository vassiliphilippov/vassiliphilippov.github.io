protoplasmic astrocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˌplæzmɪk ˈæstroʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˌplæzmɪk ˈastrəʊˌsʌɪt / (BrE)
a type of astrocyte located in the grey matter of the brain and spinal cord, characterized by their numerous short, branched processes
type of astrocyte in grey matter
medical

Examples

Protoplasmic astrocytes are primarily found in the grey matter of the cerebral cortex.
The structure of a protoplasmic astrocyte differs significantly from that of a fibrous astrocyte.
Researchers are studying how protoplasmic astrocytes support neural function and synaptic regulation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fibrous astrocyte - a type of astrocyte found in white matter

Etymology

From 'protoplasmic' (relating to protoplasm) and 'astrocyte' (from Greek 'astron' meaning star and 'kytos' meaning cell).
