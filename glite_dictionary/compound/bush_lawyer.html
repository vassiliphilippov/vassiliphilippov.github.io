bush lawyer

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈlɔɪ.ər / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈlɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
a stout-stemmed, trailing shrub native to New Zealand, known for climbing and scrambling over other vegetation
New Zealand climbing shrub

Examples

The bush lawyer grows vigorously through the forest, often tangling with other shrubs and trees.
Hikers in New Zealand are advised to watch out for the thorny stems of the bush lawyer.
Native birds feed on the berries produced by the bush lawyer each summer.
