freetailed bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌteɪld bæt / (AmE), / ˈfriːteɪld bæt / (BrE)
A small, swift insect-eating bat with leathery ears and a long tail that extends beyond the tail membrane; found in warm regions.
a small insectivorous bat with a long tail

Examples

The Mexican freetailed bat is one of the most common bats in Texas.
Scientists observed thousands of freetailed bats emerging from the cave at dusk.
A freetailed bat can catch insects mid-flight thanks to its agility.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal of the order Chiroptera
