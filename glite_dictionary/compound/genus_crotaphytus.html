genus Crotaphytus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kroʊˈtæfɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs krəʊˈtæfɪtəs / (BrE)
A genus of lizards native to North America, commonly referred to as collared lizards, known for their distinctive neck bands and agility.
genus of collared lizards

Examples

The genus Crotaphytus includes several species of brightly colored collared lizards found in arid regions.
Biologists are studying how members of the genus Crotaphytus adapt to their rocky habitats.
Fossil evidence suggests that the genus Crotaphytus has existed for millions of years in North America.

Etymology

From Greek krotaphytos (temple of the head), the scientific name for the genus.
