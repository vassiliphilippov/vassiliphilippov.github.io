Duke of Argyll's tea tree

1. NOUN

/ ˌduk əv ɑrˈgaɪlz ˈti ˌtri / (AmE), / ˌdjuːk əv ɑːˈɡaɪlz ˈtiː ˌtriː / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub with spiny branches and violet-purple flowers, bearing orange-red berries, native from southeastern Europe to China
shrubby plant, violet flowers, orange-red berries

Examples

The Duke of Argyll's tea tree is often grown for its ornamental berries and attractive foliage.
Gardeners appreciate the Duke of Argyll's tea tree for its ability to thrive in difficult soils.
After flowering, the Duke of Argyll's tea tree produces clusters of bright orange-red berries.

Etymology

Named after Archibald Campbell, 3rd Duke of Argyll, who promoted the plant in Britain in the 18th century.
