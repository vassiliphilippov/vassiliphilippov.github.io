king of the herring

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ əv ðə ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / kɪŋ əv ðə ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
a large, thin, deep-water fish, typically 20 to 30 feet long, with a bright red dorsal fin; known for its unusual shape and size
large, thin deep-sea fish with a red dorsal fin

Examples

The long, silvery king of the herring is rarely seen near the ocean surface.
Marine biologists were thrilled to find a king of the herring washed up on the beach.
Legends about the king of the herring inspired many stories of sea serpents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarfish - an extremely long, ribbon-like deep-sea fish, often called the king of the herring
