snake muishond

1. NOUN

/ sneɪk ˈmɛɪ ʃɒnd / (AmE), / sneɪk ˈmɔɪ hɒnd / (BrE)
a small, slender, burrowing mammal of southern Africa with a white patch on the top of its head, belonging to the weasel family
a burrowing mammal of southern Africa

Examples

The snake muishond is known for its white-topped head and slender body.
Researchers observed a snake muishond burrowing in the dry savanna soil.
Unlike other mustelids, the snake muishond prefers open grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped polecat - a similar-looking small carnivorous mammal from Africa, also called 'zorilla'

Etymology

'Muishond' comes from Afrikaans, meaning 'mouse dog,' from Dutch 'muis' (mouse) and 'hond' (dog).
