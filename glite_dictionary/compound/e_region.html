E region

1. NOUN

/ i ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / iː ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
the region of the ionosphere, approximately 50 to 90 miles above the Earth, that reflects medium-length radio waves
part of ionosphere reflecting radio waves

Examples

Scientists tracked the changes in the E region to study how solar activity affects radio communication.
Some radio frequencies can travel long distances at night due to reflection from the E region of the ionosphere.
The altitude of the E region typically varies depending on solar and atmospheric conditions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: F region - a higher region of the ionosphere reflecting high-frequency radio waves
  • Degree Lower: D region - a lower region of the ionosphere absorbing low-frequency radio waves
