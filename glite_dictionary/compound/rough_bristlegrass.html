rough bristlegrass

1. NOUN

/ rʌf ˈbrɪsəlˌɡræs / (AmE), / rʌf ˈbrɪslˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European grass (Setaria verticillata), naturalized in North America, known for its rough, bristly seed heads and often considered a weed
a rough, weedy grass species

Examples

Rough bristlegrass is commonly found growing along roadsides and in neglected fields.
Farmers often struggle to control rough bristlegrass in their crops because of its resilience.
The bristly seed heads of rough bristlegrass can easily stick to clothing and animal fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail - a type of grass with bushy, tail-like flower spikes
