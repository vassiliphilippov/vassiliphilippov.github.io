fixed exchange rate

1. NOUN

/ fɪkst ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ reɪt / (AmE), / fɪkst ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ reɪt / (BrE)
an exchange rate system in which the value of a currency is set and maintained by a government at a specific level, often linked to another currency or a commodity such as gold, rather than determined by market forces
exchange rate set by a government

Examples

Many countries abandoned the fixed exchange rate system in favor of more flexible policies.
Under a fixed exchange rate regime, the central bank must intervene frequently to maintain the set value.
Speculators can cause serious problems in countries with a fixed exchange rate if they believe the currency is overvalued.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: floating exchange rate - an exchange rate that is determined by market forces and can change freely
