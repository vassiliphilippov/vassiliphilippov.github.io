sweet William

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈwɪljəm / (AmE), / swiːt ˈwɪljəm / (BrE)
A garden plant (Dianthus barbatus) native to Eurasia, grown for its dense clusters of colorful, flat-topped flowers.
ornamental garden plant with colorful flowers

Examples

The sweet William in her garden bloomed in vibrant shades of red and pink.
He planted sweet William along the walkway for their cheerful appearance and pleasant fragrance.
Since childhood, she has always admired the clusters of sweet William in her grandmother's yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink - any plant of the genus Dianthus, including carnations and related species

Etymology

Named in the 16th century; possible reference to William, Duke of Cumberland, or a corruption of the French "oeillet" (carnation).
