Epiphany of Our Lord

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪfəni ʌv aʊər lɔrd / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪfəni əv aʊə lɔːd / (BrE)
A Christian festival celebrated twelve days after Christmas, commemorating the visit of the Magi to the infant Jesus.
Christian festival, twelfth day after Christmas

Examples

Many Christians attend church services on the Epiphany of Our Lord to remember the journey of the Magi.
The Epiphany of Our Lord is also known as Three Kings' Day in some cultures.
Children in Spain often receive gifts on the Epiphany of Our Lord instead of Christmas day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epiphany - Christian festival commemorating the revelation of Christ to the Gentiles

Etymology

Epiphany from Greek 'epiphaneia' meaning 'appearance' or 'manifestation'.
