greatest common factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtəst ˈkɑmən ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtɪst ˈkɒmən ˈfæktə / (BrE)
the largest number that can be exactly divided into each of two or more integers without leaving a remainder
largest integer dividing two or more numbers

Examples

To simplify the fraction, first find the greatest common factor of the numerator and denominator.
The greatest common factor of 18 and 24 is 6.
Students learned how to calculate the greatest common factor of two numbers in math class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greatest common divisor - the largest integer that divides two or more numbers
  • Antonym: least common multiple - the smallest number that is a multiple of two or more integers
