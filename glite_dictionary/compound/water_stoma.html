water stoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˈstoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈstəʊmə / (BrE)
a small pore on the surface or margin of a leaf of higher plants through which water is exuded
leaf pore for water exudation

Examples

Guttation can occur through a water stoma at the edge of a plant's leaf.
Each water stoma helps the plant release excess water overnight.
Scientists examined the function of the water stoma under different humidity levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydathode - a tissue or structure in plants that exudes water, usually via specialized pores

Etymology

From 'water' (liquid) + Greek 'stoma' (mouth, opening), referencing its function as an opening for water.
