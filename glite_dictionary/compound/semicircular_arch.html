semicircular arch

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛm.iˈsɝː.kjə.lɚ ˈɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsɛm.iˈsɜː.kjʊ.lə ˈɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch that forms a half-circle, drawn from a single center point
arch shaped as a half-circle

Examples

The Roman aqueducts are famous for their use of the semicircular arch in construction.
A semicircular arch provides both strength and elegant form to the doorway.
The bridge was rebuilt using traditional semicircular arches to preserve its historical character.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: segmental arch - an arch that forms less than a half-circle

Etymology

From Latin 'semi' (half) + 'circularis' (circular) + 'arch', referring to its shape.
