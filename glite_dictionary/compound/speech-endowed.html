speech-endowed

1. ADJ

/ spiːtʃ ɪnˈdaʊd / (AmE), / spiːtʃ ɪnˈdaʊd / (BrE)
Able to speak; having the capability of speech.
able to speak

Examples

Only humans are truly speech-endowed among all creatures.
Some researchers study the evolution of speech-endowed species.
The parrot, though not truly speech-endowed, can mimic human words.

Synset Examples

the speaking animal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mute - unable to speak
  • Antonym: speechless - temporarily unable to speak; without speech

Etymology

Formed from 'speech' and 'endowed', from Middle English 'endewen', based on 'endow' meaning to provide with a quality or ability.
