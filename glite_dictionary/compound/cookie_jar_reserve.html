cookie jar reserve

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊki dʒɑr rɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / ˈkʊki dʒɑː rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
an amount of money secretly kept by a company, used to artificially smooth or adjust quarterly earnings reports
hidden reserve for earnings management

Examples

The company dipped into their cookie jar reserve to make their quarterly results look better.
Regulators have cracked down on the use of cookie jar reserves in recent years.
Analysts warned that the firm's strong profits might be due to manipulation through a cookie jar reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accounting reserve - funds set aside for future needs or liabilities in accounting

Etymology

Metaphorically from the idea of taking treats from a cookie jar as needed, applied to financial reserves.
