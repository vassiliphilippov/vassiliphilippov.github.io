genus Gorilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɡəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɡəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
The genus of large African apes that includes all species of gorilla.
the genus of gorillas (apes)

Examples

Scientists have classified all living gorillas under the genus Gorilla.
The genus Gorilla includes both the western and eastern species.
Research on the genus Gorilla has provided insights into evolutionary biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorilla - A large ape native to central Africa

Etymology

From Latin 'Gorilla', used as the zoological genus name from the 19th century, itself from Ancient Greek 'Γόριλλαι' (Gorillai), meaning tribe of hairy women.
