peculiar velocity

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkjuːljɚ vəˈlɑːsəti / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːliə vəˈlɒsəti / (BrE)
the velocity of an object measured relative to the local standard of rest, especially in astronomy
velocity relative to the local standard of rest

Examples

The peculiar velocity of the star indicates it is moving differently from its neighbors.
Astronomers calculate the peculiar velocity to better understand galactic motion.
Changes in a galaxy's peculiar velocity can reveal interactions with other galaxies.
