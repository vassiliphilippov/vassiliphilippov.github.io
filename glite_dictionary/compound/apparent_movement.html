apparent movement

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɛrənt ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / əˈparənt ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
an optical illusion in which stationary objects viewed in rapid succession appear to be moving
illusion of motion from still images

Examples

The apparent movement of characters in animation is created by playing images rapidly in sequence.
He studied the phenomenon of apparent movement as part of his research into visual perception.
Old movies rely on apparent movement to give the illusion of life to still images.

Synset Examples

the cinema relies on apparent motion
the succession of flashing lights gave an illusion of movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparent motion - illusion of motion in a sequence of still images
  • Antonym: real movement - actual physical movement, not just an illusion
