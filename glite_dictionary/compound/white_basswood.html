white basswood

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈbæsˌwʊd / (AmE), / waɪt ˈbæsˌwʊd / (BrE)
A North American tree of the linden family (Tilia americana var. heterophylla), especially found in the Allegheny region, known for its light-colored wood.
North American linden tree; light wood

Examples

The leaves of the white basswood are paler underneath compared to other basswoods.
Carpenters often use white basswood for carving because of its fine grain and soft texture.
In the Allegheny region, the white basswood is a common sight along forest edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basswood - North American tree of the linden family, also known as American linden
