fourscore year

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌskɔr ˈjɪr / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌskɔː ˈjɪə / (BrE)
an archaic term meaning eighty years.
eighty years (archaic)
archaic

Examples

Abraham Lincoln began his speech with the words, 'Four score and seven years ago.'
The city has changed dramatically over the last fourscore years.
He lived a full life, reaching the impressive age of fourscore years.

Synset Examples

The book details events from a time that was fourscore years past.
Fourscore years ago, this land was first settled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eighty - the number 80

Etymology

From 'four' and 'score' (meaning twenty), thus four times twenty.
