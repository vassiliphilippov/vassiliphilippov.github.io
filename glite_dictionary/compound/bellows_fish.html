bellows fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛloʊz fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɛləʊz fɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, bottom-dwelling marine fish of warm seas, having a compressed body and a long, toothless snout
small marine fish with long snout

Examples

The bellows fish uses its elongated snout to probe the ocean floor for food.
Divers in tropical waters often spot the bellows fish among coral reefs.
The unique shape of the bellows fish helps it blend in with seagrass and sand.
