sour-gum family

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊr ˌɡʌm ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə ˌɡʌm ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering trees in the order Myrtales, including the sour gum (Nyssa) and related species
botanical family including sour gum trees

Examples

Several species in the sour-gum family produce fruit that is valuable to wildlife.
Botanists debated the classification of the sour-gum family for many years.
The sour-gum family is found primarily in temperate and subtropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nyssaceae - the botanical family to which sour gum trees belong
  • Degree Lower: dogwood family - a related plant family in the order Cornales
