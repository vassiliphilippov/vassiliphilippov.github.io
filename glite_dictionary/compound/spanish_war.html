Spanish War

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ wɔr / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought between the United States and Spain in 1898, resulting in the U.S. gaining territories such as Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines
war between the U.S. and Spain in 1898

Examples

Many historians study the causes and outcomes of the Spanish War of 1898.
After the Spanish War, the United States emerged as a global power.
The treaty ending the Spanish War ceded several Spanish territories to the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish-American War - the war between the United States and Spain in 1898
