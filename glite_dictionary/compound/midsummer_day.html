Midsummer Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌsʌmɚ deɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsʌmə deɪ / (BrE)
A traditional quarter day in England, Wales, and Ireland, celebrated around June 24, marking the middle of summer.
quarter day around June 24 in England, Wales, and Ireland
british

Examples

Every year, the village hosts a fair to celebrate Midsummer Day.
Contracts in some parts of England used to be renewed on Midsummer Day.
The tradition of bonfires on Midsummer Day continues in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarter day - one of four specific days dividing the year in the British calendar
  • Degree Higher: Michaelmas - a quarter day celebrated in late September in England, Wales, and Ireland

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'mid-' (middle) and 'summer', referring to the middle of summer.
