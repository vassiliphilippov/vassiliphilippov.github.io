tin disease

1. NOUN

/ tɪn dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / tɪn dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a phenomenon in which white tin transforms into a grey powder at low temperatures, causing deterioration of the metal
transformation of tin to grey powder at low temperatures

Examples

The historic organ pipes suffered from tin disease after a harsh winter in the church.
At sub-zero temperatures, tin disease can cause equipment failure by turning metal brittle and crumbly.
Scientists studied the chemical process of tin disease to develop more resilient tin alloys.
