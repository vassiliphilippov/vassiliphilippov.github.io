castor bean plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.tɚ ˈbiːn ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.stə ˈbiːn ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A large tropical shrub native to Africa and Asia, with large palmate leaves and spiny capsules containing seeds used to produce castor oil and ricin.
tropical shrub source of castor oil and ricin

Examples

The castor bean plant is grown for its seeds, which yield valuable castor oil.
She recognized the distinctive large leaves of the castor bean plant in the botanical garden.
Farmers in tropical countries sometimes cultivate the castor bean plant for commercial purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castor oil plant - another term for castor bean plant
  • Degree Higher: ricin - a highly toxic compound obtained from the castor bean plant
