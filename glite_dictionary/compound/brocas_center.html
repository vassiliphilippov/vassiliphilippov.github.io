Broca's center

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkəz ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkəz ˈsentə / (BrE)
the part of the brain, usually in the left hemisphere, responsible for the production of speech
area of the brain controlling speech production
medical

Examples

Damage to the Broca's center can result in difficulty speaking or forming sentences.
Researchers studied the Broca's center to better understand language disorders.
During surgery, the doctor took care to avoid the patient's Broca's center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Broca's area - region of the brain involved in speech production
  • Antonym: Wernicke's area - part of the brain involved in understanding language

Etymology

Named after French physician Paul Broca, who discovered its function in the 19th century.
