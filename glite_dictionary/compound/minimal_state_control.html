minimal state control

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnəməl steɪt kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməl steɪt kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
A situation in which the government exercises only a small degree of regulation or intervention in individuals' personal lives or societal affairs.
limited government intervention

Examples

The new party platform called for minimal state control over economic activities.
Some political philosophers argue that minimal state control leads to greater personal freedom.
Debates around healthcare often center on the preferred degree of minimal state control in that sector.

Synset Examples

As a libertopist, she advocated for minimal state control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limited government - a political system where legal restrictions limit government powers
