city manager

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪti ˈmænɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈsɪti ˈmanɪdʒə / (BrE)
the chief administrative officer responsible for managing the day-to-day operations of a city government, typically appointed rather than elected
chief administrator of a city

Examples

The city manager proposed a new budget to improve local services.
After the election, the council hired a new city manager to oversee operations.
The city manager addressed concerns about public transportation at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mayor - the elected head of a city, town, or municipality
