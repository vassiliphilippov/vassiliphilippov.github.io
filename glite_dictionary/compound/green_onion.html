green onion

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
A young onion harvested before the bulb has fully developed, with a long green stalk and a mild flavor, typically eaten raw in salads or as a garnish.
young onion with mild flavor, eaten raw

Examples

She added chopped green onion to the salad for extra flavor.
I prefer my omelette topped with fresh green onion slices.
The recipe calls for both garlic and green onion as garnish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallion - another term for green onion
