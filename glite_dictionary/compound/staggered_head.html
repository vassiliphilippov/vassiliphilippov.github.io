staggered head

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡɚd hɛd / (AmE), / ˈstæɡəd hɛd / (BrE)
A headline in which the first line is aligned to the left margin and each subsequent line is progressively indented to the right.
headline with each line further indented

Examples

The designer chose a staggered head to give the article a more dynamic appearance.
Each staggered head in the magazine section guided the reader to a new story.
She explained how to create a staggered head during the layout workshop.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flush head - a headline with all lines aligned to the left margin
