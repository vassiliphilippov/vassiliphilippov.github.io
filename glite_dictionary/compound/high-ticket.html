high-ticket

1. ADJ

/ haɪ ˈtɪkət / (AmE), / haɪ ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
(of a product or service) costing a large amount of money; very expensive.
expensive; costing a lot of money

Examples

Many companies offer special financing for high-ticket electronics and appliances.
She specializes in selling high-ticket consulting services to large corporations.
The store manager kept the high-ticket items locked in a display case.

Synset Examples

big-ticket items like cars and furs
a big-ticket government program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big-ticket - very expensive; costing a lot of money
  • Antonym: low-ticket - inexpensive; costing little money
