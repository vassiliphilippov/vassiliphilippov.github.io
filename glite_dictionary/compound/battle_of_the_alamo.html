Battle of the Alamo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l̩ əv ði əˈlæmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ðiː əˈlæməʊ / (BrE)
A significant 1836 siege during the Texas Revolution, in which Mexican forces captured the Alamo mission in San Antonio and killed the defenders.
1836 siege of the Alamo mission in Texas

Examples

The Battle of the Alamo is remembered as a pivotal moment in Texas history.
Many historians study the Battle of the Alamo to understand the causes of the Texas Revolution.
The bravery displayed during the Battle of the Alamo continues to inspire Americans today.
