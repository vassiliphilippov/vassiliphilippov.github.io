liberal arts

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚəl ˈɑrts / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəl ˈɑːts / (BrE)
Academic studies intended to provide general knowledge and develop intellectual abilities, rather than to train for a specific profession.
general academic studies; not vocational

Examples

She chose to study the liberal arts, focusing on literature, history, and philosophy.
Many universities require students to complete a liberal arts curriculum before selecting a major.
A liberal arts education encourages critical thinking and broad knowledge across multiple disciplines.

Synset Examples

the college of arts and sciences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanities - academic disciplines studying human culture
  • Antonym: vocational education - training focused on specific jobs or professions

Etymology

From Latin 'liberales artes', meaning 'arts suitable for a free person'.
