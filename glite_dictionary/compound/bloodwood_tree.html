bloodwood tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌwʊd tri / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌwʊd triː / (BrE)
A deciduous South African tree with large pinnate leaves, fragrant orange-yellow flowers, and red sap; valued for its heavy, strong, durable wood.
South African tree with red sap and durable wood

Examples

The bloodwood tree is known for its striking red sap and durable timber.
Carpenters often seek out bloodwood trees for high-quality furniture making.
The fragrant flowers of the bloodwood tree attract bees and other pollinators.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌwʊd tri / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌwʊd triː / (BrE)
A spiny shrub or small tree native to Central America and the West Indies, with bipinnate leaves, small bright yellow flowers, and hard brown or reddish heartwood used to make black dye.
tree with hard reddish wood for dye

Examples

The bloodwood tree produces a heartwood prized for its use in making black dye.
Local artisans often use wood from the bloodwood tree to craft durable tools and instruments.
Bright yellow flowers appear in spring on the bloodwood tree, attracting many pollinators.
