Rh positive

1. ADJ

/ ɑr eɪtʃ ˈpɑzətɪv / (AmE), / ɑː ʧ ˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
having the Rh factor (Rh antigen) present on the surface of red blood cells; belonging to the blood group whose red cells carry the Rh factor.
having the Rh antigen in red blood cells
medical

Examples

All donors must be tested to see if they are Rh positive or negative before giving blood.
If a pregnant woman is Rh positive, there is usually no risk of Rh incompatibility for her baby.
About 85% of people in the world are Rh positive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Rh negative - lacking the Rh factor (Rh antigen) on red blood cells

Etymology

From 'Rh' for Rhesus (monkey), in which the antigen was first discovered; 'positive' indicates presence of the factor.
