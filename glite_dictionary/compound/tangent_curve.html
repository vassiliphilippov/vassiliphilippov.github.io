tangent curve

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.dʒənt kɜːrv / (AmE), / ˈtæn.dʒənt kɜːv / (BrE)
the graph of the equation y = tan x, representing how the tangent function varies as the angle x changes
graph of y = tan x

Examples

The tangent curve has vertical asymptotes at every odd multiple of π/2.
When studying trigonometry, students often examine the properties of the tangent curve.
The graphing calculator can plot a tangent curve to help visualize its periodic nature.

Synset Examples

The shape of the tangent curve is the same for each full rotation of the angle and so the function is called ‘periodic’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sine curve - the graph of the equation y = sin x
  • Synonym: cosine curve - the graph of the equation y = cos x

Etymology

From Latin 'tangens' (touching), referring to the tangent function in mathematics, and 'curve', from Latin 'curvus' (bent).
