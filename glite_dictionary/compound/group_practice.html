group practice

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ɡruːp ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
a medical organization in which several physicians work together and share facilities, expenses, and staff
medical practice by a group of physicians
medical

Examples

The doctors decided to form a group practice to provide more comprehensive care to their patients.
Working in a group practice allows physicians to share on-call duties and reduce individual workload.
After completing her residency, she joined a local group practice specializing in family medicine.
