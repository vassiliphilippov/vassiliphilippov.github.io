soft touch

1. NOUN

/ sɔft tʌtʃ / (AmE), / sɒft tʌtʃ / (BrE)
a person who is easily persuaded or deceived, especially someone who is likely to give in to requests or be taken advantage of
a person easily taken advantage of

Examples

When it comes to lending money, Mark is a real soft touch.
The salespeople knew she was a soft touch and targeted her repeatedly.
Don't be such a soft touch — you need to say no sometimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pushover - a person who is easy to influence or defeat
  • Antonym: tough nut - a person who is difficult to deal with or persuade

2. NOUN

/ sɑft tʌtʃ / (AmE), / sɒft tʌtʃ / (BrE)
a gentle or considerate approach, especially in physical contact or in dealing with people
gentle or considerate approach

Examples

Her soft touch with the children made her a favorite among students.
The artist applied a soft touch to create subtle shading in the painting.
Many patients appreciate a nurse who has a soft touch during procedures.

Synset Examples

He has a soft touch when it comes to teaching.
The therapist’s soft touch helped ease my anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentle touch - delicate or careful manner, especially in handling people or objects
  • Antonym: firm touch - a manner that is confident and sometimes strict or forceful
