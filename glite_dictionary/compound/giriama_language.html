Giriama language

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪˈriːəmə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɪˈriːəmə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken in the coastal regions of eastern Kenya, primarily by the Giriama people
a Bantu language of coastal Kenya

Examples

The Giriama language is spoken by over half a million people along Kenya's coast.
Scholars have published several works on the grammar of the Giriama language.
She is proud to teach her children the Giriama language to preserve their heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swahili - a Bantu language widely spoken in East Africa

Etymology

Giriama, the name of the ethnic group who speak the language, with 'language' appended.
