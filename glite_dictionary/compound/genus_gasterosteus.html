genus Gasterosteus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɡæstəˈroʊstiəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɡastəˈrəʊstiəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Gasterosteidae, comprising small fishes commonly known as sticklebacks.
type genus of sticklebacks

Examples

The three-spined stickleback belongs to the genus Gasterosteus.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary adaptations within the genus Gasterosteus.
Many species in the genus Gasterosteus exhibit unique mating behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickleback - small fish with spines on its back

Etymology

From Greek gastēr (stomach) + osteon (bone), referring to bony plates; scientific Latin taxonomic term.
