great white heron

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt waɪt ˈhɛr.ən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt waɪt ˈhɛr.ən / (BrE)
a large white heron (Ardea herodias occidentalis) found in southern Florida and parts of the Caribbean, regarded as a white variant of the great blue heron or as a variety of the Old World white egret.
large white heron of North America

Examples

The great white heron is a striking sight in the wetlands of southern Florida.
Birdwatchers eagerly search for the elusive great white heron during migration season.
Unlike its cousin the great blue heron, the great white heron has entirely white plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egret - a group of herons with white plumage

3. NOUN

a large white heron native to Florida and the Florida Keys, sometimes considered a form of the great blue heron
large white heron of Florida

Examples

The great white heron can often be seen wading in the shallow waters of the Florida Keys.
Unlike other herons, the great white heron is entirely white and larger in size.
Birdwatchers flock to Florida hoping to catch a glimpse of the rare great white heron.
