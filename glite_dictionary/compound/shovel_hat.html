shovel hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvəl hæt / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl hæt / (BrE)
a stiff, broad-brimmed hat with the sides turned up and the brim projecting in front, traditionally worn by some clergymen in Britain
traditional stiff clerical hat (Britain)
british

Examples

The bishop adjusted his shovel hat before entering the cathedral.
In Victorian England, it was common to see clergy wearing the distinctive shovel hat.
His shovel hat gave him an air of solemn authority as he walked through the parish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvəl hæt / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl hæt / (BrE)
a wide-brimmed hat, originally worn for sun protection, with a shape resembling a shovel; historically, sometimes referring to a style of hat with a broad brim and rounded crown.
wide-brimmed protective hat

Examples

He wore a shovel hat while gardening in the bright afternoon sun.
The museum displayed a nineteenth-century shovel hat as part of its fashion exhibit.
She laughed when her brother tried on the oversized shovel hat at the costume shop.
