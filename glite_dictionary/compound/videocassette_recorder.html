videocassette recorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪdioʊkəˈsɛt rɪˌkɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌvɪdɪəʊkəˈsɛt rɪˌkɔːdə / (BrE)
an electronic device for recording and playing back television programs and other videos using magnetic tape cassettes
device for playing and recording video tapes

Examples

My parents still have a videocassette recorder connected to their old television set.
We used the videocassette recorder to record our favorite shows before digital streaming existed.
The videocassette recorder was once a must-have item in every living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: VCR - abbreviation for videocassette recorder
  • Degree Higher: DVD player - device for playing DVDs, which replaced videocassette recorders

Etymology

Formed from 'video', 'cassette', and 'recorder', first used in the 1970s with the rise of consumer video technology.
