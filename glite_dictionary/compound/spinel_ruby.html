spinel ruby

1. NOUN

/ spɪˈnɛl ˈruːbi / (AmE), / spɪˈnɛl ˈruːbi / (BrE)
a gemstone-quality spinel that is typically dark red in color, often mistaken for a true ruby
a dark red spinel gemstone

Examples

The museum displayed a magnificent spinel ruby among its collection of rare gemstones.
Many historic 'rubies' in royal crowns were actually spinel ruby rather than true ruby.
Collectors value a fine spinel ruby for its brilliant color and rarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinel - a hard glassy mineral used as a gemstone
  • Synonym: ruby - a precious stone consisting of corundum in a red variety

Etymology

'Spinel' from French 'spinelle', perhaps from Latin 'spinella' (little thorn), likely relating to the crystal form.
