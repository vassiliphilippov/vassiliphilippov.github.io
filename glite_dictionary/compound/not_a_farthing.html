not a farthing

1. X

/ nɑt ə ˈfɑrðɪŋ / (AmE), / nɒt ə ˈfɑːðɪŋ / (BrE)
not even a small amount; nothing at all
not even a small amount

Examples

He claimed he didn't owe not a farthing to anyone in the company.
Despite her hard work, she received not a farthing for her efforts.
After the long dispute, the plaintiff was awarded not a farthing in damages.

Synset Examples

Not a farthing was wasted during the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not a penny - not even the smallest amount of money
  • Synonym: not a jot - not at all; not even a tiny amount

Etymology

From 'farthing', a historical British coin of very small value.
