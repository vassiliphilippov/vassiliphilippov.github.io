radio spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the range of electromagnetic frequencies allocated for transmitting radio, television, radar, and other wireless communications
range of frequencies for wireless communications

Examples

The government regulates the use of the radio spectrum to prevent interference between different broadcasters.
Mobile phones, television, and Wi-Fi all operate within different parts of the radio spectrum.
As demand for wireless services grows, the radio spectrum is becoming an increasingly valuable resource.
