delta rhythm

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛltə ˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈdɛltə ˌrɪðəm / (BrE)
a type of brainwave characterized by high amplitude and low frequency (1–4 Hz), typically present during deep dreamless sleep
slow brainwave during deep sleep
medical

Examples

The presence of delta rhythm on the EEG indicates that the patient is in deep sleep.
Researchers studied the delta rhythm patterns in children and adults to learn about sleep stages.
Abnormalities in the delta rhythm may signal certain neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: alpha rhythm - brainwave seen during relaxed wakefulness
  • Degree Higher: theta rhythm - brainwave slower than alpha but faster than delta

Etymology

From 'delta', denoting the shape of the waveform, and 'rhythm', referring to the regular pattern.
