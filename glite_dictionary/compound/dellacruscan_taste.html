Dellacruscan taste

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛl.əˈkrʌs.kən teɪst / (AmE), / ˌdɛl.əˈkrʌs.kən teɪst / (BrE)
A preference or appreciation for antiquated or old-fashioned styles, especially in literature or art.
preference for old-fashioned style

Examples

His Dellacruscan taste made him prefer classical poetry to contemporary works.
The museum's new director sought to move away from the Dellacruscan taste of previous curators.
Those with a Dellacruscan taste often found it difficult to appreciate modern art movements.

Synset Examples

His Dellacruscan tastes often clashed with the modern trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquarian - having a love of things of the past
  • Antonym: modern - relating to present or recent times

Etymology

From Dellacruscan, referring to the literary circle inspired by the Italian poet Della Crusca in the late 18th century, known for ornate and old-fashioned style.
