beach plum

1. NOUN

/ bitʃ plʌm / (AmE), / biːtʃ plʌm / (BrE)
a shrub native to the northeastern coast of North America, producing showy white flowers and edible purple fruit
North American coastal shrub with edible fruit

Examples

The beach plum thrives in sandy soils along the Atlantic coastline.
Local jams made from beach plums are popular in New England markets.
She planted a beach plum in her seaside garden to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a type of fruit or the tree that bears it

2. NOUN

/ bitʃ plʌm / (AmE), / biːtʃ plʌm / (BrE)
a small, dark purple fruit that grows on coastal shrubs in eastern North America, used especially in jams and pies
a small dark purple fruit from coastal shrubs

Examples

We made jelly from the beach plum harvest this summer.
The coastal sand dunes are home to the wild beach plum.
She tasted the homemade beach plum pie at the fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a round, juicy fruit with a smooth skin and a hard pit
