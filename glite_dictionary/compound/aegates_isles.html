Aegates Isles

1. PROPN

/ iˈɡeɪtiːz aɪlz / (AmE), / iˈɡeɪtiːz aɪlz / (BrE)
a group of islands west of Sicily, now known as the Egadi Islands, famous as the site of a decisive Roman naval victory over Carthage in 241 BC that ended the First Punic War
islands west of Sicily, site of historic naval battle

Examples

The Romans achieved a decisive victory at the Aegates Isles, ending the First Punic War.
Today, the Aegates Isles are known as the Egadi Islands, a popular tourist destination in Italy.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient shipwrecks near the Aegates Isles related to the famous naval battle.

Etymology

From Latin Aegates Insulae, the ancient name for the Egadi Islands.
