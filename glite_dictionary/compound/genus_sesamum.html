genus Sesamum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɛsəməm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɛsəməm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical African and Indian herbs, including the sesame plant, known for their edible seeds and oil.
genus of herbs including sesame

Examples

Many edible oils are derived from the seeds of plants in the genus Sesamum.
Botanists study the genus Sesamum for its agricultural and medicinal value.
The genus Sesamum includes several species grown for their high oil content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sesame - annual plant of the genus Sesamum grown for its seeds

Etymology

From Latin 'Sesamum', from Greek 'sēsamon', referring to the sesame plant.
