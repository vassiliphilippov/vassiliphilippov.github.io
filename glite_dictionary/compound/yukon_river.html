Yukon River

1. PROPN

/ ˈjuːkɑn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈjuːkɒn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major North American river that flows westward from the Yukon Territory in Canada through central Alaska to the Bering Sea
major river in northwest North America

Examples

The Yukon River is famous for its role in the Klondike Gold Rush of the late 19th century.
Salmon migrate up the Yukon River every year to reach their spawning grounds.
Many remote communities rely on the Yukon River for transportation and fishing.

Etymology

Named after the Yukon Territory; 'Yukon' may derive from a Gwich’in word meaning 'white water river.'
