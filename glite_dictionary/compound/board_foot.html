board foot

1. NOUN

/ bɔrd fʊt / (AmE), / bɔːd fʊt / (BrE)
a unit of volume for timber, equal to a piece of wood 1 foot long, 1 foot wide, and 1 inch thick
unit of volume for timber

Examples

The carpenter ordered 50 board feet of pine for the new cabinets.
Lumber is often sold by the board foot in the United States and Canada.
Calculate the number of board feet required before purchasing the wood for your project.
