St Peter's wort

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈpiːtərz wɔrt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtəz wɔːt / (BrE)
A European perennial plant of the St John's wort family, found from Ireland and France to western Siberia.
European perennial plant, St John's wort family

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of St Peter's wort brighten the meadows each summer.
Botanists have documented St Peter's wort growing wild throughout western Europe.
Some gardeners prefer St Peter's wort for its hardiness and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St John's wort - a related plant of the Hypericum genus used for herbal remedies
