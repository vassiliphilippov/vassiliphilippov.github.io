Blenheim spaniel

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛnəm ˈspæn.əl / (AmE), / ˈblɛnɪm ˈspæn.əl / (BrE)
A red-and-white variety of the English toy spaniel, known for its affectionate nature and distinctive coloring.
red-and-white English toy spaniel

Examples

The Blenheim spaniel is easily recognized by its rich chestnut markings on a pearly white coat.
She decided to adopt a Blenheim spaniel because of their friendly and gentle temperament.
At the dog show, the Blenheim spaniel charmed the judges with its elegant appearance and lively personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English toy spaniel - a small breed of spaniel with a rounded head and large eyes

Etymology

Named after Blenheim Palace, where the variety was favored by English nobility.
