Solo man

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊloʊ mæn / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləʊ mæn / (BrE)
an extinct hominin from the late Pleistocene, known from fossil remains found in Java; the skull resembles Neanderthal man but has a smaller cranial capacity.
extinct hominin from Java, late Pleistocene

Examples

Archaeologists discovered new fossils belonging to Solo man near the banks of the Solo River.
The cranial features of Solo man show similarities to, but also differences from, Neanderthals.
Research on Solo man has contributed to debates about human evolution in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

Named after the Solo River in Java, where the fossils were discovered.
