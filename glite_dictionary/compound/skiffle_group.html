skiffle group

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪfəl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈskɪfəl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a band of musicians who perform skiffle music, typically using homemade or improvised instruments alongside guitars and other standard instruments
band playing skiffle music

Examples

The Beatles started out as a skiffle group before becoming famous worldwide.
Many British teenagers in the 1950s formed their own skiffle group with homemade instruments.
The skiffle group performed lively renditions of traditional folk songs at the community hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skiffle - a type of folk music with blues, jazz, and American folk influences, often using improvised instruments

Etymology

From 'skiffle', a style of folk music, and 'group', referring to a band of musicians.
