Turkish tobacco

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.kɪʃ təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.kɪʃ təˈbæk.əʊ / (BrE)
a dark, aromatic tobacco from eastern Europe, especially used in cigarettes
dark, aromatic cigarette tobacco from eastern Europe

Examples

Many premium cigarettes are blended with Turkish tobacco to create a rich, distinctive flavor.
The aroma of Turkish tobacco is highly prized by cigarette manufacturers worldwide.
Some pipe smokers prefer Turkish tobacco for its unique, spicy notes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Virginia tobacco - a type of mild tobacco grown in Virginia, used in cigarettes and cigars
