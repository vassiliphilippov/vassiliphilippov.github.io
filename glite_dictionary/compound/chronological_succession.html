chronological succession

1. NOUN

/ kɹəˌnɑːlədʒɪkəl səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / kɹəˌnɒlədʒɪkəl səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the sequence of events arranged in the order in which they occurred in time
sequence of events in time order

Examples

The historian arranged the events in chronological succession to show how the revolution unfolded.
A chronological succession of discoveries led to the modern understanding of genetics.
The report outlines a chronological succession of meetings that shaped the policy.

Synset Examples

the doctor saw a sequence of patients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a particular order in which related events or things follow each other
  • Synonym: series - a number of events or things of a similar kind arranged or occurring in sequence

Etymology

From Greek *khronos* (time) and Latin *successionem* (a following after).
