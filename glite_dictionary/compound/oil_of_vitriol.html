oil of vitriol

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl əv ˈvɪtriəl / (AmE), / ɔɪl əv ˈvɪtrɪəl / (BrE)
an old name for sulfuric acid (H2SO4), a highly corrosive liquid used in industry
old name for sulfuric acid

Examples

The laboratory stored bottles of highly concentrated oil of vitriol for chemical experiments.
Historians found references to oil of vitriol in early industrial manufacturing records.
Accidental spills of oil of vitriol can cause serious burns and require immediate neutralization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfuric acid - a strong, corrosive acid (H2SO4) used in industry

Etymology

From 'vitriol', derived from Latin 'vitriolum' (sulphate of iron), referring to the glassy appearance of sulfate salts.
