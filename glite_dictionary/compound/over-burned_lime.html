over-burned lime

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚˈbɝːnd ˌlaɪm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːnd ˌlaɪm / (BrE)
Lime that has been heated above 1,100°C, losing its reactivity and bonding strength.
lime fired until non-reactive

Examples

Builders avoid using over-burned lime in mortar because it does not react well with water.
The kiln operator was careful not to produce over-burned lime, which would weaken the final product.
Due to excessive firing, the batch of over-burned lime failed to provide the necessary strength for construction.

Synset Examples

Overburned lime will not hold up to to freeze-thaw weather cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lime - a white caustic alkaline substance obtained by heating limestone
