Flint River

1. PROPN

/ flɪnt ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / flɪnt ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in western Georgia, United States, that flows south to join the Chattahoochee River at the Florida border, forming the Apalachicola River.
river in western Georgia, USA

Examples

The Flint River is a vital water source for agriculture in southern Georgia.
Heavy rainfall caused the Flint River to flood several communities last year.
Researchers monitor water quality in the Flint River to protect local ecosystems.
