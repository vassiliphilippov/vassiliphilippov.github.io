clockwork universe

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑːkˌwɝk ˈjuːnəˌvɝs / (AmE), / ˈklɒkˌwɜːk ˈjuːnɪˌvɜːs / (BrE)
a philosophical idea that the universe operates like a mechanical clock, set in motion by God and following predictable laws of nature, especially as described by Newtonian mechanics
the universe as a mechanical, law-governed system

Examples

The Enlightenment thinkers often advocated for the clockwork universe theory, seeing nature as predictable and ordered.
Many scientists once believed in a clockwork universe, where all events could be explained by physical laws.
Debates arose over whether free will could exist in a clockwork universe governed entirely by mechanical rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanistic - relating to the theory that natural phenomena follow mechanical laws
