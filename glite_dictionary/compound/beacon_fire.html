beacon fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːkən ˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈbiːkən ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a fire lit on a hill or tower and visible from a distance, used as a signal or warning
signal fire visible at a distance

Examples

At dusk, the beacon fire atop the hill warned villagers of approaching danger.
In ancient times, a beacon fire would signal the arrival of important news.
The soldiers watched anxiously for the beacon fire to appear on the distant tower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beacon - a light or fire serving as a warning, signal, or guide
