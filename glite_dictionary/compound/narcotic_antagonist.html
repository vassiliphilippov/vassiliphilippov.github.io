narcotic antagonist

1. NOUN

/ nɑrˈkɑɾɪk ænˈtæɡənɪst / (AmE), / nɑːˈkɒtɪk ænˈtæɡənɪst / (BrE)
a drug that counteracts the effects of narcotics, especially by reversing respiratory depression caused by opioid overdose
drug that reverses narcotic effects
medical

Examples

The patient was given a narcotic antagonist after showing signs of opioid overdose.
Naloxone is a commonly used narcotic antagonist in emergency situations.
Doctors administer a narcotic antagonist to reverse life-threatening respiratory depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opioid antagonist - drug that blocks the effects of opioids

Etymology

narcotic from Latin 'narcoticus' (numbing), antagonist from Greek 'antagonistēs' (opponent).
