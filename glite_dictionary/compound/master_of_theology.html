Master of Theology

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɚ əv θiˈɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stər əv θiˈɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
A postgraduate academic degree in theology, usually awarded after one to two years of advanced study beyond a bachelor's degree.
postgraduate theology degree

Examples

She completed her Master of Theology at a renowned seminary in the United States.
With a Master of Theology, he pursued a career in religious education and research.
Her Master of Theology thesis focused on early Christian ethics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Bachelor of Theology - undergraduate degree in theology
  • Degree Higher: Doctor of Theology - doctoral degree in theology

Etymology

From Latin 'magister' (master) + Greek 'theologia' (theology, study of God).
