sting operation

1. NOUN

/ stɪŋ ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / stɪŋ ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an undercover operation in which law enforcement agents or investigators pose as criminals or victims to catch a person committing a crime
undercover police trap for criminals

Examples

The FBI conducted a sting operation to catch the corrupt officials in the act.
A successful sting operation led to the arrest of several drug traffickers last month.
Authorities planned the sting operation for weeks before finally apprehending the suspects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undercover operation - a police or investigative action where true identities are concealed

Etymology

From 'sting' (fraud or swindle, American slang) + 'operation' (planned activity).

2. NOUN

/ stɪŋ ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / stɪŋ ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a carefully planned operation, typically by law enforcement, intended to catch someone committing a crime in the act, usually by means of deception or undercover work
police operation to catch offenders in the act

Examples

The police carried out a sting operation to catch drug dealers in the city.
A sting operation was launched to expose fraudulent financial advisers.
Several suspects were arrested during the sting operation at the warehouse.

Synset Examples

the police conducted a sting operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrapment - the act of tricking someone into committing a crime to secure their prosecution

Etymology

From 'sting' in the sense of a trap or deception, and 'operation' meaning an organized activity.
