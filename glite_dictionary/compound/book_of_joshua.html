Book of Joshua

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˈʤɑʃuə / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˈdʒɒʃuə / (BrE)
a book in the Old Testament that recounts how Joshua led the Israelites into Canaan after Moses's death
Old Testament book about Joshua leading Israelites

Examples

The Book of Joshua details the conquest of Jericho by the Israelites.
She referenced the Book of Joshua during the Bible study session.
Many historians debate the events described in the Book of Joshua.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Joshua - successor to Moses; leader of the Israelites after the Exodus

Etymology

Joshua from Hebrew Yehoshua‘, meaning 'Yahweh is salvation'
