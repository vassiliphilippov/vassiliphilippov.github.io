recombinant deoxyribonucleic acid

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑm.bɪ.nənt diːˌɑk.sɪˌraɪ.bə.njuː.kliː.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒm.bɪ.nənt diːˌɒk.sɪˈraɪ.bə.njuː.kliː.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
genetically engineered DNA created by combining DNA fragments from different organisms
genetically engineered DNA
medical

Examples

Many medical therapies today rely on recombinant deoxyribonucleic acid technology to produce important proteins.
The creation of recombinant deoxyribonucleic acid marked a breakthrough in genetic engineering.
Researchers inserted a gene from jellyfish into recombinant deoxyribonucleic acid to make the bacteria glow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recombinant DNA - genetically engineered DNA formed by combining sequences from different organisms

Etymology

From Latin *re-* (again) + *combinare* (to combine); 'deoxyribonucleic acid' from modern scientific Latin, denoting the molecule DNA.
