electrical discharge

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the release and passage of electricity through a medium, often resulting in a visible or detectable spark, flash, or current
release of electricity through a medium

Examples

During a thunderstorm, a dramatic electrical discharge occurs when lightning strikes the ground.
Careless handling of equipment may result in an unintended electrical discharge causing damage or injury.
Scientists studied the electrical discharge patterns generated in the laboratory experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spark - a flash of light caused by electricity jumping across a gap
  • Synonym: arc - a luminous electrical discharge between two electrodes
