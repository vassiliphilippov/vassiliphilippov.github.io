oak tree

1. NOUN

/ oʊk tri / (AmE), / əʊk triː / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree of the genus Quercus, with lobed leaves and bearing acorns.
large deciduous tree with acorns

Examples

We planted an oak tree in the backyard last spring.
The oak tree is famous for its strength and longevity.
Acorns from the old oak tree littered the ground after the storm.

Synset Examples

great oaks grow from little acorns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - tree of the genus Quercus, especially valued for wood

Etymology

From Old English 'āc' (oak) and 'trēow' (tree).

2. NOUN

/ oʊk triː / (AmE), / əʊk triː / (BrE)
A large deciduous or evergreen tree of the genus Quercus, known for its strong wood and lobed leaves, common in temperate regions.
a large, strong tree (genus Quercus)

Examples

The shade from the old oak tree made the picnic much more enjoyable.
A squirrel darted up the trunk of the oak tree in the park.
They built a swing on the strong, sturdy branch of the oak tree.

Synset Examples

We found a beautiful oak tree in the graveyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree of the genus Quercus
