verse line

1. NOUN

/ vɝs laɪn / (AmE), / vɜːs laɪn / (BrE)
a single line of metrical text in poetry or verse writing
a line in poetry with meter

Examples

Each verse line of the poem follows a strict rhythm and rhyme scheme.
He memorized several verse lines from Shakespeare's sonnets for his class.
The verse line ends abruptly, leaving the reader in suspense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - a single row of words or characters in written or printed text
