proto-Reformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊ ˌrɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊ ˌrɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
any of several religious movements that preceded and paved the way for the 16th-century Protestant Reformation.
early religious movements before the Protestant Reformation

Examples

Some historians argue that the teachings of John Wycliffe represented a proto-Reformation movement.
The proto-Reformation period saw increasing calls for reform within the Catholic Church.
Scholars study the proto-Reformation to understand the roots of religious change in Europe.

Synset Examples

Thus, he read Paul as sort of proto-Reformation theologian and wrote as if the Reformation exposition of Paul was a renewal of authentic Pauline theology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Reformation - the 16th-century movement for the reform of the Roman Catholic Church that led to the creation of Protestant churches

Etymology

From Greek 'prōto-' (first, earliest) + 'Reformation', the 16th-century religious movement.
