Malayan tapir

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪən ˈteɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ.ən ˈteɪ.pɪə / (BrE)
a large, herbivorous mammal native to the forests of Malaya and Sumatra, known for its distinctive black and white coloring
large black-and-white tapir of Southeast Asia

Examples

The Malayan tapir is easily recognized by its black and white markings.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining population of the Malayan tapir.
During our trip to Malaysia, we saw a Malayan tapir at the wildlife reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - a large, heavy-bodied herbivorous mammal with a fleshy, trunk-like snout
