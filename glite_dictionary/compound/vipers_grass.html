viper's grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪpɚz ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪpəz ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial plant of southern Europe with narrow leaves, solitary yellow flower heads, and long edible black roots shaped like carrots.
European herb with yellow flowers and edible black roots

Examples

The roots of viper's grass are known for their mild, nutty flavor and high nutritional value.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate viper's grass for its ornamental yellow blooms and unique edible roots.
In traditional Mediterranean cuisine, viper's grass is used in salads and stews.
