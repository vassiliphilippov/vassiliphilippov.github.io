reef line

1. NOUN

/ rif laɪn / (AmE), / riːf laɪn / (BrE)
(Nautical) one of several short lengths of rope or cord attached to a sail, used for tying part of the sail to reduce its area in strong winds.
nautical line for tying a reef in a sail

Examples

The sailor grabbed the reef line to secure the shortened sail in the heavy winds.
Before the storm began, the crew made sure each reef line was tightly fastened.
Knowing how to tie a reef line quickly is essential for safe sailing.

Synset Examples

One crewman must pull the reefing line as another crewman lowers the sail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reef - to reduce the area of a sail

Etymology

From 'reef' (to reduce sail area; of Dutch origin) and 'line' (cord or rope).
