right to vote

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt tə ˈvoʊt / (AmE), / ˈraɪt tə ˈvəʊt / (BrE)
the legal right of citizens to participate in choosing their government by casting a vote in elections
legal right to cast a vote in elections
legal

Examples

Many people fought hard to secure the right to vote for all citizens.
After the age of eighteen, you are granted the right to vote in most countries.
Debates about expanding the right to vote remain important in many democracies today.

Synset Examples

American women got the vote in 1920

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
  • Antonym: disenfranchisement - the state of being deprived of the right to vote

2. NOUN

/ raɪt tə voʊt / (AmE), / raɪt tə vəʊt / (BrE)
the legal entitlement of individuals to participate in elections by casting a vote
legal entitlement to participate in elections

Examples

The constitution guarantees every citizen the right to vote.
Women fought for decades to obtain the right to vote in many countries.
Some laws have been passed to protect the right to vote for minority groups.

Synset Examples

The people's right to vote is fundamental.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
  • Synonym: franchise - the right to vote; electoral rights
