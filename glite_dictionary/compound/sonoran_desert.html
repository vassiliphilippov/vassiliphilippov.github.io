Sonoran Desert

1. PROPN

/ səˈnɔɹən ˈdɛzɚt / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a vast desert region in southwestern Arizona, southeastern California, and northwestern Mexico, known for its arid climate and unique flora and fauna
desert in southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Sonoran Desert is famous for its saguaro cacti, which are unique to this region.
Temperatures in the Sonoran Desert can soar above 110 degrees Fahrenheit during the summer months.
Researchers conduct important ecological studies within the Sonoran Desert due to its rich biodiversity.

Etymology

Named after the Mexican state of Sonora, bordering the desert region.
