borscht circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːrʃt ˌsɜːrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːʃt ˌsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
(informal) a group of resorts in the Catskill Mountains of New York, known for being frequented primarily by Jewish guests and for fostering a thriving entertainment scene, especially for comedians and performers.
resort area in the Catskills for Jewish guests and entertainers
informal american

Examples

Many famous comedians got their start on the borscht circuit in the Catskills.
The borscht circuit played a crucial role in shaping American stand-up comedy.
She performed her act on the borscht circuit before appearing on national television.

Synset Examples

many comedians learned their trade playing the borscht circuit

Etymology

Borscht from Yiddish (via Russian/Ukrainian) for beet soup, indicating the area's Eastern European Jewish clientele; 'circuit' for the network of venues.
