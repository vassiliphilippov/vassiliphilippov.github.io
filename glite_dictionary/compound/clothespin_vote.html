clothespin vote

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊðzˌpɪn voʊt / (AmE), / ˈkləʊðzˌpɪn vəʊt / (BrE)
An informal method of voting in which participants indicate preferences by placing clothespins, typically on options represented in some physical way.
informal voting method using clothespins

Examples

At the community meeting, they used a clothespin vote to choose the new logo.
The teacher organized a clothespin vote so everyone could have a say without writing anything down.
Our club's decisions are often made by a quick clothespin vote during lunch.

Synset Examples

We used a clothespin vote to decide on lunch.
The clothespin vote was a clever way to gauge opinions.
