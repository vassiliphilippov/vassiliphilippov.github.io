prostate cancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌsteɪt ˈkænsər / (AmE), / ˈprɒsteɪt ˈkæn.sər / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that develops in the prostate gland, commonly affecting men, especially in older age.
cancer of the prostate gland
medical

Examples

Early detection of prostate cancer can significantly increase the chances of successful treatment.
The risk of developing prostate cancer rises with age, especially in men over 50.
Doctors recommend regular screenings for men at higher risk of prostate cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - a disease caused by uncontrolled division of abnormal cells
