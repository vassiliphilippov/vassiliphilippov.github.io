celestial hierarchy

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛs.tʃəl haɪˈrɑr.ki / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛs.tʃl haɪˈrɑː.ki / (BrE)
the collective body or systematic ranking of angels, especially as organized in Christian theology
the collective body or ranks of angels

Examples

In medieval theology, the celestial hierarchy was meticulously described by scholars such as Pseudo-Dionysius.
The archangels occupy a prominent position within the celestial hierarchy.
Many Renaissance artists depicted the celestial hierarchy in their religious paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelic order - a rank or class of angels

Etymology

From Latin 'caelestis' (heavenly) and Greek 'hierarchia' (sacred order), popularized by Christian theological writings.
