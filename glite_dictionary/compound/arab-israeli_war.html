Arab-Israeli War

1. NOUN

/ ɛrəb ɪzˈreɪli wɔr / (AmE), / ˈærəb ɪzˈreɪli wɔː / (BrE)
A brief war fought in June 1967 between Israel and neighboring Arab countries, resulting in a major Israeli victory and increased regional influence.
1967 war between Israel and Arab states

Examples

The Arab-Israeli War of 1967 dramatically altered the map of the Middle East.
Many books analyze the causes and outcomes of the Arab-Israeli War.
Veterans of the Arab-Israeli War often recount their personal experiences during the conflict.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɛrəb ɪzˈreɪli wɔr / (AmE), / ˈærəb ɪzˈreɪli wɔː / (BrE)
The war fought in October 1973 between Israel and a coalition of Arab states led by Egypt and Syria, starting on Yom Kippur.
war between Israel, Egypt, and Syria in 1973

Examples

The Arab-Israeli War of 1973 dramatically shifted the balance of power in the Middle East.
Many historians study the Arab-Israeli War to understand modern Middle Eastern politics.
The ceasefire agreement at the end of the Arab-Israeli War was brokered by the United Nations.

Related Terms
