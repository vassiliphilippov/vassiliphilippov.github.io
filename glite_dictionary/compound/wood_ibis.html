wood ibis

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈaɪbɪs / (AmE), / wʊd ˈaɪbɪs / (BrE)
any of several Old World birds of the genus Ibis, typically inhabiting wetlands and recognized by their long legs and bills.
species of Old World ibises (bird)

Examples

We watched a wood ibis wading through the marsh, searching for food.
The wetlands are home to several species, including the rare wood ibis.
A wood ibis spread its wings and took flight above the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibis - a wading bird with long legs and a long down-curved bill

2. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈaɪbɪs / (AmE), / wʊd ˈaɪbɪs / (BrE)
A large wading bird found in the Americas, resembling an ibis with a long, downward-curved bill, and inhabiting wooded swamps.
large American wading bird with curved bill

Examples

The wood ibis is often seen wading through the shallow waters of southern swamps.
During migration season, flocks of wood ibises can be spotted in coastal wetlands.
The distinctive curved bill of the wood ibis helps it catch fish and amphibians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large, long-legged, long-necked wading bird family
  • Synonym: ibis - wading bird with long, down-curved bill

Etymology

From 'wood' (forest) + 'ibis' (from Latin 'ibis', a kind of wading bird).
