interstitial fluid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈstɪʃəl ˈfluɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪʃəl ˈfluːɪd / (BrE)
the fluid found between the cells of the body, providing much of the liquid environment outside cells
fluid found between body cells
medical

Examples

The accumulation of interstitial fluid can lead to swelling in the affected tissues.
Oxygen and nutrients travel from the blood through the interstitial fluid to reach the cells.
Doctors measured the concentration of glucose in the patient's interstitial fluid to assess her condition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: extracellular fluid - body fluid located outside the cells, including interstitial fluid and plasma

Etymology

From 'interstitial', from Latin *interstitium* (a space between), and 'fluid'.
