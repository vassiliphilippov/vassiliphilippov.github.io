statutory offense

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuˌtɔri əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈstatjʊtəri əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A crime defined and prohibited by a statute, rather than by common law.
crime defined by statute
legal

Examples

The new law created several statutory offenses related to cybercrime.
Tax evasion is considered a statutory offense under federal law.
Unlike theft, which is based in common law, this is a statutory offense.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: common-law offense - a crime recognized by common law rather than statute
