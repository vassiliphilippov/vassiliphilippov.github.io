real food

1. NOUN

/ riːəl fuːd / (AmE), / rɪəl fuːd / (BrE)
Food that is natural, nutritious, and wholesome, as opposed to highly processed products.
nutritious, wholesome food

Examples

Many nutritionists recommend eating real food instead of relying on meal replacements.
After traveling abroad, he craved some real food from home.
It can be hard to find real food at most fast-food restaurants.

Synset Examples

They served us McShit instead of real food.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: junk food - food that is high in calories but low in nutritional value
  • Synonym: whole food - food that is unprocessed and unrefined
