uranyl radical

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈreɪnɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / jʊˈreɪnɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a bivalent uranium-oxygen ion (UO2) that forms salts with acids
bivalent uranium-oxygen ion (UO2)
medical

Examples

The uranyl radical plays a significant role in the chemistry of nuclear fuel processing.
Salts containing the uranyl radical are typically bright yellow in color.
The toxicity of the uranyl radical is of concern in environmental chemistry research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uranyl - the divalent uranium cation UO2^2+

Etymology

From 'uranium' + '-yl' denoting a radical; originally from New Latin uranium.
