rose mallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz ˌmæl.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌmæl.əʊ / (BrE)
A tall, showy shrub of the eastern United States, especially in salt marshes, with large rose-colored flowers; also known as swamp rose-mallow (Hibiscus moscheutos).
showy marsh shrub with large pink flowers

Examples

The rose mallow brightened the wetland with its large pink blossoms in midsummer.
She planted a rose mallow by the pond to attract butterflies and bees.
Strolling through the marsh, he admired the striking flowers of the rose mallow.

Etymology

From 'rose' (the flower) + 'mallow' (from Old English 'mealwe', Latin 'malva').

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz ˌmæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌmæləʊ / (BrE)
a plant with showy white, pink, or purple flowers in terminal racemes; commonly known as the hollyhock in English cottage gardens
type of flowering garden plant

Examples

The rose mallow brightens the garden with its large, colorful blooms each summer.
She planted rose mallow to add a touch of cottage charm to her backyard.
Botanists admire the rose mallow for its resilience and striking flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollyhock - a tall plant with large showy flowers, often grown in gardens
