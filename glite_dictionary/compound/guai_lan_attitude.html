guai lan attitude

1. NOUN

/ gwaɪ læn ˈætɪˌtud / (AmE), / gwaɪ lan ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a deliberately difficult, confrontational, or uncooperative manner, especially one intended to provoke others
deliberately difficult or confrontational manner

Examples

His guai lan attitude made teamwork on the project almost impossible.
The teacher warned him to drop his guai lan attitude if he wanted to stay in the class.
People often mistake her confidence for a guai lan attitude, but she's just straightforward.

Synset Examples

Her guai lan attitude annoyed everyone in the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confrontational - ready to argue or challenge others; aggressive
  • Synonym: uncooperative - not willing to help or work with others

Etymology

From Singlish, where 'guai lan' is borrowed from Hokkien (a Chinese dialect), meaning troublesome, cocky, or defiant.
