Philippine Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɪlɪˌpin ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˌfɪlɪˌpiːn ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a large archipelago in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, consisting of over 7,000 islands, and forming the sovereign nation of the Philippines
archipelago in the southwestern Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Philippine Islands are known for their rich biodiversity and stunning beaches.
During World War II, the Philippine Islands played a crucial strategic role in the Pacific theater.
She spent her vacation exploring several of the Philippine Islands with her family.

Etymology

From Spanish *Las Islas Filipinas*, named in honor of King Philip II of Spain.
