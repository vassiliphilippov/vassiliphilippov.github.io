Tungusic language

1. NOUN

/ tʌnˈɡuːsɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʊŋˈɡuːsɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of Altaic languages spoken primarily in Mongolia and neighboring regions of Northeast Asia
language family of Northeast Asia

Examples

The Tungusic language group includes languages spoken by the Evenki and Manchu peoples.
Some researchers debate whether Korean is related to the Tungusic language family.
Documentation of Tungusic languages is crucial for preserving endangered indigenous cultures.

Etymology

From Russian 'tungus', referring to the Evenks, an indigenous people of Siberia, plus '-ic'.
