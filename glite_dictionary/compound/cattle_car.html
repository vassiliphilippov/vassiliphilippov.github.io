cattle car

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætl kɑr / (AmE), / ˈkætl kɑː / (BrE)
a rail freight car designed for transporting cattle or other livestock
rail car for transporting cattle

Examples

The farm loaded its cows into the cattle car for the journey to the market.
During the 19th century, cattle cars were a common sight on American railroads.
The old cattle car now sits abandoned on a siding, a reminder of the region’s ranching history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock car - a railroad car for transporting livestock
