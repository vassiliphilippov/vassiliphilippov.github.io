anabolic steroid

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈbɑlɪk ˈstɛrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌanəˈbɒlɪk ˈstɛrɔɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic steroid hormone that promotes muscle and bone growth, often used medically with a prescription, and sometimes misused by athletes to enhance performance
synthetic steroid hormone for muscle/bone growth

Examples

The athlete was banned for using anabolic steroids to enhance his muscle mass.
Doctors sometimes prescribe anabolic steroids to patients with muscle-wasting diseases.
Due to the risks involved, anabolic steroid use is strictly regulated in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steroid - a type of organic compound with a specific molecular structure

Etymology

From 'anabolic' (relating to muscle-building processes) and 'steroid' (from Greek 'stereos', solid, and '-oid', form).
