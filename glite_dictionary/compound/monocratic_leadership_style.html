monocratic leadership style

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈkrætɪk ˈlidərˌʃɪp staɪl / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkrætɪk ˈliːdəʃɪp staɪl / (BrE)
A system of leadership in which power and decision-making are concentrated in a single leader, with little to no distribution of authority.
leadership with centralized, single-person control

Examples

The company's monocratic leadership style discouraged team members from voicing their opinions.
Many critics believe that a monocratic leadership style can lead to inefficient organization in the long term.
Shifting away from a monocratic leadership style helped foster more collaboration among departments.

Synset Examples

The monocratic leadership style stifled creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocratic - relating to a ruler who has absolute power
  • Antonym: democratic leadership style - a leadership style where decision-making is shared among group members

Etymology

From Greek monos (alone) + kratos (power), forming 'monocratic'; combined with 'leadership style'.
