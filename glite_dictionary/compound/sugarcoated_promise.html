sugarcoated promise

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚˌkoʊtɪd ˈprɑmɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡəkəʊtɪd ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
a promise presented in an attractive or optimistic way, but which may be misleading or unlikely to be fulfilled
misleadingly optimistic promise

Examples

Voters are tired of politicians making sugarcoated promises they cannot keep.
The company lured investors with sugarcoated promises that turned out to be false.
She recognized his apology as just another sugarcoated promise with no real change behind it.

Synset Examples

They offered sugarcoated promises but delivered little.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty promise - a promise with no intention of fulfillment

Etymology

From 'sugarcoat' (to make something appear more pleasant or acceptable) + 'promise'.
