2-methylpropenoic acid

1. NOUN

/ tuː ˌmɛθəlproʊˈpiːnoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / tuː ˌmɛθəlprəʊˈpiːnəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an unsaturated organic acid (C4H6O2) used in the manufacture of resins and plastics
unsaturated organic acid for making plastics

Examples

2-methylpropenoic acid is a key ingredient in the production of acrylic resins.
To achieve the desired polymer properties, 2-methylpropenoic acid is often copolymerized with other monomers.
Researchers studied the behavior of 2-methylpropenoic acid under various reaction conditions.

Etymology

From numerical chemical nomenclature: '2-' indicating position of methyl group; 'methyl' + 'propenoic acid' (from propane + -enoic for double bond).
