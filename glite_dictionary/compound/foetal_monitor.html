foetal monitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːt̬l ˈmɑːnɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈfiːtl ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
An electronic device used to monitor a fetus's heartbeat and the mother's uterine contractions during childbirth.
device for monitoring fetal heartbeat and contractions
medical british

Examples

The nurse checked the foetal monitor to ensure the baby's heart rate was stable.
Continuous data from the foetal monitor can help detect early signs of distress.
The hospital equipped each delivery room with a state-of-the-art foetal monitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetal monitor - electronic device for monitoring fetal heartbeat and contractions (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'foetus' (offspring, bringing forth).
