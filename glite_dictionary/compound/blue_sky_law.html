blue sky law

1. NOUN

/ bluː skaɪ lɔ / (AmE), / bluː skaɪ lɔː / (BrE)
A state law in the United States regulating the sale of securities to protect the public from fraud.
state law regulating securities sales
legal american

Examples

The company was fined for violating a blue sky law that protects investors from misleading claims.
Each state has its own blue sky law to prevent fraudulent securities sales.
Compliance with every applicable blue sky law is crucial before issuing new stocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: securities law - Law regulating financial securities and their trade

Etymology

Originates from the expression 'as worthless as a patch of blue sky,' referring to fraudulent investment schemes.
