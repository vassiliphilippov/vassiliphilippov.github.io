arcuate artery of the kidney

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkjuˌeɪt ˈɑrtəri əv ðə ˈkɪdni / (AmE), / ˈɑːkjuˌeɪt ˈɑːtəri əv ðə ˈkɪdni / (BrE)
one of the curved arteries located at the border between the cortex and medulla of the kidney, supplying blood to the kidney tissues
curved artery in the kidney
medical

Examples

The arcuate artery of the kidney runs along the border between the cortex and the medulla.
Damage to the arcuate artery of the kidney can affect blood flow within the organ.
Each arcuate artery of the kidney gives rise to several interlobular arteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Latin arcuatus 'curved' and Greek arteria 'artery'.
