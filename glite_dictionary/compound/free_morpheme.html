free morpheme

1. NOUN

/ fri ˈmɔrˌfim / (AmE), / friː ˈmɔːfiːm / (BrE)
a morpheme that can stand alone as a word without needing to be attached to another morpheme
morpheme that can stand alone

Examples

The word 'book' is a free morpheme because it can stand alone as a word.
In English, most simple nouns and verbs are free morphemes.
A free morpheme does not need to be attached to other morphemes to convey meaning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bound morpheme - a morpheme that cannot stand alone and must be attached to another morpheme

Etymology

From 'free' (able to act without constraint) + 'morpheme', which comes from French 'morphème', from Greek 'morphē' (form).
