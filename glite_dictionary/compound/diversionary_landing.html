diversionary landing

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvɜrʒəˌnɛri ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɜːʃənəri ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
a military operation in which amphibious forces land at a location intended to deceive or distract the enemy from the main attack
amphibious military feint or deception

Examples

The navy executed a diversionary landing to draw enemy troops away from the primary invasion site.
As part of the operation, a small force conducted a diversionary landing on the southern coast.
The general approved a diversionary landing to confuse the defenders before the main assault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feint - a deceptive or pretended blow or attack
