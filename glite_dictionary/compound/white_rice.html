white rice

1. NOUN

/ waɪt raɪs / (AmE), / waɪt raɪs / (BrE)
rice from which the husk, bran, and germ have been removed, leaving a polished white grain.
polished rice without husk and bran

Examples

Many people prefer white rice because of its soft texture and mild flavor.
The nutritionist suggested replacing white rice with brown rice for a healthier diet.
After cooking, white rice becomes fluffy and absorbs sauces easily.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brown rice - rice with only the husk removed, retaining bran and germ
