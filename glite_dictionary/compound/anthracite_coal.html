anthracite coal

1. NOUN

/ ˈænθrəˌsaɪt koʊl / (AmE), / ˈanθrəˌsaɪt kəʊl / (BrE)
a hard, shiny coal that burns slowly and produces a high level of heat with little smoke
hard, high-grade coal

Examples

The power plant was designed to use anthracite coal as its primary fuel source.
Because of its high carbon content, anthracite coal burns with very little smoke.
Miners extracted large quantities of anthracite coal from the region in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bituminous coal - a softer type of coal with lower carbon content
  • Degree Lower: lignite - a soft, brownish coal with the lowest carbon content

Etymology

From Greek 'anthrax' (coal).
