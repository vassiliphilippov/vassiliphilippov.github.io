red-backed sandpiper

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˈsændˌpaɪpər / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈbækt ˈsanˌpʌɪpə / (BrE)
A small sandpiper (Calidris alpina) that breeds in northern or Arctic regions and migrates to southern United States or Mediterranean areas in winter.
species of migratory sandpiper

Examples

We spotted a red-backed sandpiper feeding along the muddy shoreline during our birdwatching trip.
Every winter, the red-backed sandpiper migrates thousands of miles to warmer southern regions.
The distinct plumage helps identify the red-backed sandpiper among other wading birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunlin - another common name for Calidris alpina, a species of small, migratory wading bird
