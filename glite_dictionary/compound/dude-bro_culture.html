dude-bro culture

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːd broʊ ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈdjuːd brəʊ ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
An informal culture, especially among young men, marked by hyper-masculinity, casual attitudes, and informal speech or behavior.
hyper-masculine, informal youth culture
informal american

Examples

Many college fraternities have a reputation for promoting dude-bro culture.
She found the dude-bro culture at the tech conference off-putting.
Critics argue that dude-bro culture discourages emotional openness among young men.

Synset Examples

The dude-bro culture is prevalent at the gym.
I can't stand the dude-bro talk at the party.

Etymology

Blend of 'dude', 'bro' (short for brother), and 'culture'; terms popularized in American youth slang from the late 20th century.
