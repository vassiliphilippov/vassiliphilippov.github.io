Adams-Stokes syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈædəmz stoʊks ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈædəmz stəʊks ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by sudden, recurrent attacks of unconsciousness caused by a temporary interruption in the heart's normal rhythm due to impaired impulse conduction.
sudden fainting due to heart conduction problem
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Adams-Stokes syndrome after several unexplained fainting spells.
Sudden loss of consciousness during exertion may indicate Adams-Stokes syndrome.
Doctors monitored his heartbeat closely to detect episodes of Adams-Stokes syndrome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syncope - temporary loss of consciousness due to inadequate blood flow to the brain

Etymology

Named after Irish physicians Robert Adams and William Stokes, who described the condition in the 19th century.
