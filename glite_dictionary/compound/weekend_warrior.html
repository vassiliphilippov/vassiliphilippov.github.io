weekend warrior

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkˌɛnd ˈwɔriər / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənd ˈwɒriə / (BrE)
a member of a military reserve force who fulfills their service obligations primarily on weekends
reservist serving on weekends

Examples

As a weekend warrior, she reports to the base every Saturday for training.
Many weekend warriors balance civilian jobs with their military commitments.
The Army relies on weekend warriors to maintain a strong reservist presence.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkˌɛnd ˈwɔriɚ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkˌɛnd ˈwɔːriə / (BrE)
a person who engages in difficult or ambitious activities, especially home improvement or exercise, primarily on weekends
person active in projects or activities on weekends
informal

Examples

Every Saturday, the weekend warrior tackled a new home renovation project, much to the frustration of his neighbors.
As a self-proclaimed weekend warrior, she spends her days off painting, repairing, and gardening around the house.
Many weekend warriors underestimate the time required for major remodeling jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DIYer - a person who does home improvement tasks themselves

Etymology

From 'weekend' + 'warrior', originally used for military reservists active on weekends, now applied more broadly.

3. NOUN

/ ˌwiːkˌɛnd ˈwɔriɚ / (AmE), / ˌwiːkˌend ˈwɔːriə / (BrE)
a person who participates in physical or recreational activities mainly on weekends, often as a hobby rather than professionally
someone active mainly on weekends

Examples

Many weekend warriors hit the gym after a week of office work.
She considers herself a weekend warrior, tackling home improvement projects every Saturday.
The term weekend warrior often describes people who play sports just for fun.
