choroid vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒrɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins that drain blood from the choroid coat of the eye
vein draining blood from the eye's choroid
medical

Examples

The choroid vein plays an essential role in draining deoxygenated blood from the eye.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the choroid vein.
Blockage in a choroid vein can lead to increased pressure within the eye.

Etymology

From 'choroid', from Greek 'khoroeidēs' (resembling a membrane), and 'vein', from Latin 'vena'.
