criminal law

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəl lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəl lɔː / (BrE)
The body of law that relates to crime and the punishment of those who commit criminal offenses.
law regarding crime and punishment
legal

Examples

He decided to specialize in criminal law after graduating from law school.
Criminal law distinguishes between misdemeanors and felonies in many countries.
Understanding criminal law is essential for anyone pursuing a career as a prosecutor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: civil law - the body of laws governing disputes between individuals or organizations
  • Synonym: penal code - a set of laws relating to crimes and offenses and their punishment
