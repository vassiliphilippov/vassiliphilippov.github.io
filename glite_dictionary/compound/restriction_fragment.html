restriction fragment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪkʃən ˈfræɡmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃən ˈfræɡmənt / (BrE)
a fragment of DNA produced by cutting a DNA molecule with a restriction enzyme
fragment of DNA cut by restriction enzyme

Examples

The scientist analyzed the restriction fragment pattern to identify genetic differences.
Each restriction fragment is separated by size using gel electrophoresis.
A specific restriction fragment can indicate the presence of a genetic mutation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restriction enzyme - an enzyme that cuts DNA at specific sequences

Etymology

From 'restriction enzyme' and 'fragment' in molecular biology, first used in the 1970s.
