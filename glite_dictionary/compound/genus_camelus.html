genus Camelus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈmiː.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkam.ɛ.ləs / (BrE)
The genus of mammals that comprises the camels, belonging to the family Camelidae.
the genus of camels

Examples

The genus Camelus includes both the dromedary and the Bactrian camel.
Scientists discovered new insights into the evolution of the genus Camelus.
Members of the genus Camelus are adapted to live in arid and semi-arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camel - a large, long-necked ungulate used as a beast of burden

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Camelus' (camel).
