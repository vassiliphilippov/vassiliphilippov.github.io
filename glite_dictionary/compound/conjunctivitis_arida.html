conjunctivitis arida

1. NOUN

/ kənˌdʒʌŋktɪˈvaɪtɪs əˈraɪdə / (AmE), / kənˌdʒʌŋktɪˈvaɪtɪs əˈrɪdə / (BrE)
A medical condition marked by abnormal dryness of the conjunctiva and cornea, often due to vitamin A deficiency.
abnormal dryness of the eye's conjunctiva and cornea
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist diagnosed him with conjunctivitis arida after noting severe dryness and irritation in both eyes.
Patients with conjunctivitis arida may experience discomfort and vision problems if not treated promptly.
Vitamin A supplements are often prescribed to help manage conjunctivitis arida.

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctiva' (joined together) and 'arida' (dry).
