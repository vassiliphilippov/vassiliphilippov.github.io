occult arts

1. NOUN

/ əˈkʌlt ɑrts / (AmE), / əˈkʌlt ɑːts / (BrE)
supernatural practices and techniques such as magic, alchemy, or divination, believed to operate beyond normal human understanding
supernatural practices and techniques

Examples

Many ancient civilizations practiced the occult arts in hopes of predicting the future.
She devoted herself to the study of occult arts such as alchemy and astrology.
The novel features a secret society deeply involved in the occult arts.

Synset Examples

he is a student of the occult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magic - the use of supernatural powers to influence events
  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magical skills, spells, and abilities
  • Antonym: science - systematic study of the natural world based on facts and experiments

Etymology

From Latin 'occultus' (hidden, secret) combined with 'arts' (skills or practices).
