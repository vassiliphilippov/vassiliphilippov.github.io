Master of Laws

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚ əv lɔz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə əv lɔːz / (BrE)
an advanced academic degree in law, typically taken after a first law degree, focusing on specialized areas of legal study
advanced academic law degree

Examples

After completing her Master of Laws, she specialized in international human rights.
Many lawyers pursue a Master of Laws to gain expertise in a particular area of law.
The university offers a flexible Master of Laws program for both domestic and international students.

Etymology

From Latin 'Magister Legum', following academic degree naming conventions.
