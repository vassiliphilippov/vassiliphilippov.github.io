emission spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪʃən ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪʃən ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the spectrum of electromagnetic radiation emitted by a self-luminous object or substance, typically showing characteristic lines or bands
electromagnetic spectrum emitted by an object

Examples

The bright lines in the emission spectrum of hydrogen helped scientists discover the atom's structure.
Astronomers analyze the emission spectrum of distant stars to learn about their chemical composition.
A neon sign produces its distinctive color because of its unique emission spectrum.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorption spectrum - spectrum showing dark lines or bands due to absorption of specific wavelengths

Etymology

From Latin 'emissio' (a sending forth) and New Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image).
