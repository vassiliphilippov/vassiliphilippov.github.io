Wilson's blackcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlsənz ˈblækˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈwɪlsənz ˈblækˌkap / (BrE)
a species of yellow wood warbler with a distinctive black crown, native to North America
a yellow wood warbler with a black crown

Examples

Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a Wilson's blackcap during their morning hike.
The Wilson's blackcap is easily identified by its bright yellow body and striking black crown.
Every spring, Wilson's blackcap migrates north to breed in forested areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wilson's warbler - a North American wood warbler closely related to Wilson's blackcap

Etymology

Named after Alexander Wilson, Scottish-American ornithologist.
