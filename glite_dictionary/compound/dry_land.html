dry land

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌlænd / (BrE)
the solid surface of the earth, as distinct from the sea or air
solid surface of the earth

Examples

After several days at sea, the sailors were relieved to see dry land on the horizon.
The children were excited to leave the boat and run on dry land again.
We finally reached dry land after our long kayaking trip.
