fishtail bit

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃˌteɪl bɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃteɪl bɪt / (BrE)
a type of drilling bit with cutting edges, usually hardened to resist wear, used for boring into materials such as rock or soil
drilling bit with hardened cutting edges

Examples

The fishtail bit is ideal for soft to medium formations when drilling for water wells.
Replace the worn fishtail bit before starting to bore the new hole.
Engineers selected a fishtail bit because of its ability to cut efficiently through clay and shale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill bit - a cutting tool used to create cylindrical holes

Etymology

Named for its distinctive shape, resembling a fish's tail.
