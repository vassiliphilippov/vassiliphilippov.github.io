repeating decimal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpitɪŋ ˈdɛsɪml̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːtɪŋ ˈdɛsɪməl / (BrE)
a decimal in which one or more digits repeat indefinitely after a certain point
decimal with endlessly repeating digits

Examples

The fraction 1/3 is equal to the repeating decimal 0.333....
Every rational number can be written as either a terminating or a repeating decimal.
She explained how 0.272727... is a repeating decimal because '27' repeats indefinitely.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terminating decimal - decimal that ends after a finite number of digits
  • Synonym: recurring decimal - another term for repeating decimal, used especially in British English
