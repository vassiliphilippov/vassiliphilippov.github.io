Apartheid South Africa

1. PROPN

/ əˈpɑrtˌaɪd ˌsaʊθ ˈæf.rɪ.kə / (AmE), / əˈpɑːt.haɪd ˌsaʊθ ˈæf.rɪ.kə / (BrE)
The period in South Africa's history (1948–early 1990s) marked by the policy and system of institutionalized racial segregation and discrimination against non-white citizens.
period of racial segregation in South Africa

Examples

Many countries imposed sanctions on Apartheid South Africa in protest of its racial policies.
Nelson Mandela became a global symbol for resistance against Apartheid South Africa.
The legacies of Apartheid South Africa are still felt in its society today.

Synset Examples

Many activists fought against Apartheid South Africa's segregation laws.
Historians study the effects of Apartheid South Africa on society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartheid - the system of institutionalized segregation and discrimination on grounds of race
  • Antonym: post-apartheid South Africa - South Africa after the end of apartheid

Etymology

"Apartheid" from Afrikaans, literally 'separateness'; "South Africa" from the country's name.
