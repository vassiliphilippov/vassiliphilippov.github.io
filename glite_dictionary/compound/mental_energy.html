mental energy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntəl ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈmɛntəl ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the capacity or force that enables mental activity, concentration, or effort
capacity to think or focus

Examples

After a long day at work, I often lack the mental energy to read a difficult book.
Solving complex problems can drain your mental energy quickly.
She conserved her mental energy by focusing only on essential tasks during exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willpower - control exerted to do something or restrain impulses
  • Synonym: motivation - the reason or reasons one has for acting or behaving
