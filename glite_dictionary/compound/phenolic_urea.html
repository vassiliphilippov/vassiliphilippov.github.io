phenolic urea

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɑlɪk juˈriə / (AmE), / fɪˈnɒlɪk jʊəˈriːə / (BrE)
a type of plastic made from chemically combined phenolic resins and urea, known for its strength and heat resistance
a plastic made from phenolic resins and urea
medical

Examples

The insulating properties of phenolic urea make it valuable for electrical components.
Manufacturers often choose phenolic urea when durability and heat resistance are required.
The laboratory tested samples of phenolic urea for their chemical stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenolic resin - synthetic resin obtained by the reaction of phenol with formaldehyde

Etymology

Phenolic from 'phenol' (from Greek 'phaino' meaning to show), and 'urea' (from New Latin, from Greek 'ouron' meaning urine).
