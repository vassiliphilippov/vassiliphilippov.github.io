philosophers' wool

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfərz wʊl / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfəz wʊl / (BrE)
A white powder consisting of zinc oxide, used as a pigment and in cosmetics, glass, inks, and ointments.
zinc oxide; white powder used in pigments and ointments

Examples

During the distillation process, a layer of philosophers' wool would collect inside the equipment.
Artists once used philosophers' wool to create vibrant white pigments for their paintings.
In the nineteenth century, philosophers' wool was often included in medicinal ointments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc oxide - a white compound used as a pigment and in ointments

Etymology

From the practice of early alchemists ('philosophers') who collected zinc oxide wool-like deposits during distillation.
