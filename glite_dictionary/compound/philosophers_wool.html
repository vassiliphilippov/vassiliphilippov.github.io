philosopher's wool

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfərz wʊl / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒsəfəz wʊl / (BrE)
zinc oxide, a white powder used as a pigment and in cosmetics, glass, inks, and ointments
zinc oxide (white powder)

Examples

In the nineteenth century, philosopher's wool was commonly used in medicinal ointments.
The chemist demonstrated the production of philosopher's wool through the oxidation of zinc.
Artists valued philosopher's wool for its brilliance as a white pigment in paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc oxide - a white powder used as a pigment and medicine

Etymology

So called from alchemical origins, where the substance was produced by vaporizing zinc; the term refers to its woolly, fluffy appearance.
