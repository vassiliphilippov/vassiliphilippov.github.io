family Cycadaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli saɪˌkædəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli saɪˌkædəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of ancient palmlike plants closely related to ferns, with fertilization by spermatozoids, known as cycads
family of ancient palmlike plants; cycads

Examples

The family Cycadaceae includes some of the world's most primitive seed plants.
Botanists often study the reproductive cycles within the family Cycadaceae.
Most species in the family Cycadaceae are found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - a tropical and subtropical plant of the family Cycadaceae with a stout trunk and large, tough leaves

Etymology

From New Latin Cycadaceae, based on Cycas (the type genus), from Greek kykas, related to the genus name.
