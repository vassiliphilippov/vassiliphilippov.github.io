genus Trichechus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˈkiːkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˈkiːkəs / (BrE)
the sole genus of the family Trichechidae, including the manatees, large aquatic herbivorous mammals of tropical rivers and coasts.
biological genus including manatees

Examples

The genus Trichechus encompasses all three extant species of manatees.
Scientists studying the evolution of aquatic mammals often reference the genus Trichechus.
The genus Trichechus is characterized by slow-moving, fully aquatic herbivores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - a large, plant-eating aquatic mammal found in warm coastal waters

Etymology

From Latin 'Trichechus', the systematic name for the manatee genus, itself from Greek 'trikhekos' meaning 'hairy lip'.
