common madia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmeɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmeɪdiə / (BrE)
A California annual plant with yellow flower rays marked by red-brown spots near the base.
California annual plant with spotted yellow flowers

Examples

The hills of California are dotted with blooming common madia in springtime.
Botanists easily recognize common madia by the distinctive spots at the base of its yellow petals.
Gardeners often cultivate common madia for its hardy nature and attractive flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Madia, from Chilean Spanish 'madia', ultimately from Mapuche.
