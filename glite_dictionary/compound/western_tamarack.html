western tamarack

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn təˈmæræk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən təˈmæræk / (BrE)
a tall larch tree of western North America with pale green, sharply pointed leaves and oblong cones; valued for its timber
larch tree of western North America

Examples

The western tamarack is often found in moist mountain valleys throughout the Pacific Northwest.
Lumber from the western tamarack is prized for its strength and durability.
In spring, the western tamarack puts out bright green needles that turn golden in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - any of several deciduous coniferous trees of the genus Larix
