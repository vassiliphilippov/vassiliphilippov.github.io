absolute frequency

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.səˌluːt ˈfriː.kwən.si / (AmE), / ˌæb.səˌluːt ˈfriː.kwən.si / (BrE)
the number of times an observation occurs in a specific category of a dataset
number of occurrences in a category

Examples

The absolute frequency of students who passed the exam was 35.
In the bar chart, the absolute frequency for each age group is shown.
When analyzing data, it's useful to compare absolute frequency with relative frequency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relative frequency - proportion or percentage of times an event occurs relative to the total

Etymology

From Latin 'absolutus' (complete, total) and Latin 'frequentia' (a crowd, multitude).
