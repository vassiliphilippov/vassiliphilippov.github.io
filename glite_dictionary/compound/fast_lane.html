fast lane

1. NOUN

/ fæst leɪn / (AmE), / fɑːst leɪn / (BrE)
A hectic and pressured way of living, often involving excitement, risk, and extravagance.
hectic, high-pressure lifestyle

Examples

After her promotion, she found herself living in the fast lane and rarely had time to relax.
Many celebrities are drawn to the fast lane, but not everyone can handle the pace.
Tired of the fast lane, he decided to move to the countryside for a quieter life.

Synset Examples

life in fashion's fast lane has taught her a lot

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow lane - a more relaxed or conventional way of living
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04938027-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ fɑst leɪn / (AmE), / fɑːst leɪn / (BrE)
the part of a road or highway designated for vehicles that move faster than others, usually the lane furthest from the shoulder or curb
lane for faster-moving vehicles

Examples

He always drives in the fast lane to avoid traffic jams during rush hour.
Changing from the fast lane to the exit ramp requires careful timing.
The police stopped a car for speeding in the fast lane of the motorway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow lane - the part of a road or highway designated for slower-moving vehicles
