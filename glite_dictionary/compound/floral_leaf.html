floral leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔrəl liːf / (AmE), / ˈflɔːrəl liːf / (BrE)
a modified leaf that forms part of a flower, such as a petal or sepal
modified leaf in a flower

Examples

The brightly colored floral leaves attracted pollinators to the garden.
In some plants, what seem to be petals are actually modified floral leaves.
Botanists study the different shapes of floral leaves to classify flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bract - a specialized leaf often associated with a reproductive structure in plants
  • Degree Lower: petal - a segment of the corolla of a flower, often colored
  • Degree Lower: sepal - a segment of the calyx of a flower, typically green and leaf-like
