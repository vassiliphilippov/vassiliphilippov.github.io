sweet hereafter

1. NOUN

/ swit hɪrˈæftɚ / (AmE), / swiːt hɪərˈɑːftə / (BrE)
a comforting idea of a peaceful and pleasant afterlife or future state, often used to offer solace about what happens after death
peaceful afterlife; future consolation

Examples

The poem describes the sweet hereafter as a place where everyone is free from pain.
Many find comfort in believing that loved ones wait for them in the sweet hereafter.
The story ends with the hero finally joining his family in the sweet hereafter.

Synset Examples

The poem spoke of a sweet hereafter beyond this life.
She often dreams of a sweet hereafter filled with peace.
They found solace in thoughts of a sweet hereafter.
