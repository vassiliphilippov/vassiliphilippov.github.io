order Plecoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər plɛˈkɑptərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌplikɒpˈtərə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of insects comprising the stoneflies, characterized by two pairs of membranous wings and aquatic nymphs.
order of insects: stoneflies

Examples

Freshwater streams often support larvae from the order Plecoptera, which are sensitive to pollution.
Entomologists collected several specimens belonging to the order Plecoptera during their field study.
The distinguishing feature of the order Plecoptera is their delicate, netlike wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonefly - common name for insects in the order Plecoptera

Etymology

From Greek 'plekein' (to braid) + 'pteron' (wing), describing their intricately veined wings.
