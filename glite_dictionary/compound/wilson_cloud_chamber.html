Wilson cloud chamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlsən klaʊd ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪlsən klaʊd ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a scientific device that makes the paths of charged high-energy particles visible by allowing vapor to condense on ions formed along their passage
particle detection chamber in physics

Examples

The physicists demonstrated the Wilson cloud chamber to show how subatomic particles leave visible trails.
A Wilson cloud chamber allowed scientists to observe the paths of alpha and beta particles directly.
Photographs taken with a Wilson cloud chamber often reveal intricate patterns of cosmic ray interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud chamber - a particle detector using supersaturated vapor to reveal particle tracks

Etymology

Named after Charles T. R. Wilson, the Scottish physicist who invented it.
