ecclesiastical province

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl ˈprɑːvɪns / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl ˈprɒvɪns / (BrE)
an administrative district within the jurisdiction of an archbishop or metropolitan in certain Christian churches, or a territorial division of a religious order
administrative district in church hierarchy

Examples

The church reorganized its ecclesiastical province to better serve its growing population.
The bishop's appointment affected the entire ecclesiastical province under his authority.
In historical documents, you can find references to the medieval ecclesiastical province of Canterbury.

Synset Examples

The US is divided into 33 ecclesiastical provinces

Etymology

From Late Latin ecclesiasticus (of the church) and Latin provincia (province, administrative area).
