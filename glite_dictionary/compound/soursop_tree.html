soursop tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊrˌsɑp tri / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəˌsɒp triː / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree (Annona muricata) that bears a large, succulent fruit with slightly acid flavor.
tropical tree with acidic fruit

Examples

The soursop tree thrives in warm, tropical climates and produces a unique fruit that is popular in many countries.
Farmers planted several soursop trees to harvest the fruit and sell it at the local market.
A mature soursop tree can reach up to eight meters in height and bear fruit twice a year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soursop - the large, slightly acidic fruit borne by the soursop tree
