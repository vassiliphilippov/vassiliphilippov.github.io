round-tailed muskrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd teɪld ˈmʌsˌkræt / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd teɪld ˈmʌs.kræt / (BrE)
a semi-aquatic rodent native to the wetlands of Florida, known for its round tail and dense fur
rodent species of Florida wetlands

Examples

The round-tailed muskrat builds elaborate lodges out of aquatic plants in Florida marshes.
Conservation efforts are aimed at protecting the wetland habitat of the round-tailed muskrat.
Unlike the common muskrat, the round-tailed muskrat is found only in certain parts of Florida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskrat - a large, aquatic rodent native to North America
