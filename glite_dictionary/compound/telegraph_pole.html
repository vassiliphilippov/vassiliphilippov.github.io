telegraph pole

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌɡræf poʊl / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf pəʊl / (BrE)
a tall wooden or metal pole that supports telephone or telegraph wires above the ground
pole supporting telegraph or phone wires

Examples

The crew repaired the damaged telegraph pole after the storm knocked it down.
Crows were perched along the top of the telegraph pole outside the window.
The old telegraph pole was replaced with a modern steel utility pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utility pole - pole supporting overhead power or communication lines

Etymology

From 'telegraph' + 'pole'; 'telegraph' from Greek 'tele-' (far) + 'graphein' (to write).
