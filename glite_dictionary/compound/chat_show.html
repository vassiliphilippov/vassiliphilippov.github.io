chat show

1. NOUN

/ tʃæt ʃoʊ / (AmE), / tʃæt ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a television or radio programme in which well-known people discuss topics or answer questions from the audience
TV or radio programme with guest interviews
british

Examples

She was invited to appear on a popular chat show to talk about her new book.
The comedian hosted a late-night chat show for several years.
In the UK, many celebrities gain fame after appearing on a chat show.

Synset Examples

in England they call a talk show a ‘chat show’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talk show - a programme where guests are interviewed or discuss topics
