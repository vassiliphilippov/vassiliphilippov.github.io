moxa roll

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑksə roʊl / (AmE), / ˈmɒksə rəʊl / (BrE)
A stick made from dried leaves of the mugwort plant, used in traditional Chinese medicine for moxibustion therapy.
stick of mugwort for moxibustion
medical

Examples

The practitioner lit the moxa roll and held it close to the patient’s back to warm specific points.
Many acupuncturists use a moxa roll to complement needle treatments.
Store the moxa roll in a dry place to preserve its effectiveness for future therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moxibustion - Traditional therapy using burning mugwort near the skin

Etymology

From Japanese 'moxa', from Chinese 'mógū' (mugwort) and English 'roll'.
