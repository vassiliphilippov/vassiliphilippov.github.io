pivot joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪvət dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɪvət dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a freely movable joint in which movement is limited to rotation around a single axis
joint allowing rotational movement
medical

Examples

The atlantoaxial joint in the neck is a classic example of a pivot joint.
A pivot joint allows your forearm to turn palm up or palm down.
Rotational movement in the arm is made possible by the pivot joint between the radius and ulna.

Synset Examples

the articulation of the radius and ulna in the arm is a pivot joint

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hinge joint - a joint that allows movement in one plane, like a door hinge
  • Antonym: ball-and-socket joint - a joint allowing movement in many directions

Etymology

From 'pivot' (French: pivot, meaning 'hinge' or 'pin') + 'joint'.
