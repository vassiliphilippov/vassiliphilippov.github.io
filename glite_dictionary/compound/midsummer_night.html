Midsummer Night

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪdˌsʌmɚ ˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪdˌsʌm.ə ˈnaɪt / (BrE)
the night before Midsummer Day, traditionally celebrated around the summer solstice in various European cultures
night before Midsummer Day

Examples

We stayed up late to celebrate Midsummer Night with a bonfire in the garden.
On Midsummer Night, festivities often include singing and dancing until dawn.
The tradition of lighting fires on Midsummer Night dates back hundreds of years.
