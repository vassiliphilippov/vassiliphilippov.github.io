Rio Nunez coffee

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.oʊ ˈnuː.nɛz ˈkɑː.fi / (AmE), / ˌriː.əʊ ˈnjuː.nɛz ˈkɒf.i / (BrE)
a type of coffee plant native to West Africa, known for its resistance to coffee rust and grown in various regions including Java
type of rust-resistant coffee plant

Examples

The plantation introduced Rio Nunez coffee to improve their crop's resistance to disease.
Researchers are studying Rio Nunez coffee for its unique genetic traits.
Many growers in Java prefer Rio Nunez coffee due to its hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffee - an evergreen shrub or small tree that produces coffee beans

Etymology

Named after the Rio Nunez river region in West Africa.
