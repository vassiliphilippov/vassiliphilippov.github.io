drip loop

1. NOUN

/ drɪp lup / (AmE), / drɪp luːp / (BrE)
a downward curve or loop formed in a cable or wire where it enters a building, designed to prevent water from running along the wire into the building
downward loop in a cable to prevent water entry

Examples

Make sure to install a drip loop where the cable enters the service box.
The electrician pointed out that the absence of a drip loop could cause water damage inside the house.
Properly forming a drip loop prevents rainwater from running down the wire and into the building.

Synset Examples

when it rained water would fall from the drip loop before it reached the building
