merchant marine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənt məˈrin / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃənt məˈriːn / (BrE)
the crew that operates a country's commercial ships, distinct from the navy
civilian crew of commercial ships

Examples

After years at sea, he became captain in the merchant marine.
The merchant marine played a crucial role during wartime by transporting vital supplies.
Many young people joined the merchant marine to see the world and gain seafaring experience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: navy - a country's military sea force

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənt məˈrin / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃənt məˈriːn / (BrE)
The commercial ships of a country that are used for transporting goods and passengers, rather than for military purposes.
commercial shipping fleet of a country

Examples

The country's merchant marine plays a vital role in international trade.
During wartime, the government called upon the merchant marine to transport supplies.
Many people worked in the merchant marine before joining the navy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merchant navy - the commercial shipping fleet of a country, British term
