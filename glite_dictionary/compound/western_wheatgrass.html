western wheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn ˈwiːtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈwiːtˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a perennial grass native to western North America, valued as forage for livestock
a perennial forage grass of western North America

Examples

Ranchers often plant western wheatgrass to restore overgrazed prairies.
During the drought, western wheatgrass proved more resilient than other grasses.
The field was covered in tall, green western wheatgrass swaying in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheatgrass - a general term for grasses resembling wheat, often used as forage or for health drinks
