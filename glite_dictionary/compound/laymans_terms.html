layman's terms

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪmənz tɝːmz / (AmE), / ˈleɪmənz tɜːmz / (BrE)
words or phrasing that are simple and easy to understand for someone without special knowledge of the subject
simple language for non-experts

Examples

Can you explain the new software update in layman's terms?
The scientist broke down the complex process into layman's terms for the audience.
She prefers to communicate medical information in layman's terms so her patients can understand.

Synset Examples

The doctor explained the condition in layman's terms.
Can you describe the project in layman's terms?
He summarized the theory in layman's terms for us.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jargon - specialized terminology used by a particular group
  • Synonym: plain English - clear and straightforward language

Etymology

From 'layman', meaning a non-specialist, and 'terms', meaning words or expressions.
