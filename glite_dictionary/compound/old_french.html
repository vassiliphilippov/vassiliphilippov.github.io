Old French

1. NOUN

/ oʊld frɛntʃ / (AmE), / əʊld frɛntʃ / (BrE)
the earliest form of the French language, spoken and written from the 9th to the 15th century
earliest form of French (9th–15th century)

Examples

Medieval literature was often written in Old French rather than modern dialects.
Scholars study Old French to trace the evolution of the language.
Several legal documents from the 12th century are preserved in Old French.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Middle French - the form of the French language used from the 14th to the 17th century
