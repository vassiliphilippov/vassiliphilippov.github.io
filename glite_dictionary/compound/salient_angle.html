salient angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪliənt ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪliənt ˈaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
an angle that projects outward, specifically an interior angle of a polygon that is less than 180 degrees
an outward-pointing angle in geometry

Examples

The fortress had a salient angle facing the approaching army, making defense easier.
Each salient angle of the polygon measured 120 degrees, contributing to its regular shape.
Architects must consider every salient angle when designing complex building facades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: re-entrant angle - an interior angle of a polygon greater than 180 degrees

Etymology

From Latin 'salire' meaning 'to leap,' via 'salient' (projecting outwards) and 'angle.'
