Siberian millet

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɪriən ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈbɪəriən ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a type of millet with orange to reddish grains arranged in long, bristly spikes, cultivated primarily as a fodder crop
a millet with reddish grains and bristly spikes

Examples

Farmers often plant Siberian millet as a forage crop for livestock during dry seasons.
The long bristly spikes of Siberian millet are easily recognized in the fields.
Compared to other millets, Siberian millet thrives better in cooler climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millet - a general term for small-seeded grasses cultivated as cereal crops
