hog cholera

1. NOUN

/ hɔg ˈkɑlɚə / (AmE), / hɒg ˈkɒlərə / (BrE)
a highly contagious viral disease affecting pigs, characterized by fever, skin lesions, and often fatal outcomes
contagious viral disease of pigs
medical

Examples

The outbreak of hog cholera devastated local pig farms, causing significant economic losses.
Veterinarians worked tirelessly to contain the spread of hog cholera among the livestock.
Effective vaccines have been developed to protect pigs from hog cholera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical swine fever - another term for hog cholera, referring to the same disease
