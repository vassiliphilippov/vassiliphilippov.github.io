lead tetraethyl

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˌtɛtrəˈiːθɪl / (AmE), / lɛd ˌtɛtrəˈiːθɪl / (BrE)
a clear, oily, poisonous liquid, formerly added to gasoline to prevent engine knocking
poisonous liquid once added to gasoline

Examples

The use of lead tetraethyl in fuels has been banned in many countries due to its toxicity.
Engineers once added lead tetraethyl to gasoline to reduce engine knocking.
Exposure to lead tetraethyl can have serious health effects, including neurological damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetraethyl lead - another name for lead tetraethyl, same compound

Etymology

From 'lead' (the element) and 'tetraethyl' (meaning 'four ethyl groups'); refers to its chemical structure (Pb(C₂H₅)₄).
