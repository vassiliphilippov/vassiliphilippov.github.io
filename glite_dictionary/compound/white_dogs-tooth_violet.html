white dog's-tooth violet

1. NOUN

/ waɪt dɔgz tuːθ ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / waɪt dɒɡz tuːθ ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
A North American plant of the genus Erythronium, having solitary white flowers with yellow centers and blue or pink exteriors.
North American plant with white-violet flowers

Examples

While hiking in the woods, we spotted a rare white dog's-tooth violet blooming beside the trail.
The white dog's-tooth violet is admired for its delicate, colorful petals in spring.
Gardeners often seek the white dog's-tooth violet for shady borders due to its striking flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog's-tooth violet - A Eurasian or North American plant of the genus Erythronium with lily-like flowers
