sense of wormwood

1. NOUN

/ sɛns əv ˈwɜrmˌwʊd / (AmE), / sɛns əv ˈwɜːmˌwʊd / (BrE)
a feeling of bitterness or regret, especially arising from disappointment or sorrow
feeling of bitterness or regret

Examples

After the failed negotiations, the diplomat was left with a sense of wormwood.
She spoke of her past with a sense of wormwood that was clear in her voice.
He tried to hide the sense of wormwood that lingered after the loss.

Synset Examples

He felt a sense of wormwood when he faced his mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterness - sharpness of feeling, angry or resentful emotion
  • Synonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment over something done or not done

Etymology

Refers to the bitter taste of wormwood, used metaphorically for emotional bitterness.
