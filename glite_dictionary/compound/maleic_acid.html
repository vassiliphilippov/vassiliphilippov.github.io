maleic acid

1. NOUN

/ məˈliːɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / məˈliːɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline organic acid found in some unripe fruits and used chiefly in the manufacture of polyester resins and other chemicals
a colorless crystalline organic acid

Examples

The production of certain plastics relies on maleic acid as a key ingredient.
Unripe apples contain small amounts of maleic acid, which contributes to their tartness.
Researchers studied the reaction of maleic acid with other chemicals in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fumaric acid - an isomeric acid found in some plants

Etymology

From French 'malique' (pertaining to apples), due to its former isolation from apple juice.
