wharf rat

1. NOUN

/ wɔrf ræt / (AmE), / wɔːf ræt / (BrE)
a brown rat that lives around wharves, docks, or waterfronts, often considered a pest
brown rat found around wharves or docks

Examples

The old warehouses were infested with wharf rats that scurried along the docks at night.
Local fishermen complained that wharf rats were getting into their bait and supplies.
She spotted a large wharf rat darting behind the crates near the wharf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown rat - the common rat species, Rattus norvegicus

2. NOUN

/ wɔrf ˌræt / (AmE), / wɔːf ˌræt / (BrE)
a person who lives near wharves and survives by stealing from ships or warehouses
person who pilfers near wharves

Examples

The abandoned warehouse was rumored to be inhabited by a wharf rat who prowled the docks at night.
In old harbor towns, a wharf rat was a common sight near the ships and crates.
Police increased patrols around the port to deter any wharf rat from sneaking aboard vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals, especially secretly or without open force
