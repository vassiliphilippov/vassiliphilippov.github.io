poste restante

1. NOUN

/ poʊst rɛˈstɑnt / (AmE), / pəʊst rɛˈstɒnt / (BrE)
a service provided by a post office whereby letters and parcels are kept for collection by the addressee
mail held at post office for pickup

Examples

When traveling in Europe, she had her packages sent to the poste restante at the central post office.
He checked the poste restante desk to see if his friend had left him any letters.
Before email, many backpackers relied on poste restante services to receive mail while abroad.

Etymology

From French, literally 'post remaining', referring to mail remaining at the post office for collection.

2. NOUN

/ poʊst rɛˈstɑːnt / (AmE), / pəʊst rɛˈstɑːnt / (BrE)
a service provided by post offices, where mail is held for collection by the addressee rather than delivered to their address, used especially outside the US or Canada
mail held at post office for pickup

Examples

I addressed the package to poste restante so she could collect it while traveling.
Foreign visitors often use poste restante when they do not have a local mailing address.
Your letter was sent poste restante and is waiting for you at the main post office.

Etymology

From French, literally 'remaining post', referring to mail held at a post office until collected.

Synset POS: n
