labour pains

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ ˌpeɪnz / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə ˌpeɪnz / (BrE)
recurrent spasms of pain experienced by a woman during childbirth, marking the onset and progress of labor
recurrent childbirth pain

Examples

She was rushed to hospital when her labour pains started earlier than expected.
During labour pains, the contractions become more frequent and intense.
Many women attend classes to learn breathing techniques for managing labour pains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraction - a tightening of the uterine muscles during childbirth

Etymology

From Latin *labor* (toil, work) in reference to childbirth, and "pains" as in sensation of physical discomfort.
