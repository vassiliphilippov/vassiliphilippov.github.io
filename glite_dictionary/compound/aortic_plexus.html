aortic plexus

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈɔrtɪk ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / eɪˈɔːtɪk ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a network of lymph nodes or nerves located near the lower part of the abdominal aorta
lymph node/nervous network by abdominal aorta
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the aortic plexus during the procedure.
Enlargement of the aortic plexus can indicate certain medical conditions.
Researchers are studying the role of the aortic plexus in autonomic function.

Etymology

From 'aortic', relating to the aorta, and Latin 'plexus' meaning network or braid.
