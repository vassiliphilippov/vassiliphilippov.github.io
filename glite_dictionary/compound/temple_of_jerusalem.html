Temple of Jerusalem

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛmpəl əv ˌdʒɛrʊˈsæləm / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl əv ˌdʒɛrʊˈsæləm / (BrE)
any of three successive temples in Jerusalem that were the principal center of Jewish worship, the first built by Solomon in the 10th century BC and destroyed by Nebuchadnezzar, the second built in 515 BC, and the third expanded by Herod the Great and destroyed by the Romans in AD 70.
three successive Jewish temples in Jerusalem

Examples

The destruction of the Temple of Jerusalem by the Romans in AD 70 was a turning point in Jewish history.
Pilgrims from around the world visit the site of the Temple of Jerusalem to pray at the Western Wall.
Solomon is traditionally credited with building the first Temple of Jerusalem to house the Ark of the Covenant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wailing Wall - the remaining outer wall of the Temple of Jerusalem complex, considered sacred by Jews
