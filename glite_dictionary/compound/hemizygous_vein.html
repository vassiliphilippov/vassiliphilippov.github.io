hemizygous vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˈzaɪɡəs veɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪˈzɪɡəs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that runs along the left side of the vertebral column, continuing the left ascending lumbar vein, and crosses to join the azygos vein.
vein along the left side of the vertebral column
medical

Examples

The hemizygous vein is often observed during thoracic surgeries involving the spine.
A blockage in the hemizygous vein can lead to complications in venous drainage.
Radiologists noted an unusual enlargement of the hemizygous vein on the patient's scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azygos vein - a major vein along the right side of the vertebral column

Etymology

From Greek 'hemi-' (half) + 'zygon' (yoke), reflecting its partial similarity to the azygos vein.
