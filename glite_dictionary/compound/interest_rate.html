interest rate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntrəst reɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪntrɪst reɪt / (BrE)
The percentage charged or paid for the use of money, usually calculated annually on the amount borrowed or invested.
percentage charged for borrowing money

Examples

The central bank decided to raise the interest rate to control inflation.
She was pleased to find a savings account with a higher interest rate.
Many loans have a variable interest rate that can change over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annual percentage rate - the yearly rate charged for borrowing money, including fees
  • Degree Lower: principal - the original amount of money borrowed or invested
