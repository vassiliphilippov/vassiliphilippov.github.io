empty quiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpti ˈkwɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛmpti ˈkwɪvə / (BrE)
a metaphor for being unprepared or lacking necessary resources or means
metaphor for being unprepared or lacking resources

Examples

After losing their funding, the researchers felt like an empty quiver facing the next phase of their project.
Without backup supplies, the hikers realized they were an empty quiver against the worsening weather.
He entered the negotiation as an empty quiver, totally lacking any strong arguments or leverage.

Synset Examples

He realized he was an Empty Quiver without a solid plan.
The team’s performance was so poor, it felt like an Empty Quiver.

Etymology

From the literal sense of a quiver (a case for holding arrows) being empty, figuratively representing a lack of means or preparedness.
