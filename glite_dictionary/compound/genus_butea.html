genus Butea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs buːˈtiːə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs bjuːˈtiːə / (BrE)
A genus of East Indian trees or shrubs, including species such as dhak, known for their vivid orange-red flowers.
genus of East Indian trees or shrubs

Examples

The forests of India are home to the genus Butea, admired for their vibrant blossoms.
Botanists study the genus Butea to understand its ecological importance and medicinal uses.
One well-known member of the genus Butea is the flame-of-the-forest, celebrated in local festivals.

Etymology

From Latin 'Butea,' named after John Stuart, 3rd Earl of Bute.
