abstract art

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbˌstrækt ɑrt / (AmE), / ˈæbstrækt ɑːt / (BrE)
Art that uses shapes, colors, forms, and gestural marks to achieve its effect rather than accurate depictions of visual reality.
art not representing visual reality

Examples

Many people find abstract art challenging to interpret because it doesn't depict recognizable objects.
The museum's new exhibition showcases the evolution of abstract art in the twentieth century.
She prefers abstract art over landscapes due to its vibrant use of color and form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: representational art - art that aims to depict objects or scenes as they appear in real life
  • Synonym: non-objective art - art with no recognizable subject

Etymology

From 'abstract', meaning 'not concrete', and 'art', from Latin 'ars' (skill, craft).
