ballast resistor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.əst rɪˈzɪs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əst rɪˈzɪs.tər / (BrE)
a resistor added to an electric circuit to stabilize current by compensating for changes, such as those caused by temperature fluctuations
resistor for stabilizing electrical current

Examples

The ballast resistor prevents the headlights from flickering when the engine warms up.
To ensure reliable ignition, a ballast resistor is often used in automotive circuits.
Because the ballast resistor adjusts to temperature, it keeps the current consistent despite changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistor - electrical component that resists the flow of current

Etymology

From 'ballast' (stabilizing weight) + 'resistor' (component resisting electrical current).
