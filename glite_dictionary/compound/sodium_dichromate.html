sodium dichromate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm daɪˈkroʊmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm daɪˈkrəʊmeɪt / (BrE)
a red-orange crystalline salt, Na2Cr2O7, used as a mordant and oxidizing agent in various industrial processes
a red-orange chemical salt used as a mordant
medical

Examples

The factory uses sodium dichromate as a key ingredient in the dyeing process.
Because of its toxicity, handling sodium dichromate requires proper safety precautions.
The chemist ordered a new batch of sodium dichromate for the laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium dichromate - an orange-red crystalline salt used as an oxidizing agent
  • Degree Higher: chromium trioxide - a red solid used as a strong oxidizing agent

Etymology

From sodium + dichromate, indicating its chemical composition of sodium ions and dichromate anion.
