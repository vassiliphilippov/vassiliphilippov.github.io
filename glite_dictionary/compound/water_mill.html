water mill

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌmɪl / (BrE)
A building equipped with machinery that is driven by a water wheel to grind grain, saw wood, or perform other mechanical tasks.
mill powered by a water wheel

Examples

The old water mill by the river has been carefully restored and now serves as a museum.
Farmers brought their grain to the water mill for grinding into flour.
A scenic hiking trail passes the ruins of an ancient water mill hidden in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windmill - a mill powered by the wind
