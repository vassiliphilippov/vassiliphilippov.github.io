bank examination

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˌɛɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / bæŋk ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an official inspection of a bank's accounts, records, and operations, typically conducted by a government regulator to ensure compliance with laws and regulations
official inspection of a bank's records

Examples

The annual bank examination revealed several weaknesses in the bank's internal controls.
Every bank examination must be conducted thoroughly to assess compliance with financial regulations.
During the bank examination, the examiners reviewed all loan documentation and investment portfolios.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audit - a formal examination of financial accounts
