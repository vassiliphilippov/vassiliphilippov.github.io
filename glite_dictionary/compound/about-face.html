about-face

1. NOUN

/ əˈbaʊt ˌfeɪs / (AmE), / əˈbaʊt ˌfeɪs / (BrE)
A sudden and complete reversal in attitude, opinion, or direction.
sudden complete reversal

Examples

After years of opposing the project, the mayor made an unexpected about-face and supported it.
The company's about-face regarding its environmental policy drew public attention.
Her sudden about-face on the issue left her colleagues confused and frustrated.

Synset Examples

His sudden about-face surprised everyone in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: U-turn - a reversal of policy or opinion
  • Synonym: flip-flop - an abrupt reversal of policy or opinion

Etymology

Originally a military command for turning to face the opposite direction.
