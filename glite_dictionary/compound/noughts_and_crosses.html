noughts and crosses

1. NOUN

/ nɔts ənd ˈkrɑsɪz / (AmE), / nɔːts ənd ˈkrɒsɪz / (BrE)
a game for two players who take turns marking spaces in a 3×3 grid with either a nought or a cross, aiming to complete a row of three of their own mark
two-player grid game (tic-tac-toe)
british

Examples

When it rained at school, the children played noughts and crosses during break time.
I always use the same strategy when playing noughts and crosses with my brother.
The rules of noughts and crosses are simple enough for young children to understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tic-tac-toe - the same game, chiefly used in American English
