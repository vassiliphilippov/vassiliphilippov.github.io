prairie mimosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri mɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈprɛːri mɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to North American prairies, with dense clusters of small white flowers
prairie plant with dense white flowers

Examples

The prairie mimosa blooms in the summer, attracting many pollinators to its white flower heads.
We spotted several prairie mimosa plants while hiking through the grasslands.
Restoring native prairie mimosa can help support local prairie ecosystems.
