negative stereotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈstɛriətaɪp / (BrE)
an oversimplified and usually harmful idea or belief about a particular group of people
harmful oversimplified belief about a group

Examples

The media has a responsibility to avoid promoting negative stereotypes in their reporting.
Certain jokes can unintentionally spread negative stereotypes about different cultures.
Teachers work to challenge negative stereotypes that students may have about one another.

Synset Examples

Movies often reinforce negative stereotypes about certain groups.
Using 'homowhore' only perpetuates negative stereotypes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereotype - a widely held but oversimplified idea of a particular type of person or thing
  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience, typically negative
  • Antonym: positive stereotype - an oversimplified but seemingly favorable idea about a group
