potato bug

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪɾoʊ bʌɡ / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ bʌɡ / (BrE)
A black-and-yellow beetle that feeds on potato leaves in both adult and larval stages; originally found in the eastern Rocky Mountains but now widespread worldwide.
beetle feeding on potato leaves

Examples

The farmer discovered several potato bugs eating holes in his crop's leaves.
Without intervention, potato bugs can devastate entire fields of potatoes.
We had to pick potato bugs off the plants by hand last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colorado potato beetle - The more precise name for the potato bug (Leptinotarsa decemlineata)
