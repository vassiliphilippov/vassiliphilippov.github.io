Jaws of Life

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑz əv laɪf / (AmE), / dʒɔːz əv laɪf / (BrE)
a hydraulic tool used by emergency personnel to pry open vehicles or wreckage to rescue trapped people
hydraulic rescue tool for freeing trapped persons

Examples

The firefighters used the Jaws of Life to free the driver from the mangled car.
Without the Jaws of Life, it would have taken much longer to reach the passengers.
Training on the Jaws of Life is essential for all rescue workers.

Etymology

Originally a trademark; named for the tool’s resemblance to jaws and its life-saving purpose.
