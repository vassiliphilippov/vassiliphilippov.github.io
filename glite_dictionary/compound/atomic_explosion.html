atomic explosion

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ɪkˌsploʊʒən / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ɪkˌspləʊʒən / (BrE)
an explosion resulting from the detonation of an atomic bomb, causing massive destruction through nuclear energy release
explosion of an atomic bomb

Examples

The devastating atomic explosion left the city in ruins for decades.
Scientists study the effects of an atomic explosion to understand radiation damage.
He wrote a novel inspired by the first atomic explosion witnessed in history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear explosion - explosion resulting from nuclear reactions, especially in bombs or reactors
