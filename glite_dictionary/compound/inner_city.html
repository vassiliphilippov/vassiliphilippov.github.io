inner city

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnər ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnə ˈsɪti / (BrE)
the central part of a city, often characterized by higher population density and, typically, lower-income residents or social problems
central, usually poorer, part of a city

Examples

Many government programs target the needs of the inner city population.
She taught for several years in an inner city school with limited resources.
Efforts to revitalize the inner city have led to new housing and community centers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suburb - an outlying residential district of a city
