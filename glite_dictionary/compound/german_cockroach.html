German cockroach

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmən ˈkoʊkˌroʧ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˈkɒkˌrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a small light-brown cockroach (Blattella germanica) commonly found as a household pest, originally native to Europe but widespread worldwide
a small household pest cockroach species

Examples

The German cockroach is notorious for infesting kitchens and bathrooms in apartment buildings.
Pest control experts said the German cockroach can multiply rapidly under warm indoor conditions.
We discovered several German cockroaches in our pantry and immediately called an exterminator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a flat, brown or black insect often found as a household pest
