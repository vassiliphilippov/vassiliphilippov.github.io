Plymouth Colony

1. PROPN

/ ˈplɪməθ ˈkɑləni / (AmE), / ˈplɪməθ ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
a colony established by the Pilgrims in 1620 at Plymouth Rock, which later became part of Massachusetts Bay Colony in 1691
early American English colony (1620-1691)

Examples

The legacy of the Plymouth Colony has shaped much of American history.
Many descendants of the Plymouth Colony still live in New England today.
The Plymouth Colony was founded by a group known as the Pilgrims in 1620.

Etymology

Named after the English city of Plymouth, from which the Pilgrims departed, and from Latin colonia (settlement).
