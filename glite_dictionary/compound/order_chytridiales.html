order Chytridiales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər kaɪˌtrɪdiˈeɪliz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kaɪˌtrɪdiˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of simple aquatic fungi, mostly saprophytic but some parasitic, sometimes classified in Oomycetes
an order of aquatic fungi

Examples

Many species within the order Chytridiales play essential roles in aquatic decomposition.
Scientists have discovered a new fungus belonging to the order Chytridiales affecting amphibian populations.
Research on the order Chytridiales has improved our understanding of fungal evolution.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chytridiomycota - phylum that includes Chytridiales; group of fungi
  • Degree Lower: Chytridiaceae - a family within the order Chytridiales

Etymology

From Greek 'chytra' (pot, vessel) + Neo-Latin taxonomic suffix '-idiales'.
