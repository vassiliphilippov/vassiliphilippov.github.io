double negative

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈnɛɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈnɛɡətɪv / (BrE)
a grammatical construction in which two negative words are used in the same clause, often resulting in an affirmative meaning or emphasis
two negatives in a phrase or sentence

Examples

The phrase "He didn't see nothing" is a classic double negative in English.
Writers sometimes use a double negative for rhetorical effect, as in "not uncommon."
In some languages, the double negative is standard for negation, but in formal English, it can be confusing.
