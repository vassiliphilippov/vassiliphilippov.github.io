angular unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡjələr ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡjʊlə ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure angles, such as a degree or a radian
unit for measuring angles

Examples

A full circle contains 360 angular units when measured in degrees.
The radian is the standard SI angular unit used in mathematics and physics.
Different angular units can be used depending on the context and precision required.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degree - a common unit for measuring angles, equal to 1/360 of a full rotation
  • Synonym: radian - the SI unit of angular measure, based on arc length
