becket bend

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛkɪt bɛnd / (AmE), / ˈbɛkɪt bɛnd / (BrE)
a knot used to temporarily tie a rope to the middle of another rope or to an eye
a knot for joining ropes or to an eye

Examples

The becket bend is a reliable knot when you need to attach a new rope to a loop.
Before lifting the load, he secured the rope using a becket bend.
Sailors often rely on the becket bend for temporary tie-downs on deck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheet bend - a method of joining two ropes of different thicknesses

Etymology

From 'becket' (a loop or eye for attachment on a rope or object) and 'bend' (a knot for joining ropes).
