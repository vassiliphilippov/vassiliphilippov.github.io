genus Auricularia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɔːˌrɪk.jəˈlɛə.ri.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɔːˌrɪk.jʊˈlɪə.rɪə / (BrE)
a genus of jelly fungi in the family Auriculariaceae, notable for ear-shaped fruiting bodies such as the wood ear mushroom
genus of jelly fungi in Auriculariaceae

Examples

Some edible mushrooms, like the genus Auricularia, are prized in Asian cuisine for their unique texture.
The genus Auricularia includes species commonly known as wood ear mushrooms.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Auricularia in the forests of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' meaning 'little ear', referring to the ear-shaped form of the fungi.
