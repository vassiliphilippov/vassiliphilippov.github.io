common hops

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən hɑps / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən hɒps / (BrE)
A European climbing plant (Humulus lupulus) whose flowers are used mainly to flavor beer and other malt liquors.
plant used to flavor beer

Examples

The flowers of common hops give beer its distinctive bitter flavor.
Brewers select the finest common hops to create rich and aromatic ales.
Farmers in the region have begun cultivating common hops for the local breweries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hops - the dried flowers of the hop plant, used in brewing
