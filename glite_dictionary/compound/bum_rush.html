to bum rush

1. VERB

/ bʌm rʌʃ / (AmE), / bʌm rʌʃ / (BrE)
to attack, enter, or approach a place or person suddenly and forcefully, often as a crowd
to rush at or invade suddenly and forcefully
informal american

Examples

The crowd tried to bum rush the stage when the band started playing.
Security stopped a group from bum rushing the entrance at the concert.
The protesters attempted to bum rush the city hall doors.

Synset Examples

We need to bum rush the event to get good seats.
They tried to bum rush the club before closing.
The fans attempted a bum rush when the doors opened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - to attack or enter suddenly and forcefully

Etymology

Originating in American slang, possibly from 'bum' (vagrant) and 'rush' (sudden attack).
