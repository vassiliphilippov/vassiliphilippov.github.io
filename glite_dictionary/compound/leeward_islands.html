Leeward Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈliːwɚd ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈliːwəd ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the northeastern Caribbean, forming part of the Lesser Antilles in the West Indies
group of islands in the eastern Caribbean

Examples

The Leeward Islands are known for their beautiful beaches and tropical climate.
Tourism is a major industry in the Leeward Islands.
During hurricane season, the Leeward Islands can be particularly vulnerable to storms.

Etymology

From 'leeward' (the side sheltered from the wind) + 'islands', named relative to wind direction in the region.
