matrix printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪtrɪks ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtrɪks ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a printer that forms characters and images as patterns of dots, typically using a dot matrix print head.
printer creating dots in a matrix

Examples

The office still uses a matrix printer to produce carbon copy invoices.
Unlike modern inkjet printers, a matrix printer makes a distinct noise during printing.
He explained that a matrix printer is ideal for multipart forms because of its impact printing method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dot matrix printer - printer type producing characters/images from a matrix of dots
