northern pocket gopher

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˈpɑkɪt ˈɡoʊfər / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈpɒkɪt ˈɡəʊfə / (BrE)
a grayish to brown burrowing rodent of the western and central United States, known for its external fur-lined cheek pouches
species of burrowing rodent in the U.S.

Examples

The northern pocket gopher can often be found creating mounds in open fields.
Farmers sometimes struggle with northern pocket gopher populations ruining their crops.
Biologists tagged several northern pocket gophers to study their underground behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gopher - a burrowing rodent of North and Central America
