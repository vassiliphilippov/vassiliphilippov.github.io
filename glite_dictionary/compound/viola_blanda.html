Viola blanda

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə ˈblændə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə ˈblændə / (BrE)
A species of violet native to eastern North America, with fragrant, purple-veined white flowers and short stems.
a North American violet species

Examples

Viola blanda is often found growing in moist woodlands across eastern North America.
Botanists admire the delicate white flowers and sweet scent of Viola blanda.
The conservation project aims to protect habitats where Viola blanda naturally thrives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant of the genus Viola with purple, blue, or white flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Viola' (violet) + 'blanda' (charming, pleasant).
