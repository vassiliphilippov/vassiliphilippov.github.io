high finance

1. NOUN

/ haɪ faɪˈnæns / (AmE), / haɪ faɪˈnɑːns / (BrE)
financial dealings involving large sums of money or complex financial arrangements, typically among major banks, corporations, or governments
large-scale or complex financial dealings

Examples

Sarah's new job has introduced her to the world of high finance in London's banking sector.
Reports of mergers and acquisitions often dominate headlines in the realm of high finance.
Unlike everyday banking, high finance deals with billion-dollar investments and complex negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banking - the business of managing money and providing financial services
