river boulder

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvər ˈboʊldər / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈbəʊldə / (BrE)
a large rock that has been transported by a river from its original location to a new site, often found lying in a riverbed or on the riverbank far from its source
large rock moved by a river

Examples

The glacier melted, leaving a massive river boulder in the middle of the field.
Geologists examined the river boulder to determine what kind of rock it originated from upstream.
Children played on the smooth surface of the giant river boulder beside the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boulder - a large, rounded rock or stone
