General Franco

1. PROPN

/ ˈʤɛnərəl ˈfrɑŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl ˈfræŋkəʊ / (BrE)
the Spanish general and dictator (1892–1975) who ruled Spain after leading Nationalist forces to victory in the Spanish Civil War.
Spanish dictator (1892–1975)

Examples

General Franco ruled Spain as a military dictator for nearly four decades.
Many Spaniards fled the country during General Franco's oppressive regime.
General Franco came to power after the Spanish Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Francisco Franco - the full name of General Franco; Spanish dictator (1892–1975)
