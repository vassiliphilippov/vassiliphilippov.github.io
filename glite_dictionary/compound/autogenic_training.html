autogenic training

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈdʒɛnɪk ˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈdʒɛnɪk ˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a technique in which individuals are trained to achieve deep relaxation by self-suggestion and focusing on bodily sensations
relaxation technique using self-suggestion
medical

Examples

Many therapists incorporate autogenic training into their stress management programs.
After several sessions of autogenic training, she found it easier to cope with anxiety.
He practiced autogenic training daily to help manage his chronic pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progressive muscle relaxation - a technique that involves tensing and relaxing muscle groups to reduce stress

Etymology

From Greek 'autos' (self) + 'genos' (origin, creation), indicating self-generated effects.
