garlic clove

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrlɪk kloʊv / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːlɪk kləʊv / (BrE)
one of the small segments that make up a garlic bulb, each covered in a thin, papery skin and used as a unit in cooking
a segment of a garlic bulb

Examples

Peel one garlic clove and mince it finely for the salad dressing.
The recipe calls for two crushed garlic cloves to enhance the flavor.
She roasted a whole garlic clove alongside the chicken for extra aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clove - a single segment of a bulb of garlic or similar plant
  • Degree Higher: garlic bulb - the full underground structure of garlic consisting of multiple cloves
