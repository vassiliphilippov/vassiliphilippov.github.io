family Caeciliadae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli siˌsɪliˈɑːdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæməli siːˌsɪlɪˈɑːdiː / (BrE)
A family of legless, worm-like amphibians, comprising all members of the order Gymnophiona.
family of legless amphibians (Gymnophiona)
medical

Examples

The family Caeciliadae includes worm-like amphibians that are found in tropical regions.
Biologists have studied the reproduction patterns of species within the family Caeciliadae.
Many members of the family Caeciliadae live underground and are rarely seen by humans.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name 'Caecilia', from Latin 'caecus' (blind), with zoological family suffix '-idae'.
