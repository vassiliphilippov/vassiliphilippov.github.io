bombardier beetle

1. NOUN

/ bɑmbɑˌdɪr ˈbitl̩ / (AmE), / bɒmˌbɑːdɪə ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a beetle that emits a loud, pungent vapor as a defense when startled or threatened
beetle that ejects a defensive vapor

Examples

The bombardier beetle defends itself by spraying attackers with a hot, noxious chemical.
Scientists have studied the bombardier beetle to learn more about natural chemical reactions.
When threatened, a bombardier beetle produces a loud popping sound as it discharges its vapor.
