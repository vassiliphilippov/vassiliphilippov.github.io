genus Pimenta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pɪˈmɛntə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pɪˈmɛntə / (BrE)
a small genus of tropical American trees in the myrtle family, including the allspice tree (Pimenta dioica)
botanical genus including allspice

Examples

The genus Pimenta includes several species notable for their aromatic leaves and berries.
Allspice, a popular kitchen spice, comes from a tree in the genus Pimenta.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Pimenta growing in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - the dried, unripe berry of Pimenta dioica, used as a spice

Etymology

From Latin 'pimenta', meaning pepper, referring to the spicy berries produced by the genus.
