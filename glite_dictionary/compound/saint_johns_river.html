Saint Johns River

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdʒɑnz ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒɒnz ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in northeastern Florida, United States, that flows north through Jacksonville and turns east to the Atlantic Ocean
river in northeastern Florida, USA

Examples

The Saint Johns River is one of the few rivers in the United States that flows north.
Boaters often enjoy weekend trips along the Saint Johns River near Jacksonville.
The city of Palatka is located on the western bank of the Saint Johns River.

Etymology

Named after Saint John the Baptist; the name first appeared on Spanish maps in the 16th century as 'Rio de San Juan'.
