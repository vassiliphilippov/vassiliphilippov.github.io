rose campion

1. NOUN

/ roʊz ˈkæmp.i.ən / (AmE), / rəʊz ˈkæmp.i.ən / (BrE)
a perennial plant (Lychnis coronaria) of southeastern Europe, grown for its white woolly leaves and bright crimson flowers, often found in cottage gardens
a garden plant with woolly leaves and crimson flowers

Examples

The old garden was full of rose campion, creating bursts of crimson amid the greenery.
She planted rose campion along the fence, attracted by its silvery foliage and bright flowers.
Some gardeners appreciate rose campion for its ability to thrive in poor soil and hot sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lychnis coronaria - botanical name for rose campion
  • Synonym: campion - common name for various species of flowering plants in the Caryophyllaceae family

Etymology

Campion from Old French 'campion' (champion), possibly referencing the vivid color; 'rose' for its flower's color.
