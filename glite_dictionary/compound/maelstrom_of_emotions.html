maelstrom of emotions

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlstrəm əv ɪˈmoʊʃənz / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlstrəm ɒv ɪˈməʊʃənz / (BrE)
A chaotic or turbulent state of emotional upheaval, often involving intense and conflicting feelings.
state of emotional chaos or turmoil

Examples

In the days after her loss, he tried to support her through the maelstrom of emotions.
Artists often channel their maelstrom of emotions into powerful works of art.
Caught in a maelstrom of emotions, she struggled to make sense of her thoughts.

Synset Examples

The maelstrom of emotions left her speechless.
She was caught in a maelstrom of emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotional turmoil - a state of great emotional disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - a state of tranquility or absence of disturbance

Etymology

From Dutch 'maelstrom' meaning 'whirlpool', used figuratively in English since the 19th century.
