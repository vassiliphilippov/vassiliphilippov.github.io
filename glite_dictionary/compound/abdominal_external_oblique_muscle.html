abdominal external oblique muscle

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdɑmɪnəl ɪkˈstɜrnəl oʊˈbliːk ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / æbˌdɒmɪn(ə)l ɪkˈstɜːn(ə)l əʊˈbliːk ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a diagonally arranged muscle on each side of the abdomen, forming part of the abdominal wall and involved in the movement and support of the torso
diagonal abdominal muscle at the side of the torso
medical

Examples

During exercise, the abdominal external oblique muscle helps rotate the trunk and flex the spine.
Injury to the abdominal external oblique muscle can result in pain and decreased mobility.
The abdominal external oblique muscle is essential for stabilizing the core during athletic activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectus abdominis - a paired muscle running vertically on each side of the anterior wall of the human abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'abdominalis' (relating to the abdomen), 'externus' (external), and 'obliquus' (slanting).
