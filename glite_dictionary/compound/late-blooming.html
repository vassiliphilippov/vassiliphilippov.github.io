late-blooming

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪt ˌbluːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪt ˌbluːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant) flowering or coming into bloom later than typical, especially in autumn.
flowering late, esp. in autumn

Examples

This variety of chrysanthemum is late-blooming, filling the garden with color in October.
The late-blooming asters are a welcome sight when most other flowers have faded.
Many gardeners appreciate late-blooming plants for extending the season of blossoms into the fall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: early-blooming - flowering early in the season
