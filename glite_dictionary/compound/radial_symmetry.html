radial symmetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəl ˈsɪmətri / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl ˈsɪmətri / (BrE)
a type of symmetry in which identical or similar parts are arranged around a central axis, so that the structure can be divided into identical sectors by multiple planes passing through the center
symmetry around a central axis

Examples

The starfish is a classic example of an animal with radial symmetry.
Certain flowers, such as lilies, display radial symmetry in their arrangement of petals.
In biology class, we learned to distinguish organisms by whether they have bilateral or radial symmetry.

Synset Examples

the starfish illustrates radial symmetry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bilateral symmetry - a type of symmetry in which an object can be divided into two identical halves along a single plane

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (ray) and Greek 'summetria' (symmetry, due proportion).
