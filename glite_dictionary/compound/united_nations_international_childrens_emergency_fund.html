United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund

1. PROPN

/ juːˈnaɪtɪd ˈneɪʃənz ˌɪntəˈnæʃənl ˈtʃɪldrənz ɪˈmɜrdʒənsi fʌnd / (AmE), / juːˈnaɪtɪd ˈneɪʃənz ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl ˈtʃɪldrənz ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi fʌnd / (BrE)
an agency of the United Nations responsible for providing aid in education, health, and welfare to children and mothers in developing countries
UN agency for children's health and education

Examples

The United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund has launched a new vaccination campaign in rural Africa.
Many schools benefit from the support of the United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund.
Donations to the United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund help provide clean water to thousands of children worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: UNICEF - Acronym for United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund

Etymology

Established in 1946 by the United Nations, originally named to reflect its emergency relief for children after World War II.
