cardiac murmur

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdiˌæk ˈmɝːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiæk ˈmɜːmə / (BrE)
an abnormal sound heard during a heartbeat, sometimes indicating a problem with the heart valves or blood flow
abnormal heart sound; possible valve issue
medical

Examples

The doctor detected a cardiac murmur during the patient's routine examination.
A cardiac murmur may require further testing to determine its cause and severity.
Some congenital heart defects are first noticed because of a persistent cardiac murmur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart murmur - an abnormal heart sound that may indicate heart valve problems

Etymology

From Latin 'cardiacus' (pertaining to the heart) and Middle English 'murmur' (low continuous sound).
