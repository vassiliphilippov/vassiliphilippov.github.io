sour grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊɚ ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of various coarse weedy plants, especially of the genus Oxalis, with a sour taste, sometimes used as table greens or in folk medicine
wild plant with sour-tasting leaves

Examples

Children often chew on sour grass for its tart flavor during outdoor play.
Many herbalists use sour grass in traditional remedies for its purported health benefits.
After the rain, the field was full of bright yellow sour grass blossoms.
