Iles Marquises

1. PROPN

/ il mɑrˈkiz / (AmE), / il mɑːˈkiːz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the south central Pacific Ocean, part of French Polynesia
group of islands in French Polynesia

Examples

The Iles Marquises are renowned for their dramatic volcanic landscapes and rich Polynesian culture.
He spent several months exploring the remote villages of the Iles Marquises.
Many artists and writers, including Paul Gauguin, were drawn to the beauty of the Iles Marquises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marquesas Islands - English name for the Iles Marquises

Etymology

French, meaning 'Marquesas Islands', named after the Spanish Marquis de Mendoza.
