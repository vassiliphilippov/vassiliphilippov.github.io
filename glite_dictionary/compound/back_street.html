back street

1. NOUN

/ bæk strit / (AmE), / bæk striːt / (BrE)
A narrow, often less frequented street, typically found behind or away from the main streets of a town or city and sometimes enclosed by walls on both sides.
narrow, less frequented street

Examples

The thieves disappeared down a back street to avoid the police.
Hidden cafés are often found in the city's old back streets.
She took a shortcut through a back street lined with tall, crumbling walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alley - a narrow passageway between or behind buildings
  • Antonym: main street - the principal or most important street in a town
