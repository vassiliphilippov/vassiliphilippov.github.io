kinetic art

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtɪk ɑrt / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ɑːt / (BrE)
art that incorporates real or apparent movement as a fundamental aspect of its design
art incorporating movement

Examples

The museum's exhibition on kinetic art featured sculptures that spun and shifted in response to air currents.
He specializes in kinetic art, creating pieces that move with the touch of a hand.
Many modern galleries now include kinetic art alongside traditional paintings and sculptures.

Synset Examples

Kinetic art always catches my attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobile - a type of kinetic sculpture that moves

Etymology

From Greek *kinesis* (movement) and English 'art'.
