barrel cactus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəl ˌkæktəs / (AmE), / ˈbærəl ˌkæktəs / (BrE)
a large, unbranched, barrel-shaped cactus of the genus Ferocactus, with deep ribs, numerous spines, and large funnel-shaped flowers
large, barrel-shaped cactus

Examples

The barrel cactus thrives in arid deserts, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
We spotted a blooming barrel cactus while hiking through the Sonoran Desert.
Be careful around the barrel cactus; its sharp spines can easily pierce skin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbærəl ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˈbærəl ˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A strongly ribbed, very spiny cactus of the genus Echinocactus, native to the southwestern United States and South America.
a ribbed, spiny cactus of Echinocactus

Examples

Many desert travelers keep an eye out for the barrel cactus because it stores water in its thick stem.
The distinctive round shape of the barrel cactus makes it easy to spot in arid landscapes.
She planted a barrel cactus in her rock garden to add a touch of the Southwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - A type of spiny succulent plant
