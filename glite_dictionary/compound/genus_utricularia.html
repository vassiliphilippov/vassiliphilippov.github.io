genus Utricularia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌjuːtrɪˈkjʊləriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌjuːtrɪˈkjʊːləriə / (BrE)
A large genus of aquatic carnivorous plants, known as bladderworts, found in freshwater habitats worldwide.
genus of aquatic carnivorous plants (bladderworts)

Examples

The genus Utricularia includes over 200 species of bladderworts found in wetlands worldwide.
Scientists discovered a new species of genus Utricularia in the marshes of South America.
Members of the genus Utricularia have unique bladder-like traps for capturing small aquatic prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladderwort - Any plant of the genus Utricularia, aquatic carnivorous plant

Etymology

Utricularia from Latin 'utriculus' meaning ‘small bag’, referring to the bladder-like traps on the plant.
