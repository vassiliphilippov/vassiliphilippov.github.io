Crux Australis

1. PROPN

/ krʌks ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / krʌks ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a small, bright constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, also known as the Southern Cross, located in the Milky Way near Centaurus
a constellation; the Southern Cross

Examples

During his travels in Australia, he looked up to admire the Crux Australis in the clear night sky.
The flag of several Southern Hemisphere countries features the Crux Australis as a symbol of their location.
Navigators have used the Crux Australis for centuries to help determine their direction at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Southern Cross - common English name for Crux Australis, a constellation in the southern sky

Etymology

Latin for 'Southern Cross'.
