alpha bronze

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ˌbrɑnz / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˌbrɒnz / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and tin, containing up to about 8% tin, that can be easily worked and is used for various industrial purposes.
a workable copper-tin alloy

Examples

The statue was cast from alpha bronze because of its excellent workability.
Engineers often choose alpha bronze for components that require easy shaping and machining.
Unlike other alloys, alpha bronze resists corrosion and maintains strength at higher temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bronze - a general term for copper-tin alloys

Etymology

From 'alpha', referring to the alpha phase in alloys, and 'bronze', from French 'bronze' (from Italian 'bronzo').
