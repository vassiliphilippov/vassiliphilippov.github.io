Davy's grey

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪviz ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪz ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
a slightly purplish or bluish dark grey color used in art and painting
a purplish or bluish dark grey color

Examples

The artist chose Davy's grey to capture the moody atmosphere of the scene.
Her living room walls were painted a soothing shade of Davy's grey.
Mixing blue and black produced a color close to Davy's grey for his portrait background.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grey - the color between black and white
  • Degree Higher: charcoal - a dark grey or black color

Etymology

Named after English chemist Henry Davy, who prepared the original pigment.
