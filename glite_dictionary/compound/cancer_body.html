cancer body

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænsɚ ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈkænsə ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
an abnormal inclusion body found in the plasma cells of patients with certain cancers
abnormal inclusion body in cancerous plasma cells
medical

Examples

Pathologists identified a cancer body in the patient's bone marrow sample.
The appearance of a cancer body is often used as a diagnostic clue in certain malignancies.
Research has focused on understanding the formation of the cancer body in malignant plasma cells.
