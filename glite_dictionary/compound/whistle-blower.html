whistle-blower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪsəl ˌbloʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪsl ˌbləʊə / (BrE)
a person who informs authorities or the public about wrongdoing, misconduct, or illegal activity within an organization
a person who exposes organizational wrongdoing

Examples

The company tried to silence the whistle-blower who revealed the financial fraud.
Laws have been enacted to protect whistle-blowers from retaliation by their employers.
After years of keeping quiet, she became a whistle-blower and went public with her findings.

Synset Examples

the law gives little protection to whistleblowers who feel the public has a right to know what is going on
the whistleblower was fired for exposing the conditions in mental hospitals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - a person who gives information, especially to the authorities
  • Synonym: insider - someone within an organization, often with privileged information

Etymology

From 'whistle' + 'blower', likening the action to a referee blowing a whistle to signal a foul.
