Queen's English

1. NOUN

/ kwinz ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / kwiːnz ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
the standard form of British English as spoken by educated people, traditionally those in southern England or associated with the royal family
standard form of British English
british

Examples

She was praised for speaking the Queen's English with perfect clarity.
Some believe that using the Queen's English opens doors in professional life.
Radio announcers were once required to master the Queen's English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Received Pronunciation - the standard accent of Standard English in Britain
  • Synonym: King's English - standard, correct English, term used when the monarch is male

Etymology

Refers to the English spoken at the royal court; the term changes with the gender of the monarch (see also King's English).
