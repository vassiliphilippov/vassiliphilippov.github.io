European chestnut

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌroʊpiən ˈtʃɛsnʌt / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiən ˈtʃɛsnʌt / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree (Castanea sativa) native to southern Europe, northwestern Africa, and southwestern Asia, valued for its edible nuts and timber
species of chestnut tree native to Europe

Examples

The European chestnut is prized for its sweet, edible nuts, which are harvested in autumn.
Several ancient European chestnut trees still grow in the forests of southern France.
Timber from the European chestnut has been used in furniture making for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet chestnut - common name for Castanea sativa, the same species as European chestnut
