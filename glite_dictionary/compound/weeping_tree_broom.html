weeping tree broom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːpɪŋ triː bruːm / (AmE), / ˈwiːpɪŋ triː brʊm / (BrE)
A small shrub or tree native to New Zealand, with gracefully drooping branches and clusters of white to violet flowers, followed by woolly two-seeded pods.
New Zealand shrub or tree with drooping branches and violet flowers

Examples

The weeping tree broom brightens gardens with its arching branches and purple blossoms.
Botanists are studying the unique seed pods produced by the weeping tree broom.
In New Zealand, the weeping tree broom is valued for its ornamental appearance.
