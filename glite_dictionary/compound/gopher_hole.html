gopher hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.fɚ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.fə həʊl / (BrE)
A hole in the ground created by a gopher, typically serving as the entrance to its burrow.
hole made by a gopher

Examples

The dog stuck its nose into the gopher hole, hoping to find something inside.
Farmers often fill in gopher holes to protect their crops and livestock from injury.
While walking in the field, I nearly twisted my ankle in a gopher hole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by an animal
