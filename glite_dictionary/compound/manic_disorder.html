manic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈmænɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A mood disorder characterized by excessively elevated mood, energy, and activity levels, often with impulsive or violent behavior.
mood disorder with excessive excitement or activity
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with manic disorder after experiencing periods of extreme excitement and agitation.
Treatments for manic disorder can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.
Patients with manic disorder may have trouble regulating their emotions and behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bipolar disorder - a mood disorder with episodes of mania and depression
  • Antonym: depression - a mood disorder with persistent sadness and loss of interest

Etymology

From Greek *manía* (madness, frenzy) plus English *disorder*.
