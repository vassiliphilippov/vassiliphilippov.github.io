fertilization membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝːt̬əlaɪˈzeɪʃən mɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən mɛmˌbreɪn / (BrE)
a membrane that forms around a fertilized ovum, preventing entry of additional sperm
membrane formed after an egg is fertilized
medical

Examples

The fertilization membrane forms rapidly after the sperm enters the egg, ensuring that no other sperm can penetrate.
The development of a fertilization membrane is essential for normal embryonic growth in many animals.
Researchers observed the fertilization membrane thickening shortly after fertilization occurred.
