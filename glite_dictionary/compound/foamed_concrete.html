foamed concrete

1. NOUN

/ foʊmd kɑnˈkrit / (AmE), / fəʊmd kɒnˈkriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight type of concrete made by mixing cement, water, and an air-entraining agent, resulting in a porous, aerated structure
lightweight, aerated concrete with air bubbles

Examples

The new building uses foamed concrete to improve insulation and reduce structural weight.
Engineers chose foamed concrete because it is easy to pour and shapes well for unusual designs.
With the addition of air bubbles, foamed concrete offers excellent thermal and acoustic properties.

Synset Examples

Do not confuse cellular concrete with aerated autoclaved concrete, which is manufactured off-site using an entirely different method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerated concrete - concrete with air incorporated to make it lighter
