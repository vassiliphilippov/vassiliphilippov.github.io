British English spelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the system and conventions of spelling used in the United Kingdom, Ireland, and most Commonwealth countries, often differing from American English spelling
orthography of UK and Commonwealth English
british

Examples

Many dictionaries include both British English spelling and American English variants.
Learners often struggle with the differences in British English spelling compared to American spelling.
Words like 'colour' and 'theatre' are examples of British English spelling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: American English spelling - the system and conventions of spelling used in the United States
