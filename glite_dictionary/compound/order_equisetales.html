order Equisetales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌɛkwəˈsiːteɪlz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌɛkwɪˈsiːteɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of lower vascular plants, including horsetails, existing since the Devonian period
an order of lower vascular plants (horsetails)

Examples

Fossils of the order Equisetales have been found in rocks dating back over 350 million years.
Members of the order Equisetales are characterized by their jointed stems and brush-like appearance.
The order Equisetales includes only one living genus, Equisetum, commonly known as horsetails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Equisetum - the type genus of the order Equisetales, known as horsetails

Etymology

From Latin 'Equisetum' (horsetail genus) + -ales (taxonomic order suffix).
