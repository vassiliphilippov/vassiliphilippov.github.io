bilevel rail car

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌlɛvəl reɪl kɑr / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌlɛv.əl reɪl kɑː / (BrE)
a type of train carriage or rail car with two levels of seating for passengers, designed to increase capacity
rail car with two levels of passenger seating

Examples

Commuters preferred the new bilevel rail car because it provided more seats during rush hour.
The city upgraded its fleet with bilevel rail cars to accommodate the growing number of travelers.
A bilevel rail car can carry almost twice as many passengers as a standard single-level car.

Synset Examples

When double-deck carriages (bilevel cars) were introduced on North American railways (railroads) they identified a problem in that it would be physically difficult - if not impossible - for the conductor to verify, collect payment and sell tickets to such a large concentration of passengers in one car, especially in suburban passenger service, owing to the short distance between stops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double-decker - a vehicle with two levels; specifically, a bus or train coach with two decks
