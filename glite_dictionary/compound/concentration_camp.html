concentration camp

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (BrE)
a place where large numbers of people, especially political prisoners or members of persecuted groups, are deliberately imprisoned in a relatively small area with inadequate facilities, often under harsh conditions.
penal camp for political prisoners or persecuted groups

Examples

Many historians have documented the conditions inside the Nazi concentration camp at Auschwitz.
During the war, thousands were sent to a concentration camp for political reasons.
She lost several relatives who were held in a concentration camp during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internment camp - camp where people are confined, typically during wartime, without trial

Etymology

From 'concentration', meaning gathering together, and 'camp'; the term gained prominence during the Boer War and WWII.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (BrE)
A place or situation in which large numbers of people are confined in extremely harsh and crowded conditions, often used metaphorically to refer to any severely oppressive environment.
place or situation with severe, crowded, harsh conditions

Examples

During the war, the city felt like a concentration camp with people crammed into shelters under harsh conditions.
Some workers describe their factory as a modern concentration camp because of the overcrowding and strict supervision.
The refugee camp became a concentration camp in all but name, given the severe shortage of space and resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison camp - a camp for the confinement of prisoners of war or political prisoners

Etymology

From 'concentration camp', originally used during the Boer War for camps where civilians were confined; later associated with graves atrocities in World War II.

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (BrE)
A place where large numbers of people, such as prisoners of war, political prisoners, or members of minority groups, are confined under harsh conditions, often without legal trial.
detention facility for large groups under harsh conditions

Examples

Many innocent people suffered in the concentration camp during the war.
The museum displays photographs and artifacts from a former concentration camp.
International organizations have condemned the creation of any new concentration camp for political prisoners.

Synset Examples

Survivors shared their stories from the concentration camp.
The documentary exposed the realities of life in a concentration camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internment camp - a camp where people are confined, often during wartime, typically without trial

Etymology

First used in English during the late 19th century; from Spanish 'campos de concentración', originally referring to camps used in the Cuban War of Independence.
