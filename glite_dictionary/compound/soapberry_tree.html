soapberry tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌbɛri triː / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌbɛri triː / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Sapindus, whose fruit contains a high concentration of saponin and is sometimes used as a natural soap.
tree of genus Sapindus with saponin-rich fruit

Examples

The soapberry tree produces berries that can be used as a natural detergent.
Farmers in some regions plant soapberry trees to harvest their fruit for making soap.
A mature soapberry tree can provide shade and attract wildlife to the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sapindus - genus of trees to which the soapberry tree belongs

Etymology

From 'soapberry' (the saponin-rich fruit) + 'tree'.
