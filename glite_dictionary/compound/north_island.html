North Island

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / nɔːθ ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
the smaller but more populous of the two main islands of New Zealand, separated from South Island by Cook Strait
main island of New Zealand

Examples

The majority of New Zealand's population lives on the North Island.
Auckland is the largest city on the North Island.
Tourists often travel between the North Island and South Island by ferry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South Island - the larger but less populous of the two main islands of New Zealand
