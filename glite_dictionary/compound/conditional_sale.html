conditional sale

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənəl seɪl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl seɪl / (BrE)
an arrangement in which the seller retains ownership of goods until the buyer has paid in full, providing security for the seller when credit is extended
sale in which seller retains ownership until full payment
legal

Examples

The car dealership offered a conditional sale agreement to buyers who could not pay the full amount upfront.
Under a conditional sale, the seller keeps the title to the goods until the buyer pays in full.
She purchased the machinery through a conditional sale, providing the seller with security in case of non-payment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hire purchase - a similar arrangement where ownership passes after all payments are made
  • Antonym: outright sale - a sale in which ownership transfers immediately upon sale

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənəl seɪl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl seɪl / (BrE)
a sale agreement in which the buyer gains ownership of the property only after fulfilling certain conditions, typically full payment
sale with ownership after payment or conditions
legal

Examples

The company offered a conditional sale arrangement, allowing the customer to use the car while paying in installments.
Under a conditional sale, the buyer does not own the property until all payments have been made.
A conditional sale protects the seller by retaining title until the conditions are fully satisfied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment sale - a sale in which payment is made in parts at intervals
