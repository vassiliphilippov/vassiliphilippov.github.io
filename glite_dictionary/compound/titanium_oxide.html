titanium oxide

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈteɪniəm aɪˈksaɪd / (AmE), / tɪˈteɪnɪəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white powder used as a pigment and in various industrial processes because of its high covering power and durability
white pigment and industrial compound

Examples

The paint contains titanium oxide for better whiteness and durability.
Researchers studied the photocatalytic properties of titanium oxide nanoparticles.
Many sunscreens use titanium oxide to block harmful UV rays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titanium dioxide - the most common oxide of titanium, used as a pigment (TiO2)

Etymology

From 'titanium', the metal discovered in 1791 (from Greek Titan) + 'oxide', meaning a compound of oxygen.
