fine arts

1. NOUN

/ faɪn ɑrts / (AmE), / faɪn ɑːts / (BrE)
Creative art forms such as painting, sculpture, and drawing, valued for their beauty and intellectual stimulation rather than practical function.
visual arts for aesthetic purposes

Examples

She decided to major in fine arts at university, focusing on painting and sculpture.
The museum showcases impressive collections of fine arts from the Renaissance period.
Many schools offer classes in fine arts alongside music and drama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual arts - art forms that are primarily visual
