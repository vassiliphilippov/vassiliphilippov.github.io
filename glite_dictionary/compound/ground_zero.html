Ground Zero

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈzɪroʊ / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the site in New York City where the World Trade Center stood before its destruction in the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001.
site of destroyed World Trade Center in NYC

Examples

Many visitors pay their respects at Ground Zero each year.
After September 11, the area known as Ground Zero became a symbol of resilience.
The memorial was built on the site of Ground Zero.

Etymology

Originally a military term for the point on the earth's surface closest to a detonation, popularized for this site after September 11, 2001.
