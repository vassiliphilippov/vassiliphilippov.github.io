Great Depression

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪt dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the severe worldwide economic crisis that began with the stock market crash in 1929 and continued through the 1930s
worldwide economic crisis of the 1930s

Examples

Many families lost their homes during the Great Depression due to widespread unemployment.
The Great Depression had a profound impact on global economic policies.
Historians still debate the causes and effects of the Great Depression.

2. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt dɪˈprɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt dɪˈprɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
the severe worldwide economic depression that occurred during the 1930s, marked by widespread unemployment and hardship
major worldwide economic downturn of the 1930s

Examples

Many families lost their homes during the Great Depression.
Historians often compare current economic crises to the Great Depression.
Unemployment reached record highs throughout the Great Depression.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth and prosperity
