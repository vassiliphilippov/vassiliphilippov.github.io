Dick test

1. NOUN

/ dɪk tɛst / (AmE), / dɪk tɛst / (BrE)
a skin test formerly used to determine an individual's susceptibility to scarlet fever
test for scarlet fever susceptibility (historical)
medical

Examples

The Dick test was once used in hospitals to identify children at risk for scarlet fever.
After a positive Dick test, the patient was considered likely to develop the disease if exposed.
Advancements in medicine have made the Dick test largely obsolete today.

Etymology

Named after American physicians George F. Dick and Gladys H. Dick, who developed the test in the 1920s.
