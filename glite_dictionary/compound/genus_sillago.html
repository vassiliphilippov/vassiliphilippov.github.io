genus Sillago

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːləˌgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪləɡəʊ / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Sillaginidae, comprising a group of marine fishes known as smelt-whitings.
type genus of the Sillaginidae fish family

Examples

The genus Sillago includes several species of smelt-whitings found in coastal waters.
Marine biologists often study the genus Sillago to understand coastal fish populations.
A new species was recently discovered within the genus Sillago off the Australian coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sillaginidae - the family of fish that includes smelt-whitings

Etymology

From Latin 'Sillago', the scientific genus name assigned to these fish.
