South Equatorial Current

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ ɛˌkwətɔriəl ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / saʊθ ɪˌkwætɔːriəl ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
an ocean current flowing westward just south of the equator, present in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans.
westward-flowing ocean current south of the equator

Examples

The South Equatorial Current transports warm water from east to west across the Pacific Ocean.
Fishermen in Brazil often rely on the South Equatorial Current to bring nutrient-rich waters to coastal areas.
Climate scientists study variations in the South Equatorial Current to understand changes in global weather patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Equatorial Current - any of the broad ocean currents near the equator
