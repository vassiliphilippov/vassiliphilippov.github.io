political correctitude

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪtɪkəl kəˈrɛktɪˌtud / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl kəˈrɛktɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the practice of avoiding language or actions that could exclude, marginalize, or insult socially disadvantaged or discriminated groups
practice of avoiding offensive or exclusionary language

Examples

Some critics argue that political correctitude has gone too far in restricting free speech.
The company's policy emphasizes political correctitude in all employee communications.
He questioned whether political correctitude actually fosters greater understanding among people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political correctness - the avoidance of expressions or actions that may offend certain groups of people

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'political correctness', using 'correctitude' (correct behavior or thinking).
