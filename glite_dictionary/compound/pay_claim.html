pay claim

1. NOUN

/ peɪ kleɪm / (AmE), / peɪ kleɪm / (BrE)
a formal demand made by workers, typically through their union, for an increase in wages or salary from management
demand for higher wages by workers

Examples

The union submitted a pay claim to management during the annual negotiations.
Teachers across the country are considering a new pay claim to address rising living costs.
The company rejected the workers' pay claim, citing financial difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wage claim - a demand for an increase in pay
