Spanish Guinea

1. PROPN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ɡɪˈniː / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ɡɪˈniː / (BrE)
the former name for Equatorial Guinea, a territory in west central Africa that was a Spanish colony until its independence in 1968
former name of Equatorial Guinea, ex-Spanish colony

Examples

The territory known as Spanish Guinea gained independence and became Equatorial Guinea in 1968.
During colonial times, Spanish Guinea was governed directly from Madrid.
Many documents from the mid-20th century still refer to Equatorial Guinea as Spanish Guinea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Equatorial Guinea - country in west central Africa, formerly Spanish Guinea

Etymology

Named for its status as a Spanish colony on the Gulf of Guinea.
