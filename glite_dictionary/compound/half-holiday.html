half-holiday

1. NOUN

/ hæf ˈhɑːləˌdeɪ / (AmE), / hɑːf ˈhɒlɪdeɪ / (BrE)
A day or part of a day on which one is excused from work or duty for half of the usual time.
half day off from work or duty

Examples

The company gave everyone a half-holiday on Friday to celebrate the anniversary.
Teachers often enjoy a half-holiday at the end of the term.
We planned an outing because a half-holiday had been announced for the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: half-day - a period of work or activity lasting for half a day
  • Degree Higher: holiday - a day of festivity or recreation when no work is done

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'holiday'.
