Pan American Day

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən deɪ / (AmE), / ˌpæn əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən deɪ / (BrE)
A day commemorating and promoting political and economic unity among the countries of the Americas.
commemorative day celebrating unity among American countries

Examples

Many countries in the Americas observe Pan American Day with ceremonies and cultural events.
Schools often teach children about the significance of Pan American Day.
On Pan American Day, leaders discuss ways to strengthen cooperation throughout the hemisphere.

Etymology

From 'Pan American', meaning relating to all the countries of North, Central, and South America, plus 'Day'.
