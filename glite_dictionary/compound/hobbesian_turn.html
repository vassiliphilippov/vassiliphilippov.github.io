Hobbesian turn

1. NOUN

/ hɑbˈziːən tɝn / (AmE), / hɒbˈziːən tɜːn / (BrE)
a change in attitude or perspective towards a more pessimistic and cynical view of human nature, often influenced by the philosophy of Thomas Hobbes
shift to a pessimistic view of humanity

Examples

After the political scandal, public opinion seemed to take a Hobbesian turn toward distrust and suspicion.
The novelist's later works reflect a Hobbesian turn in their portrayal of society.
The philosopher's argument marked a decisive Hobbesian turn in the field of social theory.

Synset Examples

The debate took a Hobbesian turn, focusing on human nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pessimistic - expecting the worst possible outcome
  • Antonym: optimistic - expecting the best possible outcome

Etymology

From the name of philosopher Thomas Hobbes (1588–1679), whose views on human nature were notably pessimistic.
