tracheal gill

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪkiəl ɡɪl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪkɪəl ɡɪl / (BrE)
an external filament or leaflike structure connected to the tracheae of certain aquatic insect larvae and nymphs, used for respiration
external respiratory organ in aquatic insect larvae
medical

Examples

The mayfly nymph relies on its tracheal gills to extract oxygen from the water.
Researchers observed that damage to the tracheal gill reduced the larva's ability to survive in low-oxygen conditions.
Unlike adult insects, many larvae possess tracheal gills as an adaptation to aquatic life.

Etymology

From 'tracheal', relating to the trachea (from Greek 'trakheia' for windpipe), and 'gill', a respiratory organ in aquatic animals.
