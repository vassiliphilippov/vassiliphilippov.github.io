genus Bellis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbɛl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbɛl.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, known for species such as the common daisy (Bellis perennis).
genus of plants including daisies

Examples

The genus Bellis includes several species commonly found in European meadows.
Botanists often study the genus Bellis to understand the diversity of daisies.
Bellis perennis, a member of the genus Bellis, is also known as the common daisy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - a small European plant with a yellow disk and white rays

Etymology

From Latin 'bellis', the classical name for the daisy.
