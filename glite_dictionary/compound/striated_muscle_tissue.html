striated muscle tissue

1. NOUN

/ straɪˌeɪtɪd ˈmʌsəl ˌtɪʃu / (AmE), / straɪˌeɪtɪd ˈmʌsl ˌtɪʃuː / (BrE)
muscle tissue that has a pattern of transverse stripes, such as skeletal and cardiac muscle, visible under a microscope
muscle tissue with transverse stripes
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, striated muscle tissue can be easily distinguished from smooth muscle tissue by its visible bands.
Cardiac muscle is a type of striated muscle tissue found only in the heart.
Scientists studied how exercise affects the regeneration of striated muscle tissue in mice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth muscle tissue - muscle tissue without transverse stripes

Etymology

From Latin 'stria' (furrow, stripe) + English 'muscle tissue'.
