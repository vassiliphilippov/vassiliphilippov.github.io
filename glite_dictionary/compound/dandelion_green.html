dandelion green

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæn.dəˌlaɪ.ən ɡrin / (AmE), / ˈdændɪˌlaɪ.ən ɡriːn / (BrE)
the leaves of the dandelion plant, often used as an edible green in salads or cooking
edible leaves of the dandelion plant

Examples

She added fresh dandelion greens to the salad for a slightly bitter flavor.
Many people harvest wild dandelion greens in the spring for cooking.
The market had a display of organic dandelion greens next to the spinach and kale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - a common wild plant with yellow flowers and toothed leaves

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæn.dəˌlaɪ.ən ˌɡrin / (AmE), / ˈdæn.dɪ.laɪ.ən ˌɡriːn / (BrE)
the edible leaves of the common dandelion, used in salads, cooking, and for making wine
edible leaves of the dandelion plant

Examples

She added fresh dandelion greens to the spring salad for a slightly bitter flavor.
Many foragers collect dandelion greens in early spring when the leaves are most tender.
Some traditional recipes use dandelion greens as a key ingredient in soups and stews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - a common wild plant with yellow flowers and deeply toothed leaves
