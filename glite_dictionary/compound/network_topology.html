network topology

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌwɝk təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈnɛtwɜːk təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the arrangement or configuration of the elements (such as links and nodes) in a communication network
layout of a communication network

Examples

The choice of network topology can significantly affect the performance and scalability of a computer network.
A star network topology connects each device to a central hub.
Engineers debated whether a ring or mesh network topology would be more reliable for their system.

Etymology

From 'network' + 'topology', where 'topology' comes from Greek 'topos' (place) + 'logos' (study).
