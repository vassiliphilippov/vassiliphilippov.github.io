denatured alcohol

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈneɪtʃɚd ˈælkəhɔl / (AmE), / dɪˈneɪtʃəd ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
Ethanol that has been made unfit for drinking by the addition of chemicals, used for cleaning, fuel, and industrial purposes.
ethanol rendered undrinkable by additives

Examples

Make sure you wear gloves when handling denatured alcohol as it can be toxic if absorbed through the skin.
Denatured alcohol is commonly used as a cleaning agent for glass and metal surfaces.
The laboratory stored several bottles of denatured alcohol for use in various experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methylated spirits - ethyl alcohol rendered unfit to drink by adding methanol or other chemicals
