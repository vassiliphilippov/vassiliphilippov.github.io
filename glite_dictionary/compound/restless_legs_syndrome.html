restless legs syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstlɪs lɛɡz sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈrɛstlɪs lɛɡz ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition marked by an uncontrollable urge to move the legs, often accompanied by uncomfortable sensations, usually occurring in the evening or during periods of rest.
condition causing urge to move legs at rest
medical

Examples

People with restless legs syndrome often have trouble sleeping due to the discomfort in their legs.
Treatment options for restless legs syndrome may include medication and lifestyle changes.
She was diagnosed with restless legs syndrome after complaining of strange sensations in her legs at night.
