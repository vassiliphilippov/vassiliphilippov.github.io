paranoic type schizophrenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˌnɔɪɪk taɪp skɪtsoʊˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˌparəˌnɔɪɪk tʌɪp skrɪtsəʊˈfriːnɪə / (BrE)
a subtype of schizophrenia marked by prominent delusions (often of persecution, grandeur, or jealousy), where patients otherwise may function outwardly in a seemingly normal way
schizophrenia subtype with prominent delusions
medical

Examples

Patients diagnosed with paranoic type schizophrenia often have complex delusions but can appear outwardly composed.
His symptoms matched those of paranoic type schizophrenia, with intense feelings of persecution and jealousy.
Treatment for paranoic type schizophrenia may involve antipsychotic medication and cognitive behavioral therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranoid schizophrenia - subtype of schizophrenia characterized by prominent delusions, especially of persecution or grandeur
  • Degree Lower: catatonic schizophrenia - subtype of schizophrenia featuring motor disturbance like stupor or agitation
  • Degree Lower: hebephrenic schizophrenia - subtype of schizophrenia featuring disorganized behavior and speech

Etymology

From Greek *paranoia* (madness) and *schizophrenia* (split mind).
