fine-toothed rake

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪn ˌtuðt reɪk / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn ˌtuːθt reɪk / (BrE)
a rake with closely spaced teeth, used for gathering leaves or fine debris
rake with closely spaced teeth

Examples

She used a fine-toothed rake to clear the small debris from the lawn.
The gardener prefers a fine-toothed rake for collecting delicate leaves.
After trimming the hedge, he gathered the clippings with a fine-toothed rake.

Synset Examples

The fine-toothed rake helped gather the leaves efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rake - a garden tool with a row of teeth for gathering leaves or smoothing soil
