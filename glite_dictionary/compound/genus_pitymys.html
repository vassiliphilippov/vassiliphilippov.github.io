genus Pitymys

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpɪtɪˌmɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpɪtɪˌmɪs / (BrE)
A formerly recognized genus of small rodents known as pine mice, now usually classified under the genus Microtus.
former genus of pine mice

Examples

The species once grouped under genus Pitymys are now usually placed in Microtus.
Some biologists still refer to genus Pitymys when discussing North American pine mice.
Recent taxonomic revisions have merged genus Pitymys with other vole genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Microtus - current genus including pine mice and voles

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and New Latin 'Pitymys', formed from Greek 'pitys' (pine) and 'mys' (mouse).
