iodoamino acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.əˌdoʊ.əˈminoʊ ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əʊ.dəʊ.əˈmiːnəʊ ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an amino acid in which one or more iodine atoms are incorporated into its structure
amino acid containing iodine
medical

Examples

The thyroid gland synthesizes hormones using iodoamino acids such as thyroxine.
Researchers studied the effects of different iodoamino acids on cellular metabolism.
An iodoamino acid can be created by introducing iodine into tyrosine residues.

Etymology

From 'iodo-' (containing iodine) and 'amino acid'.
