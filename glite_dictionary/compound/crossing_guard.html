crossing guard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsɪŋ ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ ɡɑːd / (BrE)
a person responsible for supervising and assisting people, especially children, to cross streets safely, typically near schools
person who helps people cross streets safely

Examples

The crossing guard stopped traffic so the children could cross safely.
Every morning, a crossing guard stands at the busy intersection near the school.
She thanked the crossing guard for helping her little brother cross the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lollipop lady - British term for a person who helps children cross streets near schools
