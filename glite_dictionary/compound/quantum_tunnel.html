to quantum tunnel

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɑn.təm ˈtʌn.əl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.təm ˈtʌn.əl / (BrE)
(of a particle) to pass through an energy barrier that it classically could not surmount, as explained by the principles of quantum mechanics.
pass through energy barrier (physics)

Examples

Electrons can quantum tunnel through insulators, allowing current to flow.
Scientists observed atoms quantum tunnel across the gap between the two barriers.
If the barrier is thin enough, particles might quantum tunnel instead of being blocked.

Synset Examples

They explained how electrons quantum tunnel through barriers.
In theory, quantum tunnel allows particles to bypass obstacles.
The particle's ability to quantum tunnel amazed the physicists.
Quantum tunnelling explains the behavior of subatomic particles.
Quantum tunnelling allows particles to pass through barriers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum tunnelling - phenomenon where particles pass through barriers due to quantum mechanics
  • Antonym: classical barrier - an energy barrier that cannot be surmounted in classical physics

Etymology

From 'quantum' (from Latin 'quantus', meaning 'how much') and 'tunnel', referencing the tunneling analogy in quantum mechanics.
