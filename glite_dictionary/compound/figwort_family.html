figwort family

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡˌwɜrt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡwɜːt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (formerly Scrophulariaceae) that includes figworts, snapdragons, foxgloves, toadflaxes, speedwells, and mulleins; classification may vary in some taxonomies
family of flowering plants (includes figwort, snapdragon, foxglove, etc.)

Examples

Many medicinal herbs belong to the figwort family, including foxglove and mullein.
The figwort family comprises hundreds of plant species found in various habitats worldwide.
Some gardeners enjoy growing plants from the figwort family for their colorful flowers and adaptability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scrophulariaceae - scientific name for the figwort family
