New York strip

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈjɔrk ˌstrɪp / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk ˌstrɪp / (BrE)
a tender cut of beef steak from the upper part of the short loin
a cut of steak from the short loin
american

Examples

He ordered a New York strip cooked medium-rare at the steakhouse.
The chef recommended the New York strip for its tenderness and flavor.
She grilled a New York strip for dinner and served it with garlic mashed potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sirloin - a cut of beef from the rear back portion of the animal
  • Degree Higher: filet mignon - a small, tender cut of beef from the narrow end of the tenderloin

Etymology

Named after New York City, where this style of steak became popular in steakhouses.
