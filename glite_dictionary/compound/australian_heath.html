Australian heath

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən hiːθ / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən hiːθ / (BrE)
Any of various heathlike plants of the family Epacridaceae, native mostly to the Australian region.
heathlike Australian plant (Epacridaceae)

Examples

The garden featured several species of Australian heath thriving in sandy soil.
Many Australian heath plants produce delicate white or pink flowers in spring.
Researchers are studying how Australian heath adapts to changing climates across the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - a type of plant of the family Ericaceae, found in moorland and open areas
