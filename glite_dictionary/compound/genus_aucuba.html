genus Aucuba

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɔːˈkuːbə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɔːˈkjuːbə / (BrE)
A genus of hardy evergreen shrubs and small trees, native to East Asia, especially Japan, known for their glossy leaves and bright red berries.
genus of East Asian evergreen shrubs

Examples

Many gardeners value the genus Aucuba for its glossy, variegated foliage and tolerance of shade.
The genus Aucuba contains several ornamental shrubs popular in temperate garden landscapes.
Researchers are studying members of the genus Aucuba for their resistance to urban pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aucuba - a plant of the genus Aucuba, commonly grown as an ornamental shrub

Etymology

From New Latin 'Aucuba', Latinized from Japanese 'aokiba', meaning 'green tree'.
