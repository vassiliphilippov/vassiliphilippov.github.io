mumification necrosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən nəˈkroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən nɪˈkrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
(Medicine) Gangrene developing due to arterial obstruction, characterized by dry, dark brown, mummified dead tissue.
dry gangrene due to arterial blockage
medical

Examples

The surgeon diagnosed the blackened toes as mumification necrosis resulting from chronic arterial insufficiency.
Patients with advanced diabetes are at higher risk for developing mumification necrosis in their extremities.
Treatment of mumification necrosis often requires surgical removal of the dead tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry gangrene - type of gangrene in which the tissue dries, shrinks, and darkens
  • Antonym: wet gangrene - type of gangrene marked by wet, swollen tissue; opposite form

Etymology

From Latin 'mūmia' (mummy) + 'necrosis' (death of tissue).
