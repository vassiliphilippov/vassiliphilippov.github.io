saddle block anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæd.əl blɑk ˌæn.əsˈθi.ʒə / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl blɒk ˌæn.əsˈθiː.zɪə / (BrE)
a type of regional anesthesia in which the areas of the body that would contact a saddle (buttocks, inner thighs, and perineum) are numbed by injecting a local anesthetic into the lower spinal cord
regional anesthesia for lower body (saddle area)
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a saddle block anaesthesia before the childbirth to numb the pelvic area.
A saddle block anaesthesia is commonly used for procedures involving the lower anus or genital area.
During the operation, the patient remained awake thanks to the saddle block anaesthesia.

Etymology

From 'saddle' (referring to the area affected) and 'block anaesthesia' (medical term for localized anesthetic blockade).
