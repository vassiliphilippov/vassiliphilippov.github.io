Indian madder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈmædər / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmadə / (BrE)
a perennial East Indian creeping or climbing herb (Rubia cordifolia) used as a source of dye, especially in the Orient
an Indian herb used for dye

Examples

The roots of Indian madder yield a red dye prized in textile industries.
Traditional medicine sometimes employs Indian madder for its purported health benefits.
In ancient Asia, Indian madder was cultivated to produce vibrant fabrics.
