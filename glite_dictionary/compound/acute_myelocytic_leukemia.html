acute myelocytic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ əˈkyut ˌmaɪəˈlɪtɪk lʊˈkimjə / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ˌmaɪəˈlɪtɪk ljuːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A type of acute leukemia characterized by the rapid growth of abnormal granular white blood cells, most common in adolescents and young adults.
type of acute leukemia involving white blood cells
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with acute myelocytic leukemia after observing abnormal white blood cells.
Treatment for acute myelocytic leukemia often includes intensive chemotherapy and sometimes a bone marrow transplant.
Adolescents and young adults are at a higher risk of developing acute myelocytic leukemia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acute lymphocytic leukemia - a type of acute leukemia affecting lymphoid cells

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'haima' (blood), and 'myelos' (marrow), combined with '-cytic' (relating to cells), referring to white blood cell malignancy originating in the bone marrow.
