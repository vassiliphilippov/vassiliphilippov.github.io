Poppy Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpi deɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɒpi deɪ / (BrE)
The Sunday nearest to November 11, observed especially in Britain and Commonwealth countries to commemorate those who died in World War I and World War II.
British remembrance day for war dead
british

Examples

Many people wear red poppies on Poppy Day to honor fallen soldiers.
The two-minute silence on Poppy Day is observed across the country.
Schools often hold special assemblies on Poppy Day to teach children about its significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Remembrance Day - The official name for November 11, commemorating those who died in wars

Etymology

Named after the poppy flower, adopted as a symbol of remembrance from the World War I poem 'In Flanders Fields.'
