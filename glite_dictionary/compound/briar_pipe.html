briar pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.ər paɪp / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə paɪp / (BrE)
A tobacco pipe made from the hard root of the tree heath, valued for its durability and resistance to heat.
pipe made from tree heath root

Examples

He took a moment to refill his briar pipe before returning to his book.
Collectors often seek out vintage briar pipes because of their unique grain and craftsmanship.
The briar pipe is favored by smokers for its resistance to burning and attractive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - a tube with a bowl used for smoking tobacco

Etymology

'Briar' from French 'bruyère' meaning 'heath'; refers to the tree heath root used for pipe making.
