social organisation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl ˌɔːrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the structure or system of relationships and roles among people within a society or group
system of relationships in society

Examples

The social organisation of England and America are very different.
Anthropologists study how social organisation varies among cultural groups.
Recent changes have transformed the social organisation of urban communities.

Synset Examples

the social organizations of England and America are very different
sociologists have studied the changing structure of the family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social structure - the organized pattern of social relationships and social institutions

Etymology

From 'social' (from Latin 'socialis') + 'organisation' (from French 'organisation', from Medieval Latin 'organizatio').
