Cotswold Hills

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑtsˌwoʊld hɪlz / (AmE), / ˈkɒtswəʊld hɪlz / (BrE)
a range of low, rolling hills in southwestern England, known for picturesque villages and distinctive limestone scenery
range of hills in southwestern England

Examples

The Cotswold Hills are famous for their charming stone villages and beautiful rural landscapes.
She spent her summer hiking across the Cotswold Hills and exploring local markets.
Many visitors are drawn to the Cotswold Hills for their scenic walking trails and historic towns.

Etymology

From Old English 'cot' (cottage) + 'weald' (wood, high land).
