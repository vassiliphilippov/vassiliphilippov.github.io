polyostotic fibrous dysplasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˌɑːˈstɑtɪk ˌfaɪbrəs dɪsˈpleɪʒə / (AmE), / ˌpɒliɒˈstɒtɪk ˌfʌɪbrəs dɪsˈpleɪzɪə / (BrE)
A form of fibrous dysplasia of bone that affects multiple bones in the body.
bone disorder affecting multiple bones
medical

Examples

The X-rays revealed that the patient had polyostotic fibrous dysplasia affecting both her legs and arms.
Treatment for polyostotic fibrous dysplasia often requires a multidisciplinary approach due to the involvement of multiple bones.
Doctors diagnosed him with polyostotic fibrous dysplasia after observing widespread lesions in the skeleton.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monostotic fibrous dysplasia - fibrous dysplasia affecting a single bone

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many), 'osteo-' (bone), and 'dysplasia' (abnormal development).
