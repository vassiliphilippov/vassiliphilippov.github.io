T-bone steak

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiː boʊn steɪk / (AmE), / ˈtiː bəʊn steɪk / (BrE)
a steak cut from the short loin of beef that includes a T-shaped bone with meat on each side, including a piece of tenderloin
beef steak with T-shaped bone

Examples

He ordered a juicy T-bone steak for dinner at the restaurant.
Grilling a T-bone steak requires careful attention to ensure both sides are cooked evenly.
The T-bone steak is prized for its combination of tenderness and flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porterhouse steak - a larger steak from the short loin with more tenderloin than a T-bone

Etymology

Named for the T-shaped bone in the cut; 'T-bone' from the shape, 'steak' from Old Norse 'steik' (roast).
