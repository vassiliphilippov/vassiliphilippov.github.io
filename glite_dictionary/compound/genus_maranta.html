genus Maranta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs məˈræn.tə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs məˈræn.tə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American herbs in the family Marantaceae, known for their ornamental foliage and sometimes edible rhizomes
genus of tropical American herbs

Examples

Many species in the genus Maranta are cultivated as houseplants for their attractive leaves.
The arrowroot plant, an important food source, belongs to the genus Maranta.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Maranta in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Marantaceae - the family to which genus Maranta belongs

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Bartolomeo Maranta (1500–1571); Latin genus (kind, type).
