Lord Privy Seal

1. NOUN

/ lɔrd ˈprɪvi sil / (AmE), / lɔːd ˈprɪvi siːl / (BrE)
a senior member of the British Cabinet who holds a historical title but has no specific official duties
senior British Cabinet minister without official duties
british

Examples

The Lord Privy Seal attended the cabinet meeting despite having no official departmental portfolio.
Historically, the Lord Privy Seal was responsible for the monarch's personal seal.
She was appointed Lord Privy Seal as part of a reshuffle in the House of Lords.

Etymology

From Old English 'lǣford' (lord), Latin 'privatus' (private), and Latin 'sigillum' (seal); originally referred to the officer in charge of the monarch's personal seal.

2. NOUN

/ lɔrd ˈprɪvi sil / (AmE), / lɔːd ˈprɪvi siːl / (BrE)
a senior official in the UK government who holds a largely ceremonial position and is sometimes given additional responsibilities within the cabinet
UK senior government official (title)
british

Examples

After decades in Parliament, he was finally appointed Lord Privy Seal.
The role of Lord Privy Seal has changed significantly over the centuries.
As Lord Privy Seal, she attended regular meetings of the cabinet.

Synset Examples

He was appointed as the Lord Privy Seal in the new administration.
As Lord Privy Seal, his influence in the cabinet was considerable.
The duties of the Lord Privy Seal include overseeing important state matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lord Chancellor - a senior official in the UK government responsible for the judiciary

Etymology

From the use of the Privy Seal of England, with 'Lord' denoting the official rank.
