Confederate Army

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛdərət ˈɑrmi / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
the army of the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War (1861–65)
the southern army in the US Civil War

Examples

Many soldiers in the Confederate Army were young men from the southern states.
After several defeats, the Confederate Army struggled to maintain morale among its troops.
General Robert E. Lee commanded the Confederate Army during much of the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union Army - the army of the United States (the North) during the American Civil War

Etymology

From 'confederate' (from Latin *confoederatus*, joined by treaty) and 'army'.
