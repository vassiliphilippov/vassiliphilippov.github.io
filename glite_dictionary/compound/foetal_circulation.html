foetal circulation

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtəl sɝːˌkjuleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəl sɜː.kjʊˌleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the system of blood vessels and structures that carry blood through the body of a fetus before birth
blood flow system in a fetus
medical british

Examples

Doctors study foetal circulation to understand how oxygen and nutrients reach the developing baby.
Abnormalities in foetal circulation can lead to congenital heart defects after birth.
The placenta plays a crucial role in foetal circulation by allowing the exchange of gases and nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetal circulation - the American English variant spelling of foetal circulation

Etymology

From Latin 'fetus' (offspring) and late Latin 'circulatio' (circulation).
