visible radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪz.ə.bəl ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɪz.ɪ.bəl ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Electromagnetic radiation that is perceptible to the human eye, producing a visual sensation; commonly known as light.
light detectable by the human eye

Examples

The scientist measured the amount of visible radiation emitted by the lamp.
Cloudy weather reduces the level of visible radiation that reaches the Earth's surface.
Plants utilize visible radiation for photosynthesis.

Synset Examples

the light was filtered through a soft glass window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light - electromagnetic radiation visible to the human eye

Etymology

From Latin 'visibilis' (visible) and 'radiatio' (radiation, shining forth).
