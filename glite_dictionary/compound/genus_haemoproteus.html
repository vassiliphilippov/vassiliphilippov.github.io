genus Haemoproteus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs hiːməˈproʊtiəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hɪəməˈprəʊtɪəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Haemoproteidae, comprising parasitic protozoa found in the blood of birds and reptiles.
type genus of Haemoproteidae (parasitic protozoa)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Haemoproteus infecting local bird populations.
The genus Haemoproteus is known for its distinctive lifecycle involving blood parasites in avian hosts.
Research on the genus Haemoproteus has contributed to our understanding of protozoan diseases in wildlife.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Haemoproteus' from Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'proteus' (a mythological shape-shifting sea god), referring to its variable forms.
