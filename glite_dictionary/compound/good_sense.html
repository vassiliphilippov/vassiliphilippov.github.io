good sense

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd sɛns / (AmE), / ɡʊd sɛns / (BrE)
the ability to make sensible decisions and judgments based on practical reasoning
sound practical judgment

Examples

She showed remarkable good sense in handling the difficult situation.
If you use your good sense, you'll avoid unnecessary risks.
It was only his good sense that kept them out of trouble.

Synset Examples

he hasn't got the sense God gave little green apples
fortunately she had the good sense to run away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common sense - sound, practical judgment concerning everyday matters
