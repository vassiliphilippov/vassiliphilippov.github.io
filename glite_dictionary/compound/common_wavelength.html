common wavelength

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈweɪv.lɛŋθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən ˈweɪv.leŋθ / (BrE)
a state of mutual understanding or agreement between people
mutual understanding or agreement

Examples

After several debates, the team members finally got on a common wavelength and made a decision.
It took a few meetings before the new manager and her staff found a common wavelength.
Friends often share a common wavelength, making communication easy and natural.

Synset Examples

We finally found a common wavelength during our discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: same wavelength - state of mutual understanding or shared opinions

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'wavelength', originally from physics, to express harmony or understanding between people.
