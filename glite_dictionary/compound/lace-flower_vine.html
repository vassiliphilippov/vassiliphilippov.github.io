lace-flower vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪs ˌflaʊər vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈleɪs ˌflaʊə vaɪn / (BrE)
a low-growing creeping perennial plant of Central America with deeply fringed white flowers, sometimes placed in the genus Episcia
creeping Central American plant with fringed white flowers

Examples

The lace-flower vine flourishes best in a warm, humid greenhouse environment.
Gardeners often admire the delicate blooms of the lace-flower vine.
You can propagate a lace-flower vine easily from cuttings.
