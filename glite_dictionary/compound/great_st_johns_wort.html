great St John's wort

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt sɛnt ˈdʒɑnz wɜrt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt sənt ˈdʒɒnz wɜːt / (BrE)
A perennial shrub with large, star-shaped yellow flowers, often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
perennial shrub with yellow star-shaped flowers

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of great St John's wort attract bees and butterflies to the garden.
Gardeners often plant great St John's wort for its ornamental blooms and hardy nature.
Last spring, she pruned her great St John's wort to encourage more flower growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St John's wort - a plant of the genus Hypericum, with yellow flowers

Etymology

Named after St John the Baptist and the Old English word 'wyrt' meaning 'plant' or 'herb'.
