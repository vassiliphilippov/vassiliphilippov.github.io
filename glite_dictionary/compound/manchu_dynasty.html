Manchu dynasty

1. PROPN

/ ˈmæn.tʃu ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tʃuː ˈdɪ.nə.sti / (BrE)
the last imperial dynasty of China, ruling from 1644 to 1912, before being overthrown by revolutionaries; also known as the Qing dynasty, during which China was ruled by the Manchu people.
last imperial dynasty of China (1644–1912)

Examples

The Manchu dynasty ruled China for nearly three centuries before its fall in 1912.
Many historical artifacts from the Manchu dynasty are preserved in the Forbidden City.
The collapse of the Manchu dynasty marked the end of imperial rule in China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Qing dynasty - the last imperial dynasty of China, also called the Manchu dynasty
  • Antonym: Ming dynasty - the preceding imperial dynasty of China, before the Manchu dynasty

Etymology

From Manchu (the ruling ethnic group) + dynasty; also commonly known as the Qing dynasty (from Mandarin 清 Qīng ‘clear, pure’).
