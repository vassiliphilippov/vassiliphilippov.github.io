natural number

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈnætʃrəl ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number that is 1 or any greater whole number, typically used for counting
a number used for counting (1, 2, 3, ...)

Examples

Children first learn to count with natural numbers like one, two, and three.
The set of natural numbers does not include zero in some definitions.
Prime numbers are a subset of natural numbers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: integer - a whole number, positive or negative, including zero
  • Synonym: whole number - a non-negative integer (0, 1, 2,...)

Etymology

From Latin 'numerus naturalis', meaning 'natural number'.
