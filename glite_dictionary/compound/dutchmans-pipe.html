Dutchman's-pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌtʃmənz ˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʌtʃmənz ˌpaɪp / (BrE)
A hardy deciduous climbing vine with large heart-shaped leaves and distinctive pipe-shaped flowers, especially of the genus Aristolochia.
climbing vine with pipe-shaped flowers

Examples

The Dutchman's-pipe is often grown in gardens for its unusual pipe-shaped blooms.
A Dutchman's-pipe vine can provide excellent cover for fences and trellises.
We recognized the Dutchman's-pipe by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive flowers.

Etymology

Named for its flowers, which resemble a traditional pipe once associated with Dutchmen.
