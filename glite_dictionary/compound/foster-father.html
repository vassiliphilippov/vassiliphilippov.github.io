foster-father

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑstər ˌfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
A man who acts as a parent to a child who is not his biological offspring, typically as part of a formal fostering arrangement.
male foster parent

Examples

After his father's death, Tom was raised by a compassionate foster-father.
The agency carefully matched each child with a suitable foster-father and foster mother.
His foster-father always encouraged him to pursue his dreams, just like a real parent would.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foster-mother - a woman who is a foster parent
  • Antonym: biological father - a child's natural, genetic male parent

Etymology

From 'foster', meaning to care for a child not one's own, and 'father'.
