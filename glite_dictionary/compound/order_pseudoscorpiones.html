order Pseudoscorpiones

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌsjuːdoʊˌskɔːrˈpiːəniːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌsjuːdəʊˌskɔːˈpiːəniːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of small arachnids called false scorpions, which resemble scorpions but lack a stinger.
order of false scorpions (arachnids)

Examples

Members of the order Pseudoscorpiones are often found living under bark or in soil.
The order Pseudoscorpiones includes over 3,000 species of tiny, harmless arachnids.
Scientists studied the ecological role of the order Pseudoscorpiones in controlling insect populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pseudoscorpion - a small arachnid resembling a scorpion but without a stinger

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'pseudo-' (false) and 'Scorpiones' (scorpions), denoting creatures resembling scorpions.
