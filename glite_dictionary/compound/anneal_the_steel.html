to anneal the steel

1. VERB

/ əˈnil ðə stil / (AmE), / əˈniːl ðə stiːl / (BrE)
Subject the steel to heat and then cool it to improve its hardness, strength, or ductility.
heat-treat steel for improved properties

Examples

Industrial processes commonly anneal the steel to make it less brittle.
To achieve the desired properties, engineers often anneal the steel after shaping it.
If you don't anneal the steel, it may crack under stress.

Synset Examples

The process to anneal the steel improved its durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anneal - to subject metal or glass to heating and slow cooling

Etymology

From the verb 'anneal', which is from Old English *onǣlan* (to set on fire, heat), with 'steel' from Old English *stēle*.
