water beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
an aquatic beetle, typically with a smooth oval body and flattened hind legs adapted for swimming
aquatic beetle with swimming adaptations

Examples

The pond was home to several species of water beetle darting just below the surface.
A water beetle uses its flattened hind legs to propel itself through the water efficiently.
We watched a shiny water beetle swimming among the lily pads during our field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetle - an insect of the order Coleoptera, with hard forewings
