horse botfly

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈbɑtˌflaɪ / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈbɒtˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a large parasitic fly (genus Gasterophilus) whose larvae develop in the digestive tract of horses
parasitic fly affecting horses

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with an infestation of horse botflies and recommended immediate treatment.
Stables must be kept clean to help reduce the risk of horse botfly infestations among horses.
You may notice the eggs of the horse botfly attached to your horse's legs or coat during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a parasitic fly whose larvae develop within the body of a mammal

Etymology

From 'bot', referring to a maggot, and 'fly'; found in reference to its parasitic nature on horses.
