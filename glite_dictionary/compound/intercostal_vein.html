intercostal vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈkɑstl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkɒstl veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the spaces between the ribs in the rib cage
vein draining spaces between ribs
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the intercostal vein before proceeding with the thoracic procedure.
Bleeding from an injured intercostal vein can be serious and may require immediate attention.
The anatomy class included a detailed study of each intercostal vein and its connections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intercostal artery - an artery supplying blood to the spaces between the ribs

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) and 'costalis' (pertaining to the ribs).
