riparian forest

1. NOUN

/ ɹɪˈpɛɹi.ən ˈfɔɹɪst / (AmE), / ɹɪˈpeə.ɹi.ən ˈfɒɹɪst / (BrE)
A woodland or area of trees and vegetation growing alongside the banks of a river or stream.
woodland along a riverbank or stream

Examples

The riparian forest along the river provides vital habitat for birds and other wildlife.
Ecologists are working to restore the riparian forest that was damaged during the flood.
A dense riparian forest helps stabilize the riverbank and prevent soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floodplain forest - a type of forest growing on flat land near rivers that floods
  • Synonym: riparian zone - the interface between land and a river or stream

Etymology

From Latin 'riparius' (of a riverbank) + English 'forest'.
