laser printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzɚ ˌprɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪzə ˌprɪntə / (BrE)
A printer that uses a laser beam to produce high-quality text and graphics by transferring toner onto paper using electrostatic charges.
printer using laser and electrostatic process

Examples

The office recently upgraded to a new laser printer to improve document quality.
She printed the presentation slides using the laser printer for crisp color images.
Many businesses prefer a laser printer because it is faster and more economical for large volumes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inkjet printer - printer that uses tiny jets of ink to print

Etymology

Formed from 'laser' (acronym for 'light amplification by stimulated emission of radiation') and 'printer'.
