alkaline earth

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌlaɪn ɝθ / (AmE), / ˌæl.kəˈlaɪn ɜːθ / (BrE)
any of the divalent metallic elements of group II of the periodic table, including calcium, strontium, barium, magnesium, and beryllium
class of divalent group II metals

Examples

Magnesium is an alkaline earth that is essential for human health.
Beryllium, though less common than other alkaline earth elements, is used in aerospace materials.
The reactivity of an alkaline earth increases as you move down the group in the periodic table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkaline earth metal - any bivalent metal of group II (alternative, more common term)

Etymology

From 'alkaline' (relating to alkalis) and 'earth', an old term for certain oxide minerals.
