internal jugular vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl ˈdʒʌɡjələr veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˈdʒʌɡjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
the large vein in the neck that carries blood from the brain, face, and neck to the heart
major neck vein returning blood from brain
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the internal jugular vein before making the next incision.
A blood clot in the internal jugular vein can be a serious medical issue.
Ultrasound guidance is often used to access the internal jugular vein safely.

Etymology

From Latin 'jugularis' (of the throat or neck) and 'vein'.
