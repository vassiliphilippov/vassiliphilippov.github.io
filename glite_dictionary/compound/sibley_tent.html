Sibley tent

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪbli tɛnt / (AmE), / ˈsɪbli tɛnt / (BrE)
a large, light, conical canvas tent supported by a central tripod and featuring a ventilator at the top, often used in military or expedition contexts
conical canvas tent supported by a tripod

Examples

The army supplied each unit with a Sibley tent for shelter during the campaign.
We assembled the Sibley tent quickly, thanks to its simple tripod design.
Ventilation at the top makes the Sibley tent comfortable even in warm weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell tent - a circular tent with a single central pole, similar to the Sibley tent

Etymology

Named after Henry Hopkins Sibley (1816–1886), the American inventor of the tent.
