fines herbes

1. NOUN

/ fin ɛərbz / (AmE), / fin ɛːb / (BrE)
a mixture of finely chopped fresh herbs, especially parsley, chives, tarragon, and chervil, used to flavor food in French cuisine
mixture of finely chopped herbs for cooking

Examples

The chef sprinkled fines herbes over the fish just before serving.
Traditional French omelets are often flavored with fines herbes for a delicate aroma.
She bought a fresh bunch of fines herbes at the market to use in her salads.

Synset Examples

an omelet flavored with fines herbes

Etymology

From French 'fines herbes' meaning 'fine herbs'.
