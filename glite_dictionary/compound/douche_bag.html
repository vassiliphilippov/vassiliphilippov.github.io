douche bag

1. NOUN

/ duːʃ bæɡ / (AmE), / duːʃ bæɡ / (BrE)
A small bag with detachable nozzles, used for administering a stream of liquid into the body, especially for vaginal lavage or enemas.
bag for vaginal or rectal irrigation
medical

Examples

The nurse prepared a douche bag for the patient's treatment.
Early medical kits often included a douche bag for personal hygiene.
She carefully cleaned the douche bag after each use to ensure sterility.

Etymology

From French 'douche' (shower) and 'bag'.
