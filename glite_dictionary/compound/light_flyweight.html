light flyweight

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ˈflaɪˌweɪt / (AmE), / laɪt ˈflaɪ.weɪt / (BrE)
an amateur boxer who weighs no more than 106 pounds, especially as a weight class in boxing
amateur boxing weight class under 106 pounds

Examples

She competed as a light flyweight and won the national championship.
The light flyweight division features some of the sport's fastest boxers.
At the Olympics, he was eliminated in the first round of the light flyweight tournament.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: flyweight - a boxer in a weight class typically above light flyweight
  • Degree Lower: minimumweight - the lowest weight class in professional boxing, sometimes called mini flyweight

Etymology

Formed from 'light' (of low weight) and 'flyweight' (a boxing weight category).
