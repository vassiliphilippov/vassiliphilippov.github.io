graphic tellurium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræfɪk tɛˈlʊriəm / (AmE), / ˈɡrafɪk tɛˈljʊərɪəm / (BrE)
A silver-white mineral consisting primarily of silver gold telluride, used as a source of gold in Australia and America.
silver-white mineral, source of gold

Examples

The miners discovered a vein of graphic tellurium deep within the Australian outback.
Geologists often study graphic tellurium samples to understand gold deposits in America.
The extraction of gold from graphic tellurium requires specialized metallurgical processes.
