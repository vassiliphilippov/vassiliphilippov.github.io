cotton bollworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtən ˈbɔlˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən ˈbɒlˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a larva of a noctuid moth (Helicoverpa armigera) that is a major pest of cotton, corn, and other crops
destructive crop pest larva

Examples

The cotton bollworm has caused significant damage to cotton fields this year.
Farmers are using new pest control methods to combat the spread of the cotton bollworm.
Without intervention, cotton bollworms can devastate entire crops of corn and tomatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bollworm - larva that feeds on the bolls of plants, especially cotton
