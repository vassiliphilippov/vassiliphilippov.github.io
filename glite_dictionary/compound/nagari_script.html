Nagari script

1. NOUN

/ nəˈɡɑːri skrɪpt / (AmE), / nəˈɡɑːri skrɪpt / (BrE)
a syllabic writing system traditionally used for Sanskrit and Hindi, known for its characteristic horizontal line above the letters
syllabic script for Sanskrit and Hindi

Examples

The ancient texts were originally written in Nagari script on palm leaves.
Learning to read Nagari script is essential for scholars of Sanskrit literature.
Hindi newspapers in India are commonly printed using the Nagari script.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Devanagari - the most widely used form of Nagari script for modern Hindi and Sanskrit

Etymology

From Sanskrit nāgarī, meaning 'of the city'.
