devil ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəl ˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəl ˌreɪ / (BrE)
a small species of manta ray, typically growing up to 4 feet, that travels in schools
species of small manta ray

Examples

Schools of devil ray glide through tropical waters near coral reefs.
Researchers observed a devil ray group feeding on plankton in the bay.
A devil ray can sometimes leap out of the water when startled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manta ray - large rays of the family Mobulidae known for their size and triangular pectoral fins
