Allegheny vine

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪˈɡeɪni vaɪn / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡeɪni vaɪn / (BrE)
a climbing plant with feathery leaves and white or pinkish flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Fumaria
climbing plant with feathery leaves and pinkish flowers

Examples

The Allegheny vine is often found growing along woodland edges in the eastern United States.
Gardeners appreciate the delicate foliage of the Allegheny vine in shaded areas.
The white or pinkish flowers of the Allegheny vine bloom in early summer.
