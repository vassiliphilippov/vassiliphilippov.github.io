digestive tract

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɛstɪv trækt / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪv trækt / (BrE)
the passage in the body consisting of a long, muscular tube from the mouth to the anus, through which food is digested and wastes are eliminated
the body's passage for digestion from mouth to anus
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the infection had spread throughout her entire digestive tract.
Certain bacteria help to keep the digestive tract healthy and functioning properly.
Blockages in the digestive tract can cause severe pain and require immediate attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alimentary canal - the entire passage along which food passes through the body
  • Synonym: gastrointestinal tract - the stomach and intestine as a continuous passage
