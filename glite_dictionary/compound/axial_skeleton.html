axial skeleton

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.si.əl ˈskɛl.ɪ.tən / (AmE), / ˈæk.si.əl ˈskɛl.ɪ.tən / (BrE)
the part of the skeleton consisting of the skull, spinal column, sternum, and ribs
main body skeleton (skull, spine, ribs, sternum)
medical

Examples

The axial skeleton protects the brain and vital organs within the chest cavity.
In anatomy class, we learned to distinguish the axial skeleton from the appendicular skeleton.
Injuries to the axial skeleton, such as a fractured spine, require immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appendicular skeleton - the part of the skeleton consisting of the limbs and girdles

Etymology

From 'axial' (relating to the axis) + 'skeleton' (from Greek 'skeletos', dried up, mummy).
