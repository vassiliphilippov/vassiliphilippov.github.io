autosomal recessive disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːtəˌsoʊməl rɪˈsɛsɪv dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˌsəʊməl rɪˈsesɪv dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease that occurs only when an individual inherits two copies of a recessive gene mutation located on an autosome (a non-sex chromosome)
genetic disease inherited with two recessive autosomal genes
medical

Examples

Cystic fibrosis is a classic example of an autosomal recessive disease affecting the lungs and digestive system.
Both parents must be carriers for a child to inherit an autosomal recessive disease.
Genetic counseling can help families understand the risk of passing on an autosomal recessive disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autosomal dominant disease - a genetic disease caused by a dominant gene on an autosome
  • Synonym: recessive disorder - a disorder caused by two copies of a recessive gene

Etymology

From 'autosome' (a non-sex chromosome), 'recessive' (from Latin 'recedere', to go back), and 'disease'.
