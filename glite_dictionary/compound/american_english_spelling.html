American English spelling

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the set of spelling conventions used in the United States, often differing from those in British English
spelling conventions of American English
american

Examples

In school, students learn American English spelling for words like 'color' and 'favorite.'
She prefers using American English spelling in her emails, even when writing to international clients.
The dictionary provides both British and American English spelling for each word.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: British English spelling - English orthography used in the United Kingdom and Commonwealth countries
  • Synonym: orthography - the conventional spelling system of a language
