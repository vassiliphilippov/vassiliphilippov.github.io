makeshift office

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌʃɪft ˈɑːfɪs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkʃɪft ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
an office or workspace that is created temporarily or from improvised materials, often to meet an immediate need
a temporary or improvised workspace

Examples

After the flood, she set up a makeshift office in her garage to continue working.
During the lockdown, many employees turned their dining tables into a makeshift office.
The team used a conference room as a makeshift office while their usual space was renovated.

Synset Examples

She created a makeshift office of sorts at home.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: office - a room or building where people work at desks
  • Synonym: workspace - a place where work is done, especially at a desk or table

Etymology

From 'makeshift' (temporary or improvised) + 'office'.
