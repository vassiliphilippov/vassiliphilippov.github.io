frog legs

1. NOUN

/ frɔɡ lɛɡz / (AmE), / frɒɡ leɡz / (BrE)
the hind legs of frogs, prepared and eaten as food, known for their resemblance in taste and texture to chicken
hind legs of frogs eaten as food

Examples

Many French restaurants serve frog legs sautéed in garlic and butter.
I've always wanted to try frog legs because people say they taste like chicken.
The chef imported fresh frog legs to make the traditional dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - meat of a domestic fowl, often compared in taste to frog legs
