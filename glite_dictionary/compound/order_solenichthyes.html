order Solenichthyes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdərˌ sɒləˈnɪkθiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəˌ sɒlɪˈnɪkθiːz / (BrE)
A small order of chiefly tropical marine fishes, including bellows fishes, shrimpfishes, cornetfishes, and pipefishes, all having a small mouth at the end of a tubular snout.
order of tropical marine fishes with tubular snouts

Examples

The order Solenichthyes includes several unusual fish species such as the pipefish and trumpetfish.
Marine biologists are fascinated by the elongated snouts that characterize members of the order Solenichthyes.
Some aquariums display fish from the order Solenichthyes to illustrate biodiversity in tropical marine environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'solen' (pipe, tube) + 'ichthys' (fish).
