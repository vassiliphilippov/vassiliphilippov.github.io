make-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk.ʌp / (BrE)
an event or activity that takes place as a replacement for one that was previously cancelled or missed
replacement for a cancelled/missed event

Examples

Because the game was rained out, a make-up was scheduled for next Saturday.
Students who missed the exam must attend a make-up session next week.
The canceled concert has a make-up date in early May.

Synset Examples

he missed the test and had to take a makeup
the two teams played a makeup one week later

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescheduling - the process of arranging for something to occur at a new time

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk.ʌp / (BrE)
Cosmetics applied to the face or body to enhance or alter appearance.
cosmetic products for face or body

Examples

She applied her make-up carefully before the interview.
Some actors wear heavy make-up for their roles on stage.
Do you usually buy your make-up online or in stores?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmetics - substances applied to the body, especially the face, to enhance or alter appearance
  • Synonym: face paint - paint or colored substances applied to the face for decoration or disguise

Etymology

From the phrase 'make up' meaning 'to put together, assemble', later used for the products used to beautify the face.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk.ʌp / (BrE)
the combination of qualities, elements, or components that form or constitute a person or thing
composition or constitution of something

Examples

Her genetic make-up predisposes her to certain health conditions.
The make-up of the committee reflects diverse backgrounds and expertise.
Soil make-up greatly affects which plants can thrive in a region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composition - the way in which something is made up or assembled
  • Synonym: structure - the arrangement of parts in something complex

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'make up' meaning 'to constitute or form', later used as a noun.
