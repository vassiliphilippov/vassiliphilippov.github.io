jambu fruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmbu frut / (AmE), / ˈdʒambuː fruːt / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with crisp, juicy flesh and a sweet flavor, commonly grown in South and Southeast Asia.
tropical sweet fruit from Asia

Examples

The jambu fruit is often sold in local markets alongside mangoes and lychees.
She enjoyed the refreshing taste of chilled jambu fruit on a hot afternoon.
The jambu fruit tree in our garden produces pink, bell-shaped fruits every spring.

Synset Examples

The jambu fruit was sweet and juicy, perfect for summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose apple - an alternate name for jambu fruit, a tropical fruit with sweet, crisp flesh

Etymology

From Malay and Indonesian 'jambu', referring to various fruits of the genus Syzygium.
