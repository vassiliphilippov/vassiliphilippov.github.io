cold medicine

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈmɛdəsɪn / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
a medicine used to relieve the symptoms of the common cold, such as congestion, cough, or sore throat
medicine for cold symptoms

Examples

She took some cold medicine before going to bed to help with her cough.
Many people rely on cold medicine to ease their symptoms during flu season.
Always check the label of cold medicine for possible drug interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cough syrup - a liquid medicine used to relieve coughing
  • Synonym: decongestant - a medicine that relieves nasal congestion
