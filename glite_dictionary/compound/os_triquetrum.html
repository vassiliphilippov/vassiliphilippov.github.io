os triquetrum

1. NOUN

/ oʊs traɪˈkwɛtrəm / (AmE), / ɒs trɪˈkwiːtrəm / (BrE)
a wrist bone that articulates with the pisiform, hamate, and lunate bones
a triangular wrist bone
medical

Examples

The os triquetrum is one of the eight small bones of the wrist.
Injuries to the os triquetrum are relatively rare compared to other carpal bones.
The ligament connects the os triquetrum to the pisiform and hamate bones.

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + triquetrum (three-cornered, triangular).
