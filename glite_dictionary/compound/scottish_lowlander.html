Scottish Lowlander

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːtɪʃ ˈloʊˌlændər / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈləʊˌlændə / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of the Lowlands region of Scotland.
person from the Lowlands of Scotland

Examples

Many Scottish Lowlanders settled in cities during the Industrial Revolution.
The traditions of a Scottish Lowlander can differ greatly from those of a Highlander.
She traced her ancestry back to proud Scottish Lowlanders of the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Highlander - a native of the Highlands of Scotland

Etymology

Scottish, from Scotland + Lowlander, denoting the Lowlands region.
