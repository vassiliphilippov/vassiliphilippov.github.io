nitro group

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtroʊ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəʊ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a functional group in chemistry consisting of a nitrogen atom bonded to three oxygen atoms (-NO3)
chemical group -NO3

Examples

The explosive power of TNT comes from its three nitro groups.
A nitro group is often found in many organic compounds used in industry.
The presence of a nitro group can enhance the reactivity of aromatic rings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrate - the anion or a salt of nitric acid containing the -NO3 group

Etymology

From 'nitro-' (relating to nitrogen) and 'group' (set of atoms in a molecule).
