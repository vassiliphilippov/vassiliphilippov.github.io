linear-quadratic-Gaussian controller

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪniər ˌkwɑːdrətɪk ˈɡoʊʒən kənˈtroʊlər / (AmE), / ˌlɪniə ˌkwɒdrətɪk ˈɡɔːʒiən kənˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
a controller that combines a Kalman filter (linear-quadratic estimator) with a linear-quadratic regulator to optimally control a system with random disturbances and measurement noise
controller combining estimator and regulator (LQE and LQR)

Examples

The linear-quadratic-Gaussian controller is widely used in modern automated systems for optimal performance.
An engineer configured a linear-quadratic-Gaussian controller to manage the aircraft's flight stability.
Because the plant model included noise, they chose a linear-quadratic-Gaussian controller for robust control.

Synset Examples

The LQG controller itself is a dynamic system like the system it controls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linear-quadratic regulator - an optimal control strategy for linear systems with quadratic cost

Etymology

The term refers to linear and quadratic mathematical properties and the Gaussian nature of noise in control theory.
