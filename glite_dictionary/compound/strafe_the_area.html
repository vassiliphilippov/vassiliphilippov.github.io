to strafe the area

1. VERB

/ streɪf ði ˈɛriə / (AmE), / streɪf ði ˈɛːriə / (BrE)
to attack a designated area from the air with machine-gun or automatic fire, usually targeting ground objects or troops
attack an area from the air with gunfire

Examples

The fighter planes were ordered to strafe the area before the infantry advanced.
Helicopters were used to strafe the area and suppress enemy fire.
They had to strafe the area in response to reports of hostile movement.

Synset Examples

They had to strafe the area to clear it of threats.
The jets began to strafe the enemy positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack with bombs, shells, or missiles
  • Synonym: to dive-bomb - to attack by diving toward a target and releasing bombs

Etymology

From German *strafen* (to punish).
