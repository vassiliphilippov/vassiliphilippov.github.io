gamma iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmə ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈɡamə ˈaɪən / (BrE)
a nonmagnetic allotrope of iron, stable between 906°C and 1403°C, which forms the basis of austenite in metallurgy.
nonmagnetic high-temperature form of iron

Examples

At temperatures above 906°C, iron exists in the gamma iron form.
Metallurgists study gamma iron because of its role in the formation of steel.
The transition from gamma iron to alpha iron changes the magnetic properties of the metal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alpha iron - the ferrite form of iron, stable at low temperatures
  • Degree Higher: delta iron - an allotrope of iron stable between 1403°C and 1538°C

Etymology

From Greek 'gamma' (third letter of the Greek alphabet), referring to the third allotrope of iron.
