standard deviation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstændɚd dɪˈvieɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstændəd dɪˈviː.eɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a measure of how much values in a set differ from the mean, calculated as the square root of the variance
statistical measure of data spread

Examples

The standard deviation of the test scores was surprisingly low, indicating most students performed similarly.
You can calculate the standard deviation to understand how much variation exists in your data set.
Financial analysts use standard deviation to assess the risk associated with investments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: variance - statistical measure of the spread between numbers in a data set

Etymology

standard from English 'standard' (norm), deviation from Latin 'deviare' (to turn aside), used in statistics since the late 19th century.
