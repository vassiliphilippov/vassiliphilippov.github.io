tri-chad

1. NOUN

/ traɪ tʃæd / (AmE), / traɪ tʃæd / (BrE)
A piece of paper that remains attached at three corners after being incompletely punched out, especially on a ballot.
chad attached at three corners

Examples

The recount team examined each ballot for any remaining tri-chad before tallying the votes.
If a tri-chad remains, the machine might not register the intended vote.
Officials disagreed on whether to count ballots with a tri-chad as valid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chad - small piece of waste paper left after punching a hole

Etymology

From 'tri-' meaning 'three' and 'chad' (a small piece of waste paper produced by punching a hole), used in the context of punch-card voting.
