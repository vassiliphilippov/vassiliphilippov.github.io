Dutch monetary unit

1. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ məˈnɛtɛri ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / dʌtʃ məˈnɛtəri ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
the currency or monetary unit formerly used in the Netherlands, especially referring to the guilder before the adoption of the euro.
currency formerly used in the Netherlands

Examples

The guilder was the primary Dutch monetary unit before the introduction of the euro.
Historians often refer to the Dutch monetary unit when discussing trade in the seventeenth century.
When the Netherlands switched to the euro, the old Dutch monetary unit ceased to be legal tender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilder - the basic monetary unit of the Netherlands before the euro
  • Antonym: euro - the basic monetary unit of the eurozone, including the Netherlands since 2002
