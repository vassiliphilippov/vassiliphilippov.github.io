knight errantry

1. NOUN

/ naɪt ˈɛrəntɹi / (AmE), / naɪt ˈɛɹəntri / (BrE)
the practice or code of conduct of a knight errant, involving traveling in search of chivalrous deeds, as in the Middle Ages
the conduct or practice of a knight errant

Examples

In tales of the Middle Ages, knight errantry was celebrated as the height of noble adventure.
The legend of Don Quixote satirizes the concept of knight errantry and unrealistic heroism.
His fascination with medieval knight errantry inspired him to take up fencing and horseback riding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalry - the medieval knightly system with its religious, moral, and social code

Etymology

From 'knight errant' (a wandering knight), with 'errantry' from Old French 'errantrie', ultimately from Latin 'errare' (to wander).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt ˌɛrəntri / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt ˌɛrəntri / (BrE)
the action or behavior characteristic of a knight-errant, especially conduct that is romantic, adventurous, or impractically chivalrous
romantic or chivalrous behavior

Examples

His pursuit of justice sometimes descended into mere knight errantry, driven by an impossible sense of chivalry.
The novel mocks the knight errantry of its idealistic protagonist as disconnected from reality.
Despite the dangers, he devoted himself to knight errantry, helping strangers and fighting perceived injustices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quixotism - impractically idealistic pursuit of ideals
  • Antonym: pragmatism - a practical approach to problems and affairs

Etymology

From Middle English 'knight' (a mounted soldier) + 'errantry' (from Old French 'erraunt', wandering).
