shot tower

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑt ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / ʃɒt ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
A tall structure once used for making lead shot by dropping molten lead through a sieve from the top into water at the bottom, so that it forms spherical pellets as it falls and cools.
tower used for making lead shot

Examples

The old shot tower in the city is now a historical monument open to visitors.
During the nineteenth century, shot towers were crucial for producing ammunition for hunters and soldiers.
Engineers carefully designed each shot tower to ensure the lead cooled and formed perfect spheres as it dropped.
