back passage

1. NOUN

/ bæk ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / bæk ˈpæsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a route, hallway, or passage in a building or area that is less commonly used or is private, often situated at the rear
a lesser-used or private route, often at the back

Examples

We decided to use the back passage to reach the garden without being noticed.
Servants would often move through the back passage to avoid disturbing the guests.
During renovations, they discovered a hidden back passage behind the old bookshelf.

Synset Examples

Let's take the back passage to get to the cafe quickly.
He found a hidden door in the back passage.
The actors used the back passage to avoid the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corridor - a long passage in a building from which doors lead into rooms
  • Antonym: main entrance - the main or most frequently used way into a place
