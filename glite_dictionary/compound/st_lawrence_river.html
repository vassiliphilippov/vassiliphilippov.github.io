St. Lawrence River

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈlɔːrəns ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒrəns ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in North America that flows from the Great Lakes to the Gulf of Saint Lawrence and the North Atlantic Ocean
major river in eastern North America

Examples

Many ships travel up the St. Lawrence River to reach the Great Lakes.
The explorer Jacques Cartier first navigated the St. Lawrence River in the 16th century.
Pollution levels in the St. Lawrence River have decreased due to recent conservation efforts.

Etymology

Named after Saint Lawrence, after being sighted on St. Lawrence's feast day in 1535.
