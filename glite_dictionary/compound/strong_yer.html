strong yer

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ jɪr / (AmE), / strɒŋ jɜː / (BrE)
In historical Slavic linguistics, a 'yer' vowel that did not reduce to an ultrashort vowel but developed into a different sound in a modern Slavic language.
historical Slavic non-reduced yer vowel

Examples

In Old Church Slavonic, a strong yer was retained and developed into a full vowel in many daughter languages.
The difference between a weak yer and a strong yer is central to understanding Slavic vowel changes.
Linguists analyze patterns of strong yers to reconstruct historical sound shifts in Slavic languages.

Synset Examples

Counting from the last yer in a word, the final yer is weak, the previous yer is strong, the previous yer is weak, etc., until a full vowel is reached, and then the pattern is started again with alternating weak then strong yers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak yer - a yer vowel that underwent reduction or disappearance in Slavic linguistics

Etymology

From the Slavic term 'yer', referring to ultra-short vowels in Proto-Slavic.
