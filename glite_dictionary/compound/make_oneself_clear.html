to make oneself clear

1. VERB

/ meɪk wʌnˈsɛlf klɪr / (AmE), / meɪk wʌnˈsɛlf klɪə / (BrE)
to express one's ideas, intentions, or meaning so that they are easily understood by others
express oneself so as to be clearly understood

Examples

During the meeting, he tried to make himself clear, but some people were still confused.
If you don't make yourself clear, your instructions might be misunderstood.
I hope I made myself clear regarding the new rules.

Synset Examples

I want to make myself clear about the deadline.
She had to make herself clear to avoid confusion.
It's important to make oneself clear in negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand by explaining it
