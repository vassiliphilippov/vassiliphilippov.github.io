jungle cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl ˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡ(ə)l ˌkat / (BrE)
a medium-sized wildcat (Felis chaus) native to Asia and northeastern Africa, typically found in wetlands and grasslands
medium-sized Asiatic wildcat species

Examples

The jungle cat is most commonly found near rivers and wetlands in India and Southeast Asia.
Unlike the domestic cat, the jungle cat has long legs and a short tail.
Researchers observed a jungle cat hunting for rodents in the tall grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildcat - a small wild member of the cat family
