shoulder joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊldər dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊldə dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
the ball-and-socket joint where the head of the humerus fits into a cavity of the scapula, allowing movement of the arm
the joint connecting the upper arm and shoulder

Examples

She injured her shoulder joint while playing tennis and needed physiotherapy.
Arthritis can cause pain and stiffness in the shoulder joint.
The surgeon explained how the shoulder joint allows a wide range of motion in the arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hip joint - the ball-and-socket joint connecting the femur and pelvis
