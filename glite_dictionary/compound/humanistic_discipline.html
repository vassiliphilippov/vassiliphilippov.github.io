humanistic discipline

1. NOUN

/ hjuːˈmænɪstɪk ˈdɪsəˌplɪn / (AmE), / hjuːˈmænɪstɪk ˈdɪsɪplɪn / (BrE)
an academic field of study focused on providing general knowledge and intellectual skills, rather than specialized professional or technical training
academic field in the humanities

Examples

She is majoring in a humanistic discipline such as philosophy or literature.
Universities often require students to take courses in at least one humanistic discipline.
A strong background in a humanistic discipline can enhance critical thinking and communication skills.

Synset Examples

the college of arts and sciences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanities - academic disciplines that study aspects of human society and culture

Etymology

From Latin 'humanitas' (human nature, culture) + discipline from Latin 'disciplina' (instruction, knowledge).
