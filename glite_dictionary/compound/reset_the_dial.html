to reset the dial

1. VERB

/ riˈsɛt ðə daɪl / (AmE), / riˈsɛt ðə daɪl / (BrE)
to adjust a control or setting so that it returns to its original state or starting point; to restore a situation to its default condition
return a control or situation to its original setting

Examples

If your watch isn't showing the correct time, you should reset the dial carefully.
After the machine malfunctioned, the technician had to reset the dial to its original position.
Sometimes in life, you need to reset the dial and start fresh.

Synset Examples

After the storm, we had to reset the dial on the barometer.
She had to reset the dial to get accurate readings again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - to change something slightly to achieve the desired effect
