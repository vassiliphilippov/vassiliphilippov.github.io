Metrazol shock therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtrəˌzɔl ʃɑk ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌmiːtrəˌzɒl ʃɒk ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which Metrazol is administered in sufficient doses to produce convulsions and coma, previously used for certain mental disorders
induction of convulsions/coma using Metrazol
medical

Examples

Doctors once used Metrazol shock therapy to treat schizophrenia before newer medications were developed.
Patients subjected to Metrazol shock therapy often experienced severe side effects from the induced convulsions.
The history of psychiatry includes controversial treatments such as Metrazol shock therapy and lobotomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - psychiatric treatment inducing seizures electrically
  • Synonym: insulin shock therapy - treatment inducing coma with insulin

Etymology

From Metrazol (trade name for pentylenetetrazol), a chemical compound, and shock therapy.
