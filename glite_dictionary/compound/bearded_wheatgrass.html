bearded wheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪrdəd ˈwiːtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbɪədɪd ˈwiːtɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a species of wheatgrass distinguished by straight, bristle-like awns on the flowering glumes
type of wheatgrass with straight awns

Examples

The field was planted with bearded wheatgrass to prevent soil erosion.
Researchers are studying bearded wheatgrass for its drought resistance and adaptability.
Many farmers prefer bearded wheatgrass because of its robust growth and hardiness.
