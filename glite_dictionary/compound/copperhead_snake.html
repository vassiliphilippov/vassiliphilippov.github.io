copperhead snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑpərˌhɛd sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒpəhɛd sneɪk / (BrE)
a venomous pit viper found in the eastern and central United States, with a coppery-brown head and distinctive cross-banded pattern
venomous snake of eastern U.S.

Examples

While clearing brush, he nearly stepped on a copperhead snake hidden beneath the leaves.
The park ranger warned us to watch out for copperhead snakes along the trail.
A copperhead snake bite requires immediate medical attention due to its venom.

Synset Examples

He spotted a copperhead snake while hiking in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottonmouth - a closely related venomous snake found in the southeastern United States

Etymology

So named for its copper-colored head; 'copper' + 'head'.
