New World jay

1. NOUN

/ nu wɝld dʒeɪ / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld dʒeɪ / (BrE)
any of several species of jay native to North and Central America, distinguished from Old World jays found in Eurasia and Africa.
a jay bird of North and Central America

Examples

The bright blue feather of a New World jay caught the hiker's attention as he walked through the forest.
Unlike their Eurasian relatives, New World jays are commonly found in American woodlands and backyards.
Ornithologists often compare the behavior of New World jay species with those of Old World jays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - a type of bird in the crow family, known for its colorful plumage and noisy calls
