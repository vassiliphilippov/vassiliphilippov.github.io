sound pressure level

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd ˈprɛʃɚ ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈprɛʃə ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
a measure of the physical intensity of sound, expressed in decibels (dB) relative to a reference pressure
intensity of sound, measured in decibels

Examples

The sound pressure level in the concert hall reached nearly 100 decibels during the final song.
She wore earplugs because the sound pressure level at the construction site was dangerously high.
Engineers measured the sound pressure level to ensure the machinery met safety regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - the loudness of a sound
  • Synonym: decibel - unit for measuring sound intensity
