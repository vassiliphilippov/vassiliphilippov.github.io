Roman times

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən taɪmz / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən taɪmz / (BrE)
the period in history when ancient Rome dominated Europe, typically from the founding of Rome (traditionally 753 BC) to the fall of the Western Roman Empire (AD 476)
historical period of ancient Rome's dominance

Examples

Many roads built during Roman times are still in use today.
The art and architecture of Roman times continue to influence modern design.
In Roman times, gladiatorial games were a popular form of entertainment.
