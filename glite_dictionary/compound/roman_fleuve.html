roman fleuve

1. NOUN

/ roʊˌmɑn ˈflɜv / (AmE), / rəʊˌmɑ̃ ˈflɶv / (BrE)
A long series of novels, often chronicling the history of a family or social group across generations.
a long novel series about a family or group

Examples

Marcel Proust's 'In Search of Lost Time' is a classic example of a roman fleuve.
The author aspired to create a roman fleuve that would depict every aspect of modern life.
Each volume in the roman fleuve adds depth to the family's saga.

Etymology

From French, literally 'river novel', referring to its flowing, extended narrative form.
