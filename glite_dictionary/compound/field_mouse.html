field mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌmaʊs / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌmaʊs / (BrE)
a small rodent with a short tail and inconspicuous ears, commonly found in fields and meadows, especially species of the genus Microtus or related groups.
small rodent found in fields and meadows

Examples

A field mouse scurried across the grass, barely visible among the tall weeds.
Barn owls often hunt the field mouse at dusk in rural meadows.
Last autumn, I found a tiny field mouse nesting in our garden shed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small rodent similar to a field mouse, often of genus Microtus

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌmaʊs / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌmaʊs / (BrE)
a small nocturnal mouse, especially of the genus Apodemus, commonly found in woods, fields, and gardens in the Old World
a small wild mouse found in fields and woods

Examples

A field mouse darted across the path and disappeared into the tall grass.
Researchers observed the diet of the field mouse throughout the changing seasons.
The barn cat left a field mouse on the doorstep as a gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood mouse - a common type of field mouse in Europe
  • Antonym: house mouse - a common mouse species that typically lives in human dwellings
