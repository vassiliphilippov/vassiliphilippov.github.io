Mexican onyx

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən ˈɑːnɪks / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈɒnɪks / (BrE)
a hard, compact variety of calcite, often banded and used for decorative objects, chiefly sourced from Mexico
decorative banded calcite from Mexico

Examples

The fireplace was beautifully decorated with Mexican onyx panels.
She collects vases made of Mexican onyx because of their colorful patterns.
This sculpture was carved from a single block of Mexican onyx.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onyx - a gemstone with parallel bands, often used in jewelry
