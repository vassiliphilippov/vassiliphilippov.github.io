beggar lice

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡɚ laɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə laɪs / (BrE)
any of several tropical or subtropical plants with trifoliate leaves and sticky seed pods that easily adhere to animals or clothing
plant with sticky seed pods

Examples

The beggar lice stuck to my pants after I walked through the tall grass.
Several species of beggar lice are common in southern forests.
I spent hours picking beggar lice off my dog's fur after our hike.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡər laɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə laɪs / (BrE)
Any of several Eurasian and North American plants whose small prickly seeds stick to clothing or fur.
prickly-seeded plant; seeds stick to clothing

Examples

My socks were covered with beggar lice after hiking through the field.
Children often complain when beggar lice get stuck on their clothes during outdoor games.
Some animals help spread beggar lice by carrying the seeds in their fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticktight - plant with seeds that stick to clothing or animals
  • Synonym: burdock - plant with burrs that cling to fabric and fur
