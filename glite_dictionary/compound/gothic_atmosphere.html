Gothic atmosphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːθɪk ˈætməsˌfɪr / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk ˈætməsfɪə / (BrE)
a dark, mysterious, and eerie mood or setting, especially characteristic of Gothic literature, architecture, or art
dark, mysterious, and eerie mood or setting

Examples

The abandoned castle had a Gothic atmosphere that sent chills down my spine.
The novelist skillfully created a Gothic atmosphere through vivid descriptions and haunting imagery.
Heavy shadows and old stone architecture contributed to the Gothic atmosphere of the cathedral.

Synset Examples

The film had a Gothic atmosphere that captivated viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gothic - related to the mysterious or supernatural style in literature and art
  • Synonym: macabre - having a grim or ghastly atmosphere
  • Antonym: cheerful atmosphere - a bright and happy mood or setting

Etymology

Gothic from Gothic (relating to the Gothic style), atmosphere from Greek atmos (vapor) + sphaira (sphere).
