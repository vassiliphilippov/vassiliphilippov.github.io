pond cypress

1. NOUN

/ pɑnd ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / pɒnd ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
A deciduous coniferous tree (Taxodium ascendens) of the cypress family, native to wetlands of the southeastern United States.
deciduous coniferous tree of U.S. wetlands

Examples

The pond cypress is commonly found in shallow wetlands across the southeastern United States.
Many birds nest in the branches of the pond cypress during spring.
Unlike the bald cypress, the pond cypress prefers still, acidic waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - A related species, Taxodium distichum, found in swamps of the southeastern U.S.

2. NOUN

/ pɑnd ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / pɒnd ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
The wood of a deciduous conifer tree (Taxodium ascendens) native to North America, valued for its durability in wet environments.
wood of a North American conifer tree

Examples

The furniture was constructed from pond cypress due to its resistance to water damage.
In the southeastern United States, pond cypress is often used for making outdoor decking.
The unique grain of pond cypress makes it a favorite among woodworkers for rustic designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - wood of a related Taxodium species, also native to North America

Etymology

From 'pond' (a small body of water) and 'cypress,' from Greek 'kyparissos.'
