constant-volume process

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnstənt ˈvɑljum ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt ˈvɒljuːm ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a thermodynamic process in which the volume of a closed system remains unchanged while other properties, such as pressure or temperature, may vary.
thermodynamic process with unchanging volume

Examples

Heating a gas in a constant-volume process results in an increase in pressure but not in volume.
The internal energy of an ideal gas changes only with temperature in a constant-volume process.
A constant-volume process is often referred to as an isochoric process in thermodynamics.

Synset Examples

In practice, a gas that receives heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume heating and at the same time it is a constant volume pressurization; a gas that looses heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume cooling and at the same time it is a constant volume depressurization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isochoric process - a thermodynamic process that occurs at constant volume
