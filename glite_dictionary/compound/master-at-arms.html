master-at-arms

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstər ət ˈɑrmz / (AmE), / ˌmɑːstər ət ˈɑːmz / (BrE)
a petty officer in the navy responsible for maintaining discipline and order aboard ship
naval petty officer responsible for discipline

Examples

The master-at-arms was called to handle the dispute between sailors.
During drills, the master-at-arms inspected every compartment for order and discipline.
Serving as master-at-arms requires a strong sense of authority and leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sergeant-at-arms - an officer maintaining order in a legislative body or organization
