sensory receptor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ɹi rɪˈsɛp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sə.ɹi rɪˈsɛp.tə / (BrE)
a specialized structure or organ with nerve endings that responds to specific types of external or internal stimuli, such as touch, light, or sound
structure that responds to sensory stimuli
medical

Examples

The skin contains several types of sensory receptors that can detect temperature and pressure changes.
Sound waves are converted into electrical signals by sensory receptors in the inner ear.
Damage to sensory receptors in the retina can result in partial or total vision loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptor - a structure in the body that receives and responds to stimuli

Etymology

From Latin sensus (feeling, perception) + receptor (receiver).
