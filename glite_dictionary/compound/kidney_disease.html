kidney disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdni dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the kidneys are damaged or do not function properly.
disease affecting the kidneys
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that her high blood pressure was caused by kidney disease.
Early detection of kidney disease can help prevent serious complications.
A healthy diet and regular check-ups are important for people at risk of kidney disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renal disease - another term for kidney disease
  • Degree Higher: kidney failure - severe loss of kidney function
