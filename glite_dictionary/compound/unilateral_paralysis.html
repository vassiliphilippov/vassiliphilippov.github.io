unilateral paralysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.nəˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl pəˈræl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈlæt.ər.əl pəˈræl.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
Paralysis affecting only one side of the body.
paralysis on one side of the body
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited unilateral paralysis after suffering a stroke on the left side of the brain.
Physical therapy can help some people regain movement lost due to unilateral paralysis.
Doctors quickly recognized the symptoms of unilateral paralysis during the neurological examination.

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'latus' (side) and Greek 'paralysis' (loss of ability to move).
