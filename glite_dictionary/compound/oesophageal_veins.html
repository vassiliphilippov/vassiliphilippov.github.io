oesophageal veins

1. NOUN

/ iˌsɑfəˈdʒiəl veɪnz / (AmE), / iˌsɒfəˈdʒiːəl veɪnz / (BrE)
small veins that drain blood from the esophagus, typically emptying into the brachiocephalic vein or the azygos veins
veins draining the esophagus
medical british

Examples

The oesophageal veins can become enlarged in certain liver diseases, leading to varices.
Surgeons must take care to avoid damaging the oesophageal veins during thoracic operations.
The oesophageal veins are important pathways for blood to return to the systemic circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esophageal veins - veins draining the esophagus (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'oisophagos' (gullet) and Latin 'vena' (vein).
