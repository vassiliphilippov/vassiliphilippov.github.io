Baroque period

1. NOUN

/ bəˈroʊk ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊk ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
The period in European history, roughly from 1600 to 1750, characterized by a distinctive style of art, architecture, and music known for its grandeur and ornamentation.
era of ornate European art (c.1600–1750)

Examples

Many masterpieces of European painting and sculpture were created during the Baroque period.
Bach and Handel are among the most celebrated composers of the Baroque period.
The Baroque period is known for its dramatic use of light and shadow in art.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Renaissance - The preceding period in European history featured classical revival (c. 14th–17th centuries)
  • Degree Higher: Classical period - The later period in Western music and art, emphasizing clarity and balance (c. 1750–1820)

Etymology

From Italian *barocco* (irregularly shaped pearl) and Latin *periodus* (cycle, period of time).
