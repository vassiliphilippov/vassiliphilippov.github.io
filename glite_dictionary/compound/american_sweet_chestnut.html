American sweet chestnut

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən swiːt ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən swiːt ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree (Castanea dentata) native to eastern North America, valued for its wood and nuts
large tree native to eastern North America

Examples

The American sweet chestnut once dominated forests in the eastern United States, providing shelter and food for wildlife.
Diseases such as chestnut blight devastated the American sweet chestnut population in the 20th century.
Many conservationists are working to restore the American sweet chestnut to its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - a tree or shrub producing edible nuts, or the nut itself
