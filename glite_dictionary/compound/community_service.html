community service

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunəti ˈsɝvɪs / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəti ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
work that is done without pay to help people in a community, often as a punishment or as voluntary charity.
voluntary or required work for the community

Examples

As part of his sentence, he was required to complete 100 hours of community service.
She chose to spend her summer vacation doing community service at the local shelter.
The students organized a community service project to clean up the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volunteer work - work done willingly without pay to help others

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunəti ˈsɝvəs / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəti ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
Unpaid work performed by an offender for the benefit of the community, imposed as part or all of a criminal sentence.
unpaid work imposed as punishment
legal

Examples

After being convicted of vandalism, he was sentenced to 100 hours of community service.
The judge ordered her to perform community service instead of going to jail.
Completing community service helped him understand the importance of giving back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probation - a period of supervised freedom for offenders instead of prison

3. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunəti ˈsɝvɪs / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəti ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
voluntary work intended to help people in a particular area, especially as part of an organized program or as a legal penalty
voluntary work for the community

Examples

The judge ordered him to perform 100 hours of community service for vandalism.
She received an award for her outstanding community service work in the neighborhood.
Many students participate in community service projects during the summer break.

Synset Examples

The jubilee marked 50 years of community service.
The Wood family is known for their community service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volunteering - working without pay to help others or benefit a cause
