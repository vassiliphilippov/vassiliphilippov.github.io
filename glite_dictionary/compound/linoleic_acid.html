linoleic acid

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnoʊliɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / lɪˈnəʊliːɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a liquid polyunsaturated fatty acid found abundantly in plant oils and fats, essential in human nutrition and used in making soap
essential polyunsaturated fatty acid
medical

Examples

Sunflower oil is a rich source of linoleic acid, which is an essential nutrient for humans.
The manufacturer advertises their soap as containing natural linoleic acid from plant oils.
A deficiency in linoleic acid can lead to various health problems, especially related to skin and hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatty acid - any of the acids that compose fats and oils

Etymology

From Latin *linum* (flax) and Greek *elaion* (oil), reflecting its presence in linseed oil.
