artesian well

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtiːʒən wɛl / (AmE), / ɑːˈtiːzɪən wɛl / (BrE)
A well drilled through impermeable rock into a water-bearing layer, where water rises to the surface due to natural pressure.
well where water rises naturally under pressure

Examples

Early settlers relied on an artesian well to provide drinking water for the village.
After drilling, they discovered an artesian well that supplied water without the need for a pump.
The farm installed a new artesian well to irrigate the fields during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well - an artificial shaft dug or bored to obtain water, oil, or gas

Etymology

From French 'artésien', referring to the Artois region in France, where such wells were first constructed in the 12th century.
