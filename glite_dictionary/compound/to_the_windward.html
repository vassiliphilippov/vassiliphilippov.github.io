to the windward

1. ADV

/ tə ðə ˈwɪndwɚd / (AmE), / tə ðə ˈwɪndwəd / (BrE)
in the direction from which the wind is blowing, especially at sea or in sailing contexts.
toward the direction the wind comes from, nautical

Examples

The sailors moved to the windward to gain an advantage in the race.
We anchored to the windward of the island to avoid the strong current.
The fishing boat drifted to the windward during the night.

Synset Examples

The captain decided to lay an anchor to the windward to stabilize the boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windward - facing or toward the direction from which the wind blows
  • Antonym: leeward - on or toward the side sheltered from the wind
