Japanese oyster

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈɔɪstər / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
a large edible oyster native to Japan, often farmed and introduced to other regions such as the Pacific coast of the United States
a species of oyster native to Japan

Examples

Scientists are studying whether the Japanese oyster can thrive in Chesapeake Bay.
Many seafood restaurants serve Japanese oyster as a delicacy.
The introduction of the Japanese oyster has affected local ecosystems on the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pacific oyster - an alternative common name for the same species (Crassostrea gigas)
