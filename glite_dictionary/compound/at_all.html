at all

1. ADV

/ ət ɔl / (AmE), / ət ɔːl / (BrE)
in any way or to any extent, especially used in questions or negative statements to emphasize the degree
to any extent; in any way

Examples

Are you at all concerned about the delay?
She didn't help me at all with the assignment.
If you need at all more information, please let me know.

Synset Examples

Are you at all interested? No, not at all
was not in the least unfriendly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the least - in any way or to any degree
  • Degree Higher: at all costs - no matter what; regardless of cost or effort
