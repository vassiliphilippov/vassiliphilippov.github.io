London plane

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌn.dən pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈlʌn.dən pleɪn / (BrE)
a very large, fast-growing tree (Platanus × acerifolia), widely planted as a street tree in cities for its tolerance of pollution and urban conditions
a large urban street tree

Examples

Rows of London plane trees provide welcome shade along the city's busiest streets.
The London plane is prized for its ability to thrive in polluted urban environments.
After the storm, several old London plane branches had to be cleared from the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plane tree - deciduous trees in the genus Platanus

Etymology

Named after the city of London, where the tree became widely planted in the 18th and 19th centuries, and 'plane' from the Latin 'platanus'.
