narrow-leaved reedmace

1. NOUN

/ ˌnæroʊ livd ˈridˌmæs / (AmE), / ˌnærəʊ liːvd ˈriːdmæs / (BrE)
a tall water plant with narrow leaves and a characteristic cylindrical brown flower spike, found in wetlands of America, Europe, North Africa, and Asia; also known as Typha angustifolia.
a wetland plant with narrow leaves and brown spike

Examples

The wetland reserve is home to vast stands of narrow-leaved reedmace swaying in the breeze.
Conservationists study the spread of narrow-leaved reedmace along the lakeshore each summer.
The narrow-leaved reedmace is easily recognized by its slender leaves and brown flower spike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reedmace - a tall water plant with a brown cylindrical flower spike; genus Typha
