the w-word

1. NOUN

/ ði ˈdʌbəl.juː wɝːd / (AmE), / ði ˈdʌbəl.juː wɜːd / (BrE)
a euphemistic way of referring to a word beginning with 'w' that is considered sensitive, offensive, or taboo, without saying the word itself
euphemism for a taboo 'w' word

Examples

During the heated debate, no one dared to say the w-word out loud.
Some parents prefer to use the w-word instead of openly discussing it with their children.
The journalist referenced the w-word to highlight ongoing social issues without causing offense.

Synset Examples

He avoided the w-word during the sensitive discussion.
The comedian joked about the w-word being taboo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the n-word - a euphemism for a highly offensive word beginning with 'n'
  • Synonym: the f-word - a euphemism for a taboo word beginning with 'f'

Etymology

Formed on the pattern of 'the n-word'; 'w' stands for an avoided word starting with that letter.
