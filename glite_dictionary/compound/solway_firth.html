Solway Firth

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑl.weɪ ˈfɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.weɪ ˈfɜːθ / (BrE)
an inlet of the Irish Sea forming part of the border between northwestern England and southwestern Scotland
inlet between England and Scotland

Examples

The Solway Firth is known for its dramatic tides and beautiful coastal landscapes.
Several rivers, including the Eden and Esk, flow into the Solway Firth.
Wildlife enthusiasts visit the Solway Firth to observe rare migrating birds.

Etymology

The name "Solway" is of Old Norse origin, likely from 'súl' (pillar) and 'vað' (ford, crossing).
