Jaish-e-Muhammad

1. PROPN

/ dʒaɪʃ eɪ mʊˈhɑːməd / (AmE), / dʒaɪʃ eɪ mʊˈhɑːməd / (BrE)
A militant Islamist organization founded in 2000, active primarily in Kashmir, known for carrying out attacks and kidnappings to secure the release of imprisoned members.
militant Islamist group active in Kashmir

Examples

The government has placed Jaish-e-Muhammad on its list of banned organizations.
Authorities believe Jaish-e-Muhammad was responsible for the recent attack in the region.
International pressure has mounted to take action against Jaish-e-Muhammad after several high-profile incidents.

Etymology

From Urdu, literally 'Army of Muhammad'.
