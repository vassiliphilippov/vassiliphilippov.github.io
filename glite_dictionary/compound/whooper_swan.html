whooper swan

1. NOUN

/ ˈhwuːpər swɑn / (AmE), / ˈwuːpə swɒn / (BrE)
a large Old World swan (Cygnus cygnus) known for its loud, whooping call
a large swan with a whooping call

Examples

The whooper swan migrates thousands of miles between its breeding and wintering grounds each year.
A group of whooper swans was spotted by birdwatchers at the lake this morning.
The whooper swan is easily recognized by its loud, trumpeting calls and yellow-and-black bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpeter swan - a large North American swan with a trumpet-like call
