mole-eyed scrutiny

1. NOUN

/ moʊl aɪd skruˈtɪni / (AmE), / məʊl aɪd skruːˈtɪni / (BrE)
An intense and unwavering critical examination, especially one that is sharp and searching.
intense, searching examination

Examples

Under her mole-eyed scrutiny, even the smallest errors could not escape notice.
The manuscript underwent mole-eyed scrutiny before it was finally approved for publication.
His mole-eyed scrutiny of the accounts revealed several discrepancies others had missed.

Synset Examples

Her mole-eyed scrutiny caught every mistake in the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrutiny - careful and thorough examination

Etymology

Formed from 'mole-eyed' (having the sharp, searching gaze of a mole) and 'scrutiny'.
