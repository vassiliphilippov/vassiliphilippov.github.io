pure iambic trimeter

1. NOUN

/ pjʊr aɪˈæmbɪk ˈtrɪmɪtɚ / (AmE), / pjʊə aɪˈæmbɪk ˈtrɪmɪtə / (BrE)
An iambic verse line of six feet in which all variable syllables (ancipitia) are short and no substitutions are allowed.
strict six-foot iambic verse form

Examples

The poet experimented with pure iambic trimeter to achieve a highly regular rhythm in his verses.
In classical poetry, pure iambic trimeter is rarely maintained due to the constraints it imposes on word choice.
Scholars debate whether any extant lines from Catullus satisfy the conditions of pure iambic trimeter.

Synset Examples

A pure iambic trimeter is an extremely rare verse, but can be found in the poetry of Catullus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: iambic trimeter - a line of verse consisting of six iambic feet, sometimes with metrical substitutions

Etymology

From Latin iambicus trimeter, referring to the metrical form; 'pure' indicates the strictness in foot composition.
