aqua regia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑkwə ˈriːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌakwə ˈriːdʒɪə / (BrE)
a highly corrosive mixture of nitric acid and hydrochloric acid, able to dissolve noble metals such as gold and platinum
mixture of acids that dissolves gold

Examples

When the necklace was tested in aqua regia, it confirmed the purity of the gold.
Chemists use aqua regia to clean laboratory glassware contaminated with metals.
Because aqua regia can dissolve platinum, it is stored with great caution.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning ‘royal water’.
