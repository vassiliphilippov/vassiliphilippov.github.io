Concord grape

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.kɔɹd ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kɔːd ɡreɪp / (BrE)
A variety of dark purple grape native to the northeastern United States, commonly used for eating, juice, and jelly.
a dark purple grape variety from the US

Examples

We planted a Concord grape vine in our backyard for homemade jelly.
The flavor of Concord grape juice is rich and sweet.
Farmers in New York grow thousands of pounds of Concord grape each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table grape - a grape variety grown for eating fresh

Etymology

Named after Concord, Massachusetts, where the variety was first cultivated in the 19th century.
