to start without me

1. VERB

/ stɑrt wɪˈðaʊt mi / (AmE), / stɑːt wɪˈðaʊt miː / (BrE)
used to tell others to begin an activity or event before you arrive or without your presence
tell others to begin without you

Examples

If I'm running late, just start without me and I'll catch up.
The meeting was scheduled for 9 a.m., so they had to start without me.
Don't wait around—if the movie begins, start without me.

Synset Examples

Just remember, if I'm not there, start without me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go ahead - proceed before someone else
