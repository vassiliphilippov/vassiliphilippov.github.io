Damascus steel

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmæskəs stil / (AmE), / dəˈmæskəs stiːl / (BrE)
A hard and resilient type of steel, often featuring distinctive patterns, historically used to make sword blades and renowned for its strength and sharpness.
patterned resilient steel for blades

Examples

The legendary swords made from Damascus steel were prized for their strength and cutting edge.
Collectors value antique Damascus steel blades for their unique wavy patterns.
Modern blacksmiths still attempt to recreate Damascus steel using similar forging techniques.

Etymology

Named after Damascus, the capital of Syria, where such steel and weapons were believed to have been produced or traded.
