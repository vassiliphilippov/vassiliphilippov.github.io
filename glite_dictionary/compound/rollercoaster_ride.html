rollercoaster ride

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlərˌkoʊstər raɪd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊləˌkəʊstə raɪd / (BrE)
an experience or period marked by many sudden or extreme changes, especially in emotions or fortunes
a period with many ups and downs

Examples

Last year was a real rollercoaster ride for the company, with profits soaring then plummeting several times.
Her emotions went on a rollercoaster ride during the final weeks before the exam results came out.
The negotiations proved to be a rollercoaster ride from start to finish.

Synset Examples

The stock market has been quite roller-coasterish this year.
His emotions were roller-coasterish during the job interview.
The project was a rollercoaster ride from start to finish.
Her career has been a rollercoaster ride since graduation.
That sports game felt like a rollercoaster ride!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: up and down - experiencing frequent or abrupt changes in situation or emotion

Etymology

From the literal use of 'roller coaster' (an amusement ride); figurative usage from late 20th century.
