wheel bug

1. NOUN

/ wiːl bʌɡ / (AmE), / wiːl bʌɡ / (BrE)
a large predatory North American assassin bug (Arilus cristatus) with a distinctive cogwheel-shaped thoracic crest, known for feeding on other insects
a large North American predatory bug with a cogwheel-shaped crest

Examples

The wheel bug is easily identified by the spiny wheel-shaped structure on its back.
Gardeners value the wheel bug for its appetite for harmful pests.
You should avoid handling a wheel bug, as its bite can be quite painful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin bug - a predatory bug of the family Reduviidae

Etymology

Named for the cogwheel-like crest on its thorax.
