open vowel

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
a vowel sound pronounced with the tongue positioned low in the mouth, leaving the mouth relatively open
vowel pronounced with tongue low in the mouth

Examples

The Spanish language contains several open vowels that give it a distinctive sound.
In phonetics class, we learned to distinguish between close and open vowels.
The pronunciation chart clearly marked each open vowel with a specific symbol.

Synset Examples

There is an addictive musicality to the language throughout the collection, with its combination of consonant-heavy Germanic monosyllables and the open vowels of Spanish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: close vowel - a vowel sound pronounced with the tongue positioned high in the mouth
