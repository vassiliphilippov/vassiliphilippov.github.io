bass voice

1. NOUN

/ beɪs vɔɪs / (AmE), / beɪs vɔɪs / (BrE)
the lowest adult male singing voice, typically with a deep and resonant tone
lowest male singing voice

Examples

He was chosen for the choir because of his rich bass voice.
A strong bass voice can add depth to any musical performance.
The solo featured a singer with an impressively deep bass voice.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: baritone - a male singing voice between bass and tenor
  • Degree Higher: tenor - the highest adult male singing voice

Etymology

From Italian 'basso' (low) and English 'voice'.
