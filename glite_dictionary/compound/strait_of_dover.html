Strait of Dover

1. PROPN

/ streɪt əv ˈdoʊvɚ / (AmE), / streɪt əv ˈdəʊvə / (BrE)
the narrow strait separating southeastern England from northern France, connecting the English Channel with the North Sea
narrow strait between England and France

Examples

The Strait of Dover is the narrowest part of the English Channel.
Many ships pass through the Strait of Dover every day en route to Europe.
During World War II, the Strait of Dover played a key role in military defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English Channel - the sea between southern England and northern France
  • Synonym: North Sea - the sea bordering northeastern England and northwestern Europe
