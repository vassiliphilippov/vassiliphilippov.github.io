Prairie State

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛr.i steɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɛː.ri steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of Illinois in the United States, referring to its prairie land
nickname for Illinois, USA

Examples

Illinois is often called the Prairie State due to its rich farmland and rolling grasslands.
The Prairie State is home to Chicago, one of the largest cities in the U.S.
Many settlers were attracted to the Prairie State for its fertile soil and open spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Illinois - a state in the midwestern United States

Etymology

Named for the extensive prairies that once covered much of Illinois.
