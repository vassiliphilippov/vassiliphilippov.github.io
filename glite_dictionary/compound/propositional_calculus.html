propositional calculus

1. NOUN

/ prəˌpɑzɪʃənl ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / prəˌpɒzɪʃənl ˈkælkjʊləs / (BrE)
a branch of symbolic logic that studies propositions and their logical relationships using connectives such as and, or, and not
branch of logic studying propositions and connectives
medical

Examples

The propositional calculus is fundamental in introductory courses on logic.
She studied inference rules in propositional calculus for her computer science degree.
Every valid argument in propositional calculus can be represented using logical connectives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predicate calculus - logical system extending propositional calculus with quantifiers and predicates

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio' (proposal, assertion) and 'calculus' (small stone, reckoning).
