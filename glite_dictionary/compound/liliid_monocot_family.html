liliid monocot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliˌɪd ˈmɑnəˌkɑt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈlɪlɪɪd ˈmɒnəkɒt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of monocotyledonous flowering plants belonging to the subclass Liliidae, typically herbaceous, with petal-like sepals and petals and compound pistils.
plant family in subclass Liliidae
medical

Examples

Members of the liliid monocot family are known for their showy flowers and herbaceous stems.
The tulip belongs to a well-known liliid monocot family found throughout temperate regions.
Botanists often study the diversity within the liliid monocot family for classification purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monocotyledon - a plant having a single cotyledon, or embryonic leaf

Etymology

From 'Liliidae', a botanical subclass, and 'monocot', short for monocotyledon, referring to plants with a single embryonic leaf.
