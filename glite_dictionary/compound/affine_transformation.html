affine transformation

1. NOUN

/ əˈfaɪn ˌtræns.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈfaɪn ˌtræns.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(mathematics) a transformation that preserves points, straight lines, and planes, and is a combination of operations such as translation, rotation, scaling, or reflection.
mathematical combination of linear transformations

Examples

The image was altered using an affine transformation to rotate and scale it.
In computer graphics, affine transformations are frequently applied to manipulate objects on the screen.
You can achieve complicated effects by composing several affine transformations in sequence.

Etymology

From French "affine", from Latin "affinis" (connected with), used in mathematical sense since the 19th century.
