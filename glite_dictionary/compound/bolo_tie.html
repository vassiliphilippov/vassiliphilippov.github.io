bolo tie

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊloʊ taɪ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊləʊ taɪ / (BrE)
A cord worn around the neck and fastened with an ornamental clasp, used as a necktie, especially in Western American fashion.
cord necktie with ornamental clasp

Examples

He wore a silver bolo tie to the formal dinner, giving his outfit a Western flair.
The bolo tie is especially popular in states like Arizona and New Mexico.
Her grandfather collected bolo ties with turquoise stones and intricate metalwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necktie - A piece of fabric worn around the neck under a collar and tied in front

Etymology

From American Spanish 'bola' (ball) referring to the clasp, plus English 'tie'.
