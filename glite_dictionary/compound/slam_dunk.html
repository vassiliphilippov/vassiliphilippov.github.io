slam dunk

1. NOUN

/ slæm dʌŋk / (AmE), / slæm dʌŋk / (BrE)
an action or outcome that is certain to succeed or occur; a foregone conclusion
something certain to succeed

Examples

Winning the championship was a slam dunk for the dominant team this year.
Everyone thought her promotion was a slam dunk after the project's success.
That proposal passing the committee is a slam dunk; no one opposes it.

Synset Examples

predicting his success was a slam dunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - something certain to happen
  • Antonym: long shot - something unlikely to succeed

Etymology

Originally from basketball, where a 'slam dunk' is a forceful shot made by jumping and slamming the ball through the hoop.

2. NOUN

/ slæm dʌŋk / (AmE), / slæm dʌŋk / (BrE)
A forceful shot in basketball in which a player jumps and scores by putting the ball directly through the basket with one or both hands.
forceful basketball shot

Examples

LeBron James thrilled the crowd with a spectacular slam dunk in the final seconds of the game.
The highlight reel featured his powerful slam dunk over two defenders.
After executing a perfect slam dunk, she celebrated with her teammates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunk - To score in basketball by thrusting the ball down into the basket with the hand(s) above the rim

Etymology

From 'slam,' meaning to strike forcefully, and 'dunk,' meaning to thrust or dip, originally in American English sporting slang.

3. NOUN

/ slæm dʌŋk / (AmE), / slæm dʌŋk / (BrE)
an action or achievement that is impressively successful or certain to succeed; an easy or guaranteed success
certain or impressive success
informal

Examples

The merger was expected to be a slam dunk for both companies.
Everyone thought the case was a slam dunk until new evidence surfaced.
Winning the championship was no slam dunk, but the team made it look easy.

Synset Examples

That presentation was a slam dunk for the CEO.
His idea was a slam dunk for the marketing team.
Getting that contract was a slam dunk for the firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sure thing - something that is certain to happen or succeed
  • Antonym: long shot - something with little chance of success

Etymology

From the basketball term 'slam dunk', first recorded in the 1970s, meaning a forceful shot where the ball is thrust down through the hoop.
