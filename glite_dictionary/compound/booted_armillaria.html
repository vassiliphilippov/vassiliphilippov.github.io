booted armillaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtɪd ɑːrˌmɪləriə / (AmE), / ˈbuːtɪd ɑːˌmɪləriə / (BrE)
a species of fungus with a brown cap, white gills, and a membranous ring halfway up the stalk, found in woodland habitats
a woodland fungus with brown cap and ringed stalk

Examples

The booted armillaria is easily recognized by the ring on its stalk.
Foragers often spot the booted armillaria growing at the base of deciduous trees.
The brown cap and white gills are key features of the booted armillaria species.
