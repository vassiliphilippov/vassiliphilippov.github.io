ivory-billed woodpecker

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvəri ˌbɪld ˈwʊdˌpɛkɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri ˌbɪld ˈwʊdˌpɛkə / (BrE)
a large black-and-white woodpecker native to the southern United States and Cuba, noted for its ivory-colored bill; believed to be critically endangered or possibly extinct
large endangered woodpecker with ivory bill

Examples

The ivory-billed woodpecker was once a common sight in the swamps of the southeastern United States.
Despite extensive searches, the ivory-billed woodpecker has not been conclusively sighted in decades.
Conservationists are working to protect any remaining habitat for the ivory-billed woodpecker.
