sailor's breastplate

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlərz ˈbrɛstˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈseɪləz ˈbrɛstˌpleɪt / (BrE)
a knot tied in a rope that is used for dragging a gun carriage, especially in naval contexts
knot used for dragging a gun carriage

Examples

The sailor's breastplate was carefully tied before the men began hauling the cannon across the deck.
Old naval manuals describe the sailor's breastplate as essential for moving heavy artillery.
He demonstrated how to make a sailor's breastplate during the reenactment of the ship's preparations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - an interlacement of the parts of one or more flexible bodies, forming a lump or knob
