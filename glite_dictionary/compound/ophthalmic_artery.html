ophthalmic artery

1. NOUN

/ ɑfˈθælmɪk ˈɑr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ɒfˈθæl.mɪk ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
a branch of the internal carotid artery that supplies blood to the eye and nearby structures
artery supplying the eye and surroundings
medical

Examples

The ophthalmic artery is crucial for delivering oxygenated blood to the eye.
Blockage of the ophthalmic artery may result in sudden vision loss.
The surgeon carefully identified the ophthalmic artery during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: central retinal artery - artery that branches from ophthalmic artery to supply retina

Etymology

From Greek 'ophthalmos' (eye) and Latin 'arteria' (artery).
