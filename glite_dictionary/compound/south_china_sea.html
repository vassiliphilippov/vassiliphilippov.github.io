South China Sea

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ ˈtʃaɪ.nə si / (AmE), / saʊθ ˈtʃaɪ.nə siː / (BrE)
a marginal sea of the western Pacific Ocean located near southeastern Asia, bordered by China, Vietnam, the Philippines, Malaysia, and other countries, and known for frequent typhoons.
marginal sea of the western Pacific, southeast Asia

Examples

Many trade routes pass through the South China Sea, making it an important area for global shipping.
Territorial disputes in the South China Sea have attracted international attention in recent years.
Fishermen often face dangerous weather when working in the South China Sea during typhoon season.
