Shasta language

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæstə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːstə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Native American language historically spoken by the Shasta people of northern California and southern Oregon
language of the Shasta people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Shasta language to learn more about the region's history.
Few fluent speakers of the Shasta language remained by the early 20th century.
Local museums offer audio recordings in the Shasta language as part of their exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shastan languages - a family of related languages including Shasta and other closely related tongues

Etymology

Named after the Shasta people, indigenous to northern California and southern Oregon.
