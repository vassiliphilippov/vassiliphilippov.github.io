guide plate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪd pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪd pleɪt / (BrE)
a metal device with an elongated slot, used in climbing to control a rope, allowing a carabiner to lock off the device when pulled.
climbing device for rope control

Examples

The climber threaded the rope through the guide plate before beginning the ascent.
Many instructors recommend using a guide plate for added safety during belaying.
Always make sure the carabiner is securely attached to the guide plate before allowing anyone to climb.
