family Trichechidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli trɪˈkiːkɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli trɪˈkiːkɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic mammals comprising only the manatees
the animal family containing the manatees

Examples

The family Trichechidae includes all living species of manatees.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting members of the family Trichechidae from habitat loss.
Scientists study the evolution of the family Trichechidae to better understand aquatic mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - a large aquatic mammal found in warm coastal waters

Etymology

From modern Latin Trichechidae, based on Trichechus, genus name for manatees, from Greek trikhēchos (hairy-lipped).
