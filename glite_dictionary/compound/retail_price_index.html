retail price index

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːteɪl ˈpraɪs ˌɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪl ˈpraɪs ˌɪndɛks / (BrE)
an official measure of the average change in prices of goods and services bought by consumers over time
measure of changes in consumer prices

Examples

The government uses the retail price index to adjust pensions for inflation.
If the retail price index rises sharply, the cost of living typically increases.
Wage negotiations are often linked to changes in the retail price index.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer price index - official measure of changes in the price level of a basket of consumer goods
