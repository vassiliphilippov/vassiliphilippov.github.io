genus Bubulcus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs bjuːˈbʌl.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bjuːˈbʌl.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of small white egrets, commonly known as cattle egrets, found in warm regions worldwide.
genus of small white egrets

Examples

The genus Bubulcus includes birds commonly seen following livestock in fields.
Researchers observed a large group belonging to the genus Bubulcus near the wetland reserve.
Unlike other egrets, the genus Bubulcus often feeds alongside cattle and horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle egret - a common name for members of genus Bubulcus

Etymology

From Latin 'bubulcus' meaning 'herdsman' or 'cowherd', referring to the bird's association with cattle.
