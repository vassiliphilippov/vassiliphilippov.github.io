bulletproof plan

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.ɪtˌpruf plæn / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.ɪtˌpruːf plan / (BrE)
a plan that is extremely secure and unlikely to fail or be affected by problems
plan that cannot fail

Examples

The team worked together to come up with a bulletproof plan for the upcoming merger.
She believed her bulletproof plan would impress the board of directors.
Despite their bulletproof plan, an unexpected error caused a delay.

Synset Examples

He has a bulletproof plan for the project launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolproof plan - a plan that cannot go wrong or fail
  • Antonym: risky plan - a plan that has a significant chance of failing

Etymology

From 'bulletproof', originally describing materials impervious to bullets, used figuratively to mean 'protected against failure'.
