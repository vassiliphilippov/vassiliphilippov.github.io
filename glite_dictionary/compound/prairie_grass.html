prairie grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A hardy, attractive North American grass, whose leaves turn pale bronze in autumn, commonly found in prairie regions.
hardy North American grass with bronze autumn foliage

Examples

The prairie grass turned a beautiful bronze color as autumn set in.
Restoration projects often use prairie grass to restore native habitats.
A field of prairie grass swayed gently in the breeze under the summer sky.
