cat's cradle

1. NOUN

/ kæts ˈkreɪdəl / (AmE), / kæts ˈkreɪdəl / (BrE)
a game in which a loop of string is twisted and passed between players' fingers to form various shapes
string game using fingers to make shapes

Examples

Before electronics, children often played cat's cradle in the schoolyard during recess.
She showed her younger brother how to loop the string for cat's cradle.
The old photograph captured two friends playing cat's cradle on a sunny afternoon.

2. NOUN

/ kæts ˈkreɪdəl / (AmE), / kæts ˈkreɪdəl / (BrE)
A children's game in which a loop of string is passed back and forth between players' hands to form intricate designs; also, a complicated or tangled situation.
a string game or a tangled situation

Examples

She showed her nephew how to weave a cat's cradle out of a piece of string.
After moving the computer, the cables underneath the desk looked like a cat's cradle.
Negotiating the rules turned into a bureaucratic cat's cradle that was hard to unravel.

Synset Examples

She taught the kids how to make a cat's cradle with string.
The yarn was tangled like a cat's cradle after the kids played.
After hours of work, the wires were a cat's cradle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangle - a confused or complicated mass
  • Synonym: maze - a complex network of paths or passages

Etymology

Alludes to the cradle shape formed by the string in the game, first recorded in the 18th century.
