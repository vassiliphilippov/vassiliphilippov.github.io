diesel hammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːzəl ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdiːzəl ˈhamə / (BrE)
A heavy machine powered by a two-stroke diesel engine, used for driving piles into the ground during construction work.
pile-driving machine with diesel engine

Examples

The construction crew used a diesel hammer to drive the steel piles deep into the riverbed.
The loud noise from the diesel hammer could be heard across the entire site.
Contractors often choose a diesel hammer for large-scale foundation projects because of its efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pile driver - a machine used to drive piles into soil to provide foundation support
