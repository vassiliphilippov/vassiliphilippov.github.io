polecat weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊl.kæt wiːd / (AmE), / ˈpəʊl.kæt wiːd / (BrE)
A deciduous, perennial, low-growing swamp plant of eastern North America, known for its fetid smell and minute flowers encased in a mottled greenish or purple cowl-shaped spathe.
fetid swamp plant of eastern North America

Examples

Many hikers complain about the pungent odor of the polecat weed in the wetlands each spring.
The polecat weed thrives in soggy, shaded areas and is easy to identify by its mottled spathe.
Researchers studied the unique pollination methods of polecat weed in eastern North American swamps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - another common name for polecat weed, a fetid swamp plant

Etymology

The name combines 'polecat' (an animal known for its strong smell) and 'weed' (a wild plant), referring to the plant's pungent odor.
