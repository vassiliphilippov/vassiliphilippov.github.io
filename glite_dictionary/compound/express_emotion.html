to express emotion

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsprɛs ɪˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛs ɪˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
to show or communicate one's feelings, especially through words, actions, or facial expressions
communicate one's feelings

Examples

It's important for children to express emotion when they're upset, rather than keeping it inside.
Some people find it hard to express emotion in front of others, preferring to remain stoic.
Actors must be able to express emotion convincingly to portray their characters well.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repress emotion - to hold back or restrain the expression of one's feelings
