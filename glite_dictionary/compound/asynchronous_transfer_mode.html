asynchronous transfer mode

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪŋkrənəs ˈtrænsfɚ moʊd / (AmE), / əˈsɪŋkrənəs ˈtrɑːnsfə məʊd / (BrE)
a digital communication method that transmits data in small fixed-sized packets at high speeds, suitable for images, voice, video, and data
high-speed digital communication method

Examples

Many companies upgraded their networks to asynchronous transfer mode to support high-speed data and video conferencing.
Asynchronous transfer mode allows different types of media, such as voice and video, to be transmitted together efficiently.
The university's new backbone network uses asynchronous transfer mode for reliable and fast communications.

Synset Examples

ATM is used for both LAN and WAN

Etymology

From 'asynchronous' (not occurring at the same time) + 'transfer mode' (a method of transmitting data); technical term from telecommunications.
