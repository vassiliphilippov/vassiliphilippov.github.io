black lotion

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈloʊʃən / (AmE), / blak ˈləʊʃən / (BrE)
a topical preparation of calomel and limewater formerly used to treat syphilitic sores and other skin conditions
medical lotion with calomel and limewater
medical archaic

Examples

Doctors once prescribed black lotion to treat skin ulcers caused by syphilis.
The patient was instructed to apply black lotion to the affected area twice daily.
Owing to its mercury content, black lotion fell out of favor as safer treatments became available.
