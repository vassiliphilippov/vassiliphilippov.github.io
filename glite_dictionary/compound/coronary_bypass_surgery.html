coronary bypass surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəˌnɛri ˈbaɪˌpæs ˈsɝdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri ˈbaɪpɑːs ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which a blocked coronary artery is bypassed using a grafted blood vessel to restore normal blood flow to the heart.
surgery to bypass a blocked coronary artery
medical

Examples

After his heart attack, the doctor recommended coronary bypass surgery to improve blood flow.
She recovered well after undergoing coronary bypass surgery last year.
Advancements in coronary bypass surgery have greatly increased survival rates for heart patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass surgery - surgery to create a new passage for blood flow, especially to avoid a blocked vessel
