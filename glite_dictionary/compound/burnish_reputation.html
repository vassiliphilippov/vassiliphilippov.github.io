to burnish reputation

1. VERB

/ ˈbɝːnɪʃ ˌrɛpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪʃ ˌrɛpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
to improve or enhance one's public image or status, especially after it has been damaged
improve or enhance public image

Examples

The CEO worked hard to burnish her reputation after the financial misstep.
Hiring a new PR team was part of his strategy to burnish his reputation in the industry.
The foundation's charitable work helped burnish its reputation among donors and the public.

Synset Examples

She tried to burnish her reputation after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restore reputation - to bring one's reputation back to a former state of respect
  • Antonym: to tarnish reputation - to damage or harm one's public image

Etymology

From 'burnish' (to polish) and 'reputation'; 'burnish' from Old French 'burnir', related to shining or polishing.
