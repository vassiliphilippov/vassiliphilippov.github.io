modal auxiliary verb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.dl ɔɡˈzɪl.iˌɛr.i vɜrb / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dl ɔːɡˈzɪl.i.əri vɜːb / (BrE)
an auxiliary verb used to express possibility, intention, necessity, or ability, such as 'can', 'may', 'must', or 'will'
auxiliary verb expressing modality (e.g. can, must)

Examples

The word 'might' is a common modal auxiliary verb used to express possibility.
Students often struggle to choose the correct modal auxiliary verb for polite requests.
In English, every modal auxiliary verb has unique usage rules depending on the context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary verb - a verb used in forming tenses, moods, and voices of other verbs

Etymology

From Latin 'modus' (mode, manner) and 'auxiliarius' (helping).
