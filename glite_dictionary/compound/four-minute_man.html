four-minute man

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr ˌmɪnɪt ˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈfɔː ˌmɪnɪt ˌmæn / (BrE)
an athlete who has run a mile in less than four minutes
athlete who ran mile in under 4 minutes

Examples

After years of training, he finally became a four-minute man at the national championships.
Breaking the barrier to become a four-minute man is still a celebrated achievement in athletics.
Roger Bannister was the first person recognized as a four-minute man in 1954.
