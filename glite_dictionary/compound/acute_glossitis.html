acute glossitis

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːt ɡlɑˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ɡlɒˈsʌɪtɪs / (BrE)
Glossitis that appears suddenly, usually due to injury or infection, and is characterized by painful swelling of the tongue.
sudden painful swelling of the tongue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with acute glossitis after experiencing severe tongue pain and swelling.
Infections and burns are common causes of acute glossitis.
Treatment for acute glossitis often focuses on relieving pain and addressing the underlying cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossitis - inflammation of the tongue

Etymology

From Latin 'acutus' (sharp, sudden) and Greek 'glōssa' (tongue) + '-itis' (inflammation).
