cellular concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljələr ˈkɑnˌkrit / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə ˈkɒŋkriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight type of concrete formed by the inclusion of air cells, often by adding air-entraining agents or lightweight aggregates such as expanded clay or vermiculite
lightweight, aerated building concrete

Examples

The new extension was built using cellular concrete to improve insulation and reduce weight.
Innovations in cellular concrete have led to more energy-efficient construction methods.
Unlike traditional concrete, cellular concrete can be easily cut and shaped on site.

Synset Examples

Do not confuse cellular concrete with aerated autoclaved concrete, which is manufactured off-site using an entirely different method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerated concrete - concrete that contains entrained air for lightness or insulation
