Old World hop hornbeam

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld hɑp ˈhɔrnˌbim / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld hɒp ˈhɔːnˌbiːm / (BrE)
a medium-sized deciduous tree (Ostrya carpinifolia) native to southern Europe and Asia Minor, valued for its hard wood and ornamental use.
tree native to southern Europe and Asia Minor

Examples

The Old World hop hornbeam is commonly found in forests across southern Europe.
Gardeners appreciate the Old World hop hornbeam for its attractive bark and durable wood.
The leaves of the Old World hop hornbeam turn yellow in the autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop hornbeam - any tree of the genus Ostrya with catkin-like fruit clusters
