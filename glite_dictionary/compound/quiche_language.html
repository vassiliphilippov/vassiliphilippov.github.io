Quiche language

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈʃeɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kiːˈʃeɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Mayan language spoken by the Quiche people of Guatemala.
Mayan language of the Quiche people

Examples

Many children in rural Guatemala learn both Spanish and Quiche language at school.
The Quiche language has a rich tradition of oral storytelling and poetry.
Linguists have documented several dialects within the Quiche language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mayan language - a language belonging to the Mayan language family

Etymology

From Spanish 'quiché', derived from the indigenous name for the people and language.
