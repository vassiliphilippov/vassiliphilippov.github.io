order Oedogoniales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrdər iːˌdoʊɡoʊˈnaɪəliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌiːdəʊˌɡəʊnɪˈɑːliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of simple or branched filamentous freshwater green algae within the class Chlorophyceae.
a group of freshwater green algae

Examples

The order Oedogoniales is characterized by simple or branched filamentous green algae found in freshwater habitats.
Studies of the order Oedogoniales have contributed to our knowledge of algal reproduction and cell division.
Algae belonging to the order Oedogoniales are often abundant in ponds during the spring.

Etymology

From the genus Oedogonium, with '-ales' denoting order in botanical Latin.
