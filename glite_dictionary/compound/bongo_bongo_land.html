Bongo Bongo Land

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑŋɡoʊ ˈbɑŋɡoʊ lænd / (AmE), / ˈbɒŋɡəʊ ˈbɒŋɡəʊ lænd / (BrE)
A fictional or imaginary place, often implied to be chaotic, absurd, or remote; sometimes used pejoratively to describe an outlandish or uncivilized location.
imaginary chaotic or remote place

Examples

After getting lost in the jungle, the explorers joked they had stumbled into Bongo Bongo Land.
The committee dismissed the unusual proposal as something more suited to Bongo Bongo Land than reality.
When bureaucracy piled up, she felt like she was living in Bongo Bongo Land.

Synset Examples

In her mind, the deserted island was like Bongo Bongo Land.
He described the remote village as Bongo Bongo Land.
They felt lost in Bongo Bongo Land during the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: never-never land - an imaginary and ideal place
  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect place or state
  • Antonym: dystopia - an imagined society marked by chaos or dysfunction

Etymology

Formed from reduplicated nonsense syllables, imitating African place names; coined in mid-20th century as an invented location.
