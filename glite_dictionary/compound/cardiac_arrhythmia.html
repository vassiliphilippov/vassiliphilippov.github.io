cardiac arrhythmia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.di.æk əˈrɪð.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.di.æk əˈrɪθ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A condition in which the heart beats with an irregular or abnormal rhythm.
abnormal heart rhythm
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with a cardiac arrhythmia after experiencing episodes of dizziness.
A cardiac arrhythmia can sometimes require immediate medical attention to prevent complications.
The doctor explained that stress can contribute to the development of a cardiac arrhythmia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrhythmia - irregularity in the rhythm of the heartbeat

Etymology

From Greek 'kardia' (heart) and 'arrhythmia' (lack of rhythm).
