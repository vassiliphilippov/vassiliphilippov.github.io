Uniate Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːniˌeɪt tʃɜːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪeɪt tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
Any of various Eastern Christian churches, especially in Eastern Europe or the Middle East, that recognize the authority of the Pope while retaining their own liturgical practices.
Eastern Christian church under papal authority

Examples

The Uniate Church maintains Eastern liturgy but recognizes the Pope's spiritual authority.
Many members of the Uniate Church live in Ukraine and other parts of Eastern Europe.
After the union with Rome, the Uniate Church preserved its own customs and traditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'uni' (one) + '-ate', meaning 'brought into unity'; originally referring to churches united with Rome.
