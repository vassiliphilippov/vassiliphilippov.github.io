cow-nosed ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊ ˌnoʊzd ˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ ˌnəʊzd ˈreɪ / (BrE)
a large species of ray found along the eastern coast of North America, characterized by a distinctly rounded head resembling a cow's nose
large ray with rounded head, North America

Examples

A group of cow-nosed rays glided gracefully through the shallow waters near the shore.
The cow-nosed ray is easily identified by its broad, rounded head.
Fishermen along the Atlantic coast often spot cow-nosed rays migrating in large schools during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a large, flat marine fish with wing-like fins
