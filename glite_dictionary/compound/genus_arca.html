genus Arca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɑːrkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɑːkə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Arcidae, comprising ark shells and blood clams.
genus of ark shells and blood clams

Examples

The genus Arca includes several species of ark shells found in warm seas.
Scientists have recently described a new member of the genus Arca from the Indo-Pacific region.
The genus Arca is notable for its distinctively ridged shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arcidae - the family of marine bivalve mollusks including ark shells

Etymology

From Latin 'arca' (a chest, box), used as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.
