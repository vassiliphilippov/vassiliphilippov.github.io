broad jump

1. NOUN

/ brɔd dʒʌmp / (AmE), / brɔːd dʒʌmp / (BrE)
a track and field event in which competitors jump as far as possible from a running start
track and field jumping event

Examples

He won the broad jump with a remarkable leap of over seven meters.
In high school, she competed in both the broad jump and the 100-meter dash.
The broad jump requires speed, strength, and precise timing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long jump - track and field event of jumping as far as possible from a running start

2. NOUN

/ brɔd dʒʌmp / (AmE), / brɔːd dʒʌmp / (BrE)
an athletic event in which a person jumps as far as possible from a running start; the long jump
athletic jump from a running start

Examples

He set a new record in the broad jump at last year's championship.
Coaches often use the broad jump to measure explosive power in athletes.
She practiced her broad jump technique every morning before school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long jump - athletic event of jumping for distance from a running start
