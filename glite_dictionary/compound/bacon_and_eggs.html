bacon and eggs

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkən ənd ˈɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkən ənd ˈɛɡz / (BrE)
a European plant with claw-shaped pods, introduced in America, commonly known as 'bacon and eggs' due to the color of its flowers
European plant with claw-shaped pods

Examples

The wildflowers included bacon and eggs, brightening the field with their yellow and orange blossoms.
Gardeners often plant bacon and eggs because it attracts native bees.
We spotted clusters of bacon and eggs growing along the countryside lane.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkən ənd ˈɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkən ənd ˈeɡz / (BrE)
A dish consisting of eggs (fried or scrambled) served with bacon, commonly eaten for breakfast.
dish of eggs with bacon

Examples

On Sunday mornings, we often make bacon and eggs for breakfast.
He prefers his bacon and eggs with toast and orange juice.
The café serves traditional bacon and eggs all day.
