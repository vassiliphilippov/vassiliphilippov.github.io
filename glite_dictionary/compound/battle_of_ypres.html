battle of Ypres

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈiːprə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈiːprə / (BrE)
a series of major battles during World War I, fought around the town of Ypres in Belgium, notably including an Allied offensive in 1917 where tanks bogged down in waterlogged soil and Germany used mustard gas for the first time
World War I battles near Ypres, Belgium

Examples

The battle of Ypres marked a significant use of chemical weapons during World War I.
Many soldiers suffered due to harsh conditions at the battle of Ypres in 1917.
Commemorative events are held annually to honor those who fought in the battle of Ypres.

Etymology

Ypres is a town in Belgium; the battle is named after its location.
