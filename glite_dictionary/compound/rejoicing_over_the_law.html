Rejoicing over the Law

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ oʊvər ðə lɔ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ əʊvə ðə lɔː / (BrE)
A Jewish holy day, also known as Simchat Torah, celebrated on the 22nd or 23rd of Tishri to mark the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings.
Jewish festival marking completion of the Torah reading cycle

Examples

Many Jewish communities gather for Rejoicing over the Law with singing and dancing.
During Rejoicing over the Law, the Torah scrolls are paraded around the synagogue.
Children often participate in Rejoicing over the Law celebrations with special flags and sweets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Simchat Torah - Jewish festival celebrating the completion of the annual Torah reading cycle
