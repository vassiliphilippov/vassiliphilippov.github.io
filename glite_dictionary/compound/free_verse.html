free verse

1. NOUN

/ fri ˈvɜrs / (AmE), / fri ˈvɜːs / (BrE)
Poetry that does not use regular rhyme or a consistent metrical pattern.
unrhymed, unmetered poetry

Examples

Modern poets often write in free verse to express ideas more freely.
Her collection features both sonnets and free verse poems.
Many classrooms encourage students to explore free verse as a form of creative writing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blank verse - poetry with regular meter but no rhyme
