oceanic abyss

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈʃænɪk əˈbɪs / (AmE), / əʊˈʃanɪk əˈbɪs / (BrE)
a long, deep, steep-sided depression found on the ocean floor, often formed at tectonic plate boundaries
deep ocean floor depression

Examples

Deep-sea exploration has revealed that many unusual species live in the oceanic abyss.
The Mariana Trench is a well-known example of an oceanic abyss.
Scientists study the oceanic abyss to learn more about plate tectonics and marine geology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abyssal trench - a deep, elongated depression on the ocean floor

Etymology

From Latin 'oceanus' (ocean) and Greek 'abyssos' (bottomless, unfathomable).
