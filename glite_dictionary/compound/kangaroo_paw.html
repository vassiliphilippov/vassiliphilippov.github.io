kangaroo paw

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæŋɡəˌruː pɔ / (AmE), / ˌkæŋɡəˌruː pɔː / (BrE)
a perennial Australian plant with long, slender leaves and clusters of tubular flowers covered with woolly hairs, resembling a kangaroo's paw; chiefly of the genus Anigozanthos.
Australian flowering plant with woolly, paw-like flowers

Examples

The vibrant red flowers of the kangaroo paw make it a popular choice for Australian gardens.
Florists often use kangaroo paw in arrangements due to its unusual shape and bright colors.
Wild kangaroo paw plants were blooming along the hiking trail near Perth.

Etymology

Named for the shape of the flower, which resembles a kangaroo's paw; from the Australian marsupial 'kangaroo' and 'paw'.
