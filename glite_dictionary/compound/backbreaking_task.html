backbreaking task

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌbreɪkɪŋ tæsk / (AmE), / ˈbækˌbreɪkɪŋ tɑːsk / (BrE)
a task or job that is extremely difficult and physically exhausting
physically exhausting job or duty

Examples

The workers faced the backbreaking task of rebuilding the bridge after the storm.
Harvesting all the crops before the rain proved to be a backbreaking task.
After hours of shoveling snow, he finally finished the backbreaking task.

Synset Examples

They completed the backbreaking task just in time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous task - a task requiring a lot of effort and endurance
  • Antonym: easy task - a task that is simple and effortless

Etymology

Formed from 'backbreaking' (from back + breaking, figuratively suggesting extreme effort) and 'task'.
