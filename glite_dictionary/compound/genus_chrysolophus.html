genus Chrysolophus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkrɪs.əˈlɒ.fəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkrɪs.əˈlɒ.fəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored pheasants native to East Asia, including the golden pheasant and Lady Amherst's pheasant.
a genus of Asian pheasants

Examples

The genus Chrysolophus includes some of the most colorful pheasants in the world.
Birdwatchers are fascinated by the plumage of birds in the genus Chrysolophus.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the genus Chrysolophus in southwestern China.

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) + *lophos* (crest), referring to the birds' golden crest.
