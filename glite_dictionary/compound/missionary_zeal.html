missionary zeal

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪʃəˌnɛri ˈziːl / (AmE), / ˌmɪʃənəri ˈziːl / (BrE)
fervent, passionate enthusiasm and dedication to a cause, often with a desire to spread or promote it enthusiastically
intense enthusiasm for a cause

Examples

He devoted himself to the project with missionary zeal and convinced others to join him.
Her missionary zeal for environmental issues led her to start a global campaign.
The new manager's missionary zeal inspired significant changes throughout the company.

Synset Examples

He approached his new job with missionary zeal and enthusiasm.
Her missionary zeal inspired others to join the cause.
With missionary zeal, they set out to change the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zeal - great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause

Etymology

From 'missionary', referring to someone undertaking missions, especially religious ones, and 'zeal', from Greek zēlos (ardor, enthusiasm).
