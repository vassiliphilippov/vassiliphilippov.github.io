Principality of Liechtenstein

1. PROPN

/ prɪnˈsɪpəlɪti əv ˌliːktənˈstaɪn / (AmE), / prɪnˈsɪpəlɪti əv ˌlɪçtənˈʃtaɪn / (BrE)
A small landlocked country in central Europe, situated in the Alps between Austria and Switzerland; a constitutional monarchy.
country in central Europe, in the Alps

Examples

The Principality of Liechtenstein is famous for its picturesque Alpine scenery and medieval castles.
Many people are surprised to learn that the Principality of Liechtenstein is one of the world's smallest countries.
The royal family has played a significant role in shaping the history of the Principality of Liechtenstein.

Etymology

Liechtenstein: from the ruling Liechtenstein family, named after Liechtenstein Castle in Austria (German 'Lichtenstein' meaning 'bright stone').
