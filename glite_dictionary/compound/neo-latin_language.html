Neo-Latin language

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊ ˈlætɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ ˈlætɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
Latin as used since the Renaissance period, especially in scientific, academic, and scholarly writings.
Latin used since the Renaissance

Examples

Many scientific terms are derived from Neo-Latin language to ensure international consistency.
Botanical names are often created using the Neo-Latin language for classification.
The rise of the Neo-Latin language marked a revival of Latin in European academia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Latin - the classical language of ancient Rome

Etymology

From 'neo-' (Greek for 'new') + 'Latin', indicating a new form of Latin developed after the Middle Ages.
