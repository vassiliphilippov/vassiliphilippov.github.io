air marshal

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
A person employed by a government and trained in counter-terrorism tactics who travels as a passenger on airline flights to protect against security threats such as hijackings.
undercover airline security officer

Examples

The presence of an air marshal on board made the passengers feel safer.
No one knew which passenger was actually the air marshal until after the incident.
After the threat was detected, the air marshal discreetly took control of the situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sky marshal - an alternative term for air marshal with the same meaning
