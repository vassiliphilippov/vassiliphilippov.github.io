elaeocarpus family

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌliː.oʊˈkɑːr.pəs ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ɪˌleɪ.əʊˈkɑː.pəs ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
A family of trees and shrubs widely distributed in warm regions, some of which yield useful timber; sometimes classified within the family Santalaceae.
a family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The elaeocarpus family includes several species that are important sources of timber in Southeast Asia.
Botanists debated whether the elaeocarpus family should be classified under Santalaceae.
Many members of the elaeocarpus family produce attractive, blue fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elaeocarpus - genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Etymology

From genus name Elaeocarpus, from Greek elaia (olive) + karpos (fruit).
