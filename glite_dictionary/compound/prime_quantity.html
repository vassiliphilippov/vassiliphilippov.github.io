prime quantity

1. NOUN

/ praɪm ˈkwɑntəti / (AmE), / praɪm ˈkwɒntəti / (BrE)
A natural number that has exactly two distinct natural number divisors: 1 and itself; a prime number.
natural number with two divisors (1 and itself)
medical

Examples

The number 7 is a prime quantity because it cannot be divided evenly by any other number except 1 and itself.
Mathematicians have studied prime quantities for centuries to unlock the secrets of number theory.
If a prime quantity appears in the solution, it simplifies the prime factorization process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime number - a number greater than 1 divisible only by 1 and itself
  • Antonym: composite number - a positive integer having more than two positive divisors

Etymology

From Latin 'primus' (first) and 'quantitas' (quantity).
