religious cult

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəs kʌlt / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs kʌlt / (BrE)
a system of religious beliefs and rituals, often centered around a particular figure or doctrine and regarded as distinct from mainstream religion
system of religious beliefs and rituals

Examples

Some anthropologists study the development of religious cults in ancient societies.
The community was cautious of new groups that resembled a religious cult.
Their beliefs and ceremonial practices marked them as a unique religious cult.

Synset Examples

devoted to the cultus of the Blessed Virgin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sect - a group with distinct religious beliefs, often seen as a subdivision of a larger religion
