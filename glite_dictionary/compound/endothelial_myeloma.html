endothelial myeloma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈθi.li.əl maɪˈɛl.oʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈθiː.li.əl maɪˈɛl.ə.mə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor of the bone marrow, usually found in the pelvis or long bones.
type of bone marrow cancer
medical

Examples

The oncologist explained that endothelial myeloma is a rare and aggressive cancer affecting the bone marrow.
Early detection of endothelial myeloma can improve treatment outcomes for patients.
Research into new therapies for endothelial myeloma is ongoing in many medical institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiple myeloma - cancer of plasma cells in the bone marrow

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within), 'thele' (nipple, used for tissue), and 'myelos' (marrow) plus '-oma' (tumor).
