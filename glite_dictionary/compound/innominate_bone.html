innominate bone

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɑmənət boʊn / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒmɪnət bəʊn / (BrE)
the large, flaring bone that forms one half of the pelvis, composed of the ilium, ischium, and pubis
pelvic bone formed of ilium, ischium, and pubis
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the fracture in the innominate bone before proceeding.
A child's innominate bone is not fully fused at birth.
The pelvis consists of two innominate bones, one on each side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvis - the basin-shaped structure of the vertebrate skeleton at the base of the spine

Etymology

From Latin 'innominatus', meaning 'unnamed', referring to the bone's irregular shape.
