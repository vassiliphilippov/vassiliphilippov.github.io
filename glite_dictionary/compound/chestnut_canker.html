chestnut canker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt ˈkæŋ.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.nʌt ˈkaeŋ.kə / (BrE)
a fungal disease that affects American chestnut trees, causing lesions on the bark and often leading to the death of the tree
fungal disease of American chestnut trees

Examples

The spread of chestnut canker devastated forests in the eastern United States.
Researchers are working to develop resistant varieties to combat chestnut canker.
Many historic chestnut trees were lost to chestnut canker in the last century.
