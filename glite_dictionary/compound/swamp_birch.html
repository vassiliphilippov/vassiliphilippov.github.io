swamp birch

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp bɝtʃ / (AmE), / swɒmp bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a birch tree native to the western United States, similar to the paper birch but with brownish bark
a birch tree with brownish bark

Examples

We found a swamp birch by the riverbank during our hike.
The swamp birch is known for its brownish bark, unlike the typical paper birch.
Botanists are studying how the swamp birch adapts to wetland environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paper birch - a birch with white, papery bark
