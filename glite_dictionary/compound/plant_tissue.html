plant tissue

1. NOUN

/ plænt ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / plɑːnt ˈtɪsjuː / (BrE)
any of the distinct types of tissue that make up the structure of a plant, such as xylem, phloem, or epidermis.
tissue forming part of a plant

Examples

Scientists are studying how plant tissue responds to environmental stress.
The leaf is composed of several types of plant tissue, each with a specific function.
Fungi can infect plant tissue and cause disease in crops.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animal tissue - the tissue that forms the organs and other parts of animals
