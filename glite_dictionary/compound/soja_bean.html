soja bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdʒə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdʒə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
An erect bushy annual plant native to Asia, cultivated for its edible seeds rich in oil and protein, commonly known as the soybean.
annual Asian plant, soybean

Examples

The farmer planted soja beans to improve the soil and boost his annual harvest.
Many vegetarian dishes use soja bean products as a primary source of protein.
Research into soja bean genetics has led to more resilient crop varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - another name for soja bean; edible seed or plant

Etymology

From Dutch 'soja', from Japanese 'shōyu' (soy sauce), ultimately from Chinese '酱油' (jiàngyóu, soy sauce).
