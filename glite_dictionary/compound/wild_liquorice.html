wild liquorice

1. NOUN

/ waɪld lɪˈkɒrɪʃ / (AmE), / waɪld lɪˈkɒrɪʃ / (BrE)
a European perennial plant with sweet-tasting roots, resembling liquorice and often found in woodlands and meadows
a European plant with sweet roots

Examples

The woodland was dotted with wild liquorice, its slender stalks swaying in the breeze.
Herbalists sometimes use wild liquorice for its sweet-tasting roots in natural remedies.
While hiking in Europe, she came across a patch of wild liquorice growing in the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquorice - a sweet-tasting root or confection made from it

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (BrE)
A North American plant resembling true licorice, with a root that shares similar sweet-tasting and medicinal properties.
North American plant similar to true licorice

Examples

Many herbal teas include wild liquorice as a natural sweetener.
Early settlers valued wild liquorice roots for their medicinal properties.
You can identify wild liquorice by its purple flowers and spreading roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - A sweet, aromatic root and the plant it comes from, used as flavoring
