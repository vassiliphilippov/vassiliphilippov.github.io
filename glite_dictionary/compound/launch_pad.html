launch pad

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔntʃ ˌpæd / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃ ˌpad / (BrE)
A platform from which rockets or spacecraft are launched into space.
platform for launching rockets or spacecraft

Examples

The rocket stood on the launch pad ready for its historic flight.
Engineers checked every system before moving the spacecraft to the launch pad.
Spectators gathered miles away to watch the liftoff from the launch pad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launching site - location used for launching rockets or missiles
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔntʃ ˌpæd / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃ ˌpæd / (BrE)
A place or situation that serves as a starting point for a project, career, or other undertaking.
starting point for an undertaking

Examples

The internship became a valuable launch pad for her career in journalism.
Their small shop acted as a launch pad for their entry into the tech industry.
Winning the competition was the launch pad he needed to start his own business.

Synset Examples

We used the old warehouse as a launch pad for our new business.
The event served as a launch pad for their careers.
The rocket stood proudly on the launch pad, ready for takeoff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springboard - something that helps launch a project or career

Etymology

Originally referred to a platform from which rockets are launched; now used figuratively as well.
