quadrilabial articulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑːdrɪˈleɪbiəl ˌɑːrtɪˈkjuːleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒdrɪˈleɪbiəl ˌɑːtɪˈkjuːleɪʃən / (BrE)
The articulation of sounds involving four lip movements or using both lips in a specific manner, including certain rare phonemes.
articulation with four lip movements
medical

Examples

The students struggled with the quadrilabial articulation required by some phonetic exercises.
Her dissertation analyzed languages that featured quadrilabial articulation in their sound systems.
Understanding quadrilabial articulation can be helpful for linguistic fieldwork in certain regions.

Synset Examples

Learning quadrilabial articulation was challenging for the students.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bilabial articulation - articulation using both lips

Etymology

From Latin 'quadri-' (four) and 'labium' (lip), plus 'articulation' (movement of speech organs).
