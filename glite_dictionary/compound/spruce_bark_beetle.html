spruce bark beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprus bɑrk ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈspruːs bɑːk ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a small beetle that bores through the bark of spruce trees, feeding on the cambium and often killing the tree
insect pest attacking spruce trees

Examples

The population of spruce bark beetles has increased dramatically due to warmer temperatures.
Infected trees show signs of spruce bark beetle infestation under their bark.
Foresters are trying new strategies to control the spread of the spruce bark beetle.

Synset Examples

the spruce bark beetle is the major tree-killing insect pest of Alaska spruce forests
