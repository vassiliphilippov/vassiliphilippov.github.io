help in kind

1. NOUN

/ hɛlp ɪn kaɪnd / (AmE), / hɛlp ɪn kaɪnd / (BrE)
assistance provided in the same form as assistance previously received, or as goods or services rather than money
assistance in the same form as received

Examples

The charity prefers to provide help in kind such as food and clothing rather than cash donations.
After receiving financial aid, she returned the favor by offering help in kind.
Many organizations rely on help in kind from volunteers, including donated materials and services.

Synset Examples

She offered help in kind after receiving their support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in kind - (of payment or assistance) in goods or services rather than money, or matching what was received
