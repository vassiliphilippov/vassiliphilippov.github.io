schizophrenic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪtsəˈfrɛnɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəˈfrɛnɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a severe mental disorder characterized by distortions of reality, disordered thinking and language, and withdrawal from social interaction
severe mental disorder with distorted reality
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that schizophrenic disorder can profoundly affect a person's perception of reality.
Researchers are studying new treatments for schizophrenic disorder to help manage symptoms more effectively.
Support groups can be helpful for individuals coping with schizophrenic disorder and their families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizophrenia - a specific type of chronic psychotic disorder

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (split) and 'phren' (mind), via 'schizophrenia'.
