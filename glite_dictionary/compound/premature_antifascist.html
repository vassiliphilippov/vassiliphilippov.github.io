premature antifascist

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːməˈtʃʊr ˌæntiˈfæʃɪst / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈtʃɔː ˌæntiˈfæʃɪst / (BrE)
a person who opposed fascism before it was widely recognized as a major threat, especially before government or mainstream consensus
one who opposes fascism before general recognition

Examples

During the 1930s, being labeled a premature antifascist could endanger one's career in certain countries.
Some veterans were later honored for being premature antifascists before their governments recognized the threat of fascism.
The government once regarded him with suspicion, calling him a premature antifascist for his early activism.

Synset Examples

Her actions were seen as those of a premature antifascist.
He claimed to be a premature antifascist at the rally.
The group was labeled as premature antifascists in the article.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antifascist - one who opposes fascism

Etymology

Formed from 'premature' (Latin 'praematurus') + 'antifascist' (anti- + fascist; fascist from Italian 'fascista').
