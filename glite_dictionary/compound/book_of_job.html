Book of Job

1. PROPN

/ ˌbʊk əv ˈdʒoʊb / (AmE), / ˌbʊk əv ˈdʒəʊb / (BrE)
a book in the Old Testament of the Bible, recounting Job's suffering, his pleas to God, and God's response
biblical book about Job's suffering and faith

Examples

Many people turn to the Book of Job for comfort in times of suffering.
The Book of Job raises profound questions about faith and adversity.
She studied the Book of Job as part of her theology course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Job - the central character of the Book of Job, known for his faith amid suffering
