machinist's vise

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃiːnɪsts vaɪs / (AmE), / məˈʃiːnɪsts vaɪs / (BrE)
a heavy vise with two parallel iron jaws, designed to hold metal or other workpieces securely while they are being worked on, especially in machining or metalworking
heavy vise for machine work

Examples

The machinist's vise held the steel bar firmly while he drilled precise holes.
Every workshop bench was equipped with a sturdy machinist's vise for heavy-duty tasks.
She tightened the machinist's vise to secure the component before filing its edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vise - a clamping tool with two jaws
