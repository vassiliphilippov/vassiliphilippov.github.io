statutory rape

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃʊˌtɔri reɪp / (AmE), / ˈstætjʊtəri reɪp / (BrE)
sexual intercourse with a person who is below the legal age of consent, even if the act is consensual by both parties; a criminal offense defined by statute.
sexual intercourse with underage person (by law)
legal

Examples

He was charged with statutory rape after having a relationship with a minor.
The law considers any sexual activity with someone under the age of consent to be statutory rape.
Even if both parties agree, statutory rape is still a criminal offense in many jurisdictions.

Etymology

From 'statutory', relating to laws or statutes, and 'rape', from Latin 'rapere' (to seize, abduct).
