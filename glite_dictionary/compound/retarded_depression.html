retarded depression

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɑrdɪd dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɑːdɪd dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
A form of clinical depression characterized by lethargy and slowed physical and mental activity, making it difficult for the individual to initiate actions.
clinical depression with lethargy and slowed responses
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with retarded depression after exhibiting extreme lethargy and slow movements.
In cases of retarded depression, individuals may find it hard to begin even simple daily tasks.
Treatment plans for retarded depression often address both mood symptoms and psychomotor slowing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agitated depression - clinical depression with restlessness and agitation
