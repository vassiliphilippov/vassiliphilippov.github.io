Erb's palsy

1. NOUN

/ ɜrbz ˈpælzi / (AmE), / ɜːbz ˈpælzi / (BrE)
a form of paralysis of the arm caused by injury to the brachial plexus, typically occurring during childbirth
paralysis of the arm due to brachial plexus injury
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with Erb's palsy after complications during delivery.
Physical therapy is often recommended for infants showing signs of Erb's palsy.
Doctors explained that Erb's palsy affects movement and sensation in the affected arm.

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm Heinrich Erb, German neurologist (1840–1921), who described the condition.
