pecking order

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛkɪŋ ˌɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈpɛkɪŋ ˌɔːdə / (BrE)
the hierarchical arrangement of individuals in a group or organization according to status or rank
hierarchical order of rank

Examples

There is a strict pecking order in the army, determining who gives and receives orders.
New employees often have to find their place in the pecking order of the office.
Seniority usually determines a teacher’s position in the pecking order at the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hierarchy - a system in which people or things are ranked one above another

Etymology

Originally referring to the hierarchy observed among chickens, from 'peck' + 'order', first used in a social sense in the 1920s.
