aminobenzoic acid

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊbɛnˈzoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊbɛnˈzəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
Any of several crystalline derivatives of benzoic acid in which one or more hydrogen atoms are replaced by an amino group, especially para-aminobenzoic acid.
a type of benzoic acid derivative
medical

Examples

The sunscreen contains aminobenzoic acid to help block harmful UV rays.
Researchers are studying the effects of aminobenzoic acid derivatives on skin health.
Certain bacteria can synthesize aminobenzoic acid as a vitamin precursor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoic acid - a simple aromatic carboxylic acid

Etymology

From amino- (referring to the amino group) + benzoic acid.
