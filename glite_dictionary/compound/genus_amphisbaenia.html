genus Amphisbaenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæm.fɪzˈbiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌam.fɪzˈbiː.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Amphisbaenidae, comprising various species of worm lizards.
type genus of worm lizards

Examples

The genus Amphisbaenia is composed of legless, burrowing reptiles known as worm lizards.
Species under the genus Amphisbaenia can be found in both South America and Africa.
Herpetologists study the genus Amphisbaenia to understand evolutionary adaptations in reptiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amphisbaenidae - the family of worm lizards related to Amphisbaenia

Etymology

From Greek 'amphis' (double) and 'bainaien' (to go), referencing the animal's ability to move in both directions.
