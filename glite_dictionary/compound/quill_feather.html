quill feather

1. NOUN

/ kwɪl ˈfɛðər / (AmE), / kwɪl ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
any of the large, stiff feathers from the wing or tail of a bird, often used historically for writing
large wing or tail feather of a bird

Examples

The artist carefully dipped the quill feather into ink before beginning to write the letter.
A single quill feather was lodged between the pages of the old book as a bookmark.
The eagle spread its wings, showing off each perfectly aligned quill feather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight feather - a large feather necessary for flying, on a bird's wing or tail

Etymology

From 'quill' (Middle English, from Middle Low German or Middle Dutch, meaning a hollow shaft or tube) and 'feather'.
