dwarf grey willow

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ɡreɪ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ɡreɪ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A low-growing willow shrub found in dry areas of the eastern United States, characterized by long, narrow leaves with a greyish, hairy underside.
a low willow shrub with greyish leaves

Examples

The dwarf grey willow thrives in sandy soils along the riverbank.
We identified a patch of dwarf grey willow during our nature hike.
The leaves of the dwarf grey willow are soft and silvery underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, known for slender leaves and flexible branches
