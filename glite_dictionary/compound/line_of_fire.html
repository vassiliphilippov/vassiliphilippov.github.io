line of fire

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv faɪr / (AmE), / laɪn ɒv faɪə / (BrE)
the path along which a bullet or other projectile travels after being fired from a weapon
path of a discharged projectile

Examples

Make sure you are not standing in the line of fire during shooting practice.
The soldier quickly moved out of the line of fire to avoid getting hit.
Emergency personnel should never enter the line of fire without proper protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossfire - gunfire from two or more directions crossing each other's paths
  • Synonym: direct fire - gunfire delivered directly at a visible target

2. NOUN

/ laɪn əv faɪər / (AmE), / laɪn əv faɪə / (BrE)
a position or situation where someone is at risk of being harmed, attacked, or criticized
situation of danger or criticism

Examples

During the protest, several journalists were caught in the line of fire.
As the company's spokesperson, she was constantly in the line of fire from reporters.
If we stay here, we'll be right in the line of fire when the debate gets heated.

Synset Examples

In negotiations, we must avoid being caught in the line of fire.
The firefighters bravely entered the line of fire to rescue the cat.
He found himself in the line of fire during the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot seat - a position in which someone is under a lot of scrutiny or pressure
  • Antonym: out of harm's way - protected from danger
