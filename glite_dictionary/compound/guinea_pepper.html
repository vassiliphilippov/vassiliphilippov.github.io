Guinea pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A tropical West African evergreen tree (Aframomum melegueta) whose pungent aromatic seeds are used as a condiment and in folk medicine; also the seeds themselves.
West African plant and spice

Examples

The chef added a pinch of Guinea pepper to give the dish a unique, spicy flavor.
In traditional remedies, Guinea pepper is valued for its supposed medicinal properties.
Traders imported Guinea pepper to Europe during the Middle Ages as an exotic spice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grains of paradise - pungent seeds from Aframomum melegueta, similar to Guinea pepper

Etymology

Named after Guinea, the West African region where the plant is native, and 'pepper' for its spicy seeds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A West African plant (Aframomum melegueta) whose seeds are used as a pungent spice, also called grains of paradise.
West African plant with spicy seeds

Examples

The chef added Guinea pepper to the stew for a rich, spicy flavor.
Traditionally, Guinea pepper has been used in West African cuisine and herbal medicine.
The market stall was stocked with exotic spices, including Guinea pepper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grains of paradise - Seeds of Aframomum melegueta used as a spice, synonym for Guinea pepper

Etymology

From the name 'Guinea', a region of West Africa, and 'pepper', referring to the pungent seeds.
