rumble seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌm.bəl siːt / (AmE), / ˈrʌm.bəl siːt / (BrE)
a folding seat built into the outside rear of some early automobiles, which could be opened to carry additional passengers
folding rear seat in old cars

Examples

The children begged to ride in the rumble seat as they drove through the countryside.
Many classic car shows feature models with a restored rumble seat in the back.
He remembers watching parades from the rumble seat of his grandfather's old Ford.

Etymology

From 'rumble', an old term for a carriage compartment, and 'seat'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmbəl siːt / (AmE), / ˈrʌmbəl siːt / (BrE)
a folding or externally mounted rear seat in an automobile, typically open to the weather, used in older cars, especially roadsters and coupes
rear seat in an old car, often open

Examples

The vintage roadster featured a rumble seat where passengers could sit outside the main cabin.
She always loved the feeling of wind in her hair while riding in the rumble seat.
During the parade, the mayor waved to the crowd from the rumble seat of a classic car.

Synset Examples

They enjoyed the summer breeze in the rumble seat.
He reminisced about riding in the rumble seat as a kid.
The old car had a rumble seat for extra passengers.

Etymology

First used in early 20th-century American English, possibly from 'rumble' (a carriage with a seat at the back).
