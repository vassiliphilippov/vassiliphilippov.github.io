hospital ward

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːspɪtl wɔrd / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪtəl wɔːd / (BrE)
A section or room in a hospital where a group of patients with similar medical needs are cared for together.
section of a hospital for similar patients

Examples

She was moved to the hospital ward after her surgery for observation.
Each hospital ward specializes in a different type of patient care, such as pediatrics or cardiology.
The nurse made her nightly rounds in the quiet hospital ward.

Synset Examples

they put her in a 4-bed ward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ward - a room or division in a hospital for particular patients

Etymology

From 'hospital' (from Latin 'hospitale' meaning guest house) and 'ward' (from Old English 'weard' meaning guard or division).
