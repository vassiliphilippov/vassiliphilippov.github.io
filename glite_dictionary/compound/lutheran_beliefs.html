Lutheran beliefs

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːθɚrən bəˈliːfs / (AmE), / ˈluːθərənd bɪˈliːfs / (BrE)
the religious principles and doctrines characteristic of Lutheranism, a branch of Protestant Christianity based on the teachings of Martin Luther
religious principles of Lutheranism

Examples

Her Lutheran beliefs have shaped her views on grace and salvation.
Many communities in Minnesota have strong Lutheran beliefs that influence their traditions.
He studied the differences between Lutheran beliefs and those of other Protestant denominations.

Synset Examples

His Lutheranist beliefs guide his charitable work.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Protestant beliefs - religious principles of Protestantism
  • Antonym: Catholic beliefs - religious principles of Catholicism

Etymology

From Lutheran, relating to Martin Luther (1483–1546), plus beliefs.
