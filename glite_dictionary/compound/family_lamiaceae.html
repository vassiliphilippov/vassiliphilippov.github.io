family Lamiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli læmˈiːəsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli læmˈiːəsiː / (BrE)
a large family of aromatic herbs and shrubs, including mint, thyme, sage, and rosemary, characterized by flowers with lip-like shapes and four-lobed ovaries yielding one-seeded nutlets
the botanical mint family of aromatic herbs and shrubs

Examples

Many popular culinary herbs, such as basil and lavender, belong to the family Lamiaceae.
Plants in the family Lamiaceae are known for their distinct aroma and square stems.
Botanists have recently described a new species within the family Lamiaceae from the Mediterranean region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - aromatic herb of the genus Mentha, a member of the Lamiaceae family
  • Synonym: sage - aromatic herb, Salvia officinalis, member of the Lamiaceae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Lamiaceae, based on the genus Lamium.
