battle cruiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəl ˌkruːzər / (AmE), / ˈbætl ˌkruːzə / (BrE)
a large warship equipped with heavy guns and designed for high speed, typically with lighter armor than a battleship
fast, heavily armed warship

Examples

The Royal Navy's battle cruiser was instrumental in the early stages of the conflict.
Historians continue to debate the effectiveness of the battle cruiser design during World War I.
The museum displays a detailed model of a famous battle cruiser from the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cruiser - a large warship, generally lighter than a battleship
  • Degree Higher: battleship - a large, heavily armored warship with the heaviest firepower
