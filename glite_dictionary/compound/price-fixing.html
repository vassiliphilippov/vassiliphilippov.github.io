price-fixing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪs ˌfɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪs ˌfɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of maintaining prices at a certain level by agreement between competing sellers, often to restrict competition
agreement among sellers to fix prices
legal

Examples

The companies were fined millions for engaging in price-fixing schemes.
Government regulators are investigating allegations of price-fixing in the pharmaceutical industry.
Consumers suffer when price-fixing eliminates fair market competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collusion - secret or illegal cooperation between rivals; often to deceive or cheat
