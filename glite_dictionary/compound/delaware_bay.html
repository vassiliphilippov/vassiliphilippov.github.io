Delaware Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛləˌwɛr beɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪwɛə beɪ / (BrE)
an inlet of the North Atlantic Ocean on the east coast of the United States, fed by the Delaware River
inlet of the North Atlantic on US east coast

Examples

Many migratory birds stop at Delaware Bay during their annual journeys.
Fishing is a popular activity in the waters of Delaware Bay.
The coastline along Delaware Bay is known for its scenic beauty and wildlife.

Etymology

Named after the Delaware River, which in turn was named after Thomas West, 3rd Baron De La Warr, an early English colonial governor.
