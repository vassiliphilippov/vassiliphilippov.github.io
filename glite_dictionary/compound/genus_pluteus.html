genus Pluteus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pluːˈtiː.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈpluː.tjuː.əs / (BrE)
A large genus of fungi in the family Pluteaceae, characterized by cap shapes that resemble a roof and often found in abundance early in summer.
genus of fungi with roof-shaped caps

Examples

Several species within the genus Pluteus can be identified by their pink spore prints.
Mycology students collected samples from the genus Pluteus during their summer fieldwork.
The roof-shaped caps make the genus Pluteus distinctive among woodland fungi.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pluteus', meaning 'shed' or 'roof', referring to the cap shape.
