citrange tree

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtreɪndʒ triː / (AmE), / sɪˈtrɒndʒ triː / (BrE)
A hybrid citrus tree resulting from a cross between a sweet orange and the trifoliate orange, with fruit that is more aromatic and acidic than ordinary oranges.
hybrid citrus tree (sweet orange × trifoliate orange)

Examples

The citrange tree is often grown as a rootstock for other citrus varieties.
Fruit from the citrange tree tends to be more sour and aromatic than typical oranges.
Botanists study the citrange tree for its disease resistance and cold tolerance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: orange tree - tree that produces oranges

Etymology

Blend of 'citrus' and 'orange'; the name reflects its hybrid origin.
