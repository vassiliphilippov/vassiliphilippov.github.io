briefcase computer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːfˌkeɪs kəmˈpjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈbriːfkeɪs kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
a portable computer housed in a box shaped like a briefcase, used especially in the early era of mobile computing
early portable computer in briefcase form

Examples

The engineer carried a briefcase computer to the job site for instant data analysis.
In the 1980s, briefcase computers were considered revolutionary for business travel.
He opened the briefcase computer and began entering financial data on the spot.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: laptop - a small, portable personal computer that can rest on the user's lap
  • Degree Lower: desktop computer - a computer designed to fit on a desk and not be portable
