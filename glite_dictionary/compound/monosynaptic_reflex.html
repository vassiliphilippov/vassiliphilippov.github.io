monosynaptic reflex

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊsɪˈnæptɪk rɪˈflɛks / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊsɪˈnæptɪk rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
a reflex arc that involves only one synapse between the sensory and motor neurons, resulting in a rapid and automatic response
reflex with a single synapse
medical

Examples

The monosynaptic reflex is crucial in controlling the knee-jerk response during a physical exam.
Doctors often test the monosynaptic reflex to assess nervous system function.
A disruption in the monosynaptic reflex may indicate a neurological disorder.

Synset Examples

The monosynaptic reflex is quick and automatic.
He studied the monosynaptic response in the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polysynaptic reflex - a reflex arc involving two or more synapses

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (single) and 'synapsis' (connection), referring to a single synapse in the neural pathway.
