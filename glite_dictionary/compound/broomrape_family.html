broomrape family

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːmˌreɪp ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈbruːmreɪp ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of brown or yellow leafless herbs, sometimes classified in the order Scrophulariales, including the genus Orobanche and related parasitic plants.
family of parasitic, leafless herbs (Orobanchaceae)

Examples

Several species in the broomrape family are known for parasitizing the roots of other plants.
The broomrape family includes unusual herbs that lack chlorophyll and live off host plants.
Botanists are studying new members of the broomrape family found in Mediterranean regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orobanchaceae - the botanical family of broomrapes, parasitic plants

Etymology

Named after the genus Bromraiche (broomrape); 'family' from Latin 'familia'.
