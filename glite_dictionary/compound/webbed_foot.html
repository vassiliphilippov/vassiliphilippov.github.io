webbed foot

1. NOUN

/ wɛbd fʊt / (AmE), / wɛbd fʊt / (BrE)
A foot, especially in birds or aquatic animals, in which the toes are connected by folds of skin that aid in swimming.
bird or animal foot with connected toes

Examples

Most ducks have a webbed foot that helps them paddle efficiently in water.
The frog's webbed foot allows it to swim quickly across the pond.
Penguins use their webbed foot for steering while swimming underwater.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clawed foot - a foot that ends in claws, rather than having webbing
