nitrogen trichloride

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrədʒən traɪˈklɔːrɪd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrɪdʒən traɪˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a yellow, pungent, and volatile oily compound (NCl3), formerly used as a bleaching and aging agent for flour
a volatile yellow chemical compound (NCl3)

Examples

Nitrogen trichloride is no longer used in food processing due to safety concerns.
Exposure to nitrogen trichloride may cause irritation of the eyes and respiratory tract.
The yellow color and strong smell make nitrogen trichloride easy to identify in the laboratory.

Etymology

Formed from 'nitrogen' and 'trichloride', indicating three chlorine atoms bonded to nitrogen.
