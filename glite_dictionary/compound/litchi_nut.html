litchi nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.tʃi ˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈlɪ.tʃi ˌnʌt / (BrE)
A small, round Chinese fruit with a thin brittle shell, sweet jellylike pulp, and a single seed, often eaten fresh or dried.
Chinese fruit with sweet pulp and a single seed

Examples

She offered me a litchi nut as a dessert after our meal.
The recipe called for dried litchi nuts to add sweetness and texture.
He had never tasted a litchi nut before visiting China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - another spelling for the same fruit

Etymology

From Chinese (Cantonese) 'laih ji'; 'nut' from Old English 'hnutu'.
