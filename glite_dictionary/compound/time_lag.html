time lag

1. NOUN

/ taɪm læɡ / (AmE), / taɪm læɡ / (BrE)
a period of delay between an event or action and its effect or result
delay between event and effect

Examples

There is often a time lag between submitting your application and receiving a response.
The time lag in the system caused significant inconvenience for our users.
Scientists observed a short time lag before the medicine took effect.

Synset Examples

instant replay caused too long a delay
he ordered a hold in the action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - a period of time by which something is late or postponed
