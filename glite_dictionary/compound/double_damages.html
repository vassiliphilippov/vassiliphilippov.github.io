double damages

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈdæmɪdʒəz / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈdamɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
A monetary award in a legal case that is twice the amount that would normally be awarded for the injury or loss.
legal award: twice the usual damages
legal

Examples

The court ordered the company to pay double damages due to its willful misconduct.
Under the statute, successful plaintiffs are entitled to double damages as a deterrent against future violations.
If the landlord is found liable, he could be required to pay double damages to the tenant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive damages - damages exceeding simple compensation and awarded to punish the defendant
