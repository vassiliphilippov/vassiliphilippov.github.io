polyconic projection

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˌkɑnɪk prəˈʤɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌkɒnɪk prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a type of map projection in which each parallel is represented by an arc of a circle, with the scale along each parallel correct, resulting in distances between meridians that match those on a globe.
a map projection with true distances between meridians

Examples

The polyconic projection is often used for large-scale mapping of countries.
Many nineteenth-century atlases featured maps drawn using the polyconic projection.
Unlike the Mercator projection, the polyconic projection preserves true distances along the central meridian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conic projection - a map projection where the globe is projected onto a cone

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'conic' (pertaining to a cone), referring to the use of many cones in the projection method.
