blood-oxygenation level dependent functional magnetic resonance imaging

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˌɑksɪˈdʒeɪʃən ˈlɛvəl dɪˈpɛndənt ˌfʌŋkʃənl ˌmægnəˈrɛzənəns ɪˈmɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / blʌd ˌɒksɪˈdeɪʃən ˈlɛvl dɪˈpɛndənt ˌfʌŋkʃənl ˌmægnɪtɪk rɪˈzɒnəns ˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) that measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood oxygenation caused by neural activity.
type of fMRI based on blood oxygenation changes
medical

Examples

Researchers used blood-oxygenation level dependent functional magnetic resonance imaging to assess the patient's brain response to visual stimuli.
The development of blood-oxygenation level dependent functional magnetic resonance imaging revolutionized our understanding of neural activation patterns.
Many neuroscience studies rely on blood-oxygenation level dependent functional magnetic resonance imaging to map cognitive processes in the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fMRI - functional magnetic resonance imaging; a neuroimaging technique measuring brain activity

Etymology

Formed by compounding: 'blood-oxygenation', 'level dependent', and 'functional magnetic resonance imaging'.
