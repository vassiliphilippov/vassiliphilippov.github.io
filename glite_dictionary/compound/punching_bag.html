punching bag

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃɪŋ bæɡ / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃɪŋ baɡ / (BrE)
A person who is a frequent target of another's anger, criticism, or abuse.
person who receives anger or criticism

Examples

She always treats her assistant like her personal punching bag whenever she's stressed.
I refuse to be your punching bag just because you had a bad day at work.
After weeks of being the team's punching bag, he finally stood up for himself.

Synset Examples

he resigned because his boss used him as a punching bag

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scapegoat - a person who is blamed for the faults of others

Etymology

From 'punching bag' (a sack used for training in boxing), used figuratively for a person treated the same way.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃɪŋ bæɡ / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃɪŋ bæɡ / (BrE)
A sturdy, often inflated or padded bag, typically suspended, that is punched repeatedly for training in boxing or other combat sports.
bag used for boxing practice

Examples

He trained every evening by hitting the punching bag in his garage.
The gym installed a new punching bag for the boxing classes.
She let out her frustration on the punching bag after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed bag - a small punching bag used to improve hand speed and timing
  • Synonym: heavy bag - a large, heavy punching bag used for strength training

Synset POS: n
