bioelectric potential

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.ɪˈlɛk.trɪk pəˈtɛn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊ.ɪˈlɛk.trɪk pəˈtɛn.ʃəl / (BrE)
an electrical potential that occurs within a living organism, often relating to the difference in electric charge across biological membranes or tissues
electrical potential in a living organism
medical

Examples

Researchers measured the bioelectric potential across the muscle cell membrane.
Damage to tissue can affect the bioelectric potential necessary for nerve signal transmission.
Medical devices sometimes monitor bioelectric potential to diagnose heart or nerve conditions.

Etymology

From 'bio-' (Greek, meaning life) + 'electric' (from Latin 'ēlectricus', relating to amber), and 'potential' (from Latin 'potentia', power).
