well-mannered

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈmænərd / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈmænəd / (BrE)
Behaving in a polite and socially acceptable way; showing good manners.
polite and well-behaved

Examples

The children at the party were all well-mannered and thanked their host politely.
Her well-mannered response impressed the committee during the interview.
Despite his success, he has remained humble and well-mannered throughout his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polite - having or showing good manners or respect for others
  • Antonym: ill-mannered - not polite; rude or impolite

Etymology

From 'well' (in a good or proper way) + 'mannered' (having manners).
