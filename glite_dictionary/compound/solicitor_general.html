solicitor general

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪsət̬ɚ ˈdʒɛnɚəl / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪtə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
a law officer appointed to assist the attorney general and often represents the government in court cases, especially appellate courts
senior legal officer assisting the attorney general
legal

Examples

The solicitor general argued the case before the Supreme Court.
Each state may appoint a solicitor general to represent its interests in higher courts.
She worked as the solicitor general before becoming the country's attorney general.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: attorney general - the chief legal officer of a state or country

Etymology

From 'solicitor' (legal advocate, from Latin 'sollicitare' to disturb or solicit) + 'general' (from Latin 'generalis')
