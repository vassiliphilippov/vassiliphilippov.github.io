amplitude modulation

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.plɪˌtuːd ˌmɑː.dʒəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈæm.plɪˌtjuːd ˌmɒd.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a method of transmitting signals by varying the amplitude of a carrier wave, especially in radio broadcasting.
modulation by varying a carrier's amplitude

Examples

The radio station transmits its signal using amplitude modulation to reach distant listeners.
Early broadcasts relied on amplitude modulation before the invention of FM radio.
Scientists study amplitude modulation to improve communication systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frequency modulation - modulation by varying the frequency of a carrier wave

Etymology

From 'amplitude' (size, strength) + 'modulation' (process of varying).
