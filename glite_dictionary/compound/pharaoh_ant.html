pharaoh ant

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr.oʊ ænt / (AmE), / ˈfeə.rəʊ ænt / (BrE)
a small, yellowish-red ant (Monomorium pharaonis), commonly found in warm regions and often established as a troublesome household pest
small house-infesting ant species

Examples

We discovered a trail of pharaoh ants marching along the kitchen counter.
The exterminator explained that pharaoh ants can be especially difficult to eliminate from homes.
Researchers study how pharaoh ants quickly adapt to different household environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small insect of the family Formicidae

Etymology

Named after the mistaken belief that these ants were one of the plagues of ancient Egypt.
