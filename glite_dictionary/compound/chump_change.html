chump change

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌmp tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / tʃʌmp tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a very small or insignificant amount of money, especially when considered in comparison to other amounts
very small amount of money

Examples

For a company that size, the fines were just chump change.
He earned only chump change for his work on the project.
Compared to the cost of the house, the repairs were chump change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pittance - a very small amount of money
  • Antonym: fortune - a large amount of money
