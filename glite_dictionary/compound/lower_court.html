lower court

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊɚ ˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈləʊə ˌkɔːt / (BrE)
a court whose decisions may be reviewed and possibly overturned by a higher court
court whose decisions can be appealed
legal

Examples

The decision made by the lower court was overturned on appeal.
Attorneys often challenge the rulings of a lower court if they believe an error was made.
A lower court typically handles the case before it goes to an appellate court.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: higher court - a court with the authority to review decisions of a lower court
  • Synonym: trial court - a court where cases are initially tried and determined
