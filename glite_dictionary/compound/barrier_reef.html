barrier reef

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæriər ˌriːf / (AmE), / ˈbæriə ˌriːf / (BrE)
A long, narrow coral reef running parallel to and separated from the shore by a lagoon or deep channel.
long coral reef parallel to shore

Examples

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest barrier reef, stretching over 2,300 kilometers off the coast of Australia.
Scientists are studying how climate change is affecting the delicate ecosystems of the barrier reef.
Tourists can snorkel along the barrier reef to see colorful marine life and vibrant corals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: coral reef - a reef built primarily from coral organisms
