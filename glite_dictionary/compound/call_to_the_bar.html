to call to the bar

1. VERB

/ kɔl tə ðə bɑr / (AmE), / kɔːl tə ðə bɑː / (BrE)
the formal process of being admitted as a barrister and authorized to practice law in a court
admission as a barrister
legal british

Examples

After years of study, she was finally called to the bar in London.
He hopes to call to the bar after completing his legal training.
She was the first woman in her family to be called to the bar.

Synset Examples

She received her degree and prepared to call to the bar.
After years of studying, he was ready to call to the bar.
His family celebrated when he finally got to call to the bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - the legal profession, particularly as barristers

Etymology

From 'bar', referring to the physical barrier in a courtroom separating legal professionals from the public.
