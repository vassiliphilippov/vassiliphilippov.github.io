Spice Islands

1. PROPN

/ spaɪs ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / spaɪs ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in eastern Indonesia, historically known as the center of the spice trade and colonized by European powers.
islands in Indonesia famous for historic spice trade

Examples

In the 17th century, the Dutch established control over the Spice Islands to monopolize the spice trade.
Nutmeg and cloves from the Spice Islands were highly prized in Europe.
The search for the Spice Islands motivated explorers and shaped world history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maluku Islands - Indonesian island group also known as the Spice Islands

Etymology

Named for their historical importance as a source of spices such as cloves and nutmeg.
