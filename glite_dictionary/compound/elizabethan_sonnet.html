Elizabethan sonnet

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪz.əˈbi.θən ˈsɒn.ɪt / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪz.əˈbiː.θən ˈsɒn.ɪt / (BrE)
A type of sonnet consisting of three quatrains and a final couplet in iambic pentameter, following the rhyme scheme abab cdcd efef gg; also known as a Shakespearean sonnet.
English sonnet form with specific rhyme scheme

Examples

Students in English class analyzed several Elizabethan sonnets to understand their structure and themes.
She tried to write her own Elizabethan sonnet using the classic rhyme scheme and iambic pentameter.
Shakespeare pioneered the Elizabethan sonnet, making it famous in English literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shakespearean sonnet - A sonnet with the same form as Elizabethan sonnet
  • Antonym: Italian sonnet - A sonnet form with a different structure and rhyme scheme (Petrarchan)

Etymology

Named after the Elizabethan era (reign of Elizabeth I of England, late 16th to early 17th centuries).
