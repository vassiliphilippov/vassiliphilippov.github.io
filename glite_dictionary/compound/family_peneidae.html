family Peneidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pɛˈniːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pɛˈniːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical prawns characterized by long antennae, commonly found in marine and brackish waters.
family of tropical prawns

Examples

The family Peneidae includes many commercially important shrimp species.
Several species within the family Peneidae are harvested for food worldwide.
Researchers are studying the breeding habits of prawns in the family Peneidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penaeus - a genus of prawns within the family Peneidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Peneidae', the family name based on genus 'Penaeus'.
