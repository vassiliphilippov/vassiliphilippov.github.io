Niemann-Pick disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːmən ˈpɪk dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / ˌniːmæn ˈpɪk dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a rare inherited disorder of lipid metabolism, causing harmful amounts of lipids to accumulate in cells and tissues
rare inherited lipid storage disorder
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the infant with Niemann-Pick disease after observing symptoms of enlarged liver and spleen.
Researchers are investigating new treatments for Niemann-Pick disease, hoping to improve patient outcomes.
Families affected by Niemann-Pick disease often seek support from genetic counselors.

Etymology

Named after German physicians Albert Niemann and Ludwig Pick, who first described the disease.
