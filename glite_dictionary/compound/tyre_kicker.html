tyre kicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪər ˌkɪkər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪə ˌkɪkə / (BrE)
a person who inspects products, especially cars, with no serious intention of buying, often wasting a salesperson's time
person who inspects without intent to buy
british informal

Examples

The dealership manager grew impatient with yet another tyre kicker browsing cars with no intention to buy.
Online sellers often try to filter out tyre kickers before arranging in-person meetings.
He asked serious questions about the price to prove he wasn't just a tyre kicker.

Synset Examples

He spent hours at the dealership, just being a tyre kicker.
Don't be a tyre kicker; just make an offer.
Tyre kickers often waste salespeople's time without buying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time-waster - person who wastes others' time

Etymology

From 'tyre' (British spelling of 'tire') + 'kicker', referring to inspecting a car by literally kicking the tires.
