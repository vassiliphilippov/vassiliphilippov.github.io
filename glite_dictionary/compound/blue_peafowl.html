blue peafowl

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈpiːˌfaʊl / (AmE), / bluː ˈpiːˌfaʊl / (BrE)
A large, brightly colored pheasant native to India and Sri Lanka, known for the male's iridescent blue plumage and ornate tail.
a large, colorful pheasant of South Asia

Examples

The blue peafowl is the national bird of India and is admired for its vibrant plumage.
In the palace gardens, a splendid blue peafowl displayed its fanned tail to impress a mate.
Many zoos in Europe now keep blue peafowl due to their striking appearance and gentle behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacock - the male of the peafowl species, especially noted for its tail feathers
  • Antonym: green peafowl - a species of peafowl native to Southeast Asia with greenish plumage
