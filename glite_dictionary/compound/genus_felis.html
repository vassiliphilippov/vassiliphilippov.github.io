genus Felis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfiːlɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of the family Felidae, comprising the true cats and most wildcats, including the domestic cat.
genus of true cats and wildcats

Examples

The domestic cat belongs to the genus Felis along with several wildcat species.
Scientists debate how many species should be classified under the genus Felis.
The African wildcat is one of the best-known members of the genus Felis.

Etymology

From Latin 'felis' (cat), used in scientific taxonomy.
