eau de cologne

1. NOUN

/ oʊ də koʊˈloʊn / (AmE), / əʊ də kɒˈləʊn / (BrE)
A lightly scented perfumed liquid made from essential oils and alcohol, originally formulated in Cologne, Germany.
lightly scented perfumed liquid

Examples

He splashed some eau de cologne on his wrists before leaving the house.
The scent of her eau de cologne lingered in the hallway.
This brand is famous for its fresh citrus eau de cologne fragrances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cologne - a type of light perfume

Etymology

From French, meaning 'water of Cologne', named after Cologne, Germany, where it was first produced.
