Berber Coast

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɜrbər koʊst / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbə kəʊst / (BrE)
the Mediterranean coastal region of northern Africa, between Egypt and Gibraltar, historically known for pirate activity from the 16th to 19th centuries
historic North African coastal region

Examples

During the 18th century, European ships feared attacks along the Berber Coast.
The Berber Coast was infamous for its pirates and slave trade.
Many historical accounts describe the cultural exchange that occurred on the Berber Coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbary Coast - historical term for North African Mediterranean coast known for piracy

Etymology

From 'Berber', referring to the indigenous people of North Africa, and 'coast'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbɜrbər ˈkoʊst / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbə ˈkəʊst / (BrE)
the Mediterranean coastal region of northern Africa, historically known for its Moorish and Berber pirates
historic North African coastal region

Examples

The Berber Coast was notorious for pirate raids on European shipping.
Merchants traveling the Mediterranean often feared the dangers along the Berber Coast.
Historical accounts detail naval battles near the Berber Coast in the 18th century.

Etymology

'Berber' from Arabic 'barbar', referring to indigenous North African peoples, and 'coast' from Old French 'coste'.
