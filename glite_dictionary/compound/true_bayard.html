true bayard

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈbeɪərd / (AmE), / truː ˈbeɪəd / (BrE)
a person who displays exceptional courage and honesty
person of great courage and honesty

Examples

During the crisis, he proved himself a true bayard, standing firm when others faltered.
In the face of adversity, only a true bayard would risk speaking out for what is right.
Everyone in the community regarded her as a true bayard because of her honesty and bravery.

Synset Examples

In the debate, she was a true bayard, unafraid to speak her mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • Antonym: coward - a person lacking courage

Etymology

From Bayard, the name of a legendary bay horse renowned for courage and loyalty in medieval romance.
