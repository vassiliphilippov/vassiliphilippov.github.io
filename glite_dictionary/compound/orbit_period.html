orbit period

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrbɪt ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪt ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the time required for an object to complete one full revolution in its orbit around a celestial body
time to complete one orbit

Examples

The orbit period of the Moon around Earth is approximately 27.3 days.
Changes in altitude can significantly alter a satellite's orbit period.
Scientists calculated the orbit period using the distance between the two celestial bodies.

Synset Examples

the orbit period depends on the altitude of the satellite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orbital period - the time a given astronomical object takes to complete one orbit around another object
