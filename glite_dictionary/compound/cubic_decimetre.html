cubic decimetre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈdɛs.ɪˌmiː.tər / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈdɛs.ɪˌmiː.tə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to one litre or 1,000 cubic centimetres, commonly used to measure capacity
metric unit of volume (1,000 cubic cm/1 litre)

Examples

A cubic decimetre of water is exactly equal to one litre.
The engine's displacement is measured in cubic decimetres or litres.
Scientifically, a cubic decimetre is defined as 1,000 cubic centimetres.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litre - metric unit of capacity equal to one cubic decimetre

Etymology

From Latin 'cubicus' (cube-shaped) + 'decimetre' (a tenth of a metre, from French 'décimètre').
