vertical stabiliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜːrtɪkəl ˈsteɪbɪˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl ˈstæbɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
the upright part of an aircraft's tail that helps keep the airplane flying straight by resisting unwanted sideways motion
upright tail fin of an airplane

Examples

The vertical stabiliser ensures that the aircraft maintains directional stability during flight.
Damage to the vertical stabiliser can make the plane difficult to control in crosswinds.
During the inspection, the engineers focused on the integrity of the vertical stabiliser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudder - movable control surface attached to the vertical stabiliser
