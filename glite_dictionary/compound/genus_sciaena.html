genus Sciaena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs skaɪˈiːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs skaɪˈiːnə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Sciaenidae, which includes the croakers, a group of marine fish.
type genus of croaker fish (Sciaenidae)

Examples

Many marine biologists study the genus Sciaena to understand the evolution of croaker fish.
Several important commercial fish species belong to the genus Sciaena.
The distinctive sound produced by members of the genus Sciaena has made them popular subjects of underwater acoustics research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croaker - any fish of the family Sciaenidae, known for the croaking noise they make

Etymology

From Latin 'Sciaena', an ancient name for a type of fish.
