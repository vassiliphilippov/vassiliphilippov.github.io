mental rejection

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntəl rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɛntəl rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
the state of doubting or refusing to accept something as true
doubt; refusal to accept

Examples

Her mental rejection of the explanation was obvious from her skeptical expression.
The scientist's mental rejection of unsupported theories drives her rigorous experiments.
Sometimes, mental rejection of new ideas stems from a lack of evidence rather than stubbornness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: acceptance - belief in the truth or reality of something
