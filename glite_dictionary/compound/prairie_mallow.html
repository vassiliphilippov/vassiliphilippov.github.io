prairie mallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈmaləʊ / (BrE)
A plant native to the western United States, known for its racemose red flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Malvastrum.
plant with racemose red flowers of western US

Examples

The prairie mallow blooms throughout the summer in the western plains.
Gardeners appreciate prairie mallow for its bright, racemose red flowers.
Wild prairie mallow attracts pollinators to native grasslands.
