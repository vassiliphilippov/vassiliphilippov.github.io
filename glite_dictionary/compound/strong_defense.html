strong defense

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / strɒŋ dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
a powerful or convincing legal argument or case presented in court
vigorous legal argument in court
legal

Examples

The lawyer prepared a strong defense to challenge the prosecution's accusations.
Her strong defense persuaded the jury to return a not-guilty verdict.
A strong defense can sometimes mean the difference between conviction and acquittal.

Synset Examples

The lawyer presented a strong defense in court.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak defense - an unconvincing or ineffective legal argument
