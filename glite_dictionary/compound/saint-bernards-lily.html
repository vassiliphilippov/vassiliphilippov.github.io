Saint-Bernard's-lily

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪnt bəˈnɑrdz ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt bəˈnɑːdz ˌlɪli / (BrE)
A southern European plant, often grown in gardens, known for its spikes of small, star-shaped greenish-white flowers.
southern European flowering plant

Examples

The Saint-Bernard's-lily is popular among gardeners for its delicate, star-shaped blossoms.
In spring, a row of Saint-Bernard's-lily brightened the edges of the old stone path.
She planted a Saint-Bernard's-lily to add a touch of elegance to her garden borders.
