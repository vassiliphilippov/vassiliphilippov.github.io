Downing Street

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnɪŋ strit / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnɪŋ striːt / (BrE)
A term used to refer to the British government, especially the Prime Minister and their offices, because the official residence of the Prime Minister is located on Downing Street in London.
the British government (metonym)
british

Examples

Downing Street announced new measures to tackle climate change yesterday.
There was speculation about a reshuffle in Downing Street following the recent elections.
Journalists gathered outside Downing Street awaiting the Prime Minister's statement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Whitehall - used as a metonym for the British civil service or government administration
  • Synonym: Westminster - refers to the UK Parliament or seat of government

Etymology

Named after Downing Street in London, where the Prime Minister's official residence is located.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdaʊnɪŋ strit / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnɪŋ striːt / (BrE)
A street in Westminster, London, famous as the site of the official residences and offices of the Prime Minister and the Chancellor of the Exchequer.
street in London housing UK's Prime Minister
british

Examples

There were reporters waiting outside Downing Street for an announcement.
The Prime Minister addressed the nation from Downing Street last night.
Major policy decisions are often discussed behind the doors of Downing Street.

Synset Examples

the Prime Minister lives at No. 10 Downing Street

Etymology

Named after Sir George Downing, who built the houses there in the 1680s.

Synset POS: n
