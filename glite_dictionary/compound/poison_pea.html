poison pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən pi / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən piː / (BrE)
any of several Australian evergreen shrubs of the genus Gastrolobium, noted for their whorled compound leaves that are poisonous to livestock and their showy yellow to deep reddish-orange flowers, followed by two-seeded pods.
Australian poisonous shrub of genus Gastrolobium

Examples

Cattle ranchers must keep their herds away from poison pea growing near the riverbanks.
The bright flowers of the poison pea make it easy to identify, despite its deadly reputation among livestock.
Researchers studied the toxins produced by the poison pea to understand their effects on grazing animals.
