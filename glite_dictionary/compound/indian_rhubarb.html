Indian rhubarb

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈruːbɑːrb / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈruːbɑːb / (BrE)
a perennial herb (Darmera peltata) with large shield-like leaves and clusters of white to bright pink flowers, native to stream banks in the Sierra Nevada, California
North American herb with large leaves and pink flowers

Examples

The broad leaves of the Indian rhubarb create dramatic shadows along the riverbank.
Gardeners prize the Indian rhubarb for its striking pink flower clusters in spring.
We spotted Indian rhubarb flourishing beside the streams in the Sierra Nevada.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ruːˌbɑːrb / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈruːbɑːb / (BrE)
An Asian herb native to the Himalayas, known for its medicinal roots and resemblance to common rhubarb.
Asian medicinal herb of the Himalayas

Examples

The roots of Indian rhubarb are used in traditional medicine across the Himalayas.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Indian rhubarb in its native habitat.
She purchased dried Indian rhubarb at the herbal market for its purported health benefits.
