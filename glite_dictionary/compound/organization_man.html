organization man

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən mæn / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən mæn / (BrE)
A man who conforms entirely to the values and demands of the organization he works for, often at the expense of his individuality.
conformist male employee

Examples

After years of working for the firm, he became the quintessential organization man who never challenged authority.
The novel portrayed the organization man as someone who put the company's needs before his own dreams.
Many people criticized him for being an organization man and lacking independent thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company man - a person, especially a man, who supports company policies unquestioningly

Etymology

Popularized by the 1956 book "The Organization Man" by William H. Whyte.
