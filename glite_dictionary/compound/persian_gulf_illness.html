Persian Gulf illness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːʒən ɡʌlf ˈɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ɡʌlf ˈɪlnɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition of uncertain cause that affected many veterans of the 1991 Gulf War, marked by symptoms such as fatigue, headaches, dizziness, nausea, skin rashes, joint pain, and respiratory problems
syndrome affecting Gulf War veterans
medical

Examples

Many veterans developed Persian Gulf illness after returning from service in the Middle East.
Researchers are still studying the causes of Persian Gulf illness and its long-term effects.
Symptoms of Persian Gulf illness can include constant fatigue, joint pain, and memory problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gulf War syndrome - another term for Persian Gulf illness, used interchangeably
