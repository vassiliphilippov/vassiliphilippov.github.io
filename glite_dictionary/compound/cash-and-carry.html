cash-and-carry

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæʃ ən ˈkæri / (AmE), / ˌkæʃ ən ˈkæri / (BrE)
(of a business or store) selling goods only for cash payment, with customers responsible for taking the goods away themselves; no delivery service provided.
sold for cash, taken by buyer (no delivery)

Examples

The warehouse operates on a cash-and-carry basis, so customers must pay onsite and transport goods themselves.
Many small businesses buy supplies from cash-and-carry wholesalers to save on delivery fees.
We chose a cash-and-carry store because we needed the items immediately and could take them ourselves.

Synset Examples

cash-and-carry business

Etymology

Formed from 'cash' (payment in money) and 'carry' (to take away).
