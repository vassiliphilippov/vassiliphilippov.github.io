osculating circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑskjəˌleɪtɪŋ ˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / ˈɒskjʊleɪtɪŋ ˈsɜːkl̩ / (BrE)
a circle that touches a given curve at a point, lying on the concave side, and sharing the same radius of curvature at that point
circle matching curve's curvature at a point

Examples

The osculating circle at a point on a parabola closely approximates the curve near that point.
Engineers sometimes use the osculating circle to analyze the local curvature of a track.
At the inflection point, the osculating circle becomes infinitely large as the curve's curvature approaches zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangent - a line that touches a curve at a single point without crossing it
  • Synonym: radius of curvature - the radius of the osculating circle at a particular point on a curve

Etymology

From Latin 'osculare' (to kiss, to touch), referring to the circle 'kissing' the curve.
