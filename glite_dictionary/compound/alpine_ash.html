alpine ash

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn æʃ / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn æʃ / (BrE)
a tall timber tree of southeastern Australia, known for its hard, pinkish or light brown wood
a tall Australian timber tree

Examples

The alpine ash forests in Victoria provide critical habitat for many species.
Furniture made from alpine ash is highly valued for its durability and appearance.
The bushfire devastated large areas of alpine ash woodland in the national park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucalyptus delegatensis - the scientific name of alpine ash, a tall eucalyptus tree of southeastern Australia
