federation of tribes

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən əv traɪbz / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən ɒv traɪbz / (BrE)
a formal alliance or union of multiple tribes, especially in historical or cultural contexts such as among Native American peoples
an alliance or union of tribes

Examples

The Iroquois Confederacy was a federation of tribes that played a major role in North American history.
Several regional leaders discussed forming a federation of tribes to strengthen their resistance.
Anthropologists studied the traditions and governance systems within the federation of tribes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confederation - a group of people, countries, organizations, etc., that join together for a common purpose
  • Synonym: tribe - a social group consisting of people of the same race, beliefs, or ways of life
