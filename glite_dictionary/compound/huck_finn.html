Huck Finn

1. PROPN

/ hʌk fɪn / (AmE), / hʌk fɪn / (BrE)
the protagonist, a mischievous and adventurous boy, in the novel 'The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn' by Mark Twain
Mark Twain's mischievous boy protagonist

Examples

Many critics consider Huck Finn to be the embodiment of youthful rebellion.
She called her son a real Huck Finn because of his wild adventures.
The moral journey of Huck Finn has inspired generations of readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tom Sawyer - Huck Finn's friend and fellow adventurer in novels by Mark Twain
