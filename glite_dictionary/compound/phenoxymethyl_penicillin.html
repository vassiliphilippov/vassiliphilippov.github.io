phenoxymethyl penicillin

1. NOUN

/ fiˌnɑksiˌmɛθəl pɛˈnɪsɪlɪn / (AmE), / fiˌnɒksɪˌmiːθəl pəˈnɪsɪlɪn / (BrE)
a crystalline antibiotic similar in action to penicillin G but more resistant to stomach acids, often used to treat bacterial infections
antibiotic resistant to gastric acid
medical

Examples

Doctors may prescribe phenoxymethyl penicillin for minor throat infections caused by susceptible bacteria.
Unlike penicillin G, phenoxymethyl penicillin can be taken orally because it is stable in stomach acid.
The patient's infection responded quickly to a course of phenoxymethyl penicillin tablets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: penicillin G - the original form of penicillin antibiotic, less resistant to gastric acid
  • Synonym: antibiotic - a substance used to kill or inhibit bacteria

Etymology

From phenoxy- (a chemical prefix), methyl (a chemical group), and penicillin (from the antibiotic's base structure).
