Coffey still

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑfi stɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒfi stɪl / (BrE)
A type of continuous still used for the industrial fractional distillation of ethanol from fermentation, especially in spirits production.
apparatus for industrial distillation of ethanol

Examples

The distillery installed a new Coffey still to increase its whisky production.
Unlike traditional pot stills, a Coffey still operates continuously and is highly efficient.
Many grain whiskies are produced using a Coffey still rather than the older batch process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column still - a still that allows continuous distillation using columns
  • Antonym: pot still - a type of distillation apparatus used especially in traditional distilling

Etymology

Named after Aeneas Coffey, the Irish inventor who patented it in 1830.
