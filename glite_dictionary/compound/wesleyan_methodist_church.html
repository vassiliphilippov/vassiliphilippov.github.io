Wesleyan Methodist Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛzli.ən ˈmɛθədɪst tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɛzli.ən ˈmɛθədɪst tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The original Methodist Church founded on the teachings of John Wesley, which became a major Protestant denomination.
founding Methodist denomination

Examples

The Wesleyan Methodist Church played a pivotal role in social reform during the 19th century.
Many rural communities still maintain historic Wesleyan Methodist Church buildings.
She attended Sunday services at the local Wesleyan Methodist Church every week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Methodist Church - a Protestant denomination founded on Wesleyan teachings

Etymology

Named after John Wesley (1703–1791), founder of Methodism.
