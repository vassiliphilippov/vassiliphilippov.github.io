slate club

1. NOUN

/ sleɪt klʌb / (AmE), / sleɪt klʌb / (BrE)
a group of people who regularly contribute money to a common fund, typically distributed among members at Christmas or for a specific purpose
group saving club, usually for Christmas
british

Examples

Members paid into the slate club each week to receive a payout just before Christmas.
My grandmother joined a slate club at her local church for holiday savings.
The village slate club has helped families manage their expenses during the festive season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saving club - a group where members save money together for future distribution

Etymology

From 'slate', referring to the slate used to keep accounts, and 'club' (association of people).
