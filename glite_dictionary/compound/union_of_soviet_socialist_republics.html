Union of Soviet Socialist Republics

1. PROPN

/ ˈjun.jən əv soʊˈvi.ət ˈsoʊ.ʃə.lɪst rɪˈpʌb.lɪks / (AmE), / ˈjuː.njən əv səʊˈviː.ət ˈsəʊ.ʃə.lɪst rɪˈpʌb.lɪks / (BrE)
the official name of the USSR, a former communist country in Eastern Europe and Northern Asia, established in 1922 and dissolved in 1991.
official name of former USSR (1922–1991)

Examples

The Union of Soviet Socialist Republics was officially established in 1922 after a series of revolutionary events.
Many Cold War events centered around the actions of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics.
After the dissolution of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, several independent countries emerged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: USSR - abbreviation for Union of Soviet Socialist Republics
  • Synonym: Soviet Union - another common name for the same former country
