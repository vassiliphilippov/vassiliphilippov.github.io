Monterey Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑːn.təˈreɪ ˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.təˈreɪ ˈbeɪ / (BrE)
an inlet of the Pacific Ocean located on the central coast of California, known for its rich marine life and scenic beauty
inlet of the Pacific Ocean in California

Examples

The marine sanctuary in Monterey Bay is home to hundreds of unique animal species.
Each summer, tourists flock to Monterey Bay for whale watching and kayaking adventures.
Researchers at the aquarium study the delicate ecosystem of Monterey Bay year-round.

Etymology

Named after Sebastián Vizcaíno's title, Count of Monterey, from Spanish 'Monterrey'.
