calcium lactate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm ˈlæk.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælsɪəm ˈlæk.teɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt made by the reaction of lactic acid with calcium carbonate, used as a food additive and as a calcium supplement in medicine
white crystalline salt used in food and medicine
medical

Examples

The bakery adds calcium lactate to their bread as a dough conditioner.
Doctors sometimes prescribe calcium lactate as a supplement for patients with low calcium levels.
Calcium lactate is often listed as an ingredient in fortified foods.

Etymology

From 'calcium', from Latin 'calx' (lime) + 'lactate', from Latin 'lac, lactis' (milk).
