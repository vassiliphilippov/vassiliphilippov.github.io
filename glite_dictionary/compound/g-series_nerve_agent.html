G-series nerve agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒi ˌsɪriːz ˈnɜrv ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː ˌsɪəriːz ˈnɜːv ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a group of toxic nerve agents first synthesized before or during World War II, including tabun, sarin, and soman, characterized by high toxicity and rapid action
class of early nerve agents
medical

Examples

During the Cold War, several countries stockpiled G-series nerve agents for military purposes.
Sarin is perhaps the best-known G-series nerve agent and was first developed in Germany in the 1930s.
Protective equipment is vital when handling even trace amounts of any G-series nerve agent.

Synset Examples

GB was the only G agent that was fielded by the US as a munition, specifically in rockets, aerial bombs, and artillery shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve agent - a toxic chemical causing disruption of the nervous system
  • Degree Higher: V-series nerve agent - a later-developed, more persistent class of nerve agents

Etymology

Named after the German origin and the series of development (A, G, V); term G stands for 'German.'
