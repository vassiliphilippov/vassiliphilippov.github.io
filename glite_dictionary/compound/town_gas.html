town gas

1. NOUN

/ taʊn ɡæs / (AmE), / taʊn ɡas / (BrE)
A gas produced from coal and used for lighting, heating, and cooking, especially in urban areas before the widespread adoption of natural gas.
manufactured coal gas for domestic use

Examples

Before the introduction of natural gas, many homes relied on town gas for heating and lighting.
The factory was converted to use town gas during the early twentieth century.
Leaks from the old town gas mains sometimes caused safety concerns in city neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coal gas - gas obtained by the destructive distillation of coal
  • Antonym: natural gas - flammable gas consisting chiefly of methane, used as fuel
