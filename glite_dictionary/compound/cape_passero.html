Cape Passero

1. PROPN

/ keɪp pəˈsɛroʊ / (AmE), / keɪp pəˈsɛrəʊ / (BrE)
A cape that forms the southeastern corner of the island of Sicily, Italy.
cape at southeast tip of Sicily

Examples

The fishing boats sailed past Cape Passero on their way to deeper waters.
Strong winds are common near Cape Passero, making navigation challenging for small vessels.
During the 1943 Allied invasion of Sicily, landings occurred close to Cape Passero.

Etymology

From Italian 'Capo Passero', meaning 'Sparrow Cape'.
