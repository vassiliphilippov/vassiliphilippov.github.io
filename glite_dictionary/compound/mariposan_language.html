Mariposan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈpoʊzən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈpəʊzən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Penutian language spoken by the Yokuts people in California's San Joaquin Valley
a language spoken by the Yokuts in California

Examples

The Mariposan language was once spoken by the Yokuts in California's Central Valley.
Linguists have studied the Mariposan language to understand the cultural history of the Yokuts people.
Many words from the Mariposan language have been preserved in historical documents and research archives.

Etymology

Named after Mariposa County, California, where the language was spoken; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
