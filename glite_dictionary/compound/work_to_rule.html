work to rule

1. NOUN

/ wɝk tə ruːl / (AmE), / wɜːk tə ruːl / (BrE)
a form of industrial action where employees slow down work by strictly following all rules and regulations, doing only the minimum required by their contracts
industrial action by minimal compliance with rules

Examples

The union threatened to work to rule if management refused to negotiate.
As part of their protest, employees began to work to rule, causing delays in production.
When the dispute was not resolved, staff decided to work to rule rather than strike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - a refusal to work as a form of protest
  • Synonym: slowdown - a deliberate reduction in the rate of work
  • Antonym: lockout - an exclusion of employees by their employer from their workplace

2. VERB

/ wɝːk tə ruːl / (AmE), / wɜːk tə ruːl / (BrE)
to follow all official rules and regulations very strictly as a form of protest, especially by employees in order to reduce efficiency without striking
protest by rigidly following rules at work

Examples

The union advised its members to work to rule as a protest against the management’s new policy.
Faced with unfair conditions, staff began to work to rule rather than strike.
When negotiations failed, the workers decided to work to rule to pressure the company.

Synset Examples

They opted to work to rule to emphasize their concerns.
The employees decided to work to rule during the strike.
During the protest, they chose to work to rule strictly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - a refusal to work as a form of protest
