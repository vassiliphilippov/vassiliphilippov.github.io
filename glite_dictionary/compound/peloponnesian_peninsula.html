Peloponnesian Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛləpəˈniʒən pəˈnɪnsələ / (AmE), / ˌpɛləpəˈniːzɪən pəˈnɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
the large peninsula in southern Greece, noted for its historical significance and ancient cities such as Sparta
large southern peninsula of Greece

Examples

The ancient city-state of Sparta was located on the Peloponnesian Peninsula.
Many important battles of Greek history took place on the Peloponnesian Peninsula.
Tourists often visit archaeological sites across the Peloponnesian Peninsula to learn about ancient Greece.

Etymology

From Peloponnēsos, the Greek name for the peninsula, meaning 'island of Pelops', a figure from Greek mythology.
