to broadcast live on air

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæst laɪv ɑn ɛr / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːst laɪv ɒn eə / (BrE)
to transmit a program or event to the public in real-time using radio or television
transmit in real-time on TV or radio

Examples

The concert will be broadcast live on air so fans everywhere can listen as it happens.
Major sporting events are often broadcast live on air to reach the widest possible audience.
The president's speech was broadcast live on air across multiple national networks.

Synset Examples

The show will be broadcast live on air tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit a program by radio or television
