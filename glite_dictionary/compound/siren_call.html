siren call

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪrən kɔl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪərən kɔːl / (BrE)
an appeal that is alluring but potentially dangerous or deceptive
an alluring but dangerous appeal

Examples

She couldn't resist the siren call of adventure, despite the risks involved.
For many investors, the siren call of quick profits leads to poor decisions.
Even experienced hikers sometimes fall for the siren call of unexplored trails.

Synset Examples

he succumbed to the siren call of the wilderness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temptation - something that entices or allures, especially to do something wrong or unwise
  • Antonym: warning - an indication or advice of impending danger

Etymology

From the mythological Sirens of Greek legend, whose enchanting singing lured sailors to their doom.
