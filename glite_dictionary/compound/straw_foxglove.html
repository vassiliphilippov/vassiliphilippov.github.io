straw foxglove

1. NOUN

/ strɔ fɑksˌɡlʌv / (AmE), / strɔː ˈfɒksˌɡlʌv / (BrE)
A European plant with yellow, tubular flowers, belonging to the foxglove family.
European yellow-flowered foxglove plant

Examples

The straw foxglove brightened the meadow with its delicate yellow blooms.
Gardeners appreciate the straw foxglove for its ability to thrive in poor soils.
Unlike the common purple foxglove, the straw foxglove displays soft yellow flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxglove - a tall plant with spikes of tubular flowers, typically purple, and heart-shaped leaves
