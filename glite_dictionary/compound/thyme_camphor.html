thyme camphor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪm ˈkæmfər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm ˈkæmfə / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline solid used in perfumes, preserving biological specimens, embalming, or medically as a fungicide or antiseptic
crystalline antiseptic compound from thyme
medical

Examples

The laboratory used thyme camphor as a preservative for its botanical specimens.
Thyme camphor is valued in perfumery for its distinctive, aromatic fragrance.
Doctors sometimes employ thyme camphor as an antifungal agent in medical treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thymol - an aromatic compound from thyme, used as an antiseptic

Etymology

"Thyme" from Latin *thymum* (the herb), combined with "camphor" from Arabic *kāfūr*, from Sanskrit *karpūra* (camphor).
