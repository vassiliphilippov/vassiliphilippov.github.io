olive ridley

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑlɪv ˈrɪdli / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪv ˈrɪdli / (BrE)
a small olive-colored sea turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea) found in tropical regions of the Pacific, Indian, and southern Atlantic Oceans
species of olive-colored sea turtle

Examples

The olive ridley is known for its massive nesting aggregations called arribadas.
Researchers monitor olive ridley populations to assess the health of marine ecosystems.
Many conservation groups work to protect olive ridley hatchlings from predators.

Etymology

Named after its olive-colored shell and the naturalist H. Nidham Ridley.
