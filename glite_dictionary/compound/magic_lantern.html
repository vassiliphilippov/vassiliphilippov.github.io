magic lantern

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪk ˈlæntɚn / (AmE), / ˈmædʒɪk ˈlæntən / (BrE)
an early type of image projector that used pictures on glass slides, often lit by a lamp, to display images on a wall or screen
early image projector using slides

Examples

The magic lantern fascinated audiences long before the invention of modern cinema.
At the museum, we saw a restored magic lantern from the nineteenth century.
Lecturers used the magic lantern to illustrate scientific presentations to their audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide projector - a device for projecting photographic slides
