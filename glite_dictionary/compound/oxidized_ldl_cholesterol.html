oxidized LDL cholesterol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksɪˌdaɪzd ˌɛl di ˌɛl koʊˈlɛstərˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪzd ˌɛl diː ˌɛl kəʊˈlɛstərɒl / (BrE)
LDL cholesterol that has been chemically modified by oxidation, which is believed to contribute to the development of atherosclerosis.
LDL cholesterol altered by oxidation
medical

Examples

Doctors are concerned that elevated levels of oxidized LDL cholesterol may increase the risk of heart disease.
A healthy diet rich in antioxidants can help reduce oxidized LDL cholesterol in the bloodstream.
Researchers are developing drugs that specifically target oxidized LDL cholesterol to prevent plaque formation in arteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LDL cholesterol - a type of cholesterol known as low-density lipoprotein, often called 'bad' cholesterol
