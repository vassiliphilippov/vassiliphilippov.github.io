Pennine Chain

1. PROPN

/ pəˈnaɪn tʃeɪn / (AmE), / pəˈnaɪn tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a range of hills in northern England extending from the Scottish border to the River Trent, forming the main watershed for English rivers
range of hills in northern England
british

Examples

The Pennine Chain is often called the 'backbone of England' due to its central position.
Hikers enjoy traversing the Pennine Chain for its scenic landscapes and varied wildlife.
Many rivers in England have their sources in the Pennine Chain.

Etymology

From 'Pennine', possibly from Latin *penninus* (relating to mountains), and 'chain' meaning a series or line of hills.
