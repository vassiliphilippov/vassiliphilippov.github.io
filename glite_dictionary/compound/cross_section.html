cross section

1. NOUN

/ krɑs ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
a group or sample chosen to represent the wider population or the range of something being studied
sample representing a whole population

Examples

The survey interviewed a cross section of the local community to get diverse opinions.
Researchers selected a cross section of students from different grades for the study.
The exhibit attracted a cross section of art lovers, ranging from teenagers to retirees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a small part intended to show what the whole is like

2. NOUN

/ krɑs ˈsɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈsɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
(physics) A measure of the probability that a specific interaction, such as capture or ionization, will occur between particles, typically quantified in barns.
probability measure of particle interaction

Examples

The total cross section for neutron capture was measured in the experiment.
Understanding the cross section is crucial for calculating reaction rates in nuclear physics.
A larger cross section indicates a higher probability of interaction between the particles.

Etymology

From the use of 'section' to indicate a cut or slice, and its extension in physics to probability measures.

3. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
A surface or shape that is obtained by cutting through an object, especially at right angles to its longest axis.
section made by a perpendicular cut

Examples

The engineer examined the cross section of the pipe to check for corrosion.
This diagram shows the cross section of a tree trunk, revealing its growth rings.
The architect provided a detailed cross section of the building in the blueprints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slice - a thin, broad piece cut from a larger object
