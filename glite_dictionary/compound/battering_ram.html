battering ram

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ ræm / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ər.ɪŋ ræm / (BrE)
A heavy beam or similar device used to break down the doors or walls of fortified buildings, originally by being swung or rammed against them.
device for breaking down doors or walls

Examples

The police used a battering ram to break down the reinforced front door during the raid.
Ancient armies employed a battering ram to breach the city gates.
The old castle's thick walls were built to withstand attacks by a battering ram.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breaching tool - device used by police or military to force open doors or barriers

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'batter' (to strike repeatedly) and 'ram' (the animal, from its use in ramming), referencing the forceful striking motion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbætərɪŋ ræm / (AmE), / ˈbætərɪŋ ræm / (BrE)
A heavy object, often with a metal head, used to break down doors or walls; also, a person or thing that forcefully overcomes obstacles.
heavy object for breaking barriers; forceful person/thing

Examples

The police used a battering ram to smash through the reinforced door.
He acted as a battering ram during negotiations, relentlessly pushing his agenda.
Firefighters brought out the battering ram in an attempt to rescue people trapped inside.

Synset Examples

He felt like a battering ram, charging through the crowd.
The firefighters used a battering ram to break down the door.
The police deployed a battering ram during the standoff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ram - A similar device used for striking or breaking through objects

Etymology

From 'batter' (to strike repeatedly) and 'ram' (a male sheep), originally referring to ancient siege weaponry.
