historical revisionism

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkəl rɪˈvɪʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəl rɪˈvɪʒənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The reinterpretation or re-examination of historical events, often resulting in a change in the accepted understanding or narrative.
reinterpretation of historical events

Examples

Many scholars engage in historical revisionism to provide new perspectives on well-known events.
Accusations of historical revisionism often arise when new research challenges widely held beliefs.
The documentary was accused of promoting historical revisionism by omitting key facts.

Synset Examples

The book was criticized for its historical revisionism.
Historical revisionism can reshape public perception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revisionist history - an account of history that revises the traditional view
