myotonia congenita

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈtoʊ.ni.ə kənˈdʒɛn.ɪ.t̬ə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈtəʊ.ni.ə kɒnˈdʒɛn.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
a mild, rare hereditary disorder characterized by muscle stiffness due to delayed relaxation after voluntary contraction
rare genetic muscle stiffness disorder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myotonia congenita after reporting muscle stiffness since early childhood.
Individuals with myotonia congenita often experience difficulty relaxing their muscles after exercise.
Genetic counseling may be recommended for families affected by myotonia congenita.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myotonia - delayed muscle relaxation after contraction

Etymology

From 'myotonia' (from Greek 'mys', muscle, and 'tonos', tension) and 'congenita' (from Latin 'congenitus', born with), meaning 'muscle tension present from birth'.
