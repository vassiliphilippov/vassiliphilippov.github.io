family Castoridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kæˈstɔrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kæsˈtɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, semi-aquatic rodents commonly known as beavers, native to North America and Eurasia.
the beaver family (rodent, animal)

Examples

The family Castoridae comprises only two living species: the North American beaver and the Eurasian beaver.
In zoology, all members of the family Castoridae exhibit webbed hind feet and a broad, flat tail.
Researchers studied the dam-building behavior unique to rodents in the family Castoridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaver - large aquatic rodent of genus Castor

Etymology

From scientific Latin; 'Castoridae' derives from genus 'Castor' and the family suffix '-idae'.
