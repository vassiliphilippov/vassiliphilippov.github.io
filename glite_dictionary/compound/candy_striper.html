candy striper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændi ˌstraɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkændi ˌstraɪpə / (BrE)
a young volunteer, usually a girl or young woman, who assists in a hospital, especially in the United States
young hospital volunteer (mainly US)
american

Examples

When she was sixteen, Maria volunteered as a candy striper at the local hospital.
The hospital relies on candy stripers to help with non-medical tasks and patient support.
During summer breaks, many teenagers apply to be candy stripers to gain experience in healthcare.

Etymology

From the striped uniforms resembling candy stripes worn by such volunteers.
