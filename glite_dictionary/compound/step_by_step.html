step by step

1. ADV

/ ˌstɛp baɪ ˈstɛp / (AmE), / ˌstɛp baɪ ˈstɛp / (BrE)
in a gradual manner; proceeding steadily or one stage at a time
in a gradual, steady manner

Examples

The project will be completed step by step, ensuring every detail is checked.
She learned the new language step by step, starting with basic phrases and then grammar.
We need to approach this problem step by step rather than rushing.

Synset Examples

the snake moved gradually toward its victim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradually - in a slow, incremental manner
Source: WordNet

2. ADV

/ ˌstɛp baɪ ˈstɛp / (AmE), / ˌstɛp baɪ ˈstɛp / (BrE)
gradually and in a sequence of distinct stages; proceeding one stage at a time
gradually, stage by stage

Examples

She explained the process step by step so everyone could understand.
We followed the instructions step by step to assemble the furniture correctly.
Learning a new language happens step by step, not overnight.

Synset Examples

the voltage was increased stepwise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradually - slowly or in gradual stages
  • Antonym: all at once - happening suddenly and not in stages

/ stɛp baɪ stɛp / (AmE), / stɛp baɪ stɛp / (BrE)
gradually or in a series of stages, making progress slowly and carefully
gradually, in stages

Examples

We built our company step by step over many years.
If you take it step by step, learning a new language won't feel so overwhelming.

Synset Examples
