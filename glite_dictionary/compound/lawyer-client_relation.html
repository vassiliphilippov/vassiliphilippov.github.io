lawyer-client relation

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪər ˈklaɪənt rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɔːjə ˈklaɪənt rɪˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the professional relationship in which a lawyer is obligated to act in the best interests of their client
professional duty between lawyer and client
legal

Examples

It is unethical for a lawyer to breach the lawyer-client relation by revealing private information.
The integrity of the lawyer-client relation is fundamental to the legal system.
Confidentiality is a key aspect of the lawyer-client relation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney-client privilege - the legal right of a client to confidentiality with their attorney
