yellow parilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛloʊ pəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / ˌjɛləʊ pəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
a woody vine native to eastern North America with large oval leaves, small white flowers, and purple to blue-black fruits
woody North American vine with oval leaves and dark fruits

Examples

The herbalist recommended yellow parilla as part of the treatment for certain ailments.
We found yellow parilla climbing along the fence at the forest's edge.
In spring, the yellow parilla covers itself with clusters of small white flowers.

Etymology

From Spanish 'parrilla', diminutive of 'parra' (vine).
