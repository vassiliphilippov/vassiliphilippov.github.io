earning per share

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrnɪŋ pər ʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪŋ pə ʃeə / (BrE)
the portion of a company's profit allocated to each outstanding share of common stock, used as an indicator of a company's profitability
profit per company share

Examples

The company's earning per share rose significantly last quarter, reflecting improved profitability.
Many investors use earning per share as a key metric when analyzing companies.
An increasing earning per share often signals that a company is growing in value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: EPS - abbreviation for earning per share
