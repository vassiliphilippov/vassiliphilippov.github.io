adjutant bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒə.tənt bɝd / (AmE), / ˈædʒə.tənt bɜːd / (BrE)
a large stork found in India, notable for its tall stature and formal, upright gait, resembling that of a military adjutant
large Indian stork with upright gait

Examples

The adjutant bird is easily recognized in India by its tall stance and bare head.
We spotted an adjutant bird wading through the marshes near the village.
The zoo recently welcomed a pair of adjutant birds to their new aviary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marabou stork - large African stork related to the adjutant bird

Etymology

Named for its supposed resemblance to a military adjutant's gait.
