sex chromosome

1. NOUN

/ sɛks ˈkɹoʊ.məˌsoʊm / (AmE), / sɛks ˈkɹəʊ.mə.səʊm / (BrE)
a chromosome that determines the sex of an individual organism
chromosome determining an organism's sex
medical

Examples

In humans, the presence of a Y sex chromosome typically results in a male individual.
Birds have a different system of sex chromosomes than mammals do.
Scientists studied the evolution of the sex chromosome in different species.

Synset Examples

mammals normally have two sex chromosomes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autosome - a chromosome not directly involved in determining sex

Etymology

From 'sex', referring to biological gender, and 'chromosome', from Greek 'khrōma' (color) and 'sōma' (body).
