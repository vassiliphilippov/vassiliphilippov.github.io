reentering angle

1. NOUN

/ riˈɛntərɪŋ ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / riˈɛntərɪŋ ˈaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
An interior angle of a polygon that is greater than 180 degrees.
angle in a polygon greater than 180°

Examples

A star-shaped polygon often has at least one reentering angle in its structure.
The architect had to account for the reentering angle at the corner of the building when designing the façade.
Polygons with a reentering angle appear in advanced geometry problems.

Etymology

From 'reenter', meaning to turn inward, and 'angle', from Latin angulus (corner, angle).
