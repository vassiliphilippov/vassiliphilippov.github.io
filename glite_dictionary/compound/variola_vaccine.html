variola vaccine

1. NOUN

/ vəˈraɪ.ə.lə vækˈsiːn / (AmE), / vəˈraɪ.ə.lə vækˈsiːn / (BrE)
a vaccine prepared from the virus causing cowpox, used to induce immunity to smallpox in humans; typically produces a localized infection and a scar
vaccine against smallpox using cowpox virus
medical

Examples

The introduction of the variola vaccine was a turning point in the fight against smallpox.
After receiving the variola vaccine, patients often noticed a small scar at the site of inoculation.
In the 19th century, widespread use of the variola vaccine greatly reduced smallpox outbreaks worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox vaccine - a general term for any vaccine protecting against smallpox

Etymology

From Latin 'variola' (smallpox) and 'vaccine', from Latin 'vacca' (cow), referencing the cowpox virus used in immunization.
