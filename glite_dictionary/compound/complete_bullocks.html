complete bullocks

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplit ˈbʊləks / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːt ˈbʊləks / (BrE)
nonsense; something that is untrue or without value
nonsense; falsehoods (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

The report he gave was complete bullocks from start to finish.
Don't listen to her excuses; they're complete bullocks.
He claimed he met the Queen, but that's complete bullocks.

Synset Examples

Their excuses for being late were complete bullocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Synonym: rubbish - nonsense or something untrue, especially in British English

Etymology

Alteration of 'bollocks', British slang meaning 'nonsense'.
