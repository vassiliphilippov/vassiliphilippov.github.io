Labrador retriever

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæbrədɔːr rɪˈtriːvər / (AmE), / ˈlæbrədɔː rɪˈtriːvə / (BrE)
A large, short-haired breed of retriever originally from Newfoundland, typically with a black, yellow, or chocolate coat, known for its intelligence and gentle nature.
breed of large retriever dog

Examples

The Labrador retriever is one of the most popular family dogs in the United States.
She adopted a playful Labrador retriever from the animal shelter last year.
Their Labrador retriever loves to swim and play fetch at the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden retriever - a large, intelligent breed of retriever with a golden coat
  • Degree Higher: dog - domesticated carnivorous mammal, Canis familiaris

Etymology

From Labrador (Canadian region) and 'retriever' (dog that retrieves); breed developed from dogs in Newfoundland and Labrador.
