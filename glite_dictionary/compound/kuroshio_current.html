Kuroshio current

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈroʊʃioʊ ˈkɝːənt / (AmE), / kʊˌrɒʃiˈəʊ ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a warm ocean current flowing northeastward off the coast of Japan into the northern Pacific Ocean
warm ocean current off Japan

Examples

Fishermen benefit from the Kuroshio current bringing warm waters to the Japanese coast.
The path of the Kuroshio current influences the climate of the nearby regions.
Scientists study the Kuroshio current to understand how it affects marine biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gulf Stream - a warm ocean current in the Atlantic Ocean
  • Antonym: Oyashio current - a cold subarctic ocean current off the coast of Japan

Etymology

From Japanese 'Kuroshio' (黒潮), meaning 'black tide' or 'black current'.
