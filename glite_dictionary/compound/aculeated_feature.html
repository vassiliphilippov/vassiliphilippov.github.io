aculeated feature

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːliˌeɪtɪd ˈfiːtʃər / (AmE), / əˈkjuːliːeɪtɪd ˈfiːtʃə / (BrE)
a sharp or stinging structure, especially in animals or plants, used for defense or attack
sharp or stinging defensive structure

Examples

The wasp's aculeated feature enables it to deter predators effectively.
Many desert plants have evolved aculeated features to protect themselves from herbivores.
The scientist pointed out the aculeated feature on the insect's tail during the lecture.

Synset Examples

Some insects have aculeated features for self-defense.

Etymology

From Latin aculeatus (prickly), from aculeus (sting, spine) + feature.
