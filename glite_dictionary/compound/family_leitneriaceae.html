family Leitneriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaemɪli laɪtˌnɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli laɪtˌnɪəriˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants consisting solely of the genus Leitneria, often classified in its own distinct order.
plant family containing the genus Leitneria

Examples

The family Leitneriaceae is notable for having only a single genus, Leitneria.
Botanists often place the family Leitneriaceae in a distinct order due to its unique characteristics.
Researchers have studied the wood anatomy of plants in the family Leitneriaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leitneria - genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees forming the family Leitneriaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Leitneria, named after Austrian naturalist E.F. Leitner, with the botanical family suffix -aceae.
