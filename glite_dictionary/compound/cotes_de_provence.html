Cotes de Provence

1. NOUN

/ koʊt də proʊˈvɑ̃s / (AmE), / kəʊt də prɒˈvɑ̃s / (BrE)
A type of wine produced in the Côtes de Provence region of southeastern France, known for its rosés and located along the Mediterranean coast.
wine from southeastern France's Provence region

Examples

We enjoyed a chilled bottle of Cotes de Provence with our seafood dinner.
The wine list featured several varieties of Cotes de Provence from renowned vineyards.
She recommended pairing the light flavors of Cotes de Provence with summer salads.

Etymology

French, literally 'slopes of Provence'.
