gold leaf

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld liːf / (AmE), / ɡəʊld liːf / (BrE)
very thin sheets of gold, often used for decoration or gilding.
very thin sheet of gold

Examples

The artist carefully applied gold leaf to the frame for a luxurious finish.
The ancient manuscript was decorated with intricate patterns in gold leaf.
Chefs sometimes use gold leaf to garnish desserts for an elegant touch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gold foil - thin sheet of gold, typically thicker than gold leaf
