silver bromide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.vɚ ˈbroʊˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.və ˈbrəʊ.maɪd / (BrE)
a white or pale yellow salt (AgBr) that darkens upon exposure to light, chiefly used in photographic emulsions
light-sensitive chemical used in photography

Examples

Early photographic films relied on silver bromide crystals to capture images.
The sensitivity of silver bromide to light makes it essential in black-and-white photography.
Chemists studied how silver bromide darkens when exposed to sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver chloride - a light-sensitive chemical compound used in photography
  • Synonym: silver nitrate - a compound used in photography and medicine
  • Degree Lower: sodium bromide - a related bromide compound

Etymology

Silver from Old English ‘seolfor’, and bromide from ‘bromine’ + ‘-ide’.
