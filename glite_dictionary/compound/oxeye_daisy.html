oxeye daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑkˌsaɪ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈɒksaɪ ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a tall Eurasian perennial plant with white, daisy-like flowers, widely naturalized in many regions
tall Eurasian daisy-like flower

Examples

The oxeye daisy is easily recognized by its white petals and yellow center.
She planted several oxeye daisies in the wildflower garden to attract butterflies.
In many fields, the oxeye daisy blooms abundantly throughout the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marguerite - a similar daisy-like flower; often regarded as a synonym for oxeye daisy

Etymology

From 'oxeye' (Middle English 'oxeie'), meaning an eye resembling that of an ox, and 'daisy'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑkˌsaɪ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈɒks.aɪ ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a common wildflower with white petals and a yellow center, belonging to the species Leucanthemum vulgare
wildflower with white petals and yellow center

Examples

A cluster of oxeye daisy bloomed along the roadside in early summer.
The oxeye daisy is often found in meadows and fields across Europe and North America.
Gardeners sometimes plant oxeye daisy for its cheerful, classic appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - a common name for plants of the family Asteraceae with white petals and yellow centers

Etymology

From 'oxeye', originally referring to its prominent yellow center, and 'daisy', from Old English 'dæges ēage' (day's eye).
