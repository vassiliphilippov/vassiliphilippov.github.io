little potato

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
A disease of potatoes caused by rhizoctonia fungus, which results in stunted or abnormal tubers.
potato disease (rhizoctonia)

Examples

Some farmers lost entire crops due to the spread of little potato in the wet season.
Symptoms of little potato include small, misshapen tubers with black scurf.
Controlling little potato requires crop rotation and certified seed potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhizoctonia disease - fungal disease of potatoes caused by Rhizoctonia solani
