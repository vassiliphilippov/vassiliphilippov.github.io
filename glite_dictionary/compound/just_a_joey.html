just a joey

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌst ə ˈdʒoʊ.i / (AmE), / dʒʌst ə ˈdʒəʊ.i / (BrE)
a young or inexperienced person; someone new to a group or activity
young or inexperienced person
informal

Examples

Everyone was patient with me at first because I was just a joey in the company.
At the first rehearsal, she looked like just a joey compared to the older dancers.
It's tough being just a joey on your first day at school.

Synset Examples

That new guy is just a joey trying to fit in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation
  • Synonym: rookie - a person new to an activity or organization, especially in sports or the military
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with much experience in a particular field

Etymology

From 'joey', Australian term for a young kangaroo, extended metaphorically to refer to inexperienced people.
