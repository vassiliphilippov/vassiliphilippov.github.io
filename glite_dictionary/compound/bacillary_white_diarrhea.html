bacillary white diarrhea

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪləri waɪt daɪəˈriə / (AmE), / bəˈsɪləri waɪt dɪəˈrɪə / (BrE)
a serious bacterial disease affecting young chickens, characterized by white diarrhea and high mortality
bacterial disease in young chickens
medical

Examples

The poultry farmer was alarmed to find bacillary white diarrhea spreading rapidly among his young chicks.
Effective sanitation is crucial to prevent bacillary white diarrhea outbreaks in chicken coops.
Veterinarians warned that bacillary white diarrhea can cause high losses if not treated promptly.

Etymology

From 'bacillary', relating to rod-shaped bacteria, and 'diarrhea', from Greek 'diarrhoia'.
