ordeal bean

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈdil bin / (AmE), / ɔːˈdiːl biːn / (BrE)
the highly poisonous dark brown seed of the Calabar bean vine, used traditionally in West African ordeals and as a source of the drug physostigmine
poisonous seed of the Calabar bean vine

Examples

The poison from the ordeal bean was once used to determine guilt in tribal trials.
Researchers extracted physostigmine from the ordeal bean to study its effects on the nervous system.
The ordeal bean is notorious for being deadly, yet has important medicinal applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calabar bean - seed of the African climbing plant Physostigma venenosum

Etymology

From its use in traditional 'ordeals' in West African trials; 'bean' from Old English 'bēan'.
