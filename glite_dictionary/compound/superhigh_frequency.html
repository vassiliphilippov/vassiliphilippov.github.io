superhigh frequency

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈhaɪ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈhaɪ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (BrE)
a radio frequency in the range between 3 and 30 gigahertz, used in radar, microwave communication, and satellite transmission
radio frequency between 3 and 30 gigahertz

Examples

Microwaves operate in the superhigh frequency range, making them suitable for satellite communications.
Many modern radar systems rely on superhigh frequency signals to detect aircraft and ships.
The 5G network utilizes part of the superhigh frequency spectrum for faster data transmission.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ultrahigh frequency - radio frequency immediately below superhigh frequency, 300 to 3,000 megahertz
  • Degree Higher: extremely high frequency - radio frequency band above superhigh frequency, 30 to 300 gigahertz
