cuneate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːni.eɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈkjuːni.eɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf shaped like a wedge, with a narrow, acute angle at its base.
wedge-shaped leaf with acute base

Examples

The cuneate leaf is easily identified by its tapered base.
Many species in this genus are characterized by a cuneate leaf shape.
Botanists use the presence of a cuneate leaf as a key identifying feature.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: obcordate leaf - a leaf shaped like an inverted heart with the notch at the apex

Etymology

From Latin cuneatus (wedge-shaped), from cuneus (wedge).
