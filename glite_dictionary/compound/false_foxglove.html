false foxglove

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔls ˈfɑɡzˌlʌv / (AmE), / ˈfɔːls ˈfɒksˌɡlʌv / (BrE)
A North American plant with solitary dark golden-yellow flowers that resemble those of the foxglove, sometimes classified in the genus Gerardia.
North American plant with foxglove-like yellow flowers

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of the false foxglove attract many pollinators in late summer.
We saw a patch of false foxglove growing along the edge of the forest trail.
Unlike true foxgloves, false foxglove prefers sandy, open areas in the wild.
