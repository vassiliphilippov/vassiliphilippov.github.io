genus Taraxacum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌtær.əˈzæk.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌtær.əˈzæk.əm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Compositae, which includes dandelions
genus of plants including dandelions

Examples

Many species within the genus Taraxacum are commonly referred to as dandelions.
The genus Taraxacum includes a variety of plants found throughout temperate regions worldwide.
Botanists have documented over 2,000 different forms within the genus Taraxacum alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - a common flowering plant with yellow heads, part of genus Taraxacum

Etymology

From New Latin *Taraxacum*, the scientific name for dandelion, possibly from Arabic *tarakhshaqūn* (dandelion).
