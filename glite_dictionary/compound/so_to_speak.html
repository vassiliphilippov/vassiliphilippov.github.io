so to speak

1. ADV

/ soʊ tə spiːk / (AmE), / səʊ tə spiːk / (BrE)
used to indicate that a word or phrase is not to be understood literally, but is used in a figurative or imaginative way.
to speak figuratively

Examples

The meeting was a wake-up call, so to speak, for everyone involved.
He's the backbone of the team, so to speak.
We had to start from scratch, so to speak, after the data was lost.

Synset Examples

the feeling is, as we say, quite dead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: as it were - used to indicate the same figurative meaning as 'so to speak'
