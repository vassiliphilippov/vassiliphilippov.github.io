dehydrated foods

1. NOUN

/ diˈhaɪdreɪtɪd fuːdz / (AmE), / diˈhaɪdreɪtɪd fuːdz / (BrE)
Foods that have had their water content removed to preserve them or make them easier to store and transport.
foods preserved by removing water

Examples

Hikers often pack dehydrated foods because they weigh less and last longer on the trail.
To prepare the soup, just add water to the dehydrated foods in the packet.
Many emergency kits include dehydrated foods in case of natural disasters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dried foods - foods preserved by drying
  • Antonym: fresh foods - foods not preserved or processed
