cardinal tetra

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdɪnəl ˈtɛtrə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl ˈtɛtrə / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored aquarium fish (Paracheirodon axelrodi) with vivid red and blue stripes, native to streams in Brazil and Colombia
a small, vividly colored aquarium fish

Examples

I added a new cardinal tetra to my aquarium last week.
The vivid colors of the cardinal tetra make it a favorite among fish enthusiasts.
We observed a school of cardinal tetras swimming gracefully under the aquarium light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neon tetra - a similar small aquarium fish with blue and red coloring (Paracheirodon innesi)

Etymology

From 'cardinal,' referring to its bright red color, and 'tetra,' a common name for small characin fishes.
