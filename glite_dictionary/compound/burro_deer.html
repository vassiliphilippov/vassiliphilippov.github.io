burro deer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜːroʊ dɪr / (AmE), / ˈbʊərəʊ dɪə / (BrE)
a mule deer of western North America, notable for its large ears and two-pronged antlers
a mule deer of western North America

Examples

We spotted a burro deer grazing near the edge of the forest.
The burro deer gets its name from its unusually large ears, resembling those of a donkey.
Unlike other deer, the burro deer has distinctive forked antlers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mule deer - a large deer of western North America with large ears

Etymology

From Spanish 'burro' (donkey), referring to the deer’s donkey-like ears.
