genus Pteretis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ptəˈriːtɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ptəˈriː.tɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of ferns sometimes included in the genus Onoclea; in some classifications, both are placed in the family Polypodiaceae.
genus of ferns, sometimes with Onoclea

Examples

Several species in the genus Pteretis are native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.
Some botanists now classify genus Pteretis under the broader group Onoclea.
Research papers discuss the taxonomic differences between genus Pteretis and closely related genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Onoclea - a related genus of ferns sometimes grouped with Pteretis

Etymology

Pteretis from Greek 'pteretis', meaning 'fern'; used in botanical Latin classification.
