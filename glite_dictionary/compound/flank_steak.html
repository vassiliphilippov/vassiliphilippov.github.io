flank steak

1. NOUN

/ flæŋk steɪk / (AmE), / flæŋk steɪk / (BrE)
a cut of beef from the lower chest or abdominal muscles of a cow, known for its rich flavor and coarse texture
cut of beef from the flank

Examples

He grilled a flank steak and sliced it thinly for the tacos.
The recipe calls for marinating the flank steak overnight to tenderize it.
At the butcher's, she asked specifically for a flank steak for her barbecue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skirt steak - a cut of beef from the diaphragm muscles of the cow
  • Degree Higher: sirloin - a cut of beef from the back of the animal, behind the ribs
