petty cash

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛti kæʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛti kaʃ / (BrE)
a small amount of money kept at hand by an organization for minor or incidental expenses
cash fund for minor expenses

Examples

The office manager is responsible for managing the petty cash and reimbursing employees for small expenses.
Receipts must be submitted when requesting money from the petty cash fund.
We use petty cash to buy office supplies and pay for minor repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of coins or notes

Etymology

From 'petty' (small, trivial) and 'cash'; recorded in use since the 18th century for office accounts.
