authorized stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːθəˌraɪzd stɑːk / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəraɪzd stɒk / (BrE)
the maximum number of shares that a corporation is legally allowed to issue, as stated in its articles of incorporation
maximum shares a corporation may issue
legal

Examples

The board decided to increase the authorized stock to allow for future expansion.
Only a portion of the authorized stock has been issued to investors so far.
Changes to the authorized stock must be approved by the shareholders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: issued stock - shares that have actually been issued by a corporation
  • Degree Lower: outstanding stock - shares currently held by shareholders (excluding treasury stock)
  • Synonym: capital stock - the total amount of a company's shares
