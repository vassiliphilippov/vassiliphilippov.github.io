posthypnotic amnesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊst.hɪpˈnɑ.tɪk æmˈni.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst.hɪpˈnɒ.tɪk amˈniː.zɪə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person cannot recall events that occurred during hypnosis or information specified by the hypnotist, typically occurring after the hypnotic session has ended.
memory loss following hypnosis
medical

Examples

After the session, the patient experienced posthypnotic amnesia and could not remember anything discussed during hypnosis.
Researchers studied posthypnotic amnesia to better understand how suggestions affect memory.
The hypnotist gave instructions for posthypnotic amnesia, so the volunteer forgot the questions asked under hypnosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnesia - partial or total loss of memory

Etymology

From 'post-' (after) + 'hypnotic' (relating to hypnosis) + 'amnesia' (memory loss, from Greek 'amnesia', forgetfulness).
