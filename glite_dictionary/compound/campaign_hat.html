campaign hat

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpeɪn hæt / (AmE), / kæmˈpeɪn hæt / (BrE)
a broad-brimmed felt hat with a high crown, traditionally worn as part of the uniform by United States Army and Marine Corps personnel
broad-brimmed felt military hat

Examples

The drill sergeant wore his distinctive campaign hat while addressing the recruits.
A campaign hat is often associated with park rangers and state troopers as well as military personnel.
He placed his campaign hat on the shelf at the end of his shift.

Etymology

From 'campaign', referring to military campaigns, and 'hat'; popularized as military headgear since the late 19th century.
