pre-Jurassic

1. ADJ

/ pri dʒʊˈræsɪk / (AmE), / pri dʒʊəˈræsɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting the time before the Jurassic period in geologic history.
before the Jurassic period (geology)

Examples

Fossils from the pre-Jurassic era are rare and often poorly preserved.
The formation contains rocks that date back to pre-Jurassic times.
Geologists are interested in studying pre-Jurassic strata to understand the Earth's early history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Jurassic - Relating to the Jurassic period in geologic history

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'Jurassic', referring to the Jurassic period of the Mesozoic Era.
