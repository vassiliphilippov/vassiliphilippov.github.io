Fort Knox

1. NOUN

/ fɔrt nɑks / (AmE), / fɔːt nɒks / (BrE)
a place, building, or system that is extremely secure or difficult to access, often used figuratively to describe something that is very well-protected
very secure place or system

Examples

After the upgrade, their computer network was like Fort Knox—almost impossible to hack.
Gaining access to the manager's files is harder than breaking into Fort Knox.
Her diary is guarded like Fort Knox; no one is allowed to read it.

Synset Examples

The bank vault was designed to be as safe as Fort Knox.
He guards his secrets like they're in Fort Knox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - a secure room or compartment for storing valuables
  • Synonym: safe - a strong container or room for protecting valuable things

Etymology

Named after the United States Bullion Depository at Fort Knox, Kentucky, famous for its high security.
