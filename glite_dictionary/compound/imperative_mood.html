imperative mood

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrətɪv muːd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrətɪv mjuːd / (BrE)
the grammatical mood used to express commands, requests, or instructions
grammatical mood for commands or requests

Examples

The imperative mood is used in sentences like 'Sit down!'
In teaching English, instructors often highlight the imperative mood when discussing giving directions.
The verb form changes in the imperative mood to issue a command.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indicative mood - grammatical mood used to state facts
  • Antonym: subjunctive mood - grammatical mood expressing wishes or hypothetical situations

Etymology

From Latin 'imperativus' (commanding) and 'modus' (mood).
