biceps brachii

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪsɛps ˈbreɪki.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪsɛps ˈbreɪki.aɪ / (BrE)
a large muscle in the upper arm that bends the elbow and turns the forearm so the palm faces upward
muscle of the upper arm
medical

Examples

He injured his biceps brachii while lifting heavy weights at the gym.
The biceps brachii contracts to flex the elbow and rotate the forearm.
Doctors examined the patient's biceps brachii after he reported arm pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biceps - the large muscle at the front of the upper arm
  • Antonym: triceps brachii - muscle on the back of the upper arm, responsible for extending the forearm

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'two-headed muscle of the arm' (biceps = two-headed, brachii = of the arm).
