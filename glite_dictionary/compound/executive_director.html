executive director

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv dəˈrɛktər / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɛkjʊtɪv dɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
the person with chief responsibility for the day-to-day management of an organization or business, typically reporting to a board of directors
senior manager of an organization

Examples

As the executive director, Maria oversees all major projects and coordinates the staff.
The company appointed a new executive director to improve its operations.
Every year, the executive director reports progress and challenges to the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chief executive officer - the highest-ranking person in a company or organization, ultimately responsible for making managerial decisions
  • Synonym: managing director - the person responsible for the daily operations of a company, especially in British English
