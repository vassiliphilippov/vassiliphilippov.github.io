pulse oximeter

1. NOUN

/ pʌls ɑkˈsɪmɪtɚ / (AmE), / pʌls ɒkˈsɪmɪtə / (BrE)
A small medical device that non-invasively measures the oxygen saturation of someone's blood, usually by attaching to a fingertip or earlobe.
device for measuring blood oxygen saturation
medical

Examples

The nurse clipped a pulse oximeter to my finger to check my oxygen levels.
Many athletes use a pulse oximeter during training at high altitudes.
Your doctor may recommend using a pulse oximeter at home if you have a respiratory condition.

Synset Examples

A typical pulse oximeter utilizes an electronic processor and a pair of small light-emitting diodes (LEDs) facing a photodiode through a translucent part of the patient's body, usually a fingertip or an earlobe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oximeter - an instrument for measuring oxygen concentration in the blood

Etymology

From 'pulse' (heartbeat) and 'oximeter' (from Greek 'oxys', sharp/acid, and 'metron', measure), referring to measuring oxygen saturation via pulse.
