lacrimal duct

1. NOUN

/ ˈlækrəməl dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈlakrɪməl dʌkt / (BrE)
a small tube that carries tears from the lacrimal gland to the surface of the eye
tear-carrying duct of the eye
medical

Examples

When the lacrimal duct is blocked, tears may overflow onto the cheek.
Doctors examined the patient's lacrimal duct to check for any signs of infection.
Inflammation of the lacrimal duct can cause discomfort and watery eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear duct - the duct that conveys tears from the eye to the nasal cavity

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrima' meaning 'tear' and Latin 'ductus' meaning 'leading' or 'duct'.
