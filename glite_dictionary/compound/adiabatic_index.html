adiabatic index

1. NOUN

/ ˌædiəˈbætɪk ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˌædɪəˈbatɪk ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
the ratio of the heat capacity at constant pressure to the heat capacity at constant volume for a gas, commonly denoted as γ (gamma)
ratio of heat capacities for a gas

Examples

The adiabatic index for an ideal monoatomic gas is typically 5/3.
Understanding the adiabatic index is essential in calculations of engine efficiency.
The change in pressure and temperature during compression can be predicted using the adiabatic index.

Synset Examples

The experimental value for the heat capacity ratio of argon is the same as the theoretical heat capacity ratio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat capacity ratio - another term for adiabatic index, ratio of heat capacities at constant pressure and volume

Etymology

From 'adiabatic', meaning occurring without heat transfer, and 'index', from Latin 'indicare', to point out.
