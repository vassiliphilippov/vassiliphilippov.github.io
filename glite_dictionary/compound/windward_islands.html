Windward Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪndwɚd ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈwɪndwəd ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the southeastern West Indies, forming the southern part of the Lesser Antilles
islands in the southeastern West Indies

Examples

Many Caribbean cruises include a stop at the Windward Islands for their beautiful beaches.
Hurricanes often track through the Windward Islands before heading toward North America.
The culture of the Windward Islands reflects a rich blend of African, European, and indigenous influences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Leeward Islands - a nearby group of islands forming the northern part of the Lesser Antilles

Etymology

From 'windward', referring to being exposed to the prevailing winds, and 'islands'.
