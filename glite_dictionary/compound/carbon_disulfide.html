carbon disulfide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən daɪˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən daɪˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a toxic, colorless, flammable liquid chemical (CS₂) used in making rayon, cellophane, and as a solvent for rubber
toxic chemical solvent (CS₂)
medical

Examples

The factory uses carbon disulfide in the production of synthetic fibers.
Exposure to carbon disulfide can be hazardous without proper ventilation.
Researchers studied the effects of carbon disulfide on the nervous system.

Etymology

Formed from 'carbon' and 'disulfide,' indicating a molecule of one carbon and two sulfur atoms.
