conversion factor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɜːrʒən ˈfæktər / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːʃən ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a ratio or number used to multiply a quantity expressed in one unit to convert it to an equivalent quantity in another unit
number used to convert between units

Examples

To change inches to centimeters, use the conversion factor of 2.54.
The conversion factor from pounds to kilograms is 0.4536.
Scientists rely on accurate conversion factors when performing unit conversions in calculations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coefficient - a number placed before a variable in mathematics
