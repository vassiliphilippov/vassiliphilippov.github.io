fairy cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri kʌp / (AmE), / ˈfeəri kʌp / (BrE)
a small scarlet European fungus with a cup-shaped fruiting body (ascocarp)
a scarlet cup-shaped fungus

Examples

The vibrant fairy cup stood out among the moss on the forest floor.
Scientists discovered several fairy cups growing near the old oak tree.
Children marvelled at the bright red fairy cup during their nature walk.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri ˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈfeəri ˌkʌp / (BrE)
A small perennial plant of northeastern North America, usually with two opposite leaves on upright stems that produce racemes of white flowers; also known as miterwort.
a North American woodland plant; miterwort

Examples

The delicate fairy cup could be seen blooming along the forest path in early spring.
Many gardeners cherish the fairy cup for its tiny white blossoms and unique foliage.
We stopped to admire a patch of fairy cups growing beneath the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miterwort - a woodland plant of North America and East Asia, with white flowers; also called bishop's cap
