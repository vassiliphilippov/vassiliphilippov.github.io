sea poppy

1. NOUN

/ si ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / si ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
A yellow-flowered, glaucous herb of Eurasian origin that grows on sandy shores and is naturalized in eastern North America.
yellow-flowered coastal Eurasian herb

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of the sea poppy stand out against the grey sand dunes.
Botanists observed that sea poppy populations were increasing along the Atlantic coast.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate the sea poppy for its unusual foliage and blooms.
