thick-footed morel

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪk ˌfʊtɪd məˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˈθɪk ˌfʊtɪd mɒˈrɛl / (BrE)
A type of edible morel mushroom distinguished by its thick stem and conical cap with deep, irregular pits.
edible morel mushroom with a thick stem

Examples

The thick-footed morel is prized by gourmet chefs for its distinctive flavor and meaty texture.
After the spring rains, we found several thick-footed morels growing near the base of old oak trees.
Caution is needed when foraging for thick-footed morel to avoid confusing it with toxic lookalikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - any edible mushroom of the genus Morchella
