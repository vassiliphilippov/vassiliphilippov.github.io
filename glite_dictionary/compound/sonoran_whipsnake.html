Sonoran whipsnake

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɔɹən ˈwɪpsneɪk / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrən ˈwɪpsneɪk / (BrE)
A species of slender, fast-moving snake found in both terrestrial and arboreal habitats in the southwestern United States.
a fast, slender snake of the southwestern US

Examples

The Sonoran whipsnake is often seen darting quickly through desert shrubs.
Herpetologists study the Sonoran whipsnake to understand its arboreal habits.
We spotted a Sonoran whipsnake basking on a sunny rock near Tucson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipsnake - any of several slender, fast-moving snakes

Etymology

Named after the Sonoran Desert, part of its natural range, and 'whipsnake' for its slender, whip-like body.
