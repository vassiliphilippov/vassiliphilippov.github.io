blood group

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌd ˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈblʌd ˌɡruːp / (BrE)
a classification of human blood based on the presence or absence of inherited antigens on the surface of red blood cells
classification of blood by antigens
medical

Examples

The doctor needed to determine my blood group before the transfusion.
People with the same blood group can usually donate blood to each other safely.
The rarest blood group in this country is AB negative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood type - a classification of blood based on inherited antigenic substances
