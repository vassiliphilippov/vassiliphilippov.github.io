2. PROPN

/ ˈleɪbɚ ˌpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
A major political party in the United Kingdom, established in 1900, traditionally associated with representing workers' interests and promoting social democratic policies.
British political party representing workers' interests
british

Examples

The Labour Party introduced significant social reforms after winning the 1945 general election.
Many young voters are showing renewed interest in the Labour Party.
The Labour Party's platform focuses on public services, the environment, and workers' rights.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Conservative Party - The main opposing political party in the United Kingdom, representing center-right policies.
  • Synonym: Labour - Relating to the political party or movement advocating workers' interests.

Etymology

From 'labour' (work) and 'party', referencing its origins advocating for the working class; officially formed in Britain, 1900.
