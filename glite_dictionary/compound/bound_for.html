bound for

1. ADJ

/ baʊnd fɔr / (AmE), / baʊnd fə / (BrE)
on the way to or destined for a particular place or experience
headed toward a destination

Examples

The flight was bound for London and departed right on time.
With his backpack ready, he set off bound for the mountains.
The ship, bound for Australia, carried hundreds of hopeful settlers.

Synset Examples

The package is bound for delivery by tomorrow.
They are bound for adventure on their road trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: en route to - in the process of going to a place
  • Antonym: departing - going away from or leaving for a place
