watch glass

1. NOUN

/ wɑtʃ ɡlæs / (AmE), / wɒtʃ ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A shallow, round glass dish used in laboratories as an evaporating surface or to cover a beaker.
shallow laboratory glass dish

Examples

The chemist placed the crystals on a watch glass to allow them to air-dry.
Cover the beaker with a watch glass to prevent contamination during the reaction.
During the experiment, a watch glass was used as an evaporating dish for the solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaker - a lipped cylindrical glass container used in laboratories

2. NOUN

/ wɑtʃ ɡlæs / (AmE), / wɒtʃ ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a clear, protective cover over the face of a watch
protective cover for a watch face

Examples

The watch glass was scratched after I dropped my wristwatch on the floor.
Many antique watches have domed watch glasses made of acrylic.
A jeweler can replace your broken watch glass with a new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crystal - the glass or transparent cover over a watch face
