Noah and the Flood

1. PROPN

/ ˈnoʊ.ə ənðə flʌd / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.ə ənðə flʌd / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the catastrophic flood sent by God to destroy the world during the time of Noah, as described in the Book of Genesis.
Biblical flood in Noah's time

Examples

Many artists have depicted Noah and the Flood in classic paintings throughout history.
The story of Noah and the Flood is often cited as a lesson on obedience and faith in religious teachings.
Researchers have explored historical and geological explanations for the legend of Noah and the Flood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Flood - another name for the flood described in the story of Noah
