Bermuda maidenhair fern

1. NOUN

/ bərˈmjuːdə ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr fɜrn / (AmE), / bəˈmjuːdə ˈmeɪdənˌhɛə fɜːn / (BrE)
A delicate fern species endemic to Bermuda, featuring a creeping rootstock and fine fronds.
fern species native to Bermuda

Examples

The Bermuda maidenhair fern thrives in the shaded limestone crevices of the island.
Conservationists are working to protect the rare Bermuda maidenhair fern from habitat loss.
She photographed the delicate fronds of the Bermuda maidenhair fern during her hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a common name for various species of the genus Adiantum, known for fine, delicate fronds
