mackerel shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækərəl ˌʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈmæk(ə)rəl ˌʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large, fast-swimming shark of the family Lamnidae, typically found in open ocean and known for its speed and predatory behavior
a large predatory oceanic shark

Examples

The mackerel shark is revered for its impressive speed and agility in open waters.
Scientists studied the migratory patterns of the mackerel shark to understand their seasonal movements.
A mackerel shark can be identified by its streamlined body and distinctive pointed snout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamnid - a shark of the family Lamnidae
  • Degree Higher: great white shark - a large predatory lamnid shark, also called white shark

Etymology

Named for its apparent habit of preying on mackerel and similar fish.
