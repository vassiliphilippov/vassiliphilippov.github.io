epic poem

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪk ˈpoʊəm / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk ˈpəʊəm / (BrE)
A long narrative poem, often detailing the adventures and deeds of a legendary or historical hero.
long narrative poem about a hero

Examples

The Iliad is perhaps the most famous epic poem in Western literature.
He spent years studying ancient epic poems from Greece and India.
Writing an original epic poem is a monumental literary undertaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic - A long narrative poem, typically about heroic deeds, or grand in scale.

Etymology

From Latin 'epicus' (from Greek 'epikos', relating to poetry sung or recited) and 'poem', from Greek 'poiēma'.
