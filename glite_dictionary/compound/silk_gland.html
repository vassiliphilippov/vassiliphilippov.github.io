silk gland

1. NOUN

/ sɪlk ɡlænd / (AmE), / sɪlk ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland in certain insects and spiders that produces silk, especially in silkworms and spiders for making cocoons, webs, or threads
gland in insects or spiders producing silk

Examples

The silk gland of a silkworm is essential for the production of commercial silk.
Spiders use their silk glands to create intricate webs.
Scientists study the proteins from the silk gland to develop new materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gland - an organ in the body that secretes particular chemical substances
