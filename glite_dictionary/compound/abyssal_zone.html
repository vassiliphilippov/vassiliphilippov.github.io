abyssal zone

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɪs.əl zoʊn / (AmE), / əˈbɪs.əl zəʊn / (BrE)
the region of the deep ocean typically below 2,000 meters where no light penetrates and conditions are very cold and high-pressure
deep oceanic region below 2,000 meters

Examples

The creatures living in the abyssal zone have adapted to survive without any sunlight.
The abyssal zone covers vast portions of the ocean floor, far beyond the reach of most submarines.
Scientists sent a probe down into the abyssal zone to study its unique environment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bathyal zone - the oceanic zone above the abyssal zone, between 1,000 and 2,000 meters
  • Degree Higher: hadal zone - the deepest part of the ocean below the abyssal zone, usually deeper than 6,000 meters

Etymology

From Greek abyssos (bottomless) + zone (belt, region).
