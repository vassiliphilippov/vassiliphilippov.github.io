green light

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin laɪt / (AmE), / ɡriːn laɪt / (BrE)
a signal or permission to proceed, especially as indicated by a green traffic signal
signal or permission to proceed

Examples

We had to wait for the green light before crossing the intersection.
The manager gave the project a green light to move forward next month.
After getting the green light from the authorities, construction began immediately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: red light - a signal to stop, especially a red traffic light

2. NOUN

/ ɡrin laɪt / (AmE), / ɡriːn laɪt / (BrE)
official permission to begin or continue a project or activity
official permission to proceed

Examples

The city council finally gave the green light to the new sports complex.
We're waiting for management to give us the green light before we start the campaign.
Once the project received the green light, the team began work immediately.

Synset Examples

they gave the green light for construction to begin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go-ahead - an informal synonym meaning official permission to proceed
  • Antonym: red light - a signal meaning stop or not allowed, the opposite of green light

Etymology

From the use of green traffic lights to indicate permission to go.
