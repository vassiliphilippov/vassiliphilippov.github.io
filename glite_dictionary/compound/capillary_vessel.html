capillary vessel

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / kəˈpɪləri ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
any of the smallest blood vessels that connect arterioles to venules, allowing exchange of substances between blood and tissues
smallest blood vessel connecting arterioles and venules
medical

Examples

Oxygen and nutrients pass through the walls of the capillary vessels to nourish surrounding cells.
Damage to capillary vessels can lead to bruising under the skin.
The human body contains billions of capillary vessels that form an intricate network.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capillary - a tiny blood vessel where substances are exchanged between blood and tissues

Etymology

From Latin ‘capillaris’ (hair-like) and ‘vascellum’ (small vessel).
