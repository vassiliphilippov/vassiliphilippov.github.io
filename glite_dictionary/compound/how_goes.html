to how goes

1. VERB

/ haʊ ɡoʊz / (AmE), / haʊ ɡəʊz / (BrE)
used to ask about the progress or current state of something, typically in a somewhat formal or literary style
ask about progress or situation

Examples

The manager stopped by and asked, "How goes the new project?"
Every time I visit my grandfather, he greets me with, "How goes your studies?"
In a letter to her friend, she wrote, "How goes life in the countryside?"

Synset Examples

The general inquired, 'How goes the enemy’s advance?'
We often ask each other, 'How goes the war with deadlines?'
