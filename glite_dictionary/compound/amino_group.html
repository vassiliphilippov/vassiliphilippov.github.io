amino group

1. NOUN

/ əˈmiːnoʊ ɡruːp / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəʊ ɡruːp / (BrE)
A functional group in organic chemistry with the formula –NH₂, consisting of a nitrogen atom attached to two hydrogen atoms.
chemical group –NH₂
medical

Examples

The amino group is essential for the formation of amino acids.
Many drugs are modified by adding an amino group to their structure.
This molecule contains both a carboxyl group and an amino group.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: amino acid - organic compound containing both an amino and a carboxyl group
  • Degree Higher: amine - organic compound derived from ammonia by replacement of hydrogen atoms

Etymology

From Latin 'ammoniacum' (gum ammoniac) and 'group' (collection of atoms), used in chemical nomenclature.
