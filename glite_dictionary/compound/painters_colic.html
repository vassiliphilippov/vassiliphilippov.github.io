painter's colic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪntərz ˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntəz ˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
A form of chronic lead poisoning, especially among painters, characterized by severe constipation and abdominal pain.
lead poisoning marked by constipation
medical

Examples

In the 19th century, painter's colic was a common occupational hazard for decorators and artists.
The symptoms of painter's colic include severe abdominal pain and persistent constipation due to chronic lead exposure.
Doctors eventually identified painter's colic as being caused by the ingestion of lead-based paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead poisoning - illness caused by absorption of lead

Etymology

From the occupation 'painter', who were historically exposed to lead in paint, and 'colic' (Latin 'colicus', relating to the colon).
