Book of Ezra

1. PROPN

/ ˌbʊk əv ˈɛzrə / (AmE), / ˌbʊk əv ˈɛzrə / (BrE)
a book in the Old Testament describing the efforts of Ezra to restore Jewish law and worship in Jerusalem after the Babylonian Captivity
Old Testament book on Ezra restoring Jewish law

Examples

Many scholars have debated the historical accuracy of the Book of Ezra.
The Book of Ezra discusses the return of the Israelites to Jerusalem after their exile.
She referenced the Book of Ezra while writing her thesis on post-exilic Judaism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ezra - the scribe and priest who led the Jewish people after the Babylonian exile

Etymology

Ezra is from Hebrew ʻEzrā, the name of the priest and scribe in biblical tradition.
