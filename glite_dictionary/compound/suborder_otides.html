suborder Otides

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdɚ oʊˈtaɪdiːz / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə əʊˈtiːdiːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder comprising terrestrial game birds of the Old World and Australia, including bustards.
bird suborder including bustards

Examples

The suborder Otides includes several species of bustards native to Eurasia and Africa.
Researchers studying avian evolution focus on the suborder Otides due to its unique adaptations.
Many conservation programs aim to protect endangered birds within the suborder Otides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustard - large terrestrial game bird found in the Old World and Australia

Etymology

From New Latin 'Otides', based on the type genus 'Otis'.
