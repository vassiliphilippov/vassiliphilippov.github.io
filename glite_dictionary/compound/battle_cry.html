battle cry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəl ˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈbætəl ˌkraɪ / (BrE)
a loud shout or phrase used to encourage soldiers in battle or to rally people to action
rallying shout used in battle

Examples

The general's battle cry inspired the troops to charge forward without fear.
"Freedom!" became the battle cry of the revolutionaries during their struggle.
The team adopted a new battle cry to motivate themselves before every game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a memorable phrase used in advertising or rallying support

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l ˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl ˌkraɪ / (BrE)
A phrase or slogan shouted or used to encourage people to support a cause or to motivate members of a group, especially in times of challenge or conflict.
slogan to rally support

Examples

The slogan 'Save Our Planet' became the battle cry for environmental activists worldwide.
During the protest, the crowd chanted their battle cry with passion and unity.
'Freedom now!' served as a powerful battle cry for the movement.

Synset Examples

a cry to arms
our watchword will be ‘democracy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a short and striking or memorable phrase used in advertising or promotion
  • Synonym: rallying cry - a word or phrase used to unite people in support of a cause or movement

Etymology

From 'battle' (conflict) and 'cry' (shout), first used in connection with war but now used figuratively.
