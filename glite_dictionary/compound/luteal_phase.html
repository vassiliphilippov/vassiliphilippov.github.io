luteal phase

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtiəl feɪz / (AmE), / ˈluːtɪəl feɪz / (BrE)
the phase in the menstrual cycle following ovulation, during which the corpus luteum produces progesterone to prepare the uterus for possible pregnancy
menstrual cycle phase after ovulation
medical

Examples

Many women experience mild symptoms, such as bloating, during the luteal phase of their cycle.
Progesterone levels rise sharply after ovulation in the luteal phase.
A short luteal phase can sometimes make it harder to conceive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: follicular phase - the phase of the menstrual cycle before ovulation

Etymology

From 'luteal' (relating to the corpus luteum, Latin luteus 'yellow') + 'phase'.
