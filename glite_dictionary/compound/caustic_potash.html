caustic potash

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔstɪk pəˈtæʃ / (AmE), / ˌkɔːstɪk pəˈtæʃ / (BrE)
potassium hydroxide, a strong alkaline compound used in industry and agriculture
potassium hydroxide, a strong alkali

Examples

The soap was made with caustic potash, which helps break down animal fats.
Workers must wear gloves when handling caustic potash to avoid chemical burns.
Caustic potash is often used in the manufacture of fertilizers and detergents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium hydroxide - another name for caustic potash, KOH

Etymology

From 'caustic' (burning, corrosive) and 'potash,' originally referring to potassium compounds derived from wood ashes.
