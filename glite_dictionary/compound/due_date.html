due date

1. NOUN

/ ˈdu ˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdjuː ˌdeɪt / (BrE)
the date by which a payment or obligation must be made or repaid
date for payment or obligation

Examples

Your credit card bill must be paid by the due date to avoid late fees.
The library book has a due date printed inside the cover.
She requested an extension because she knew she couldn't meet the due date for her loan repayment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadline - the latest time or date by which something should be completed

Etymology

From 'due', meaning payable, and 'date', meaning a specific day.
