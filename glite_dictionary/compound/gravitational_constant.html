gravitational constant

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˌveɪʃənəl ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ɡravɪˈteɪʃən(ə)l ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a universal constant (symbol G) that relates force to mass and distance in Newton's law of gravitation; it determines the strength of the gravitational force between two bodies
universal constant in Newton's law of gravitation

Examples

The value of the gravitational constant is crucial for calculating the force between two masses.
Scientists have measured the gravitational constant with increasing precision over the years.
Newton's law of universal gravitation includes the gravitational constant as a fundamental factor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Newton's law of gravitation - the law describing the gravitational force between two bodies
