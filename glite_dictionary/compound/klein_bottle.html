Klein bottle

1. NOUN

/ klaɪn ˈbɑːtəl / (AmE), / klaɪn ˈbɒtəl / (BrE)
A closed surface with only one side and no boundary, formed by joining the ends of a tube after passing one end through its side, used in mathematics and topology.
one-sided closed mathematical surface

Examples

The Klein bottle is an object in topology that has only one continuous surface.
A Klein bottle cannot exist in three-dimensional space without intersecting itself.
Collectors sometimes purchase glass models of a Klein bottle as mathematical curiosities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Möbius strip - a surface with only one side formed by twisting a strip and joining the ends

Etymology

Named after German mathematician Felix Klein (1849–1925), who first described it.
