family Cercopithecidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli sɚˌkɑːpɪˈθiːsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmli ˌsɜː.kə.pɪˈθiː.sɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of Old World monkeys including species such as the guenon, baboon, colobus monkey, langur, macaque, mandrill, mangabey, patas, and proboscis monkey
a family of Old World monkeys

Examples

The family Cercopithecidae includes baboons, macaques, and other Old World monkeys found in Africa and Asia.
Scientists studied the behavior of several species within the family Cercopithecidae to understand primate evolution.
Members of the family Cercopithecidae are distinguished from New World monkeys by their downward-facing nostrils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old World monkey - any monkey of the family Cercopithecidae native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Modern Latin Cercopithecidae, based on Greek kerkos (tail) + pithēkos (ape).
