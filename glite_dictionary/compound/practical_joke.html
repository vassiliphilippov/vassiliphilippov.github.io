practical joke

1. NOUN

/ ˈpræktɪkəl ˈdʒoʊk / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəl ˈdʒəʊk / (BrE)
a trick or prank played on someone, often intended to make them look foolish or to amuse others
trick played to make someone look foolish

Examples

He decided to play a practical joke on his brother by putting salt in the sugar bowl.
April Fools' Day is traditionally a time for harmless practical jokes and laughter.
The practical joke at the office meeting left everyone in stitches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank - a playful or mischievous act
  • Synonym: hoax - a trick or deception, especially a deliberate one
