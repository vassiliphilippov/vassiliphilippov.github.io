antipsychotic agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌænti.saɪˈkɑtɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌanti.saɪˈkɒtɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a type of drug used to treat psychotic disorders by reducing symptoms such as hallucinations and delusions, often with a calming effect
drug treating psychotic disorders
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antipsychotic agent to help alleviate the patient's hallucinations.
Some antipsychotic agents may cause fewer side effects than others.
Recent research focuses on developing new antipsychotic agents with improved efficacy and safety profiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - a drug used to manage psychosis

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'psychotic' (relating to psychosis) + 'agent' (acting substance).
