blocking agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑkɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈblɒkɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a drug or other substance that inhibits or blocks a specific biological process in the body
drug that inhibits a biological process
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a blocking agent to reduce the effects of the excess hormone.
Some antihistamines act as a blocking agent in allergic reactions.
Beta blockers are a well-known type of blocking agent used in treating heart conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a particular chemical reaction or other process
