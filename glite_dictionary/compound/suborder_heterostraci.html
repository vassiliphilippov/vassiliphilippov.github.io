suborder Heterostraci

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈɔrdɚ ˌhɛtərəˈstreɪˌsaɪ / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːdə ˌhɛtərəˈstrɑːsaɪ / (BrE)
An extinct group of armored, jawless fish or fish-like vertebrates, known from the Paleozoic era; their classification is uncertain.
extinct armored jawless fish group

Examples

Fossils of the suborder Heterostraci reveal complex armored plates and jawless forms.
Many paleontologists study the suborder Heterostraci to understand early vertebrate evolution.
The suborder Heterostraci thrived in Devonian seas before eventually becoming extinct.

Etymology

From Greek 'heteros' (different) + 'ostrakon' (shell), referring to distinctive armor plates.
