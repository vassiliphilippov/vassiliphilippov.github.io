genus Chimonanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kaɪ.moʊˈnæn.θəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kɪ.məʊˈnæn.θəs / (BrE)
A small genus of Asian deciduous or evergreen shrubs known for their fragrant, typically winter-blooming flowers, commonly referred to as wintersweet.
genus of Asian shrubs with fragrant winter flowers

Examples

The genus Chimonanthus is prized for its fragrant yellow flowers that appear in winter.
Botanists have classified several species under the genus Chimonanthus for their unique blooming habits.
Gardeners often plant members of the genus Chimonanthus to brighten their landscapes during the colder months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintersweet - deciduous shrub with sweetly scented winter flowers, main species of Chimonanthus

Etymology

From New Latin 'Chimonanthus', Greek 'cheimon' (winter) + 'anthos' (flower).
