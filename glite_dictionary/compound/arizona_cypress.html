Arizona cypress

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪzoʊnə ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / əˈrɪzənə ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
A coniferous tree native to the southwestern United States, especially Arizona, with bluish-silvery, scale-like foliage.
conifer tree of Arizona with bluish foliage

Examples

The Arizona cypress thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often planted for windbreaks.
With its bluish-silver leaves, the Arizona cypress adds unique color to any landscape.
They chose an Arizona cypress for their backyard for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any tree or shrub of the genus Cupressus, with dark-green foliage and small round cones
