Mojave Desert

1. PROPN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi ˈdɛzərt / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a desert region in southern California, with parts extending into Nevada, Arizona, and Utah, known for its arid climate and unique flora and fauna
desert region in southwestern United States

Examples

The Joshua trees are a famous feature of the Mojave Desert.
Temperatures in the Mojave Desert can soar above 45 degrees Celsius in summer.
Many movies have been filmed in the rugged landscape of the Mojave Desert.

Etymology

Named after the Mojave people, indigenous to the area; 'desert' from Old French 'desert', from Latin 'desertum' (an abandoned place).
