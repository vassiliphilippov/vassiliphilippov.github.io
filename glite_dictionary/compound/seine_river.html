Seine River

1. PROPN

/ seɪn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / seɪn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in northern France that flows through Paris and empties into the English Channel
river in northern France, flows through Paris

Examples

The Seine River is famous for the beautiful bridges that cross it in Paris.
Many tourists enjoy taking a boat ride along the Seine River to see the city’s landmarks.
Artists have long been inspired by the light and scenery along the Seine River.

Etymology

From French Seine, from Latin Sequana (ancient name of the river).
