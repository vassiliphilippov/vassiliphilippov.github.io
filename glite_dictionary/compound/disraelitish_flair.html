Disraelitish flair

1. NOUN

/ dɪzˈreɪlɪtɪʃ flɛr / (AmE), / dɪzˈreɪlɪtɪʃ fleə / (BrE)
a distinctive style characterized by the wit, sophistication, or flamboyance associated with Benjamin Disraeli
style reminiscent of Disraeli
british

Examples

The prime minister's remarks had a certain Disraelitish flair that charmed the audience.
Her writing possesses a Disraelitish flair, blending sophistication with sly humor.
Critics noted the Disraelitish flair in his approach to political debate.

Synset Examples

The speech had a distinctly Disraelitish flair.

Etymology

From 'Disraeli,' referring to Benjamin Disraeli (1804–1881), British statesman and writer, plus '-ish' and 'flair.'
