First State

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɝst steɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of Delaware, the first state to ratify the U.S. Constitution
nickname for Delaware (U.S. state)
american

Examples

Delaware is often called the First State because it was the first to join the Union.
Tourists visiting the First State enjoy its historic towns and beautiful beaches.
The nickname First State is proudly displayed on Delaware's license plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Delaware - the U.S. state; one of the original 13 colonies

Etymology

Refers to Delaware being the first to ratify the U.S. Constitution in 1787.
