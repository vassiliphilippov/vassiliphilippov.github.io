chamber music

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbɚ ˌmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə ˌmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Serious music composed for a small group of instruments, typically intended for performance in an intimate setting rather than a concert hall.
music for a small group of instruments

Examples

She enjoys listening to chamber music in the evenings, especially string quartets.
The festival featured several masterpieces of chamber music performed by renowned artists.
Unlike orchestral works, chamber music allows for more intimate musical expression.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orchestral music - music composed for a full orchestra
  • Synonym: ensemble - a group of musicians who perform together

Etymology

From 'chamber' (room) and 'music'; originally for performance in private rooms rather than large venues.
