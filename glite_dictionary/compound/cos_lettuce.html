cos lettuce

1. NOUN

/ kɑs ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / kɒs ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
a variety of lettuce with long dark-green leaves forming a loosely packed, elongated head; also known as romaine lettuce.
lettuce with long, upright leaves

Examples

I always use cos lettuce in my Caesar salads for its crisp texture.
Fresh cos lettuce was delivered to the restaurant every morning.
They planted rows of cos lettuce in their vegetable garden this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romaine lettuce - lettuce with long upright leaves and a firm elongated head

Etymology

'Cos' is named after the Greek island of Cos (Kos), from where this type of lettuce was believed to have originated.

2. NOUN

/ kɑs ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / kɒs ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
a variety of lettuce with long, crisp, dark-green leaves that form a tall head, also known as romaine lettuce
variety of lettuce with long, dark-green leaves

Examples

The salad was made with fresh cos lettuce, tomatoes, and feta cheese.
I prefer using cos lettuce in Caesar salads for its crunchy texture.
She bought a head of cos lettuce from the farmers' market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romaine lettuce - another name for cos lettuce, especially in North America

Etymology

Perhaps from the Greek island of Cos (Kos), from which the lettuce was reputedly exported.
