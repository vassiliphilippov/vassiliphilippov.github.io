cotton gin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtn ˌdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn ˌdʒɪn / (BrE)
a machine used to separate seeds from raw cotton fibers
machine for separating cotton seeds from fiber

Examples

The invention of the cotton gin revolutionized the cotton industry in the 18th century.
After the cotton gin was introduced, cotton production increased dramatically in the southern United States.
Eli Whitney is famously known for inventing the cotton gin.

Etymology

From 'cotton' and 'gin', a shortened form of 'engine'.
