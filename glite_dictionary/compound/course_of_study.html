course of study

1. NOUN

/ kɔrs əv ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / kɔːs əv ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
An integrated set of academic subjects or classes that forms a curriculum, usually leading to a qualification or degree.
organized academic program or curriculum

Examples

She enrolled in a challenging course of study to prepare for her medical career.
Each student must complete a course of study approved by the department to graduate.
The university revised its course of study to include more practical skills.

Synset Examples

he was admitted to a new program at the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curriculum - the subjects comprising a course of study in a school or college
  • Synonym: syllabus - an outline of the subjects in a course of study or teaching

2. NOUN

/ kɔrs əv ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / kɔːs əv ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
a series of lessons or meetings in which a particular subject is taught; a program of instruction
series of lessons on a subject

Examples

After completing the course of study, she received her diploma in biology.
Students are required to choose a course of study in their first year at university.
The college offers a rigorous course of study in engineering.

Synset Examples

he took a course in basket weaving
flirting is not unknown in college classes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curriculum - the subjects comprising a course of study in a school or college
  • Synonym: course - a set of classes or a plan of study on a particular subject
