lieutenant commander

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈutɛnənt kəˈmændər / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt kəˈmɑːndə / (BrE)
A commissioned officer in the Navy or Coast Guard ranking above a lieutenant and below a commander.
naval rank above lieutenant

Examples

After years of dedicated service, she was promoted to lieutenant commander.
The lieutenant commander led the ship's crew during the exercise.
During the ceremony, several officers were honored, including a newly appointed lieutenant commander.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: commander - naval officer above lieutenant commander
  • Degree Lower: lieutenant - naval officer below lieutenant commander

Etymology

From 'lieutenant' and 'commander', indicating a rank between the two.
