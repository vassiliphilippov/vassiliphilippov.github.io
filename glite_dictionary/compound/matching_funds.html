matching funds

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃɪŋ fʌndz / (AmE), / ˈmætʃɪŋ fʌndz / (BrE)
money provided equal to or in proportion with amounts raised from other sources, often as a condition for a grant or funding program
money equal to amounts from other sources

Examples

The non-profit received matching funds from the government when they secured private donations.
The university offers matching funds to encourage alumni contributions to scholarships.
Many grant programs require applicants to obtain matching funds from other sources before approval.
