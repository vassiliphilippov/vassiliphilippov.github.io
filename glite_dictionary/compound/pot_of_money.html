pot of money

1. NOUN

/ pɑt əv ˈmʌni / (AmE), / pɒt əv ˈmʌni / (BrE)
a sum of money that is available for use, typically collected or accumulated for a particular purpose
sum of money available for use

Examples

After years of careful saving, they finally had a pot of money to buy a new house.
The charity auction raised a generous pot of money for local schools.
She invested her pot of money in various stocks and mutual funds.

Synset Examples

Winning the lottery gave her a huge pot of money.
He saved up a nice pot of money for his vacation.
Investing wisely can grow your pot of money over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fund - a sum of money saved or made available for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: pool - a supply of money or resources that is shared by a group

Etymology

From 'pot' (a container for holding things) symbolizing a collection, and 'money'.
