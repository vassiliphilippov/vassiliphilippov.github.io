Maltese lira

1. NOUN

/ mɔlˈtiz ˈlɪrə / (AmE), / mɔːlˈtiːz ˈlɪərə / (BrE)
the former basic monetary unit of Malta, equal to 100 cents, replaced by the euro in 2008
former basic currency of Malta

Examples

Before switching to the euro, Malta used the Maltese lira as its currency.
He exchanged his euros for Maltese lira during his trip in 2007.
The Maltese lira is now a part of the nation's monetary history.

Etymology

From Maltese 'lira' (pound), influenced by Italian 'lira', meaning pound.
