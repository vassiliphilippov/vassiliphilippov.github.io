squeegee bandit

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːdʒi ˈbændɪt / (AmE), / ˈskwiːdʒi ˈbændɪt / (BrE)
a person, often working at traffic intersections, who cleans car windows for money or tips, sometimes aggressively or without permission
person cleaning car windows for tips, often aggressively

Examples

Police have increased patrols at crowded intersections known for squeegee bandit activity.
Many drivers feel uncomfortable when approached by a squeegee bandit at a red light.
There is ongoing debate about how to address the problem of squeegee bandits in the city.

Synset Examples

The squeegee bandit was caught on camera last night.
They dubbed him the squeegee bandit after his antics.
Watch out for the squeegee bandit at the intersection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeegee man - person who cleans car windows at stoplights for tips, often aggressively

Etymology

Formed from 'squeegee' (a tool for cleaning windows, from English dialect) and 'bandit' (from Italian 'bandito', outlaw).
