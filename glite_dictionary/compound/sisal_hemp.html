sisal hemp

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪsəl hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəl hɛmp / (BrE)
a strong, durable plant fiber from the sisal plant, used chiefly for making rope, twine, and matting
fiber from the sisal plant used for rope

Examples

The ropes on the old ship were made from sisal hemp because of its durability in saltwater.
Gardeners often use sisal hemp twine for tying plants, as it is strong and biodegradable.
Traditional rugs and mats may be woven from sisal hemp for a natural, rustic appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sisal - the agave plant yielding sisal hemp

Etymology

From Spanish *sisal*, the name of a port in Mexico from which the fiber was exported, and 'hemp', a general term for fibrous plants.
