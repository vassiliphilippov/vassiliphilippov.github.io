Rhone River

1. PROPN

/ roʊn ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / rəʊn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in southeastern France, rising in the Swiss Alps and flowing into the Mediterranean Sea near Marseilles
major river in southeastern France

Examples

The Rhone River flows through the scenic vineyards of southern France.
Many picturesque towns are located along the banks of the Rhone River.
After heavy rainfall, the Rhone River can rise rapidly, threatening nearby villages.

Synset Examples

the valley of the Rhone is famous for its vineyards

Etymology

From French 'Rhône', from Latin 'Rhodanus', ultimately from Ancient Greek 'Ῥοδανός' (Rhodanós).
