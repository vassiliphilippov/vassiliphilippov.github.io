genus Hippopotamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhɪp.əˈpɑː.tə.məs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhɪp.əˈpɒ.tə.məs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Hippopotamidae, comprising large, chiefly aquatic African mammals such as the hippopotamus.
type genus of Hippopotamidae (hippopotamuses)

Examples

The genus Hippopotamus includes the common hippopotamus and several extinct relatives.
Scientists are studying fossils to determine the evolutionary history of the genus Hippopotamus.
The genus Hippopotamus is notable for its members' semi-aquatic habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hippopotamus - a large, mostly herbivorous mammal native to sub-Saharan Africa

Etymology

Genus from Latin 'genus' (kind), Hippopotamus from Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'potamos' (river).
