watered stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərd stɑk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəd stɒk / (BrE)
Shares of a corporation whose stated value is much greater than the actual value of its assets; stock that is overvalued and does not reflect the company's true worth.
overvalued corporate shares

Examples

The company was accused of issuing watered stock to attract naive investors.
In the nineteenth century, unscrupulous businessmen often sold watered stock to inflate their profits.
Regulators introduced rules to prevent the creation of watered stock in public markets.

Etymology

From the practice of inflating the value of livestock by making them drink large amounts of water before weighing; transferred to overvalued stocks in the 19th century.
