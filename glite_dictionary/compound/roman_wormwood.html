Roman wormwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈwɜrmˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈwɜːmˌwʊd / (BrE)
a glaucous herb native to northeastern North America, bearing loose racemes of yellow-tipped pink flowers; sometimes classified within the genus Fumaria
a herbaceous plant of NE North America

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare specimen of Roman wormwood in its North American section.
Researchers study the medicinal properties of Roman wormwood for potential pharmaceutical uses.
Gardeners appreciate Roman wormwood for its attractive pink and yellow flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmən ˈwɜrmˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmən ˈwɜːmˌwʊd / (BrE)
A European species of wormwood (Artemisia pontica), sometimes used as a minor source of absinthe.
a European species of wormwood

Examples

Roman wormwood is sometimes cultivated for use in flavoring spirits such as absinthe.
The leaves of Roman wormwood have a distinctive bitter taste.
Botanists classify Roman wormwood as Artemisia pontica, native to southern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - a bitter aromatic herb of the genus Artemisia
