stratospheric ambition

1. NOUN

/ ˌstræt̬əˈsfɪrɪk æmˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstrætəˈsfɪərɪk æmˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
extremely high or unrealistic ambitions; aspirations that are far beyond what is considered achievable
extremely high or unrealistic ambitions

Examples

The candidate's stratospheric ambition impressed some, but others found it unrealistic.
In her memoir, she writes about the dangers of stratospheric ambition without a solid plan.
His stratospheric ambition pushed the company to innovate, but also led to several costly mistakes.

Synset Examples

His ambitions are stratospheric compared to his current situation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ambition - strong desire to achieve something

Etymology

From 'stratospheric' (relating to the stratosphere, the high layer of the earth's atmosphere), used metaphorically to mean extremely high, combined with 'ambition'.
