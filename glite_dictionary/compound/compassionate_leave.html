compassionate leave

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpæʃənət liːv / (AmE), / kəmˈpæʃənət liːv / (BrE)
leave of absence granted to an employee or service member to deal with an emergency, typically involving illness or death of a close relative.
leave for family emergency or bereavement

Examples

She was given compassionate leave after her father was hospitalized unexpectedly.
Many companies offer compassionate leave to support employees during family crises.
The soldier applied for compassionate leave so he could attend his mother's funeral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bereavement leave - leave granted due to the death of a family member
  • Degree Lower: sick leave - leave granted for employee illness
