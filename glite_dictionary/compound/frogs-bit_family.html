frog's-bit family

1. NOUN

/ frɔɡz bɪt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / frɒɡz bɪt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of small, nearly stemless freshwater aquatic plants, widely distributed in temperate regions
family of freshwater aquatic plants

Examples

The frog's-bit family includes several aquatic plants found in ponds and marshes.
Botanists classify Limnobium among the frog's-bit family due to its unique floating leaves.
Many members of the frog's-bit family are important for maintaining healthy wetland ecosystems.
