sweet melon

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / swiːt ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
any of several varieties of melon with a netted rind, sweet edible flesh, and a musky aroma, such as the cantaloupe and muskmelon.
melon with sweet flesh and netted rind

Examples

The market had a fresh shipment of sweet melon this morning, perfect for a summer salad.
She cut the sweet melon into cubes and offered them to the children as a snack.
The refreshing taste of sweet melon makes it a popular dessert in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cantaloupe - a type of sweet melon with orange flesh
  • Synonym: muskmelon - a sweet, edible melon with a netted rind

2. NOUN

/ swit ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / swiːt ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
A type of melon with sweet, juicy flesh, such as cantaloupe or honeydew, typically eaten fresh.
a type of sweet, edible melon

Examples

In the summer, we often enjoy fresh slices of sweet melon with breakfast.
The market was selling a variety of sweet melons, including cantaloupe and honeydew.
She added some chilled sweet melon to the fruit salad for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskmelon - a type of melon with sweet, fragrant flesh
  • Synonym: cantaloupe - a variety of muskmelon with orange flesh
  • Antonym: watermelon - a large fruit with sweet, juicy red flesh; not a muskmelon
