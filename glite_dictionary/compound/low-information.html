low-information

1. ADJ

/ loʊ ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ləʊ ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
providing little or no valuable or substantial information; lacking in useful content
lacking substantive or valuable information

Examples

The report was criticized for being too low-information and lacking important details.
Voters who rely on low-information news sources may be less informed about key issues.
Many found the guide to be low-information and not very helpful in making a decision.

Synset Examples

His presentation was interesting but ultimately low-information.
The article was criticized for being low-information and lacking depth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: informative - providing useful or interesting information
