genus Alces

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈælˌsiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈæl.siːz / (BrE)
A genus of large deer that includes the moose or elk, native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.
genus of large deer (moose/elk)

Examples

The genus Alces contains only one extant species, the moose, found across the northern hemisphere.
Paleontologists have discovered fossil species within the genus Alces from the Pleistocene era.
Conservation efforts for the genus Alces focus mainly on habitat preservation for the moose.

Etymology

From Latin 'Alces', the classical name for elk or moose, used as a taxonomic genus name in zoology.
