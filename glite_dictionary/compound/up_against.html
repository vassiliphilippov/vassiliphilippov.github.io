up against

1. ADJ

/ ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
facing a difficult situation, obstacle, or opposing force that must be dealt with
facing a difficult challenge

Examples

We're up against a lot of pressure to finish this project by Friday.
When you are up against financial troubles, it's important to seek advice.
The team found themselves up against stronger opponents than they had expected.

Synset Examples

The team is up against a challenging deadline.
She was up against tough competition in the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with a problem or difficulty
  • Degree Higher: to overcome - to successfully deal with or gain control of something difficult
