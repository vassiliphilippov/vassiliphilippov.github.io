lateral cerebral sulcus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl səˈriː.brəl ˈsʌl.kəs / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl səˈriː.brəl ˈsʌl.kəs / (BrE)
the deep groove in the brain's cortex that separates the frontal and temporal lobes in both hemispheres
major brain groove separating frontal and temporal lobes
medical

Examples

The lateral cerebral sulcus is an important landmark in neurosurgery.
Damage near the lateral cerebral sulcus can affect language abilities.
The MRI scan clearly revealed the lateral cerebral sulcus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sylvian fissure - another name for the lateral cerebral sulcus

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of the side), 'cerebrum' (brain), and 'sulcus' (groove).
