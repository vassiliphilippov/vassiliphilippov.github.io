BOLD fMRI

1. NOUN

/ boʊld ɛf ɛm ɑːr aɪ / (AmE), / bəʊld ɛf ɛm ɑː r aɪ / (BrE)
a type of functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) that measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood oxygenation
functional MRI technique detecting blood oxygen changes
medical

Examples

Researchers used BOLD fMRI to observe which brain regions were activated during the memory task.
The results from BOLD fMRI scans have advanced our understanding of cognitive functions.
Because BOLD fMRI detects changes in blood oxygen levels, it is sensitive to neuronal activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fMRI - functional magnetic resonance imaging; a technique for measuring brain activity

Etymology

BOLD: acronym for Blood Oxygen Level-Dependent, from the technique's reliance on changes in blood oxygenation.
