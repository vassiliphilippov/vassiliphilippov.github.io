agent-in-place

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənt ɪn pleɪs / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt ɪn pleɪs / (BrE)
an operative who is established within a target organization or area, working covertly as part of an intelligence operation
covert operative inside target organization

Examples

The foreign government managed to recruit an agent-in-place at the embassy.
Intelligence agencies value an agent-in-place for access to sensitive information.
After years of building trust, she became a highly effective agent-in-place within the rival organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - an undercover agent who infiltrates an organization for espionage purposes
  • Synonym: double agent - an agent who pretends to spy for one side while actually spying for another
