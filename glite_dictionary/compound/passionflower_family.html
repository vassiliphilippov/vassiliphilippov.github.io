passionflower family

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃənˌflaʊər ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈpæʃənˌflaʊə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of tropical woody climbing vines, herbs, or shrubs, known for their showy flowers, scientifically called Passifloraceae
family of tropical climbing vines (Passifloraceae)

Examples

Many species in the passionflower family are prized for their intricate and colorful blooms.
The passionflower family includes plants that are native to tropical and subtropical regions.
Some fruits from the passionflower family are edible and used in juices and desserts.
