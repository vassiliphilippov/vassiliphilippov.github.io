Cumbre del Laudo

1. PROPN

/ ˈkum.breɪ dɛl ˈlaʊ.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈkʊm.breɪ dɛl ˈlaʊ.dəʊ / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Andes of Argentina, rising to 20,997 feet (6,401 meters) above sea level
mountain peak in the Andes, Argentina

Examples

The climbers reached the summit of Cumbre del Laudo after a week-long expedition.
Cumbre del Laudo is well known among mountaineers for its challenging terrain.
Photographs from the top of Cumbre del Laudo offer breathtaking panoramic views of the Andes.

Etymology

From Spanish 'cumbre' (summit) and 'laudo' (award, arbitration), referring to the peak named after a border arbitration.
