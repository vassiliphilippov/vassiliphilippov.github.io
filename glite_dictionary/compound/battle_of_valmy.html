Battle of Valmy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv vælˈmi / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv vælˈmiː / (BrE)
a battle in 1792 during the French Revolutionary Wars in which the French army defeated Austrian and Prussian forces, marking a significant turning point in French history
1792 French victory over Austrians and Prussians

Examples

The Battle of Valmy marked the first major victory of the French revolutionary armies.
Historians consider the Battle of Valmy a turning point that saved the French Revolution from foreign intervention.
The cannonade at the Battle of Valmy became legendary among both French and European military leaders.

Etymology

From French 'Valmy', the location in northeastern France where the battle occurred.
