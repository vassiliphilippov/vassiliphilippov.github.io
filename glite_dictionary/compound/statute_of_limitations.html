statute of limitations

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuːt əv lɪˌmɪteɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈstætjuːt əv lɪmɪˈteɪʃənz / (BrE)
a law that sets the maximum time after an event within which legal proceedings may be initiated
law limiting time for legal action
legal

Examples

The theft charge was dismissed because the statute of limitations had expired.
Under the statute of limitations, victims must file claims within two years of the incident.
The lawyer reviewed whether the statute of limitations applied to the case.

Etymology

From Latin *statutum* (law) and *limitatio* (restriction), via legal English.
