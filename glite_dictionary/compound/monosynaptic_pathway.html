monosynaptic pathway

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊsɪˈnæptɪk ˈpæθweɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊsɪˈnæptɪk ˈpɑːθweɪ / (BrE)
a nerve pathway that involves transmission of a signal across only one synapse between two neurons
nerve pathway with a single synapse
medical

Examples

The knee-jerk reflex is an example of a monosynaptic pathway in action.
Damage to a monosynaptic pathway can disrupt simple reflexes.
Researchers studied the monosynaptic pathway to understand how reflexes are transmitted.

Synset Examples

Monosynaptic pathways are crucial for reflex actions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polysynaptic pathway - a nerve pathway involving more than one synapse

Etymology

From 'mono-' (one) and 'synapse' (from Greek synapsis, joining together).
