antidiuretic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌdaɪjʊəˈrɛtɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a medication that reduces urine production by limiting the excretion of water by the kidneys
a drug that limits urine formation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antidiuretic drug to control the patient's excessive urination.
After taking the antidiuretic drug, she noticed a significant decrease in urine output.
Vasopressin is a commonly used antidiuretic drug in the treatment of diabetes insipidus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diuretic - a drug that increases urine production

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + diuretic, from Greek diourein (to urinate).
