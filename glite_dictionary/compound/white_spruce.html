white spruce

1. NOUN

/ waɪt sprus / (AmE), / waɪt spruːs / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen tree native to northeastern North America, with short blue-green needles and slender cones.
North American evergreen tree species

Examples

The white spruce thrives in cold climates and is common in boreal forests across Canada.
We planted a white spruce in our backyard to provide shade and shelter for birds.
The lumber from white spruce is valued for its strength and light color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - a type of evergreen tree of the genus Picea
