pocket change

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːkɪt tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small amount of money, especially coins, that someone carries with them for minor expenses
small amount of coins or money

Examples

I usually keep some pocket change for vending machines and parking meters.
She donated her pocket change to the local animal shelter every week.
After buying the ticket, he had only pocket change left in his wallet.

Synset Examples

The coffee cost only a few dollars, just pocket change.
She tossed in some pocket change for the charity.
He found some pocket change in his old jacket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small change - coins of low value; small amount of money
