fore-topmast staysail

1. NOUN

/ fɔːr ˈtɑpˌmæst ˈsteɪˌseɪl / (AmE), / fɔː ˈtɒpmɑːst ˈsteɪseɪl / (BrE)
the innermost jib sail situated just ahead of the foremast on a large square-rigged ship
innermost jib sail ahead of foremast on a square-rigged ship

Examples

The crew hoisted the fore-topmast staysail to gain extra speed in the freshening wind.
Due to damage on the jib, the captain ordered the fore-topmast staysail to be used instead.
While learning to sail, Mark studied the function of the fore-topmast staysail and other sails on the ship.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: jib - a triangular sail set ahead of the foremast on a ship

Etymology

From nautical terms: 'fore-topmast' (mast above the foremast) and 'staysail' (a sail set on a stay).
