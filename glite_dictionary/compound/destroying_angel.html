destroying angel

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɔɪɪŋ ˈeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪɪŋ ˈeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
an extremely poisonous white mushroom with a prominent cup-shaped base, belonging to the genus Amanita, that closely resembles edible mushrooms but is highly toxic if ingested
extremely poisonous white mushroom

Examples

The hiker mistakenly picked a destroying angel, believing it to be an edible mushroom.
Consuming even a small amount of a destroying angel can result in severe poisoning or death.
Experts warn foragers to be cautious, as the destroying angel closely resembles harmless varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death cap - another highly toxic mushroom of the Amanita genus

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɔɪɪŋ ˈeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪɪŋ ˈeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
a highly poisonous white mushroom of the genus Amanita, similar to the death cap, capable of causing fatal poisoning if ingested
a deadly poisonous white mushroom

Examples

Many mushroom species look harmless, but the destroying angel can be deadly if eaten.
The forager used a guidebook to avoid poisonous fungi like the destroying angel.
Symptoms of poisoning by a destroying angel may not appear until several hours after ingestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death cap - a deadly mushroom (Amanita phalloides) closely related to the destroying angel
