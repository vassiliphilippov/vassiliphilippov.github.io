links course

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋks kɔrs / (AmE), / lɪŋks kɔːs / (BrE)
a golf course, especially one situated on coastal sandy ground with few trees and undulating terrain
golf course on sandy coastal ground

Examples

The historic links course was shaped by natural dunes along the coastline.
Playing on a true links course requires adapting to windy conditions and firm ground.
The championship will be held at a famous Scottish links course next summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golf course - a course where golf is played

Etymology

From Scottish English ‘links’ meaning sandy coastal ground, commonly used for golf courses.
