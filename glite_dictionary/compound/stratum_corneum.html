stratum corneum

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtəm kɔrˈniəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm ˈkɔːniəm / (BrE)
the outermost layer of the epidermis, composed of dead cells that are continually shed
outermost layer of the epidermis
medical

Examples

Daily exfoliation can help remove dead cells from the stratum corneum and reveal fresher skin beneath.
The thickness of the stratum corneum varies depending on the area of the body.
A damaged stratum corneum can result in increased skin sensitivity and dryness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: epidermis - the outer layer of skin in vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'stratum' (layer) and 'corneus' (horny, of horn), referring to its tough, protective nature.
