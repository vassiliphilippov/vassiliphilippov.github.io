severe acute respiratory syndrome

1. NOUN

/ səˈvɪr əˈkjuːt rɪˌspɪrətɔri ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪə əˈkjuːt rɪˌspɪərəˈtɒri ˈsɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
a serious and sometimes fatal respiratory disease, first identified in China in 2003, marked by fever, cough, and breathing difficulties
serious respiratory disease identified in 2003
medical

Examples

The outbreak of severe acute respiratory syndrome in 2003 caused widespread concern in Asia and beyond.
Doctors quickly recognized the symptoms of severe acute respiratory syndrome in the new patient.
Precautions were taken in airports to prevent the spread of severe acute respiratory syndrome.

Etymology

Formed from medical terms: 'severe', 'acute', 'respiratory', and 'syndrome'. Commonly abbreviated as SARS.
