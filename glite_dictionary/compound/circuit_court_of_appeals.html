circuit court of appeals

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːkɪt ˌkɔrt əv əˈpilz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkɪt ˌkɔːt əv əˈpiːlz / (BrE)
one of the twelve federal courts in the United States that reviews cases from district courts within a particular group of states, known as a circuit
federal appellate court in the U.S.
legal american

Examples

The case was heard and decided by the circuit court of appeals for the Ninth Circuit.
Attorneys filed an appeal with the circuit court of appeals after losing in the district court.
The circuit court of appeals plays a critical role in shaping U.S. federal law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court of appeals - a court that hears appeals from lower courts
  • Degree Lower: district court - a lower federal trial court in the U.S. judicial system
