Eurasian otter

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈreɪʒən ˈɑtər / (AmE), / jʊəˈreɪʒən ˈɒtə / (BrE)
a species of otter native to Europe and Asia, known for its dense fur and aquatic lifestyle
species of otter found in Europe and Asia

Examples

Conservation efforts have helped the population of the Eurasian otter increase across several European countries.
The Eurasian otter is distinguished by its sleek, waterproof fur and webbed feet.
Researchers observed a family of Eurasian otters playing along the riverbank in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otter - a semiaquatic mammal known for its playful nature
