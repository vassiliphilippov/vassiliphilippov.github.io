Great White Way

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt waɪt weɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪt waɪt weɪ / (BrE)
the nickname for a stretch of Broadway in Manhattan, especially around Times Square, renowned for its bright lights and theaters
nickname for Broadway's theater district in Manhattan

Examples

Many aspiring actors dream of performing on the Great White Way one day.
The Great White Way comes alive every night with dazzling lights and world-class productions.
Tickets to the latest show on the Great White Way sold out within hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Broadway - main theater district in New York City

Etymology

Named for the bright electric lights that illuminated Broadway's theaters in the early 20th century.
