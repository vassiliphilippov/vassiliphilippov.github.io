gentian family

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnʃən ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnʃən ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of chiefly herbaceous flowering plants (Gentianaceae), often with showy, funnel-shaped flowers, some of which are grown as ornamentals
family of flowering plants (Gentianaceae)

Examples

Many alpine plants belong to the gentian family, boasting brightly colored blossoms.
The gentian family includes several species prized for their medicinal properties.
Gardeners often cultivate species from the gentian family for their vibrant blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gentianaceae - the botanical family name for the gentian family
