Cycladic culture

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈklædɪk ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / sɪˈklædɪk ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the Bronze Age civilization that flourished on the Cyclades islands in the southern Aegean Sea from about 3000 to 1100 BC
ancient Aegean Bronze Age civilization

Examples

Artifacts from the Cycladic culture are renowned for their minimalist marble figurines.
The exhibition showcased pottery and sculptures characteristic of the Cycladic culture.
Scholars debate the connections between Minoan civilization and the Cycladic culture.

Etymology

From the name of the Cyclades islands, derived from Greek Kyklades (“encircling islands”), and 'culture'.
