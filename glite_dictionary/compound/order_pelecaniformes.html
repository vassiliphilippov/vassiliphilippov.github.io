order Pelecaniformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌpɛlɪˌkænɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌpɛlɪˌkænɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of aquatic birds that includes pelicans, cormorants, gannets, and related species.
taxonomic order of aquatic birds

Examples

The order Pelecaniformes contains birds that are skilled at catching fish in water.
Pelicans are perhaps the most well-known members of the order Pelecaniformes.
Students studied the anatomical features unique to the order Pelecaniformes during their ornithology class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelican - Large water bird with a distinctive pouch under its beak

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Pelecaniformes', based on 'Pelecanus' (pelican) + Latin '-formes' (having the form of).
