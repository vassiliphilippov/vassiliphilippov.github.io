phylum Ectoprocta

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm ˌɛktəˈprɑktə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm ˌɛktəˈprɒktə / (BrE)
A major division of aquatic invertebrate animals, often considered coextensive with or a subphylum of Bryozoa, comprising moss animals.
aquatic invertebrate animal phylum (Bryozoa)
medical

Examples

Most members of the phylum Ectoprocta are colonial and found in freshwater or marine environments.
Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the phylum Ectoprocta in the coral reefs.
The phylum Ectoprocta is often studied for its complex colony structures and filter-feeding methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bryozoa - A phylum of aquatic invertebrate animals also known as moss animals

Etymology

From Greek 'ektos' (outside) and 'proktos' (anus), referring to the location of the anus in these organisms.
