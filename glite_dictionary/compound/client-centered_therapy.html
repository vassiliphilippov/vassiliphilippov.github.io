client-centered therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪənt ˌsɛntɚd ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˈklaɪənt ˌsɛntəd ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
A method of psychotherapy in which the client guides the direction and pace of sessions, emphasizing their personal experience and agency.
psychotherapy method focusing on client direction
medical

Examples

Client-centered therapy empowers individuals to lead the conversation and focus on their own concerns.
In client-centered therapy, the therapist shows empathy and unconditional positive regard for the client.
Many people find client-centered therapy helpful for building self-awareness and achieving personal growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: person-centered therapy - A synonym for client-centered therapy, emphasizing attention to the individual rather than the problem
  • Degree Higher: psychotherapy - The treatment of mental disorder by psychological rather than medical means
  • Degree Lower: counseling - Professional guidance using psychological methods, often less intensive than psychotherapy

Etymology

Developed by the American psychologist Carl Rogers in the 1940s; 'client-centered' emphasizes the importance of the individual's perspective.
