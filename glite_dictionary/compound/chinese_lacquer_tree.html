Chinese lacquer tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈlæk.ər triː / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌɪ.niːz ˈlak.ə triː / (BrE)
A small tree native to Asia, especially China and Japan, that produces a toxic sap used to make lacquer.
tree producing lacquer sap in Asia

Examples

The Chinese lacquer tree is cultivated for its valuable sap used in traditional lacquerware.
Workers carefully collect sap from the Chinese lacquer tree, wearing protective gear because it is toxic.
Lacquerware artisans value the Chinese lacquer tree for the quality of its natural resin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquer tree - tree that yields a toxic sap for making lacquer
