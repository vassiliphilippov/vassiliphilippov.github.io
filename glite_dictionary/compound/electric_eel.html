electric eel

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktrɪk ˈil / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪk ˈiːl / (BrE)
A large eel-shaped freshwater fish of South America able to produce electric shocks with specialized organs in its body.
South American freshwater fish that generates electricity

Examples

The electric eel can generate powerful bursts of electricity to stun its prey.
During our trip to the Amazon, we spotted a massive electric eel gliding through the murky water.
Scientists study the electric eel to learn how animals generate and control electrical energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eel - a long, snake-like fish
