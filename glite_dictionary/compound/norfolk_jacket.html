Norfolk jacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔr.fək ˈdʒæk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.fək ˈdʒæk.ɪt / (BrE)
a loose-fitting, single-breasted jacket, often with a belt and pleats, traditionally worn for country sports
loose-fitting belted jacket with pleats

Examples

He wore a Norfolk jacket and tweed trousers while hiking in the countryside.
The Norfolk jacket was once a popular choice for British hunters and sportsmen.
She bought a vintage Norfolk jacket at the antique clothing store.

Etymology

Named after the county of Norfolk in England, where the style became popular.
