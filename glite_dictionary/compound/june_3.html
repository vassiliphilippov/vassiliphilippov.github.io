June 3

1. NOUN

/ dʒun θri / (AmE), / dʒuːn θriː / (BrE)
a holiday observed in some southern states of the United States to mark the birth date of Jefferson Davis, president of the Confederacy during the American Civil War
U.S. holiday marking Jefferson Davis's birthday

Examples

Several southern states still recognize June 3 as Jefferson Davis's birthday.
The celebration on June 3 often includes historical reenactments and speeches.
Many schools in the region are closed in observance of June 3.
