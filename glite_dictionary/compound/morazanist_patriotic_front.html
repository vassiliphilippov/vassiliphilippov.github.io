Morazanist Patriotic Front

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɔːrəˈzænɪst pəˈtriːətɪk frʌnt / (AmE), / ˌmɒrəˈzanɪst pəˈtriːətɪk frʌnt / (BrE)
a radical leftist group formed in Honduras in the late 1980s aiming to prevent United States intervention in Honduran economic and political affairs, known for militant activities
Honduran radical leftist militant group (1980s)

Examples

The Morazanist Patriotic Front carried out several attacks in Honduras during the late 1980s.
Analysts debated the influence of the Morazanist Patriotic Front on Central American politics.
The government sought to negotiate with the Morazanist Patriotic Front to end the violence.

Etymology

Named after Francisco Morazán, a 19th-century Honduran national hero, combined with 'patriotic front'.
