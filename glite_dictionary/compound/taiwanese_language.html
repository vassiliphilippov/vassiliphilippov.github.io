Taiwanese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌtaɪwəˈniːz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌtaɪwəˈniːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Sinitic language spoken mainly in Taiwan and Fukien province, also known as Hokkien or Minnan.
Chinese language variety spoken in Taiwan and Fujian

Examples

Many people in southern Taiwan speak Taiwanese language at home with their families.
Some schools offer courses in the Taiwanese language to preserve local culture.
The Taiwanese language uses different pronunciation and vocabulary from Mandarin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hokkien - A variety of Chinese closely related to Taiwanese language
  • Antonym: Mandarin - The official state language of China, distinct from Taiwanese

Etymology

Taiwanese, from Taiwan + -ese (denoting language or people); language from Latin lingua (tongue, language).
