ringworm bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋwɝm ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋwəːm ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a tropical shrub, especially in the Americas, with yellow flowers and large leaves, whose juice is used to treat ringworm and poisonous bites; sometimes classified in the genus Cassia
tropical shrub used as cure for ringworm

Examples

The juice extracted from the ringworm bush is traditionally applied to fungal skin infections.
We spotted several ringworm bushes with bright yellow flowers growing by the forest path.
Some herbal remedies use leaves of the ringworm bush to treat various skin ailments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassia - a genus of plants with yellow flowers, to which the ringworm bush is sometimes assigned
