simple protein

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl proʊˈtiːn / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl prəʊˈtiːn / (BrE)
a protein that yields only amino acids when hydrolyzed, containing no non-protein groups
protein yielding only amino acids on hydrolysis

Examples

Albumin is an example of a simple protein found in egg whites.
When hydrolyzed, simple proteins produce only amino acids and no other substances.
Scientists distinguish simple protein from conjugated protein in biochemical studies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conjugated protein - a protein that yields both amino acids and other substances when hydrolyzed
