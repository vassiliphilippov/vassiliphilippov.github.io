dirty money

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːti ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti ˈmʌni / (BrE)
Money or assets obtained by illegal or dishonest means, especially through crime.
illegally obtained money or assets

Examples

The government is taking strict measures to prevent dirty money from entering the financial system.
Many criminals try to disguise dirty money through complex financial transactions.
She refused to accept dirty money for her charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-gotten gains - money or possessions acquired through illegal or unethical means
  • Antonym: clean money - money obtained through legal and legitimate means

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːdi ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money that has been obtained illegally or through dishonest means
illegally obtained money

Examples

The authorities seized millions of dollars in dirty money during the raid.
He was arrested on suspicion of laundering dirty money through offshore accounts.
Many businesses refuse to accept investments linked to dirty money.

Synset Examples

They refused to accept donations from sources linked to dirty money.
She wanted nothing to do with his dirty money schemes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: laundered money - money that has been made to appear legal after being illegally obtained
