Chinook language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈnʊk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the indigenous language of the Chinook people, belonging to the Penutian language family, traditionally spoken in the Pacific Northwest region of North America
indigenous language of the Chinook people

Examples

The Chinook language was traditionally spoken along the lower Columbia River.
Today, only a few elders are fluent in the Chinook language.
There are ongoing efforts to preserve and revitalize the Chinook language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinook Jargon - simplified trade language based partly on Chinook

Etymology

Named after the Chinook people, indigenous to the Pacific Northwest, with uncertain further origin.
