Yenisei-Samoyed language

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛnɪˌseɪ səˈmɔɪɛd ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌjɛnɪˌseɪ səˈmɔɪɛd ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a now-extinct Uralic language once spoken by the Yeniseian people in northern Siberia.
extinct Uralic language of Siberia

Examples

The Yenisei-Samoyed language is of great interest to researchers studying ancient Siberian cultures.
Few written records exist for the Yenisei-Samoyed language, making its reconstruction challenging for linguists.
The last known speaker of the Yenisei-Samoyed language reportedly died in the early 19th century.

Etymology

From 'Yenisei', a river in Siberia, and 'Samoyed', an historical term for several indigenous peoples of northern Russia.
