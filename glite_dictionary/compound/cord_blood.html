cord blood

1. NOUN

/ kɔrd blʌd / (AmE), / kɔːd blʌd / (BrE)
blood that is collected from the umbilical cord and placenta immediately after birth, often used for medical research or transplantation due to its high concentration of stem cells.
blood from the umbilical cord at birth
medical

Examples

Doctors collected cord blood from the newborn to use in stem cell research.
Many families choose to store their baby's cord blood for potential future medical treatments.
The use of cord blood has advanced therapies for various blood disorders.
