pox doctor

1. NOUN

/ pɑks ˈdɑktər / (AmE), / pɒks ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
(historical) A physician who treated smallpox or other contagious diseases, especially during outbreaks.
historical doctor treating smallpox
archaic

Examples

During the epidemic, the townspeople relied on the pox doctor for treatment and advice.
A pox doctor would travel between villages, carrying remedies and protective masks.
Stories of the pox doctor's bravery became part of the town's folklore.

Synset Examples

The pox doctor's clerk knew every detail about the outbreak.
As the pox doctor's clerk, he scheduled all vaccinations.
The pox doctor's clerk handled all patient inquiries efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague doctor - historical physician treating plague victims
