bay willow

1. NOUN

/ beɪ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / beɪ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A willow tree (Salix pentandra) native to Europe, with glossy, leathery leaves, often found in wetlands and widely naturalized in eastern North America.
a European willow tree with glossy leaves

Examples

The bay willow is often planted to help stabilize riverbanks and prevent erosion.
In the marshy areas, you can see the glossy leaves of the bay willow shimmering in the sun.
Gardeners value the bay willow for its attractive appearance and resilience in damp soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically with slender branches and narrow leaves
