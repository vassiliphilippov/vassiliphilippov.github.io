Kamia language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmiə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæmɪə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Kamia people, indigenous to parts of southern California and northern Baja California.
language of the Kamia people

Examples

Very few fluent speakers of the Kamia language remain today.
Linguists are documenting the Kamia language to help preserve it for future generations.
The dictionary project aims to record as many words as possible from the Kamia language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman language - a group of related Native American languages spoken in the southwestern US and northwestern Mexico
