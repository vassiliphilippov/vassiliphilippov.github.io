tsunami of reaction

1. NOUN

/ tsuˈnɑmi əv riˈækʃən / (AmE), / tsuˈnɑːmi əv rɪˈækʃən / (BrE)
A sudden and overwhelming surge of responses or reactions, often following an event or announcement.
an overwhelming surge of responses

Examples

The celebrity's tweet triggered a tsunami of reaction from her followers.
After the CEO resigned, the company faced a tsunami of reaction from investors and employees alike.
The controversial law produced a tsunami of reaction across social media platforms.

Synset Examples

The announcement caused a tsunami of reactions online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave of reaction - a large number of responses occurring at once
  • Synonym: flood of response - an overwhelming number of responses

Etymology

From Japanese *tsunami* (harbor wave), used metaphorically in English to describe a massive surge; combined with 'reaction'.
