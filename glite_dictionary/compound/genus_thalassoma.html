genus Thalassoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs θəˌlæsˈoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs θəˌlæsˈəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the wrasse family (Labridae), containing about 28 species, commonly known as wrasses.
a genus of wrasses (marine fish)

Examples

Many colorful species within the genus Thalassoma are popular in saltwater aquariums.
Researchers studied the mating behaviors of several wrasses from the genus Thalassoma.
The genus Thalassoma includes fish found in tropical and subtropical reefs around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrasse - a type of brightly colored marine fish in the family Labridae
  • Degree Higher: Labridae - the biological family that includes wrasses

Etymology

From New Latin 'Thalassoma,' from Greek 'thalassa' (sea) + 'sōma' (body).
