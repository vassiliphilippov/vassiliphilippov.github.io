curtain airbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːtn̩ ˈɛrˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtn̩ ˈeəˌbæɡ / (BrE)
a type of airbag installed along the roof edge of a vehicle, which inflates during a collision to protect occupants' heads and provide rollover protection
automotive side-impact airbag

Examples

The new car model features a curtain airbag for added protection in side collisions.
During the accident, the curtain airbag deployed instantly, shielding the passengers' heads from impact.
Curtain airbags are now standard in many vehicles to help prevent serious injuries during rollovers.

Synset Examples

Curtain airbags have been said to reduce brain injury or fatalities by up to 45% in a side impact with an SUV.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airbag - a safety device in vehicles that inflates rapidly during a collision

Etymology

From 'curtain' (referring to its shape when deployed) + 'airbag'.
