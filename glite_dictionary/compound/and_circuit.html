AND circuit

1. NOUN

/ ænd ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ænd ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electronic circuit that produces an output signal only when all its input signals are present, following the logic of the AND operation
circuit producing output only when all inputs are on

Examples

The AND circuit ensures that the door opens only when both security codes are entered correctly.
By connecting sensors to an AND circuit, the machine activates only under safe conditions.
Engineers tested the AND circuit to verify it only outputs a signal when all switches are turned on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: AND gate - a logic gate that implements the AND operation
  • Antonym: OR circuit - a circuit that fires when any of its inputs fire

Etymology

From 'AND', the logical operation, and 'circuit'. 'AND' as an operator derives from the English conjunction.
