methyl ethyl ketone

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːθəl ˈɛθəl kiːˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəl ˈɛθəl ˈkiːtəʊn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid ketone used as a solvent in resins, paints, lacquers, adhesives, and cleaning fluids
a solvent and industrial chemical

Examples

The factory uses methyl ethyl ketone as a solvent for producing plastics.
Due to its strong dissolving power, methyl ethyl ketone is a popular choice in paint removers.
Proper ventilation is important when working with methyl ethyl ketone to avoid health risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butanone - another name for methyl ethyl ketone (the chemical compound)

Etymology

From the names of its two alkyl groups: methyl (from methane) and ethyl (from ethane), plus 'ketone' (from German 'Keton', from 'acetone').
