truth serum

1. NOUN

/ truθ ˈsɪrəm / (AmE), / truːθ ˈsɪəm / (BrE)
A drug, usually administered intravenously, that induces relaxation and lowers inhibitions, sometimes used in attempts to make someone reveal information.
drug believed to induce truth-telling

Examples

The detective considered using a truth serum to get a confession from the suspect.
Some people believe that truth serum can force anyone to reveal their secrets.
Doctors rarely use truth serum today because its effects are unreliable and controversial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium pentothal - a specific chemical used as a truth serum
  • Synonym: barbiturate - a class of drugs to which truth serums often belong
