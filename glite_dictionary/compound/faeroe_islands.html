Faeroe Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈfer.oʊ ˌaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈfeə.rəʊ ˌaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of 21 volcanic islands in the North Atlantic, situated between Iceland and the Shetland Islands, constituting a self-governing territory within the Kingdom of Denmark
North Atlantic island group (Faroes)

Examples

The Faeroe Islands are known for their dramatic cliffs and sweeping ocean views.
He traveled to the Faeroe Islands last summer to explore their rich Viking history.
The weather on the Faeroe Islands can change rapidly, even within a single day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Faroe Islands - Alternative spelling for the same group of North Atlantic islands

Etymology

From Danish 'Færøerne', meaning 'Sheep Islands'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈfɛroʊ ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈfeərəʊ ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of self-governing islands in the North Atlantic, which are a possession of Denmark
self-governing Danish islands in North Atlantic

Examples

The Faeroe Islands are known for their stunning cliffs and unspoiled nature.
She traveled to the Faeroe Islands for research on its unique bird species.
Fishing is a major industry on the Faeroe Islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Faroe Islands - self-governing Danish islands in the North Atlantic

Etymology

From Danish 'Færøerne', meaning 'Sheep Islands'.
