soil horizon

1. NOUN

/ sɔɪl həˈraɪzn̩ / (AmE), / sɔɪl həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
A distinct layer within a soil profile, differing in physical, chemical, or biological properties from layers above or below it.
distinct layer in a soil profile

Examples

The top soil horizon is rich in organic material from decayed plants and animals.
Scientists studied each soil horizon to better understand how nutrients move through the earth.
The color and texture of a soil horizon can reveal past environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil profile - a vertical section of soil showing its horizons
