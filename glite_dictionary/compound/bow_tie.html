bow tie

1. NOUN

/ boʊ taɪ / (AmE), / bəʊ taɪ / (BrE)
A narrow piece of cloth tied around the collar in a bow, worn especially by men as a formal necktie.
a tie fastened in a bow, worn around the neck

Examples

He wore a black bow tie to the formal dinner.
Her grandfather always dressed elegantly, complete with a crisp shirt and a bow tie.
The magician adjusted his bow tie before stepping onto the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necktie - a long, narrow piece of cloth worn around the neck and tied in front

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ taɪ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ taɪ / (BrE)
A type of necktie worn by men, consisting of a fabric strip tied in a symmetrical bow, usually worn for formal occasions.
tie fastened in a bow, often formal

Examples

He wore a crisp white shirt and a silk bow tie to the wedding.
The magician adjusted his red bow tie before performing his trick.
Some people find tying a bow tie challenging without a mirror.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necktie - a band of decorative fabric worn around the neck, tied in a knot
