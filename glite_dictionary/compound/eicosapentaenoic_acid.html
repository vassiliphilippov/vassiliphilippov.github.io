eicosapentaenoic acid

1. NOUN

/ iˌkoʊsəˌpɛntəˌenoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪkəʊsəˌpɛntəˌiːnəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an omega-3 fatty acid found in certain fish, consisting of 20 carbon atoms and 5 double bonds, important for human health
omega-3 fatty acid in fish
medical

Examples

Fish oil supplements are rich in eicosapentaenoic acid, which supports heart health.
Tuna and salmon contain high levels of eicosapentaenoic acid and other omega-3 fatty acids.
Researchers studied the effects of eicosapentaenoic acid on inflammation in the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: docosahexaenoic acid - another major omega-3 fatty acid found in fish oils
  • Degree Lower: arachidonic acid - a related fatty acid, precursor in metabolic processes

Etymology

From Greek 'eikosi' (twenty) and 'penta' (five), describing its chemical structure.
