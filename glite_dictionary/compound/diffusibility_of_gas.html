diffusibility of gas

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌfjuːsəˈbɪlɪti əv ɡæs / (AmE), / dɪˌfjuːsəˈbɪlɪti əv ɡæs / (BrE)
the ability of a gas to spread or disperse evenly through another substance, such as a liquid or another gas
ability of a gas to disperse in a medium
medical

Examples

The diffusibility of gas is an important factor in respiratory physiology.
Scientists study the diffusibility of gas when designing air filtration systems.
Temperature strongly influences the diffusibility of gas in liquids.

Synset Examples

The diffusibility of gases varies with temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the process of spreading of particles through a medium

Etymology

From 'diffusibility,' ultimately from Latin 'diffundere' (to spread out), and 'gas,' coined by Jan Baptist van Helmont.
