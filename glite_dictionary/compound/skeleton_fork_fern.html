skeleton fork fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛlətən fɔrk fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈskɛlɪtən fɔːk fɜːn / (BrE)
a pantropical whisk fern with usually dull yellow branches and tiny leaves, found as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant in America, Japan, and Australia
species of whisk fern with yellow branches

Examples

The skeleton fork fern thrives in humid forests where it can grow on tree bark.
Botanists discovered a rare skeleton fork fern specimen in the Australian rainforest.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate the skeleton fork fern for its unique, yellowish branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Psilotum nudum - scientific name for skeleton fork fern
