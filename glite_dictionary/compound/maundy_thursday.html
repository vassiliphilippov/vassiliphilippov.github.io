Maundy Thursday

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔndi ˈθɜrzdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːndi ˈθɜːzdeɪ / (BrE)
The Thursday before Easter, commemorating the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with the Apostles.
Christian holy day before Easter

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Maundy Thursday to commemorate the Last Supper.
Maundy Thursday marks the beginning of the Easter Triduum in the Christian calendar.
The Queen participates in the annual distribution of alms on Maundy Thursday in the United Kingdom.

Etymology

From Old French 'mandé', from Latin 'mandatum' (commandment), referring to Jesus' commandment to love one another, given at the Last Supper.
