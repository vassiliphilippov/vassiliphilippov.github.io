lumber jacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚ ˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbə ˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
a short, warm outer jacket, typically made of plaid wool, often associated with outdoor work or casual wear
short, warm outer jacket

Examples

He wore a thick lumber jacket to keep out the cold during the hike.
I bought a red plaid lumber jacket for my fall wardrobe.
A classic lumber jacket pairs well with jeans and boots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaid jacket - a jacket made of plaid-patterned fabric, similar style
  • Degree Higher: parka - a thick, insulated outer jacket with a hood, typically longer
  • Degree Lower: vest - a sleeveless garment for the upper body, lighter than a jacket

Etymology

From 'lumber' (as in lumberjack, a person who fells trees), relating to the traditional attire of lumberjacks.
