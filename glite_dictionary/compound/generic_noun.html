generic noun

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛrɪk naʊn / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛrɪk naʊn / (BrE)
a noun that does not specify either masculine or feminine gender, but refers to any member or all members of a class without regard to gender
noun referring to any gender

Examples

A generic noun like 'parent' can refer to either a mother or a father.
In English, many professions are described using a generic noun rather than gender-specific terms.
The teacher used the generic noun 'actor' to include both men and women in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common noun - a noun that denotes a class of objects or a concept rather than a particular individual
  • Antonym: gendered noun - a noun that is explicitly masculine or feminine in form or usage
