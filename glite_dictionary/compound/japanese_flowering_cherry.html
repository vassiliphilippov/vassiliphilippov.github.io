Japanese flowering cherry

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs ˈflaʊərɪŋ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈflaʊərɪŋ ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
an ornamental tree (Prunus serrulata) native to East Asia, widely cultivated for its decorative white or pink blossoms and inedible fruit
ornamental tree with decorative white or pink blossoms

Examples

The Japanese flowering cherry is prized for its beautiful springtime blossoms.
Many parks feature avenues lined with Japanese flowering cherry trees.
A Japanese flowering cherry in full bloom attracts photographers and sightseers alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry tree - a tree that produces cherries or cherry blossoms

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈflaʊərɪŋ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈflaʊərɪŋ ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
an ornamental tree of the species Prunus serrulata, cultivated for its showy pink blossoms and inedible fruit
ornamental tree with pink blossoms

Examples

Each spring, the Japanese flowering cherry trees in the park attract thousands of visitors.
She planted a Japanese flowering cherry in her garden for its stunning pink blooms.
The Japanese flowering cherry is renowned for its beautiful blossoms but does not bear edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry blossom - the flower of various cherry trees, especially those of Japanese origin
