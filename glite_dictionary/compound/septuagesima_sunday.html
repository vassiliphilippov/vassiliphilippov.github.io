Septuagesima Sunday

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛptjuˈædʒɪsmə ˈsʌndeɪ / (AmE), / ˌsɛptjuːəˈdʒɛsɪmə ˈsʌndeɪ / (BrE)
the third Sunday before Lent (the ninth before Easter), formerly observed as the beginning of the pre-Lenten season in the Christian liturgical calendar.
third Sunday before Lent in Christian calendar

Examples

Many churches used to observe Septuagesima Sunday with special services and hymns.
Before the changes to the liturgical calendar, Septuagesima Sunday marked the start of a pre-Lenten period.
During Septuagesima Sunday, some congregations begin a gradual preparation for Lent.

Etymology

From Latin 'septuagesimus' meaning 'seventieth', referring to about seventy days before Easter.
