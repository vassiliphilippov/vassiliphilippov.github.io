ball nightshade

1. NOUN

/ bɔl ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / bɔːl ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
a coarse, prickly weed with pale yellow flowers and yellow, berrylike fruit, common in southern and eastern United States
a prickly weed with yellow fruit

Examples

The field was overrun by ball nightshade after the heavy rains in early summer.
Gardeners often struggle to remove ball nightshade because of its tough, prickly stems.
The yellow berries of the ball nightshade are sometimes mistaken for edible fruit, but they can be toxic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solanum torvum - the scientific name for ball nightshade
  • Degree Higher: nightshade - any plant of the genus Solanum, often with poisonous properties
