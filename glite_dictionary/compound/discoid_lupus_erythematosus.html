discoid lupus erythematosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkɔɪd ˈluːpəs ˌɛrɪˌθiːməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɔɪd ˈluː.pəs ˌɛr.ɪˌθiː.məˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A chronic skin disease, mainly affecting women between ages 20 and 40, characterized by red lesions on the cheeks and bridge of the nose.
chronic autoimmune skin disease with red facial lesions
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed her with discoid lupus erythematosus after examining the distinctive rash on her cheeks.
Treatment for discoid lupus erythematosus usually involves topical steroids to reduce inflammation.
Because discoid lupus erythematosus can leave scars, early treatment is recommended to prevent permanent skin damage.

Etymology

From 'discoid' (disk-shaped), 'lupus' (Latin for 'wolf'), and 'erythematosus' (from Greek 'erythēma' meaning 'redness').
