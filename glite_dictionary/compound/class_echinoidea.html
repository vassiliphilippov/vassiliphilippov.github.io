class Echinoidea

1. NOUN

/ klæs ˌɛk.ɪˈnɔɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / klɑːs ˌɛk.ɪˈnɔɪ.di.ə / (BrE)
A class of marine echinoderms that includes sea urchins and sand dollars, characterized by a spiny shell and radial symmetry.
class of marine animals: sea urchins, sand dollars

Examples

The class Echinoidea contains both sea urchins and sand dollars, diverse in form and habitat.
Marine biologists study the evolutionary adaptations of the class Echinoidea to understand their role in ocean ecosystems.
Fossils from the class Echinoidea provide valuable insights into ancient marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea urchin - marine animal with a spiny shell, member of class Echinoidea
  • Synonym: sand dollar - a type of flattened, burrowing echinoid, member of class Echinoidea

Etymology

From Greek 'echinos' (hedgehog, sea urchin) + '-oidea' (resembling).
