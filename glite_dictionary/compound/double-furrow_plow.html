double-furrow plow

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈfɝːroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈfʌrə plau / (BrE)
A plow equipped with two moldboards, allowing it to turn over two furrows of soil at once.
plow with two moldboards

Examples

The farmer used a double-furrow plow to prepare the large field for planting.
Early spring is the best time to operate a double-furrow plow on heavy soils.
The purchase of a double-furrow plow helped increase their farm's efficiency.

Synset Examples

Heavy volcanic loam soils, such as are found in New Zealand, require the use of four heavy draught horses to pull a double-furrow plough.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single-furrow plow - a plow with one moldboard
  • Degree Higher: multiple-furrow plow - a plow with more than two moldboards
