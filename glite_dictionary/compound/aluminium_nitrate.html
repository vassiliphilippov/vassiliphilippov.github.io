aluminium nitrate

1. NOUN

/ əˌluːmɪnəm ˈnaɪ.treɪt / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪnɪəm ˈnaɪ.treɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt formed from aluminium and nitric acid, typically found as a nonahydrate and used in tanning, dyeing, and as a chemical reagent
a white crystalline aluminium salt used in chemistry
medical

Examples

The laboratory ordered new supplies of aluminium nitrate for chemical synthesis.
When heated, aluminium nitrate decomposes to release nitrogen dioxide gas.
Aluminium nitrate is commonly used as a catalyst in organic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aluminium sulfate - a salt of aluminium and sulfuric acid, used in water purification
  • Degree Lower: nitrate - a salt or ester of nitric acid, containing the anion NO3−

Etymology

From 'aluminium' and 'nitrate', reflecting its composition of aluminium and nitrate ions.
