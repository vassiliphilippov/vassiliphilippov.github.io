metatarsal artery

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈtɑrsəl ˈɑrtɚi / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈtɑːsəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
Any of the arteries supplying blood to the metatarsal region of the foot, including both dorsal and plantar sides.
artery in the foot's metatarsal region
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully traced the metatarsal artery during the foot operation.
Damage to the metatarsal artery can seriously affect blood flow to the toes.
An injury to a metatarsal artery may cause excessive bleeding in the foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood from the heart to the body

Etymology

From Greek meta- (after, beyond) + tarsos (ankle) and Latin arteria (artery).
