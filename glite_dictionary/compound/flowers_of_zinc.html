flowers of zinc

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊɚz əv zɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈflaʊəz əv zɪŋk / (BrE)
A white powder, zinc oxide, used as a pigment, in cosmetics, glass, inks, and zinc ointment.
zinc oxide; white powder pigment
medical

Examples

Pharmacists once prepared flowers of zinc for use in ointments to treat skin irritations.
The white pigment in the paint was derived from flowers of zinc.
Old chemistry textbooks often list flowers of zinc under the names of mineral substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc oxide - a white powder used as a pigment and in ointments; ZnO

Etymology

Early usage referred to the woolly deposits formed when zinc is burned; here 'flowers' means a fine powder.
