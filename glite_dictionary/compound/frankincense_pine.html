frankincense pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋkɪnˌsɛns paɪn / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkɪnsɛns paɪn / (BrE)
a tall, spreading pine tree of the southeastern United States with reddish-brown fissured bark and a full, bushy crown, distinguished by its three-needled clusters
a pine tree native to southeastern US

Examples

A mature frankincense pine can reach impressive heights in the forests of Georgia.
The bark of the frankincense pine is noted for its deep, reddish fissures.
Botanists studied the three-needled leaves of the frankincense pine to distinguish it from other species.
