pork jelly

1. NOUN

/ pɔrk ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / pɔːk ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
A savory jelly or aspic made from low-grade cuts of pork, such as trotters, containing a high amount of connective tissue, giving it a gelatinous texture when cooled.
savory jelly made from pork cuts

Examples

My grandmother prepares pork jelly every winter using pig's feet and spices.
For the holiday feast, they served slices of homemade pork jelly with pickles and bread.
Eastern European cuisine often features pork jelly as a classic appetizer.

Synset Examples

Pork jelly is a popular appetizer and, nowadays, is sometimes prepared in a more modern version using lean meat, with or without pig leftovers which are substituted with store-bought gelatin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspic - a savory jelly made with meat stock, often used to encase other ingredients
