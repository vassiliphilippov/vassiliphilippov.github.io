seller's market

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlɚz ˈmɑrkət / (AmE), / ˈsɛləz ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a market condition in which demand for goods or property exceeds supply, giving sellers an advantage over buyers
market favoring sellers due to high demand

Examples

With so few houses available, it is a seller's market right now.
In a seller's market, home prices tend to rise quickly due to competition.
During the boom, every real estate agent said it was clearly a seller's market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: buyer's market - a market in which supply exceeds demand, giving buyers an advantage

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlɚz ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛləz ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a market condition in which sellers have an advantage, typically because demand exceeds supply, leading to higher prices and faster sales
market where sellers have an advantage

Examples

The current housing shortage has created a true seller's market in many cities.
During a seller's market, homeowners often receive multiple offers above the asking price.
It's harder for buyers to negotiate in a seller's market because competition is so high.

Synset Examples

Real estate experts predict a continued seller's market this year.
It's a seller's market, so expect bidding wars on houses.
In a seller's market, homes sell quickly and for high prices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: buyer's market - a market condition where buyers have an advantage, with supply exceeding demand
