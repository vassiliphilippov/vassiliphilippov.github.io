autoclaved concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtəˌkleɪvd kɑnˈkrit / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌkleɪvd ˈkɒnkriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight, precast concrete building material made by curing concrete under high pressure and temperature in an autoclave, offering structural strength as well as insulation and resistance to fire and mold
lightweight, precast concrete cured in an autoclave

Examples

Buildings constructed with autoclaved concrete can achieve significant energy savings thanks to its insulating properties.
Many architects prefer autoclaved concrete for its combination of strength and fire resistance in modern construction.
The use of autoclaved concrete panels helped the project meet strict environmental standards.

Synset Examples

Better thermal efficiency of AAC makes it suitable for use in areas with extreme temperature as it eliminates need for separate materials for construction and insulation leading to faster construction and savings.

Etymology

From 'autoclave', a pressurized steam chamber, and 'concrete', from Latin 'concretus' (condensed, hardened).
