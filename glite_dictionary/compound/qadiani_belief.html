Qadiani belief

1. NOUN

/ kɑːdiˈɑːni bəˈliːf / (AmE), / kɑːdiˈɑːni bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
The set of religious doctrines associated with the Ahmadiyya movement, particularly as held by its followers, sometimes referred to as Qadianis.
religious doctrines of the Ahmadiyya movement

Examples

Many people misunderstand the core principles of Qadiani belief due to widespread misinformation.
Academic courses on comparative religion sometimes cover the origins and tenets of Qadiani belief.
Her views closely align with traditional Qadiani beliefs as taught by the movement's founders.

Synset Examples

She converted to Qadiani beliefs after extensive research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ahmadiyya - An Islamic religious movement founded in India in the 19th century

Etymology

From Qadian, the town in Punjab, India, where the Ahmadiyya movement was founded in the late 19th century.
