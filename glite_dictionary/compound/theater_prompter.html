theater prompter

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːət̬ɚ ˈprɑːmp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə ˈprɒmp.tə / (BrE)
a person who assists a performer during a play by discreetly providing forgotten lines or cues
person giving forgotten lines to actors

Examples

During the performance, the theater prompter whispered a forgotten line to the nervous actor.
The theater prompter sat in the wings, ready to help if anyone forgot their lines.
Traditionally, a theater prompter would be hidden from view but close enough to be heard by the cast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompter - person who gives cues or lines to actors
