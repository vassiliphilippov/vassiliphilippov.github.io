gladdon iris

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæd.ən ˈaɪ.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡlæd.ən ˈaɪ.rɪs / (BrE)
A species of iris with purple flowers and foul-smelling leaves, native to southern and western Europe and North Africa.
species of iris with purple flowers

Examples

The gladdon iris is often found growing wild along Mediterranean coastlines.
Gardeners sometimes avoid the gladdon iris because of the odor of its leaves.
She planted a row of gladdon irises to add color to the border of her garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - genus of plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From 'gladdon,' an old term for certain irises, of uncertain origin.
