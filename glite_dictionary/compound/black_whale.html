black whale

1. NOUN

/ blæk weɪl / (AmE), / blæk weɪl / (BrE)
a small, dark-colored whale found along the Atlantic coast of the United States, with the largest male acting as the leader of the group
small dark Atlantic whale; group leader is largest male

Examples

A pod of black whales was spotted near the New England coastline.
The black whale is recognized by its dark color and social behavior.
The largest male in the black whale school typically leads the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot whale - a small toothed whale known for group swimming, often called blackfish

2. NOUN

/ blæk weɪl / (AmE), / blæk weɪl / (BrE)
a large whale, especially the sperm whale, with a cavity in the head containing spermaceti and oil; also a source of ambergris
large whale, esp. sperm whale

Examples

The black whale was once extensively hunted for its valuable oil.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the black whale in the Pacific Ocean.
Ambergris, used in perfume, is sometimes found in the intestines of the black whale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sperm whale - large toothed whale with a large head containing spermaceti
