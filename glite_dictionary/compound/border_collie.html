Border collie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdər ˌkɑli / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə ˌkɒli / (BrE)
a breed of dog originally developed on the border between Scotland and England, known for its intelligence and herding abilities, typically having a black and white coat
breed of intelligent herding dog

Examples

The Border collie excelled in the agility competition, impressing judges with its speed and intelligence.
Many farmers rely on a Border collie to help manage their sheep and cattle.
We adopted a rescue Border collie last year, and she's incredibly quick to learn new tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collie - a breed of sheepdog, including Border collie and other varieties

Etymology

From 'border', referring to the Scottish-English border, and 'collie', a Scottish term for herding dogs.
