Stayman Winesap

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪ.mən ˈwaɪnˌsæp / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.mən ˈwaɪn.sæp / (BrE)
A crisp, tart variety of apple suitable for eating raw and for cooking.
variety of tart, crisp apple

Examples

The orchard boasts many Stayman Winesap trees that produce fruit each autumn.
She prefers to bake pies with Stayman Winesap apples because of their tart flavor.
You can enjoy Stayman Winesap apples fresh or use them in your favorite recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Winesap - a sweet-tart variety of American apple

Etymology

Named after Dr. Joseph Stayman, who developed the variety in Kansas in the late 19th century, and its relation to the Winesap apple.
