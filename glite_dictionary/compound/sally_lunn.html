Sally Lunn

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæli ˈlʌn / (AmE), / ˌsæli ˈlʌn / (BrE)
a type of flat, round, slightly sweet teacake, typically served hot and often associated with traditional English tea
a slightly sweet, round English teacake

Examples

We enjoyed a warm Sally Lunn with clotted cream at the tea shop.
The traditional Sally Lunn is often made with yeast and served hot.
She baked a batch of Sally Lunns for the afternoon tea party.

Etymology

The name is believed to derive from a woman named Sally Lunn in Bath, England, in the late 17th or early 18th century, though the exact origin is debated.
