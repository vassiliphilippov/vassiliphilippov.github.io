sophomoronic antic

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːfəˈmɔːrɑnɪk ˈæntɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɒfəˈmɒrɒnɪk ˈæntɪk / (BrE)
an immature or foolish action or behavior typical of someone who is a sophomore, especially in a humorous or critical sense
immature, foolish behavior typical of a sophomore

Examples

The teacher was unimpressed by his sophomoronic antic during the assembly.
Their constant sophomoronic antics made it hard for the group to focus on the project.
She apologized for her sophomoronic antics at the party last night.

Synset Examples

The group laughed at his sophomoronic antics.

Etymology

Blend of 'sophomore' and the pejorative suffix '-moronic', combined with 'antic.'
