long beech fern

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ bitʃ fɜrn / (AmE), / lɒŋ biːtʃ fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of beech fern (Phegopteris connectilis) native to North America and Eurasia, characterized by long, slender fronds.
a species of beech fern

Examples

The shady hillside was covered in long beech fern, giving the forest floor a delicate, lacy appearance.
Gardeners often choose long beech fern for its graceful fronds and ability to thrive in damp, wooded areas.
In the park, we identified several native plants, including the long beech fern near the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech fern - any fern of the genus Phegopteris
