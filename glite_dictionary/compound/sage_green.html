sage green

1. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ ɡriːn / (AmE), / seɪdʒ ɡriːn / (BrE)
A soft, muted greyish-green color resembling the leaves of the sage plant.
a soft greyish-green color

Examples

She painted the walls a calming shade of sage green to create a relaxing atmosphere.
The bridesmaids wore elegant dresses in sage green for the spring wedding.
The designer recommended adding sage green accents to complement the natural wood tones.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: olive green - a yellowish-green color resembling that of green olives
  • Degree Lower: mint green - a pale green color resembling that of mint leaves

Etymology

Formed from 'sage' (the herb, from Old French 'sauge', from Latin 'salvia') and 'green' (from Old English 'grēne').
