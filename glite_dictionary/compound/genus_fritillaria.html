genus Fritillaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs frɪˌtɪlɪˈɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs frɪˌtɪlɪˈeəriə / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian and North American plants in the lily family, known for their bell-shaped, often spotted flowers.
a genus of flowering plants (fritillary)

Examples

Many gardens feature genus Fritillaria for their unique, nodding flowers.
Several species within the genus Fritillaria are prized for ornamental use.
The checkered petals of the genus Fritillaria distinguish it from other lilies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fritillary - A common name for species in the genus Fritillaria, referring to both the plant and its distinctive flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'fritillus' (dice box), referring to the spotted pattern found on some species' flowers, plus the Latin 'genus' (kind, type).
