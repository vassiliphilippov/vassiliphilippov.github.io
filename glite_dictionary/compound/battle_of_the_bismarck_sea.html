battle of the Bismarck Sea

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ðə ˈbɪzmɑːrk siː / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ðə ˈbɪzmɑːk siː / (BrE)
A naval battle during World War II in which Allied land-based bombers destroyed a Japanese convoy in the Bismarck Sea in March 1943.
naval battle in World War II (1943, Bismarck Sea)

Examples

The battle of the Bismarck Sea marked a turning point in the Pacific campaign during World War II.
Historians often highlight the significance of the battle of the Bismarck Sea in reducing Japanese naval power.
A documentary examined strategies used by the Allies in the battle of the Bismarck Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Midway - a decisive naval battle in the Pacific during World War II (June 1942)
  • Synonym: Battle of the Coral Sea - a major naval battle in the Pacific during World War II (May 1942)
