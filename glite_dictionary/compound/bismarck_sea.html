Bismarck Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪzmɑrk si / (AmE), / ˈbɪzˌmɑːk siː / (BrE)
an arm of the southwestern Pacific Ocean, located to the southwest of the Bismarck Archipelago, north of Papua New Guinea
sea near Bismarck Archipelago in Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Bismarck Sea is known for the important World War II battle fought in its waters.
Fishermen from Papua New Guinea rely on the rich marine life of the Bismarck Sea.
Several rivers from New Britain island flow into the Bismarck Sea.

Etymology

Named after Otto von Bismarck, former German chancellor, as the region was formerly a German colony.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbɪzˌmɑrk si / (AmE), / ˈbɪzˌmɑːk siː / (BrE)
a naval battle in World War II (March 1943) in which Allied bombers destroyed a Japanese convoy in the Bismarck Sea, north of New Guinea
WWII naval battle in the Pacific

Examples

The Battle of the Bismarck Sea marked a turning point in the Pacific campaign during World War II.
Many historians consider the Allied victory at the Bismarck Sea crucial to halting Japanese advances.
An Australian commemoration ceremony was held for those lost in the Bismarck Sea engagement.

Etymology

Named after the Bismarck Sea, itself named after German Chancellor Otto von Bismarck.
