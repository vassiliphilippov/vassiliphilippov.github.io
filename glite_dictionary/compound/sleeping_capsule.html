sleeping capsule

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ ˌkæpsjul / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˌkæpsjuːl / (BrE)
A capsule-shaped pill containing a drug taken to induce sleep.
pill for inducing sleep

Examples

She took a sleeping capsule before bed to help with her insomnia.
The doctor prescribed a mild sleeping capsule to aid his recovery.
Some people rely on a sleeping capsule when experiencing jet lag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleeping pill - a drug taken in pill form to induce sleep
  • Synonym: soporific - a drug or substance that induces sleep
