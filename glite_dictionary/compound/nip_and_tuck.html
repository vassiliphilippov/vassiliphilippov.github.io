nip and tuck

1. NOUN

/ nɪp ənd tʌk / (AmE), / nɪp ənd tʌk / (BrE)
A cosmetic surgical procedure to remove wrinkles and other signs of aging from the face, usually involving incisions, tightening of the skin, and removal of excess tissue.
facial cosmetic surgery procedure

Examples

After her nip and tuck, she looked ten years younger.
Some celebrities have undergone several nip and tuck procedures over the years.
The clinic specializes in quick nip and tuck surgeries for busy professionals.

Synset Examples

some actresses have more than one face lift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face-lift - a cosmetic surgical procedure to remove wrinkles from the face

Etymology

Originally an idiomatic phrase describing a close contest, later extended (mid-20th century) to refer to minor surgical procedures that 'nip' and 'tuck' the skin.
