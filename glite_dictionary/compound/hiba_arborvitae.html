hiba arborvitae

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːbə ɑːrbərˌvaɪti / (AmE), / ˈhiːbə ɑːbɔːˌvɪti / (BrE)
A slow-growing, medium to large evergreen tree native to Japan, cultivated as an ornamental for its attractive foliage.
Japanese ornamental evergreen tree

Examples

The hiba arborvitae makes an excellent addition to any landscaped garden due to its dense, green foliage.
In Japan, the hiba arborvitae is valued for both its beauty and its fragrant wood.
Gardeners often choose the hiba arborvitae as a privacy screen because it grows thick and tall over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborvitae - An evergreen coniferous tree of the cypress family, known for its use as an ornamental plant.

Etymology

From Japanese 'hiba' and Latin 'arborvitae' (tree of life).
