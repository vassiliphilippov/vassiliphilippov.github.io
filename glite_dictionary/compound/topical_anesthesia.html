topical anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːpɪkəl ænəsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪkəl ænɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
loss of sensation in a localized area of the skin or mucous membrane, produced by applying an anesthetic directly to the surface
localized numbness from surface-applied anesthetic
medical

Examples

The dentist applied topical anesthesia to numb the area before the injection.
Using topical anesthesia made the minor skin procedure painless for the patient.
You may experience temporary numbness if topical anesthesia is used during treatment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: local anesthesia - loss of sensation in a limited area by regional application of an anesthetic
  • Degree Higher: general anesthesia - complete loss of sensation including consciousness, usually produced by inhalation or intravenous administration

Etymology

From 'topical', from Greek 'topikos' (pertaining to a place), and 'anesthesia', from Greek 'anaisthēsia' (insensibility).
