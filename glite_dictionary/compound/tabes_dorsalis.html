tabes dorsalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌteɪbiːz dɔrˈseɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌteɪbiːz dɔːˈseɪlɪs / (BrE)
A form of late-stage syphilis affecting the spinal cord, characterized by degeneration of sensory neurons, causing stabbing pain, unsteady gait, incontinence, and impotence.
degenerative spinal disease due to syphilis
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with tabes dorsalis after a series of symptoms appeared.
Early treatment of syphilis can prevent complications like tabes dorsalis.
He suffered severe leg pain and incontinence due to tabes dorsalis.

Etymology

From Latin 'tabes' (wasting) + 'dorsalis' (of the back).
