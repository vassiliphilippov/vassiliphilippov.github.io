chalcid wasp

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælˌsɪd wɒsp / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪd wɒsp / (BrE)
a tiny parasitic wasp whose larvae feed on the eggs and larvae of other insects, often beneficial for controlling harmful insect populations
tiny parasitic wasp of the family Chalcididae

Examples

The chalcid wasp lays its eggs inside the larvae of pest insects, helping to control their populations naturally.
Farmers encourage the presence of chalcid wasps in their fields as a form of biological pest control.
A single chalcid wasp can parasitize multiple insect eggs during its lifetime.

Etymology

From Greek khalkos (copper, possibly referring to the metallic coloration of some species).
