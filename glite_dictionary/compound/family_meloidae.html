family Meloidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli məˈloʊɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli mɪˈləʊɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles commonly known as blister beetles, characterized by their ability to secrete a blistering agent called cantharidin.
a family of beetles known as blister beetles

Examples

The family Meloidae includes several species that can cause blistering of human skin upon contact.
Researchers observed the family Meloidae in the grasslands during their field study.
Certain chemicals produced by the family Meloidae have been investigated for medical use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister beetle - a beetle of the family Meloidae, known for secreting cantharidin

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Meloidae,' derived from the genus name 'Meloe.'
