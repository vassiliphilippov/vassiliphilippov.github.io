Atlantic walrus

1. NOUN

/ əˈtlæn.tɪk ˈwɔːl.rəs / (AmE), / əˈtlæn.tɪk ˈwɒl.rəs / (BrE)
a subspecies of walrus found in the northern Atlantic and Arctic waters
subspecies of walrus in the North Atlantic

Examples

The Atlantic walrus is known for its impressive tusks and thick blubber, which helps it survive in cold waters.
Scientists are monitoring the population of the Atlantic walrus as climate change affects Arctic habitats.
Unlike its Pacific relative, the Atlantic walrus tends to form smaller herds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walrus - a large marine mammal with tusks and whiskers
