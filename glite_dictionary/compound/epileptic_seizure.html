epileptic seizure

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪk ˈsiːʒər / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪk ˈsiːʒə / (BrE)
a sudden episode of convulsions and loss or alteration of consciousness, typically caused by abnormal electrical activity in the brain, often associated with epilepsy
seizure involving convulsions and loss of consciousness
medical

Examples

The patient experienced an epileptic seizure during the examination, requiring immediate medical attention.
People with epilepsy may be at risk of having an epileptic seizure without warning.
Strobe lights have been known to trigger an epileptic seizure in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - a sudden attack of illness, especially one affecting the brain

Etymology

From Greek epileptikos (to seize) and seizure from seize (to take possession of).
