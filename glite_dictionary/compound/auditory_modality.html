auditory modality

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdəˌtɔri moʊˈdæləti / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtəri məʊˈdælɪti / (BrE)
the capability or faculty of hearing; the ability to perceive sound through the ears
the ability to hear; hearing faculty

Examples

Researchers studied how the auditory modality affects language processing in the brain.
Damage to the ear can impair the auditory modality in affected individuals.
The experiment tested both visual and auditory modality responses in participants.

Synset Examples

his hearing was impaired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearing - the faculty of perceiving sounds

Etymology

From Latin audīre (to hear) + modality, from Latin modulus (measure, mode).
