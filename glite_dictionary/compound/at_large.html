at large

1. ADV

/ ət ˈlɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ət ˈlɑːdʒ / (BrE)
In a general or broad way, without focusing on details or specifics.
in a general way; broadly

Examples

The professor spoke at large about the changes needed in the education system.
He usually discusses politics at large, rarely going into specifics.
In her memoir, she wrote at large about her experiences abroad.

Synset Examples

he talked at large about his plans

Etymology

From Middle English 'at large', where 'large' meant 'free' or 'unrestricted'.
