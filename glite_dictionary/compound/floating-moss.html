floating-moss

1. NOUN

/ ˈfloʊtɪŋ mɔs / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtɪŋ mɒs / (BrE)
A type of aquatic fern that floats freely on the surface of water, often found in ponds and slow-moving streams.
free-floating aquatic fern

Examples

The pond was covered in a dense layer of floating-moss that blocked out the sunlight.
Researchers studied how floating-moss affects the oxygen levels in freshwater ecosystems.
In some gardens, floating-moss is used as a decorative aquatic plant.
