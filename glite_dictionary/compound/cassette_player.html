cassette player

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsɛt ˌpleɪər / (AmE), / kəˈset ˌpleɪə / (BrE)
a device for playing audio cassettes, typically consisting of a tape deck and speaker(s) or headphone output
device for playing audio cassettes

Examples

She found her old cassette player in the attic next to a box of tapes.
The battery in my cassette player lasted for hours when I was a kid.
Many vintage music lovers still use a cassette player for their collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape recorder - a machine that records and plays back sound on magnetic tape
  • Degree Higher: CD player - a device for playing audio compact discs
