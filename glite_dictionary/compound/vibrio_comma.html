Vibrio comma

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪbri.oʊ ˈkɒmə / (AmE), / ˈvɪbri.əʊ ˈkɒmə / (BrE)
an obsolete name for the bacterium that causes Asiatic cholera, now known as Vibrio cholerae
obsolete name for Asiatic cholera bacterium
medical

Examples

The scientist identified Vibrio comma under the microscope while studying the cause of cholera.
Historical records document outbreaks of cholera caused by Vibrio comma in the 19th century.
Although Vibrio comma is no longer the accepted term, it is frequently mentioned in early medical literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vibrio cholerae - the modern name for the bacterium causing cholera

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrio' (to quiver), describing the bacterium's shape and movement, and 'comma' for its curved form.
