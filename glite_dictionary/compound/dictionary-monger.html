dictionary-monger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪkʃəˌnɛri ˌmʌŋər / (AmE), / ˈdɪkʃənəri ˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who is excessively concerned with definitions, word meanings, or linguistic correctness
someone obsessed with word meanings
informal

Examples

At every gathering, the dictionary-monger finds a way to correct someone's use of language.
Some consider being a dictionary-monger helpful, while others find it annoying.
His tendency to act as a dictionary-monger often disrupts casual conversation.

Synset Examples

The dictionary-monger at the party bored everyone with definitions.
Being a dictionary-monger can make conversations tedious.
She's such a dictionary-monger, always correcting our grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedant - a person who is excessively concerned with minor details or rules

Etymology

Formed by analogy with compounds like 'scaremonger' and 'warmonger': 'dictionary' + 'monger' (dealer or promoter).
