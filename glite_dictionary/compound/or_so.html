or so

1. ADV

/ ɔɹ soʊ / (AmE), / ɔː səʊ / (BrE)
used after a number or quantity to show that it is approximate, not exact
used to mean 'approximately' after a number

Examples

There were twenty or so students waiting outside the classroom.
The hike should take two hours or so, depending on the weather.
He spent a thousand dollars or so on his new computer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exactly - precisely; without any approximation
