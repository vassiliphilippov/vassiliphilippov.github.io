layperson's terms

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪˌpɜrsənz tɜrmz / (AmE), / ˈleɪˌpɜːsənz tɜːmz / (BrE)
words or explanations expressed in a manner that is simple and easy for non-experts to understand
simple, non-technical language

Examples

The doctor described the procedure in layperson's terms so everyone could understand.
This manual is written entirely in layperson's terms for easy comprehension.
Can you explain the new software update in layperson's terms?

Synset Examples

She explained the science in layperson's terms to the audience.
The report was written in layperson's terms for clarity.
Can you simplify this in layperson's terms for me?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain English - clear and straightforward English, especially legal or official writing
  • Antonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a profession or group, often difficult for outsiders to understand

Etymology

From 'layperson' (non-expert) + 'terms' (words or language).
