lotus position

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.təs pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A sitting posture in which the legs are crossed and the feet are placed on the opposite thighs, commonly used in yoga and meditation.
yoga sitting posture with crossed legs

Examples

She sat in the lotus position to begin her morning meditation.
After stretching, he eased himself into the lotus position for yoga practice.
It takes time and flexibility to master the lotus position safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-legged - sitting with legs crossed in a similar manner

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'padmasana' (padma 'lotus' + āsana 'seat, posture'), translated into English as 'lotus position'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.t̬əs pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A sitting posture used in yoga, in which each foot is placed on the opposite thigh with legs crossed.
yoga sitting posture with crossed legs

Examples

He sat in the lotus position to begin his meditation practice.
Yoga instructors often recommend the lotus position for deep breathing exercises.
It took her months of practice to comfortably hold the lotus position for several minutes.

Synset Examples

He sat down in the lotus position to meditate.
Practicing yoga, she often used the lotus position for relaxation.
Finding peace, he settled into the lotus position effortlessly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-legged - sitting with legs crossed, often on the floor

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'padmasana', with 'lotus' referring to the lotus flower, symbolizing purity and spiritual enlightenment in yoga traditions.
