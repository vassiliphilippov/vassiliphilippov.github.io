green gram

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ɡræm / (AmE), / ɡriːn ɡræm / (BrE)
the small, green seed of the mung bean plant, used as food, especially in Asian cuisine
seed of the mung bean plant (food)

Examples

Indian recipes often call for green gram as a main ingredient in soups and curries.
He soaked the green gram overnight before cooking it for breakfast.
Sprouted green gram is considered a healthy addition to salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - the plant bearing green gram seeds; also refers to the seed itself

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrin ɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn ɡræm / (BrE)
the seed of the mung bean plant, commonly used as food, especially in South and Southeast Asian cuisine
seed of the mung bean used as food

Examples

You can make a nutritious salad by adding boiled green gram to fresh vegetables.
Green gram is often used to prepare traditional Indian dishes like dal and sprouted salads.
The price of green gram has increased this season due to low rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - the plant whose seeds are called green gram
  • Degree Higher: lentil - edible legume similar to green gram
