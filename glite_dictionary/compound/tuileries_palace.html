Tuileries Palace

1. PROPN

/ ˌtwiːləˈriːz ˈpælɪs / (AmE), / ˌtwiːləˈriːz ˈpælɪs / (BrE)
A former palace and royal residence in Paris, built in 1564 for Catherine de Medici, destroyed by fire in 1871; now only its gardens remain.
former royal palace in Paris, France

Examples

The Tuileries Palace once stood as a symbol of French royal power until its destruction in 1871.
Thousands of Parisians gathered outside the Tuileries Palace during the tumultuous days of the French Revolution.
Today, visitors wander the formal gardens that remain where the Tuileries Palace once existed.

Etymology

French: 'Tuileries' from 'tuile' (tile), as the site was originally a tile factory.
