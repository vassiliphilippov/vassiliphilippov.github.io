pedal extremity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəl ɪkˈstrɛmɪti / (AmE), / ˈpiːdəl ɪkˈstrɛmɪti / (BrE)
the end part of the limb in vertebrates, typically referring to the foot
anatomical term for the foot
medical

Examples

The injury was limited to the patient's right pedal extremity.
In some animals, the pedal extremity is highly specialized for locomotion.
Doctors noted swelling around the pedal extremity after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foot - the lower extremity of the leg below the ankle

Etymology

From Latin 'pes, pedis' (foot) and 'extremitas' (outermost part).
