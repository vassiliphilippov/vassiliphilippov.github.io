novemdecillion dollars

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊ.vəm.dɛˈsɪl.jən ˈdɑː.lɚz / (AmE), / ˌnɒv.əm.dɛˈsɪl.jən ˈdɒl.əz / (BrE)
an extremely large sum of money equal to a novemdecillion (10^60 in the US, 10^114 in some European countries) dollars; often used hyperbolically to indicate an unimaginably high amount of money
an extremely large sum of money

Examples

He joked that only a novemdecillion dollars could buy the moon.
The fundraising target seemed as high as novemdecillion dollars to most participants.
Economists laughed at the idea of printing novemdecillion dollars to solve the crisis.

Synset Examples

You’d need a novemdecillion dollars to fund that project.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: million dollars - a sum of one million dollars; a very large amount of money
  • Degree Lower: quadrillion dollars - an extremely large sum of money equal to a quadrillion dollars
  • Degree Higher: vigintillion dollars - a sum of money equal to a vigintillion dollars (one order higher than novemdecillion)

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'novemdecim' (nineteen) + '-illion' (patterned after million, billion, etc.)
