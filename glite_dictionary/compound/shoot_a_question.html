to shoot a question

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt ə ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / ʃuːt ə ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
to ask a question, usually in a direct or spontaneous manner
ask a question directly
informal

Examples

If you have any doubts, feel free to shoot a question during the presentation.
She didn't hesitate to shoot a question at the guest speaker.
After the lecture, students lined up to shoot questions about the new research.

Synset Examples

Don't hesitate to shoot any questions you have.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ask - to request information from someone
  • Synonym: pose a question - to present a question for consideration
