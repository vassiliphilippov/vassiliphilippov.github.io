nanny state

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæni steɪt / (AmE), / ˈnæni steɪt / (BrE)
A government regarded as overprotective or as interfering unduly with personal choice and freedom.
overprotective government

Examples

Some politicians use the term nanny state to criticize health and safety regulations.
Opponents of the new laws claim they are evidence of a growing nanny state.
The article discusses whether welfare programs contribute to a nanny state mentality.

Synset Examples

He believes too many regulations create a nanny state.
Critics say the government acts like a nanny state.
Some argue that a nanny state limits personal freedoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big government - government regarded as excessively involved in people's lives
  • Antonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimal governmental interference

Etymology

From 'nanny' (child's nurse or caretaker) + 'state', comparing government to an overbearing caregiver. Used since the mid-20th century.
