umbrageous tree

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbreɪdʒəs triː / (AmE), / ʌmˈbreɪdʒɪəs triː / (BrE)
a tree with thick foliage that offers plentiful shade
tree with dense shade

Examples

They picnicked beneath the umbrageous tree to escape the midday sun.
A bench sat invitingly under an old umbrageous tree by the pond.
The children loved to read under the umbrageous tree each summer afternoon.

Synset Examples

The umbrageous tree provided shade on a hot day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade tree - a tree planted or grown primarily to provide shade

Etymology

From Latin 'umbra' meaning 'shade', via 'umbrageous', plus 'tree'.
