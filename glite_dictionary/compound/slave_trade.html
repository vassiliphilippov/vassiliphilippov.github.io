slave trade

1. NOUN

/ sleɪv treɪd / (AmE), / sleɪv treɪd / (BrE)
the buying, selling, and transportation of people as slaves, especially the trading of Black Africans to the Americas from the 16th to 19th centuries
the buying and selling of slaves, especially historically

Examples

Historians have studied the slave trade and its devastating effects on African communities.
The abolition of the slave trade in the 19th century marked a turning point in world history.
The museum features exhibits on the transatlantic slave trade and its legacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slavery - the state or system of being a slave
  • Antonym: abolition - the act of ending slavery or the slave trade
