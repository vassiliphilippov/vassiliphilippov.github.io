suborder Serpentes

1. NOUN

/ səbˌɔrdər sərˈpɛntiːz / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə sɛˈpɛnˌtiːz / (BrE)
the taxonomic suborder that comprises all snakes, distinguished from other reptiles by elongated, legless bodies
the taxonomic group of snakes

Examples

The suborder Serpentes includes more than 3,000 different species of snakes.
Scientists have discovered new fossil evidence within the suborder Serpentes.
All true snakes are classified under the suborder Serpentes in biological taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - a legless, carnivorous reptile

Etymology

From Latin 'serpens' (snake), used in modern taxonomy.
