mea culpa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪə ˈkʊl.pə / (AmE), / ˌmeɪə ˈkʊl.pə / (BrE)
an acknowledgment or admission of one's fault or error
an admission of fault or error

Examples

She offered a mea culpa after realizing her mistake during the meeting.
The politician issued a public mea culpa for his controversial remarks.
His heartfelt mea culpa helped restore trust among his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apology - a statement expressing sorrow for an error or offense

Etymology

From Latin 'mea culpa' meaning 'through my fault'.
