Turkey red

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːki ˌrɛd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki ˌrɛd / (BrE)
a bright orange-red color, traditionally produced on cotton cloth using alizarin dye
a bright orange-red dye or color

Examples

The tablecloth was dyed a deep Turkey red that made the whole room feel warm.
She chose a Turkey red fabric for her new curtains because of its vibrant color.
During the 19th century, Turkey red became a popular choice for fashionable European textiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet - a bright red color with a slightly orange tinge
  • Degree Lower: crimson - a deep red color inclining to purple

Etymology

Named after Turkey, where the dyeing process originated, and 'red' for the color produced.
