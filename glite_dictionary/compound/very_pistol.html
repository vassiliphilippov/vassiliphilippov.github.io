Very pistol

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛri ˌpɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɛri ˌpɪstl̩ / (BrE)
a pistol designed for firing Very-light flares, used as a signal device, especially in maritime or military situations
pistol for firing signal flares

Examples

The ship's officer used a Very pistol to send a distress signal into the night sky.
Soldiers were trained to operate the Very pistol for emergency communications.
During the exercise, a red flare was launched from a Very pistol to signal for help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flare gun - a gun for firing signal flares

Etymology

Named after Edward Very (1847–1910), U.S. naval officer and inventor.
