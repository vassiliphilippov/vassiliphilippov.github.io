mental reservation

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntəl ˌrɛzərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɛntəl ˌrɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of doubt or hesitation that is not expressed openly and prevents wholehearted acceptance or agreement.
unstated doubt or hesitation

Examples

She agreed to the proposal, but with a mental reservation about its feasibility.
Despite his mental reservation, he went along with the group's decision.
You may have a mental reservation, even though you are outwardly supportive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misgiving - a feeling of doubt or apprehension
  • Synonym: reservation - a doubt or reason for not accepting something completely
