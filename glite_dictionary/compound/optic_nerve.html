optic nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪk nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪk nɜːv / (BrE)
the second cranial nerve that carries visual information from the retina to the brain
cranial nerve for vision from retina to brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the optic nerve can result in partial or complete loss of vision.
The doctor examined the patient's optic nerve for signs of glaucoma.
Information captured by the retina is transmitted via the optic nerve to the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retina - the light-sensitive membrane at the back of the eyeball

Etymology

From Greek 'optikos' (of seeing) and Latin 'nervus' (nerve).
