hedgehog cereus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒˌhɔɡ ˈsɪəriəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒˌhɒɡ ˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
a cactus of the genus Echinocereus, known for its spiny stems and brightly colored flowers
a spiny cactus (Echinocereus genus)

Examples

The hedgehog cereus blooms in spring with vivid pink flowers.
Gardeners often cultivate the hedgehog cereus for its unique appearance and resilience.
A row of hedgehog cereus plants lined the desert trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Echinocereus - a genus of cacti with spiny stems and showy flowers

Etymology

From 'hedgehog' referencing its spiny appearance and 'cereus', from Latin for 'wax', used in plant names for cacti with waxy stems.
