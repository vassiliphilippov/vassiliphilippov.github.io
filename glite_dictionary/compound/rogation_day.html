Rogation Day

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈɡeɪʃən deɪ / (AmE), / rəʊˈɡeɪʃən deɪ / (BrE)
one of the three days before Ascension Day, observed by some Christians as days of prayer and supplication for God's mercy and blessings
Christian day of prayer before Ascension Day

Examples

The parish celebrated Rogation Day with a procession and special prayers for the harvest.
Farmers in the village gathered on Rogation Day to ask for favorable weather and good crops.
During Rogation Day services, it is customary to walk the boundaries of the parish while praying.

Etymology

From Latin 'rogatio' (an asking or supplication), from 'rogare' (to ask).
