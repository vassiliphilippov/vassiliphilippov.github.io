converging lens

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝːdʒɪŋ lɛnz / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒɪŋ lɛnz / (BrE)
A lens that brings light rays passing through it to a point or focus.
lens that brings light to a focus

Examples

A converging lens is often used in magnifying glasses to focus light on a point.
His experiment clearly demonstrated how a converging lens could form an image on a screen.
Microscopes typically use a converging lens system to enlarge tiny objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convex lens - a lens that is thicker in the middle and converges light rays
  • Antonym: diverging lens - a lens that spreads out light rays passing through it
