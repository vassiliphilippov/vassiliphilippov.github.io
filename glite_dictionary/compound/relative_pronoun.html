relative pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv ˈproʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun such as 'who,' 'which,' or 'that' used to introduce a relative clause and refer back to an antecedent noun
pronoun introducing a relative clause

Examples

In the sentence 'The book that you lent me was interesting,' the word 'that' is a relative pronoun.
The teacher explained how who and which are both types of relative pronoun.
A relative pronoun connects a dependent clause to a main clause in a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demonstrative pronoun - pronoun used to point to specific things (e.g., 'this', 'those')

Etymology

From Latin 'relativus' (having reference or relation) and 'pronomen' (pronoun).
