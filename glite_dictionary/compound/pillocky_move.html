pillocky move

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪləki muv / (AmE), / ˈpɪləki muːv / (BrE)
an action that is foolish or silly
foolish or silly action
informal british

Examples

Forgetting his passport was such a pillocky move before the trip.
That was a pillocky move, leaving your keys inside the locked car.
She realized it was a pillocky move to email the wrong document to her boss.

Synset Examples

That was a pillocky move, forgetting the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish move - an act lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: sensible move - an act showing good sense or judgment

Etymology

From British slang 'pillock' meaning a stupid person, with the adjective suffix '-y'.
