graving dock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvɪŋ dɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvɪŋ dɒk / (BrE)
A large dock from which water can be pumped out, used for building or repairing ships below their waterline.
dry dock for ship building or repair

Examples

The new vessel was towed into the graving dock for hull repairs.
Without a graving dock, it would be impossible to maintain the ship's underside.
The historic port includes a restored graving dock dating back to the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry dock - an enclosed dock from which water can be removed

Etymology

From 'grave' (to dig, carve) + 'dock'; refers to the act of digging out or hollowing the dock area.
