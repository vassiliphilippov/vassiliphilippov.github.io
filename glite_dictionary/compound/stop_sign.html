stop sign

1. NOUN

/ stɑp saɪn / (AmE), / stɒp saɪn / (BrE)
a sign placed at a road intersection, instructing drivers to come to a complete stop before proceeding
road sign instructing drivers to stop

Examples

The driver failed to notice the stop sign at the intersection.
You must come to a complete halt at every stop sign.
After the new stop sign was installed, accidents at the crossing decreased significantly.

Synset Examples

The 1968 Vienna Convention on Road Signs and Signals allows for two types of stop sign, as well as three acceptable variants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield sign - a road sign telling drivers to give way to other traffic
