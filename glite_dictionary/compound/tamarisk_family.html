tamarisk family

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæmərɪsk ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈtæmərɪsk ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of mostly desert shrubs and trees, many of which tolerate salt and arid conditions (the Tamaricaceae family)
family of salt-tolerant desert shrubs and trees

Examples

Many species in the tamarisk family thrive in saline soils where few other plants can survive.
The tamarisk family includes several striking shrubs used in landscaping for dry regions.
Botanists studied the adaptations of the tamarisk family to harsh, arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tamarix - a genus of shrubs and small trees in the tamarisk family
