castling long

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.lɪŋ lɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːs.lɪŋ lɒŋ / (BrE)
In chess, the move of castling on the queenside, where the king moves two squares toward the queenside rook and the rook moves to the square next to the king.
castling on the queenside (chess)

Examples

Many players choose castling long to create a more aggressive position on the kingside.
After castling long, White often prepares a pawn storm against Black's king.
The a-file can become a target for your opponent after castling long.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: castling short - castling on the kingside in chess
