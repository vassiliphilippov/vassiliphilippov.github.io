squeeze play

1. NOUN

/ skwiz pleɪ / (AmE), / skwiːz pleɪ / (BrE)
(Baseball) A play in which a runner on third base attempts to score as the batter bunts the pitch.
baseball scoring play with bunt

Examples

The team won the game with a daring squeeze play in the ninth inning.
They called for a squeeze play, and the runner slid home safely.
Executing a successful squeeze play requires skill and perfect timing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunt - short, controlled hit in baseball
