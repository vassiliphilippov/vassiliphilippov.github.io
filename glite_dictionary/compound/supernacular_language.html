supernacular language

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˈnækjələr ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈnækjʊlə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
language that is excessively complex or obscure, making it difficult to understand
overly complex or obscure language

Examples

The professor's supernacular language left many students puzzled and unable to follow the lecture.
Critics complained that the novel's supernacular language made it inaccessible to most readers.
Avoid using supernacular language in your reports so that they are clear to everyone.

Synset Examples

The supernacular language of the text was hard to decipher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - specialized language that is difficult to understand
  • Antonym: plain language - clear and straightforward language

Etymology

From 'supernacular' (from Latin 'super' meaning 'above' and possibly 'naculum', related to 'cup'), here used figuratively to suggest excessiveness, plus 'language'.
