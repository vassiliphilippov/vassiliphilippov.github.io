silver certificate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ sərˌtɪfɪkət / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə səˌtɪfɪkət / (BrE)
a former U.S. banknote issued by the United States Treasury that could be exchanged for a specific amount of silver.
historic U.S. banknote exchangeable for silver

Examples

He found an old silver certificate in his grandfather’s collection of rare banknotes.
Before 1964, you could exchange a silver certificate for actual silver dollars at the bank.
Collectors prize the silver certificate for its historical and numismatic value.
