full plate armour

1. NOUN

/ fʊl pleɪt ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / fʊl pleɪt ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A type of personal armor, made from iron or steel plates, that entirely encased the wearer and was used chiefly from the late Middle Ages to the early modern period.
body armor made of interlocking metal plates
british

Examples

The knight rode into battle wearing full plate armour that gleamed in the sunlight.
After centuries of warfare, full plate armour became a symbol of nobility and chivalry.
Museum visitors admired the intricacy of the full plate armour displayed in the main hall.

Synset Examples

Full plate armour was expensive to produce and remained therefore restricted to the upper strata of society; lavishly decorated suits of armour remained the fashion with 18th-century nobles and generals long after they had ceased to be militarily useful on the battlefield due to the advent of inexpensive muskets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chain mail - armor made of interlinked metal rings
  • Degree Lower: brigandine - body armor made of metal plates sewn inside a fabric covering

Etymology

From Middle English 'plate' (a flat piece of metal) and 'armour', from Old French 'armeure'.
