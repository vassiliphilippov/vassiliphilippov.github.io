Mojave language

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Mohave people of the Lower Colorado River region in the southwestern United States.
Yuman language of the Mohave people

Examples

The Mojave language is endangered, with only a small number of fluent speakers remaining today.
Linguists are documenting the Mojave language to help preserve it for future generations.
She grew up listening to stories in the Mojave language, passed down by her grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a group of Native American languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Mojave (Mohave) people, themselves from their own autonym 'Aha Makhav' (beside the water), plus 'language'.
