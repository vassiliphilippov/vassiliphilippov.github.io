Demotic script

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑːtɪk skrɪpt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒtɪk skrɪpt / (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian script derived from hieratic and used for everyday writing, characterized by its simplified, cursive form
simplified ancient Egyptian writing system

Examples

The Demotic script was commonly used for everyday administrative documents in ancient Egypt.
Scholars have deciphered part of the Rosetta Stone by understanding the Demotic script.
Learning to read Demotic script is essential for studying late Egyptian texts.

Synset Examples

Demotic script was eventually replaced by Greek

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hieratic script - earlier cursive Egyptian script from which Demotic developed
  • Degree Higher: hieroglyphics - formal ancient Egyptian writing system using pictorial symbols

Etymology

From Greek demōtikos (popular, of the people), referring to its use in daily life, and script, from Latin scriptum (something written).
