low-sodium diet

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈsoʊdiəm ˈdaɪət / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈsəʊdiəm ˈdaɪət / (BrE)
A diet that restricts the amount of sodium, typically by limiting the intake of salt, often prescribed for treating conditions such as hypertension or edema.
diet restricting sodium intake
medical

Examples

My doctor recommended a low-sodium diet to help manage my blood pressure.
People with heart conditions are often advised to follow a low-sodium diet.
Restaurants are beginning to offer more low-sodium diet options for health-conscious customers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: salt-free diet - a diet excluding salt entirely
