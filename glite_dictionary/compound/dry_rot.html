dry rot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌrɒt / (BrE)
A serious fungal condition that causes wood, bulbs, or certain vegetables to become dry, crumbly, and structurally weak.
fungal decay in wood or plant material

Examples

Extensive dry rot was discovered in the basement beams during the renovation.
Homeowners must address dry rot quickly to prevent major structural damage.
The fungus responsible for dry rot can destroy wood in a surprisingly short time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wet rot - Fungal decay of wood in damp conditions

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌrɒt / (BrE)
A type of decay in wood, caused by certain fungi, leading to the crumbling and weakening of timber, often in buildings.
fungal decay of wood

Examples

The inspector found severe dry rot in the attic beams during the renovation.
If left untreated, dry rot can compromise the structural integrity of wooden houses.
They replaced the porch steps because dry rot had made them unsafe.

Synset Examples

The old house had dry rot in the wooden beams.
The contractor warned us about potential dry rot if we didn't fix the leak.
We need to replace the flooring due to dry rot damage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rot - general process of decay or decomposition
