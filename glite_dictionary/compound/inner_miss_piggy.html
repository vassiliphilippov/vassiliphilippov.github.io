inner Miss Piggy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɚ mɪs ˈpɪɡi / (AmE), / ˈɪnə mɪs ˈpɪɡi / (BrE)
a confident or flamboyant aspect of someone's personality, inspired by the Muppet character Miss Piggy, typically revealed in a performative or expressive context
confident, flamboyant side (like Miss Piggy)

Examples

Whenever she's nervous on stage, she tries to channel her inner Miss Piggy to gain confidence.
You don't have to be shy—sometimes it's good to let your inner Miss Piggy shine.
During the fashion show, he embraced his inner Miss Piggy and strutted down the runway.

Synset Examples

She always channels her inner Miss Piggy when performing.

Etymology

Alludes to Miss Piggy, a bold, flamboyant character from the Muppets created by Jim Henson.
