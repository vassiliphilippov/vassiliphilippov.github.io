genus Tectona

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tɛkˈtoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tɛkˈtəʊnə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical trees in southeastern Asia, including the teak tree.
genus of tropical trees including teak

Examples

The genus Tectona comprises several species, most notably the teak tree.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of logging within the genus Tectona in Southeast Asia.
In botany class, we learned that genus Tectona is native to tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teak - a large deciduous tree, Tectona grandis, valued for its durable wood

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Tectona', the scientific name for teak, from Malayalam 'tekka'.
