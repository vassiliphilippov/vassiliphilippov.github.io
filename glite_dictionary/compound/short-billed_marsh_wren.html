short-billed marsh wren

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃɔrt bɪld ˈmɑrʃ ˌrɛn / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt bɪld ˈmɑːʃ ˌrɛn / (BrE)
a small North American wren (Cistothorus platensis) that lives in wet, sedgy meadows, distinguished by its short bill
small North American wren in wet meadows

Examples

The short-billed marsh wren builds its nest low among dense reeds in wet meadows.
Birdwatchers spotted a short-billed marsh wren near the edge of the marsh this spring.
Unlike other wrens, the short-billed marsh wren prefers habitats with tall, sedgy grasses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: marsh wren - a small North American songbird often found in marshes
