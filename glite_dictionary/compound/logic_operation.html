logic operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪk ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪk ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an operation that is performed according to the principles of symbolic logic, such as AND, OR, or NOT
operation based on symbolic logic rules

Examples

The computer uses logic operations to process information quickly and accurately.
Understanding basic logic operations like AND and OR is essential in digital circuit design.
In programming, combining conditions with logic operations helps control the flow of a program.
