retirement community

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtaɪɚmənt kəˈmjunəti / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪəmənt kəˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
a residential community designed for people who have retired from full-time employment, often offering various amenities and services for older adults
planned housing for retired people

Examples

After selling their house, they decided to move into a retirement community closer to their grandchildren.
Many retirement communities offer fitness programs, transportation, and group social activities.
The couple appreciates the sense of security and companionship they found in their retirement community.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nursing home - a place where people who are old or ill live and are cared for
