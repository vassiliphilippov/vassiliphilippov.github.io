Gentiana crinita

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈtaɪənə krɪˈnaɪtə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈtiːənə krɪˈnɪtə / (BrE)
A tall fringed gentian native to eastern North America, known for its violet-blue or white fringed flowers.
a North American fringed gentian species

Examples

The delicate flowers of the Gentiana crinita brighten wetlands each autumn.
Botanists documented a population of Gentiana crinita along the lakeside trail.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats for Gentiana crinita in eastern forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringed gentian - a group of gentian species with fringed petals

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Gentiana' and species epithet 'crinita' meaning 'hairy' or 'with long hair'.
