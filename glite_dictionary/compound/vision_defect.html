vision defect

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒən dɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒ(ə)n dɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
an impairment or abnormality of the sense of sight
impairment of sight

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a mild vision defect that could be corrected by glasses.
Children with an untreated vision defect may have difficulty learning in school.
Regular check-ups can help detect a vision defect early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual impairment - reduced ability to see to a degree that causes problems not fixable by usual means
  • Degree Higher: blindness - the condition of lacking visual perception due to physiological or neurological factors
