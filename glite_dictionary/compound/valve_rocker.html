valve rocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælv ˌrɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈvælv ˌrɒkə / (BrE)
a lever pivoted at its center, used especially to push a valve down in an internal-combustion engine
lever that actuates an engine valve

Examples

The valve rocker transfers motion from the camshaft to the engine's valves.
A broken valve rocker can cause serious engine problems if not replaced promptly.
Modern cars use improved valve rocker designs for greater fuel efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocker arm - a lever that transmits a camshaft's movement to a valve in an engine
