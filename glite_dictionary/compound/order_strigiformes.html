order Strigiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər straɪdʒɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌstrɪdʒɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
the scientific order of birds comprising all owls
order of birds: owls

Examples

The order Strigiformes includes all species of owls found worldwide.
Researchers study the adaptations specific to birds in the order Strigiformes.
Barn owls and great horned owls are members of the order Strigiformes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with a large head and forward-facing eyes

Etymology

From Latin 'strix, strigis' (owl) + '-formes' (having the form of).
