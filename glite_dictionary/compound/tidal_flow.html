tidal flow

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.dəl floʊ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dəl fləʊ / (BrE)
the movement of water in the sea, rivers, or estuaries caused by the rising and falling of the tides
water movement caused by tides

Examples

The strength of the tidal flow in this bay makes it an ideal spot for generating tidal power.
Fishermen must be aware of the tidal flow to safely navigate the estuary.
Erosion of the coastline is largely influenced by the direction and speed of the tidal flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tidal current - a current resulting from tidal movement
