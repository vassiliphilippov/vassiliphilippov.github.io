learning disability

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ dɪsəˌbɪləti / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ dɪsəˌbɪləti / (BrE)
A condition in which a person of normal intelligence has difficulty acquiring specific academic skills such as reading, writing, or mathematics.
difficulty in learning specific skills despite normal intelligence
medical

Examples

The school offers special programs to support students with a learning disability.
Having a learning disability does not mean a person lacks intelligence or potential.
Her learning disability was diagnosed after she struggled with reading and writing in the first grade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyslexia - Impairment in reading ability; a common specific learning disability
  • Antonym: intellectual disability - Significantly below-average general intellectual functioning; distinct from specific learning disability

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ dɪˌsəbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ dɪˌsəbɪlɪti / (BrE)
a condition that affects a person's ability to learn in a typical manner, often impacting skills such as reading, writing, or mathematics
condition affecting learning ability

Examples

The school provides special support for students with a learning disability.
Early identification of a learning disability can make a significant difference in a child's education.
People with a learning disability may need accommodations to reach their full potential.

Synset Examples

Her neuropsych assessments helped diagnose learning disabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning difficulty - condition causing problems with learning, often used in British English
