radial pulse

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəl pʌls / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl pʌls / (BrE)
the pulse felt over the radial artery at the wrist, commonly used to measure heart rate
pulse felt at the wrist
medical

Examples

The nurse checked the patient's radial pulse to monitor their heart rate.
A weak or irregular radial pulse may indicate circulatory problems.
Doctors often use the radial pulse as a quick way to assess cardiac function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - rhythmic throbbing of arteries as blood is propelled through them

Etymology

From 'radial,' relating to the radius bone in the forearm, and 'pulse' from Latin pulsus (a beating).
