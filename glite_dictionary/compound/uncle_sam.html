Uncle Sam

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌŋkəl ˈsæm / (AmE), / ˌʌŋkl ˈsæm / (BrE)
a personification of the United States government, often depicted as a tall, elderly man with a white beard, wearing a top hat and star-spangled suit
personification of the U.S. government

Examples

Many people associate Uncle Sam with calls for patriotism and support of the military.
During tax season, Americans often joke that Uncle Sam wants more of their money.
The poster showed Uncle Sam pointing with the words 'I Want You for U.S. Army.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sam - male given name (short for Samuel)

Etymology

From the initials 'U.S.' for United States, popularized in the early 19th century; possibly linked to Samuel Wilson, a meat packer during the War of 1812.

2. PROPN

/ ˈʌŋkəl sæm / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkl̩ sæm / (BrE)
the personification of the United States government, often depicted as a tall, elderly man with a white beard and a top hat decorated with stars and stripes
personification of U.S. government

Examples

Every year, Uncle Sam collects billions of dollars in taxes from Americans.
When war broke out, Uncle Sam called upon young men to join the army.
Many people believe that Uncle Sam should do more to support education funding.

Synset Examples

Every tax season, I realize how much I owe Uncle Sam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the government - the governing body of a nation, state, or community
  • Antonym: John Bull - personification of the United Kingdom or England

Etymology

From the initials 'U.S.' stamped on government supplies during the War of 1812, supposedly associated with Samuel Wilson, a meat packer known as 'Uncle Sam'.
