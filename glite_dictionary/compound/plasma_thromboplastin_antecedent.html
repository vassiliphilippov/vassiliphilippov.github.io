plasma thromboplastin antecedent

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæzmə θrəmˌbɑːˈplæs.tɪn ænˈtiːsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈplæzmə ˌθrɒm.bəʊˈplæs.tɪn ænˈtiːsɪdənt / (BrE)
a plasma protein, also known as coagulation factor XI, whose deficiency results in a tendency to bleed abnormally
blood clotting protein (factor XI)
medical

Examples

A deficiency of plasma thromboplastin antecedent can cause excessive bleeding even after minor injuries.
Doctors often measure plasma thromboplastin antecedent levels when patients present with unexplained bruising or hemorrhage.
The laboratory confirmed the patient's bleeding disorder was due to low plasma thromboplastin antecedent activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor XI - another term for plasma thromboplastin antecedent, a coagulation factor
  • Synonym: hemophilia - a condition involving a tendency to bleed due to coagulation factor deficiency

Etymology

From 'plasma' (the fluid part of blood), 'thromboplastin' (a clotting factor), and 'antecedent' (something that comes before).
