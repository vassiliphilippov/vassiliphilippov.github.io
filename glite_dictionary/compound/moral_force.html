moral force

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl ˌfɔrs / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl ˌfɔːs / (BrE)
the influence of ethical principles or values as a motivating or driving factor.
influence of ethical values

Examples

The new policy became a moral force for change in the organization.
Her words carried a moral force that inspired many to act.
History shows that moral force can sometimes be more powerful than physical coercion.

Synset Examples

they hoped it would act as a spiritual dynamic on all churches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethical force - influence stemming from ethical considerations
