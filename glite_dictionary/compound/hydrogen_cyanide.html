hydrogen cyanide

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən saɪˈænaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrɪdʒən saɪˈənaɪd / (BrE)
a highly poisonous, volatile liquid or gas with the smell of bitter almonds, used in manufacturing and capable of causing death if inhaled
highly poisonous chemical compound

Examples

Accidental exposure to hydrogen cyanide can be fatal even in small amounts.
Factories must have strict safety protocols when handling hydrogen cyanide.
The bitter almond smell is a notorious warning sign of hydrogen cyanide presence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prussic acid - another name for hydrogen cyanide, especially in solution

Etymology

Formed from 'hydrogen' and 'cyanide', with 'cyanide' from Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue).
