logical system

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A set of principles, rules, and methods forming a framework for reasoning or deriving conclusions.
set of rules for reasoning

Examples

The mathematician developed a new logical system to address complex problems.
Different philosophers have proposed their own logical system to explain rational thought.
First-order logic is a well-known logical system used in computer science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logic - the study of the principles of reasoning
