Battle of Lützen

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəl əv ˈluːtsən / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈlʊtsən / (BrE)
a battle in the Thirty Years’ War (1632), during which Swedish forces led by Gustavus Adolphus defeated Imperial troops under Wallenstein, resulting in the death of Gustavus Adolphus
1632 battle in the Thirty Years’ War

Examples

The Battle of Lützen marked a turning point in the Thirty Years' War.
Gustavus Adolphus was killed during the Battle of Lützen in 1632.
Historians often debate the long-term impact of the Battle of Lützen on European politics.

Etymology

Named after Lützen, a town in Germany where the battle occurred.
