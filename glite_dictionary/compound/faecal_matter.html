faecal matter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːkəl ˈmætər / (AmE), / ˈfiːkəl ˈmatə / (BrE)
solid waste excreted from the bowels; feces.
solid excretory waste; feces
medical british

Examples

The scientist collected samples of faecal matter to test for harmful bacteria.
After the flood, traces of faecal matter were found in the drinking water supply.
Proper disposal of faecal matter is essential for preventing disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feces - solid excretory waste from the bowels
  • Synonym: stool - solid waste discharged from the body

Etymology

From Latin 'faex' (dregs) and Latin 'materia' (matter, substance).
