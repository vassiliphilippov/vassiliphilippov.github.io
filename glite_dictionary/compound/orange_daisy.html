orange daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪndʒ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a mat-forming herb native to Turkestan with bright orange-yellow, almost double flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant
mat-forming herb with orange-yellow flowers

Examples

The orange daisy brightened the entire flowerbed with its cheerful blooms.
Gardeners often choose the orange daisy for its vibrant color and easy-to-grow nature.
Last year, I planted several orange daisies along the walkway to add a splash of color.
