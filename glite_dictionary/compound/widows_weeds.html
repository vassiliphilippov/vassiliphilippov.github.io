widow's weeds

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊz wiːdz / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊz wiːdz / (BrE)
a black dress or set of garments traditionally worn by a widow during mourning
mourning clothes worn by a widow
archaic

Examples

She appeared at the funeral in her widow's weeds, her face hidden beneath a black veil.
In Victorian times, women were expected to wear widow's weeds for a year after their husbands' deaths.
The old portrait shows her dressed in widow's weeds, solemn yet dignified.

Etymology

From 'weed' in the archaic sense meaning 'garment', from Old English 'wǣd' (garment, clothing).
