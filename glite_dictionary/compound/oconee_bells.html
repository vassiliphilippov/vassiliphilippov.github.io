Oconee bells

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈkoʊni bɛlz / (AmE), / əʊˈkəʊni bɛlz / (BrE)
a plant of the southeastern United States, having solitary white funnel-shaped flowers tinged with pink and large glossy green leaves that turn bronze-red in autumn
a southeastern U.S. plant with white-pink bell flowers

Examples

Oconee bells are prized among gardeners for their unusual springtime blooms.
Botanists spent years searching for wild populations of Oconee bells in remote forest areas.
The glossy leaves of Oconee bells turn a beautiful bronze-red as autumn approaches.

Etymology

Named after the Oconee region in the southeastern United States.
