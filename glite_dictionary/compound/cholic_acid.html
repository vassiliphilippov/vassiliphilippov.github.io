cholic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline organic acid found in bile, important in the digestion of fats
a bile acid involved in fat digestion
medical

Examples

Cholic acid is one of the main components responsible for the emulsification of dietary fats in the intestine.
The doctor explained that certain liver disorders affect the production of cholic acid.
Scientists studied how cholic acid and other bile acids impact cholesterol metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bile acid - any of several acids found in bile, including cholic acid

Etymology

From Greek 'chole' (bile) + 'acid'.
