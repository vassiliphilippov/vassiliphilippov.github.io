wake-up call

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk ʌp kɔl / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ʌp kɔːl / (BrE)
an event or situation that alerts people to a problem and prompts them to take action
event that prompts urgent action

Examples

The economic crisis was a wake-up call for governments to reform financial regulations.
Her diagnosis served as a wake-up call to prioritize her health and well-being.
For many people, climate change is the ultimate wake-up call demanding immediate action.

Synset Examples

the bombing was a wake-up call to strengthen domestic security

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - something that serves to warn of danger or problems

Etymology

From the literal use in hotels where a phone call wakes a guest, extended figuratively to mean an urgent alert.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk ˌʌp kɔl / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ˌʌp kɔːl / (BrE)
a telephone call arranged to be received at a specified time in order to wake someone up, typically in a hotel
telephone call to wake someone up

Examples

The hotel receptionist scheduled a wake-up call for me at 6:30 a.m.
If you need a wake-up call, just let the front desk know.
She forgot to request a wake-up call and almost missed her flight.

Synset Examples

she left a wake-up call for 7 a.m.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm call - a telephone call to wake someone up, especially in hotels
  • Synonym: alarm clock - a clock that can be set to sound an alarm at a desired time

3. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk ʌp kɔl / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ʌp kɔːl / (BrE)
an event that makes someone realize they must take action or change their behavior, often because of a problem or danger
an event prompting realization or change

Examples

His heart attack was a real wake-up call to start living healthier.
The recent data breach should be a wake-up call for companies to improve their security.
Failing that exam was the wake-up call he needed to take his studies seriously.

Synset Examples

This health scare served as a wake-up call to the community.
Ill health can often be a wake-up call for lifestyle changes.
Her failing grades were a wake-up call for her parents.
Taking the weeder course was a wake-up call for her.
The accident was a wake-up call for him to change his lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - a warning or call to attention of danger or a problem

Etymology

From the literal meaning of a telephone call to wake someone, used figuratively since the mid-20th century.
