genus Najas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈneɪdʒəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈneɪdʒəs / (BrE)
The sole genus in the family Naiadaceae, consisting of aquatic plants commonly known as water nymphs or naiads.
genus of aquatic plants (naiads)

Examples

A new species within the genus Najas was recently discovered in Southeast Asia.
The genus Najas is characterized by delicate, submerged aquatic plants found worldwide.
Researchers studied the ecological role of the genus Najas in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Najas - a genus of aquatic plants known as water nymphs or naiads

Etymology

From Latin 'Najas', from Greek 'Naias' (water nymph).
