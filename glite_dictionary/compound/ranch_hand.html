ranch hand

1. NOUN

/ ˈræntʃ hænd / (AmE), / ˈrɑːntʃ hænd / (BrE)
a person employed to do manual work on a ranch, especially involving livestock and general maintenance
manual worker on a ranch

Examples

After moving to Texas, he found work as a ranch hand on a cattle farm.
The ranch hand woke up before dawn every day to tend the horses.
During the busy season, the ranch hired several new ranch hands to help with the workload.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a man who works primarily with cattle on a ranch, especially in the western US
