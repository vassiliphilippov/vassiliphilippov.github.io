infectious agent

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a microorganism or other agent, such as a virus or bacterium, that can cause infection or disease in a host
microorganism causing infection
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the flu is caused by an infectious agent known as the influenza virus.
Proper hand washing can prevent the spread of infectious agents in hospitals.
Scientists are studying how this new infectious agent is transmitted between animals and humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathogen - an organism that causes disease
