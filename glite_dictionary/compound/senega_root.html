senega root

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪɡə ruːt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪɡə ruːt / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to eastern North America, known for its terminal cluster of small white flowers and medicinal roots used in traditional medicine.
medicinal root of a North American plant

Examples

Traditional healers valued senega root for its expectorant properties in treating respiratory ailments.
You can find senega root in some herbal cough remedies at specialized health stores.
The botanist collected samples of senega root during her fieldwork in North America.

Etymology

From Seneca, the name of a Native American tribe, who used the root for medicinal purposes.
