CD4 T cell

1. NOUN

/ si di fɔr ti sɛl / (AmE), / siː diː fɔː tiː sɛl / (BrE)
A type of T lymphocyte that has the CD4 receptor on its surface, recognizes antigens, and helps coordinate the immune response, especially by stimulating B cells and killer T cells; also known as a helper T cell.
immune cell with CD4 receptor; helper T cell
medical

Examples

HIV targets and destroys CD4 T cells, weakening the immune system.
A drop in a patient's CD4 T cell count may indicate the progression of AIDS.
Researchers study how CD4 T cells coordinate the body's immune response during infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helper T cell - type of T lymphocyte that assists other white blood cells in immunologic processes
  • Antonym: cytotoxic T cell - type of T cell that kills infected cells directly

Etymology

From CD4, the designation for a surface protein (Cluster of Differentiation 4) and T cell, a type of lymphocyte.
