bush willow

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small South African tree with creamy yellow, fragrant flowers, typically found growing along stream banks.
small South African tree with fragrant flowers

Examples

The bush willow adds a splash of color to the riverbank each spring.
Local wildlife are often seen feeding on the seeds of the bush willow.
They planted a bush willow near the pond to provide shade and beauty.

2. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of the Transvaal region, noted for its spikes of yellow flowers.
small tree with yellow flowers (Transvaal)

Examples

The bush willow brightened the landscape with its striking yellow blossoms.
Several species of bush willow thrive in the dry woodlands of South Africa.
During spring, the bush willow attracts a variety of pollinators with its colorful flowers.
