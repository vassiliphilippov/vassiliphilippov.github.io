Conservative Party

1. PROPN

/ kənˈsɝː.və.tɪv ˈpɑːr.ti / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪv ˈpɑː.ti / (BrE)
A major political party, especially in Great Britain and Australia, known for supporting a capitalist economic system with private ownership and limited state control.
centre-right political party

Examples

The Conservative Party promised to reduce taxes and encourage business investment during the election campaign.
She has supported the Conservative Party since she first became eligible to vote.
In the last decade, the Conservative Party has won several general elections in the United Kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Labour Party - A major left-leaning political party in the UK

Etymology

Conservative from Latin 'conservare' (to preserve), referencing the party's preference for preserving traditional institutions.
