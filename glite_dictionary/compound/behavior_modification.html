behavior modification

1. NOUN

/ bəˈheɪvjər ˌmɑdəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə ˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of psychotherapy aimed at changing undesirable behaviors by reinforcing desired ones and discouraging undesired ones
a method for changing unwanted behavior
medical

Examples

The psychologist recommended behavior modification techniques to help children manage their anxiety.
Many schools use behavior modification programs to reduce disruptive actions in the classroom.
Through consistent behavior modification, patients can learn to replace harmful habits with healthier ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavior therapy - psychotherapy focused on changing behavior patterns
  • Antonym: cognitive therapy - psychotherapy aimed at changing thought patterns
