uveoscleral pathway

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːvioʊˈsklɪrəl ˈpæθweɪ / (AmE), / ˌjuːvɪəʊˈsklɪərəl ˈpɑːθweɪ / (BrE)
a route in the eye through which excess aqueous humor is drained, involving the uveal tract and sclera
route in the eye draining aqueous humor
medical

Examples

The uveoscleral pathway contributes significantly to the drainage of intraocular fluid.
Some glaucoma medications work by increasing outflow through the uveoscleral pathway.
Blockage of the uveoscleral pathway can lead to elevated eye pressure and optic nerve damage.

Etymology

From uvea (the middle layer of the eye) + sclera (the white outer layer of the eyeball) + pathway.
