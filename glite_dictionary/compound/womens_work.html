women's work

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmɪnz wɝk / (AmE), / ˈwɪmɪnz wɜːk / (BrE)
tasks or duties traditionally regarded as the responsibility of women, especially domestic chores such as cooking, cleaning, and child care
tasks traditionally done by women

Examples

For centuries, spinning and weaving were considered women's work in many cultures.
Some people still expect cooking to be women's work in the household.
There is growing recognition that women's work deserves equal respect and pay.

Synset Examples

Caring for children is often viewed as women's work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: men's work - tasks traditionally regarded as the responsibility of men
