petty bourgeoisie

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛti ˌbʊrʒwɑːˈzi / (AmE), / ˌpɛti ˌbʊəʒwɑːˈziː / (BrE)
the social class made up of small business owners, shopkeepers, and lower-rank professionals, typically considered lower middle class
lower middle class; small business owners

Examples

During the 19th century, the petty bourgeoisie played a crucial role in the development of local economies.
Some political theories discuss the petty bourgeoisie as a group with interests distinct from the working class and upper class.
Her grandparents, members of the petty bourgeoisie, owned a small bakery in the city.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bourgeoisie - the middle class, especially in Marxist contexts
  • Degree Lower: proletariat - the working class, especially in Marxist theory

Etymology

From French 'petite bourgeoisie' meaning 'small bourgeoisie' or lower middle class.
