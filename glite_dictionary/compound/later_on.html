later on

1. ADV

/ ˈleɪtɚ ɑn / (AmE), / ˈleɪtə ɒn / (BrE)
at a time after the present or after the time mentioned
at a subsequent time

Examples

I didn't understand the lecture at first, but it became clearer later on.
We're meeting for dinner now, but we can go for a walk later on if you like.
You might not see the benefits immediately, but you'll notice them later on.

Synset Examples

he apologized subsequently
he's going to the store but he'll be back here later
it didn't happen until afterward
two hours after that

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afterward - at a later time; subsequently
  • Antonym: earlier - at or near the beginning of a period; before the time mentioned
