law of common fate

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈkɑmən feɪt / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈkɒmən feɪt / (BrE)
a Gestalt principle stating that elements moving in the same direction or functioning similarly are perceived as a group or unit
Gestalt principle in perception

Examples

When birds fly in a V-formation, the law of common fate leads us to perceive them as a single group.
According to the law of common fate, cars moving together in a convoy appear as a unified collection rather than separate vehicles.
Designers use the law of common fate to help users perceive moving objects as related on digital interfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gestalt principle - a rule describing how humans organize visual elements into groups or unified wholes

Etymology

Gestalt theory term from early 20th-century German psychology.
