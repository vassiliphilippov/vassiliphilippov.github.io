legal blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːɡəl ˈblaɪndnəs / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈblaɪndnəs / (BrE)
Visual impairment defined by law, usually as vision of 20/200 or worse in the better eye with the best possible correction, or a severely limited field of vision.
vision severely limited by legal definition
medical legal

Examples

Because of her legal blindness, she was eligible for certain disability benefits.
Children diagnosed with legal blindness often require specialized educational support.
The doctor confirmed that his vision loss met the criteria for legal blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blindness - total or partial lack of vision
