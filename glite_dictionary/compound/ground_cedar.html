ground cedar

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈsɛdɚ / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A trailing or low-growing variety of common juniper, often found in woodland areas and resembling true cedar in appearance.
trailing variety of juniper plant

Examples

The forest floor was covered in ground cedar, giving it a lush, green carpet.
Hiking through the woods, she stopped to admire a patch of ground cedar by the trail.
Gardeners sometimes use ground cedar as a decorative plant for shady areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - an evergreen shrub or small tree of the genus Juniperus

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌsidɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌsiːdə / (BrE)
A North American club moss (Lycopodium complanatum) resembling true cedars, with creeping stems and evergreen branches; also known as creeping Jenny or northern running-pine.
a North American club moss (Lycopodium complanatum)

Examples

We found a patch of ground cedar growing near the old stone wall.
The forest floor was carpeted with vibrant green ground cedar.
Botanists collected samples of ground cedar for their research on club mosses.
