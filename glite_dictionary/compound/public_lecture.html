public lecture

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈlɛkt͡ʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈlɛktʃə / (BrE)
a lecture or talk on a particular subject, given to an audience to which anyone may be admitted
lecture open to the public

Examples

The university organized a public lecture on climate change that attracted hundreds of attendees.
She was invited to deliver a public lecture at the local science museum.
Many of the city's public lectures are free and open to anyone interested.

Synset Examples

he attended a lecture on telecommunications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - an educational talk to an audience, especially one of students in a university
