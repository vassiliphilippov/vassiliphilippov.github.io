white fungus

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / waɪt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
a disease-causing fungus that infects living fish, tadpoles, and their eggs, producing a white, cotton-like growth on their bodies, especially on fins and other extremities
fungus causing white disease on fish and amphibians

Examples

The goldfish developed white fungus on its tail after being injured in the tank.
Veterinarians recommend isolating any fish showing signs of white fungus to prevent spread.
White fungus often appears as a cottony growth on the fins of affected fish.
