commie block

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmi blɑk / (AmE), / ˈkɒmi blɒk / (BrE)
A large, utilitarian apartment building, typically associated with communist-era architecture, characterized by a stark, functional design.
utilitarian communist-era apartment building
informal

Examples

My grandparents still live in a commie block on the outskirts of the city.
Many immigrants were first housed in a commie block after arriving in the country.
The new film features scenes set inside a dilapidated commie block.

Synset Examples

They lived in a massive commie block that housed hundreds of families.
The city's skyline was dominated by a stark commie block.
The old apartment complex looked just like a commie block from the 70s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panelák - prefabricated apartment building common in former Eastern Bloc countries

Etymology

‘Commie’ is an informal abbreviation of ‘communist’, combined with ‘block’ (apartment building).
