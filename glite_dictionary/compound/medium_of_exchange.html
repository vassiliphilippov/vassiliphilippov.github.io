medium of exchange

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiəm əv ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəm əv ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
something that is widely accepted in payment for goods and services and recognized as a standard of value within an economy, such as money.
generally accepted means of payment

Examples

Throughout history, gold has served as a medium of exchange in many societies.
Bartering is no longer common because we now use money as a medium of exchange.
Credit cards act as a modern medium of exchange, facilitating online purchases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money - official currency or coins used to pay for things

Etymology

From Latin 'medium' (means, intermediate) and English 'exchange'.
