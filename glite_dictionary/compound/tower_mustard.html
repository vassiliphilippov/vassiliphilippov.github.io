tower mustard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊ.ɚ ˈmʌs.tɚd / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə ˈmʌs.təd / (BrE)
A tall, erect cress (Arabis glabra) widely distributed in Europe, known for its narrow leaves and yellow flowers.
a tall European cress plant

Examples

You can find tower mustard growing along dry, grassy banks in many parts of Europe.
The yellow blossoms of tower mustard appear in early summer.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining populations of tower mustard in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabis glabra - species name for tower mustard

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊər ˌmʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈtaʊə ˌmʌstəd / (BrE)
A European plant with stiff, erect stems, sometimes classified in the genus Turritis; also known as Arabis glabra.
a European cress with stiff erect stems

Examples

The tower mustard grows best in dry, calcareous soils across much of Europe.
Botanists often debate whether tower mustard should be placed in the genus Turritis or Arabis.
You can recognize tower mustard by its tall, slender stems and small yellowish flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabis glabra - scientific name for tower mustard
  • Synonym: Turritis glabra - alternative scientific classification of tower mustard

Etymology

Likely from the resemblance of the plant's tall flower spikes to a tower; 'mustard' from its cress-like flavor.
