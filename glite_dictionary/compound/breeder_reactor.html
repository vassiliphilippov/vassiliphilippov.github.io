breeder reactor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːdər riˈæktər / (AmE), / ˈbriːdə rɪˈæktə / (BrE)
a nuclear reactor that generates more fissile material than it consumes during operation
nuclear reactor producing more fuel than it uses

Examples

A breeder reactor can generate more nuclear fuel than it consumes, making it highly efficient.
Many experts believe that breeder reactors could help address future energy shortages.
The construction of a breeder reactor often raises complex environmental and safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear reactor - a device used to initiate and control a sustained nuclear chain reaction
