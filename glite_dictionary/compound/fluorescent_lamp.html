fluorescent lamp

1. NOUN

/ fluəˈrɛsənt læmp / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɛsənt lamp / (BrE)
An electric lamp consisting of a glass tube coated inside with a fluorescent material, where mercury vapor produces ultraviolet light that the coating converts into visible light.
type of electric light with a fluorescent tube

Examples

The new office was fitted with energy-efficient fluorescent lamps to reduce electricity costs.
He replaced the old bulb with a fluorescent lamp to get brighter, cooler light in the kitchen.
Many schools use fluorescent lamps because they last much longer than incandescent bulbs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incandescent lamp - lamp that produces light by heating a filament until it glows
  • Synonym: fluorescent tube - the tubular part of a fluorescent lamp

Etymology

From 'fluorescent', relating to fluorescence, and 'lamp'; 'fluorescent' from Latin 'fluo' (to flow) via 'fluorescence'.
