English setter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsɛtər / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsɛtə / (BrE)
a breed of dog originating in England, with a long silky white coat, often marked with colored patches, and a feathered tail, bred primarily as a hunting and retrieving dog
breed of dog with silky white coat, England

Examples

The English setter is known for its friendly temperament and elegant appearance.
She adopted an English setter with chestnut markings from the local animal shelter.
During the hunt, the English setter expertly located birds in the tall grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setter - breed of dog trained to indicate the presence of game birds

Etymology

From English, denoting origin, and setter, referring to the dog's hunting behavior of 'setting' (pointing out) game.
