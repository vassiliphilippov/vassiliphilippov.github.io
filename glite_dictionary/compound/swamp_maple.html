swamp maple

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈmeɪ.pəl / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈmeɪ.pəl / (BrE)
A maple tree found in eastern and central North America, known for its five-lobed leaves that turn scarlet and yellow in autumn.
North American maple with colorful autumn leaves

Examples

The swamp maple is prized for its brilliant red foliage in the fall.
Wetlands across the region are dotted with clusters of swamp maple.
A swamp maple can thrive in areas where the soil is frequently saturated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red maple - another common name for Acer rubrum, a North American maple species
