psychotic belief

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑtɪk bɪˈlif / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒtɪk bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
an erroneous or false belief that persists despite evidence to the contrary, typically occurring in psychotic disorders
false belief held despite contrary evidence (psychology)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited a psychotic belief that he was being watched by invisible agents.
One symptom of schizophrenia can be holding a psychotic belief despite all evidence to the contrary.
Challenging a psychotic belief in therapy requires sensitivity and patience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a firmly held false belief resistant to contrary evidence
  • Antonym: rational belief - a belief based on logic, reason, or evidence
