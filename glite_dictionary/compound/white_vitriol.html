white vitriol

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈvɪtri.əl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈvɪtrɪ.əl / (BrE)
a colorless, water-soluble crystalline powder (zinc sulfate) used as a mordant, wood preservative, or in the electrodeposition of zinc
a form of zinc sulfate used in industry

Examples

The laboratory technician measured a small amount of white vitriol for the experiment.
White vitriol is commonly used in the process of electroplating to deposit zinc on metal surfaces.
They treated the wood with white vitriol to help protect it from decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc sulfate - a colorless, crystalline, water-soluble compound used in industry and medicine

Etymology

From the 'white' color of the crystals and 'vitriol', an old name for various sulfates.
