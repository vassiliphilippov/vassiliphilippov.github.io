genus Lasiocampa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs læsi.oʊˈkæmpə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs læsi.əʊˈkæmpə / (BrE)
The type genus of the moth family Lasiocampidae, commonly known as eggars.
type genus of eggar moths

Examples

The genus Lasiocampa includes a number of large, hairy moth species found in Europe and Asia.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Lasiocampa.
Members of the genus Lasiocampa are commonly called eggars due to the appearance of their cocoons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lasiocampidae - A family of moths known as eggars, snout moths, or lappet moths

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and Greek 'lasios' (hairy) + 'kampē' (caterpillar).
