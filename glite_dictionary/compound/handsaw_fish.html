handsaw fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌsɔ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhændˌsɔː fɪʃ / (BrE)
a soft-finned marine fish of the genus Alepisaurus, known for its elongated body and sharp, saw-like snout.
marine fish of Alepisaurus genus

Examples

The handsaw fish is noted for its sharp, blade-like jaw which it uses to catch prey.
Marine biologists encountered a rare handsaw fish during their deep-sea expedition.
The unique features of the handsaw fish make it easily distinguishable from other predatory fish.
