food allergy

1. NOUN

/ fuːd ˈælɚdʒi / (AmE), / fuːd ˈælədʒi / (BrE)
An abnormal immune response triggered by eating certain foods, resulting in symptoms such as hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing.
allergic reaction to food
medical

Examples

Many schools have adopted policies to help children with food allergies stay safe.
After eating peanuts, his food allergy caused him to break out in hives.
She carries an epinephrine injector in case of a severe food allergy reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allergy - hypersensitivity reaction by the immune system
  • Antonym: food intolerance - digestive system reaction to food, not immune-based
