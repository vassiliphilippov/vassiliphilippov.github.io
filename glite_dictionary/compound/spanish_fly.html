Spanish fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ flʌɪ / (BrE)
a small, bright green beetle (Lytta vesicatoria) of southern Europe, known for a substance it produces that causes blisters
a bright green blister beetle of southern Europe

Examples

The Spanish fly is notorious for its toxic properties and striking green color.
Collectors sometimes seek out the Spanish fly for scientific study.
Farmers in southern Europe are well aware of the effects of the Spanish fly on livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister beetle - beetle that secretes a substance causing blisters
