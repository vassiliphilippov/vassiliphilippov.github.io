false nettle

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈnɛtl̩ / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Boehmeria, resembling a nettle but lacking stinging hairs
plant resembling nettle, no stinging hairs

Examples

The garden was overrun with false nettle thriving in the shade.
Unlike true nettles, false nettle will not sting if you touch its leaves.
We identified the plant as false nettle by its soft, hairless stems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nettle - any plant of the genus Urtica; stinging herb

Etymology

So named because the plant resembles true nettles (genus Urtica) but lacks their stinging hairs.
