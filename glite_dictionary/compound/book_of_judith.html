Book of Judith

1. PROPN

/ ˌbʊk əv ˈdʒuːdɪθ / (AmE), / ˌbʊk əv ˈdʒuːdɪθ / (BrE)
An Apocryphal book recounting how Judith saved her people, included in some versions of the Bible.
an Apocryphal Biblical book about Judith's heroism

Examples

The Book of Judith tells the story of a courageous woman who saved her people from invasion.
Scholars often debate the historical accuracy of the events described in the Book of Judith.
In some Christian traditions, the Book of Judith is read during religious festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apocrypha - biblical or related writings not forming part of the accepted canon of Scripture
