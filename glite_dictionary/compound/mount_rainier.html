Mount Rainier

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ɹeɪˈnɪɹ / (AmE), / maʊnt reɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a mountain peak in central Washington, USA, and the highest point in the Cascade Range, reaching 14,410 feet (4,392 meters) in elevation
a high mountain peak in Washington, USA

Examples

Many climbers attempt to summit Mount Rainier each summer, drawn by its challenging conditions.
On a clear day, you can see Mount Rainier towering over the Seattle skyline.
The glaciers of Mount Rainier provide fresh water to surrounding areas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cascade Range - a major mountain range in western North America

Etymology

Named after Rear Admiral Peter Rainier, a British Royal Navy officer.
