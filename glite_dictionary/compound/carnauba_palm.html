carnauba palm

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈnaʊbə palm / (AmE), / kɑːˈnaʊbə palm / (BrE)
A Brazilian fan palm (Copernicia prunifera) with an edible root; notable as the source of carnauba wax and a useful leaf fiber.
Brazilian palm tree source of carnauba wax

Examples

The carnauba palm is prized for its wax, often used in polishes and cosmetics.
Farmers in northeastern Brazil harvest the leaves of the carnauba palm to extract fiber and wax.
Known as the 'tree of life' in its native region, the carnauba palm plays a crucial role in local economies.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'carnaúba', of Tupi origin.
