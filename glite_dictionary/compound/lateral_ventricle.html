lateral ventricle

1. NOUN

/ ˈlætərəl ˈvɛntrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlatərəl ˈvɛntrɪkəl / (BrE)
either of two horseshoe-shaped cavities in each cerebral hemisphere of the brain, which are filled with cerebrospinal fluid and communicate with the third ventricle via the foramen of Monro
cavity in the brain's cerebral hemisphere
medical

Examples

Swelling in a lateral ventricle can cause increased pressure in the brain.
The MRI scan revealed fluid accumulation in the left lateral ventricle.
Each cerebral hemisphere contains a lateral ventricle filled with cerebrospinal fluid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: third ventricle - the midline ventricle of the brain connected to lateral ventricles via the foramen of Monro

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of or belonging to the side) and 'ventriculus' (small cavity or chamber).
