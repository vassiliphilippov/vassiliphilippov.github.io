to leave the field fallow

1. VERB

/ liv ðə fiːld ˈfæloʊ / (AmE), / liːv ðə fiːld ˈfæləʊ / (BrE)
to refrain from cultivating a field for a period so that it can recover its fertility
not cultivate land for recovery

Examples

After harvesting, the farmer left the field fallow to allow the soil to recover.
Crop rotation requires that you leave the field fallow every few years.
They decided to leave the field fallow during the dry season to improve next year’s yield.

Synset Examples

The farmer left the field fallow to restore its nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallow - (of land) ploughed but left unsown to restore fertility
