common fraction

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
A fraction in which both the numerator and the denominator are integers, typically written with one integer above another separated by a line (e.g. 3/4).
fraction expressed as numerator/denominator

Examples

Write three-quarters as a common fraction before converting to a decimal.
In the equation, 5/8 is represented as a common fraction.
A common fraction such as 1/2 can also be written as a decimal, 0.5.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decimal fraction - fraction where the denominator is a power of ten, written with a decimal point
