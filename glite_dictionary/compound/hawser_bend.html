hawser bend

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔzɚ bɛnd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːzə bend / (BrE)
a knot used to join the ends of two ropes, especially when thick or heavy lines are involved
knot for joining two ropes

Examples

The sailors used a hawser bend to join the two thick mooring lines together.
Before towing the boat, he secured the ropes with a hawser bend to ensure they would not come apart.
Learning to tie a strong hawser bend is essential for working with heavy-duty cables at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheet bend - a type of knot for joining two ropes of different thicknesses
  • Synonym: reef knot - a knot used to join two ends of a rope, typically not for heavy loads

Etymology

From 'hawser', meaning a thick rope or cable, and 'bend', an old term for tying a knot.
