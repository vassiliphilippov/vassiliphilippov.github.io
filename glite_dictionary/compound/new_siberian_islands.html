New Siberian Islands

1. PROPN

/ nuː saɪˈbɪriən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / njuː saɪˈbɪəriən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the Arctic Ocean, north of Siberia and east of the Laptev Sea
islands north of Siberia in the Arctic Ocean

Examples

Few people have ever set foot on the New Siberian Islands due to their remote location.
Scientists travel to the New Siberian Islands to study Arctic wildlife and permafrost.
The climate of the New Siberian Islands is among the harshest on the planet.

Etymology

Named for their location near Siberia and their relatively later discovery compared to the Old Siberian Islands.
