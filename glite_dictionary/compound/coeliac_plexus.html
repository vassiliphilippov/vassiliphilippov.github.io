coeliac plexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːli.æk ˈplɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈsiːli.æk ˈplɛk.səs / (BrE)
a large network of sympathetic nerves in the abdomen located behind the stomach, involved in the innervation of abdominal organs
network of nerves behind the stomach
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the coeliac plexus.
Pain in the upper abdomen can sometimes be traced back to the coeliac plexus.
A block of the coeliac plexus is often performed to relieve certain types of chronic abdominal pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solar plexus - a complex network of nerves in the abdomen; often referred to synonymously with coeliac plexus

Etymology

From Greek 'koilia' (belly, abdomen) and Latin 'plexus' (braid, network).
