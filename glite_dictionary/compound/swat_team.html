SWAT team

1. NOUN

/ swɑt tim / (AmE), / swɒt tiːm / (BrE)
a specialized group of police officers trained to handle especially dangerous or violent incidents, such as hostage situations or armed confrontations
special police unit for dangerous situations
american

Examples

The SWAT team arrived quickly to handle the hostage crisis.
After hours of negotiation, the SWAT team entered the building and arrested the suspects.
A SWAT team is often called in when there are reports of armed and dangerous criminals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police squad - a group of police officers who work together

Etymology

Acronym from 'Special Weapons And Tactics', originally used by police departments in the United States.
