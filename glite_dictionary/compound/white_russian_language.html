White Russian language

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈrʌʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈrʌʃən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the East Slavic language spoken in Belarus, also known as Belarusian.
East Slavic language spoken in Belarus

Examples

He studied the White Russian language at university as part of his degree in Slavic studies.
The White Russian language is rich in proverbs and folk traditions.
Some older texts refer to Belarusian as the White Russian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belarusian - the official language of Belarus

Etymology

The term 'White Russian' comes from an old name for the region of Belarus, meaning 'White Ruthenia' or 'White Rus'.
