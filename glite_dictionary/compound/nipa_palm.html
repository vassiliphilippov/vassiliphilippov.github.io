nipa palm

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːpə pɑm / (AmE), / ˈniːpə pɑːm / (BrE)
a creeping semi-aquatic palm of the genus Nipa found in mangrove swamps and tidal estuaries, valued for its sap (used to make liquor), thatching leaves, and edible seeds
tropical palm found in mangrove swamps

Examples

The leaves of the nipa palm are traditionally used for thatching roofs in Southeast Asia.
Farmers collect the sweet sap from the nipa palm to produce a local alcoholic beverage.
The fruit of the nipa palm contains edible seeds enjoyed as a snack in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangrove - tropical coastal tree growing in tidal estuaries and swamps

Etymology

From Malay *nipah*, referring to the palm species.
