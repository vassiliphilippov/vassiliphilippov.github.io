azo dye

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪzoʊ daɪ / (AmE), / ˈæzəʊ daɪ / (BrE)
any of a group of synthetic dyes that contain one or more azo groups (–N=N–) in their molecular structure.
synthetic dye with azo group
medical

Examples

Many textiles are colored using azo dye because of its vivid shades.
Regulations limit the use of certain azo dyes due to their potential health risks.
The bright red in the fabric was achieved with an azo dye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aniline dye - any dye derived from aniline, often related to azo dyes

Etymology

From French 'azote' (nitrogen), itself from Greek 'azotos' (lifeless) + 'dye'.
