mumbo jumbo

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌmboʊ ˈdʒʌmboʊ / (AmE), / ˌmʌmbəʊ ˈdʒʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
language, ideas, or rituals that are confusing or meaningless, often intended to obscure the truth or impress others
meaningless or confusing language or rituals

Examples

The politician's speech was full of mumbo jumbo and failed to answer any real questions.
They performed an ancient mumbo jumbo ceremony to scare away evil spirits.
I don't understand all this technical mumbo jumbo—can you explain it in plain English?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - meaningless or unintelligible speech or writing

Etymology

Probably from Mandingo *Maamajomboo*, name of a masked dancer involved in rituals.
