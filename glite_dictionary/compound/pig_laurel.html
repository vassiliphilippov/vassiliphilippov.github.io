pig laurel

1. NOUN

/ pɪɡ ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / pɪɡ ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
a North American dwarf shrub (Kalmia angustifolia) with narrow leaves and small red flowers, poisonous to young livestock
North American shrub with narrow leaves and red flowers

Examples

The farmer warned that pig laurel could be toxic if ingested by calves.
We found a cluster of pig laurel blooming along the edge of the forest.
Gardeners should be cautious when planting pig laurel near areas grazed by livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain laurel - North American shrub with broader leaves and pink or white flowers
