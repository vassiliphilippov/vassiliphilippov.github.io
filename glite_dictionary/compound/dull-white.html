dull-white

1. ADJ

/ dʌl waɪt / (AmE), / dʌl waɪt / (BrE)
having a white color that is not bright or shiny; lacking in gloss or vividness.
white color lacking brightness or shine

Examples

The sky was a dull-white as snow clouds gathered on the horizon.
She wore a dull-white dress that looked faded with age.
The walls had been painted a dull-white, giving the room a gloomy appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off-white - a white color with a slight shade of another color
