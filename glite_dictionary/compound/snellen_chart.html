Snellen chart

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɛlən tʃɑrt / (AmE), / ˈsnɛlən tʃɑːt / (BrE)
a chart consisting of rows of letters in decreasing size, used to measure visual acuity
eye chart for testing vision
medical

Examples

The optometrist asked me to read the Snellen chart to determine my visual acuity.
Most clinics use a Snellen chart as part of routine eye examinations.
Her blurred vision became obvious when she could not read the largest line on the Snellen chart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye chart - a chart used to measure visual acuity

Etymology

Named after Dutch ophthalmologist Herman Snellen, who developed the chart in 1862.
