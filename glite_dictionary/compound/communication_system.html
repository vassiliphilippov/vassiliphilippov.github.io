communication system

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system comprising physical plants and equipment that enables the transmission and exchange of information.
facility for transmitting information

Examples

The military invested heavily in a secure communication system to coordinate their operations.
Upgrading the hospital's communication system improved response times during emergencies.
Modern businesses rely on a robust communication system to connect offices across the globe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network - an interconnected group or system for communication
  • Synonym: telecommunication - communication over a distance by cable, telegraph, telephone, or broadcasting

2. NOUN

/ kəˌmjunəˈkeɪʃən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A structured arrangement of devices, methods, or processes used to transmit, receive, or exchange information.
system for transmitting information

Examples

The company invested in a new communication system to improve coordination between offices.
Birds use a complex communication system involving calls and displays.
The failure in the communication system caused a delay in emergency response.
