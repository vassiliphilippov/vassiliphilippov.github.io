throwing stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊɪŋ stɪk / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊɪŋ stɪk / (BrE)
a curved piece of wood that, when thrown correctly, returns to the thrower; commonly associated with the boomerang
a curved stick that returns when thrown

Examples

The Aboriginal people are skilled in crafting and using the throwing stick.
My grandfather brought back a throwing stick from his travels in Australia.
With the proper technique, a throwing stick will curve and return to your hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boomerang - a curved flat piece of wood that can return to the thrower

2. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊɪŋ stɪk / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊɪŋ stɪk / (BrE)
a tool used to propel a dart or spear, especially in some Indigenous and ancient societies, increasing distance and speed
tool for propelling darts or spears

Examples

The hunter skillfully used his throwing stick to launch the spear farther than by hand alone.
Archaeologists discovered an ancient throwing stick among the artifacts at the dig site.
Some Aboriginal Australians traditionally employ a throwing stick for hunting small game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atlatl - a stick used to throw spears with greater force and distance
