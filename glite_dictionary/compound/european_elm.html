European elm

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ɛlm / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ɛlm / (BrE)
a broad, spreading elm tree with rough leaves, native to and widespread in Europe, also cultivated elsewhere
a common European elm tree species

Examples

The European elm is highly valued for its strong, durable wood.
Many parks across Europe feature the majestic European elm as a centerpiece.
Due to disease, the population of European elms has sharply declined in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a tree of the genus Ulmus
