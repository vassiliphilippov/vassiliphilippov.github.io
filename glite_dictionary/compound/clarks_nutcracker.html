Clark's nutcracker

1. NOUN

/ klɑrks ˈnʌtˌkrækər / (AmE), / klɑːks ˈnʌtˌkrækə / (BrE)
a grey-and-black bird of the crow family found in the mountains of western North America, known for feeding on pine seeds
western North American bird species

Examples

The Clark's nutcracker is often seen in high mountain pine forests gathering seeds.
Biologists study how the Clark's nutcracker helps pine trees regenerate by dispersing their seeds.
During our hike, we spotted a Clark's nutcracker perched on a lodgepole pine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutcracker - bird of the genus Nucifraga, related to crows, that cracks nuts

Etymology

Named after William Clark, American explorer, plus 'nutcracker' for its feeding habits.
