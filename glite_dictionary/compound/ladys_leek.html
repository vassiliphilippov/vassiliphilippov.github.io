lady's leek

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz liːk / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz liːk / (BrE)
A wild onion native to North America, having white to pinkish flowers, known scientifically as Allium tricoccum or related species.
North American wild onion with white to pink flowers

Examples

Every spring, hikers search for lady's leek in the forest to add to their meals.
The delicate flowers of lady's leek range in color from white to pale rose.
Some chefs prize lady's leek for its mild onion flavor and tender leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild leek - a North American wild onion closely related to lady's leek
  • Degree Higher: onion - edible bulb plant related to leeks and garlic
