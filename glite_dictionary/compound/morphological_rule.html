morphological rule

1. NOUN

/ mɔrfəˈlɑdʒɪkəl rul / (AmE), / mɔːfəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ruːl / (BrE)
a rule in linguistics that governs how words are formed from morphemes and how word forms are structured
linguistic rule for word formation

Examples

The English plural -s is formed according to a common morphological rule.
Linguists study morphological rules to understand how new words are created in different languages.
A morphological rule explains why 'unhappiness' is constructed from 'un-', 'happy', and '-ness'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonological rule - a rule describing how sounds change in a language

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form, shape) + -logia (study) + English rule.
