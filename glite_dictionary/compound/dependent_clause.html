dependent clause

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndənt klɔz / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt klɑːz / (BrE)
A clause in a complex sentence that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence and functions as a noun, adjective, or adverb within the sentence.
subordinate grammatical clause

Examples

When you see a dependent clause, remember that it cannot stand alone as a complete sentence.
The teacher asked students to identify the dependent clause in each example.
A dependent clause often begins with words like 'although' or 'because'.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independent clause - a clause that can stand alone as a complete sentence
  • Synonym: subordinate clause - another term for dependent clause, used interchangeably
