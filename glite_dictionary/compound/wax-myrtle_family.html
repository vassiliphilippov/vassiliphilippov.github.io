wax-myrtle family

1. NOUN

/ wæks ˈmɝːtəl ˈfæməli / (AmE), / wæks ˈmɜːtl ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, constituting the order Myricales, including aromatic shrubs and small trees such as the wax myrtle.
family of flowering, aromatic plants

Examples

The wax-myrtle family includes several shrubs that are prized for their fragrant leaves.
Scientists are studying the ecological roles of plants in the wax-myrtle family.
Members of the wax-myrtle family are often found in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bayberry - an aromatic shrub in the wax-myrtle family
