thoracic cavity

1. NOUN

/ θəˈræsɪk ˈkævəti / (AmE), / θəˈræsɪk ˈkævɪti / (BrE)
the space within the chest bounded by the ribs, diaphragm, and neck, containing the lungs, heart, and other organs
chest cavity containing lungs and heart
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctors carefully opened the thoracic cavity to access the heart.
An injury to the thoracic cavity can be life-threatening if it affects the lungs or major blood vessels.
The lungs are housed within the thoracic cavity, protected by the ribcage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abdominal cavity - body cavity below the diaphragm containing digestive organs

Etymology

From Greek 'thōrax' (breastplate, chest) and Latin 'cavitas' (cavity, hollow).
