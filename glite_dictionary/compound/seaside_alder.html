seaside alder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌsaɪd ˈɔːldər / (AmE), / ˈsiːsaɪd ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, with soft light brown wood, typically found in wet or coastal areas
shrub or tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The seaside alder grows along riverbanks and wetlands in the southeastern United States.
Botanists studied the distribution of the seaside alder in Alabama and Georgia.
The wood of the seaside alder is valued for its lightness and softness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a type of tree or shrub in the birch family, commonly found near water
