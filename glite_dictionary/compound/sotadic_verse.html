sotadic verse

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈtædɪk vɜrs / (AmE), / səʊˈtadɪk vɜːs / (BrE)
Palindromic verse in which the sequence of characters or words is the same when read forwards and backwards; named after Sotades, an ancient Greek poet.
verse that reads the same forwards and backwards

Examples

The poet crafted a sotadic verse that could be read identically in both directions.
Classic examples of sotadic verse are prized for their linguistic ingenuity.
She spent months composing a sotadic verse for the literary competition.

Synset Examples

The sotadic metre or sotadic verse, which has been classified by ancient and modern scholars as a form of ionic metre, is one that reads backwards and forwards the same, as ‘llewd did I live, and evil I did dwell’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palindrome - a word or phrase that reads the same backwards as forwards

Etymology

Named after Sotades, an ancient Greek poet known for composing such verses.
