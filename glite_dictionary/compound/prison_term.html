prison term

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən ˌtɜrm / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən ˌtɜːm / (BrE)
the period of time that a person is required to spend in prison as punishment for a crime
period of time spent in prison

Examples

After being convicted, he began serving his prison term immediately.
She received a five-year prison term for fraud.
The judge imposed a lengthy prison term as a deterrent to others.

Synset Examples

he served a prison term of 15 months
his sentence was 5 to 10 years
he is doing time in the county jail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentence - the punishment assigned to a defendant found guilty by a court
