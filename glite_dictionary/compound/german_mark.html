German mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmən mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən mɑːk / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Germany before the adoption of the euro in 2002
former basic unit of currency in Germany

Examples

He exchanged his old German mark coins for euros at the bank.
The German mark was once one of Europe's strongest currencies.
I found a 50 German mark note in my grandfather's collection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - the official currency of the eurozone, which replaced the German mark in Germany
